detestable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈtɛstəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛstəbl / (BrE)
extremely unpleasant, offensive, or deserving of intense dislike
extremely unpleasant or offensive

Examples

Her detestable actions shocked everyone in the community.
I find animal cruelty utterly detestable and inexcusable.
The movie villain was so detestable that audiences cheered at his downfall.

Synset Examples

an abhorrent deed
the obscene massacre at Wounded Knee
morally repugnant customs
repulsive behavior
the most repulsive character in recent novels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abhorrent - causing disgust or hatred; repugnant
  • Synonym: repugnant - extremely distasteful or unacceptable
  • Antonym: delightful - highly pleasing; charming

Etymology

From Latin 'detestabilis' (to be detested), from 'detestari' (to curse or detest).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈtɛstəbl̩ / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛstəbl̩ / (BrE)
Deserving intense dislike or hatred; extremely unpleasant or offensive.
extremely unpleasant or hateful

Examples

The dictator's detestable actions shocked the entire world.
She found the practice of animal cruelty utterly detestable.
His detestable habit of lying made it hard to trust him.

Synset Examples

abominable treatment of prisoners
detestable vices
execrable crimes
consequences odious to those you govern

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abominable - causing moral revulsion; very bad or unpleasant
  • Synonym: odious - extremely unpleasant; repulsive
  • Antonym: admirable - arousing or deserving respect and approval

Etymology

From Old French detester, from Latin detestabilis (to be detested).
