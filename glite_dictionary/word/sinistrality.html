sinistrality

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌnɪˈstræl.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / sɪn.ɪsˈtræl.ə.ti / (BrE)
preference for using the left hand or side, especially in writing or other manual activities
preference for using the left hand

Examples

His pronounced sinistrality made him an outlier in a family of right-handed people.
Studies of sinistrality have shown correlations with certain brain structures.
The teacher accommodated the student's sinistrality by adjusting the seating arrangement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: left-handedness - the condition of being left-handed
  • Antonym: dexterity - skill in using the hands

Etymology

From Latin 'sinister' (left) + English suffix '-ality' (indicating quality or state).
