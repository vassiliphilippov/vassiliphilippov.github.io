to bestir

1. VERB

/ bɪˈstɝ / (AmE), / bɪˈstɜː / (BrE)
to make the effort to become active or to rouse oneself into action, especially when reluctant or inactive
to rouse oneself to action
archaic

Examples

He finally bestirred himself and got out of bed.
If we want to finish on time, we must bestir ourselves now.
Despite the loud alarm, she did not bestir until the sun was fully up.

Synset Examples

He finally bestirred himself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rouse - to wake or provoke someone into action
  • Synonym: stir - to begin to move or become active
  • Antonym: to laze - to be idle or inactive

Etymology

From Middle English bestirren, formed with be- prefix + stir (to move).
