freshly

1. ADV

/ ˈfrɛʃli / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃli / (BrE)
very recently; just done, made, or experienced.
very recently; just done

Examples

The bread was freshly baked and still warm from the oven.
She walked into a freshly painted room, the smell of paint still lingering in the air.
After the rain, the garden looked freshly watered and vibrant.

Synset Examples

they are newly married
newly raised objections
a newly arranged hairdo
grass new washed by the rain
a freshly cleaned floor
we are fresh out of tomatoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newly - very recently; just done or made

Etymology

From 'fresh' + '-ly', forming an adverb; 'fresh' from Old French 'frais', ultimately from Latin 'friscare'.
