counterperson

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌpɜːrsən / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌpɜːsən / (BrE)
a person who serves customers from behind a counter, especially in a restaurant, diner, or retail establishment
someone serving customers at a counter

Examples

The counterperson greeted every customer with a warm smile as they entered the diner.
As a counterperson, she prepared sandwiches and rang up orders during the lunch rush.
After a few weeks, the new counterperson had learned the names of all the regulars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: server - someone who serves food or drinks in a restaurant
  • Synonym: waiter - a man who serves food at a restaurant
  • Synonym: waitress - a woman who serves food at a restaurant

Etymology

Formed from 'counter' (a surface on which transactions take place) plus 'person'.
