lividness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvɪdnɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɪvɪdnɪs / (BrE)
an unnatural lack of color in the skin, as caused by bruising, illness, or extreme emotion
unnatural paleness or discoloration of skin

Examples

The lividness of his face alarmed the nurse, who called for immediate help.
After the fall, a distinct lividness spread across her cheek, indicating a deep bruise.
Emotional shock can cause a sudden lividness in one's complexion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pallor - unnatural paleness of the skin
  • Antonym: flush - a healthy reddish color in the skin

Etymology

From Latin 'lividus' (bluish, lead-colored) via French 'livide'.
