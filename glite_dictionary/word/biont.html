biont

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.ɒnt / (AmE), / ˈbʌɪ.ɒnt / (BrE)
An individual organism or discrete unit of living matter, especially as considered in biology.
individual living organism

Examples

Some scientists define a biont as any individual biological entity, from single cells to complex animals.
The study focused on the behavior of each biont in a controlled environment.
An ecosystem consists of many interacting bionts and their non-living surroundings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organism - any living thing; individual animal, plant, or single-celled life form

Etymology

From Greek 'bios' (life) + '-ont' (being, entity, from 'ontos', being).
