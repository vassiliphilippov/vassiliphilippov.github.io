dangerous

1. ADJ

/ ˈdeɪndʒərəs / (AmE), / ˈdeɪndʒərəs / (BrE)
likely to cause harm or injury; involving risk or danger.
likely to cause harm or risk

Examples

It is dangerous to drive on icy roads without proper tires.
The chemicals stored in that cabinet are extremely dangerous if handled incorrectly.
That was a dangerous decision, but it saved many lives.

Synset Examples

a dangerous criminal
a dangerous bridge
unemployment reached dangerous proportions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: safe - not likely to cause harm; free from danger
  • Synonym: hazardous - involving risk or danger

Etymology

From Latin 'periculosus' (dangerous), via Old French 'dangeros'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdeɪndʒərəs / (AmE), / ˈdeɪndʒərəs / (BrE)
likely to cause harm, injury, or serious consequences; involving risk or threat to safety or wellbeing.
likely to cause harm or risk

Examples

The mountain trail becomes especially dangerous during heavy rainstorms.
Handling toxic chemicals without proper equipment can be extremely dangerous.
People were evacuated from the dangerous area near the collapsing bridge.

Synset Examples

a dangerous operation
a grave situation
a grave illness
grievous bodily harm
a serious wound
a serious turn of events
a severe case of pneumonia
a life-threatening disease

Related Terms

  • Antonym: safe - not likely to cause harm or danger
  • Synonym: hazardous - involving risk or danger

Etymology

From Old French *dangereus*, from *danger* (power, peril).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00654061-s
Synset POS: s
