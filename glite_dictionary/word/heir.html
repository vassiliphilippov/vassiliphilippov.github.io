heir

1. NOUN

/ ɛr / (AmE), / eə / (BrE)
a person who is legally entitled to receive the property, title, or rights of someone who has died, either by law or by the terms of a will
person entitled to inherit property

Examples

As the only heir, she inherited her father's entire fortune.
The king named his nephew as his official heir to the throne.
When the will was read, it became clear who the rightful heir was.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heiress - female person who inherits property
  • Antonym: testator - a person who makes a will

Etymology

From Old French *eir*, from Latin *heres* (heir).

2. NOUN

/ ɛr / (AmE), / eə / (BrE)
a person who receives or has the legal right to receive a title, office, or property when its previous holder dies
person inheriting title or office

Examples

The king had no direct heir to the throne, which led to a lengthy succession crisis.
After her father's death, she became the sole heir to his estate.
The company's founder announced his daughter as the official heir to the presidency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: successor - a person who takes over an office or position after someone else
  • Antonym: predecessor - a person who held an office or position before someone else

Etymology

From Old French 'eir', from Latin 'heres' meaning 'heir'.
