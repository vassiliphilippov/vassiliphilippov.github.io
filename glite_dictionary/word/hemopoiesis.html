hemopoiesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmoʊpɔɪˈiːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊpɔɪˈiːsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which blood cells are formed, especially within the bone marrow
formation of blood cells
medical

Examples

The disruption of hemopoiesis can lead to various blood disorders.
Bone marrow is the primary site of hemopoiesis in adults.
Certain diseases may negatively affect the process of hemopoiesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hematopoiesis - the formation of blood cellular components

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + -poiesis (making, formation).
