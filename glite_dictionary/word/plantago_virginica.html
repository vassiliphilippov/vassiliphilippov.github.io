Plantago virginica

1. NOUN

/ plænˈteɪɡoʊ vərˈdʒɪnɪkə / (AmE), / plænˈtɑːɡəʊ vɜːˈdʒɪnɪkə / (BrE)
A North American annual or biennial plant with long soft hairs on the leaves, commonly known as Virginia plantain.
North American plant with hairy leaves

Examples

Plantago virginica is often found growing in open fields and along roadsides in the eastern United States.
The leaves of Plantago virginica can be recognized by their distinctive soft hairs.
Gardeners sometimes mistake Plantago virginica for other species of plantain due to its similar appearance.

Etymology

From the genus name Plantago (Latin for 'plantain') and the species epithet virginica, referring to Virginia, USA.
