tetragen

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛtrəˌdʒæn / (AmE), / ˈtɛtrɪˌdʒan / (BrE)
any of the chemical elements in the carbon group of the periodic table, which includes carbon, silicon, germanium, tin, lead, and flerovium.
element of the carbon group

Examples

Silicon is a well-known tetragen with widespread applications in the electronics industry.
The properties of a tetragen can vary greatly from carbon to lead.
Recent studies have explored the behavior of each tetragen under high-pressure conditions.

Synset Examples

Carbon, the lightest carbon group element, sublimates at 3825 °C.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon group - the chemical group in the periodic table containing carbon and related elements

Etymology

From Greek 'tetra-' (four) and '-genēs' (born from or produced), referring to group 14 (carbon group) having four valence electrons.
