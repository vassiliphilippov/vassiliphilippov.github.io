Tapiridae

1. NOUN

/ təˈpɪrəˌdiː / (AmE), / təˈpɪərɪˌdiː / (BrE)
The biological family comprising tapirs and their extinct relatives.
biological family of tapirs

Examples

The family Tapiridae includes all living species of tapirs as well as their extinct relatives.
Fossils belonging to Tapiridae have been discovered in both the Old and New Worlds.
Members of Tapiridae are known for their distinctive, flexible snouts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapir - large, herbivorous mammal with a short, prehensile snout

Etymology

From New Latin, from *Tapirus* (tapir genus) + -idae (indicating family in zoological taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ təˈpɪrɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / təˈpɪərɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of large, herbivorous mammals comprising the tapirs and their extinct relatives
mammal family including tapirs

Examples

The Tapiridae family includes both living tapirs and many extinct species.
Researchers study the evolutionary history of Tapiridae to understand their adaptation.
Fossil records suggest that Tapiridae were once much more widespread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapir - a large, nocturnal, plant-eating mammal with a flexible snout

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Tapiridae', from genus name 'Tapirus' + suffix '-idae' (used for animal families).
