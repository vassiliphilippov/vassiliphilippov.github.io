grief

1. NOUN

/ ɡriːf / (AmE), / ɡriːf / (BrE)
A cause of deep sadness or unhappiness; something that brings about sorrow.
something causing great sadness

Examples

The accident was a lasting grief to the whole family.
Losing his childhood home became a lifelong grief for him.
The sudden end of her career was a source of deep grief.

Synset Examples

her death was a great grief to John

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorrow - deep distress or sadness caused by loss or disappointment

Etymology

From Old French 'grief' (wrong, injustice, misfortune), from Latin 'gravis' (heavy).

2. NOUN

/ ɡriːf / (AmE), / ɡriːf / (BrE)
Deep and intense sorrow, especially caused by the death of someone loved.
deep sorrow, especially from loss

Examples

He was overcome with grief after the passing of his grandmother.
The community shared a profound sense of grief at the loss of their leader.
Coping with grief can take a long time and requires support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorrow - a feeling of deep distress caused by loss or disappointment
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness

Etymology

From Old French *grief* (wrong, misfortune, hardship), from Latin *gravare* (to make heavy), from *gravis* (heavy).
