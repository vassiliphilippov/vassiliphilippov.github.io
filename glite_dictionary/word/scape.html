scape

1. NOUN

/ skeɪp / (AmE), / skeɪp / (BrE)
a long, leafless flower stalk that rises directly from the ground, as in tulips or onions
leafless flower stalk from ground

Examples

The tulip's scape supported a single, vibrant red blossom.
A garlic scape can be harvested and used in cooking for its mild flavor.
Some plants, like dandelions, grow a tall scape before they flower.

Etymology

From Latin scapus (shaft, stalk), used in botanical terminology.
