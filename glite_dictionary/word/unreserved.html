unreserved

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈzɝːvd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈzɜːvd / (BrE)
Not holding back; open, candid, or without restraint or caution.
not holding back; open or candid

Examples

Her unreserved remarks surprised everyone at the meeting.
He welcomed us with unreserved enthusiasm and friendliness.
The artist expressed her opinions in an unreserved manner during the interview.

Synset Examples

unreserved behavior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reserved - slow to reveal emotion or opinions; restrained
  • Synonym: candid - truthful and straightforward; frank

Etymology

Formed with prefix un- (not) + reserved (from reserve).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈzɝːvd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈzɜːvd / (BrE)
not restricted, held back, or kept for a particular person or purpose; free and open.
not restricted or kept back; open

Examples

His unreserved support for the proposal surprised everyone in the meeting.
Tickets for the concert are for unreserved seating, so arrive early for the best spots.
She spoke with unreserved honesty about the challenges she had faced.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reserved - kept for a particular person or purpose; restrained

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + reserved (from Latin reservare, to keep back).
