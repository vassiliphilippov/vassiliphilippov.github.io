leprose

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛpˌroʊs / (AmE), / ˈlɛprəʊs / (BrE)
(of a surface) rough or scaly to the touch; covered with scales or scurf.
rough or scaly to the touch

Examples

The leprose bark of the tree made it uncomfortable to lean against.
Some lichens have a distinctly leprose texture, appearing powdery or scurf-like.
Under the microscope, the insect's wing was revealed to be leprose, covered in tiny scales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scaly - covered with or resembling scales
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; free from perceptible projections, lumps, or indentations

Etymology

From New Latin leprosus, from Latin lepra (leprosy), referring to the appearance of scales.
