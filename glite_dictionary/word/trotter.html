trotter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɑtər / (AmE), / ˈtrɒtə / (BrE)
the foot of a pig or sheep, especially when prepared and eaten as food
foot of a pig or sheep, used as food

Examples

She ordered a plate of spicy trotters at the restaurant.
Cooking trotters requires slow simmering to achieve a tender texture.
The chef prepared the trotter with herbs and vegetables for added flavor.

Etymology

From the verb 'trot', relating to the animal's gait.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɑːt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɒtə / (BrE)
a horse that has been trained to trot, especially one used in harness racing
horse trained for harness racing

Examples

The trotter won first place in the annual harness race.
Breeders hope to raise a champion trotter for competitive racing.
Each trotter must maintain a steady pace throughout the track.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pacer - a horse trained to move both legs on the same side together, often used in harness racing
