counterespionage

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaʊntərɪˈspɪɒnɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntərˈɛspɪɒnɪdʒ / (BrE)
The activity of detecting and preventing espionage by enemy agents; measures taken to spy on or thwart spies.
detecting and preventing espionage

Examples

The agency increased its counterespionage efforts after several security breaches were discovered.
He worked in counterespionage during the Cold War, tracking enemy spies on home soil.
Effective counterespionage is vital to national security operations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: espionage - the practice of spying or using spies

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) + 'espionage,' from French 'espionnage' (spying).
