chronograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑːnəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈkrɒnəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A watch or device that is capable of measuring and recording short time intervals with precision, often in addition to displaying the time.
precise stopwatch or timer

Examples

The new sports model includes a chronograph for timing races.
He bought a luxury chronograph to add precision timing to his collection.
Pilots often rely on a chronograph for accurate flight calculations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stopwatch - a handheld timepiece designed to measure the exact duration of an event

Etymology

From Greek 'khronos' (time) + '-graph' (writing).
