Hasidism

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑː.sɪˌdɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈhæs.ɪˌdɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A Jewish religious movement originating in Eastern Europe in the 18th century, characterized by mystical religious experience, strict religious observance, and strong community life.
sect of Orthodox Judaism; mystical Jewish movement

Examples

Many followers of Hasidism dress in distinctive traditional clothing.
Hasidism emphasizes joyful worship and spiritual connection with God.
The history of Hasidism traces back to 18th-century Eastern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Orthodox Judaism - The branch of Judaism strictly observing traditional beliefs and practices

Etymology

From Hebrew ḥasid (pious) + -ism; rooted in the Hebrew word for 'piety'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑː.sɪ.dɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈhæs.ɪ.dɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A Jewish religious movement originating in eastern Europe in the 18th century, characterized by strict adherence to traditional laws, mysticism, and the leadership of a Rebbe.
Jewish religious movement from 18th-century eastern Europe

Examples

Many Jewish communities in New York include members of Hasidism.
The spiritual teachings of Hasidism focus on joy and devotion in worship.
Modern Hasidism is known for its emphasis on the leadership of a Rebbe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Orthodox Judaism - The branch of Judaism that strictly observes traditional beliefs and practices

Etymology

From Hebrew ḥasid (pious), meaning 'piety' or 'kindness'.
