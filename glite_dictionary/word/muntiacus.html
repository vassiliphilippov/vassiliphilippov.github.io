Muntiacus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌn.tiˈeɪ.kəs / (AmE), / ˌmʌn.tiˈɑː.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of small deer native to South and Southeast Asia, commonly known as muntjacs.
a genus of small Asian deer

Examples

The genus Muntiacus includes several species of barking deer found in Asia.
Scientists studied the distribution of Muntiacus in tropical forests.
Conservation efforts aim to protect various species within Muntiacus from habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muntjac - a small deer of the genus Muntiacus

Etymology

From modern Latin, named by Rafinesque in 1815, from the Sundanese 'muntjak' (name for the animal).
