to manoeuver

1. VERB

/ məˈnuːvər / (AmE), / məˈnuːvə / (BrE)
to perform a movement or series of movements, especially in military or naval tactics, to gain an advantage in attack or defense
perform a tactical movement (military/naval)

Examples

The navy attempted to manoeuver around the enemy fleet at dawn.
The general manoeuvered his troops into a better position for attack.
They are manoeuvering carefully to avoid direct confrontation with the opposition forces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maneuver - alternative spelling, same meaning (US)

Etymology

From French 'manœuvrer' (to handle, operate), based on Latin 'manu operari' (work by hand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01112274-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ məˈnuːvər / (AmE), / məˈnjuːvə / (BrE)
to control or guide the movement or course of something or someone
to control or guide direction or movement

Examples

She skillfully manoeuvered the car through the narrow streets of the old city.
The captain manoeuvered the ship away from the rocks just in time.
He manoeuvered his way through the crowded room to reach the exit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steer - to guide or control the course of a vehicle or vessel
  • Synonym: direct - to manage or guide by advice, helpful information, instruction, etc.

Etymology

From French 'manoeuvrer', from Latin 'manu operari' (to work by hand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01935739-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ məˈnuːvər / (AmE), / məˈnjuːvə / (BrE)
to act skillfully or strategically in order to achieve a particular goal
act skillfully to achieve a goal

Examples

He manoeuvered skillfully through office politics to secure the promotion.
She manoeuvered herself into a position of influence within the organization.
They manoeuvered to gain control of the board by forming strategic alliances.

Synset Examples

He maneuvered to get the chairmanship
She maneuvered herself into the directorship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scheme - to make plans, especially in a cunning way to achieve a goal
  • Synonym: plot - to plan secretly, typically something harmful or illegal

Etymology

From French 'manœuvrer', ultimately from Latin 'manu' (hand) + 'operari' (to work).
