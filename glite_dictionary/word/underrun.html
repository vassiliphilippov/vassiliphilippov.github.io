to underrun

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈrʌn / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈrʌn / (BrE)
(of a rope, cable, or net) to haul onto a boat, especially for inspection or repair.
haul (rope or cable) onto a boat

Examples

The crew had to underrun the cable after the storm caused tangles.
We underrun the fishing nets every morning to check for damage.
They underran the line to inspect it for wear and tear.

Synset Examples

underrun the cable for repair

2. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈrʌn / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈrʌn / (BrE)
to run or pass underneath something.
to pass below something

Examples

The cable must underrun the bridge to avoid interfering with traffic above.
In this method, the pipeline will underrun the existing structure.
The river underruns the walkway for several miles before emerging on the other side.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overrun - to run or pass over or above something

Etymology

Formed from 'under-' (beneath) + 'run'.
