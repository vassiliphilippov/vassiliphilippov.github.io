Avena sterilis

1. NOUN

/ əˈviːnə stəˈrɪlɪs / (AmE), / əˈviːnə stəˈrɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of wild Mediterranean oat believed to be the ancestor of modern cultivated oats.
species of wild Mediterranean oat

Examples

Researchers studied Avena sterilis to understand the genetic origins of domesticated oats.
In the wild, Avena sterilis thrives in the Mediterranean climate.
Many botanists believe that Avena sterilis is the progenitor of the oats we grow today.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Avena sativa - modern cultivated oat

Etymology

From Latin, 'Avena' meaning 'oat' and 'sterilis' meaning 'barren' or 'unproductive'.
