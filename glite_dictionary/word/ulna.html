ulna

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌl.nə / (AmE), / ˈʌl.nə / (BrE)
the inner and longer of the two bones in the human forearm, extending from the elbow to the wrist on the side opposite the thumb
inner forearm bone
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that my fractured ulna would heal with proper care.
X-rays showed a clean break in the middle of the ulna.
Pain along the length of the ulna may indicate a stress injury.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: radius - the outer and slightly shorter of the two bones in the forearm
  • Synonym: humerus - the bone of the upper arm, connecting the shoulder to the elbow

Etymology

From Latin 'ulna' meaning 'forearm' or 'elbow'.
