placentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌplæsənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌplæsənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the formation and development of the placenta in the uterus during pregnancy
formation of the placenta in the uterus
medical

Examples

The process of placentation is vital for the exchange of nutrients between mother and fetus.
Scientists studied abnormalities in placentation to understand certain pregnancy complications.
Mammals exhibit different patterns of placentation depending on their evolutionary adaptations.

Etymology

From Latin placenta (cake, placenta) + -ation (forming nouns denoting action or process).

2. NOUN

/ ˌplæsənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌplæsɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the arrangement of ovules within the placenta of a plant ovary, or of the placentas within the ovary
arrangement of ovules/placentas in a plant ovary
medical

Examples

The type of placentation can help botanists identify different species of flowering plants.
Marginal placentation is commonly found in pea and bean plants.
The study focused on how different forms of placentation influence seed development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placenta - the organ in the uterus of pregnant mammals or the tissue by which nutrients pass to the seed in plants
  • Synonym: ovule - a structure in seed plants that develops into a seed after fertilization

Etymology

From Latin 'placenta' (cake, flat cake, later membrane in the uterus) + English suffix '-ation'.
