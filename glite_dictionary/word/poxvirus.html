poxvirus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑksˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒksˌvaɪərəs / (BrE)
any of a group of viruses that can cause pox diseases, such as smallpox or cowpox, in vertebrates.
virus causing pox diseases
medical

Examples

The poxvirus family includes the organism responsible for smallpox.
Researchers discovered a new type of poxvirus in wild rodents.
A poxvirus infection can cause characteristic skin lesions in infected animals.

Etymology

From 'pox' (referring to pox diseases) + 'virus', from Latin 'virus' (poison, slimy liquid).
