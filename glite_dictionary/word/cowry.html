cowry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊri / (AmE), / ˈkaʊəri / (BrE)
a small tropical marine mollusc of the genus Cypraea, having a glossy, brightly marked shell, often used as currency or ornament
marine mollusc with a shiny, patterned shell

Examples

Ancient cultures sometimes used cowry shells as a form of money or ornamentation.
The diver found a beautiful cowry among the coral reefs on her trip to the Maldives.
This necklace is made from polished cowry shells collected on tropical beaches.

Etymology

From Hindi 'kaurī', ultimately from Sanskrit 'kapardikā' (a kind of shell).
