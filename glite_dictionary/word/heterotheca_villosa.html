Heterotheca villosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬əroʊˈθiːkə vɪˈloʊsə / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˈθiːkə vɪˈləʊsə / (BrE)
A hairy perennial plant with yellow flower heads in branched clusters, commonly found in dry areas of western and central North America; sometimes classified as Chrysopsis villosa.
a hairy yellow-flowered perennial plant of North America

Examples

Heterotheca villosa thrives in dry, rocky soils across western North America.
The botanical garden recently added Heterotheca villosa to its collection of native wildflowers.
Despite its tough appearance, Heterotheca villosa produces bright yellow flowers each summer.

Etymology

From Greek heteros (different) + theke (case) and Latin villosa (hairy).
