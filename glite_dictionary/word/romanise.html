to Romanise

1. VERB

/ ˈroʊ.məˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.məˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to write or represent a word or text using the Latin (Roman) alphabet, often by transliteration
write in the Latin alphabet

Examples

Many Korean signs have been Romanised to help tourists find their way.
The students learned how to Romanise Chinese characters using Pinyin.
After the reforms, most street names were Romanised to standardize spelling.

Synset Examples

many shops in Japan now carry neon signs with Romanized names

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to Romanize - to write in the Latin alphabet (American spelling)

Etymology

From Latin 'Romanus' (Roman), meaning 'of Rome.'
