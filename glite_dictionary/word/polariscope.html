polariscope

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈlɛr.əˌskoʊp / (AmE), / pəˈlar.ɪ.skəʊp / (BrE)
an optical instrument used for observing and measuring the effect of substances on polarized light, typically to examine the rotation of the plane of polarization
optical device for measuring polarized light

Examples

The scientist used a polariscope to study the crystal's optical properties.
When examined under a polariscope, the specimen revealed internal stress patterns.
A polariscope is commonly employed in quality control of transparent plastics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polarimeter - an instrument measuring the rotation of polarized light

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'polar' (from 'polarization') and '-scope' (instrument for viewing).
