Nephrolepis exaltata bostoniensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛf.rəˈliː.pɪs ɪɡˈzæl.teɪ.tə bɒsˌtoʊ.niˈɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌnɛf.rəˈliː.pɪs ɪɡˈzæl.teɪ.tə bɒsˌtəʊ.niˈɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
A variety of sword fern with arching or drooping pinnate fronds, commonly grown as a houseplant and known as the Boston fern.
a popular houseplant fern variety

Examples

The Nephrolepis exaltata bostoniensis thrives in humid environments and indirect sunlight.
Many people decorate their living rooms with a lush Nephrolepis exaltata bostoniensis for its elegant, arching fronds.
Routine misting and regular watering keep the Nephrolepis exaltata bostoniensis looking healthy and green.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Boston fern - Common name for Nephrolepis exaltata bostoniensis, a popular ornamental fern

Etymology

From Latin; Nephrolepis from Greek 'nephros' (kidney) and 'lepis' (scale), exaltata meaning 'lofty', bostoniensis referring to Boston.
