inharmonic

1. ADJ

/ ɪn.hɑrˈmɑ.nɪk / (AmE), / ɪn.hɑːˈmɒ.nɪk / (BrE)
Not harmonious; lacking in harmony, especially in sound or musical tone.
lacking in harmony; not harmonious

Examples

The instrument produced an inharmonic sound that was unsettling to the audience.
Some bells are deliberately designed to have inharmonic partials for a unique effect.
Her composition was criticized for its inharmonic chords and lack of melody.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harmonic - relating to harmony or pleasing sound; harmonious

Etymology

Formed by prefix in- (not) + harmonic (from Latin harmonicus).
