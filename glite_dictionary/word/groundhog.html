groundhog

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndˌhɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌhɒɡ / (BrE)
a reddish-brown North American rodent of the marmot family, known for burrowing and commonly called a woodchuck
North American marmot (rodent)

Examples

We saw a groundhog emerging from its burrow early in the morning.
Every February, people watch to see if the groundhog will see its shadow.
The groundhog is also commonly known as a woodchuck in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodchuck - alternative name for the groundhog
