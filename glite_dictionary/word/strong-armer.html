strong-armer

1. NOUN

/ strɔŋ ˈɑːrmər / (AmE), / strɒŋ ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
an aggressive and violent young criminal, especially one who uses force or threats to commit crimes
aggressive young criminal

Examples

The police arrested a known strong-armer who had been terrorizing the neighborhood for weeks.
Parents worried that their children might fall in with strong-armers at the local youth club.
Authorities are cracking down on strong-armers who target elderly citizens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thug - violent criminal or ruffian
  • Synonym: bully - person who habitually seeks to harm or intimidate those they perceive as vulnerable

Etymology

From 'strong-arm' (to use force or violence), plus agent suffix '-er'.
