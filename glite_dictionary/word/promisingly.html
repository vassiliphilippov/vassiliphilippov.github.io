promisingly

1. ADV

/ ˈprɑː.mɪ.sɪŋ.li / (AmE), / ˈprɒ.mɪ.sɪŋ.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows signs of future success or offers hope for a positive outcome.
in a way that gives hope for success

Examples

The team started the project promisingly, achieving their first milestone ahead of schedule.
Her new novel began promisingly, capturing the reader's attention from the very first page.
Although the negotiations opened promisingly, they ultimately broke down over minor disagreements.

Synset Examples

the afternoon had begun so promisingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hopefully - in a hopeful manner

Etymology

From 'promising' + '-ly', with 'promising' from late Middle English promise (from Latin promissum).
