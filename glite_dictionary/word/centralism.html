centralism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntrəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the political system or principle of concentrating power and authority in a central government or organization, often at the expense of local or regional bodies
concentration of political power in a central authority

Examples

The country's move toward centralism sparked protests from regional leaders who wanted greater autonomy.
Debates about centralism versus decentralization are common in discussions about government structure.
Many critics argue that centralism can lead to inefficiency and lack of local representation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: federalism - the system of government in which power is divided between a central authority and constituent political units

Etymology

From French 'centralisme', from 'central' + '-ism'.
