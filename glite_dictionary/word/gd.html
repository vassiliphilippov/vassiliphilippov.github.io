GD

1. NOUN

/ dʒi di / (AmE), / dʒiː diː / (BrE)
a highly toxic nerve agent (soman) that inhibits cholinesterase, used as a chemical weapon and easily absorbed by the body
toxic nerve agent; chemical weapon

Examples

The military stockpiled GD as part of its chemical weapons arsenal during the Cold War.
Exposure to GD can be fatal within minutes due to respiratory failure.
International treaties now prohibit the production and use of GD in warfare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soman - a toxic nerve agent used as a chemical weapon

Etymology

Initialism of German 'G-Agent, D' (third compound developed).
