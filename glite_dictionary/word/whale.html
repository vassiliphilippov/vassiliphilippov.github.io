whale

1. NOUN

/ weɪl / (AmE), / weɪl / (BrE)
A very large or impressive person, often remarkable in size or qualities.
very large or impressive person
informal

Examples

Everyone was amazed when the whale of a man squeezed into the tiny car.
In the world of professional wrestling, he's a real whale both in talent and in stature.
That company's CEO is a whale in the industry, dominating every market he enters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - very large and strong person
  • Antonym: dwarf - person of unusually small size

2. NOUN

/ weɪl / (AmE), / weɪl / (BrE)
a very large marine mammal with a streamlined body and a blowhole on the head for breathing, belonging to the order Cetacea
large marine mammal with a blowhole

Examples

A blue whale can weigh more than 150 tons and is the largest animal on Earth.
Scientists are tracking the migration patterns of whales across the Pacific Ocean.
We spotted a pod of whales breaching off the coast during our boat trip.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dolphin - a smaller cetacean mammal known for intelligence and sociability
  • Degree Lower: porpoise - a small, robust cetacean mammal related to dolphins and whales

Etymology

From Old English 'hwæl', related to Old Norse 'hvalr' and Latin 'cetus' (large sea animal).

3. VERB

/ weɪl / (AmE), / weɪl / (BrE)
to hunt whales for their oil, meat, or other products
hunt whales
archaic

Examples

In the 19th century, many ships whaled in the icy waters of the Arctic Ocean.
They no longer whale in these regions due to international bans.
Before conservation efforts, entire communities depended on whaling for their livelihood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whaling - the practice or industry of hunting whales
