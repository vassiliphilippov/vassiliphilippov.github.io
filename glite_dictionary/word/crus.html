crus

1. NOUN

/ kruːs / (AmE), / kruːs / (BrE)
the part of the leg between the knee and the foot, especially as referred to in anatomy or zoology
part of leg between knee and foot
medical

Examples

The fracture was located in the upper part of the crus, just below the knee.
In many animals, powerful muscles run along the crus to control movement of the foot.
During the dissection, the anatomy professor pointed out the various structures within the crus.

Etymology

From Latin 'crus' (leg, shank).
