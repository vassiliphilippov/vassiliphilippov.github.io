lordly

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔrd.li / (AmE), / ˈlɔːd.li / (BrE)
having or showing an attitude of arrogant superiority and disdain toward others considered unworthy
showing arrogant superiority

Examples

The manager gave a lordly wave and ignored our questions.
He spoke with a lordly tone that irritated everyone in the room.
Her lordly attitude made it hard for her to make friends.

Synset Examples

some economists are disdainful of their colleagues in other social disciplines
haughty aristocrats
his lordly manners were offensive
walked with a prideful swagger
very sniffy about breaches of etiquette
his mother eyed my clothes with a supercilious air
a more swaggering mood than usual

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haughty - arrogantly superior and disdainful
  • Synonym: supercilious - behaving as though one thinks one is superior to others
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From Old English 'hlāfordlic', from 'hlāford' meaning 'lord'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɔrdli / (AmE), / ˈlɔːdli / (BrE)
of, relating to, or befitting a lord; grand, dignified, or haughty in manner or appearance.
befitting a lord; grand or dignified

Examples

The heir enjoyed a lordly inheritance that set him apart from others.
She swept through the hall with a lordly bearing, commanding attention.
From their lordly manor, the family oversaw the entire countryside.

Synset Examples

heir to a lordly fortune
of august lineage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: majestic - having or showing impressive beauty or dignity
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From Old English *hlāfordlic*, based on *hlāford* (lord) + *-ly* (having the qualities of).
