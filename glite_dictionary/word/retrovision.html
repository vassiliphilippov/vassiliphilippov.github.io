retrovision

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtroʊˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəʊˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
the supposed phenomenon of seeing or visualizing events that occurred in the distant past
seeing events in the distant past

Examples

Some science fiction novels feature characters who possess the power of retrovision.
The concept of retrovision fascinates those interested in time travel and historical mysteries.
Through retrovision, the psychic claimed to witness scenes from ancient civilizations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: precognition - knowledge or awareness of the future, especially by extrasensory perception

Etymology

From Latin 'retro' (back, backward) + 'vision' (seeing).
