withy

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪði / (AmE), / ˈwɪði / (BrE)
a strong, flexible twig, especially of willow, often used in basketry or tying
strong, flexible twig (esp. willow)

Examples

The basket was woven from fresh withy cut from the willow trees.
Farmers sometimes use a withy to tie together bundles of produce.
She learned to make fences by plaiting withy branches into a sturdy hedge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub with slender branches, often used for basketry

Etymology

From Old English wīthig (willow tree), related to German Weide (willow).
