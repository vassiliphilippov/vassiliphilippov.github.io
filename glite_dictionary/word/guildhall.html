guildhall

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪldˌhɔl / (AmE), / ˈɡɪldˌhɔːl / (BrE)
A building used as the meeting place or headquarters of a guild or corporation, especially in historical contexts.
hall used by a guild or corporation

Examples

The ancient guildhall in the city center is now a museum.
The council met at the guildhall to discuss the town's future.
Many medieval guildhalls featured elaborate woodwork and stained glass.

Etymology

From 'guild' (an association of craftsmen or merchants) + 'hall' (large room or building).
