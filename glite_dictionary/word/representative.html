representative

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛprəˈzɛntətɪv / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪˈzɛntətɪv / (BrE)
a person who acts or speaks on behalf of others, especially in an official capacity
person acting on behalf of others

Examples

Each state elects a representative to serve in Congress.
She worked as the company's representative at the conference.
The union representative discussed the workers' concerns with management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delegate - a person chosen to represent others
  • Synonym: agent - a person who acts on behalf of another
  • Antonym: principal - the person being represented

Etymology

From Latin repraesentativus, from repraesentare (to show, exhibit, represent).

2. ADJ

/ ˌrɛprɪˈzɛntətɪv / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪˈzɛntətɪv / (BrE)

of a government by elected officials

Examples

The country adopted a representative system where citizens elect their leaders.
A representative democracy allows people to have a voice through their chosen officials.
The constitution guarantees a representative form of government for all states.
