blacktopping

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌtɑpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌtɒpɪŋ / (BrE)
A black bituminous material used for paving roads or other surfaces, typically spread over a base of crushed stone.
black bituminous paving material

Examples

The crew spread blacktopping over the old gravel road to improve the surface.
After the winter thaw, the driveway needed a fresh layer of blacktopping.
City workers completed blacktopping the parking lot by noon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asphalt - a mixture of dark bituminous pitch used for paving
