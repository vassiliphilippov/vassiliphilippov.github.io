adnexa

1. NOUN

/ ædˈnɛksə / (AmE), / ædˈnɛksə / (BrE)
the accessory or adjoining anatomical parts or appendages attached to an organ, especially in medical contexts
accessory anatomical parts of an organ
medical

Examples

During the examination, the doctor checked the uterine adnexa for any abnormalities.
Inflammation of the adnexa can cause pelvic pain in women.
The surgeon noted that both ovaries, as parts of the adnexa, appeared healthy.

Synset Examples

Fallopian tubes and ovaries are adnexa of the uterus

Etymology

From Latin adnexus, past participle of 'adnectere' meaning 'to tie to'.
