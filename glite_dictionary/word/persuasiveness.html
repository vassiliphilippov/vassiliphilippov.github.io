persuasiveness

1. NOUN

/ pərˈsweɪsɪvnəs / (AmE), / pəˈsweɪsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being able to convince someone to do or believe something through reasoning or appeal
the ability to persuade effectively

Examples

Her persuasiveness won over even the most skeptical members of the panel.
He relied on his persuasiveness rather than force to achieve his goals.
The sales team's persuasiveness was key to the company's record profits this quarter.

Synset Examples

the strength of his argument settled the matter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convincingness - the quality of being convincing
  • Synonym: influence - the capacity to have an effect on someone's character or behavior
  • Antonym: unpersuasiveness - the quality of failing to persuade

Etymology

From Latin 'persuadere' (to persuade) via Old French and Middle English.
