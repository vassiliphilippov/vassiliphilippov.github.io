bower

1. VERB

/ ˈbaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈbaʊ.ə / (BrE)
to enclose or surround with or as if with a bower (an attractive leafy shelter or retreat)
enclose in a leafy shelter

Examples

A canopy of vines bowered the garden path in deep shade.
The old cottage was bowered in wisteria and roses.
Children bowered themselves under blankets, pretending to be in a secret hideaway.

Etymology

From Old English *būr* (dwelling, chamber).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01392864-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈbaʊ.ə / (BrE)
a pleasant, shaded shelter, often made by trees or leafy plants, or a framework that supports climbing plants
shaded shelter or plant framework

Examples

The roses grew over the bower, creating a cool retreat from the afternoon sun.
We enjoyed tea beneath the leafy bower at the garden's edge.
A wooden bower supports the wisteria vines in the courtyard.

Synset Examples

the arbor provided a shady resting place in the park

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arbor - a shelter of vines or branches or of latticework covered with climbing shrubs or vines

Etymology

From Old English *bur* (dwelling, chamber).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
