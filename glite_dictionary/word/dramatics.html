dramatics

1. NOUN

/ drəˈmætɪks / (AmE), / drəˈmætɪks / (BrE)
the activity of participating in the performance or production of plays, especially as a school or extracurricular activity
participation in theatrical productions

Examples

She joined dramatics in high school and performed in several plays.
Many colleges offer dramatics as an extracurricular activity for students interested in theatre.
His passion for dramatics led him to organize the school's annual play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theatre - the art or activity of performing plays on stage
  • Synonym: acting - performing a role in a play or film

Etymology

From "drama" + "-ics", patterned after other academic or artistic disciplines.

2. NOUN

/ drəˈmætɪks / (AmE), / drəˈmætɪks / (BrE)
the art or practice of writing, producing, and performing plays; the study of drama as a discipline
the art of writing and producing plays

Examples

She majored in dramatics at university before joining a professional theater company.
The school offers after-school dramatics clubs for students interested in acting and stage production.
His passion for dramatics led him to write several acclaimed plays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theatre - the activity or profession of acting, producing, directing, or writing plays
  • Synonym: playwriting - the art or craft of writing plays

Etymology

From 'drama' (Greek drāma, meaning 'action, play') plus the suffix '-ics'.

Synset POS: n
