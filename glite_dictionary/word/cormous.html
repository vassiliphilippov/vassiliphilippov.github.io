cormous

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔːr.məs / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.məs / (BrE)
(of a plant) having or producing corms.
having or producing corms (botany)

Examples

Many spring-flowering plants, such as crocuses, are cormous perennials.
The garden bed features several cormous species that store nutrients underground.
Experts recommend planting cormous flowers in the fall for an early bloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corm - a swollen underground plant stem serving as a storage organ

Etymology

From 'corm', the botanical term for a swollen underground plant stem, plus the suffix '-ous' meaning 'having' or 'full of'.
