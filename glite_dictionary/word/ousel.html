1. NOUN

/ ˈuːzəl / (AmE), / ˈuːzəl / (BrE)
A black European thrush, especially the common blackbird or ring ouzel.
black European thrush

Examples

The ring ousel is known for its distinctive white breast band.
As dawn broke, an ousel sang from a nearby hedge.
Ousels are often seen foraging on the forest floor for insects and worms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackbird - a common black European thrush

Etymology

From Old English ōsle, related to German Amsel (blackbird).
