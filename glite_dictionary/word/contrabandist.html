contrabandist

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.trəˈbænd.ɪst / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.trəˈbænd.ɪst / (BrE)
A person who imports or exports goods illegally, especially without paying required duties.
person who smuggles goods

Examples

The contrabandist managed to avoid customs officers at the border.
Authorities arrested a notorious contrabandist who had been smuggling electronics.
Local fisherman suspected that a contrabandist was using the harbor for illicit trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smuggler - a person who imports or exports goods illegally

Etymology

From 'contraband' (from Italian 'contrabbando'), with the suffix '-ist' indicating a person engaged in an activity.
