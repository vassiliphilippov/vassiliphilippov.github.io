furfuraldehyde

1. NOUN

/ fɝːˌfjʊrˈældəˌhaɪd / (AmE), / fjɜːˌfjʊərˈældɪˌhaɪd / (BrE)
A colorless or yellowish liquid aldehyde with a strong odor, derived from plant materials such as corncobs or oat hulls, and used as a solvent and in making furan compounds.
liquid aldehyde used as a solvent and chemical intermediate
medical

Examples

Furfuraldehyde is often produced during the processing of agricultural waste such as oat husks.
The distinct odor of furfuraldehyde can be detected even at low concentrations in the laboratory.
Industrial chemists use furfuraldehyde as a precursor in the manufacture of resins and solvents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furfural - a colorless liquid aldehyde derived from agricultural byproducts, used in chemical synthesis

Etymology

From furfural (itself from Latin 'furfur' meaning bran) and aldehyde.
