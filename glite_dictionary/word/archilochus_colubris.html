Archilochus colubris

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkɪˈlɒkəs kəˈluːbrɪs / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈlɒkəs kəˈluːbrɪs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the ruby-throated hummingbird, a small North American bird known for its iridescent throat and rapid wing movement
scientific name of ruby-throated hummingbird

Examples

The Archilochus colubris is often seen hovering near bright flowers in summer gardens.
Many bird enthusiasts enjoy identifying the Archilochus colubris during its migration across eastern North America.
The iridescent throat of the male Archilochus colubris makes it easy to distinguish from other hummingbirds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruby-throated hummingbird - a small hummingbird with a distinctive red throat found in eastern North America

Etymology

From Greek Archilochus (genus name) and Latin colubris (species epithet).
