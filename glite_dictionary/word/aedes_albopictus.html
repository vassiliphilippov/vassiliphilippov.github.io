Aedes albopictus

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈiːdiːz ˌælboʊˈpɪktəs / (AmE), / aɪˈiːdiːz ˌalbəʊˈpɪktəs / (BrE)
a species of mosquito native to Asia, now widespread in other regions, known for its striped appearance and ability to transmit diseases such as dengue and Zika
species of mosquito known as Asian tiger mosquito

Examples

The invasion of Aedes albopictus in new territories has become a public health concern.
Researchers found Aedes albopictus larvae in stagnant water after the heavy rains.
Public awareness campaigns are crucial to control the spread of Aedes albopictus and prevent disease outbreaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mosquito - a small flying insect that feeds on blood

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Aedes (from Greek aēdēs, 'unpleasant') and species epithet albopictus (from Latin albus, 'white', and pictus, 'painted').
