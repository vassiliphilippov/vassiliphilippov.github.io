reprover

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpruvɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈpruːvə / (BrE)
a person who expresses disapproval or criticism, often by pointing out faults or blaming others
person who expresses disapproval or criticism

Examples

The reprover pointed out every mistake I made during the meeting.
She acted as a gentle reprover, offering constructive criticism rather than harsh words.
No one likes to be called a reprover, but sometimes honesty is necessary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critic - person who judges or evaluates
  • Antonym: approver - person who approves or gives approval

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French ‘reprover’, from Latin ‘reprobare’ (to disapprove).
