roughly

1. ADV

/ ˈrʌf.li / (AmE), / ˈrʌf.li / (BrE)
In a rough or violent manner; without gentleness or care.
in a rough or violent way

Examples

He was pushed roughly aside by the impatient crowd.
The guards handled the prisoners roughly, showing little concern for their welfare.
She woke up suddenly after being shaken roughly by her brother.

Synset Examples

he was pushed roughly aside
they treated him rough

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gently - in a mild, kind, or tender manner

2. ADV

/ ˈrʌf.li / (AmE), / ˈrʌf.li / (BrE)
approximately; not exactly but close to a particular number, amount, or degree.
approximately, not exactly

Examples

There were roughly twenty people at the meeting last night.
The journey took roughly three hours by train.
Tickets for the concert cost roughly fifty dollars each.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approximately - close to but not exactly a particular number or amount
  • Antonym: exactly - in a precise and accurate way, with no inaccuracy or approximation

Etymology

From rough + -ly, meaning 'in a rough manner'.
