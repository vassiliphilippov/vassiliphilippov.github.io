chanter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæntər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːntə / (BrE)
A reed pipe with finger holes on which the melody is played, especially in bagpipes.
melody pipe of a bagpipe

Examples

The bagpiper skillfully played the melody on the chanter while maintaining a steady drone.
Traditional Scottish tunes are often performed on a wooden chanter before using full bagpipes.
He repaired the broken reed in his chanter to improve the instrument's sound quality.

Etymology

From Old French *chantere*, from chanter (to sing), from Latin *cantare* (to sing).
