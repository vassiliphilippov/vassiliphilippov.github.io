muddiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌd.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌd.i.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being cloudy, turbid, or not clear, particularly in liquids or sound.
cloudy or unclear quality

Examples

The muddiness of the river increased after the storm washed soil into the water.
There was a muddiness in the sound, making the speaker difficult to understand.
The muddiness of his explanation left everyone more confused than before.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear
  • Synonym: turbidity - the quality of being cloudy or opaque in a liquid

Etymology

From 'muddy' + '-ness'; 'muddy' from Middle English 'mudy', related to 'mud'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌd.i.nɪs / (BrE)
a mental state in which thoughts are unclear and disorganized; lack of mental clarity or order
lack of mental clarity or order

Examples

The muddiness of his thoughts prevented him from making a firm decision.
After a sleepless night, she faced the meeting with mental muddiness.
The report was criticized for the muddiness of its logic and arguments.

Synset Examples

a confusion of impressions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - a state of being unclear or disorganized in mind or understanding
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear, in particular mental sharpness and lucidity

Etymology

From 'muddy' + '-ness', where 'muddy' comes from Middle English 'mudi' (covered with mud).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmʌdinəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌdinɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being covered in or containing mud, especially with wet or dirty conditions
state of being muddy or dirty with wetness

Examples

The heavy rain increased the muddiness of the hiking trail, making it difficult to walk.
After the flood, the muddiness of the river made it impossible to see the bottom.
Players complained about the muddiness of the football field after last night's storm.

Synset Examples

the baseball game was canceled because of the wateriness of the outfield
the water's muddiness made it undrinkable
the sloppiness of a rainy November day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wetness - the fact or condition of being wet
  • Antonym: dryness - the condition of being dry, absence of moisture

Etymology

From 'muddy' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the state or quality of being muddy.
