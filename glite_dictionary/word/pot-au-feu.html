pot-au-feu

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊ.t̬oʊˈfə / (AmE), / ˌpɒtəˈfə / (BrE)
A traditional French stew made from beef and vegetables, simmered slowly in water or stock.
French stew of beef and vegetables

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious pot-au-feu for the winter menu.
A classic pot-au-feu features beef shank, marrow bones, and root vegetables.
She followed her grandmother's recipe for pot-au-feu to serve at the family dinner.

Etymology

From French 'pot-au-feu' meaning 'pot on the fire'.
