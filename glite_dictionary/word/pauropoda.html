Pauropoda

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɔrəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / ˌpɔːrəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
A class of small, soil-dwelling arthropods characterized by branched antennae and having eight to ten pairs of legs.
class of minute soil arthropods

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species of Pauropoda during their soil examination.
Although Pauropoda are rarely seen, they play an important role in soil ecosystems.
The presence of branched antennae helps to distinguish Pauropoda from centipedes.

Etymology

From Greek 'pauros' (small) + 'pous, podos' (foot).

2. NOUN

/ pɔːˈrɑːpədə / (AmE), / pɔːˈrɒpədə / (BrE)
a class of minute, soft-bodied arthropods with branched antennae and 8 to 10 pairs of legs, found in soil and leaf litter
class of small soil-dwelling arthropods

Examples

Pauropoda are often overlooked due to their tiny size and hidden lifestyle.
Scientists discovered a new species within the class Pauropoda in the forest soil.
The study of Pauropoda helps researchers understand soil biodiversity.

Etymology

From New Latin Pauropoda, from Greek 'pauros' (small) + 'pous, podos' (foot).
