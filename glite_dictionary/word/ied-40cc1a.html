I.E.D.

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.iˈdi / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.iːˈdiː / (BrE)
an explosive device made and deployed in an improvised, non-standard way, typically used by insurgents or terrorists.
improvised explosive device

Examples

The convoy halted when an I.E.D. detonated near the roadside.
Security forces discovered several I.E.D.s hidden under debris.
Many injuries in the conflict were caused by I.E.D. attacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bomb - a shell, mine, or other explosive weapon

Etymology

Acronym of 'improvised explosive device'.
