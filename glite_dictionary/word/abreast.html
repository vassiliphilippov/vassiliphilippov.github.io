abreast

1. ADJ

/ əˈbrɛst / (AmE), / əˈbrɛst / (BrE)
having current knowledge of recent developments; up to date.
up to date; informed

Examples

I try to keep abreast of all the latest technological advances in my field.
The newsletter helps readers stay abreast of important local events.
It's important for executives to be abreast of changes in the market.

Synset Examples

kept abreast of the latest developments
constant revision keeps the book au courant
always au fait on the latest events
up on the news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informed - having or showing knowledge; aware of facts
  • Antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness in general or about something

Etymology

From Middle English 'a brest', meaning 'at the breast', indicating being side by side.

2. ADV

/ əˈbrɛst / (AmE), / əˈbrɛst / (BrE)
side by side and facing in the same direction
side by side; facing the same way

Examples

The runners remained abreast as they approached the finish line.
The two ships sailed abreast down the river.
They walked abreast along the narrow path, talking quietly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: side by side - positioned next to each other

Etymology

From Old French 'a bras' (at side), from 'a' (at) + 'bras' (arm).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00251953-r
Synset POS: r
