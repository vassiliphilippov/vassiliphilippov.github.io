prehistoric

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriː.hɪˈstɔːr.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpriː.hɪˈstɒr.ɪk / (BrE)
(informal) Extremely old-fashioned; outdated or no longer considered modern.
very old-fashioned; outdated
informal

Examples

Her father still uses a prehistoric typewriter that barely works.
Some of the company's policies are downright prehistoric and need to be updated.
He called their fashion sense prehistoric, joking that it belonged in another century.

Synset Examples

my mother has these prehistoric ideas about proper clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: old-fashioned - not modern; belonging to an earlier time
  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times

Etymology

From pre- + historic; originally referring to periods before recorded history, now also used figuratively for things considered outdated.

2. ADJ

/ ˌpriː.hɪˈstɔːr.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpriː.hɪˈstɒr.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or belonging to the period before written records or history began
before written history

Examples

The fossils date back to a prehistoric era, long before humans appeared.
Researchers uncovered evidence of a prehistoric settlement beneath the field.
Some prehistoric animals, like the mammoth, have been extinct for thousands of years.

Synset Examples

prehistoric archeology

Related Terms

  • Antonym: historic - famous or important in history, or having happened in the past
  • Synonym: ancient - belonging to the very distant past

Etymology

From French préhistorique, from pré- (before) + historique (historic).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02957696-a
Synset POS: a
