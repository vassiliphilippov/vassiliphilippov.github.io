Dermochelyidae

1. NOUN

/ dɜr.moʊˈki.li.ɪ.di / (AmE), / dɜː.məʊˈkiː.lɪ.aɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of large marine turtles known as leatherback sea turtles, distinguished by their leathery shells and wide distribution in warm oceans.
family of large marine turtles (leatherbacks)

Examples

The Dermochelyidae family includes the remarkable leatherback turtle.
Scientists are studying migration patterns within the Dermochelyidae to better protect these endangered turtles.
Unlike other sea turtles, members of the Dermochelyidae have leathery rather than hard shells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leatherback turtle - the largest living turtle, only extant species of Dermochelyidae

Etymology

From Greek 'derma' (skin) + 'chelys' (turtle), referring to their leathery shells.
