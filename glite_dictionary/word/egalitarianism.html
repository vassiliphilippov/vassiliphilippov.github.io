egalitarianism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌɡælɪˈtɛriəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˌɡalɪˈtɛːrɪənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The belief in or advocacy of the principle that all people are equal and deserve equal rights and opportunities, especially in political, economic, or social life.
belief in social and political equality

Examples

Her political views are strongly influenced by egalitarianism and the idea that everyone should have the same opportunities.
Many social movements throughout history have promoted egalitarianism as a fundamental principle.
Some critics argue that perfect egalitarianism is difficult to achieve in complex societies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equality - the state of being equal, especially in status or opportunities
  • Antonym: inequality - the state of not being equal, especially in status, rights, or opportunities

Etymology

From French 'égalitaire' (equal), from Latin 'aequalitas' (equality) + '-ism'.
