easily

1. ADV

/ ˈiːzəli / (AmE), / ˈiːzəli / (BrE)
very likely; in all probability; used to emphasize that something could happen or be true without difficulty or doubt
very probably; in all likelihood

Examples

The match could easily be postponed if the weather gets worse.
She may easily have forgotten about our meeting—let's call to check.
This explanation could easily account for all the strange occurrences.

Synset Examples

I might well do it
a mistake that could easily have ended in disaster
you may well need your umbrella
he could equally well be trying to deceive us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probably - likely to happen or be the case
  • Degree Lower: possibly - might be the case or happen, but less certain
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈiːzəli / (AmE), / ˈiːzɪli / (BrE)
without difficulty or effort; with ease.
without difficulty or effort

Examples

She learned to play the piano easily compared to her classmates.
This book can be easily understood, even by beginners.
The vase was so fragile that it broke easily when it fell.

Synset Examples

she was easily excited
was easily confused
he won easily
this china breaks very easily
success came too easy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effortlessly - without the need for effort; synonym of 'easily'
  • Antonym: hardly - with difficulty; antonym of 'easily'

Etymology

From Middle English *esily*, from Old French *aisément*, from *aise* (ease).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00148912-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈiːzəli / (AmE), / ˈiːzəli / (BrE)
without any doubt; by a clear margin or obviously
without any doubt; clearly

Examples

She is easily the best player on the team.
This film is easily my favorite of the year.
He easily outperformed all his competitors in the race.

Synset Examples

easily the best book she's written

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainly - without any doubt; surely
  • Synonym: undoubtedly - without doubt; certainly
  • Antonym: possibly - perhaps; with a chance, but not certain

Etymology

From Old French aisié, past participle of aisier 'to ease', from Latin adjacēre 'to be near'.
