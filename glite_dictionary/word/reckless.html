reckless

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛkləs / (AmE), / ˈrɛkləs / (BrE)
Having or showing a lack of care about danger or the possible results of one's actions; careless and unconcerned about consequences.
careless; unconcerned about risks or consequences

Examples

He made a reckless decision to invest all his money without proper research.
The driver was fined for his reckless disregard for the speed limits.
Her reckless behavior worried everyone who cared about her safety.

Synset Examples

the heedless generosity and the spasmodic extravagance of persons used to large fortunes
reckless squandering of public funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careless - not giving sufficient attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors
  • Antonym: cautious - showing care and attention to avoid danger or trouble

Etymology

From Middle English 'rekeles', from Old English 'rēcelēas' (careless), from 'rēċan' (to care) + '-lēs' (without).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛkləs / (AmE), / ˈrɛkləs / (BrE)
Showing a careless disregard for danger or the consequences of one's actions.
careless disregard for danger or consequences

Examples

The teenager's reckless driving caused several near accidents on the highway.
She made a reckless decision without considering the possible consequences.
His reckless disregard for the rules eventually led to his suspension.

Synset Examples

foolhardy enough to try to seize the gun from the hijacker
became the fiercest and most reckless of partisans
a reckless driver
a rash attempt to climb Mount Everest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careless - lacking attention or concern; not taking care
  • Antonym: cautious - showing care and attention; avoiding risk
  • Synonym: rash - acting without forethought or regard to danger

Etymology

From Old English 'rece-less' meaning 'careless', from 'reccan' (to care) + '-less'.
