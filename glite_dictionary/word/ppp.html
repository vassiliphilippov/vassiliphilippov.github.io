PPP

1. NOUN

/ pi pi pi / (AmE), / piː piː piː / (BrE)
A surgical procedure (palatopharyngoplasty) involving the removal of unnecessary palatal and oropharyngeal tissue to widen the airway, mainly to treat severe snoring or sleep apnea.
surgical procedure for severe snoring or sleep apnea
medical

Examples

After years of struggling with sleep apnea, Mark decided to undergo PPP to improve his breathing.
The surgeon explained that PPP might help reduce the patient's severe snoring.
Recovery from PPP typically involves mild throat discomfort and dietary restrictions.

Etymology

Abbreviation of palatopharyngoplasty, formed from Greek roots 'palato-' (palate), 'pharyngo-' (pharynx), and 'plasty' (molding or shaping).
