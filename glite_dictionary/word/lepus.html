Lepus

1. PROPN

/ ˈliːpəs / (AmE), / ˈliːpəs / (BrE)
A constellation in the southern sky, located just south of Orion and near Columba.
constellation near Orion

Examples

Many amateur astronomers enjoy observing Lepus during the winter months.
The constellation Lepus is often depicted as a hare fleeing from Orion.
With clear southern skies, Lepus can be seen alongside several other notable constellations.

Etymology

From Latin 'lepus' meaning 'hare'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈliːpəs / (AmE), / ˈliːpəs / (BrE)
The genus of mammals comprising the hares, characterized by long ears and powerful hind legs.
genus of hares

Examples

The European brown hare belongs to the genus Lepus.
Researchers studied various species within Lepus to compare their behavior.
Lepus species are found throughout Europe, Asia, and North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hare - a swift mammal of the genus Lepus, resembling a large rabbit

Etymology

From Latin *lepus* (hare), used in scientific classification.
