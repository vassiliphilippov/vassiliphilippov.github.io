crossopterygian

1. NOUN

/ krɑːˌsɑptəˈrɪdʒiən / (AmE), / krɒsˌɒptəˈrɪdʒiən / (BrE)
any fish belonging to the order Crossopterygii, an extinct group of lobe-finned fishes known chiefly from fossils.
extinct lobe-finned fish (Crossopterygii)

Examples

The discovery of a crossopterygian fossil shed new light on the evolution of land vertebrates.
Scientists often study crossopterygians to understand the transition from aquatic to terrestrial life.
The crossopterygian lineage includes some of the earliest creatures with limb-like fins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coelacanth - living lobe-finned fish, once thought extinct

Etymology

From Greek 'krossos' (fringe) + 'pterygion' (fin, diminutive of 'pteryx', wing).
