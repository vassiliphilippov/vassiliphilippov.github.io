Flammulina velutipes

1. NOUN

/ flæməˈluːnə vəˌluːtəˈpiːz / (AmE), / flamjuːˈliːnə vɛljuˈtʌɪpiːz / (BrE)
An edible mushroom with a viscid orange to brown cap, velvety stalk that darkens with age, and pallid gills, commonly found in clusters in early spring or late fall; also known as enoki or enokitake.
a species of edible mushroom

Examples

The chef added fresh Flammulina velutipes to the soup for a delicate texture and flavor.
In the wild, Flammulina velutipes can often be found growing in clusters on dead wood during chilly months.
Diners recognized the thin, white stems of Flammulina velutipes from popular Asian dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enoki - common name for Flammulina velutipes, used in cuisine

Etymology

From Latin: flammeus (flame-colored) + velutipes (velvet-footed).
