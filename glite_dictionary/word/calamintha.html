Calamintha

1. PROPN

/ ˌkæləˈmɪnθə / (AmE), / ˌkaləˈmɪnθə / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic herbaceous plants in the mint family (Lamiaceae), commonly known as calamints, native to Europe, Asia, and North America.
a genus of aromatic plants (calamints)

Examples

Calamintha is often grown in herb gardens for its fragrant leaves and tiny flowers.
Botanists recently discovered a new Calamintha species in southern Europe.
The essential oil of Calamintha has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calamint - any herbaceous plant of the genus Calamintha

Etymology

From Latin 'calamintha', from Ancient Greek 'kalamínthē' (a kind of mint).
