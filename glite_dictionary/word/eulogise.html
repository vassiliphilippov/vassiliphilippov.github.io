to eulogise

1. VERB

/ ˈjuː.ləˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuː.ləɡ.aɪz / (BrE)
to praise someone or something formally and eloquently, especially in a speech at a funeral
praise someone formally, often at a funeral
british

Examples

Many colleagues stood up to eulogise her contributions to the department.
He was eulogised at the memorial service for his decades of community service.
The biography eulogises the artist's impact on modern sculpture.

Synset Examples

The dead woman was eulogized at the funeral

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eulogize - to praise formally and eloquently (American spelling)
  • Antonym: to criticize - to express disapproval or point out faults

Etymology

From Greek eulogein (to praise), via Late Latin eulogiare and French élogier.
