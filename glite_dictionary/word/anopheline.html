anopheline

1. ADJ

/ əˈnɑːfəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / əˈnɒfəˌlaɪn / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of mosquitoes of the genus Anopheles, which transmit malaria.
relating to malaria-transmitting mosquitoes
medical

Examples

The researchers studied the anopheline population in areas with high malaria transmission rates.
Only female anopheline mosquitoes are capable of transmitting malaria to humans.
Insecticide-treated nets are an effective strategy against anopheline vectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anopheles - a genus of mosquitoes, some of which transmit malaria

Etymology

From New Latin Anopheles (the genus name) + -ine (adjective suffix).

2. NOUN

/ əˈnɑː.fəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / əˈnɒ.fəˌlaɪn / (BrE)
any mosquito of the genus Anopheles, a group of mosquitoes that can transmit malaria.
mosquito of the genus Anopheles
medical

Examples

The spread of malaria is closely linked to the presence of anopheline mosquitoes in the region.
Scientists collected several anophelines for study during their fieldwork in Africa.
Control measures focus on reducing anopheline breeding sites near human settlements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anopheles - a genus of mosquitoes capable of transmitting malaria

Etymology

From New Latin Anopheles (genus name) plus English suffix -ine.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02204149-n
Synset POS: n
