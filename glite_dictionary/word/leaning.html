leaning

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈliːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or state of being inclined or deviating from a vertical or upright position
deviation from vertical position

Examples

The sudden leaning of the tower alarmed the engineers.
He corrected the leaning of the bookshelf to prevent it from toppling over.
Structural problems were revealed by the gradual leaning of the building's front wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tilt - an inclination from the horizontal or vertical
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00071183-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈliːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈliːnɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a structure or object) departing or slanting away from the true vertical or horizontal position; not upright or level.
not upright or level; slanting

Examples

The old barn had become noticeably leaning over the years due to strong winds.
We visited the famous leaning tower of Pisa on our trip to Italy.
The picture frame was leaning to one side and needed to be straightened.

Synset Examples

the leaning tower of Pisa
the headstones were tilted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tilted - slanted or inclined from a straight position
  • Antonym: upright - in a vertical position, not slanted

Etymology

Present participle of 'lean', from Old English *hleonian* (to lean).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈliːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈliːnɪŋ / (BrE)
a tendency or preference for a particular idea or activity
slight preference or tendency

Examples

She has a strong leaning toward classical music and often attends concerts.
During the debate, he revealed his political leanings to the audience.
My father's leanings are more traditional, especially regarding family values.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inclination - a tendency toward a particular characteristic or type of behavior
  • Antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination

Etymology

From verb 'lean' + -ing; 'lean' from Old English 'hlinian' (to incline, bend).
