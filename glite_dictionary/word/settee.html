settee

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈti / (AmE), / sɛˈtiː / (BrE)
a long wooden bench with a back, used for seating several people
long bench with a back

Examples

We placed the antique settee along the hallway to provide extra seating.
The old wooden settee creaked whenever someone sat down.
They refurbished the garden settee to match the new patio furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sofa - a comfortable upholstered seat for more than one person

Etymology

From French 'ceté', variant of 'assise' (seat).

2. NOUN

/ sɛˈti / (AmE), / sɛˈtiː / (BrE)
A long upholstered seat with a back and arms, typically accommodating two or more people; a small sofa.
a small upholstered sofa

Examples

She bought a beautiful vintage settee for her living room.
The old settee in the hallway is surprisingly comfortable.
They sat together on the settee, drinking tea and talking quietly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sofa - a long upholstered seat with a back and arms, for several people
  • Synonym: couch - an upholstered seat long enough to accommodate more than one sitter

Etymology

From Old French *sete* (seat), from Latin *sella* (seat, chair).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
