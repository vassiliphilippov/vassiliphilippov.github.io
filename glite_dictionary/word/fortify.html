to fortify

1. VERB

/ ˈfɔːr.t̬ə.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to add alcohol to (a drink or beverage) in order to increase its alcoholic strength
add alcohol to a drink

Examples

Some wines are fortified with brandy to enhance their alcohol content.
They decided to fortify the eggnog with a splash of rum for the party.
She noticed that her dessert wine had been fortified during the production process.

Synset Examples

the punch is spiked!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spike - to add alcohol or a stimulant to a drink (usually secretly)

2. VERB

/ ˈfɔrtɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːtɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to prepare oneself or military forces for a confrontation or conflict, especially by strengthening defenses or readiness
prepare for military confrontation

Examples

The country's armed forces began to fortify their position along the border.
As tensions rose, the general ordered the troops to fortify before dawn.
She decided to fortify herself mentally for the challenges of the upcoming battle.

Synset Examples

The U.S. is girding for a conflict in the Middle East
troops are building up on the Iraqi border

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strengthen - to make stronger

Etymology

From Latin fortificare, from fortis (strong) + facere (to make).

3. VERB

/ ˈfɔr.tə.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to add nutrients or vitamins to (food or drink) to increase its nutritional value
add nutrients or vitamins to food or drink

Examples

Many breakfast cereals are fortified with vitamins and minerals to improve their health benefits.
Some governments require producers to fortify flour with folic acid and iron.
Manufacturers often fortify orange juice with extra calcium for added nutritional value.
