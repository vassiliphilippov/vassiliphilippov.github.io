north-northwest

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɔrθˌnɔrθˈwɛst / (AmE), / ˌnɔːθˌnɔːθˈwɛst / (BrE)
the direction or compass point lying halfway between north and northwest, at an angle of 337.5° clockwise from due north
compass point between north and northwest

Examples

The ship was sailing north-northwest to avoid the storm.
Winds are expected from the north-northwest at ten miles per hour.
If you head north-northwest from here, you'll reach the coast by sunset.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: northwest - the compass point between north and west
  • Degree Lower: north - the compass point at 0 degrees; the direction towards the North Pole

Etymology

Formed from 'north' and 'northwest', indicating the intermediate compass direction.

2. ADV

/ ˌnɔrθ nɔrθˈwɛst / (AmE), / ˌnɔːθ nɔːθˈwɛst / (BrE)
to, toward, or in the direction between north and northwest (approximately 337.5° on a compass rose)
toward the direction between north and northwest

Examples

The ship was headed north-northwest to avoid the storm.
The hikers decided to travel north-northwest from the base camp to reach the summit.
He glanced at his compass and began walking north-northwest through the dense forest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: north - the direction corresponding to 0°/360° on a compass; one of the four cardinal points
  • Degree Lower: northwest - the direction between north and west; approximately 315° on a compass
  • Degree Higher: north by west - the compass point between north and north-northwest (approximately 348.75°)

Etymology

Formed by combining 'north' and 'northwest' to denote an intermediate compass direction.
