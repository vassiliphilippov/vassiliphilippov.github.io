manufacturing

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæn.jəˈfæk.tʃɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌmæn.jʊˈfæk.tʃər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making goods or products from raw materials, especially in factories
making products from raw materials

Examples

Manufacturing plays a crucial role in the nation's economic growth and employment rates.
Advances in technology have greatly increased the efficiency of manufacturing processes.
Many towns in the region relied on textile manufacturing for their livelihoods.

Synset Examples

the synthesis and fabrication of single crystals
an improvement in the manufacture of explosives
manufacturing is vital to Great Britain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: production - the process of creating goods and services
  • Antonym: consumption - the using up of goods and services

Etymology

From manufacture, ultimately from Latin 'manu factus' (made by hand).
