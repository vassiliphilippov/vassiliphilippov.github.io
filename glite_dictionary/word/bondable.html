bondable

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɑndəbəl / (AmE), / ˈbɒndəbəl / (BrE)
able to be fastened, joined, or secured by a bond or bonding process
able to be bonded or joined

Examples

These materials are bondable with most common adhesives.
The engineer specified a bondable surface to ensure a strong connection.
Only bondable metals can be used in this assembly process.

Etymology

Formed from 'bond' + '-able'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɑndəbəl / (AmE), / ˈbɒndəbəl / (BrE)
capable of forming bonds or adhering, especially referring to particles, surfaces, or materials that can be joined together firmly
able to form bonds or stick together

Examples

The glue creates a bondable surface for attaching the tiles securely.
Certain metals are more bondable than others when treated with special chemicals.
A bondable fabric is essential for this type of garment construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adhesive - able to stick or adhere firmly to another surface
  • Synonym: cohesive - tending to stick or hold together as a mass

Etymology

From 'bond' (to join or stick together) + '-able' (capable of).
