fomentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊmənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊmɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the application of warm, moist coverings to a part of the body to relieve pain and reduce inflammation
application of warm, moist covering for relief
medical

Examples

The nurse prepared a fomentation to ease the patient's sore muscles.
He was advised to use a warm fomentation on his injured knee several times a day.
Applying a fomentation can help reduce swelling after a minor injury.

Etymology

From Latin fomentatio, from fomentare (to warm, cherish), from fomentum (application of warmth).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of instigating or encouraging trouble or discord, usually intentionally
deliberate instigation of trouble

Examples

The fomentation of unrest by outside agitators led to days of protests.
His speech was criticized for its fomentation of public anger.
Authorities are cracking down on the fomentation of racial discord.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incitement - the act of encouraging others to act, especially unlawfully
  • Antonym: suppression - the act of putting an end to something, typically forcefully

Etymology

From Latin fomentatio (a warming or encouragement), from fovēre (to warm, cherish).

3. NOUN

/ foʊ.mɛnˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / fəʊ.menˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a substance applied as a warm, moist compress to treat pain, inflammation, or injury
warm moist medicinal compress
medical archaic

Examples

The doctor recommended a warm fomentation to ease the swelling in her ankle.
Traditional remedies often include applying a herbal fomentation to the affected area.
He prepared a fomentation of vinegar and herbs as part of the treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poultice - a soft, moist mass applied to the body to relieve soreness
  • Synonym: compress - a pad of absorbent material pressed onto part of the body to relieve inflammation or stop bleeding

Etymology

From Latin fomentatio (application of warm compresses), from fomentare (to heat, cherish).
