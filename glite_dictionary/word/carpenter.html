carpenter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.pən.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.pən.tə / (BrE)
a person whose job is making or repairing wooden structures or objects
woodworker; maker/repairer of wooden objects

Examples

The carpenter built a sturdy bookshelf for the library.
As a skilled carpenter, she specialized in restoring old furniture.
They hired a carpenter to repair the creaking wooden stairs in their home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joiner - skilled worker who joins wood, often for finer work than a carpenter

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French 'carpentier', from Latin 'carpentarius' (wagon maker), from 'carpentum' (wagon).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːr.pən.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.pɪn.tə / (BrE)
to work as a carpenter; to make or repair wooden objects and structures
work as a carpenter

Examples

He used to carpenter in his father's workshop during the summers.
They carpentered a sturdy table from reclaimed wood last weekend.
She has been carpentering part-time while studying architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodwork - the act of working with wood

Etymology

From Old French *carpentier*, from Latin *carpentarius* (wagon maker), from *carpentum* (wagon).
