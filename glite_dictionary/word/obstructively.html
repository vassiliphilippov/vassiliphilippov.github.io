obstructively

1. ADV

/ əbˈstrʌktɪvli / (AmE), / əbˈstrʌktɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that deliberately hinders or blocks progress, action, or movement.
in an obstructive or hindering way

Examples

The employee behaved obstructively during the meeting, making productive discussion difficult.
The new regulations were implemented obstructively, leading to confusion and delays.
She answered obstructively whenever asked about the missing files, avoiding any clear explanations.

Synset Examples

he acted very obstructively when we tried to carry out our project

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstructive - causing a blockage or hindrance

Etymology

From 'obstructive' + '-ly', where 'obstructive' is from Latin 'obstruere' (to block up).
