drumhead

1. ADJ

/ ˈdrʌmˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈdrʌmˌhɛd / (BrE)
(of a proceeding) carried out hastily and without usual legal formalities, especially in a military context.
carried out quickly without formalities

Examples

The soldiers faced a drumhead court-martial after the mutiny was suppressed.
Critics condemned the measures as drumhead justice, lacking proper legal procedures.
He was executed by a drumhead tribunal, with no chance to defend himself.

Synset Examples

a summary execution
summary justice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summary - (of a judicial process) carried out quickly and without usual formalities

Etymology

From the practice of using a drumhead as an improvised writing surface in field military courts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrʌmˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈdrʌmˌhɛd / (BrE)
The membrane that is stretched tightly over the open end of a drum and struck to produce sound.
membrane stretched over a drum

Examples

The drummer replaced the worn drumhead before the concert began.
A tight drumhead produces a sharper, higher-pitched sound than a loose one.
He carefully tuned the drumhead to get the perfect tone for his solo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drum - a musical instrument sounded by being struck, typically with sticks or the hands
