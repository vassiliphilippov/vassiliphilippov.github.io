necromancer

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛkrəˌmænsər / (AmE), / ˈnɛkrəˌmɑːnsə / (BrE)
a person who claims to communicate with or raise the dead in order to predict the future or gain secret knowledge
one who claims to conjure the dead

Examples

In the legend, the necromancer summoned spirits to reveal secrets about the future.
Many people feared the old man, believing he was a necromancer capable of controlling the dead.
The fantasy novel featured a powerful necromancer as the main antagonist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorcerer - a person who claims to have magical powers
  • Synonym: witch - a person, especially a woman, who practices magic

Etymology

From Latin 'necromantia', from Greek 'nekromanteia' (divination by the dead): 'nekros' (dead body) + 'manteia' (divination).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɛk.rəˌmæn.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈnɛk.rəˌmæn.sə / (BrE)
a person who practices magic, especially involving communication with the dead or the use of supernatural powers
one who practices magic or sorcery

Examples

The legend spoke of a powerful necromancer who could raise the dead from their graves.
Some villagers believed a necromancer was responsible for the strange events in the forest.
In fantasy novels, a necromancer often controls dark forces to achieve their goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorcerer - a person who claims or is believed to have magic powers
  • Synonym: wizard - a man who has magical powers
  • Synonym: witch - a person, typically a woman, who is credited with magic powers
  • Synonym: magician - a person with magical powers

Etymology

From Greek 'nekromanteia' (divination from the dead) via Late Latin 'necromantia'.
