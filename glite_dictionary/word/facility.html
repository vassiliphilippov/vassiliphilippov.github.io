facility

1. NOUN

/ fəˈsɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / fəˈsɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a building, place, or piece of equipment provided for a particular purpose or activity
place or equipment for a specific purpose

Examples

The university recently opened new facilities for science and engineering students.
All our hotel facilities, including the gym and the pool, are available to guests at no extra charge.
Modern sports facilities are essential for hosting international competitions.

Synset Examples

catering facilities
toilet facilities
educational facilities

Etymology

From Latin facilitas (easiness), from facilis (easy).

WordNet ID: oewn-00586443-n

2. NOUN

/ fəˈsɪləti / (AmE), / fəˈsɪlɪti / (BrE)
an ability to do something easily or skillfully
skill or ease in doing something

Examples

She demonstrated remarkable facility with foreign languages during the conference.
His facility for playing the piano impressed everyone at the recital.
The young scientist's facility with complex theories allowed her to advance quickly in her field.

Synset Examples

his quick adeptness was a product of good design
he was famous for his facility as an archer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skill - the ability to do something well
  • Synonym: proficiency - a high degree of competence or skill
  • Antonym: difficulty - something that is hard to accomplish; the opposite of ease

Etymology

From Latin facilitas (easiness), from facilis (easy).
