agenda

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʒɛndə / (AmE), / əˈdʒɛndə / (BrE)
a list or plan of things to be considered or done at a meeting or event, arranged in order
list or plan of items for discussion or action

Examples

The board meeting began with a review of the agenda for the day.
Please send me the agenda before our conference tomorrow so I know what will be discussed.
There are five items on the agenda that we need to address.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schedule - a plan of activities or events and when they will happen
  • Synonym: itinerary - a planned route or journey

Etymology

From Latin 'agenda' (things to be done), plural of 'agendum'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈdʒɛndə / (AmE), / əˈdʒendə / (BrE)
a list of items to be discussed or acted upon, especially at a meeting
list of items for a meeting

Examples

The meeting began with a review of the agenda for the day.
Please let me know if you have any topics to add to the agenda.
We didn't have time to discuss all the items on the agenda.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schedule - a plan listing events or activities and when they will happen
  • Antonym: minutes - a written record of what was discussed at a meeting

Etymology

From Latin 'agenda', meaning 'things to be done', plural of 'agendum'.

Synset POS: n
