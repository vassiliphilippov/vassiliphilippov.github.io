Otus asio

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊtəs ˈeɪsioʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊtəs ˈeɪsiəʊ / (BrE)
A small North American owl with hornlike tufts of feathers, known for a call that sounds like a quavering whistle; also called the Eastern screech owl.
North American owl species (Eastern screech owl)

Examples

The Otus asio is commonly found in wooded urban areas throughout eastern North America.
At dusk, the distinctive call of the Otus asio can be heard echoing through the forest.
Bird enthusiasts often spot the Otus asio by its hornlike feather tufts and camouflaged plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eastern screech owl - Common name for Otus asio

Etymology

From New Latin; Otus is a genus name derived from Greek ōtos (of the ear, referring to ear tufts), and asio is a species epithet used for various owls.
