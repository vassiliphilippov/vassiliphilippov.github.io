Samarkand

1. PROPN

/ ˌsæm.ərˈkænd / (AmE), / ˌsæm.əˈkænd / (BrE)
a city in southern Uzbekistan, historically known as Tamerlane's opulent capital in the 14th century, and famous for its ancient architecture and role along the Silk Road.
historic city in Uzbekistan

Examples

She traveled to Samarkand to see the magnificent Registan Square and ancient mosques.
Samarkand was a key city along the Silk Road, connecting China with the Mediterranean world.
The vibrant markets of Samarkand are renowned for their colorful textiles and spices.

Etymology

From Persian سمرقند (Samarqand), an ancient name for the city.
