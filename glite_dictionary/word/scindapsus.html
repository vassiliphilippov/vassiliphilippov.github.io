Scindapsus

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈdæpsəs / (AmE), / sɪnˈdapsəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen climbing plants native to southeastern Asia and Brazil, known for their adhesive adventitious roots.
genus of evergreen climbing plants

Examples

The Scindapsus is a popular houseplant due to its striking foliage and low maintenance.
Botanists recently discovered a new species of Scindapsus in the forests of Malaysia.
She decorated her living room with several Scindapsus vines climbing up a tall trellis.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek roots 'skindapsos' (a kind of ivy).
