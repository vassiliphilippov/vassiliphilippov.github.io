incisive

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsaɪsɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪsɪv / (BrE)
(of a tool, tooth, or edge) suitable for cutting or piercing; having a sharp or cutting edge.
sharp or suitable for cutting

Examples

The tiger's incisive teeth are perfect for slicing through flesh.
A surgeon relies on incisive instruments to perform delicate operations.
The fossil showed several rows of incisive teeth adapted for catching prey.

Synset Examples

incisive teeth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharp - having a thin edge or point that can cut easily

Etymology

From Latin 'incisivus' (cutting), from 'incidere' (to cut into).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsaɪsɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪsɪv / (BrE)
showing a clear and deep understanding of something; able to express ideas clearly and sharply
clear and sharp in thought or expression

Examples

Her incisive analysis impressed everyone at the meeting.
He is known for his incisive writing style, which makes complex issues clear.
The interviewer asked a series of incisive questions that revealed new information.

Synset Examples

an acute observer of politics and politicians
incisive comments
icy knifelike reasoning
as sharp and incisive as the stroke of a fang
penetrating insight
frequent penetrative observations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penetrating - showing an ability to understand things deeply
  • Synonym: acute - having or showing a perceptive understanding
  • Antonym: blunt - not sharp or clear in perception or expression

Etymology

From Latin *incisivus*, from *incidere* (to cut into).
