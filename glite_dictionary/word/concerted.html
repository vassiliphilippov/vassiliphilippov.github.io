concerted

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɝːtɪd / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːtɪd / (BrE)
(of an action or effort) done with cooperation and coordination by two or more people, groups, or organizations; planned or performed together.
done together in a coordinated way

Examples

The company made a concerted effort to improve customer service across all departments.
A concerted response by several nations was required to tackle the global crisis.
They launched a concerted campaign to raise awareness about the environmental issues.

Synset Examples

concerted action
the conjunct influence of fire and strong wind
the conjunctive focus of political opposition
a cooperative effort
a united effort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooperative - involving working together toward a common goal
  • Synonym: united - joined together for a common purpose or by common feelings
  • Antonym: independent - not influenced or controlled by others in matters of opinion, conduct, etc.

Etymology

From French 'concerné', past participle of 'concerter' (to act in harmony), from Italian 'concertare'.
