Elanoides forficatus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlænoʊˈaɪdiːz fɔːrˈfɪkətəs / (AmE), / ɪˌlæniːˈəʊdiːz fɔːˈfɪkətəs / (BrE)
A species of graceful black-and-white kite native to North America, commonly known as the swallow-tailed kite.
North American swallow-tailed kite

Examples

The Elanoides forficatus soars effortlessly over the southern wetlands each spring.
Many birdwatchers travel to Florida to observe the distinctive flight of the Elanoides forficatus.
The deeply forked tail helps the Elanoides forficatus maneuver swiftly while hunting insects in flight.

Etymology

From Greek 'elanos' (kite) and Latin 'forficatus' (forked), referring to its forked tail.
