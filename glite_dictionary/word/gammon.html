gammon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæm.ən / (AmE), / ˈɡæm.ən / (BrE)
the hind leg or thigh of a pig, especially after it has been cured or smoked for use as bacon or ham
cured hind leg of a pig (bacon/ham)
british

Examples

He served thick slices of gammon with pineapple and chips for dinner.
The butcher recommended the smoked gammon for our holiday feast.
British breakfasts sometimes include grilled gammon as a hearty option.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ham - meat from the hind leg of a pig, especially when cured or smoked

Etymology

From Middle English *gambon*, from Old North French, from Old French *jambe* (leg).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæm.ən / (AmE), / ˈɡæm.ən / (BrE)
a cut of meat from the hind leg of a pig, typically cured or smoked like ham
meat from a pig's hind leg, usually smoked or cured

Examples

The chef served a thick slice of gammon with eggs and chips for dinner.
We bought a smoked gammon for the holiday feast.
After roasting the gammon, she glazed it with honey and mustard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ham - meat from the upper part of a pig's leg, cured or smoked

Etymology

From Middle English gammon, from Old North French *gambon* (ham), from *gambe* (leg).
