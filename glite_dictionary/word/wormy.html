wormy

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɜrmi / (AmE), / ˈwɜːmi / (BrE)
Infested with or damaged, as if eaten, by worms.
infested or damaged by worms

Examples

The apple was wormy and had to be thrown away.
Farmers often discard wormy potatoes during the harvest.
She refused to eat the wormy peach she found at the bottom of the basket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infested - occupied or overrun by pests or parasites
  • Antonym: sound - not damaged or decayed (as of fruit)

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɜrmi / (AmE), / ˈwɜːmi / (BrE)
(of a person) showing extreme submissiveness or lack of spirit; resembling a worm in being weak, timid, or servile.
totally submissive; weak or timid

Examples

He was so wormy at work that he never stood up for himself.
Her wormy attitude made her an easy target for bullies.
The manager criticized his team for being too wormy in difficult negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority or will of others
  • Antonym: assertive - having or showing a confident and forceful personality

Etymology

From 'worm' + '-y', originally describing something infested with worms; later extended figuratively to describe weak or timid character.
