coxa

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːk.sə / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.sə / (BrE)
the ball-and-socket joint formed between the head of the femur and the acetabulum in the hip region
the hip joint (femur-acetabulum connection)
medical

Examples

The surgeon examined the coxa for any signs of injury before proceeding with the operation.
Arthritis can often affect the coxa, leading to pain and stiffness in the hip area.
X-ray images showed that the patient's coxa was dislocated after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hip joint - the joint where the thigh bone meets the pelvis

Etymology

From Latin 'coxa' (hip, hip joint).
