paleoecology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪli.oʊ.iˈkɑːl.ə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌpæli.əʊ.iˈkɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of the relationships between ancient organisms and their environments
study of ancient ecosystems

Examples

Researchers in paleoecology analyze fossil records to reconstruct past environments.
Advances in paleoecology have deepened our understanding of mass extinction events.
He specialized in paleoecology, focusing on plant life during the Ice Age.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ecology - the study of relationships between organisms and their environments

Etymology

From Greek 'palaios' (ancient) + 'ecology'.
