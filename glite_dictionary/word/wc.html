W.C.

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbəl.juː ˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəl.juː ˈsiː / (BrE)
a room or compartment with a toilet, especially in Britain; a water closet
British term for toilet
british

Examples

The sign on the door clearly indicated the W.C. for guests.
Please ask the manager where the nearest W.C. is located.
On older trains in the UK, W.C. was commonly used instead of 'toilet.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toilet - a fixture for urination and defecation

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'water closet', originally referring to a flush toilet or room containing one.
