Moldavia

1. PROPN

/ mɒlˈdeɪviə / (AmE), / mɒlˈdeɪviə / (BrE)
a former region and historical principality in eastern Europe, now largely corresponding to the modern Republic of Moldova and parts of Romania; sometimes used as an alternate name for Moldova.
historical region in eastern Europe

Examples

The principality of Moldavia played a significant role in medieval European history.
He traveled across Moldavia to study its culture and traditions.
After the dissolution of the Soviet Union, Moldavia became known as the Republic of Moldova.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Moldova - a republic in eastern Europe; formerly part of the Soviet Union

Etymology

From Latin 'Moldavia', named after the Moldova river.
