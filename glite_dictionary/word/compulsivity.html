compulsivity

1. NOUN

/ kəmˌpəlsɪˈvɪɾi / (AmE), / kəmˌpʌl.sɪˈvɪɪ.ti / (BrE)
the psychological tendency to perform actions persistently and repetitively, often against one's conscious wishes or intentions
tendency to act compulsively
medical

Examples

Her compulsivity made it difficult for her to resist checking the lock multiple times.
Researchers are studying the neurological basis of compulsivity in obsessive-compulsive disorder.
He struggled with compulsivity in his habits, often repeating actions without thinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compulsiveness - the quality of being compulsive

Etymology

From 'compulsive' + '-ity', modeled on similar traits such as 'impulsivity'.
