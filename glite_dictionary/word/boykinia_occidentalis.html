Boykinia occidentalis

1. NOUN

/ bɔɪˈkɪniə ˌɑksɪˈdɛntəlɪs / (AmE), / bɔɪˈkɪniə ˌɒksɪˈdɛntəlɪs / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant native to western North America, with mostly basal leaves and branched clusters of small white flowers.
a plant species native to western North America

Examples

The moist ground was covered in Boykinia occidentalis, their white flowers standing out among the greenery.
Hikers in Oregon often encounter Boykinia occidentalis along shaded stream banks.
Scientists collected samples of Boykinia occidentalis to study its adaptation to rocky soils.

Etymology

Genus named after American botanist S. B. Boykin; 'occidentalis' is Latin for 'western.'
