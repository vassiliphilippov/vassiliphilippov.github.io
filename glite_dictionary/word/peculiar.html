peculiar

1. ADJ

/ pɪˈkjuljɚ / (AmE), / pɪˈkjuːliə / (BrE)
different from what is normal or expected; unusual or strange.
unusual; strange

Examples

The room had a peculiar smell that I couldn't quite identify.
She noticed a peculiar expression on his face during the meeting.
There is something peculiar about the way those birds move.

Synset Examples

a curious hybrid accent
her speech has a funny twang
they have some funny ideas about war
had an odd name
the peculiar aromatic odor of cloves
something definitely queer about this town
what a rum fellow
singular behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: odd - unusual or out of the ordinary
  • Synonym: strange - unusual in a way that is surprising or difficult to understand
  • Antonym: normal - usual, typical, or expected

Etymology

From Latin 'peculiaris' (of private property, special), from 'peculium' (private property).

3. ADJ

/ pɪˈkjuːljɚ / (AmE), / pɪˈkjuːljə / (BrE)
Belonging or relating exclusively to one person, group, or place; characteristic or distinctive.
distinctive; characteristic of one only

Examples

That legal system has features peculiar to the country’s unique history.
Each family has traditions peculiar to their own heritage.
