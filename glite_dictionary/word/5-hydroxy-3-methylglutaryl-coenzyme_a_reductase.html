5-hydroxy-3-methylglutaryl-coenzyme A reductase

1. NOUN

/ faɪv haɪˈdrɒksi θri ˈmiːθəlˌɡluːtəˌreɪl koʊˈɛnzaɪm eɪ rɪˈdʌkteɪs / (AmE), / faɪv hʌɪˈdrɒksi θri ˈmiːθɪlˌɡluːtəˌreɪl kəʊˈɛnzaɪm eɪ rɪˈdʌkteɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme in the liver that plays a key role in the production of cholesterol
liver enzyme for cholesterol synthesis
medical

Examples

Statins block the activity of 5-hydroxy-3-methylglutaryl-coenzyme A reductase, leading to lower cholesterol levels.
Researchers studied how mutations in 5-hydroxy-3-methylglutaryl-coenzyme A reductase can affect cholesterol synthesis.
The doctor explained that 5-hydroxy-3-methylglutaryl-coenzyme A reductase is crucial for regulating blood cholesterol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a substance produced by living organisms which acts as a catalyst to bring about biochemical reactions
  • Degree Lower: statin - a class of drugs that inhibit this enzyme to lower cholesterol

Etymology

Composed of chemical terms describing its structure and its function as a reductase enzyme, related to HMG-CoA.
