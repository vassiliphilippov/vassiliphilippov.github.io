Polycarp

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɑliˌkɑrp / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌkɑːp / (BrE)
(c. 69–155) A Greek bishop of Smyrna and early Christian martyr, renowned for refusing to renounce his faith and being burned to death.
early Christian bishop and martyr

Examples

The martyrdom of Polycarp is celebrated annually in the Christian calendar.
Many early Christian writings mention Polycarp as an important bishop in Asia Minor.
As a pupil of the apostle John, Polycarp played a key role in the development of Christian doctrine.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Πολύκαρπος (Polúkarpos), meaning 'much fruit'.
