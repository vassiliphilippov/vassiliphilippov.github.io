articulatio trochoidea

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˌtɪkjəˌleɪʃioʊ troʊˈkɔɪdiə / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪkjʊˌleɪʃɪəʊ trəˈkɔɪdɪə / (BrE)
A freely moving joint in which movement is limited to rotation, such as between the radius and ulna in the arm; also called a pivot joint.
type of rotating joint (pivot joint)
medical

Examples

The articulatio trochoidea enables the forearm to rotate smoothly during motion.
Doctors studied the damage to the articulatio trochoidea after the accident.
Movement of the head from side to side is a result of the articulatio trochoidea in the neck.

Synset Examples

the articulation of the radius and ulna in the arm is a pivot joint

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pivot joint - a joint that allows only rotary movement

Etymology

From Latin 'articulatio' (joint) and 'trochoidea' (like a wheel), reflecting rotational motion.
