lactide

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæk.taɪd / (AmE), / ˈlæk.tɪd / (BrE)
a cyclic compound formed by the dehydration of lactic acid, considered the anhydride of lactic acid and used in polymer chemistry
cyclic ester formed from lactic acid

Examples

The production of biodegradable plastics often begins with the polymerization of lactide.
Researchers studied the crystallization process of lactide to improve polymer properties.
Pure lactide was obtained through the distillation of lactic acid under reduced pressure.

Synset Examples

Lactides may be prepared by heating lactic acid in the presence of an acid catalyst.

Etymology

From 'lactic' (acid) and 'anhydride', referring to its structure as an internal anhydride of lactic acid.
