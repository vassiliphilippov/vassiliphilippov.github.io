bronchopneumonia

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrɑŋkoʊnuːˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌbrɒŋkəʊnjuːˈməʊniə / (BrE)
A form of pneumonia involving acute inflammation of the walls of the bronchioles.
type of pneumonia in bronchioles
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with bronchopneumonia after a chest X-ray revealed inflammation in the bronchioles.
Children and elderly people are particularly at risk of developing bronchopneumonia during flu season.
Treatment for bronchopneumonia typically involves antibiotics and supportive care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pneumonia - lung infection causing inflammation of the air sacs

Etymology

From Greek 'bronchos' (windpipe) + 'pneumon' (lung).
