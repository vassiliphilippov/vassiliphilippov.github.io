Callistephus

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪstəfəs / (AmE), / kəˈlɪstəfəs / (BrE)
A genus of Asiatic herbs in the daisy family, notable for species with large, showy flowers; especially refers to the China aster.
genus of Asiatic flowering herbs

Examples

The gardener planted several varieties of Callistephus along the walkway.
Callistephus is often grown for its vibrant, colorful blooms in summer gardens.
Many florists use Callistephus as a centerpiece in floral arrangements due to its showy flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: China aster - type of ornamental flower; species Callistephus chinensis

Etymology

From Greek kallistos (most beautiful) + -ephos (appearance).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪstəfəs / (AmE), / kəˈlɪstəfəs / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, native to Asia, known for a single species with large, colorful flowers (C. chinensis, the China aster)
genus of Asian flowering plants (China aster)

Examples

The vibrant blooms of Callistephus are a favorite in summer gardens worldwide.
Botanists have classified the China aster under the genus Callistephus.
Many gardeners choose Callistephus for its striking colors and long-lasting flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'kallos' (beauty) + 'stephos' (crown, wreath), referring to the attractive flower heads.
