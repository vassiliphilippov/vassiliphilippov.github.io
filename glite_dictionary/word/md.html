John grew up in MD before moving to New York.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛm ˈdi / (AmE), / ˌɛm ˈdiː / (BrE)
a doctor's degree in medicine; a person who holds this degree, especially one licensed to practice as a medical doctor.
Doctor of Medicine degree or title
medical

Examples

After years of study, she finally earned her MD and started practicing medicine.
To apply for the position, you must be an MD with at least five years of experience.
Dr. Roberts, an MD, specializes in internal medicine at the city hospital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Doctor of Medicine - the full form of MD, a medical degree

Etymology

Abbreviation of the Latin 'Medicinae Doctor' (Doctor of Medicine).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛmˈdi / (AmE), / ˌɛmˈdiː / (BrE)
a person who is qualified and licensed to practice medicine; a doctor of medicine
licensed medical doctor
medical

Examples

After years of study, she finally became an MD and started her own clinic.
The hospital has several MDs on call at all times.
You should see an MD if your symptoms persist for more than a few days.

Synset Examples

I felt so bad I went to see my doctor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctor - a qualified practitioner of medicine; a physician
  • Synonym: physician - a person qualified to practice medicine, especially as opposed to a surgeon

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin Medicinae Doctor (Doctor of Medicine).
