Ciconiidae

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌkoʊˈniːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / sɪˌkəʊˈnɪːɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large wading birds that includes all storks.
the stork family of birds

Examples

The Ciconiidae are known for their long legs and impressive migratory journeys.
Researchers observed several species within the Ciconiidae during their field study in Africa.
Members of the Ciconiidae family can be found on every continent except Antarctica.

Etymology

From modern Latin Ciconiidae, from Ciconia (the type genus, meaning 'stork' in Latin) + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
