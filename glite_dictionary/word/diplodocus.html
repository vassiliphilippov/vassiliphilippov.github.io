diplodocus

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈplɑːdəkəs, daɪˈploʊdəkəs / (AmE), / dɪˈplɒdəkəs, daɪˈpləʊdəkəs / (BrE)
a large, long-necked herbivorous dinosaur of the late Jurassic period, known for its long tail and found in western North America.
large long-necked Jurassic dinosaur

Examples

The museum features a life-sized skeleton of a diplodocus in the main hall.
Children are often fascinated by the long neck and tail of the diplodocus.
Paleontologists discovered new fossils belonging to a diplodocus in the Colorado Rockies.

Etymology

From modern Latin *Diplodocus*, coined in 1878 from Greek 'diploos' (double) and 'dokos' (beam), referring to the double-beamed structure of the dinosaur’s tail vertebrae.
