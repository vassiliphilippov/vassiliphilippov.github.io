tyrannical

1. ADJ

/ təˈrænɪkəl / (AmE), / tɪˈrænɪkəl / (BrE)
using power in a cruel and unjust way; showing oppression or injustice through arbitrary or severe behavior.
using power cruelly and unjustly

Examples

The tyrannical rule of the dictator led to widespread fear and unrest in the country.
Many workers quit because of their boss’s tyrannical management style.
Under her tyrannical control, the children were rarely allowed to express themselves freely.

Synset Examples

the oppressive government
oppressive laws
a tyrannical parent
tyrannous disregard of human rights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oppressive - using unjust or harsh power; causing discomfort or anxiety
  • Antonym: benign - gentle and kindly, not harmful

Etymology

From Late Latin tyrannicus, from Greek tyrannikos, relating to a tyrant.

2. ADJ

/ təˈrænɪkəl / (AmE), / tɪˈrænɪkəl / (BrE)
using power in a cruel and oppressive way; characteristic of a tyrant or absolute ruler.
cruel and oppressive use of power

Examples

The tyrannical regime punished anyone who dared to oppose it.
People lived in fear under the tyrannical rule of the king.
Her tyrannical management style drove many employees to quit.

Synset Examples

an authoritarian regime
autocratic government
despotic rulers
a dictatorial rule that lasted for the duration of the war
a tyrannical government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autocratic - relating to a ruler with absolute power
  • Synonym: despotic - exercising power in a cruel or oppressive way
  • Synonym: authoritarian - favoring or enforcing strict obedience to authority
  • Antonym: democratic - relating to or supporting democracy or its principles

Etymology

From Latin tyrannicus, from Greek tyrannikos, relating to a tyrant.
