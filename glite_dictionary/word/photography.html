photography

1. NOUN

/ fəˈtɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / fəˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the art, process, or occupation of taking and printing photographs or making movies
taking and printing photographs; making movies

Examples

She decided to pursue photography as a full-time career after graduating.
The exhibit showcases the best of wildlife photography from around the world.
Digital photography has changed the way we capture and share memories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinematography - the art or practice of making motion pictures

Etymology

From French *photographie*, from Greek *phōt-* (light) + *-graphē* (drawing, writing).

2. NOUN

/ fəˈtɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / fəˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the art, process, or job of producing images by recording light on a photosensitive surface, especially film or a digital sensor
the process of taking photographs

Examples

She developed a passion for photography after receiving her first camera.
Photography allows us to capture memories and preserve them forever.
He studied photography at university and now works as a professional photographer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photograph - a picture made using a camera

Etymology

From Greek 'phōt-', meaning light, and 'graphia', meaning writing or drawing.

3. NOUN

/ fəˈtɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / fəˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
The art or practice of taking and processing photographs.
the art of taking photographs

Examples

She has always been passionate about photography and now works as a professional photographer.
Modern photography relies heavily on digital technology and editing software.
He took up photography as a hobby to capture the beauty of nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photograph - a picture made using a camera
  • Degree Higher: cinematography - the art of photography and camerawork in film-making

Etymology

From Greek 'phōt-' (light) + 'graphia' (writing).
