doubling

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌblɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of increasing something by a factor of two
increase by a factor of two

Examples

The doubling of production led to increased profits for the company.
Rapid doubling of cases has alarmed public health officials.
With the right investment, doubling your savings in five years is possible.

Synset Examples

doubling with a computer took no time at all

Related Terms

  • Antonym: halving - the process of dividing something by two
  • Synonym: multiplication - the process of increasing in number or amount by combining with another

Etymology

From the verb 'double' (to make twice as much); ultimately from Latin 'duplus' (twofold).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌblɪŋ / (BrE)
an act of raising the stakes in a card game by double the previous amount, often as a strategic move to challenge opponents
raising stakes by 2x in a card game

Examples

He surprised everyone at the table with a bold doubling in the second round.
Her doubling forced the opponents to reconsider their strategy.
In bridge, doubling can be a powerful way to signal confidence in your hand.

Synset Examples

I decided his double was a bluff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double - to increase by twofold; to become twice as great
