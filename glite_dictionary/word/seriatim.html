seriatim

1. ADV

/ ˌsɪriˈætəm / (AmE), / ˌsɪəriˈætɪm / (BrE)
Taking one subject after another in a series; point by point.
one after another; point by point
legal

Examples

The judge addressed the arguments seriatim, responding to each issue in turn.
The committee discussed each point seriatim rather than as a whole.
The report examines the proposed policies seriatim, listing the advantages and disadvantages of each.

Etymology

From Latin 'seriatim', from 'series' (row, succession) + '-atim' (adverbial suffix).
