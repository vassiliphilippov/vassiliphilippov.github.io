to retool

1. VERB

/ riˈtuːl / (AmE), / riːˈtuːl / (BrE)
to reorganize or revise something, especially in order to improve or update it
revise or reorganize to improve

Examples

The company had to retool its production line to meet new emission standards.
After the merger, they decided to retool their entire marketing strategy.
We should retool our approach if we want to increase customer engagement.

Synset Examples

We must retool the town's economy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to modernize - to make something more modern or up to date
  • Synonym: to revamp - to renovate or improve something

Etymology

From re- (again) + tool (to equip with tools); originally meant to equip a factory with new tools for a new purpose.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02438396-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈtuːl / (AmE), / riːˈtuːl / (BrE)
to equip (a workshop or factory) with new or different tools or machinery, often to adapt to new production needs
equip with new tools or machinery

Examples

After acquiring new machinery, the company decided to retool the entire factory for electric car production.
The old plant will retool next year to focus on advanced robotics components.
Faced with changes in demand, management opted to retool its workshop to produce ventilators during the crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refit - to equip again or anew, especially with new or improved equipment

Etymology

re- (again) + tool, meaning to tool again or anew.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02343105-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ riˈtuːl / (AmE), / riːˈtuːl / (BrE)
to make changes to something, especially a system or process, in order to update or improve it; to reorganize or revise for greater effectiveness
revise or reorganize for improvement

Examples

The company decided to retool its marketing strategy to appeal to younger consumers.
After the merger, management began to retool the organization for greater efficiency.
The university plans to retool its curriculum to include more digital skills.

Synset Examples

We must retool the town's economy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revamp - to give new and improved form, structure, or appearance to something
  • Synonym: overhaul - to take apart for examination and repair; to thoroughly renovate

Etymology

Formed from prefix 're-' (again) + 'tool', originally referring to changing the tools in a factory.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02438396-v
Synset POS: v
