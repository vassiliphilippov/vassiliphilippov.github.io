Huayna Capac

1. PROPN

/ waɪˈnɑ kɑːˈpak / (AmE), / waɪˈnɑː kɑːˈpak / (BrE)
the Incan ruler under whom the Incan empire reached its greatest territorial extent, dying in 1525
Incan emperor who died in 1525

Examples

Huayna Capac expanded the Incan empire to its largest size before his death in 1525.
According to historians, Huayna Capac's rule marked the peak of Incan power in South America.
The death of Huayna Capac led to a civil war between his sons, weakening the Incan empire.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Atahualpa - last Incan emperor, son of Huayna Capac
  • Degree Lower: Pachacuti - Incan ruler who expanded the empire before Huayna Capac

Etymology

From Quechua *Wayna Qhapaq* meaning 'young noble' or 'young sovereign'.
