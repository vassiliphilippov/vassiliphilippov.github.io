bonus

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.nəs / (BrE)
an extra amount of money given to an employee, typically as a reward for good performance or to increase productivity
extra payment to employees

Examples

The company gave every employee a generous bonus after reaching their sales targets.
He received a year-end bonus for his exceptional performance at work.
Many workers are motivated to be more productive when a bonus is offered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incentive - something that encourages a person to do something or to work harder
  • Antonym: salary - regular payment from employer to employee

Etymology

From Latin 'bonus' (good).
