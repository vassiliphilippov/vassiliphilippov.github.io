indelible

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdɛləbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛlɪbəl / (BrE)
(of marks, memories, or impressions) not able to be removed, erased, or forgotten.
not able to be erased or forgotten

Examples

The children left an indelible mark on the freshly painted wall.
Her kindness made an indelible impression on everyone she met.
The experience was so powerful that it became an indelible memory.

Synset Examples

an indelible stain
indelible memories

Related Terms

  • Antonym: erasable - able to be erased or removed
  • Synonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely

Etymology

From Latin 'indelibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'delere' (to delete, destroy).
