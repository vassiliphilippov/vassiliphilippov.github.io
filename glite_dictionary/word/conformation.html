conformation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnfɔːrˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnfɔːˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the shape or structure of something, especially as determined by its outline or arrangement of parts
shape or structural arrangement

Examples

The conformation of the molecule determines its chemical properties.
Horse breeders pay close attention to the conformation of the animals they raise.
The artist studied the conformation of the landscape to create realistic paintings.

Synset Examples

he could barely make out their shapes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shape - the external form or appearance of something
  • Synonym: structure - the arrangement of parts in something complex

Etymology

From Latin conformare (to form), via French conformation.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.fɔːrˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.fɔːˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the shape or structure of something, especially as formed by a symmetrical arrangement of its parts
the shape or structure of something

Examples

The breed is recognized for its distinctive conformation, which sets it apart from others.
Architects admired the building's elegant conformation and harmonious proportions.
Changes in the molecular conformation can significantly affect its biological activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: configuration - the arrangement of parts or elements in a particular form, figure, or combination

Etymology

From Latin 'conformatio' (a forming, shaping), from 'conformare' (to shape, fashion).
