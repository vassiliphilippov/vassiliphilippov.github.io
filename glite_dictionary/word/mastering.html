mastering

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstərɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of preparing and transferring audio to create a master recording from which all copies are produced
preparing an audio master for duplication

Examples

After the album was recorded, professional mastering was done to ensure consistent sound quality.
The studio charges extra fees for mastering each track before release.
Good mastering can make a big difference in the final sound of a song.

Synset Examples

he received a bill for mastering the concert and making 100 copies

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mixing - combining audio tracks before the mastering stage

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæstərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstərɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of becoming highly proficient or skilled in a particular activity or subject.
becoming highly skilled at something

Examples

His mastering of the guitar impressed everyone at the concert.
It took her years of dedicated study before mastering advanced mathematics.
The chef's mastering of French cuisine is evident in every dish he makes.

Synset Examples

his mastering the art of cooking took a long time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastery - comprehensive knowledge or skill in a subject or accomplishment
  • Degree Lower: practice - the repeated exercise of an activity to gain skill

Etymology

From the verb 'master', ultimately from Old French 'maistre' and Latin 'magister' (teacher, master).
