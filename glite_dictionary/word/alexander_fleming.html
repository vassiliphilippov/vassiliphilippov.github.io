Alexander Fleming

1. PROPN

/ ˌæləɡˈzændər ˈflɛmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌælɪɡˈzændə ˈflɛmɪŋ / (BrE)
(1881–1955) Scottish bacteriologist who discovered penicillin.
discoverer of penicillin

Examples

Alexander Fleming was awarded the Nobel Prize in 1945 for his discovery of penicillin.
The work of Alexander Fleming revolutionized modern medicine and saved countless lives.
Many students learn about Alexander Fleming when studying the history of antibiotics.
