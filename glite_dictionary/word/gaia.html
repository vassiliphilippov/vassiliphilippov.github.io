Gaia

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡaɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪ.ə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the goddess who personifies the Earth and is regarded as the ancestral mother of all life, mother of Cronus and the Titans.
goddess of the Earth in Greek mythology

Examples

According to Greek mythology, Gaia was the primordial goddess who gave birth to the Titans.
Many ancient texts describe Gaia as the personification of the Earth itself.
The myth says Gaia and Uranus were the parents of Cronus and many other gods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaea - alternative spelling of Gaia, goddess of the Earth in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Γαῖα (Gaia), meaning 'earth'.
