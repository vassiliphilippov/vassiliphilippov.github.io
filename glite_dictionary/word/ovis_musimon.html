Ovis musimon

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vɪs ˈmjuː.sɪ.mən / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vɪs ˈmjuː.sɪ.mɒn / (BrE)
A wild mountain sheep native to Corsica and Sardinia, known for its curved horns and considered one of the ancestors of domestic sheep.
wild mountain sheep of Corsica and Sardinia

Examples

The Ovis musimon is often regarded as the wild ancestor of modern domestic sheep.
Researchers traveled to Corsica to observe a herd of Ovis musimon in its native habitat.
The curved horns of the male Ovis musimon are a distinctive feature of this wild sheep species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouflon - a wild sheep found in Corsica, Sardinia, and parts of Europe

Etymology

From Latin scientific nomenclature: genus Ovis (sheep) and species musimon (from Latin 'mouflon').
