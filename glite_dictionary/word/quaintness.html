quaintness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkweɪnt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkweɪnt.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being attractively unusual or old-fashioned
attractive old-fashioned quality

Examples

The quaintness of the village drew many tourists eager for a glimpse of the past.
Her grandmother's house was filled with a quaintness that made everyone feel at home.
Jack admired the quaintness of the hand-painted signs in the old town.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkweɪntnəs / (AmE), / ˈkweɪntnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being attractively unusual or old-fashioned
quality of being quaint or old-fashioned

Examples

She admired the quaintness of the small village, with its cobblestone streets and historic cottages.
The quaintness of the décor gave the café a cozy, inviting atmosphere.
His stories had a certain quaintness that reminded listeners of an earlier era.

Synset Examples

she liked the old cottage; its quaintness was appealing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charm - a pleasing or attractive quality
  • Antonym: modernity - the quality of being modern

Etymology

From 'quaint' + '-ness', with 'quaint' from Old French 'cointe' (expert, clever, fine), from Latin 'cognitus' (known).
