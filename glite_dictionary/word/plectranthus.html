Plectranthus

1. NOUN

/ plɛkˈtrænθəs / (AmE), / plɛkˈtranθəs / (BrE)
a genus of ornamental flowering plants in the mint family, which includes several species sometimes formerly classified as Coleus.
a genus of ornamental plants in the mint family

Examples

Several varieties of Plectranthus are popular as houseplants due to their attractive foliage.
The vibrant purple blooms of Plectranthus brightened the shady corner of the garden.
Botanists often discuss the classification of Plectranthus and its relationship to the genus Coleus.

Etymology

From Greek *plektron* (spur) + *anthos* (flower), alluding to the shape of the flower.
