perithelial

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛrɪˈθiːliəl / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈθiːliəl / (BrE)
relating to the tissue layer surrounding small blood vessels, especially in biological and medical contexts
relating to tissue around small blood vessels
medical

Examples

Recent studies have shown changes in perithelial cells associated with vascular diseases.
Disorders affecting the perithelial region can compromise blood vessel function.
The perithelial tissue acts as an interface between the vessel wall and surrounding structures.

Etymology

From peri- (around) + Greek thēlē (nipple, used in biology to refer to tissue layers) + -al.
