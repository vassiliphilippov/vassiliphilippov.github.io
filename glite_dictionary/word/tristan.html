Tristan

1. PROPN

/ ˈtrɪstən / (AmE), / ˈtrɪstən / (BrE)
A legendary hero of medieval romance, the nephew of the king of Cornwall, who fell in love with Iseult, his uncle's bride, after they drank a love potion.
hero of medieval legend; lover of Iseult

Examples

Tristan is best known for his tragic love affair with Iseult in medieval literature.
Many adaptations of the story of Tristan and Iseult have appeared in opera and film.
The legend portrays Tristan as both a brave knight and a devoted lover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iseult - the legendary lover of Tristan in medieval romance

Etymology

From Old French Tristan, from Celtic name Drustanus; popularized by medieval romances.
