bister

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪstər / (AmE), / ˈbɪstə / (BrE)
a brownish-yellow, water-soluble pigment made from wood soot, used especially in drawing and painting
brownish-yellow pigment from wood soot

Examples

The artist used bister to create delicate shadows in the landscape sketch.
Renaissance drawings often feature bister washes for a warm, soft effect.
She preferred bister over ink for its translucent, earthy tone.

Etymology

From French 'bistre', possibly of Germanic origin.
