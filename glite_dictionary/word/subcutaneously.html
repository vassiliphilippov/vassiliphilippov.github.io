subcutaneously

1. ADV

/ sʌbˌkjuːtəˈneɪəsli / (AmE), / sʌbˌkjuːtəˈneɪəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is applied or introduced under the skin, especially by injection
under the skin (adverb); by injection
medical

Examples

The medication should be administered subcutaneously once a week for optimal results.
Insulin is often injected subcutaneously into the thigh or abdomen.
Vaccines may sometimes be given subcutaneously rather than intramuscularly.

Synset Examples

inject subcutaneously

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intravenously - in a way that is applied or introduced into a vein
  • Synonym: subcutaneous - situated or applied under the skin (adjective)

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under) + 'cutis' (skin) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
