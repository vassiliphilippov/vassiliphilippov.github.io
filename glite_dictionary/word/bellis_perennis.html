Bellis perennis

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛlɪs pəˈrɛnɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɛlɪs pəˈrɛnɪs / (BrE)
a low-growing Eurasian plant, commonly known as the common daisy, with a yellow central disc and pinkish-white outer ray flowers
common daisy species

Examples

The lawn was dotted with Bellis perennis blooming among the grass.
Gardeners often appreciate Bellis perennis for its resilience and cheerful flowers.
Children made daisy chains from the Bellis perennis growing in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daisy - any of several plants of the genus Bellis, especially Bellis perennis, with white or pinkish rays and yellow center

Etymology

From Latin: 'bellis' (pretty) and 'perennis' (perennial, lasting throughout the year).
