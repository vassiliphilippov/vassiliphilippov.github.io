patient

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪʃənt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪʃənt / (BrE)
a person who is receiving medical treatment or care, especially from a doctor or hospital
person receiving medical care

Examples

The patient was admitted to the hospital after complaining of chest pain.
Doctors must ensure the confidentiality of every patient's records.
The nurse checked on each patient in the ward throughout the night.

Synset Examples

the number of emergency patients has grown rapidly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: doctor - someone qualified to treat people's illnesses

Etymology

From Latin 'patiens', present participle of 'pati' (to suffer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪʃənt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪʃənt / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The entity that is affected by or undergoes the action of a verb in a clause, rather than performing the action.
entity affected by a verb (linguistics)

Examples

In the sentence 'The window was broken by the ball,' 'the window' is the patient.
Linguists often distinguish between the patient and the agent in a sentence.
The verb usually marks the patient differently from the agent in some languages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agent - the entity that performs the action of a verb (linguistics)

Etymology

From Latin patiens, present participle of pati (to suffer, undergo).

3. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪʃənt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪʃənt / (BrE)
able to accept or tolerate delays, suffering, or difficult situations without becoming annoyed or anxious.
tolerant and accepting in adversity

Examples

She remained patient even when the meeting ran late and others were growing irritated.
It is important to be patient with yourself while learning a new skill.
He listened with a patient smile as the children told their stories.

Synset Examples

a patient smile
was patient with the children
an exact and patient scientist
please be patient

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impatient - easily annoyed by delays or difficulties; unable to wait calmly

Etymology

From Latin 'patientem' (suffering, enduring), from 'pati' (to suffer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01739985-a
Synset POS: a
