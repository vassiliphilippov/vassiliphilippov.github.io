chaff

1. NOUN

/ tʃæf / (AmE), / tʃɑːf / (BrE)
the husks of grains and grasses separated during threshing, or the seed coverings and small, dry pieces left after seeds are processed
husks or seed coverings from grain

Examples

The farmer used a machine to separate the chaff from the wheat.
Wind scattered the chaff across the barn floor during threshing.
After harvesting, the seeds were cleaned of all chaff and debris.

Etymology

Old English *ceaf* (husk, chaff).

2. NOUN

/ tʃæf / (AmE), / tʃɑːf / (BrE)
Thin strips of metal or foil that are released into the air as a countermeasure to confuse enemy radar.
thin metal strips used to confuse radar

Examples

The pilot released chaff to evade the incoming missile.
Modern fighter jets are equipped with both chaff and flares for self-defense.
The military exercise included a demonstration of chaff deployment to obscure radar signatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radar jammer - device/method to block or deceive radar systems

3. VERB

/ tʃæf / (AmE), / tʃɑːf / (BrE)
to tease or joke with someone in a friendly or playful way
tease or joke in a friendly way

Examples

The children chaffed each other as they waited for the bus.
She enjoys chaffing her coworkers about their coffee habits.
They often chaff him about his terrible puns, but it's all in good fun.

Synset Examples

After we relaxed, we just kidded around

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tease - to make fun of or provoke in a playful way
  • Degree Higher: mock - to make fun of someone, sometimes not kindly

Etymology

From Middle English 'chaffen', from Old French 'chaffier' (to chafe, to warm), later influenced by the sense of teasing or light banter.

4. NOUN

/ tʃæf / (AmE), / tʃɑːf / (BrE)
worthless or trivial matter; things that are unimportant or of no value
worthless or trivial things

Examples

Much of what he said was just chaff that distracted from the main issue.
It's important to separate the chaff from the valuable information in these reports.
Amid the flood of online content, learning to ignore the chaff is an essential skill.

Synset Examples

Don't listen to the chaff; focus on the important advice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dross - something regarded as worthless or of low quality
  • Antonym: worthwhile - important or useful; deserving effort or attention

Etymology

From Old English 'ceaf' (husks, refuse of grain).
