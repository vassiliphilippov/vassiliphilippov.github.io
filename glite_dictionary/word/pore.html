pore

1. NOUN

/ pɔr / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
a very small opening in a surface, especially one that allows liquids or gases to pass through
tiny opening in a surface

Examples

Sweat escapes from the skin through tiny pores all over the body.
Some fabrics have microscopic pores that allow air but not water to pass through.
Oil can build up in the pores of your face and cause acne.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opening - an act or place of something being opened

Etymology

From Latin 'porus' (passage, pore), from Ancient Greek 'poros' (passage, pore).

2. NOUN

/ pɔr / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
A small opening on the surface of the skin or outer layer of an animal, through which gases, liquids, or microscopic particles may pass.
small skin opening

Examples

The skin's pores can become clogged with oil and dirt.
Sweat is released through tiny pores in the skin.
After washing her face, she could feel her pores tighten.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: follicle - small cavity in the skin, often associated with hair roots
  • Synonym: duct - a tube or channel in the body for carrying substances

Etymology

From Latin 'porus', from Greek 'poros' (passage, pore).

3. VERB

/ pɔr / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
to read or look at something very carefully and attentively, especially for a long time
to study something intently

Examples

She pored over the old letters, searching for clues about her ancestry.
He pores over scientific journals every evening after dinner.
The students were poring over their notes before the big exam.

Synset Examples

Please focus on your studies and not on your hobbies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: study - to devote time and attention to acquiring knowledge
  • Antonym: skim - to read something quickly without paying close attention

Etymology

From Middle English *poren* (to gaze intently); origin uncertain.

4. NOUN

/ pɔr / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
a minute opening in the epidermis of a leaf or stem, through which gases and water vapor pass
small opening in plant leaves or stems

Examples

Water vapor escapes through each pore on the leaf surface during transpiration.
The plant's stem contains specialized pores that allow gas exchange with the atmosphere.
Scientists examined the pores under a microscope to study how the leaves breathe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoma - a microscopic opening in the epidermis of plants, similar to a pore

Etymology

From Latin 'porus', from Ancient Greek 'poros' (passage, pore).

Synset POS: n
