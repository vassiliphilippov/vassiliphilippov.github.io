apolemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈliːmiə / (AmE), / ˌapəˈlɛmiə / (BrE)
a genus of large siphonophores, colonial marine animals that can reach lengths of up to 50 feet
a large colonial marine siphonophore
medical

Examples

Marine biologists were amazed by the sight of a massive apolemia trailing its translucent tentacles in the deep sea.
The apolemia is considered one of the longest animals in the world, rivalling even the blue whale.
Researchers photographed a rare apolemia specimen during their deep-sea expedition off the coast of Australia.

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Apolemia', coined as a genus name in zoological classification.
