slugger

1. NOUN

/ ˈslʌɡər / (AmE), / ˈslʌɡə / (BrE)
a baseball player who is known for powerful batting, especially one who often hits home runs
powerful baseball batter

Examples

The team's best slugger hit twenty home runs this season.
Fans cheered as the famous slugger stepped up to the plate.
She quickly gained a reputation as a powerful slugger in the league.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batter - a player who is batting in baseball

Etymology

From 'slug' (to hit hard) + -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈslʌɡər / (AmE), / ˈslʌɡə / (BrE)
a boxer who is known for their ability to punch very hard, rather than relying on speed or finesse
a power-punching boxer

Examples

The young slugger quickly gained a reputation for knocking out opponents in the first round.
He was more of a tactical fighter than a slugger, relying on strategy rather than brute force.
Fans love to watch a slugger because of the excitement hard punches bring to a match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxer - a person who fights as a sport, especially professionally

Etymology

From 'slug' (to hit hard) + -er (agent noun suffix), originally American English.

Synset POS: n
