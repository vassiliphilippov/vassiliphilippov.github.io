amalgamated

1. ADJ

/ əˈmæl.ɡəˌmeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / əˈmal.ɡə.meɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
formed by combining two or more things into a single, unified whole; merged.
combined or merged into one

Examples

The two companies were amalgamated to improve efficiency and reduce costs.
After the villages were amalgamated, local services were managed from a single center.
Several smaller charities were amalgamated into one large organization.

Synset Examples

the amalgamated colleges constituted a university
a consolidated school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merged - combined or blended to form a single entity
  • Antonym: separated - set apart or divided into different parts

Etymology

From amalgamate, from medieval Latin amalgamat-, past participle of amalgamare (to amalgamate).

2. ADJ

/ əˈmæl.ɡəˌmeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / əˈmæl.ɡəˌmeɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of organizations or elements) joined together to form a single unit.
joined together; united as a whole

Examples

The two banks were amalgamated last year to increase their market share.
The amalgamated company now operates in over 30 countries worldwide.
Through restructuring, the amalgamated departments improved efficiency across the organization.

Synset Examples

the amalgamated colleges constituted a university
a consolidated school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merged - (of companies or organizations) combined to form a single entity
  • Synonym: united - joined together; combined
  • Antonym: separate - not joined or united; kept apart

Etymology

From amalgamate, from Medieval Latin amalgamatus, past participle of amalgamare (to alloy with mercury).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
