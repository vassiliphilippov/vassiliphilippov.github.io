enfranchised

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfræn.tʃaɪzd / (AmE), / ɪnˈfræn.tʃaɪzd / (BrE)
Given the rights of citizenship, especially the right to vote.
having the rights of citizenship

Examples

After years of struggle, women were finally enfranchised and could cast their ballots in national elections.
The newly enfranchised groups participated actively in the democratic process.
Civil rights activists fought tirelessly to ensure all citizens became enfranchised.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disenfranchised - deprived of the rights of citizenship, especially the right to vote

Etymology

From the verb 'enfranchise', ultimately from Old French 'enfranchir' meaning 'to set free'.
