wisely

1. ADV

/ ˈwaɪzli / (AmE), / ˈwaɪzli / (BrE)
In a way that shows wisdom, good judgment, or intelligence.
in a wise or sensible way

Examples

She invested her savings wisely and reaped the benefits years later.
You must choose wisely when deciding your future career path.
He handled the situation wisely, avoiding unnecessary conflict.

Synset Examples

she acted wisely when she invited her parents

Related Terms

  • Antonym: foolishly - in a way that lacks good sense or judgment

Etymology

From wise + -ly, with 'wise' from Old English 'wīs'.
