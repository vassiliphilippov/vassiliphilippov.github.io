hectograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛktəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈhɛktəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a duplicating device that uses a gelatin plate from which ink can be transferred to make copies
duplicating device using gelatin plate

Examples

The teacher produced worksheets for the class using a hectograph.
Early illustrators sometimes relied on the hectograph to duplicate their sketches.
The hectograph was eventually replaced by more modern copying technologies.

Etymology

From Greek 'hecto-' meaning 'hundred' and '-graph' meaning 'to write'; originally referring to its ability to make many copies.

2. VERB

/ ˈhɛktəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈhɛktəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
to reproduce or copy (something) using a hectograph duplicator, an early device for making multiple copies of written or drawn material
make copies using a hectograph
archaic

Examples

The teacher used to hectograph exam sheets for the entire class.
Before photocopiers became common, people would hectograph newsletters at home.
They hectographed dozens of handouts for the club meeting.

Synset Examples

hectograph the hand-outs

Etymology

From Greek hektos (sixth) + -graph, referring to duplicating up to six copies.
