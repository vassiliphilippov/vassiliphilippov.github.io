Prunus padus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruː.nəs ˈpeɪ.dəs / (AmE), / ˈpruː.nəs ˈpæd.əs / (BrE)
a small European cherry tree with clusters of fragrant white flowers and small bitter black cherries, known as bird cherry
species of European cherry tree

Examples

The fragrant blossoms of the Prunus padus attract many bees in early spring.
In the old estate garden, several Prunus padus trees form a shady avenue.
Birds feed on the small, bitter fruits produced by the Prunus padus each summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chokecherry - a closely related North American cherry tree (Prunus virginiana)

Etymology

From Latin: 'prunus' (plum/cherry tree), 'padus' (referring to this species).
