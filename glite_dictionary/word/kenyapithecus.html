Kenyapithecus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛniəˈpɪθɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌkɛniəˈpɪθɪkəs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of primate with large molars and strong chewing muscles, known from fossils found in Kenya
extinct genus of primate from Kenya

Examples

Fossils of Kenyapithecus were discovered in the Miocene deposits of eastern Africa.
Scientists believe that Kenyapithecus had a diet requiring powerful jaws and robust molars.
The discovery of Kenyapithecus provided important clues about primate evolution.

Etymology

From Kenya (the country) + Ancient Greek píthēkos (ape).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɛniəˈpɪθɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌkɛniːəˈpɪθɪkəs / (BrE)
An extinct genus of primates known for powerful chewing muscles, large molars, and small incisors, with fossils discovered in Kenya.
extinct genus of primates from Kenya

Examples

Fossils of Kenyapithecus provide important clues about the evolution of early apes.
The powerful chewing muscles of Kenyapithecus suggest an adaptation to a tough diet.
Paleontologists discovered new specimens of Kenyapithecus at a site in northern Kenya.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proconsul - another extinct genus of fossil primates from Africa
  • Degree Higher: hominid - member of the biological family including humans, great apes, and their ancestors

Etymology

From Kenya (country in Africa) + Ancient Greek píthēkos (ape, monkey).
