subpoena ad testificandum

1. NOUN

/ səˌpinə æd təsˌtɪfɪˈkændəm / (AmE), / sʌˌpiːnə æd tɛstɪfɪˈkændəm / (BrE)
a writ issued by a court ordering a person to appear and give testimony as a witness at a judicial proceeding; failure to comply may be punished as contempt of court
writ requiring court witness testimony
legal

Examples

The court issued a subpoena ad testificandum compelling the witness to appear and testify at the trial.
Failure to obey a subpoena ad testificandum may result in contempt of court charges.
Her lawyer received a subpoena ad testificandum, requiring her presence in court as a witness.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: subpoena duces tecum - writ requiring witness to produce documents or evidence

Etymology

From Latin: 'subpoena' (under penalty) and 'ad testificandum' (to testify).
