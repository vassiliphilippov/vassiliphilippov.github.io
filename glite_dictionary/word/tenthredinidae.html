Tenthredinidae

1. NOUN

/ tɛnˌθrɛdəˈnɪdiː / (AmE), / tɛnˌθrɛdɪˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects known as sawflies, characterized by the saw-like ovipositor of the females; members of the order Hymenoptera.
family of sawflies (insects)

Examples

Many plants are susceptible to damage from larvae of the Tenthredinidae.
The Tenthredinidae family includes hundreds of species found worldwide.
Scientists discovered a new genus within Tenthredinidae in the Amazon rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sawfly - an insect of the family Tenthredinidae, with a saw-like ovipositor

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Tenthredo.

2. NOUN

/ tɛnθrɪˈdɪnɪˌdi / (AmE), / tɛnθrɪˈdɪnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of insects known as sawflies, characterized by larvae that resemble caterpillars and adults with membranous wings, belonging to the order Hymenoptera.
a family of insects known as sawflies

Examples

The larvae of many Tenthredinidae species feed on the leaves of trees and shrubs.
Researchers have studied Tenthredinidae to understand their ecological impact on forests.
Some garden plants can be damaged by Tenthredinidae infestations during the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sawfly - a common name for insects of the family Tenthredinidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Tenthredinidae, based on the genus Tenthredo.
