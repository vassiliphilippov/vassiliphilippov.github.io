alienation

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.li.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.li.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of making someone feel isolated or unfriendly, or the process of causing someone to withdraw or turn away
the act of making someone feel isolated

Examples

The manager's harsh criticism led to the alienation of several employees from the team.
Some teenagers experience alienation from their families during difficult times.
Prolonged conflict can result in the alienation of long-time friends.

Synset Examples

his behavior alienated the other students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estrangement - the state of being no longer friendly or close

Etymology

From Latin alienatio (separation), from alienare (to estrange), from alienus (belonging to another).

2. NOUN

/ ˌeɪliəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌeɪliəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a feeling of being isolated or estranged from other people, or from one's surroundings
feeling of isolation or estrangement

Examples

He struggled with a deep sense of alienation after moving to a new city.
The novel explores the alienation many teenagers feel from their families and peers.
Technology can sometimes increase feelings of alienation instead of bringing people together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estrangement - the state of being alienated or separated; loss of mutual affection

Etymology

From Latin alienatio(n-), from alienare 'estrange', from alienus 'of another'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌeɪliəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌeɪliəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a state of being isolated or estranged from others, often as a result of hostility or conflict
estrangement due to hostility

Examples

The feeling of alienation among the team members grew after the heated argument.
Cultural differences can sometimes lead to alienation within immigrant communities.
She described her alienation from her old friends after the dispute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estrangement - the state of being alienated or separated
  • Antonym: reconciliation - restoration of friendly relations

Etymology

From Latin alienatio(n-), from alienare (to estrange), from alienus (of another).

4. NOUN

/ ˌeɪliəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌeɪliəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(Law) the voluntary and complete transfer of ownership and possession of real property from one party to another.
legal transfer of property ownership
legal

Examples

The alienation of the estate required the owner's written consent.
A clause restricting alienation was included in the property agreement.
Alienation of land often involves complex legal procedures.

Synset Examples

the power of alienation is an essential ingredient of ownership

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conveyance - the legal process of transferring property from one owner to another

Etymology

From Latin alienatio (transfer, estrangement), from alienare (to estrange, to transfer property).
