Madame du Barry

1. PROPN

/ mæˈdæm duː ˈbæri / (AmE), / maˈdɑːm dy ˈbæri / (BrE)
(1743–1793) The last chief mistress of King Louis XV of France, influential at court, who was executed during the French Revolution.
French noblewoman, mistress of Louis XV

Examples

Madame du Barry was famed for her beauty and her influence over the French king.
Historians often debate the political power that Madame du Barry held at the court of Louis XV.
The tragic fate of Madame du Barry during the Revolution exemplifies the dangers faced by the aristocracy.

Etymology

French title 'Madame du Barry', referring to Jeanne Bécu, Comtesse du Barry.
