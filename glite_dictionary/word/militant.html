militant

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlənt / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtənt / (BrE)
a person who is vigorously active and aggressive, especially in support of a cause
aggressive supporter of a cause

Examples

The militant led demonstrations demanding government reform.
Police arrested several militants during the protest for disrupting public order.
As a young militant, she passionately campaigned for women's rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: activist - a person who campaigns for social or political change

Etymology

From Latin militans, present participle of militare (to serve as a soldier).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɪlɪtənt / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtənt / (BrE)
aggressively active, combative, or confrontational in support of a cause or belief
aggressively combative or confrontational

Examples

The militant protesters refused to back down despite police warnings.
She took a militant stance on animal rights, demanding immediate action from lawmakers.
His militant approach to union negotiations often led to heated arguments.

Synset Examples

highly competitive sales representative
militant in fighting for better wages for workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggressive - ready or likely to attack or confront; forceful
  • Antonym: peaceful - not involving conflict or violence; calm

Etymology

From Latin militans, present participle of militare (to serve as a soldier).

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɪlɪtənt / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtənt / (BrE)
engaged in warfare or combat; actively involved in fighting or hostilities
actively engaged in war or combat

Examples

The region has witnessed fierce clashes between militant groups and government forces.
Several militant factions declared their intent to continue fighting.
Security forces remain on high alert due to the ongoing activities of militant organizations.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: belligerent - engaged in war or conflict

Etymology

4. ADJ

/ ˈmɪlɪtənt / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtənt / (BrE)
Favoring confrontational or aggressive methods, especially in support of a cause or in government policy.
aggressively supportive of a cause or policy

Examples

Some militant groups advocate violence as a means of achieving their objectives.
The government adopted a militant stance against its rivals, refusing all diplomatic negotiations.
Her militant attitude toward environmental protection inspired others to join the movement.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warlike - inclined to or ready for war
  • Antonym: peaceful - not inclined to violence or war

Etymology

From Latin militans, present participle of militare (to serve as a soldier), from miles (soldier).
