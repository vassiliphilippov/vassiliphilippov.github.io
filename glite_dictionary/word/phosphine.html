phosphine

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːsfiːn / (AmE), / ˈfɒsfiːn / (BrE)
a colorless, flammable, and toxic gas with a strong fishy odor, used as a pesticide and in chemical synthesis
toxic, colorless gas used as pesticide

Examples

Exposure to phosphine gas can be hazardous without proper ventilation and safety precautions.
The use of phosphine as a fumigant is strictly regulated in many countries.
Scientists detected traces of phosphine in the atmosphere of Venus, sparking interest in possible biological processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphorus - chemical element with symbol P, essential for life

Etymology

From phosphorus + -ine, indicating its derivation as a phosphorus compound.
