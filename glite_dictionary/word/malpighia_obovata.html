Malpighia obovata

1. NOUN

/ mælˈpɪɡiə oʊboʊˈveɪtə / (AmE), / malˈpɪɡiə əʊbəʊˈvɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of Cuban timber tree with hard wood very resistant to moisture.
Cuban timber tree species

Examples

The wood of Malpighia obovata is highly prized for its resistance to moisture and durability.
Botanists have studied Malpighia obovata for its unique timber properties.
Many homes in Cuba were constructed using Malpighia obovata as a primary building material.

Etymology

Genus name from Italian naturalist Marcello Malpighi; species epithet from Latin 'obovatus' (egg-shaped).
