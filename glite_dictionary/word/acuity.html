acuity

1. NOUN

/ əˈkjuːəti / (AmE), / əˈkjuːɪti / (BrE)
the ability to think and understand things quickly and clearly; sharpness of mind
quick and sharp intelligence

Examples

Her mental acuity made her an exceptional lawyer.
Tests are designed to measure the acuity of a child’s reasoning ability.
He impressed everyone with his remarkable acuity during the debate.

Synset Examples

he argued with great acuteness
I admired the keenness of his mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acumen - the ability to make good judgments and quick decisions
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of intelligence or mental sharpness

Etymology

From Latin 'acuitas' (sharpness), from 'acuere' (to sharpen).

2. NOUN

/ əˈkjuːəti / (AmE), / əˈkjuːɪti / (BrE)
the sharpness or keenness of vision, especially as measured by the ability to distinguish fine detail
sharpness or keenness of vision
medical

Examples

Her visual acuity was tested with a standard eye chart during the examination.
Poor lighting can affect acuity and make it difficult to see small details clearly.
Children with high acuity can pick out tiny objects from a distance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acuteness - the quality of being acutely perceptive or sharp, especially relating to senses

Etymology

From Latin acuitas (sharpness), from acuere (to sharpen).
