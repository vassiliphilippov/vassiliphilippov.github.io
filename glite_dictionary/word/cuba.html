Cuba

1. PROPN

/ ˈkjuːbə / (AmE), / ˈkjuːbə / (BrE)
The largest island in the West Indies, located in the northern Caribbean, forming most of the country of Cuba.
largest island in the Caribbean; West Indies

Examples

I have always dreamed of visiting Cuba to explore its beautiful beaches and vibrant culture.
Hurricane Irma caused significant damage when it struck Cuba in 2017.
Cuba is renowned for its cigars, music, and colonial architecture.

Etymology

From Taíno *Cubao* or *Coabana*, likely meaning 'where fertile land is abundant'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈkjuːbə / (AmE), / ˈkjuːbə / (BrE)
A country in the Caribbean, consisting of the island of Cuba and several smaller islands, known for its communist government.
country in the Caribbean

Examples

He spent a year living in Cuba and learning Spanish.
The embargo between the United States and Cuba has lasted for decades.
Many people visit Cuba to experience its vibrant culture and history.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Cuba', the indigenous name for the island.
