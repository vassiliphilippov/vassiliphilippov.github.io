recessed

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈsɛst / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛst / (BrE)
(of a space or part) set back or formed so as to resemble an alcove or nook; indented into a surface.
set back like an alcove

Examples

The living room has a recessed shelf perfect for displaying art.
They installed lights in the recessed areas above the fireplace.
A recessed window seat created a cozy reading nook.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: protruding - extending outward from a surface

Etymology

From Latin recessus (a retreat, a going back), past participle of recedere (to go back).

WordNet ID: oewn-00539218-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈsɛst / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛst / (BrE)
(of a surface or area) set back or sunken below the surrounding level.
set back or sunken below surroundings

Examples

The shelves were built into a recessed section of the wall.
She admired the recessed panels on the antique door.
His eyes appeared recessed due to lack of sleep.

Synset Examples

hunger gave their faces a sunken look

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunken - having a depressed or hollowed surface or area

Etymology

From Latin recessus (a going back), via Middle English from Old French recesser.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02272277-s
Synset POS: s
