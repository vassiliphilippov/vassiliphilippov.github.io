paloverde

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæloʊˈvɜrdi / (AmE), / ˌpæləʊˈvɜːdi / (BrE)
a thorny shrub or small tree of the genus Cercidium, found in dry regions of the southwestern United States and Mexico, known for its smooth green bark, yellow flowers, and small leaves.
thorny shrub of the genus Cercidium

Examples

The paloverde blooms in spring, covering the desert with splashes of yellow flowers.
We found shade under a graceful paloverde during our hike in Arizona.
Many animals rely on the paloverde as a crucial source of food and shelter.

Etymology

From Spanish 'palo verde' meaning 'green stick' or 'green tree'.
