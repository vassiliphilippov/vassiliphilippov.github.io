Kongo

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
a Bantu language spoken by the Kongo people in the tropical forests of the Democratic Republic of the Congo, the Republic of the Congo, and Angola
Bantu language of the Kongo people

Examples

Many traditional stories of the Kongo people are told in Kongo.
She studied Kongo as part of her research on Central African languages.
The Kongo Bible translation project has made religious texts available in Kongo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kikongo - another common name for the Kongo language

Etymology

From the name of the Kongo people, indigenous to Central Africa.
