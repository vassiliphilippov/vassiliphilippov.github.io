bootstrap

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːtˌstræp / (AmE), / ˈbuːtstræp / (BrE)
a looped strap sewn to the top of a boot, used to help pull the boot on
strap for pulling on a boot

Examples

He grabbed the bootstrap to help slide his boot onto his foot.
The old leather bootstrap was worn, making it hard to pull the boot on.
Some modern boots no longer feature a bootstrap at the top.

Etymology

From 'boot' + 'strap', literally referring to a strap on a boot.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02877992-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈbuːtˌstræp / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌstræp / (BrE)
to improve one's situation by one's own efforts, often using minimal resources or improvised methods
improve by one's own efforts

Examples

After losing his job, he decided to bootstrap a new business from his garage.
With limited funding, the team managed to bootstrap their project to completion.
She had to bootstrap her education, taking free online courses and studying late into the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-help - the practice of improving oneself without outside assistance
  • Synonym: to improvise - to create or perform without preparation or with what is available

Etymology

Originally from the phrase 'to pull oneself up by one's bootstraps', 19th-century American English.
