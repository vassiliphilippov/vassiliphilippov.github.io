mullion

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌl.jən / (AmE), / ˈmʌl.jən / (BrE)
A vertical bar or strip, usually nonstructural, that separates the panes of glass or panels in a window or screen.
vertical bar between window panes

Examples

The stained glass was divided by a slender mullion in the center of the window.
Restorers carefully replaced the missing mullions to match the original style of the house.
Each casement was separated by a wooden mullion, lending the room an elegant appearance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transom - a horizontal crosspiece across a window or between a door and a window above it

Etymology

From Old French 'mouillon' (vertical jamb), of uncertain origin.
