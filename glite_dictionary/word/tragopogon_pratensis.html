Tragopogon pratensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌtræɡəˈpoʊɡən præˈtɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌtræɡəˈpɒɡən præˈtɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A weedy European plant in the daisy family, with yellow flowers, commonly called meadow salsify or goat's-beard, naturalized in North America.
weedy European plant with yellow flowers

Examples

Tragopogon pratensis is often found in meadows and grassy roadsides during late spring.
Gardeners sometimes mistake Tragopogon pratensis for a type of wild dandelion due to its yellow blooms.
The seeds of Tragopogon pratensis are dispersed by the wind, helping it to colonize new areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goat's-beard - common name for Tragopogon pratensis

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Tragopogon' from Greek 'tragos' (goat) + 'pogon' (beard), and 'pratensis' meaning 'of a meadow.'
