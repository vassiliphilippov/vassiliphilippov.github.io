Nordic

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɔːrdɪk / (AmE), / ˈnɔː.dɪk / (BrE)
relating to the Scandinavian group of languages (including Danish, Norwegian, Swedish, Icelandic, and Faroese)
relating to Scandinavian languages

Examples

Many linguists study the Nordic languages to understand their evolution and structure.
She enrolled in a course about Nordic grammar and pronunciation.
The Nordic linguistic tradition includes both modern and historical dialects.

Synset Examples

Nordic languages have a gender system

Etymology

From French *nordique*, from *nord* (north), ultimately from Old Norse *norðr* (north).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03104119-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrdɪk / (AmE), / ˈnɔːdɪk / (BrE)
the northern family of Germanic languages spoken in Scandinavia and Iceland, including Danish, Swedish, Norwegian, Icelandic, and Faroese
the Scandinavian and Icelandic language family

Examples

Old Nordic manuscripts have been invaluable to linguists studying the origins of Scandinavian languages.
Icelandic, Swedish, and Danish all belong to the Nordic group of languages.
The Nordic language family shares many features with other Germanic languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: North Germanic - the family of Germanic languages of Scandinavia and Iceland

Etymology

From Latin 'Nordicus' (northern), from 'nord' (north).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06966471-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈnɔrdɪk / (AmE), / ˈnɔːdɪk / (BrE)
relating to Scandinavia, Finland, Iceland, and often the northern Germanic peoples or countries of Northern Europe.
relating to Scandinavia or Northern Europe

Examples

The Nordic countries are known for their high quality of life and natural beauty.
She participated in a Nordic skiing competition last winter.
The Nordic model of welfare is admired by many other nations.

Synset Examples

Hitler wanted Nordic people to rule Europe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scandinavian - relating to Scandinavia or its peoples, languages, or cultures

Etymology

From French 'nordique', from 'nord' (north), ultimately from Old English 'norð'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03103985-a
Synset POS: a
