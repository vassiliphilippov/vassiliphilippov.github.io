nonsuch

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌnˌsʌtʃ / (AmE), / ˈnʌnˌsʌtʃ / (BrE)
A person or thing regarded as a perfect example; one that has no equal.
person or thing with no equal

Examples

The ancient castle was considered a nonsuch of medieval architecture.
She was a nonsuch in her field, unmatched in both talent and dedication.
Many regarded the legendary athlete as a nonsuch of determination and skill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paragon - a person or thing viewed as a model of excellence
  • Antonym: peer - a person of equal standing with another

Etymology

From Middle English none such (‘none such’), literally ‘not such another’.
