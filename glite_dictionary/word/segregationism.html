segregationism

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛɡ.rəˈɡeɪ.ʃəˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌsɛɡ.rɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
the policy or advocacy of keeping different racial, religious, or social groups separate from each other, especially as a political principle.
policy supporting racial or social segregation

Examples

The rise of segregationism in the mid-20th century shaped many laws in the southern United States.
Civil rights activists worked tirelessly against the spread of segregationism.
Modern democracies generally reject segregationism as incompatible with equality and human rights.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: integrationism - the policy or practice of encouraging integration across groups
  • Synonym: segregationist - a person who supports or practices racial or social segregation

Etymology

From segregation + -ism, patterned after similar -ism formations indicating ideology.
