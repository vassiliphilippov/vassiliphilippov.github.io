Dryas octopetala

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ.əs ˌɑk.təˈpiː.tələ / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ.əs ˌɒk.təˈpiː.tələ / (BrE)
A creeping evergreen shrub with large white flowers, found widely in northern regions of Eurasia and North America, commonly known as mountain avens.
species of creeping evergreen shrub

Examples

The Dryas octopetala blooms early in the alpine spring, brightening rocky slopes with its white petals.
Researchers studied the distribution of Dryas octopetala across Arctic tundra ecosystems.
Gardens with well-drained soil can successfully cultivate Dryas octopetala for its ornamental flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'dryas' (oak nymph) and Latin 'octopetala' (eight-petaled), referring to its eight-petaled flowers.
