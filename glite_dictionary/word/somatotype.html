somatotype

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.mə.t̬əˌtaɪp / (AmE), / səˈmæ.tə.taɪp / (BrE)
a category of physique characterized by a particular body shape and composition, often classified as ectomorph, mesomorph, or endomorph
category of body type or physique

Examples

Researchers studied the distribution of somatotype among school children to assess growth patterns.
Athletes often display a particular somatotype that favors their sport.
Understanding your somatotype can help tailor your nutrition and exercise plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: body type - general shape or structure of a person's body

Etymology

From Greek sōma (body) + type (form or kind).
