preposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɛp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌprɛp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a word used before a noun, pronoun, or noun phrase to show place, time, direction, or other relationship to another word in the sentence
grammatical word showing relationship

Examples

The word 'on' is a common preposition used to indicate position.
In the sentence 'She walked to the store,' 'to' functions as a preposition.
Students often have difficulty identifying the preposition in complex sentences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjunction - a word used to connect clauses or sentences
  • Antonym: noun - a word used to identify any of a class of people, places, or things

Etymology

From Latin praepositio (a putting before), from praeponere (to put before).
