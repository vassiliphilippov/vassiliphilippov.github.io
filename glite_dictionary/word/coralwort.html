coralwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrəlˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəlˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A European perennial plant of the cress family, known for its knotted white rootstock.
European cress with knotted white rootstock

Examples

The coralwort thrives in moist, shady woodland areas across Europe.
Botanists are studying the medicinal properties of coralwort roots.
Gardeners value coralwort for its delicate white flowers and unusual root system.

Etymology

From 'coral' (referring to the appearance of the rootstock) + 'wort' (Old English for 'plant' or 'herb').
