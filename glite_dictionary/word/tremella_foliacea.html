Tremella foliacea

1. NOUN

/ trəˈmɛl.ə foʊ.liˈeɪ.ʃə / (AmE), / trəˈmɛl.ə fəʊ.lɪˈeɪ.sɪə / (BrE)
a species of jelly fungus with a broad, gelatinous, brownish fruiting body resembling a bunch of leaf lettuce, found mostly on decaying wood
a species of brown jelly fungus

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with clusters of Tremella foliacea after the heavy rains.
Mycology students examined a sample of Tremella foliacea under the microscope to study its gelatinous texture.
She mistook Tremella foliacea for a patch of decaying leaves until she touched its jelly-like surface.

Etymology

From Latin: 'tremella' (jelly fungus) and 'foliacea' (leafy, from 'folium' meaning leaf).
