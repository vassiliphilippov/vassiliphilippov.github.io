manakin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænəkɪn / (AmE), / ˈmanəkɪn / (BrE)
a small, brightly colored bird found in Central and South America, known for its short bill and elaborate courtship displays
small, colorful South American bird

Examples

The male manakin performs an incredible dance to attract a mate.
Researchers observed several species of manakin in the rainforest canopy.
Some manakins create snapping sounds with their wings during their displays.

Etymology

From French *manakin*, from Dutch *manneken* (little man), possibly referencing the bird's upright posture.
