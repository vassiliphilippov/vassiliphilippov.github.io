Anguilliformes

1. NOUN

/ æŋˌɡwɪləˈfɔrˌmiːz / (AmE), / æŋˌɡwɪlɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of elongate, snake-like fishes, including the true eels, with absent or greatly reduced pelvic fins and girdle
order of eel-like fishes

Examples

The order Anguilliformes encompasses all true eels found in marine and freshwater environments.
Scientists study the unique body structure of Anguilliformes to understand their swimming mechanisms.
Unlike many other fishes, members of Anguilliformes lack pelvic fins entirely.

Etymology

From Latin 'anguilla' (eel) and 'formes' (shape, form).

2. NOUN

/ æŋˌɡwɪl.ɪˈfɔr.miːz / (AmE), / æŋˌɡwɪl.ɪˈfɔː.miːz / (BrE)
an order of elongate fishes, commonly known as eels, characterized by the absence or reduction of pelvic fins and girdle
an order of elongated eels (fishes)

Examples

The Anguilliformes are distinguished by their elongated bodies and reduced or absent pelvic fins.
Marine biologists study the habitat and behavior of fishes belonging to the Anguilliformes order.
Most species in the Anguilliformes are nocturnal predators found in both fresh and salt water.

Etymology

From Latin anguilla (eel) + -formes (having the form of), meaning 'eel-shaped'.
