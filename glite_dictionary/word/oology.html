oology

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / əʊˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of eggs, especially birds’ eggs, including their size, shape, color, and number
study of eggs, especially birds' eggs

Examples

She developed a keen interest in oology after observing various bird species in her garden.
The museum’s collection includes rare books on oology and bird conservation.
Modern oology relies on non-invasive techniques to study eggs without disturbing nests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zoology - the scientific study of animals and animal life

Etymology

From Greek ōion (egg) + -logy (study of).
