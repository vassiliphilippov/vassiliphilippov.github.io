amity

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈæm.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a friendly and peaceful relationship; mutual harmony or cordiality between individuals or groups
friendly or peaceful relationship

Examples

The two countries have lived in amity for many years, setting an example for the region.
Their amity was apparent in the way they supported each other at work.
The peace treaty was intended to ensure lasting amity among the neighboring nations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enmity - the state or feeling of being actively opposed or hostile
  • Synonym: friendship - the emotions or conduct of friends; the state of being friends

Etymology

From Old French amitié (friendship), from Latin amicitia, from amicus (friend).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæmət̬i / (AmE), / ˈæmɪti / (BrE)
a friendly relationship or peaceful harmony between people or nations
friendly relationship; harmony

Examples

The two countries lived in amity after signing the peace treaty.
A spirit of amity prevailed among the members of the international team.
Their decades-long amity was admired by all their friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friendship - the emotion or conduct of friends; the state of being friends
  • Antonym: enmity - the state or feeling of being actively opposed or hostile to someone

Etymology

From Old French amité (friendship), from Latin *amicitas*, from *amicus* (friend).
