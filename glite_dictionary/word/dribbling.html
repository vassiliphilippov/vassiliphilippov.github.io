dribbling

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪb.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɪb.lɪŋ / (BrE)
the action of moving a ball forward by a series of short taps or kicks, especially in sports such as football or basketball
moving a ball by taps or kicks (sports)

Examples

Her dribbling skills helped the team maintain possession throughout the match.
The coach asked us to practice dribbling around the cones every day.
Good dribbling can allow a player to evade defenders and create scoring opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dribble - to move a ball with repeated small bounces or taps

Etymology

From 'dribble' meaning to let fall or trickle, later applied to ball control in sports.
