consolidated

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɑː.lɪ.deɪ.t̬ɪd / (AmE), / kənˈsɒ.lɪ.deɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
formed into a solid or compact mass; made stronger or more unified.
formed into a solid or compact mass

Examples

After cooling, the mixture became a consolidated mass that could be easily handled.
The river deposited layers of silt which gradually formed a consolidated bank.
The building rests on a consolidated foundation of stone and mortar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solid - firm and stable in shape; not liquid or fluid

Etymology

From Latin consolidare (to make firm), from con- (together) + solidare (to make solid).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈsɑlɪˌdeɪtɪd / (AmE), / kənˈsɒlɪdeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of things) joined together into a single whole; unified or made stronger by combining separate parts.
joined or unified into a whole

Examples

After several mergers, the company became a consolidated industry leader.
The schools were consolidated to improve efficiency and save resources.
This report presents the consolidated results from all regional offices.

Synset Examples

the amalgamated colleges constituted a university
a consolidated school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amalgamated - joined together to form one organization or structure
  • Synonym: unified - made into a single unit
  • Antonym: fragmented - broken into parts or not unified

Etymology

From Latin 'consolidatus', past participle of 'consolidare' (to make firm or solid).
