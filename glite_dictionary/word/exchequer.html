exchequer

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˈstʃɛkər / (AmE), / ɪksˈtʃɛkə / (BrE)
the funds or finances of a government, institution, or individual
government or institutional funds

Examples

The nation's exchequer was strained by the cost of the war.
He donated a substantial amount from his personal exchequer to the charity.
The university's exchequer grew thanks to successful fundraising efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treasury - the funds or revenue of a government, corporation, or institution

Etymology

From Old French 'eschequier', from Medieval Latin 'scaccarium' (chessboard), originally referring to the checkered cloth on which accounts were kept.
