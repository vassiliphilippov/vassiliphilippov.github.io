Brassia lawrenceana

1. NOUN

/ ˌbræsiə lɔːˈrɛnsianə / (AmE), / ˌbrasɪə lɔːˈrɛnsɪanə / (BrE)
A South American orchid species with spiderlike pale-yellow to pale-green flowers.
a South American orchid with distinctive flowers

Examples

The Brassia lawrenceana thrives in humid, tropical forests of South America.
Many orchid enthusiasts appreciate the unique spiderlike blooms of Brassia lawrenceana.
Botanists recently discovered a new population of Brassia lawrenceana in the Amazon.

Etymology

Genus named after British botanist William Brass; species named in honor of Sir William Lawrence.
