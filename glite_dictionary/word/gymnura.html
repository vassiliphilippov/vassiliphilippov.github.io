Gymnura

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˈnjuːrə / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈnjʊərə / (BrE)
A genus of rays in the family Gymnuridae, commonly known as butterfly rays, found in warm coastal waters.
genus of butterfly rays

Examples

The Gymnura are noted for their wide, wing-like pectoral fins resembling butterfly wings.
A species of Gymnura was recently discovered off the coast of Australia.
Scientists study the movement patterns of Gymnura to better understand their habitat preferences.

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) and 'oura' (tail).

2. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˈnjʊrə / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈnjʊərə / (BrE)
A genus of large rays known as butterfly rays, found in warm coastal waters and defined by their broad, flat bodies and wing-like pectoral fins.
a genus of butterfly rays

Examples

The Gymnura are characterized by their exceptionally broad bodies and short tails.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Gymnura genus off the coast of Brazil.
The aquarium installed a large tank specifically to house its collection of Gymnura specimens.

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' meaning 'naked' and 'oura' meaning 'tail', referring to the appearance of the tail.
