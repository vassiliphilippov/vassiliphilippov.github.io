rhodium

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊdiəm / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdiəm / (BrE)
a white, hard metallic chemical element of the platinum group, found in platinum ores and used in alloys with platinum and as a catalyst; symbol: Rh
metallic chemical element (Rh)

Examples

The jeweler added rhodium to the ring to improve its durability and shine.
Catalytic converters often contain small amounts of rhodium for its catalytic properties.
Rhodium is one of the rarest and most valuable precious metals in the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platinum - a precious, silvery-white metallic element of the platinum group

Etymology

From Greek 'rhodon' (rose), referring to the rose-colored salts of the element.
