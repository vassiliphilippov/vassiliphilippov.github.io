LTM

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛl ˌti ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌɛl ˌtiː ˈɛm / (BrE)
abbreviation for 'long-term memory'; your general store of remembered information that can be retained for a long period
abbreviation for long-term memory

Examples

The psychologist explained that LTM is responsible for storing facts learned throughout life.
Damage to LTM can make it difficult to recall distant childhood events.
Researchers are studying how information is transferred from short-term memory to LTM.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long-term memory - the system for permanently storing, managing, and retrieving information for later use

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'long-term memory'.
