St. Gregory of Nazianzen

1. PROPN

/ sənt ˈɡrɛɡəri əv ˌnæziˈænzən / (AmE), / sənt ˈɡrɛɡəri əv ˌnæziˈænzən / (BrE)
A 4th-century Christian theologian and Church Father, known for defending orthodox doctrine and designated as a Doctor of the Church.
4th-century theologian and Church Father

Examples

St. Gregory of Nazianzen is widely regarded as one of the most influential theologians of the early Christian church.
The sermons of St. Gregory of Nazianzen are still studied in seminaries around the world today.
Many scholars praise St. Gregory of Nazianzen for his eloquence and defense of Christian doctrine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St. Gregory of Nazianzus - Alternative form; refers to the same Church Father

Etymology

Nazianzen refers to Nazianzus, an ancient town in Cappadocia (modern-day Turkey), Gregory's place of birth.
