moss

1. NOUN

/ mɑs / (AmE), / mɒs / (BrE)
A small, green, non-flowering plant with leafy stems that typically grows in dense mats or clumps in damp or shady locations.
tiny, flowerless green plant

Examples

The stone walls were covered in a thick layer of moss.
After the rain, the moss in the forest glistened with moisture.
She knelt down to examine the delicate moss growing beneath the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liverwort - another small, non-flowering plant related to mosses

Etymology

Old English *mos* (bog, marsh), related to German *Moos* (moss).
