corbel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrbəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːbəl / (BrE)
an architectural bracket of brick or stone projecting from a wall to support a structure above it, often triangular in shape
projecting architectural bracket

Examples

The architect designed an elaborate balcony supported by a single stone corbel.
Each of the old church's windows was accented by a decorative corbel beneath the arch.
Masons skillfully carved the corbel to help bear the weight of the upper floor.

Etymology

From Old French *corbeau* (crow, bracket), influenced by the resemblance of the bracket to a crow's beak; ultimately from Latin *corvus* (crow).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɔrbəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːbəl / (BrE)
to support or furnish with a corbel, especially in architecture; to project (a structure or part) by means of a corbel.
to support or furnish with a corbel

Examples

The architect decided to corbel the balcony, giving it an elegant, projecting look.
Stone blocks were carefully corbelled to create the decorative ledge above the doorway.
The ancient temple's roof was corbelled out over the entrance for additional shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to bear the weight of something; to hold up

Etymology

From Old French 'corbel' (little raven, bracket), from Late Latin 'corvellus', diminutive of 'corvus' (raven), referencing the shape.
