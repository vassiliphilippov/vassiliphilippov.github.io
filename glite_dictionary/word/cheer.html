cheer

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪr / (AmE), / tʃɪə / (BrE)
a feeling or quality of cheerfulness and the ability to make people feel less sad or gloomy
feeling or quality of cheerfulness

Examples

Her laughter brought a welcome sense of cheer to the family gathering.
The bright colors in the classroom added some cheer to the gloomy winter day.
He sent flowers to bring a little cheer to his friend in the hospital.

Synset Examples

flowers added a note of cheerfulness to the drab room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerfulness - the state of being noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Antonym: gloom - a state of depression or sadness

Etymology

From Old English 'chēre' (face, countenance, mood), from Old French 'chiere'.

2. VERB

/ tʃɪr / (AmE), / tʃɪə / (BrE)
to make someone feel happier or more cheerful
make someone feel happier

Examples

Her friends tried to cheer her after she lost the match.
A funny movie often helps to cheer people on a gloomy day.
We brought flowers to cheer him up while he was in the hospital.

Synset Examples

She tried to cheer up the disappointed child when he failed to win the spelling bee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encourage - give support or confidence to someone
  • Synonym: comfort - make someone feel less unhappy or worried

Etymology

From Old French 'chier' (face, mood), from Latin 'cara' (face).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00860989-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ tʃɪr / (AmE), / tʃɪə / (BrE)
a loud shout or cry expressing approval, encouragement, or enthusiasm
shout expressing approval or encouragement

Examples

A loud cheer erupted from the crowd when the team scored the winning goal.
The children let out a cheer as the parade passed by their school.
With a final cheer, the audience showed its appreciation for the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: applause - showing approval by clapping hands
  • Antonym: boo - an exclamation used to show disapproval

Etymology

From Old French *chere* (face, expression), later meaning mood or spirit.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06705317-n
Synset POS: n
