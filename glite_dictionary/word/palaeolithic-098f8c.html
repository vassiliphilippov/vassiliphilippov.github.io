palaeolithic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpeɪ.li.oʊˈlɪθ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpæl.i.əʊˈlɪθ.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to the early phase of the Stone Age, characterized by the use of stone tools by early humans
early Stone Age period

Examples

Archaeologists discovered palaeolithic tools buried in the cave.
The museum features a remarkable collection of palaeolithic artifacts.
These palaeolithic paintings reveal the early development of artistic expression.

Synset Examples

paleolithic artifacts

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mesolithic - relating to the middle period of the Stone Age
  • Degree Higher: neolithic - relating to the later part of the Stone Age

Etymology

From Greek palaios (ancient) + lithos (stone).
