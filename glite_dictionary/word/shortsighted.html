shortsighted

1. ADJ

/ ʃɔrtˌsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʃɔːtˌsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
unable to see objects clearly unless they are close; nearsighted
unable to see distant objects clearly

Examples

Without his glasses, Paul is extremely shortsighted and cannot read the board from his seat.
The optometrist told her she was shortsighted and needed corrective lenses.
Many shortsighted children find it difficult to see the teacher from the back of the classroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nearsighted - unable to see distant objects clearly
  • Antonym: farsighted - able to see distant objects clearly but not close ones
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02164641-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃɔrtˌsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtˌsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
Lacking foresight or careful consideration of the future; making decisions only with regard to immediate results.
lacking foresight or vision

Examples

The company's shortsighted strategy ignored the long-term risks involved.
Critics argued that the new law was a shortsighted solution to a complex problem.
Her shortsighted decision to cut research funding hindered innovation in the industry.

Synset Examples

a short view of the problem
shortsighted policies
shortsighted critics derided the plan
myopic thinking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myopic - lacking foresight or discernment; narrow-minded
  • Antonym: foresighted - having or showing awareness of and planning for the future
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01902113-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈʃɔrtˌsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtsʌɪtɪd / (BrE)
Lacking foresight or consideration for the future; not giving careful thought to possible consequences.
lacking foresight; not considering future consequences

Examples

It would be shortsighted to ignore the possible effects of climate change.
The company's shortsighted decision to cut research funding hurt its long-term prospects.
Many criticized the new law as shortsighted and likely to cause future problems.

Synset Examples

ill-considered actions often result in disaster
an ill-judged attempt

Related Terms

  • Antonym: foresighted - having foresight; considering the long-term future
  • Synonym: imprudent - not showing care for the consequences of an action

Etymology

Formed from 'short-' (limited) + 'sighted' (having sight); originally referred to vision, later metaphorically to lack of foresight.
