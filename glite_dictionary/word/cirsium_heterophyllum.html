Cirsium heterophyllum

1. NOUN

/ sɜːrˈsaɪ.əm ˌhɛt.əˈrɑː.fɪ.ləm / (AmE), / sɜːˈsɪ.əm ˌhɛt.əˈrɒ.fɪ.ləm / (BrE)
A perennial thistle native to northern Europe, characterized by lance-shaped basal leaves and typically solitary reddish-purple flower heads.
European thistle with reddish-purple flowers

Examples

The Cirsium heterophyllum blooms in midsummer along the edges of moist meadows.
Researchers studied the distribution of Cirsium heterophyllum in Scotland's highlands.
Gardeners sometimes plant Cirsium heterophyllum as an ornamental species for its attractive flowers.

Etymology

From Latin: genus Cirsium from Greek 'kirsion' (thistle); heterophyllum meaning 'with different leaves'.
