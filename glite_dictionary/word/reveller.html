reveller

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛvələr / (AmE), / ˈrɛvələ / (BrE)
a person who takes part in loud or lively celebrations, especially at a party or festival
person celebrating noisily

Examples

As midnight approached, the revellers filled the streets, cheering and dancing in celebration.
Police kept a watchful eye as hundreds of revellers gathered for the festival downtown.
The club was packed with revellers enjoying the music and flashing lights.

Synset Examples

the clubs attract revelers as young as thirteen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebrant - a person who celebrates or takes part in a celebration
  • Synonym: partygoer - a person who attends parties
  • Antonym: mourner - a person who is sad, especially at a funeral; opposite context of reveller

Etymology

From Old French *reveler* (to rebel, make merry), from Latin *rebellare* (to rebel).
