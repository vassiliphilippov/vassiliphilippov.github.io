Ming

1. PROPN

/ mɪŋ / (AmE), / mɪŋ / (BrE)
the imperial dynasty that ruled China from 1368 to 1644, noted for its cultural achievements and expansion
Chinese imperial dynasty, 1368–1644

Examples

The palace in Beijing was constructed during the Ming dynasty.
Many art objects from the Ming period are considered masterpieces.
Historians often study the Ming era for its significant developments in literature and architecture.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Qing - the imperial dynasty of China that followed the Ming, 1644–1912

Etymology

From Chinese 明 (Míng), meaning 'bright' or 'brilliant', the name of the dynasty.
