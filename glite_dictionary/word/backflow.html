backflow

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbæk.fləʊ / (BrE)
a flow of liquid or gas that moves in the reverse direction, returning toward its source, often unintentionally
reverse flow back to source

Examples

After heavy rain, backflow from the sewer lines caused flooding in the basement.
The valve is designed to prevent backflow into the main water supply.
Engineers monitor pipes closely to avoid contamination from backflow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflux - the backward flow of a liquid, typically in medical or technical contexts
  • Antonym: outflow - a flow outward from a source (opposite of backflow)

Etymology

Formed from 'back' + 'flow', first used in technical contexts in the 20th century.
