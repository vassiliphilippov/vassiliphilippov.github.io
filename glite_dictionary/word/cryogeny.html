cryogeny

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˈɑdʒəni / (AmE), / kraɪˈɒdʒəni / (BrE)
the branch of physics that studies phenomena occurring at very low temperatures
study of phenomena at very low temperatures

Examples

The laboratory specializes in cryogeny, exploring materials at temperatures near absolute zero.
Recent advances in cryogeny have enabled the development of more efficient superconductors.
He is pursuing a doctorate in cryogeny, focusing on cooling techniques for quantum computers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryogenics - the study of the production and effects of very low temperatures

Etymology

From Greek kryos (frost, cold) + -geny (origin, creation).
