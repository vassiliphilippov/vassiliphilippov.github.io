allspice

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔlˌspaɪs / (AmE), / ˈɔːlˌspaɪs / (BrE)
The dried, ground berries of a tropical West Indian tree, used as a spice and having a flavor resembling a mixture of cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves.
spice similar to cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves

Examples

I added a pinch of allspice to the cake batter for extra warmth.
The recipe calls for ground allspice as one of the main ingredients.
Jamaican cuisine often features allspice in its traditional dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pimento - another name for allspice, the dried berries used as a spice

Etymology

Named for its aroma, which resembles a combination of several spices; first used in the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔlˌspaɪs / (AmE), / ˈɔːlˌspaɪs / (BrE)
An aromatic tree native to the West Indies (Pimenta dioica) that produces berries used as a spice.
aromatic tree producing allspice berries

Examples

The allspice tree grows abundantly in Jamaica’s warm, humid climate.
Farmers carefully harvest the berries of the allspice for use in cooking and baking.
Botanists study the allspice for its unique aromatic qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allspice - the dried berry or spice derived from the allspice tree

Etymology

Named for its flavor, which resembles a blend of cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves.
