mire

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪə / (BrE)
Deep, soft, wet mud, especially in a bog or swamp, that is difficult or unpleasant to walk through.
deep, soft mud or slush

Examples

Horses can easily get trapped in the thick mire of the marshlands.
After the rain, the path turned into a treacherous mire that slowed our progress.
The car's wheels spun helplessly in the mire after we tried to drive through the flooded field.

Synset Examples

they waded through the slop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mud - soft, wet earth
  • Synonym: quagmire - soft, boggy area; predicament

Etymology

From Old French *mirer* (to look at oneself in a mirror/to be stuck in the mud), of Germanic origin.
