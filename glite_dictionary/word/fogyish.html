fogyish

1. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊɡi.ɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊɡi.ɪʃ / (BrE)
Old-fashioned in style, ideas, or behavior, often used with mild disapproval.
old-fashioned; out of date

Examples

His fogyish opinions about technology made him seem out of touch with young people.
She rejected the fogyish traditions that her grandparents tried to impose.
The committee's fogyish rules hindered any real progress or innovation.

Synset Examples

moss-grown ideas about family life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: old-fashioned - out of date or not in style
  • Antonym: modern - of or relating to the present or recent times

Etymology

From 'fogy' (old-fashioned person) + '-ish' (adjectival suffix).
