psychogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkəˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪkə(ʊ)ˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
the origin and development of psychological processes or aspects of the mind
origin and development of the mind

Examples

Freud’s theories often focused on the psychogenesis of neuroses.
Researchers debate the psychogenesis of certain childhood behavioral disorders.
The study examines the psychogenesis of emotions in early development.

Etymology

From Greek 'psyche' (mind, soul) and 'genesis' (origin, creation).

2. NOUN

/ saɪˌkɑː.dʒəˈnɛsɪs / (AmE), / saɪˌkɒ.dʒəˈnɛsɪs / (BrE)
the development of a disorder or condition in an individual as a result of psychological, rather than physiological, causes
development of disorder from psychological causes
medical

Examples

Some researchers argue that the patient's symptoms are a result of psychogenesis rather than any physical ailment.
The theory of psychogenesis helps explain how traumatic events can lead to mental disorders.
Understanding the process of psychogenesis is crucial for treating many types of emotional disturbances.

Etymology

From Greek 'psyche' (soul, mind) + 'genesis' (origin, creation).
