cling

1. VERB

/ klɪŋ / (AmE), / klɪŋ / (BrE)
to remain emotionally or intellectually attached, often despite changes or contrary evidence
remain emotionally/intellectually attached

Examples

Even after many years, he continues to cling to his childhood beliefs.
People often cling to hope in difficult situations.
She clung to the idea of returning home, despite all obstacles.

Synset Examples

He clings to the idea that she might still love him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adhere - to stick fast to something; to remain attached
  • Antonym: to detach - to separate or remove (especially emotionally or intellectually)

Etymology

From Old English *clingan* (to adhere, shrivel up), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ klɪŋ / (AmE), / klɪŋ / (BrE)
A type of fruit, especially a peach, whose flesh sticks firmly to the pit and does not separate easily.
peach or fruit with flesh stuck to the pit

Examples

The jam was made from ripe cling peaches picked that morning.
Some people prefer cling varieties for canning because of their sweet, juicy texture.
This orchard grows both cling and freestone peaches for different markets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freestone - a fruit, especially a peach, whose flesh separates easily from the pit
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07754200-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ klɪŋ / (AmE), / klɪŋ / (BrE)
to hold on tightly or refuse to let go, especially for support or protection
hold on tightly

Examples

The frightened kitten clung to its owner's sweater during the thunderstorm.
She clings to her beliefs, even when others disagree.
He clung to the edge of the rock, afraid to let go.

Synset Examples

hang on to your father's hands
The child clung to his mother's apron

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grip - to hold something tightly
  • Antonym: release - to let go of something held

Etymology

From Old English 'clingan' (to adhere, shrink), related to Dutch 'klingen'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01218895-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ klɪŋ / (AmE), / klɪŋ / (BrE)
to stick closely to someone or something, or to hold together and resist separation
stick closely to or hold together

Examples

The wet shirt clung to his back after the rainstorm.
Tiny bits of paper cling to the adhesive tape.
Even after several washes, the smell clings to the fabric.

Synset Examples

The dress clings to her body
The label stuck to the box
The sushi rice grains cohere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stick - to adhere to a surface or object
  • Synonym: to adhere - to stick fast to a surface or substance
  • Antonym: to detach - to separate or remove from something else

Etymology

From Old English 'clingan' (to adhere or shrivel up), related to Middle Dutch 'klingen' (to clink, stick).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
