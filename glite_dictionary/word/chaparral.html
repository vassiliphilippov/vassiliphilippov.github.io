chaparral

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃæpəˈræl / (AmE), / ˌtʃapəˈral / (BrE)
dense, often impenetrable vegetation consisting of stunted trees and shrubs, typically found in dry regions such as California and the Mediterranean.
dense growth of shrubs and small trees

Examples

Wildfires are a natural part of the chaparral ecosystem in southern California.
The hikers made their way through thick chaparral that covered most of the hillside.
Many animals have adapted to living in the harsh, dry chaparral environment.

Etymology

From Spanish 'chaparral', from 'chaparro' (dwarf evergreen oak).
