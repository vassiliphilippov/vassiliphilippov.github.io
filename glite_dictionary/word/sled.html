sled

1. VERB

/ slɛd / (AmE), / slɛd / (BrE)
to ride or travel on a sled, especially over snow or ice
ride on a sled

Examples

The children love to sled down the big hill after a fresh snowfall.
We sledded for hours until it got dark.
She is planning to sled with her friends this weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toboggan - to ride on a long, narrow sled without runners
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01943033-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ slɛd / (AmE), / slɛd / (BrE)
a vehicle with runners, usually pulled by animals or gravity, used for travel over snow
vehicle for travel over snow

Examples

The children rode their sled down the snowy hill all afternoon.
In Alaska, dog teams pull a sled across the frozen landscape.
He loaded the supplies onto the sled before setting off into the woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sledge - a vehicle for traveling over snow, often larger and heavier than a sled
  • Synonym: toboggan - a long, light, narrow sled without runners, used for sliding over snow or ice

Etymology

From Middle English sledde, from Old Dutch *sledde (sliding vehicle).
