Enkaid

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.keɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛn.keɪd / (BrE)
A former trade name for an antiarrhythmic drug (encainide) used to treat dangerous heart rhythm disorders, now discontinued due to safety concerns.
antiarrhythmic drug, trade name (discontinued)
medical

Examples

The use of Enkaid was discontinued after studies revealed increased risks for certain patients.
Before its withdrawal, doctors sometimes prescribed Enkaid for serious arrhythmias.
Patients treated with Enkaid needed close monitoring due to possible side effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encainide - the generic drug formerly sold as Enkaid; antiarrhythmic medication
