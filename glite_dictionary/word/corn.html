2. NOUN

/ kɔrn / (AmE), / kɔːn / (BrE)
a tall cereal plant (Zea mays) with large yellow kernels, commonly grown as a staple crop, especially in the Americas
a major cereal crop; maize
american

Examples

Farmers planted corn across hundreds of acres this spring.
Many traditional Mexican dishes use corn as a main ingredient.
The corn stood tall and golden in the late summer sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maize - the cereal plant known in American English as 'corn'

Etymology

From Old English 'corn' (grain, seed), in American English referring specifically to maize (Zea mays) introduced from the Americas.
