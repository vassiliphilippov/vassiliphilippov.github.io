elution

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈluʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the process of extracting one substance from another by washing it with a solvent, especially to remove adsorbed material from an adsorbent
process of extracting with a solvent
medical

Examples

The scientist used elution to separate the desired compound from the column.
Proper elution is essential for accurate results in chromatography.
During elution, a solvent is passed through the material to collect the targeted substance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extraction - the process of removing something by force or effort, especially by chemical means

Etymology

From Latin 'eluere' meaning 'to wash out'.
