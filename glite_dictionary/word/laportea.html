Laportea

1. NOUN

/ ləˈpɔrti.ə / (AmE), / ləˈpɔːti.ə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical stinging herbs or trees belonging to the nettle family, known for their irritating hairs
genus of tropical stinging nettles

Examples

Many species of Laportea have stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation on contact.
Researchers collected samples of Laportea from the tropical rain forests of Southeast Asia.
The gardener warned us to avoid touching the Laportea growing near the path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nettle - any plant of the genus Urtica or related genera, known for stinging hairs

Etymology

Named after French botanist François Louis de La Porte, Count of Castelnau.

2. NOUN

/ ləˈpɔrti.ə / (AmE), / ləˈpɔːti.ə / (BrE)
a genus of mostly tropical herbs or shrubs in the nettle family, many of which have stinging hairs
a genus of tropical stinging nettles

Examples

Several species of Laportea are known for their irritating stinging hairs.
The tropical rainforests are home to many types of Laportea plants.
Botanists have identified a new Laportea species in Central America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nettle - a plant with stinging hairs, related to Laportea

Etymology

Named after French naturalist François Louis Laporte, comte de Castelnau.
