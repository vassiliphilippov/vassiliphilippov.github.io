Cedrela

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈdriːlə / (AmE), / sɪˈdriːlə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American trees in the mahogany family, valued for their timber
a genus of tropical American trees

Examples

The wood of Cedrela is highly prized for its durability and fragrance.
In South America, Cedrela species are commonly used in furniture making.
Researchers discovered a previously undocumented Cedrela tree in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

From Latin, the name for a kind of cedar, from Ancient Greek kedros (cedar).

2. NOUN

/ sɛˈdrɛlə / (AmE), / sɪˈdrɛlə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American trees in the mahogany family, known for their fragrant wood and use in timber
a genus of tropical American trees

Examples

The wood of Cedrela trees is highly valued for making furniture and musical instruments.
Some species within the genus Cedrela are cultivated in plantations for sustainable timber production.
Botanists have identified new hybrids in the Cedrela genus across its native Central and South American range.

Etymology

From Latin *cedrela*, ultimately from Greek *kedros* (cedar), referring to the cedar-like scent of the wood.
