maximisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.sɪ.məˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmæk.sɪ.maɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something as great in amount, size, or importance as possible
the act of making something as large as possible

Examples

The maximisation of profits is the primary goal of most businesses.
Efficient use of resources is crucial for the maximisation of production output.
Environmental concerns must be balanced with the maximisation of economic growth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minimisation - the act of making something as small as possible
  • Synonym: optimization - the process of making something as functional or effective as possible
  • Synonym: maximization - US spelling of 'maximisation'

Etymology

From Latin 'maximus' (greatest) via French and English 'maximise' + '-ation'.
