periwinkle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛriˌwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪˌwɪŋkl̩ / (BrE)
a small edible marine snail with a spiral shell, commonly found on rocky shores; often eaten steamed or baked
small edible marine snail

Examples

At low tide, children collected periwinkles from the rocks to bring home for dinner.
The restaurant served a dish of steamed periwinkles in a garlic-butter sauce.
She was surprised by how delicate and flavorful the periwinkles tasted after being baked with herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winkle - another small edible marine snail, sometimes used as a synonym

Etymology

From Latin 'periwinkle', via Old English 'perwince', ultimately of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛɹiˌwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛɹɪˌwɪŋkəl / (BrE)
a trailing evergreen plant with blue or purple flowers, of the genus Vinca, often grown as ground cover and some species are poisonous
evergreen plant with blue flowers

Examples

The periwinkle spread quickly, covering the garden with its blue blossoms.
Some species of periwinkle are considered invasive in certain regions.
Children are often warned not to eat the leaves of the periwinkle plant because they are poisonous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vinca - genus of periwinkle plants

Etymology

From Old English 'perwince', from Latin 'vinca', meaning 'to bind'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛriˌwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪˌwɪŋkl / (BrE)
a woody plant of the genus Vinca, native to the Old World and often cultivated, with large pinkish to red flowers
a woody plant with pinkish-red flowers (Vinca)

Examples

The periwinkle in her garden blooms with vibrant red flowers every spring.
He planted a row of periwinkles along the old stone path to add color to the yard.
Many gardeners favor periwinkle for its attractive foliage and easy maintenance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vinca - another name for the periwinkle plant, especially in botanical contexts

Etymology

From Old English *perwince*, from Latin *pervinca* (periwinkle).

4. NOUN

/ ˈper.iˌwɪŋ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛr.iˌwɪŋ.kəl / (BrE)
a small edible sea snail with a spiral shell, commonly found on rocky shores
small edible marine snail

Examples

We collected periwinkles from the rocks at low tide and cooked them for dinner.
Many coastal recipes include periwinkle as a key ingredient in seafood stews.
Children often hunt for periwinkles in tidal pools during summer holidays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winkle - another name for the edible sea snail, especially in British English

Etymology

Alteration of Middle English perwinke (the plant), from Old English perwince, probably by association with the snail's shell; precise connection unclear.
