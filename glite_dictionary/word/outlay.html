outlay

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊt.leɪ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.leɪ / (BrE)
an amount of money spent on something; expenditure
money spent; expenditure

Examples

The initial outlay for the new equipment was higher than anticipated.
We considered the monthly outlay before signing the lease.
A large financial outlay is required to start a new business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expenditure - the act of spending money
  • Antonym: income - money received, especially on a regular basis

Etymology

From out + lay, originally meaning 'to lay out (money)'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.leɪ / (BrE)
an amount of money spent on something; the act of spending or disbursing money
the spending of money

Examples

The initial outlay for the project was higher than expected.
With a small outlay, you can upgrade your computer's performance.
They recovered their outlay within the first year of operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expenditure - the act of spending money; an amount spent
  • Antonym: income - money received, especially on a regular basis, for work or through investments

Etymology

From the verb 'outlay', from 'out-' + 'lay', meaning to lay out (spend) money.

Synset POS: n
