Heuchera sanguinea

1. NOUN

/ ˌhjuːkɚə sæŋˈɡwiːniə / (AmE), / ˌhjuːkərə sæŋˈɡwiːniːə / (BrE)
A species of perennial plant native to the western United States, noted for its bright red, feathery spike-shaped flowers and commonly grown as an ornamental.
ornamental plant with red flowers

Examples

Many gardeners plant Heuchera sanguinea in shaded borders for a splash of red color.
The feathery flower spikes of Heuchera sanguinea attract hummingbirds in late spring.
A pot of Heuchera sanguinea brightened the patio with its vivid blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral bells - Common name for Heuchera sanguinea

Etymology

Genus name from German physician Johann Heinrich von Heucher; 'sanguinea' from Latin meaning 'bloody' or 'blood-red', referring to the flower color.
