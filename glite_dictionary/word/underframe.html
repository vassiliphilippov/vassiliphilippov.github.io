underframe

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndərˌfreɪm / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌfreɪm / (BrE)
the internal supporting framework of a vehicle, machine, or structure, which gives it strength and shape
internal supporting framework

Examples

The train’s underframe was reinforced to support heavier cargo.
After the accident, the car’s underframe had to be inspected for damage.
Restoration began with repairing the original wooden underframe of the old bridge.

Synset Examples

the building has a steel skeleton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeleton - the supporting framework of a structure or organism
  • Antonym: superstructure - the part of a structure above the underframe or base

Etymology

From 'under-' (beneath) + 'frame'; referring to the supporting frame beneath an object.
