tong ho

1. NOUN

/ tɔŋ hoʊ / (AmE), / tɒŋ həʊ / (BrE)
an East Asian leafy vegetable, also known as chrysanthemum greens, grown for its tender, edible leaves used in cooking
East Asian leafy vegetable used in cooking

Examples

She added fresh tong ho to the hotpot for extra flavor and nutrition.
You can find tong ho in most Asian supermarkets during the spring season.
The soup was garnished with chopped tong ho leaves for a delicious herbal aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chrysanthemum greens - another name for tong ho, a leafy vegetable used in Asian cuisine

Etymology

From Cantonese 唐苣 (tong4 ho4), a common name for chrysanthemum greens.
