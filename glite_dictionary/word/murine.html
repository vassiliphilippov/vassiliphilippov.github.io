murine

1. ADJ

/ ˈmjʊr.iːn / (AmE), / ˈmjʊə.riːn / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of mice, rats, or members of the family Muridae.
relating to mice or rats
medical

Examples

The laboratory used murine models to study the disease progression.
Symptoms of the murine virus were observed in both wild and captive populations.
Researchers developed a murine antibody for experimental cancer treatments.

Synset Examples

a murine plague

Etymology

From Latin 'mus, muris' (mouse) with scientific suffix '-ine'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmjʊr.iːn / (AmE), / ˈmjʊə.riːn / (BrE)
A rodent that belongs to the family Muridae, which includes mice, rats, and related species.
rodent of the family Muridae
medical

Examples

The laboratory was infested with murine species that compromised the experiment.
Scientists studied the immune response in various murine models.
Zoologists discovered a new murine on the remote island.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rodent - a mammal of the order Rodentia

Etymology

From Latin *mus* (mouse), via New Latin *murina* (pertaining to mice).
