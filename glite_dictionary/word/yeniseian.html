Yeniseian

1. NOUN

/ ˌjɛnɪˈsiːən / (AmE), / ˌjɛnɪˈsiːən / (BrE)
a member of a small family of languages formerly spoken along the Yenisei River in Siberia, or any one of these languages
language family of Siberia

Examples

The Yeniseian languages are now considered nearly extinct, with only a few speakers remaining.
Linguists study the Yeniseian family to trace connections between ancient peoples of Siberia.
He specialized in the grammar and history of Yeniseian languages for his doctorate.

Etymology

From Yenisei, the name of a river in Siberia, combined with the English suffix -an.

2. NOUN

/ jɛˈniːsiən / (AmE), / jɛˈniːsiən / (BrE)
a member of one of the indigenous groups living in the Yenisei river valley in western Siberia
member of Yenisei river valley peoples

Examples

The Yeniseian people have a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in their traditional folklore.
Archaeologists have uncovered ancient artifacts belonging to the Yeniseian tribes along the riverbanks.
Linguists study the Yeniseian languages to learn more about the region's early history.

Etymology

From Yenisei (a major river in Siberia) + -an (denoting belonging or relation).
