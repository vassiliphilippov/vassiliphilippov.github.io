muskellunge

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskəˌlʌndʒ / (AmE), / ˌmʌskəˈlʌndʒ / (BrE)
A large predatory freshwater fish native to North America, valued as a game fish; also, its flesh used as food.
large North American game fish

Examples

He caught a massive muskellunge while fishing in the lake.
The muskellunge is prized by anglers for its size and strength.
Locals enjoy grilling the tender flesh of the muskellunge during summer barbecues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pike - large predatory freshwater fish

Etymology

Adapted from Ojibwa *maashkinoozhe* (ugly pike).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskəˌlʌndʒ / (AmE), / ˈmʌskəˌlʌndʒ / (BrE)
a large predatory freshwater fish of North America, popular as a game fish and known for its elongated body and sharp teeth
large North American freshwater game fish

Examples

The angler was thrilled to catch a 50-inch muskellunge on his trip to Wisconsin.
Muskellunge are prized by sport fishers for their size and elusive nature.
Lake St. Clair is famous for its healthy population of muskellunge each summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pike - large predatory freshwater fish
  • Degree Lower: walleye - North American freshwater fish, related sport fish

Etymology

Of Algonquian origin; from Ojibwe *maashkinoozhe* (ugly pike).
