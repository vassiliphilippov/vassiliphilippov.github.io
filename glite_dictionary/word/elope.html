to elope

1. VERB

/ ɪˈloʊp / (AmE), / ɪˈləʊp / (BrE)
to run away secretly in order to get married, especially without parental consent
run away secretly to get married

Examples

The young couple decided to elope rather than have a big wedding.
Rumor has it that Sarah and Tom plan to elope next weekend.
They shocked their families when they eloped to Paris and got married.

Synset Examples

The young couple eloped and got married in Las Vegas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to marry - to become someone's husband or wife

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *ontlopen* (to run away), from *ont-* (away) + *lopen* (to run).
