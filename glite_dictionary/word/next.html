1. ADJ

/ nɛkst / (AmE), / nɛkst / (BrE)
(of an elected officer) having been elected but not yet serving in office.
elected but not yet serving (official)

Examples

The next president will take office in January.
Reporters asked the next mayor about his plans for the city.
Everyone is speculating about the policies of the next governor.

Synset Examples

our next president

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incumbent - currently holding office

Etymology

From Old English 'nexte', comparative of 'neah' (near).

2. ADJ

/ nɛkst / (AmE), / nɛkst / (BrE)
coming immediately after the present one or the one just mentioned in time, order, or place.
immediately following in time or order

Examples

The next train will arrive in five minutes.
Please move to the next page when you are finished reading.
Who's next in line at the counter?

Synset Examples

next in line
the next president
the next item on the list

Related Terms

  • Antonym: previous - coming before in time or order

Etymology

From Old English 'nēhst', superlative of 'nēah' (near), meaning 'nearest'.

4. ADJ

/ nɛkst / (AmE), / nɛkst / (BrE)
(of a day of the week) the day occurring after the present instance of that day; the following (for example, 'next Saturday' means the Saturday after this one).
the following day of the week after the current one

Examples

I'm busy this Friday, but I'm free next Friday.
The meeting is scheduled for next Tuesday at 10 a.m.
We always visit our grandparents next Sunday after church.

Synset Examples

not this Saturday, next Saturday
on Tuesday next

Related Terms

  • Antonym: this - referring to the current or upcoming day in a week
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00129027-s
Synset POS: s
