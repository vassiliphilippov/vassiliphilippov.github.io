regency

1. NOUN

/ ˈriː.dʒən.si / (AmE), / ˈriː.dʒən.si / (BrE)
The period of time during which a regent rules in place of a monarch who is unable to govern, especially due to minority, absence, or incapacity.
period governed by a regent

Examples

The country flourished during the regency as reforms were implemented by the council.
Throughout the regency, political power was exercised by the prince's uncle.
After the king fell ill, a regency was declared until his son came of age.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *regentia* (office of a ruler), from Latin *regens* (ruling).

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːdʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒənsi / (BrE)
the office, period, or authority of a regent; the position of ruling on behalf of a monarch who is unable to rule personally
office or period of a regent

Examples

During the king's illness, a regency was established to govern the country.
His reforms were implemented during the regency following the queen's abdication.
The regency lasted until the young prince reached adulthood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regent - a person appointed to rule in place of a monarch

Etymology

From Medieval Latin regentia (government by a regent) from Latin regens (ruling).
