pitch-perfect

1. ADJ

/ pɪtʃˈpɜrfɪkt / (AmE), / pɪtʃˈpɜːfɪkt / (BrE)
exactly accurate or flawless, especially in tone, performance, or execution
exactly accurate; flawless

Examples

Her pitch-perfect imitation of the singer wowed the judges.
The director praised the cast for their pitch-perfect timing in the comedy scenes.
His response to the criticism was pitch-perfect, addressing every concern calmly and clearly.

Synset Examples

He delivered a pitch-perfect presentation that impressed everyone.
Her performance was pitch-perfect, earning her a standing ovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfect - completely free from faults or defects; as good as possible
  • Antonym: imperfect - not perfect; having faults or defects

Etymology

From 'pitch', meaning the quality of a musical note, plus 'perfect', referring to being without flaws.
