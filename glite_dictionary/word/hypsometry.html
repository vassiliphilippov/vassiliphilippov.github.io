hypsometry

1. NOUN

/ hɪpˈsɑːmətri / (AmE), / hɪpˈsɒmɪtri / (BrE)
the measurement of the elevation of land above sea level
measurement of land elevation above sea level

Examples

Modern hypsometry relies on satellite data to accurately measure the planet’s surface elevations.
He published a paper detailing the hypsometry of the newly mapped mountain range.
Advances in hypsometry have improved our understanding of global topography.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: altimetry - measurement of altitude, especially in the atmosphere or above ground

Etymology

From Greek hupsos (height) + -metry (measurement).
