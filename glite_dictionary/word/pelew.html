Pelew

1. PROPN

/ pəˈluː / (AmE), / pəˈluː / (BrE)
an old name for a chain of more than 200 islands in the western central Pacific Ocean, now known as Palau.
former name for the islands of Palau

Examples

The explorer wrote about his voyage to Pelew in the 18th century.
Many maps from that era label Palau as Pelew Islands.
Historical treaties referenced Pelew as a strategic point in the Pacific.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Palau - modern name for the island country formerly called Pelew

Etymology

From earlier English rendering of native or Spanish forms of the name (now Palau).
