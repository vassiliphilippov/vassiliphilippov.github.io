alpenstock

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.pənˌstɑk / (AmE), / ˈalpənstɒk / (BrE)
a sturdy wooden staff with a metal tip, used by mountain climbers for support and balance
mountain climber's pointed staff

Examples

The climber gripped his alpenstock firmly as he ascended the rocky slope.
Before the invention of the modern ice axe, mountaineers relied on the alpenstock for safety.
She used an alpenstock to steady herself on the steep mountain path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ice axe - pick-like mountaineering tool for cutting steps and support

Etymology

From German Alpenstock (Alpen 'Alps' + Stock 'stick, staff').
