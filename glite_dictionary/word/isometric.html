isometric

1. ADJ

/ ˌaɪ.səˈmɛ.trɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səʊˈmɛ.trɪk / (BrE)
Having equal dimensions or measurements; relating to or denoting a method or condition where measurements or dimensions remain equal.
having equal dimensions or measurements

Examples

The artist used an isometric projection to create the illusion of depth in the drawing.
In an isometric exercise, the muscles contract without changing length.
The crystals formed in an isometric shape, having all sides of equal length.

Etymology

From Greek isos (equal) + metron (measure).

2. ADJ

/ ˌaɪ.səˈmɛ.trɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səʊˈmɛ.trɪk / (BrE)
(of muscular contraction) involving increased tension without change in muscle length.
muscle contraction with no length change
medical

Examples

Holding a plank position is an example of an isometric exercise.
During an isometric contraction, the muscle does not visibly change length despite increased tension.
Physical therapists often include isometric movements to build strength without joint motion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: isotonic - muscular contraction in which tension remains the same while length changes

Etymology

From Greek isos (equal) + metron (measure).

3. ADJ

/ ˌaɪ.səˈmɛt.rɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səʊˈmɛt.rɪk / (BrE)
(of a crystal system) characterized by having three axes of equal length intersecting at right angles to each other.
having three equal axes at right angles (crystal system)

Examples

Many gemstones, such as garnet, crystallize in the isometric system.
The isometric crystal system is also called the cubic system due to its symmetry.
Fluorite displays an isometric crystal habit with symmetrical cubic faces.

Etymology

From Greek isos (equal) + metron (measure).

4. ADJ

/ ˌaɪ.səˈmɛ.trɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səʊˈmɛ.trɪk / (BrE)
(of figures, shapes, or spaces) having equal measurements or related by an isometry, especially preserving distances in geometry.
having equal measurements or preserving distances

Examples

The two triangles are isometric, as there is a transformation that preserves distances between them.
An isometric mapping ensures that the original and the image have identical lengths between corresponding points.
In geometry, a function is isometric if it preserves the distance between all pairs of points.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congruent - (of figures) having the same shape and size
  • Degree Lower: similar - having the same shape but not necessarily the same size

Etymology

From Greek isometrikos, from iso- (equal) + metron (measure).
