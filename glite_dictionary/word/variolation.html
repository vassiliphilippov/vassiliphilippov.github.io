variolation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛrioʊˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvəːrɪə(ʊ)ˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the obsolete practice of inoculating a person with material taken from a smallpox patient to induce immunity to smallpox
historical smallpox inoculation method
archaic medical

Examples

Before the development of vaccines, variolation was used in several countries to protect against smallpox.
The risks of variolation included the spread of other diseases and severe illness in some patients.
Historical records show that variolation significantly reduced the mortality rate from smallpox outbreaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaccination - the act of introducing a vaccine into the body to produce immunity

Etymology

From New Latin variola (smallpox) + -ation, referring to the disease and the act of treating it.
