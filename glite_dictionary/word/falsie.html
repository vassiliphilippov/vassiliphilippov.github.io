falsie

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔlzi / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlzi / (BrE)
a pad or other device worn inside a brassiere to give the appearance of fuller breasts
a pad worn in a bra for fullness
informal

Examples

Some women wear falsies to enhance their appearance in formal dresses.
She discreetly slipped a pair of falsies into her bra before the interview.
The store offers several types of falsies in different shapes and sizes.

Etymology

From 'false' + diminutive suffix '-ie', originally American slang, mid-20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔl.si / (AmE), / ˈfɔːl.si / (BrE)
an artificial eyelash used to enhance the appearance of natural lashes
artificial eyelash

Examples

She wore a pair of falsies to make her eyes look more dramatic for the party.
After losing one falsie, she quickly fixed her makeup before going on stage.
Many people use falsies for special occasions when they want extra-long lashes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyelash extension - a semi-permanent enhancement added to eyelashes
  • Degree Lower: mascara - cosmetic used to darken and thicken eyelashes

Etymology

From "false" + diminutive suffix "-ie", modeled after similar slang formations (mid-20th century).
