sphalerite

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfæləˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈsfaləraɪt / (BrE)
a mineral consisting largely of crystalline zinc sulfide, the chief ore of zinc
zinc ore mineral

Examples

The mining company extracted sphalerite to produce zinc for industrial use.
Under the microscope, sphalerite crystals are often brown or black in color.
Geologists identified a rich deposit of sphalerite in the mountainous region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zinc blende - another name for sphalerite, used especially in older literature

Etymology

From Greek sphaleros (deceptive), due to its variable appearance.
