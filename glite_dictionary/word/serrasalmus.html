Serrasalmus

1. NOUN

/ sɛrəˈsælməs / (AmE), / sɛrəˈsælməs / (BrE)
A genus of South American freshwater fish known as piranhas, characterized by their sharp teeth and aggressive predatory behavior.
a genus of piranhas

Examples

The Serrasalmus species are notorious for their powerful jaws and sharp teeth.
Scientists recently discovered a new type of Serrasalmus in the Amazon River.
Aquariums often avoid keeping Serrasalmus fish together due to their aggressive nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piranha - a freshwater fish known for sharp teeth and aggressive behavior

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Latin 'serra' (saw) and Greek 'salmo' (salmon).
