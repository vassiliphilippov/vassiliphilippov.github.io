to feminize

1. VERB

/ ˈfɛm.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɛm.ɪ.naɪz / (BrE)
to give (someone or something) more feminine qualities or characteristics, or to become more feminine
to make or become more feminine

Examples

The use of certain words has feminized the language over time.
Exposure to pollutants can feminize fish and other wildlife.
Some fashion designers aim to feminize traditional menswear.

Synset Examples

feminized language
feminized frogs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to masculinize - to give or become more masculine characteristics
  • Degree Higher: feminine - having qualities traditionally associated with women

Etymology

From Latin 'femina' (woman) + '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈfɛm.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɛm.ɪ.naɪz / (BrE)
to give a more feminine quality, appearance, or character to someone or something; to make more feminine
make more feminine

Examples

The new fashion trend aims to feminize traditional men's clothing styles.
Certain hormones can feminize physical characteristics as part of medical treatment.
The director wanted to feminize the role to offer a new perspective on the character.

Synset Examples

This hairdo feminizes the man

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to masculinize - to give a more masculine quality to someone or something
  • Synonym: to effeminize - to make or become effeminate

Etymology

From Latin femina (woman) + -ize (verb forming suffix).
