cornstarch

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnˌstɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnˌstɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a fine, white powder made from the starchy part of corn kernels, used in cooking to thicken sauces and soups
starch from corn, used as thickener

Examples

To make the sauce thicker, add a tablespoon of cornstarch mixed with water.
She used cornstarch to give the dessert a smoother texture.
If the soup is too thin, you can stir in some cornstarch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starch - a carbohydrate used to thicken foods
