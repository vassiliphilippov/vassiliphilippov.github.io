dredge

1. VERB

/ drɛdʒ / (AmE), / drɛdʒ / (BrE)
to remove mud, sand, or other material from the bottom of a river, harbor, or other area of water, typically using machinery
remove material from water's bottom

Examples

The crew used a barge to dredge the riverbed and deepen the channel.
Authorities plan to dredge the harbor next spring to allow larger ships to dock.
After years of neglect, it took weeks to dredge the accumulated silt from the canal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excavate - to dig out and remove earth, especially from the ground

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch *dregghe* (dragnet).

2. VERB

/ drɛdʒ / (AmE), / drɛdʒ / (BrE)
to search the bottom of a body of water for something valuable or lost, often using a scoop or dragnet
search underwater for valuables or lost items

Examples

They used a large net to dredge the riverbed for missing jewelry.
Divers were called in to dredge the lake for evidence after the accident.
Rescuers had to dredge the harbor floor to locate the sunken boat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drag - pull something with force, often along the ground or through water

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch 'dregghe' meaning dragnet or grappling hook.

3. NOUN

/ drɛdʒ / (AmE), / drɛdʒ / (BrE)
a large machine or vessel equipped with a power shovel used to remove sand, mud, or other material from the bottom of rivers, harbours, or other bodies of water.
machine for removing material from riverbeds or channels

Examples

The crew used a dredge to clear silt from the harbor entrance after the storm.
A massive dredge slowly scooped sand from the bottom of the river to keep shipping lanes deep.
When the channel began to fill with debris, engineers brought in a dredge to restore navigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excavator - a large machine for digging and removing earth

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch *dregghe* (dragnet for fishing).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ drɛdʒ / (AmE), / drɛdʒ / (BrE)
to lightly coat food with a dry ingredient such as flour or breadcrumbs before cooking
coat food with dry ingredient before cooking

Examples

Before frying, dredge the fish fillets in seasoned flour for a crispier texture.
The chef quickly dredged the cutlets in breadcrumbs before placing them in the pan.
You should always dredge the chicken in cornstarch to help the sauce stick better.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coat - to cover something with a layer of substance

Etymology
