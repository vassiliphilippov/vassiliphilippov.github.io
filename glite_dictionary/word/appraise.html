to appraise

1. VERB

/ əˈpreɪz / (AmE), / əˈpreɪz / (BrE)
to consider or assess something carefully and comprehensively
assess or consider carefully

Examples

She appraised the evidence thoroughly before giving her opinion.
The panel appraises each proposal based on its merit and feasibility.
Before making a decision, he appraised all possible outcomes.

Synset Examples

He appraised the situation carefully before acting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evaluate - to assess the value or quality of something
  • Synonym: estimate - to form an approximate judgment about the value of something
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something

Etymology

From Old French 'apriser', based on Latin 'appretiare' (to value).

2. VERB

/ əˈpreɪz / (AmE), / əˈpreɪz / (BrE)
to assess or estimate the value, quality, or significance of something
assess value or quality

Examples

We asked a specialist to appraise the value of the antique vase.
Before purchasing the house, she had an expert appraise its condition.
The company's assets are appraised each year for tax purposes.

Synset Examples

I will have the family jewels appraised by a professional
access all the factors when taking a risk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evaluate - form an idea of the amount, number, or value of
  • Synonym: to assess - evaluate or estimate the nature, ability, or quality of
  • Antonym: to depreciate - diminish in value over a period of time

Etymology

From Old French *apreisier* (to value), from Late Latin *appretiare* (to value, set a price).
