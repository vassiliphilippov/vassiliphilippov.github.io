Erysimum cheiri

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌrɪsɪməm ˈkɪri / (AmE), / ɛˌrɪsɪməm ˈkɪəri / (BrE)
A perennial plant of southern Europe with fragrant yellow or orange flowers, often growing on old walls or cliffs; also known as the wallflower.
a perennial plant, also called wallflower

Examples

The old stone wall was covered with blooming Erysimum cheiri in the spring.
Gardeners often plant Erysimum cheiri for its vibrant yellow and orange flowers.
You can smell the scent of Erysimum cheiri from several meters away when it is in full bloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallflower - common name for Erysimum cheiri, a fragrant garden plant

Etymology

From New Latin, Erysimum (genus name) + cheiri, from Greek kheirion (wallflower).
