aspergillosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæspərˌdʒɪˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌaspəˌdʒɪˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
An infection caused by a fungus of the genus Aspergillus, typically leading to inflammation and lesions in the lungs, ears, or other organs.
infection by Aspergillus fungus
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with aspergillosis after experiencing persistent respiratory symptoms.
Veterinarians often check birds for signs of aspergillosis in aviaries with poor ventilation.
People with weakened immune systems are at higher risk of developing aspergillosis.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Aspergillus' (the fungal genus) and '-osis' (disease condition).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæspərˌdʒɪˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌaspəˌdʒɪˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disease caused by infection with Aspergillus fungi, typically affecting the lungs and sometimes causing lumps in the skin, ears, or respiratory organs, especially in people with weakened immune systems or those exposed to moldy environments.
fungal infection by Aspergillus
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with aspergillosis after finding fungal growth in his lungs.
Agricultural workers are at higher risk of developing aspergillosis due to frequent exposure to moldy crops.
Symptoms of aspergillosis can range from mild respiratory issues to severe, life-threatening infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspergillus - a genus of fungi causing aspergillosis

Etymology

From New Latin, from Aspergillus (the fungal genus) + -osis (indicating a disease).

3. NOUN

/ ˌæspərˌdʒɪˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌaspəˌdʒɪˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disease caused by infection with fungi of the genus Aspergillus, affecting the respiratory system of birds and mammals and often leading to pneumonia.
fungal respiratory disease (esp. in birds)
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the flock with aspergillosis after several birds developed labored breathing.
Poultry farmers are advised to prevent aspergillosis by keeping their animal housing clean and dry.
Young turkeys are particularly susceptible to aspergillosis when exposed to contaminated feed or bedding.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Aspergillus' (the genus of fungi) + '-osis' (disease condition).
