transposed

1. ADJ

/ trænˈspoʊzd / (AmE), / trænˈspəʊzd / (BrE)
(of things) changed in order or position; moved so that elements switch places.
changed in order or position

Examples

When two numbers are transposed, the answer to the equation changes.
She accidentally transposed the final two digits of the phone number.
The editor corrected the transposed letters in the printed text.

Synset Examples

transposed letters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reversed - turned the other way around or in opposite order

Etymology

From Latin transponere, transposit- (to place across).
