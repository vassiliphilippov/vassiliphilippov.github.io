characin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkær.ə.sɪn / (AmE), / ˈkar.ə.sɪn / (BrE)
A freshwater fish of the family Characidae, found mainly in Central and South America and Africa, known for their diversity and often vibrant colors.
freshwater fish of the family Characidae

Examples

Many species of characin are popular in home aquariums for their bright colors and active behavior.
The Amazon River is home to an incredible diversity of characin fishes.
Researchers discovered a new species of characin in the South American rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tetra - a small, brightly colored characin fish popular in aquariums

Etymology

From modern Latin Characinus, from Greek kharax ('a kind of fish').
