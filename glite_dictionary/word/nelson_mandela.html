Nelson Mandela

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɛl.sən mænˈdɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˈnɛl.sən mænˈdɛl.ə / (BrE)
(1918–2013) South African anti-apartheid revolutionary and statesman, who became the country's first Black president (1994–1999) after 27 years of imprisonment.
South African anti-apartheid leader and president

Examples

Nelson Mandela spent 27 years in prison before becoming president of South Africa.
The leadership of Nelson Mandela is admired around the world.
Many people study the life and achievements of Nelson Mandela in history classes.
