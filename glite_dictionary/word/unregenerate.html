unregenerate

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈdʒɛn.ɚ.ɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈdʒɛn.ər.ət / (BrE)
not reformed in moral or spiritual character; stubbornly holding to former beliefs or behaviors
not morally or spiritually reformed

Examples

Despite years of guidance, he remained unregenerate in his views.
The novel depicts an unregenerate criminal who shows no remorse for his actions.
She was unapologetically unregenerate in her opposition to change.

Synset Examples

unregenerate human nature
unregenerate conservatism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incorrigible - unable to be corrected or reformed

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'un-' (not) + 'regeneratus' (regenerated, reborn), meaning not made new or improved.

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnrɪˈdʒɛnərət / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈdʒɛnərət / (BrE)
not feeling remorse or a desire to change; stubbornly unrepentant and incapable of being reformed.
stubbornly unrepentant or unchanging

Examples

Despite years in prison, he remained an unregenerate criminal, showing no remorse for his actions.
She was an unregenerate supporter of the old regime, refusing to accept any calls for reform.
Even after the scandal, the politician remained unregenerate and unapologetic in public.

Synset Examples

an unregenerate criminal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impenitent - not feeling regret or remorse for one's actions
  • Antonym: penitent - feeling or expressing remorse for misdeeds

Etymology

From Latin 'un-' (not) + 'regeneratus' (made new again, regenerated).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00638447-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈdʒɛn.ə.rət / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈdʒɛn.ər.ət / (BrE)
unwilling to change one's beliefs or actions; stubbornly persistent in holding an opinion or position, especially one considered wrong or outdated.
unwilling to change; stubborn

Examples

Despite numerous appeals for compromise, he remained unregenerate in his opposition to the new policy.
She was an unregenerate skeptic, refusing to believe anything without concrete evidence.
Even after the verdict, the defendant was unregenerate and showed no remorse for his actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recalcitrant - resisting authority or control; not obedient or compliant
  • Synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or chosen course of action
  • Antonym: yielding - willing to give in or submit to others

Etymology

Prefix 'un-' (not) + 'regenerate' from Latin 'regenare', meaning 'to be born again'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02334687-a
Synset POS: a
