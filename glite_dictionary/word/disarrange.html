to disarrange

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.əˈreɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈreɪndʒ / (BrE)
to make (something) untidy or not in the correct order; to disturb the arrangement of
make untidy or out of order

Examples

Someone had disarranged the books on my desk.
A sudden wind disarranged her carefully styled hair.
Please try not to disarrange the furniture while you clean the room.

Synset Examples

disarrange the papers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to arrange - to put things in a neat, correct, or desired order

Etymology

From French désarranger, from dés- (dis-) + arranger (to arrange).

2. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.əˈreɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈreɪndʒ / (BrE)
to disturb the arrangement or order of something; to make untidy or disorganized.
make untidy or disorganized

Examples

Please don't disarrange the books after I've just sorted them.
A strong gust of wind managed to disarrange her carefully styled hair.
Every time he moves, he seems to disarrange the things on the table.

Synset Examples

My son disarranged the papers on my desk

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to arrange - to put things in a neat or required order

Etymology

From dis- (opposite of) + arrange, from Old French arranger.
