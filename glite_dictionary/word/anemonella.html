Anemonella

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəmoʊˈnɛlə / (AmE), / ˌænɪməˈnɛlə / (BrE)
A small North American genus of perennial herbs in the buttercup family, commonly known as rue anemone, with delicate, white or pinkish flowers.
genus of herbaceous plants (rue anemone)

Examples

We saw the delicate white Anemonella blooming in the forest in early spring.
The woodland floor was dotted with Anemonella alongside trilliums and violets.
Botanists once debated whether Anemonella should be classified as a separate genus or within Thalictrum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rue anemone - common name for Anemonella thalictroides, a woodland wildflower

Etymology

From New Latin, diminutive of 'Anemone', referring to its resemblance to the anemone plant.

2. NOUN

/ əˌnɛməˈnɛlə / (AmE), / əˌnɛməˈnɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the buttercup family, containing a single species, the rue anemone, native to eastern North America.
plant genus; includes rue anemone

Examples

Anemonella is well known among botanists for its delicate flowers.
The woodland floor was dotted with clusters of Anemonella in early spring.
Anemonella thrives in moist, shady environments throughout eastern North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rue anemone - the only species of Anemonella, a small woodland wildflower

Etymology

From Greek anemōnē (anemone) with diminutive suffix -ella.
