abasement

1. NOUN

/ əˈbeɪsmənt / (AmE), / əˈbeɪsmənt / (BrE)
a state of being humiliated or lowered in rank, status, or dignity
state of humiliation or degradation

Examples

Her public apology was an act of abasement in front of her peers.
The constant criticism led him to a state of abasement.
He endured the abasement without protest, despite feeling deeply humiliated.

Synset Examples

each confession brought her into an attitude of abasement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humiliation - the act of making someone feel ashamed or foolish
  • Synonym: degradation - the process of being brought to a lower state or condition
  • Antonym: exaltation - the action of raising someone in rank, power, or character; the opposite of abasement

Etymology

From Middle French 'abaissement', from abaisser (to lower), from Old French 'abaissier', from Latin 'ad' (to) + 'bassus' (low).

2. NOUN

/ əˈbeɪsmənt / (AmE), / əˈbeɪsmənt / (BrE)
the act of humiliating or degrading someone, leading to a loss of self-esteem or dignity
humiliation; loss of self-esteem

Examples

The constant criticism led to his deep abasement in front of colleagues.
Many people struggle with feelings of abasement after being bullied.
Her apology was offered with a sense of genuine abasement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humiliation - the act of making someone feel ashamed or foolish
  • Synonym: degradation - the process of being treated or regarded with contempt or disrespect
  • Antonym: exaltation - the act of raising someone in rank, power, or character

Etymology

From Old French *abaissement*, from *abaisser* (to lower), ultimately from Latin *bassus* (low).
