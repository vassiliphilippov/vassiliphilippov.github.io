Pholidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊ.lɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈfɒl.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of mostly elongated marine fishes in the suborder Blennioidea, commonly known as gunnels, found in cold and temperate northern waters.
family of elongated marine fishes (gunnels)

Examples

The Pholidae are often found hiding among seaweed and rocks in coastal regions.
Scientists have identified several new species within the Pholidae family.
Unlike other blennies, members of the Pholidae have long, slender bodies adapted for narrow crevices.

Etymology

From New Latin Pholidae, based on the type genus Pholis.
