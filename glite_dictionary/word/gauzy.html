gauzy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɔzi / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːzi / (BrE)
very thin and light, often semi-transparent, allowing light to pass through
thin and semi-transparent

Examples

A gauzy curtain drifted in the breeze, letting sunlight softly filter through.
She wore a gauzy scarf that shimmered in the afternoon light.
The painter captured the gauzy glow of early morning mist over the fields.

Synset Examples

a hat with a diaphanous veil
filmy wings of a moth
gauzy clouds of dandelion down
gossamer cobwebs
sheer silk stockings
transparent chiffon
vaporous silks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diaphanous - light, delicate, and translucent
  • Antonym: opaque - not allowing light to pass through

Etymology

From 'gauze' + '-y', referring to a material's thin and light characteristics.
