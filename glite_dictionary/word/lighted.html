lighted

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of something) set afire or burning; made to produce light by ignition.
set afire or burning

Examples

She placed the lighted candle on the windowsill and watched it flicker.
Please do not enter the museum with a lighted cigarette.
A row of lighted lanterns lined the path during the festival.

Synset Examples

the lighted candles
a lighted cigarette
a lit firecracker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lit - set afire or burning (alternative form)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00477115-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtɪd / (BrE)
illuminated by artificial light; having been provided with light, especially electric or other artificial light sources.
illuminated by artificial light

Examples

The entire path was lighted by lanterns hanging from the trees.
She admired the lighted signs along the bustling city street at night.
We walked up the lighted staircase, feeling safer in the bright glow.

Synset Examples

illuminated advertising
looked up at the lighted windows
a brightly lit room
a well-lighted stairwell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lit - illuminated by artificial light; alternative past participle/adjective of 'light'

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'light'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00272446-s
Synset POS: s
