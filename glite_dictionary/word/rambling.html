rambling

1. ADJ

/ ˈræmblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræmblɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a path, road, or stream) following a winding or indirect course; meandering.
winding or meandering (especially of a path or stream)

Examples

We walked along a rambling trail that twisted through the dense forest.
The rambling stream made its way lazily through the meadow.
A rambling country road led us past fields and old barns.

Synset Examples

meandering streams
rambling forest paths
the river followed its wandering course
a winding country road

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meandering - following a winding or curving path
  • Synonym: winding - having twists and turns; not straight

Etymology

From Middle English 'ramblen', of uncertain origin, related to 'ramble' (to wander).

2. ADJ

/ ˈræm.blɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræm.blɪŋ / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) lengthy and confused or inconsequential, often digressing from the main point or covering a wide range of topics.
digressive; lacking clear focus

Examples

Her rambling speech made it difficult to understand her main argument.
The book is fascinating but becomes quite rambling in the later chapters.
He tends to give rambling answers that drift away from the original question.

Synset Examples

amusingly digressive with satirical thrusts at women's fashions among other things
a rambling discursive book
his excursive remarks
a rambling speech about this and that

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discursive - digressing from subject to subject
  • Antonym: concise - giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words

Etymology

From the verb 'ramble' (to wander), probably from Middle Dutch 'rammelen' (to wander about).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00771957-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈræmblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræmblɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an object or structure) spreading out in different directions or distributed irregularly; sprawling or straggling.
spreading irregularly; sprawling

Examples

The old mansion had a rambling layout, with rooms and corridors spreading in every direction.
He tried to read her rambling handwriting but found it difficult to follow.
Wild roses grew in rambling clusters along the garden fence.

Synset Examples

sprawling handwriting
straggling branches
straggly hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprawling - spreading out over a large area in an untidy or irregular way
  • Synonym: straggling - growing or spreading out in an irregular or untidy way
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
