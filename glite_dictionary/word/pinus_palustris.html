Pinus palustris

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.nəs pəˈlʌs.trɪs / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.nəs pəˈlʌs.trɪs / (BrE)
A species of large pine tree native to the southeastern United States, known for its long needles, twisted limbs, and valuable timber.
a large pine tree of the southeastern US

Examples

The ecosystem of the southeastern United States once consisted largely of Pinus palustris forests.
Many conservation efforts focus on restoring the habitat of Pinus palustris.
The lumber from Pinus palustris is highly valued for its strength and durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longleaf pine - common name for Pinus palustris, a southern US pine tree
  • Synonym: loblolly pine - another large pine of the southeastern United States

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pinus' meaning 'pine' and 'palustris' meaning 'of the marsh'.
