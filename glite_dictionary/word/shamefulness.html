shamefulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪmfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪmfʊlnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being disgraceful or deserving of public shame
quality of being disgraceful

Examples

The shamefulness of his actions stunned everyone at the meeting.
She spoke openly about the shamefulness she felt after breaking the rules.
The documentary exposed the shamefulness of the corrupt officials' conduct.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shame - a painful feeling of humiliation caused by wrongdoing
  • Antonym: guiltlessness - the state of being free from guilt or blame

Etymology

From 'shameful' + '-ness', forming a noun for the state or quality.
