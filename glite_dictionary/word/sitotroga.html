Sitotroga

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪtəˈtroʊɡə / (AmE), / ˌsɪtəˈtrəʊɡə / (BrE)
A genus of small moths known as grain moths, some of which are pests affecting stored grain.
genus of grain moths

Examples

The larvae of Sitotroga species can destroy entire batches of stored wheat.
Farmers have to take precautions against infestations of Sitotroga in grain silos.
Proper storage conditions can help prevent Sitotroga from damaging crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grain moth - common name for moths of the genus Sitotroga that infest stored grain

Etymology

From Greek 'sitos' (grain) + 'trogein' (to gnaw), indicating their habit of feeding on grain.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪtəˈtroʊɡə / (AmE), / ˌsɪtəˈtrəʊɡə / (BrE)
A genus of small moths whose larvae infest and feed on stored grains.
genus of grain-infesting moths

Examples

The Sitotroga moth is a well-known pest in grain storage facilities worldwide.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Sitotroga to develop better pest control methods.
Proper storage conditions are essential to prevent Sitotroga infestations in harvested wheat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grain moth - a small moth whose larvae feed on stored grains, often causing damage

Etymology

From Greek 'sitos' (grain) + 'trogein' (to gnaw/eat).
