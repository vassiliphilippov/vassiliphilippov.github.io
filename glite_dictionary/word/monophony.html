monophony

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑːfəni / (AmE), / məˈnɒfəni / (BrE)
Music that consists of a single melodic line or vocal part, typically without any harmonic accompaniment.
single melodic line in music

Examples

Gregorian chant is a famous example of monophony in Western music history.
Early church music was often composed in monophony, with a single melody sung in unison.
Unlike polyphony, monophony does not include harmonizing voices or chords.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polyphony - music with two or more independent melodic lines

Etymology

From Greek monos (single) + phōnē (sound, voice).
