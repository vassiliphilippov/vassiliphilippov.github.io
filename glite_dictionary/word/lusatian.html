Lusatian

1. NOUN

/ luˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / luˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
a West Slavic language spoken in rural areas of southeastern Germany, particularly in Lusatia; also known as Sorbian
West Slavic language spoken in Germany

Examples

Many children in southeastern Germany grow up speaking Lusatian in their homes.
The Lusatian language preserves many features lost in other Slavic languages.
He is fluent in both German and Lusatian, reflecting his cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sorbian - the West Slavic language also called Lusatian, spoken by the Sorbs in Germany

Etymology

From Lusatia, the region in southeastern Germany where the language is spoken; related to the Slavic people of Lusatia.
