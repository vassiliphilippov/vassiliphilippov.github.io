eurypterid

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈrɪptərɪd / (AmE), / jʊəˈrɪptərɪd / (BrE)
an extinct group of large, scorpion-like aquatic arthropods related to horseshoe crabs, known from fossil records
extinct aquatic arthropod (sea scorpion)

Examples

Fossils of the eurypterid have been discovered in many parts of the world.
The eurypterid is often called the sea scorpion due to its resemblance to modern scorpions.
Scientists believe the eurypterid preyed on smaller marine animals during the Paleozoic era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea scorpion - common name for eurypterid

Etymology

From New Latin Eurypterida, from Greek 'eurys' (broad) + 'pteron' (wing, fin).
