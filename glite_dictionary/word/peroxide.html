peroxide

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɑːkˌsaɪd / (AmE), / pəˈrɒk.saɪd / (BrE)
a viscous liquid with strong oxidizing properties, used especially as a bleaching agent, mild disinfectant, or, in concentrated form, as an oxidant in rocket fuels
powerful oxidizing liquid, bleaching agent

Examples

Be careful not to spill the peroxide when cleaning the kitchen surfaces.
The hairdresser used peroxide to lighten her client’s hair.
Scientists studied the reaction of concentrated peroxide in rocket engines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrogen peroxide - a common type of peroxide used as bleach and disinfectant

Etymology

From per- (indicating 'through' or 'thoroughly') + oxide.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈrɑksaɪd / (AmE), / pəˈrɒksaɪd / (BrE)
an inorganic compound containing the divalent -O-O- ion, often used as a bleaching or disinfecting agent
inorganic compound with -O-O- ion

Examples

Exposure to peroxide solutions can cause irritation to skin and eyes.
Scientists used a peroxide compound as a catalyst in the reaction.
Most hair bleaching products contain hydrogen peroxide as the active ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrogen peroxide - a common peroxide compound, H2O2, used as bleach and antiseptic

Etymology

From French 'peroxyde', based on 'oxide' and the prefix 'per-' meaning 'through', 'excess'.

3. VERB

/ pəˈrɑːksaɪd / (AmE), / pəˈrɒksaɪd / (BrE)
to bleach or treat something, especially hair, with hydrogen peroxide to make it lighter in color
bleach or treat with peroxide

Examples

She decided to peroxide her hair before her wedding.
Many people peroxide their hair to achieve a platinum blonde look.
If you peroxide your hair too often, it may become dry and brittle.

Synset Examples

She must peroxide her hair-it looks unnaturally blond

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleach - to make something lighter in color by chemical means

Etymology

Formed from 'peroxide' (the noun, a chemical compound containing oxygen), used as a verb since the late 19th century.
