Desmodium purpureum

1. NOUN

/ dɛzˈmoʊdiəm pɜrˈpjʊəriəm / (AmE), / dɛzˈməʊdiəm pɜːˈpjʊərɪəm / (BrE)
A West Indian forage plant of the legume family, cultivated in the southern United States as animal feed and to improve soil fertility.
a West Indian forage legume

Examples

Farmers in the region use Desmodium purpureum as a reliable source of livestock fodder.
The introduction of Desmodium purpureum has improved soil quality on many southern U.S. farms.
Agronomists recommend planting Desmodium purpureum to restore nitrogen content in depleted fields.

Etymology

From genus Desmodium (Greek desmos 'bond' + -idium, diminutive) and Latin purpureum, meaning 'purple'.
