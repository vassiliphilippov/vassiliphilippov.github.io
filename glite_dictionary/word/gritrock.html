gritrock

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪtˌrɑk / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪtˌrɒk / (BrE)
a hard, coarse-grained siliceous sandstone, often used in building or found in geological formations
hard coarse-grained sandstone

Examples

The cliffs are made primarily of gritrock, giving them a rough texture.
Geologists found fossils embedded in the layers of gritrock.
The old building was constructed using sturdy blocks of local gritrock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandstone - a sedimentary rock composed mainly of sand-sized minerals or rock grains

Etymology

Formed from 'grit' (coarse sand) and 'rock'; 'grit' from Old English 'greot' (sand, gravel).
