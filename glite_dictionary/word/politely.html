politely

1. ADV

/ pəˈlaɪtli / (AmE), / pəˈlaɪtli / (BrE)
in a way that shows good manners or respect for others
in a polite manner

Examples

She greeted everyone politely when she entered the room.
The customer politely asked for a refund after receiving the wrong item.
Although he disagreed, he expressed his opinion politely.

Synset Examples

the policeman answered politely, ‘Now look here, lady … ’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rudely - in a manner lacking courtesy or politeness

Etymology

Formed from 'polite' + '-ly', with 'polite' from Latin 'politus' (polished, refined).
