wooden

1. ADJ

/ ˈwʊdən / (AmE), / ˈwʊd(ə)n / (BrE)
made of, consisting of, or using wood, wholly or in part
made of wood

Examples

The cabin was built from wooden planks harvested from the local forest.
She stored her childhood toys in a large wooden chest at the foot of her bed.
A wooden fence surrounded the garden, keeping the rabbits out.

Synset Examples

a wooden box
an ancient cart with wooden wheels

Etymology

From Old English wuden, from wood (wood) + -en (made of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈwʊdən / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.ən / (BrE)
(of a person's behavior or appearance) lacking in natural ease, grace, or warmth; stiff and awkward.
lacking ease or grace; stiff

Examples

His speech sounded wooden and rehearsed, lacking any genuine emotion.
She forced a wooden smile when she saw her ex at the party.
The actor's wooden performance made the scene less believable.

Synset Examples

the actor's performance was wooden
a wooden smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stilted - (of a manner or performance) stiff and unnatural
  • Synonym: awkward - not smooth or graceful; socially clumsy
  • Antonym: graceful - showing elegance or ease of movement or style
