bookishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊkɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈbʊkɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being excessively fond of books or devoted to academic study, especially in a way that is overly studious or lacking in practical experience
excessive devotion to books or study

Examples

Her bookishness made her an outstanding scholar but sometimes left her out of touch with everyday concerns.
He was gently teased for his bookishness and preference for reading over playing sports.
While some admired his bookishness, others found it a little intimidating at social gatherings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: studiousness - quality of spending a lot of time studying
  • Antonym: practicality - focus on practical matters rather than theory or study

Etymology

From 'bookish' + '-ness'; 'bookish' from Middle English, formed from 'book' + '-ish'.
