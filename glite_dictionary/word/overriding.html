overriding

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈraɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈraɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
More important or influential than anything else; taking priority over all other considerations.
more important than anything else

Examples

His overriding concern is always the safety of the children.
The overriding principle is to act in the best interests of the patient.
Despite many objections, the overriding factor was the urgent need for a new hospital.

Synset Examples

the predominant mood among policy-makers is optimism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predominant - more important or noticeable than others
  • Antonym: minor - less important or influential

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'override'.
