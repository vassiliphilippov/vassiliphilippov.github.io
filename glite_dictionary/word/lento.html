lento

1. ADV

/ ˈlɛn.toʊ / (AmE), / ˈlɛn.təʊ / (BrE)
(in music) to be played or sung slowly; at a slow tempo.
at a slow tempo (music)

Examples

The conductor instructed the orchestra to begin the movement lento.
During the rehearsal, she asked the pianist to repeat the passage lento.
The final section of the piece is marked lento to create a solemn mood.

Synset Examples

Play this lento, please

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adagio - at a slow tempo (music)
  • Antonym: allegro - at a fast tempo (music)

Etymology

From Italian 'lento' meaning 'slow'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɛn.toʊ / (AmE), / ˈlɛn.təʊ / (BrE)
(in music) to be played or sung in a slow tempo.
slow tempo in music

Examples

The first movement is played lento, creating a calm and peaceful atmosphere.
The conductor instructed the orchestra to take the passage lento.
This section is marked lento to contrast with the faster allegro parts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adagio - slow tempo in music
  • Antonym: presto - very fast tempo in music
  • Degree Higher: andante - moderately slow tempo in music

Etymology

From Italian 'lento' meaning 'slow'.

Synset POS: s
