effectuation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌfɛktʃuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌfɛktjʊˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something happen or putting something into effect
the act of implementing or carrying out

Examples

The successful effectuation of the project depended on careful planning and teamwork.
Policy effectuation will require approval from several government agencies.
Delays in effectuation can undermine confidence in new initiatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implementation - the process of putting a decision or plan into effect

Etymology

From Late Latin effectuationem, from effectus (effect).
