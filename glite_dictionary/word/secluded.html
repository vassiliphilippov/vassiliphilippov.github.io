secluded

1. ADJ

/ sɪˈkludɪd / (AmE), / sɪˈkluːdɪd / (BrE)
(of a place) not seen or visited by many people; situated away from general view or use; hidden or private.
hidden from general view or use

Examples

They enjoyed a picnic in a secluded corner of the park.
The resort offers secluded villas surrounded by lush gardens.
We found a secluded beach that was perfect for a quiet swim.

Synset Examples

a privy place to rest and think
a secluded romantic spot
a secret garden

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolated - far away from other places or people
  • Antonym: exposed - in a position where something is not sheltered or hidden

Etymology

From Latin 'secludere' (to shut off, separate), from 'se-' (apart) + 'claudere' (to shut).

2. ADJ

/ sɪˈklu.dɪd / (AmE), / sɪˈkluː.dɪd / (BrE)
(of a place) not seen or visited by many people; sheltered, private, or hidden away from general view or activity.
private or hidden away

Examples

The small cabin was located in a secluded forest far from the main road.
They enjoyed a picnic in a secluded garden, away from the noise of the city.
Her favorite reading spot is a secluded corner of the library.

Synset Examples

the cloistered academic world of books
sat close together in the sequestered pergola
sitting under the reclusive calm of a shade tree
a secluded romantic spot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: private - not accessible to the public; intended for or restricted to the use of a particular person, group, or class
  • Antonym: public - open to or shared by all people

Etymology

From Late Latin 'secludere' (to shut off, keep out).
