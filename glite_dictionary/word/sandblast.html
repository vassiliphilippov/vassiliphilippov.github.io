sandblast

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈsændˌblɑːst / (BrE)
a strong current of air carrying sand, often eroding surfaces or causing damage
air blast carrying sand

Examples

A sudden sandblast forced the hikers to take shelter behind large rocks.
Protective gear is essential when working in areas prone to sandblasts.
After the sandblast, a fine layer of grit covered everything in sight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandstorm - a strong wind carrying clouds of sand over a large area
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ ˈsændˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈsændˌblɑːst / (BrE)
to clean or shape a surface by directing a high-pressure stream of sand or other abrasive material at it
clean or shape using a stream of abrasive material

Examples

Workers will sandblast the old paint off the metal beams before repainting.
The company was hired to sandblast graffiti from the historic stone walls.
After years of weathering, the statues needed to be sandblasted to restore their original appearance.

Synset Examples

sandblast the facade of the building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abrade - to scrape or wear away by friction
  • Degree Lower: polish - to make a surface smooth and shiny by rubbing

Etymology

From 'sand' + 'blast', referring to blasting with sand.
