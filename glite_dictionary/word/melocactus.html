Melocactus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛl.oʊˈkæktəs / (AmE), / ˌmiːl.əˈkæktəs / (BrE)
A genus of strongly ribbed, globular or spherical cacti native to tropical South and Central America and the Caribbean.
genus of globular tropical cacti

Examples

Many collectors prize the Melocactus for its unusual cephalium and attractive spines.
The Melocactus is found mainly in the arid regions of the Caribbean and northern South America.
A well-cared-for Melocactus can thrive in a sunny indoor environment with minimal watering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cactus - a plant of the family Cactaceae

Etymology

From Greek 'mēlon' (apple) and Latin 'cactus' (cactus), referring to the shape of the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛloʊˈkæktəs / (AmE), / ˌmiːləʊˈkaktəs / (BrE)
A genus of strongly ribbed, globose or spheroid cacti native to tropical regions of South and Central America and the Caribbean.
genus of tropical ribbed cacti

Examples

The distinctive cephalium is a characteristic feature of Melocactus species.
Botanists discovered a rare Melocactus in the Brazilian rainforest.
Many collectors value Melocactus for its unusual appearance and colorful fruit.

Etymology

From Greek 'mēlon' (apple) + 'kaktos' (spiny plant), referring to the plant's rounded shape.
