myotomy

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈɑːtəmi / (AmE), / maɪˈɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a muscle is cut or divided to relieve constriction or improve function
surgical cutting of a muscle
medical

Examples

The surgeon performed a myotomy to treat the patient's esophageal disorder.
A Heller myotomy is often used to address achalasia.
Recovery from a myotomy typically involves several days of rest and monitoring.

Etymology

From Greek 'mys' (muscle) + 'tomia' (cutting).
