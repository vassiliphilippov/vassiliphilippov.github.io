oarswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈɔːzwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who rows a boat, especially as a sport or in competition
female rower

Examples

The Olympic gold medalist was an exceptional oarswoman known for her strength and endurance.
As the only oarswoman on the team, she inspired many young girls to take up rowing.
The oarswoman glided smoothly across the calm surface of the lake during practice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oarsman - a man who rows a boat
  • Synonym: rower - a person who rows a boat

Etymology

Formed from 'oar' and 'woman,' modeled after 'oarsman.'
