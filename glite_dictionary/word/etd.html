ETD

1. NOUN

/ i ti ˈdi / (AmE), / iː tiː ˈdiː / (BrE)
a system used to detect trace amounts of explosives, typically by analyzing swabs taken from luggage or other surfaces, especially in airport security
system for trace explosives detection

Examples

Airport security uses an ETD to check hand luggage for traces of explosives.
After the swab tested positive on the ETD, the passenger was taken aside for further screening.
Maintenance staff are trained to operate the ETD machines efficiently and safely.

Etymology

Abbreviation of explosives trace detection.
