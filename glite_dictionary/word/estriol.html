estriol

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛstriˌɒl / (AmE), / ˈiːstriɒl / (BrE)
a naturally occurring estrogenic hormone, especially significant during pregnancy; also used in its synthetic form to treat estrogen deficiency
a naturally occurring estrogenic hormone
medical

Examples

Levels of estriol rise significantly in pregnant women.
The doctor prescribed synthetic estriol to manage her symptoms.
Research is ongoing into the benefits of estriol therapy for certain conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estrogen - a group of steroid hormones promoting the development of female characteristics

Etymology

From 'estr-' (relating to estrogen) + '-iol', indicating its status as a triol (a molecule with three hydroxyl groups).
