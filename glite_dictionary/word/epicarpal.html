epicarpal

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛpɪˈkɑrpəl / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈkɑːpəl / (BrE)
relating to or situated on the epicarp (the outer layer of the fruit wall)
relating to the outer fruit layer (epicarp)
medical

Examples

The epicarpal tissue plays a crucial role in protecting the developing fruit.
Certain pests only attack the epicarpal layer of citrus fruits.
Researchers examined the epicarpal structure under a microscope to study its wax composition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epicarp - the outermost layer of a fruit's pericarp

Etymology

From 'epicarp' + '-al', with 'epicarp' from Greek 'epi' (upon) + 'karpos' (fruit).
