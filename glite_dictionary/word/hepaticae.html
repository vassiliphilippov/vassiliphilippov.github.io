Hepaticae

1. NOUN

/ hɛˈpætɪˌsiː / (AmE), / hɛˈpætɪˌsiː / (BrE)
A former class of non-vascular plants, commonly known as liverworts, comprising several botanical orders such as Anthocerotales, Jungermanniales, Marchantiales, and Sphaerocarpales.
class of non-vascular plants; liverworts
medical

Examples

The Hepaticae are distinguished from mosses by their different reproductive structures.
Many botanists now include the Hepaticae within the division Marchantiophyta.
Traditional classification placed liverworts in the class Hepaticae, though modern systems differ.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liverwort - a small non-vascular plant in the division Marchantiophyta

Etymology

From Latin *hepatica*, meaning 'liverwort', from Greek *hēpatikos* (of the liver), referring to the liver-shaped leaves of some species.
