shiner

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪnə / (BrE)
any of several small, silvery North American freshwater fish, especially of the genus Notropis, commonly found in streams and rivers.
small silvery North American freshwater fish

Examples

The creek was full of tiny shiners darting through the clear water.
Anglers often use shiners as bait when fishing for larger species.
As a child, she loved catching shiners with a net in the summer.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪnə / (BrE)
a swollen bruise or black eye, especially one caused by a blow to the area around the eye
swollen bruise around the eye
informal

Examples

It took over a week for his shiner to heal completely.
