Oestrus ovis

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːstrəs ˈoʊvɪs / (AmE), / ˌiːstrəs ˈəʊvɪs / (BrE)
a species of botfly whose larvae are parasitic on the nasal passages of sheep
sheep botfly species

Examples

The larvae of Oestrus ovis commonly infest the nasal cavities of sheep.
Veterinarians are trained to recognize symptoms of Oestrus ovis infestation in livestock.
Farmers may use preventive measures to protect their flocks from Oestrus ovis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botfly - a type of fly whose larvae are internal parasites of mammals

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Oestrus' (from Greek 'oistros' meaning 'gadfly') and species name 'ovis' (Latin for 'sheep').
