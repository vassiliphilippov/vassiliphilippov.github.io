to brandish

1. VERB

/ ˈbræn.dɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbræn.dɪʃ / (BrE)
to wave or display (something, especially a weapon) in a threatening or showy manner
wave or display something threateningly

Examples

The pirate began to brandish his cutlass as he shouted orders to the crew.
She brandished the frying pan at the intruder to scare him away.
Police officers saw the suspect brandishing a weapon in the crowded marketplace.

Synset Examples

brandish a sword

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - move to and fro in the air

Etymology

From Old French brandir (to flourish a sword), from brand (sword).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02146993-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈbræn.dɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbræn.dɪʃ / (BrE)
to wave or swing something, especially a weapon, in a threatening or excited way
wave or swing (something, esp. weapon)

Examples

The knight brandished his sword as he entered the arena.
She brandished the stick at the stray dog to scare it away.
During the parade, protesters brandished banners high above their heads.

Synset Examples

She waved her gun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wield - to hold and use (a weapon or tool)

Etymology

From Old French 'brandir' (to flourish a sword), from 'brand' (sword).
