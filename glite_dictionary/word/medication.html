medication

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛdɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
A substance or drug used to treat, prevent, or alleviate the symptoms of disease.
drug or substance for treating illness
medical

Examples

He takes his medication every morning to manage his blood pressure.
The doctor prescribed a new medication for her allergies.
It's important not to stop your medication without consulting a healthcare professional.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medicine - a compound or preparation used for the treatment or prevention of disease
  • Synonym: remedy - a treatment that relieves or cures a disease or disorder

Etymology

From Latin 'medicatio' (treatment with medicine), from 'medicari' (to heal).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛdɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of treating an illness or condition with medicines or remedies
treatment using medicines
medical

Examples

The medication of her infection required strict adherence to the doctor’s instructions.
Prompt medication of the wound reduced the risk of serious complications.
Successful medication relies on both choosing the right drugs and ensuring proper dosage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treatment - medical care for an illness or injury
  • Synonym: cure - relief from the symptoms of a disease or condition

Etymology

From Latin 'medicatio', from 'medicari' (to heal, give medicine).
