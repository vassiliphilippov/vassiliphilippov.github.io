Delacroix

1. PROPN

/ də.ləˈkrwɑ / (AmE), / də.ləˈkrwɑ / (BrE)
(1798–1863) A French Romantic painter, renowned for his expressive brushwork and vibrant color, whose works influenced the development of modern art.
French Romantic painter (1798–1863)

Examples

The paintings of Delacroix had a profound influence on later artists like Van Gogh and Cézanne.
At the museum, we admired an original work by Delacroix depicting a dramatic historical scene.
Art history students often study Delacroix for his innovative use of color and composition.

Etymology

French surname, meaning 'of the cross' (de la croix).
