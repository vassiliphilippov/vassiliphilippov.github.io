whiffer

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪfər / (AmE), / ˈwɪfə / (BrE)
(informal) A batter in baseball who strikes out by swinging and missing at the third strike.
baseball batter who strikes out swinging
informal american

Examples

The rookie was called a whiffer after missing the pitch three times in a row.
Fans groaned as the star hitter turned into a whiffer in the bottom of the ninth inning.
He worked hard to avoid being labeled a whiffer by his teammates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strikeout - an instance when a batter receives three strikes and is out

Etymology

From 'whiff', meaning to strike out by swinging and missing, originally imitating the sound of swinging through air.
