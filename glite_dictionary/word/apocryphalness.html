apocryphalness

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑː.krə.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / əˈpɒ.krɪ.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being of doubtful authenticity or accuracy; the condition of being apocryphal.
quality of being dubious or untrue

Examples

The apocryphalness of the legend makes historians doubt its accuracy.
Given the apocryphalness of the rumor, we should seek more reliable sources.
Many urban myths are notable for their sheer apocryphalness despite widespread belief.

Synset Examples

Not sure of the relative apocryphalness of this story, but I've always heard that the Chevrolet Nova didn't sell too well in Latin America because ‘no va’ in Spanish means ‘it does not go’.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: authenticity - the quality of being genuine or true

Etymology

From 'apocryphal' + '-ness', where 'apocryphal' is from Late Latin 'apocryphus', from Greek 'apokryphos' (hidden).

2. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːkrəfəl nɪs / (AmE), / əˈpɒkrɪf(ə)lnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being of doubtful authenticity or not considered genuine or canonical.
the state of being of doubtful authenticity

Examples

The apocryphalness of the manuscript raised doubts about its historical value.
Many historians debated the apocryphalness of the newly discovered gospel.
He questioned the apocryphalness of the legend that had long been accepted as fact.

Synset Examples

The ultimate sources of these English-Celtic legends are of two kinds: (1) the old Greek-Roman mythology; (2) the Jewish-Christian Bible legends, of various degrees of apocryphalness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: authenticity - the quality of being genuine or real
  • Synonym: apocryphal - of doubtful authenticity; not genuine

Etymology

Formed from 'apocryphal' (from Late Latin apocryphus, from Greek apokryphos 'hidden') + -ness.
