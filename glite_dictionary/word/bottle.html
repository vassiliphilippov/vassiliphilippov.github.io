bottle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈbɒt.əl / (BrE)
A container with a flexible teat used to feed milk or formula to infants and very young children as a substitute for breastfeeding.
container for feeding infants

Examples

The baby was happily drinking from her bottle in the living room.
Parents are advised to sterilize the bottle before each use.
When he was an infant, he refused to take the bottle at night.

/ ˈbɑːt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəl / (BrE)
the amount of liquid or other substance that a bottle holds or contains
the amount a bottle holds

Examples

The family finished two bottles of milk at breakfast.

3. VERB

/ ˈbɑːtəl / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəl / (BrE)
to store (liquids or gases) in bottles for preservation or sale
store in bottles

Examples

They bottle the juice immediately after squeezing to maintain freshness.
After fermentation, the winemaker will bottle the wine for aging.
The company bottles spring water from the mountains for commercial sale.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to decant - to pour liquid from one container into another

Etymology

From Old French *botel*, diminutive of *bote* (cask), from Medieval Latin *buticula*.

4. VERB

/ ˈbɑːt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈbɒt.əl / (BrE)
to put (a liquid or substance) into bottles for storage or sale
put in bottles

Examples

After making the juice, they decided to bottle it for their friends.
The company will bottle the new sparkling water in eco-friendly glass containers.
It can take several hours to bottle all the wine after the fermentation process is finished.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Old French 'botel' (bottle), from medieval Latin 'butticula', diminutive of 'buttis' (cask, wine-skin); verb formation from the noun.

5. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈbɒt.l̩ / (BrE)
a glass or plastic container with a narrow neck, used for storing drinks or other liquids
container for liquids, usually with a narrow neck

Examples

She poured the wine into a clean bottle and sealed it with a cork.
There was a half-empty bottle of water on the kitchen table.
Please recycle your glass bottle after you finish drinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flask - a container for liquids, often for carrying alcoholic drinks

Etymology

From Old French 'botel', from Medieval Latin 'buticula', diminutive of Latin 'buttis' (wine cask).
