venire facias

1. NOUN

/ vəˌnaɪri fəˈkaɪəs / (AmE), / vɛˌniːreɪ ˈfæsiəs / (BrE)
a judicial writ ordering a sheriff to summon people for jury duty
writ to summon jurors (legal)
legal

Examples

The court issued a venire facias to compel attendance of jurors at the upcoming trial.
Without a venire facias, the sheriff had no authority to summon a jury.
The lawyer referenced the venire facias during his explanation of the jury selection process.

Etymology

From Latin 'venire facias', meaning 'that you cause to come'.
