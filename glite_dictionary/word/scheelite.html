scheelite

1. NOUN

/ skiːˌliːt / (AmE), / skiːˌliːt / (BrE)
a yellowish-white to brownish mineral consisting of calcium tungstate, used as an ore of tungsten
a mineral ore of tungsten

Examples

A large deposit of scheelite was discovered in the mountainous region.
The mining company extracted scheelite to obtain tungsten for industrial use.
Geologists identified the mineral specimen as scheelite because of its unique fluorescence.

Etymology

From German Scheelit, named after Carl Wilhelm Scheele, Swedish chemist.
