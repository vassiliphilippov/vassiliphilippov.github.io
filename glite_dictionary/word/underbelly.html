underbelly

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndərˌbɛli / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌbɛli / (BrE)
the underside or lower surface of an object, animal, or structure
underside or lower surface

Examples

The underbelly of the airplane was painted a matte gray.
In the photograph, the underbelly of the whale was clearly visible as it breached.
Heavy rain turned the underbelly of the tent muddy and damp.

Synset Examples

the underbellies of clouds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underside - the lower surface of something

Etymology

Formed from 'under-' (below) + 'belly' (stomach, underside).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌbɛli / (AmE), / ˈʌndəbɛli / (BrE)
the vulnerable or weakest part of something, often the part most susceptible to attack or damage
vulnerable or weak part of something

Examples

Some critics argue that the company’s expansive growth strategy exposes its underbelly to competitors.
The documentary exposed the dark underbelly of the supposedly prosperous city.
During the war, the southern border was considered the country’s soft underbelly.

Synset Examples

the soft underbelly of the Axis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakness - a lack of strength or a weak point
  • Antonym: strength - a strong or secure part; the opposite of weakness

3. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌbɛli / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌbɛli / (BrE)
the soft, underside part of an animal's body
soft underside of an animal's body

Examples

A crocodile's underbelly is surprisingly soft compared to its armored back.
The veterinarian gently examined the cat's underbelly for any signs of injury.
Predators often target the underbelly because it is the most vulnerable area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belly - the front part of an animal's or human's body below the ribs

4. NOUN

/ ˈʌndərˌbɛli / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌbɛli / (BrE)
The hidden, often unpleasant or vulnerable side of something, especially of a city, organization, or society.
hidden or vulnerable side (often unpleasant)

Examples

Many novels set in big cities reveal the underbelly of urban life.
The journalist's report exposed the underbelly of corruption within the government.
Tourists rarely see the true underbelly of the city beyond the popular attractions.

Synset Examples

The underbelly of the city reveals its hidden struggles.
Investigators exposed the underbelly of the crime ring.
The documentary explores the underbelly of the fashion industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dark side - the wicked, evil, or morally questionable aspect of something
  • Antonym: surface - the outward or visible aspect of something, especially compared to what lies beneath

Etymology

Originally from the literal sense of 'underbelly,' meaning the underside of an animal, extending metaphorically to mean the vulnerable or less visible side.
