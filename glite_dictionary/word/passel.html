passel

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsəl / (AmE), / ˈpæsəl / (BrE)
a large number or group of people or things, often used with 'of'
a large group or amount

Examples

A passel of children played in the yard until sunset.
She brought a passel of cookies for the office party.
We received a passel of questions after the lecture.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batch - a quantity or collection of things or people dealt with as a group
  • Synonym: slew - a large number or quantity of something
  • Synonym: lot - a large number or amount

Etymology

Alteration of 'parcel' in American English, first recorded in 19th century.
