fradicin

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrædɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˈfrædɪsɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic obtained from an actinomycete, used as an intestinal antiseptic under the trade name Neobiotic, especially in surgical contexts
antibiotic used as an intestinal antiseptic
medical

Examples

The surgeon administered fradicin before the operation to prevent intestinal infections.
Doctors often prescribe fradicin in cases requiring effective local intestinal antiseptics.
Before its trade ban, fradicin was commonly known under the brand name Neobiotic.
