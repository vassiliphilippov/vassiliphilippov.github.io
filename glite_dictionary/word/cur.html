cur

1. NOUN

/ kɜr / (AmE), / kɜː / (BrE)
an aggressive or inferior dog, especially one of mixed breed
inferior or mixed-breed dog

Examples

The old man chased the cur away from his front porch.
She took pity on the hungry cur wandering the alley.
In the story, the hero rescues a loyal cur from cruel children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mongrel - a dog of no definable type or breed
  • Antonym: pedigree dog - a dog of pure breed

Etymology

From Middle English *curdogge*, probably imitative of the sound used to call dogs, or from Old Norse *kurra* (to growl).

2. NOUN

/ kɝː / (AmE), / kɜː / (BrE)
an insulting term for a person regarded as cowardly or despicable
cowardly or contemptible person (insult)

Examples

He called his betrayer a vile cur in front of the assembled crowd.
The general refused to negotiate with such a cur and insisted on unconditional surrender.
No one respected the cur who deserted his friends in a time of need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Antonym: hero - a person admired for courage or noble qualities (opposite in character)

Etymology

From Old Norse 'kurra' (to grumble), originally referring to a mongrel or worthless dog.
