Saint Ambrose

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈæm.broʊz / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈæm.brəʊz / (BrE)
(340?–397) An influential Roman priest, bishop of Milan, and early Church Father known for shaping Christian doctrine, composing hymns, and strengthening the power of the early Church.
early Christian bishop and Church Father

Examples

Saint Ambrose was instrumental in establishing many traditions of the early Christian Church.
The hymns written by Saint Ambrose are still sung in some churches today.
Many scholars study the sermons of Saint Ambrose to understand early Christian theology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St. Augustine - early Christian theologian and philosopher; another Church Father
  • Synonym: St. Jerome - early Christian priest and theologian; another influential Church Father

Etymology

From Latin Ambrosius, from Greek Ambrosios, meaning ‘immortal’.
