stirring

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɜrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɜːrɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of mixing a liquid or substance with a tool such as a spoon or stick
mixing a liquid or substance

Examples

The recipe requires constant stirring to prevent lumps from forming.
After several minutes of stirring, the sugar dissolved completely.
She used a wooden spoon for stirring the soup on the stove.

Synset Examples

constant stirring prevents it from burning on the bottom of the pan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mixing - the act of combining or blending substances

Etymology

Present participle of 'stir,' from Middle English 'stiren' (to move, agitate).

2. ADJ

/ ˈstɝːɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɜːrɪŋ / (BrE)
causing strong, positive emotions; inspiring or moving
inspiring; emotionally moving

Examples

Synset Examples

a stirring speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspiring - giving someone the urge or ability to do or feel something
  • Synonym: moving - causing strong feelings of sadness or sympathy

Etymology
