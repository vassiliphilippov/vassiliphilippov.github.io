to classify

1. VERB

/ ˈklæs.ɪ.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈklɑː.sɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to officially declare information or documents to be secret for security or other official reasons
declare documents secret for security reasons

Examples

The government decided to classify the report for national security reasons.
Only authorized personnel can access documents that are classified by the agency.
They will classify the files so the public cannot view them.

Synset Examples

Classify these documents

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to declassify - declare secret information to be no longer secret

Etymology

From French classifier, from Latin classis (class, division) + -ify.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02514452-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈklæs.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈklɑː.sɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to arrange or organize people or things into groups or categories based on shared characteristics
arrange into groups or categories

Examples

Scientists classify animals based on their evolutionary relationships.
The librarian will classify these new books under science fiction.
It's important to classify information so it can be easily found later.

Synset Examples

How would you classify these pottery shards — are they prehistoric?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to categorize - to place in a particular category
  • Synonym: group - to put into groups; to form into a group
  • Synonym: sort - to arrange according to kind, class, or category

Etymology

From French 'classifier', from Latin 'classis' (class) + '-fy' (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00656296-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈklæsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪfʌɪ / (BrE)
to arrange or assign (things or people) into groups or categories according to shared qualities or characteristics
arrange into categories or groups

Examples

Scientists classify animals based on their physical characteristics and genetic similarities.
Books in the library are classified according to subject to make them easier to find.
Can you classify these samples into the right chemical groups?

Synset Examples

How should algae be classified?
People argue about how to relegate certain mushrooms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to categorize - to place in a particular class or group
  • Synonym: group - to put together into a group; to arrange together
  • Synonym: to relegate - to assign to a particular class or category, typically a lower one
  • Antonym: to disorganize - to remove from any system of organization or classification

Etymology

From French 'classifier', from 'classe' (class) + '-fier' (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00741667-v
Synset POS: v
