Rameses

1. PROPN

/ ˈræməsiːz / (AmE), / ˈræməsiːz / (BrE)
The name of any of eleven kings who ruled ancient Egypt between 1315 and 1090 BC, especially Rameses II, one of Egypt's most powerful pharaohs.
name of ancient Egyptian kings

Examples

Among ancient Egypt's great leaders, Rameses II is perhaps the most famous for his long reign and impressive monuments.
Scholars debate whether the biblical pharaoh of Moses's time was Rameses II or another king.
The temple at Abu Simbel was constructed during the reign of Rameses II.

Etymology

From Greek Rhamsēs, from Egyptian Ra-ms-sw meaning 'Ra has fashioned him'.
