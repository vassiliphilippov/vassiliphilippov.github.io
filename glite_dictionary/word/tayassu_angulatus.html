Tayassu angulatus

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈæs.u æŋˈɡjuː.lə.təs / (AmE), / taɪˈæs.juː æŋˈɡjuː.lə.təs / (BrE)
a dark grey species of peccary with an indistinct white collar, native to semi-desert areas of Mexico and the southwestern United States
species of wild peccary

Examples

The Tayassu angulatus roams in herds across arid landscapes of Arizona and New Mexico.
Researchers studied the diet of Tayassu angulatus in both desert and forested environments.
Farmers sometimes encounter Tayassu angulatus near their crops, where the animals search for roots and fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collared peccary - alternative common name for Tayassu angulatus

Etymology

From genus name Tayassu (from the indigenous Tupi language) and Latin angulatus (angled or with corners).
