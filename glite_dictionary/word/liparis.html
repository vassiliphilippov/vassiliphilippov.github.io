liparis

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ.pə.rɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɪp.ə.rɪs / (BrE)
A genus of orchids with few leaves and typically small yellow-green or dull purple flowers arranged in terminal racemes.
a genus of small-flowered orchids

Examples

The liparis thrives in damp, shaded woodlands across parts of Asia and North America.
Botanists were excited to discover a rare species of liparis during their rainforest expedition.
Several varieties of liparis are prized by orchid enthusiasts for their subtle, delicate blooms.

Etymology

From Greek 'liparos' meaning 'shiny' or 'oily', referring to the leaves of some species.
