Liparis

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪpərɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɪpərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small marine fishes (snailfishes) in the family Liparidae, found in cold northern waters.
genus of small marine snailfishes

Examples

The genus Liparis includes many species of snailfishes found in northern oceans.
Researchers discovered a new species in the Liparis genus near the Arctic coast.
Liparis is distinguished by its soft, gelatinous body and adapted to deep-sea habitats.

Etymology

From New Latin, derived from Greek 'liparos' (shiny, oily), given to the smooth, slippery appearance of these fishes.

3. NOUN

/ lɪˈper.ɪs / (AmE), / lɪˈpɛː.rɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small marine fishes in the family Liparidae, commonly known as snailfishes, found mostly in cold northern waters.
a genus of small marine snailfishes

Examples

The Liparis species are known for their gelatinous bodies adapted to deep-sea environments.
Researchers discovered a new Liparis specimen off the coast of Alaska.
Many members of the genus Liparis inhabit cold waters near the Arctic Ocean.

Etymology

From Greek *liparos* (oily, greasy), likely referring to the slippery texture of these fishes.
