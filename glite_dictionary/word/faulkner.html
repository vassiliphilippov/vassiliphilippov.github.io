Faulkner

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɔːknɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːknə / (BrE)
(William) Faulkner (1897–1962), American novelist noted for his works set in the American South, exploring themes of history, memory, and the complexity of human experience.
American novelist (1897–1962)

Examples

William Faulkner is widely regarded as one of the greatest American writers of the twentieth century.
Many students analyze Faulkner's use of stream of consciousness in their literature courses.
The novels of Faulkner often explore deep social and psychological themes.

Etymology

Originally Falkner; surname of English origin.
