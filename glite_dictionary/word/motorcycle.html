motorcycle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊt̬ərˌsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈməʊtəˌsaɪkl / (BrE)
A vehicle with two wheels and an engine, typically ridden by one or two people.
two-wheeled motor vehicle

Examples

He rode his motorcycle across the country last summer.
A loud motorcycle passed by the café, startling the customers.
She bought a vintage motorcycle for weekend road trips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bike - a two-wheeled vehicle powered by pedals or a motor
  • Degree Lower: scooter - a light two-wheeled motor vehicle with a low footboard

Etymology

From motor + cycle; patterned after bicycle and motorcar.

2. VERB

/ ˈmoʊtərˌsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈməʊtəˌsaɪkl / (BrE)
to ride a motorcycle, especially as a means of transportation or for recreation
to ride a motorcycle

Examples

She loves to motorcycle along the coastal highways on weekends.
We motorcycled across three states during our summer vacation.
He has been motorcycling since he was a teenager.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ride - to sit on and control the movement of (a bicycle, motorcycle, or horse)

Etymology

From the noun 'motorcycle', itself from 'motor-' and 'bicycle'.

Synset POS: v
