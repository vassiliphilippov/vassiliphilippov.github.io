to maraud

1. VERB

/ məˈrɔd / (AmE), / məˈrɔːd / (BrE)
to roam or raid a place in search of things to steal or people to attack, especially during war or conflict
raid or roam in search of plunder

Examples

Pirates would often maraud along the coastline, seeking ships to attack and treasures to seize.
The villagers feared that bandits would maraud their town at night.
During the war, soldiers began to maraud enemy settlements for food and valuables.

Synset Examples

marauding rebels overran the countryside

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunder - to steal goods, typically using force and in a time of disorder
  • Synonym: loot - to steal goods, especially during war or riot

Etymology

From French 'marauder' (to roam in search of plunder), of uncertain origin.
