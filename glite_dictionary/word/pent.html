pent

1. ADJ

/ pɛnt / (AmE), / pɛnt / (BrE)
closely confined or shut in; unable to escape or be released.
closely confined; shut in

Examples

She kept her feelings pent inside for years, never expressing them aloud.
The animals had been pent up in their cages all winter.
A pent crowd waited anxiously for the doors to open.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confined - restricted in movement or space
  • Antonym: free - not confined or restrained

Etymology

Past participle of 'pen' (to enclose, confine).
