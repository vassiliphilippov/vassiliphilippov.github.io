whisk

1. NOUN

/ wɪsk / (AmE), / wɪsk / (BrE)
a small short-handled broom used for brushing dust or lint from clothes or surfaces
small broom for brushing clothes

Examples

She grabbed a whisk to quickly brush the lint from her jacket.
The old tailor kept a whisk on his counter to dust off customers' clothes.
After gardening, he used a whisk to clean the dirt from his trousers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush - an implement with bristles used for cleaning or painting

3. NOUN

/ wɪsk / (AmE), / wɪsk / (BrE)
a kitchen utensil consisting of a handle and a coil or loop of wires, used for whipping, beating, or stirring ingredients such as eggs or cream
kitchen tool for whipping or beating

Examples

She used a whisk to beat the eggs until they were frothy.
The recipe calls for you to mix the cream with a whisk until it thickens.
He grabbed a whisk from the drawer to stir the sauce quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beater - a kitchen implement with blades or wires used for mixing or beating eggs, cream, etc.

Etymology

Of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse viska (wisp, small bundle).
