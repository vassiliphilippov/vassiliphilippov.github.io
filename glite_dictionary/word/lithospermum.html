Lithospermum

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪθəˈspɜrməm / (AmE), / ˌlɪθəʊˈspɜːməm / (BrE)
A genus of annual or perennial herbaceous or shrubby plants, found almost worldwide except in Australia.
a plant genus of herbaceous or shrubby species

Examples

The roots of Lithospermum were traditionally used in herbal medicine.
Several species of Lithospermum display bright yellow or blue flowers in spring.
Researchers studied the genetic variation among Lithospermum populations from different regions.

Etymology

From Greek lithos (stone) + sperma (seed), referring to the plant’s hard, stony seeds.
