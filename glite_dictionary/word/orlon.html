Orlon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrˌlɑn / (AmE), / ˈɔːlɒn / (BrE)
A synthetic acrylic fiber or the lightweight, crease-resistant fabric made from it, used chiefly in clothing and upholstery.
a type of synthetic acrylic fiber or fabric

Examples

This sweater is made of Orlon, so it's both warm and easy to wash.
Manufacturers often choose Orlon for its resistance to creasing and shrinking.
The jacket blends Orlon with wool for a lightweight yet durable fabric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrylic - a type of synthetic fiber made from acrylic resin

Etymology

Trademark, registered in 1941 by the chemical company DuPont.
