verb

1. NOUN

/ vɝb / (AmE), / vɜːb / (BrE)
a word that expresses an action, an occurrence, or a state and typically serves as the predicate of a sentence
a word class for actions, states, or occurrences

Examples

The word 'run' is a common verb in English.
In the sentence 'She sings,' 'sings' is the verb.
Students learned how to identify the verb in each sentence during the lesson.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noun - a word that refers to a person, place, thing, or idea
  • Antonym: adjective - a word describing a noun

Etymology

From Latin 'verbum' (word).

2. NOUN

/ vɜrb / (AmE), / vɜːb / (BrE)
A word that expresses an action, an occurrence, or a state of being.
word expressing action or state

Examples

Every English sentence must have at least one verb to show what is happening.
In the sentence 'She runs quickly,' 'runs' is the verb.
Students often have trouble identifying the main verb in complex sentences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noun - a word used to identify any of a class of people, places, or things
  • Antonym: adjective - a word that modifies a noun or pronoun, describing a quality or state
  • Synonym: predicate - the part of a sentence or clause containing a verb and stating something about the subject

Etymology

From Latin 'verbum' (word).
