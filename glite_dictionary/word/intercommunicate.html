to intercommunicate

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərkəˈmjunɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkəˈmjuːnɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to exchange thoughts, feelings, or information with each other
to exchange ideas or feelings

Examples

Some animals are able to intercommunicate through subtle sounds and gestures.
The two scientists would often intercommunicate about their research findings late into the night.
In therapy, it is important for both partners to intercommunicate their feelings honestly.

Synset Examples

He communicated his anxieties to the psychiatrist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to communicate - to share or exchange information, news, or ideas

Etymology

From Latin inter- (between) + communicare (to share, to communicate).

2. VERB

/ ˌɪntərkəˈmjuːnɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkəˈmjuːnɪkeɪt / (BrE)
(of rooms, spaces, or systems) to be directly connected so as to allow passage or exchange between them.
be interconnected; allow passage

Examples

The two offices intercommunicate through a hidden doorway.
All the rooms on this floor intercommunicate for easy access during emergencies.
In this design, the library and lounge intercommunicate by means of a sliding partition.

Synset Examples

These rooms intercommunicate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to connect - to join or link together; to be joined
  • Antonym: disconnect - to separate or break the connection

Etymology

From Latin 'intercommunicare' (to communicate mutually), from 'inter-' (between) + 'communicare' (to communicate).

Synset POS: v
