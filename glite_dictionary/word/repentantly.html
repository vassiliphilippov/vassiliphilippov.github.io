repentantly

1. ADV

/ rɪˈpɛntəntli / (AmE), / rɪˈpɛntəntli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows regret or remorse for wrongdoing.
in a remorseful way

Examples

He apologized repentantly for his harsh words.
She looked repentantly at her friend after realizing her mistake.
The student explained himself repentantly to the principal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regretfully - with a feeling of regret
  • Antonym: unrepentantly - in a manner not showing regret or remorse

Etymology

From 'repentant' + '-ly'; 'repentant' from Old French *repentant*, present participle of *repentir* (to repent).
