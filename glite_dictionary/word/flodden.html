Flodden

1. PROPN

/ ˈflɑdən / (AmE), / ˈflɒdən / (BrE)
the Battle of Flodden, fought in 1513 between England and Scotland, in which the English defeated the Scots and King James IV of Scotland was killed
the 1513 battle between England and Scotland

Examples

The defeat at Flodden marked a significant turning point in Scottish history.
Many legends and songs have been inspired by the tragic outcome of Flodden.
James IV of Scotland lost his life at the Battle of Flodden in 1513.

2. PROPN

/ ˈflɑːdən / (AmE), / ˈflɒdən / (BrE)
a hill in Northumberland, England, notable as the site of the Battle of Flodden in 1513, where the Scots were defeated by the English.
hill in Northumberland, site of 1513 battle

Examples

The Battle of Flodden was a turning point in Anglo-Scottish relations.
Historians often visit Flodden to study the site of the 1513 conflict.
Many memorials have been erected near Flodden to commemorate those who fell in battle.
