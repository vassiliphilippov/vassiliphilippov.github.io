comfortably

1. ADV

/ ˈkʌmfərtəbli / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətəbli / (BrE)
In a way that provides enough money for a good standard of living, without financial worry.
with enough money for a good living

Examples

After getting the promotion, he was finally able to live comfortably.
She retired early and is now living comfortably in the countryside.
Thanks to wise investments, they can support their children comfortably.

Synset Examples

They live well
she has been able to live comfortably since her husband died

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affluently - in a wealthy or rich manner
  • Antonym: poorly - with little money or resources

Etymology

From comfortable + -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˈkʌmfərtəbli / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətəbli / (BrE)
in a way that provides physical comfort or ease
in a physically comfortable way

Examples

She settled comfortably into the armchair with her favorite book.
The children slept comfortably throughout the long car ride.
He finished the marathon comfortably ahead of the other runners.

Synset Examples

she could have been lying comfortably in bed getting the same relief

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncomfortably - in a way that lacks comfort or causes bodily discomfort
  • Synonym: cozily - in a warm and comfortable manner

Etymology

Formed from 'comfortable' + '-ly'; 'comfortable' from late Latin *confortare* (to strengthen).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈkʌmfərtəbli / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is free from stress, worry, or difficulty; with mental comfort or ease.
without stress or difficulty

Examples

She passed the exam comfortably without any anxiety.
He now lives comfortably in retirement, enjoying a peaceful life.
The company can comfortably meet its financial obligations this year.

Synset Examples

he works comfortably on three continents

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncomfortably - in a way that causes discomfort or unease
  • Synonym: easily - without difficulty or effort

Etymology

From 'comfortable' + '-ly', where 'comfortable' comes from Old French 'confortable'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00156018-r
Synset POS: r
