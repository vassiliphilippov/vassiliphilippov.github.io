to becharm

1. VERB

/ bɪˈtʃɑrm / (AmE), / bɪˈtʃɑːm / (BrE)
To control or influence by means of magic spells; to enchant or bewitch.
enchant or bewitch by magic

Examples

The wicked sorceress tried to becharm the villagers into obeying her every word.
He believed the old witch had becharmed his mind with her strange incantations.
Legends tell of a flute so powerful it could becharm even the fiercest of beasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bewitch - to cast a spell on and gain control over someone; to enchant
  • Synonym: to enchant - to cast a spell on; to charm or delight as if by magic

Etymology

From prefix 'be-' and 'charm' (from Old French 'charme', from Latin 'carmen' meaning 'song, spell').

2. VERB

/ bɪˈtʃɑrm / (AmE), / bɪˈtʃɑːm / (BrE)
to enchant or attract someone by charm; to captivate or cause to be enamored
to charm or enchant
archaic

Examples

The storyteller sought to becharm his audience with fantastic tales of adventure.
She hoped her beauty would becharm everyone at the royal ball.
It was the melody that seemed to becharm all who heard it, leaving them spellbound.

Synset Examples

She captured all the men's hearts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charm - to attract or delight greatly
  • Synonym: to enchant - to fill with great delight; to charm

Etymology

Formed by prefixing be- to charm, patterned after similar verbs in English.
