reclusiveness

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkluːsɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈkluːsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of preferring to live in seclusion or isolation; the tendency to avoid social contact
preference for seclusion or isolation

Examples

His reclusiveness made it difficult for colleagues to get to know him beyond a professional level.
The author's legendary reclusiveness only added to the intrigue surrounding her novels.
Many see her reclusiveness as a sign of shyness, but she simply values her privacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solitude - the state of being alone
  • Antonym: sociability - the quality of enjoying company

Etymology

From 'reclusive' + '-ness', with 'reclusive' from Latin 'recludere' (to shut up, seclude).
