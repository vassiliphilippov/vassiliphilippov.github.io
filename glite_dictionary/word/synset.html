synset

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈsɪnˌsɛt / (BrE)
a set of one or more words or phrases in a language that are synonyms and share the same meaning, especially as a unit in computational linguistics
set of synonyms (linguistics)

Examples

Researchers use a synset to group words with similar meanings in computational linguistics.
Each synset in WordNet contains synonyms related to a single concept.
She examined the synset to understand the subtle differences among the terms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synonym - a word or phrase that means exactly or nearly the same as another word

Etymology

Blend of 'synonym' and 'set', originally from usage in WordNet.
