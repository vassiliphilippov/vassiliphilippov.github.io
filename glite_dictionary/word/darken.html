to darken

1. VERB

/ ˈdɑrkən / (AmE), / ˈdɑːkən / (BrE)
to make or become dark or darker
make or become darker

Examples

The clouds began to darken the sky as the storm approached.
Please darken the room by closing the curtains before the presentation.
As the evening progressed, the watercolors in the painting seemed to darken.

Synset Examples

darken a room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to lighten - to make or become lighter (in color or brightness)

Etymology

From Old English 'deorcian' (to become dark).

2. VERB

/ ˈdɑrkən / (AmE), / ˈdɑːkən / (BrE)
to become dark or darker in color or light; to make something less bright
become dark or darker

Examples

The sky began to darken as the storm approached.
Her mood seemed to darken after hearing the bad news.
The room darkened when the lights went out.

Synset Examples

The sky darkened

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to lighten - to become lighter or less dark
  • Synonym: dim - to become less bright
Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ ˈdɑrkən / (AmE), / ˈdɑːkən / (BrE)
to spoil, tarnish, or damage (something, especially a reputation or good name)
to tarnish or damage reputation

Examples

The revelations darkened the politician's reputation irreparably.
A single error can darken an otherwise brilliant career.
His involvement in the scandal darkened the company's image for years.

Synset Examples

a scandal that darkened the family's good name

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarnish - make or become less valuable or respected; to damage in reputation
  • Synonym: stain - to damage or spoil the reputation of someone or something
  • Antonym: to restore - to bring back (a reputation) to its original condition; to repair a damaged reputation
Source: WordNet
