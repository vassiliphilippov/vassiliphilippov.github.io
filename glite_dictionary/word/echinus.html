echinus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkaɪnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈkaɪnəs / (BrE)
An ovolo molding positioned between the shaft and the abacus of a Doric column in classical architecture.
ovolo molding of a Doric column

Examples

The capitals were distinguished by the elegantly carved echinus beneath the abacus.
He studied the profile of the echinus to understand its role in classical architecture.
A proper restoration required reshaping the echinus to its original rounded form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ovolo - a convex molding profile used in architecture

Etymology

From Latin *echinus*, from Greek *ekhinos* (sea urchin, hedgehog), referring to the molding's form.
