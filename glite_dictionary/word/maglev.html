maglev

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡ.lɛv / (AmE), / ˈmaɡ.lɛv / (BrE)
A high-speed train that is suspended and propelled by powerful magnets so that it travels above a magnetized track without touching it, eliminating friction.
high-speed train using magnetic levitation

Examples

The new maglev in Shanghai can reach speeds of over 400 kilometers per hour.
Scientists are researching ways to make maglev trains more energy efficient.
Japan's maglev technology has set several world speed records for trains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetic levitation - the process by which an object is suspended with no support other than magnetic fields

Etymology

Shortened form of 'magnetic levitation'.
