Populus heterophylla

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːpjələs ˌhɛtərəˈfaɪlə / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊləs ˌhɛtərəˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A species of North American poplar tree with large rounded scalloped leaves and brownish bark, commonly known as swamp cottonwood.
a North American poplar tree species
medical

Examples

The Populus heterophylla thrives in wet lowland woods and swamps across parts of the eastern United States.
Botanists identified the specimen as Populus heterophylla based on its distinctive leaves and bark.
Many animals use the branches of Populus heterophylla for shelter during the rainy season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swamp cottonwood - common name for Populus heterophylla
  • Synonym: poplar - a type of tall tree in the genus Populus

Etymology

From Latin: Populus (poplar) + heterophylla (with different leaves), referring to the varied leaf shapes.
