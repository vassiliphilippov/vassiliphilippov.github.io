temperate

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛmpərət / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərət / (BrE)
showing moderation or self-restraint in behavior, especially by not being excessive or extreme
showing moderation or self-restraint

Examples

Despite the challenging situation, she remained temperate in her response.
He was known for his temperate habits and moderate lifestyle.
It is wise to be temperate in your comments, even when you feel provoked.

Synset Examples

temperate in his habits
a temperate response to an insult
temperate in his eating and drinking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderate - avoiding extremes; average in amount, intensity, or degree
  • Antonym: extreme - exceeding what is usual or reasonable; far from moderate

Etymology

From Latin temperatus, past participle of temperare (to moderate, regulate).

Examples

3. ADJ

/ ˈtɛmpərət / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərət / (BrE)
(of weather or climate) having moderate temperatures; not subject to extremes of heat or cold.
mild or moderate climate or weather

Examples

The Mediterranean is known for its temperate climate, with warm summers and mild winters.
Many species of plants thrive only in temperate zones where the weather is generally mild.
She moved to a temperate coastal town to escape the harsh winters of the north.

Synset Examples

a temperate region
the temperate zones
temperate plants

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extreme - weather or climate with intense conditions

Etymology

From Latin temperatus (moderate, restrained), past participle of temperare (to moderate).
