downpour

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌpɔr / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌpɔː / (BrE)
A heavy and sudden fall of rain.
a heavy, sudden rain

Examples

We were caught in a sudden downpour on our way home.
The downpour flooded several streets within minutes.
After the downpour, the sky cleared and the sun came out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rainstorm - a storm with heavy rain
  • Degree Lower: drizzle - light rain falling in very fine drops

Etymology

From 'down' + 'pour', meaning 'to pour down'.
