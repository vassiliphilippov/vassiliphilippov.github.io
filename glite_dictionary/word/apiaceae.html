Apiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.piˈeɪ.si.i / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.pɪˈeɪ.siː.iː / (BrE)
A large family of aromatic flowering plants, including celery, carrot, parsley, dill, and others, with flowers typically arranged in umbels.
the parsley or carrot family of plants

Examples

Many herbs used in cooking, such as dill and parsley, belong to the Apiaceae family.
The distinctive umbel-shaped flowers are a common feature of plants in the Apiaceae.
Carrots, a staple root vegetable, are a prominent member of the Apiaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Umbelliferae - older name for the Apiaceae plant family

Etymology

From Latin 'apium' (parsley), with scientific family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌeɪpiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌeɪpɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a large family of plants characterized by umbrella-shaped clusters of small flowers, including parsley, carrot, celery, dill, and related species
plant family including parsley and carrot

Examples

The Apiaceae family includes many common kitchen herbs and vegetables.
Carrots are a well-known member of the Apiaceae.
Gardeners often plant several species from the Apiaceae for their diverse flavors.

Etymology

From the type genus Apium (Latin for 'parsley') + -aceae, a standard suffix for plant families.
