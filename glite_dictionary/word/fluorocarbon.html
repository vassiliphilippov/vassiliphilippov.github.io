fluorocarbon

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʊr.oʊˌkɑr.bən / (AmE), / ˈflɔː.rəʊˌkɑː.bən / (BrE)
a chemical compound in which some or all hydrogen atoms of a hydrocarbon have been replaced by fluorine, often used in refrigeration and aerosol products
halocarbon with fluorine replacing hydrogen

Examples

Many refrigerators used to contain fluorocarbon gases as coolant.
Concerns over ozone depletion led to restrictions on some fluorocarbon compounds.
Scientists are developing new fluorocarbon alternatives to reduce environmental impact.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: chlorofluorocarbon - a class of compounds containing chlorine, fluorine, and carbon, often used in refrigeration

Etymology

From fluorine + hydrocarbon.
