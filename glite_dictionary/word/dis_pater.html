Dis Pater

1. PROPN

/ dɪs ˈpeɪ.tər / (AmE), / dɪs ˈpɑː.tə / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the god of the underworld, regarded as the counterpart of the Greek god Hades.
Roman god of the underworld

Examples

In ancient Roman religion, Dis Pater ruled over the souls of the dead.
Offerings were often made to Dis Pater during rites concerning the underworld.
The temple dedicated to Dis Pater was considered a sacred site by the Romans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hades - Greek god of the underworld

Etymology

From Latin 'Dis Pater' meaning 'Rich Father'; from 'dives' (rich) and 'pater' (father).
