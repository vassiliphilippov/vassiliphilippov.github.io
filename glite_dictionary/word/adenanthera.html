Adenanthera

1. NOUN

/ ˌædəˈnænθərə / (AmE), / ˌædɪˈnanθərə / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical Asian and Pacific trees in the pea family, known for their ornamental seeds and feathery leaves.
genus of tropical Asian and Pacific trees

Examples

The Adenanthera tree is often planted for its bright red seeds and shade.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Adenanthera species.
In some villages, Adenanthera wood is used to make tools and furniture.

Etymology

From Greek 'aden' (gland) + 'anthera' (anther), referring to features of the flower.
