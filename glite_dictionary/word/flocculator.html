flocculator

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɑːkjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈflɒkjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
A device or apparatus used to promote the process of flocculation, especially in water treatment or chemical processes.
apparatus for flocculation
medical

Examples

The laboratory used a flocculator to aid in the separation of suspended particles from water.
Engineers adjusted the flocculator settings to ensure optimal water clarity.
Regular maintenance of the flocculator is essential for effective wastewater treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flocculation - process where particles aggregate into clumps or flocs

Etymology

From 'flocculate' (to form into small clumps) + '-or' (thing that does), modeled after Latin-based agent nouns.
