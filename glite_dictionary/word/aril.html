aril

1. NOUN

/ ˈærɪl / (AmE), / ˈærɪl / (BrE)
A fleshy and often brightly colored outer covering of some seeds that develops from the ovule stalk, partially or entirely enclosing the seed.
fleshy seed covering (botany)
medical

Examples

The bright red aril of the yew seed is attractive to birds.
Each nutmeg seed is surrounded by a fleshy aril known as mace.
The scientist carefully separated the aril from the seed for study.

Etymology

From New Latin arillus, of uncertain origin, possibly from Medieval Latin.
