Physaria

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈzɛəriə / (AmE), / fɪˈzɛəriə / (BrE)
A small genus of western North American herbs in the mustard family, known as bladderpods, similar to those in the genus Lesquerella.
genus of western North American bladderpod herbs

Examples

The rare Physaria species can only be found in certain parts of the Rocky Mountains.
Many botanists study Physaria for its unique bladderpod-shaped fruits.
One Physaria plant stood out with its bright yellow flowers amid the desert landscape.

Etymology

From Greek phusa (bladder) and -aria (a suffix forming nouns), referring to the bladder-like seed pods.

2. NOUN

/ faɪˈzɛəriə / (AmE), / fɪˈzɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of small herbs, native to western North America, commonly known as bladderpods, with yellow flowers and inflated seed pods.
genus of western North American bladderpods

Examples

The rare Physaria plant thrives in the arid regions of the American West.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Physaria genus.
Many species of Physaria have distinctive yellow flowers and inflated pods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lesquerella - a related genus of bladderpods, previously grouped together

Etymology

From Greek phusa (bladder) + -aria (suffix), referring to the shape of the seed pods.
