Mandrillus leucophaeus

1. NOUN

/ mænˈdrɪləs liːˌoʊkoʊˈfiːəs / (AmE), / manˈdrɪləs liːˌuːkəʊˈfiːəs / (BrE)
A large Old World monkey from West Africa, similar to the mandrill but smaller and less brightly colored, commonly known as the drill.
a West African monkey, the drill

Examples

The Mandrillus leucophaeus is native to the rainforests of Cameroon and Nigeria.
Unlike the vibrant mandrill, the Mandrillus leucophaeus has more subdued facial coloring.
Conservation efforts are crucial for the survival of the endangered Mandrillus leucophaeus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drill - a large West African monkey closely related to the mandrill
  • Degree Higher: mandrill - a large, colorful Old World monkey

Etymology

From New Latin: Mandrillus (genus name, from 'mandrill') + leucophaeus (Greek 'leukos', white + 'phaios', dusky).
