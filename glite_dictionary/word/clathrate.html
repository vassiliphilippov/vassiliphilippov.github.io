clathrate

1. ADJ

/ ˈklæθˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈklæθˌreɪt / (BrE)
having a lattice-like structure with regularly spaced holes or openings, resembling a cage or network
latticelike with holes or windows

Examples

The mineral forms a clathrate structure that traps gas molecules within its cavities.
Scientists are researching clathrate hydrates as a potential source of energy.
Certain synthetic polymers exhibit a clathrate pattern at the microscopic level.

Etymology

From Latin clathratus (provided with a lattice), from clathri (lattice, grate).

2. ADJ

/ ˈklæθˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈklæθ.reɪt / (BrE)
designating or relating to a compound in which one component is physically enclosed within the crystal structure of another
compound where one substance is trapped within another's crystal structure

Examples

Many natural gas deposits exist as clathrate hydrates beneath the ocean floor.
Researchers are studying clathrate compounds for their ability to store gases efficiently.
A clathrate structure can trap small molecules within its crystalline lattice.

Etymology

From Latin clathratus (provided with a lattice), from clathri (lattice, grating).
