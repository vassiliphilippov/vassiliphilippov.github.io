exemplariness

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɛmplərɪnəs / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛmplərɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being exemplary; serving as a model or ideal example for others to follow
the quality of being exemplary

Examples

The exemplariness of her leadership inspired the entire team to strive for higher standards.
Historians praise the exemplariness of the code in establishing moral conduct.
Awards are often given in recognition of exemplariness in community service.

Synset Examples

Not so, according to Plutarch: the exemplariness of the Lycurgan constitution was widely acknowledged amongst ancient philosophers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excellence - the quality of being outstanding or extremely good
  • Antonym: mediocrity - the quality of being average or not very good

Etymology

From exemplary + -ness, modeled after the pattern of abstract nouns in English.
