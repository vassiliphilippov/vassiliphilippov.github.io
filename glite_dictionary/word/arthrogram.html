arthrogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrθroʊˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈɑːθrəˌɡræm / (BrE)
an X-ray image of a joint taken after the injection of a contrast medium to allow clearer visualization
X-ray of a joint using contrast medium
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered an arthrogram to diagnose the cause of the patient's persistent shoulder pain.
After reviewing the arthrogram, the radiologist noted significant joint damage.
An arthrogram may reveal ligament tears that are not visible on standard X-rays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arthrography - imaging technique for visualizing joints using contrast medium

Etymology

From Greek 'arthro-' (joint) + '-gram' (something written/drawn).
