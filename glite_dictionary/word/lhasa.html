Lhasa

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɑːsə / (AmE), / ˈlɑːsə / (BrE)
The capital city of Tibet, regarded as a sacred center of Tibetan Buddhism and historically known as the Forbidden City for its former inaccessibility to outsiders.
capital city of Tibet, sacred in Buddhism

Examples

For centuries, Lhasa was off-limits to foreign visitors.
Pilgrims from all over Tibet travel to Lhasa to worship at its holy sites.
The Potala Palace is one of Lhasa's most iconic landmarks.

Etymology

From Tibetan ལྷ་ས་ (lha-sa), meaning 'place of the gods'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɑː.sə / (AmE), / ˈlɑː.sə / (BrE)
a breed of small terrier with a long, heavy coat, originally raised in Tibet as a watchdog
Tibetan terrier breed

Examples

The Lhasa is known for its long, flowing coat and keen sense of hearing.
Many families choose a Lhasa because it makes an excellent apartment dog.
Her Lhasa always barks when someone approaches the door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lhasa Apso - A full name for the Lhasa breed of dog

Etymology

From Lhasa, the capital of Tibet, where the breed originated.
