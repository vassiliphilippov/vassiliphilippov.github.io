noneffervescent

1. ADJ

/ nʌnˌɛfərˈvɛsənt / (AmE), / nʌnˌɛfəˈvɛsənt / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) not producing bubbles, fizz, or sparkling; lacking effervescence.
not producing bubbles or fizz

Examples

Unlike soda, this mineral water is completely noneffervescent and flat.
The chemist chose a noneffervescent solution to avoid unwanted reactions.
Many people prefer noneffervescent tablets because they dissolve more slowly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: effervescent - giving off bubbles; fizzy, lively

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'effervescent' (bubbling, sparkling from Latin 'effervescere').

2. ADJ

/ nʌnˌɛfərˈvɛsənt / (AmE), / nʌnˌɛfəˈvɛsənt / (BrE)
not sparkling; lacking bubbles or fizziness, especially in drinks such as wine or mineral water.
not sparkling; without bubbles (in drinks)

Examples

The menu listed both effervescent and noneffervescent mineral waters.
She prefers noneffervescent wine with her meal, as bubbles upset her stomach.
Requesting a noneffervescent beverage, he ordered still water instead of sparkling.

Synset Examples

a still wine
still mineral water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: still - (of drinks) not sparkling or effervescent
  • Antonym: effervescent - giving off bubbles; sparkling or fizzy

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'non-' (not) + 'effervescent' (boiling up, sparkling).
