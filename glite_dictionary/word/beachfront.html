beachfront

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːtʃˌfrʌnt / (AmE), / ˈbiːtʃˌfrʌnt / (BrE)
A strip of land along the edge of a beach, often developed for residential or commercial use.
strip of land along a beach

Examples

Many luxury hotels are located on the beachfront, offering stunning ocean views.
After a walk along the beachfront, we stopped for ice cream at a seaside café.
The city council plans to improve the beachfront with new parks and benches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterfront - a part of a town or city next to a body of water

Etymology

Formed from 'beach' + 'front'.
