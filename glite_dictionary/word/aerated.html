aerated

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛrˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈeəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a liquid) supplied or infused with carbon dioxide, usually to make it fizzy.
containing carbon dioxide; fizzy

Examples

She prefers aerated water to still water with her meals.
Many soft drinks are aerated to give them a pleasant fizz.
The aerated beverage lost its bubbles after being left open overnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbonated - (of a liquid) charged with carbon dioxide gas
  • Antonym: still - (of a drink) without bubbles or carbonation

Etymology

From 'aerate', from Latin 'aer' (air) + '-ate'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛrˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈeəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a liquid) treated by having air passed or bubbled through it, often to purify or improve its quality.
liquid treated with air for purification

Examples

The company supplies aerated water to restaurants across the city.
After passing through the filter, the aerated liquid is much safer to drink.
We installed an aerated faucet to help reduce impurities in our tap water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbonated - liquid with dissolved carbon dioxide (often fizzy drinks)

Etymology

From Latin 'aer' (air) + English '-ate' (verbal suffix).
