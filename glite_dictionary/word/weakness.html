weakness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːknəs / (AmE), / ˈwiːknɪs / (BrE)
A state in which a currency, market, or financial entity is not strong or stable, often showing lower value or poor performance.
financial instability or lack of strength

Examples

The weakness of the euro led to increased import prices across Europe.
Investors were concerned about the weakness in the country's banking sector.
Recent weakness in the stock market has prompted many to reconsider their portfolios.

Synset Examples

the weakness of the dollar against the yen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strength - the quality or state of being strong, especially financially

2. NOUN

/ ˈwiknəs / (AmE), / ˈwiːknəs / (BrE)
a strong liking for something, even if it may be unwise or harmful
fondness for something, often unwise

Examples

She always had a weakness for sweet pastries and could never say no to dessert.
His weakness for gambling sometimes got him into trouble.
Despite being on a diet, her weakness is still potato chips.

Synset Examples

he has a weakness for chocolate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penchant - strong or habitual liking for something
  • Synonym: soft spot - a special liking for someone or something

3. NOUN

/ ˈwiːknəs / (AmE), / ˈwiːknəs / (BrE)
the state or condition of lacking power or ability to act; a lack of strength or effectiveness
lack of power or ability to act

Examples

Despite their weakness, the activists continued to push for change.
The team’s weakness became apparent when their leader resigned.
His weakness prevented him from making a decision at a crucial moment.

Synset Examples

in spite of their weakness the group remains active

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerlessness - lack of power or ability to control or influence
  • Antonym: strength - the quality or state of being physically strong or effective

Etymology

From Old English wēacness (weakness), from wēac (weak) + -ness.
