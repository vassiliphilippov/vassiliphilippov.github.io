to opacify

1. VERB

/ oʊˈpæsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / əʊˈpæsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
(of a material or tissue) to become or make opaque; to lose transparency or cause transparency to be lost.
become or make opaque
medical

Examples

If the liquid is left standing for too long, it may opacify and lose its usefulness.
The surgeon explained that scar tissue could opacify the lens after the operation.
Aging sometimes causes the cornea to gradually opacify over the years.

Synset Examples

the tissue in the eye's cornea may opacify and the patient may go blind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloud - to make less clear or transparent
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make (something) clearer or more transparent

Etymology

From Latin 'opacus' (shaded, dark), combined with '-fy' (to make).

2. VERB

/ oʊˈpæsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / əʊˈpæsɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to make or become opaque; to cause to lose transparency or translucency
make or become opaque

Examples

The laboratory technician will opacify the solution before the next stage of the experiment.
X-rays can reveal whether cataracts have opacified the patient's lens.
To ensure privacy, they decided to opacify the office windows.

Synset Examples

The glass was opacified for greater privacy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to clarify - to make clear or transparent

Etymology

From French 'opacifier', from 'opaque'.
