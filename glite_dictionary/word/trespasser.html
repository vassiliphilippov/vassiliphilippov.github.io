trespasser

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛsˌpæsər / (AmE), / ˈtrɛspəsə / (BrE)
A person who enters someone's land or property without permission.
person who enters property without permission

Examples

The sign warned that any trespasser would be prosecuted.
Police arrested a trespasser who entered the building after hours.
The landowner set up fences to deter trespassers from crossing his fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intruder - person who enters a place without permission
  • Synonym: invader - someone who enters by force in order to conquer

Etymology

From Old French 'trespasser' (to pass beyond), from 'tres-' (across) + 'passer' (to pass).
