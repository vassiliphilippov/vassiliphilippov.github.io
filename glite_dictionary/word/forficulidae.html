Forficulidae

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈfɪkjʊlɪˌdi / (AmE), / fɔːˈfɪkjʊlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of insects comprising the typical earwigs, distinguished by their forceps-like cerci and elongated bodies
family of typical earwigs

Examples

The Forficulidae family is recognized by their distinctive pincers on the abdomen.
Biologists often study Forficulidae to understand insect social behavior.
Most common earwigs found in gardens belong to the Forficulidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earwig - an insect with pincers at the end of its abdomen

Etymology

From New Latin Forficula (type genus) + -idae (standard zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ fɔrˌfɪkjʊˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / fɔːˌfɪkjʊˈlɪdɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of insects comprising the typical earwigs, distinguished by their elongated bodies and forceps-like pincers.
family of typical earwigs

Examples

The Forficulidae are widespread and commonly found in gardens and under stones.
Researchers studied several species within the Forficulidae to understand their social behavior.
Members of the Forficulidae family use their forceps-like cerci for defense and mating displays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earwig - a small insect with pincers on its abdomen

Etymology

From the genus name Forficula, from Latin 'forfex' (scissors).
