amphitropous

1. ADJ

/ ˌæmfɪˈtroʊpəs / (AmE), / ˌamfɪˈtrəʊpəs / (BrE)
(of a plant ovule) partly inverted so that it is turned back about 90 degrees on its stalk.
partly inverted ovule in plants
medical

Examples

The amphitropous ovule can be distinguished by its characteristic curved shape.
Botanists observed that many species in this family possess amphitropous ovules.
The structure of the seed depends on whether the ovule is amphitropous or of another type.

Etymology

From Greek 'amphi' (both, around) and 'tropos' (a turning).
