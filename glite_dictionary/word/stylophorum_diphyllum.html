Stylophorum diphyllum

1. NOUN

/ staɪ.loʊˈfɔːr.əm dɪˈfɪl.əm / (AmE), / staɪ.ləˈfɔː.rəm dɪˈfɪl.əm / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to the woodlands of the eastern United States, recognized by its yellow flowers and lobed leaves.
North American woodland herb with yellow flowers

Examples

The woodland floor was dotted with the bright yellow blooms of Stylophorum diphyllum.
Many gardeners cultivate Stylophorum diphyllum for its ornamental value and native appeal.
In early spring, Stylophorum diphyllum adds a splash of color to shaded gardens.

Etymology

From Greek 'stylos' (pillar) and 'phoros' (bearing), referring to the style of the flower; 'diphyllum' from Greek 'di-' (two) and 'phyllon' (leaf), describing its paired leaves.
