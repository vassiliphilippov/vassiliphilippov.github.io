scombroid

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑm.brɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈskɒm.brɔɪd / (BrE)
any marine fish of the family Scombridae (such as mackerels and tunas), found in tropical and temperate seas and valued as food and for sport
a marine fish of the mackerel or tuna family

Examples

Some species of scombroid can maintain body temperatures above that of the surrounding water.
The scombroid catch this season included several large tunas and mackerels.
Scombroid poisoning can occur after consuming spoiled scombroid fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuna - large, fast-swimming scombroid fish valued as food
  • Synonym: mackerel - a common scombroid fish of the genus Scomber

Etymology

From Modern Latin Scombridae (the mackerel family), from Latin scomber (mackerel), from Greek skombros.
