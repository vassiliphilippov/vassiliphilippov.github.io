Caesalpinia sepiaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌsizælˈpɪniə ˌsɛpiˈɛriə / (AmE), / ˌsiːzælˈpɪnɪə ˌsɛpɪˈɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A spreading thorny shrub native to tropical Asia, bearing large upright clusters of yellow flowers marked with red.
thorny Asian shrub with yellow-red flowers

Examples

The dense growth of Caesalpinia sepiaria provides excellent shelter for birds.
Gardeners appreciate Caesalpinia sepiaria for its vibrant yellow flowers and ornamental value.
In some regions, Caesalpinia sepiaria is planted as a hedge to repel livestock.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Caesalpinia', after Italian botanist Andrea Cesalpino, and species 'sepiaria', meaning 'of a hedge'.
