brilliance

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪliəns / (AmE), / ˈbrɪljəns / (BrE)
the quality of being magnificent, splendid, or grand in appearance or effect
magnificence or splendour

Examples

The palace's brilliance impressed all who attended the royal ball.
Critics praised the brilliance of the production's set design and costumes.
The artist's use of color added a sense of brilliance to the gallery room.

Synset Examples

for magnificence and personal service there is the Queen's hotel
his ‘Hamlet’ lacks the brilliance that one expects
it is the university that gives the scene its stately splendor
an imaginative mix of old-fashioned grandeur and colorful art
advertisers capitalize on the grandness and elegance it brings to their products

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splendor - magnificent and splendid appearance; grandeur
  • Synonym: grandeur - impressive beauty or scale
  • Antonym: mediocrity - the quality of being average or unremarkable

Etymology

From French *brillance* (brightness, shine), from *briller* (to shine).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪl.jəns / (AmE), / ˈbrɪl.jəns / (BrE)
exceptional intelligence or outstanding mental ability
exceptional intelligence

Examples

Her brilliance impressed everyone at the conference.
The chess champion's brilliance was obvious throughout the tournament.
Few could match the brilliance of his innovative ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genius - exceptional intellectual or creative power
  • Antonym: stupidity - lack of intelligence or mental ability

Etymology

From French *brillance* (shining), from *briller* (to shine).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪljəns / (AmE), / ˈbrɪl.jəns / (BrE)
a very bright light, especially one that is much brighter than what the eyes are accustomed to
intense brightness of light

Examples

The brilliance of the midday sun made it hard to see the road ahead.
A sudden brilliance shone through the window, lighting up the entire room.
He shielded his eyes from the brilliance of the stadium floodlights.

Synset Examples

a glare of sunlight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glare - harsh, dazzling light that is difficult to look at

Etymology

From French *brillance*, from *briller* (to shine), ultimately from Italian *brillare* (to sparkle).
