singleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of focusing with complete commitment on one aim or objective
quality of devoted single-minded focus

Examples

Her singleness of purpose inspired the whole team to strive for their goal.
He approached the challenge with remarkable singleness of mind, ignoring all distractions.
The singleness with which she pursued justice impressed everyone around her.

Synset Examples

his singleness of purpose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: single-mindedness - the quality of being determined to achieve a single aim

Etymology

From Middle English singlenes, from single + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡl̩nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl̩nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being sincere and undivided, without hypocrisy or pretense
sincerity; lack of hypocrisy

Examples

Her singleness of heart was evident in every action she took.
He faced the task with singleness of purpose, never wavering from his goal.
The singleness of her intentions made her trustworthy among her peers.

Synset Examples

the singleness of his motives could not be questioned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sincerity - the absence of pretense or hypocrisy
  • Antonym: hypocrisy - the practice of claiming beliefs or feelings one does not possess

Etymology

From Middle English singlenes, from single + -ness.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04874931-n
Synset POS: n
