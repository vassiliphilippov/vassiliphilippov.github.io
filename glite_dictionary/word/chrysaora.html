Chrysaora

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˈseɪərə / (AmE), / kraɪˈseɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of jellyfish in the family Pelagiidae, commonly known as sea nettles, found in oceans worldwide.
genus of jellyfish (sea nettles)

Examples

The tentacles of Chrysaora deliver a painful sting to swimmers who get too close.
Scientists observed several species of Chrysaora during the marine expedition.
The aquarium features a stunning display of Chrysaora in its jellyfish exhibit.

Etymology

From Greek khrusos (gold), referring to the golden color of some species.

a genus of jellyfish (sea nettles)

Examples

The Chrysaora genus includes several species of sea nettles found in oceans worldwide.
Researchers observed that Chrysaora jellyfish are responsible for many stings along the Atlantic coast.
The aquarium features a display of Chrysaora, highlighting their graceful tentacles and distinctive coloration.

Etymology

From Greek *chrysos* (gold), referring to the gold color of some species.
