acuminate

1. ADJ

/ əˈkjuː.mə.nət / (AmE), / əˈkjuː.mɪ.nət / (BrE)
(of a leaf) tapering gradually to a slender, pointed tip.
narrowing to a slender point (leaf shape)
medical

Examples

The acuminate leaves of this plant help distinguish it from similar species.
Many tropical trees are identified by their acuminate leaf-tips, which channel water away.
The botanist noted the acuminate shape of the specimen's foliage during her field study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acute - narrowing to a sharp point (as of a leaf)

Etymology

From Latin acuminatus (pointed), from acuminare (to sharpen), from acumen (a point).

2. VERB

/ əˈkjuːmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / əˈkjuːmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to make sharp or taper to a point; to shape something into a pointed form
make sharp; taper to a point

Examples

The craftsman skillfully acuminated the wooden stick until it formed a perfect spear point.
Many leaves acuminate at the tip, giving them a distinctive sharp appearance.
Using the knife, she carefully acuminated the end of the pencil for easier writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sharpen - to make or become sharp or sharper

Etymology

From Latin acuminatus, past participle of acuminare (to sharpen to a point), from acumen (point, sharpness).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
