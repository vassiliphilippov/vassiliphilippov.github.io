cultivator

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌltɪˌveɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkʌltɪveɪtə / (BrE)
a farm implement used to break up and aerate the soil, control weeds, and conserve moisture
agricultural soil-tilling tool

Examples

The farmer used a cultivator to loosen the soil before planting the seeds.
Modern cultivators make it easier to manage weeds between rows of crops.
After the rain, he took the cultivator out to break up the compacted ground.

Etymology

From Latin *cultivare* (to till), via Medieval Latin *cultivator*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌltɪˌveɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkʌltɪveɪtə / (BrE)
a person who is involved in the science, art, or business of cultivating the soil, especially for growing crops or gardening
person who cultivates soil

Examples

The cultivator carefully prepared the soil before planting the new crop.
Many successful harvests depend on the knowledge and dedication of the cultivator.
In ancient societies, the cultivator played a crucial role in community survival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmer - a person who owns or manages a farm
  • Synonym: gardener - a person who tends and cultivates a garden as a pastime or for a living

Etymology

From Latin 'cultivare' (to till, to cultivate).
