strawflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔːˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈstrɔːˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A plant of the southern and southeastern United States, cultivated for its yellow flowers that retain their color and shape when dried.
plant grown for its dryable yellow flowers

Examples

She decorated the kitchen with a vase full of strawflowers to brighten the room.
Strawflower arrangements last much longer than fresh bouquets because the blooms dry so well.
Gardeners in the South often grow strawflowers for use in crafts and dried flower displays.

Etymology

From 'straw' (referring to the texture of the dried flower) and 'flower'.
