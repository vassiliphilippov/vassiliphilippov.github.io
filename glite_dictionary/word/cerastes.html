cerastes

1. NOUN

/ səˈræstiːz / (AmE), / sɪˈrɑːstiːz / (BrE)
A highly venomous viper found in northern Africa and southwestern Asia, characterized by a horny spine above each eye.
venomous viper of northern Africa and southwestern Asia

Examples

The desert-dwelling cerastes is well adapted to its arid environment with its distinctive horn-like scales.
Researchers carefully observed a cerastes as it slithered across the sand dunes of the Sahara.
The venom of the cerastes makes it one of the most dangerous snakes in its range.

Etymology

From Latin *cerastes*, from Ancient Greek *kerastēs* (horned), referring to the horn-like spines above the eyes.
