hassock

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæsək / (AmE), / ˈhæsək / (BrE)
A thick, firm cushion used as a seat or footstool.
thick cushion for seat or footstool

Examples

She placed a hassock by the fireplace for extra seating during the party.
He propped his feet on a comfortable hassock while reading his favorite book.
The ornate hassock matched the rest of the living room decor perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ottoman - upholstered seat or footstool without arms or back

Etymology

From Middle English *hassok* (tuft of grass), of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæsək / (AmE), / ˈhæsək / (BrE)
A thick cushion used for kneeling, especially when praying in church.
cushion for kneeling (esp. in church)

Examples

She knelt on the hassock during the morning prayer service.
The church aisle was lined with embroidered hassocks for the congregation.
He donated a beautifully crafted hassock to the chapel in memory of his grandmother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kneeler - A piece of furniture or cushion for kneeling, especially in a church

Etymology

From Middle English *hassok* (tussock of grass).
