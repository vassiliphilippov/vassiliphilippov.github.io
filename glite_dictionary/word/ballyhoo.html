ballyhoo

1. VERB

/ ˈbæliˌhu / (AmE), / ˈbæliˌhuː / (BrE)
to publicize or praise something loudly, noisily, or with exaggeration, often to attract attention
promote noisily or exaggeratedly

Examples

The new movie was ballyhooed all over the media before its release.
The company ballyhooed its latest smartphone with flashy advertisements and events.
Critics complained that the festival was ballyhooed as a cultural breakthrough, but failed to deliver on its promises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to advertise - to make something known to the public, usually in order to sell it
  • Synonym: to promote - to support or actively encourage
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important or significant

Etymology

Possibly from American English circus slang (early 20th century), origin uncertain; may relate to the term for noisy commotion.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæliˌhu / (AmE), / ˈbæliˌhuː / (BrE)
blatant or sensational promotion or publicity, especially when exaggerated or noisy.
loud or flashy publicity

Examples

The new movie's release was accompanied by a great deal of ballyhoo in the media.
Despite the ballyhoo surrounding the product launch, sales remained disappointing.
He was tired of all the ballyhoo and preferred to focus on the work itself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hype - excessive or exaggerated publicity
  • Synonym: publicity - notice or attention given to someone or something by the media
  • Antonym: understatement - the presentation of something as being smaller or less important than it is

Etymology

American English, possibly from circus slang; exact origin uncertain.
