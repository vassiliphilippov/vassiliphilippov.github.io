cryptorchidism

1. NOUN

/ krɪpˈtɔr.kɪˌdɪz.əm / (AmE), / krɪpˈtɔː.kɪˌdɪz.əm / (BrE)
A medical condition in which one or both testes fail to descend into the scrotum during fetal development.
failure of testes to descend
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the infant with cryptorchidism during a routine examination.
Treatment for cryptorchidism may involve surgery if the testis does not descend on its own.
Cryptorchidism increases the risk of fertility problems later in life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undescended testicle - a testicle that has not moved into the scrotum as normal

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden) + orchis (testicle) + -ism (condition).
