waterloo

1. NOUN

/ ˌwɔt̬ɚˈlu / (AmE), / ˌwɔːtəˈluː / (BrE)
a decisive or devastating defeat, especially one that marks the end of someone's success or ambitions
decisive final defeat

Examples

Many companies have met their waterloo after failing to adapt to new technology.
The team's undefeated streak came to a waterloo in the championship game.
She feared this project might be her professional waterloo if it failed.

Synset Examples

he met his waterloo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeat - loss in a battle or contest

Etymology

From the Battle of Waterloo (1815), where Napoleon was decisively defeated.
