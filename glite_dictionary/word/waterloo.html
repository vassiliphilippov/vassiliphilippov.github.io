Waterloo

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɔt̬.ɚˌluː / (AmE), / ˌwɔː.təˈluː / (BrE)
the decisive battle fought on 18 June 1815 near Waterloo in Belgium, where British and Prussian forces defeated Napoleon Bonaparte's French army, effectively ending his rule as Emperor of the French
battle in 1815 that ended Napoleon's rule

Examples

The Battle of Waterloo marked the end of Napoleon's empire.
Many historians consider Waterloo to be a turning point in European history.
Tourists often visit the site of Waterloo to learn about the famous battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stalingrad - decisive battle in World War II (1942-43) in the Soviet Union

Etymology

Named after Waterloo, a village in Belgium where the battle took place.

2. PROPN

/ ˈwɔtərˌlu, ˌwɔtərˈlu / (AmE), / ˈwɔːt.ə.luː / (BrE)
a town in central Belgium, famous as the site of Napoleon's final defeat in 1815
site of Napoleon's defeat, Belgium (1815)

Examples

After years of success, the company met its Waterloo when the new product failed.
Historians often refer to Waterloo as the battle that changed European history.
Many tourists visit Waterloo to see the famous battlefield where Napoleon was defeated.
