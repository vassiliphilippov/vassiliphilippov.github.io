damselfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæmsəlˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdæmslˌflʌɪ / (BrE)
A slender, non-stinging insect similar to but smaller than the dragonfly, typically resting with its wings folded together.
slender insect similar to a dragonfly

Examples

We watched a bright blue damselfly hover over the pond in the summer sun.
Unlike a dragonfly, a resting damselfly keeps its wings together above its body.
The children were fascinated by the delicate structure of the damselfly's wings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragonfly - large predatory insect of the order Odonata

Etymology

From 'damsel' (young woman) + 'fly', probably referring to its delicate form.
