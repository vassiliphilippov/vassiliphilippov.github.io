countersink

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntɚˌsɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəsɪŋk / (BrE)
A cone-shaped hole in a material, especially wood, with the top part enlarged to allow the head of a screw or bolt to sit flush or below the surface.
hole with enlarged top for screw or bolt head

Examples

The carpenter drilled a countersink for the screw to sit flush with the surface.
Be sure to use a countersink before inserting the bolt.
Each hole had a countersink to allow the screw heads to be hidden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countersink - to create a countersink; the act of making such a hole

Etymology

From 'counter-' (against, opposite) + 'sink', reflecting the sinking of a screw head below the surface.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌsɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəsɪŋk / (BrE)
A tool or drill bit for enlarging the upper part of a hole to allow the head of a screw to sit flush with or below the surface.
tool for enlarging hole for screw head

Examples

He used a countersink to prepare the hole for the screw head.
Make sure to choose the correct countersink size for the hardware you're using.
This toolkit comes with a high-quality countersink for finishing woodwork projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countersink - to enlarge the upper part of a hole (verb)
  • Synonym: reamer - tool for widening or smoothing a hole

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) and 'sink' (to go down), referring to making a recess for a screw or bolt head.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈkaʊntərˌsɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəsɪŋk / (BrE)
to insert a nail, screw, or bolt so that its head is below or level with the surface, often by enlarging the opening with a conical cut
set a screw or nail below a surface

Examples

He carefully countersank each screw to create a smooth, even surface on the wood.
Be sure to countersink the bolts so they do not catch on passing objects.
They countersink all the nails before applying the finish to the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recess - to make a hollow space so something fits flush or inside

Etymology

From counter- (against, opposite) + sink; originally referred to the conical hole cut for a screw head.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
