roadblock

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊdˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdˌblɒk / (BrE)
an obstacle or condition that prevents progress or makes achieving an objective difficult
an obstacle to progress or achievement

Examples

Financial difficulties became a serious roadblock in launching the project on time.
Cultural misunderstandings can often act as a roadblock to international cooperation.
He refused to let failure become a roadblock to his future success.

Synset Examples

intolerance is a barrier to understanding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress
  • Synonym: hindrance - something that provides resistance or delay

Etymology

From 'road' + 'block', originally meaning a physical barrier across a road.

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊdˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdˌblɒk / (BrE)
a barrier erected, especially by police, to stop or control traffic on a road, usually to inspect vehicles or catch a suspect
barrier set up to stop/control road traffic

Examples

The police set up a roadblock to catch the escaping suspects.
Protesters created a makeshift roadblock to stop traffic entering the city center.
We were delayed for an hour because of a roadblock on the highway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barricade - a barrier across a street or road, often to block movement
  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress

Etymology

Formed from 'road' + 'block', indicating a blockage on a road.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04103942-n
Synset POS: n
