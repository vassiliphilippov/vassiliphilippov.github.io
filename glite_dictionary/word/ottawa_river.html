Ottawa River

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑtəwə ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒtəwə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in southeastern Canada, forming part of the boundary between Quebec and Ontario before joining the Saint Lawrence River near Montreal
major river in southeastern Canada

Examples

The Ottawa River is known for its beautiful scenery and abundant wildlife.
Several towns and cities have developed along the banks of the Ottawa River.
In spring, the Ottawa River often experiences flooding due to melting snow.

Etymology

From Algonquin Odawa, the name of the Indigenous people after whom the river is named.
