Aythya

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪθiə / (AmE), / ˈaɪθiə / (BrE)
A genus of diving ducks including the canvasback, redhead, and pochard.
genus of diving ducks

Examples

The genus Aythya includes well-known species such as the canvasback and the pochard.
Scientists study Aythya to understand migration patterns of diving ducks.
Field guides often have a separate section for Aythya species due to their unique behaviors.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek aithuia (a seabird, perhaps a duck or diver). Used in scientific taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪθiə / (AmE), / ˈaɪθɪə / (BrE)
A genus of diving ducks in the family Anatidae, including species such as the canvasback, redhead, and pochard.
a genus of diving ducks

Examples

The canvasback, a species in the genus Aythya, is renowned for its distinctive sloping head.
Biologists studied several species within the Aythya genus to understand diving behavior.
The redhead is a North American duck belonging to the genus Aythya.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pochard - a type of diving duck in the genus Aythya
  • Degree Higher: duck - a waterbird with a broad blunt bill, member of the family Anatidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek aithuia (a kind of seabird).
