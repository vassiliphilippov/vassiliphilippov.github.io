algin

1. NOUN

/ ˈældʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈældʒɪn / (BrE)
a viscous substance obtained from seaweed, used especially as a thickener or emulsifier in food and industrial products
seaweed-derived thickener or emulsifier
medical

Examples

Ice cream often contains algin to improve its texture and stability.
The laboratory ordered a supply of algin for their experiments in food science.
Algin is extracted from certain types of brown seaweed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alginic acid - a compound from seaweed used to make algin
  • Synonym: agar - a gelatinous substance from seaweed, used in foods and labs

Etymology

Shortened from 'alginic acid', from Latin 'alga' (seaweed).
