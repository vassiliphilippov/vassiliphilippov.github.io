psychopharmacological

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪkoʊˌfɑːrməˈkɑːlədʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˌfɑːməkəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the study or use of drugs that affect the mind or behavior
relating to drugs affecting the mind
medical

Examples

The new psychopharmacological treatment showed promising results in the clinical trial.
She specializes in psychopharmacological research involving antidepressant medications.
Advances in psychopharmacological therapies have improved outcomes for many psychiatric conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychopharmacology - the study of the effects of drugs on the mind and behavior

Etymology

From psycho- (mind) + pharmacological (relating to drugs)
