conquering

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋkərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋkərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of gaining control of a place or people by military force
the act of taking control by force

Examples

The conquering of new territories was the king’s primary ambition.
Her conquering of her fears inspired others to face their own challenges.
Historians debate the motives behind the conquering of the ancient world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victory - success in defeating an opponent or enemy
  • Antonym: defeat - the act of overcoming someone in battle or contest

Etymology

From Middle English 'conqueren', from Old French 'conquerre', from Latin 'conquaerere'.
