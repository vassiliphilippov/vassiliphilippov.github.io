hiccup

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪkʌp / (AmE), / ˈhɪkʌp / (BrE)
An involuntary spasm of the diaphragm and respiratory organs, often recurring, causing a sharp, sudden sound from the closure of the glottis.
involuntary spasm causing sudden sound

Examples

After eating too quickly, he suddenly started getting hiccups.
Drinking water slowly can sometimes help stop hiccups.
She tried holding her breath to get rid of the annoying hiccups.

Synset Examples

how do you cure the hiccups?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belch - an expulsion of gas from the stomach through the mouth

Etymology

Imitative in origin, formed from the sound of the spasm (variant of earlier 'hickop').

2. VERB

/ ˈhɪkˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈhɪkʌp / (BrE)
to make a sudden, involuntary spasm of the diaphragm followed by a quick closure of the vocal cords, producing a characteristic sound
make a spasm causing a hic sound

Examples

Whenever I eat too quickly, I sometimes hiccup for several minutes afterward.
She tried to hold her breath to stop herself from hiccuping during the meeting.
He hiccuped so loudly that everyone in the room turned to look at him.

Synset Examples

When you have to hiccup, drink a glass of cold water

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced, first recorded in Middle English.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
