weakened

1. ADJ

/ ˈwiːkənd / (AmE), / ˈwiːkənd / (BrE)
made weak or less strong; having lost strength or effectiveness.
made weak or less strong

Examples

After the surgery, her weakened state required several weeks of rest.
The building's weakened foundation posed a serious safety risk.
His weakened immune system made him more susceptible to infections.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strong - having great physical power or ability
  • Synonym: weak - lacking strength or power

2. ADJ

/ ˈwikənd / (AmE), / ˈwiːkənd / (BrE)
made less strong or effective; impaired in strength, quality, or condition.
impaired or made less strong

Examples

After the accident, his weakened arm needed months of physical therapy.
The storm left the building's structure weakened and unsafe.
She felt weakened by the long illness but determined to recover.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strengthened - made stronger or more effective
  • Synonym: to weaken - to make or become less strong

Etymology

Past participle of 'weaken', from Old Norse veikja (to weaken).
