sizzling

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪz.əl.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪz.əl.ɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely hot, as if burning or producing a hissing sound.
extremely hot; burning

Examples

The sizzling pavement made it unbearable to walk barefoot in the afternoon sun.
They served the sizzling fajitas straight from the grill.
During the sizzling summer, the city often felt like an oven.

Synset Examples

a sizzling steak
a sizzling spell of weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorching - extremely hot
  • Antonym: freezing - extremely cold

Etymology

Present participle of 'sizzle', from imitative origin representing the hissing sound of something frying or burning.

/ ˈsɪzlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪzlɪŋ / (BrE)
full of intense excitement, emotion, or interest; very lively or passionate
full of intense excitement or passion

Examples

His sizzling performance kept the audience on the edge of their seats.
The sizzling debate over the issue drew national attention.
She wrote a sizzling review that captured everyone's imagination.

Synset Examples

a red-hot speech
sizzling political issues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exciting - causing great enthusiasm and eagerness
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From the verb 'sizzle', imitative of the sound of frying or hissing.
