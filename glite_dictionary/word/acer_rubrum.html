Acer rubrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪsər ˈruːbrəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪsə ˈruːbrəm / (BrE)
A species of maple native to eastern and central North America, known for its five-lobed leaves that turn red or yellow in autumn.
red maple tree of North America

Examples

In the fall, the Acer rubrum displays brilliant red foliage throughout the forest.
Botanists studied the leaf patterns of Acer rubrum to better understand seasonal color changes.
Gardeners often plant Acer rubrum for its attractive autumn colors and shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red maple - Common name for Acer rubrum, a North American maple tree

Etymology

From Latin: 'Acer' meaning 'maple' and 'rubrum' meaning 'red'.
