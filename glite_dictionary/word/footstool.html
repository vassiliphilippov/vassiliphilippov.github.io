footstool

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌstuːl / (AmE), / ˈfʊt.stuːl / (BrE)
A low stool or support for resting the feet of a person while seated.
a low stool for resting one's feet

Examples

She placed her tired feet on the footstool after a long day at work.
The old leather footstool matched the armchair perfectly in the living room.
He bought a small footstool to go with his office chair for extra comfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ottoman - a padded seat or footstool without a back or arms

Etymology

From 'foot' (Old English 'fōt') + 'stool' (Old English 'stol').
