Carduus

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈduː.əs / (AmE), / kɑːˈdjuː.əs / (BrE)
a genus of annual or perennial prickly thistles native to the Old World, commonly found in Europe, Asia, and North Africa
genus of prickly thistles

Examples

Many species within Carduus are considered noxious weeds in certain regions.
The field was dominated by Carduus plants, their spiny leaves deterring grazing animals.
Botanists often study Carduus to understand the spread of invasive thistle species.

Etymology

From Latin 'carduus' meaning 'thistle'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.duːs / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.djʊs / (BrE)
A genus of annual or perennial prickly thistles native to the Old World, commonly known as plumeless thistles.
genus of Old World prickly thistles

Examples

Several species within the Carduus genus are considered invasive in North America.
The botanist collected samples of Carduus for her research on native thistles.
Some types of Carduus can be recognized by their sharp spines and purple flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'carduus' meaning 'thistle'.
