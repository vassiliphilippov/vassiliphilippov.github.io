democratism

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɑːkrəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒkrəˌtɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory, system, or principles of democracy; advocacy or support for democratic forms of government.
theory or principles of democracy

Examples

His political philosophy was rooted in democratism and the belief in popular sovereignty.
The rise of democratism challenged centuries of authoritarian rule in the region.
Some critics argue that pure democratism can lead to instability in newly formed governments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: democracy - government by the people or their elected representatives
  • Antonym: autocracy - a system of government by one person with absolute power

Etymology

Formed from 'democracy' + '-ism', a suffix denoting doctrine or system.
