existential

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛɡ.zɪˈstɛn.ʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɛɡ.zɪˈstɛn.ʃəl / (BrE)
relating to existence or to existentialism, especially as a philosophical movement concerned with individual freedom and choice
relating to existentialism or existence

Examples

Many novels from the 20th century explore existential themes influenced by philosophers like Sartre and Camus.
He faced an existential crisis when questioning the meaning of his own life.
The film presents an existential dilemma about freedom and responsibility.

Synset Examples

an existential moment of choice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: existentialism - a philosophical theory emphasizing individual existence, freedom, and choice

Etymology

From French 'existentiel', from Latin 'existentia' (existence).

relating to existence or the experience of existing, especially as a philosophical concept

Examples
