carack

1. NOUN

/ kəˈræk / (AmE), / kəˈræk / (BrE)
A large, often heavily-armed merchant ship used in the Mediterranean, particularly during the 15th to 17th centuries.
large Mediterranean merchant ship

Examples

The carack was loaded with spices bound for Venice.
Pirates often targeted a heavily-laden carack on its journey across the Mediterranean.
In the 16th century, a carack was among the largest ships afloat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: galleon - a large sailing ship used especially by Spain from the 15th to 18th centuries

Etymology

From Old French 'caraque', from Spanish or Portuguese 'carraca', likely of Arabic or Italian origin.
