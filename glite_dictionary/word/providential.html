providential

1. ADJ

/ ˌprɑːvəˈdɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌprɒvɪˈdɛnʃəl / (BrE)
resulting from or seeming to result from divine providence; occurring as if by God's intervention
resulting from divine providence

Examples

The family saw their rescue as nothing short of providential.
Her arrival at the moment of crisis seemed entirely providential to everyone present.
They believed that the discovery of water in the desert was a providential sign.

Synset Examples

providential care
a providential visitation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortuitous - happening by chance, often in a lucky way

Etymology

From Latin 'providentialis', from 'providentia' (foreseeing, providence).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01183907-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌproʊ.vɪˈdɛn.ʃəl / (AmE), / ˌprɒ.vɪˈden.ʃəl / (BrE)
Happening as if by divine intervention; relating to or resulting from providence (the protective care of God or of nature).
occurring by divine intervention; relating to providence

Examples

His discovery of the lost keys at that moment was truly providential.
The rescue was described as providential because help arrived just in time.
They saw the favorable weather as a providential sign for their journey.

Synset Examples

assumption that nature operates only according to a providential plan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortunate - happening by chance in a way that is lucky or beneficial
  • Antonym: accidental - happening by chance, often unintentionally

Etymology

From Late Latin 'providentialis', from 'providentia' meaning foresight or providence.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03018021-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˌprɑːvəˈdɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌprɒvɪˈdɛnʃəl / (BrE)
happening at a favorable time, as if by divine intervention; lucky or fortunate in a way that seems to result from providence
fortunate as if by divine intervention

Examples

The team's providential victory came just when they needed it most.
A providential phone call prevented her from making a terrible mistake.
The farmer considered the rainfall providential after weeks of drought.

Synset Examples

a heaven-sent rain saved the crops
a providential recovery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortunate - receiving good results by chance; lucky
  • Antonym: unlucky - having bad luck; not fortunate

Etymology

From Latin 'providentia' (foresight), influenced by 'providence'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01052675-s
Synset POS: s
