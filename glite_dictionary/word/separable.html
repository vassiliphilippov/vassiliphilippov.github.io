separable

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛpərəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛpərəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being divided, separated, or dissociated from something else.
able to be separated or divided

Examples

The art and its meaning are not easily separable in this context.
The financial and legal issues proved to be separable after further investigation.
The two substances are separable through a simple chemical process.

Synset Examples

often drugs and crime are not dissociable
the siamese twins were not considered separable
a song … never conceived of as severable from the melody

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inseparable - incapable of being separated
  • Synonym: divisible - capable of being divided

Etymology

From Late Latin separabilis, from Latin separare (to separate).
