jacinth

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæ.sɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈdʒæ.sɪnθ / (BrE)
a reddish-orange transparent variety of zircon used as a gemstone
reddish-orange zircon gemstone

Examples

The necklace was set with a brilliant jacinth at its center.
Collectors prize the jacinth for its fiery red color and clarity.
Ancient texts often refer to jacinth alongside other precious stones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zircon - a mineral used as a gemstone, sometimes in reddish form

Etymology

From Latin 'hyacinthus', from Greek 'hyakinthos' (blue larkspur, sapphire, or similar gemstone).
