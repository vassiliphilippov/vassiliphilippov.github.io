battering

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbæt.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of attacking someone or something with repeated blows or force; violent assault or criticism
violent attack or repeated blows

Examples

The old castle walls showed signs of prolonged battering from enemy cannons.
The politician took a battering from the media after the scandal emerged.
Decades of storms have given the coastline a relentless battering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assault - a violent physical or verbal attack
  • Synonym: attack - an act of trying to hurt or damage someone or something
  • Antonym: defense - the act of protecting against attack

Etymology

From Middle English 'bateren' (to beat repeatedly), from Old French 'batre', from Latin 'battuere' (to beat).
