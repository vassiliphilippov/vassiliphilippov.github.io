nitre

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtə / (BrE)
A white crystalline compound, potassium nitrate (KNO3), used especially as a fertilizer and in making explosives.
potassium nitrate compound

Examples

The old factory stored large amounts of nitre for the production of gunpowder.
Gardeners sometimes add nitre to soil as a source of potassium and nitrogen.
Historically, nitre was extracted from caves and used in various chemical processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saltpeter - common name for potassium nitrate

Etymology

From French 'nitre', from Latin 'nitrum', from Greek 'nitron', referring to a natural mineral.
