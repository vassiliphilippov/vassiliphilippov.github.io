offensiveness

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɛnsɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˈfɛnsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being offensive or likely to cause resentment, hurt, or annoyance
the quality of being offensive

Examples

The offensiveness of his remarks shocked everyone in the room.
Many artists debate the offensiveness of controversial works.
He apologized for the offensiveness of his joke after realizing it upset people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offense - something that causes someone to feel hurt or upset
  • Antonym: inoffensiveness - the quality of not causing offense

Etymology

From "offensive" + "-ness"; "offensive" from Latin offensa (an offense).
