veterancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛt̬ɚənsi / (AmE), / ˈvɛtərənsi / (BrE)
the state or quality of being a veteran, especially as gained through long experience or service
quality or status of being a veteran

Examples

His veterancy was evident from the way he handled the difficult situation.
Players with great veterancy are highly valued in strategy games for their expertise.
The soldier's veterancy was recognized with several medals for distinguished service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veteran - an experienced person, especially a former member of the armed forces

Etymology

From 'veteran' + suffix '-cy', indicating a state or quality.
