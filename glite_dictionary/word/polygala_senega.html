Polygala senega

1. NOUN

/ pəˌlɪɡələ səˈniːɡə / (AmE), / pɒˌlɪɡələ səˈniːɡə / (BrE)
an eastern North American plant with small white flowers in a terminal cluster and roots used for medicinal purposes
eastern North American medicinal plant

Examples

The root of Polygala senega has long been used in traditional medicine for respiratory ailments.
Botanists can easily identify Polygala senega by its cluster of small white flowers at the stem tip.
Many herbal remedies contain extracts from Polygala senega due to its purported medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Seneca snakeroot - common name for Polygala senega

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Polygala and the Seneca people, who used its root medicinally.
