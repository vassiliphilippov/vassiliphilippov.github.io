Tragelaphus strepsiceros

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrædʒəˈlæfəs ˌstrɛpsɪˈsiːrəs / (AmE), / ˌtræɡɪˈlæfəs ˌstrɛpsɪˈsiərɒs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the greater kudu, a large antelope native to eastern and southern Africa, recognized by its twisted horns and distinctive body stripes
scientific name for the greater kudu

Examples

The Tragelaphus strepsiceros is renowned for its striking spiral horns and impressive leaping ability.
Researchers observed several Tragelaphus strepsiceros grazing quietly in the African savanna.
Conservation programs are in place to protect the habitat of the Tragelaphus strepsiceros.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kudu - a large African antelope with twisted horns

Etymology

From Greek 'tragos' (goat) + 'elaphos' (deer) and Greek 'strepsis' (twisting) + 'keras' (horn).
