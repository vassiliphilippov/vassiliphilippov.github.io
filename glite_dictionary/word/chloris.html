Chloris

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔrɪs / (AmE), / ˈklɔːrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of grasses, known as finger grass or windmill grass, characterized by tufted or running growth, found in warm regions.
a genus of grasses; finger grass

Examples

The Chloris species are often used to stabilize soil in tropical and subtropical areas.
Researchers studied how Chloris responds to drought conditions.
We identified several types of Chloris growing wild along the path.

Etymology

From Greek 'Chloris', a nymph associated with greenery; related to 'chloros' meaning 'green'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɔrɪs / (AmE), / ˈklɔːrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of grasses, commonly known as finger grasses or windmill grasses, characterized by being tufted, perennial, or annual with runners.
a genus of grasses (finger grass, windmill grass)

Examples

The Chloris genus includes several grasses known for their distinctive finger-like seed heads.
Many species within the Chloris genus are important for soil conservation in tropical regions.
Botanists examined samples of Chloris collected from the grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finger grass - a common name for grasses of the genus Chloris
  • Synonym: windmill grass - a common name for grasses of the genus Chloris

Etymology

From Greek Chloris, a name meaning 'pale green' or 'fresh.'
