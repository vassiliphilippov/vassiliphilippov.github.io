orrery

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrəri / (AmE), / ˈɒrəri / (BrE)
A mechanical model of the solar system that shows the relative positions and motions of the planets and moons by means of rotating and revolving spheres, often driven by clockwork.
mechanical model of the solar system

Examples

The science museum displayed a beautifully crafted orrery that fascinated both children and adults.
Using the orrery, the teacher demonstrated how the planets revolve around the Sun.
He inherited an antique orrery that had been in the family for generations.

Etymology

Named after Charles Boyle, 4th Earl of Orrery, for whom one of the first modern examples was made in the early 18th century.
