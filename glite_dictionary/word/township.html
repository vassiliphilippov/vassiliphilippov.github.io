township

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊnʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈtaʊnʃɪp / (BrE)
an administrative division of a county, often with its own local government
county administrative division

Examples

Each township elects its own board of supervisors to oversee services.
The new law affects property taxes across every township in the county.
Snow removal in rural areas is managed at the township level.

Synset Examples

the town is responsible for snow removal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: municipality - a town or district with its own local government
  • Degree Higher: city - a large town or urban area with self-government
  • Degree Lower: village - a small group of houses in a rural area

Etymology

From Middle English 'tounship', meaning the district or jurisdiction of a 'town'.
