brachistochrone

1. NOUN

/ brəˈkɪstəˌkroʊn / (AmE), / brəˈkɪstəkrəʊn / (BrE)
A curve between two points, down which a body moves under gravity in the shortest possible time.
curve of fastest descent under gravity

Examples

The brachistochrone is fundamental to understanding problems of quickest descent in physics.
Mathematicians in the eighteenth century competed to solve the brachistochrone problem.
On a blackboard, the professor drew the brachistochrone curve for the students to examine.

Synset Examples

The brachistochrone is a cycloid with a horizontal base and with its cusp at the point A.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycloid - curve traced by a point on the rim of a rolling circle; shape of the brachistochrone

Etymology

From Greek brakhistos (shortest) + khronos (time).
