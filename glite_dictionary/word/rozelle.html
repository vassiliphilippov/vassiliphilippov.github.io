rozelle

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈzɛl / (AmE), / rəʊˈzɛl / (BrE)
An East Indian annual herb or perennial subshrub (Hibiscus sabdariffa) widely cultivated for its fleshy red calyxes used in making tarts, jellies, and drinks, and for its bast fiber.
East Indian herb grown for edible calyxes and fiber

Examples

Many people enjoy drinking tea made from rozelle calyxes during the hot season.
The red, tangy flavor of rozelle makes it a popular ingredient in jellies and syrups.
Local farmers harvest rozelle not only for food products but also for its valuable bast fiber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roselle - Alternative spelling of the same plant (Hibiscus sabdariffa)
