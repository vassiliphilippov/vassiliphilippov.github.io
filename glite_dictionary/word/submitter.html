submitter

1. NOUN

/ səbˈmɪtər / (AmE), / səbˈmɪtə / (BrE)
a person who formally presents something, such as an application, proposal, or document, for consideration or decision by others
person who submits something for consideration

Examples

Every submitter must include a cover letter with their manuscript.
The competition organizers thanked each submitter for participating in the event.
As the original submitter, she was responsible for providing all the supporting documents.

Synset Examples

he was a prolific submitter of proposals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: applicant - someone who applies for something, especially a job or place at a university
  • Antonym: responder - someone who responds to a request or call for submissions

Etymology

From 'submit' + '-er', with 'submit' from Latin 'submittere' (to let down, submit).

2. NOUN

/ səbˈmɪtər / (AmE), / səbˈmɪtə / (BrE)
a person who yields to the authority, control, or will of another person or force
person who submits or yields

Examples

The submitter obeyed every command given without question.
She was criticized for being a submitter rather than standing up for herself.
In the story, the submitter accepts her fate, unlike the rebellious protagonist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submissive - inclined or ready to submit to others
  • Antonym: resister - a person who resists or opposes

Etymology

From 'submit' + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10689166-n
Synset POS: n
