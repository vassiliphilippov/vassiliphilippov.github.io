niece

1. NOUN

/ nis / (AmE), / niÀês / (BrE)
a daughter of your brother or sister, or of your spouse's brother or sister
daughter of one's sibling

Examples

My niece is coming to visit us during the summer holidays.
He bought a birthday present for his young niece.
Whenever she travels, she sends postcards to her nieces and nephews.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nephew - a son of your brother or sister

Etymology

From Old French 'niece', from Latin 'neptia' (niece, granddaughter).
