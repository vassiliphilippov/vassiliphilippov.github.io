cutlas

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌt.ləs / (AmE), / ˈkʌt.ləs / (BrE)
a short, heavy sword with a curved single-edged blade, formerly used especially by sailors
short curved sailors' sword

Examples

The pirate drew his cutlas as the ship prepared for battle.
Antique dealers often sell replicas of the traditional sailor's cutlas.
He swung the cutlas with surprising strength for such an old man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutlass - a short, heavy, curved sword used especially by sailors

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'cutlass', from French 'coutelas' (large knife), from Italian 'coltellaccio', augmentative of 'coltello' (knife), from Latin 'cultellus'.
