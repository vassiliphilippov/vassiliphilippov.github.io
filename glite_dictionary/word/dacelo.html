Dacelo

1. NOUN

/ dəˈsiːloʊ / (AmE), / dəˈsiːləʊ / (BrE)
A genus of large kingfishers native to Australasia, commonly known as kookaburras.
genus of Australasian kingfishers

Examples

The Dacelo genus includes the iconic laughing kookaburra of Australia.
Many birdwatchers travel to Australia to observe Dacelo species in their natural habitat.
The call of Dacelo birds is often described as echoing human laughter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kookaburra - a bird species within the genus Dacelo, known for its distinctive call

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Dacelo, an anagram of 'alcedo' (Latin for kingfisher).

2. NOUN

/ dəˈsɛloʊ / (AmE), / dəˈsiːləʊ / (BrE)
A genus of Australasian kingfishers commonly known as kookaburras, native to Australia and nearby regions.
genus of Australasian kingfishers (kookaburras)

Examples

The laughing Dacelo is a familiar sight in Australian woodlands.
Researchers observed several species within the Dacelo genus during their field study.
Dacelo birds are renowned for their distinctive vocalizations at dawn and dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kookaburra - a bird of the genus Dacelo, known for its distinctive call

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, used in taxonomic classification; possibly based on a Latinized version of a native Australian word.
