Thomas à Kempis

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑːməs ə ˈkɛmpɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɒməs ə ˈkɛmpɪs / (BrE)
(c. 1380–1471) A German-Dutch religious writer and monk, best known as the author of 'The Imitation of Christ'.
German-Dutch religious writer and monk

Examples

Many spiritual writers have been influenced by Thomas à Kempis and his works.
The teachings of Thomas à Kempis continue to inspire readers around the world.
Scholars regard Thomas à Kempis as one of the most important authors of Christian devotional literature.

Etymology

From Latinized form of his Dutch name, meaning 'Thomas from Kempen' (his birthplace).
