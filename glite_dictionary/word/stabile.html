stabile

1. ADJ

/ ˈsteɪˌbəl / (AmE), / ˈstæb.aɪl / (BrE)
(of a substance or system, especially in chemistry, physics, or biology) resistant to change or not readily altered under given conditions.
resistant to change (science)
medical

Examples

This compound remains stabile even when exposed to heat and light.
The DNA structure is remarkably stabile under normal biological conditions.
Engineers designed an alloy that is stabile in highly corrosive environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stable - not likely to change or fail; firmly established

Etymology

From Latin stabilis (firm, steadfast), related to 'stable'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪˌbiːl / (AmE), / ˈsteɪ.bɪl / (BrE)
a sculpture consisting of fixed parts, usually made of sheet metal, supported on a stationary base
stationary sculpture with fixed parts

Examples

The museum displayed a large stabile in the sculpture garden.
This artist is famous for designing both mobiles and stabiles.
A brightly colored stabile stands near the building's entrance, catching the eye of visitors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mobile - a sculpture with moving parts, suspended or balanced to move freely

Etymology

From French, coined in contrast to 'mobile' by sculptor Alexander Calder, from Latin 'stabilis' (stable).
