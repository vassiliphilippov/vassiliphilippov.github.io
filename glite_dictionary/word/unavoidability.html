unavoidability

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnəˌvɔɪdəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˌvɔɪdəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible to avoid or evade.
quality of being impossible to avoid

Examples

The unavoidability of death has fascinated philosophers for centuries.
She faced the unavoidability of change with courage and acceptance.
Many experts discussed the unavoidability of economic downturns in a global recession.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inevitability - the quality of being certain to happen
  • Antonym: avoidability - the quality of being able to be avoided

Etymology

From un- (not) + avoidable + -ity (forming nouns denoting quality or condition).
