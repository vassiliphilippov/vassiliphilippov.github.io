ineptitude

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɛptəˌtud / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛptɪtjuːd / (BrE)
lack of skill or ability; incompetence, especially resulting from insufficient training or experience.
lack of skill; incompetence

Examples

Her ineptitude at managing finances led to several costly mistakes.
The team's ineptitude was obvious after they lost every game of the season.
He was dismissed because his ineptitude endangered the safety of his coworkers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: competence - the ability to do something successfully or efficiently
  • Synonym: incompetence - inability to do something successfully

Etymology

From French 'ineptitude', from Latin 'ineptus' (unsuitable, foolish).
