acridid

1. NOUN

/ ˈækrɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈakrɪdɪd / (BrE)
A grasshopper of the family Acrididae, typically characterized by short antennae.
a grasshopper with short antennae (family Acrididae)

Examples

The scientist observed several acridid species in the grasslands during spring.
Short-horned grasshoppers, known as acridids, are found in many temperate regions.
The agricultural report noted a sharp increase in acridid populations this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grasshopper - a jumping insect of the order Orthoptera

Etymology

From modern Latin Acrididae, the family name, from Greek akris (grasshopper).
