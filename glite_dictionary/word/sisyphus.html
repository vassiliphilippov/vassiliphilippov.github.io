Sisyphus

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɪsəfəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪsɪfəs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a king who was condemned by Zeus to roll a heavy boulder endlessly up a hill, only for it to roll down each time he neared the top.
mythical king condemned to endless labor

Examples

The story of Sisyphus symbolizes a never-ending or futile task.
Philosophers often refer to Sisyphus to illustrate existential struggles.
She felt like Sisyphus, endlessly repeating the same pointless chores every day.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Σίσυφος (Sísuphos), the name of the mythological king.
