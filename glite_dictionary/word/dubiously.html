dubiously

1. ADV

/ ˈduːbiəsli / (AmE), / ˈdjuːbiəsli / (BrE)
In a manner showing doubt or suspicion; with hesitation or uncertainty.
in a doubtful or suspicious manner

Examples

She looked at the contract dubiously, unsure whether she should sign it.
He dubiously agreed to the proposal, clearly unconvinced by the argument.
The clerk raised an eyebrow dubiously when I asked for a refund without a receipt.

Synset Examples

Gerald shook his head doubtfully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubtfully - in a manner expressing doubt
  • Antonym: confidently - in a manner full of confidence

Etymology

From Latin 'dubiosus' (doubtful), from 'dubium' (doubt).

2. ADV

/ ˈduː.bi.əs.li / (AmE), / ˈdjuː.bi.əs.li / (BrE)
in a way that shows doubt or is open to question; in a doubtful or suspicious manner
in a doubtful or questionable manner

Examples

She glanced dubiously at the unfamiliar dish before taking a bite.
The manager dubiously agreed to the risky proposal after much hesitation.
He dubiously claimed he could finish all the work by tomorrow.

Synset Examples

these were estates his father questionably acquired

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubtfully - in a manner expressing or showing doubt
  • Antonym: confidently - in a manner full of confidence or certainty

Etymology

From 'dubious' + '-ly'; 'dubious' from Latin 'dubiosus' (doubtful).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
