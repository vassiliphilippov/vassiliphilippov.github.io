Anadenanthera

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˌdɛnəˈnɪθərə / (AmE), / ˌænədɛnəˈniːθərə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American shrubs or trees, comprising two species that are known for their psychoactive seeds.
genus of tropical American shrubs or trees

Examples

The seeds of Anadenanthera have been used traditionally in South American rituals.
Several species of Anadenanthera grow in the forests of Brazil and Argentina.
Scientists are studying the chemical compounds found in Anadenanthera bark.

Etymology

From Greek 'anás' (up, again) and 'dent-', relating to 'tooth,' referring to aspects of the plant's pod shape.

2. NOUN

/ ænəˌdɛnənˈθɪrə / (AmE), / ænəˌdɛnənˈθɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American shrubs or trees containing two species, known for their use in traditional medicine and rituals.
genus of tropical American shrubs or trees

Examples

The seeds of the Anadenanthera tree have been used in traditional South American ceremonies.
Botanists have identified only two species in the Anadenanthera genus.
Researchers study the alkaloid content in Anadenanthera for its potential medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Greek 'anadenan' (to rise, spring up) and 'anthera' (anther, part of a flower).
