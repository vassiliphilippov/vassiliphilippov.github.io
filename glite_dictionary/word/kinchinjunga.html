Kinchinjunga

1. PROPN

/ kɪn.tʃɪnˈdʒʌŋ.ɡə / (AmE), / kɪn.tʃɪnˈdʒʌŋ.ɡə / (BrE)
a mountain in the Himalayas, located on the border between Nepal and the Indian state of Sikkim, and the third highest mountain in the world (28,169 feet or 8,586 meters high)
third highest mountain in the world

Examples

The peak of Kinchinjunga is often hidden by clouds.
Climbers face many challenges while attempting to scale Kinchinjunga.
From the town of Darjeeling, the view of Kinchinjunga at sunrise is breathtaking.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Everest - the highest mountain in the world, also in the Himalayas
  • Degree Lower: Lhotse - the fourth highest mountain in the world, in the Himalayas

Etymology

From Tibetan 'Kangchen Dzönga', meaning 'The Five Treasures of Snow'.
