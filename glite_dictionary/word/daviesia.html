Daviesia

1. NOUN

/ dəˈviːziə / (AmE), / dəˈviːziə / (BrE)
a genus of Australasian shrubs and subshrubs bearing small yellow or purple flowers followed by short triangular pods
genus of Australasian shrubs

Examples

The rare Daviesia shrubs are native to the woodlands of Australia.
Several species in the Daviesia genus are valued for their distinctive flowers.
A botanist discovered a new Daviesia growing along the coastal cliffs.

Etymology

Named after Welsh botanist Hugh Davies (1739–1821).

2. NOUN

/ dəˈviːziə / (AmE), / dəˈviːziə / (BrE)
a genus of Australasian shrubs and subshrubs in the pea family, typically bearing small yellow or purple flowers and short, triangular pods
genus of Australasian flowering shrubs

Examples

Several species of Daviesia grow naturally in the dry forests of Western Australia.
The flowers of Daviesia plants are often visited by native bees for pollination.
Botanists collected fresh specimens of Daviesia to study their unique triangular seed pods.

Etymology

Named after Hugh Davies, a Welsh botanist (1739–1821).
