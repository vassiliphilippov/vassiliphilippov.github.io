looking

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of searching or examining by using one's eyes
the act of visually searching

Examples

After hours of careful looking, she finally found her missing earring.
He spent the afternoon looking for the perfect birthday gift for his friend.
A little looking can often reveal surprising details in old photographs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: searching - the act of trying to find something by looking
  • Degree Lower: glancing - the act of looking quickly or briefly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00948248-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of directing one's eyes toward something and perceiving it visually; the action of looking
the act of directing the eyes

Examples

He gave the painting a careful looking before deciding to buy it.
With just one looking, she noticed the mistake on the page.
His thorough looking at the evidence helped solve the case.

Synset Examples

he went out to have a look
his look was fixed on her eyes
he gave it a good looking at
his camera does his looking for him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: look - an act of directing one's gaze
