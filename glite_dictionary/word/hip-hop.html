hip-hop

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪp hɑp / (AmE), / ˈhɪp hɒp / (BrE)
A genre of popular music developed by African-American communities in the 1980s and 1990s, characterized by rhythmic spoken lyrics (rap) over a backing track; also refers to the broader cultural movement surrounding this music.
genre of rhythmic rap music and culture

Examples

The influence of hip-hop can be seen in fashion, language, and art worldwide.
She grew up listening to classic hip-hop artists from the 1990s.
The festival featured live hip-hop performances by local and international stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rap - rhythmic spoken lyrics over a musical beat

Etymology

From the repetitive chanting of 'hip' and 'hop' in early rap songs; popularized by African-American musicians in the late 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɪp.hɑp / (AmE), / ˈhɪp.hɒp / (BrE)
A cultural movement and musical genre originating in urban communities, especially among African-American youth, characterized by rap music, DJing, breakdancing, and distinctive fashion.
urban youth culture and music genre

Examples

The influence of hip-hop is evident in fashion, language, and art around the world.
She has been dancing to hip-hop music since she was a teenager.
Many schools now offer classes in hip-hop dance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rap - Musical style with rhythmic spoken lyrics, closely associated with hip-hop

Etymology

From 'hip' (slang for fashionable or in the know) and 'hop' (suggesting movement); term popularized in the 1970s Bronx, New York.
