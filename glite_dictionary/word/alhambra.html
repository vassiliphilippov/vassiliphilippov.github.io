Alhambra

1. PROPN

/ ælˈhæmbrə / (AmE), / ælˈhæmbrə / (BrE)
A famous fortified palace and citadel built by the Moors near Granada, Spain, during the Middle Ages.
Moorish palace in Granada, Spain

Examples

We visited the Alhambra during our trip to southern Spain.
The architecture of the Alhambra reflects the rich history of Moorish Spain.
The gardens of the Alhambra are renowned for their beauty and tranquility.

Etymology

From Arabic 'al-Ḥamrāʼ' (the red), referring to the reddish walls and towers.
