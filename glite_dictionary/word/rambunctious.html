rambunctious

1. ADJ

/ ræmˈbʌŋk.ʃəs / (AmE), / ræmˈbʌŋk.ʃəs / (BrE)
noisy, energetic, and difficult to control; lacking in restraint or discipline
noisy and unruly

Examples

The rambunctious children ran through the house, laughing and shouting at the top of their lungs.
His rambunctious puppy knocked over a lamp while chasing its tail.
After too much sugar, the kids became so rambunctious that it was hard to settle them down.

Synset Examples

a boisterous crowd
a social gathering that became rambunctious and out of hand
a robustious group of teenagers
beneath the rumbustious surface of his paintings is sympathy for the vulnerability of ordinary human beings
an unruly class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boisterous - noisy, energetic, and cheerful; rowdy
  • Synonym: unruly - disorderly and disruptive and not amenable to discipline

Etymology

Origin uncertain, perhaps an alteration of 'rumbustious'.
