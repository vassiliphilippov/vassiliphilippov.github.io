Saxicola

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæksɪˈkoʊlə / (AmE), / ˌsæksɪˈkəʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of small Old World songbirds in the thrush family, commonly known as chats, found in Europe, Asia, and Africa.
genus of Old World chats (birds)

Examples

The Saxicola genus includes the well-known European stonechat.
Researchers observed several species of Saxicola inhabiting the rocky grasslands.
The striking plumage of the Saxicola makes it popular among birdwatchers.

Etymology

From Latin saxum (rock) + incola (inhabitant), referring to their rocky habitats.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæksɪˈkoʊlə / (AmE), / ˌsæksɪˈkəʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of small Old World birds known as stonechats or chats, found mainly in Europe, Asia, and Africa.
a genus of Old World birds, stonechats

Examples

The European stonechat belongs to the genus Saxicola.
Members of Saxicola are often found in open, rocky habitats.
The ornithologist studied several species within the genus Saxicola.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - a type of small songbird, especially in the Old World

Etymology

From Latin saxum (rock) + incola (dweller), referring to the birds' rocky habitats.
