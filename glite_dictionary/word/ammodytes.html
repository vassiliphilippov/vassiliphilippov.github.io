Ammodytes

1. NOUN

/ ˈæməˌdaɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˈamədɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small, elongated fishes known as sand eels or sand lances, found in marine and coastal waters.
genus of sand eel fishes

Examples

The Ammodytes genus is an important food source for many seabirds in the North Atlantic.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Ammodytes group along the coastal shelf.
Juvenile Ammodytes bury themselves in sandy beaches to avoid predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand eel - common name for fish of the genus Ammodytes

Etymology

From Greek ammodytēs (sand-burrower), from ammos (sand) + dytēs (diver).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæməˌdaɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˈamədɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of slender marine fish known as sand lances, which are type genus of the family Ammodytidae.
genus of marine sand lances

Examples

The Atlantic Ammodytes are a critical food source for many seabirds.
Scientists studied the burrowing behavior of the common Ammodytes species.
Commercial fisheries sometimes target Ammodytes due to their abundance in coastal waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand lance - a small, slender fish that burrows in sand; common name for Ammodytes species

Etymology

From Greek ammos (sand) + dytēs (diver), referring to the fishes’ habit of burrowing in sand.
