grainy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪni / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪni / (BrE)
having a texture, appearance, or consistency composed of small, grain-like particles; rough or granular
having a granular or rough texture

Examples

The old film reel gave the scene a grainy quality that suited the mood.
When you rub the sand between your fingers, it feels grainy and dry.
She disliked the grainy texture of the soup, which reminded her of uncooked meal.

Synset Examples

granular sugar
the photographs were grainy and indistinct
it left a mealy residue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: granular - having a surface or structure composed of small grains or particles
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; free from projections or roughness

Etymology

From 'grain' (small hard particle), with suffix '-y' meaning 'characterized by'.
