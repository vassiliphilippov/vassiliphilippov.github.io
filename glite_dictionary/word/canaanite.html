Canaanite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪnəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.nə.naɪt / (BrE)
an extinct Semitic language formerly spoken by the people who occupied Canaan prior to the Israelite conquest
extinct Semitic language of ancient Canaan

Examples

Archaeologists discovered inscriptions written in Canaanite dating back to the second millennium BCE.
The Canaanite language influenced the development of Hebrew and Phoenician.
Few people today can read texts in ancient Canaanite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hebrew - the Semitic language of the ancient Israelites

Etymology

From Canaan, the ancient region in the Levant, plus the English suffix '-ite' denoting association.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.nəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.nə.naɪt / (BrE)
a member of an ancient Semitic people who lived in the region of Canaan before its conquest by the Israelites
member of an ancient Semitic people of Canaan

Examples

Archaeologists have discovered artifacts belonging to the Canaanite civilization in the region of modern-day Israel.
The Canaanite religion was known for its pantheon of gods and goddesses.
Many ancient texts mention the Canaanite people as skilled traders and builders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phoenician - a member of an ancient Semitic people closely related to the Canaanites
  • Antonym: Israelite - a member of the ancient Hebrew nation that conquered Canaan

Etymology

From Latin Canaanita, from Greek Kananitēs, from Hebrew Kena‘an (Canaan).
