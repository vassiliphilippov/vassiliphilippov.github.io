officer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑfəsər / (AmE), / ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
a member of a police force
member of a police force

Examples

The officer questioned witnesses at the scene of the robbery.
A uniformed officer directed traffic during the parade.
She thanked the officer for his help after the accident.

Synset Examples

it was an accident, officer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: policeman - male member of a police force
  • Synonym: policewoman - female member of a police force
  • Antonym: civilian - person not in the police or armed forces

Etymology

From Old French *oficier*, from Latin *officiarius* (official).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10468557-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈɑfəsɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
to act as an officer by directing or commanding others, especially in a military or organizational context
to command as an officer

Examples

She was chosen to officer the new unit because of her extensive leadership experience.
Several senior members were brought in to officer the expedition through the desert.
The battalion was officered by veterans with years of combat experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command - to give orders and be in charge
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00754341-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːfəsər / (AmE), / ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
a person in the armed forces who holds a position of authority or command
person with authority in the military

Examples

The commanding officer addressed the troops before the mission began.
He trained for years to become a high-ranking officer in the navy.
As an officer, she is responsible for the discipline and safety of her unit.

Synset Examples

an officer is responsible for the lives of his men

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soldier - a member of the army who is not an officer or who does not hold a position of command

Etymology

From Old French *officier*, from Latin *officiarius* (an official, attendant), from *officium* (duty, service).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10336665-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.fɪ.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒf.ɪ.sə / (BrE)
a person who is appointed or elected to a position of authority or trust within an organization, such as a company, club, or government body
person holding position of authority or trust

Examples

She was elected as an officer of the student council for the next academic year.
Every club must have at least three officers: a president, a treasurer, and a secretary.
He served as an officer of the charity organization for over ten years.

Synset Examples

he is an officer of the court
the club elected its officers for the coming year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: official - a person holding public office or having official duties

Etymology

From Old French 'oficier', from Medieval Latin 'officiarius', from Latin 'officium' (duty, service).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10391149-n
Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ ˈɑːfəsər / (AmE), / ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
a person holding a position of authority and responsibility on a ship, often in charge of specific operations or departments
person with authority on a ship

Examples

The chief officer checked the navigation equipment before departure.
Each officer on the vessel has specific duties during an emergency.
She was promoted to second officer after only three years at sea.

Synset Examples

he is the officer in charge of the ship's engines

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crew member - person working on a ship who is not an officer
  • Degree Higher: captain - the person in overall command of a ship

Etymology

From Old French *oficier*, from Latin *officiarius* (an official).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10391440-n
Synset POS: n
