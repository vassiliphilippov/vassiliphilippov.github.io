Lanius

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪniəs / (AmE), / ˈleɪniəs / (BrE)
the genus of birds that includes the typical shrikes, in the family Laniidae
genus of typical shrikes

Examples

The ornithologist studied several species within the genus Lanius during his research.
Members of Lanius are commonly known as butcherbirds due to their hunting habits.
A new subspecies of Lanius was recently discovered in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrike - a bird of the family Laniidae known for impaling prey

Etymology

From Latin *lanius* (butcher), referring to the shrike’s habit of impaling prey.
