to retrieve

1. VERB

/ rɪˈtriv / (AmE), / rɪˈtriːv / (BrE)
(of a dog) to run after something, pick it up, and bring it back to its owner or handler.
(of a dog) bring back something

Examples

The trainer taught the dog to retrieve the ball from the lake.
Some breeds, like retrievers, are especially good at retrieving game birds.
During the competition, the dog quickly retrieved the thrown dummy and returned it to its handler.

Synset Examples

train the dog to retrieve

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fetch - (of a dog) to go for and bring back something, especially on command

Etymology

From Anglo-French *retriever* (bring back), from Old French *retrover* (to find again), from Latin *re-* (back) + *trovare* (to find).

WordNet ID: oewn-01436442-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈtriv / (AmE), / rɪˈtriːv / (BrE)
to go to a place and bring something back, especially something that has been lost, left, or stored
go and bring back; recover

Examples

She had to retrieve her suitcase from the baggage claim area.
The police managed to retrieve the stolen painting.
Can you retrieve my book from the living room table?

Synset Examples

retrieve the car from the parking garage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recover - to get back something lost or taken
  • Synonym: fetch - to go for and then bring back (something or someone)
  • Antonym: to lose - to be unable to find something; the opposite of retrieving

Etymology

From Old French *retrouver* (find again), from re- (again) + trouver (to find).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01436624-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rɪˈtriv / (AmE), / rɪˈtriːv / (BrE)
to bring a memory, fact, or piece of information back into your mind; to recall something mentally
recall from memory

Examples

He could not retrieve the name of his first-grade teacher, no matter how hard he tried.
When asked about the incident, she tried to retrieve exactly what had happened.

4. VERB

/ rɪˈtriv / (AmE), / rɪˈtriːv / (BrE)
to get or bring something back, especially something that was lost or difficult to find; to recover the use of something.
to get back or recover

Examples

Can you retrieve the file I accidentally deleted from the server?
After a few minutes searching in the bushes, she managed to retrieve her lost keys.
The athlete worked hard to retrieve his fitness after the injury.

Synset Examples

She regained control of herself
She found her voice and replied quickly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recover - to regain possession of something lost
  • Antonym: to lose - to be deprived of or cease to have something

Etymology

From Old French 'retrever' (to find again), from re- (again) + 'trover' (to find).
