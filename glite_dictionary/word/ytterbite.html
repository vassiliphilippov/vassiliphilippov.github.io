ytterbite

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪtərˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪtəbaɪt / (BrE)
a rare mineral composed of silicates of iron, beryllium, cerium, yttrium, and erbium, and once used as a source of rare earth elements
a rare mineral containing rare earths

Examples

The geologist identified the black specimen as ytterbite, which was once the principal source of yttrium.
Early chemists first isolated several rare earth elements from ytterbite.
Samples of ytterbite were sent to European laboratories for further analysis in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gadolinite - a similar mineral containing rare earth elements, first called ytterbite

Etymology

Named after the Swedish village of Ytterby, where it was first discovered, combined with 'bite', meaning 'mineral' in Swedish.
