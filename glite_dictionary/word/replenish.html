to replenish

1. VERB

/ rɪˈplɛnɪʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈplɛnɪʃ / (BrE)
to fill something again after it has been used up or emptied
fill something that was emptied

Examples

After a long hike, I needed to replenish my water bottle before heading back.
The company decided to replenish its inventory to meet the increasing demand.
Farmers must replenish the soil with nutrients every season to ensure healthy crops.

Synset Examples

refill my glass, please

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refill - to fill something again

Etymology

From Old French *repleniss-, stem of *replenir* (to fill up), from Latin *replenire* (to fill up again).
