adumbrative

1. ADJ

/ əˈdʌmbrətɪv / (AmE), / əˈdʌmbrətɪv / (BrE)
suggesting or foreshadowing in a vague or shadowy way; vaguely prophetic.
vaguely prophetic; foreshadowing

Examples

The novel's opening chapter was intensely adumbrative, hinting at tragedies to come.
His adumbrative remarks left the audience uncertain about his true intentions.
In her poem, she used adumbrative imagery to foreshadow the character's downfall.

Etymology

From Latin adumbratus, past participle of adumbrare (to cast a shadow, represent faintly), from ad- (to) + umbra (shadow).
