sextette

1. NOUN

/ sɛkˈstɛt / (AmE), / sɛkˈstɛt / (BrE)
A musical composition written for six performers or instruments.
composition for six performers

Examples

The ensemble performed a sextette composed by a contemporary artist.
Beethoven's Opus 81b is an example of a famous sextette.
They commissioned a new sextette for strings and piano to debut at the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sextet - composition for six performers; alternative spelling

Etymology

From Italian 'sestetto', from 'sei' (six).

2. NOUN

/ sɛkˈstɛt / (AmE), / sɛkˈstɛt / (BrE)
A group or set of six similar things considered as a unit.
group of six things

Examples

The jazz band performed as a sextette for the first time last night.
A sextette of brightly colored chairs surrounded the round table.
In the final scene, a sextette of dancers took the stage together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sextet - group of six musicians or things

Etymology

From Italian 'sestetto', from 'sestetto' (sixth), influenced by Latin 'sex' (six).

3. NOUN

/ sɛkˈstɛt / (AmE), / sɛksˈtɛt / (BrE)
A group of six musicians or singers who perform together.
group of six musicians or singers

Examples

The jazz sextette delighted the audience with their lively performance.
She joined a vocal sextette that sang both classical and contemporary music.
After years of playing alone, he was thrilled to become part of a renowned sextette.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quartet - a group of four musicians or singers who perform together
  • Degree Higher: octet - a group of eight musicians or singers who perform together
  • Synonym: sextet - alternative spelling for a group of six musicians

Etymology

From Italian 'sestetto' meaning a group of six, from 'sei' (six).
