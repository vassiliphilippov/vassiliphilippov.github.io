otitis

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / əʊˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the ear, especially as a medical condition
inflammation of the ear
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with otitis after experiencing ear pain and fever.
Chronic otitis can lead to hearing problems if left untreated.
Swimmers are at risk of developing otitis due to frequent exposure to water.

Etymology

From Greek ōt-, ōs (ear) + -itis (inflammation).
