Cystopteris fragilis

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌstɑptərɪs ˈfrædʒɪlɪs / (AmE), / sɪˌstɒptərɪs ˈfragɪlɪs / (BrE)
A delicate fern species found across North America and Europe, known for its thin, divided fronds and brittle stems.
delicate fern species with brittle stems

Examples

Cystopteris fragilis often grows on rocky slopes at high elevations.
The fronds of Cystopteris fragilis are easily damaged because of their brittle stems.
Botanists studied Cystopteris fragilis to understand its adaptation to different environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'kystis' (bladder) and 'pteris' (fern); 'fragilis' from Latin, meaning 'fragile' or 'brittle'.
