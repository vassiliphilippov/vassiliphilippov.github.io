to excommunicate

1. VERB

/ ˌɛkskəˈmjunɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌɛkskəˈmjuːnɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to officially exclude someone from participation in the sacraments and services of a Christian Church
officially exclude from Church participation

Examples

The church decided to excommunicate the member after his public statements.
Several historical figures were excommunicated for challenging church doctrine.
If you persist in these actions, they may excommunicate you from the community.

Synset Examples

The gay priest was excommunicated when he married his partner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expel - to force someone to leave a place or organization

Etymology

From Latin excommunicare, from ex- (out) + communicare (to share).

2. VERB

/ ˌɛkskəˈmjuːnɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌɛkskəˈmjuːnɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to officially expel someone from membership in a church or group, especially as a formal punishment
officially exclude from a group, especially a church

Examples

The church decided to excommunicate the priest for his actions.
If you break the strict rules, they may excommunicate you from the organization.
In medieval times, being excommunicated was considered a severe punishment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expel - to force someone to leave an organization or place
  • Antonym: to include - to allow someone to be part of a group or organization

Etymology

From Latin excommunicare (to put out of the community), from ex- (out) + communicare (to share).

3. VERB

/ ˌɛkskəˈmjunɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌɛkskəˈmjuːnɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to officially exclude someone from participation in the sacraments and services of a Christian Church, or more generally from a group or organization
officially exclude from a group, especially a church

Examples

The church decided to excommunicate him after he repeatedly violated its teachings.
Some organizations may excommunicate those who break strict internal codes of conduct.
She feared they would excommunicate her if she spoke out against their beliefs.

Synset Examples

He threatened to excommunicate anyone who broke the rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expel - to officially force someone to leave a group or organization
  • Antonym: to include - to allow someone to be part of a group or activity

Etymology

From Latin excommunicare (to put out of the community), from ex- (out) + communis (common, shared).
