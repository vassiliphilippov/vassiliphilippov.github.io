accessible

1. ADJ

/ əkˈsɛsəbl̩ / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsɪbəl / (BrE)
able to be reached or entered; easy to approach or obtain.
able to be reached or entered

Examples

The remote village is not easily accessible by road, especially during winter.
The exhibition is accessible to visitors with disabilities.
Most public libraries are accessible via public transportation.

Synset Examples

a town accessible by rail

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inaccessible - not able to be reached or approached

Etymology

From Latin accessibilis, from accessus (a coming to).

3. ADJ

/ əkˈsɛsəbəl / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of a book, text, idea, etc.) easy to understand or appreciate; able to be read or enjoyed by most people, especially those without specialized knowledge.
easy to understand or appreciate

Examples

The author's style is accessible to readers who are new to the subject.
She writes in an accessible way that makes complex ideas easy to grasp.
Critics praised the novel for being both profound and accessible.

Synset Examples

readily accessible to the nonprofessional reader
the tales seem more approachable than his more difficult novels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approachable - (of a text or subject) easy to understand or deal with
  • Antonym: incomprehensible - impossible or very difficult to understand

Etymology

From Latin accessibilis, from accessus (approach) + -ibilis (-ible, able to be).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00535441-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ əkˈsɛsəbəl / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsɪbəl / (BrE)
(of a person) easy to approach, talk to, or get along with; friendly and open.
easy to talk to; friendly and approachable (person)

Examples

The manager is accessible and always has time for questions.
Despite his fame, the author remains accessible to his fans.
People appreciate leaders who are accessible and willing to listen.

Synset Examples

an accessible and genial man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approachable - friendly and easy to talk to
  • Antonym: unapproachable - not easy to talk to or interact with

Etymology

From Latin 'accessibilis', from 'accessus' (approach).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00135322-s
