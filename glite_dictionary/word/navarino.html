Navarino

1. PROPN

/ ˌnævəˈriːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˌnavəˈriːnəʊ / (BrE)
the site of a decisive 1827 naval battle during the War of Greek Independence, where British, French, and Russian forces defeated the Ottoman and Egyptian fleet
site of 1827 naval battle in Greek War of Independence

Examples

The Battle of Navarino marked a turning point in the Greek struggle for independence.
Many historians consider Navarino one of the most significant naval clashes of the 19th century.
A monument stands near Navarino to commemorate those who fought in the famous battle.

Etymology

From the Greek Ναυαρίνο (Navaríno), the name of the bay and town in southwestern Greece.
