holozoic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhoʊləˈzoʊɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɒləˈzəʊɪk / (BrE)
(of organisms) obtaining nourishment by ingesting complex organic matter, as animals do.
obtaining food by ingesting complex organic matter
medical

Examples

Many protozoa are holozoic, ingesting bacteria and other small organisms as food.
Animals with a holozoic mode of nutrition must consume solid organic matter to survive.
Unlike plants, which are autotrophic, humans have a holozoic way of obtaining nutrients.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: saprophytic - obtaining nourishment by absorbing dissolved organic material, as fungi do
  • Antonym: autotrophic - capable of synthesizing its own food from inorganic substances

Etymology

From Greek 'holos' (whole) + 'zōion' (animal).
