Jean Sibelius

1. PROPN

/ ʒɑn sɪˈbeɪliəs / (AmE), / ʒɑːn sɪˈbiːliəs / (BrE)
(1865–1957) Finnish composer renowned for his symphonies and contributions to nationalist music.
Finnish composer (1865–1957)

Examples

Many orchestras enjoy performing works by Jean Sibelius for their dramatic intensity.
Jean Sibelius composed the famous piece "Finlandia" as an anthem of national pride.
Music critics often praise the symphonies of Jean Sibelius for their innovative structure.

2. PROPN

/ ʒɑn sɪˈbeɪliəs / (AmE), / ˈʒɑːn sɪˈbiːliəs / (BrE)
(1865–1957) A Finnish composer known for his symphonies and his influential role in developing a national style of classical music in Finland.
Finnish composer, wrote symphonies

Examples

Many orchestras perform Jean Sibelius's Symphony No. 2 due to its emotional depth.
The music of Jean Sibelius is closely connected with Finnish national identity.
We studied Jean Sibelius in our 20th-century music class.

Etymology

Born Johan Julius Christian Sibelius, he adopted 'Jean' as the French form of his name, common in European classical tradition.
