ruggedisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrʌɡ.ɪ.daɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌrʌɡ.ɪ.daɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of making equipment more durable or resistant to wear, damage, or harsh conditions
making equipment more durable or rugged

Examples

Military laptops undergo ruggedisation to ensure functionality in extreme environments.
The ruggedisation of mobile devices is important for use on construction sites.
Automotive components often require ruggedisation to withstand vibration and heat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruggedization - the US spelling for ruggedisation

Etymology

From 'rugged' + '-isation', formed after 'ruggedize' (to make rugged).
