whacking

1. ADV

/ ˈwækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwækɪŋ / (BrE)
To an unusually large or extreme extent; exceedingly.
to an unusually large extent; exceedingly
informal british

Examples

The house was a whacking big place, far larger than we expected.
They paid a whacking sum for that rare book at the auction.
She made a whacking error during the presentation, but handled it gracefully.

Synset Examples

a whacking good story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: very - to a great degree (intensifier)
  • Antonym: slightly - to a small degree
  • Synonym: fantastically - to an extraordinary extent

Etymology

From the verb 'whack' (to strike forcefully), figuratively extended in British English as an intensifier since the 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwækɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of striking someone repeatedly as a form of corporal punishment
repeated blows as corporal punishment

Examples

The teacher threatened a whacking for anyone caught cheating on the test.
After his misbehavior at school, he received a proper whacking from his father.
Stories of strict discipline often included the occasional whacking with a ruler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beating - the act of striking repeatedly; often used interchangeably for corporal punishment
  • Synonym: spanking - striking the buttocks as a form of corporal punishment

Etymology

From the verb 'whack' (to strike forcefully), likely imitative in origin.
