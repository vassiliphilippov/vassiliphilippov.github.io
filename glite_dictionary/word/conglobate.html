to conglobate

1. VERB

/ kənˈɡloʊˌbeɪt / (AmE), / kənˈɡləʊˌbeɪt / (BrE)
to form into a spherical or rounded mass; to assume a globular shape
form into a spherical mass

Examples

The small particles began to conglobate into larger clusters.
Under pressure, the wet clay would conglobate into dense balls.
Certain animals may conglobate as a form of defense against predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to coalesce - to come together to form one mass or whole

Etymology

From Latin 'conglobatus', past participle of 'conglobare' (to form into a ball).
