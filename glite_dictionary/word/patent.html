patent

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætənt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪtnt / (BrE)
an official document granting an inventor the exclusive right to make, use, or sell an invention for a certain number of years
official inventor's right document
legal

Examples

She was thrilled to receive a patent for her innovative new device.
The company holds several patents on cutting-edge medical technology.
Applying for a patent can be a lengthy and expensive process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copyright - an exclusive legal right to reproduce or distribute creative works
  • Synonym: trademark - a symbol or word legally registered to represent a company or product

Etymology

From Latin 'patent-', present participle of 'patere' (to lie open, be accessible), via Old French and Middle English.
