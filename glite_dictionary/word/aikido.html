aikido

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈkidoʊ / (AmE), / aɪˈkiːdəʊ / (BrE)
a Japanese martial art that uses throws and joint locks to redirect an opponent's energy rather than opposing it directly
Japanese martial art using throws and locks

Examples

He has been training in aikido for over five years and recently earned his black belt.
The philosophy of aikido emphasizes harmony and non-violence in conflict resolution.
Many police officers find the techniques learned in aikido helpful for self-defense.

Etymology

From Japanese 合気道 (aikidō), from 合 (ai, joining) + 気 (ki, spirit) + 道 (dō, way).
