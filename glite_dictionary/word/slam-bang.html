slam-bang

1. ADV

/ slæmˈbæŋ / (AmE), / slæmˈbæŋ / (BrE)

in a violent or noisy manner
informal

Examples

He arrived slam-bang in the middle of dinner, making quite an entrance.
The books fell slam-bang onto the floor, echoing through the house.

2. ADV

/ ˈslæmˌbæŋ / (AmE), / ˈslæmˌbæŋ / (BrE)
In a careless, reckless, or forceful manner, often without attention to detail or caution.
carelessly or recklessly
informal american

Examples

The team completed the repairs in a slam-bang fashion, ignoring the instructions.
He always does his chores slam-bang, leaving a mess behind every time.
The movie’s slam-bang pacing thrilled the audience but left little time for character development.

Synset Examples

the shelves were put up slapdash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slapdash - in a hurried, careless, or haphazard manner
  • Antonym: carefully - with attention or caution, opposite of reckless manner

Etymology

Imitative formation from 'slam' (to strike with force) and 'bang' (a sudden loud noise); first recorded in 19th-century American English.

Examples
