aristocratism

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪstəˌkrætɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈrɪstəˌkrætɪzəm / (BrE)
The principles, practices, or characteristics of an aristocracy; advocacy of government by an aristocratic class.
belief in or practices of aristocracy

Examples

Among the old families, there was a sense of aristocratism that shaped their customs and traditions.
Some criticized the club's atmosphere for its blatant aristocratism, which excluded newcomers.
The poet's disdain for aristocratism is evident in his satirical verse.

Synset Examples

This design is absolutely stunning and carries the spirit of aristocratism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristocracy - Government by a privileged class or the hereditary nobility

Etymology

From 'aristocrat' + '-ism', ultimately from Greek 'aristos' (best) + '-kratēs' (ruler).
