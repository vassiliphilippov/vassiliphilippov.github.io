detribalization

1. NOUN

/ diˌtraɪbələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌtraɪbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of reducing or eliminating the social, cultural, or political structures of a tribal society
removal of tribal organization

Examples

The policy of detribalization led to the loss of traditional customs among the indigenous communities.
Rapid urbanization contributed to the detribalization of many rural populations.
Historians studied the effects of detribalization on cultural identity in postcolonial societies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tribalization - the process of forming or organizing into tribes

Etymology

From de- (removal) + tribal + -ization (forming nouns meaning process or result).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdiːˌtraɪbələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌtraɪbələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which members of a tribe are made to abandon their traditional customs and culture, often adopting those of urban or dominant societies.
loss of tribal customs and adoption of urban ways

Examples

The rapid detribalization of indigenous communities often leads to loss of cultural identity.
Colonial policies contributed significantly to the detribalization of several African societies.
Efforts to reverse detribalization focus on reviving traditional practices and languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acculturation - the process of adopting cultural traits of another group
  • Synonym: assimilation - process of becoming similar to something else, especially culturally
  • Antonym: tribalization - the act or process of organizing into tribes or tribal systems

Etymology

From de- (removal) + tribal + -ization.
