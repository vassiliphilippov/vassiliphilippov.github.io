Heilong

1. PROPN

/ ˈheɪˌlɒŋ / (AmE), / ˈheɪˌlɒŋ / (BrE)
a river in Asia forming part of the border between China and Russia, flowing into the Sea of Okhotsk
a river between China and Russia

Examples

The Heilong River is vital for agriculture in northeastern China.
Many rare fish species thrive in the Heilong as it flows toward the Sea of Okhotsk.
The border between Russia and China largely follows the course of the Heilong.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amur - the river known as the Heilong in Chinese, forming the border between China and Russia

Etymology

From Chinese 黑龙江 (Hēilóng Jiāng), meaning 'Black Dragon River'.
