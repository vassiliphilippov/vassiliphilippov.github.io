achimenes

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɪmɪˌniːz / (AmE), / əˈkɪmɪniːz / (BrE)
A genus of tropical perennial plants in the family Gesneriaceae, known for their showy, bell-shaped flowers resembling gloxinias.
genus of flowering plants with bell-shaped blooms

Examples

The gardener planted several achimenes in the shaded border to add vibrant color.
Many species of achimenes thrive best in humid, tropical conditions and do not tolerate frost.
Their delicate, bell-shaped flowers make achimenes a favorite among collectors of exotic plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloxinia - a plant with bell-shaped flowers related to achimenes

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) and 'cheimaino' (to suffer from cold), referring to the plant's intolerance of cold.
