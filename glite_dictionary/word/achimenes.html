Achimenes

1. NOUN

/ ˌækɪˈmiːniːz / (AmE), / ˌækɪˈmiːniːz / (BrE)
A genus of tropical perennial flowering plants native to Central and South America, commonly grown for their showy flowers.
genus of tropical flowering plants

Examples

Gardeners in warm climates often cultivate Achimenes for their vibrant, tubular flowers.
Many species of Achimenes require moist, well-drained soil and indirect sunlight to thrive.
The delicate blooms of Achimenes are prized by horticulturists around the world.

Etymology

From Greek *a-* (not) + *kheimainō* (to endure winter), referring to the plant's sensitivity to cold.
