jook

1. NOUN

/ dʒʊk / (AmE), / dʒʊk / (BrE)
a Chinese rice porridge, often eaten for breakfast
Chinese rice porridge

Examples

Many Chinese families enjoy a bowl of jook for breakfast, often adding savory toppings.
You can make jook by simmering rice in plenty of water until it becomes creamy and soft.
At the dim sum restaurant, I ordered a steaming bowl of chicken jook to start my meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congee - a type of rice porridge widespread in Asia

Etymology

From Cantonese 粥 (zuk1, "rice gruel, porridge")

2. NOUN

/ dʒuk / (AmE), / dʒuːk / (BrE)
A small roadside bar or club in the southeastern United States, often offering food, drinks, dancing, and music from a jukebox.
roadside bar/club in the southeastern US

Examples

On weekends, the locals gather at the old jook for music and dancing.
The band started playing as soon as we entered the lively jook.
Many blues musicians got their start performing in southern jooks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juke joint - A small establishment providing music, dancing, and drinks in the southern United States

Etymology

From Gullah *juke* (disorderly, wicked), possibly of West African origin.
