galactic

1. ADJ

/ ɡəˈlæktɪk / (AmE), / ɡəˈlæktɪk / (BrE)
relating to a galaxy, especially the Milky Way galaxy.
relating to a galaxy

Examples

Astronomers study the galactic structure to understand the origins of our universe.
The solar system is located within the galactic disk of the Milky Way.
Scientists are mapping the galactic center to search for black holes.

Synset Examples

the galactic plane

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: intergalactic - existing or occurring between galaxies
  • Degree Lower: stellar - relating to stars

Etymology

From Greek galaxias (milky), relating to the Milky Way.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02859983-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɡəˈlæktɪk / (AmE), / ɡəˈlæktɪk / (BrE)
Extremely large or immense; vast beyond ordinary comprehension.
inconceivably large; immense

Examples

The company posted galactic profits last year, far exceeding all expectations.
The project came with a galactic price tag that only a few corporations could afford.
Her ambition for success is truly galactic in scale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gigantic - extremely large in size, very big
  • Antonym: minute - extremely small; tiny

Etymology

From Greek galaxias (milky, as in Milky Way), relating to the galaxy.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01386516-s
Synset POS: s
