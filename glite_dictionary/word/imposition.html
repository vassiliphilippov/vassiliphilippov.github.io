imposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of establishing or applying something such as a rule, tax, or restriction
the act of imposing something

Examples

The imposition of strict regulations made business more difficult for small companies.
Many citizens opposed the imposition of new taxes by the government.
The sudden imposition of a curfew took the residents by surprise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enforcement - the act of making people obey a law or rule
  • Antonym: removal - the act of taking something away

Etymology

From Latin 'impositio' (putting upon), from 'imponere' (to place upon).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
an unwelcome demand or burden that is unfairly placed on someone
an unwelcome burden or demand

Examples

I hope it's not an imposition for you to stay late and help us.
He considered the request an unreasonable imposition on his time.
She apologized for the imposition of asking for another favor.

Synset Examples

he listened but resented the imposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - a heavy load, especially one that's difficult to carry; a figurative obligation
  • Antonym: privilege - a special right or advantage not enjoyed by others; the opposite of being imposed upon

Etymology

From Latin 'impositio' (a laying upon); from 'imponere' (to impose).
