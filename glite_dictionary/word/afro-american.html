Afro-American

1. ADJ

/ ˌæfroʊ əˈmɛrɪkən / (AmE), / ˌafrəʊ əˈmɛrɪkən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Americans of African ancestry
relating to African-American people or culture

Examples

The museum featured an exhibition of Afro-American art and music.
He researched the role of Afro-American writers in early 20th-century literature.
Many universities offer courses in Afro-American history and studies.

Synset Examples

African-American culture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African-American - relating to Americans of African descent

Etymology

Formed from Latin 'Afer' (African) + American, by analogy with similar demonyms.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæfroʊ əˈmɛrɪkən / (AmE), / ˌafrəʊ əˈmɛrɪkən / (BrE)
A person in the United States whose ancestors were African, particularly one descended from enslaved Black Africans.
American of African ancestry

Examples

Langston Hughes was a prominent Afro-American poet and social activist.
The museum features an exhibit on Afro-American culture and history.
In the 1960s, the term Afro-American gained popularity among civil rights groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African-American - An American of African descent; more commonly used term today

Etymology

Formed by combining 'Afro-' (referring to Africa) and 'American', first popularized in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-84046311-n
Synset POS: n
