rhinorrhea

1. NOUN

/ raɪ.noʊˈri.ə / (AmE), / raɪ.nəˈrɪə / (BrE)
persistent watery discharge of mucus from the nose, typically occurring with a cold or allergies
watery nasal mucus discharge
medical

Examples

The patient presented with rhinorrhea and frequent sneezing due to seasonal allergies.
Doctors noted persistent rhinorrhea as one of her main symptoms during the examination.
After exposure to the virus, he developed a fever and rhinorrhea within two days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: runny nose - informal term for watery nasal discharge

Etymology

From Greek 'rhis, rhinos' (nose) + 'rhoia' (flow, discharge).
