Pluvianus aegyptius

1. NOUN

/ pluːˈviː.ə.nəs iːˈdʒɪp.ʃi.əs / (AmE), / pluːˈvɪə.nəs iːˈdʒɪp.ʃɪ.əs / (BrE)
A species of bird found in Africa, known for feeding on insect parasites from crocodiles; commonly called the Egyptian plover.
a bird that feeds on pests on crocodiles

Examples

The Pluvianus aegyptius is often seen walking along riverbanks in sub-Saharan Africa.
Many people admire the unique relationship between Pluvianus aegyptius and crocodiles.
Researchers studied how Pluvianus aegyptius helps control crocodile parasites.

Etymology

From Latin 'Pluvianus' (relating to rain) and 'aegyptius' (Egyptian), referencing its habitat and early naming.
