to antagonise

1. VERB

/ ænˈtæɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ænˈtæɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to cause someone to become hostile or unfriendly; to oppose or provoke the anger of someone
provoke hostility or opposition

Examples

His comments did nothing but antagonise the crowd, making the situation worse.
She tried not to antagonise her boss by raising controversial issues at the meeting.
Repeated criticism will only antagonise your colleagues and damage relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - to deliberately make someone feel angry or annoyed
  • Antonym: to conciliate - to stop someone from being angry; to pacify
  • Antonym: to appease - to make someone less angry or hostile

Etymology

From Greek antagōnizesthai (to struggle against), from anti- (against) + agōnizesthai (to contend).

2. VERB

/ ænˈtæɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ænˈtæɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to cause someone to become hostile or unfriendly; to provoke the anger or opposition of someone
provoke hostility or opposition

Examples

Try not to antagonise your classmates if you want to work well together.
His comments only served to antagonise the whole team.
She didn’t mean to antagonise her neighbor with the loud music.

Synset Examples

Don't antagonize your boss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - to cause a particular reaction or feeling, especially anger
  • Antonym: to placate - to make someone less angry or hostile

Etymology

From Greek antagōnizesthai (to struggle against), from anti- (against) + agōnizesthai (to contend for a prize).
