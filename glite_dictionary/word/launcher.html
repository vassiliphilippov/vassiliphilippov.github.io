launcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔntʃər / (AmE), / ˈlɔːntʃə / (BrE)
a device or mechanism used to launch aircraft from a warship
device launching aircraft from a warship

Examples

The launcher was inspected regularly to ensure it could safely send aircraft into the air.
Without a functioning launcher, the ship could not deploy its fighter jets efficiently.
Modern warships are equipped with a hydraulic launcher for quick aircraft takeoff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catapult - a device used to launch aircraft from a ship
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02985687-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔnt͡ʃər / (AmE), / ˈlɔːntʃə / (BrE)
a device designed to launch rockets, missiles, or similar projectiles
device that launches rockets or missiles

Examples

The military tested a new launcher capable of firing multiple rockets at once.
Each launcher on the ship was loaded and ready for deployment.
Technicians inspected the launcher to ensure it was safe before the countdown began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: missile - an object that is forcibly propelled at a target, especially a weapon
  • Synonym: rocket - a missile, spacecraft, or vehicle that moves by expelling gases from a rocket engine

Etymology

Formed from 'launch' + '-er', meaning one that launches.
