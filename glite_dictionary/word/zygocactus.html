Zygocactus

1. NOUN

/ ˌzaɪɡəˈkæktəs / (AmE), / ˌzaɪɡəˈkæktəs / (BrE)
A small genus of Brazilian cacti with flat, fleshy, usually branched segments and showy red or pink flowers followed by red fruits.
genus of Brazilian cacti with showy flowers

Examples

The Zygocactus produces stunning pink blossoms throughout the winter months.
She keeps a Zygocactus on her windowsill because it thrives in indirect light.
Many gardeners value Zygocactus for its unusual jointed stems and colorful flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'zygon' (yoke) + 'cactus', referring to the joined segments.
