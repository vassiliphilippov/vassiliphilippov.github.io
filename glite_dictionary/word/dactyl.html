dactyl

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæktɪl / (AmE), / ˈdæktɪl / (BrE)
A finger or toe in humans, or a corresponding digit or body part in other vertebrates.
a finger or toe; a vertebrate digit

Examples

The examination revealed that the patient's third dactyl was swollen and painful.
Frogs have long, slender dactyls that help them grasp branches in their environment.
Some prehistoric reptiles possessed more than five dactyls on a single limb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digit - a finger, thumb, or toe

Etymology

From Greek daktulos (finger, toe).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdæk.təl / (AmE), / ˈdæk.tɪl / (BrE)
A metrical foot in poetry consisting of one stressed syllable followed by two unstressed syllables.
metrical unit in poetry: stressed-unstressed-unstressed

Examples

The word 'beautiful' is a perfect example of a dactyl in English.
Many epic poems in Greek and Latin use dactyls as a main rhythmic unit.
You can hear the dactyl rhythm in the line, 'Just for a handful of silver he left us.'

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anapest - metrical foot with two unstressed followed by a stressed syllable

Etymology

From Greek daktylos (finger), the sequence mirroring the length of finger bones (one long, two short).
