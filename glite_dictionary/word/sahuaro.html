sahuaro

1. NOUN

/ səˈwɑːroʊ / (AmE), / səˈwɑːrəʊ / (BrE)
A very large, treelike cactus native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, noted for its thick, columnar trunk, few branches, white flowers, and edible red fruit. Also known as 'saguaro'.
giant desert cactus of southwestern U.S.

Examples

The sahuaro is an iconic symbol of the American Southwest, often seen silhouetted against desert sunsets.
During our hike in Arizona, we spotted a massive sahuaro standing more than forty feet tall.
Many birds make their nests in the arms of the sahuaro cactus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saguaro - giant cactus of the Sonoran Desert

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'saguaro', from Spanish (possibly from O'odham language).
