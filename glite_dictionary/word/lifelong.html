lifelong

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪfˌlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf.lɒŋ / (BrE)
Lasting or continuing for the whole of a person's life.
lasting for a whole lifetime

Examples

They have been lifelong friends, ever since they met in kindergarten.
Her lifelong dream was to travel around the world.
He developed a lifelong love of music after hearing his first symphony.

Synset Examples

a lifelong friend
from lifelong habit
his lifelong study of Greek art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perpetual - lasting forever or a very long time
  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period

Etymology

Formed from 'life' + 'long', first recorded in the 18th century.
