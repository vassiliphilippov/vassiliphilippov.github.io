harmonium

1. NOUN

/ hɑrˈmoʊniəm / (AmE), / hɑːˈməʊniəm / (BrE)
A musical instrument with free reeds and a keyboard, in which air is forced through the reeds by bellows, producing sound.
keyboard instrument with free reeds

Examples

The old church still had a harmonium in the corner, used for traditional hymns.
She learned to play folk tunes on the harmonium as a child in India.
A musician skillfully pumped the bellows of the harmonium, filling the room with warm melodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reed organ - a keyboard instrument producing sound by air passing through reeds
  • Synonym: accordion - portable keyboard instrument with free reeds and bellows

Etymology

From French 'harmonium', from Greek 'harmonia' (harmony, agreement).
