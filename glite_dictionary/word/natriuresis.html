natriuresis

1. NOUN

/ ˌneɪtri.jʊˈriː.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌneɪ.tri.jʊˈriː.sɪs / (BrE)
the excretion of an unusually large amount of sodium in the urine
excretion of excess sodium in urine
medical

Examples

In patients with heart failure, increased natriuresis may help reduce fluid overload.
The doctor ordered tests to check for abnormal natriuresis as part of the diagnosis.
Some medications can induce natriuresis to manage high blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diuresis - increased or excessive production of urine
  • Antonym: antidiuresis - decreased or suppressed urine production

Etymology

From Latin 'natrium' (sodium) and Greek 'ouresis' (urination).
