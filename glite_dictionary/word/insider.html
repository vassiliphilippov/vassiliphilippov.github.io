insider

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsaɪdər / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪdə / (BrE)
a person within an organization or group, especially an officer of a corporation, who has access to private or confidential information about its operations
person with privileged access inside a group or company

Examples

As an insider, she was required to report all of her stock trades to the regulatory authorities.
Rumors about the merger were leaked to the press by an anonymous insider.
Only an insider would have known about the changes before the public announcement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outsider - a person who is not a member of a particular group or organization

Etymology

From 'inside' + '-er'.
