insaneness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈseɪn.nəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈseɪn.nɪs / (BrE)
(Archaic) The state of being insane; madness, particularly as a legal condition.
archaic term for insanity
archaic

Examples

The court had to consider the prisoner's insaneness as a possible defense.
Writers of the past often referred to insaneness rather than insanity.
His sudden insaneness shocked the entire community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insanity - state of being seriously mentally ill; madness

Etymology

From Latin 'insanus' (mad, insane) + English suffix '-ness'.
