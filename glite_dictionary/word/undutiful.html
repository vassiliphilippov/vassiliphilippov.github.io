undutiful

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈduːtəfəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈdjuːtɪfəl / (BrE)
not showing proper respect, care, or a sense of duty, especially toward parents or authority.
not showing proper respect or duty

Examples

Her undutiful behavior disappointed her family greatly.
It is considered undutiful for children to ignore their parents' wishes in some cultures.
The novel's protagonist struggles with feelings of being undutiful toward his late father.

Synset Examples

impious toward one's parents
an undutiful son

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dutiful - showing a sense of duty or respect

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix un- (not) to dutiful.
