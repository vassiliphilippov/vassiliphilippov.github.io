assuagement

1. NOUN

/ əˈsweɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / əˈsweɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
The feeling of relief or comfort that occurs when something unpleasant, burdensome, or painful is lessened or removed.
the feeling of relief from a burden

Examples

His sense of assuagement was obvious after the exam results were announced.
The warm words of his friend brought immediate assuagement to his troubled mind.
She found assuagement in her favorite music after a stressful day.

Synset Examples

as he heard the news he was suddenly flooded with relief

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relief - the removal or reduction of something burdensome or unpleasant

Etymology

From Old French *assuagement*, from *assuager* (to alleviate), from Latin *assuaviare*.
