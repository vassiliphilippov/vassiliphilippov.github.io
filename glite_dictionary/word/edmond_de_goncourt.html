Edmond de Goncourt

1. PROPN

/ ɛdˈmɒ̃ də gɔ̃ˈkuːr / (AmE), / ɛdˈmɒ̃ də gɒ̃ˈkʊər / (BrE)
(1822–1896) A French writer who, with his brother Jules de Goncourt, collaborated on many works and established the Prix Goncourt literary award.
French writer; founder of the Prix Goncourt

Examples

Edmond de Goncourt and his brother are credited with founding one of France's most prestigious literary prizes.
The works of Edmond de Goncourt often reflect the social and cultural changes of 19th-century France.
With his bequest, Edmond de Goncourt ensured a lasting legacy for French literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jules de Goncourt - French writer and Edmond's brother and collaborator
  • Synonym: Prix Goncourt - prestigious French literary prize established by Edmond de Goncourt
