sillabub

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪl.ə.bʌb / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.ə.bʌb / (BrE)
a cold dessert made of sweetened cream thickened with wine or other liquor, often flavored and sometimes served with fruit
sweetened cream dessert with wine or liquor

Examples

The hostess prepared a bowl of sillabub for the eighteenth-century themed dinner party.
After the meal, guests enjoyed sillabub topped with raspberries and orange zest.
Historical cookbooks often include recipes for sillabub as a festive dessert.

Etymology

From early modern English 'syllabub', of uncertain origin, attested from the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪl.ə.bʌb / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.ə.bʌb / (BrE)
A sweet, frothy drink made with milk or cream, typically mixed with wine or cider and sometimes flavored with spices.
sweet drink of milk and wine/cider

Examples

At the old inn, we enjoyed a traditional sillabub served with freshly baked pastries.
The recipe for sillabub calls for whipping cream, white wine, sugar, and lemon zest.
During the festival, they offered sillabub alongside other historical desserts.

Etymology

From early modern English 'syllabub', origin unclear, possibly from dialectal or onomatopoeic roots relating to frothing or bubbling.
