imperialist

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɪriəlɪst / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪəriəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or believes in imperialism, especially the policy of extending a country's power and influence through colonization, use of military force, or other means
supporter of imperialism

Examples

The politician was widely criticized as an imperialist for supporting overseas expansion.
Many people protested against the actions of the imperialists during the era of colonization.
Some historians argue that the country's foreign policy was driven by imperialist ambitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colonialist - someone who supports or practices colonialism
  • Antonym: anti-imperialist - someone opposed to imperialism

Etymology

From imperial + -ist, formed in the 19th century from Latin 'imperium' (command, empire).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɪriəlɪst / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪəriəlɪst / (BrE)
relating to or supporting imperialism or the extension of a nation's power through diplomacy or military force
relating to imperialism or its supporters

Examples

The country adopted an imperialist policy to expand its influence overseas.
Many historians have criticized the imperialist ambitions of the great powers in the 19th century.
The protesters denounced the government’s imperialist actions in neighboring territories.

Synset Examples

imperialistic wars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperialistic - relating to imperialism (adjective)
  • Antonym: anti-imperialist - opposed to imperialism or its practice

Etymology

From imperialism + -ist, based on the word 'imperial'.
