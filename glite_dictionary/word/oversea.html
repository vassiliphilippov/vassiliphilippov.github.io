oversea

1. ADV

/ ˌoʊvərˈsi / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsiː / (BrE)
beyond or across the sea; to or in a foreign country across the sea.
across the sea; in a foreign country

Examples

Many young professionals choose to work oversea to gain international experience.
Her brother studied oversea for most of his undergraduate degree.
The company frequently sends employees oversea for training and development.

Synset Examples

He lived overseas for many years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overseas - in or to a foreign country across the sea; across the sea (adverb, adjective)

Etymology

From Middle English oversea, from over + sea.

2. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈsi / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsi / (BrE)
situated, happening, or passing over or across the sea; relating to or involving foreign countries separated by the sea.
across or over the sea; foreign (especially by sea)

Examples

The company established oversea offices to expand its international presence.
Many students dream of studying at an oversea university to experience a new culture.
Historically, much of the nation's wealth came from oversea trade routes.

Synset Examples

some overseas trade in grain arose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overseas - situated, happening, or to or from a foreign country across the sea

Etymology

From over + sea; originally meant 'beyond the sea'.
