scaled

1. ADJ

/ skeɪld / (AmE), / skeɪld / (BrE)
(of armor) composed of overlapping metal plates attached to a leather or fabric backing, resembling the scales of a fish or reptile.
having overlapping metal plates (of armor)

Examples

The knight wore a scaled cuirass that gleamed in the sunlight.
Museum exhibits included a rare scaled helmet from the early Middle Ages.
Historians studied the effectiveness of scaled armor compared to chain mail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scale armor - armor made of small, overlapping metal plates

2. ADJ

/ skeɪld / (AmE), / skeɪld / (BrE)
(of an animal) having the body covered or partially covered with thin horny plates or scales, as in some fish and reptiles.
having a body covered with scales

Examples

The scaled body of the snake helps it move smoothly across rough terrain.
Many species of fish are scaled, providing them with protection from predators and parasites.
The lizard's scaled skin glistened in the sunlight as it basked on the rock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scale - a thin plate covering the skin of fish and reptiles
  • Antonym: scaleless - lacking scales on the body, especially of fish or reptiles
