frolicsome

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrɑlɪksəm / (AmE), / ˈfrɒlɪksəm / (BrE)
full of playful and lively fun; inclined to frolic or be merry
playful and lively

Examples

The children were frolicsome as they played in the park on a sunny afternoon.
Her frolicsome spirit made every gathering feel like a celebration.
Despite his age, the old dog remained frolicsome whenever visitors arrived.

Synset Examples

frolicsome students celebrated their graduation with parties and practical jokes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: playful - fond of games and fun; lighthearted
  • Synonym: boisterous - noisy, energetic, and cheerful
  • Antonym: solemn - serious, not cheerful or playful

Etymology

From frolic +‎ -some, modeled after similar English adjectives formed with '-some'.
