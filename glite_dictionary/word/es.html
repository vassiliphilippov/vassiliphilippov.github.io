Es

1. NOUN

/ iː ɛs / (AmE), / iː ɛs / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for einsteinium, a radioactive transuranic element produced by bombarding plutonium with neutrons
chemical symbol for einsteinium

Examples

The radioactive isotope Es-253 was used in scientific experiments to investigate new element behaviors.
Researchers discovered Es in the debris of the first hydrogen bomb explosion.
Pure Es is extremely rare and has no significant commercial applications due to its high radioactivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: einsteinium - a radioactive transuranic element with atomic number 99

Etymology

From the name 'einsteinium,' derived after physicist Albert Einstein, with 'Es' as its official element symbol.
