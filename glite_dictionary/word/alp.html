alp

1. NOUN

/ ælp / (AmE), / ælp / (BrE)
a high mountain, especially any of the mountains in the Alps; any high, snow-covered peak
a high mountain, especially in the Alps

Examples

The snow-capped alp towered over the valley, creating a breathtaking view.
He dreamed of climbing every alp in Switzerland during his lifetime.
From the train window, she admired the rugged beauty of each distant alp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain - a large natural elevation of the earth's surface
  • Degree Lower: hill - a naturally raised area of land, not as high or craggy as a mountain

Etymology

From Latin 'Alpes', the name of the mountain range separating Italy from the rest of Europe.
