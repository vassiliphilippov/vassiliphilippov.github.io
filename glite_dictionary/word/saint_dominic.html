Saint Dominic

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈdɑmɪnɪk / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈdɒmɪnɪk / (BrE)
(circa 1170–1221) Spanish priest who founded the Dominican Order, a Roman Catholic religious order.
founder of the Dominican Order

Examples

Saint Dominic founded the Dominican Order in the early 13th century.
Many churches across Europe are named after Saint Dominic.
The life of Saint Dominic inspired many followers to join his religious order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dominican Order - Roman Catholic religious order founded by Saint Dominic
