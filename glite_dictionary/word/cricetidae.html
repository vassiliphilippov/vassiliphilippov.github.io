Cricetidae

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˈsiːtɪˌdi / (AmE), / kraɪˈsiːtɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of mostly small rodents found mainly in the New World, including mice, voles, lemmings, and hamsters
family of small New World rodents

Examples

The family Cricetidae includes many species of hamsters that are popular as pets.
Research on the evolutionary history of Cricetidae has shed light on rodent diversification.
Several members of Cricetidae, such as lemmings and voles, inhabit cold northern environments.

Etymology

From New Latin Cricetidae, based on Cricetus (the type genus, meaning 'hamster').

2. NOUN

/ kraɪˈsiːtɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kraɪˈsiːtɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of mostly small rodents native to the New World, including hamsters, voles, lemmings, and New World mice.
a family of small New World rodents

Examples

Scientists believe that many species in the Cricetidae family evolved to adapt to various habitats across the Americas.
The golden hamster, a popular pet, actually belongs to the Cricetidae family.
Unlike the Muridae, the Cricetidae include lemmings and voles as part of their group.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Muridae - the family including Old World mice and rats
  • Synonym: hamster - a small rodent commonly kept as a pet, member of Cricetidae

Etymology

From New Latin Cricetidae, based on the type genus Cricetus (the hamster).
