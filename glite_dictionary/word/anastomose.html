to anastomose

1. VERB

/ əˈnæstəˌmoʊz / (AmE), / əˈnæstəməʊz / (BrE)
(of blood vessels, tubular structures, or parts) to join or open into each other by anastomosis; to connect surgically or naturally.
join or connect by anastomosis
medical

Examples

The surgeon will anastomose the arteries to restore blood flow.
Veins and arteries often anastomose to ensure continuous circulation.
When two segments of bowel are anastomosed, it allows passage through the digestive tract.

Synset Examples

anastomose blood vessels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuse - to join together to form a single entity
  • Antonym: separate - to set or keep apart

Etymology

From Greek anastomoun (to provide with a mouth), from anastomosis.

2. VERB

/ əˈnæstəˌmoʊz / (AmE), / əˈnastəməʊz / (BrE)
(of tubular structures, especially blood vessels or ducts) to join or connect by forming a continuous channel or network.
join or connect (as blood vessels)
medical

Examples

During surgery, the doctors carefully anastomosed the two ends of the intestine.
Roots of neighboring trees may anastomose underground to share nutrients and water.
Several small blood vessels anastomose to form a larger vein in this region.

Synset Examples

the blood vessels anastomose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to connect - to join together so as to provide access or communication

Etymology

From Greek anastomōsis (to provide with a mouth or opening).
