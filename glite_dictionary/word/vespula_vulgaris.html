Vespula vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ vɛsˈpjuːlə vʌlˈɡɛrɪs / (AmE), / vɛsˈpjuːlə vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A common species of wasp found in Eurasia, known for its yellow and black markings and social colonies.
a species of common wasp

Examples

The nest in our attic turned out to belong to Vespula vulgaris.
Vespula vulgaris is often mistaken for other yellow jacket species due to its similar appearance.
Researchers observed the behavior of Vespula vulgaris colonies during late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wasp - a stinging winged insect related to bees and ants

Etymology

From Latin: 'Vespula' (little wasp) and 'vulgaris' (common).
