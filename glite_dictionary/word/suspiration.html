suspiration

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌspɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an audible sigh or exhalation; the act of breathing out with a sound, often expressing emotion.
an audible sigh or exhalation

Examples

With a weary suspiration, she put down the heavy box and looked out the window.
A long suspiration escaped him as he finally received the good news.
There was a slight suspiration in the room after the announcement, as people absorbed the information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sigh - a long, deep audible breath expressing fatigue, relief, or longing

Etymology

From Latin suspiratio, from suspirare ('to sigh').
