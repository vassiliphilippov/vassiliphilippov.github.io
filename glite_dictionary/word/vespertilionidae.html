Vespertilionidae

1. NOUN

/ vɛspərˌtɪliˈɒnɪdiː / (AmE), / vɛspəˌtɪlɪˈɒnɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of bats commonly found in temperate regions, known as the evening or common bats
family of common temperate bats
medical

Examples

The Vespertilionidae are one of the largest families of bats worldwide.
Many species in the Vespertilionidae family are found in forests and urban areas alike.
Researchers study the Vespertilionidae to learn more about echolocation in bats.

Etymology

From Latin vespertilio (bat) and the taxonomic suffix -idae, used for animal families.

2. NOUN

/ vɛspərˌtɪliˈɒnɪdi / (AmE), / vɛspəˌtɪliˈɒnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of bats comprising most of the common species found in temperate regions worldwide.
family of common temperate bats

Examples

The Vespertilionidae family includes nearly 400 species of bats worldwide.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of several Vespertilionidae species.
Many familiar bats in Europe and North America belong to the Vespertilionidae.

Etymology

From New Latin Vespertilionidae, after genus Vespertilio (Latin for 'bat').
