to submerge

1. VERB

/ səbˈmɝrdʒ / (AmE), / səbˈmɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to cover or hide completely, especially by being under a surface or by making something imperceptible
to cover or hide completely

Examples

The entire village was submerged by floodwaters after the storm.
Her doubts were quickly submerged by a wave of excitement.
The announcement was submerged in the noise of the cheering crowd.

Synset Examples

I was drowned in work
The noise drowned out her speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to immerse - to plunge or dip completely under a liquid

Etymology

From Latin submergere, from sub- (under) + mergere (to plunge).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01341976-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ səbˈmɝːdʒ / (AmE), / səbˈmɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to put or cause to be under water or another liquid, usually so as to cover completely
put under water or liquid

Examples

Divers must submerge themselves for several minutes to explore the wreck.
She forgot to submerge the vegetables completely while boiling them.
During the experiment, the metal block was submerged in water to measure its displacement.

Synset Examples

submerge your head completely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to immerse - to put something completely into liquid
  • Antonym: to emerge - to come out into view, especially from water or a liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'submergere', from 'sub-' (under) + 'mergere' (to plunge, dip).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01581103-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ səbˈmɝrdʒ / (AmE), / səbˈmɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to cause something to be completely covered with or under a liquid, especially water
cover or sink below liquid

Examples

Heavy rains can submerge the entire road in a matter of minutes.
The divers carefully submerged themselves into the icy lake.
He prefers to submerge vegetables in cold water before cooking them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to immerse - to put something completely into a liquid
  • Antonym: surface - to come up or appear at the surface of a liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'submergere' (to plunge under).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00218126-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ səbˈmɝːdʒ / (AmE), / səbˈmɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to go below the surface of water or another liquid, or to cause something to do so
go under water or liquid

Examples

The floodwaters began to submerge the fields along the riverbank.
Divers must submerge completely before starting their underwater tasks.
She quickly submerged the vegetables in boiling water to blanch them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to immerse - to put something completely under a liquid
  • Antonym: surface - to come up or rise to the surface, as from water

Etymology

From Latin 'submergere': 'sub-' (under) + 'mergere' (to plunge).
