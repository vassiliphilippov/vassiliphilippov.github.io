Akhenaten

1. PROPN

/ ɑːkəˈnɑːtən / (AmE), / ˌækɪˈnɑːtən / (BrE)
an ancient Egyptian pharaoh (reigned c. 1353–1336 BC) known for abandoning traditional Egyptian polytheism and introducing worship centered on the sun disk, Aten.
Egyptian pharaoh who promoted sun worship

Examples

Akhenaten is remembered for transforming Egypt's religion to focus on a single god, Aten.
The artistic style of Akhenaten's reign was notably different from that of earlier pharaohs.
Historians debate whether Akhenaten was a visionary or a heretic.

Etymology

From Egyptian 'Akhenaten', meaning 'Effective for Aten', Aten being the sun disk deity.
