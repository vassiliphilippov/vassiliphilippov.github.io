strabotomy

1. NOUN

/ strəˈbɑtəmi / (AmE), / strəˈbɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical operation involving the cutting of an eye muscle or tendon to correct strabismus (misalignment of the eyes)
eye surgery for correcting strabismus
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist recommended a strabotomy to correct the child's severe eye misalignment.
After undergoing strabotomy, the patient's double vision was significantly reduced.
Advances in strabotomy techniques have improved outcomes for people with strabismus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strabismus - misalignment of the eyes

Etymology

From Greek 'strabos' (squinting) + '-tomy' (cutting).
