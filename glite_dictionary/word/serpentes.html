Serpentes

1. NOUN

/ sərˈpɛnteɪz / (AmE), / səˈpɛntiːz / (BrE)
the suborder of reptiles comprising all snakes; used chiefly in scientific or zoological contexts
suborder of reptiles: snakes

Examples

The suborder Serpentes is distinguished by the absence of limbs and the presence of a highly flexible body.
Most species in the Serpentes suborder are nonvenomous, although some have highly potent venom.
Herpetologists study the evolution of Serpentes to understand adaptations in snakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snake - a long, legless, carnivorous reptile

Etymology

From Latin 'serpentes', plural of 'serpens' (snake).
