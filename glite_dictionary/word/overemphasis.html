overemphasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvɚˈɛmfəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˈɛm.fə.sɪs / (BrE)
Excessive or undue emphasis on something.
excessive emphasis

Examples

The overemphasis on test scores can lead to a narrow education.
Critics argue that there is an overemphasis on appearance in the fashion industry.
Her overemphasis on punctuality annoyed many of her friends.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: emphasis - special importance or attention given to something
  • Antonym: understatement - the presentation of something as being smaller or less important than it actually is

Etymology

From over- (excessive) + emphasis (from Greek 'emphainein', to indicate).
