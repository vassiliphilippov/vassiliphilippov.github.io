Paleacrita vernata

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪliˈækrɪtə vərˈneɪtə / (AmE), / ˌpeɪliˈækrɪtə vəˈnɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of moth whose larvae are known as spring cankerworms, often considered a pest of shade trees.
a moth species; spring cankerworm larva

Examples

The larvae of Paleacrita vernata often defoliate oak and elm trees in early spring.
Gardeners took steps to control Paleacrita vernata infestations in their yards.
Entomologists studied the life cycle of Paleacrita vernata to develop better pest management strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spring cankerworm - common name for the larval stage of Paleacrita vernata

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name Paleacrita and species epithet vernata meaning 'of the spring' in Latin.
