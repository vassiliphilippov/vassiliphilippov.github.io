offhandedly

1. ADV

/ ɔfˈhændɪdli / (AmE), / ɒfˈhændɪdli / (BrE)
in a casually inconsiderate or unprepared manner; without previous thought or consideration
in a casually inconsiderate or unprepared way

Examples

He offhandedly dismissed her suggestion, barely pausing to consider it.
She apologized offhandedly, not seeming to care about the mistake she made.
The manager offhandedly mentioned a possible promotion, but gave no further details.

Synset Examples

replied offhand, his mind a million miles away
she threw him over offhandedly without even a Dear-John letter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casually - in a relaxed or informal manner
  • Synonym: spontaneously - without premeditation or external stimulus
  • Antonym: deliberately - in a careful and intentional way

Etymology

From 'offhand' + '-ly', where 'offhand' comes from the phrase 'off hand' meaning without preparation.

2. ADV

/ ˌɔfˈhændɪdli / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈhændɪdli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of previous thought or preparation; casually or without careful consideration.
without preparation; casually

Examples

She answered the question offhandedly, as if it required no thought.
He apologized offhandedly, not seeming to care much about the mistake.
During the meeting, Jane made a suggestion offhandedly that sparked an interesting discussion.

Synset Examples

couldn't give the figures offhand
we decided offhand to go to Canada
she had made these remarks offhandedly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offhand - without preparation or consideration; casual
  • Synonym: spontaneously - done without being planned or rehearsed

Etymology

From 'offhand' + adverbial suffix '-ly', where 'offhand' is from the phrase 'off hand', meaning unprepared.
