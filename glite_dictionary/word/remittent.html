remittent

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈmɪtnt / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪtnt / (BrE)
(of a disease or symptom) characterized by periods where the severity lessens but does not disappear entirely.
fluctuating in intensity (esp. disease)
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited a remittent fever that rose and fell throughout the week.
He was diagnosed with a remittent illness, which improved at times but never fully disappeared.
Some tropical infections cause a remittent pattern of symptoms rather than constant severity.

Synset Examples

a remittent fever

Etymology

From Latin remittere (to send back, slacken), via Medieval Latin remittens, remittent- (slackening).
