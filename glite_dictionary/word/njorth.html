Njorth

1. PROPN

/ nyɔrth / (AmE), / njɔːθ / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, the chief of the Vanir; god of the sea, winds, and prosperity, and father of Frey and Freya.
Norse god of sea, winds, and prosperity

Examples

Many sailors prayed to Njorth for safe voyages across the treacherous seas.
In Norse mythology, Njorth was known for bringing wealth and good fortune to his followers.
As the father of Frey and Freya, Njorth played a significant role among the Vanir gods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Freyr - Norse god of fertility and son of Njorth
  • Synonym: Freya - Norse goddess and daughter of Njorth
  • Synonym: Nerthus - Teutonic earth goddess sometimes associated with Njorth

Etymology

From Old Norse Njǫrðr, related to Proto-Germanic *Nerþuz.
