waspish

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɑːspɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɒspɪʃ / (BrE)
easily irritated or quickly angered, often showing it in speech or manner; snappish or spiteful.
irritable; snappish or spiteful

Examples

Her waspish remarks made everyone in the meeting uncomfortable.
He adopted a waspish tone when asked about his recent failures.
It's best to avoid her in the mornings, as she can be quite waspish before coffee.

Synset Examples

bristly exchanges between the White House and the press
he became prickly and spiteful
witty and waspish about his colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Antonym: amiable - friendly and pleasant

Etymology

From 'wasp', referring to the insect's tendency to sting, plus '-ish'.
