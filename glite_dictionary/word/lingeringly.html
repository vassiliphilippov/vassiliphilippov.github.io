lingeringly

1. ADV

/ ˈlɪŋɡərɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋɡərɪŋli / (BrE)
in a slow, leisurely, or prolonged way; in a manner that is hesitant to end or depart
in a slow or prolonged way

Examples

She gazed lingeringly at the old photograph before putting it away.
The fragrance of the perfume remained lingeringly in the air long after she left.
He spoke lingeringly, as if reluctant for the conversation to end.

Synset Examples

her voice was swift, yet ever the last words fell lingeringly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slowly - at a slow speed; not quickly
  • Antonym: quickly - at a fast speed; rapidly

Etymology

From 'lingering' (remaining or lasting for a long time) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
