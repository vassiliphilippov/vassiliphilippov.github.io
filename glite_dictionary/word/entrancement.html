entrancement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtrænsmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɑːnsmənt / (BrE)
the state of being filled with wonder and delight; a feeling of enchantment or fascination
state of delighted enchantment

Examples

She gazed at the magician's performance in utter entrancement, unable to look away.
The audience watched the ballet with entrancement, captivated by every graceful move.
He listened in entrancement as the storyteller brought the legend to life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enchantment - a feeling of great pleasure and attraction; the state of being enchanted
  • Synonym: fascination - the power of fascinating or captivating; being intensely interested
  • Antonym: boredom - the state of being bored; lack of interest

Etymology

From French 'enchanter' (to enchant) via 'entrance' + '-ment'.
