adscripted

1. ADJ

/ ædˈskrɪptɪd / (AmE), / ədˈskrɪptɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) bound to a particular tract of land and whose status or service can be transferred along with the land to a new owner.
bound to land; transferable status
archaic

Examples

In feudal societies, peasants could be adscripted to the land, serving whichever lord owned it.
The estate’s adscripted workers had no right to leave without the owner's permission.
Laws prohibiting the sale of adscripted laborers without the land were passed in that era.

Synset Examples

an adscript serf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serf - an agricultural laborer bound under the feudal system to work on his lord's estate
  • Antonym: free - not under the control or in the power of another; able to act or be done as one wishes

Etymology

From Latin 'adscriptus', past participle of 'adscribere' (to enroll, assign).
