cutout

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌtˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈkʌt.aʊt / (BrE)
a shape or section that has been cut out or is intended to be cut out from a larger material or surface
a shape or piece cut out from something

Examples

The children decorated the walls with colorful paper cutouts of animals and flowers.
The mechanic installed a metal cutout over the damaged section of the panel.
She traced the pattern onto fabric before making the final cutout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut-out - a shape cut out from paper, card, etc.

Etymology

From the verb 'cut out', combining 'cut' + 'out'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌtˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈkʌt.aʊt / (BrE)
a device or switch that automatically interrupts an electric circuit when it is overloaded, to prevent damage or hazards
a switch for circuit interruption in case of overload

Examples

The electrician replaced the faulty cutout to protect the building's wiring from overloads.
Modern power systems rely on an automatic cutout to ensure safety during electrical faults.
If the current exceeds a safe limit, the cutout will shut off the circuit to prevent damage.

Etymology

From 'cut' + 'out', referring to the device's function of cutting out power.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03158614-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkʌt.aʊt / (AmE), / ˈkʌt.aʊt / (BrE)
a photograph or image from which the background has been removed, leaving only the main subject
photo with background removed

Examples

The magazine featured a cutout of the actress displayed on a vibrant color background.
She sent me a cutout of the company logo to use on the website.
To make the poster, we used a cutout of the team captain from last season’s group photo.

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'cut out', meaning to remove by cutting, adapted to photography and design contexts.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03158487-n
Synset POS: n
