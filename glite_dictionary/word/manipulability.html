manipulability

1. NOUN

/ məˌnɪpjəˌlɪˈbɪləti / (AmE), / məˌnɪpjʊləˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being easily handled or controlled by skilled movements, especially by the hands.
quality of being easy to handle with the hands

Examples

The manipulability of the new robotic arm allows surgeons to perform more precise operations.
High manipulability is essential for tools designed for delicate assembly work.
The cube's small size increases its manipulability for young children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maneuverability - quality of being easily moved or steered

Etymology

Formed from 'manipulate' + '-bility', after 'ability'.
