smouldering

1. ADJ

/ ˈsmoʊldərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsməʊldərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of anger or emotion) showing barely concealed strong feelings, especially anger or passion.
showing barely suppressed anger or emotion

Examples

She answered with a smouldering glare that made her anger obvious to everyone in the room.
His smouldering resentment eventually erupted into a heated argument.
A smouldering silence hung between them after the harsh exchange of words.

Synset Examples

her tone was … conversational although … her eyes were smoldering

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seething - filled with intense but unexpressed anger
  • Antonym: calm - not showing anger or emotion

Etymology

From the verb 'smoulder', from Middle English 'smolderen' (to smother).
