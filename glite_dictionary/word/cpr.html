CPR

1. NOUN

/ siː piː ɑːr / (AmE), / siː piː ɑː / (BrE)
an emergency medical procedure involving chest compressions and artificial respiration, used to restore heartbeat and breathing in someone who has stopped breathing or whose heart has stopped
emergency procedure to revive someone who has stopped breathing
medical

Examples

The nurse performed CPR on the patient until the ambulance arrived.
It's crucial to learn CPR in case of a medical emergency at home or work.
After collapsing on the field, the athlete was revived thanks to immediate CPR.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardiopulmonary resuscitation - the full term for CPR, emergency medical procedure to restore breathing and heartbeat

Etymology

Acronym for 'cardiopulmonary resuscitation'.
