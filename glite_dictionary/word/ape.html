ape

1. NOUN

/ eɪp / (AmE), / eɪp / (BrE)
a primate of a group that includes the gibbons, great apes, and humans, distinguished by having no tail or a very short tail
tailless or short-tailed primate

Examples

Chimpanzees are a type of ape closely related to humans.
The zoo recently welcomed a newborn ape into its primate exhibit.
Some species of ape use simple tools to obtain food.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monkey - a primate with a long tail, typically smaller and more agile than apes

Etymology

From Old English *apa*, of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ eɪp / (AmE), / eɪp / (BrE)
A person who resembles or behaves in a way reminiscent of a nonhuman primate, sometimes used insultingly.
person resembling a nonhuman primate
offensive

Examples

Some bullies at school called him an ape because of his size and posture.
The cartoon depicted the character as an ape, exaggerating his features for comic effect.
It's offensive to refer to someone as an ape due to the hurtful connotation.

to imitate someone in an unoriginal or mocking way, often closely copying their actions or behavior
imitate or mimic (often mockingly)

Examples

He tried to ape her dance moves, but couldn't keep up with the rhythm.
Some comedians ape celebrities to make their audience laugh.
She was annoyed when her brother would ape everything she said and did.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imitate - to copy someone or something
  • Synonym: mock - to imitate someone in a way intended to ridicule
