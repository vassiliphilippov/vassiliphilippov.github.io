Picardy

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɪkərdi / (AmE), / ˈpɪkədi / (BrE)
a historical region in northern France, located on the English Channel, known for its role in French history and World War I.
region of northern France

Examples

Many famous battles of World War I took place in Picardy.
The beautiful countryside of Picardy attracts tourists from around the world.
She spent her summer vacation exploring the medieval towns of Picardy.

Etymology

From French Picardie, the name of the region.
