coastline

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊstˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkəʊstˌlaɪn / (BrE)
the outline or shape of a coast, especially as seen from above or on a map; the land along a coast regarded as a continuous line
outline or shape of a coast

Examples

The rugged coastline offered dramatic views of cliffs and crashing waves.
Tourists love to drive along the Mediterranean coastline for its beautiful scenery.
Much of the country's coastline is protected as a national park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoreline - the line along which a large body of water meets the land

Etymology

Formed from 'coast' + 'line', first recorded in the mid-19th century.
