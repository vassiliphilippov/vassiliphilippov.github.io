periwigged

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛrɪˈwɪɡd / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈwɪɡd / (BrE)
Wearing a periwig, especially as was fashionable among men in the 17th and 18th centuries.
wearing a period-style wig
archaic

Examples

The periwigged gentlemen strolled through the candlelit ballroom with great elegance.
In many old portraits, judges are depicted as periwigged and dressed in elaborate robes.
He attended the masquerade in a periwigged costume, reminiscent of the French court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wigged - wearing a wig

Etymology

From 'periwig' (a wig), itself from French 'perruque'.
