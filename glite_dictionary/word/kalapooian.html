Kalapooian

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈpuːiən / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈpuːiən / (BrE)
the extinct Penutian language once spoken by the Kalapuya people of western Oregon, USA
extinct language of the Kalapuya people

Examples

Very few recordings of the Kalapooian language survive, making its study a challenge for linguists.
Her research focused on the grammar of Kalapooian and its relationship to other Native American languages.
The last known speaker of Kalapooian passed away in the mid-twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Penutian - a proposed family of Native American languages

Etymology

From Kalapuya, the name of the Native American people of western Oregon, plus English suffix -an.
