lion

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.ən / (BrE)
(astrology) A person born while the sun is in the zodiac sign Leo, typically between July 23 and August 22.
person born under Leo (astrology)

Examples

As a lion, she is known for her confidence and leadership.
Many people believe that being a lion gives them a strong and creative personality.
My brother is a lion, born at the end of July.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leo - the zodiac sign represented by the lion

Etymology

From Latin Leo (the zodiac sign), from Greek 'leōn' (lion).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.ən / (BrE)
a person who is much admired or celebrated, especially a celebrity who is widely sought after
celebrated or sought-after person

Examples

The young novelist quickly became the social lion of the literary scene in London.
After his breakthrough performance, the actor was the lion of fashionable parties and events.
She was regarded as the lion among artists, always in demand at every gathering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebrity - a famous person

Etymology

Extended figurative use from the fame and majesty associated with the animal, the lion.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.ən / (BrE)
a large wild animal of the cat family with a tawny coat, found in Africa and India, the male of which has a shaggy mane
large wild feline with a mane

Examples

The lion is often called the king of the jungle.
We saw a male lion with a magnificent mane at the wildlife reserve.
Young cubs stay close to the pride for protection in a group of lions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiger - large predatory cat native to Asia with a striped coat
  • Synonym: leopard - large spotted wild cat native to Africa and Asia

Etymology

From Latin 'leo', from Ancient Greek 'λέων' (leōn).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02131817-n
Synset POS: n
