wheedling

1. NOUN

/ ˈwidlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwiːdlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of persuading someone by means of flattery or gentle, persistent urging
persuasion by flattery or coaxing

Examples

Her wheedling eventually convinced him to lend her the car.
The child's wheedling voice made it hard for her parents to say no.
Despite all his wheedling, she refused to change her mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coaxing - the act of gently persuading someone to do something
  • Antonym: bullying - the act of intimidating or threatening to persuade

Etymology

From the verb 'wheedle', possibly of Scottish origin, perhaps imitative.
