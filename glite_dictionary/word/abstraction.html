abstraction

1. NOUN

/ æbˈstrækʃən / (AmE), / æbˈstrækʃən / (BrE)
the process of considering something independently of its associations or attributes, often by formulating general concepts from specific instances
process of forming general concepts

Examples

The mathematician relied on abstraction to identify patterns among the various equations.
Through abstraction, we can group different animals into categories based on shared characteristics.
Effective programming often requires a high level of abstraction to manage complex systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generalization - the act of forming broad concepts from specific instances

Etymology

From Latin abstractio (a drawing away), from abstrahere (to draw away).

2. NOUN

/ æbˈstræk.ʃən / (AmE), / æbˈstræk.ʃən / (BrE)
a painting in which the representation of real objects is altered or omitted to focus on shapes, colors, and forms rather than realistic depiction
a work of abstract art

Examples

The gallery featured several stunning abstractions alongside more traditional works.
She bought a brightly colored abstraction to hang above her sofa.
Critics praised the artist's use of texture and movement in his latest abstraction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstract - a painting characterized by non-representational forms and colors

Etymology

From Latin abstractio (a drawing away); modern art sense developed in the 20th century.

5. NOUN

/ æbˈstrækʃən / (AmE), / əbˈstrækʃən / (BrE)
a general concept or idea that is not related to any specific instance or object
general concept or idea

Examples

The philosopher spoke about justice as an abstraction rather than a practical policy.
Many people struggle to understand mathematics because it often deals with pure abstraction.
Love, as an abstraction, is difficult to define but easy to feel.

Related Terms

6. NOUN

/ əbˈstrækʃən / (AmE), / əbˈstrækʃən / (BrE)
the process of forming a general concept by identifying common features among specific examples
formation of a general concept from specific examples

Examples

Mathematics is full of abstraction, where complex ideas are reduced to general principles.
Her painting is an abstraction based on the shapes she observed in nature.
The scientist used abstraction to develop a theory that applied to all cases, not just one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generalization - the process of making a general statement or concept from specific cases
  • Antonym: concretion - the opposite process of making something more specific or concrete

Etymology

From Latin abstractio, from abstrahere meaning 'to draw away'.
