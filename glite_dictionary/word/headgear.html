headgear

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌɡɪr / (AmE), / ˈhɛdɡɪə / (BrE)
the parts of a horse's harness or equipment that fit around its head, such as the bridle and halter
horse's harness for the head

Examples

The groom carefully checked the headgear before leading the horse out of the stable.
She replaced the worn leather straps of her horse's headgear with new ones.
Properly fitted headgear is essential for both the safety and control of the horse.

Etymology

From 'head' + 'gear'; in equestrian use since at least the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌɡɪr / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌɡɪə / (BrE)
anything worn on the head, especially as clothing or for protection, decoration, or ceremonial purposes.
clothing or coverings for the head

Examples

The safety regulations require all workers to wear headgear while on site.
She chose elegant headgear for the royal wedding.
Many athletes use special headgear to protect themselves during competitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hat - a covering for the head, typically with a brim

Etymology

From 'head' + 'gear', both of Old English origin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌɡɪr / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌɡɪə / (BrE)
The supporting framework and hoisting equipment situated above the shaft at the entrance of a mine, used for raising ore or workers.
framework for hoisting at a mine shaft

Examples

The old coal mine still has its original headgear standing as a historical monument.
Routine inspections of the headgear are essential for ensuring the safety of miners and equipment.
The collapse of the headgear halted all operations until repairs could be made.
