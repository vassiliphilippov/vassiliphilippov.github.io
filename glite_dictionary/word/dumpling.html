dumpling

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌmplɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌmplɪŋ / (BrE)
A dessert made by baking fruit, such as apples, wrapped in a layer of pastry.
fruit dessert in pastry

Examples

She served warm dumpling with vanilla ice cream for dessert.
My grandmother makes a delicious peach dumpling every autumn.
The menu featured a cherry dumpling as a house specialty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple dumpling - a type of dumpling made by baking an apple wrapped in pastry

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌmplɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌmplɪŋ / (BrE)
A small ball or strip of dough, typically boiled or steamed and served in soups, stews, or as a side dish.
small ball or strip of boiled/steamed dough

Examples

I made chicken soup with dumplings for dinner last night.
The restaurant serves delicious pork dumplings with spicy dipping sauce.
She carefully rolled out the dough to make dumplings for the stew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gnocchi - small Italian dumplings made from potato, semolina, or flour
  • Synonym: pierogi - eastern European dumplings of unleavened dough filled with various ingredients

Etymology

Possibly from the English dialect word 'dump', meaning a lump, plus diminutive -ling.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʌm.plɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌm.plɪŋ / (BrE)
a small ball or strip of dough, often filled or plain, boiled or steamed and sometimes fried, eaten as a food
small ball or strip of boiled or steamed dough

Examples

She made a batch of dumplings for the family dinner.
The chicken stew was served with fluffy dumplings on top.
Some Asian cuisines use rice flour to make their dumplings extra delicate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gnocchi - Italian small dumplings made from potato, flour, or semolina
  • Synonym: ravioli - pasta dumplings filled with various ingredients
  • Degree Lower: bun - a small bread or cake, sometimes similar to a steamed or baked dumpling

Etymology

From German dialectal *Dampfling* meaning 'lump,' related to 'damp' (steam).
