semicoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmiˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A state of impaired consciousness in which a person is only partially comatose and can be roused by strong stimuli.
partial or mild comatose state
medical

Examples

The patient was in a semicoma following the accident but responded to loud sounds.
Doctors monitored her closely while she remained in a semicoma for several days.
He emerged from the semicoma as the effects of the medication began to wear off.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: coma - a state of deep unconsciousness

Etymology

From semi- (meaning 'half' or 'partial') + coma, from Greek kōma (deep sleep).
