ungenerous

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈdʒɛnərəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈdʒɛnərəs / (BrE)
unwilling to give or share; not generous, especially with money, time, or resources.
not willing to give or share

Examples

Her ungenerous attitude made it difficult to raise funds for the charity.
The criticism he offered was harsh and ungenerous considering her efforts.
It would be ungenerous to ignore the help we received from our neighbors.

Synset Examples

she practices economy without being stingy
an ungenerous response to the appeal for funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stingy - unwilling to spend money or use resources; not generous
  • Antonym: generous - willing to give or share unsparingly

Etymology

From un- (not) + generous, from Latin 'generosus' (noble, generous).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈdʒɛnərəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈdʒɛnərəs / (BrE)
not willing to give or share things, especially money; lacking in generosity or magnanimity.
not generous; lacking generosity

Examples

It would be ungenerous not to acknowledge all the help we received.
Her comments about her colleagues were extremely ungenerous.
He made an ungenerous donation despite his considerable wealth.

Synset Examples

it seems ungenerous to end this review of a splendid work of scholarship on a critical note
a meanspirited man unwilling to forgive

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generous - willing to give or share freely; showing kindness
  • Synonym: mean - unwilling to give or share things; unkind or spiteful

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (meaning 'not') and 'generous', from Latin 'generosus'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
