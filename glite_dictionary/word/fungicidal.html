fungicidal

1. ADJ

/ ˌfʌn.dʒɪˈsaɪ.dəl / (AmE), / ˌfʌn.dʒɪˈsaɪ.dəl / (BrE)
(of a substance) capable of destroying fungi or inhibiting their growth.
able to destroy fungi

Examples

The fungicidal spray was effective against the mildew attacking the plants.
Doctors often prescribe fungicidal creams for athlete's foot.
Some paints are formulated to have fungicidal properties for use in damp environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungicide - a substance that kills fungi
  • Synonym: bactericidal - capable of destroying bacteria

Etymology

From Latin 'fungus' (mushroom) + '-cidal', from Latin 'caedere' (to kill).
