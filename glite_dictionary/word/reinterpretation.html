reinterpretation

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.ɪn.tɚ.prəˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪnˌtɜː.prɪˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of giving something a new or different meaning or interpretation.
the act of giving a new meaning

Examples

The director's reinterpretation of the classic novel gave it a fresh, modern twist.
Each generation brings its own reinterpretation to historical events.
His reinterpretation of the song added new emotional depth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpretation - the act of explaining or giving meaning

Etymology

From re- (again) + interpretation, from Latin interpretatio (explanation, translation).

2. NOUN

/ ˌriː.ɪnˌtɚ.prəˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪnˌtɜː.prɪˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of explaining or understanding something in a new or different way
a new or different interpretation

Examples

The director's reinterpretation of the classic play offered a fresh perspective on its main themes.
Modern artists often create a reinterpretation of traditional works to challenge existing conventions.
Her reinterpretation of the law was controversial but gained support in academic circles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpretation - the act of explaining or understanding the meaning of something

Etymology

From re- (again) + interpretation, after interpret.
