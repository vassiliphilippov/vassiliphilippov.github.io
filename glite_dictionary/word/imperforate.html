imperforate

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɜrfərət / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːfərət / (BrE)
not perforated; having no opening or hole
not having an opening or hole

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the infant with an imperforate anus, requiring immediate surgery.
Some marine snails have imperforate shells, completely lacking an opening at the base.
An imperforate hymen is a rare congenital condition that may cause health problems if untreated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to perforate - to make a hole or holes through

Etymology

From Latin 'imperforatus', from 'in-' (not) + 'perforatus' (perforated).
