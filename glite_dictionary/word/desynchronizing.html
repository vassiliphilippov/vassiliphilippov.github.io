desynchronizing

1. NOUN

/ diːˈsɪŋkrəˌnaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / diːˈsɪŋkrənaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or result of causing things to lose synchrony or fail to occur at the same time
process of losing synchrony

Examples

The new medication has a desynchronizing effect on the body's natural sleep rhythms.
Cutting power at irregular times may cause desynchronizing between production lines.
Stress can result in the desynchronizing of various biological clocks in the body.

Synset Examples

the stimulus produced a desynchronizing of the brain waves

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synchronizing - the process of bringing things to happen together in time

Etymology

From de- (expressing removal) + synchronize, from Greek syn- (together) + khronos (time).
