Lithuanian

1. NOUN

/ lɪθjuˈeɪniən / (AmE), / lɪθjuˈeɪniən / (BrE)
the official language of Lithuania, belonging to the Baltic branch of the Indo-European language family
the official language of Lithuania

Examples

Many people in Lithuania speak Lithuanian as their first language.
She decided to learn Lithuanian before traveling to Vilnius.
The book was translated from Lithuanian into English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latvian - the official language of Latvia, a related Baltic language
  • Antonym: Russian - the main Slavic language neighboring Lithuania

Etymology

From Lithuania, the name of the country, plus English -an.

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈθweɪniən / (AmE), / lɪˈθweɪniən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Lithuania.
native or inhabitant of Lithuania

Examples

The famous writer is a proud Lithuanian who often writes about his homeland.
Many Lithuanians celebrate their independence day with music and dance.
She met a Lithuanian while she was traveling in Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latvian - native or inhabitant of Latvia
  • Synonym: Estonian - native or inhabitant of Estonia

Etymology

From Lithuania, the name of the country, with English suffix -an.

3. ADJ

/ lɪθjuˈeɪniən / (AmE), / lɪθjuˈeɪniən / (BrE)
relating to Lithuania, its people, or its language
of or relating to Lithuania

Examples

The Lithuanian language is one of the oldest in Europe.
She enjoyed tasting traditional Lithuanian dishes during her trip.
The museum features many Lithuanian artifacts and artworks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lithuania - a country in the Baltic region of Europe

Etymology

From Lithuania, the country name, + English adjectival suffix -an.
