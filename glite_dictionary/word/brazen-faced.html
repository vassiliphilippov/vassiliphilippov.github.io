brazen-faced

1. ADJ

/ ˈbreɪzən ˌfeɪst / (AmE), / ˈbreɪzn ˌfeɪst / (BrE)
shamelessly bold and unrestrained by convention or propriety; showing a lack of shame or embarrassment.
shamelessly bold; audacious

Examples

His brazen-faced lies shocked everyone in the courtroom.
She made a brazen-faced attempt to take credit for someone else's work.
The brazen-faced thief walked out of the store in broad daylight.

Synset Examples

an audacious trick to pull
a barefaced hypocrite
the most bodacious display of tourism this side of Anaheim
bald-faced lies
brazen arrogance
the modern world with its quick material successes and insolent belief in the boundless possibilities of progress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barefaced - shameless; without disguise or concealment
  • Synonym: bold - showing a willingness to take risks; confident and courageous
  • Synonym: shameless - without shame; showing a lack of embarrassment or guilt
  • Antonym: modest - not excessively bold or confident; showing humility or reserve

Etymology

From 'brazen', meaning 'made of brass, shameless' (Old English), and 'faced', referring to having a particular expression.
