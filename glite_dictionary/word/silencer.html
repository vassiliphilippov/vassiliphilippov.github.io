silencer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪlən sər / (AmE), / ˈsaɪlənsə / (BrE)
a tubular device fitted to an exhaust system, such as on a car or motorcycle, to reduce noise produced by the engine
device reducing exhaust noise

Examples

The mechanic replaced the old silencer to make the car run more quietly.
The silencer on his motorcycle was damaged, making it unusually loud.
Cars in the city are required to have a functioning silencer to meet noise regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muffler - device for reducing the noise emitted by the exhaust system of an internal combustion engine

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪlənsər / (AmE), / ˈsaɪlənsə / (BrE)
A device, typically a short tube, attached to the muzzle of a firearm to reduce the noise of firing.
device that reduces gunfire noise

Examples

The hitman screwed the silencer onto his pistol before entering the warehouse.
Gun laws in some countries strictly regulate the possession of a silencer.
The silencer made the rifle shot much quieter, but not completely silent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppressor - device that reduces the sound of a firearm
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04225840-n
Synset POS: n
