polyvinyl-formaldehyde

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈvaɪnəl ˌfɔːrməlˌdɛhaɪd / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈvʌɪnɪl ˌfɔːm(ə)lˌdiːhaɪd / (BrE)
a synthetic polymer produced by polymerizing vinyl formaldehyde, used in coatings and adhesives
a synthetic polymer material

Examples

The adhesive used in the project contained polyvinyl-formaldehyde for enhanced durability.
Researchers studied the flexibility of polyvinyl-formaldehyde in various temperature conditions.
Many coatings are formulated with polyvinyl-formaldehyde for improved resistance to chemicals.

Etymology

Formed from 'polyvinyl' (multiple vinyl units) and 'formaldehyde'.
