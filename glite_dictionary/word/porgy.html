porgy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrɡi / (AmE), / ˈpɔːɡi / (BrE)
a deep-bodied marine fish found in warm and tropical coastal waters worldwide, valued as both food and for sport fishing
a marine food and sport fish

Examples

The fisherman caught a large porgy off the coast during their trip.
Grilled porgy is a delicacy in many Mediterranean restaurants.
Schools of porgy often gather near coral reefs in warm waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea bream - a similar deep-bodied fish, often used as a synonym for porgy
  • Synonym: snapper - another popular marine sport and food fish, sometimes confused with porgy

Etymology

Probably from Spanish *pargo* (type of fish) or Portuguese *porgo*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrɡi / (AmE), / ˈpɔːɡi / (BrE)
The lean flesh of a fish found in warm waters along the southern Atlantic coast of the United States, often used as food.
edible fish of the southern Atlantic coast

Examples

Many local restaurants serve grilled porgy as a specialty dish.
Fishermen caught several large porgies just off the Florida coast this morning.
The mild flavor of porgy makes it popular in Southern seafood recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scup - an edible fish of the Atlantic coast of North America, closely related to the porgy

Etymology

From Spanish *porgi* or Portuguese *porgo*, from Latin *porgus* (a kind of fish).
