casein

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.siˌɪn / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.siː.ɪn / (BrE)
a protein found in milk and used in making products such as plastics, adhesives, and foods
a milk protein used in industry and food

Examples

Many cheeses are made by curdling milk to separate the casein from the whey.
Some paints use casein as a binding agent for pigments.
Casein can trigger allergies in some individuals sensitive to milk proteins.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: whey - the watery part of milk separated from the curds, remaining after casein is removed

Etymology

From Latin 'caseus' (cheese), via French 'caséine'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪsiɪn / (AmE), / ˈkeɪsiːn / (BrE)
a water-based paint made from a milk protein, used as an artist's medium or binder
water-based paint made from milk protein

Examples

The mural was painted using casein for its quick-drying properties and matte finish.
Many artists prefer casein paints because they combine the traits of watercolor and oil paint.
After mixing the pigment with casein, she applied the paint to the wood panel.

Etymology

From Latin caseus (cheese), originally referring to the milk protein.
