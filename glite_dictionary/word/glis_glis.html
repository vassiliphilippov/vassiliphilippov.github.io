Glis glis

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɪs ɡlɪs / (AmE), / ɡlɪs ɡlɪs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the edible dormouse, a large European rodent known for its long periods of hibernation.
large European dormouse species

Examples

The Glis glis is native to forests throughout much of Europe.
Researchers have tracked the hibernation cycles of Glis glis to better understand its survival adaptations.
In some regions, the Glis glis is considered a pest because it invades homes and attics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edible dormouse - common name for Glis glis, a large European dormouse

Etymology

From Latin 'glis' meaning dormouse; used as the zoological binomial name.
