Ascomycotina

1. NOUN

/ ˌæskəˌmaɪkəˈtaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌæskəˌmaɪkəˈtiːnə / (BrE)
a large subdivision of fungi, also known as sac fungi, that includes various classes such as Hemiascomycetes and Discomycetes, characterized by producing spores in specialized sacs called asci
a major group of sac fungi
medical

Examples

Many species within the Ascomycotina are economically important, especially as pathogens of crops.
Researchers studied the reproductive structures unique to Ascomycotina under the microscope.
The baker's yeast, a valuable organism in bread-making, belongs to the Ascomycotina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungus - any member of a large group of eukaryotic organisms that includes mushrooms, yeasts, and molds

Etymology

From New Latin, meaning 'sac fungi', from Greek 'askos' (sac) and '-mycota' (fungus).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæs.kəˌmaɪ.kəˈtaɪ.nə / (AmE), / ˌæs.kəˌmɪ.kəˈtiː.nə / (BrE)
a major subdivision of the kingdom Fungi, comprising the sac fungi, which includes Hemiascomycetes, Plectomycetes, Pyrenomycetes, and Discomycetes
major fungal subdivision; sac fungi
medical

Examples

Many plant diseases are caused by fungi in the Ascomycotina subdivision.
The Ascomycotina is notable for producing spores inside specialized sacs called asci.
Researchers are studying the genetic diversity within Ascomycotina to understand fungal evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sac fungi - fungi whose spores develop in saclike structures called asci

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'askos' (sac) and '-mycetes' (fungi) with '-ina' indicating a subdivision.
