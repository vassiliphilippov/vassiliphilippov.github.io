rounder

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊndər / (AmE), / ˈraʊndə / (BrE)
a person, usually a man, who is morally unrestrained and often indulges in dissolute or debauched behavior
a morally unrestrained, dissolute person

Examples

After years of living as a rounder, he finally decided to turn his life around.
The old saloon used to attract every rounder in town.
He earned a reputation as a rounder who rarely refused a night of drinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roué - a debauched or morally unrestrained man
  • Synonym: profligate - an extremely extravagant or dissolute person

2. NOUN

/ ˈraʊndər / (AmE), / ˈraʊndə / (BrE)
a tool used to round off corners or edges, typically in woodworking or metalworking
tool for rounding edges or corners

Examples

The craftsman used a rounder to smooth the sharp edges of the table.
You will need a rounder to get that perfect curved finish on the wood.
A metal rounder is ideal for softening the corners of sheet metal pieces.
