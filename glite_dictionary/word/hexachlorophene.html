hexachlorophene

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛksəˈklɔrəˌfin / (AmE), / ˌhɛksəˈklɔːrəˌfiːn / (BrE)
a white, water-soluble powder with antibacterial properties, used in antiseptic soaps and toothpaste
antibacterial powder used in soaps and toothpaste
medical

Examples

Hospitals once commonly used hexachlorophene in disinfectant products to prevent infection.
Some toothpaste brands previously contained hexachlorophene as an antibacterial agent.
Due to safety concerns, the use of hexachlorophene in consumer products is now restricted.

Etymology

From hexa- (six) + chloro- (chlorine) + -phene (from phenol), indicating its chemical structure.
