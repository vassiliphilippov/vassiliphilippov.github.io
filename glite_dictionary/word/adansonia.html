Adansonia

1. NOUN

/ ˌædænˈsoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌædanˈsəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of trees commonly known as baobabs, native to Africa, Madagascar, and Australia, noted for their thick trunks and longevity.
genus of baobab trees

Examples

The Adansonia tree is a striking feature of the African landscape.
Many legends surround the origins of the Adansonia in Madagascar.
Researchers are studying how climate change affects the growth of Adansonia species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baobab - the common name for trees in the genus Adansonia

Etymology

Named after French naturalist Michel Adanson (1727–1806).
