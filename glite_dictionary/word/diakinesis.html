diakinesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.ə.kɪˈniː.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌdʌɪ.ə.kɪˈniː.sɪs / (BrE)
the final stage of prophase during meiosis, in which chromosomes condense further and prepare for metaphase
final prophase stage in meiosis
medical

Examples

During diakinesis, the chromosomes are fully condensed and the nucleolus disappears.
Researchers observed abnormalities in diakinesis in certain mutant strains.
The pairing of homologous chromosomes is complete by the time diakinesis occurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meiosis - the process of cell division that reduces the chromosome number by half

Etymology

From Greek 'dia-' (through) + 'kinesis' (movement).
