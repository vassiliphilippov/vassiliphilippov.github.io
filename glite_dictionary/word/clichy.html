Clichy

1. PROPN

/ ˈkliːʃi / (AmE), / ˈkliːʃi / (BrE)
A northwestern suburb of Paris, historically known as the residence of Merovingian royalty in the 7th century.
northwestern suburb of Paris

Examples

Many artists once lived in Clichy during the late nineteenth century.
The royal family resided in Clichy during the Merovingian period.
Today, Clichy is an integral part of the Greater Paris metropolitan area.
