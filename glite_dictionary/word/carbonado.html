carbonado

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrbəˈnɑːdoʊ / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbənˈɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
A dark, opaque variety of diamond, usually of lower quality, used mainly for industrial purposes such as drilling and polishing.
inferior, dark industrial diamond

Examples

The mining company exported carbonado for use in the manufacturing of drill bits.
Unlike clear gemstones, carbonado is valued for its toughness in industrial applications.
They replaced the standard drill tip with a carbonado to cut through the hard rock.

Etymology

From Portuguese carbonado, meaning 'burned' or 'carbonized', referring to its dark appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrbəˈneɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbənˈɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
A piece of meat or fish that has been scored and broiled or grilled.
scored and broiled piece of meat or fish

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious carbonado for the special dinner menu.
A traditional carbonado is scored to allow the heat to penetrate the meat evenly.
They enjoyed a grilled carbonado with a side of roasted vegetables.

Etymology

From Spanish 'carbonada', from 'carbón' meaning 'charcoal', referring to cooking over flames.
