musicology

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːzɪˈkɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌmjuːzɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scholarly and scientific study of music, including its history, forms, and cultural context
the scholarly study of music

Examples

She decided to specialize in musicology after discovering her passion for the history of jazz.
Many universities now offer advanced degrees in musicology.
The conference brought together experts from all areas of musicology to discuss recent research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethnomusicology - the study of music from different cultural contexts

Etymology

From French 'musicologie', from 'musique' (music) and Greek '-logia' (study of).
