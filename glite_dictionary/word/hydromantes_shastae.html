Hydromantes shastae

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdroʊˈmæntiːz ˈʃæstiː / (AmE), / hʌɪˌdrəʊˈmantiːz ˈʃæstiː / (BrE)
A species of salamander native to the caves and surrounding habitats of the Mount Shasta area in California.
species of salamander in Mount Shasta area

Examples

The Hydromantes shastae is adapted to life in the cool, damp conditions of local caves.
Scientists are studying Hydromantes shastae to learn more about its unique respiratory system.
Efforts are underway to protect the habitat of Hydromantes shastae from environmental threats.

Etymology

From modern Latin; 'Hydromantes' (genus) and the specific epithet 'shastae' referring to Mount Shasta.
