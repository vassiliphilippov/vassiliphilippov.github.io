ninja

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪndʒə / (AmE), / ˈnɪndʒə / (BrE)
A member of a class of Japanese warriors trained in stealth, espionage, and assassination; also, a person highly skilled in a particular activity, especially stealthily.
a Japanese covert agent; expert in stealth or skills

Examples

The movie tells the story of a skilled ninja who must protect his village from intruders.
She moved through the house like a ninja, silent and undetected.
Modern martial arts schools sometimes teach the ancient techniques of the ninja.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spy - someone who secretly collects information

Etymology

From Japanese 忍者 (ninja), meaning 'one who endures' or 'stealth person'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɪn.dʒə / (AmE), / ˈnɪn.dʒə / (BrE)
a member of a class of 14th-century Japanese warriors trained in martial arts, who were hired for espionage, sabotage, and assassination
Japanese martial artist skilled in espionage

Examples

The ninja moved silently through the shadows, unseen by his enemies.
Many kids are fascinated by stories of ninja and their secret missions.
The film depicted a skilled ninja carrying out a daring rescue under the cover of night.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: samurai - Japanese warrior of the military aristocracy

Etymology

From Japanese 忍者 (ninja), meaning 'one who endures' or 'covert agent'.
