sullenness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌl.ən.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌl.ɪn.nɪs / (BrE)
a gloomy or morose mood, characterized by resentment or silent ill temper
gloomy ill-tempered mood

Examples

His sullenness was obvious to everyone at the dinner table.
She sat in sullenness, refusing to join the conversation.
The child's sullenness faded as soon as he saw the puppy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloominess - a state of being dark or depressed
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the state of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From 'sullen' (Middle English, from Old French 'soelain', from Latin 'solus' meaning 'alone').

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlənnəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌlənnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being darkly moody, silent, and resentfully withdrawn
moody, resentful disposition

Examples

The sullenness in his demeanor made others reluctant to approach him.
After the argument, a heavy sullenness settled over the room.
Her sullenness faded once she began talking about her passions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sullen - moody, silent, and resentful

Etymology

From 'sullen' (Middle English, probably from Anglo-French *suellin*, from Old French *solain* meaning 'alone').
