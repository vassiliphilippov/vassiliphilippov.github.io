integumental

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˌtɛɡjəˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɛɡjʊˈmɛntəl / (BrE)
relating to or forming an outer covering, such as skin or a membrane, especially in animals or plants
relating to an outer covering (skin, membrane, etc.)

Examples

The integumental cells protect the plant from dehydration and pathogens.
Some reptiles have evolved complex integumental structures, such as scales and spines.
The integumental system in humans mainly consists of the skin, hair, and nails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integument - the natural outer covering or layer of an organism

Etymology

From Latin 'integumentum' (a covering) + '-al' (forming adjectives)
