mantiger

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænˌtaɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈmænˌtɪɡə / (BrE)
A mythical creature with the head of a man (sometimes with horns), the body of a lion, and the tail of a scorpion.
mythical creature: man, lion, and scorpion parts

Examples

Legends describe a mantiger as a fearsome guardian of ancient treasures.
The explorer claimed to have seen the tracks of a mantiger in the desert.
In the old manuscript, a mantiger is depicted battling a brave knight.

Etymology

Formed from 'man' and 'tiger,' modeled after similar hybrid creature names.
