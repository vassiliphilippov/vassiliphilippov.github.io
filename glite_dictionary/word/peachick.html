peachick

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːˌtʃɪk / (AmE), / ˈpiːˌtʃɪk / (BrE)
a young peafowl, especially a peacock or peahen that is still in its juvenile stage
young peafowl

Examples

A single peachick hatched from the eggs this morning at the sanctuary.
The peachick followed its mother closely through the grass.
It's important to provide warmth and protection for a newly hatched peachick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peafowl - a bird species that includes peacocks and peahens

Etymology

Formed from 'pea-' (for 'peafowl') + 'chick' (young bird).
