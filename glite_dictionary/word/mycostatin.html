Mycostatin

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkoʊˈstætɪn / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkəʊˈstætɪn / (BrE)
A trade name for nystatin, an antifungal and antibiotic drug used to treat infections caused by fungi.
antifungal/antibiotic drug (trade name)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Mycostatin to treat the patient's oral fungal infection.
Mycostatin is commonly used in hospitals for patients with weakened immune systems.
Always follow your physician's instructions when taking Mycostatin for a fungal infection.
