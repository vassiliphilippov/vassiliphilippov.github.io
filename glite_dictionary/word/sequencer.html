sequencer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːkwənsər / (AmE), / ˈsiːkwənsə / (BrE)
a device or program, especially in computing, that arranges data or instructions into a specific order
device or program that sequences data

Examples

The sequencer organizes the instructions for efficient processing by the CPU.
Many databases include a sequencer to manage incoming records.
The new hardware sequencer dramatically improves system performance.

Etymology

From 'sequence' (arranged order) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04180405-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːkwənsər / (AmE), / ˈsiːkwənsə / (BrE)
An apparatus that determines the order of monomers in a polymer, such as DNA or proteins.
device that determines molecular sequence
medical

Examples

The laboratory upgraded its equipment to a next-generation sequencer for faster genetic analysis.
A sequencer can reveal mutations in DNA by identifying the exact order of bases.
Researchers used a protein sequencer to determine the structure of the newly discovered enzyme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analyzer - a device that analyzes substances, often chemically

Etymology

From sequence + -er, meaning 'one that sequences.'
