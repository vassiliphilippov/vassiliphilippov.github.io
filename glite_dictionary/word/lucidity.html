lucidity

1. NOUN

/ luˈsɪdəti / (AmE), / luˈsɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being clear in thought or expression; clarity of mind
clarity or clearness of mind

Examples

She spoke with remarkable lucidity despite her illness.
Moments of lucidity returned to him after several days of confusion.
The patient's sudden lucidity surprised the doctors and family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarity - the quality of being clear, in particular of thought or expression
  • Antonym: confusion - the state of being unclear or bewildered

Etymology

From Latin 'lucidus' (clear, bright) via French 'lucidité'.

2. NOUN

/ luˈsɪdəti / (AmE), / luːˈsɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality of being easy to understand or think about; clarity of expression or thought
clarity; ease of understanding

Examples

The lucidity of her explanation made the complex topic easy to grasp.
He spoke with remarkable lucidity, even after his long illness.
The essay was praised for its structure and lucidity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarity - the quality of being clear or easy to understand

Etymology

From Latin 'luciditas' (clearness), from 'lucidus' (clear, bright).
