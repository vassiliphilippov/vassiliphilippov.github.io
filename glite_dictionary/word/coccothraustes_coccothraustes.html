Coccothraustes coccothraustes

1. NOUN

/ koʊkəˈθroʊstiːz koʊkəˈθroʊstiːz / (AmE), / kɒkəˈθrɔːstiːz kɒkəˈθrɔːstiːz / (BrE)
a large Eurasian finch, commonly known as the hawfinch, recognized for its massive bill and strong seed-cracking ability
a large Eurasian finch (hawfinch)

Examples

The Coccothraustes coccothraustes is known for its powerful beak, capable of cracking hard seeds.
Birdwatchers in Europe are often excited to spot a Coccothraustes coccothraustes among other finches.
As winter approaches, flocks of Coccothraustes coccothraustes may be seen in search of food in the woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawfinch - common name for Coccothraustes coccothraustes, a large Eurasian finch

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek kokkos (berry) and thraustēs (breaker), referring to its diet and strong bill.
