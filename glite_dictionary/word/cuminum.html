Cuminum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːmɪnəm / (AmE), / ˈkjuːmɪnəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Apiaceae, best known for Cuminum cyminum (cumin), cultivated for its aromatic seeds used as a spice.
genus of plants including cumin

Examples

Cuminum cyminum is widely cultivated for its aromatic seeds.
The genus Cuminum belongs to the family Apiaceae.
Botanists identified several species within the Cuminum genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cumin - The aromatic seeds of Cuminum cyminum, used as a spice

Etymology

From Latin *Cuminum*, from Greek *kyminon* (cumin).
