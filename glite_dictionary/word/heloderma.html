Heloderma

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːloʊˈdɜːrmə / (AmE), / ˌhiːləˈdɜːmə / (BrE)
a genus of venomous lizards native to the Americas, including species such as the Gila monster and the Mexican beaded lizard.
genus of venomous lizards

Examples

The Heloderma genus comprises the only known venomous lizards native to the United States.
Researchers studied Heloderma to better understand the evolution of venom in reptiles.
The Gila monster, a member of the Heloderma genus, is protected by law in several states.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gila monster - a species of venomous lizard found in the southwestern United States, belonging to the genus Heloderma
  • Synonym: beaded lizard - a venomous lizard found in Mexico and Central America, also within genus Heloderma

Etymology

From Greek hēlos (the head of a nail, stud) + derma (skin), referring to the beaded texture of the lizards' skin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛl.oʊˈdɜr.mə / (AmE), / ˌhɛl.əʊˈdɜː.mə / (BrE)
A genus of venomous lizards native to the Americas, including the Gila monster and beaded lizard, and the type genus of the family Helodermatidae.
genus of American venomous lizards
medical

Examples

The Heloderma genus includes the well-known Gila monster and beaded lizard.
Scientists study the venom composition of Heloderma to develop new medicines.
Heloderma lizards are the only known venomous lizards native to the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gila monster - A species of venomous lizard found in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico

Etymology

From Greek hēlos (the head of a nail or a wart) + derma (skin).
