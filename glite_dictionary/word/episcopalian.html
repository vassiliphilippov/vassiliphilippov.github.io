Episcopalian

1. ADJ

/ ɪˌpɪskəˈpeɪliən / (AmE), / ɪˌpɪskəˈpeɪlɪən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the Episcopal Church.
related to the Episcopal Church

Examples

She grew up in an Episcopalian household and attended services every Sunday.
The Episcopalian community holds an annual charity event for the neighborhood.
An Episcopalian choir performed traditional hymns during the ceremony.

Synset Examples

the Episcopal hierarchy
married by an Episcopalian minister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Episcopal - of, relating to, or characteristic of a bishop or bishops; specifically, relating to the Episcopal Church

Etymology

From Episcopal + -ian, denoting belonging or relating to the Episcopal Church.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌpɪskəˈpeɪliən / (AmE), / ɪˌpɪskəˈpeɪliən / (BrE)
A member of the Episcopal Church, especially in the United States, or more generally someone who adheres to episcopal forms of church government.
member of the Episcopal Church

Examples

Her grandfather was a devout Episcopalian who attended services every Sunday.
The Episcopalian community organized a charity event for local families.
As an Episcopalian, he valued tradition and inclusivity within the church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anglican - a member of the Church of England or a related church

Etymology

From 'Episcopal' (relating to bishops), based on Latin episcopalis from Greek episkopos (overseer, bishop) + '-ian'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10082082-n
Synset POS: n
