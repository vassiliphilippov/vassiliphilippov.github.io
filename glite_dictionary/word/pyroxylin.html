pyroxylin

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈrɑksəlɪn / (AmE), / paɪˈrɒksɪlɪn / (BrE)
a highly flammable form of nitrocellulose used in the manufacture of collodion, plastics, and lacquers
flammable nitrocellulose used in plastics/lacquers

Examples

The film base was originally made from pyroxylin, which proved extremely flammable.
Manufacturers used pyroxylin to produce early plastics and photographic films.
Because of its volatility, regulations tightly controlled the storage of pyroxylin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrocellulose - highly flammable compound formed by nitrating cellulose

Etymology

From German 'Pyroxylin', based on Greek 'pyr' (fire) and 'xylon' (wood).
