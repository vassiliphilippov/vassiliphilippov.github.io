woolgatherer

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊlˌɡæðɚɚ / (AmE), / ˈwʊlˌɡæðərə / (BrE)
a person who lets their mind wander aimlessly; someone who indulges in idle or absentminded daydreaming
person given to daydreaming
archaic

Examples

My grandmother sometimes calls me a woolgatherer when I drift off into my imagination.
He was such a woolgatherer during meetings that he often missed important details.
Some people view being a woolgatherer as a flaw, but creativity often springs from daydreams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daydreamer - a person who is often lost in thought or fantasy
  • Antonym: realist - a person who accepts a situation as it is and is prepared to deal with it accordingly

Etymology

From 'wool' + 'gatherer'; originally referred to gathering stray wool but came to mean 'daydreamer' in the 16th century.
