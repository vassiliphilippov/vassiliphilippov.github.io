dachshund

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːkshʊnd / (AmE), / ˈdakshʊnd / (BrE)
A small dog breed with a long body, short legs, and long drooping ears, originally bred in Germany for hunting animals in burrows.
a small, long-bodied dog breed

Examples

The couple adopted a dachshund from the animal shelter last weekend.
Her family's dachshund loves to burrow under blankets during winter.
We saw a parade featuring dozens of dachshunds dressed in little costumes.

Etymology

From German 'Dachshund', meaning 'badger dog' (Dachs = badger, Hund = dog).
