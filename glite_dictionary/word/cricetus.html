Cricetus

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˈsiːtəs / (AmE), / krɪˈsiːtəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Cricetidae, comprising certain Old World hamsters.
genus of Old World hamsters

Examples

The golden hamster belongs to the genus Cricetus.
Scientists studied the behaviors of several species within Cricetus.
Members of the Cricetus genus are commonly found in eastern Europe and Asia.

Etymology

From Latin *cricetus*, meaning 'hamster', from Slavic origin.

2. NOUN

/ kraɪˈsiːtəs / (AmE), / kraɪˈsiːtəs / (BrE)
The genus of Old World hamsters, including the European hamster, in the family Cricetidae.
genus of Old World hamsters

Examples

The European hamster is classified in the genus Cricetus within the family Cricetidae.
Scientists recently studied genetic diversity in populations of Cricetus across Europe.
Members of the genus Cricetus are widely distributed in eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hamster - small rodent commonly kept as a pet

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'kriketos' (a kind of rodent).
