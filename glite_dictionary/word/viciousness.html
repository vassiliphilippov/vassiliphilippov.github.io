viciousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʃ.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪʃ.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being extremely cruel, violent, or aggressive
extreme cruelty or violence

Examples

The viciousness of the attack shocked the entire community.
She was alarmed by the sheer viciousness in his voice when he spoke.
The film depicted the viciousness with which rival gangs fought for control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruelty - callous indifference to or pleasure in causing pain or suffering
  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate

Etymology

From 'vicious' + '-ness'; 'vicious' from Old French 'vicios', from Latin 'vitiosus' (full of faults)
