gangdom

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæŋ.dəm / (AmE), / ˈɡæŋ.dəm / (BrE)
The world or sphere of organized gangs, especially those involved in criminal activities.
world of gangs or underworld organizations

Examples

The documentary explores the history of gangdom in the early twentieth century.
After his release from prison, he tried to distance himself from gangdom and its dangerous lifestyle.
Legends of gangdom have captured the public's imagination for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underworld - the criminal world or society involved in organized crime
  • Synonym: gangland - the world of organized criminal gangs

Etymology

Formed from 'gang' + '-dom', denoting a domain or sphere.
