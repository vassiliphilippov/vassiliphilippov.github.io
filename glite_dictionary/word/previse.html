to previse

1. VERB

/ priˈvaɪz / (AmE), / priˈvaɪz / (BrE)
To foresee or anticipate something before it happens.
foresee in advance

Examples

It is impossible to previse every possible outcome of an experiment.
She tried to previse what issues might arise during the meeting.
The company failed to previse the rapid changes in technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anticipate - to regard as probable; expect or predict
  • Synonym: to foresee - to be aware of beforehand; predict

Etymology

From Latin praevisus, past participle of providēre (to foresee).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ prɪˈvaɪz / (AmE), / prɪˈvaɪz / (BrE)
to warn or inform someone of something in advance; to give early warning
warn or inform in advance

Examples

It is wise to previse your colleagues about possible changes in the schedule.
The scientist tried to previse the consequences of climate inaction.
If we had been prevised of the risks, we would have taken precautions.

Synset Examples

I forewarned him of the trouble that would arise if he showed up at his ex-wife's house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to forewarn - to warn someone in advance
  • Synonym: alert - to warn someone of danger or a problem

Etymology

From Latin 'praevisus', past participle of 'praevidere' (to foresee).

WordNet ID: oewn-00872604-v
