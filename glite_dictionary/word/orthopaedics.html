orthopaedics

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrθəˈpidɪks / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəˈpiːdɪks / (BrE)
The branch of medicine concerned with the correction or prevention of disorders of the bones, joints, and muscles.
branch of medicine for bones and joints
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in orthopaedics after witnessing several sports injuries.
The hospital has a renowned orthopaedics department treating bone and joint disorders.
Advances in orthopaedics have greatly improved recovery times for patients with fractures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthopedics - alternate spelling, chiefly American

Etymology

From Greek 'orthos' (straight) + 'paideia' (rearing of children); originally related to correcting skeletal deformities in children.
