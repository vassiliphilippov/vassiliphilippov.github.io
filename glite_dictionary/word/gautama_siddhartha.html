Gautama Siddhartha

1. PROPN

/ ɡaʊˈtɑːmə sɪˈdɑːrtə / (AmE), / ɡɔːˈtɑːmə sɪˈdɑːtə / (BrE)
the founder of Buddhism, who lived in ancient India around 563–483 BC, also known as the Buddha
founder of Buddhism, ancient India

Examples

Many people travel to Bodh Gaya to honor Gautama Siddhartha and his path to enlightenment.
Gautama Siddhartha taught that suffering could be ended by following the Eightfold Path.
The teachings of Gautama Siddhartha continue to inspire millions of Buddhists around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buddha - the Enlightened One; title of Siddhartha Gautama after his enlightenment

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Gautama' (family name) and 'Siddhartha' (he who has attained his goals).
