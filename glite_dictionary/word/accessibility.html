accessibility

1. NOUN

/ əkˌsɛsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / əkˌsɛsəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being easy to approach, reach, or deal with
quality of being easy to approach or deal with

Examples

The accessibility of the new manager made employees feel comfortable sharing their concerns.
Increased accessibility to public transportation benefits the whole community.
The accessibility of this online service means anyone can get help at any time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inaccessibility - the quality of being difficult or impossible to reach or approach

Etymology

From French accessibilité or from Medieval Latin accessibilitas, from Latin accessibilis (approachable).

2. NOUN

/ əkˌsɛsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / əkˌsɛsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being easy to reach, obtain, or use
the state of being easy to access

Examples

The accessibility of basic healthcare services is crucial for every community.
Improvements in public transport have increased the accessibility of the city center.
We chose a venue for the conference based on its accessibility to all attendees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: availability - the state of being able to be used or obtained

Etymology

From French 'accessibilité', from Latin 'accessibilis' (approachable) + '-ity'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04726275-n
Synset POS: n
