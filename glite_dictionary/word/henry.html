henry

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛnri / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri / (BrE)
the SI unit of inductance, equal to the inductance in which an electromotive force of one volt is produced by a current changing at the rate of one ampere per second
unit of inductance (SI)

Examples

The circuit had an inductance of 2 henries, which was sufficient for the experiment.
Most modern transformers are rated in terms of henries.
Measuring inductance in henry is standard in electrical engineering.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: millihenry - one thousandth of a henry, unit of inductance
  • Degree Lower: microhenry - one millionth of a henry, unit of inductance

Etymology

Named after Joseph Henry (1797–1878), American physicist.
