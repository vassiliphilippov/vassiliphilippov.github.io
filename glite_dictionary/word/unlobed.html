unlobed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈloʊbd / (AmE), / ʌnˈləʊbd / (BrE)
(of a leaf, organ, or structure) having no lobes or divisions; not lobed.
without lobes

Examples

The simple, unlobed leaves make identification of the species straightforward.
This maple variety is known for its unlobed foliage unlike the typical deeply cut leaves.
When compared with the lobed types, the unlobed specimens are easier to sketch.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lobed - having one or more distinct rounded divisions (lobes)

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'lobed', from 'lobe'.
