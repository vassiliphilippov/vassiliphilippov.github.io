prosthetic

1. ADJ

/ prɑˈsθɛtɪk / (AmE), / prɒsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting an artificial body part, such as a limb, heart, or tooth, that replaces a missing or defective one.
relating to artificial body parts
medical

Examples

The clinic specializes in prosthetic limbs for children and adults.
Recent advances in prosthetic technology have improved mobility for many patients.
After the accident, he learned to walk again with a prosthetic leg.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosthesis - an artificial device that replaces a missing or defective body part

Etymology

From Greek prosthetikos, from prosthesis (addition).

2. ADJ

/ prɑːsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / prɒsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or serving as an artificial body part, especially a limb, that replaces a missing one.
relating to an artificial body part
medical

Examples

Her doctor recommended a prosthetic limb after her accident.
Advancements in prosthetic technology have greatly improved patients' quality of life.
The prosthetic device fit so well that it was barely noticeable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificial - made by human beings, not natural

Etymology

From Greek prosthetikos (of addition), from prostithenai (to add to).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02796218-a
Synset POS: a
