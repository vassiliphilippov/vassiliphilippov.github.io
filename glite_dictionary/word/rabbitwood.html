rabbitwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪtˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈræbɪtˌwʊd / (BrE)
A shrub native to the southeastern United States, parasitic on hemlock roots, with sparse spikes of greenish flowers and pulpy drupes.
parasitic shrub of southeastern US

Examples

The dense thicket was dotted with rabbitwood, its pale flowers barely visible among the ferns.
Botanists studied how rabbitwood draws nutrients from nearby hemlock trees.
Hikers in the region often mistake rabbitwood for similar shrubs due to its sparse spikes of flowers.
