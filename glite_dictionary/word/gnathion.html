gnathion

1. NOUN

/ næˈθaɪ.ən / (AmE), / næˈθaɪ.ən / (BrE)
the lowest point on the lower jaw (mandible) in the middle, used as a reference in anatomy and dentistry
most inferior midline point on the mandible
medical

Examples

The orthodontist measured the distance from the gnathion to other cranial landmarks during the assessment.
Precise identification of the gnathion is essential in cephalometric analysis.
The surgeon marked the gnathion before beginning the corrective jaw procedure.

Etymology

From Greek gnathion, diminutive of gnathos (jaw).
