Clangula

1. NOUN

/ klænˈɡjuːlə / (AmE), / klæŋˈɡjuːlə / (BrE)
A genus of sea ducks in the family Anatidae, notable for including the long-tailed duck.
genus of sea ducks

Examples

The genus Clangula contains the long-tailed duck, a bird common in Arctic regions.
Ornithologists discussed the migration patterns of birds in the Clangula genus.
Research on Clangula has provided insights into how sea ducks adapt to cold environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long-tailed duck - a species of sea duck, Clangula hyemalis, the only extant species of the genus Clangula

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Clangula, formed from Latin clangor (a loud noise, clanging) in reference to the bird's call.
