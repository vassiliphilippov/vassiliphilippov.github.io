Akeridae

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / əˈkɛrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine gastropod mollusks known as bubble shells, typically characterized by thin, globular shells.
family of bubble shell sea snails

Examples

The Akeridae are distinguished by their delicate, bubble-shaped shells which are found in shallow marine waters.
Marine biologists often study Akeridae to learn more about the evolution of gastropods.
Several members of the Akeridae family inhabit tropical reef environments around the world.

Etymology

From the type genus 'Akera' plus the zoological family suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.kəˈraɪ.diː / (AmE), / ˌɑː.kəˈriː.diː / (BrE)
A family of small marine gastropod mollusks known as bubble shells, characterized by their thin, fragile shells and found in shallow sea waters.
family of marine mollusks (bubble shells)

Examples

The Akeridae family includes several unique species of bubble shells found in coastal waters.
Marine biologists are studying the distribution of Akeridae in the North Atlantic.
The delicate shells of Akeridae make them popular among shell collectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bubble shell - a type of marine mollusk with a thin, inflated shell

Etymology

From the type genus Akera + taxonomic suffix -idae, used for animal families.
