Fumaria sempervirens

1. NOUN

/ fuːˈmɛəriə sɛmpərˈvaɪrɛnz / (AmE), / fjʊˈmɑːrɪə sɛmpəˈvaɪrɛnz / (BrE)
a glaucous herb native to northeastern United States and Canada, notable for its loose racemes of yellow-tipped pink flowers; sometimes classified in the genus Fumaria
herbaceous plant with pink and yellow flowers

Examples

The botanical garden has a rare specimen of Fumaria sempervirens in its northeastern section.
Researchers studied Fumaria sempervirens to analyze its unique flower coloration.
Fumaria sempervirens is occasionally found growing wild along forest edges in Canada.

Etymology

From Latin: Fumaria, the genus name, and sempervirens meaning 'evergreen'.
