overhanded

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊvərˌhændɪd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌhændɪd / (BrE)
(of a throw, stroke, or similar action) performed with the hand moving forward and downward from above shoulder level.
done with hand moving down from above shoulder

Examples

The pitcher threw an overhanded fastball that surprised the batter.
She delivered an overhanded serve across the tennis net with great speed.
His overhanded stroke gave the team an advantage in the swimming relay.

Synset Examples

an overhand pitch
an overhand stroke

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underhanded - done with the hand below shoulder level

Etymology

Formed from 'over' + 'handed,' referring to movement of the hand over the shoulder.
