to toiling

1. VERB

/ ˈtɔɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɔɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
working extremely hard, especially at physically difficult or unpleasant tasks
working arduously or laboriously

Examples

The farmers spent the day toiling in the fields under the hot sun.
After toiling for hours, she finally completed the project.
The workers were toiling deep inside the mine with little rest.

Synset Examples

drudging peasants
the bent backs of laboring slaves picking cotton
toiling coal miners in the black deeps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laboring - working hard or at unskilled tasks
  • Synonym: drudging - doing dull, hard, or menial work
