Rapateaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌræpətiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌrapətiːˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of South American herbs resembling rushes, comprising the single family of the order Rapales
a family of South American herbs

Examples

The Rapateaceae are native mainly to tropical South America.
Botanists have discovered new species in the Rapateaceae family.
Members of the Rapateaceae thrive in wet habitats similar to those of rushes.

Etymology

From New Latin Rapateaceae, named after the type genus Rapatea.

2. NOUN

/ ˌræpəˈtiːsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌrapəˈtiːsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of South American herbs with grass-like appearance, resembling the rush family (Juncaceae), found mainly in tropical regions.
family of South American herbs (botany)

Examples

The Rapateaceae are known for their unique inflorescence structures in South American wetlands.
Botanists often compare Rapateaceae to the rush family due to their similar habitat preferences.
Several species within the Rapateaceae family are adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Etymology

From the type genus Rapatea + the botanical suffix -aceae, indicating a plant family.
