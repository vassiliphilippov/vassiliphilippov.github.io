excise

1. VERB

/ ˈɛkˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛksaɪz / (BrE)
to levy an excise tax on goods or commodities
impose an excise tax

Examples

The government plans to excise luxury goods to increase revenue.
Importers were heavily excised for bringing tobacco into the country.
Many products are excised at the border to control consumption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax - a compulsory contribution to state revenue
  • Antonym: exempt - to free from a duty or tax

Etymology

From Latin 'excisus' (cut out), referring to a tax 'cut out' or imposed on goods.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02312862-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛksaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛksaɪz / (BrE)
a tax levied on certain goods and commodities produced or sold within a country, or on certain business transactions, excluding property and income taxes
a tax on certain goods or business transactions

Examples

The government imposed a new excise on tobacco products to discourage smoking.
Alcohol carries a high excise in many countries, contributing to state revenue.
Businesses protested the increase in excise on fuel, citing higher costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duty - a tax on the import or export of goods
  • Synonym: tax - a compulsory contribution to state revenue

Etymology

From Old French 'excise', from Dutch 'excijs', referring to an internal tax.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13337354-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈɛkˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛk.saɪz / (BrE)
to remove something completely by cutting it out, especially from the body or from text
remove by cutting out

Examples

The surgeon carefully excised the infected tissue to stop the spread of the disease.
It took hours to excise all of the irrelevant information from the report.
Doctors sometimes excise moles that could potentially become cancerous.

Synset Examples

The surgeon excised the tumor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - to take away or eliminate something
  • Synonym: extract - to remove something with effort or skill

Etymology

From Latin 'excidere', from 'ex-' (out) + 'caedere' (to cut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00473802-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈɛkˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛksaɪz / (BrE)
to remove something by cutting it out or erasing it, especially from a text or document
remove by cutting or erasing

Examples

The editor decided to excise several paragraphs from the final version of the article.
Sensitive information was excised from the official report before it was released.
You should excise any irrelevant details from your application to keep it concise.

Synset Examples

Please strike this remark from the record
scratch that remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to delete - remove written or printed material
  • Synonym: to expunge - erase or remove completely (something unwanted)

Etymology

From Latin 'excidere' (to cut out).

Source: WordNet
