cuticle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkjuːtɪkəl / (BrE)
the layer of dead skin at the base of a fingernail or toenail
dead skin at base of nail
medical

Examples

Always use a cuticle oil to keep your cuticle healthy and moisturized.
Gently push back the cuticle rather than cutting it to avoid infections.
A manicure usually involves caring for the nail and the cuticle.

Etymology

From Latin cuticula (little skin), diminutive of cutis (skin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkjuːtɪkəl / (BrE)
the hard outer covering or protective layer of certain organisms such as arthropods and turtles
hard outer covering of some animals

Examples

The beetle's cuticle provides protection against predators and dehydration.
Biologists studied the cuticle of the turtle to understand how it resists wear and tear.
The cuticle can vary in thickness between different species of arthropods.

Etymology

From Latin 'cuticula', diminutive of 'cutis' (skin).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkjuː.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
the outer layer of skin covering the surface of the body in vertebrates, especially at the base of fingernails and toenails
outer layer of skin or nail

Examples

Too much cleaning with harsh soap can dry out the cuticle around your fingernails.
During a manicure, the technician gently pushed back the cuticle to tidy up the nails.
Sometimes, damage to the cuticle can lead to painful infections near the nails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epidermis - the outermost layer of skin in animals and humans

Etymology

From Latin cuticula, diminutive of cutis (‘skin’).
