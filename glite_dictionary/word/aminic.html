aminic

1. ADJ

/ əˈmɪnɪk / (AmE), / əˈmɪnɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or containing amines, a group of organic compounds derived from ammonia and containing nitrogen.
relating to amines (organic nitrogen compounds)
medical

Examples

The researcher analyzed the aminic structure of the compound under the microscope.
Some pharmaceuticals possess aminic groups that affect their biological activity.
The aminic content in water samples can indicate contamination by industrial waste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amine - an organic compound derived from ammonia by replacement of one or more hydrogen atoms by organic groups

Etymology

From 'amine' + '-ic', indicating relation or belonging.
