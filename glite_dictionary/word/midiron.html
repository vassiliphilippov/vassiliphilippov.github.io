midiron

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌaɪɚn / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌaɪən / (BrE)
(in golf, historical) a type of iron club with a nearly vertical face, formerly used for mid-distance shots.
obsolete golf club with vertical face

Examples

The old golfer still has a midiron among his collection of antique clubs.
Before modern clubs, players used a midiron for many fairway shots.
She displayed a vintage midiron in a case at the museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iron - a golf club with a metal head, used for various shots

Etymology

From 'mid' (middle) + 'iron', indicating its use for mid-range shots among iron clubs.
