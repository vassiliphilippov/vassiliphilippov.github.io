hematuria

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˈtʊriə / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈtjʊəriə / (BrE)
the presence of blood in the urine, often indicating disease or injury of the urinary tract
blood in the urine (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered several tests when he observed hematuria during the patient's examination.
Hematuria can be a sign of kidney stones or urinary tract infections.
If you notice signs of hematuria, seek medical attention promptly.

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'ouron' (urine).
