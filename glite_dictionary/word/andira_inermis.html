Andira inermis

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdaɪrə ɪˈnɜːrmɪs / (AmE), / ænˈdaɪərə ɪˈnɜːmɪs / (BrE)
A tropical tree with shaggy, unpleasant-smelling, toxic bark, known for its strong durable wood, whose bark and seeds are used as a purgative, vermifuge, and narcotic.
tropical tree with toxic bark and medicinal uses

Examples

The bark of Andira inermis is known for its strong purgative properties in traditional medicine.
Carpenters often use wood from Andira inermis because of its remarkable durability.
Local communities warn against touching the toxic bark of Andira inermis without protection.

Etymology

From genus name 'Andira', derived from Tupi; 'inermis' is Latin for 'unarmed' or 'without thorns'.
