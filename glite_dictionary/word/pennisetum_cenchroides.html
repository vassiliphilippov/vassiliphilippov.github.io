Pennisetum cenchroides

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnɪˈsitəm sɛnˈkrɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈsiːtəm sɛnˈkrɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
An erect, tussock-forming perennial grass, often used in South Africa and Australia as pasture and forage.
pasture grass used in South Africa and Australia

Examples

Pennisetum cenchroides is commonly planted to improve grazing land in arid regions.
Farmers often rely on Pennisetum cenchroides to provide high-quality forage for livestock.
The rapid growth of Pennisetum cenchroides makes it an ideal choice for pasture rehabilitation.

Etymology

From Latin genus *Pennisetum* (feather bristle) and species name *cenchroides*, from Greek *kenchros* (millet-like grass) + -oides (resembling).
