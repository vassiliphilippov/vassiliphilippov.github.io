Galium lanceolatum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪliəm ˌlænsioʊˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪliəm ˌlansiəˈleɪtəm / (BrE)
A species of bedstraw native to North America, known for its sweetish roots.
a North American species of bedstraw

Examples

The roots of Galium lanceolatum are said to have a sweet flavor.
Botanists identified the plant as Galium lanceolatum during the field survey.
Native to forested areas, Galium lanceolatum often grows in moist, shaded places.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedstraw - a genus of herbaceous plants, also known as Galium

Etymology

From Latin: 'Galium' (the genus), from Greek 'gala' (milk), and 'lanceolatum' meaning 'lance-shaped' leaves.
