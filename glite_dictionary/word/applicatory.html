applicatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈæplɪkəˌtɔri / (AmE), / əˈplɪkətəri / (BrE)
readily applicable or practical; capable of being applied to a particular purpose or use
readily applicable or practical

Examples

The theory has immediate applicatory value in industrial settings.
She offered applicatory suggestions to address the current challenges.
His research focuses on the applicatory aspects of the technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: applicable - relevant or appropriate; capable of being applied
  • Synonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice
  • Antonym: impractical - not sensible or realistic; not practical

Etymology

From Medieval Latin applicatorius (‘pertaining to application’), from Latin applicare (‘to apply’).
