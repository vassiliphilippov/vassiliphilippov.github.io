Bartramia longicauda

1. NOUN

/ bɑrˈtreɪmiə lɒnˈɡɪkədə / (AmE), / bɑːˈtreɪmiə lɒŋˈɡɪkədə / (BrE)
A large plover-like sandpiper native to North American fields and uplands, also known as the upland sandpiper.
upland North American sandpiper

Examples

The Bartramia longicauda can often be found in grasslands during the breeding season.
Researchers observed several Bartramia longicauda in the conservation area last spring.
Unlike many shorebirds, the Bartramia longicauda prefers upland habitats far from the coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upland sandpiper - common English name for Bartramia longicauda

Etymology

From Latin: Bartramia honors American naturalist William Bartram; longicauda means 'long-tailed.'
