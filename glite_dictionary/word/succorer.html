succorer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌkərər / (AmE), / ˈsʌkərə / (BrE)
a person who provides help or assistance to someone in need, especially in difficult or distressing situations
someone who helps in times of need

Examples

In her darkest hour, the stranger became her succorer and offered a helping hand.
He was praised as the succorer of the poor and the lost in the village.
Throughout history, every society has needed a succorer during times of crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helper - a person who helps or assists
  • Synonym: rescuer - a person who saves someone from a dangerous or difficult situation
  • Synonym: benefactor - a person who gives help, especially financial aid

Etymology

From Old French 'socorreur', from Latin 'succurrere' (to help, to run to the aid of).
