boastful

1. ADJ

/ ˈboʊstfəl / (AmE), / ˈbəʊstfəl / (BrE)
Showing excessive pride and self-satisfaction in one's achievements, possessions, or abilities; given to boasting.
proudly self-important; inclined to boast

Examples

Her boastful remarks about her accomplishments annoyed everyone at the meeting.
Although he is talented, his boastful attitude makes it hard to work with him.
The boastful tone of the brochure exaggerated the company's success.

Synset Examples

big talk

Related Terms

  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From 'boast' + '-ful'; 'boast' from Middle English bost (arrogance, bragging).
