upholsterer

1. NOUN

/ ʌpˈhoʊlstərər / (AmE), / ʌpˈhɒlstərə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to cover chairs, sofas, and other furniture with padding, springs, and fabric or leather
person who fits covers on furniture

Examples

The upholsterer restored the antique armchair to its former glory.
We hired a skilled upholsterer to re-cover our old sofa with new fabric.
As a professional upholsterer, she often works with both modern and vintage furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upholstery - the materials used to cushion and cover furniture

Etymology

From 'upholster' (to supply with covering and stuffing) + '-er' (agent suffix).
