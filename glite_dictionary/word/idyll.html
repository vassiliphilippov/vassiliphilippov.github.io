idyll

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.dəl / (AmE), / ˈɪd.ɪl / (BrE)
A short poem or prose piece that describes peaceful, rural, or pastoral life, often idealized.
short poem about rural life

Examples

The poet's idyll painted a serene and harmonious countryside far from city life.
She read an idyll that evoked the simplicity and beauty of rural living.
Tennyson's collection of idylls remains a classic example of pastoral poetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastoral - a work of literature portraying country life, especially in an idealized form

Etymology

From Latin 'idyllium', from Greek 'eidyllion' (short poem, little picture).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.dəl / (AmE), / ˈɪ.dɪl / (BrE)
A period or experience of peaceful, happy, and picturesque charm, especially one that is characteristic of rural or romantic life.
a peaceful or romantic episode or scene

Examples

The summer they spent in the countryside was a perfect idyll to remember.
Their brief idyll ended when they returned to the city and their busy lives.
She looked back on those early days of marriage as an idyll of happiness and simplicity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interlude - a period or event that comes between others and has a different character

Etymology

From Latin 'idyllium', from Greek 'eidyllion' (short poem or little form).
