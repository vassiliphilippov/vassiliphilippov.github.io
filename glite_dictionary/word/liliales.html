Liliales

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈlaɪ.əˌliːz / (AmE), / lɪˈlaɪ.əˌliːz / (BrE)
an order of monocotyledonous flowering plants that includes families such as the lilies (Liliaceae), amaryllises (Amaryllidaceae), and irises (Iridaceae)
order of monocotyledonous plants

Examples

The Liliales order contains many well-known ornamental plants including tulips and lilies.
Botanists often study the evolutionary relationships within the Liliales to understand plant diversification.
Recent research has led to some reclassification of certain families within the Liliales.

Etymology

From modern Latin plural of Liliaceae (the lily family), based on Latin lilium (lily).
