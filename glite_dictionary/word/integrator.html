integrator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.təˌɡreɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tɪˌɡreɪ.tə / (BrE)
a measuring instrument used to calculate the area of an irregular plane figure, often by integrating its outline
instrument for measuring area by integration

Examples

The scientist used an integrator to find the area enclosed by the experimental curve.
Before digital computers, a mechanical integrator was essential for surveying and mapping lands with irregular boundaries.
The engineer demonstrated how the integrator could calculate areas of complicated shapes quickly and accurately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planimeter - an instrument for measuring the area of a two-dimensional shape

Etymology

From Latin integrare (to make whole) + -tor (agent suffix).
