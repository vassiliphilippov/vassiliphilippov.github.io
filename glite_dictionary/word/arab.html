Arab

1. NOUN

/ ˈærəb / (AmE), / ˈærəb / (BrE)
a spirited, graceful, and intelligent breed of riding horse originally from Arabia, known for its stamina and distinctive appearance
breed of horse from Arabia

Examples

The Arab is prized for its endurance and speed in long-distance races.
She owns a beautiful Arab that has won several equestrian competitions.
Many breeders admire the distinct profile and intelligence of the Arab horse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arabian horse - a horse of the Arabian breed, similar meaning

Etymology

From the use of 'Arab' to mean 'from Arabia'; refers to the horse's geographic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈær.əb / (AmE), / ˈær.əb / (BrE)
a member of a Semitic people originally from the Arabian Peninsula and surrounding regions, who speaks Arabic and inhabits much of the Middle East and North Africa
member of an Arabic-speaking Semitic people

Examples

Many Arabs live in countries across the Middle East and North Africa.
She is an Arab who grew up in Egypt and speaks several dialects of Arabic.
The Arabs have a long history of scholarship, trade, and cultural exchange.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arabian - relating to Arabia or its people
  • Antonym: Jew - a member of the Hebrew people or cultural group

Etymology

From Latin 'Arabus', from Greek 'Araps', from earlier Arabic 'ʿarab'.
