hypericism

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɛrɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / haɪˈpɛrɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
a severe form of dermatitis in herbivorous domestic animals caused by photosensitivity after ingesting Saint John's wort
photosensitivity-induced dermatitis in animals
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the sheep with hypericism after noticing lesions on their exposed skin.
Cattle are susceptible to hypericism if they graze on pastures where Saint John's wort grows abundantly.
Farmers should be aware that certain weeds can cause hypericism in grazing livestock.

Etymology

From Greek hyperikon (St John's wort) + -ism.
