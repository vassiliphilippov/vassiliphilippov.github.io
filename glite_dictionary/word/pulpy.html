pulpy

1. ADJ

/ ˈpʌlpi / (AmE), / ˈpʌlpi / (BrE)
having the soft, moist texture or consistency of pulp; resembling or consisting of pulp, especially as in overripe fruit or soft matter.
having a soft, pulp-like texture

Examples

The overripe peach had turned pulpy and started to leak juice.
After boiling for hours, the vegetables became soft and pulpy.
The magazine's pulpy pages tore easily when they got wet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushy - having a soft, spongy texture
  • Antonym: fibrous - containing or resembling fibers, often used as an antonym in texture

Etymology

From 'pulp' + '-y'.
