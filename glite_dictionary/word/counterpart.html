counterpart

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌpɑrt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəpɑːt / (BrE)
a person or thing that has the same function or characteristics as another person or thing in a different place or situation
person or thing with the same function as another

Examples

The American ambassador met his British counterpart to discuss the trade agreement.
My job is similar to that of my counterpart in the New York office.
Each senator has a counterpart in the House of Representatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equivalent - a person or thing that is equal to another in value, amount, function, or meaning
  • Antonym: opposite - a person or thing that is completely different from another

Etymology

From French 'contrepartie', meaning 'corresponding part'.
