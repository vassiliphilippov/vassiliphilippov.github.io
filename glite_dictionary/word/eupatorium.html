Eupatorium

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːpəˈtɔːriəm / (AmE), / ˌjuːpəˈtɔːriəm / (BrE)
A large genus of chiefly tropical plants in the daisy family, with clusters of small white or purplish flowers.
a genus of tropical flowering plants

Examples

Several species of Eupatorium are grown for their attractive flowers in garden borders.
The Eupatorium genus includes both annual and perennial plants.
Some herbalists use Eupatorium for traditional medicinal purposes.

Etymology

From Latin 'Eupatorion', from Greek, named after Mithridates Eupator, a king of Pontus reputed to have discovered medicinal uses for the plant.

2. NOUN

/ juːˌpeɪˈtɔːriəm / (AmE), / juːˌpeɪˈtɔːriəm / (BrE)
A genus of mainly tropical herbaceous plants with heads of white or purplish flowers, belonging to the aster family.
genus of tropical flowering herbs

Examples

The Eupatorium genus includes well-known wildflowers such as Joe-Pye weed and boneset.
Gardeners value some Eupatorium species for their clusters of white or purple blooms.
Botanists studied the medicinal uses of various Eupatorium plants throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: plant - any living organism of the kingdom Plantae

Etymology

From Latin *Eupatorium*, named after Mithridates Eupator, an ancient king reputed to use such plants medicinally.
