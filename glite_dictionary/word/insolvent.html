insolvent

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɑːlvənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɒlvənt / (BrE)
Unable to pay debts owed; having insufficient assets to cover financial obligations.
unable to pay debts

Examples

After years of financial trouble, the company was declared insolvent by the court.
The charity became insolvent after losing its major source of funding.
If you are insolvent, you may need to seek advice from a financial counselor.

Synset Examples

an insolvent person
an insolvent estate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bankrupt - legally declared unable to pay debts
  • Antonym: solvent - having enough money to pay all debts

Etymology

From Latin 'insolvens', from 'in-' (not) + 'solvere' (to loosen, pay).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɑlvənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɒlvənt / (BrE)
A person who is unable to pay their debts because they do not have enough money or assets.
a person unable to pay their debts

Examples

After the market crash, many investors became insolvent and were unable to pay their creditors.
As an insolvent, he was no longer allowed to operate his business until his debts were settled.
The court declared her an insolvent after reviewing the state of her finances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bankrupt - a person or organization declared in law as unable to pay debts

Etymology

From Latin *insolvens*, from in- (not) + solvere (to loosen, to pay).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09857758-n
Synset POS: n
