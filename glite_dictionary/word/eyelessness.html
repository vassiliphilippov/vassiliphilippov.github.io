eyelessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈaɪlɪsnɪs / (BrE)
the condition of being without eyes, especially due to loss or absence of the eyes
state of being without eyes

Examples

After the accident, his complete eyelessness made daily activities extremely challenging.
The cavefish's eyelessness is an adaptation to its dark environment.
She described the statue's eyelessness as both haunting and strangely beautiful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blindness - the condition of lacking visual perception

Etymology

Formed from 'eyeless' (without eyes) + '-ness' (state or quality).
