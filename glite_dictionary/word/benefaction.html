benefaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛnəˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌbɛnɪˈfakʃən / (BrE)
a contribution, especially of money or assistance, given to help a person or organization
a gift or act of help

Examples

The museum survived thanks to a generous benefaction from a local businessman.
His education was made possible by the benefaction of a charitable foundation.
They honored him for his repeated benefactions to the school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donation - a gift, especially of money, to a charity or other good cause
  • Synonym: contribution - something that is given to help a person or organization

Etymology

From Latin 'benefactio' (an act of doing good), from 'bene' (well) + 'facere' (to do).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbɛn.əˈfæk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌbɛnɪˈfækʃən / (BrE)
an act of kindness or a charitable donation; the giving of help, especially money, to others
an act of kindness or charity

Examples

The foundation received a generous benefaction from an anonymous donor.
Her benefaction helped provide scholarships for dozens of students in need.
Thanks to his benefaction, the hospital was able to purchase new medical equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donation - something that is given to a person or organization, such as money or goods
  • Synonym: charity - the voluntary giving of help, typically in the form of money, to those in need
  • Antonym: malefaction - an evil deed or crime

Etymology

From Latin 'benefactio', from 'bene' (well) + 'facere' (to do).
