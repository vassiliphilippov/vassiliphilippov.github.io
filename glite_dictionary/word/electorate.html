electorate

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktərət / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktərət / (BrE)
all the people in a country or area who are entitled to vote in an election
the body of people eligible to vote

Examples

The government was concerned about the views of the entire electorate.
Only a small percentage of the electorate turned out to vote in the last election.
Political parties often tailor their campaigns to appeal to the younger electorate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voters - people who vote in an election
  • Antonym: candidates - people standing for election

Etymology

From Latin 'elector' (chooser, voter) with the suffix '-ate'.
