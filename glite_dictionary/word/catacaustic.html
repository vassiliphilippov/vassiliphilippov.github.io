catacaustic

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˈkɔstɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈkɔːstɪk / (BrE)
In mathematics and physics, a curve formed as the envelope of rays reflected from a curved surface.
curve formed by reflected rays

Examples

The shape of the catacaustic depends on the geometry of the reflecting surface.
A coffee cup can create a visible catacaustic pattern when light shines on it.
He studied how the catacaustic changes when the mirror's curvature is altered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caustic curve - curve formed by light reflecting or refracting from a surface

Etymology

From Greek 'katakaustikos', from 'kata-' (down, against) + 'kaiein' (to burn).
