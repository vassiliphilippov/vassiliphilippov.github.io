to liquidize

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪkwəˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪdaɪz / (BrE)
to sell off or dispose of all merchandise or assets, often to convert them into cash
sell or dispose of merchandise/assets

Examples

The company decided to liquidize its remaining stock before closing down.
When demand fell, she had to liquidize most of her inventory at discount prices.
They are planning to liquidize all their assets to settle outstanding debts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to liquidate - to sell assets for cash, often to pay debts

Etymology

Formed from 'liquid' + '-ize', relating to the process of converting assets into liquid (cash) form.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02252366-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈlɪkwəˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪdaɪz / (BrE)
to make or become liquid, especially by heating or blending
make into a liquid

Examples

The chef used a blender to liquidize the vegetables for the soup.
The metal will liquidize when exposed to high temperatures.
She added water to liquidize the thick sauce.

Synset Examples

liquefy the silver

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to liquefy - make or become liquid, especially by heating
  • Antonym: to solidify - make or become solid

Etymology

From 'liquid' + '-ize'.
