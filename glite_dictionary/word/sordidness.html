sordidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrdɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɔːdɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being dirty, squalid, or morally degraded
state of being dirty or morally degraded

Examples

The sordidness of the abandoned house made it unsafe to enter without protection.
He was appalled by the sordidness he uncovered in the city’s underworld.
Despite its sordidness, the alley was bustling with life at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squalor - the state of being extremely dirty and unpleasant
  • Antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean or kept clean

Etymology

From 'sordid' + '-ness'; ultimately from Latin sordidus (dirty).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔɹ.dɪd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɔː.dɪd.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being morally degraded, dirty, or lacking in high values
lack of moral values; moral filth

Examples

The sordidness of the scandal shocked even the most jaded politicians.
She was repelled by the sordidness that permeated the criminal underworld.
His novel exposes the sordidness lurking beneath the city's glamorous surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depravity - moral corruption; wickedness
  • Antonym: virtue - high moral standards

Etymology

From Latin 'sordidus' (dirty, base, mean) via Old French and Middle English.
