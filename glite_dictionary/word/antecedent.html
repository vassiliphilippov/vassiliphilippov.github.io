antecedent

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.t̬əˈsiː.dənt / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dənt / (BrE)
a person from whom one is descended, typically more distant than a grandparent; an ancestor
distant ancestor

Examples

The researcher traced her family tree to find her earliest antecedent in the 17th century.
His antecedent emigrated from Ireland nearly 200 years ago.
Some cultures place great importance on honoring their antecedents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestor - a person in your family who lived a long time ago

Etymology

From Latin antecedentem, meaning 'going before'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dənt / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dənt / (BrE)
A word, phrase, or clause to which an anaphoric pronoun refers in a sentence.
word or phrase referred to by an anaphor

Examples

The antecedent of 'she' in the sentence 'Sarah arrived because she was late' is 'Sarah'.
In grammar, it is important to identify the antecedent of each pronoun clearly.
In the phrase 'the dog that barked', 'the dog' is the antecedent of 'that'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: referent - the thing that a word or phrase denotes or stands for
  • Antonym: consequent - the part of a sentence that follows 'then' in a conditional statement; opposite of antecedent in logic

Etymology

From Latin antecedentem (going before), from antecedere (to go before).

3. ADJ

/ ˌæn.təˈsiː.dənt / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dənt / (BrE)
Preceding in time or order; coming before something else.
preceding; earlier in time or order

Examples

The committee reviewed all antecedent events before making a decision.
Historical documents often contain laws antecedent to current regulations.
Their agreement was influenced by antecedent discussions held months earlier.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subsequent - coming after in time or order
  • Synonym: preceding - existing or coming before in time or order

Etymology

From Latin antecedentem, present participle of antecedere (to go before), from ante (before) + cedere (to go).

4. NOUN

/ ˌæn.təˈsiː.dənt / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dənt / (BrE)
an event or circumstance that happens before another, especially as a cause or precursor
preceding event or cause

Examples

The judge considered the antecedent events leading up to the accident.
Economic instability was the antecedent to the political crisis.
Understanding the antecedent causes can help prevent future incidents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precursor - a person or thing that comes before another of the same kind; a forerunner
  • Antonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition

Etymology

From Latin 'antecedens', present participle of 'antecedere' (to go before).

5. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dənt / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dənt / (BrE)
a thing or event that existed before or logically precedes another, especially one that influences a later development
something that precedes another

Examples

Phrenology was an important antecedent of modern neuroscience.
Many historians study the antecedents of contemporary political systems.
The telephone had several technological antecedents before it was fully developed.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predecessor - a person or thing that came before another
  • Antonym: successor - a person or thing that comes after another

Etymology

From Latin antecedentem, from antecedere 'go before'.
