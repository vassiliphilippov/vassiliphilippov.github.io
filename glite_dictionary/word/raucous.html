raucous

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɔk.əs / (AmE), / ˈrɔː.kəs / (BrE)
loud and harsh; making a disturbingly rough or noisy sound.
loud, harsh, and noisy

Examples

The raucous laughter from the bar could be heard down the street.
After the raucous concert, my ears were ringing for hours.
Their raucous celebration woke up half the neighborhood.

Synset Examples

a raucous party
rowdy teenagers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rowdy - noisy, disorderly, and unruly

Etymology

From Latin 'raucus' meaning 'hoarse', via late Latin 'raucosus'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɔk.əs / (AmE), / ˈrɔː.kəs / (BrE)
Unpleasantly loud, harsh, or disorderly.
unpleasantly loud and harsh

Examples

The raucous laughter of the crowd echoed through the hall.
We struggled to talk over the raucous noise from the construction outside.
Her raucous voice could be heard above everyone else's.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harsh - unpleasantly rough or jarring to the senses
  • Antonym: soft - not loud or harsh

Etymology

From Latin 'raucus' (hoarse, harsh).

WordNet ID: oewn-00301225-s
Synset POS: s
