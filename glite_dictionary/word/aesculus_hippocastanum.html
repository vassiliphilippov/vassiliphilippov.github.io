Aesculus hippocastanum

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːsˈkjuːləs hɪˌpɑːkəˈstænəm / (AmE), / ˌiːsˈkjuːləs hɪˌpɒkəˈstænəm / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree native to southeastern Europe, with palmate leaves, upright clusters of white or red flowers, and shiny brown inedible seeds, commonly known as the horse chestnut.
large deciduous tree; the horse chestnut

Examples

The seeds of Aesculus hippocastanum are shiny and brown, but they are not edible like sweet chestnuts.
In spring, Aesculus hippocastanum produces striking clusters of white flowers in parks across Europe.
Doctors once used extracts from Aesculus hippocastanum to treat various medical conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse chestnut - common name for Aesculus hippocastanum

Etymology

From Latin, genus name 'Aesculus' and species epithet 'hippocastanum', meaning 'horse chestnut'.
