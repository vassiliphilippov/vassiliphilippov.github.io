nightingale

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtɪnˌɡeɪl / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtɪnɡeɪl / (BrE)
A small European songbird known for its powerful and melodious nocturnal song.
a European songbird with a melodious song

Examples

The nightingale is famous for singing at night when most other birds are silent.
Poets have often written about the beautiful song of the nightingale.
A nightingale can be heard in the woods during late spring evenings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrush - a type of songbird related to the nightingale

Etymology

From Old English 'nihtegale', meaning 'night singer' (niht 'night' + galan 'to sing').
