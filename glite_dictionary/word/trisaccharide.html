trisaccharide

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈsæk.əˌraɪd / (AmE), / traɪˈsak.ə.raɪd / (BrE)
a carbohydrate that yields three monosaccharide molecules when completely hydrolyzed
carbohydrate yielding three monosaccharides

Examples

Scientists isolated a trisaccharide from the plant to study its properties.
On hydrolysis, a trisaccharide produces three distinct monosaccharide units.
The nutritional analysis revealed the presence of a unique trisaccharide in the grain.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: disaccharide - a carbohydrate that yields two monosaccharides on hydrolysis
  • Degree Higher: tetrasaccharide - a carbohydrate that yields four monosaccharides on hydrolysis

Etymology

From Greek 'tri-' meaning 'three' and 'sacchar' meaning 'sugar'.
