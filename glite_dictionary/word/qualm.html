qualm

1. NOUN

/ kwɑm / (AmE), / kwɑːm / (BrE)
a feeling of uneasiness or doubt, especially about whether one is doing the right thing
feeling of doubt or uneasiness

Examples

She felt a qualm about lying to her friend, but did it anyway.
Despite his qualms, he went along with the risky plan.
He had no qualm about speaking his mind openly in the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scruple - a feeling of doubt or hesitation with regard to morality or propriety
  • Synonym: misgiving - a feeling of doubt or apprehension about the outcome or consequences of something

Etymology

From German *Qualm* (smoke, confusion), influenced by English usage of the late 16th century to mean a vague feeling or sudden sick feeling.

2. NOUN

/ kwɑm / (AmE), / kwɑːm / (BrE)
a slight feeling of nausea or uneasiness in the stomach
slight feeling of nausea

Examples

She felt a sudden qualm after eating the questionable seafood.
During the boat ride, a qualm of nausea hit him unexpectedly.
He managed to hide his qualm when the strong smell reached him.

Etymology

From Old English cwealm (pain, death, sickness).
