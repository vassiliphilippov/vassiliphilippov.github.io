amphiprostylar

1. ADJ

/ ˌæm.fɪˈprɑː.stɪ.lɚ / (AmE), / ˌam.fɪˈprɒs.tɪ.lə / (BrE)
(of a building) having porticoes with free columns at both ends or on both sides.
having porticoes with columns at both ends or sides

Examples

The temple's amphiprostylar design was distinctive, featuring columned porticoes at both ends.
Scholars admire the amphiprostylar structure for its symmetry and classical proportions.
Ancient architects often employed the amphiprostylar style to create a sense of grandeur.

Etymology

From Greek amphi- (on both sides) + prostylos (with columns at the front).
