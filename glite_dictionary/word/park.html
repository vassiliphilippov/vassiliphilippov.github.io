park

1. NOUN

/ pɑrk / (AmE), / pɑːk / (BrE)
a stadium or large field where ball games, especially baseball, are played
sports facility (esp. for baseball)
american

Examples

We spent Saturday afternoon watching a doubleheader at the park.
The city decided to build a new park downtown for the baseball team.
Fans cheered as their favorite player hit a home run out of the park.

Synset Examples

take me out to the ballpark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballpark - a stadium where baseball is played
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02785801-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ pɑrk / (AmE), / pɑːk / (BrE)
a large public area of land in a town or city, kept for recreation and enjoyment, usually with grass, trees, and places for people to walk, sit, or play
public area for recreation in a city or town

Examples

Let's meet at the park after work and go for a stroll.
Families often have picnics in the park on sunny weekends.
The new park in the city center has a playground and a lake.

Synset Examples

they went for a walk in the park

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garden - a piece of ground, often with flowers or vegetables, next to a house or in a public space

Etymology

From Old French parc, from Medieval Latin parcus (enclosure, game preserve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08632949-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ pɑrk / (AmE), / pɑːk / (BrE)
the gear position in an automatic vehicle that locks the transmission, preventing the vehicle from moving when stationary
gear position for immobilizing a vehicle

Examples

When you stop the car, make sure it is in park before turning off the engine.
He accidentally tried to start the car while it was still in park.
The instructor reminded the student to shift into park before getting out.

Synset Examples

they put the car in park and got out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutral - gear position in which the engine is disengaged from the wheels
  • Antonym: drive - gear position used for moving forward in an automatic vehicle

5. NOUN

/ pɑrk / (AmE), / pɑːk / (BrE)
A large area of land, usually kept in its natural state, set aside for public use and recreation.
large public natural area

Examples

Yellowstone is the most famous park in the United States.
Many families enjoy picnics in the city's largest park.
The park offers hiking trails and campsites for visitors.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserve - an area of land set aside for a particular purpose, especially conservation
  • Degree Lower: garden - a smaller, cultivated outdoor public space with plants and flowers

Etymology

From Old French *parc*, from Medieval Latin *parricus* (enclosure).

7. VERB

/ pɑrk / (AmE), / pɑːk / (BrE)
to leave a vehicle or other object in a particular place, typically for a short time; to place something or someone somewhere temporarily
leave or place temporarily

Examples

You can't park your car here overnight without a permit.
She parked her suitcase in the hallway while waiting for her room.
I told him to park the kids with their grandparents for the afternoon.

Synset Examples

park the car in the yard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: station - to position or place something or someone for a purpose
  • Antonym: remove - to take something away from where it was placed

Etymology

From French *parc* (an enclosure), later extended to the sense of enclosing or placing something.
