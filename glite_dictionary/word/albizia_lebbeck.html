Albizia lebbeck

1. NOUN

/ ælˈbɪziə lɛbˈbɛk / (AmE), / ælˈbɪziə lɛbˈbɛk / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree native to the Old World, known for its large leaves, spherical clusters of greenish-yellow flowers, and long seed pods that rattle in the wind; commonly called the siris tree or woman's tongue tree
species of large Old World tropical tree

Examples

The Albizia lebbeck is often planted as a shade tree in tropical regions.
In the garden, the distinctive seed pods of Albizia lebbeck make a characteristic clattering sound in the wind.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Albizia lebbeck bark for potential health benefits.

Etymology

Genus name Albizia from Italian nobleman Filippo degli Albizzi; species name from the Arabic 'labakh'.
