bridegroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪdˌɡruːm / (AmE), / ˈbraɪdɡruːm / (BrE)
a man who has recently been married, especially on his wedding day
man recently married

Examples

The bridegroom gave a heartfelt speech at the reception.
After the ceremony, the bridegroom was congratulated by his friends and family.
The bridegroom wore a classic black tuxedo for the wedding.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bride - a woman who has recently been married

Etymology

From Old English brydguma, from bryd (bride) + guma (man).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪdˌɡrum / (AmE), / ˈbraɪdˌɡruːm / (BrE)
a man who is about to get married or has just been married, especially on his wedding day
man at his own wedding

Examples

The bridegroom waited nervously at the altar for his bride to arrive.
Everyone cheered as the bridegroom kissed his new wife.
Traditionally, the bridegroom gives a speech at the wedding reception.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bride - a woman at her own wedding

Etymology

From Old English 'brydguma' (bride + man).
