to autotomise

1. VERB

/ ɔːˈtɑːtəmaɪz / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒtəmaɪz / (BrE)
(of an animal) to cause a body part to be shed or detached as a defense mechanism or response to injury.
shed or detach a body part (animal)

Examples

Some species of lizards can autotomise their tails to escape from predators.
Certain sea slugs are able to autotomise parts of their bodies when threatened.
The crab may autotomise a limb if it becomes trapped or severely damaged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autotomy - the act of self-amputation of a body part in animals

Etymology

From 'autotomy' + '-ise', where 'autotomy' is from Greek 'auto-' (self) + 'tome' (cutting).
