acknowledgement

1. NOUN

/ əkˈnɑlɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / əkˈnɒlɪdʒm(ə)nt / (BrE)
the state or fact of being recognized, accepted, or acknowledged for something
recognition or acceptance

Examples

She received public acknowledgement for her contributions to the project.
Winning the award was an acknowledgement of all his hard work.
The letter offered a formal acknowledgement of their generous donation.

Synset Examples

the partners were delighted with the recognition of their work
she seems to avoid much in the way of recognition or acknowledgement of feminist work prior to her own

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recognition - the act of recognizing or the state of being recognized
  • Antonym: denial - the act of refusing to acknowledge or accept something

Etymology

From Old French 'acknowledgment', from acknowledge + -ment.

2. NOUN

/ əkˈnɑlɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / əkˈnɒlɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
a statement or action showing that something or someone has been recognized or accepted; an expression of gratitude or recognition
recognition or expression of thanks

Examples

The author's acknowledgement section thanked everyone who contributed to the book.
She nodded in acknowledgement of his compliment, smiling politely.
He sent a brief email as an acknowledgement that he had received the documents.

Synset Examples

she must have seen him but she gave no sign of acknowledgment
the preface contained an acknowledgment of those who had helped her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recognition - the act of recognizing or acknowledging

Etymology

From acknowledge +‎ -ment, modeled after similar French noun forms.
