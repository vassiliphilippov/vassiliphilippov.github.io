homesick

1. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊmˌsɪk / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌsɪk / (BrE)
feeling unhappy or longing because one is away from home
longing for home

Examples

After moving to college, she felt homesick for her family and old friends.
He became homesick after spending six months working abroad in a different country.
The children grew homesick while staying at summer camp for the first time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nostalgic - feeling sentimental longing for the past, especially one's home or childhood
  • Antonym: content - feeling satisfied and at ease, especially with being away from home

Etymology

From home + sick, first attested in the 18th century, modeled on German Heimweh.
