chimera

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈmɪrə / (AmE), / kɪˈmɪərə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a fire-breathing female monster with a lion's head, a goat's body, and a serpent's tail.
mythical fire-breathing monster (lion, goat, serpent)

Examples

In the ancient myth, the hero Bellerophon slays the fearsome chimera.
The statue in the museum is modeled after the legendary chimera of Greek mythology.
She described her nightmare as being chased by a monstrous chimera through a burning city.

Etymology

From Greek Khimaira (mythical she-goat monster).

2. NOUN

/ kaɪˈmɪrə / (AmE), / kɪˈmɪərə / (BrE)
a thing that is hoped for or imagined but is illusory or impossible to achieve; a grotesque or fanciful creation of the imagination
grotesque or illusory imaginary creation

Examples

The idea of a perfect society is often dismissed as a chimera by realists.
Her theory was ultimately regarded as a chimera with no basis in fact.
Chasing that dream felt more and more like pursuing a chimera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fantasy - the faculty or activity of imagining things, especially things that are impossible or improbable
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Greek 'khimaira', a mythological fire-breathing monster.
