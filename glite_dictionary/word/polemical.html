polemical

1. ADJ

/ pəˈlɛmɪkəl / (AmE), / pəˈlɛmɪkəl / (BrE)
involving strongly critical, controversial, or disputatious writing or speech
controversial or argumentative

Examples

The author's polemical style often provoked strong reactions from critics and supporters alike.
Her polemical essay challenged the policies of the current administration.
The debate quickly turned polemical as both sides began accusing each other of spreading misinformation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argumentative - fond of or given to argument and dispute
  • Antonym: conciliatory - intended or likely to placate or pacify

Etymology

From Greek *polemikos* (warlike, hostile), via Late Latin *polemicus*.
