Praunus

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːnəs / (AmE), / ˈprɔːnəs / (BrE)
A genus of small crustaceans in the family Mysidae, known as opossum shrimps.
a genus of shrimp-like crustaceans

Examples

Species of Praunus are commonly found in coastal waters of Europe.
The biologist collected several specimens of Praunus during the marine survey.
Members of the genus Praunus play an important role in the aquatic food web.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpreɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈprɔː.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of small shrimp-like crustaceans in the family Mysidae, found in marine environments.
genus of small marine crustaceans

Examples

The Praunus genus is often found in cold marine waters around Europe.
Marine biologists collected several specimens of Praunus during the survey.
Many species in Praunus display transparent bodies for camouflage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Mysidae - family of small marine crustaceans

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, arbitrary genus name following taxonomic conventions.
