might

1. NOUN

/ maɪt / (AmE), / maɪt / (BrE)
Great physical strength or power.
physical strength or power

Examples

The wrestler displayed his incredible might in the final round of the match.
With the might of the waves, the ship was tossed back and forth.
She lifted the heavy box with all her might.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strength - the quality or state of being physically strong
  • Synonym: power - ability to do something or act in a particular way, especially physical ability
  • Antonym: weakness - the state of lacking physical strength or power

Etymology

Old English *miht* (power, strength), related to *magan* (to be able).
