Hevea brasiliensis

1. NOUN

/ hiˈviːə brəˌzɪliˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / hɛˈviːə bræˌzɪliˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to the Amazon and Orinoco regions, known for producing the milky latex that is the primary source of natural rubber.
South American rubber tree

Examples

The plantation was filled with rows of Hevea brasiliensis trees ready for tapping.
Commercial production of natural rubber relies heavily on Hevea brasiliensis.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Hevea brasiliensis in different climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubber tree - a tree producing latex used for natural rubber

Etymology

From Latin Hevea (genus name) and brasiliensis, meaning 'from Brazil'.
