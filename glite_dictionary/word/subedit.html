to subedit

1. VERB

/ ˌsʌbˈɛdɪt / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈɛdɪt / (BrE)
to edit and correct written or printed material, especially as part of the production process in publishing or journalism
edit written or printed material

Examples

She was hired to subedit articles before they went to print.
You must subedit this report for grammar and clarity before the deadline.
He spent most of his career learning how to subedit technical documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to edit - to prepare written material for publication by correcting, revising, or adapting it

Etymology

Formed from 'sub-' (meaning 'under') + 'edit', referring to the subordinate editorial role.
