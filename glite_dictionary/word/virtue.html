virtue

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝː.tʃuː / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tʃuː / (BrE)
The quality of conforming to moral principles; doing what is right and avoiding what is wrong.
moral goodness; righteousness

Examples

Honesty is often considered a virtue in many cultures around the world.
She was admired for her kindness and moral virtue.
Patience is a difficult virtue to master, especially in stressful situations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vice - immoral or wicked behavior
  • Synonym: morality - principles distinguishing right from wrong

Etymology

From Latin virtus (moral strength, valor), from vir (man).
