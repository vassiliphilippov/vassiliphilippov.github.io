jarful

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑrˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɑːfʊl / (BrE)
An amount that fills a jar.
the amount that fills a jar

Examples

She opened a new jarful of homemade jam for breakfast.
The recipe requires one jarful of olives, pitted and chopped.
After several hours of picking, we had a jarful of fresh honey.

Synset Examples

he drank a jar of beer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cupful - the amount held by a cup
  • Synonym: jugful - the amount held by a jug

Etymology

Formed from 'jar' + 'ful', following the pattern of compound quantifying nouns.
