Calymmatobacterium granulomatis

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪm.məˌtoʊ.bækˌtɪr.i.əm ˌɡræn.jə.ləˈmæ.tɪs / (AmE), / kəˌlɪm.məˌtɒ.bækˌtɪə.rɪ.əm ˌɡræn.jʊ.ləˈmæt.ɪs / (BrE)
a species of bacteria that causes granuloma inguinale, a sexually transmitted infection
bacterial species causing granuloma inguinale
medical

Examples

Calymmatobacterium granulomatis is responsible for causing granuloma inguinale, a rare sexually transmitted infection.
The patient was diagnosed with an infection by Calymmatobacterium granulomatis after laboratory analysis of a lesion sample.
Due to taxonomic revisions, Calymmatobacterium granulomatis is now often classified as Klebsiella granulomatis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Klebsiella granulomatis - alternative name for same bacterial species

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek kalymma (covering), bacterium (bacterium), and granulomatis (relating to granuloma).
