hydrokinetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪdroʊkɪˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəʊkɪˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the movement of fluids or the forces that produce or result from such movement
relating to moving fluids or their forces

Examples

The new turbine utilizes hydrokinetic energy from river currents to generate electricity.
Recent advances in hydrokinetic technology have improved power generation from tidal flows.
Studying hydrokinetic forces is essential for designing efficient underwater vehicles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrodynamic - relating to the motion of fluids and the forces acting on solid bodies in them

Etymology

From hydro- (relating to water or fluids) + kinetic (from Greek kinētikos, relating to motion).
