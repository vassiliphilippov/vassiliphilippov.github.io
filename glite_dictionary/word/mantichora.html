mantichora

1. NOUN

/ mænˈtɪkərə / (AmE), / mænˈtɪkərə / (BrE)
a mythical creature with the head of a man (often with horns), the body of a lion, and the tail of a scorpion
a mythological monster with a man's head, lion's body, and scorpion's tail

Examples

Ancient legends often describe the mantichora as a fearsome guardian of hidden treasures.
The explorer claimed to have seen a creature resembling a mantichora deep in the jungle.
Artists in medieval times painted the mantichora with a human face and a deadly scorpion tail.

Etymology

From Latin 'mantichora', from Greek 'mantikhoras', probably from Old Persian, meaning 'man-eater'.
