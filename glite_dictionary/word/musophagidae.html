Musophagidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːsəˈfædʒɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌmjuːsəˈfædʒɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of brightly colored, fruit-eating African birds, commonly known as turacos.
family of African birds; turacos

Examples

Many species in the Musophagidae family have striking green or blue feathers.
Birdwatchers often travel to Africa to observe members of the Musophagidae, such as turacos, in their natural habitats.
Unlike other birds, the pigments found in Musophagidae feathers are unique to this family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turaco - brightly colored African bird in the Musophagidae family

Etymology

From Modern Latin, from Greek 'mûs' (mouse) + 'phagein' (to eat), originally believed to eat plantains (mistakenly called 'musa'), plus -idae indicating a family.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːsəˈfædʒɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌmjuːsəˈfædʒɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of African birds known as turacos, characterized by their bright plumage and unique pigments.
family of African birds (turacos)

Examples

Turacos, members of the Musophagidae, are known for their vibrant green and red feathers.
The Musophagidae family is native to sub-Saharan Africa and includes several colorful species.
Scientists study Musophagidae to understand the unique pigments present in their plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turaco - common name for birds in the family Musophagidae

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on Greek 'mûs' (mouse) and 'phagein' (to eat), reflecting a mistaken belief about their diet.
