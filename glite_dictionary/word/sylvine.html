sylvine

1. NOUN

/ sɪlˈvaɪn / (AmE), / sɪlˈviːn / (BrE)
A mineral consisting of naturally occurring potassium chloride, used as an important source of potassium.
mineral; native potassium chloride

Examples

Large deposits of sylvine are mined for use in fertilizers worldwide.
The crystal structure of sylvine is similar to that of halite, or rock salt.
Scientists analyzed the purity of the sylvine sample extracted from the sedimentary rock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sylvite - another name for sylvine (mineral form of potassium chloride)

Etymology

From French *sylvine*, named after Latin *Sylvius*, the surname of the French chemist François Sylvius.
