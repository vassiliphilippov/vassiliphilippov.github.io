soda

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊdə / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdə / (BrE)
A sodium salt of carbonic acid, commonly used in the manufacture of soap, glass, and paper; sodium carbonate.
sodium carbonate, used in industry

Examples

The factory produces large quantities of soda for use in making glass and paper.
Chemists use soda as a cleaning agent because of its alkalinity.
Soap manufacturers rely on soda to help break down fats during production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium carbonate - the chemical compound also known as soda, with formula Na2CO3

Etymology

From Italian 'soda', referring to a type of saltwort from which sodium carbonate was originally obtained.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊdə / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdə / (BrE)
A sweet, fizzy drink made with carbonated water and flavoring, often served chilled.
sweet carbonated soft drink

Examples

Would you like a soda with your meal?
He opened a can of soda and poured it over ice.
In some regions, people refer to soda as 'pop'.

Synset Examples

in New England they call sodas ‘tonics’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pop - another term for sweet carbonated soft drink
  • Synonym: soft drink - a non-alcoholic, usually carbonated drink

Etymology

From Italian soda (as in soda water), referring to sodium carbonate in the original formula.
