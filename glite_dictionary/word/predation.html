predation

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of hunting, killing, and eating another animal for food
the act of hunting and eating prey

Examples

The lion's predation on the antelope is a classic example of the food chain in action.
Many species have evolved defenses to protect themselves from predation.
Changes in the ecosystem led to a decrease in predation on smaller mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predator - an animal that hunts and eats other animals
  • Antonym: herbivory - eating plants (by animals)

Etymology

From Latin praedatio (a plundering, robbing), from praedari (to plunder).

2. NOUN

/ prɛˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of attacking and robbing or plundering; the practice of pillaging or marauding.
the act of plundering or pillaging

Examples

The predation of the coastal villages left many families homeless and afraid.
Centuries ago, predation by roving bands shaped the history of entire regions.
The company's aggressive takeovers were seen as a kind of corporate predation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunder - to steal goods, typically using force and in a time of disorder
  • Synonym: pillaging - the act of robbing a place using violence, especially in wartime
  • Antonym: peace - freedom from disturbance; absence of war or violence

Etymology

From Latin praedatio (plundering), from praedari (to plunder).
