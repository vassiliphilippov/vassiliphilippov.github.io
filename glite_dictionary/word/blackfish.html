blackfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæk.fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈblæk.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a female salmon that has recently spawned and is often in poor condition
female salmon after spawning

Examples

Local fishermen avoid catching blackfish because their quality is considered poor after spawning.
The riverbanks were lined with blackfish resting after their exhausting journey up the stream.
Biologists monitor the health of blackfish to study post-spawning salmon populations.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈblæk.fɪʃ / (BrE)
A large, dark-colored food fish found along the Atlantic coast of North America; especially the tautog or related species.
a large dark Atlantic food fish

Examples

Fishermen along the coast often catch blackfish for their firm, white flesh.
The restaurant’s special tonight features freshly grilled blackfish served with seasonal vegetables.
Marine biologists are studying the population decline of blackfish in the region.

3. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈblakˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A small, dark-colored whale found along the Atlantic coast of the United States, with the largest male often acting as the leader of the group.
small dark-colored whale of the Atlantic coast of the US

Examples

The pod of blackfish surfaced near the shore, drawing the attention of local fishermen.
Marine biologists observed the leading male blackfish guiding the rest of the school.
During the expedition, several blackfish were spotted swimming close together.
