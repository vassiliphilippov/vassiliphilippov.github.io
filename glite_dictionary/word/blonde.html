blonde

1. NOUN

/ blɑnd / (AmE), / blɒnd / (BrE)
a light shade of yellowish or nearly white color, typically referring to hair or certain types of wood or materials
a light yellowish or nearly white color

Examples

She chose to dye her hair a pale blonde for summer.
The cabinet was made from a smooth blonde wood.
We selected a rug with subtle blonde tones to brighten the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blond - same color as 'blonde', alternate spelling

Etymology

From French 'blond' (fair, light-colored), from Old French 'blont', likely of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ blɑːnd / (AmE), / blɒnd / (BrE)
A woman or girl with fair or light-coloured hair, often with fair skin.
woman or girl with fair hair

Examples

She has always admired how confident a blonde looks in the summer sunlight.
The novel features a mysterious blonde who moves into the house next door.
At the party, a tall blonde introduced herself to everyone with a smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blond - a man or boy with fair hair

Etymology

From French 'blonde' (fair-haired woman), feminine of 'blond'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-85758093-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ blɑnd / (AmE), / blɒnd / (BrE)
(of a person) having light-colored hair and often light skin and blue or grey eyes.
having light hair and often fair skin

Examples

Several of the children in the photograph are blonde and have striking blue eyes.
Her blonde hair shone brightly in the summer sunlight.
Many blonde people also have fair skin and light-colored eyes.

Synset Examples

blond Scandinavians
a house full of light-haired children

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brunette - having dark brown or black hair

Etymology

From French *blond* (light-colored, fair), of Germanic origin.
