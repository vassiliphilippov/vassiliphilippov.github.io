Neohygrophorus

1. NOUN

/ niː.oʊ.haɪˈɡrɒfərəs / (AmE), / niː.əʊ.haɪˈɡrɒfərəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Hygrophoraceae, which includes gilled mushrooms typically found near snowbanks.
a genus of gilled fungi

Examples

The genus Neohygrophorus is known for its association with melting snow in mountainous regions.
Researchers recently described a new species within the Neohygrophorus genus.
Specimens of Neohygrophorus are often collected by mycologists studying alpine fungi.

Etymology

From Greek neos (new) + Hygrophorus (a related fungal genus).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnioʊˌhaɪɡrəˈfɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˌhaɪɡrəˈfɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Hygrophoraceae, known for its gilled mushrooms, with Neohygrophorus group species often found in snowbank environments.
genus of gilled snowbank fungi

Examples

Several species within the Neohygrophorus genus are adapted to snowy montane environments.
Scientists recently described a new member of Neohygrophorus from the Rocky Mountains.
Unlike other fungi, Neohygrophorus typically fruits near receding snowbanks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hygrophoraceae - the family of fungi to which Neohygrophorus belongs

Etymology

From Greek 'neo-' (new) + 'Hygrophorus' (a related fungal genus).
