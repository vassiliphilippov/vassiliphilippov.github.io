Myrciaria

1. NOUN

/ mɪrˈsiːəriə / (AmE), / mɪəˈsɪərɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees and shrubs in the myrtle family, which includes plants such as jaboticaba.
genus of tropical American myrtle family plants

Examples

The fruit of the Myrciaria tree, known as jaboticaba, is popular in Brazil.
Researchers studied the growth habits of various Myrciaria species in tropical climates.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Myrciaria genus.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek myría (countless, abundant), referencing the myrtle family.

2. NOUN

/ mɜːrˌsiˈeɪriə / (AmE), / mɜːˌsɪˈɑːriə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees and shrubs of the myrtle family, known for species such as jabuticaba.
a genus of tropical American myrtle trees

Examples

The Myrciaria genus includes several fruit-bearing trees native to South America.
Researchers have discovered new medicinal properties in the bark of Myrciaria species.
The jabuticaba, a popular fruit in Brazil, comes from the Myrciaria genus.

Etymology

From modern Latin, formed from Greek 'myrti-' (myrtle) + '-aria' (denoting connection).
