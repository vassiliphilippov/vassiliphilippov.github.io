Solea lascaris

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈliː.ə læˈskɛrɪs / (AmE), / səʊˈliː.ə læˈskærɪs / (BrE)
A species of small flatfish, commonly known as the sand sole, found in European coastal waters.
species of small European flatfish

Examples

The Solea lascaris is valued for its delicate flavor in Mediterranean cuisine.
Marine biologists conducted a survey to track populations of the Solea lascaris along European coastlines.
Fishermen often catch Solea lascaris using small trawl nets near sandy sea floors.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Solea and the species name lascaris.
