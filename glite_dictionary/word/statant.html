statant

1. ADJ

/ ˈsteɪtənt / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtənt / (BrE)
(of an animal, especially in heraldry) standing on all four feet.
standing on all four feet (heraldic term)

Examples

In the family crest, the lion is depicted statant with its tail curved over its back.
The heraldic description specifies a wolf statant beneath a tree.
Several medieval shields show hounds statant as a symbol of loyalty.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rampant - (of an animal in heraldry) rearing with forepaws in the air

Etymology

From Latin 'stare' (to stand) via French 'statant'.
