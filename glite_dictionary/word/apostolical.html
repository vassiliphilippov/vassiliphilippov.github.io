apostolical

1. ADJ

/ əˌpɑːstəˈlɪkəl / (AmE), / əˌpɒstəˈlɪkəl / (BrE)
of or relating to the Apostles or their teachings
relating to the Apostles or their teachings

Examples

The bishop advocated for a return to apostolical traditions within the church.
Her thesis examined the influence of apostolical doctrine on early Christian communities.
The ceremony was conducted according to apostolical custom, following ancient rites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apostolic - of or relating to the Apostles or their teachings; alternative form

Etymology

From Late Latin apostolicus, from Greek apostolikos, from apostolos (apostle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02870589-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ əˌpɑːstəˈlɪkəl / (AmE), / əˌpɒstəˈlɪkəl / (BrE)
of, relating to, or proceeding from a pope or the papacy as regarded as the successor of the Apostles; papal.
relating to a pope or the papacy

Examples

The new decree was issued under apostolical authority.
He claimed apostolical succession as the basis for his office.
The council debated measures within their apostolical powers.

Synset Examples

papal dispensation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: papal - having to do with a pope or the papacy

Etymology

From Late Latin apostolicus, from Greek apostolikos (of or pertaining to an apostle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02890942-a
Synset POS: a
