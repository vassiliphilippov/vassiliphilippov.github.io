crotch

1. NOUN

/ krɑtʃ / (AmE), / krɒtʃ / (BrE)
the area where two branches or limbs meet and form an angle, especially in a tree
area where two branches meet

Examples

He found a comfortable spot to sit in the crotch of the old oak tree.
A bird nestled in the crotch formed by two thick branches above our roof.
Rainwater pooled in the crotch of the tree after the storm.

Synset Examples

they took the south fork
he climbed into the crotch of a tree

/ krɑtʃ / (AmE), / krɒtʃ / (BrE)

Examples

Sweat had soaked through the crotch of his shorts after the run.

3. NOUN

/ krɑtʃ / (AmE), / krɒtʃ / (BrE)
the area at the front of the human body where the inner sides of the thighs meet the trunk
area between thighs and trunk

Examples

She had a tear in her jeans at the crotch after climbing the fence.
The toddler scratched his crotch and giggled innocently.
Make sure the seam at the crotch is comfortable in your new trousers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groin - the fold or hollow on either side of the body where the thigh joins the abdomen

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse 'krokr' (hook).
