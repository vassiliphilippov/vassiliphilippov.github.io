to re-examine

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.ɪgˈzæm.ɪn / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪgˈzæm.ɪn / (BrE)
In law, to question a witness again after cross-examination, typically by the original examining counsel.
to question again in court after cross-examination
legal

Examples

The attorney decided to re-examine the witness to clarify her previous statement.
After cross-examination, the defense lawyer was permitted to re-examine his client.
The court granted permission for the prosecution to re-examine the key witness.

Synset Examples

re-examine one's witness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to cross-examine - to question a witness called by the opposing party

Etymology

Formed from the prefix re- (again) + examine.
