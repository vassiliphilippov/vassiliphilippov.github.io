retentively

1. ADV

/ rɪˈtɛntɪvli / (AmE), / rɪˈtɛntɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner that is able to retain or remember things easily.
in a way that retains or remembers easily

Examples

She listens retentively to every lecture, ensuring she absorbs all the key points.
Children often absorb information retentively when the subject interests them.
He studied vocabulary retentively, making it easier to recall words during the test.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retentive - having the ability to retain or remember things

Etymology

From 'retentive' + '-ly.' 'Retentive' from Latin retentivus (holding back).
