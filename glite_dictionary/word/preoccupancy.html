preoccupancy

1. NOUN

/ priˈɑk.jə.pən.si / (AmE), / priˈɒk.jʊ.pən.si / (BrE)
the state of being mentally absorbed or engrossed in something; the condition of having one's attention dominated by a particular thought or concern
state of being mentally absorbed

Examples

Her constant preoccupancy with work left little time for family or friends.
The preoccupancy of his mind with the problem made him seem distant to others.
During meditation, one seeks to let go of the preoccupancy with daily worries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preoccupation - the state of being engrossed or absorbed in thought
  • Antonym: distraction - lack of focus or attention

Etymology

From Latin 'praeoccupare' (to seize beforehand) + English suffix '-ancy', forming a noun of state.

2. NOUN

/ priˈɑkjəpənsi / (AmE), / priˈɒkjəpənsi / (BrE)
the act of taking possession or occupying a place before someone else does
taking possession beforehand

Examples

The early preoccupancy of the land gave the settlers legal rights over later arrivals.
They disputed the preoccupancy of the building after both groups claimed to be first.
She secured preoccupancy of the apartment by signing the lease before anyone else.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: occupancy - the act of taking or holding possession of a property

Etymology

From Latin 'praeoccupare' (to seize before).
