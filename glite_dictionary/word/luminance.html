luminance

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːmɪnəns / (AmE), / ˈluːmɪnəns / (BrE)
the measurable brightness of a surface, especially as perceived by the human eye or as quantified in physics and photography
measurable brightness of a surface

Examples

The luminance of the screen can be adjusted to suit different lighting conditions.
In photography, proper luminance is crucial for capturing clear and detailed images.
Scientists use special instruments to measure the luminance of various materials.

Synset Examples

its luminosity is measured relative to that of our sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brightness - the quality of being bright, used generally or subjectively
  • Synonym: luminosity - the intrinsic brightness of a celestial body

Etymology

From Latin 'luminare' (to illuminate), influenced by 'luminous.'
