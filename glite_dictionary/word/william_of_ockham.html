William of Ockham

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm əv ˈɑkəm / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm əv ˈɒkəm / (BrE)
(1285–1349) English scholastic philosopher and theologian, assumed author of Occam's Razor, noted for his work on nominalism and logic.
English scholastic philosopher and author of Occam's Razor

Examples

William of Ockham is best known for formulating the principle commonly called Occam's Razor.
Many students study the writings of William of Ockham in philosophy courses.
The influence of William of Ockham can still be seen in modern discussions of logic and science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Occam's Razor - the principle that the simplest explanation is usually correct
