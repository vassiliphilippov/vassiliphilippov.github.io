ingestion

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒɛs.tʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛs.tʃən / (BrE)
the process of taking food, drink, or another substance into the body through the mouth
taking food or substance into the mouth
medical

Examples

Proper ingestion of nutrients is essential for good health.
Accidental ingestion of chemicals can be dangerous and require immediate medical attention.
The first step in digestion is the ingestion of food through the mouth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: digestion - the process of breaking down food in the body
  • Degree Higher: absorption - the process in which nutrients are taken up by the body after digestion

Etymology

From Latin *ingestio* (a carrying in), from *ingerere* (to put in, carry in).
