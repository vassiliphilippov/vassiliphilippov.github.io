Arabia

1. PROPN

/ əˈreɪ.bi.ə / (AmE), / əˈreɪ.bi.ə / (BrE)
a large peninsula in Southwest Asia between the Red Sea and the Persian Gulf, known for its deserts and oil resources; also known as the Arabian Peninsula.
peninsula in Southwest Asia

Examples

The vast deserts of Arabia have long been the subject of legends and stories.
Oil exports from Arabia play a crucial role in the global economy.
During ancient times, caravans crossed Arabia to trade spices and incense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arabian Peninsula - the peninsula largely synonymous with Arabia
  • Degree Lower: Arabian Desert - the principal desert in Arabia

Etymology

From Latin Arabia, from Ancient Greek Arábia, referring to the region occupied by Arabs.
