gingery

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɪn.dʒɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪn.dʒ.ər.i / (BrE)
(especially of hair or fur) having a bright orange-brown color.
bright orange-brown in color

Examples

Her gingery hair glowed in the afternoon sunlight.
They adopted a gingery kitten from the animal shelter.
His beard was thick and gingery, matching the color of autumn leaves.

Synset Examples

a man with gingery hair and bright blue eyes
a ginger kitten

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ginger - bright orange-brown color, especially for hair

Etymology

From 'ginger' + '-y', meaning 'resembling ginger in color.'

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɪn.dʒɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪn.dʒər.i / (BrE)
Having a flavor or smell like that of ginger; tasting or smelling of ginger.
tasting or smelling of ginger

Examples

The sauce has a pleasantly gingery flavor that complements the fish perfectly.
She sipped a gingery tea to soothe her sore throat.
They baked gingery cookies for the holiday party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ginger - a pungent aromatic spice made from the rhizome of a plant, used in cooking and medicine

Etymology

Formed from 'ginger' + '-y', denoting having the quality or characteristic of ginger.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
