cured

1. ADJ

/ kjʊrd / (AmE), / kjʊəd / (BrE)
(of rubber or similar materials) treated by a chemical or physical process to improve its properties, such as hardness, strength, odor, or elasticity.
rubber processed to improve properties

Examples

The cured rubber sheets are used to manufacture tires for cars and trucks.
Certain adhesives contain cured compounds to enhance their strength and flexibility.
A cured rubber sole lasts much longer than an untreated one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulcanized - (of rubber) treated with sulfur or other agents to improve durability and elasticity

2. ADJ

/ kjʊrd / (AmE), / kjʊəd / (BrE)
(of concrete or mortar) kept moist to allow proper hardening during the setting process.
concrete/mortar kept moist to harden

Examples

The freshly poured concrete must be cured for several days to reach maximum strength.
Properly cured mortar will resist cracking and weathering over time.
If the surface is not cured correctly, the concrete may dry too quickly and become weak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set - of cement, concrete, etc.: to become hard or solid

5. ADJ

/ kjʊrd / (AmE), / kjʊəd / (BrE)
(of food, especially meat or fish) preserved by salting, drying, smoking, or brining.
preserved by salting, drying, or brining

Examples

The deli offers a wide selection of cured meats, including ham and salami.
She prefers cured salmon on her bagel rather than fresh fish.
Many traditional sausages are cured with a special blend of spices and salt.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fresh - not preserved by salting, drying, smoking, or brining

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'cure', from Old French 'curer' (to take care of), from Latin 'curare'.
