cockade

1. NOUN

/ kɑˈkeɪd / (AmE), / kɒˈkeɪd / (BrE)
an ornament, such as a knot of ribbon or a rosette, usually worn on a hat as a badge or decoration
ornamental badge worn on a hat

Examples

The soldier wore a cockade on his hat to signify his rank.
During the parade, each participant sported a bright blue cockade.
The history museum displayed a collection of military cockades from the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosette - an ornament shaped like a rose, used as a badge

Etymology

From French 'cocarde', probably from Provençal 'cocarda', deriving from 'coq' (rooster), symbolizing bravery or distinction.
