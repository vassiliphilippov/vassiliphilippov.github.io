oddly

1. ADV

/ ˈɑdli / (AmE), / ˈɒdli / (BrE)
In a strange or unusual manner.
in a strange or unusual manner

Examples

He behaved oddly at the meeting, making everyone feel uncomfortable.
The clock was oddly silent, though everything else in the house seemed normal.
I found the painting oddly beautiful, despite its unusual colors and shapes.

Synset Examples

a queerly inscribed sheet of paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangely - in a strange or surprising way
  • Antonym: normally - in a normal or usual way

Etymology

From 'odd' + '-ly', where 'odd' derives from Old Norse 'oddi' (point, triangle, third or additional one).

2. ADV

/ ˈɑdli / (AmE), / ˈɒdli / (BrE)
In a way that is strange, unusual, or unexpected.
in an unusual or unexpected way

Examples

She answered oddly, leaving everyone puzzled by her response.
The room was oddly quiet despite the crowded event.
I found his behavior oddly familiar, though we had never met before.

Synset Examples

had a curiously husky voice
he's behaving rather peculiarly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangely - in a strange or unexpected way
  • Synonym: peculiarly - in a manner that is unusual or different

Etymology

From 'odd' + '-ly', formed in English.
