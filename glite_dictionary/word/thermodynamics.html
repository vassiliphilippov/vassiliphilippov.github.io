thermodynamics

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɜːrmoʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics that deals with the relationships and conversions between different forms of energy, especially heat and mechanical work
physics: study of energy conversions

Examples

He is studying thermodynamics for his engineering degree.
Thermodynamics explains how energy is transferred in engines.
Understanding thermodynamics is essential for chemical engineers.

Etymology

From Greek thermē (heat) and dynamis (power, force).
