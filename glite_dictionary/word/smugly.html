smugly

1. ADV

/ ˈsmʌɡli / (AmE), / ˈsmʌɡli / (BrE)
In a way that shows excessive satisfaction or self-importance.
in a self-satisfied manner

Examples

She smiled smugly after answering all the questions correctly.
He smugly told us that he'd already finished his project days ago.
The coach watched smugly as his team took the lead.

Synset Examples

the bureaucrats explained smugly that the facts provided by their own experts show no cause for concern

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smug - having or showing an excessive pride in oneself
