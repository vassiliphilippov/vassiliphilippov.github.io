undershrub

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌʃrʌb / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌʃrʌb / (BrE)
a small or low-growing shrub, especially one that does not reach the height of a typical shrub
a small, low-growing shrub

Examples

Many wildflowers grow beneath the shelter of a dense undershrub canopy.
The botanist identified the small plant as an undershrub native to the region.
Unlike tall bushes, an undershrub rarely grows above one meter in height.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: shrub - a woody plant smaller than a tree with several main stems

Etymology

From under- (beneath, lower) + shrub (bushy plant), indicating a shrub of lower height.
