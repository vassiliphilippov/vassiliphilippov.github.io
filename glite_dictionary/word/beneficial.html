beneficial

1. ADJ

/ ˌbɛnəˈfɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˌbɛnɪˈfɪʃəl / (BrE)
promoting or resulting in good; having a helpful or advantageous effect
producing good or advantageous effects

Examples

Regular exercise has many beneficial effects on both physical and mental health.
The new policy changes will be beneficial to small business owners.
Eating a balanced diet is highly beneficial for children as they grow.

Synset Examples

an arms limitation agreement beneficial to all countries
the beneficial effects of a temperate climate
the experience was good for her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advantageous - giving an advantage or favorable outcome
  • Antonym: harmful - causing damage or injury

Etymology

From Latin 'beneficialis' (favorable, generous), from 'beneficium' (benefit).
