autopilot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.t̬oʊˌpaɪ.lət / (AmE), / ˈɔː.təʊˌpaɪ.lət / (BrE)
a device that automatically controls the course of an aircraft, ship, or spacecraft without constant human intervention
automatic navigational control device

Examples

The new jet is equipped with an advanced autopilot that can handle takeoff and landing.
During the long flight, the captain relied on the autopilot to maintain altitude and direction.
Sailors activated the autopilot so the ship could hold its course overnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automatic pilot - another term for autopilot

Etymology

From 'auto-' (self) and 'pilot', coined in early 20th-century aviation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.t̬oʊˌpaɪ.lət / (AmE), / ˈɔː.təʊˌpaɪ.lət / (BrE)
A mental state in which actions are carried out automatically and without conscious thought, typically due to habit or lack of attention.
doing things unconsciously or automatically

Examples

During his long commute, he often drives on autopilot and hardly remembers the journey.
She completed the routine tasks on autopilot, barely aware of what she was doing.
After so many years in the job, he operated on autopilot most days.

Synset Examples

she went about her chores on automatic pilot
too much of the writing seems to have been done on automatic pilot
she talked and he dozed and my mind went on autopilot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automatic pilot - Cognitive state of performing actions automatically, synonym for this sense of 'autopilot'
  • Antonym: consciousness - The state of being aware of and responsive to one's surroundings, antonym for this sense

Etymology

Originally an aviation term for an automatic flight control system, later extended metaphorically to describe mental states.
