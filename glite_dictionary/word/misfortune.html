misfortune

1. NOUN

/ mɪsˈfɔɹ.tʃən / (AmE), / mɪsˈfɔː.tʃən / (BrE)
bad luck; an unfortunate condition or event resulting from unfavorable outcomes
bad luck; unlucky state or event

Examples

She faced great misfortune after losing her job and her home in the same month.
Many legends tell of heroes who overcome misfortune through courage and determination.
After a series of misfortunes, he remained optimistic about the future.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fortune - good luck or chance; opposite of misfortune
  • Synonym: luck - chance events, especially as they affect someone

Etymology

From Old French 'mesfortune', from 'mes-' (mis-) + 'fortune' (luck, fate).

2. NOUN

/ mɪsˈfɔrtʃən / (AmE), / mɪsˈfɔːtʃuːn / (BrE)
an unfortunate or distressing event; bad luck or trouble that happens unexpectedly and unnecessarily
bad luck; an unfortunate event

Examples

Losing his job was a great misfortune for him and his family.
She suffered the misfortune of having her luggage lost during the trip.
Through every misfortune, he managed to remain optimistic and determined.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fortune - good luck or success
  • Synonym: bad luck - lack of success or prosperity due to chance

Etymology

From Old French *mesfortune* (bad luck), from *mes-* (mis-) + *fortune* (fate, luck).
