eudiometer

1. NOUN

/ juːdiˈɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / juːdɪˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
A graduated glass tube used for measuring changes in gas volume during chemical reactions.
instrument for measuring gas volume changes

Examples

The chemist used a eudiometer to monitor the reaction between hydrogen and oxygen gases.
A eudiometer can help determine the composition of an unknown gas sample.
Careful reading of the eudiometer scale is important for accurate experimental results.

Etymology

From Greek 'eudia' (fair weather) and 'metron' (measure), referring to early use in assessing air quality.
