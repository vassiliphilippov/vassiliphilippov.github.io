Euglenophyta

1. NOUN

/ juːˌɡliːnəˈfaɪtə / (AmE), / juːˌɡliːnəˈfʌɪtə / (BrE)
A division of mostly single-celled aquatic algae that move by means of flagella, commonly known as euglenoids.
a division of single-celled flagellate algae
medical

Examples

The microscope revealed several Euglenophyta cells swimming rapidly in the water sample.
Researchers study Euglenophyta to better understand photosynthetic mechanisms in single-celled organisms.
Some species within Euglenophyta can survive in polluted water environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Euglena' (a genus of these algae) + Greek 'phyton' (plant).
