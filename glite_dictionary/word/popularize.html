to popularize

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑːpjələˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊləˌraɪz / (BrE)
to make an idea, subject, or activity easy to understand or widely accepted by the general public
make widely known or accessible

Examples

The scientist worked to popularize complex physics theories for a broader audience.
Her efforts helped popularize yoga in Western countries.
The documentary series aimed to popularize marine biology among young students.

Synset Examples

Carl Sagan popularized cosmology in his books

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to popularise - British spelling of 'popularize'

Etymology

From French 'populariser', from 'populaire' (popular).

WordNet ID: oewn-00579585-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈpɑːpjələˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊləˌraɪz / (BrE)
to make something widely popular or accepted; to present an idea or product in a way that appeals to many people
make widely popular or accepted

Examples

The documentary helped popularize the science of astronomy among children.
She worked hard to popularize the sport in rural areas.
New technology can sometimes take years to popularize and become part of everyday life.

Synset Examples

They popularized coffee in Washington State
Relativity Theory was vulgarized by these authors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vulgarize - make something common or accessible to the general public

Etymology

From popular + -ize, meaning to make popular.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00972247-v
Synset POS: v
