pluralism

1. NOUN

/ ˈplʊr.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈplʊə.rəˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The doctrine that reality is composed of multiple distinct substances or elements, rather than being reducible to a single principle or substance.
philosophy: reality made up of many elements

Examples

In metaphysics, pluralism asserts that the world is constituted by many different substances.
The philosopher defended pluralism against the idea that reality can be explained by a single principle.
Debates between pluralism and monism have shaped philosophical discussions for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monism - the philosophical view that reality is ultimately composed of one substance or principle

Etymology

From Latin pluralis (more than one) + -ism.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplʊr.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈplʊərəˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
The practice, especially in the Christian Church, of one person holding more than one benefice or church office at the same time.
holding more than one church benefice

Examples

In the Middle Ages, pluralism was a common issue, with clergy holding multiple church positions.
The Council attempted to curb pluralism by enforcing stricter rules about church appointments.
He was accused of pluralism after accepting two benefices in neighboring parishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefice - a permanent church appointment, typically involving property and income

Etymology

From Medieval Latin pluralismus, from Latin pluralis (‘of or belonging to more than one’).

3. NOUN

/ ˈplʊr.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈplʊə.rə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
the condition or system in which different racial, religious, ethnic, or cultural groups are accepted and maintained within a society
acceptance of multiple groups in society

Examples

Modern democracies are often characterized by pluralism, allowing many different communities to flourish.
The university prides itself on its commitment to cultural pluralism and open dialogue.
Some critics argue that pluralism can lead to social fragmentation if not balanced with shared values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multiculturalism - the presence and co-existence of diverse cultures within a society

Etymology

From Latin 'pluralis' (more than one) + English suffix '-ism'.
