to overvalue

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈvælju / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈvæljuː / (BrE)
to assign too high a value to something; to estimate or rate something as worth more than it really is
assign too high a value

Examples

Many investors tend to overvalue new technology companies when the market is booming.
She realized she had overvalued the significance of the awards.
It is easy to overvalue possessions and forget what truly matters in life.

Synset Examples

You are overestimating the value of your old car

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to undervalue - to assign too low a value to something
  • Synonym: overestimate - to estimate something as being greater than it actually is

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + value (from Latin valere, to be worth).
