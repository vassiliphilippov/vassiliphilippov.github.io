eluate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛljuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛljuːeɪt / (BrE)
a liquid solution obtained after a substance has been eluted, especially during a chemical separation process
liquid from an elution process

Examples

After passing through the column, the eluate was collected for further analysis.
The scientist tested the eluate to check for the presence of impurities.
Each fraction of eluate was labeled and stored for examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eluent - a solvent used to carry out elution

Etymology

From Latin 'eluere' (to wash out), via German 'Eluat'.
