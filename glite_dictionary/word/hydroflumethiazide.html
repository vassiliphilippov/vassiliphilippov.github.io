hydroflumethiazide

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊˌfluːməˈθaɪəzaɪd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊˌfluːməˈθaɪəzaɪd / (BrE)
A synthetic diuretic drug used to treat high blood pressure and fluid retention (edema).
diuretic medication for hypertension and edema
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed hydroflumethiazide to help control her high blood pressure.
Patients taking hydroflumethiazide should have their electrolyte levels monitored regularly.
Hydroflumethiazide is often used to reduce swelling caused by fluid retention.

Etymology

From hydro- (water), flu- (fluorine), meth- (methyl group), thiazide (a class of diuretics).
