lignum vitae

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪɡnəm ˈvaɪti, -ˈvaɪtaɪ / (AmE), / ˈlɪɡnəm ˈvaɪti, -ˈvaɪtaɪ / (BrE)
A small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America, known for its extremely hard wood and medicinal resin.
tropical tree with very hard wood

Examples

The propeller shaft was made from lignum vitae due to its strength and resistance to water.
Early doctors valued lignum vitae for the medicinal resin it produced.
The dense lignum vitae wood is still highly sought after for specialized uses.

Etymology

From Latin 'lignum vitae' meaning 'wood of life', referring to its reputed medicinal properties.
