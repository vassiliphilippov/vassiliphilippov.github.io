John D. Rockefeller

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒɑn ˌdi ˌrɑkəˈfɛlər / (AmE), / ˌdʒɒn ˌdi ˌrɒkəˈfɛlə / (BrE)
(1839–1937) An American industrialist and philanthropist who founded the Standard Oil Company and became one of the world's wealthiest individuals, noted for his charitable giving.
American oil magnate and philanthropist (1839–1937)

Examples

John D. Rockefeller was instrumental in shaping the American oil industry in the late 19th century.
Many museums and universities today still benefit from the philanthropic work of John D. Rockefeller.
People often cite John D. Rockefeller as the richest man in modern history.
