Gladiolus

1. NOUN

/ ɡlædˈiːələs / (AmE), / ˌɡlæd.iˈəʊ.ləs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the iris family, known for their tall spikes of brightly colored flowers, commonly called gladiolas.
genus of flowering plants (gladiolas)

Examples

She planted several Gladiolus bulbs along the garden path for a burst of summer color.
The vibrant blooms of the Gladiolus made it a favorite among flower arrangers.
My grandmother always grew Gladiolus because she loved their tall, elegant spikes of flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gladiola - common name for plants of the genus Gladiolus

Etymology

From Latin 'gladius' (sword), referring to the sword-shaped leaves.
