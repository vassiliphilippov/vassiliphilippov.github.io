gladiolus

1. NOUN

/ ɡlædˈioʊləs / (AmE), / ɡlædɪˈəʊləs / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Gladiolus, chiefly native to Africa, with sword-shaped leaves and tall spikes of brightly colored funnel-shaped flowers, widely cultivated as an ornamental.
flowering plant with sword-shaped leaves

Examples

The vibrant gladiolus decorated the garden with their colorful blooms each summer.
She arranged a bouquet of pink gladioli for the wedding ceremony.
The florist recommends planting gladiolus bulbs in spring for a spectacular display.

Etymology

From Latin 'gladiolus' (little sword), diminutive of 'gladius' (sword), referring to the plant’s sword-shaped leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ɡlædˈioʊləs / (AmE), / ɡlædɪˈəʊləs / (BrE)
the broad, central part of the breastbone (sternum), located between the manubrium above and the xiphoid process below
central part of the breastbone
medical

Examples

The surgeon made an incision just below the gladiolus to access the heart.
A fracture of the gladiolus is rare but may occur with severe chest trauma.
The sternum is divided into the manubrium, the gladiolus, and the xiphoid process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sternum - the breastbone, the long flat bone in the center of the chest

Etymology

From Latin 'gladiolus', meaning 'little sword', due to its shape.
