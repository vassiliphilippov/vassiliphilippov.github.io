supple

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌp.əl / (AmE), / ˈsʌp.əl / (BrE)
able to bend and move easily and gracefully; flexible and limber.
flexible; bending and moving with ease

Examples

The dancer's supple movements captivated the entire audience.
She wore a jacket made of supple leather that fit perfectly.
Yoga helps keep your body supple and strong.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexible - able to bend easily without breaking
  • Antonym: rigid - not able to bend or be changed; stiff

Etymology

From Old French *souple*, from Latin *supplex* (submissive, suppliant).

3. ADJ

/ ˈsʌp.əl / (AmE), / ˈsʌp.əl / (BrE)
(of a body or part of the body) able to move and bend easily and smoothly.
capable of bending and moving freely

Examples

The dancer's supple body allowed her to perform the most challenging moves with grace.
Yoga has made his muscles more supple over the years.
She moved with a supple elegance that captured everyone's attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexible - able to bend easily without breaking
  • Antonym: stiff - not easily bent or changed in shape

Etymology

From Old French *souple* (pliant, flexible), from Latin *supplex* (submissive, bending under).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01026401-s

4. VERB

/ ˈsʌp.əl / (AmE), / ˈsʌp.əl / (BrE)
to make something soft, pliant, or flexible, especially by use or treatment
make pliant or flexible

Examples

Daily conditioning will supple the leather straps and prevent cracking.
Constant use has supplied these saddles until they are soft and comfortable.
Oils can help to supple the fabric, making it easier to shape.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to soften - to make or become soft or softer
  • Antonym: to stiffen - to make or become stiff or rigid

Etymology

From Latin 'supplex' (submissive, soft, pliant).
