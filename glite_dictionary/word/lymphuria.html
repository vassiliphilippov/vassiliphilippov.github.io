lymphuria

1. NOUN

/ lɪmˈfjʊriə / (AmE), / lɪmˈfjʊəriə / (BrE)
The presence of lymph in the urine, usually indicating an abnormal connection between the lymphatic system and urinary tract.
lymph in the urine (medical)
medical

Examples

The diagnosis of lymphuria was confirmed after a milky appearance was observed in the patient's urine.
In rare cases, lymphuria may be a symptom of underlying parasitic infection causing lymphatic obstruction.
Laboratory analysis showed elevated triglyceride levels in the urine, consistent with lymphuria.

Etymology

From 'lymph' + Greek 'ouria' (urine).
