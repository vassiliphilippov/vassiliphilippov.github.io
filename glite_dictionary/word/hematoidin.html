hematoidin

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˈtɔɪdɪn / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈtɔɪdɪn / (BrE)
an orange-yellow pigment derived from hemoglobin, found in bile and sometimes deposited in tissues, especially under pathological conditions such as jaundice
orange-yellow pigment from hemoglobin in bile
medical

Examples

The accumulation of hematoidin can indicate certain liver disorders.
Researchers observed deposits of hematoidin in the damaged tissue.
Excess hematoidin in the blood is often associated with jaundice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bilirubin - a yellow compound that occurs in the normal catabolic pathway that breaks down heme in vertebrates

Etymology

From hemato- (relating to blood) + -idin (chemical suffix).
