lexicographer

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛksɪˈkɑːɡrəfər / (AmE), / ˌlɛksɪˈkɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
a person who compiles, writes, or edits dictionaries
dictionary compiler or writer

Examples

The lexicographer spent years researching word origins for her new dictionary.
Samuel Johnson is perhaps the most famous English lexicographer.
A good lexicographer must pay careful attention to usage and meaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lexicography - the practice of compiling dictionaries

Etymology

From Greek lexikographos, from lexis (word) and -graphos (writer).
