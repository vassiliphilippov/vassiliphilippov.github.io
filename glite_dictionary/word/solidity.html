solidity

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɪdədi / (AmE), / səˈlɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being solid, especially with respect to its firmness, density, or consistency
state or quality of being solid

Examples

The solidity of the ice made it safe to walk across the pond.
Scientists measured the solidity of the substance to determine its density.
Her sculpture impressed everyone with the solidity and strength of its materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firmness - the quality of being firm or stable
  • Antonym: softness - the quality of being soft or yielding

Etymology

From Latin soliditas, from solidus (solid).

3. NOUN

/ səˈlɪdəti / (AmE), / səˈlɪdəti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being solid, with the interior filled with matter and not hollow.
state of being solid or not hollow

Examples

The solidity of the marble block made it perfect for sculpture.
Engineers tested the solidity of the bridge’s supports before opening it to traffic.
Unlike the hollow statue, the new one had solidity throughout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solidity - firmness or resistance to pressure
  • Antonym: hollowness - the state of being hollow or empty inside

Etymology

From Latin soliditas, from solidus (solid).
