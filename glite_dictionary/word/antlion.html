antlion

1. NOUN

/ ˈæntˌlaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈæntˌlaɪ.ən / (BrE)
an insect whose larva digs conical pits in sandy soil to trap ants and other small insects; the adult resembles a dragonfly
predatory insect larva; adult resembles dragonfly

Examples

The antlion larva lays traps for ants by digging small, sandy pits.
We watched an adult antlion resting on a blade of grass, its wings delicate and long.
Children often marvel at the ingenious hunting technique of the antlion in sandy playgrounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doodlebug - another name for the larva of an antlion

Etymology

From 'ant' + 'lion', referring to the larva's predation on ants.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæntˌlaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈæntˌlaɪ.ən / (BrE)
the larva of certain insects of the family Myrmeleontidae, known for digging conical pits to trap ants and other prey.
larva of insects that traps ants

Examples

The antlion digs a funnel-shaped trap in the sand to catch its prey.
Children watched the antlion patiently at work near the edge of the playground.
Scientists study the unique hunting strategy of the antlion larva.

Etymology

From 'ant' + 'lion', referring to the larva's predatory nature.

3. NOUN

/ ˈæntˌlaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈæntˌlaɪ.ən / (BrE)
A winged insect that resembles a dragonfly; its larvae, known as doodlebugs, dig conical pits in sandy soil to trap ants and other insects.
insect whose larvae trap ants in pits

Examples

The sandy soil in my backyard is full of antlion pits waiting for unsuspecting insects.
Children love to watch antlion larvae build their trap-like homes in the dust.
An adult antlion looks much like a delicate dragonfly hovering over the grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doodlebug - larval stage of antlion

Etymology

From 'ant' + 'lion', referring to the larvae's predatory behavior toward ants.
