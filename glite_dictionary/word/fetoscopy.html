fetoscopy

1. NOUN

/ fəˈtɑːskəpi / (AmE), / fiːˈtɒskəpi / (BrE)
A prenatal diagnostic procedure that involves direct observation of a fetus in the uterus and enables the withdrawal of fetal blood.
prenatal observation of a fetus
medical

Examples

Fetoscopy is used in complicated pregnancies to directly assess fetal health and development.
Advances in fetoscopy have improved the diagnosis of certain genetic conditions before birth.
During the fetoscopy, the doctor was able to obtain a small sample of fetal blood for testing.

Etymology

From 'fetus' + Greek 'skopein' (to look or examine).
