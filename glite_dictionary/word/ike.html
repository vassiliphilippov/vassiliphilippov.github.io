Ike

1. PROPN

/ aɪk / (AmE), / aɪk / (BrE)
Nickname for Dwight D. Eisenhower (1890–1969), United States general and 34th President.
nickname for Dwight D. Eisenhower

Examples

Ike became a household name during World War II for his role as Supreme Allied Commander.
Many Americans admired Ike for his calm leadership and honest demeanor.
The 'I Like Ike' campaign slogan became famous during the 1952 presidential election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eisenhower - Dwight D. Eisenhower, 34th U.S. President and WWII general

Etymology

Shortened form of Eisenhower's first name, used as a nickname.
