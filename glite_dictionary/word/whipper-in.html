whipper-in

1. NOUN

/ ˌwɪp.ɚˈɪn / (AmE), / ˌwɪp.ərˈɪn / (BrE)
a person who assists the huntsman by keeping the hounds together during a hunt, especially in traditional fox hunting
assistant to a huntsman managing hounds

Examples

The whipper-in rode swiftly to round up the stray hounds during the hunt.
As a whipper-in, her main job was to assist the huntsman and keep the dogs in order.
Traditionally, the whipper-in uses a whip to help control the pack.

Etymology

From 'whip' and 'in', referring to the role of whipping hounds back into the pack.
