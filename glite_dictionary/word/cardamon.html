cardamon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrdəmən / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdəmən / (BrE)
an aromatic spice made from the seeds of certain plants, used to flavor foods such as curries, baked goods, and drinks
aromatic spice used in cooking

Examples

Indian chai often includes cardamon to give it a rich, aromatic flavor.
She sprinkled ground cardamon into the cookie dough for a unique twist.
Roasted vegetables seasoned with cardamon offer a warm and distinctive taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardamom - alternative spelling for the same aromatic spice

Etymology

From Latin cardamomum, from Greek kardamōmon, referring to the spice plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑr.dəˌmɒn / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.də.mən / (BrE)
a tropical plant of India with aromatic seeds used as a seasoning; the seeds themselves used as a spice
aromatic Indian plant; its seeds as a spice

Examples

Traditional Indian desserts often include cardamon to enhance their flavor.
She sprinkled a pinch of cardamon into her morning coffee for a fragrant twist.
The market stall sold fresh spices including cinnamon, cloves, and cardamon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardamom - alternative spelling of cardamon, aromatic spice plant

Etymology

From Latin 'cardamomum', from Greek 'kardamōmon', from 'kardamon' (cress) + 'amomon' (an Indian spice plant).
