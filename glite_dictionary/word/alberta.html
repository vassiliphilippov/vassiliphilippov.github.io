Alberta

1. PROPN

/ ælˈbɝːtə / (AmE), / ælˈbɜːtə / (BrE)
A province in western Canada, known for its prairies and rich reserves of oil, natural gas, and minerals.
province in western Canada

Examples

Many tourists visit Alberta to experience the stunning scenery of the Canadian Rockies.
The oil industry is a major part of Alberta's economy.
Winters in Alberta can be harsh, with heavy snowfall and freezing temperatures.

Etymology

Named after Princess Louise Caroline Alberta, daughter of Queen Victoria.
