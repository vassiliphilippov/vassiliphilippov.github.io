trapeze

1. NOUN

/ trəˈpiz / (AmE), / trəˈpiːz / (BrE)
a horizontal bar hanging by two ropes, used by acrobats for swinging and performing tricks, especially in a circus
swing used by circus acrobats

Examples

The daring acrobat leapt from one trapeze to another high above the circus ring.
She trained for months before performing her first solo routine on the trapeze.
The children stared in awe as the performer spun gracefully on the swinging trapeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swing - a seat suspended by ropes for swinging

Etymology

From French 'trapèze', from Latin 'trapezium', from Ancient Greek 'trapézion' (table, little table).
