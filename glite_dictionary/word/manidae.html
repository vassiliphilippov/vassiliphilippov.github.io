Manidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈmænɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of mammals comprising all species of pangolins, characterized by protective keratin scales covering their skin; coextensive with the order Pholidota.
family of mammals including all pangolins

Examples

All living species of pangolins are classified within the family Manidae.
The evolutionary history of Manidae is the subject of ongoing zoological research.
Members of Manidae are distinguished by their overlapping scales and insectivorous diet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pangolin - an armored insect-eating mammal

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Manidae', family name based on 'Manis', the type genus, coined by Linnaeus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈmanɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of mammals comprising all pangolins, coextensive with the order Pholidota.
the mammal family of all pangolins

Examples

All species of pangolins belong to the family Manidae.
The classification of Manidae places it within the order Pholidota.
Research on Manidae has helped conservationists understand threats facing pangolins globally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pangolin - scaly anteater of the family Manidae

Etymology

From Modern Latin Manidae, from genus name Manis, given by Linnaeus.
