Sphenodon

1. NOUN

/ sfɪˈnoʊdɑn / (AmE), / sfɪˈnəʊdɒn / (BrE)
A genus of reptiles comprising the tuatara, native to New Zealand and the only living representative of the order Rhynchocephalia.
genus of reptiles: tuatara

Examples

The Sphenodon is the sole surviving member of an ancient group of reptiles.
Scientists studied the Sphenodon to learn more about its unique evolutionary history.
The conservation of Sphenodon is crucial due to its limited distribution in New Zealand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuatara - A reptile native to New Zealand, the only surviving member of the genus Sphenodon

Etymology

From Greek sphēn (wedge) + odous, odontos (tooth), referring to the shape of its teeth.
