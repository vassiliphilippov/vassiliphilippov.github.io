intersection

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.t̬ɚˈsɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈsɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
a point or set of points that are shared by two or more geometric figures, lines, or surfaces
shared point or region of geometric figures

Examples

The intersection of those two circles forms a lens-shaped area.
If two lines are parallel, their intersection does not exist.
In geometry, we often calculate the intersection of different planes.

Etymology

From Latin intersectio (a cutting between), from intersecare (to cut between).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.t̬ɚˈsɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈsɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
a place where two or more streets or roads cross each other
a place where roads cross

Examples

The traffic lights at the intersection turned red, causing all cars to stop.
There was a small café on the corner of the busy intersection.
Pedestrians should always be careful when crossing an intersection in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junction - a place where roads or railway lines meet or cross
  • Synonym: crossroads - a place where two roads meet and cross each other

Etymology

From Latin intersectio (a cutting between), from inter- (between) and secare (to cut).
