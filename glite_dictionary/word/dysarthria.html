dysarthria

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɑrθriə / (AmE), / dɪsˈɑːθriə / (BrE)
A speech disorder characterized by impaired articulation due to weakness or damage in the muscles or nerves involved in speaking.
speech disorder affecting articulation
medical

Examples

After his stroke, his speech therapist noted that he had developed dysarthria.
The neurologist explained that dysarthria was affecting John's ability to pronounce words clearly.
Parkinson's disease can sometimes result in dysarthria, making communication more difficult.

Etymology

From Greek 'dys-' (bad, difficult) + 'arthron' (joint, articulation).
