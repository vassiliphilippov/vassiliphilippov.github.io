Golgotha

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡɑlɡəθə / (AmE), / ˈɡɒlɡəθə / (BrE)
the hill outside Jerusalem where Jesus was crucified, according to the New Testament; also called Calvary.
hill near Jerusalem where Jesus was crucified

Examples

Many pilgrims visit Golgotha each year to reflect on its religious significance.
The story of the crucifixion places Jesus on Golgotha, a site known as the 'place of the skull.'
The paintings depicted the sorrowful scene at Golgotha with dramatic intensity.

Etymology

From Latin, via Greek *Golgotha*, from Aramaic *gulgultā* (place of the skull).
