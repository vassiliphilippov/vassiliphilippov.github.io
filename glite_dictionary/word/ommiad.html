Ommiad

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːm.i.æd / (AmE), / ˈɒm.i.æd / (BrE)
a member of the first dynasty of Arab caliphs, which had its capital in Damascus and ruled from AD 661 to 750.
member of the first Arab caliph dynasty in Damascus

Examples

The rise of the Ommiad dynasty marked a significant shift in Islamic history.
Many architectural achievements are attributed to the Ommiad caliphs in Damascus.
Historians debate the legacy left by the Ommiads during their rule.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Umayyad - member or relating to the Umayyad dynasty, alternative spelling

Etymology

From Arabic 'Umayyad', relating to Umayya, ancestor of the dynasty.
