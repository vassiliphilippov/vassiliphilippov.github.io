peasecod

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːsˌkɑd / (AmE), / ˈpiːzˌkɒd / (BrE)
the pod or husk of a pea, sometimes edible in certain varieties of garden peas
pod or husk of a pea

Examples

She picked a fresh peasecod from the vine and popped it open to eat the peas inside.
In some dishes, the entire peasecod is cooked and served as a vegetable.
During the tour of the garden, we learned that not all peasecods are edible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea pod - the pod containing the seeds of the pea plant

Etymology

From Middle English 'pesecod', formed from 'pease' (pea) + 'cod' (pod, husk).
