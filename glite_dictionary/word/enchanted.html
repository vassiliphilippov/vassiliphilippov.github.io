enchanted

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtʃæntɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈtʃɑːntɪd / (BrE)
under the influence of magic or charms; having a magical or delightful quality
under magical influence

Examples

The enchanted forest glowed with an otherworldly light as night fell.
She looked enchanted by the music, lost in its magic.
Visitors often feel as if time stands still in the enchanted gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bewitched - under a spell; enchanted by magic
  • Antonym: cursed - affected by a curse; under evil magic

Etymology

From Old French 'enchanter', from Latin 'incantare' (to chant upon, cast a spell).
