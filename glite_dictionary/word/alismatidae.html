Alismatidae

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɪs.məˈtaɪ.diː / (AmE), / əˌlɪs.məˈtiː.diː / (BrE)
a subclass of monocotyledonous plants, comprising about 500 species of aquatic and semiaquatic herbs in 14 families
subclass of aquatic monocots

Examples

The family Hydrocharitaceae is included in the Alismatidae subclass by most botanists.
Aquatic environments provide ideal conditions for many members of Alismatidae.
Recent genetic studies have clarified the relationships within the Alismatidae.

Etymology

From genus name Alisma (type genus of the family Alismataceae) + Latin suffix -idae (indicating a taxonomic group).

2. NOUN

/ əˌlɪs.məˈtaɪ.diː / (AmE), / əˌlɪs.məˈtiː.diː / (BrE)
a botanical subclass or superorder of monocotyledonous plants, including about 500 species of aquatic and semiaquatic herbs in 14 families
subclass of aquatic monocotyledonous plants

Examples

The Alismatidae contain several important genera of aquatic plants used in wetland restoration.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the Alismatidae in South American marshes.
Many members of the Alismatidae subclass thrive in shallow freshwater habitats.

Etymology

From botanical Latin, derived from the genus name Alisma (water-plantain) and the suffix -idae (denoting a group).
