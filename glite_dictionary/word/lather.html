lather

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæðər / (AmE), / ˈlɑːðə / (BrE)
Foam or froth produced by excessive sweating, especially on the skin of a horse or other animal.
foam from sweating, esp. on a horse

Examples

After a long ride, the horse was covered in lather and breathing heavily.
The jockey wiped away the lather from the horse's neck before leading it to water.
Working in the heat, the oxen were soon in a lather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: froth - bubbles or foam, especially on liquids or sweat
  • Synonym: perspiration - sweating; the process of sweating

Etymology

From Old English *lēather*, related to 'foam' or 'froth'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlæðɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɑːðə / (BrE)
a mass of tiny bubbles formed by soap, detergent, or similar cleaning substances when mixed with water
froth produced by soap or detergent

Examples

She worked the soap into a thick lather before washing her hands.
The barber covered his client's face with a creamy lather before shaving him.
The car gleamed as the sponge created a rich lather on its surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foam - a mass of small bubbles formed on or in liquid, often by agitation
  • Synonym: scum - a layer of froth or dirt on the surface of a liquid

Etymology

From Old English *lēaþor*, meaning 'foam or froth', later influenced by Middle English.
