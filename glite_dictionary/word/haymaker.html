haymaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈheɪˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a machine used on farms to turn, spread, or condition hay to promote faster and more even drying
machine for drying hay faster

Examples

The old haymaker in the barn is still used every summer for drying alfalfa.
After mowing the field, they used a haymaker to speed up the drying process.
The farm recently invested in a modern haymaker to improve their hay production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tedder - a machine that turns and spreads hay to aid drying

2. NOUN

/ ˈheɪˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈheɪˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a powerful punch intended to knock out an opponent in boxing or a fight
a strong knockout punch

Examples

The boxer landed a devastating haymaker in the final round to win the match.
She avoided his wild haymaker with a quick step to the side.
He swung a haymaker, hoping for a quick knockout, but missed completely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knockout - a blow that renders an opponent unconscious in boxing

Etymology

Originally referred to someone who makes hay; the boxing sense compares the wide, swinging motion to that of swinging a scythe when making hay.
