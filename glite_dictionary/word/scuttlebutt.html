scuttlebutt

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌt̬lˌbʌt / (AmE), / ˈskʌt.əl.bʌt / (BrE)
rumor or gossip, especially information that is not confirmed and may be spread informally
rumor; gossip

Examples

There's a lot of scuttlebutt going around about who will be promoted next.
According to the latest scuttlebutt, the company might be merging with a rival.
She tried not to listen to the scuttlebutt at the office, but it was hard to avoid.

Synset Examples

the divorce caused much gossip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - casual or unconstrained conversation about others, often involving rumors
  • Synonym: rumor - unverified information spread informally
  • Antonym: fact - something known or proved to be true

Etymology

Originally nautical slang: from 'scuttle butt', a cask with a hole in it for dispensing drinking water, where sailors exchanged gossip.
