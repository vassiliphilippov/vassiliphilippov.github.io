Megalobatrachus maximus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəloʊbəˈtrækəs ˈmæksɪməs / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡələʊbəˈtrækəs ˈmæksɪməs / (BrE)
a large edible salamander native to Asia, reaching lengths of over three feet
large Asian edible salamander species

Examples

The Megalobatrachus maximus inhabits rivers and streams in East Asia, where it is known for its impressive size.
Scientists are concerned about the declining population of the Megalobatrachus maximus due to habitat loss.
Some cultures prize the Megalobatrachus maximus both as a delicacy and for traditional medicinal uses.

Etymology

From Greek megalo- (large) + batrakhos (frog) and Latin maximus (greatest).
