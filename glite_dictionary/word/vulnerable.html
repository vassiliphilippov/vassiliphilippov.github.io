vulnerable

1. ADJ

/ ˈvʌlnərəb(ə)l / (AmE), / ˈvʌln(ə)rəb(ə)l / (BrE)
exposed to the possibility of being attacked or harmed, either physically or emotionally
susceptible to attack or harm

Examples

The old fortress was vulnerable to enemy attack from the sea.
Children are often vulnerable to colds and other illnesses.
The company’s outdated software made it vulnerable to cyber threats.

Synset Examples

a vulnerable bridge

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invulnerable - impossible to harm or damage

Etymology

From Latin vulnerabilis (wounding), from vulnus (wound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02533434-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈvʌlnərəbəl / (AmE), / ˈvʌlnərəbl / (BrE)
able to be easily harmed, physically or emotionally; open to attack or damage.
able to be easily harmed or attacked

Examples

Children are especially vulnerable to diseases during winter.
The castle's walls were thick, but its gate was vulnerable to attack.
She felt vulnerable sharing her story with the group.

Synset Examples

vulnerable parts of the body

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invulnerable - impossible to harm or damage
  • Synonym: defenseless - without protection; unable to defend oneself

Etymology

From Latin vulnerabilis (wounding), from vulnus (wound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02334251-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈvʌlnɚəbəl / (AmE), / ˈvʌln(ə)rəbl / (BrE)
Susceptible to being influenced, attacked, or persuaded, especially by criticism, temptation, or other external forces.
easily influenced or attacked

Examples

The committee's proposal was vulnerable to sharp criticism from experts in the field.
Young people are often vulnerable to peer pressure and quick to make impulsive decisions.
His theory seemed vulnerable to attack because it lacked supporting evidence.

Synset Examples

vulnerable to bribery
an argument vulnerable to refutation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: susceptible - likely to be influenced or harmed; easily affected
  • Antonym: resistant - not easily affected by something; able to withstand

Etymology

From Latin 'vulnerabilis', from 'vulnerare' (to wound), from 'vulnus' (wound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02371734-s
Synset POS: s
