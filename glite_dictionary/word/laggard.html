laggard

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæɡərd / (AmE), / ˈlæɡəd / (BrE)
a person or thing that makes slower progress than others and falls behind
someone who falls behind

Examples

The company can't afford to have a laggard slowing down the entire team.
As technology advances, laggard countries may struggle to keep up with global trends.
Teachers often give extra support to any laggard in the classroom to help them catch up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straggler - a person or thing that lags behind the main group
  • Antonym: leader - a person who leads or is ahead of others

Etymology

From late Middle English, probably from the verb 'lag' + -ard, a pejorative suffix of French origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlæɡərd / (AmE), / ˈlæɡəd / (BrE)
slower than desired or expected; tending to waste time or fall behind others
slow or tending to fall behind

Examples

Her laggard response made us miss the train.
The laggard team delayed the project's completion.
His laggard pace frustrated the rest of the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slow - not quick; lacking speed
  • Antonym: prompt - done without delay; immediate

Etymology

From lag (to fall behind) + -ard (pejorative agent suffix), patterned after French -ard.

Synset POS: s
