Ferdinand Magellan

1. PROPN

/ ˌfɜrdɪˌnænd məˈʤɛlən / (AmE), / ˌfɜːdɪˌnænd məˈdʒɛlən / (BrE)
(1480–1521) A Portuguese explorer who, serving Spain, led the first expedition to circumnavigate the globe.
Portuguese explorer; led first circumnavigation of Earth

Examples

Ferdinand Magellan's voyage proved that the Earth could be circumnavigated by sea.
Many historians credit Ferdinand Magellan with opening new trade routes to the East.
The Strait of Magellan, named after Ferdinand Magellan, is located at the southern tip of South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christopher Columbus - Italian explorer credited with discovering the Americas for Europe
  • Synonym: Vasco da Gama - Portuguese navigator who sailed around Africa to India

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Fernão de Magalhães', Latinized as 'Ferdinandus Magellanus'.
