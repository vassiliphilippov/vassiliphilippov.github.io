to suspire

1. VERB

/ səˈspaɪr / (AmE), / səˈspaɪə / (BrE)
to utter or emit a long, deep breath or sigh, often as a sign of relief, sadness, or longing
to sigh or breathe deeply
archaic

Examples

As the music faded, she could not help but suspire softly.
He suspires heavily before sharing the sad news.
The weary traveler suspired at the sight of his home.

Synset Examples

She sighed sadly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sigh - to emit a long, audible breath expressing emotion

Etymology

From Latin suspīrāre (to sigh), from sub- (up from below) + spīrāre (to breathe).
