Parascalops breweri

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈskeɪlɒps ˈbruːəri / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈskalɒps ˈbruːəri / (BrE)
A species of mole found in eastern North America, commonly known as the hairy-tailed mole.
a mole species of eastern North America

Examples

The Parascalops breweri digs extensive tunnels beneath forest floors in eastern North America.
Biologists studied the diet of Parascalops breweri to better understand local insect populations.
Unlike other moles, the Parascalops breweri has a noticeably hairy tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hairy-tailed mole - common name for Parascalops breweri
  • Degree Lower: mole - small burrowing mammal, family Talpidae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name Parascalops and species name breweri, honoring Dr. Thomas Mayo Brewer, an American naturalist.
