disjointedness

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈdʒɔɪntɪdnəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒɔɪntɪdnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of lacking clear connection, order, or coherence.
lack of order or coherence

Examples

The disjointedness of the report made it hard to follow the argument.
Some critics noted the disjointedness in the movie’s plot structure.
Her presentation was marked by a certain disjointedness that confused the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incoherence - lack of logical connection or clarity
  • Antonym: orderliness - the quality of being organized and systematic

Etymology

From 'disjointed' + '-ness', forming a noun from the adjective.
