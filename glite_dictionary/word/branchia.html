branchia

1. NOUN

/ ˈbræŋkiə / (AmE), / ˈbraŋkɪə / (BrE)
A gill; a respiratory organ of aquatic animals that enables them to breathe oxygen dissolved in water.
gill of aquatic animals
medical

Examples

Fish use their branchia to extract oxygen from water as they swim.
The biologist studied the branchia of various marine species to understand their adaptation to different environments.
Damage to the branchia can be fatal for many aquatic animals, as it impairs their ability to breathe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gill - an organ that allows aquatic animals to extract oxygen from water

Etymology

From Latin 'branchia', from Greek 'bránkhia' (gills).
