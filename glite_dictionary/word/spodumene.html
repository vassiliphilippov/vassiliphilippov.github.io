spodumene

1. NOUN

/ spoʊˈdjuːmiːn / (AmE), / spɒˈdjuːmiːn / (BrE)
A mineral consisting of lithium aluminum silicate, occurring in prismatic crystals and an important source of lithium.
lithium aluminum silicate mineral

Examples

Large deposits of spodumene are mined for the extraction of lithium.
The pale green color of this crystal indicates it is a variety of spodumene known as hiddenite.
Scientists analyzed the spodumene sample to determine its purity before processing.

Etymology

From French 'spodumène', from Greek 'spodoumenos', meaning 'reduced to ashes', referring to its ashy color when burnt.
