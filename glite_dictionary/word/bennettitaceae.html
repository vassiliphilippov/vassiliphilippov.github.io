Bennettitaceae

1. NOUN

/ bɛˌnɛtɪˈteɪsiː / (AmE), / bɛnˌɛtɪˈteɪsiː / (BrE)
an extinct family of seed plants (gymnosperms) known from fossils, especially from the Carboniferous period
extinct family of fossil gymnosperm plants

Examples

Fossils of the Bennettitaceae have been found in sedimentary rocks from the Carboniferous period.
The Bennettitaceae are important for understanding the evolution of gymnosperms.
Paleobotanists study members of the Bennettitaceae to learn about ancient plant life.

Etymology

From the genus name Bennettites (named after British botanist John Joseph Bennett) + the botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ bəˌnɛtəˈteɪsi.i / (AmE), / bɛˌnɛtɪˈteɪsɪ.iː / (BrE)
an extinct family of gymnospermous plants known from the Carboniferous period, identifiable by their fossil remains
extinct family of fossil gymnosperms

Examples

Fossils from the Bennettitaceae family provide insight into plant evolution during the Carboniferous period.
The Bennettitaceae are characterized by reproductive structures similar to those of modern cycads.
Paleobotanists have discovered new species within the Bennettitaceae in several coal mines.

Etymology

From the genus Bennettites, named after British botanist John Joseph Bennett, with standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
