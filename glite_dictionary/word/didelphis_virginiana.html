Didelphis virginiana

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈdɛlfɪs vɜːrdʒɪˈneɪnə / (AmE), / daɪˈdɛlfɪs vɜːdʒɪˈneɪnə / (BrE)
the Virginia opossum, an omnivorous marsupial native to eastern North America, known for feigning death when threatened
Virginia opossum species

Examples

The Didelphis virginiana is the only marsupial found north of Mexico.
When threatened, Didelphis virginiana often pretends to be dead, a behavior known as 'playing possum'.
Researchers observed a Didelphis virginiana foraging near the edge of the forest at dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opossum - any of various American marsupials with a prehensile tail

Etymology

From Latin: 'Didelphis' meaning 'double womb', and 'virginiana' referring to Virginia (region of discovery).
