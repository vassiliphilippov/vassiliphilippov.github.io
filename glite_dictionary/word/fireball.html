fireball

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌbɔːl / (BrE)
the brilliant, glowing center of a nuclear explosion, where extremely high temperatures and intense light are produced
center of a nuclear explosion

Examples

The fireball illuminated the sky for miles after the nuclear detonation.
Scientists studied the formation of the fireball to better understand atomic explosions.
The intense heat from the fireball caused widespread destruction in the surrounding area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blast wave - shock wave caused by an explosion

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌbɔːl / (BrE)
a very bright meteor that sometimes explodes as it passes through the atmosphere
bright meteor, sometimes exploding

Examples

The astronomers watched in awe as a fireball streaked across the night sky.
A massive fireball was briefly visible before it exploded over the desert.
Local residents reported seeing a bright fireball followed by a loud boom.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌbɑl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌbɔːl / (BrE)
a ball of fire, especially one caused by an explosion, lightning, or seen as a meteor in the sky
a ball of fire, often from an explosion or meteor

Examples

The explosion sent a massive fireball into the sky, lighting up the night.
A fireball streaked across the early morning sky, astonishing onlookers around the world.
Lightning split the tree and created a glowing fireball that flashed before vanishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meteor - a small body from outer space that enters the Earth's atmosphere and appears as a streak of light

4. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚˌbɑl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌbɔːl / (BrE)
A person who is extremely energetic, dynamic, and full of enthusiasm.
highly energetic person

Examples

She may be small, but she's a real fireball when it comes to getting things done.
The new manager entered the office like a fireball, bringing energy and excitement to the team.
You need a fireball like him to turn the company around.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dynamo - a person with a lot of energy and enthusiasm
  • Synonym: live wire - an energetic and unpredictable person

Etymology

Figurative use of the literal word 'fireball', referring to a ball of fire.
