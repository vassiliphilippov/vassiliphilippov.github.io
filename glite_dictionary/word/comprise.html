to comprise

1. VERB

/ kəmˈpraɪz / (AmE), / kəmˈprʌɪz / (BrE)
to consist of; to be made up of (something)
to be made up of

Examples

The committee comprises ten members from different departments.
This book comprises several short stories by contemporary authors.
Her responsibilities comprise overseeing the project and managing the team.

Synset Examples

The land he conquered comprised several provinces
What does this dish consist of?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to consist - to be composed or made up; have as its essential features
  • Synonym: to include - to contain, as part of a whole
  • Antonym: to exclude - to leave out or not include

Etymology

From French *comprendre* (to include), based on Latin *comprehendere* (to seize, include).

3. VERB

/ kəmˈpraɪz / (AmE), / kəmˈprʌɪz / (BrE)
to consist of or be made up of (parts, members, etc.); to include as a part or member
to consist of; include as parts

Examples

The committee will comprise ten experts from various industries.
The collection comprises rare manuscripts dating back to the 16th century.
Their research team is comprised of scientists from ten countries.

Synset Examples

This money is my only income
The stone wall was the backdrop for the performance
These constitute my entire belonging
The children made up the chorus
This sum represents my entire income for a year
These few men comprise his entire army

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to consist - to be composed or made up of
  • Synonym: to constitute - to make up or form (something)
  • Antonym: to exclude - to keep out; to not include as part of a whole

Etymology

From French *comprendre* (to include), from Latin *comprehendere* (to grasp, include).
