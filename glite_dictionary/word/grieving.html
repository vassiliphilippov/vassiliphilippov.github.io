grieving

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡriviŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡriːvɪŋ / (BrE)
feeling or showing deep sorrow, especially following a loss or death
sorrowful due to loss or death

Examples

She spent many sleepless nights as a grieving mother after the accident.
The grieving family gathered to support each other at the funeral.
His words brought comfort to the grieving community.

Synset Examples

bereft of hope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mourning - the act of expressing grief, especially after death
  • Synonym: bereaved - suffering the loss of a loved one
  • Antonym: joyful - feeling, expressing, or causing great pleasure and happiness

Etymology

From verb 'grieve' (Middle English, from Old French 'grever', from Latin 'gravare', to burden).
