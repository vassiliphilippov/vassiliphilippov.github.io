concession

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / kənˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
the act of conceding or yielding to a demand, argument, or opposing view
the act of conceding or yielding

Examples

After hours of negotiation, his concession allowed the talks to move forward.
Granting her opponent's point was seen as a major concession in the debate.
The company made a concession to employee demands regarding vacation time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yielding - the act of giving way or surrendering
  • Antonym: refusal - the act of declining or denying a request or demand

Etymology

From Latin concessio (a granting, permission), from concedere (to concede).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈsɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / kənˈsɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
something that is given up or yielded, especially during a negotiation or argument
something yielded in negotiation

Examples

The union managed to secure several important concessions from the management during the talks.
He made a concession to the opposition to ensure the bill was passed.
No major concession was offered, despite hours of negotiation.

Synset Examples

they won all the concessions they asked for

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compromise - an agreement reached by each side making concessions
  • Antonym: demand - something requested or insisted upon in negotiations
  • Degree Higher: surrender - the act of giving up something, especially in response to force

Etymology

From Latin concessio, from concedere (to grant, yield).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07231072-n

3. NOUN

/ kənˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / kənˈsɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a contract or official right to operate a business, especially within another organization's property or venue
right to run a business in a specific place

Examples

We secured the snack concession for the summer festival.
The stadium awarded the food and beverage concession to a local company.
His company lost the parking concession after the contract expired.

Synset Examples

he got the beer concession at the ball park

Related Terms

  • Synonym: franchise - a right or license granted to operate a business

Etymology

From Latin concessio (an allowing, granting), from concedere (to concede, grant).
