Turdus

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɝːdəs / (AmE), / ˈtɜːdəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Turdidae, comprising thrushes such as the song thrush and the blackbird.
type genus of thrushes (Turdidae)

Examples

The genus Turdus includes the common blackbird and several other familiar songbirds.
Many European thrushes belong to the Turdus genus.
Researchers studied migratory patterns in species of Turdus throughout the continent.

Etymology

From Latin turdus (thrush, a kind of bird).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.dəs / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.dəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds that includes the typical thrushes, such as the blackbird and the song thrush, in the family Turdidae.
a genus of thrushes (birds)

Examples

The European blackbird belongs to the genus Turdus.
Several species in the Turdus genus are renowned for their melodious songs.
Scientists recently discovered a new member of Turdus in South America.

Etymology

From Latin 'turdus' meaning 'thrush' (the bird).
