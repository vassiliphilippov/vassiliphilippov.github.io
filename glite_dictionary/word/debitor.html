debitor

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛb.ɪ.tɔr / (AmE), / ˈdɛb.ɪ.tɔː / (BrE)
a person who owes money or is obligated to pay a debt to another; a debtor
person who owes a debt
legal

Examples

The debitor failed to repay the amount by the agreed deadline.
In the contract, the rights and responsibilities of the debitor are clearly defined.
Lawyers advised the debitor on possible options for settling the outstanding debt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debtor - a person who owes money; a more common English synonym
  • Antonym: creditor - a person or entity to whom money is owed

Etymology

From Latin debitor (one who owes), from debere (to owe).
