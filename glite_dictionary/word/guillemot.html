guillemot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪlɪˌmɑt / (AmE), / ˈɡɪlɪmɒt / (BrE)
a small seabird of northern seas in the auk family, typically black and white or brown and speckled, that nests on cliffs.
a seabird of the auk family

Examples

Large colonies of guillemots nest on the rocky cliffs each summer.
We spotted a single guillemot diving for fish near the boat.
The black-and-white plumage helps the guillemot blend in with the waves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auk - a seabird of the family Alcidae

Etymology

From French 'guillemot', diminutive of 'Guillaume' (William), possibly given in reference to the bird’s call.
