Friesland

1. PROPN

/ ˈfriːz.lænd / (AmE), / ˈfriːz.lənd / (BrE)
a province in the northern part of the Netherlands, known for its unique language and culture
province in northern Netherlands

Examples

She spent her summer holiday exploring the lakes and small towns of Friesland.
Many traditional sports, such as skating, are popular in Friesland during the winter months.
The official language of Friesland is Frisian, alongside Dutch.

Etymology

From Dutch 'Friesland', meaning 'land of the Frisians'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈfriːz.lænd / (AmE), / ˈfriːz.lənd / (BrE)
a coastal region in northern Europe that forms the western part of the ancient region of Frisia; today, part of Friesland is a province in the northern Netherlands
region and province in the northern Netherlands

Examples

Many tourists visit Friesland to explore its lakes and unique Frisian culture.
The province of Friesland is known for its distinct language and traditions.
Friesland lies along the coast of the North Sea, offering beautiful scenery and historic towns.

Etymology

From Dutch 'Friesland' (land of the Frisians), from Old Frisian 'Fryslânde'.
