Menticirrhus americanus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛn.tɪˈsɪr.əs əˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛn.tɪˈsɪə.rəs əˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nəs / (BrE)
a species of whiting found along the southeastern coast of North America, valued as a food fish
species of whiting fish from southeast North America

Examples

Menticirrhus americanus is a popular catch among anglers along the South Carolina coast.
Many seafood restaurants serve dishes made with Menticirrhus americanus.
Fishermen often refer to Menticirrhus americanus as southern kingfish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiting - a common name for various species of fish, especially in the Atlantic

Etymology

From Latin, genus name 'Menticirrhus' and species epithet 'americanus' meaning 'of America'.
