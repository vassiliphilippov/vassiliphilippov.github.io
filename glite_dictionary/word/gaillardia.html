gaillardia

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪˈlɑrdiə / (AmE), / ɡeɪˈlɑːdɪə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Gaillardia, native to western America, with hairy leaves and long-stalked flowers in bright colors such as yellow, copper, and burgundy.
a bright-flowered plant of the genus Gaillardia

Examples

Many gardeners plant gaillardia for its ability to attract butterflies and its low maintenance requirements.
The vibrant blooms of the gaillardia brightened up the entire garden throughout the summer.
She chose a mix of gaillardias to add color to the borders of her yard.

Etymology

Named after M. Gaillard de Charentonneau, an 18th-century French botanist.
