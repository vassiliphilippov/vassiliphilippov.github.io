hominy

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːmɪni / (AmE), / ˈhɒmɪni / (BrE)
corn kernels that have had their hull and germ removed, typically by soaking in an alkali solution, commonly used in North American cooking
corn with hull and germ removed

Examples

In the South, hominy is often served as a side dish with butter and salt.
She made a traditional soup with hominy, pork, and chiles.
Hominy can be ground to make grits or masa for tortillas.

Etymology

From Powhatan (an Algonquian language) *uskatahomen* (parched corn).
