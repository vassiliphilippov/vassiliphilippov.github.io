piastre

1. NOUN

/ piˈæstər / (AmE), / piˈæstə / (BrE)
a monetary unit of Turkey equal to one hundredth of a lira; also used in other countries as a subdivision of currency
a currency unit equal to 1/100 lira (Turkey)

Examples

The shopkeeper gave me change in piastres when I bought the souvenirs.
In early twentieth-century Turkey, the piastre was commonly used for small purchases.
Tourists often confused Turkish piastres with similar coins from other countries.

Etymology

From Italian *piastra* (thin metal plate, coin), originally from Latin *plastra* (plate).

2. NOUN

/ piˈæstər / (AmE), / piˈastə / (BrE)
a monetary unit equal to one hundredth of the basic currency unit in Egypt, Lebanon, Sudan, and Syria
fractional currency unit in several countries

Examples

The price was listed as 50 pounds and 25 piastres on the receipt.
Old coins worth one piastre are still found in some markets in Lebanon.
You will need several piastres to make small purchases in Egypt.

Etymology

From French 'piastre', from Italian 'piastra' (metal plate, coin).
