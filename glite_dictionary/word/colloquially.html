colloquially

1. ADV

/ kəˈloʊ.kwi.əli / (AmE), / kəˈlɒk.wɪ.ə.li / (BrE)
in an informal or conversational way; using everyday or non-standard language
in informal language; conversationally

Examples

The disease is colloquially called the flu, though its formal name is influenza.
In Australia, McDonald's is colloquially known as "Macca's".
This vegetable is colloquially referred to as a 'spud' in some regions.

Synset Examples

this building is colloquially referred to as The Barn

Related Terms

  • Antonym: formally - in an official or conventional way

Etymology

From Latin colloquium (conversation) + English adverbial suffix -ly.
