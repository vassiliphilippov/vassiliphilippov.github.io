Capparis flexuosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæp.ə.rɪs flɛkˈsuː.ə.sə / (AmE), / ˌkæp.ər.ɪs flɛkˈsjʊə.sə / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to southern Florida, Central America, and South America, belonging to the caper family.
a shrub or small tree of the caper family

Examples

The Capparis flexuosa flourishes in the subtropical areas of southern Florida.
Botanists often study Capparis flexuosa for its unique adaptation to different climates.
Local wildlife relies on Capparis flexuosa as a source of shelter and food.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Capparis' is the genus name for capers, and 'flexuosa' means 'full of bends' or 'winding'.
