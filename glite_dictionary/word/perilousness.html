perilousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛr.ə.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpɛr.ɪ.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being very dangerous or full of risk.
the state of being dangerous

Examples

The perilousness of the journey discouraged many potential explorers from attempting it.
She underestimated the perilousness of swimming in rough seas at night.
The report highlights the perilousness of neglecting proper safety measures in construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dangerousness - the quality of being dangerous
  • Antonym: safety - the condition of being safe; freedom from danger

Etymology

From Middle English 'perilous' (dangerous) + '-ness' (noun suffix), from Old French 'peril' (danger).
