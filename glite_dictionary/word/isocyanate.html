isocyanate

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsoʊˈsaɪəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊˈsaɪəneɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester derived from isocyanic acid, commonly used in the manufacture of plastics and foams
salt or ester of isocyanic acid
medical

Examples

Polyurethanes are made by reacting polyols with isocyanate compounds.
Exposure to isocyanate vapors can cause respiratory problems in workers.
An isocyanate group is highly reactive, making it useful in industrial chemistry.

Etymology

From iso- (variant of) and cyanate, reflecting its chemical structure.
