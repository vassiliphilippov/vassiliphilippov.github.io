archbishopric

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrtʃˈbɪʃəpˌrɪk / (AmE), / ˌɑːtʃˈbɪʃəpˌrɪk / (BrE)
The district or see over which an archbishop has authority.
territory governed by an archbishop

Examples

The archbishopric of Canterbury is one of the most influential in the Anglican Church.
After his appointment, he traveled throughout his archbishopric to meet the clergy.
The boundaries of the archbishopric were defined in the eleventh century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diocese - the district under the pastoral care of a bishop
  • Synonym: archdiocese - the district under the jurisdiction of an archbishop

Etymology

From Old English arcebiscoprīċ (archbishop’s see), from archbishop +‎ -ric (realm, rule).
