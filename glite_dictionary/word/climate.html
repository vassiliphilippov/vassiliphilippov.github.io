climate

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪ.mət / (AmE), / ˈklaɪ.mət / (BrE)
the typical weather conditions of a region, averaged over a long period of time
average weather conditions of a region

Examples

The Mediterranean climate is ideal for growing olives and grapes.
Scientists are concerned about changes in global climate.
The harsh climate of the desert makes life there challenging.

Synset Examples

the dank climate of southern Wales
plants from a cold clime travel best in winter

Related Terms

  • Antonym: weather - the day-to-day state of the atmosphere

Etymology

From Greek klima (region, zone), via Latin climat- (slope, region).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪmət / (AmE), / ˈklaɪmət / (BrE)
The general feeling, mood, or attitudes that exist in a particular place or situation at a given time.
prevailing mood or atmosphere

Examples

The political climate has made it harder to reach agreement on important issues.
There has been a noticeable shift in the climate of public opinion regarding environmental matters.
A positive climate at work encourages collaboration and creativity among employees.

Synset Examples

the climate of opinion
the national mood had changed radically since the last election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atmosphere - the general mood or feeling in a place or situation
  • Synonym: mood - the prevailing emotional state or feeling

Etymology

From Greek 'klima' (region, zone), through Latin 'clima'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14548263-n
Synset POS: n
