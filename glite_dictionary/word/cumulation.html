cumulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkjuːmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkjuːmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a collection or accumulation of objects or things, especially when gathered or piled up together
collection or accumulation of things

Examples

The gradual cumulation of debris blocked the river.
His desk was covered with a cumulation of papers and books.
Over the years, a cumulation of old clothes filled the attic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accumulation - the process of collecting or gathering things over time

Etymology

From Latin 'cumulatio', from 'cumulare' meaning 'to heap up'.
