rum

1. NOUN

/ rʌm / (AmE), / rʌm / (BrE)
a strong alcoholic drink made by distilling fermented sugarcane juice or molasses, typically produced in the Caribbean and other sugar-producing regions
a distilled alcoholic drink from sugarcane or molasses

Examples

The bartender poured a generous measure of rum into the glass.
Many tropical cocktails use rum as the main ingredient.
He brought back a bottle of aged rum from his trip to Jamaica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whisky - a distilled alcoholic drink made from fermented grain mash
  • Synonym: vodka - a clear distilled alcoholic beverage of Russian origin

Etymology

From English, possibly shortened from 'rumbullion,' a 17th-century term for an uproar or tumult, later used as the name for the spirit.

3. NOUN

/ rʌm / (AmE), / rʌm / (BrE)
A card game in which players try to form sets and runs of cards; the winner is the first to meld all their cards.
card game forming sets and runs

Examples

We spent the afternoon playing rum with a deck of old cards.
His favorite game at family gatherings is rum.
After dinner, they dealt the cards and started a game of rum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rummy - A family of card games based on matching cards of the same rank or sequence

Etymology

Perhaps a shortening of 'rummy', itself of uncertain origin.
