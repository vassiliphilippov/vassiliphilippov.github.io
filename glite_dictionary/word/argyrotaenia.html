Argyrotaenia

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌdʒaɪrəˈtiːniə / (AmE), / ɑːˌdʒɪərəˈtiːniə / (BrE)
a genus of moths in the family Tortricidae, including the orange tortrix and related species
genus of moths (Tortricidae)

Examples

Several species of Argyrotaenia are known pests of fruit crops.
The orange tortrix, a member of the genus Argyrotaenia, can damage vineyards.
Scientists studied Argyrotaenia larvae to develop better pest management strategies.

Etymology

From Greek 'argyros' (silver) + 'tainia' (band or ribbon); refers to markings on the moths.

2. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌɡaɪrəˈtiːniə / (AmE), / ɑːˌɡaɪrəˈtiːniə / (BrE)
A genus of small moths in the family Tortricidae, which includes the orange tortrix and related species.
genus of tortrix moths

Examples

The Argyrotaenia genus comprises several important agricultural pests.
Scientists discovered a new species belonging to Argyrotaenia in the mountains of Peru.
The orange tortrix, a member of Argyrotaenia, can cause significant damage to crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tortrix - a type of moth in the family Tortricidae

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'argyros' (silver) and 'tainia' (band).
