supergiant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːpərˌdʒaɪənt / (AmE), / ˈsuːpədʒʌɪənt / (BrE)
an extremely luminous star with a very large diameter and low density, typically much larger than the Sun and often nearing the end of its life cycle
very large, bright star type

Examples

Betelgeuse is a well-known supergiant visible in the constellation Orion.
Astronomers believe that a supergiant can eventually explode as a supernova.
The mass of a red supergiant is many times greater than that of the sun.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: giant star - a large, bright star with a lower surface temperature than supergiants

Etymology

Formed from 'super-' (above, beyond) and 'giant', in astronomical context since early 20th century.
