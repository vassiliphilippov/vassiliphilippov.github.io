rarefied

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛrəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈreərɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(of air or a gas) having had some of its density reduced; thin or less dense than usual.
having low density, especially of air or gas

Examples

The hikers struggled to breathe in the rarefied air at the summit.
Astronomers study phenomena occurring in the rarefied gases of the upper atmosphere.
Jet engines must be designed to operate in the rarefied conditions found at high altitudes.

Synset Examples

rare gasses
lightheaded from the rarefied mountain air

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dense - having high density; closely compacted

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *rarefactus*, past participle of *rarefacere* (to make thin, rare).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛrɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈreərɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(of ideas, interests, or language) very lofty, elevated, or highly intellectual; intended for or understood by an elite or select group.
very elevated or intellectual

Examples

His ideas are so rarefied that only a handful of scholars truly comprehend them.
The discussion quickly moved into rarefied philosophical territory, far above everyday concerns.
She enjoyed the rarefied atmosphere of academic debate at the university.

Synset Examples

an exalted ideal
argue in terms of high-flown ideals
a noble and lofty concept
a grand purpose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lofty - very elevated in nature or style
  • Synonym: elevated - raised to a higher level, especially in moral or intellectual terms
  • Antonym: mundane - lacking interest or excitement; dull, ordinary

Etymology

From Latin 'rarefactus', past participle of 'rarefacere' (to make thin, rare).
