Trigonella foenumgraecum

1. NOUN

/ trɪgəˈnɛlə foʊˌnəmˈɡriːkəm / (AmE), / trɪgəˈnɛlə fəʊˌnəmˈɡriːkəm / (BrE)
an annual herb native to southern Europe and eastern Asia, with off-white flowers and aromatic seeds used in medicine and cooking, especially in curries
herb with aromatic seeds used in cooking and medicine

Examples

Trigonella foenumgraecum is valued for its seeds, which are an essential spice in many Indian dishes.
Researchers are studying how extracts of Trigonella foenumgraecum could benefit blood sugar control.
Traditional medicine has long used Trigonella foenumgraecum to aid digestion and reduce inflammation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fenugreek - common name for Trigonella foenumgraecum, an aromatic herb used in cooking and medicine

Etymology

From Latin trigonella (a kind of clover) and foenum graecum (Greek hay).
