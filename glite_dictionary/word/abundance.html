abundance

1. NOUN

/ əˈbʌn.dəns / (AmE), / əˈbʌn.dəns / (BrE)
A very large quantity of something; more than is needed.
a very large quantity or supply

Examples

Despite the drought, this year's harvest produced an abundance of fresh vegetables.
She was overwhelmed by the abundance of choices at the market.
During the holidays, there is an abundance of food and good cheer.

Synset Examples

an age of abundance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plenty - a large or sufficient amount or quantity; more than enough
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being scarce or in short supply; shortage

Etymology

From Latin abundantia (fullness, copiousness), from abundare (to overflow).

2. NOUN

/ əˈbʌndəns / (AmE), / əˈbʌndəns / (BrE)
(Chemistry) The ratio of the total mass of an element in the earth's crust to the total mass of the earth's crust, typically expressed as a percentage or in parts per million.
ratio of an element in earth's crust (chemistry)
medical

Examples

The abundance of silicon in the earth's crust is much higher than that of copper.
Scientists measure the abundance of various elements to understand the composition of minerals.
Oxygen has a high abundance in the earth's crust, making it one of its most common elements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentration - the amount of a substance in a given volume or mass
  • Antonym: scarcity - lack or shortage, the opposite of abundance

Etymology

From Latin abundantia (fullness, plenty), from abundare (to overflow).

3. NOUN

/ əˈbʌndəns / (AmE), / əˈbʌndəns / (BrE)
(Physics) The ratio of the number of atoms of a particular isotope of an element to the total number of atoms of all its isotopes.
ratio of atoms of an isotope to total isotopes
medical

Examples

The abundance of carbon-14 in a sample is used for radiocarbon dating.
Scientists measured the abundance of each isotope to determine the sample's origin.
Oxygen-16 has the highest natural abundance among oxygen isotopes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentration - the amount of a substance in a given volume or area

Etymology

From Latin abundantia (fullness, plenty).
