harquebus

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrk(w)ɪˌbəs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːkwɪbəs / (BrE)
an early firearm with a long barrel, used in the 15th to 17th centuries; now obsolete
obsolete long-barreled firearm
archaic

Examples

The soldiers lined up with their harquebus ready for the command to fire.
He carefully cleaned the old harquebus displayed on the mantelpiece.
During the reenactment, she demonstrated how to load a harquebus as it was done centuries ago.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: musket - a type of long-barreled firearm used after the harquebus

Etymology

From French *arquebuse*, from Old French *harquebuse* or *arquebuse*, from Dutch *haakbus* ('hook-gun').
