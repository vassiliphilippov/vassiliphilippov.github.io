troubled

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrʌbəld / (AmE), / ˈtrʌbəld / (BrE)
experiencing or showing distress, worry, or difficulties; affected by problems or conflict.
experiencing distress or difficulties

Examples

Her troubled expression showed that something was seriously bothering her.
Many troubled teens need additional support to cope with their challenges.
The region has been troubled by unrest for decades.

Synset Examples

troubled areas
fell into a troubled sleep
a troubled expression
troubled teenagers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distressed - suffering from anxiety, sorrow, or pain

Etymology

Formed from 'trouble' (Middle English, from Old French *troubler*) + '-ed' (past participle suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtrʌbəld / (AmE), / ˈtrʌbəld / (BrE)
(of a place, period, or situation) experiencing unrest, disorder, or disturbance.
experiencing unrest or disorder

Examples

The region has suffered for years from repeated violence and troubled relations between communities.
She grew up during troubled times marked by protests and civil unrest.
The report highlights several troubled neighborhoods that need urgent attention from the authorities.

Synset Examples

effects of the struggle will be violent and disruptive
riotous times
these troubled areas
the tumultuous years of his administration
a turbulent and unruly childhood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peaceful - free from disturbance; calm
  • Synonym: tumultuous - full of disorder or confusion
  • Synonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict or disorder

Etymology

From the verb 'trouble', from Old French 'troubler', from Vulgar Latin *turbulare (to disturb), from Latin 'turbare'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01930119-s
Synset POS: s
