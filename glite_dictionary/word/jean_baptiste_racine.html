Jean Baptiste Racine

1. PROPN

/ ʒɑ̃ bɑːˈpist ræˈsin / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ bæsˈtiːst ræˈsiːn / (BrE)
(1639–1699) French playwright and poet, renowned for his tragedies based on classical Greek and Roman themes and his association with Jansenism.
French tragedian and playwright (1639–1699)

Examples

Many of Jean Baptiste Racine's plays are considered masterpieces of French classical tragedy.
The influence of Jean Baptiste Racine can be seen in later European drama.
Students often study Jean Baptiste Racine when learning about 17th-century French literature.

Etymology

French personal name, after Jean Baptiste Racine.
