cleaver

1. NOUN

/ ˈkliːvər / (AmE), / ˈkliːvə / (BrE)
A heavy knife with a large, square blade, used by butchers for cutting meat and bone.
heavy, square-bladed butcher's knife

Examples

The butcher swung the cleaver down and split the bone with a single strike.
She used a heavy cleaver to chop vegetables for dinner.
The chef added a new cleaver to his collection of kitchen knives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knife - a cutting tool with a blade and handle

Etymology

From Old English *clēofan* (to split, cleave) + -er (agent suffix).
