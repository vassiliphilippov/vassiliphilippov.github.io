birthplace

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝθˌpleɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɜːθˌpleɪs / (BrE)
the place where a person was born
the place where someone was born

Examples

He traveled back to his birthplace to reconnect with his roots.
The museum was built on the birthplace of the famous writer.
Her birthplace is mentioned in her official biography.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hometown - the town where one was born or grew up

Etymology

Formed from 'birth' + 'place'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrθˌpleɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɜːθˌpleɪs / (BrE)
the place where something or someone originates or is first developed
place of origin or development

Examples

Athens is often called the birthplace of democracy.
This valley is recognized as the birthplace of the modern environmental movement.
She visited her grandfather's birthplace in Ireland last summer.

Synset Examples

the birthplace of civilization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: origin - the point or place where something begins
  • Synonym: cradle - the place where something begins or first flourishes

Etymology

Formed from 'birth' + 'place', attested in English since the 17th century.
