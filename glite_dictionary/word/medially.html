medially

1. ADV

/ ˈmiːdiəli / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiəli / (BrE)
in a medial position; in the middle or toward the center
in the middle or central position

Examples

The stress in this word falls medially, not at the beginning or end.
This letter appears medially in many English words.
In anatomy, the nerve runs medially to the artery.

Synset Examples

this consonant always occurs medially

Related Terms

  • Antonym: laterally - toward the side or away from the middle
  • Synonym: medial - relating to the middle; situated in the middle

Etymology

From 'medial' + '-ly'; 'medial' from Latin 'medialis' (of the middle).
