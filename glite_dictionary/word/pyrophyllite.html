pyrophyllite

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪrəˈfɪlaɪt / (AmE), / ˌpʌɪrəˈfɪlʌɪt / (BrE)
a soft white or greenish aluminum silicate mineral, typically occurring in foliated masses and resembling talc
a soft white or greenish mineral resembling talc

Examples

The ceramic industry often uses pyrophyllite as a raw material due to its thermal stability.
Geologists discovered a vein of pyrophyllite running through the ancient rock formation.
When ground into powder, pyrophyllite can serve as a filler in paints and plastics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talc - a soft, white, gray, or green mineral used in powder and as a lubricant

Etymology

From Greek 'pyro' (fire) and 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the mineral's leaf-like structure and its reaction to heat.
