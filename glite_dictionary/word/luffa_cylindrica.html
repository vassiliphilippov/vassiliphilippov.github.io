Luffa cylindrica

1. NOUN

/ ˈluf.ə sɪˈlɪn.drɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈluː.fə sɪˈlɪn.drɪ.kə / (BrE)
A tropical climbing plant of the gourd family, cultivated for its cylindrical fruit, which is used as a vegetable when young and as a sponge when mature.
tropical plant with cylindrical fruit used as loofah

Examples

The fruit of the Luffa cylindrica is often used as a natural scrubbing sponge in kitchens and bathrooms.
Gardeners in warm climates can easily grow Luffa cylindrica on trellises for both food and utility purposes.
Young Luffa cylindrica gourds are edible and commonly used in Asian cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loofah - the fibrous interior of certain gourds, used as a sponge

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name Luffa and Latin cylindrica (cylindrical).
