Berbery

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɜr.bə.ri / (AmE), / ˈbɜː.bəri / (BrE)
a historical region of northern Africa along the Mediterranean coast, known from the 16th to 19th centuries as a base for pirates
historic region in North Africa
archaic

Examples

The pirates of Berbery were notorious for attacking ships in the Mediterranean.
European powers launched expeditions to suppress piracy along the Berbery coast.
In the 17th century, many captives were sold into slavery in Berbery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Barbary - historical region in North Africa, variant spelling

Etymology

From French 'Barbarie,' ultimately from the name of the Berber people.
