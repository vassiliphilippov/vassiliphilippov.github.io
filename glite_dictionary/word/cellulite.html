cellulite

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.jəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.jʊˌlaɪt / (BrE)
lumpy or dimpled deposits of fat just beneath the skin, commonly found on the thighs, hips, and buttocks, especially in women.
lumpy subcutaneous body fat, especially on thighs, hips, etc.

Examples

Many people try creams and massages to reduce the appearance of cellulite on their thighs.
The magazine article discussed several myths about cellulite and its causes.
Wearing shorts made her feel self-conscious about the cellulite on her legs.

Etymology

From French *cellulite*, formed from 'cellule' (cell) and '-ite' (pertaining to), first used in the early 20th century.
