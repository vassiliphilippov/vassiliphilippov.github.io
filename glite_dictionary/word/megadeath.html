megadeath

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛɡəˌdɛθ / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡəˌdɛθ / (BrE)
the death of a million people, especially as a unit for measuring fatalities in a nuclear explosion or catastrophe
the death of a million people

Examples

The scientist explained the devastating potential of nuclear war using the term megadeath.
Military strategists assess the consequences of certain weapons in terms of megadeaths.
During the Cold War, the concept of megadeath was used to describe the possible loss of life from atomic bombs.

Synset Examples

they calibrate the effects of atom bombs in megadeaths

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: kilodeath - death of a thousand people

Etymology

Formed from mega- (meaning 'million') and death, popularized during the Cold War.
