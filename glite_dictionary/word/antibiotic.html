antibiotic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ti.baɪˈɑ.t̬ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.baɪˈɒ.tɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or containing antibiotics; having the property of inhibiting the growth of or destroying microorganisms.
relating to antibiotics or their effects
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antibiotic ointment to treat the infected wound.
Researchers are developing new antibiotic agents to combat resistant bacteria.
This antibiotic cream should be applied twice daily for best results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antimicrobial - Preventing the growth of microorganisms

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + biōtikos (fit for life), originally meaning 'opposed to life'; referring to substances that destroy microorganisms.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.baɪˈɑ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.baɪˈɒ.tɪk / (BrE)
a substance produced by microorganisms or synthesized, used to destroy or inhibit the growth of bacteria and cure bacterial infections
substance used to treat bacterial infections
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antibiotic to treat her ear infection.
Overuse of antibiotics can lead to resistant strains of bacteria.
Not all illnesses require an antibiotic; some are caused by viruses, not bacteria.

Synset Examples

when antibiotics were first discovered they were called wonder drugs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antimicrobial - an agent that kills or inhibits microorganisms, including bacteria, fungi, and viruses
  • Antonym: probiotic - a substance that promotes the growth of beneficial microorganisms, especially in the gut

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + biotikos (fit for life).
