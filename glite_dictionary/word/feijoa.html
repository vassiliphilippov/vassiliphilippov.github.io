feijoa

1. NOUN

/ feɪˈhoʊ.ə / (AmE), / feɪˈdʒəʊ.ə / (BrE)
A small, green, egg-shaped tropical fruit with sweet, aromatic white flesh, often used for jellies and preserves.
egg-shaped tropical fruit with white flesh

Examples

The jam was made from fresh feijoa picked from the garden.
She sliced a ripe feijoa and spooned out the fragrant flesh.
Feijoas are in season during late autumn and are popular in desserts.

Etymology

From Portuguese Feijoa, named after João da Silva Feijó, a Brazilian botanist.

2. NOUN

/ feɪˈʒoʊ.ə / (AmE), / feɪˈʒəʊ.ə / (BrE)
A small evergreen shrub native to South America, cultivated for its edible green, plum-shaped fruit with a sweet, aromatic flavor.
South American shrub and its edible fruit

Examples

The garden's feijoa bush produced dozens of fragrant green fruit this year.
She made a delicious jam from fresh feijoas picked in her backyard.
Have you ever tasted the sweet, tangy flavor of a ripe feijoa?

Etymology

From the genus name Feijoa, named after Portuguese naturalist João da Silva Feijó.
