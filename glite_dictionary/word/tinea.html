Tinea

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪniə / (AmE), / ˈtɪniə / (BrE)
a genus of small moths in the family Tineidae, commonly known as clothes moths, whose larvae feed on fabrics and other materials
genus of clothes moths

Examples

Tinea is a genus of moths whose larvae are known to damage wool and other fabrics.
Some museums employ preventive measures to protect textiles from Tinea infestations.
Scientists studied the life cycle of Tinea species to develop better pest control methods.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Tineidae - the family to which Tinea moths belong

Etymology

From Latin tinea (a moth or worm that eats clothes or books).
