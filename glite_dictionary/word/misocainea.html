misocainea

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsoʊˈkeɪniə / (AmE), / ˌmɪsəʊˈkeɪniə / (BrE)
Hatred or strong aversion to new ideas or change.
hatred of new ideas

Examples

Academic institutions sometimes struggle with misocainea whenever radical theories are proposed.
The company's downfall was hastened by a culture of misocainea among its leadership.
Overcoming misocainea is essential for fostering innovation and progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neophobia - fear of new things or experiences

Etymology

From Greek 'misos' (hatred) + 'kainos' (new).
