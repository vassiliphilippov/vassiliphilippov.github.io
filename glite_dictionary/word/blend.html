blend

1. NOUN

/ blɛnd / (AmE), / blɛnd / (BrE)
an act or result of mixing things thoroughly together to form a single substance or mixture
thorough mixing of substances

Examples

This coffee is a blend of beans from Colombia and Brazil.
The artist created a beautiful color blend by mixing different pigments.
The chef recommends a special spice blend for grilling meat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mixture - a substance made by mixing other substances together
  • Antonym: separation - the act or process of moving things apart

Etymology

From Old English blendan (to mix), related to Old Norse blanda (to mix).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ blɛnd / (AmE), / blɛnd / (BrE)
A word formed by merging parts of two or more other words and combining their meanings, such as 'brunch' from 'breakfast' and 'lunch'.
word formed from parts of others

Examples

The word 'smog' is a blend of 'smoke' and 'fog'.
'Motel' is an example of a blend combining 'motor' and 'hotel'.
Linguists study how blends are created and used in daily language.

Synset Examples

‘motel’ is a portmanteau word made by combining ‘motor’ and ‘hotel’
‘brunch’ is a well-known portmanteau

Related Terms

  • Synonym: portmanteau - A word formed by merging the sounds and meanings of two or more words

3. VERB

/ blɛnd / (AmE), / blɛnd / (BrE)
to combine or harmonize smoothly with something else; to mix so that individual elements are indistinguishable or work well together
combine or harmonize with something

Examples

The blue curtains blend perfectly with the walls in the living room.
She wanted her makeup to blend naturally with her skin tone.
The new wing of the building blends well with the original architecture.

Synset Examples

This flavor will blend with those in your dish
This sofa won't go with the chairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mix - to combine two or more substances so that they become a single substance; synonym of this sense of 'blend'
  • Antonym: clash - fail to harmonize; antonym in the context of colors, styles, or flavors

Etymology

From Middle English 'blenden', from Old Norse 'blanda' (to mix).
