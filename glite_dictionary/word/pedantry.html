pedantry

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdəntri / (AmE), / ˈpɛdəntri / (BrE)
An excessive or inappropriate concern with minor details and rules, especially in displaying one's learning.
excessive concern with minor details or rules

Examples

Her constant pedantry during meetings made discussions tedious and unproductive.
The professor's pedantry was evident when he corrected every minor grammatical error.
Many found his attention to footnotes to be pure pedantry rather than genuine scholarship.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: precision - focus on accuracy and exactness
  • Degree Higher: erudition - deep, extensive learning
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or learning

Etymology

From French 'pédanterie', from 'pédant' (schoolmaster), probably via Italian 'pedante'.
