Albizia saman

1. NOUN

/ ælˈbɪziə səˈmæn / (AmE), / ælˈbɪziə səˈmæn / (BrE)
A large tropical American tree with feathery bipinnate leaves and clusters of flowers with crimson stamens; its seed pods are eaten by cattle. Also known as the rain tree.
large tropical American tree, also called rain tree

Examples

The Albizia saman is often planted along roadsides for its impressive canopy and shade.
Farmers appreciate the Albizia saman because its seed pods provide nutrition for cattle.
The park’s centerpiece was a massive Albizia saman covered in a spectacular flush of crimson flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rain tree - common name for Albizia saman

Etymology

From the genus name Albizia, honoring Italian naturalist Filippo degli Albizzi, and 'saman', from Spanish adaptation of a native South American name.
