mackintosh

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæk.ɪnˌtɑːʃ / (AmE), / ˈmæk.ɪn.tɒʃ / (BrE)
a waterproof coat made of rubberized fabric, worn as rain protection
waterproof raincoat

Examples

She put on her mackintosh before stepping outside into the pouring rain.
The children wore bright yellow mackintoshes during their walk to school.
His old mackintosh still kept him dry despite its faded color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raincoat - a coat worn to protect the body from rain

Etymology

Named after Charles Macintosh (1766–1843), a Scottish chemist who invented the waterproof fabric.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæk.ɪnˌtɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈmæk.ɪn.tɒʃ / (BrE)
a lightweight waterproof fabric, usually rubberized, used especially for raincoats
waterproof fabric, often rubberized

Examples

The rain had soaked everything except her mackintosh and boots.
He wrapped the fragile package in a piece of mackintosh to protect it from the weather.
Early mackintosh fabrics were famous for their distinctive rubbery odor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oilskin - waterproof cloth treated with oil, used for rainwear
  • Synonym: gabardine - a durable, tightly woven fabric often used for raincoats

Etymology

From the name of Charles Macintosh (1766–1843), Scottish chemist and inventor.
