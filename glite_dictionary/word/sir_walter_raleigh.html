Sir Walter Raleigh

1. PROPN

/ sɚ ˈwɔltɚ ˈræli / (AmE), / sə ˈwɔːltə ˈrɔːli / (BrE)
(1552–1618) An English courtier, explorer, and writer, noted for his attempts to establish colonies in Virginia and for introducing potatoes and tobacco to England.
English explorer and courtier (1552–1618)

Examples

Sir Walter Raleigh is credited with popularizing tobacco in England during the late 16th century.
Many history books discuss how Sir Walter Raleigh attempted to establish a colony at Roanoke Island.
Queen Elizabeth I was known to have favored Sir Walter Raleigh at her court.
