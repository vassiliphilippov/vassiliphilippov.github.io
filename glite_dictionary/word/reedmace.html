reedmace

1. NOUN

/ ˈridˌmeɪs / (AmE), / ˈriːd.meɪs / (BrE)
a tall marsh plant with cylindrical brown seed heads, long flat leaves, and downy seeds, found in wetlands; its leaves are often used to make mats and chair seats
tall marsh plant with cylindrical seed head

Examples

Dense stands of reedmace bordered the banks of the slow-moving river.
The leaves of reedmace can be woven into mats and baskets by skilled artisans.
Children watched in fascination as the reedmace seed heads burst, releasing clouds of fluffy seeds into the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulrush - another common name for reedmace in British English
  • Synonym: cattail - common North American name for Typha species
