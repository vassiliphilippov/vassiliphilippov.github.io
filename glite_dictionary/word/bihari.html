Bihari

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈhɑri / (AmE), / bɪˈhɑːri / (BrE)
a group of Indic languages spoken in the Indian state of Bihar and surrounding regions, and by communities in Pakistan and Bangladesh
group of Indic languages spoken in Bihar

Examples

She is fluent in three languages, including Bihari.
Many migrants from Bihar continue to speak Bihari in their new communities.
The Bihari language family includes Maithili, Bhojpuri, and Magahi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hindi - the most widely spoken Indic language of India
  • Synonym: Bengali - the Indic language spoken in Bangladesh and eastern India

Etymology

From Bihar, the name of the Indian state, plus the English suffix -i indicating origin or affiliation.
