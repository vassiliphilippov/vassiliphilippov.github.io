urethrocele

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈriːθrəˌsiːl / (AmE), / jʊəˈriːθrəˌsiːl / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the urethra prolapses or bulges into the vagina
prolapse of the urethra into the vagina
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a urethrocele after reporting symptoms of urinary incontinence.
A urethrocele can sometimes be mistaken for other pelvic floor disorders.
Surgical intervention may be required to repair a severe urethrocele.

Etymology

From Greek 'ourēthrā' (urethra) + 'kēlē' (tumor, hernia).
