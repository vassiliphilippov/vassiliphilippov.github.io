Erythronium grandiflorum

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌrɪθroʊniəm ˌɡrændəˈflɔrəm / (AmE), / ɛˌrɪθrəʊniəm ˌɡrændɪˈflɔːrəm / (BrE)
A species of dogtooth violet native to western North America, bearing bright yellow flowers.
a yellow-flowered dogtooth violet

Examples

The meadows were carpeted with blooming Erythronium grandiflorum in early spring.
Botanists often study Erythronium grandiflorum because of its striking yellow petals.
We spotted several clumps of Erythronium grandiflorum along the hiking trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogtooth violet - common name for several Erythronium species

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' (red) and Latin 'grandiflorum' (large-flowered); the genus name refers to the related Erythronium species, though this one is yellow-flowered.
