Sir John Hawkyns

1. PROPN

/ sɚ dʒɑn ˈhɔkɪnz / (AmE), / sə dʒɒn ˈhɔːkɪnz / (BrE)
(1532–1595) An English naval commander, privateer, and slave trader who played a key role in the defeat of the Spanish Armada.
English naval commander and privateer (1532–1595)

Examples

Sir John Hawkyns was instrumental in modernizing the English navy before the battle with the Spanish Armada.
The career of Sir John Hawkyns was marked by both privateering and involvement in the transatlantic slave trade.
Historians credit Sir John Hawkyns with introducing important innovations in ship design.
