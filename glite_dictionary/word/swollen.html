swollen

1. ADJ

/ ˈswoʊlən / (AmE), / ˈswəʊlən / (BrE)
(of a person or their attitude) having an exaggerated sense of self-importance; excessively proud or conceited.
excessively proud or conceited (figurative)

Examples

After receiving the award, he became quite swollen and started treating others rudely.
Success has left her feeling swollen with pride and unable to accept criticism.
His swollen attitude made him unpopular among his colleagues.

Synset Examples

an attitude of self-conceited arrogance
an egotistical disregard of others
so swollen by victory that he was unfit for normal duty
growing ever more swollen-headed and arbitrary
vain about her clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conceited - having an excessively high opinion of oneself
  • Synonym: arrogant - having or revealing an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

2. ADJ

/ ˈswoʊlən / (AmE), / ˈswəʊlən / (BrE)
(of a part of the body or object) abnormally enlarged or distended, especially as a result of an accumulation of fluid or gas.
abnormally enlarged or distended

Examples

Her ankle was swollen after she twisted it playing basketball.
The river became swollen from the heavy rains, threatening to overflow its banks.
He touched his swollen eyelid gingerly, worried it might be infected.

Synset Examples

hungry children with bloated stomachs
he had a grossly distended stomach
eyes with puffed (or puffy) lids
swollen hands
tumescent tissue
puffy tumid flesh

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloated - swollen with fluid or gas; excessively enlarged
  • Synonym: distended - swollen outward beyond normal size, especially due to internal pressure
  • Antonym: flat - having a level surface; not swollen or raised

Etymology

Past participle of 'swell', from Old English 'swellan'.
