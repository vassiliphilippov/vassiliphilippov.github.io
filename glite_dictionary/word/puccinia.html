Puccinia

1. NOUN

/ pʊkˈsɪniə / (AmE), / pʊkˈsɪniə / (BrE)
A large genus of parasitic fungi that includes many species destructive to crops and other economic plants.
parasitic fungus genus affecting plants

Examples

Puccinia species are responsible for many damaging rust diseases in cereal crops worldwide.
Wheat farmers monitor their fields closely for signs of Puccinia infection each year.
The study focused on the life cycle of Puccinia in order to develop disease-resistant plants.

Etymology

Named after Italian botanist Tommaso Puccini (died 1811).
