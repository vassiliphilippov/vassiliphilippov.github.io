posthouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstˌhaʊs / (BrE)
an inn where post horses were changed and travelers could rest, typically used in past centuries for mail and stagecoach routes.
inn for changing post horses and lodging travelers
archaic

Examples

The weary travelers stopped at the posthouse to change horses and get some rest.
In the nineteenth century, the village’s posthouse became an important hub on the mail route.
He recalled tales of drivers meeting at the posthouse before heading out on snowy roads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coaching inn - an inn along a coach route, providing food and lodging to travelers

Etymology

From 'post', referring to mail carried on fixed routes, and 'house', meaning an inn or lodging.
