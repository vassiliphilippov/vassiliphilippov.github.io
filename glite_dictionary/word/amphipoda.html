Amphipoda

1. NOUN

/ æmˈfɪp.ə.də / (AmE), / æmˈfɪp.ə.də / (BrE)
An order of small, laterally compressed crustaceans that includes sand-hoppers, whale lice, and skeleton shrimp.
order of small crustaceans

Examples

Many beaches are home to Amphipoda, which play an important role in the ecosystem.
Scientists discovered a new species within the order Amphipoda in the deep sea.
Some Amphipoda species live among seaweed, while others burrow in sand.

Etymology

From Greek amphi- (on both sides) + podos (foot), referring to their different types of limbs.
