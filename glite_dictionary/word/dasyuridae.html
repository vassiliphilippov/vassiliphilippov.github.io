Dasyuridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæziˈjʊrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌdæsɪˈjʊərɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized carnivorous marsupials native to Australia and New Guinea, including the Tasmanian devil, quolls, and other similar mammals.
family of carnivorous marsupials (e.g., Tasmanian devil)

Examples

The Dasyuridae family includes well-known species such as the Tasmanian devil and the quoll.
Researchers study Dasyuridae to better understand marsupial carnivore evolution in Australia.
Several members of the Dasyuridae family are threatened due to habitat loss.

Etymology

From Greek dasys (hairy, shaggy) + oura (tail), with -idae denoting animal family in biological taxonomy.
