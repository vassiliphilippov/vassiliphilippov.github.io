uneven

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈivən / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːvən / (BrE)
not smooth, flat, or equal in surface, shape, or arrangement; varying in level, quality, or consistency.
not smooth, flat, or uniform

Examples

The table had an uneven surface, making it difficult to write on.
Walk carefully; the trail ahead is quite uneven and rocky.
His performance was uneven, with some excellent moments and some weak ones.

Synset Examples

an uneven color
uneven ground
uneven margins
wood with an uneven grain

Related Terms

  • Antonym: even - smooth or regular in surface or structure

Etymology

From un- (not) + even (flat, level).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00914441-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈivən / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːvən / (BrE)
(of a contest or competitors) not fairly matched in strength, ability, or resources, so that one side has a clear advantage.
not fairly matched as opponents

Examples

The final was an uneven contest, with one team dominating from the start.
Many critics felt the match was too uneven to be truly exciting.
The negotiations turned out to be uneven, as one party held all the leverage.

Synset Examples

vaudeville … waged an uneven battle against the church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: one-sided - unfairly or overwhelmingly favoring one side; not balanced
  • Synonym: lopsided - heavily unbalanced or favoring one side, often used for contests or results
  • Antonym: even - fairly matched in strength or ability; equal
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00896066-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈivən / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːvən / (BrE)
not regular or consistent; lacking uniformity or steady quality.
lacking consistency or regularity

Examples

His performance in the tournament was uneven, with both impressive and poor results.
The movie received uneven reviews from critics, ranging from rave to highly critical.
Attendance at the meetings has been uneven during the past few months.

Synset Examples

the golfer hit the ball well but his putting was spotty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inconsistent - not staying the same throughout, lacking consistency
  • Synonym: variable - liable to change; not consistent
  • Antonym: reliable - consistently good in quality or performance

Etymology

From un- (not) + even (level, equal); 'even' from Old English 'efen'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00581013-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ʌnˈivən / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːvən / (BrE)
not regular or uniform in occurrence, consistency, or spacing; variable and recurring at irregular intervals.
not uniform, irregular in pattern

Examples

The uneven distribution of rainfall caused drought in some areas and floods in others.
Her breathing became uneven as she ran up the hill.
There are uneven gaps between the fence posts, making it look poorly built.

Synset Examples

an uneven gait
uneven spacing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irregular - not even or uniform in pattern or occurrence
  • Antonym: even - level, regular, or uniform in pattern or occurrence

Etymology

From un- (not) + even (level, regular; from Old English 'efen').
