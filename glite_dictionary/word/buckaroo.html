buckaroo

1. NOUN

/ ˌbʌkəˈru / (AmE), / ˌbʌkəˈruː / (BrE)
(in the western US) a cowboy, especially one from California or Nevada.
cowboy in the western US

Examples

The seasoned buckaroo rode effortlessly across the open range, herding cattle before sunset.
Legend has it that the best buckaroo in Nevada could rope a wild horse in under a minute.
At the rodeo, young buckaroos showed off their impressive lasso skills to the cheering crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowboy - a person who tends cattle, especially in the western US
  • Synonym: vaquero - Spanish term for cowboy, especially in Texas and the southwest

Etymology

From Spanish vaquero (cowboy), adapted in American English in the western United States.
