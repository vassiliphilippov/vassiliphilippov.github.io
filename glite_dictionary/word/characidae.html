Characidae

1. NOUN

/ kəˈræsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kəˈræsɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of tropical freshwater fishes found in Africa, Central America, and South America, including tetras and their relatives.
family of tropical freshwater fishes

Examples

Many aquarists keep Characidae species, such as neons and cardinals, in their home aquariums.
The Amazon River is home to a remarkable diversity of Characidae.
Researchers discovered a new genus within the Characidae family in Central America.

Etymology

From modern Latin Characidae, based on Greek kharax (a pointed stake or fish of this family).
