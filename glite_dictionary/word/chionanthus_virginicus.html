Chionanthus virginicus

1. NOUN

/ kaɪəˈnænθəs vəˈdʒɪnɪkəs / (AmE), / kaɪəˈnænθəs vɪˈdʒɪnɪkəs / (BrE)
A small bushy tree of the southeastern United States with profuse clusters of white flowers; also known as the fringe tree.
fringe tree of southeastern US

Examples

Every spring, the Chionanthus virginicus in my backyard produces a cascade of fragrant white blossoms.
Botanists often recommend planting Chionanthus virginicus for its ornamental beauty and ease of care.
The delicate flowers of Chionanthus virginicus attract a variety of pollinators to local gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fringe tree - Common name for Chionanthus virginicus

Etymology

From Greek *chion* (snow) + *anthos* (flower); species name from Latin *virginicus* (of Virginia).
