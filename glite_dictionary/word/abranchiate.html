abranchiate

1. ADJ

/ əˈbræn.ki.eɪt / (AmE), / əˈbræn.kɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
(of an animal) lacking gills.
lacking gills (biology)
medical

Examples

Certain land-dwelling mollusks are abranchiate and rely on lungs for respiration.
The evolutionary shift from branchiate to abranchiate organisms marks a significant adaptation to terrestrial life.
Some amphibians become abranchiate as they mature, losing their gills after metamorphosis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: branchiate - having gills

Etymology

From New Latin 'abranchiatus', from Greek 'a-' (not) + 'branchia' (gills).
