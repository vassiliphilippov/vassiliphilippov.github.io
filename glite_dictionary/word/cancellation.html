cancellation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæn.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæn.səlˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of officially revoking, annulling, or making something void
act of revoking or annulling

Examples

The cancellation of the concert disappointed many fans.
She received a notice of cancellation for her flight due to bad weather.
The company announced the cancellation of the project after budget cuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revocation - the act of officially cancelling something
  • Synonym: annulment - the formal invalidation of something

Etymology

From Latin 'cancellatio' (a crossing out), from 'cancellare' (to cross out).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæn.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæn.səlˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of deciding that an event, agreement, or arrangement will not take place or continue
the act of cancelling something

Examples

Due to the severe storm, there was a sudden cancellation of all flights at the airport.
We regret to inform you of the cancellation of tonight's concert due to illness.
His cancellation of the meeting was announced just an hour before it was scheduled to begin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revocation - the official cancellation of a decision or promise
  • Antonym: confirmation - the act of making something certain or definite, especially an arrangement

Etymology

From Latin 'cancellatio(n-)', from 'cancellare' (to cross out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00233253-n
Synset POS: n
