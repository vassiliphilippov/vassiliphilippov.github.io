Innsbruck

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪnzˌbrʊk / (AmE), / ˈɪnzˌbrʊk / (BrE)
a city in southwestern Austria, famous as a resort for both summer and winter sports, and known for hosting major international events
city in southwestern Austria

Examples

The 1976 Winter Olympics were held in Innsbruck, attracting athletes from around the world.
During our trip to Austria, we spent several days exploring the beautiful city of Innsbruck.
Innsbruck is nestled in the Alps, making it a popular destination for skiing and mountaineering.

Etymology

From German 'Innsbruck' meaning 'bridge over the Inn (river)'.
