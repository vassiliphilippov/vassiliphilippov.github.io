ornamentalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrnəˈmɛntəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɔːnəˈmɛntəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or style of using decorative features or ornate design, often to excess, especially in architecture, art, or furnishings.
the use of ornamental display or decoration

Examples

The Victorian era was known for its ornamentalism in architecture and interior design.
Some critics argue that excessive ornamentalism can distract from the true function of an object.
The museum's new exhibit explores trends of ornamentalism in nineteenth-century furniture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minimalism - a style characterized by simplicity and lack of ornamentation

Etymology

From ornament + -al + -ism, patterning after 'ornamental'.
