Cardamine bulbifera

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrˈdæmɪn bʌlˈbɪfərə / (AmE), / kɑːˈdamɪni bʌlˈbɪfərə / (BrE)
A European perennial plant of the mustard family, known as coralroot bittercress, characterized by its knotted white rootstock and clusters of pale lilac flowers.
a European perennial plant with a knotted white rootstock

Examples

The shady woodland was full of Cardamine bulbifera, their pale flowers peeking through the undergrowth.
Botanists recorded a rare specimen of Cardamine bulbifera near the river bank.
Gardeners appreciate Cardamine bulbifera for its decorative clusters of lilac flowers.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cardamine' (a genus of plants) and 'bulbifera' (bulb-bearing).
