ruling

1. ADJ

/ ˈruːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈruːlɪŋ / (BrE)
Having control of or holding official power over a country, organization, or group; exercising authority.
exercising power or authority

Examples

The ruling party introduced several controversial reforms last year.
Members of the ruling family have governed the country for decades.
Critics argue that the ruling elite is out of touch with ordinary citizens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: governing - having authority to control or regulate
  • Antonym: subordinate - lower in rank or authority, not in control

Etymology

Present participle of 'rule', from Anglo-Norman 'reuler', from Latin 'regulare'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈruːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈruːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the official statement by a court giving the reasons for its judgment, as distinct from the verdict itself
legal explanation for a court's judgment
legal

Examples

The judge issued a ruling explaining the legal basis for her decision.
In his ruling, the justice cited several precedents to support the court's judgment.
The ruling clarified the interpretation of the law, even though the verdict was already known.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judgment - the final decision of a court in a legal case

Etymology

From the verb 'rule', meaning to exercise judicial authority, or to decide.
