Annona squamosa

1. NOUN

/ əˈnoʊnə skwəˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / əˈnəʊnə skwəˈməʊsə / (BrE)
A tropical American tree bearing sweet, pulpy fruit with a thick scaly rind and shiny black seeds, commonly known as the sugar apple or custard apple.
tropical fruit tree (sugar apple)

Examples

The Annona squamosa thrives in warm climates and produces delicious, sweet fruit.
Farmers in South America cultivate Annona squamosa for both local consumption and export.
The scaly rind of Annona squamosa fruit must be peeled to reach the creamy pulp inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar apple - common name for Annona squamosa
  • Synonym: custard apple - another common name for Annona squamosa fruit

Etymology

From Latin: 'Annona' (a genus name), 'squamosa' (scaly), referring to the fruit's rind.
