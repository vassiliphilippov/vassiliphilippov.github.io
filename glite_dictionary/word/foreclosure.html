foreclosure

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈkloʊʒər / (AmE), / fɔːˈkləʊʒə / (BrE)
the legal process by which a lender takes possession of a property due to the borrower's failure to repay a loan
legal process of repossessing property for loan default
legal

Examples

The couple lost their home to foreclosure after failing to make mortgage payments.
During the recession, foreclosure rates increased sharply across the country.
She was able to stop the foreclosure by negotiating a payment plan with the bank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repossession - the action of taking back property by a lender

Etymology

From foreclose + -ure, from Old French 'forclore' (to exclude) and Latin 'claudere' (to close).
