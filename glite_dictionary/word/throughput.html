throughput

1. NOUN

/ ˈθruːˌpʊt / (AmE), / ˈθruːˌpʊt / (BrE)
The amount of material, information, or items passing through a system, process, or device in a given period, especially referring to output produced relative to input.
rate of output through a system

Examples

The network upgrade significantly increased the server's throughput.
This router can handle a throughput of up to 1 gigabit per second.
Improving throughput is critical for reducing delays in data processing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: output - the amount produced by a system
  • Degree Higher: capacity - the maximum amount that can be contained or produced

Etymology

Formed from 'through' + 'put', as in 'to put through'.
