trainman

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪnmæn / (AmE), / ˈtreɪnmən / (BrE)
a male employee who works on a train, especially one responsible for operations or for assisting passengers and the conductor
male railroad employee

Examples

The trainman checked the tickets as the passengers boarded the carriage.
After working as a trainman for fifteen years, he knew every part of the route by heart.
The conductor and trainman coordinated to ensure the train's safety during the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: railroader - an employee of a railroad

Etymology

From 'train' (railroad vehicle) + 'man' (male person), patterned after similar occupational terms.
