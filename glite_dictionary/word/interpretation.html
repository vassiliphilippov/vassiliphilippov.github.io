interpretation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɜːrprəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɜːprɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a particular way of performing, presenting, or expressing an artistic work, such as a piece of music, drama, or poetry
an artistic performance or presentation

Examples

Her interpretation of the concerto captured the emotions of the composer beautifully.
The actor's unique interpretation of Hamlet drew praise from critics.
Each dancer brings their own interpretation to the choreography, making each performance distinct.

Synset Examples

her rendition of Milton's verse was extraordinarily moving

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rendition - a performance or interpretation, especially of a piece of music or drama

Etymology

From Latin interpretatio (explanation, exposition).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɝːprəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɜːprɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an explanation or understanding of the meaning of something, especially when it is not immediately clear
explanation or understanding of meaning

Examples

The judge’s interpretation of the law differed from that of other legal scholars.
There are many possible interpretations of this abstract painting, each revealing new meaning.
His interpretation of her remarks suggested she was unhappy with the outcome.

Synset Examples

the edict was subject to many interpretations
he annoyed us with his interpreting of parables
often imitations are extended to provide a more accurate rendition of the child's intended meaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explanation - a statement that makes something clear
  • Antonym: misinterpretation - a misunderstanding or incorrect explanation

Etymology

From Latin 'interpretatio' (explanation, exposition), from 'interpretari' (to explain, expound).
