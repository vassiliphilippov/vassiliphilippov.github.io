Sothis

1. PROPN

/ ˈsoʊθɪs / (AmE), / ˈsəʊθɪs / (BrE)
an ancient name for Sirius, the brightest star in the night sky, found in the constellation Canis Major
ancient name for Sirius, the brightest star

Examples

In ancient Egyptian astronomy, Sothis was revered as a harbinger of the Nile's flooding.
The annual rise of Sothis marked the start of the new year in Egypt.
Some historians believe festivals were dedicated to Sothis as the bringer of prosperity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sirius - the brightest star in the night sky, in Canis Major

Etymology

From Greek Sōthis, used in ancient Egypt to refer to Sirius.
