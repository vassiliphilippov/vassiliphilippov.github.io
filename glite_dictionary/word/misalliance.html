misalliance

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪs.əˈlaɪ.əns / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.əˈlaɪ.əns / (BrE)
A marriage or alliance considered unsuitable or inappropriate, especially due to differences in social rank, values, or background.
unsuitable marriage or alliance

Examples

Her family disapproved of the misalliance and feared it would bring shame.
The novel centers on a misalliance between a wealthy heiress and a penniless artist.
Rumors of a misalliance spread quickly through the small, conservative town.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alliance - a relationship formed for mutual benefit, especially between countries or organizations

Etymology

From French 'mésalliance', from 'mes-' (wrong) + 'alliance' (alliance).
