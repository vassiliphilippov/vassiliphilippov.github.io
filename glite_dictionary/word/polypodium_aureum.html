Polypodium aureum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːlɪˈpoʊdiəm ɔːˈriəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈpəʊdiəm ɔːˈriːəm / (BrE)
a tropical American fern with brown scaly rhizomes, cultivated for its large, deeply lobed blue-green fronds; sometimes classified in the genus Polypodium
species of tropical American fern

Examples

The Polypodium aureum thrives in warm, humid environments and is popular as a houseplant.
Botanists often study Polypodium aureum for its unique, deeply lobed fronds.
Garden centers sometimes sell Polypodium aureum under the name 'blue star fern'.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Polypodium' (genus name, from Greek poly- 'many' + podion 'little foot') and 'aureum' (golden).
