forever

1. ADV

/ fərˈɛvər / (AmE), / fəˈrɛvə / (BrE)
For all time; for an endless or limitless duration.
for a limitless time; always

Examples

I will love you forever, no matter what happens.
The mountain seemed as if it had stood there forever.
Some people believe that diamonds last forever.

Synset Examples

no one can live forever
brightly beams our Father's mercy from his lighthouse evermore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eternally - In a manner that lasts forever; for all time.
  • Antonym: never - At no time; not ever (opposite of forever in some contexts).

Etymology

From Middle English 'for ever', a combination of 'for' and 'ever'.

2. ADV

/ fəˈrɛvɚ / (AmE), / fəˈrɛvə / (BrE)
For a very long time; for what seems like an endless period.
for a very long time

Examples

It felt like we waited forever for our food to arrive at the restaurant.
The movie seemed to last forever, and I was glad when it finally ended.
If you leave metal outside, it won't last forever without rusting.

Synset Examples

she took forever to write the paper
we had to wait forever and a day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eternally - in a way that lasts forever; for all time
  • Antonym: never - at no time in the past or future; not ever

Etymology

From Middle English 'for ever', literally 'for ever'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00089564-r
Synset POS: r
