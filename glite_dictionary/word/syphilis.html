syphilis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪf.əl.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪf.ɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
a sexually transmitted disease caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum, characterized by progressive stages and a variety of symptoms; it can also be passed from mother to child during pregnancy.
a sexually transmitted disease
medical

Examples

He was diagnosed with syphilis after a series of unexplained symptoms appeared.
If left untreated, syphilis can lead to serious health problems in its later stages.
Doctors recommend regular screening for syphilis and other sexually transmitted infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gonorrhea - another common sexually transmitted disease
  • Synonym: chlamydia - a common sexually transmitted infection caused by bacteria

Etymology

Coined in New Latin from 'Syphilus', the title character of a 16th-century poem by Girolamo Fracastoro, who contracted the disease.
