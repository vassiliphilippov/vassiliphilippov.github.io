/ ˈblɛsɪd / (AmE), / ˈblɛsɪd / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) declared by the pope to be deserving of veneration, typically as a stage before canonization as a saint.

Examples

Pilgrims traveled from around the world to visit the shrine of the blessed martyr.
He was named blessed after a rigorous process of verification of miracles.
