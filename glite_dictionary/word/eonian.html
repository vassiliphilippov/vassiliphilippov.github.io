eonian

1. ADJ

/ iˈoʊniən / (AmE), / iˈəʊniən / (BrE)
Relating to or lasting for an eon; of or pertaining to a geological eon, a period longer than an era.
relating to a geological eon

Examples

The formation of the Grand Canyon is the result of eonian geological processes.
Scientists discuss eonian changes when referring to shifts spanning billions of years.
In literature, timeless love is sometimes described as eonian devotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eternal - lasting or existing forever; without end
  • Degree Lower: epochal - relating to an epoch or significant period

Etymology

From Greek aiōnios (eternal), from aiōn (age, eon).
