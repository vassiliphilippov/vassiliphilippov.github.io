anarchy

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɚki / (AmE), / ˈanəki / (BrE)
a state of society without government or law, often resulting in disorder and chaos
absence of government; disorder

Examples

After the collapse of the regime, the country descended into anarchy for several months.
The sudden withdrawal of the police created anarchy in the region, as criminal groups took over.
Writers often warn that without shared laws, society risks falling into anarchy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lawlessness - a state where laws are not obeyed
  • Synonym: chaos - complete disorder and confusion
  • Antonym: order - a state in which rules and laws are obeyed

Etymology

From Greek 'anarkhia', from 'an-' (without) + 'arkhos' (ruler).
