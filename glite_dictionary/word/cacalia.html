Cacalia

1. NOUN

/ kəˈkeɪliə / (AmE), / kəˈkeɪlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tall, smooth herbs found in forested mountainous regions of Europe and Asia Minor, sometimes including species usually classified in the genus Emilia.
genus of tall mountain herbs
medical

Examples

The flowers of Cacalia are often found in mountainous forests across Europe.
Botanists have reclassified some species from Cacalia to other genera such as Emilia.
The tall stems and smooth leaves are characteristic of Cacalia plants.

Etymology

From Latin Cacalia, from Greek kakalía, an ancient plant name.
