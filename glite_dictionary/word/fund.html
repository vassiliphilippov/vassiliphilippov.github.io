fund

1. NOUN

/ fʌnd / (AmE), / fʌnd / (BrE)
a supply or reserve of something available for future use
supply kept for future use

Examples

She kept a fund of patience for dealing with difficult customers.
The library has a fund of classic novels in its archives.
We should always maintain a fund of emergency supplies just in case.

Synset Examples

he brought back a large store of Cuban cigars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserve - a supply set aside for future use
  • Synonym: store - a supply kept for later use

Etymology

From Latin 'fundus' meaning 'bottom, foundation', later generalizing to a stock or supply.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13388243-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ fʌnd / (AmE), / fʌnd / (BrE)
a financial institution that pools money from investors to buy securities such as stocks or bonds, and sells shares to individuals.
financial institution for pooled investment

Examples

She invested in an international fund to diversify her portfolio.
The fund offers shares to individual investors and manages assets worth billions.
Many people rely on a retirement fund to secure their financial future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutual fund - an investment fund managed for a group of investors
  • Synonym: pension fund - a fund set up to pay retirement benefits

Etymology

From Latin 'fundus' (bottom, foundation), later developing the financial sense in English.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
