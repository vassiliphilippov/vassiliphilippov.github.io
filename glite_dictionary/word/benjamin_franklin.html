Benjamin Franklin

1. PROPN

/ ˌbɛndʒəmɪn ˈfræŋklɪn / (AmE), / ˌbɛndʒəmɪn ˈfræŋklɪn / (BrE)
(1706–1790) An American statesman, inventor, scientist, and author, notable for his role in drafting the Declaration of Independence and the Constitution and for his research in electricity.
American statesman, inventor, and scientist (1706–1790)

Examples

Benjamin Franklin played a significant role in the drafting of the United States Constitution.
The famous experiment with a kite and a key was conducted by Benjamin Franklin.
Many of Benjamin Franklin's proverbs are still quoted today.
