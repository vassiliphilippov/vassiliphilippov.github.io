neurosarcoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʊroʊsɑrˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊsɑːˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor arising from nerve tissue and involving fibrous or connective tissue.
malignant tumor of nerve tissue
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a rare neurosarcoma in the lower spinal region.
Treatment options for neurosarcoma often include surgery, radiation, and chemotherapy.
Her research focuses on improving outcomes for children affected by neurosarcoma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcoma - a malignant tumor of connective or other non-epithelial tissue

Etymology

From Greek neuron (nerve) + sarx, sarkos (flesh) + -oma (tumor).
