dapple-grey

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæp.əl ˌɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈdæp.əl ˌɡreɪ / (BrE)
(of a horse) having a grey coat marked with lighter or darker spots or patches.
a horse with a spotted grey coat

Examples

The dapple-grey trotted gracefully across the field, its coat shimmering in the sunlight.
She always dreamed of riding a dapple-grey through the countryside.
The stable had several horses, but the dapple-grey was considered the most beautiful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grey - having a color intermediate between black and white
  • Degree Lower: roan - a horse with a coat of a main color mixed with white hairs

Etymology

From 'dapple', meaning 'mottled', of uncertain origin, combined with 'grey' (Old English 'grǣg').
