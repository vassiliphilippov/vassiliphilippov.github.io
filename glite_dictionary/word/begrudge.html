to begrudge

1. VERB

/ bɪˈɡrʌdʒ / (AmE), / bɪˈɡrʌdʒ / (BrE)
To envy someone the possession or enjoyment of something, or to give or allow something unwillingly.
envy or resent giving something

Examples

He didn’t begrudge his sister her success, even though he worked just as hard.
I don’t begrudge you a moment of your happiness.
She begrudges spending money on herself, preferring to save for her children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to resent - to feel bitter or indignant about something
  • Synonym: envy - to desire what someone else has

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + grudge, from Middle English 'grudgen', from Old French 'grouchier' (to grumble).

2. VERB

/ bɪˈɡrʌdʒ / (AmE), / bɪˈɡrʌdʒ / (BrE)
to feel unhappy about giving or allowing something, or to wish something did not happen to someone; to give or allow unwillingly or with resentment
to give or allow unwillingly; to resent

Examples

She didn't begrudge him his success, even though she worked just as hard.
He may begrudge every penny he spends on repairs.
I don't begrudge you the opportunity to travel, but I do wish I could join you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to resent - to feel bitter or indignant about something
  • Antonym: to allow - to permit, let happen

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + grudge, from Old French 'grouchier' (to murmur, grumble).
