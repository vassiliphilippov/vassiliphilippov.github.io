stupid

1. NOUN

/ ˈstuːpɪd / (AmE), / ˈstjuːpɪd / (BrE)
A person regarded as lacking intelligence or common sense.
a person regarded as unintelligent
informal

Examples

I can't believe you forgot your own birthday, you stupid!
Don't be such a stupid—think before you act.
They blamed the mistake on some stupid in accounting.

Synset Examples

The economy, stupid!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiot - a person of low intelligence; a very foolish person
  • Antonym: genius - a person of great intelligence

Etymology

From Latin stupidus (amazed, stunned), from stupēre (to be numb or amazed).

/ ˈstuː.pɪd / (AmE), / ˈstjuː.pɪd / (BrE)
lacking intelligence or common sense; showing a lack of good judgment or sense.
lacking intelligence; senseless

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dumb - lacking intelligence or good judgment
  • Antonym: intelligent - having or showing intelligence

Etymology

From Latin stupidus (amazed, stunned), from stupere (to be amazed or stunned).

3. ADJ

/ ˈstuː.pɪd, ˈstjuː.pɪd / (AmE), / ˈstjuː.pɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) temporarily unable to think clearly or react normally, especially as a result of shock, fatigue, or a blow; mentally numb.
mentally numb or dazed (temporary)

Examples

He was so stupid with exhaustion that he could hardly answer their questions.
After hitting his head, he sat on the ground, stupid and motionless for several minutes.
The news left her stupid with shock, unable to process what had happened.

Synset Examples

was stupid from fatigue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dazed - unable to think or react properly; confused
  • Antonym: alert - able to think or react quickly and clearly

Etymology

From Latin stūpidus (amazed, stunned), from stūpere (be stunned or amazed).

4. ADJ

/ ˈstuː.pɪd / (AmE), / ˈstjuː.pɪd / (BrE)
lacking intelligence or common sense; showing a low level of mental ability
lacking intelligence or sense

Examples

It was a stupid mistake to leave the door unlocked overnight.
I felt stupid for forgetting her birthday again this year.
The movie has a stupid plot, but it was still entertaining.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dumb - lacking intelligence or common sense
  • Synonym: foolish - showing lack of good sense or judgement
  • Antonym: intelligent - having or showing intelligence, especially of a high level

Etymology

From Latin 'stupidus' (amazed, stunned), from 'stupere' (to be numb or astonished).
