Crataegus biltmoreana

1. NOUN

/ krəˈtiː.ɡəs bɪltˌmɔː.riˈæn.ə / (AmE), / krəˈtiː.ɡəs bɪltˌmɔː.riˈæn.ə / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree of the eastern United States, noted for its few thorns, white corymbed flowers, and bright orange-red berries
a North American shrub or small tree with white flowers and orange-red berries

Examples

The Crataegus biltmoreana is often found along woodland edges in the eastern United States.
Gardeners appreciate Crataegus biltmoreana for its attractive orange-red berries in the fall.
Unlike other hawthorns, Crataegus biltmoreana has relatively few thorns on its branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - a plant of the genus Crataegus, commonly called hawthorn

Etymology

From Latin botanical nomenclature: genus 'Crataegus' plus the specific epithet 'biltmoreana', indicating association with the Biltmore Estate in North Carolina.
