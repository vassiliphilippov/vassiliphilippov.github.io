thievish

1. ADJ

/ ˈθiːvɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈθiːvɪʃ / (BrE)
Having the characteristics or tendencies of a thief; prone to or suggestive of stealing.
having or showing the qualities of a thief

Examples

The thievish look in his eyes made me suspicious of his intentions.
A thievish raccoon managed to get into the garbage and scatter everything around.
She distrusted his thievish grin as he eyed her wallet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thieving - given to or characterized by stealing
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit or untruthfulness; sincere

Etymology

Formed from 'thief' + '-ish'.
