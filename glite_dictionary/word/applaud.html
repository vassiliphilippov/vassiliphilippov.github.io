to applaud

1. VERB

/ əˈplɔd / (AmE), / əˈplɔːd / (BrE)
to show approval by clapping one's hands, especially after a performance, speech, or event
show approval by clapping

Examples

The audience applauded loudly at the end of the show.
Everyone stood up to applaud the performers for their remarkable act.
She applauded her friend's courage during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheer - to shout in encouragement or approval
  • Antonym: boo - to shout to show disapproval

Etymology

From Latin 'applaudere', meaning 'to clap at, approve'.

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈplɔd / (AmE), / əˈplɔːd / (BrE)
to express approval, praise, or admiration for someone or something, especially publicly
express approval or praise

Examples

Many experts applaud the new policy for its positive environmental impact.
I must applaud your dedication to helping others in your community.
The critics applauded the author’s courage in telling her story.

Synset Examples

I applaud your efforts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - express strong approval or admiration
  • Antonym: to criticize - to express disapproval of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin applaudere, from ad- (to) + plaudere (to clap).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00861956-v
Synset POS: v
