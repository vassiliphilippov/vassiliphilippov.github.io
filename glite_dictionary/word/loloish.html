Loloish

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊloʊˌɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈləʊləʊɪʃ / (BrE)
a branch of the Sino-Tibetan language family, comprising languages spoken by various hill tribes in northern Myanmar and neighboring regions
branch of Sino-Tibetan languages

Examples

Linguists have documented several Loloish dialects in Yunnan Province.
The Loloish languages share many structural features with other Tibeto-Burman languages.
Research into Loloish has contributed valuable insights into the history of the Sino-Tibetan language family.

Etymology

From the name 'Lolo', a former name for the Yi people of China, with English suffix '-ish'.
