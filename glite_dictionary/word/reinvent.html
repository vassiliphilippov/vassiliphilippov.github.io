to reinvent

1. VERB

/ ˌriːɪnˈvɛnt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈvɛnt / (BrE)
to create or change something again in a different way, especially to make it seem new or improved
create or change something again

Examples

After years in the business, she decided to reinvent herself as a fashion designer.
Many companies must reinvent their products to stay competitive in the market.
The classic story was reinvented in a modern setting for a new generation.

Synset Examples

The candidate reinvented the concept of national health care so that he would get elected

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - to bring something back to life or use
  • Synonym: to restore - to bring back to a former condition

Etymology

From re- (again) + invent (from Latin invenire, to find, discover).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01741804-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌriːɪnˈvɛnt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈvɛnt / (BrE)
to create something anew, especially by making significant changes to improve or adapt it for a new purpose
create or remake something anew

Examples

After losing his job, he decided to reinvent himself as a freelance writer.
The band tried to reinvent their music to appeal to a younger audience.
She managed to reinvent the traditional recipe with modern ingredients.

Synset Examples

He reinvented African music for American listeners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transform - to change something completely in form or appearance
  • Antonym: to restore - to bring back to a former condition

Etymology

From re- (again) + invent, Latin *inventus* (found, devised).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01741659-v
Synset POS: v
