seasick

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiˌsɪk / (AmE), / ˈsiːsɪk / (BrE)
feeling sick or nauseous because of the motion of a boat or ship at sea
feeling ill from boat movement

Examples

After only an hour on the boat, Sarah felt extremely seasick and had to sit outside.
Many passengers become seasick during rough weather at sea.
He brought medicine in case he got seasick on the ferry ride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motion sickness - nausea caused by motion, such as in a vehicle
  • Degree Lower: nauseous - feeling as if you might vomit

Etymology

From 'sea' + 'sick', first recorded in the 17th century.
