Sisyrinchium

1. NOUN

/ sɪzɪˈrɪŋkiəm / (AmE), / sɪzɪˈrɪŋkɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly North American grasslike perennial herbs in the iris family, often bearing small, star-shaped flowers.
genus of North American grasslike herbs

Examples

The field was dotted with delicate Sisyrinchium flowers among the tall grass.
Botanists identified several species of Sisyrinchium during their survey of the prairie.
Some gardeners cultivate Sisyrinchium for its attractive blue or violet blossoms.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek "sisyrinchion" (a plant, possibly the Barbary nut).

2. NOUN

/ sɪzɪˈrɪŋkiəm / (AmE), / sɪzɪˈrɪŋkɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly North American grasslike herbs in the iris family, often bearing blue, yellow, or white flowers.
a genus of grasslike flowering herbs

Examples

The Sisyrinchium plants in the meadow bloomed with delicate blue flowers every spring.
Many gardeners plant Sisyrinchium as a decorative border because of its grasslike appearance.
Wild bees are often attracted to the vibrant clusters of Sisyrinchium in prairies.

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Greek *sisyrinchion* (a kind of wild iris).
