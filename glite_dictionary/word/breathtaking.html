breathtaking

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɛθ.teɪˌkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɛθ.teɪ.kɪŋ / (BrE)
Astonishing or awe-inspiring in a way that takes away one’s breath.
astonishing; awe-inspiring

Examples

The view from the mountain summit was simply breathtaking.
Her breathtaking performance left the entire audience speechless.
The artist captured a breathtaking sunset over the ocean.

Synset Examples

a breathless flight
breathtaking adventure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stunning - extremely impressive or attractive
  • Synonym: amazing - causing great surprise or wonder
  • Antonym: ordinary - not unusual or remarkable

Etymology

Formed from 'breath' + present participle of 'take', referring to something so remarkable it seems to make breathing difficult.

/ ˈbrɛθˌteɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɛθˌteɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely impressive, beautiful, or astonishing in a way that takes one's breath away
extremely impressive or beautiful

Examples

The view from the top of the mountain was simply breathtaking.
She wore a breathtaking gown that captured everyone's attention at the gala.
The documentary featured breathtaking shots of wildlife in the African savannah.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stunning - very impressive or beautiful, causing surprise or admiration
  • Synonym: magnificent - extremely beautiful, elaborate, or impressive
  • Antonym: ordinary - with no special or distinctive features; normal

Etymology

From ‘breath’ + ‘taking’, originally meaning 'taking one's breath away' from astonishment or beauty.
