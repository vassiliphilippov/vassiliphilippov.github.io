indulgent

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdʌl.dʒənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌl.dʒənt / (BrE)
Willing to allow someone to have or do what they want, especially when it is not good for them; lenient or overly generous.
lenient or overly generous

Examples

Her indulgent parents let her stay up as late as she wanted on weekends.
The teacher was indulgent with the students, often overlooking minor rule violations.
He smiled an indulgent smile as his son told another tall tale.

Synset Examples

indulgent grandparents

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strict - demanding obedience or discipline; not allowing freedom or leniency
  • Synonym: lenient - tolerant or permissive; allowing much freedom; not strict

Etymology

From Latin 'indulgent-', from the verb 'indulgere' (be kind or tender).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdʌldʒənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌldʒənt / (BrE)
Willing to allow someone to enjoy something or to have more than is good for them; permissive or lenient, especially with regard to someone's faults or desires.
tolerant or lenient; permissive

Examples

She is an indulgent grandmother who always gives the children extra treats.
Some teachers are too indulgent with disruptive students.
His indulgent attitude sometimes lets bad habits go unchecked.

Synset Examples

indulgent parents risk spoiling their children
too soft on the children
they are soft on crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lenient - permissive; not strict or harsh
  • Antonym: strict - demanding firm discipline or obedience

Etymology

From Latin indulgent-, from indulgere (to be kind or lenient).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01767671-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdʌldʒənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌldʒənt / (BrE)
Tending to allow someone to have or do whatever they want; showing excessive willingness to be lenient or generous.
willing to be lenient or generous

Examples

Her indulgent parents rarely punished her for breaking the rules.
The teacher gave an indulgent smile and ignored the mistake.
He is sometimes too indulgent with his grandchildren, buying them treats every day.

Synset Examples

an indulgent attitude

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lenient - showing tolerance or mercy, not strict
  • Antonym: strict - demanding that rules are obeyed, not flexible

Etymology

From Latin indulgent-, present participle of indulgere (to be kind or lenient).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00999762-s
Synset POS: s
