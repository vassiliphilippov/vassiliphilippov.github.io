ocean

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈəʊʃən / (BrE)
A very large area of salt water that covers most of the Earth's surface, separating continents.
major saltwater body on Earth

Examples

The Pacific ocean is the largest and deepest of the world's oceans.
After months at sea, the sailors were overjoyed to see the vast ocean stretching before them.
She dreams of studying marine life in the ocean one day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea - a large body of salt water smaller than an ocean, often partly enclosed by land

Etymology

From Greek 'ōkeanos' (the ocean, a great stream encircling the earth).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈəʊʃən / (BrE)
A very large or unlimited quantity or extent of something; vastness or abundance.
a vast or limitless quantity

Examples

There was an ocean of paperwork on her desk by Monday morning.
He has an ocean of experience in the industry.
The project still faces an ocean of challenges before it can be completed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea - a large quantity or expanse of something (figurative)

Etymology

From Old French *occean*, from Latin *Oceanus*, from Greek *Okeanos*, the name of the great river believed to encircle the earth.
