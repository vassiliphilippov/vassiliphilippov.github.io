picturesque

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɪktʃərˈɛsk / (AmE), / ˌpɪktʃərˈɛsk / (BrE)
(of language or description) vividly expressive or striking in effect, often creating strong images in the mind.
vividly expressive; creating striking images

Examples

Her picturesque account of the festival made us feel as though we were really there.
The novel is filled with picturesque dialogue that captures the charm of the setting.
He gave a picturesque explanation of the city's lively streets, painting vivid scenes with his words.

Synset Examples

a picturesque description of the rainforest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vivid - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind

Etymology

From French 'pittoresque', from Italian 'pittoresco', from 'pittore' meaning 'painter'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌpɪktʃərˈɛsk / (AmE), / ˌpɪktʃərˈɛsk / (BrE)
(of a place, building, scene, etc.) visually attractive, especially in a quaint or charming way; suitable for being depicted in a picture.
visually attractive or charming

Examples

The picturesque village attracted many tourists each year.
We enjoyed a picnic by the river, surrounded by picturesque scenery.
Her cottage was set in a picturesque valley, perfect for painting.

Synset Examples

a picturesque village

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charming - delightfully pleasing or attractive
  • Antonym: ugly - unpleasant or unattractive to look at

Etymology

From French pittoresque, from Italian pittoresco, from pittore (painter), from Latin pictor.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00221516-s
Synset POS: s
