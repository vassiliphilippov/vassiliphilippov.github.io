placoid

1. ADJ

/ ˈpleɪ.kɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈpleɪ.kɔɪd / (BrE)
(of scales) having the form of hard, flattened plates, as in sharks and rays.
having hard, flat scales (as in sharks)

Examples

The shark’s skin is covered in placoid scales that reduce friction as it swims.
Scientists study the unique structure of placoid scales to design better swimsuits.
Unlike bony fish, rays have a covering of placoid plates for protection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ganoid - (of scales) diamond-shaped, found in some fish such as sturgeon
  • Synonym: cycloid - (of fish scales) smooth-edged scales, characteristic of salmon and carp
  • Synonym: ctenoid - (of fish scales) comb-like edged scales, common in perches

Etymology

From Greek plax, plakos (flat plate) + -oid (like, resembling).
