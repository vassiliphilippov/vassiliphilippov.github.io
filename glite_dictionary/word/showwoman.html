showwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman who organizes, produces, or presents public entertainments or exhibitions.
female organizer of public entertainments

Examples

She made a name for herself as a brilliant showwoman in the city's theater scene.
The event was a resounding success, thanks to the efforts of the dedicated showwoman.
As a showwoman, she was known for her creativity and flair in staging large productions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showman - a person who produces or presents public entertainments
  • Degree Lower: showman - male counterpart of 'showwoman' in entertainment industry

Etymology

Formed from 'show' + 'woman', after 'showman.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who is skilled at giving entertaining or effective presentations or performances, especially in public settings
woman adept at presentations or performances

Examples

She proved herself a true showwoman with her captivating stage presence.
As a showwoman, she knows how to hold an audience's attention for hours.
The awards ceremony was expertly hosted by a skilled showwoman who kept the crowd engaged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showman - a man skilled at making effective presentations or performances

Etymology

Formed in English by combining 'show' and 'woman', following the pattern of 'showman'.
