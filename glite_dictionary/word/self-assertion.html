self-assertion

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf əˈsɜrʃən / (AmE), / sɛlf əˈsɜːʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the confident and forceful expression of one's own opinions, often in a way that may appear boastful or imply a sense of superiority
confident forceful expression of one's opinions

Examples

Her self-assertion during the meeting surprised her colleagues, who were used to her quiet demeanor.
Some people view self-assertion as confidence, while others see it as arrogance.
The coach encouraged healthy self-assertion among the players to improve communication on the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertiveness - confidence and directness in stating one's opinions, without being aggressive
  • Antonym: humility - modest view of one's importance; lack of self-assertion

Etymology

From 'self-' meaning 'oneself' + 'assertion' from Latin 'assertio' (a declaration, assertion).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf əˈsɝːʃən / (AmE), / ˌself əˈsɜːʃən / (BrE)
confidently expressing or defending one's own rights, opinions, or desires, especially in a forceful way
confident self-expression or defense

Examples

Her self-assertion during the meeting impressed her colleagues and won her new opportunities.
The therapist encouraged him to work on self-assertion as part of his personal growth.
Too much self-assertion can sometimes be perceived as arrogance or aggression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertiveness - the quality of being self-assured and confident without being aggressive
  • Antonym: submissiveness - the quality of being willing to submit to others; lack of self-assertion

Etymology

From 'self' + 'assertion', based on 'assert' (from Latin 'assertus', past participle of 'asserere' to claim).
