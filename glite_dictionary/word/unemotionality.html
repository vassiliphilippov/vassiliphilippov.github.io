unemotionality

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnɪˌmoʊʃəˈnælɪti / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪˌməʊʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of showing little or no emotion.
lack of emotion

Examples

His unemotionality made it hard for others to understand what he was feeling.
Despite the chaos, she handled the crisis with complete unemotionality.
People sometimes mistake unemotionality for indifference, but it can simply mean self-control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emotionlessness - the state of having no emotions
  • Antonym: emotionality - the state of having or expressing emotion

Etymology

Formed from 'unemotional' + '-ity', modeled on similar Latinate abstract nouns.

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.ɪˌmoʊ.ʃəˈnæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪˌməʊ.ʃəˈnæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of showing little or no emotional response or feeling
lack of emotional response

Examples

His unemotionality during the crisis surprised everyone at the meeting.
Some leaders are admired for their unemotionality in the face of difficult decisions.
She attributes her unemotionality to years of working in a high-stress environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern
  • Antonym: emotionality - the observable behavioral and physiological component of emotion

Etymology

From 'unemotional' + '-ity', modeled after 'emotion' (French émotion, from Latin emovere, to move out).
