Durham

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɜrəm / (AmE), / ˈdʌrəm / (BrE)
A city in north central North Carolina, United States, known as the site of Duke University.
city in North Carolina, US

Examples

Many students travel to Durham each year to attend Duke University.
The city of Durham is known for its rich history and vibrant arts scene.
We spent the afternoon exploring the museums and parks in Durham.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɜːrəm / (AmE), / ˈdʌrəm / (BrE)
An English breed of short-horned cattle, known for its meat and milk production.
English breed of short-horned cattle

Examples

The farmer raised a herd of Durham cattle for both milk and beef production.
Durham cattle are renowned for their calm temperament and high yields.
He won first prize at the show with his champion Durham.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shorthorn - A breed of cattle developed from the Durham, used for beef and dairy

Etymology

Named after Durham, a county in northern England, where the breed originated.
