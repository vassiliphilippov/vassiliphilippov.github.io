to play-act

1. VERB

/ ˈpleɪˌækt / (AmE), / ˈpleɪˌækt / (BrE)
to perform or pretend to act out a scenario, role, or character, often for practice, entertainment, or demonstration
to pretend or act out a role

Examples

The teacher asked the students to play-act a scene from the novel.
During therapy, patients may play-act difficult conversations to practice new skills.
They often play-act with costumes to make their stories more realistic.

Synset Examples

They play-act scenarios to prepare for real-life negotiations.
He decided to play-act as a superhero for the charity event.
The children love to play-act their favorite movie scenes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretend - to behave as if something is true when it is not
  • Synonym: to improvise - to create and perform spontaneously

Etymology

Formed from 'play' + 'act', first recorded in the late 19th century.
