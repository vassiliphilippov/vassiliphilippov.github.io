Wahhabism

1. NOUN

/ wɑˈhɑːbɪzəm / (AmE), / wəˈhɑːbɪzəm / (BrE)
A conservative and strict movement within Sunni Islam, originating in the 18th century and influential in Saudi Arabia.
conservative movement in Sunni Islam

Examples

Many religious scholars have debated the significance of Wahhabism in modern Saudi society.
The strict interpretation promoted by Wahhabism has influenced laws and social norms in the country.
His writings are often critical of Wahhabism and its role in Middle Eastern politics.

Synset Examples

Osama bin Laden and his followers practice Wahhabism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Salafism - a fundamentalist Islamic movement closely related to Wahhabism

Etymology

From the name of Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahhab (1703–1792), founder of the movement, plus -ism.

2. NOUN

/ wəˈhɑːbɪzəm / (AmE), / wəˈhæbɪzəm / (BrE)
a strict, conservative form of Sunni Islam, originating in the 18th century, that emphasizes a return to the earliest practices of the faith and is predominant in Saudi Arabia
conservative branch of Sunni Islam

Examples

Some critics argue that Wahhabism has contributed to religious intolerance in certain regions.
The kingdom's legal system is deeply influenced by Wahhabism.
He wrote a book that studies the origins and impact of Wahhabism in modern Islamic societies.

Synset Examples

Osama bin Laden and his followers practice Wahhabism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Salafism - a related conservative movement within Sunni Islam seeking a return to early traditions

Etymology

From the name of Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahhab (1703–1792), the movement's founder.
