botanical

1. ADJ

/ bəˈtænɪkəl / (AmE), / bəˈtænɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to plants or the scientific study of plants (botany)
relating to plants or botany

Examples

She visited the city's famous botanical gardens during her vacation.
Researchers are studying the botanical diversity of the forest.
The botanical illustration captured every detail of the rare flower.

Synset Examples

botanical garden

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botanic - alternative form meaning 'relating to plants or botany'

Etymology

From medieval Latin botanicus, from Greek botanikos (of herbs), from botanē (plant, herb).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02678963-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ bəˈtænɪkəl / (AmE), / bəˈtænɪkəl / (BrE)
a substance or drug obtained from parts of a plant, such as its bark, root, or leaves, especially used for medicinal purposes
plant-derived drug or substance

Examples

Many health supplements are made from botanical extracts found in rare plants.
The clinic specializes in treatments using traditional botanical medicines.
Researchers are studying a new botanical that may help lower blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herbal - a medicine made from plants

Etymology

From Latin botanicus (of herbs), via French botanique, relating to plants.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02879779-n
Synset POS: n
