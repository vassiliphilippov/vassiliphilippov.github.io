Branta canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈbræntə kəˈneɪdɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈbræntə kəˈneɪdənsɪs / (BrE)
A common greyish-brown wild goose of North America, known for its loud, trumpeting call; also known as the Canada goose.
species of North American wild goose

Examples

A flock of Branta canadensis was seen migrating south for the winter.
The park is home to many species, including the ubiquitous Branta canadensis.
Wildlife biologists are studying the nesting habits of Branta canadensis near urban lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canada goose - Common name for Branta canadensis, a North American wild goose

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, where 'Branta' is the genus for black geese and 'canadensis' means 'of Canada'.
