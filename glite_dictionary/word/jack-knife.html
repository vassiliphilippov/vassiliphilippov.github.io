jack-knife

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌnaɪf / (AmE), / ˈdʒækˌnaɪf / (BrE)
A large pocketknife with a folding blade that can be locked into position.
a folding pocketknife with a locking blade

Examples

He took his old jack-knife out of his pocket and began to whittle a stick.
The scout leader showed the children how to safely open a jack-knife.
Please remember to fold your jack-knife before putting it away.

Synset Examples

Be careful with that jack-knife while cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pocketknife - a small folding knife carried in the pocket

Etymology

From 'jack' (a generic term for a man or common object) + 'knife'.
