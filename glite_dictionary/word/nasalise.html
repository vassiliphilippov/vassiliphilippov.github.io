to nasalise

1. VERB

/ ˈneɪzəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈneɪzəlaɪz / (BrE)
To pronounce a sound with the velum lowered so that air escapes through the nose, giving the sound a nasal quality.
pronounce with a nasal quality

Examples

Some regional accents tend to nasalise certain vowels.
She tried not to nasalise her speech, but the cold made it difficult.
Linguists study how languages nasalise different sounds across the world.

Synset Examples

She nasalizes all her vowels

Related Terms

  • Antonym: denasalise - to pronounce a sound with the nose blocked, removing the nasal quality

Etymology

From French nasaliser, from Latin nasus (nose).

2. VERB

/ ˈneɪzəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈneɪzəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to pronounce speech sounds with nasal resonance by allowing air to escape through the nose; to speak in a nasal manner
speak with nasal sound

Examples

Some regional accents tend to nasalise certain vowels.
When she has a cold, she tends to nasalise her words more noticeably.
Actors must learn when to nasalise their speech for certain character roles.

Synset Examples

In this part of the country, people tend to nasalize

Related Terms

  • Antonym: denasalise - to make a sound less nasal

Etymology

From French 'nasaliser', from 'nasal' (nose-related) + '-ize'.

Synset POS: v
