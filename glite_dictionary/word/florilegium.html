florilegium

1. NOUN

/ ˌflɔːrəˈlɪdʒiəm / (AmE), / ˌflɒrɪˈliːdʒɪəm / (BrE)
a collection of short literary works such as poems, ballads, or extracts, especially with a focus on notable or beautiful passages
anthology of literary works

Examples

The museum displayed a rare florilegium containing poems from the Renaissance period.
She contributed a story to a florilegium dedicated to emerging writers.
A well-curated florilegium can introduce readers to a wide array of literary voices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthology - a published collection of poems or other pieces of writing

Etymology

From Latin *florilegium* (a gathering of flowers), from *flos* (flower) + *legere* (to gather).
