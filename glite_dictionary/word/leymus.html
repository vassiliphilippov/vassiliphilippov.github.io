Leymus

1. PROPN

/ ˈleɪ.məs / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.məs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial grasses commonly known as wild rye, found mainly in temperate regions.
genus of perennial grasses (wild rye)

Examples

Several species within the genus Leymus are used for soil erosion control.
The scientist collected samples of Leymus from the coastal dunes for analysis.
Some farmers plant Leymus because it is hardy and tolerates poor soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Elymus - a closely related genus of grasses, sometimes overlapping with Leymus in classification

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on the Greek word leymos meaning 'meadow'.
