pier

1. NOUN

/ pɪr / (AmE), / pɪə / (BrE)
a vertical support, usually a solid structure, that supports two adjacent spans of a bridge
bridge support column or pillar

Examples

The damaged pier weakened the overall stability of the bridge.
During construction, each pier must be precisely located to support the spans above.
Engineers inspected the concrete pier for signs of cracks or erosion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abutment - a structure supporting the end of a bridge span

Etymology

From Old French *pier* (stone), from Latin *petra* (rock).

2. NOUN

/ pɪr / (AmE), / pɪə / (BrE)
A vertical supporting structure, especially a solid section of a wall between two openings such as doors or windows.
vertical supporting structure in architecture

Examples

The arched doorway was flanked by a thick pier supporting the vaulted ceiling.
Between each pair of windows stood a slender pier that divided the light.
In classical architecture, a pier is often used to carry the load of heavy masonry arches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: column - a vertical, cylindrical supporting pillar

Etymology

From Old French *pierre* (stone), from Latin *petra* (rock).

3. NOUN

/ pɪr / (AmE), / pɪə / (BrE)
A structure built out from the shore into the water, supported by piles, used for docking boats and providing access to ships.
structure projecting into water for boats

Examples

We walked along the pier to watch the boats come in at sunset.
The fishermen tied their boat to the old wooden pier after a long day at sea.
Crowds gathered on the pier to celebrate the arrival of the new cruise ship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dock - an area of water for receiving ships next to the land
  • Synonym: wharf - a level quayside area to which a ship may be moored
  • Synonym: jetty - a narrow structure projecting into the water to protect or provide landing

Etymology

From Old French *pire*, from Latin *petra* (stone, rock).
