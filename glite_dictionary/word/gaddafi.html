Gaddafi

1. PROPN

/ ɡəˈdɑːfi / (AmE), / ɡəˈdæfi / (BrE)
Muammar Gaddafi (1942–2011), Libyan military officer and political leader who ruled Libya from 1969 until 2011 after leading a coup d’état.
Libyan leader (ruled 1969–2011)

Examples

Gaddafi ruled Libya for more than four decades before being overthrown in 2011.
International sanctions were imposed on Libya during Gaddafi's regime.
Many Libyans remember both the repression and modernization that occurred under Gaddafi.

Etymology

From Arabic معمر القذافي (Muʿammar al-Qaḏḏāfī), his full name.
