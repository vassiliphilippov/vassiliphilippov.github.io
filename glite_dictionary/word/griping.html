griping

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡraɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
(dated) Severe, sudden pain in the abdomen, especially in infants or children.
acute abdominal pain (often in infants)
archaic

Examples

The baby suffered from terrible griping after drinking the formula.
Doctors in the past often used herbal remedies to relieve griping in children.
The patient complained of sudden griping that caused him to double over in pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colic - severe, often fluctuating pain in the abdomen

Etymology

From Middle English gripen (to grasp or seize), referring to a gripping pain.
