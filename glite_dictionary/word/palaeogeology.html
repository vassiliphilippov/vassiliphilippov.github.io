palaeogeology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪli.oʊ.dʒiˈɑl.ə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌpæli.əʊ.dʒiˈɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
the branch of geology concerned with studying geologic features that were once at the surface of the Earth but are now buried beneath rock layers
study of buried ancient geologic features

Examples

Recent advances in palaeogeology have revealed ancient river systems hidden beneath the Sahara Desert.
She specializes in palaeogeology, focusing on the buried landscapes that shaped our planet's history.
The textbook includes a chapter on palaeogeology and how deep rock layers conceal evidence of ancient environments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: geology - the science that deals with Earth's physical structure and substance
  • Synonym: palaeontology - the study of fossilized remains of plants and animals

Etymology

From Greek palaios (ancient) + geology.
