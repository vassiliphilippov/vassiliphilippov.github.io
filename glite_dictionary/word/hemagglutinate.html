to hemagglutinate

1. VERB

/ ˌhiːməˈɡluːtɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈɡluːtɪneɪt / (BrE)
to cause the clumping together of red blood cells
cause red blood cells to clump
medical

Examples

Some viruses hemagglutinate red blood cells, causing clumps to form in the sample.
In laboratory tests, certain antibodies can hemagglutinate donor blood cells.
Scientists observed that the toxin did not hemagglutinate the cells under these conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agglutinate - to cause cells or particles to clump together

Etymology

From hema- (blood) + agglutinate (to glue together).
