Lythrum salicaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪθrəm sælɪˈkæriə / (AmE), / ˈlɪθrəm sælɪˈkeəriə / (BrE)
A marsh plant with tall spikes of purple flowers, native to Europe but widespread in eastern North America.
plant with purple flower spikes

Examples

The wetland was invaded by Lythrum salicaria, threatening native plant species.
Gardeners were warned not to plant Lythrum salicaria due to its invasiveness.
Researchers studied how Lythrum salicaria affects the ecosystem of the marsh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purple loosestrife - Common name for Lythrum salicaria, the same plant species

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Lythrum' from Greek 'lýthron' (blood), species 'salicaria' from Latin 'salix' (willow), referring to leaf shape.
