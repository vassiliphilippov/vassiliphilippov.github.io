isomerase

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈsoʊməˌreɪs / (AmE), / aɪˈsɒmərəz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the conversion of a compound into one of its isomers
enzyme converting a compound to its isomer
medical

Examples

The enzyme isomerase plays a crucial role in cellular metabolism.
Researchers discovered a new isomerase involved in the conversion of glucose to fructose.
Deficiency in a specific isomerase can lead to metabolic disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a substance produced by living organisms that accelerates chemical reactions

Etymology

From isomer + -ase, the standard suffix for enzymes.
