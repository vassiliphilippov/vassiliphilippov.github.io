gossiper

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːsəpər / (AmE), / ˈɡɒsɪpə / (BrE)
a person who habitually talks about the private affairs of others, often spreading rumors or personal information
person who often gossips

Examples

Everyone at the office knew that Mark was a notorious gossiper who loved to spread rumors.
Being called a gossiper hurt her feelings, but it made her reflect on her behavior.
The gossiper in the group always manages to find out everyone's secrets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - casual or unconstrained conversation about other people, typically involving details that are not confirmed as true

Etymology

From 'gossip' (originally meaning 'godparent, friend'), with the agentive suffix '-er'.
