crutch

1. NOUN

/ krʌtʃ / (AmE), / krʌtʃ / (BrE)
something that provides support or helps someone deal with a difficult situation, often used metaphorically for a thing relied on to cope with hardship or stress.
support or expedient relied on

Examples

For many people, television can become an emotional crutch during stressful times.
She used her busy schedule as a crutch to avoid dealing with her problems.
Relying on routine too much may serve as a comfortable crutch, but can hinder personal growth.

Synset Examples

he uses drugs as a psychological crutch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - something that serves as a foundation or aid
  • Synonym: prop - an object that supports or steadies

Etymology

From Old English 'crycc' meaning 'staff or stick'.

2. NOUN

/ krʌtʃ / (AmE), / krʌtʃ / (BrE)
a support made of wood or metal that fits under a person's armpit and is used to aid walking if they have difficulty using one or both legs
support used for walking by disabled people

Examples

After his surgery, Mark had to use crutches to walk around the house.
The nurse showed her how to properly adjust the height of the crutch.
She leaned heavily on her crutch as she crossed the parking lot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cane - a walking stick used for support while walking

Etymology

Old English 'crycce' (staff), from Proto-Germanic roots.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
