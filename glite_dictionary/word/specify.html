to specify

1. VERB

/ ˈspɛs.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈspɛs.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to determine or describe the essential qualities or characteristics of something
determine or describe essential qualities

Examples

The committee must specify what makes a successful project before awarding the grant.
The regulations specify the essential properties required for the material to be classified as safe.
Scientists attempt to specify the key characteristics that define a new species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to define - to state or describe exactly the nature, scope, or meaning of

Etymology

From Latin 'specificare', from 'specie' (a kind or type).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02617665-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈspɛs.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈspɛs.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to state or identify something clearly and definitely, often by listing details or conditions
to state or set clearly and definitely

Examples

Please specify the time and location of the meeting.
The technician did not specify which part needed replacement.
You must specify your dietary requirements when booking the event.

Synset Examples

fix the variables
specify the parameters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detail - to describe or list item by item
  • Antonym: to generalize - to make a general or broad statement

Etymology

From Latin 'specificare' (to mention specifically) from 'species' (kind, sort) and 'facere' (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00949109-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈspɛsɪfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈspɛsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to design for a particular purpose or destine for a specific role or function
designate or destine for a purpose

Examples

She was specified as the leader of the new research team.
The position was specified for someone with extensive management experience.
He was specified to lead the delegation at the international conference.

Synset Examples

She was intended to become the director

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appoint - to assign a job or role to someone

Etymology

6. VERB

/ ˈspɛs.ɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈspɛs.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to state something, especially as a condition in a contract or agreement, in a clear and exact way
state as a condition or requirement

Examples

The contract specifies that all payments must be made by the end of the month.
Her lease specifies that pets are not allowed in the apartment.
You must specify any dietary restrictions when booking the event.

Synset Examples

The will stipulates that she can live in the house for the rest of her life
The contract stipulates the dates of the payments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stipulate - to demand or specify a requirement, typically as part of an agreement
  • Degree Lower: to include - to make part of a whole or set, often in official terms
  • Degree Higher: mandate - to officially require or order something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *specificare*, from Latin *species* (appearance, kind).
