baldly

1. ADV

/ ˈbɔldli / (AmE), / ˈbɔːldli / (BrE)
in a way that is plain and direct, without trying to disguise unpleasant facts or soften the impact
plainly and directly, without disguise

Examples

He stated baldly that the company was losing money.
To put it baldly, we simply cannot afford the project.
She baldly described the failures without trying to justify them.

Synset Examples

this book is, to put it baldly, an uneven work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluntly - in a very direct way, without trying to be polite or considerate
  • Synonym: frankly - in an open, honest, and direct manner

Etymology

From 'bald' + '-ly'; 'bald' meaning 'bare, without adornment' (Old English 'bald').
