cloudberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaʊdˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈklaʊdˌbɛri / (BrE)
A creeping perennial plant native to northern regions, bearing edible yellow or orange berries.
creeping plant with yellow or orange berries

Examples

The arctic tundra is home to the delicious and rare cloudberry.
Locals gather cloudberries every summer for making jam and desserts.
The pale orange cloudberry is highly prized in Scandinavian cuisine.

Etymology

From 'cloud' + 'berry', possibly from Scandinavian influences (compare Norwegian 'multebær'); related to the cloud-like appearance of the berry clusters.
