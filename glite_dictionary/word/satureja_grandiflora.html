Satureja grandiflora

1. NOUN

/ ˌsætəˈriːə ɡrænˈdɪflərə / (AmE), / ˌsatjʊˈriːə ɡranˈdɪflərə / (BrE)
an aromatic herb with large pink flowers, native to southern and southeastern Europe, Anatolia, and northern Iran
aromatic herb with large pink flowers

Examples

The gardener planted Satureja grandiflora to add color and fragrance to the border.
Satureja grandiflora thrives in well-drained soils under full sun.
Cooks sometimes use Satureja grandiflora leaves to flavor traditional Mediterranean dishes.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Satureja' and species 'grandiflora' meaning 'large-flowered'.
