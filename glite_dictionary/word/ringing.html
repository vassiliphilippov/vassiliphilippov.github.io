ringing

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
The quality of being loud and resonant, often producing a deep, clear, echoing sound.
loud, resonant quality or sound

Examples

Her voice had a ringing clarity that captivated the audience.
The ringing of church bells echoed through the valley.
He gave a speech with ringing tones of conviction and purpose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonance - the quality in a sound of being deep, full, and reverberating

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the clear, resonant sound produced by a bell or similar object when struck
sound made by a bell or similar object

Examples

The ringing of the church bells could be heard all over the town.
She was startled by the sudden ringing of the phone late at night.
The ringing outside signaled the start of the celebration.

Synset Examples

the distinctive ring of the church bell
the ringing of the telephone
the tintinnabulation that so voluminously swells from the ringing and the dinging of the bells

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peal - a loud, prolonged ringing of bells
  • Synonym: chime - a musical sound made by a bell or a set of bells

Etymology

From the verb 'ring' (Middle English ringen), referring to making a resonant sound.
