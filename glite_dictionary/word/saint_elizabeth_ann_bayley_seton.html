Saint Elizabeth Ann Bayley Seton

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ɪˈlɪzəˌbɛθ æn ˈbeɪli ˈsiːtən / (AmE), / seɪnt ɪˈlɪzəbəθ æn ˈbeɪli ˈsiːtən / (BrE)
(1774–1821) The first person born in the United States to be canonized as a saint; she was a religious leader and founder of the first Catholic girls' school in the U.S.
first U.S.-born canonized saint

Examples

Many schools and churches across the United States are named after Saint Elizabeth Ann Bayley Seton.
The life of Saint Elizabeth Ann Bayley Seton continues to inspire countless American Catholics today.
In 1975, Saint Elizabeth Ann Bayley Seton was canonized by Pope Paul VI.
