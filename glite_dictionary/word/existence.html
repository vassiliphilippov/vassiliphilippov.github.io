existence

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
the state or fact of being real or living, or everything that has being
the state of being; all that exists

Examples

Scientists debate the origins of existence and the nature of the universe.
The ancient ruins are proof of a past civilization's existence.
She questioned the existence of life beyond our planet.

Synset Examples

they study the evolution of the universe
the biggest tree in existence

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonexistence - the state of not existing or not being real
  • Synonym: being - the state or quality of having existence

Etymology

From Latin existentia (being, existence), from existere (to arise, to exist).

2. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
The state of being real or having actual being; the fact or condition of having objective reality.
the state or fact of existing

Examples

Philosophers have long debated the meaning of human existence.
Some species are in danger of losing their existence due to habitat loss.
The law is still in existence, even though it is rarely enforced.

Synset Examples

a point of view gradually coming into being
laws in existence for centuries
he appeared on the face of the earth one day

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonexistence - the state of not existing; absence of existence
  • Synonym: being - the state or fact of existing; existence

Etymology

From Latin 'existentia' (being, existence), from 'existere' (to stand out, exist).
