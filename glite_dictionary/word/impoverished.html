impoverished

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɑvɚɪʃt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɒvərɪʃt / (BrE)
lacking sufficient money or resources; very poor.
very poor; lacking resources

Examples

Many impoverished families rely on food banks to meet their daily needs.
The impoverished village lacked access to clean water and basic healthcare.
After years of drought, the land became barren and impoverished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poor - lacking sufficient money; not rich
  • Degree Higher: destitute - extremely poor and lacking basic necessities

Etymology

From Old French 'empovriss-', from 'empovrir' (to impoverish), based on Latin 'pauper' (poor).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɑvɚɪʃt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɒvərɪʃt / (BrE)
Having lost all or most of one’s money; made very poor financially.
made poor; financially ruined

Examples

After years of bad investments, the once-prosperous family became impoverished.
The war left many communities impoverished and struggling to recover.
His impoverished state forced him to sell the family estate.

Synset Examples

the broken fortunes of the family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poor - having little or no money, not rich
  • Degree Higher: destitute - extremely poor, lacking the means to provide for oneself
  • Antonym: wealthy - having a great deal of money, resources, or assets

Etymology

From impoverish (verb), from Old French *empovriss-, based on povre (poor), from Latin *pauper* (poor).
