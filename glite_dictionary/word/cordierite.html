cordierite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrdiəˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdɪəraɪt / (BrE)
a blue mineral composed of magnesium, iron, aluminum, silicon, and oxygen, often used as a gemstone
a blue mineral used as a gemstone

Examples

The jeweler identified the stone as cordierite because of its distinctive blue hue.
Some geologists study cordierite to learn about the pressure and temperature conditions during rock formation.
Cordierite is sometimes chosen as an alternative to sapphire in fine jewelry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iolite - another name for the gemstone form of cordierite

Etymology

Named after French geologist Pierre Cordier (1777–1861).
