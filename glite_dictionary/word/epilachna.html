Epilachna

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈlæk.nə / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈlæk.nə / (BrE)
A genus of ladybugs native to Mexico and Central America whose larvae and adults feed on plants.
genus of plant-feeding ladybugs

Examples

Researchers studied the feeding habits of Epilachna to control crop damage.
Unlike most ladybugs, Epilachna species can cause harm to plants.
The farmer identified the insect as an Epilachna after noticing holes in the leaves.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'epi' (upon) and 'lachne' (soft hair or down).

A genus of ladybugs native to Mexico and Central America, whose larvae and adults feed on plants.
genus of plant-feeding ladybugs
medical

Examples

The Epilachna genus includes several species that are harmful to crops due to their feeding habits.
Unlike many ladybugs that eat pests, Epilachna beetles feed directly on plants.
Researchers found a new species within the Epilachna genus in Central America.

Related Terms

From New Latin, based on Greek epilakhno 'forgetful,' as used in scientific naming conventions.
