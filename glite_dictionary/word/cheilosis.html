cheilosis

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / kaɪˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disorder of the lips characterized by scaling and cracks at the corners of the mouth, often caused by a deficiency of riboflavin.
lip disorder with scaling and cracks
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with cheilosis after noticing cracks at the corners of her mouth.
Riboflavin supplements are often prescribed to treat cheilosis.
Persistent cheilosis may indicate an underlying nutritional deficiency.

Etymology

From Greek 'kheilos' meaning 'lip', combined with the medical suffix '-osis' indicating a pathological condition.
