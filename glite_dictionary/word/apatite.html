apatite

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpəˌtaɪt / (AmE), / ˈapətʌɪt / (BrE)
a common group of minerals consisting of calcium phosphate with fluorine, chlorine, or hydroxyl, used as a source of phosphorus
a mineral; calcium phosphate source

Examples

Geologists often look for apatite crystals as indicators of phosphate deposits.
The fertilizer industry extracts phosphorus from apatite found in sedimentary rock.
A sample containing apatite may have different colors due to its variable composition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphate - a salt or ester of phosphoric acid used as fertilizer or in industry

Etymology

From French *apatite*, from Greek *apatē* (deceit), from the mineral's variable appearance.
