mi

1. NOUN

/ maɪl / (AmE), / maɪl / (BrE)
An abbreviation for mile, a unit of length equal to 1,760 yards or 5,280 feet (1,609.344 meters).
abbreviation for mile (unit of length)

Examples

The city is located 15 mi north of the capital.
The road sign read 'Exit 22, 1 mi ahead.'
The hiking trail covers 12 mi through the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mile - a unit of length equal to 1,760 yards or 5,280 feet

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'mile', from Latin 'milia' (thousand, as in 'milia passuum', a thousand paces).

2. NOUN

/ mi / (AmE), / mi / (BrE)
the syllable used to name the third note of a major scale in solmization (solfège)
third note of major scale (solfège)

Examples

She sang the scale from mi up to do with perfect pitch.
On the piano, the note mi corresponds to E in the C major scale.
The children practiced the sequence: do, re, mi, fa, so.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: do - first note of the major scale (solfège)
  • Degree Lower: re - second note of the major scale (solfège)
  • Degree Higher: fa - fourth note of the major scale (solfège)
  • Degree Higher: so - fifth note of the major scale (solfège)

Etymology

From the Italian 'mi', from the first syllables of a Latin hymn used to teach solfège.

3. NOUN

/ maɪ / (AmE), / maɪ / (BrE)
A former British unit of length equal to 6,080 feet (1,853.184 meters), used especially in navigation; also known as a nautical mile.
former British unit of length (nautical mile)

Examples

The ship covered 200 mi before it reached the coast.
The old charts are marked in mi rather than kilometers.
One mi at sea was longer than a land mile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nautical mile - a unit of length used in navigation, equal to 1,852 meters
  • Antonym: statute mile - a unit of length equal to 5,280 feet (1,609.344 meters)

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'mile', from Latin 'milia' (thousand, as in 'mille passuum').
