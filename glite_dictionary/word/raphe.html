raphe

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪfi / (AmE), / ˈreɪfi / (BrE)
A ridge or seam-like line of tissue that marks the joining of two parts, especially in anatomy or botany.
ridge or seam between joined parts
medical

Examples

The surgeon pointed to the raphe where the two muscle tissues met.
In some plants, the raphe is visible along the seed's surface.
The medial raphe of the tongue is an important anatomical landmark.

Etymology

From Greek rhaphē (a seam, suture) via Latin.
