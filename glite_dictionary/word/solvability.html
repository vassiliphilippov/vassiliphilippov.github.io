solvability

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑːlvəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌsɒlvəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or condition of being able to be solved or resolved
ability of something to be solved

Examples

The solvability of the puzzle was questioned after hours of unsuccessful attempts.
Researchers are studying the solvability of certain complex mathematical problems.
Legal experts debated the solvability of the long-standing dispute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soluble - able to be solved or dissolved
  • Antonym: unsolvability - the quality of being impossible to solve

Etymology

From solve +‎ -ability, patterned after solvable +‎ -ity.
