muscadelle

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌskəˈdɛl / (AmE), / ˌmʌskəˈdɛl / (BrE)
A variety of white grape used in winemaking, especially in Bordeaux; also, a wine made from this grape.
a type of white wine grape or the wine from it

Examples

The dessert was served with a glass of muscadelle to enhance its sweetness.
French vineyards are famous for producing high-quality muscadelle wines.
Winemakers often blend muscadelle with other white grape varieties to create complex flavors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscat - a type of grape or wine; synonym in some contexts

Etymology

French, diminutive of 'muscat', a grape variety; ultimately from Greek 'moskos' (musk).
