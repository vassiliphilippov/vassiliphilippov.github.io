Iguanodontidae

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌɡwɑːnoʊˈdɒntɪdiː / (AmE), / ɪˌɡwɑːnəʊˈdɒntɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large herbivorous dinosaurs that includes the iguanodons, characterized by their beaked jaws and thumb spikes, which lived during the Late Jurassic and Early Cretaceous periods.
family of herbivorous dinosaurs (iguanodons)

Examples

Fossils of Iguanodontidae have been discovered on several continents, indicating their wide distribution.
The distinctive thumb spikes of Iguanodontidae may have been used for defense or foraging.
Paleontologists still debate the diversity within the family Iguanodontidae and its evolutionary relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iguanodon - a genus of large herbivorous dinosaur within Iguanodontidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Iguanodon + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌɡwɑːnəˈdɑːntɪdiː / (AmE), / ɪˌɡwɑːnəˈdɒntɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of herbivorous dinosaurs known for their characteristic thumb spikes, including the iguanodons, from the Early Cretaceous period.
family of herbivorous dinosaurs (iguanodons)

Examples

The Iguanodontidae thrived during the Early Cretaceous period across Europe and North America.
Scientists discovered new fossils belonging to the Iguanodontidae in Mongolia.
Members of the Iguanodontidae family are known for their distinctive thumb spikes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iguanodon - a genus of large herbivorous dinosaurs with a beak-like mouth and thumb spike

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus name Iguanodon + suffix -idae used for zoological families.
