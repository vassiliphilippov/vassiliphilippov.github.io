Hydrocharidaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊˌkærɪˈdeɪsi.i / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəʊˌkarɪˈdeɪsɪ.iː / (BrE)
a family of simple, almost stemless freshwater aquatic plants found widely around the world
family of aquatic freshwater plants

Examples

Several species in the Hydrocharidaceae family are used in aquariums for their decorative floating leaves.
The Hydrocharidaceae are found on every continent except Antarctica in freshwater habitats.
Scientists studied the reproductive habits of Hydrocharidaceae to understand the spread of invasive aquatic plants.

Etymology

From New Latin Hydrocharidaceae, from genus Hydrocharis plus the botanical suffix -aceae (denoting family).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊkəˌrɪdəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəʊkəˌrɪdəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of simple, nearly stemless, freshwater aquatic plants widely distributed across the world
family of aquatic plants

Examples

The Hydrocharidaceae are found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers worldwide.
Members of the Hydrocharidaceae family, such as Elodea and frogbit, play an important role in aquatic ecosystems.
Botanists have identified over a dozen genera within the Hydrocharidaceae.

Etymology

From New Latin Hydrocharidaceae, based on genus Hydrocharis and the family suffix -aceae.
