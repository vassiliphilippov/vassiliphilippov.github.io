plaguey

1. ADJ

/ ˈpleɪɡi / (AmE), / ˈpleɪɡi / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) likely to spread and cause an epidemic; pestilential.
likely to spread disease
archaic

Examples

During the hot, damp summer, plaguey fevers spread rapidly through the village.
Doctors in the 18th century often feared plaguey infections during outbreaks.
The town was quarantined to prevent the plaguey illness from spreading further.

Synset Examples

a pestilential malignancy in the air
plaguey fevers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pestilential - likely to cause pestilence or epidemic disease
  • Antonym: benign - not harmful or likely to spread (medical context)

Etymology

From 'plague' + '-y', referring to the infectious plague.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpleɪɡi / (AmE), / ˈpleɪɡi / (BrE)
Irritating or causing annoyance; troublesome.
causing irritation or annoyance
archaic

Examples

That plaguey door keeps sticking every time I try to open it.
She couldn't stand the plaguey buzzing of the flies in the kitchen.
His plaguey questions made the meeting drag on far longer than expected.

  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

/ ˈpleɪɡi / (AmE), / ˈpleɪɡi / (BrE)
(archaic) In an annoying or troublesome manner; extremely or excessively (often used to intensify annoyance or difficulty).
annoyingly troublesome (archaic intensifier)
archaic

Examples

It's plaguey hard to get this old lock open in winter.
The weather turned plaguey cold last night, even for December.
He found his new boots plaguey uncomfortable on long walks.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoyingly - in a manner that causes irritation
  • Synonym: troublesomely - in a troublesome or difficult manner

Etymology

Formed from 'plague' with the suffix '-y', used as an intensifier in the 17th–19th centuries.
