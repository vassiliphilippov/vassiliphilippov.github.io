downmarket

1. ADJ

/ ˈdaʊnˌmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
Intended to appeal to or be affordable for people with less money or lower social status; of inferior quality or cheap.
intended for lower-income consumers

Examples

The company released a downmarket smartphone to attract budget-conscious buyers.
Many luxury brands avoid producing downmarket versions to protect their image.
They moved to a more downmarket neighborhood after his job loss.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upmarket - intended for wealthy consumers; high quality

Etymology

From 'down' (lower) + 'market' (economic sphere), indicating movement toward the lower end of the market.
