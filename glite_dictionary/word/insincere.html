insincere

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.sɪnˈsɪr / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪnˈsɪə / (BrE)
not expressing genuine feelings or intentions; pretending or not being honest in what one says or does.
not genuine or honest

Examples

She apologized, but her tone was obviously insincere.
Their compliments sounded insincere to everyone at the table.
He gave an insincere promise to help, which he quickly forgot.

Synset Examples

a charming but thoroughly insincere woman
their praise was extravagant and insincere

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sincere - genuine; saying what one really means or feels

Etymology

From Latin 'insincerus', meaning not pure or genuine.
