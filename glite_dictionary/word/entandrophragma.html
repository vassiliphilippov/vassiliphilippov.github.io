Entandrophragma

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˌtændroʊˈfrægmə / (AmE), / ɛnˌtændrəʊˈfrægmə / (BrE)
A genus of large tropical African trees in the mahogany family, valued for their hardwood timber.
genus of African mahogany trees

Examples

The timber from Entandrophragma is highly prized for making quality furniture and flooring.
Several species of Entandrophragma are commonly referred to as African mahogany.
Research on Entandrophragma helps in the conservation of valuable hardwood forests.

Etymology

From Greek 'en' (in), 'andros' (man), 'phragma' (fence), from the structure of the staminal tube.

2. NOUN

/ ɛnˌtændroʊˈfræɡmə / (AmE), / ɛnˌtandrəˈfræɡmə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical African trees in the mahogany family, known for their valuable timber including sapele and utile.
genus of tropical mahogany trees

Examples

The Entandrophragma genus includes several species prized for their high-quality timber.
Sapele, from Entandrophragma, is often used for furniture and veneers.
Researchers study growth patterns of Entandrophragma trees to promote sustainable forestry in Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - hard reddish-brown timber from several tropical trees

Etymology

From Greek entandros (with prominent male) + -phragma (fence or barrier), referring to botanical features of the genus.
