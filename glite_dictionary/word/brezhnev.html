Brezhnev

1. PROPN

/ brɛʒ.nɛf / (AmE), / brɛʒ.nɛf / (BrE)
Leonid Brezhnev (1906–1982), Soviet statesman; General Secretary of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union from 1964 to 1982 and President of the Soviet Union from 1977 to 1982.
Soviet leader (1906–1982)

Examples

During the 1970s, Brezhnev was a central figure in shaping Soviet foreign policy.
The era of Brezhnev is often remembered for political stagnation in the Soviet Union.
Historians continue to debate the legacy of Brezhnev and his leadership style.

Etymology

Transliteration of Russian Брежнев (Brezhnev), a surname.
