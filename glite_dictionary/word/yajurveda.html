Yajurveda

1. NOUN

/ jɑːˈdʒʊrˌveɪdə / (AmE), / jɑːˈdʒʊəˌveɪdə / (BrE)
one of the four Vedas, consisting of a collection of sacrificial formulas and prayers used in Hindu rituals
Hindu sacred text of sacrificial formulas

Examples

The Yajurveda is widely studied by scholars interested in the history of Hindu rituals.
Priests recited passages from the Yajurveda during important ceremonies.
He explained the meaning of several mantras found in the Yajurveda.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rigveda - the oldest of the four Vedas, a Hindu sacred text of hymns
  • Synonym: Sama Veda - a collection of melodies and chants, one of the Vedas
  • Synonym: Atharvaveda - the fourth Veda, containing spells and incantations

Etymology

From Sanskrit यजुर्वेद (yajurveda), from yajus (sacrificial formula) + veda (knowledge).
