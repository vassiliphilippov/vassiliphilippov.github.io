oxen

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑksən / (AmE), / ˈɒksən / (BrE)
plural of ox; domesticated cattle, especially those used as draft animals, regardless of sex or age.
plural of ox; domesticated cattle

Examples

A team of oxen pulled the heavy wagon across the field.
Farmers often used oxen to plow their land before the advent of tractors.
The oxen rested in the shade after a long day's work.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ox - a domesticated bovine animal, especially a castrated male used as a draft animal

Etymology

Plural of 'ox'; from Old English 'oxan', plural of 'oxa'.
