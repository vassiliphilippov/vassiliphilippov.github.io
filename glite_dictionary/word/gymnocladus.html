Gymnocladus

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˈnɑklədəs / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈnɒklədəs / (BrE)
a small genus of deciduous trees native to China and the United States, known for their paniculate flowers and thick, pulpy pods
genus of deciduous trees with pulpy pods

Examples

The Kentucky coffeetree is a species within the genus Gymnocladus.
Several botanists studied the growth patterns of Gymnocladus trees in North America.
Gymnocladus produces large pods that are easily recognizable among local flora.

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) and 'klados' (branch), referring to the tree's bare branches in winter.
