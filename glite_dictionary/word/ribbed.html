ribbed

1. ADJ

/ rɪbd / (AmE), / rɪbd / (BrE)
(of a surface or material) having raised lines, ridges, or a rib-like texture.
having rib-like ridges or texture

Examples

The ribbed surface of the sweater gave it a unique appearance.
She chose a ribbed glass vase for its elegant texture.
Many yoga mats are made with a ribbed pattern to prevent slipping.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface, free from roughness or projections
  • Synonym: corrugated - shaped into alternate ridges and grooves

Etymology

From rib + -ed, meaning 'provided with ribs or ridges'.

2. ADJ

/ rɪbd / (AmE), / rɪbd / (BrE)
having raised lines, ridges, or a structure strengthened or decorated with ribs.
having raised lines or ribs

Examples

She chose a ribbed sweater for extra warmth on the chilly evening.
The architect designed the ceiling with a ribbed vault for decorative effect.
The glass had a ribbed surface to provide better grip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridged - having long raised strips

Etymology

From 'rib' + -ed, denoting presence of rib-like features.

WordNet ID: oewn-02028753-a
Synset POS: a
