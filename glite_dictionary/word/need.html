need

1. NOUN

/ nid / (AmE), / niːd / (BrE)
a thing that is necessary or required but lacking; something essential that is wanted or demanded
something necessary or essential

Examples

Basic healthcare is a fundamental need for every human being.
The shelter tries to address the needs of the homeless community.
Food, water, and shelter are the most urgent needs after a natural disaster.

Synset Examples

he had sufficient means to meet his simple needs
I tried to supply his wants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: want - something desired but not necessarily required

Etymology

Old English nēad, nēd (distress, necessity, compulsion).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09390888-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ nid / (AmE), / niːd / (BrE)
a situation in which something is required or wanted very much; a state of lacking something necessary or desirable
a state of requiring something

Examples

There is a growing need for affordable housing in urban areas.
His need for companionship led him to adopt a pet.
The organization tries to help those in need during the winter months.

Synset Examples

she satisfied his need for affection
God has no need of men to accomplish His work
there is a demand for jobs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: want - a desire for something not essential
  • Antonym: luxury - something desirable but not essential

Etymology

From Old English nied, nēd (necessity, compulsion), related to Old High German not (distress, need).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14472592-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ nid / (AmE), / niːd / (BrE)
a state of extreme poverty or destitution; the condition of lacking the basic necessities of life
state of poverty or destitution

Examples

Many children in the area live in need and struggle to get enough food.
Aid organizations are working to alleviate need in rural communities affected by drought.
In times of war, need often becomes widespread due to displacement and loss of resources.

Synset Examples

their indigence appalled him
a general state of need exists among the homeless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigence - extreme poverty; destitution
  • Synonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14517300-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ nid / (AmE), / niːd / (BrE)
to require something because it is necessary, useful, or appropriate
require as necessary or appropriate

Examples

If you need anything, please let me know.
He needed help with his homework, so I offered to explain the problem.
This recipe needs two cups of flour and one egg.

Synset Examples

It takes nerve to do what she did
success usually requires hard work
This job asks a lot of patience and skill
This position demands a lot of personal sacrifice
This dinner calls for a spectacular dessert
This intervention does not postulate a patient's consent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to require - to need something for a particular purpose
  • Degree Lower: want - to wish for something, often less strictly necessary than 'need'

Etymology

From Old English *nēodian* ('to require, wish for'), related to *nēad* ('necessity, compulsion').
