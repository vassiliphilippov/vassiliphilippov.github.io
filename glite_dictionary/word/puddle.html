puddle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌdəl / (AmE), / ˈpʌd.əl / (BrE)
a small pool of liquid, especially of rainwater, on the ground
small pool of liquid on the ground

Examples

Children like to splash in a puddle after it rains.
He accidentally stepped into a puddle and soaked his shoes.
A small puddle formed under the leaking pipe in the kitchen.

Synset Examples

there were puddles of muddy water in the road after the rain
the body lay in a pool of blood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pool - a small body of still water

Etymology

From Middle English 'pudel', probably a diminutive of 'pud', meaning a small pool.
