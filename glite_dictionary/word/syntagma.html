syntagma

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈtæɡmə / (AmE), / sɪnˈtæɡmə / (BrE)
A sequence of words forming a syntactic unit within a sentence, functioning as a component of a larger grammatical structure.
syntactic unit in a sentence

Examples

In structural linguistics, a syntagma refers to any sequence of words that forms a functional unit.
The prepositional phrase in the sentence is a syntagma contributing to its overall meaning.
Linguists analyze sentences by breaking them down into their component syntagmas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constituent - A word or group of words functioning as a unit in a syntactic structure

Etymology

From Greek 'syntagma' (something arranged together), from 'syn-' (together) + 'tagma' (that which is arranged).
