Mevacor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛvəˌkɔr / (AmE), / ˈmiːvəˌkɔː / (BrE)
A trademark for a prescription drug taken orally to lower blood cholesterol levels, especially when dietary measures have not been effective.
cholesterol-lowering drug (trade name)
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed Mevacor to help lower my cholesterol after lifestyle changes didn't work.
Patients taking Mevacor should follow their doctor's advice regarding diet and exercise.
The effectiveness of Mevacor has been supported by several clinical studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lovastatin - the generic name for Mevacor, a cholesterol-lowering drug
