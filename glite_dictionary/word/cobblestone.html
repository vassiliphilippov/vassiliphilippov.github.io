cobblestone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑbəlˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɒbəlˌstəʊn / (BrE)
A naturally rounded stone, often rectangular with a curved top, used in the past for paving roads.
rounded paving stone used for roads

Examples

The old city center is famous for its narrow cobblestone streets.
Horse-drawn carriages still travel along the cobblestone road by the river.
She tripped over a loose cobblestone while walking through the historic district.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: setts - rectangular quarried stones used for paving streets, similar but distinct from natural cobblestones

Etymology

From 'cobble' (a round stone) + 'stone'; 'cobble' from Middle English, perhaps from Low German.

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑbəlˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɒbəlstəʊn / (BrE)
to pave a surface, especially a road or path, with cobblestones
pave with cobblestones

Examples

The workers began to cobblestone the old city square, restoring its historic charm.
They plan to cobblestone the new pedestrian walkway next month.
Many European towns were originally cobblestoned, giving the streets a distinctive appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pave - to cover a surface, especially a road, with a firm material
