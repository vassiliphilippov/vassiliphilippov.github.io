lasting

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an object or quality) retained and not shed; continuing to exist or remain in place over a period of time.
remaining; not shed

Examples

The lasting leaves on the old oak tree remain through winter.
Some mushrooms have lasting gills that do not decay quickly after maturity.
Evergreen plants are known for their lasting foliage throughout the year.

Synset Examples

persistent leaves remain attached past maturity
the persistent gills of fishes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persistent - continuing to exist or endure over a long period

Etymology

Present participle of 'last', from Old English 'lǣstan' (to continue, endure).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
Continuing to exist or have an effect for a long time; enduring without significant change.
continuing for a long time; enduring

Examples

Their friendship developed into a lasting bond that endured throughout their lives.
The country is seeking a lasting solution to the ongoing conflict.
The artist created works with a lasting impact on the world of painting.

Synset Examples

a lasting relationship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enduring - lasting for a long time
  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period

Etymology

From Middle English 'lasten' (to last) + -ing.
