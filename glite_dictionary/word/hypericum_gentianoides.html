Hypericum gentianoides

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɛrɪkəm ˌdʒɛntʃiəˈnɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / hɪˈpɛrɪkəm ˌdʒɛntɪəˈnɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
An annual North American plant with wiry stems, minute scalelike leaves, and small yellow flowers.
annual North American weed with small yellow flowers

Examples

Hypericum gentianoides is often found in sandy soils and grasslands throughout eastern North America.
The small yellow flowers of Hypericum gentianoides appear in late summer.
Botanists studied the habitat preferences of Hypericum gentianoides during their fieldwork.

Etymology

From the genus name Hypericum (ancient Greek ὑπέρεικος, hyperikos) and species gentianoides, meaning resembling Gentiana.
