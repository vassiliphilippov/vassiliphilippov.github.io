Louis Sullivan

1. PROPN

/ ˈluː.i ˈsʌl.ɪ.vən / (AmE), / ˈluː.i ˈsʌl.ɪ.vən / (BrE)
(1856–1924) United States architect noted for his pioneering steel-framed skyscrapers and for coining the phrase ‘form follows function’.
American architect; coined 'form follows function'

Examples

Louis Sullivan is often called the 'father of skyscrapers' for his innovative designs in Chicago.
The famous phrase 'form follows function' was first articulated by Louis Sullivan in his architectural philosophy.
Many architects today are influenced by the ideas of Louis Sullivan regarding building design.
