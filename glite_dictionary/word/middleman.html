middleman

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪd.əlˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl.mæn / (BrE)
(historical) In minstrel shows, the male performer positioned in the center of the line, who engages the other performers in dialogue.
central performer in a minstrel show
archaic

Examples

The middleman would lead the jokes and banter during the minstrel act.
As the middleman, he kept the audience entertained with witty exchanges.
Historic minstrel shows often featured a middleman flanked by interlocutors.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪd.əlˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl.mæn / (BrE)
a person or company that buys goods in large quantities and resells them to retailers or other merchants, rather than to the ultimate consumers
wholesaler or intermediary in trade

Examples

The company eliminated the middleman to sell products directly to customers.
Many farmers rely on a middleman to distribute their produce to grocery stores.
Acting as a middleman, she bought goods in bulk and resold them to small businesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intermediary - a person or organization acting as a link between people or groups
  • Antonym: retailer - a person or business that sells goods to the public
  • Synonym: wholesaler - a person or company selling goods in large quantities at low prices to retailers

Etymology

Formed from 'middle' + 'man', indicating one in an intermediate position.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdəlˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈmɪdəlˌman / (BrE)
a person who acts as an intermediary, especially one who arranges deals or helps others gain access or assistance by connecting them with the right people
person who arranges deals or acts as intermediary

Examples

He acted as a middleman to help negotiate the contract between the two companies.
Large companies often prefer to deal directly with suppliers, cutting out the middleman.
If you need an introduction, I can put you in touch with a reliable middleman.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intermediary - a person who acts as a link between people in order to try to bring about an agreement
  • Synonym: go-between - a person who acts as an intermediary between two people or groups
  • Antonym: client - a person who receives assistance or services, as opposed to the provider

Etymology

From 'middle' + 'man'; in use since the 17th century to mean an intermediary.
