Cinchona pubescens

1. NOUN

/ sɪŋˈkoʊ.nə pjuːˈbɛs.ənz / (AmE), / sɪŋˈkəʊ.nə pjuːˈbɛs.ɛnz / (BrE)
A species of small tree native to Ecuador and Peru, known for its large glossy leaves, fragrant pink flowers, and medicinal bark.
species of medicinal tree from South America

Examples

The bark of Cinchona pubescens has historically been used to treat malaria.
Botanists study Cinchona pubescens for its important alkaloids.
The hillside was covered with blooming Cinchona pubescens during spring.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Cinchona and the species epithet pubescens, meaning 'downy' in Latin.
