Epacridaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːpækrɪˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌiːpakrɪˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
a former family of Australasian shrubs and small trees, now usually included in the family Ericaceae
former family of Australasian shrubs/small trees

Examples

During his expedition, the botanist encountered several rare members of the Epacridaceae in the Tasmanian wilderness.
Many plants formerly classified in the Epacridaceae are now considered part of the Ericaceae family.
The gardens featured a diverse selection of Epacridaceae species native to Australia and New Zealand.

Etymology

From genus name Epacris + Latin plural family suffix -aceae, used in botanical nomenclature.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛpəkraɪˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌiːpakrɪˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of Australasian shrubs or small trees, formerly recognized in botanical classification, now usually included in the Ericaceae (heath family)
family of Australasian shrubs or small trees

Examples

Several species in the Epacridaceae are adapted to survive in Australia's nutrient-poor soils.
Botanists once considered Epacridaceae distinct from other families, but now include them in Ericaceae.
Field guides sometimes still list plants under the older family name Epacridaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ericaceae - the family of flowering plants known as the heath or heather family

Etymology

From New Latin *Epacridaceae*, based on the type genus *Epacris*.
