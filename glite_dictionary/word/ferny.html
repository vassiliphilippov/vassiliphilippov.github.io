ferny

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɜrni / (AmE), / ˈfɜːni / (BrE)
Resembling or characteristic of ferns, especially in the shape or appearance of leaves.
resembling ferns in appearance

Examples

The garden was filled with ferny undergrowth that provided lush greenery.
Her sketches showed ferny patterns winding across the page, delicate and intricate.
Through the window, we could see ferny shadows dancing on the living room wall.

Synset Examples

the ferny shadows of locust leaves

Etymology

From 'fern' + '-y' (adjective-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00210982-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɝːni / (AmE), / ˈfɜːni / (BrE)
(of land or an area) covered with or abounding in ferns.
covered with or full of ferns

Examples

The cottage was hidden among ferny hills and winding paths.
A stream trickled quietly through the ferny glade.
The hikers paused to rest in the cool, ferny shade of the valley.

Synset Examples

the ferny undergrowth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leafy - having many leaves or foliage

Etymology

Formed from 'fern' + '-y', denoting 'full of' or 'characterized by'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00210671-a
Synset POS: a
