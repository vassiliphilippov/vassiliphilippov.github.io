Agastache

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡæstəki / (AmE), / əˈɡastəki / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic herbaceous plants in the mint family, commonly known as giant hyssop or Mexican hyssop, valued for their colorful flower spikes and fragrant leaves.
genus of aromatic herbs (giant hyssop)

Examples

The Agastache in our garden attracts bees and butterflies all summer long.
Many herbalists use Agastache for its fragrant leaves and edible flowers.
Planting Agastache can add vibrant color and scent to a flower border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyssop - an aromatic plant used in cooking and medicine

Etymology

From Greek 'agan' (very much) and 'stachys' (an ear of grain, spike), referring to the flower spike.

2. NOUN

/ əˈɡæstəki; ˌæɡəˈstɑːki / (AmE), / əˈɡæstəki; ˌæɡəˈstɑːki / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic herbaceous plants in the mint family, known as giant hyssop or Mexican hyssop, native to North America and eastern Asia.
a genus of aromatic herbs (giant hyssop)

Examples

The Agastache in her garden attracted dozens of hummingbirds throughout the summer.
Many herbalists use Agastache as a flavoring and medicinal plant.
Agastache hybrids are popular in ornamental landscapes due to their colorful blooms and fragrant leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant hyssop - common name for species of Agastache

Etymology

From Greek 'agathos' (good) and 'stachys' (ear of grain, spike), referring to the plant's appearance.
