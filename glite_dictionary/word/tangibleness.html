tangibleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæn.dʒə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtan.dʒɪ.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be perceived by touch; the condition of being tangible
the quality of being tangible

Examples

The tangibleness of the sculpture made it more engaging for the blind visitors.
Philosophers often debate the tangibleness of abstract concepts like love or justice.
She doubted the tangibleness of his promises, demanding something she could actually see or touch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangibility - the quality of being tangible
  • Antonym: intangibility - the quality of being intangible; not able to be touched

Etymology

From 'tangible' + '-ness', ultimately from Latin 'tangibilis' (that can be touched).
