to peptise

1. VERB

/ ˈpɛp.taɪz / (AmE), / ˈpɛp.tʌɪz / (BrE)
to disperse a substance in a medium so that it forms a colloidal solution
disperse into a colloidal state

Examples

The chemist attempted to peptise the protein for further study.
Some clays must be peptised before they can be used in industrial processes.
Heating the solution can help to peptise certain colloidal particles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to peptize - disperse a substance in a medium to form a colloidal solution (US spelling)

Etymology

From 'peptone' (a soluble protein derivative) + '-ise', modeled after 'peptize' (US spelling).
