compartmentalisation

1. NOUN

/ kəmˌpɑrt.mɛn.təl.əˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kəmˌpɑːt.mɛn.tə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a psychological process in which conflicting thoughts or feelings are separated into distinct categories, resulting in a mild state of dissociation
a psychological state of mild dissociation

Examples

Therapists often discuss compartmentalisation as a coping strategy for stressful emotions.
His compartmentalisation of work and personal life helped him manage overwhelming feelings.
Excessive compartmentalisation may prevent someone from addressing unresolved trauma.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dissociation - a mental process of disconnecting from thoughts or feelings

Etymology

From 'compartmentalise', based on French 'compartiment' (section) and suffix '-ation'.

2. NOUN

/ kəmˌpɑːrtˌmentl̩əˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəmˌpɑːtməntəlʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of dividing something into separate sections, categories, or compartments, often to organize or manage complexity
division into separate sections or categories

Examples

The compartmentalisation of tasks helped the team work more efficiently.
Mental compartmentalisation allows some people to separate work and personal life.
The scientist criticized the compartmentalisation of knowledge into narrow fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to classify - to arrange in categories based on shared qualities
  • Synonym: categorization - the process of placing things into categories
  • Antonym: integration - the process of combining parts into a whole

Etymology

From 'compartment' + '-alisation', modeled after 'compartmentalize', ultimately from French 'compartiment'.
