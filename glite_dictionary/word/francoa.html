Francoa

1. NOUN

/ frænˈkoʊ.ə / (AmE), / fraŋˈkəʊ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial evergreen herbs with white or pink flowers, native to Chile.
genus of Chilean flowering herbs

Examples

The Francoa is prized for its delicate pink blossoms in many ornamental gardens.
Several species of Francoa thrive in the mild, moist conditions of coastal Chile.
Botanists are studying the pollination methods of Francoa in their native habitats.

Etymology

Named in honor of Spanish botanist Francisco Franco (not to be confused with the 20th-century Spanish dictator).

2. NOUN

/ frænˈkoʊ.ə / (AmE), / franˈkəʊ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial evergreen herbs with white or pink flowers, native to Chile.
genus of evergreen herbs from Chile

Examples

The Francoa is prized by gardeners for its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
Several species of Francoa thrive in mild, frost-free climates.
Botanists discovered a new variant of Francoa in the mountains of central Chile.

Etymology

Named after Francisco Franco, an 18th-century Spanish botanist.
