prentice

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛn.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɛn.tɪs / (BrE)
an apprentice; a person who works for an expert to learn a trade, especially in historical contexts
apprentice (archaic or literary)
archaic

Examples

The young prentice worked long hours to master the craft of his trade.
In Shakespearean times, a prentice would live with his master during his training.
The guild accepted a new prentice after a rigorous selection process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apprentice - a person who learns a trade from a skilled employer

Etymology

Old contraction of 'apprentice', from Old French 'aprentiz' (learner).
