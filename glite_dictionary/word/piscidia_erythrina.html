Piscidia erythrina

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈsɪdiə ɛrɪˈθraɪnə / (AmE), / pɪˈsɪdiə ɛrɪˈθrʌɪnə / (BrE)
a small tree native to the West Indies and Florida, having large pinnate leaves, panicles of red-striped purple to white flowers, and curly winged seedpods, known for yielding fish poisons
a tropical tree yielding fish poisons

Examples

The Piscidia erythrina tree is valued for its beautiful winged seedpods.
Local fishermen once used extracts from Piscidia erythrina as a fish poison.
In botanical gardens, Piscidia erythrina is sometimes grown for its decorative flowers.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name 'Piscidia', referring to 'fish-killing', and species name from Greek 'erythros' meaning 'red'.
