Blennius pholis

1. NOUN

/ blɛˈniː.əs ˈfoʊ.lɪs / (AmE), / blɪˈniː.əs ˈfɒ.lɪs / (BrE)
A species of scaleless blenny found in European coastal waters, also known as the shanny.
species of European scaleless blenny

Examples

The Blennius pholis is commonly found in tide pools along the western coasts of Europe.
Marine biologists study the Blennius pholis to learn more about coastal fish habitats.
Unlike many other fish, the Blennius pholis does not have scales covering its body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shanny - common name for Blennius pholis
  • Degree Higher: blenny - general term for fishes in the family Blenniidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'blennius' (blenny) and Greek 'pholis' (a scale-less fish).
