Pithecellobium dulce

1. NOUN

/ pɪθəˌsɛloʊˈbiːəm ˈdʊlsi / (AmE), / pɪθəˌsɛlɪˈbəʊbiːəm ˈdʊlsi / (BrE)
A thorny tropical American tree with yellow flowers, sickle-shaped edible pods, and wood, gum, and dye of commercial value.
tropical tree with edible pods and valuable timber

Examples

The Pithecellobium dulce tree is often planted for shade and its edible pods.
Farmers value Pithecellobium dulce for its timber and gum production.
Children enjoy the sweet pulp found inside the pods of Pithecellobium dulce.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'pithēkos' (ape) and 'lobos' (pod), referring to the shape of the fruit; 'dulce' from Latin for 'sweet'.
