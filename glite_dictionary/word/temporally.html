temporally

1. ADV

/ ˈtɛmpɚəli / (AmE), / ˈtɛmp(ə)rəli / (BrE)
In relation to time or the order of events; with reference to temporal sequence.
in relation to time or temporal order

Examples

The data were analyzed temporally to determine the sequence of events.
These changes occur temporally before any physical symptoms appear.
The movie's plot is structured temporally, moving from past to present.

Synset Examples

temporally processed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spatially - in relation to space or spatial order

Etymology

From Late Latin 'temporalis' (of time), from Latin 'tempus' (time).
