shortener

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrtənər / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtənə / (BrE)
a person or thing that makes something shorter in length, duration, or extent
agent that makes something shorter

Examples

The constant delays acted as a shortener of the project's timeline.
Exposure to harsh chemicals is a known shortener of fabric durability.
Stress is often cited as a shortener of human lifespan.

Synset Examples

alcohol is a great shortener of life

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lengthener - a person or thing that makes something longer

Etymology

From the verb 'shorten' + -er (agent suffix), formed in English.
