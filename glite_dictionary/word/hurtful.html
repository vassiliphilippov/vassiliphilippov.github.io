hurtful

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɜrtfəl / (AmE), / ˈhɜːtf(ə)l / (BrE)
causing pain, harm, or distress; likely to hurt someone, especially emotionally
causing pain or distress

Examples

His hurtful remarks left her feeling upset for days.
The teacher warned the students not to use hurtful language in class.
Sometimes, even small jokes can be unintentionally hurtful.

Synset Examples

her hurtful unconsidered words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmful - causing or likely to cause harm
  • Synonym: painful - causing physical or emotional pain
  • Antonym: helpful - giving help or assistance

Etymology

From Middle English *hurte* (injury) + *-ful* (full of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɜrtfəl / (AmE), / ˈhɜːtfəl / (BrE)
causing harm, injury, or damage to living things
causing harm or injury

Examples

Certain chemicals in cleaning products can be hurtful to children and pets.
He realized too late that his quick solution was hurtful to the plants in the garden.
We must avoid introducing any hurtful substances into the river ecosystem.

Synset Examples

deleterious chemical additives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmful - causing or likely to cause harm
  • Antonym: beneficial - resulting in good; advantageous

Etymology

From hurt + -ful, formed in Middle English.
