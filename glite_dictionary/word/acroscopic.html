acroscopic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæk.rəˈskɑ.pɪk / (AmE), / ˌak.rəˈskɒ.pɪk / (BrE)
(in botany and biology) facing or situated on the side toward the apex of an organ or structure.
facing toward the apex (esp. in botany)
medical

Examples

The leaflets on the branch are acroscopic, pointing toward the tip of the stem.
In ferns, the sori are sometimes located on the acroscopic side of the pinna.
Researchers noted the difference in spore arrangement between the basiscopic and acroscopic sides.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: basiscopic - facing or on the side toward the base (especially in botany)

Etymology

From Greek 'akros' (top, tip) + 'skopein' (to look at).
