lengthy

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛŋθi / (AmE), / ˈlɛŋθi / (BrE)
lasting for a long time, especially longer than is necessary; prolonged or tediously long.
long in duration; prolonged

Examples

The meeting turned out to be quite a lengthy affair, lasting over three hours.
She wrote a lengthy letter explaining her side of the story.
Despite his lengthy explanation, many people remained unconvinced.

Synset Examples

a drawn-out argument
an extended discussion
a lengthy visit from her mother-in-law
a prolonged and bitter struggle
protracted negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prolonged - continuing for a long time or longer than usual
  • Synonym: protracted - lasting for a long time or made to last longer than necessary
  • Antonym: brief - lasting only a short time

Etymology

Formed from 'length' + '-y' (adjective suffix), first attested in the 18th century.
