Xylopia

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈloʊ.pi.ə / (AmE), / zaɪˈləʊ.pi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical evergreen trees and shrubs, chiefly found in Africa.
genus of tropical African trees/shrubs

Examples

The botanist discovered several species of Xylopia in the West African rainforest.
Many traditional medicines use seeds from Xylopia trees.
Researchers studied the ecological role of Xylopia in tropical forest regeneration.

Etymology

From Greek xylon (wood) + opion (little), referring to the small woody fruits.

2. NOUN

/ zaɪˈloʊ.pi.ə / (AmE), / zaɪˈləʊ.pi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical evergreen trees or shrubs, chiefly native to Africa, belonging to the family Annonaceae.
genus of tropical evergreen trees or shrubs

Examples

Several species of Xylopia are valued for their aromatic bark.
Researchers discovered a new Xylopia tree in the rainforests of West Africa.
The fruits of Xylopia are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Etymology

From Greek xylon (wood) + ops (appearance), referring to the wood-like characteristics.
