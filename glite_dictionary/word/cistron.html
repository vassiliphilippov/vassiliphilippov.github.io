cistron

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstrɑn / (AmE), / ˈsɪstrɒn / (BrE)
(Genetics) A segment of DNA that corresponds to a functional unit of heredity and is involved in producing a specific polypeptide chain, often including both coding and non-coding regions.
segment of DNA as a unit of heredity
medical

Examples

The scientist identified a single cistron responsible for the production of the enzyme.
Mutations within a cistron can lead to defective proteins and disease.
Each cistron can include both exons and introns within the DNA sequence.

Synset Examples

genes were formerly called factors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gene - unit of heredity in DNA

Etymology

From French cistron, coined in the 1950s by combining -on (as in gene) with cistr-, from 'cis-', referring to genetic complementation tests.
