Gnaphalium sylvaticum

1. NOUN

/ næˈfæl.i.əm sɪlˈvæt.ɪ.kəm / (AmE), / næˈfæl.i.əm sɪlˈvæt.ɪ.kəm / (BrE)
A weedy perennial plant of northern temperate regions, with woolly leaves and dirty white flowers borne in leafy spikes.
a woolly-leaved perennial plant species

Examples

Gnaphalium sylvaticum thrives in the moist meadows and open woods of northern Europe.
Her research focused on the medicinal properties of Gnaphalium sylvaticum.
We identified several patches of Gnaphalium sylvaticum during our botany field trip.

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Gnaphalium' from Greek 'gnaphalon' (lock of wool), and 'sylvaticum' meaning 'of the woods'.
