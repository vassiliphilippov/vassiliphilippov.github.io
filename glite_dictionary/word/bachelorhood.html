bachelorhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætʃlərˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈbætʃələhʊd / (BrE)
the state or period of being a bachelor; the time in a man's life before he is married
state of being an unmarried man

Examples

He enjoyed the freedom and independence of bachelorhood before deciding to settle down.
His messy apartment was a clear sign of prolonged bachelorhood.
Many men reflect on their bachelorhood with both nostalgia and relief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: singlehood - the state of being single or unmarried

Etymology

From 'bachelor' (Middle English, from Old French 'bacheler') + '-hood' (state or condition).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbætʃ.lɚ.hʊd / (AmE), / ˈbætʃ.lə.hʊd / (BrE)
the state or period of being an unmarried man
state of being an unmarried man

Examples

He enjoyed his years of bachelorhood before deciding to settle down.
Some people value the freedom that bachelorhood offers them.
His apartment reflected his long period of bachelorhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: singlehood - the state of being single, especially not married or in a relationship
  • Antonym: marriage - the state of being married

Etymology

From 'bachelor' (from Old French 'bacheler') + '-hood' (state or condition).
