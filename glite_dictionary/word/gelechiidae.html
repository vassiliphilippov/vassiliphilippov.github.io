Gelechiidae

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˌlɛkiˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / dʒɛlˌɛkɪˈiːdiː / (BrE)
A large family of small moths, many of which are significant agricultural pests.
family of small moths (many are pests)

Examples

Some species within the Gelechiidae family can cause extensive damage to crops.
Researchers are studying ways to control Gelechiidae infestations in agricultural regions.
The Gelechiidae are also known as twirler moths due to their larval habits.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Gelechia.

2. NOUN

/ dʒəˌlɛkiˈɪdieɪ / (AmE), / dʒiːˌlɛkiˈɪdɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of small moths, many of whose species are considered significant agricultural pests
family of small moths, many are pests

Examples

Several species in the Gelechiidae family can cause severe damage to crops like cotton and potatoes.
Entomologists are studying ways to control Gelechiidae infestations in greenhouses.
The Gelechiidae are sometimes called 'twirler moths' due to the peculiar movement of their larvae.

Etymology

From the New Latin genus name *Gelechia*, combined with the standard zoological suffix *-idae* for animal families.
