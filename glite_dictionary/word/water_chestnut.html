water chestnut

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtər ˌtʃɛsˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌtʃɛs.nʌt / (BrE)
the crisp, white, bulbous tuber of a Chinese aquatic plant, eaten as a vegetable, especially in Asian cuisine
edible tuber used in cooking

Examples

She added sliced water chestnut to the stir-fry for a pleasant crunch.
Fresh water chestnut is often used in Chinese dumplings and salads.
The grocery store stocks canned water chestnut in the Asian foods aisle.
