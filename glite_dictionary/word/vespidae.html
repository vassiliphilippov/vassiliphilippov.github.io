Vespidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛspɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈvɛspɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of wasps in the order Hymenoptera, including yellow jackets, hornets, and mason wasps
family of wasps including hornets and yellow jackets

Examples

Many species in the Vespidae family construct paper nests using chewed plant fibers.
Both hornets and yellow jackets are classified as members of the Vespidae family.
Scientists study Vespidae to better understand social behavior in insects.

Etymology

From Latin 'vespa' (wasp), with suffix '-idae' denoting a zoological family.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛspɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈvɛspɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of wasps in the order Hymenoptera, including yellowjackets, hornets, and mason wasps
family of wasps including yellowjackets and hornets
medical

Examples

The Vespidae family includes some of the most recognizable social wasps, such as hornets and yellowjackets.
Entomologists study Vespidae to learn about the complex social behavior of wasps.
Certain varieties within Vespidae play important ecological roles as both predators and pollinators.

Etymology

From Latin 'vespa' (wasp) + '-idae', a zoological suffix denoting a family.
