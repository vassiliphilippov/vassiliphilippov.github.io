drollery

1. NOUN

/ ˈdroʊləri / (AmE), / ˈdrəʊləri / (BrE)
an amusing or whimsical remark or jest, often with a quaint or odd quality
an amusing or whimsical jest

Examples

The author's drollery brought a smile to everyone's face during the reading.
Her stories were full of drollery that made even the sternest listeners laugh.
I appreciated the drollery in the speaker's otherwise serious presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jest - a joke or witty remark
  • Antonym: seriousness - a lack of humor or jest

Etymology

From French *drôlerie* (joke, whimsy), from *drôle* (funny), possibly from Middle Dutch *drolle* (goblin, imp).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdroʊl.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈdrəʊ.lə.ri / (BrE)
an amusing or comic incident or series of incidents; something that provokes laughter or amusement
amusing or comic incident

Examples

The group's drollery kept the audience laughing throughout the evening.
His letters were often filled with drollery that brightened his friends' days.
A moment of unexpected drollery relieved the tension in the courtroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comedy - entertainment consisting of jokes or satirical sketches
  • Synonym: farce - a comic dramatic work using buffoonery and horseplay

Etymology

From Old French *drollerie* (comic entertainment), from *drolle* (imp, jester).
