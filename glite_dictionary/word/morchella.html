Morchella

1. NOUN

/ mɔrˈkɛlə / (AmE), / mɔːˈkɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of edible fungi called morels, known for their honeycomb appearance and prized in cooking.
genus of edible fungi: morel

Examples

The chef sourced fresh Morchella mushrooms for the spring menu.
Researchers are studying the lifecycle of Morchella to improve cultivation methods.
Foragers often search the woods in April hoping to find wild Morchella.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morel - an edible mushroom with a honeycomb cap; member of Morchella genus

Etymology

From Latin 'morchella', a kind of mushroom.
