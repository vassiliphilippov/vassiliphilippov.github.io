scorn

1. NOUN

/ skɔrn / (AmE), / skɔːn / (BrE)
A strong feeling that someone or something is worthless or deserving of contempt; lack of respect combined with intense dislike.
strong contempt or disrespect

Examples

His words were met with scorn from the audience.
She looked at the broken vase with obvious scorn.
The proposal was dismissed with scorn by the committee.

Synset Examples

he was held in contempt
the despite in which outsiders were held is legendary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contempt - the feeling that something is worthless or beneath consideration
  • Antonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration for someone or something

Etymology

From Old French *escarn* (mockery, derision), from Vulgar Latin *escarnium*.

2. NOUN

/ skɔrn / (AmE), / skɔːn / (BrE)
open contempt or disdain for someone or something considered unworthy; lack of respect shown openly
open contempt or disdain

Examples

He spoke about the proposal with obvious scorn in his voice.

The critic's review was full of scorn for the new film.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contempt - the feeling that a person or a thing is beneath consideration or respect
  • Antonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration for someone or something

Etymology

3. VERB

/ skɔrn / (AmE), / skɔːn / (BrE)
to refuse or reject someone or something with contempt or disdain
reject with contempt or disdain

Examples

The board scorned his proposal, calling it impractical.
She scorned the offer of help and insisted on managing alone.
After much thought, he scorned the idea of collaboration.

Synset Examples

She spurned his advances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spurn - to reject with disdain or contempt
  • Antonym: to accept - to receive or agree to something offered

Etymology

From Old French *escarnir* (to mock), from Vulgar Latin *excarnire*, meaning to make fun of.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ skɔrn / (AmE), / skɔːn / (BrE)
to feel or express contempt or disdain for someone or something; to reject as unworthy or inferior
feel or show contempt; reject with disdain

Examples

She scorned the idea of buying secondhand clothes, believing only in new fashion.
The critics scorned his latest book as unoriginal and dull.
He scorns anyone who doesn't share his political views.

Synset Examples

He despises the people he has to work for
The professor scorns the students who don't catch on immediately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to despise - to feel a strong dislike or lack of respect for someone or something
  • Antonym: to admire - to regard someone or something with respect or approval

Etymology

From Old French escarn (mockery, derision), from Vulgar Latin *escarnum.
