mesenteric

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛsənˈtɛrɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛsɛnˈtɛrɪk / (BrE)
relating to the mesentery, a fold of tissue that attaches the intestines to the abdominal wall and holds them in place.
relating to the mesentery
medical

Examples

The surgeon examined the mesenteric vessels during the procedure.
Inflammation of the mesenteric lymph nodes can cause abdominal pain in children.
A blockage in the mesenteric arteries can lead to life-threatening conditions.

Etymology

From Greek mesenterion (middle intestine), from meso- (middle) + enteron (intestine).
