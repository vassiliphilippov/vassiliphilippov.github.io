spitefully

1. ADV

/ ˈspaɪtfəli / (AmE), / ˈspaɪtfəli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows or is motivated by spite; with the intention of hurting, offending, or upsetting someone.
in a malicious or spiteful manner

Examples

She spitefully ignored his invitation, even though she really wanted to attend the party.
He spitefully spread rumors about his former friend after their argument.
The review was written so spitefully that it seemed more personal than professional.

Synset Examples

pray for them that despitefully use us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maliciously - in a way that shows a desire to harm others
  • Antonym: kindly - in a friendly, generous, or considerate manner

Etymology

From 'spiteful' + suffix '-ly'. 'Spite' from Old French 'despit', from Latin 'despectus' (a looking down on).

2. ADV

/ ˈspaɪt.fəli / (AmE), / ˈspaɪt.fəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows or is motivated by spite; with the intention of hurting, annoying, or offending someone.
in a spiteful manner

Examples

She spoke spitefully to her former friend, making sure her words would hurt.
He spitefully ignored all invitations to the event after being left out before.
The review was written spitefully, with no attempt to be fair or constructive.

Synset Examples

he answered his accusers spitefully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maliciously - in a way that shows a desire to harm or upset someone
  • Antonym: kindly - in a way that shows kindness

Etymology

From 'spiteful' + '-ly'. 'Spiteful' from 'spite' (malice).

Synset POS: r
