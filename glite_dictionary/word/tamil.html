Tamil

1. ADJ

/ ˈtæmɪl / (AmE), / ˈtamɪl / (BrE)
relating to the Tamil language, its speakers, or their culture
relating to Tamil language or people

Examples

Many Tamil films are produced each year in Chennai.
She is studying Tamil cultural traditions for her thesis.
He teaches Tamil language classes to children every weekend.

Synset Examples

Tamil agglutinative phrases

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tamilian - a person from Tamil Nadu or of Tamil descent

Etymology

From Tamil *tamiḻ* (the name of the language and people).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæməl / (AmE), / ˈtamɪl / (BrE)
a Dravidian language spoken in southern India and Sri Lanka, and by Tamil communities worldwide
a Dravidian language of southern India and Sri Lanka

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Tamil.
The novel was originally written in Tamil before being translated into several languages.
At home, we speak Tamil to preserve our cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Telugu - another major Dravidian language spoken in southern India
  • Synonym: Kannada - a Dravidian language spoken in Karnataka, India
  • Antonym: Hindi - an Indo-Aryan language spoken in northern India

Etymology

From Tamil *tamiḻ* (the name of the language).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06992975-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtæmɪl, təˈmil / (AmE), / ˈtæmɪl, ˈtʌmɪl / (BrE)
a member of a people of southern India and Sri Lanka, primarily speaking the Tamil language
a person from a Dravidian ethnic group in India and Sri Lanka

Examples

Many Tamils in Sri Lanka continue to celebrate traditional festivals.
The Tamil population has a rich and ancient culture.
He grew up in a community of Tamil immigrants in London.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Sinhalese - a member of the majority ethnic group of Sri Lanka

Etymology

From Tamil *tamiḻ* (the name of the language and people).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09694878-n
Synset POS: n
