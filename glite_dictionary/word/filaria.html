filaria

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɛriə / (AmE), / fɪˈlɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A slender, threadlike parasitic roundworm that lives in the blood and tissues of vertebrate animals, often transmitted by biting insects.
parasitic roundworm transmitted by insects
medical

Examples

Doctors identified a filaria infection in the patient's lymphatic system.
Mosquitoes can transmit filaria larvae to humans during a bite.
The microscope revealed the slender body of a filaria among the blood cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nematode - a type of roundworm, especially as a member of the phylum Nematoda

Etymology

From Latin 'filum' (thread) + '-aria' (connected with), referring to its threadlike appearance.

2. NOUN

/ fɪˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / fɪˈlɛəriə / (BrE)
A European plant naturalized in southwestern United States and Mexico, with reddish stems, fernlike leaves, and reddish-lavender flowers, often grown for forage.
forage plant with reddish stems and flowers

Examples

The farmer planted filaria in his fields to use as animal forage.
In southwestern states, filaria has adapted well to the local climate.
The filaria blooms in late spring, adding a splash of red and lavender to the field.
