inherency

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhɪrənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪərənsɪ / (BrE)
the quality or state of being inherent; the condition of being a permanent, essential, or characteristic attribute of something
the state of being inherent

Examples

The inherency of risk in any investment cannot be underestimated.
Some philosophers debate the inherency of moral values in human society.
The inherency of cultural differences presents challenges and opportunities for diplomacy.

Synset Examples

the inherence of polysemy in human language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inherence - the state of inhering; intrinsic existence or quality
  • Antonym: extrinsicality - the state of being extrinsic or not inherent

Etymology

From Latin *inhaerens* (adhering to), from *inhaerere* (to be closely connected with).
