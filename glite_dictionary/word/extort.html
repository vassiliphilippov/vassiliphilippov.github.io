to extort

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɔrt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɔːt / (BrE)
to obtain something, especially money, from someone by using force, threats, or intimidation
obtain by force or threats

Examples

The gang tried to extort money from local businesses.
He was arrested for attempting to extort a confession through threats.
They used incriminating photos to extort favors from the politician.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackmail - to obtain money from someone by threatening to reveal damaging information
  • Synonym: to coerce - to force someone to do something against their will

Etymology

From Latin extorquere, from ex- (out) + torquere (to twist).

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɔrt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɔːt / (BrE)
to obtain something, especially money, by using threats, force, or other forms of intimidation
obtain by threats or force

Examples

The gang tried to extort a large sum of money from the businessman.
She was arrested for attempting to extort confidential information from her former employer.
He denied any attempt to extort favors by threatening legal action.

Synset Examples

They extorted money from the executive by threatening to reveal his past to the company boss
They squeezed money from the owner of the business by threatening him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackmail - the act of demanding payment by threatening to reveal damaging information
  • Synonym: to coerce - to force someone to do something by threats or pressure

Etymology

From Latin extortus, past participle of extorquere (to wrench out).
