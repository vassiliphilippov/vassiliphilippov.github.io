Eriophorum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛriˈɑfərəm / (AmE), / ˌɛriˈɒfərəm / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the sedge family, commonly known as cotton grass, found in boggy areas and characterized by fluffy, cotton-like seed heads.
a genus of plants known as cotton grass

Examples

Eriophorum is often found growing in wet, marshy environments across the Northern Hemisphere.
The soft, white tufts of Eriophorum can easily be mistaken for cotton from a distance.
Botanists studied the habitat requirements of various Eriophorum species in the peat bog.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton grass - common name for Eriophorum plants

Etymology

From Greek erion (wool) + phorós (bearing), referring to the woolly appearance of the seed heads.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛriˈɑːfərəm / (AmE), / ˌɛriˈɒfərəm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial, grass-like plants in the sedge family, known as cotton grass, characterized by tufted, fluffy, white seed heads found in wetlands and bogs.
genus of grass-like plants (cotton grass)

Examples

The bog was dotted with patches of Eriophorum swaying in the breeze.
Many birds nest in areas thick with Eriophorum plants, which provide shelter and food.
Scientists collected samples of Eriophorum to study its adaptation to wetland environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton grass - common name for plants in the genus Eriophorum

Etymology

From Greek 'erion' (wool) + 'phoros' (bearing), referring to the woolly appearance of the plant.
