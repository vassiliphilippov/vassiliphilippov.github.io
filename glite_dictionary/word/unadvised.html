unadvised

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.ədˈvaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ədˈvaɪzd / (BrE)
done without careful thought, consideration, or advice; heedless or rash
without careful thought or advice

Examples

It would be unadvised to invest so much money without proper research.
Their unadvised decision led to a series of unexpected problems.
He took the unadvised step of quitting his job without another offer.

Synset Examples

ill-advised efforts
it would be ill-advised to accept the offer
took the unadvised measure of going public with the accusations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ill-advised - done without wise or prudent judgment
  • Synonym: reckless - without thinking or caring about the consequences

Etymology

From un- (not) + advised (considered or counseled).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.ədˈvaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ədˈvaɪzd / (BrE)
not having been given advice or information; unaware due to lack of guidance.
not having received advice or guidance

Examples

The patient made an unadvised decision without consulting a doctor.
Entering the building was an unadvised move given the hazardous conditions.
He was unadvised of the possible risks before signing the contract.

Synset Examples

a defendant unadvised of her legal rights

Related Terms

  • Antonym: advised - having received information or counsel

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'advised' (from advise, meaning to inform or counsel).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01312481-s
Synset POS: s
