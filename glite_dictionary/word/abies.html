Abies

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.bi.iːz / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.bi.iːz / (BrE)
A genus of coniferous trees known as the true firs, characterized by needle-like leaves and upright cones.
genus of true fir trees

Examples

The forest contained several majestic Abies species, contributing to the rich diversity of evergreens.
Many Christmas trees are cultivated from Abies, particularly the noble and balsam firs.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Abies in different climate zones.

Etymology

From Latin 'Abies', meaning 'fir tree'.
