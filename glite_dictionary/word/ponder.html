to ponder

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑːndər / (AmE), / ˈpɒndə / (BrE)
to think about something carefully and deeply, especially for a prolonged time
think deeply about something

Examples

She likes to ponder big questions about life and existence late at night.
He sat by the window, quietly pondering the decision he had to make.
After the meeting, I pondered his words for hours before responding.

Synset Examples

I mulled over the events of the afternoon
philosophers have speculated on the question of God for thousands of years
The scientist must stop to observe and start to excogitate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to contemplate - to think deeply or consider carefully
  • Synonym: to reflect - to think deeply or carefully about something
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something

Etymology

From Latin 'ponderare' (to weigh, consider), from 'pondus' (weight).
