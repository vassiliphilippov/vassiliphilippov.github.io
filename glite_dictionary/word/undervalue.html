to undervalue

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈvæljuː / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈvæljuː / (BrE)
to assign a value to something that is lower than its actual worth
give too low a value

Examples

Many investors tend to undervalue the potential of emerging technologies.
You shouldn't undervalue your skills when negotiating for a new job.
The antique dealer realized he had undervalued the vase after discovering its true origin.

Synset Examples

Don't underestimate the value of this heirloom-you may sell it at a good price

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to overvalue - to assign too high a value to something
  • Synonym: underestimate - to estimate something as being less important or smaller than it is

Etymology

From under- (insufficient) + value, modeled after similar formations (18th century).

2. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈvælju / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈvæljuː / (BrE)
(of a currency, asset, etc.) to decrease in value; to depreciate over time.
to decrease in value

Examples

Investment in the region fell as the local currency continued to undervalue against the dollar.
If assets undervalue rapidly, it can signal trouble in the market.
The stock began to undervalue shortly after the disappointing earnings report.

Synset Examples

The dollar depreciated again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depreciate - to decrease in value over time

Etymology

From under- (below) + value, indicating worth below an expected level.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00315821-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈvælju / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈvaljuː / (BrE)
to rate or esteem something or someone as being less important or less valuable than they really are
regard as less valuable or important

Examples

Many people tend to undervalue their own achievements.
She realized too late that she had undervalued her employees' dedication.
It's a mistake to undervalue the importance of mental health.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to overvalue - to rate or esteem too highly
  • Synonym: to depreciate - to reduce the value or worth of something
  • Antonym: to appreciate - to recognize the full worth of something

Etymology

Prefix 'under-' (below, beneath) + 'value', from Latin 'valere' (to be worth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00696632-v
Synset POS: v
