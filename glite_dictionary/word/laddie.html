laddie

1. NOUN

/ ˈlædi / (AmE), / ˈlædi / (BrE)
A boy or young man, used especially as a term of endearment or in Scottish or northern English contexts.
boy or young man (esp. Scottish usage)

Examples

The old man smiled kindly at the young laddie who had helped him cross the street.
He's a bright laddie, always eager to learn new things at school.
'Be careful out there, laddie,' the fisherman called to his son as he set off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lad - a boy or young man
  • Antonym: lass - a girl or young woman, often in Scottish contexts

Etymology

Scottish variant of 'lad', meaning boy or young man.
