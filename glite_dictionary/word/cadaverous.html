cadaverous

1. ADJ

/ kəˈdævərəs / (AmE), / kəˈdævərəs / (BrE)
having the appearance of a corpse; very pale, thin, or gaunt
corpse-like in appearance

Examples

After weeks of illness, his cadaverous face alarmed his friends.
She wore makeup to conceal her cadaverous pallor at the party.
The refugees looked cadaverous after days without food.

Synset Examples

we had long anticipated his cadaverous end

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corpse-like - resembling a dead body
  • Antonym: lively - full of life and energy

Etymology

From Latin 'cadaverosus' (pertaining to a corpse), from 'cadaver' (corpse).

2. ADJ

/ kəˈdæv.ɚ.əs / (AmE), / kəˈdæv.ər.əs / (BrE)
very thin and pale, especially because of illness, hunger, or cold; resembling a corpse.
very thin and pale (corpse-like)

Examples

After weeks at sea with little food, the sailors' faces looked cadaverous.
The patient was so cadaverous that the doctors feared for his life.
Her cadaverous appearance shocked everyone at the reunion.

Synset Examples

a nightmare population of gaunt men and skeletal boys
eyes were haggard and cavernous
small pinched faces
kept life in his wasted frame only by grim concentration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaunt - very thin and bony, especially because of illness or hunger
  • Synonym: emaciated - abnormally thin or weak, especially because of illness or lack of food
  • Antonym: plump - having a full and rounded shape

Etymology

From Latin 'cadaverosus', from 'cadaver' (corpse).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00992194-s
Synset POS: s
