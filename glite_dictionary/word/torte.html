torte

1. NOUN

/ tɔrt / (AmE), / tɔːt / (BrE)
A rich cake, often layered and typically covered with cream and fruit or nuts, originally from Austria.
rich layered cake, often with cream or fruit

Examples

He ordered a slice of chocolate torte for dessert at the café.
The bakery is famous for its raspberry cream torte.
She baked a classic Viennese torte to impress her guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cake - a sweet baked dessert, typically made from flour, sugar, and eggs

Etymology

From German 'Torte', itself from Italian 'torta' (cake, tart).
