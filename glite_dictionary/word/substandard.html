substandard

1. ADJ

/ sʌbˈstændɚd / (AmE), / sʌbˈstændəd / (BrE)
below the usual or required standard in quality or performance
below required standard

Examples

The inspector reported that the apartment's wiring was substandard and posed a safety risk.
The company was fined for producing substandard goods that did not meet safety regulations.
Many families are forced to live in substandard housing due to high rents.

Synset Examples

substandard housing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inferior - of lesser quality or value
  • Antonym: adequate - satisfactory or acceptable in quality or quantity

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (below) + 'standard' (from Old French 'estandard', meaning a norm or measure).
