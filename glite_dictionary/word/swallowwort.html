swallowwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɑloʊˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈswɒləʊˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A tropical herb with orange-red flowers and seed pods resembling a swallow's outspread wings, considered a weed in many tropical regions.
tropical herbaceous plant; a weed with red flowers

Examples

The invasive swallowwort has spread rapidly across the local fields.
Gardeners find it difficult to remove swallowwort once it takes root in the soil.
The unique pods of the swallowwort inspired its name due to their resemblance to a bird in flight.

Etymology

From 'swallow' (the bird) and 'wort' (Old English for plant), referring to the pod's resemblance to a swallow's wings.

2. NOUN

/ ˈswɑːloʊˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈswɒləʊˌwɔːt / (BrE)
A perennial herbaceous plant with branched woody stems and bright yellow flowers, especially of the genus Vincetoxicum or related genera.
perennial herb with yellow flowers

Examples

The fields were dotted with swallowwort blooming brightly in early summer.
Gardeners often mistake swallowwort for other wildflowers due to its yellow blossoms.
Some species of swallowwort can spread aggressively and become invasive.

Etymology

From 'swallow' (the bird) + 'wort' (Old English for plant or herb), possibly relating to folk beliefs about medicinal uses or blooming season.
