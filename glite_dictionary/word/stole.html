stole

1. NOUN

/ stoʊl / (AmE), / stəʊl / (BrE)
a long, wide scarf or shawl worn loosely over the shoulders, especially by women
a wide scarf or shawl for women

Examples

She draped a silk stole over her evening gown for the gala.
A fox-fur stole was a fashionable accessory in the 1920s.
Her mother lent her a delicate lace stole for the wedding.

Etymology

From Latin stola (a long robe), via Old French and late Latin.
