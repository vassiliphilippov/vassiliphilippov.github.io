pre-emptor

1. NOUN

/ priˈɛmptər / (AmE), / priˈɛmptə / (BrE)
a person who acquires land or property by preemption, especially under certain legal provisions giving priority rights
one who acquires land by preemption

Examples

The pre-emptor filed his claim before the government opened the land to the public.
As a pre-emptor, he was entitled to purchase the property at a reduced rate.
Historical records often mention pre-emptors settling on frontier lands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preemptor - variant spelling and synonym for pre-emptor
  • Synonym: preemption - the act or right of acquiring something before others

Etymology

From pre-empt + -or, modeled on Latin agent nouns and influenced by legal terminology.

2. NOUN

/ priˈɛmptər / (AmE), / priˈɛmptə / (BrE)
A player in the card game bridge who makes a preemptive bid to disrupt the opponents' bidding.
bridge player making a preemptive bid

Examples

As the pre-emptor, she opened with a weak three-bid in hearts to block her opponents.
The pre-emptor hoped his bold bid would disrupt the opposing team's communication.
Experienced players can often identify a pre-emptor by their aggressive early bids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preemptive bid - an intentionally high bid in bridge made to hinder opponents

Etymology

From 'pre-empt' (to forestall), based on 'preemptive' used in contract bridge.
