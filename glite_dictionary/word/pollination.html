pollination

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑlɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of transferring pollen from the anther (male part) of a flower to the stigma (female part), enabling fertilization in plants
transfer of pollen for plant fertilization

Examples

Bees play a crucial role in the pollination of many food crops.
Without successful pollination, fruit and seeds would not develop on the plant.
Wind can also assist in the pollination of certain types of grasses and trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertilization - fusion of male and female gametes in plants or animals
  • Degree Lower: self-pollination - pollination within the same flower or plant
  • Degree Higher: cross-pollination - pollination involving pollen from a different plant

Etymology

From Latin 'pollen' (fine flour, dust) + English suffix '-ation'.
