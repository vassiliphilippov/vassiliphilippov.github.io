Eadwig

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛd.wɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈiːd.wɪɡ / (BrE)
(c. 940–959) King of England from 955 to 959, whose rule was opposed in Northumbria in favor of his brother Edgar.
King of England (955–959)

Examples

King Eadwig was crowned at a young age, but his reign was marked by internal conflict.
The division of the kingdom under Eadwig and his brother Edgar illustrates the unstable politics of early medieval England.
Historians often debate the reasons for Northumbria's rejection of Eadwig in favor of Edgar.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Edgar - King of England, brother and successor to Eadwig

Etymology

Old English name combining 'ead' (wealth, fortune) and 'wig' (war, battle).
