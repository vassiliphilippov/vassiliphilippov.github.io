to petrify

1. VERB

/ ˈpɛtrəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɛtrɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make someone so frightened or shocked that they are unable to move or think; to stun or paralyze with fear or extreme emotion
to paralyze with fear or shock

Examples

The sudden appearance of the snake petrified him with fear.
She was so petrified during the thunderstorm that she couldn't move.
His loud scream petrified everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

The horror petrified his feelings
Fear petrified her thinking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stun - to shock or surprise someone so that they cannot react immediately
  • Synonym: to paralyze - to make someone unable to move or act, especially from fear or shock

Etymology

From Greek *petra* (rock) + Latin *-ficare* (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈpɛtrɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɛtrɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make something rigid, fixed, or unable to change, especially by making it set in a conventional or unchanging pattern
make rigid or fixed in pattern

Examples

Old traditions can petrify a society, preventing progress and adaptation to new circumstances.
Excessive bureaucracy tends to petrify innovation within large organizations.
She warned that complacency would petrify the team's creative spirit.

Synset Examples

rigidify the training schedule
ossified teaching methods
slogans petrify our thinking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ossify - to become or make something rigid and unwilling to change
  • Synonym: to rigidify - to make or become stiff or inflexible

Etymology

From Greek 'petra' (rock), via Latin 'petrificare' (to turn into stone).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00419035-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈpɛtrɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɛtrɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to change or convert (organic matter) into stone or a stony substance, especially by a natural process
change (something) into stone

Examples

The ancient forest was gradually petrified over millions of years.
Scientists discovered a petrified log embedded in the cliff.
Mineral-rich water can slowly petrify wood, preserving its external structure.

Synset Examples

the wood petrified with time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fossilize - to become preserved as a fossil

Etymology

From Greek *petra* (rock) via Latin *petrificare* (to make into stone).

4. VERB

/ ˈpɛtrəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɛtrɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to so frighten or shock someone that they are unable to move or think
to paralyze with fear or shock

Examples

The ghost story so petrified her that she could barely speak.
Thunder petrified the children, making them cling to their mother.
He was petrified with fear when he saw the snake in his bed.

Synset Examples

His sudden appearance would petrify anyone in the dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to terrify - to frighten someone greatly
  • Antonym: calm - to make someone peaceful and relaxed

Etymology

From Latin 'petrificare', from 'petra' (rock) + 'facere' (to make).
