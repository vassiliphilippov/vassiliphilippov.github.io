Dipsacus sylvestris

1. NOUN

/ dɪpˈsækəs sɪlˈvɛstrɪs / (AmE), / dɪpˈsakəs sɪlˈvɛstrɪs / (BrE)
A species of teasel, native to Europe, bearing white to pink flowers and now naturalized in the United States; also known as wild teasel.
species of European teasel plant

Examples

Dipsacus sylvestris is often found in meadows and along roadsides across Europe and North America.
Gardeners sometimes plant Dipsacus sylvestris for its decorative seed heads.
Researchers are studying Dipsacus sylvestris for its potential use in traditional herbal remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild teasel - Common name for Dipsacus sylvestris

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Dipsacus, species epithet sylvestris meaning 'of the woods' or 'wild'.
