Laguncularia racemosa

1. NOUN

/ ləˌgʌŋkjəˈlɛr.i.ə ˌræs.əˈmoʊ.sə / (AmE), / ləˌgʌŋkjʊˈlɑː.rɪ.ə ˌræs.ɪˈməʊ.sə / (BrE)
a shrub or moderately large tree, commonly known as white mangrove, found in brackish coastal waters of western Africa and tropical America, and valued locally as a source of tannin
species of mangrove tree in tropical coastal areas

Examples

The Laguncularia racemosa is often found in brackish waters along tropical coasts.
Many coastal communities use the bark of Laguncularia racemosa to produce tannins for dyeing.
Restoration projects sometimes plant Laguncularia racemosa to help stabilize coastal ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white mangrove - common name for Laguncularia racemosa

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Laguncularia, species name racemosa, describing the clustered (racemose) arrangement of its flowers.
