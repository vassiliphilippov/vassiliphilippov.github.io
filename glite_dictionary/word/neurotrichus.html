Neurotrichus

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʊroʊˈtrɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəˈtrɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of small insectivorous mammals known as shrew-moles, native to North America; includes the American shrew-mole.
genus of shrew-moles

Examples

The genus Neurotrichus contains only one extant species, the American shrew-mole.
Scientists have studied the habitat preferences of Neurotrichus in the Pacific Northwest.
Unlike most moles, Neurotrichus has adaptations for both digging and surface activity.

Etymology

From Greek 'neuron' (nerve) and 'thrix' (hair), used in New Latin scientific naming.
