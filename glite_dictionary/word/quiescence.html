quiescence

1. NOUN

/ kwiˈɛsəns / (AmE), / kwiˈɛsəns / (BrE)
A state of quietness, inactivity, or restfulness.
state of quiet inactivity or rest

Examples

After months of intense activity, the volcano entered a period of quiescence.
The town settled into quiescence as night fell, with hardly a sound to be heard.
A brief quiescence in the negotiations allowed both sides to reconsider their positions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dormancy - a state of reduced activity or suspended growth
  • Antonym: activity - the state of being active or in motion

Etymology

From Latin "quiescentia" (being quiet), from "quiescere" (to rest).

2. NOUN

/ kwiˈɛsəns / (AmE), / kwɪˈɛsəns / (BrE)
a state of quietness or inactivity, especially when temporary
state of inactivity or quiet

Examples

After years of quiescence, the old factory suddenly came alive with new workers.
The animal entered a state of quiescence during the harsh winter months.
Periods of quiescence are sometimes followed by bursts of creativity.

Synset Examples

the volcano erupted after centuries of dormancy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dormancy - state of being temporarily inactive
  • Antonym: activity - state of being active or engaged

Etymology

From Latin 'quiescentia', from 'quiescens', present participle of 'quiescere' (to become quiet).
