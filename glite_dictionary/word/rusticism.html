rusticism

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌstɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈrʌstɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
a word, phrase, or expression characteristic of rural speech.
rural idiom or expression

Examples

The author's use of rusticism gives the dialogue an authentic, rural flavor.
That phrase is a classic rusticism rarely heard outside the countryside.
Linguists study rusticism to understand regional language patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regionalism - a linguistic feature characteristic of a particular region
  • Antonym: urbanism - expression or way of life characteristic of cities

Etymology

From Latin 'rusticus' (rural) + -ism.
