Marmota caligata

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈmoʊtə ˌkælɪˈɡeɪtə / (AmE), / mɑːˈməʊtə ˌkælɪˈɡeɪtə / (BrE)
A large marmot species native to the mountains of northwestern North America, commonly known as the hoary marmot.
hoary marmot species of North America

Examples

During our hike in the Rockies, we spotted a Marmota caligata sunning itself on a boulder.
Unlike other marmots, the Marmota caligata lives at higher elevations and is well-adapted to cold.
The distinctive whistle of the Marmota caligata often warns its colony of approaching predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoary marmot - Common name for Marmota caligata, a large North American marmot

Etymology

From scientific Latin, Marmota (genus of marmots) + caligata (species name, from Latin 'caligatus' meaning 'booted').
