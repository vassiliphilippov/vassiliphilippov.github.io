Chou

1. PROPN

/ tʃoʊ / (AmE), / tʃəʊ / (BrE)
the imperial Chinese dynasty from about 1122 to 221 BC, during which Confucianism and Taoism emerged and developed.
Chinese dynasty (1122–221 BC)

Examples

The Chou dynasty marked a turning point in Chinese philosophy and governance.
Many ancient Chinese texts from the Chou era have survived to modern times.
During the Chou dynasty, both Confucianism and Taoism began to flourish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zhou - an alternative spelling of the same Chinese dynasty name

Etymology

From Wade-Giles romanization of Mandarin 周 (Zhōu), the name of the dynasty.
