bacchant

1. NOUN

/ bəˈkænt / (AmE), / ˈbækənt / (BrE)
A person who participates in noisy or drunken festivities, especially as a devotee of Bacchus; a reveller.
drunken reveller, follower of Bacchus

Examples

During the festival, every bacchant danced wildly through the city streets.
The old painting depicted a group of bacchants celebrating in a moonlit forest.
She feared the arrival of the bacchants, whose revelry often led to chaos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reveller - a person who enjoys noisy festivities, especially those involving drinking

Etymology

From Latin bacchant-, bacchans (celebrating the festival of Bacchus), from Bacchus, the Roman god of wine.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbækənt / (AmE), / ˈbakənt / (BrE)
a person who engages in wild, drunken revelry or festivities, especially in the manner of the Bacchanalia of ancient Rome or Greece
one who takes part in drunken revelry

Examples

The wealthy merchant became a bacchant at every festival, infamous for his wild parties and excessive drinking.
In the ancient play, each bacchant was lost in a trance of ecstatic celebration.
No one expected the reserved professor to reveal his inner bacchant during the university reunion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reveller - a person enjoying noisy festivities, especially parties involving drinking
  • Antonym: teetotaler - a person who abstains completely from alcoholic drink

Etymology

From Latin 'bacchant-', from 'Bacchari' (to celebrate the festival of Bacchus), Bacchus being the Roman god of wine.

3. NOUN

/ bəˈkænt / (AmE), / bəˈkant / (BrE)
A priest, follower, or devotee of Bacchus, especially one participating in Bacchanalian rites in classical mythology.
devotee or priest of Bacchus

Examples

He dressed as a bacchant in the festival's re-enactment of ancient rituals.
The wild revelry of the bacchant was echoed in the painting's chaotic energy.
In literature, a bacchant is often associated with intoxication and celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maenad - female worshiper of Bacchus
  • Synonym: bacchanal - participant in Bacchanalia, follower of Bacchus

Etymology

From Latin 'bacchant-', from Greek 'bakchantes', relating to Bacchus, the god of wine.
