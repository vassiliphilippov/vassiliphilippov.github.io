to street-walk

1. VERB

/ ˈstriːt ˌwɔk / (AmE), / ˈstriːt ˌwɔːk / (BrE)
(of a prostitute) to walk the streets in search of customers.
walk streets seeking customers (as a prostitute)

Examples

She was forced to street-walk after losing her job.
Many tried to help young women who were street-walking downtown.
Rumors spread that he had seen her street-walking late at night.

Synset Examples

The prostitute is street-walking every night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to solicit - to seek to obtain by persuasion or entreaty; often used for the act of seeking customers as a prostitute
