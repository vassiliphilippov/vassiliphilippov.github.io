impetus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪmpɪˌtəs / (AmE), / ˈɪmpɪtəs / (BrE)
the force or energy with which a body moves; the action of suddenly applying force to cause movement
sudden applied force or energy

Examples

The sudden impetus of the wind knocked over the garden chairs.
A quick impetus sent the boat gliding away from the shore.
The runner gained momentum with an impressive impetus at the starting gun.

Synset Examples

the impulse knocked him over

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impulse - a sudden strong and unreflective urge or movement

Etymology

From Latin impetus (attack, assault, force), from impetere (to attack, rush at).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪmpətəs / (AmE), / ˈɪmpɪtəs / (BrE)
A force or influence that makes something happen or move; the energy or motivation behind an action.
driving force or motivation

Examples

The new manager provided the impetus the team needed to reach its goals.
The discovery gave fresh impetus to the research project.
Rising fuel prices served as the impetus for developing alternative energy sources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: momentum - the quantity of motion of a moving body
  • Antonym: inertia - resistance to change in motion or state

Etymology

From Latin impetus (an attack, impulse), from impetere (to attack).

Synset POS: n
