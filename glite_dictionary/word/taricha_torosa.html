Taricha torosa

1. NOUN

/ təˈriːkə təˈroʊsə / (AmE), / təˈriːkə təˈrɒsə / (BrE)
A species of newt native to California, similar to Taricha granulosa in appearance and habitat.
a species of newt found in California

Examples

The population of Taricha torosa in Southern California has declined due to habitat loss.
Researchers studied the breeding habits of Taricha torosa in upland streams.
The skin of Taricha torosa secretes toxins for defense against predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taricha granulosa - a similar species of newt found in western North America

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name 'Taricha' and species name 'torosa'.
