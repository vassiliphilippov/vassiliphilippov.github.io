shading

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪ.dɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪ.dɪŋ / (BrE)
the use of graded markings in a drawing or painting to indicate areas of light and shadow
graded markings showing light and shadow in art

Examples

The artist used careful shading to make the portrait appear three-dimensional.
Notice the subtle shading around the eyes, which gives the painting depth.
Good shading can create the illusion of light coming from a specific direction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hatching - drawing technique using closely spaced lines to suggest shading or texture

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
a subtle difference or gradation between similar things, especially in color, meaning, or quality
subtle gradation or difference

Examples

There was a shading of blue in the sky that made the sunset even more beautiful.
The director captured every shading of emotion in the actor's performance.
The novel explores the subtle shading between friendship and love.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuance - a subtle distinction or variation in meaning or quality
  • Synonym: gradation - a gradual change, or stage in a series of changes

Etymology

From 'shade' + '-ing', referring to the process or result of shading.
