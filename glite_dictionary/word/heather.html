heather

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɛðə / (BrE)
A fabric or yarn with interwoven strands of mixed colors that produce a muted, greyish shade with flecks of color.
fabric made with mixed-color yarns

Examples

She bought a heather sweater that blended blues and greys.
The designer chose heather fabric for its subtle look and versatility.
This scarf uses a heather yarn to create a soft, speckled effect.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɛðə / (BrE)
a low, evergreen shrub with small purple or pinkish flowers, common on moors and heaths in Europe and parts of North America
evergreen shrub with small purple or pink flowers

Examples

The hills were covered in blooming heather during late summer.
She picked a sprig of heather for her bouquet.
Heather thrives in poor, acidic soil on windswept moors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heath - an area of open uncultivated land, often overgrown with shrubs such as heather

Etymology

From Old English *hæth* (heath, uncultivated land).
