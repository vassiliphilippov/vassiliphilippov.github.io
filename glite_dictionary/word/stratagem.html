stratagem

1. NOUN

/ ˈstræt̬.ə.dʒəm / (AmE), / ˈstræt.ə.dʒəm / (BrE)
a clever plan or scheme, especially one used to outwit an opponent or achieve an end by deception
deceptive or clever scheme

Examples

The company developed an intricate stratagem to outperform its competitors in the market.
His resignation was merely a stratagem to gain more power within the organization.
She exposed the villain's stratagem before anyone could be harmed.

Synset Examples

his testimony was just a contrivance to throw us off the track

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruse - a trick or action intended to deceive someone
  • Synonym: trick - an act or plan intended to deceive or outwit
  • Synonym: ploy - a cunning plan or action designed to turn a situation to one's own advantage
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and straightforward, not deceitful

Etymology

From Greek 'stratēgēma' (an act of generalship), from 'stratēgein' (to be a general) + '-ma'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstrætədʒəm / (AmE), / ˈstrætədʒəm / (BrE)
a clever plan or scheme, especially one used to outwit an opponent or achieve an advantage, often by deceit or trickery
clever scheme or trick

Examples

She devised a brilliant stratagem to win the chess match.
His stratagem in the debate caught everyone by surprise.
The company's latest stratagem was aimed at outmaneuvering its competitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trick - a cunning act or scheme intended to deceive or outwit
  • Synonym: ruse - a cunning plan or maneuver to deceive others
  • Antonym: honesty - quality of being honest; lack of deceit or trickery

Etymology

From Latin 'stratēgēma' (artifice), from Greek 'strategēma' (the act of a general, strategy, artifice).
