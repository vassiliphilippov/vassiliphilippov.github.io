meed

1. NOUN

/ miːd / (AmE), / miːd / (BrE)
A deserved reward or recompense; something given in recognition of service or achievement, especially in poetic or archaic usage.
fitting reward; recompense (archaic)
archaic

Examples

For his bravery in battle, the king granted him his meed.
She seeks no meed for her charity, only the joy of helping others.
In tales of old, knights would risk their lives in hope of rich meed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reward - something given in return for effort, service, or achievement
  • Antonym: penalty - a punishment for breaking a law, rule, or contract

Etymology

From Old English *mēd* (reward), related to Old High German *miata* (reward).
