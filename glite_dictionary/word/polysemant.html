polysemant

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈsiːmənt / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈsiːmənt / (BrE)
A word that has more than one meaning.
word with multiple meanings

Examples

The word 'bank' is a classic polysemant, referring to both a financial institution and the land beside a river.
In linguistic studies, identifying a polysemant can reveal the evolution of language and meaning.
Teachers often use examples of a polysemant to illustrate the complexity of English vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyseme - a word with several meanings

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'semant' (meaning), related to 'polysemy.'
