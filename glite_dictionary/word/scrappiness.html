scrappiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈskræpinəs / (AmE), / ˈskræpinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being determined, energetic, and willing to fight or struggle for something; pugnaciousness.
quality of being determined or pugnacious

Examples

Her scrappiness on the basketball court inspired her teammates to never give up.
Despite his small size, his scrappiness made him a formidable opponent.
The startup's scrappiness helped it survive its difficult first year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determination - the quality of being determined
  • Synonym: pugnacity - the quality of being eager to fight or argue

Etymology

From scrappy + -ness, where "scrappy" comes from "scrap" (a fight).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskræpinɪs / (AmE), / ˈskræpinɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being determined, spirited, and ready to persist or fight, especially despite disadvantage or small size.
determined or spirited quality

Examples

Although the team was outmatched, their scrappiness kept them in the game until the very end.
Her scrappiness in business negotiations earned her a reputation as a tough competitor.
Despite only being five feet tall, his scrappiness made him a formidable opponent.

Synset Examples

Sarabi was the smallest of the litter but made up for her lack of size by her feistiness!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenacity - the quality of being determined and persistent
  • Synonym: feistiness - the quality of being lively, determined, and courageous

Etymology

From 'scrappy' (spirited, determined) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).
