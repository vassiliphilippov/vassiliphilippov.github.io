benzine

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnziːn / (AmE), / ˈbɛnzɪn / (BrE)
A colorless, highly flammable mixture of liquid hydrocarbons obtained from petroleum, used as a solvent and in cleaning, and sometimes confused with benzene.
flammable liquid hydrocarbon solvent

Examples

The factory stored barrels of benzine in a secure area due to its flammability.
Make sure the room is well ventilated before using benzine as a cleaning solvent.
Exposure to benzine can be hazardous, so protective gear is required.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benzene - an aromatic hydrocarbon, C6H6, similar in use, but chemically distinct

Etymology

From French 'benzine', from 'benz-' (from benzoin) + '-ine'.
