blowpipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈbloʊˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈbləʊˌpaɪp / (BrE)
A tube used to shoot darts or other projectiles by blowing through it.
tube for shooting darts by blowing

Examples

The hunter skillfully aimed the blowpipe at the target.
Ancient tribes often used a blowpipe for hunting small animals.
He crafted a blowpipe from bamboo and practiced shooting darts in the backyard.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbloʊˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈbləʊˌpaɪp / (BrE)
A tube through which air or gas is blown to intensify the heat of a flame, commonly used in metalworking or laboratory work.
tube for intensifying flame heat

Examples

The jeweler used a blowpipe to heat the small piece of gold.
A blowpipe can be used to analyze minerals in a laboratory setting.
The glassblower inserted air through the blowpipe to shape the molten glass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blowlamp - a portable device that produces a flame for heating

Etymology

From 'blow' (to expel air) + 'pipe', referring to a pipe used for blowing air or gas.
