crispate

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪs.peɪt / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.peɪt / (BrE)
having a wavy or irregularly curled edge or surface, especially in reference to leaves or petals
having a wavy or curled form

Examples

The plant's crispate leaves are easily recognized by their irregular wavy edges.
Under magnification, the fungus displayed crispate margins on its spores.
Botanists often describe oak leaf galls as having a crispate appearance due to their notched, curled surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crisped - having a finely curled or wavy form

Etymology

From Latin crispatus, past participle of crispāre (to curl).
