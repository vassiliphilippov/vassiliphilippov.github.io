Allium ascalonicum

1. NOUN

/ əˈliːəm ˌæskəˈlɒnɪkəm / (AmE), / əˈliːəm ˌaskəˈlɒnɪkəm / (BrE)
A type of onion plant that produces small, clustered, mild-flavored bulbs, commonly used as a seasoning in cooking; also known as the shallot.
species of onion producing mild, clustered bulbs

Examples

The chef finely chopped some Allium ascalonicum to add a mild flavor to the sauce.
Farmers often grow Allium ascalonicum for use in gourmet dishes and pickling.
Botanists classify the Allium ascalonicum as a distinct species within the onion family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shallot - a mild-flavored variety of onion with clustered bulbs, used in cooking

Etymology

From Latin 'Allium' (onion or garlic) and medieval Latin 'Ascalonicum' (of Ascalon, an ancient city, now Ashkelon in Israel, once believed to be the origin of the shallot).
