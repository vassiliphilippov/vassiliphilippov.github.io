goal

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊl / (AmE), / ɡəʊl / (BrE)
the area or structure in sports, such as soccer or hockey, that players aim at in order to score points
area or structure in sports for scoring

Examples

The soccer ball hit the goal and bounced back into play.
She guarded the goal fiercely and saved her team from losing points.
Hockey players raced toward the goal to try to score.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: net - the mesh structure forming the barrier in a goal in sports

2. NOUN

/ ɡoʊl / (AmE), / ɡəʊl / (BrE)
the result or outcome that a person or organization intends to achieve through effort or planning
desired result; intended outcome

Examples

After months of hard work, they finally reached their goal of opening a new store.
Setting a specific goal can help keep you motivated and focused.
The goal of the project is to reduce energy consumption by 20%.

Synset Examples

the ends justify the means

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aim - a purpose or intention; a desired outcome
  • Synonym: objective - something that you plan to achieve
  • Antonym: means - a method or action used to achieve an end

Etymology

From Middle English 'gol' (boundary, limit); origin uncertain.

3. NOUN

/ goʊl / (AmE), / ɡəʊl / (BrE)
the place that marks the end point of a race, journey, or movement
end point of a race or journey

Examples

A large crowd cheered as the runners approached the goal at the end of the marathon.
After hiking for hours, the lake at the mountain's summit was finally our goal.
He sprinted towards the goal, determined to finish first in the race.

4. NOUN

/ ɡoʊl / (AmE), / ɡəʊl / (BrE)
A point scored in a game or sport when a player or team succeeds in getting the ball or object into a particular area, such as a net or goalpost.
a point scored in a game or sport

Examples

The team celebrated as soon as he scored the winning goal in the last minute.
She trained hard every day, hoping to score her first goal for the club.
After a tense match, the only goal was made in the second half.

Synset Examples

the winning goal came with less than a minute left to play

Related Terms

  • Synonym: point - a unit of scoring in a game or sport

Etymology

From Middle English gol (boundary, limit), origin uncertain; sense shifting to 'object of effort' and 'point scored' in sports by 16th century.
