comeliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmli.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌmli.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being pleasant to look at; physical attractiveness.
attractiveness; pleasing appearance

Examples

Her extraordinary comeliness drew admirers wherever she went.
The painting captures the quiet comeliness of rural life.
He was known not just for his strength, but also for his natural comeliness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beauty - a combination of qualities that pleases the senses, especially the sight
  • Antonym: ugliness - the quality of being unpleasant to look at

Etymology

From Middle English 'comly', from Old English 'cymlic' (lovely, glorious), related to 'becoming'.
