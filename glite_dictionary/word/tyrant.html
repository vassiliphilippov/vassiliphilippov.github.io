tyrant

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪrənt / (AmE), / ˈtaɪərənt / (BrE)
(in ancient Greece) a ruler who seized power and governed without legal right to it, often ruling oppressively.
usurping ruler in ancient Greece

Examples

The city was once ruled by a tyrant who took power by force.
Many tyrants in ancient Greece began as champions of the people but became oppressive.
The legacy of the Athenian tyrant Peisistratus is still debated by historians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictator - a ruler with total power, typically one who has obtained control by force

Etymology

From Latin tyrannus, from Ancient Greek τύραννος (týrannos, 'absolute ruler').

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪrənt / (AmE), / ˈtaɪərənt / (BrE)
A ruler who exercises power in a cruel, harsh, or oppressive way, often using force or fear.
cruel and oppressive ruler

Examples

The country suffered for decades under the rule of a brutal tyrant.
Throughout history, tyrants have often been overthrown by popular uprisings.
Many people fled the region to escape the oppression of a tyrant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictator - a ruler with absolute power, often obtained by force
  • Synonym: despot - a ruler with absolute and often oppressive power
  • Antonym: liberator - a person who frees others from oppression

Etymology

From Latin 'tyrannus', from Greek 'turannos' (absolute ruler).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪrənt / (AmE), / ˈtaɪərənt / (BrE)
a person who uses power or authority in a cruel or oppressive way
cruel or oppressive ruler

Examples

The country suffered for years under the rule of a tyrant who silenced all opposition.
Many people feared him, calling him a tyrant behind his back.
Her boss acted like a tyrant, demanding absolute obedience from every employee.

Synset Examples

his father was a tyrant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despot - a ruler or person who holds absolute power, typically in a cruel or oppressive way
  • Synonym: dictator - a ruler with total power over a country, typically one who has obtained control by force
  • Antonym: benefactor - someone who gives help, often in a position of authority, in a kind way

Etymology

From Latin tyrannus, from Ancient Greek τύραννος (túrannos) meaning 'absolute ruler'.
