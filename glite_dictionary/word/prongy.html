prongy

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɔŋi / (AmE), / ˈprɒŋi / (BrE)
having prongs or resembling a fork; divided or separated into two or more branches or points.
having prongs or branches

Examples

The plant developed prongy roots after being transplanted into sandy soil.
Some species of algae have prongy branches to maximize sunlight exposure.
After the storm, the tree's prongy limbs reached out in several directions.
