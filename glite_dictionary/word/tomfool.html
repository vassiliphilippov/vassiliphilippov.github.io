tomfool

1. NOUN

/ tɑmˈfuːl / (AmE), / tɒmˈfuːl / (BrE)
a person who behaves in a silly or foolish way; someone who lacks good judgment
a foolish or silly person

Examples

If you keep acting like a tomfool, no one will take you seriously.
She dismissed him as a harmless tomfool always ready with a prank or a joke.
The play features a comedic tomfool who constantly gets into trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or judgment
  • Synonym: dunce - a person who is slow to learn or lacks intelligence
  • Antonym: sage - a profoundly wise person

Etymology

From Tom (a generic given name) + fool, originally used in 16th century England.
