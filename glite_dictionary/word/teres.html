teres

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪr.iːz / (AmE), / ˈtɪə.riːz / (BrE)
either of two muscles in the shoulder region (teres major and teres minor) that assist in moving the shoulders and arms
muscle in the shoulder region
medical

Examples

The teres major muscle helps to rotate and adduct the arm.
An injury to the teres minor can affect shoulder mobility.
Proper stretching exercises can strengthen both the teres muscles and improve flexibility.

Etymology

From Latin 'teres' meaning 'rounded' or 'smooth'.
