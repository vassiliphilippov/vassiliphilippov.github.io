genip

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɪp / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnɪp / (BrE)
A small, round Caribbean fruit with green leathery skin and sweet, juicy, translucent pulp, commonly eaten fresh like grapes.
small Caribbean fruit with sweet pulp

Examples

We bought a bag of fresh genip from a street vendor in Jamaica.
The sweet flesh of the genip makes it a popular summertime treat in the Caribbean.
After peeling the skin, she popped the juicy genip into her mouth.

Etymology

From Spanish 'genipa', from the Taíno word for the fruit.

2. NOUN

/ dəˈnip / (AmE), / dʒəˈniːp / (BrE)
A tropical American tree (Melicoccus bijugatus) that produces a small edible fruit with green leathery skin and sweet, juicy, translucent pulp.
tropical tree with edible fruit

Examples

The children eagerly collected genip from the tree during the summer harvest.
She made a delicious juice from fresh genip pulp.
Vendors at the local market were selling bags of ripe genip along the roadside.

Etymology

From Spanish 'genipa', possibly of Taino origin.
