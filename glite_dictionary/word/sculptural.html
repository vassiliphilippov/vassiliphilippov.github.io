sculptural

1. ADJ

/ ˈskʌlp.tʃɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈskʌlp.tʃər.əl / (BrE)
relating to or resembling sculpture; having the qualities or appearance of sculpture.
relating to sculpture

Examples

The museum displayed sculptural works from all over the world.
Her dress had a sculptural quality, with bold lines and shapes.
Architects praised the building’s sculptural facade for its artistic design.

Synset Examples

sculptural embellishments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sculpture - art of making figures by carving or modeling

Etymology

From Latin 'sculptura' (sculpture) + '-al' (forming adjectives).

2. ADJ

/ ˈskʌlptʃərəl / (AmE), / ˈskʌlptʃərəl / (BrE)
having the qualities or appearance of sculpture; resembling or relating to sculpture in form or style.
having qualities of sculpture

Examples

The artist's new installation has a distinctly sculptural presence in the gallery.
Her dress featured bold, sculptural lines that made it stand out on the runway.
The architect chose a sculptural form for the building, giving it a unique silhouette.

Synset Examples

her finely modeled features

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sculpturesque - having the grace, dignity, or proportion of sculpture

Etymology

From sculpture + -al, meaning 'pertaining to or resembling sculpture'.
