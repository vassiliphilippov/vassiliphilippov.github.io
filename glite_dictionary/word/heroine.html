heroine

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛroʊɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɛrəʊɪn / (BrE)
A woman admired for her courage, outstanding achievements, or noble qualities; a female hero.
female hero; courageous woman

Examples

She became a national heroine after saving the children from the burning building.
The novel's main heroine is a young scientist who travels the world alone.
Many people see her as a heroine of the civil rights movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hero - a person who is admired for their courage and noble qualities, especially a man

Etymology

From French héroïne, from Latin heroina, from Ancient Greek hērōinē (female hero).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛroʊˌɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɛrəʊɪn / (BrE)
the main female character in a book, play, film, or story, who is admired for her courage or outstanding qualities
main female character in a story

Examples

The heroine of the novel defied expectations and saved her city from disaster.
Many young girls look up to the heroine in classic fairy tales for inspiration.
In the film, the heroine must overcome her fears to rescue her friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hero - the main male character in a story, or a person admired for courage
  • Antonym: villain - the main evil character in a work of fiction

Etymology

From Latin 'heroina', from Greek 'hērōinē' (female hero).
