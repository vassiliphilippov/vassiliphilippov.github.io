circumpolar

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɝːkəmˈpoʊlɚ / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈpəʊlə / (BrE)
Situated in or relating to the area around either the North or South Pole; found throughout a polar region.
situated around or throughout a polar region

Examples

The circumpolar Arctic is home to unique wildlife and indigenous peoples.
Some constellations appear circumpolar and never set below the horizon at high latitudes.
Explorers studied circumpolar currents to understand the climate near the poles.

Etymology

From Latin 'circum' (around) + 'polaris' (of the pole).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɜrkəmˈpoʊlər / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈpəʊlə / (BrE)
(of a celestial object) remaining continually visible above the horizon throughout its daily path, never setting as viewed from a specific latitude.
always above horizon; never sets (astronomy)

Examples

The North Star is a circumpolar star for observers in the Northern Hemisphere.
In high latitudes, many constellations appear circumpolar and never dip below the horizon.
Astronomers often study circumpolar stars because they are visible throughout the year.

Synset Examples

a circumpolar star

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polar - of or relating to the celestial or geographic poles

Etymology

From Latin 'circum' (around) + 'polaris' (of the pole).
