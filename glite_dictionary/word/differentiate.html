to differentiate

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪf.əˈren.ʃi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪf.əˈren.ʃi.eɪt / (BrE)
(of a cell or tissue) to develop and change so as to become specialized for a particular function.
become specialized during development (biology)

Examples

During embryonic development, stem cells differentiate into various specialized cell types.
As the organism grows, its tissues begin to differentiate and perform distinct functions.
Plant cells differentiate based on environmental cues and genetic instructions.

Synset Examples

cells differentiate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to specialize - to adapt or become adapted for a special function or environment (in biology)

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *differentiare*, from Latin *differentia* (difference).
