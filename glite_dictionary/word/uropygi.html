Uropygi

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊəroʊˈpaɪdʒaɪ / (AmE), / ˌjʊə.rəʊˈpɪdʒ.aɪ / (BrE)
An order of arachnids known as whip scorpions, characterized by a whip-like tail and found mainly in tropical regions.
order of whip scorpions

Examples

The order Uropygi includes species that are commonly called whip scorpions.
Scientists studying Uropygi have discovered new behaviors unique to these arachnids.
Unlike true scorpions, Uropygi do not possess venomous stingers.

Etymology

From Greek 'oura' (tail) + 'pyge' (rump), referring to the distinct tail structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˌjʊrəˈpaɪdʒi / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəʊˈpɪdʒi / (BrE)
an order of arachnids commonly known as whip scorpions, characterized by a whip-like tail and found mainly in tropical and subtropical regions.
an order of whip scorpions (arachnids)

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Uropygi order in the rainforest.
Members of the Uropygi are often called whip scorpions due to their elongated tails.
The Uropygi are fascinating for their ability to spray defensive acid when threatened.

Etymology

From Greek 'oura' (tail) + 'pyge' (rump), referencing the tail structure of these arachnids.
