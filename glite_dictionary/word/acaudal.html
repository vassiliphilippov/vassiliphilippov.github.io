acaudal

1. ADJ

/ əˈkɔdəl / (AmE), / əˈkɔːdəl / (BrE)
(of an animal or part) lacking a tail or taillike appendage.
lacking a tail (zoology/anatomy)

Examples

Some fish species are naturally acaudal, having evolved to lose their tails over time.
The mutation resulted in an acaudal mouse, which lacked the typical caudal vertebrae.
In marine biology, acaudal forms are of particular interest due to their unique evolutionary paths.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: caudal - having a tail or relating to the tail

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'cauda' (tail).
