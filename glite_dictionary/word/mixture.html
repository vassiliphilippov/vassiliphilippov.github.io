mixture

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪkstʃər / (AmE), / ˈmɪkstʃə / (BrE)
the act or process of combining two or more substances or things together
the act of mixing things together

Examples

The mixture of paint colors resulted in a beautiful shade of green.
Careful mixture of the ingredients is essential for the recipe to turn out well.
The mixture of different musical genres created a unique sound for the album.

Synset Examples

paste made by a mix of flour and water
the mixing of sound channels in the recording studio

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mixing - the act of mixing; combining or blending

Etymology

From Latin mixtūra (a mixing), from mixtus, past participle of miscēre (to mix).

5. NOUN

/ ˈmɪkstʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪkstʃə / (BrE)
(chemistry) a substance made by combining two or more different substances without chemical bonding, and not in fixed proportions.
a substance made by mixing other substances
medical

Examples

The chemist carefully prepared a mixture of two liquids for the experiment.
Air is a mixture of several different gases, including oxygen and nitrogen.
Unlike a compound, a mixture does not have components chemically bonded together.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compound - a substance formed from two or more elements chemically united in fixed proportions

Etymology

From Latin mixtūra (a mixing), from mixtus, past participle of miscēre (to mix).
