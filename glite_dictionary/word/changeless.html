changeless

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃeɪndʒləs / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒləs / (BrE)
not subject or susceptible to change; remaining the same over time.
not changeable; remaining the same

Examples

Even after many years, their friendship remained changeless.
The desert's changeless landscape fascinated the explorers.
She longed for something changeless in her unpredictable life.

Synset Examples

the view of that time was that all species were immutable, created by God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immutable - unchanging over time or unable to be changed
  • Antonym: changeable - able to be changed or modified

Etymology

From 'change' + '-less', Old French 'changier'; suffix '-less' indicating absence.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃeɪndʒləs / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪn(d)ʒləs / (BrE)
remaining the same and never changing; unaltered by time or circumstances.
not changing; unalterable

Examples

Despite the years, his loyalty remained changeless and unwavering.
The landscape possessed a changeless beauty that captivated artists for centuries.
The company's core values have stayed changeless since it was founded.

Synset Examples

maintained a constant temperature
principles of unvarying validity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unchanging - remaining the same; not changing
  • Antonym: mutable - liable to change

Etymology

From 'change' + '-less', with '-less' meaning 'without'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈtʃeɪndʒləs / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒləs / (BrE)
not changing; remaining the same over a long period of time.
not changing over time

Examples

Despite the passing years, her love for her childhood home remained changeless.
The landscape seemed changeless, unaffected by the seasons or the weather.
Some traditions are held changeless through generations, providing a sense of stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unchanging - not changing or able to be changed
  • Antonym: mutable - liable to change; changeable

Etymology

From 'change' + '-less', forming an adjective meaning 'without change'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00348502-s
Synset POS: s
