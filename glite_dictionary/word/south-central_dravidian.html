South-Central Dravidian

1. NOUN

/ saʊθ ˈsɛntrəl drəˈvɪdiən / (AmE), / saʊθ ˈsɛntrəl drəˈvɪdiən / (BrE)
a Dravidian language spoken primarily in the south-central region of India
a Dravidian language of south-central India

Examples

Scholars have conducted extensive research on the South-Central Dravidian languages to trace their historical development.
The South-Central Dravidian dialects are known for certain unique phonological features.
Many communities in Andhra Pradesh primarily speak South-Central Dravidian languages in their daily lives.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Dravidian - a family of languages spoken in southern India and Sri Lanka

Etymology

Formed from 'South-Central' and 'Dravidian', referring to its geographic and linguistic classification.
