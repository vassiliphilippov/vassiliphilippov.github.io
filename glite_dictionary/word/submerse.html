to submerse

1. VERB

/ səbˈmɜrs / (AmE), / səbˈmɜːs / (BrE)
to cause to be under the surface of a liquid, especially water; to sink or go below the surface
sink below the surface (esp. water)

Examples

The diver carefully submersed himself in the clear blue water.
Heavy rains can quickly submerse low-lying roads and fields.
She watched the stone submerse beneath the surface of the pond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to submerge - to go or be under water or another liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'submersus', past participle of 'submergere' (to plunge under).

2. VERB

/ səbˈmɜrs / (AmE), / səbˈmɜːs / (BrE)
to put or place something under water; to immerse completely in a liquid.
put under water; immerse

Examples

Please submerse the test tubes in the water bath for five minutes.
The diver submersed himself to observe the coral reef up close.
Before painting, submerse the brush in water to prevent the bristles from drying out.

Synset Examples

submerge your head completely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to submerge - to put or go under the surface of water or another liquid

Etymology

From Latin submersus, past participle of submergere (to plunge under, sink).
