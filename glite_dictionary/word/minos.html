Minos

1. PROPN

/ ˈmaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnɒs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the son of Zeus and Europa who became king of ancient Crete; he is noted for commissioning the Labyrinth and, after death, becoming a judge in the underworld.
mythical king of Crete and underworld judge

Examples

According to legend, Minos demanded tribute from Athens to feed the Minotaur in the Labyrinth.
Daedalus built the famous Labyrinth for King Minos on the island of Crete.
After his death, Minos became a judge of souls in the Greek underworld.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhadamanthus - another judge of the dead in Greek mythology, Minos' brother
  • Synonym: Aeacus - another judge of the dead in the Greek underworld, Minos' brother

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Μίνως (Minōs), the legendary king of Crete.
