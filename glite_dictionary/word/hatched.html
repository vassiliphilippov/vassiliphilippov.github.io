hatched

1. ADJ

/ hætʃt / (AmE), / hætʃt / (BrE)
(of a young animal) having emerged from an egg.
emerged from an egg

Examples

The newly hatched chicks huddled together under the heat lamp.
Most reptiles are independent soon after they are hatched.
These birds remain in their nests for several days after being hatched.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unhatched - not yet emerged from an egg

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'hatch', from Middle English hachen, probably of Germanic origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhætʃt / (AmE), / ˈhætʃt / (BrE)
(of a drawing or area) shaded with fine parallel or crossed lines to create the effect of shadow or texture.
shaded with fine lines

Examples

The architect presented a drawing with hatched areas indicating proposed changes.
In technical illustrations, hatched sections often represent cut surfaces.
The artist carefully hatched the shadow beneath the table for a realistic effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crosshatched - shaded with sets of intersecting parallel lines
  • Antonym: stippling - shading using dots instead of lines

Etymology

From the verb 'hatch' (to shade with lines), from Middle English hachier, from Old French hachier (to chop, engrave).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
