Rumex scutatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːmɛks skuːˈteɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈruːmɛks skuːˈteɪtəs / (BrE)
a low perennial herb with small silvery-green ovate to arrow-shaped leaves, commonly known as French sorrel
a species of herb, French sorrel

Examples

The chef added fresh Rumex scutatus leaves to the salad for a tangy flavor.
Gardeners often grow Rumex scutatus for its attractive foliage and culinary uses.
Young leaves of Rumex scutatus are prized in French cuisine for their lemony taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: French sorrel - common name for Rumex scutatus, an edible herb

Etymology

From Latin 'Rumex' (dock or sorrel) and 'scutatus' (shield-shaped) referring to the leaf shape.
