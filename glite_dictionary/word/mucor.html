Mucor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːkɔr / (AmE), / ˈmjuːkɔː / (BrE)
A genus of molds in the family Mucoraceae, characterized by cylindrical or pear-shaped sporangia not restricted to the location of rhizoids.
type genus of Mucoraceae molds

Examples

The laboratory identified the mold as Mucor, which can cause infections in immunocompromised patients.
Under a microscope, Mucor species reveal characteristic sporangia structures.
Proper storage conditions can help prevent the growth of Mucor on food products.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek ‘mukēs’ (mushroom, fungus).
