mucor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːkɔr / (AmE), / ˈmjuːkɔː / (BrE)
a type of mold belonging to the genus Mucor, commonly found on decaying organic matter and sometimes causing infections in humans and animals
a type of mold in the genus Mucor
medical

Examples

The laboratory identified the growth as mucor, a common type of mold found in the soil.
Patients with compromised immune systems are at risk of infections caused by mucor.
Researchers are studying the environmental distribution of mucor in hospital settings.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Mucor', from Latin 'mūcor' meaning 'mold' or 'slime'.
