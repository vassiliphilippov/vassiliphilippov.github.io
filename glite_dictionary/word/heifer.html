heifer

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛfər / (AmE), / ˈhɛfə / (BrE)
a young female cow that has not borne a calf
young female cow

Examples

The farmer bought a new heifer to add to his herd.
After a year, the heifer will mature and be able to produce milk.
Each heifer was tagged for identification at the agricultural fair.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cow - a fully grown female of cattle
  • Degree Lower: calf - a young domestic cow or bull

Etymology

From Old English *heahfore*, related to Middle Low German *hever* (heifer).
