to heal

1. VERB

/ hil / (AmE), / hiːl / (BrE)
(of an injury, wound, or person) become sound or healthy again; to recover from injury or illness.
recover or become healthy again

Examples

It took several weeks for his wound to heal fully.
After her operation, she needed time to heal both physically and emotionally.
Doctors said the bone would heal faster if he rested properly.

Synset Examples

My broken leg is mending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cure - to relieve a person or animal of the symptoms of a disease or condition
  • Synonym: to recover - to return to a normal state of health, mind, or strength
  • Antonym: to worsen - to become or make worse

Etymology

Old English hælan (make whole, sound, or well); related to whole.

2. VERB

/ hil / (AmE), / hiːl / (BrE)
to become healthy again; to return to a normal or good condition after being injured or ill
become healthy again

Examples

The doctor told me it would take six weeks for my arm to heal.
Her heart will heal with time after the loss of her pet.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: to recover - to return to a normal state of health, mind, or strength
