disaffected

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪs.əˈfɛk.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈfɛk.tɪd / (BrE)
no longer satisfied with the people in authority and no longer willing to support them
discontented or dissatisfied with authority

Examples

A growing number of disaffected youth have turned to protest movements for a sense of belonging.
He became disaffected with the government after years of broken promises.
The school struggled to engage its disaffected students who were often absent or disruptive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discontented - unhappy or dissatisfied, especially with one's circumstances
  • Antonym: contented - feeling or showing satisfaction with one's situation

Etymology

From Latin dis- (expressing reversal) + affectus (influenced, disposed, state of mind).
