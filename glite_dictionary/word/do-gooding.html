do-gooding

1. NOUN

/ ˌduˈɡʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdəʊˈɡʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of engaging in charitable or helpful activities, often with a sense of earnest or self-righteous intention
engaging in charitable activities

Examples

She quickly grew tired of her do-gooding when it was not appreciated by the community.
His constant do-gooding sometimes annoyed people who felt patronized.
Despite some criticism, their do-gooding has made a real difference in the neighborhood.

Synset Examples

She spent her summer do-gooding in the local shelters.
Do-gooding can be exhausting but rewarding.
Their do-gooding efforts are appreciated by all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philanthropy - the desire to promote the welfare of others, often by donating money
  • Synonym: altruism - selfless concern for the well-being of others
  • Antonym: self-interest - regard for one's own advantage or welfare

Etymology

From 'do-good' plus the suffix '-ing'.
