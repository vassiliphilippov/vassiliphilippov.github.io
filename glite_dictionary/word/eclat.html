eclat

1. NOUN

/ eɪˈklɑː / (AmE), / eɪˈklɑː / (BrE)
brilliant success or a dazzling effect; great public acclaim or praise, often resulting from an impressive achievement
brilliant success or public acclaim

Examples

The innovative product launch was greeted with eclat in the tech world.
Winning the prestigious award brought her international eclat.
The orchestra performed the challenging symphony with remarkable eclat.

Synset Examples

the eclat of a great achievement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose

Etymology

From French éclat (brilliance, splendor), from Old French esclat (splinter, burst out), from escler (to burst out).
