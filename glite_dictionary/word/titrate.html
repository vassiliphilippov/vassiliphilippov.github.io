to titrate

1. VERB

/ ˈtaɪˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtreɪt / (BrE)
to measure the concentration of a solution by gradually adding a reagent of known strength until a reaction is completed, especially in chemistry
determine concentration by titration
medical

Examples

The chemist will titrate the acid to determine its exact strength.
To measure the chloride content, the solution must first be titrated with silver nitrate.
She is titrating the mixture carefully to avoid exceeding the endpoint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titration - the process of determining concentration by titration

Etymology

From French *titrer* (to determine the concentration of) and Latin *titulus* (inscription, title).
