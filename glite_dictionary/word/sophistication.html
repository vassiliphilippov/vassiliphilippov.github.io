sophistication

1. NOUN

/ səˌfɪstəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / səˌfɪstɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the quality of being refined, cultured, or intellectually developed; the process or result of becoming more knowledgeable or enlightened.
refined knowledge or enlightenment

Examples

Her travels around the world added a certain sophistication to her views.
The sophistication of the city's art scene attracts creative people from everywhere.
His writing gradually gained sophistication as he read more widely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refinement - the process of improving something by making it more polished or cultured

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *sophisticatio*, from Latin *sophisticare* (to tamper with, make wise), from Greek *sophos* (wise).

2. NOUN

/ səˌfɪstəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / səˌfɪstɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the quality of having a refined knowledge, taste, or experience, especially as a result of education or worldly learnings
refined knowledge or worldly experience

Examples

Her sophistication impressed everyone at the dinner party, especially when discussing world politics.
The film's sophistication appeals more to adults who appreciate complex themes.
Years of travel brought a new level of sophistication to his writing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: naivety - lack of experience, wisdom, or sophistication

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'sophisticatio', from Latin 'sophisticatus' (tampered with, adulterated).

the quality of having a high level of knowledge or expertise in a specific technical or complex subject

The financial analyst explained the market's sophistication to the board of directors.

4. NOUN

/ səˌfɪstəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / səˌfɪstɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of misleading or falsifying by means of sophistry or deceptive reasoning.
misleading by deceptive reasoning
archaic

Examples

The lawyer's sophistication of the facts confused the jury.
Academic debates can sometimes descend into sophistication that obscures the real issues.
He was skilled in the sophistication of arguments to win any side.

Synset Examples

he practiced the art of sophistication upon reason

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving someone
  • Antonym: truthfulness - the quality of being truthful

Etymology

From Medieval Latin sophisticatio (tampering, adulteration), from Greek sophistikos (skilled in subtle argument).
