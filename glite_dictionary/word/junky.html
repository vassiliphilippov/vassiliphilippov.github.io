junky

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋki / (BrE)
a person who is extremely enthusiastic or obsessed with a particular activity, subject, or thing, in a way that resembles addiction
person obsessed with an activity or subject
informal

Examples

He is a real news junky and checks headlines every hour.
As a video game junky, she spends most weekends playing online.
The gym is always full of fitness junkies in January.

Synset Examples

a golf addict
a car nut
a bodybuilding freak
a news junkie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fanatic - a person filled with excessive enthusiasm for something
  • Synonym: enthusiast - someone who is very interested in a particular activity or subject
  • Synonym: addict - a person who is addicted to a particular substance or activity

Etymology

From 'junkie', originally referring to a drug addict, extended figuratively to enthusiastic devotion.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋki / (BrE)
(slang) A person who is addicted to narcotic drugs.
person addicted to narcotic drugs (slang)
slang

Examples

The police found the old warehouse occupied by a group of junkies.
He started out experimenting with drugs, but soon became a full-fledged junky.
Social workers try to help junkies get off the streets and into rehab programs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addict - a person addicted to a substance or activity
  • Synonym: user - a person who uses drugs habitually

Etymology

From 'junk' (slang for narcotics) + '-y' (a suffix forming nouns or adjectives).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10055677-n
Synset POS: n
