sodden

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɑd.ən / (AmE), / ˈsɒd.ən / (BrE)
completely soaked with liquid; thoroughly wet
completely soaked; thoroughly wet

Examples

The ground was so sodden after the storm that it squelched underfoot.
She handed him a sodden towel after he got caught in the rain.
His socks were sodden from walking through the flooded path.

Synset Examples

stood at the door drenched (or soaked) by the rain
the speaker's sodden collar
soppy clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soaked - thoroughly wet, saturated with liquid
  • Antonym: dry - free from moisture or liquid; not wet or moist
  • Degree Lower: damp - slightly wet or moist

Etymology

From Old English *soden*, past participle of *seethe* (to boil), originally meaning 'boiled' or 'soaked'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɑdən / (AmE), / ˈsɒdən / (BrE)
completely soaked and heavy with water or other liquid; saturated
soaked, saturated with liquid

Examples

The fields were sodden after three days of continuous rain.
He placed his sodden coat on a hook near the fireplace to dry.
Walking on the sodden path, her shoes quickly became soaked through.

Synset Examples

After the rain, the clothes were sodden and heavy.
She stepped on the sodden grass, leaving wet footprints.
The sodden ground made walking difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soaked - thoroughly wet; saturated with liquid
  • Antonym: dry - lacking moisture or liquid; not wet

Etymology

From Old English sōd (boiled), past participle of sēthe (to seethe).
