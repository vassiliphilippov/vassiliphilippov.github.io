Budorcas

1. NOUN

/ buːˈdɔrkəs / (AmE), / bjuːˈdɔːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of large goat-antelopes found in the eastern Himalayas, including the takin.
genus of Himalayan goat-antelopes

Examples

The Budorcas genus is best known for its only living species, the takin.
Researchers observed a herd of Budorcas grazing on the mountain slopes.
The conservation status of Budorcas has drawn attention from wildlife organizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: takin - a large Himalayan ungulate, sole species of Budorcas

Etymology

From Greek bous (ox) + dorkas (gazelle or antelope).

2. NOUN

/ bjuˈdɔrkəs / (AmE), / bjuːˈdɔːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, goat-like mammals native to Asia, known as gnu goats, with the takin as its only living species.
genus of large goat-like mammals (gnu goats)

Examples

The Budorcas is represented today by a single species, the takin, found in the Himalayas.
Scientists observed the Budorcas grazing on mountain slopes in western China.
Members of the genus Budorcas have adaptations suited to rugged, high-altitude environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: takin - a large, goat-antelope from the eastern Himalayas, the only extant species of Budorcas

Etymology

From Greek 'bous' (ox) + 'dorkas' (gazelle, antelope), referencing the animal's mixed appearance.
