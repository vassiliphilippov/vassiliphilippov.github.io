Mephitinae

1. NOUN

/ məˈfɪt.əˌniː / (AmE), / mɛˈfɪt.ɪˌniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of mammals in the family Mustelidae (or Mephitidae), including skunks and related species.
subfamily of animals including skunks

Examples

The Mephitinae subfamily is distinguished by the presence of well-developed scent glands.
Taxonomists sometimes debate whether Mephitinae should be classified within the Mustelidae family or as a separate family, Mephitidae.
Skunks, members of the Mephitinae, are known for their ability to spray a foul-smelling liquid as a defense mechanism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk - small mammal known for its defensive spray

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'mephitis' (foul odor) + '-inae', a zoological suffix for subfamilies.

2. NOUN

/ mɛˌfɪtɪˈniː / (AmE), / mɛˌfɪtɪˈniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of mammals in the family Mephitidae, including the skunks and related species.
subfamily of skunks and relatives

Examples

All members of the Mephitinae possess well-developed scent glands for defense.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Mephitinae in Central America.
The striped skunk is one of the best-known representatives of the Mephitinae.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: skunk - a small mammal known for its strong smell

Etymology

From Latin *mephitis* (noxious vapor, skunk) plus New Latin subfamily suffix *-inae*.
