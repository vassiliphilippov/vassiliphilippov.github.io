foreboding

1. ADJ

/ fɔrˈboʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / fɔːˈbəʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
giving a strong sense of something bad or harmful that will happen; ominous or predicting evil
giving an ominous warning

Examples

A foreboding silence filled the air before the storm broke.
The novel opens with a foreboding tone that hints at tragedy to come.
Dark clouds gathered with a foreboding presence over the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ominous - giving the impression that something bad is going to happen
  • Antonym: hopeful - giving a sense that good things will happen

Etymology

From Old English 'forbeodan' (to forbid, warn), via Middle English, related to 'forebode'.

2. NOUN

/ fɔːrˈboʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / fɔːˈbəʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
a feeling that something bad will happen; an omen or sign of future misfortune
a sense of impending misfortune

Examples

A sudden foreboding filled her as she entered the abandoned house.
He had a strong foreboding that something terrible was about to happen.
There was a sense of foreboding in the air before the storm struck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: premonition - a strong feeling something is about to happen, usually unpleasant
  • Synonym: omen - an event regarded as a sign of future good or evil
  • Synonym: presentiment - an intuitive feeling about the future, especially something bad

Etymology

From Old English 'forbodian', meaning to forbid or foretell evil, from 'fore-' (before) + 'bodian' (to announce).

3. NOUN

/ fɔrˈboʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / fɔːˈbəʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
a strong feeling or sense that something bad or unpleasant is going to happen
a sense of approaching evil or trouble

Examples

A foreboding chilled the room as the storm clouds gathered outside.
She had a sudden foreboding that something terrible was about to occur.
Despite his foreboding, he agreed to proceed with the risky plan.

Synset Examples

a steadily escalating sense of foreboding
the lawyer had a presentiment that the judge would dismiss the case

Related Terms

  • Synonym: premonition - a strong feeling that something is about to happen, especially something negative
  • Synonym: dread - a strong feeling of fear or apprehension about something that may happen
  • Antonym: optimism - hopefulness and confidence about the future

Etymology

From Old English forbōd (prohibition), influenced by 'fore-' (before) and 'bode' (to announce).

Synset POS: n
