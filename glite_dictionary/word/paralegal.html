paralegal

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəˈliɡəl / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈliːɡəl / (BrE)
A person trained to assist lawyers in legal work but not qualified to practice law independently.
trained assistant to a lawyer

Examples

She worked as a paralegal before deciding to attend law school.
The law firm is hiring a new paralegal to help prepare client documents.
As a paralegal, he performed extensive research for the attorneys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legal assistant - a person who assists lawyers in legal matters

Etymology

From 'para-' (beside) + 'legal' (relating to the law); coined in the 20th century.
