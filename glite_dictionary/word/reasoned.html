reasoned

1. ADJ

/ ˈriːzənd / (AmE), / ˈriːzənd / (BrE)
(of an argument or statement) based on careful thought, logic, or reasoning; logically valid.
based on careful thought or logic

Examples

She presented a reasoned analysis of the evidence during the meeting.
A reasoned decision is always preferable to a rushed one.
Their disagreement was settled through a reasoned discussion rather than an argument.

Synset Examples

a sound argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logical - using or according to strict principles of validity
  • Antonym: irrational - not logical or reasonable

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'reason' and the suffix '-ed', meaning 'having been reasoned'.
