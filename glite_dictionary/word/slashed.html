slashed

1. ADJ

/ slæʃt / (AmE), / slæʃt / (BrE)
(of a garment) having long, narrow ornamental cuts that show an underlying fabric, especially as a historical or decorative fashion feature.
decorated with cuts revealing fabric beneath

Examples

The queen wore a slashed velvet gown with bright silk lining peeking through the cuts.
The actor's costume featured slashed sleeves that revealed gold brocade beneath.
The exhibition showcased a doublet with slashed cuffs, typical of Renaissance fashion.

Synset Examples

a slashed doublet
slashed cuffs showing the scarlet lining

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - made with an opening or incision

2. ADJ

/ slæʃt / (AmE), / slɑːʃt / (BrE)
(of rates or prices) reduced sharply, especially by a large amount.
sharply reduced (of prices/rates)

Examples

The store advertised slashed prices throughout the entire holiday weekend.
Many airlines offered slashed fares to boost passenger numbers during the off-season.
Thanks to the slashed rent, she was finally able to move into an apartment in the city.

Synset Examples

the slashed prices attracted buyers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - (of prices) lowered or reduced
  • Antonym: raised - (of prices) increased

3. ADJ

/ slæʃt / (AmE), / slæʃt / (BrE)
Having patterns or markings made by sweeping, often diagonal strokes of color or lines.
marked by sweeping color strokes

Examples

The artist's canvas was slashed with bold streaks of crimson and gold.
She wore a robe slashed with silver, shimmering in the dim light.
The fabric was deep blue, slashed with bright green lines, giving it a dramatic look.

Synset Examples

brown iris … slashed with yellow

Etymology

From ‘slash’ (to cut or strike with a sweeping motion), used metaphorically for strokes of color.
