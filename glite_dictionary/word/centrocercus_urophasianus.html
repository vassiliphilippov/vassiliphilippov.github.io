Centrocercus urophasianus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntroʊˈsɜːrkəs juːˈroʊˌfeɪziˈænəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəʊˈsɜːkəs juːˈrəʊfeɪziˈænəs / (BrE)
a large species of grouse native to the sagebrush regions of North America, commonly known as the greater sage-grouse.
large grouse of North American sagebrush

Examples

The Centrocercus urophasianus relies on sagebrush habitats for its survival.
Researchers monitored a population of Centrocercus urophasianus during the breeding season.
Efforts to conserve Centrocercus urophasianus are crucial for preserving sagebrush ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greater sage-grouse - common name for Centrocercus urophasianus

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Centrocercus' from Greek 'kentron' (spur) and 'kerkos' (tail); 'urophasianus' from Greek 'oura' (tail) and 'phasianos' (pheasant).
