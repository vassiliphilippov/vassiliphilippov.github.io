haversack

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæv.ɚ.sæk / (AmE), / ˈhæv.ə.sæk / (BrE)
a small, sturdy bag carried by a single shoulder strap, typically used for carrying supplies or personal belongings
a bag with a single shoulder strap

Examples

He packed his lunch in a haversack before heading out for his hike.
The soldier slung his haversack over one shoulder and marched onward.
My grandfather used to carry his fishing gear in a worn leather haversack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backpack - a bag carried on the back with two shoulder straps
  • Synonym: satchel - a bag with a strap, usually for carrying books

Etymology

From French *havresac*, via German *Habersack* (oat-sack or ration bag).
