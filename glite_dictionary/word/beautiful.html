beautiful

1. ADJ

/ ˈbjuːtəfəl / (AmE), / ˈbjuːtəfəl / (BrE)
(of weather) highly enjoyable and pleasant.
of weather: highly enjoyable

Examples

It was a beautiful day for a picnic, with clear skies and warm sunshine.
We woke up to beautiful weather, perfect for a long walk in the park.
The forecast promises beautiful conditions for the outdoor concert this evening.

Synset Examples

what a beautiful day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lovely - (of weather) very pleasant or enjoyable

Etymology

From Old French *beaulté, beauté* (beauty), from Latin *bellus* (pretty, handsome).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbjuːtəfəl / (AmE), / ˈbjuːtəf(ə)l / (BrE)
pleasing to the senses or to the mind; giving great pleasure, especially in appearance, sound, or thought
pleasing to the senses or mind

Examples

The garden looked especially beautiful after the rain, with every flower glistening in the light.
She gave a beautiful performance that captivated the entire audience.
Many people consider the mountain landscape to be one of the most beautiful sights in the world.

Synset Examples

a beautiful child
beautiful country
a beautiful painting
a beautiful theory
a beautiful party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lovely - very attractive or beautiful
  • Antonym: ugly - unpleasant to look at; not beautiful

Etymology

From Old French 'bel' or 'beau' (beautiful) and '-ful' (full of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00219320-a
Synset POS: a
