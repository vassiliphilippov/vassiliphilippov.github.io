clappers

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæpɚz / (AmE), / ˈklæpəz / (BrE)
A percussion instrument made of a pair of hollow pieces of wood or bone, which are clicked together in rhythm, typically used by dancers.
percussion instrument clicked together by dancers

Examples

The flamenco dancer used clappers to keep time with her intricate footwork.
Children made homemade clappers for the school music project.
He tapped the clappers together sharply, accenting the rhythm of the traditional song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castanets - small percussion instruments clicked together, often used in Spanish music and dance
