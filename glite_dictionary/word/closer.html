closer

1. ADV

/ ˈkloʊsər / (AmE), / ˈkləʊsə / (BrE)
At a shorter distance from a point or person than before or than someone or something else.
at a shorter distance (comparative adverb)

Examples

Could you please come closer so I can hear you better?
As the train approached, it seemed to get closer and louder.
She moved closer to the painting to examine the details.

Synset Examples

come closer, my dear!
they drew nearer
getting nearer to the true explanation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nearer - comparative form of 'near', meaning at a shorter distance
  • Antonym: further - at a greater distance
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊzər / (AmE), / ˈkləʊzə / (BrE)
a person who closes something, such as a building, business, or event, especially at the end of a day or activity
person who closes something

Examples

The closer is responsible for setting the store alarm each night.
She works as a closer at the local café, locking up after everyone leaves.
After everyone else leaves, the closer checks each room to make sure all the lights are off.

Synset Examples

whoever is the closer has to turn out the lights and lock up

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opener - a person who opens something

Etymology

From the verb 'close' + agent noun suffix '-er'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09949922-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊzər / (AmE), / ˈkləʊzə / (BrE)
(Baseball) A relief pitcher who is brought in to finish a game by protecting a team's lead in the final inning or innings.
baseball relief pitcher for final innings

Examples

The team's closer struck out the final batter to secure the win.
As a rookie closer, he quickly made a name for himself with his fastball.
They brought in their best closer to finish the ninth inning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relief pitcher - a pitcher who enters the game after the starting pitcher
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
