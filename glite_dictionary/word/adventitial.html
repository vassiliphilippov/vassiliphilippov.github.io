adventitial

1. ADJ

/ ˌæd.vɛnˈtɪʃ.əl / (AmE), / ˌad.vɛnˈtɪʃ.əl / (BrE)
relating to the adventitia, the outermost connective tissue covering of an organ, vessel, or other structure in the body
relating to the outer tissue layer (adventitia)
medical

Examples

The adventitial layer of the artery provides structural support and flexibility.
Inflammation limited to the adventitial tissue can complicate vascular diseases.
Researchers studied changes in adventitial cells during the development of atherosclerosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adventitia - the outermost connective tissue covering of an organ or vessel

Etymology

From adventitia (Latin for 'coming from outside'), with the suffix -al.
