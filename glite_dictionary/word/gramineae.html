Gramineae

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈmɪniˌi / (AmE), / ɡrəˈmɪniːiː / (BrE)
a large family of chiefly herbaceous plants, including all true grasses such as cereals, bamboos, reeds, and sugar cane
the botanical family of grasses

Examples

Bamboo is a member of the Gramineae, alongside more familiar grasses like wheat and corn.
The Gramineae family includes plants that are crucial for food production worldwide.
Scientists study the Gramineae to learn more about crop evolution and adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Poaceae - the scientific name currently preferred for the grass family

Etymology

From Latin 'gramineus' meaning 'grassy', from 'gramen' (grass).
