whirring

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːrɪŋ / (BrE)
a continuous, low, rapid sound produced by something moving quickly or spinning
continuous low, rapid sound

Examples

The whirring of the fan lulled him to sleep on the hot afternoon.
She was distracted by the whirring in the hallway as the vacuum cleaner passed by.
A sudden whirring from the garden made them look up just in time to see a hummingbird fly past.

Synset Examples

whir of a bird's wings
the whir of the propellers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buzz - a continuous humming sound
  • Synonym: hum - a low, steady, continuous sound
  • Antonym: silence - absence of sound

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɜrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːrɪŋ / (BrE)
making a continuous, soft, rapid buzzing or humming sound, typically like that of rapidly vibrating wings or machinery.
making a continuous buzzing or humming sound

Examples

The whirring of the ceiling fan lulled her to sleep during the hot afternoon.
A whirring noise came from the hummingbird's wings as it hovered by the feeder.
He was distracted by the whirring sound of the printer starting up behind him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buzzing - making a low, continuous humming sound
