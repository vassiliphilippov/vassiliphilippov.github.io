adrenocorticotropin

1. NOUN

/ əˌdrinoʊˌkɔrtɪkoʊˈtroʊpɪn / (AmE), / əˌdriːnəʊˌkɔːtɪkəʊˈtrəʊpɪn / (BrE)
A hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the adrenal cortex to release corticosteroids.
pituitary hormone stimulating adrenal cortex
medical

Examples

The production of adrenocorticotropin increases in response to stress.
Doctors measured the patient's adrenocorticotropin levels to assess adrenal function.
A deficiency of adrenocorticotropin can lead to adrenal insufficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adrenocorticotropic hormone - alternative name for adrenocorticotropin

Etymology

From adreno- (relating to the adrenal glands) + cortico- (referring to the cortex) + tropin (‘stimulating’ hormone).
