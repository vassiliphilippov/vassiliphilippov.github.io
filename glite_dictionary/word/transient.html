transient

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrænziənt / (AmE), / ˈtrænziənt / (BrE)
lasting only for a short time; not permanent
lasting briefly; not permanent

Examples

Many city residents are only transient visitors, staying for a few months before moving on.
The beauty of the sunset was transient, fading away in a matter of minutes.
Happiness can be transient, so it's important to cherish joyful moments while they last.

Synset Examples

the ephemeral joys of childhood
a passing fancy
youth's transient beauty
love is transitory but it is eternal
fugacious blossoms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ephemeral - lasting for a very short time
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely without change
  • Synonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time

Etymology

From Latin 'transiens', present participle of 'transire' (to go across).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrænziənt / (AmE), / ˈtrænziənt / (BrE)
a person or worker who stays in a place for only a short period of time
a person staying only briefly

Examples

The shelter provides beds for transients passing through the city.
Many transients find short-term work during harvest season.
Hotels near the station often cater to transients who need accommodations for just one night.

Synset Examples

transient laborers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visitor - a person visiting a place but not permanently residing there
  • Antonym: resident - a person who lives somewhere permanently or on a long-term basis

Etymology

From Latin transiens, present participle of transīre (to go across).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtrænziənt / (AmE), / ˈtrænziənt / (BrE)
(physics) A short-lived oscillation in a system that occurs following a sudden change in voltage, current, or load.
short-lived oscillation in physics
medical

Examples

The amplifier produced a noticeable transient when it was first switched on.
Electrical equipment can be damaged by a sudden transient on the power line.
Engineers analyzed the transient to understand the system's response to the power surge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surge - A sudden, temporary increase in electrical current or voltage
  • Antonym: steady state - The condition where a system's properties are unchanging in time

Etymology

From Latin transiens, present participle of transire (to go across).
