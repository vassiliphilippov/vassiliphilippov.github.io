hypovolaemia

1. NOUN

/ haɪ.poʊ.vəˈliː.mi.ə / (AmE), / haɪ.pəʊ.vəˈliː.mi.ə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which there is a decreased volume of circulating blood in the body
decreased blood volume condition
medical

Examples

The patient developed hypovolaemia after severe blood loss from the accident.
Doctors quickly treated his hypovolaemia with intravenous fluids.
If left untreated, hypovolaemia can lead to shock and organ failure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypovolemia - decreased blood volume condition (US spelling)
  • Antonym: hypervolemia - increased blood volume condition

Etymology

From Greek hypo- (under) + Latin volum(en) (volume) + -aemia (blood condition).
