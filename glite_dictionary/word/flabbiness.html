flabbiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæbinəs / (AmE), / ˈflabɪnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being soft, loose, or lacking firmness, especially in body tissue or muscles.
softness; lack of firmness (esp. body)

Examples

The flabbiness of his arms showed he hadn't exercised in months.
She worried about the increasing flabbiness of her stomach after the holidays.
The physical therapist designed a routine to address muscle flabbiness in elderly patients.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: firmness - the quality of being solid or strong, not soft
  • Synonym: softness - the quality of being soft

Etymology

From flabby + -ness (suffix forming nouns denoting quality or state).
