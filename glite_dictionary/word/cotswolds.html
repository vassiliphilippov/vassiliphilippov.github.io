Cotswolds

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɑtsˌwoʊldz / (AmE), / ˈkɒtsˌwəʊldz / (BrE)
an area of rolling hills and picturesque countryside in southwestern and central England, noted for its charming villages and scenic beauty
hilly region in southwestern England

Examples

She spent a relaxing weekend exploring the charming villages of the Cotswolds.
The Cotswolds are famous for their picturesque landscapes and traditional stone cottages.
Many tourists visit the Cotswolds each year to enjoy walking and cycling trails.

Etymology

From Old English 'cot' (cottage) and 'wold' (wooded upland or hill).
