Ashir

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːʃɪr / (AmE), / ˈɑːʃɪə / (BrE)
The chief god of the Assyrians, associated with military power and empire, and identified with the Babylonian god Anshar.
chief god of the Assyrians

Examples

In ancient Assyria, Ashir was venerated as the supreme deity and protector of the empire.
King Ashurbanipal claimed that Ashir granted him victory over his enemies.
The temples dedicated to Ashir were central to Assyrian religious life.

Etymology

From ancient Assyrian, possibly related to Anshar of Babylonian mythology.
