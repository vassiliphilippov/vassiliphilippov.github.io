Spartina pectinmata

1. NOUN

/ spɑrˈtaɪnə pɛkˈtɪnmeɪtə / (AmE), / spɑːˈtaɪnə pɛkˈtɪnmɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of North American cordgrass distinguished by its leaves with dry, membranous margins and glumes that have long awns.
a North American cordgrass species

Examples

The wetland restoration project included planting Spartina pectinmata along the shoreline.
Researchers studied the unique glumes of Spartina pectinmata for their role in salt tolerance.
Local wildlife relies on Spartina pectinmata habitats for nesting and feeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cordgrass - any plant of the genus Spartina, saltmarsh grass

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Spartina' denotes a genus of grasses and 'pectinmata' derives from 'pecten' meaning comb, referring to the comb-like appearance of features on the plant.
