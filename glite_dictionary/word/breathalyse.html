to breathalyse

1. VERB

/ ˈbrɛθəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbrɛθəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to test someone's alcohol level in their blood by using a breathalyzer
test for alcohol using breathalyzer
british

Examples

The police decided to breathalyse the driver after he was seen swerving on the road.
All motorists involved in the accident were breathalysed by the officers at the scene.
She was relieved when she passed the test after being breathalysed late at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to breathalyze - American English spelling of 'breathalyse'

Etymology

From 'breath' and '-alyse', modeled after 'analyse', first used in the UK.
