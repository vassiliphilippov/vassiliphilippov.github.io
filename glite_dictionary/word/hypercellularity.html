hypercellularity

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərˌsɛl.jəˈlɛr.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪ.pəˌsɛl.jʊˈlær.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the condition of having an abnormally high number of cells in a tissue or organ, often indicating disease
abnormally high cell count
medical

Examples

A diagnosis of hypercellularity was confirmed by examining the bone marrow biopsy.
Hypercellularity in the tissue sample suggested an ongoing pathological process.
The pathologist noted marked hypercellularity in several areas of the tumor.

Etymology

From hyper- (excessive) + cellular + -ity, indicating a state with excess cells.
