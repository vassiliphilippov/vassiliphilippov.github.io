angiotelectasia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.dʒioʊ.tɛlˈteɪ.ʒə / (AmE), / ˌan.dʒi.əʊ.tɛlˈteɪ.zɪə / (BrE)
Abnormal dilation and enlargement of arterioles.
dilation of arterioles
medical

Examples

The patient's diagnosis revealed significant angiotelectasia in the small intestine.
Chronic hypertension can sometimes result in angiotelectasia within affected organs.
Researchers studied the relationship between angiotelectasia and increased vascular permeability.

Etymology

From Greek 'angeion' (vessel) + 'telos' (end) + 'ektasis' (expansion, dilation).
