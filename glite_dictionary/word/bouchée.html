bouchée

1. NOUN

/ buːˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / buːˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
a small shell of puff pastry filled with a savory mixture, served as an appetizer or hors d'oeuvre
small puff pastry shell for appetizers

Examples

The chef filled each bouchée with a delicate mushroom and cream sauce.
We served crab salad in tiny bouchées at the party.
A well-made bouchée should be crisp and golden on the outside.

Etymology

From French 'bouchée' (mouthful).
