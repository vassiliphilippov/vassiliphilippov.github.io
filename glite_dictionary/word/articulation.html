articulation

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌtɪkjuˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of expressing thoughts or feelings clearly in words
clear verbal expression

Examples

Her articulation of complex ideas made the presentation easy to understand.
He struggled with the articulation of his emotions during the meeting.
The articulation of policy goals was clear and convincing.

Synset Examples

the articulation of my feelings
I gave voice to my feelings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expression - the process of making known one's thoughts or feelings
  • Synonym: voicing - the act of expressing or stating something in words

Etymology

From Latin articulatio (a joint, joining), from articulare (to divide into joints).

5. NOUN

Examples

Engineers studied the articulation of the robot's limbs to improve its mobility.
Proper articulation in machinery helps reduce wear and tear between moving parts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joint - a point at which two parts are joined to allow movement

Etymology

From Latin articulatio (a joint, a joining), from articulare (to divide into joints).
