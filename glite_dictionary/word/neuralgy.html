neuralgy

1. NOUN

/ nʊˈrældʒi / (AmE), / njʊəˈrældʒi / (BrE)
A condition characterized by acute, spasmodic pain along the course of one or more nerves.
acute nerve pain disorder
medical

Examples

Her doctor diagnosed the persistent facial pain as a form of neuralgy.
Patients suffering from neuralgy may experience sudden, severe pain that comes and goes unpredictably.
He sought treatment for neuralgy after experiencing sharp stabs of pain running down his arm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neuralgia - acute sharp pain along a nerve or nerves

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'neuralgia,' from Greek neuron (nerve) + algos (pain).
