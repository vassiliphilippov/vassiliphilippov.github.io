semifinal

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmiˈfaɪnəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈfaɪnəl / (BrE)
one of the two matches or rounds that come immediately before the final in a competition, the winners of which compete in the final
one of two matches before a final

Examples

Both teams played their best in the semifinal to secure a spot in the championship game.
She was disappointed when she lost in the semifinal after leading most of the match.
The semifinals are scheduled for next Saturday at the main stadium.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: final - the last match or round of a competition
  • Degree Lower: quarterfinal - one of the four matches or rounds before the semifinals in a competition

Etymology

From semi- (half) + final, meaning the round before the final.
