blueberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌbɛri / (BrE)
A small, sweet, edible dark-blue berry that grows on various shrubs, often used in cooking and baking.
small, edible dark-blue berry

Examples

She added fresh blueberries to her morning cereal for extra flavor.
The blueberry pie was a hit at the family picnic.
Wild blueberries are smaller and have a more intense taste than cultivated ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bilberry - a similar small edible blue-black berry from a different shrub

Etymology

From blue + berry, referring to its color and nature as a berry.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbluˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌbɛri / (BrE)
Any of various shrubs of the genus Vaccinium that produce small, typically blue edible berries.
a shrub producing edible blue berries

Examples

The blueberry bush in our backyard produces fruit every summer.
Wild blueberry plants grow abundantly in northern forests.
She planted three blueberry shrubs along the garden path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranberry - a shrub of the genus Vaccinium with edible red berries
  • Synonym: huckleberry - a shrub similar to blueberry producing edible berries
