knawel

1. NOUN

/ nɔːˈwɛl / (AmE), / ˈnɔːwəl / (BrE)
A low-growing Eurasian herb with narrow leaves and inconspicuous green flowers, widely distributed in sandy soils.
low-growing Eurasian herb

Examples

The sandy field was covered in patches of knawel thriving among the grasses.
Recently, botanists identified both annual and perennial knawel in the area.
Gardeners sometimes struggle to control knawel due to its rapid spread in poor soils.

Etymology

From German *Knäuel* (ball of thread; applied to the plant for its tangled growth), ultimately from Middle High German.
