demoniac

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmoʊniˌæk / (AmE), / dɪˈməʊniˌak / (BrE)
A person who behaves as if possessed by a demon; one believed to be controlled or driven by evil spirits.
person said to be possessed by a demon
archaic

Examples

The villagers believed that the wild-eyed demoniac was responsible for the strange happenings.
In medieval times, exorcisms were performed on anyone thought to be a demoniac.
The priest offered prayers to help the demoniac find peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: madman - a person who behaves in a wildly foolish manner
  • Antonym: sane person - someone of sound mind

Etymology

From Latin *daemoniacus*, from Greek *daimoniakos* (possessed by a demon).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈmoʊniˌæk / (AmE), / dɪˈməʊniˌak / (BrE)
Resembling or relating to a demon, or affected by demonic possession.
like or relating to a demon

Examples

There was a demoniac fury in his eyes as he shouted at the crowd.
The novel describes the demoniac possession of a young girl in vivid detail.
His demoniac laughter echoed through the empty halls, sending chills down everyone's spine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demonic - relating to or characteristic of demons

Etymology

From Latin 'daemoniacus', from Greek 'daimoniakos' (possessed by a demon).
