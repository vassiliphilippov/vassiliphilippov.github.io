accidentally

1. ADV

/ ˌæk.sɪˈdɛn.t̬əl.i / (AmE), / ˌæk.sɪˈden.təl.i / (BrE)
In a way that happens by chance or without intention; not planned or deliberate.
by chance; not intentionally

Examples

I accidentally deleted the important file from my computer.
She accidentally spilled her coffee on the report.
They met accidentally at a café while traveling in Europe.

Synset Examples

they met accidentally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deliberately - in a way that is planned or intended
  • Antonym: intentionally - on purpose; with intention

Etymology

From 'accidental' (from Latin accidentalis), with -ly forming the adverb.

2. ADV

/ ˌæksɪˈdɛntəli / (AmE), / ˌæksɪˈdentəli / (BrE)
In a way that happens by accident; without intending to.
by accident; unintentionally

Examples

He accidentally deleted all of the important files from his computer.
She accidentally spilled coffee on her shirt before the meeting began.
I must have accidentally switched their names in the registration form.

Synset Examples

she hit him unintentionally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intentionally - on purpose; with intention
  • Antonym: deliberately - on purpose; intentionally
  • Synonym: unintentionally - without intention; not on purpose

Etymology

From the adjective 'accidental' + adverbial suffix '-ly', originating from Latin 'accidentalis'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00063188-r
Synset POS: r
