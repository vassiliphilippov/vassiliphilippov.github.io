steerer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪrər / (AmE), / ˈstɪərə / (BrE)
a person who cunningly leads someone into danger, typically as part of a scheme or plot
one who leads others into danger by trickery

Examples

The unsuspecting tourist was targeted by a steerer working with local thieves.
She realized too late that the charming man was actually a steerer leading her into a trap.
Police arrested the steerer who had lured several victims to the scam artists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoy - a person who entices someone into danger
  • Degree Lower: guide - a person who leads or directs others

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɪrər / (AmE), / ˈstɪərə / (BrE)
a person who steers a ship or other vehicle
person who steers a ship

Examples

The steerer kept the boat on course despite the strong currents.
In the canoe race, the steerer must constantly adjust the direction.
As soon as the fog lifted, the steerer spotted the lighthouse ahead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helmsman - person who steers a ship, especially at the wheel
  • Synonym: pilot - person who navigates or steers a ship

Etymology

From steer + -er, formed by adding the agent suffix -er to the verb 'steer'.
