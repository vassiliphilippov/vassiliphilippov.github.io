Sapindus

1. NOUN

/ səˈpɪn.dəs / (AmE), / səˈpɪn.dəs / (BrE)
A genus of trees and shrubs in the family Sapindaceae, commonly known as soapberries or soapnuts, native to warm temperate and tropical regions.
a genus of trees and shrubs (soapberries)

Examples

The seeds of Sapindus species are often used as natural soap in some cultures.
Many Sapindus trees produce berries that can be used for cleaning purposes.
The botanist presented her research on the distribution of Sapindus across Asia and the Americas.

Etymology

From Latin *sapindus*, from 'sapo' (soap) + 'indus' (of India), referring to the soap-like qualities of its fruit.
