Cnidosporidia

1. NOUN

/ naɪdəˌspɔːˈrɪdiə / (AmE), / naɪdəˌspɔːˈrɪdiə / (BrE)
A group of single-host microscopic parasites that infect lower vertebrates and invertebrates.
group of single-host microscopic parasites
medical

Examples

Cnidosporidia are frequently found in the tissues of freshwater fish, causing various diseases.
Researchers are studying how Cnidosporidia infect their invertebrate hosts in aquatic environments.
The life cycle of Cnidosporidia does not require more than one host species.

Etymology

From Greek 'knidē' (nettle) and 'spora' (seed, spore), referring to the spore-forming nature and stinging cells of these organisms.
