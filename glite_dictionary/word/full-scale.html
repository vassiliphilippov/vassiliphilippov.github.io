full-scale

1. ADJ

/ fʊl skeɪl / (AmE), / fʊl skeɪl / (BrE)
using all available resources or carried out to the fullest extent; complete or total in scope
using all available resources; complete

Examples

The government launched a full-scale investigation into the incident.
Negotiations broke down, leading to a full-scale conflict between the two organizations.
They are preparing for a full-scale marketing campaign to promote the new product.

Synset Examples

all-out war
a full-scale campaign against nuclear power plants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all-out - using all one's resources; with maximum effort
  • Antonym: partial - not complete; existing only in part
