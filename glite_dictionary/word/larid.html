larid

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛrɪd / (AmE), / ˈlærɪd / (BrE)
a long-winged, web-footed aquatic bird belonging to the gull family (Laridae)
bird of the gull family

Examples

The coastline is home to several species of larid that can be seen soaring overhead.
Researchers observed the feeding behavior of a larid colony during the nesting season.
Unlike some other seabirds, larids are known for their adaptability to urban environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gull - a common long-winged seabird related to the larid family

Etymology

From New Latin Laridae, the family name, based on genus Larus.
