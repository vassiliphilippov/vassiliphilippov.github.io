Chrysanthemum ptarmiciflorum

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˌsænθəˈmʌm ptɑːrˌmɪsɪˈflɔːrəm / (AmE), / krɪˌsænθɪˈmʌm ptɑːˌmɪsɪˈflɔːrəm / (BrE)
A shrubby perennial plant native to the Canary Islands, with white flowers, white leaves, and hairy stems covered in dustlike down, sometimes classified under the genus Chrysanthemum.
plant species from the Canary Islands

Examples

Many gardeners admire the Chrysanthemum ptarmiciflorum for its striking white foliage and delicate blooms.
The Chrysanthemum ptarmiciflorum thrives in well-drained soil and sunny locations typical of the Canary Islands.
Researchers are studying how Chrysanthemum ptarmiciflorum adapts to arid environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) + 'anthemon' (flower) and 'ptarmicos' (causing sneezing) + 'florum' (flower).
