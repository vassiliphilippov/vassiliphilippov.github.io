Newcastle upon Tyne

1. PROPN

/ ˌnuːˌkæsəl əˌpɑn ˈtaɪn / (AmE), / ˌnjuːˌkɑːsəl əˌpɒn ˈtaɪn / (BrE)
A city and port in northeastern England on the River Tyne, historically known for coal exports and industry.
city in northeastern England

Examples

He was born and raised in Newcastle upon Tyne before moving to London for university.
Many tourists visit Newcastle upon Tyne to admire its historic bridges and vibrant nightlife.
The River Tyne runs through the heart of Newcastle upon Tyne, giving the city its distinctive character.

Etymology

From 'New Castle' (Norman castle built in 1080) and 'upon Tyne' describing its location on the River Tyne.

2. PROPN

/ ˈnuːˌkæsəl əˈpɑn taɪn / (AmE), / ˈnjuːˌkɑːsəl əˈpɒn taɪn / (BrE)
a city in northeastern England on the River Tyne, known for its history as a port and center for coal exports
city in northeastern England

Examples

Many tourists visit Newcastle upon Tyne to explore its vibrant nightlife and historic landmarks.
The River Tyne runs through Newcastle upon Tyne, shaping much of the city's industrial heritage.
She moved to Newcastle upon Tyne for university and quickly fell in love with the city.

Etymology

Named for the 'New Castle' built in 1080 by Robert Curthose on the River Tyne.
