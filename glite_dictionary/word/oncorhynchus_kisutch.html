Oncorhynchus kisutch

1. NOUN

/ ɑŋ.kəˈraɪŋ.kəs kɪˈsutʃ / (AmE), / ɒŋ.kəˈrɪŋ.kəs kɪˈsʊtʃ / (BrE)
a small salmon native to the northern Pacific coasts and introduced to the Great Lakes, valued for its flesh
species of salmon (coho)

Examples

The population of Oncorhynchus kisutch has increased in the Great Lakes due to conservation efforts.
Many anglers travel to Alaska each year to fish for Oncorhynchus kisutch during the spawning season.
The diet of Oncorhynchus kisutch primarily consists of small fish and aquatic insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coho salmon - another name for Oncorhynchus kisutch

Etymology

From New Latin; genus Oncorhynchus from Greek onkos (hook) + rhynchos (snout), species name from Russian (kisutch).
