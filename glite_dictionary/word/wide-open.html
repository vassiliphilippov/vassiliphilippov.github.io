wide-open

1. ADJ

/ waɪd ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / waɪd ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
completely open to the widest extent possible; not closed or partly closed
completely open; fully open

Examples

She left the window wide-open to let in the fresh air.
The main gate stood wide-open after the parade.
His mouth was wide-open in amazement at the news.

Synset Examples

left the doors wide-open

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: open - not closed or fastened

2. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪd ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / ˈwaɪd ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
(of a place or system) not strictly regulated or controlled; unusually lax in enforcing laws or rules.
lax in enforcing laws or rules

Examples

The city became wide-open during Prohibition, with illegal bars operating everywhere.
Corruption was rampant in the wide-open port town of the 1920s.
Some politicians accused the local government of maintaining a wide-open approach to gambling laws.

Synset Examples

a wide-open town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lawless - not governed by or obedient to laws; disorderly
  • Antonym: strict - enforcing rules or laws rigorously

Synset POS: s
