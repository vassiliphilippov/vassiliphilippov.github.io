obsessive-compulsive

1. NOUN

/ əbˌsɛsɪv kəmˈpʌlsɪv / (AmE), / əbˌsɛsɪv kəmˈpʌlsɪv / (BrE)
a person who has obsessive thoughts and compulsive behavior, especially as a symptom of obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD)
person with obsessive-compulsive tendencies
medical

Examples

Her brother is an obsessive-compulsive who spends hours cleaning every surface in the house.
Some obsessive-compulsives find it difficult to leave home without completing their rituals.
The support group welcomes anyone who identifies as an obsessive-compulsive or has been diagnosed with OCD.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: OCD - obsessive-compulsive disorder, a mental health condition

2. ADJ

/ əbˌsɛsɪv kəmˈpʌlsɪv / (AmE), / əbˌsɛsɪv kəmˈpʌlsɪv / (BrE)
characterized by a tendency to experience recurrent, unwanted thoughts (obsessions) and to perform repetitive behaviors (compulsions), often in a way that interferes with daily life
having obsessions and compulsions
medical

Examples

She has been diagnosed as obsessive-compulsive by her doctor after several months of recurring rituals.
His obsessive-compulsive need for order made sharing an office difficult for his colleagues.
Many people mistakenly believe that being obsessive-compulsive means just liking things clean, but it's much more complex.

Synset Examples

obsessive-compulsive neurosis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: OCD - a mental disorder marked by obsessive thoughts and compulsive behaviors
  • Degree Lower: phobic - characterized by an irrational or excessive fear, often leading to avoidance

Etymology

From 'obsession' (from Latin 'obsessio', a siege) and 'compulsion' (from Latin 'compellere', to drive together).
