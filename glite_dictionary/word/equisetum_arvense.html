Equisetum arvense

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkwɪˈsiːtəm ɑːrˈvɛnseɪ / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈsiːtəm ɑːˈvɛnsɪ / (BrE)
A species of horsetail plant native to Eurasia, Greenland, and North America, known for its feathery, brush-like stems.
species of horsetail plant

Examples

The dried Equisetum arvense is sometimes used in herbal remedies for its diuretic properties.
You can often spot Equisetum arvense growing along riverbanks in spring and early summer.
Due to its rapid spread, Equisetum arvense can quickly become a nuisance in gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsetail - a plant of the genus Equisetum

Etymology

From Latin: 'equisetum' meaning horsetail, 'arvense' meaning of fields.
