horseshoe

1. VERB

/ ˈhɔrsˌʃu / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsʃuː / (BrE)
to fit a horse with a horseshoe or horseshoes
fit a horse with horseshoes

Examples

The blacksmith will horseshoe the mare tomorrow morning.
Experienced farriers know exactly how to horseshoe even the most restless horses.
They horseshoed all the horses before the long journey began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoe - to fit (an animal, especially a horse) with a shoe

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌʃu / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsˌʃuː / (BrE)
A U-shaped metal plate nailed to the underside of a horse's hoof to protect it from wear.
U-shaped plate for horse's hoof

Examples

The blacksmith fitted a new horseshoe to the mare's hoof.
A horseshoe protects the horse's hoof from rough ground and injury.
During the game, participants tossed a horseshoe toward a metal stake.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: shoe - A covering for the foot, typically made of leather, having a sturdy sole and not reaching above the ankle

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌʃu / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsʃuː / (BrE)
an open ring of iron, often resembling the shape of a U, used as a piece of equipment in the game of horseshoes
iron ring used in the game of horseshoes

Examples

The players took turns tossing the horseshoe toward the stake in the center of the lawn.
Each horseshoe must be thrown from behind the designated line in official tournaments.
She bought a new set of horseshoes before hosting the backyard competition.
