boniface

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːnɪˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɒnɪˌfeɪs / (BrE)
an innkeeper, especially the owner or manager of an inn
innkeeper; inn owner/manager

Examples

The cheerful boniface greeted every traveler by name as they entered the inn.
In classic novels, the boniface often serves as a source of local gossip and wisdom.
We stopped at a small tavern, where the boniface prepared us a hearty supper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innkeeper - the person who owns or manages an inn

Etymology

From the name Boniface, referring to a jovial innkeeper in Farquhar's 1707 play 'The Beaux' Stratagem.'
