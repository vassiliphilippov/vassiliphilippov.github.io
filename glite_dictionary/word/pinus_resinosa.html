Pinus resinosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.nəs rɛˈzɪn.oʊ.sə / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.nəs rɛˈzɪn.əʊ.sə / (BrE)
a species of pine tree native to eastern North America, noted for its long needles in pairs and reddish bark; also known as red pine
North American pine tree species

Examples

The Pinus resinosa is a common sight in forests throughout the northeastern United States.
Foresters are working to protect stands of Pinus resinosa from invasive pests.
Many wooden structures in the region are built from the strong timber of Pinus resinosa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red pine - common name for Pinus resinosa, a North American pine species

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pinus' meaning pine, 'resinosa' meaning resinous.
