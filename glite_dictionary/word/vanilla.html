vanilla

1. NOUN

/ vəˈnɪlə / (AmE), / vəˈnɪlə / (BrE)
A distinctive sweet and fragrant flavor characteristic of vanilla beans, widely used in cooking and baking.
sweet, fragrant flavor from vanilla beans

Examples

I prefer vanilla ice cream to chocolate.
The cake has a subtle vanilla flavor that everyone enjoyed.
She added a teaspoon of vanilla to the batter for extra aroma.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chocolate - a flavor made from cocoa beans
  • Degree Lower: strawberry - a sweet flavor made from strawberries

Etymology

From Spanish 'vainilla', diminutive of 'vaina' (pod), referring to the shape of the vanilla bean.

4. NOUN

/ vəˈnɪlə / (AmE), / vəˈnɪlə / (BrE)
A tropical climbing plant of the genus Vanilla, with fleshy leaves and clusters of large, fragrant white, green, or yellowish flowers, cultivated for its pods used in flavoring.
a tropical climbing plant used for flavoring

Examples

The vanilla plant thrives in the humid climates of Madagascar and Mexico.
Farmers carefully pollinate the flowers of the vanilla to ensure a good harvest of pods.
Botanists classify vanilla among orchids because of its distinctive flowers.

Etymology

From Spanish 'vainilla', diminutive of 'vaina' (pod), from Latin 'vāgīna' (sheath).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-12107056-n
Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ vəˈnɪlə / (AmE), / vəˈnɪlə / (BrE)
a flavoring made by extracting compounds from vanilla beans in alcohol, or an artificial flavor that imitates vanilla
flavoring from vanilla beans or imitation

Examples

She added a drop of vanilla to the cake batter for extra flavor.
The ice cream tastes more natural when made with real vanilla instead of artificial flavors.
You can buy vanilla extract at most grocery stores in the baking aisle.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Spanish 'vainilla', diminutive of 'vaina' (pod), referring to the pod-like fruit.
