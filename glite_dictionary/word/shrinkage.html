shrinkage

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃrɪŋkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪŋkɪdʒ / (BrE)
the process or result of becoming smaller or reduced in size, amount, or value
process of becoming smaller or less

Examples

There was significant shrinkage in the fabric after it was washed.
Companies often face shrinkage in inventory due to theft or loss.
The shrinkage of glacier ice is a clear sign of climate change.

Synset Examples

the material lost 2 inches per yard in shrinkage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller or less
  • Antonym: expansion - the process of becoming larger or more extensive

Etymology

From shrink + -age, denoting the process or result of shrinking.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃrɪŋkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪŋkɪdʒ / (BrE)
the amount by which something decreases in size, quantity, or value, especially as a result of shrinking
amount by which something shrinks

Examples

The cotton shirt experienced noticeable shrinkage after being washed in hot water.
Retailers track inventory shrinkage to minimize losses from theft or damage.
He was concerned about the shrinkage in profits over the past year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduction - the act or process of making something smaller
  • Antonym: expansion - the process of becoming larger or more extensive

Etymology

From 'shrink' + '-age', denoting result or process.

3. NOUN

/ ˈʃrɪŋkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪŋkɪdʒ / (BrE)
the loss of goods from a store, especially as a result of theft by customers or employees
loss of goods from a store due to theft

Examples

Several stores reported an increase in shrinkage during the busy holiday season.
The manager implemented new security measures to reduce shrinkage and protect inventory.
Employee theft is one of the major contributors to retail shrinkage.

Synset Examples

shrinkage is the retail trade's euphemism for shoplifting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoplifting - the act of stealing goods from a store while pretending to shop
  • Synonym: theft - the act of stealing something
