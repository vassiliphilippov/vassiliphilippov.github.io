ante meridiem

1. ADV

/ ˌæn.teɪ məˈrɪd.i.əm / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ məˈrɪd.i.əm / (BrE)
before midday; used chiefly with times to indicate hours before 12 o’clock noon.
before midday (a.m. time)

Examples

The train leaves at 10 ante meridiem every weekday.
Her doctor’s appointment is scheduled for nine ante meridiem.
We are required to clock in by seven ante meridiem during the summer shift.

Synset Examples

let's meet at 11 A.M.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: post meridiem - after midday; used for times after 12 o’clock noon (p.m.)

Etymology

From Latin 'ante meridiem' meaning 'before midday'.
