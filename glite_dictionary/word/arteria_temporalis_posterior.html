arteria temporalis posterior

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪriə tɛmˈpɔrəlɪs pɑsˈtɪriər / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə tɛmˈpɔːrəlɪs pɒsˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
A branch of the temporal artery that supplies blood to the posterior part of the temporal lobe's cortex.
posterior branch of the temporal artery
medical

Examples

The surgeon identified the arteria temporalis posterior during the delicate brain operation.
Damage to the arteria temporalis posterior can impact blood flow to the temporal lobe.
Detailed maps of the arteria temporalis posterior are found in anatomical atlases.

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery), 'temporalis' (of the temple), 'posterior' (behind).
