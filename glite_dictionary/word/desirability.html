desirability

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌzaɪrəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / dɪˌzaɪərəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being desirable or worthy of being desired
quality of being desirable

Examples

The desirability of the area has increased due to recent renovations.
Employers often consider the desirability of a candidate's skills and experience.
Rising property prices reflect the desirability of living in the city center.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undesirability - the quality of being undesirable or not wanted
  • Synonym: attractiveness - the quality of being attractive

Etymology

From Latin 'desiderabilis' (worthy of desire) via French 'désirabilité'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˌzaɪrəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / dɪˌzaɪrəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being sexually attractive or appealing, especially to the opposite sex
sexual attractiveness or appeal

Examples

Her desirability made her the center of attention at every party.
The magazine ranked celebrities based on their desirability.
He worked hard to improve his desirability by staying fit and confident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appeal - the quality of being attractive or interesting
  • Synonym: allure - the quality of being powerfully and mysteriously attractive
  • Antonym: unattractiveness - the lack of being attractive or desirable

Etymology

From desirable + -ity, patterned after Latin-derived nouns.
