William Gladstone

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm ˈɡlædˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm ˈɡlæd.stən / (BrE)
A British liberal statesman who served as Prime Minister four times between 1868 and 1894.
British Prime Minister (1809–1898)

Examples

William Gladstone was known for his commitment to liberal reforms in 19th-century Britain.
The policies of William Gladstone had a lasting impact on British politics.
Many historians regard William Gladstone as one of Britain's greatest prime ministers.
