Jesus

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒiːzəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːzəs / (BrE)
The central figure of Christianity, believed by Christians to be the Son of God and whose teachings form the basis of the religion.
central figure of Christianity

Examples

Many Christians pray to Jesus for guidance and strength in their daily lives.
The teachings of Jesus emphasize love, forgiveness, and compassion for others.
During Christmas, people around the world celebrate the birth of Jesus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christ - Title for Jesus, meaning 'the anointed one' or Messiah
  • Synonym: Messiah - The promised deliverer in Jewish and Christian belief, often identified as Jesus in Christianity

Etymology

From Latin Iesus, from Greek Iēsous, from Hebrew Yēšūaʿ (short for Yehōšūaʿ, meaning 'God is salvation').
