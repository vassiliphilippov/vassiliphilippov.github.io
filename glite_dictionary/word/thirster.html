thirster

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɝːstər / (AmE), / ˈθɜːstə / (BrE)
A person who has an intense or eager desire for something.
person with strong desire for something

Examples

Among treasure hunters, he quickly earned a reputation as a thirster for fortune and adventure.
She was a thirster after knowledge, spending hours each night reading and researching new subjects.
In those troubled times, many became thirsters after justice, demanding change from their leaders.

Synset Examples

a longer for money
a thirster after blood
a yearner for knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yearner - a person who longs for or desires something deeply
  • Synonym: craver - a person who craves something intensely

Etymology

Formed from 'thirst' (from Old English 'þyrstan') plus the agentive suffix '-er'.
