dabchick

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæbˌtʃɪk / (AmE), / ˈdab.tʃɪk / (BrE)
a small grebe found in Europe, especially the little grebe (Tachybaptus ruficollis)
small European grebe

Examples

The dabchick is often seen diving for insects and small fish on quiet European ponds.
During spring, a pair of dabchicks built a floating nest among the reeds.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare dabchick during their walk along the nature reserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grebe - aquatic diving bird with lobed toes

Etymology

From dab + chick; possibly referring to its dabbling behavior and chick-like appearance.
