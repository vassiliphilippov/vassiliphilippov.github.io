Cabassous unicinctus

1. NOUN

/ kəˈbæsoʊs ˌjuːnɪˈsɪŋktəs / (AmE), / kəˈbæsəs ˌjuːnɪˈsɪŋktəs / (BrE)
A species of naked-tailed armadillo native to tropical South America.
species of naked-tailed armadillo

Examples

The Cabassous unicinctus is known for its distinctive hairless tail and burrowing habits.
Researchers observed several Cabassous unicinctus individuals in the rainforests of Brazil.
The Cabassous unicinctus plays an important role in the ecosystem by aerating the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naked-tailed armadillo - armadillo species with a hairless tail, native to South America

Etymology

From Latin; Cabassous is the genus name and unicinctus means 'single-banded'.
