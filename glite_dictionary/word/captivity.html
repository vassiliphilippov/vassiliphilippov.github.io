captivity

1. NOUN

/ kæpˈtɪv.ə.ti / (AmE), / kæpˈtɪv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state of being held as a slave and not free; the condition of being under the control of someone else
state of being a slave or held prisoner

Examples

After years in captivity, he longed for his freedom.
They were held in captivity and forced to work against their will.
The story describes the horrors of a life spent in captivity as a slave.

Synset Examples

So every bondman in his own hand bears the power to cancel his captivity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freedom - the state of being free rather than enslaved or imprisoned
  • Synonym: slavery - the state or condition of being a slave

Etymology

From Latin captivitas (state of being captive), from captivus (captive).

2. NOUN

/ kæpˈtɪv.ə.ti / (AmE), / kæpˈtɪv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state of being kept as a prisoner or in confinement and not allowed to leave
state of being imprisoned or confined

Examples

The tiger spent years in captivity before being released back into the wild.
After the plane crash, the survivors endured months of captivity in a remote village.
The documentary highlighted the psychological effects of long-term captivity on prisoners.

Synset Examples

he was held in captivity until he died
the imprisonment of captured soldiers
his ignominious incarceration in the local jail
he practiced the immurement of his enemies in the castle dungeon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprisonment - the state of being imprisoned
  • Antonym: freedom - the power or right to act, speak, or think without hindrance or restraint

Etymology

From Latin captivitas, from captivus meaning 'captive.'
