fledged

1. ADJ

/ flɛdʒd / (AmE), / flɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of a bird) having grown the feathers necessary for flight; often used after 'fully' or in combination with another word.
having developed feathers (birds)

Examples

The fledged chicks left the nest for the first time yesterday.
By spring, the sparrows were fully fledged and ready to fly.
A newly fledged robin perched on the garden fence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfledged - (of a bird) not yet having developed feathers

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'fledge', from Old English 'flycge' (having feathers).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01099607-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ flɛdʒd / (AmE), / flɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of an arrow) equipped with feathers for stabilization in flight.
of an arrow, having feathers attached

Examples

The archer selected a fledged arrow from his quiver before taking aim.
Historically, only fledged arrows were used in competitions for accuracy and range.
Without properly fledged arrows, archers would struggle to maintain a straight flight path.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfledged - of an arrow, not equipped with feathers

Etymology

From Old English *fleged*, related to 'fledge', meaning to equip with feathers.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01001800-s
Synset POS: s
