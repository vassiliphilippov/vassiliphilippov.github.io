kilohertz

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪloʊˌhɜrts / (AmE), / ˈkɪləˌhɜːts / (BrE)
A unit of frequency equal to one thousand cycles per second.
unit of frequency (1,000 Hz)

Examples

The processor operates at a frequency of 500 kilohertz.
Most AM radio stations broadcast in the kilohertz range.
Ultrasound machines often emit sound waves above several thousand kilohertz.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hertz - unit of frequency equal to one cycle per second
  • Degree Higher: megahertz - unit of frequency equal to one million cycles per second

Etymology

From kilo- (thousand) + hertz, after Heinrich Hertz.
