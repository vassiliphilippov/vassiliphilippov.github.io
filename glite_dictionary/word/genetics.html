genetics

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛtɪks / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtɪks / (BrE)
The branch of biology concerned with the study of heredity and variation in organisms.
study of heredity in biology

Examples

She is studying genetics at university to better understand inherited diseases.
Recent advances in genetics have revolutionized cancer research.
Understanding genetics helps explain why certain traits run in families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heredity - the passing of traits from parents to offspring
  • Degree Higher: biology - the scientific study of living organisms

Etymology

From the German 'Genetik', derived from Greek 'genesis' (origin, birth).
