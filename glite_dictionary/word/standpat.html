standpat

1. ADJ

/ ˈstændˌpæt / (AmE), / ˈstandˌpat / (BrE)
Holding traditional views and resistant to change; old-fashioned and out of date.
old-fashioned, resistant to change

Examples

The company's standpat policies failed to adapt to the new market trends.
Critics described the administration as standpat, unwilling to implement necessary reforms.
Her standpat attitude frustrated younger colleagues eager for innovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservative - favoring traditional views and values, resistant to change
  • Antonym: progressive - favoring or advocating progress, change, or improvement

Etymology

Originally from the poker term 'stand pat', meaning to keep one's hand, first used figuratively in US politics in the early 20th century.
