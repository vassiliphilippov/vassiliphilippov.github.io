brakeman

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪk.mən / (AmE), / ˈbreɪk.mən / (BrE)
a railroad employee responsible for operating the brakes on a train
railroad employee who operates brakes

Examples

The brakeman signaled to the engineer to slow down the train as it neared the station.
In the early days of railroads, the brakeman had to move along the tops of the cars to apply each brake by hand.
After years of working as a brakeman, he knew every stretch of track by heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conductor - person in charge of a train and its crew

Etymology

From 'brake' (device for slowing/halting motion) + 'man'.
