Lymantriidae

1. NOUN

/ laɪˌmænˈtriːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / lɪˌmanˈtrɪːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of moths commonly known as tussock moths, many of which are noted for their hairy caterpillars that can irritate human skin.
family of tussock moths
medical

Examples

Several species in the Lymantriidae family are considered serious pests in forestry.
The hairy caterpillars of Lymantriidae can cause skin irritation in humans.
Scientists have reclassified some genera previously included in Lymantriidae due to advances in molecular genetics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tussock moth - common name for moths of the Lymantriidae family

Etymology

From New Latin 'Lymantriidae', based on 'Lymantria' (a genus name), ultimately from Greek 'lymantrian' meaning 'destroyer'.
