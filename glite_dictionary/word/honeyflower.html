honeyflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌniˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈhʌniˌflaʊə / (BrE)
An Australian shrub whose flowers produce abundant nectar, often attracting bees.
Australian nectar-producing shrub

Examples

Bees are especially fond of the honeyflower for its sweet, plentiful nectar.
The honeyflower adds vibrant color and attracts pollinators to Australian gardens.
He planted several honeyflowers near the apiary to support the local bee population.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌniˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈhʌniˌflaʊə / (BrE)
an erect bushy shrub native to eastern Australia, with terminal clusters of red flowers that produce abundant nectar
Australian shrub with nectar-rich red flowers

Examples

The bright red blossoms of the honeyflower attract many native birds to the garden.
Beekeepers prize the honeyflower for its rich nectar, which helps support healthy hives.
We planted a honeyflower shrub along the fence to add color and attract pollinators.

Etymology

From 'honey' + 'flower', referring to the flower's nectar-producing quality.
