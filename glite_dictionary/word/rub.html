rub

1. NOUN

/ rʌb / (AmE), / rʌb / (BrE)
an act of moving one's hand or a cloth repeatedly over a surface, typically to clean, dry, or polish it
act of rubbing or wiping

Examples

He gave the table a quick rub to remove the dust.
After a brisk rub with a towel, the dog was finally dry.
The silverware regained its shine after a good rub.

Synset Examples

he gave the hood a quick rub

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wipe - an act of wiping something
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ rʌb / (AmE), / rʌb / (BrE)
to move one's hand or an object across a surface, especially to relieve itching or irritation
move hand/object across surface (esp. relieve itching)

Examples

She began to rub her arm where the mosquito had bitten her.
If your skin itches, try not to rub it too hard.
He rubbed his eyes, hoping to relieve the irritation from the dust.

Synset Examples

Don't scratch your insect bites!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scratch - to scrape with nails or something sharp, often to relieve itching

/ rʌb / (AmE), / rʌb / (BrE)
an unforeseen difficulty or obstacle that makes a situation more complicated
unforeseen obstacle or difficulty

Examples

He agreed to the proposal, but there was a rub with the timeline that needed to be solved.
Here's the rub: we lack enough funds to finish the project.

4. VERB

/ rʌb / (AmE), / rʌb / (BrE)
to move something over a surface or object with pressure and a repeated motion
move something over a surface with pressure

Examples

She began to rub lotion onto her arms after a long day in the sun.
He tried to rub the stain out of his shirt, but it wouldn't budge.
The dog loves it when you rub its belly gently.

Synset Examples

rub my hands
rub oil into her skin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrub - to clean something by rubbing it hard
  • Synonym: wipe - to clean or dry by moving something over a surface
  • Antonym: pat - to touch lightly, often repeatedly

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *rubben*, perhaps of Scandinavian origin.

5. VERB

/ rʌb / (AmE), / rʌb / (BrE)
to move something against a surface with firm pressure, often producing friction
move something against a surface

Examples

She tried to rub the stain out of her shirt.
He would rub his hands together to keep warm in winter.
If you rub two sticks together, you can sometimes produce heat.

Synset Examples

my sweater scratches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrub - to clean or rub hard with a brush or cloth
  • Synonym: polish - to make smooth and shiny by rubbing
  • Antonym: stroke - to move one's hand gently over a surface

Etymology

From Middle English 'rubben', probably of Scandinavian origin, akin to Swedish 'rubba' (to move or displace).
