shaded

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪdɪd / (BrE)
partially or fully protected from direct sunlight by shade or shadow.
protected from direct sunlight by shade

Examples

We sat on a shaded bench to escape the hot afternoon sun.
Many plants thrive in shaded areas of the garden.
The street was lined with shaded walkways, making it pleasant for pedestrians.

Synset Examples

shaded avenues
o'er the shaded billows rushed the night

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sunlit - illuminated by sunlight
  • Synonym: shadowed - cast or covered in shadow

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪdɪd / (BrE)
(of a picture or drawing) having areas rendered with gradations of shadow to suggest depth or volume.
with shadow gradations (art)

Examples

The artist skillfully used shaded lines to give the drawing a three-dimensional effect.
In the portrait, the shaded areas add depth and realism to the face.
She gently blended the pencil marks to create shaded regions in her sketch.

Synset Examples

the shaded areas of the face seemed to recede

Etymology

From the verb 'shade', meaning to darken or render with shadow; ultimately from Old English 'sceadu' (shadow).
