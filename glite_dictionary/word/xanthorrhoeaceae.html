Xanthorrhoeaceae

1. NOUN

/ zænˌθɔroʊˈisiː / (AmE), / zænˌθɒrəʊˈiːsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants comprising the subfamilies Asphodeloideae, Hemerocallidoideae, and Xanthorrhoeoideae, sometimes recognized as a separate family but often included within Liliaceae in some classifications
a family of flowering plants in botany

Examples

Xanthorrhoeaceae are known for species such as the Australian grass tree.
Some taxonomists classify certain daylilies within the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.
Botanists sometimes debate whether Xanthorrhoeaceae is a distinct family or part of Liliaceae.

Etymology

From New Latin Xanthorrhoea (a genus name), from Greek xanthos (yellow) + rhōia (flow, stream), referencing the plant's sap.

2. NOUN

/ zænˌθɔroʊˈiːəsiː / (AmE), / zanˌθɒrəʊˈiːəsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, formerly classified within the Liliaceae, that includes the genera Asphodelus, Hemerocallis, and Xanthorrhoea, though its use is not widely accepted in current taxonomy
a family of flowering plants (see note on taxonomy)

Examples

The genus Xanthorrhoea is part of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family, known for its grass trees native to Australia.
Recent taxonomic revisions have changed the circumscription of Xanthorrhoeaceae in plant classification.
Some botanists no longer recognize Xanthorrhoeaceae as a separate family, preferring to divide it into smaller subfamilies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Asphodeloideae - a subfamily within Xanthorrhoeaceae including asphodels
  • Degree Lower: Hemerocallidoideae - a subfamily within Xanthorrhoeaceae including daylilies
  • Degree Lower: Xanthorrhoeoideae - a subfamily within Xanthorrhoeaceae including grass trees

Etymology

From Modern Latin *Xanthorrhoeaceae*, based on the genus name Xanthorrhoea (from Greek xanthos 'yellow' + rheo 'to flow', referring to resin).
