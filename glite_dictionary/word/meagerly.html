meagerly

1. ADV

/ ˈmiːɡɚli / (AmE), / ˈmiːɡəli / (BrE)
In a way that is deficient in amount, quality, or extent; scantily.
in a scant or insufficient manner

Examples

The meal was meagerly portioned, leaving everyone still hungry afterward.
She was meagerly compensated for all the extra hours she put in at work.
The room was meagerly furnished, with just a bed and a chair.

Synset Examples

meager resources
meager fare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scantily - in a scant or insufficient manner
  • Antonym: generously - in a generous or abundant manner

Etymology

From Latin 'macrum' (lean) via Old French 'maigre' and Middle English 'megre'.

2. ADV

/ ˈmiːɡərli / (AmE), / ˈmiːɡəli / (BrE)
To a meager degree; in a way that is insufficient or lacking in quantity or quality.
in an insufficient or scanty manner

Examples

The school was meagerly funded, limiting the resources available to students.
Food was distributed meagerly among the refugees, and many remained hungry.
Their efforts were meagerly rewarded despite their dedication to the project.

Synset Examples

these voices are meagerly represented at the conference
the area is slenderly endowed with natural resources

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scantily - in a small or insufficient amount

Etymology

From 'meager' + adverbial suffix '-ly'; ultimately from Latin 'macer' (thin, lean).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00398603-r
Synset POS: r
