Friedrich Wilhelm Nietzsche

1. PROPN

/ ˈniːtʃə / (AmE), / ˈniːtʃə / (BrE)
(1844–1900) An influential German philosopher known for his critique of traditional moral values and Christianity, and for introducing the concept of the 'superman'; regarded as a founder of existentialism.
German philosopher and existentialist thinker (1844–1900)

Examples

Many university students read Friedrich Wilhelm Nietzsche as part of their philosophy curriculum.
The writings of Friedrich Wilhelm Nietzsche have influenced modern existential thought.
Some critics argue that Friedrich Wilhelm Nietzsche's ideas are often misunderstood.
