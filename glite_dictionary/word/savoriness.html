savoriness

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvəri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈseɪvəri.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of having an appetizing, pleasing, or rich flavor; the state of being savory.
appetizing or rich flavor quality

Examples

The savoriness of the soup came from the slow-cooked vegetables and herbs.
Some cheeses are prized for their intense savoriness and complex flavors.
She enjoyed the savoriness of the roasted meat far more than any dessert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savory - having a pleasantly salty or spicy taste
  • Antonym: sweetness - the quality of being sweet in taste

Etymology

From savory + -ness, indicating the state or quality of being savory.
