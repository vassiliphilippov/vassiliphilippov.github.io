slicing

1. NOUN

/ ˈslaɪ.sɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈslaɪ.sɪŋ / (BrE)
in golf, a shot that curves dramatically to the right for a right-handed player, usually unintentionally
golf shot curving right (right-handed)

Examples

He struggled with slicing off the tee until he changed his grip.
Her slicing was causing her shots to end up in the rough on the right.
Many beginners find slicing to be their most common mistake in golf.

Synset Examples

he took lessons to cure his slicing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hooking - a golf shot that curves to the left for a right-handed golfer

2. NOUN

/ ˈslaɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈslaɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of cutting something into thin pieces or slices
the act of cutting into slices

Examples

The slicing of the tomatoes should be done as thinly as possible.
Proper slicing of the bread ensures even toasting.
She watched the chef's quick slicing of the vegetables with admiration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutting - the act of dividing or opening something with a sharp instrument
  • Synonym: chopping - the act of cutting something into pieces, usually with repeated blows

Etymology

From the verb 'slice' + -ing, forming a noun meaning the action of slicing.
