Selachii

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlæki.aɪ / (AmE), / sɪˈlækɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
A former subclass of cartilaginous fishes that includes sharks, rays, dogfishes, and skates.
group of fishes: sharks, rays, skates

Examples

Many marine biologists study Selachii to better understand the evolutionary history of sharks and rays.
The Selachii exhibit a wide variety of body forms, from the slender shark to the flattened ray.
Fossil records show that Selachii have existed for hundreds of millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shark - large predatory cartilaginous fish
  • Synonym: ray - flat-bodied cartilaginous fish

Etymology

From Greek selachos (‘cartilaginous fish, shark’).

2. NOUN

/ səˈlɑːki.aɪ / (AmE), / sɛˈlakɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
A subclass of cartilaginous fish that includes sharks, rays, skates, and related species.
subclass of cartilaginous fish: sharks, rays, skates
medical

Examples

The Selachii are distinguished from other fish by their cartilaginous skeletons and multiple gill slits.
Several ancient marine fossils provide insight into the evolution of the Selachii.
Researchers study the behavior of Selachii to understand more about their adaptations to ocean life.

Etymology

From Greek selachos (cartilaginous fish, shark).
