inhalator

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈheɪleɪtər / (AmE), / ɪnˈheɪlətə / (BrE)
a device for administering artificial respiration by supplying air or oxygen to the lungs over a period of time
device for artificial respiration
medical

Examples

The patient was placed on an inhalator after experiencing severe respiratory distress.
Emergency kits are often equipped with an inhalator for use in cases of smoke inhalation.
The old-style inhalator required manual operation by hospital staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respirator - a device designed to help a person breathe

Etymology

From Latin 'inhalare' (to breathe in) and English '-ator', a suffix forming agent nouns.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈheɪleɪtər / (AmE), / ɪnˈheɪleɪtə / (BrE)
a device that releases a chemical vapor to be inhaled, often to relieve nasal congestion or for medicinal purposes
device for inhaling medicinal vapor
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended using an inhalator to ease my blocked sinuses.
She reached for her inhalator whenever her congestion made it hard to breathe.
Many cold sufferers keep an inhalator at home for quick relief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhaler - a small device for breathing in medicine

Etymology

From Latin 'inhalare' (to breathe in), combined with the English agent noun suffix '-or'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03576431-n
Synset POS: n
