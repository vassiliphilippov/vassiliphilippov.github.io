amaranthine

1. ADJ

/ ˌæməˈrænθin / (AmE), / ˌaməˈranθiːn / (BrE)
Of or relating to the amaranth plant.
relating to the amaranth plant

Examples

The amaranthine hue of the petals made the garden especially vivid.
Scientists studied the amaranthine stems for their nutritional properties.
Many South American dishes include grains derived from the amaranthine plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaranth - a plant of the genus Amaranthus, often cultivated for its colorful flowers or edible seeds

Etymology

From Latin amarantinus, from Greek amárantos (unfading), from amárantos (amaranth), a mythical undying flower.

2. ADJ

/ ˌæməˈrænθiːn / (AmE), / ˌaməˈranθiːn / (BrE)
relating to or resembling an imaginary flower that never fades; unfading or everlasting.
unfading; everlasting (like a mythical flower)

Examples

Like the amaranthine bloom of myth, her beauty never seemed to diminish with age.
The poet spoke of amaranthine love, one that would never fade or die.
A sense of amaranthine hope lingered in his heart, no matter the hardships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: everlasting - lasting forever; never ending
  • Antonym: ephemeral - lasting for a very short time

Etymology

From Greek amarantos (unfading), from amaranthos (amaranth flower), with -ine suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01562479-s
Synset POS: s
