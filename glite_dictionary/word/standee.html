standee

1. NOUN

/ stænˈdi / (AmE), / stænˈdiː / (BrE)
a person who stands in a place where others might sit, such as a passenger standing on a crowded bus or train, or a spectator standing in a theater's standing room
person standing where seating is unavailable

Examples

The train was so crowded that many standees had to hold onto the overhead rails.
A notice at the theater entrance limited the number of standees allowed in the balcony.
The bus driver announced that no more standees would be permitted for safety reasons.

Synset Examples

the allowed number of standees is posted

2. NOUN

/ stænˈdi / (AmE), / stanˈdiː / (BrE)
a life-size upright cutout, typically made of cardboard and often depicting a celebrity or character, used especially for promotional purposes
life-size cardboard cutout, often of a celebrity

Examples

The movie theater displayed a standee of the main character near the entrance.
She posed for a photo with a standee of her favorite singer at the mall.
A standee advertising the new video game caught everyone's attention in the store.

Synset Examples

he had his picture taken with a standee of the president
