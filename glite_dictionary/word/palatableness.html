palatableness

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlætəblnəs / (AmE), / pəˈlætəblnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasant or acceptable to the taste
pleasantness to the taste

Examples

The palatableness of the new recipe impressed all the dinner guests.
Most chefs strive for both nutritional value and palatableness in their dishes.
Improving the palatableness of healthy food can encourage children to eat better.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palatability - the quality of being pleasant to taste, alternative form
  • Antonym: unpalatableness - the quality of being unpleasant to taste

Etymology

From Latin 'palatum' (palate) + English suffix '-able' + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæl.ə.tə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpæl.ə.tə.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasant or agreeable to the mind, feelings, or senses, especially taste.
quality of being pleasant or agreeable

Examples

The palatableness of her proposal won over even her harshest critics.
Some foods are engineered to maximize their palatableness and keep customers coming back.
He questioned the palatableness of compromising his principles for political gain.

Synset Examples

the pursuit of electoral palatability

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palatability - the quality of being acceptable or pleasant, especially to the taste

Etymology

From "palatable" + "-ness"; "palatable" from Late Latin palatabilis, from Latin palatum (palate).
