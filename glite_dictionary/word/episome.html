episome

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪsəʊm / (BrE)
a genetic element, such as certain types of DNA, that can exist independently in a cell or become integrated with the chromosomal DNA, replicating along with the host genome
genetic element replicating with or apart from genome
medical

Examples

Some viruses carry their genetic material as an episome within bacterial cells.
The episome can either integrate into the chromosome or remain separate in the cytoplasm.
Researchers studied how the episome replicates along with the bacterial host DNA.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plasmid - a small DNA molecule independent of chromosomal DNA in bacteria

Etymology

From Greek 'epi-' (upon) + 'soma' (body).
