kowhai

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ.faɪ / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand and Chile, with drooping clusters of tubular golden-yellow flowers and hard, strong wood
shrub or tree with yellow flowers, native to New Zealand/Chile

Examples

The kowhai tree burst into bloom, brightening the garden with its golden flowers.
Many birds are attracted to the kowhai for its nectar-rich blossoms.
Carpenters prize kowhai wood for its hardness and durability.

Etymology

From Maori 'kōwhai', referring to the color yellow and the plant itself.
