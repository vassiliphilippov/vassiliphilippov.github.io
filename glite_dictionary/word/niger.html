Niger

1. PROPN

/ ˈnaɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈniːʒə, naɪˈdʒɛə / (BrE)
a landlocked country in West Africa, largely covered by the Sahara Desert, which became independent from France in 1960
country in West Africa

Examples

The president of Niger addressed the United Nations about climate change and security.
Much of Niger consists of arid desert, making agriculture especially difficult.
French is the official language of Niger, reflecting its colonial history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nigeria - a neighboring country in West Africa, also named after the Niger River

Etymology

From French Niger, from the Niger River, which is thought to derive from a local Tuareg name.

2. PROPN

/ ˈnaɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈniːdʒə / (BrE)
A major river in West Africa that flows through several countries and empties into the Gulf of Guinea in the South Atlantic Ocean.
major river in West Africa

Examples

The Niger is crucial for agriculture in Mali and Niger.
Fishermen rely on the Niger for their daily catch.
The annual flooding of the Niger creates fertile land for thousands of farmers.

Etymology

From Latin Niger (meaning 'black'), referring to the river.
