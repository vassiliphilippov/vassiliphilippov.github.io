ornithischian

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrnɪˈθɪskiən / (AmE), / ˌɔːnɪˈθɪskɪən / (BrE)
a herbivorous dinosaur of the order Ornithischia, characterized by a pelvis structure similar to that of birds
herbivorous dinosaur with bird-like pelvis

Examples

Stegosaurus is a well-known example of an ornithischian.
Most ornithischians were plant-eating and walked on all fours or on their hind legs.
Paleontologists discovered the fossil of a new ornithischian species in South America.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: saurischian - dinosaur with a lizard-like pelvis, contrasting ornithischians

Etymology

From New Latin Ornithischia, from Greek ornith-, ornithos (bird) + ischion (hip joint).
