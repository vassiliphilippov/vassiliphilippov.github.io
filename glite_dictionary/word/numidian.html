Numidian

1. ADJ

/ nuˈmɪdiən / (AmE), / njuˈmɪdiən / (BrE)
relating to ancient Numidia, its people, or their culture
of ancient Numidia or its people/culture

Examples

The Numidian cavalry played a decisive role during the Punic Wars.
Archaeologists discovered Numidian artifacts dating back to the second century BC.
He conducted extensive research on Numidian tribal customs and society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Numidia - an ancient kingdom in North Africa

Etymology

From Latin Numidia (ancient North African kingdom) + -an.

2. NOUN

/ nuˈmɪdiən / (AmE), / njuˈmɪdiən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of ancient Numidia, a region of North Africa during Roman times
inhabitant of ancient Numidia

Examples

The Numidian cavalry played a crucial role in Hannibal's campaigns against Rome.
Artifacts from Numidian settlements have been discovered by archaeologists in present-day Algeria.
A Numidian was often employed as a mercenary in ancient military forces.

Etymology

From Latin Numidiae (Numidia), the name of an ancient kingdom in North Africa.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10386391-n
Synset POS: n
