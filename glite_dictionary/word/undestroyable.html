undestroyable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.dɪˈstrɔɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈstrɔɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
impossible to destroy; not able to be ruined or broken down
impossible to destroy

Examples

The fortress was said to be undestroyable by any conventional weapon.
This ancient tablet appears undestroyable after surviving thousands of years underground.
They believed the magical shield was undestroyable and would protect the kingdom forever.

Synset Examples

less durable rocks were gradually worn away to form valleys
the perdurable granite of the ancient Appalachian spine of the continent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indestructible - impossible to destroy; not able to be damaged

Etymology

From un- (not) + destroy + -able (able to be).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɪˈstrɔɪəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈstrɔɪəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to destroy; not capable of being destroyed.
impossible to destroy

Examples

The superhero's shield is rumored to be undestroyable by any known weapon.
Scientists are searching for an undestroyable material to use in spacecraft.
Her will was so strong, it seemed almost undestroyable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indestructible - impossible to destroy

Etymology

Prefix un- (not) + destroy + suffix -able (capable of).
