dappled

1. ADJ

/ ˈdæpəld / (AmE), / ˈdæpəld / (BrE)
marked with spots or patches of different colors or shades, usually in an irregular pattern
marked with spots or patches of color

Examples

The sunlight filtered through the trees, creating a dappled pattern on the forest floor.
The horse had a beautiful dappled coat that shimmered in the sun.
She chose a fabric with a dappled blue and white design for the curtains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mottled - marked with irregular spots or streaks of color
  • Synonym: spotted - having spots or marks of different colors
  • Antonym: plain - without spots or patterns; uniform in color

Etymology

From Middle English *dapled*, probably from a root related to the word 'dapple' meaning 'spot'.
