spaghetti

1. NOUN

/ spəˈɡɛti / (AmE), / spəˈɡɛti / (BrE)
A type of pasta made in long, thin strands, commonly served with a tomato sauce.
long, thin pasta, often with tomato sauce

Examples

She made a delicious plate of spaghetti with homemade tomato sauce for dinner.
Children often enjoy eating spaghetti because of its fun shape and tasty sauce.
At the Italian restaurant, I ordered spaghetti as my main course.

Etymology

From Italian 'spaghetto', diminutive of 'spago' meaning 'thin string'.

2. NOUN

/ spəˈɡɛti / (AmE), / spəˈɡɛti / (BrE)
a type of pasta made in long, thin strings, usually served with a sauce
long, thin pasta

Examples

She cooked a big pot of spaghetti for dinner last night.
The children love to eat spaghetti with tomato sauce and meatballs.
He twirled the spaghetti around his fork before taking a bite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noodle - another type of long, thin dough food, common in Asian cuisine
  • Degree Lower: macaroni - short, tube-shaped pasta
  • Degree Higher: fettuccine - pasta in long, flat ribbons

Etymology

From Italian 'spaghetti', plural of 'spaghetto', a diminutive of 'spago' (string, twine).
