accidental

1. NOUN

/ ˌæksɪˈdɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌæksɪˈdɛntəl / (BrE)
a symbol in music notation used to raise or lower the pitch of a note, making it sharp, flat, or natural, regardless of the key signature
music symbol that alters a note's pitch

Examples

The composer used an accidental to indicate a sharp note outside the key signature.
Each time you see an accidental in the score, alter the pitch of the note as directed.
This measure contains several accidentals, making it more challenging to play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accidentals - alternative plural noun for musical notation symbols altering pitch

Etymology

From Latin accidentalis (non-essential), later used in music for notes not in the key.

2. ADJ

/ ˌæksəˈdɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌæksɪˈdɛntəl / (BrE)
happening by chance or without intention; not planned or deliberate.
happening by chance; unintentional

Examples

The fire was caused by an accidental electrical fault in the basement.
She gave him an accidental push as she hurried past.
There was an accidental spillage of paint in the hallway.

Synset Examples

with an inadvertent gesture she swept the vase off the table
accidental poisoning
an accidental shooting

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intentional - done on purpose; deliberate
  • Synonym: inadvertent - not resulting from or achieved through deliberate planning
  • Synonym: unintentional - not done on purpose

Etymology

From Late Latin accidentalis, from Latin accidēns (befalling, happening).

3. ADJ

/ ˌæksɪˈdɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌæksɪˈdɛntəl / (BrE)
not essential or central; occurring as a minor or secondary element
not essential; secondary

Examples

The missing document was accidental to the core investigation.
Background music is often accidental to the cinematic narrative.
His fame was largely accidental to his main achievements in science.

Synset Examples

nonessential to the integral meanings of poetry
the character's motives remain accidental to the plot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonessential - not essential; not necessary
  • Antonym: essential - absolutely necessary; extremely important

Etymology

From Latin accidentalis, from accidens (happening), from accidere (to happen).
