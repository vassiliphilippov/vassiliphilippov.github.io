jaundiced

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɔn.dɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːn.dɪst / (BrE)
having or showing a negative attitude, especially due to envy, prejudice, or distaste
prejudiced or negative in attitude

Examples

She viewed the new policy with a jaundiced eye, suspecting hidden motives.
His jaundiced attitude prevented him from enjoying the success of his colleagues.
Many critics take a jaundiced view of corporate promises for reform.

Synset Examples

looked with a jaundiced eye on the growth of regimentation
takes a jaundiced view of societies and clubs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cynical - distrusting or disparaging the motives of others
  • Antonym: impartial - treating all rivals or disputants equally; fair and just

Etymology

Originally from French 'jaunisse' (jaundice), literal sense of yellowish; figurative sense refers to bitterness or envy, as if one were yellowed by disease.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɔn.dɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːn.dɪst / (BrE)
affected by jaundice, a medical condition causing yellowing of the skin, eyes, or mucous membranes
having yellowed skin due to jaundice
medical

Examples

The doctor noticed her jaundiced appearance and ordered blood tests.
Babies can develop a jaundiced complexion shortly after birth.
His eyes looked jaundiced, which concerned the nurse during her examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: icteric - showing symptoms of jaundice

Etymology

From Old French 'jaunisse' (yellowness), from 'jaune' (yellow).
