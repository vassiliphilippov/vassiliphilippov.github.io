omophagia

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊməˈfeɪdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌɒməˈfeɪdʒɪə / (BrE)
The practice or act of eating raw food, especially raw flesh, often in a ritual context.
the eating of raw food, especially raw flesh
medical

Examples

Ancient rituals sometimes involved omophagia as a symbol of communion with the divine.
Scholars have studied omophagia in the context of early Greek religious practices.
The priestess described the significance of omophagia during the sacred ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raw foodism - a diet consisting mainly or entirely of uncooked and unprocessed foods

Etymology

From Greek omos (raw) + phagein (to eat).
