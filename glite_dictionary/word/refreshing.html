refreshing

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈfrɛʃɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɛʃɪŋ / (BrE)
Making you feel more lively, energetic, or revitalized; giving a pleasant feeling of new strength or energy.
invigorating; giving new energy

Examples

A cold glass of lemonade was truly refreshing after the long hike.
The refreshing breeze revived everyone at the picnic.
She found his honest attitude both refreshing and encouraging.

Synset Examples

the bracing mountain air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invigorating - making you feel more lively and energetic
  • Antonym: tiring - causing you to feel tired or fatigued

Etymology

From verb 'refresh,' ultimately from Old French *refreschier* (to refresh, renew).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈfrɛʃɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɛʃɪŋ / (BrE)
pleasantly new, different, or invigorating; making you feel more lively or cheerful
pleasantly new or invigorating

Examples

The cool breeze on a hot day was truly refreshing.
Her refreshing honesty surprised everyone at the meeting.
It was refreshing to hear such original ideas in the discussion.

Synset Examples

common sense of a most refreshing sort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invigorating - making you feel more energetic or lively
  • Synonym: stimulating - encouraging new ideas or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: stale - lacking freshness or newness, dull

Etymology

From the verb 'refresh', itself from Old French *refreschir*, from Latin *re-* (again) + *friscus* (fresh).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01646687-s
Synset POS: s
