to Frenchify

1. VERB

/ ˈfrɛntʃɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfrɛntʃɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to make something French in appearance, style, or character
make French in appearance or character

Examples

They decided to Frenchify their menu with more baguettes and pastries.
The hotel was completely Frenchified after the recent renovations, from the décor to the cuisine.
We could Frenchify the event by serving champagne and playing Parisian music.

Synset Examples

let's Frenchify the restaurant and charge more money

Etymology

From 'French' + '-ify', patterned after 'Frenchified' (made to appear French).

2. VERB

/ ˈfrɛntʃɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfrɛntʃɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to make or become French in appearance, character, or style
make or become French in style or character

Examples

The menu was completely Frenchified after the new chef arrived.
Many English words were Frenchified after the Norman Conquest.
The café is trying to Frenchify its décor to appeal to tourists.

Synset Examples

This restaurant has Frenchified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gallicize - to make or become French in character

Etymology

From French ‘franciser’ and English ‘French’ + ‘-ify’ (make, become).
