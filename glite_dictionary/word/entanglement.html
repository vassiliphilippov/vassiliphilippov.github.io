entanglement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtæŋɡəlmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtæŋɡlmənt / (BrE)
a complicated or tricky situation or device that entraps or ensnares someone or something
complicated trap or snare

Examples

The hero narrowly escaped the entanglement set by his enemies in the forest.
She became caught in an entanglement of lies and deceit that was difficult to escape.
The fisherman warned about the entanglement of hidden nets in these waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snare - a device or situation that traps someone or something
  • Synonym: trap - a device or arrangement for catching someone or something

Etymology

From English 'entangle' + '-ment'; 'entangle' from Middle English, influenced by Old French 'estangler' (to strangle).
