computation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmpjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of calculating or determining something, especially using mathematical or logical methods
the act of calculating

Examples

The computation of the total cost took several hours.
Modern computers can perform complex computation very quickly.
Her research focuses on the efficient computation of large mathematical problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculation - the process of using numbers to find out a result

Etymology

From Latin computatio (a reckoning, calculation), from computare (to calculate, sum up).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmpjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the use of mathematical methods or calculation to solve problems involving numbers or quantities
mathematical calculation or problem-solving

Examples

Her job involves the computation of financial data for monthly reports.
Accurate computation is essential in scientific research and engineering projects.
The computation required advanced mathematical skills and careful attention to detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculation - the process of using mathematics; careful thinking or planning

Etymology

From Latin computatio (a reckoning, calculation), from computare (to compute).
