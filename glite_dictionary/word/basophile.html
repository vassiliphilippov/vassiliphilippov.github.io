basophile

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsəʊfaɪl / (BrE)
A type of white blood cell containing granules that are easily stained by basic dyes, involved in immune responses.
type of white blood cell
medical

Examples

The doctor noted an increase in basophile count in the patient's blood test.
Basophile granules stain dark blue with basic dyes under the microscope.
As part of the immune system, the basophile plays a role in allergic reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basophil - alternative spelling for the same type of white blood cell

Etymology

From French 'basophile', from Greek 'basis' (base) + 'philos' (loving).
