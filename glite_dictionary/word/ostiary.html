ostiary

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊstiˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈɒstɪəri / (BrE)
a person appointed to guard the entrance of a church or building, especially an usher or doorkeeper in early Christian churches
doorkeeper or usher, especially in a church

Examples

The ancient church appointed an ostiary to welcome worshippers and keep order at the entrance.
During the ceremony, the ostiary quietly opened the heavy door for the bishop.
In early Christian communities, the ostiary was responsible for guarding the entrance to sacred spaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doorkeeper - a person who guards or controls access to a door
  • Synonym: usher - a person who shows people to their seats, especially in a church or theater

Etymology

From Latin 'ostiarius', from 'ostium' (door, entrance).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːstiˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈɒstɪəri / (BrE)
the lowest of the minor Holy Orders in the unreformed Western Church, formerly responsible for the church door, now suppressed by the Roman Catholic Church
minor Holy Order in the pre-reformed Western Church

Examples

In medieval times, the ostiary was responsible for watching over the doors of the church.
The role of ostiary was eventually abolished in the Roman Catholic Church.
Historical records describe the duties of an ostiary during ecclesiastical ceremonies.

Etymology

From Latin *ostiarius* (doorkeeper), from *ostium* (door).
