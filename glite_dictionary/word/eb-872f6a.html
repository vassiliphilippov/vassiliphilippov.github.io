Eb

1. NOUN

/ iː biː / (AmE), / iː biː / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to one quintillion (10^18) bits, used especially in computing and digital communications
unit of information, 10^18 bits

Examples

The new data center is capable of storing over one Eb of information.
By 2030, global internet traffic is projected to reach several Eb per month.
Transferring an Eb of data would take years over a standard home internet connection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exabit - a unit of information equal to 10^18 bits
  • Degree Lower: petabit - a unit of information equal to 10^15 bits

Etymology

Abbreviation of exabit, from 'exa-' (a factor of 10^18) + 'bit'.
