boulder

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊldər / (AmE), / ˈbəʊldə / (BrE)
a large, rounded mass of rock that has been detached from its original location, typically lying on the ground
large detached rock

Examples

A huge boulder blocked the entrance to the cave after the landslide.
Children enjoyed climbing onto the boulder by the riverbank.
The trail wound around a massive boulder covered in moss.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: rock - a solid mineral material forming part of the earth's surface
  • Degree Lower: pebble - a small, smooth stone found on a beach or riverbed
  • Degree Lower: stone - a hard, solid nonmetallic mineral matter of which rock is made

Etymology

From Middle English 'bulder' or 'bowlder', possibly from a Scandinavian source, such as Swedish 'bullersten' (noisy stone).
