Ericales

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪˈkeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌɪərɪˈkeɪliːz / (BrE)
An order of flowering plants that includes families such as Ericaceae (heath family), Clethraceae, Diapensiaceae, and several others.
an order of flowering plants

Examples

The Ericales order contains many acid-loving plants found in temperate regions.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Ericales in South America.
Many members of the Ericales have distinctive bell-shaped flowers.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the genus Erica + -ales (a standard suffix for plant orders).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪˈkeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌɪərɪˈkeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants that includes families such as the heaths (Ericaceae), tea plants (Theaceae), and others
order of flowering plants

Examples

The Ericales include a diverse range of plants, from blueberries to azaleas.
Botanists are currently revising the classification within the Ericales order.
Many ornamental shrubs, such as rhododendrons, are part of the Ericales family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ericaceae - the heath or heather family, a main family within Ericales

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Erica (heather) + -ales (order suffix).
