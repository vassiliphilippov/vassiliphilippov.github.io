ayatollah

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.jəˈtoʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.əˈtɒl.ə / (BrE)
A high-ranking Shiite religious leader who is considered an authority on Islamic law and interpretation and may also hold significant political power.
high-ranking Shiite religious leader

Examples

The country's supreme leader is an ayatollah with great influence over religious and political affairs.
During the revolution, the people rallied behind an ayatollah who promised change.
Several ayatollahs issued statements regarding the new legislation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imam - a leader of prayer in a mosque; Muslim religious leader
  • Degree Lower: mullah - a Muslim trained in Islamic law and theology

Etymology

From Persian *āyatollāh*, from Arabic *āyat allāh* meaning 'sign of God'.
