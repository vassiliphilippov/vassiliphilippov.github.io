barrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈbærəl / (AmE), / ˈbærəl / (BrE)
The long, cylindrical part of a firearm through which the bullet travels when the gun is fired.
tube of a gun through which bullets travel

Examples

He cleaned the barrel of his rifle after the hunting trip.
The barrel was so hot after firing that you couldn't touch it.
A longer barrel can improve the accuracy of a firearm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bore - the interior of a gun barrel
  • Synonym: muzzle - the open end of a gun barrel

2. VERB

/ ˈbærəl / (AmE), / ˈbærəl / (BrE)
to put or pack something into a barrel or barrels for storage or transport
put into a barrel or barrels

Examples

After the olives are harvested, workers barrel them for export.
The winery barrels its best wines to age them properly.
Every autumn, they barrel apples to make cider over the winter.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrəl / (AmE), / ˈbærəl / (BrE)
A standard unit of capacity for liquids, varying according to the commodity and country but commonly 31 gallons for beer or 42 gallons for oil in the US.
a unit of liquid capacity

Examples

The price of oil is often quoted per barrel on international markets.
A standard American beer barrel holds 31 gallons.
Last year, the company produced 10,000 barrels of wine.

Synset Examples

a barrel of beer is 31 gallons and a barrel of oil is 42 gallons

Etymology

From Old French *baril*, of uncertain origin.

4. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrəl / (AmE), / ˈbærəl / (BrE)
A unit of measure equal to the amount that a barrel of liquid or other substance can contain, often varying for different substances or countries.
a unit for the amount a barrel contains

Examples

The tanker carried 500,000 barrels of crude oil across the ocean.
A barrel of beer contains more than thirty gallons.
Oil prices are usually quoted in dollars per barrel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallon - a unit of liquid capacity equal to four quarts

Etymology

From Old French baril (barrel, cask), of uncertain origin.

6. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrəl / (AmE), / ˈbærəl / (BrE)
a large, usually cylindrical container, typically made of wood or metal, used for storing or transporting liquids such as wine or oil
cylindrical liquid container

Examples

The winery stores its best wine in oak barrels for several years.
They rolled a heavy barrel of oil across the dock.
Rainwater collected in an old barrel beside the shed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cask - a large container for storing alcoholic drinks, especially wine or beer

Etymology

From Old French *baril* (barrel, cask), of unknown origin.
