Hymenomycetes

1. NOUN

/ haɪmɪnoʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / hɪmɛnəʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
a former class of fungi, including mushrooms, toadstools, agarics, and bracket fungi, often coextensive with the order Agaricales.
former class of fungi (mushrooms, agarics, etc.)

Examples

The group known as Hymenomycetes includes many familiar mushroom species.
Recent phylogenetic studies have reclassified members of the Hymenomycetes into other groups.
Botanists once regarded Hymenomycetes as a major class within fungi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Agaricales - an order of fungi including most of the common mushrooms

Etymology

From Greek *hymen* (membrane) + *mycetes* (fungus).

2. NOUN

/ haɪməˌnoʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / hɪməˌnəʊmɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
a former class of fungi that included mushrooms, toadstools, agarics, and bracket fungi, now largely replaced by more specific groupings
former class of fungi (includes mushrooms and toadstools)

Examples

Many familiar mushrooms fall under the former class Hymenomycetes.
Bracket fungi, once classified as Hymenomycetes, are now divided among several new groups.
The term Hymenomycetes is still encountered in older scientific literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Agaricales - an order of fungi including most mushrooms and toadstools

Etymology

From Greek hymēn (membrane) and mykēs (fungus).
