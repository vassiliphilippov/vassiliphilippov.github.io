chylomicron

1. NOUN

/ kaɪloʊˈmaɪkrɑn / (AmE), / kaɪləʊˈmɪkrɒn / (BrE)
A microscopic particle containing triglycerides formed in the intestines during digestion, which transports fats through the bloodstream.
microscopic fat-transport particle in the blood
medical

Examples

A high level of chylomicron in the blood can indicate improper fat metabolism.
After a fatty meal, the concentration of chylomicron increases in the bloodstream.
Researchers studied how chylomicron particles deliver triglycerides to body tissues.

Etymology

From Greek 'chylos' (juice) and 'micron' (small).
