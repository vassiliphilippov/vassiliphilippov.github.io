Tridacnidae

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈdæksnɪdiː / (AmE), / trɪˈdaknɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large marine bivalve mollusks, known as giant clams, found in tropical coral reefs.
family of large marine clams

Examples

The Tridacnidae are often referred to as giant clams due to their impressive size.
Conservationists monitor Tridacnidae populations to protect coral reef ecosystems.
The shells of Tridacnidae have been prized for their beauty and used in ornamental art.

Etymology

From New Latin Tridacna (type genus) + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ traɪˈdæknɪˌdiː / (AmE), / trɪˈdæknɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of large marine hard-shell clams, including the giant clam, found chiefly in the Indo-Pacific region
family of large marine clams

Examples

The Tridacnidae are known for their size and colorful mantles.
Scientists study the Tridacnidae to understand reef ecosystems.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the endangered Tridacnidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant clam - a very large marine bivalve mollusk of the genus Tridacna

Etymology

From New Latin Tridacna (a genus name) + -idae (taxonomic suffix for animal families).
