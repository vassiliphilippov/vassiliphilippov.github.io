worshipful

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɜrʃəpfəl / (AmE), / ˈwɜːʃɪpfʊl / (BrE)
showing or expressing deep respect, reverence, or adoration
expressing deep reverence or adoration

Examples

The choir sang with a worshipful attitude throughout the ceremony.
She looked at the painting with worshipful admiration, captivated by its beauty.
His worshipful tone made it clear he greatly admired his mentor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverent - feeling or showing deep and solemn respect
  • Synonym: adoring - showing deep love and respect
  • Antonym: disrespectful - showing a lack of respect or courtesy

Etymology

From Middle English 'worschipful', from 'worship' + '-ful'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɝːʃɪpfəl / (AmE), / ˈwɜːʃɪpfʊl / (BrE)
showing or expressing great reverence and respect, especially for God or a deity; deserving of worship
showing great reverence for God

Examples

The congregation regarded their new priest as a truly worshipful leader.
She entered the ancient temple with a worshipful attitude, moved by the sacred atmosphere.
His worshipful devotion to tradition inspired others to deepen their faith.

Synset Examples

a godly man
leading a godly life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverent - feeling or showing deep and solemn respect
  • Antonym: irreverent - showing a lack of respect for people or things that are generally taken seriously

Etymology

From Middle English 'worschipful', from 'worschip' (worship) + '-ful'.
