to recuperate

1. VERB

/ rɪˈkuːpəˌreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈkjuːpəreɪt / (BrE)
to recover from illness, injury, or fatigue; to regain health or strength after a setback
recover after illness or setback

Examples

After her surgery, she needed time to recuperate before returning to work.
He spent two weeks at home to recuperate from the flu.
The team took a brief break to recuperate after the exhausting competition.

Synset Examples

The patient is recuperating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recover - to return to a normal state of health or strength
  • Antonym: relapse - to fall back into illness after improvement

Etymology

From Latin recuperare (to recover, regain).

2. VERB

/ rəˈkuːpəˌreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈkjuːpəreɪt / (BrE)
(of a person, organization, or asset) to regain or recover a former financial condition or status after a loss.
regain financial status after loss

Examples

The company hopes to recuperate its losses by the end of the fiscal year.
After the market downturn, it took several months to recuperate financially.
Investors were relieved when the business started to recuperate and show profits again.

Synset Examples

We expect the stocks to recover to $2.90
The company managed to recuperate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recover - to return to a normal state after a financial loss

Etymology

From Latin recuperare (to recover), from re- (back) + capere (to take).
