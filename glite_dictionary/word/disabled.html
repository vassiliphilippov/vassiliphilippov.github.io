disabled

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈseɪbəld / (AmE), / dɪˈseɪbld / (BrE)
people collectively who are physically or mentally impaired, especially those with a long-term disability.
people with physical or mental disabilities

Examples

The government has increased support services for the disabled in recent years.
Many buildings now have improved access for the disabled to ensure inclusivity.
Technology can greatly benefit the lives of the elderly and the disabled.

Synset Examples

technology to help the elderly and the disabled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the handicapped - people collectively who are physically or mentally handicapped
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈseɪbəld / (AmE), / dɪˈseɪbəld / (BrE)
Having a physical or mental condition that limits movements, senses, or activities; lacking one or more bodily or mental functions.
having a physical or mental disability

Examples

The stadium has special facilities for disabled people.
She works as an advocate for the rights of disabled students.
Accessible parking spaces are reserved for disabled drivers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: able-bodied - not disabled; having no physical or mental disability
  • Synonym: handicapped - having a physical or mental disability; dated or sometimes offensive term
