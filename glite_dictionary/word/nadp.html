NADP

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛnˌeɪˌdiːˈpi / (AmE), / ˌɛnˌeɪˌdiːˈpiː / (BrE)
a coenzyme found in most living cells, similar to NAD, that acts as a reductant in various metabolic processes
coenzyme involved in metabolic processes
medical

Examples

NADP plays a key role in photosynthesis by transferring electrons in plants.
Scientists measured NADP levels in the liver tissue to study metabolic pathways.
Without sufficient NADP, certain cellular reactions cannot occur efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: NAD - coenzyme involved in redox reactions, related to NADP

Etymology

Abbreviation of nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate.
