buckskin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈbʌkˌskɪn / (BrE)
a soft, yellowish suede leather originally made from deerskin but now usually derived from sheepskin
soft, yellowish leather; originally deerskin

Examples

He wore a jacket made of buckskin during the hunting trip.
The craftsman tanned the hide into soft buckskin for gloves.
Authentic buckskin has a distinctive yellowish hue and smooth texture.

Etymology

From 'buck' (male deer) and 'skin', originally referring to deerskin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈbʌkskɪn / (BrE)
a horse of a light yellowish-dun color with a dark mane and tail
light yellowish-dun horse with dark mane and tail

Examples

The rancher rode a sturdy buckskin through the open prairie.
Her favorite horse was a beautiful buckskin with a striking black mane and tail.
At the show, the judge admired the buckskin for its unique coloring and gentle temperament.

Etymology

Formed from 'buck' (male deer) + 'skin', originally referring to the deerskin color, later extended to horses of similar color.
