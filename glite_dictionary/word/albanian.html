Albanian

1. NOUN

/ ælˈbeɪniən / (AmE), / ælˈbeɪniən / (BrE)
the Indo-European language spoken by the people of Albania
language of Albania

Examples

She learned to speak Albanian during her stay in Tirana.
Many books and newspapers in Albania are written in Albanian.
He is fluent in English, French, and Albanian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Albania - a country in southeastern Europe

Etymology

From Albania, the country, plus the English suffix -an.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06955014-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ælˈbeɪniən / (AmE), / ælˈbeɪniən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Albania, or a person of Albanian descent
a person from Albania

Examples

She met an Albanian tourist while traveling in Europe.
The Albanian gave us directions to the city center.
Many Albanians have immigrated to different countries in search of a better life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Albania - a country in southeastern Europe on the Balkan Peninsula

Etymology

From Albania (country name) + -an (denoting nationality).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09709354-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ælˈbeɪniən / (AmE), / ælˈbeɪniən / (BrE)
relating to Albania, its people, their language, or their culture
of Albania or its people/culture

Examples

The Albanian language has a unique place among European languages.
She enjoyed traditional Albanian music at the festival.
Many Albanian dishes include fresh herbs and vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Albania - a country in southeastern Europe

Etymology

From Latin Albanus (of Alba, an ancient city), later influenced by the native name for the country, Shqipëri.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02620230-a
Synset POS: a
