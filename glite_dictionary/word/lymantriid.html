lymantriid

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈmæntriɪd / (AmE), / lɪˈmantrɪɪd / (BrE)
A moth of the family Lymantriidae, typically dull-colored, with larvae that have tufts of hair and feed on the leaves of many deciduous trees.
moth of the Lymantriidae family

Examples

The lymantriid caterpillar can defoliate entire trees when present in large numbers.
Several lymantriid species are considered pests in forested regions worldwide.
A scientist collected a lymantriid specimen to study its larval development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gypsy moth - a species of lymantriid moth known for causing damage to forests

Etymology

From New Latin Lymantriidae, the scientific family name, derived from the type genus Lymantria.
