Pontederia

1. NOUN

/ pɒn.təˈdɪər.i.ə / (AmE), / pɒn.tɪˈdɪə.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic plants in the family Pontederiaceae, commonly known as pickerelweed, native to the Americas and often found in wetlands.
genus of aquatic plants (pickerelweed)

Examples

Pontederia is often seen growing at the edges of freshwater ponds and lakes.
The vibrant purple flowers of Pontederia attract bees and butterflies.
Gardeners appreciate Pontederia for its ability to improve water quality in garden ponds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickerelweed - a common name for aquatic plants of the genus Pontederia

Etymology

Named after Italian botanist Giulio Pontedera (1688–1757).

2. NOUN

/ pɒn.təˈdɪəriə / (AmE), / pɒn.tɪˈdɪəriə / (BrE)
A small genus of aquatic perennial plants in the family Pontederiaceae, including pickerelweed, found in American wetlands.
genus of aquatic plants (pickerelweed)

Examples

The wetland was dominated by Pontederia, making the shoreline colorful in summer.
Researchers observed increased biodiversity in habitats with abundant Pontederia populations.
Gardeners often plant Pontederia to attract pollinators and stabilize pond edges.
