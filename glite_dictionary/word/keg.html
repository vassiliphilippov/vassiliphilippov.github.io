keg

1. NOUN

/ kɛɡ / (AmE), / kɛɡ / (BrE)
the amount of liquid or substance held in a keg, especially as a unit of measurement
amount held in a keg

Examples

They ordered a keg of beer for the party, which lasted all night.
The brewery delivered two kegs to the bar this morning.
He drank nearly a whole keg of cider with his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrel - the quantity held in a barrel

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch *kegge* (small cask).

2. NOUN

/ kɛɡ / (AmE), / kɛɡ / (BrE)
a small barrel, usually used for storing and transporting liquids such as alcohol, especially beer
small barrel for liquids, esp. beer

Examples

They bought a keg of beer for the party.
The brewery delivered a fresh keg to the restaurant every week.
After the race, everyone gathered around the keg to celebrate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrel - a large cylindrical container, typically for storing liquids
  • Synonym: cask - a container used for storing alcoholic drinks

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle Dutch *kegge* (a small keg).
