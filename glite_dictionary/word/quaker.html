Quaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈkweɪkər / (AmE), / ˈkweɪkə / (BrE)
a member of the Religious Society of Friends, a Christian movement founded in the 17th century emphasizing simplicity, peace, and equality
member of the Religious Society of Friends

Examples

Her grandfather was a respected Quaker who promoted nonviolence and social justice.
The Quaker community played a key role in the abolition of slavery in the United States.
As a Quaker, she attends silent meetings for worship every Sunday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Friend - another term for a member of the Religious Society of Friends

Etymology

From 'quake' (to tremble), originally coined as a nickname for members who were said to 'quake' with religious fervor.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkweɪkər / (AmE), / ˈkweɪkə / (BrE)
A member of the Religious Society of Friends, a Christian movement founded in the 17th century that emphasizes direct experience of God and pacifism.
member of a Christian sect (Society of Friends)

Examples

Quakers are known for their commitment to peace and social justice.
Several famous abolitionists in the nineteenth century were Quakers.
Many Quakers gather for silent worship as part of their religious practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Friend - member of the Religious Society of Friends, also called a Quaker

Etymology

From quaker, originally a term of reproach, later adopted by the members; first used in 17th century England.
