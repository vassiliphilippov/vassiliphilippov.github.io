Varanidae

1. NOUN

/ vəˈrænɪˌdi / (AmE), / vəˈrænɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, carnivorous lizards known as monitor lizards, native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.
family of monitor lizards

Examples

The Varanidae are among the most intelligent reptiles known to science.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Varanidae in Indonesia.
All members of the Varanidae exhibit similar body structures and behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monitor lizard - large terrestrial lizard of the family Varanidae

Etymology

From New Latin Varanidae, from genus name Varanus (monitor lizard), itself from Arabic waran (lizard).
