well-off

1. ADJ

/ wɛl ˈɔf / (AmE), / wɛl ˈɒf / (BrE)
having plenty of money or being in a comfortable financial or social position; prosperous or fortunate.
prosperous or comfortably situated

Examples

Although Jane grew up well-off, she has always been generous to those less fortunate.
The school is located in a well-off neighborhood with many expensive homes.
His new job means they are much more well-off than they were before.

Synset Examples

doesn't know when he's well-off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wealthy - having a great deal of money, resources, or assets
  • Antonym: poor - lacking sufficient money to live comfortably

2. ADJ

/ wɛl ˈɔf / (AmE), / wɛl ˈɒf / (BrE)
having enough money to live comfortably; moderately wealthy or prosperous
having enough money; moderately wealthy

Examples

Although not rich, their family is considered well-off compared to most.
Many well-off neighborhoods have excellent schools and public services.
She grew up in a well-off household but worked hard to achieve her own success.
