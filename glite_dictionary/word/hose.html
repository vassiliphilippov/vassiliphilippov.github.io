hose

1. NOUN

/ hoʊz / (AmE), / həʊz / (BrE)
a flexible tube or pipe used for conveying liquids or gases from one place to another
flexible pipe for liquids or gases

Examples

Could you please water the garden with the hose?
The firefighter grabbed the hose to extinguish the flames.
After washing the car, he coiled up the hose neatly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipe - a tube through which liquids or gases can flow
  • Synonym: tube - a long hollow cylinder used for conveying fluids

Etymology

From Old English 'hosa' (covering for the leg), later extended to hoses for pipes.

4. NOUN

/ hoʊz / (AmE), / həʊz / (BrE)
A man's close-fitting garment covering the legs and extending up to the waist, worn in the 16th and 17th centuries, typically with a doublet.
men's leg garment from 16th–17th century
archaic

Examples

The actor wore authentic hose and a doublet to portray an Elizabethan nobleman.
In portraits from the Renaissance, men are often depicted in colorful hose.
Shakespearean costumes require specialized hose to achieve the correct period appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stocking - a close-fitting covering for the foot and lower leg, worn under shoes or boots
  • Synonym: breeches - short trousers fastened just below the knee, formerly daily wear for men

Etymology

From Old English hosa (covering for the leg), related to German Hose (trousers).
