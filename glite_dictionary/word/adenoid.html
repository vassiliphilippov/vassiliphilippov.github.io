adenoid

1. ADJ

/ ˈædəˌnɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈadɪnɔɪd / (BrE)
relating to or resembling lymphatic glands or lymphoid tissue, especially the adenoids in the pharynx.
relating to lymphatic glands
medical

Examples

His symptoms were associated with adenoid tissue obstructing the nasal airway.
The pathology report described an adenoid appearance in the biopsy sample.
Scientists studied the adenoid characteristics of the lymphatic cells.

Etymology

From Greek adenoeidēs (gland-like), from adēn (gland) + -oeidēs (-oid, form).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæd.əˌnɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈæd.ɪ.nɔɪd / (BrE)
a mass of lymphatic tissue located in the upper part of the throat behind the nose, which can become enlarged, especially in children
lymphatic tissue in the upper throat
medical

Examples

The doctor said that my son's adenoid was enlarged and needed to be checked.
Chronic infections can cause the adenoid to swell and make breathing difficult.
After her adenoid was removed, she slept much better at night.

Synset Examples

hypertrophy of the pharyngeal tonsils is called adenoids
enlarged adenoids may restrict the breathing of children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tonsil - either of two oval lymphatic tissues at the back of the throat

Etymology

From Greek 'adēn' (gland) + '-oid' (resembling).
