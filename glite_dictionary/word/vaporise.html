to vaporise

1. VERB

/ ˈveɪpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈveɪpəraɪz / (BrE)
to cause (a liquid or solid) to change into a vapor, especially by heating
cause to change into a vapor

Examples

The heat will vaporise the liquid almost instantly.
She used a special device to vaporise the chemicals in the laboratory.
If you vaporise the solution, only the solid residue remains.

Synset Examples

The chemist evaporated the water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evaporate - to change from liquid or solid into vapor
  • Antonym: to condense - to change from vapor to liquid or solid

Etymology

From vapor + -ise; compare American 'vaporize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈveɪpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈveɪpəˌraɪz / (BrE)
to cause a liquid to turn into vapor, often leaving behind a more concentrated residue
to turn into vapor, leaving concentrate

Examples

When you vaporise a salt solution, the water disappears and the salt becomes more concentrated.
The chef vaporises some of the milk to make a thicker sauce for the recipe.
If the sample is vaporised too quickly, important residue may be lost.

Synset Examples

evaporate milk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evaporate - to turn from liquid into vapor

Etymology

From Latin 'vapor' (steam, vapor), via French 'vaporiser'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈveɪpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈveɪpəraɪz / (BrE)
to change or be changed into vapor, especially by heating
change into vapor, especially by heating
british

Examples

When heated enough, water will vaporise and become steam.
Many chemicals can vaporise at room temperature, producing fumes.
The sun's warmth caused the morning dew to vaporise quickly.

Synset Examples

The water evaporated in front of our eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evaporate - to change from liquid to vapor, usually by heat
  • Antonym: to condense - to change from vapor to liquid

Etymology

From vapor + -ise, influenced by French vaporiser and Latin vapor (steam).

4. VERB

/ ˈveɪpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈveɪpəraɪz / (BrE)
to turn (a liquid or solid) into vapor; to change into a gas, especially by heating
turn into gas

Examples

When water is heated to 100°C, it will vaporise into steam.
Many chemicals vaporise quickly when left in an open container.
The intense heat caused the liquid to vaporise almost instantly.

Synset Examples

The substance gasified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evaporate - to change from a liquid into a vapor

Etymology

From Latin 'vapor' (steam) + English suffix '-ise', patterned after French 'vaporiser'.
