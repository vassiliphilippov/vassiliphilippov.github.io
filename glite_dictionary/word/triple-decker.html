triple-decker

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪpəl ˌdɛkɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpl ˌdɛkə / (BrE)
A sandwich made with three slices of bread, typically with two layers of filling.
sandwich with three slices of bread

Examples

He ordered a triple-decker stacked high with ham, cheese, and lettuce.
The restaurant is famous for its enormous triple-decker sandwiches.
You need a big appetite to finish that triple-decker for lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: club sandwich - a sandwich of toasted bread, usually with two layers of filling

Etymology

Formed from 'triple' (meaning three) and 'decker' (something arranged in layers), modeled after terms like 'double-decker'.
