toggle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtɒɡəl / (BrE)
a hinged switch that can be set to either of two positions to control a device or circuit
two-position electrical switch

Examples

The lamp is turned on by flipping the toggle up.
She installed a new toggle in the living room to control the ceiling fan.
Each machine has a safety toggle to quickly cut the power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switch - a device for making and breaking the connection in an electric circuit

Etymology

Originally referred to a pin or peg used as a fastener; extended in 20th-century technical usage.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04453086-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈtɑɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtɒɡəl / (BrE)
to provide with a toggle or toggles, especially as a method of fastening
to provide with a toggle (fastening)

Examples

She decided to toggle her coat to protect herself from the wind.
The craftsman will toggle each bag by hand to ensure sturdiness.
Some traditional duffle coats are still toggled rather than buttoned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: button - to provide with buttons (as fasteners)

Etymology

Originating from 18th-century English, possibly of dialectal origin, referring to a pin or rod used as a fastening.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02362560-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈtɑːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtɒɡəl / (BrE)
to fasten with, or as if with, a toggle (a short rod or pin used as a fastening)
fasten with a toggle

Examples

She quickly toggled her coat before stepping outside into the wind.
The bag can be securely toggled with a wooden peg to keep it closed.
He toggled the tent flap shut as the rain started to pour.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to unfasten - to undo or open something that is fastened

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly from earlier English dialect, related to 'toggle' (fastening pin).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01610908-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈtɑɡl̩ / (AmE), / ˈtɒɡl̩ / (BrE)
a fastener consisting of a short rod, pin, or crosspiece that is inserted through a loop or eye at the end of a rope, chain, or cable to fasten it securely
rod or pin fastener for ropes or cables

Examples

The anchor was secured using a toggle and rope.
He fastened the sail by slipping the toggle through a loop in the canvas.
The old trunk had a simple toggle to keep it closed tightly.

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *togyl*, of unknown origin; related to the sense of a pin or short rod.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04452461-n
Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ ˈtɑɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtɒɡəl / (BrE)
to release, activate, or operate by means of a toggle switch
operate by toggle switch

Examples

The pilot toggled the landing gear to deploy as the plane approached the runway.
To test the alarm, you should toggle the switch and listen for the beep.
With one quick motion, she toggled the emergency power system on.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to activate - make (a device or system) active or operative

Etymology

From earlier noun 'toggle' (pin or clasp), possibly related to 18th-century English dialect word 'togle' (a dowel or peg).

6. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtɒɡəl / (BrE)
a command or instruction that alternates between two opposite states, such as on and off, each time it is used
instruction that alternates between two states

Examples

You can enable dark mode with the toggle in the settings menu.
This toggle lets you switch the subtitles on and off during playback.
The mute toggle works by pressing the same button to mute and unmute the audio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switch - a device or control for turning something on or off
  • Synonym: button - a small control that you press to operate a device
