Lactuca serriola

1. NOUN

/ lækˈtuːkə səˈraɪələ / (AmE), / lækˈtjuːkə səˈrɪələ / (BrE)
A species of wild lettuce native to Europe and Asia, with prickly stems and leaves, often considered a weed in North America.
species of wild prickly lettuce

Examples

Lactuca serriola is often found growing along roadsides and in disturbed soils.
Some gardeners struggle to control Lactuca serriola because it spreads quickly as a weed.
Researchers study Lactuca serriola for its potential resistance to certain plant diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prickly lettuce - common English name for Lactuca serriola

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lactuca' meaning lettuce and 'serriola' referring to 'little saw', describing the serrated leaves.
