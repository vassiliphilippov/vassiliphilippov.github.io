galley

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæli / (AmE), / ˈɡæli / (BrE)
the kitchen area on an airliner, ship, or train where food is prepared and cooked
kitchen area on an aircraft or ship

Examples

The flight attendant prepared the meals in the galley during the flight.
Access to the galley is restricted to crew members only.
The galley was fully stocked with snacks and drinks for the long-haul journey.

Etymology

From Old French 'galie' (a galley ship), originally referring to ships and later to their kitchens.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæli / (AmE), / ˈɡali / (BrE)
In classical antiquity, a crescent-shaped seagoing vessel propelled chiefly by oars, used especially as a warship.
ancient oar-powered warship

Examples

The Roman fleet advanced with each galley cutting smoothly through the waves.
A well-trained crew was essential for maneuvering a galley during battle in ancient times.
Historians found drawings of a large galley on the walls of the ruins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trireme - an ancient Greek or Roman war galley with three banks of oars

Etymology

From Old French 'galie', from Medieval Greek 'galea' (galley, a kind of ship).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡæli / (AmE), / ˈɡæli / (BrE)
a large medieval ship with a single deck, propelled by sails and oars, armed at the stern and prow, and used mainly in the Mediterranean for war and trade
large medieval ship propelled by sails and oars

Examples

The Ottoman navy sailed a powerful galley across the Mediterranean during the 16th century.
Several hundred men were needed to row a medieval galley into battle.
Artifacts from a sunken galley revealed much about ancient maritime warfare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trireme - an ancient warship with three rows of oars on each side
  • Degree Higher: frigate - a fast, medium-sized warship

Etymology

From Old French 'galie', from Medieval Latin 'galea', meaning 'galley'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈɡæli / (AmE), / ˈɡali / (BrE)
the kitchen or area for preparing food on a ship, aircraft, or train
kitchen area on a ship or vehicle

Examples

The chef prepared several meals each day in the galley below deck.
After the storm, the galley was in disarray but the crew needed hot food.
On the yacht, the compact galley was equipped with a small stove and sink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kitchen - a room or area where food is prepared and cooked

Etymology

From Old French *galie* (a galley ship), adopted in English nautical use for shipboard kitchens.
