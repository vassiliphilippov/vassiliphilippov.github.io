disgruntled

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɡrʌntəld / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡrʌntəld / (BrE)
Annoyed, dissatisfied, or unhappy, typically because of unfair treatment or disappointment.
annoyed or dissatisfied

Examples

Several disgruntled employees voiced their complaints at the meeting.
She sounded disgruntled after the changes to the schedule were announced.
The manager tried to appease the disgruntled customers with a discount.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: satisfied - content, pleased, or fulfilled
  • Antonym: contented - feeling satisfied or at ease
  • Synonym: annoyed - slightly angry or irritated

Etymology

Formed from 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'gruntle' (obsolete meaning: to grumble).
