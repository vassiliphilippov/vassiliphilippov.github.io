Solzhenitsyn

1. PROPN

/ səlˈʒɛnɪtsɪn / (AmE), / sɒlˈʒɛnɪtsɪn / (BrE)
Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn (1918–2008), a Soviet writer and political dissident known for exposing the brutality of Soviet labor camps in his novels.
Soviet writer and political dissident

Examples

Many readers discovered the horrors of the Gulag through Solzhenitsyn's powerful books.
The works of Solzhenitsyn remain influential in discussions about human rights and totalitarianism.
After his exile, Solzhenitsyn continued to write and speak about freedom and oppression.

Etymology

Transliteration of the Russian surname Солженицын (Solzhenitsyn).
