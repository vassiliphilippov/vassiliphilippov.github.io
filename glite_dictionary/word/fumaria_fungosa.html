Fumaria fungosa

1. NOUN

/ fjuːˈmɛəriə fʌŋˈɡoʊsə / (AmE), / fjuːˈmɑːriə fʌŋˈɡəʊsə / (BrE)
A vine with feathery leaves and white or pinkish flowers, sometimes classified within the genus Fumaria.
a species of flowering vine in Fumaria

Examples

The delicate flowers of Fumaria fungosa add a gentle color to woodland gardens.
Botanists debated whether Fumaria fungosa should be placed in its own genus or not.
A cluster of Fumaria fungosa was discovered growing alongside other wildflowers in the meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fumaria officinalis - A related Fumaria species known as common fumitory

Etymology

From Latin: 'fumaria' meaning smoke, a reference to the genus; 'fungosa' from Latin 'fungosus' meaning 'spongy' or 'mushroom-like'.
