punch

1. VERB

/ pʌntʃ / (AmE), / pʌntʃ / (BrE)
to make a hole in something using a special tool, especially to allow for easier separation or organization
make a hole with a tool

Examples

Please punch holes in these documents before filing them.
She punched a ticket at the entrance using a handheld tool.
The machine automatically punches holes in each sheet for binding.

Synset Examples

perforate the sheets of paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to perforate - to pierce and make a line of holes in something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01445398-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ pʌntʃ / (AmE), / pʌntʃ / (BrE)
an iced mixed drink, often containing alcohol and fruit juices, prepared in large quantities and served from a bowl
alcoholic mixed drink served in a bowl

Examples

She served a delicious bowl of punch at the holiday party.
Everyone gathered around the table for a refreshing glass of punch.
The recipe for the summer punch included rum, fruit juice, and sparkling water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cocktail - an alcoholic mixed drink typically prepared individually

Etymology

Probably from Hindi 'pāñc' (five), referring to the original five ingredients of the drink.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ pʌntʃ / (AmE), / pʌntʃ / (BrE)
to hit someone or something quickly and forcefully with a closed fist
to strike with a closed fist

Examples

He was so angry that he punched the wall.
During the boxing match, she punched her opponent in the jaw.
Please do not punch anyone, even if you are upset.

Synset Examples

he punched me in the stomach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit - to strike someone or something with force
  • Degree Lower: slap - to hit with an open hand
  • Synonym: strike - to deliver a blow to someone or something

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from Middle English punchen (to thrust, push), related to Old French ponchonner (to stamp, emboss).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01417939-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ pʌntʃ / (AmE), / pʌntʃ / (BrE)
A forceful blow made with a closed fist, especially in boxing or fighting.
a blow with the fist

Examples

He threw a powerful punch that knocked his opponent to the ground.
During the fight, Lisa landed a punch right on her brother's chin.
The boxer blocked the first punch but couldn't avoid the second one.

Synset Examples

I gave him a clout on his nose

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: slap - a sharp blow with the open hand
  • Synonym: strike - to hit forcibly and deliberately

Etymology

Probably from Middle English punchen (to thrust, prod), from Old French poinson (a pointed tool), from Latin punctio (a pricking).

Source: WordNet

5. NOUN

/ pʌntʃ / (AmE), / pʌntʃ / (BrE)
A tool with a hard, pointed end, used for making holes or indentations in various materials by striking it with a hammer or by pressing.
tool for making holes or indentations

Examples

The metalworker used a punch to mark the position of the hole.
You need a punch to make a neat hole in the leather belt.
I bought a new punch for my craft projects at the hardware store.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awl - a pointed tool for making holes, especially in leather

Etymology

From Middle English pounce, punche (pointed tool), possibly from Old French ponchon (pointed tool).

Source: WordNet
