bet

1. NOUN

/ bɛt / (AmE), / bɛt / (BrE)
an act of risking money or something valuable on the outcome of an event, game, or situation; a wager
an act of gambling; a wager

Examples

She placed a bet on the outcome of the football match.
He lost his bet and had to pay for everyone's dinner.
On a dare and a bet, he jumped into the freezing lake.

Synset Examples

he did it on a bet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wager - an act of betting or staking something on an event
  • Synonym: gamble - to bet money or valuables on an uncertain outcome

Etymology

From Old English *beot* (a vow or boast).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00507669-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ bɛt / (AmE), / bɛt / (BrE)
an amount of money risked on the outcome of a future event, especially in gambling
money risked on a gamble

Examples

She placed a bet on her favorite horse to win the race.
He lost his bet after the team unexpectedly lost the game.
The total bet on the roulette table exceeded two thousand dollars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wager - an amount of money or something valuable risked in a bet

Etymology

Probably from the obsolete English word 'bat' meaning 'good' or 'advantage'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13364541-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ bɛt / (AmE), / bɛt / (BrE)
(informal) To be sure or confident that something will happen or is true; to rely on or trust.
be confident something will happen
informal

Examples

You can bet I'll be at your party next week.
I wouldn't bet on him arriving on time, but it's possible.
You can always bet that Sarah will help if you ask.

Synset Examples

you can count on me to help you any time
Look to your friends for support
You can bet on that!
Depend on your family in times of crisis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to count on - to rely on, trust
  • Synonym: to depend on - to place trust or confidence in
  • Antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about

Etymology

Originally from Old English 'bettan', related to gambling or wagering, later extended informally to expressions of certainty.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00714537-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ bɛt / (AmE), / bɛt / (BrE)
to state confidently that something will happen, often as if making a wager; to wager money or something of value on the outcome of an event
state with confidence; wager

Examples

I bet she will win the competition this year.
You can bet that prices will rise again before summer.
He bet his friend that it would rain tomorrow.

Synset Examples

I bet she will be there!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wager - to risk money or something of value on the outcome of an event
  • Degree Lower: to predict - to say that something will happen in the future
  • Degree Higher: guarantee - promise with certainty

Etymology

From Middle English betten (to pledge as a wager), probably from Old English *beatan* (to beat), but influenced in sense by late 16th-century usage.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00920551-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ bɛt / (AmE), / bɛt / (BrE)
to risk money or something valuable against the result of an event or a competition, such as a horse race, game, or election
to stake money on an outcome

Examples

She decided to bet all her savings on her favorite team.
Many people bet on horse races during the annual festival.
He likes to bet a small amount on every football game he watches.

Synset Examples

I bet $100 on that new horse
She played all her money on the dark horse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wager - risking money or something valuable on an outcome
  • Synonym: gamble - to take risky action in the hope of a desired result, especially involving money

Etymology

From Middle English 'betten', related to Old English 'beot' (a pledge or vow).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
