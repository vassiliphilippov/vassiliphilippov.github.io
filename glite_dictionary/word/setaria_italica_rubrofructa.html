Setaria italica rubrofructa

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈtɛɹiə ɪˈtælɪkə ˌruːbroʊˈfrʌktə / (AmE), / sɛˈtɛːriə ɪˈtalɪkə ˌruːbrəʊˈfrʌktə / (BrE)
a variety of millet characterized by orange to reddish grains in long bristly spikes
millet with orange-reddish grains

Examples

Farmers in this region often cultivate Setaria italica rubrofructa for its nutritious, colorful grains.
The long bristly spikes of Setaria italica rubrofructa distinguish it from other millet varieties.
Researchers are studying the drought resistance of Setaria italica rubrofructa compared to other millets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millet - a group of small-seeded grasses, widely grown as cereals

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; 'Setaria' from Latin 'seta' (bristle), 'italica' meaning 'Italian', and 'rubrofructa' from Latin 'rubro-' (red) + 'fructus' (fruit).
