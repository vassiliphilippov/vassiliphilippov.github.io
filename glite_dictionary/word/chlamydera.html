Chlamydera

1. NOUN

/ klæˈmɪdərə / (AmE), / klæˈmɪdərə / (BrE)
A genus of bowerbirds native to Australia and New Guinea, belonging to the family Ptilonorhynchidae.
genus of Australasian bowerbirds

Examples

The genus Chlamydera includes several species known for building intricate bowers to attract mates.
Scientists observed a member of the Chlamydera genus constructing its bower with blue objects.
Members of Chlamydera are found primarily in the forests of Australia and New Guinea.

Etymology

From Greek 'chlamys' (cloak) and 'dera' (neck), referring to the bird's feather features.
