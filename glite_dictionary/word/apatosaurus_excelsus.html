Apatosaurus excelsus

1. NOUN

/ əˌpæt̬.əˈsɔːr.əs ɪkˈsɛl.səs / (AmE), / əˌpæt.əˈsɔː.rəs ɪkˈsel.səs / (BrE)
a large, long-necked herbivorous dinosaur of the genus Apatosaurus, common in North America during the Late Jurassic period
species of large Jurassic herbivorous dinosaur

Examples

The fossils of Apatosaurus excelsus have been found throughout the western United States.
Many children can recognize Apatosaurus excelsus because of its long neck and tail.
Paleontologists believe that Apatosaurus excelsus lived in herds during the Jurassic period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brontosaurus - another large Jurassic sauropod dinosaur, formerly considered a separate genus

Etymology

From Greek 'apatē' (deception) and 'sauros' (lizard); 'excelsus' is Latin for 'lofty' or 'high'.
