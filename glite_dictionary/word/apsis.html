apsis

1. NOUN

/ ˈæp.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈæp.sɪs / (BrE)
a domed or vaulted recess, usually at the east end of a church, typically containing the altar
recess at end of a church for altar

Examples

The altar was positioned in the apsis, directly beneath the ornate stained-glass window.
During the restoration, artisans carefully cleaned the mosaics decorating the ancient apsis.
The cathedral's apsis is one of the most beautifully constructed parts of the building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apse - recess at the end of a church building

Etymology

From Latin 'apsis' (arch, vault), from Greek 'hapsis' (arch, vault).
