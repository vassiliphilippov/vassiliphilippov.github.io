monkey

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋki / (BrE)
A small to medium-sized primate with a long tail, belonging to a group distinct from prosimians and apes.
a long-tailed primate

Examples

A curious monkey watched us from the branches above.
The zoo has several species of monkey in their rainforest exhibit.
Scientists often study the behavior of monkeys to understand primate intelligence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ape - a primate without a tail or with a very short tail

Etymology

From Middle Low German *monnik*, possibly originally a proper name, later applied to the animal.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋki / (BrE)
a person, especially a child, who is playfully mischievous
playfully mischievous person

Examples

Stop climbing the furniture, you little monkey!
Her nephew is a real monkey, always playing tricks on everyone.
The teacher laughed as the class clown acted like a monkey during recess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rascal - a mischievous or cheeky person
  • Synonym: imp - a mischievous child

/ ˈmʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋki / (BrE)

4. VERB

/ ˈmʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋki / (BrE)
to engage in random, unplanned activities or spend time idly, often without achieving anything significant.
spend time idly or playfully
informal

Examples

The kids spent the afternoon monkeying around in the backyard instead of doing their chores.
He was supposed to fix the car, but he just monkeyed with it for hours.
They've been monkeying around in the garage, probably building something silly.

Synset Examples

The old lady is usually mucking about in her little house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idle - to spend time doing nothing or little of importance
  • Antonym: work - to engage in productive activity
