composite

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈpɑzɪt / (AmE), / kəmˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
of, relating to, or belonging to the plant family Compositae (now commonly called Asteraceae), which includes daisies, sunflowers, and related plants.
relating to the daisy family of plants

Examples

Sunflowers are classic examples of composite flowers, with many tiny florets arranged in a single head.
The composite family includes such familiar plants as daisies and asters.
Botanists often study the reproductive structures of composite species to understand plant evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Asteraceae - the plant family commonly known as the daisy family

Etymology

From Latin compositus (put together), used in botanical naming for the plant family Compositae.

4. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɑzɪt / (AmE), / kəmˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
a plant of a large family (Compositae or Asteraceae) characterized by dense flower heads composed of many small florets that together resemble a single flower.
a plant with dense flower heads (Asteraceae family)

Examples

The sunflower is a well-known example of a composite in the plant kingdom.
Many composites have brightly colored florets that attract pollinators to the dense flower head.
Botanists often study composite species because of their evolutionary success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - a plant in the Asteraceae family with star-shaped flower heads
  • Synonym: daisy - a common type of composite flower

Etymology

From Latin compositus (put together, arranged), later applied in botany to describe plants with composite flower heads.
