Chelone

1. PROPN

/ kɪˈloʊni / (AmE), / kɪˈləʊni / (BrE)
A genus of North American herbaceous perennial plants with tubular flowers, commonly called shellflowers.
genus of shellflower plants

Examples

The garden was full of Chelone blooming in late summer.
Chelone species thrive in moist woodland settings and along stream banks.
Botanists often refer to Chelone as shellflower because of its distinctive flower shape.

Etymology

From Greek 'khelṓnē' meaning 'tortoise', referring to the flower shape.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈloʊni / (AmE), / kɪˈləʊni / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous perennial plants in the family Plantaginaceae, commonly known as shellflowers, native to North America.
genus of herbaceous perennials (shellflowers)

Examples

Many gardeners appreciate the attractive blossoms of Chelone in shady borders.
The wetlands were filled with vibrant Chelone plants in late summer.
Botanists catalogued several species within the genus Chelone during their fieldwork.

Etymology

From Greek 'khelōnē', meaning 'tortoise', referring to the flower's shape.
