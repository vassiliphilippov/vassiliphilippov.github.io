Momos

1. PROPN

/ ˈmoʊ.mɒs / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.mɒs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the god of blame and mockery, known for criticizing the flaws of gods and humans.
Greek god of blame and mockery

Examples

In ancient tales, Momos would mock even the most powerful Olympian gods.
Writers often invoke Momos when satirizing the follies of society.
The sharp humor of Momos spared no one, not even Zeus.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Μῶμος (Mômos), meaning blame or censure.
