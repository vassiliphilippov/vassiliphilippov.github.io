Brugmansia sanguinea

1. NOUN

/ bruːɡˈmænziə sænˈɡwiːniə / (AmE), / bruːɡˈmanziə sanˈɡwiːnɪə / (BrE)
a South American shrub or small tree characterized by very large, pendulous orange-red flowers
South American shrub with orange-red flowers

Examples

The striking orange-red blooms of the Brugmansia sanguinea attract many garden enthusiasts.
She decided to plant a Brugmansia sanguinea next to the fence for its vibrant color.
The botanical garden features a rare collection of Brugmansia sanguinea in its South American exhibit.

Etymology

The genus Brugmansia is named after Dutch botanist Sebald Justinus Brugmans; 'sanguinea' is Latin for 'blood red', referring to the flower color.
