Huron

1. PROPN

/ ˈhjʊrɑn / (AmE), / ˈhjʊərɒn / (BrE)
The second largest of the Great Lakes of North America, located between the United States and Canada.
the second largest Great Lake

Examples

Lake Huron is known for its beautiful clear waters and sandy beaches.
The freighter crossed Huron before heading into Lake Michigan.
Many tourists visit the shores of Huron during summer vacation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Superior - the largest of the Great Lakes of North America
  • Degree Lower: Erie - the fourth largest of the Great Lakes of North America

Etymology

From French 'Huron', adapted from Algonquian languages, originally the name of the Huron people.
