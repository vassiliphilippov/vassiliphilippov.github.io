distastefulness

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈteɪstfəlnəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈteɪstfəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being unpleasant, offensive, or causing dislike
quality of being offensive or unpleasant

Examples

Many critics commented on the distastefulness of the advertisement's humor.
The distastefulness of the remarks shocked everyone in the meeting.
He was unaware of the distastefulness his actions caused among his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offensiveness - the quality of being offensive
  • Antonym: appeal - attractiveness or charm

Etymology

From 'distasteful' + '-ness', where 'distasteful' comes from 'distaste' (from dis- expressing reversal + taste) + '-ful'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈteɪstfəlnəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈteɪstfʊlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely unpleasant to taste or experience; the state of being unpalatable or offensive.
extreme unpleasantness or unpalatability

Examples

The distastefulness of the medicine made it hard for children to swallow.
Many people complained about the distastefulness of the dish at the banquet.
She commented on the distastefulness of the joke, finding it quite offensive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpalatability - the quality of being unpleasant to taste
  • Antonym: pleasantness - the quality of being pleasant or pleasing to the senses

Etymology

From 'distasteful' + '-ness'.
