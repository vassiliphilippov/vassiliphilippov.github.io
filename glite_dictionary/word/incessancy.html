incessancy

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɛsənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛsənsi / (BrE)
the quality of continuing without pause or interruption
continuity without interruption

Examples

The incessancy of the rain made it impossible to plan any outdoor activities.
She found the incessancy of the city noise exhausting after a week.
Many poets have written about the incessancy of the waves crashing against the shore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incessance - the state of being incessant; unceasingness
  • Antonym: interruption - the act of stopping or breaking the continuity of something

Etymology

From Latin 'incessans' (uninterrupted, ceaseless), from 'in-' (not) + 'cessare' (to cease).
