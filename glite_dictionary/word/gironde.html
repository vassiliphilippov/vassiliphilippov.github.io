Gironde

1. NOUN

/ ʒɪˈrɒnd / (AmE), / ʒɪˈrɒnd / (BrE)
the French moderate political party that held power from 1791 to 1793 during the French Revolution, known for advocating constitutional government and opposing radicalism.
French moderate political party (1791–1793)

Examples

The Gironde played a crucial role in shaping the early years of the French Revolution.
Historians often debate the policies enacted by the Gironde during their time in power.
Many moderate revolutionaries in the National Convention were aligned with the Gironde.

Etymology

From French 'Gironde', after the Gironde department in France, where many leaders came from.
