to dispose

1. VERB

/ dɪˈspoʊz / (AmE), / dɪˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to place or arrange things in a particular order or position
arrange or put in order

Examples

The chairs were disposed in a circle for the meeting.
Artwork was tastefully disposed around the gallery walls.
Please dispose the materials according to the layout instructions.

Synset Examples

the dots are unevenly disposed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arrange - to put things in a particular order or pattern

Etymology

From Old French *disposer*, from Latin *disponere* (to arrange).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01499136-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈspoʊz / (AmE), / dɪˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to make someone ready or willing to do something, or to have a particular attitude or belief
make willing or inclined

Examples

Her calm demeanor disposed the team to trust her judgment.
The pleasant atmosphere disposed guests to stay longer after dinner.
His generous actions disposed others towards friendship and cooperation.

Synset Examples

Their language inclines us to believe them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incline - make someone feel willing or disposed to do something

Etymology

From Latin disponere (to arrange), via Old French disposer.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00682760-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪsˈpoʊz / (AmE), / dɪsˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to make someone or something ready or prepared for a particular purpose; to arrange or incline
make fit or prepared

Examples

A good night's sleep will dispose you to learn more effectively.
Her calm demeanor disposed the team to trust her leadership.
The training sessions disposed the staff to handle emergencies more confidently.

Synset Examples

Your education qualifies you for this job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prepare - to make ready for use or consideration
  • Antonym: to disqualify - to make unfit or not prepared for something

Etymology

From Latin 'disponere' (to arrange), via Old French 'disposer'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00301542-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ dɪˈspoʊz / (AmE), / dɪˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to give, sell, or transfer something to another person; to deal with or get rid of, especially by placing in the hands of someone else
to transfer or get rid of something

Examples

She disposed of her late uncle's book collection by donating it to a library.
After deciding to downsize, they disposed of many household items through an online auction.
The company disposed of its non-core assets to focus on its main business.

Synset Examples

She disposed of her parents' possessions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to retain - to keep possession of something
  • Synonym: discard - to throw away or get rid of something

Etymology

From Old French 'disposer', from Latin 'disponere' (to arrange).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02359918-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ dɪˈspoʊz / (AmE), / dɪˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to get rid of something, especially by throwing it away or otherwise discarding it
to get rid of; throw away

Examples

Many people do not know how to properly dispose of hazardous waste.
After reviewing the old documents, she decided to dispose of most of them.
It is illegal to dispose batteries in regular household trash in some areas.

Synset Examples

Put away your worries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to throw something away; to get rid of something as no longer useful
  • Antonym: to retain - to keep possession of something instead of getting rid of it

Etymology

From Latin disponere (to arrange), via Old French disposer.

Source: WordNet
