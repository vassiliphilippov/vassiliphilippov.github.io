seasnail

1. NOUN

/ siːˌsneɪl / (AmE), / siːˌsneɪl / (BrE)
a small cold-water fish with a soft, tadpole-shaped body and a pelvic fin modified into a sucker, related to the lumpfish
small cold-water fish with sucker fin

Examples

The seasnail uses its sucker-like fin to cling to rocks in rough waters.
Divers occasionally spot a seasnail camouflaged against the pebbles on the sea floor.
Researchers studied the seasnail to understand its adaptation to cold marine environments.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːˌsneɪl / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌsneɪl / (BrE)
a small marine gastropod mollusk with a spirally coiled shell, such as a whelk or moon snail
marine gastropod with a coiled shell

Examples

She found a beautiful seasnail shell while walking along the shore.
Many seasnails feed on algae growing on rocks under the sea.
The diver spotted a colorful seasnail gliding slowly across the ocean floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whelk - a type of sea snail with a spiral shell, often edible
  • Synonym: moon snail - a type of sea snail with a smooth, rounded shell

Etymology

From 'sea' + 'snail', describing its marine habitat and resemblance to land snails.
