abstainer

1. NOUN

/ əbˈsteɪnər / (AmE), / əbˈsteɪnə / (BrE)
a person who chooses not to drink alcoholic beverages
person who does not drink alcohol

Examples

As an abstainer, she always declined offers of wine at dinner parties.
Many health organizations encourage people to become abstainers to reduce alcohol-related risks.
His friends respected his decision to be an abstainer during the celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teetotaler - someone who never drinks alcohol

Etymology

From Latin abstinēre (to withhold).

2. NOUN

/ əbˈsteɪnɚ / (AmE), / əbˈsteɪnə / (BrE)
A person who deliberately refrains from certain pleasures or indulgences, especially as a spiritual or moral discipline.
person who refrains from indulgences

Examples

As an abstainer, she refused to eat sweets during Lent.
Many monks are known to be lifelong abstainers from alcohol and rich foods.
The club encouraged its members to be abstainers from all worldly temptations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascetic - a person who practices severe self-discipline and abstention
  • Antonym: hedonist - a person who pursues pleasure as a goal

Etymology

From abstain + -er; 'abstain' from Latin 'abstinere' (to withhold).
