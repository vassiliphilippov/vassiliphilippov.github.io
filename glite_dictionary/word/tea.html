tea

1. NOUN

/ tiː / (AmE), / tiː / (BrE)
A hot or cold beverage made by infusing dried tea leaves in water, often served with milk, sugar, or lemon.
drink made by steeping tea leaves

Examples

Would you like a cup of tea with your breakfast?
She prefers her tea with a slice of lemon and a bit of honey.
On hot days, I enjoy drinking iced tea in the afternoon.

Synset Examples

iced tea is a cooling drink

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: herbal tea - a drink made by infusing herbs or botanicals in water, not real tea leaves
  • Antonym: coffee - a popular beverage made from roasted coffee beans

Etymology

From Chinese (Amoy dialect) 'te', meaning tea, via Malay.

2. NOUN

/ tiː / (AmE), / tiː / (BrE)
the dried leaves of the tea plant, used to make a hot drink by infusing them in boiling water
dried leaves for making a hot drink

Examples

Loose leaf tea is often considered superior to bagged versions.
They imported a shipment of rare Chinese tea last month.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tea - a hot drink made by infusing tea leaves in boiling water

Etymology

3. NOUN

/ tiː / (AmE), / tiː / (BrE)
A light midafternoon meal, usually including tea to drink and sandwiches or cakes to eat.
afternoon meal with tea and snacks
british

Examples

Every afternoon at four, Mary sits down for her tea with scones and jam.
In some British households, tea is considered more important than dinner.
They invited us to join them for tea in the garden.

Synset Examples

an Englishman would interrupt a war to have his afternoon tea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afternoon tea - a light meal including tea, sandwiches, and cakes, taken in the afternoon

Etymology

From Chinese (Min Nan dialect) 'te', entered English via Dutch 'thee'.

4. NOUN

/ ti / (AmE), / tiː / (BrE)
a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree (Camellia sinensis) whose leaves are used to make the beverage tea, native to Asia and widely cultivated
the plant that produces tea leaves

Examples

The tea plant thrives in humid climates with plenty of rainfall.
Farmers harvest tea leaves by hand in many regions of India.
The fragrant white flowers of the tea shrub bloom in spring.

Synset Examples

tea has fragrant white flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tea - a drink made by infusing the dried, crushed leaves of the tea plant in boiling water

Etymology

From Chinese (Amoy dialect) 'te' or Cantonese 'cha'; adopted via Malay.
