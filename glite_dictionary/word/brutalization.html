brutalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌbruːtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbruːtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of treating someone with extreme cruelty or violence
act of treating with extreme cruelty

Examples

The brutalization of prisoners during the war was condemned by human rights organizations.
Constant exposure to violence can lead to the brutalization of young people.
He spoke out against the brutalization he witnessed in the prison system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruelty - deliberate infliction of pain or suffering
  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly or considerate

Etymology

From 'brutalize' + '-ation', formed after 'brutal' from Latin 'brutalis' (dull, stupid, like a brute).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbruːtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbruːtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or act of making someone cruel, insensitive, or less able to feel normal human emotions through exposure to violence or harsh treatment
making someone cruel or insensitive

Examples

Some critics believe that exposure to violent media can lead to the brutalization of young people.
The brutalization of prisoners was condemned by the international community.
Years of war caused the brutalization of society, making compassion rare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dehumanization - the process of depriving a person or group of positive human qualities

Etymology

From 'brutalize' + '-ation'; 'brutalize' from 'brutal' (from French 'brutal', from Latin 'brutalis', from 'brutus', meaning 'dull, insensitive').

3. NOUN

/ ˌbrudl̩ɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbruːtəlʌɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the condition or process of being treated in a cruel, violent, or inhumane way
the act or state of being made brutal

Examples

The brutalization of prisoners during the war was widely condemned by the international community.
Years of conflict led to the brutalization of the entire population.
She spoke out against the brutalization of minorities in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruelty - deliberate infliction of pain or suffering
  • Antonym: humanization - the process of making something more humane

Etymology

From 'brutalize' (to make brutal) + '-ation', from Latin 'brutalis'.
