sissy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪsi / (AmE), / ˈsɪsi / (BrE)
a boy or man regarded as effeminate, timid, or cowardly
timid or effeminate boy or man

Examples

If you don't want to climb the tree, don't let anyone call you a sissy.
He was bullied at school for being a sissy and not liking sports.
She told her brother to stop being such a sissy and join the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coward - a person who lacks courage
  • Antonym: brave - showing courage

Etymology

Diminutive of 'sister', used as a disparaging term for effeminate males.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪsi / (AmE), / ˈsɪsi / (BrE)
(of a boy or man) having characteristics regarded as traditionally feminine; lacking in traditional masculinity.
showing traditionally feminine qualities (disapproving)
offensive

Examples

Some boys were bullied at school for being too sissy to play football.
He was teased by his friends for his sissy mannerisms and voice.
Wearing pink was once unfairly considered a sissy thing for boys to do.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effeminate - having characteristics regarded as typical of a woman; unmanly
  • Antonym: manly - having qualities traditionally associated with men

Etymology

From sister, with diminutive suffix -y, originally as a childish or mocking form.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪsi / (AmE), / ˈsɪsi / (BrE)
a person, especially a boy or man, who is regarded as weak, cowardly, or effeminate, often used as an insult
person regarded as weak or cowardly (insult)
offensive

Examples

The other boys called him a sissy because he refused to fight.
Don't be such a sissy—it's only a little spider!
He was teased at school and labeled a sissy for enjoying ballet.

Synset Examples

The kids teased him, calling him a sissy for crying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coward - a person who lacks courage
  • Antonym: brave - possessing or displaying courage

Etymology

Probably from 'sister', used in a derogatory sense for effeminate boys or men.
