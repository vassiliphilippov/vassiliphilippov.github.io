Lydia

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɪdiə / (AmE), / ˈlɪdɪə / (BrE)
an ancient region in western Asia Minor, known as a powerful kingdom until it was conquered by the Persians in 546 BC
ancient region in western Asia Minor

Examples

The riches of Lydia were legendary in the ancient world.
Historians have studied the decline of Lydia after its conquest by Persia.
Artifacts from Lydia provide insight into early Anatolian civilization.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Λυδία (Lydia), the name of the region and people.
