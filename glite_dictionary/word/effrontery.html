effrontery

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfrʌn.tə.ri / (AmE), / ɪˈfrʌn.təri / (BrE)
rude or arrogant boldness; shameless or impudent behavior that shocks or offends others
shameless or arrogant boldness

Examples

She had the effrontery to ask for a pay raise after arriving late every day.
His effrontery stunned everyone in the meeting, as he openly criticized the director.
It takes some effrontery to challenge the judge in court like that.

Synset Examples

he despised them for their presumptuousness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impudence - rudeness or lack of respect; shamelessness
  • Synonym: presumption - behavior that is too confident or bold, often disrespectful
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of self-confidence or boldness; shyness

Etymology

From French 'effronterie', from 'effronté' (shameless), from Latin 'effrons' (barefaced).
