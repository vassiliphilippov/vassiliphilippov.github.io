ground swell

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndˌswɛl / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌswɛl / (BrE)
a broad, deep wave or series of waves in the ocean, typically caused by distant storms or seismic activity
broad deep ocean wave or undulation

Examples

The fishing boat rocked gently on the ground swell as it neared the coast.
A massive ground swell made swimming dangerous at the beach that morning.
Surfers waited patiently for the next ground swell to approach the shore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swell - a long wave on water that moves continuously without breaking
