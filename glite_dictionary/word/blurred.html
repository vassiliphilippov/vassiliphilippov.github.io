blurred

1. ADJ

/ blɝd / (AmE), / blɜːd / (BrE)
Not clear in form, outline, or meaning; indistinct or difficult to perceive.
unclear; indistinct; hard to perceive

Examples

The edges of the photograph were blurred, making it hard to see the details.
Her memory of that day has become blurred over time.
The line between friendship and romance can sometimes be blurred.

Synset Examples

the blurred aims of the group
sometimes one understood clearly and sometimes the meaning was clouded

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clear - easy to perceive; sharp, not blurred
  • Synonym: obscure - not clear or easy to understand

2. ADJ

/ blɝd / (AmE), / blɜːd / (BrE)
not clear or distinct in outline or detail; indistinct or hazy.
indistinct or hazy in outline

Examples

Through the rain-soaked window, the city lights appeared blurred and distant.
Her memories of childhood are only blurred images now.
The artist deliberately painted blurred figures to create a sense of mystery.

Synset Examples

a landscape of blurred outlines
the trees were just blurry shapes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret

Etymology

Past participle of 'blur', probably of Scandinavian origin.
