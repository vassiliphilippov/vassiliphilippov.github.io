Judas Maccabeus

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒuːdəs ˌmækəˈbiːəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːdəs ˌmækəˈbiːəs / (BrE)
a Jewish leader who led a revolt in Judea and helped recover Jerusalem around 166 BC; regarded as a hero in the Apocryphal books I Maccabees and II Maccabees
Jewish leader of a revolt in Judea (c. 166 BC)

Examples

Many historians consider Judas Maccabeus a key figure in the history of ancient Israel.
The bravery of Judas Maccabeus is celebrated during Hanukkah traditions.
In religious studies, students read about Judas Maccabeus in the books of Maccabees.

Etymology

From Greek Ioudas Makkabaios, the name given in early Jewish and Christian sources.
