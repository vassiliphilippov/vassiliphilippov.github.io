Mantophasmatodea

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæn.toʊ.fæzˈmeɪ.tə.di.ə / (AmE), / ˌmæn.təʊ.fazˈmæt.ə.dɪə / (BrE)
an order of predatory insects discovered in 2002, known from fossil and extant species, native to southern Africa and first identified in Baltic amber
an order of predatory insects discovered in 2002

Examples

Researchers recently discovered living Mantophasmatodea in Namibia, overturning the belief that they were only known from fossils.
The unique leg structure of Mantophasmatodea distinguishes them from other insect orders.
Scientists examine genetic data to better understand the evolutionary history of Mantophasmatodea.

Etymology

From Greek 'mantis' (prophet or praying mantis) + 'phasmatodea' (walking stick insects), referring to its mixture of features.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæntoʊˌfæzməˈtoʊdiə / (AmE), / ˌmantəʊˌfazməˈtəʊdɪə / (BrE)
An order of predatory, wingless insects first identified in Baltic amber from about 45 million years ago and described as a new group in 2002.
an order of wingless predatory insects

Examples

Fossils of Mantophasmatodea have been discovered preserved in Baltic amber.
Scientists were excited by the identification of Mantophasmatodea as a new insect order.
The unique predatory behavior of Mantophasmatodea sets them apart from other related insects.

Etymology

From Greek 'mantis' (prophet, praying mantis) and 'phasma' (apparition, phantom), referring to morphological similarities to mantids and stick insects.
