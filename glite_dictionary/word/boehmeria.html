Boehmeria

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈmɛriə / (AmE), / bəʊˈmɪərɪə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the nettle family (Urticaceae), commonly known as false nettles, native to Asia, Africa, and the Americas.
a genus of plants called false nettles

Examples

Several species of Boehmeria are commonly known as false nettles due to their resemblance to true nettles.
Boehmeria leaves are often used in herbal medicine in some cultures.
The botanist collected samples of Boehmeria from the tropical forest for research purposes.

Etymology

Named after German botanist Georg Rudolf Boehmer (1723–1803).

2. NOUN

/ boʊˈmɪəriə / (AmE), / bəʊˈmɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the nettle family (Urticaceae), commonly known as false nettles, found in tropical and subtropical regions.
a genus of plants called false nettles

Examples

The leaves of Boehmeria species are often mistaken for those of true nettles.
Many gardens cultivate Boehmeria for its ornamental foliage and fiber.
Researchers studied the properties of Boehmeria bark in traditional textile production.

Etymology

Named after German botanist Georg Rudolf Boehmer (1723–1803).
