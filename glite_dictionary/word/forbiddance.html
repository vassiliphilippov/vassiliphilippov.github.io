forbiddance

1. NOUN

/ fərˈbɪdən(t)s / (AmE), / fəˈbɪdəns / (BrE)
the act of formally or officially forbidding something; a prohibition.
the act of forbidding; a prohibition

Examples

Despite his father's forbiddance, Tim snuck out to join his friends.
The school's forbiddance of mobile phones was strictly enforced.
Her forbiddance was clear: no one was to enter the laboratory after hours.

Synset Examples

they were restrained by a prohibition in their charter
a medical inhibition of alcoholic beverages
he ignored his parents' forbiddance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prohibition - the act of forbidding something by law
  • Antonym: permission - the act of allowing something

Etymology

From Old English 'forbiddan' (to forbid) plus the suffix '-ance'.

2. NOUN

/ fərˈbɪdns / (AmE), / fəˈbɪdnəns / (BrE)
an official order or act prohibiting something; the state of being forbidden
official prohibition or act of forbidding

Examples

The government issued a forbiddance on all unauthorized gatherings in the city.
Despite the forbiddance, many people continued to access the restricted site.
The priest's stern forbiddance of alcohol was respected by the entire community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prohibition - the act of forbidding something by law or other authority
  • Antonym: permission - the act of allowing something

Etymology

From Old English 'forbidden', influenced by 'forbid' + '-ance' (a noun-forming suffix).

Synset POS: n
