hijacker

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌdʒækər / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌdʒækə / (BrE)
a person who illegally seizes or takes control of a vehicle, especially by force, often to steal from it or demand something
person who seizes control of a vehicle illegally

Examples

The hijacker forced the bus to stop and demanded the passengers' valuables.
After a tense standoff, the police arrested the hijacker at the airport.
Authorities warned drivers to remain vigilant after reports of a hijacker targeting delivery trucks in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robber - someone who takes property unlawfully from another person by force or threat
  • Synonym: pirate - a person who attacks and robs ships at sea

Etymology

From 'hijack', probably from 'high' + 'jack', originally slang for highway robbery.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌdʒækər / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌdʒækə / (BrE)
a person who seizes control of a vehicle, especially an aircraft, by force or threat, often to divert it to a different destination
person seizing a vehicle by force

Examples

The hijacker demanded that the plane be flown to a different country.
Authorities negotiated for hours with the hijacker before safely rescuing the passengers.
The hijackers were apprehended after landing the aircraft at an isolated airstrip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pirate - person who attacks and robs ships at sea
  • Synonym: skyjacker - person who hijacks an aircraft

Etymology

From 'hijack', likely from 'high' and 'jack' (rob), with usage originating in early 20th-century American slang.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
