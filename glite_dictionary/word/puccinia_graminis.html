Puccinia graminis

1. NOUN

/ puˈsɪniə ˈɡræmɪnɪs / (AmE), / pʊˈsɪniə ˈɡræmɪnɪs / (BrE)
a species of rust fungus that causes stem rust in wheat and other grasses
a fungus causing stem rust in wheat

Examples

Puccinia graminis is one of the most destructive pathogens affecting wheat crops worldwide.
Researchers are developing resistant wheat varieties to prevent Puccinia graminis outbreaks.
The presence of Puccinia graminis can lead to significant losses for farmers.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Puccinia' is the genus name, and 'graminis' means 'of grass'.
