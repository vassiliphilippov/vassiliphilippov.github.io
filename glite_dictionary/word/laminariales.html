Laminariales

1. NOUN

/ læmɪˌnɛriˈeɪliz / (AmE), / læmɪˌnɑːrɪˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of large marine brown algae, commonly known as kelp, typically found in cold or polar seas
order of marine brown algae (kelp)
medical

Examples

Scientists often study Laminariales to understand marine ecosystem dynamics in cold seas.
Many commercially valuable seaweeds, such as kelp, belong to the order Laminariales.
The dense underwater forests formed by Laminariales provide habitats for numerous marine species.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Laminariaceae - family of brown algae within Laminariales

Etymology

From Latin 'Laminaria' (a genus of kelp) + '-ales' (a botanical suffix denoting an order).
