histoincompatibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪstoʊɪnkəmˌpætəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌhɪstəʊɪnkəmˌpætəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
incompatibility between the tissues of different individuals, making tissue or organ transplantation from one to another unsuccessful unless compatibility is ensured
incompatibility of tissues for transplant
medical

Examples

Histoincompatibility is a major concern in organ transplantation, often leading to rejection of the donated tissue.
Doctors test for histoincompatibility before proceeding with a bone marrow transplant.
The success of the surgery depended on the absence of histoincompatibility between the donor and recipient.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: histocompatibility - compatibility of tissues for transplantation

Etymology

From Greek histos (tissue) and incompatibility.
