plenarily

1. ADV

/ ˈplɛn.əˌrɛl.i / (AmE), / ˈplɛn.əˌrɛ.li / (BrE)
In a plenary manner; with full or complete authority, attendance, or power.
in a plenary or full manner

Examples

The committee will meet plenarily to discuss the final report next Monday.
All members voted plenarily in favor of the proposed amendment.
The contract grants the board the right to act plenarily on all executive matters.

Synset Examples

an empire destined to enter the Commonwealth plenarily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fully - completely or entirely

Etymology

From 'plenary' (from Latin 'plenarius', from 'plenus' meaning full) + '-ly'.
