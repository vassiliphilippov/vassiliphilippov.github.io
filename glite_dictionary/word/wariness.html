wariness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛr.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈweə.ri.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being cautious and watchful, especially to avoid danger or trouble
cautiousness; watchfulness

Examples

Her wariness around strangers helped her avoid many risky situations.
You could sense a certain wariness in his voice when he answered personal questions.
Despite his wariness, he eventually agreed to try something new.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caution - care taken to avoid danger or mistakes
  • Antonym: recklessness - lack of caution; acting without considering risks

Etymology

From wary + -ness; 'wary' from Old English 'wær' (cautious, aware).
