honorably

1. ADV

/ ˈɑːnərəbli / (AmE), / ˈɒnərəbli / (BrE)
In a way that shows high moral standards, fairness, or integrity.
in a manner showing honor or integrity

Examples

Despite the pressure, she conducted herself honorably throughout the investigation.
The company tried to resolve the dispute honorably rather than resorting to legal tricks.
He always chooses to act honorably, even when it is difficult.

Synset Examples

he acted honorably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honourably - in an honorable manner (British spelling)

Etymology

From the adjective "honorable" + the adverbial suffix "-ly"; "honorable" from Old French "honorable," from Latin "honorabilis" (worthy of honor).

2. ADV

/ ˈɑːnərəbli / (AmE), / ˈɒnərəbli / (BrE)
In a way that shows integrity or high moral standards; with honor.
in an honorable manner

Examples

He served his country honorably for more than two decades.
She always tries to act honorably, even in difficult situations.
After the investigation, the officer was honorably exonerated of all charges.

Synset Examples

he was honorably discharged after many years of service

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dishonorably - in a manner lacking integrity or honor
  • Synonym: honorably discharged - released from military service in a manner deemed honorable

Etymology

From 'honorable' + '-ly'; ultimately from Latin 'honorabilis'.

Source: WordNet
