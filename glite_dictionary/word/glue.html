glue

1. NOUN

/ ɡlu / (AmE), / ɡluː / (BrE)
a sticky substance used for joining things together; an adhesive
adhesive substance

Examples

She used glue to stick the broken vase pieces back together.
Children should be careful not to get glue on their hands during art projects.
This model kit requires a special type of glue to assemble the parts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adhesive - a substance used to stick objects or materials together
  • Synonym: paste - a soft, wet substance used as an adhesive
  • Synonym: cement - a substance for binding materials together

Etymology

From Old English *glēo*, probably from Latin *glus* (glue), ultimately from Greek *glia* (glue).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14727036-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ɡluː / (AmE), / ɡluː / (BrE)
to fasten or fix firmly, as if with glue; to cause to be immovably focused or attached
fix or attach as if with glue

Examples

The children were glued to the television during the cartoon show.
Her attention was glued on the speaker throughout the presentation.
I was so interested in the novel that I glued myself to the couch and finished it in one sitting.

Synset Examples

His eyes were glued on her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adhere - to stick or remain fixed to a surface
  • Antonym: to detach - to remove or separate; to become unfastened

Etymology

From Middle English glu, from Old French glu (glue), from Latin gluten (glue).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00487786-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡluː / (AmE), / ɡluː / (BrE)
to join or attach something using glue; to stick things together as with glue
join or attach with glue

Examples

He carefully glued the fragments of the vase back together.
Please glue the label to the package before shipping it.
The children spent the afternoon gluing colored paper onto their art projects.

Synset Examples

paste the sign on the wall
cut and paste the sentence in the text

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stick - to attach things so that they stay together, especially using an adhesive
  • Antonym: to detach - to separate or unfasten something that is attached

Etymology

From Middle English 'glew', from Old English 'glēo' (glue, gum), from Latin 'gluten' (glue).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01334887-v
Synset POS: v
