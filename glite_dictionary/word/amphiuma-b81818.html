Amphiuma

1. NOUN

/ æmˈfjuːmə / (AmE), / æmˈfjuːmə / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic, eel-like salamanders native to the southeastern United States, commonly known as congo snakes or amphiumas.
a genus of aquatic salamanders

Examples

The Amphiuma genus includes species with reduced limbs and elongated bodies.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of the Amphiuma in southern wetlands.
An Amphiuma can survive for long periods without food during droughts.

Etymology

From New Latin Amphiuma, origin uncertain but used in zoological taxonomies.
