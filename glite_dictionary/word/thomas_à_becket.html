Thomas à Becket

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑməs ə ˈbɛkɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɒməs ə ˈbɛkɪt / (BrE)
(c. 1118–1170) Archbishop of Canterbury from 1162 to 1170, who was murdered following his opposition to King Henry II's attempts to control the church; venerated as a saint in the Roman Catholic Church.
Archbishop of Canterbury, murdered in 1170

Examples

The story of Thomas à Becket has inspired countless plays and books.
Pilgrims visited Canterbury Cathedral to honor Thomas à Becket after his martyrdom.
Thomas à Becket became a symbol of resistance to royal interference in the Church.
