swift

1. NOUN

/ swɪft / (AmE), / swɪft / (BrE)
A common species of small western North American lizard, often found on logs or rocks.
a small western lizard

Examples

We spotted a swift basking in the sun on a fallen log.
The swift darted quickly across the rocks as we approached.
Biologists study the habits of the swift in its natural habitat.

2. NOUN

/ swɪft / (AmE), / swɪft / (BrE)
A small bird with long wings and a forked tail, known for its fast, agile flight and often resembling a swallow.
small fast-flying bird

Examples

Every summer, dozens of swifts circle above the rooftops at dusk.
The migration of the swift covers thousands of miles each year.
A swift caught insects in midair with its remarkable speed and agility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallow - a small bird with a slender body and long pointed wings, similar in appearance to a swift

Etymology

From Old English 'swift' (swift, fast), related to its quick flight.

3. ADJ

/ swɪft / (AmE), / swɪft / (BrE)
moving or able to move at great speed; happening quickly
moving very fast; rapid

Examples

The river's swift current carried the leaves downstream in seconds.
Her swift reaction prevented a serious accident.
A swift runner can complete the race in under ten minutes.

Synset Examples

fleet of foot
the fleet scurrying of squirrels
a swift current
swift flight of an arrow
a swift runner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fast - moving or capable of moving at high speed
  • Antonym: slow - moving at a low speed

Etymology

From Old English swift (fast, quick), related to Old Norse sviptir (swift) and Middle Dutch swiften (to move rapidly).
