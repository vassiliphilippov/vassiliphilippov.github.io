Protea mellifera

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtiːə mɛˈlɪfərə / (AmE), / prəʊˈtiːə mɛˈlɪfərə / (BrE)
An Australian shrub whose flowers produce a large amount of honey.
Australian shrub with honey-producing flowers

Examples

The Protea mellifera is commonly cultivated for its abundant honey production.
Many beekeepers in the region plant Protea mellifera to support their hives.
The blossoms of Protea mellifera attract large numbers of bees during flowering season.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Protea' from Greek mythological figure Proteus (due to the genus's diversity), 'mellifera' from Latin meaning 'honey-bearing'.
