topiary

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.piˌɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈtɒp.i.ə.ri / (BrE)
the art or practice of trimming shrubs or trees into ornamental shapes
art of trimming shrubs into decorative shapes

Examples

The garden featured several topiary animals shaped like elephants and lions.
She studied the ancient art of topiary during her horticulture course.
Visitors admired the meticulous topiary at the palace grounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrubbery - a collection of shrubs, sometimes pruned into shapes

Etymology

From Latin topiarius (gardener in ornamental grounds), from topia (ornamental gardening).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.piˌɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈtɒp.i.əri / (BrE)
a garden or the art of shaping trees and shrubs into ornamental shapes, especially those resembling animals or geometric forms
decorative garden with shaped shrubs

Examples

The palace grounds feature a beautiful topiary shaped like a peacock.
Visitors admired the elaborate topiary animals in the garden.
She learned the art of topiary from her grandfather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedge - a fence or boundary formed by closely growing bushes or shrubs

Etymology

From Latin topiarius (of ornamental gardening), from topia (ornamental gardening), from Greek topia (place, landscape).
