to isomerise

1. VERB

/ aɪˈsɑːmɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / aɪˈsɒməˌaɪz / (BrE)
to cause (a chemical compound) to change into an isomer or to undergo isomerization
change a chemical compound into its isomer

Examples

The catalyst can isomerise straight-chain hydrocarbons to improve fuel quality.
Researchers have developed a new method to isomerise glucose into fructose.
Under heat and pressure, certain molecules will isomerise more rapidly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to isomerize - American spelling: to cause to change into an isomer

Etymology

From 'isomer' (a compound with same formula but different arrangement) + '-ise' (verb-forming suffix).

2. VERB

/ aɪˈsɑːməˌraɪz / (AmE), / aɪˈsɒməˌraɪz / (BrE)
(of a substance) to change into an isomer or to convert a compound into one of its isomers.
change into an isomer
british

Examples

Some enzymes can isomerise glucose into fructose as part of the metabolic process.
The compound will isomerise under ultraviolet light, forming a different molecular arrangement.
Catalysts are often used to isomerise hydrocarbons during industrial refining.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to isomerize - to change into an isomer; American spelling of 'isomerise'

Etymology

From 'isomer' + '-ise', chiefly British spelling corresponding to American 'isomerize'.
