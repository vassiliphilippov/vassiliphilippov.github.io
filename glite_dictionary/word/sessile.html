sessile

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛsaɪl / (AmE), / ˈsɛsaɪl / (BrE)
(of an organism or part) attached directly by its base, without a stalk or peduncle.
attached directly by the base, without a stalk

Examples

Many barnacles are sessile and remain fixed to rocks throughout their lives.
These flowers are sessile because they lack stems holding them away from the plant.
The leaves are sessile, attaching directly to the branch without a petiole.

Synset Examples

sessile flowers
the shell of a sessile barnacle is attached directly to a substrate

Etymology

From Latin sessilis (sitting, resting), from sedere (to sit).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛs.aɪl / (AmE), / ˈsɛs.aɪl / (BrE)
(of an organism) permanently attached to a surface and unable to move about freely.
permanently attached; immobile (biology)
medical

Examples

Barnacles are sessile creatures that attach themselves permanently to rocks or ship hulls.
Unlike fish, corals are sessile and remain fixed in one spot throughout their lives.
Some plants have sessile leaves that are attached directly to the stem without a stalk.

Synset Examples

sessile marine animals and plants

Related Terms

  • Antonym: motile - capable of movement

Etymology

From Latin sessilis (sitting, resting), from sedere (to sit).
