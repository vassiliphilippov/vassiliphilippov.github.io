plebiscite

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛb.əˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈpliː.bɪ.saɪt / (BrE)
A direct vote by the electorate on an important public question such as a change to the constitution or independence.
direct vote on an important public issue

Examples

The government announced a plebiscite to decide on the new constitution.
After heated debates, the result of the plebiscite was accepted by all political parties.
A national plebiscite will be held to determine support for independence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: referendum - a general vote by the electorate on a single political question

Etymology

From French plébiscite, from Latin plebiscitum (a decree of the people).
