cubist

1. ADJ

/ ˈkjuːbɪst / (AmE), / ˈkjuːbɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Cubism, an early 20th-century art movement that emphasized abstract, geometric forms.
relating to Cubism (art style)

Examples

Her paintings feature a distinct cubist style, with fragmented shapes and bold lines.
The exhibit showcases several cubist works by Picasso and Braque.
He adopted a cubist approach to architecture, emphasizing geometric simplicity.

Synset Examples

cubist art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cubism - an art movement focusing on geometric forms and fragmentations

Etymology

From 'Cubism', named for the use of cube-like forms in the movement.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːbɪst / (AmE), / ˈkjuːbɪst / (BrE)
an artist who works according to the principles of cubism, an early 20th-century art movement characterized by abstracted and geometric forms
artist belonging to cubism movement

Examples

Pablo Picasso is often regarded as a leading cubist of the twentieth century.
Many art collectors are eager to acquire works by a renowned cubist.
The exhibition features several masterpieces by the famous French cubist, Georges Braque.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cubism - an early 20th-century art movement characterized by use of geometric shapes and abstraction

Etymology

From French 'cubisme', referring to the art movement; 'cubist' formed in English to denote its adherents.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10001543-n
Synset POS: n
