finnan

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪn.ən / (AmE), / ˈfɪn.ən / (BrE)
A smoked haddock, usually baked or broiled with plenty of butter, especially as part of a traditional British dish.
a smoked haddock dish
british

Examples

For breakfast, she ordered the finnan with a side of poached eggs.
Traditional Scottish restaurants often feature finnan on their menus.
He prepared the finnan by broiling it with butter and herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finnan haddie - another name for the dish made from smoked haddock

Etymology

Shortened from 'Finnan haddock', named after Finnan (Findon), a place near Aberdeen, Scotland, where the preparation originated.
