acinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈæsɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈæsɪnəs / (BrE)
a small sac or saclike cavity in a compound gland, where secretions are produced
small sac in a compound gland
medical

Examples

Each acinus in the pancreas produces digestive enzymes that are released into the ducts.
The gland's acini can be seen clearly under the microscope due to their rounded structure.
Damage to the salivary acini can affect saliva production.

Etymology

From Latin acinus (berry, grape), referring to the sac's resemblance to a berry.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæsɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈasɪnəs / (BrE)
one of the small drupes or drupelets that collectively form an aggregate or multiple fruit, such as a blackberry or raspberry
small drupe of aggregate fruit

Examples

Each acinus in a raspberry contains its own tiny seed.
Under a microscope, the acinus of a blackberry can be seen as a separate unit.
The fruit is made up of many acini clustered together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drupe - a fleshy fruit with a single stone or pit, like a cherry or peach

Etymology

From Latin *acinus* (grape, berry).
