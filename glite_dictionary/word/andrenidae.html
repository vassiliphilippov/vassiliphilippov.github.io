Andrenidae

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdrɛnəˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ænˈdrɛnɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of solitary, short-tongued bees, most of which burrow in the ground
family of solitary, ground-burrowing bees

Examples

Most members of the Andrenidae family prefer to nest in sandy soil where they dig deep burrows.
Unlike honeybees, Andrenidae are solitary and do not form colonies.
Many wildflowers are pollinated by species belonging to the Andrenidae family.

Etymology

From New Latin Andrenidae, based on the type genus Andrena.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈdrɛnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ænˈdriːnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a large family of solitary, short-tongued bees, most of which burrow in the ground
family of ground-nesting solitary bees

Examples

The Andrenidae are important pollinators in many ecosystems.
Unlike honeybees, Andrenidae typically nest underground and lead solitary lives.
Researchers discovered several new species within the Andrenidae in the woodland reserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee - an insect known for its role in pollination and producing honey

Etymology

From the genus Andrena (type genus) + -idae, a standard suffix for animal family names in zoological taxonomy.
