stonefly

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊnˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstəʊnˌflaɪ / (BrE)
a primitive winged insect with a flattened body, often used as bait by fishermen; its aquatic larvae live under stones and are carnivorous
a primitive aquatic insect; fishing bait

Examples

Fishermen often use stonefly larvae as bait during trout season.
The stonefly has a flattened body well adapted for life under rocks in streams.
Many species of stonefly are indicators of clean, well-oxygenated water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayfly - an aquatic insect with a short adult life stage
