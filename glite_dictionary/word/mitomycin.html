mitomycin

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪtəˈmaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌmɪtəˈmaɪsɪn / (BrE)
a group of antibiotic compounds produced by a Streptomyces bacterium, with some forms used as anticancer drugs
antibiotic compounds, some used as anticancer drugs
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended treatment with mitomycin after evaluating several chemotherapy options.
Laboratory studies have shown mitomycin to be effective against certain types of cancer cells.
Researchers are studying how mitomycin interacts with other anticancer drugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - a substance that destroys or inhibits the growth of microorganisms
  • Synonym: Mutamycin - trade name for a form of mitomycin used as an anticancer drug

Etymology

From 'mito-' (related to mitosis) and 'mycin' (from Greek 'mykes', fungus), reflecting its antibiotic origin.
