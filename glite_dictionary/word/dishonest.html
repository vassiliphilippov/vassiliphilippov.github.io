dishonest

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈʃɑːnəst / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnɪst / (BrE)
not truthful or trustworthy; tending to deceive, cheat, or defraud
not truthful or trustworthy

Examples

It's wrong to be dishonest with your friends about something important.
Several dishonest employees were caught stealing from the company.
The politician denied any dishonest behavior during the campaign.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honest - truthful and trustworthy
  • Synonym: deceitful - dishonest; misleading or false

Etymology

From Latin 'dishonestus', meaning 'disgraceful, unseemly'.

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɑnəst / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnɪst / (BrE)
easily influenced to act immorally or corruptly, especially for personal gain.
susceptible to corruption or bribery

Examples

Voters were disappointed to learn their mayor was dishonest and accepted bribes.
The committee avoided appointing anyone with a dishonest reputation to positions of power.
She warned her son not to trust dishonest officials who might exploit him.

Synset Examples

corruptible judges
dishonest politicians
a purchasable senator
a venal police officer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corruptible - easily bribed or led to act immorally
  • Antonym: incorruptible - not susceptible to bribery or corruption
