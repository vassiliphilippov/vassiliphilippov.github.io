ingenuousness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒɛnjuəsnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛnjuːəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being open, honest, and straightforward; frankness or innocence.
openness or frankness

Examples

Her ingenuousness made her a trusted friend to everyone she met.
He answered the difficult questions with a remarkable ingenuousness that impressed the interviewers.
Some people mistake ingenuousness for naivety, when it is actually a sign of honesty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frankness - the quality of being open and honest; straightforwardness
  • Synonym: candor - the quality of being open and honest in expression
  • Antonym: guile - sly or cunning intelligence

Etymology

From Latin ingenuus (natural, frank) + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒɛnjuəsnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛnjuːəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being innocent and unsuspecting, showing openness or frankness without deceit
innocent openness or frankness

Examples

Children often display a charming ingenuousness when meeting new people.
His ingenuousness made others feel at ease, as he always spoke sincerely.
Some took advantage of her ingenuousness, mistaking her honesty for foolishness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naivete - innocence or simplicity of nature, lack of experience
  • Antonym: guile - sly or cunning intelligence, deceitful behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'ingenuus' (frank, candid) + '-ness' (state or quality).
