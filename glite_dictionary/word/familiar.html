familiar

1. ADJ

/ fəˈmɪljər / (AmE), / fəˈmɪliə / (BrE)
well known or easily recognized because of previous encounters or experience
well known or easily recognized

Examples

The city skyline was a familiar sight to anyone who grew up there.
She hummed a familiar tune as she worked in the kitchen.
Many familiar faces appeared at the annual reunion.

Synset Examples

a familiar figure
familiar songs
familiar guests

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfamiliar - not known or recognized; not familiar
  • Synonym: well-known - known by many people or easily recognized

Etymology

From Latin 'familiaris' (of a household, domestic), from 'familia' (family).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00968795-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ fəˈmɪljər / (AmE), / fəˈmɪliə / (BrE)
a person who serves in the household of a high official, such as a pope or bishop, in return for support
household servant of a high official
archaic

Examples

In medieval times, a familiar would often manage many of the bishop's household affairs.
The familiar lived at the cardinal's residence and served him loyally for years.
He was appointed familiar to the archbishop shortly after his ordination.

Etymology

From Latin 'familiaris' (of a household, domestic servant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10096886-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ fəˈmɪljɚ / (AmE), / fəˈmɪliə / (BrE)
(of people or relationships) having a close and friendly relationship; well-acquainted or on intimate terms.
on close or friendly terms

Examples

They have grown quite familiar with each other after years of working together.
She spoke to her neighbors in a noticeably familiar manner, as if they were old friends.
You shouldn't be too familiar with your boss until you know him better.

Synset Examples

on familiar terms
pretending she is on an intimate footing with those she slanders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friendly - kind and pleasant; behaving as a friend
  • Synonym: intimate - closely acquainted; familiar
  • Antonym: distant - not close or friendly

Etymology

From Latin familiaris (domestic, of a household, a close friend or servant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00455086-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ fəˈmɪljɚ / (AmE), / fəˈmɪliə / (BrE)
well informed about or thoroughly acquainted with someone or something, especially through experience or close association (usually followed by 'with').
well informed about; acquainted with

Examples

She is familiar with the latest scientific research in her field.
Most tourists are not familiar with the local customs and traditions.
After working here for years, he became very familiar with every shortcut in the building.

Synset Examples

conversant with business trends
familiar with the complex machinery
he was familiar with those roads

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness in general or about something specific
  • Synonym: acquainted - having personal knowledge or experience of something

Etymology

From Old French *familier*, from Latin *familiaris* (of a household, intimate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01310022-s
Synset POS: s

5. ADJ

/ fəˈmɪljɚ / (AmE), / fəˈmɪliə / (BrE)
well known from everyday experience; common or ordinary and not strange
well known; common or ordinary

Examples

The bustling scene in the kitchen was a familiar sight to everyone in the family.
She gave me a familiar excuse for being late again this morning.
Walking the familiar path through the park always made him feel at home.

Synset Examples

familiar ordinary objects found in every home
a familiar everyday scene
a familiar excuse
a day like any other filled with familiar duties and experiences

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfamiliar - not known or recognized; strange or unusual
  • Synonym: common - occurring, found, or done often; prevalent

Etymology

From Latin familiaris (domestic, belonging to a family), from familia (family).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00973438-a

6. NOUN

/ fəˈmɪljər / (AmE), / fəˈmɪlɪə / (BrE)
(in folklore) a spirit, often taking the form of an animal, that serves as an assistant to a witch or wizard.
a spirit companion, often animal-shaped, aiding a witch or wizard

Examples

The witch's familiar appeared in the form of a black cat.
Legends often mention a familiar aiding witches in their magical tasks.
Her familiar was rumored to be an owl that never left her side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imp - a small mischievous devil or sprite, often used as a synonym in folklore

Etymology

From Old French 'familier' (intimate, friendly), from Latin 'familiaris' (of a household, domestic servant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09568300-n
Synset POS: n
