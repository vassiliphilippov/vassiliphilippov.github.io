cross-question

1. NOUN

/ krɔs ˈkwɛstʃən / (AmE), / krɒs ˈkwɛstʃən / (BrE)
a question asked to a witness during cross-examination, typically to test the accuracy or credibility of their previous statements
question asked in cross-examination

Examples

The lawyer asked a sharp cross-question to challenge the witness's testimony.
She struggled to answer the cross-question posed by the defense attorney.
Every cross-question was designed to highlight inconsistencies in his story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross-examination - the questioning of a witness by the opposing side in a court case
  • Degree Lower: question - a sentence worded to elicit information

2. VERB

/ krɔs ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (AmE), / krɒs ˈkwes.tʃən / (BrE)
to question a witness closely, especially after they have already been questioned by the other side in a legal proceeding; to cross-examine
to closely question, esp. in court
legal

Examples

The lawyer began to cross-question the witness after the prosecution finished their examination.
During the trial, each side had an opportunity to cross-question the other's witnesses.
She felt anxious when the defense counsel started to cross-question her on the stand.

Synset Examples

The witness was cross-examined by the defense

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cross-examine - to question a witness closely after direct examination, especially in court

Etymology

Formed from 'cross' (from legal term 'cross-examine') + 'question'.
