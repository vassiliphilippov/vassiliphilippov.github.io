Morchella esculenta

1. NOUN

/ mɔrˈkɛl.ə ɛsˌkjuːˈlɛn.tə / (AmE), / mɔːˈkɛl.ə ɛs.kjʊˈlɛn.tə / (BrE)
A species of edible morel mushroom, recognized by its globular to elongated cap with an irregular pattern of pits and ridges; commonly known as the common morel.
a species of edible morel mushroom

Examples

Spring is the best season to hunt for Morchella esculenta in the forests near our town.
Chefs prize Morchella esculenta for its rich, earthy taste and unique texture in gourmet dishes.
Scientists are researching how to cultivate Morchella esculenta commercially due to its culinary popularity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morel - an edible wild mushroom with a honeycomb-like cap

Etymology

From Latin: *Morchella* (genus of morel mushrooms), *esculenta* meaning edible.
