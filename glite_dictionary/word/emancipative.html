emancipative

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmænsəˌpeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈmansɪˌpeɪtɪv / (BrE)
tending or having the power to set free from restraint, control, or the power of another
tending to set free

Examples

The new law had an emancipative effect on previously restricted groups.
Her writing took on an emancipative role in the struggle for equal rights.
Many viewed the policy changes as emancipative for women in the workforce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emancipatory - seeking or intended to liberate people from legal, social, or political restrictions
  • Antonym: oppressive - exercising unjust or cruel power

Etymology

From Latin emancipare (to set free) via 'emancipate' + -ive.
