ecumenical

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛk.juˈmɛn.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌiː.kjuːˈmɛn.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
having worldwide scope or influence; universal.
universal; worldwide in scope

Examples

The organization held an ecumenical conference that brought together leaders from all over the world.
Her views on environmental responsibility were decidedly ecumenical and embraced by people of many nations.
The charity has an ecumenical mission, reaching out to communities across the globe.

Synset Examples

an issue of cosmopolitan import
the shrewdest political and ecumenical comment of our time
universal experience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: universal - applicable everywhere or in all cases; general

Etymology

From Greek oikoumenikos, from oikoumene (the inhabited world).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛk.jəˈmɛn.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌiː.kjuːˈmɛn.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to or promoting unity among the world's Christian churches or, more broadly, among religions.
promoting unity among churches or religions

Examples

Leaders from different denominations gathered for an ecumenical service celebrating Christian unity.
The ecumenical movement seeks to foster dialogue and cooperation between churches.
Her approach to theology is highly ecumenical, embracing diverse traditions.

Synset Examples

ecumenical thinking
ecumenical activities
the ecumenical movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interfaith - involving or representing different religions or faiths

Etymology

From Late Latin 'oecumenicus', from Greek 'oikoumenikos' (from 'oikoumene' meaning 'the inhabited world').
