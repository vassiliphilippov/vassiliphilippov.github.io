symphysis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪm.fə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.fɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
the union or growing together of two bones or parts of the body, typically by cartilage
growing together of body parts, especially bones
medical

Examples

The pubic symphysis is a joint where the left and right pelvic bones meet at the front of the pelvis.
In older adults, the symphysis may become less flexible due to ossification of cartilage.
The surgeon inspected the mandibular symphysis for any signs of abnormal growth.

Etymology

From Greek 'symphysis' (growing together), from 'syn-' (together) + 'phyein' (to grow).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmfəsɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪmfɪsɪs / (BrE)
an abnormal or natural fusion of two anatomical structures, often referring to a type of joint where bones are joined by fibrocartilage
a joint or fusion between bones or structures
medical

Examples

The symphysis of the mandible is the point where the two halves of the jawbone meet in the midline.
In rare cases, an abnormal symphysis can cause restricted movement and discomfort.
The radiograph revealed a clear symphysis between the affected bones.

Etymology

From Greek 'symphysis' (growing together), from 'syn-' (together) + 'physis' (growth, nature).
