face-off

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪs ˌɑf / (AmE), / ˈfeɪs ˌɒf / (BrE)
(in ice hockey and similar sports) the method of starting play, in which a referee drops the puck between two opposing players.
ice hockey method of starting play

Examples

The game began with a face-off at center ice.
After every goal, there is another face-off to restart the game.
She won the face-off and quickly passed the puck to her teammate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully-off - method of starting play in field hockey

2. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪsˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈfeɪsɒf / (BrE)
a direct confrontation or conflict between two opposing parties, often involving a tense or competitive atmosphere
direct confrontation or contest

Examples

The two leaders had a tense face-off during the meeting.
Yesterday’s game ended with an exciting face-off between the rival teams.
The debate turned into a heated face-off over policy differences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confrontation - a hostile or argumentative meeting or situation between opposing parties
  • Synonym: showdown - a decisive confrontation or contest
