sudatory

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːdəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈsjuːdəˌtɔːri / (BrE)
A medicine or agent that causes or increases sweating.
medicine that induces sweating
medical

Examples

The physician prescribed a sudatory to help lower the patient's fever.
Certain herbal teas have a mild sudatory effect and are used in traditional medicine.
In the past, doctors relied on sudatory remedies to treat various illnesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diaphoretic - a drug that promotes sweating

Etymology

From Latin 'sudatorium' (a sweating place, sweat bath), from 'sudare' (to sweat).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuːdəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈsjuːdəˌtɔːri / (BrE)
a bathhouse or room designed for taking hot air or steam baths; a sweat bath
hot air or steam bathhouse
archaic

Examples

The ancient Romans built elaborate sudatory complexes as part of their public bathhouses.
He opened the door to the sudatory and was immediately enveloped in warm, fragrant steam.
During the spa retreat, guests spent time in a sudatory to relax and cleanse their skin.

Etymology

From Latin 'sūdātorium', from 'sūdāre' (to sweat).
