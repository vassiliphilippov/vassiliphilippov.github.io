Lagarostrobus

1. NOUN

/ ləˌɡæroʊˈstroʊbəs / (AmE), / ləˌɡærəˈstrəʊbəs / (BrE)
A genus of dioecious evergreen trees found in New Zealand and Tasmania, closely related to the genus Dacrydium.
genus of evergreen trees (New Zealand, Tasmania)

Examples

The genus Lagarostrobus is native to the temperate rainforests of Tasmania and New Zealand.
Scientists recently studied the reproductive biology of Lagarostrobus species.
Unlike some related genera, Lagarostrobus trees have slender cones and are dioecious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dacrydium - genus of southern hemisphere evergreen trees

Etymology

From Greek 'lagaros' (slender) + 'strobos' (cone), referring to the appearance of the cones.

2. NOUN

/ ləˌgæroʊˈstroʊbəs / (AmE), / ləˌɡarəʊˈstrəʊbəs / (BrE)
A genus of dioecious evergreen trees native to New Zealand and Tasmania, closely related to genus Dacrydium.
genus of evergreen trees (New Zealand, Tasmania)

Examples

Lagarostrobus is notable for its slender, evergreen appearance in the forests of Tasmania.
Botanists studied the reproductive cycle of Lagarostrobus to understand its adaptation to wet climates.
Many New Zealand wetlands are home to the distinctive trees of the Lagarostrobus genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'lagaros' (slender) and 'strobus' (pine cone).
