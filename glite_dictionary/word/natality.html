natality

1. NOUN

/ nəˈtæləti / (AmE), / nəˈtalɪti / (BrE)
the ratio of live births in a particular area to the population of that area, typically expressed per 1,000 people per year
birth rate in a population

Examples

The country's declining natality has raised concerns about its future workforce.
Public health policies can significantly influence natality over time.
Statisticians noted a slight increase in natality following the economic recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birth rate - the number of live births per 1,000 population per year
  • Antonym: mortality - the number of deaths in a population in a given period

Etymology

From Latin natalitas (birth), from natus (born).
