civilian

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈvɪliən / (AmE), / sɪˈvɪliən / (BrE)
a person who is not a member of the armed forces or the police
nonmilitary person

Examples

After retiring from the army, he adjusted to life as a civilian.
The government promised to protect all civilians during the conflict.
Emergency assistance was delivered to the civilian population in the disaster area.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soldier - a person serving in an army

Etymology

From Old French *civilien* (civil), from Latin *civilis* (relating to citizens).

2. ADJ

/ sɪˈvɪljən / (AmE), / sɪˈvɪljən / (BrE)
relating to civil life or to people who are not members of the armed forces or the police.
not military or police; civil

Examples

The city is under civilian control after many years of military rule.
Only civilian vehicles are allowed beyond this checkpoint.
He decided to return to civilian life after serving in the navy.

Synset Examples

civilian clothing
civilian life

Related Terms

  • Antonym: military - relating to or characteristic of soldiers or armed forces

Etymology

From Old French civilien, from Latin civilis (of a citizen).
