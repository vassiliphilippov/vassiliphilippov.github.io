to bedraggle

1. VERB

/ bɪˈdræɡəl / (AmE), / bɪˈdræɡl / (BrE)
to make (someone or something) wet and dirty, typically by trailing through mud or water
make wet and dirty

Examples

The children returned home completely bedraggled after playing in the rain.
She didn't want to bedraggle her dress by walking through the muddy field.
His hair was bedraggled after the sudden downpour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soak - to make something very wet
  • Antonym: clean - to remove dirt or make free from dirt

Etymology

From the prefix 'be-' meaning 'thoroughly' + 'draggle' (to make or become wet and dirty).
