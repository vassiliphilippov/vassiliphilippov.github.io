sadly

1. ADV

/ ˈsædli / (AmE), / ˈsædli / (BrE)
In a sad or sorrowful manner; with sadness.
in a sad manner

Examples

He shook his head sadly and turned away from the window.
She spoke sadly about the loss of her old friend.
The team members sadly accepted their defeat after the final match.

Synset Examples

‘She died last night,’ he said sadly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a happy or cheerful manner

Etymology

Formed from "sad" + "-ly," following the standard English adverbial suffix pattern.

2. ADV

/ ˈsædli / (AmE), / ˈsadli / (BrE)
In an unfortunate or regrettable way.
in an unfortunate way

Examples

Sadly, we missed the last train and had to find a hotel for the night.
Many rare species have, sadly, become extinct in recent years.
Sadly, she never got the chance to pursue her dream of becoming an artist.

Synset Examples

sadly he died before he could see his grandchild

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfortunately - in an unfortunate manner; by bad luck
  • Antonym: luckily - in a fortunate manner; by good luck
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00043024-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈsædli / (AmE), / ˈsadli / (BrE)
In a way that is unfortunate or regrettable; in a manner causing disappointment or concern.
in an unfortunate or regrettable manner

Examples

The playground was sadly neglected by the local council.
That building is sadly in need of repair.
The event was sadly underfunded, leading to many cancellations.

Synset Examples

he was sadly neglected
it was woefully inadequate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woefully - in an extremely bad or serious way
  • Antonym: fortunately - in a lucky or fortunate manner

Etymology

From 'sad' + '-ly', Middle English 'sadli', meaning 'seriously, gravely'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00093820-r
Synset POS: r
