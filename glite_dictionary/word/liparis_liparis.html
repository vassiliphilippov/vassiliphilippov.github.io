Liparis liparis

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈpɛərɪs lɪˈpɛərɪs / (AmE), / lɪˈpɑːrɪs lɪˈpɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A small tadpole-shaped cold-water fish, in which the pelvic fins form a sucker; also called the common seasnail.
a small cold-water fish (common seasnail)

Examples

The Liparis liparis is often found in the cold waters of the North Atlantic.
Biologists studied the reproductive behavior of Liparis liparis in its natural habitat.
The unique sucker formed by the pelvic fins helps the Liparis liparis cling to rocks.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Liparis', from Greek 'liparos' (fat, oily).
