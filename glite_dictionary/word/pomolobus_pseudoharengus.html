Pomolobus pseudoharengus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊmoʊˈloʊbəs ˌsuːdoʊhəˈrɛŋɡəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒmələʊˈbʌs ˌsjuːdəʊhəˈrɛŋɡəs / (BrE)
A species of shad-like fish, commonly known as the alewife, that migrates up rivers to spawn, and is often salted or smoked for food.
species of shad-like fish (alewife)

Examples

The Pomolobus pseudoharengus migrates each spring from the ocean into freshwater rivers to spawn.
Smoked Pomolobus pseudoharengus is a traditional delicacy in some coastal communities.
Researchers are studying the population decline of Pomolobus pseudoharengus in the Great Lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alewife - common name for Pomolobus pseudoharengus, a shad-like food fish

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: Pomolobus (genus) + pseudoharengus (meaning 'false herring', from Greek pseudes 'false' + Latin harengus 'herring').
