precancerous

1. ADJ

/ priˈkænsəɹəs / (AmE), / priˈkænsərəs / (BrE)
(of a growth or cell) not malignant, but likely to develop into cancer if untreated.
likely to develop into cancer
medical

Examples

The doctor found a precancerous lesion during my routine screening.
Removing precancerous polyps can prevent the development of colon cancer.
She was relieved to hear that the cells were only precancerous, not malignant.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: benign - (of a tumor) not harmful in effect; not malignant
  • Degree Higher: malignant - (of a tumor) very virulent or infectious, cancerous

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'pre-' (before) + 'cancerous' (relating to cancer).
