Normandie

1. PROPN

/ nɔrˈmɑndi / (AmE), / nɔːˈmɑːndi / (BrE)
a former province in northwestern France on the English Channel, now divided into Haute-Normandie and Basse-Normandie
former province in northwestern France

Examples

During their vacation, the couple explored the picturesque coastline of Normandie.
Many important events in World War II took place in Normandie.
The cheeses produced in Normandie are famous throughout France and beyond.

Etymology

French Normandie, from Old French, ultimately from Latin Normannia (land of the Northmen, i.e., Norse Vikings).
