Sempach

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛmˌpɑk / (AmE), / ˈsɛmpak / (BrE)
A town in Switzerland, known chiefly as the site of a pivotal battle in 1386 where the Swiss Confederation defeated Hapsburg forces, leading to Swiss independence.
Swiss town; site of 1386 Swiss victory

Examples

The Battle of Sempach marked a turning point in Swiss history.
Many Swiss citizens celebrate the anniversary of Sempach each year.
The victory at Sempach is remembered as a symbol of Swiss independence.

Etymology

From the place name Sempach in the canton of Lucerne, Switzerland.
