hypothyroidism

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌpoʊˈθaɪrɔɪdɪzəm / (AmE), / hʌɪˌpəʊˈθʌɪərɔɪdɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the thyroid gland is underactive and produces insufficient thyroid hormones, leading to a slowing of bodily functions.
underactive thyroid gland disorder
medical

Examples

The most common cause of hypothyroidism worldwide is a lack of iodine in the diet.
People with hypothyroidism may experience fatigue, weight gain, and cold intolerance.
Doctors typically diagnose hypothyroidism with a blood test measuring thyroid hormone levels.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hyperthyroidism - a condition caused by overproduction of thyroid hormones

Etymology

From hypo- (under, below) + thyroid + -ism (condition or disease).
