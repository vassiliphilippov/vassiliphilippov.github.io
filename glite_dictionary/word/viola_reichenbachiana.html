Viola reichenbachiana

1. NOUN

/ viˈoʊlə ˌraɪkənˈbɑːkiənə / (AmE), / viˈəʊlə ˌraɪkənˈbækɪənə / (BrE)
A common European violet species that grows in woods and hedgerows, known for its heart-shaped leaves and purple flowers.
common European violet species

Examples

In the spring, the Viola reichenbachiana carpets the woodland floor with its delicate purple flowers.
Gardeners often confuse Viola reichenbachiana with other native violets due to their similar appearance.
The Viola reichenbachiana thrives in shady hedgerows and is valued for its resilience and beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet violet - another European violet species, Viola odorata

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Viola' for violets; species named after Ludwig Reichenbach, German botanist.
