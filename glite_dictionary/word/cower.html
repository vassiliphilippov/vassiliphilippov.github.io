to cower

1. VERB

/ ˈkaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ.ə / (BrE)
to crouch or shrink back in fear or submission
to crouch or shrink back in fear

Examples

The dog began to cower in the corner when the thunder roared.
She would cower behind her desk whenever her boss yelled.
The villagers cowered in fear as the storm approached.

Synset Examples

They huddled outside in the rain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cringe - to shrink back in fear or embarrassment
  • Antonym: stand tall - to stand upright and confidently

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *couren* (to crouch), related to Old Norse *kuga* (to oppress).

2. VERB

/ ˈkaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ.ə / (BrE)
to crouch down or move backward in fear or submission
crouch down in fear or submission

Examples

The dog would cower in the corner whenever there was a loud thunderstorm.
She tried to stand her ground instead of cowering before her angry boss.
The small animal cowered at the sight of the approaching predator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cringe - to react with discomfort or fear, often physically drawing back
  • Synonym: fear - an emotion caused by the belief that someone or something is dangerous
  • Antonym: stand tall - to act or appear courageous, not yielding to fear

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *couren* (to crouch) of Scandinavian origin, related to Norwegian *kauera* (to crouch, cower).

Synset POS: v
