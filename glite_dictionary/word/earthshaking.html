earthshaking

1. ADJ

/ ˈɜrθˌʃeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌʃeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a sound or event) so powerful or loud that it seems to shake the earth.
very powerful or loud (like an earthquake)

Examples

The explosion was so earthshaking that windows shattered blocks away.
An earthshaking roar erupted from the stadium after the final goal.
The movie's earthshaking soundtrack rumbled through the theater seats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thunderous - extremely loud, like thunder

Etymology

Formed from 'earth' + 'shaking', modelling the impact of an earthquake.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɜrθˌʃeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌʃeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely significant or important; having a very great impact or influence.
extremely significant or impactful

Examples

The scientist's discovery was considered an earthshaking event in the field of physics.
Her resignation was an earthshaking moment for the entire organization.
No earthshaking changes are expected in this year's budget.

Synset Examples

earthshaking proposals
the contest was no world-shaking affair
the conversation … could hardly be called world-shattering

Related Terms

  • Synonym: world-shaking - having an effect significant enough to affect the entire world
  • Synonym: momentous - of great importance or significance, especially in having a bearing on future events
  • Antonym: insignificant - too small or unimportant to be worth consideration

Etymology

From 'earth' + 'shaking', evoking the image of an earthquake's force.
