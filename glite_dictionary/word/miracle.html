miracle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪrəkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪrəkəl / (BrE)
An extraordinary and welcome event that is not explicable by natural or scientific laws and is therefore attributed to divine agency.
extraordinary, supernatural event

Examples

Some people considered his recovery a medical miracle.
Winning the lottery twice is often described as a miracle.
They prayed for a miracle when the storm hit their village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wonder - something that causes amazement or admiration
  • Synonym: phenomenon - an observable event, especially one that is impressive or extraordinary

Etymology

From Latin miraculum (object of wonder), from mirari (to wonder).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪrəkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪrəkəl / (BrE)
an extraordinary and welcome event that is not explicable by natural or scientific laws and is therefore attributed to a divine agency; an amazing or wonderful occurrence
amazing or wonderful occurrence

Examples

Winning the lottery was nothing short of a miracle for the struggling family.
Some people consider the birth of a healthy baby a miracle.
It will take a miracle for the team to win the championship this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wonder - a feeling of surprise and admiration, caused by something beautiful, unexpected, or inexplicable
  • Synonym: phenomenon - a remarkable person or event

Etymology

From Latin miraculum (object of wonder), from mirari (to wonder).
