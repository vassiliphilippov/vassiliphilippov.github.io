siren

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪrən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪərən / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a sea nymph with the head and upper body of a woman and the lower body of a bird, who lured sailors to shipwreck with her enchanting music and singing.
mythical creature luring sailors

Examples

In Homer’s Odyssey, Odysseus bravely resisted the call of the siren by having himself tied to the mast.
The tale warned sailors of the siren’s seductive song echoing over the waves.
Ancient myths often described sirens as beautiful yet deadly, tempting men toward dangerous rocks.

Synset Examples

Odysseus ordered his crew to plug their ears so they would not hear the Siren's fatal song

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mermaid - a mythical sea creature with the head and upper body of a woman and the tail of a fish

Etymology

From Latin 'Siren', from Greek 'Seirēn', the name of one of these creatures in mythology.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪrən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪərən / (BrE)
An aquatic North American salamander with an eel-like body, small forelimbs, no hind limbs, and permanent external gills.
an eel-like aquatic North American salamander

Examples

The biologist carefully transferred the siren to a larger tank to study its swimming behavior.
Sighting a siren in the wild is rare because they tend to hide in thick vegetation.
Unlike most salamanders, the siren retains its external gills throughout its life.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪrən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪərən / (BrE)
a device that makes a loud wailing sound as a warning or signal, typically to alert people to danger
device that emits a loud warning sound

Examples

The fire truck's siren could be heard from several blocks away.
As soon as the tornado siren sounded, everyone rushed to the shelter.
A loud siren warned the neighborhood of the approaching storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alarm - a device that warns of danger or alerts people by making a loud sound

Etymology

From Greek Seirēn, a mythical creature whose singing lured sailors to destruction. Later applied to warning devices due to their attention-grabbing sound.
