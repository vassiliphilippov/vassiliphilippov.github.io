Siren

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪrən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪərən / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic salamanders in the family Sirenidae, characterized by elongated bodies, external gills, and the absence of hind limbs.
genus of aquatic salamanders

Examples

The Siren is known for its long, eel-like body and lack of hind legs.
Scientists observed a population of Siren in the freshwater marsh.
The Siren genus is unique among salamanders for retaining gills throughout life.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Sirenidae - family of aquatic salamanders that includes genus Siren
  • Degree Higher: salamander - amphibian with a lizard-like appearance

Etymology

From Latin 'Sīrēn', borrowed from Greek 'Seirēn', also giving rise to the mythical siren.
