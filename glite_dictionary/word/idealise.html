to idealise

1. VERB

/ aɪˈdiəlaɪz / (AmE), / aɪˈdɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to regard or represent something or someone as perfect or better than in reality, often ignoring flaws or limitations
see or present as perfect
british

Examples

It is common for young people to idealise celebrities and overlook their flaws.
After moving away, she tended to idealise her hometown and remember only the good times.
The director tried not to idealise the historical figures, portraying them with all their strengths and weaknesses.

Synset Examples

She idealized her husband after his death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to idealize - to view or present something as perfect; American spelling
  • Synonym: to romanticise - to deal with or describe in an idealized or unrealistic fashion
  • Antonym: to realise - to become fully aware of something as it really is; opposite sense

Etymology

From Late Latin idealizare, from Greek idea (form, pattern).

2. VERB

/ aɪˈdiəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / aɪˈdɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to regard or represent as perfect or better than in reality; to form an ideal version of something or someone
regard or represent as perfect

Examples

Many people tend to idealise their childhood memories, forgetting the difficult moments.
Her biography clearly idealises the achievements of its subject.
It can be dangerous to idealise someone you have just met.

Synset Examples

Man has always idealized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to romanticize - to describe or view something in an idealized or romantic way
  • Antonym: to realise - to see or represent things as they actually are

Etymology

From French 'idéaliser', from 'idéal' (ideal).
