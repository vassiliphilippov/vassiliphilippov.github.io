Habakkuk

1. PROPN

/ həˈbækək / (AmE), / həˈbækək / (BrE)
A Hebrew prophet in the Old Testament, traditionally regarded as the author of the Book of Habakkuk, which includes prophecies about the Chaldeans.
Hebrew prophet; book of the Old Testament

Examples

Habakkuk is considered one of the twelve minor prophets in the Old Testament.
Many scholars study the Book of Habakkuk to understand ancient prophecies about the Chaldeans.
At the seminar, the lecturer discussed the relevance of Habakkuk's message for modern times.

Etymology

From Hebrew Ḥăbhaqquq, the prophet's name as used in the Old Testament.

2. PROPN

/ həˈbækək / (AmE), / həˈbækək / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament containing the prophecies attributed to the prophet Habakkuk.
Old Testament prophetic book

Examples

Many scholars have analyzed the poetic language found in Habakkuk.
She cited verses from Habakkuk during her presentation on biblical prophets.
The class discussed the themes of justice in the Book of Habakkuk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Book of Zephaniah - An Old Testament book containing Zephaniah's prophecies
  • Synonym: Habakkuk - A Hebrew prophet of the Old Testament

Etymology

From Hebrew Ḥăḇhaqquq, the name of the prophet.
