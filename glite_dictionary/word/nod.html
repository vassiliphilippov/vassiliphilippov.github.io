nod

1. VERB

/ nɑd / (AmE), / nɒd / (BrE)
to move gently back and forth, especially in a slow or swaying motion, like flowers or branches
move gently back and forth

Examples

The sunflowers nodded in the afternoon breeze.
Tall grasses nodded along the side of the road as cars drove past.
The trees nodded gently in the wind, creating a soothing rustling sound.

Synset Examples

the flowers were nodding in the breeze

2. VERB

/ nɑd / (AmE), / nɒd / (BrE)
to lower and raise the head slightly and briefly, especially to show agreement, understanding, or greeting
move head to show agreement or understanding

Examples

She gave a quick nod to show she understood the instructions.
The manager nodded in approval as the team presented their ideas.
He kept nodding during the conversation to show he agreed.

Synset Examples

The teacher nodded when the student gave the right answer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shake - move head from side to side to indicate disagreement or refusal

Etymology

From Middle English nodden, of uncertain origin.

3. NOUN

/ nɑd / (AmE), / nɒd / (BrE)
a quick downward movement of the head, often as a signal or gesture of agreement, greeting, or understanding
a downward movement of the head

Examples

She gave a friendly nod as she entered the room.
With a quick nod, he signaled that he understood the instructions.
The teacher acknowledged the answer with a nod of approval.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shake - the act of shaking the head, often to indicate 'no' or disagreement

Etymology

From Middle English nodden (to move the head quickly), of uncertain origin.
