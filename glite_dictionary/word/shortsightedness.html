shortsightedness

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrtˈsaɪtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ʃɔːtˈsaɪtɪdnəs / (BrE)
The quality of not considering the long-term consequences of actions, especially when managing resources; lack of foresight or prudence.
lack of foresight or prudence

Examples

The company's shortsightedness led to financial trouble when they failed to plan for the future.
Environmental shortsightedness often results in the depletion of natural resources.
His shortsightedness was apparent when he ignored the experts' warnings about the risks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: foresight - the ability to anticipate future events
  • Antonym: prudence - the quality of being cautious and wise

Etymology

From 'shortsighted' (unable to see far; lacking foresight) + '-ness' (forming nouns).

2. NOUN

/ ˌʃɔrtˈsaɪtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌʃɔːtˈsaɪtɪdnəs / (BrE)
the condition of being unable to see distant objects clearly because of a defect in the eye's focusing ability; myopia
inability to see distant objects clearly; myopia
medical

Examples

Her shortsightedness made it difficult to read signs from a distance.
Modern medicine offers several ways to correct shortsightedness, such as glasses or laser surgery.
Despite his shortsightedness, he refused to wear his prescription glasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myopia - medical term for shortsightedness; inability to see distant objects clearly
  • Antonym: farsightedness - condition of being able to see distant objects better than close ones (hyperopia)

Etymology

From short-sighted + -ness; short-sighted from 'short' + 'sight'.
