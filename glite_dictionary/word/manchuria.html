Manchuria

1. PROPN

/ mænˈtʃʊriə / (AmE), / manˈtʃʊəriə / (BrE)
a region in northeastern China, traditionally comprising the provinces of Liaoning, Jilin, and Heilongjiang, known for its historical and geopolitical significance.
region in northeastern China

Examples

The Japanese occupation of Manchuria in 1931 marked a turning point in Asian history.
Many rare animal species inhabit the forests of Manchuria.
My grandfather was born in Manchuria before moving to Beijing.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Manchúria', based on the name of the Manchu people.
