endothelium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛndoʊˈθiliəm / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈθiːlɪəm / (BrE)
A thin layer of flattened cells, of mesoblastic origin, that lines the inner surface of some blood vessels, lymphatic vessels, and body cavities.
thin lining of blood vessels and body cavities
medical

Examples

The endothelium plays a crucial role in regulating blood flow and vascular resistance.
Damage to the endothelium can lead to atherosclerosis and other cardiovascular diseases.
Scientists studied the endothelium to understand how nutrients pass from blood vessels into tissues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epithelium - thin tissue forming the outer layer of a body's surface and lining hollow structures

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek endon (within) + thele (nipple) + -ium (neuter diminutive suffix).
