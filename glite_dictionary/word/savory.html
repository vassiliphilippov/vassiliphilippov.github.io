savory

1. ADJ

/ ˈseɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈseɪvəri / (BrE)
Having a pleasant, appetizing taste; pleasing to the sense of taste.
pleasant-tasting; appetizing

Examples

The soup had a savory flavor that everyone enjoyed.
They served savory snacks at the reception, much to the guests’ delight.
The chef is famous for her savory pies and quiches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tasty - having a pleasant flavor
  • Antonym: unsavory - not pleasant in taste or smell

Etymology

From Old French 'savoure', from Latin 'saporem' (taste, flavor).

3. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈseɪvəri / (BrE)
either of two aromatic herbs of the mint family, commonly used to flavor food, especially summer savory (Satureja hortensis) and winter savory (Satureja montana)
aromatic herb used in cooking

Examples

I always add fresh savory to my bean soup for extra flavor.
Gardeners often grow savory alongside other herbs in their kitchen gardens.
Winter savory has a stronger taste than its summer counterpart.

Etymology

From Old French 'savoree', from Latin 'satureia' (a kind of herb).

/ ˈseɪ.vɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.vər.i / (BrE)

aromatic herb used in cooking

Examples

The chef added fresh savory to the stew for extra flavor.
She grows both summer and winter savory in her herb garden.
Savory is a key ingredient in many Mediterranean dishes.

Etymology

From Old French 'savoure' and Latin 'satureia,' referring to the herb.
