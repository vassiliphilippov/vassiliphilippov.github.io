permanganate

1. NOUN

/ pərˈmæŋɡəˌneɪt / (AmE), / pəˈmaeŋɡəneɪt / (BrE)
a dark purple compound containing the MnO4− ion, commonly used as a disinfectant and antiseptic in solution
a dark purple chemical compound (MnO4− ion)
medical

Examples

The laboratory used permanganate solution to sanitize equipment.
A deep purple color indicates the presence of permanganate in the sample.
Doctors sometimes prescribe a diluted permanganate wash for treating skin infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potassium permanganate - a common permanganate salt used as a disinfectant

Etymology

From Latin 'per-' (thoroughly) + 'manganum' (manganese) + '-ate' (salt or ester of acid)
