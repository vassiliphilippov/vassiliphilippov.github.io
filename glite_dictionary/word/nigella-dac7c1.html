nigella

1. NOUN

/ naɪˈdʒɛlə / (AmE), / nɪˈdʒɛlə / (BrE)
any plant of the genus Nigella, especially those with finely divided leaves and usually blue or white flowers, such as love-in-a-mist.
a plant of the genus Nigella

Examples

The delicate flowers of nigella brighten up many summer gardens.
She enjoys growing nigella for its ornamental blossoms and unique seed pods.
Nigella seeds are often used as a spice in Middle Eastern cuisine.

Etymology

From Latin 'nigella', diminutive of 'niger' meaning 'black', referring to the dark seeds.
