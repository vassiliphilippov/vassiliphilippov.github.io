synchronicity

1. NOUN

/ sɪŋˌkɹɑːˈnɪsɪti / (AmE), / sɪŋˌkrɒˈnɪsɪti / (BrE)
the occurrence of events at the same time or in a way that appears meaningfully related
simultaneous occurrence of events

Examples

The band performed with perfect synchronicity, making the music sound flawless.
There was a remarkable synchronicity between the two dancers as they moved across the stage.
Scientists observed synchronicity in the firing of neurons during the experiment.

Synset Examples

the drug produces an increased synchrony of the brain waves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchrony - simultaneous action or occurrence
  • Antonym: asynchrony - lack of simultaneous occurrence

Etymology

Coined by Swiss psychologist Carl Jung from Greek syn- (together) and khronos (time).
