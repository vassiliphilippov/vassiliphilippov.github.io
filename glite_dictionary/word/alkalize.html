to alkalize

1. VERB

/ ˈæl.kəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.laɪz / (BrE)
to make (a substance or solution) more alkaline or less acidic
make more alkaline; lessen acidity
medical

Examples

Doctors sometimes recommend supplements to alkalize the body and correct blood pH imbalances.
Adding baking soda will alkalize the solution, reducing its acidity.
Some diets aim to alkalize your system by encouraging the consumption of certain foods.

Synset Examples

the solution alkalized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to acidify - to make or become more acidic

Etymology

From 'alkali' (from Arabic 'al-qaly', ashes of saltwort), plus the suffix '-ize'.
