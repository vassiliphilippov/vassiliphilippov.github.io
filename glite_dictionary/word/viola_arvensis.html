Viola arvensis

1. NOUN

/ viˈoʊlə ɑrˈvɛnsɪs / (AmE), / viˈəʊlə ɑːˈvɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A European wild pansy with creamy or sometimes violet-tinged flowers, commonly found in fields and grassland.
a wild European pansy

Examples

The Viola arvensis blooms in early spring along the edges of cultivated fields.
Botanists collected specimens of Viola arvensis for their research on native European wildflowers.
Gardeners often confuse Viola arvensis with other species of wild pansies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: field pansy - another name for Viola arvensis
  • Synonym: wild pansy - related wildflower of the genus Viola

Etymology

From Latin, 'viola' meaning violet and 'arvensis' meaning 'of the field.'
