high-muck-a-muck

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ ˈmʌk ə ˌmʌk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ ˈmʌk ə ˌmʌk / (BrE)
an important or self-important person, especially one who is arrogant or conceited (usually used humorously or disparagingly)
arrogant or self-important person (slang)
slang

Examples

After the merger, all the high-muck-a-mucks got corner offices on the top floor.
He strutted into the meeting like some kind of high-muck-a-muck expecting everyone to listen to him.
The party was filled with high-muck-a-mucks from various industries, each trying to outdo the others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bigwig - an important or influential person
  • Synonym: VIP - a very important person
  • Antonym: nobody - a person of no importance

Etymology

North American slang, likely an alteration of Chinook Jargon 'muckamuck' (food, to eat), later applied to people of rank.
