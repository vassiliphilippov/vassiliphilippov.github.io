Thielavia

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˈlæviə / (AmE), / θaɪˈleɪviə / (BrE)
a genus of fungi that produce spherical brown perithecia, some of which bear conidia in chains; some species are known to cause root rot in plants
genus of fungi, some cause plant root rot
medical

Examples

Thielavia is commonly found in soil and decaying plant material.
Several species within the genus Thielavia are known to cause serious root rot in crops.
The laboratory identified the pathogen as Thielavia after microscopic examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungus - a group of eukaryotic organisms that includes yeasts, molds, and mushrooms

Etymology

From the name of German botanist Friedrich von Thielau, with standard Latin genus suffix -ia.

2. NOUN

/ θaɪˈleɪ.vi.ə / (AmE), / θɪˈlɛɪ.vi.ə / (BrE)
a genus of fungi characterized by spherical brown fruiting bodies (perithecia) and some spores (conidia) borne in chains, known to cause root rot in plants
genus of fungi causing root rot

Examples

Thielavia is known among plant pathologists for its role in causing root rot in crops.
Researchers discovered a widespread outbreak of Thielavia in greenhouse soils.
The fungus Thielavia forms distinctive brown perithecia during its reproductive cycle.

Etymology

From the name of German botanist Fernand Thielavius + New Latin suffix -ia (used to form names of genera).
