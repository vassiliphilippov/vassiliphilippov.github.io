tenuously

1. ADV

/ ˈtɛnjuəsli / (AmE), / ˈtɛnjʊəsli / (BrE)
In a way that is very weak, slight, or insubstantial; barely or with little strength or substance.
in a weak or insubstantial way

Examples

Her argument was tenuously supported by the available evidence, making it less convincing to the committee.
The two countries remained tenuously connected through a trade agreement despite years of tension.
The theory is tenuously based on a handful of outdated studies.

Synset Examples

his works tenuously survive in the minds of a few scholars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakly - in a weak manner

Etymology

From 'tenuous' (Latin tenuis, meaning thin or slight) + -ly.
