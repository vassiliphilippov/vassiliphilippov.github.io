Susquehanna

1. PROPN

/ ˌsʌskwəˈhænə / (AmE), / ˌsʌskwɪˈhænə / (BrE)
a river in the northeastern United States that rises in New York and flows south through Pennsylvania and Maryland into Chesapeake Bay
a major river in the northeastern US

Examples

The Susquehanna River is known for its long, winding route through Pennsylvania.
After heavy rains, the Susquehanna often floods low-lying areas along its banks.
Many small towns were founded along the Susquehanna due to its importance for trade and transportation.

Etymology

From the name of a Native American tribe, the Susquehannock, historically associated with the river's region.
