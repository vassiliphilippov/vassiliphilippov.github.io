convent

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.vənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.vənt / (BrE)
a building or group of buildings where a community of nuns live and work together as part of a religious order
building where nuns live and work

Examples

The young woman decided to join a convent and dedicate her life to service.
The convent was known for its beautiful gardens and peaceful atmosphere.
After retiring, she often volunteered at the local convent, helping the elderly nuns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monastery - a building where a community of monks lives
  • Synonym: nunnery - an alternative term for a convent; a place where nuns live

Etymology

From Latin 'conventus' (assembly, meeting), from 'convenire' (to come together).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.vənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.vənt / (BrE)
a building or community in which nuns live together under religious vows
a religious residence for nuns

Examples

After joining the convent, she dedicated her life to prayer and service.
The old stone convent overlooked the peaceful countryside.
Visitors are only allowed inside the convent during certain hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monastery - a residence for a community of monks
  • Synonym: nunnery - an abbey or residence for nuns
  • Synonym: abbey - a building where monks or nuns live and worship

Etymology

From Latin 'conventus' (assembly, gathering), from 'convenire' (to come together).
