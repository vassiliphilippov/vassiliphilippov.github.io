5. NOUN

/ keɪ / (AmE), / keɪ / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to 1,024 bytes, commonly used to measure computer memory or storage capacity.
unit of 1,024 bytes (computer)

Examples

The file size was just over 10K, making it easy to download quickly.
Early home computers often shipped with only 16K of memory.
That simple text document takes up less than 4K on the disk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kilobyte - unit of 1,024 bytes in computing

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'kilo-', from Greek 'chilioi' (thousand), adapted in computing to mean 1,024 due to binary systems.

6. NOUN

/ keɪ / (AmE), / keɪ / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to 1,000 bytes, commonly used in computing to denote kilobytes (not to be confused with 1,024 bytes, which is sometimes also called a kilobyte in computer science).
unit of 1,000 bytes (computing)

Examples

The image file was only 120K in size, making it easy to email.
Our monthly data usage is limited to 100,000K per account.
This document will occupy about 15K on your hard drive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kilobyte - a unit of digital information, either 1,000 or 1,024 bytes
  • Degree Higher: megabyte - unit of information equal to 1,000,000 bytes

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'kilo,' from Greek 'khilioi' (thousand), used as a prefix for 1,000 in the metric system.
