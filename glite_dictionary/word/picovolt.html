picovolt

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkoʊˌvoʊlt / (AmE), / ˈpɪkəʊˌvəʊlt / (BrE)
a unit of electric potential equal to one trillionth (10⁻¹²) of a volt
one trillionth of a volt (unit)

Examples

The signal measured only a few picovolts, requiring extremely sensitive equipment to detect.
Researchers detected fluctuations as small as tens of picovolts during the experiment.
Voltage changes in the brain can be as subtle as single-digit picovolts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: volt - unit of electric potential
  • Degree Lower: nanovolt - one billionth of a volt (unit)

Etymology

From prefix 'pico-' (denoting a factor of 10⁻¹², from Italian 'pico', meaning 'small') and 'volt', named after Alessandro Volta.
