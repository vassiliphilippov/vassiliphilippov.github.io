Trichostema lanatum

1. NOUN

/ trɪˌkɑːstiːmə ləˈneɪtəm / (AmE), / trɪˌkɒstɪmə ləˈneɪtəm / (BrE)
an aromatic plant with woolly leaves native to southern California and Mexico, also known as woolly bluecurls
an aromatic plant of southern California and Mexico

Examples

Trichostema lanatum is prized by gardeners for its fragrant purple flowers and drought resistance.
Botanists often study Trichostema lanatum to learn more about native Californian flora.
The soft, woolly leaves help Trichostema lanatum retain moisture in arid environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'tricho-' (hair) and 'stema' (thread), referring to the plant's hairy appearance; 'lanatum' from Latin, meaning 'woolly'.
