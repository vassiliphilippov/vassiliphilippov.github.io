satinwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæt.ən.wʊd / (AmE), / ˈsæt.ɪn.wʊd / (BrE)
a West Indian tree with smooth, lustrous, and slightly oily wood, often used in fine woodworking and furniture-making
West Indian tree with lustrous wood

Examples

The cabinetmaker preferred to work with satinwood due to its attractive sheen and durability.
Some antique desks are crafted from rare satinwood, lending them a distinctive golden appearance.
The rich grain of satinwood makes it desirable for inlay work in fine furniture.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæt̬.ən.wʊd / (AmE), / ˈsæt.ɪn.wʊd / (BrE)
a hard, yellowish wood with a satiny sheen, obtained from certain tropical trees and used in fine cabinetwork and tools
yellowish lustrous wood used in fine furniture

Examples

The cabinet was crafted from expensive satinwood imported from South Asia.
Collectors value antique desks made of satinwood for their rich color and smooth finish.
She admired the gleaming satinwood veneers on the 18th-century side table.

Etymology

Named for its distinctive satiny sheen, resembling satin fabric.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsætnˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈsætɪnwʊd / (BrE)
a tree native to South and Southeast Asia, valued for its hard, glossy, yellowish wood often used in fine furniture and inlay work
tree with lustrous yellowish wood

Examples

The cabinet was crafted from rare satinwood, giving it a beautiful golden sheen.
Antique dealers often seek out satinwood furniture for its distinctive color and shine.
Southeast Asia is known for exporting high-quality satinwood used in interior design.

Etymology

Named for its smooth, glossy 'satin'-like appearance of the wood.
