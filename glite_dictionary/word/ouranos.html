Ouranos

1. PROPN

/ ʊˈreɪnɒs / (AmE), / juːˈræənɒs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the god of the sky and heavens, son and husband of Gaea and father of the Titans.
Greek god of the sky and heavens

Examples

In ancient myths, Ouranos was overthrown by his son Cronus.
The story of Ouranos and Gaea explains the origin of the Titans.
Artists have often depicted Ouranos as a figure arching over the earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uranus - The Roman equivalent of Ouranos, god of the sky

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Οὐρανός (Ouranós), meaning 'sky' or 'heaven'.
