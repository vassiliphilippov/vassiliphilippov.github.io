Chenopodium

1. NOUN

/ kɛˈnoʊˌpoʊdiəm / (AmE), / kɛˈnɒpədiəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the amaranth family, commonly known as goosefoot or pigweed.
a genus of flowering plants (goosefoot)

Examples

The leaves of Chenopodium are often shaped like a goose's foot.
Some species of Chenopodium are cultivated for their edible seeds.
We found wild Chenopodium growing along the edge of the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goosefoot - common name for species in the Chenopodium genus
  • Synonym: pigweed - another common name for species in the Chenopodium genus

Etymology

From Greek *khēn* (goose) + *pous, podos* (foot), referring to the leaf shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkiːnəˈpoʊdiəm / (AmE), / ˌkiːnəˈpəʊdiəm / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants commonly known as goosefoot or pigweed, including several species used as food or weeds
genus of plants including goosefoot and pigweed

Examples

The seeds of Chenopodium species have been used as a food source since ancient times.
Some gardeners consider Chenopodium a troublesome weed in their vegetable patches.
Quinoa, once classified as Chenopodium quinoa, is now recognized as an important crop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goosefoot - common name for plants in the genus Chenopodium
  • Synonym: pigweed - common name for various weedy species in the genus Chenopodium

Etymology

From Greek 'chen' (goose) and 'podion' (little foot), referring to the shape of the leaves.
