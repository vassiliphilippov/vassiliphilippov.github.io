juke

1. NOUN

/ dʒuːk / (AmE), / dʒuːk / (BrE)
(US, regional) A small roadside bar or establishment, especially in the southeastern United States, where food, drinks, and dancing to music from a jukebox are offered.
small roadside bar/club in the US South
american

Examples

On Friday nights, the juke down the highway was packed with people dancing and laughing.
Local musicians often performed in the juke, giving the place a lively atmosphere.
Many travelers stopped at the juke for a meal and some music after a long day's drive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juke joint - A similar term for a small southern bar with music and dancing

Etymology

Probably from Gullah, ultimately from West African languages such as Wolof *dzug*, meaning 'to misbehave or lead a disorderly life'.

2. NOUN

/ dʒuːk / (AmE), / dʒuːk / (BrE)
In American football, a deceptive movement or feint made by a player to evade a defender.
deceptive move in football
american

Examples

The running back made a brilliant juke to get past the defender.
His quick juke left the cornerback grasping at air.
Coaches praised her for the smooth juke that turned the play around.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feint - a deceptive or pretended movement
  • Synonym: fake - a misleading movement, especially in sports

Etymology

Probably alteration of 'jook', of Gullah origin, meaning 'to poke' or 'stab'.
