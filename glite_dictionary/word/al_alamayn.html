Al Alamayn

1. PROPN

/ ˌæl əˈlɑː.maɪn / (AmE), / ˌæl əˈlæm.aɪn / (BrE)
A decisive pitched battle in World War II (1942) in which Allied forces under Montgomery defeated German troops led by Rommel in North Africa.
WWII battle in North Africa, 1942

Examples

The Battle of Al Alamayn marked a turning point in the North African campaign of World War II.
Historians often study Al Alamayn for its strategic importance and the tactics used by both sides.
Montgomery's leadership at Al Alamayn was widely praised and contributed to Allied morale.

Etymology

From Arabic العلمين (al-ʿAlamayn), meaning 'the two flags,' referring to the site in Egypt.
