longanberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋənˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋənˌbɛri / (BrE)
A small, round, sweet fruit native to Asia, similar to the lychee but with a more translucent flesh and a brownish shell.
small Asian fruit similar to lychee

Examples

She brought a bag of fresh longanberries from the market to share with her family.
The sweet, fragrant flavor of longanberries makes them a popular addition to Asian desserts.
I had never tasted a longanberry before my trip to Thailand, but I loved their delicate sweetness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lychee - a small, round Asian fruit with sweet white flesh and a rough pinkish-red skin

Etymology

From Cantonese lùhng-ngáahn (dragon eye), referring to the fruit's appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋ.ənˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋ.ənˌbɛri / (BrE)
A tropical tree native to southeastern Asia and Australia, grown for its sweet edible fruit that resembles the lychee.
tropical fruit tree of Asia and Australia

Examples

The market had a fresh shipment of longanberries alongside other tropical fruits.
She enjoys making desserts with longanberries because of their subtle sweetness.
The longanberry tree flourishes in warm, humid climates similar to that of its native regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longan - a tropical Asian fruit similar to lychee
  • Synonym: lychee - a tropical fruit with a rough pink-red rind and sweet white flesh

Etymology

From Cantonese lùhng-ngáahn (dragon's eye), referring to the fruit's appearance.
