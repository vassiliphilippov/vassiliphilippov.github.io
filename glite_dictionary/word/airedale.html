Airedale

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌdeɪl / (AmE), / ˈɛədeɪl / (BrE)
a large breed of terrier with a wiry coat, originally bred in Yorkshire
large terrier breed from Yorkshire

Examples

The Airedale is known for its intelligence and versatility as a working dog.
Her family adopted an Airedale from a local animal shelter last year.
Many police forces have used Airedales for tracking and guarding purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrier - a type of dog breed known for hunting and burrowing

Etymology

Named after the Aire valley (Airedale) in Yorkshire, England, where the breed was developed.
