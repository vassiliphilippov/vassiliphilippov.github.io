prepossession

1. NOUN

/ priˌpoʊˈzɛʃən / (AmE), / priːpəˈzɛʃən / (BrE)
an opinion or feeling formed beforehand, especially without proper evidence; a preconceived notion or prejudice
an opinion formed ahead of experience

Examples

His prepossession against modern art made it difficult for him to appreciate the exhibition.
Despite her prepossession in favor of the new manager, she tried to remain objective.
It is important to avoid prepossession when making a fair judgment.

Synset Examples

he did not even try to confirm his preconceptions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience
  • Synonym: bias - unfair inclination or preference for or against something

Etymology

From Latin 'prae-' (before) + 'possidere' (to possess), via French 'prépossession'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌprɛpəˈzɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːpəˈzɛʃən / (BrE)
a preconceived opinion or feeling about something; or the state of being favorably disposed toward someone or something, often before knowing much about it
preconceived opinion or favorable bias

Examples

Her prepossession in favor of the new proposal was clear during the meeting.
He spoke without prepossession, aiming to be as objective as possible.
The judge must be free from any prepossession to ensure a fair trial.

Synset Examples

the king's prepossession in my favor is very valuable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience
  • Synonym: bias - inclination or prejudice for or against something
  • Antonym: impartiality - fairness, absence of bias or prepossession

Etymology

From Latin praepossessio (preoccupation, preconceived opinion), from prae- (before) + possessio (possession).
