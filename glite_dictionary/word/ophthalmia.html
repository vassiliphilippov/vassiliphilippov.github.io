ophthalmia

1. NOUN

/ ɑfˈθælmiə / (AmE), / ɒfˈθælmiə / (BrE)
severe inflammation of the conjunctiva of the eye; severe conjunctivitis
severe conjunctivitis (eye inflammation)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with ophthalmia after presenting with redness and swelling in both eyes.
Severe ophthalmia can cause discomfort and visual disturbances if left untreated.
Doctors recommend prompt treatment to reduce the risk of complications arising from ophthalmia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjunctivitis - inflammation of the conjunctiva of the eye

Etymology

From Late Latin ophthalmia, from Ancient Greek ophthalmía (pain in the eye), from ophthalmos (eye).
