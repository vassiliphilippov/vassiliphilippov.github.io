fabricator

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæbrɪˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈfæbrɪkeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who invents false stories or lies; a liar
someone who tells lies

Examples

The journalist turned out to be a fabricator of sensational stories.
Many believed his alibi until evidence proved him a skilled fabricator.
In court, the witness was exposed as a fabricator who twisted the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liar - a person who tells lies

Etymology

From Latin 'fabricari' (to fashion, make), via 'fabricate'.
