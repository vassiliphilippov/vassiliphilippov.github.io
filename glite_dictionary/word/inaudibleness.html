inaudibleness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɔdəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈnɔːdɪb(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible to hear.
quality of being impossible to hear

Examples

The inaudibleness of the alarm cost them precious minutes during the emergency.
Due to the inaudibleness of his whisper, nobody understood the message.
Engineers worked to reduce the inaudibleness of important safety signals in the factory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inaudibility - the quality of being inaudible
  • Antonym: audibility - the quality of being able to be heard

Etymology

From inaudible + -ness, ultimately from Latin 'inaudibilis'.
