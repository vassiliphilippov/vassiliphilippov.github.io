os

1. NOUN

/ ɑs / (AmE), / ɒs / (BrE)
an opening or orifice, especially a mouth or mouthlike opening in anatomy or biology
mouth or mouthlike opening
medical

Examples

During the procedure, the doctor examined the os of the cervix for any abnormalities.
Certain marine animals have an os that helps them filter water for food.
The term 'oral' is derived from the Latin word os, meaning mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orifice - an opening, particularly in the body

Etymology

From Latin 'os' meaning 'mouth'.

2. NOUN

/ ɑs / (AmE), / ɒs / (BrE)
(Anatomy) a bone; the rigid connective tissue that forms the skeleton of vertebrates.
bone; rigid tissue in vertebrate skeleton
medical

Examples

The X-ray revealed a fracture in the os of his forearm.
Ancient medical texts often use the word os to describe bones of the body.
During the anatomy lecture, the professor explained the structure of each os.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bone - any of the rigid structural units forming the skeleton in vertebrates

Etymology

From Latin os (bone).
