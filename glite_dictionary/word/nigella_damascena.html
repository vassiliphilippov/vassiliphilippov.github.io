Nigella damascena

1. NOUN

/ naɪˈdʒɛlə dəˌmæˈsiːnə / (AmE), / nɪˈdʒɛlə dəˌmæˈsiːnə / (BrE)
A European garden plant with finely divided leaves and white or pale blue flowers, commonly known as love-in-a-mist.
European garden plant; love-in-a-mist

Examples

The delicate flowers of Nigella damascena add a charming touch to cottage gardens.
Many gardeners sow Nigella damascena in spring for a beautiful display in early summer.
Dried seed pods from Nigella damascena are often used in floral arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: love-in-a-mist - Common name for Nigella damascena, a garden plant with feathery foliage and blue flowers

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Nigella' means 'little black' (referring to its seeds), and 'damascena' indicates an origin from Damascus.
