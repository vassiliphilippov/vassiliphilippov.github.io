Sylvilagus floridanus

1. NOUN

/ sɪlˈvɪl.əɡəs flɔːˈrɪd.ə.nəs / (AmE), / sɪlˈvɪl.ə.ɡəs flɒˈrɪd.ə.nəs / (BrE)
the eastern cottontail rabbit, a species widely distributed in the United States except for the northwest and far west
eastern cottontail rabbit species

Examples

The Sylvilagus floridanus is frequently seen in suburban gardens across the eastern United States.
Biologists studied the habitat preferences of the Sylvilagus floridanus in restored prairies.
Unlike other rabbits, the Sylvilagus floridanus adapts well to environments altered by humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eastern cottontail - common name for Sylvilagus floridanus, a widely distributed rabbit in North America

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sylvilagus' (forest hare) and 'floridanus' (of Florida).
