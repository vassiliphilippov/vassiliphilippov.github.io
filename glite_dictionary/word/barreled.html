barreled

1. ADJ

/ ˈbærəld / (AmE), / ˈbærəld / (BrE)
(of an arrow or similar object) shaped so that it tapers toward both ends, being widest in the middle.
tapered at both ends, widest in middle (archery)

Examples

The archer selected a barreled shaft for greater stability in flight.
Skilled craftsmen produced barreled arrows to reduce air resistance.
The barreled design made the arrow faster and more accurate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapered - gradually narrowing toward one end
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01815633-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈbær.əl̩d / (AmE), / ˈbær.əld / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) put in or stored in a barrel.
stored in a barrel

Examples

The brewery offers several barreled ales aged for over a year.
After fermentation, the wine was barreled to enhance its flavor.
Their barreled bourbon has a rich, smoky taste that comes from the oak casks.

Synset Examples

barreled beer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bottled - (of a drink) put into bottles for storage or sale
