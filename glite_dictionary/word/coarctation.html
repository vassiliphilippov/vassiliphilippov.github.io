coarctation

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌɑrkˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌɑːkˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
(biology, medicine) an abnormal narrowing or constriction of a vessel or canal in the body, especially a congenital narrowing of the aorta.
narrowing of a vessel in the body
medical

Examples

The newborn was diagnosed with a coarctation of the aorta requiring immediate surgery.
Doctors discovered a severe coarctation during the patient's routine cardiac examination.
If untreated, coarctation of major arteries can lead to high blood pressure and heart failure.

Etymology

From Latin 'coarctatio' (a narrowing), from 'coarctare' (to compress).

2. NOUN

/ koʊˌɑrkˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌɑːkˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a narrowing or constriction of a vessel, canal, or duct in the body, especially an abnormal narrowing of a blood vessel
abnormal narrowing of a bodily passage
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a coarctation of the aorta, requiring surgical intervention.
Doctors detected a coarctation in the infant's heart during a routine check-up.
Severe coarctation can lead to high blood pressure and reduced blood flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stenosis - the abnormal narrowing of a bodily passage

Etymology

From Latin coarctatio (a narrowing), from coarctare (to press together).
