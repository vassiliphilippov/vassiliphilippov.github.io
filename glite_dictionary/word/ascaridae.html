Ascaridae

1. NOUN

/ əˈskær.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / æsˈkær.ɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of large parasitic roundworms that infest the intestines of vertebrates.
family of large parasitic roundworms
medical

Examples

The veterinarian identified the parasite as a member of the Ascaridae family.
Several species within Ascaridae can infect both humans and animals.
Infection with Ascaridae can cause serious digestive problems in livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascarid - a type of roundworm from the Ascaridae family

Etymology

From New Latin 'Ascaris' (a type of roundworm) + '-idae' (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ əˈskɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / əˈskærɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large roundworms that are parasites in the intestines of vertebrates.
family of parasitic roundworms
medical

Examples

The Ascaridae family includes many roundworms that cause infections in humans and animals.
Scientists studied the lifecycle of parasites in the Ascaridae to develop effective treatments.
Farmers implemented measures to protect livestock from Ascaridae infestations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roundworm - parasitic or free-living worm of the phylum Nematoda

Etymology

From modern Latin Ascaridae, from Ascaris (the type genus) and -idae (family suffix in zoological names).
