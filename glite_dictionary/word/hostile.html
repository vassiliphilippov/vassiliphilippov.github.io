hostile

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːstaɪl / (AmE), / ˈhɒstaɪl / (BrE)
a member of an enemy military force; an enemy soldier or combatant
enemy soldier or combatant

Examples

The platoon ran into several hostiles while patrolling the area.
We received orders to engage the hostiles approaching from the north ridge.
After the firefight, only a few hostiles remained in the sector.

Synset Examples

the platoon ran into a pack of hostiles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile to someone or something
  • Antonym: ally - a state or person cooperating with another for a military or other purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'hostilis' (of an enemy), from 'hostis' (enemy).
