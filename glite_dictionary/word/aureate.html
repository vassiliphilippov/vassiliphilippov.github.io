aureate

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔriˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːriət / (BrE)
(of language or style) elaborately or excessively ornamented, often with ornate or grandiloquent expression.
elaborately or excessively ornate (esp. of style)

Examples

The poet's aureate style was admired for its rich and elaborate imagery.
Critics argued that her aureate language made the novel unnecessarily difficult to read.
The speaker adopted an aureate tone during the ceremony, using flowery expressions and grand metaphors.

Synset Examples

flamboyant handwriting
the senator's florid speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: florid - elaborately or excessively intricate or complicated; especially of style
  • Synonym: ornate - elaborately adorned, especially in language or decoration

Etymology

From Latin 'aureatus' (gilded, golden), from 'aurum' (gold).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔriˌeɪt, ˈɔr.i.ət / (AmE), / ˈɔː.ri.eɪt, ɔːˈriː.ət / (BrE)
having a deep, slightly brownish golden color; resembling gold in color
of a gold or golden color

Examples

Her dress shone in the sunlight with an aureate brilliance.
The poet described the fields as waves of aureate wheat.
Long aureate hair cascaded down her back.

Synset Examples

long aureate (or golden) hair
a gold carpet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden - having the color of gold
  • Synonym: gilded - covered with a thin layer of gold or resembling gold

Etymology

From Latin aureatus, from aureus (golden), from aurum (gold).
