Ricinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪsɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪsɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the spurge family, containing only the castor-oil plant species.
plant genus containing the castor-oil plant

Examples

The seeds of Ricinus are used to produce castor oil.
Ricinus is recognized for its unique spiny capsules and large leaves.
Botanists have classified the castor-oil plant under the genus Ricinus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castor-oil plant - the only species in the genus Ricinus; the source of castor oil

Etymology

From Latin 'ricinus' meaning 'tick', from the resemblance of the castor bean to a tick.

2. NOUN

/ ˈraɪsɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪsɪnəs / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae), consisting of a single species, Ricinus communis (the castor-oil plant)
genus of the castor-oil plant

Examples

The seeds of Ricinus are the primary source of commercial castor oil.
Botanists classify the castor-oil plant within the genus Ricinus.
Ricinus is native to the southeastern Mediterranean Basin, Eastern Africa, and India.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castor-oil plant - plant species Ricinus communis, source of castor oil

Etymology

From Latin 'ricinus' meaning 'tick', from the perceived resemblance of the seed to a tick.
