comfit

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌm.fɪt / (AmE), / ˈkʌm.fɪt / (BrE)
A sweet made from a fruit, nut, or seed coated in sugar candy.
sugar-coated candy with fruit or nut

Examples

She offered her guests a dish filled with colorful comfits after dinner.
The children enjoyed eating comfit almonds at the holiday festival.
He savored the taste of the rose-flavored comfit that reminded him of his childhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragée - a sugar-coated seed, nut, or candy; similar confection

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *confit*, from Latin *confectum* (prepared, made).
