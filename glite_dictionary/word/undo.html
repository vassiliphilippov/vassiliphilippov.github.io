to undo

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈdu / (AmE), / ʌnˈduː / (BrE)
to remove or reverse the effect or result of something, often by depriving it of particular qualities or characteristics
remove or reverse an effect or property

Examples

The new evidence will undo much of the public's confidence in the report.
Years of progress were undone by a single careless mistake.
She tried to undo the damage caused by her earlier comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverse - to turn something the opposite way or change the effect to its opposite
  • Antonym: to redo - to do something again or restore its state

Etymology

From Old English 'undōn', from 'un-' (reverse) + 'dōn' (do).
