imitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪməˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of copying the behavior, style, or actions of another person or thing
the act of copying someone or something

Examples

Children often learn new skills through imitation rather than direct instruction.
The comedian's imitation of the president made the audience laugh.
Parrots are known for their remarkable imitation of human speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emulation - effort to match or surpass someone by imitation
  • Antonym: originality - the quality of being novel or unusual, not imitative

Etymology

From Latin imitatio (copying, imitation), from imitari (to copy).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪməˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
(in art or aesthetics) The doctrine that artistic representations should closely and accurately reproduce nature or human behavior.
theory in art: accurate representation

Examples

Ancient Greek art was heavily influenced by the doctrine of imitation of nature.
Some philosophers argue that imitation is the highest aim of artistic creation.
The concept of imitation was central to debates about realism in literature and painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mimesis - artistic imitation or representation of reality

Etymology

From Latin imitatio (copying, imitation), from imitari (to imitate).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪməˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a thing that is made to look, feel, or seem like something else, especially in order to deceive or as a substitute for the original
something made as a copy of an original

Examples

The jewelry was just an imitation of the designer brand, but it looked very convincing.
This cheese is actually an imitation made from soy, not real dairy.
He bought an imitation of the famous painting for his office wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copy - something made to be like another
  • Antonym: original - something serving as a model or source for copies

Etymology

From Latin imitātiōn- (process of imitating), from imitārī (to imitate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03567306-n
Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ ˌɪməˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an exaggerated representation or copy of a person, often created for comic effect
comic exaggeration of a person

Examples

The comedian's imitation of the mayor had the entire audience laughing.
Her imitation of her teacher is so spot-on that everyone recognized who she meant.
Saturday Night Live is famous for its clever political imitations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caricature - a drawing or description that exaggerates features for comic effect

Etymology

From Latin imitatio, from imitari (to copy, imitate).
