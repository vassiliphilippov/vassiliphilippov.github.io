unshorn

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈʃɔrn / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃɔːn / (BrE)
not having been sheared; not cut or trimmed, especially referring to wool or hair.
not sheared or trimmed

Examples

The unshorn sheep wandered the fields, their wool thick and tangled.
He looked rugged with an unshorn beard covering his face.
The festival featured a contest for the longest unshorn fleece.

Synset Examples

a grizzly unshorn beard
unshorn sheep

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shorn - having had the wool, hair, or fleece cut
  • Antonym: sheared - trimmed or cut, especially of hair or wool

Etymology

From un- (not) + shorn, past participle of 'shear'.
