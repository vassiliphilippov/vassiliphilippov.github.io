Chimaeridae

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈmɪəriˌdi / (AmE), / kɪˈmɪəriˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of cartilaginous fishes, comprising the chimaeras, within the subclass Holocephali.
family of cartilaginous fishes (chimaeras)
medical

Examples

Members of the Chimaeridae family are sometimes called ghost sharks.
The Chimaeridae are found in deep waters across the world's oceans.
Researchers studied the unique jaw structure of Chimaeridae species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimaera - a type of cartilaginous fish in the family Chimaeridae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Chimaera', the type genus, from Greek 'khimaira' (chimera, monster).
