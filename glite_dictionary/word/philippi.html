Philippi

1. PROPN

/ fəˈlɪpaɪ / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪpaɪ / (BrE)
an ancient city in Macedonia, famous as the site of the decisive battle in 42 BC where the forces of Octavian and Mark Antony defeated Brutus and Cassius.
ancient Macedonian city, battle site

Examples

The Battle of Philippi marked a turning point in Roman history.
Historians often discuss the strategies used at Philippi by both sides.
He visited the ruins of Philippi during his trip to Greece.

2. PROPN

/ fɪˈlɪp.aɪ / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪp.aɪ / (BrE)
an ancient city in Macedonia, significant in Roman history and early Christianity, and the site of a famous battle in 42 BCE.
ancient Macedonian city, important in Christianity

Examples

The Apostle Paul wrote a letter to the church in Philippi, which became the biblical Epistle to the Philippians.
A decisive battle in Roman history was fought at Philippi in 42 BCE.
Many ancient ruins of Philippi can still be visited in northern Greece today.

Etymology

From ancient Greek Φίλιπποι (Phílippoi), named after Philip II of Macedon.
