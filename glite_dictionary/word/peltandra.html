Peltandra

1. PROPN

/ pɛlˈtændrə / (AmE), / pɛlˈtændrə / (BrE)
A small genus of North American marsh or aquatic herbs in the arum family, including species such as arrow arum.
genus of North American marsh herbs

Examples

The wetlands were rich with Peltandra, providing a lush habitat for local wildlife.
Botanists studied the distribution of Peltandra species across the marshes of North America.
We identified several Peltandra plants by their arrow-shaped leaves during our aquatic plant survey.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek roots: 'pelte' (small shield) and 'andros' (man), referring to shield-shaped anther connectives.
