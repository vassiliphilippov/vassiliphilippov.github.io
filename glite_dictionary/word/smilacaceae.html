Smilacaceae

1. NOUN

/ smaɪləˈkeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / smɪləsɪˈiːsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants commonly known as greenbriars, typically characterized by climbing, woody vines with tendrils
family of plants known as greenbriars

Examples

The Smilacaceae family includes many species of thorny, climbing vines found in woodlands.
Researchers are studying the medicinal uses of plants in the Smilacaceae.
Most members of the Smilacaceae are recognizable by their glossy leaves and tendrils.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Smilax (the type genus) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant family names).
