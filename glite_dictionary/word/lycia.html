Lycia

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɪʃiə / (AmE), / ˈlɪsɪə / (BrE)
an ancient region located on the southwestern coast of Asia Minor (now part of modern-day Turkey), known for its unique culture and history in antiquity
ancient region in southwest Asia Minor

Examples

Ancient tombs can still be found throughout Lycia, providing insight into its unique burial traditions.
The coastline of Lycia was famous for its natural beauty and strategic harbors in antiquity.
Historians often study Lycia to learn more about ancient Anatolian cultures.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'Λυκία' (Lukía), Latinized as 'Lycia'.
