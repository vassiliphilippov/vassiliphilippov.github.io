Quercus myrtifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs ˌmɜrtɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˌmɜːtɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A small evergreen shrub or tree native to the southeastern United States, often forming dense thickets in sandy coastal areas.
a small evergreen oak of the southeastern U.S.

Examples

Quercus myrtifolia is commonly found growing in sandy coastal regions of Florida.
Dense thickets of Quercus myrtifolia can make hiking difficult in some southeastern forests.
Conservationists are studying Quercus myrtifolia because of its important role in local habitats.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' (oak) and 'myrtifolia' (myrtle-leaved, from 'myrtus' myrtle and 'folia' leaves).
