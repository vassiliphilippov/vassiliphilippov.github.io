accumulation

1. NOUN

/ əˌkjumjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌkjuːmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a collection or gathering of things over time
a collection of things gathered together

Examples

The accumulation of books in her study made it difficult to find anything.
An accumulation of dust covered the old furniture in the attic.
Over the years, the accumulation of experience helped her excel in her career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collection - a group of things that have been collected

Etymology

From Latin 'accumulatio', from 'accumulare' (to heap up).

2. NOUN

/ əˌkjumjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌkjuːmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of gradually increasing or collecting more of something over time, often as a result of natural growth or repeated additions
gradual increase or collection over time

Examples

The accumulation of dust on the shelves was obvious after several months.
Continuous rainfall can lead to the accumulation of water in low-lying areas.
Her knowledge grew through the gradual accumulation of experience and study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collection - the action or process of gathering things together
  • Antonym: depletion - the reduction in number or quantity of something

Etymology

From Latin accumulatio (a heap, accumulation), from accumulare (to heap up).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13446038-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ əˌkjumjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌkjuːmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(finance) Profits not distributed as dividends but added to the company's capital base.
retained profits in finance

Examples

The company's accumulation of profits increased the overall value of its assets.
Strong accumulation over several years allowed the firm to expand without needing outside investment.
Analysts praised the strategy of maximizing accumulation rather than paying out higher dividends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retained earnings - Profits kept in a company instead of being paid out as dividends

Etymology

From Latin accumulatio (a gathering together).

4. NOUN

/ əˌkjuːmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌkjuːmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or action of gradually gathering or collecting something over time
the process of collecting or gathering

Examples

The accumulation of dust in the corners showed that the room had not been cleaned for weeks.
Over the years, his accumulation of books filled every shelf in the house.
Slow accumulation of snow overnight made the morning roads slippery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collection - the act of collecting or gathering things together

Etymology

From Latin 'accumulatio', from 'accumulare' (to heap up).
