bated

1. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈbeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of emotion, expectations, or enthusiasm) diminished or restrained; moderated in intensity.
diminished or restrained; moderated in intensity

Examples

We listened with bated anticipation for the results to be announced.
Her bated excitement showed she was trying to hide her enthusiasm.
His bated expectations reflected a cautious optimism after last year's disappointment.

Synset Examples

our bated enthusiasm
his bated hopes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restrained - held back, controlled, or kept within limits
  • Synonym: diminished - reduced in size, importance, or intensity
  • Antonym: unrestrained - not checked or controlled; free

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'bate', from Old French 'batre' (to beat), related to reducing or restraining force.
