Isoetales

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsoʊˈɛtəliːz / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊˈɛtəliːz / (BrE)
an order of aquatic or marsh-growing fern allies, known to have existed since the Cenozoic era, sometimes included in Lycopodiales
order of aquatic fern allies

Examples

The Isoetales are often found in shallow lakes and wetland habitats.
Scientists study Isoetales to understand plant evolution since the Cenozoic era.
Some taxonomists classify Isoetales separately from the order Lycopodiales.

Etymology

From the genus name Isoetes, which comes from Greek isos (equal) + etos (year).

2. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsoʊˈitəliːz / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊˈiːteɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of aquatic or marsh-growing fern-like plants, known as fern allies, which have existed since the Cenozoic era
order of aquatic fern-like plants

Examples

The Isoetales can be found in shallow ponds and marshes worldwide.
Scientists have studied fossils that show Isoetales have existed since the Cenozoic era.
Unlike many other plant groups, the Isoetales are true fern allies with unique adaptations to aquatic habitats.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Lycopodiales - an order of clubmosses, sometimes considered to include Isoetales

Etymology

From the genus name Isoetes, derived from Greek isoetes (a kind of plant).
