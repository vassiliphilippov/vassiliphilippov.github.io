Aleurites

1. PROPN

/ əˈlʊr.ɪˌtiːz / (AmE), / aljʊˈraɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees in the spurge family, including the candlenut tree, known for their large oily seeds.
genus of tropical trees (candlenut genus)

Examples

The oil extracted from Aleurites seeds is used in traditional medicine and cooking.
Many Pacific cultures have cultivated Aleurites for centuries.
Scientists studied the growth patterns of Aleurites in varying soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candlenut - the tree Aleurites moluccana or its nut, used for oil and formerly for lighting

Etymology

From Greek aleuron (flour), referring to the mealy coating on the seeds.

2. NOUN

/ əˈlɛw.raɪ.tiz / (AmE), / ˌæl.jʊˈraɪ.tiːz / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae, including species such as the candlenut tree
genus of flowering plants including candlenut

Examples

The seeds of Aleurites are often pressed to produce oil used in cooking and light.
Botanists have identified several species within the Aleurites genus, most notably the candlenut tree.
Aleurites grows in tropical regions and is valued for its versatile seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candlenut - the tree Aleurites moluccanus, or its seed, used as a source of oil and light

Etymology

From Greek aleuron (flour), referring to the floury appearance of the seeds.
