capitalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæpɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkapɪtəlʌɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the total value of a company's shares or assets as estimated in financial terms; the process of estimating such value
estimation of a company's value

Examples

The company's capitalisation reached over $10 billion after its successful IPO.
Accurately determining the capitalisation of a business is essential for attracting investors.
Changes in the economic climate can greatly affect a firm's capitalisation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market capitalization - the total value of a company's shares of stock on the market

Etymology

From capital + -isation, after Latin 'capitale' (principal, property).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæpɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæpɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of writing a letter or word with capital letters
writing in capital letters
british

Examples

Proper capitalisation is required in formal writing.
The document was rejected because of incorrect capitalisation.
She highlighted the capitalisation errors in the students’ essays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capitalization - writing in capital letters (American spelling)

Etymology

From French 'capitalisation', from 'capitaliser' (to capitalize), from 'capital' (principal, uppercase letter).
