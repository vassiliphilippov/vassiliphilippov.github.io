Momordica

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈmɔːrdɪkə / (AmE), / məˈmɔːdɪkə / (BrE)
a genus of Old World tropical vines in the gourd family, including species like bitter melon, known for their distinctive fruit
genus of Old World tropical vines

Examples

The Momordica genus includes the widely cultivated bitter melon.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Momordica in tropical environments.
Many traditional medicines use extracts from plants of the Momordica genus.

Etymology

From Latin 'momordicus', meaning 'having bitten', referring to the appearance of the seeds.

2. NOUN

/ moʊˈmɔrdɪkə / (AmE), / məʊˈmɔːdɪkə / (BrE)
a genus of Old World tropical vines in the gourd family, including several edible and medicinal species such as the bitter melon
genus of tropical vines (gourd family)

Examples

The genus Momordica is well known for producing bitter gourds, which are widely used in Asian cuisine.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the Momordica genus in the forests of Madagascar.
Many species in the Momordica genus are valued for their medicinal properties and unique fruits.

Etymology

From Latin 'momordicus', from 'momordere' (to bite), referring to the bitten appearance of the seed coats.
