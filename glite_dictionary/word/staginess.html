staginess

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪdʒi nɪs / (AmE), / ˈsteɪdʒɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being artificial, affected, or overly theatrical, as if intended for the stage rather than real life.
artificial or theatrical quality

Examples

The staginess of his performance made it hard to take the character seriously.
Critics complained about the staginess of the dialogue in the new play.
Despite its staginess, the film was praised for its emotional impact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificiality - the quality of being artificial rather than natural
  • Antonym: naturalness - the quality of being natural and unaffected

Etymology

From 'stagey' + '-ness'. 'Stagey' derives from 'stage', meaning theatrical.
