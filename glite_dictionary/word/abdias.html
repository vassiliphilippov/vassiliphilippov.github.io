Abdias

1. PROPN

/ ˈæb.di.əs / (AmE), / ˈab.dɪ.as / (BrE)
A Hebrew minor prophet in the Old Testament, known for prophesying against Edom.
Hebrew minor prophet; biblical figure

Examples

The book of Abdias contains a single chapter of prophetic warning.
Some scholars debate the historical identity of Abdias.
In the Old Testament, Abdias delivers a message condemning Edom.

Etymology

From Latin ‘Abdias’, from Greek ‘Abdías’, from Hebrew ‘Obhadyah’ meaning ‘servant of the Lord’.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæbdiəs / (AmE), / ˈabdiːas / (BrE)
another name for the Book of Obadiah, a short book in the Old Testament containing Obadiah's prophecies; noted as the shortest book in the Christian Bible.
variant name of the Book of Obadiah (Bible)

Examples

The book of Abdias contains only one chapter, making it unique among the Old Testament books.
Some older Bible translations refer to Obadiah as Abdias.
Biblical scholars often analyze the historical context of Abdias to understand its message.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Obadiah - an Old Testament book containing the prophecies of Obadiah

Etymology

From Latin 'Abdias', from Greek 'Abdías', from Hebrew 'Ovadyah' (servant of Yahweh).

Synset POS: n
