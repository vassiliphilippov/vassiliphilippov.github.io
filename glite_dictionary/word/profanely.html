profanely

1. ADV

/ prəˈfeɪnli / (AmE), / prəˈfeɪnli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows disrespect or irreverence, especially towards religious or sacred things.
in an irreverent or disrespectful way

Examples

The comedian spoke profanely about sacred subjects, which shocked some audience members.
He muttered profanely under his breath when things went wrong at work.
She criticized the ceremony profanely, offending several of the participants.

Synset Examples

he kept wondering profanely why everything bad happened to him

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reverently - in a deeply respectful manner
  • Synonym: blasphemously - in a way that shows disrespect for religious things

Etymology

From 'profane' + '-ly', with 'profane' from Latin 'profanus' (not sacred).

2. ADV

/ prəˈfeɪnli / (AmE), / prəˈfeɪnli / (BrE)
in a way that shows disrespect for something sacred, especially by using obscene or blasphemous language
in a blasphemous or obscene manner

Examples

He shouted profanely at the referee after the controversial call.
The drunken man was muttering profanely as he stumbled down the street.
She was reprimanded for speaking profanely during the meeting.

Synset Examples

muttering profanely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blasphemously - in a way that shows disrespect toward God or sacred things
  • Antonym: reverently - in a deeply respectful way, especially toward the sacred

Etymology

From Latin 'profanus' (unholy, not sacred) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
