breathing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbriðɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbriːðɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of inhaling air into the lungs and exhaling it, enabling oxygen to enter the body and carbon dioxide to be expelled
process of inhaling and exhaling air

Examples

After the long run, his breathing became heavy and rapid.
Practicing deep breathing can help reduce stress and anxiety.
The doctor listened carefully to her breathing during the examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respiration - the process of breathing, especially at the biological or cellular level

Etymology

From Old English brēothan (to breathe), with the suffix -ing forming a noun.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbriðɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbriːðɪŋ / (BrE)
passing air in and out of the lungs; living or capable of respiration; sometimes used to emphasize that someone or something is alive or vivid
passing air in and out of the lungs; living

Examples

The scientist described the cell as a breathing, functioning organism, not just a static structure.
Standing before the lion, he suddenly realized it was a breathing creature and not just a statue.
The nurse checked on the breathing patient throughout the night to monitor her recovery.

Synset Examples

the boy was disappointed to find only skeletons instead of living breathing dinosaurs
the heavy-breathing person on the telephone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: living - alive; existing; not dead
  • Antonym: dead - no longer alive

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'breathe,' from Old English 'brēothian.'
