incomputable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.kəmˈpjuː.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kəmˈpjuː.tə.bəl / (BrE)
impossible to calculate or measure; beyond computation
impossible to calculate or measure

Examples

The damage caused by the hurricane resulted in incomputable losses for local businesses.
There is an incomputable number of ways this ancient riddle could be interpreted.
Science often confronts phenomena whose complexity seems nearly incomputable with current technology.

Synset Examples

an incomputable amount
jewels of inestimable value
immeasurable wealth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immeasurable - too large, extensive, or extreme to measure
  • Synonym: incalculable - too great to be calculated or estimated

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'computabilis' (calculable).
