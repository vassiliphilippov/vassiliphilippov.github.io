subsequentness

1. NOUN

/ səbˈsɪkwəntnəs / (AmE), / səbˈsɪkwɪntnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being subsequent; the condition of following in time or order.
state of following in time

Examples

The subsequentness of the event made it difficult to anticipate.
Historians debate the subsequentness of the reforms relative to other changes.
In evaluating causes, we must consider the subsequentness of each action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: posteriority - the state of being later or after in time
  • Antonym: precedence - the condition of coming before in time or order

Etymology

From 'subsequent' + '-ness'. 'Subsequent' from Latin 'subsequens', present participle of 'subsequi' (to follow after).
