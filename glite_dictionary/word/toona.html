Toona

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːnə / (AmE), / ˈtuːnə / (BrE)
A genus of trees in the mahogany family, native to Asia and Australasia, formerly sometimes included in the genus Cedrela.
genus of trees in the mahogany family

Examples

The wood of Toona is valued for furniture and construction.
Several species of Toona are cultivated for their ornamental foliage.
Botanists have reclassified some plants formerly placed in Toona into other genera.

Etymology

From Hindi tūn, referring to Toona ciliata.

Examples
