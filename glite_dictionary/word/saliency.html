saliency

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪliənsi / (AmE), / ˈseɪliənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being particularly noticeable or important; prominence
quality of standing out; prominence

Examples

The saliency of the candidate's remarks attracted widespread media coverage.
Researchers studied the saliency of different features in the image to understand attention.
His speech had such saliency that it was remembered for years afterward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salience - the state or quality of being salient
  • Antonym: obscurity - the quality of being difficult to see, not prominent

Etymology

From Latin 'salire' (to leap), via 'salient' (leaping, prominent).
