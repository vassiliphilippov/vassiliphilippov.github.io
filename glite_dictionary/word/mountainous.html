mountainous

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaʊn.tən.əs / (AmE), / ˈmaʊn.tɪ.nəs / (BrE)
(of a region or terrain) containing many mountains; having a lot of mountains.
containing many mountains

Examples

The region is mountainous and difficult to traverse by car.
Switzerland is known for its mountainous landscapes and scenic views.
They settled in a mountainous area far from the city.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hilly - having many hills
  • Antonym: flat - with no mountains or hills; level

Etymology

From mountain + -ous, from Old French montaigne, from Latin montanea.

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈmaʊntənəs / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪnəs / (BrE)
Very large or impressive in size, like a mountain.
very large or impressive in size

Examples

The ship was rocked by mountainous waves during the storm.
They struggled to move the mountainous pile of paperwork on his desk.
A mountainous man stood in the doorway, blocking the light.

Synset Examples

mountainous waves
a mountainous dark man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huge - Extremely large in size or amount
  • Synonym: enormous - Very large; much bigger than usual
  • Antonym: tiny - Very small in size

Etymology

From mountain + -ous, forming an adjective meaning 'full of or resembling a mountain.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01392780-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈmaʊntənəs / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪnəs / (BrE)
(of a region or terrain) having many mountains or resembling a mountain; very hilly or craggy.
having many mountains or hills

Examples

The region is very mountainous, making transportation difficult.
They enjoy hiking through mountainous landscapes during their vacations.
The mountainous terrain provided a breathtaking view of the valley below.

Synset Examples

hilly terrain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hilly - having many hills, not flat

Etymology

From mountain + -ous, from Latin 'montanus' (of a mountain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02248234-s
Synset POS: s
