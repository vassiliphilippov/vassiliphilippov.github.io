biogenic

1. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.ə(ʊ)ˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (BrE)
Produced by living organisms or resulting from biological processes.
produced by living organisms

Examples

The methane found in swamps is often biogenic rather than of geological origin.
Scientists study biogenic emissions to better understand their impact on air quality.
Some minerals form through biogenic processes involving bacteria and algae.

Synset Examples

fermentation is a biogenic process

Etymology

From Greek bios (life) + -genic (producing or produced by).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02676202-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌbɑɪ.əʊˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (BrE)
Essential for maintaining fundamental life processes, especially in reference to factors required by living organisms.
essential for sustaining life

Examples

Water and oxygen are considered biogenic needs for most living organisms.
Scientists study biogenic elements to understand necessities for sustaining life.
Adequate sleep is one of the biogenic requirements for human health.

Synset Examples

sleep and food and water are among the biogenic needs of the organism

Etymology

From Greek bios (life) + -genic (producing or generated by).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00904335-s
Synset POS: s
