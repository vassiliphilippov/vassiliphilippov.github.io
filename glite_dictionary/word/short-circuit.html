to short-circuit

1. VERB

/ ˌʃɔrt ˈsɝːkɪt / (AmE), / ˌʃɔːt ˈsɜːkɪt / (BrE)
to create a short circuit in an electrical device or circuit, typically by causing a path of lower resistance to bypass the intended circuit
to create a short circuit

Examples

If you short-circuit the wires, the machine could be ruined.
A loose connection could easily short-circuit the entire system.
He accidentally short-circuited the toy when he spilled water on it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overload - damage by too much current
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01621903-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌʃɔrt ˈsɜr.kɪt / (AmE), / ˌʃɔːt ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
to avoid or circumvent (something unpleasant, difficult, or time-consuming), typically by finding a quick or easier way
bypass or avoid a difficulty

Examples

He tried to short-circuit the lengthy approval process by contacting the director directly.
Some companies attempt to short-circuit regulations, risking legal trouble in the process.
You can't simply short-circuit hard work if you want lasting results.

Synset Examples

You cannot bypass these rules!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bypass - to go around or avoid something

Etymology

Originally from the technical term for an electrical short circuit, used metaphorically for bypassing processes.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00812833-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌʃɔrt ˈsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / ˌʃɔːt ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
To prevent something from developing or progressing; to hamper or impede.
hinder or impede progress

Examples

Budget cuts could short-circuit the entire research project before it even starts.
Fear of failure may short-circuit your attempts to try new things.
New regulations could short-circuit the adoption of innovative technology.

Synset Examples

short-circuit warm feelings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impede - to delay or prevent by obstructing; hinder
  • Synonym: hinder - to create difficulties resulting in delay or obstruction
  • Antonym: advance - to move forward or make progress

Etymology

From the electrical term 'short circuit,' where the normal flow of current is interrupted or diverted.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
