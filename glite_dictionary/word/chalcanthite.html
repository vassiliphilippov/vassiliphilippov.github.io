chalcanthite

1. NOUN

/ kælˈkænθaɪt / (AmE), / kælˈkænθaɪt / (BrE)
a hydrated blue crystalline mineral form of copper sulfate, occurring naturally in arid environments
a blue mineral; hydrated copper sulfate

Examples

The mineral chalcanthite is prized for its vivid blue color and crystal formations.
Collectors often display chalcanthite alongside other colorful minerals in their cabinets.
Exposure to humidity can cause chalcanthite crystals to dissolve and lose their shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copper sulfate - a chemical compound made of copper, sulfur, and oxygen; often forming blue crystals

Etymology

From Greek chalkos (copper) and anthos (flower), referring to its crystal form.
