progymnosperm

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈdʒɪm.nəˌspɝm / (AmE), / prəʊˈdʒɪm.nə.spɜːm / (BrE)
an extinct group of ancient plants believed to be ancestral to modern gymnosperms, showing features of both ferns and seed plants
extinct ancestral plant to gymnosperms
medical

Examples

Fossil evidence suggests that progymnosperms were widespread during the Devonian period.
The structure of progymnosperm wood is remarkably similar to that of modern conifers.
Studying progymnosperms has helped scientists understand the evolution of seed-bearing plants.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gymnosperm - a plant of the group that includes conifers and their relatives

Etymology

From Greek 'pro-' (before) + 'gymnosperm', referring to an earlier evolutionary stage.
