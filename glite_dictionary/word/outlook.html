outlook

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌlʊk / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.lʊk / (BrE)
the act of looking out or observing from a vantage point
the act of looking out or observing

Examples

From the tower's outlook, you could see the entire valley spread beneath you.
The narrow window provided a poor outlook onto the busy street below.
He took up a position with the best outlook to watch for approaching ships.

Etymology

From out + look, originally meaning 'the act of looking out.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊt.lʊk / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.lʊk / (BrE)
a person's general attitude or expectation about what will happen in the future
general attitude or expectation about the future

Examples

Her positive outlook helped her stay hopeful despite difficult times.
The company revised its financial outlook for the year due to market changes.
With a more optimistic outlook, he approached new opportunities eagerly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prospect - the possibility or likelihood of some future event
  • Synonym: perspective - a particular attitude toward or way of regarding something
  • Antonym: pessimism - a tendency to see the worst aspect of things or believe the worst will happen
  • Antonym: optimism - hopefulness and confidence about the future

Etymology

From Middle English 'outlouk', from 'out' + 'look', literally meaning 'a looking out'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈaʊt.lʊk / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.lʊk / (BrE)
a habitual or characteristic attitude or way of thinking about life, situations, or the future
attitude or way of thinking

Examples

Despite her difficulties, Maria maintained a positive outlook on life.
Traveling to new countries can broaden your outlook and change how you see the world.
His pessimistic outlook made it hard for him to appreciate good news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perspective - a particular attitude toward or way of regarding something; a point of view
  • Synonym: attitude - a settled way of thinking or feeling about something
  • Antonym: pessimism - the tendency to expect the worst or see the worst aspect of things
  • Degree Higher: optimism - hopefulness and confidence about the future or the success of something

Etymology

From out + look, originally meaning 'a place with a view.'
