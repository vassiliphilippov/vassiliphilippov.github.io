Lambertia

1. NOUN

/ lӕmˈbɜːrtiə / (AmE), / lamˈbɜːtiə / (BrE)
a small genus of Australian shrubs in the family Proteaceae, known for their tubular flowers and leathery leaves
genus of Australian shrubs

Examples

The Lambertia species are often found in sandy soils of southwestern Australia.
Botanists studied the unique flowers of Lambertia during their fieldwork.
Gardeners appreciate Lambertia for its showy, bird-attracting blossoms.

Etymology

Named after English botanist Aylmer Bourke Lambert (1761–1842).
