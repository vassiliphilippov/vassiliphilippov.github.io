Capparidaceae

1. NOUN

/ kæˌpærɪˈdeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / kæˌparɪˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants in the order Brassicales, including genera such as Capparis, Cleome, Crateva, and Polanisia.
a family of flowering plants
medical

Examples

The Capparidaceae family comprises a variety of shrubs and small trees found in tropical regions.
Botanists once placed Cleome in the Capparidaceae, but recent studies have moved it to a different family.
Many species in Capparidaceae are known for their medicinal and culinary uses.

Etymology

From modern Latin Capparidaceae, based on the genus Capparis (caper plants).

2. NOUN

/ kæpˌɛrɪˈdeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / kæpˌærɪˈdeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants in the order Brassicales, including genera such as Capparis, Cleome, Crateva, and Polanisia
plant family including capers and relatives

Examples

The Capparidaceae family includes the well-known caper bush used in Mediterranean cuisine.
Recent genetic studies have led to the reclassification of some plants formerly in Capparidaceae.
Botanists collected several species from Capparidaceae during the field survey.

Etymology

From Capparis (the type genus) + Latin -aceae, a standard suffix for plant family names.
