Branchiobdella

1. NOUN

/ ˌbræŋkiˌɑbˈdɛlə / (AmE), / ˌbraŋkɪˌɒbˈdɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of small annelid worms that attach to the gills or surface of crayfish, often living as ectosymbionts.
genus of worms living on crayfish

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species of Branchiobdella inhabiting the freshwater crayfish in the lake.
Branchiobdella attach themselves to their host's gills using a specialized sucker.
The presence of Branchiobdella can indicate ecological interactions between worms and their crayfish hosts.

Etymology

From Greek 'branchia' (gills) + 'bdella' (leech).
