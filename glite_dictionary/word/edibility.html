edibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛdəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɛdɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being fit or safe to eat.
fitness or safety for eating

Examples

The edibility of wild mushrooms should always be verified before picking them.
Concerns about the edibility of processed foods have led to more food safety regulations.
She questioned the edibility of the leftovers after they had been in the fridge for a week.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inedibility - the quality of being not fit or safe to eat
  • Synonym: edible - fit or safe to eat (adjective)

Etymology

From Latin 'edibilis' (edible) + English suffix '-ity'.
