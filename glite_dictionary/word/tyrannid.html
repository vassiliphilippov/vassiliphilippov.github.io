tyrannid

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈrænɪd / (AmE), / tɪˈranɪd / (BrE)
A bird of the family Tyrannidae, the largest family of passerine birds found in the Americas, commonly known as tyrant flycatchers.
a member of the tyrant flycatcher bird family

Examples

The tyrannid is well known for its aggressive territorial behavior.
Researchers observed a rare species of tyrannid in the Amazon rainforest.
Several tyrannids were spotted feeding on insects near the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tyrant flycatcher - common name for birds in the family Tyrannidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Tyrannidae (family name), from Greek tyrannos (tyrant), referring to the aggressive habits of some species.
