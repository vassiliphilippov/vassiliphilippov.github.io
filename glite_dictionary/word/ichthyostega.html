Ichthyostega

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪkθi.oʊˈstiːɡə / (AmE), / ˌɪkθɪəʊˈstiːɡə / (BrE)
an extinct genus of early tetrapod amphibians from the Devonian period, known from fossils found in Greenland
extinct Devonian amphibian genus

Examples

The fossils of Ichthyostega provide important evidence of the transition from aquatic to terrestrial life.
Paleontologists believe that Ichthyostega had both gills and primitive lungs.
In the Devonian period, Ichthyostega inhabited the regions that are now Greenland.

Etymology

From Greek 'ichthys' (fish) and 'stegē' (roof), referring to its fish-like and reptilian features.

2. PROPN

/ ˌɪkθioʊˈstiɡə / (AmE), / ˌɪkθiəˈstiːɡə / (BrE)
an extinct genus of early tetrapod amphibians found in Greenland, significant for understanding the transition from water to land in vertebrate evolution
early extinct amphibian genus found in Greenland

Examples

Fossils of Ichthyostega provide crucial evidence for studying the evolution of vertebrates from water to land.
Paleontologists discovered new Ichthyostega remains in Devonian-aged rocks in Greenland.
The limb structure of Ichthyostega reveals a mix of aquatic and terrestrial adaptations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tetrapod - a four-limbed vertebrate animal
  • Synonym: amphibian - a cold-blooded vertebrate animal of a class that comprises frogs, toads, newts, and salamanders

Etymology

From Greek 'ichthys' (fish) + 'stegē' (roof), referencing features of its skull.
