cholestasis

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈlɛstəsɪs / (AmE), / kɒˈlɛstəsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the flow of bile from the liver is reduced or blocked.
reduced or blocked bile flow
medical

Examples

The patient developed cholestasis after taking certain medications, leading to itching and dark urine.
Obstruction of the bile ducts is a common cause of cholestasis.
Early diagnosis of cholestasis is important to prevent severe liver damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaundice - yellowing of the skin caused by excess bilirubin, often related to bile flow problems

Etymology

From Greek 'cholē' (bile) and 'stasis' (standing still, stoppage).
