irritable

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪrɪtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪtəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of living tissue or an organism) capable of reacting to stimuli.
able to react to stimuli (biology/medicine)
medical

Examples

Certain irritable tissues contract in response to a mild electric shock.
The study examined how irritable cells respond to different chemical signals.
Some plants are highly irritable and close their leaves quickly when touched.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪrɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪtəbl̩ / (BrE)
Easily annoyed or angered; likely to become impatient or upset.
easily annoyed or angered

Examples

After a long day at work, Mark was especially irritable and snapped at small annoyances.
Lack of sleep can make anyone irritable and short-tempered.
The irritable cat hissed whenever someone came near her.

Synset Examples

an incorrigibly fractious young man
not the least nettlesome of his countrymen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not easily angered or upset
  • Synonym: touchy - easily upset or offended

Etymology

From Latin 'irritabilis', meaning 'easily excited'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈɪrɪtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪtəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of a body, organ, or tissue) abnormally sensitive to stimuli or excitation.
abnormally sensitive to stimuli (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient's skin became irritable after repeated exposure to the chemical.
Certain plants are highly irritable and respond rapidly to touch.
Some people have irritable airways that react strongly to dust and pollen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensitive - quick to respond to slight changes or signals

Etymology

From Latin irritabilis (easily excited).
