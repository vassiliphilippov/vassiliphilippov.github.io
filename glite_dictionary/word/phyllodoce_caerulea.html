Phyllodoce caerulea

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈloʊdəˌsi siːəˈruːliə / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒdəˌsi siːəˈruːliə / (BrE)
a small evergreen shrub with tiny leaves and pink or purple flowers, found in alpine and subarctic regions of Asia, Europe, and North America
alpine shrub with purple or pink flowers

Examples

The delicate flowers of Phyllodoce caerulea brighten alpine meadows every spring.
Botanists recorded several clusters of Phyllodoce caerulea at the high-altitude research station.
Hikers spotted Phyllodoce caerulea growing among the rocks above the treeline.

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name Phyllodoce from Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) and 'dokhē' (receptacle); species epithet 'caerulea' from Latin for 'blue'.
