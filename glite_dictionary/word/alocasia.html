alocasia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæləˈkeɪziə / (AmE), / ˌaləˈkeɪsiə / (BrE)
A tropical plant of the genus Alocasia, known for its large, ornamental leaves and boat-shaped spathe.
a tropical plant genus with large leaves

Examples

The alocasia in her living room grew massive, glossy leaves that attracted everyone's attention.
Gardeners often prize alocasia for its striking, sculptural foliage and dramatic presence.
Many species of alocasia have adapted to thrive in humid, shaded tropical environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elephant ear - any plant with very large leaves, often referring to Alocasia or related genera
  • Synonym: colocasia - a related genus of aquatic plants, including taro

Etymology

From scientific Latin Alocasia, named from Malay.
