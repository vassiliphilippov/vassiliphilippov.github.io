scumble

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈskʌmbəl / (BrE)
a very thin or semi-opaque layer of color applied over a painting to soften or modify its appearance
thin layer of color in painting

Examples

The artist used a scumble to give the landscape a misty appearance.
You can achieve a softer look by applying a light scumble over the dried paint.
Her technique involved several layers of scumble to modify the colors underneath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glaze - a thin, transparent layer of paint

Etymology

From obsolete Dutch 'schommelen' (to smother, to cover).
