kettleful

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛt̬lˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈkɛtəlˌfʊl / (BrE)
The amount that a kettle can contain or hold.
amount a kettle holds

Examples

She poured a kettleful of boiling water into the teapot.
We need another kettleful of water to finish the cooking.
A single kettleful should be enough to fill all the mugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cupful - the amount a cup can contain
  • Synonym: teapotful - the quantity a teapot will hold

Etymology

Formed from 'kettle' + 'ful', by analogy with other measure nouns like 'cupful'.
