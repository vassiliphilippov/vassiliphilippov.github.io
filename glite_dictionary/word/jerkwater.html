jerkwater

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɝːkˌwɑtɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːkˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
(of a place or institution) small, remote, and considered unimportant or insignificant.
small, insignificant, and remote (place or institution)
informal american

Examples

He grew up in a jerkwater town in the Midwest far from major cities.
The company was dismissed as a jerkwater operation with little influence in the industry.
She left her jerkwater college for a university in a larger city.

Synset Examples

a jerkwater college
passed a series of poky little one-horse towns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insignificant - too small or unimportant to be worth consideration
  • Synonym: provincial - regarded as unsophisticated or narrow-minded, typically because of small-town or rural origin
  • Antonym: important - of great significance or value

Etymology

Originally referring to small railway towns where trains took on water by jerking a bucket from a water tank; from US railroad slang.
