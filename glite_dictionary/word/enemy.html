enemy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnəmi / (AmE), / ˈɛnəmi / (BrE)
A person who actively opposes or is hostile to someone.
a person who opposes or is hostile to someone

Examples

She considered her former colleague an enemy after their bitter dispute.
Over the years, he made more than one powerful enemy in the business world.
Forgiveness is difficult when your enemy has wronged you deeply.

Synset Examples

they had been political foes for years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foe - a person who is an enemy
  • Antonym: friend - a person with whom one has a bond of mutual affection

Etymology

From Old French 'enemi', from Latin 'inimicus' (hostile, unfriendly).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛnəmi / (AmE), / ˈɛnəmi / (BrE)
a group of people who are hostile to or in conflict with someone; an opposing group or force
hostile group or opposing force

Examples

During the war, the two nations became fierce enemies on the battlefield.
The politician accused the media of being his enemy in the upcoming election.
Throughout history, rival families have regarded each other as enemies.

Synset Examples

he viewed lawyers as the real enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foe - an enemy or opponent
  • Antonym: ally - a person or group that cooperates with or helps another

Etymology

From Old French *enemi*, from Latin *inimicus* (hostile, unfriendly), from *in-* (not) + *amicus* (friend).

4. NOUN

/ ˈɛnəmi / (AmE), / ˈɛnəmi / (BrE)

an armed adversary, especially in war

Examples

A good commander must anticipate the strategies of the enemy.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foe - an enemy or opponent, especially in war
  • Antonym: ally - a person or group that cooperates with or helps another, especially in a war

Etymology

From Old French *enemi* (enemy), from Latin *inimicus* (unfriendly, hostile), from *in-* (not) + *amicus* (friend).
