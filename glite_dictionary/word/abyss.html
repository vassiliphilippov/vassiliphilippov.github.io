abyss

1. NOUN

/ əˈbɪs / (AmE), / əˈbɪs / (BrE)
A deep or seemingly bottomless chasm; an immeasurable or profound void, often used figuratively for something immense or unfathomable.
bottomless chasm or void

Examples

He stared into the abyss, unable to see the bottom through the darkness.
After the financial crisis, the company found itself on the edge of an abyss.
Her loneliness sometimes felt like an endless abyss from which she could not escape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chasm - a deep fissure in the earth or in another surface
  • Synonym: gulf - a deep ravine or abyss; a wide separation
  • Synonym: pit - a large hole in the ground, sometimes used for very deep places

Etymology

From Greek abyssos (bottomless), from a- (without) + byssos (bottom).
