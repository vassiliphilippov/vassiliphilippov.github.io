retardation

1. NOUN

/ ˌritɑrˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːtɑːˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
a decrease in the speed, rate, or progress of something; slowing down.
slowing down or decrease in rate

Examples

Friction causes a noticeable retardation in the movement of the vehicle.
The new policy led to a retardation in the growth of the economy.
Measuring the retardation of the reaction helped scientists understand its underlying mechanisms.

Synset Examples

the deceleration of the arms race

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceleration - a reduction in speed or rate

Etymology

From Latin 'retardatio', from 'retardare' (to delay).

2. NOUN

/ ˌriː.tɑːrˈdeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.tɑːˈdeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the action or process of delaying or being delayed; the extent to which something is slowed down or held back
the extent of delay or slowing down

Examples

The scientists measured the retardation of the chemical reaction under low temperatures.
Any retardation in the delivery schedule could cause significant problems for the project.
The material shows significant retardation of water flow due to its dense structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acceleration - an increase in the rate or speed of something

Etymology

From Latin retardatio (a delaying), from retardare (to delay).

3. NOUN

/ ˌriːtɑːrˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːtɑːˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of slowing down or causing something to move or happen more slowly than usual
the act of slowing down

Examples

The chemical process involves retardation of the reaction with certain inhibitors.
A sudden retardation in sales growth concerned the company's managers.
Engineers measured the retardation of the train as it approached the station.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acceleration - the process of increasing speed
  • Synonym: delay - the act of making something happen later than planned

Etymology

From Latin retardatio (a delaying), from retardo (to delay).

Examples

In the past, schools used the term retardation, but now they prefer 'intellectual disability'.
A team of specialists assessed her for potential retardation due to genetic factors.

Related Terms
