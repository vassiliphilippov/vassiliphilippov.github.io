chimney

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪmni / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪmni / (BrE)
a glass tube or enclosure surrounding the flame of an oil lamp to protect it and improve combustion
glass tube on an oil lamp

Examples

The old lantern still had its original chimney despite years of use.
Make sure the chimney is placed carefully over the wick before lighting the lamp.
He cleaned the chimney of the oil lamp to ensure a brighter flame.

Etymology

From Old French *cheminée* (fireplace, chimney), from Latin *caminus* (furnace, fireplace).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪmni / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪmni / (BrE)
A vertical structure on a building that provides a passage for smoke or gases from a fire to escape outside.
vertical structure for smoke ventilation

Examples

Smoke rose steadily from the old brick chimney atop the cottage.
Birds sometimes make their nests inside an unused chimney.
The house needed repairs after a storm damaged the chimney.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flue - a duct for smoke and waste gases produced by a fire, a gas heater, a power station, or other source of combustion
  • Synonym: vent - an opening that allows air, gas, or liquid to pass out of or into a confined space

Etymology

From Old French 'cheminée', from Late Latin 'caminata' (fireplace, chimney), from Latin 'caminus' (furnace, fireplace).
