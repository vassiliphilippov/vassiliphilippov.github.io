shackle

1. VERB

/ ˈʃækəl / (AmE), / ˈʃækəl / (BrE)
to restrain or bind someone's arms or legs with shackles or chains, especially to limit their freedom
restrain with shackles or chains

Examples

The guards shackled the prisoner before taking him to the courtroom.
They threatened to shackle anyone who tried to escape from the camp.
Protesters claimed the new laws would shackle their freedom of expression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrain - to prevent someone or something from moving freely
  • Antonym: free - to release from confinement or restraint

Etymology

From Middle English schakel, from Old English sceacul (fetter, shackle).

2. VERB

/ ˈʃækəl / (AmE), / ˈʃækəl / (BrE)
to restrain or confine, especially by binding the ankles or wrists with chains or fetters
to restrain with chains or fetters

Examples

The prisoners were shackled by their captors before being transported.
Heavy chains shackled his ankles, limiting his ability to move.
They feared that new regulations would shackle innovation in the industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrain - to limit or control the actions or movement of someone or something
  • Antonym: to unbind - to release from restraint, bonds, or shackles

Etymology

From Middle English 'schakle', from Old English 'sceacul' (fetter, shackle).

3. NOUN

/ ˈʃækəl / (AmE), / ˈʃækəl / (BrE)
a U-shaped metal bar with a removable pin used to link chain or rope, especially in lifting or securing objects
U-shaped metal fastener

Examples

The towing cable was secured with a heavy shackle to the vehicle's frame.
A rusted shackle joined the anchor chain to the boat.
Before lifting, the workers inspected each shackle for signs of wear or damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clevis - a U-shaped fastening device secured by a pin, similar to a shackle

Etymology

From Middle English 'shakel' (fetter), from Old English 'sceacul'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈʃækəl / (AmE), / ˈʃækəl / (BrE)
a metal fastening, usually one of a pair, for securing a person's wrists or ankles together; any restraint that confines or restricts freedom
a metal restraint for confining movement

Examples

The prisoner struggled against the shackle binding his ankles.
He worked to break free from the shackles of his oppressive upbringing.
A single shackle lay rusted on the floor of the abandoned cell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fetter - a chain or manacle used to restrain a prisoner, typically placed around the ankles
  • Synonym: chain - a series of linked metal rings used for fastening or restraining
  • Antonym: liberty - the state of being free within society from oppressive restrictions

Etymology

From Middle English 'schakel', from Old English 'sceacel' (a shackle, fetter).
