Ananias

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈnaɪəs / (AmE), / ˌænəˈnaɪəs / (BrE)
a person who habitually lies; a liar, especially one who lies brazenly or flagrantly
habitual liar (from the Bible)

Examples

In the debate, he was called an Ananias for his repeated fabrications.
The novelist portrayed the corrupt politician as a modern Ananias.
After hearing his excuse, she dismissed him as an Ananias unworthy of her trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liar - a person who tells lies
  • Antonym: truth-teller - a person who tells the truth

Etymology

From the name Ananias, a character in the New Testament (Acts 5), who was struck dead for lying.
