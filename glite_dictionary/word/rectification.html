rectification

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛktəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of determining the length of a curve, especially by finding a straight line equal in length to the curve
determining curve length in mathematics

Examples

The rectification of a circle's circumference is a classic problem in calculus.
He studied the rectification of curves as part of his research in differential geometry.
Modern mathematics provides several methods for the rectification of complex curves.

Etymology

From Latin 'rectificare' (to make right, to straighten).
