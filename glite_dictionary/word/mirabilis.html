Mirabilis

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈræbɪlɪs / (AmE), / mɪˈræbɪlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Nyctaginaceae, commonly known as four o'clocks, native to the Americas.

Related Terms

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪrəˈbaɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɪərəˈbɪlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants, commonly known as four o'clocks, native to the Americas and valued for their colorful, fragrant, evening-blooming flowers.
genus of flowering plants (four o'clocks)

Examples

The Mirabilis blooms in the evening, filling the garden with its sweet fragrance.
Gardeners often plant Mirabilis for its vibrant and varied flower colors.
Certain varieties of Mirabilis can self-seed and return year after year.

Related Terms
