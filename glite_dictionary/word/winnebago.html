Winnebago

1. NOUN

/ ˌwɪnəˈbeɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌwɪnəˈbeɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people formerly living in eastern Wisconsin, historically Siouan-speaking and associated with the region south of Green Bay.
member of a Native American people of Wisconsin

Examples

The Winnebago were known for their traditional crafts and close ties to the land in Wisconsin.
During the 17th century, the Winnebago had alliances with the Menomini tribe.
Many Winnebago descendants now identify as Ho-Chunk Nation members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ho-Chunk - self-designation for the people formerly known as Winnebago

Etymology

From Algonquian, originally referring to people living near Lake Winnebago.

2. NOUN

/ ˌwɪnəˈbeɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌwɪnɪˈbeɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
the Siouan language traditionally spoken by the Winnebago (Ho-Chunk) people of the United States.
the Siouan language of the Winnebago people

Examples

Few people today are fluent speakers of Winnebago due to language loss.
Researchers have compiled a dictionary of the Winnebago language.
She learned Winnebago from her grandparents and now teaches it to children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ho-Chunk - the Ho-Chunk people, also known as the Winnebago

Etymology

From the name 'Winnebago,' which refers to the Ho-Chunk people, likely derived from a word in an Algonquian language meaning 'people of the dirty water.'
