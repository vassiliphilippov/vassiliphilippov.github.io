wrestle

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈrɛsəl / (BrE)
to struggle determinedly to overcome a difficult problem or internal conflict
struggle to overcome a problem

Examples

She continued to wrestle with her conscience after the difficult decision.
Many scientists still wrestle with the unanswered mysteries of the universe.
He wrestled with self-doubt before his major presentation.

Synset Examples

He wrestled all his life with his feeling of inferiority

Related Terms

  • Synonym: struggle - to make forceful or violent efforts to get free of restraint or resist attack

Etymology

From Old English *wraestlian*, related to 'wrest' (to twist, to force by twisting).

WordNet ID: oewn-01095392-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈrɛsəl / (BrE)
an act of engaging in close hand-to-hand combat, often as a sport or struggle
an act of close hand-to-hand combat

Examples

The two boys had a quick wrestle in the backyard before dinner.
After a long and exhausting wrestle, the champion finally pinned his opponent.
Their argument almost turned physical, but it ended with just a brief wrestle.

Synset Examples

they had a fierce wrestle
we watched his grappling and wrestling with the bully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrestling - the sport or activity of wrestle; grappling

Etymology

From Old English *wrestlian* (to wrestle), related to *wrǣstan* (to twist, wrench).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00623506-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈrɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈrɛsəl / (BrE)
to twist and struggle with movements of the body, often in an attempt to escape or as a reaction to discomfort or restraint
to twist and struggle the body

Examples

The injured bird wrestled in the grass, trying to get back on its feet.
The toddler wrestled in his sleep, turning and twisting under the blankets.
She wrestled with the tangled ropes, barely able to move her arms.

Synset Examples

The prisoner writhed in discomfort
The child tried to wriggle free from his aunt's embrace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to writhe - to twist the body, especially as from pain or struggle
  • Synonym: wriggle - to twist and turn the body with quick movements

Etymology

From Old English *wræstlian* (to contend, struggle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01872363-v
Synset POS: v

/ ˈrɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈrɛsəl / (BrE)
to fight with someone by holding and trying to throw or force them to the ground, especially as a sport; to engage in a wrestling match
engage in a wrestling match

She used to wrestle competitively while she was in college.

Related Terms

5. VERB

/ ˈrɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈrɛsəl / (BrE)
to struggle mentally with a difficult problem, decision, or idea over a period of time
struggle mentally with a problem or decision

Examples

She had to wrestle with her conscience before making the final decision.
He spent the night wrestling with the question of whether to move abroad.
For weeks, I've wrestled with how to deliver the bad news.

Synset Examples

I wrestled with this decision for years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: struggle - to try hard to deal with or overcome a problem
  • Synonym: grapple - to struggle with or try hard to deal with something difficult

Etymology

From Old English *wrestlian*, related to *wræstan* (to twist).
