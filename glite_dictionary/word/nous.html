nous

1. NOUN

/ naʊs / (AmE), / naʊs / (BrE)
Practical intelligence or common sense; the ability to make good judgments and decisions.
common sense; practical intelligence
british

Examples

She has great social nous and always knows the right thing to say.
It takes a bit of financial nous to make wise investments.
His lack of nous led to several poor decisions at work.

Synset Examples

she has great social nous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sense - capacity for sound practical judgment
  • Synonym: wit - keen intelligence and judgment

Etymology

From Ancient Greek νοῦς (noûs), meaning 'mind' or 'intellect,' via French and late Latin.

2. NOUN

/ nuːs / (AmE), / naʊs / (BrE)
the faculty of the mind that enables reasoning, perception, and conscious thought; intellect or intelligence, especially in philosophical contexts
faculty of intellect or reasoning

Examples

Plato believed that the highest part of the soul is the nous.
Her philosophical writings discuss the nature of nous and consciousness.
Many scientists argue that nous distinguishes humans from other animals.

Synset Examples

his mind wandered
I couldn't get his words out of my head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mind - the element of a person that enables thinking, feeling, and awareness
  • Synonym: intellect - the faculty of reasoning and understanding objectively

Etymology

From Greek nous (mind, intellect) via Latin.
