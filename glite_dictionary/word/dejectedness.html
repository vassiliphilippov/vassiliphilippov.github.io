dejectedness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈdʒɛktɪdnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛktɪdnɪs / (BrE)
A feeling of unhappiness and low spirits; the state of being dejected.
a feeling of low spirits

Examples

A wave of dejectedness swept over her after hearing the disappointing news.
There was a sense of dejectedness in his posture as he left the meeting.
After failing the exam, Tom's dejectedness was evident to all his friends.

Synset Examples

he felt responsible for her lowness of spirits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dejection - the state of being dejected or in low spirits
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the state of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Latin 'dejectus', past participle of 'deicere' (to cast down), plus the English suffix '-ness'.
