pubescence

1. NOUN

/ pjuˈbɛsəns / (AmE), / pjuːˈbɛsns / (BrE)
the period in a person's life when the sex glands become functional and secondary sexual characteristics develop; the onset of puberty
period when puberty begins

Examples

The onset of pubescence marks significant physical and emotional changes in adolescents.
During pubescence, adolescents often experience growth spurts and voice changes.
Researchers studied the effects of nutrition on the timing of pubescence in different populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puberty - the period during which adolescents reach sexual maturity

Etymology

From Latin pubescentia (the state of reaching maturity), from pubescere (to reach puberty).
