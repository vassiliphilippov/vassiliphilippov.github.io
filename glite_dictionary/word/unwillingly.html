unwillingly

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈwɪlɪŋli / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɪlɪŋli / (BrE)
in a reluctant or unenthusiastic manner; without wanting to do something
in a reluctant way

Examples

She unwillingly agreed to attend the meeting after much persuasion.
He unwillingly handed over the keys to his car.
Children often do chores unwillingly, wishing they were playing instead.

Synset Examples

he had sinned against her unwillingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reluctantly - in a way that shows hesitation or unwillingness

Etymology

Formed from 'unwilling' + '-ly'.
