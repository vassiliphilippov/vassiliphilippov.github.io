tisane

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈzæn / (AmE), / tɪˈzæn / (BrE)
An infusion made from dried or fresh flowers, leaves, herbs, or other plant material, usually consumed as a beverage.
herbal infusion beverage

Examples

The hotel offered a selection of tisane after dinner to help aid digestion.
She brewed a calming tisane using chamomile and mint from her garden.
Instead of coffee, he ordered a tisane to enjoy a caffeine-free drink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herbal tea - a drink made by infusing herbs, not true tea leaves

Etymology

From French 'tisane', from Latin 'ptisana' (a drink made from barley), ultimately from Greek 'ptisánē'.
