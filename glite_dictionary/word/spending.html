spending

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of paying out or using money for goods or services.
the act of using money

Examples

The government's spending on healthcare has increased significantly this year.
During the holidays, household spending usually rises as people buy gifts and decorations.
They are trying to reduce unnecessary spending to save money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expenditure - the act of spending money or using up resources

Etymology

From the verb 'spend' + -ing, used as a noun.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
The amount of money paid out or expended, especially by a government, organization, or individual.
money paid out; expenditure

Examples

Government spending on education has increased significantly this year.
We need to cut down on unnecessary spending if we want to save money.
The company's spending is closely monitored by the finance department.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expenditure - the act of spending money; an amount spent

Etymology

From the verb 'spend', from Old English spendan, from Latin expendere (to pay out).
