to misidentify

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪs.aɪˈdɛn.tɪ.faɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.aɪˈdɛn.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to identify someone or something incorrectly
identify incorrectly

Examples

The witness may misidentify the suspect during the police lineup.
Sometimes people misidentify edible mushrooms as poisonous varieties, leading to dangerous mistakes.
It's easy to misidentify birds when watching them from a distance.

Synset Examples

Don't mistake her for her twin sister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistake - to incorrectly take for something or someone else
  • Synonym: misrecognize - to fail to recognize correctly

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'mis-' meaning 'wrongly' and 'identify'.
