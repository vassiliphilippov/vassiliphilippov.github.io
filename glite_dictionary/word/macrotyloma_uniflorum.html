Macrotyloma uniflorum

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.roʊˈtɪl.ə.mə ˌjuː.nəˈflɔr.əm / (AmE), / ˌmak.rəʊˈtɪl.ə.mə ˌjuː.nɪˈflɔː.rəm / (BrE)
a twining herb native to the Old World tropics, cultivated in India for food and fodder, also known as horse gram
Old World tropical herb; horse gram

Examples

Macrotyloma uniflorum is a drought-resistant crop commonly cultivated in southern India.
The seeds of Macrotyloma uniflorum are used in traditional Indian cuisine and animal feed.
Agricultural studies have highlighted the nutritional benefits of Macrotyloma uniflorum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse gram - an edible legume native to India, alternative name for Macrotyloma uniflorum

Etymology

From Latinized botanical Latin; genus name from Greek makrós (large) + tylos (knot, swelling), species epithet from Latin uni- (one) + florum (flower).
