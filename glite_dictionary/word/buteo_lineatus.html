Buteo lineatus

1. NOUN

/ buːˈtiːoʊ laɪˈneɪətəs / (AmE), / bjuːˈtiːəʊ lɪˈneɪətəs / (BrE)
A North American hawk species with reddish-brown shoulders, commonly known as the Red-shouldered Hawk.
North American hawk species

Examples

We spotted a Buteo lineatus soaring above the forest during our birdwatching trip.
The Buteo lineatus is recognizable by its distinctive reddish-brown shoulders and loud call.
A Buteo lineatus perched on the branch, scanning the ground for its next meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Red-shouldered Hawk - Common name for Buteo lineatus, a North American hawk with reddish-brown shoulders.

Etymology

From Latin: 'buteo' (a type of hawk) and 'lineatus' (lined, striped).
