pureness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊrnəs / (AmE), / ˈpjʊənəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being morally pure or free from sin or wrongdoing
state of being morally pure

Examples

Many admired her pureness and unwavering commitment to honesty.
The pureness of his heart was evident in all his actions.
They viewed childhood as a time of innocence and pureness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purity - the quality of being morally or physically clean, pure

Etymology

From pure + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊrnəs / (AmE), / ˈpjʊənəs / (BrE)
The quality of being morally clean, specifically referring to a woman's virtue or chastity.
chastity; moral innocence (esp. of a woman)

Examples

Some ancient cultures placed a high value on a woman's pureness before marriage.
The heroine was admired throughout the novel for her unwavering pureness.
Certain traditional societies judged women harshly if their pureness was ever questioned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chastity - the state of being sexually pure or abstinent
  • Antonym: impurity - lack of moral or sexual purity

Etymology

Formed from 'pure' + '-ness'; 'pure' from Latin 'purus' (clean, clear).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊrnəs / (AmE), / ˈpjʊənɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being pure, undiluted, or unmixed with anything else
the state of being pure or unmixed

Examples

The pureness of the mountain water made it safe to drink without any treatment.
She admired the pureness of his intentions in helping the poor.
Scientists tested the pureness of the chemical sample before using it in the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purity - the state of being pure
  • Antonym: impurity - the state of being impure or mixed with other elements

Etymology

From pure + -ness, after Middle English purenesse.
