conformist

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfɔrmɪst / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːmɪst / (BrE)
following established customs, rules, or styles; not willing to challenge or change accepted standards.
adhering to accepted rules or customs

Examples

Despite her creative talent, her artwork is surprisingly conformist in style.
The conformist attitude among students discouraged any new ideas from emerging.
His views became more conformist as he grew older and settled into a routine.

Synset Examples

underneath the radical image teenagers are surprisingly conformist

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonconformist - someone or something not conforming to established customs or rules
  • Synonym: orthodox - conforming to traditional or established beliefs

Etymology

From 'conform' + '-ist', influenced by French 'conformiste'.

2. ADJ

/ kənˈfɔrmɪst / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːmɪst / (BrE)
following established customs or accepted standards, especially in religion or society; conventional.
conventional, following accepted standards

Examples

His views are decidedly conformist, always aligning with the dominant beliefs in his community.
The school encouraged a conformist attitude, discouraging students from questioning established traditions.
In a conformist society, challenging religious doctrine can be difficult and even risky.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonconformist - a person who does not conform to established customs or doctrines
  • Synonym: traditionalist - a person who adheres to traditional ideas or practices

Etymology

From 'conform' (to comply with rules or standards) + '-ist' (noun-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01694901-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ kənˈfɔrmɪst / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːmɪst / (BrE)
a person who conforms to accepted rules, standards, or customs, especially in matters of conduct or religious practices
person who conforms to accepted norms

Examples

He was considered a conformist who rarely questioned authority.
Most members of the group were conformists unwilling to challenge social norms.
In religious communities, a conformist typically follows established rituals and beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonconformist - a person who does not conform to accepted standards or customs
  • Synonym: traditionalist - a person who supports or upholds tradition

Etymology

From Late Latin conformis (of like form) + -ist (one who practices or is concerned with).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09977093-n
Synset POS: n
