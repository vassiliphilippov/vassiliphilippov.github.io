Araceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / ˌɑːrəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, including species such as anthurium, calla lily, jack-in-the-pulpit, and philodendron, characterized by having flowers arranged on a spike surrounded by a sheath-like spathe
a family of flowering plants (anthurium, calla lily, etc.)
medical

Examples

The Araceae family includes popular houseplants such as the philodendron and the peace lily.
Botanists often study the diverse floral structures found in the Araceae.
Many members of the Araceae are cultivated for their striking foliage and unusual flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin Araceae, from Arum, the type genus.
