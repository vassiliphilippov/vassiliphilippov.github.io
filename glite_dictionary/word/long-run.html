long-run

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔŋ ˌrʌn / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋ ˌrʌn / (BrE)
relating to or extending over a relatively long period of time, especially in contrast with the immediate or short-term
extending over a long period of time

Examples

The company is focusing on its long-run prospects rather than short-term profits.
Many economists study the long-run effects of policy changes on the economy.
Investments in education yield significant long-run benefits for society.

Synset Examples

the long-run significance of the elections
the long-term reconstruction of countries damaged by the war
a long-term investment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short-run - relating to a short period of time
  • Synonym: long-term - lasting or extending over a long period; often interchangeable with 'long-run'
