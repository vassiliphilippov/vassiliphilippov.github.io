deoxythymidine monophosphate

1. NOUN

/ diˌɑksiˌθaɪmɪˌdin ˌmɑnəˈfɑsˌfeɪt / (AmE), / diˌɒksɪˌθɪmɪˌdiːn ˌmɒnəˈfɒsˌfeɪt / (BrE)
a nucleotide consisting of a thymine base attached to a deoxyribose sugar and a single phosphate group, used as a building block of DNA
nucleotide building block of DNA
medical

Examples

During DNA replication, deoxythymidine monophosphate pairs with deoxyadenosine monophosphate in the growing DNA strand.
Biochemists can quantify deoxythymidine monophosphate to measure DNA synthesis rates.
The mutation affected the enzyme needed to produce deoxythymidine monophosphate in the cell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nucleotide - basic structural unit of nucleic acids like DNA and RNA
  • Synonym: deoxyribonucleotide - any nucleotide containing deoxyribose, a component of DNA

Etymology

Formed from 'deoxy-' (removal of an oxygen atom), 'thymidine' (nucleoside with thymine base), and 'monophosphate' (one phosphate group).
