Rappahannock

1. PROPN

/ ˌræpəˈhænək / (AmE), / ˌrapəˈhanək / (BrE)
a river in eastern Virginia, United States, flowing eastward into the Chesapeake Bay
river in eastern Virginia, USA

Examples

The Rappahannock River played a significant role in the American Civil War.
We drove along the Rappahannock during our trip through Virginia.
Fishing is popular in the Rappahannock due to its rich aquatic life.

Etymology

From an Algonquian word possibly meaning 'river of quick rising water.'
