quickly

1. ADV

/ ˈkwɪk.li / (AmE), / ˈkwɪk.li / (BrE)
At a fast speed; rapidly.
at a fast speed

Examples

He finished the exam quickly and left the room before anyone else.
If you act quickly, you may still catch the early train.
The chef prepared the meal quickly despite the busy kitchen.

Synset Examples

he works quickly
John got ready in short order

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapidly - at great speed; very quickly
  • Antonym: slowly - at a slow speed

Etymology

From 'quick' + '-ly', where 'quick' is from Old English 'cwic' (alive, lively).

2. ADV

/ ˈkwɪkli / (AmE), / ˈkwɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is done with little care or attention; hastily or cursory.
without taking pains; hastily

Examples

He flipped through the pages quickly, barely glancing at the headlines.
She signed the document quickly and handed it back without reading it carefully.
The manager quickly checked the report before the meeting, missing a few key details.

Synset Examples

he looked cursorily through the magazine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hastily - in a hurried or careless manner
  • Antonym: carefully - with great attention and thoroughness
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00292249-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈkwɪkli / (AmE), / ˈkwɪkli / (BrE)
In a rapid manner; with little or no delay.
at a rapid speed; without delay

Examples

She finished her homework quickly so she could watch her favorite show.
The fire spread quickly through the dry forest.
Please respond quickly if you are able to attend the meeting.

Synset Examples

the rescue squad arrived promptly
come here, quick!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapidly - at a high speed; very quickly
  • Antonym: slowly - in a slow manner; not quickly

Etymology

Formed from 'quick' (Old English *cwic*, 'alive, lively') + -ly suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00106290-r
Synset POS: r
