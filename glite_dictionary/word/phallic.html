phallic

1. ADJ

/ ˈfælɪk / (AmE), / ˈfælɪk / (BrE)
resembling or relating to a phallus, often used to describe objects or symbols that are shaped like or evoke the male genitalia.
resembling a phallus; penis-shaped

Examples

Ancient cultures often used phallic symbols to represent fertility and power.
The sculpture included several phallic shapes incorporated into its design.
Some plants, like the banana, are frequently described as phallic due to their form.

Synset Examples

a phallic symbol
phallic eroticism
priapic figurines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priapic - relating to or resembling a phallus, especially in reference to male sexuality

Etymology

From Greek phallikos, from phallos (phallus).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfælɪk / (AmE), / ˈfælɪk / (BrE)
relating to or resembling a phallus, especially as a symbol of generative power or fertility
relating to a phallus or its symbolism

Examples

The ancient statue had a distinctly phallic shape, symbolizing fertility and creation.
Certain rituals in history have featured phallic symbols to invoke generative power.
The novel makes subtle references to phallic imagery in its exploration of masculinity.

Synset Examples

a phallic cult
phallic worship

Related Terms

  • Antonym: yonic - relating to or resembling a vulva, symbolic counterpart to 'phallic'

Etymology

From Greek phallikos, from phallos (phallus).
