Mantinea

1. PROPN

/ ˌmæn.tɪˈni.ə / (AmE), / ˌmæn.tɪˈniː.ə / (BrE)
an ancient town in the Peloponnese of Greece, famous as the site of three major battles between Greek city-states in 418 BC, 362 BC, and 207 BC
ancient Greek town, site of major battles

Examples

Many historians study the Battle of Mantinea as a turning point in the Peloponnesian War.
During their travels in Greece, they visited the ruins of ancient Mantinea.
The significance of Mantinea lies in the pivotal battles fought there between rival Greek states.
