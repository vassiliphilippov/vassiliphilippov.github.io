to resize

1. VERB

/ riːˈsaɪz / (AmE), / riːˈsaɪz / (BrE)
to change the size of something, especially to make it more suitable or appropriate
change the size of something

Examples

You may need to resize the image before uploading it to the website.
She resized the window to fit both documents side by side.
I am resizing the table to make it easier for four people to sit.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: to enlarge - to make something bigger
  • Degree Lower: shrink - to make or become smaller in size

Etymology

From re- (again) + size (from Old French sise, meaning 'size').
