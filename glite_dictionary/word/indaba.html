indaba

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɑːbə / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɑːbə / (BrE)
A meeting or council among indigenous peoples of southern Africa, especially to discuss important matters.
council meeting of southern African peoples

Examples

The community elders called an indaba to address the water crisis.
Representatives from several tribes attended the indaba to resolve longstanding disputes.
The annual indaba focused on preserving cultural traditions and languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: council - a formal meeting for discussion and decision-making

Etymology

From Zulu *indaba* (matter, affair, council).
