Gettysburg

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡɛtizəˌbɝɡ / (AmE), / ˈɡɛtɪzbɜːɡ / (BrE)
A small town in southern Pennsylvania, USA, known as the site of a major Civil War battle and a national cemetery.
town in southern Pennsylvania, Civil War site

Examples

The Battle of Gettysburg was a turning point in the American Civil War.
Thousands of tourists visit Gettysburg each year to learn about its history.
President Lincoln delivered the Gettysburg Address at the national cemetery in Gettysburg.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɡɛtisˌbɜrɡ / (AmE), / ˈɡɛtɪzbɜːɡ / (BrE)
a major battle of the American Civil War fought in 1863, resulting in a significant Union victory over the Confederate forces.
Civil War battle, 1863
american

Examples

The Battle of Gettysburg marked a turning point in the American Civil War.
Many tourists visit Gettysburg to walk the famous battlefield and learn about its history.
Abraham Lincoln delivered the Gettysburg Address shortly after the victory at Gettysburg.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Antietam - another major Civil War battle
  • Synonym: Waterloo - site of a decisive battle, often referenced similarly

Etymology

Named after the town of Gettysburg in Pennsylvania, where the battle took place.
