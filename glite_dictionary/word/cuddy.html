cuddy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌdi / (AmE), / ˈkʌdi / (BrE)
the galley or pantry of a small ship, used for preparing and storing food
galley or pantry of a small ship

Examples

The captain opened the cuddy to check the provisions for the next leg of the journey.
After a stormy night, the crew gathered in the cuddy to share a meal and dry off.
She stored the fresh bread in the cuddy to keep it safe from the salty air.

Etymology

From Scottish dialect, possibly derived from 'cuddy' meaning 'hut' or 'small room'.
