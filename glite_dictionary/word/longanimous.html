longanimous

1. ADJ

/ lɔŋˈænəməs / (AmE), / lɒŋˈænɪməs / (BrE)
showing patient and unruffled self-control and restraint under adversity; slow to retaliate or express resentment
patient and unruffled under adversity

Examples

Despite the harsh criticism, she remained longanimous and refused to retaliate.
He was admired for his longanimous response to repeated provocation.
In times of trouble, a longanimous spirit can inspire others to endure adversity.

Synset Examples

seemly and forbearing … yet strong enough to resist aggression
was longanimous in the face of suffering

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forbearing - patiently restraining from anger or retaliation
  • Degree Lower: patient - able to accept or tolerate delays, problems, or suffering
  • Antonym: implacable - unable to be calmed down or appeased; unable to forgive

Etymology

From Latin 'longanimitas', from 'longus' (long) + 'animus' (mind, spirit).
