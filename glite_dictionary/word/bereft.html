bereft

1. ADJ

/ bəˈrɛft / (AmE), / bɪˈrɛft / (BrE)
Sad, lonely, or deprived because of the loss or absence of something or someone.
deprived and sorrowful; lacking something

Examples

She felt utterly bereft after the unexpected loss of her beloved dog.
The village was bereft of any form of entertainment for the youth.
He stared at the empty room, bereft of memories and warmth.

Synset Examples

bereft of hope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deprived - lacking the necessities of life; suffering from deprivation
  • Synonym: forlorn - sad and abandoned or lonely
  • Antonym: hopeful - having or showing hope

Etymology

Past participle of 'bereave', from Old English 'berēafian' (to deprive).

2. ADJ

/ bɪˈrɛft / (AmE), / bɪˈrɛft / (BrE)
Unhappy in love because of loss or unrequited affection; deprived and suffering emotionally due to the absence of love.
unhappy in love; deprived of affection

Examples

After her fiancé left, she felt utterly bereft and alone.
He gave her a bereft look when she danced with someone else at the party.
Many poets write about feeling bereft after a broken romance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unrequited - (of love or affection) not returned by the other person
  • Synonym: lovesick - unhappy because of unreturned or unfulfilled love

Etymology

Past participle of 'bereave', from Old English 'bereafian' (to deprive).

Synset POS: s
