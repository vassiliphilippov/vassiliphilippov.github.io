spectre

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛktər / (AmE), / ˈspɛktə / (BrE)
A disturbing or haunting image or memory, often from the past, that troubles the mind.
haunting memory or image
british

Examples

The spectre of his past mistakes haunted him for years.
Whenever she returned to the old house, the spectre of childhood fears surfaced.
The country's economic decline raised the spectre of widespread unemployment.

Synset Examples

he looked like he had seen a ghost
it aroused specters from his past

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person, especially as seen by the living
  • Synonym: phantom - something that is seen or sensed but has no physical reality; a ghost

Etymology

From Latin 'spectrum' (appearance, image, apparition), via French 'spectre'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛktɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɛktə / (BrE)
A ghost or apparition, especially one that is frightening or disturbing in appearance.
ghostly apparition; ghost

Examples

A chilling spectre was said to haunt the old castle at night.
She felt as if a spectre were watching her every move in the darkness.
The sudden appearance of a spectre left the guests trembling with fear.

Synset Examples

we were unprepared for the apparition that confronted us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person, especially one believed to appear to the living
  • Synonym: phantom - a ghostly figure; an apparition
  • Synonym: apparition - an appearance of a ghost or ghostlike figure

Etymology

From Latin 'spectrum' (appearance, image), via French 'spectre' (ghost, apparition).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09570240-n
Synset POS: n
