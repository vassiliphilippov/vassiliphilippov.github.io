enforcer

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɔrsər / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːsə / (BrE)
a person whose job it is to carry out unpleasant or difficult tasks for someone in authority, often using force or intimidation
person carrying out tough tasks for a superior

Examples

As the company's enforcer, she often had to deliver bad news to employees.
The mob sent their enforcer to collect the overdue payments.
He was known as the team's enforcer, always handling disputes on behalf of the manager.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enforce - to make sure that laws or rules are obeyed

Etymology

From enforce + -er.
