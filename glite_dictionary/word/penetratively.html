penetratively

1. ADV

/ ˈpɛn.əˌtreɪ.tɪv.li / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.ɪ.trə.tɪv.li / (BrE)
in a way that shows the ability to understand or see deeply into something; with insight or perceptiveness
in a penetrating or insightful manner

Examples

She examined the data penetratively, uncovering patterns others had missed.
The critic wrote penetratively about the novel's themes and symbolism.
He listened penetratively to every word, understanding the underlying message.

Synset Examples

the author treats his subject penetratingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penetratingly - in an insightful or perceptive manner

Etymology

From 'penetrative' + suffix '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'penetratus'.
