carangid

1. ADJ

/ kəˈræŋɪd / (AmE), / kəˈræŋɪd / (BrE)
relating to or belonging to the family Carangidae, a group of marine fish that includes jacks, pompanos, and trevallies.
of or relating to the Carangidae fish family

Examples

The waters off the coast are teeming with carangid species during the summer months.
Marine biologists observed a decline in carangid populations over the past decade.
Many carangid fishes are prized by sport fishermen for their speed and strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jack - a common name for several species in the Carangidae family
  • Synonym: pomfret - a type of fish in the Carangidae family

Etymology

From modern Latin Carangidae, the scientific name of the family, which is based on the genus Caranx.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈrændʒɪd / (AmE), / kəˈraŋɪd / (BrE)
a marine fish belonging to the family Carangidae, which includes jacks, pompanos, and trevallies, typically found in warm seas
a fish of the Carangidae (jack) family

Examples

The carangid is prized by fishermen for its speed and fighting ability.
Marine biologists recorded a new species of carangid off the coast of Indonesia.
Many carangids travel in large schools near coral reefs.

Etymology

From New Latin Carangidae (the family name), from French carangue, a type of fish.
