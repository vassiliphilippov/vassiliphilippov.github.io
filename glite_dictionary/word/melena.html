melena

1. NOUN

/ məˈlinə / (AmE), / mɪˈliːnə / (BrE)
The passage of abnormally dark, tarry feces containing blood, usually as a result of gastrointestinal bleeding.
dark, blood-tainted feces (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor noted the presence of melena during the patient's examination.
Gastrointestinal bleeding is often indicated by the appearance of melena.
Patients experiencing melena should seek medical attention promptly.

Etymology

From Greek melaina (feminine of melas, meaning 'black').
