impermissible

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪm.pɚˈmɪs.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pəˈmɪs.ə.bəl / (BrE)
not allowed by rules, laws, or accepted standards; not permitted.
not allowed; not permitted

Examples

The judge ruled that such evidence was impermissible in court.
It is impermissible to discriminate against employees based on their gender.
His actions were deemed impermissible by the school's code of conduct.

Synset Examples

impermissible behavior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permissible - allowed; permitted by rules or law

Etymology

From im- (not) + permissible (from Latin permittere 'to allow').

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪmpərˈmɪsəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪmpəˈmɪsɪbəl / (BrE)
not allowed by law or rules; not permissible
not allowed; not permissible

Examples

The judge ruled that the evidence was impermissible in court.
Such behavior is strictly impermissible at this school.
It would be impermissible to reveal confidential information to outsiders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permissible - allowed by law or rules; acceptable

Etymology

From Latin prefix *im-* (not) + *permissible* (able to be permitted), from Latin *permittere* (to allow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01767260-s
Synset POS: s
