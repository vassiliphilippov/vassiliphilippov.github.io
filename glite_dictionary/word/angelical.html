angelical

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.dʒəˈlɪk.əl / (AmE), / ˌæn.dʒɪˈlɪk.əl / (BrE)
having the qualities of an angel; exceptionally kind, pure, or beautiful in character or appearance.
having qualities of an angel

Examples

Her angelical voice soothed everyone in the room during the ceremony.
He gazed upon her with an angelical expression that radiated pure kindness.
The child's angelical smile melted the hearts of everyone she met.

Synset Examples

angelic beneficence
a beatific smile
a saintly concern for his fellow men
my sainted mother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angelic - having the nature of an angel; pure and kind

Etymology

From Late Latin 'angelicus', from Greek 'angelikos', meaning 'angelic'.

3. ADJ

/ ˌændʒəˈlɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌændʒɪˈlɪkəl / (BrE)
Having a sweet, pure, or innocent nature resembling that of an angel.
like an angel in purity or sweetness

Examples

Her angelical voice captivated everyone in the audience.
He wore an angelical expression that made it impossible to be angry with him.
The child’s angelical nature brought comfort to those around her.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

From Late Latin angelicus (angelic), from angelus (angel).
