Spiranthes romanzoffiana

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈrænθiːz roʊˌmænzəˈfɪənə / (AmE), / spaɪˈranθiːz rɒˌmanzəˈfɪənə / (BrE)
A species of orchid native to western North America, characterized by dense clusters of creamy white flowers that spiral gently around the stem, with two upper petals forming a hood.
a species of spiraling white orchid

Examples

The Spiranthes romanzoffiana blooms in late summer along the wet meadows of western North America.
Botanists were excited to find a thriving population of Spiranthes romanzoffiana in the nature preserve.
The spiraling arrangement of the flowers makes Spiranthes romanzoffiana easy to distinguish from other orchids.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'speira' (coil, spiral) and 'anthos' (flower); species likely named after N. Romanzoff, a Russian noble.
