utahraptor

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːtɑːˌræptər / (AmE), / ˈjuːtɑːˌræptə / (BrE)
a large, swift carnivorous dinosaur of the early Cretaceous period, distinguished by a long, curved claw on each hind foot
a large, swift carnivorous dinosaur

Examples

The fossilized remains of a utahraptor were discovered in eastern Utah.
Paleontologists believe the utahraptor hunted in packs, using its deadly sickle-shaped claw.
The distinctive foot claw of a utahraptor could grow up to 15 inches long.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dromaeosaur - a family of swift, predatory, bird-like dinosaurs
  • Synonym: velociraptor - a smaller, closely related carnivorous dinosaur

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Utah (the U.S. state where it was discovered) + raptor (Latin for 'thief' or 'plunderer').
