psychotic

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈkɑtɪk / (AmE), / saɪˈkɒtɪk / (BrE)
a person suffering from psychosis, a severe mental disorder characterized by a disconnection from reality
person with severe mental disorder
medical

Examples

The hospital has a special unit for treating psychotics who require intensive care.
Some psychotics may experience vivid hallucinations and strong delusional beliefs.
After years of therapy, the psychotic gradually learned to distinguish reality from his illusions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychosis - a severe mental disorder involving loss of contact with reality

Etymology

From Greek psykhos (soul, mind) + -otic (relating to), from psychosis.

2. ADJ

/ saɪˈkɑtɪk / (AmE), / saɪˈkɒtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to, characteristic of, or suffering from psychosis; having severely impaired contact with reality.
suffering from or relating to psychosis
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed as psychotic after experiencing hallucinations and delusions.
Her psychotic symptoms included hearing voices and intense paranoia.
A psychotic episode can be frightening for both the individual and their family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delusional - having or characterized by false beliefs
  • Synonym: psychosis - a severe mental disorder in which thought and emotions are so impaired that contact is lost with external reality

Etymology

From Greek psukhōsis (mental condition) plus -ic.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02084599-s
Synset POS: s
