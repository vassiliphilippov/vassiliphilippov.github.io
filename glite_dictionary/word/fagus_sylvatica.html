Fagus sylvatica

1. NOUN

/ ˌfeɪɡəs sɪlˈvætɪkə / (AmE), / ˌfeɪɡəs sɪlˈvætɪkə / (BrE)
a large European beech tree with finely toothed leaves, widely planted as an ornamental in North America and elsewhere
European beech tree species

Examples

The Fagus sylvatica in her garden is over a hundred years old.
Many parks in North America feature Fagus sylvatica as a decorative shade tree.
The distinctive smooth gray bark helps identify Fagus sylvatica among native species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European beech - common name for Fagus sylvatica, a widespread European tree

Etymology

From Latin: 'Fagus' meaning beech and 'sylvatica' meaning of the forest or woodland.
