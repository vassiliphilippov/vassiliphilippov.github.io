greensand

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrinˌsænd / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnsænd / (BrE)
an olive-green sandstone containing glauconite, commonly found in marine sedimentary deposits
olive-green sandstone with glauconite

Examples

The cliffs in the region are composed largely of greensand, giving the landscape a distinctive color.
Geologists examined the layer of greensand to learn about the area's ancient marine environment.
Some soils derived from greensand are highly valued for agriculture due to their mineral content.
