bleached

1. ADJ

/ bliːtʃt / (AmE), / bliːtʃt / (BrE)
having become pale or colorless, often due to exposure to sunlight or chemicals
having lost color or brightness

Examples

After years of sitting in the sun, the garden table looked bleached and worn.
He wore a pair of bleached jeans that looked almost white.
The photograph had grown so old and bleached that the faces were barely recognizable.

Synset Examples

sun-bleached deck chairs
faded jeans
a very pale washed-out blue
washy colors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faded - having lost brightness or color
  • Synonym: washed-out - having lost much of its color; pale

Etymology

From the verb 'bleach,' from Old English 'blǣcan' (to whiten, blanch).

2. ADJ

/ bliːtʃt / (AmE), / bliːtʃt / (BrE)
(of color, especially of hair) having been made lighter or whiter by a chemical process, not occurring naturally.
artificially lightened in color

Examples

She became a bleached blonde after visiting the salon.
The bleached sections of his hair contrasted sharply with the natural brown.
Many surfers sport bleached hair from hours in the sun and saltwater.

Synset Examples

a bleached blonde

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dyed - (of hair or fabric) colored artificially, especially with dye

Etymology

From the verb 'bleach,' from Middle English blechen, from Old English blǣcan (to whiten).

Source: WordNet
