revelry

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛvəlri / (AmE), / ˈrɛv(ə)lri / (BrE)
lively and noisy celebration, especially when involving drinking and dancing
lively celebration or partying

Examples

The sounds of revelry echoed through the streets well past midnight.
After the championship win, the team's revelry lasted all night long.
The wedding was followed by much revelry and dancing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: festivity - a celebration or party, often with joy and entertainment
  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of being sober; moderation or abstention from excess

Etymology

From Old French *revelerie*, from *reveler* (to make merry).
