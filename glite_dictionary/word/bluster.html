bluster

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌstər / (AmE), / ˈblʌstə / (BrE)
a violent, gusty wind or storm
a violent gusty wind

Examples

The fierce bluster battered the coastline, uprooting trees and downing power lines.
As night fell, the bluster outside intensified, making the old house shudder.
We stayed indoors to avoid the sudden bluster that swept across the fields.

Etymology

From Middle English blustren, probably of Low German origin (cf. Middle Low German blüsteren, meaning 'to blow violently').

2. NOUN

/ ˈblʌstɚ / (AmE), / ˈblʌstə / (BrE)
loud, aggressive, or boastful talk with little effect; empty boasting or threats.
boastful or aggressive talk with little effect

Examples

His bluster could not hide his lack of experience.
Despite all her bluster, she failed to impress the audience.
The manager's bluster faded when tough questions were asked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bragging - boasting or expressing excessive pride
  • Antonym: humility - the quality of being humble

Etymology

From Middle English blustren, probably from Middle Low German blüsteren (to blow violently).

3. VERB

/ ˈblʌstər / (AmE), / ˈblʌstə / (BrE)
to behave in a loud, aggressive, or arrogant way, often to impress or intimidate others
act in a loud or arrogant way

Examples

The manager tended to bluster when his authority was challenged, making everyone uncomfortable.
She tried to bluster her way through the meeting, but no one was convinced.
He often blusters about his achievements, but few take him seriously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swagger - to walk or behave in a very confident and typically arrogant or aggressive way
  • Synonym: bully - to act in a threatening way towards others, especially weaker people
  • Antonym: to cower - to crouch down in fear, often the opposite of arrogant behavior

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'blusten', related to Middle Low German 'blüsteren' (to blow violently).
