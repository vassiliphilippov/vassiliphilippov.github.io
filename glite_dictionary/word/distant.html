distant

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪstənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪstənt / (BrE)
far away in space; separated by a great distance.
far away in space

Examples

We could hear the distant roar of the ocean from our campsite.
The village is so distant that few tourists ever visit it.
A distant mountain peak was just visible through the early morning fog.

Synset Examples

distant villages
the sound of distant traffic
a distant sound
a distant telephone call

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remote - far away from places of human habitation
  • Antonym: near - at or within a short distance in space

Etymology

From Latin 'distantem', present participle of 'distare' (to stand apart).

Examples
