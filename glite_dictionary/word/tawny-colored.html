tawny-colored

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɔːni ˌkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈtɔːni ˌkʌləd / (BrE)
Having a tawny color; of a warm sandy, yellowish-brown, or light brown hue.
of a tawny (yellowish-brown) color

Examples

The lion's tawny-colored fur blended perfectly with the grassy savanna.
She wore a tawny-colored scarf that complemented her complexion.
The bird's tawny-colored feathers made it difficult to spot among the autumn leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tawny - a warm, sandy yellowish-brown color

Etymology

From 'tawny' (from Old French taunne, referring to tannin or tan-colored) + 'colored'.
