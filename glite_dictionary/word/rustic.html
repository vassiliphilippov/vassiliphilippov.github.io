rustic

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈrʌstɪk / (BrE)
a person from the countryside, typically regarded as simple or unsophisticated
unsophisticated country person

Examples

The novel describes the encounters between city merchants and curious rustics in the village market.
Many city dwellers underestimated the intelligence of the rustic.
He was mocked as a rustic for his plain manners and rural accent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peasant - a poor farmer of low social status who owns or rents a small piece of land
  • Antonym: urbanite - a person who lives in a city or town

Etymology

From Latin 'rusticus' (of the country), from 'rus' (country).
