hierocracy

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈɑːkrəsi / (AmE), / hɪəˈrɒkrəsi / (BrE)
a government or ruling body in which the clergy hold political power; rule by religious leaders
rule by clergy or religious leaders

Examples

During the Middle Ages, the Catholic Church was often seen as a hierocracy with vast political power.
Some historians argue that the state's transformation into a hierocracy limited democratic development.
The influence of the hierocracy was challenged during the Enlightenment when secular ideas gained prominence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theocracy - government by religious authorities or according to religious law
  • Antonym: secularism - the principle of separation of the state from religious institutions

Etymology

From Greek 'hieros' (sacred) + 'kratos' (power, rule).
