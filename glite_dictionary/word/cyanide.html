cyanide

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪəˌnaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪ.ə.naɪd / (BrE)
any of a class of chemical compounds containing the cyano group –CN, known for their toxicity; also refers to specific salts such as potassium or sodium cyanide.
a chemical compound containing the –CN group

Examples

Laboratory workers must handle cyanide with extreme caution due to its high toxicity.
The detective discovered traces of cyanide in the victim's glass.
Certain industrial processes can produce harmful cyanide compounds as byproducts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrate - a salt or ester of nitric acid, NO3−, used in fertilizers and explosives
  • Synonym: arsenide - a compound containing arsenic and another element, usually metallic

Etymology

From Greek 'kyanos' (dark blue), referencing certain blue pigments originally derived from cyanide compounds.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪəˌnaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsaɪənaɪd / (BrE)
An extremely poisonous chemical compound containing the cyano group (CN), often in the form of a salt or ester of hydrocyanic acid.
poisonous chemical compound (CN)

Examples

Laboratory workers handle cyanide with extreme caution due to its toxicity.
Several infamous poisonings have involved the deliberate use of cyanide.
The industrial accident released a dangerous amount of cyanide into the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison - a substance that causes harm or death when introduced into a living organism

Etymology

From French cyanide, from Greek kyanos (dark blue), referring to the blue color of some compounds formed with cyanide.
