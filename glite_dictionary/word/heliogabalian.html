Heliogabalian

1. ADJ

/ ˌhiːliˌoʊgəˈbeɪliən / (AmE), / ˌhiːliəʊgəˈbæliən / (BrE)
Relating to the reign, culture, or period of the Roman emperor Heliogabalus (AD 218–222).
of the reign of emperor Heliogabalus

Examples

The museum hosted an exhibition on Heliogabalian art and architecture.
Historians debate whether Heliogabalian customs influenced later Roman society.
The lavish feasts described in texts are typical of Heliogabalian decadence.

Synset Examples

The Heliogabalian culture is rich in traditions.

Etymology

From Heliogabalus, name of the Roman emperor (c. 203–222), with the suffix -ian.
