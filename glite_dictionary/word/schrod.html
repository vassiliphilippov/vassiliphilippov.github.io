schrod

1. NOUN

/ ʃrɑd / (AmE), / ʃrɒd / (BrE)
a young Atlantic cod or haddock, especially one that has been split and boned for cooking
young Atlantic cod/haddock prepared for cooking

Examples

Fresh schrod was featured as the catch of the day at the seafood restaurant.
The chef prepared the schrod by splitting and boning it before grilling.
Many visitors to New England try baked schrod as a local delicacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrod - young cod or haddock split and boned for cooking

Etymology

Probably alteration of 'scrod', of uncertain origin, possibly from Dutch or Scandinavian.

2. NOUN

/ ʃrɑd / (AmE), / ʃrɒd / (BrE)
the flesh of young Atlantic cod, haddock, or pollock, weighing up to 2 pounds, eaten as food, often broiled
flesh of young cod, haddock, or pollock

Examples

The restaurant specializes in fresh schrod served with lemon and herbs.
He ordered broiled schrod for dinner at the seafood bistro.
Many visitors to Boston enjoy tasting local dishes made from schrod.

Etymology

Alteration of 'scrod,' which may derive from a New England dialect or Dutch 'schrood' (piece cut off).
