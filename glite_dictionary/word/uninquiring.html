uninquiring

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈkwaɪərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈkwaɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
not interested in asking questions or seeking information; lacking curiosity.
not curious or questioning

Examples

She remained uninquiring about her neighbor's unusual habits, preferring not to get involved.
The student's uninquiring attitude meant he rarely participated in class discussions.
Their uninquiring acceptance of the rules led to few changes in the organization.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inquisitive - curious, eager to ask questions

Etymology

From un- (not) + inquiring (from inquire, ultimately from Latin inquirere).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00883362-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈkwaɪ(ə)rɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈkwaɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
not showing curiosity; lacking a desire to know or seek information.
lacking curiosity

Examples

She accepted the explanation with an uninquiring mind, never bothering to look deeper.
His uninquiring nature often meant he missed key details others noticed.
The audience sat uninquiring, content to listen without asking questions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curious - eager to know or learn something

Etymology

From un- (not) + inquiring (seeking knowledge).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00669185-s
Synset POS: s
