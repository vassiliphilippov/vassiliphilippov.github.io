to commend

1. VERB

/ kəˈmɛnd / (AmE), / kəˈmɛnd / (BrE)
to praise someone or something formally or enthusiastically; to express approval of
to praise or express approval of

Examples

The teacher took a moment to commend the students for their excellent work.
She was commended by her manager for her dedication and professionalism.
I would like to commend you on your outstanding performance in the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - to express admiration or approval for someone or something
  • Antonym: to criticize - to express disapproval of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin commendare (to entrust, to praise), from com- (intensive prefix) + mandare (to commit to one's charge).

3. VERB

/ kəˈmɛnd / (AmE), / kəˈmɛnd / (BrE)
to formally entrust someone or something to the care or protection of another
formally entrust to another's care

Examples

Before leaving for her trip, she commended her pets to her neighbor's care.
He commends the students to the new principal's supervision each year.
In his letter, the general commended the soldiers to God's protection.

Related Terms
