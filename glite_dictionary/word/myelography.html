myelography

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪəˈlɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌmaɪəˈlɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
A diagnostic procedure in which X-rays are used to examine the spinal cord after injection of a contrast medium into the subarachnoid space.
X-ray imaging of the spinal cord with contrast medium
medical

Examples

The neurologist ordered a myelography to investigate possible spinal cord injuries.
Complications from myelography are rare but can include headaches or allergic reactions to the contrast medium.
Before magnetic resonance imaging became common, myelography was frequently used to diagnose spinal disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumbar puncture - a procedure in which a needle is inserted into the lower back to collect cerebrospinal fluid

Etymology

From Greek 'myelos' (marrow, spinal cord) + '-graphy' (writing or recording).
