shrunken

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃrʌŋ.kən / (AmE), / ˈʃrʌŋ.kən / (BrE)
Made or having become reduced in size, strength, effect, or intensity.
reduced in size or intensity

Examples

The region's shrunken economy led to higher unemployment rates.
She looked at the shrunken profits in disappointment.
Consumers felt the impact of the shrunken value of the dollar.

Synset Examples

our shriveled receipts during the storm
as the project wore on she found her enthusiasm shriveled
the dollar's shrunken buying power

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shriveled - reduced in size, usually from loss of moisture
  • Synonym: diminished - made smaller or less
  • Antonym: enlarged - made larger in size or scope

Etymology

Past participle of 'shrink', from Old English 'scrincan' (to contract).

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃrʌŋkən / (AmE), / ˈʃrʌŋkən / (BrE)
(of a person or part of the body) lean and wrinkled as a result of age, illness, or shrinkage.
lean and wrinkled from age or illness

Examples

After months of illness, his face appeared shrunken and his clothes hung loosely on his body.
The fruit had become shrunken and unappetizing after being left out in the sun.
They found her sitting by the fire, a shrunken figure wrapped in blankets.

Synset Examples

the old woman's shriveled skin
he looked shriveled and ill
a shrunken old man
a lanky scarecrow of a man with withered face and lantern jaws
he did well despite his withered arm
a wizened little man with frizzy grey hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shriveled - wrinkled and contracted, especially due to loss of moisture or age
  • Synonym: withered - dried out and shriveled, especially due to age or disease
  • Synonym: wizened - shrunken and wrinkled, often with age

Etymology

Past participle of the verb shrink, from Old English 'scrincan'.

Source: WordNet
