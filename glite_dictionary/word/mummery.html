mummery

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌməri / (AmE), / ˈmʌməri / (BrE)
pretentious or meaningless ceremonies, especially those considered insincere or showy forms of flattery or ritual
empty or insincere ceremonial display

Examples

The lavish reception turned out to be mere mummery designed to impress the guests.
Many people see political inaugurations as outdated mummery with little real significance.
He grew impatient with the endless mummery and empty speeches at the awards ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ritual - a religious or solemn ceremony
  • Synonym: pageantry - elaborate display or ceremony

Etymology

From French *mommerie* (a masked performance), from *mommer* (to mask, to act in a masquerade).
