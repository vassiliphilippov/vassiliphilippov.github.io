Veronica agrestis

1. NOUN

/ vəˈrɑnɪkə əˈɡrɛstɪs / (AmE), / vəˈrɒnɪkə əˈɡrɛstɪs / (BrE)
a low European plant with small blue flowers borne on long stalks, now widely naturalized in North America
a small blue-flowered European plant

Examples

Veronica agrestis can often be found in meadows and gardens across Europe and North America.
Botanists recognize Veronica agrestis by its tiny blue flowers and hairy leaves.
The spread of Veronica agrestis in North America has been attributed to imported soil and seeds.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Veronica'; species epithet 'agrestis' meaning 'of the fields' or 'wild'.
