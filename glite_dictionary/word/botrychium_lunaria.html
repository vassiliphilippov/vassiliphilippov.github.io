Botrychium lunaria

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈtrɪkiəm luːˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / bəʊˈtrɪkiəm luːˈnɑːriə / (BrE)
A small fern known as moonwort, found in America, Eurasia, and Australia, with rounded leaflets resembling a crescent moon.
species of small fern (moonwort)

Examples

The rare Botrychium lunaria was discovered growing in the meadow last spring.
Botanists study the distribution of Botrychium lunaria across different continents.
Botrychium lunaria is easily identified by its crescent-shaped leaflets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonwort - common name for Botrychium lunaria, a small fern

Etymology

From Latin: 'Botrychium' from Greek 'botrys' (bunch of grapes), 'lunaria' referring to 'luna' (moon) for the leaf shape.
