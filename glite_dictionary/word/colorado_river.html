Colorado River

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑləˌreɪdoʊ ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌkɒləˌrɑːdəʊ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, noted for its length, its passage through the Grand Canyon, and its importance as a source of water for the region
major river in southwestern US and NW Mexico

Examples

The Colorado River carved out the stunning Grand Canyon over millions of years.
Farmers in Arizona rely on water from the Colorado River for irrigation.
The drought has raised concerns about water levels in the Colorado River.

Etymology

Named after the Spanish word 'colorado' meaning 'red' due to the reddish silt carried by the river.

2. PROPN

/ ˌkɑːləˈrædoʊ ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌkɒlərˈɑːdəʊ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A major river in the southwestern United States, rising in the Rocky Mountains of northern Colorado and flowing through Utah, Arizona (including the Grand Canyon), and along the border of California and Arizona before reaching Mexico and emptying into the Gulf of California.
major river in the southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

The Colorado River carved the Grand Canyon over millions of years.
Water from the Colorado River is vital for agriculture in southern California.
Many recreational activities, such as rafting, take place along the Colorado River.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rio Grande - another major river in the southwestern United States and Mexico

Etymology

From Spanish "Colorado" meaning "colored red", referencing the river's reddish silt.
