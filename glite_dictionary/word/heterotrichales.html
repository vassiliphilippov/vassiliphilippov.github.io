Heterotrichales

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬əroʊˈtrɪkeɪlz / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˈtrɪkiːliːz / (BrE)
An order of yellow-green algae with simple or branching filaments, comprising the family Tribonemaceae.
order of yellow-green algae

Examples

The Heterotrichales are studied extensively for their unique filament structures.
Scientists collected samples of Heterotrichales from freshwater habitats across Europe.
Members of the Heterotrichales are characterized by their simple or branching filaments.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'hetero-' (different) and Greek 'trichos' (hair), referring to the structure of the filaments.
