lawfully

1. ADV

/ ˈlɔːfəli / (AmE), / ˈlɔːfəli / (BrE)
in a manner that is allowed or recognized by law; legally or in accordance with established rules or customs
in a way that is legal or permitted

Examples

The company acted lawfully when it obtained the necessary permits.
He claimed that the land had been acquired lawfully and not by force.
Only those who participate lawfully will be eligible for the prize.

Synset Examples

you cannot do this legitimately!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legally - in a way that is permitted by law
  • Antonym: illegally - in a way that is not permitted by law

Etymology

From 'lawful' (Middle English, from Old English 'lagu' meaning 'law' + '-ful') + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈlɔːfəli / (AmE), / ˈlɔːfəli / (BrE)
In a way that conforms to or is allowed by the law.
in accordance with the law

Examples

The contract was lawfully signed by both parties.
They were lawfully married last weekend in a small ceremony.
You may only enter the premises if you are lawfully authorized.

Synset Examples

we are lawfully wedded now

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illegally - in a way that is not allowed by law
  • Synonym: legally - in a way that is permitted by law

Etymology

From 'lawful' + '-ly'; 'lawful' from Old English 'lagu' (law) + '-ful'.

Synset POS: r
