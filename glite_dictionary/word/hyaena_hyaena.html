Hyaena hyaena

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈiːnə haɪˈiːnə / (AmE), / haɪˈiːnə haɪˈiːnə / (BrE)
The scientific name for the striped hyena, a medium-sized carnivorous mammal native to northern Africa, Arabia, and India.
scientific name for the striped hyena

Examples

The Hyaena hyaena is primarily nocturnal and rarely seen during the day.
Researchers observed several Hyaena hyaena near the edge of the desert in India.
Unlike other hyena species, Hyaena hyaena has a striped coat and a more solitary lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped hyena - A medium-sized hyena species found in northern Africa and Asia

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek 'hyaena', referring to a type of wild animal; duplication for species binomial.
