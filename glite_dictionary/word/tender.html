tender

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛndə / (BrE)
a small boat used to ferry people or supplies between a ship and the shore
small boat for ship-to-shore transport

Examples

The crew used the tender to bring passengers ashore from the cruise ship.
After anchoring offshore, supplies were delivered to the yacht by tender.
A small tender was waiting at the dock to transport us to the main vessel.

Etymology

From Latin 'tendere' (to stretch, extend) via Old French 'tendre,' referring to one who attends or serves.
