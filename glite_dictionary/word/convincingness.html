convincingness

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɪnsɪŋnəs / (AmE), / kənˈvɪnsɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being able to persuade someone or make them believe something is true or valid
quality of being convincing

Examples

The convincingness of her argument won over even the most skeptical listeners.
He doubted the convincingness of the evidence presented in court.
The advertisement's convincingness depends on its logical appeal and emotional impact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persuasiveness - the ability to convince someone to believe or do something
  • Antonym: unconvincingness - the quality of not being able to persuade or convince

Etymology

From 'convincing' + '-ness', a noun-forming suffix.
