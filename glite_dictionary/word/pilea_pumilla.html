Pilea pumilla

1. NOUN

/ paɪˌliːə pjuˈmɪlə / (AmE), / paɪˌliːə pjuˈmɪlə / (BrE)
A species of low-growing plant in the nettle family with drooping green flower clusters and smooth, translucent stems and leaves, commonly known as clearweed.
a species of plant called clearweed

Examples

Pilea pumilla often grows in shaded, moist forest areas across eastern North America.
Gardeners appreciate Pilea pumilla for its delicate, translucent stems and minimal care requirements.
You can easily identify Pilea pumilla by its drooping clusters of tiny green flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clearweed - common name for Pilea pumilla

Etymology

From Latin; 'Pilea' is a genus name, and 'pumilla' means 'dwarfish' or 'small'.
