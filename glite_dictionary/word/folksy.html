folksy

1. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊksi / (AmE), / ˈfəʊksi / (BrE)
simple and friendly in a way that is typical of country people; informal and familiar in style or manner.
informal and friendly in a simple, rural way
informal american

Examples

The politician adopted a folksy tone to appeal to rural voters.
Her folksy manner made everyone feel comfortable at the meeting.
He hosts a folksy morning radio show with plenty of jokes and stories.

Synset Examples

a folksy radio commentator
a folksy style

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informal - having a relaxed, friendly, or unofficial style or manner
  • Synonym: rustic - having a simplicity and charm typical of the countryside
  • Antonym: formal - done in accordance with rules of convention or etiquette; stiffly conventional

Etymology

From 'folk' + '-sy', formed in American English in the 19th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊksi / (AmE), / ˈfəʊksi / (BrE)
characteristic of simple, unpretentious, or friendly manners and style, especially as associated with ordinary people
having a simple, friendly, informal style

Examples

The politician's folksy charm won over many voters at the town hall meeting.
She decorated her kitchen in a folksy style, with handmade curtains and wooden utensils.
His folksy humor makes even the most boring meetings enjoyable.

Synset Examples

folksy humor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rustic - having a simple, rural, or country style
  • Antonym: urbane - notably polite or polished in manner, sophisticated

Etymology

From 'folk' (common people) + '-sy' (adjectival suffix), US English, early 20th century.
