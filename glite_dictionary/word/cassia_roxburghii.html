Cassia roxburghii

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæsiə rɒksˈbɜːrɡi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌkæsiə rɒksˈbɜːɡi.aɪ / (BrE)
An East Indian tree with long pods containing a black pulp traditionally used as a cathartic, especially in veterinary medicine.
East Indian tree used in veterinary medicine

Examples

The pods of Cassia roxburghii are collected for their medicinal properties.
Veterinarians in India often use Cassia roxburghii to treat horses.
The striking pink flowers of Cassia roxburghii make it a popular ornamental tree.

Etymology

Cassia from Latin, genus name; roxburghii commemorates Scottish botanist William Roxburgh (1751–1815).
