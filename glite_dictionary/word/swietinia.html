Swietinia

1. NOUN

/ swiːˈtɪniə / (AmE), / swɪˈiːtɪnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees commonly known as mahogany, valued for their hard, reddish-brown wood.
genus of mahogany trees

Examples

The endangered Swietinia species are protected by international trade agreements.
Carpenters prize wood from Swietinia for its durability and deep color.
Forests with abundant Swietinia help support local biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - hard, reddish-brown timber from Swietinia trees

Etymology

Named for Swedish botanist Magnus von Swietin.
