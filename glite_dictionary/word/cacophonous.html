cacophonous

1. ADJ

/ kəˈkɑfənəs / (AmE), / kəˈkɒfənəs / (BrE)
having a harsh, discordant, or unpleasant sound
having a harsh or unpleasant sound

Examples

The cacophonous roar of traffic made it hard to concentrate on my work.
At dawn, the cacophonous calls of birds filled the air outside her window.
The band's cacophonous performance left the audience covering their ears.

Synset Examples

as cacophonous as a henyard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discordant - lacking harmony; harsh-sounding
  • Antonym: euphonic - pleasant-sounding; harmonious

Etymology

From Greek kakophōnos, from kakos (bad) + phōnē (voice, sound).
