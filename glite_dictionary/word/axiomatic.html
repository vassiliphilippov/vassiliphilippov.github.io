axiomatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæksiəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌaksiəˈmatɪk / (BrE)
containing or expressing general truths or maxims; taken as established or self-evident
containing general truths or maxims

Examples

He delivered axiomatic advice that everyone should treat others as they wish to be treated.
Her book is filled with axiomatic principles about success and happiness.
Many proverbs contain axiomatic truths that have stood the test of time.

Synset Examples

axiomatic wisdom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphoristic - expressing general truths or maxims in a concise way

Etymology

From Greek axiōmatikos (fit, proper), from axiōma (that which is thought worthy or fit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02664356-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌæk.si.oʊˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæk.si.əˈmæt.ɪk / (BrE)
clearly true and therefore not needing to be proved or explained; self-evident
self-evident; not needing proof

Examples

It is axiomatic that democracy requires free speech to function well.
The notion that actions have consequences is almost axiomatic in philosophy.
Her argument rested on the axiomatic assumption that all people seek happiness.

Synset Examples

an axiomatic truth
we hold these truths to be self-evident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-evident - obvious without proof or explanation

Etymology

From Greek axiōmatikos (fit to be demanded, authoritative), from axiōma (that which is thought worthy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01623076-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌæk.si.əˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæk.si.əˈmæt.ɪk / (BrE)
of, relating to, or based on axioms; self-evident or unquestionable by virtue of being derived from accepted principles.
relating to or based on axioms

Examples

In mathematics, the principles of geometry are considered axiomatic and provide the foundation for further reasoning.
The scientist argued that the laws of physics should not be viewed as axiomatic but open to empirical testing.
It became axiomatic in the company that customer satisfaction was the top priority.

Synset Examples

axiomatic physics
the postulational method was applied to geometry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postulational - relating to postulates; assumed without proof
  • Synonym: theoretical - concerned with the theory of a subject rather than its practical application

Etymology

From Greek axiōmatikos (fit, proper), from axiōma (that which is thought worthy).
