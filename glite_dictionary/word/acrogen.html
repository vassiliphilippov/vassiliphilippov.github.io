acrogen

1. NOUN

/ ˈækrəˌdʒɛn / (AmE), / ˈakrɪdʒən / (BrE)
a flowerless plant, such as a fern or moss, that grows only at the tip of its main stem
flowerless plant with tip growth

Examples

Ferns and mosses are typical examples of acrogens in the plant kingdom.
Unlike most flowering plants, acrogens lack true flowers and seeds.
The structure of an acrogen allows it to grow continuously from the tip.

Etymology

From Greek akros (highest) + -genēs (born, produced).
