to situate

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪtʃuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪtʃueɪt / (BrE)
to put or place something in a particular position or location, often in a firm or deliberate manner
to place in a particular position

Examples

She carefully situated the vase in the center of the table.
The museum is situated at the top of the hill, overlooking the city.
He situated himself near the exit for a quick getaway.

Synset Examples

She posited her hand on his shoulder
deposit the suitcase on the bench
fix your eyes on this spot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: position - to put something in a particular place
  • Synonym: to locate - to put something in a specific or defined spot

Etymology

From Late Latin situatus, past participle of situare (to place), from Latin situs (position, site).

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪtʃuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪtʃueɪt / (BrE)
to determine or mark the place, site, or limits of something; to place or position within a particular context or location
to determine or mark a position or limits

Examples

The surveyor will situate the new building precisely on the lot.
Using maps and GPS, researchers situated each archaeological find accurately.
Before we expand the garden, we need to situate its exact boundaries.

Synset Examples

Our sense of sight enables us to locate objects in space
Locate the boundaries of the property

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to locate - to discover the exact position of something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin situatus, past participle of situare (to place), from Latin situs (position, site).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02700775-v
Synset POS: v
