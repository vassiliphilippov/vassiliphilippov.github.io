Selenarctos thibetanus

1. NOUN

/ sɛlɪˈnɑːrktəs θɪˈbeɪtənəs / (AmE), / sɛlɪˈnɑːktɒs θɪˈbiːtənəs / (BrE)
a species of bear with a black coat, native to central and eastern Asia; also known as the Asiatic black bear or moon bear
Asian black bear species

Examples

The Selenarctos thibetanus is recognized by the distinctive white patch on its chest.
Conservation efforts are crucial for protecting Selenarctos thibetanus in its native habitats.
Many zoos around the world are home to Selenarctos thibetanus due to the species' declining wild population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Asiatic black bear - common name for Selenarctos thibetanus

Etymology

From Greek selēnē (moon) + arktos (bear); thibetanus referring to Tibet.
