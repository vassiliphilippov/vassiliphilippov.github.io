calyptra

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪptrə / (AmE), / kəˈlɪptrə / (BrE)
a hood or cap-like structure covering the calyx or reproductive organs of certain plants, such as the California poppy
hood-like covering on plants

Examples

The calyptra of the moss protects its developing spores from drying out.
In the California poppy, the calyptra falls off as the flower blooms.
Botanists examined the calyptra under a microscope to study its cellular structure.

Etymology

From Greek kalyptra (covering, veil), from kalyptein (to cover).
