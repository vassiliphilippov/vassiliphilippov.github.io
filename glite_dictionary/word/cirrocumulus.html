cirrocumulus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪroʊˈkjuːmjələs / (AmE), / ˌsɪrəʊˈkjuːmjʊləs / (BrE)
a type of high-altitude cloud consisting of a series of small, white patches or ripples, often arranged in rows
high-altitude, patchy ripple cloud

Examples

A band of cirrocumulus clouds rippled across the early morning sky, heralding a change in weather.
Pilots are trained to identify cirrocumulus clouds because they can indicate turbulence at high altitudes.
The textbook showed a photograph of cirrocumulus formations to illustrate different cloud types.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: altocumulus - a mid-altitude cloud type forming white or grey layers or patches
  • Degree Lower: stratocumulus - a low-altitude cloud type with large, dark, rounded masses
  • Synonym: cirrostratus - a high-altitude, thin, sheet-like cloud

Etymology

From Latin 'cirrus' (curl of hair) and 'cumulus' (heap, pile).
