phenolphthalein

1. NOUN

/ ˌfiːnəlˈθæliːn / (AmE), / ˌfiːnɒlˈθæliːn / (BrE)
a white or pale yellow crystalline compound used as an acid-base indicator and formerly as a laxative; it turns pink in alkaline solutions above pH 8
acid-base indicator and laxative compound
medical

Examples

Phenolphthalein is commonly used in laboratory titrations to indicate the endpoint of a weak acid and strong base reaction.
When exposed to an alkaline solution, phenolphthalein changes color from colorless to bright pink.
Medical professionals used phenolphthalein as a laxative before its safety concerns became widely recognized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: litmus - an acid-base indicator obtained from lichens; changes color in response to acidity/alkalinity

Etymology

From German 'Phenolphthalein', formed from 'phenol' + 'phthalein', the latter from 'phthalic acid'.
