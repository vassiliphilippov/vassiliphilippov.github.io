parhelic

1. ADJ

/ pɑrˈhiːlɪk / (AmE), / pɑːˈhiːlɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling a parhelion, especially describing halos or arcs in the atmosphere that are parallel to the horizon.
relating to a parhelion or sun halo

Examples

The parhelic circle could be seen stretching across the afternoon sky.
She studied rare parhelic phenomena as part of her atmospheric research.
Under certain conditions, a parhelic halo may appear as a luminous band parallel to the horizon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parhelion - a bright spot in the sky appearing on either side of the sun, also called a sundog

Etymology

From 'parhelion' (halo), based on Greek 'para' (beside) + 'hēlios' (sun).
