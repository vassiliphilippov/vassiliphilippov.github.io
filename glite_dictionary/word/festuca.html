Festuca

1. NOUN

/ fɛˈstuːkə / (AmE), / fɛsˈtjuːkə / (BrE)
A genus of tufted perennial grasses in the family Poaceae, commonly known as fescues.
genus of perennial grasses (fescues)

Examples

Several species within the genus Festuca are used as turf grasses for lawns and sports fields.
Botanists collected samples of Festuca from the alpine meadows for further study.
Many cattle graze on Festuca because of its nutritional value and resilience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fescue - A type of grass in the genus Festuca

Etymology

From Latin 'festuca' meaning 'stalk' or 'straw'.

2. NOUN

/ fɛsˈtuːkə / (AmE), / fɛsˈtjuːkə / (BrE)
a genus of tufted perennial grasses, commonly called fescues, found in temperate regions and used for pastures and lawns
a genus of grasses (fescues)

Examples

Many golf courses use Festuca species for their fairways because of their durability and fine texture.
Several varieties of Festuca are popular for creating low-maintenance lawns.
The botanist collected samples of Festuca during her fieldwork in the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fescue - any grass of the genus Festuca

Etymology

From Latin *festuca* (straw, blade of grass).
