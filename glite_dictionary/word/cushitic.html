Cushitic

1. NOUN

/ kʊˈʃɪtɪk / (AmE), / kʊˈʃɪtɪk / (BrE)
A branch of the Afroasiatic language family, comprising languages spoken in Ethiopia, Somalia, northwestern Kenya, and surrounding regions.
branch of Afroasiatic languages in East Africa

Examples

Several languages in the Cushitic family are spoken by millions in Ethiopia.
The Cushitic languages differ greatly from neighboring Bantu tongues.
Researchers have mapped the spread of Cushitic dialects across the Horn of Africa.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Afroasiatic - a large language family including Cushitic and Semitic languages
  • Degree Higher: Semitic - another main branch of the Afroasiatic language family

Etymology

From Cushite, an ethnic/geographic designation, with the suffix -ic forming an adjective or noun.
