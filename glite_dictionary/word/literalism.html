literalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬.ə.rəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.ər.əˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
The doctrine or practice of representing things in art or literature in a realistic or literal manner, avoiding figurative or symbolic interpretations.
realistic depiction in art or literature

Examples

Some critics praised the painting's literalism, while others found it unimaginative.
His writing style is marked by literalism, as he tends to avoid metaphor or allegory.
The novel's extreme literalism gave the story a sense of authenticity but limited its emotional depth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realism - the technique or principle of depicting subjects as they appear in everyday life
  • Antonym: symbolism - the use of symbols to represent ideas in art or literature

Etymology

From 'literal' + '-ism', patterned after terms like 'realism' in the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬.əˌrəl.ɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.ər.əl.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
the tendency to interpret words, statements, or texts in their most basic and straightforward sense, rather than recognizing figurative or metaphorical meanings.
interpretation by literal meaning

Examples

Her literalism made it difficult for her to understand jokes that relied on wordplay.
Academic debates sometimes stall because of too much literalism in interpreting texts.
A strict literalism can overlook the richness of metaphorical language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: literalist - a person who interprets texts or statements literally
  • Antonym: figurative - involving a metaphorical or symbolic interpretation

Etymology

From 'literal' + '-ism', after French 'littéralisme'.
