Elizabeth Peabody

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈlɪzəbɪθ ˈpiːbɒdi / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪzəbɪθ ˈpiːbɒdi / (BrE)
(1804–1894) An American educator who founded the first kindergarten in the United States and was a leader in early childhood education reform.
American educator; kindergarten founder

Examples

Elizabeth Peabody played a key role in introducing the concept of kindergarten to America.
Many education courses cover the pioneering work of Elizabeth Peabody in early childhood development.
The legacy of Elizabeth Peabody continues to influence the way children are taught in the United States.
