bray

1. NOUN

/ breɪ / (AmE), / breɪ / (BrE)
the loud, harsh cry of a donkey or similar animals
the cry of a donkey

Examples

The bray of the donkey echoed across the quiet valley.
We heard the loud bray of a mule from the stables.
Suddenly, a harsh bray startled the children nearby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neigh - the characteristic sound made by a horse

Etymology

From Old French *braiier* (to bray), from Vulgar Latin *bragire* (to cry out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07392659-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ breɪ / (AmE), / breɪ / (BrE)
(of a donkey) to utter a loud, harsh cry; to make a characteristic donkey noise.
donkey's harsh cry (verb)

Examples

The donkey began to bray loudly in the early morning, waking up the farm.
When startled, the stubborn animal would often bray and refuse to move.
Children laughed as they heard the donkey braying at the petting zoo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neigh - the sound made by a horse

Etymology

Middle English from Old French *braire* (to bray), of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01056425-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ breɪ / (AmE), / breɪ / (BrE)
to laugh loudly and harshly, resembling the sound of a donkey
to laugh loudly and harshly

Examples

The group began to bray with laughter at the comedian's outrageous joke.
She brayed so loudly that everyone turned to stare.
Their loud braying echoed down the hallway, interrupting the quiet meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guffaw - to laugh loudly and boisterously
  • Degree Lower: snicker - to laugh quietly and disrespectfully

Etymology

From Old French *braire* (to cry out), from Vulgar Latin *bragire*.

4. VERB

/ breɪ / (AmE), / breɪ / (BrE)
to crush or grind something into small pieces or powder, especially using a mortar and pestle
crush or grind to powder

Examples

The cook began to bray the spices in a heavy mortar.
You should bray the dried herbs thoroughly before adding them to the mixture.
Ancient apothecaries would bray medicinal roots for their remedies.

Synset Examples

grind the spices in a mortar
mash the garlic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grind - to reduce something to small particles or powder by crushing it
  • Synonym: crush - to press something so hard that it breaks or changes shape

Etymology

From Old French 'braier', from Latin 'batare' (to beat).
