Sterculia acerifolia

1. NOUN

/ stɜːrˈkuːliə ˌæsəriˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / stəːˈkjuːlɪə ˌæsərɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A species of Australian tree known for its panicles of brilliant scarlet flowers; commonly called the flame tree.
Australian tree with scarlet flowers

Examples

The Sterculia acerifolia is admired for its spectacular display of red flowers each spring.
Many parks in Australia have Sterculia acerifolia planted for their ornamental beauty.
During the blooming season, the Sterculia acerifolia attracts numerous birds and photographers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flame tree - Common name for Sterculia acerifolia, known for its bright red flowers

Etymology

From Latin genus name Sterculia and species epithet acerifolia, meaning 'maple-leaved.'
