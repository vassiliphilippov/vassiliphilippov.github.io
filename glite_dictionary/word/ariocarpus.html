Ariocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛər.i.oʊˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / ˌær.i.əˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of slow-growing, geophytic cacti native to northern and eastern Mexico and southern Texas.
genus of slow-growing North American cacti

Examples

Many collectors prize Ariocarpus species for their unique geometric shapes and slow growth.
The Ariocarpus genus is well adapted to arid environments in northern Mexico.
Some Ariocarpus plants can live for decades, surviving harsh desert conditions.

Etymology

From Greek 'aria', a type of oak, and 'karpos', meaning fruit, referring to the fruit's resemblance to acorns.
