Kshatriya

1. NOUN

/ kʃəˈtriːjə / (AmE), / kʃəˈtriːjə / (BrE)
A member of the royal or warrior Hindu caste, traditionally responsible for protection and governance.
a member of the Hindu warrior caste

Examples

As a Kshatriya, he was expected to uphold honor and bravely defend his kingdom.
Many ancient Indian epics feature legendary Kshatriya warriors and their heroic deeds.
The Kshatriya class played a central role in governing and maintaining order in traditional Hindu society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brahmin - a member of the priestly Hindu caste
  • Synonym: vaishya - a member of the merchant or agricultural Hindu caste
  • Synonym: shudra - a member of the laborer Hindu caste

Etymology

From Sanskrit kṣatriya (क्षत्रिय), meaning 'warrior' or 'ruler'.
