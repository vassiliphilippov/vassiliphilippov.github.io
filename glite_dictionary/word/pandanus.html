pandanus

1. NOUN

/ pænˈdænəs / (AmE), / pænˈdeɪnəs / (BrE)
A fiber obtained from the leaves of the pandanus tree, used for weaving mats and other articles.
fiber from pandanus tree leaves

Examples

Traditional baskets are woven from pandanus in many Pacific island cultures.
The artist used strips of pandanus to create an intricate mat.
Local women demonstrated how pandanus is prepared and woven into hats.

Etymology

From New Latin Pandanus, the scientific name of the genus, from Malay pandan.

2. NOUN

/ pænˈdeɪnəs / (AmE), / pænˈdeɪnəs / (BrE)
A tropical plant of the genus Pandanus, having large prop roots, edible cone-shaped fruits, and long, narrow leaves often used for weaving or cooking.
tropical plant with prop roots and long leaves

Examples

The villagers use pandanus leaves to weave baskets and mats.
A mature pandanus tree can be recognized by its prominent stilt-like roots.
The fruit of the pandanus is an important food source in many Pacific islands.

Etymology

From the Malay word 'pandan', adopted in modern Latin as the genus name.
