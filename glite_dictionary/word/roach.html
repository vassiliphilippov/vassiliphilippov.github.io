roach

1. NOUN

/ roʊtʃ / (AmE), / rəʊtʃ / (BrE)
A small European freshwater fish (Rutilus rutilus) with a greenish back, commonly found in rivers and lakes.
European freshwater fish with greenish back

Examples

Anglers in Europe often catch roach in slow-moving rivers and small lakes.
The roach is recognized by its silver body and greenish back.
We spotted a school of roach swimming near the reeds.

Etymology

Possibly from Old French roche, used in names for several kinds of fish.
