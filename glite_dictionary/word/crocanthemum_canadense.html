Crocanthemum canadense

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈkænθəməm kəˈnædənsi / (AmE), / krəʊˈkænθɪməm kəˈnædənsiː / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to eastern North America, noted for producing early solitary yellow flowers and later petalless flowers; commonly known as 'frostweed' because of ice crystals forming on it during early frosts.
a perennial plant native to eastern North America

Examples

The Crocanthemum canadense blooms in early spring with vibrant yellow petals.
During the first frost, you can often see ice crystals forming on Crocanthemum canadense stems.
Botanists identify Crocanthemum canadense in the wild by its unique flowering pattern.

Etymology

From the genus name Crocanthemum, from Greek 'krokos' (saffron) and 'anthemon' (flower), and species name referencing Canada.
