suborder Ceratosaura

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌɔrdɚ sɛrətoʊˈsɔrə / (AmE), / sʌbˌɔːdə sɛrətəʊˈsɔːrə / (BrE)
a suborder of primitive saurischian dinosaurs notable for certain skeletal features
a group of primitive saurischian dinosaurs

Examples

The suborder Ceratosaura includes some of the earliest known saurischian dinosaurs.
Paleontologists study fossils from the suborder Ceratosaura to understand dinosaur evolution.
Distinct skeletal features help classify dinosaurs into the suborder Ceratosaura.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saurischian - relating to a major group of dinosaurs with lizard-like hips

Etymology

From New Latin 'Ceratosaura', combining Greek 'keras' (horn) and 'sauros' (lizard).
