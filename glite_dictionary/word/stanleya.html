Stanleya

1. NOUN

/ ˈstænliə / (AmE), / ˈstanlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of North American plants in the mustard family (Brassicaceae), commonly known as prince's plume, noted for their tall spikes of yellow flowers.
genus of North American plants (prince's plume)

Examples

The tall, feathery plumes of Stanleya add a striking vertical element to desert gardens.
Botanists collected several specimens of Stanleya during their expedition through the American Southwest.
Many pollinators, including bees, are attracted to the bright yellow flowers of Stanleya.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prince's plume - common name for plants of the genus Stanleya

Etymology

Named after Edward Stanley, an English nobleman and patron of science.
