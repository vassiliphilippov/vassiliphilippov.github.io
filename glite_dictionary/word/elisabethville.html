Elisabethville

1. PROPN

/ əˈlɪzəbɛθˌvɪl / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪzəˌbɪl / (BrE)
the former name (until 1966) for the city of Lubumbashi in southeastern Democratic Republic of the Congo, known as a copper mining center near the Zambian border.
former name of Lubumbashi, Congo

Examples

Many colonial-era maps still mark Elisabethville instead of Lubumbashi.
The rich copper mines near Elisabethville attracted workers from across Africa.
After independence, Elisabethville was renamed Lubumbashi in 1966.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lubumbashi - current name of the city formerly known as Elisabethville

Etymology

Named after Elisabeth, Queen of the Belgians, during the period of Belgian colonial rule.
