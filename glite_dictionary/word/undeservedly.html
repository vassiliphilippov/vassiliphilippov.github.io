undeservedly

1. ADV

/ ˌʌndɪˈzɜrvɪdli / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈzɜːvɪdli / (BrE)
in a way that is not justified or merited; without being earned or warranted
without having earned or merited

Examples

She was promoted undeservedly, many colleagues thought someone else deserved the recognition.
He was undeservedly blamed for the mistake, even though he wasn't involved at all.
The movie received praise undeservedly and left critics baffled by its sudden popularity.

Synset Examples

the team chalked up another victory, the last one quite undeservedly, in my opinion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deservedly - in a way that is rightfully earned or warranted

Etymology

Formed from 'undeserved' (from un- ‘not’ + deserved) + -ly adverbial suffix.
