to lysogenize

1. VERB

/ laɪˈsɑdʒəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / laɪˈsɒdʒənaɪz / (BrE)
(of a bacteriophage) to become incorporated into the genome of a bacterium, resulting in lysogeny.
integrate into bacterial genome (biology)
medical

Examples

The bacteriophage can lysogenize its bacterial host, allowing the viral DNA to persist within the cell.
Some viruses lysogenize their hosts instead of immediately causing lysis.
Scientists study how certain bacteriophages lysogenize bacteria to understand gene transfer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lytic - describing a bacteriophage that destroys its host cell

Etymology

From 'lysogeny' + '-ize'. 'Lysogeny' from French 'lysogénie', influenced by Greek 'lysis' (loosening) and 'gene'.
