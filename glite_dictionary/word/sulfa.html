sulfa

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌl.fə / (AmE), / ˈsʌl.fə / (BrE)
any of a group of synthetic antibacterial drugs containing sulfonamides, used to inhibit the growth of bacteria
synthetic antibacterial drug (sulfonamide)
medical

Examples

Some patients are allergic to sulfa drugs and must avoid them.
The doctor prescribed a sulfa antibiotic to treat the infection.
If you experience a rash after taking sulfa, notify your physician immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfonamide - any of a group of compounds containing the sulfonamide group, used as antibiotics

Etymology

Shortened from sulfonamide, referring to the chemical structure of these compounds.
