curd

1. NOUN

/ kɝd / (AmE), / kɜːd / (BrE)
A soft, white substance formed when milk coagulates, used to make cheese and other dairy products.
coagulated milk used for cheese making

Examples

She added lemon juice to milk to form curd for the recipe.
The farmer collected fresh curd every morning to make cheese.
Miss Muffet was eating curd and whey when the spider appeared.

Synset Examples

Little Miss Muffet sat on a tuffet eating some curds and whey

Related Terms

  • Antonym: whey - the watery part of milk that remains after the formation of curds

Etymology

From Middle English 'curd' of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old English 'crudan' (to press).

2. NOUN

/ kɝd / (AmE), / kɜːd / (BrE)
a soft, thickened substance formed by the coagulation of a liquid, especially milk or similar foods
soft, thickened substance from coagulated liquid

Examples

For dessert, she made tarts filled with homemade lemon curd.
Tofu, also known as bean curd, is a staple in many Asian cuisines.
The curd was smooth, creamy, and perfectly sweetened for the cake.

Synset Examples

bean curd
lemon curd

Related Terms

  • Antonym: whey - the watery part of milk separated from curd
  • Synonym: bean curd - soft, white food made by coagulating soy milk (tofu)
  • Synonym: lemon curd - a thick, creamy spread made from lemon juice, eggs, sugar, and butter

Etymology

From Middle English 'curd', possibly related to Old English 'crudan' (to press, drive).
