rejuvenation

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌdʒuːvəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌdʒuːvəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of making someone or something look or feel younger, fresher, or more lively again
process of becoming young or fresh again

Examples

After a week at the spa, she felt a sense of rejuvenation and renewed energy.
Spring brings the annual rejuvenation of city parks, with flowers blooming everywhere.
The company's rejuvenation was evident after introducing innovative new products.

Synset Examples

the annual rejuvenation of the landscape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revitalization - the act of giving new life or energy to something

Etymology

From Latin rejuvenare (to make young again), from re- (again) + juvenis (young).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˌdʒuːvəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌdʒuːvəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making someone or something look or feel younger, fresher, or more lively
making something look or feel younger

Examples

After the spa treatment, she felt a sense of rejuvenation and renewed energy.
The government invested in the rejuvenation of the historic city center.
Regular exercise is known to contribute to the rejuvenation of both body and mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revitalization - the process of giving new life or energy to something
  • Antonym: aging - the process of becoming older or showing signs of age

Etymology

From Latin 'juvenis' (young) via 'rejuvenate'.
