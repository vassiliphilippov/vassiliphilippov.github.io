hogshead

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑɡzˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈhɒɡzˌhɛd / (BrE)
A British unit of capacity for alcoholic beverages, traditionally equal to 54 imperial gallons (about 245 liters).
British liquid measure for alcohol
british

Examples

The brewery delivered a hogshead of ale to the tavern.
In the cellar, there was an old hogshead used for storing wine.
A hogshead of rum was auctioned off to collectors at the estate sale.

Etymology

From Middle English hoggeshed, of uncertain origin, possibly from 'hog' + 'head' due to the size; used since the 15th century for large casks.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑɡzˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈhɒɡzˌhɛd / (BrE)
a large cask or barrel, especially one with a capacity of about 63 gallons (238 litres), used chiefly for storing liquids such as wine or ale
large cask or barrel (approx. 63 gallons)

Examples

The winery stored its finest vintage in a massive hogshead in the cellar.
A single hogshead of rum was enough to supply the crew for weeks at sea.
Collectors sometimes purchase old hogsheads as decorative antiques for their homes.

Etymology

From Middle English 'hoggeshede', probably from 'hog' + 'head', possibly referring to a cask marked with a hog's head as a symbol.
