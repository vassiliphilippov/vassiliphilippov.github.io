Chrysolophus pictus

1. NOUN

/ krɪsəˈlɑfəs ˈpɪktəs / (AmE), / krɪsəˈlɒfəs ˈpɪktəs / (BrE)
A brightly colored crested pheasant, commonly known as the golden pheasant, native to the mountains of western and central Asia.
a brightly colored Asian pheasant (golden pheasant)

Examples

The Chrysolophus pictus is admired for its vivid golden and red plumage.
Wild populations of Chrysolophus pictus can be found in the forests of western China.
Many zoos feature the Chrysolophus pictus due to its striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden pheasant - common name for Chrysolophus pictus, a brightly colored pheasant

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) and 'lophos' (crest); Latin 'pictus' (painted).
