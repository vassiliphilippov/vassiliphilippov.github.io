jumbal

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌm.bəl / (BrE)
a small, flat, ring-shaped cake or cookie, often made from sweet dough and sometimes flavored with spices
ring-shaped cake or cookie
archaic

Examples

She baked a batch of jumbals for the historical reenactment.
The old cookbook included a recipe for traditional jumbal biscuits.
At the fair, vendors sold sweet jumbals alongside gingerbread and other treats.

Etymology

From French *jumelle* (twin), referring to the paired or ring form; adopted in Early Modern English.
