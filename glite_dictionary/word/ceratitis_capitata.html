Ceratitis capitata

1. NOUN

/ sɛrəˈtaɪtɪs ˌkæpɪˈteɪtə / (AmE), / sɛrəˈtaɪtɪs ˌkæpɪˈteɪtə / (BrE)
a small black-and-white fruit fly, also known as the Mediterranean fruit fly, that damages citrus and other fruits by laying eggs that hatch inside the fruit
a fruit fly pest of citrus and other fruits

Examples

Ceratitis capitata is considered one of the most destructive agricultural pests worldwide.
The orchard's fruit yield dropped dramatically after an infestation of Ceratitis capitata.
Strict quarantine measures were put in place to prevent the spread of Ceratitis capitata in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mediterranean fruit fly - common name for Ceratitis capitata, a pest of fruits

Etymology

From Greek 'keros' (horn) and Latin 'capitata' (headed), referring to its physical features.
