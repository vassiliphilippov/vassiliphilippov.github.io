peewee

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiˌwi / (AmE), / ˈpiːwiː / (BrE)
A small olive-colored woodland flycatcher found in eastern North America, belonging to the genus Contopus.
small North American woodland flycatcher

Examples

We spotted a peewee perched on a branch, calling out in the early morning.
The peewee is easily recognized by its mournful whistle in wooded areas.
Several peewees migrate south for the winter every year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood pewee - another species of woodland flycatcher in North America

Etymology

Imitative, from the bird's distinctive call.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpiˌwi / (AmE), / ˈpiːwiː / (BrE)
a person or thing that is very small, often used in a disparaging or informal way; a very small person.
a very small person or thing
informal

Examples

Because he was the peewee of the group, he always got the front seat in photos.
The basketball team called their youngest member a peewee, but he was quick on his feet.
She may be a peewee, but she's stronger than most people twice her size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dwarf - a person of unusually short stature
  • Antonym: giant - an extremely large person

Etymology

Reduplication of 'wee' (small), chiefly North American slang, first recorded late 19th century.
