endozoan

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛn.doʊˈzoʊ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəʊˈzəʊ.ən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of organisms, especially parasites, that live inside the bodies of animals.
living inside animals (zoology)
medical

Examples

Many endozoan parasites cause diseases in their animal hosts.
The research focused on endozoan communities within marine invertebrates.
Certain worms are classified as endozoan because they live entirely inside animal bodies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ectozoan - relating to organisms living on the outside of animals

Etymology

From Greek endon (within) + zōion (animal).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛndoʊˈzoʊ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈzəʊ.ən / (BrE)
a parasite that lives inside the internal organs of an animal, especially intestinal worms
internal animal parasite

Examples

Intestinal roundworms are a common type of endozoan that affect both humans and animals.
Scientists discovered a new endozoan species residing in the digestive tract of fish.
Treatment must target the endozoan without harming the host animal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ectozoan - a parasite that lives on the surface of an animal

Etymology

From Greek endo- (inside) and zōion (animal).

WordNet ID: oewn-01387658-n
Synset POS: n
