Achoerodus

1. NOUN

/ æˌkoʊ.əˈroʊ.dəs / (AmE), / əˌkəʊ.əˈrəʊ.dəs / (BrE)
a genus of marine fish in the family Labridae, commonly known as blue gropers, found primarily in Australian waters
genus of fish called blue gropers

Examples

The Achoerodus is well known among divers for its vivid blue coloring.
Conservation measures have been implemented to protect several Achoerodus species.
Juvenile Achoerodus often display different coloration from adults.

Etymology

From Greek *achoeros* (without a pig) and *odus* (tooth), referring to fish jaw structure.
