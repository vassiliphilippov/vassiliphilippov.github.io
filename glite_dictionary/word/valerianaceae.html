Valerianaceae

1. NOUN

/ vəˌlɪəri.əˈneɪsi.i / (AmE), / vəˌlɪəri.əˈneɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
a genus of mostly herbaceous plants known for their characteristic strong, often unpleasant smell
a genus of strong-smelling herbs
medical

Examples

The Valerianaceae family includes plants commonly used for medicinal purposes.
Botanists have studied the unique aroma produced by flowers of the Valerianaceae.
Valerian root, from the Valerianaceae, is often found in herbal supplements.

Etymology

From Latin Valeriana (the type genus) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant family names).
