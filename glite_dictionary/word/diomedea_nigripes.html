Diomedea nigripes

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈmidi.ə naɪˈgriːpɛz / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈmiːdi.ə naɪˈgriːpeez / (BrE)
A species of albatross, commonly known as the black-footed albatross, found in the North Pacific with dark plumage and distinctive black feet.
species of albatross with black feet

Examples

The Diomedea nigripes nests primarily on islands in the North Pacific Ocean.
Wildlife biologists track populations of Diomedea nigripes to monitor ecosystem health.
Unlike other albatrosses, the Diomedea nigripes has unmistakable dark plumage and black feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black-footed albatross - common name for Diomedea nigripes

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Diomedea (albatrosses), species name nigripes ('black-footed' from Latin niger 'black' + pes 'foot').
