entremets

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːntrəˌmeɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒntrəˌmeɪ / (BrE)
A dish served with a main course, but subordinate to it, often as a side or intermediate course.
dish served alongside the main course

Examples

The chef prepared a delicate entremets to complement the roast beef.
During the banquet, an elegant vegetable entremets was served between courses.
Traditional French dinners often include an entremets to enhance the meal's variety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: side dish - a food served as an accompaniment to a main dish

Etymology

From French 'entremets', from 'entre' (between) and 'mets' (dishes, from Latin 'mittere', to send).
