suspicious

1. ADJ

/ səˈspɪʃəs / (AmE), / səˈspɪʃəs / (BrE)
arousing the feeling that something is wrong, illegal, or dishonest; not as expected and likely to cause mistrust or doubt
arousing mistrust or doubt

Examples

The police thought his story sounded suspicious and decided to investigate further.
Her suspicious behavior at the office made everyone wonder what was going on.
There was a suspicious package left at the entrance to the building.

Synset Examples

there was something fishy about the accident
up to some funny business
some definitely queer goings-on
a shady deal
her motives were suspect
suspicious behavior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trustworthy - deserving of trust or confidence; reliable
  • Synonym: fishy - arousing suspicion; not as expected

Etymology

From Latin suspiciosus (full of suspicion), from suspicere (to mistrust).

2. ADJ

/ səˈspɪʃəs / (AmE), / səˈspɪʃəs / (BrE)
unwilling to trust others or confide in them; showing distrust or doubt.
distrustful; wary of others

Examples

She grew suspicious of his evasive answers and refused to tell him anything personal.
After the incident, the villagers became suspicious of strangers passing through town.
He gave her a suspicious look, unsure if he could trust her intentions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trusting - willing to trust others or confide; opposite of suspicious
  • Synonym: distrustful - feeling or showing distrust; similar in meaning to suspicious

Etymology

From Old French 'suspecious', from Latin 'suspiciosus' (full of suspicion), from 'suspicio' (mistrust).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02473505-s
Synset POS: s
