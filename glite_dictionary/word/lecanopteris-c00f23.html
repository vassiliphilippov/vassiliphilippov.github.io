lecanopteris

1. NOUN

/ ˌliːkənˈɒptərɪs / (AmE), / ˌlɛkənˈɒptərɪs / (BrE)
a genus of unusual ferns with swollen, hollow rhizomes that provide shelter for symbiotic ants
a genus of ferns with ant-housing rhizomes

Examples

The roots of lecanopteris provide a safe haven for colonies of ants in the rainforest.
Botanists are fascinated by the unique relationship between lecanopteris and ants.
Several species of lecanopteris are grown as ornamental plants in shaded greenhouses.

Etymology

From Greek 'lekanē' (basin, dish) + 'pteris' (fern).
