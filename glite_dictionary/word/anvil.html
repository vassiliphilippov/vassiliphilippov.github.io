anvil

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.vəl / (AmE), / ˈæn.vəl / (BrE)
a heavy block of iron or steel with a flat top, used by blacksmiths and metalworkers for shaping hot metals by hammering
metalworking block for shaping metals

Examples

The blacksmith placed the heated horseshoe on the anvil and began to hammer it into shape.
During the demonstration, sparks flew from the anvil with each strike of the hammer.
Modern artists sometimes use an anvil to craft metal sculptures by hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forge - a blacksmith's workshop; a furnace for working metal

Etymology

Old English *anfilt(e)*, of Germanic origin; related to Old High German *anafalt* (anvil).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæn.vəl / (AmE), / ˈæn.vɪl / (BrE)
a small bone in the middle ear that is located between the malleus and the stapes and transmits sound vibrations; also called the incus
middle ear bone; incus
medical

Examples

The anvil receives vibrations from the malleus and passes them to the stapes.
Damage to the anvil can result in hearing loss or reduced sound conduction.
Medical students must learn the function of the anvil in the auditory system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incus - the medical term for the anvil bone in the middle ear
  • Degree Lower: malleus - the hammer-shaped bone in the middle ear, adjacent to the anvil
  • Degree Higher: stapes - the stirrup-shaped bone in the middle ear, adjacent to the anvil
