playday

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈpleɪ.deɪ / (BrE)
A day or a period set aside for play, recreation, or diversion from regular work or studies.
a day set aside for play or recreation

Examples

The school organizes a playday every spring to let students relax and participate in games.
After a long week of exams, the teachers declared Friday to be a special playday for all classes.
Many children look forward to their annual playday at the community park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holiday - a day of festivity or recreation when no work is done

Etymology

From 'play' + 'day', meaning a day for play.
