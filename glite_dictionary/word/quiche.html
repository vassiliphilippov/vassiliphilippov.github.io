Quiche

1. NOUN

/ kiːˈtʃeɪ / (AmE), / kiːˈtʃeɪ / (BrE)
the Mayan language spoken primarily by the Quiche people of the western highlands of Guatemala.
a Mayan language of Guatemala

Examples

Many ancient texts are written in Quiche, shedding light on Mayan civilization.
Linguists are studying how Quiche has evolved over the centuries.
Her grandmother still speaks Quiche at home every day.

Etymology

From Spanish 'quiché', from the Maya self-designation 'Kʼicheʼ'.

2. NOUN

/ kiˈtʃeɪ / (AmE), / kiˈtʃeɪ / (BrE)
A member of a Mayan people inhabiting south-central Guatemala.
Mayan people of Guatemala

Examples

Many Quiche still speak their ancestral language in daily life.
The Quiche have a rich tradition of weaving and storytelling.
A group of Quiche elders gathered to perform the ritual.

Etymology

From Spanish 'quiché', from the Quiché people's self-designation 'K’iche’'.
