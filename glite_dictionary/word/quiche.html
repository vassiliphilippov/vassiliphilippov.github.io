quiche

1. NOUN

/ kiʃ / (AmE), / kiːʃ / (BrE)
a baked tart with a rich unsweetened egg custard filling, often containing cheese, meat, seafood, or vegetables
savoury baked custard tart

Examples

We served a delicious spinach quiche at the picnic yesterday.
The bakery offers both vegetarian and ham quiches every morning.
She learned how to bake a classic quiche during her cooking class.

Etymology

From French 'quiche', originally from Lorraine dialect (German 'Kuchen' meaning 'cake').
