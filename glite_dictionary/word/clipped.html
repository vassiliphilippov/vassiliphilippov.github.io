clipped

1. ADJ

/ klɪpt / (AmE), / klɪpt / (BrE)
cut or trimmed by clipping, especially in a tidy or precise manner
cut or trimmed by clipping

Examples

The clipped bushes gave the garden a neat appearance.
She admired his neat, clipped beard in the mirror.
A clipped hedge lined the edge of the property.

Synset Examples

a handsome man with a clipped moustache
clipped hedges
close-clipped lawns
a clipped poodle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trimmed - made neat or tidy by cutting away irregular parts

Etymology

From the verb 'clip' (to cut), from Old Norse 'klippa' (to shear, cut).

2. ADJ

/ klɪpt / (AmE), / klɪpt / (BrE)
(of speech or pronunciation) characterized by quick, short, and precise sounds, often sounding abrupt or concise.
having quick, short, precise sounds (of speech)

Examples

The professor spoke in a clipped tone that demanded attention.
She was recognized immediately by her clipped British accent.
His instructions were clipped and to the point, leaving no room for confusion.

Synset Examples

a clipped upper-class accent

Related Terms

  • Antonym: drawled - spoken with slow, drawn-out vowels

Etymology

From the verb 'clip' (to cut short).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01445865-s
Synset POS: s
