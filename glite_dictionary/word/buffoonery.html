buffoonery

1. NOUN

/ bəˈfuːnəri / (AmE), / bəˈfuːnəri / (BrE)
foolish, silly, or clown-like behavior intended to amuse others
foolish or clownish behavior

Examples

His constant buffoonery during the meeting annoyed his colleagues.
Tired of her brother’s buffoonery, she left the room in frustration.
The actor’s buffoonery made the children laugh throughout the entire performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomfoolery - foolish or silly behavior

Etymology

From French 'bouffonnerie' (clowning), from 'bouffon' (jester, clown).
