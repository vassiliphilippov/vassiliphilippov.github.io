mischievousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪstʃəvəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪs.tʃɪ.vəs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of showing a fondness for causing trouble in a playful or harmless way; naughtiness.
playful naughtiness; tendency to cause harmless trouble

Examples

A glint of mischievousness sparkled in her eyes as she plotted the harmless prank.
His mischievousness often got him into amusing situations at school.
Despite her reputation for mischievousness, everyone knew she meant no real harm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischief - playful misbehavior or troublemaking
  • Antonym: innocence - lack of wrongdoing or mischief

Etymology

From 'mischievous' + '-ness', ultimately from Old French 'meschever' (to end badly).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪstʃɪvəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪs.tʃɪv.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being playful and causing trouble in a light-hearted or teasing way
playful or teasing quality

Examples

Her mischievousness often brought a playful energy to the classroom.
There was a look of mischievousness in his eyes as he planned the prank.
The puppy’s mischievousness resulted in many chewed shoes around the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischief - playful misbehavior or troublemaking
  • Synonym: naughtiness - the quality of being naughty or disobedient

Etymology

From 'mischievous' with the suffix '-ness'. 'Mischievous' from Old French 'meschever' (to bring to grief).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪs.tʃɪ.vəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪs.tʃɪ.vəs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of behaving in a playful or troublesome way, often causing annoyance or minor harm
playful or troublesome behavior

Examples

Her mischievousness often got her into trouble at school.
There was a look of mischievousness in his eyes as he plotted his next prank.
His mischievousness sometimes annoyed his older siblings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischief - playful misbehavior or troublemaking

Etymology

From mischievous + -ness, ultimately from Old French meschever (to end badly, come to grief).
