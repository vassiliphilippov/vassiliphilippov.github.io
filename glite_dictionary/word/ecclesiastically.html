ecclesiastically

1. ADV

/ ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪkli / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːziˈæstɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner relating to the church or the clergy
in a church-related way

Examples

The ceremony was conducted ecclesiastically according to ancient tradition.
Their marriage was ecclesiastically recognized, even before civil authorities approved it.
Decisions were sometimes made ecclesiastically, reflecting the influence of the church.

Synset Examples

the candidate was ecclesiastically endorsed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecclesiastical - relating to the Christian Church or its clergy

Etymology

From ecclesiastical + -ly, ultimately from Greek ekklēsiastikos (of the assembly/church).
