ketoheptose

1. NOUN

/ ˌkiːtoʊˈhɛptoʊs / (AmE), / ˌkiːtəʊˈhɛptəʊs / (BrE)
A monosaccharide (simple sugar) containing seven carbon atoms and a ketone group.
seven-carbon sugar with a ketone group

Examples

The biochemistry textbook described ketoheptose as a rare sugar found in certain bacteria.
A major difference between an aldoheptose and a ketoheptose is the location of the carbonyl group.
Scientists synthesized a new ketoheptose to study its metabolic pathways.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aldose - a sugar containing an aldehyde group
  • Synonym: heptose - a monosaccharide with seven carbon atoms

Etymology

From 'keto-' (relating to a ketone group) and 'heptose' (a seven-carbon sugar).
