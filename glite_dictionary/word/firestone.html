firestone

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəstəʊn / (BrE)
A type of sandstone that can withstand intense heat, used to line fireplaces, furnaces, and kilns.
heat-resistant sandstone for lining fireplaces

Examples

The old cottage had a hearth lined with firestone to withstand the flames.
Kilns are often constructed using firestone because of its resistance to high temperatures.
She sourced firestone for the fireplace renovation to ensure durability and safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandstone - a sedimentary rock consisting of sand or quartz grains cemented together

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəstəʊn / (BrE)
a piece of flint used to produce sparks for lighting a fire
piece of flint for lighting fires

Examples

The blacksmith struck the firestone against steel to create a shower of sparks.
Early travelers always carried a firestone to help them make campfires.
With only a firestone and some dry leaves, she managed to light a small fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flint - a hard stone used to produce sparks

Etymology

From Old English 'fȳr' (fire) + 'stān' (stone), meaning 'fire stone'.
