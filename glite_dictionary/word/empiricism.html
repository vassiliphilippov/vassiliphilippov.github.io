empiricism

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈpɪr.ɪ.sɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪr.ɪ.sɪzəm / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience, emphasizing evidence from observation and experimentation.
theory that knowledge comes from experience

Examples

Some philosophers argue that empiricism provides a solid foundation for scientific method.
The debate between empiricism and rationalism shaped much of modern philosophy.
Locke's philosophy is often cited as a classic defense of empiricism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rationalism - the theory that reason rather than experience is the foundation of knowledge

Etymology

From Greek empeiria (experience) via French empirisme and Medieval Latin empirismus.

2. NOUN

/ ɛmˈpɪr.ɪˌsɪz.əm / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪr.ɪ.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The practice of treating patients or giving medical advice based solely on practical experience and observation, disregarding established scientific findings or theory.
medical practice based on experience, not science

Examples

Older physicians sometimes relied on empiricism rather than modern scientific methods.
The dangers of empiricism became evident when ineffective remedies persisted for centuries.
Medical students are taught to be wary of untested empiricism in clinical decision-making.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: evidence-based medicine - medical practice based on scientific evidence

Etymology

From Greek *empeiria* (experience) via late Latin *empiricus*.

3. NOUN

/ ɛmˈpɪr.ɪˌsɪz.əm / (AmE), / ɛmˈpɪr.ɪ.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the theory or practice that knowledge comes primarily from sensory experience or observation, especially as applied in science and art through empirical methods
doctrine that knowledge comes from experience or observation

Examples

Modern science is fundamentally based on empiricism, relying on observation and experiment rather than abstract theory alone.
The rise of empiricism in the seventeenth century changed the way science was conducted.
She discussed the merits of empiricism versus rationalism in her philosophy essay.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rationalism - doctrine that reason is the primary source of knowledge
  • Synonym: observation - the act of noticing or observing something carefully

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'empirismus', from Greek 'empeiria' (experience).
