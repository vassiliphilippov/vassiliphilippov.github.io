erythrocyte

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɪθrəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪθrəˌsaɪt / (BrE)
a mature red blood cell that contains hemoglobin and carries oxygen to body tissues; it is typically a biconcave disc without a nucleus
a red blood cell
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a low erythrocyte count could indicate anemia.
Hemoglobin is found inside every erythrocyte, enabling the transport of oxygen.
A healthy adult typically has millions of erythrocytes in each drop of blood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red blood cell - a common term for an erythrocyte
  • Antonym: leukocyte - a white blood cell

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' (red) + 'kytos' (cell).
