roughshod

1. ADJ

/ ˈrʌfˌʃɑd / (AmE), / ˈrʌfʃɒd / (BrE)
(of a person or their actions) showing no pity or concern for others; harsh or cruel.
showing no concern for others; harsh

Examples

The manager's roughshod approach left many employees feeling undervalued and mistreated.
The regime's roughshod tactics violated basic human rights.
She objected to her colleague's roughshod style of leadership, which ignored everyone's input.

Synset Examples

a barbarous crime
brutal beatings
cruel tortures
Stalin's roughshod treatment of the kulaks
a savage slap
vicious kicks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brutal - showing extreme cruelty or lack of feeling
  • Synonym: cruel - showing a desire to cause pain or suffering
  • Antonym: gentle - having or showing a mild, kind, or tender temperament

Etymology

Originally from the literal phrase 'shod with rough shoes', referring to horses wearing horseshoes with protruding nails for better grip.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrʌfˌʃɑd / (AmE), / ˈrʌfʃɒd / (BrE)
(of a horse or horseshoe) having horseshoes fitted with projecting nails or points to prevent slipping.
with horseshoes that prevent slipping

Examples

The blacksmith ensured the mare was roughshod before riding on icy roads.
Farmers in the region often have their horses roughshod for the winter months.
The old painting depicted a knight on a roughshod steed.

Etymology

From 'rough' + 'shod', meaning shod (fitted with shoes) in a rough manner.

3. ADJ

/ ˈrʌfˌʃɑd / (AmE), / ˈrʌfʃɒd / (BrE)
(of a person or actions) domineering, harsh, or treating others without fairness or consideration; unjustly overbearing.
domineering or overbearing

Examples

The new supervisor ran roughshod over the established rules, ignoring everyone's objections.
Many citizens felt the policy changes were implemented roughshod and without proper debate.
He has a reputation for taking a roughshod approach to negotiations, rarely listening to opposing views.

Synset Examples

incensed at the government's heavy-handed economic policies
a manager who rode roughshod over all opposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heavy-handed - overly forceful or oppressive in manner
  • Synonym: tyrannical - exercising power in a cruel or arbitrary way
  • Antonym: fair - treating people equally without injustice

Etymology

Originally referring to horses shod with shoes having projecting nails for better grip, from 'rough' + 'shod'.
