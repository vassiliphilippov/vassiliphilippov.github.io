Calycophyllum candidissimum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæ.lɪ.siˈɑː.fɪləm ˌkæn.dɪˈdɪs.ɪ.məm / (AmE), / ˌkælɪsɪˈɒfɪləm ˌkændɪˈdɪsɪməm / (BrE)
A tree native to Central America and the West Indies, known for its tough, elastic wood.
Central American tree with tough wood

Examples

Furniture makers value Calycophyllum candidissimum for its durable and flexible wood.
The leaves of Calycophyllum candidissimum are known for their bright white color beneath.
Conservationists are working to protect Calycophyllum candidissimum habitats from deforestation.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'kalyx' (calyx) and 'phyllon' (leaf), plus Latin 'candidissimum' (most bright, very white) – referring to features of the tree.
