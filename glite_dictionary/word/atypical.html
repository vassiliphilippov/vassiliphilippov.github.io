atypical

1. ADJ

/ eɪˈtɪpɪkəl / (AmE), / eɪˈtɪpɪkəl / (BrE)
not representative of a type, group, or class; not typical or usual.
not typical; uncharacteristic

Examples

The test results were atypical and prompted further investigation by the doctors.
His atypical approach to problem-solving often led to innovative solutions.
It is atypical for her to arrive late to meetings, as she is usually very punctual.

Synset Examples

a class of atypical mosses
atypical behavior is not the accepted type of response that we expect from children

Related Terms

  • Antonym: typical - having the usual qualities or features of a particular type of person or thing

Etymology

From prefix 'a-' (not) + 'typical'.

2. ADJ

/ eɪˈtɪpɪkəl / (AmE), / eɪˈtɪpɪkəl / (BrE)
not typical; deviating from what is usual or expected; somewhat odd or abnormal.
not typical; unusual or irregular

Examples

The patient's symptoms were considered atypical for that disease.
Her atypical response surprised everyone at the meeting.
This year's weather has been remarkably atypical for the region.

Synset Examples

these days large families are atypical
atypical clinical findings
atypical pneumonia
highly irregular behavior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: typical - having the usual qualities of a particular type or group

Etymology

From Greek a- (not) + typical, based on Latin typicus.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01601391-s
Synset POS: s
