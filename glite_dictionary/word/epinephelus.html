Epinephelus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈnɛfələs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈnɛfɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of large marine fish in the grouper or sea bass family, found in warm seas worldwide.
genus of groupers or sea bass

Examples

Several species within the genus Epinephelus are prized in commercial fisheries worldwide.
The Epinephelus genus includes the well-known Nassau grouper and the gag grouper.
Biologists discovered a new Epinephelus species living among the coral reefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grouper - large marine fish, member of the Epinephelus genus or related genera

Etymology

From Greek 'epinephelos', meaning 'cloudy above'; used as a genus name in zoological Latin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈnɛfələs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈnɛfɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of groupers or sea bass, large marine fishes found in warm seas; includes many commercially important species.
genus of marine groupers or sea bass

Examples

The Epinephelus genus includes some of the largest groupers found in coral reefs.
Several species in the Epinephelus genus are important for commercial fishing.
Scientists are studying how climate change affects populations of Epinephelus in the Indian Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grouper - large marine fish in the family Epinephelidae, including the genus Epinephelus
  • Synonym: sea bass - common name for various marine fish including groupers

Etymology

From Greek 'epinephēlos' meaning 'cloudy above', used as a classical name.
