myopic

1. ADJ

/ maɪˈɑpɪk / (AmE), / maɪˈɒpɪk / (BrE)
Unable to see distant objects clearly; suffering from myopia.
unable to see distant objects clearly

Examples

He was diagnosed as myopic at the age of ten and needed glasses for distance vision.
Without her glasses, everything more than a few feet away appears myopic to her.
The optometrist explained that being myopic means you'll need correction to see distant objects clearly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nearsighted - unable to see distant objects clearly
  • Antonym: hyperopic - able to see distant objects more easily; farsighted

Etymology

From Greek myōps (short-sighted), from myein (to close) + ōps (eye).

2. ADJ

/ maɪˈɑpɪk / (AmE), / maɪˈɒpɪk / (BrE)
Lacking foresight or the ability to think or plan for the future; having a narrow or limited perspective.
lacking foresight or broad view

Examples

His myopic approach to business ignored long-term consequences.
Critics accused the government of making myopic decisions regarding environmental policy.
We cannot afford to be myopic in planning the city's future.

Synset Examples

a short view of the problem
shortsighted policies
shortsighted critics derided the plan
myopic thinking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortsighted - lacking foresight or scope; unable to plan for the future
  • Antonym: far-sighted - having the ability to imagine or plan for the future; showing foresight

Etymology

From Greek myōpikos (nearsighted), from myōps (one who shuts the eyes, shortsighted).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01902113-s
Synset POS: s
