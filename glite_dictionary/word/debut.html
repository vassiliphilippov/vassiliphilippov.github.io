debut

1. NOUN

/ deɪˈbju / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.bju / (BrE)
the formal introduction of a young woman, especially a debutante, into society.
formal introduction to society (of a debutante)

Examples

The family's oldest daughter made her debut at the annual charity ball.
Her debut was the highlight of the social season in Paris.
They attended the elegant debut of their cousin last spring.

Etymology

From French 'début' (a first appearance), from 'débuter' (to lead off).

2. NOUN

/ deɪˈbju / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.bju / (BrE)
the first public appearance or presentation of someone or something; the beginning of something new, especially in an official or formal way
first appearance or introduction

Examples

After months of rehearsals, the band finally made their debut at the music festival.
The company's latest smartphone made its debut in stores last Friday.
Her acting debut in the lead role was widely praised by critics.

Synset Examples

they looked forward to the debut of their new product line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: premiere - first performance or showing of a play, film, etc.

Etymology

From French début (a first appearance), from débuter (to begin).

Source: WordNet

/ dəˈbju / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.bjuː / (BrE)
