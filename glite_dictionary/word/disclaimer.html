disclaimer

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkleɪmɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈkleɪmə / (BrE)
(Law) A formal statement declining a legal claim, right, or responsibility for something.
formal statement declining legal claim or responsibility
legal

Examples

The company issued a disclaimer stating it was not liable for any damages caused by misuse.
By signing the disclaimer, she formally gave up her claim to the property.
Every published report included a legal disclaimer in the fine print.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renunciation - the act of giving up a right, claim, or possession
  • Antonym: assertion - the act of stating or claiming something forcefully

Etymology

From Middle English, from Anglo-Norman *disclamer*, derived from Old French *desclamer* (to disclaim), from Latin *dis-* (apart) + *clamare* (to call out).

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkleɪmɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈkleɪmə / (BrE)
A statement that denies responsibility or connection with something; a formal denial of knowledge, affiliation, or involvement.
denial of responsibility or connection

Examples

The company issued a disclaimer stating it was not responsible for any losses incurred.
He included a disclaimer at the end of his article denying any personal involvement in the matter.
Without a proper disclaimer, readers might assume the opinions expressed are those of the organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denial - statement that something is not true or real
  • Antonym: admission - acknowledgement of truth or connection

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *disclamer*, from Old French *desclamer* (“to disclaim”), ultimately from Latin *clamare* (“to call out”).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07220606-n
Synset POS: n
