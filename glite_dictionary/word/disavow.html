to disavow

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsəˈvaʊ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈvaʊ / (BrE)
to deny responsibility for or connection with something; to refuse to acknowledge or accept.
deny responsibility or association

Examples

After the scandal broke, the politician quickly disavowed any knowledge of the illegal activities.
The company disavowed responsibility for the defective products found in the market.
Despite the rumors, she firmly disavowed any connection to the controversial group.

Synset Examples

Her husband disavowed her after 30 years of marriage and six children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deny - to state that something is not true or that you do not believe something
  • Antonym: to acknowledge - to accept or admit the existence or truth of something

Etymology

From Old French *desavouer*, from Latin *dis-* (apart, away) + *avow* (to affirm, acknowledge).
