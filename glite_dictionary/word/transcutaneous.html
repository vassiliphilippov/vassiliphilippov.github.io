transcutaneous

1. ADJ

/ ˌtrænz.kjuːˈteɪ.ni.əs / (AmE), / ˌtræns.kjuːˈteɪ.ni.əs / (BrE)
(of a medical procedure or administration) carried out or applied through unbroken skin, as with certain medications or treatments.
through the unbroken skin
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a transcutaneous pain patch to deliver medication steadily over several days.
Researchers evaluated the safety of transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation for chronic back pain.
A transcutaneous device measures oxygen levels without the need for a blood sample.

Synset Examples

transdermal estrogen
percutaneous absorption

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transdermal - administered through the skin, especially of medication via a patch
  • Synonym: percutaneous - effected or performed through the skin

Etymology

From Latin 'trans-' (across) + 'cutis' (skin) + English suffix '-ous'.
