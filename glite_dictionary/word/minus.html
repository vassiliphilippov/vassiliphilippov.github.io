minus

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
on the negative side or below a defined standard or scale, often used with numbers or grades.
negative, below standard or zero

Examples

The temperature dropped to minus ten degrees overnight.
She received a grade of C minus on her essay.
His bank account balance is now minus fifty dollars.

Synset Examples

minus 5 degrees
a grade of B minus

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plus - on the positive side or above a defined standard or scale

Etymology

From Latin minus (less).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01825325-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
an arithmetic operation that calculates the difference between two numbers by subtracting one from the other
arithmetic subtraction operation

Examples

In the equation five minus two, the answer is three.
The teacher explained how to use minus when subtracting numbers.
Write the number sentence as seven minus four equals three.

Synset Examples

the subtraction of three from four leaves one
four minus three equals one

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subtraction - the process of taking one number or amount away from another
  • Antonym: plus - the arithmetic operation of addition
  • Antonym: addition - an arithmetic operation for finding the total of two or more numbers

Etymology

From Latin minus (less).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00873540-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
(used before a noun) involving a disadvantage or harmful effect; negative.
indicating a disadvantage or harmful factor

Examples

A high staff turnover rate is often seen as a minus factor in company performance.
In the list of pros and cons, the lack of parking was a major minus.
The high price could be viewed as a significant minus for potential buyers.

Synset Examples

minus (or negative) factors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negative - expressing or meaning a disadvantage or an adverse quality
  • Antonym: plus - involving an advantage or beneficial factor

Etymology

From Latin minus (less), used in English since the 15th century, later extended to non-mathematical senses.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00066276-s
Synset POS: s
