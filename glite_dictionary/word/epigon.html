epigon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪˌɡɒn / (BrE)
A person who imitates the work of a distinguished writer, artist, or musician, usually regarded as inferior to the original.
inferior imitator in the arts

Examples

Many critics dismissed the composer as an epigon lacking the innovation of his mentor.
He feared being labeled an epigon rather than an original thinker.
In art history, some painters are overshadowed by the masters they epigonize.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitator - someone who copies the style of another
  • Antonym: original - the creator or first author of a work

Etymology

From German 'Epigone', from Latin 'Epigonus', from Greek 'Epígonos' (descendant).
