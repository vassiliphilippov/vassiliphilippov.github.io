augmentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːɡ.menˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
In music, the statement of a theme in notes of greater duration than the original, typically making each note twice as long.
musical theme stated in longer notes

Examples

The composer used augmentation to give the melody a slower, more solemn character.
In the fugue, the subject appears in augmentation in the bass voice.
Through augmentation, the familiar theme becomes more dramatic and expansive.

Etymology

From Latin augmentatio (an increase), from augmentare (to increase).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɔːɡmənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːɡmənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the amount by which something is made greater or increased
an increase in amount or size

Examples

There has been a significant augmentation in the company's profits this year.
The augmentation of rainfall over the last month has improved the harvest.
The committee approved an augmentation of funds for the new project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - an instance of becoming greater in size, amount, or degree
  • Antonym: reduction - the amount by which something is made smaller or less

Etymology

From Latin 'augmentatio', from 'augmentare' (to increase).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɔːɡmənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːɡmɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something greater in size, amount, or degree; the act of increasing or enhancing something
the act of increasing or enhancing

Examples

The company invested in the augmentation of its production capacity to meet rising demand.
Medical researchers are exploring brain augmentation to enhance human memory and cognition.
The augmentation of staff numbers helped improve customer service at the help desk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - the act of becoming greater or making greater
  • Antonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller; the opposite of increase or augmentation

Etymology

From Latin augmentatio (an increase), from augmentare (to increase).
