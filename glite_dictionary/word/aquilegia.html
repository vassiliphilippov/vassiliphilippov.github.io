aquilegia

1. NOUN

/ ˌækwɪˈliːdʒə / (AmE), / ˌakwɪˈliːdʒiə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Aquilegia, known for its irregular, showy spurred flowers, native to north temperate regions, especially mountainous areas.
genus of flowering plants with spurred flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with blooming aquilegia in vibrant shades of purple and blue.
Mountain meadows often feature wild aquilegia growing among the rocks.
She planted several varieties of aquilegia to attract bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: columbine - common name for plants in the genus Aquilegia

Etymology

From Latin *aquilegia*, meaning 'columbine'.
