Aquilegia

1. NOUN

/ ˌækwəˈliːdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌakwɪˈliːdʒɪə / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous perennial plants, commonly known as columbines, with distinctive spurred flowers, native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
a genus of flowering plants (columbine)

Examples

Several species of Aquilegia are popular in ornamental gardens for their delicate flowers.
The vibrant blue flowers of Aquilegia can often be seen in woodland areas.
Botanists are studying the genetic diversity within the Aquilegia genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: columbine - a common name for plants of the genus Aquilegia

Etymology

From Latin *aquilegia*, meaning 'columbine', possibly from 'aquila' (eagle), referring to the shape of the flower's spurs.
