change-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪndʒˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒʌp / (BrE)
a slow pitch thrown in baseball to deceive the batter, who is expecting a fastball
slow, deceptive baseball pitch
american

Examples

The pitcher threw a brilliant change-up that caught the batter off guard.
His change-up is almost indistinguishable from his fastball, making him tough to hit.
After two fastballs, he fooled the hitter with a slow change-up down the middle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fastball - a high-velocity pitch in baseball
  • Synonym: curveball - a pitch in baseball that curves as it approaches the batter

Etymology

From 'change' + 'up', indicating a change of pace; first used in American baseball in the late 19th century.
