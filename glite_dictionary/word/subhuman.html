subhuman

1. ADJ

/ sʌbˈhjumən / (AmE), / sʌbˈhjuːmən / (BrE)
(of conditions, treatment, etc.) below the level considered acceptable for human beings; unfit for human life.
unfit for human beings; below human standards

Examples

The refugees were living in subhuman conditions with little food or shelter.
The factory workers protested against the subhuman environment in which they were forced to work.
Access to clean water and sanitation put an end to the subhuman circumstances in the neighborhood.

Synset Examples

subhuman conditions of life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhuman - lacking human qualities of compassion and mercy; cruel or barbaric

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (below) + 'humanus' (human).

2. ADJ

/ sʌbˈhjuːmən / (AmE), / sʌbˈhjuːmən / (BrE)
regarded as less than human or not deserving the rights or dignity of a human being
less than human; undignified or inhumane

Examples

The dictator's regime treated its political prisoners as subhuman beings, denying them basic rights.
Many activists protest the subhuman living conditions found in overcrowded refugee camps.
Some early scientists classified certain primates as subhuman, though this view is now considered outdated.

Synset Examples

treated natives as subhuman
a subhuman spectacle
the subhuman primates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhuman - lacking human qualities of compassion or mercy
  • Antonym: humane - showing compassion or benevolence

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under, below) + 'humanus' (human).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01264600-a
Synset POS: a
