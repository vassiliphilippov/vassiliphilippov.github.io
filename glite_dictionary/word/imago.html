imago

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɑːɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɪˈmɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
(psychoanalysis) an idealized mental image of a person, especially a parent, formed in childhood and carried into adulthood.
idealized mental image, esp. of a parent (psychoanalysis)

Examples

In therapy, she explored how her childhood imago influenced her adult relationships.
His persistent search for perfection was shaped by an unrealistic imago of his father.
Psychoanalytic theory suggests the imago plays a crucial role in personality development.

Etymology

From Latin *imago* (image, likeness).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈmeɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɪˈmɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
The adult stage of an insect following metamorphosis, in which it is sexually mature.
adult insect after metamorphosis
medical

Examples

The butterfly's imago emerges from the chrysalis with fully developed wings.
Entomologists carefully examined the imago to identify the species.
After pupating, the insect reached its final imago stage and was ready to reproduce.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: larva - the immature, wingless, feeding stage of an insect
  • Degree Lower: pupa - the non-feeding, usually inactive stage between larva and imago

Etymology

From Latin imago (image, likeness, form).
