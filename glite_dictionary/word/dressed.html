dressed

1. ADJ

/ drɛst / (AmE), / drɛst / (BrE)
(of lumber or stone) trimmed and smoothed to create an even surface.
trimmed and smoothed (material)

Examples

The carpenter chose dressed lumber for the interior walls.
Only dressed stone was used in the exterior facade of the building.
They prefer dressed wood because it is smoother and easier to paint.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rough - (of lumber or stone) not trimmed or smoothed

2. ADJ

/ drɛst / (AmE), / drɛst / (BrE)
(of a wound) treated with medications and covered with a protective dressing.
wound treated and covered
medical

Examples

The nurse quickly had the patient's wound dressed to prevent infection.
All injuries must be properly dressed before transporting the casualty.
His arm was carefully dressed after the accident to keep it clean.

  • Synonym: bandaged - having a bandage applied
