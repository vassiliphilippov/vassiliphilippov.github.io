farandole

1. NOUN

/ ˌfærənˈdoʊl / (AmE), / ˌfar.ənˈdɒl / (BrE)
a lively traditional dance from Provence in which dancers join hands and perform various figures
lively Provençal chain dance

Examples

The festival concluded with a festive farandole that brought everyone onto the village square.
Children eagerly joined the farandole, weaving in and out of the lively procession.
Musicians played traditional tunes while dancers performed a colorful farandole.

Etymology

From French 'farandole', likely from Provençal.
