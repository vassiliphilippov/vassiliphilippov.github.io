immotility

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmoʊˈtɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ɪˌməʊˈtɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state of being unable to move or lacking the ability for spontaneous movement, especially in a biological or medical context
lack of ability to move (medical/scientific)
medical

Examples

The immotility of the sperm cells was a significant factor in the couple's infertility.
In some neurological disorders, immotility can affect a person's ability to perform basic movements.
The researchers studied the effects of chemicals on the immotility of certain bacteria under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immobility - the state of not moving; motionlessness

Etymology

From Latin 'immobilis' ('immovable'), with the suffix '-ity' forming nouns of quality or state.
