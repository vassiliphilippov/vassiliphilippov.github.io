Scopolia carniolica

1. NOUN

/ skoʊˈpoʊliə ˌkɑrniˈɑlɪkə / (AmE), / skəʊˈpəʊliə ˌkɑːnɪˈɒlɪkə / (BrE)
A European herbaceous plant in the nightshade family, known as a source of the compound scopolamine.
herbaceous plant of the nightshade family

Examples

Scopolia carniolica contains alkaloids that have been used for medicinal purposes.
In Europe, Scopolia carniolica is cultivated mainly for its pharmaceutical value.
Botanists often study Scopolia carniolica alongside other members of the nightshade family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scopolamine - a tropane alkaloid drug obtained from various plants including Scopolia carniolica

Etymology

Named after Italian physician Giovanni Antonio Scopoli; 'carniolica' refers to Carniola, a region in Slovenia.
