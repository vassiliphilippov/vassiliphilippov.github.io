transparence

1. NOUN

/ trænˈspɛɹəns / (AmE), / trænˈspærəns / (BrE)
The quality or state of allowing light or other electromagnetic radiation to pass through; transparency.
quality of allowing passage of light

Examples

The transparence of the glass allowed sunlight to flood the room.
Scientists measured the transparence of the material using specialized equipment.
A high degree of transparence is essential for optical lenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transparency - the quality of being transparent
  • Antonym: opacity - the quality of not allowing light to pass through

Etymology

From Middle French transparence, from Medieval Latin transparentia, from transparentem meaning transparent.

2. NOUN

/ trænˈspɛr əns / (AmE), / trænˈspær əns / (BrE)
the quality or state of being clear and allowing light to pass through; transparency
state of being clear and see-through

Examples

The transparence of the crystal allowed the sunlight to pass through effortlessly.
Her explanation had a clarity and transparence that made the concept easy to grasp.
Painters valued the transparence of the glaze to create luminous effects on the canvas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transparency - the quality of being transparent
  • Antonym: opacity - the quality of being opaque

Etymology

From French *transparence*, from Medieval Latin *transparentia*.
