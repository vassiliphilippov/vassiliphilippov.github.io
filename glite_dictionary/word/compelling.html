compelling

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈpɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an argument, evidence, or reason) evoking interest, attention, or admiration in a powerfully irresistible way; persuasive and convincing.
convincing; persuasive

Examples

The lawyer presented compelling evidence that changed the course of the trial.
Her compelling argument convinced even the doubters in the audience.
There is no compelling reason to change the current policy.

Synset Examples

new and compelling evidence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convincing - able to persuade someone to believe that something is true or valid
  • Antonym: unconvincing - not capable of persuading someone; not believable

Etymology

Present participle of 'compel,' from Latin 'compellere' (to drive together, force).

2. ADJ

/ kəmˈpɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
Having a powerful or irresistible effect; able to drive or force someone to act.
having a powerful or driving effect

Examples

His compelling ambition drove him to achieve great things.
The speech offered a compelling reason for immediate action.
Her compelling desire to help others inspired the entire community.

Synset Examples

compelling ambition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irresistible - impossible to resist; overpowering
  • Synonym: convincing - able to persuade; causing belief
  • Antonym: unimpressive - lacking in effect or force; not powerful

Etymology

Present participle of 'compel', from Latin 'compellere' (to force, drive).
