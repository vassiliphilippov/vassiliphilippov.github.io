feverish

1. ADJ

/ ˈfiːvərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfiːvərɪʃ / (BrE)
having or showing symptoms of a fever, such as high temperature or restlessness; also, displaying intense emotion or activity resembling a fever
having or showing a fever

Examples

She felt feverish and weak, so she decided to stay home from school.
His feverish excitement was evident as he waited for the concert to begin.
The child's feverish cheeks worried his mother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: febrile - having or showing symptoms of a fever

Etymology

From Old French *fievre* (fever) and English suffix *-ish* (having the quality of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfiːvərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfiːvərɪʃ / (BrE)
Showing or involving intense excitement, agitation, or activity.
intensely excited or agitated

Examples

After hearing the news, the crowd grew feverish with anticipation.
She worked at a feverish pace to meet the deadline.
There was a feverish excitement in the air before the concert began.

Synset Examples

worked at a feverish pace

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing agitation or strong emotion

Etymology

From 'fever' + '-ish', relating to fever or fever-like states.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00086895-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfiːvərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfiːvərɪʃ / (BrE)
showing symptoms of fever or relating to a fever; feeling hotter than normal and often unwell.
having or relating to a fever

Examples

She felt feverish and weak, so she stayed home from work.
His feverish skin was hot to the touch after hours in bed.
Doctors checked for feverish reactions after administering the vaccine.

Synset Examples

a febrile reaction caused by an allergen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: febrile - relating to or characterized by fever

Etymology

From Middle English fevere (fever) + -ish (adjective suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02737451-a
Synset POS: a
