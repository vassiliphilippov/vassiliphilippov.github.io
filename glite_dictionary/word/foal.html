foal

1. NOUN

/ foʊl / (AmE), / fəʊl / (BrE)
A young horse, especially one that is less than one year old.
young horse

Examples

The mare watched over her foal as it took its first steps.
Every spring, several foals are born on the farm.
A newborn foal needs lots of care and attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colt - a young male horse
  • Synonym: filly - a young female horse

Etymology

From Old English 'fola', related to Old Norse 'foli' (foal).

2. VERB

/ foʊl / (AmE), / fəʊl / (BrE)
(Of a mare) to give birth to a foal.
(of a mare) give birth to a foal

Examples

The mare foaled early in the morning, producing a healthy colt.
All the horses in the stable foal around the same time each spring.
After she foals, the mare is carefully monitored to ensure her health and the foal's.

Synset Examples

the mare foaled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give birth - to produce offspring

Etymology

From Old English fola (young horse).
