Druid

1. NOUN

/ ˈdruɪd / (AmE), / ˈdruːɪd / (BrE)
a member of a high-ranking class of ancient Celtic priests, especially in Britain, Ireland, and Gaul before the arrival of Christianity
ancient Celtic priest

Examples

The Druids played a crucial role in ancient Celtic society, acting as priests, teachers, and judges.
Legends describe the Druids as wise men who understood the secrets of nature.
During the festival, a Druid led the community in a traditional ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priest - a person who performs religious ceremonies

Etymology

From Latin 'Druidae', from Gaulish 'druides'.
