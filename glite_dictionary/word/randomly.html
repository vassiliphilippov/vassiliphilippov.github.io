randomly

1. ADV

/ ˈræn.dəm.li / (AmE), / ˈræn.dəm.li / (BrE)
in a way that happens without a specific pattern, order, or method
in a random manner

Examples

The computers assigned seating randomly for the exam.
She picked three names randomly from the list for the prize drawing.
The samples were selected randomly to avoid bias.

Synset Examples

the houses were randomly scattered
bullets were fired into the crowd at random

Related Terms

  • Antonym: systematically - in a methodical or organized manner
  • Synonym: random - having no specific pattern, order, or purpose (adjective)

Etymology

From 'random' + '-ly', where 'random' comes from Old French 'randon' (force, impulse, speed).
