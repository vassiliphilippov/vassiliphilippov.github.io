lowbrow

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊˌbraʊ / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌbraʊ / (BrE)
(of a person or their tastes) not highly intellectual or cultured; regarded as less sophisticated.
not intellectual or cultured

Examples

Some people enjoy lowbrow comedies more than highbrow dramas.
Her lowbrow taste in art surprised her sophisticated friends.
The critics dismissed the novel as too lowbrow for serious readers.

Synset Examples

lowbrow tastes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: highbrow - having intellectual or cultured tastes
  • Degree Higher: middlebrow - having moderately intellectual or cultured tastes

Etymology

From 'low' + 'brow', originally referring to the physical brow, coming to represent a lack of intellectual refinement.

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊˌbraʊ / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌbraʊ / (BrE)
a person who is not interested in or does not appreciate intellectual or artistic matters
person uninterested in intellectual pursuits

Examples

He was criticized as a lowbrow with no appreciation for classical music or fine art.
Although she loved pop culture, she resented being dismissed as a lowbrow.
The museum hopes to attract more than just lowbrow visitors interested in blockbuster exhibitions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: highbrow - a person with intellectual or cultured interests
  • Synonym: philistine - a person who is indifferent or hostile to culture and the arts

Etymology

Formed from 'low' + 'brow', originally contrasting with 'highbrow'; 'brow' refers to the forehead, used figuratively for intellect.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10442776-n
Synset POS: n
