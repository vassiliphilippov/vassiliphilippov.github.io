Anchusa

1. NOUN

/ ænˈkjuːsə / (AmE), / anˈkjuːsə / (BrE)
a genus of rough and hairy Old World herbs in the borage family, with bright blue or violet flowers.
a genus of rough Old World herbs

Examples

The vivid blue flowers of Anchusa are popular in cottage gardens.
Many species within the Anchusa genus are cultivated for their striking color.
The leaves of Anchusa plants are characteristically rough and hairy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: borage - a plant of the family Boraginaceae, to which Anchusa belongs

Etymology

From Latin Anchusa, the name of the plant.
