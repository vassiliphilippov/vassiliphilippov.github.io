tenderisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛn.dɚ.ɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛn.dər.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of making meat tender, typically by pounding, marinating, or using enzymes.
making meat tender
british

Examples

The tenderisation of the steak made it much easier to chew.
Modern cooking often relies on chemical tenderisation to improve tough cuts of meat.
Prolonged marinating is a common method of tenderisation in many cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenderization - the act of making meat tender by pounding or marinating it (American spelling)

Etymology

From tender + -isation (British form of -ization), meaning the process of making tender.
