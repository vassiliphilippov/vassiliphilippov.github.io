Atriplex mexicana

1. NOUN

/ əˈtrɪplɛks ˌmɛksɪˈkænə / (AmE), / əˈtrɪplɛks ˌmɛksɪˈkeɪnə / (BrE)
A species of Eurasian aromatic herb in the goosefoot family, with oak-shaped leaves and yellow-green flowers, naturalized in North America.
aromatic Eurasian goosefoot plant

Examples

The fields were covered with Atriplex mexicana after the wild seeds spread.
Botanists are studying how Atriplex mexicana adapts to various climates in North America.
We identified the plant as Atriplex mexicana based on its oak-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orache - any plant of the genus Atriplex, also known as orach or mountain spinach
  • Synonym: goosefoot - common name for plants of the genus Chenopodium and related genera, including Atriplex

Etymology

From Latin: Atriplex, the genus name for orache plants, and 'mexicana', indicating Mexico as the region of discovery.
