treetop

1. NOUN

/ ˈtriˌtɑp / (AmE), / ˈtriːtɒp / (BrE)
the uppermost branches and leaves of a tree
uppermost branches and leaves of a tree

Examples

The monkey swung from one treetop to another with incredible agility.
From the balcony, we could see the sun setting just above the treetops.
A flock of birds landed in the treetop and began to sing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canopy - the upper layer of branches and foliage formed by tree crowns in a forest
