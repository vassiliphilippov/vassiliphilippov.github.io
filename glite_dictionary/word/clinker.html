clinker

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪŋkər / (AmE), / ˈklɪŋkə / (BrE)
a hard brick, often with a vitrified surface, used for paving streets or floors
a hard brick used for paving

Examples

The driveway was laid with clinker bricks for added durability.
Years of wear had barely affected the sturdy clinker pavement in the old courtyard.
Many historic streets in Europe are still paved with clinker blocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brick - a rectangular block used in building construction

Etymology

Originally from clink (imitative of the ringing sound), referring to its hard, ringing quality when struck.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɪŋkər / (AmE), / ˈklɪŋkə / (BrE)
a hard mass of incombustible matter left after the burning of coal, wood, or charcoal
incombustible residue after a fire

Examples

After the fire burned out, a large clinker remained at the bottom of the grate.
The furnace had to be cleaned to remove the buildup of old clinker.
He picked up a clinker and tossed it aside with the ashes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash - the powdery residue left after the burning of a substance

Etymology

Possibly from Middle Dutch *klinkaerd* (clinker, brick that rings),

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09268118-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈklɪŋkər / (AmE), / ˈklɪŋkə / (BrE)
(of a material) to become or form clinker as a result of burning at high temperatures.
turn into clinker by burning

Examples

If the temperature is not controlled, the furnace ash may clinker and damage the equipment.
Low-quality coal can clinker easily when used in industrial boilers.
Operators must be alert to prevent fuel from clinkering during the combustion process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuse - to become liquid or plastic through heat; to melt together
