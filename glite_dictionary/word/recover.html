to recover

1. VERB

/ rəˈkʌvər / (AmE), / rɪˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to return to a previous financial state after a loss or setback
regain financial condition after a loss

Examples

The market began to recover just a few months after the crash.
It took several years for the business to recover from its substantial losses.
She worked hard to recover the family's financial stability after the recession.

Synset Examples

We expect the stocks to recover to $2.90
The company managed to recuperate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recuperate - to recover, especially from financial loss or illness
  • Antonym: decline - to decrease in value or condition, especially financially

Etymology

From Old French *recovrer*, from Latin *recuperare* (to get back).
