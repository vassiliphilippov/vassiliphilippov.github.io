rustling

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌs.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌs.lɪŋ / (BrE)
a soft, light, and continuous sound, like that made by leaves, silk, or paper moving gently
soft, continuous noise (like leaves)

Examples

The rustling of the autumn leaves could be heard through the open window.
She was startled by the rustling in the bushes behind her.
The only sound in the silent library was the rustling of turning pages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murmur - a low, continuous background noise
  • Synonym: whisper - a soft, hushed sound or noise

Etymology

From rustle + -ing, formed as a noun from the verb 'rustle'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07407383-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌslɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌslɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of stealing cattle, especially from a farm or ranch
the theft of cattle

Examples

The sheriff was determined to put an end to rustling in the county.
Historically, rustling posed a serious problem for ranch owners in the West.
Modern technology has made it harder for criminals to get away with rustling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattle theft - the crime of stealing cows or other livestock

Etymology

From the verb 'rustle', meaning to drive off cattle, of uncertain origin; related sense first attested in the American West in the late 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00968608-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈrʌslɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌslɪŋ / (BrE)
making a soft, gentle, or whispering sound, like leaves or paper moving gently.
making a soft, gentle sound

Examples

The rustling leaves created a peaceful atmosphere in the garden.
She fell asleep to the rustling sound of the wind outside her window.
A rustling movement in the bushes caught his attention during the evening walk.

Synset Examples

a murmurous brook
a soughing wind in the pines
a slow sad susurrous rustle like the wind fingering the pines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murmurous - characterized by a low, continuous, soft sound
  • Synonym: susurrant - making a soft, murmuring, or whispering sound

Etymology

From the verb 'rustle', of imitative origin, referring to a soft, whispering sound.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01459000-s
Synset POS: s
