unambiguously

1. ADV

/ ˌʌn.æmˈbɪɡ.ju.əs.li / (AmE), / ˌʌn.æmˈbɪɡ.ju.əs.li / (BrE)
In a way that is clear and not open to more than one interpretation.
clearly; without ambiguity

Examples

The witness unambiguously identified the suspect in court.
The instructions were written unambiguously to avoid any confusion.
She unambiguously denied all the allegations against her.

Synset Examples

she stated her intentions unequivocally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ambiguously - in a way that is open to more than one interpretation

Etymology

Formed from 'unambiguous' + '-ly'; 'unambiguous' from Latin 'ambiguus' (doubtful, uncertain) with prefix 'un-' (not).

2. ADV

/ ˌʌn.æmˈbɪɡ.ju.əs.li / (AmE), / ˌʌn.æmˈbɪɡ.ju.əs.li / (BrE)
in a way that is clear and can be understood in only one way; without ambiguity
clearly, without ambiguity

Examples

The instructions were written unambiguously to avoid any confusion.
She stated unambiguously that she would not tolerate any delays.
The legal contract needs to specify the terms unambiguously.

Synset Examples

he could determine uniquely the properties of the compound

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ambiguously - in a way that is open to more than one interpretation

Etymology

Formed from 'unambiguous' + '-ly'; 'unambiguous' from Latin 'ambigere' (to wander) with prefix 'un-' (not).
