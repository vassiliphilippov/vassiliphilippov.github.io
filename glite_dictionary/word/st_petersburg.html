St. Petersburg

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈpiːtɚzˌbɜrɡ / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈpiːtəzˌbɜːɡ / (BrE)
A city in western Florida, on Tampa Bay, known as a popular winter resort.
city in western Florida, USA

Examples

Many retirees move to St. Petersburg for its warm climate and beautiful beaches.
The Salvador Dalí Museum in St. Petersburg attracts visitors from around the world.
We spent our vacation exploring the vibrant art scene in St. Petersburg, Florida.

Etymology

Named after Saint Petersburg in Russia, itself named after Saint Peter.

2. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈpiːtərzˌbɜrɡ / (AmE), / sənt ˈpiːtəzbɜːɡ / (BrE)
The second largest city in Russia, located in the northwest of the country at the head of the Gulf of Finland; formerly the capital.
second largest city in Russia

Examples

She traveled to St. Petersburg to see the famous Hermitage Museum.
The White Nights Festival is an annual event held in St. Petersburg.
St. Petersburg served as the capital of Russia for more than two centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Moscow - capital city of Russia

Etymology

Named after Saint Peter, originally founded as 'Saint Petersburg' by Tsar Peter the Great in 1703.
