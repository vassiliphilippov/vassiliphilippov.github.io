amebiasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.əˈbaɪ.əs.ɪs / (AmE), / ˌæm.ɪˈbaɪ.ə.sɪs / (BrE)
A disease caused by infection with amoebae, especially Entamoeba histolytica, typically resulting in symptoms such as diarrhea and abdominal pain.
infection with amoebae causing illness
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her illness as amebiasis after reviewing the lab results.
Untreated amebiasis can lead to severe complications in some patients.
Travelers are advised to take precautions to avoid amebiasis when visiting regions where the infection is common.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dysentery - inflammatory disorder of the intestine causing severe diarrhea

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek *amoibē* (change) and medical suffix *-iasis* (disease condition).
