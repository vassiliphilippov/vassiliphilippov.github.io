estuary

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛstʃuˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈɛstjʊəri / (BrE)
the wide lower part of a river where it meets the sea and fresh and salt water mix
wide river mouth where fresh and salt water mix

Examples

The Thames estuary is a vital habitat for many bird species.
Cities often develop around the estuary, benefiting from access to both inland and sea routes.
As the river widened, we sailed into the estuary and could smell the salty air.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: delta - a landform at the mouth of a river where it splits into several streams before entering a sea or lake

Etymology

From Latin aestuarium (tidal marsh), from aestus (tide, surge).
