Lithospermum canescens

1. NOUN

/ lɪθ.oʊˈspɜːrməm kəˈnes.əns / (AmE), / lɪθ.əˈspɜː.məm kəˈnes.əns / (BrE)
A perennial North American plant with greyish hairy foliage, yielding a red or yellow pigment.
North American plant with hairy leaves and pigment

Examples

Lithospermum canescens is commonly known as hoary puccoon in the Midwest prairies.
The roots of Lithospermum canescens were once used by Native Americans to produce dyes.
Gardeners appreciate Lithospermum canescens for its clusters of bright yellow-orange flowers.

Etymology

From Latin: 'lithospermum' meaning 'stone seed', and 'canescens' meaning 'becoming gray'.
