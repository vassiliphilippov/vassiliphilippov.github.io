biopsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.ɑp.si / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.ɒp.si / (BrE)
the medical examination of tissue or fluid taken from a living body to determine the presence or cause of a disease
medical tissue or fluid examination
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a biopsy to determine whether the lump was cancerous.
After the biopsy, the results showed no signs of infection.
She was nervous about undergoing the biopsy, but it was over quickly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: autopsy - examination of a dead body to determine cause of death

Etymology

From Greek 'bios' (life) + 'opsis' (sight, view), coined in late 19th century.
