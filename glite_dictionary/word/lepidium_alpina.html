Lepidium alpina

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈpɪdiəm ælˈpaɪnə / (AmE), / lɪˈpɪdiəm alˈpiːnə / (BrE)
a small perennial herb found in the mountains of central and southern Europe, with very small flowers on usually leafless stems, sometimes classified in the genus Lepidium
a mountain herb of central and southern Europe

Examples

The rugged slopes were dotted with Lepidium alpina thriving in the rocky soil.
A botanist collected specimens of Lepidium alpina during her hike in the Alps.
Lepidium alpina is sometimes difficult to distinguish from related alpine herbs.

Etymology

From Latin *Lepidium* (a plant genus) and *alpina* (of the Alps, mountainous).
