Tulipa clusiana

1. NOUN

/ tuˈliːpə kluˈsiːənə / (AmE), / tjuːˈliːpə kluˈsiːənə / (BrE)
A species of Eurasian tulip with small flowers that are blotched at the base, commonly known as the lady tulip.
a species of Eurasian tulip

Examples

The Tulipa clusiana blooms in early spring, displaying its unique striped petals.
Gardeners admire the delicate beauty of the Tulipa clusiana in rock gardens and borders.
Unlike many other tulips, the Tulipa clusiana has slender flowers with contrasting colors at the base.

Etymology

From the botanical genus Tulipa and named after Carolus Clusius, a Flemish botanist.
