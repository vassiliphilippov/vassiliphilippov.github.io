salopettes

1. NOUN

/ sæləˈpɛts / (AmE), / ˌsæl.əˈpɛts / (BrE)
trousers with shoulder straps, usually made of waterproof material, worn especially for skiing or other outdoor activities
ski trousers with straps

Examples

He pulled on his salopettes before heading out onto the snowy slopes.
Most skiers wear insulated salopettes to stay warm and dry.
She bought a new pair of bright red salopettes for her winter holiday.

Etymology

From French 'salopette', meaning dungarees or overalls.
