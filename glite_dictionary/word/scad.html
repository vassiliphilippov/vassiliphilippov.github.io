scad

1. NOUN

/ skæd / (AmE), / skæd / (BrE)
any of several marine fishes of the family Carangidae, often found in warm seas and known for their streamlined bodies.
a type of fish in the Carangidae family

Examples

The fishermen caught several scad along the coastline this morning.
Grilled scad is a popular dish in many seaside towns.
The scad swam in large schools near the surface of the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jack - a common name for fishes of the Carangidae family

Etymology

Probably from Dutch schaad, meaning a type of fish.
