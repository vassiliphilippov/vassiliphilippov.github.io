disparagingly

1. ADV

/ dɪˈspærədʒɪŋli / (AmE), / dɪˈspɑːrɪdʒɪŋli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows disrespect or belittles someone or something.
in a disrespectful or belittling manner

Examples

He spoke disparagingly of his rival's achievements during the interview.
The critics referred to the new art style disparagingly as a passing fad.
Sarah refused to comment disparagingly about her former colleagues.

Synset Examples

these mythological figures are described disparagingly as belonging ‘only to a story’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: respectfully - in a respectful manner

Etymology

From the adjective 'disparaging' + adverbial suffix '-ly'. 'Disparaging' comes from Old French 'desparagier' (to degrade).
