lampblack

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmpˌblæk / (AmE), / ˈlampˌblak / (BrE)
a fine black pigment made from soot, composed mainly of amorphous carbon, used in inks, paints, and dyes
black pigment made from soot

Examples

The artists mixed lampblack with oil to create a deep, rich black paint.
Early printers used lampblack as the primary pigment in their inks.
Traces of lampblack were found on the walls, evidence of years of oil lamp use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soot - black powdery deposit from burning organic matter

Etymology

Derived from 'lamp' + 'black', indicating soot collected from oil lamps.
