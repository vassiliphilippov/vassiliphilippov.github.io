retrospection

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtrəˈspɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəˈspɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of looking back on or reflecting about past events or experiences
reflection on past events

Examples

Her retrospection revealed how much she had grown since her childhood.
The memoir is filled with deep retrospection about the author's early career.
In moments of retrospection, he pondered the decisions that shaped his life.

Synset Examples

some psychologists tried to contrast retrospection and introspection

Related Terms

  • Antonym: introspection - examination of one's own thoughts and feelings
  • Synonym: reflection - serious thought or consideration

Etymology

From Latin 'retrospectus', meaning 'a looking back'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtroʊˈspɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəʊˈspɛkʃən / (BrE)
the action or process of looking back on or considering past events or situations
looking back on past events

Examples

The author's retrospection added depth to the memoir.
A period of retrospection often follows major life changes.
The film uses retrospection to reveal the main character's past.

Synset Examples

the story begins with no introductory retrospections

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflection - serious thought or consideration, often about past events
  • Antonym: anticipation - realization or consideration of something before it happens (opposite in time direction)

Etymology

From Latin 'retrospectio', from 'retro-' (back) + 'specere' (to look).
