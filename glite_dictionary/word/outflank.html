to outflank

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈflæŋk / (AmE), / aʊtˈflæŋk / (BrE)
to gain an advantage over someone, especially by using clever or indirect methods; to get the better of an opponent
gain an advantage over; get the better of

Examples

The team managed to outflank their competition by launching a new product first.
She tried to outflank her adversary in the negotiations with a surprising offer.
Lawyers often attempt to outflank their opponents with technical legal strategies.

Synset Examples

the goal was to best the competition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outmaneuver - to gain an advantage over by skillful or clever moves
  • Synonym: to outwit - to get the better of by being more clever
  • Antonym: to succumb - to fail to resist; to be defeated by

Etymology

Formed from 'out-' (surpassing) + 'flank', originally a military term for attacking the side of an opposing force.

2. VERB

/ aʊtˈflæŋk / (AmE), / aʊtˈflæŋk / (BrE)
to move around the side of an opposing force, especially to gain a tactical advantage in battle
go around the side of an opponent (military)

Examples

The general tried to outflank the enemy forces by sending troops to the east.
In the last battle, their cavalry managed to outflank us and attack from behind.
Modern armies often seek to outflank their opponents to gain a strategic advantage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outmaneuver - to gain an advantage over an opponent by skillful maneuvering
  • Synonym: to encircle - to surround completely, often for tactical advantage

Etymology

From 'out-' (meaning beyond) + 'flank' (side of an army), originally military terminology.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
