stalwartness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɔl.wɚt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈstɔːl.wət.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being strong, firm, and resolute in character or purpose.
strength and resoluteness

Examples

Her stalwartness during the crisis inspired confidence in everyone around her.
He handled the tough negotiations with impressive stalwartness and determination.
Despite the challenges, their stalwartness never wavered throughout the ordeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strength - the quality or state of being physically strong
  • Antonym: weakness - the state or condition of lacking strength
  • Synonym: resoluteness - firm determination; unwavering purpose

Etymology

From 'stalwart', of Middle English origin, from Old English 'stælwierþe' (serviceable, good), influenced by 'worth'.
