agleam

1. ADJ

/ əˈɡlim / (AmE), / əˈɡliːm / (BrE)
shining with a steady but soft or subdued brightness; glowing or gleaming.
shining with a soft brightness

Examples

The newly polished silverware lay agleam on the dining table.
Her eyes were agleam with excitement as she shared the news.
The pavement was agleam after the afternoon rain.

Synset Examples

from the plane we saw the city below agleam with lights
the gleaming brass on the altar
Nereids beneath the nitid moon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gleaming - shining brightly, especially with reflected light

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'a-' (on, in, at) and 'gleam' (a flash or beam of light).
