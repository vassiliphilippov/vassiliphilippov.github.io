cystic

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a cyst, especially the gallbladder or urinary bladder
relating to a cyst or bladder
medical

Examples

The surgeon examined the cystic structure to determine if removal was necessary.
The cystic duct connects the gallbladder to the common bile duct.
A cystic abnormality may require further medical evaluation.

Etymology

From Greek kystis (bladder, sac).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling a cyst, especially in reference to abnormal sacs containing fluid or semi-solid material in tissues.
relating to a cyst
medical

Examples

She was diagnosed with a cystic mass on her ovary that required surgical removal.
The doctor explained that cystic acne often results in deep, painful lesions under the skin.
A cystic structure was detected during the ultrasound examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyst - an abnormal, typically noncancerous, sac in the body containing fluid or semi-solid material

Etymology

From Greek kystis (bladder, sac).
