Neurotrichus gibbsii

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʊroʊˈtrɪkəs ˈɡɪbsi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəˈtrɪkəs ˈɡɪbsi.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of small, greyish-black shrew mole native to the United States and Canada.
species of North American shrew mole

Examples

The Neurotrichus gibbsii is the smallest member of the Talpidae family in North America.
Unlike regular moles, Neurotrichus gibbsii prefers moist forests and woodlands.
Researchers observed that Neurotrichus gibbsii has an exceptionally pointed snout useful for foraging.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'neuron' (nerve) and 'thrix' (hair); species named after George Gibbs, a naturalist.
