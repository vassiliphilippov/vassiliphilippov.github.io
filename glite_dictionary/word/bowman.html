bowman

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊ.mən / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.mən / (BrE)
a person, especially a man, who is skilled in using a bow and arrow
an archer or person skilled with a bow

Examples

The skilled bowman took aim and let his arrow fly towards the target.
During the medieval battle, each bowman played a crucial role in the army's defense.
Legends often speak of a lone bowman standing against overwhelming odds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archer - a person who shoots with a bow and arrow

Etymology

From Middle English 'boweman', from 'bow' (weapon) + 'man'.
