Fraxinus americana

1. NOUN

/ ˌfræksɪnəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (AmE), / ˌfræksɪnəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (BrE)
A species of deciduous tree native to eastern North America, commonly called white ash, with pale green or silvery leaves beneath and valued for its hard, brownish wood.
a species of North American ash tree

Examples

The wood of the Fraxinus americana is prized for making baseball bats due to its strength and flexibility.
In city parks, Fraxinus americana is often planted for its attractive foliage and shade.
The silvery undersides of the leaves can help you identify a Fraxinus americana in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white ash - Common name for Fraxinus americana, a North American ash tree

Etymology

From Latin: 'Fraxinus' meaning 'ash tree' and 'americana' referring to America.
