unpersuasive

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.pərˈsweɪ.sɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌn.pɜːˈsweɪ.sɪv / (BrE)
Not able to convince someone to believe or do something; lacking the ability to persuade.
not able to persuade

Examples

His explanation for being late was completely unpersuasive to the teacher.
The lawyer's arguments were unpersuasive and failed to sway the jury.
She found the advertisement unpersuasive and chose not to buy the product.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: persuasive - good at convincing someone to do or believe something
  • Synonym: convincing - able to persuade or make someone believe

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) + persuasive.
