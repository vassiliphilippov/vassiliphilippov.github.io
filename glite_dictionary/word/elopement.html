elopement

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈloʊpmənt / (AmE), / ɪˈləʊpmənt / (BrE)
the act of running away secretly with a lover, especially to get married without parental consent
secret marriage or romantic escape

Examples

Their sudden elopement surprised everyone in the small town.
Rumors of an elopement quickly spread through the family.
The couple decided on an elopement rather than a traditional wedding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to elope - to run away secretly in order to get married

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *allopare* (to seize, carry off), related to the verb 'elope'.
