dyeweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪˌwid / (AmE), / ˈdaɪˌwiːd / (BrE)
A small Eurasian shrub with clusters of yellow flowers that yield a dye, commonly found as a weed in Britain and the United States, and sometimes grown as an ornamental plant.
shrub with yellow flowers used for dye

Examples

The bright yellow blooms of dyeweed make it a lovely addition to the garden.
Historically, dyeweed was used to produce natural dyes for textiles.
In some regions, dyeweed grows so prolifically that it is often considered a weed.

Etymology

From 'dye' (a coloring substance) + 'weed' (wild plant).
