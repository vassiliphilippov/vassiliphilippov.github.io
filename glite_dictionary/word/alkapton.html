alkapton

1. NOUN

/ ælˈkæp.tɒn / (AmE), / ælˈkæp.tɒn / (BrE)
an acid that is an intermediate product in the metabolism of the amino acids tyrosine and phenylalanine
acid in amino acid metabolism
medical

Examples

The accumulation of alkapton in the body can lead to a rare metabolic disorder called alkaptonuria.
Researchers measured the level of alkapton in the patient's urine as part of the diagnostic process.
Defective metabolism of tyrosine and phenylalanine may result in increased alkapton production.

Etymology

From German 'Alkapton', from Arabic 'al-' (the) + Greek 'kapton' (to take in).
