MS

1. NOUN

/ ɛm ɛs / (AmE), / ɛm ɛs / (BrE)
a chronic, progressive disease of the nervous system characterized by loss of the myelin sheath surrounding certain nerve fibers, leading to varied neurological symptoms; abbreviation of multiple sclerosis.
abbreviation for multiple sclerosis, a nervous system disease
medical

Examples

After years of unexplained symptoms, she was finally diagnosed with MS.
Many patients with MS experience periods of remission and relapse.
The support group meets weekly to assist people living with MS.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multiple sclerosis - a chronic progressive disease involving the nervous system

Etymology

Abbreviation of multiple sclerosis.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛm ˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌɛm ˈɛs / (BrE)
A master's degree in science, typically awarded after completing postgraduate study in scientific or technical subjects.
master's degree in science

Examples

She earned her MS in biology from a prestigious university.
Many research positions require at least an MS in a relevant scientific field.
After completing his MS, he pursued a PhD in chemistry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: MSc - Master of Science, primarily used in British English
  • Antonym: MA - master's degree in arts or humanities

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Master of Science.'

3. PROPN

/ ɛm ɛs / (AmE), / ɛm ɛs / (BrE)
abbreviation for Mississippi, a state in the southeastern United States on the Gulf of Mexico; one of the Confederate States during the American Civil War
abbreviation for Mississippi (U.S. state)
american

Examples

She was born and raised in MS before moving to New York.
The weather in MS can be hot and humid in the summer.
Jackson is the capital city of MS and its largest urban area.

Etymology

Abbreviation of Mississippi, U.S. state name, itself from the French rendering of an Ojibwe word meaning 'great river'.
