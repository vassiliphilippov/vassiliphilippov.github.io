Salix purpurea

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.lɪks pɝːˈpjʊr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.lɪks pəːˈpjʊə.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
A species of willow native to Europe and western Asia, known for its reddish or purple twigs and tannin-rich bark; commonly called purple willow or purple osier.
a species of willow with purple twigs

Examples

The Salix purpurea is often cultivated for its flexible branches, used in basket weaving.
Researchers studied the tannin content in the bark of Salix purpurea for potential medicinal uses.
Gardeners appreciate Salix purpurea for its ornamental value and striking purple-hued stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osier - a willow whose pliable twigs are used for basketry
  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix

Etymology

From Latin: 'salix' meaning willow and 'purpurea' meaning purple, referring to the plant's characteristic twig color.
