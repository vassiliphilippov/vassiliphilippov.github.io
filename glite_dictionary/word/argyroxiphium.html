Argyroxiphium

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌɡɪroʊˈzɪfiəm / (AmE), / ɑːˌɡɪrəʊˈzɪfiəm / (BrE)
A genus of spreading and rosette-forming shrubs native to Hawaii, belonging to the sunflower family.
genus of Hawaiian rosette-forming shrubs

Examples

Several species within the genus Argyroxiphium are known as Hawaiian silverswords.
The Argyroxiphium plants have adapted to the harsh volcanic environments of Hawaii.
Botanists are studying Argyroxiphium to understand how plants can survive in extreme climates.

Etymology

From Greek argyros (silver) and xiphion (iris), referring to the silvery leaves and iris-like appearance.
