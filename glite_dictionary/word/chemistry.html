chemistry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪstri / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪstri / (BrE)
the complex emotional or psychological connection between two people, especially when they feel attracted to each other
emotional connection between people

Examples

There was instant chemistry between the two actors on stage, making their scenes very believable.
Some couples have great chemistry, while others just don’t click at all.
Despite their differences, Sarah and Tom felt a special chemistry from their very first meeting.

Synset Examples

their chemistry was wrong from the beginning — they hated each other
a mysterious alchemy brought them together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapport - a close and harmonious relationship where people understand each other's feelings or ideas

Etymology

Figurative use from the scientific term 'chemistry', meaning the study of substances and their reactions; here referring metaphorically to a 'reaction' between people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪstri / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪstri / (BrE)
the branch of science that deals with the composition, structure, properties, and changes of matter
science of matter and its changes

Examples

She decided to major in chemistry because she was fascinated by chemical reactions.
The chemistry lab was equipped with various instruments for experiments.
Understanding chemistry is essential for many scientific careers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physics - the science of matter, energy, and their interactions
  • Synonym: biology - the science of life and living organisms

Etymology

From French 'chimie', from Medieval Latin 'alchimia', meaning alchemy.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06094057-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɛmɪstri / (AmE), / ˈkɛmɪstri / (BrE)
the chemical composition and properties of a substance or object
chemical properties of a substance

Examples

Soil scientists often study the chemistry of different types of soil.
The chemistry of seawater affects the kinds of marine life that can survive.
Researchers analyzed the chemistry of the new alloy to determine its durability.

Synset Examples

the chemistry of soil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: composition - the way in which a substance or mixture is made up

Etymology

From French 'chimie', from Medieval Latin 'alchimia' (alchemy).
