unmarketable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmɑrkətəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɑːkɪtəbəl / (BrE)
not capable of being sold or not suitable for sale.
not able to be sold

Examples

The damaged goods were deemed unmarketable by the store manager.
Because of new regulations, some products became unmarketable overnight.
If the artwork is too niche, it may be considered unmarketable by most galleries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: marketable - able or fit to be sold

Etymology

From un- (not) + marketable, meaning not able to be sold.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmɑrkətəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɑːkɪtəbəl / (BrE)
not suitable or fit for sale; unable to be sold due to poor quality, legal restrictions, or lack of demand.
not fit to be sold

Examples

The damaged fruit was declared unmarketable by the grocery store manager.
Because the manuscript was poorly written, the publisher considered it unmarketable.
Many old homes are unmarketable unless they are extensively renovated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: marketable - fit to be sold; able to be sold

Etymology

Formed from prefix un- (not) + marketable (fit to be sold).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02069969-s
Synset POS: s
