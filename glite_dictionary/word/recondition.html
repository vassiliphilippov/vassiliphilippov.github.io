to recondition

1. VERB

/ ˌriːkənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
to repair and restore something to a better or usable state, especially machinery or equipment
repair and restore to working order

Examples

The mechanic was able to recondition the engine and make it run smoothly again.
Many companies recondition old electronics for resale at a lower price.
Before donating the bicycles, we will recondition them so they are safe to ride.

Synset Examples

He reconditioned the old appliances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refurbish - to renovate and redecorate something, especially a building
  • Synonym: to restore - to bring back to a former condition or position

Etymology

From re- (again) + condition (bring into a good state).
