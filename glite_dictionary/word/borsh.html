borsh

1. NOUN

/ bɔrʃ / (AmE), / bɔːʃ / (BrE)
A soup of Eastern European origin, typically made with beetroot as the main ingredient, often served hot or cold.
a beetroot-based Eastern European soup

Examples

She served a steaming bowl of borsh with a dollop of sour cream on top.
During our trip to Poland, we tasted homemade borsh at every local restaurant.
The earthy flavor of beetroot gives borsh its distinctive color and taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: borscht - alternative spelling for the beetroot-based Eastern European soup

Etymology

From Russian борщ (borshch) and Yiddish באָרשט (borsht), referring to the traditional soup.
