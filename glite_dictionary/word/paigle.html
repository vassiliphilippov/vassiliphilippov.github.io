paigle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪɡl̩ / (BrE)
A Eurasian primrose with yellow flowers clustered in a one-sided umbel; typically refers to certain species of cowslip.
a kind of Eurasian primrose
archaic

Examples

The field was dotted with bright yellow paigle blossoms swaying in the breeze.
In old English poetry, the paigle is praised for its cheerful color and early bloom.
She gathered a handful of paigles to decorate the spring festival table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowslip - a common European primrose with yellow flowers in a cluster

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪɡl̩ / (BrE)
an early spring flower found in the British Isles, with fragrant yellow or sometimes purple flowers; especially cowslip or primrose
early spring flower, esp. cowslip/primrose
british

Examples

In the meadow, clusters of paigles brightened the landscape with their yellow blooms.
Every spring, she would gather paigles to decorate the kitchen table.
The poet compared her beauty to that of a delicate paigle among the grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowslip - a yellow-flowered spring plant related to the primrose
  • Synonym: primrose - a common spring-flowering plant, often with pale yellow flowers

Etymology

Probably a regional (English dialect) alteration of 'pague', of uncertain origin; used since at least the 16th century.
