engrossment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡroʊsmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡrəʊsmənt / (BrE)
the state of being completely absorbed or occupied by something; intense mental involvement
complete absorption or intense focus

Examples

His engrossment in the book was so deep that he didn’t hear his phone ring.
She watched the movie with total engrossment, forgetting where she was.
Children often display engrossment when they are playing their favorite games.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorption - complete attention to something
  • Antonym: distraction - the process of diverting attention

Etymology

From Old French engrossier (to make large or thick), from Latin ingrossare.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈgroʊsmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈgrəʊsmənt / (BrE)
the state or quality of being fully absorbed or deeply concentrated in something
state of deep concentration

Examples

Her engrossment in the novel was so deep that she missed her train stop.
During the lecture, his engrossment was obvious as he took detailed notes.
The artist's engrossment in her work made her lose track of time.

Synset Examples

  • Antonym: distraction - lack of concentration or attention

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡroʊsmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡrəʊsmənt / (BrE)
the state of being deeply absorbed or engaged in something; intense preoccupation
deep absorption or preoccupation

Examples

His engrossment in the novel was so complete that he didn't hear the phone ring.
She listened with total engrossment as the speaker described his adventures.
During the film, their engrossment was evident from their focused expressions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorption - deep mental involvement in something
  • Antonym: distraction - the state of being drawn away from focus

Etymology

From the verb 'engross' (to absorb completely), with the suffix '-ment' indicating the state or result.
