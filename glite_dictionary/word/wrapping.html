wrapping

1. NOUN

/ ˈræpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræpɪŋ / (BrE)
a piece of material used to cover, bind, or protect a part of the body, especially as a bandage
material used to bind or cover, esp. as a bandage

Examples

The nurse applied a fresh wrapping to the patient's injured arm.
Keep the wrapping clean and dry to prevent infection.
He removed the old wrapping before cleaning the wound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandage - a strip of cloth used to bind a wound or protect an injured part of the body

Etymology

From the verb 'wrap', which has Germanic origins.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræpɪŋ / (BrE)
material, such as paper or cellophane, used to cover or enclose something, typically for protection or decoration.
material used to cover objects

Examples

The birthday present was hidden beneath colorful wrapping and shiny ribbons.
Please dispose of the plastic wrapping before using the product.
She admired the elaborate wrapping before opening the gift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: package - an object wrapped or boxed for transport or sale
  • Antonym: unwrapping - the removal of a covering or wrapping

Etymology

From the verb 'wrap' + -ing, denoting the material used for wrapping.
