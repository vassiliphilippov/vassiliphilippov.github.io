to atomize

1. VERB

/ ˈæ.tə.maɪz / (AmE), / ˈæ.tə.maɪz / (BrE)
to spray or reduce a liquid into a fine mist or very small particles
spray into fine mist

Examples

The machine is designed to atomize the disinfectant for even coverage.
Modern perfumes are often atomized rather than dabbed onto the skin.
The chef used a special nozzle to atomize the oil over the salad.

Synset Examples

atomize perfume

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray - to disperse liquid in small drops

Etymology

From atom + -ize, modeled after the process of breaking into atoms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01375803-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈæt.əˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæt.ə.maɪz / (BrE)
to break up or reduce something into very small particles or droplets, often by means of a spray or force
break up into tiny particles

Examples

The scientist used a device to atomize the liquid for the experiment.
Many perfumes are atomized into a fine mist when sprayed from the bottle.
After the explosion, the debris was almost completely atomized.

Synset Examples

the fine powder had been atomized by air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pulverize - to crush into a powder or dust
  • Antonym: to condense - to make something denser or more concentrated (opposite process)

Etymology

From atom + -ize, influenced by French atomiser.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00330954-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈætəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈætəmaɪz / (BrE)
to destroy or hit with powerful weapons, such as bombs, especially reducing something to small fragments or particles; to annihilate through force
to destroy with powerful weapons

Examples

The air force planned to atomize the enemy bunkers with a series of precision strikes.
They threatened to atomize the entire complex if peace talks failed.
The movie depicted a city being atomized by a catastrophic explosion.

Synset Examples

zap the enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annihilate - to destroy completely
  • Synonym: to obliterate - to wipe out; destroy utterly

Etymology

From atom + -ize, meaning to reduce to atoms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01135714-v
Synset POS: v
