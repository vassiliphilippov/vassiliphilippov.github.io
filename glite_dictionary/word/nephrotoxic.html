nephrotoxic

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɛfroʊˈtɑksɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɛfrəʊˈtɒksɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance) poisonous or damaging to the kidneys.
toxic or harmful to the kidneys
medical

Examples

Some antibiotics are known to be nephrotoxic and require careful monitoring.
Long-term use of certain painkillers can have nephrotoxic effects on the body.
The doctor avoided prescribing nephrotoxic drugs to patients with kidney disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hepatotoxic - toxic to the liver
  • Antonym: nephroprotective - protective of the kidneys

Etymology

From Greek 'nephros' (kidney) + 'toxic' (poisonous).
