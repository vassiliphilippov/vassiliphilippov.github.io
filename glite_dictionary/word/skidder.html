skidder

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪdər / (AmE), / ˈskɪdə / (BrE)
a heavy vehicle or tractor used for dragging logs from a forest to a loading site over rough terrain
tractor for hauling logs in forests

Examples

The skidder made its way through the muddy forest to pull out fallen logs.
Workers used a skidder to transport timber to the roadside loading area.
A modern skidder can maneuver easily over rocky ground and dense brush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tractor - a powerful vehicle for pulling farm machinery or loads

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɪdɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɪdə / (BrE)
a worker in logging who uses a skid to move logs from where they are felled to a collection point
worker who moves logs with a skid

Examples

The skidder guided the heavy logs down the slope to the loading area.
In the early morning, the skidder prepared his equipment for another long day in the forest.
Many skidders take pride in their ability to move logs efficiently and safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logger - a person whose work is logging

Etymology

From 'skid' (a sled-like device for dragging logs) + agent suffix '-er'.
