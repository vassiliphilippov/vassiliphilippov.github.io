Nyssa sylvatica

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɪsə sɪlˈvætɪkə / (AmE), / ˌnɪsə sɪlˈvætɪkə / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to eastern North America, known for its columnar shape, horizontal branches, small late-emerging leaves, and brilliant fall coloration.
North American deciduous tree with colorful fall leaves

Examples

The Nyssa sylvatica in our yard turns a brilliant red every autumn.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Nyssa sylvatica in its native forest habitats.
Many birds are attracted to the berries produced by Nyssa sylvatica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black gum - Common name for Nyssa sylvatica
  • Synonym: tupelo - Another common name for Nyssa sylvatica and related trees

Etymology

From Latin, 'Nyssa' the genus name after a water nymph in Greek mythology, and 'sylvatica' meaning 'of the woods'.
