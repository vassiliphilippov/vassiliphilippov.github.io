postiche

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈstiʃ / (AmE), / pɒˈstiːʃ / (BrE)
a covering or bundle of human or artificial hair worn for disguise or ornament, such as a wig or hairpiece
hairpiece, wig for disguise or adornment

Examples

He wore a postiche to conceal his thinning hair.
In the play, the actor donned a flamboyant postiche for a comedic effect.
The detective quickly noticed that the suspect's beard was actually a postiche.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wig - a head covering made from human or synthetic hair
  • Synonym: toupee - a small wig, usually worn to cover a bald spot

Etymology

From French postiche, meaning 'false' or 'artificial'.

2. NOUN

/ poʊˈstiʃ / (AmE), / pɒˈstiːʃ / (BrE)
an imitation or counterfeit; something that is not genuine or is made to deceive
imitation; counterfeit

Examples

The painting was revealed to be a postiche, fooling even experienced collectors.
His charm seemed real, but it turned out to be entirely postiche.
Among the heirlooms, they discovered a postiche hidden among the authentic treasures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fake - something that is not genuine; a forgery or counterfeit
  • Antonym: genuine - authentic; actually what it is said to be

Etymology

From French 'postiche', from Italian 'posticcio' (artificial, counterfeit).
