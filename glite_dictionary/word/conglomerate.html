conglomerate

1. NOUN

/ kənˈɡlɑː.mɚ.ət / (AmE), / kənˈɡlɒm.ər.ət / (BrE)
a large corporation formed by the merging of several different companies, often operating in various industries, under a single ownership or management
large corporation with diverse subsidiaries

Examples

The media conglomerate owns television stations, newspapers, and magazines across the country.
Over the past decade, the company has grown into a global conglomerate through mergers and acquisitions.
A conglomerate may operate in industries as varied as food production, energy, and entertainment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corporation - a large company or group of companies authorized to act as a single entity

Etymology

From Latin conglomeratus (heaped together), from conglomero (to roll together).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈɡlɑmɚət / (AmE), / kənˈɡlɒmərət / (BrE)
composed of different elements or parts gathered together into a unified whole; consisting of varied or diverse components.
composed of varied elements or parts

Examples

The city's population is a conglomerate mix of cultures and traditions.
The exhibition showcased a conglomerate collection of artworks from around the world.
Her latest novel features a conglomerate group of characters, each with distinct backgrounds.

Synset Examples

the conglomerate peoples of New England

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heterogeneous - consisting of dissimilar or diverse ingredients or constituents
  • Antonym: homogeneous - consisting of parts or elements that are all of the same kind

Etymology

From Latin 'conglomeratus', past participle of 'conglomerare' (to wind into a ball), from 'com-' (together) and 'glomerare' (to wind, gather into a ball).

3. NOUN

/ kənˈɡlɑːm.ɚ.ət / (AmE), / kənˈɡlɒm.ər.ət / (BrE)
a coarse-grained sedimentary rock composed of rounded fragments or clasts of varying size cemented together
a sedimentary rock of mixed-size fragments

Examples

The cliff was composed primarily of conglomerate interspersed with layers of sandstone.
Geologists identified the rock sample as a conglomerate due to its rounded pebbles and coarse texture.
A conglomerate forms when different-sized sediments are naturally cemented together over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breccia - a rock similar to conglomerate but with angular fragments

Etymology

From Latin conglomeratus (heaped together), from con- (together) + glomerare (to wind into a ball).
