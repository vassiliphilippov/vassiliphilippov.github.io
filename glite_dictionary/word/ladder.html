ladder

1. NOUN

/ ˈlædɚ / (AmE), / ˈlædə / (BrE)
A structure with steps or rungs between two long side pieces, used for climbing up or down.
structure with rungs for climbing

Examples

He climbed the ladder to fix the broken roof tile.
The painter set up his ladder against the wall before starting work.
Be careful not to stand on the top rung of the ladder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staircase - a set of stairs for going from one level to another
  • Antonym: elevator - a device used for raising and lowering people or things between floors

Etymology

From Old English hlǣdder, related to Dutch ladder and German Leiter.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlædɚ / (AmE), / ˈladə / (BrE)
a series of stages or levels through which someone or something can progress, especially in a career or organization
stages or levels of progression

Examples

She quickly moved up the ladder in her company through hard work and dedication.
Many people strive to climb the social ladder to improve their status.
Reaching the top of the academic ladder requires years of study and research.

Synset Examples

he climbed the career ladder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hierarchy - a system in which people or things are ranked one above another

Etymology

From Old English hlǣder, meaning 'steps' or 'stair'; originally referring to a structure of rungs, later extended metaphorically.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈlædər / (AmE), / ˈladə / (BrE)
(of stockings, tights, or similar fabric) to develop a long hole or run as the threads come undone, typically due to a snag.
develop a run in fabric by snagging

Examples

She gasped when she noticed her tights had laddered during the commute.
Be careful not to snag your stockings or they might ladder.
His socks laddered after catching on the rough edge of the bench.

Synset Examples

Her nylons were running

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run - (of fabric) to develop a long, thin hole as the threads come undone

Etymology

Metaphorical use from the resemblance of runs in stockings to the rungs of a ladder.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00335613-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈlædɚ / (AmE), / ˈladə / (BrE)
a run or row of unravelled stitches in a knitted fabric, especially in stockings or tights
run in fabric (esp. stockings)
british

Examples

She noticed a ladder in her tights just before leaving for work.
Carefully pulling on her stockings, she tried not to create another ladder.
A small hole in the sock soon became a ladder that ran all the way down.

Synset Examples

she got a run in her stocking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: run - an unravelled section in hosiery or knitted fabric
