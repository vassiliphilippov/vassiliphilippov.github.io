to derail

1. VERB

/ dɪˈreɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈreɪl / (BrE)
to cause (a train or vehicle on rails) to leave its tracks accidentally or deliberately
cause a train to leave its tracks

Examples

The investigators discovered that a small object on the tracks had derailed the passenger train.
Saboteurs attempted to derail the freight train to disrupt the supply chain.
Heavy rains damaged the tracks and ultimately derailed the cargo train.

Synset Examples

they had planned to derail the trains that carried atomic waste

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to divert - to turn aside from a course or direction

Etymology

From French 'dérailler', from 'de-' (off) + 'rail' (rail).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈreɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈreɪl / (BrE)
(of a train or rail vehicle) to leave the tracks accidentally or intentionally.
go off the tracks (train, etc.)

Examples

The freight train derailed after a heavy storm washed out part of the track.
Luckily, no one was injured when the subway car derailed near the city center.
Investigators are looking into what caused the newly built tram to derail.

Synset Examples

the train derailed because a cow was standing on the tracks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dislodge - to remove forcibly from a position or place
  • Synonym: wreck - to cause severe damage to a vehicle or structure
  • Antonym: to restore - to bring back to former position or condition

Etymology

From French 'dérailler', from 'de-' (off) + 'rail'.
