to enfeeble

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈfiːbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈfiːbəl / (BrE)
to make someone or something very weak
make or become very weak

Examples

The disease began to enfeeble her, making daily tasks difficult.
Years of neglect had enfeebled the old mansion, leaving it in a state of disrepair.
The oppressive heat seemed to enfeeble the athletes during the marathon.

Synset Examples

Life in the camp drained him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to weaken - to make or become weaker
  • Antonym: to strengthen - to make or become stronger

Etymology

From Old French 'enfeeblir', from 'en-' (make) + 'feble' (weak), from Latin 'flebilis' (lamentable, feeble).
