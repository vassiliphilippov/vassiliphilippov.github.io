Luxemburg

1. PROPN

/ ˈlʌksəmˌbɜrɡ / (AmE), / ˈlʌksəmbɜːɡ / (BrE)
the capital and largest city of Luxembourg, located in western Europe
capital city of Luxembourg

Examples

Many international banks have their European headquarters in Luxemburg.
Tourists are drawn to the old quarters and fortifications of Luxemburg.
The cultural scene in Luxemburg is lively, with numerous museums and galleries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Luxembourg - the capital city of Luxembourg, alternative and official English spelling

Etymology

Named after the country of Luxembourg; from Old High German *Lucilinburhuc* (little castle).

2. PROPN

/ ˈlʌksəmˌbɜrɡ / (AmE), / ˈlʌksəmˌbɜːɡ / (BrE)
A country in northwestern Europe, officially the Grand Duchy of Luxemburg, bordered by Belgium, France, and Germany; known for its status as an international financial center.
country in northwestern Europe

Examples

The small nation of Luxemburg is famous for its medieval castles and scenic countryside.
After completing her studies, she was offered a banking job in Luxemburg.
Many international corporations have their European headquarters in Luxemburg.

Etymology

From French 'Luxembourg', from Old High German 'Luci(n)linpurc' (little fortress).

Synset POS: n
