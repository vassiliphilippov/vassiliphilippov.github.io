syllabus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪləbəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪləbəs / (BrE)
a summary or outline of the subjects included in a course of study
outline of a course's topics

Examples

The professor distributed the syllabus on the first day of class.
Please review the syllabus to see the list of required readings and assignments.
Changes to the syllabus will be announced during lectures.

Synset Examples

he was admitted to a new program at the university

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curriculum - the subjects comprising a course of study in a school or college

Etymology

From Latin syllabus, probably via a misreading of Greek sittybos (table of contents, label of a papyrus roll).
