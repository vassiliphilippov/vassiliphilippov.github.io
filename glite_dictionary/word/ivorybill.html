ivorybill

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪvəriˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈaɪvəriˌbɪl / (BrE)
a large black-and-white woodpecker of the southern United States and Cuba, known for its ivory-colored bill; now nearly extinct
large, nearly extinct woodpecker

Examples

The ivorybill was once believed to be extinct, but there have been recent unconfirmed sightings.
Birdwatchers travelled to the swamps in hopes of glimpsing an ivorybill.
The striking pattern and large size make the ivorybill distinct from other woodpeckers.

Etymology

Named for its distinctive ivory-colored bill.
