chiding

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of rebuking or scolding someone, often harshly or with disapproval
act of rebuking or scolding

Examples

After her reckless driving, she received a stern chiding from her father.
The teacher’s chiding was enough to make the students reconsider their actions.
Despite the public chiding, he remained unapologetic about his mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuke - an expression of sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Synonym: reprimand - a formal expression of disapproval

Etymology

From verb 'chide', from Old English *cīdan* (to contend, chide).
