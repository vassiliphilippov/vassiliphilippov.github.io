whetstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛtˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈwɛt.stəʊn / (BrE)
A flat stone used for sharpening the edges of cutting tools or knives.
stone for sharpening blades

Examples

He used a whetstone to sharpen the edge of his knife before heading out to work.
The old carpenter kept a whetstone in his toolbox at all times.
After a few strokes on the whetstone, the chisel was as sharp as new.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharpener - a tool or device for making blades sharper

Etymology

From Old English *hwetstān*, from *hwettan* (to whet, sharpen) + *stān* (stone).
