to encase

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkeɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkeɪs / (BrE)
to completely cover or surround something, especially so that nothing can get in or out
to completely cover or surround

Examples

The ancient mummy was carefully encased in protective wrappings.
Snow had encased the trees in a sparkling white shell.
To preserve its freshness, the cheese is encased in wax.

Synset Examples

my feet were encased in mud

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enclose - to surround or close off on all sides

Etymology

From Old French *encasé* (in a case), from en- 'in' + case 'box, container'.
