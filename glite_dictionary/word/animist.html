animist

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəmɪst / (AmE), / ˈænɪmɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that natural objects, natural phenomena, and the universe itself possess souls or consciousness; one who follows the doctrine of animism
person who believes in animism

Examples

As an animist, he believes that rivers and trees possess a spiritual essence.
Many indigenous communities include animist practices in their religious traditions.
The anthropologist interviewed several animists during her fieldwork in West Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animism - the belief that objects and natural phenomena have souls

Etymology

From Latin anima (soul, life) + -ist (adherent of a doctrine).

2. ADJ

/ ˈænɪmɪst / (AmE), / ˈænɪmɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of animism or its believers.
relating to animism

Examples

The traditional rituals of the tribe are deeply animist in nature.
Some anthropologists study animist beliefs among indigenous peoples.
The animist worldview sees spirits present in all aspects of the natural world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animistic - pertaining to animism; alternative adjective form
  • Antonym: materialist - relating to the doctrine that nothing exists except matter and its movements

Etymology

From animate + -ist, referring to belief in spiritual animacy.
