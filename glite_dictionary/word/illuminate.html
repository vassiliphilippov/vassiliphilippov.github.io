to illuminate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈluːməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈljuːmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to make something clear or easier to understand; to clarify or explain
clarify or explain

Examples

The professor's explanation helped to illuminate the complex theory for the students.
Her research managed to illuminate an important aspect of the debate that had been previously overlooked.
The article illuminates the reasons behind the sudden change in policy.

Synset Examples

Could you clarify these remarks?
Clear up the question of who is at fault

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clarify - to make clear or easier to understand
  • Antonym: obscure - to make unclear or difficult to understand

Etymology

From Latin *illuminare* (to light up, make clear).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈluːməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈljuːmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to light up a place or make something visible by shining light on it
to shine light on; light up

Examples

Powerful floodlights were used to illuminate the football field at night.
The rising sun began to illuminate the city skyline through the morning mist.
A single bulb illuminated the dark hallway, casting long shadows on the wall.

Synset Examples

This lamp lightens the room a bit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light - to make something bright or provide with light

Etymology

From Latin illuminare, from in- (upon) + lumen (light).

3. VERB

/ ɪˈluːməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈljuːmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to decorate (a manuscript) with gold, silver, or colorful designs and pictures, especially in medieval texts
decorate a manuscript with gold, designs, etc.

Examples

The monks would often illuminate the texts with intricate gold and vibrant colors.
She learned how to illuminate manuscripts in the style of the Middle Ages.
Many surviving medieval books were carefully illuminated by skilled artists.

Etymology

From Latin illuminare (to light up, make bright).

4. VERB

/ ɪˈluːməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈljuːmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to light up or make bright; to provide light to something so that it is visible
to light up an area

Examples

The rising sun gradually began to illuminate the empty streets.
A spotlight was used to illuminate the stage during the performance.
The lanterns illuminated the garden with a soft, golden glow.

Synset Examples

The lamppost illuminated the street during our late-night stroll.
The stars will illumine our path tonight.
Her smile seemed to illumine the entire room.
The dawn will soon illumine the sky with colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to light up - to make bright or light by supplying with light

Etymology

From Latin 'illuminare' (to light up), from 'in-' (upon) + 'luminare' (to light).
