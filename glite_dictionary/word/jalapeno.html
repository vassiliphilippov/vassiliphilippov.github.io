jalapeno

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑː.ləˈpeɪ.njoʊ / (AmE), / ˌhæ.ləˈpeɪ.njəʊ / (BrE)
A medium-sized, mild to hot chili pepper with a smooth, dark green or red skin, commonly used in Mexican and Southwestern United States cuisine.
type of chili pepper

Examples

He added a chopped jalapeno to the salsa for extra heat.
I prefer my nachos topped with sliced jalapenos and melted cheese.
The jalapeno gives the chili its signature spicy kick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili pepper - a small, hot-tasting pod used as a spice

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish jalapeño, meaning 'from Jalapa' (a city in Mexico).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɑːləˈpeɪnjoʊ / (AmE), / ˌhæl.əˈpeɪ.njəʊ / (BrE)
A small, spicy chili pepper, usually green or red, from a plant native to Mexico; also, the plant itself.
a small, spicy chili pepper

Examples

She added chopped jalapeno to the salsa for extra heat.
The jalapeno plant thrives in warm climates with plenty of sunlight.
If you can't handle spicy food, avoid dishes with jalapeno.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili - any of several kinds of spicy peppers
  • Degree Lower: bell pepper - a large, mild form of pepper

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish, after Xalapa (Jalapa), a city in Mexico.
