to categorise

1. VERB

/ ˈkætəɡəˌraɪz / (AmE), / kəˈtɛɡərʌɪz / (BrE)
to place or assign something into a particular category or group based on shared characteristics
assign to a category or group
british

Examples

It is important to categorise the books by genre for easy reference.
Children often categorise objects by their colours or shapes.
We need to categorise the expenses to track our budget accurately.

Synset Examples

Children learn early on to categorize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to categorize - assign to a category or group (American spelling)
  • Synonym: to classify - arrange in classes or categories

Etymology

From Greek 'katēgoria' (accusation, category) via Medieval Latin and Old French 'catégoriser'.
