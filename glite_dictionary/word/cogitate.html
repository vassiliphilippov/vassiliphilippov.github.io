to cogitate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːdʒɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒdʒɪteɪt / (BrE)
to think deeply about something; to consider or reflect carefully
think deeply; reflect

Examples

She needed a quiet place to cogitate before making such an important decision.
During the meeting, he sat in silence, cogitating on the alternatives.
After hearing the proposal, they cogitated for hours before reaching an agreement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ponder - to think about something carefully, especially before making a decision
  • Synonym: think - to use one's mind to consider or reason about something
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember or give consideration to something

Etymology

From Latin 'cogitare' (to think, consider).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːdʒɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒdʒɪteɪt / (BrE)
to think deeply or carefully about something; to consider with thorough attention or reflection
think deeply; ponder

Examples

She needed time to cogitate before making her final decision.
He sat quietly by the window, cogitating on the meaning of life.
Before answering, the professor cogitated for a moment to consider all perspectives.

Synset Examples

I've been thinking all day and getting nowhere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ponder - think about something carefully
  • Synonym: to contemplate - think deeply about something
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something

Etymology

From Latin 'cogitatus', past participle of 'cogitare' (to think, reflect).
