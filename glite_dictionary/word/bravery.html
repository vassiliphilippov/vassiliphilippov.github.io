bravery

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈbreɪvəri / (BrE)
the quality of being courageous and willing to face danger, difficulty, or pain without fear
courage; lack of fear in facing danger

Examples

She showed remarkable bravery by speaking out against injustice.
Acts of bravery are often honored by medals and awards.
Despite his fear, he found the bravery to climb the mountain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courage - the ability to do something that frightens one
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of bravery; fearfulness

Etymology

From French *braverie* (bravado), from *brave* (brave, splendid).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈbreɪvəri / (BrE)
the quality of facing danger, difficulty, or pain without fear; courage
courage in facing danger or pain

Examples

The firefighter's bravery during the rescue operation was widely praised.
It takes bravery to stand up for what you believe in, even when others disagree.
She showed remarkable bravery in overcoming her illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courage - the ability to do something that frightens one; bravery
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of bravery; excessive fear in the face of danger or pain

Etymology

From French *braverie*, from *brave* (courageous).
