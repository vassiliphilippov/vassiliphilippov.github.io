Bundesbank

1. PROPN

/ ˈbʊndəsˌbæŋk / (AmE), / ˈbʊndəsˌbaŋk / (BrE)
the central bank of Germany, responsible for managing the country's currency and monetary policy
Germany's central bank

Examples

The Bundesbank announced new measures to stabilize the euro in the wake of financial uncertainty.
After reunification, the Bundesbank played a key role in integrating East German currency.
Many economists closely monitor statements from the Bundesbank for insights into European monetary policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Deutsche Bundesbank - the full official name of the Bundesbank

Etymology

From German 'Bundesbank', meaning 'Federal Bank' ('Bundes-' federal + 'Bank' bank).
