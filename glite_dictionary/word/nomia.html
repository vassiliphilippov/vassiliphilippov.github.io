Nomia

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊ.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.mi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of bees, some of which are important pollinators of leguminous plants.
genus of bees, pollinators of legumes

Examples

The Nomia bees are recognized for their efficiency in pollinating alfalfa crops.
Scientists have discovered several new species within the genus Nomia.
Farmers value Nomia as essential pollinators for various legumes.

Etymology

From Greek *nomia*, possibly related to pasture or grazing, as used in zoological naming.
