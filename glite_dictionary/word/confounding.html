confounding

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈfaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
causing confusion or making something difficult to understand; contradictory or perplexing.
causing confusion or perplexity

Examples

The detective encountered a confounding series of clues that only deepened the mystery.
The results showed a confounding effect that made interpretation difficult.
His confounding behavior left everyone in the room bewildered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perplexing - causing confusion or puzzlement
  • Antonym: clarifying - making something clear or less confusing

Etymology

Present participle of 'confound', from Latin 'confundere' (to mix together, confuse).
