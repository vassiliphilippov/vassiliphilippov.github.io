money

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ˈmʌni / (BrE)
wealth considered in terms of currency, assets, or funds that can be used to buy goods or services.
wealth in the form of currency or assets

Examples

She inherited a large sum of money from her grandmother.
He invested his money in stocks and bonds rather than in real estate.
They raised money for charity through donations and fundraising events.

Synset Examples

all his money is in real estate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wealth - an abundance of valuable possessions or money
  • Antonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor

Etymology

From Latin 'moneta', the surname of the goddess Juno, in whose temple money was coined; later meaning coinage, money.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ˈmʌni / (BrE)
The official currency issued by a government or national bank, used as a medium of exchange.
official currency of a country

Examples

Tourists must exchange their money before traveling to a different country.
The price of the ticket must be paid in local money.
After the currency reform, old money was no longer accepted.

Synset Examples

he changed his money into francs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: currency - system of money in general use

Etymology

From Old French *moneie*, from Latin *moneta* (money, mint).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13406050-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmʌni / (AmE), / ˈmʌni / (BrE)
The official medium of exchange in the form of coins and banknotes; currency used to pay for goods and services or to settle debts.
currency used as official exchange

Examples

She saved her money to buy a new computer.
Travelers often exchange money at the airport when going abroad.
The restaurant refused to accept cash because all money must be paid electronically.

Synset Examples

we tried to collect the money he owed us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: currency - system of money in general use in a particular country

Etymology

From Old French 'moneie', from Latin 'moneta' (mint, coin).

Source: WordNet
