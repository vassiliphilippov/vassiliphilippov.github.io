eruditeness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛr.ʊˈdaɪt.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɛr.juːˈdaɪt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of having or showing great scholarly knowledge or learning
profound scholarly knowledge

Examples

She impressed the audience with her eruditeness on historical matters.
His eruditeness was evident from the depth of his lectures.
Few authors write with the eruditeness that she brings to her books.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: learning - knowledge acquired through study
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From Latin eruditus (learned), past participle of erudire (to instruct), from e- (out) + rudis (unskilled, rough).
