intrastate

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntrəˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈsteɪt / (BrE)
Existing or occurring within the boundaries of a single state, especially in relation to commerce, laws, or activities.
within a single state

Examples

The new regulations apply only to intrastate businesses and not to those operating across state lines.
There has been significant growth in intrastate transportation of goods in recent years.
Each state government manages its own intrastate affairs independently of the federal government.

Synset Examples

intrastate as well as interstate commerce

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interstate - existing between or involving different states

Etymology

Formed from Latin 'intra' (within) + 'state'.
