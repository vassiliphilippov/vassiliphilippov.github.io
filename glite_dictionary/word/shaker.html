Shaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkə / (BrE)
a member of a celibate Christian sect, founded in 18th-century England, known for communal living, simplicity, and innovative furniture design, especially active in the United States
member of a celibate Christian sect

Examples

Many Shakers were renowned for their craftsmanship and simple way of life.
The Shakers believed in communal ownership and strict celibacy.
Museums today preserve many of the objects made by the Shakers in their communities.

Etymology

Named from their practice of ecstatic dancing ('shaking') during worship services.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkə / (BrE)
a member of a Christian sect known for practicing celibacy, communal living, collective ownership of property, and separation from worldly affairs
member of a Christian sect

Examples

The Shaker community was renowned for its craftsmanship and simple lifestyle.
She studied the beliefs of the Shakers for her thesis on American religious movements.
Many historic Shaker villages are now open as museums to the public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quaker - member of the Religious Society of Friends

Etymology

Originally from 'Shaking Quaker', referring to their ecstatic worship practices.
