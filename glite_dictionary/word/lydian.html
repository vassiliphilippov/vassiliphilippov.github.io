Lydian

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪdiən / (AmE), / ˈlɪdiən / (BrE)
the extinct Anatolian language once spoken in the region of Lydia, in western Asia Minor.
extinct Anatolian language of Lydia

Examples

Scholars have deciphered many inscriptions written in Lydian found on ancient monuments.
The Lydian language became extinct after the 1st century BCE.
He specialized in the study of Lydian and other Anatolian tongues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hittite - another Anatolian language of ancient Asia Minor
  • Synonym: Luwian - Anatolian language related to Lydian and Hittite

Etymology

From Latin Lydus, from Greek Lū́dos (Λυδία), the ancient name of Lydia.
