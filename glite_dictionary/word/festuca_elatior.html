Festuca elatior

1. NOUN

/ fɛsˈtuːkə ɛˈleɪtiˌɔr / (AmE), / fɛsˈtjuːkə ɪˈleɪtɪɔː / (BrE)
a species of grass with wide flat leaves, cultivated in Europe and America for permanent pasture, hay, and lawns
a species of grass used for pasture and lawns

Examples

Farmers often sow Festuca elatior in their fields to produce high-quality hay.
The wide, flat leaves of Festuca elatior help create a lush appearance for lawns.
Studies show that Festuca elatior thrives in both European and American climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meadow fescue - a common name for Festuca elatior, a species of grass used for fodder

Etymology

From Latin festuca (stalk of grass) + elatior (taller, higher).
