rancor

1. NOUN

/ ˈræŋkər / (AmE), / ˈræŋkə / (BrE)
A feeling of deep-seated bitterness and long-standing ill will or anger towards someone or something.
deep, bitter anger or resentment

Examples

Even after all those years, she still harbored rancor toward her former business partner.
Political debates often generate rancor between opposing parties.
His apology did little to ease the rancor that had built up after the incident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resentment - A feeling of persistent ill will or anger
  • Antonym: forgiveness - The act of letting go of anger or resentment

Etymology

From Latin 'rancor' (a stinking smell, grudge), from 'rancere' (to stink).
