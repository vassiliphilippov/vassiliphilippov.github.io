irruption

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrʌpʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌpʃən / (BrE)
a sudden and violent entry or invasion into a place or situation
sudden violent entry or invasion

Examples

The protest led to the irruption of people into the parliament building.
There was a sudden irruption of laughter after his unexpected joke.
The city suffered an irruption of illegal activities during the festival.

Synset Examples

the recent irruption of bad manners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eruption - a sudden outbreak or release, especially of something forceful like lava, emotion, etc.

Etymology

From Latin 'irruptio', from 'irrumpere' meaning 'to break in'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈrʌpʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌpʃən / (BrE)
a sudden and violent outbreak, especially of something undesirable
sudden violent outbreak

Examples

The irruption of violence caught the authorities by surprise.
The town was unprepared for the irruption of disease that spread overnight.
An irruption of protesters flooded the square, demanding change.

Synset Examples

the outbreak of hostilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outbreak - sudden occurrence of something unwelcome
  • Synonym: eruption - sudden outburst, especially of noise or violence

Etymology

From Latin 'irruptio', from 'irrumpere' (to burst in).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈrʌpʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌpʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a sudden sharp increase in the number of individuals in a population, especially of animals or birds in a particular area
sudden sharp population increase

Examples

The unexpected irruption of locusts devastated the region's crops in just a few days.
Every winter brings the possibility of an irruption of snowy owls to the northern United States.
Scientists are studying the causes behind the recent irruption in the deer population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eruption - a sudden powerful outburst, especially of volcanoes or feelings

Etymology

From Latin 'irruptio' (a bursting in), from 'irrumpere' (to burst in).
