to Christianize

1. VERB

/ ˈkrɪstʃəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstʃənaɪz / (BrE)
to make Christian in character or to convert to Christianity; to adapt something so it aligns with Christian principles or beliefs
make or adapt to Christianity

Examples

There were efforts to Christianize the indigenous population during the colonial period.
Some rulers sought to Christianize pagan festivals and rituals in their domains.
Missionaries aimed to Christianize remote villages by building churches and schools.

Synset Examples

some people want to Christianize ancient pagan sites

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convert - to change the religious beliefs of someone, especially to Christianity
  • Antonym: to secularize - to make something non-religious, or remove religious influence

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Christianizare, from Christianus (Christian).

2. VERB

/ ˈkrɪstʃəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstʃənaɪz / (BrE)
to convert a person, group, or place to Christianity; to make Christian in character or influence
convert to Christianity

Examples

During the 16th century, missionaries sought to Christianize the indigenous peoples of South America.
Many schools in the region were established to Christianize local children through religious education.
The old festival was eventually Christianized and became known as All Saints' Day.

Synset Examples

missionaries have tried to Christianize native people all over the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convert - to change someone's religious faith or beliefs
  • Antonym: to secularize - to remove religious influence or character

Etymology

From Late Latin christianizare (to make Christian) from christianus (Christian).
