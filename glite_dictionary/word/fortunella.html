Fortunella

1. NOUN

/ fɔr.tʃuˈnɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˌfɔːtʃʊˈnɛl.ə / (BrE)
a small genus of shrubs native to southern China, producing small, ovoid citrus fruits similar to oranges; includes the kumquat
genus of shrubs; includes kumquats

Examples

The genus Fortunella is best known for its production of the kumquat fruit.
Many citrus growers cultivate Fortunella species for their ornamental value and unique flavor.
Botanists once classified kumquats under Citrus, but now place them in the genus Fortunella.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kumquat - a small edible orange-like fruit; a plant of the genus Fortunella

Etymology

Named after Robert Fortune, Scottish botanist, with Latinized ending.
