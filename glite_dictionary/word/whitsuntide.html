Whitsuntide

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪt.sən.taɪd / (AmE), / ˈwɪt.sən.taɪd / (BrE)
The week beginning on Whitsunday (Pentecost), especially the first three days, observed as a Christian holiday period.
the week starting with Whitsunday (Pentecost)

Examples

Many villages in England hold traditional fairs and celebrations during Whitsuntide.
Schoolchildren often had a week-long holiday at Whitsuntide in the past.
Churches decorate their interiors with flowers to mark the arrival of Whitsuntide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Whitsun - another name for Pentecost; the seventh Sunday after Easter
  • Synonym: Pentecost - Christian festival marking the descent of the Holy Spirit, seventh Sunday after Easter

Etymology

Derived from 'Whitsun' (Whitsunday) and 'tide' (season, period).
