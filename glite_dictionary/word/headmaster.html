headmaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
The person in charge of a school, especially a private school or in British usage.
person in charge of a school
british

Examples

The headmaster greeted all the students at the entrance every morning.
After years of teaching, she was promoted to headmaster of the school.
Parents requested a meeting with the headmaster to discuss the new policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: principal - person in charge of a school (chiefly American English)

Etymology

From 'head' (leader) + 'master' (person in authority).
