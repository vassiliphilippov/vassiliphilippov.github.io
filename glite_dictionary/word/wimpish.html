wimpish

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪm.pɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɪm.pɪʃ / (BrE)
lacking strength or determination; weak and ineffectual.
weak or ineffectual

Examples

He was too wimpish to stand up to his critics.
Her wimpish response disappointed everyone who expected her to be assertive.
The hero’s brother was portrayed as a wimpish sidekick, scared of his own shadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence
  • Synonym: cowardly - lacking courage
  • Antonym: bold - showing courage; fearless

Etymology

From 'wimp' (a weak or ineffectual person) + '-ish'.
