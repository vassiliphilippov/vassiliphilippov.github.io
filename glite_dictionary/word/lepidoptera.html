Lepidoptera

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪˈdɑptərə / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪˈdɒptərə / (BrE)
A large order of insects that includes moths and butterflies, characterized by wings covered in tiny scales.
order of insects: moths and butterflies

Examples

The Lepidoptera are studied by entomologists interested in the diversity of moths and butterflies.
Some species within the Lepidoptera are vital pollinators in various ecosystems.
Many children love observing Lepidoptera in butterfly gardens during the summer.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'lepido-' (scale) + 'pteron' (wing).
