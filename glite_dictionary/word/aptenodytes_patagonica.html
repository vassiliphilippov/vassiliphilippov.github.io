Aptenodytes patagonica

1. NOUN

/ æpˌtɛnəˈdaɪtiːz ˌpætəˈɡoʊnɪkə / (AmE), / æpˌtɛnəˈdaɪtiːz ˌpætəˈɡəʊnɪkə / (BrE)
a large species of penguin found on islands bordering the Antarctic Circle, commonly known as the King penguin
species of large Antarctic penguin

Examples

The Aptenodytes patagonica is the second largest species of penguin in the world.
Researchers observed a colony of Aptenodytes patagonica during their expedition to South Georgia Island.
The striking coloration of Aptenodytes patagonica makes it easily distinguishable from other penguins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king penguin - common English name for Aptenodytes patagonica

Etymology

From Greek 'aptenodytes' (diver without wings) and Latin 'patagonica', referring to Patagonia.
