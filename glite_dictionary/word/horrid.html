horrid

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɔːrɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɒrɪd / (BrE)
Extremely unpleasant, offensive, or causing horror.
offensive or causing horror

Examples

The children found the sight so horrid they couldn't look away.
He apologized for making a horrid remark at dinner last night.
The conditions in the abandoned building were truly horrid.

Synset Examples

subjected to outrageous cruelty
a hideous pattern of injustice
horrific conditions in the mining industry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horrible - causing horror or disgust
  • Synonym: hideous - extremely ugly or unpleasant

Etymology

From Latin horridus (bristling, dreadful), from horrēre (to bristle, shudder).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɔr.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɒr.ɪd / (BrE)
Extremely unpleasant, bad, or causing horror; dreadful or offensive.
extremely unpleasant or bad

Examples

The food at that restaurant was absolutely horrid and I could not finish my meal.
She had a horrid cold and felt miserable all week.
His behavior at the party last night was simply horrid and upset everyone.

Synset Examples

when she was bad she was horrid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horrible - Causing horror or shock; extremely unpleasant
  • Synonym: disgusting - Repulsive or offensive; extremely unpleasant
  • Antonym: wonderful - Extremely good; inspiring delight or admiration

Etymology

From Latin 'horridus' (bristling, rough, dreadful), from 'horrēre' (to bristle, shudder).
