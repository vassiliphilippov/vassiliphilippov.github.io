F. D. Roosevelt

1. PROPN

/ ˌɛf ˌdi ˈroʊzəˌvɛlt / (AmE), / ˌɛf ˌdi ˈrəʊzəvɛlt / (BrE)
(1882–1945) the 32nd President of the United States, who led the country during the Great Depression and World War II, and introduced the New Deal.
32nd U.S. President (1933–1945)

Examples

F. D. Roosevelt was the only American president elected to four terms in office.
Many historians credit F. D. Roosevelt with helping the United States recover from the Great Depression.
During World War II, F. D. Roosevelt played a crucial role as a leader of the Allied powers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Franklin D. Roosevelt - Full name of F. D. Roosevelt

Etymology

From the initials of Franklin Delano Roosevelt, his full name.
