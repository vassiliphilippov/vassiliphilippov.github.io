success

1. NOUN

/ səkˈsɛs / (AmE), / səkˈsɛs / (BrE)
a person who achieves a great deal or attains desired aims, especially in their career or life
a person who achieves significant success

Examples

After years of hard work, she is finally considered a success by her peers.
His older brother was always the family success, but he never felt overshadowed.
Dressing like a success can boost your confidence in the workplace.

Synset Examples

his son would never be the achiever that his father was
only winners need apply
if you want to be a success, you have to dress like a success

Related Terms

  • Antonym: failure - a person who does not succeed or falls short of desired aims

Etymology

From Latin successus (an advance, a coming up), from succedere (come after, succeed).

2. NOUN

/ səkˈsɛs / (AmE), / səkˈsɛs / (BrE)
The state of achieving wealth, fame, or prosperity; the fact of having achieved something desired or intended.
state of achieving prosperity or fame

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally achieved great success in her career.
His business ventures brought him both success and recognition in the industry.
Not everyone measures success by wealth or fame; some value happiness instead.

Synset Examples

he is enjoying great success
he does not consider wealth synonymous with success

Related Terms

  • Antonym: failure - lack of success; the opposite state of not achieving one's goals

Etymology

From Latin successus (an advance, a coming up; a good result).
