chimeric

1. ADJ

/ kaɪˈmɪrɪk / (AmE), / kʌɪˈmɪərɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling a chimera; fanciful, imaginary, or wildly improbable. Also, composed of parts of different origin or nature, especially in biology or genetics.
like a chimera; fanciful or composite

Examples

The scientist engineered a chimeric protein using genes from two different species.
Their plan for world peace was dismissed as a chimeric dream by the critics.
In myths, monsters often have chimeric features, combining parts from various animals.

Synset Examples

his Utopia is not as chimeric commonwealth but a practical improvement on what already exists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fantastical - imaginative or fanciful, especially in a bizarre way
  • Antonym: realistic - representing things in a way that is accurate and true to life

Etymology

From chimera, after Latin chimaera and Greek khimaira (mythical fire-breathing monster); suffix -ic forms the adjective.
