Egyptian

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈdʒɪpʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪpʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
of or relating to Egypt, its people, or their language
of Egypt or its people

Examples

She is proud of her Egyptian heritage and traditions.
The museum displayed ancient Egyptian artifacts in a special exhibit.
They enjoyed learning about Egyptian culture during their trip to Cairo.

Etymology

From Latin Aegyptius, from Greek Aigyptios, from Aigyptos (Egypt).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɪpʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪpʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Egypt.
native or resident of Egypt

Examples

The museum displayed ancient artifacts collected by an Egyptian.
Many Egyptians live along the banks of the Nile River.
She befriended an Egyptian while traveling in Cairo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Egyptian - relating to Egypt or its people (adjective)

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɪpʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪpʃən / (BrE)
the ancient and now extinct Afro-Asiatic language spoken in Egypt under the Pharaohs, with written records dating back to 3000 BC
ancient language of Egypt

Examples

The Egyptian language was inscribed on ancient monuments throughout the Nile valley.
Linguists have deciphered much of the Egyptian texts thanks to the Rosetta Stone.
Classical Egyptian was used in religious and literary writings during the reign of the Pharaohs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Coptic - the latest stage of the Egyptian language, used in Christian Egypt

Etymology

From Latin 'Aegyptianus', from Greek 'Aigyptios', from 'Aigyptos' (Egypt).

Synset POS: n
