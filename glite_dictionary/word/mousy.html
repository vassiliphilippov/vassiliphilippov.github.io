mousy

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaʊsi / (AmE), / ˈmaʊsi / (BrE)
(of a place) infested with mice.
infested with mice

Examples

The old farmhouse was so mousy that traps had to be set in every room.
After months of neglect, the abandoned cabin became mousy and uninhabitable.
Despite her efforts, the cellar remained mousy throughout the winter.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmaʊsi / (AmE), / ˈmaʊzi / (BrE)
having a dull, pale brown or brownish-grey color, similar to that of a mouse, often used to describe hair or fabrics
dull pale brown color (like a mouse)

Examples

Her mousy hair made her seem almost invisible in the crowd.
He chose a mousy brown paint for the living room walls, giving it a muted look.
The coat was a mousy color that faded even more with age.

Synset Examples

a mousy brownish-grey color
mousy hair
mouse-colored hair

Etymology

From mouse + -y, meaning 'resembling a mouse' in appearance or color.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00379342-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈmaʊsi / (AmE), / ˈmaʊzi / (BrE)
(of a person) quiet, timid, and lacking in confidence or effectiveness; shy or unassuming.
quiet and timid in manner

Examples

Although Sarah was always mousy in meetings, her ideas were excellent when she finally spoke up.
He gave a mousy reply and quickly averted his eyes.
The mousy student rarely participated in class but was a keen observer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timid - shy, lacking in courage or confidence
  • Antonym: confident - sure of oneself; possessing self-assurance

Etymology

Derived from 'mouse' (the small rodent), referring to timidity or shyness associated with mice, plus the adjective-forming suffix '-y'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00253692-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈmaʊsi / (AmE), / ˈmaʊzi / (BrE)
(of a color) drab pale brown or brownish-grey, resembling the color of a mouse, especially used to describe hair.
having a drab pale brownish-grey color

Examples

She always wished her mousy hair could be a more striking color.
He wore a mousy brown jacket that blended into the background.
The walls were painted a dull, mousy shade that made the room feel gloomy.

Synset Examples

a mousy brownish-grey color
mousy hair
mouse-colored hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drab - a dull light brown color

Etymology

From mouse + -y, referring to the typical color of a mouse's fur.
