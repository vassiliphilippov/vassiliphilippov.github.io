Anglian

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡliən / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡlɪən / (BrE)
a major group of Old English dialects, including Mercian and Northumbrian, spoken in parts of central and northern England
group of Old English dialects

Examples

The Anglian dialects influenced the development of modern northern English.
Linguists often classify Old English texts as either West Saxon or Anglian.
The poem was originally written in an Anglian version before being copied in West Saxon.

Etymology

From Latin 'Angliānus', relating to the Angles, a Germanic tribe.
