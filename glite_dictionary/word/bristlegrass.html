bristlegrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪsəlˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsəlˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
Any of several grasses of grasslands and woodlands characterized by large, arching spikes with long bristles below each spikelet, especially of the genus Setaria.
a grass with long bristly spikes

Examples

The field was overgrown with bristlegrass swaying gently in the wind.
Farmers often struggle to control bristlegrass in their cornfields.
Some birds use the seeds of bristlegrass as a source of food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxtail - a similar grass with bushy, bristle-like flower spikes

Etymology

From 'bristle' (stiff hair or bristle) + 'grass'.
