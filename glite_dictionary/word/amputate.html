to amputate

1. VERB

/ ˈæm.pjəˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈæm.pjʊ.teɪt / (BrE)
to cut off a limb or other body part from the body, usually by surgery
remove a body part surgically

Examples

The surgeon had to amputate his leg to save his life.
Doctors may amputate a limb that is severely infected.
After the accident, they were forced to amputate his left hand.

Synset Examples

amputate limbs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - take something away from a place or position
  • Antonym: to attach - to join or fasten something to something else

Etymology

From Latin 'amputare' (to cut off), from 'am-' (off) + 'putare' (to prune).
