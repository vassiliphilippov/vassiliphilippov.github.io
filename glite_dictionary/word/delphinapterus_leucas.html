Delphinapterus leucas

1. NOUN

/ dɛlˌfɪn.əpˈtɛɹ.əs luːˈkæs / (AmE), / dɛlˌfɪn.əpˈtɛə.rəs ljuːˈkæs / (BrE)
a small northern whale, white when adult, commonly known as the beluga whale
beluga whale; small white northern whale

Examples

The Delphinapterus leucas is easily recognized by its striking white coloration.
Researchers studied a group of Delphinapterus leucas in the shallow Arctic waters.
Unlike most whales, the Delphinapterus leucas lacks a dorsal fin, making it well-adapted to icy environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beluga whale - common name for Delphinapterus leucas, a small white whale

Etymology

From Greek delphin (dolphin) + apterus (without fin) + leucas (white).
