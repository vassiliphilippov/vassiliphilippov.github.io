to emaciate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈmeɪʃieɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈmasɪeɪt / (BrE)
to make abnormally thin or weak, especially as a result of illness or lack of food
cause to become abnormally thin or weak

Examples

The harsh diet began to emaciate him after several weeks.
Illness can emaciate even the strongest individuals if left untreated.
The disease quickly emaciated the once robust stray dog.

Synset Examples

The treatment emaciated him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to weaken - to make or become weaker
  • Antonym: to strengthen - to make or become stronger

Etymology

From Latin emaciatus, past participle of emaciare (to make lean), from e- (out) + macies (leanness).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈmeɪ.si.eɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈmæ.sɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
To become abnormally thin or weak, often because of illness or lack of food.
to become abnormally thin or weak

Examples

After weeks of starvation, the prisoners began to emaciate rapidly.
The disease caused her to emaciate despite her efforts to eat well.
If left untreated, chronic illnesses can emaciate even the healthiest individuals.

Synset Examples

She emaciated during the chemotherapy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to waste away - to gradually become very weak or thin, especially from illness
  • Antonym: to fatten - to make or become fatter or more nourished

Etymology

From Latin emaciatus, past participle of emaciare (to make thin), from e- (out) + macies (leanness).
