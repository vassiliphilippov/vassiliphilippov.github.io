richly

1. ADV

/ ˈrɪtʃli / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃli / (BrE)
In a rich, luxurious, or elaborate manner.
in a rich or elaborate manner

Examples

The room was richly decorated with gold and velvet drapes.
She was richly rewarded for her years of hard work.
The cake was richly flavored with chocolate and spices.

Synset Examples

he lives high

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavishly - in a lavish manner; with extravagance
  • Antonym: simply - in a simple manner; plainly, not richly

2. ADV

/ ˈrɪt͡ʃli / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃli / (BrE)
To a great or plentiful degree; in an abundant or ample manner.
to an abundant degree; amply

Examples

The newly decorated hall was richly adorned with gold leaf and velvet curtains.
He richly deserves the recognition he is finally receiving.
The play's atmosphere is richly evocative of the Renaissance period.

Synset Examples

these voices were amply represented
we benefited richly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amply - to an ample extent or degree
  • Antonym: scarcely - only just; almost not

Etymology

From Middle English 'richeli', from 'rich' + '-ly'.

3. ADV

/ ˈrɪtʃ.li / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃ.li / (BrE)
In a rich, elaborate, or sumptuous manner; with great decoration, quality, or abundance.
In a rich or luxurious manner

Examples

The hall was richly decorated with gold and marble.
She was richly dressed for the evening gala.
The cake was richly flavored with spices and fruit.

Synset Examples

lavishly decorated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavishly - in a luxurious or extravagant manner

Etymology

Formed from 'rich' + '-ly' (adverbial suffix), from Old English 'rice' (powerful, wealthy).
