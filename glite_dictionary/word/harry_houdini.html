Harry Houdini

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛri huːˈdiːni / (AmE), / ˈhæri huːˈdiːni / (BrE)
(1874–1926) a famous Hungarian-born American magician and escape artist, renowned for his sensational escape acts involving chains, handcuffs, and locked containers.
famous magician and escape artist (1874–1926)

Examples

Many people regard Harry Houdini as the greatest escape artist of all time.
In 1912, Harry Houdini amazed audiences with his famous underwater box escape.
The name Harry Houdini is still synonymous with daring magic and illusion.

Etymology

Born Erik Weisz in Hungary; adopted the stage name Houdini after French magician Jean Eugène Robert-Houdin.
