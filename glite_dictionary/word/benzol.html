benzol

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnˌzɔl / (AmE), / ˈbɛn.zɒl / (BrE)
a colorless, highly flammable, and carcinogenic liquid hydrocarbon, the simplest aromatic compound; another name for benzene or a mixture containing benzene
a colorless, carcinogenic hydrocarbon liquid

Examples

The factory produced large quantities of benzol used in chemical manufacturing.
Prolonged exposure to benzol can pose serious health risks due to its carcinogenic nature.
Strict regulations control the transport and storage of benzol because of its flammability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benzene - a colorless, volatile, flammable liquid aromatic hydrocarbon, C6H6

Etymology

From German Benzol, from Benzin (benzene) + -ol (indicating an alcohol or hydrocarbon).
