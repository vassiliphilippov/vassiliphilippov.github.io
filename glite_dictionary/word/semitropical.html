semitropical

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˈtrɑpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈtrɒpɪkəl / (BrE)
of or relating to the regions or climate just outside the tropics, typically warm and humid but not as hot or wet as tropical areas
characteristic of the subtropics

Examples

The coastal region has a semitropical climate, with mild winters and warm, humid summers.
Many semitropical plants can survive outdoors as long as temperatures stay above freezing.
The city is known for its semitropical gardens, which feature lush, exotic vegetation.

Synset Examples

even near the equator vegetation at 5000 ft is subtropical rather than tropical

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subtropical - of or relating to the subtropics
  • Degree Higher: tropical - of or relating to the tropics, especially very hot and humid

Etymology

From semi- (meaning 'half' or 'partly') + tropical, indicating partial resemblance to tropical climate.
