DAT

1. NOUN

/ diː eɪ tiː / (AmE), / diː eɪ tiː / (BrE)
a digital audio tape used for recording and storing sound, especially in professional audio settings
digital audio tape for recording sound

Examples

The band recorded their latest album directly onto DAT for higher sound quality.
We found an old DAT with unreleased tracks in the studio archive.
Some professional studios still use DAT for backup recordings.

Etymology

Abbreviation of Digital Audio Tape.
