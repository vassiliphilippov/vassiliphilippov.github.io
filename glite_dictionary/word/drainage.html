drainage

1. NOUN

/ ˈdreɪnɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdreɪnɪdʒ / (BrE)
the process of removing liquid from a place or object, typically by allowing it to flow away through channels or holes
the removal of liquid, usually by channels

Examples

Proper drainage prevents water from accumulating in the basement after heavy rain.
The surgery required a tube for drainage of excess fluids from the wound.
Farmers improved the field’s drainage to protect crops from flooding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drain - to remove liquid from something; to cause liquid to run out

Etymology

From French 'drainage', based on English 'drain'.
