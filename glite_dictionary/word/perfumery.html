perfumery

1. NOUN

/ pərˈfjuːməri / (AmE), / pəˈfjuːməri / (BrE)
an establishment where perfumes are manufactured or sold
place where perfumes are made or sold

Examples

The tour included a visit to a famous perfumery in the south of France.
She learned the art of fragrance blending while working at a local perfumery.
Many new scents are created every year in this renowned perfumery near Grasse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfumer - a person who makes perfumes

Etymology

From French 'parfumerie', from 'parfum' (perfume).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03922408-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ pərˈfjuməri / (AmE), / pəˈfjuːməri / (BrE)
the art or process of making perfumes
the art of making perfumes

Examples

After studying perfumery in France, she launched her own fragrance line.
Many historic techniques are still used in modern perfumery.
He considers perfumery to be both an art and a science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfumist - a person who makes perfumes
  • Synonym: perfumemaking - the process of making perfumes

Etymology

From French parfumerie, from parfumer (to perfume) + -erie (place of business or art).
