Circaea alpina

1. NOUN

/ sɜrˈsiːə ælˈpaɪnə / (AmE), / sɜːˈsiːə ælˈpiːnə / (BrE)
A species of enchanter's nightshade found in alpine and subalpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
alpine enchanter's nightshade

Examples

The delicate flowers of Circaea alpina bloom in shaded, mountainous forests during the summer.
Researchers discovered a population of Circaea alpina in the high-altitude meadows of Japan.
Gardeners interested in native alpine species admire the simple beauty of Circaea alpina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enchanter's nightshade - a plant of the genus Circaea

Etymology

From Latin 'Circaea' (the genus, named after the enchantress Circe in Greek mythology) and 'alpina' (alpine, of the Alps or high mountains).
