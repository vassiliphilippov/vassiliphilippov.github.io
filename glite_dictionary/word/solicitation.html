solicitation

1. NOUN

/ səˌlɪsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / səˌlɪsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of requesting or seeking something, especially money, assistance, or support, from someone
the act of asking for something, especially money

Examples

The charity sent out a solicitation for donations to support their new project.
We received a solicitation in the mail asking us to contribute to disaster relief funds.
His solicitation of money from local businesses was met with mixed responses.

Synset Examples

an appeal to raise money for starving children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: request - the act of asking for something
  • Synonym: appeal - a serious or urgent request, typically for help or money

Etymology

From Latin sollicitatio (an urging), from sollicitare (to agitate, urge).

2. NOUN

/ səˌlɪsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / səˌlɪsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a formal request or entreaty, especially one made to someone in a position of higher authority or status
formal request, especially to a superior

Examples

The ambassador submitted a solicitation to the president, asking for assistance on behalf of his country.
Their solicitation for a pardon was discussed at the highest level of government.
He ignored the solicitation from his subordinates to address their concerns during the meeting.

Synset Examples

a solicitation to the king for relief

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entreaty - an earnest or humble request
  • Synonym: petition - a formal written request, typically to an authority

Etymology

From Latin sollicitatio (an urging), from sollicitare (to disturb, entreat).

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ səˌlɪsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / səˌlɪsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of enticing or attempting to persuade someone to do something wrong or illegal, especially offering sex for money
enticing someone to do something illegal, esp. sex for money

Examples

He was arrested for solicitation after offering money to an undercover officer.
Laws against solicitation are intended to curb prostitution in the city.
The court found her guilty of solicitation based on the recorded conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostitution - the practice or occupation of engaging in sexual activity for payment
  • Synonym: incitement - the act of encouraging someone to do something illegal or violent
  • Antonym: dissuasion - the act of persuading someone not to take a particular course of action

Etymology

From Latin sollicitatio (incitement), from sollicitare (to disturb, agitate).
