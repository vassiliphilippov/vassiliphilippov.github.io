Erysimum allionii

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈrɪsɪməm ælɪˈoʊni.aɪ / (AmE), / ɛˈrɪsɪmjəm ælɪˈəʊnɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of showy, upright biennial or short-lived perennial flower, cultivated for its orange-yellow blossoms appearing in terminal clusters; sometimes classified under the genus Cheiranthus.
a species of orange-yellow flowering plant

Examples

The gardener planted several Erysimum allionii along the border for a bright splash of orange color.
Each spring, the Erysimum allionii blooms attract butterflies to the cottage garden.
Botanists debated whether Erysimum allionii should remain in its current genus or be reclassified.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Erysimum and the species epithet allionii, honoring Italian botanist Carlo Allioni.
