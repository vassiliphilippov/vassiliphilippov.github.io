springbok

1. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋˌbɑk / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋˌbɒk / (BrE)
a slender South African antelope (Antidorcas marsupialis) known for its leaps into the air and brown-and-white coloring
South African antelope known for jumping

Examples

A herd of springbok grazed on the open plains of South Africa.
The springbok is famous for its high, graceful leaps called pronking.
During the safari, we watched a young springbok bound away from a predator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gazelle - a small, slender antelope known for its speed and grace

Etymology

From Afrikaans, from Dutch 'spring' (jump) + 'bok' (antelope, goat).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋˌbɑk / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋˌbɒk / (BrE)
a small antelope native to southern Africa, known for its tendency to leap high into the air when alarmed or excited
a small leaping African antelope

Examples

The springbok can leap up to two meters when startled by predators.
A herd of springboks grazed peacefully on the open savanna.
The national rugby team of South Africa is nicknamed the Springbok.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gazelle - a small, slender antelope of Africa and Asia

Etymology

From Afrikaans, literally 'jumping buck', from Dutch 'springen' (to jump) + 'bok' (male goat, antelope).
