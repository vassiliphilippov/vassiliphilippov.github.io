Larvacea

1. NOUN

/ lɑrˈveɪʃiə / (AmE), / lɑːˈveɪsɪə / (BrE)
a class of small, free-swimming tunicate animals with tadpole-like bodies, some classified as an order in zoological taxonomy
a class of small free-swimming tunicate animals

Examples

Scientists study Larvacea to understand their role in ocean ecosystems.
The transparent bodies of Larvacea make them challenging to observe in their natural habitat.
Unlike other tunicates, Larvacea retain their tadpole-like shape throughout life.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, from Latin 'larva' (ghost, mask), referring to their larval form.
