mytilid

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪtɪlɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɪtɪlɪd / (BrE)
A marine bivalve mollusk of the family Mytilidae, typically having a dark elongated shell and living attached to solid surfaces in intertidal zones.
marine bivalve mollusk (mussel family)

Examples

Large populations of mytilid mussels thrive in rocky intertidal areas.
The study focused on the feeding habits of mytilid species along the coast.
Scientists observed that mytilid larvae settled preferentially on submerged pilings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mussel - a common edible bivalve mollusk related to mytilids

Etymology

From New Latin Mytilidae, the scientific family name, from Latin mytilus (mussel).
