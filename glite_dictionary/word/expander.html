expander

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspændər / (AmE), / ɪkˈspændə / (BrE)
an electronic device used to increase the dynamic range of an audio signal by making quiet sounds quieter or loud sounds louder
audio device increasing dynamic range

Examples

The audio engineer used a expander to enhance the clarity of the recording.
In noisy environments, a expander helps reduce the audibility of background hum.
Modern digital mixers often include a built-in expander for dynamic sound control.

Synset Examples

Expanders are generally used to make quiet sounds even quieter by reducing the level of an audio signal that falls below a set threshold level.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compressor - an electronic device that reduces the dynamic range of an audio signal

Etymology

From 'expand' + '-er', meaning one that expands; adopted in electronics for devices altering signal dynamics.
