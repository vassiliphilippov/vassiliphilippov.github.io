Quercus stellata

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs stəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs stɛˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of small deciduous oak tree native to eastern and central United States, with tough, moisture-resistant wood commonly used for fence posts.
species of oak tree native to east-central US

Examples

The dense wood of the Quercus stellata makes it ideal for constructing durable fence posts.
In the park, several mature Quercus stellata trees provide shade for visitors.
Many wildlife species rely on the acorns produced by the Quercus stellata each fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: post oak - common name for Quercus stellata

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Quercus' (oak), species name 'stellata' (starred, referring to the star-shaped leaf hairs).
