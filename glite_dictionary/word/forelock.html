forelock

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌlɒk / (BrE)
a lock of hair on a horse's mane that grows forward between the ears
a forward-growing lock of a horse's mane

Examples

The horse's forelock was neatly braided for the show.
Dust gathered around the pony's shaggy forelock as it grazed in the field.
She gently brushed the tangled forelock away from the stallion's eyes.

Etymology

From Middle English fore- (before) + lock (of hair).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01902647-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlɒk / (BrE)
a lock of hair that grows or falls over the forehead
lock of hair over the forehead

Examples

The horse's forelock was neatly brushed before the competition.
He brushed his forelock aside as he walked into the room.
A strand of her forelock kept falling into her eyes while she worked.

Etymology

From Middle English forelok, from fore- (before) + lock (a tress of hair).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05265534-n
Synset POS: n
