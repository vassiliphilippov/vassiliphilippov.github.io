Paderewski

1. PROPN

/ ˌpædərˈɛfski / (AmE), / ˌpadəˈrɛfski / (BrE)
Ignacy Jan Paderewski (1860–1941), Polish pianist, composer, and statesman who served as the first Prime Minister of independent Poland in 1919.
Polish pianist and statesman (1860–1941)

Examples

Paderewski performed to sold-out audiences across Europe and America.
In 1919, Paderewski became the first Prime Minister of independent Poland.
The legacy of Paderewski lives on through his contributions to music and politics.

Etymology

From Polish surname Paderewski.
