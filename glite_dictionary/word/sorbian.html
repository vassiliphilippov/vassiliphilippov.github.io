Sorbian

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔr.bi.ən / (AmE), / ˈsɔː.bi.ən / (BrE)
a member of a Slavic ethnic group from eastern Germany, or a speaker of the Sorbian language
member of a Slavic ethnic group or language speaker

Examples

Many Sorbians live in the regions of Lusatia in eastern Germany.
The festival attracted Sorbian speakers from across Europe.
She is proud of her Sorbian heritage and language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sorbs - the Slavic ethnic group to which Sorbians belong

Etymology

From German 'Sorben', the name for the Sorb people, plus '-ian'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔr.bi.ən / (AmE), / ˈsɔː.bi.ən / (BrE)
either of two closely related West Slavic languages spoken by the Sorbs in southeastern Germany
West Slavic language spoken in Germany

Examples

The Upper Sorbian language is still taught in some schools in Saxony.
He is fluent in both Sorbian and German.
Many cultural traditions are preserved through Sorbian literature and music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Slavic - relating to the family of languages including Sorbian

Etymology

From Sorb, the name of the people, + -ian; related to the Slavic ethnonym *Serbъ.
