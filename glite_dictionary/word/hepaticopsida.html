Hepaticopsida

1. NOUN

/ hɪˌpætɪˈkɒpsɪdə / (AmE), / hɛˌpætɪˈkɒpsɪdə / (BrE)
A class of non-vascular plants commonly known as liverworts, which includes several orders such as Anthocerotales, Jungermanniales, Marchantiales, and Sphaerocarpales.
class of liverworts (plants)
medical

Examples

The Hepaticopsida are an ancient group of plants often studied in botany courses.
Some species in Hepaticopsida can be found thriving in damp forest environments.
Researchers discovered a new order within the Hepaticopsida during the recent field expedition.

Etymology

From Greek *hēpar* (liver) and *-opsida* (appearance, form); named for the liver-shaped appearance of some species.

2. NOUN

/ hɛˌpætɪˈkɒpsɪdə / (AmE), / hɛˌpatɪˈkɒpsɪdə / (BrE)
A class of non-vascular plants known as liverworts, including several orders such as Anthocerotales and Marchantiales.
taxonomic class of liverworts

Examples

Researchers discovered a rare species within the Hepaticopsida class during their expedition in the rainforest.
The Hepaticopsida are distinguished by their simple leaf-like structures and reproduction method.
Studying Hepaticopsida helps scientists understand the evolution of land plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liverwort - a non-vascular plant in the division Marchantiophyta

Etymology

From Greek *hēpatikos* (of the liver) + New Latin *-opsida* (suffix used in plant taxonomy).
