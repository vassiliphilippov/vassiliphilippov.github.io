Dr.

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə / (BrE)
a person who is qualified and licensed to practice medicine; a medical doctor
licensed medical practitioner

Examples

I made an appointment with my Dr. to discuss the test results.
Whenever you feel unwell, it's best to consult your Dr. first.
Her Dr. prescribed medication for her allergies.

Synset Examples

I felt so bad I went to see my doctor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctor - a person who is qualified to treat people who are ill

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'doctor,' from Latin 'doctor' (teacher, learned man).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10040615-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktər / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə / (BrE)
a title used before the name of a person who has obtained a doctorate, such as a Ph.D.
title for someone with a doctorate

Examples

Dr. Lee will be giving the keynote speech at the conference on Saturday.
After earning her Ph.D., she officially became Dr. Smith.
Both Dr. Patel and Dr. Johnson contributed to the research article.

Synset Examples

she is a doctor of philosophy in physics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Doctor - a person with a doctorate degree

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'doctor', from Latin 'doctor' (teacher).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10041617-n
Synset POS: n
