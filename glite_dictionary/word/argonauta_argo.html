Argonauta argo

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrɡəˈnɔːtə ˈɑːrɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɑːɡənɔːtə ˈɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
A species of cephalopod mollusk found in warm seas, known for the females' delicate, papery, spiral shells.
species of shell-bearing cephalopod mollusk

Examples

The female Argonauta argo creates a beautiful, paper-thin shell to protect her eggs.
Marine biologists observed several Argonauta argo drifting near the surface of the Mediterranean Sea.
Shell collectors prize the delicate spirals produced by the Argonauta argo.

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining Greek 'Argonautēs' (Argonaut) and Latin 'argo' (relating to Argonauts in Greek mythology).
