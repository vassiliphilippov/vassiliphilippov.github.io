trephine

1. NOUN

/ trəˈfaɪn / (AmE), / trɪˈfiːn / (BrE)
a surgical instrument with a cylindrical blade used to remove circular sections of bone, especially from the skull.
surgical instrument for removing bone
medical

Examples

The surgeon used a trephine to cut a precise opening in the patient's skull.
After sterilizing the trephine, the neurosurgeon began the delicate procedure.
A trephine is often employed in cranial surgeries to create access for further treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trepan - a similar surgical instrument for cutting out circular pieces of bone

Etymology

From French 'tréphine', from Latin 'tres' (three) + 'finis' (end), originally relating to the three-edged shape of early instruments.

2. VERB

/ trəˈfaɪn / (AmE), / trɪˈfiːn / (BrE)
to operate on (the skull or another bone) using a trephine, typically by cutting out a circular piece.
operate with a trephine (medical)
medical

Examples

The surgeon decided to trephine the patient to relieve intracranial pressure.
They trephined the skull to access the affected brain tissue.
Modern techniques have made trephining much safer than in ancient times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trepan - to operate on the skull with a trepan; an alternative surgical instrument/verb

Etymology

From French 'tréphine', from Latin 'tres' (three) + 'finis' (end), relating to the shape of the instrument.
