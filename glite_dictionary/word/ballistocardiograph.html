ballistocardiograph

1. NOUN

/ bəˌlɪstoʊˈkɑːrdiəˌɡræf / (AmE), / bəˌlɪstəʊˈkɑːdiəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a medical instrument that measures the mechanical force of heart contractions and the amount of blood pumped by recording the body's recoil with each heartbeat
medical device for measuring heart's force via body recoil
medical

Examples

The ballistocardiograph records subtle body movements associated with each heartbeat.
A ballistocardiograph can help physicians analyze cardiac function without invasive procedures.
She underwent a test with a ballistocardiograph to evaluate her heart's performance.

Etymology

From Greek 'ballistos' (to throw) + 'cardio' (heart) + 'graph' (writing instrument).
