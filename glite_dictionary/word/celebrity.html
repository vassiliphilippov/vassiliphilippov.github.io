celebrity

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɛbrəti / (AmE), / səˈlɛbrəti / (BrE)
A famous person, especially in entertainment or sport, who is widely recognized by the public.
famous person; widely recognized figure

Examples

The celebrity was swarmed by fans as soon as he arrived at the airport.
Many young people dream of becoming a celebrity one day.
Despite her status as a celebrity, she tries to live a normal life.

Synset Examples

he was a baseball celebrity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star - a person who is famous, especially in film, music, or sport
  • Antonym: unknown - a person who is not known or not famous

Etymology

From Latin 'celebritas' (fame, throng), from 'celeber' (frequented, famous).

2. NOUN

/ səˈlɛbrəti / (AmE), / səˈlɛbrəti / (BrE)
The state of being famous and widely recognized or acclaimed.
the state of being famous

Examples

Winning the award brought her instant celebrity in the film industry.
He enjoyed the celebrity that came with being a best-selling author.
The sudden celebrity of the scientist surprised everyone in the university.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fame - the state of being known or talked about by many people
  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or not recognized

Etymology

From Old French 'celebrité', from Latin 'celebritas' (fame, crowd).
