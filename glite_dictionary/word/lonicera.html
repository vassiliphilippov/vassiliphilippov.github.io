Lonicera

1. PROPN

/ ləˈnɪsərə / (AmE), / ləˈnɪsərə / (BrE)
A genus of climbing or arching shrubs in the honeysuckle family, including various species known as woodbines or honeysuckles.
genus of plants; honeysuckles/woodbines

Examples

Many gardens feature Lonicera for its fragrant, tubular flowers and attractive berries.
The Lonicera genus includes both evergreen and deciduous woody vines.
Some species of Lonicera have become invasive outside their native range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - common name for the plants in the Lonicera genus

Etymology

Named for the German botanist Adam Lonitzer (1528–1586).

2. NOUN

/ ləˈnɪsərə / (AmE), / lɒˈnɪsərə / (BrE)
A genus of climbing or sprawling shrubs in the honeysuckle family, commonly known as honeysuckle or woodbine, with fragrant tubular flowers.
genus of honeysuckle shrubs

Examples

The flowers of Lonicera attract many species of hummingbirds due to their fragrance and nectar.
Gardeners often plant Lonicera along trellises for its rapid growth and beautiful blossoms.
Several species within the genus Lonicera are known for their sweetly scented flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - a common name for plants of the genus Lonicera
  • Synonym: woodbine - another common name for Lonicera species

Etymology

Named after the German botanist Adam Lonicer (1528–1586).
