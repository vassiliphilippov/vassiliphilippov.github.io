persona

1. NOUN

/ pərˈsoʊnə / (AmE), / pəˈsəʊnə / (BrE)
the role or character that an actor portrays in a play, film, or other performance
character played by an actor

Examples

The actor slipped easily into the persona of the cunning villain.
In her latest film, she adopted a completely different persona from her usual roles.
He struggled to maintain his stage persona during the long tour.

Synset Examples

she played the part of Desdemona

Related Terms

  • Synonym: character - a role played by an actor in a play or film

Etymology

From Latin persona (mask, character in a play).

2. NOUN

/ pərˈsoʊnə / (AmE), / pəˈsəʊnə / (BrE)
(in Jungian psychology) the aspect of someone's character that is presented to or perceived by others, as opposed to their true self.
outward personality or facade

Examples

Many celebrities develop a persona that is quite different from their true selves.
She maintained a professional persona at work, but her friends knew she was much more relaxed outside the office.
His cheerful persona hid feelings of anxiety and insecurity.

Synset Examples

a public image is as fragile as Humpty Dumpty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mask - a covering used to conceal the face, figuratively used for a facade
  • Synonym: facade - an outward appearance maintained to conceal a less pleasant reality
  • Antonym: self - the true or inner identity of a person

Etymology

From Latin persona (mask, character, role).
