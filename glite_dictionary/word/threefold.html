threefold

1. ADJ

/ ˈθriˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈθriːfəʊld / (BrE)
Having three distinct parts, aspects, or elements; triple in character or effect.
having three parts or aspects

Examples

The professor's responsibilities are threefold: teaching, researching, and advising students.
The new policy was designed with a threefold purpose to improve quality, cut costs, and boost morale.
The organization faces a threefold challenge involving funding, staffing, and community engagement.

Synset Examples

a double (or dual) role for an actor
the office of a clergyman is twofold; public preaching and private influence
every episode has its double and treble meaning

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: twofold - having two parts or aspects
  • Synonym: triple - consisting of three things or parts

Etymology

From Old English 'þrēo' (three) + 'fold' (times, multiplied).
