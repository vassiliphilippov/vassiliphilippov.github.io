cantillation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæn.tɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæn.tɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the ritual chanting of texts, especially in religious services, according to traditional melodies and rhythms
liturgical chanting of texts

Examples

The Torah reading was marked by elaborate cantillation that followed ancient traditions.
Scholars study the origins of cantillation in both Jewish and Christian liturgical practices.
She practiced the cantillation of Psalms for weeks before the synagogue ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chanting - singing or intoning words, especially in a ritual or religious context

Etymology

From Latin 'cantillatio', from 'cantillare' (to chant, sing softly), diminutive of 'cantare' (to sing).
