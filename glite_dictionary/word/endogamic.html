endogamic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛn.doʊˈɡæ.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəʊˈɡæ.mɪk / (BrE)
(of a plant) characterized by or suitable for fertilization by pollen from another flower of the same species.
able to be fertilized by same-species pollen

Examples

Certain plants are endogamic, allowing cross-fertilization within the same species.
The endogamic nature of this flower increases its chances of successful pollination.
Botanists observed that endogamic species often have mechanisms to encourage intra-species pollination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exogamic - characterized by fertilization involving pollen from a different species

Etymology

From Greek endon (within) and gamos (marriage).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛn.doʊˈɡæ.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəʊˈɡæ.mɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by the custom of marrying only within a specific clan, tribe, or social group.
relating to marriage within a group

Examples

The tribe has long upheld endogamic traditions to preserve its cultural heritage.
Membership in certain castes was maintained through strictly endogamic marriages.
Their community is known for its endogamic practices, marrying only within their own social group.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exogamic - relating to marriage outside a group

Etymology

From endogamy, from Greek endon (within) + gamos (marriage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00874359-a
Synset POS: a
