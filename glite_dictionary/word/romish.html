romish

1. ADJ

/ ˈroʊmɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmɪʃ / (BrE)
of or relating to the Roman Catholic Church (often used in a derogatory or disparaging way, especially in earlier English)
relating to the Roman Catholic Church
archaic

Examples

Many Protestant authors in the 19th century referred to Catholic practices as romish rituals.
The pamphlet warned against what it called the dangers of romish influence in public schools.
Certain traditions in the church were dismissed as being overly romish by reformers.

Synset Examples

the Roman Catholic Church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catholic - of or relating to the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Rome + -ish, meaning 'of or relating to Rome or Roman Catholicism.'
