Wolffiella gladiata

1. NOUN

/ wʌlˈfiːələ ɡlædiˈeɪtə / (AmE), / wɒlˈfiːələ ˌɡlædɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of small aquatic plant with narrow, flat, sickle-shaped submerged fronds, found in North America.
aquatic plant species of North America

Examples

Wolffiella gladiata can often be found floating in shallow freshwater ponds in the southeastern United States.
The narrow fronds of Wolffiella gladiata make it easy to distinguish from other duckweeds.
Researchers are studying the habitat preferences of Wolffiella gladiata due to its restricted distribution.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Wolffiella' and Latin 'gladiata' (sword-shaped), referring to its leaf shape.
