bare-boned

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛr ˌboʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbeə ˌbəʊnd / (BrE)
having only the basic or essential elements; stripped of nonessentials, minimalist
minimalist; stripped down to basics

Examples

The company's bare-boned website loads quickly and is easy to navigate.
They presented a bare-boned version of the proposal to the investors.
Her writing style is bare-boned, avoiding any unnecessary words or embellishments.

Synset Examples

The presentation was bare-boned but effective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minimal - very small in amount; only what is necessary
  • Synonym: austere - simple or plain; without decoration or luxury
  • Antonym: elaborate - involving many careful details; richly detailed or decorative

Etymology

From 'bare' + past participle 'boned', referring originally to having only bones; figuratively, stripped down to the essentials.
