shark

1. VERB

/ ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ʃɑːk / (BrE)
(of a person) to behave in a cunning or deceptive way, especially to take advantage of others.
behave cunningly or deceptively

Examples

They accused him of trying to shark his way into a better deal.
Some gamblers will shark their opponents by pretending to be inexperienced.
She refused to shark her colleagues to get ahead at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or fraudulently
  • Synonym: swindle - to use deception to deprive someone of money or possessions

to hunt or catch sharks

3. NOUN

/ ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a large, predatory fish with a long body, a heterocercal tail, and tough, toothlike scales, found mostly in the ocean
large predatory marine fish

Examples

Swimmers were warned to stay out of the water after a shark was sighted near the beach.
The documentary explored the hunting behavior of the great white shark.
Some species of shark can be dangerous to humans, but attacks are rare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fish - cold-blooded aquatic vertebrate animal

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English 'sharke' (villain, sharper), later applied to the fish.

/ ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a person who is extremely skilled at an activity, especially in a way that is considered aggressive or slightly dishonest
an expert or aggressive, skillful person
informal

Examples

You have to watch out for sharks in the real estate industry.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation

5. NOUN

/ ʃɑrk / (AmE), / ʃɑːk / (BrE)
a person who is ruthlessly ambitious, greedy, and often dishonest, especially in business or gambling
ruthlessly ambitious or dishonest person

Examples

Everyone in the office knows he's a shark who will step on anyone to get ahead.
The world of high-stakes poker is full of sharks looking to take advantage of newcomers.
Watch out for business sharks who promise big profits with little risk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindler - a person who cheats others out of money or assets
  • Synonym: fraudster - someone who commits fraud, especially for personal gain
  • Antonym: honest person - a person who is truthful and fair

Etymology

Metaphoric use of 'shark' (the fish, known as a predatory animal) to describe aggressive or unscrupulous people, first recorded in the 16th century.
