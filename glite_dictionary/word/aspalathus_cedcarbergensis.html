Aspalathus cedcarbergensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæspəˈleɪθəs sɪdˌkɑrˈbɜrɡɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌaspəˈleɪθəs sɪdˌkɑːˈbɜːɡɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A South African shrub with flat, pointed leaves and yellow flowers, whose aromatic dried leaves are used to make herbal tea.
South African shrub used for herbal tea

Examples

The leaves of Aspalathus cedcarbergensis are harvested and dried to brew a distinctive herbal tea.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Aspalathus cedcarbergensis in South Africa.
Aspalathus cedcarbergensis is well-known for its bright yellow flowers and aromatic, acuminate leaves.

Etymology

Aspalathus is from Greek ‘aspalathos’, a thorny plant; cedcarbergensis refers to the Cedarberg region in South Africa.
