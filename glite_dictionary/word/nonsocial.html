nonsocial

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈsoʊʃəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈsəʊʃəl / (BrE)
(of animals or plants) not living or growing in groups or colonies; solitary in habit.
not living or growing in groups; solitary

Examples

Many species of nonsocial insects are active hunters or solitary nesters.
The scientist compared the behaviors of nonsocial and social animals in the same habitat.
Unlike ants and bees, nonsocial species rarely cooperate for food gathering.

Synset Examples

solitary bees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solitary - (of animals) living alone or in pairs, not in groups

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' meaning 'not' and 'social' from Latin 'socialis' (of companionship).
