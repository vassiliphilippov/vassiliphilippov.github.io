Midas

1. PROPN

/ ˈmaɪdəs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪdəs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a king of Phrygia who was given the power to turn everything he touched into gold, often associated with great wealth or greed.
mythical king with golden touch

Examples

Many investors wished they had Midas's Midas touch during the booming economy.
The story of Midas warns of the dangers of greed and desire for wealth.
In literature, Midas is often depicted as a symbol of both fortune and folly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Midas touch - the ability to turn any project into a success or to make money easily

Etymology

From Greek Μίδας (Mídas), the name of the legendary king.
