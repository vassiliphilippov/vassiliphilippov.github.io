cadetship

1. NOUN

/ kəˈdɛtˌʃɪp / (AmE), / kəˈdɛtʃɪp / (BrE)
the period or position of being a cadet, especially in a military or police training institution
position or period of being a cadet

Examples

After completing his cadetship, he was commissioned as a junior officer.
The cadetship program provides rigorous training for future police officers.
Her cadetship at the naval academy lasted for four years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cadet - a young trainee in the armed services or police force

Etymology

From 'cadet' + '-ship', denoting state or condition.
