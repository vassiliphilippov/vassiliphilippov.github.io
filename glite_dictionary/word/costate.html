costate

1. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊˌsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒs.teɪt / (BrE)
(of an organism or structure) having ribs or rib-like ridges.
having ribs or rib-like ridges

Examples

The costate leaves of the fern are easily identifiable by their prominent ridges.
Some mollusks develop costate shells as a form of structural reinforcement.
The fossil showed a costate pattern along the thoracic region, indicating rib development.

Etymology

From Latin 'costatus', from 'costa' meaning 'rib'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊˌsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒsteɪt / (BrE)
(of a surface) having a ribbed or riblike texture, marked with ribs or ridges.
having a ribbed texture

Examples

The shell of the fossil displayed a distinctly costate surface.
Under the microscope, the leaf appeared costate due to its prominent veins.
Many marine organisms have costate exoskeletons for added strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striate - marked with stripes or grooves
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface

Etymology

From Latin 'costatus' meaning 'ribbed', from 'costa' (rib).
