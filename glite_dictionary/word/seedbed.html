seedbed

1. NOUN

/ ˈsidˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈsiːdbɛd / (BrE)
An area of prepared soil where seeds are sown and seedlings are grown before being transplanted.
area for growing seedlings

Examples

The gardener carefully prepared the seedbed for this year's crop of vegetables.
Young plants grown in a seedbed are usually stronger and healthier when transplanted.
A moist and fertile seedbed is essential for successful germination of seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nursery - a place where plants are grown for transplanting, sale, or experimentation

Etymology

From 'seed' + 'bed', denoting a bed of soil for seed cultivation.
