sachet

1. NOUN

/ sæˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˈsæʃeɪ / (BrE)
a small soft bag filled with perfumed powder or dried herbs, used to scent clothes in drawers or chests
small perfumed bag for scenting clothes

Examples

She placed a lavender sachet in her dresser drawer to keep her clothes smelling fresh.
The gift box included a silk sachet filled with dried rose petals.
You can make your own sachet by filling a small fabric bag with herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: packet - a small package or container

Etymology

From French 'sachet' (small bag), diminutive of 'sac' (bag), from Latin 'saccus'.
