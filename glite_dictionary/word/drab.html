drab

1. ADJ

/ dræb / (AmE), / dræb / (BrE)
of a light brownish-green color, often used to describe dull-colored fabric or natural tones.
light brownish-green color

Examples

He wore a drab uniform that blended into the forest surroundings.
The curtains were a faded drab color, giving the room a somber feel.
Many military vehicles are painted in drab shades to avoid detection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: olive - yellowish-green color similar to drab
  • Synonym: khaki - light brownish or yellowish-brown color, often used for military uniforms

Etymology

From Old French 'drabe' (a kind of cloth), possibly from Medieval Latin 'drappus' (cloth).

2. ADJ

/ dræb / (AmE), / dræb / (BrE)
Lacking brightness or color; dull and without interest or liveliness.
dull, lacking color or brightness

Examples

The walls of the old office were painted a drab shade of gray, making the room feel gloomy.
She avoided wearing drab colors and always chose bright, cheerful outfits.
Despite the drab weather, they decided to go for a walk in the park.

Synset Examples

drab faded curtains
sober Puritan grey
children in somber brown clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - lacking brightness, vividness, or sheen
  • Antonym: vivid - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images; bright and distinct

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English 'drabbe' (mud, filth) or from Old French 'drabe' (dirty woman).

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ dræb / (AmE), / dræb / (BrE)
dull and lacking in liveliness, charm, or surprise; not interesting or exciting.
dull; lacking liveliness or charm

Examples

She found the room drab and uninspiring, so she decided to redecorate it.
His clothes were so drab that he blended into the crowd without anyone noticing.
After months of drab routine, she craved something new and exciting.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - not interesting or exciting; lacking brightness or vividness
  • Antonym: vivid - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images; lively
  • Synonym: dreary - dull, bleak, and lifeless; depressing

Etymology

From Middle English *drabbe* (mud), later also 'dull, light brown' possibly from Scots.

4. NOUN

/ dræb / (AmE), / dræb / (BrE)
a dull, greyish to yellowish or light olive brown colour
a dull brownish colour

Examples

The walls were painted in a pale drab that made the room look even colder.
Old army uniforms were often made in shades of drab to provide camouflage.
A carpet of drab covered the floor, giving the room an understated elegance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: khaki - a light brownish-yellow colour
  • Synonym: beige - a pale sandy yellowish-brown colour
  • Degree Higher: olive - a yellowish-green colour

Etymology

From the color of undyed fabric; related to Old French drap (cloth).

Examples
