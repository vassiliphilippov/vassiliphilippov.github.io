Oligoplites

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːlɪˈɡɑːplaɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪˈɡɒplaɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of fishes in the family Carangidae, commonly known as leatherjackets, found in warm coastal waters.
a genus of leatherjacket fishes

Examples

Species of Oligoplites are often recognized by their streamlined, silver bodies.
The Oligoplites genus is frequently found in tropical marine waters.
Researchers studied the behavior of Oligoplites during the spawning season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leatherjacket - a common name for various fishes, including those in the genus Oligoplites

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'oligos' (few, little) + 'hoplites' (armored man).
