Trachelospermum

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrækiˌlɔˈspɜrməm / (AmE), / ˌtrækɪlɒˈspɜːməm / (BrE)
a genus of woody, Asiatic vines characterized by milky sap in their leaves and stems

Examples

The fragrant flowers of Trachelospermum make it a popular ornamental plant.
Some species of Trachelospermum are commonly trained to climb trellises in gardens.

2. NOUN

/ trəˌkɛloʊˈspɜrməm / (AmE), / trakɪˌlɒspɜːˈmʌm / (BrE)
A genus of Asiatic woody vines with milky sap in leaves and stems, commonly known as star jasmine.
genus of Asiatic woody vines

Examples

The Trachelospermum in my garden produces fragrant white flowers every summer.
Botanists studied several species within the Trachelospermum genus native to East Asia.
Many gardeners appreciate Trachelospermum for its ability to climb walls and trellises.

Etymology

From Greek trachēlos (neck) and sperma (seed), referring to the shape of the seeds.
