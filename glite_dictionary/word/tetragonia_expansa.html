Tetragonia expansa

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˈɡoʊniə ɪkˈspænsə / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈɡəʊniə ɪkˈspansə / (BrE)
A coarse, sprawling plant native to Australasia, cultivated for its edible shoots and succulent leaves; also known as New Zealand spinach.
Australasian plant with edible leaves

Examples

The leaves of Tetragonia expansa are often used as a substitute for spinach in cooking.
Farmers in coastal regions sometimes grow Tetragonia expansa for its resilience and nutritional value.
Tetragonia expansa thrives in sandy soils and is popular in temperate gardens.

Etymology

From genus name Tetragonia (Greek: tetra- 'four' + gōnia 'angle') and species epithet expansa, Latin for 'expanded'.
