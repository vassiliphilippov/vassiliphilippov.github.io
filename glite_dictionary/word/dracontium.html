Dracontium

1. NOUN

/ drəˈkɑːn.ti.əm / (AmE), / drəˈkɒn.ti.əm / (BrE)
A genus of strongly malodorous tropical American plants in the arum family, usually with gigantic leaves.
genus of tropical malodorous plants

Examples

The enormous leaves of the Dracontium make it a distinctive plant in tropical gardens.
Scientists observed the unpleasant odor emitted by the Dracontium when it bloomed.
Collectors value Dracontium species for their unusual size and appearance.

Etymology

From Latin 'dracontium', from Greek 'drakontion', diminutive of 'drakōn' (dragon), referring to the plant's appearance.

2. NOUN

/ drəˈkɒn.ti.əm / (AmE), / drəˈkɒn.ti.əm / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical American herbs with corms, belonging to the arum family.
genus of tropical American cormous herbs

Examples

The Dracontium genus is known for its distinctive cormous herbs native to tropical America.
Botanists are studying a rare Dracontium species found in the Amazon rainforest.
Several ornamental plants are classified within the Dracontium genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arum - plant of the Araceae family

Etymology

From Greek drakontion, a kind of arum, from drakōn (dragon, serpent) referencing the plant's appearance.
