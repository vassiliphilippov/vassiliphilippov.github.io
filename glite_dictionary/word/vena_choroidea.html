vena choroidea

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə kəˌrɔɪˈdiː.ə / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə kɒˌrɔɪˈdiː.ə / (BrE)
either of two veins that drain blood from the choroid coat of the eye
vein of the choroid layer of the eye
medical

Examples

Damage to the vena choroidea can affect blood flow in the choroid layer.
Ophthalmologists examined the patient's vena choroidea during the procedure.
The vena choroidea plays a crucial role in draining blood from the eye's choroid coat.

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'choroidea' (relating to the choroid).
