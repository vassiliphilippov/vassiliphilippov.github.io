bicephalous

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / baɪˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
Having two heads.
having two heads

Examples

The ancient statue depicts a bicephalous deity watching over the temple.
Some rare animals are born bicephalous, an unusual biological phenomenon.
In mythology, the bicephalous serpent is often considered a symbol of duality.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tricephalous - having three heads
  • Degree Lower: monocepahalous - having one head

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + Greek 'kephalē' (head) via scientific Latin.
