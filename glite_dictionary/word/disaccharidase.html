disaccharidase

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.sæ.kəˈraɪ.deɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɪ.sæk.əˈraɪ.deɪs / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the breakdown of disaccharides into monosaccharides
enzyme breaking down disaccharides
medical

Examples

Lactase is a type of disaccharidase that breaks down lactose in the small intestine.
A deficiency in disaccharidase can lead to digestive problems with certain sugars.
Researchers measured the activity of each disaccharidase in the intestinal tissue samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a protein that catalyzes chemical reactions in living organisms

Etymology

From 'disaccharide' + '-ase' (enzyme suffix).
