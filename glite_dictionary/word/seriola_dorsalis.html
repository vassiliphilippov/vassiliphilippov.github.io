Seriola dorsalis

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈroʊliə dɔrˈsælɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈrɒliə dɔːˈsælɪs / (BrE)
A game fish of southern California and Mexico, distinguished by its yellow tail fin.
a game fish with a yellow tail found off California and Mexico

Examples

Many anglers travel to Baja California to catch Seriola dorsalis during the peak fishing season.
The distinctive yellow tail fin makes Seriola dorsalis easy to identify among other game fish.
Cooks often prepare Seriola dorsalis as grilled fillets or sashimi due to its firm, flavorful flesh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellowtail - common name for Seriola dorsalis, a fish with a yellow tail

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Seriola' as the genus and 'dorsalis' meaning 'of the back'.
