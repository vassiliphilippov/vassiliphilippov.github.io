setose

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiːˌtoʊs / (AmE), / ˈsiːtəʊs / (BrE)
(of an organism or part) having or covered with bristles or bristle-like structures; bristly.
having bristles or bristle-like structures

Examples

Several species of caterpillars are distinguished by their setose bodies.
The plant's setose stems made it difficult to handle without gloves.
Under the microscope, the insect's setose legs were clearly visible.

Synset Examples

a horse with a short bristly mane
bristly shrubs
burred fruits
setaceous whiskers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bristly - having a stiff and prickly texture like bristles

Etymology

From Latin 'setosus' meaning 'bristly', from 'seta' (bristle).
