distraught

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstrɔt / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɔːt / (BrE)
extremely upset and anxious, so that you cannot think clearly; deeply agitated by emotion.
deeply upset and agitated

Examples

She was distraught over the news of her friend's accident.
His distraught parents searched the neighborhood all night.
Appearing distraught, he could barely speak during the interview.

Synset Examples

distraught with grief

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From Middle English, variant of 'distract', from Latin 'distractus'.
