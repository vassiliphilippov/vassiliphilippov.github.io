adhesiveness

1. NOUN

/ ədˈhiːsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ədˈhiːsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to stick or hold together firmly, especially the tendency of different surfaces to cling to each other
the quality of sticking together

Examples

The adhesiveness of the tape made it perfect for sealing boxes during the move.
Scientists measured the adhesiveness of the new polymer on metal surfaces.
Because of its strong adhesiveness, Super Glue can bond a wide variety of materials.

Synset Examples

the mutual adhesiveness of cells
a heated hydraulic press was required for adhesion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adhesion - the action or process of adhering to a surface or object
  • Synonym: cohesiveness - the quality of sticking together of the same kind of molecules or materials

Etymology

From Latin *adhaesivus* (tending to stick), based on *adhaerere* (to stick to).
