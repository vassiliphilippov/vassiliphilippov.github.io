atole

1. NOUN

/ əˈtoʊleɪ / (AmE), / əˈtəʊleɪ / (BrE)
A traditional Mexican and Central American drink or porridge made from masa (ground corn), sweetened and often flavored with chocolate, fruit, or spices.
traditional Mexican corn-based drink or porridge

Examples

Every morning, the vendor served hot atole to the workers at the market.
We drank a cup of chocolate atole to warm up during the festival.
She made strawberry atole as a special treat for her family.

Etymology

From Spanish 'atole', from Nahuatl 'atolli' meaning 'watery' or 'diluted'.
