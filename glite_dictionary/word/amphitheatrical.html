amphitheatrical

1. ADJ

/ ˌæm.fə.θiˈæ.trɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌam.fɪ.θiˈæ.trɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of an amphitheater
relating to an amphitheater

Examples

The opera was staged in an amphitheatrical setting that resembled ancient Rome.
The architect chose an amphitheatrical design to maximize visibility for the audience.
Her speech echoed through the amphitheatrical hall, reaching every listener.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphitheater - a round or oval building with tiers of seats around a central area

Etymology

From 'amphitheater' + '-ical', modeled after the Latin 'amphitheatrum'.
