Middle Greek

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdəl ˈɡrik / (AmE), / ˈmɪdəl ˈɡriːk / (BrE)
the stage of the Greek language spoken and written from about 600 to 1200 AD, between Ancient and Modern Greek
stage of Greek, 600-1200 AD

Examples

Many important Byzantine texts were written in Middle Greek.
Scholars often study Middle Greek to understand the transition from Ancient to Modern Greek.
The pronunciation of Middle Greek differs from both its ancient and modern forms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Ancient Greek - the Greek language before about 600 AD
  • Degree Higher: Modern Greek - the Greek language after about 1453 AD

Etymology

From English 'middle' + 'Greek', indicating the historical position between Ancient and Modern Greek.
