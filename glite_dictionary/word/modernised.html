modernised

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑdərnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈmɒdənaɪzd / (BrE)
Brought up to date or adapted to modern needs or habits.
brought up to date
british

Examples

The modernised kitchen now has energy-efficient appliances and sleek cabinets.
During the renovations, the theatre was completely modernised to improve comfort and accessibility.
Many old textbooks have been modernised with updated information and colorful illustrations.

Synset Examples

modernized methods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modernized - brought up to date (American spelling)
  • Antonym: antiquated - old-fashioned or outdated

Etymology

From 'modern' + '-ise' (British variant of '-ize'), meaning to make modern.
