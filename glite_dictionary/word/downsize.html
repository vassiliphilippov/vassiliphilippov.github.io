to downsize

1. VERB

/ ˈdaʊn.saɪz / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.saɪz / (BrE)
to reduce the number of employees in a company, usually to cut costs
reduce workforce; lay off staff

Examples

The company plans to downsize its staff by fifteen percent this year.
After the merger, several departments were forced to downsize.
Many workers were downsized as the factory struggled to remain profitable.

Synset Examples

three secretaries were downsized during the financial crisis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lay off - to dismiss employees, usually for economic reasons

2. VERB

/ ˈdaʊn.saɪz / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.saɪz / (BrE)
(of a company or organization) to reduce the number of employees, or decrease in size, typically to cut costs.
reduce staff or size (company)

Examples

The company was forced to downsize after several quarters of declining profits.
After the merger, management decided to downsize the marketing department.
Many employees were worried that the firm might downsize before the end of the year.

Synset Examples

the company downsized its research staff

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to expand - to increase in size or number (especially of a business or staff)

Etymology

Formed from 'down' + 'size'; first used in American business English in the 1980s.

3. VERB

/ ˈdaʊn.saɪz / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.saɪz / (BrE)
to design or manufacture in a smaller size
make or design something smaller

Examples

The company decided to downsize their printers to better fit on customers' desks.
Many electronics manufacturers now downsize their devices to make them more portable.
They had to downsize the machine to meet new environmental regulations.

Synset Examples

the car makers downsized the SUVs when fuel became very expensive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reduce - to make something smaller in amount, size, or degree
  • Antonym: to enlarge - to make something larger

Etymology

Formed from 'down' (reduce) + 'size'; used since the late 20th century.

4. VERB

/ ˈdaʊn.saɪz / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.saɪz / (BrE)
to make something smaller, especially by reducing the number of people employed, the size of an organization, or the space you occupy
reduce size or scale (esp. business or home)

Examples

Last year, the company was forced to downsize due to declining sales.
After retirement, they chose to downsize from a house to an apartment.
Many organizations downsize in order to remain profitable during economic downturns.

Synset Examples

The company plans to downsize its operations next quarter.
We decided to downsize our living space for simplicity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to expand - to increase in size, number, or importance
  • Synonym: shrink - to become or make smaller in size

Etymology

From 'down' (reduce, lower) + 'size' (magnitude, dimensions), first popularized in business contexts in the 1980s.
