terrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛriər / (AmE), / ˈtɛriə / (BrE)
any of several small, short-bodied breeds of dog, originally bred to hunt animals living underground, such as foxes and badgers
a small breed of hunting dog

Examples

The farmer kept a terrier to control the population of rats in his barn.
At the dog show, her spirited terrier impressed the judges with its energy.
They adopted a rescue terrier that quickly became part of the family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog - a domesticated carnivorous mammal kept as a pet or for work

Etymology

From Old French *terrier* (earth-dog), from Latin *terra* (earth).
