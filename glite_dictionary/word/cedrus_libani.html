Cedrus libani

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdrəs lɪˈbeɪnaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsiːdrəs lɪˈbɑːni / (BrE)
a large cedar tree native to Lebanon and northwestern Syria, known for its longevity and height
cedar tree native to Lebanon and Syria

Examples

The ancient temples were built with wood from the Cedrus libani.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting the endangered Cedrus libani populations in the wild.
Many national emblems in the Middle East feature the Cedrus libani.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedar of Lebanon - common name for Cedrus libani, a large cedar tree native to the Middle East
  • Degree Higher: cedar - any of several evergreen conifers of the genus Cedrus

Etymology

From Latin scientific name; 'Cedrus' from Greek 'kedros' (cedar), 'libani' refers to Lebanon.
