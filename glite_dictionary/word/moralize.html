to moralize

1. VERB

/ ˈmɔrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to attempt to improve the moral standards or behavior of someone or something, often by giving moral advice
improve someone's morals by advice

Examples

The teacher tried to moralize the students, encouraging them to act with honesty and integrity.
Some parents attempt to moralize their children's behavior through stories about right and wrong.
It's easy to moralize when you're not facing the same challenges as others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to preach - to give moral advice, especially in a tiresome way
  • Synonym: lecture - to give a long, serious talk intended to criticize or instruct

Etymology

From French 'moraliser', from Latin 'moralis' (moral).

2. VERB

/ ˈmɔrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to speak, write, or behave as if giving a moral lesson, often expressing judgments about what is right or wrong
give moral advice or judgments

Examples

He tends to moralize whenever the conversation turns to politics.
Instead of offering practical advice, she chose to moralize about our choices.
Readers may find the author begins to moralize rather than let the story speak for itself.

Synset Examples

This man always sermonizes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sermonize - to deliver moral or religious lectures; to preach
  • Synonym: to preach - to deliver a religious or moral sermon
  • Antonym: to condone - to accept or allow behavior that is morally wrong

Etymology

From French moraliser, from Latin moralis (moral).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00830035-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈmɔːrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to interpret or explain the moral meaning of something, especially a story or event
interpret as having moral meaning

Examples

The teacher tried to moralize the fable for the students so they would learn a life lesson.
It is tempting to moralize historical events by assigning good and bad characters to each side.
Critics often moralize works of fiction, looking for deeper ethical meanings.

Synset Examples

moralize a story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to preach - to deliver a moral or religious message, often with moral instruction
  • Synonym: to sermonize - to deliver or compose moral lessons, especially in a tedious way

Etymology

From Late Latin moralizare (to explain morally), from Latin moralis (moral).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00940652-v
Synset POS: v
