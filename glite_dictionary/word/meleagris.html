Meleagris

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛliˈæɡrɪs / (AmE), / ˌmiːliˈæɡrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Meleagrididae, comprising the wild and domestic turkeys.
genus of wild and domestic turkeys

Examples

The genus Meleagris includes both wild and domestic turkeys.
Researchers compared different species within Meleagris to understand their evolutionary history.
The Meleagris gallopavo is widely farmed for its meat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turkey - a large bird native to North America, domesticated for food

Etymology

From Latin *Meleagris* (guinea-fowl, turkey), from Ancient Greek *meleagris*.
