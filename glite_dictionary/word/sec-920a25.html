sec

1. NOUN

/ sɛk / (AmE), / sɛk / (BrE)
A second; the basic unit of time equal to 1/60 of a minute, recognized under the International System of Units (SI).
one second (unit of time)

Examples

He finished the race in just five sec.
Please wait a few sec before opening the file.
The timer was set for thirty sec to complete the task.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: second - the base SI unit of time equivalent to 1/60 of a minute

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'second', from Latin 'secunda' (pars minuta), meaning 'second (small part)'.

2. ADJ

/ sɛk / (AmE), / sɛk / (BrE)
(of champagne or other sparkling wine) moderately dry; less sweet than 'demi-sec' but sweeter than 'brut'.
moderately dry (of champagne)

Examples

This bottle of champagne is labeled as sec, so it should taste moderately dry.
If you prefer your sparkling wine less sweet, look for a sec on the menu.
The sommelier recommended a sec champagne to pair with our seafood platter.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: brut - very dry (of champagne)
  • Degree Lower: demi-sec - somewhat sweet (of champagne)

Etymology

From French 'sec' meaning 'dry'.

3. NOUN

/ sɛk / (AmE), / sɛk / (BrE)
the trigonometric function that gives the ratio of the hypotenuse to the adjacent side in a right-angled triangle; the secant
trigonometric function; secant

Examples

The sec of an angle is equal to one divided by the cosine of that angle.
In trigonometry, sec is often used alongside sine, cosine, and tangent.
To solve the problem, she calculated the sec of 45 degrees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secant - the trigonometric function representing the ratio of the hypotenuse to the adjacent side
  • Antonym: cos - the cosine function, reciprocal of secant

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'secant', from Latin 'secant-', present participle of 'secare' (to cut).
