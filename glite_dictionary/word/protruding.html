protruding

1. ADJ

/ proʊˈtruːdɪŋ / (AmE), / prəˈtruːdɪŋ / (BrE)
sticking out from a surface or boundary.
sticking out; projecting

Examples

The dog's protruding teeth made it look fierce.
A protruding branch nearly poked me as I walked by.
His protruding ribs were visible after weeks of sickness.

Synset Examples

the jutting limb of a tree
massive projected buttresses
his protruding ribs
a pile of boards sticking over the end of his truck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: projecting - extending outward beyond a surface
  • Antonym: recessed - set back or indented from a surface

Etymology

From Latin 'protrudere' (to thrust forward), via Latin 'protrudens, -entis' (pressing forward).
