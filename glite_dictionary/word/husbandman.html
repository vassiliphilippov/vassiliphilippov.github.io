husbandman

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌzbəndmən / (AmE), / ˈhʌzbəndmən / (BrE)
A man who cultivates and manages a farm; a farmer, especially in historical or literary contexts.
male farmer (historical/literary)
archaic

Examples

The husbandman rose at dawn to tend to his fields and livestock.
Medieval literature often describes the life of the humble husbandman.
As a skilled husbandman, he knew how to make the most of poor soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmer - a person who owns or operates a farm

Etymology

From Old English 'husbonda' (master of the house) and 'man'.
