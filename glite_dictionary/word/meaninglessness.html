meaninglessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiː.nɪŋ.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmiː.nɪŋ.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of lacking meaning, value, or significance.
lack of meaning, value, or significance

Examples

He felt overwhelmed by the meaninglessness of his daily routine.
The novel explores the meaninglessness that can arise in modern life.
Many people struggle with feelings of meaninglessness at some point in their lives.

Synset Examples

he resented the meaninglessness of the tasks they assigned him

Related Terms

  • Antonym: meaning - the thing or idea that a word, expression, or sign represents

2. NOUN

/ ˈminɪŋlɪsnəs / (AmE), / ˈmiːnɪŋlɪsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having no meaning or purpose
lack of meaning or purpose

Examples

The utter meaninglessness of his speech left everyone confused and bored.
She struggled with feelings of meaninglessness after losing her job.
Many critics commented on the meaninglessness of the abstract painting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: meaning - the thing or idea that a word, expression, or sign represents
  • Antonym: sense - a meaning or reason in language or action

Etymology

From 'meaningless' + '-ness', Middle English 'mēning' (meaning) + '-less' + '-ness.'
