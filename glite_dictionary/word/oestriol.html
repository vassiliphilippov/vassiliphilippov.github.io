oestriol

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːstriˈɒl / (AmE), / ˌiːstrɪˈɒl / (BrE)
A naturally occurring estrogenic hormone, with a synthetic form sometimes used to treat estrogen deficiency.
a type of estrogenic hormone
medical british

Examples

Levels of oestriol are often measured during pregnancy to assess fetal health.
The doctor prescribed a synthetic form of oestriol for her hormone therapy.
Research into oestriol has provided new insights into estrogen's role in the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estriol - an alternative spelling, primarily used in American English

Etymology

From Greek oistros (frenzy, gadfly) and -ol (chemical suffix for alcohols).
