promiscuous

1. ADJ

/ prəˈmɪskjuəs / (AmE), / prəˈmɪskjuːəs / (BrE)
having or involving many transient sexual relationships or partners without regard for selectivity or exclusiveness
having many sexual partners

Examples

She was unfairly labeled as promiscuous because of rumors about her dating life.
Some studies examine whether media influences promiscuous behavior among teenagers.
The novel depicts the promiscuous lifestyle of its main characters without moral judgment.

Synset Examples

her easy virtue
he was told to avoid loose (or light) women
wanton behavior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monogamous - having only one sexual partner at a time
  • Synonym: licentious - lacking legal or moral restraints, especially in sexual conduct

Etymology

From Latin 'promiscuus' (mixed, indiscriminate), from 'pro-' (forward) + 'miscere' (to mix).

2. ADJ

/ prəˈmɪskjuəs / (AmE), / prəˈmɪskjuːəs / (BrE)
not limited to one type or group; indiscriminate in choice or selection
indiscriminate; not selective

Examples

The committee's promiscuous approval of applications led to inconsistent project quality.
He has a promiscuous taste in music, enjoying everything from jazz to heavy metal and classical.
Critics argued that the company's promiscuous hiring practices reduced overall staff competence.

Synset Examples

Clinton was criticized for his promiscuous solicitation of campaign money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indiscriminate - not showing careful choice; done at random or without careful selection
  • Antonym: selective - tending to choose carefully or with discrimination

Etymology

From Latin 'promiscuus' (mixed, indiscriminate).
