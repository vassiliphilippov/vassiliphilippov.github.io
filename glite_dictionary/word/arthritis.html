arthritis

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈθraɪtɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈθraɪtɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition causing inflammation, pain, and stiffness in one or more joints.
inflammation of joints
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with arthritis after she complained of joint pain and swelling.
He takes medication daily to manage the symptoms of his arthritis.
Many elderly people suffer from arthritis, which can limit their mobility.

Etymology

From Greek 'arthron' (joint) + '-itis' (inflammation).
