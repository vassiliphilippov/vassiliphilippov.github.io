billion

1. NUM

/ ˈbɪljən / (AmE), / ˈbɪljən / (BrE)
The number one thousand million (1,000,000,000).
one thousand million (1,000,000,000)

Examples

The new stadium cost over a billion dollars to build.
There are more than seven billion people living on Earth.
A light-year is nearly six billion miles long.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: million - the number 1,000,000
  • Degree Higher: trillion - the number 1,000,000,000,000

Etymology

From French 'billion', from 'bi-' (two) + 'million'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈbɪljən / (AmE), / ˈbɪljən / (BrE)
An extremely large but unspecified number, used informally or as exaggeration rather than literally.
very large, indefinite number (hyperbolic)
informal

Examples

I must have told you a billion times to clean your room.
There were billions of stars visible that night.
He has billions of ideas for his next project.

Synset Examples

there were millions of flies

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: million - a very large indefinite number (used hyperbolically)

Etymology

From French 'billion', formed from 'bi-' (two) + '-illion' (as in 'million') originally meaning 10¹² in continental Europe or 10⁹ in English usage.

5. ADJ

/ ˈbɪljən / (AmE), / ˈbɪljən / (BrE)
(formerly in Britain) denoting a quantity consisting of one million million (1,000,000,000,000) items or units.
one million million; former British usage
archaic british

Examples

In reports from the early 20th century, a billion pounds meant one million million pounds in the UK.
Britain once used billion to represent a figure with twelve zeros, not just nine.
Historical documents may use billion in the older British sense, which can cause confusion today.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: billion - a thousand million; standard modern usage

Etymology

From French *billion*, formed from *bi-* (two) + *-illion* (as in million), originally denoting a million million.

  • Degree Lower: million - one thousand thousand (1,000,000)

From French 'billion', from 'bi-' (two) + 'million'; originally meaning a million million but now used for a thousand million in most English-speaking countries.
