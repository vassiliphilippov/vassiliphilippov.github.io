Megalonychidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˌloʊˈnɪkɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡələʊˈnɪkɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of mammals consisting of the two-toed sloths of Central and South America.
family of two-toed sloths

Examples

The family Megalonychidae includes all living two-toed sloths.
Researchers have studied the evolutionary history of Megalonychidae for insight into sloth adaptation.
Within Megalonychidae, species differ in their diet and habitat preferences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sloth - slow-moving, tree-dwelling mammal of Central and South America
  • Antonym: Bradypodidae - family of three-toed sloths

Etymology

From New Latin *Megalonychidae*, based on *Megalonyx* (genus of extinct ground sloths), from Greek *megas* 'great' + *onyx* 'claw'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˌlɒˈnɪkɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˌlɒnɪˈkiːdiː / (BrE)
a family of mammals comprising the two-toed sloths
family of two-toed sloths

Examples

The Megalonychidae family includes species commonly known as two-toed sloths.
Fossil evidence suggests that members of the Megalonychidae once lived in North America.
Biologists study the unique adaptations of Megalonychidae to understand arboreal lifestyles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sloth - slow-moving tree-dwelling mammal of Central and South America

Etymology

From Greek 'mega' (large) + 'onyx' (claw), referring to the sloths' large claws, combined with the taxonomic suffix '-idae' used for animal families.
