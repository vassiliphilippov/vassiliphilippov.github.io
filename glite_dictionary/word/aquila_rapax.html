Aquila rapax

1. NOUN

/ əˈkwɪlə ˈræpæks / (AmE), / əˈkwɪlə ˈræpaks / (BrE)
A medium-sized brownish eagle found in Africa and parts of Asia, commonly known as the tawny eagle.
tawny eagle species

Examples

The Aquila rapax soars high above the savanna in search of prey.
Many birdwatchers travel to Africa to observe the majestic Aquila rapax in its natural habitat.
Diet studies have shown that Aquila rapax is an opportunistic feeder, eating small mammals and carrion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tawny eagle - Common English name for Aquila rapax, a brownish eagle of Africa and Asia

Etymology

From Latin: 'Aquila' meaning 'eagle', 'rapax' meaning 'rapacious' or 'greedy'.
