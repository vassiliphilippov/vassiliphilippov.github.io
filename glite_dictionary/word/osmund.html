osmund

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑzmənd / (AmE), / ˈɒzmənd / (BrE)
Any fern of the genus Osmunda, characterized by large size, creeping rhizomes, and sporangia on modified fronds resembling flower clusters.
a genus of large ferns (Osmunda)

Examples

The royal osmund flourishes in damp woodland areas throughout the summer.
Gardeners prize the cinnamon osmund for its striking, plume-like fronds.
Some species of osmund are cultivated as ornamental plants in water gardens.

Etymology

From Latin Osmunda, an old genus name of uncertain origin, possibly after the Saxon god Osmund or a given name.
