HUJI

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪtʃ ˈjuː ˈdʒi ˈaɪ / (AmE), / ˈeɪtʃ ˈjuː ˈdʒiː ˈaɪ / (BrE)
an extremist militant group based in Pakistan-administered Kashmir that seeks to establish an Islamic government and has had close links with the Taliban in Afghanistan.
Islamist militant group in Pakistan-administered Kashmir

Examples

The Pakistani government has banned HUJI due to its involvement in militant activities.
Reports suggest that HUJI has maintained close ties with other extremist groups in the region.
Security forces arrested several members of HUJI near the border.

Etymology

Acronym of Harkat-ul-Jihad-al-Islami (Arabic: Movement of Islamic Jihad).
