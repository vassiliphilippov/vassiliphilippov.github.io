Zhou

1. PROPN

/ ʒoʊ / (AmE), / dʒəʊ / (BrE)
the imperial dynasty that ruled China from around 1122 to 221 BC, notable for the development of Confucianism and Taoism
imperial Chinese dynasty (1122–221 BC)

Examples

The Zhou dynasty is often credited with laying the foundation for Chinese philosophy and governance.
Many important philosophical works were written during the Zhou period.
The end of the Zhou dynasty marked the beginning of the Qin dynasty.

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese 周 (Zhōu), the name of the dynasty.
