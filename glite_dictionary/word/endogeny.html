endogeny

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈdɑːdʒəni / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɒdʒəni / (BrE)
A geological process or phenomenon that originates below the Earth's surface.
process beneath Earth's surface

Examples

Volcanic activity is a classic example of endogeny shaping the Earth's surface.
The study focused on the role of endogeny in mountain formation.
Processes such as faulting and folding are forms of endogeny that influence geology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exogeny - A geological phenomenon that originates outside or at the Earth's surface

Etymology

From Greek endon (within) + -geny (origin or production).
