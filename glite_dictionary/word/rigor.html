rigor

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡə / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely thorough, accurate, and strict in approach or judgment
thoroughness and strictness

Examples

The rigor of the scientific process helps ensure reliable results.
Her argument was praised for its intellectual rigor and clarity.
Academic work often demands a level of rigor that can be challenging for students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strictness - the quality of being strict or severe
  • Antonym: leniency - the quality of being more merciful or tolerant than expected

Etymology

From Latin rigor (stiffness, strictness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡə / (BrE)

strictness; harshness; severity

Examples

During the military academy, cadets were pushed to endure the rigor of constant drills and inspections.
Many critics questioned the rigor of the new workplace regulations.

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡə / (BrE)
a severe or harsh condition that is difficult to endure, especially relating to hardship or adversity
severe hardship or adversity

Examples

The explorers braved the rigors of the Arctic winter for months on end.
New recruits often underestimate the rigor of military training.
Doctors must endure the rigors of long, exhausting shifts in the emergency room.

Synset Examples

the asperity of northern winters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardship - severe suffering or privation
  • Antonym: ease - absence of difficulty or effort

Etymology

From Latin 'rigor' (stiffness, hardness, severity).
