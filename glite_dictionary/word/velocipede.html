velocipede

1. NOUN

/ vəˈlɑsəˌpid / (AmE), / vɪˈlɒsɪˌpiːd / (BrE)
an early type of bicycle with pedals attached to the front wheel
early bicycle with front-wheel pedals

Examples

In the museum, you can see a restored velocipede from the 1860s.
Riding a velocipede required balance and strong legs due to its heavy design.
The invention of the velocipede paved the way for modern bicycles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penny-farthing - a type of early bicycle with a large front wheel
  • Degree Higher: bicycle - a vehicle with two wheels, pedals, and handlebars

Etymology

From French 'vélocipède', from Latin 'vēlōx' (swift) + 'pedis' (foot).

2. NOUN

/ vəˈlɑsəˌpid / (AmE), / vɪˈlɒsɪˌpiːd / (BrE)
an early type of vehicle with two or three wheels, propelled by the rider's feet on pedals
early pedal-powered vehicle with wheels

Examples

In the museum, you can see a restored velocipede from the 1800s.
She rode her antique velocipede down the quiet lane, turning heads as she passed.
The child's toy was modeled after a classic velocipede with three wheels and bright red paint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bicycle - a vehicle with two wheels, propelled by pedals

Etymology

From French vélocipède, based on Latin velox (swift) + pes, ped- (foot).
