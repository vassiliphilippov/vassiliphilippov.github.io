prodigally

1. ADV

/ ˈprɑːdɪɡəli / (AmE), / ˈprɒdɪɡəli / (BrE)
In a wasteful or extravagant manner; spending or using resources freely and recklessly.
in a wasteful or extravagant way

Examples

Despite earning a modest salary, he spent his money prodigally on luxury goods.
The land was prodigally fertile, yielding abundant crops each season.
They gave prodigally to charity, showing little concern for their own savings.

Synset Examples

we are still prodigally rich compared to others

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavishly - in a very generous or extravagant manner
  • Antonym: thriftily - in a frugal or economical manner

Etymology

From prodigal + -ly; prodigal from Latin prodigus (‘lavish’).
