aneuploid

1. ADJ

/ ˈæn.juˌplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈanjʊˌplɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having a chromosome number that is not an exact multiple of the haploid number, resulting in a genetic abnormality.
having an abnormal chromosome number
medical

Examples

The scientist discovered that the sample contained aneuploid cells.
Certain types of cancer are characterized by aneuploid chromosome numbers.
Researchers are studying how aneuploid conditions can affect cell function.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: euploid - having a chromosome number that is an exact multiple of the haploid number

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (not) + 'eu' (good) + 'ploos' (fold, referring to chromosomes).
