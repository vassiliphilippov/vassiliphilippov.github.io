erstwhile

1. ADV

/ ˈɜrstwaɪl / (AmE), / ˈɜːstwaɪl / (BrE)
In the past; formerly.
formerly; in the past

Examples

The erstwhile CEO returned to speak at the company's anniversary celebration.
Many erstwhile allies have now distanced themselves from the project.
The two nations, erstwhile enemies, forged a lasting peace.

Synset Examples

at one time he loved her
her erstwhile writing
she was a dancer once

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formerly - at a previous time; in the past
  • Antonym: currently - at the present time; now

Etymology

From Old English 'ǣrest' (first) + 'while' (time period).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɜrstˌwaɪl / (AmE), / ˈɜːstˌwaɪl / (BrE)
former; belonging to an earlier time; previous.
former; previous

Examples

His erstwhile colleagues barely recognized him at the reunion.
The castle was the erstwhile residence of the royal family.
Several erstwhile rivals have now joined forces for a common cause.

Synset Examples

erstwhile friend
our former glory
the once capital of the state
her quondam lover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: former - previous or earlier in time
  • Antonym: current - belonging to the present time

Etymology

From Old English ǣrest (earliest) + while (time).
