halogeton

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæl.əˈdʒiː.tɑn / (AmE), / ˌhal.əˈdʒiː.tɒn / (BrE)
a coarse, annual herb (Halogeton glomeratus) native to Eurasia and introduced into North America, known for its high oxalate content and toxicity to livestock such as sheep and cattle.
toxic annual herb harmful to livestock

Examples

Halogeton rapidly invaded large areas of western U.S. rangelands, posing a threat to grazing animals.
Sheep are particularly vulnerable to poisoning if they consume halogeton while grazing.
Efforts to control halogeton help protect cattle and sheep from toxic exposure.

Etymology

From New Latin Halogeton, from Greek halos (salt) + geitōn (neighbor), referring to its habitat in saline soils.
