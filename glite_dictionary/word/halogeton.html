Halogeton

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæləˈdʒiːtən / (AmE), / ˌhæl.əˈdʒiː.tɒn / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the family Amaranthaceae, known for species growing in saline or alkaline soils.
genus of salt-tolerant plants

Examples

Halogeton species are well adapted to survive in areas with high soil salinity.
Invasive Halogeton has been a problem for rangeland management in certain regions.
A botanist collected samples of Halogeton while studying salt desert flora.

Etymology

From Greek 'hals' (salt) and 'geiton' (neighbor), referring to the plant's affinity for saline soils.
