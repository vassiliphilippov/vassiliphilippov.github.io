evenhanded

1. ADJ

/ ˌivənˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌiːvənˈhændɪd / (BrE)
dealing fairly and without showing favoritism or bias; impartial.
fair, impartial, unbiased

Examples

The judge was known for her evenhanded treatment of all parties in the courtroom.
They tried to be evenhanded in their evaluation, considering both sides carefully.
His evenhanded approach helped resolve the dispute peacefully.

Synset Examples

evenhanded justice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impartial - treating all rivals or disputants equally; fair and just
  • Antonym: biased - unfairly prejudiced for or against someone or something

Etymology

Formed from 'even' (equal or balanced) and 'handed' (having a hand or manner), literally meaning 'with equal hand'.
