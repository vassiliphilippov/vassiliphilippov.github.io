dread

1. VERB

/ drɛd / (AmE), / drɛd / (BrE)
to feel great fear or apprehension about something
to fear or be deeply afraid of

Examples

Many students dread taking final exams at the end of the semester.
She dreaded the thought of moving to a new city alone.
I always dread going to the dentist, even for a simple checkup.

Synset Examples

I fear the winters in Moscow
We should not fear the Communists!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fear - to be afraid of something or someone
  • Antonym: to anticipate - to expect or look forward to (normally with a positive attitude, but can be negative)

Etymology

From Old English 'drǣdan', related to Old Norse 'drātha' (to fear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01784021-v
Synset POS: v

2. ADJ

/ drɛd / (AmE), / drɛd / (BrE)
Causing great fear, anxiety, or awe; inspiring dread.
causing fear or great anxiety

Examples

The children stood in silence before the dread presence of the principal.
Polio was once considered a dread disease, but is now largely eradicated.
The villagers spoke of a dread curse that haunted the land for generations.

Synset Examples

the awful war
an awful risk
dire news
a career or vengeance so direful that London was shocked
the dread presence of the headmaster
polio is no longer the dreaded disease it once was
a dreadful storm
a fearful howling
horrendous explosions shook the city
a terrible curse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dreadful - causing great fear or suffering
  • Synonym: terrible - of great fear or severity; causing terror
  • Antonym: comforting - causing comfort, easing fear or anxiety

Etymology

From Old English 'drēadan' (to fear, be anxious).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00195400-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ drɛd / (AmE), / drɛd / (BrE)
A strong feeling of fear or worry about something that may happen.
fearful expectation or anticipation

Examples

The thought of speaking in public filled her with dread.
As the deadline approached, he felt a rising sense of dread about the exam.
There was a feeling of dread in the air before the storm hit.

Synset Examples

the student looked around the examination room with apprehension

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fear - an unpleasant emotion caused by the belief that someone or something is dangerous
  • Synonym: anticipation - the act of expecting or predicting something, especially with anxiety
  • Antonym: hope - a feeling of expectation and desire for a certain thing to happen

Etymology

From Old English 'drēadan', meaning 'to fear' or 'to dread'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07536913-n
Synset POS: n
