Heterodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛt̬.əˌrɑːd.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈhɛt.ə.rəʊ.dɒn / (BrE)
A genus of small colubrid snakes native to North America, commonly known as hognose snakes.
genus of North American hognose snakes

Examples

The Heterodon genus includes several species of hognose snakes found in North America.
Researchers studied the behavior of Heterodon in various habitats across the continent.
The upturned snout is a distinctive feature of snakes in the Heterodon genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'heteros' (different) and 'odous, odont-' (tooth), referring to their distinctive teeth.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛt̬əˌrɑn / (AmE), / ˈhɛtərəˌdɒn / (BrE)
A genus of small colubrid snakes native to North America, commonly called hognose snakes.
a genus of North American hognose snakes

Examples

The Heterodon genus is easily recognized by the distinctive upturned snout of its members.
Several species within Heterodon are known for their dramatic bluffing behaviors when threatened.
Researchers observed a Heterodon digging in sandy soil, searching for prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hognose snake - a common name for snakes of the genus Heterodon, with upturned snouts

Etymology

From Greek heteros (different) + odon (tooth), referring to the snakes' distinctive teeth.
