aether

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːθər / (AmE), / ˈiːθə / (BrE)
a substance once believed to fill all space and carry light or electromagnetic waves; now discarded in modern physics
hypothetical substance filling space (obsolete physics)
archaic

Examples

In the 19th century, scientists believed the aether was necessary for the transmission of light.
The theory of relativity made the concept of aether obsolete in modern physics.
Many early experiments tried to detect the aether, but ultimately found no evidence for its existence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ether - alternative spelling and synonym for 'aether'

Etymology

From Greek 'aithēr' (upper air, pure fresh air, sky).
