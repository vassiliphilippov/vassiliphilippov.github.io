cenobite

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnəˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsiːnəbaɪt / (BrE)
a member of a religious order who lives in community rather than in solitude
member of communal religious order

Examples

The monastery was founded for cenobites who wished to worship together.
Unlike hermits, cenobites dedicate their lives to communal religious practice.
Many medieval cenobites contributed to the preservation of ancient texts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anchorite - a religious recluse or hermit
  • Synonym: monk - a member of a male religious community

Etymology

From Late Latin cenobita, from Ancient Greek koinobion (community life).
