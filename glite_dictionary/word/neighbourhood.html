neighbourhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪbɚˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈneɪbəhʊd / (BrE)
The people living in a particular area, considered collectively as a community.
community of people living nearby
british

Examples

The neighbourhood gathered for a summer street party last weekend.
Many children in the neighbourhood play together after school every day.
She enjoys volunteering to help improve her neighbourhood.

Synset Examples

it is a friendly neighborhood
my neighborhood voted for Bush

Related Terms

  • Synonym: community - a group of people living in the same place
  • Synonym: neighborhood - American spelling of 'neighbourhood'

Etymology

From neighbour + -hood, after Middle English neghburhod.

2. NOUN

/ ˈneɪbɚˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈneɪbəˌhʊd / (BrE)
A district or area surrounding a particular place or person, especially as regarded in relation to its inhabitants.
local area or district
british

Examples

She grew up in a close-knit neighbourhood where everyone knew each other.
The new park became a popular spot for children in the neighbourhood.
There has been a rise in property values throughout the neighbourhood over the past year.

Synset Examples

the plane crashed in the vicinity of Asheville
it is a rugged locality
he always blames someone else in the immediate neighborhood
I will drop in on you the next time I am in this neck of the woods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vicinity - the area near or surrounding a particular place
  • Synonym: locality - a particular neighborhood, place, or district

Etymology

From Middle English 'neigheborhed', from 'neighbour' + '-hood'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08658688-n
Synset POS: n
