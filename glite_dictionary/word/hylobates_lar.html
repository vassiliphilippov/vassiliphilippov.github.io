Hylobates lar

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈloʊbətiːz lɑr / (AmE), / hɪˈlɒbəteɪz lɑː / (BrE)
A species of gibbon, known as the white-handed gibbon, found in southern Asia and the East Indies, notable for its long arms and lack of tail.
species of gibbon (white-handed gibbon)

Examples

The Hylobates lar is commonly seen swinging through the canopies of Southeast Asian forests.
Researchers studied the vocalizations of the Hylobates lar to understand their social behavior.
Unlike many other primates, the Hylobates lar forms lifelong monogamous pairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gibbon - a small, slender, long-armed arboreal ape

Etymology

From New Latin, genus Hylobates (from Greek hylē 'wood' + batēs 'walker') and species name lar.
