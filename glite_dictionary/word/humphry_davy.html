Humphry Davy

1. PROPN

/ ˈhʌmfri ˈdeɪvi / (AmE), / ˈhʌmfri ˈdeɪvi / (BrE)
(1778–1829) English chemist who pioneered electrochemistry and isolated several chemical elements, including sodium, potassium, and chlorine.
English chemist, pioneer of electrochemistry

Examples

Humphry Davy discovered several new elements using electrolysis, greatly advancing chemical science.
The safety lamp invented by Humphry Davy saved countless miners from dangerous explosions.
Many modern chemists credit Humphry Davy as a founder of electrochemistry.
