flexion

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of bending a joint, especially so that the angle between the bones of a limb is decreased
bending of a joint
medical

Examples

The physiotherapist measured the flexion of her knee after the surgery.
Reduced flexion in the fingers can indicate arthritis.
During yoga, deep flexion of the spine should be performed carefully to avoid injury.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extension - the act of straightening or increasing the angle at a joint

Etymology

From Latin 'flexio', from 'flectere' (to bend).

2. NOUN

/ ˈflɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈflɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or state of bending a limb or joint, or the resulting position in which it is bent
bending of a limb or joint
medical

Examples

The doctor measured the patient's knee flexion after surgery.
Proper flexion of the elbow is essential for lifting weights safely.
He experienced pain during flexion of his wrist.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extension - the act of straightening a limb or joint

Etymology

From Latin flexio, flexionis (a bending), from flectere (to bend).

3. NOUN

/ ˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the action of bending or the condition of being bent, especially a limb or joint; deviation from a straight or normal course
bending of a limb or joint
medical

Examples

The physiotherapist measured the flexion of the patient's elbow during the exercise.
Limited flexion in the knee can make walking or climbing stairs difficult.
In yoga, certain poses require deep flexion of the spine and hips.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extension - the straightening movement that increases the angle between body parts

Etymology

From Latin 'flexio' (a bending), from 'flectere' (to bend).
