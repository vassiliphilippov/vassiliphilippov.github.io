downtime

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.taɪm / (BrE)
A period when a machine, system, or facility is not functioning, especially due to technical problems or maintenance.
period when something is not operating

Examples

The factory experienced several hours of downtime after the power outage.
Unexpected downtime can cost companies thousands of dollars in lost productivity.
They scheduled routine maintenance to minimize downtime during working hours.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uptime - period when a machine or system is operating normally

Etymology

From 'down' (not functioning) + 'time' (period).
