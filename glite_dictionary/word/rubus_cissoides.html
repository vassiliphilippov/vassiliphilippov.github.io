Rubus cissoides

1. NOUN

/ ˈruː.bəs sɪˈsɔɪ.diːz / (AmE), / ˈruː.bəs sɪˈsɔɪ.diːz / (BrE)
a stout-stemmed, trailing shrub native to New Zealand, known for scrambling over other vegetation
a New Zealand scrambling shrub species

Examples

The dense growth of Rubus cissoides can provide shelter for native birds in New Zealand forests.
Scientists studied how Rubus cissoides competes with other plants by rapidly covering available space.
Gardeners often remove Rubus cissoides to prevent it from overtaking ornamental shrubs.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Rubus' (bramble) and 'cissoides' (resembling ivy), referring to its vine-like habit.
