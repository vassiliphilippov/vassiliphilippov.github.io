biddable

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɪdəbəl / (AmE), / ˈbɪdəb(ə)l / (BrE)
(of a person) willing to do what they are told; easily influenced or controlled.
willing to obey; easily led

Examples

The puppy was so biddable that it learned new commands quickly.
Her biddable nature made her popular with teachers and supervisors.
He found his new assistant refreshingly biddable compared to his last one.

Synset Examples

too acquiescent to challenge authority
a gentle, biddable soul

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compliant - willing to comply with orders or requests
  • Antonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or actions

Etymology

From Middle English 'bidden' (to ask, command) + -able.
