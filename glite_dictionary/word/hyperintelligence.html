hyperintelligence

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpərɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
An exceptionally high degree of intelligence, surpassing the level of a genius.
exceptionally high intelligence

Examples

The main character's hyperintelligence enables her to solve complex problems in minutes.
Scientists debate whether hyperintelligence could ever be achieved by artificial systems.
His hyperintelligence made social interactions challenging, as others struggled to keep up.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: genius - exceptional natural intellectual ability or creativity

Etymology

From hyper- (meaning 'above, beyond') + intelligence.
