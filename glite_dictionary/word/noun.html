1. NOUN

/ naʊn / (AmE), / naʊn / (BrE)
A word that refers to a person, place, thing, quality, or action, and can function as the subject or object in a sentence.
a word for a person, place, or thing

Examples

Please underline every noun in the following sentence.
'Happiness' is an abstract noun that refers to a feeling.
The teacher explained that a noun can represent a person, place, or thing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: verb - a word that describes an action, state, or occurrence

Etymology

From Latin 'nomen' (name), via Old French 'nom'.

2. NOUN

/ naʊn / (AmE), / naʊn / (BrE)
A word (such as 'cat', 'table', or 'happiness') that refers to a person, place, thing, quality, or idea and can function as the subject or object of a verb, the object of a preposition, or in apposition.
a word for a person, place, or thing

Examples

Every sentence must contain at least one noun to make sense.
The word 'dog' is a common noun that refers to an animal.
She taught her students how to identify a noun in a sentence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: verb - a word that expresses an action, state, or occurrence
  • Synonym: pronoun - a word that can substitute for a noun

Etymology

From Latin nomen (name).
