impeachment

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpitʃmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpiːtʃmənt / (BrE)
The process or act of formally accusing a public official of misconduct in office, especially in order to remove them from their position.
formal accusation of public official
legal

Examples

The House of Representatives initiated impeachment proceedings against the president.
After the impeachment trial, the official was acquitted and remained in office.
The threat of impeachment serves as a check on the abuse of executive power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indictment - a formal charge or accusation of a serious crime

Etymology

From Old French *empechement*, from *empecher* (impede), ultimately from Latin *impedicare* (to entangle, impede).
