Ceratopogon

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛrətoʊˈpoʊɡən / (AmE), / ˌsɛrətəˈpəʊɡɒn / (BrE)
A genus of small biting midges in the family Ceratopogonidae, known for their biting habits and wide distribution.
genus of biting midges (insects)

Examples

The larvae of Ceratopogon are commonly found in moist soil and aquatic habitats.
Several species of Ceratopogon are notorious for biting humans and animals.
Researchers study Ceratopogon to understand the transmission of certain animal diseases.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Ceratopogonidae - family of biting midges including Ceratopogon

Etymology

From Greek keras (horn) + pous (foot), referring to horn-like projections on the legs of some species.
