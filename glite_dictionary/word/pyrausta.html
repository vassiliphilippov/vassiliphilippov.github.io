Pyrausta

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈrɔstə / (AmE), / pɪˈrɔːstə / (BrE)
A genus of moths whose larvae are known as corn borers.
genus of moths (corn borers)

Examples

The larvae of the Pyrausta moth are notorious corn borers in many agricultural regions.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Pyrausta to develop better pest management strategies.
Farmers often monitor fields for signs of Pyrausta infestation to protect their crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moth - an insect related to butterflies, generally nocturnal

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name, based on Greek pur (fire) and austomai (to burn).

2. NOUN

/ paɪˈrɔstə / (AmE), / pɪˈraʊstə / (BrE)
A genus of moths whose larvae are known as corn borers, some species of which are agricultural pests.
a genus of moths, corn borer larvae

Examples

The larvae of Pyrausta frequently bore into corn stalks, causing significant crop damage.
Entomologists studied specimens from the genus Pyrausta to understand their life cycles.
Several species within Pyrausta are considered agricultural pests due to their destructive larvae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moth - an insect related to butterflies, typically nocturnal
  • Synonym: corn borer - larva that bores into corn stems and cobs

Etymology

From New Latin Pyrausta, the genus name given by naturalists.
