turntable

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrnˌteɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnˌteɪbəl / (BrE)
a circular, rotating tray placed on a dining table to make food or condiments easily accessible to everyone
revolving tray for dining table

Examples

The guests took turns spinning the turntable to serve themselves.
Please place the salad on the turntable so everyone can reach it.
A glass turntable in the center of the table made sharing dishes easy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnˌteɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnˌteɪbəl / (BrE)
A circular, rotating platform with tracks, used to turn locomotives or railway cars.
platform for turning locomotives or cars

Examples

The old steam engine was driven onto the turntable so it could be rotated for the return journey.
Most roundhouses are built adjacent to a central turntable for turning locomotives.
Technicians carefully aligned the tracks before rotating the turntable to the desired direction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolving stage - a circular stage that rotates, similar in mechanism

Etymology

From 'turn' + 'table', indicating its function as a table (platform) that turns.

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnˌteɪbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnˌteɪbl̩ / (BrE)
a circular horizontal platform that rotates a phonograph record while it is being played, allowing the record to be heard
rotating platform for playing records

Examples

He placed the vinyl record gently on the turntable before lowering the needle.
Many DJs use a turntable to mix and scratch music at parties.
My grandparents still listen to music on their old turntable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record player - a machine for playing phonograph records

Etymology

Formed from 'turn' + 'table', describing its function as a rotating table.
