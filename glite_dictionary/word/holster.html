holster

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊl.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɒl.stə / (BrE)
a belt or holder with loops or slots for carrying small hand tools, such as wrenches or screwdrivers
holder or belt for carrying tools

Examples

The electrician wore a holster full of screwdrivers and pliers while working on the wiring.
She reached into her tool holster for a wrench to tighten the bolt.
His holster kept all his essential tools within easy reach during the repairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tool belt - a belt worn to hold tools

Etymology

Probably from Dutch *holster* (case for a pistol), related to *holen* (to fetch).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊl.stər / (AmE), / ˈhɒl.stə / (BrE)
a leather or fabric case for carrying a handgun, typically worn on a belt or shoulder strap
case for carrying a handgun

Examples

The officer drew his pistol from the holster on his hip.
She made sure her revolver was secure in its holster before leaving the house.
Leather holsters are popular among gun enthusiasts for their durability and style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheath - a close-fitting cover for a blade or tool

Etymology

From Dutch 'holster' (pistol case).
