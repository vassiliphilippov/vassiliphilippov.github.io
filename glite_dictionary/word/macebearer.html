macebearer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪsˌbɛrər / (AmE), / ˈmeɪsbɛərə / (BrE)
an official who carries a ceremonial mace as a symbol of authority, especially in formal processions or ceremonies
official carrying a ceremonial mace

Examples

At the university graduation, the macebearer led the academic procession.
The macebearer wore traditional robes as he walked ahead of the mayor.
During the ceremony, the macebearer carried the ornate staff with solemn dignity.

Etymology

From 'mace' (a ceremonial staff) + 'bearer' (one who carries something).
