polished

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑlɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪʃt / (BrE)
shiny and smooth from being rubbed, cleaned, or perfected; refined or accomplished in appearance, style, or manner
shiny, smooth, or refined

Examples

Her polished performance impressed both the audience and the judges.
He wore a suit with polished shoes to the interview.
The table looked brand new after it was polished.

Synset Examples

his polished prose
in a freshly ironed dress and polished shoes
freshly polished silver

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refined - elegantly improved, free from roughness
  • Antonym: rough - having an uneven or unrefined surface, not smooth

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'polish,' from Middle French 'polir', from Latin 'polire' (to smooth, polish).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːlɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪʃt / (BrE)
showing refinement, elegance, and confidence, especially as a result of wide social experience
refined and elegant, socially assured

Examples

Her polished presentation impressed everyone at the meeting.
He greeted his guests with a polished charm that made them feel at ease.
The diplomat's polished manners were evident at every event he attended.

Synset Examples

his polished manner
maintained an urbane tone in his letters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urbane - having refined manners and suave sophistication
  • Synonym: sophisticated - having worldly knowledge and experienced refinement
  • Antonym: crude - lacking culture, refinement, or social polish

3. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːlɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪʃt / (BrE)
(of wood, stone, or other surfaces) trimmed and made smooth, often by rubbing or chemical treatment.
trimmed and smoothed (wood/stone)

Examples

The polished marble floor gleamed under the hallway lights.
Each polished plank of wood was carefully placed to form the new table.
They admired the polished granite countertop after the renovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smooth - having an even and flat surface; not rough

Etymology

From Polish (verb), from Middle French polir, from Latin polire (to smooth, polish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01008895-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːlɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪʃt / (BrE)
(of grains, especially rice) having the husk or outer layers removed, typically resulting in a smooth, white appearance.
grains with outer layers removed

Examples

Many people prefer polished rice for its smooth texture and mild flavor.
After harvesting, the grain is often polished to remove the bran and germ layers.
Health experts sometimes recommend choosing unpolished over polished grains for more fiber.

Synset Examples

polished rice

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpolished - (of grains) with the outer layers left intact

Etymology

From the verb 'polish,' from Middle French 'polir,' from Latin 'polire' (to smooth, to polish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01958975-s
Synset POS: s
