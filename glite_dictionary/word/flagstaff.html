flagstaff

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæɡ.stæf / (AmE), / ˈflæɡ.stɑːf / (BrE)
a tall pole on which a flag is raised or displayed
pole for displaying a flag

Examples

The national flag was hoisted on the flagstaff outside city hall.
A strong wind caused the flagstaff to sway dangerously during the storm.
The old wooden flagstaff was replaced with a steel one to support a larger flag.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flagpole - a pole on which a flag is raised

Etymology

From 'flag' + 'staff', originally meaning a staff for a flag.
