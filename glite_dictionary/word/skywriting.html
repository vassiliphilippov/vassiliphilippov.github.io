skywriting

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaɪˌraɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌraɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
writing or drawings formed in the sky by smoke released from an aircraft, typically for advertising or entertainment purposes.
writing in the sky made by smoke from an airplane

Examples

The marriage proposal was spelled out in skywriting for everyone to see.
Modern technology has almost replaced traditional skywriting advertisements.
Children gazed up in wonder as the airplane traced skywriting across the blue afternoon sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerial advertising - the use of aircraft to promote products or convey messages

Etymology

Formed from 'sky' + 'writing', first recorded in the 1920s with the advent of aviation shows.
