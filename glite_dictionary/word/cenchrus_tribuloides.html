Cenchrus tribuloides

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.krəs trɪˌbjuː.lɔɪˈdiːz / (AmE), / ˈsɛŋ.krəs trɪˌbjuː.lɔɪˈdiːz / (BrE)
A grass native to the eastern United States and tropical America, known for its spikelets enclosed in prickly burs.
prickly grass of eastern US and tropical America

Examples

Farmers often struggle to remove Cenchrus tribuloides from their fields due to its sharp burs.
Children sometimes get Cenchrus tribuloides burs stuck to their shoes while playing outdoors.
The spread of Cenchrus tribuloides can impact native plant communities in sandy soils.

Etymology

From genus name Cenchrus (Greek 'kenchros', a kind of millet) and specific epithet tribuloides (Latin 'Tribulus'-like, after a kind of spiny plant).
