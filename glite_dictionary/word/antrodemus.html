Antrodemus

1. PROPN

/ ˌæntrəˈdiːməs / (AmE), / ˌæntrəˈdiːməs / (BrE)
a genus of carnivorous dinosaurs from North America, known from the Late Jurassic period; now usually regarded as synonymous with Allosaurus
genus of carnivorous Jurassic dinosaur

Examples

The fossil remains of Antrodemus were discovered in Colorado in the 19th century.
For many years, paleontologists debated whether Antrodemus was distinct from Allosaurus.
The classification of Antrodemus has influenced our understanding of Jurassic theropods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Allosaurus - a large carnivorous dinosaur from the Late Jurassic period

Etymology

From Greek antro- (cavity) and demas (body), referring to a feature of the vertebrae.
