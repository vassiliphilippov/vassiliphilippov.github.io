binturong

1. NOUN

/ bɪnˈtʊrɒŋ / (AmE), / bɪnˈtʊərɒŋ / (BrE)
a large, arboreal civet (Arctictis binturong) of Southeast Asia with shaggy black fur and a long prehensile tail
large arboreal Asian civet; bearcat

Examples

The binturong spends most of its time high in the forest canopy.
A binturong can use its prehensile tail to grip branches like a fifth limb.
Visitors to the wildlife sanctuary were excited to see a rare binturong up close.

Etymology

From Malay 'binturong', the native name for the animal.

2. NOUN

/ bɪnˈtʊr.ɒŋ / (AmE), / bɪnˈtʊər.ɒŋ / (BrE)
A large, arboreal civet native to Southeast Asia, with shaggy black fur and a long, prehensile tail.
arboreal civet of Southeast Asia

Examples

The binturong spends most of its time in the trees of Southeast Asian forests.
A distinctive feature of the binturong is its prehensile tail, which helps it climb branches.
Many zoos work to educate the public about the endangered status of the binturong.

Etymology

From Malay 'binturong'.
