to authenticate

1. VERB

/ əˈθɛntɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɛntɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to prove that something is genuine or real, especially by examining or testing it
prove something is genuine

Examples

The museum hired experts to authenticate the ancient manuscript before displaying it.
You must authenticate your identity to access the secure website.
The jeweler was asked to authenticate the diamond ring for the insurance claim.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to verify - to make sure or demonstrate that something is true or accurate
  • Antonym: to falsify - to make something false by altering it deceitfully

Etymology

From Medieval Latin authenticatus, from Greek authentikos (genuine, original).
