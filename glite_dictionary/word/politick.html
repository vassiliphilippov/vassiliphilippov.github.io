to politick

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑlɪˌtɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌtɪk / (BrE)
to engage in political activity, often for personal or party advantage
engage in political activity

Examples

He tends to politick during every office meeting, seeking influence and favor.
Several candidates were busy politicking at the local fair to attract new supporters.
Instead of working together, they chose to politick and undermine each other's proposals.

Synset Examples

This colleague is always politicking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaign - to take part in organized political activities
  • Synonym: politics - the activities associated with the governance of a country or area

Etymology

Back-formation from 'politics', patterned after verbs like 'frolic'.
