moulting

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊltɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈməʊltɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which arthropods shed their cuticle or reptiles shed their outer skin, typically as part of growth or development
periodic shedding of skin or outer layer

Examples

The snake began moulting after outgrowing its old skin.
Many insects go through several phases of moulting before reaching adulthood.
During moulting, birds often lose many feathers and temporarily look scruffy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecdysis - the process of shedding the old skin in reptiles and certain other animals

Etymology

From 'moult', from Middle English 'mouten', from Old French 'moter' (to shed)
