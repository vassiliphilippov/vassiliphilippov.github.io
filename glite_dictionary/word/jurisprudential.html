jurisprudential

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒʊrəspruːˈdɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌdʒʊərəspɹuːˈdɛnʃəl / (BrE)
Relating to the theory, philosophy, or science of law or a system of laws.
relating to the philosophy of law
legal

Examples

The court’s decision was based on jurisprudential principles established over centuries.
Her research explores the jurisprudential differences between common law and civil law systems.
There is an ongoing jurisprudential debate about the interpretation of constitutional rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legal - relating to the law or the legal profession

Etymology

From jurisprudence, from Latin 'jurisprudentia' (knowledge of law), plus the suffix '-al'.
