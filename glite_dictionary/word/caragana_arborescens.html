Caragana arborescens

1. NOUN

/ ˌkærəˈɡænə ɑːrbəˈrɛsənz / (AmE), / ˌkarəˈɡænə ɑːbəˈrɛsɛnz / (BrE)
a large spiny shrub native to eastern Asia, with clusters of yellow flowers, often grown for shelterbelts and hedges
spiny shrub of eastern Asia; yellow flowers

Examples

Caragana arborescens is commonly planted as a windbreak on farms in cold climates.
Gardeners appreciate Caragana arborescens for its resilience and bright yellow spring flowers.
The hedge of Caragana arborescens provides excellent shelter for birds and pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siberian pea shrub - common name for Caragana arborescens

Etymology

From the genus name Caragana (of Turkic origin) and the Latin 'arborescens' meaning 'becoming tree-like'.
