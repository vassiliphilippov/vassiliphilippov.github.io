Montia

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑntiə / (AmE), / ˈmɒntɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of densely tufted annual herbs found in north temperate regions, South America, tropical Africa, and Asia.
genus of annual tufted herbs

Examples

Several species in the Montia genus are found in alpine meadows across the northern hemisphere.
The botanist identified the tiny flowering plant as belonging to Montia.
Recent studies have revealed new distributions of Montia species in tropical Asia.

Etymology

Named after Giuseppe Monti, Italian botanist (1682–1760).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɒnti.ə / (AmE), / ˈmɒnti.ə / (BrE)
A small genus of densely tufted annual herbs found in north temperate regions, as well as South America, tropical Africa, and Asia.
a genus of small annual herbs

Examples

Several species within the Montia genus are known for their succulent leaves and small pink flowers.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Montia thriving in the alpine meadows.
Gardeners often cultivate Montia as a ground cover because of its dense, tufted growth.

Etymology

Named after Italian botanist Giuseppe Monti (1682–1760).
