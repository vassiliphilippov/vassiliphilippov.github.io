tailgater

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈteɪlˌɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a driver who follows another vehicle too closely, especially in a way that is considered dangerous or aggressive
driver who follows too closely

Examples

I had to switch lanes because a tailgater was making me nervous.
Police issued several tickets to tailgaters on the highway last weekend.
If you encounter a tailgater, it is safest to let them pass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tailgate - to drive closely behind another vehicle

Etymology

From 'tailgate' (to drive closely behind another vehicle) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
