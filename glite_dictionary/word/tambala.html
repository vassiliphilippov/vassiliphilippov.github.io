tambala

1. NOUN

/ tæmˈbɑːlə / (AmE), / tæmˈbɑːlə / (BrE)
a monetary unit of Malawi, equal to one hundredth of a kwacha
Malawian monetary unit (1/100 kwacha)

Examples

Each tambala is worth one hundredth of a Malawian kwacha.
She found several old tambalas at the bottom of her drawer.
You will need at least fifty tambala to make this purchase.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: kwacha - the basic monetary unit of Malawi

Etymology

From the Chewa word 'tambala' meaning 'cock' or 'rooster'.
