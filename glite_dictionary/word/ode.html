ode

1. NOUN

/ oʊd / (AmE), / əʊd / (BrE)
a lyric poem, typically addressed to a particular subject, with intricate or elaborate stanza structure
a lyric poem with complex stanza forms

Examples

Keats wrote an ode to a nightingale that became one of his best-known works.
The poet composed an ode celebrating the beauty of the changing seasons.
Her latest ode explored themes of love and loss with intricate stanzas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poem - a piece of writing in verse form

Etymology

From Greek ōdē (song, poem), via Latin oda.
