amphigory

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.fɪˌɡɔɹ.i / (AmE), / ˈam.fɪˌɡɒɹ.i / (BrE)
A piece of writing, especially verse, that is nonsensical or meaningless.
nonsensical or meaningless verse

Examples

The poet delighted in composing amphigory to amuse his friends at parties.
Many children's books include playful amphigory that entertains young readers.
Her latest collection is a mix of satire and amphigory that defies serious analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense verse - verse consisting of meaningless or absurd language
  • Synonym: doggerel - comic verse composed in irregular rhythm, often trivial or bad

Etymology

From French 'amphigouri', of uncertain origin.
