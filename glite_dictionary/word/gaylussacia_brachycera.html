Gaylussacia brachycera

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪlʌˈseɪʃə ˌbrækiˈsɪrə / (AmE), / ɡeɪlʌˈseɪsiə ˌbrækiˈsɪərə / (BrE)
A creeping evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States, noted for its small shiny, boxlike leaves and flavorless berries.
creeping shrub of southeastern U.S.

Examples

The Gaylussacia brachycera thrives in the understory of southeastern pine forests.
Botanists often study Gaylussacia brachycera for its unique leaf structure.
Unlike some related species, Gaylussacia brachycera produces berries that are nearly flavorless.

Etymology

Named after French chemist Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, combined with Greek roots for 'short' and 'horn'.
