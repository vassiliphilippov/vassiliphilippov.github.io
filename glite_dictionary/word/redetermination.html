redetermination

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.dɪˌtɝː.məˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.dɪˌtɜː.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of determining something again or anew, especially after a review or reconsideration
act of determining again

Examples

After reviewing the new evidence, a redetermination of the case was ordered by the court.
The agency offers a formal process for the redetermination of benefits if your circumstances change.
Tax authorities may conduct a redetermination if the initial assessment was based on incomplete information.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: determination - the act of making a decision or deciding something

Etymology

Formed from re- (again) and determination.
