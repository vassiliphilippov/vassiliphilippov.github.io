raison d'être

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛzɑ̃ dɛˈtʁə / (AmE), / ˌreɪzɒn dˈɛtrə / (BrE)
the most important reason or purpose for someone or something's existence
reason or justification for existing

Examples

His art became his raison d'être after he retired from teaching.
For many, their children are their raison d'être.
The charity's raison d'être is to support homeless people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purpose - the reason for which something is done or exists
  • Antonym: pretext - a reason given in justification that is not the real reason

Etymology

French, literally ‘reason for being’.

2. NOUN

/ ˌreɪzɒn dˈɛtrə / (AmE), / ˌreɪzɒ̃ dˈɛtrə / (BrE)
the most important reason or purpose for someone or something's existence
most important reason for existence

Examples

Her children are her raison d'être and give her life meaning.
The company's raison d'être is to create innovative solutions for renewable energy.
For many artists, their work is their true raison d'être.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purpose - the reason for which something is done or created or for which something exists
  • Synonym: function - the special activity or purpose of a person or thing

Etymology

French, literally 'reason for being.'
