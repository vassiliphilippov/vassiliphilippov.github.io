exceptional

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsɛpʃənəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛpʃənəl / (BrE)
deviating widely from a norm of physical or mental ability, especially referring to children with abilities significantly different from the average, often used in educational contexts.
deviating from normal ability, esp. in children

Examples

The school provides resources for exceptional children who require additional support.
He was identified as an exceptional student due to his advanced mathematical abilities.
There are programs designed specifically for exceptional learners with unique educational needs.

Synset Examples

special educational provisions for exceptional children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: special - relating to particular needs or abilities, often used regarding education

Etymology

From Latin 'exceptio' (exception) + '-al'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsɛpʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛpʃənl / (BrE)
Unusually good, outstanding, or much greater than is usual or expected.
unusually good or uncommon

Examples

Her performance in the play was truly exceptional and won her several awards.
The school offers programs for exceptional students who need extra challenges.
It was an exceptional year for the company, with profits higher than ever before.

Synset Examples

he paid especial attention to her
exceptional kindness
a matter of particular and unusual importance
a special occasion
a special reason to confide in her
what's so special about the year 2000?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ordinary - common or usual; not exceptional
  • Synonym: special - better, greater, or different from what is usual

Etymology

From exception + -al, influenced by Latin 'exceptio' (a leaving out).

3. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsɛpʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛpʃənəl / (BrE)
unusually good, or much greater than normal; far beyond what is usual in degree or magnitude
much better or greater than usual

Examples

She has an exceptional talent for music, stunning everyone who hears her play.
His exceptional performance earned him a special award at the ceremony.
The restaurant is known for offering exceptional service to all its guests.

Synset Examples

a night of exceeding darkness
an exceptional memory
olympian efforts to save the city from bankruptcy
the young Mozart's prodigious talents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extraordinary - very unusual or remarkable; far beyond the ordinary
  • Antonym: unremarkable - not special or surprising in any way; ordinary

Etymology

From Medieval Latin exceptionalis (pertaining to an exception), from Latin exceptio (exception).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
