Celtis occidentalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛltɪs ˌɑksɪˈdɛntəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɛltɪs ˌɒksɪˈdɛntəlɪs / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree native to North America, commonly known as the hackberry, with small deep purple berries.
a large deciduous North American tree (hackberry)

Examples

The Celtis occidentalis is favored for urban planting due to its toughness and adaptability.
As autumn arrives, the berries of the Celtis occidentalis turn a rich purple color, attracting birds.
Experts recommend the Celtis occidentalis for shade in southern gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hackberry - Common name for Celtis occidentalis, a North American deciduous tree

Etymology

From Latin: Celtis (the genus), occidentalis (western), referring to its native region.
