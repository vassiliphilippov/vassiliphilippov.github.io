trout

1. NOUN

/ traʊt / (AmE), / traʊt / (BrE)
the flesh of any of several chiefly freshwater game and food fishes, used as food
flesh of the trout fish, as food

Examples

We ordered grilled trout with a side of roasted vegetables at the restaurant.
Fresh trout is often considered a delicacy in many regions.
Smoked trout makes an excellent addition to salads and appetizers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmon - flesh of salmon fish as food

Etymology

From Old English truht, via Medieval Latin tructa, from Greek troktēs (‘a kind of fish’).

2. NOUN

/ traʊt / (AmE), / traʊt / (BrE)
a freshwater fish of the salmon family, typically with a speckled body, valued for sport and as food
a freshwater game fish, related to the salmon

Examples

The trout in this river are known for their bright colors and lively nature.
We caught three trout during our fishing trip in the mountains.
Grilled trout is a popular dish in many fine restaurants.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: salmon - a large edible fish that migrates to spawn in freshwater
  • Degree Lower: char - a closely related type of cold-water fish
  • Synonym: bass - another type of freshwater game fish

Etymology

From Old English *truht* via Late Latin *tructa*, from Greek *trōktēs* (a kind of fish, literally 'nibbler').
