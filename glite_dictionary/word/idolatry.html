idolatry

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈdɑːlətɹi / (AmE), / aɪˈdɒlətri / (BrE)
the worship of idols or physical objects as gods
worship of idols; idol worship

Examples

The ancient temple was built for idolatry and rituals honoring many gods.
Some religions strictly prohibit idolatry, viewing the worship of images as blasphemous.
Throughout history, accusations of idolatry have led to conflicts and religious reforms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: iconoclasm - the rejection or destruction of religious images or idols
  • Synonym: to idolize - to admire, revere, or worship as an idol

Etymology

From Old French idolatrie, from Latin idololatria, from Greek eidololatreia (idol worship).
