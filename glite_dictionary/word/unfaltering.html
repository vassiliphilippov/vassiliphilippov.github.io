unfaltering

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɔltərɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɔːltərɪŋ / (BrE)
not weakening or hesitating; steady and resolute, especially in attitude or determination.
steady and resolute; not wavering

Examples

She delivered her speech with unfaltering confidence and poise.
Through unfaltering support, his friends helped him face every obstacle.
His unfaltering dedication to justice inspired everyone around him.

Synset Examples

firm convictions
a firm mouth
steadfast resolve
a man of unbendable perseverance
unwavering loyalty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steadfast - firm and unwavering in purpose or loyalty
  • Antonym: wavering - becoming weaker or indecisive; not steady

Etymology

From un- (not) + faltering (present participle of 'falter', from Middle English 'faltren').
