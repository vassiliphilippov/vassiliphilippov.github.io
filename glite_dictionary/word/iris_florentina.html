Iris florentina

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪs flɔːˈrɛntɪnə / (AmE), / ˈaɪərɪs flɒˈrɛntɪnə / (BrE)
a species of iris with large white flowers tinged with lavender and a fragrant rhizome, commonly known as Florentine iris
a species of iris with large white flowers

Examples

The Iris florentina is prized for its beautiful, fragrant white flowers in gardens across Europe.
In perfume making, oils are often extracted from the rhizome of the Iris florentina.
The botanical garden features a rare collection of Iris florentina blooming in the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iris germanica - a related species of iris known for its purple flowers

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'Florentine iris', referencing Florence, Italy.
