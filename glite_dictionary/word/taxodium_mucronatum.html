Taxodium mucronatum

1. NOUN

/ tækˈsoʊdiəm mjʊˌkroʊˈneɪtəm / (AmE), / tækˈsəʊdiəm mjʊˌkrəʊˈneɪtəm / (BrE)
a cypress tree native to river valleys of the Mexican highlands, known for its large trunk and longevity
Mexican cypress tree species

Examples

The ancient Taxodium mucronatum near the village attracts thousands of tourists each year.
Botanists study the Taxodium mucronatum for its remarkable drought resistance.
One of the largest Taxodium mucronatum trees, known as El Árbol del Tule, stands in Oaxaca, Mexico.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Montezuma cypress - common name for Taxodium mucronatum
  • Synonym: bald cypress - related North American tree species, Taxodium distichum

Etymology

From Latin: 'Taxodium' (genus name), and 'mucronatum' meaning 'pointed', referring to the leaves.
