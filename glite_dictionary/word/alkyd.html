alkyd

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kɪd / (AmE), / ˈal.kɪd / (BrE)
a type of durable synthetic resin used chiefly in paints, varnishes, and adhesives
synthetic resin used in paints and adhesives

Examples

The furniture was coated with an alkyd paint that offered excellent protection.
Most industrial enamels are based on alkyd resins because of their toughness.
She chose an alkyd adhesive for the project due to its durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resin - a sticky substance produced by plants or synthetically, used in polymers and varnishes

Etymology

From 'alcohol' and 'acid', indicating its components in synthesis.
