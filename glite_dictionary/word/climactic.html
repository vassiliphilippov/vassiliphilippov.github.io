climactic

1. ADJ

/ klaɪˈmæktɪk / (AmE), / klaɪˈmæktɪk / (BrE)
forming or relating to a climax; of the most intense or important part of something
relating to a climax; most intense point

Examples

The story reached its climactic scene in the final chapter.
After hours of build-up, the movie's climactic battle did not disappoint.
Everyone was waiting for the climactic moment when the winner would be announced.

Synset Examples

a climactic development

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anticlimactic - referring to something disappointing after a buildup

Etymology

From Greek klimaktikos (pertaining to a climax), from klimax (ladder, climax).
