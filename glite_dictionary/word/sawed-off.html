sawed-off

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɔdˌɑf / (AmE), / ˈsɔːd ɒf / (BrE)
shortened by sawing, especially referring to an object that has had part of its length cut off
shortened by sawing

Examples

The police confiscated a sawed-off shotgun from the vehicle.
He used a sawed-off broomstick to reach the ceiling.
A sawed-off piece of pipe was lying on the ground.

Synset Examples

a sawed-off shotgun
a sawed-off broomstick
the shortened rope was easier to use

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortened - made short or shorter in length

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɔd ˌɑf / (AmE), / ˈsɔːd ˌɒf / (BrE)
(informal, typically American) Very short in height or stature; well below average height.
very short in height
informal american

Examples

Despite his sawed-off frame, he made an excellent basketball player.
She always hated being called sawed-off by her older brothers.
The new recruit was a sawed-off little fellow with a big attitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short - having little height; not tall
  • Antonym: tall - of greater than average height

Etymology

From the analogy of something literally cut off to a short length, especially referencing 'sawed-off shotgun'.
