to erupt

1. VERB

/ əˈrʌpt / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌpt / (BrE)
(of spots, a rash, etc.) to suddenly appear on the skin.
to suddenly appear on the skin

Examples

A painful rash erupted on his back overnight, causing him to worry.
Red spots often erupt on children's skin during chickenpox.
Blisters can erupt suddenly as a reaction to an allergen.

Synset Examples

A rash erupted on her arms after she had touched the exotic plant

Etymology

From Latin 'erumpere' meaning 'to burst forth'.

/ ɪˈrʌpt / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌpt / (BrE)
(of a volcano or geyser) to become active and eject lava, ash, rocks, or steam suddenly and violently.
to spew forth lava, rocks, or ash suddenly

Examples

Mount Etna can erupt several times in a single year.
Scientists are monitoring the volcano in case it erupts again soon.
The island was evacuated when the volcano began to erupt violently.
