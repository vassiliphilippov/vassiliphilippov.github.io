lightly

1. ADV

/ ˈlaɪtli / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtli / (BrE)
to a slight degree; not strongly or intensely
to a slight degree

Examples

The cake should be lightly dusted with powdered sugar before serving.
She touched his arm lightly, afraid to disturb him.
The curtains are only lightly stained and can be cleaned easily.

Synset Examples

her speech is only lightly accented

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slightly - to a small degree; not considerably

Etymology

From 'light', meaning 'not heavy' or 'gentle', with the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
