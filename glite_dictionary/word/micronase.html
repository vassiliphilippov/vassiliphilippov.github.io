Micronase

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkrəˌneɪs / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪ.krəˌneɪs / (BrE)
A trademarked medication used to manage type 2 diabetes by stimulating the release of insulin from the pancreas.
oral antidiabetic drug (trade name)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Micronase to help control her patient's blood sugar levels.
Some patients taking Micronase may experience episodes of low blood sugar if not monitored closely.
He switched from another brand to Micronase due to better tolerance of the medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DiaBeta - another trade name for glyburide, an oral antidiabetic drug
