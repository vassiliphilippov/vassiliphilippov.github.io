outing

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
a trip or social event, often outdoors, taken for pleasure, relaxation, or recreation, usually lasting a day
social or recreational day trip

Examples

Our company is organizing an outing to the national park this weekend.
The children eagerly packed their lunches for the school outing.
We enjoyed a wonderful family outing by the lake on Sunday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excursion - a short journey or trip, especially one engaged in as a leisure activity
  • Synonym: picnic - an occasion when a packed meal is eaten outdoors, especially during a trip

Etymology

From out + -ing, first used in 19th-century English to describe excursions outside the home.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaʊ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
a short journey taken for pleasure, especially a trip made by a group of people
pleasure trip or excursion

Examples

We organized a family outing to the beach last weekend.
The school's annual outing to the zoo is always popular with students.
They planned a group outing to the countryside for a picnic.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excursion - a short journey or trip, especially one taken as a leisure activity
  • Synonym: trip - a journey or voyage

Etymology

From out + -ing, first used in late 19th-century English.
