predicatively

1. ADV

/ prɪˈdɪk.ə.tɪv.li / (AmE), / prɪˈdɪk.ət.ɪv.li / (BrE)
in a way that occurs within the predicate phrase, typically describing how an adjective or phrase is used in grammar
in the predicate phrase (grammar)

Examples

In the sentence 'The sky is blue', the adjective is used predicatively.
Some adjectives can only occur predicatively after a verb, not before a noun.
'Asleep' appears predicatively in 'The child is asleep', but not before the noun.

Synset Examples

predicatively used adjectives

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attributively - in a way that forms part of a noun phrase, typically preceding the noun (grammar)

Etymology

From predicate + -ive + -ly. "Predicate" from Latin praedicatum (that which is said of the subject).
