bloomers

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːmɚz / (AmE), / ˈbluːməz / (BrE)
a type of loose-fitting underpants worn by women, especially in the past
loose-fitting women's underpants
archaic

Examples

In Victorian times, women commonly wore bloomers under their long dresses.
She laughed when her old-fashioned bloomers were mentioned in the story.
The museum displayed a pair of 19th-century bloomers as part of its exhibit on women's fashion.

Synset Examples

she was afraid that her bloomers might have been showing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panties - women's or girls' underpants

Etymology

Named after Amelia Bloomer (1818–1894), an American women's rights advocate who promoted this style of clothing.
