capped

1. ADJ

/ kæpt / (AmE), / kapt / (BrE)
covered on top as if with a cap or crown, often used with a modifier to indicate the type of covering
having a cap or top covering

Examples

The city's skyline was dominated by snow-capped peaks shimmering in the sunlight.
She admired the red-capped mushrooms growing at the edge of the forest.
At dawn, cloud-capped hills appeared mysterious and enchanting.

Synset Examples

cloud-capped mountains
brown-capped mushrooms
snow-capped peaks

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncapped - not covered with a cap or top
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00657148-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ kæpt / (AmE), / kæpt / (BrE)
(of teeth) having an artificial crown fitted over them.
having an artificial crown (especially of teeth)

Examples

She smiled confidently, showing her capped front teeth.
After the accident, his dentist recommended getting several capped teeth.
The dentist explained how capped teeth could improve both appearance and function.

Synset Examples

capped teeth gave her a beautiful smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowned - having a crown fitted (of a tooth)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00658315-s
Synset POS: s
