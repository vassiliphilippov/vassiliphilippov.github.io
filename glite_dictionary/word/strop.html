strop

1. NOUN

/ strɑp / (AmE), / strɒp / (BrE)
a strip of leather, typically mounted on a handle, used for sharpening straight razors by aligning their edges
leather strap for sharpening razors

Examples

He ran the blade along the strop to sharpen it before shaving.
My grandfather keeps an old strop hanging by the bathroom mirror.
Barbers often use a leather strop to maintain the edge of their straight razors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strap - a strip of leather, cloth, or other flexible material

Etymology

From Middle English strop (a band, thong), from Latin *stroppus* (cord, band).

2. VERB

/ strɑp / (AmE), / strɒp / (BrE)
to sharpen (a razor or blade) by rubbing it on a strop
sharpen with a strop

Examples

He carefully stropped his razor before every shave to keep it sharp.
Barbers used to strop their blades on a leather strap between customers.
You should strop the knife regularly to maintain its fine edge.

Synset Examples

strop razors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hone - to sharpen by rubbing on a stone or with precision

Etymology

From strop (noun), from Old English *strop* (strap).

Synset POS: v
