inebriety

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌnibriˈaɪəti / (AmE), / ɪˌniːbriˈaɪəti / (BrE)
A temporary state of being drunk or intoxicated caused by excessive consumption of alcohol.
state of drunkenness

Examples

His obvious inebriety at the banquet caused quite a stir among the guests.
The symptoms of inebriety include slurred speech and impaired motor skills.
After an evening of heavy drinking, his inebriety was apparent to everyone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of being sober or not affected by alcohol
  • Synonym: intoxication - the state of being intoxicated, especially by alcohol

Etymology

From Latin 'inebrietas', from 'inebrius' (drunk).
