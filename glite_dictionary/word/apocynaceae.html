Apocynaceae

1. NOUN

/ əˌpɑːsɪˈneɪsi.iː / (AmE), / əˌpɒsɪˈneɪsi.iː / (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical trees, shrubs, or herbs, often with milky sap and showy flowers, some of which are sources of drugs.
family of tropical plants with milky sap

Examples

The Apocynaceae family includes many plants prized for their ornamental value, such as oleander and frangipani.
Some members of the Apocynaceae are a source of traditional medicines and alkaloids.
Botanists have discovered several new species in the Apocynaceae throughout tropical rainforests.

Etymology

From modern Latin Apocynaceae, based on Apocynum (a genus name) from Greek apokynon (dogbane).

2. NOUN

/ əˌpɑːsəˈneɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / ˌapə(ʊ)sɪˈneɪsɪiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical trees, shrubs, or herbs with milky sap and often showy flowers; many provide medicinal substances
tropical plant family with milky sap, showy flowers

Examples

Many members of the Apocynaceae family are known for their strikingly beautiful flowers.
The milky sap of some Apocynaceae plants has long been used in traditional medicine.
Gardeners value Apocynaceae species for both their ornamental appeal and their hardiness.

Etymology

From Latin *Apocynum* (dogbane), from Greek *apokyon* (‘dog-away’), the type genus.
