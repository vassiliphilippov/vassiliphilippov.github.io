Abronia umbellata

1. NOUN

/ əˈbroʊniə ʌmˈbɛlətə / (AmE), / əˈbrəʊniə ʌmˈbɛlətə / (BrE)
A prostrate flowering herb with clusters of deep pink to white flowers, native to coastal dunes from British Columbia to Baja California.
flowering herb native to coastal dunes

Examples

The rare Abronia umbellata blooms along the sandy coastline every spring.
Conservationists are working to protect Abronia umbellata from habitat loss due to urban development.
Beachgoers admired the vibrant flowers of Abronia umbellata scattered across the dunes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand verbena - common name for Abronia umbellata

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Abronia'; species name 'umbellata' meaning 'bearing umbels'.
