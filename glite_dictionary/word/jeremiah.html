Jeremiah

1. PROPN

/ dʒɛr.əˈmaɪ.ə / (AmE), / dʒɛr.ɪˈmaɪ.ə / (BrE)
(in the Bible) an Israelite prophet of the 7th–6th century BC, known for his prophecies and lamentations about the wickedness of his people.
Israelite prophet in the Old Testament

Examples

Jeremiah warned the people of Jerusalem about the coming destruction if they did not change their ways.
The Book of Jeremiah contains many vivid accounts of his struggles and prophecies.
Many scholars study Jeremiah for insights into ancient Israelite society and religion.

Etymology

From Hebrew Yirməyāhū (meaning 'Yahweh will exalt').

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛrəˈmaɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛrəˈmaɪ.ə / (BrE)
a book in the Old Testament of the Bible that contains the prophecies and oracles attributed to the prophet Jeremiah
biblical book of prophecies by Jeremiah

Examples

The Book of Jeremiah contains many warnings to the people of Judah.
In her thesis, she analyzed the poetic structure of Jeremiah.
Many find comfort and guidance in the verses of Jeremiah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lamentations - a book in the Old Testament traditionally attributed to Jeremiah

Etymology

From the Hebrew name Yirməyāhū (meaning 'Yahweh will exalt').
