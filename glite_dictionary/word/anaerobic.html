anaerobic

1. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈroʊbɪk / (AmE), / ˌanəˈrəʊbɪk / (BrE)
(of a process or an organism) not requiring oxygen for growth or activity; occurring or able to occur without oxygen.
not requiring oxygen
medical

Examples

Most bacteria in the soil are anaerobic and thrive where oxygen is absent.
During intense exercise, the body may rely on anaerobic metabolism to produce energy.
Some medical conditions encourage the growth of anaerobic bacteria in wounds.

Synset Examples

isometric exercises are anaerobic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aerobic - requiring oxygen

Etymology

From Greek an- (not) + aer (air) + bios (life).

2. ADJ

/ ˌæn.əˈroʊ.bɪk / (AmE), / ˌan.ɪəˈrəʊ.bɪk / (BrE)
living, occurring, or active in the absence of free oxygen
living or occurring without oxygen
medical

Examples

Some bacteria are anaerobic and thrive in environments without oxygen.
The treatment plant uses anaerobic digestion to break down waste.
Yeast can reproduce under anaerobic conditions, producing alcohol as a by-product.

Synset Examples

anaerobic bacteria

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aerobic - living or active only in the presence of oxygen

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (without) + 'aēr' (air) + '-obic', from 'bios' (life).

Synset POS: a
