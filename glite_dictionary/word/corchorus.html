Corchorus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrkərəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːkərəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical herbs or subshrubs, widely distributed and especially found in Asia, known for species used as sources of jute fiber.
genus of tropical herbs; source of jute fiber

Examples

Corchorus is cultivated in many Asian countries for its strong natural fiber.
The stems of Corchorus plants are a principal source of jute.
Botanists identified several species within the Corchorus genus in the tropical region.

Etymology

From Latin 'Corchorus', from Greek 'korkhoros', an Egyptian plant.
