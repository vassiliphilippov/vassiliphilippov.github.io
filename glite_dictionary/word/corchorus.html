corchorus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.kə.rəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.kə.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical plants with large leaves and yellow flowers, several species of which are cultivated as a source of jute fiber.
genus of plants used for jute fiber

Examples

The farmers grew corchorus to harvest the valuable jute fibers from its stems.
Research shows that corchorus thrives in warm, humid climates with ample rainfall.
Traditional mats and ropes are often made from fibers extracted from corchorus plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jute - a strong fiber obtained from plants of the genus Corchorus

Etymology

From Latin 'Corchorus', from Greek 'korkhoros', an ancient name for a wild plant.
