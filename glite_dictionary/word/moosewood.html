moosewood

1. NOUN

/ ˈmusˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈmuːswʊd / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub of eastern North America with tough, flexible branches, pliable bark, and small yellow flowers.
North American deciduous shrub

Examples

The moosewood thrives in moist, shady forests throughout the northeastern United States.
She used strips of moosewood bark to weave baskets as her ancestors had done.
In spring, yellow blossoms appear on the branches of the moosewood, brightening the understory.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmuːs.wʊd / (AmE), / ˈmuːs.wʊd / (BrE)
A maple tree (Acer pensylvanicum) of eastern North America, having striped bark and large two-lobed leaves that turn clear yellow in autumn.
a North American maple with striped bark

Examples

The moosewood is easily recognized by its green and white striped bark.
In autumn, the leaves of the moosewood change to a bright, clear yellow.
Hikers often find moosewood growing in the shady understory of eastern forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped maple - another common name for Acer pensylvanicum, the same tree as moosewood
