Mesozoic

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛzəˈzoʊɪk / (AmE), / ˌmiːzəʊˈzəʊɪk / (BrE)
the era in Earth's history from about 230 to 63 million years ago, following the Paleozoic and preceding the Cenozoic, characterized by the dominance of dinosaurs
geological era (230–63 million years ago)

Examples

The Mesozoic era is often called the Age of Reptiles due to the dominance of dinosaurs.
Many of the world's major landmasses began breaking apart during the Mesozoic.
Important plant and animal evolution occurred throughout the Mesozoic period.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Paleozoic - geological era before the Mesozoic
  • Degree Higher: Cenozoic - geological era after the Mesozoic

Etymology

From Greek mesos (middle) + zōē (life); coined to denote the 'middle life' era.

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɛz.əˈzoʊ.ɪk, ˌmiːz- / (AmE), / ˌmɛz.əˈzəʊ.ɪk, ˌmiːz- / (BrE)
relating to or denoting the Mesozoic era, a geological era between the Paleozoic and Cenozoic eras, characterized by the rise of dinosaurs and the development of flowering plants.
relating to the Mesozoic geological era

Examples

Many dinosaur fossils date back to the Mesozoic period.
Geologists studied rock formations from the Mesozoic era to learn about ancient climates.
The Mesozoic landscape was dominated by large reptiles and early mammals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Cenozoic - relating to the geological era after the Mesozoic
  • Degree Lower: Paleozoic - relating to the geological era before the Mesozoic

Etymology

From Greek 'mesos' (middle) + 'zōion' (animal), referring to 'middle life'.
