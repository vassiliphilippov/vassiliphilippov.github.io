perigone

1. NOUN

/ ˈperɪˌɡoʊn / (AmE), / ˈperɪɡəʊn / (BrE)
the outer part of a flower, collectively referring to the calyx and corolla, especially when they are similar in appearance and not clearly separated
outer collective floral envelope
medical

Examples

The lily's perigone is made up of six petal-like segments that surround the reproductive organs.
Unlike many other flowers, the tulip has a brightly colored perigone instead of distinct petals and sepals.
Botanists often use the structure of the perigone to help identify species in certain plant families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perianth - the outer part of a flower collectively, especially when calyx and corolla are not distinct

Etymology

From Greek 'perigonion', from 'peri-' (around) and 'gonē' (seed, generation).
