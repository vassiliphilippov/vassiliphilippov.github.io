Brugmansia arborea

1. NOUN

/ bruɡˈmænziə ɑrˈbɔriə / (AmE), / bruːɡˈmænziə ɑːˈbɔːrɪə / (BrE)
A South American shrub or small tree cultivated for its large, fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers.
flowering South American shrub or tree

Examples

The garden's centerpiece was a magnificent Brugmansia arborea filled with fragrant white flowers.
Horticulturists often prize Brugmansia arborea for its showy, trumpet-shaped blooms.
She planted a Brugmansia arborea along the walkway to attract evening pollinators.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Brugmansia', after Dutch naturalist Sebald Justinus Brugmans, and Latin 'arborea' meaning 'tree-like'.
