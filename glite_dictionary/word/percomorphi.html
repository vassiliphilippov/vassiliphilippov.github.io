Percomorphi

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɜrkəˈmɔrfaɪ / (AmE), / ˌpɜːkəˈmɔːfaɪ / (BrE)
a large natural group of fishes that includes true perches, basses, tuna, and many related species, found in both marine and freshwater environments
group of fishes (perches, basses, tuna, etc.)

Examples

Many commercially important species, such as tuna and sea bass, belong to the Percomorphi.
The Percomorphi are known for their diversity in size and habitat.
Scientists have debated the evolutionary relationships within the Percomorphi for decades.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'perke' (perch) and 'morphē' (form/shape).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɜrkəˈmɔːrfaɪ / (AmE), / ˌpɜːkəˈmɔːfaɪ / (BrE)
a former taxonomic order comprising a large group of marine and freshwater fishes, including perches, basses, and tunas
a large group of fishes (perches, basses, tunas)
medical

Examples

The Percomorphi include many familiar species found in both oceans and freshwater lakes.
Scientists debated the reclassification of several families previously grouped under Percomorphi.
Tunas, part of the Percomorphi order, are among the fastest and most commercially important fishes.

Etymology

From New Latin Percomorphi, from Latin perca (perch) + Greek morphē (form, shape).
