prokaryote

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈkæriˌoʊt / (AmE), / prəˈkærɪəʊt / (BrE)
A unicellular organism whose cells lack a distinct membrane-bound nucleus, such as bacteria and cyanobacteria.
unicellular organism lacking a nucleus
medical

Examples

Bacteria are a type of prokaryote found in almost every environment on Earth.
Unlike a prokaryote, a eukaryote has a nucleus enclosed by a membrane.
The study focused on the genetic structure of prokaryotes in marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eukaryote - an organism whose cells have a nucleus enclosed within membranes

Etymology

From Greek 'pro-' (before) + 'karyon' (nut, kernel, nucleus).
