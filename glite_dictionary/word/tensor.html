tensor

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛn.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.sə / (BrE)
A muscle that tightens or tenses a part of the body, making it firm or taut.
a muscle that tightens a body part
medical

Examples

The tensor fasciae latae is an important muscle in the thigh.
Damage to the tensor can affect joint stability.
Doctors examined the patient's tensor muscles for signs of inflammation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relaxer - a muscle that relaxes or loosens a part of the body

Etymology

From Latin 'tendere' (to stretch), via Medieval Latin 'tensor' (stretcher, muscle that stretches).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛn.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.sə / (BrE)
a mathematical object that generalizes the concepts of scalars, vectors, and matrices, and is used to represent relationships between sets of geometric or physical quantities
mathematical object generalizing vectors and matrices

Examples

In physics, the stress tensor describes the internal forces within a solid body.
Machine learning models often rely on manipulating large data stored as tensors.
The curvature of spacetime in general relativity is represented by a complex mathematical tensor.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: vector - a quantity with both magnitude and direction
  • Degree Lower: matrix - a rectangular array of numbers or expressions

Etymology

From Latin 'tendere' (to stretch), reflecting its original use in mathematics and mechanics.
