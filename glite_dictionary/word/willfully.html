willfully

1. ADV

/ ˈwɪlfəli / (AmE), / ˈwɪlfʊli / (BrE)
In a deliberate and intentional manner, often with a stubborn or determined purpose.
deliberately; intentionally

Examples

He willfully ignored the warnings posted on the door.
The company was fined for willfully violating environmental regulations.
She willfully misunderstood his intentions to make her argument stronger.

Synset Examples

she had willfully deceived me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deliberately - in a careful, intentional way
  • Antonym: accidentally - by chance, not intentionally

Etymology

From 'willful' + adverbial suffix '-ly'. 'Willful' from Middle English, meaning 'full of will'.
