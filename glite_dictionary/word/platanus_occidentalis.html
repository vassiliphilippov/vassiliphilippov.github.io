Platanus occidentalis

1. NOUN

/ pləˈtæn.əs ˌɑk.sɪˈden.tə.lɪs / (AmE), / pləˈtæn.əs ˌɒk.sɪˈden.tə.lɪs / (BrE)
A very large, widely spreading plane tree native to eastern and central North America, also known as the American sycamore.
large North American plane tree (American sycamore)

Examples

The Platanus occidentalis can live for several centuries and grow to massive heights.
Many parks feature Platanus occidentalis for its broad canopy and resilience to pollution.
The bark of the Platanus occidentalis peels off in patches, leaving a distinctive mottled appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American sycamore - common name for Platanus occidentalis

Etymology

From Latin: 'Platanus' (plane tree) and 'occidentalis' (western).
