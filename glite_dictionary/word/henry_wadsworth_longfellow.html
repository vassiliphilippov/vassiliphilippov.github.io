Henry Wadsworth Longfellow

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛnri ˈwɑːdzˌwɜrθ ˈlɔŋˌfɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri ˈwɒdzˌwɜːθ ˈlɒŋˌfɛləʊ / (BrE)
(1807–1882) an American poet famous for his long narrative poems, including 'The Song of Hiawatha' and 'Paul Revere's Ride'.
American poet, author of narrative poems

Examples

Henry Wadsworth Longfellow is best known for his poem 'Paul Revere's Ride', which captures a moment in American history.
Many students in the U.S. study works by Henry Wadsworth Longfellow during their literature classes.
The house where Henry Wadsworth Longfellow lived is now a popular museum.
