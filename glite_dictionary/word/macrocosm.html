macrocosm

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæk.rəˌkɑː.zəm / (AmE), / ˈmæk.rəʊˌkɒz.əm / (BrE)
the whole of the universe; everything that exists considered as a single entity, often contrasted with microcosm
the entire universe; everything that exists

Examples

The ancient philosophers believed the human body mirrored the macrocosm of the universe.
In her writing, the city was a macrocosm that reflected the diversity of humankind.
Astrophysicists constantly seek to understand the origins and structure of the macrocosm.

Synset Examples

they study the evolution of the universe
the biggest tree in existence

Related Terms

  • Antonym: microcosm - a small, representative system having analogies to a larger system

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) + kosmos (order, universe).
