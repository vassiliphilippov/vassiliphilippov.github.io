boldface

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊldˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊldˌfeɪs / (BrE)
a typeface or style of print having thick, heavy lines, used especially for emphasis
typeface with thick, heavy lines

Examples

The headlines were printed in boldface to grab the reader's attention.
Please use boldface for section titles in your report.
Words in boldface are defined in the glossary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bold - having a strong, thick line in print
  • Antonym: italic - typeface with slanted characters, typically used for emphasis

Etymology

From 'bold' + 'face', originally referring to the 'face' or style of type in printing.

2. VERB

/ ˈboʊldˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊldˌfeɪs / (BrE)
to print or write (text) in a bold typeface; to make text bold.
make text bold in print or on screen

Examples

Please boldface the key terms in your report for greater emphasis.
He boldfaced the date so it would stand out on the invitation.
If you boldface headings, the document becomes much easier to scan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to italicize - to print or write in italics
  • Synonym: underline - to draw a line under text for emphasis

Etymology

From 'bold' (courageous, thick) + 'face' (typeface style). Refers to type that stands out.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈboʊldˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊldˌfeɪs / (BrE)
A typeface with thick, heavy lines used for emphasis.
a typeface with thick, heavy lines

Examples

Please print the headline in boldface to make it stand out.
He highlighted the most important points with boldface type.
Instruction manuals often use boldface for warnings and notes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: italic - a typeface with slanted, cursive characters
  • Degree Lower: regular - a typeface with standard, upright weight
  • Synonym: bold - a typeface or font weight with thick, heavy lines
