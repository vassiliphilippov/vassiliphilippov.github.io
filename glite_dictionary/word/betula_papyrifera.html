Betula papyrifera

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtuːlə ˌpæpɪˈraɪfə / (AmE), / bɪˈtjuːlə ˌpapɪˈrʌɪfə / (BrE)
A North American birch tree with distinctive white, peeling bark, commonly known as paper birch and used for various crafts.
North American paper birch tree

Examples

The white bark of Betula papyrifera peels off easily and was often used by Indigenous peoples to make containers.
We saw a beautiful grove of Betula papyrifera along the edge of the lake.
Many artists appreciate Betula papyrifera for its striking appearance in landscape paintings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paper birch - common name for Betula papyrifera

Etymology

From Latin: Betula (birch genus) + papyrifera ('paper-bearing').
