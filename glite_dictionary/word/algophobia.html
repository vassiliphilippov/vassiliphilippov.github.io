algophobia

1. NOUN

/ ˌælɡəˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌalɡəˈfəʊbɪə / (BrE)
An abnormal or morbid fear of pain, especially as a recognized psychological disorder.
morbid fear of pain
medical

Examples

He was diagnosed with algophobia after developing extreme anxiety about medical procedures.
For those with algophobia, even minor discomfort can provoke intense fear.
Therapy may help patients overcome their algophobia and improve their quality of life.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pain - physical suffering or discomfort
  • Antonym: analgesia - absence of pain in response to stimulation

Etymology

From Greek 'algos' (pain) + 'phobia' (fear).
