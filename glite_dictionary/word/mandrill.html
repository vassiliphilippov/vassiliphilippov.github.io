mandrill

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.drɪl / (AmE), / ˈmæn.drɪl / (BrE)
A large West African baboon (Mandrillus sphinx) known for its brightly colored red and blue muzzle and blue hindquarters.
large brightly colored West African baboon

Examples

The mandrill is easily recognized by its red and blue facial markings.
At the zoo, children were fascinated by the colorful mandrill eating fruit.
Although related to baboons, the mandrill is noted for its striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baboon - large primate of Africa or Arabia
  • Degree Lower: drill - related West African primate with less vivid coloration

Etymology

From French mandrille, of unknown origin; scientific name Mandrillus sphinx.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.drɪl / (AmE), / ˈmæn.drɪl / (BrE)
a large West African baboon with a bright red and blue muzzle and blue hindquarters
a large African baboon with colorful face

Examples

The mandrill is known for its strikingly colorful face and rump.
During our safari, we spotted a group of mandrills foraging in the forest.
A male mandrill can weigh more than 50 pounds and has prominent canine teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baboon - a large Old World monkey with a doglike snout

Etymology

From French mandrille, of uncertain origin, possibly related to 'man' and 'drill' (an ape).
