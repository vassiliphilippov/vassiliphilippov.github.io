selective

1. ADJ

/ səˈlɛktɪv / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktɪv / (BrE)
characterized by careful or discriminating choice; tending to choose carefully among options rather than accepting many or all.
carefully choosing; discriminating

Examples

The university is extremely selective about which students it admits.
When hiring, the company is selective to ensure they choose the best candidate.
She became more selective about the projects she agreed to undertake.

Synset Examples

the school was very selective in its admissions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discriminating - showing careful judgment or taste in choosing
  • Synonym: exclusive - limiting or excluding others from consideration or entry
  • Antonym: inclusive - not selective; including everyone or everything

Etymology

From Latin 'selectus', past participle of 'seligere' (to select), plus English suffix '-ive'.

2. ADJ

/ səˈlɛktɪv / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktɪv / (BrE)
tending to make careful choices; characterized by selecting only particular people or things from among many.
carefully choosing; discriminating

Examples

She is very selective about the books she reads, always choosing only the best.
The university has a highly selective admissions process.
Being selective in hiring new employees helps build a stronger team.

Synset Examples

an exceptionally quick and selective reader

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discriminating - showing careful judgment, especially in matters of taste
  • Antonym: indiscriminate - not using or exercising selection; random

Etymology

From Latin 'selectus', past participle of 'seligere' (to select), plus '-ive'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00779252-s
Synset POS: s
