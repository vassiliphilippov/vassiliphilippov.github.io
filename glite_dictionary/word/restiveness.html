restiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛstɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛstɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being restless, uneasy, or difficult to control, especially due to nervousness or impatience
state of being restless or uneasy

Examples

The horse's restiveness made it difficult for the rider to maintain control.
There was an air of restiveness in the crowd as the speech dragged on.
Her restiveness was apparent as she tapped her foot impatiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restlessness - the state of being unable to rest, relax, or remain still
  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being free from agitation or strong emotion

Etymology

From 'restive' plus the suffix '-ness', originally from Latin 'restare' (to remain).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛstɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛstɪvnɪs / (BrE)
a feeling of restlessness or unease, especially when unable to remain still or silent
state of being restless or uneasy

Examples

His restiveness was obvious as he kept tapping his foot during the long meeting.
The teacher noticed the students' restiveness after hours of sitting still.
A sense of restiveness spread through the crowd as the event was delayed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restlessness - the state of being unable to rest or relax
  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being still, peaceful, and relaxed

Etymology

From 'restive' (unwilling to be still) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or condition).

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛstɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛstɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being difficult to control or unwilling to be guided; stubbornness or resistance.
state of being difficult to control

Examples

The manager was frustrated by the restiveness of the team during the meeting.
After hours of waiting, the children's restiveness became difficult for the teachers to handle.
Political restiveness spread throughout the country as protests increased.

Synset Examples

Also none of the studies reviewed gave information on the prevalence of aggression and restiveness of the youths in Anambra State.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restive - difficult to control; stubborn or restless
  • Antonym: obedience - the act or quality of obeying or being willing to comply

Etymology

From 'restive' + '-ness', where 'restive' comes from French 'restif' (restive, unwilling to move).
