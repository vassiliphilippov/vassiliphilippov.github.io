fiery

1. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪəri / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəri / (BrE)
Full of strong or intense emotion; passionate or fervent.
full of intense emotion; passionate

Examples

Her fiery speech inspired the entire crowd to take action.
He is known for his fiery temperament and passionate arguments.
The debate became especially fiery when the two experts strongly disagreed.

Synset Examples

ardent love
an ardent lover
a fervent desire to change society
a fervent admirer
fiery oratory
an impassioned appeal
a torrid love affair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impassioned - filled with intense emotion
  • Synonym: passionate - showing or caused by strong feelings
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling strong emotions

Etymology

From Old English *fyr* (fire) + -y.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪəri / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəri / (BrE)
having or showing strong emotion, especially passion or a quick temper; intensely emotional or easily provoked to anger
passionate or quick-tempered

Examples

She gave a fiery speech that inspired everyone in the room.
His fiery personality sometimes gets him into trouble at work.
The coach’s fiery response motivated the players to try harder.

Synset Examples

a fiery temper
flaming passions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passionate - showing or caused by strong feelings or a strong belief
  • Antonym: cool-headed - calm and not easily angered or agitated

Etymology

From Middle English 'firy', from Old English 'fȳr' (fire) + '-y'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01259807-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪəri / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəri / (BrE)
having qualities of fire; very hot, burning, or suggestive of flames
suggestive of fire or flames

Examples

A fiery sunset lit up the entire sky with shades of orange and red.
The fiery furnace radiated heat throughout the cold stone chamber.
A fiery meteor streaked across the night with a brilliant, burning trail.

Synset Examples

a fiery desert wind
an igneous desert atmosphere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blazing - burning brightly or intensely
  • Antonym: cool - having a low temperature or lacking warmth

Etymology

From Middle English 'firy', from Old English 'fȳr' (fire) + '-y'.
