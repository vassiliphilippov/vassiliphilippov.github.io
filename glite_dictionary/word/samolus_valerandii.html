Samolus valerandii

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæməˌləs vəˌlɛˈrændiː / (AmE), / ˈsamələs vaːˈlɛrændiː / (BrE)
A species of small perennial herb known as water pimpernel, native to wetlands from Europe to China, characterized by its small white or pink flowers.
a wetland plant, water pimpernel

Examples

The Samolus valerandii thrives in damp meadows and along stream banks across Europe.
Researchers studied the distribution of Samolus valerandii in various wetland habitats.
Gardeners sometimes call Samolus valerandii the water pimpernel because of its preferred environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water pimpernel - common name for Samolus valerandii, a small wetland herb

Etymology

Samolus: from Latin, a plant name; valerandii: named after French botanist Dominique Valeran.
