Malacostraca

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæl.əˈkɑ.strə.kə / (AmE), / ˌmæl.əˈkɒs.trə.kə / (BrE)
the largest subclass of crustaceans, including most well-known marine, freshwater, and terrestrial species such as crabs, lobsters, and shrimps
large subclass of crustaceans

Examples

Crabs, lobsters, and shrimp all belong to the Malacostraca subclass of crustaceans.
The biologist specialized in studying freshwater species within Malacostraca.
Many edible shellfish are classified under Malacostraca due to their shared anatomical features.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek malakos (soft) and ostrakon (shell).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæləkəˈstrɑkə / (AmE), / ˌmaləkɒˈstrɑːkə / (BrE)
the largest subclass of crustaceans, including crabs, lobsters, shrimp, and similar widely known aquatic and terrestrial species
largest subclass of crustaceans

Examples

The Malacostraca subclass contains many familiar species, including crabs, lobsters, and shrimps.
Scientists have discovered new members of the Malacostraca in deep-sea habitats.
Many students study Malacostraca to learn about the diversity of crustaceans.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: crustacean - any animal of the large group that includes crabs, lobsters, and shrimp
  • Synonym: crab - a crustacean with a broad carapace and five pairs of legs, including a pair of pincers

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'malakos' (soft) + 'ostrakon' (shell).
