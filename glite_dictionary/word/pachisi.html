pachisi

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtʃisi / (AmE), / pəˈtʃiːzi / (BrE)
an ancient board game from India in which players move pieces around a cross-shaped board based on the throw of dice or cowrie shells
ancient Indian board game

Examples

We played pachisi late into the evening, enjoying the challenge and camaraderie.
The rules of pachisi are easy to learn, but mastering strategy takes time.
Many board game historians trace the origins of modern games to pachisi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ludo - a simplified modern board game derived from pachisi

Etymology

From Hindi 'pachīsī', related to 'pachīs' meaning 'twenty-five', referring to the highest score in the game.
