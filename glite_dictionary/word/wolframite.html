wolframite

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊl.frəˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈwʊl.frə.maɪt / (BrE)
A mineral composed of iron and manganese tungstate, occurring as brown to black tabular crystals and being the chief ore of tungsten.
chief ore of tungsten mineral

Examples

The mining company extracted large quantities of wolframite from the mountain site.
Demand for wolframite increased due to its role in tungsten production.
Geologists discovered a new vein of wolframite in the granite bedrock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tungsten - a chemical element, symbol W, obtained chiefly from wolframite

Etymology

From German Wolframit, from Wolfram (tungsten) + -it (mineral suffix).
