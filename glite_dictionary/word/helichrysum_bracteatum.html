Helichrysum bracteatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛlɪˈkraɪsəm brækˈtiːətəm / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪˈkrɪsəm brækˈtiːətəm / (BrE)
an Australian plant with papery yellow to gold flowers, often used in dried floral arrangements; commonly known as strawflower
a plant with yellow or gold papery flowers (strawflower)

Examples

The vibrant Helichrysum bracteatum brightened the garden with its golden-yellow blossoms.
She chose Helichrysum bracteatum for her bouquet because it dries beautifully and retains its color.
Many florists prefer Helichrysum bracteatum as it is ideal for long-lasting decorative arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strawflower - common name for Helichrysum bracteatum, known for its papery flowers

Etymology

From Greek helikhrūsum (sun-gold: helios 'sun' + khrūsos 'gold').
