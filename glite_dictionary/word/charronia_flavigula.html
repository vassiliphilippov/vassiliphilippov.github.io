Charronia flavigula

1. NOUN

/ kəˈroʊniə ˌflævɪˈɡjuːlə / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊniə ˌflævɪˈɡjuːlə / (BrE)
a large yellow and black marten native to southern China and Burma, also known as the yellow-throated marten.
a marten species from China and Burma

Examples

The Charronia flavigula is known for its striking yellow throat and active hunting behavior.
Researchers observed the Charronia flavigula moving swiftly through the forests of Yunnan province.
Unlike some other martens, the Charronia flavigula is often spotted during the daytime.

Etymology

From New Latin, with genus name 'Charronia' and species 'flavigula', meaning 'yellow-throated' (from Latin 'flavus' yellow + 'gula' throat).
