behalf

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈhæf / (AmE), / bɪˈhɑːf / (BrE)
Benefit or interest; used especially in the phrases 'in behalf of' (for the benefit of) or 'on behalf of' (as a representative of).
benefit or interest (esp. in set phrases)

Examples

She accepted the award on behalf of her team, who could not attend the ceremony.
Donations were made in behalf of the disaster victims to support relief efforts.
The lawyer spoke on behalf of the defendant during the trial.

Synset Examples

in your behalf
campaigning in his own behalf
spoke a good word in his friend's behalf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interest - something that benefits or is of advantage to someone

Etymology

From Middle English 'behalf' (meaning 'advantage, favor'), from Old English 'behalfan' (by half, on behalf).

2. NOUN

/ bɪˈhæf / (AmE), / bɪˈhɑːf / (BrE)
as the representative or agent of someone; for the benefit or interest of someone (usually in the phrase 'on behalf of').
as someone's representative or for their benefit

Examples

She accepted the award on behalf of her colleagues who could not attend.
The lawyer spoke on behalf of the defendant in court.
Thank you for your generous donation on behalf of the entire organization.

Synset Examples

the guardian signed the contract on behalf of the minor child
this letter is written on behalf of my client

Etymology

From Middle English 'behalve' (advantage, behalf), from 'be-' (by) + 'half' (side).
