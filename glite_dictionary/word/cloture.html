cloture

1. VERB

/ ˈkloʊ.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkləʊ.tʃə / (BrE)
to bring a debate to an end by calling for an immediate vote, especially in a legislative assembly.
end debate by vote (parliamentary)

Examples

The senator moved to cloture the lengthy debate on the bill.
The chairman decided it was necessary to cloture after hours of discussion.
A two-thirds majority was required to cloture the debate and proceed to a vote.

Synset Examples

debate was closured
cloture the discussion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: closure - the act of ending a debate and proceeding to vote, especially in British contexts

Etymology

From French 'clôture' meaning 'closure' or 'ending'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊ.tʃər / (AmE), / ˈkləʊ.tʃə / (BrE)
a procedure by which debate is formally ended or limited in a legislative assembly, enabling a final vote to be taken
procedure to end debate in legislature

Examples

The Senate invoked cloture to end the filibuster and proceed to a vote.
A motion for cloture requires a specific number of votes to pass.
After hours of debate, cloture was used to bring the discussion to a close.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: closure - the act of closing a debate in parliament

Etymology

From French 'clôture' (closure, shutting); related to 'close'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
