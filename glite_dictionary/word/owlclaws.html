owlclaws

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊlˌklɔz / (AmE), / ˈaʊlˌklɔːz / (BrE)
A stout perennial herb native to the western United States, with flower heads that have drooping orange-yellow rays; known to cause spewing sickness in sheep.
western U.S. herb; toxic to sheep

Examples

Ranchers warn that grazing sheep near owlclaws can be dangerous due to the risk of spewing sickness.
In spring, the fields were dotted with yellow blooms from the wild owlclaws.
A botanist identified the unfamiliar plant as owlclaws during her survey of western prairie flora.
