whisky

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪski / (AmE), / ˈwɪski / (BrE)
A strong alcoholic drink distilled from fermented grain mash, typically aged in wooden casks.
grain-based distilled alcoholic drink

Examples

He poured himself a glass of whisky to unwind after a long day.
The distillery is known for producing some of the finest single malt whisky in Scotland.
Would you prefer your whisky neat or with ice?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiskey - Alternative spelling, especially common in American and Irish usage

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'uisge beatha' meaning 'water of life', via Old Irish 'uisce beatha'.
