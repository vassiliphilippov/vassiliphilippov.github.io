Trichys

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪkɪs / (AmE), / ˈtrɪkɪs / (BrE)
A genus of porcupines in the family Hystricidae, containing the long-tailed porcupine.
genus of long-tailed porcupines

Examples

The genus Trichys includes only the long-tailed porcupine found in Southeast Asia.
Researchers studied the habitat of Trichys to better understand porcupine ecology.
Specimens of Trichys are often distinguished by their unusually long tails.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hystricidae - the porcupine family of rodents
  • Synonym: long-tailed porcupine - the main species in the genus Trichys

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪkɪs / (AmE), / ˈtrɪkɪs / (BrE)
a genus of porcupines in the family Hystricidae, including the long-tailed porcupine.
genus of porcupines (rodents)

Examples

The long-tailed porcupine belongs to the genus Trichys.
Researchers have identified a new species within the genus Trichys.
Unlike other porcupines, members of Trichys have a notably long tail.

Etymology

From Greek trichys, from 'thrix' meaning 'hair'.
