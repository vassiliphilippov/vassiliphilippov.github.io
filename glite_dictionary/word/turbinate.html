turbinate

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɜːr.bɪ.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.bɪ.nət / (BrE)
shaped like a coil or spiral; having a form that resembles a top or a whorl.
shaped like a coil or spiral

Examples

The snail has a turbinate shell that spirals tightly.
Scientists described the new fossil as having a turbinate bone formation.
The vase's turbinate shape added a unique touch to the display.

Etymology

From Latin 'turbinatus', from 'turbo' meaning 'spinning top'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrbɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbɪneɪt / (BrE)
any of the spongy, scroll-shaped bones located inside the nasal passages of humans and other vertebrates, which help to filter and humidify the air we breathe
scroll-shaped nasal bone
medical

Examples

Allergies can cause swelling of the turbinate bones, leading to nasal congestion.
The surgeon carefully examined the patient's turbinate during the procedure.
In mammals, the turbinate serves to warm and humidify the air before it reaches the lungs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasal concha - another term for turbinate, used interchangeably in anatomy

Etymology

From Latin 'turbinatus,' meaning 'shaped like a top or spinning object.'

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˈtɝː.bəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.bɪ.nət / (BrE)
relating to the scroll-shaped turbinate bones in the nasal passages, which help filter and humidify inhaled air
relating to the nasal turbinate bones
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the turbinate tissue for any signs of swelling.
Enlarged turbinate bones can cause breathing difficulties in some patients.
Chronic inflammation may affect the turbinate area, resulting in nasal congestion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasal - relating to the nose

Etymology

From Latin 'turbīnātus' meaning shaped like a spinning top, from 'turbo' (top, whirl).
