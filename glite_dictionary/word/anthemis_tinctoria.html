Anthemis tinctoria

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθɛmɪs tɪŋkˈtɔriə / (AmE), / ænˈθɛmɪs tɪŋkˈtɔːrɪə / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb with hairy divided leaves and yellow flowers; commonly known as golden marguerite and naturalized in North America.
species of yellow-flowered Eurasian herb

Examples

The garden was filled with bright Anthemis tinctoria, adding a splash of yellow to the landscape.
Botanists studied Anthemis tinctoria for its traditional use in producing natural dyes.
In North America, Anthemis tinctoria can sometimes be found growing wild along roadsides.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Anthemis' is the genus name and 'tinctoria' refers to dyeing, as the plant was used for dye.
