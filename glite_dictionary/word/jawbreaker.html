jawbreaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔˌbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːˌbreɪkə / (BrE)
a large, round, very hard candy that dissolves slowly in the mouth
a large, hard candy

Examples

He gave his nephew a huge jawbreaker as a treat after dinner.
It took her nearly an hour to finish the entire jawbreaker.
The children traded marbles and jawbreakers at recess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gobstopper - a large, hard spherical candy that is sucked on and dissolves slowly

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔˌbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːˌbreɪkə / (BrE)
a word that is very difficult to pronounce, especially because it is long or has complex syllables
a word difficult to pronounce

Examples

The scientific term for that mineral is a real jawbreaker.
I always stumble over jawbreakers like 'otorhinolaryngologist'.
Children often laugh when they try to pronounce a jawbreaker word.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tongue-twister - a sequence of words or sounds that are difficult to pronounce quickly and correctly
