disintegration

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈteɡrəʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈteɡrəʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a radioactive substance spontaneously breaks apart, emitting ionizing radiation
breakdown of a radioactive substance
medical

Examples

The disintegration of uranium atoms releases energy in the form of radiation.
Scientists measure the rate of disintegration to determine the age of ancient rocks.
Radioactive disintegration plays a crucial role in nuclear power generation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radioactive decay - the process in which an unstable atomic nucleus loses energy by emitting radiation

Etymology

From Latin 'disintegrare' (to break into pieces), from 'dis-' (apart) + 'integer' (whole).

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsɪnˌteɡrəˈʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˌtɪɡrəˈʃən / (BrE)
The process of breaking or being broken into smaller parts or fragments; the act or state of being separated into component parts.
breaking into parts; separation

Examples

The rapid disintegration of the ancient empire shocked historians.
Exposure to harsh chemicals caused the disintegration of the fabric.
His sudden resignation led to the disintegration of the leadership team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragmentation - the process of breaking or being broken into fragments
  • Antonym: integration - the process of combining or adding parts to make a whole

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'integrare' (to make whole).
