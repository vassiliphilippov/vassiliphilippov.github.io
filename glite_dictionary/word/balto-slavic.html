Balto-Slavic

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɔltoʊˈslævɪk / (AmE), / ˌbæl.təʊˈslɑː.vɪk / (BrE)
a branch of the Indo-European language family comprising the Baltic and Slavic languages
Indo-European language family branch

Examples

Many linguists study the development of Balto-Slavic languages to trace Indo-European roots.
The Balto-Slavic family includes both Slavic and Baltic tongues such as Russian and Lithuanian.
Differences within the Balto-Slavic group reveal much about the history of Eastern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Indo-European - the family of languages that includes most languages of Europe and many of Asia
  • Degree Lower: Slavic - the group of languages forming part of Balto-Slavic, spoken mainly in eastern Europe
  • Degree Lower: Baltic - the group of languages forming part of Balto-Slavic, spoken in the Baltic region

Etymology

Formed from the names of the Baltic and Slavic language groups.
