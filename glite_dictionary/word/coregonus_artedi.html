Coregonus artedi

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˈreɪɡənəs ɑrˈtidi / (AmE), / kɔːˈreɪɡənəs ɑːˈtiːdiː / (BrE)
a species of freshwater whitefish found in cold, deep lakes of North America, valued as a food fish
a North American freshwater whitefish

Examples

Coregonus artedi populations have declined in some lakes due to changes in water quality.
Anglers often seek out Coregonus artedi during the winter ice fishing season.
Biologists monitor Coregonus artedi as an indicator species for ecosystem health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cisco - another name for Coregonus artedi, a freshwater whitefish

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name Coregonus and species epithet artedi, after Swedish naturalist Peter Artedi.
