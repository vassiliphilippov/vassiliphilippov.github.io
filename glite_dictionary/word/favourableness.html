favourableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪvərəblnəs / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərəblnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being encouraging or likely to produce a successful result
encouraging or promising quality

Examples

The favourableness of the weather convinced them to hold the event outdoors.
Investors were encouraged by the favourableness of the market conditions this quarter.
We cannot ignore the favourableness of her initial response to the proposal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: favorableness - quality of being favorable (US spelling)
  • Antonym: unfavourableness - the quality of being not favourable or not promising a positive outcome

Etymology

From favourable + -ness; 'favourable' from Latin 'favorabilis', from 'favor'.
