Pediculus corporis

1. NOUN

/ pəˈdɪkjələs ˈkɔrpərɪs / (AmE), / pɛˈdɪkjʊləs ˈkɔːpərɪs / (BrE)
A species of parasitic louse that infests the human body, commonly known as the body louse.
parasitic body louse species
medical

Examples

Infestation with Pediculus corporis can lead to intense itching and skin irritation.
The spread of Pediculus corporis is commonly associated with overcrowded living conditions.
Doctors identified the source of the rash as an allergic reaction to bites from Pediculus corporis.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: head louse - species of louse that infests human scalp (Pediculus humanus capitis)
  • Degree Lower: crab louse - parasitic louse infesting pubic hair (Pthirus pubis)

Etymology

From Latin: 'pediculus' (louse) and 'corporis' (of the body).
