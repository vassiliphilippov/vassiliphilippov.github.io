sublunary

1. ADJ

/ sʌbˈluːnɛri / (AmE), / sʌbˈluːnəri / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the earthly world rather than the heavenly or spiritual realm; earthly or mundane.
earthly; of this world
archaic

Examples

The philosopher contrasted eternal truths with sublunary existence.
He sought to escape the burdens of sublunary life through meditation.
Poets often lament the fleeting nature of sublunary pleasures.

Synset Examples

transcendental motives for sublunary actions
fleeting sublunary pleasures
the nearest to an angelic being that treads this terrestrial ball

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrestrial - of or relating to the earth
  • Antonym: celestial - relating to the sky or heavens

Etymology

From Medieval Latin sublunaris ‘below the moon’, from Latin sub- ‘under’ + luna ‘moon’.

2. ADJ

/ sʌbˈluːnɛri / (AmE), / sʌbˈljuːnəri / (BrE)
Situated between the earth and the moon; belonging to this world as opposed to the heavenly or celestial.
earthly; relating to this world
archaic

Examples

Philosophers once believed that sublunary matter was subject to decay, unlike the incorruptible heavens.
She found little comfort in sublunary pleasures, always yearning for something more transcendent.
The poet contrasted eternal truths with the fleeting concerns of our sublunary world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthly - relating to this world or mundane existence
  • Antonym: celestial - relating to the sky or outer space, especially as observed in astronomy

Etymology

From Medieval Latin sublunaris, from Latin sub- (under) + luna (moon).

Synset POS: a
