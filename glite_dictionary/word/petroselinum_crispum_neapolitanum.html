Petroselinum crispum neapolitanum

1. NOUN

/ pɛtɹəˈsɛlɪnəm ˈkrɪspəm ˌneɪəˌpɒlɪˈtɑːnəm / (AmE), / pɛtɾəˈselɪnəm ˈkrɪspəm ˌneɪəˌpɒlɪˈtænəm / (BrE)
a variety of parsley with flat leaves, commonly known as Italian parsley, used in cooking for its strong flavor
flat-leaf variety of parsley

Examples

Many chefs prefer Petroselinum crispum neapolitanum for its robust flavor in Mediterranean dishes.
You can substitute Petroselinum crispum neapolitanum for curly parsley in most recipes.
The market was out of Petroselinum crispum neapolitanum, so I used another herb instead.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: parsley - a common herb with bright green, feather-like leaves, used for garnish and flavoring

Etymology

From Latin: Petroselinum (parsley genus) + crispum (curled) + neapolitanum (of Naples), indicating its botanical origins.
