adequate

1. ADJ

/ ˈædɪkwət / (AmE), / ˈadɪkwət / (BrE)
enough in quantity or quality to meet the requirements of a situation or purpose
sufficient; enough for the purpose

Examples

His explanation was adequate to satisfy the committee's concerns.
We had adequate supplies for the entire trip, so no one went without.
The lighting in this room is just adequate for reading, but not for detailed work.

Synset Examples

an adequate income
the food was adequate
enough food
food enough

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sufficient - as much as is needed; adequate
  • Antonym: insufficient - not enough for the purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'adequatus', past participle of 'adaequare' (to make equal to), from 'ad-' (to) + 'aequare' (to make equal).

2. ADJ

/ ˈædɪkwət / (AmE), / ˈædɪkwət / (BrE)
satisfactory or acceptable in quality or quantity; enough to meet a particular need or requirement.
sufficient or acceptable; enough

Examples

The facilities at the hotel were adequate for our needs but nothing more.
Make sure you have adequate time to complete the assignment before the deadline.
His explanation was adequate, but it left some questions unanswered.

Synset Examples

her training was adequate

he was equal to the task

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inadequate - not sufficient or acceptable; lacking what is necessary
  • Synonym: sufficient - enough; as much as needed

Etymology

From Latin 'adaequatus', past participle of 'adaequare' (to make equal to).

3. ADJ

/ ˈædɪkwət / (AmE), / ˈadɪkwət / (BrE)
satisfactory or acceptable in quantity or quality; sufficient for a particular purpose but not outstanding.
satisfactory or sufficient; acceptable

Examples

The report provided adequate information for our decision-making process.
She made an adequate effort to complete the project on time.
His performance was adequate, but it did not exceed expectations.

Synset Examples

more than adequate as a secretary

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inadequate - not sufficient or not acceptable in amount or quality
  • Synonym: sufficient - enough; as much as needed

Etymology

From Latin 'adequatus', past participle of 'adaequare' (to make equal), from 'ad-' (to) + 'aequus' (equal).
