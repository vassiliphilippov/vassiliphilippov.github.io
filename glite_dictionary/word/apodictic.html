apodictic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæpəˈdɪktɪk / (AmE), / ˌapəˈdɪktɪk / (BrE)
(of a proposition or statement) necessarily true or logically certain; beyond dispute.
logically certain; necessarily true

Examples

The philosopher claimed that the law of non-contradiction is an apodictic truth.
Not all scientific theories can be considered apodictic, as many depend on empirical verification.
He distinguished between apodictic certainty and statements that are merely probable.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: assertoric - (of a statement) stating a fact or assertion without necessity or impossibility
  • Degree Lower: problematic - (of a proposition) not certain; open to doubt or questioning

Etymology

From Greek apodeiktikos (demonstrative), from apodeiknynai (to demonstrate).
