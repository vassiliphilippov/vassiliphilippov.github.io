Mirabilis jalapa

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈræbɪlɪs həˈlɑːpə / (AmE), / mɪˈræbɪlɪs jəˈlɑːpə / (BrE)
A garden plant native to tropical America, with fragrant flowers in various colors that open in the late afternoon, also known as four o'clock.
ornamental plant with fragrant evening flowers

Examples

We planted Mirabilis jalapa in our backyard to enjoy its fragrant blossoms every afternoon.
Children love watching the colorful flowers of Mirabilis jalapa open as the sun sets.
Gardeners often choose Mirabilis jalapa for its ability to attract butterflies and hummingbirds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: four o'clock - another common name for Mirabilis jalapa

Etymology

From Latin 'mirabilis' (wonderful) and 'jalapa', a region in Mexico.
