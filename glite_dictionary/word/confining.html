confining

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a space or situation) uncomfortably small, crowded, or restrictive; making one feel trapped or enclosed.
restrictive or cramped; crowded

Examples

She found the small windowless office rather confining after working in open spaces.
Many people consider airplane seats to be confining on long-haul flights.
The confining classroom left the students eager for recess outside.

Synset Examples

close quarters

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spacious - (of a room or other area) large and with plenty of space
  • Synonym: cramped - uncomfortably small or restricted

Etymology

From the verb 'confine', which comes from Latin 'confinare' (to border on, limit).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈfaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
restricting or limiting freedom, movement, or scope
restricting; limiting in scope or freedom

Examples

The prison cell was a confining space that left him feeling frustrated.
She found her new job environment too confining after years of working independently.
Wearing a tight cast can be quite confining and uncomfortable for some patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restrictive - limiting freedom or preventing something from increasing
  • Antonym: liberating - freeing from restraint or restriction

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'confine', from Latin 'confinare' (to border upon, limit).
