tumultuously

1. ADV

/ təˈmʌltʃuəsli / (AmE), / tjuːˈmʌltjʊəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is loud, chaotic, and full of disorder or excitement
in a loud and chaotic manner

Examples

The parade ended tumultuously as hundreds of people flooded the streets cheering and waving banners.
She burst into the room tumultuously, startling everyone with her excitement and loud laughter.
Protesters gathered tumultuously outside the city hall, demanding immediate government action.

Synset Examples

the crowd was demonstrating tumultuously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaotically - in a completely confused or disordered way
  • Antonym: calmly - in a peaceful and quiet way, without disturbance

Etymology

From Latin tumultuosus (full of uproar), from tumultus (uproar, commotion) + English adverbial suffix -ly.
