consecutive

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɛkjətɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsɛkjʊtɪv / (BrE)
Following continuously; in unbroken or logical sequence.
following in uninterrupted succession

Examples

She won three consecutive championships in a row.
The company posted profits for five consecutive years.
They worked for ten consecutive hours without a break.

Synset Examples

serial concerts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: successive - following in order or sequence

Etymology

From Latin consecutivus, from consequi (to follow closely).

WordNet ID: oewn-01671982-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ kənˈsɛkjətɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsɛkjʊtɪv / (BrE)
(of events, numbers, etc.) following one after another in unbroken sequence; successive without interruption.
successive; following one after another

Examples

She ran five consecutive marathons without a single day of rest.
The team won seven consecutive games, breaking their previous record.
It rained for three consecutive days during our holiday.

Synset Examples

sick for five straight days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: successive - following in order, especially without interruption
  • Antonym: intermittent - occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous

Etymology

From Latin consecutivus ‘following closely’, from consequi ‘to follow closely’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00599155-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ kənˈsɛkjətɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsɛkjʊtɪv / (BrE)
Following continuously; in unbroken or successive order.
in unbroken, successive order

Examples

The team won five consecutive games this season.
He worked for three consecutive weeks without a day off.
The festival has been held on consecutive weekends throughout the summer.

Synset Examples

back-to-back home runs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: successive - following one after the other without interruption
  • Antonym: simultaneous - occurring at the same time

Etymology

From Latin consecutivus, from consequi (to follow closely).
