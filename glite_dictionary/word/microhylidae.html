Microhylidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈhɪl.ɪ.diː / (AmE), / ˌmɪ.krəʊˈhɪl.ɪ.diː / (BrE)
a family of frogs known as narrow-mouthed toads and sheep frogs, some of which burrow and some are arboreal, found in various regions worldwide
a family of narrow-mouthed frogs

Examples

The Microhylidae family includes many species adapted to very different habitats.
Scientists recently discovered a new member of the Microhylidae in the Amazon rainforest.
Certain Microhylidae species are known for their burrowing behavior.

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) and 'hyle' (wood, forest), referring to their small size and woodland habitats.
