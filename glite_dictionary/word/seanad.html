Seanad

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈnɑd / (AmE), / ʃəˈnɑːd / (BrE)
the upper house of the parliament of the Republic of Ireland, officially known as Seanad Éireann
upper house of Irish parliament

Examples

The Seanad debated the proposed legislation for several hours.
Membership in the Seanad is not determined by direct public election.
He was appointed to the Seanad after a distinguished legal career.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Dáil - the lower house of the parliament of the Republic of Ireland
  • Synonym: Senate - an upper chamber in other legislative systems

Etymology

From Irish 'Seanad' meaning 'senate', related to the Old Irish 'senad' (assembly).
