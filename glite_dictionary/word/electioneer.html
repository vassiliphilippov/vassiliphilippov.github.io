to electioneer

1. VERB

/ ɪˌlɛkʃəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkʃəˈnɪə / (BrE)
to work actively for the election of someone to political office, especially by campaigning for a candidate or party
campaign for a candidate or party

Examples

Many volunteers electioneer in their local communities during major campaigns.
She spent the weekend electioneering for her favorite candidate.
As the mayoral race heated up, the rival parties electioneered door-to-door.

Synset Examples

My neighbors are busy electioneering during the Presidential election campaign

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaign - work in an organized way towards a particular political goal

Etymology

Formed in the United States in the late 18th century from 'election' + '-eer' (as in 'privateer').
