Pompey

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɑm.pi / (AmE), / ˈpɒm.pi / (BrE)
(106–48 BC) A prominent Roman general and statesman who was an ally and later rival of Julius Caesar, eventually murdered in Egypt.
Roman general and statesman (106–48 BC)

Examples

Pompey was once an ally of Julius Caesar before their bitter rivalry led to civil war.
Many historians regard Pompey as one of the most influential figures of the late Roman Republic.
After his defeat at the Battle of Pharsalus, Pompey sought refuge in Egypt, where he was ultimately killed.

Etymology

From Latin 'Pompeius', the family name of Gnaeus Pompeius Magnus.

2. PROPN

/ ˈpɒm.pi / (AmE), / ˈpɒm.pi / (BrE)
A nickname for Portsmouth, a port city in southern England, known as a major British naval base.
nickname for the city of Portsmouth, UK
british

Examples

Many locals proudly refer to Portsmouth as Pompey when discussing their hometown.
The Royal Navy has maintained a base at Pompey for centuries.
Football fans often chant songs about Pompey during the matches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Portsmouth - a port city in southern England, official name of 'Pompey'

Etymology

Nickname for Portsmouth; origin possibly from the French ship La Pompee or from HMS Pompey, but origin is uncertain.
