Fabaceae

1. NOUN

/ fəˈbeɪ.si.i / (AmE), / fəˈbeɪ.si.i / (BrE)
a large family of plants, including trees, shrubs, vines, and herbs, that produce bean pods and are commonly known as the legume or pea family
the legume or pea plant family

Examples

Many important food crops, such as soybeans and lentils, belong to the Fabaceae family.
The Fabaceae are easily recognized by their characteristic seed pods.
Botanists have classified thousands of species within the Fabaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legume - a plant of the pea family, or its edible seed

Etymology

From Latin *faba* (bean) with the family suffix *-aceae*.

2. NOUN

/ fəˈbeɪsi.i / (AmE), / fəˈbeɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, including trees, shrubs, vines, and herbs, known for producing bean pods; commonly called the legume or bean family
a plant family of legumes or beans

Examples

The Fabaceae family includes important crops such as peas, beans, and lentils.
Acacia trees, which belong to the Fabaceae, thrive in warm climates around the world.
Botanists have classified many nitrogen-fixing plants under the Fabaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Leguminosae - alternative name for the Fabaceae family

Etymology

From Latin 'Faba' (bean) + botanical suffix '-aceae'.
