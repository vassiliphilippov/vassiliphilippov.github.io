XC

1. NOUN

/ ɛks ˈsiː / (AmE), / ɛks ˈsiː / (BrE)
the Roman numeral representing the number 90
Roman numeral for 90

Examples

The Roman numerals on the clock include XC for ninety.
His collection of antique coins featured one marked with XC.
The inscription reads 'Anno Domini XC,' denoting the year 90.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: 90 - the number ninety

Etymology

From Latin numerals: X (10) placed before C (100) to indicate subtraction (100−10 = 90).

2. NUM

/ ɛks si / (AmE), / ɛks si / (BrE)
the Roman numeral for the number ninety (90)
Roman numeral for 90

Examples

The clock face was marked with XC, indicating the ninety-minute position.
In Roman numerals, seventy-five is LXXV while ninety is XC.
The anniversary celebrated its XC year with a grand ceremony.

Etymology

From Latin numerals, where 'X' is ten and 'C' is one hundred; 'XC' means ten less than one hundred.

Synset POS: s
