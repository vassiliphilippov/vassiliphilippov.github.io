Irvingia

1. NOUN

/ ɜːrˈvɪndʒiə / (AmE), / ɜːˈvɪndɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical African and Southeast Asian trees whose edible fruit is commonly known as wild mango.
a genus of tropical trees producing wild mango

Examples

The seeds of the Irvingia tree are often used to thicken traditional West African soups.
Botanists study Irvingia for its nutritional properties and economic importance.
Wild mangoes, or Irvingia, are harvested from forests during the rainy season.

Etymology

Named after Scottish surgeon and plant collector Edward George Irving.

2. NOUN

/ ɜːrˈvɪŋɡiə / (AmE), / ɜːˈvɪŋɡɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical African and Asian trees known as wild mango, valued for their edible fruit and seeds.
genus of wild mango trees

Examples

The seeds of Irvingia trees are widely used in West African cuisine.
Researchers are studying Irvingia for its potential health benefits.
The fruit of Irvingia, known as wild mango, is both nutritious and tasty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild mango - common name for the fruit of Irvingia species

Etymology

Named after the Scottish naval surgeon Edward George Irving.
