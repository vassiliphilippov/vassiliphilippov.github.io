Caprifoliaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæprɪˌfoʊliˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌkæprɪfəʊlɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of shrubs, small trees, and woody vines, commonly known as the honeysuckle family
plant family: the honeysuckles

Examples

Many ornamental plants belong to the Caprifoliaceae family, including honeysuckles and viburnums.
The Caprifoliaceae are found throughout the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
Botanists debated the classification of Caprifoliaceae due to its diversity of flowering shrubs.

Etymology

From Latin *caprifolium* (honeysuckle) + -aceae (family suffix in botanical Latin).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæprəˌfoʊliˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌkæprɪfəʊliˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of shrubs, small trees, and woody vines commonly known as the honeysuckle family, including honeysuckles and related plants.
honeysuckle plant family

Examples

Many gardeners cultivate members of the Caprifoliaceae for their fragrant flowers.
The Caprifoliaceae family includes both ornamental and wild species of honeysuckle.
Plants in the Caprifoliaceae are often appreciated for their climbing growth habit.

Etymology

From Latin 'caprifolium' (honeysuckle) + '-aceae', a standard botanical suffix for plant families.
