possum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːsəm / (AmE), / ˈpɒsəm / (BrE)
A small, furry Australian marsupial that lives in trees and has a long, usually prehensile tail.
small Australian tree-dwelling marsupial

Examples

A possum climbed across the power lines into the neighbor's yard last night.
The Australian possum uses its prehensile tail to grasp tree branches.
We installed a special box in the garden to provide shelter for the local possums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opossum - small to medium-sized marsupial found in the Americas

Etymology

Shortened from 'opossum', originally from the Powhatan language *apasum* meaning 'white animal'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːsəm / (AmE), / ˈpɒsəm / (BrE)
A small, nocturnal, tree-dwelling marsupial native to the Americas, known for its prehensile tail and ability to play dead.
nocturnal tree-dwelling marsupial of the Americas

Examples

We saw a possum climbing the fence last night.
When threatened, a possum may play dead to avoid predators.
I found a family of possums living in the old tree in our backyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opossum - a marsupial of the Americas known for feigning death

Etymology

Shortened form of 'opossum', from Powhatan 'aposoum' (white animal).
