torr

1. NOUN

/ tɔr / (AmE), / tɔː / (BrE)
a unit of pressure equal to 1/760 of a standard atmosphere, commonly used in scientific measurement
unit of pressure (1/760 atmosphere)

Examples

The vacuum chamber was maintained at a pressure of 0.5 torr during the experiment.
Pressures below 1 torr are typically measured with a special gauge.
One torr is approximately equal to 133.3 pascals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: atmosphere - unit of pressure equal to the air pressure at sea level

Etymology

Named after Evangelista Torricelli (1608–1647), Italian physicist and mathematician.
