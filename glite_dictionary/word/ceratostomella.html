Ceratostomella

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛrətəˌstoʊˈmɛlə / (AmE), / ˌsɛrətəˌstɒˈmɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi characterized by forming continuous hyaline spores.
genus of fungi forming hyaline spores

Examples

Some species of Ceratostomella are important in forest ecology as wood decomposers.
The research focused on identifying new strains within the genus Ceratostomella.
Microscopic examination revealed hyaline spores typical of Ceratostomella.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, incorporating Greek 'keras' (horn) and 'stoma' (mouth), with the diminutive suffix '-ella'.
