Depression

1. NOUN

/ dəˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
The severe worldwide economic downturn that occurred during the 1930s, marked by widespread unemployment and hardship; often referred to as the Great Depression.
worldwide economic crisis of the 1930s

Examples

Many families lost their homes during the Depression of the 1930s.
The Depression triggered significant changes in government economic policy.
He grew up hearing stories about how his grandparents struggled in the Depression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Great Depression - the period of worldwide economic crisis in the 1930s
  • Degree Lower: recession - a period of temporary economic decline
  • Antonym: boom - a period of rapid economic growth
