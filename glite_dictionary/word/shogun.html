shogun

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊ.ɡən / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊ.ɡʌn / (BrE)
a hereditary military ruler of Japan who exercised ultimate power, especially from the 12th to the 19th century.
hereditary military ruler of Japan

Examples

The last shogun of Japan resigned in 1867, marking the end of the era.
During the Edo period, the shogun held more political power than the emperor.
Many castles in Japan were built on the orders of powerful shoguns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: samurai - a member of the Japanese warrior class

Etymology

From Japanese 将軍 (shōgun), literally 'military commander'.
