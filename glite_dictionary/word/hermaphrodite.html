hermaphrodite

1. ADJ

/ hərˈmæf.rəˌdaɪt / (AmE), / hɜːˈmæf.rə.daɪt / (BrE)
(of an animal or plant) having both male and female reproductive organs.
having both male and female reproductive organs

Examples

Many earthworms are hermaphrodite and can fertilize each other during mating.
The hermaphrodite flowers of the plant allow it to self-pollinate.
Some snails are hermaphrodite, possessing both ovaries and testes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unisexual - having only one sex or reproductive organ

Etymology

From Greek Hermaphroditos, mythological figure with traits of both sexes.

2. NOUN

/ hərˈmæfɹəˌdaɪt / (AmE), / hɜːˈmæf.rə.daɪt / (BrE)
a person, animal, or plant that has both male and female reproductive organs or sexual characteristics
organism with both male and female sexual traits
medical

Examples

The rare flower is a hermaphrodite with both stamens and pistils in the same blossom.
Historically, some animals were mistakenly classified as hermaphrodites due to ambiguous physical traits.
In literature, the hermaphrodite often symbolizes a union of opposites.

Etymology

From Greek Hermaphroditos, the child of Hermes and Aphrodite, believed to embody both sexes.
