scraggly

1. ADJ

/ ˈskræɡli / (AmE), / ˈskræɡli / (BrE)
thin, uneven, or sparse in appearance, often in an untidy or irregular way
thin and uneven in appearance

Examples

The old man’s scraggly beard made him look much older than he was.
A scraggly dog wandered through the alley, its ribs showing under matted fur.
Her scraggly hair fell across her face as she worked in the garden.

Synset Examples

a child with skinny freckled legs
a long scrawny neck
pale bony hands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrawny - very thin and bony
  • Synonym: skinny - very thin, especially unattractively so
  • Synonym: bony - very thin so that bones show

2. ADJ

/ ˈskræɡli / (AmE), / ˈskræɡli / (BrE)
thin, uneven, or untidy in appearance, often with a rough or irregular outline
untidy or irregular in appearance

Examples

The cat had a scraggly tail and looked like it hadn't eaten in days.
He wore a scraggly scarf that was unraveling at the edges.
A scraggly hedge bordered the neglected front yard.

Synset Examples

the old man's scraggly beard
a scraggly little path to the door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ragged - having a rough or uneven edge or surface; untidy in appearance
  • Antonym: neat - having a tidy and orderly appearance
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02435043-s
Synset POS: s
