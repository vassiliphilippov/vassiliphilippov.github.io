pest

1. NOUN

/ pɛst / (AmE), / pɛst / (BrE)
any epidemic disease characterized by a high death rate, especially historically significant contagious illnesses such as the plague
epidemic disease with high death rate

Examples

The village was devastated by a pest that claimed hundreds of lives.
During the Middle Ages, many communities were afflicted by a deadly pest.
Some historians believe the sudden decline was caused by an unknown pest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plague - a serious infectious disease that spreads quickly and kills many people
  • Synonym: epidemic - widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community at a particular time

Etymology

From Latin *pestis* (plague, pestilence).

2. NOUN

/ pɛst / (AmE), / pɛst / (BrE)
a person who is persistently annoying or bothersome
annoying or bothersome person

Examples

Stop being such a pest and let your sister do her homework.
The little boy was a real pest during the long car ride.
I wish you wouldn't call during dinner; you're becoming a bit of a pest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuisance - a person or thing causing inconvenience or annoyance
  • Synonym: menace - a person or thing likely to cause harm or annoyance

Etymology

From Latin pestis (plague, pestilence).

3. NOUN

/ pɛst / (AmE), / pɛst / (BrE)
an unwanted and destructive insect or small animal that attacks food, crops, or livestock
insect or animal harming crops or food

Examples

The farmer used traps to keep pests away from his vegetables.
Fruit pests can destroy entire orchards if left unchecked.
Some pests are becoming resistant to commonly used chemicals.

Synset Examples

he sprayed the garden to get rid of pests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vermin - harmful or objectionable animals, especially of small size

Etymology

From Latin pestis (plague).
