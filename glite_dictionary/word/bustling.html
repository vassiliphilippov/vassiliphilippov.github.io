bustling

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʌs.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌs.lɪŋ / (BrE)
full of energetic and noisy activity; lively and busy
lively and busy; full of activity

Examples

The bustling marketplace was filled with shoppers, street vendors, and the sounds of music.
She loved the bustling energy of the city, especially during the festival season.
The cafe, usually quiet in the mornings, was bustling with activity today.

Synset Examples

a bustling city

Related Terms

  • Synonym: busy - being full of activity or work
  • Antonym: quiet - making little or no noise; not busy

Etymology

Present participle of 'bustle', possibly from Middle English bustelen (to move or stir quickly).
