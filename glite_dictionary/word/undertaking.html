undertaking

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndərˈteɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈteɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the business or occupation of preparing dead bodies for burial and arranging funerals; the work of a funeral director
funeral directing as a profession

Examples

He took over the family undertaking business after his father's retirement.
For generations, their undertaking firm has served the local community.
She studied mortuary science before entering the field of undertaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funeral director - a person whose profession is to arrange and manage funerals

Etymology

From undertake + -ing; in this sense, referring to the person who 'undertakes' funeral arrangements.

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌndərˈteɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈteɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
a task, project, or piece of work, especially one that is important or difficult
important task or project

Examples

Building the bridge was a major undertaking that required hundreds of workers.
She considered moving abroad to be an exciting undertaking.
Starting your own business is a challenging undertaking that demands dedication and hard work.

Synset Examples

he prepared for great undertakings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: project - a planned piece of work with a specific aim
  • Synonym: enterprise - a difficult or important project or undertaking
  • Degree Lower: chore - a routine or minor task or job

Etymology

From undertake + -ing; originally Middle English, meaning an act of taking upon oneself.
