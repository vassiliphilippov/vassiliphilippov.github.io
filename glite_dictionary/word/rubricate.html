to rubricate

1. VERB

/ ˈruː.brɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈruː.brɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
to decorate a manuscript or text with letters or markings in red, especially for emphasis or headings
decorate with red letters, esp. in manuscripts

Examples

The monks would carefully rubricate the initial letters to highlight important passages.
It was common practice to rubricate headings in medieval manuscripts for visual emphasis.
They decided to rubricate each chapter title in bright red ink for authenticity.

Synset Examples

In this beautiful book, all the place names are rubricated

Etymology

From Latin 'rubricatus', past participle of 'rubricare', from 'rubrica' (red ochre, red lettering).

2. VERB

/ ˈruː.brɪ.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈruː.brɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
(in ecclesiastical use) to designate as a red-letter day in the church calendar to honor a saint.
make a day a church feast

Examples

The pope decided to rubricate the anniversary as a special feast day.
Several early martyrs were formally rubricated in the church's calendar of saints.
Each time a new saint is canonized, the Church may rubricate their feast in the liturgical year.

Synset Examples

She was rubricated by the pope

Etymology

From Latin 'rubricatus', past participle of 'rubricare' (to color red), from 'rubrica' (red ochre, red letter).

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈruːbrɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈruːbrɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to mark or decorate (a manuscript) with rubrics; to supply with headings, directions, or instructions, often in red ink
to decorate a manuscript with rubrics

Examples

The medieval monks would often rubricate the initial letters to highlight important passages.
It's rare to find a modern publisher who will rubricate chapters in red ink.
The scribe rubricated the manuscript's first page, making it stand out beautifully.

Synset Examples

the manuscript is not rubricated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to illuminate - decorate (a manuscript) with gold, silver, or elaborate designs

Etymology

From Latin rubricatus, past participle of rubricare, from rubrica (red ochre, heading in red).
