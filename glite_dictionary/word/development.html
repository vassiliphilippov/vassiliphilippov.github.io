8. NOUN

/ dɪˈvɛləpmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈvɛləpmənt / (BrE)
(music) The section of a musical composition, especially in sonata form, where the main themes are expanded and elaborated.

Examples

The best part of the symphony is the development, where the composer plays creatively with the main themes.
During the development section, the motif is explored in different keys and variations.
In sonata form, the development often contains intense modulation and thematic transformation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: recapitulation - the section in sonata form following the development, restating main themes
  • Degree Lower: exposition - the initial section in sonata form that presents main themes
