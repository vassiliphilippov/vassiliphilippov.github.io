Tanacetum ptarmiciflorum

1. NOUN

/ təˈnæs ɪtəm tɑːrˌmɪs əˈflɔːr əm / (AmE), / təˈnæs ɪtəm tɑːˌmɪs ɪˈflɔːr əm / (BrE)
A shrubby perennial plant native to the Canary Islands, characterized by white flowers, hairy stems, and dustlike down; sometimes classified in the genus Chrysanthemum.
plant of the Canary Islands with white flowers and hairy stems

Examples

Tanacetum ptarmiciflorum is cultivated in gardens for its attractive silver foliage.
Botanists recently confirmed that Tanacetum ptarmiciflorum is native to the Canary Islands.
The stems of Tanacetum ptarmiciflorum are notable for their dustlike down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chrysanthemum - a related genus of flowering plants

Etymology

From Latin genus Tanacetum and species epithet ptarmiciflorum, referring to 'sneeze-inducing flowers'.
