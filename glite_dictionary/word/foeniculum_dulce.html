Foeniculum dulce

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈniːkjʊləm ˈdʊlseɪ / (AmE), / fəʊˈniːkjʊləm ˈdʊlseɪ / (BrE)
A variety of fennel cultivated for its edible, aromatic, bulb-like stem that is used as a vegetable.
a variety of fennel with an edible bulb

Examples

The chef added finely sliced Foeniculum dulce to the salad for a fresh, anise-like flavor.
Farmers in Italy have cultivated Foeniculum dulce for centuries due to its unique taste and texture.
Grilled Foeniculum dulce pairs well with citrus and seafood dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fennel - an aromatic Mediterranean plant cultivated for its feathery leaves and seeds

Etymology

From Latin: 'foeniculum' (fennel) + 'dulce' (sweet).
