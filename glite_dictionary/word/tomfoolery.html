tomfoolery

1. NOUN

/ tɑmˈfuːlɚi / (AmE), / tɒmˈfuːləri / (BrE)
foolish or silly behavior; playful or mischievous conduct that lacks seriousness
foolish or playful behavior

Examples

The children’s constant tomfoolery made it hard for the teacher to regain control of the classroom.
After a few minutes of tomfoolery, they finally got down to business.
There will be no more tomfoolery in this meeting; let’s focus on the agenda.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgement; silly behavior
  • Synonym: horseplay - rough or boisterous play; mischievous behavior
  • Antonym: seriousness - acting or speaking sincerely and in a thoughtful way; not playful

Etymology

From 'Tom Fool', an archetypal name for a fool in English popular tradition, combined with the suffix '-ery'.
