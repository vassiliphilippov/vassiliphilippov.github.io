fiasco

1. NOUN

/ fiˈæˌskoʊ / (AmE), / fiˈæskəʊ / (BrE)
a complete and humiliating failure, especially one that is sudden and disastrous
sudden, disastrous failure

Examples

The new product launch turned into a complete fiasco after multiple technical failures.
His attempt to fix the plumbing was a fiasco that flooded the entire basement.
Critics called the event a fiasco due to poor organization and low attendance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disaster - an event causing great damage or failure
  • Antonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose

Etymology

From Italian 'fiasco' (flask, bottle), used in the phrase 'fare fiasco' (to make a bottle), meaning to fail.
