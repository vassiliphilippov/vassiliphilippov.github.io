butyric

1. ADJ

/ bjuˈtɪrɪk / (AmE), / bjuːˈtɪrɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or producing butyric acid, a fatty acid found in butter and some other substances.
relating to butyric acid
medical

Examples

The distinctive smell of rancid butter is due to butyric acid.
Certain bacteria in the gut are capable of butyric fermentation.
The chemical properties of butyric compounds are studied in organic chemistry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butyric acid - a fatty acid (C4H8O2) found in butter, body odor, and other substances

Etymology

From Latin 'butyrum' (butter), relating to butyric acid first found in butter.
