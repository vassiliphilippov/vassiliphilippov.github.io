reign

1. NOUN

/ reɪn / (AmE), / reɪn / (BrE)
the period of time during which a king, queen, or other monarch rules a country or territory
period a monarch rules

Examples

Queen Victoria's reign saw significant changes in British society.
Historians often debate the impact of his reign on the nation.
Monuments from that reign can still be seen throughout the city.

Synset Examples

during the reign of Henry VIII

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rule - the period during which someone governs or is in charge
  • Antonym: interregnum - a period when normal government is suspended between successive reigns

Etymology

From Latin 'regnum' (kingship, reign), from 'rex' (king), via Old French 'reign'/'reigne'.

2. NOUN

/ reɪn / (AmE), / reɪn / (BrE)
A period during which a person, organization, or thing is dominant or has a prevailing influence.
period of dominance or prevailing influence

Examples

The company's reign as market leader lasted for over a decade.
Many historians debate the reign of terror during that political era.
During her reign as editor, the magazine became highly influential.

Synset Examples

he was helpless under the reign of his egotism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rule - period or act of governing or controlling
  • Synonym: domination - the exercise of control or influence over someone or something

Etymology

From Old French *reign*, from Latin *regnum* (kingdom, rule), from *rex* (king).

3. NOUN

/ reɪn / (AmE), / reɪn / (BrE)
the period during which a sovereign rules or royal authority is held by a monarch
period of rule by a monarch

Examples

Queen Victoria's reign lasted for more than sixty years.
During his reign, the country experienced significant social change.
The castle was built during the reign of Henry II.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rule - the exercise of authority or control
  • Antonym: interregnum - a period when normal government is suspended, especially between successive reigns or regimes

Etymology

From Old French 'reign', from Latin 'regnum' (kingdom, rule), from 'rex' (king).

4. VERB

/ reɪn / (AmE), / reɪn / (BrE)
to be the most important or powerful influence in a particular place or situation; to predominate
be dominant or most powerful

Examples

In this industry, caution tends to reign over innovation.
Tradition still reigns in this rural community.
In many households, chaos reigned during the holiday season.

Synset Examples

Money reigns supreme here
Hispanics predominate in this neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to predominate - to be the most common or powerful
  • Synonym: to dominate - to have control or power over
  • Antonym: wane - to decrease in power or importance

Etymology

From Latin regnare (to rule), from regnum (kingdom), related to rex (king).

5. VERB

/ reɪn / (AmE), / reɪn / (BrE)
to rule as a monarch or sovereign, especially for a specified period
to rule as monarch or sovereign

Examples

Queen Elizabeth II reigned over the United Kingdom for more than seventy years.
Many believe that a just and fair king should reign with wisdom and compassion.
For centuries, emperors reigned over vast empires stretching across continents.

Synset Examples

Henry VIII reigned for a long time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rule - to exercise control or authority over a country or people
  • Antonym: to abdicate - to give up the throne or sovereign power

Etymology

From Old French 'reignier', from Latin 'regnare' (to rule).
