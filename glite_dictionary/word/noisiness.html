noisiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔɪzinəs / (AmE), / ˈnɔɪzinɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being noisy; a condition characterized by loud or persistent sounds
state of being noisy

Examples

The noisiness of the construction site made it hard to concentrate.
She complained about the noisiness of the children in the next room.
Despite the noisiness of the party, I managed to fall asleep.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: quietness - the state of being quiet or free from noise
  • Synonym: noise - a sound, especially one that is loud or unpleasant

Etymology

From 'noisy' + '-ness', where 'noisy' comes from noise (ultimately from Old French 'noise').
