divi-divi

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪviˈdiːvi / (AmE), / ˌdɪviˈdiːvi / (BrE)
the twisted seed pods of the divi-divi tree, used as a source of tannin
twisted seed pods from the divi-divi tree

Examples

The local tanneries use divi-divi to process leather due to its high tannin content.
She collected divi-divi pods on the beach for her natural dye project.
The twisted shape of the divi-divi makes it easily recognizable among other seed pods.

Etymology

From Spanish 'dividivi', from a Cariban language of the region.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdiːviːˈdiːviː / (AmE), / ˌdiːviːˈdiːviː / (BrE)
a small thornless tree or shrub (Caesalpinia coriaria) native to tropical America, whose curved seed pods are a source of tannin
tropical American tree with tannin-rich pods

Examples

The divi-divi tree is easily recognized by its distinctive, wind-shaped appearance on Caribbean islands.
Tannins extracted from the seed pods of divi-divi are used in leather production.
On the beach, the shade from a divi-divi provided a cool spot to rest.

Etymology

From Spanish 'dividivi', probably of indigenous Caribbean origin.
