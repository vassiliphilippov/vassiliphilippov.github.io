inhabitable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈhæbɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈhæbɪtəbəl / (BrE)
suitable to be lived in; fit for habitation.
fit to be lived in

Examples

Archaeologists discovered the island had once been inhabitable before rising sea levels flooded it.
The company assessed whether the old building was still inhabitable after the earthquake.
After years of neglect, the house was finally repaired and made inhabitable again.

Synset Examples

the habitable world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habitable - suitable or good enough to live in
  • Antonym: uninhabitable - not suitable to live in

Etymology

From Latin inhabitabĭlis (that may be inhabited), from inhabitare (to inhabit).
