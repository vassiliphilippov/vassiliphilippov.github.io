nemertine

1. NOUN

/ nəˈmɝːtin / (AmE), / nɪˈmɜːtiːn / (BrE)
any of a group of soft, unsegmented marine worms with a long proboscis and the ability to stretch and contract; member of the phylum Nemertea.
soft unsegmented marine worm (phylum Nemertea)

Examples

During the marine survey, researchers found several nemertines hiding under rocks.
The nemertine's proboscis can extend rapidly to catch prey.
Unlike segmented worms, a nemertine has a smooth, ribbon-like body.

Etymology

From modern Latin Nemertina (plural), the taxonomic name, based on Nemertes, a sea nymph in Greek mythology.
