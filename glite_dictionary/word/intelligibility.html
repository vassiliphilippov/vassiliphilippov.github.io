intelligibility

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɛlədʒəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɛlɪdʒɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being clear enough to be understood; comprehensibility, especially in spoken or written language
the quality of being understood

Examples

The intelligibility of his speech improved after several weeks of therapy.
Noise from the street reduced the intelligibility of the teacher's voice.
Technical jargon can hinder the intelligibility of scientific papers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comprehensibility - the quality of being able to be understood
  • Antonym: unintelligibility - the quality of being impossible or difficult to understand

Etymology

From Medieval Latin intelligibilitas, from Latin intelligibilis (intelligible).
