boldness

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊld.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊld.nəs / (BrE)
the willingness to take risks and face danger; courage or daring in action
willingness to take risks; courage

Examples

Her boldness in speaking up impressed the entire committee.
You need a certain boldness to try skydiving for the first time.
The boldness of his plan surprised everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

the proposal required great boldness
the plan required great hardiness of heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courage - the ability to do something that frightens one; bravery
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence; shyness

Etymology

From Old English *beald* (bold, brave) with the suffix *-ness* (state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊld.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊld.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being strikingly distinct or noticeable
quality of being noticeable or distinct

Examples

The boldness of her design made the building stand out in the city skyline.
He admired the boldness with which the artist used color and shape.
The boldness of the pattern made the dress an immediate conversation piece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prominence - the state of being important or famous
  • Antonym: subtlety - the quality of being subtle or not obvious

Etymology

From Old English *beald* (bold) + -ness.

3. NOUN

/ ˈboʊldnəs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊldnəs / (BrE)
Rude or disrespectful confidence; impudent aggressiveness.
impudent aggressiveness; disrespectful confidence

Examples

Her boldness in questioning the manager surprised everyone in the meeting.
I was shocked by his boldness when he interrupted the speaker.
You have to admire the boldness with which she challenged authority, even if it was inappropriate.

Synset Examples

I couldn't believe her boldness
he had the cheek to question my honesty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheek - rude or disrespectful behavior; impudence
  • Synonym: audacity - willingness to take bold risks, often with shockingly rude behavior
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence; shyness

Etymology

From 'bold' + '-ness'; 'bold' from Old English 'beald' (brave, confident).
