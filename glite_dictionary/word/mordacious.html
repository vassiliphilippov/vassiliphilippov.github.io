mordacious

1. ADJ

/ mɔrˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / mɔːˈdeɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) sharply critical or biting; caustic in tone.
sharply critical; biting

Examples

Her mordacious wit left her colleagues at a loss for words.
The article was filled with mordacious remarks about the political system.
His review was so mordacious that the author refused to comment.

Synset Examples

a barbed compliment
a biting aphorism
pungent satire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biting - sharply or cruelly critical speech or writing
  • Synonym: caustic - severely critical, especially in speech or writing
  • Antonym: gentle - kind or mild in tone

Etymology

From Latin mordax, mordac- (from mordere ‘to bite’) + -ious.

2. ADJ

/ mɔːrˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / mɔːˈdeɪ.ʃəs / (BrE)
having a tendency to bite; biting or sharp in manner or style
tending to bite; biting or sharp

Examples

The horse turned suddenly unruly and mordacious, making it difficult to control.
Her mordacious wit left everyone slightly unsettled at the dinner table.
Some animals become more mordacious when they feel threatened.

Synset Examples

they deliberately gave me a skittish and mordacious mount

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcastic - using irony to mock or convey contempt
  • Synonym: biting - harsh or cutting (of remarks); also, inclined to bite

Etymology

From Latin mordax, mordac- (biting), from mordere (to bite).
