bromide

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊˌmaɪd / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.maɪd / (BrE)
a statement or idea that is unoriginal, boring, or obvious, often expressed as a cliché
a trite or obvious remark; cliché

Examples

The manager's speech was full of bromides rather than practical advice.
He offered the usual bromide about success coming to those who work hard.
Tired of hearing the same old bromides, the team wanted real solutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cliché - an overused phrase or idea

Etymology

Originally from chemical compounds of bromine, once used as sedatives; figurative sense evolved as 'something dull or soothing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊ.maɪd / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.maɪd / (BrE)
any of the salts of hydrobromic acid, especially those once used as sedatives
salt of hydrobromic acid; old sedative drug

Examples

Potassium bromide was once widely prescribed as a sedative in the 19th century.
The laboratory ordered a sample of sodium bromide for chemical analysis.
Medical professionals now use safer alternatives instead of bromide to treat anxiety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedative - a substance that calms or induces sleep

Etymology

From bromine + -ide, indicating a compound of bromine.
