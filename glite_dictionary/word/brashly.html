brashly

1. ADV

/ ˈbræʃli / (AmE), / ˈbræʃli / (BrE)
In a bold, rude, or impudent manner; showing a lack of respect or caution.
in a bold or impudent manner

Examples

She brashly challenged the manager's decision in front of the entire team.
The candidate brashly promised to solve all the country's problems in a year.
He brashly interrupted the speaker without waiting for his turn.

Synset Examples

brashly, she asked for a rebate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boldly - in a confident and courageous way
  • Antonym: cautiously - in a careful and wary way

Etymology

From 'brash' + '-ly'. 'Brash' is of uncertain origin, first recorded in English in the 19th century.
