circumstance

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.kəm.stæns / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kəm.stɑːns / (BrE)
A fact or condition connected with and affecting an event or situation.
fact or condition affecting an event

Examples

She handled the difficult circumstance with great composure.
The project was cancelled due to unforeseen circumstances.
Each circumstance must be carefully considered before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condition - a situation or state affecting something

Etymology

From Latin circumstantia (surrounding condition), from circumstare (to stand around).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrkəmˌstæns / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəmstəns / (BrE)
a fact or condition connected with an event or action, especially something that influences a decision
factor or condition affecting a decision

Examples

You should take every circumstance into account when planning the trip.
Financial circumstances often play a role in choosing a college.
The judge considered the unique circumstance of the case before making a ruling.

Synset Examples

another consideration is the time it would take

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consideration - a factor to be thought about when making a decision
  • Synonym: factor - an element that contributes to a result or situation

Etymology

From Latin 'circumstantia' (surrounding condition), from 'circumstare' (stand around).
