yellowwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊˌwʊd / (BrE)
any of several trees with yellowish wood or that produce a yellow dye or extract, especially species in Cladrastis or Podocarpus.
tree with yellowish wood or dye

Examples

The yellowwood tree is prized for its strong, yellow-hued timber.
In autumn, the leaves of the yellowwood turn a brilliant gold color.
Some craftspeople use yellowwood bark to create natural dyes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊˌwʊd / (BrE)
the yellow-colored wood obtained from any of various yellowwood trees, used in carpentry and woodworking
yellow wood from certain trees

Examples

Furniture made from yellowwood is valued for its durability and distinct color.
The craftsman selected a plank of yellowwood for the tabletop, appreciating its bright hue.
Some antique cabinets feature panels of yellowwood that have deepened to a rich gold with age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellowwood tree - any of several trees that produce yellow-colored wood
