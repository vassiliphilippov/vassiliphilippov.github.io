Cabernet

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæbərˈneɪ / (AmE), / ˌkæbəˈneɪ / (BrE)
A high-quality red wine made from the Cabernet grape, especially varietals such as Cabernet Sauvignon or Cabernet Franc.
type of red wine grape/wine

Examples

We ordered a bottle of Cabernet to go with our steak dinner.
His favorite wine is a robust Cabernet from California's Napa Valley.
At the tasting, she compared Merlot and Cabernet to see which paired better with cheese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cabernet Sauvignon - a full-bodied red wine variety made from Cabernet Sauvignon grapes
  • Synonym: Cabernet Franc - a red grape variety and the wine made from it, closely related to Cabernet Sauvignon
  • Synonym: Merlot - a major red wine grape variety, often blended with Cabernet

Etymology

From French 'cabernet', the name of the grape variety.
