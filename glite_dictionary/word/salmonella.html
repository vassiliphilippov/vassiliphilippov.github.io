Salmonella

1. NOUN

/ ˌsælməˈnɛlə / (AmE), / ˌsælməˈnɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of rod-shaped Gram-negative bacteria that includes pathogens responsible for illnesses such as food poisoning and typhoid fever.
genus of rod-shaped bacteria causing foodborne illnesses
medical

Examples

Salmonella infections are a common cause of foodborne illness worldwide.
The laboratory confirmed the presence of Salmonella in the chicken sample.
Proper handling and cooking of poultry can help prevent Salmonella contamination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Escherichia - genus of rod-shaped bacteria including E. coli
  • Synonym: Staphylococcus - genus of spherical Gram-positive bacteria

Etymology

Named after American veterinary surgeon Daniel E. Salmon (1850–1914).
