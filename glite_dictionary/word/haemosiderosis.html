haemosiderosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmoʊˌsɪdəˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊsɪdəˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by abnormal deposits of hemosiderin in body tissues, often associated with diseases like thalassemia or hemochromatosis.
abnormal deposit of hemosiderin in tissues
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with haemosiderosis after noticing iron deposits in his liver scans.
Chronic blood transfusions can sometimes lead to secondary haemosiderosis in patients.
Researchers are studying new treatments to prevent organ damage caused by haemosiderosis.

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + sideros (iron) + -osis (condition).
