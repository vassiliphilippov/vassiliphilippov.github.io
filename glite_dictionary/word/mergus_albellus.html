Mergus albellus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrɡəs ælˈbɛləs / (AmE), / ˈmɜːɡəs ælˈbɛləs / (BrE)
A small species of merganser, commonly known as the smew, found in northern Eurasia and noted as an expert diver.
species of small merganser (smew)

Examples

The Mergus albellus is easily recognized by the striking plumage of the male.
Winter is the best season to observe Mergus albellus on northern European lakes.
Ornithologists often study the diving behavior and habitat of Mergus albellus during migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smew - common name for Mergus albellus, a small diving duck

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Mergus' (diver), species epithet 'albellus' (whitish, diminutive of 'albus', white).
