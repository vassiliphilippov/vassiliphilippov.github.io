depraved

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈpreɪvd / (AmE), / dɪˈpreɪvd / (BrE)
Morally corrupt; wicked or perverted.
morally corrupt or wicked

Examples

The novel's antagonist is portrayed as truly depraved, capable of unimaginable cruelty.
Many viewed the dictator's actions as depraved beyond comprehension.
The journalist exposed the depraved activities taking place within the organization.

Synset Examples

depraved criminals
a perverted sense of loyalty
the reprobate conduct of a gambling aristocrat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrupt - having or showing a willingness to act dishonestly for personal gain
  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Latin 'depravare' (to pervert, corrupt).
