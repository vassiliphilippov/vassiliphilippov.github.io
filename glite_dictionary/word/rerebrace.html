rerebrace

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪrˌbreɪs / (AmE), / ˈrɪəˌbreɪs / (BrE)
A piece of plate armor designed to protect the upper arm, worn especially in medieval times.
upper-arm plate armor piece
archaic

Examples

The knight's rerebrace was finely engraved with his family crest.
During the reenactment, he carefully adjusted the rerebrace to protect his upper arm.
Museum exhibits often display a complete set of armor, including the helmet, cuirass, and rerebrace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vambrace - piece of plate armor for the forearm
  • Synonym: pauldron - plate armor for the shoulder

Etymology

From Middle English rerebrase, from Old French rere (back) + brace (arm).
