Trigonella

1. PROPN

/ trɪɡəˈnɛlə / (AmE), / trɪɡəˈnɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of frequently aromatic herbs native to the Old World, including species such as fenugreek.
genus of aromatic Old World herbs

Examples

Trigonella is best known for the species fenugreek, used as both a spice and a medicinal plant.
Some species in the Trigonella genus have been studied for their nutritional and medicinal properties.
The botanist sorted the specimens into the Trigonella category based on their distinctive seed pods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fenugreek - an aromatic annual herb (Trigonella foenum-graecum) of the Trigonella genus

Etymology

From Latin 'trigonella', diminutive of Greek 'trigonon' (triangle), referring to the triangular shape of some parts of the plant.
