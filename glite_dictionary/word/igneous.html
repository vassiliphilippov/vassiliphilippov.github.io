igneous

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪɡniəs / (AmE), / ˈɪɡniəs / (BrE)
(literary) Like or suggestive of fire; fiery.
like or suggestive of fire

Examples

The sun set in an igneous blaze, casting a warm glow over the hills.
She described the igneous passion that fueled the revolution.
An igneous glow illuminated the sky after the eruption.

Synset Examples

a fiery desert wind
an igneous desert atmosphere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiery - like or consisting of fire; burning brightly

Etymology

From Latin ignis (fire) + -ous.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪɡniəs / (AmE), / ˈɪɡ.ni.əs / (BrE)
(of rocks) formed by the solidification of molten magma or lava, especially as a result of intense heat.
formed by fire or intense heat (rocks)

Examples

The coastline is dotted with igneous rocks that were formed from ancient volcanic activity.
Granite and basalt are two common types of igneous rock.
Geologists study igneous formations to learn about the Earth's interior processes.

Synset Examples

rocks formed by igneous agents

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sedimentary - rocks formed by the accumulation of sediment
  • Antonym: metamorphic - rocks altered by heat and pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'igneus' (of fire), from 'ignis' (fire).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01357773-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈɪɡniəs / (AmE), / ˈɪɡniəs / (BrE)
(of rocks or processes) formed by or relating to the solidification of molten material, especially from lava or magma, or produced under intense heat.
formed by intense heat, especially from molten rock

Examples

Geologists often study igneous rocks to understand the Earth's interior processes.
The mountain range was formed primarily from igneous material that cooled after volcanic eruptions.
Granite and basalt are two common types of igneous rock found on Earth.

Synset Examples

igneous rock is rock formed by solidification from a molten state; especially from molten magma
igneous fusion is fusion by heat alone
pyrogenic strata

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: metamorphic - characterized by alteration due to heat and pressure (as in 'metamorphic rock')
  • Antonym: sedimentary - relating to rocks formed by the accumulation of sediment

Etymology

From Latin 'igneus' (of fire), from 'ignis' (fire).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02863150-a
Synset POS: a
