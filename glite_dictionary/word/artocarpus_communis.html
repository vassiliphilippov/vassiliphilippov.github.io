Artocarpus communis

1. NOUN

/ ɑr.təˈkɑr.pəs kəˈmju.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɑː.təˈkɑː.pəs kəˈmjuː.nɪs / (BrE)
a species of tropical tree native to Pacific islands, known for its large, starchy edible fruit with a texture similar to bread.
tropical tree with edible starchy fruit

Examples

The villagers cultivated Artocarpus communis for its nourishing fruit.
Many recipes in Polynesia use the starchy flesh of Artocarpus communis.
Botanists regard Artocarpus communis as an important food plant in the tropics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breadfruit - the common name for Artocarpus communis and its fruit

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Arto-' relates to bread (Greek 'artos') and '-carpus' means fruit.
