Spiranthes cernua

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈrænθiːz ˈsɜrnjuə / (AmE), / spaɪˈrænθiːz ˈsɜːnjuə / (BrE)
A North American orchid with tall, erect, densely flowered spiraling clusters of creamy white, vanilla-scented flowers, commonly found in damp areas in the east and central regions.
a North American vanilla-scented orchid

Examples

The Spiranthes cernua blooms in late summer, filling the marsh with a sweet vanilla fragrance.
Botanists were excited to find a rare cluster of Spiranthes cernua near the wetland reserve.
Gardeners appreciate Spiranthes cernua for its delicate spiraling flowers and pleasant scent.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Spiranthes' from Greek 'speira' (coil) and 'anthos' (flower); species name 'cernua' means 'nodding' in Latin.
