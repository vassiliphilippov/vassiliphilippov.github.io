hero

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪroʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɪərəʊ / (BrE)
a person admired for having exceptional courage, outstanding achievements, or noble qualities, especially in the face of danger or adversity
person of courage and noble qualities

Examples

He became a hero when he rescued the children from the burning building.
Many consider her a hero for standing up to injustice.
Comic books often portray the hero as someone with extraordinary powers.

Synset Examples

RAF pilots were the heroes of the Battle of Britain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heroine - female person admired for courage or noble qualities
  • Antonym: villain - person who is morally bad or opposes the hero

Etymology

From Greek hērōs (ἥρως), meaning 'protector' or 'defender'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɪroʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɪərəʊ / (BrE)
(in classical mythology) a being of great strength and courage, often celebrated for daring exploits and typically the child of a mortal and a god.
mythical being of great strength and courage

Examples

In Greek mythology, Hercules is regarded as a celebrated hero known for his extraordinary feats.
Many classical stories describe the adventures and labors of a hero born from both human and divine parents.
Achilles was a legendary hero whose courage and strength became the subject of epic poetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demigod - a being with partial divine status, often the offspring of a god and a mortal

Etymology

From Greek hērōs (ἥρως), meaning 'hero, demi-god'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɪroʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɪərəʊ / (BrE)
a person who is admired for brave actions or fighting for a cause
person admired for bravery or a cause

Examples

The firefighter became a hero after rescuing several people from the burning building.
She was hailed as a hero for her tireless advocacy of human rights.
Many see him as a hero for standing up against injustice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: villain - person who is opposed to the hero or acts wickedly
  • Synonym: champion - someone who vigorously supports or fights for a cause

Etymology

From Greek hērōs (hero, demigod).

4. NOUN

/ ˈhɪroʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɪərəʊ / (BrE)
The principal character in a play, movie, novel, or poem, typically the one with whom the audience identifies most closely.
principal character in a story

Examples

The hero of the novel faces many challenges before finding happiness.
Audiences often cheer for the hero as the central figure in the story.
In classical tragedies, the hero is not always morally perfect but is always central to the plot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protagonist - the main character in a story or drama
  • Antonym: antagonist - the main opponent of the hero or protagonist in a story

Etymology

From Greek hērōs (ἥρως) meaning 'demi-god, illustrious man.'

5. NOUN

/ ˈhiːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɪərəʊ / (BrE)
(North American) A large sandwich made from a long, split roll filled with meats, cheese, vegetables, and condiments; also known as a submarine, hoagie, or grinder in different regions.
large sandwich on a long roll (US)
american

Examples

I ordered a roast beef hero for lunch from the deli.
She prefers her hero with extra onions and hot peppers.
In New York, a sandwich like that is called a hero, not a sub.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submarine - another term for a large sandwich made on a long roll, especially in the US
  • Synonym: hoagie - regional US name for a similar large sandwich
  • Synonym: grinder - another US regional term for a large sandwich on an elongated roll

Etymology

Originally New York City slang, possibly from the idea that eating such a large sandwich required a 'heroic' effort.
