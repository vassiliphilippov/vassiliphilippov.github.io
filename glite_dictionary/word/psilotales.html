Psilotales

1. NOUN

/ saɪləˈteɪliːz / (AmE), / saɪləˈteɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of primitive vascular plants characterized by dichotomously branched shoots, absence of true roots, and a simple structure
order of primitive vascular plants

Examples

The Psilotales are distinguished by their lack of true leaves and roots.
Botanists study the Psilotales to understand the evolution of vascular plants.
Fossils of Psilotales provide insight into ancient plant forms.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Psilotum (from Greek 'psilos', bare or naked) + '-ales' (order suffix).

2. NOUN

/ saɪloʊˈteɪliːz / (AmE), / saɪləʊˈteɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of lower vascular plants characterized by a dichotomously branched sporophyte divided into an aerial shoot and a rhizome, and lacking true roots
order of primitive vascular plants

Examples

The Psilotales are considered among the most primitive living vascular plants.
Members of the Psilotales lack true roots, which distinguishes them from many other ferns.
Botanists study Psilotales to understand the evolution of vascular plants.

Etymology

From New Latin Psilotales, from the genus name Psilotum plus -ales (denoting botanical orders).
