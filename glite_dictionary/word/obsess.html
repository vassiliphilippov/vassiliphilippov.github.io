to obsess

1. VERB

/ əbˈsɛs / (AmE), / əbˈsɛs / (BrE)
(of a thought or feeling) to occupy or fill the mind of (someone) continually and to a troubling extent; to preoccupy persistently.
to preoccupy or trouble the mind continuously

Examples

He let worries about his career obsess him day and night.
Memories of the incident continued to obsess her for years.
If you obsess over small details, you'll never finish the project.

Synset Examples

Fear of illness haunts her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haunt - to continually trouble or preoccupy the mind
  • Synonym: to preoccupy - to absorb or occupy the mind to the exclusion of other thoughts

Etymology

From Latin 'obsessus', past participle of 'obsidere' (to besiege).

2. VERB

/ əbˈsɛs / (AmE), / əbˈsɛs / (BrE)
to dominate or fill the mind of (someone) continually and to a troubling extent; to be preoccupied with something persistently
be preoccupied with something repeatedly

Examples

He tends to obsess about every small detail before an important meeting.
Some people obsess over social media likes and comments.
She started to obsess with the idea of moving to a different city.

Synset Examples

She is obsessing over her weight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to preoccupy - to dominate or engross the mind
  • Synonym: worry - to experience mental distress or agitation
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to

Etymology

From Latin 'obsessus', past participle of 'obsidere' (to besiege).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01835085-v
Synset POS: v
