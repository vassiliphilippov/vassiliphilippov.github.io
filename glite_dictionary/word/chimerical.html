chimerical

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmɪr.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / kɪˈmɪər.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
existing only as the product of unchecked imagination; fantastically imaginary or improbable
wildly imaginative; fantastical

Examples

Many deemed the inventor's plans too chimerical to ever succeed, yet he persisted nonetheless.
Their proposal for a city in the clouds sounded chimerical, but it captured everyone’s imagination.
The book is filled with chimerical tales of mythical creatures and impossible adventures.

Synset Examples

his Utopia is not a chimerical commonwealth but a practical improvement on what already exists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fantastical - imaginative or fanciful, especially in an extravagant way
  • Antonym: practical - concerned with what is actual rather than imaginary or visionary

Etymology

From Latin chimaera (chimera), from Greek khimaira (she-goat, monster in Greek mythology).

2. ADJ

/ kəˈmɪrɪkəl / (AmE), / kɪˈmɪərɪkəl / (BrE)
existing only as the product of unchecked imagination; fantastically visionary or improbable; highly unrealistic.
wildly fanciful or highly improbable

Examples

The idea of building a city on the moon seemed quite chimerical at the time.
Her chimerical plans for the future were a source of amusement to her friends.
Critics dismissed the proposal as a chimerical scheme that would never be implemented.

Synset Examples

his Utopia is not as chimeric commonwealth but a practical improvement on what already exists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fantastical - imaginative or fanciful, remote from reality
  • Antonym: realistic - representing things in a way that is accurate and true to life

Etymology

From Latin 'chimaericus', from Greek 'khimairikos', relating to 'chimera', a mythological creature.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02704984-a
Synset POS: a
