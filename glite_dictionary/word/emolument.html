emolument

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɑːljəmənt / (AmE), / ɪˈmɒljʊmənt / (BrE)
a salary, fee, or profit from employment or office
salary or fee from employment or office

Examples

The CEO's emolument included bonuses and additional benefits beyond his base salary.
The constitution prohibits government officials from accepting emoluments from foreign states.
Her emoluments as a university professor were subject to annual review by the board.

Synset Examples

a clause in the U.S. constitution prevents sitting legislators from receiving emoluments from their own votes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remuneration - money paid for work or a service
  • Synonym: wage - money paid to an employee for work
  • Synonym: salary - fixed regular payment, typically monthly, from employer to employee

Etymology

From Latin emolumentum (profit, gain), from emolere ‘to grind out’.
