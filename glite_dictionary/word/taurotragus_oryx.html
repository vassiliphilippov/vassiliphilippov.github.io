Taurotragus oryx

1. NOUN

/ taʊˌroʊˈtræɡəs ˈɔrɪks / (AmE), / tɔːˌrɒtrəɡəs ˈɔːrɪks / (BrE)
a large, dark fawn-colored African antelope, also known as the common eland, native to southern and eastern Africa
large African antelope (common eland)

Examples

The Taurotragus oryx is the largest species of antelope found in Africa.
Herds of Taurotragus oryx often graze on the open plains at dusk.
Conservationists are studying the migration patterns of Taurotragus oryx to protect their habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eland - another name for the common eland, Taurotragus oryx

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus and species names; 'Taurotragus' from Greek 'tauros' (bull) + 'tragos' (goat), 'oryx' is a classical name for an antelope.
