Eriodictyon californicum

1. NOUN

/ ɛri.oʊˈdɪkti.ɑn ˌkælɪˈfɔrnɪkəm / (AmE), / ɛrɪ.əˈdɪktɪ.ɒn ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkəm / (BrE)
A viscid evergreen shrub of the western United States, with sticky aromatic leaves that are used in treating bronchial and pulmonary illnesses.
evergreen shrub used medicinally

Examples

Many herbalists use Eriodictyon californicum to prepare remedies for respiratory ailments.
The fragrant leaves of Eriodictyon californicum are often brewed into teas for medicinal purposes.
Eriodictyon californicum grows abundantly on dry slopes in California and Oregon.

Etymology

From Greek 'erion' (wool) and 'diktyon' (net, mesh), referring to the netted veins of the leaves; 'californicum' from California.
