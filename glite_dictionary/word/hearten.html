to hearten

1. VERB

/ ˈhɑːrtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtn̩ / (BrE)
to make someone feel more cheerful or confident; to give encouragement to
to encourage or uplift

Examples

Her kind words helped to hearten everyone during a difficult time.
The team's early success seemed to hearten the fans.
We were heartened by the positive response from the community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to discourage - cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
  • Synonym: to encourage - give support, confidence, or hope to someone

Etymology

From Old English 'heortian', from 'heorte' (heart); related to the idea of giving heart.
