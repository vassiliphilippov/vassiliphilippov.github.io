weeper

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːpər / (AmE), / ˈwiːpə / (BrE)
a person hired to express grief or lamentation at a funeral, especially by crying or wailing
person hired to mourn at funerals

Examples

In ancient times, a wealthy family might employ a weeper to mourn at the graveside.
The weeper let out loud cries during the ceremony, as was the custom.
Historical accounts often mention the presence of professional weepers at royal funerals.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwiːpər / (AmE), / ˈwiːpə / (BrE)
a person who weeps or cries, especially habitually or in a particular situation
person who cries

Examples

After hearing the tragic news, she became the household's chief weeper.
The funeral was filled with mournful weepers paying their last respects.
Despite his tough reputation, he is known to be a secret weeper at movies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crybaby - a person, especially a child, who cries or complains easily or frequently
  • Antonym: laugher - a person who laughs

Etymology

From Middle English wepen, from Old English wēpan (to weep), plus the agent suffix -er.
