rivetter

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪvətər / (AmE), / ˈrɪvɪtə / (BrE)
a machine used for driving rivets into materials such as metal or leather
machine for driving rivets

Examples

The rivetter sped up the assembly process on the factory floor.
A new hydraulic rivetter was installed to join the steel beams more efficiently.
Operators must wear protective gear when using the automatic rivetter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rivet gun - a handheld tool for driving rivets

Etymology

From 'rivet' (a metal pin) + '-er' (agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪvətər / (AmE), / ˈrɪvɪtə / (BrE)
a worker whose job is to insert and hammer rivets, especially in construction or manufacturing
worker who inserts and hammers rivets

Examples

During the war, she became a skilled rivetter at the local shipyard.
The rivetter fastened each section of the metal frame firmly in place.
Many factories once employed hundreds of rivetters to assemble machinery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riveter - alternative spelling; worker who inserts and hammers rivets

Etymology

From 'rivet', from Old French *riveter* (to clinch or fasten), with agent suffix '-er'.
