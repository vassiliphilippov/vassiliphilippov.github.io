boney

1. ADJ

/ ˈboʊni / (AmE), / ˈbəʊni / (BrE)
having many or prominent bones; thin or angular with clearly visible bones.
having many or prominent bones

Examples

The old dog grew increasingly boney as he aged, despite eating well.
You could see his boney fingers gripping the handle tightly.
Their cat is so boney that you can feel every rib when you pet her.

Synset Examples

a bony shad fillet
her bony wrist
bony fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bony - having many or prominent bones
  • Antonym: fleshy - having much flesh; plump or overweight

Etymology

From bone + -y suffix, analogous to 'bony'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈboʊni / (AmE), / ˈbəʊni / (BrE)
Very thin, with prominent bones easily visible under the skin.
very thin; having prominent bones

Examples

The boney dog searched the streets for scraps of food.
She wrapped her coat tightly around her boney shoulders to keep warm.
He shook my hand, and I noticed how boney his fingers were.

Synset Examples

a child with skinny freckled legs
a long scrawny neck
pale bony hands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bony - very thin; having visible bones
  • Synonym: scrawny - unattractively thin and bony
  • Synonym: skinny - very thin, often unattractively so

Etymology

Variant of 'bony', from bone + -y.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00993331-s
Synset POS: s
