enormous

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɔrməs / (AmE), / ɪˈnɔːməs / (BrE)
extremely large in size, quantity, or degree; much greater than usual.
extremely large or great

Examples

The storm caused enormous damage throughout the city.
She felt enormous relief when she heard the good news.
The company posted an enormous profit last quarter.

Synset Examples

an enormous boulder
enormous expenses
tremendous sweeping plains
a tremendous fact in human experience; that a whole civilization should be dependent on technology
a plane took off with a tremendous noise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huge - very large in size or amount
  • Synonym: gigantic - extremely large; like a giant
  • Antonym: tiny - very small in size or amount
  • Synonym: immense - extremely large or vast

Etymology

From Latin 'enormis' meaning 'irregular, vast' via French 'énorme'.
