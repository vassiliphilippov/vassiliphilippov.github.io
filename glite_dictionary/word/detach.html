to detach

1. VERB

/ dɪˈtætʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈtatʃ / (BrE)
to remove or separate something from something it is connected to; to take off.
to separate or remove

Examples

Before serving, detach the leaves from the stem.
He tried to detach the label without tearing it.
She carefully detached her earring and placed it on the table.

Synset Examples

detach the skin from the chicken before you eat it

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to attach - to fasten or join one thing to another
  • Synonym: separate - to set or keep apart

Etymology

From French détacher, from Old French destachier, from des- (dis-) + attachier (to attach).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈtætʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈtatʃ / (BrE)
to separate a part or unit from something larger, especially in order to perform a special task or assignment
separate a part for a special duty

Examples

The commander decided to detach a small squad for reconnaissance duty.
They plan to detach a research team from the main group to study the phenomenon.
You can detach certain resources for special assignments as needed.

Synset Examples

detach a regiment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separate - to move or be apart; to set apart
  • Antonym: to attach - to join or fasten one thing to another

Etymology

From French détacher, from Old French destachier (to unfasten).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02035467-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈtætʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈtatʃ / (BrE)
(of something) to come apart or separate from something to which it is attached, often unintentionally or as a result of a process.
to come apart or separate

Examples

The leaf detached from the branch in the strong wind.
Over time, the label detached and fell to the floor.
His retina detached, requiring immediate medical attention.

Synset Examples

His retina detached and he had to be rushed into surgery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separate - to move or be apart; to stop being joined
  • Antonym: to attach - to join or fasten; the opposite process

Etymology

From French détacher (to unfasten, detach), from de- (off) + attacher (to attach).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01302344-v
Synset POS: v
