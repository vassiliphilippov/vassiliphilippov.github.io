fistula

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪs.tʃə.lə / (AmE), / ˈfɪs.tjʊ.lə / (BrE)
A chronic inflammation, especially of the withers in horses, resulting in an abnormal passage or sore.
chronic inflammation of horse's withers
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the horse with a fistula after observing inflammation at its withers.
Chronic fistula can cause significant discomfort and require extended treatment for horses.
Early intervention is important when a fistula starts to develop, to prevent infection spreading.

Etymology

From Latin *fistula* (pipe, tube, ulcer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪs.tʃə.lə / (AmE), / ˈfɪs.tjʊ.lə / (BrE)
An abnormal passage or duct formed between two organs or leading from an internal cavity or organ to the surface of the body, often as a result of injury, infection, or inflammation.
abnormal bodily passage or duct
medical

Examples

The patient developed a fistula that connected her intestine to the skin surface after the surgery.
Treatment of a fistula often requires surgical intervention to prevent further infection.
Doctors found that the chronic abscess had led to the formation of a fistula.

Etymology

From Latin *fistula* (pipe, tube).
