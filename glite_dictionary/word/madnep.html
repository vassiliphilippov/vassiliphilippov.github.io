madnep

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæd.nɛp / (AmE), / ˈmad.nɛp / (BrE)
A biennial weed native to Europe and America, with large pinnate leaves, yellow flowers, and a bitter, slightly poisonous root; considered the wild ancestor of the cultivated parsnip.
wild ancestor of the parsnip

Examples

The roots of the madnep are bitter and should not be eaten raw.
Botanists believe that madnep is the progenitor of the modern cultivated parsnip.
Some meadows in Europe are covered with wild madnep during the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsnip - a root vegetable cultivated for its edible taproot
