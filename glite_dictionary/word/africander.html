Africander

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfrɪˈkændər / (AmE), / ˌafrɪˈkɑːndə / (BrE)
a large, tall, humped breed of cattle from South Africa, known for their large horns and used for meat or draft work
South African cattle breed

Examples

The farmer raised a herd of Africander cattle for both meat and labor.
The Africander is well adapted to the hot and dry climate of South Africa.
At the livestock show, a prize Africander bull drew much attention from visitors.

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'Afrikaner', meaning 'African' (referring to the breed's origin).
