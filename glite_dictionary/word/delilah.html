Delilah

1. PROPN

/ dəˈlaɪlə / (AmE), / dɪˈlaɪlə / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the Philistine woman who betrayed Samson by cutting off his hair, thereby depriving him of his strength.
betrayer of Samson in the Bible

Examples

The story of Delilah and Samson is found in the Book of Judges.
Some people refer to a woman who betrays trust as a modern Delilah.
Artists have been inspired to paint scenes featuring Delilah and Samson.

Etymology

From Hebrew Dəlīlāh, meaning 'delicate' or 'weak'.

2. NOUN

/ dəˈlaɪlə / (AmE), / dɪˈlaɪlə / (BrE)
a woman who is dangerously seductive and likely to lead men into compromising or damaging situations; a femme fatale.
dangerously seductive woman

Examples

His downfall was inevitable once he became captivated by a modern Delilah.
In literature, the character is portrayed as a classic Delilah, manipulating those around her with ease.
They warned him not to trust her, calling her a real Delilah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: femme fatale - an alluring and mysterious woman who seduces and brings disaster to men

Etymology

From the name of Delilah, a character in the Bible who betrayed Samson (Judges 16).

Synset POS: n
