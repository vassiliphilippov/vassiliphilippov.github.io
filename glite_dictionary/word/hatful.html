hatful

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæt.fəl / (AmE), / ˈhæt.fʊl / (BrE)
An amount as much as a hat can hold; a large quantity or number of something.
as much as a hat will hold

Examples

She brought back a hatful of wildflowers from her walk.
The team scored a hatful of goals in last night's match.
He received a hatful of compliments for his performance.

Etymology

Formed from 'hat' + suffix '-ful', meaning 'as much as a hat will hold'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæt.fəl / (AmE), / ˈhæt.fəl / (BrE)
(often followed by 'of') a large number or quantity of something.
large number or amount

Examples

He won a hatful of awards at the ceremony last night.
The company received a hatful of complaints after the product launch.
After years of saving, they had collected a hatful of money for their dream house.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhæt.fəl / (AmE), / ˈhæt.fʊl / (BrE)
A quantity that fills a hat; a large amount or number.
a large quantity or amount

Examples

She arrived at the party carrying a hatful of presents for everyone.
After years of writing, the author finally received a hatful of awards for his work.
He left the meeting with a hatful of new ideas to try out.

Synset Examples

She brought a hatful of gifts for the party.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: handful - a small number or amount; as much as the hand will hold
  • Degree Higher: armful - as much as can be carried in the arms; a large quantity

Etymology

Formed from 'hat' + the suffix '-ful', patterned after 'handful', 'bagful', etc.
