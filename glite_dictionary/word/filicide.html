filicide

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪsʌɪd / (BrE)
the act of killing one's own son or daughter
killing of one's own child
legal

Examples

The police arrested him for filicide after they discovered the tragic evidence.
Psychologists have studied the motives behind filicide in various cultures.
That novel explores the theme of filicide and its emotional consequences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parricide - the killing of one's parent or close relative
  • Degree Lower: infanticide - the killing of an infant

Etymology

From Latin 'filius' (son) or 'filia' (daughter) + '-cide' (killing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪˌsaɪd / (BrE)
a parent who kills their own son or daughter
a parent who kills their own child

Examples

The court heard that the accused had committed filicide under extreme psychological distress.
Cases of filicide often prompt debates about mental health support for parents.
In literature, filicide is sometimes portrayed as the ultimate form of tragedy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patricide - the killing of one's father
  • Synonym: matericide - the killing of one's mother
  • Synonym: infanticide - the killing of an infant or very young child

Etymology

From Latin 'filius' (son) or 'filia' (daughter) and '-cide' (killer).
