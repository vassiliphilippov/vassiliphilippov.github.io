rococo

1. NOUN

/ rəˈkoʊˌkoʊ / (AmE), / rəˈkəʊkəʊ / (BrE)
an ornate, decorative style of art, architecture, and design characterized by elaborate, graceful, and asymmetrical ornamentation, especially popular in 18th-century France
style of ornate art and architecture

Examples

The palace was decorated in the rococo style, with elaborate gold trim and pastel colors.
Collectors admired the delicate carvings and gilded details of the rococo furniture.
The museum's new exhibit features paintings from the rococo period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baroque - an earlier, similarly ornate style in art and architecture

Etymology

From French rococo, possibly from rocaille (rockwork, shellwork) or Italian barocco (Baroque).

2. ADJ

/ roʊˈkoʊkoʊ / (AmE), / rəˈkəʊkəʊ / (BrE)
(of art, architecture, or decoration) characterized by elaborate, asymmetrical ornamentation, often with intricate detail and curves.
elaborately ornate and asymmetrical style

Examples

The ceiling of the palace was covered in rococo plasterwork and golden reliefs.
She bought a rococo clock with intricate designs and delicate curves for her mantelpiece.
The museum features a room filled with rococo furniture and art from the 18th century.

Synset Examples

an exquisite gilded rococo mirror

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: baroque - an artistic style preceding rococo, known for grandeur and opulence
  • Synonym: ornate - elaborately or highly decorated
  • Antonym: simple - free from ornamentation or complexity

Etymology

From French rococo, probably a diminutive of rocaille (shellwork, pebble-work).
