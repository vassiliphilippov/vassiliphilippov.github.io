ectasia

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˈteɪʒə / (AmE), / ɛkˈteɪzɪə / (BrE)
The abnormal dilatation or distension of a hollow organ, especially as a medical condition.
abnormal dilatation of a hollow organ
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with corneal ectasia following eye surgery.
Bronchial ectasia can lead to chronic respiratory problems if not properly treated.
Magnetic resonance imaging revealed ectasia of the aorta.

Etymology

From Greek ektasis (extension, stretching), from ekteinō (to stretch out).
