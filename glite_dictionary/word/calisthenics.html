calisthenics

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.ɪsˈθɛn.ɪks / (AmE), / ˌkæl.ɪsˈθen.ɪks / (BrE)
physical exercises, often rhythmical and without equipment, designed to develop strength, flexibility, and overall fitness
bodyweight exercises for fitness

Examples

He performs calisthenics every morning as part of his workout routine.
Many schools include calisthenics in their physical education programs.
Doctors often recommend calisthenics for people recovering from injuries.

Synset Examples

calisthenics is recommended for general good health

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerobics - a system of exercise using rhythmic activity to improve fitness

Etymology

From Greek 'kallos' (beauty) + 'sthenos' (strength).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæləsˈθɛnɪks / (AmE), / ˌkælɪsˈθɛnɪks / (BrE)
physical exercises that are performed without special equipment and are intended to improve overall fitness and flexibility
bodyweight fitness exercises

Examples

She incorporates calisthenics into her morning routine to stay healthy.
The coach led the team through a series of calisthenics before practice.
Many schools require students to do calisthenics in physical education classes.

Synset Examples

several different calisthenics were illustrated in the video

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerobics - a system of exercises that increase the body's ability to take in and use oxygen

Etymology

From Greek *kallos* (beauty) + *sthenos* (strength).
