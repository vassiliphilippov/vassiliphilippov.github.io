Aureolaria

1. NOUN

/ ɔːriəˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / ɔːrɪəˈlɛːrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of North American herbaceous plants, often parasitic on roots, with golden-yellow flowers, sometimes classified within the genus Gerardia
genus of North American plants with yellow flowers

Examples

The botanical garden recently added several Aureolaria species to its North American collection.
Researchers discovered that the Aureolaria plants rely on nearby tree roots for nutrients.
Many wildflowers in the area, including Aureolaria, bloom in late summer.

Etymology

From Latin 'aureolus' (golden) + '-aria' (relating to).

2. NOUN

/ ɔːr.i.oʊˈlɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / ɔː.rɪ.əˈlɛə.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
a small genus of North American herbaceous plants, often root-parasitic, with golden-yellow flowers; sometimes classified under the genus Gerardia
a genus of North American root-parasitic herbs

Examples

Several species within the genus Aureolaria are known for their striking golden-yellow flowers.
Botanists debated whether to place these North American plants in Aureolaria or in the genus Gerardia.
Aureolaria species are often root-parasitic, drawing nutrients from the roots of nearby trees.

Etymology

From Latin 'aureolus' (golden) + '-aria', referring to the golden color of the flowers.
