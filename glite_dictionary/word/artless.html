artless

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑrtləs / (AmE), / ˈɑːtləs / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) without deception, simple, and sincere; showing an inability or unwillingness to hide feelings or intentions.
sincere; without deception

Examples

Her artless laughter made everyone in the room smile.
He gave an artless apology, making it clear he truly regretted his mistake.
Despite her artless manner, she impressed the interviewers with her honesty.

Synset Examples

an ingenuous admission of responsibility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingenuous - innocent and unsuspecting; openly straightforward
  • Antonym: devious - showing a skillful use of underhanded tactics

Etymology

From 'art' (meaning craft or cunning) + '-less' (without).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑrtləs / (AmE), / ˈɑːtlɪs / (BrE)
(of a person) lacking knowledge, skill, or artificiality; without guile or craft.
without skill, knowledge, or guile

Examples

She gave an artless reply, unaware of the subtle politics behind the question.
Despite his artless manner, he managed to charm everyone in the room.
The artless enthusiasm of the beginner was refreshing to the experienced musicians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or judgment
  • Antonym: sophisticated - having much worldly knowledge or refinement

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *artless*, from Latin *ars* (art) + *-less* (without).
