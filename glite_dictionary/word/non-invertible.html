non-invertible

1. ADJ

/ nɑn ɪnˈvɜrtəbl̩ / (AmE), / nɒn ɪnˈvɜːtɪbl̩ / (BrE)
(of a mathematical object) not having an additive or multiplicative inverse; unable to be inverted.
not having a mathematical inverse

Examples

A matrix with a determinant of zero is non-invertible.
Certain linear transformations are non-invertible and cannot be reversed.
The function is non-invertible because it does not have a unique inverse for each output.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invertible - (of a mathematical object) having an inverse

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) and 'invertible' (able to be inverted).
