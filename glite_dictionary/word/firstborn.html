firstborn

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝst.bɔrn / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst.bɔːn / (BrE)
the child who is born first in the order of siblings
child born first among siblings

Examples

As the firstborn, she always felt a sense of responsibility for her younger siblings.
Parents often expect their firstborn to set a good example.
The firstborn usually receives the most attention from grandparents.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lastborn - the youngest child in the family

Etymology

From Old English 'fyrst' (first) + 'born' (to be born).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɝstˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈfɜːstˌbɔːn / (BrE)
(of a child) born first in the order of birth among siblings; eldest.
eldest child by birth order

Examples

As the firstborn son, he inherited most of his father's estate.
Their firstborn daughter just started college this fall.
In many cultures, the firstborn carries special responsibilities within the family.

Synset Examples

the firstborn child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eldest - (of a child) born first among siblings
  • Antonym: lastborn - the youngest child in the order of birth

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English 'fyrst' (first) + 'born' (born).
