Zacharias

1. PROPN

/ ˌzækəˈraɪəs / (AmE), / ˌzakəˈrʌɪəs / (BrE)
A Hebrew minor prophet of the late 6th century BC, known from the Old Testament.
Hebrew minor prophet (6th century BC)

Examples

Zacharias is traditionally regarded as the author of the biblical Book of Zechariah.
Many scholars study the prophecies of Zacharias for insights into post-exilic Judaism.
The visions experienced by Zacharias have been depicted in numerous works of art.

Etymology

From Greek Zacharias, from Hebrew Zəkharyāh, meaning 'Yahweh has remembered.'

2. PROPN

/ ˌzækəˈraɪəs / (AmE), / ˌzakəˈraɪəs / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament containing the prophecies of Zechariah, chiefly concerning the renewal of Israel after the Babylonian Captivity.
Old Testament book of prophecies by Zechariah

Examples

The Book of Zacharias is often studied by theologians interested in post-exilic prophecy.
In his sermon, the pastor quoted from Zacharias to emphasize hope and restoration.
Scholars debate the historical context of Zacharias and its visions of Jerusalem's future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zechariah - Alternative spelling and common form for the same prophet and biblical book

Etymology

From Latin *Zacharias*, from Greek *Zacharias*, from Hebrew *Zəḵaryāh* (‘God remembers’).
