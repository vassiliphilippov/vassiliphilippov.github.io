benzoquinone

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛnzoʊkwɪˈnoʊn / (AmE), / ˌbɛnzəʊkwɪˈnəʊn / (BrE)
a yellow aromatic compound used in dyes and as a coenzyme or electron acceptor, found in several biologically important forms
aromatic yellow compound used in chemistry

Examples

Benzoquinone is commonly used as an intermediate in the manufacture of dyes.
Some forms of benzoquinone play key roles in biological electron transfer.
The laboratory synthesized benzoquinone to study its properties as an oxidizing agent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quinone - any of a class of aromatic compounds with a six-membered unsaturated ring

Etymology

From 'benzo-' (from benzene) and 'quinone', indicating its structure as a benzene-derived quinone.
