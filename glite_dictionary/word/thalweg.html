thalweg

1. NOUN

/ ˈtælˌwɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈtalwɛɡ / (BrE)
the line following the lowest elevation within a watercourse, especially the middle of the chief navigable channel that serves as a boundary between states
the line of deepest channel in a river, often a boundary
legal

Examples

The boundary between the two countries follows the thalweg of the river.
In international law, the thalweg principle determines the border in navigable rivers.
Engineers surveyed the river's thalweg to update the border map.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: median line - a line equally distant from the banks of a river, sometimes used as a boundary

Etymology

From German 'Thalweg', from 'Tal' (valley) + 'Weg' (way).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːlˌwɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈtɑːl.wɛɡ / (BrE)
A line connecting the lowest points along a valley or riverbed, often used to delineate the main channel of a river.
line along the lowest points of a valley or riverbed

Examples

The treaty set the thalweg of the river as the official border between the two countries.
Sediments often accumulate along the thalweg after heavy rainfall.
Geologists measured the thalweg to assess the potential for flooding in the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: channel - main course of a river or stream

Etymology

From German 'Thalweg', from 'Thal' (valley) + 'Weg' (way, path).
