colpoxerosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɒl.pəɡˈzɛr.ə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒl.pəʊkˈzɛr.ə.sɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the vagina is abnormally dry, often due to hormonal changes or other health factors
abnormal vaginal dryness (medical)
medical

Examples

Some women may experience colpoxerosis during menopause due to hormonal changes.
Treatment for colpoxerosis often includes topical estrogen therapy or other moisturizers.
Persistent colpoxerosis can cause discomfort and affect sexual activity.

Etymology

From Greek 'kolpos' (vagina) + 'xeros' (dryness).
