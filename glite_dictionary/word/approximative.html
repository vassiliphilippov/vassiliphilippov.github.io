approximative

1. ADJ

/ əˈprɑksəmətɪv / (AmE), / əˈprɒksɪmətɪv / (BrE)
not quite exact or correct; close to the actual but not completely accurate or precise
not quite exact; nearly correct

Examples

The results provided only an approximative answer to the research question.
We used an approximative method to quickly estimate the cost.
His report offered an approximative description of the scene rather than exact details.

Synset Examples

the approximate time was 10 o'clock
a rough guess
a rough estimate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approximate - close to the actual, but not completely accurate or exact
  • Antonym: exact - fully correct or accurate
  • Synonym: rough - not precise or detailed

Etymology

From Medieval Latin approximativus, from Latin approximare (to approach).
