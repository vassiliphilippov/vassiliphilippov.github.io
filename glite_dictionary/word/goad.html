goad

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊd / (AmE), / ɡəʊd / (BrE)
to provoke or urge someone to take action, especially by annoying or taunting them
provoke or urge to action

Examples

She tried to goad her brother into apologizing with sarcastic comments.
The manager's constant criticism goaded him to improve his performance.
They kept goading the bull until it finally charged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - to stimulate or incite someone to do something
  • Degree Lower: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • Degree Higher: to incite - to stir up or urge on to action, especially unlawful or violent action

Etymology

From Old English 'gād' (goad, point, spear).
