Erysiphaceae

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌrɪsɪˈfeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪsɪˈfeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi that are mostly parasitic on plant leaves, including the powdery mildews.
family of fungi including powdery mildews
medical

Examples

The Erysiphaceae family is responsible for many cases of powdery mildew affecting crops.
Researchers studied how the Erysiphaceae infect the leaves of various fruit trees.
Many gardeners use fungicides to combat Erysiphaceae infections on their plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powdery mildew - fungal disease causing white powdery coating on plants

Etymology

From modern Latin Erysiphaceae, formed from the type genus Erysiphe and the family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ɛr.ɪˌsɪfəˈsi.aɪ / (AmE), / ɛr.ɪˌsɪfiˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of fungi that are parasitic mainly on leaves, including the powdery mildews
family of parasitic fungi (powdery mildews)
medical

Examples

Powdery mildew diseases are caused by fungi belonging to the Erysiphaceae.
Research has focused on the genetic diversity within the Erysiphaceae family.
Many plants are susceptible to infection by members of the Erysiphaceae.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name 'Erysiphe' with the suffix '-aceae' denoting a family.
