Templetonia retusa

1. NOUN

/ tɛm.pəlˈtoʊ.ni.ə rɪˈtuː.sə / (AmE), / tɛm.pəlˈtəʊ.ni.ə rɪˈtjuː.sə / (BrE)
An Australian shrub with simple obovate leaves and brilliant scarlet flowers, commonly known as cockies tongues.
Australian shrub with scarlet flowers

Examples

The Templetonia retusa is often planted in Australian gardens for its vibrant scarlet blossoms.
We spotted several Templetonia retusa shrubs along the hiking trail in Western Australia.
Gardeners appreciate the drought tolerance of Templetonia retusa compared to other native species.

Etymology

Genus name from English botanist John Templeton (1766–1825); species from Latin 'retusus' meaning blunt or notched, referring to the leaf shape.
