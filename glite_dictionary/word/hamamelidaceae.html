Hamamelidaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæməˌmɛlɪˈdeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌhaməˌmɛlɪˈdeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes witch hazel and related genera, comprising Hamamelis, Corylopsis, Fothergilla, Liquidambar, Parrotia, and others
family of flowering plants (witch hazel family)

Examples

The Hamamelidaceae family includes several ornamental shrubs prized for their autumn foliage.
Botanists recently reclassified certain species within the Hamamelidaceae.
Witch hazel, a well-known member of the Hamamelidaceae family, is used in skin care products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witch hazel - a shrub belonging to the Hamamelidaceae family

Etymology

From New Latin, from genus Hamamelis + -aceae, the standard suffix for plant family names.
