shellbark

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛlˌbɑrk / (AmE), / ˈʃɛlˌbɑːk / (BrE)
A North American hickory tree (Carya laciniosa) with loose, shaggy grey bark and edible nuts.
type of North American hickory tree

Examples

The shellbark hickory produces large, sweet nuts prized by wildlife and people alike.
You can recognize a shellbark by its thick, peeling bark.
Several shellbarks grow along the river, providing shade and food for local animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shagbark - a hickory tree with shaggy peeling bark

Etymology

From 'shell' + 'bark', referring to the characteristic bark of the tree.
