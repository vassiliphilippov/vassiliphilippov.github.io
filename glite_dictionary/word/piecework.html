piecework

1. NOUN

/ ˈpisˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈpiːswɜːk / (BrE)
work that is paid for according to the amount produced rather than by the hour or day
work paid by quantity produced

Examples

She preferred piecework because she could earn more by working faster.
The factory pays employees on a piecework basis rather than an hourly wage.
Many garment workers are engaged in piecework, sewing as many garments as they can each day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hourly wage - payment based on the number of hours worked
  • Synonym: task work - work paid for completion of a specific task or task unit

Etymology

Formed from 'piece' + 'work,' referring to work paid for each piece made or completed.
