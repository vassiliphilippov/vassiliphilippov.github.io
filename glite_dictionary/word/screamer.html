screamer

1. NOUN

/ ˈskriːmər / (AmE), / ˈskriːmə / (BrE)
A large, gooselike aquatic bird of South America with a harsh, trumpeting call, belonging to the family Anhimidae.
South American aquatic bird with trumpeting call

Examples

The horned screamer inhabits marshes and wet grasslands in South America.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of the screamer during the rainy season.
Unlike most waterfowl, the screamer has partially webbed feet and a unique call.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskriːmər / (AmE), / ˈskriːmə / (BrE)
a person who shouts or speaks in a very loud and high-pitched voice, often for emphasis or out of excitement or fear
person who shouts loudly

Examples

The baby was quite a screamer and could be heard from the other room.
My brother is a real screamer at football games, cheering for his team with all his might.
She became known as the office screamer because of her loud reactions to mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shouter - a person who shouts

Etymology

From the verb 'scream' + -er.

/ ˈskriːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈskriːmə / (BrE)

a very hard hit ball (in sports)

Examples

The crowd gasped as the striker fired a screamer from outside the box.
He dove to stop the screamer, but the ball was just too fast.
