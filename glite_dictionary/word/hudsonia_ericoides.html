Hudsonia ericoides

1. NOUN

/ hʌdˈsoʊni.ə ˌɛrɪˈkoʊdiːz / (AmE), / hʌdˈsəʊni.ə ˌɛrɪˈkəʊdiːz / (BrE)
A North American evergreen shrub with heathlike leaves and yellow flowers, found in sandy soils.
North American evergreen shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

The sandy dunes were dotted with Hudsonia ericoides in full bloom.
Botanists studied the habitat preferences of Hudsonia ericoides along the Atlantic coast.
Efforts are being made to protect Hudsonia ericoides due to its limited geographic range.

Etymology

Genus name 'Hudsonia' honors British botanist William Hudson, species 'ericoides' from Latin 'Erica' (heath) + '-oides' (resembling).
