Aristotelia racemosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌærɪstoʊˈtiːliə rəˈsiːmoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌærɪstəʊˈtiːlɪə rəˈsiːməsə / (BrE)
A graceful deciduous shrub or small tree with attractive foliage and small red berries that turn black when mature, sometimes used to make wine.
species of shrub or small tree with red-black berries

Examples

The Aristotelia racemosa in our garden produces berries that are popular with native birds.
Winemakers sometimes use the fruit of Aristotelia racemosa to create a unique variety of wine.
During spring, the leaves of Aristotelia racemosa add a graceful touch to woodland landscapes.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek 'Aristotelēs' (the philosopher Aristotle), used in botanical nomenclature, and Latin 'racemosa' meaning 'having racemes'.
