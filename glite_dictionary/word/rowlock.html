rowlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.lɑk / (AmE), / ˈrɒ.lək / (BrE)
a device fitted to the side of a boat to hold an oar in place and serve as a pivot while rowing
device for holding an oar on a boat

Examples

The old wooden boat still had a rowlock on each side for the oars.
He noticed that the rowlock was loose, making it difficult to row smoothly.
With a clatter, the oar slipped out of the rowlock and splashed into the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oarlock - holder attached to a boat for oars, especially in American English

Etymology

From 'row' (to propel a boat) + 'lock' (something that holds or secures), first used in the 15th century.
