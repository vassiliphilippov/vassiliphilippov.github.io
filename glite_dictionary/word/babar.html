Babar

1. PROPN

/ bəˈbɑr / (AmE), / bəˈbɑː / (BrE)
An imaginary elephant who is the main character in a popular series of French children's books, known for his adventures and royal status.
fictional elephant from children's books

Examples

When I was a child, I loved reading the stories about Babar and his adventures in the jungle.
The first edition of Babar is now considered a valuable collector's item.
Many children learn important lessons about kindness and leadership from Babar's character.

Etymology

From the French name 'Babar', given to the elephant character created by Jean de Brunhoff in 1931.
