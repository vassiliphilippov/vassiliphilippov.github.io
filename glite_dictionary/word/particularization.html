particularization

1. NOUN

/ pərˌtɪkjələraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌtɪkjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of providing a detailed or individualized description of a specific instance or case
individualized description or specification

Examples

Her essay was praised for its particularization of each historical event.
Through careful particularization, the lawyer differentiated her client's case from previous ones.
The story lacked detail until the author added greater particularization of the setting and characters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generalization - the process of making a general statement or forming a general concept from specific cases

Etymology

From particular + -ization; based on Latin particularis (of a part).
