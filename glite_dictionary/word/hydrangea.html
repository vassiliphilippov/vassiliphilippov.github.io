Hydrangea

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdreɪndʒə / (AmE), / hʌɪˈdreɪndʒɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants including shrubs, small trees, and climbing vines, often grown for their large clusters of white, pink, or blue flowers.
genus of flowering shrubs and vines

Examples

The Hydrangea in our garden blooms with vibrant blue flowers every summer.
Some gardeners prefer to grow Hydrangea shrubs in partial shade for best results.
Pruning a Hydrangea at the wrong time can affect next year's blossoms.

Etymology

From Greek 'hydor' (water) + 'angeion' (vessel), referring to the shape of the seed capsules.
