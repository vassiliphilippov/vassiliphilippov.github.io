fullerene

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊlɚˌin / (AmE), / ˈfʊl.əˌriːn / (BrE)
a form of carbon whose molecules consist of a hollow cage of sixty or more carbon atoms arranged in a spherical, ellipsoidal, or tubular shape
form of carbon with hollow-caged molecules

Examples

Scientists have discovered that fullerene molecules can trap other atoms inside their carbon cages.
The unique structure of fullerene makes it useful in a range of nanotechnology applications.
Research into fullerene has opened new possibilities for advanced materials and electronics.

Etymology

Named after architect Buckminster Fuller, due to resemblance to his geodesic domes, plus '-ene' (denoting an unsaturated hydrocarbon).
