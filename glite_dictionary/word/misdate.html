to misdate

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈdeɪt / (AmE), / mɪsˈdeɪt / (BrE)
to assign an incorrect date to something
assign a wrong date

Examples

The historian accidentally misdated the manuscript, placing it a century too early.
If you misdate a document, it could cause confusion later.
She realized she had misdated the check after reviewing her bank statements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to backdate - to assign a date earlier than the actual one
  • Synonym: to predate - to determine or establish as earlier in time

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'date' (to assign a date).
