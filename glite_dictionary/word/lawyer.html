lawyer

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔː.jɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɔː.jə / (BrE)
A person who is professionally qualified to practice law, represent clients in legal matters, and provide legal advice.
a person qualified to practice law

Examples

After graduating from law school, she became a successful lawyer in a large firm.
Their lawyer advised them against signing the contract without further discussion.
If you have any legal questions, it's best to consult a lawyer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attorney - a person appointed to act for another in legal matters
  • Synonym: solicitor - a type of lawyer in the UK qualified to advise clients and represent them in lower courts
  • Antonym: client - a person who engages a lawyer's services

Etymology

From Middle English 'lawyer', from 'law' + '-yer'.
