avaritia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːvəˈrɪtiə / (AmE), / ˌævəˈrɪtiə / (BrE)
Extreme and insatiable greed for wealth or gain; in Christian doctrine, personified as one of the seven deadly sins.
extreme greed; one of the seven deadly sins

Examples

Medieval theologians considered avaritia to be a grave moral failing.
The painting depicted avaritia as a figure clutching bags of gold.
In sermons, priests often warned against the dangers of avaritia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - intense or selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power
  • Antonym: generosity - the quality of being kind and giving

Etymology

From Latin 'avaritia' (greed), related to 'avarus' (greedy).
