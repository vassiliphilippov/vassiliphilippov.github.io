Vultur gryphus

1. NOUN

/ ˈvʌltʃər ˈɡrɪfəs / (AmE), / ˈvʌltʃə ˈɡrɪfəs / (BrE)
a large vulture of the high Andes with black feathers and a white neck ruff, also known as the Andean condor
Andean condor, a large South American vulture

Examples

The Vultur gryphus soars majestically over the Andean mountains, searching for carrion.
Conservation efforts have been implemented to protect the endangered Vultur gryphus population.
The white neck ruff is a distinguishing feature of the Vultur gryphus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Andean condor - the common English name for Vultur gryphus
  • Degree Lower: vulture - a large bird of prey that feeds on carrion

Etymology

From Latin: 'vultur' (vulture) and 'gryphus' (griffin, vulture).
