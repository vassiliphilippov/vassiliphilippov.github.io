algology

1. NOUN

/ ælˈɡɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ælˈɡɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of algae, especially as a branch of botany
study of algae (botany)

Examples

He pursued his doctorate in algology, focusing on marine algae.
The university's department of algology is internationally recognized for its research.
A strong background in algology is essential for studying aquatic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phycology - the scientific study of algae

Etymology

From French 'algologie', from 'algue' (alga) and Greek 'logia' (study of).
