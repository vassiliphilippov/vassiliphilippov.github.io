backscratcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌskrætʃər / (AmE), / ˈbækˌskrætʃə / (BrE)
a tool with a long handle used for scratching one's back, especially in places difficult to reach by hand
long-handled tool for scratching the back

Examples

After buying a new backscratcher, Amy finally found relief from her itchy back.
The hotel provided a wooden backscratcher as a quirky guestroom amenity.
He reached for his backscratcher to ease the irritation under his shirt.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌskrætʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌskrætʃə / (BrE)
a person who exchanges favors or services with others for mutual benefit, often in a way that is considered improper or unethical
person who trades favors for mutual benefit

Examples

As a skilled backscratcher, he always seemed to have friends in high places willing to help.
The investigation uncovered a network of political backscratchers exchanging jobs and favors.
She refused to become a backscratcher just to get ahead in her career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quid pro quo - a favor or advantage granted in return for something
  • Synonym: favoritism - the practice of giving unfair preferential treatment to one person or group

Etymology

From 'back' + 'scratcher', originally referring to a tool for scratching one's back, later figuratively extended to refer to people who 'scratch each other's backs'.
