chrome

1. NOUN

/ kroʊm / (AmE), / krəʊm / (BrE)
Chromium, especially when used in dyes or pigments to impart color.
chromium used in dyes or pigments

Examples

The artist preferred using chrome for its brilliant yellow shades.
Some green pigments in historic paintings were made from chrome.
The use of chrome in dyes gave fabrics vibrant, lasting colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromium - A hard, bluish-white metallic element, symbol Cr.

Etymology

Shortened form of 'chromium', from Greek 'khrōma' (color).

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ kɹoʊm / (AmE), / kɹəʊm / (BrE)
to treat or coat (an object) with a chromium compound, especially to produce a shiny, protective surface
treat with a chromium compound

Examples

The factory will chrome the car bumpers for durability and appearance.
Many motorcycle enthusiasts prefer to chrome their engine parts for that classic look.
It is expensive to chrome each individual piece, but the result is stunning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to chromium-plate - to coat with a thin layer of chromium

Etymology

Derived from 'chromium', itself from French 'chrome', from Greek 'khrōma' (color).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00518208-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈkroʊm / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊm / (BrE)
to cover or plate (a metal object) with a thin layer of chromium, typically for protection or to give a shiny appearance
to plate with chromium

Examples

The car parts were chromed to improve both appearance and corrosion resistance.
They decided to chrome the bathroom fixtures for a modern look.
After restoration, the vintage bicycle had all its metal pieces chromed to a mirror finish.

Synset Examples

chrome bathroom fixtures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroplate - to coat an object with a thin layer of metal using electrolysis

Etymology

From chromium, the chemical element, used for plating.
