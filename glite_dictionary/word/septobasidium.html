Septobasidium

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛptəʊbəˈsɪdiəm / (AmE), / ˌsɛptəbəˈsɪdɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of smooth shelf fungi in the family Septobasidiaceae, characterized by a well-developed and sometimes thick-walled hypobasidium.
genus of shelf fungi (Septobasidiaceae)
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered several new species within the Septobasidium genus in tropical forests.
Septobasidium fungi often form smooth, shelf-like structures on the bark of trees.
The defining feature of Septobasidium is its thick-walled hypobasidium, important in spore production.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'septo-' (referring to a septum or partition) and 'basidium' (a fungal spore-producing structure).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛptoʊbəˈsɪdiəm / (AmE), / ˌsɛptəbəˈsɪdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of shelf fungi in the family Septobasidiaceae, typically bearing smooth fruiting bodies and a well-developed, sometimes thick-walled hypobasidium.
a genus of shelf fungi

Examples

The Septobasidium genus is notable for its association with scale insects on plant surfaces.
Fungi in the Septobasidium group can form extensive, smooth mats on tree branches.
Researchers collected several specimens of Septobasidium during their tropical rainforest expedition.

Etymology

From Greek 'septo-' (putrefying, rotting) and 'basidium' (small base), a reference to the structure of the fungus.
