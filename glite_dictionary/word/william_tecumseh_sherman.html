William Tecumseh Sherman

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm tɪˈkʌmsə ˈʃɜrmən / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm tɪˈkʌmsɪ ˈʃɜːmən / (BrE)
(1820–1891) United States general who was commander of all Union troops in the Western Theater during the Civil War; known for capturing Atlanta and his destructive 'March to the Sea' that split the Confederacy.
US Civil War general, led March to the Sea

Examples

William Tecumseh Sherman played a pivotal role in the Union victory during the American Civil War.
Many historians study the leadership tactics of William Tecumseh Sherman in the Western Theater.
The city of Atlanta fell to William Tecumseh Sherman before his famous march to the sea.
