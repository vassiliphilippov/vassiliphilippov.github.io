octroi

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˈtrɔɪ / (AmE), / ɒkˈtrwɑː / (BrE)
a tax levied on certain goods as they enter a town or city, especially formerly in France or India
tax on goods entering a town

Examples

The merchants were required to pay octroi before bringing their goods into the city.
Many cities abolished octroi in the twentieth century to simplify trade.
The city's revenue from octroi declined after new tax laws were implemented.

Etymology

From French 'octroi' (grant, toll), from Old French 'octroyer' (to grant).
