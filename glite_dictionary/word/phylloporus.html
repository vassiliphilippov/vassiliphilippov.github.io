Phylloporus

1. PROPN

/ fɪˈlɑːpɔːrəs / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒpərəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Boletaceae, comprising about 50 species mostly found in tropical regions.
genus of fungi in Boletaceae

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species of Phylloporus in the Amazon rainforest.
Members of the Phylloporus genus are distinguished by their unique gill-like structures.
The yellow capped mushroom she found in the forest was identified as Phylloporus.

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) and 'poros' (pore), referring to the leaf-like gills of the fungus.

2. NOUN

/ fɪˈlɑpərəs / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒpərəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Boletaceae, consisting of about 50 species found mostly in tropical regions.
a genus of fungi in Boletaceae

Examples

Many species in the Phylloporus genus are found growing on decaying wood in tropical forests.
Researchers recently discovered a new member of the Phylloporus genus in Central America.
The unique pore structure of Phylloporus helps distinguish it from other fungi in Boletaceae.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Boletaceae - family of fungi including boletes

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) + 'poros' (pore), referring to the leaf-like pores of the fungus.
