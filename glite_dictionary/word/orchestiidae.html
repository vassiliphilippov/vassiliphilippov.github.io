Orchestiidae

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˌkɛstiːˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ɔːˌkɛstiːˈiːdaɪ / (BrE)
a family of small, jumping amphipod crustaceans commonly known as beach fleas, typically found on seashores.
family of beach flea crustaceans

Examples

Researchers collected several specimens of Orchestiidae from the tide line for their study.
The Orchestiidae play an important role in the coastal ecosystem by breaking down seaweed.
You can often spot Orchestiidae hopping along the sandy shoreline after high tide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beach flea - a small, jumping amphipod crustacean found on beaches

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Orchestia + the zoological family suffix -idae.

2. NOUN

/ ɔːrˌkɛs.tiˈaɪ.di / (AmE), / ɔːˌkɛs.tiˈiː.daɪ / (BrE)
a family of small amphipod crustaceans known as beach fleas, commonly found on seashores and in coastal habitats
family of beach flea crustaceans

Examples

Members of the Orchestiidae family are commonly called beach fleas due to their jumping ability.
The Orchestiidae are often seen scurrying along the shoreline after waves recede.
Researchers studied the distribution of Orchestiidae in various coastal ecosystems.

Etymology

From the genus name Orchestia in scientific Latin.
