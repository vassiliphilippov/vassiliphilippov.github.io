latter

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈlatə / (BrE)
the second of two people or things previously mentioned
the second of two mentioned

Examples

Between coffee and tea, I prefer the latter.
She had to choose between London and Paris, and she selected the latter.
Of the two brothers, the latter is more outgoing.

Synset Examples

Tom and Dick were both heroes but only the latter is remembered today

Related Terms

  • Antonym: former - the first of two people or things mentioned

Etymology

From Old English 'lættra', comparative of 'læt' (late).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13868905-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈlæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈlætə / (BrE)
Referring to the second of two people or things, or the last one or ones mentioned of several.
the second of two; last-mentioned

Examples

Between tea and coffee, I prefer the latter when I need an energy boost.
Of the two proposals, the latter offers a more practical solution to our problem.
He lived in Paris and London, but it was the latter that truly felt like home.

Synset Examples

in the latter case

Related Terms

  • Antonym: former - referring to the first of two things or persons mentioned

Etymology

From Old English 'lætra' (comparative of 'late').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01051260-a
Synset POS: a
