eyeglass

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪˌɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a single lens worn over one eye to correct defective vision, typically held in place by the facial muscles
single corrective lens for one eye

Examples

He adjusted his eyeglass carefully before reading the letter.
The aristocrat wore a gold-rimmed eyeglass at the party, giving him a distinguished look.
She peered through her eyeglass, trying to make out the distant inscription.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monocle - a single round eyeglass for one eye, usually held in place by the muscles around the eye
  • Degree Higher: spectacles - a pair of lenses set in a frame worn to correct vision

Etymology

From 'eye' + 'glass', first recorded in the 18th century as a term for a single spectacle lens.
