testcross

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛstkɹɒs / (AmE), / ˈtɛstkɹɒs / (BrE)
a genetic cross between an individual of unknown genotype and a homozygous recessive individual, used to determine the unknown genotype from the traits of the offspring
genetic cross with homozygous recessive to identify genotype
medical

Examples

To determine if the pea plant was heterozygous or homozygous for the trait, the scientist performed a testcross.
A testcross can help reveal the genotype of an organism exhibiting a dominant phenotype.
After analyzing the offspring from the testcross, the researcher concluded that the parent was heterozygous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to backcross - crossing a hybrid organism with one of its parents or someone genetically similar

Etymology

Formed from 'test' + 'cross', indicating a cross for testing genotypes.
