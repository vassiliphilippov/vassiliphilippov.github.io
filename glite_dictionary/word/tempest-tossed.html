tempest-tossed

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛmpəst tɔst / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpɪst tɒst / (BrE)
battered or thrown about by storms or, figuratively, by great difficulties or misfortunes
battered by storms or adversity

Examples

The tempest-tossed sailors finally reached the safety of the harbor.
In her speech, she offered hope to the tempest-tossed and downtrodden.
After years of tempest-tossed struggles, he found a moment of peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm-tossed - thrown about by storms
  • Antonym: calm - free from disturbance or rough conditions

Etymology

From 'tempest' (violent storm) and past participle 'tossed', popularized by Emma Lazarus's poem 'The New Colossus' (1883).
