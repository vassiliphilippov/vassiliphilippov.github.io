putoff

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊtˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈpʊtɒf / (BrE)
An excuse or pretext used to delay or avoid action.
an excuse for delaying action
informal

Examples

I'm tired of hearing the same old putoff whenever I ask for an update.
His promise to look into the matter was just a clever putoff.
She always comes up with a new putoff when it's time to make a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse - a reason or explanation to justify a fault or offense
