hawk's-beard

1. NOUN

/ hɔksˌbɪrd / (AmE), / hɔːksˌbɪəd / (BrE)
any of various plants of the genus Crepis, with loose heads of yellow flowers on long branched leafy stems, native to the Northern Hemisphere
plant of the genus Crepis with yellow flowers

Examples

The meadow was dotted with bright hawk's-beards swaying in the breeze.
Gardeners often mistake hawk's-beard for dandelion because of its similar yellow flowers.
Several species of hawk's-beard thrive in the temperate climates of northern Europe.

Etymology

Named for the supposed resemblance of the flower's seed-heads to the beard of a hawk.
