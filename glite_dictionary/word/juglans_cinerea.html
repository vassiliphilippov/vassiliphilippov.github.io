Juglans cinerea

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌɡ.lænz sɪˈnɪr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡ.lænz sɪˈnɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A species of North American walnut tree, commonly called butternut, noted for its light-brown wood, edible nuts, and as a source of a light-brown dye.
a North American walnut tree (butternut)

Examples

The wood of Juglans cinerea is prized for its light color and easy workability.
Many wildlife species rely on the nuts produced by Juglans cinerea for food.
Fungal diseases have greatly reduced the population of Juglans cinerea in its native range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butternut - another common name for Juglans cinerea
  • Degree Higher: walnut - a tree of the genus Juglans, or its edible nut

Etymology

From Latin: 'Juglans' meaning 'walnut'; 'cinerea' meaning 'ash-colored', referring to the bark.
