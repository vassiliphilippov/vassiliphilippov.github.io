asterism

1. NOUN

/ ˈæs.təˌrɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈas.tə.rɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A star-shaped figure with six rays visible in certain crystals when viewed under reflected or transmitted light, especially in gemstones.
six-rayed star pattern in crystals
medical

Examples

The asterism in the sapphire made it particularly valuable to collectors.
Under the jeweler's lamp, a clear asterism appeared on the surface of the gem.
Star sapphires are prized for the beautiful asterism seen when they are moved in light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star stone - A gemstone, such as a ruby or sapphire, exhibiting asterism

Etymology

From Greek *asterismos* (marking with stars), from *astēr* (star).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæstərˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈastərɪzəm / (BrE)
a group or recognizable pattern of stars in the night sky, often smaller than a constellation
group of stars forming a pattern

Examples

The Summer Triangle is a well-known asterism visible in the northern hemisphere's summer sky.
The Big Dipper is an asterism that forms part of the constellation Ursa Major.
Many amateur astronomers enjoy finding different asterisms with their telescopes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: constellation - a recognized group of stars forming a pattern, officially named

Etymology

From Greek 'asterismos' (a marking with stars), from 'astēr' (star).
