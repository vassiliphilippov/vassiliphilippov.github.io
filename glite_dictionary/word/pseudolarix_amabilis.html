Pseudolarix amabilis

1. NOUN

/ sudoʊˈlærɪks əˈmæbɪlɪs / (AmE), / sjuːdəʊˈlærɪks əˈmabɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of deciduous conifer native to China, resembling a larch and known for its golden yellow autumn leaves; also called the golden larch.
Chinese deciduous conifer tree species

Examples

The Pseudolarix amabilis is prized in gardens for its striking golden foliage in autumn.
Botanists were excited to discover a mature Pseudolarix amabilis in the remote mountains of China.
Unlike true larches, Pseudolarix amabilis cones disintegrate at maturity rather than falling whole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden larch - Common English name for Pseudolarix amabilis, a Chinese deciduous conifer

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name from pseudo- (false) + Larix (larch), species name from Latin 'amabilis' (lovely, pleasing).
