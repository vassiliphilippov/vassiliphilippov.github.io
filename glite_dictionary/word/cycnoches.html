Cycnoches

1. NOUN

/ sɪkˈnoʊkiːz / (AmE), / sɪkˈnəʊkiːz / (BrE)
A genus of epiphytic or terrestrial tropical American orchids, known for their distinctive flowers.
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The rare Cycnoches orchid bloomed unexpectedly in the greenhouse this year.
Botanists often classify these unusual orchids under the genus Cycnoches.
Collectors value Cycnoches for their striking, swan-shaped flowers.

Etymology

From Greek *kyknos* (swan) and *oikos* (house), referring to the swan-like shape of some flower parts.

/ sɪkˈnoʊkiːz / (AmE), / sɪkˈnəʊkiːz / (BrE)
A genus of epiphytic or terrestrial orchids native to tropical America, known for their distinctive flowers.
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The greenhouse boasts a rare specimen of Cycnoches in full bloom.
Botanists conducted a detailed study of Cycnoches species in the Amazon rainforest.
Collectors prize Cycnoches orchids for their unusual swan-shaped flowers.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Greek 'kyknos' (swan) + '-o-' + 'ches' (neck), referring to the swan-like shape of some species' flowers.
