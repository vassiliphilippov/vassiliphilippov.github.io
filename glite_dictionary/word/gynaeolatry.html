gynaeolatry

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡaɪniˈɒlətri / (AmE), / ˌɡaɪniˈɒlətri / (BrE)
the worship or excessive adoration of women
worship of women

Examples

His fascination with powerful female figures bordered on gynaeolatry.
The novel explores society's relationship with women, sometimes verging on gynaeolatry.
Critics accused the artist of gynaeolatry due to the reverential way he depicted women in his work.

Etymology

From Greek gynē (woman) + latreia (worship).
