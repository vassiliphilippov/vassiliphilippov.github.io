choral

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔr.əl / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.rəl / (BrE)
relating to or written for a choir or chorus; intended to be sung by a group of singers.
relating to a choir or chorus

Examples

The school organized a choral concert featuring songs from different cultures.
She joined a choral group to improve her singing skills and meet new people.
His latest work is a choral piece inspired by traditional folk melodies.

Synset Examples

choral composition
choral ensemble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chorus - a group of singers or the music written for them

Etymology

From Latin choralis (of a chorus or choir), from chorus (chorus).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02885586-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.əl / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.rəl / (BrE)
a hymn tune, especially one associated with Protestant (particularly Lutheran) traditions, characterized by a stately and harmonized style
stately Protestant hymn tune

Examples

The choir performed a beautiful choral at the Sunday service.
Bach's compositions often include a choral at the end of each cantata.
She studied the structure of the Lutheran choral for her musicology thesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chorale - a hymn tune sung in church, usually in four-part harmony

Etymology

From German 'Choral', itself from Latin 'choralis' (relating to a choir).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07050074-n
Synset POS: n
