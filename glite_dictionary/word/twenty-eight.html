3. NUM

/ ˌtwɛntiˈeɪt / (AmE), / ˌtwɛntiˈeɪt / (BrE)
The number that is eight more than twenty; the cardinal number 28.
the number 28

Examples

There are twenty-eight days in February during a common year.
She baked twenty-eight cookies for the school fundraiser.
The train arrives at platform twenty-eight every morning at eight o'clock.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: twenty-seven - the number 27
  • Degree Higher: twenty-nine - the number 29

Etymology

From the combination of 'twenty' (Old English 'twēntig') and 'eight' (Old English 'eahta').
