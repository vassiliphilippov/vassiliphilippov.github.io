Firmiana

1. NOUN

/ fɚˈmaɪ.ə.nə / (AmE), / fɜːˈmiː.ə.nə / (BrE)
a genus of Asian trees or shrubs in the mallow family, known for their ornamental foliage and distinctive flowers
genus of Asian trees/shrubs

Examples

Several species in the genus Firmiana are cultivated for their decorative leaves and shade.
The Firmiana simplex, also known as the Chinese parasol tree, is popular in landscaping.
Botanists have identified new varieties within the Firmiana genus in Southeast Asia.

Etymology

Named after Karl Joseph von Firmian, an Austrian statesman and patron of botany.

2. NOUN

/ fɚˈmiː.ə.nə / (AmE), / fɜːˈmiː.ə.nə / (BrE)
a small genus of Asian trees or shrubs in the mallow family, known for their ornamental value and large leaves
genus of Asian trees and shrubs

Examples

The Firmiana tree is prized for its elegant shape and broad leaves in ornamental gardens.
Botanists discovered a new species belonging to the genus Firmiana in Southeast Asia.
Some species in the Firmiana genus are valued for their useful wood and fibrous bark.

Etymology

Named after Karl Joseph von Firmian, Austrian diplomat and patron of botany.
