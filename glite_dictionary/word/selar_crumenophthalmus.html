Selar crumenophthalmus

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlɑr kruˌmɛn.ɑfˈθæl.məs / (AmE), / sɛˈlɑː kruˌmɛn.ɒfˈθæl.məs / (BrE)
A species of fish found in Atlantic coastal waters, commonly used as bait in fishing.
species of bait fish

Examples

Fishermen often catch Selar crumenophthalmus to use as live bait for larger game fish.
In some coastal markets, Selar crumenophthalmus is sold alongside other small pelagic fishes.
Marine biologists study the migration patterns of Selar crumenophthalmus in the Atlantic Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bigeye scad - another common name for Selar crumenophthalmus

Etymology

From New Latin genus Selar + species crumenophthalmus, meaning 'pouch-eyed', referring to a characteristic of the fish.
