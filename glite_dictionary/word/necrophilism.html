necrophilism

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛk.rəˌfɪ.lɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈnɛk.rəˌfɪl.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
a sexual attraction or sexual desire directed toward dead bodies; necrophilia
sexual attraction to dead bodies
medical

Examples

The criminal was found to have a history of necrophilism that shocked the community.
Psychiatric studies sometimes address rare disorders like necrophilism in forensic contexts.
His novel touched briefly on the disturbing subject of necrophilism among other taboos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necrophilia - sexual attraction to dead bodies

Etymology

From Greek nekros (dead body) + philos (loving) + -ism (suffix indicating practice or condition).
