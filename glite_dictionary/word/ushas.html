Ushas

1. PROPN

/ ˈuːʃɑs / (AmE), / ˈuːʃɑːs / (BrE)
In Hindu mythology, the goddess of dawn, regarded as the daughter of the sky and sister of the night.
Hindu goddess of dawn

Examples

In ancient Vedic hymns, Ushas is praised as the bringer of light and renewal.
Many prayers in the Rigveda are dedicated to Ushas, celebrating her beauty and grace.
The daily arrival of dawn is symbolically attributed to Ushas in Hindu tradition.

Etymology

From Sanskrit उषस् (uṣás, meaning "dawn").
