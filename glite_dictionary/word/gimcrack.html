gimcrack

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪm.kræk / (AmE), / ˈɡɪm.kræk / (BrE)
A showy but worthless or cheap ornament; a trinket or bauble of little value.
cheap decorative trinket

Examples

She filled her shelves with gimcracks collected from flea markets.
The mantel was cluttered with old gimcracks and dusty photographs.
He dismissed the figurines as mere gimcracks not worth keeping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trinket - a small decorative object of little value
  • Synonym: bauble - a small, inexpensive decorative item

Etymology

Origin unknown; attested from the 17th century, possibly imitative or related to earlier forms 'gewgaw' or 'gimcrackery'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡɪmˌkræk / (AmE), / ˈɡɪm.kræk / (BrE)
showy but poorly made and of little value; tastelessly flashy or cheap
showy but cheap and poorly made

Examples

The living room was decorated with gimcrack ornaments that glittered but looked cheap.
She wore a gimcrack necklace that caught everyone’s eye, but upon closer inspection, it was just plastic.
The shop was full of gimcrack souvenirs that appealed to tourists looking for something flashy.

Synset Examples

a meretricious yet stylish book
tawdry ornaments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaudy - brilliantly or excessively showy; tastelessly flashy
  • Synonym: tawdry - showy but cheap and of poor quality
  • Antonym: tasteful - having or showing good taste

Etymology

From obsolete gimcrack (a showy object), origin uncertain, possibly imitative.
