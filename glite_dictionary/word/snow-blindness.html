snow-blindness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌblaɪnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌblaɪnd.nəs / (BrE)
Temporary loss of vision caused by exposure to the sun’s ultraviolet rays reflected off snow or ice.
temporary blindness from snow glare
medical

Examples

The hiker suffered from snow-blindness after trekking across the glacier without sunglasses.
Wearing proper eye protection can help prevent snow-blindness during winter activities.
Doctors treated her snow-blindness with rest and soothing eyedrops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photokeratitis - inflammation of the cornea caused by UV light, often from snow reflection
