topsy-turvily

1. ADV

/ ˌtɑpsi ˈtɝvəli / (AmE), / ˌtɒpsi ˈtɜːvɪli / (BrE)
In a disorderly or confused manner; in a state of disarray or confusion.
in a disorderly or confused way

Examples

He tossed his papers topsy-turvily onto the desk in frustration.
The wind blew the leaves topsy-turvily across the empty street all afternoon.
They rushed topsy-turvily to the exit when the alarm sounded.

Synset Examples

we ran head over heels toward the shelter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: topsy-turvy - in a state of confusion or disorder (adjective/adverb)

Etymology

From 'topsy-turvy', which originated as a reduplication of 'top' with a fanciful alteration, used since the 16th century.
