resolute

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛzəˌlut / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəluːt / (BrE)
(of a person or their actions) quick and firm in purpose or belief; displaying determination and unwavering commitment.
quick and firm in purpose or belief

Examples

Despite opposition, her resolute actions inspired her team to move forward.
His resolute answer left no room for doubt or discussion.
The board appreciated the manager's resolute decision-making in a time of crisis.

Synset Examples

his reply was unhesitating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determined - having made a firm decision and being resolved not to change it
  • Synonym: steadfast - firm and unwavering
  • Antonym: hesitant - unsure or slow in acting or speaking
  • Antonym: wavering - showing indecision or hesitation

Etymology

From Latin resolūtus (determined, resolved), past participle of resolvere (to loosen, release).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛzəˌlut / (AmE), / ˈrɛzəluːt / (BrE)
determined in character, action, or belief; unwavering and firm in purpose
resolutely firm or determined

Examples

She remained resolute throughout the long and difficult negotiations.
His resolute refusal to comply surprised everyone in the meeting.
Despite the criticism, the team stayed resolute in its pursuit of success.

Synset Examples

stood resolute against the enemy
faced with a resolute opposition
a resolute and unshakeable faith

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determined - having made a firm decision and being resolved not to change it
  • Synonym: steadfast - firm and unwavering
  • Antonym: hesitant - uncertain or unwilling to take a clear action
  • Antonym: wavering - being undecided between two opinions or courses of action

Etymology

From Latin 'resolutus', past participle of 'resolvere' (to loosen, resolve).
