to recommend

1. VERB

/ ˌrɛk.əˈmɛnd / (AmE), / ˌrɛk.əˈmɛnd / (BrE)
to make someone or something appear attractive or worthy of approval
make attractive or acceptable

Examples

Her generosity recommends her to everyone she meets.
A pleasant voice recommends a radio announcer for the job.
The hotel's excellent service recommends it to business travelers.

Synset Examples

Honesty recommends any person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commend - to praise or present as worthy
  • Antonym: discredit - to harm the reputation or attractiveness of

Etymology

From Latin recommendare, from re- (again) + commendare (to commend).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00579810-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌrɛk.əˈmɛnd / (AmE), / ˌrɛk.əˈmɛnd / (BrE)
to advise someone to do something or suggest something as a suitable choice or course of action
advise or suggest as best course

Examples

I recommend that you try the new Italian restaurant downtown.
Doctors often recommend regular exercise for a healthy lifestyle.
She recommended taking an umbrella, as it looked like it might rain.

Synset Examples

The travel agent recommended strongly that we not travel on Thanksgiving Day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suggest - to mention as a possible option or idea
  • Synonym: to advise - to give advice to someone
  • Antonym: to discourage - to advise against something

Etymology

From Latin recommendare, from re- (again) + commendare (to commend).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00876672-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌrɛk.əˈmɛnd / (AmE), / ˌrɛk.əˈmɛnd / (BrE)
to say that someone or something is good or suitable for a particular purpose or job; to express approval or support for someone or something
express approval or suggest as suitable

Examples

I would recommend this book to anyone interested in history.
Many doctors recommend daily exercise to improve overall health.
She recommended a new restaurant that recently opened downtown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endorse - to publicly express support or approval for someone or something
  • Antonym: to disparage - to speak about someone or something in a derogatory or negative way

Etymology

From Latin recommendare, from re- (again) + commendare (to commit to, entrust).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00884469-v
Synset POS: v
