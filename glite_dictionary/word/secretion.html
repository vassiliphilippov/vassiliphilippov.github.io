secretion

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkriʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈkriːʃən / (BrE)
a substance produced and released by a cell or gland, especially one serving a particular function rather than being waste
substance produced by a cell or gland
medical

Examples

Saliva is a secretion produced by glands in the mouth.
The doctor studied the secretion from the infected wound to diagnose the illness.
Hormones are released into the bloodstream as a secretion from endocrine glands.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: excretion - the process of eliminating waste products from the body

Etymology

From Latin secretio (a separating, separation), from secernere (to separate, distinguish).

2. NOUN

/ səˈkriʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈkriːʃən / (BrE)
the process by which cells or glands produce and release a substance, especially for a particular function in the body
production and release of a substance by cells or glands
medical

Examples

The salivary glands are responsible for the secretion of saliva.
Increased secretion of hormones can indicate an endocrine disorder.
The doctor explained the importance of mucus secretion in protecting the respiratory tract.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: excretion - the process of eliminating waste products from the body

Etymology

From Latin 'secretio' (separation), from 'secernere' (to separate).
