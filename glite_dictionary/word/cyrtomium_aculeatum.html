Cyrtomium aculeatum

1. NOUN

/ sɪrˈtoʊmi.əm ˌækjuˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / sɪəˈtəʊmɪəm ˌækjʊˈleɪtəm / (BrE)
A tropical Old World fern with glossy, holly-like fronds, sometimes classified in the genus Polystichum.
tropical fern with holly-like fronds

Examples

The shiny fronds of Cyrtomium aculeatum make it a popular choice for shaded gardens.
Botanists sometimes place Cyrtomium aculeatum within the genus Polystichum.
In its native habitat, Cyrtomium aculeatum thrives in damp, wooded areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holly fern - fern with leaves resembling those of holly

Etymology

From Greek 'kyrtos' (curved) and Latin 'aculeatus' (prickly).
