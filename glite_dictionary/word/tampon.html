tampon

1. VERB

/ ˈtæm.pɑn / (AmE), / ˈtam.pɒn / (BrE)
to stop the flow of blood or other fluids by inserting a tampon or similar absorbent material into a body cavity or wound
stop flow by inserting tampon
medical

Examples

The surgeon decided to tampon the wound to control the bleeding.
Doctors had to tampon the nasal cavity after the injury.
They quickly tamponed the open wound with gauze to stem the blood flow.

Etymology

From French 'tampon' (plug, pad), from Old French 'tampion' (plug or stopper).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæmpɑn / (AmE), / ˈtæmpɒn / (BrE)
a plug of cotton, wool, or other absorbent material inserted into a wound or body cavity to absorb bodily fluids, especially blood
absorbent plug for wounds or cavities

Examples

The nurse inserted a tampon to control the bleeding from the wound.
Doctors may use a surgical tampon during certain procedures to absorb blood.
After the accident, a tampon was applied to the injury until help arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pad - an absorbent item used externally to absorb fluids, such as menstrual pads

Etymology

From French *tampon* (plug, stopper), from Old French *taper* (to tamp or seal).
