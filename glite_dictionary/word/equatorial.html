equatorial

1. ADJ

/ ˌekwəˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ˌekwəˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
of, relating to, or located at the equator or an equatorial region
relating to the equator

Examples

Rainforests are common in equatorial regions because of the warm, humid climate.
The city lies almost directly on the equatorial line.
The satellite uses an equatorial orbit to maintain a constant position above the Earth's surface.

Synset Examples

equatorial diameter

Etymology

From Medieval Latin aequatorialis, from Latin aequator (equalizer, the equator).

4. NOUN

/ ˌekwəˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ˌekwəˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
an astronomical telescope or its mounting with two axes, one parallel to the Earth's axis and one perpendicular, allowing the telescope to track celestial objects as the Earth rotates
a telescope or mounting with two axes for tracking objects

Examples

The observatory recently installed a new equatorial to improve its tracking accuracy.
Using an equatorial, astronomers can easily follow stars as they move across the night sky.
The old equatorial in the dome requires careful calibration before use.

Etymology

From equator + -ial, referring to alignment with the Earth's equator.
