boycott

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪkɑt / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪkɒt / (BrE)
an organized refusal to buy, use, or participate in something as a way of protesting against policies or actions
organized protest by refusing to deal

Examples

The workers called for a boycott of the company’s products until better labor practices were adopted.
A national boycott led to a significant drop in sales for the brand.
Many activists organized a boycott of the store in response to its environmental record.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - an act of expressing strong objection or disapproval
  • Synonym: embargo - an official ban on trade or other commercial activity
  • Antonym: to patronize - to support a business by being a customer

Etymology

From Captain Charles C. Boycott, 19th-century Irish land agent, who was subject to this form of protest.

2. VERB

/ ˈbɔɪˌkɑt / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪˌkɒt / (BrE)
to refuse to buy, use, or participate in something as a way of protesting or forcing change
refuse to do business with

Examples

Several groups agreed to boycott the company's products until their demands were met.
The students decided to boycott the lecture in protest against the new policy.
Many countries chose to boycott the Olympics as a form of political protest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: support - to give assistance or approval to something

Etymology

From the name of Captain Charles C. Boycott (1832–1897), an English land agent ostracized by his Irish community.
