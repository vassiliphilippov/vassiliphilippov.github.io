Arizona elegans

1. NOUN

/ əˌrɪzoʊnə ˈɛlɪɡænz / (AmE), / əˌrɪzəʊnə ˈɛlɪɡænz / (BrE)
A species of nocturnal, burrowing snake found in the western United States, with shiny tan scales; also known as the glossy snake.
nocturnal burrowing snake of western US

Examples

The Arizona elegans prefers sandy deserts where it can burrow easily.
Researchers tracked the movement patterns of Arizona elegans during their nocturnal foraging.
A juvenile Arizona elegans was found hiding under a rock near the hiking trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glossy snake - common name for Arizona elegans, a burrowing snake of the southwestern United States

Etymology

From Latin: 'Arizona' (the U.S. state) + 'elegans' (elegant).
