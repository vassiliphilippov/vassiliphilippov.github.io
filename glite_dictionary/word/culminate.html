to culminate

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌlmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkʌlmɪneɪt / (BrE)
(of an object or structure) to rise to or form a highest point or summit.
rise to or form a summit

Examples

The mountain range culminates in a snow-capped peak.
The ancient temple culminates in a magnificent dome.
Her elaborate hairstyle culminated in a dramatic twist at the crown.

Synset Examples

The helmet culminated in a crest

Etymology

From Latin culminare (to crown, to top), from culmen (summit, peak).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00143860-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkʌlməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkʌlmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to reach the highest or most important point; to arrive at a final or climactic stage
reach the highest point or climax

Examples

Months of preparation will culminate in the opening ceremony next Friday.
Their disagreement culminated in a heated argument.
The movie culminates with an unexpected twist in the final scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climax - the most intense or decisive point in an event or story
  • Antonym: to begin - to start; to come into existence or take the first step

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *culminare* (to crown, top), from Latin *culmen* (summit, peak).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02532600-v
Synset POS: v

/ ˈkʌlmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkʌlmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to reach the highest or most decisive point, especially as the final stage of a process or series of events
reach the highest or final point

Examples

The year-long investigation culminated with the arrest of several high-ranking officials.
Her efforts culminated in a stunning performance at the concert.
The festival culminates in a magnificent fireworks display.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conclude - to bring something to an end
  • Synonym: finish - to bring to completion
  • Antonym: to begin - to start something; the opposite of ending

Etymology

From Latin culminare (to crown, finish), from culmen (summit, peak).

4. VERB

/ ˈkʌlməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkʌlmɪneɪt / (BrE)
(of a celestial body) to reach the highest point in the sky or the meridian.
reach highest point in the sky (astronomy)

Examples

The planet Mars will culminate at midnight, appearing directly overhead.
During the equinox, the sun culminates exactly at the zenith for locations on the equator.
The star Sirius culminated early this morning, marking its highest point above the horizon.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'culminare' (to crown or top), from Latin 'culmen' (summit, peak).
