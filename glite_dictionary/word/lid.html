lid

1. NOUN

/ lɪd / (AmE), / lɪd / (BrE)
a movable top or cover for closing the opening of a container, such as a box, jar, or pan
movable cover for a container

Examples

She lifted the lid of the pot to check if the soup was boiling.
Please remember to put the lid back on the jar after using it.
He carefully closed the piano lid to protect the keys.

Synset Examples

he raised the piano lid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - something that lies over or encloses something else

Etymology

From Middle English lyd, from Old English hlid (cover, lid, gateway).

2. NOUN

/ lɪd / (AmE), / lɪd / (BrE)
either of the two folds of skin that can be moved to cover or uncover the eye; eyelid
the movable fold of skin over the eye

Examples

She closed her lid to block out the bright sunlight.
The doctor examined the swelling on his upper lid.
Tears slipped from beneath her trembling lid.

Synset Examples

his lids would stay open no longer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyelid - the movable fold of skin that covers and protects the eye
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05321347-n
Synset POS: n
