Eriocaulon aquaticum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛri.oʊˈkɔːlɒn əˈkwætɪkəm / (AmE), / ˌɛri.əʊˈkɔːlɒn əˈkwatɪkəm / (BrE)
A species of aquatic perennial plant found in North America, Ireland, and the Hebrides, noted for its translucent green leaves arranged in a basal spiral and dense, buttonlike clusters of tiny white flowers.
aquatic perennial plant species

Examples

The dense flower heads of Eriocaulon aquaticum are a distinctive feature along northern lakeshores.
Botanists documented an extensive population of Eriocaulon aquaticum in western Ireland's bogs.
Gardeners appreciate Eriocaulon aquaticum for its ability to thrive in aquatic plant collections.

Etymology

From Greek 'erion' (wool) + 'kaulos' (stalk) referring to the genus, and Latin 'aquaticum' meaning 'of water' for the species epithet.
