grigri

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːˌɡriː / (AmE), / ˈɡriːˌɡriː / (BrE)
An amulet or charm, especially one originating from West African tradition, believed to protect against evil or bring good luck.
African amulet or charm

Examples

The traveler wore a grigri around his neck for protection during his journey.
She believed that the grigri would bring good fortune to her family.
Vendors at the market offered many types of handmade grigris to ward off bad spirits.

Etymology

From West African French, perhaps originally from Mandinka or Bambara languages.
