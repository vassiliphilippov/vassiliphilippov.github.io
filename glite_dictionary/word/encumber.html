to encumber

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkʌmbər / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌmbə / (BrE)
to restrict or burden (someone or something) in a way that impedes movement or action
to restrict or burden movement or action

Examples

Heavy equipment can encumber workers, reducing their efficiency.
The hikers were encumbered by their oversized backpacks.
Strict policies encumber the ability of the company to innovate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - to load heavily or weigh down
  • Synonym: hinder - to create difficulties that slow down or impede
  • Antonym: free - to make (someone or something) unrestricted

Etymology

From Old French 'encombrer', from 'en-' (in) + 'combrer' (to block or hinder).
