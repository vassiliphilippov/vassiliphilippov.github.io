cycloid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪˌklɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.klɔɪd / (BrE)
a curve traced by a point on the circumference of a circle as it rolls along a straight line without slipping
mathematical curve traced by a rolling circle

Examples

The path traced by the tip of a bicycle wheel is a perfect cycloid.
Mathematicians often study the properties of the cycloid in the context of physics and engineering.
She drew a cycloid on the whiteboard to explain its significance in classical mechanics.

Etymology

From Greek kykloeidēs (circle-like), from kyklos (circle).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsaɪklɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈsaɪklɔɪd / (BrE)
having the form or appearance of a circle; circular in shape.
circular in shape

Examples

The artist drew a cycloid pattern on the canvas, evoking the shape of a wheel.
Many ancient buildings feature cycloid arches in their design.
The logo's cycloid outline gives it a sense of symmetry and balance.

Related Terms

Etymology
