interdependency

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tɚ.dɪˈpɛn.dən.si / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tə.dɪˈpen.dən.si / (BrE)
The condition of two or more things depending on each other for support, help, or existence.
mutual dependence; reciprocal relationship

Examples

The interdependency of global economies became evident during the financial crisis.
Effective teamwork requires an understanding of interdependency among team members.
Environmental issues highlight the interdependency between humans and the natural world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interdependence - the state of being mutually dependent
  • Antonym: independence - the state of not depending on others
  • Antonym: dependence - the state of relying on or being controlled by someone or something else

Etymology

Formed from 'inter-' (between) and 'dependency' (the state of being dependent).
