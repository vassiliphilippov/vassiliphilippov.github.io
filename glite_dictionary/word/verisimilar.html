verisimilar

1. ADJ

/ ˌvɛrɪˈsɪmələr / (AmE), / ˌvɛrɪˈsɪmɪlə / (BrE)
appearing to be true or real; having the appearance of truth.
appearing true or real

Examples

The film's setting was so verisimilar that viewers felt transported to another era.
She told a verisimilar story that convinced everyone in the room.
The novel is praised for its verisimilar portrayal of historical events.

Synset Examples

a verisimilar tale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plausible - seeming reasonable or probable; appearing true
  • Antonym: implausible - not seeming reasonable or probable; failing to convince

Etymology

From Latin verisimilis (having the appearance of truth), from verus (true) + similis (like).
