solid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːlɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪd / (BrE)
a three-dimensional geometric object that is structurally rigid and usually resistant to changes of shape or volume
three-dimensional geometric object

Examples

A cube is a perfect example of a solid in geometry.
The mathematician studied the properties of each solid carefully.
Ice is the solid form of water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: figure - a geometric form or shape

Etymology

From Latin solidus (firm, whole, undivided).

/ ˈsɑlɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪd / (BrE)

Examples

Related Terms
