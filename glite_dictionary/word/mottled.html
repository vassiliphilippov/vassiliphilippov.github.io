mottled

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːt̬əld / (AmE), / ˈmɒtəld / (BrE)
marked with spots, patches, or streaks of different colors or shades.
marked with spots or patches of color

Examples

The old stone wall was mottled with patches of lichen and moss.
Her dress had a beautiful mottled pattern of greens and browns.
The puppy's fur appeared mottled in the afternoon sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speckled - covered or marked with small spots or specks
  • Synonym: spotted - having spots or round marks of color or shade
  • Antonym: uniform - not varying or having the same color throughout

Etymology

From Middle English motley (spotted or multi-colored), derived from 'mote' (speck).
