Pyrocephalus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪroʊˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / ˌpʌɪrəʊˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the tyrant flycatcher family, including the vermilion flycatcher and its relatives, found in the Americas.
genus of flycatcher birds

Examples

The bright plumage of the Pyrocephalus makes it a favorite among bird watchers.
Several species in the genus Pyrocephalus inhabit both mainland and island ecosystems in the Americas.
The Pyrocephalus is easily recognized by its vivid red head and underparts.

Etymology

From Greek 'pyro-' (fire) and 'kephalē' (head), referring to the bright red coloring of some species' heads.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪroʊˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / ˌpɪərəˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
A genus of small, brightly colored flycatcher birds in the Tyrannidae family, native to the Americas.
genus of flycatcher birds

Examples

The Pyrocephalus genus is best known for the strikingly red plumage of its males.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Pyrocephalus genus in South America.
Birdwatchers in the Galápagos Islands often hope to spot the unique Pyrocephalus flycatchers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Tyrannidae - family of birds known as tyrant flycatchers

Etymology

From Greek pyro- (fire) + kephalē (head), referring to the vivid coloration of the birds' heads.
