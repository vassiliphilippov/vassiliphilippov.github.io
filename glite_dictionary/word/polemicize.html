to polemicize

1. VERB

/ pəˈlɛmɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / pəˈlɛmɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to engage in controversy or argumentative discussion, especially on controversial topics
engage in controversy or argue forcefully

Examples

The scholars continued to polemicize over the interpretation of the ancient texts.
She tends to polemicize whenever political topics arise in conversation.
Historians frequently polemicize about the causes of major world events.

Synset Examples

The two historians polemicized for years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to argue - to give reasons for or against something; to debate

Etymology

From 'polemic' (dispute or controversy), derived from Greek 'polemikos' (warlike).
