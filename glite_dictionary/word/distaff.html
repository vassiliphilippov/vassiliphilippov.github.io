distaff

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪˌstæf / (AmE), / ˈdɪ.stɑːf / (BrE)
Relating to women or female roles; characteristic of or peculiar to women.
relating to women or the female sex

Examples

The committee was dominated by its distaff members, who often shaped the agenda.
She wrote a book chronicling the distaff side of her family's history.
During the awards season, the distaff categories garnered significant attention from the media.

Synset Examples

female sensitiveness
female suffrage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: female - relating to or characteristic of women or the female sex
  • Antonym: male - relating to men or the male sex

Etymology

From Old English 'distæf', meaning 'staff for holding flax in spinning'. Originally referencing women's work, later extended to mean relating to women.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪstæf / (AmE), / ˈdɪstɑːf / (BrE)
a staff on which wool or flax is wound for spinning by hand
tool for spinning wool or flax

Examples

The spinner placed the wool on her distaff before beginning her work.
In the old cottage, a wooden distaff leaned against the fireplace, a reminder of earlier days.
She skillfully wound the flax onto the distaff for spinning.

Etymology

From Old English distæf, di- (possibly relating to 'dise', a bunch of flax) + 'staff'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪstæf / (AmE), / ˈdɪstɑːf / (BrE)
the sphere of activities, work, or interests traditionally associated with women
sphere of women's activities
archaic

Examples

For centuries, teaching was largely considered the distaff side of academia.
She broke into a field once dominated by the distaff, making her success all the more remarkable.
The committee, once a distaff preserve, now includes men as well.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: men's sphere - the sphere of work by men

Etymology

From Old English distæf (a staff for holding flax); later used figuratively for women's work and domain.
