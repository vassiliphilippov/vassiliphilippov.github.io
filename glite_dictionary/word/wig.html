wig

1. NOUN

/ wɪɡ / (AmE), / wɪɡ / (BrE)
An artificial covering of hair for all or most of the head, typically made from real or synthetic hair and worn to conceal baldness or for theatrical or fashion purposes.
artificial covering of hair for the head

Examples

She wore a wig to cover her thinning hair after chemotherapy.
The actor put on a curly wig for his role in the play.
In the 18th century, white powdered wigs were a symbol of status.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hairpiece - a small or partial wig used to cover a section of the head

Etymology

Shortened from periwig, from French perruque.

2. NOUN

/ wɪɡ / (AmE), / wɪɡ / (BrE)
(British slang) A scolding or telling-off, especially in an abrupt or severe manner.
British slang for a scolding
slang british

Examples

After coming home late, Jamie got a real wig from his mum.
If you keep making noise, you'll get a wig from the teacher.
He tried to sneak in quietly to avoid another wig.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telling-off - an act of criticizing or reprimanding someone
  • Synonym: reprimand - a formal expression of disapproval
