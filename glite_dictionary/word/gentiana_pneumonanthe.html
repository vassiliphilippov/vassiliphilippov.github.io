Gentiana pneumonanthe

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛntiˈænə njuːˌmɒnˈænθi / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn.tiˈɑː.nə ˌnjuː.məˈnæn.θi / (BrE)
A perennial Eurasian gentian with sky-blue funnel-shaped flowers, typically found in damp open heaths.
species of Eurasian gentian with blue flowers

Examples

The Gentiana pneumonanthe blooms in late summer across the wet meadows of Europe.
Botanists admire the striking blue flowers of Gentiana pneumonanthe during their field surveys.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect habitats supporting Gentiana pneumonanthe due to its declining numbers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentian - a plant of the genus Gentiana, known for its blue flowers and medicinal uses

Etymology

From the genus name 'Gentiana', named after Gentius, an Illyrian king, and the specific epithet 'pneumonanthe' from Greek 'pneumōn' (lung) and 'anthos' (flower).
