family Calycanthaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli ˌkælɪkænˈθeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪliː ˌkælɪkænˈθeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of shrubs or small trees with aromatic bark, native to eastern North America and eastern Asia.
plant family of aromatic shrubs or small trees
medical

Examples

Several members of the family Calycanthaceae are cultivated for their attractive flowers and fragrance.
The family Calycanthaceae is known for its aromatic bark and unique flower structure.
Botanists have identified new species within the family Calycanthaceae in eastern Asia.

Etymology

From New Latin Calycanthaceae, based on Calycanthus (type genus) + -aceae (family suffix).
