axial

1. ADJ

/ ˈæk.si.əl / (AmE), / ˈæks.i.əl / (BrE)
relating to or situated around an axis.
relating to an axis

Examples

The MRI scan showed an axial image of the brain.
Engineers measured the axial alignment of the rotating part.
The flowers develop along the axial stem of the plant.

Synset Examples

axial angle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lateral - relating to or situated at the side

Etymology

From Latin axis (axle, axis) + -al (adjectival suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæk.si.əl / (AmE), / ˈæk.si.əl / (BrE)
relating to or resembling an axis; situated around, on, or along an axis of rotation.
relating to an axis of rotation

Examples

The MRI revealed damage to the axial skeleton.
The turbine spins around its axial shaft to generate power.
Engineers studied the axial forces affecting the stability of the structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: radial - relating to the radius or radii, or extending from a center or axis

Etymology

From Latin axis (axle, axis) + -al (forming adjectives).

3. ADJ

/ ˈæk.si.əl / (AmE), / ˈæk.si.əl / (BrE)
Situated on, along, or in the direction of an axis.
along or relating to an axis

Examples

The axial alignment of the wheels is crucial for vehicle stability.
In MRI images, the axial view allows doctors to see cross-sections of the brain.
The screw is subjected to both axial and radial forces during operation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lateral - situated at or extending to the side

Etymology

From Latin axis (axle, axis) + -al (forming adjectives).
