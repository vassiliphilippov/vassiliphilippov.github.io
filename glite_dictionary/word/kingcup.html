kingcup

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋˌkʌp / (BrE)
a swamp plant of Europe and North America, also known as marsh marigold, that has bright yellow flowers resembling buttercups
swamp plant with yellow flowers

Examples

The wet meadows were full of blooming kingcups in early spring.
A cluster of bright yellow kingcups grew along the edge of the pond.
In the marshy fields, children picked armfuls of kingcups for their mothers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh marigold - a swamp plant with yellow flowers, also called kingcup
  • Degree Lower: buttercup - a different plant with similar yellow flowers to the kingcup

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋˌkʌp / (BrE)
a plant of the buttercup family, especially the marsh marigold (Caltha palustris), known for its bright yellow flowers
a marsh marigold or similar plant

Examples

The pond was lined with bright kingcups blooming among the reeds.
We collected a bouquet of kingcups and buttercups from the riverbank.
In spring, the kingcup adds a splash of yellow to the wet meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh marigold - a yellow-flowered plant of wetlands (Caltha palustris), also known as kingcup
  • Degree Higher: buttercup - a plant of the Ranunculus genus with typically yellow flowers
