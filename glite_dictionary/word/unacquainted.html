unacquainted

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈkweɪn.t̬ɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈkweɪn.tɪd / (BrE)
having little or no knowledge or experience of something; not familiar with
not familiar with; lacking knowledge of

Examples

She remained unacquainted with the rules of the game until her friend explained them.
Many tourists are unacquainted with local customs when visiting a new country.
He was entirely unacquainted with the software, so he struggled to complete the project.

Synset Examples

unacquainted with city ways

Related Terms

  • Antonym: familiar - well known from long or close association
  • Synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness in general or about something

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'acquainted' (familiar, known).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈkweɪn.t̬ɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈkweɪn.tɪd / (BrE)
not familiar with or not having knowledge of someone or something; not having experience of something specified
not familiar or knowledgeable about something

Examples

Many new students feel unacquainted with the university's traditions when they first arrive.
He was completely unacquainted with the subject matter discussed at the meeting.
Travelers often find themselves unacquainted with the local language and customs.

Synset Examples

American tourists wholly innocent of French
a person unacquainted with our customs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: familiar - having knowledge of something; well-acquainted
  • Synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness in general or about something specific

Etymology

Formed from un- (not) + acquainted (from Old French 'acointier', from Latin 'accognitare', to make known).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01312175-s
Synset POS: s
