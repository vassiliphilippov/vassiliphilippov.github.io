thirstily

1. ADV

/ ˈθɝː.stə.li / (AmE), / ˈθɜː.stɪ.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows a strong desire for drink or as if very thirsty.
in a thirsty manner

Examples

After hiking for hours, he drank thirstily from the cold stream.
The children gulped their lemonade thirstily after playing soccer in the sun.
She eyed the water bottle thirstily, barely able to wait for her turn.

Synset Examples

we drank thirstily from the bottle that was passed around

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eagerly - in a very keen or enthusiastic manner
  • Synonym: hungrily - in a way that shows hunger or strong desire for food

Etymology

From 'thirsty' + '-ly', formed in Middle English.

2. ADV

/ ˈθɝːstɪli / (AmE), / ˈθɜːstɪli / (BrE)
in an eager or enthusiastic manner; with intense desire or longing
in an eager or intense manner

Examples

The children drank the lemonade thirstily after playing in the sun all afternoon.
She thirstily read every book she could find on the subject.
He listened thirstily to every word, eager to learn more.

Synset Examples

the news was eagerly awaited

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eagerly - in a way that shows keen or enthusiastic desire
  • Synonym: hungrily - in a manner showing strong desire, especially for food or something needed

Etymology

From 'thirsty' + '-ly', forming the adverb.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00202206-r
Synset POS: r
