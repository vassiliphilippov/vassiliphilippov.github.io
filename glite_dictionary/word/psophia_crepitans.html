Psophia crepitans

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈfiː.ə krɛˈpɪt.ænz / (AmE), / səʊˈfiː.ə krɛˈpɪt.ænz / (BrE)
A species of trumpeter bird native to Brazil and Guiana, often kept to protect poultry in Brazil.
a species of trumpeter bird from South America

Examples

The Psophia crepitans is known for its loud, trumpet-like calls in the rainforests of South America.
Farmers in Brazil often keep Psophia crepitans to protect their chickens from snakes and other predators.
I spotted a Psophia crepitans while birdwatching near the Amazon River.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trumpeter - any bird of the genus Psophia

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek *psophia* (noise, sound) and Latin *crepitans* (crackling, rattling).
