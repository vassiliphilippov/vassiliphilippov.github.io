invisible

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɪzəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪzəbl̩ / (BrE)
impossible or nearly impossible to see; not visible to the eye.
impossible to see; not visible

Examples

The spy wore an invisible earpiece during the mission.
Special technology made the aircraft almost invisible to radar.
Some stains are invisible to the naked eye but show up under ultraviolet light.

Synset Examples

the invisible man
invisible rays
an invisible hinge
invisible mending

Related Terms

  • Antonym: visible - able to be seen

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'visibilis' (visible).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɪzəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪzəbl / (BrE)
not able to be seen or not readily noticeable; not easily detected or prominent
not able to be seen or noticed

Examples

The stain on the carpet was almost invisible in the dim light.
His efforts often went invisible to the public, though they made a big difference.
She wore make-up that made her scars practically invisible.

Synset Examples

he pushed the string through an inconspicuous hole
the invisible man

Related Terms

  • Antonym: visible - able to be seen; apparent
  • Antonym: conspicuous - easily seen or noticed; attracting attention
  • Synonym: unnoticeable - not easily noticed; inconspicuous

Etymology

From Latin invisibilis, from in- (not) + visibilis (visible).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00583752-a
Synset POS: a
