Gypaetus

1. NOUN

/ dʒaɪˈpiːətəs / (AmE), / dʒɪˈpiːətəs / (BrE)
A genus of large Old World vultures, including the bearded vulture (lammergeier), in some classifications considered the type genus of the family Aegypiidae.
genus of Old World vultures

Examples

The Gypaetus genus is noted for its unique bone-eating behavior.
Researchers observed a Gypaetus soaring over the mountainous terrain.
The bearded vulture is the best-known species in the Gypaetus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bearded vulture - a large vulture also known as lammergeier, of the genus Gypaetus

Etymology

From Greek gupaietos (vulture), from gups (vulture).

2. NOUN

/ dʒaɪˈpiːɪtəs / (AmE), / dʒɪˈpiːətəs / (BrE)
A genus of large Old World vultures, including the bearded vulture, in some classifications considered the type genus of the family Aegypiidae.
a genus of Old World vultures

Examples

The Gypaetus genus contains only the bearded vulture, a distinctive and rare bird found in mountainous regions.
Ornithologists debated whether Gypaetus should be separated from other vulture genera due to its unique characteristics.
The conservation of Gypaetus has become a priority for many wildlife organizations in Europe.

Etymology

From Greek gypaetos (vulture, especially the bearded vulture).
