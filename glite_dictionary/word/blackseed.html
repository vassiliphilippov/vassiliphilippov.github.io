blackseed

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæk.siːd / (AmE), / ˈblæk.siːd / (BrE)
A grass native to the West Indies and common in the southern United States, characterized by tufted wiry stems and often infested with a dark fungus.
a grass with tufted wiry stems, often fungal-infested

Examples

The field was covered with patches of blackseed flourishing among other grasses.
Botanists noted that blackseed often hosts a distinctive dark fungus on its stems.
In the southern United States, blackseed is a common sight in open meadows.
