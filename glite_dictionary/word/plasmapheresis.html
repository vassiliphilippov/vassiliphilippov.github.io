plasmapheresis

1. NOUN

/ ˌplæzməfəˈriːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌplazməfəˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which blood plasma is separated from whole blood, and the remaining components are returned to the donor
plasma separation medical procedure
medical

Examples

The donor underwent plasmapheresis to provide plasma for immunotherapy treatments.
Doctors often use plasmapheresis to treat patients with certain autoimmune diseases.
Regular plasmapheresis can help reduce harmful antibodies in the bloodstream.

Etymology

From plasma + Greek aphaeresis (a taking away), after German 'Plasmapherese'.
