Sarracenia minor

1. NOUN

/ sæɹ.əˈsiː.ni.ə ˈmaɪ.nɚ / (AmE), / sær.əˈsiː.ni.ə ˈmaɪ.nə / (BrE)
A yellow-flowered pitcher plant native to the southeastern United States, with trumpet-shaped leaves topped by an arched hood.
yellow-flowered pitcher plant of southeastern US

Examples

The bog was home to several Sarracenia minor plants with their distinctive yellow flowers.
Botanists are studying how Sarracenia minor traps insects in its pitcher-shaped leaves.
We saw a large population of Sarracenia minor thriving in the wetlands of Georgia.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sarracenia' (genus name) from Michel Sarrazin, a French botanist, and 'minor' meaning smaller.
