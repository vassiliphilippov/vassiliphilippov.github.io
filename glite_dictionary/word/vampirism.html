vampirism

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæmˌpaɪ.rɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvæm.pɪ.rɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the actions, practices, or characteristics traditionally attributed to vampires, such as feeding on blood
actions or practices of a vampire

Examples

Legends about vampirism have fascinated people for centuries across many different cultures.
The film explores vampirism as both a physical and psychological condition.
His obsession with vampirism led him to study ancient folklore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vampire - a creature in folklore that drinks blood

Etymology

From French vampirisme, from vampire (ultimately from Hungarian vámpír).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvæm.paɪˌrɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈvæm.pɪˌrɪz.əm / (BrE)
the belief in the existence of vampires, or practices and customs associated with vampires
belief in the existence of vampires

Examples

Stories about vampirism have fascinated people for centuries.
He studied the folklore and superstitions surrounding vampirism in Eastern Europe.
The novel explores themes of vampirism and immortality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vampire - a mythical creature that drinks blood

Etymology

From vampire + -ism, modeled after late 18th-century European usage.
