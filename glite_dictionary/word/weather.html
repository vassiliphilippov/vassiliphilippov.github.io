weather

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɛðə / (BrE)
Situated on or facing the side exposed to the wind.
on the windward side

Examples

The sailors moved to the weather side of the boat to balance it against the wind.
Standing on the weather deck, he faced the full force of the gale.
Make sure all loose equipment is secured on the weather rail.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lee - the sheltered side away from the wind
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɛðər / (AmE), / ˈwɛðə / (BrE)
The condition of the atmosphere at a particular place and time, including factors such as temperature, wind, clouds, and precipitation.
state of the atmosphere (temperature, wind, etc.)

Examples

The weather tomorrow is expected to be sunny and warm.
Bad weather forced the cancellation of the outdoor concert.
She checked the weather report before deciding what to wear.

Synset Examples

they were hoping for good weather
every day we have weather conditions and yesterday was no exception
the conditions were too rainy for playing in the snow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climate - the general or average weather conditions over a long period

Etymology

From Old English 'weder', meaning 'air, sky, weather'.

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈwɛðər / (AmE), / ˈwɛðə / (BrE)
(of a ship or sailor) to sail to the windward side of something, especially to avoid danger or make headway against the wind.
sail to windward side (nautical)

Examples

The captain ordered the crew to weather the headland despite the strong winds.
The ship managed to weather the rocky shoal and reach safe waters.
They skillfully weathered the cape, avoiding the reefs in the process.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01932236-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈwɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɛðə / (BrE)
to withstand or endure (something difficult or dangerous), especially by being strong or brave
endure or withstand difficulties

Examples

The company managed to weather the financial crisis and emerged even stronger.
We had to weather several setbacks before completing the project.
He was able to weather the storm of criticism with dignity and composure.

Synset Examples

She braved the elements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withstand - to resist or hold out against something
  • Antonym: to succumb - to fail to resist and yield to something

Etymology

From Old English *weder* (weather, air), later extended to refer metaphorically to enduring hardship as one would endure storms.

Source: WordNet

5. VERB

/ ˈwɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɛðə / (BrE)
(of a material or object) to be changed in appearance or condition due to long exposure to the weather.
change due to exposure to weather

Examples

The wooden fence began to weather after years of rain and sun.
Over time, the statue weathered and lost its sharp edges.
Their boat weathered many storms before it finally needed repairs.

Synset Examples

A weathered old hut

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erode - to gradually wear away by natural forces
  • Antonym: preserve - to keep something in its original state
