sulfur-yellow

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌlfɚ ˈjɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə ˈjɛləʊ / (BrE)
having the bright, pale yellow color characteristic of sulfur
a bright yellow color like sulfur

Examples

The artist painted the bird with sulfur-yellow feathers to make it stand out.
A sulfur-yellow dress caught everyone's eye at the spring festival.
The minerals glowed sulfur-yellow under the museum lights.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: lemon-yellow - having the color of a lemon; a lighter shade of yellow
  • Degree Higher: golden - having the color or shine of gold; a deeper yellow

Etymology

From sulfur, the chemical element, referring to its typical yellow color, combined with 'yellow.'
