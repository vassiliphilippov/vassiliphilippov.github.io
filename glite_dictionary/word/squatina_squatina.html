Squatina squatina

1. NOUN

/ skwəˈtiːnə skwəˈtiːnə / (AmE), / skwəˈtiːnə skwəˈtiːnə / (BrE)
A species of angelshark distinguished by its broad, flat body and winglike pectoral fins, native to the northeastern Atlantic Ocean and Mediterranean Sea.
a species of angelshark

Examples

The Squatina squatina is now considered critically endangered due to overfishing and habitat loss.
Researchers tagged several Squatina squatina specimens to study their migration patterns in the Mediterranean.
The unique shape of Squatina squatina allows it to lie camouflaged on the ocean floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angelshark - a family of sharks with flattened bodies and broad pectoral fins

Etymology

From Latin squatina (a kind of shark), duplicated as a binomial scientific name.
