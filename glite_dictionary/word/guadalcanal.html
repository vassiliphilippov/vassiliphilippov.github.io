Guadalcanal

1. PROPN

/ ˌɡwɑːdəlkəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˌɡwɑːd(ə)lkəˈnæl / (BrE)
an island in the southwestern Pacific, site of a major World War II battle between Japanese and Allied forces (1942–43), resulting in Allied victory
Southwest Pacific island; WWII battle site

Examples

The Battle of Guadalcanal marked a turning point in the Pacific War.
Many veterans who fought at Guadalcanal remembered the difficult conditions on the island.
Documentaries about World War II often highlight the struggle for Guadalcanal and its significance.

Etymology

Named after Guadalcanal, a town in Andalusia, Spain.

2. PROPN

/ ˌɡwɑː.dəl.kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˌɡwæd.əl.kəˈnæl / (BrE)
a mountainous island, the largest of the Solomon Islands, notable for its historical significance during World War II
largest island of the Solomon Islands

Examples

The Battle of Guadalcanal was a turning point in the Pacific during World War II.
She spent several months working on environmental projects in Guadalcanal.
Tourists often visit Guadalcanal to explore its lush mountains and rich history.

Etymology

Named by Spanish explorer Álvaro de Mendaña after his home town Guadalcanal in Andalusia, Spain.
