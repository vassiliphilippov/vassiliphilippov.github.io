platelet

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪtlət / (AmE), / ˈpleɪtlɪt / (BrE)
a small, disc-shaped cell fragment in the blood that is essential for clotting
small blood cell fragment for clotting
medical

Examples

A low platelet count can increase the risk of bleeding after surgery.
She donated platelets to help patients undergoing cancer treatment.
Doctors closely monitored his platelet levels throughout his illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrombocyte - another term for platelet, blood clotting cell

Etymology

From diminutive of 'plate', referring to its flat shape.
