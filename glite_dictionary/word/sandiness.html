sandiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsændɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of containing or resembling sand.
the quality of being sandy

Examples

The sandiness of the cookies added a unique texture to the dessert.
Due to its sandiness, the beach was ideal for building sandcastles.
He wasn't fond of the sandiness in the playground, which got into his shoes.

Synset Examples

The sandiness of the soil face extending into the night above and in front of us was due to the gradual pulverization of the rock by rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandy - containing or covered with sand

Etymology

Formed from 'sandy' + '-ness' (noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsændi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsændi.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or texture of being like sand; resembling sand in feel or appearance
the quality of being like sand

Examples

The sandiness of the soil made it perfect for growing certain types of vegetables.
She disliked the sandiness of the poorly made cookies.
The artist tried to capture the sandiness of the beach in his painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandy - having the texture or color of sand
  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of being smooth

Etymology

From 'sandy' + '-ness', forming nouns indicating a state or quality.
