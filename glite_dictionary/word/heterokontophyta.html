Heterokontophyta

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬əroʊˌkɑːnˈtɑːfɪtə / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˌkɒnˈtɒfɪtə / (BrE)
A group of algae characterized by the presence of chlorophyll a (and often c) and typically having flagella of unequal lengths; used in some classifications instead of 'Chrysophyta.'
a group of algae with unequal flagella

Examples

Heterokontophyta are distinguished by their chloroplast pigments and the presence of unequal flagella.
Some scientists now classify brown algae under Heterokontophyta rather than Chrysophyta.
The diversity within Heterokontophyta has led to ongoing debates in algal taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chrysophyta - a group of algae, often including golden-brown algae

Etymology

From Greek 'hetero-' (different) + 'kontos' (pole, referring to the flagella) + 'phyta' (plants).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬əroʊˌkɑːntəˈfaɪtə / (AmE), / ˌhɛtəˌrɒntəˈfaɪtə / (BrE)
a division of algae characterized by the presence of chlorophyll a and usually c, with flagella of unequal lengths; in some classifications, it replaces Chrysophyta.
division of algae with unequal flagella

Examples

The Heterokontophyta include many important groups of algae such as diatoms and brown algae.
Researchers studied the flagella movement in Heterokontophyta to better understand cell motility.
The classification of certain algae was revised, moving them from Chrysophyta to Heterokontophyta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chrysophyta - a former division of algae, now largely superseded by Heterokontophyta in some classifications

Etymology

From Greek 'heteros' (different) and 'kontos' (pole, referring to flagella), plus New Latin 'phyta' (plants).
