Phytelephas

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪtəˈlɛfəs / (AmE), / ˌfaɪtɛˈliːfəs / (BrE)
A small genus of feather palms native to South America, known for seeds used as vegetable ivory.
genus of South American feather palms

Examples

The seeds of Phytelephas are harvested to make buttons and ornaments.
Several species within the Phytelephas genus are valued for their sustainable ‘ivory’.
Botanists conducted a survey of Phytelephas palms in the Amazon region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vegetable ivory - hard, white seed produced by Phytelephas palms, used as a substitute for true ivory

Etymology

From Greek phyton (plant) + elephas (elephant), referring to the ivory-like seeds.
