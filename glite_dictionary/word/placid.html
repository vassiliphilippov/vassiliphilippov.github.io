placid

1. ADJ

/ ˈplæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈplæsɪd / (BrE)
calm and not easily upset or excited; not easily irritated
calm and not easily upset

Examples

Despite the chaos around her, she remained placid and composed.
His placid demeanor made him well-liked by everyone in the office.
The child showed a placid expression even when teased by his classmates.

Synset Examples

an equable temper
not everyone shared his placid temperament
remained placid despite the repeated delays

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions
  • Antonym: agitated - feeling or appearing troubled or nervous

Etymology

From Latin placidus (pleasant, gentle).

2. ADJ

/ ˈplæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈplæsɪd / (BrE)
(of a body of water) calm and free from disturbance by waves or wind.
calm and undisturbed (of water)

Examples

The surface of the lake was placid, reflecting the trees perfectly.
After the storm, the sea became placid and inviting once more.
They enjoyed an afternoon of fishing on the placid river.

Synset Examples

a ribbon of sand between the angry sea and the placid bay
the quiet waters of a lagoon
a lake of tranquil blue water reflecting a tranquil blue sky
a smooth channel crossing
scarcely a ripple on the still water
unruffled water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions; also of water: free from disturbance
  • Synonym: tranquil - free from disturbance; peaceful
  • Antonym: turbulent - (of water, air, or emotion) characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion; not controlled or calm

Etymology

From Latin placidus (gentle, calm, peaceful).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00303750-s
Synset POS: s
