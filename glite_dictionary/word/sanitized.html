sanitized

1. ADJ

/ ˈsæn.ə.taɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsan.ɪ.taɪzd / (BrE)
made clean and free from bacteria or other harmful substances; made sanitary.
made clean; made sanitary

Examples

All of the hospital equipment must be sanitized regularly to prevent infection.
After the flood, the kitchen was thoroughly sanitized before anyone was allowed to prepare food.
Public restrooms are sanitized several times a day for health and safety reasons.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsanitized - not made sanitary or clean
  • Synonym: sterilized - made completely free from bacteria or other living microorganisms

Etymology

From the verb 'sanitize', which comes from Latin 'sanitas' (health).
