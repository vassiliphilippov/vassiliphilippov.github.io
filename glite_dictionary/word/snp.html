SNP

1. NOUN

/ ɛs ɛn pi / (AmE), / ɛs ɛn pi / (BrE)
a variation in a DNA sequence where a single nucleotide differs between members of a species or paired chromosomes
single-nucleotide genetic variation
medical

Examples

Research has shown that a particular SNP is associated with an increased risk of heart disease.
Scientists analyzed thousands of SNPs to understand genetic differences between populations.
Each SNP can help trace inheritance patterns of certain traits in families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: single nucleotide polymorphism - the full term for SNP, a DNA sequence variation involving a single nucleotide
  • Degree Lower: mutation - a change in a DNA sequence

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'single nucleotide polymorphism'.
