philter

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪltər / (AmE), / ˈfɪltə / (BrE)
A drink or potion believed to have magical properties, especially one that causes the person who takes it to fall in love with the giver.
magical love potion

Examples

The witch brewed a philter to make the prince fall madly in love with her.
In ancient tales, a single drop of philter could change the course of a person's heart.
Jealous rivals sometimes sought a philter to win back lost lovers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: love potion - a potion intended to cause someone to fall in love

Etymology

From Greek 'philtron' (love charm), via Latin 'philtrum'.
