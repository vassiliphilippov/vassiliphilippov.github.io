Camptosorus

1. NOUN

/ kæmpˈtəsɔːrəs / (AmE), / kæmpˈtəʊsərəs / (BrE)
A former genus of ferns, now usually included in the genus Asplenium.
former genus of ferns

Examples

The genus Camptosorus is no longer recognized and its species are now classified under Asplenium.
Botanists once studied Camptosorus for its unique leaf shape.
Researchers debated whether to retain Camptosorus as a separate genus or merge it with Asplenium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Asplenium - genus of ferns including those formerly classified as Camptosorus

Etymology

From Greek 'kamptos' (bent) and 'soros' (heap, referring to spore clusters).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæmptəˈsɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˌkæmptəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a former genus of ferns, now usually included in the genus Asplenium
former genus of ferns

Examples

The walking fern was once classified as Camptosorus rhizophyllus before being moved to Asplenium.
Botanists now consider Camptosorus to be synonymous with Asplenium.
Older flora guides may list certain ferns under the genus Camptosorus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Asplenium - a genus of ferns, including species formerly classified as Camptosorus

Etymology

From Greek 'kamptos' (bent) + 'sōros' (heap), referring to the plant's curved sori (spore cases).
