retinitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtəˈnaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌrɛtɪˈnaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the retina of the eye, often causing vision problems.
inflammation of the retina
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with retinitis after she reported blurred vision and discomfort.
Some forms of retinitis can lead to permanent damage if not treated promptly.
Researchers are studying genetic factors that may contribute to retinitis in younger patients.

Etymology

From Latin 'retina' (net-like membrane of the eye) + '-itis' (indicating inflammation).
