mouthful

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊθ.fʊl / (AmE), / ˈmaʊθ.fʊl / (BrE)
a small amount of food or drink that fills the mouth; as much as can be put in the mouth at one time
a small amount taken into the mouth

Examples

She took a mouthful of soup and smiled with satisfaction.
He couldn't speak after swallowing a huge mouthful of cake.
Try a mouthful of this juice; it's delicious.

Synset Examples

take a taste — you'll like it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bite - a small piece of food bitten off

Etymology

From 'mouth' + '-ful', a suffix meaning ‘as much as fills’.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊθ.fʊl / (AmE), / ˈmaʊθ.fʊl / (BrE)
the amount of food or drink that fills your mouth or that you can put into your mouth at one time
the quantity to fill the mouth at once

Examples

He took a big mouthful of cake and tried to talk at the same time.
After a mouthful of soup, she smiled and nodded in approval.
Chew each mouthful slowly to help with digestion.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sip - a small mouthful, especially of a drink
  • Degree Higher: gulp - a large and quick mouthful, especially of a drink

Etymology

From 'mouth' + '-ful', meaning 'as much as a mouth can hold'.
