maleficence

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɛfɪsəns / (AmE), / məˈlɛfɪsəns / (BrE)
The quality or state of being harmful or causing evil; the act of doing harm or evil.
the quality of being harmful or evil

Examples

The jury was shocked by the sheer maleficence of his actions.
Some critics argued that the policy, though well-intentioned, resulted in unintended maleficence.
Philosophers have debated whether maleficence is inherent in human nature.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: benevolence - the quality of being kind and well-meaning

Etymology

From Latin maleficentia (wickedness), from maleficus (wicked, doing evil).

2. NOUN

/ məˈlɛfəsəns / (AmE), / məˈlɛfɪsəns / (BrE)
The act of doing harm or causing evil; wrongdoing or mischief.
the act of causing harm or evil

Examples

The villain's maleficence was evident in every scheme he devised.
The court was shocked by the sheer maleficence of the accused's actions.
In stories of folklore, witches were often accused of maleficence against villagers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: benevolence - the quality of being well-meaning; kindness

Etymology

From Latin 'maleficentia' (wicked deed), from 'maleficus' (doing evil).
