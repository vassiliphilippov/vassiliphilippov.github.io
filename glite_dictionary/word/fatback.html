fatback

1. NOUN

/ ˈfætˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈfætˌbæk / (BrE)
salted pork taken from the back of a hog, used especially in cooking or as a flavoring.
salted pork from the back of a hog

Examples

The chef added fatback to the beans to give them a rich, smoky flavor.
Southerners often use fatback in traditional recipes like collard greens.
He bought a slab of fatback from the butcher for his stew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salt pork - cured pork preserved with salt

Etymology

From 'fat' + 'back', referring to the cut's location and composition.
