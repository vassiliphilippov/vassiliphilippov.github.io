haberdashery

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæbərˈdæʃəri / (AmE), / ˌhæbəˈdæʃəri / (BrE)
a shop that sells men's clothing and other related accessories, especially hats, ties, and shirts
shop selling men's clothing and accessories

Examples

He opened a small haberdashery after moving to the city.
The haberdashery on Main Street is known for its fine selection of silk ties.
We stopped by the haberdashery to buy a new shirt and hat.

Etymology

From Middle English haberdassher, possibly from Anglo-French haberdasher.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhæbərˈdæʃəri / (AmE), / ˌhæbəˈdæʃəri / (BrE)
small items used in sewing, such as buttons, zippers, and thread, collectively sold by a haberdasher.
sewing items sold by a haberdasher

Examples

She purchased the haberdashery she needed to finish her dress project.
The shop specializes in haberdashery such as buttons, ribbons, and needles.
His aunt gifted him a box full of vintage haberdashery from her sewing kit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notions - small useful sewing items such as buttons and thread

Etymology

From Middle English haberdassher, from Anglo-French haberdasher, of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03478827-n
Synset POS: n
