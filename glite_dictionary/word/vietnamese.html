Vietnamese

1. NOUN

/ viˌɛtnəˈmiz / (AmE), / viˌɛtnəˈmiːz / (BrE)
the Austroasiatic language spoken mainly in Vietnam and by Vietnamese communities worldwide.
Austroasiatic language of Vietnam

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Vietnamese.
The novel was translated from Vietnamese into several languages.
He studied Vietnamese to communicate more easily during his travels in Hanoi.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Vietnamese - a person from Vietnam

Etymology

From Vietnam + -ese, a suffix denoting origin or language.

WordNet ID: oewn-06950006-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˌviːətˌnæˈmiz / (AmE), / ˌviːətnəˈmiːz / (BrE)
of or relating to Vietnam, its people, or its language.
relating to Vietnam or its people/language

Examples

The Vietnamese capital city is Hanoi.
Her family serves traditional Vietnamese dishes during holidays.
He is learning the Vietnamese language at the university.

Synset Examples

the Vietnamese countryside
the Vietnamese tones
Vietnamese boat people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vietnam - a country in Southeast Asia

Etymology

From Vietnam + -ese (suffix denoting origin or nationality, after French -ais).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03140890-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ viˌɛtnəˈmiz / (AmE), / viˌɛtnəˈmiːz / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Vietnam.
person from Vietnam

Examples

My friend is a Vietnamese who moved here last year.
Many Vietnamese celebrate Tet, the Lunar New Year, with special foods and traditions.
The restaurant is owned by a group of local Vietnamese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vietnam - a country in Southeast Asia

Etymology

From Vietnam, the country's name, with the English adjectival suffix -ese, modelled after Portuguese -ês.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09766522-n
Synset POS: n
