halo

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈheɪ.ləʊ / (BrE)
A circle of light appearing to surround the sun, moon, or other luminous body, caused by the refraction and reflection of light by ice crystals in the atmosphere.
circle of light around sun or moon

Examples

A faint halo appeared around the moon as high clouds drifted in.
Photographers captured a brilliant halo encircling the sun over the city skyline.
Ice crystals in the atmosphere can cause a halo to form around the sun on cold days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aureole - a circle of light or brightness around something, especially as depicted in art

Etymology

From Latin 'halō' (circle of light around the sun or moon).

2. NOUN

/ ˈheɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈheɪləʊ / (BrE)
A ring-shaped object, form, or area, especially one resembling a circle of light or smoke.
ring-shaped object or area

Examples

A halo of mist surrounded the mountain peak at dawn.
The artist painted a glowing halo around the angel's head.
A halo of smoke rose above the burning building.

Synset Examples

a ring of ships in the harbor
a halo of smoke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring - a circular object or shape

Etymology

From Latin 'halo', from Greek 'halōs' (disk of the sun or moon, ring).

3. NOUN

/ ˈheɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈheɪləʊ / (BrE)
a circle of radiant light appearing around the head of a saint or holy person in religious art, symbolizing holiness
circle of light around a saint's head

Examples

A golden halo surrounded the head of the Virgin Mary in the painting.
The artist depicted each saint with a luminous halo above their head.
The halo serves as a symbol of divinity in many religious artworks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aureole - a radiant circle or disk, especially around the head of a sacred personage

Etymology

From Latin 'halo' (disk, threshing floor), from Greek 'halōs' (threshing floor, disk of the sun or moon).
