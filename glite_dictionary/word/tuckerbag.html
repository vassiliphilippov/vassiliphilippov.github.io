tuckerbag

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌkərˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈtʌkəbæɡ / (BrE)
A bag used for carrying food, especially by travelers or workers in Australia.
bag for carrying food (Australia)
british

Examples

The swagman filled his tuckerbag before heading into the bush.
She packed sandwiches and fruit into her tuckerbag for the long hike.
Every station worker kept a tuckerbag close at hand during the sheep muster.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From Australian English 'tucker' (food) + 'bag.'
