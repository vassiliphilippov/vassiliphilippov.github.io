unhealthy

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈhɛlθi / (AmE), / ʌnˈhɛlθi / (BrE)
Harmful to health; likely to cause illness or injury.
detrimental to health

Examples

Doctors warn that eating too much junk food is unhealthy for children.
She gave up her unhealthy habit of smoking last year.
Working long hours in a stressful environment can be very unhealthy for your mind and body.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: healthy - in good health; beneficial to health
  • Synonym: insalubrious - unpleasant, unhealthy; not conducive to health

Etymology

From un- (not) + healthy.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈhɛlθi / (AmE), / ʌnˈhɛlθi / (BrE)
not having or showing good health in body or mind; likely to harm health.
not in good health; harmful to health

Examples

Years of smoking had left him unhealthy and weak.
She developed an unhealthy obsession with dieting.
The air in the crowded room quickly became unhealthy to breathe.

Synset Examples

unhealthy ulcers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: healthy - in good health; not ill or injured
  • Synonym: ill - not in good health; sick

Etymology

From un- (not) + healthy, modeled on similar English formations.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01176433-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈhɛlθi / (AmE), / ʌnˈhɛlθi / (BrE)
not good for your health; likely to cause illness or harm to health
not good for health

Examples

Doctors warn that an unhealthy lifestyle can lead to serious medical problems.
She realized her frequent consumption of soda was an unhealthy habit.
The city’s air quality has reached unhealthy levels due to pollution.

Synset Examples

an unhealthy diet of fast foods
an unhealthy climate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: healthy - having good health or promoting good health

Etymology

From un- (not) + healthy, formed in English.
