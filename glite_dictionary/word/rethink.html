rethink

1. NOUN

/ ˈriˌθɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈriːθɪŋk / (BrE)
the act of thinking again about a decision or plan, especially to consider changing it
act of reconsidering a decision or plan

Examples

After the company announced layoffs, a complete rethink of its strategy became necessary.
His experience in the new job led to a rethink of his career goals.
The government called for a rethink on the proposed education reforms.

Synset Examples

he had second thoughts about his purchase

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reconsideration - the act of considering something again
  • Synonym: review - an act of looking over or examining again

Etymology

From re- (again) + think; modeled after the verb 'rethink'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05798017-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ riˈθɪŋk / (AmE), / riːˈθɪŋk / (BrE)
to reconsider or think about (something) again, especially with the possibility of changing a previous decision or opinion
to reconsider or change one's mind

Examples

After hearing the new evidence, she decided to rethink her stance on the issue.
The company will have to rethink its marketing strategy after the disappointing sales results.
He rethought his decision to move abroad after talking with his family.

Synset Examples

He rethought his decision to take a vacation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reconsider - to think about something again, especially in order to change a decision
  • Synonym: change one's mind - to change a previously held opinion or decision

Etymology

From re- (again) + think; modelled after similar constructions in English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00690510-v
Synset POS: v
