Chrysemys picta

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˈsiːmɪs ˈpɪktə / (AmE), / kraɪˈsiːmɪs ˈpɪktə / (BrE)
A species of freshwater turtle native to North America, known for its bright yellow and red markings and commonly called the painted turtle.
species of freshwater turtle

Examples

The Chrysemys picta is often seen basking on logs in shallow ponds.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Chrysemys picta in the wild.
Children were fascinated by the colorful markings of the Chrysemys picta during the field trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: painted turtle - common name for Chrysemys picta, a freshwater turtle with yellow and red markings

Etymology

From Greek 'chryseos' (golden) and 'emys' (turtle), and Latin 'picta' (painted).
