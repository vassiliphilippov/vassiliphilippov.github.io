derangement

1. NOUN

/ dəˈreɪndʒmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈreɪndʒmənt / (BrE)
the act or process of disturbing or upsetting the normal order or function of something, especially the mind or body
disturbance of normal order or function

Examples

The scientist studied the derangement of sleep patterns caused by jet lag.
His sudden derangement made it difficult for him to think clearly.
Severe illness can lead to a derangement of the body’s normal functions.

Synset Examples

his carelessness could have caused an ecological upset
she was unprepared for this sudden overthrow of their normal way of living

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturbance - an interruption of a settled condition
  • Antonym: order - a state in which everything is in its correct place

Etymology

From French *dérangement*, from Old French *desrengier* (to put out of order).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈreɪndʒmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈreɪndʒmənt / (BrE)
a state of mental disturbance and disorientation; severe disruption of normal mental functioning
mental disturbance or disorientation

Examples

The patient's sudden derangement alarmed the medical staff, who called for a psychiatric evaluation.
Periods of derangement often accompany severe psychological disorders and can require immediate intervention.
After days without sleep, he slipped into a state of mental derangement and could no longer recognize his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insanity - severe mental illness; derangement of the mind
  • Synonym: madness - state of severe mental illness or disorder
  • Antonym: sanity - the quality of being sane; soundness of mind

Etymology

From French 'dérangement', from 'déranger' (to disturb).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14421118-n
Synset POS: n
