New York

1. PROPN

/ nju ˈjɔrk / (AmE), / njuː ˈjɔːk / (BrE)
The largest city in the United States, located in southeastern New York State at the mouth of the Hudson River; a major financial and cultural center.
largest city in the United States

Examples

She moved to New York to pursue a career in fashion design.
New York is famous for its skyscrapers, diverse neighborhoods, and vibrant nightlife.
Many world-famous landmarks are located in New York, such as Times Square and Central Park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New York City - major city in the United States, same as 'New York' in common use

Etymology

Named after the Duke of York, later King James II of England.

2. PROPN

/ nuː ˈjɔrk / (AmE), / njuː ˈjɔːk / (BrE)
A state in the northeastern United States; formerly a British colony, with Albany as its capital and New York City as its largest city.
state in the northeastern US; former British colony

Examples

Many immigrants arrived in New York during the late nineteenth century.
The state of New York was originally one of the thirteen British colonies.
Upstate New York is known for its beautiful scenery and changing seasons.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: New York City - the largest city in New York State and the United States

Etymology

Named after the Duke of York in 1664, from the English city of York.

3. PROPN

/ nu ˈjɔrk / (AmE), / njuː ˈjɔːk / (BrE)
A state in the northeastern United States, one of the original 13 colonies, with its capital at Albany.
northeastern US state; capital Albany

Examples

I grew up in New York and love the diversity of its cities.
New York experiences cold winters and hot summers due to its varied climate.
The governor of New York announced a new education policy yesterday.

Etymology

Named after the Duke of York (later King James II of England) in the 17th century.
