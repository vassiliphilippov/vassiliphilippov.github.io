majorette

1. NOUN

/ ˌmeɪdʒəˈrɛt / (AmE), / ˌmædʒəˈrɛt / (BrE)
A girl or woman who twirls a baton and often dances as part of a marching band or parade.
female baton twirler in marching bands

Examples

The majorette led the parade with flawless baton twirling and impressive high kicks.
At halftime, the majorettes dazzled the crowd with their perfectly synchronized routines.
Her dream was to become a majorette in her high school's award-winning marching band.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baton twirler - a person who twirls a baton, especially as a performance

Etymology

From French 'majorette', feminine of 'major', originally denoting the leader of a marching band.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmeɪdʒəˈrɛt / (AmE), / ˌmadʒəˈrɛt / (BrE)
A girl or woman who performs choreographed routines, often twirling a baton, in parades or alongside a marching band.
female baton twirler in a marching band

Examples

The majorette led the parade with a series of dazzling baton twirls.
She trained for months to become the town’s star majorette.
The marching band and its majorettes wowed the crowds at the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drum major - the leader of a marching band or parade who directs the performance

Etymology

From French 'majorette', diminutive of 'major'.

Synset POS: n
