Verbesina helianthoides

1. NOUN

/ vɚˈbiːsɪnə hɛliænˈθɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / vəːˈbiːsɪnə hɛliænˈθɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of perennial herb with yellow flowers, native to the southern and south central United States.
species of perennial herb with yellow flowers

Examples

The fields were dotted with Verbesina helianthoides in full bloom during summer.
Botanists noted that Verbesina helianthoides thrives in moist, sunny areas.
Many gardeners value Verbesina helianthoides for its vibrant yellow flowers.

Etymology

Verbesina: from Latin, a plant name; helianthoides: from Greek 'helios' (sun) and 'anthos' (flower), meaning 'sunflower-like.'
