Aesculus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛskjələs / (AmE), / ˈiːskjʊləs / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous trees and shrubs, including the horse chestnut and buckeyes, native to North America, southeastern Europe, and eastern Asia
genus of trees and shrubs: horse chestnuts, buckeyes

Examples

The large leaves of Aesculus species turn vibrant colors in autumn.
Botanists identified the tree as Aesculus due to its distinctive flowers and fruit.
Several ornamental Aesculus varieties are popular in parks and gardens around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse chestnut - a tree species in the Aesculus genus, known for its large seeds
  • Synonym: buckeye - any of several North American trees or shrubs in the Aesculus genus

Etymology

From Latin *Aesculus* (a kind of oak or edible acorn tree), later used in botanical Latin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛskjələs / (AmE), / ˈiːskjʊləs / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous trees and shrubs including the horse chestnut and buckeye, native to North America, southeastern Europe, and eastern Asia
genus of horse chestnut and buckeye trees

Examples

The Aesculus genus includes several species commonly known as horse chestnuts and buckeyes.
Botanists studied various members of the Aesculus group found in eastern Asia.
Leaves of Aesculus trees are typically large and palmately compound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse chestnut - a tree bearing large, spiny fruits containing shiny seeds; member of genus Aesculus
  • Synonym: buckeye - North American trees and shrubs in the genus Aesculus, especially noted for their nuts

Etymology

From Latin 'Aesculus' (oak or a kind of tree), used in botanical Latin.
