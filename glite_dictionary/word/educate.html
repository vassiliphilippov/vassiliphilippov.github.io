to educate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛdʒʊˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛdjʊˌkeɪt / (BrE)
to teach or refine someone so that they become more discriminating in taste or judgment
teach to develop taste or judgment

Examples

His travels helped educate his palate to appreciate fine wines.
We try to educate our children in matters of good taste and judgment.
Attending art galleries can educate your sense of visual style.

Synset Examples

Cultivate your musical taste
Train your tastebuds
She is well schooled in poetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cultivate - to improve or refine by education or training
  • Synonym: train - to teach someone skills or habits through instruction

Etymology

From Latin educare (to bring up, to train).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02393545-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɛdʒəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛdjʊkeɪt / (BrE)
to give someone knowledge or skills through teaching or training, especially over a period of time
to teach or train someone

Examples

Parents have a responsibility to educate their children both academically and morally.
The program aims to educate young people about the dangers of drug use.
She was educated at one of the top universities in the country.

Synset Examples

The old master is training world-class violinists
we develop the leaders for the future

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to teach - to give instruction to someone
  • Synonym: train - to teach skills or improve ability through practice

Etymology

From Latin educare (to rear, bring up), from ex- (out) + ducere (to lead).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00604693-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈɛdʒʊˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛd.jʊ.keɪt / (BrE)
to give someone knowledge, skills, or training, especially in a school or through instruction
to give knowledge or training

Examples

It is important to educate children about environmental issues from a young age.
The government plans to educate people about the benefits of healthy eating.
She has dedicated her career to educating students in rural communities.

Synset Examples

We must educate our youngsters better

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to teach - to impart knowledge or skill to someone
  • Synonym: train - to develop skills or knowledge through practice and instruction

Etymology

From Latin educare (to bring up, rear, educate), from educere (to lead out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02392628-v
Synset POS: v
