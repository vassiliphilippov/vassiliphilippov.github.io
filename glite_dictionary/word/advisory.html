advisory

1. ADJ

/ ədˈvaɪzəri / (AmE), / ədˈvaɪzəri / (BrE)
having the power or duty to give advice but not to make decisions; providing recommendations or suggestions.
giving advice; providing recommendations

Examples

The panel serves in an advisory capacity to the board of directors.
We received an advisory report offering several courses of action.
An advisory committee was formed to review the proposed policy.

Synset Examples

an advisory memorandum
his function was purely consultative

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consultative - giving advice or opinions, especially as a specialist

Etymology

From advise + -ory, patterned after Late Latin *advisorius* (giving advice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01307737-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ədˈvaɪzəri / (AmE), / ədˈvaɪzəri / (BrE)
an official announcement that warns or gives advice to the public, especially about possible danger or difficult conditions
official warning or advice announcement

Examples

The National Weather Service issued a advisory about the approaching storm.
A travel advisory was released following the civil unrest in the region.
Motorists should pay attention to the fog advisory before planning their routes.

Synset Examples

a frost advisory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warning - a statement telling people about possible danger or problems

Etymology

From 'advise' + '-ory' (adjective and noun-forming suffix), ultimately from Latin 'advisare'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06759721-n
Synset POS: n
