Lashkar-e-Toiba

1. PROPN

/ læʃˌkɑr eɪ toʊˈiːbə / (AmE), / læʃˌkɑː eɪ təʊˈiːbə / (BrE)
an Islamist militant group based in Pakistan, known for carrying out attacks in Kashmir and India with the aim of establishing Islamic rule in the region
Islamist militant group in South Asia

Examples

The Indian government has held Lashkar-e-Toiba responsible for several high-profile attacks in Kashmir.
Lashkar-e-Toiba was founded in the late 1980s with roots linked to Pakistan.
Sanctions have been imposed internationally against Lashkar-e-Toiba due to its involvement in terrorism.

Synset Examples

Lashkar-e-Toiba has committed mass murders of civilian Hindus

Etymology

From Urdu لشکرِ طیبہ (army of the good/pure).
