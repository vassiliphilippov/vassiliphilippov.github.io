to deep-dye

1. VERB

/ diːp daɪ / (AmE), / diːp daɪ / (BrE)
to dye something thoroughly or evenly throughout its entire thickness
dye thoroughly or completely

Examples

The manufacturer decided to deep-dye the fabric to achieve a richer color.
You should deep-dye the wool to make sure the color doesn't fade after washing.
These curtains were deep-dyed for a uniform and lasting shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dye - to change the color of something by soaking it in a colored liquid
