amyl

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmɪl / (AmE), / ˈæmɪl / (BrE)
A hydrocarbon radical with five carbon atoms (C5H11), occurring in various organic compounds.
hydrocarbon radical (C5H11) in organic compounds
medical

Examples

The compound contained an amyl group attached to the aromatic ring.
Chemists often refer to the amyl radical as pentyl.
Various amyl derivatives are used in the synthesis of pharmaceuticals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pentyl - alternative name for the same chemical radical (C5H11)

Etymology

From Latin 'amylum' (starch); first applied to compounds derived from starch.
