disillusioning

1. ADJ

/ dɪsɪˈluːʒənɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsɪˈluːʒənɪŋ / (BrE)
causing someone to lose their illusions or false beliefs; making someone realize that something is not as good as it seemed
causing loss of illusions or false beliefs

Examples

Her first year in the job was a disillusioning experience that made her reconsider her career.
The documentary was disillusioning for viewers who believed in the company's innocence.
It can be disillusioning to discover that your heroes are flawed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: disillusioned - having lost faith or trust in something formerly regarded as good or true
  • Antonym: illusion - a false idea or belief

Etymology

From "disillusion" + "-ing".
