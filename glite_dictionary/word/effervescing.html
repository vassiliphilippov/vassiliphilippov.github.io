effervescing

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛfərˈvɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɛfəˈvɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
giving off bubbles of gas; bubbling or fizzing, especially as a result of carbonation or fermentation.
emitting bubbles; bubbling

Examples

The freshly poured soda was effervescing as tiny bubbles rose to the surface.
A glass of effervescing mineral water can be refreshing on a hot day.
The winemaker observed the effervescing must as fermentation began.

Synset Examples

bubbling champagne
foamy (or frothy) beer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bubbling - giving off bubbles; foaming
  • Antonym: flat - not bubbling; lacking effervescence (of a drink)

Etymology

From Latin effervescere (to boil up), from ex- (out) + fervescere (begin to boil).
