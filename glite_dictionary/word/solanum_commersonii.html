Solanum commersonii

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈlænəm ˌkoʊmərˈsoʊniː / (AmE), / səˈleɪnəm ˌkɒməsəʊˈnaɪ / (BrE)
A species of wild potato native to South America, often used in breeding programs for cultivated potatoes due to its disease resistance.
South American wild potato species

Examples

The wild potato Solanum commersonii has been studied for its cold resistance.
Researchers are exploring Solanum commersonii for its potential in crop improvement programs.
Unlike the common potato, Solanum commersonii is not typically cultivated for food.

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'Solanum' (nightshade); species named after French botanist Philibert Commerson.
