retroflexion

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtroʊˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəʊˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the action of turning the tip of the tongue back against the roof of the mouth, especially in producing certain speech sounds
articulation with tongue turned back

Examples

Many languages, such as Hindi, include sounds produced by retroflexion of the tongue.
Students of phonetics practice retroflexion to master unfamiliar speech sounds.
The teacher demonstrated retroflexion to help the class understand the difference between 't' and 'ṭ'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retroflex - (of a sound) articulated with the tongue curled back against the palate

Etymology

From Latin retroflexio (a bending back), from retroflexus (bent backward).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtroʊˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəʊˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of bending backward, especially in anatomical or technical contexts
the act of bending backward

Examples

The patient's back pain was caused by excessive retroflexion of the spine.
During the examination, the doctor noted a slight retroflexion of the uterus.
Some yoga poses require a gentle retroflexion of the neck or back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexion - the act of bending a limb or joint
  • Antonym: extension - the act of straightening a limb or joint

Etymology

From Latin 'retroflexio', from 'retro-' (backward) + 'flectere' (to bend).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛtroʊˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəʊˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act or condition of bending or turning an organ or body part backward
backward bending of an organ
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed a retroflexion of the uterus during the examination.
Abnormal retroflexion of the spine may cause discomfort or pain.
Surgical intervention might be required to correct severe retroflexion of internal organs.

Synset Examples

retroversion of the uterus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retroversion - a turning backward, especially of an organ

Etymology

From Latin 'retroflexio', from 'retro-' (backward) + 'flexio' (bending).
