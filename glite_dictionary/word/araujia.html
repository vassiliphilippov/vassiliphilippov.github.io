Araujia

1. NOUN

/ əˈraʊdʒiə / (AmE), / əˈraʊdʒiə / (BrE)
a small genus of evergreen vines native to South America, noted for their twining habit and milky sap
South American evergreen vine genus

Examples

The invasive Araujia vine has spread rapidly in parts of Australia.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of several Araujia species.
Gardeners are warned about the potential hazards of planting Araujia near other delicate flora.

Etymology

Named after António de Araújo e Azevedo, a Portuguese statesman.
