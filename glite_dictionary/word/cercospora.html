Cercospora

1. NOUN

/ sɚˌkɑːrˈspɔːrə / (AmE), / sɜːˌkɔːˈspɔːrə / (BrE)
A genus of imperfect fungi that are leaf parasites with long, slender spores, commonly causing leaf spot diseases in plants.
genus of parasitic fungi affecting plants

Examples

The fungus Cercospora is responsible for the widespread leaf spot on sugar beets.
Researchers studied how Cercospora infections affected the soybean yields in the region.
Proper identification of Cercospora helps in choosing effective control measures for infected crops.

Etymology

From Greek *kerkos* (tail) and *spora* (seed, spore), referring to the shape of the spores.

2. NOUN

/ sɜrˌkɑːˈspɔrə / (AmE), / sɜːˌkɒˈspɔːrə / (BrE)
A genus of imperfect fungi that are leaf parasites, producing long slender spores.
genus of parasitic fungi with slender spores

Examples

Several crop diseases are caused by Cercospora species infecting the leaves.
Researchers identified Cercospora under the microscope by its long, slender spores.
The outbreak of leaf spot was traced to a type of Cercospora fungus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungus - any member of the group of eukaryotic organisms that includes yeasts, molds, and mushrooms

Etymology

From Greek *kerkos* (tail) and *spora* (spore), referring to the shape of the spores.
