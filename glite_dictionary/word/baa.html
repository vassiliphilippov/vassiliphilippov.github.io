baa

1. NOUN

/ bɑ / (AmE), / bɑː / (BrE)
the sound made by a sheep or lamb, typically written as 'baa' to represent the animal's cry
the cry made by sheep

Examples

The loud baa of a lamb echoed across the field.
We heard a distant baa as the farmer herded his flock.
The children's book described the gentle baa of the newborn sheep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleat - the cry of a sheep, goat, or calf

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by sheep.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07390768-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ bɑː / (AmE), / bɑː / (BrE)
(of a sheep or lamb) to make the characteristic bleating cry.
make the cry of a sheep or lamb

Examples

The lonely lamb began to baa when it couldn't find its mother.
Every morning, the sheep baa loudly waiting to be fed.
In the petting zoo, children laughed as the goats baaed for attention.

Synset Examples

The lambs were bleating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleat - to make the wavering cry of a sheep, goat, or calf

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by sheep.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
