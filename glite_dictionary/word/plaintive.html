plaintive

1. ADJ

/ ˈpleɪn.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈpleɪn.tɪv / (BrE)
sounding sad or expressing sorrow.
expressing sadness or sorrow

Examples

There was a plaintive note in her voice when she spoke about her childhood.
The plaintive cry of the bird echoed through the empty forest.
He played a plaintive melody on the piano that moved everyone to silence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mournful - feeling, expressing, or inducing sadness or grief
  • Antonym: cheerful - showing or expressing happiness and optimism

Etymology

From Old French plaintif (mournful), from plainte (lament), ultimately from Latin plangere (to lament).
