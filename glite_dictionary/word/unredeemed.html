unredeemed

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈdimd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈdiːmd / (BrE)
(in religious contexts) Not saved from sin or its consequences; in danger of eternal punishment.
not saved from sin; facing eternal punishment

Examples

The preacher warned that the unredeemed would face judgment unless they repented.
She feared for the souls of the unredeemed in the afterlife.
In the story, the unredeemed wander endlessly, seeking forgiveness.

Synset Examples

poor damned souls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damned - condemned to eternal punishment in Hell
  • Antonym: redeemed - saved from sin or its consequences

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + redeem, from Latin redimere (to buy back, recover).
