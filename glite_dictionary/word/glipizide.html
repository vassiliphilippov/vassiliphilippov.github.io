glipizide

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɪpɪˌzaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪpɪˌzaɪd / (BrE)
an oral medication used to control blood sugar levels in people with type 2 diabetes by stimulating insulin release from the pancreas
oral antidiabetic drug
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with type 2 diabetes, her doctor prescribed glipizide to help manage her blood sugar.
The pharmacist explained the potential side effects of glipizide to the patient before dispensing the medication.
He switched from metformin to glipizide after discussing treatment options with his endocrinologist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glibenclamide - an oral antidiabetic drug of the same class as glipizide
