Pityrogramma argentea

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪtiəˈɡræmə ɑːrˌdʒɛnˈtiːə / (AmE), / ˌpɪtɪəˈɡræmə ɑːdʒɛnˈtiːə / (BrE)
A fern native to southern tropical Africa, known for its fronds with white undersides.
African fern with white-backed fronds

Examples

The Pityrogramma argentea is prized by gardeners for its striking white-backed fronds.
We spotted a cluster of Pityrogramma argentea growing along the riverbank in Zimbabwe.
Botanists are studying how Pityrogramma argentea adapts to different soil types in tropical Africa.
