lunger

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌŋər / (AmE), / ˈlʌŋə / (BrE)
a person who makes a sudden forward movement, especially in fencing
someone who lunges (esp. in fencing)

Examples

The experienced lunger caught his opponent off guard with a swift move.
As a skilled lunger, she excelled in quick attacks during fencing bouts.
Every lunger needs precise timing to land a successful strike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fencer - a person who participates in fencing

Etymology

From lunge + -er, meaning one who lunges.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌŋɚ / (AmE), / ˈlʌŋə / (BrE)
a person suffering from pulmonary tuberculosis, especially as used in historical contexts
person with pulmonary tuberculosis

Examples

After years in the mines, he became a lunger and was forced to seek treatment in the mountains.
Stories of lungers heading west for a cure were common in the 1800s.
The sanatorium housed many lungers hoping to recover their health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consumptive - person with tuberculosis (old use)

Etymology

From 'lung' + '-er', referring to a person affected in the lungs; used in 19th- and early 20th-century English.
