hatchet

1. NOUN

/ ˈhætʃɪt / (AmE), / ˈhætʃɪt / (BrE)
a small axe with a short handle, designed to be used with one hand, typically for chopping wood
small axe with short handle

Examples

He used a hatchet to split the small logs for the fire.
The camper packed a hatchet among other essential survival tools.
She swung the hatchet with surprising strength, chopping through the thick branch easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axe - a tool with a heavy bladed head used for chopping

Etymology

From Old French hachette, diminutive of hache (axe); related to the word 'hatchet' for a small axe.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhætʃət / (AmE), / ˈhætʃɪt / (BrE)
A small ax with a short handle, used as a tool or weapon, notably by North American Indians as a fighting ax.
small ax with a short handle, sometimes used as a weapon

Examples

The warrior carried a finely crafted hatchet into battle.
A hatchet was displayed among the tribal artifacts at the museum.
He learned to throw a hatchet accurately during survival training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomahawk - a light ax used as a tool or weapon by North American Indians

Etymology

From Middle English hachet, from Old French hachette, diminutive of hache (ax).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
