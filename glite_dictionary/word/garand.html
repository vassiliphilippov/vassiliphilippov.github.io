Garand

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈrænd / (AmE), / ˈɡærənd / (BrE)
a semiautomatic rifle, especially the M1 Garand, used by the U.S. military during World War II and the Korean War.
semiautomatic rifle (esp. M1 Garand)

Examples

The soldier cleaned his Garand after a long day of training.
My grandfather used a Garand during his service in World War II.
Collectors value the Garand for its historical significance and reliability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: M1 rifle - standard-issue U.S. service rifle in WWII and Korea

Etymology

Named after John C. Garand (1888–1974), its Canadian-American inventor.
