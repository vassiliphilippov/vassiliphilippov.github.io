neolith

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːəˌlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈniːəˌlɪθ / (BrE)
A stone tool made and used during the Neolithic Age, typically characterized by polished surfaces and specialized forms.
stone tool from the Neolithic Age

Examples

Archaeologists discovered a neolith while excavating the ancient settlement.
The neolith displayed intricate craftsmanship and showed evidence of regular use.
Many museums feature neoliths to illustrate advances in prehistoric technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stone tool - an implement or weapon made of stone

Etymology

From Neo- (new) + Greek lithos (stone).
