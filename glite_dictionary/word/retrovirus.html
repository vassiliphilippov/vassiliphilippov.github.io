retrovirus

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛtroʊˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌvaɪərəs / (BrE)
a virus that contains RNA as its genetic material and uses reverse transcriptase to convert its RNA into DNA within a host cell, integrating into the host's genome
virus with RNA and reverse transcription
medical

Examples

The HIV virus is a well-known example of a retrovirus that attacks the immune system.
Researchers are developing new drugs to inhibit the replication of retrovirus in infected patients.
Once a retrovirus enters a cell, it integrates its genetic material into the host's DNA.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: virus - microscopic infectious agent that replicates only inside living cells

Etymology

From Latin 'retro-' (backward) and 'virus' (poison, slimy liquid).
