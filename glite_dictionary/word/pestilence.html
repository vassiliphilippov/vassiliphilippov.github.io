pestilence

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛstɪl(ə)ns / (AmE), / ˈpɛstɪl(ə)ns / (BrE)
A pernicious and harmful influence that is difficult to eliminate, often likened to an infectious disease.
malign, harmful influence

Examples

Corruption has spread like a pestilence throughout the organization.
Social media can become a pestilence if misinformation is allowed to flourish.
In his speech, the minister referred to hatred as a pestilence destroying the community.

Synset Examples

racism is a pestilence at the heart of the nation
according to him, I was the canker in their midst

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blight - something that spoils or damages severely
  • Synonym: scourge - a cause of great trouble or suffering
  • Antonym: blessing - a beneficial influence or condition

Etymology

From Latin pestilentia (plague, pestilence).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛstɪləns / (AmE), / ˈpɛstɪləns / (BrE)
A fatal epidemic disease, especially one with a high death rate and widespread impact.
deadly epidemic disease

Examples

The ancient city was devastated by a pestilence that killed thousands of inhabitants.
Fear of pestilence spread quickly as reports of illness multiplied.
The crops failed, and soon hunger and pestilence swept through the land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plague - a contagious bacterial disease causing high mortality
  • Synonym: disease - an illness that affects people, animals, or plants

Etymology

From Latin 'pestilentia' (a plague, unwholesome condition).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14099961-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛstələns / (AmE), / ˈpɛstɪləns / (BrE)
a serious infectious disease, especially one caused by Yersinia pestis, that can infect rodents and may be transmitted to humans by fleas, often resulting in high mortality
serious infectious disease, often the plague

Examples

The arrival of the pestilence in the city led to widespread panic and fear.
Rats and fleas played a significant role in transmitting the pestilence during medieval outbreaks.
Scientists identified Yersinia pestis as the bacterium responsible for the deadly pestilence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plague - a contagious bacterial disease, especially the bubonic plague

Etymology

From Latin 'pestilentia' (plague, infection).
