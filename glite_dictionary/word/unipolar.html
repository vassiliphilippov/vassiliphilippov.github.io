unipolar

1. ADJ

/ ˌjuː.niˈpoʊ.lɚ / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪˈpəʊ.lə / (BrE)
Having or involving a single pole or center; especially used in science and technology to describe systems, devices, or phenomena characterized by a single pole.
having a single pole (science, technology)

Examples

The unipolar neuron has only one process extending from its cell body.
Modern electronics sometimes use unipolar transistor designs for specific applications.
In a unipolar world order, one country dominates international relations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bipolar - having two poles (contrasting with 'unipolar')

Etymology

From Latin 'uni-' (one) + 'polaris' (pertaining to a pole).
