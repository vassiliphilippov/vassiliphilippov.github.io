fovea

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊ.vi.ə / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.vɪ.ə / (BrE)
a small depression in the retina of the eye where visual acuity is highest, containing a high concentration of cone cells
depression in retina with sharpest vision
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that the fovea is crucial for tasks like reading and recognizing faces.
Damage to the fovea can result in a significant loss of central vision.
Color perception is most accurate in the fovea because of its concentration of cone cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macula - area of the retina surrounding the fovea, responsible for central vision

Etymology

From Latin 'fovea' meaning 'small pit' or 'ditch'.
