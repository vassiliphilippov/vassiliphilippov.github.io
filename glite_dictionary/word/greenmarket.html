greenmarket

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrinˌmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnˌmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
an open-air market where farm products, such as fresh fruits and vegetables, are sold directly by growers
outdoor marketplace for farm products

Examples

Every Saturday, the city holds a greenmarket where residents buy fresh produce directly from farmers.
We picked up organic tomatoes and strawberries at the greenmarket downtown.
Local growers gather at the greenmarket to sell their harvest and homemade goods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmers' market - an outdoor market where local farmers sell their produce directly to consumers

Etymology

Formed from 'green' (referring to fresh produce) + 'market'.
