Damaliscus

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmælɪskəs / (AmE), / dəˈmalɪskəs / (BrE)
A genus of African antelopes known as sassabies, characterized by their long legs and distinctive horns.
genus of African antelopes (sassabies)

Examples

The Damaliscus is easily distinguished by its slightly S-shaped horns.
Researchers observed a decline in Damaliscus populations due to habitat loss.
Conservationists are working to reintroduce Damaliscus to areas where they have disappeared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antelope - a graceful, fast-running, ruminant mammal found in Africa and Asia
  • Synonym: sassaby - a large African antelope, also called Damaliscus lunatus

Etymology

From modern Latin Damaliscus, formed from Greek damalis (heifer, young cow).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdæməˈlɪskəs / (AmE), / ˌdaməˈlɪskəs / (BrE)
A genus of African antelopes in the family Bovidae, including the sassabies and topis.
genus of African antelopes

Examples

The Damaliscus genus includes several swift and graceful African antelopes.
Researchers have documented the migration patterns of Damaliscus species across savannas.
Many naturalists focus on the conservation of Damaliscus due to habitat loss.

Etymology

From modern Latin Damaliscus, formed after Damalis (a genus of antelope from Greek δάμαλις, 'heifer').
