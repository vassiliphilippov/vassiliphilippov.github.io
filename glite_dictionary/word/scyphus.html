scyphus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.fəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪ.fəs / (BrE)
an ancient Greek drinking cup with two handles and a footed base.
ancient Greek drinking cup

Examples

The museum displayed a finely preserved scyphus from the 5th century BC.
She admired the delicate patterns etched onto the silver scyphus.
During the lecture, the professor passed around a replica scyphus for the students to examine.

Etymology

From Latin scyphus, from Greek skyphos (cup).
