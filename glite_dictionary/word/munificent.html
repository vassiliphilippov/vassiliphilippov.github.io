munificent

1. ADJ

/ mjuˈnɪfəsənt / (AmE), / mjuːˈnɪfɪsənt / (BrE)
extremely generous, often giving more than is usual or necessary.
very generous or liberal

Examples

The foundation made a munificent donation to the university, supporting scholarships for hundreds of students.
She thanked her uncle for his munificent gift, which allowed her to travel abroad.
The artist was known for his munificent support of young talent in the community.

Synset Examples

distributed gifts with a lavish hand
the critics were lavish in their praise
a munificent gift
his father gave him a half-dollar and his mother a quarter and he thought them munificent
unsparing generosity
his unstinted devotion
called for unstinting aid to Britain
lavish praise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generous - willing to give more of something than is strictly necessary or expected
  • Antonym: stingy - unwilling to give or spend; ungenerous

Etymology

From Latin munificus (generous, bountiful), from munus (gift, service) + facere (to make).
