dazzle

1. VERB

/ ˈdæzəl / (AmE), / ˈdæzəl / (BrE)
to amaze or impress greatly by exceptional brilliance, skill, wit, or intellect
amaze or impress greatly

Examples

Her performance will dazzle even the most experienced critics.
The scientist's brilliance dazzled his peers at the conference.
They hoped to dazzle the judges with their innovative design.

Synset Examples

Her arguments dazzled everyone
The dancer dazzled the audience with his turns and jumps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to astonish - to surprise or amaze greatly
  • Synonym: to stun - to overwhelm with admiration or shock
  • Antonym: bore - to cause to feel weary or uninterested

Etymology

From Middle English 'daselen', frequentative of 'dasen' (to daze), of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈdæzəl / (AmE), / ˈdæzəl / (BrE)
to cause someone to lose clear vision, especially by shining a very bright light into their eyes
to blind temporarily with intense light

Examples

The camera flash dazzled her, making it hard to see for a few seconds.
Drivers can easily be dazzled by oncoming headlights at night.
The sun dazzled him as he stepped out of the dark theater.

Synset Examples

She was dazzled by the bright headlights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blind - to prevent someone from seeing, either temporarily or permanently

Etymology

From Middle English 'dasel', of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02166865-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈdæzəl / (AmE), / ˈdæzəl / (BrE)
a brightness so intense that it partially and temporarily blinds or confuses the eyes
intense brightness that temporarily blinds

Examples

The dazzle of her diamond necklace caught everyone's eye at the party.
After stepping into the sunlight from the theater, the dazzle made me squint.
The snow created a white dazzle that was almost blinding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glare - a strong, harsh light
  • Antonym: dimness - lack of brightness or light

Etymology

From Middle English 'daselen', frequentative of Old English 'daseian' (to become weary).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04959998-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈdæzəl / (AmE), / ˈdæzəl / (BrE)
to impress or amaze someone greatly, especially by being extremely skillful, beautiful, or clever
to impress or amaze greatly

Examples

The magician's tricks never fail to dazzle his audience.
Her innovative designs continue to dazzle the fashion world.
The new smartphone features are sure to dazzle customers.

Synset Examples

The fireworks will dazzle everyone at the festival.
He managed to dazzle his boss with his presentation.
Her performance was sure to dazzle the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impress - to make someone feel admiration and respect
  • Synonym: to amaze - to surprise someone greatly; to astonish
  • Antonym: bore - to make someone feel bored or uninterested

Etymology

From Old French 'daser', probably of Germanic origin, related to Middle Low German 'dasen' (to daze).
