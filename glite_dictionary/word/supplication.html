supplication

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
an earnest or humble request or prayer, especially to someone in authority or to a deity
humble or earnest request

Examples

Her supplication for mercy moved the judge to show leniency.
In times of crisis, people often turn to prayer and supplication.
He made a supplication to the king, hoping for a pardon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plea - an earnest request or appeal
  • Antonym: demand - a forceful or authoritative request

Etymology

From Latin supplicatio (an entreaty, prayer), from supplicare (to kneel, to entreat).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsʌplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌplɪˈkeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a humble request or prayer, especially one asking for help from God
humble prayer or request for help

Examples

The congregation bowed their heads in supplication during the service.
His supplication for strength was uttered in a soft, trembling voice.
She knelt in supplication, asking for guidance in her prayers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prayer - a solemn request for help or expression of thanks addressed to God or another deity
  • Synonym: invocation - the act of calling upon a deity or spirit for aid

Etymology

From Latin supplicatio (an act of supplicating, public prayer), from supplicare (to plead humbly).

3. NOUN

/ ˌsʌplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of asking or begging for something earnestly or humbly, especially as a prayer to a deity
earnest or humble request, often in prayer

Examples

In times of crisis, people often turn to supplication as a way to seek comfort and guidance.
Her sincere supplication moved the congregation to tears.
The act of supplication can provide a sense of peace and hope.

Synset Examples

the priest sank to his knees in prayer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prayer - a solemn request for help or expression of thanks addressed to God or another deity
  • Synonym: entreaty - an earnest or humble request
  • Antonym: demand - an insistent and peremptory request; the opposite of asking humbly

Etymology

From Latin supplicatio, from supplicare (to beseech)
