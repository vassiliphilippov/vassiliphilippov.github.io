god-awful

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑdˌɔfəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdˌɔːfəl / (BrE)
extremely unpleasant; very bad or dreadful (informal)
very unpleasant; extremely bad
informal

Examples

I couldn't stand the god-awful smell coming from the kitchen.
The film was so god-awful that we left before it ended.
Why do you wear that god-awful tie to every meeting?

Synset Examples

hellish weather
stop that god-awful racket

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horrible - very unpleasant; causing horror
  • Antonym: delightful - highly pleasing, enjoyable

Etymology

Formed from 'God' + 'awful', originally meaning 'awful in a way evoking God', now used as an intensifier for 'awful'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑdˌɔfəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdˌɔːf(ə)l / (BrE)
extremely bad or unpleasant; dreadful
extremely bad; dreadful
informal

Examples

The food at that restaurant was god-awful and I couldn't finish my meal.
I woke up with a god-awful headache this morning.
Their new album is god-awful; I couldn't listen to more than two songs.

Synset Examples

His attitude during the meeting was simply god-awful.
That movie was god-awful; I can't believe I watched it.
The weather yesterday was just god-awful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awful - very bad or unpleasant
  • Synonym: terrible - extremely bad or serious
  • Antonym: wonderful - extremely good or delightful

Etymology

Formed from 'God' and 'awful,' originally meaning 'inspiring awe (of God),' but now used as an emphatic intensifier for 'awful.'
