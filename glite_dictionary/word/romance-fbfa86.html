romance

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈmæns / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæns / (BrE)
a novel or other narrative that describes idealized, often adventurous or heroic events, typically removed from everyday reality
novel with idealized or adventurous events

Examples

Her favorite romance transports readers to a mythical kingdom full of daring knights and mysterious quests.
The library's collection includes several classic romances from the nineteenth century.
He dreams of writing a sweeping historical romance set in ancient Persia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romantic novel - a novel focused on idealized love and relationships

Etymology

From Old French 'romanz', originally meaning a work in the vernacular Romance language, later referring to adventurous tales.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06380839-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ roʊˈmæns / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæns / (BrE)
a story, novel, or film that focuses on a love relationship, often with emotional or sentimental elements
love story; romantic novel or film

Examples

She loves reading romance novels on rainy afternoons.
The latest movie is a romance set in Paris during springtime.
The author's new romance quickly became a bestseller.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: love story - a story about a romantic relationship
  • Antonym: tragedy - a story with a sad or disastrous ending, often contrasted with romance

Etymology

From Old French 'romanz' (verse narrative), from Medieval Latin 'romanice' (in the Romanic language).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06382890-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ roʊˈmæns / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmæns / (BrE)
a close or passionate relationship between two people who are in love, often involving emotional attachment and affection
love relationship between two people

Examples

Their romance began during their final year of college and grew stronger over time.
Many classic movies tell the story of an unexpected romance blossoming between unlikely partners.
Despite the distance, their romance survived and even flourished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affair - a romantic or sexual relationship, often secret
  • Synonym: relationship - an emotional or romantic connection between people
  • Antonym: friendship - a close and trusting relationship, not necessarily romantic

Etymology

From Old French *romanz* (a story in the vernacular), originally referring to tales of chivalry and love.
