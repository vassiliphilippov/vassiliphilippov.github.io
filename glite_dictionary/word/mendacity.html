mendacity

1. NOUN

/ mɛnˈdæsəti / (AmE), / mɛnˈdæsɪti / (BrE)
the quality or tendency of being untruthful; dishonesty or lying
tendency to be untruthful

Examples

The politician's mendacity was exposed when the truth finally came out.
Her habitual mendacity made it difficult for anyone to trust her.
Critics accused the company of mendacity in its response to the scandal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest and truthful
  • Synonym: falsehood - the state of being untrue or a lie

Etymology

From Latin mendacitas, from mendax (lying, false).
