silkily

1. ADV

/ ˈsɪkəli / (AmE), / ˈsɪkɪli / (BrE)
In a way that is smooth, soft, or resembling silk, either literally or figuratively.
in a smooth, silky manner

Examples

The young wheat shone silkily in the morning sun.
She spoke silkily, her voice calm but full of warning.
The cat's fur felt silkily soft between his fingers.

Synset Examples

the young wheat shone silkily
‘Darling,’ she said silkily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoothly - in a smooth manner
  • Antonym: roughly - in a rough manner; opposite of smoothly

Etymology

Formed from 'silky' + '-ly', indicating 'in the manner of silk'.
