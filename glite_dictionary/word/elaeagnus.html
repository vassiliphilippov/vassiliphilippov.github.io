Elaeagnus

1. NOUN

/ ɛliˈæɡnəs / (AmE), / ɛliˈæɡnəs / (BrE)
a genus of shrubs or small trees known as oleasters, with silvery foliage and edible fruits, found in temperate regions.
genus of shrubs or small trees (oleaster)

Examples

The Elaeagnus in our garden produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring.
Some species of Elaeagnus are cultivated for their edible berries.
Elaeagnus is valued for its hardy nature and attractive silvery leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oleaster - another name for shrubs of the genus Elaeagnus

Etymology

From Greek *elaia* (olive) + *agnos* (chaste tree), indicating similarities to these plants.

2. NOUN

/ iˌliˈæɡnəs / (AmE), / ɛˌliːˈæɡnəs / (BrE)
a genus of shrubs and small trees known as oleasters, with silvery or scaly foliage and edible fruits, found chiefly in temperate and subtropical regions.
a genus of shrubs and small trees (oleaster)

Examples

The Elaeagnus species are valued for their drought resistance and ornamental uses in gardens.
Researchers studied the fruiting patterns of Elaeagnus in various climates.
The hedge was planted with Elaeagnus to provide both privacy and edible berries.

Etymology

From Greek elaia (olive) + agnos (chaste tree), referring to the resemblance of its fruits and leaves.
