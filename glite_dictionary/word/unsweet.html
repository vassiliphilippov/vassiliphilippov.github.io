unsweet

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈswiːt / (AmE), / ʌnˈswiːt / (BrE)
(of champagne or other wines) moderately dry; not sweet in taste.
moderately dry (esp. of champagne)

Examples

This vintage is an unsweet champagne ideal for those who prefer less sugar.
The sommelier recommended an unsweet sparkling wine to accompany the seafood.
I enjoy choosing a slightly unsweet wine for dinner parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dry - containing little or no sweetness, especially of wine or champagne
  • Antonym: sweet - having a sugary taste; not dry, especially of wine

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈswit / (AmE), / ʌnˈswiːt / (BrE)
not pleasant or agreeable; distasteful or disagreeable.
distasteful; not pleasant

Examples

He found the experience unsweet, wishing things had turned out differently.
After the argument, their friendship became strangely unsweet.
Life can sometimes feel unsweet during difficult times.

Synset Examples

he found life unsweet

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweet - pleasant or agreeable
  • Synonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'sweet', meaning 'not sweet'.
