rival

1. ADJ

/ ˈraɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈraɪvəl / (BrE)
competing with another; having the same claim, desire, or status as another in a competition or rivalry.
competing; in rivalry

Examples

The two rival teams faced each other in the championship match.
Several rival companies launched new products at the same time.
She found herself in a rival position to her former business partner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competing - actively striving against others
  • Antonym: cooperating - working together toward the same goal

Etymology

From Latin rivalis (competing for the same stream), from rivus (stream).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-80644890-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈraɪ.vəl / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.vəl / (BrE)
a person, group, or organization competing with another for the same objective; a competitor.
competitor; person or group in competition

Examples

She trained hard all summer to outperform her rival in the championship.
The two companies have been rivals in the tech industry for years.
Despite being fierce rivals, the athletes congratulated each other at the finish line.

Synset Examples

he had respect for his rivals
he wanted to know what the competition was doing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competitor - a person or group that competes with another
  • Synonym: opponent - someone who competes against or fights another
  • Antonym: ally - a person or group that cooperates with another

Etymology

From Latin 'rivalis' (a person using the same stream), from 'rivus' (stream).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10552570-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈraɪ.vəl / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.vəl / (BrE)
to be as good as or compete with someone or something; to be the rival of.
compete with or match (verb)

Examples

These two companies rival each other in technological innovation.
No other athlete could rival her speed on the track.
The new artist's paintings rival those of the old masters in beauty.

Synset Examples

we are rivaling for first place in the race

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compete - strive to gain or win something by defeating or establishing superiority over others

Etymology

From Latin rivalis (a competitor, originally one who uses the same stream).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01124620-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈraɪ.vəl / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.vəl / (BrE)
to be as good as or nearly as good as someone or something; to match in quality, ability, or achievement
to match in quality or ability

Examples

Her cooking skills rival those of professional chefs.
No other artist can rival his level of creativity.
This smartphone's battery life rivals that of much more expensive models.

Synset Examples

Nothing can rival cotton for durability
Your performance doesn't even touch that of your colleagues
Her persistence and ambition only matches that of her parents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equal - to be as good as or the same in quality as something else
  • Antonym: to surpass - to exceed or go beyond in achievement or quality

Etymology

From Latin 'rivalis' (one using the same stream as another, a competitor).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02677976-v
Synset POS: v
