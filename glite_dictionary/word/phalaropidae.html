Phalaropidae

1. NOUN

/ fəˌlærəˈpɪdiː / (AmE), / fəˌlarəˈpɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small wading birds known as phalaropes, characterized by their lobed toes and habit of swimming in water.
family of wading birds (phalaropes)

Examples

The Phalaropidae are distinguished by their unusual feeding behavior, often spinning in circles on the water.
Researchers observed several members of the Phalaropidae family during the migratory season.
Unlike many other shorebirds, most Phalaropidae species are known to swim rather than wade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phalarope - a type of small wading bird in the Phalaropidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, from genus name Phalaropus plus the family suffix -idae.

2. NOUN

/ fæləˈrɑːpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / faləˈrəʊpɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small wading birds known as phalaropes, noted for their unusual feeding behavior and often found in northern regions.
family of wading birds (phalaropes)

Examples

The Phalaropidae are distinguished by their lobed toes, which aid them in swimming.
Scientists have studied the migratory patterns of birds in the Phalaropidae family.
Females in Phalaropidae species are typically more brightly colored than males.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phalarope - a small wading bird of the genus Phalaropus, belonging to Phalaropidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Phalaropus.
