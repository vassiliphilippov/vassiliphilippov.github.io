Balaenoptera musculus

1. NOUN

/ bəˌliːnɑpˈtɛrə ˌməsˈkjuːləs / (AmE), / baˌliːnɒpˈtɛərə ˌmʌsˈkjuːləs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the blue whale, the largest known animal, a bluish-grey migratory baleen whale found worldwide, especially in the Southern Hemisphere
scientific name for the blue whale

Examples

Scientists have long studied the migratory patterns of Balaenoptera musculus in the Southern Ocean.
The Balaenoptera musculus can reach lengths of over 30 meters, making it the largest animal to have ever lived.
Marine biologists were excited to spot a pair of Balaenoptera musculus off the coast during their expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue whale - the largest animal on earth; common name for Balaenoptera musculus

Etymology

From Latin: 'balaena' (whale) + 'ptera' (fin) + 'musculus' (little mouse); 'musculus' was early used as a term for a type of whale.
