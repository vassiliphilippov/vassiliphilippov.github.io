Ratibida

1. NOUN

/ rəˈtɪbɪdə / (AmE), / rəˈtɪbɪdə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial wildflowers native to the plains and prairies of North America, known for their showy flower heads; often cultivated as ornamental plants.
genus of North American wildflowers

Examples

The garden features several Ratibida species that bloom from midsummer to fall.
Many gardeners choose Ratibida for its vibrant, cone-shaped flowers.
Ratibida thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Latin roots, used as a botanical genus name.
