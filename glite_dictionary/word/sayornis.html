Sayornis

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈɔrnɪs / (AmE), / saɪˈɔːnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small insect-eating birds in the tyrant flycatcher family, commonly known as phoebes.
genus of birds (phoebes)

Examples

The Eastern Sayornis is often seen perched near streams and open woodlands.
Several species within the Sayornis genus are known for their gentle call notes.
Birdwatchers spotted a rare Sayornis in the migratory stopover region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phoebe - a small North American flycatcher bird

Etymology

From scientific Latin, probably combining 'Say' (in honor of American naturalist Thomas Say) and Greek 'ornis' (bird).

2. NOUN

/ seɪˈɔrnɪs / (AmE), / saɪˈɔːnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small North and Central American birds in the tyrant flycatcher family, commonly known as phoebes.
a genus of birds (phoebes)

Examples

The Eastern Sayornis is easily identified by its distinctive call.
Researchers observed several Sayornises nesting along the riverbank.
Unlike other flycatchers, the Sayornis often wags its tail while perched.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phoebe - a small songbird of North America, also a member of Sayornis

Etymology

From scientific Latin, coined by H. W. Henshaw in 1858, combining 'Say' (after Thomas Say, American naturalist) and 'ornis' (Greek for 'bird').
