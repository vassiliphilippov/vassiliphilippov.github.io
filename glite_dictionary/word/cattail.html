cattail

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈkætˌteɪl / (BrE)
a tall plant with long, flat leaves and a cylindrical brown flower spike, commonly found in marshes and wetlands
marsh plant with brown flower spike

Examples

Large colonies of cattail often thrive along the edges of ponds and lakes.
The brown, sausage-shaped spike of the cattail is easily recognizable in wetlands.
Birds sometimes use parts of the cattail to build their nests.

Etymology

Named for its flower spike, which resembles a cat's tail.
