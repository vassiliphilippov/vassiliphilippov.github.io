Avena barbata

1. NOUN

/ əˈviːnə bɑːrˈbeɪtə / (AmE), / əˈviːnə bɑːˈbɑːtə / (BrE)
a species of wild oat native to southern Europe and southwestern Asia
species of wild oat

Examples

Avena barbata is commonly found growing in Mediterranean grasslands.
Researchers study Avena barbata to understand plant adaptation to arid climates.
The spread of Avena barbata has impacted native ecosystems in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild oat - any of several grasses of the genus Avena that grow wild; the broader group to which Avena barbata belongs

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Avena' (oat) and species 'barbata' (bearded).
