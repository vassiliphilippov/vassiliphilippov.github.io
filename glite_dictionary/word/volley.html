volley

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑːli / (AmE), / ˈvɒli / (BrE)
(in tennis) a shot or return in which the ball is struck before it touches the ground.
tennis shot hit before bouncing

Examples

She finished the point with a beautifully timed volley at the net.
He moved forward and hit a volley to surprise his opponent.
Practicing your volley can significantly improve your tennis game.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: half-volley - a tennis shot where the ball is hit immediately after it bounces

Etymology

From Middle French *volée* (flight), from Old French *voler* (to fly), from Latin *volare* (to fly).
