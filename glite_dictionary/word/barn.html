barn

1. NOUN

/ bɑrn / (AmE), / bɑːn / (BrE)
(Physics) A unit of area used in nuclear physics to measure the effective cross-sectional area of atomic nuclei, equal to 10⁻²⁸ square meters.
unit of nuclear area (10⁻²⁸ m²)

Examples

The proton-proton scattering cross section is typically measured in barns.
For some reactions, the probability is less than one barn.
The unit barn is convenient for expressing extremely small nuclear areas.

Etymology

Coined humorously by American physicists around 1942, suggesting 'as easy to hit as the side of a barn.'

2. NOUN

/ bɑrn / (AmE), / bɑːn / (BrE)
a large farm building used for storing grain, hay, or straw or for housing livestock
a farm building for storage or animals

Examples

The old barn was filled with bales of hay and the smell of animals.
We painted the barn red to match the rest of the farm buildings.
Horses are usually kept in a stable, but sometimes they stay in a barn during winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shed - a simple roofed structure, often used for storage
  • Synonym: stable - a building for housing horses or other livestock

Etymology

From Old English bern (barley-house), from bere (barley) + ærn (house).
