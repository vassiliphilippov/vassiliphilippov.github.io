edger

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛdʒər / (AmE), / ˈɛdʒə / (BrE)
a tool used to trim and define the edge of a lawn or garden bed
tool for trimming lawn edges

Examples

I bought a new edger to keep my lawn looking neat.
Using an edger makes the borders between the grass and walkway crisp and clean.
After mowing, she finished the job by running the edger along the driveway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lawn mower - machine for cutting grass

Etymology

From 'edge' + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛdʒər / (AmE), / ˈɛdʒə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to put finishing edges on garments
person finishing garment edges

Examples

She worked as an edger in a clothing factory for several years.
The edger ensured the garment had a smooth, professional hemline.
Each dress was passed to the edger before it was ready for sale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finisher - a person who completes the final details of a product, especially in manufacturing
