Asterope

1. PROPN

/ əˈstɛr.ə.pi / (AmE), / əˈstɛr.ə.pi / (BrE)
one of the stars in the Pleiades star cluster, located in the constellation Taurus.
star in the Pleiades cluster

Examples

Astronomers observed Asterope while studying the Pleiades cluster.
Asterope is sometimes visible to the naked eye on clear nights.
The legend of Asterope originates from Greek mythology, like many other Pleiades stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pleiades - group of stars in the constellation Taurus

Etymology

From Greek 'Asteropē', a name in mythology associated with the Pleiades.

2. PROPN

/ əˈstɛrəˌpi / (AmE), / əˈstɛərəˌpiː / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, one of the seven Pleiades, the daughters of Atlas and Pleione.
one of the seven Pleiades in Greek mythology

Examples

The myth tells that Asterope was pursued by the god Ares.
A sculpture in the museum is believed to represent Asterope with her sisters.
Many ancient poets wrote about Asterope and her place among the Pleiades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pleiades - the seven daughters of Atlas and Pleione in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀστερόπη (*Asteropē*), meaning 'lightning' or 'star-faced'.
