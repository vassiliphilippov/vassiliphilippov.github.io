welkin

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛl.kɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɛl.kɪn / (BrE)
the sky or the celestial dome; the apparent vault of heaven
the sky; the heavens

Examples

The thunder echoed through the welkin with a fearsome roar.
In old poetry, the welkin often symbolizes the vastness of the heavens.
Stars began to appear one by one across the dusky welkin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sky - the region of the atmosphere and outer space seen from the earth

Etymology

From Middle English 'welken', from Old English 'wolcen' (cloud, sky), related to Dutch 'wolk' (cloud).
