to install

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈstɔl / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɔːl / (BrE)
to set up equipment, machinery, or software so that it is ready to use
set up for use (equipment, software, etc.)

Examples

The technician will install your new dishwasher tomorrow afternoon.
Before using the program, you must install it on your computer.
We hired a plumber to install a new water heater in our basement.

Synset Examples

install the washer and dryer
We put in a new sink

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uninstall - to remove installed equipment or software
  • Synonym: to set up - to prepare or arrange something for use

Etymology

From French *installer*, from Italian *installare*, from Medieval Latin *installare* (to place in position).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01572394-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈstɔl / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɔːl / (BrE)
to formally place someone in an official position or office
place in office or position

Examples

The new board members were installed during a formal ceremony last Friday.
She was installed as CEO after the former director retired.
They install a new chairperson every two years in accordance with their bylaws.

Synset Examples

the new president was installed immediately after the election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inaugurate - to formally begin or induct into office
  • Antonym: remove - to take someone out of an office or position

Etymology

From Latin *installare*, from in- 'in' + stallum 'stall, place'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02389183-v
Synset POS: v
