Comandra pallida

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈmændrə pəˈlaɪdə / (AmE), / kəˈmændrə pəˈliːdə / (BrE)
A woody, creeping parasitic plant native to western North America, with thick powdery leaves and clusters of small dull-white flowers.
woody parasitic plant of western North America

Examples

The arid slopes were dotted with Comandra pallida, blending in with the surrounding shrubs.
Researchers studied the unique parasitic adaptations of Comandra pallida in western North America.
I learned to identify Comandra pallida by its powdery leaves during our field trip.
