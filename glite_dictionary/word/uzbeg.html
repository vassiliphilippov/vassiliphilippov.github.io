Uzbeg

1. NOUN

/ ˈʊz.bɛg / (AmE), / ˈʌz.bɛg / (BrE)
a member of a Turkic ethnic group native to Uzbekistan and neighboring regions
a member of a Turkic group from Uzbekistan

Examples

The Uzbeg people have a rich cultural heritage rooted in Central Asia.
Many Uzbeg communities celebrate the Nowruz festival with traditional music and dance.
An Uzbeg chef prepared delicious plov at the international food fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uzbek - a member of a Turkic ethnic group from Uzbekistan

Etymology

From Uzbek "Oʻzbek", referring to the ethnic group and related state.

2. NOUN

/ uzˈbɛɡ / (AmE), / ʊzˈbɛɡ / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken by the Uzbek people of Central Asia.
Turkic language of the Uzbek people

Examples

She is fluent in both Russian and Uzbeg.
The novel was translated from Uzbeg into English last year.
Many schools in the region offer classes taught in Uzbeg.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uzbek - the Turkic language spoken by the Uzbek people

Etymology

From Uzbek, ultimately from Turkic roots referring to 'independent' or 'ruler' (Uzbek Khan).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
