auction

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈɔːk.ʃən / (BrE)
a public sale in which goods or property are sold to the person who offers the highest price
public sale to highest bidder

Examples

The painting was sold at an auction for a record price.
They decided to hold an auction to raise money for charity.
He bid on several items during the auction but didn't win any.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sale - the exchange of goods for money
  • Antonym: purchase - the act of buying something

Etymology

From Latin 'auctio' (an increasing, auction), from 'augere' (to increase).

2. VERB

/ ˈɑk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈɔːk.ʃən / (BrE)
to sell something at a public sale in which goods or property are sold to the highest bidder
sell at a public sale to the highest bidder

Examples

They decided to auction the old paintings to raise money for charity.
The company will auction off several rare artifacts next month.
She auctioned her car online instead of selling it privately.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bid - to offer a price at an auction

Etymology

From Latin 'auctio' (an increasing, an auction).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔkʃən / (AmE), / ˈɔːkʃən / (BrE)
A form of the card game bridge, in which tricks made in excess of the contract are scored toward game; now generally replaced by contract bridge.
a former variant of bridge (card game)

Examples

My grandfather used to play auction with his friends every weekend.
Before contract bridge became popular, many clubs organized auction tournaments.
Learning auction helps you appreciate the evolution of modern bridge.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: contract bridge - the modern form of bridge, which replaced auction bridge
