bimanual

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈmænjuəl / (AmE), / baɪˈmanjuːəl / (BrE)
involving or requiring the use of both hands
requiring both hands

Examples

The surgeon performed the delicate procedure using a bimanual technique.
This instrument is bimanual, so you'll need both hands to use it safely.
Piano players often develop strong bimanual coordination through practice.

Synset Examples

a two-handed sledgehammer
a two-handed crosscut saw
a machine designed for bimanual operation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unimanual - involving or requiring the use of one hand

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'manualis' (of the hand).
