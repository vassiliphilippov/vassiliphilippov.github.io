Pyxidanthera barbulata

1. NOUN

/ pɪkˌsɪdənˈθɪrə bɑːrˈbjuːlətə / (AmE), / pɪkˌsɪdanˈθɪərə bɑːˈbjuːlətə / (BrE)
A creeping evergreen shrub native to the pine barrens of New Jersey and the Carolinas, with narrow overlapping leaves and early white star-shaped flowers.
creeping evergreen shrub of pine barrens, US

Examples

Pyxidanthera barbulata can be found blooming in the sandy pine barrens in early spring.
The delicate flowers of Pyxidanthera barbulata are a sure sign that the season is changing.
Botanists are studying how Pyxidanthera barbulata adapts to the unique soil of the New Jersey pine barrens.

Etymology

From Greek 'pyxis' (box) and 'anthera' (anther); species name from Latin 'barbulatus' (bearded).
