portable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɔːrtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtəbəl / (BrE)
(of an engine or piece of equipment) designed to be easily carried or moved and used in different places, especially as attached to the outside of a boat's hull.
easily carried or moved; detachable equipment

Examples

We bought a portable outboard motor for our small fishing boat.
The portable engine can be attached to the hull when needed and removed for storage.
Modern boats often rely on portable motors for greater convenience and safety.

Synset Examples

a portable outboard motor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detachable - able to be removed from something else

Etymology

From Medieval Latin portabilis, from Latin portare (to carry).

WordNet ID: oewn-01294712-s

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtəbəl / (BrE)
a small, lightweight typewriter designed to be easily carried, often with its own case
a small, easily carried typewriter

Examples

She packed her portable before leaving for her writing retreat in the mountains.
The antique shop displayed a 1950s portable in a leather case.
Journalists in the past often depended on a trusty portable for their reports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typewriter - a machine with keys for producing characters similar to those produced by printers

Etymology

From Latin 'portabilis', from 'portare' meaning 'to carry'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03991427-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈpɔːrtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtəbəl / (BrE)
able to be easily carried or moved, especially because of being a lighter and smaller version than usual.
easy to carry or move

Examples

The portable speaker lasted for hours on a single charge during our picnic.
She prefers using a portable computer so she can work from different locations.
They bought a portable air conditioner to keep the bedroom cool during the summer.

Synset Examples

a portable television set

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mobile - able to move or be moved easily and freely
  • Antonym: stationary - not moving or not intended to be moved

Etymology

From Latin portare (to carry).

Synset POS: a
