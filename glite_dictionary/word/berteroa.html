Berteroa

1. NOUN

/ bɜːrˈtɛr.oʊ.ə / (AmE), / bɜːˈtɪə.rəʊ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the mustard family (Brassicaceae), including hoary alyssum, native to Eurasia.
genus of plants, incl. hoary alyssum

Examples

Berteroa is known for its woolly leaves and small white flowers.
The most common species in the genus Berteroa is hoary alyssum.
Botanists recently discovered a new population of Berteroa in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoary alyssum - A species of Berteroa, an invasive plant in North America

Etymology

Named for Italian botanist Carlo Bertero (1789–1831).
