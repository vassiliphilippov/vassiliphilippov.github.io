chummy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃəmi / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌmi / (BrE)
friendly or on close, familiar terms, especially in an informal way
friendly; on close terms (informal)
informal

Examples

Despite their rivalry, the two leaders appeared quite chummy during the press conference.
He's become chummy with almost everyone at the new office.
Don't get too chummy with him; you barely know him.

Synset Examples

the bartender was chummy with the regular customers
the two were thick as thieves for months

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friendly - kind and pleasant in demeanor or actions
  • Antonym: aloof - not friendly or forthcoming; cool and distant

Etymology

From English 'chum' (close friend) + '-y' (adjective suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃʌmi / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌmi / (BrE)
behaving in a very friendly or familiar way; like close friends.
very friendly; like close friends
informal

Examples

They became quite chummy after working together for a few weeks.
Julia and Mark are so chummy that people often mistake them for siblings.
His chummy attitude made everyone feel welcome at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friendly - kind and pleasant; showing friendship
  • Antonym: aloof - not friendly or forthcoming; cool and distant

Etymology

From 'chum' (close friend), likely from 17th-century slang for 'roommate' or 'companion'.
