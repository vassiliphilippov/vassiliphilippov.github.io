Taxodium

1. NOUN

/ tækˈsoʊdiəm / (AmE), / tækˈsəʊdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of coniferous trees in the cypress family, commonly known as bald cypress or swamp cypress, native to North America and often found in wetlands.
genus of swamp or bald cypress trees

Examples

The Taxodium trees in the wetlands create a unique landscape with their distinctive roots.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the genus Taxodium.
Many southern swamps are dominated by Taxodium, also known as bald cypress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bald cypress - common name for Taxodium distichum, a North American swamp tree
  • Synonym: swamp cypress - common name for trees of the genus Taxodium found in wetlands

Etymology

From Greek 'taxos' (yew) and 'eidos' (form, resemblance), referring to the yew-like appearance.

2. NOUN

/ tækˈsoʊdiəm / (AmE), / tækˈsəʊdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of coniferous trees in the cypress family, native to North America, including the bald cypress and swamp cypress, typically found in wetland areas.
a genus of cypress trees, including bald cypress

Examples

The towering Taxodium trees lined the banks of the southern bayou.
Many wetlands are home to species belonging to the genus Taxodium.
Scientists studied the unique root structures of Taxodium in flood-prone areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bald cypress - a deciduous coniferous tree of the species Taxodium distichum
  • Synonym: swamp cypress - another name for bald cypress or Taxodium species

Etymology

From Greek 'taxos' (yew) and 'eidos' (form, likeness).
