recompense

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkəmˌpɛns / (AmE), / ˈrɛkəmˌpɛns / (BrE)
Compensation or reward given for loss, harm, or effort.
compensation or reward for loss or service

Examples

The company offered financial recompense to employees harmed by the accident.
As recompense for her help, he gave her a generous gift.
Victims of the disaster are seeking recompense for their losses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensation - something, typically money, awarded to someone for loss, suffering, or injury
  • Synonym: reward - something given in return for service or effort

Etymology

From Old French 'recompensier', from Late Latin 'recompensare' (to weigh one thing against another).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛk.əmˌpɛns / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.əm.pɛns / (BrE)
payment or reward given for services rendered or as compensation for loss or harm suffered
payment or reward for service or loss

Examples

The company offered generous recompense for extra hours worked during the busy season.
Victims of the error received no recompense for their inconvenience.
As a token of appreciation, he was given a small recompense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensation - the act of making amends for loss or harm; payment
  • Synonym: reward - something given in return for service or achievement
  • Antonym: punishment - a penalty given for wrongdoing

Etymology

From Old French *recompense* (reward), from Late Latin *recompensare* (to compensate).
