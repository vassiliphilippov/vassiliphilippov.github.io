nonconducting

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnkənˈdʌktɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈdʌktɪŋ / (BrE)
not allowing heat, electricity, or sound to pass through; not conducting.
not conducting heat, electricity, or sound

Examples

Rubber is a nonconducting material often used to coat wires.
The researchers used a nonconducting barrier to isolate the electrical components.
Glass is generally considered nonconducting with respect to electricity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insulating - preventing passage of electricity, heat, or sound
  • Antonym: conducting - allowing heat, electricity, or sound to pass through

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'conducting' (carrying or transmitting).
