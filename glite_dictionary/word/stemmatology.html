stemmatology

1. NOUN

/ stɛmˌmætəˈlɑdʒi / (AmE), / stɛmˌmætəˈlɒdʒi / (BrE)
the study of the relationships among manuscripts of a text and the attempt to reconstruct its transmission history by analyzing those relationships
study of manuscript relationships

Examples

Stemmatology is crucial when studying ancient texts with multiple surviving manuscripts.
Advances in digital tools have revolutionized research in stemmatology.
Many literary scholars rely on stemmatology to trace the origins of textual variants.

Synset Examples

stemmatology also plays an important role in musicology
transcription errors are of decisive importance in stemmatics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stemmatics - the theory or practice of reconstructing the relationships among textual witnesses

Etymology

From Greek 'stemma' (family tree, lineage) + '-logy' (study of).
