Jean Baptiste Lully

1. PROPN

/ ʒɑ̃ bɑːˈtist ˈluːli / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ bæsˈtiːst ˈlʊli / (BrE)
(1632–1687) A French composer of Italian origin, noted as the court composer to Louis XIV and regarded as the founder of French national opera.
French composer, founder of French opera

Examples

Jean Baptiste Lully introduced a new style of opera to the French court.
The works of Jean Baptiste Lully greatly influenced baroque music across Europe.
Many music historians credit Jean Baptiste Lully with shaping the development of ballet music.

Etymology

Italian-born; original name Giovanni Battista Lulli.
