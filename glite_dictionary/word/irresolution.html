irresolution

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪrˌrɛzəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪrɛzəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the state or quality of being uncertain or indecisive; lack of firmness or determination
lack of decisiveness or firmness

Examples

Her irresolution led her to miss several important opportunities.
The committee's irresolution frustrated those who wanted swift action.
Faced with two equally attractive offers, his irresolution was understandable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resolution - the quality of being firmly determined; decisiveness
  • Synonym: indecision - inability to make a decision quickly

Etymology

From Latin 'irresolutio', from 'irresolutus' (not resolved).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪrəˈzuːlʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪrɛzəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
The state of being uncertain or indecisive; lack of firmness or determination in making a decision.
lack of decision or certainty

Examples

Her irresolution caused her to miss several good opportunities.
He struggled with irresolution before finally making up his mind.
Moments of irresolution are normal when faced with life-changing decisions.

Synset Examples

his indecision was only momentary but the opportunity was lost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indecision - difficulty in making a decision
  • Synonym: uncertainty - the state of being unsure
  • Antonym: resolution - the quality of being determined or decisive

Etymology

From Latin 'irresolutio' (want of resolution), from 'in-' (not) + 'resolutio' (loosening, solution).
