incandescence

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.kænˈdɛs.əns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kænˈdɛs.əns / (BrE)
the emission of visible light by a body as a result of its high temperature
light emission due to heat

Examples

The incandescence of molten lava lit up the night sky.
Traditional light bulbs produce incandescence by passing electricity through a filament.
The stove’s element glowed with a brilliant incandescence after being switched on.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: luminescence - light emission not resulting from heat

Etymology

From Latin 'incandescere' (to glow, become hot), from 'in-' (into) + 'candescere' (to become white, to glow).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.kænˈdɛs.əns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kænˈdɛs.əns / (BrE)
the emission of visible light by a hot object as its temperature increases
emission of light due to heat

Examples

The incandescence of the metal could be seen from across the room as it glowed red-hot.
Old-fashioned light bulbs work by producing incandescence when electricity heats the filament.
The potter admired the incandescence of the kiln just before opening it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: luminescence - the emission of light not caused by heat

Etymology

From French incandescent, from Latin incandescere (to become white with heat), from in- (into) + candescere (to become bright, to glow).
