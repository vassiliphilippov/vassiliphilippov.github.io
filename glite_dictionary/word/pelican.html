pelican

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛlɪkən / (AmE), / ˈpelɪkən / (BrE)
a large seabird with a long bill and a distinctive throat pouch used for catching fish, typically found in warm regions
large seabird with a throat pouch

Examples

A pelican soared gracefully above the waves, scanning the water for fish.
The children watched in amazement as a pelican scooped up its meal with its huge bill.
Many tourists come to the lake to photograph the resident pelicans during the breeding season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cormorant - a large, dark waterbird with a long neck and hooked bill
  • Synonym: albatross - a very large seabird known for its long wings and gliding flight

Etymology

From Greek 'pelekan', possibly related to 'pelekys' (axe), referencing its long beak.
