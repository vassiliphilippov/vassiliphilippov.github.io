Balsaminaceae

1. NOUN

/ bælˌsæməˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / bɔːlˌsamɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, including the impatiens and balsam, distinguished by their irregular flowers
family of flowering plants (impatiens, balsam)

Examples

The Balsaminaceae family includes popular garden plants like busy Lizzie.
Botanists classify impatiens as members of the Balsaminaceae.
Distinctive, irregular flowers are a hallmark of the Balsaminaceae.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Geraniaceae - family of flowering plants including geraniums

Etymology

From New Latin Balsaminaceae, based on the genus Balsamina.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbæl.səˌmɪˈneɪ.siːˌiː / (AmE), / ˌbæl.səˌmɪˈneɪ.siːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants distinguished by irregular flowers, including the garden balsam and touch-me-not.
plant family with irregular flowers

Examples

The Balsaminaceae family comprises annual and perennial plants with succulent stems.
Impatiens is a well-known genus in the Balsaminaceae family, popular for its colorful flowers.
Botanists easily distinguish Balsaminaceae from similar families due to their unique floral structure.

Etymology

From New Latin, after the type genus Balsamina.
