miscount

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / mɪsˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
to count something incorrectly; to make an error in counting
count incorrectly

Examples

If you miscount the number of guests, there may not be enough food for everyone.
She accidentally miscounted the steps and tripped halfway down the staircase.
Double-check your math so you don't miscount the total amount.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: count - to determine the total number of something

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) to 'count'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00950634-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈmɪsˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
An inaccurate calculation, especially in counting the number of items.
an inaccurate count

Examples

The election was delayed due to a miscount of the ballots.
After realizing his miscount, he started the inventory all over again.
A single miscount can significantly impact the final score.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: error - a mistake or inaccuracy
  • Antonym: correct count - an accurate or correct counting result

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'count' (to number items).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00654962-n
Synset POS: n
