methanal

1. NOUN

/ mɛˈθænæl / (AmE), / ˈmɛθ.ə.næl / (BrE)
a colorless, poisonous gas formed by the oxidation of methanol, commonly used as a disinfectant and preservative
a poisonous chemical gas (formaldehyde)
medical

Examples

Laboratory safety procedures require careful handling of methanal due to its toxic properties.
Industrial production of resins often involves the use of methanal as a reactant.
Exposure to high levels of methanal can irritate the respiratory system and eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formaldehyde - another common name for methanal, a toxic chemical compound

Etymology

Formed from 'methane' + '-al' (ending indicating an aldehyde).
