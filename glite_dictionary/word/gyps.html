Gyps

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪps / (AmE), / dʒɪps / (BrE)
a genus of large Old World vultures in the family Accipitridae, found in Europe, Asia, and Africa
genus of Old World vultures

Examples

The Gyps genus includes several species of large vultures found across Eurasia and Africa.
Scientists have studied Gyps populations to understand vulture declines globally.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect endangered members of the Gyps genus.

Etymology

From Latin *gyps*, from Ancient Greek γύψ (gúps), meaning vulture.

2. NOUN

/ dʒɪps / (AmE), / dʒɪps / (BrE)
A genus of large Old World vultures in the family Accipitridae, found mainly in Europe, Asia, and Africa.
genus of Old World vultures

Examples

The Gyps genus includes several species of vultures that are vital for ecosystem health.
Researchers observed a decline in Gyps populations across South Asia.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting nesting sites of the Gyps vultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulture - a large bird of prey that feeds chiefly on carrion

Etymology

From Latin 'gyps', from Greek 'gyps' (vulture).
