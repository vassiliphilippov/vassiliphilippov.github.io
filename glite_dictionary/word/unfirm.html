unfirm

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɜrm / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɜːm / (BrE)
(of soil or other substances) not stable or solid; loose or liable to shift.
not stable; loose or shifting

Examples

The hikers found the unfirm soil difficult to walk on after the heavy rains.
Construction was delayed because the foundation was laid on unfirm ground.
Sand dunes are often made up of unfirm earth that shifts with the wind.

Synset Examples

shifting sands
unfirm earth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: firm - stable or solid; not likely to move or give way
  • Synonym: unstable - prone to change, fail, or give way; not stable

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' (not) to 'firm'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɝːm / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɜːm / (BrE)
not firmly or solidly positioned; lacking firmness or stability
not firm or stable

Examples

The doctor noticed that his patient stood in an unfirm position after the surgery.
She felt the ground beneath her feet was unfirm and hesitated to continue walking.
The sculpture remained unfirm until it was securely anchored to the base.

Synset Examples

climbing carefully up the unsteady ladder
an unfirm stance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unstable - likely to give way; not stable
  • Antonym: firm - solidly in place; not easily moved

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) + firm.

Source: WordNet
