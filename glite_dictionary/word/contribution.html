contribution

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːntrɪˈbjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrɪˈbjuːʃən / (BrE)
an amount of money given, especially to a charity or fund
an amount of money given

Examples

Her monthly contribution helps support the local food bank.
The museum relies on generous contributions from the community.
He made a significant contribution to the disaster relief fund.

Synset Examples

he expected his contribution to be repaid with interest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donation - something, especially money, that is given to a charity or cause

Etymology

From Latin contributio, from contribuere (to bring together, add).

4. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːntrəˈbjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrɪˈbjuːʃən / (BrE)
an act of giving money, help, ideas, or time, especially together with others, for a common purpose or to help a cause such as a charity
act of giving for a common purpose

Examples

Her generous contribution to the scholarship fund helped several students attend college.
The community made a joint contribution to support families after the hurricane.
Every contribution counts, no matter how small, when you're raising money for charity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donation - something that is given to a charity or organization, especially money
  • Synonym: gift - something voluntarily given to another person or group
  • Antonym: withdrawal - the act of removing or taking something away

Etymology

From Latin 'contributio' (a bringing together), from 'contribuere' (to bring together, add).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01091702-n
Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːntrɪˈbjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrɪˈbjuːʃən / (BrE)
the effort or part played by a person in achieving a result or advancing a project
individual effort toward a result

Examples

She was praised for her significant contribution to the charity's successful fundraising campaign.
Every team member's contribution is essential for the project's overall success.
His contribution in the meeting helped clarify the main issues and led to productive solutions.

Synset Examples

I am proud of my contribution in advancing the project
they all did their share of the work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: input - something that is put in or contributed, often effort or ideas
  • Synonym: effort - physical or mental energy used to achieve something
  • Antonym: hindrance - something that impedes or makes achievement more difficult

Etymology

From Latin 'contributio', from 'contribuere' (to bring together, to contribute).

Source: WordNet
