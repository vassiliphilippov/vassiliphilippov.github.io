4. NOUN

/ aɪ / (AmE), / aɪ / (BrE)
the organ in the head of humans and animals that detects light and enables vision
organ of sight; part of the body that sees

Examples

Her blue eye sparkled in the sunlight.
The doctor examined his eye for signs of infection.
An eagle has very sharp eye sight, allowing it to spot prey from far away.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ear - organ of hearing and balance
  • Antonym: nose - organ used for smelling and breathing

Etymology

From Old English *ēage*, from Proto-Germanic *augō* (eye).
