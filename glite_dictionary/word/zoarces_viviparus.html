Zoarces viviparus

1. NOUN

/ zoʊˈɑːrsiːz vɪˈvɪpərəs / (AmE), / zəʊˈɑːsiːz vɪˈvɪpərəs / (BrE)
a species of eelpout found in northern Europe, notable for giving birth to live young rather than laying eggs
a viviparous northern European eelpout

Examples

The Zoarces viviparus can survive in cold, brackish waters along the European coast.
Scientists studied the reproductive habits of Zoarces viviparus to understand viviparity in fish.
Fishermen occasionally catch Zoarces viviparus when trawling in northern European seas.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Zoarces (genus name) and viviparus (Latin for 'bearing live young').
