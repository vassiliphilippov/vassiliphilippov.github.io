Mississippian

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsɪˈsɪpiən / (AmE), / ˌmɪsɪˈsɪpiən / (BrE)
a native or resident of the US state of Mississippi
native or resident of Mississippi

Examples

Her grandmother is a proud Mississippian who has lived in Jackson all her life.
As a Mississippian, he takes great pride in the state's musical heritage.
Many Mississippians celebrate their local festivals with enthusiasm each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Louisianan - a native or resident of Louisiana
  • Synonym: Mississippi - the US state related to Mississippian

Etymology

From Mississippi, the name of the US state, + -an (forming demonyms).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsɪˈsɪpiən / (AmE), / ˌmɪsɪˈsɪpiən / (BrE)
A geological period from about 345 to 310 million years ago, marked by expansion of land areas and the development of primitive ammonites and winged insects.
geological period, 345–310 million years ago

Examples

Geologists have discovered new fossils from the Mississippian in the Appalachian Mountains.
During the Mississippian, vast swamps covered much of what is now North America.
The Mississippian is known for the appearance of early winged insects and extensive limestone deposits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pennsylvanian - a geological period following the Mississippian
  • Degree Lower: Devonian - a geological period preceding the Mississippian

Etymology

Named after the Mississippi River region in the United States, where rocks from this period were first studied.
