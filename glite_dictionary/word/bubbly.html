bubbly

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʌbli / (AmE), / ˈbʌbli / (BrE)
(of a person) cheerful, lively, and full of high spirits.
cheerful and lively (personality)

Examples

Her bubbly personality made her popular at every party she attended.
He greeted us with a bubbly enthusiasm that was impossible to resist.
The new teacher has a bubbly energy that keeps her students engaged.

Synset Examples

bright bubbly children
a bubbly personality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerful - happy and showing positive feelings
  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Antonym: depressed - very unhappy, lacking enthusiasm

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌbli / (AmE), / ˈbʌbli / (BrE)
A white sparkling wine, typically from the Champagne region, or any wine resembling it.
sparkling wine, especially Champagne
informal

Examples

We opened a bottle of bubbly to celebrate the good news.
She poured herself a glass of bubbly at the party.
They toasted the new year with some delicious bubbly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: champagne - a sparkling white wine from the Champagne region of France
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07909617-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈbʌb.li / (AmE), / ˈbʌb.li / (BrE)
containing or producing many bubbles, especially as a result of carbonation or fermentation
full of bubbles; carbonated or foaming

Examples

The glass of bubbly water fizzed as she poured it over ice.
He prefers his cider bubbly and cold, straight from the bottle.
The surface of the homemade root beer was thick and bubbly after fermentation.

Synset Examples

bubbling champagne
foamy (or frothy) beer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frothy - full of or covered with a mass of small bubbles
  • Antonym: flat - not fizzy or not containing bubbles, especially in drinks
