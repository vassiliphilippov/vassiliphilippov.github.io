butterflyfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚˌflaɪˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəˌflaɪˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small tropical marine fish with a laterally compressed body and bright coloration, noted for its large fanlike pectoral fins; found primarily in coral reefs and unrelated to searobins.
small colorful tropical marine fish

Examples

The vibrant colors of the butterflyfish make it a favorite among aquarium enthusiasts.
While snorkeling in the coral reef, we spotted a school of butterflyfish darting between the rocks.
Unlike the searobin, the butterflyfish uses its fanlike fins for graceful maneuvering through the water.

Etymology

Named for its colorful patterns and delicate appearance resembling a butterfly.
