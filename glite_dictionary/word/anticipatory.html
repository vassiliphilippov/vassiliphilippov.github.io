anticipatory

1. ADJ

/ ænˌtɪsɪˈpeɪtɔri / (AmE), / ænˌtɪsɪˈpeɪtəri / (BrE)
done or felt in anticipation of something; showing that something is expected.
done in anticipation

Examples

The employees felt an anticipatory excitement before the big announcement.
She made an anticipatory gesture, opening the door before his arrival.
There was an anticipatory hush in the crowd as the band prepared to perform.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expectant - having or showing an excited feeling that something is about to happen

Etymology

From Latin anticipatorius, from anticipatus (taken before).
