surrenderer

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɛndərər / (AmE), / səˈrɛndərə / (BrE)
a person who yields or gives up, especially in a conflict or competition
person who yields or gives up

Examples

The army's general was the first surrenderer when defeat became inevitable.
Negotiators spoke to the surrenderer to understand his motives for giving up.
During the ceremony, the surrenderer signed the official documents in front of both parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surrender - to yield, give up, or submit to another
  • Antonym: resister - a person who opposes or resists

Etymology

From 'surrender' + '-er', meaning one who surrenders.
