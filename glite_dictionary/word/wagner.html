Wagner

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑɡnər / (AmE), / ˈvæɡnə / (BrE)
the music composed by Richard Wagner, especially his operas
music of Richard Wagner

Examples

She loves going to the opera, especially when they're performing Wagner.
During the festival, the orchestra played several pieces of Wagner.
His collection includes rare recordings of Wagner conducted by famous maestros.

Synset Examples

they say that Hitler listened only to Wagner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Beethoven - the music of Ludwig van Beethoven
  • Synonym: Mozart - the music of Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart

Etymology

From the name of the German composer Richard Wagner (1813–1883).

2. PROPN

/ ˈvɑːɡnər / (AmE), / ˈvɑːɡnə / (BrE)
(1813–1883) A German composer known for his operas and for creating the concept of the musical drama, integrating music, drama, and spectacle.
German composer of operas (1813–1883)

Examples

Many consider Wagner's operas to be some of the most influential works in Western music.
Her fascination with Wagner led her to attend the annual Bayreuth Festival.
Wagner's Ring Cycle is renowned for its complexity and length.

Etymology

From the German surname Wagner, notably borne by Richard Wagner, the composer.
