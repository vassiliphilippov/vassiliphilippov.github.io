bergenia

1. NOUN

/ bərˈgɛniə / (AmE), / bəˈɡiːniə / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen perennial plants with large leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers, often grown as ground cover in gardens
a genus of evergreen flowering plants

Examples

The hardy bergenia in my garden survived the harsh winter unscathed.
Large clusters of pink flowers bloom on the bergenia every spring.
Gardeners often choose bergenia for its attractive foliage and ability to cover ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saxifrage - a related genus of plants in the Saxifragaceae family

Etymology

Named after Karl August von Bergen (1704–1759), German botanist.
