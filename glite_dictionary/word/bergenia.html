Bergenia

1. NOUN

/ bərˈɡiːniə / (AmE), / bɜːˈɡiːniə / (BrE)
a genus of perennial, spring-blooming rhizomatous herbs with thick evergreen leaves, native to eastern Asia
genus of perennial Asian herbs

Examples

Several Bergenia species are grown in gardens for their ornamental leaves and flowers.
The thick, leathery leaves of Bergenia stay green even in winter.
Bergenia thrives in shady borders and woodland gardens.

Etymology

Named after German botanist Karl August von Bergen (1704–1759).
