Seriola grandis

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈroʊliə ˈɡrændɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪəliə ˈɡrandɪs / (BrE)
a large game fish found in the waters of Australia and New Zealand, known for its size and sporting qualities
large game fish of Australia and New Zealand

Examples

Many anglers travel to New Zealand hoping to catch a Seriola grandis during the summer season.
The Seriola grandis is prized for its fighting ability and impressive size.
Researchers are studying the population dynamics of Seriola grandis in Australian waters.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Seriola' and species epithet 'grandis', meaning 'large'.
