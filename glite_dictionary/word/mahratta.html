Mahratta

1. NOUN

/ məˈrɑːtə / (AmE), / məˈrætə / (BrE)
a member of an ethnic group from Maharashtra, India, historically known for their warrior and ruling traditions
member of an Indian ethnic group (Maharashtra)

Examples

The Mahratta rulers played a significant role in Indian history.
Several Mahratta families still maintain traditional customs in Maharashtra.
He traced his ancestry to a prominent Mahratta clan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maratha - an alternative spelling; member of the same group

Etymology

From Hindi Marathi (मऱ्हाटा maṟhāṭā), referring to the Marathi-speaking people of Maharashtra.
