monograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A detailed written study or treatise on a single specialized subject, often by a single author.
detailed study of a single subject

Examples

The scientist published a monograph on climate change impacts in the Arctic.
Her groundbreaking monograph received several awards in the field of art history.
This monograph provides an in-depth analysis of Renaissance architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treatise - a written work dealing formally and systematically with a subject

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'graphein' (to write).
