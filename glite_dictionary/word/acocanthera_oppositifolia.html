Acocanthera oppositifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌækoʊˈkænθərə əˌpɑːzɪtəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌakəʊˈkanθərə əˌpɒzɪtɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub or small tree native to South Africa, known for its glossy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers
South African evergreen shrub or tree

Examples

The Acocanthera oppositifolia is often planted in gardens for its attractive flowers and foliage.
Some parts of the Acocanthera oppositifolia are toxic and should be handled with care.
In South Africa, the Acocanthera oppositifolia is sometimes used for traditional medicinal purposes.

Etymology

From genus name Acocanthera and Latin oppositifolia (with opposite leaves).
