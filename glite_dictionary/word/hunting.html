hunting

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhʌntɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of pursuing and killing or capturing wild animals, especially for food or their pelts
pursuit and killing of wild animals

Examples

Many people in rural areas rely on hunting to provide food for their families.
Laws regulate hunting to protect endangered species from extinction.
The ancient cave paintings depict scenes of early humans engaged in hunting.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: poaching - illegal hunting or capturing of wild animals

Etymology

From Old English huntian (to chase, capture wild animals).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhʌn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of searching carefully in order to find something or someone
careful search for something

Examples

After hours of hunting through old boxes, she finally found the missing photograph.
The detectives' hunting for clues led them to the abandoned warehouse.
We spent the afternoon hunting for the perfect birthday gift in the crowded mall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: searching - the act of trying to find something by looking carefully
  • Antonym: hiding - concealing oneself or something to avoid being found

Etymology

From Old English 'hunting', derived from 'huntian' (to hunt, chase).

3. NOUN

/ ˈhʌn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhʌn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of chasing and killing or capturing wild animals, especially as a sport
pursuit and killing of animals for sport

Examples

The group went hunting in the forest early in the morning.
He has a collection of rifles used only for hunting.
Many conservationists argue that regulated hunting helps control animal populations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: poaching - illegal hunting or capturing of wild animals

Etymology

From Old English 'hunting', related to 'hunt' (to chase game).
