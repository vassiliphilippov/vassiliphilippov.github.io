Cicadellidae

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌkædəˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / sɪˌkadəˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of small plant-feeding insects, commonly known as leafhoppers, characterized by their jumping ability and wedge-shaped bodies.
family of insects called leafhoppers

Examples

The Cicadellidae are known for their ability to jump long distances between plants.
Many species within the Cicadellidae family can cause damage to crops by spreading plant diseases.
Entomologists presented new research on the diversity of Cicadellidae in South American forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leafhopper - a small, jumping insect that feeds on plants

Etymology

From New Latin 'Cicadellidae', formed from 'Cicadella' (a genus name) + the family suffix '-idae'.
