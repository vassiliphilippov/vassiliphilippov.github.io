lawman

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈlɔːˌman / (BrE)
a male officer who works to enforce the law, especially in the context of the American West or law enforcement history
male officer of the law

Examples

The legendary lawman was known for his bravery and sense of justice.
During the Old West, it was risky for a lawman to confront dangerous outlaws alone.
The new sheriff is a seasoned lawman with years of city experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: law enforcement officer - person whose job is to uphold and enforce the law
  • Antonym: outlaw - a person who has broken the law and is hiding or on the run

Etymology

Formed from 'law' + 'man', patterned on similar compounds like 'salesman'.
