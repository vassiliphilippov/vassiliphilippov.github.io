Marattia

1. NOUN

/ məˈrætiə / (AmE), / məˈrætiə / (BrE)
A genus of ferns in the family Marattiaceae, characterized by having sporangia fused together in two rows.
genus of ferns in Marattiaceae

Examples

The Marattia genus is notable for its large, fleshy rhizomes and tropical distribution.
Botanists studied several species within Marattia found in South American rainforests.
Some fossils show that Marattia dates back to the Carboniferous period.

Etymology

Named after Italian botanist Giovanni Francesco Maratti (1723–1790).
