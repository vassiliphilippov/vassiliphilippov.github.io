Pluteus cervinus

1. NOUN

/ pluːˈtiːəs sərˈvaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈpluːtiːəs səˈvɪnəs / (BrE)
A small edible mushroom with a slender stalk, usually found growing on rotting hardwoods; commonly known as the deer mushroom.
a small edible mushroom found on hardwoods

Examples

The Pluteus cervinus grows abundantly in damp forests during the autumn months.
Foragers value the Pluteus cervinus for its agreeable taste and edibility.
She photographed a cluster of Pluteus cervinus on a decaying log in the woods.

Etymology

From Latin 'Pluteus' (a genus of mushrooms) and 'cervinus' (of a deer), referring to the deer-like color of the cap.
