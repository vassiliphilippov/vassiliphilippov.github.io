Lagopus scoticus

1. NOUN

/ ləˈɡoʊpəs skoʊˈtaɪkəs / (AmE), / ləˈɡəʊpəs skɒˈtɪkəs / (BrE)
a reddish-brown grouse found in the upland moors of Great Britain, commonly known as the red grouse
red grouse of Great Britain

Examples

The Lagopus scoticus inhabits the heather-covered moorlands of Scotland.
Scientists have observed a decline in Lagopus scoticus populations in recent years.
Photographers often travel to the Highlands to capture images of the elusive Lagopus scoticus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red grouse - common name for Lagopus scoticus
  • Degree Higher: grouse - any of various plump, chickenlike game birds

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'lagōpous' (hare-footed) and Latin 'Scoticus' (Scottish).
