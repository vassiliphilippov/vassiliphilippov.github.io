barbarization

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːrbərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbɑːbəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or act of making people or a society primitive, uncivilized, or less culturally advanced
process of making uncivilized

Examples

The barbarization of society alarmed historians studying the decline of the empire.
Many fear that prolonged war will lead to the barbarization of once peaceful communities.
His writing warns against the barbarization of culture through ignorance and violence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: civilization - the stage of human social development and organization which is considered most advanced
  • Synonym: barbarism - absence of culture and civilization; extreme cruelty or brutality

Etymology

Formed from 'barbarian' + '-ization', patterned after Latin and Greek roots for 'barbarian'.
