patrilineal

1. ADJ

/ ˌpætrəˈlɪniəl / (AmE), / ˌpætrɪˈlɪniəl / (BrE)
Relating to or based on descent or inheritance through the male line.
tracing descent through the male line

Examples

Many traditional societies are organized along patrilineal lines, with inheritance passing from father to son.
In some cultures, only men are recognized in the patrilineal line of ancestry.
The country's royal family follows a strict patrilineal succession system.

Synset Examples

a patrilineal society

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matrilineal - tracing descent through the female line

Etymology

From Latin 'pater' (father) + 'linea' (line).
