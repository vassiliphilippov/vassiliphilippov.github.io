Giacomo Puccini

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒɑːˈkoʊmoʊ puˈtʃiːni / (AmE), / ˌdʒɑːˈkəʊməʊ pʊˈtʃiːni / (BrE)
(1858–1924) An Italian composer renowned for his operas, noted for their dramatic realism and lyrical melodies.
Italian composer of operas

Examples

Giacomo Puccini is celebrated for composing some of the most beloved operas in history, such as 'La Bohème' and 'Tosca'.
Many music enthusiasts regard Giacomo Puccini as a master of the verismo style in opera.
The works of Giacomo Puccini are regularly performed by major opera companies around the world.
