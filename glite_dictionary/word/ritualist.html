ritualist

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃuəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃuəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who is expert in or specializes in rites and ceremonies, especially in religion or anthropology
person expert in rites and ceremonies

Examples

As a ritualist, she studied the sacred rites of multiple ancient civilizations.
The museum hired a ritualist to curate the new exhibition on religious ceremonies.
He became a renowned ritualist after years of fieldwork among indigenous tribes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceremonialist - a person who is skilled in or adheres strictly to ceremonies or rites

Etymology

From 'ritual' + '-ist' (denoting a person who practices or is concerned with something).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃuəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃʊəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who advocates or practices strict observance of established rituals, especially in religion or ceremony
person advocating strict observance of rituals

Examples

As a devoted ritualist, she insisted on following every detail of the ceremony.
Some members of the church accused the new priest of being too much of a ritualist.
His ritualist approach made the meetings long but highly organized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formalist - a person who places excessive emphasis on formality or established forms
  • Antonym: iconoclast - a person who attacks or rejects cherished beliefs or institutions, particularly established rituals

Etymology

From 'ritual' + '-ist', denoting someone who adheres to or specializes in rituals.
