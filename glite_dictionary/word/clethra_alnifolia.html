Clethra alnifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkliːθrə ælˈnɪfəliə / (AmE), / ˈkliːθrə ælˈnɪfəliə / (BrE)
A shrub native to eastern and southern coastal regions of the United States, known for its clusters of fragrant white flowers.
ornamental shrub with fragrant white flowers

Examples

Gardeners often plant Clethra alnifolia for its sweet-smelling summer blossoms.
The Clethra alnifolia thrives in moist, acidic soils along the Atlantic seaboard.
In July, the Clethra alnifolia attracts many butterflies to its white flower spikes.

Etymology

From genus name Clethra and Latin alnifolia, meaning 'with leaves like an alder'.
