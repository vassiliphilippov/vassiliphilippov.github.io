tower

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtaʊ.ə / (BrE)
a thing resembling a tall, narrow structure, especially one rising high above its surroundings
something tall and narrow in shape

Examples

A tower of books stacked on his desk nearly toppled over.
As the volcano erupted, a tower of ash reached into the sky.
She built a tower of colorful blocks during playtime.

Synset Examples

the test tube held a column of white powder
a tower of dust rose above the horizon
a thin pillar of smoke betrayed their campsite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: column - a tall, cylindrical structure
  • Synonym: pillar - an upright, typically tall structure serving as support or decoration

Etymology

From Old French 'tour', from Latin 'turris' (tower).

2. VERB

/ ˈtaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtaʊ.ə / (BrE)
to appear very large or to occupy a commanding or dominant position.
appear dominant or very large

Examples

The new skyscraper towers over all the other buildings in the city center.
During the presentation, her confidence towered above that of her colleagues.
Mount Everest towers impressively above the surrounding peaks.

Synset Examples

The huge sculpture predominates over the fountain
Large shadows loomed on the canyon wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to predominate - to be the strongest or main element
  • Synonym: dwarf - to make something seem small by comparison
  • Antonym: shrink - to become or make smaller or less significant

Etymology

From Old English *tor* (high building) via Old French *tur*, from Latin *turris* (tower).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02702345-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtaʊ.ə / (BrE)
A tall structure, often much higher than its width, that can stand alone or be part of a larger building.
tall, narrow building or structure

Examples

The church had a magnificent stone tower that could be seen for miles around.
We climbed the observation tower to enjoy a panoramic view of the city.
A medieval tower still stands at the edge of the old fortress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skyscraper - a very tall, modern building
  • Degree Higher: spire - a tapering conical or pyramidal structure on the top of a building
  • Degree Lower: hut - a small, simple shelter or dwelling

Etymology

From Old French 'tour', from Latin 'turris' (tower).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04467365-n
Synset POS: n
