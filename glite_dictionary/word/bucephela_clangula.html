Bucephela clangula

1. NOUN

/ bjuːˌsɛfələ ˈklæŋɡjələ / (AmE), / bjuːˌsɛfɪlə ˈklæŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
a large-headed, swift-flying diving duck native to Arctic regions; also known as the common goldeneye
species of Arctic diving duck (common goldeneye)

Examples

The Bucephela clangula is easily recognized by its striking yellow eyes and distinctive head shape.
During their migration, flocks of Bucephela clangula can be seen diving for food in northern lakes.
Ornithologists studied the breeding habits of Bucephela clangula in remote Arctic environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common goldeneye - common name for Bucephela clangula, a diving duck of the northern hemisphere

Etymology

From New Latin, 'Bucephala' meaning 'bull-headed' (Greek 'bous' bull, 'kephalē' head), and Latin 'clangula' meaning 'small noisy bird'.
