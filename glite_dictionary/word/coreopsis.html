coreopsis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːriˈɑːpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒːriˈɒpsɪs / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants native to North and South America, known for their showy, usually yellow, daisy-like flowers
a genus of daisy-like flowering plants

Examples

Many gardeners choose coreopsis for their vibrant yellow flowers and long blooming season.
The meadow was dotted with patches of coreopsis among the tall grasses.
She planted several coreopsis to attract butterflies to her backyard.

Etymology

From Greek 'koris' (bug) and 'opsis' (appearance), referring to the shape of the seeds.
