conjoined

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdʒɔɪnd / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɔɪnd / (BrE)
(of two or more things) joined together; united or combined.
joined together; united

Examples

The conjoined companies now operate as a single international firm.
In the museum, a sculpture showed two conjoined figures sharing a single torso.
Their conjoined efforts resulted in a successful community festival.

Synset Examples

the interplay of these conjoined yet opposed factors
social order and prosperity, the conjoint aims of government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connected - joined or linked together
  • Antonym: separate - not joined or connected; distinct

Etymology

From Latin 'coniunctus,' past participle of 'coniungere' (to join together).
