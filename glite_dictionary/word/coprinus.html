Coprinus

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈpraɪnəs / (AmE), / kɒˈprɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of mushrooms in which the cap typically dissolves into an inky fluid when mature; known as inky caps.
genus of mushrooms dissolving into ink-like fluid
medical

Examples

The mushroom she found in the garden belonged to the genus Coprinus.
Many species in Coprinus are called 'inky caps' due to their dissolving caps.
Mycologists often study Coprinus to understand spore dispersal mechanisms.

Etymology

From New Latin, adopted from Greek 'koprinos' (dung), referring to the habitat of certain species.

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈpraɪnəs / (AmE), / kɒˈprɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of mushrooms in which the caps often break down into an inky fluid at maturity, sometimes classified in its own family, Coprinaceae.
genus of mushrooms with inky caps

Examples

The distinctive feature of the Coprinus genus is that many species develop inky caps as they mature.
Some Coprinus mushrooms are commonly called inkcaps because of their unusual way of decomposing.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of various Coprinus species in temperate forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agaric - a mushroom with gills under the cap

Etymology

From Latin 'coprinus' (of or pertaining to dung), from Greek 'koprinos' (dung), referring to the habitat of some species.
