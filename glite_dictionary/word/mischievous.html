mischievous

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪstʃəvəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪs.tʃɪ.vəs / (BrE)
Intended to cause harm or trouble.
causing harm or trouble

Examples

The article spread mischievous lies that damaged several reputations.
He was accused of making mischievous comments intended to provoke conflict.
Mischievous actions by competitors disrupted the project.

Synset Examples

mischievous rumors and falsehoods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malicious - Intending or intended to do harm
  • Synonym: harmful - Causing or likely to cause harm

Etymology

From Old French 'meschevous' (unfortunate, unhappy), from 'meschever' (to end badly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01166177-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɪstʃɪvəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪs.tʃɪ.vəs / (BrE)
Showing a fondness for causing trouble in a playful or harmless way; naughtily or annoyingly playful.
naughtily playful; causing harmless trouble

Examples

The child had a mischievous grin after hiding his friend's pencil.
Her mischievous comment made everyone at the table laugh.
A mischievous breeze kept blowing the papers off my desk.

Synset Examples

teasing and worrying with impish laughter
a wicked prank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: playful - full of fun and high spirits
  • Synonym: impish - showing mischievousness or a fondness for pranks
  • Antonym: well-behaved - acting in a proper, disciplined manner

Etymology

From Middle English 'mischiefous', from 'mischief' (harm, injury) + '-ous'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02130013-s
Synset POS: s
