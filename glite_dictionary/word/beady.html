beady

1. ADJ

/ ˈbiːdi / (AmE), / ˈbiːdi / (BrE)
decorated or covered with beads, jewels, or sequins
covered with beads or jewels

Examples

Her dress was beady, sparkling under the lights as she danced.
The costume featured a beady pattern along the sleeves and collar.
He admired the beady ornaments that adorned the fabric.

Etymology

From 'bead' + '-y', meaning 'having the qualities of beads'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbiːdi / (AmE), / ˈbiːdi / (BrE)
(of eyes or objects) small, round, and shining, especially suggesting watchfulness or intensity.
small, shiny, and round (esp. eyes)

Examples

She noticed his beady eyes watching her from the shadows.
The crow cocked its head, its beady gaze unblinking and intense.
Her beady little eyes darted around the room, missing nothing.

Synset Examples

bright beady eyes
black buttony eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttony - like a button; small and round

Etymology

From 'bead' + '-y', meaning having the qualities of a bead.
