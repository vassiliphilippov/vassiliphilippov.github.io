kylie

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪli / (AmE), / ˈkaɪli / (BrE)
An Australian boomerang, typically with one side flat and the other convex, used traditionally as a hunting weapon.
Australian boomerang with flat and convex sides

Examples

The museum displayed a rare kylie used by Indigenous Australians for hunting.
A skillful throw of the kylie sent it spinning through the air toward its target.
Unlike the typical returning boomerang, the kylie was mainly designed for long-distance flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boomerang - A curved, flat piece of wood that can be thrown to return to the thrower

Etymology

From the Nyungar word 'kaili', referring to a curved throwing stick.
