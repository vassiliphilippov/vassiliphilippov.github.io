Na-Dene

1. NOUN

/ nɑː dəˈneɪ / (AmE), / nɑː dəˈneɪ / (BrE)
A family of North American indigenous languages spoken chiefly in Alaska, western Canada, and the southwestern United States.
North American indigenous language family

Examples

Linguists have debated the origins of the Na-Dene language family for decades.
Athabaskan is one of the best-documented branches of the Na-Dene languages.
The Na-Dene peoples have rich oral traditions and histories.

Etymology

From Navajo and Tlingit words for 'people' (Na) and 'people' (Dene). Coined in linguistics in the early 20th century.
