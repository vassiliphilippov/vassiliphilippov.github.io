to enhance

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈhæns / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɑːns / (BrE)
to increase or improve the quality, value, or extent of something
increase or improve something

Examples

The new lighting will enhance the atmosphere in the restaurant.
These vitamins are believed to enhance memory and concentration.
Using spices can enhance the flavors of a simple dish.

Synset Examples

This will enhance your enjoyment
heighten the tension

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improve - make or become better
  • Antonym: to diminish - make or become less

Etymology

From Old French 'enhauncer', from Latin 'inaltare' (to raise, make high).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00230031-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈhæns / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɑːns / (BrE)
to improve the quality, value, or attractiveness of something
improve or make more attractive

Examples

A fresh coat of paint can enhance the appearance of your home.
The new features are designed to enhance the user experience.
Adding spices will enhance the flavor of the soup.

Synset Examples

This sauce will enhance the flavor of the meat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improve - to make something better
  • Antonym: to diminish - to make something seem less important or attractive

Etymology

From Old French 'enhauncer' (to raise, make higher), from Late Latin 'inaltare'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00230459-v
Synset POS: v
