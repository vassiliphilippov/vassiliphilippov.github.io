Pentecost

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.tɪˌkɑst / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.tɪˌkɒst / (BrE)
The seventh Sunday after Easter, commemorating the descent of the Holy Spirit upon the Apostles; a major Christian festival.
Christian festival seventh Sunday after Easter

Examples

Many churches hold special services on Pentecost to celebrate the coming of the Holy Spirit.
The feast of Pentecost marks an important moment in the Christian liturgical calendar.
In Scotland, Pentecost was traditionally observed as a quarter day for settling rents.

Etymology

From Greek *Pentēkostē* (fiftieth), referring to the fiftieth day after Easter.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.tɪˌkɑst / (AmE), / ˈpɛntɪkɒst / (BrE)
In Judaism, a holy day observed on the sixth of Sivan, commemorating the giving of the Ten Commandments to Moses on Mount Sinai.
Jewish holy day marking the giving of the Ten Commandments

Examples

Many Jewish communities gather to read scripture and pray on Pentecost.
The traditions of Pentecost include the reading of the Ten Commandments in synagogue.
On Pentecost, families often decorate their homes with flowers and greenery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shavuot - Jewish festival also known as Pentecost, celebrating the giving of the Torah

Etymology

From Greek *pentēkostē* (fiftieth), referring to the fiftieth day after Passover.
