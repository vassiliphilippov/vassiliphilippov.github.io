Phacelia campanularia

1. NOUN

/ fəˈsiːliə kæmˌpænjʊˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / fəˈsiːliə kam.pa.njʊˈlɛː.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
An annual plant native to southern California, known for its intricately branched stems and clusters of deep blue, aromatic, bell-shaped flowers.
annual plant with deep blue bell-shaped flowers

Examples

Phacelia campanularia is a popular choice in wildflower gardens for its vibrant blue flowers.
Botanists note that Phacelia campanularia thrives in sandy, well-drained soils.
During the spring, the hillsides in southern California are covered with blooming Phacelia campanularia.

Etymology

From modern Latin: 'Phacelia', the genus, and 'campanularia', from Latin 'campanula' meaning 'little bell', referring to the flower shape.
