to inaugurate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈnɔɡjəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɔːɡjʊreɪt / (BrE)
to mark the beginning of a major change or event; to introduce something new or to be the precursor of something significant
mark the beginning; usher in

Examples

The discovery of penicillin inaugurated a new era in the treatment of infections.
His election inaugurated a period of rapid social change.
The invention of the steam engine inaugurated the Industrial Revolution.

Synset Examples

The fall of the Berlin Wall ushered in the post-Cold War period

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to usher in - to signal or mark the beginning of something new

Etymology

From Latin 'inaugurare' (to install under good omens, to consecrate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00350246-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪˈnɔɡjəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɔːɡjʊreɪt / (BrE)
to mark the official opening or beginning of something, especially with a formal ceremony
formally begin or open something

Examples

The mayor will inaugurate the new community center next week with a ribbon-cutting ceremony.
The art museum was inaugurated in 2020 with a gala attended by local dignitaries.
They plan to inaugurate a new annual festival to celebrate the town's heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open - to start or make accessible for use
  • Antonym: close - to bring something to an end, especially officially or formally

Etymology

From Latin inauguratus, past participle of inaugurare (to consecrate, install).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02430184-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪˈnɔɡjəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɔːɡjʊˌreɪt / (BrE)
to formally begin or introduce something, especially with an official ceremony
formally begin or introduce

Examples

The mayor will inaugurate the new community center next week.
The festival was inaugurated with a grand fireworks display.
They plan to inaugurate an annual charity run for local schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commence - to begin or start something
  • Antonym: to terminate - to bring to an end or finish

Etymology

From Latin *inaugurare* (to consecrate, install under good omens).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02400924-v
Synset POS: v
