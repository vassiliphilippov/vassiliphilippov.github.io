bastnasite

1. NOUN

/ bæsˈnæsaɪt / (AmE), / bæsˈnɑːsaɪt / (BrE)
a yellow to brown mineral consisting of a rare earth carbonate, which is an important source of rare earth elements
yellow-brown rare earth mineral

Examples

The mine was rich in bastnasite, a crucial mineral for rare earth extraction.
Geologists identified bastnasite crystals within the rock samples from the new deposit.
Many industrial applications depend on bastnasite as a primary source of rare earth elements.

Etymology

Named after Bastnäs, a locality in Sweden where it was first discovered, plus mineral suffix -ite.
