catastrophically

1. ADV

/ kəˌtæstrəˈfɪkəli / (AmE), / kəˌtæstrəˈfɪkəli / (BrE)
In a way that causes or involves great damage, suffering, or failure.
in a disastrous manner

Examples

The building collapsed catastrophically, leaving nothing but rubble behind.
If the experiment fails catastrophically, we may need to start over from scratch.
Their plan went catastrophically wrong due to unforeseen circumstances.

Synset Examples

catastrophically complex

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disastrously - in a way that causes great harm or damage

Etymology

From 'catastrophic' + suffix '-ally'. 'Catastrophic' from Greek 'katastrophē' (overturning, disaster).
