Komi

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.mi / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.mi / (BrE)
A member of an indigenous Finno-Ugric people living in the northwestern Urals region of Russia.
Finno-Ugric ethnic group of Russia

Examples

The Komi have preserved many of their traditional customs despite outside influences.
Her grandfather was a proud Komi who taught her the ancient language.
Anthropologists have studied the unique folklore of the Komi people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.mi / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.mi / (BrE)
the Finnic language spoken by the Komi people in north-eastern European Russia
a Finnic language of north-eastern Russia

Examples

She is studying Komi as part of her research on Uralic languages.
The Komi language has several dialects spoken throughout the region.
Few people outside Russia are fluent in Komi.

Etymology

From Russian Коми (Komi), the name of the ethnic group and their language.
