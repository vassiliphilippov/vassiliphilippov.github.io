Linaria

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / lɪˈnɑːriə / (BrE)
a genus of herbs and subshrubs in the family Plantaginaceae, known for their showy, spurred flowers resembling snapdragons.
genus of flowering herbs and subshrubs

Examples

Several species of Linaria are popular as ornamental garden plants.
The bright yellow blooms of Linaria attracted many bees in the meadow.
Botanists have reclassified some plants formerly included in the genus Linaria.

Etymology

From Latin 'linaria', meaning 'pertaining to flax', referring to the similarity of its leaves to those of flax.

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / lɪˈneəriə / (BrE)
a genus of herbs and subshrubs, often called toadflax, in the plantain family, known for their showy, spurred flowers resembling snapdragons.
genus of herbs with spurred, snapdragon-like flowers

Examples

The bright yellow flowers of Linaria brighten meadows in early summer.
Gardeners often plant Linaria species for their delicate, snapdragon-like blooms.
Some wild varieties of Linaria are considered invasive in certain regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toadflax - common name for species in the Linaria genus

Etymology

From Latin 'linaria', meaning 'pertaining to flax', as some species resemble flax (Linum).
