Rioja

1. NOUN

/ riˈoʊhə / (AmE), / riˈɒhə / (BrE)
A dry red wine from the Rioja region of northern Spain, known for its distinctive flavour and quality.
Spanish red wine from Rioja region

Examples

The restaurant offers an excellent selection of Rioja to pair with grilled meats.
She poured herself a glass of Rioja while preparing dinner.
Many wine enthusiasts praise the aging potential of Rioja wines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tempranillo - Spanish red grape variety often used in Rioja

Etymology

From Spanish Rioja, the name of the wine-producing region in northern Spain.
