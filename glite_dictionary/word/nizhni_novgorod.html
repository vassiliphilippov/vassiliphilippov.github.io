Nizhni Novgorod

1. PROPN

/ ˌniːʒni nəvˈgɔːrd / (AmE), / ˌniːʒni nɒvˈɡɒrəd / (BrE)
an industrial city in western Russia on the Volga River, known for its historical significance and industry; birthplace of Maksim Gorky
industrial city in western Russia

Examples

Many tourists visit Nizhni Novgorod to see its historic Kremlin and beautiful river views.
She was born in Nizhni Novgorod, one of Russia's oldest and most important industrial cities.
The company is opening a new office in Nizhni Novgorod to expand its business in Russia.

Etymology

From Russian Нижний Новгород (Nizhniy Novgorod), meaning 'Lower Newtown'.
