Ophrys sphegodes

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːfrɪs ˈsfɛɡəʊdiːz / (AmE), / ˈɒfrɪs ˈsfɛɡəʊdiːz / (BrE)
a spring-blooming species of spider orchid with yellow, green, or pink sepals and a broad brown velvety lip, native to Europe and western Asia
a species of spider orchid

Examples

The Ophrys sphegodes can be found growing wild in chalk grasslands across southern England.
Many photographers travel each spring to capture the delicate beauty of Ophrys sphegodes in bloom.
Botanists note that the lip of Ophrys sphegodes mimics the appearance of a spider to attract pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: early spider orchid - common name for Ophrys sphegodes

Etymology

From Latin genus name Ophrys and species epithet sphegodes, meaning 'wasp-like', from Greek sphegodes (like a wasp).
