defensively

1. ADV

/ dɪˈfɛnsɪvli / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛnsɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that is intended to defend or protect against attack or criticism.
in a defensive manner

Examples

She reacted defensively to the criticism, refusing to admit any mistakes.
The team played defensively in the second half to protect their lead.
He answered the questions defensively, as if he felt under attack.

Synset Examples

the general conviction that our side is in the right and acting defensively over what Russians call the German question and Americans the Berlin crisis

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aggressively - in a manner showing readiness to attack or confront

Etymology

Formed from 'defensive' + '-ly', where 'defensive' comes from Old French 'defensif' and Latin 'defensivus'.

2. ADV

/ dɪˈfɛnsɪvli / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛnsɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner intended to defend oneself from criticism, attack, or blame; showing a defensive attitude.
in a defensive or self-protective way

Examples

He reacted defensively when questioned about his decision.
She answered defensively, as if expecting criticism.
The team played defensively throughout the second half.

Synset Examples

‘I felt it better you should know,’ said Sir Cedric defensively

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aggressively - in a manner showing aggression or forcefulness

Etymology

From 'defensive' + '-ly'. 'Defensive' from Latin 'defensivus', from 'defendere' (to ward off).
