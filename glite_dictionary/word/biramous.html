biramous

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈreɪməs / (AmE), / bɪˈreɪməs / (BrE)
(especially of the limbs or appendages of some animals) divided into two branches or forks.
having two branches; forked

Examples

The crustacean's biramous antennae help it sense its environment underwater.
Unlike uniramous appendages, biramous limbs are split into two distinct branches.
Scientists identified the fossil by its distinctive biramous legs.

Synset Examples

the biramous appendages of an arthropod
long branched hairs on its legs on which pollen collects
a forked river
a forked tail
forked lightning
horseradish grown in poor soil may develop prongy roots

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uniramous - having a single branch or limb (esp. in zoology)

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'ramus' (branch).
