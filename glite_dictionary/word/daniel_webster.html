Daniel Webster

1. PROPN

/ ˈdænjəl ˈwɛbstər / (AmE), / ˈdænjəl ˈwɛbstə / (BrE)
(1782–1852) A United States statesman and orator known for his influential speeches and leadership in American politics.
American politician and orator

Examples

Daniel Webster delivered some of the most powerful speeches in the history of the U.S. Senate.
Many historians regard Daniel Webster as one of America's finest orators.
The writings and debates of Daniel Webster continue to be studied in political science courses.
