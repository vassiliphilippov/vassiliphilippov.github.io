Salix fragilis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælɪks frəˈdʒɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈsælɪks frəˈdʒɪlɪs / (BrE)
A large species of willow tree, also known as the crack willow, with stiff branches that are easily broken.
a large willow tree species (crack willow)

Examples

The Salix fragilis can be easily recognized by its brittle branches that snap off in the wind.
Ecologists often plant Salix fragilis along riverbanks to prevent soil erosion.
Many birds use the branches of Salix fragilis to build their nests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crack willow - common name for Salix fragilis

Etymology

From Latin: 'salix' meaning willow, 'fragilis' meaning easily broken.
