daggerboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæɡərˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈdæɡəbɔːd / (BrE)
A removable, vertically sliding board on a small sailboat that can be lowered into the water to act as a keel and improve stability and directional control.
removable keel board on a small sailboat

Examples

The sailor lowered the daggerboard before heading out into deeper waters.
After hitting a sandbar, she checked to make sure the daggerboard wasn't damaged.
Some racing dinghies use a daggerboard instead of a fixed keel for better performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centerboard - a board lowered from the center of a boat to act as a keel

Etymology

From 'dagger' (referring to its thin, blade-like shape) + 'board'.
