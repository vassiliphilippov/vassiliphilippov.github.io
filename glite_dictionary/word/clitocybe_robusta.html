Clitocybe robusta

1. NOUN

/ klaɪˈtɒsɪˌbi roʊˈbʌstə / (AmE), / klaɪˈtɒsɪˌbi rəʊˈbʌstə / (BrE)
a large, white species of agaric mushroom that is edible but not particularly palatable.
large, edible white mushroom species

Examples

The field guide noted that the Clitocybe robusta is technically edible but not highly regarded for its flavor.
While hiking, we discovered a patch of Clitocybe robusta growing in the shade of the old trees.
Mycologists often identify Clitocybe robusta by its robust size and distinctive white cap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agaric - a type of mushroom with gills under the cap

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Clitocybe' from Greek 'klitos' (slope) and 'kybe' (head), denoting the mushroom's shape, and 'robusta' from Latin, meaning 'strong' or 'robust'.
