Skaw

1. PROPN

/ skɔ / (AmE), / skɔː / (BrE)
a cape on the northernmost tip of Jutland, Denmark, lying between the Skagerrak and the Kattegat seas
cape at the northern tip of Jutland, Denmark

Examples

Tourists often travel to the Skaw to see where the two seas meet.
The lighthouse at Skaw is a popular attraction for visitors to northern Denmark.
Ships sailing around Skaw must navigate challenging waters between the Skagerrak and Kattegat.

Etymology

From Danish 'Skagen', the name of the locality at the cape.
