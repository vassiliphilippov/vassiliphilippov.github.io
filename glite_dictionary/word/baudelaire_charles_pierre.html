Baudelaire, Charles Pierre

1. PROPN

/ boʊˌdəˈlɛr, ʃɑrl pjɛr / (AmE), / ˌbəʊdəˈlɛə, ʃɑːl pjɛə / (BrE)
(1821–1867) A French poet renowned for his macabre imagery, evocative language, and influence on modern poetry.
famous French poet (1821–1867)

Examples

Many consider Baudelaire the father of modern poetry due to his influential works.
The poems of Baudelaire are celebrated for their striking imagery and emotional depth.
In university, we studied Baudelaire’s collection, 'Les Fleurs du mal', in great detail.

Etymology

French surname; Charles Pierre Baudelaire (1821–1867).
