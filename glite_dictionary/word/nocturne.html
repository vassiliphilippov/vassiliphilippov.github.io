nocturne

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑk.tɝn / (AmE), / ˈnɒk.tɜːn / (BrE)
a short, lyrical musical composition, usually for piano, evocative of night or having a dreamy character
a lyrical night-inspired piano piece

Examples

Chopin's nocturne in E-flat major is a favorite among pianists.
The recital opened with a beautifully played nocturne that captivated the audience.
She composed a nocturne inspired by the quiet beauty of the evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etude - a musical composition designed to provide practice material for perfecting a particular technical skill

Etymology

From French 'nocturne', from Latin 'nocturnus' (of the night).
