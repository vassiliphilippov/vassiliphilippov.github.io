cowgirl

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌɡɝl / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌɡɜːl / (BrE)
a woman who works on a ranch, especially one who tends cattle and performs the traditional duties of a cowboy
female ranch worker; woman cowboy

Examples

As a cowgirl, she could ride, rope, and wrangle cattle as well as any man.
The young cowgirl won several awards at the annual rodeo for her impressive skills.
Dressed in boots and a hat, the cowgirl led the herd across the open plains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowboy - a man who herds and tends cattle, typically on horseback
  • Synonym: rancher - a person who owns or works on a ranch

Etymology

Formed by combining 'cow' and 'girl', after 'cowboy' (mid-19th century, US).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌɡɝl / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌɡɜːl / (BrE)
A woman who performs skilled horseback riding, roping, and other rodeo activities, often as an entertainer or competitor.
female rodeo performer

Examples

The talented cowgirl wowed the crowd with her impressive roping tricks at the rodeo.
She grew up on a ranch and dreamed of becoming a professional cowgirl.
Dressed in boots and a hat, the cowgirl took first place in the barrel racing competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowboy - a man who herds and tends cattle, especially in the western US, or a male rodeo performer

Etymology

Formed from "cow" (as in "cowboy") + "girl", modeled after "cowboy".
