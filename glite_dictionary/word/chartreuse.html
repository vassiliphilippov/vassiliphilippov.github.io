chartreuse

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑrˈtruːz / (AmE), / ʃɑːˈtrɜːz / (BrE)
an aromatic green or yellow liqueur, flavored with herbs such as orange peel, hyssop, and peppermint, originally made by Carthusian monks near Grenoble, France
herbal liqueur from France

Examples

He offered me a glass of chartreuse after dinner.
The distinctive green color of chartreuse comes from the blend of herbal extracts.
Many cocktail recipes call for yellow chartreuse to add a unique flavor.

Etymology

From French 'Chartreuse', the name of the Carthusian monastery near Grenoble where it was first made.

2. ADJ

/ ʃɑrˈtruːz / (AmE), / ʃɑːˈtrɜːz / (BrE)
a pale yellowish-green color, resembling that of Chartreuse liqueur
pale yellowish-green color

Examples

She wore a stunning chartreuse dress to the party, making her stand out in the crowd.
The walls of the kitchen were painted a soft chartreuse that brightened the entire space.
A chartreuse accent pillow added a pop of color to the otherwise neutral sofa.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: lime - a bright yellowish-green color
  • Degree Lower: olive - a darker, duller green color

Etymology

Named after Chartreuse liqueur, which is produced by Carthusian monks in France and exhibits this distinctive color.

3. NOUN

/ ʃɑrˈtruːz / (AmE), / ʃɑːˈtrɜːz / (BrE)
a pale yellowish-green color, between green and yellow
yellow-green color

Examples

She chose a dress in chartreuse for the summer party.
The walls of the kitchen were painted a bright chartreuse that cheered up the space.
His tie added a splash of chartreuse to his otherwise conservative suit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lime - a bright greenish-yellow color
  • Degree Lower: olive - a dark yellowish-green color
  • Degree Higher: yellow - a primary color, more yellow than chartreuse

Etymology

From French 'chartreuse', referring to the greenish-yellow liqueur made by Carthusian monks.
