Physalis ixocarpa

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈseɪlɪs ɪkˈsoʊkɑːrpə / (AmE), / fɪˈseɪlɪs ˌɪksəʊˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
A species of annual plant native to Mexico and the southern United States, with edible purple, sticky fruit similar in appearance to small tomatoes; commonly known as the tomatillo.
Mexican plant producing tomatillo fruit

Examples

In Mexican cuisine, Physalis ixocarpa is often used to make green sauces and salsas.
The sticky husk around the fruit of Physalis ixocarpa must be removed before cooking.
Gardeners in the southern United States sometimes grow Physalis ixocarpa for its edible fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomatillo - The common name for the fruit of Physalis ixocarpa

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Physalis' (from Greek 'physalis', meaning 'bladder') and species name 'ixocarpa' (from Greek 'ixos', mistletoe/viscous, and 'karpos', fruit).
