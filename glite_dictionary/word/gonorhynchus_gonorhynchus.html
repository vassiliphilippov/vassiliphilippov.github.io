Gonorhynchus gonorhynchus

1. NOUN

/ ɡɒnəˈrɪŋkəs ɡɒnəˈrɪŋkəs / (AmE), / ɡɒnəˈrɪŋkəs ɡɒnəˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
A species of fish found in sandy areas of the western Pacific and Indian oceans, notable for its angular snout used for burrowing into sand.
species of fish with angular snout, Pacific/Indian oceans

Examples

The Gonorhynchus gonorhynchus is known for its ability to burrow quickly into sandy seabeds.
Marine biologists studied the peculiar snout of Gonorhynchus gonorhynchus to understand its burrowing habits.
The population of Gonorhynchus gonorhynchus has declined in some regions due to habitat disturbance.

Etymology

From Greek 'gonos' (seed, offspring) and 'rhynchos' (snout), reflecting its distinctive snout shape.
