supposed

1. ADJ

/ səˈpoʊzd / (AmE), / səˈpəʊzd / (BrE)
believed or assumed to be true, often wrongly or without proof
mistakenly believed; assumed to be true

Examples

The supposed threat turned out to be nothing more than a rumor.
Many doubt the supposed benefits of the new treatment.
She questioned the supposed expertise of the so-called expert.

Synset Examples

the supposed existence of ghosts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alleged - claimed or asserted to be true without proof
  • Antonym: real - actually existing as a fact

Etymology

Past participle of 'suppose', from Old French 'supposer', from Latin 'supponere' (to put under, assume).

assumed or believed to be true, often without strong evidence or proof

Examples
