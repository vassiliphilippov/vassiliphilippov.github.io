Rhenish

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.nɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.nɪʃ / (BrE)
A white wine produced in the Rhine River valley in Germany.
white wine from Rhine valley, Germany

Examples

She poured herself a glass of Rhenish to enjoy with dinner.
The restaurant offers a fine selection of Rhenish wines.
Historically, English poets often referenced Rhenish in their verses about feasting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hock - British term for German white wine from the Rhine region

Etymology

From Rhine, the river in Germany, with -ish denoting origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈraɪnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈriːnɪʃ / (BrE)
relating to the Rhine River or the region surrounding it, especially in reference to its wines or culture
of or relating to the Rhine region

Examples

The restaurant's menu features a selection of Rhenish wines from Germany.
Her family comes from a small Rhenish village along the river.
We enjoyed traditional Rhenish dishes at the local festival.

Synset Examples

Rhenish wines tend to be sweet

Etymology

From Latin Rhenus (the Rhine) + -ish (adjectival suffix).
