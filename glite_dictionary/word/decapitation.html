decapitation

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌkæpɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌkæpɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of cutting off a person's or animal's head, especially as a form of execution
act of cutting off the head

Examples

The guillotine became a symbol of decapitation during the French Revolution.
Historical records document several cases of decapitation used as punishment for serious crimes.
The threat of decapitation was intended to deter traitors from opposing the king.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beheading - execution or killing by cutting off the head

Etymology

From Late Latin decapitare (to behead), from Latin de- (down, off) + caput (head).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˌkæpɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌkapɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of killing by cutting off the head of a person or animal.
beheading; act of cutting off the head

Examples

The historical film depicted the decapitation of the king after a dramatic trial.
Many cultures had rituals involving decapitation as a form of capital punishment.
The sudden decapitation of the general left the army in chaos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beheading - the act of cutting off someone's head

Etymology

From Late Latin decapitare (to behead) + -ion, from Latin caput (head).
