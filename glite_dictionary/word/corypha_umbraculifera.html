Corypha umbraculifera

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrɪfə ˌʌmbrəˌkjuːlɪˈfɪərə / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪfə ˌʌmbrəˌkjuːlɪˈfɪərə / (BrE)
a tall palm tree native to southern India and Sri Lanka, with huge fan-shaped leaves used for making umbrellas, fans, and writing material
a tall Asian palm tree with large leaves

Examples

The enormous leaves of the Corypha umbraculifera are often used to make fans in villages.
Ancient manuscripts were once written on the leaves of the Corypha umbraculifera palm.
During the rainy season, locals use the broad leaves of Corypha umbraculifera as natural umbrellas.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Corypha' and species name 'umbraculifera' meaning 'umbrella-bearing'.
