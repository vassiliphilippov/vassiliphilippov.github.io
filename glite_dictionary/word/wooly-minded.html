wooly-minded

1. ADJ

/ ˈwʊliˌmaɪndɪd / (AmE), / ˈwʊliˌmaɪndɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or their ideas) confused and vague in their thinking; lacking clear thought or logic.
confused or vague in thinking

Examples

His wooly-minded approach to problem-solving made discussions frustrating for the team.
The policy proposals were criticized for being wooly-minded and lacking practical details.
She admitted her thoughts were a bit wooly-minded after the long and exhausting meeting.

Synset Examples

muddleheaded ideas
your addled little brain
woolly thinking
woolly-headed ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muddleheaded - having confused or disorderly thinking
  • Antonym: clear-headed - having or showing a clear and logical mind

Etymology

From 'woolly' (covered with or resembling wool) plus 'minded', suggesting a brain 'covered' or fogged by wool, i.e., not clear.
