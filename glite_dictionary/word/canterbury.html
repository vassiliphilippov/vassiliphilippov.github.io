Canterbury

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæntəˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈkæntəb(ə)ri / (BrE)
A historic city in Kent, southeastern England, famous for its cathedral, the site of Thomas Becket's martyrdom and the seat of the Archbishop of the Anglican Church.
historic city in southern England

Examples

Thousands of tourists visit Canterbury each year to see the famous cathedral.
Geoffrey Chaucer's 'The Canterbury Tales' is set during a pilgrimage to this city.
After arriving in Canterbury, we took a guided tour of the medieval streets.

Etymology

From Old English 'Cantwaraburh', meaning 'fortress of the people of Kent'.
