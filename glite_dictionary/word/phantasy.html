phantasy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.tə.si / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tə.si / (BrE)
imaginative fiction or literature that features unreal or fantastical elements; an alternate spelling of 'fantasy'.
imaginative or fantastical fiction

Examples

He devoted himself to writing phantasy novels set in mythical worlds.
The artist's work blurs the line between reality and phantasy.
Children often lose themselves in phantasy when playing pretend games.

Synset Examples

she made a lot of money writing romantic fantasies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fantasy - imaginative fiction involving magic or supernatural elements

Etymology

From Greek phantasia (appearance, imagination), via Latin phantasia; variant of 'fantasy'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06380048-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.tə.si / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tə.si / (BrE)
Imagination that is not limited by reality; especially, the freely creative or fanciful activity of the mind.
imagination unrestricted by reality
archaic

Examples

As a child, her mind was filled with phantasy and daydreams.
The novel explores a world of phantasy far removed from everyday life.
Surrealist art often draws from the depths of phantasy rather than logical thought.

Synset Examples

a schoolgirl fantasy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fantasy - imagination unrestricted by reality

Etymology

From late Middle English, altered spelling of 'fantasy', from Old French 'fantasie', from Latin 'phantasia', from Greek 'phantasia' (appearance, imagination).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05640291-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈfæn.tə.si / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tə.si / (BrE)
a widely held but false belief; illusion or mistaken idea
false belief; illusion
archaic british

Examples

The idea that the lost city is filled with gold is mere phantasy.
He lived under the phantasy that he was destined for greatness.
Some politicians exploit the phantasy that social problems can be solved overnight.

Synset Examples

they have the illusion that I am very wealthy
He fancies himself to be a local celebrity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - something that deceives by producing a false or misleading impression of reality
  • Synonym: delusion - a belief that is not true; a false idea

Etymology

Alternative spelling of "fantasy", from Greek "phantasia" (appearance, imagination).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05904666-n
Synset POS: n
