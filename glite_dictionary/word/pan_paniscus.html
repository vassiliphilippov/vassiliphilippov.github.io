Pan paniscus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæn pəˈnɪskəs / (AmE), / ˌpan paˈnɪskəs / (BrE)
A species of great ape closely related to the chimpanzee, commonly known as the bonobo, native to swamp forests of the Congo Basin.
the bonobo, a species of great ape

Examples

A group of Pan paniscus was observed using tools to obtain food in the wild.
Conservationists are working hard to protect Pan paniscus from habitat loss and poaching.
The social structure of Pan paniscus differs significantly from that of its close relative, the common chimpanzee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonobo - common name of Pan paniscus
  • Synonym: chimpanzee - Pan troglodytes, closely related ape species

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pan' (genus of chimpanzees) + 'paniscus' (diminutive of Pan, a Greek god), alluding to its smaller size.
