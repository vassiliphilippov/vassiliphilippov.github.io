pitiable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɪtiəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpɪtiəbl̩ / (BrE)
Deserving or arousing pity, often in a way that also evokes contempt or scorn.
deserving pity, often with contempt

Examples

The refugees lived in pitiable conditions after the disaster struck.
His excuses for not helping were truly pitiable and failed to impress anyone.
She made a pitiable attempt at an apology, which only made matters worse.

Synset Examples

their efforts were pathetic
pitiable lack of character
pitiful exhibition of cowardice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitiful - deserving or arousing pity
  • Synonym: pathetic - arousing pity, especially through vulnerability or sadness

Etymology

From Old French *pitiable* "compassionate, merciful," from *pitié* (pity).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɪtiəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpɪtiəbl̩ / (BrE)
deserving or arousing pity; worthy of compassion or sympathy due to suffering or misfortune
deserving of pity or compassion

Examples

The abandoned puppy was in a truly pitiable condition when it was found.
His pitiable attempt at an apology only made her angrier.
They lived in pitiable circumstances after the disaster struck the region.

Synset Examples

a hapless victim
miserable victims of war
the shabby room struck her as extraordinarily pathetic
piteous appeals for help
pitiable homeless children
a pitiful fate
Oh, you poor thing
his poor distorted limbs
a wretched life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitiful - deserving pity or compassion
  • Synonym: pathetic - arousing pity, often through vulnerability or sadness
  • Synonym: wretched - in a very unhappy or unfortunate state
  • Antonym: fortunate - receiving good from uncertain or unexpected sources

Etymology

From Old French pitiable, from pitié (pity) + -able (able to).
