contemporaneous

1. ADJ

/ kənˌtɛmpəˈreɪniəs / (AmE), / kənˌtɛmpəˈreɪniəs / (BrE)
existing or occurring in the same period of time as something else
existing at the same time

Examples

The two novels are contemporaneous, both written in the early twentieth century.
Several contemporaneous accounts describe the events in remarkable detail.
His paintings were contemporaneous with the rise of abstract art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simultaneous - occurring or done at the same time

Etymology

From Latin 'contemporaneus', from 'con-' (together) + 'temporaneus' (of time, timely).

2. ADJ

/ kənˌtɛmpəˈreɪniəs / (AmE), / kənˌtɛmpəˈreɪniəs / (BrE)
existing or happening in the same period of time as something else.
existing or occurring at the same time

Examples

The economic reforms were contemporaneous with significant changes in government policy.
Archaeologists discovered two contemporaneous settlements in the region.
Technological advancements were contemporaneous with social transformations during that era.

Synset Examples

a rise in interest rates is often contemporaneous with an increase in inflation
the composer Salieri was contemporary with Mozart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simultaneous - occurring at the same time
  • Synonym: contemporary - existing or occurring at the same time, also means belonging to the present

Etymology

From late Latin contemporaneus, from com- (together) + temporaneus
