Australopithecus

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˌstreɪloʊpɪˈθikəs / (AmE), / ɒˌstraləʊpɪˈθiːkəs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of early hominids that lived in Africa and are considered among the ancestors of modern humans
extinct African hominid genus

Examples

The discovery of Australopithecus fossils helped scientists understand human evolution.
Many anthropologists believe Australopithecus walked upright on two legs.
The famous fossil known as "Lucy" is classified as Australopithecus afarensis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hominid - a member of the biological family of great apes, including humans

Etymology

From Latinized Greek; 'australis' (southern) + 'pithēkos' (ape).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.strələˈpɪθəkəs / (AmE), / ˌɒ.strələˈpɪθəkəs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of early hominids from Africa, considered to be among the ancestors of modern humans
extinct African hominid genus

Examples

Scientists discovered a nearly complete Australopithecus skeleton in Ethiopia.
Many anthropologists believe that Australopithecus played a crucial role in human evolution.
The famous fossil known as Lucy belongs to the Australopithecus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hominid - member of the biological family that includes humans and their ancestors

Etymology

From Latin 'australis' (southern) and Greek 'pithēkos' (ape).
