to disport

1. VERB

/ dɪˈspɔrt / (AmE), / dɪˈspɔːt / (BrE)
to amuse oneself in a playful or carefree manner; to occupy oneself in an enjoyable activity
amuse or entertain oneself playfully

Examples

On sunny afternoons, children disport themselves in the park with laughter and games.
The guests disported themselves by singing and dancing late into the night.
As the yacht sailed, she disported on the deck, relaxed and carefree.

Synset Examples

The play amused the ladies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to amuse - to entertain or occupy in a pleasant manner
  • Synonym: to entertain - to provide with enjoyment or amusement

Etymology

From Old French *desporter* (to amuse oneself), from Latin *disportare* (to carry away).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈspɔrt / (AmE), / dɪˈspɔːt / (BrE)
to amuse oneself by playing or behaving in a lively, energetic way
to play or amuse oneself energetically

Examples

The children disported themselves in the sun-drenched park all afternoon.
Young animals can often be seen disporting in the fields at springtime.
At the festival, revelers disported themselves with music and laughter.

Synset Examples

The children frolicked in the garden
the gamboling lambs in the meadows
The toddlers romped in the playroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolic - to play and move about cheerfully, excitedly, or energetically
  • Synonym: romp - to play roughly and energetically

Etymology

From Old French 'desporter' (to amuse oneself), from Latin 'deportare' (to carry away).

Synset POS: v
