Lemuel Gulliver

1. PROPN

/ ləˈmjuːəl ˈɡʌlɪvər / (AmE), / lɪˈmjuːəl ˈɡʌlɪvə / (BrE)
the fictional English protagonist of Jonathan Swift's satirical novel 'Gulliver's Travels', known for his voyages to imaginary lands, including Lilliput
fictional traveler in 'Gulliver's Travels'

Examples

Lemuel Gulliver is the narrator and main character of Jonathan Swift's famous novel 'Gulliver's Travels'.
In literature classes, students often analyze the journeys of Lemuel Gulliver to understand satire and political commentary.
The image of Lemuel Gulliver tied down by tiny Lilliputians is an iconic scene in English literature.

Etymology

Lemuel (given name of Hebrew origin) + Gulliver (surname created by Swift for the character); from 'Gulliver's Travels' (1726).
