protractile

1. ADJ

/ proʊˈtræk.taɪl / (AmE), / prəˈtræk.taɪl / (BrE)
capable of being extended forward or protruded, especially in reference to body parts or muscles
capable of being extended

Examples

The chameleon caught the insect with its protractile tongue.
This species of fish has a highly protractile mouth for catching prey.
Scientists studied the protractile muscles in the animal's jaw.

Synset Examples

protractile muscle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: retractile - capable of being drawn back

Etymology

From Latin protractus (extended), from pro (forward) + trahere (to draw, pull) + -ile (able to).
