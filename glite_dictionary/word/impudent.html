impudent

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪmpjədənt / (AmE), / ˈɪmpjʊdənt / (BrE)
rude and showing a lack of respect, especially for someone older or in authority; boldly disrespectful.
rude and disrespectful

Examples

The boy's impudent remarks shocked the teacher.
She gave an impudent grin when asked about her tardiness.
It was impudent of him to challenge the manager in front of everyone.

Synset Examples

don't be fresh with me
impertinent of a child to lecture a grownup
an impudent boy given to insulting strangers
Don't get wise with me!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impertinent - rude and not showing respect
  • Antonym: respectful - showing politeness and respect

Etymology

From Latin *impudens* (shameless, immodest).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪmpjədənt / (AmE), / ˈɪmpjʊdənt / (BrE)
showing a lack of respect and shamelessly bold or rude
disrespectful; shamelessly bold

Examples

The impudent child responded to every adult with a cheeky comment.
Her impudent remark shocked everyone at the meeting.
Despite being warned, he gave an impudent grin to the teacher.

Synset Examples

a flip answer to serious question
the student was kept in for impudent behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impertinent - rude and showing a lack of respect
  • Antonym: respectful - showing politeness and honor toward others

Etymology

From Latin 'impudens', meaning 'shameless' (in- 'not' + pudens 'ashamed').
