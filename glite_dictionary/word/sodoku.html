sodoku

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈdoʊku / (AmE), / səʊˈdəʊkuː / (BrE)
A form of rat-bite fever, chiefly occurring in the Far East, caused by the bacterium Spirillum minus and characterized by fever, rash, and recurring infection at the bite site.
form of rat-bite fever (Far East)
medical

Examples

After being bitten by a rat in Japan, the patient was diagnosed with sodoku.
Treatment for sodoku generally involves antibiotics to combat the infection.
Unlike other forms of rat-bite fever, sodoku is more prevalent in East Asian countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat-bite fever - an infectious disease transmitted by the bite of a rat

Etymology

From Japanese *sodoku*, meaning 'rat-bite poisoning.'
