Culex quinquefasciatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːlɛks ˌkwɪŋkwəfəˈsiaɪɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈkjuːlɛks ˌkwɪŋkwɪfəˈsɪːətəs / (BrE)
A widespread tropical mosquito species known for transmitting filarial worms and other diseases to humans.
tropical mosquito species; disease vector
medical

Examples

Culex quinquefasciatus mosquitoes are common carriers of filarial worms in many tropical countries.
The spread of Culex quinquefasciatus has increased the risk of mosquito-borne diseases in urban areas.
Researchers are studying the behavior of Culex quinquefasciatus to develop new methods of vector control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mosquito - a small flying insect that sucks blood

Etymology

From Latin; 'Culex' means gnat or mosquito, 'quinquefasciatus' means five-banded.
