tahini

1. NOUN

/ təˈhiːni / (AmE), / təˈhiːni / (BrE)
A thick paste made from ground sesame seeds, commonly used in Middle Eastern cuisine.
sesame seed paste used in Middle Eastern food

Examples

She drizzled tahini over the roasted vegetables for extra flavor.
Many hummus recipes call for a generous spoonful of tahini.
You can find jars of tahini in most grocery stores these days.

Etymology

From Arabic ṭaḥīna (ground, milled), related to the word ṭaḥan (to grind).
