diatomite

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəˌtəmaɪt / (AmE), / daɪˈætəmaɪt / (BrE)
A light, porous sedimentary material composed mainly of the remains of diatoms, used especially in filtration and as an absorbent.
porous siliceous material from diatoms

Examples

The laboratory uses diatomite to filter impurities from chemical solutions.
Farmers sometimes add diatomite to soil to improve its quality and aeration.
Diatomite is prized for its ability to absorb liquids and act as a gentle abrasive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diatomaceous earth - a naturally occurring, soft, siliceous sedimentary rock easily crumbled into a fine white powder

Etymology

From 'diatom', after the microscopic algae whose remains make up the material, plus '-ite' (mineral suffix).
