kern

1. NOUN

/ kɝn / (AmE), / kɜːn / (BrE)
the part of a piece of metal type that projects beyond its body, allowing the letter to overhang adjacent type
overhanging part of metal type

Examples

The kern on the letter 'f' allows it to overlap slightly with adjacent letters.
During the digital transition, the concept of the kern was adapted for software-based typography.
In traditional printing, a damaged kern could cause unsightly gaps between letters.

Etymology

From French *carne* (corner, projecting angle), influenced by Italian *carno* (horn, tip).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03616382-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ kɜrn / (AmE), / kɜːn / (BrE)
(in typography) to adjust or remove a portion of space between adjacent letters to improve appearance or fit.
adjust space between letters (typography)

Examples

It is important to kern pairs like "A" and "V" for a balanced look.
The designer kerned the headline text to achieve perfect alignment.
In this software, you can manually kern every letter pair for custom typography.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: track - to adjust overall spacing between letters in a word (typography)

Etymology

From French *carner* (to cut corners), related to printing and type design.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02370935-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kɜrn / (AmE), / kɜːn / (BrE)
to adjust the spacing between characters in a piece of text, especially in typesetting, by projecting part of a letter beyond its normal width.
adjust letter spacing in typesetting

Examples

The designer decided to kern the headline so the letters would be more visually balanced.
Many fonts require you to kern certain letter pairs manually for the best appearance.
Once you kern the text properly, the final layout will look much more professional.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kerning - the process of adjusting the spacing between letters

Etymology

From Old French *carner* (to notch), related to medieval Latin *carna* (a notch or incision).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02371104-v
Synset POS: v
