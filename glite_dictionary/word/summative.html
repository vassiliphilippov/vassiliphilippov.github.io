summative

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌmətɪv / (AmE), / ˈsʌmətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or resulting from a summation; serving to summarize or assess the total of something, especially knowledge or achievement.
relating to a summation or total assessment

Examples

The final exam is a summative assessment of everything the students learned during the course.
At the end of each project, we prepare a summative report for the board of directors.
Unlike formative feedback, summative evaluations focus on the overall outcome rather than the process.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: formative - relating to development or formation, especially in learning; often contrasted with summative in assessment

Etymology

From Medieval Latin summativus, from Latin summare (to sum), from summa (sum, total).
