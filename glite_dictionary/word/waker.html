waker

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪkər / (AmE), / ˈweɪkə / (BrE)
a person who wakes up or awakens
a person who wakes up

Examples

My brother is not a light waker and often misses his morning alarm.
Early wakers usually have time to enjoy a quiet breakfast before work.
The baby is a frequent waker during the night, waking up every few hours.

Synset Examples

an early waker

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sleeper - a person who is asleep

Etymology

From the verb 'wake' + '-er', denoting someone who performs an action.

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪkər / (AmE), / ˈweɪkə / (BrE)
a person or thing that wakes others from sleep
person or thing that wakes others

Examples

The hotel employed a waker to knock on guests' doors in the morning.
My little brother is an early waker and loves to jump on my bed.
Before alarm clocks, a waker would go around the village to rouse people for work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awakener - someone who awakens others
  • Antonym: sleeper - someone who is asleep

Etymology

From the verb 'wake' + -er suffix, forming agent nouns.
