storksbill

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɔrksˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈstɔːksˌbɪl / (BrE)
any of various flowering plants of the genus Erodium, typically with deeply lobed leaves and elongated beak-like fruits.
plant of the genus Erodium

Examples

Several species of storksbill can be found blooming in sandy meadows in spring.
The fruits of the storksbill are long and slender, resembling a bird’s beak.
Some gardeners grow storksbill for its delicate pink and purple flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranesbill - plants of the genus Geranium, similar in appearance to storksbill

Etymology

Named for the beak-like shape of the fruit, resembling a stork's bill.
