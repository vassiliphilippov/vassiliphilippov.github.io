hendiadys

1. NOUN

/ hɛnˈdaɪ.ɪs / (AmE), / hɛnˈdaɪ.ɪs / (BrE)
A rhetorical device in which two nouns joined by a conjunction are used instead of a noun and its modifier to express a single idea.
rhetorical device using two nouns for one idea

Examples

The phrase 'sound and fury' is an example of hendiadys in Shakespeare.
In classical literature, hendiadys is often used for poetic effect.
The teacher explained hendiadys by giving examples from well-known speeches.

Etymology

From Greek 'hen dia dyoin' meaning 'one through two'.
