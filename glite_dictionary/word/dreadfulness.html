dreadfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɛd.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdrɛd.fʊl.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being extremely unpleasant, frightening, or causing great fear.
extreme unpleasantness or fearfulness

Examples

The dreadfulness of the storm left everyone shaken for days afterward.
She could never forget the dreadfulness of that night in the abandoned house.
The film captured the dreadfulness of war in a way that was both moving and disturbing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terror - intense fear, something causing terror
  • Antonym: pleasantness - quality or state of being pleasant

Etymology

Formed from "dreadful" + "-ness".
