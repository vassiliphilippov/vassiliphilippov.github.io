prowler

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraʊlɚ / (AmE), / ˈpraʊlə / (BrE)
a person who moves quietly and secretly around a place, often with the intention of committing a crime
person who sneaks around, often for crime

Examples

The neighborhood watch was established after several reports of a prowler were made.
Police arrested a suspected prowler lurking behind the houses late at night.
The alarm went off when a prowler tried to enter through the back door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burglar - person who breaks into houses to steal
  • Synonym: intruder - person who enters a place without permission

Etymology

From the verb 'prowl,' which comes from Old French *prolér* (to search or prowl).
