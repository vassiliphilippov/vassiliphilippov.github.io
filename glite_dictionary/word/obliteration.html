obliteration

1. NOUN

/ əˌblɪtəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌblɪtəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the complete destruction or removal of every trace of something
complete destruction or erasure

Examples

The ancient city faced obliteration after years of relentless warfare.
The evidence was removed to ensure the total obliteration of any connection to the crime.
The artist feared the obliteration of her legacy if the museum was demolished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destruction - the act of destroying; causing so much damage to something that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired
  • Antonym: preservation - the act of keeping or protecting something from harm or destruction

Etymology

From Latin 'oblitteratio', from 'oblitterare' meaning 'to blot out, erase'.

2. NOUN

/ əˌblɪtəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌblɪtəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of completely destroying or wiping out something so that no trace remains
total destruction; annihilation

Examples

The town's obliteration during the war left nothing but ruins behind.
Any evidence of the ancient village's existence faced gradual obliteration by the encroaching forest.
Total obliteration of the disease became the ultimate goal of medical researchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destruction - the act of destroying or ruining something
  • Antonym: creation - the act of bringing something into existence

Etymology

From Latin oblitteratio (erasure, destruction), from oblitterare (to blot out).
