boxwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑksˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈbɒks.wʊd / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub or small tree of the genus Buxus, often grown for hedges or used as a source of hard, fine-grained wood
evergreen shrub or its wood

Examples

The gardener trimmed the boxwood hedge into a perfect square.
Many antique instruments were crafted from boxwood because of its hardness.
A row of boxwood shrubs lined the walkway to the front door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bush - a low, densely branched shrub

Etymology

Named from 'box' (the genus Buxus) and 'wood'; the plant's wood was historically used for making boxes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑksˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈbɒksˌwʊd / (BrE)
the hard, close-grained, light yellow wood from the box tree, used for fine woodwork such as musical instruments, inlays, and engraving blocks
hard wood from the box tree, used in fine woodworking

Examples

The violin maker selected only the finest boxwood for the instrument's pegs.
Detailed engravings were carved on blocks of boxwood for old printing presses.
Because of its hardness, boxwood is prized by craftsmen for delicate inlay work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: box - an evergreen shrub or small tree; the source of boxwood

Etymology

From 'box' (the tree) + 'wood'.
