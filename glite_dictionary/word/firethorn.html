firethorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪrˌθɔrn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌθɔːn / (BrE)
a thorny evergreen shrub of the genus Pyracantha, bearing clusters of small white flowers and bright red or orange berries, often grown as an ornamental plant
a thorny shrub with white flowers and red/orange berries

Examples

The garden is bordered by a row of firethorn to deter intruders.
Bright clusters of berries hung from the branches of the firethorn.
Many birds are attracted to the firethorn because of its vivid fruit.

Etymology

From 'fire' + 'thorn', referring to the bright red berries and the thorny stems.
