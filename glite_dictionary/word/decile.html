decile

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛsaɪl / (AmE), / ˈdɛsaɪl / (BrE)
(statistics) any of nine values that divide a set of ranked data into ten equal parts, each containing one-tenth of the data.
each of nine dividing points in ranked data (statistics)

Examples

Students in the top decile scored above 90% on the test.
The income distribution was analyzed by splitting households into deciles.
Researchers compared the lowest decile of nutrition scores to the highest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quartile - each of three points dividing data into four equal parts
  • Synonym: percentile - each of ninety-nine points dividing data into one hundred parts

Etymology

From Latin 'decem' (ten), after 'quartile' and 'percentile'.
