urethritis

1. NOUN

/ jʊˌriːˈθraɪtɪs / (AmE), / jʊəˌriːˈθraɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the urethra, typically causing pain during urination
inflammation of the urethra
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with urethritis after she complained of painful urination.
Certain infections can lead to urethritis in both men and women.
Prompt treatment of urethritis can help prevent further complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cystitis - inflammation of the urinary bladder
  • Synonym: urethra - the duct by which urine is conveyed out of the body from the bladder

Etymology

From Greek ourēthra (urethra) + -itis (inflammation).
