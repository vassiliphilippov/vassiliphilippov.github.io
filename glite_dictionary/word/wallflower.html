wallflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔlˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈwɔːlˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a perennial plant of southern Europe, often growing on old walls or cliffs, with clusters of fragrant flowers, especially yellow or orange
a fragrant flowering plant, often found on walls or cliffs

Examples

The old castle was overgrown with wallflowers blooming in bright yellow and orange.
He admired the wallflower for its resilience on steep, rocky cliffs.
Gardeners often plant wallflowers for their sweet fragrance and long flowering season.

Etymology

From 'wall' + 'flower', referring to its growth on walls.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔlˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈwɔːlˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Erysimum, typically having fragrant yellow, orange, or brownish flowers, often found growing on old walls and rocky places.
a fragrant plant with yellow or orange flowers

Examples

The wallflower bloomed brightly in the garden beside the old brick wall.
She picked a bunch of wallflowers to brighten up her living room.
Many gardeners plant wallflower seeds in early autumn for spring flowering.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɔlˌflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːlˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
a person who remains on the sidelines at a social event, often because they are shy or not invited to dance or participate
shy person at a social event

Examples

At the school dance, he felt like a total wallflower, standing by himself in the corner.
She was determined not to be a wallflower this time and mingled with everyone at the party.
Though he was a wallflower in high school, he became more outgoing in college.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introvert - a person who prefers solitary activities and avoids large groups

Etymology

Originally referred to a type of plant growing on old walls; figurative sense for a shy person dates from the 19th century.
