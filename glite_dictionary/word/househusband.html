househusband

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊsˌhʌzbənd / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌhʌzbənd / (BrE)
A married man who manages household tasks and care of the home while his wife is the family's main earner.
husband managing the household

Examples

Ever since his promotion, Maria has been the primary earner while Tom has become a dedicated househusband.
As a househusband, he takes care of the children and manages all the household chores.
The rise of the househusband reflects changing gender roles in modern families.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: housewife - a married woman whose main occupation is caring for the home and family

Etymology

Formed as a compound of 'house' and 'husband', by analogy with 'housewife'.
