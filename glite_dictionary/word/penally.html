penally

1. ADV

/ ˈpɛnəli / (AmE), / ˈpiːnəli / (BrE)
in a manner intended as punishment; by way of punishment
in a manner intended as punishment

Examples

The law was applied penally to those who violated the agreement.
Taxes were penally increased for repeat offenders of the regulation.
The prisoners were penally isolated following their failed escape attempt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punitive - intended as punishment

Etymology

From Latin 'poenalis' (pertaining to punishment), via Late Middle English and Anglo-Norman French.
