scramasax

1. NOUN

/ ˈskræməˌsæks / (AmE), / ˈskræməˌsæks / (BrE)
A single-edged knife or short sword used by the Anglo-Saxons, typically for fighting and hunting.
Anglo-Saxon single-edged knife or sword

Examples

A well-preserved scramasax was discovered during the archaeological dig.
The warrior carried a scramasax at his belt for close combat.
Museum visitors admired the ancient scramasax displayed behind glass.

Etymology

From Old English *scramasax*, from Frankish or Proto-Germanic roots meaning 'wounding knife'.
