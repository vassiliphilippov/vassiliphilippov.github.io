unacceptable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnəkˈsɛptəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnəkˈsɛptəbl / (BrE)
not satisfactory or allowable; not good enough to be accepted or approved
not satisfactory; not allowed

Examples

Such rude behavior is simply unacceptable in this restaurant.
The company's proposal was deemed unacceptable by the board of directors.
It is unacceptable for students to submit their assignments late without a valid reason.

Synset Examples

the coach told his players that defeat was unacceptable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acceptable - good enough to be allowed or approved

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'acceptable', from Latin 'acceptare' (to accept).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əkˈsɛp.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əkˈsɛp.tə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) not acceptable or reasonable; too bad or inappropriate to be allowed or tolerated.
not acceptable; too bad to tolerate

Examples

The manager found his repeated lateness to be unacceptable.
Her rude comments at the dinner table were considered unacceptable by everyone present.
It is unacceptable to ignore the rules of the workplace.

Synset Examples

impossible behavior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acceptable - capable of being accepted; satisfactory
  • Synonym: intolerable - too bad or unpleasant to endure; unbearable

Etymology

From un- (not) + acceptable, from Old French accepter (to receive, approve).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əkˈsɛp.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əkˈsɛp.tə.bəl / (BrE)
not regarded as appropriate or correct, especially because it does not conform to accepted standards or usage.
not in conformity with accepted standards

Examples

Their behavior at the ceremony was deemed unacceptable by traditional standards.
The use of double negatives was once considered unacceptable in formal English.
Her proposal contained terminology that was unacceptable to the editing committee.

Synset Examples

the following use of ‘access’ was judged unacceptable by a panel of linguists: ‘You can access your cash at any of 300 automatic tellers’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acceptable - considered satisfactory or permissible
  • Synonym: improper - not in accordance with accepted rules or standards

Etymology

From un- (not) + acceptable, based on Latin acceptabilis.
