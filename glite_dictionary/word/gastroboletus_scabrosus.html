Gastroboletus scabrosus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊboʊˈliːtəs skəˈbroʊsəs / (AmE), / ˌɡastrəʊbəʊˈliːtəs skəˈbrəʊsəs / (BrE)
a species of fungus with a dingy yellow-brown cap and a rough stalk, superficially resembling a bolete
species of yellow-brown bolete-like fungus

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with specimens of Gastroboletus scabrosus after the recent rain.
Researchers identified the mushroom as Gastroboletus scabrosus by its distinctive rough stalk.
Amateur mycologists are often excited to find Gastroboletus scabrosus during their autumn forays.

Etymology

From Greek gastēr (stomach) and boletus (mushroom), referring to its stomach-shaped fruiting body; scabrosus is Latin for 'rough'.
