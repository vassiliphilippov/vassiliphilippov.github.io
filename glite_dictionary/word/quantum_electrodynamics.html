quantum electrodynamics

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑn.təm ɪˌlɛk.troʊˌdaɪˈnæm.ɪks / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.təm ɪˌlɛk.trəʊˌdaɪˈnæm.ɪks / (BrE)
The branch of physics that deals with the quantum theory of the electromagnetic interactions of photons, electrons, and similar particles.
quantum theory of electromagnetic interactions
medical

Examples

Richard Feynman made significant contributions to quantum electrodynamics in the twentieth century.
Quantum electrodynamics describes how light and matter interact at the quantum level.
The precision of predictions made by quantum electrodynamics is among the highest for any physical theory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: QED - abbreviation for quantum electrodynamics

Etymology

From 'quantum' (Latin 'quantus', how much) + 'electrodynamics', itself from 'electro-' + 'dynamics'.
