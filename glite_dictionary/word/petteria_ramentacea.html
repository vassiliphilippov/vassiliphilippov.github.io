Petteria ramentacea

1. NOUN

/ pɛˈtɪəriə ræmənˈteɪsiə / (AmE), / pɛˈtɪəriə ræmənˈteɪsiə / (BrE)
an erect shrub native to southeastern Europe, with large trifoliate leaves, dense clusters of yellow flowers, and poisonous seeds; sometimes classified under the genus Cytisus
a southeastern European shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

The botanical garden features a rare specimen of Petteria ramentacea in its Balkan section.
Researchers studied the toxicity of the seeds produced by Petteria ramentacea.
Petteria ramentacea is often admired for its vibrant yellow flower clusters in early summer.
