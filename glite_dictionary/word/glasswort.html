glasswort

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlæsˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈɡlɑːswɜːt / (BrE)
a bushy plant of salt marshes and sea beaches, especially in the Old World, with prickly leaves, sometimes burned to produce crude soda ash
a salt marsh plant with prickly leaves

Examples

The tidal flats were covered in dense patches of glasswort, turning red in the autumn sunlight.
Historically, glasswort was burned to create soda ash for glass production.
A botanist walked along the estuary collecting samples of glasswort for her research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: samphire - another plant of salt marshes, sometimes confused with glasswort

Etymology

Named for its historical use in glassmaking, as it was burned to produce soda ash for glass production.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡlæsˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈɡlɑːswɜːt / (BrE)
a fleshy plant found in coastal areas, with jointed, succulent stems, tiny scale-like leaves, and small flower spikes; formerly used in glass-making
succulent coastal plant, once used in glass-making

Examples

The marsh along the coast was dotted with patches of glasswort thriving in the salty environment.
Early glassmakers burned glasswort to obtain soda ash for their furnaces.
We spotted a cluster of glasswort during our walk in the tidal wetlands.

Etymology

From 'glass' + 'wort' (Old English 'wyrt' meaning 'plant' or 'herb'), referring to its historical use in glass production.
