remains

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmeɪnz / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪnz / (BrE)
objects or material left after the rest has been used, consumed, or removed.
leftover objects or material

Examples

She cleared the remains of the picnic from the grass.
After the storm, only the remains of the old barn could be seen.
He fed the remains of his lunch to the birds in the park.

Synset Examples

I threw out the remains of my dinner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leftovers - food remaining after a meal
  • Synonym: scraps - small pieces left after something is removed

Etymology

From Latin 'remanes', from 'remanēre' (to remain).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈmeɪnz / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪnz / (BrE)
objects or materials from the past, especially those that have been discovered through excavation and are of archaeological or historical interest.
excavated archaeological relics

Examples

The ancient remains were uncovered during the construction of the new railway line.
Archaeologists carefully catalogued the remains found at the historic dig site.
Some of the earliest human remains in the region date back over 5,000 years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relic - an object surviving from an earlier time, usually of historical or sentimental interest
  • Synonym: artifact - an object made by a human being, typically an item of cultural or historical interest

Etymology

From Latin remanēre (to remain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09226555-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈmeɪnz / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪnz / (BrE)
the body of a dead person, especially when referred to formally or in the context of burial or cremation
body of a dead person

Examples

After the funeral, the remains were buried in the family plot.
The archaeologists discovered the remains of an ancient king in the tomb.
Police identified the victim based on the remains recovered from the scene.

Synset Examples

the cadaver was intended for dissection
the end of the police search was the discovery of a corpse
the murderer confessed that he threw the stiff in the river
honor comes to bless the turf that wraps their clay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corpse - a dead body, especially of a human rather than an animal
  • Synonym: cadaver - a dead human body, typically one used in medical research or education
  • Synonym: body - the physical structure of a person or an animal, especially when dead

Etymology

From Latin remanēre (to remain) via Old French remaindre.
