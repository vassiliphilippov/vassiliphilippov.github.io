focalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of bringing something into focus or making something concentrated on a particular point
the act of bringing into focus

Examples

The focalisation of the camera lens made the image much clearer.
In narrative theory, focalisation determines through whose perspective the story is presented.
The experiment required the focalisation of the laser beam on a very small target.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - the center of interest or activity; the point at which rays or waves meet

Etymology

From French 'focalisation', derived from 'focal' (focus) + '-isation' (-ization).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the restriction of an infection or disease process to a limited or specific area within the body
confinement of infection to an area
medical

Examples

The doctor noted that the focalisation of the infection made treatment easier.
Early focalisation can prevent the spread of bacteria to other organs.
Without proper care, the focalisation may break down and allow the infection to become systemic.

Etymology

From French 'focalisation', from 'focal' (focus) + '-isation' (-ization).
