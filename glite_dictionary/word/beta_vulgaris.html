Beta vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ ˌbeɪtə vʌlˈgɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˌbiːtə vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A biennial Eurasian plant species with a swollen edible root, widely cultivated as a food crop; includes subspecies such as beetroot and sugar beet.
species of beet plant

Examples

Beta vulgaris is cultivated worldwide for both its edible roots and leafy greens.
Researchers are studying the genetic diversity of Beta vulgaris to improve crop yields.
The sugar extracted from Beta vulgaris is a key ingredient in many food products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beet - the edible root of Beta vulgaris, commonly used as a vegetable

Etymology

From Latin; 'Beta' means 'beet,' 'vulgaris' means 'common.'
