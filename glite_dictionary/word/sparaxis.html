Sparaxis

1. NOUN

/ spəˈræksɪs / (AmE), / spəˈræksɪs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous perennial herbs native to South Africa, grown for their brightly colored, funnel-shaped flowers.
genus of South African flowering plants

Examples

Sparaxis bulbs are best planted in the autumn for a burst of color in spring.
Gardeners appreciate Sparaxis for its vivid, star-shaped flowers and easy cultivation.
Several varieties of Sparaxis can thrive in well-drained, sunny borders.

Etymology

From Greek 'sparasso' meaning to tear, referring to the shape of the floral segments.

2. NOUN

/ spəˈræksɪs / (AmE), / spəˈraksɪs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous perennial herbs native to South Africa, valued for their colorful, iris-like flowers.
genus of South African flowering herbs

Examples

Sparaxis bulbs are often planted in gardens for their striking spring blooms.
Many species in the Sparaxis genus thrive in well-drained, sunny locations.
The bright colors of Sparaxis flowers attract a variety of pollinating insects.

Etymology

From Greek *sparaxis*, meaning 'tearing' or 'laceration', probably referring to the shape of the flower's parts.
