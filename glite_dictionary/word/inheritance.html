inheritance

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhɛrətəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪtəns / (BrE)
In genetics, the process by which characteristics are passed from parents to offspring through their genes.
genetic transmission of traits
medical

Examples

Eye color is determined by inheritance from both parents.
Many genetic diseases occur through inheritance rather than environmental factors.
Gregor Mendel studied patterns of inheritance in pea plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heredity - the passing of traits from parents to offspring

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French *enheritaunce*, based on *inherit* from Latin *hereditare* (to inherit).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhɛrɪtəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪtəns / (BrE)
the act of receiving property, a title, or an office from someone after their death, typically as an heir
property, title, or office received after death

Examples

After his uncle passed away, James received a large inheritance that included both money and land.
The dispute over the inheritance caused tension among the siblings for years.
She used her inheritance to start a new business in her hometown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legacy - something left to a person by a will
  • Synonym: bequest - a gift of personal property by will
  • Antonym: disinheritance - the act of depriving someone of a share in an inheritance

Etymology

From Latin 'inheritare' (to make heir), from 'hereditare' (to inherit).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhɛrətəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪtəns / (BrE)
a quality, attribute, tradition, or right passed down to a person from their ancestors, especially something non-material such as a blessing or reputation
immaterial attribute or tradition received from ancestors

Examples

She considered her strong sense of justice as part of her inheritance from her ancestors.
His most valuable inheritance was the wisdom his father had shared with him.
Language is an inheritance handed down from generation to generation.

Synset Examples

my only inheritance was my mother's blessing
the world's heritage of knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heritage - something inherited, especially traditions or immaterial qualities

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'inheritantia', from Latin 'hereditare' (to inherit).

4. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhɛrɪtəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪtəns / (BrE)
property, money, or a title received from someone after they have died, especially as stated in a will or by law
property or rights received from someone after death

Examples

After his aunt passed away, he received a large inheritance that changed his life.
The family disputed the division of the inheritance for several years.
She invested her inheritance wisely to secure her children's future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heritage - something inherited from the past, especially traditions or cultural property

Etymology

From Medieval Latin hereditantia, from Latin heres (heir) + -ance.
