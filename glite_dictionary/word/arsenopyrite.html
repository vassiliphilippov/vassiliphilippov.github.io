arsenopyrite

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑr.sɪˈnoʊ.paɪˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˌɑː.sɪˈnəʊ.pɑɪˌrʌɪt / (BrE)
a silver-white to steel-grey mineral consisting of iron arsenic sulfide, the principal ore of arsenic
a mineral ore of arsenic

Examples

Geologists discovered veins of arsenopyrite in the old mine shaft.
The silvery sheen of arsenopyrite makes it sometimes mistaken for other minerals.
Arsenopyrite is a significant source of arsenic used in various industrial processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrite - a common iron sulfide mineral with a metallic luster

Etymology

From arsenic + -o- + pyrite; pyrite from Greek pyr (fire).
