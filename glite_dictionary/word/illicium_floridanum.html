Illicium floridanum

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɪsiəm flɔːrɪˈdeɪnəm / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪsiəm flɒrɪˈdeɪnəm / (BrE)
A small, aromatic evergreen shrub or tree with purple-maroon flowers, native to wet soils in the southeastern United States, also known as the Florida anise tree.
aromatic shrub/tree native to southeastern US

Examples

The garden features several Illicium floridanum plants because of their striking purple flowers and fragrant leaves.
Wildlife often seeks shelter under the dense foliage of the Illicium floridanum in southern wetlands.
Botanists study Illicium floridanum for its unique aromatic oils and adaptation to damp habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Florida anise - Common name for Illicium floridanum, an aromatic native shrub

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'illicium' (allurement); species name refers to Florida, USA.
