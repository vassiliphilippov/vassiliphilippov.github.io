sthene

1. NOUN

/ ˈsθiːn / (AmE), / ˈsθiːn / (BrE)
a unit of force equal to 1,000 newtons, used chiefly in the metre–tonne–second (MTS) system
unit of force (1,000 newtons)

Examples

The force required was measured as 2 sthenes on the experimental apparatus.
While rarely used today, the sthene once played a role in engineering calculations.
One sthene is equivalent to one kilonewton in modern SI units.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newton - SI unit of force equal to the force required to accelerate a mass of one kilogram at a rate of one meter per second squared

Etymology

From Greek sthenos (strength).
