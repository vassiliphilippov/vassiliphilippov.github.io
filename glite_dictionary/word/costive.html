costive

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːstɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɒstɪv / (BrE)
Tending to cause or suffer from constipation; constipating or slow in evacuating the bowels.
causing or suffering from constipation
archaic medical

Examples

After taking the new medication, she began to feel costive and uncomfortable.
A costive disposition can result from a lack of fiber in the diet.
The doctor prescribed a remedy for his costive condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constipated - suffering from difficulty in emptying the bowels

Etymology

From Latin 'constipatus', meaning pressed together or stopped up.
