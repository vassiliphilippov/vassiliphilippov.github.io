crop-dusting

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑp ˌdʌstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒp ˌdʌstɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of spraying crops with pesticides, fertilizers, or other chemicals from an aircraft, typically at low altitude
aerial spraying of chemicals on crops

Examples

The farmer hired a company specializing in crop-dusting to treat the fields for pests.
Modern crop-dusting uses GPS-guided aircraft to ensure even coverage of farmland.
Strict regulations govern the chemicals that can be used in crop-dusting operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerial spraying - the act of applying chemicals to crops from the air

Etymology

From 'crop' (cultivated plant) and 'dusting' (the act of sprinkling fine particles).
