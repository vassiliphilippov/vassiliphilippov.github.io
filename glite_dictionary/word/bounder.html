bounder

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊndər / (AmE), / ˈbaʊndə / (BrE)
a person who jumps, leaps, or bounds, especially in sports or competitions
person who leaps or jumps in competition

Examples

The long bounder thrilled the crowd with her impressive leaps.
At the track meet, each bounder tried to outdo the others in distance.
As a skilled bounder, he excelled in both the triple jump and the high jump.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jumper - person who jumps, especially in sporting events

Etymology

From 'bound' (to leap) + '-er', denoting someone who performs an action.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊndər / (AmE), / ˈbaʊndə / (BrE)
a man whose behaviour is regarded as morally unacceptable or dishonourable
dishonourable or unscrupulous man

Examples

Everyone at the club thought Charles was a real bounder after his latest scandal.
She warned her daughter to stay away from that bounder who only cared for himself.
In old novels, the villain is often described as a bounder and a cad.

Synset Examples

you dirty dog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cad - a man who behaves dishonourably, especially toward women
  • Antonym: gentleman - a man of honourable and courteous conduct
