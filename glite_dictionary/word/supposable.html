supposable

1. ADJ

/ səˈpoʊzəbəl / (AmE), / səˈpəʊzəbl / (BrE)
(of a fact or situation) capable of being supposed, imagined, or inferred, especially on slight or limited evidence.
capable of being supposed or imagined

Examples

Is it even supposable that no one noticed the broken window all day?
A supposable cause for the delay might be heavy traffic, though we lack proof.
It is barely supposable that such a coincidence happened by chance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imaginable - capable of being imagined
  • Antonym: inconceivable - impossible to imagine or believe

Etymology

From suppose + -able, dating from the mid 17th century.
