Mugil

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːdʒɪl / (AmE), / ˈmjuːdʒɪl / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Mugilidae, including various species of mullets found in coastal and brackish waters.
type genus of the mullet family

Examples

Species in the genus Mugil are commonly found in tropical and subtropical coastal waters.
The biologist explained that Mugil includes some of the best-known mullet species.
Fishermen in the region catch Mugil for both food and local trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mullet - a common name for fish in the Mugilidae family

Etymology

From Latin *mugil* (mullet).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːdʒɪl / (AmE), / ˈmjuːdʒɪl / (BrE)
A genus of fishes in the family Mugilidae, commonly known as mullets, found in coastal temperate and tropical waters.
genus of mullet fishes

Examples

The genus Mugil includes several species commonly referred to as mullets.
Marine biologists have studied the behavior of Mugil in different habitats.
Several commercial fisheries target species from the Mugil genus for their economic value.

Etymology

From Latin *mugil* (mullet, a type of fish).
