scout

1. NOUN

/ skaʊt / (AmE), / skaʊt / (BrE)
a person whose job is to search for and recruit talented individuals, especially in sports or entertainment
talent recruiter in sports or entertainment

Examples

The football team hired a new scout to find promising young players.
She worked as a scout for a major film studio, always searching for fresh talent.
A scout offered him a contract after watching his performance at the tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talent agent - a person who represents and finds work for talented individuals

Etymology

From Middle French *escouter* (to listen) and Old French *escoute*, meaning 'one who listens or observes'.

2. NOUN

/ skaʊt / (AmE), / skaʊt / (BrE)
a person sent out to explore, obtain information, or find routes through unfamiliar territory, especially ahead of a main group
person who explores or gathers information

Examples

The army sent a scout ahead to check for enemy activity.
As a scout, she was responsible for finding the safest route through the mountain pass.
Before the main expedition arrived, a lone scout mapped out the terrain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explorer - someone who travels in search of discovery
  • Synonym: guide - a person who leads or shows the way to others

Etymology

From Old French 'escouter' (to listen), from Latin 'auscultare' (to listen to).

3. VERB

/ skaʊt / (AmE), / skaʊt / (BrE)
to go ahead or explore an area, often to search for something or somebody or to gather information
explore or search for something or someone

Examples

They sent a team to scout the area before the main expedition arrived.
She was hired to scout new talent for the sports team.
The hikers decided to scout ahead to find a better campsite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - to look carefully for something or someone
  • Synonym: to explore - to travel through an area to learn about it

Etymology

From Old French scouter, from Italian ascoltare (to listen), of Germanic origin.

4. NOUN

/ skaʊt / (AmE), / skaʊt / (BrE)
a person whose job is to watch for something or someone, often to provide information, warn of danger, or report developments, especially in military or sporting contexts
person employed to keep watch

Examples

The army sent a scout ahead to gather information about enemy movements.
As a scout for the baseball team, her job was to watch young players in local leagues.
During the night, the scout remained alert to warn the others of approaching danger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentinel - a person or thing that watches or stands guard
  • Synonym: lookout - a person maintaining a watch for danger or trouble
  • Synonym: spy - a person who secretly collects and reports information

Etymology

From Old French 'escouter' (to listen) and Middle English 'scouten' (to listen or spy out), ultimately from Latin 'auscultāre' (to listen).
