to reinvigorate

1. VERB

/ ˌriːɪnˈvɪɡəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈvɪɡəreɪt / (BrE)
to give new energy, strength, or enthusiasm to something or someone
give renewed strength or energy

Examples

The government hopes the new policy will reinvigorate the struggling economy.
A weekend at the beach really reinvigorated me after a stressful week at work.
They are seeking ways to reinvigorate interest in the local theater.

Synset Examples

Exercise is invigorating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invigorate - to give strength or energy to
  • Antonym: drain - to cause to lose energy or vitality

Etymology

From Latin 're-' (again) + 'invigorate', based on 'vigor' (energy, strength).
