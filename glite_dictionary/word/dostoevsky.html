Dostoevsky

1. PROPN

/ ˌdɑstəˈjɛfski / (AmE), / ˌdɒstɔɪˈɛfski / (BrE)
(1821–1881) A Russian novelist noted for exploring human suffering, morality, and psychology, author of works such as 'Crime and Punishment' and 'The Brothers Karamazov'.
Russian novelist (1821–1881)

Examples

Many consider Dostoevsky to be one of the greatest novelists in literary history.
Her thesis discusses the existential themes in Dostoevsky's works.
Reading Dostoevsky can be an intense psychological experience.

Etymology

Transliteration of Russian Достоевский (Dostoyevsky), the surname of Fyodor Dostoevsky.
