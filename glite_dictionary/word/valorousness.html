valorousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvælɚəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈvæl.ər.əs.nəs / (BrE)
exceptional bravery and courage, especially when facing danger or in battle
quality of great courage or bravery

Examples

The valorousness of the firefighters saved many lives during the blaze.
She was honored for her valorousness during the crisis.
His valorousness in combat earned him the nation's highest military award.

Synset Examples

he showed great heroism in battle
he received a medal for valor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valor - courage or bravery, especially in battle
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of courage to face danger, opposite of bravery

Etymology

From 'valorous' + '-ness'; 'valorous' from Old French *valeureus*, based on Latin *valor* (worth, bravery).
