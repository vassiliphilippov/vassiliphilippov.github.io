creative

1. ADJ

/ kɹiˈeɪtɪv / (AmE), / kɹiːˈeɪtɪv / (BrE)
Relating to or involving the use of imagination or original ideas to create something; promoting construction or creation.
involving creation or original ideas

Examples

The novel was praised for its creative approach to storytelling and unique narrative style.
She decorated her apartment with creative artwork that she made herself.
The company encourages employees to find creative solutions to everyday problems.

Synset Examples

creative work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inventive - having the ability to create or design new things

Etymology

From Latin creativus (productive), from creare (to create).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00587863-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ kriˈeɪtɪv / (AmE), / kriˈeɪtɪv / (BrE)
having the ability or power to invent or produce original ideas, works, or things
able to invent or produce original things

Examples

He has a very creative mind and always comes up with original solutions.
The company is looking for someone with a creative approach to design.
Her creative energy inspired the whole team.

Synset Examples

a creative imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inventive - having the ability to invent or devise new ideas
  • Antonym: unimaginative - lacking originality or inventiveness

Etymology

From Latin creare (to create) via Medieval Latin creativus.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00646272-a
Synset POS: a
