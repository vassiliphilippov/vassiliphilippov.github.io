to corrugate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɔr.əˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.ʊ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to shape or fold something into parallel ridges and grooves
to shape into ridges or grooves

Examples

The machine corrugates sheets of steel to increase their strength.
Cardboard can be corrugated for use in packaging to provide extra protection.
During manufacturing, they corrugate the material to improve its rigidity.

Synset Examples

corrugate iron

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrinkle - to make small lines or folds in something
  • Antonym: to flatten - to make something flat or level

Etymology

From Latin corrugatus, past participle of corrugare (to wrinkle), from com- (together) + rugare (to wrinkle).
