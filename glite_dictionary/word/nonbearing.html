nonbearing

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˈbɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈbeərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a wall or structural member) not supporting any vertical load other than its own weight.
supporting no vertical load except its own

Examples

They removed a nonbearing wall to create an open-concept kitchen.
Because the partition is nonbearing, it can be taken down without affecting the structure of the house.
The plans specify that only nonbearing interior walls can be altered during renovation.

Synset Examples

they took out a nonbearing wall

Related Terms

  • Antonym: load-bearing - (of a wall or structure) supporting structural weight in addition to its own

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'bearing' (supporting weight).
