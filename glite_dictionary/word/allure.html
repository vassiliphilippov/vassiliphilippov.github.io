allure

1. NOUN

/ əˈlʊr / (AmE), / əˈljʊə / (BrE)
the quality of being powerfully attractive or charming; the power to entice or attract through personal charm
the power to attract or charm

Examples

The city’s allure draws millions of tourists each year.
Many were captivated by the allure of her mysterious smile.
The allure of wealth and fame proved irresistible to him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appeal - the quality of being attractive or interesting
  • Synonym: charm - the power or quality of delighting, attracting, or fascinating others
  • Antonym: repulsion - a feeling of intense distaste or disgust; the power to repel

Etymology

From Old French 'aleurer' (to attract), from à l'ure (to the lure).

2. VERB

/ əˈlʊr / (AmE), / əˈljʊə / (BrE)
to attract or tempt someone by charm or appeal
to attract or entice by charm

Examples

The bright city lights allured him to move from his rural hometown.
She tried to allure the guests with her charming smile and friendly manner.
New products are designed to allure consumers looking for the latest technology.

Synset Examples

We were tempted by the delicious-looking food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tempt - to try to attract or entice someone to do something
  • Synonym: to entice - to attract or persuade by offering pleasure or advantage
  • Antonym: to repel - to drive or force back; to be distasteful or offensive

Etymology

From Old French *aleurer* (to attract), based on Latin *ad* (to) + *lura* (lure).
