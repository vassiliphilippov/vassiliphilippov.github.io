eon

1. NOUN

/ ˈiː.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈiː.ɒn / (BrE)
The largest division of geological time, comprising several eras.
longest division of geological time

Examples

The Precambrian is the earliest eon in Earth's history.
Life on Earth began during the Archean eon billions of years ago.
Geologists divide Earth's history into several eons, eras, and periods.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: era - a major division of geological time, smaller than an eon

Etymology

From Greek 'aiōn' (age, vital force, eternity).

2. NOUN

/ ˈiː.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈiː.ɒn / (BrE)
An immeasurably or indefinitely long period of time, often used hyperbolically or in reference to geological eras.
an immeasurably long period

Examples

It felt like eons passed before the phone finally rang.
Scientists believe the Earth formed over four and a half eons ago.
They hadn't seen each other in eons, but their friendship remained unchanged.

Synset Examples

oh, that happened eons ago

Related Terms

  • Synonym: era - a distinct period of history
  • Synonym: epoch - a significant period in history or a person's life

Etymology

From Greek aiōn (age, eternity).

3. NOUN

/ ˈiː.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈiː.ɒn / (BrE)
(in Gnosticism) a divine being or spiritual emanation arising from the Supreme Being, often playing a role in the creation or administration of the universe.
Gnostic divine being or emanation

Examples

According to Gnostic texts, each eon represents a distinct aspect of divine will.
The doctrine described the structure of the universe as composed of multiple eons.
Some scholars believe every eon has a counterpart, linking material and spiritual realms.

Etymology

From Greek aiōn (age, vital force, a period of existence), later used in Gnostic philosophy to denote divine entities.
