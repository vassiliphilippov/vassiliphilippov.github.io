scenic

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiː.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈsiː.nɪk / (BrE)
relating to the stage or theatrical scenery, especially the design, construction, or arrangement of stage settings and props
relating to theatrical stage scenery

Examples

The director praised the scenic elements that brought the play to life.
She pursued a career in scenic design after studying theater arts.
The awards recognized the exceptional scenic work in last year's production.

Synset Examples

scenic design

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theatrical - relating to the theater or stage

Etymology

From French 'scénique', from Latin 'scenicus', from Greek 'skēnikos' (of the stage), from 'skēnē' (stage).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsiː.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈsiː.nɪk / (BrE)
(of a place or route) having beautiful natural scenery.
having beautiful natural scenery

Examples

We took a scenic route that passed through the mountains and along the coast.
The hotel is located in a scenic area known for its breathtaking views and peaceful surroundings.
Many tourists visit the park to enjoy its scenic beauty and natural wildlife.

Synset Examples

scenic drives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: picturesque - visually attractive, especially in a quaint or charming way

Etymology

From Latin scēnicus, from Greek skēnikos (of the stage, dramatic).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00222192-s
Synset POS: s
