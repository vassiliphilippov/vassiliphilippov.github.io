Onobrychis

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.nəˈbraɪ.kɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒ.nəˈbraɪ.kɪs / (BrE)
a genus of herbs native to Europe and Asia, having pinnate leaves and pink or white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by flat, unjointed seed pods
a genus of Old World flowering herbs

Examples

Onobrychis is commonly known as sainfoin and is valued as fodder for livestock.
Many species in the genus Onobrychis thrive in dry, calcareous soils across Europe and Asia.
Researchers are studying Onobrychis for its role in sustainable agriculture and soil improvement.

Etymology

From Greek *onos* (donkey) + *brykein* (to bray), possibly referring to the plant's use as fodder for donkeys.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊnəˈbraɪkɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒnəˈbraɪkɪs / (BrE)
A genus of Old World herbs with pinnate leaves and pink or white racemose flowers, followed by flat, unjointed pods.
a genus of herbs with pink or white flowers

Examples

Onobrychis species are often cultivated for animal forage in dry regions.
The flowers of Onobrychis are usually pink or white and attract many insects.
Researchers studied the drought tolerance of several Onobrychis plants in the Mediterranean.

Etymology

From Greek *onos* (ass) + *brychis* (fodder), referring to its use as animal feed.
