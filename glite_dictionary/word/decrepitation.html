decrepitation

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌkrɛpɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌkrɛpɪˈteɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the crackling or breaking apart of certain crystals when heated, often accompanied by audible sounds
crackling of crystals when heated

Examples

When heated, some minerals undergo decrepitation, producing a distinct crackling noise.
The laboratory technician noted the decrepitation of the salt crystals during the experiment.
Geologists often observe decrepitation as a diagnostic property when analyzing mineral samples.

Etymology

From Latin 'decrepitare' (to crackle), related to 'crepitare' (to rattle, crackle).
