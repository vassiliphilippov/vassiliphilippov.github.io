boomerang

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːməˌræŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuːməræŋ / (BrE)
A curved, flat piece of wood or other material that, when thrown correctly, returns to the thrower; traditionally used by Indigenous Australians for hunting or sport.
curved throwing stick that returns to thrower

Examples

He skillfully threw the boomerang across the field and watched it curve back toward him.
The museum displayed an ancient Aboriginal boomerang among other traditional tools.
After practicing for weeks, she finally managed to make her boomerang return.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throwing stick - a stick designed to be thrown as a weapon or for sport

Etymology

From Dharug (an Aboriginal language of Australia) *bumarang* (throwing stick that returns).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbuːməræŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuːməræŋ / (BrE)
An action or plan that backfires, causing problems for the person who initiated it.
something that backfires on its originator

Examples

The new policy turned out to be a boomerang, harming the company’s reputation instead of improving it.
His attempt to hurt his rival became a boomerang that damaged his own career.
Spreading rumors can act as a boomerang and end up hurting you instead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backfire - Have the opposite effect to what was intended, especially causing harm to the initiator
  • Synonym: misfire - Fail to have the intended effect or outcome

Etymology

From Dharug (Australian Aboriginal language) *bumarang*, a curved throwing stick.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00073852-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈbuːmɚˌræŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuːməræŋ / (BrE)
(of an object or action) to return to the place or person of origin, especially unexpectedly or with unintended consequences.
to return to the origin, like a boomerang

Examples

The new policy boomeranged on the government, creating more issues than it solved.
Her harsh words eventually boomeranged and hurt her own reputation.
If you spread rumors, they might boomerang back to you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebound - to bounce back after hitting something
  • Synonym: ricochet - to rebound off a surface in a sequence

Etymology

From the noun 'boomerang', originally from Dharug (an Australian Aboriginal language) *bumariny* or similar, denoting a curved throwing stick.

Synset POS: v
