Slavic

1. ADJ

/ ˈslævɪk / (AmE), / ˈslɑːvɪk / (BrE)
relating to the Slavic languages or to the peoples who speak them
relating to Slavic languages or peoples

Examples

She is studying the Slavic languages at university.
Many Slavic traditions are celebrated throughout Eastern Europe.
The Slavic alphabet has influenced many other writing systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Slavonic - relating to the Slavic languages or peoples

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Slāvicus, from Slāvus ‘a Slav’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03130458-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈslævɪk / (AmE), / ˈslaːvɪk / (BrE)
a branch of the Indo-European language family that includes Russian, Polish, Czech, Serbian, and related languages
branch of Indo-European languages

Examples

The Slavic languages are spoken by millions of people across Eastern Europe.
Russian and Polish are both classified as Slavic languages.
Linguists study the evolution of Slavic from Proto-Indo-European roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Slavonic - an alternative term for the Slavic language branch

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Slavicus, from Slavi, referring to the Slavs.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06956533-n
Synset POS: n
