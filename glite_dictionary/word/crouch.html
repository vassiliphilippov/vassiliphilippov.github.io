crouch

1. NOUN

/ kraʊtʃ / (AmE), / kraʊtʃ / (BrE)
a position in which the body is bent low with the limbs close to the torso, typically in preparation for movement or to avoid being seen
the act of bending low with limbs close to the body

Examples

The runner waited in a crouch before the starting gun sounded.
His sudden crouch behind the sofa saved him from being seen.
She stayed in a crouch to avoid attracting attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squat - a posture where the knees are bent and the body is lowered close to the ground

Etymology

From Old French crochir (to bend, become crooked), from Medieval Latin croccare.

2. VERB

/ kraʊtʃ / (AmE), / kraʊtʃ / (BrE)
to lower your body close to the ground by bending your knees and leaning forward
bend down low by lowering body

Examples

The cat crouched behind the bush, waiting to pounce on its prey.
She crouched down to tie her shoelaces on the busy sidewalk.
Soldiers crouched in the trench as bullets flew overhead.

Synset Examples

he crouched down
She bowed before the Queen
The young man stooped to pick up the girl's purse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoop - to bend the body forward and downward
  • Antonym: stand - to be in an upright position on the feet

Etymology

From Old French *croucier* (to bend, to stoop).

3. VERB

/ kraʊtʃ / (AmE), / kraʊtʃ / (BrE)
to lower your body close to the ground by bending your knees and leaning forward, often balancing on your feet
lower body by bending knees

Examples

The cat crouched low in the grass, waiting to pounce on its prey.
She crouched behind the sofa when she heard a noise in the hall.
The children crouched down to look at the tiny insects under the rock.

Synset Examples

In some cultures, the women give birth while squatting
The children hunkered down to protect themselves from the sandstorm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squat - to sit with one's knees bent and one's heels close to or touching the buttocks or the back of the thighs

Etymology

From Middle English crouchen, from Old French crochir (to bend, become crooked), from Latin crocāre.
