Sidney Webb

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɪdni wɛb / (AmE), / ˈsɪdni wɛb / (BrE)
(1859–1947) English sociologist and economist, influential member of the Fabian Society and co-founder of the London School of Economics.
English sociologist, economist, Fabian Society leader

Examples

Sidney Webb was instrumental in shaping modern social policy in Britain.
The ideas of Sidney Webb influenced the development of the welfare state.
