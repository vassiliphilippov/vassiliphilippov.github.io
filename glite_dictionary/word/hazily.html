hazily

1. ADV

/ ˈheɪzɪli / (AmE), / ˈheɪzɪli / (BrE)
In a way that is indistinct, unclear, or lacking in sharpness.
in an indistinct or unclear way

Examples

He could only remember the events of that night hazily now.
The distant mountains appeared hazily in the early morning fog.
She spoke hazily about her childhood memories, unsure of the details.

Synset Examples

he remembered her only hazily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaguely - in a way that is not clearly expressed or perceived
  • Antonym: clearly - in a clear and distinct manner

Etymology

Formed from "hazy" (from haze) + "-ly" adverbial suffix.

2. ADV

/ ˈheɪzɪli / (AmE), / ˈheɪzɪli / (BrE)
In a way that is not clear, distinct, or easy to see; as if through a haze.
in a hazy or unclear way

Examples

The mountains appeared hazily in the morning light, shrouded by mist.
She remembered the events of that evening only hazily.
The city skyline could just be seen hazily on the horizon.

Synset Examples

we saw the distant hills hazily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaguely - in a way that is not clear or definite
  • Antonym: clearly - in a way that is easy to see, hear, or understand

Etymology

From 'hazy' (obscured by haze) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00356221-r
Synset POS: r
