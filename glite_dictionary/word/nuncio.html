nuncio

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌnsiˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈnʌn.si.əʊ / (BrE)
a papal diplomatic representative with ambassadorial status, appointed by the Pope, especially to a foreign country
papal diplomatic representative (ambassador)

Examples

The Vatican appointed a new nuncio to oversee relations with the local church authorities.
During the ceremony, the nuncio delivered a message from the Pope.
The nuncio played a key role in diplomatic negotiations between the two countries.

Etymology

From Italian 'nuncio', from Latin 'nuntius' (messenger).
