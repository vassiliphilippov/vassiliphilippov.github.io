uncolored

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkʌlərd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌləd / (BrE)
not artificially colored or bleached; in its natural state without added color or dye.
not artificially colored or bleached

Examples

She prefers her linens to be uncolored and made from natural fibers.
His hair remained uncolored, giving him a distinctive, natural look among his peers.
The uncolored yarn is popular with customers who have sensitive skin.

Synset Examples

unbleached blonde hair
her hair is uncolored
undyed cotton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undyed - not colored with dye; in a natural state
  • Synonym: unbleached - not whitened by chemicals; in a natural state

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkʌlərd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌləd / (BrE)
not having any color; lacking color or pigment.
without color; colorless

Examples

The solution remained uncolored after the chemical reaction.
Her speech was direct and uncolored by emotion.
This mineral is usually found in an uncolored form.

Synset Examples

pure water is uncolored

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colorless - having no color or pigment
  • Antonym: colored - having color

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'colored'.
