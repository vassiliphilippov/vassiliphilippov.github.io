Centaurea cineraria

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtɔriə sɪˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɔːrɪə sɪˈnɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A plant of the daisy family with silvery, downy leaves and stems, commonly grown as an ornamental and known as dusty miller.
ornamental plant with silvery leaves

Examples

We planted Centaurea cineraria along the walkway for its attractive silvery foliage.
The soft, woolly leaves of Centaurea cineraria make it a favorite in ornamental gardens.
Gardeners often choose Centaurea cineraria for borders and containers due to its unique color and texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dusty miller - common name for Centaurea cineraria, an ornamental plant with silver-gray leaves

Etymology

From Latin: Centaurea (the genus name), and cineraria (from Latin 'cinerarius', pertaining to ashes, referring to the ashy color of the leaves).
