foundling

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊndlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfaʊndlɪŋ / (BrE)
an infant who has been abandoned by its parents and is discovered and cared for by others
abandoned infant of unknown parentage

Examples

The orphanage took in a foundling left on their doorstep during the night.
A foundling was discovered in the park by a passerby and brought to the authorities.
Stories about a foundling growing up to find their true family are common in literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orphan - a child whose parents are dead

Etymology

From Middle English, from 'found' (past participle of 'find') + '-ling' (diminutive suffix).
