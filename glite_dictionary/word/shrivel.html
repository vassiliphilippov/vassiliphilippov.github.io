to shrivel

1. VERB

/ ˈʃrɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪvəl / (BrE)
to become wrinkled, shrunken, or dry, especially from loss of moisture
become dry and shrunken

Examples

As the sun beat down, the petals began to shrivel and fall off the flower.
If you forget to water houseplants, their leaves may shrivel and turn brown.
The once juicy plums shrivel into dried fruit after weeks in the sun.

Synset Examples

The fruit dried and shriveled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wither - to become dry and weak
  • Synonym: wilt - to lose freshness and become limp, especially for plants

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'shrivelen', of imitative origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈʃrɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪv(ə)l / (BrE)
to become or make something smaller and wrinkled, especially due to loss of moisture, or to decrease in strength, importance, or confidence
become wrinkled or decrease in amount

Examples

After months without rain, the leaves on the tree began to shrivel and fall.
My confidence seemed to shrivel in the face of so much criticism.
The market for those products has started to shrivel due to changing consumer tastes.

Synset Examples

His earnings shrank
My courage shrivelled when I saw the task before me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wither - to dry up or become weak and shriveled
  • Antonym: to grow - to increase in size or amount

Etymology

From Middle English 'shrivelen', probably of Scandinavian origin; compare with Swedish 'skryvla' (to wrinkle).
