archegoniate

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑr.kəˈɡoʊ.ni.eɪt / (AmE), / ˌɑː.kɪˈɡəʊ.nɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of an archegonium, the female reproductive organ found in certain plants such as mosses, ferns, and some gymnosperms.
relating to an archegonium (plant reproductive organ)
medical

Examples

The archegoniate plants reproduce using specialized female structures called archegonia.
Bryophytes are often studied for their unique archegoniate reproductive systems.
The evolution of archegoniate features marked a significant step in plant adaptation to land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archegonium - the female sex organ in mosses, ferns, and similar plants

Etymology

From New Latin archegonium (from Greek arkhegonion, 'origin') + -ate.
