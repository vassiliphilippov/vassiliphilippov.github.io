misanthropical

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɪs.ænˈθrɑ.pɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.ænˈθrɒ.pɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Having or showing a dislike or distrust of humankind.
disliking or distrustful of humankind

Examples

He became increasingly misanthropical after years of disappointment with society.
The misanthropical tone of the novel alienated some readers but intrigued others.
Her misanthropical worldview made it difficult for her to trust even close friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misanthropic - disliking or distrusting humankind
  • Antonym: philanthropic - seeking to promote the welfare of others

Etymology

From Greek misanthrōpos (hating mankind) + -ical.

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɪs.ænˈθrɑː.pɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.ænˈθrɒ.pɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Having or showing a general dislike, distrust, or contempt for people or human nature.
disliking or distrusting people

Examples

The old man's misanthropical attitude made him suspicious of everyone who visited.
Her misanthropical comments alienated her from her colleagues at work.
Writers with a misanthropical view often portray bleak societies in their novels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misanthropic - disliking or distrusting humankind

Etymology

From Greek misanthrōpos (hating mankind) + -ical.
