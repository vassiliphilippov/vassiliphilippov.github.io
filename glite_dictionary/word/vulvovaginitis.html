vulvovaginitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌvʌlvoʊˌvædʒɪˈnaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌvʌlvəʊˌvædʒɪˈnaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the vulva and the vagina, typically causing discomfort, itching, and discharge
inflammation of vulva and vagina
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with vulvovaginitis after reviewing her symptoms and test results.
Certain infections and irritants can lead to vulvovaginitis in women of all ages.
Good hygiene practices may help prevent cases of vulvovaginitis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaginitis - inflammation of the vagina

Etymology

From Latin 'vulva' (womb, female external genitalia) and 'vagina', with 'itis' (inflammation).
