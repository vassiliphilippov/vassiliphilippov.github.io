monolatry

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑːlətri / (AmE), / məˈnɒlətri / (BrE)
the worship of a single god without asserting that only one god exists
worship of one god (others may exist)

Examples

Some ancient societies practiced monolatry rather than monotheism, worshipping one god without denying the existence of others.
Biblical scholars debate whether early Israelites practiced monolatry before moving toward strict monotheism.
The concept of monolatry helps explain the religious diversity found in the ancient Near East.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monotheism - the belief that only one god exists

Etymology

From Greek mono- (single) + latreia (worship).
