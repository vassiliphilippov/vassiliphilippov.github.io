prosy

1. ADJ

/ ˈproʊzi / (AmE), / ˈprəʊzi / (BrE)
lacking imagination or originality; dull and uninteresting
dull, lacking imagination or originality

Examples

His lecture was so prosy that several students fell asleep.
The novel was criticized for its prosy style and lack of excitement.
Despite the interesting topic, the documentary turned out to be rather prosy.

Synset Examples

a pedestrian movie plot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedestrian - lacking inspiration or excitement; dull
  • Antonym: imaginative - having or showing creativity or inventiveness
  • Antonym: inspired - filled with creative power or imagination

Etymology

From 'prose' + '-y', meaning like prose (as opposed to poetry).
