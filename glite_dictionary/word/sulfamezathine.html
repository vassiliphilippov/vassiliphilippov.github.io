sulfamezathine

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌlfəˈmɛzəˌθiːn / (AmE), / ˌsʌlfəˈmiːzəθiːn / (BrE)
a sulfa drug used to treat bacterial infections in humans and animals, similar to sulfadiazine
sulfa drug for bacterial infections
medical

Examples

The veterinarian prescribed sulfamezathine to treat the sheep's bacterial infection.
Some strains of bacteria have developed resistance to sulfamezathine over time.
Doctors may use sulfamezathine as an alternative when patients are allergic to penicillin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfadiazine - a sulfa drug used to treat infections

Etymology

From 'sulfa-' (relating to sulfonamides) and chemical suffixes indicating structure.
