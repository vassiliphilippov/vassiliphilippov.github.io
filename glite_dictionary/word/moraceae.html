Moraceae

1. NOUN

/ mɔːˈreɪsiˌi / (AmE), / mɒˈreɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a large family of trees and shrubs, often with milky sap, that includes mulberries, figs, and related plants
plant family including mulberries and figs

Examples

Many edible fruits, such as figs and mulberries, come from the Moraceae family.
The Moraceae family is characterized by plants that often bleed a milky sap when cut.
Botanists study the diversity of Moraceae species across different tropical regions.

Etymology

From Latin Morus (mulberry) + -aceae (a suffix denoting plant families).
