concerto

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtʃɛrtoʊ / (AmE), / kənˈtʃɛətəʊ / (BrE)
A musical composition written for an orchestra and a soloist, typically in several movements.
composition for orchestra and soloist

Examples

The orchestra performed a Beethoven concerto with an internationally renowned pianist.
She practiced for months to prepare for her violin concerto debut.
The composer wrote a new concerto for cello and orchestra.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: symphony - An elaborate musical composition for full orchestra, generally without soloist

Etymology

From Italian 'concerto', meaning 'concert' or 'agreement'; from Latin 'concertare' (to contend, to act together).
