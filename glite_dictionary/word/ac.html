AC

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪˈsi / (AmE), / ˌeɪˈsiː / (BrE)
(abbreviation) An electric current that reverses direction periodically, typically in a sinusoidal pattern; alternating current.
alternating electric current
informal

Examples

Most household appliances in the U.S. operate on AC provided by the electrical grid.
The laboratory experiment compared the effects of AC and DC on different circuits.
She learned about AC and its practical uses during her physics class.

Synset Examples

In the US most household current is AC at 60 cycles per second

Related Terms

  • Antonym: DC - direct current - an electric current flowing in one direction only

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'alternating current'.
