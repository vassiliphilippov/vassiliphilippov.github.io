Danaea

1. NOUN

/ dəˈniːə / (AmE), / dəˈniːə / (BrE)
A genus of small terrestrial ferns native to tropical America.
genus of small tropical American ferns

Examples

The rare Danaea ferns thrive in the humid understory of the Amazon rainforest.
Several species of Danaea have been studied for their unique reproductive cycles.
Botanists discovered a new population of Danaea in the mountain forests of Costa Rica.

2. NOUN

/ dəˈniːə / (AmE), / dəˈniːə / (BrE)
A genus of small terrestrial ferns native to tropical America.
genus of tropical American ferns

Examples

The Danaea ferns thrive in the humid undergrowth of tropical rainforests.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Danaea genus in Central America.
Danaea species are recognized for their simple, undivided fronds.

Etymology

Named after Danaë, a figure in Greek mythology; genus name established in botanical Latin.
