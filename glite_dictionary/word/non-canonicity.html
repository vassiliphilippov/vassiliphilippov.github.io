non-canonicity

1. NOUN

/ nɑn kəˌnɑnɪˈsɪɾi / (AmE), / nɒn kəˌnɒnɪˈsɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of not being canonical; not conforming to established rules, standards, or the accepted canon.
state of not being canonical

Examples

The non-canonicity of the novel invites readers to question literary traditions.
Academic debates often revolve around the non-canonicity of certain texts.
Its non-canonicity set the work apart from mainstream scientific theories.

Synset Examples

This holds minor literature hostage to the revolutionary political project of the critic and subordinates its non-canonicity in requiring an attack on the canon.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: canonicity - state of being canonical
  • Degree Lower: canonical - conforming to established rules or a canon

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'non-' meaning 'not' and 'canonicity' from 'canonical,' meaning conforming to a canon.
