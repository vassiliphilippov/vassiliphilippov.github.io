Progne

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːɡni / (AmE), / ˈprɒɡni / (BrE)
A genus of American swallows in the family Hirundinidae, including the purple martin and related species.
a genus of American swallows

Examples

The Progne genus includes the well-known purple martin found throughout North America.
Several species within the Progne genus migrate seasonally across the Americas.
Ornithologists have observed interesting nesting behavior among different Progne species.

Etymology

From Latin 'Progne', derived from the Greek mythological figure Procne, who was transformed into a swallow.

genus of American swallows

The Progne genus includes several well-known species such as the purple martin.

Bird enthusiasts often refer to Progne when discussing different types of American martins.

Etymology

From Latin Progne, the mythological figure Procne, daughter of Pandion, in reference to the swallow.
