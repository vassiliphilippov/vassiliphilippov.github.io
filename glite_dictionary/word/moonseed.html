moonseed

1. NOUN

/ ˈmunˌsid / (AmE), / ˈmuːnˌsiːd / (BrE)
A plant of the family Menispermaceae, bearing red or black fruit that contain crescent- or ring-shaped seeds.
plant with crescent-shaped seeds

Examples

The woods behind the house are filled with moonseed vines twining along the fence.
The poisonous berries of the moonseed can be mistaken for edible grapes by the unwary.
Botanists identified the plant as a species of moonseed due to the shape of its seeds.

Etymology

Named for the crescent (moon-like) shape of its seeds.
