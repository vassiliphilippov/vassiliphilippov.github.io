Badaga

1. NOUN

/ bəˈdɑːɡə / (AmE), / bəˈdɑːɡə / (BrE)
a member of an agricultural people of the Nilgiri Hills in southern India.
member of a southern Indian ethnic group

Examples

The Badaga have a rich tradition of folk music and dance.
Many Badaga communities are found in the Nilgiri district of Tamil Nadu.
He studied the customs of the Badaga during his visit to southern India.

Etymology

From Kannada badaga (northern), used as a self-designation by the Badaga people.

2. NOUN

/ bɑˈdɑːɡə / (AmE), / bəˈdɑːɡə / (BrE)
the Dravidian dialect of Kannada spoken by the Badaga people of the Nilgiri Hills in southern India
a dialect of Kannada spoken in southern India

Examples

The Badaga language preserves unique words not found in standard Kannada.
Researchers documented folktales in Badaga to help preserve the dialect.
She learned to speak Badaga after moving to the Nilgiri Hills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kannada - Dravidian language spoken in Karnataka, India

Etymology

From the name Badaga, referring to the people and their language in southern India.
