Mark Twain

1. PROPN

/ mɑrk ˈtweɪn / (AmE), / mɑːk ˈtweɪn / (BrE)
The pen name of Samuel Langhorne Clemens (1835–1910), a celebrated American writer and humorist known for works such as 'The Adventures of Tom Sawyer' and 'Adventures of Huckleberry Finn'.
American writer and humorist (1835–1910)

Examples

Mark Twain is regarded as one of the greatest humorists in American literature.
Many schoolchildren read 'Mark Twain's classic novels as part of their curriculum.
The wit and insight of Mark Twain continue to influence writers today.

Etymology

Pen name adopted by Samuel Langhorne Clemens; 'mark twain' was a riverboat term meaning 'two fathoms deep'.
