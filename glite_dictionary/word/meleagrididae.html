Meleagrididae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛliˌæɡrɪˈdaɪdi / (AmE), / ˌmɛliˌaɪɡrɪˈdiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds in the order Galliformes, comprising the turkeys and some extinct related species.
family of birds including turkeys

Examples

The domesticated turkey belongs to the family Meleagrididae.
Fossil evidence suggests that Meleagrididae once comprised several now-extinct species.
Researchers compared the characteristics of Meleagrididae with other families within Galliformes.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Meleagris (turkey) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ mɛˌliːəˌɡrɪˈdiːdiː / (AmE), / mɛˌliːəˌɡrɪˈdiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds in the order Galliformes that includes turkeys and several extinct relatives.
bird family including turkeys

Examples

The Meleagrididae family comprises all modern turkeys and certain extinct species.
Researchers studied fossils belonging to the Meleagrididae to trace avian evolution.
Unlike pheasants, members of the Meleagrididae are native to North America.

Etymology

From the genus name Meleagris (turkey), derived from Greek 'meleagris' (guinea fowl).
