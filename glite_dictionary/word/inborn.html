inborn

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪnˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌbɔːn / (BrE)
existing from birth; present at the time of birth rather than acquired or learned.
existing from birth

Examples

She has an inborn talent for music that was evident from an early age.
Certain reflexes are inborn in infants and do not have to be taught.
His inborn curiosity drove him to explore the world around him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innate - existing naturally or from birth in a person or animal
  • Antonym: acquired - developed or learned after birth

Etymology

From Old English 'in' (in) + 'born' (past participle of 'bear').

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪnˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌbɔːn / (BrE)
(of a quality or ability) present at birth; innate rather than acquired or learned.
present at birth; innate

Examples

Her inborn talent for music was evident from a very young age.
Some animal instincts are inborn and not learned from the environment.
His inborn kindness made him universally liked.

Synset Examples

mankind's connatural sense of the good

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innate - existing naturally rather than acquired; present from birth
  • Antonym: acquired - developed or learned after birth, not present from the beginning

Etymology

Formed from 'in-' (in) + 'born' (given birth to); attested since the 15th century.
