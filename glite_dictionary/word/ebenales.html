Ebenales

1. NOUN

/ ˌebəˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌiːbəˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
a former order of flowering plants that included trees and shrubs from the families Ebenaceae, Sapotaceae, Styracaceae, and Symplocaceae
former plant order including certain trees and shrubs

Examples

The family Ebenaceae, known for ebony trees, was traditionally placed in the Ebenales.
Modern classifications have largely replaced the Ebenales with other plant groupings.
Researchers used to study fruit characteristics unique to the Ebenales in their taxonomy work.

Etymology

From Latin 'ebenus' (ebony tree) + '-ales' (order), used in botanical taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˌiːbəˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌiːbəˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
a former botanical order of trees and shrubs including the families Ebenaceae, Sapotaceae, Styracaceae, and Symplocaceae.
former botanical order of certain trees and shrubs

Examples

The family Ebenaceae, known for ebony wood, was once classified under the Ebenales.
Botanists now rarely use the term Ebenales, preferring a more modern classification.
The Ebenales order included trees with valuable hardwoods and fruit-bearing species.

Etymology

From New Latin *Ebenus* (genus name: ebony) + Greek-derived suffix -ales (denoting order).
