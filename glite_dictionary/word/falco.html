Falco

1. NOUN

/ ˈfælkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈfæl.kəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the falcon family (Falconidae), containing the true falcons.
genus of true falcons

Examples

The peregrine, a member of the genus Falco, is known for its speed.
The classification of Falco includes several species of raptors.
Scientists studied the differences among various Falco species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falcon - a bird of prey in the Falconidae family

Etymology

From Latin 'falco', meaning falcon.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfælkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈfælkəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of birds of prey in the family Falconidae, comprising the true falcons.
genus of true falcons

Examples

The genus Falco includes well-known species such as the peregrine falcon and the kestrel.
Birdwatchers often study members of the Falco genus in their natural habitat.
Ornithologists have classified several swift-flying hawks under the Falco genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falcon - a bird of prey with long pointed wings

Etymology

From Latin *falco*, meaning 'falcon'.
