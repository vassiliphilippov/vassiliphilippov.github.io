goggle-eyed

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑɡəl aɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡɒɡl aɪd / (BrE)
Having eyes that are wide open or protruding, often due to surprise, amazement, or fear.
having wide, staring eyes

Examples

The children listened with goggle-eyed fascination as the magician performed his tricks.
She stood goggle-eyed at the news, unable to believe what she was hearing.
He watched the fireworks, goggle-eyed with excitement and awe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wide-eyed - having eyes wide open, often due to wonder or innocence
