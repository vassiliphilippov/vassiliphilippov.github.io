to etymologize

1. VERB

/ ˌɛtəməˌlɑdʒaɪz / (AmE), / ˌɛtɪˈmɒlədʒaɪz / (BrE)
To explain or investigate the origin and historical development of a word.
explain a word's origin

Examples

Linguists often etymologize obscure terms to uncover their hidden histories.
She attempted to etymologize every unusual word she encountered in the old manuscript.
Scholars disagree when they etymologize the origins of the word 'algebra'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etymology - the study of the origin and history of words

Etymology

From Greek etymologeō (to explain the true sense of a word), via Medieval Latin etymologizare.

2. VERB

/ ˌɛtəˈmɑlədʒaɪz / (AmE), / ˌɛtɪˈmɒlədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to explain the origin and historical development of a word or suggest its etymology
explain word origins

Examples

Many historians attempt to etymologize words found in ancient manuscripts.
She loves to etymologize unusual terms in everyday conversation.
While reading the novel, he couldn't resist the urge to etymologize several archaic words.

Synset Examples

The linguist probably etymologized the words incorrectly
Although he is not trained in this, his hobby is etymologizing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to derive - to obtain or draw from a source, especially to trace word origins

Etymology

From French étymologiser, from Greek etymología (etymology) + -ize.
