micrometeor

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊˈmiːtiər / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈmiːtɪə / (BrE)
A meteorite or meteoroid so small that it passes through Earth's atmosphere without intense heating.
very small meteoroid or meteorite

Examples

Scientists studied micrometeors collected from the upper atmosphere for hints about the solar system's origins.
Unlike larger meteorites, a micrometeor can drift down to Earth without burning up.
The satellite was damaged by a micrometeor that struck its solar panel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: micrometeoroid - a very small meteoroid in space
  • Degree Higher: meteorite - a solid piece of debris from an object, such as a comet or asteroid, that originates in outer space and survives its passage through the atmosphere to reach the surface of a planet or moon
  • Degree Higher: meteoroid - a small body moving in the solar system that would become a meteor if it entered the earth's atmosphere

Etymology

From micro- (small) + meteor.
