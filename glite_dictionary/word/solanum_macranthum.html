Solanum macranthum

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈlænəm ˌmækˈrænθəm / (AmE), / səˈleɪnəm ˌmækˈranθəm / (BrE)
A South American shrub or small tree of the nightshade family, widely cultivated in tropical regions for its ornamental flowers; not a true potato.
South American ornamental shrub or tree

Examples

The vibrant flowers of Solanum macranthum make it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
Despite its resemblance to other nightshades, Solanum macranthum is not a true potato.
Many gardeners cultivate Solanum macranthum for its ornamental value rather than its fruit.

Etymology

From Latin genus name Solanum and species macranthum, meaning 'large-flowered'.
