Indicatoridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dɪˌkeɪ.təˈrɪd.iː / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˌkeɪ.təˈrɪd.ɪ.iː / (BrE)
A family of small Old World birds known as honeyguides, some species of which lead humans to beehives.
family of birds called honeyguides

Examples

Members of the Indicatoridae family are noted for their unusual behavior of guiding animals to bee nests.
The Indicatoridae are native to sub-Saharan Africa and parts of Asia.
Ornithologists have long studied the cooperative behavior exhibited by Indicatoridae species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeyguide - a bird belonging to the Indicatoridae family, known for leading humans to beehives

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Indicator' (type genus) and Greek '-idae' (denoting animal families).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dɪˌkeɪ.təˈrɪd.iː / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˌkeɪ.təˈrɪd.iː / (BrE)
a family of small Old World birds known as honeyguides, which are noted for their habit of leading humans or animals to bee nests.
family of birds: honeyguides

Examples

Several species within the Indicatoridae have evolved a unique relationship with humans and animals.
The family Indicatoridae is native to sub-Saharan Africa and parts of Asia.
Researchers are fascinated by the interaction between Indicatoridae birds and local honey-hunters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeyguide - a type of bird in the family Indicatoridae, known for its guiding behavior

Etymology

From New Latin Indicatoridae, based on the type genus Indicator, from Latin 'indicator' (one who points out or indicates).
