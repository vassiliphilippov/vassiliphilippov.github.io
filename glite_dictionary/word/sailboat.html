sailboat

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈseɪlˌbəʊt / (BrE)
a small boat propelled by sails, typically with a single mast
small boat powered by sails

Examples

The children watched in awe as a sailboat glided gracefully across the lake.
He spent his weekends restoring an old sailboat he bought last summer.
During the race, each sailboat tried to catch the best breeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yacht - a medium-sized or large pleasure boat powered by sail or engine
  • Antonym: motorboat - a boat powered by an engine, not by sails

Etymology

Formed from 'sail' and 'boat' in English.
