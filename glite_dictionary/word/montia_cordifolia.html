Montia cordifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɒn.ti.ə ˌkɔːr.dɪˈfoʊ.li.ə / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.tɪ.ə ˌkɔː.dɪˈfəʊ.lɪ.ə / (BrE)
A perennial succulent plant native to western North America, with mostly basal, heart-shaped leaves and loose clusters of small white flowers.
a North American succulent plant with heart-shaped leaves

Examples

The shady woodland was carpeted with Montia cordifolia in full bloom.
Gardeners in the Pacific Northwest often cultivate Montia cordifolia for its attractive foliage.
Botanists surveyed populations of Montia cordifolia along the mountain streams.

Etymology

From genus name 'Montia' (after Giuseppe Monti, Italian botanist) and Latin 'cordifolia' (heart-leaved, from 'cor' meaning heart and 'folium' meaning leaf).
