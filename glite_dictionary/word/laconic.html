laconic

1. ADJ

/ ləˈkɑnɪk / (AmE), / ləˈkɒnɪk / (BrE)
using very few words; concise to the point of seeming rude or mysterious.
using few words; concise

Examples

His laconic answers made conversation difficult.
When asked about his plans, he gave a laconic 'no comment'.
The coach's laconic instructions were clear and effective.

Synset Examples

a crisp retort
a response so curt as to be almost rude
the laconic reply: ‘yes’
short and terse and easy to understand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terse - using few words; brief and direct
  • Antonym: verbose - using more words than needed; wordy

Etymology

From Latin 'Laconicus', from Greek 'Lakonikos', relating to Laconia (Sparta), whose people were known for their terse speech.
