Archeopteryx

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrkioʊˈtɜːrɪks / (AmE), / ˌɑːkiˈɒptərɪks / (BrE)
an extinct genus of primitive toothed birds from the Jurassic period, recognized as one of the earliest known birds and possessing both avian and reptilian features
extinct early bird genus from the Jurassic

Examples

The fossil of an Archeopteryx revealed both bird-like feathers and reptilian features.
Scientists long debated whether Archeopteryx could actually fly or just glide between trees.
Paleontologists view Archeopteryx as a crucial link in the evolution of modern birds.

Etymology

From German 'Archaeopteryx', from Greek 'arkhaios' (ancient) + 'pteryx' (feather, wing).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑrkiˈɑptərɪks / (AmE), / ˌɑːkiˈɒptərɪks / (BrE)
an extinct genus of early bird-like dinosaurs, considered transitional between non-avian dinosaurs and modern birds, known from the Late Jurassic period
transitional early bird-like dinosaur genus

Examples

The discovery of Archeopteryx provided crucial evidence for the theory of evolution.
Paleontologists believe Archeopteryx had both reptilian and avian characteristics.
Fossils of Archeopteryx have been found in Germany dating to the Late Jurassic period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Archaeopteryx - alternate spelling of Archeopteryx, transitional fossil genus

Etymology

From Greek 'arkhaios' (ancient) + 'pteryx' (feather or wing).
