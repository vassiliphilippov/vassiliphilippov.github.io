to amend

1. VERB

/ əˈmɛnd / (AmE), / əˈmɛnd / (BrE)
to make changes to a document, law, or statement, usually to improve it or correct errors
make changes to improve or correct

Examples

The committee decided to amend the policy after considering the feedback.
Congress voted to amend the Constitution.
You can amend your application before submitting it.

Synset Examples

amend the document

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to modify - to make minor changes to something
  • Synonym: correct - to remove errors or faults
  • Antonym: damage - to harm or spoil something

Etymology

From Old French 'amender', from Latin 'emendare' (to free from fault).

3. VERB

/ əˈmɛnd / (AmE), / əˈmɛnd / (BrE)
to change something, especially a document or law, in order to make it more accurate, fair, or correct a fault
make changes to improve or correct

Examples

The city council voted to amend the regulations to better protect pedestrians.
She decided to amend her earlier statement after reviewing the new evidence.
The contract was amended to include additional conditions suggested by the client.

Synset Examples

remedy these deficiencies
rectify the inequities in salaries
repair an oversight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rectify - to put right; to correct
  • Synonym: remedy - to set right, especially a wrong or difficulty
  • Antonym: to worsen - to make or become worse

Etymology

From Old French 'amender', from Latin 'emendare' (to correct, free from fault).
