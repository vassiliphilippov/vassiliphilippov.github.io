epikeratophakia

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːpiːkɛˌrætəˈfeɪkiə / (AmE), / ˌiːpɪˌkɛrətəˈfeɪkiə / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a piece of donor corneal tissue is grafted onto the eye to restore vision, often following cataract removal
corneal graft surgery after cataract removal
medical

Examples

After losing her sight to cataracts, she underwent epikeratophakia to regain partial vision.
The ophthalmologist suggested epikeratophakia as an alternative to a full corneal transplant.
Advancements in epikeratophakia have improved the outcomes for many patients with severe corneal damage.

Synset Examples

epikeratophakia gave her a living contact lens

Etymology

From Greek 'epi' (upon), 'kerato' (cornea), and 'phakia' (lens).
