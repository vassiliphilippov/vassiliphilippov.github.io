plumbable

1. ADJ

/ ˈplʌməbəl / (AmE), / ˈplʌməb(ə)l / (BrE)
(of a depth or body of water) capable of being measured or sounded to determine its depth.
capable of being measured for depth

Examples

The lake was barely plumbable due to thick vegetation along the shore.
Make sure the area is plumbable before lowering any measuring instrument.
Some sea caves are not plumbable because of their narrow, winding entrances.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unplumbable - not capable of being measured for depth

Etymology

From 'plumb' (to measure depth) + -able (capable of).
