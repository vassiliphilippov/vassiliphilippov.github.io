miscegenation

1. NOUN

/ mɪˌsɛdʒ.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / mɪˌsɛdʒ.ɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the interbreeding of people considered to be of different racial backgrounds, especially as a social or historical phenomenon
interbreeding of people of different races

Examples

Laws prohibiting miscegenation were enforced in several countries until the late 20th century.
Discussions of miscegenation often reveal underlying social and historical tensions.
The film addressed the impact of miscegenation bans on families in the segregation era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interracial marriage - marriage between people of different races

Etymology

From Latin miscere (to mix) + genus (race, kind). Coined in 19th-century America.
