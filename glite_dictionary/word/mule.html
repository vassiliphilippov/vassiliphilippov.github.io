mule

1. NOUN

/ mjuﾋ人 / (AmE), / mjuﾋ人 / (BrE)
A hybrid animal that is the offspring of a male donkey and a female horse, usually sterile.
hybrid offspring of donkey and horse

Examples

The mule is prized for its strength and endurance in agricultural work.
Unlike horses, a mule cannot usually reproduce.
Farmers often prefer using a mule for carrying heavy loads up mountainous terrain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hinny - hybrid offspring of a male horse and a female donkey

Etymology

From Old French 'mul', from Latin 'mulus' (mule).

2. NOUN

/ mjuﾋ人 / (AmE), / mjuﾋ人 / (BrE)
a slipper or shoe without a back or constraint around the heel, leaving the heel exposed
slipper or shoe without a heel back

Examples

She slipped on her favorite mules before leaving the bedroom.
He bought a pair of stylish mules for the summer season.
Those soft mules are perfect for wearing around the house.

Etymology

From French *mule*, from Latin *mula* (female mule), possibly by analogy to the animal's hybrid nature; applied to backless slippers in the 16th century.
