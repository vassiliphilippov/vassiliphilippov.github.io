poor

1. ADJ

/ pɔr / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
lacking sufficient quality or desirable substances; not meeting an expected standard
lacking quality or substance

Examples

Despite beautiful scenery, the soil there is poor and unsuitable for farming.
Students complained that the cafeteria meals were poor in both taste and nutrition.
The region is poor in natural resources, with little timber or mineral wealth.

Synset Examples

a poor land
the area was poor in timber and coal
food poor in nutritive value
the food in the cafeteria was of poor quality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inferior - of lower quality or standard
  • Synonym: deficient - lacking in some necessary quality or element
  • Antonym: rich - containing much of something, especially nutrients or quality

Etymology

From Old French *povre*, from Latin *pauper* (poor, not wealthy).
