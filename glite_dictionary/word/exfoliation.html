exfoliation

1. NOUN

/ ɛksˌfoʊliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɛksˌfəʊliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a thin flake of dead skin shed from the surface of the body
thin flake of dead skin
medical

Examples

After her sunburn, small exfoliations could be seen on her shoulders.
The dermatologist noted several exfoliations on the patient’s arms.
Regular use of moisturizer can help prevent exfoliation of dry skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scale - a thin flat piece of dead skin that peels off

Etymology

From Latin 'exfoliare' (to strip of leaves), from 'ex-' (out) + 'folium' (leaf).

2. NOUN

/ ɛkˌfoʊliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɛksˌfəʊliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of shedding or peeling off in thin flakes or scales, especially of the outer layer of skin or bark
shedding of skin or bark in flakes

Examples

Sun damage can lead to increased exfoliation of the skin.
Some trees experience natural exfoliation of their bark, revealing colorful layers underneath.
Regular exfoliation helps remove dead skin cells and keep your complexion smooth.

Synset Examples

exfoliation is increased by sunburn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desquamation - the shedding of the outer layers of the skin

Etymology

From Latin 'exfoliare,' from 'ex-' (out) + 'folium' (leaf).
