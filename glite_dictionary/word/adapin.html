Adapin

1. NOUN

/ ˈædəˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈædəˌpɪn / (BrE)
Trade name for a tricyclic antidepressant used to treat depression and anxiety disorders, known for side effects such as dry mouth and sedation.
tricyclic antidepressant (trade name)
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed Adapin to help manage my depression.
Some patients experience drowsiness as a side effect of Adapin.
Adapin is sometimes used in the treatment of anxiety disorders as well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doxepin - the generic form of Adapin, a tricyclic antidepressant
  • Synonym: Sinequan - another trade name for doxepin, a tricyclic antidepressant
