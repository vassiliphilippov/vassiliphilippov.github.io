to hot-wire

1. VERB

/ ˈhɑtˌwaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɒtˌwaɪə / (BrE)
to start a vehicle's engine without a key by bypassing the ignition system, usually by connecting wires directly
start a vehicle without a key by connecting wires

Examples

After losing his keys, he had to hot-wire the car to get home.
The thief managed to hot-wire the truck in less than a minute.
She was surprised to learn how easy it was to hot-wire an old vehicle.

Synset Examples

The woman who lost the car keys had to hot-wire her van

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take something without permission, especially unlawfully

Etymology

From 'hot' + 'wire', referring to connecting live wires to start the engine.
