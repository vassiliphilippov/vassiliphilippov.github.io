preconceived

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriːkənˈsiːvd / (AmE), / ˌpriːkɒnˈsiːvd / (BrE)
(of an idea or opinion) formed before having evidence or experience, especially due to bias or prejudice.
formed without evidence or prior to experience

Examples

Her preconceived notions about the city changed after she visited it.
It's important to avoid preconceived ideas when evaluating new evidence.
Many people hold preconceived opinions without realizing their origins.

Synset Examples

certain preconceived notions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudiced - having or showing a dislike or distrust that is derived from prejudice; biased
  • Antonym: impartial - treating all rivals or disputants equally; fair and just

Etymology

From Latin 'prae-' (before) + 'concipere' (to conceive).
