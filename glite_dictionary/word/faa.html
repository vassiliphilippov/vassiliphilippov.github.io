FAA

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛf.eɪˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛf.eɪˈeɪ / (BrE)
the Federal Aviation Administration, a US government agency responsible for regulating civil aviation and promoting air safety
US agency for aviation safety
american

Examples

The FAA implemented new safety regulations for commercial flights last year.
Pilots are required to comply with FAA guidelines on flight operations.
The FAA investigates any incidents involving aircraft within U.S. airspace.

Etymology

Acronym for Federal Aviation Administration.
