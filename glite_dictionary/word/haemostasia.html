haemostasia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmoʊˈsteɪziə / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈsteɪzɪə / (BrE)
A surgical procedure for stopping the flow of blood, typically by means of instruments or medication.
surgical stopping of blood flow
medical british

Examples

The surgeon ensured haemostasia was achieved before closing the wound.
Prompt haemostasia is critical in managing major trauma cases.
Various techniques can be employed for haemostasia during an operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemostasis - the process of stopping bleeding; can refer to both natural and surgical processes

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + stasis (stopping, standing).
