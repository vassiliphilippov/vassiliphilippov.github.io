Oreamnos

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈriːæmnɒs / (AmE), / ɔːˈriːamnɒs / (BrE)
A genus of North American mammals comprising the mountain goat.
genus of mountain goats

Examples

The genus Oreamnos includes the American mountain goat, found in the Rocky Mountains.
Biologists discovered several adaptations unique to Oreamnos in their high-altitude habitat.
Oreamnos is well known for its thick white coat and ability to climb steep cliffs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain goat - a large, white, goatlike mammal native to North American mountains

Etymology

From Greek oreos (mountain) and amnos (lamb).

2. NOUN

/ ɔˈriːæmnɒs / (AmE), / ɔːˈriːamnɒs / (BrE)
A genus of North American mountain goats, including species adapted to mountainous regions.
genus of mountain goats

Examples

The genus Oreamnos is represented today by a single living species, the mountain goat.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Oreamnos in the Rocky Mountains.
Fossil evidence shows that Oreamnos once had a wider historical range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain goat - a species of goat found in North American mountains (Oreamnos americanus)

Etymology

From Greek 'oreas' (mountain) and 'amnos' (lamb).
