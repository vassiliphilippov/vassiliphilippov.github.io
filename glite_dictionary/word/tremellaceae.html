Tremellaceae

1. NOUN

/ trɛˌmɛləˈsiː.i / (AmE), / trɛmɛləˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of basidiomycete fungi in the order Tremellales, characterized by basidia divided longitudinally
family of fungi in Tremellales

Examples

Members of the Tremellaceae are known for their gelatinous fruiting bodies.
Recent studies have reclassified several species within the Tremellaceae.
The Tremellaceae includes fungi that often live as parasites on other fungi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tremella - the type genus of the family Tremellaceae

Etymology

From the genus Tremella, combined with the standard family suffix -aceae in scientific Latin.

a family of fungi in the order Tremellales
medical

Examples

Several species within the Tremellaceae family are known for their gelatinous fruiting bodies.
Researchers studied the Tremellaceae under the microscope to observe their unique basidia.
The Tremellaceae include fungi often found on decaying wood in moist environments.

Etymology

From the genus Tremella, from Latin 'tremere' meaning 'to tremble', with the family suffix '-aceae'.
