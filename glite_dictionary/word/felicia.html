Felicia

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɪʃiə / (AmE), / fəˈlɪsɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical African herbs or subshrubs, typically with blue flowers, in the family Asteraceae.
genus of African flowering plants

Examples

Many gardeners favor planting Felicia for its vibrant blue blossoms during the summer.
Several species of Felicia can thrive in both sandy soil and full sunlight.
The botanical garden features a variety of Felicia among its African plants collection.

Etymology

Named after Felice Massalongo (1824–1860), Italian botanist.

2. NOUN

/ fəˈlɪʃiə / (AmE), / fəˈlɪsiə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical African annual or perennial herbs and subshrubs, usually with blue flowers, in the family Asteraceae.
genus of African herbs and subshrubs

Examples

Many species of Felicia are prized for their vibrant blue flowers in gardens.
The Felicia genus is native to southern Africa and is widely cultivated.
Botanists recognize more than eighty species belonging to the Felicia genus.

Etymology

Named after Fortunato Felice, an 18th-century Italian naturalist.
