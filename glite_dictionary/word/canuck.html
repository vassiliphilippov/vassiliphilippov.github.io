Canuck

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnʌk / (AmE), / kəˈnʌk / (BrE)
an informal term for a Canadian, sometimes referring particularly to a French Canadian
informal term for a Canadian
informal

Examples

Many people are surprised to learn that the famous actor is a proud Canuck.
The hockey team, made up almost entirely of Canucks, dominated the international tournament.
During her travels abroad, she met another Canuck in Paris and they quickly became friends.

Etymology

Probably from American English, first recorded in the 19th century; origin uncertain, but perhaps an alteration of 'Canada' or influenced by Algonquian languages.
