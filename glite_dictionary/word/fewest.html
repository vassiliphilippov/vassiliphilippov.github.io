fewest

1. ADJ

/ ˈfjuɪst / (AmE), / ˈfjuːɪst / (BrE)
Superlative form of 'few'; the smallest number of people or things.
smallest number (superlative of 'few')

Examples

Of all the teams, ours had the fewest players available for the match.
She made the fewest mistakes on the test and received a perfect score.
The new policy resulted in the fewest complaints from residents in years.

Synset Examples

the fewest birds in recent memory

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: few - small in number; not many
  • Antonym: most - the greatest in number (superlative of 'many')

Etymology

Superlative form of 'few', from Old English 'fēawe.'
