rhythmic

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪð.mɪk / (AmE), / ˈrɪð.mɪk / (BrE)
having a regular, repeated pattern of sounds or movements
having a regular pattern

Examples

The rhythmic clapping of the audience filled the concert hall.
Her rhythmic breathing helped her relax before the race.
The poet’s verses have a rhythmic quality that makes them pleasant to read aloud.

Synset Examples

the rhythmic chiming of church bells
rhythmical prose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhythmical - having rhythm; alternate form of 'rhythmic'

Etymology

From Greek *rhythmos* (measured flow, movement).
