WWW

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbəl.juː ˌdʌbəl.juː ˌdʌbəl.juː / (AmE), / ˌdʌbl.juː ˌdʌbl.juː ˌdʌbl.juː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for the World Wide Web, a system of interlinked hypertext documents and resources accessed via the internet using the HTTP protocol
abbreviation of 'World Wide Web' (internet system)

Examples

You can find an endless amount of information on the WWW.
Many websites begin their addresses with WWW.
The WWW has transformed the way people communicate and access information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: World Wide Web - an information system of interlinked hypertext documents accessed via the internet

Etymology

Initialism of 'World Wide Web,' coined in 1989 by Tim Berners-Lee.
