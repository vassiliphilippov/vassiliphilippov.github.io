septal

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛp.təl / (AmE), / ˈsɛp.təl / (BrE)
Relating to a septum, especially in anatomical contexts.
relating to a septum
medical

Examples

The doctor noted a septal defect in the infant’s heart.
Nasal septal surgery may be necessary to improve breathing.
Microscopic examination revealed thickening of the septal wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: septum - a partition separating two chambers, such as in the heart or nose

Etymology

From Latin septum (partition), with the adjective-forming suffix -al.
