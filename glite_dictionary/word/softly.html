softly

1. ADV

/ ˈsɔftli / (AmE), / ˈsɒftli / (BrE)
In a quiet or low voice; with little volume.
in a quiet or low voice

Examples

She closed the door softly so as not to wake anyone.
The music played softly in the background during dinner.
He asked his question softly, barely above a whisper.

Synset Examples

speak softly but carry a big stick
she spoke quietly to the child
the radio was playing softly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quietly - in a quiet manner; with little noise

Etymology

From 'soft' + '-ly'; 'soft' from Old English 'sōfte'.

2. ADV

/ ˈsɔftli / (AmE), / ˈsɒftli / (BrE)
(used as a musical direction) to be played or sung in a quiet or gentle manner, at a low volume.
quietly; at low volume (music)

Examples

The instructions said to play the passage softly throughout the movement.
She asked the pianist to begin the song softly and gradually grow louder.
When indicated, the musicians transitioned softly into the next section.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piano - (in music) played or sung softly or quietly
  • Antonym: loudly - with a great deal of volume
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00345456-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈsɔft.li / (AmE), / ˈsɒft.li / (BrE)
In a gentle way, with little weight or force.
in a gentle or low-force manner

Examples

She shut the door softly so as not to wake the baby.
He spoke softly, afraid of disturbing the silence in the room.
The snow fell softly onto the ground, covering everything in white.

Synset Examples

she kissed him lightly on the forehead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gently - in a mild, careful, or tender manner
  • Synonym: lightly - with little weight, force, or intensity
  • Antonym: firmly - with strength or force

Etymology

From "soft" + "-ly," Old English "softe" + "-līce".
