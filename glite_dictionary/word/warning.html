warning

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɔrnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
Giving a caution, signal, or indication to prevent danger or harm.
giving a signal of caution or danger

Examples

The warning signs on the road helped drivers avoid the construction zone.
He ignored the warning lights on the dashboard and kept driving.
Schools sent out a warning letter to parents about the upcoming storm.

Synset Examples

shook a monitory finger at him
an exemplary jail sentence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cautionary - serving as a warning or alarm
  • Synonym: advisory - giving advice or a warning

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
a statement or indication that alerts someone to a possible danger or problem
caution or statement about possible danger

Examples

The weather service issued a warning about the approaching storm.
She gave me a warning not to touch the hot stove.
There was no warning before the sudden blackout.

Synset Examples

a letter of admonition about the dangers of immorality
the warning was to beware of surprises
his final word of advice was not to play with matches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caution - care or warning to avoid danger or mistakes
  • Synonym: alert - a warning of danger, especially in military or emergency contexts
  • Antonym: threat - a statement of an intention to cause harm

Etymology

From Middle English 'warnyng', from Old English 'warnian' (to warn) + '-ing'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
A statement or sign that gives notice or advice about a potential danger or problem, usually before it happens.
advance notification or caution

Examples

The weather service issued a warning about the approaching storm.
She gave him a warning not to touch the freshly painted wall.
Without any warning, the fire alarm started ringing.

Synset Examples

they gave little warning of their arrival
she had only had four days' warning before leaving Berlin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alert - a signal that warns of danger or the need for action
  • Synonym: caution - care taken to avoid danger or mistakes; warning
  • Synonym: notice - advanced information or notification of something
  • Antonym: surprise - an unexpected event with no prior warning

Etymology

From Middle English warnyng, from warnen (to warn) + -ing.

4. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
a statement or message that informs people of possible danger, problems, or other unpleasant things that may happen
statement alerting to danger or problems

Examples

The weather service issued a warning about the approaching storm.
He ignored the warning on the package and suffered an allergic reaction.
A loud alarm served as a warning that the factory equipment was overheating.

Synset Examples

a warning that still more bombs could explode

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alert - notice given to warn of danger or emergency
  • Synonym: caution - care or warning to avoid danger or mistakes
  • Antonym: encouragement - words or actions that give support or confidence

Etymology

From Old English 'wærnung', from 'warnian' (to warn).
