betaine

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiː.təˌiːn / (AmE), / ˈbiː.tə.iːn / (BrE)
a naturally occurring compound with a sweet taste, found especially in sugar beets and used in biochemistry and nutrition
naturally occurring compound in sugar beets

Examples

Betaine is commonly used as a dietary supplement to support liver function.
Sugar beets are the primary commercial source of betaine in the food industry.
The scientist measured the amount of betaine present in the sample.

Etymology

From Greek 'beet,' via its presence in sugar beets.
