Rangifer caribou

1. NOUN

/ ˈræn.dʒɪ.fər kəˈriː.bu / (AmE), / ˈran.dʒɪ.fə kaˈriː.buː / (BrE)
A large caribou living in coniferous forests of southern Canada, sometimes classified as part of Rangifer tarandus.
a type of caribou in southern Canada

Examples

The Rangifer caribou is well adapted to the coniferous forests of southern Canada.
Researchers are monitoring the migration patterns of the Rangifer caribou to better understand their habitat needs.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the dwindling numbers of Rangifer caribou in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodland caribou - A common term for Rangifer caribou, a subspecies or population of caribou
  • Degree Higher: Rangifer tarandus - The species to which Rangifer caribou is sometimes considered to belong

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Latin 'rangifer' (reindeer) and the North American term 'caribou'.
