to antiquate

1. VERB

/ ˈæn.tɪ.kweɪt / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪ.kweɪt / (BrE)
to cause something to become outdated, obsolete, or old-fashioned
make obsolete or outdated

Examples

The rapid development of new technology will antiquate many traditional methods.
Society's changing values can antiquate customs that were once common practice.
Certain laws have been antiquated by advances in civil rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsolete - no longer produced or used; out of date

Etymology

From Latin 'antiquatus', past participle of 'antiquare' (to make old), from 'antiquus' (ancient).

2. VERB

/ ˈæn.tɪˌkweɪt / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪ.kweɪt / (BrE)
to give an antique appearance to something
give an antique appearance

Examples

The designer decided to antiquate the cabinet to match the room's vintage theme.
Artisans often antiquate picture frames to complement classical artworks.
Some manufacturers use special treatments to antiquate new furniture for a rustic look.

Synset Examples

antique furniture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antique - make (something) look old or from a previous era

Etymology

From Latin 'antiquatus', past participle of 'antiquare' (to make old), from 'antiquus' (ancient).
