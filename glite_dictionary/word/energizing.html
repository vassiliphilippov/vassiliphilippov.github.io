energizing

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛnərˌdʒaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒʌɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
providing or supplying energy, power, or motive force.
providing energy or motive force

Examples

The coach gave an energizing speech before the game to motivate the team.
This beverage contains energizing ingredients intended to boost alertness and stamina.
The music had an energizing effect on everyone at the party.

Synset Examples

the complex civilization of which Rome was the kinetic center

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulating - making someone feel more active or alert
  • Antonym: debilitating - making someone weak or feeble

Etymology

From 'energize', based on Greek 'energein' (to operate, work) via 'energy'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛnərˌdʒaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛnəˌdʒʌɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of causing someone or something to become more energetic or active
the act of giving energy or activity

Examples

A morning walk can be an energizing way to start your day.
The energizing effects of the new project were felt across the team.
Yoga classes often include energizing breathing exercises to boost vitality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invigorating - the act of making someone feel more lively or refreshed

Etymology

From the verb 'energize', which comes from Greek 'energein' (to be in action).
