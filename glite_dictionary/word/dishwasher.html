dishwasher

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪʃˌwɑʃər / (AmE), / ˈdɪʃˌwɒʃə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to wash dishes, especially in a restaurant or kitchen
person who washes dishes

Examples

She started her job as a dishwasher in a busy city restaurant.
The dishwasher worked hard throughout the evening to keep up with the dirty plates.
Many students find part-time work as a dishwasher to support themselves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kitchen porter - a person employed to do menial tasks in a kitchen, including washing dishes

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪʃˌwɑːʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɪʃˌwɒʃə / (BrE)
A machine used for washing dishes, cookware, and utensils automatically.
machine for washing dishes automatically

Examples

After dinner, we loaded all the plates into the dishwasher.
Our new dishwasher is much quieter than the old one.
If you don’t have a dishwasher, you’ll have to wash the dishes by hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: washing machine - machine for washing clothes
  • Antonym: sink - Basin for washing dishes by hand

Etymology

Formed from 'dish' + 'washer', denoting a person or thing that washes dishes; applied to the machine since mid-20th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪʃˌwɑʃər / (AmE), / ˈdɪʃˌwɒʃə / (BrE)
a machine that washes plates, cutlery, and other kitchenware automatically
machine for washing dishes

Examples

We finally bought a new dishwasher to save time on cleaning up after dinner.
The dishwasher was full, so I couldn't add any more plates.
After the party, we loaded all the glasses into the dishwasher for easy cleaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: washing machine - machine for washing clothes automatically

Etymology

From 'dish' + 'washer', first used in the late 19th century for people who wash dishes; now more commonly refers to the machine.
