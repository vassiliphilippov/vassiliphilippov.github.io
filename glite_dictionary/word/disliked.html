disliked

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈlaɪkt / (AmE), / dɪsˈlaɪkt / (BrE)
Regarded with aversion or distaste; not liked.
regarded with aversion; not liked

Examples

Despite his achievements, the politician remained disliked by many voters.
She always felt disliked when her classmates ignored her ideas.
The new manager quickly became the most disliked person in the office.

Synset Examples

he was intensely disliked

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liked - regarded with pleasure or approval
  • Degree Higher: hated - regarded with intense dislike or hatred
  • Degree Lower: tolerated - endured or allowed without strong feeling

Etymology

From the verb 'dislike' + -ed.
