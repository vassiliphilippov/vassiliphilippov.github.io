hearing

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
an official meeting where someone has the opportunity to present their case or viewpoint before a decision is made
official opportunity to present one's case

Examples

The court set a hearing for next Monday to review the evidence.
She requested a hearing before the disciplinary committee to explain her actions.
Without a proper hearing, the accused was denied the chance to defend himself.

Synset Examples

they condemned him without a hearing
he saw that he had lost his audience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trial - a formal examination of evidence in court
  • Synonym: inquiry - an official investigation or hearing

Etymology

From Old English *hēran* (to hear) + -ing (verbal noun suffix).
