Pygoscelis adeliae

1. NOUN

/ paɪɡəˈsiːlɪs ˌædəˈliːiː / (AmE), / paɪɡəˈsiːlɪs ˌædəˈliːiː / (BrE)
A species of medium-sized penguin native to Antarctica, known for forming large breeding colonies along the Adelie Coast.
species of Antarctic penguin

Examples

Researchers observed a large group of Pygoscelis adeliae nesting along the Antarctic coast.
The diet of Pygoscelis adeliae mainly consists of krill and small fish.
Climate change could significantly impact the habitat of Pygoscelis adeliae in the coming decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Adelie penguin - Common name for Pygoscelis adeliae, an Antarctic penguin species

Etymology

From Greek 'pygos' (rump) and 'skelos' (leg), and from 'Adélie', the French name of the coast named by explorer Dumont d'Urville.
