Mary Harris Jones

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛɹi ˈhæɹɪs dʒoʊnz / (AmE), / ˈmæɹi ˈhærɪs dʒəʊnz / (BrE)
An Irish-born American labor leader (1830–1930), known for her role in founding the Industrial Workers of the World and advocating for workers' rights.
Irish-American labor leader, activist (1830–1930)

Examples

Mary Harris Jones led several important strikes to improve conditions for workers in the early 20th century.
As a passionate activist, Mary Harris Jones earned the nickname 'Mother Jones' among miners.
The legacy of Mary Harris Jones continues to inspire labor movements around the world.
