electrophoresis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊfəˈriːsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊfəˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
the movement of charged particles through a fluid or gel under the influence of an electric field, often used for separating mixtures in laboratory settings
movement of charged particles in an electric field
medical

Examples

The scientist used electrophoresis to separate DNA fragments based on their size.
During electrophoresis, the negatively charged molecules migrated toward the positive electrode.
In the lab, protein samples are analyzed with electrophoresis to identify different components.

Etymology

From Greek 'ēlektron' (amber) + 'phoresis' (carrying).
