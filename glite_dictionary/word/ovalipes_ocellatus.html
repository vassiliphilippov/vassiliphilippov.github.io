Ovalipes ocellatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvəˈlaɪpiːz oʊˈsɛlətəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈlaɪpiːz əʊˈsɛlətəs / (BrE)
a species of brightly spotted crab found on sandy beaches along the Atlantic coast of the United States
a species of brightly spotted Atlantic beach crab

Examples

The Ovalipes ocellatus is commonly seen burrowing in the sands of New Jersey's beaches.
Marine biologists study the Ovalipes ocellatus to understand its role in coastal ecosystems.
The shell of the Ovalipes ocellatus features distinctive bright spots that make it easy to identify.

Etymology

From Latin: Ovalipes (genus name, meaning 'oval-footed') and ocellatus (spotted, from 'ocellus', a small eye/spot).
