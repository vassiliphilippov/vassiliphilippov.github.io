paragraph

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈparəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A distinct section of a piece of writing, usually dealing with a single theme and indicated by a new line, indentation, or numbering.
a section of writing in text

Examples

Each paragraph in the essay should focus on a single main idea.
She rewrote the introduction, breaking it into shorter paragraphs for clarity.
Please indent the first line of every new paragraph in your report.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sentence - a set of words that expresses a complete thought
  • Degree Higher: chapter - a main division of a book, typically with a number or title

Etymology

From French *paragraphe*, from Late Latin *paragraphus*, from Greek *paragraphos* (written mark beside text).

2. VERB

/ ˈpæɹəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈpærəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
to divide written text into paragraphs
divide text into paragraphs

Examples

The teacher instructed the students to paragraph their essays more clearly.
After the first draft, I paragraphed the story to improve readability.
Editors often paragraph long articles before publication.

Synset Examples

This story is well paragraphed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: format - arrange or organize text or data in a particular way

Etymology

From French 'paragraphe', from Late Latin 'paragraphus' (short stroke in writing to mark change of subject).
