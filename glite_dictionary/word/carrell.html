carrell

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærəl / (AmE), / ˈkarəl / (BrE)
a small individual study area in a library, often with a desk and partitions for privacy
individual library study area

Examples

She reserved a carrell in the library to help her focus on her studies.
Each carrell is equipped with a reading lamp and electrical outlets for laptops.
Students appreciate the quiet and privacy offered by a carrell during exam periods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cubicle - a small, partitioned-off area of a room, often in an office

Etymology

From Middle English *carole*, from Latin *carula* (small enclosure).
