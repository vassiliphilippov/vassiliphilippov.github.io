Salvia verbenaca

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæl.vi.ə vərˈbɛn.ə.kə / (AmE), / ˌsal.vɪ.ə vɜːˈbɛn.ə.kə / (BrE)
A species of sage native to Eurasia, with blue flowers and foliage resembling verbena, now naturalized in the United States.
a Eurasian sage with blue flowers

Examples

Salvia verbenaca is often used as an ornamental plant in Mediterranean gardens.
The blue flowers of Salvia verbenaca attract many pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
In some regions, Salvia verbenaca has naturalized and grows wild along roadsides.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Salvia' meaning sage, and 'verbenaca,' resembling verbena.
