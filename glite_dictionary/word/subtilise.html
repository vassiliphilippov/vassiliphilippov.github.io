to subtilise

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌb.təˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.tɪ.laɪz / (BrE)
to make (the senses, perception, or intellect) more keen, refined, or subtle
make more keen or refined

Examples

Years of training helped subtilise his sense of taste until he could identify the slightest hint of spice.
Meditation is said to subtilise the mind, increasing awareness and clarity.
Repeated exposure to fine music can subtilise one’s hearing, making every nuance distinct.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refine - to improve something by making it more pure or subtle
  • Antonym: blunt - to make something less sharp or keen

Etymology

From Medieval Latin subtilisare, from Latin subtilis (fine, thin, subtle). The spelling reflects English adoption of the French 'subtiliser'.
