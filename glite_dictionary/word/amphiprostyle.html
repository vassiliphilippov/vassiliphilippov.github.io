amphiprostyle

1. ADJ

/ ˌæmfɪˈproʊstaɪl / (AmE), / ˌamfɪˈprəʊstaɪl / (BrE)
(of a building) having porticoes with free-standing columns at both ends, but not along the sides.
architecture: columns at both ends

Examples

The ancient temple was designed in the amphiprostyle manner, with columns only at the front and rear.
Some of the most famous Greek structures are amphiprostyle, giving a balanced appearance to their facades.
The architect chose an amphiprostyle layout to emphasize both entrances of the building.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: prostyle - columns in front only, not both ends
  • Degree Higher: peripteral - columns surrounding all sides

Etymology

From Greek 'amphi-' (on both sides) + 'prostylos' (having columns in front).
