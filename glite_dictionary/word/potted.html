potted

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑtɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɒtɪd / (BrE)
(of a plant) planted or grown in a pot rather than in the ground.
grown in a pot (of plants)

Examples

She placed the potted fern on the windowsill to catch the morning sun.
A row of potted herbs lined the kitchen counter, ready to be used in cooking.
They bought several potted flowers to brighten up the apartment balcony.

Synset Examples

potted geraniums

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɑt̬ɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɒtɪd / (BrE)
(of food) preserved or cooked and stored in a pot, jar, or can, often covered with fat or jelly.
preserved or cooked in a pot or jar

Examples

The picnic basket included potted salmon, which was spread on fresh bread.
She enjoyed potted meats as part of a traditional English breakfast.
You can buy potted shrimp at many specialty food shops.

Synset Examples

potted meat
potted shrimp

Etymology

From the verb 'pot' meaning to put into a pot; later extended to preserved foods.

3. ADJ

/ ˈpɑt̬ɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɒtɪd / (BrE)
(British, informal) Summarized or abridged; presented in a shortened or condensed form.
summarized or abridged (British informal)
informal british

Examples

The teacher gave us a potted history of the country's political evolution.
She bought a potted guide to Italian art before her trip.
Here's a potted version of the annual report for those in a hurry.

Synset Examples

a potted version of a novel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abridged - shortened by leaving out parts
  • Antonym: full-length - not shortened or abridged
