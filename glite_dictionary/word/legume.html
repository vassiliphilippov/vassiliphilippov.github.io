legume

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛɡ.jum / (AmE), / ˈlɛɡ.juːm / (BrE)
the seedpod of a leguminous plant, such as peas, beans, or lentils, often used as food
the seedpod of plants like peas or beans

Examples

Chickpeas are a type of legume commonly used in Middle Eastern dishes.
The farmer planted several varieties of legume to enrich the soil.
Eating a legume every day can provide important nutrients such as protein and fiber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulse - an edible seed from a leguminous plant, especially dried beans and lentils
  • Synonym: vegetable - a plant or part of a plant used as food

Etymology

From Latin 'legumen' (pulse, bean, seedpod).

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈɡum / (AmE), / lɪˈɡjuːm / (BrE)
the fruit or seed of plants in the pea family, typically contained in a pod that splits open on both sides when ripe
fruit or seed of a pea or bean plant

Examples

Chickpeas are a type of legume commonly used in Mediterranean cuisine.
After harvesting, the legumes were dried and stored for the winter.
Eating at least one serving of legume each day can improve nutrition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulse - an edible seed from a leguminous plant

Etymology

From Latin legumen (vegetable, pulse).

3. NOUN

/ ləˈɡum / (AmE), / lɪˈɡjuːm / (BrE)
a plant of the pea or bean family that typically has pods containing seeds, such as beans, lentils, or peas
plant of the pea/bean family

Examples

Chickpeas are a type of legume commonly used in Middle Eastern cuisine.
Growing a legume can help restore nitrogen to the soil.
The salad was topped with a mix of fresh legumes and greens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulse - edible seeds of leguminous plants, often used as food

Etymology

From Latin 'legumen' (pulse, leguminous plant).
