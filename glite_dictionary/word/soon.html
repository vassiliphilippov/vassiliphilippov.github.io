soon

1. ADV

/ suːn / (AmE), / suːn / (BrE)
in a short time from now; in the near future
in the near future

Examples

The meeting will begin soon, so please take your seats.
She hopes to hear back from the company soon.
They promised to fix the leak soon, but we're still waiting.

Synset Examples

the doctor will soon be here
the book will appear shortly
she will arrive presently
we should have news before long

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortly - in a short time; soon
  • Synonym: presently - in a short while; soon
  • Degree Higher: eventually - at some later time; in the end

Etymology

From Old English sōna (immediately, at once).
