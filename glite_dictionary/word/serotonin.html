serotonin

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛrəˈtoʊnɪn / (AmE), / ˌsɛrəˈtəʊnɪn / (BrE)
a neurotransmitter in the brain that influences mood, sleep, and other bodily functions
a brain neurotransmitter affecting mood and sleep
medical

Examples

Low levels of serotonin are often associated with depression and anxiety disorders.
Medications that increase serotonin levels are commonly prescribed for mood disorders.
Regular exercise can help boost your serotonin naturally.

Etymology

From sero- (serum) + tonic (affecting tone); first identified in blood serum.
