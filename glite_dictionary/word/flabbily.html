flabbily

1. ADV

/ ˈflæbɪli / (AmE), / ˈflæbɪli / (BrE)
In a way that is soft, loose, or lacking firmness; in a manner characteristic of being flabby.
in a soft or loose manner

Examples

The child’s arms hung flabbily by his sides after a long nap.
The flag drooped flabbily in the windless afternoon.
She slouched flabbily on the couch, too tired to move.

Synset Examples

the old man's muscles were sagging flabbily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flabby - soft and lacking firmness or muscle tone

Etymology

Formed from flabby + -ly.
