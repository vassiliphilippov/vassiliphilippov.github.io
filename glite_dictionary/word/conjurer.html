conjurer

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.dʒə.rɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dʒə.rə / (BrE)
A person who entertains an audience by performing magic tricks or illusions.
person performing magic tricks

Examples

The talented conjurer amazed everyone with his card tricks.
Children gathered around the conjurer at the birthday party, eager to see his next illusion.
After the show, the conjurer revealed that there was no real magic involved, only skill and practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magician - person skilled in performing magic tricks
  • Synonym: illusionist - entertainer skilled in creating illusions

Etymology

From Old French 'conjureor', from Latin 'coniūrātor' (swearer together, conspirator).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːndʒərər / (AmE), / ˈkɒndʒərə / (BrE)
A person who performs magic or practices conjury, especially one believed to use supernatural powers as a witch doctor.
person who performs magic or conjury

Examples

The village conjurer was called upon to heal the sick child.
Stories of a powerful conjurer who protected the tribe have been passed down for generations.
The conjurer performed secret rituals believed to ward off evil spirits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magician - a person with magical powers or who performs magic tricks
  • Synonym: sorcerer - a person who claims or is believed to have magic powers
  • Synonym: witch doctor - a traditional healer or practitioner of magic among some peoples

Etymology

From Middle English *conjurer* (one who conjures), from Old French *conjureor*, from Latin *conjurator* (one who swears together, conspirator), from *conjurare* (to swear together, to conspire).
