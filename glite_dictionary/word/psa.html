PSA

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiː ɛs ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌpiː ɛs ˈeɪ / (BrE)
a protein produced by the prostate gland, often measured in blood to screen for or monitor prostate conditions such as cancer or benign prostatic hyperplasia
prostate-specific antigen protein
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a blood test to check his PSA level.
An elevated PSA can be a sign of prostate cancer or benign conditions.
Men over 50 are often advised to have their PSA monitored regularly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostate-specific antigen - the full term for PSA, a protein produced by the prostate gland

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'prostate-specific antigen'.
