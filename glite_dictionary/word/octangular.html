octangular

1. ADJ

/ ɑkˈtæŋɡjələr / (AmE), / ɒkˈtaŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
having eight angles or sides; shaped like an octagon.
having eight angles or sides

Examples

The ancient coin featured an octangular design that set it apart from other currencies.
We sat around an octangular table for the meeting, each side seating two people.
The architect proposed an octangular layout for the new library, giving it a distinctive look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: octagonal - having eight sides and eight angles

Etymology

From Latin 'octangulus' (having eight corners), from 'octo' (eight) + 'angulus' (angle).
