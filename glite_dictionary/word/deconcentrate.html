to deconcentrate

1. VERB

/ ˌdiːkənˈsɛn.treɪt / (AmE), / ˌdiːkənˈsɛn.treɪt / (BrE)
to make (something) less central or less concentrated, especially by redistributing administrative powers or functions away from a central authority
make less central or concentrated

Examples

The reforms aimed to deconcentrate decision-making power within the ministry.
Efforts to deconcentrate control have led to greater autonomy for regional offices.
The government plans to deconcentrate key services to improve local responsiveness.

Synset Examples

After the revolution, food distribution was decentralized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decentralize - transfer authority from a central to a local government or organization

Etymology

From de- (opposite of) + concentrate.
