ligament

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪɡəmənt / (AmE), / ˈlɪɡəmənt / (BrE)
a strong band or sheet of tough fibrous tissue that connects bones or cartilages, or supports muscles or organs
strong connective tissue band in the body
medical

Examples

She tore a ligament in her knee while playing soccer.
The surgeon repaired the damaged ligament during the operation.
Strong ligaments help keep the joints stable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tendon - a flexible but inelastic cord of strong fibrous collagen tissue attaching a muscle to a bone

Etymology

From Latin ligamentum (a band, tie), from ligare (to bind).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪɡəmənt / (AmE), / ˈlɪɡəmənt / (BrE)
a thing that unites people or things; a bond or connection that brings them together
unifying bond or connection

Examples

Trust can act as the ligament that holds a community together.
Shared values are the ligament binding members of the organization to its mission.
The treaty provided a ligament between the two formerly opposing countries.

Etymology

From Latin 'ligamentum' (band, tie), from 'ligare' (to bind).
