superstitious

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈstɪʃ.əs / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈstɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
Believing in or influenced by superstitions, especially showing a lack of scientific understanding and relying on magic or chance.
believing in superstitions, magic, or chance

Examples

She avoided walking under ladders because she was extremely superstitious.
His superstitious beliefs made him carry a rabbit's foot everywhere.
Some people are so superstitious that they panic whenever a black cat crosses their path.

Synset Examples

finally realized that the horror he felt was superstitious in origin

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rational - based on clear thought and reason
  • Antonym: skeptical - having doubts or not easily convinced about beliefs
  • Synonym: credulous - willing to believe or trust too easily

Etymology

From Latin superstitiōsus (full of superstition), from superstitio (superstition).
