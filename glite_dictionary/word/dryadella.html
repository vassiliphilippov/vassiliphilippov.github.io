Dryadella

1. NOUN

/ ˌdraɪ.əˈdɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˌdraɪ.əˈdɛl.ə / (BrE)
A genus of very small tropical American orchids, with tufted, usually single-leaf stems and solitary flowers, formerly included in the genus Masdevallia.
a genus of dwarf tropical orchids

Examples

Many orchid enthusiasts grow Dryadella species for their unique miniature flowers.
The Dryadella plant is well suited for terrarium cultivation due to its small size.
Botanists recently discovered a new Dryadella species in the Andean cloud forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Masdevallia - a related genus of orchids; Dryadella species were formerly classified here

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Dryad' (tree nymph in Greek mythology) and the diminutive suffix '-ella'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdraɪəˈdɛlə / (AmE), / ˌdraɪəˈdɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of very small, tufted, tropical American orchids with short stems and typically single leaves, usually producing solitary flowers.
genus of small tropical American orchids

Examples

The Dryadella orchids are prized by collectors for their tiny yet intricate flowers.
Most species in the genus Dryadella thrive in humid, tropical environments.
Botanists recently discovered a new Dryadella species in the rainforests of Brazil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Masdevallia - a related genus of orchids, often closely associated or formerly grouped with Dryadella

Etymology

From New Latin, probably named after 'Dryad' (tree nymph in Greek mythology) and the diminutive suffix '-ella'.
