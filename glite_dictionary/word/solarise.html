to solarise

1. VERB

/ ˈsoʊ.ləˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.lə.raɪz / (BrE)
to expose to sunlight; to subject to the effect of sunlight
expose to sunlight

Examples

To disinfect the soil, they decided to solarise the area for several weeks.
Delicate artworks can be damaged if you solarise them excessively.
It is important not to solarise young plants too quickly after moving them outdoors.

Synset Examples

insolated paper may turn yellow and crumble
These herbs suffer when sunned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sun - to expose to the sun

Etymology

From 'solar' (relating to the sun) + '-ise', from Latin 'solaris' (of the sun).

2. VERB

/ ˈsoʊləˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊləˌraɪz / (BrE)
(in photography) to partially reverse the tones of a negative or print and create pronounced outlines of highlights by briefly exposing it to light, then washing and redeveloping it.
reverse tones in a photograph by brief light exposure

Examples

The photographer decided to solarise the print for a surreal effect.
If you solarise a negative, you'll see dramatic contrasts in the image.
She experimented with different ways to solarise her black-and-white photographs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to solarize - Alternate spelling of 'solarise' (photography technique)

Etymology

From 'solar' + '-ise', relating to the effect of sunlight on photographic materials.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02118916-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsoʊləˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊləˌraɪz / (BrE)
to overexpose photographic film or an image to sunlight, causing alteration of the image
overexpose to sunlight (photography)

Examples

Photographers must avoid solarising their negatives during development.
If you solarise the film, you'll ruin the picture quality.
She accidentally solarised the image by leaving it in direct sunlight too long.

Synset Examples

be careful not to solarize the photographic film

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overexpose - to expose film or an image to too much light

Etymology

From 'solar' (relating to the sun) + '-ise', after the effect of sunlight on film.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02118333-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈsoʊləˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊləraɪz / (BrE)
(of a photograph or film) to become overexposed, especially so that tones are reversed or the image is altered by excess light.
to become overexposed (film/photography)

Examples

If you leave the old film in the sun, it might solarise and spoil the pictures.
Due to a mistake in development, several negatives started to solarise unexpectedly.
Be careful with exposure time, or the image will solarise and lose its detail.

Synset Examples

The film solarized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to solarize - to become overexposed, especially in US English spelling

Etymology

From 'solar' (sun, light) and the suffix '-ise', referring to exposure to strong light.
