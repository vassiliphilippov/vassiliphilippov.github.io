Fyodor Mikhailovich Dostoevsky

1. PROPN

/ ˈfjɔdər mɪˌxaɪləˌvɪtʃ ˌdɑstoʊˈjɛfski / (AmE), / ˈfjɔːdɔː mɪˌxajləˌvɪtʃ ˌdɒstəˈjɛfski / (BrE)
(1821–1881) Russian novelist known for exploring human psychology and suffering in works such as 'Crime and Punishment' and 'The Brothers Karamazov'.
Russian novelist and psychologist of human nature

Examples

Many regard Fyodor Mikhailovich Dostoevsky as one of the founders of modern psychological fiction.
The works of Fyodor Mikhailovich Dostoevsky are regularly studied in literature courses worldwide.
Fyodor Mikhailovich Dostoevsky's exploration of morality and free will continues to influence writers today.
