Eibit

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛbɪt / (AmE), / ˈiːbɪt / (BrE)
A unit of digital information equal to 1,152,921,504,606,846,976 (2^60) bits, commonly used in computing.
unit: 2^60 (1,152,921,504,606,846,976) digital bits

Examples

A single Eibit is used to measure massive amounts of network data transmission.
Cloud storage providers rarely advertise their capacities in Eibits due to the enormous size.
The difference between an Eibit and an exabit lies in the binary versus decimal calculation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pebibit - a unit of information equal to 2^50 bits

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'exbi-' (for 2^60, from 'exabyte' with binary root 'bi') and 'bit'.
