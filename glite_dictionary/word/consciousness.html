consciousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.ʃəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.ʃəs.nəs / (BrE)
the state of being aware of oneself, one's thoughts, and one's surroundings
awareness of self and surroundings

Examples

After the accident, he lost consciousness for several minutes and could not remember what happened.
She slowly regained consciousness and opened her eyes as the anesthesia wore off.
The patient drifted in and out of consciousness during the night, making communication difficult.

Synset Examples

he lost consciousness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unconsciousness - the state of not being aware of oneself or one's surroundings
  • Synonym: awareness - knowledge or perception of a situation or fact

Etymology

From Latin 'conscientia' (knowledge within oneself), from 'con-' (together) + 'scire' (to know).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.ʃəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.ʃəs.nəs / (BrE)
the state of being aware of and able to think about one's surroundings, thoughts, or feelings; awareness or realization of something
state of being aware; awareness

Examples

The consciousness of his error filled him with sudden embarrassment.
With growing consciousness, she realized she was responsible for the mistake.
Their consciousness of the risks helped them make better decisions.

Synset Examples

he had no awareness of his mistakes
his sudden consciousness of the problem he faced
their intelligence and general knowingness was impressive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awareness - knowledge or perception of a situation or fact
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From Latin conscius (knowing, aware) + -ness.
