moreen

1. NOUN

/ mɔˈrin / (AmE), / mɔˈriːn / (BrE)
a heavy, ribbed fabric of wool or wool and cotton, used mainly for upholstery or curtains
heavy ribbed textile used in upholstery or curtains

Examples

The antique sofa was reupholstered in a rich burgundy moreen that added elegance to the room.
Heavy green moreen curtains blocked the sunlight from the library's tall windows.
In the Victorian era, moreen was commonly chosen for durable and stylish draperies.

Etymology

From French 'mourine', of uncertain origin.
