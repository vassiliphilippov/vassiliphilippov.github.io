quadrille

1. NOUN

/ kwɑˈdrɪl / (AmE), / kwɒˈdrɪl / (BrE)
a lively square dance for four or more couples, traditionally featuring five or more distinct figures
a type of square dance for four couples

Examples

The dancers formed lines on the floor as the quadrille began.
She learned to perform the quadrille at a historical ballroom event.
At the festival, couples dressed in period costumes to dance a traditional quadrille.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: square dance - a type of folk dance performed by couples arranged in a square

Etymology

From French, originally Italian quadriglia (a group of four), from Latin quattuor (four).

2. NOUN

/ kwəˈdrɪl / (AmE), / kwɒˈdrɪl / (BrE)
A piece of music composed to accompany the quadrille, a lively dance for four couples, popular especially in the 18th and 19th centuries.
music for the quadrille dance

Examples

The orchestra delighted the guests with a lively quadrille during the ball.
Several classic quadrilles were featured on the evening's program, transporting listeners back in time.
She learned to play the quadrille on the piano for the historical reenactment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quadrille - a square dance for four couples

Etymology

From French 'quadrille', originally from Spanish 'cuadrilla' (a small squad), referring to the dance's arrangement.
