dada

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːdɑː / (AmE), / ˈdɑːdɑː / (BrE)
an informal word used by young children to refer to their father.
informal word for father
informal

Examples

The little boy called out for his dada when he got scared in the night.
At daycare, she always draws pictures for her dada.
Hearing his daughter say dada for the first time made him smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daddy - an informal word for father, often used by children
  • Synonym: papa - an informal or affectionate term for father
  • Synonym: father - a male parent

Etymology

Imitative of the first baby sounds; likely from infantile speech patterns in many languages.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑː.dɑː / (AmE), / ˈdɑː.dɑː / (BrE)
an early 20th-century European art movement characterized by deliberate irrationality and the rejection of traditional aesthetic standards
early 20th-century anti-art movement

Examples

The dada movement challenged conventional ideas about art and meaning.
Many dada artists created works using found objects and random compositions.
Modern exhibitions often explore the influence of dada on contemporary art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dadaism - the principles and practices of the dada movement
  • Degree Higher: Surrealism - a later art movement influenced by dada that emphasized dreamlike, illogical imagery

Etymology

From French 'Dada', supposedly chosen at random from a dictionary; literal meaning is 'hobbyhorse' in French.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
