/ ti / (AmE), / tiː / (BrE)

Examples

2. NOUN

/ ti / (AmE), / tiː / (BrE)
A small stand used to hold a football upright above the ground just before it is kicked off.
support for holding a football before kickoff

Examples

The placekicker carefully positioned the ball on the tee before the game began.
Without a tee, it's much harder to get a clean kickoff in football.
The coach forgot to bring the tee, so they improvised with a water bottle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golf tee - a small peg used to elevate a golf ball for the first stroke

Etymology

From Scottish English 'tee' (in golf), of uncertain origin.

3. NOUN

/ tiː / (AmE), / tiː / (BrE)
A small, usually wooden or plastic peg placed in the ground to support a golf ball before it is struck at the beginning of a hole or drive.
golf peg for holding ball

Examples

She placed the ball carefully on the tee before taking her first swing.
Make sure your tee is at the right height to get a good drive.
I always carry extra tees in my golf bag in case I lose one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golf ball - ball used in the game of golf

Etymology

Originated in the 17th century from Scottish 'teaz', meaning a small heap or mark from which a ball is struck in golf.
