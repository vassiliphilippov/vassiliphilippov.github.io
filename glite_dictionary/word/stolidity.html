stolidity

1. NOUN

/ stoʊˈlɪdəti / (AmE), / stəˈlɪdɪti / (BrE)
A lack of emotional response; showing little or no feeling or sensitivity.
lack of emotional response

Examples

His stolidity during the crisis made others doubt he even cared.
Despite the tragedy, she faced her loss with a remarkable stolidity.
The stolidity of the witness frustrated the lawyer's attempts to provoke a reaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of interest or concern
  • Synonym: stoicism - endurance of pain or hardship without display of feelings
  • Antonym: sensitivity - the quality of being sensitive or easily affected emotionally

Etymology

From Latin 'stolidus' (dull, insensitive) + English '-ity'.

2. NOUN

/ stoʊˈlɪdəti / (AmE), / stəˈlɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality of showing little or no emotion or interest, especially in response to pleasure or pain
lack of emotional response; indifference

Examples

His stolidity in the face of danger impressed everyone around him.
Even after hearing the tragic news, her stolidity remained unbroken.
The movie's protagonist is defined by his stolidity and refusal to show emotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of interest or enthusiasm
  • Synonym: stoicism - the endurance of pain or hardship without displaying emotion
  • Antonym: sensitivity - the quality of being sensitive to feelings or emotions; the opposite state

Etymology

From Latin 'stolidus' meaning dull, slow, or obtuse.
