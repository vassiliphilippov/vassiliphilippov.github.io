to defenestrate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈfɛnəˌstreɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛnɪstreɪt / (BrE)
to throw someone or something out of a window
throw out of a window

Examples

In a moment of anger, he threatened to defenestrate his malfunctioning computer.
The play depicted a king who was defenestrated by his enemies.
Some people claim they want to defenestrate outdated rules and regulations.

Synset Examples

The rebels stormed the palace and defenestrated the President

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eject - to force someone or something out
  • Synonym: throw - to propel something with force through the air

Etymology

From Latin 'de-' (down from) + 'fenestra' (window).
