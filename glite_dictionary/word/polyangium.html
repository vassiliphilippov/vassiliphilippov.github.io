Polyangium

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈændʒiəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈændʒiəm / (BrE)
A genus of myxobacteria characterized by rounded fruiting bodies that are enclosed within a membrane.
genus of myxobacteria with rounded fruiting bodies

Examples

Researchers observed several colonies of Polyangium under the microscope.
Polyangium is notable for its unique membrane-enclosed fruiting bodies.
The family Polyangiaceae is named after the genus Polyangium.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'angion' (vessel), referring to the structure of the fruiting bodies.

genus of myxobacteria

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species of Polyangium in the soil sample.
Polyangium forms distinctive fruiting bodies during its life cycle.
Research into Polyangium has shed light on the diversity of myxobacteria.

Related Terms
