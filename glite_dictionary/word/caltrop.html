caltrop

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæltrɑp / (AmE), / ˈkæltrəp / (BrE)
A tropical annual creeping poisonous plant whose fruit splits into five spiny sections; considered a serious weed in pastures.
poisonous weed with spiny fruit

Examples

The pasture was overrun by caltrop, making it dangerous for livestock.
Farmers struggle to control the spread of caltrop because of its tough, spiny seeds.
Walking barefoot in the field, he stepped on a caltrop and hurt his foot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goathead - another name for the caltrop plant
  • Synonym: Tribulus terrestris - the scientific name for the caltrop plant

Etymology

From Latin 'calcatrippa', meaning 'foot trap', originally referencing the spiked weapon and later applied to spiny plants.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæltrɑp / (AmE), / ˈkæltrɒp / (BrE)
a water plant of the genus Trapa, bearing hard, spiny, four-pronged edible fruits, often known as water chestnut
water plant with spiny edible fruits

Examples

The spiny fruit of the caltrop is harvested for food in some Asian countries.
Researchers studied how the caltrop adapts to wetlands and slow-moving rivers.
Some people mistake the dried caltrop seeds for small, dangerous objects due to their shape.

Etymology

From Old French 'caltrappe', possibly via Latin 'calcatrippa' ('foot-trap'), originally referring to the spiked weapon, later applied to the fruit's similar shape.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.trɑp / (AmE), / ˈkæl.trɒp / (BrE)
A Mediterranean annual or biennial herb with pinkish to purple flowers surrounded by spine-tipped scales, often found naturalized in America.
spiny Mediterranean herb with purple flowers

Examples

The field was infested with caltrop, making it difficult to walk barefoot.
Botanists studied how the caltrop plant spread so quickly across the region.
With its sharp spines, the caltrop can deter grazing animals from eating it.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *calcatrippa*, referring to a spiky instrument or plant.
