to misestimate

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪsˈɛs.tɪ.meɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈɛs.tɪ.meɪt / (BrE)
to estimate something incorrectly; to form a wrong judgment about the value, amount, or nature of something
estimate incorrectly

Examples

She tends to misestimate how long complex projects will take.
The company misestimated the market demand for their new product.
If we misestimate the distance, we could miss the turn entirely.

Synset Examples

I had misestimated his determination

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underestimate - to estimate something as less than its actual value or importance
  • Antonym: overestimate - to estimate something as greater than its actual value or importance

Etymology

Formed from 'mis-' (wrongly) and 'estimate', from Latin 'aestimare' (to value).

2. VERB

/ ˌmɪsˈɛstəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈɛstɪmeɪt / (BrE)
To estimate something incorrectly; to make an incorrect assessment or judgment of quantity, value, or extent.
estimate incorrectly

Examples

Economists sometimes misestimate the impact of new government policies.
If you misestimate the project's cost, it could affect the entire budget.
The team misestimated how long it would take to complete the renovation.

Synset Examples

I miscalculated the number of guests at the wedding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misjudge - form an incorrect opinion or conclusion about
  • Synonym: to miscalculate - calculate wrongly or inaccurately

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'estimate', patterned by analogy with similar English verbs.

3. VERB

/ ˌmɪsˈɛstɪˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈɛstɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to judge or calculate the size, amount, or effect of something incorrectly
to judge incorrectly; make a wrong estimate

Examples

Analysts sometimes misestimate the impact of changes in policy and predict the wrong outcomes.
It is easy to misestimate the impact of technological advances before they are widely adopted.
We should not misestimate the impact of small gestures on people’s morale.

Synset Examples

Don't misestimate the impact of her words.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: underestimate - to estimate something as being less than it actually is
  • Degree Higher: overestimate - to estimate something as being more than it actually is
  • Synonym: to misjudge - to judge something incorrectly

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'estimate', from Latin 'aestimare' (to value).
