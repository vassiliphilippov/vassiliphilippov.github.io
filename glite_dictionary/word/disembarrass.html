to disembarrass

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsɪmˈbærəs / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪmˈbærəs / (BrE)
to free someone or something from something troublesome, unwanted, or burdensome
free from something troublesome or unwanted

Examples

The reforms were meant to disembarrass the government of unnecessary regulations.
She worked hard to disembarrass the organization of its outdated equipment.
He hoped to disembarrass himself of debts before starting his new business.

Synset Examples

Rid the house of pests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rid - to make someone or something free of something unwanted; to remove
  • Synonym: to relieve - to remove a burden or something troublesome from

Etymology

From French 'dés-' + 'embarrasser' (to free from obstruction or hindrance).
