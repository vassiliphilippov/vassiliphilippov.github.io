validity

1. NOUN

/ vəˈlɪdəti / (AmE), / vəˈlɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality of having legal force or effectiveness; the state of being legally recognized or binding
legal force or effectiveness
legal

Examples

The validity of the contract was questioned in court.
She doubted the validity of the agreement due to missing signatures.
Any challenge to the validity of the will must be filed within six months.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invalidity - the state of not having legal force or being void

Etymology

From Latin validitas (strength, efficacy), from validus (strong, effective).

2. NOUN

/ vəˈlɪdəti / (AmE), / vəˈlɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality of being logically or factually sound; the state of being valid, effective, or rigorous
quality of being valid or sound

Examples

The scientist questioned the validity of the experiment's results due to a procedural error.
Before accepting the contract, she checked the validity of all the documents.
Doubts were raised about the validity of his arguments in the debate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invalidity - the state of being not valid or lacking force

Etymology

From Latin 'validitas', from 'validus' (strong, powerful).
