parhelion

1. NOUN

/ pɑrˈhiliən / (AmE), / pɑːˈhiːlɪən / (BrE)
A bright spot in the sky, often appearing on either side of the Sun, caused by sunlight passing through ice crystals in the atmosphere.
bright spot seen beside the Sun

Examples

A rare parhelion could be seen to the right of the morning sun.
Photographers love capturing the beautiful colors of a parhelion.
Several parhelia appeared during the cold winter afternoon.

Synset Examples

two or more parhelia are usually seen at once

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sundog - bright spot beside the Sun caused by ice crystals

Etymology

From Greek parēlion, from para (beside) + hēlios (sun).
