Micropogonias

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkrəpəˈɡoʊniəs / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəpəˈɡəʊniəs / (BrE)
a genus of fishes in the family Sciaenidae, known as finebarbel croakers, found in coastal waters of the Americas
a genus of croaker fishes

Examples

The Micropogonias genus includes several economically important species of croaker fishes.
Scientists recently documented new habitats for Micropogonias populations along the Atlantic coast.
Fishermen often target species within the Micropogonias genus due to their culinary value.

Etymology

From Greek mikros (small) + pogon (beard), referring to the fine barbels on the fish's chin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊˌpoʊˈɡoʊniəs / (AmE), / ˌmɪkrəˌpɒˈɡəʊniəs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Sciaenidae, commonly known as finebarbel croakers, native to coastal waters of the Americas.
a genus of croaker fish

Examples

The Micropogonias genus includes several species found along the Atlantic coast.
Researchers studied the spawning habits of Micropogonias croakers in the Gulf of Mexico.
Commercial fisheries target species from the Micropogonias group due to their economic value.

Etymology

From Greek mikros (small) + pōgōn (beard) + Latin suffix -ias, referring to the fine barbels on the fish's chin.
