Vaccinium pennsylvanicum

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm ˌpɛn.səlˈvænɪ.kəm / (AmE), / vækˈsɪniəm ˌpɛn.səlˈvæn.ɪ.kəm / (BrE)
A low-growing deciduous shrub native to northeastern North America, known for its compact racemes of flowers and sweet dark blue berries, commonly called the lowbush blueberry.
lowbush blueberry shrub of northeast North America

Examples

The Vaccinium pennsylvanicum thrives in the acidic soils of northeastern forests.
Wild berries of Vaccinium pennsylvanicum are often harvested for use in jams and pies.
Researchers study the nutrition value of Vaccinium pennsylvanicum berries due to their high antioxidant content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lowbush blueberry - common name for Vaccinium pennsylvanicum, a wild blueberry species

Etymology

From the genus name Vaccinium (Latin, possibly for blueberry or cowberry) and the species epithet pennsylvanicum, meaning 'of Pennsylvania'.
