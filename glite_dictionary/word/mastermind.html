mastermind

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstərˌmaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəmaɪnd / (BrE)
a person who invents, plans, or directs the creation of new things, often with great skill or intelligence
inventive creator or planner

Examples

He was the mastermind behind the revolutionary new technology.
The company's success is due to its mastermind, who continually develops creative solutions.
As the mastermind of the project, she guided the team through every step.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innovator - a person who introduces new ideas or methods

Etymology

From 'master' + 'mind', originally meaning 'chief planner or intellect' (first attested in the late 19th century).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæstərˌmaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəmaɪnd / (BrE)
a person with exceptional intellectual ability and creativity, often the originator behind complex plans or projects
person of exceptional intellect and creativity

Examples

The criminal organization was taken down thanks to the arrest of its mastermind.
She was considered the mastermind behind the innovative new product.
Despite being quiet, he was the mastermind of the entire operation.

Synset Examples

Mozart was a child genius
he's smart but he's no Einstein

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genius - a person with extraordinary intellectual power and creative ability
  • Synonym: prodigy - a young person with exceptional qualities or abilities
  • Antonym: dullard - a person who is slow or limited in understanding

Etymology

From 'master' + 'mind', indicating dominance or expertise of mind.

3. VERB

/ ˈmæstərˌmaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəmaɪnd / (BrE)
to plan and direct a complex or difficult project or activity, especially one that is secret or illegal
plan and direct a complex undertaking

Examples

Detectives believe he masterminded the entire operation from his prison cell.
She masterminded the fundraising campaign that saved the school from closure.
They accused him of masterminding a sophisticated fraud scheme.

Synset Examples

he masterminded the robbery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to orchestrate - to organize or arrange something carefully, especially a complex operation
  • Synonym: plan - to decide on and arrange in advance
  • Antonym: foil - to prevent the success of (something planned)

Etymology

From 'master' + 'mind', originally used as a noun for someone with outstanding intellect or leadership skills; later developed use as a verb.
