Carissa grandiflora

1. PROPN

/ kəˈrɪsə ˌɡrændɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / kəˈrɪsə ˌɡrændɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A very large, closely branched South African shrub with forked bright green spines and glossy leaves, cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
South African shrub, also known as Natal plum

Examples

The Carissa grandiflora in his backyard produced fruit all summer long.
Gardeners often choose Carissa grandiflora for its attractive appearance and edible berries.
Carissa grandiflora is well suited to warm climates and coastal gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Natal plum - Common name for Carissa grandiflora, edible fruit-bearing shrub

Etymology

From Latin; 'Carissa' is a genus name, and 'grandiflora' means 'large-flowered'.
