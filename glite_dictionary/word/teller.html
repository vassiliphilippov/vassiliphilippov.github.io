teller

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛlə / (BrE)
a person who tells stories or recounts events to others
person who tells stories

Examples

She is a gifted teller of fairy tales and legends.
The teller drew the children in with his vivid descriptions and expressive voice.
Every group of friends has at least one teller who keeps everyone entertained.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storyteller - a person who tells stories, especially as a profession

Etymology

From Old English 'tellan' (to count, recount, tell).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛlə / (BrE)
an official appointed to count votes, especially in a legislative assembly or at a formal meeting
official who counts votes

Examples

The teller announced the final result after carefully counting the votes.
Each party appointed a teller to oversee the voting process during the assembly.
The tellers reported a narrow victory for the bill after a lengthy debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrutineer - person who observes or checks votes, especially in elections

Etymology

From Middle English *tellen* (to count, narrate), from Old English *tellan*; the term 'teller' in this sense relates to 'to count'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10718595-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛlə / (BrE)
an employee of a bank whose job is to receive and pay out money to customers
bank employee handling customer transactions

Examples

The teller at the bank window greeted each customer with a friendly smile.
She waited in line to speak with the teller about depositing her paycheck.
Many banks now offer automated machines in addition to traditional teller services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cashier - an employee who handles payments and receipts, especially in a store or bank

Etymology

From Old English *tellan* (to count, reckon).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
