palaeencephalon

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪliˌɛnˈsɛfəlɒn / (AmE), / ˌpalɪɛnˈsɛfəlɒn / (BrE)
the primitive part of the brain comprising all structures except the cerebral cortex; considered phylogenetically older than the neocortex
primitive part of the brain; all but the cortex
medical

Examples

Damage to the palaeencephalon can affect basic functions such as breathing and heart rate.
The evolutionary distinction between the palaeencephalon and neocortex is crucial in brain research.
Unlike the neocortex, the palaeencephalon manages instinctive and survival behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neocortex - the part of the cerebral cortex concerned with sight and hearing in mammals, regarded as the most recently evolved part of the cortex
  • Antonym: cerebral cortex - the outermost layer of the brain, responsible for complex functions like perception, thought, and decision-making

Etymology

From Greek palaios (ancient) + enkephalos (brain).
