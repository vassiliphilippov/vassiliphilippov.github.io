waning

1. ADJ

/ ˈweɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
(of the Moon) relating to the period when the illuminated portion visible from Earth is decreasing.
decreasing lunar phase

Examples

The harvest festival was held under a waning moon.
As the waning moon rose, the night became darker and quieter.
Farmers often used the waning phase of the moon to determine the best time for planting certain crops.

Synset Examples

after full moon comes the waning moon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: waxing - (of the Moon) relating to the period when the illuminated portion is increasing

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'wane', from Old English 'wanian' (to decrease).

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪ.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪ.nɪŋ / (BrE)
a gradual decrease in magnitude, intensity, or extent
a gradual decrease in size or intensity

Examples

The waning of consumer interest forced the company to reconsider its product line.
After a period of prosperity, there was a noticeable waning of the country's economic power.
The poet described the waning of the moon as a symbol of fading hope.

Synset Examples

the waning of his enthusiasm was obvious
the waxing and waning of the moon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: waxing - a gradual increase in magnitude or extent

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'wane', originally from Old English 'wanian' (to decrease).
