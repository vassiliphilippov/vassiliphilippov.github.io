unerasable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnəˈreɪsəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪˈreɪsəbəl / (BrE)
Impossible to remove or erase; not capable of being erased.
impossible to erase or remove

Examples

The marker left an unerasable line on the whiteboard that wouldn't come off.
Some childhood traumas can leave unerasable marks on one's memory.
The contract was signed with unerasable ink, so no changes could be made.

Synset Examples

an indelible stain
indelible memories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indelible - impossible to erase or remove
  • Antonym: erasable - able to be erased or removed

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' and 'erasable' (from Latin 'erāre' via 'erase').
