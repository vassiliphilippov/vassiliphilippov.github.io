scuff

1. NOUN

/ skʌf / (AmE), / skʌf / (BrE)
A slipper or light shoe that is backless, having no covering or support for the heel.
backless slipper or shoe

Examples

She slipped her feet into comfortable scuffs before heading to the kitchen.
The hotel provided complimentary scuffs for guests to use in the room.
His favorite scuff was worn out from years of daily use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slipper - a light, comfortable shoe for indoors

Etymology

Probably a variant of 'scuff' (to scrape), from Scandinavian roots.

2. NOUN

/ skʌf / (AmE), / skʌf / (BrE)
an act of scraping, dragging, or rubbing the feet, especially along the ground, often resulting in a mark or abrupt noise
act of scraping or dragging the feet

Examples

The hallway was quiet except for the scuff of her shoes on the tiled floor.
He heard a sudden scuff behind him and turned around quickly.
Years of children's scuffs had left marks all along the gymnasium floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrape - the act of scraping, especially making a mark or abrasion

3. VERB

/ skʌf / (AmE), / skʌf / (BrE)
to poke at or move something by scraping or rubbing it with the foot or toe, often slightly or unintentionally
poke or scrape with the foot

Examples

He scuffed the loose stone with his shoe as he waited nervously.
Please don’t scuff the door with your boots when you come in.
She absentmindedly scuffed at the dirt while talking on the phone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrape - to rub an object with something rough or sharp
  • Synonym: kick - to strike something with the foot

Etymology

Probably from Scandinavian origin; compare Swedish *skuffa* (to push, shove).

4. VERB

/ skʌf / (AmE), / skʌf / (BrE)
to walk by sliding or dragging the feet rather than lifting them properly
walk dragging the feet

Examples

He tends to scuff when he is tired, making a soft shuffling noise across the carpet.
The children scuffed along the sidewalk, not eager to get to school.
She scuffed through the fallen leaves, enjoying the crisp sound as she dragged her feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shuffle - to walk by dragging the feet along the ground without lifting them fully

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of dragging or scraping the feet.
