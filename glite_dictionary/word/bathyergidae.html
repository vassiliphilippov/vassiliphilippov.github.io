Bathyergidae

1. NOUN

/ bæθiˈɜrdʒɪdi / (AmE), / bæθɪˈɜːdʒɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of burrowing rodents known as mole rats or sand rats, native to Africa.
family of African mole rats

Examples

The Bathyergidae family includes several subterranean rodents adapted to life underground.
Many researchers study Bathyergidae to understand the evolution of social behavior in mammals.
Unlike other rodents, members of Bathyergidae have specialized teeth for digging through hard soil.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Bathyergidae, based on genus Bathyergus, from Greek bathys (deep) + ergon (work).
