prey

1. NOUN

/ preɪ / (AmE), / preɪ / (BrE)
a person who is the target of an attack, exploitation, or ridicule by another person or influence
person targeted by attack or exploitation

Examples

The elderly woman became easy prey for a group of scammers posing as police officers.
Children can fall prey to cyberbullying if they are not careful online.
He always seems to make prey of people who trust him too quickly.

Synset Examples

he fell prey to muggers
everyone was fair game
the target of a manhunt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victim - a person who is harmed or killed as a result of a crime, accident, or other event
  • Antonym: predator - a person or animal that preys on others

Etymology

From Latin praeda (booty, plunder).

2. NOUN

/ preɪ / (AmE), / preɪ / (BrE)
An animal that is hunted and killed by another for food.
animal hunted for food

Examples

Lions stalk their prey quietly before attacking.
Many birds of prey have keen eyesight to spot their prey from afar.
The deer froze, sensing the presence of a potential prey nearby.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: predator - an animal that hunts and eats other animals

Etymology

From Old French 'preie', from Latin 'praeda' (booty, plunder).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02155533-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ preɪ / (AmE), / preɪ / (BrE)
to take advantage of someone or something in an unfair or exploitative way, often for personal gain
to exploit or take advantage of

Examples

Scammers often prey on elderly people by pretending to be bank officials.
He would prey on people's fears to sell his useless products.
Unscrupulous companies prey on vulnerable customers during difficult economic times.

Synset Examples

He feeds on her insecurity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploit - to use someone or something unfairly for one's own advantage
  • Synonym: to victimize - to make someone a victim, especially by treating them unfairly

Etymology

From Old French preier, from Latin praedari (to plunder), from praeda (booty, prey).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01206335-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ preɪ / (AmE), / preɪ / (BrE)
(of an animal) to hunt, catch, and feed on another animal for food.
hunt and feed on another animal

Examples

Owls prey on small mammals, such as mice and voles.
Some fish prey on insects that fall into the water.
Wolves usually prey on the weak or sick members of a herd.

Synset Examples

These mammals predate certain eggs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to predate - to hunt and kill for food
  • Antonym: to scavenge - to search for and feed on dead animals

Etymology

From Old French *preer* (to plunder) and Latin *praedari* (to plunder, rob).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01206120-v
Synset POS: v
