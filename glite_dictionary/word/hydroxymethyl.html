hydroxymethyl

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdrɒksɪˈmɛθɪl / (AmE), / haɪˌdrɒksɪˈmɛθɪl / (BrE)
a univalent radical or functional group derived from methyl by replacing one hydrogen atom with a hydroxyl group (–CH2OH)
a chemical group –CH2OH

Examples

The hydroxymethyl group plays a significant role in the reactivity of certain organic compounds.
A common example of a hydroxymethyl group is found in formaldehyde derivatives.
Introducing a hydroxymethyl substituent can alter the solubility of a molecule.

Etymology

From hydroxy- (hydroxyl) and methyl.
