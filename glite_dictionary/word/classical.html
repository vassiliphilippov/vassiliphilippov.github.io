classical

1. ADJ

/ ˈklæsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈklɑːsɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the study or literature of ancient Greece and Rome.
of ancient Greek and Roman literature

Examples

She is a classical scholar specializing in ancient Greek literature.
The university offers a classical studies program focusing on Latin and Greek texts.
His interest in classical antiquity led him to study the works of Homer and Virgil.

Synset Examples

a classical scholar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classicism - a style or outlook inspired by principles of classical antiquity

Etymology

From Latin classicus (of the highest class), from classis (class, division).

3. ADJ

/ ˈklæsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈklɑːsɪkəl / (BrE)
(of physics) relating to theories or concepts developed before the advent of relativity and quantum mechanics, especially Newtonian mechanics and electromagnetism.
relating to pre-quantum/relativity physics

Examples

In high school physics, we studied the classical laws of motion developed by Newton.
The classical view of electromagnetism breaks down at extremely small scales.
Einstein's discoveries marked a departure from classical mechanics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modern - relating to or characteristic of present or recent times, particularly in science (as opposed to classical theories)

Etymology

From Latin classicus (of the highest class), later generalized to mean 'traditional' or 'established.'

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
