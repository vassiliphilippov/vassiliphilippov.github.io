anchylosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋ.kəˈloʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌaŋ.kɪˈləʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
abnormal adhesion and rigidity of the bones of a joint, leading to immobility
stiffness or immobility of a joint
medical

Examples

Longstanding arthritis can sometimes lead to anchylosis in the affected joint.
Treatment aims to prevent anchylosis and preserve as much movement as possible.
The patient suffered from severe anchylosis of the knee, making walking very difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ankylosis - alternative spelling; abnormal stiffness of a joint

Etymology

From Greek ankylōsis (stiffening of a joint), from ankylos (crooked, bent).
