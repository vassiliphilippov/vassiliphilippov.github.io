Agaricales

1. NOUN

/ əˌgær.ɪˈkeɪlz / (AmE), / əˌgær.ɪˈkeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi in the subdivision Basidiomycota that includes the typical gilled mushrooms.
order of gilled mushrooms

Examples

Many edible mushrooms, such as the button mushroom, belong to the Agaricales.
Scientists are studying the biodiversity of Agaricales in temperate forests.
The Agaricales order contains both poisonous and edible species.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Basidiomycota - a large division of fungi including mushrooms, puffballs, and shelf fungi

Etymology

From New Latin Agaricales, from Agaricus (a genus of mushrooms) + -ales (denoting order).

2. NOUN

/ əˌɡærɪˈkeɪliːz / (AmE), / əˌɡarɪˈkeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi comprising the typical gilled mushrooms, belonging to the subdivision Basidiomycota
order of typical gilled mushrooms

Examples

The Agaricales include many of the best-known edible and poisonous mushrooms.
Scientists have discovered new species within the Agaricales in tropical forests.
The study of Agaricales is important for understanding fungal diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - the fleshy, spore-bearing fruiting body of a fungus

Etymology

From New Latin, from Agaricus (a genus of mushrooms) + -ales (denoting order).
