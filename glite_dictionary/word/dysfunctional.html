dysfunctional

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈfʌŋkʃənl / (AmE), / dɪsˈfʌŋkʃənəl / (BrE)
Not working or operating normally or properly; especially of a bodily system or organ.
not working normally; impaired function

Examples

The doctor explained that the patient's liver was dysfunctional due to long-term alcohol abuse.
A dysfunctional immune system can make people more susceptible to infections.
The test results indicated a dysfunctional thyroid gland, which required immediate intervention.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: functional - working or operating normally

Etymology

From 'dys-' (Greek, 'bad, abnormal') + 'functional'.

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈfʌŋkʃənəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈfʌŋkʃənəl / (BrE)
Not operating or functioning normally or effectively; failing to serve its intended or useful purpose.
not functioning normally; ineffective

Examples

The study focused on dysfunctional behavior in the workplace and its impact on morale.
A dysfunctional immune system may fail to fight off infections effectively.
Therapists often work with families with longstanding dysfunctional patterns of communication.

Synset Examples

dysfunctional behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malfunctioning - not functioning properly; faulty
  • Antonym: functional - working or operating in a normal, useful way

Etymology

From dis- (expressing negation or reversal) + functional.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00047012-s
Synset POS: s
