excellent

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛksələnt / (AmE), / ˈɛksələnt / (BrE)
of the highest quality; extremely good or outstanding in a particular respect.
of the highest quality; extremely good

Examples

Her performance in the play was simply excellent and received a standing ovation.
The restaurant is known for its excellent service and delicious food.
He gave an excellent presentation that impressed the entire audience.

Synset Examples

made an excellent speech
he was a splendid teacher
a first-class mind
famous for a mean backhand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superb - of very high quality; excellent
  • Synonym: outstanding - exceptionally good; clearly very much better than usual
  • Antonym: mediocre - of only average quality; not very good
  • Degree Lower: good - of a high standard or quality, though less than excellent

Etymology

From Latin 'excellens', present participle of 'excellere' (to surpass, to outshine).
