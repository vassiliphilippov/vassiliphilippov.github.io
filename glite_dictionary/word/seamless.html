seamless

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiːmləs / (AmE), / ˈsiːmləs / (BrE)
(of a fabric, garment, or object) made without seams; not having or joined by a seam or seams.
without seams or joints

Examples

She bought a pair of seamless stockings for extra comfort.
The seamless design makes the shirt ideal for sports activities.
Modern technology allows manufacturers to create seamless metal pipes for industrial use.

Synset Examples

seamless stockings

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seamed - having seams or joints

Etymology

From 'seam' + '-less', Middle English 'seemeles'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02262020-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈsiːmləs / (AmE), / ˈsiːmləs / (BrE)
(of skin) perfectly smooth and lacking wrinkles or visible lines.
smooth, wrinkle-free skin

Examples

Her seamless complexion made her look much younger than her age.
The model's seamless skin was praised by the photographer for its flawless texture.
After the facial treatment, his cheeks felt especially seamless and soft.

Synset Examples

his cheeks were unlined
his unseamed face

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wrinkled - (of skin) having wrinkles or visible lines

Etymology

From seam + -less, indicating 'without seams' or interruptions.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02245722-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈsiːmləs / (AmE), / ˈsiːmləs / (BrE)
having no apparent gaps, inconsistencies, or interruptions; perfectly consistent and coherent.
perfectly consistent and coherent

Examples

The design of the app offers a seamless user experience from start to finish.
Her presentation was so seamless that the audience barely noticed the transitions between topics.
The two companies merged in a seamless manner, with no disruption to customers.

Synset Examples

the novel's seamless plot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coherent - logical and consistent; forming a unified whole
  • Antonym: fragmented - broken into separate or disconnected parts; lacking unity

Etymology

From 'seam' + '-less', indicating 'without seams'; originally referring to fabric, later used figuratively.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00466836-s
Synset POS: s
