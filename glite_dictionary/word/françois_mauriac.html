François Mauriac

1. PROPN

/ fʀɑ̃ˈswɑ moʊˈriɑk / (AmE), / fʀɑ̃ˈswa mɔːˈrjak / (BrE)
(1885–1970) A French novelist and Nobel laureate known for works exploring the conflict between desire and religious faith.
French novelist and Nobel laureate

Examples

François Mauriac received the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1952 for his deeply spiritual novels.
Many critics admire the psychological depth found in the works of François Mauriac.
François Mauriac often depicted the struggles between personal desire and religious morals in his stories.
