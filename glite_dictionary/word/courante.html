courante

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈrɑːnt / (AmE), / kʊəˈrɑːnt / (BrE)
a lively court dance of the 16th and 17th centuries, involving short advances and retreats, often performed in triple time
16th–17th century court dance

Examples

A courante typically opened a suite of Baroque dances in European courts.
The dancers performed a graceful courante, gliding across the polished floor.
Learning the steps of a courante gave her insight into Renaissance music and culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarabande - a slow, stately dance popular in the Baroque era

Etymology

From French 'courante', feminine of 'courant' (running), from 'courir' (to run).
