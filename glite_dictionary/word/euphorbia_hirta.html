Euphorbia hirta

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔrbiə ˈhɜrtə / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪə ˈhɜːtə / (BrE)
A species of annual herb native to the Americas, growing in open grasslands and disturbed areas, commonly used in traditional herbal medicine.
species of annual herb used in herbal medicine

Examples

Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Euphorbia hirta in treating respiratory ailments.
The roadside was lined with patches of Euphorbia hirta during the rainy season.
Traditional healers in some regions use extracts from Euphorbia hirta to prepare herbal remedies.

Etymology

From the genus Euphorbia, named after Euphorbus, a Greek physician, and Latin species name 'hirta' meaning 'hairy'.
