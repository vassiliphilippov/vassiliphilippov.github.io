vespid

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛs.pɪd / (AmE), / ˈvɛs.pɪd / (BrE)
a wasp belonging to the family Vespidae, consisting mostly of social nest-building wasps such as yellowjackets, hornets, and paper wasps
a social nest-building wasp (family Vespidae)

Examples

The vespid wasps built their distinctive paper nest under the eaves.
Most vespid species are social insects, living in colonies with a queen and workers.
Researchers observed the foraging behavior of several North American vespids during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wasp - a stinging insect of the order Hymenoptera and suborder Apocrita, but not a bee or ant

Etymology

From New Latin Vespidae, from Latin vespa (wasp).
