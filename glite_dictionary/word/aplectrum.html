Aplectrum

1. NOUN

/ əˈplɛktrəm / (AmE), / əˈplɛktrəm / (BrE)
A genus of North American orchids containing the single species Aplectrum hyemale, known as the putty root.
a genus of North American orchids

Examples

The Aplectrum blooms in late spring with its distinctive striped flowers.
Botanists found several specimens of Aplectrum in the shaded woodland.
The Aplectrum is commonly known as putty root due to its sticky tuber.

Etymology

From Greek aplektron (a spur or thong), referencing the shape of the flower.

2. NOUN

/ əˈplɛktrəm / (AmE), / əˈplɛktrəm / (BrE)
A genus of orchids in the family Orchidaceae, consisting of the single species Aplectrum hyemale, commonly known as putty root.
a genus of orchid plants

Examples

The rare Aplectrum can be found in shaded woodlands of eastern North America.
Researchers studied the growing conditions of Aplectrum to learn more about orchid habitats.
The distinctive leaves of Aplectrum appear in late autumn and persist through winter.

Etymology

From Greek aplektron (spurless), used in botanical Latin.
