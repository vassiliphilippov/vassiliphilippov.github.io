vallecula

1. NOUN

/ vəˈlɛkjʊlə / (AmE), / vəˈlɛkjʊlə / (BrE)
(Anatomy) A furrow or groove on a bodily structure, such as between parts of an organ or tissue.
anatomical furrow or groove
medical

Examples

The epiglottic vallecula is an important landmark during intubation.
Fluid can sometimes collect in the vallecula during certain swallowing disorders.
Surgical instruments must be inserted carefully to avoid damaging the vallecula.

Etymology

From Latin 'vallecula' (small valley), diminutive of 'vallis' (valley).
