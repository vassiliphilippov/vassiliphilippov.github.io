Anatolian

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈtoʊliən / (AmE), / ˌanəˈtəʊliən / (BrE)
an extinct branch of the Indo-European family of languages, known from inscriptions, important in the reconstruction of Proto-Indo-European
extinct Indo-European language branch

Examples

The discovery of Anatolian inscriptions provided valuable evidence for linguists studying ancient languages.
Hittite, a well-known Anatolian language, played a key role in Indo-European studies.
Research into the Anatolian branch has shed light on early grammar and vocabulary of Proto-Indo-European.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Indo-European - the family of languages including most of the languages of Europe and parts of Asia

Etymology

From Anatolia, the historical name for the region of modern-day Turkey, where these languages were spoken.
