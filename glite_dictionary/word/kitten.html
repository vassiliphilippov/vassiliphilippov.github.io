kitten

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪt̬n̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɪt.ən / (BrE)
a young domestic cat, especially one less than a year old
young domestic cat

Examples

The children were thrilled to see the newborn kitten sleeping in the basket.
She adopted a stray kitten from the animal shelter last weekend.
As the kitten grew, it became more playful and curious around the house.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cat - a small domesticated carnivorous mammal
  • Antonym: puppy - a young domestic dog

Etymology

From Middle English 'kitoun', from Old French 'chitoun' (diminutive of 'chit', cat).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɪt.ən / (BrE)
(of a cat) to give birth to kittens.
(of a cat) give birth to kittens

Examples

Our cat kittened last spring and had four healthy kittens.
She was nervous as her Siamese was about to kitten for the first time.
The shelter volunteers watched carefully when the stray began to kitten.

Synset Examples

our cat kittened again this year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whelp - (of some animals) to give birth to young

Etymology

Formed from the noun 'kitten' (a young cat); verb sense attested since 19th century.
