abstruse

1. ADJ

/ æbˈstruːs / (AmE), / əbˈstruːs / (BrE)
Difficult to understand; obscure and complex.
difficult to understand

Examples

The philosopher's explanation of time was so abstruse that few could follow it.
She struggled to make sense of the abstruse mathematical theorem in the textbook.
Many readers found the novel overly abstruse and gave up halfway through.

Synset Examples

the professor's lectures were so abstruse that students tended to avoid them
a deep metaphysical theory
some recondite problem in historiography

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recondite - (of a subject or knowledge) little known; abstruse
  • Synonym: obscure - not discovered or known about; uncertain or difficult to understand
  • Antonym: simple - easily understood or done; presenting no difficulty

Etymology

From Latin 'abstrusus', past participle of 'abstrudere' (to conceal).
