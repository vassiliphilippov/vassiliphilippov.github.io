konimeter

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnɪˈmiːtər / (AmE), / ˌkɒnɪˈmiːtə / (BrE)
A device used to measure the concentration of airborne dust, typically by drawing air over a grease-coated glass plate to collect particles.
device for measuring airborne dust

Examples

The laboratory technician used a konimeter to monitor dust levels in the workplace air.
A konimeter can provide accurate readings of airborne particles during industrial safety inspections.
Regular use of a konimeter is recommended in mines to reduce health risks from dust exposure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerometer - an instrument for measuring the density of gases

Etymology

From Greek 'koni-' (dust) + '-meter', meaning a measuring device.
