polymorph

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑliˌmɔrf / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌmɔːf / (BrE)
an organism that can exist in more than one distinct adult form, especially in reference to castes in social insects like ants or termites.
organism with more than one adult form

Examples

The soldier ants are a type of polymorph within their colony, designed specifically for defense.
Some termites exhibit polymorph characteristics, taking on different physical forms depending on their social role.
The concept of a polymorph helps biologists understand the complexity of insect societies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polymorphism - the occurrence of different forms among the individuals of a species

Etymology

From Greek polumorphos, from polu- (many) + morphē (form).
