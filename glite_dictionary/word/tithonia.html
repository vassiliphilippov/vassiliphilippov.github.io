tithonia

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈθoʊniə / (AmE), / tɪˈθəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of tall, coarse herbs or shrubs native to Mexico to Panama, with large sunflower-like flower heads bearing yellow to orange-scarlet rays.
a genus of tall, sunflower-like plants

Examples

The tithonia bloomed magnificently in her garden, attracting butterflies all summer long.
He planted a row of tithonias along the fence for their vibrant orange flowers.
Although commonly called Mexican sunflower, tithonia is a separate genus from true sunflowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunflower - a tall plant with a large yellow flower head, genus Helianthus

Etymology

From the genus name Tithonia, assigned in modern Latin; possibly named after Tithonus, a figure in Greek mythology.
