debile

1. ADJ

/ dəˈbiːl / (AmE), / ˈdiːbaɪl / (BrE)
physically weak; lacking bodily or muscular strength or vitality
lacking bodily strength; weak
archaic

Examples

After months of illness, he remained debile and unable to resume his usual activities.
The debile patient found it difficult to perform even simple tasks without assistance.
She spoke with a debile voice, barely audible in the quiet room.

Synset Examples

a feeble old woman
her body looked sapless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feeble - lacking physical strength, especially as a result of age or illness
  • Synonym: weak - lacking strength or power
  • Antonym: strong - having physical power or strength

Etymology

From Latin 'debilis' (weak, feeble).
