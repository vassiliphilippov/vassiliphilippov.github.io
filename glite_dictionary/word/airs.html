airs

1. NOUN

/ ɛrz / (AmE), / eəz / (BrE)
affected or pretentious behavior or manners intended to impress others
affected or pretentious manners

Examples

She always puts on airs when she meets new people.
He dropped his airs once he realized nobody was impressed.
Don't give me those airs; just be yourself.

Synset Examples

don't put on airs with me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretension - the act of pretending to be more important or knowledgeable than is actually the case
  • Antonym: humility - a modest or low view of one's own importance; absence of pride

Etymology

From French 'air' (appearance, manner), adopted into English in the 15th century.
