Allium ampeloprasum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm ˌæmpəloʊˈpreɪsəm / (AmE), / ˈæliəm ˌampɪləʊˈpresəm / (BrE)
A species of wild leek native to the Old World, with a large bulb and tall stalk bearing greenish, purple-tinged flowers; widely naturalized.
wild leek species; Old World plant

Examples

Allium ampeloprasum is often found growing wild in Mediterranean regions.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Allium ampeloprasum for its edible bulbs and ornamental flowers.
The robust nature of Allium ampeloprasum makes it suitable for naturalizing in open fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leek - a cultivated vegetable closely related to Allium ampeloprasum

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Allium (garlic, onion, etc.) and species epithet ampeloprasum ('vine-leek', from Greek ampelos (vine) + prason (leek)).
