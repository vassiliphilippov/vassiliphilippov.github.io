betwixt

1. ADV

/ bɪˈtwɪkst / (AmE), / bɪˈtwɪkst / (BrE)
in the space separating two things or during the interval between two events or times (archaic or literary).
in the interval or space between (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The house lies betwixt the river and the forest.
There was much confusion betwixt the announcement and the actual event.
He could not decide, lingering betwixt hope and doubt.

Synset Examples

dancing all the dances with little rest between

Related Terms

  • Synonym: between - in the space or interval separating two points, places, or objects

Etymology

Old English betwēox, related to 'between.'
