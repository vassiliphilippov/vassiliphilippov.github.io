to embrown

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈbraʊn / (AmE), / ɪmˈbraʊn / (BrE)
to make or become brown in color
make or become brown

Examples

The late afternoon sun began to embrown the fields with a golden hue.
Years of smoke from the hearth would embrown the walls of the old cottage.
Autumn winds embrown the leaves before they fall to the ground.

Synset Examples

the drought browned the leaves on the trees in the yard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown - change to a brown color, especially by cooking or exposure

Etymology

From en- (cause to be) + brown.

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈbraʊn / (AmE), / ɪmˈbraʊn / (BrE)
to make or become brown or darker in color
to make or become brown

Examples

The setting sun began to embrown the golden fields.
Time and weather will embrown the once-bright paint on the barn.
Her cheeks were embrowned by years of working outdoors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to darken - to make or become dark or darker
  • Synonym: brown - to become or make brown in color
  • Antonym: to lighten - to make or become lighter in color

Etymology

From French 'embrunir', from 'en-' (make) + 'brun' (brown).
