anteroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.tɚˌrum / (AmE), / ˈæn.tə.ruːm / (BrE)
a small room leading to a main room, used as an entryway or waiting area; vestibule or outer room
small entry or waiting room

Examples

We waited in the anteroom until the conference began.
Guests were asked to leave their coats in the anteroom before entering the main hall.
The old mansion's anteroom was decorated with portraits of former owners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vestibule - a small entrance hall or passage
  • Synonym: foyer - an entrance hall or lobby

Etymology

From Latin *ante* (before) + *room*, i.e. a room before another.
