Ambystoma tigrinum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.bɪˈstoʊ.mə taɪˈɡriː.nəm / (AmE), / ˌæm.bɪˈstəʊ.mə tɪˈɡriː.nəm / (BrE)
A large North American salamander, usually brown or black with vertical yellowish blotches, also known as the tiger salamander.
North American tiger salamander species

Examples

The Ambystoma tigrinum is known for its bold, tiger-like markings across its dark body.
Scientists studied the Ambystoma tigrinum population to monitor environmental changes in the wetland.
Many herpetologists regard Ambystoma tigrinum as a model organism for amphibian research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiger salamander - common name for Ambystoma tigrinum, a large North American salamander

Etymology

From Greek amblys (blunt) and stoma (mouth); Latin tigrinum (tiger-like).
