Pan

1. NOUN

/ pæn / (AmE), / pan / (BrE)
The genus of primates comprising the chimpanzees, closely related to humans and more closely related to Australopithecus than to other great apes.
genus of chimpanzees (primates)

Examples

Members of the genus Pan are our closest living relatives in the animal kingdom.
The two species classified under Pan are the common chimpanzee and the bonobo.
Researchers observed social behavior in Pan that is remarkably similar to that of early hominids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimpanzee - a large African ape of the genus Pan

Etymology

From Latin 'Pan', adopted from Greek Πάν, a figure in Greek mythology; applied in modern taxonomy.

2. PROPN

/ pæn / (AmE), / pæn / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the god of the fields, woods, shepherds, and flocks, often depicted as a man with the legs, horns, and ears of a goat; identified with the Roman gods Sylvanus and Faunus.
Greek god of nature and shepherds

Examples

Many ancient statues depict Pan playing his pipes in the forests.
In myths, Pan is known for his wild nature and musical abilities.
The Romans often identified their woodland god Faunus with the Greek Pan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Faunus - Roman god of the countryside, similar to Pan
  • Synonym: Sylvanus - Roman woodland deity, associated with the Greek Pan

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Πάν (Pán), the name of the god, possibly related to the Greek word for 'all'.

3. NOUN

/ pæn / (AmE), / pan / (BrE)
A genus of great apes that includes chimpanzees and bonobos, native to Africa and closely related to humans.
genus of chimpanzees and bonobos

Examples

The Pan genus consists of both chimpanzees and bonobos.
Researchers study Pan species to understand primate evolution.
Fossil records show that Pan is closely related to humans and Australopithecus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimpanzee - species of great ape in the genus Pan
  • Synonym: bonobo - species of great ape in the genus Pan

Etymology

From the Greek god Pan, chosen as a genus name by taxonomists.
