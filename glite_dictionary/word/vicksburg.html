Vicksburg

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪksˌbɜːrɡ / (AmE), / ˈvɪks.bɜːɡ / (BrE)
a decisive battle in the American Civil War in 1863, in which Union forces besieged and captured the city of Vicksburg, leading to Confederate surrender after nearly seven weeks
a key Civil War battle in 1863

Examples

The Siege of Vicksburg marked a turning point in the American Civil War.
Historians often cite the Union victory at Vicksburg as pivotal for control of the Mississippi River.
General Grant's strategy during the battle of Vicksburg is still studied in military academies today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gettysburg - another major battle of the American Civil War in 1863

2. PROPN

/ ˈvɪks.bɜːrɡ / (AmE), / ˈvɪks.bɜːɡ / (BrE)
A town in western Mississippi, situated on bluffs above the Mississippi River, known for being the site of a major Civil War campaign.
town in Mississippi; Civil War site

Examples

During their road trip across the South, they stopped in Vicksburg to tour the historic battlefields.
The Siege of Vicksburg was a turning point in the American Civil War.
Many visitors come to Vicksburg each year to learn more about its pivotal role in history.
