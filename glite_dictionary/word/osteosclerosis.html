osteosclerosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːstioʊskləˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒstiəʊskləˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
abnormal hardening or increased density of bone tissue, often as a result of disease or genetic disorder
abnormal hardening of bone
medical

Examples

The X-ray revealed osteosclerosis in the patient's femur, suggesting underlying metabolic issues.
Cases of osteosclerosis are often seen in individuals with certain genetic bone disorders.
Chronic inflammation around the joint can sometimes lead to local osteosclerosis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: osteomalacia - abnormal softening of bone
  • Antonym: osteoporosis - a condition in which bones become weak and brittle

Etymology

From Greek osteon (bone) + sklerosis (hardening).
