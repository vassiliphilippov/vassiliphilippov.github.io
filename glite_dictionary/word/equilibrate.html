to equilibrate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈkwɪləˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪlɪˌbreɪt / (BrE)
to bring into balance or maintain in a state of equilibrium
bring into balance

Examples

The scientist allowed the solution to equilibrate before taking a measurement.
It takes several hours for the gases in the chamber to equilibrate.
After mixing, you must equilibrate the temperature of the two liquids.

Synset Examples

She has to balance work and her domestic duties
balance the two weights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balance - to keep or put (something) in a steady position so that it does not fall; to bring into equilibrium
  • Antonym: to destabilize - to make unstable

Etymology

From Latin 'aequilibrare' (to balance equally), from 'aequus' (equal) + 'libra' (balance).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈkwɪləˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪlɪbreɪt / (BrE)
to bring or come to a state of equilibrium, especially in a chemical or physical process
bring or come to equilibrium

Examples

The technician allowed the solution to equilibrate before taking further measurements.
After mixing the gases, the scientist waited for the system to equilibrate.
It is important to equilibrate the sample at room temperature to ensure accurate results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balance - to keep or put something in a steady position so that it does not fall
  • Antonym: to destabilize - to make unstable or upset the stability or equilibrium of

Etymology

From Latin 'aequilibrare' (to balance equally), from 'aequus' (equal) and 'libra' (balance).
