to banish

1. VERB

/ ˈbænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbanɪʃ / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a community, group, or place as punishment or by official order
expel from a community or place

Examples

The club decided to banish him after repeated violations of the rules.
She was banished from the group for spreading false rumors.
They threatened to banish anyone who refused to cooperate with the authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expel - to officially force someone to leave a place or organization
  • Synonym: to ostracize - to exclude someone from a group or society
  • Antonym: welcome - to accept or receive someone gladly into a group or place

Etymology

From Middle English banishen, from Old French banir (to proclaim, to banish), of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈbænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbanɪʃ / (BrE)
to send someone away from a place as an official punishment
send away as punishment

Examples

The dictator threatened to banish anyone who opposed his rule.
After the trial, the judge decided to banish her from the city for five years.
Many were banished to distant lands as punishment for their crimes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exile - to force someone to live away from their home country, often as punishment
  • Synonym: to expel - to force someone to leave a place, especially a country, organization, or school
  • Antonym: welcome - to receive or accept someone with pleasure or kindness

Etymology

From Middle English banishen, from Old French banir (proclaim, banish), of Germanic origin.

3. VERB

/ ˈbænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbænɪʃ / (BrE)
To send someone away from a place, especially as an official punishment or by authority, and not allow them to return.
expel from a place as punishment

Examples

After the coup, the new government decided to banish all former leaders from the country.
Her disruptive behavior led the school board to banish her from campus for a year.
Legend says the witch was banished to a remote island for her crimes.

Synset Examples

he was banished from his own country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expel - to officially force someone to leave a place or organization
  • Synonym: exile - the state or act of being sent away from one's country
  • Antonym: welcome - to greet someone in a friendly way or admit them

Etymology

From Middle English baneisshen, from Old French banir (to proclaim, banish), of Germanic origin.

4. VERB

/ ˈbænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbanɪʃ / (BrE)
to get rid of something unwanted, such as a thought or feeling, so that it no longer affects you
drive away a thought or feeling

Examples

She tried to banish all worries from her mind before the interview.
Meditation can help you banish negative thoughts and stress.
He closed his eyes and took a deep breath, determined to banish his fears.

Synset Examples

banish bad thoughts
banish gloom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dispel - make (a doubt, feeling, or belief) disappear
  • Antonym: welcome - deliberately allow or invite in a thought or feeling

Etymology

From Old French *banir* (to proclaim, banish), from Vulgar Latin *banīre*.
