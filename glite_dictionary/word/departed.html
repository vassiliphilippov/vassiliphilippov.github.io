departed

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɑrtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈpɑːtɪd / (BrE)
a person who has died; the deceased.
a deceased person

Examples

We gathered to honor the departed at the memorial service.
She placed flowers on the grave of her departed.
Stories of the departed were shared by family members during the ceremony.

Synset Examples

I wonder what the dead person would have done

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceased - a person who has died
  • Antonym: living - a person who is alive

Etymology

Past participle of 'depart', from Old French 'departir' (to divide, separate), from Latin 'departire'.

Examples

The novel is set in a departed age when handwritten letters were the only means of distant communication.
