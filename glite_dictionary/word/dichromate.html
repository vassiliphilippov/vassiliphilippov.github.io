dichromate

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈkroʊmeɪt / (AmE), / daɪˈkrəʊmeɪt / (BrE)
a salt derived from dichromic acid, typically containing the anion Cr2O7^2−
a salt of dichromic acid

Examples

Potassium dichromate is commonly used as an oxidizing agent in laboratories.
Exposure to dichromate compounds can be hazardous to health.
The bright orange color of the solution indicated the presence of dichromate ions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: chromate - a salt of chromic acid, containing the anion CrO4^2−

Etymology

From di- (two) and chromate, reflecting the presence of two chromium atoms.
