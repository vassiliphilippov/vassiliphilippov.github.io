to aphorise

1. VERB

/ ˈæfərˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈafərʌɪz / (BrE)
to speak or write in aphorisms; to express ideas concisely and memorably
to express in aphorisms

Examples

The philosopher would often aphorise during his lectures, leaving the audience with memorable maxims.
Writers who aphorise can make profound observations in just a few words.
She tends to aphorise rather than elaborate, giving brief but insightful comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to aphorize - to speak or write in aphorisms (American spelling)

Etymology

From French 'aphoriser', from 'aphorisme', ultimately from Greek 'aphorismos' (definition, distinction).
