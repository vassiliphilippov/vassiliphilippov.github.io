sitcom

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪt.kɑm / (AmE), / ˈsɪt.kɒm / (BrE)
a humorous television or radio series based on situations that might occur in everyday life
humorous TV or radio series

Examples

Friends is a classic sitcom that remains popular decades after it first aired.
Many people relax after work by watching their favorite sitcom.
The new sitcom received critical acclaim for its witty writing and relatable characters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comedy - entertainment consisting of jokes and intended to make an audience laugh

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'situation comedy'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtˌkɑm / (AmE), / ˈsɪtˌkɒm / (BrE)
a humorous television series featuring recurring characters in situations drawn from everyday life
humorous television series

Examples

Friends is a popular sitcom that first aired in the 1990s.
She dreams of writing her own sitcom someday.
Many people relax in the evening by watching their favorite sitcom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comedy - a play, film, or other work intended to make an audience laugh

Etymology

Shortened form of 'situation comedy'.
