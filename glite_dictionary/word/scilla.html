Scilla

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlə / (AmE), / ˈsɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of bulbous flowering plants, sometimes placed in the subfamily Hyacinthaceae, known for their blue or purple spring-blooming flowers.
genus of spring-flowering plants

Examples

The garden was filled with blooming Scilla in early spring.
Botanists debated whether Scilla belongs in the Hyacinthaceae family.
She planted several bulbs of Scilla to brighten up her flower beds.

Etymology

From Latin *Scilla*, from Greek *skilla* (a kind of sea onion).
