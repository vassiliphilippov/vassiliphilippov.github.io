scilla

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlə / (AmE), / ˈsɪlə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Scilla, native to the Old World, with narrow basal leaves and pink, blue, or white flowers arranged in racemes.
a plant of the genus Scilla

Examples

The scilla bloomed early in the spring, its blue flowers brightening the garden.
She planted several scillas along the walkway for a splash of color in March.
Botanists admire the delicate structure of the scilla's racemose flowers.

Etymology

From Latin *scilla*, from Greek *skilla*, referring to the sea onion or related plants.
