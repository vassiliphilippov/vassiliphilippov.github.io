separationist

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who advocates or supports the separation of a group from a larger entity, such as a nation or an established church
person who supports separation from a larger group

Examples

The separationist argued for independence from the central government.
Many separationist movements have shaped the course of history.
She joined a separationist group that sought regional autonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secessionist - person who supports secession from a larger body

Etymology

From separation + -ist, forming a noun for a person who supports separation.
