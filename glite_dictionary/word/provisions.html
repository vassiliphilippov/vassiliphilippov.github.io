provisions

1. NOUN

/ prəˈvɪʒənz / (AmE), / prəˈvɪʒ(ə)nz / (BrE)
supplies of food and other essentials, especially for a journey or period away from access to stores
supplies of food and essentials

Examples

The explorers packed enough provisions to last them for two months in the wilderness.
Fresh provisions were delivered to the outpost every week by boat.
We checked our provisions before setting out on the long hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplies - general stocks of food or materials needed for a purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'provisio' (a providing, foreseeing), via Old French 'provision'.
