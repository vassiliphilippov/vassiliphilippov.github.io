Pternohyla fodiens

1. NOUN

/ ptɜːr.noʊˈhaɪ.lə ˈfoʊ.di.ɛnz / (AmE), / ptɜː.nəˈhaɪ.lə ˈfəʊ.di.ɛnz / (BrE)
A species of burrowing nocturnal frog native to the southwestern United States, characterized by a very hard upper surface of the head and found in grassy areas and scrub forests.
burrowing nocturnal frog species of U.S. southwest

Examples

The Pternohyla fodiens spends most of the day hidden underground.
Researchers discovered a Pternohyla fodiens in a remote area of the Arizona desert.
The distinctive head shape helps the Pternohyla fodiens burrow into hard soils.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'pterna' (heel) and 'hyle' (wood, forest); species name from Latin 'fodiens' (digging).
