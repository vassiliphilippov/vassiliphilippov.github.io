Agrostis palustris

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɑstɪs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (AmE), / əˈɡrɒstɪs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (BrE)
A species of grass commonly used for pastures or lawns, known for spreading by long runners.
a pasture or lawn grass species

Examples

Golf courses often plant Agrostis palustris for its dense, smooth turf.
The landscaper recommended Agrostis palustris because it thrives in moist soil conditions.
Due to its ability to spread quickly, Agrostis palustris is ideal for repairing damaged lawns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bentgrass - a general term for grasses of the genus Agrostis

Etymology

From New Latin, Agrostis (a genus of grasses) + Latin palustris (of marshes).
