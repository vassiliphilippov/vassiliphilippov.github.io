Dermaptera

1. NOUN

/ dɝːˈmæptərə / (AmE), / dɜːˈmæptərə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of insects comprising the earwigs and a few closely related forms, characterized by elongated bodies and forceps-like pincers at the end of the abdomen
order of insects including earwigs

Examples

The order Dermaptera includes both common earwigs and some less familiar relatives.
Biologists have studied Dermaptera to understand their unique pincers and reproductive behavior.
Specimens of Dermaptera are often collected for entomological research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earwig - a type of insect with pincer-like appendages, member of Dermaptera

Etymology

From Greek 'derma' (skin) and 'pteron' (wing), referring to the leathery forewings.

2. NOUN

/ dɜːrˈmæptərə / (AmE), / dɜːˈmæptərə / (BrE)
an order of insects that includes earwigs and a few closely related forms, characterized by pincer-like cerci and short, leathery forewings
order of insects including earwigs

Examples

The Dermaptera are known for the characteristic forceps-like pincers on their abdomens.
Many garden earwigs belong to the insect order Dermaptera.
Researchers study Dermaptera to understand their evolutionary adaptations among other insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earwig - a common insect of order Dermaptera with pincer-like appendages

Etymology

From Greek 'derma' (skin) and 'pteron' (wing).
