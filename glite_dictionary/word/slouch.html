slouch

1. NOUN

/ slaʊtʃ / (AmE), / slaʊtʃ / (BrE)
A posture in which the shoulders and head are bent forward, giving an awkward or ungainly appearance while standing or walking.
stooped posture or stance

Examples

Her slouch made her look much shorter than she actually was.
The teacher told him to fix his slouch and stand up straight.
Years of working at a desk gave him a noticeable slouch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoop - A posture with the body bent forward
  • Antonym: upright - An erect or straight posture

2. NOUN

/ slaʊtʃ / (AmE), / slaʊtʃ / (BrE)
An incompetent or unskilled person, usually used in negative statements to emphasize someone's competence.
incompetent person (informal, usually in negatives)
informal

Examples

When it comes to mathematics, she is no slouch.
Don't underestimate him—he's not a slouch in negotiations.
She's no slouch when it comes to organizing big events.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: expert - a person with a high level of skill or knowledge
  • Synonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation

3. VERB

/ slaʊtʃ / (AmE), / slaʊtʃ / (BrE)
to walk, stand, or move in a lazy or drooping way, with shoulders and head bent forward
walk or move with poor posture

Examples

He always slouches when he walks down the hall, making him look uninterested.
The students slouched into the classroom after a long day.
Try not to slouch as you walk; it makes you look tired and unconfident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slump - to walk or sit with a bent posture
  • Antonym: stand tall - stand or walk upright with good posture

Etymology

Origin unknown; possibly imitative or related to Middle English slouken, meaning to become slack.

4. VERB

/ slaʊtʃ / (AmE), / slaʊtʃ / (BrE)
to stand, sit, or move with a drooping posture, typically with shoulders and head bent forward
assume a drooping posture

Examples

He tends to slouch in his chair when he gets tired.
Don't slouch while you are standing; it looks unprofessional.
She realized she was slouching and quickly straightened her back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slump - sit, lean, or fall heavily and limply
  • Antonym: to straighten - to make or become erect or upright

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly a blend of 'slouch' (noun) and Middle English 'slouchen' (to hang loosely).
