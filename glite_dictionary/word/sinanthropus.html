Sinanthropus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈnænθrəpəs / (AmE), / saɪˈnanθrəpəs / (BrE)
a former genus name used for Peking man, an extinct early human known from fossils found near Beijing; now classified as Homo erectus
former genus of Peking man (early human)

Examples

The fossils of Sinanthropus were discovered in Zhoukoudian near Beijing in the 1920s.
For many years, scientists classified Peking man under the genus Sinanthropus.
Current research now places Sinanthropus within the species Homo erectus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Homo erectus - the current scientific name for Peking man and related early humans

Etymology

From Greek 'Sina' (China) + 'anthrōpos' (man).

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈnænθrəpəs / (AmE), / saɪˈnænθrəpəs / (BrE)
A former genus of extinct hominids to which Peking Man was previously assigned; now usually classified as Homo erectus.
former genus for Peking Man fossils

Examples

Fossils once assigned to Sinanthropus are now classified as Homo erectus.
The discovery of Sinanthropus remains in China marked a milestone in paleoanthropology.
The name Sinanthropus is no longer commonly used in modern scientific literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Homo erectus - species of early humans, now including Peking Man

Etymology

From Greek 'Sina' (China) and 'anthrōpos' (human being).
