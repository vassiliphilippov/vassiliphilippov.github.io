illation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of drawing a conclusion or making an inference based on evidence or reasoning rather than direct observation
inference or logical conclusion

Examples

The philosopher's argument rests on an illation that may not be universally accepted.
Her illation from the available data was both logical and compelling.
In critical thinking, a sound illation is vital to avoid faulty conclusions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inference - the process of reaching a conclusion based on evidence and reasoning

Etymology

From Latin illatio (a bringing in, inference), from inferre (to bring in).
