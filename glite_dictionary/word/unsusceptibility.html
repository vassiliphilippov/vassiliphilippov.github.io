unsusceptibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.səˌsɛp.təˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌʌn.səˌsɛp.tɪˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state of not being likely to be affected or influenced by something
state of not being susceptible

Examples

The new coating provides unsusceptibility to water damage, which is crucial for outdoor furniture.
His genetic makeup showed a remarkable unsusceptibility to the disease affecting others in the population.
Engineers are striving for materials whose unsusceptibility to corrosion extends the lifespan of critical components.

Synset Examples

unsusceptibility to rust

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunity - the state of being immune or resistant to something
  • Antonym: susceptibility - the quality of being likely to be influenced or harmed by something

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'susceptibility' (capacity to be affected).
