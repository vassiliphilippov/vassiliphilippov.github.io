containment

1. NOUN

/ kənˈteɪnmənt / (AmE), / kənˈteɪnmənt / (BrE)
A system designed to prevent the accidental release of radioactive material from a reactor.
system to prevent radioactive leaks from a reactor

Examples

The containment was tested to ensure it could withstand extreme conditions during an emergency.
Engineers inspected the containment for any signs of leakage after the reactor shut down.
Safety drills regularly include scenarios in which the containment must prevent the escape of radioactive materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrier - something that blocks or restricts movement

Etymology

From contain + -ment, referring to the act or means of containing.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈteɪnmənt / (AmE), / kənˈteɪnmənt / (BrE)
the action of keeping something harmful under control or within limits; the act of preventing the spread of something
the act of restraining or controlling something

Examples

International organizations worked towards the containment of the outbreak to protect neighboring countries.
Firefighters focused on the containment of the blaze before it reached the nearby woods.
The government's policy emphasized the containment of extremist groups within its borders.

Synset Examples

the containment of the AIDS epidemic
the containment of the rebellion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restraint - the action of keeping something under control
  • Antonym: spread - the process of extending over a larger area

Etymology

From contain + -ment, formed from Latin continēre (to hold together) + English suffix -ment.

3. NOUN

/ kənˈteɪnmənt / (AmE), / kənˈteɪnmənt / (BrE)
the policy of preventing the expansion of a hostile power or ideology, especially by forming strategic alliances or using diplomatic measures
policy to limit a hostile power's expansion

Examples

The doctrine of containment shaped U.S. foreign policy during the Cold War.
Some historians debate the effectiveness of containment in limiting Soviet influence in Eastern Europe.
Containment required a network of military alliances and significant financial commitment.

Synset Examples

containment of communist expansion was a central principle of United States' foreign policy from 1947 to the 1975

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deterrence - the policy of discouraging an enemy from taking an action by instilling doubt or fear of the consequences
  • Antonym: appeasement - the policy of making concessions to avoid conflict with a hostile power

Etymology

From contain + -ment; policy sense first used in 20th-century US foreign relations terminology.
