piquantness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːkəntnəs / (AmE), / ˈpiːkəntnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasantly stimulating or appetizingly sharp, especially in taste or flavor
pleasantly stimulating sharpness; appetizing quality

Examples

The piquantness of the salsa added excitement to the otherwise simple meal.
Chefs admire the piquantness that certain spices bring to their dishes.
She commented on the piquantness of the chutney, noting its lively and refreshing flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piquancy - the quality of being pleasantly stimulating or exciting to the mind or palate

Etymology

From 'piquant' + '-ness'; 'piquant' from French 'piquant' (pricking, stimulating), from piquer (to prick).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpiːkəntnəs / (AmE), / ˈpiːkəntnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasantly stimulating or exciting, especially to the mind or senses
pleasantly exciting quality

Examples

The piquantness of her conversation captivated everyone at the dinner table.
Critics praised the piquantness of the novel's dialogue and plot twists.
Some dishes stand out not only for their taste but also for the piquantness they bring to the palate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piquancy - the quality of being agreeably stimulating or interesting
  • Antonym: blandness - the quality of being dull or lacking stimulation

Etymology

Formed from 'piquant' (French, meaning 'pricking, stimulating'), with the suffix '-ness'.
