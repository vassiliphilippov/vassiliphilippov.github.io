mediaeval

1. ADJ

/ ˌmiː.diˈiː.vəl / (AmE), / ˌmiː.dɪˈiː.vəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the Middle Ages, especially its chivalry, customs, or knighthood.
of or relating to the Middle Ages

Examples

The castle is a fine example of mediaeval architecture.
She wrote her thesis on mediaeval knights and their code of conduct.
The festival recreated the look and feel of mediaeval times.

Synset Examples

chivalric rites
the knightly years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medieval - alternative spelling referring to the Middle Ages

Etymology

From Latin medium aevum (the Middle Age) via Old French médiéval.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01733389-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌmiːdiˈiːvəl / (AmE), / ˌmiːdɪˈiːvəl / (BrE)
As if belonging to the Middle Ages; old-fashioned and unenlightened.
characteristic of the Middle Ages; outdated

Examples

Her views on gender roles are so mediaeval that they shocked everyone at the meeting.
The school was criticized for using mediaeval disciplinary methods.
Some people argue that banning certain books is a mediaeval practice in today's world.

Synset Examples

a medieval attitude toward dating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medieval - belonging to or resembling the Middle Ages; very old-fashioned
  • Antonym: modern - characteristic of the present or recent times; not ancient or old-fashioned

Etymology

From Latin 'medium aevum' (the Middle Age), via Old French 'medieval', meaning 'of the Middle Ages'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01541543-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌmiːdiˈivəl / (AmE), / ˌmiːdɪˈiːvəl / (BrE)
Relating to or belonging to the Middle Ages (approximately the 5th to the late 15th century).
relating to the Middle Ages
british

Examples

The architecture of the old castle is distinctly mediaeval in style.
Her research focuses on mediaeval literature and its influence on modern society.
Many mediaeval manuscripts have survived in remarkably good condition.

Synset Examples

Medieval scholars
Medieval times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medieval - relating to the Middle Ages; standard spelling, especially in American English

Etymology

From Latin 'medium aevum' meaning 'the Middle Age'; variant spelling of 'medieval', chiefly British.
