peeress

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪrˌɛs / (AmE), / ˈpɪə.rɛs / (BrE)
a woman who holds the rank of peer or is the wife or widow of a peer, especially in the British peerage system.
a woman of the British peerage
british

Examples

The peeress attended the royal banquet in her ceremonial robes.
After her husband's death, she became a peeress in her own right.
Several peeresses spoke in the House of Lords during the debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peer - a member of the British nobility

Etymology

Formed from 'peer', meaning a noble, with the feminine suffix '-ess'.
