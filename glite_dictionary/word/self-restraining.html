self-restraining

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf rɪˈstreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf rɪˈstreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
exercising control over one's own actions, feelings, or desires; refraining from indulgence
exercising self-control

Examples

He is known for his self-restraining attitude, even when others give in to temptation.
A self-restraining person can avoid unhealthy habits more easily than others.
Her self-restraining nature helped her stay focused during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-controlled - having control over one's own actions and emotions
  • Antonym: indulgent - allowing oneself or others to enjoy pleasures freely

Etymology

From 'self' + 'restraining', based on 'restrain' from Old French 'restraindre', Latin 'restringere' (to bind back).
