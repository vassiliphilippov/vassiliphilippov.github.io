Cercidium floridum

1. NOUN

/ sərˈsɪdiəm flɔːˈrɪdəm / (AmE), / səˈsɪdɪəm flɒˈrɪdəm / (BrE)
a spiny, densely branched tree native to the southwestern United States, known for its showy yellow flowers and blue-green bark; also called blue palo verde
a spiny southwestern US tree with yellow flowers

Examples

The Cercidium floridum is widely used in desert landscaping for its resilience and colorful blooms.
Blue-green bark is a distinctive feature of Cercidium floridum in arid regions.
Botanists identified the tree as Cercidium floridum during their field survey in Arizona.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Cercidium' is the genus name and 'floridum' means 'flowery'.
