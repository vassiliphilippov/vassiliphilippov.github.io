aqualung

1. NOUN

/ ˈækwəˌlʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈakwəˌlʌŋ / (BrE)
a portable breathing apparatus for divers, allowing them to breathe underwater; originally a brand name, now a generic term for scuba equipment
underwater breathing apparatus for divers

Examples

The diver checked her aqualung before descending into the coral reef.
Early underwater explorers relied on an aqualung to study marine life at depth.
Proper maintenance of your aqualung is crucial for safe and enjoyable dives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scuba - equipment enabling divers to breathe underwater; acronym for self-contained underwater breathing apparatus

Etymology

Registered trademark of Aqua-Lung, invented by Jacques Cousteau and Émile Gagnan in the 1940s, from English 'aqua' (water) + 'lung'.
