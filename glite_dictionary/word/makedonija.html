Makedonija

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɑːkɛˈdoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌmakɛˈdəʊniə / (BrE)
an ancient kingdom in the southeastern Balkans, ruled by Philip II and Alexander the Great, now divided among parts of modern North Macedonia, Greece, and Bulgaria
ancient Balkan kingdom, home of Alexander the Great

Examples

The rise of Makedonija under Philip II changed the course of ancient history.
Archaeologists have uncovered many relics from Makedonija in the modern region of northern Greece.
Texts from antiquity frequently mention the rulers of Makedonija and their campaigns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Macedonia - historical region and ancient kingdom in the Balkans

Etymology

From the ancient Greek 'Makedonía' (Μακεδονία), the name for Macedonia.
