acetal

1. NOUN

/ ˈæsəˌtæl / (AmE), / ˈæsɪtæl / (BrE)
an organic compound formed when two alcohol molecules react with an aldehyde; commonly used as a solvent or intermediate in chemical synthesis
organic compound formed from alcohol and aldehyde
medical

Examples

The chemist synthesized a new acetal as an intermediate for the pharmaceutical process.
Acetals are often used to protect carbonyl groups during organic synthesis.
The stability of an acetal depends on the nature of its substituent groups.

Etymology

From German Acetal, formed from 'acet-' (relating to acetic acid) and 'al' (aldehyde).
