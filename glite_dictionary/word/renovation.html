renovation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛnəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛnəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of restoring a building or object to a former good condition
restoration to former good condition

Examples

The old mansion was closed for renovation after many years of neglect.
Many businesses remain open during renovation to minimize losses.
After a major renovation, the library now looks as good as new.

Synset Examples

the inn was a renovation of a Colonial house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restoration - the act of bringing something back to its original condition

Etymology

From Latin 'renovatio' (renewal), from 'renovare' (to renew).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛnəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛnəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of improving something by repairing, updating, or restoring it to a better condition
improving by repairing or updating

Examples

After months of renovation, the historic theater finally reopened to the public.
They decided a complete renovation of the kitchen was necessary before moving in.
The museum closed for extensive renovation to modernize its galleries.

Synset Examples

they are pursuing a general program of renovation to the entire property
a major overhaul of the healthcare system was proposed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restoration - the act of returning something to a former condition
  • Synonym: repair - to restore something broken or damaged to good condition
  • Antonym: decay - the process of declining or falling into disrepair

Etymology

From Latin 'renovatio', from 'renovare' (to renew).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
