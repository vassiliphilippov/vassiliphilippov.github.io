Quercus mongolica

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs mɒnˈɡɑlɪkə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs mɒŋˈɡɒlɪkə / (BrE)
A species of oak tree native to East Asia, known for its moderately light, fine-grained wood, especially found in Japan, China, Korea, and Russia.
East Asian oak tree species

Examples

The wood of the Quercus mongolica is often used in traditional Japanese carpentry.
Botanists are studying the distribution of Quercus mongolica across northern Asia.
Many urban parks in Korea feature the resilient Quercus mongolica among their tree species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - A tree or shrub in the genus Quercus, bearing acorns as fruit

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' meaning 'oak' and 'mongolica' referring to Mongolia, indicating its native range.
