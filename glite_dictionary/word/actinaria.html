Actinaria

1. NOUN

/ ækˈtɪnəriə / (AmE), / ækˈtɪnəriə / (BrE)
An order of marine animals, commonly known as sea anemones, characterized by their colorful, fleshy bodies and numerous tentacles.
order of sea anemones

Examples

The coral reef was home to several species belonging to the Actinaria.
Scientists study Actinaria to understand the evolution of symbiotic relationships in marine ecosystems.
Many brightly colored members of the Actinaria are popular in public aquariums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea anemone - marine animals with tentacles, order Actinaria

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'aktis' (ray) + '-aria' (relating to).
