multiculturalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌltiˈkʌltʃərəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌmʌltiˈkʌltʃərəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or policy that several different cultures can coexist harmoniously and with equal rights in a single country or society
belief in peaceful coexistence of multiple cultures

Examples

The country embraced multiculturalism to promote social harmony among its diverse population.
Debates about multiculturalism are common in many societies with immigrants from numerous backgrounds.
Educational institutions often encourage multiculturalism to foster respect and understanding among students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pluralism - a system in which multiple groups, beliefs, or sources of authority coexist
  • Antonym: assimilation - the process by which a minority group adopts the customs and attitudes of the prevailing culture

Etymology

Formed from 'multi-' (Latin for 'many') + 'culturalism'.
