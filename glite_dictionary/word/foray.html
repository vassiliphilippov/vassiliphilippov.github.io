foray

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːreɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɒreɪ / (BrE)
a sudden or brief attack, especially to obtain something or make a raid into enemy territory
a sudden brief attack or raid

Examples

The army launched a foray into enemy territory before dawn.
Her first foray into journalism was covering local events for the newspaper.
Vikings were known for making sudden forays along the coastlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raid - a sudden attack on a place by soldiers or other armed forces
  • Synonym: assault - a sudden violent attack

Etymology

From Old French 'forrer' (to forage or pillage) and Middle English 'forrayen'.

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɒr.eɪ / (BrE)
an initial attempt or venture, especially into an unfamiliar area or subject
an initial attempt or venture

Examples

Her brief foray into acting ended after just one film.
The tech company's foray into the automotive market surprised many analysts.
He made his first foray into politics last year.

Synset Examples

scientists' forays into politics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: venture - a risky or daring journey or undertaking
  • Synonym: expedition - a journey undertaken for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Old French 'forrer' (to forage, raid), from Frankish *forrōn (to pillage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00788961-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈfɔːˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɒr.eɪ / (BrE)
to make a brief or sudden attack or incursion into enemy territory; to venture into an area or activity briefly and often for the first time
to make a brief incursion or attack

Examples

The soldiers decided to foray into enemy land under the cover of darkness.
For months, the scouts would foray across the border to gather intelligence.
She decided to foray into the competitive world of business for the first time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raid - a sudden attack, especially on enemy territory
  • Antonym: retreat - to withdraw from enemy territory or an activity

Etymology

From Middle English *forrayen* (to forage, plunder), from Old French *forrer* (to forage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01231199-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈfɔːˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɒr.eɪ / (BrE)
to make a sudden attack or raid in order to steal goods; to plunder or loot
to raid or loot

Examples

During the chaos, several youths forayed into the abandoned shops for valuables.
Vikings often forayed neighboring villages for food and treasures.
In the lawless period, gangs would foray rural estates and seize whatever they could carry.

Synset Examples

During the earthquake people looted the stores that were deserted by their owners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loot - to steal goods from a place, typically during a war or riot
  • Synonym: pillage - to rob a place using violence, especially in wartime
  • Synonym: plunder - to steal goods, typically using force and in a time of disorder

Etymology

From Middle English *forrayen*, from Old French *forayer* (to forage), from *forêt* (forest).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
