palaeobiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊbaɪˈɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpæliəʊbaɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of paleontology concerned with the study of the origin, development, and structure of fossil organisms as living entities.
study of fossil organisms as living beings
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in palaeobiology because she was fascinated by the evolution of prehistoric life.
Recent discoveries in palaeobiology have changed our understanding of early plant development.
Many advances in palaeobiology rely on computer simulations to reconstruct ancient ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paleontology - the scientific study of life in the geologic past, especially through fossils

Etymology

From Greek 'palaios' (ancient) + 'bios' (life) + 'logia' (study).
