inexperience

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnɪkˈspɪriəns / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪkˈspɪəriəns / (BrE)
lack of knowledge, skill, or judgment that comes from not having enough experience in a particular activity or situation.
lack of experience or skill

Examples

Her inexperience showed when she struggled to answer basic questions during the interview.
Due to his inexperience, he made several mistakes completing the assignment.
The team lost the game, largely because of the inexperience of its new players.

Synset Examples

procedural inexperience created difficulties
their poor behavior was due to the rawness of the troops

Related Terms

  • Antonym: experience - knowledge or skill gained by doing or seeing things
  • Antonym: expertise - special skill or knowledge in a particular field
  • Synonym: naivety - lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + experience (from Latin experientia, trial, experience).
