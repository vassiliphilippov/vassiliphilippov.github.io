exemplary

1. ADJ

/ ɪgˈzɛm.plə.ri / (AmE), / ɪgˈzɛm.plə.ri / (BrE)
serving as a typical example or illustration of a particular kind or quality
serving as a typical example

Examples

His achievements are truly exemplary of dedication and perseverance.
The city is exemplary for its efforts in promoting environmental sustainability.
Her community service is exemplary of the positive impact one individual can have.

Synset Examples

the free discussion that is emblematic of democracy
an action exemplary of his conduct

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emblematic - serving as a symbol or representative of something
  • Synonym: typical - having the usual qualities of a particular type

Etymology

From Latin exemplaris (serving as a pattern), from exemplum (example).

2. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɛmplɛri / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛmpləri / (BrE)
serving as a warning or deterrent; intended to set an example to discourage similar behavior in others, especially by means of punishment
serving as a warning or deterrent

Examples

The judge imposed an exemplary fine to discourage others from committing the same crime.
The company received an exemplary penalty for their environmental violations.
Her exemplary suspension served as a warning to other employees about breaking the rules.

Synset Examples

shook a monitory finger at him
an exemplary jail sentence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punitive - intended as punishment

Etymology

From Latin exemplaris (serving as an example), from exemplum (example).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01775648-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɛmpləri / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛmpləri / (BrE)
Deserving to be imitated; serving as a desirable model.
worthy of imitation; model

Examples

The teacher gave an exemplary explanation that helped everyone understand the concept.
Her exemplary conduct during the crisis impressed her colleagues.
The volunteers set an exemplary standard for community service.

Synset Examples

exemplary behavior
model citizens

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - being a standard or example for others to follow
  • Antonym: imperfect - not ideal or not worthy of imitation

Etymology

From Late Latin exemplaris (serving as an example), from Latin exemplum (example).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02596887-s
Synset POS: s
