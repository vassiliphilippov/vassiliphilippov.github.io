infinite

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.fə.nət / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fɪ.nət / (BrE)
extremely large or numerous; too many to be counted
too numerous to be counted

Examples

There seemed to be an infinite number of stars visible in the night sky.
She had infinite patience with her students, no matter how challenging the day became.
The company faces infinite possibilities as it expands into new markets.

Synset Examples

countless hours
an infinite number of reasons
innumerable difficulties
the multitudinous seas
myriad stars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countless - too many to be counted
  • Synonym: innumerable - too numerous to be counted
  • Antonym: finite - having limits or bounds

Etymology

From Latin infinitus, meaning 'unbounded' or 'endless'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.fə.nət / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fɪ.nət / (BrE)
without limits in extent, size, number, or degree; limitless or endless.
without limits; endless

Examples

The universe is often described as infinite in all directions.
She seemed to have infinite patience when dealing with children.
Mathematics deals with infinite series that never come to an end.

Synset Examples

the infinite ingenuity of man
infinite wealth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: finite - having limits or bounds

Etymology

From Latin 'infinitus', from 'in-' (not) + 'finis' (end).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.fə.nət / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fɪ.nət / (BrE)
an unlimited expanse or space; boundless or limitless extent, especially as a concept in philosophy, mathematics, or cosmology
limitless expanse or space (noun)

Examples

The philosopher spoke of the mysteries hidden within the infinite.
For centuries, people have gazed at the night sky, pondering the vastness of the infinite.
Mathematicians often contemplate the properties of the infinite in their theories.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infinity - the state or quality of being infinite; boundlessness

Etymology
