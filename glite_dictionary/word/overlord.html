overlord

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌlɔrd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəlɔːd / (BrE)
a person with general authority or control over others, especially one who rules over subordinate leaders or landholders
person with supreme authority over others

Examples

After the conquest, the king became the overlord of the entire region.
The local barons were required to pay tribute to their overlord.
He acted like an overlord, making decisions for everyone without their input.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superior - a person higher in rank or position
  • Antonym: underling - a person lower in rank or subordinate to another

Etymology

From Middle English, from over- (above) + lord (ruler).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vɚˌlɔrd / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.və.lɔːd / (BrE)
a person of great power or authority who exercises control over others, especially in a domineering or oppressive way
person with dominant power or control

Examples

The region was governed by a cruel overlord who imposed harsh taxes.
She acted as the overlord of the project, making all the key decisions.
After the takeover, the new overlord quickly asserted his dominance.

Synset Examples

The game features an overlord character that players must defeat.
She felt like the overlord of her chaotic household.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruler - a person who governs or exercises authority
  • Antonym: underling - a person lower in status or rank, subordinate

Etymology

From Middle English overlord, from over- (above) + lord.
