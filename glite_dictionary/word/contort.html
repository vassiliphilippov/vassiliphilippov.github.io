to contort

1. VERB

/ kənˈtɔrt / (AmE), / kənˈtɔːt / (BrE)
to twist or bend something out of its normal shape, often in a violent or unnatural way
twist out of shape

Examples

Her face began to contort with pain as the cramp intensified.
The metal bars seemed to contort under the intense heat of the fire.
He could contort his body into shapes that seemed impossible for most people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twist - to turn something so that it has a different shape or position
  • Synonym: to distort - to pull or twist out of shape; to alter the normal appearance

Etymology

From Latin contorquere, meaning 'to twist together'.
