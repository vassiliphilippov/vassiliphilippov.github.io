boneset

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊnˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈbəʊnˌsɛt / (BrE)
a North American perennial herb (Eupatorium perfoliatum) with clusters of white flowers, formerly used in traditional medicine
a medicinal North American herb

Examples

The old herbal remedies included boneset for reducing fevers and inflammation.
She planted boneset in her garden to attract butterflies and bees.
Early settlers brewed boneset tea as a folk remedy for colds and flu.

Etymology

Named for its traditional use in treating 'breakbone fever' (dengue) and as a supposed remedy for broken bones.

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊnˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈbəʊnˌsɛt / (BrE)
a European herb with small white, pink, or purple flowers, naturalized as a weed in North America; also known for species used in traditional medicine
European herb with small flowers, weed in North America

Examples

The field was full of boneset and other wildflowers in late summer.
Her grandmother brewed a tea from boneset to ease the symptoms of a cold.
Gardeners sometimes remove boneset because it spreads rapidly in moist soil.

Etymology

From its traditional use in folk medicine to treat bone fractures and fevers.
