humourist

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmərɪst / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmərɪst / (BrE)
a person who writes, speaks, or performs in an amusing or humorous way; a creator or performer of humor
someone who creates or presents humor
british

Examples

The well-known humourist kept the audience laughing throughout the evening.
Her dream is to become a published humourist and share her funny stories with the world.
A talented humourist often finds creative ways to make people smile even in difficult times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humorist - someone who acts, speaks or writes in an amusing way (American spelling)

Etymology

From humour + -ist, modelled after 'humorist', derived from the English word 'humour'.
