Habenaria

1. NOUN

/ hɑːbəˈnɛriə / (AmE), / habɪˈnɑːriə / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly terrestrial orchids with tuberous or fleshy roots, often having long slender spurs and divided petals or lip lobes
a genus of terrestrial orchids

Examples

The rare Habenaria bloomed late in the summer, displaying delicate white flowers.
Several species within the Habenaria genus are prized by collectors for their unusual floral structures.
Botanists discovered a new type of Habenaria in the mountainous region last year.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Swedish botanist Olof Habenarius's name.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhæbəˈnɛriə / (AmE), / ˌhabəˈnɛːrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of mostly terrestrial orchids with tubers or fleshy roots, often having long slender spurs, petals, and lobed lips; includes several species formerly classified under Gymnadeniopsis.
a genus of terrestrial orchids

Examples

The rare Habenaria species was spotted blooming in the rainforest.
Many Habenaria orchids are known for their delicate, spurred flowers.
Botanists collected several Habenaria specimens for study in the lab.

Etymology

From Latin *habena* (rein, strap), referring to the shape of the flower's spur.
