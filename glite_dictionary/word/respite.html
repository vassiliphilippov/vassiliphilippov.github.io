respite

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛspɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛspɪt / (BrE)
A short period of rest or relief from something difficult or unpleasant.
short period of relief or rest

Examples

After working for hours, we were grateful for a brief respite from our tasks.
The rain provided a welcome respite from the summer heat.
She took a respite from her studies to relax in the garden.

Synset Examples

we took a 10-minute break
he took time out to recuperate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprieve - a temporary relief or delay from something unpleasant

Etymology

From Latin 'respitum' (refuge, consideration), from 'respicio' (to look back).

4. VERB

/ ˈrɛs.paɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛs.pɪt / (BrE)
(law) To postpone the punishment of a convicted person, especially a death sentence.
postpone a criminal's punishment
legal

Examples

The governor decided to respite the execution, granting the prisoner more time to appeal.
The judge may respite a sentence in cases of new evidence.
The president has the power to respite the punishment of convicted criminals under certain circumstances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprieve - to cancel or postpone the punishment of someone, especially a criminal sentenced to death

Etymology

From Old French *respiter* (to delay), from Latin *respectare* (to look back, delay).
