possibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌpɒsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the fact or state of being possible; a chance that something may happen or be true
a chance that something may happen

Examples

There's a possibility of rain tomorrow, so bring an umbrella just in case.
He hasn't ruled out the possibility of moving abroad for work.
The doctors are exploring every possibility to treat her illness.

Synset Examples

there is a possibility that his sense of smell has been impaired

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: probability - likelihood or chance of something happening
  • Antonym: impossibility - state of being impossible

Etymology

From Latin possibilitas, from possibilis (that can be done).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌpɒsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
A thing that may happen, be done, or be true; a possible alternative or option.
a possible alternative or option

Examples

There is a real possibility of rain this afternoon, so you should take an umbrella.
The company is considering every possibility before making a final decision.
Moving to another city is one possibility they are seriously discussing.

Synset Examples

bankruptcy is always a possibility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: option - something that may be chosen
  • Antonym: impossibility - something that cannot occur or be done

Etymology

From Latin 'possibilitas', from 'possibilis' (that can be done).

3. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌpɒsəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
a chance or prospect that something may happen or exist, especially in the future; potential for development or success
prospect or potential for something to occur

Examples

The empty lot offers an exciting possibility for building a new park.
She considered every possibility before making her decision.
There's a real possibility that the team will win the championship this year.

Synset Examples

this room has great possibilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potential - the capacity to develop into something in the future
  • Antonym: impossibility - something that cannot happen or exist

Etymology

From Latin possibilitas, from possibilis (able to be done).
