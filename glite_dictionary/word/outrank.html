to outrank

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈræŋk / (AmE), / aʊtˈræŋk / (BrE)
to have a higher rank or position than someone else; to be superior in rank or status
have a higher rank than someone

Examples

In the company hierarchy, vice presidents usually outrank directors.
Despite his young age, she outranks most of her colleagues in the army.
The general's authority outranked that of every other officer in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surpass - to be greater than or superior to
  • Synonym: to outrun - to run faster than

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' (meaning 'beyond, surpass') + 'rank'.
