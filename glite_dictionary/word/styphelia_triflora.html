Styphelia triflora

1. NOUN

/ staɪˈfili.ə traɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / stɪˈfɛli.ə trɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A heathlike shrub native to southwestern Australia, known for its sharply scented leaves, pink flowers, and pentagonal fruit.
Australian shrub with scented foliage and pink flowers

Examples

The garden featured a beautiful specimen of Styphelia triflora in full bloom.
Botanists often admire Styphelia triflora for its striking pink flowers and distinctive scent.
In southwestern Australia, Styphelia triflora is a common sight in heathland areas.

Etymology

From genus Styphelia and Latin 'tri-' (three) + '-flora' (flowered), referring to the floral structure.
