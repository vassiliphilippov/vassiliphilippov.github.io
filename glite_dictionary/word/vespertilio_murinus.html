Vespertilio murinus

1. NOUN

/ vɛspɚˈtɪlioʊ mjuˈraɪnəs / (AmE), / vɛspəˈtɪlɪəʊ mjʊˈrɪnəs / (BrE)
a common Eurasian bat with white-tipped hairs in its coat, also known as the parti-coloured bat
a species of Eurasian bat

Examples

The Vespertilio murinus is often found near water bodies throughout Eurasia.
Researchers studied the unique coloration of the Vespertilio murinus for clues about its habitat.
At dusk, the Vespertilio murinus emerges from its roost to feed on flying insects.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vespertilio' (bat) and 'murinus' (mouse-like).
