confined

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfaɪnd / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪnd / (BrE)
(of a disease or tumor) restricted to a specific area and not spreading to surrounding healthy tissue.
restricted to a specific area (medical)
medical

Examples

The biopsy revealed that the tumor was confined and had not spread beyond the original site.
Early-stage cancers are often confined to a single organ, making treatment more effective.
Doctors were relieved to find the infection confined to the lower lobe of the lung.

2. ADJ

/ kənˈfaɪnd / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪnd / (BrE)
kept within a particular space; unable to leave or escape, especially as a result of captivity or restriction
kept captive or restricted

Examples

The rescued animals had spent years confined in tiny cages.
Due to illness, she was confined to her room for several weeks.
The prisoners remained confined within the high walls until their release.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprisoned - kept in prison or captivity
  • Antonym: free - not confined or restricted

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'confine', from Old French 'confiner', from Latin 'confinare' (to border, limit).

3. ADJ

/ kənˈfaɪnd / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪnd / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) forced to stay in a particular place; not free to move or leave.
not free to move about

Examples

The patients are confined to their rooms for safety reasons.
After the accident, he was confined to a wheelchair for several months.
Many animals in zoos are confined to small enclosures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unconfined - not restricted; free to move or go anywhere
  • Synonym: restricted - limited in movement or access

Etymology

From the verb 'confine', from Latin 'confinare' (to border, limit).
