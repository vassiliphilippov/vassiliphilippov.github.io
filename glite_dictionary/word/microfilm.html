microfilm

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkroʊˌfɪlm / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌfɪlm / (BrE)
a length of film on which printed materials are photographed in a greatly reduced size for storage and reading by magnification
photographic film for miniature document storage

Examples

The library keeps old newspapers on microfilm to save space.
Researchers viewed census records on microfilm using a special reader.
She requested a copy of the article from the microfilm archive.

Etymology

From micro- (small) + film, first used in the 1920s.

2. VERB

/ ˈmaɪ.kroʊˌfɪlm / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.krəʊ.fɪlm / (BrE)
to record documents or images on microfilm for storage or preservation.
record on microfilm

Examples

The archivist decided to microfilm the collection to preserve it for future generations.
They have already microfilmed all the old newspapers for easier access and storage.
We are currently microfilming rare manuscripts to minimize handling of the originals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photograph - to make a photograph of something

Etymology

From 'micro-' (small) and 'film', first used in the early 20th century with advances in microphotography.
