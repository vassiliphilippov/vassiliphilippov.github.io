Nereid

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪr.i.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈnɪəriːɪd / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, any of the 50 sea nymphs who were daughters of the sea god Nereus and Doris, often depicted as beautiful maidens inhabiting the Mediterranean Sea.
sea nymph of Greek mythology

Examples

The painting portrays a Nereid riding gracefully on the back of a dolphin.
According to legend, each Nereid had her own unique beauty and style.
Ancient sailors believed a Nereid could protect them during perilous sea journeys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naiad - a water nymph in Greek mythology associated with fresh water

Etymology

From Greek 'Nēreïs,' daughter of Nereus.
