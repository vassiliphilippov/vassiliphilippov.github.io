ally

1. NOUN

/ ˈæ.laɪ / (AmE), / ˈæ.laɪ / (BrE)
a country that supports and cooperates with another country, especially in times of war or conflict
friendly nation; supporter

Examples

The United States regarded Britain as its most important ally during World War II.
Several small countries became allies to protect themselves from invasion.
As an ally of France, Canada provided military support in the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enemy - a country that opposes or fights against another country
  • Degree Lower: friend - a person or entity who supports or helps another

Etymology

From Latin alligare (to bind to), via Old French alier.

2. VERB

/ əˈlaɪ / (AmE), / əˈlaɪ / (BrE)
to join or unite with another person, group, or country for a common purpose or in support of a cause
join or unite for a common purpose

Examples

Several small nations chose to ally themselves with the larger neighboring country for protection.
After years of competition, the two companies decided to ally to develop new technology.
He refused to ally with any political party, preferring to remain independent.

Synset Examples

He allied himself with the Communists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: associate - to connect with as a partner or ally

Etymology

From Latin 'alligare' meaning 'to bind to'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02435038-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈæˌlaɪ / (AmE), / ˈælaɪ / (BrE)
a person, group, or country that supports or helps another in a common purpose
an associate who helps or supports another

Examples

She became a trusted ally during the negotiations.
The two countries remained allies throughout the war.
It's important to have an ally when facing tough challenges.

Synset Examples

he's a good ally in fight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friend - a person you know well and like
  • Antonym: enemy - a person who actively opposes someone or something

Etymology

From Latin *alligare* (to bind to), via Old French *alier*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09804483-n
Synset POS: n
