handful

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈhan(d)fʊl / (BrE)
the amount that can be held in one hand
quantity that fits in one hand

Examples

She grabbed a handful of nuts before leaving the house.
He tossed a handful of coins onto the counter.
The child picked a handful of flowers from the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fistful - the quantity that can be held in a closed fist

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈhænd.fəl / (AmE), / ˈhæn(d)fʊl / (BrE)
A small number of people or things; a small quantity.
a small number or amount

Examples

Only a handful of students attended the optional seminar.
She picked up a handful of coins from her purse.
There were just a handful of spectators at the early morning match.

Synset Examples

only a handful of responses were received

Related Terms

  • Synonym: few - a small number of people or things
  • Antonym: many - a large number of people or things
