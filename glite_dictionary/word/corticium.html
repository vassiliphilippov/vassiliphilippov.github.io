Corticium

1. NOUN

/ kɔːrˈtɪsiəm / (AmE), / kɔːˈtɪsɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of fungi with smooth, simple fruiting bodies; some species are parasitic on wood or crops
genus of smooth fungi, some parasitic

Examples

The fungus Corticium can cause significant disease in agricultural crops.
A specimen of Corticium was discovered growing on decaying wood in the forest.
Some species within the genus Corticium are notorious for their destructive impact on trees.

Etymology

From Latin 'corticium' (covering), from 'cortex' meaning 'bark'.

2. NOUN

/ kɔrˈtɪsiəm / (AmE), / kɔːˈtɪsiəm / (BrE)
a genus of fungi with simple, smooth sporophores, some of which are parasitic on wood or economic crops
genus of fungi with smooth sporophores

Examples

The fungal genus Corticium includes species that are notable plant pathogens.
Researchers identified a Corticium infection affecting the wooden structure of the old barn.
Some species of Corticium were once classified in the genus Rhizoctinia.

Etymology

From Latin 'corticium' meaning 'pertaining to bark.'
