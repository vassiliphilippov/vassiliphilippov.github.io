Pycnanthemum virginianum

1. NOUN

/ pɪkˌnænˈθiːməm vɜːrˌdʒɪˈneɪnəm / (AmE), / pɪkˌnænˈθiːməm vɜːdʒɪˈneɪnəm / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to the eastern United States, with narrow, aromatic leaves and small greenish flowers, commonly known as Virginia mountain mint.
aromatic herb, Virginia mountain mint

Examples

Gardeners often plant Pycnanthemum virginianum to attract butterflies and bees.
Crushing the leaves of Pycnanthemum virginianum releases a strong minty aroma.
Pycnanthemum virginianum is valued for its role in supporting native pollinators in restoration projects.

Etymology

From Greek 'pyknos' (dense) and 'anthemon' (flower); 'virginianum' referring to Virginia, USA.
