Chrysanthemum cinerariifolium

1. PROPN

/ krəˌsænθəˌməm sɪˌnɛrəriəˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / krɪˌsænθɪˌməm sɪˌnɛrəriəˈfəʊliəm / (BrE)
A species of white-flowered plant native to the Balkan region, known for its pinnate, silky-hairy leaves and as a primary source of natural pyrethrum insecticide.
white-flowered Balkan plant, source of pyrethrum
medical

Examples

The extract from Chrysanthemum cinerariifolium is widely used as an organic insecticide in agriculture.
Farmers in the Balkans cultivate Chrysanthemum cinerariifolium for its valuable pyrethrum-rich flowers.
Studying Chrysanthemum cinerariifolium can offer insights into the development of natural pest control methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrethrum - insecticidal preparation from certain chrysanthemum flowers

Etymology

From Greek *chrysos* (gold) and *anthemon* (flower) for genus; species name from Latin, meaning 'ash-colored leaves.'
