sweater

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɛt̬ər / (AmE), / ˈswɛt.ə / (BrE)
A warm knitted or crocheted garment worn on the upper part of the body, usually with long sleeves and no opening at the front.
knitted garment for the upper body

Examples

She pulled on her favorite sweater before heading out into the chilly morning.
My grandmother knitted me a blue sweater for my birthday.
He tied his sweater around his waist when it got too warm outside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jumper - British term for a knitted garment covering the upper body

Etymology

From 'sweat' + '-er', originally referring to a garment worn to induce sweating during exercise.

2. NOUN

/ ˈswɛdər / (AmE), / ˈswɛtə / (BrE)
a person who sweats, particularly someone who perspires noticeably or excessively
person who sweats

Examples

As a sweater, she always carried a handkerchief to wipe her brow.
Some people are naturally heavy sweaters during exercise.
He became self-conscious about being a sweater in warm weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perspirer - a person who perspires

Etymology

From the verb 'sweat' + -er (agent noun suffix).
