commuting

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmjuː.tɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈmjuː.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the regular travel between home and work or school, especially over a considerable distance
regular travel to work or school

Examples

Commuting takes up a large part of her morning every day.
The rise of remote work has changed traditional commuting patterns in major cities.
He listened to audiobooks during his daily commuting to pass the time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commute - to travel regularly between home and work

Etymology

From the verb 'commute', which derives from Latin 'commutare' (to change, exchange).
