devitalization

1. NOUN

/ diˌvaɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌvaɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of depriving something of vitality or energy
removal or reduction of vitality

Examples

The devitalization of the company led to lower employee morale and productivity.
Certain chemicals can cause the devitalization of plant tissue during laboratory experiments.
The surgeon performed a devitalization of the nerve to alleviate the patient's pain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: revitalization - the act of restoring vitality

Etymology

From de- (removal of) + vital (from Latin vitalis, meaning 'of life') + -ization.
