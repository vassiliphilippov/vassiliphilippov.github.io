smelling

1. ADJ

/ ˈsmɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsmɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
having a noticeable or distinct smell, especially used with 'of' or 'with'.
having a noticeable smell

Examples

The kitchen was smelling of freshly baked bread when I walked in.
His coat came back from the camp smelling of smoke and pine needles.
The garden was smelling with the fragrance of blooming roses after the rain.

Synset Examples

the hall was redolent of floor wax
air redolent with the fumes of beer and whiskey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redolent - strongly reminiscent of or suggestive of (something); fragrant or evocative

2. NOUN

/ ˈsmɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsmɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of perceiving an odor through the nose
act of perceiving an odor

Examples

Smelling freshly baked bread always reminds me of my grandmother’s kitchen.
The dog’s keen sense of smelling helped it find the missing child.
She lost her sense of smelling after the illness, which made eating less enjoyable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sniffing - the act of drawing air audibly up the nose to detect an odor

Etymology

From the verb 'smell' + -ing, forming the gerund or verbal noun.
