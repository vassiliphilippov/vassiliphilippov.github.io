Abkhazia

1. PROPN

/ æbˈkɑziə / (AmE), / æbˈkɑːziə / (BrE)
a region in the Caucasus recognized by some states as an independent republic but considered by most of the international community as part of Georgia
region/republic in the Caucasus

Examples

She traveled to Abkhazia to conduct research on the region's political situation.
Recent talks failed to resolve the dispute over the status of Abkhazia.
The coastline of Abkhazia along the Black Sea is known for its natural beauty.

Etymology

From Russian Абха́зия (Abkháziya), ultimately from the native Abkhaz name Аҧсны (Apsny).
