Mammon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæmən / (AmE), / ˈmæmən / (BrE)
(in the Bible) wealth or material riches regarded as an evil influence or false object of worship and devotion.
personification of wealth and greed (Biblical)

Examples

In the sermon, the preacher warned against worshipping Mammon above all else.
Many believe that modern society is increasingly devoted to the pursuit of Mammon.
The novel depicts a world corrupted by the influence of Mammon.

Synset Examples

ye cannot serve God and Mammon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avarice - extreme greed for wealth
  • Synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth
  • Antonym: charity - generosity and helpfulness toward the needy

Etymology

From Late Latin Mammon, from Greek Mamōnas, from Aramaic māmōnā (wealth, riches).
