banyan

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæn.jən / (AmE), / ˈbæn.jən / (BrE)
a loose-fitting jacket or gown, traditionally worn at home, especially by men, originally from India
loose-fitting jacket or gown, originally from India

Examples

In colonial times, European gentlemen often wore a banyan while relaxing at home.
The portrait shows the scholar dressed in a richly patterned banyan.
He changed out of his formal coat and into a comfortable banyan for the evening.

Etymology

From Hindi 'bānyān', originally referring to a type of garment worn by Indian merchants.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæn.jən / (AmE), / ˈbæn.jən / (BrE)
A large East Indian fig tree (Ficus benghalensis) that produces aerial roots which grow down into the soil to form additional trunks.
East Indian tree with aerial roots; Ficus benghalensis

Examples

The enormous banyan tree in the village square provides shade for everyone.
A banyan can spread across a large area as its aerial roots become new trunks.
Many pilgrims gathered under the ancient banyan for shelter during the monsoon.

Etymology

From Portuguese *banyan*, from Gujarati *vāṇiyo* (merchant), as banyan trees often sheltered merchants.
