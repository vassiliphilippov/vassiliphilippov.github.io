avenged

1. ADJ

/ əˈvɛndʒd / (AmE), / əˈvɛndʒd / (BrE)
(of a wrong or injury) for which revenge or retribution has been taken.
having had revenge taken for

Examples

The murder of his brother was finally avenged after many years.
In the legend, the hero's father's death is avenged with great cunning.
The injustice was avenged by those who believed in his cause.

Synset Examples

an avenged injury

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unavenged - (of a wrong or injury) for which no revenge has been taken

Etymology

Past participle of 'avenge', from Old French 'avengier', from Latin 'vindicare' (to claim, avenge).
