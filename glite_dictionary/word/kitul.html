kitul

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪtuːl / (AmE), / ˈkɪtuːl / (BrE)
a tall palm tree native to India and Southeast Asia (Caryota urens), whose sap is used to produce a brown sugar called jaggery and whose trunk pith yields sago.
a palm tree producing jaggery and sago

Examples

The locals tap the kitul to collect sweet sap for making traditional jaggery.
Sago derived from the pith of the kitul is used as a food staple in some regions.
A kitul in the garden can provide both shade and ingredients for various desserts.

Etymology

From Sinhalese *kitul*, referring to the Caryota urens palm.
