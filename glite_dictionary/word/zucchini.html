zucchini

1. NOUN

/ zuˈkini / (AmE), / zʊˈkiːni / (BrE)
A type of summer squash, usually dark green, whose immature fruit is eaten as a vegetable.
type of summer squash

Examples

She sliced the zucchini thinly and added it to the salad.
Grilled zucchini makes a perfect side dish for summer meals.
The recipe calls for one large zucchini, grated and drained.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courgette - British term for the same vegetable as zucchini

Etymology

From Italian 'zucchini', plural of 'zucchino' (small squash), diminutive of 'zucca' (gourd).

2. NOUN

/ zuˈkini / (AmE), / zuˈkiːni / (BrE)
A long, green vegetable of the squash family with smooth, dark skin and mild flavour, eaten cooked as a vegetable.
a long, green vegetable marrow

Examples

She sliced the zucchini thinly before adding it to the stir-fry.
Grilled zucchini makes a delicious and healthy side dish in the summer.
The recipe calls for one large zucchini and a handful of cherry tomatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courgette - The British English term for zucchini, a long, green vegetable marrow

Etymology

From Italian 'zucchini', plural of 'zucchino', diminutive of 'zucca' (gourd).
