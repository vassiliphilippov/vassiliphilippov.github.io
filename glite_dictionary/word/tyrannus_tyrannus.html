Tyrannus tyrannus

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈræn.əs tɪˈræn.əs / (AmE), / tɪˈran.əs tɪˈran.əs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the Eastern Kingbird, a large American flycatcher found in North America.
scientific name for the Eastern Kingbird

Examples

The Tyrannus tyrannus is known for its bold and aggressive defense of territory.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a Tyrannus tyrannus during the migration season.
Field guides list Tyrannus tyrannus as one of the most widespread kingbirds in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingbird - A common name for members of the genus Tyrannus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Tyrannus' meaning 'tyrant', referencing the bird's aggressive behavior.
