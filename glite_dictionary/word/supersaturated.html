supersaturated

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuːpərˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈsætʃəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a solution or vapor) containing more dissolved material than could normally be held at a given temperature and pressure, and thus not in equilibrium.
having more dissolved substance than normally possible

Examples

The solution became supersaturated, causing crystals to form when disturbed.
A supersaturated atmosphere can lead to the rapid formation of fog or precipitation.
When sugar is added slowly to a supersaturated solution, it precipitates out as crystals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: saturated - containing as much dissolved substance as possible under normal conditions

Etymology

super- (above, exceeding) + saturated, from Latin saturare (to fill, sate).
