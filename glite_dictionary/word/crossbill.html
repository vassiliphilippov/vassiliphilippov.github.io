crossbill

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈkrɒs.bɪl / (BrE)
a finch with a curved bill whose tips cross over each other, adapted for extracting seeds from cones
type of finch with crossed bill

Examples

The male crossbill displayed its bright plumage among the pine branches.
A unique feature of the crossbill is its bill, which crosses at the tip.
During winter, flocks of crossbills migrate in search of conifer seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finch - small songbird of the family Fringillidae
