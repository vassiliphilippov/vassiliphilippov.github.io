mores

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔːr.eɪz, ˈmoʊr.eɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.reɪz / (BrE)
the accepted traditional customs and moral attitudes of a particular social group or community
customs and values of a group

Examples

Social mores often determine what is considered acceptable behavior within a community.
Attitudes toward marriage have changed as societies' mores have evolved over time.
The book explores the mores of 19th-century England in fascinating detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: customs - traditional practices followed by a group
  • Degree Lower: folkways - informal social conventions that are less strictly enforced than mores

Etymology

From Latin mores (plural of mos), meaning 'custom, manner, habit'.
