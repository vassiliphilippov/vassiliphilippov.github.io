lascar

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæskər / (AmE), / ˈlæskə / (BrE)
a sailor from South Asia, especially one employed on British or European ships in the past
South Asian sailor on European ships

Examples

The British merchant ship was crewed mainly by lascars hired in Calcutta.
Many lascars settled in England after years at sea.
Historical records often mention lascars working alongside European sailors.

Etymology

From Persian 'lashkar' (army, camp), via Hindi 'lāśkar'.
