palaeodendrology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪ.li.oʊ.dɛnˈdrɑl.ə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌpeɪ.lɪ.əʊ.dɛnˈdrɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
The scientific study of fossil trees, especially as a branch of paleobotany.
study of fossil trees (paleobotany branch)
medical

Examples

Advances in palaeodendrology have revealed much about prehistoric climate conditions.
She specializes in palaeodendrology, focusing on the structure of ancient trees.
The field of palaeodendrology is crucial to understanding plant evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paleobotany - the study of fossil plants

Etymology

From Greek palaio- (ancient), dendron (tree), and -logy (study).
