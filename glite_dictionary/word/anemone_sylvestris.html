Anemone sylvestris

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛməni sɪlˈvɛstrɪs / (AmE), / əˈnɛməni sɪlˈvɛstrɪs / (BrE)
A perennial Eurasian herb with solitary, nodding, fragrant white flowers, commonly found in woodlands and gardens.
Eurasian herb with white nodding flowers

Examples

The Anemone sylvestris blooms in spring with its delicate, nodding white flowers.
Gardeners often plant Anemone sylvestris for its fragrance and early-season color.
In the wild, Anemone sylvestris thrives in woodland clearings and meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windflower - any plant of the genus Anemone; common name for anemones

Etymology

From Latin: 'anemone' (windflower) and 'sylvestris' (of the woods).
