dollhouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑlˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈdɒlˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A house that is very small, especially one that resembles a child's toy house in size or appearance.
very small house; like a toy house

Examples

The tiny cottage looked like a dollhouse nestled at the foot of the mountain.
From the outside, the apartment building appeared no bigger than a dollhouse.
Visitors joked that the historic inn resembled a dollhouse with its miniature rooms and furnishings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toy house - a small model house for children's play

Etymology

Formed from 'doll' + 'house'; originally referred to a toy house for dolls.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːlˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈdɒlˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A small model of a house, typically furnished and used as a toy by children.
miniature toy house for children

Examples

As a child, she spent hours decorating her dollhouse with tiny furniture.
The antique dollhouse in the museum is over a hundred years old.
He bought a new set of miniature dishes for his sister's dollhouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doll's house - British term for a small model house for children
