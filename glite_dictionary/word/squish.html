squish

1. NOUN

/ skwɪʃ / (AmE), / skwɪʃ / (BrE)
a soft, sucking noise made when stepping on or compressing something wet or soft, such as mud
soft sucking noise, e.g. in mud

Examples

He stepped into the mud with a loud squish under his boot.
The squish of wet grass echoed as we crossed the field.
As she walked through the marsh, each step made a distinct squish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squelch - a crushing or sucking sound, especially when walking on wet ground
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07410467-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ skwɪʃ / (AmE), / skwɪʃ / (BrE)
to walk with a soft, wet, sucking sound, especially through mud or something similarly squelchy
walk with a wet, sucking sound

Examples

My boots squished as I walked across the soggy field after the rain.
He could feel the mud squishing beneath his feet with every step.
They squished through the marsh to reach the other side before dusk.

Synset Examples

We had to splosh across the wet meadow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squelch - to make a soft sucking sound, as when walking through mud
  • Synonym: trudge - to walk with slow, heavy steps, often through something difficult like mud
  • Antonym: tiptoe - to walk quietly and carefully on one's toes

Etymology

Imitative/onomatopoeic formation, mimicking the sound produced by walking through wet material.
