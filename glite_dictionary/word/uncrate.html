to uncrate

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈkreɪt / (AmE), / ʌnˈkreɪt / (BrE)
to remove (something) from a crate
remove from a crate

Examples

The movers uncrate the furniture as soon as it arrives.
Please uncrate the new computers and set them up in the office.
After the long journey, they carefully uncrated the artwork to inspect for damage.

Synset Examples

uncrate the glasses carefully!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crate - a large shipping container, especially one made of wood
  • Antonym: package - to put something into a package or crate for storage or transport

Etymology

From prefix un- (reverse of) + crate.
