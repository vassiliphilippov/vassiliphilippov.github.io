Tyranni

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈræn.aɪ / (AmE), / tɪˈran.ɪ / (BrE)
A suborder of birds comprising the New World flycatchers, antbirds, ovenbirds, and woodhewers; also known as suboscines.
a suborder of birds (suboscines)

Examples

The suborder Tyranni includes hundreds of bird species unique to the Americas.
Certain families within the Tyranni are known for their simple vocalizations.
Researchers study Tyranni to understand the evolution of bird song and behavior.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Passeri - the suborder of perching birds known as songbirds or oscines

Etymology

From Modern Latin, from Latin 'tyrannus' (tyrant), used in taxonomic naming.
