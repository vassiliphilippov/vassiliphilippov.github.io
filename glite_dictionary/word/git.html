git

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪt / (AmE), / ɡɪt / (BrE)
an unpleasant or contemptible person, used as an insult, especially in British English
contemptible or unpleasant person (British insult)
british informal

Examples

He can be such a git when things don't go his way.
I can't believe you forgot my birthday, you git!
Don't listen to him—he's just an old git with nothing nice to say.

Synset Examples

only a rotter would do that
kill the rat
throw the bum out
you cowardly little pukes!
the British call a contemptible person a ‘git’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerk - an annoying or contemptible person (informal, American)

Etymology

Probably British slang, first attested in the early 20th century, possibly a variant of 'get' meaning 'offspring' or 'child'.
