extract

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkˌstrækt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.strækt / (BrE)
a short passage taken from a book, article, or other written work
short passage from a larger work

Examples

The teacher read an extract from the novel to the class.
Several extracts from the report were discussed during the meeting.
This magazine usually publishes an extract from upcoming books.

Synset Examples

he presented excerpts from William James' philosophical writings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excerpt - a short extract from a film, broadcast, or piece of music or writing

Etymology

From Latin 'extractus', past participle of 'extrahere' (to draw out).
