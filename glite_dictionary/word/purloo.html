purloo

1. NOUN

/ pɜrˈluː / (AmE), / pəˈluː / (BrE)
A thick stew made with rice, chicken, and sometimes small game, especially in the southern United States.
Southern U.S. rice and meat stew
american

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious purloo with rice, chicken, and local herbs for the family gathering.
Many Southern cookbooks include recipes for purloo as a traditional comfort food.
We enjoyed a warming bowl of purloo on a chilly evening in Charleston.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilaf - rice cooked in stock with spices or meat
  • Synonym: jambalaya - rice dish of Creole origin with meat and vegetables

Etymology

Possibly from French 'perlou' or 'pilau', influenced by West African and Caribbean rice dishes.
