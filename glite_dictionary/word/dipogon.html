Dipogon

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpoʊɡɒn / (AmE), / dɪˈpəʊɡɒn / (BrE)
A genus of Australian pea plants, with a single species known for its pea-like flowers.
genus of Australian pea plants

Examples

The Dipogon is native to Australia and is admired for its delicate pink flowers.
Botanists are studying the growth habits of Dipogon in various soil types.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Dipogon as an ornamental plant due to its attractive blooms.

Etymology

From Greek di (two) + pogon (beard), referring to the flower's structure.

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈpoʊɡɒn / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒɡɒn / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the pea family native to Australia, comprising one species distinguished by its pea-like flowers.
Australian plant genus in the pea family

Examples

The genus Dipogon is known for its ornamental pea-like flowers.
Botanists recently discovered a new variant within the Australian Dipogon.
Gardeners in temperate regions occasionally cultivate Dipogon for its delicate blossoms.

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) + 'pogon' (beard), referring to the plant's flower structure.
