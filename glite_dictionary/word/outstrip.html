to outstrip

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈstrɪp / (AmE), / aʊtˈstrɪp / (BrE)
to move faster or become greater than someone or something; to surpass or exceed
to surpass or go beyond

Examples

Technology companies continue to outstrip their competitors in terms of innovation.
The company's sales outstripped expectations this quarter, leading to a rise in its stock price.
She trained hard and managed to outstrip all her rivals in the final race.

Synset Examples

He outdistanced the other runners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surpass - to exceed or be greater than something or someone
  • Synonym: to outperform - to perform better than someone or something

Etymology

From out- (beyond) + strip (to move swiftly), originally referring to running ahead in a race.

2. VERB

/ aʊtˈstrɪp / (AmE), / aʊtˈstrɪp / (BrE)
to be or do something to a greater degree than someone or something else; to surpass or exceed
surpass; do better than

Examples

Sales of electric cars continue to outstrip those of traditional vehicles in many countries.
The company's growth has outstripped initial expectations this quarter.
Her achievements in science easily outstrip those of her peers.

Synset Examples

her performance surpasses that of any other student I know
She outdoes all other athletes
This exceeds all my expectations
This car outperforms all others in its class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surpass - to be greater, better, or stronger than
  • Synonym: to exceed - to go beyond in quantity, degree, or value
  • Antonym: to fall behind - to fail to keep up with

Etymology

From 'out-' (beyond) + 'strip' (run quickly), originally meaning to run faster than.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01108050-v
Synset POS: v
