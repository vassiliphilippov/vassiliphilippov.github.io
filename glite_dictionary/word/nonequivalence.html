nonequivalence

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʌn.ɪˈkwɪv.ə.ləns / (AmE), / ˌnʌn.ɪˈkwɪv.ɪ.ləns / (BrE)
the state or condition of not being equivalent or interchangeable
lack of equivalence or interchangeability

Examples

There is a clear nonequivalence between these two mathematical expressions.
The study highlights the nonequivalence of the translated terms in different languages.
Legal experts argue the nonequivalence of the two contracts under local law.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: equivalence - the state of being equal or interchangeable

Etymology

Formed from prefix non- (not) + equivalence (from Latin aequivalentia)
