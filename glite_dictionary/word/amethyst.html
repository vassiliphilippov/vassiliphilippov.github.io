amethyst

1. ADJ

/ ˈæməˌθɪst / (AmE), / ˈæm.ɪ.θɪst / (BrE)
Having a moderate purple color, resembling that of the amethyst gemstone.
moderate purple color

Examples

She wore an amethyst dress that complemented her complexion perfectly.
The sky turned an amethyst hue as the sun began to set.
Artists often choose amethyst shades for paintings of twilight scenes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purple - a color between red and blue
  • Degree Higher: violet - bluish purple color

Etymology

From Greek 'amethystos', meaning 'not intoxicated', referring originally to the gemstone, whose color gives this sense.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæməˌθɪst / (AmE), / ˈaməθɪst / (BrE)
a transparent purple variety of quartz used as a gemstone
purple variety of quartz (gemstone)

Examples

She wore a necklace set with a beautiful amethyst pendant.
Collectors prize rare, deep-colored amethysts for their vivid hue.
The museum displays a large raw amethyst crystal in its geology section.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quartz - a hard mineral composed of silicon dioxide, of which amethyst is a variety

Etymology

From Greek amethystos, meaning 'not intoxicating,' believed to protect against drunkenness.
