chorus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːrəs / (BrE)
A group of actors in classical Greek drama who comment on the action of the play, often speaking or singing together.
group of actors in Greek drama

Examples

The chorus entered the stage to narrate the upcoming events in the tragedy.
In many ancient Greek plays, the chorus sang odes between the acts.
The chorus provided insights and moral commentary throughout the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choir - an organized group of singers, typically in a church

Etymology

From Greek *khoros* (a band of dancers or singers).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːrəs / (BrE)
the part of a song that is repeated and sung by a group of singers, often following each verse and joined by the soloist
repeated part of a song sung by a group

Examples

The chorus of the song was so catchy that everyone started singing along.
After each verse, the soloist motioned for the audience to join in on the chorus.
The band's new single has a powerful chorus that repeats several times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refrain - the repeated line or group of lines in a song or poem

Etymology

From Latin 'chorus', from Greek 'khoros' (dance in a ring, band of dancers and singers).
