Megapodius

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈpoʊdiəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈpəʊdiəs / (BrE)
A genus of large ground-dwelling birds known as megapodes, native to Australasia and surrounding islands.
genus of large ground-dwelling birds

Examples

The Megapodius species are notable for building large nesting mounds to incubate their eggs.
Researchers discovered a new population of Megapodius on the remote island.
The Megapodius is unique among birds for its remarkable nesting behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: megapode - a member of the Megapodiidae family; mound-building ground birds

Etymology

From Greek 'mega' (large) + 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to their large feet.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈpoʊdiəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈpəʊdiəs / (BrE)
A genus of mound-building birds, known as megapodes, found in Australasia and surrounding regions.
genus of mound-building birds (megapodes)

Examples

The species within the genus Megapodius are remarkable for their unique nesting mounds.
Researchers discovered a new member of Megapodius on a remote island.
Eggs of Megapodius birds are incubated by heat generated from decomposing vegetation.

Etymology

From Greek 'megalo-' (large) + 'pous' (foot), referring to their large feet for digging.
