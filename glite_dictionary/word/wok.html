wok

1. NOUN

/ wɑk / (AmE), / wɒk / (BrE)
a large, round-bottomed pan, typically made of metal, used for frying and stir-frying food, especially in Chinese and East Asian cooking
round-bottomed frying pan, esp. in Chinese cooking

Examples

She quickly tossed the vegetables in her wok over high heat.
A traditional wok distributes heat evenly for perfect stir-fries.
He bought a new wok to try making authentic Chinese dishes at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pan - a container used for cooking food

Etymology

From Cantonese 粵語 *wok* (鑊, meaning 'cooking pot').
