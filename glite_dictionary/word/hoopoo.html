hoopoo

1. NOUN

/ ˈhuːpuː / (AmE), / ˈhuːpuː / (BrE)
a crested Old World bird with a slender, downward-curved bill, known for its distinctive call and plumage: the hoopoe
crested Old World bird with curved bill

Examples

The hoopoo is easily recognized by its striking crest and long, curved bill.
We spotted a hoopoo foraging for insects on the ground near the riverbank.
During migration season, flocks of hoopoos can be seen in southern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoopoe - another spelling for the same crested Old World bird

Etymology

From Latin *upupa*, through French *huppe*, imitative of the bird's call.
