quick-sighted

1. ADJ

/ kwɪkˈsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / kwɪkˈsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
Able to see things very clearly or quickly; having very keen vision.
having very keen vision

Examples

Her quick-sighted eyes spotted the tiny insect across the room.
The hunter was known for his quick-sighted abilities on the trail.
Being quick-sighted is essential for a good pilot.

Synset Examples

quick-sighted as a cat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharp-sighted - having very keen vision
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02165853-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈkwɪkˌsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkwɪkˌsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
Able to perceive or notice things quickly; keenly perceptive or alert.
keenly perceptive or alert

Examples

The detective was so quick-sighted that no detail escaped his attention.
Her quick-sighted nature made her an excellent editor, spotting errors others missed.
Only a quick-sighted observer could have noticed the subtle change in his expression.

Synset Examples

quick-sighted into the faults of the time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptive - having or showing keenness of insight or understanding
  • Synonym: alert - quick to notice and respond to possible danger or problems
  • Antonym: dull - lacking sharpness of perception; slow to notice things
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01749902-s
Synset POS: s
