laborious

1. ADJ

/ ləˈbɔriəs / (AmE), / ləˈbɔːriəs / (BrE)
requiring considerable effort and time; involving hard or tedious work
requiring hard work or effort

Examples

The research proved to be a laborious process, demanding many late nights and early mornings.
Digging the trench by hand was a laborious task for the workers.
His laborious attention to detail ensured the final report was free from errors.

Synset Examples

worked their arduous way up the mining valley
a grueling campaign
hard labor
heavy work
heavy going
spent many laborious hours on the project
set a punishing pace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arduous - requiring a lot of effort
  • Antonym: easy - not needing much effort

Etymology

From Latin laboriosus (toilful, industrious), from labor (work).
