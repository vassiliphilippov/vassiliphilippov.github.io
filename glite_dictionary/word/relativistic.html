relativistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌrɛləˌtɪvˈɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌrɛlətɪˈvɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the philosophical doctrine of relativism.
relating to relativism (philosophy)

Examples

Some philosophers argue that a relativistic approach allows for greater tolerance in ethical debates.
The theory adopts a relativistic stance, rejecting the existence of universal moral principles.
His relativistic perspective led him to question the objectivity of all forms of knowledge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relativism - the belief that truth and moral values are not absolute but are relative to the persons or groups holding them

Etymology

From 'relativism' + '-istic', patterned on 'realistic', etc.

2. ADJ

/ ˌrɛləˌtɪvˈɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌrɛlətɪˈvɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the theory of relativity, especially those phenomena or effects predicted by Einstein's special or general relativity.
relating to relativity (physics)

Examples

The physicist explained that the electron's behavior becomes relativistic at high velocities.
A relativistic approach is necessary for accurately describing particles moving near the speed of light.
In astronomy, some jets from black holes contain relativistic particles traveling at nearly light speed.

Synset Examples

relativistic quantum mechanics
relativistic increase in mass
radiation from relativistic particles

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonrelativistic - not involving principles of relativity; classical (pre-relativity) physics
  • Synonym: relativity - the physical theory explaining the interrelations of space, time, and mass

Etymology

From 'relativity' (the theory) + suffix '-istic'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02846772-a
Synset POS: a
