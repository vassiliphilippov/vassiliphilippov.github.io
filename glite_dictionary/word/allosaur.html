allosaur

1. NOUN

/ ˈæləˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈal.ə.sɔː / (BrE)
a large, carnivorous dinosaur from the late Jurassic period, slightly smaller than Tyrannosaurus rex
large Jurassic carnivorous dinosaur

Examples

The museum's fossil exhibit features a complete allosaur skeleton.
Paleontologists believe the allosaur was one of the top predators of its time.
Children were fascinated by the sharp teeth of the allosaur on display.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tyrannosaurus - large carnivorous dinosaur from the Cretaceous period

Etymology

From Greek 'allos' (other) + 'sauros' (lizard).
