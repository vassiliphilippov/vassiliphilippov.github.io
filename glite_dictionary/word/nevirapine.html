nevirapine

1. NOUN

/ nəˈvɪrəˌpin / (AmE), / nɛˈvɪrəpiːn / (BrE)
a non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor used in the treatment of HIV and AIDS
antiretroviral drug for HIV/AIDS
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed nevirapine as part of the antiretroviral therapy regimen.
Some patients experience side effects when starting nevirapine for HIV treatment.
Regular monitoring is important during nevirapine therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: efavirenz - another non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor used for HIV treatment
  • Degree Lower: zidovudine - an antiretroviral drug of a different class used for HIV/AIDS
