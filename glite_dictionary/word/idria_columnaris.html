Idria columnaris

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdri.ə kəˈlʌmnərɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪdri.ə kəˈlʌmnərɪs / (BrE)
A species of candlewood native to Mexico and southwestern California, known for its tall columnar stems and honey-scented creamy yellow flowers.
candlewood plant with columnar stems

Examples

The distinctive Idria columnaris stands tall in the Baja California desert.
Botanists were amazed by the size and unique structure of Idria columnaris.
The creamy yellow flowers of Idria columnaris are famous for their sweet, honey-like scent.

Etymology

From the genus name Idria and Latin 'columnaris' (columnar, shaped like a column).
