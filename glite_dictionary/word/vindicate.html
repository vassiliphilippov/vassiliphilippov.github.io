to vindicate

1. VERB

/ ˈvɪn.dɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɪn.dɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
to maintain, uphold, or defend something, such as rights or a position, especially against opposition or criticism
maintain or uphold, especially rights or claims

Examples

The lawyer sought to vindicate her client's rights in court.
Activists work tirelessly to vindicate environmental protections against legislative rollbacks.
She wrote articles to vindicate the freedom of the press.

Synset Examples

vindicate the rights of the citizens

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defend - to protect from harm or attack
  • Synonym: to uphold - to support or maintain, especially principles or laws

Etymology

From Latin vindicatus, past participle of vindicare (to claim, avenge).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00906643-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈvɪn.dɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɪn.dɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
to clear someone of blame, accusation, suspicion, or doubt by providing supporting proof
clear from blame or suspicion

Examples

The new evidence helped vindicate the wrongly accused scientist.
She felt relieved when the court finally vindicated her reputation.
He worked hard to vindicate himself after the allegations were made.

Synset Examples

You must vindicate yourself and fight this libel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exonerate - to officially state that someone is not guilty
  • Antonym: to accuse - to say someone has done something wrong or illegal

Etymology

From Latin vindicatus, past participle of vindicare (to claim, avenge, set free).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00906447-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈvɪndɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɪndɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to show or prove that someone or something is right, reasonable, or justified
prove right or justified

Examples

The new evidence helped to vindicate her claim of innocence.
After months of criticism, his actions were finally vindicated by the official report.
Scientists sought to vindicate their theory through a series of experiments.

Synset Examples

vindicate a claim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to justify - to show or prove to be right or reasonable
  • Antonym: to disprove - to prove that something is false or incorrect

Etymology

From Latin vindicatus, past participle of vindicare (to claim, avenge, set free).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00897737-v
Synset POS: v
