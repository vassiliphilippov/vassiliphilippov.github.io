Symplocus

1. NOUN

/ sɪmˈploʊkəs / (AmE), / sɪmˈpləʊkəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Symplocaceae, including species commonly known as sweetleaf.
genus of flowering plants (sweetleaf)

Examples

The Symplocus genus consists of trees and shrubs found in tropical and subtropical regions.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Symplocus genus in Southeast Asia.
The leaves of some Symplocus species have been traditionally used for medicinal purposes.

Etymology

From Greek *symplokē* (interweaving, connection), referring to the clustered arrangement of the flowers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpləkəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpləkəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Symplocaceae, commonly known as sweetleaf or horse sugar.
a plant genus in Symplocaceae (sweetleaf)

Examples

Botanists discovered a new species in the Symplocus genus in the South American rainforest.
The leaves of Symplocus trees have long been used in traditional medicine.
Several Symplocus species are cultivated for their attractive flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'symploke' (intertwining), referring to the clustered arrangement of flowers or leaves.
