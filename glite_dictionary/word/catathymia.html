catathymia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˈθaɪmiə / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈθɪmɪə / (BrE)
A psychological state in which mental processes are strongly influenced or dominated by emotions.
mental state dominated by emotion
medical

Examples

Some clinicians believe that catathymia can contribute to distorted thinking patterns in certain patients.
The psychiatrist discussed the role of catathymia in the development of the patient's delusions.
Research into catathymia explores how emotional states can shape one's perceptions and beliefs.

Synset Examples

The original idea of catathymia forwarded by Maier (1912), Wertham (1937), and other contemporaries was that the condition constituted its own clinical identity.

Etymology

From Greek kata- (down; according to) and thymos (spirit, mind, emotions).
