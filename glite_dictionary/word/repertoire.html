repertoire

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛpərˌtwɑr / (AmE), / ˈrɛpətwɑː / (BrE)
the complete list of works such as plays, songs, operas, or pieces that an artist or company can perform and regularly does perform
the works an artist or group can perform

Examples

The theatre company added a new comedy to its repertoire this season.
She has a vast repertoire of classical piano pieces for every occasion.
Jazz musicians often expand their repertoire by learning new standards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalogue - a complete list, usually of items or works

Etymology

From French 'répertoire', from Late Latin 'repertorium' (inventory, list).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛpərˌtwɑr / (AmE), / ˈrɛp.ə.twɑː / (BrE)
the entire range of skills, techniques, or devices available to a person or used in a particular field or activity
entire range of skills or techniques

Examples

As a musician, she has an impressive repertoire of classical and modern pieces.
Negotiation is just one of the many skills in his repertoire.
Every chef brings a unique repertoire of techniques to their kitchen.

Synset Examples

the repertory of the supposed feats of mesmerism
has a large repertory of dialects and characters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inventory - a complete list of items, such as skills or possessions
  • Synonym: arsenal - a collection of resources or skills available for use

Etymology

From French 'répertoire', from Late Latin 'repertorium' meaning 'inventory, list'.
