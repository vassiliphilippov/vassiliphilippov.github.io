outfield

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfild / (AmE), / ˈaʊtfiːld / (BrE)
the part of a baseball field beyond the infield and between the foul lines, or the area of a cricket field furthest from the batsman
area beyond the infield in baseball or cricket

Examples

The ball sailed over the infield and landed in the outfield.
He plays in the outfield because of his strong throwing arm.
The grass in the outfield was wet from the morning dew.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infield - the area of a baseball field within the bases; opposite of outfield

Etymology

From 'out' + 'field', reflecting its position away from the infield.
