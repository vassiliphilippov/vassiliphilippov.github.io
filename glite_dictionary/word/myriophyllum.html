Myriophyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪr.i.oʊˈfɪl.i.əm / (AmE), / ˌmɪr.i.əˈfɪl.i.əm / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly aquatic flowering plants, commonly known as milfoils, found in freshwater habitats
genus of aquatic plants; milfoils

Examples

A thick growth of Myriophyllum covered the edge of the lake.
Many aquarists add Myriophyllum to their tanks to improve water quality.
The invasive Myriophyllum species threatens native aquatic vegetation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milfoil - common name for plants in the Myriophyllum genus

Etymology

From Greek myrios (countless) + phyllon (leaf), referring to the finely divided leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪrioʊˈfɪliəm / (AmE), / ˌmɪriəˈfɪliəm / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic or semi-aquatic herbs, commonly known as milfoils, with finely divided leaves, chiefly monoecious.
genus of aquatic plants (milfoils)

Examples

Several species of Myriophyllum are used in aquariums due to their attractive feathery foliage.
Invasive Myriophyllum plants can disrupt freshwater ecosystems if not properly managed.
Botanists collected samples of Myriophyllum from the pond to study their reproductive habits.

Etymology

From Greek myrio- (countless, many) + phyllon (leaf), referring to the numerous finely divided leaves.
