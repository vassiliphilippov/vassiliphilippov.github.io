four-wheeler

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌwiːlər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌwiːlə / (BrE)
a carriage or vehicle with four wheels, especially a hackney carriage used for hire
four-wheeled carriage or vehicle

Examples

The museum showcased a restored four-wheeler that was once used as a city hackney carriage.
In Victorian times, a four-wheeler was a common sight on London's streets.
She hailed a four-wheeler to take her home after the theatre.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carriage - a horse-drawn vehicle for carrying people
  • Antonym: two-wheeler - a vehicle with two wheels, such as a bicycle or motorcycle

Etymology

Formed from 'four' referring to the number of wheels and 'wheeler', meaning something that wheels or is equipped with wheels.
