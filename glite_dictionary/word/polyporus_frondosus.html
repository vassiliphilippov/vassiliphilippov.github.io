Polyporus frondosus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑlɪˈpɔrəs frɒnˈdoʊsəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈpɔːrəs frɒnˈdəʊsəs / (BrE)
a large, greyish-brown edible fungus with a mass of overlapping caps, often found at the base of trees; also known as 'hen of the woods'
a large edible fungus, hen of the woods

Examples

Wild mushroom hunters often search for Polyporus frondosus at the base of old oak trees in autumn.
The chef created a delicious risotto using fresh Polyporus frondosus he found in the forest.
Mycologists are fascinated by the clustered growth pattern of Polyporus frondosus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hen of the woods - common name for Polyporus frondosus, edible fungus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: Latin 'polyporus' (many-pored) and 'frondosus' (leafy), describing the fungus's appearance.
