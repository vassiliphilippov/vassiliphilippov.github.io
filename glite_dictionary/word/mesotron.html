mesotron

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛzəˌtrɑn / (AmE), / ˈmɛzəʊˌtrɒn / (BrE)
an elementary particle in physics, formerly used to refer to what is now known as the meson, a particle responsible for the forces within the atomic nucleus and having a baryon number of 0.
elementary particle (obsolete: meson)

Examples

The discovery of the mesotron played a significant role in the development of nuclear physics.
Physicists later realized that the particle called the mesotron was actually a type of meson.
Historical papers often refer to the mesotron when discussing the strong nuclear force.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meson - a subatomic particle with zero baryon number; modern term for mesotron

Etymology

From Greek 'mesos' (middle) and '-tron' (particle suffix); earlier name for 'meson'.
