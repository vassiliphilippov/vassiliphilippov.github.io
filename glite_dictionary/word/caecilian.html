caecilian

1. NOUN

/ siˈsɪliən / (AmE), / siːˈsɪliən / (BrE)
a small, slender, limbless burrowing amphibian of the order Gymnophiona, typically inhabiting moist tropical soil
limbless burrowing amphibian (order Gymnophiona)
medical

Examples

The caecilian is rarely seen above ground because it spends most of its life burrowing in moist soil.
Researchers discovered a new species of caecilian in the rainforest of South America.
Unlike frogs and salamanders, caecilians have no limbs and resemble large worms or snakes.

Etymology

From Latin 'caecus' meaning 'blind', relating to the animal's reduced or absent eyes.

2. ADJ

/ siˈsɪliən / (AmE), / siˈsɪliən / (BrE)
relating to or belonging to the family Caeciliidae, a group of limbless, burrowing amphibians.
relating to limbless burrowing amphibians (Caeciliidae)

Examples

The caecilian species are often mistaken for worms or snakes due to their shape.
Some caecilian creatures can be found burrowing deep in tropical soils.
Researchers are studying the reproductive adaptations unique to caecilian amphibians.

Etymology

From Latin 'caecilia', a type of blindworm, from 'caecus' (blind).
