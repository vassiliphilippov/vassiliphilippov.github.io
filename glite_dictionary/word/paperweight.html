paperweight

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpɚˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpəˌweɪt / (BrE)
a small, heavy object placed on a pile of papers to keep them from blowing away or getting disarranged
a small weight for holding down papers

Examples

She placed a paperweight on the stack of documents to keep them from blowing away.
The crystal paperweight on his desk caught the morning light beautifully.
After tidying her office, she realized her favorite paperweight was missing.

Etymology

Formed from 'paper' and 'weight,' first recorded in the 1800s.
