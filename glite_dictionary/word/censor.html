censor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to examine books, films, letters, etc., and remove or suppress parts considered offensive, immoral, or politically sensitive
official who removes objectionable content

Examples

The government appointed a censor to review all foreign films before they were released in the country.
As a censor, she had the authority to delete scenes she found inappropriate.
The newspaper was sent to the censor prior to publication to ensure no sensitive information was revealed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censure - to express severe disapproval of someone or something, especially in a formal statement
  • Synonym: critic - person who forms and expresses judgments of the merits and faults of something

Etymology

From Latin 'censor' (Roman magistrate who assessed taxes and supervised morals).

2. VERB

/ ˈsɛnsɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsə / (BrE)
to examine books, films, letters, etc., and remove or suppress parts considered objectionable on moral, political, or religious grounds.
remove or suppress content deemed objectionable

Examples

The government decided to censor several scenes that were deemed too controversial for public viewing.
Many authors in that era had their works censored before publication.
Social media platforms sometimes censor content that violates their policies.

Synset Examples

This magazine is censored by the government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - to put an end to something forcibly or prevent its development
  • Synonym: ban - to officially or legally prohibit something

Etymology

From Latin 'censor', an official who assessed taxes and oversaw public morals.

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsɛnsər / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsə / (BrE)
to remove, suppress, or prohibit parts of books, films, newspapers, or other media considered objectionable or offensive by authorities.
suppress or remove objectionable material

Examples

The government decided to censor the controversial documentary before its release.
Journalists protested when the editor tried to censor parts of their article.
They censored several scenes from the film to comply with local regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ban - to officially or legally prohibit something
  • Synonym: to suppress - to forcibly put an end to something; prevent the dissemination of information
  • Antonym: to publish - to make information or material publicly available

Etymology

From Latin 'censor' (magistrate who assessed morals).

Synset POS: v
