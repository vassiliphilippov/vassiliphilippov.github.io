somersault

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmərˌsɔlt / (AmE), / ˈsʌməsɒlt / (BrE)
an acrobatic movement in which a person turns head over heels in the air or on the ground, bringing the feet over the head, either forwards or backwards
acrobatic head-over-heels movement

Examples

The gymnast performed a flawless somersault during her floor routine.
As a child, I loved doing forward somersaults on the lawn.
He lost his balance and nearly did a somersault down the stairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flip - an acrobatic movement involving a rotation through the air

Etymology

From French 'sombresault', from Old Occitan 'sobressaut' (jump, leap).

2. VERB

/ ˈsʌmɚˌsɔlt / (AmE), / ˈsʌməsɒlt / (BrE)
to turn over completely in the air with the body head over heels
perform a head-over-heels flip

Examples

The gymnast somersaulted across the mat with incredible speed and grace.
He lost his balance and somersaulted into the pool, making everyone laugh.
We watched dolphins somersault out of the water during the show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flip - to turn over or jump in the air acrobatically

Etymology

From old French 'sombresault', from Latin 'supina', meaning backward, and 'saltus', a jump.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
