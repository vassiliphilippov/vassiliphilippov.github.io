Merlangus

1. NOUN

/ mərˈlæŋɡəs / (AmE), / məˈlæŋɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the cod family, including the whiting.
genus of fish including whiting

Examples

The genus Merlangus includes the commercially important whiting.
Researchers identified several new species within Merlangus from Atlantic waters.
Members of Merlangus are commonly found in European coastal regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiting - a common marine fish, Merlangius merlangus, in the cod family
  • Degree Higher: Gadidae - the cod family of marine fish

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on the French 'merlan' (whiting).
