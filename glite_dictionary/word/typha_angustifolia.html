Typha angustifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪfə æŋˌɡʌstɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈtɪfə æŋˌɡʌstɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of cattail with narrow leaves, native to wetlands in America, Europe, North Africa, and Asia.
species of narrowleaf cattail

Examples

Wetland restoration projects often include Typha angustifolia to improve habitat diversity.
The narrow leaves of Typha angustifolia make it easy to distinguish from other cattail species.
Ecologists surveyed the distribution of Typha angustifolia across several continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattail - any of various tall marsh plants of the genus Typha

Etymology

From Latin: *typha* (the reed genus) and *angustifolia* ('narrow-leaved').
