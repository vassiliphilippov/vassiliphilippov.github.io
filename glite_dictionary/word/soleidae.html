Soleidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊˈliːɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌsəʊˈliːɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of flatfishes, known as soles, characterized by their elongated, flattened bodies and commonly found in marine and freshwater environments
family of flatfishes (soles)

Examples

Marine biologists have discovered a new species within the Soleidae family.
Many commercial fisheries target members of the Soleidae for their delicate texture and mild flavor.
The unique body shape of Soleidae helps them blend into sandy sea floors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sole - any flatfish of the Soleidae family

Etymology

From scientific Latin Soleidae, derived from the genus name Solea, meaning 'sandal' or 'sole' in Latin.
