starling

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑrlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɑːlɪŋ / (BrE)
A small to medium-sized, gregarious songbird of the family Sturnidae, native to the Old World, often with dark, glossy plumage and noted for its mimicking abilities.
a type of small, social songbird

Examples

A flock of starlings filled the evening sky with dazzling patterns.
The starling is known for its remarkable ability to mimic the songs of other birds.
Many farmers consider the starling both a pest and a beautiful singer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackbird - a similar-looking, unrelated songbird in the thrush family

Etymology

From Old English *stærlinc*, diminutive of *stær* (starling).
