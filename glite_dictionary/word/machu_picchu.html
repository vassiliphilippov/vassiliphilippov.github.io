Machu Picchu

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɑːtʃuː ˈpiːtʃuː / (AmE), / ˌmætʃuː ˈpɪktʃuː / (BrE)
An ancient Inca fortress city located in the Andes Mountains of Peru, built in the 15th century and rediscovered in 1911.
Inca fortress city in the Andes, Peru

Examples

Many tourists dream of visiting Machu Picchu to marvel at its stunning mountain views and ancient architecture.
The ruins of Machu Picchu are one of the most important archaeological sites in South America.
Researchers continue to study Machu Picchu to learn more about Inca civilization.

Etymology

From Quechua, meaning 'old peak.'
