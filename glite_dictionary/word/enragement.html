enragement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈreɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈreɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
a feeling or state of intense anger and impatience
intense anger or impatience

Examples

Her enragement was evident when she shouted at the team for missing the deadline.
He struggled to control his enragement during the heated argument.
The announcement was met with widespread enragement among the employees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rage - violent, uncontrollable anger
  • Antonym: calm - the state of being peaceful and free from anger

Etymology

From enrage (to make very angry) + -ment (forming nouns of action or state).
