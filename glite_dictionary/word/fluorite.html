fluorite

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʊrˌaɪt / (AmE), / ˈflɔːr.aɪt / (BrE)
a soft mineral consisting of calcium fluoride, often occurring in cubic crystals and fluorescent under ultraviolet light; a chief source of fluorine
a mineral; source of fluorine

Examples

The museum has a stunning specimen of fluorite that glows under ultraviolet light.
Miners extract fluorite as a primary source of industrial fluorine.
The cubic crystals of fluorite are prized by collectors for their vivid colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluorine - a chemical element; a highly reactive, pale yellow gas

Etymology

From Latin 'fluor' (flowing), used for flux in smelting, with '-ite' denoting minerals.
