Olea lanceolata

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌliːəˌ lænsiəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / əʊˌliːəˌ lænsɪəˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A small tree native to New Zealand, known for its red, pulpy, one-seeded fruit.
small New Zealand tree with red fruit

Examples

The Olea lanceolata is appreciated for its attractive red fruits in native New Zealand gardens.
Botanists recently discovered a mature Olea lanceolata growing near the forest edge.
Birds often feed on the ripe fruit of the Olea lanceolata during the summer months.

Etymology

From Latin: Olea (olive) + lanceolata (lance-shaped, referring to the leaves).
