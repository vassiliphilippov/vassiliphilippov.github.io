panellist

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæn.ə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈpæn.əl.ɪst / (BrE)
a person who is a member of a panel, especially one taking part in a discussion or on a television or radio show
member of a panel (e.g. in discussions)

Examples

Each panellist shared their opinion on the topic during the live broadcast.
The conference invited several expert panellists to discuss climate change.
As a panellist, she was well prepared to answer audience questions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panelist - alternative spelling of 'panellist'

Etymology

From 'panel' (Middle English, from Old French) + '-ist', denoting someone involved with or practicing a certain thing.
