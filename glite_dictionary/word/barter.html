barter

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrtər / (AmE), / ˈbɑːtə / (BrE)
the act or system of exchanging goods or services directly for other goods or services without using money
exchange of goods without money

Examples

In ancient times, people would barter instead of using coins or bills.
The villagers relied on barter to obtain food and supplies during the economic crisis.
Without cash, the community turned to barter as the main form of exchange.

Synset Examples

we had no money so we had to live by barter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exchange - the act of giving one thing and receiving another
  • Synonym: trade - the activity of buying, selling, or exchanging goods and services

Etymology

From Old French *barater* (to exchange or barter).

2. VERB

/ ˈbɑrtɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɑːtə / (BrE)
to exchange goods or services directly for other goods or services, without using money
exchange goods or services without money

Examples

Instead of paying cash, they decided to barter their homegrown vegetables for fresh eggs.
Many indigenous communities still barter goods and services as a form of economic exchange.
During the economic crisis, neighbors often bartered labor and products to get what they needed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trade - to buy and sell goods and services
  • Antonym: purchase - to acquire something by paying money for it

Etymology

From Old French *barater* (to barter, cheat) and possibly from Catalan or Italian roots.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02264048-v
Synset POS: v
