thirteenth

1. ADJ

/ θɜːrˈtinθ / (AmE), / θɜːˈtiːnθ / (BrE)
coming next after the twelfth in a sequence; 13th
ordinal number: 13th in sequence

Examples

She finished in thirteenth place in the marathon, just ahead of her teammate.
Their anniversary falls on the thirteenth of April every year.
This is the thirteenth time we've visited this beautiful city.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: twelfth - coming next after the eleventh in position; 12th
  • Degree Higher: fourteenth - coming next after the thirteenth in position; 14th

Etymology

Formed from 'thirteen' (from Old English 'þreotīene') + suffix '-th' (indicating ordinal).

2. NOUN

/ ˌθɜrˈtinθ / (AmE), / ˌθɜːˈtiːnθ / (BrE)
the person or thing that is number thirteen in a series; 13th
13th in a sequence

Examples

She finished in thirteenth place in the race, just missing the top ten.
Today marks the thirteenth anniversary of their marriage.
The thirteenth chapter of the book contains the main argument.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: twelfth - 12th in a sequence
  • Degree Higher: fourteenth - 14th in a sequence
