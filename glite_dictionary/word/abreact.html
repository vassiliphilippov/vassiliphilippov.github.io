to abreact

1. VERB

/ ˌæbˈriːækt / (AmE), / ˌæbˈriːækt / (BrE)
to release strong or repressed emotions, especially by talking about past experiences, typically as a way of relieving psychological tension
release repressed emotions by expression
medical

Examples

In therapy sessions, patients are encouraged to abreact traumatic memories by talking openly about them.
She abreacted her feelings of anger during the group discussion and felt a sense of relief afterward.
Some believe that writing in a journal can help individuals abreact intense emotions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catharsis - the process of releasing strong or repressed emotions

Etymology

From German 'abreaktieren,' from 'ab-' (off, away) + 'Reaktion' (reaction).
