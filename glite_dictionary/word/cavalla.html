cavalla

1. NOUN

/ kəˈvælə / (AmE), / kəˈvalə / (BrE)
A large mackerel with a long pointed snout, important as a food and game fish along the eastern Atlantic coast south to Brazil.
large Atlantic mackerel species

Examples

Fishermen often catch cavalla along the eastern shores of South America.
The grilled cavalla was the highlight of the local seafood restaurant's menu.
Because of its size and strength, cavalla is popular among sport fishers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mackerel - an edible marine fish of the family Scombridae

Etymology

From Spanish 'cavalla', feminine of 'caballo' meaning horse, possibly referring to the fish's shape or speed.
