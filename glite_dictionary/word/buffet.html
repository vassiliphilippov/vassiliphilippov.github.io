buffet

1. NOUN

/ bəˈfeɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʌf.ɪt / (BrE)
A piece of furniture, typically placed along the wall of a dining room, with shelves and drawers for storing dishes, silverware, and table linens.
sideboard with shelves and drawers

Examples

The antique buffet in the dining room was inherited from my grandmother.
We arranged the plates and cutlery neatly inside the drawers of the buffet.
A large mirror hung above the buffet, reflecting the soft light from the chandelier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sideboard - a piece of furniture for holding dishes and serving food

Etymology

From French *buffet* (sideboard).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02915411-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ bəˈfeɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfeɪ / (BrE)
a meal where guests serve themselves from a variety of dishes laid out on a table or counter
self-service meal

Examples

The hotel offers a large buffet breakfast every morning.
We enjoyed the wedding buffet with its wide selection of dishes.
Guests lined up to serve themselves at the holiday buffet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smorgasbord - a meal with a variety of hot and cold dishes served buffet-style

Etymology

From French 'buffet' (sideboard, refreshment table).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07591747-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈbʌf.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʌf.ɪt / (BrE)
(of wind, waves, or other forces) to strike repeatedly and forcefully against something.
strike repeatedly and forcefully

Examples

The small boat was buffeted by the storm all night.
Strong winds buffet the coastline every winter, eroding the cliffs.
The airplane was buffeted by turbulence during the flight.

Synset Examples

Winds buffeted the tent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batter - to strike repeatedly with hard blows

Etymology

From Old French *bufet* (a blow), diminutive of *bufer* (to strike, puff).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01420359-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ bəˈfeɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfeɪ / (BrE)
a restaurant or eating place, often inexpensive, where people serve themselves from a selection of food and drinks, typically laid out on a counter or table
self-service restaurant or counter

Examples

We decided to have lunch at the new buffet around the corner.
The hotel offers a breakfast buffet with a wide selection of dishes.
Many travelers stop at the station buffet for a quick drink or snack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cafeteria - a restaurant where customers serve themselves or are served at a counter
  • Synonym: smorgasbord - a meal with a variety of hot and cold dishes laid out for guests to serve themselves
  • Synonym: restaurant - a place where people pay to sit and eat meals

Etymology

From French *buffet* (sideboard, meal served from a sideboard).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04254864-n
Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ ˈbʌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʌfɪt / (BrE)
(of wind, waves, or something similar) to strike or beat repeatedly and with force, often causing difficulty or discomfort.
strike repeatedly (wind, waves, etc.)

Examples

The small boat was buffeted by the powerful waves throughout the night.
For hours, the hikers were buffeted by fierce winds on the exposed ridge.
The city was buffeted by a series of violent storms last winter.

Synset Examples

The wind buffeted him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batter - to strike repeatedly with hard blows

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *buffeter* (to strike, cuff).

Synset POS: v
