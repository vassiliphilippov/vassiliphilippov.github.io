AM

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˈɛm / (BrE)
The period of time between midnight and noon, used in stating the time of day.
time from midnight to noon

Examples

The train leaves at 7:30 AM every weekday.
Please arrive before 10:00 AM for registration.
He usually finishes his morning run by 6:00 AM.

Synset Examples

Made in the A.M.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: PM - the period between noon and midnight

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'ante meridiem' meaning 'before midday'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-84502472-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˈɛm / (BrE)
a method of modulating a radio signal in which the amplitude of the carrier wave is varied in accordance with the signal being sent
radio signal modulation technique

Examples

Many classic radio stations still broadcast in AM.
The quality of AM radio is susceptible to interference from electrical equipment.
Early car radios often only received AM frequencies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: FM - modulation of the frequency of a carrier wave (frequency modulation)

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'amplitude modulation'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06292247-n
Synset POS: n
