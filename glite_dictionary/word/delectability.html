delectability

1. NOUN

/ dəˌlɛktəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / dɪˌlɛktəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely appetizing or delightful, especially in taste or appearance
extreme appetizingness; deliciousness

Examples

The delectability of the chef's dessert left the guests wanting more.
She praised the delectability of the handmade chocolates in her review.
No one could resist the delectability of the freshly baked bread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deliciousness - the quality of being delicious
  • Antonym: unpalatability - the quality of being unpleasant to taste

Etymology

From Latin *delectabilis* (delightful) via Old French *delectable* + English suffix *-ity*.
