Lepiota rhacodes

1. NOUN

/ lɛˈpaɪ.ətə ˈræ.kədiːz / (AmE), / lɛˈpɪ.ətə ˈrak.ədiːz / (BrE)
a large brown-capped mushroom with shaggy scales and a thick stalk, commonly known as the shaggy parasol
a species of shaggy parasol mushroom

Examples

The Lepiota rhacodes grows in shaded woodland areas during late summer and autumn.
Care should be taken, as some people may have allergic reactions to the Lepiota rhacodes.
We found a cluster of Lepiota rhacodes while foraging in the forest.

Etymology

From modern Latin: Lepiota (genus name), from Greek lepis (scale); rhacodes from Greek rhakos (rag, shred), referring to its shaggy cap.
