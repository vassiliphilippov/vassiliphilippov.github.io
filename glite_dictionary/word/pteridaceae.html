Pteridaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrɪˈdeɪsiaˌi / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɪˈdeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A large family of ferns found worldwide, comprising various genera and species, often characterized by their divided fronds.
a family of ferns
medical

Examples

Many tropical forests are home to a remarkable diversity of Pteridaceae.
Some species within the Pteridaceae family are commonly cultivated as ornamental plants.
Botanists debate the precise classification of Pteridaceae among the ferns.

Etymology

From New Latin, derived from Greek 'pteris' (fern) + '-aceae' (a taxonomic family suffix).
