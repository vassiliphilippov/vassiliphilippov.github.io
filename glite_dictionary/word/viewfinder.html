viewfinder

1. NOUN

/ ˈvjuːˌfaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈvjuːˌfaɪndə / (BrE)
an optical device on a camera or other instrument used to frame, focus, and locate the target of interest before capturing an image or measurement
optical device for framing a target

Examples

He adjusted the viewfinder to make sure the mountain was perfectly centered in the photograph.
Modern cameras often have an electronic viewfinder as well as an LCD screen.
Looking through the viewfinder, she framed the scene before pressing the shutter button.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rangefinder - a device on a camera that measures subject distance to focus automatically

Etymology

From 'view' + 'finder', indicating a device that helps find the correct view.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvjuˌfaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈvjuːˌfaɪndə / (BrE)
an optical device in a camera or other instrument that shows the field of view and helps the user frame or locate the subject
optical framing device in cameras

Examples

Using the viewfinder, she carefully framed the photograph before pressing the shutter.
Many modern cameras offer both an electronic viewfinder and a traditional optical one.
He found it easier to track birds through the viewfinder than by looking directly at the sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rangefinder - camera device for measuring distance to aid focusing

Etymology

Formed from 'view' + 'finder', describing its function to help the user find the view to be photographed.
