telemetry

1. NOUN

/ təˈlɛm.ə.tri / (AmE), / tɪˈlɛm.ɪ.tri / (BrE)
the process of automatically measuring and transmitting data from remote or inaccessible sources to receiving equipment for monitoring or analysis
automatic remote measurement and data transmission

Examples

The spacecraft uses telemetry to send scientific data back to mission control on Earth.
Engineers analyzed the telemetry from the test vehicle to assess its performance during the trial.
Modern wildlife tracking relies on telemetry to monitor animals' locations and movements remotely.

Etymology

From Greek 'tele-' (far) + 'metron' (measure), via French 'télémétrie'.
