Grindelia

1. PROPN

/ ɡrɪnˈdiːliə / (AmE), / ɡrɪnˈdiːliə / (BrE)
A genus of coarse, sticky herbs native to western North and Central America, some species of which have medicinal uses.
genus of sticky herbs native to the Americas

Examples

Species in the Grindelia genus are often found growing in dry, open habitats.
Traditional remedies sometimes use extracts from Grindelia plants for their medicinal properties.
Botanists discovered a new species of Grindelia in northern Mexico.

Etymology

Named after David Hieronymus Grindel, Latvian botanist (1776–1836).

2. NOUN

/ ɡraɪnˈdiːliə / (AmE), / ɡrɪnˈdiːliə / (BrE)
A genus of coarse, sticky herbs native to western North and Central America, belonging to the daisy family.
genus of sticky herbs (botany)

Examples

The sticky leaves of Grindelia are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Grindelia genus in California.
Grindelia plants thrive in dry, open habitats across North America.

Etymology

Named after David Hieronymus Grindel, a Latvian botanist.
