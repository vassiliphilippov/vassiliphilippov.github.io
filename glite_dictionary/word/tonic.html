tonic

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of a language) employing variations in pitch or tone to distinguish meanings of otherwise similar words.
using tone to distinguish word meanings (linguistics)

Examples

Mandarin is a tonic language, meaning the pitch or intonation changes the meaning of words.
Many African languages are classified as tonic because their word meanings depend on tone.
In tonic languages, the same syllable pronounced with different pitches can have different meanings.

Synset Examples

Chinese is a tonal language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tonal - (of language) distinguishing word meaning by tone or pitch

Etymology

From Greek tonos (tone).

4. ADJ

/ ˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of a syllable) bearing the main stress in a word, often accompanied by a pitch change.

Examples

The tonic syllable in 'about' is the second syllable, not the first.
In English pronunciation, the tonic syllable often receives a noticeable pitch change.
When teaching intonation, it's important to identify the tonic syllable in each phrase.
