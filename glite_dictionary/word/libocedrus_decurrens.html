Libocedrus decurrens

1. NOUN

/ laɪboʊˈsiːdrəs dɪˈkɜrənz / (AmE), / lɪbəˈsiːdrəs dɪˈkʌrənz / (BrE)
a tall tree native to the Pacific coast of North America, with cypress-like foliage and cinnamon-red bark; also known as incense cedar
Pacific coast tall tree; incense cedar

Examples

The Libocedrus decurrens is prized for its aromatic wood and attractive, red-brown bark.
Many parks in California feature mature Libocedrus decurrens specimens for their shade and beauty.
Botanists often study the foliage of Libocedrus decurrens to compare it with other cypress relatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incense cedar - common name for Libocedrus decurrens

Etymology

From Greek 'libanos' (frankincense) and Latin 'cedrus' (cedar); 'decurrens' means 'running downwards' in Latin, referring to the leaf bases.
