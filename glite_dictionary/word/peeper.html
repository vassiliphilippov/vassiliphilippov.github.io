2. NOUN

/ ˈpiːpər / (AmE), / ˈpiːpə / (BrE)
a person who secretly observes others, especially for sexual gratification
person who spies for sexual gratification

Examples

The police arrested the peeper after several complaints from local residents.
She felt uneasy, suspecting someone was acting as a peeper outside her window.
A notorious peeper was finally caught by security cameras at the hotel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voyeur - a person who gains sexual pleasure from watching others when they are naked or engaged in sexual activity

Etymology

From the verb 'peep' (to look quickly or secretly), plus the agent noun suffix '-er'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpipər / (AmE), / ˈpiːpə / (BrE)
an animal, especially a small frog, that makes short high-pitched sounds, typically in spring

Examples

At night in the wetlands, the peeper chorus is astonishingly loud.
Every spring, the distinctive call of the peeper signals the end of winter.
Biologists listened carefully for the first peeper calls to measure the start of the amphibian breeding season.
