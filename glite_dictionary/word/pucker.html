pucker

1. VERB

/ ˈpʌk.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈpʌk.ə / (BrE)
(of a part of the body, fabric, etc.) to become wrinkled or gathered into small folds, especially by contracting or tightening.
to become wrinkled or drawn together

Examples

She couldn't help but pucker her lips before blowing a kiss.
His brow puckered in concentration as he read the letter.
The fabric puckered after being washed in hot water.

Synset Examples

her lips puckered

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - to make or become smooth or even

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *pukeren* or related to Dutch *pokkeren* (to wrinkle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01281618-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʌkɚ / (AmE), / ˈpʌkə / (BrE)
an irregular fold or wrinkle in an otherwise smooth surface, especially in cloth or fabric
an uneven fold or wrinkle in fabric

Examples

She noticed a small pucker near the seam of her new dress.
The tailor carefully ironed out every pucker from the suit jacket.
A faint pucker ran across the smooth tablecloth after it was washed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrinkle - a small line or fold, especially in skin or fabric
  • Synonym: crease - a line or ridge produced by folding

Etymology

From Middle English 'puken' (to draw together), perhaps imitative in origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13930861-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈpʌkər / (AmE), / ˈpʌkə / (BrE)
to draw or gather (something, especially skin or fabric) into small folds or wrinkles
draw into small folds or wrinkles

Examples

She puckered her lips before giving him a kiss.
The tailor warned that improper sewing could pucker the fabric.
Cold wind made his skin pucker and tighten.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - remove folds or wrinkles; make smooth
  • Synonym: wrinkle - make small lines or folds in

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'poken' (to poke, thrust), influenced by 'pouch'.

4. VERB

to gather or contract into small wrinkles or folds
to form small wrinkles or folds

Examples

He tried not to pucker his forehead while thinking deeply.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
