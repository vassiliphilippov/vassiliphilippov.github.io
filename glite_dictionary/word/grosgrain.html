grosgrain

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡroʊˌɡreɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊˌɡreɪn / (BrE)
a strong fabric with a ribbed surface, often made of silk or a silk-like material, used especially for ribbons and trimmings
ribbed silk or silk-like fabric

Examples

The dress was trimmed with a wide grosgrain ribbon around the waist.
She chose grosgrain for the hatband because of its durability and elegant texture.
Many luxury gift boxes are tied with colorful grosgrain ribbons.

Etymology

From French 'gros grain', meaning 'coarse texture'.
