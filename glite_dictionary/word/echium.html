Echium

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkiəm / (AmE), / ˈɛkɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of bristly herbs and shrubs in the family Boraginaceae, including species such as viper's bugloss, often known for their colorful flowers and rough stems
a genus of bristly herbs and shrubs

Examples

Many gardeners plant Echium for its vibrant blue or purple flower spikes in spring and summer.
Viper's bugloss, an Echium species, is popular with pollinators like bees and butterflies.
The rough, bristly leaves are characteristic of plants in the Echium genus.

Etymology

From Greek ekhion (viper's bugloss), due to the appearance of the seeds.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛkiəm / (AmE), / ˈɛkɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of bristly herbs and shrubs in the family Boraginaceae, including several species with blue or purple flowers
genus of bristly herbs and shrubs (Botany)

Examples

The garden features several species from the Echium genus known for their tall, spiky flowers.
One variety of Echium can thrive in coastal environments with poor soil.
Echium plants are popular with bees due to their abundant nectar.

Etymology

From Latin *echium*, from Greek *ekhion* (viper's bugloss).
