rolled

1. ADJ

/ roʊld / (AmE), / rəʊld / (BrE)
(of a consonant, especially 'r') pronounced with a trill, as by vibrating the tongue or uvula.
pronounced with a trill (linguistics)

Examples

Some Spanish words contain a rolled 'r' sound.
She practiced her rolled consonants before the performance.
The Scottish accent often features a rolled 'r'.

Synset Examples

she used rolling r's as in Spanish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trilled - (of a sound) produced with a rapid vibration, especially by the tongue or uvula

2. ADJ

/ roʊld / (AmE), / rəʊld / (BrE)
(of petals or leaves in bud) having the edges or margins curled or rolled inward.
petals or leaves with edges rolled inward

Examples

The rolled leaves protect the young plant from harsh sunshine.
Many wildflowers develop rolled petals before they fully open.
The botanist noted that the rolled margins distinguish this species from its relatives.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: revolute - (of botanical margins) rolled backward or downward
  • Synonym: involute - (of botanical margins) with edges rolled inward, similar meaning
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02325735-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ roʊld / (AmE), / rəʊld / (BrE)
(of something) folded or wound into a cylindrical shape and secured; wrapped or coiled up tightly.
folded or wound into a cylindrical shape

Examples

He carried a rolled umbrella as rain was expected.
The artist stored her sketches in a rolled sheet of paper.
A rolled carpet was left in the hallway, ready to be laid.

Synset Examples

furled sails bound securely to the spar
a furled flag
his rolled umbrella hanging on his arm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furled - rolled up and secured (especially of sails or flags)
  • Antonym: unrolled - not rolled up; spread out
Source: WordNet
