Guarnerius

1. NOUN

/ ɡwɑːrˈnɪəriəs / (AmE), / ɡwɑːˈnɪəriəs / (BrE)
A violin made by a member of the Guarneri family, renowned for their craftsmanship in Cremona, Italy.
violin made by Guarneri family

Examples

The priceless Guarnerius was displayed in a secure glass case at the museum.
She performed the concerto on a rare Guarnerius from the eighteenth century.
Experts authenticated the violin as a genuine Guarnerius crafted in Cremona.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stradivarius - a violin made by a member of the Stradivari family

Etymology

From Italian Guarneri, the surname of a family of master violin makers of Cremona.

2. PROPN

/ ɡwɑrˈnɛriəs / (AmE), / ɡwɑːˈnɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
A member of a famous Italian family of violin makers, especially Giuseppe Guarneri; Guarnerius violins are highly prized by musicians.
famous Italian violin maker or family

Examples

The violinist played a rare Guarnerius that was over 200 years old.
Collectors value a Guarnerius almost as highly as a Stradivarius due to their exceptional sound.
A Guarnerius from the 18th century can fetch millions at an auction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stradivarius - violins made by Antonio Stradivari, also highly prized
  • Synonym: Amati - another renowned family of Italian violin makers

Etymology

Latinized form of the Italian surname 'Guarneri'.
