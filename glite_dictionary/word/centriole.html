centriole

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɹiˌoʊl / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tri.əʊl / (BrE)
a small cylindrical organelle near the nucleus in animal cells, involved in the development of spindle fibers during cell division
cylindrical cell organelle in animal cells
medical

Examples

The centriole plays a key role in organizing microtubules during cell division.
Researchers observed that the centriole duplicates once per cell cycle.
Without a functioning centriole, animal cells cannot properly form the mitotic spindle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organelle - a specialized structure within a cell

Etymology

From French *centriole*, from Greek *kentron* (center) + -iole (diminutive suffix).
