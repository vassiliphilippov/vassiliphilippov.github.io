vast

1. ADJ

/ væst / (AmE), / vɑːst / (BrE)
extremely large in size, amount, extent, or degree; immense.
extremely large or immense

Examples

The Sahara Desert is known for its vast stretches of sand dunes.
There was a vast improvement in the city's air quality after new regulations were enforced.
She stood in awe, gazing at the vast expanse of the ocean before her.

Synset Examples

huge government spending

the Los Angeles aqueduct winds like an immense snake along the base of the mountains
immense numbers of birds
at vast (or immense) expense
the vast reaches of outer space
the vast accumulation of knowledge … which we call civilization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immense - extremely large or great
  • Synonym: huge - very large in size or amount
  • Antonym: tiny - very small in size or amount

Etymology

From Latin 'vastus' (empty, enormous).
