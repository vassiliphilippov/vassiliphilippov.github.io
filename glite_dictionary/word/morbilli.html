morbilli

1. NOUN

/ mɔrˈbɪlaɪ / (AmE), / mɔːˈbɪlɪ / (BrE)
An acute and highly contagious viral disease characterized by distinct red spots followed by a rash, mainly affecting children; another name for measles.
acute viral disease; measles
medical archaic

Examples

Several outbreaks of morbilli were reported in the 19th century before the vaccine was developed.
In medical literature, morbilli is often used to refer to measles.
The symptoms of morbilli include a high fever and a characteristic red rash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measles - An acute and highly contagious viral disease with red rash, mostly in children

Etymology

From Latin 'morbilli' (measles, little diseases), diminutive of 'morbus' (disease).
