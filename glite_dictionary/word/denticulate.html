denticulate

1. ADJ

/ dɛnˈtɪkjələt / (AmE), / dɛnˈtɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
having a margin or edge finely toothed with small, close-set projections; minutely serrated, especially in reference to leaves or other natural edges.
finely toothed or serrated (of a margin)

Examples

The leaf margins were denticulate, making them rough to the touch.
Viewed under the microscope, the shell appeared distinctly denticulate along its edge.
The botanist described the species as having denticulate sepals and soft, hairy stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serrated - having a notched or sawlike edge

Etymology

From Latin 'denticula' (small tooth), diminutive of 'dens' (tooth).
