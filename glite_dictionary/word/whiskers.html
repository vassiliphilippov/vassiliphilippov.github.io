whiskers

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪskərz / (AmE), / ˈwɪskəz / (BrE)
the hairs growing on the lower part of a man's face, especially on the cheeks and chin
facial hair on cheeks and chin

Examples

He decided to grow out his whiskers over the winter.
The old man's whiskers were long and turning grey.
She playfully tugged at his whiskers as he smiled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beard - hair growing on the chin and cheeks of a man
  • Degree Lower: mustache - hair growing on the upper lip

Etymology

From 'whisker', likely from 'whisk', referring to something that sweeps or brushes, used for bristly hairs.
