twofer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuː.fɚ / (AmE), / ˈtuː.fə / (BrE)
a coupon, offer, or deal that allows the purchase of two items for the price of one
two for one deal or coupon
informal american

Examples

The theater offered a twofer promotion for Friday night's performance.
I picked up a twofer coupon for coffee at the café.
During the holiday sale, many stores featured twofer deals on select merchandise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: BOGO - deal meaning 'buy one, get one'
  • Degree Lower: discount - a reduction in price

Etymology

Blend of 'two' and 'for (the price of) [one]'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtuːfər / (AmE), / ˈtuːfə / (BrE)
an offer in which two items are sold for the price of one
offer of two for the price of one
informal

Examples

Today at the cinema, there's a twofer deal on all movie tickets.
She went to buy shoes and scored a twofer at her favorite store.
The pizza place is running a twofer special every Friday night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bargain - an advantageous purchase; something bought or offered at a low price

Etymology

From the phrase 'two for (the price of one)'.
