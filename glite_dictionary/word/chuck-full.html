chuck-full

1. ADJ

/ tʃʌkˈfʊl / (AmE), / tʃʌkˈfʊl / (BrE)
Filled to capacity; completely full.
packed full to capacity

Examples

The basket was chuck-full of ripe strawberries after our morning in the fields.
By noon, his schedule was chuck-full with meetings and appointments.
Her mind was chuck-full of new ideas after reading the inspiring book.

Synset Examples

chowder chockablock with pieces of fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chock-full - completely full; filled to capacity
  • Synonym: brimming - filled to the brim

Etymology

Originates as an alteration of 'chock-full', possibly from Middle English 'chokeful' (crammed full).
