witloof

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪt.luːf / (AmE), / ˈwɪt.luːf / (BrE)
A type of chicory with leaves, either curly or broad and often blanched, valued as a salad green.
salad vegetable, type of chicory

Examples

She added chopped witloof to the salad for a hint of bitterness.
The market sells both curly and flat-leaf varieties of witloof.
Blanched witloof is often served as a delicacy in European cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicory - a plant with bitter leaves, also used as a salad green

Etymology

From Dutch witloof, meaning 'white leaf'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtˌloʊf / (AmE), / ˈwɪt.luːf / (BrE)
A variety of endive with broad, pale leaves formed by growing the plant in darkness, commonly used as a vegetable in salads and cooking.
variety of endive grown in darkness

Examples

The salad featured roasted witloof with a light vinaigrette.
She grew witloof in her garden for its crisp, slightly bitter flavor.
Sautéed witloof makes a delicious side dish for roasted meats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Belgian endive - cultivated endive with pale, tightly packed leaves
  • Synonym: chicory - herbaceous plant of the daisy family, related to endive

Etymology

From Dutch 'witloof', meaning 'white leaf'.
