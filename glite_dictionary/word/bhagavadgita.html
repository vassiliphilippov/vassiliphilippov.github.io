Bhagavadgita

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːɡəˌvɑːˈɡiːtə / (AmE), / ˌbʌɡəˌvʌˈɡiːtə / (BrE)
a sacred Hindu scripture, part of the Mahabharata, featuring a philosophical dialogue between Krishna and Arjuna about duty and life
Hindu sacred scripture, part of Mahabharata

Examples

The teachings of the Bhagavadgita have influenced many leaders, including Mahatma Gandhi.
In college, we studied the philosophical ideas presented in the Bhagavadgita.
He quoted passages from the Bhagavadgita during his lecture on world religions.

Etymology

From Sanskrit Bhagavad-gītā, meaning 'Song of the Lord'.
