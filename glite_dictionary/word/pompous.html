pompous

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːmpəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒmpəs / (BrE)
having or showing an excessive sense of self-importance or grandeur, often in a way that seems pretentious or overly formal
excessively grand or self-important

Examples

The speech sounded far too pompous for such a simple occasion.
He walked into the room with a pompous air, as if expecting applause.
The ceremony was unnecessarily pompous, complete with trumpets and elaborate robes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretentious - attempting to impress by affecting greater importance or merit
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From Late Latin 'pomposus', from Latin 'pompa' (procession, display).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːm.pəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒm.pəs / (BrE)
Having or showing an excessive sense of self-importance, often in speech or manner; characterized by exaggerated dignity or self-importance.
excessively self-important or grand

Examples

He gave a pompous speech that made everyone roll their eyes.
The professor's pompous attitude discouraged students from asking questions.
Critics called the ceremony overly formal and pompous.

Synset Examples

a grandiloquent and boastful manner
overblown oratory
a pompous speech
pseudo-scientific gobbledygook and pontifical hooey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretentious - attempting to impress by affecting greater importance
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From Old French 'pompeux', from Latin 'pomposus' (stately, ceremonious), from 'pompa' (procession, display).
