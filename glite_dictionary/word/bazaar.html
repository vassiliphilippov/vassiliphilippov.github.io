bazaar

1. NOUN

/ bəˈzɑr / (AmE), / bəˈzɑː / (BrE)
An event where miscellaneous items are sold, often to raise money for charity.
charitable sale event

Examples

The local school hosted a bazaar to raise funds for new equipment.
She found unique crafts at the annual church bazaar.
Many volunteers donated homemade goods for the charity bazaar.

Synset Examples

the church bazaar

Etymology

From Persian *bāzār* (market).

2. NOUN

/ bəˈzɑr / (AmE), / bəˈzɑː / (BrE)
a street or area of small shops selling various goods, especially in Middle Eastern or Asian countries; a marketplace
marketplace with small shops

Examples

She loves exploring the colorful bazaar when she travels to Istanbul.
Vendors in the old bazaar offer spices, carpets, and handmade jewelry.
The city's bazaar bustled with activity from sunrise to sunset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market - an open space or building where goods are bought and sold

Etymology

From Persian *bāzār* (market), via Italian and French.

3. NOUN

/ bəˈzɑr / (AmE), / bəˈzɑː / (BrE)
a shop or marketplace where a wide variety of goods are sold, often in a lively or open-air setting
marketplace selling diverse goods

Examples

We spent the afternoon wandering through the bustling bazaar in search of unique souvenirs.
Each stall in the bazaar offered something different, from spices to handcrafted jewelry.
The annual charity bazaar raised funds by selling homemade goods and gifts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market - an area or event where goods are bought and sold
  • Synonym: shop - a place where goods are sold to customers

Etymology

From Persian *bāzār* (marketplace), via Turkish and Arabic.
