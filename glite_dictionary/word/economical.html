economical

1. ADJ

/ ˌiːkəˈnɑːmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪkəl / (BrE)
using no more of something than is necessary; avoiding waste of money, resources, or time
avoiding waste; efficient with resources or time

Examples

The new car is much more economical than her previous one, saving money on fuel.
She prefers an economical style of cooking that makes use of every ingredient.
Their economical approach to travel allowed them to visit many countries on a tight budget.

Synset Examples

an economic use of home heating oil
a modern economical heating system
an economical use of her time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: efficient - working well without wasting time or resources
  • Antonym: wasteful - using resources carelessly or inefficiently

Etymology

From Greek oikonomikos (practiced in the management of a household or family), via Latin oeconomicus and Old French économique.

2. ADJ

/ ˌikəˈnɑmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to an economy or the system of production and management of a country's material wealth.
relating to an economy or system of wealth production

Examples

The committee discussed economical reforms to improve national productivity.
This report examines the economical relationships between different sectors of the industry.
Their research focuses on the economical structure of developing countries.

Synset Examples

economic growth
aspects of social, political, and economical life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: economic - relating to the economy or economics

Etymology

From Greek oikonomikos (skilled in household management), from oikonomia (management of a household).

3. ADJ

/ ˌikəˈnɑmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪkəl / (BrE)
using resources carefully to avoid waste; not spending more than necessary.
avoiding waste; saving resources

Examples

Her new car is very economical on fuel and requires little maintenance.
They found it more economical to cook at home rather than eat out every day.
We've adopted several economical measures to reduce waste in our office.

Synset Examples

an economical meal
an economical shopper
a frugal farmer
a frugal lunch
a sparing father and a spending son
sparing in their use of heat and light
stinting in bestowing gifts
‘scotch’ is used only informally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrifty - using money and resources carefully; not wasteful
  • Synonym: frugal - careful about spending money; not wasteful
  • Antonym: extravagant - spending too much money or using too many resources

Etymology

From Greek oikonomikos (skilled in household management), from oikonomia (management of a household).
