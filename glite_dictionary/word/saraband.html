saraband

1. NOUN

/ ˈsær.əˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈsær.əˌbænd / (BrE)
A slow, stately dance in triple time, or music composed for such a dance, popular in the 17th and 18th centuries.
slow, stately dance or its music

Examples

The composer included a saraband as the third movement of his suite.
She danced a graceful saraband at the baroque music festival.
Many baroque keyboard pieces feature a saraband with expressive melodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minuet - a slow, graceful dance in triple time, popular during the same period

Etymology

From French 'sarabande', from Spanish 'zarabanda', originally of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsærəˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈsærəbænd / (BrE)
a slow, stately court dance popular in the 17th and 18th centuries, usually performed in triple time
slow, stately 17th–18th-century dance

Examples

The musicians played a saraband as the dancers moved gracefully across the floor.
A saraband features prominently in many Baroque music suites.
During the performance, the elegance of the saraband captivated the audience.

Etymology

From French sarabande, from Spanish zarabanda, possibly ultimately from Persian saraband (a type of dance).
