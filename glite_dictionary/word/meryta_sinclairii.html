Meryta sinclairii

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛr.i.tə sɪŋˈklɛr.i.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɛr.ɪ.tə sɪŋˈklɛː.rɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
A small, round-headed tree native to New Zealand, known for its large, glossy, resinous leaves and clusters of greenish-white flowers.
tree species native to New Zealand

Examples

The Meryta sinclairii is prized in gardens for its distinctive round canopy and glossy leaves.
Botanists were excited to find a mature Meryta sinclairii growing in the remote forest.
Many New Zealand parks feature the Meryta sinclairii for its unique appearance and native status.

Etymology

Named after Scottish botanist Andrew Sinclair and genus Meryta.
