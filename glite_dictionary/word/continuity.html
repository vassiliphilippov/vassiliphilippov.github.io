continuity

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːntɪˈnuːɪti / (AmE), / ˌkɒntɪˈnjuːɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of continuing without interruption; an unbroken and consistent duration or existence over a period of time
uninterrupted duration or existence

Examples

The film's story lost its continuity due to several abrupt flashbacks.
Her daily meditation helped her maintain a sense of continuity through stressful times.
Historians debate the continuity of cultural practices from ancient to modern societies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discontinuity - interruption or gap in a sequence or duration

Etymology

From Latin 'continuĭtas', from 'continuus' (uninterrupted, continuous).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːntəˈnuːəti / (AmE), / ˌkɒntɪˈnjuːɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being continuous and connected without interruption
unbroken connection or flow

Examples

The director ensured continuity between different scenes in the movie.
Good teachers provide continuity in their students' learning experience.
After the merger, maintaining continuity of services became a top priority.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discontinuity - a break or interruption in the connection or sequence
  • Synonym: sequence - a continuous series or succession of things

Etymology

From Latin 'continuitas' (an unbroken connection or succession).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.t̬əˈnuː.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.tɪˈnjuː.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
A detailed script or plan used in filmmaking to ensure visual and narrative consistency from shot to shot.
script ensuring consistency in film shots

Examples

The continuity required the actors to wear the same costumes throughout the scene.
She double-checked the continuity to make sure every prop was in place for each shot.
Errors in the continuity were quickly spotted by the sharp-eyed film editor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: script - the written text of a film, play, or broadcast
