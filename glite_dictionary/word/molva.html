Molva

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊlvə / (AmE), / ˈmɒlvə / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the cod family, including the ling and related species, found in North Atlantic waters.
a genus of cod-like marine fish

Examples

The Molva genus includes several commercially important fish species.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of Molva in the North Atlantic.
The largest member of the Molva genus is the common ling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ling - a large, long-bodied marine fish of the cod family, Molva molva

Etymology

From Latin scientific name *Molva*, used in zoological classification.
