unbounded

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbaʊndɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈbaʊndɪd / (BrE)
without limits; seemingly infinite in amount, number, degree, or extent
without limits or boundaries

Examples

Her unbounded curiosity led her to explore every corner of the old mansion.
The team's unbounded optimism inspired everyone during the challenging project.
He approached the task with unbounded enthusiasm, never tiring despite the obstacles.

Synset Examples

unbounded enthusiasm
children with boundless energy
a limitless supply of money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundless - without any limits; infinite
  • Synonym: limitless - without limits in any respect
  • Antonym: restricted - limited in extent or scope

Etymology

Prefix 'un-' (not) + 'bounded' (having bounds or limits), from Middle English 'bounden', past participle of 'binden' (to bind).
