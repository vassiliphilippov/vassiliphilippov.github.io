Habenaria albiflora

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæbəˈnɛəriə ˌæl.bɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / ˌhabəˈnɛːrɪə ˌal.bɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A species of bog orchid native to eastern North America, characterized by a spike of pure white fringed flowers.
a bog orchid with white fringed flowers

Examples

The rare Habenaria albiflora blooms in secluded wetlands every summer.
Botanists documented a population of Habenaria albiflora in the protected marshland.
The pure white flowers of Habenaria albiflora are easily distinguished from other native orchids.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Habenaria' (the genus name) and 'albiflora' meaning 'white-flowered'.
