Dasyurinae

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæziˈjʊərɪˌniː / (AmE), / ˌdæsjʊˈrɪːniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of small to medium-sized carnivorous marsupials native to Australia and New Guinea, including dasyures, native cats, pouched mice, banded anteaters, and Tasmanian devils.
a marsupial subfamily (carnivorous species)

Examples

The Dasyurinae subfamily includes the well-known Tasmanian devil.
Researchers observed several species within the Dasyurinae hunting for insects at night.
Conservation efforts have increased for rare members of the Dasyurinae, such as the northern quoll.

Etymology

From Greek dasys (hairy) + oura (tail), referring to the bushy tails of many species.
