acceptor

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛptɚ / (AmE), / əkˈsɛptə / (BrE)
(Chemistry) A substance or atom that receives a pair of electrons from another atom or molecule to form a coordinate bond.
chemical compound that receives electrons
medical

Examples

In this reaction, the oxygen atom acts as the acceptor of the electron pair.
A coordinate bond forms when an electron acceptor combines with a donor molecule.
The acceptor plays a crucial role in the transfer of electrons during the process.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: donor - (Chemistry) a compound that donates an electron pair to another compound in bond formation

Etymology

From Latin acceptor (receiver), from accipere (to receive).

2. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛptɚ / (AmE), / əkˈsɛptə / (BrE)
a person or institution that accepts a bill of exchange, check, or draft and assumes responsibility for paying the amount specified when it becomes due
person or institution accepting responsibility for payment of a draft

Examples

The acceptor of the draft is required to make the payment when it is due.
Once the check has been endorsed, the acceptor becomes legally obliged to pay the sum.
In international trade, the bank often acts as the acceptor to guarantee the transaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawee - the party on whom a bill of exchange is drawn and who is expected to pay it
  • Antonym: drawer - the person who writes a bill of exchange, check, or draft

Etymology

From Latin acceptor (receiver), from acceptare (to accept).
