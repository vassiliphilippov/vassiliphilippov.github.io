tody

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊdi / (AmE), / ˈtəʊdi / (BrE)
a small, brightly colored insectivorous bird native to the West Indies, distinguished by its red and green plumage and long, straight bill
tiny West Indian insectivorous bird

Examples

The vibrant feathers of the tody make it easy to spot among the dense Caribbean foliage.
Researchers observed a tody catching insects with its slender bill.
Several todies gathered near the stream at dawn, chirping softly.
