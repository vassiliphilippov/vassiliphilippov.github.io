Callitris calcarata

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈtraɪs ˌkælkəˈreɪtə / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈtraɪs ˌkælkəˈrɑːtə / (BrE)
an Australian coniferous tree with small, flattened scale-like leaves and numerous dark brown seeds, valued for its timber and resin
Australian coniferous tree noted for timber and resin

Examples

The timber from Callitris calcarata is highly prized for its durability.
Many regions in Australia rely on Callitris calcarata for both building materials and resin extraction.
The small, scale-like leaves make Callitris calcarata easily distinguishable from other native trees.

Etymology

From the genus name Callitris (Greek kallos, 'beauty' + itris, 'tree') and species name calcarata (Latin, 'spurred').
