insurrection

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnsəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnsəˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
An organized attempt by a group of people to overthrow their government or the authority in power, often involving violence.
violent uprising against authority

Examples

The government quickly responded to the insurrection with a state of emergency.
During the insurrection, several key buildings were seized by the rebels.
Historians study the causes behind each insurrection to understand political change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebellion - organized, open resistance to authority, often less violent than insurrection
  • Synonym: revolt - an attempt to overthrow authority, usually on a local scale
  • Degree Higher: revolution - a forcible overthrow of government or social order, typically broader than insurrection

Etymology

From Latin insurrectio (a rising up), from insurgere (to rise up).
