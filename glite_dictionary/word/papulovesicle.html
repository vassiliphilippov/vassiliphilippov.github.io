papulovesicle

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæpjəloʊˈvɛsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpapjʊləʊˈvɛsɪk(ə)l / (BrE)
A small, raised skin lesion (papule) that develops into a blister containing fluid.
a skin lesion turning into a blister
medical

Examples

Patients with chickenpox often develop papulovesicles on their skin.
The dermatologist identified a red papulovesicle on the patient's forearm.
A papulovesicle is commonly seen in various viral skin infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vesicle - a small fluid-filled blister on the skin

Etymology

From Latin papula (pimple) + vesicula (small bladder, blister).
