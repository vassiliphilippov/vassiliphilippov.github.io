to commingle

1. VERB

/ kəˈmɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / kəˈmɪŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to mix or blend different things together; to combine into one mass or whole
to mix or blend together

Examples

The festival aims to commingle cultures from all over the world.
The company is not allowed to commingle customer funds with its own assets.
The artist likes to commingle different media in her work.

Synset Examples

His book commingles sarcasm and sadness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mix - to combine or blend into one mass or mixture
  • Antonym: separate - to set apart or divide into different elements

Etymology

From Latin 'com-' (together) + 'minglere' (to mix).

2. VERB

/ kəˈmɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / kəˈmɪŋɡl / (BrE)
to mix different substances, elements, or groups together so that they become combined or difficult to distinguish separately
to mix or blend together

Examples

Rainwater tends to commingle with untreated waste in the old city sewers.
The novel's plot commingles mystery with romance to create a unique story.
Some investors avoid funds that commingle assets, preferring separate accounts.

Synset Examples

The colors blend well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blend - to mix different things together thoroughly
  • Antonym: separate - to set or keep apart; not mix

Etymology

From Latin 'com-' (together) + 'minglere' (to mix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00395719-v
Synset POS: v
