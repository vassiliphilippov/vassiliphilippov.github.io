knout

1. NOUN

/ naʊt / (AmE), / naʊt / (BrE)
a heavy whip with a lash of leather thongs twisted with wire, formerly used in Russia for punishing prisoners
Russian punishment whip

Examples

The prisoner was cruelly beaten with a knout as a form of punishment.
He described the scars left on his back by the knout.
The museum exhibit displayed a knout once used in imperial Russia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whip - an instrument for flogging or driving animals

Etymology

From Russian 'knot', via French and Dutch.
