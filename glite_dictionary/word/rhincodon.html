Rhincodon

1. NOUN

/ raɪnˈkoʊˌdɑn / (AmE), / rɪŋˈkəʊdɒn / (BrE)
A genus of large filter-feeding sharks, containing only the whale shark, the largest known extant fish species.
genus of whale sharks

Examples

The Rhincodon is recognized as the sole genus of whale sharks in the family Rhincodontidae.
Scientists study the migratory patterns of Rhincodon to better understand these gentle giants.
A single Rhincodon can filter thousands of liters of water every hour while feeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whale shark - the largest living species of fish, known for filter-feeding

Etymology

From Greek 'rhinos' (nose) + 'odous' (tooth).
