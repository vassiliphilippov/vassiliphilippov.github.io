to outcuss

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈkʌs / (AmE), / aʊtˈkʌs / (BrE)
to swear or use profanities more than someone known to swear a lot, especially a sailor; to outdo someone in cursing
to swear more than someone else
informal

Examples

No one in the office could outcuss Mark when his computer crashed.
She could easily outcuss her brothers during a heated argument.
At the game, the fans tried to outcuss each other after the referee's call.

Synset Examples

They joked that he could outcuss a sailor on shore leave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outswear - to swear more than someone else
  • Synonym: curse - to use offensive words or profane language
  • Antonym: to bless - to speak well of someone, the opposite of to curse

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' (to surpass) and 'cuss' (slang for 'curse', meaning to swear).
