highbrow

1. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪˌbraʊ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌbraʊ / (BrE)
intellectually or culturally superior; relating to people or activities considered highly cultured or educated.
culturally or intellectually superior

Examples

The museum is known for its highbrow exhibitions that attract art enthusiasts.
She prefers highbrow literature to popular novels.
His taste in music became increasingly highbrow after college.

Synset Examples

highbrow events such as the ballet or opera
a highbrowed literary critic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowbrow - not highly intellectual or cultured
  • Synonym: intellectual - relating to the intellect or to high-level thinking

Etymology

From the notion that people with high foreheads (brows) are more intelligent; first used in late 19th century America.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌbraʊ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪbraʊ / (BrE)
a person who has intellectual or cultured interests; someone who prefers or appreciates serious or complex artistic, literary, or academic work
person with intellectual or cultured tastes

Examples

The new art gallery attracted many highbrows from the city.
At dinner parties, he likes to engage highbrows in debates on philosophy.
Some critics dismissed the novel as too difficult for anyone except dedicated highbrows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intellectual - a person possessing a highly developed intellect
  • Antonym: lowbrow - a person with less intellectual or less cultured tastes

Etymology

Originally from the idea that a high forehead was a sign of intelligence. First used in the early 20th century.
