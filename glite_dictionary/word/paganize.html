to paganize

1. VERB

/ ˈpeɪɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpeɪɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to make something pagan in character or influence
make pagan in character

Examples

Many rural traditions were gradually paganized over the centuries.
The emperor sought to paganize certain Christian holidays to win support from other groups.
Some scholars argue that the celebrations were intentionally paganized to encourage broader participation.

Synset Examples

The Church paganized Christianity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to Christianize - to make Christian in character or influence
  • Synonym: to secularize - to make more secular or less religious

Etymology

Formed from 'pagan' + '-ize', modeled after similar Latin-derived formations.
