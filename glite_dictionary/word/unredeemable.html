unredeemable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈdim.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈdiː.mə.bəl / (BrE)
impossible to reform, correct, or improve; incapable of being redeemed
incapable of being reformed

Examples

Despite years of effort, the city considered the building unredeemable and scheduled it for demolition.
Some critics viewed the character as unredeemable after his repeated acts of betrayal.
Her addiction seemed unredeemable to her family, though she eventually sought help.

Synset Examples

vicious irreclaimable boys
irredeemable sinners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irredeemable - impossible to change or improve for the better
  • Antonym: reformable - able to be improved or changed for the better

Etymology

Formed from un- (not) + redeemable (from Latin redimere, 'to buy back')
