potation

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / pəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an alcoholic drink or the act of drinking, especially alcohol
alcoholic drink

Examples

After work, they gathered at the pub for a potation and friendly conversation.
He avoided any potation before giving his speech at the banquet.
The festive evening featured every variety of potation imaginable.

Synset Examples

they served beer on draft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: libation - an alcoholic drink poured out as an offering or consumed ceremonially

Etymology

From Latin 'potatio' (act of drinking), from 'potare' (to drink).

2. NOUN

/ poʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / pəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of drinking, especially of an alcoholic beverage
the act of drinking, esp. alcohol

Examples

The revelers enjoyed another potation before returning to the festivities.
He raised his glass in silent potation, savoring the rich wine.
After several potations, their laughter echoed across the hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drink - the act of taking liquid into the mouth and swallowing

Etymology

From Latin potatio (drinking), from potare (to drink).
