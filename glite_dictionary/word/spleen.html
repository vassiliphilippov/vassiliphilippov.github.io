spleen

1. NOUN

/ spliːn / (AmE), / spliːn / (BrE)
a feeling of bitterness or resentful anger, especially long-lasting or ill-tempered; spite
resentful anger; spite
archaic

Examples

He was full of spleen after hearing the unfair criticism.
She vented her spleen on her unsuspecting colleagues during the meeting.
The writer's spleen is evident in his harsh review of the play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • Synonym: malice - the intention or desire to do evil; ill will

Etymology

From Old French esplen, from Latin splen, from Greek splēn (the organ, believed to be the seat of emotions).

2. NOUN

/ spliːn / (AmE), / spliːn / (BrE)
A large, dark-red organ located on the left side of the abdomen that plays a role in immune response and blood cell maintenance.
large abdominal organ involved in immunity
medical

Examples

After the car accident, doctors discovered an injury to his spleen that required surgery.
The spleen is an important part of the body's immune system, helping to fight infections.
Some medical conditions can cause the spleen to become enlarged and tender.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liver - large organ that detoxifies chemicals and metabolizes drugs
  • Synonym: pancreas - glandular organ behind the stomach involved in digestion and insulin production

Etymology

From Old French 'esplen', from Latin 'splen', from Greek 'splēn' (spleen, milt).
