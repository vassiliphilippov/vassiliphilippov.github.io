eczema

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛksəmə, ɪɡˈziːmə / (AmE), / ˈɛksɪmə, ɪɡˈziːmə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which patches of skin become inflamed, itchy, cracked, and rough, sometimes with blisters.
inflammatory skin condition
medical

Examples

My son has had eczema since he was a baby, so we use special creams for his skin.
Scratching can make eczema worse and lead to infection.
Many children eventually outgrow their eczema, but some adults continue to have symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dermatitis - inflammation of the skin

Etymology

From Greek *ekzema* (an eruption), from *ekzein* (to boil over)
