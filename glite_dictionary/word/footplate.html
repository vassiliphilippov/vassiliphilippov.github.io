footplate

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈfʊtˌpleɪt / (BrE)
the platform in the cab of a locomotive on which the driver or engineer stands to operate the controls
locomotive driver's platform

Examples

The engineer stood on the footplate as he guided the train through the mountains.
She spent her childhood dreaming of working on the footplate alongside real locomotive drivers.
Regular cleaning of the footplate is essential for safe train operation.

Etymology

From 'foot' + 'plate', referring to a plate for standing on with the feet.
