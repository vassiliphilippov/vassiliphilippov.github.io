vena gastroomentalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə ˌɡæstroʊ.mɛnˈteɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə ˌɡæstrəʊ.mɛnˈteɪlɪs / (BrE)
Either of two veins that accompany the arteries along the greater curvature of the stomach and drain blood from that region.
vein of the stomach's greater curvature
medical

Examples

During surgery, the vena gastroomentalis must be carefully identified and preserved.
The vena gastroomentalis runs parallel to the corresponding artery along the greater curvature of the stomach.
Inflammation near the vena gastroomentalis can lead to complications in abdominal procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arteria gastroomentalis - artery accompanying the vein along the stomach's greater curvature

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'gastroomentalis' (from 'gaster', stomach, and 'omentalis', related to the omentum).
