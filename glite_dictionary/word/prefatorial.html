prefatorial

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriːfəˈtɔːriəl / (AmE), / ˌpriːfəˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
serving as an introduction or preface; relating to a preface
serving as a preface or introduction

Examples

The author included a prefatorial statement explaining the purpose of the book.
Her prefatorial remarks set the stage for the main presentation.
The essay begins with a prefatorial overview of the historical context.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prefatory - serving as a preface or introduction

Etymology

From Latin 'praefatio' (preface) + English suffix '-al'.
