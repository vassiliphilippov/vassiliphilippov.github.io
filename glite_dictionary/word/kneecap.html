kneecap

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈniːˌkap / (BrE)
the small, flat, triangular bone at the front of the knee joint, protecting the knee
bone at front of the knee

Examples

She slipped on the wet floor and injured her kneecap.
The doctor explained that a fractured kneecap can make walking very painful.
Wearing knee pads can help protect your kneecaps during sports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patella - the anatomical name for the kneecap

2. VERB

/ ˈniˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈniːˌkæp / (BrE)
to shoot or injure someone in the knee, especially as a form of punishment or warning, often associated with paramilitary or terrorist groups
to shoot or injure in the knee as punishment

Examples

The rival gang threatened to kneecap anyone who spoke to the police.
Several activists were kneecapped by paramilitary groups as a warning to others.
The leader ordered his men to kneecap the informant for betraying their secrets.

Synset Examples

They kneecapped the industrialist

Etymology

From the noun 'kneecap' (the patella bone), used figuratively as a verb since the late 20th century.
