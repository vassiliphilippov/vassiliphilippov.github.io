foul-mouthed

1. ADJ

/ faʊl ˌmaʊðd / (AmE), / faʊl ˌmaʊðt / (BrE)
using obscene, vulgar, or offensive language frequently.
using offensive or obscene language

Examples

Her foul-mouthed comments shocked everyone at the dinner party.
The comedian is known for his foul-mouthed humor on stage.
Despite his foul-mouthed reputation, he is actually very kind-hearted.

Synset Examples

noisy foul-mouthed women all shouting at once

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profane - using language that shows disrespect for something sacred
  • Antonym: polite - showing good manners and respectful language

Etymology

Formed from 'foul' (offensive, impure) and 'mouthed' (relating to one's manner of speaking).
