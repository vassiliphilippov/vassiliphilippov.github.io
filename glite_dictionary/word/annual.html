annual

1. NOUN

/ ˈænjuəl / (AmE), / ˈanjʊəl / (BrE)
A book or publication issued once each year, often containing information, reports, or records for that year.
yearly publication or book

Examples

The school publishes an annual that highlights students' achievements throughout the year.
I bought the company's annual to review their financial progress.
Children often looked forward to receiving their favorite comic annual each Christmas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almanac - an annual calendar containing important dates and statistical information

Etymology

From Latin annuus, from annus meaning 'year'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈænjuəl / (AmE), / ˈanjʊəl / (BrE)
A plant that completes its entire life cycle, from germination to seed production, within one year and then dies.
plant that lives for one year

Examples

Many gardeners plant annuals like marigolds and petunias for vibrant summer color.
Because annuals complete their life cycle in one season, they need to be replanted each year.
She prefers annuals because they bloom more profusely than most perennials.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perennial - a plant that lives for more than two years
  • Antonym: biennial - a plant that completes its life cycle in two years

Etymology

From Latin 'annualis', from 'annus' (year).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11573257-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈænjuəl / (AmE), / ˈanjʊəl / (BrE)
(of a plant) living for only one year or season, completing its entire life cycle from germination to death within that time.
plant living for one year or season

Examples

Many gardeners prefer annual plants for their vibrant, long-lasting blooms throughout the summer.
The marigold is a classic example of an annual that must be replanted each year.
After the first frost, most annual flowers will die off completely.

Synset Examples

a border of annual flowering plants

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perennial - (of a plant) living for more than two years
  • Antonym: biennial - (of a plant) living for two years

Etymology

From Latin annuus (yearly), from annus (year).
