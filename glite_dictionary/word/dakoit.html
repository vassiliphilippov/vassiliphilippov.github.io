dakoit

1. NOUN

/ dəˈkɔɪt / (AmE), / dəˈkɔɪt / (BrE)
a member of a band of armed robbers in India or South Asia
member of a band of armed robbers (India)

Examples

The police finally captured the notorious dakoit who had terrorized the region for months.
Stories of dakoits hiding in the forests have been told in the village for generations.
He was mistaken for a dakoit because of his rugged appearance and travel through dangerous areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandit - an outlaw who robs people, especially as part of a gang
  • Synonym: robber - a person who takes property unlawfully by force or threat of force

Etymology

From Hindi ḍakait, derived from Sanskrit ḍakṣa (cunning, clever).
