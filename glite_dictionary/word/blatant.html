blatant

1. ADJ

/ ˈbleɪtnt / (AmE), / ˈbleɪtənt / (BrE)
(of an action or behavior) very obvious and often done without shame; offensively loud or conspicuous.
very obvious or offensively loud

Examples

The politician's blatant lies were quickly exposed by journalists.
He played his music at a blatant volume late into the night, annoying his neighbors.
Their blatant disregard for the rules shocked everyone at the meeting.

Synset Examples

blatant radios
a clamorous uproar
strident demands
a vociferous mob

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clamorous - making a loud, confused noise
  • Synonym: strident - loud and harsh; grating
  • Antonym: subtle - not obvious, not easily noticeable

Etymology

Perhaps from Latin 'blatire' (to babble, to prattle), though origin uncertain.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbleɪtnt / (AmE), / ˈbleɪtənt / (BrE)
(of bad behavior) done openly and unashamedly; completely obvious and lacking in subtlety or concealment, especially in a disapproving sense.
completely obvious; done openly and shamelessly

Examples

His blatant disregard for the rules shocked everyone in the meeting.
The advertisement was a blatant attempt to manipulate consumers.
She told a blatant lie without even trying to hide it.

Synset Examples

blatant disregard of the law
a blatant appeal to vanity
a blazing indiscretion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear, self-evident
  • Antonym: covert - not openly acknowledged or displayed; hidden

Etymology

Probably from Latin *blatire* (to babble), possibly influenced by English *blatant* (noisy, clamorous).

Source: WordNet
