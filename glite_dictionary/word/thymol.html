thymol

1. NOUN

/ ˈθaɪˌmɑl / (AmE), / ˈθaɪmɒl / (BrE)
a colorless crystalline compound with a pleasant aromatic odor, used as an antiseptic, fungicide, and in perfumes and embalming
aromatic antiseptic compound
medical

Examples

Thymol is often added to mouthwashes because of its strong antiseptic properties.
Researchers used thymol to preserve the biological specimens in the lab.
Certain perfumes include a trace amount of thymol for its fresh scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenol - an aromatic organic compound, basis for thymol

Etymology

From Latin 'thymum' (thyme) + '-ol' (chemical suffix for alcohols); originally isolated from thyme oil.
