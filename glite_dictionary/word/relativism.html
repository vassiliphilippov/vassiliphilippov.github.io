relativism

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛl.ə.tɪˌvɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈrɛl.ə.tɪ.vɪ.zəm / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The doctrine that knowledge, truth, or morality exists in relation to culture, society, or historical context, and is not absolute.
doctrine that truth or morality is not absolute

Examples

Many argue that relativism undermines the possibility of universal human rights.
Her approach to ethics is rooted in cultural relativism.
Debates about relativism continue to shape modern philosophy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absolutism - philosophical belief that truth or morality is absolute and universal

Etymology

From relative + -ism, first used in the 19th century.
