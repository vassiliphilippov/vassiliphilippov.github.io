to nationalise

1. VERB

/ ˈnæʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to bring a private industry or company under government ownership or control
bring under state ownership

Examples

The government decided to nationalise the railway system after years of private mismanagement.
Several industries were nationalised in the 20th century to protect public interests.
Rising unemployment led the new administration to nationalise key energy companies.

Synset Examples

Mitterand nationalized the banks

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to privatise - to transfer from public to private ownership
  • Synonym: to expropriate - to take property from its owner for public use

Etymology

From French 'nationaliser', from 'nation' + '-aliser' (equivalent of English '-ise').

2. VERB

/ ˈnæʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make national in character, scope, or importance; to give a national focus or significance to something
make national in character or scope

Examples

The politician tried to nationalise the festival, turning it into a celebration of cultural unity.
Writers often nationalise folk tales by emphasizing their country's values and traditions.
The government aimed to nationalise the campaign to increase public awareness across the country.

Synset Examples

His heroic deeds were nationalized by the press

Etymology

From nation + -alise, ultimately from Latin 'natio' (nation) and French '-iser' (to make).
