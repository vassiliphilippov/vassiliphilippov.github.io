Telemann

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɛləˌmɑn / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌman / (BrE)
Georg Philipp Telemann (1681–1767), a prominent German composer of the Baroque period, known for his prolific output and influence on classical music.
German Baroque composer (1681–1767)

Examples

Many music scholars consider Telemann to be one of the most versatile composers of the Baroque period.
The orchestra will perform several works by Telemann at their upcoming concert.
Unlike Bach and Handel, Telemann was highly celebrated during his own lifetime.

Etymology

From the German surname Telemann, referring to Georg Philipp Telemann.
