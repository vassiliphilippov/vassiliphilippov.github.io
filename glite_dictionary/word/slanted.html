slanted

1. ADJ

/ ˈslæntəd / (AmE), / ˈslɑːntɪd / (BrE)
presented in a way that shows an unfair preference for one person, side, or group over others; biased or partial.
showing unfair preference; biased

Examples

The newspaper article was clearly slanted in favor of the city's mayor.
Viewers complained that the documentary was slanted and did not show both sides of the issue.
Her summary was slanted to make her team look better than the competition.

Synset Examples

a biased account of the trial
a decision that was partial to the defendant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biased - showing an unreasonable preference or prejudice
  • Synonym: partial - favoring one side over another
  • Antonym: impartial - showing no preference or bias; fair

Etymology

Past participle of 'slant,' meaning to slope or deviate from the horizontal, later extended to mean 'deviating from impartiality.'

2. ADJ

/ ˈslæntɪd / (AmE), / ˈslɑːntɪd / (BrE)
at an angle; not straight or level, but sloping or oblique
at an angle; not straight or level

Examples

The sunlight filtered through the slanted blinds, casting stripes across the floor.
He admired the slanted roof of the old cottage, covered in moss and ivy.
The picture on the wall was hanging at a slanted angle, so I straightened it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sloped - at an incline or slope, not level
  • Antonym: straight - not curved or at an angle; without a bend

Etymology

From the verb 'slant,' likely from Old Norse slanta (to slant or slope).
