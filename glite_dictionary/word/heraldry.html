heraldry

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛr.əl.dri / (AmE), / ˈhɛr.əl.dri / (BrE)
the study and design of coats of arms and armorial bearings, including the tracing of genealogies
the study of coats of arms and armorial bearings

Examples

She has a deep interest in heraldry and can identify many family crests.
Many medieval manuscripts contain elaborate examples of heraldry.
The museum's new exhibit explores the history and symbolism of heraldry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genealogy - the study of family ancestries and histories

Etymology

From Old French *heraud* (herald) + -ry, referring to the duties of heralds.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛr.əl.dɹi / (AmE), / ˈhɛr.əld.ri / (BrE)
the system by which coats of arms and other armorial bearings are devised, described, and regulated; also, the emblems or designs indicating the right to bear such arms
science of coats of arms; emblems of armory

Examples

The ancient art of heraldry played a crucial role in medieval society and identification.
He studied the complex symbols and colors used in heraldry to distinguish noble families.
The museum displayed a fascinating collection of heraldry from across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coat of arms - a unique heraldic design on a shield or escutcheon or on a surcoat or tabard
  • Synonym: blazon - a formal description of armorial bearings
  • Synonym: insignia - a badge or distinguishing mark of military rank, office, or membership

Etymology

From Old French herald, from Germanic roots relating to a messenger or envoy.
