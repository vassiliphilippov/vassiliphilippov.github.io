Burns

1. PROPN

/ bɝːnz / (AmE), / bɜːnz / (BrE)
Robert Burns (1759–1796), a celebrated Scottish poet known for his poems and songs in Scots and English, and considered the national poet of Scotland.
Scottish national poet (1759–1796)
british

Examples

Many people recite Burns' poems on Burns Night in Scotland.
The works of Burns have inspired generations of poets worldwide.
A statue of Burns stands prominently in the center of the city.
