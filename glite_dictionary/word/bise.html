bise

1. NOUN

/ biːz / (AmE), / biːz / (BrE)
A cold, dry north wind that blows in southeastern France and neighboring regions.
cold north wind in southeastern France

Examples

The bise swept across the Rhône Valley, bringing a sharp chill to the morning air.
Vineyards in the region are accustomed to the fierce bise that blows in winter.
Locals bundled up to protect themselves from the biting cold of the bise.

Etymology

From French 'bise' (north wind), possibly of Germanic origin.
