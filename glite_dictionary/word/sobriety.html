sobriety

1. NOUN

/ səˈbraɪəti / (AmE), / səˈbrʌɪəti / (BrE)
the state of being sober, especially by not consuming alcoholic drinks; the condition of not being intoxicated by alcohol
the state of not being intoxicated by alcohol

Examples

Achieving sobriety was a turning point in his life.
After years of addiction, she finally celebrated one year of sobriety.
The program supports individuals on their journey to sobriety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soberness - the state of being sober; not intoxicated
  • Antonym: intoxication - the state of being intoxicated, especially by alcohol

Etymology

From Latin sobrietas, from sobrius (sober).

2. NOUN

/ soʊˈbraɪ.ə.ti / (AmE), / səʊˈbraɪ.ə.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being serious, solemn, and sensible in manner
seriousness or solemnity of manner

Examples

The judge's sobriety during the sentencing was evident to everyone in the courtroom.
Her speech was delivered with a sobriety that reflected the gravity of the occasion.
Despite the festive atmosphere, there was a noticeable sobriety to his demeanor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious in manner or attitude
  • Antonym: levity - lightness of manner or lack of seriousness

Etymology

From Latin 'sobrietas', from 'sobrius' (not drunk, moderate).

3. NOUN

/ soʊˈbraɪəti / (AmE), / səˈbrʌɪəti / (BrE)
The state of being sober, especially avoiding excess in the use of alcohol or other intoxicants.
abstinence from excess, especially alcohol

Examples

After years of addiction, he embraced sobriety and began a new chapter in his life.
Her sobriety was evident at the party, as she stuck to water all evening.
Maintaining sobriety requires determination and support from friends and family.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intoxication - the state of being intoxicated, especially by alcohol

Etymology

From Old French 'sobrieté', from Latin 'sobrietas' (temperance, moderation).

4. NOUN

/ soʊˈbraɪəti / (AmE), / səˈbraɪəti / (BrE)
the state of not drinking alcohol or taking drugs; moderation in or abstinence from intoxicating substances
the state of not drinking alcohol or using drugs

Examples

After years of struggling with addiction, he finally achieved sobriety and started a new life.
The organization promotes sobriety among young people through education and support services.
She celebrated five years of sobriety by running a marathon for charity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstinence - the practice of refraining from something, typically alcohol
  • Antonym: intoxication - the state of being affected by alcohol or drugs

Etymology

From Latin sobrietas, from sobrius (not drunk).
