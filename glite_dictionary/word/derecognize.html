to derecognize

1. VERB

/ ˌdiːˈrɛkəɡˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈrɛkəɡˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to withdraw official approval or acceptance of someone or something, especially a government, organization, or status
withdraw official approval or acceptance

Examples

The university decided to derecognize the student organization after repeated violations of the code of conduct.
Several countries threatened to derecognize the regime if it continued its human rights abuses.
After policy changes, the committee officially derecognized the union as a bargaining representative.

Synset Examples

Carter derecognized Taiwan in 1979 after the U.S. recognized the People's Republic of China

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to recognize - to acknowledge the existence or legality of something

Etymology

Formed from de- (removal) + recognize.
