conspiratorial

1. ADJ

/ kənˌspɪr.iˈtɔr.i.əl / (AmE), / kənˌspɪr.iˈtɔː.ri.əl / (BrE)
Suggesting or characteristic of a secret plan by a group to do something unlawful or harmful.
suggestive of conspiracy

Examples

His conspiratorial tone made everyone at the table uneasy.
They exchanged conspiratorial glances before slipping out of the room.
She lowered her voice to a conspiratorial whisper so no one else would hear.

Synset Examples

a conspiratorial whisper
the discovery of possible conspirative codes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conspirative - relating to or characteristic of conspiracy

Etymology

From 'conspiracy', ultimately from Latin 'conspirare' (to plot, conspire) + '-al' (forming adjectives).
