Calocedrus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæloʊˈsiːdrəs / (AmE), / ˌkaləˈsiːdrəs / (BrE)
A genus of tall evergreen trees native to western North America and eastern Asia, known for their aromatic wood; sometimes called incense-cedars.
genus of evergreen trees (incense-cedars)

Examples

The Calocedrus trees in the forest emitted a pleasant, spicy fragrance.
Botanists studied Calocedrus specimens collected from both Asia and North America.
The durable wood of Calocedrus is often used for making pencils and building materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Libocedrus - a related genus of evergreen trees, formerly included Calocedrus species

Etymology

From Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) + 'kedros' (cedar).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæloʊˈsiːdrəs / (AmE), / ˌkæləsˈiːdrəs / (BrE)
A genus of tall evergreen trees native to western North America and eastern Asia, formerly classified in the genus Libocedrus.
a genus of evergreen trees

Examples

The Calocedrus genus includes the well-known incense cedar.
Several species within Calocedrus are prized for their aromatic wood.
Botanists debated whether to classify these trees under Calocedrus or Libocedrus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Libocedrus - a related genus of evergreen coniferous trees

Etymology

From Greek *kalos* (beautiful) + *kedros* (cedar).
