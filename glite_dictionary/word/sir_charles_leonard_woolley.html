Sir Charles Leonard Woolley

1. PROPN

/ sɝ ˈtʃɑrlz ˈlɛnərd ˈwʊli / (AmE), / sə ˈtʃɑːlz ˈlɛnəd ˈwʊli / (BrE)
(1880–1960) An English archaeologist renowned for supervising the excavations at the ancient Mesopotamian city of Ur.
English archaeologist, excavator of Ur

Examples

Sir Charles Leonard Woolley made significant contributions to the field of archaeology through his work in Mesopotamia.
The excavation of Ur, led by Sir Charles Leonard Woolley, revealed many important artifacts.
Many historians credit Sir Charles Leonard Woolley with advancing our understanding of ancient Sumerian civilization.
