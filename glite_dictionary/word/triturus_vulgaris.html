Triturus vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ trɪˈtʃuːrəs vʌlˈɡɛrɪs / (AmE), / trɪˈtjuːrəs vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A small semiaquatic salamander species, commonly known as the smooth newt, native to Europe and parts of Asia.
species of smooth newt (salamander)

Examples

The Triturus vulgaris is often found in ponds and wetlands across Europe.
During the breeding season, the male Triturus vulgaris develops a strikingly colorful crest.
Researchers studied the population dynamics of Triturus vulgaris in restored habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smooth newt - common name for Triturus vulgaris

Etymology

From Latin: 'Triturus' (a genus of newts) and 'vulgaris' (common, ordinary).
