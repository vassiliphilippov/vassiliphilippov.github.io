Terence Rattigan

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɛrəns ˈrætɪɡən / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.əns ˈræt.ɪ.ɡən / (BrE)
(1911–1977) British playwright known for his sophisticated dramas and comedies, including 'The Winslow Boy' and 'The Browning Version'.
British playwright (1911–1977)

Examples

Terence Rattigan was celebrated for his skillful portrayal of middle-class English life on stage.
The theater staged a revival of Terence Rattigan's classic play 'The Deep Blue Sea'.
Many critics regard Terence Rattigan as one of the leading dramatists of the twentieth century.
