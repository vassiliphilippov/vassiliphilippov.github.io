lobbyism

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑː.biˌɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈlɒb.i.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
the organized practice of attempting to influence decisions made by officials, especially legislators, on behalf of a special interest group
practice of influencing legislation or officials

Examples

Concerns have been raised about the impact of lobbyism on democratic decision-making.
The government's new regulations aim to increase transparency around lobbyism.
He became interested in politics after studying lobbyism at university.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobbying - the act of influencing legislators or public officials

Etymology

From English 'lobby' (a group seeking to influence politicians) + '-ism'.
