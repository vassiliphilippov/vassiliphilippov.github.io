one-sided

1. ADJ

/ wʌn ˈsaɪdɪd / (AmE), / wʌn ˈsaɪdɪd / (BrE)
(of an object or material) not reversible or capable of having either side exposed; intended to be used with only one particular side facing out.
not reversible; has only one usable side

Examples

The invitation was printed on one-sided glossy paper, with a blank reverse.
These jackets are one-sided, so you can't turn them inside out to wear them differently.
Our new banners are one-sided, making them lighter and more cost-effective for indoor events.
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈwʌnˌsaɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈwʌnˌsaɪdɪd / (BrE)
involving or affecting only one side, person, or group; not mutual or balanced
involving only one side or part

Examples

The competition was one-sided, with one team dominating from start to finish.
She was frustrated by his one-sided argument, which ignored all other perspectives.
Their friendship felt one-sided because she was always the one making an effort.

Synset Examples

unilateral paralysis
a unilateral decision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unilateral - involving or affecting only one side
  • Antonym: mutual - experienced or done by each of two or more parties toward the other(s)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00238581-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ wʌn ˈsaɪdɪd / (AmE), / wʌn ˈsaɪdɪd / (BrE)
unfairly supporting one person, group, or opinion over others; not considering all sides equally
favoring one side over another

Examples

The newspaper published a one-sided report that ignored important facts from the other perspective.
His argument was one-sided and didn't give the opposition a fair chance to be heard.
The referee's one-sided decisions frustrated the losing team.

Synset Examples

a biased account of the trial
a decision that was partial to the defendant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biased - showing unfair preference for or against someone or something
  • Antonym: impartial - treating all sides equally; fair and just
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
