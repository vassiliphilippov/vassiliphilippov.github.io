Capsicum annuum conoides

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpsɪkəm əˈnuːəm ˌkɒnɔɪˈdiːz / (AmE), / ˈkæpsɪkəm əˈnjuːəm ˌkɒnɔɪˈdiːz / (BrE)
A variety of chili pepper plant with upright, pungent, conical fruits in red, yellow, or purple; sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
variety of chili pepper plant with upright conical fruits

Examples

The Capsicum annuum conoides is often prized for the vibrant colors of its fruits, which make it a popular ornamental plant.
Chefs use Capsicum annuum conoides in their recipes to add a pungent kick to sauces and salsas.
Gardeners sometimes choose to grow Capsicum annuum conoides indoors because of its decorative and edible peppers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili pepper - the fruit of plants from the genus Capsicum, commonly used as a spice
  • Synonym: Capsicum annuum - species of the pepper plant including many cultivated varieties

Etymology

From Latin: 'Capsicum' (pepper genus), 'annuum' (annual), and modern Latin 'conoides' (conical-shaped), from Greek 'kōne' (cone).
