Ethiopian

1. ADJ

/ ˌiːθiˈoʊpiən / (AmE), / ˌiːθiˈəʊpiən / (BrE)
of or relating to Ethiopia, its people, or its languages.
relating to Ethiopia or its people

Examples

Many Ethiopian restaurants serve injera, a traditional flatbread.
She studies Ethiopian history at university.
The Ethiopian community in the city celebrates its national holidays with music and dance.

Synset Examples

Ethiopian immigrants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ethiopia - a country in East Africa

Etymology

From Latin 'Aethiopēs', from Greek 'Aithiops', meaning 'of burnt face', originally referring to inhabitants of Africa south of Egypt.

Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ iˌθioʊˈpiən / (AmE), / iˌθiːəˈpiːən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Ethiopia
person from Ethiopia

Examples

The Ethiopian greeted visitors with a warm smile.
As an Ethiopian, he is proud of his nation's long history.
A group of Ethiopian students participated in the exchange program.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ethiopia - country in the Horn of Africa

Etymology

From Latin Aethiops, from Greek Aithiops (meaning 'of burnt face'), historically referring to African peoples.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09724520-n
Synset POS: n
