Myacidae

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈæsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / maɪˈæsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of soft-shell clams found in marine environments, characterized by their thin shells and burrowing habits.
family of soft-shell clams

Examples

The North Atlantic is home to several species within the Myacidae family.
Researchers studied the burrowing behavior of Myacidae to understand sediment movement.
Economic shellfish harvesting often targets members of the Myacidae group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soft-shell clam - a marine bivalve mollusc with a thin, brittle shell (genus Mya)

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Mya + -idae (denoting zoological family).
