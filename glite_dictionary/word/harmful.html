harmful

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrmfəl / (AmE), / ˈhɑːmfʊl / (BrE)
Causing or likely to cause damage, injury, or negative effects.
causing damage or injury

Examples

Excessive noise can be harmful to your hearing over time.
The chemicals in this cleaning product are potentially harmful if inhaled.
Doctors warn that a poor diet may have harmful consequences for your health.

Synset Examples

too much sun is harmful to the skin
harmful effects of smoking

Related Terms

  • Antonym: innocuous - not harmful or offensive
  • Synonym: dangerous - likely to cause harm or injury

Etymology

From English 'harm' + suffix '-ful'.
