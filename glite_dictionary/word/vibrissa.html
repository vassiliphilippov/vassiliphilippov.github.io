vibrissa

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈbrɪsə / (AmE), / vɪˈbrɪsə / (BrE)
A long, stiff, sensitive hair growing from the snout or brow of certain mammals, such as cats, commonly functioning as a tactile organ.
whisker-like sensory hair of mammals
medical

Examples

A cat relies on each vibrissa to navigate in the dark by sensing even the slightest changes in air movement.
Scientists studied the seal’s vibrissae to understand how they detect underwater vibrations.
The veterinarian examined the dog’s injured vibrissa to ensure it would grow back properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whisker - one of the long stiff hairs growing near the mouth of some animals, used for touch

Etymology

From Latin 'vibrissa' (nostril hair), from 'vibrare' meaning to vibrate.
