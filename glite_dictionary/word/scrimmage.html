scrimmage

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrɪmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈskrɪmɪdʒ / (BrE)
(in American football) a practice session in which members of the same team play against each other to simulate a real game.
football practice game between team squads
american

Examples

The coach organized a scrimmage to prepare the team for Saturday's game.
During practice, the players took part in a heated scrimmage.
Many rookies get their first taste of real competition in a scrimmage against their teammates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skirmish - a minor battle or conflict; the word from which 'scrimmage' derives

Etymology

Alteration of 'scrimmage', a variant of 'skirmish', from Middle English 'skyrmisshe'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskrɪmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈskrɪmɪdʒ / (BrE)
A noisy, confused fight or scuffle, especially involving a group of people.
noisy group fight or scuffle

Examples

A scrimmage broke out in front of the store when the sale began.
Several people were injured during the scrimmage outside the stadium.
The heated debate quickly turned into a physical scrimmage between the two groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skirmish - a brief, minor fight or encounter

Etymology

Altered from 'skirmish', via Middle English and Old French *eskirmir* (to fence, fight).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00555237-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈskrɪmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈskrɪmɪdʒ / (BrE)
to engage in a practice game or informal match, especially in sports such as football or rugby
engage in a practice game (sport)

Examples

The team will scrimmage against local high school players tomorrow afternoon.
Before the season starts, they scrimmaged to test out new strategies.
We scrimmage twice a week to improve our teamwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practice - to do an activity repeatedly or regularly as a way of improving one's skill

Etymology

From earlier noun use, from alteration of 'scrimmage,' variant of 'skirmish.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01727456-v
Synset POS: v
