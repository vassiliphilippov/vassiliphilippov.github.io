pyrotechnic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpaɪrəˈtɛknɪk / (AmE), / ˌpʌɪrəˈtɛknɪk / (BrE)
(of display, skill, or style) dazzling, brilliant, or spectacular, like a display of fireworks.
dazzling or showy, like fireworks

Examples

The pianist stunned the audience with his pyrotechnic performance.
Her pyrotechnic storytelling captured everyone's attention throughout the evening.
The comedian is famous for his pyrotechnic wit and quick comebacks.

Synset Examples

pyrotechnic keyboard virtuosity
a pyrotechnic wit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brilliant - very bright or impressive in appearance or skill
  • Antonym: unremarkable - not particularly interesting or impressive

Etymology

From French pyrotechnique, from Greek pur (fire) + tekhnikos (of art or skill).

3. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪ.ɹəˈtɛk.nɪk / (AmE), / ˌpaɪ.rəˈtɛk.nɪk / (BrE)
A device, usually one of several, that contains an explosive or chemical composition and produces bright colored flames or effects when ignited; often used for signaling or illumination.
device producing colored flames or effects

Examples

The military used pyrotechnics to signal their position to friendly aircraft.
A dazzling display of pyrotechnics lit up the night sky during the festival.
Specialized pyrotechnic devices are often used for emergency signaling at sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firework - an object containing gunpowder and other chemicals that explodes in the air to produce bright colors and loud noises

Etymology

From French pyrotechnique, from Greek pyr (fire) + technē (art).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03353300-n
Synset POS: n
