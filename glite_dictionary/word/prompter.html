prompter

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːmp.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈprɒmp.tə / (BrE)
a person who helps a performer remember their lines by supplying forgotten words or cues during a performance
theater assistant who cues performers

Examples

When the actor forgot his line, the prompter quietly whispered the words to him.
Many traditional theaters still employ a prompter for live performances.
The prompter sits in the wings, ready to assist if a performer stumbles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cue - a signal for a performer to begin or continue speaking or performing

Etymology

From Latin 'promptus' (brought forth, ready), via French 'prompteur'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːmp.tər / (AmE), / ˈprɒmp.tə / (BrE)
a device that displays words for people to read, especially during a performance or broadcast
device displaying words for reading aloud

Examples

The news anchor relied on the prompter to deliver her lines smoothly.
During the play, the actors occasionally glanced at the prompter when they forgot their lines.
Television presenters often use a prompter to help them read lengthy scripts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teleprompter - an electronic device that displays a script for a speaker
  • Synonym: autocue - a British term for a teleprompter
Source: WordNet
