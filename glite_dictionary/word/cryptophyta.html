Cryptophyta

1. NOUN

/ krɪpˈtɑfiˌtə / (AmE), / krɪpˈtɒfɪtə / (BrE)
a phylum of mostly unicellular, flagellated algae found in freshwater and marine environments, also known as cryptomonads
phylum of unicellular flagellated algae

Examples

Cryptophyta play an important role in aquatic food webs as primary producers.
Marine biologists often study Cryptophyta to understand nutrient cycles in oceans.
Recent research has identified new species within the Cryptophyta phylum.

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'phyton' (plant).
