ironically

1. ADV

/ aɪˈrɑːnɪkli / (AmE), / aɪˈrɒnɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that shows irony, expressing meaning by saying the opposite of what is actually meant or by highlighting an unexpected or incongruous situation.
in an ironic manner

Examples

She smiled ironically after hearing the compliment she knew was insincere.
Ironically, the firefighter’s house was destroyed by fire.
He ironically praised the team for their terrible performance.

Synset Examples

she began to mimic him ironically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcastically - in a sarcastic manner; using sarcasm

Etymology

From 'ironic' + '-ally' (adverbial suffix).

2. ADV

/ aɪˈrɑː.nɪ.kli / (AmE), / aɪˈrɒ.nɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a way that is contrary to what is expected or intended, often producing an amusing or pointed result.
contrary to expectation or intention

Examples

Ironically, the fire station burned down last night.
He worked hard to avoid mistakes but, ironically, ended up making more than ever.
Ironically, she forgot the umbrella she bought just for rainy days like this.

Synset Examples

ironically, he ended up losing money under his own plan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unexpectedly - in a way that was not expected
  • Antonym: predictably - in a way that happens as expected

Etymology

From 'ironic' + '-ally'. 'Ironic' from French 'ironique', from Latin 'ironicus', from Greek 'eirōnikos' (dissembling).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00384453-r
Synset POS: r
