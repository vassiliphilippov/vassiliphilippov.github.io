deadening

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛdənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɛd(ə)nɪŋ / (BrE)
making something so dull, repetitive, or uninteresting as to cause mental weariness or boredom.
causing mental fatigue or boredom

Examples

The deadening routine of his job made him long for a change.
She tried to escape the deadening boredom of the small town.
That meeting had a truly deadening effect on the whole team.

Synset Examples

a boring evening with uninteresting people
the deadening effect of some routine tasks
a dull play
his competent but dull performance
a ho-hum speaker who couldn't capture their attention
what an irksome task the writing of long letters is
tedious days on the train
the tiresome chirping of a cricket
other people's dreams are dreadfully wearisome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boring - not interesting; dull
  • Synonym: tedious - too long, slow, or dull; tiresome or monotonous
  • Antonym: stimulating - encouraging interest or enthusiasm

Etymology

From the verb 'deaden' + suffix '-ing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛd.ənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɛd.ənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making something less effective, interesting, or lively, often by making it routine or monotonous
the act of making something dull or ineffective

Examples

The repetitive nature of the task contributed to the deadening of his enthusiasm.
Many complained about the deadening effect of bureaucracy on creativity.
Routine paperwork can have a deadening influence on innovative thinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dulling - the act of making something dull or less sharp/vivid
  • Antonym: enlivening - the act of making something more lively or interesting

Etymology

From the verb 'deaden' + -ing suffix.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
