proteinase

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊtiˌneɪs / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtiːˌneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that breaks down proteins into smaller peptides and amino acids by the process of proteolysis
enzyme that breaks down proteins
medical

Examples

The proteinase in the digestive fluid helps to break down dietary proteins.
Researchers are studying a specific proteinase involved in the disease process.
A deficiency in a certain proteinase can lead to metabolic disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protease - enzyme that breaks down proteins by proteolysis

Etymology

Formed from 'protein' + '-ase', the enzyme suffix used in scientific terminology.
