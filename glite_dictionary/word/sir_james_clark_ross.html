Sir James Clark Ross

1. PROPN

/ sɝ d͡ʒeɪmz klɑrk rɑs / (AmE), / sə d͡ʒeɪmz klɑːk rɒs / (BrE)
(1800–1862) A British explorer of the Arctic and Antarctic regions who located the North Magnetic Pole in 1831 and discovered the Ross Sea in Antarctica; nephew of Sir John Ross.
British Arctic and Antarctic explorer

Examples

Sir James Clark Ross is renowned for his discovery of the North Magnetic Pole in 1831.
The Ross Sea in Antarctica was named after Sir James Clark Ross following his expedition.
Few explorers have matched the achievements of Sir James Clark Ross in both the Arctic and Antarctic.
