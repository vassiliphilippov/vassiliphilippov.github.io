pressmark

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛsˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈprɛsˌmɑːk / (BrE)
a mark, often consisting of numbers or letters, written or affixed on a book to indicate its shelf location in a library or collection
library code marking for shelf location

Examples

The librarian carefully wrote the new pressmark inside the front cover of the book.
Books with the same pressmark are usually stored together on the same shelf.
He used the pressmark to quickly find the volume he needed in the archive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: call number - a number or code on books indicating their location in a library

Etymology

From 'press' (meaning bookcase or cupboard) + 'mark' (sign), historically referring to the mark indicating a book's location in a press.
