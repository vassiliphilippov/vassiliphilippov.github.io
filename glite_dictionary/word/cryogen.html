cryogen

1. NOUN

/ ˈkraɪ.oʊˌdʒɛn / (AmE), / ˈkraɪ.əʊ.dʒən / (BrE)
a substance, especially a liquid, that boils at below -160°C and is used for refrigeration at extremely low temperatures
liquid used for ultra-low-temperature refrigeration

Examples

Liquid nitrogen is a common cryogen used in laboratories for preserving biological samples.
The company specializes in the storage of cryogen for use in space exploration technologies.
When handling a cryogen like liquid helium, safety precautions must be strictly followed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refrigerant - a substance used to provide cooling in a refrigeration system

Etymology

From Greek 'kryos' (frost, cold) + 'gen' (producing).
