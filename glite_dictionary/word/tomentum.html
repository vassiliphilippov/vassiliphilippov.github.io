tomentum

1. NOUN

/ toʊˈmɛntəm / (AmE), / təˈmɛntəm / (BrE)
A dense layer of short, matted, woolly hairs covering the surface of a plant, often giving it a fuzzy or felted appearance.
dense plant hair covering
medical

Examples

The leaves of the plant are covered with a thick tomentum that gives them a silvery sheen.
A peach's skin exhibits a fine tomentum, often referred to as peach fuzz.
Many alpine plants develop a tomentum to protect themselves from cold and dehydration.

Synset Examples

peach fuzz

Etymology

From Latin *tomentum* (cushion stuffing, flock, down), from *tondere* (to shear).
