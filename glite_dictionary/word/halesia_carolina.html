Halesia carolina

1. NOUN

/ həˈliːziə kəˈroʊlɪnə / (AmE), / həˈliːziə kəˈrɒlɪnə / (BrE)
A medium-sized deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States, known for its bell-shaped white flowers.
species of flowering tree native to southeastern U.S.

Examples

The Halesia carolina produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in spring.
Gardeners in the southeastern United States often plant Halesia carolina for its ornamental value.
Because of its graceful branches and blooms, Halesia carolina is admired in many arboretums.

Etymology

Named after English botanist Stephen Hales and the region of Carolina in the U.S.
