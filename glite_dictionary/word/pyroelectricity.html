pyroelectricity

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪroʊˌɛlɛkˈtrɪsɪti / (AmE), / ˌpaɪərəʊˌɪlɛkˈtrɪsɪti / (BrE)
the property of certain crystals to generate an electric charge in response to a change in temperature
generation of electric charge due to temperature change in crystals

Examples

Tourmaline crystals exhibit pyroelectricity when subjected to rapid heating or cooling.
The phenomenon of pyroelectricity is useful in designing thermal sensors and infrared detectors.
Researchers study pyroelectricity to develop new materials for energy harvesting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piezoelectricity - generation of electric charge in some materials by mechanical stress

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire), 'ēlektron' (amber, electricity), and the suffix '-icity'.
