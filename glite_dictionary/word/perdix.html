Perdix

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrdɪks / (AmE), / ˈpɜːdɪks / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Phasianidae, commonly known as partridges, found mainly in Eurasia.
genus of partridges (birds)

Examples

The grey partridge belongs to the genus Perdix and is common across Europe and Asia.
Ornithologists studied the habitat preferences of Perdix species in various agricultural regions.
The decline of Perdix populations has raised concerns among conservationists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partridge - a game bird in the family Phasianidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Perdix', from Ancient Greek 'Πέρδιξ' (Pérdix), meaning 'partridge'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɝː.dɪks / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.dɪks / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the subfamily Perdicinae, comprising the true partridges.
genus of birds (partridges)

Examples

The grey partridge belongs to the genus Perdix, which is native to Eurasia.
Recent studies have examined the migration habits of birds in the genus Perdix.
Hunters in Europe once favored birds of the Perdix genus for their meat and feathers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partridge - a kind of medium-sized game bird

Etymology

From Latin Perdix, from Ancient Greek perdix (πέρδιξ) meaning 'partridge'.
