paragneiss

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈnaɪs / (AmE), / ˌparəˈnaɪs / (BrE)
A type of gneiss that is formed by the metamorphism of sedimentary rock.
gneiss derived from sedimentary rock

Examples

The geologist identified the rock as paragneiss based on its composition and structure.
Layers of paragneiss can often be found alongside other metamorphic rocks in mountain regions.
Thin sections of paragneiss reveal the minerals typical of metamorphosed sedimentary origins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gneiss - a metamorphic rock with banded layers

Etymology

From Greek 'para' (beside, alongside) + 'gneiss', referring to its origin alongside or from sedimentary rock.
