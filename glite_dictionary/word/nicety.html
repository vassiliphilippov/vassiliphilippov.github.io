nicety

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪsəti / (AmE), / ˈnaɪsəti / (BrE)
a subtle or fine distinction in meaning, opinion, or attitude; a small but important detail or point
fine distinction or subtle point

Examples

She explained the legal nicety that made the difference between two similar cases.
Understanding the niceties of etiquette can be challenging when visiting a new country.
The discussion involved the niceties of philosophical theory rather than practical concerns.

Synset Examples

without understanding the finer nuances you can't enjoy the humor
don't argue about shades of meaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuance - a subtle difference in meaning or opinion
  • Synonym: subtlety - the quality of being delicate or precise
  • Antonym: broadness - lack of precision or detail; generality

Etymology

From Old French *niceté* (foolishness, delicacy), from Latin *nescius* (ignorant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪsəti / (AmE), / ˈnaɪsɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being in strict conformity with an aesthetic or social standard; careful attention to correctness or propriety.
attention to correctness or propriety

Examples

The violinist played the passage with remarkable nicety, impressing the entire audience.
When editing his essay, he focused on every nicety of style and grammar.
She appreciated the nicety with which the ceremony adhered to tradition.

Synset Examples

it was performed with justness and beauty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precision - the quality of being exact and accurate
  • Synonym: accuracy - the quality of being correct or true

Etymology

From Old French 'niceté', from 'nice' (foolish, simple), later meaning 'refinement'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
