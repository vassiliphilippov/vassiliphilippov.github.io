to beatify

1. VERB

/ biˈædəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / biˈætɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to declare that (a dead person) is blessed, as the first step towards canonization as a saint in the Roman Catholic Church
declare blessed in step toward sainthood

Examples

On Sunday, the Church will beatify the martyr in a special ceremony.
The pope may beatify someone after evidence of a miracle is confirmed.
Sister Lucia was beatified decades after her death.

Synset Examples

On Sunday, the martyr will be beatified by the Vatican

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: to canonize - to officially declare a person to be a saint

Etymology

From Latin 'beatus' (blessed) + '-fy' (to make).

Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ biˈæt̬.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / biˈæt.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make someone extremely happy or blissful
make extremely happy

Examples

His success at the competition seemed to beatify him for days afterward.
The news of her recovery beatified the anxious family.
Her presence alone could beatify even the gloomiest room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bless - to make someone or something holy or happy
  • Synonym: to exalt - to elevate someone in rank, power, or character; to glorify

Etymology

From Medieval Latin beatificare (to make happy or blessed), from Latin beatus (happy, blessed) + facere (to make).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
