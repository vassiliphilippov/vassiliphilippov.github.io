pennant

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛnənt / (BrE)
a long, narrow flag that tapers to a point, often used for signaling or as a symbol of achievement
narrow, tapering flag

Examples

The winning team waved their pennant as they circled the field.
Each ship in the fleet displayed a colorful pennant from its mast.
A triangular pennant hung from the wall, showing the school's name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flag - a piece of cloth used as a symbol, signal, or decoration

Etymology

From Latin 'penna' (feather, wing), via Old French 'pennon'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛnənt / (BrE)
A long, narrow flag that tapers to a point, often used for signaling or as a decorative emblem.
tapering flag or banner

Examples

Ships often display a pennant to indicate their nationality or status.
The winning team proudly waved their championship pennant during the parade.
A colorful pennant fluttered from the top of the tent, catching everyone's attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banner - a long strip of cloth bearing a slogan or design, used as a flag or standard
  • Synonym: flag - a piece of cloth with a distinctive design used as a symbol or signal

Etymology

From Latin *penna* (feather, wing), via Old French *penon* (a streamer attached to a spear or lance).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03917985-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛnənt / (BrE)
an award in the form of a flag or banner, given to the champion of a sports league or competition, especially in baseball
award flag given to a league champion

Examples

The team celebrated after clinching the pennant for the third consecutive year.
Winning the pennant was a dream come true for the players and their fans.
After a tough season, the club finally secured its first pennant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trophy - a cup or other decorative object awarded as a prize for a victory or success

Etymology

From Latin *pannus* (cloth, rag) via Middle English and Old French.
