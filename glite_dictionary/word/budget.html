budget

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʌdʒɪt / (BrE)
to plan how much money you will spend on something over a particular period of time
plan future spending

Examples

We need to budget carefully if we want to save for the trip.
She budgeted $50 a week for groceries.
When I started living alone, I learned how to budget my expenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - to decide on and arrange in advance

Etymology

From Old French 'bougette' (small leather bag), diminutive of 'bouge' (leather bag).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00725991-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌdʒət / (AmE), / ˈbʌdʒɪt / (BrE)
an estimate of income and expenditure for a set period of time, typically with proposals for how to meet expenses
plan of income and expenses

Examples

The finance committee approved the budget for the coming year after much debate.
Every household should have a budget to manage their expenses efficiently.
The school's budget was reduced due to funding cuts.

Synset Examples

the president submitted the annual budget to Congress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allowance - an amount of money set aside regularly for a purpose
  • Synonym: spending plan - a plan for how money will be spent

Etymology

From Old French 'bougette', diminutive of 'bouge' meaning 'leather bag', later used metaphorically for a financial plan.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13442635-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbʌdʒ.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʌdʒ.ɪt / (BrE)
an amount of money set aside for a specific purpose or period, often as part of a plan for spending and saving
a set amount of money for a purpose

Examples

Our research department operates on a tight budget to limit expenses.
The festival was canceled because they could not secure the necessary budget.
You need to prepare a monthly budget for your household expenses.

Synset Examples

the laboratory runs on a budget of a million a year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allowance - a sum of money given regularly for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: fund - a sum of money saved or made available for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: to overspend - to spend more money than is available in a budget

Etymology

From Old French 'bougette' (small bag or purse), diminutive of 'bouge' (leather bag), from Latin 'bulga'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13443005-n
Synset POS: n
