yard

1. NOUN

/ jɑrd / (AmE), / jɑːd / (BrE)
An area of land where logs are collected and stored, typically before processing or transportation.
area where logs are stored

Examples

The logs were piled high in the yard waiting to be transported to the mill.
After weeks of cutting, the workers brought the timber to the yard.
A security guard patrols the yard every night to prevent theft of valuable logs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timber yard - a place where timber is stored, often for sale or processing
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ jɑrd / (AmE), / jɑːd / (BrE)
an enclosed area of land, often next to a building, used for keeping animals such as chickens or livestock
enclosure for animals

Examples

The chickens roamed freely in the yard behind the farmhouse.
The farmer built a sturdy yard for his cattle to keep them safe at night.
After the storm, the animals were gathered into the yard until the fences could be repaired.

Etymology

From Old English geard (enclosure, garden, yard), related to Old High German gart (enclosure) and Old Norse garðr.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ jɑrd / (AmE), / jɑːd / (BrE)
a unit of length equal to three feet or 36 inches, standardized as 91.44 centimeters
unit of length (3 feet; 91.44 cm)

Examples

The football field is one hundred yards long.
She measured the fabric and bought two yards for her project.
He stood about ten yards away from the goal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meter - the SI base unit of length (approximately 1.094 yards)
  • Degree Lower: foot - a unit of length equal to 12 inches or one third of a yard

Etymology

From Old English 'geard' (rod, stick, measure), distinct from the word 'yard' meaning garden or enclosure.

7. NOUN

/ jɑrd / (AmE), / jɑːd / (BrE)
A unit of volume used especially for sand, gravel, and similar materials, equal to one cubic yard (0.7646 cubic meters).
unit of volume (cubic yard)

Examples

They ordered five yards of gravel for the driveway construction.
The truck can carry up to ten yards of sand at a time.
He calculated the cost based on the number of yards needed for the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cubic yard - a unit of volume equal to the volume of a cube one yard on each side

From Old English 'geard' (enclosure, measuring place); this sense extended from the measurement of length.
