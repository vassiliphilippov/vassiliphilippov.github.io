tub-thumper

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌbˌθʌmpər / (AmE), / ˈtʌbˌθʌmpə / (BrE)
a person who speaks loudly and passionately in public, often promoting a cause in a forceful or ranting manner
noisy, passionate public speaker

Examples

The crowd was drawn to the tub-thumper on the street corner, shouting about government reform.
Although he was a brilliant tub-thumper, his speeches often lacked substance.
Some dismissed her as just a tub-thumper, but others found inspiration in her passionate delivery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demagogue - a political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires rather than rational argument

Etymology

From 'tub-thump', referring to speakers pounding a tub as a platform in public meetings (19th century, British English).
