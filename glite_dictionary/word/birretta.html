birretta

1. NOUN

/ bəˈrɛtə / (AmE), / bɪˈrɛtə / (BrE)
A stiff square cap with three or four ridges or peaks on its crown, worn by Roman Catholic clergy.
square cap worn by Catholic clergy

Examples

The priest adjusted his birretta before stepping up to the altar.
Cardinals are often recognized by their distinctive red birretta.
He removed his birretta as a sign of respect during the ceremony.

Etymology

From Italian 'berretta', from Old Provençal 'berret' (cap), possibly from Late Latin 'birrus' (a hooded cloak).
