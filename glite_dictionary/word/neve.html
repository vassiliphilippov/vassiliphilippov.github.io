neve

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪveɪ / (AmE), / ˈneɪveɪ / (BrE)
the upper part of a glacier, where accumulated snow gradually compacts and turns into ice
upper glacier part where snow becomes ice

Examples

The climbers set up camp on the neve before making their final ascent.
The neve is critical for understanding glacier formation and movement.
Fresh snowfall covered the neve, making it difficult to distinguish from the surrounding ice.

Etymology

From French *névé*, from Swiss French, of uncertain origin.
