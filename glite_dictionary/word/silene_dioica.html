Silene dioica

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌliːni daɪˈoʊkə / (AmE), / sɪˌliːni daɪˈəʊkə / (BrE)
A biennial or perennial European plant of the pink family, with red or pink flowers, commonly known as red campion.
a European plant with red or pink flowers

Examples

Silene dioica is often seen brightening woodland edges and hedgerows in spring and summer.
Gardeners appreciate the vivid colors of Silene dioica when creating wildflower meadows.
Conservationists are working to protect native populations of Silene dioica across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red campion - common English name for Silene dioica

Etymology

From the genus name Silene and species epithet dioica, from Greek 'dioikos' meaning 'of two households', referring to separate male and female plants.
