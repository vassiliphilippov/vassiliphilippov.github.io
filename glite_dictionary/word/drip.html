drip

1. NOUN

/ drɪp / (AmE), / drɪp / (BrE)
(architecture) A projection from a cornice or sill designed to direct rainwater away from the building, protecting the area below (such as over a window or doorway).
protective projection on a cornice or sill

Examples

The old mansion featured a drip over every window to prevent water damage to the façade.
Over time, the drip on the stone sill eroded but continued to keep the wall below dry.
Architects paid careful attention to the design of the drip above the entryway.

3. NOUN

/ drɪp / (AmE), / drɪp / (BrE)
A small drop of liquid that falls or forms, especially as part of a series of drops.
a falling drop of liquid

Examples

The constant drip from the leaky faucet kept her awake all night.
I placed a bucket under the drip in the ceiling to catch the water.
There was a slow drip of rainwater coming through the window frame.

Synset Examples

there's a drip through the roof

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drop - a small round-shaped amount of liquid that forms on a surface

Etymology

From Middle English drippen, related to Old English dryppan.

5. VERB

/ drɪp / (AmE), / drɪp / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to fall in small drops, or to let fall drops of liquid.
to fall or let fall in drops

Examples

Rainwater began to drip from the edge of the old roof.
The paint continued to drip onto the floor, forming a colorful puddle.
After washing his hands, water would drip from his fingers as he reached for a towel.

Synset Examples

Water is dripping from the faucet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trickle - to flow in a small stream or in drops
  • Antonym: gush - to flow out suddenly or forcefully

Etymology

From Middle English 'drippen', probably of imitative origin.
