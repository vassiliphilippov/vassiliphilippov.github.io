wastage

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪstɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈweɪstɪdʒ / (BrE)
The process or amount of losing, destroying, or using up something needlessly.
process or amount of wasting

Examples

The factory reduced wastage by streamlining its production process.
There is a significant wastage of food in restaurants each day.
Hospitals are searching for new ways to minimize medical supply wastage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waste - to use or expend carelessly, extravagantly, or to no purpose

Etymology

Formed from "waste" + "-age." The suffix "-age" denotes an action, process, or result.

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪstɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈweɪstɪdʒ / (BrE)
items or material lost, wasted, or rendered unusable through use, wear, or inefficiency.
material lost or wasted

Examples

The factory is working on ways to reduce wastage during production.
Significant wastage of food occurs throughout the supply chain.
Regular equipment maintenance helps minimize wastage due to wear and tear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waste - use or expenditure of resources carelessly, extravagantly, or to no purpose

Etymology

From 'waste' + '-age', Middle English formation indicating result or process.
