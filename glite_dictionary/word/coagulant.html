coagulant

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈæɡjələnt / (AmE), / kəʊˈæɡjʊlənt / (BrE)
a substance or agent that causes liquid, especially blood, to become thick or solid by forming clots
substance that causes coagulation

Examples

The doctor administered a coagulant to stop the patient's bleeding.
Milk processors often add a coagulant to make cheese.
Some water purification systems use a coagulant to remove impurities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anticoagulant - a substance that prevents blood from clotting

Etymology

From Latin 'coagulans', present participle of 'coagulare' (to curdle).
