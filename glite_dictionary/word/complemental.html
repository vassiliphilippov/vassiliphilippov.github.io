complemental

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑmpləˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒmplɪˈmɛntəl / (BrE)
acting to complete or make something whole; serving as a complement
serving as a complement

Examples

The two studies are complemental, each providing crucial information that the other lacks.
A complemental nutrient can make a diet more balanced and effective.
Her skills proved complemental to those of her team members, ensuring their project's success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complementary - serving to enhance or complete; forming a complement

Etymology

From Latin complementum (that which fills up or completes) + -al.
