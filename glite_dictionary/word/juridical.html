juridical

1. ADJ

/ dʒəˈrɪdɪkəl / (AmE), / dʒʊəˈrɪdɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the law, legal proceedings, or jurisprudence; judicial.
relating to law or legal matters

Examples

The contract was declared void for lack of juridical authority.
Only a juridical decision can resolve the dispute between the parties.
He specializes in the juridical aspects of international business.

Synset Examples

juridical days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legal - relating to the law; permitted by law
  • Synonym: judicial - concerning courts of law or judges

Etymology

From Latin juridicus (of right, legal), from jus (law, right).

2. ADJ

/ dʒəˈrɪdɪkəl / (AmE), / dʒʊəˈrɪdɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to the administration of justice, the law, or the function of a judge.
relating to law or legal justice
legal

Examples

The court's decision was based on strictly juridical considerations, not on political concerns.
A sound juridical system is essential for the protection of citizens' rights.
She published an article analyzing the juridical aspects of international treaties.

Synset Examples

judicial system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judicial - relating to courts or judges or the administration of justice

Etymology

From Latin juridicus (of right, of justice), from jus (law, right) + dicere (to say).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02763759-a
Synset POS: a
