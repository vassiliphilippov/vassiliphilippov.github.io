studio

1. NOUN

/ ˈstuːdioʊ / (AmE), / ˈstjuːdiəʊ / (BrE)
an apartment consisting of a single main room serving as living, sleeping, and kitchen area, with a separate bathroom
single-room apartment with kitchen and bathroom

Examples

He lives in a small studio near downtown and enjoys the simplicity of the space.
Finding an affordable studio in this city can be quite challenging.
Her first apartment was a cozy studio that she decorated with bright colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apartment - a set of rooms forming a separate residence within a building
  • Synonym: flat - a set of rooms for living in, located in a building with other such units (British)
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈstuːdiːoʊ / (AmE), / ˈstjuːdiəʊ / (BrE)
a room or building where movies, television shows, or radio programs are produced and recorded
place where media is produced or recorded

Examples

The new movie was filmed in a state-of-the-art studio in Los Angeles.
She works as a sound engineer in a radio studio.
Several television shows are produced at the downtown studio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: workshop - place where creative work is done, especially by an artist or craftsperson

Etymology

From Italian 'studio' (study, studio), from Latin 'studium' (zeal, study).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04351622-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈstuːdioʊ / (AmE), / ˈstjuːdiəʊ / (BrE)
a room or building where artists, dancers, musicians, or photographers practice or teach their art
workplace for artists or art instruction

Examples

She teaches ballet classes every afternoon in her studio.
The artist spent hours alone in his studio preparing his latest paintings.
Many photography students rent a studio to practice lighting techniques.

Synset Examples

she ran a dance studio
the music department provided studios for their students
you don't need a studio to make a passport photograph

Related Terms

  • Synonym: workshop - a room or building where practical work is done, especially by an artist or craftsman

Etymology

From Italian 'studio' (study, studio), from Latin 'studium' (zeal, pursuit).
