bawdiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔːdinəs / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being indecent or obscene, especially in speech or humor
indecency; obscene humor or behavior

Examples

The comedian's bawdiness drew laughter from some and shock from others in the audience.
Victorian literature often shied away from bawdiness in favor of more restrained expression.
Her stories are loved for their wit and occasional bawdiness, which adds to their charm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bawdy - indecent or obscene, especially in a humorous way
  • Synonym: lewdness - the quality of being sexually unchaste or obscene
  • Antonym: innocence - the state of being pure or free from indecency

Etymology

From Middle English 'bawdy', related to 'bawd' (a procurer or pander), plus the suffix '-iness'.
