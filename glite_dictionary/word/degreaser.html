degreaser

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈɡriːsər / (AmE), / dɪˈɡriːzə / (BrE)
a chemical substance used to remove grease or oil, especially from surfaces or machinery.
chemical for removing grease or oil

Examples

Before repainting the engine, wipe it down with a degreaser to ensure the surface is clean.
The mechanic sprayed degreaser on the tools to remove stubborn oil stains.
Household degreaser makes cleaning greasy kitchen appliances much easier.

Synset Examples

I'll need to buy a professional degreaser to clean this mess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solvent - a liquid that dissolves a substance to form a solution

Etymology

From de- (removal) + grease + -er (agent noun suffix).
