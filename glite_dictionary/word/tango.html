tango

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtæŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
A style of music in duple time, originally from Argentina, composed for or associated with the tango dance.
music for the tango dance

Examples

The orchestra played a passionate tango that filled the dance floor with energy.
He loves listening to traditional Argentine tango on vinyl records.
The film’s soundtrack features a haunting tango that captures the emotional tension of the scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tango - a ballroom dance of Latin-American origin
  • Antonym: waltz - a ballroom dance and its music in triple time

Etymology

From Spanish 'tango', originally referring to the dance and its music, of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtæŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
a ballroom dance of Latin-American origin characterized by marked rhythms and postures
Latin-American ballroom dance

Examples

They learned to dance the tango before their trip to Argentina.
The music began, and the couple glided across the floor in a passionate tango.
She wore a red dress for her tango performance at the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waltz - a classic ballroom dance in triple time
  • Synonym: salsa - a lively dance of Latin-American origin
  • Synonym: foxtrot - a smooth, progressive ballroom dance

Etymology

From Spanish "tango," possibly of African origin.

3. VERB

/ ˈtæŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtæŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
to dance the tango, a lively ballroom dance of Latin American origin
to dance the tango

Examples

They learned to tango before their wedding reception.
She loves to tango in elegant ballrooms around the world.
Last night, they tangoed until midnight at the club.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waltz - to dance the waltz; to perform a waltz

Etymology

From Spanish 'tango', a style of dance and music originating in Argentina.
