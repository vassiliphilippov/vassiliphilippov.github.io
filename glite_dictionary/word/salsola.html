Salsola

1. NOUN

/ sælˈsoʊlə / (AmE), / salˈsəʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of mostly Old World herbs or shrubs known as saltworts, often found in saline environments.
genus of saltwort plants

Examples

The desert landscape was dotted with Salsola plants thriving in the salty soil.
Researchers studied the adaptive features of various Salsola species to understand salt tolerance mechanisms.
Many species of Salsola are used as forage for livestock in arid regions.

Etymology

From Latin salsus (salty), referring to the saline habitats of these plants.

2. PROPN

/ sælˈsoʊlə / (AmE), / salˈsəʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of mostly Old World herbs or shrubs, commonly known as saltworts, some species of which are adapted to saline soils.
a genus of saltwort plants

Examples

Several species within the Salsola genus are used in traditional medicine for their purported benefits.
The salt marsh was dominated by Salsola plants thriving in the high salinity.
Biologists identified the shrub as part of the Salsola genus, known for its resilience in arid regions.

Etymology

From Latin *salsus* meaning 'salty', referring to the plant's preference for saline habitats.
