amber

1. ADJ

/ ˈæm.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈæm.bə / (BrE)
Of a medium to dark brownish yellow color, resembling that of amber resin.
medium to dark brownish yellow color

Examples

The cat's eyes glowed with an amber light in the darkness.
She wore a dress in a warm amber shade for the ceremony.
The evening sun filled the room with a soft, amber glow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden - having a color similar to gold

Etymology

From Old French ambre, from Medieval Latin ambra (amber, ambergris).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæm.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈæm.bə / (BrE)
A hard, yellowish to brownish translucent fossil resin used in jewelry and ornaments.
yellowish fossil resin for jewelry

Examples

The necklace was made of polished amber beads that glowed softly in the light.
Insects trapped in amber millions of years ago offer clues about ancient ecosystems.
Collectors prize pieces of amber that contain perfectly preserved prehistoric creatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resin - a sticky substance exuded by some trees and plants

Etymology

From Old French ambre, from Medieval Latin ambra.
