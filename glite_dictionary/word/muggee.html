muggee

1. NOUN

/ mʌɡˈi / (AmE), / mʌɡˈiː / (BrE)
a person who is the victim of a mugging
victim of a mugging
informal

Examples

The muggee was able to give the police a detailed description of the suspect.
In the aftermath of the attack, the muggee received support from the local community.
The court considered the trauma suffered by the muggee during sentencing.

Synset Examples

the law seems to give more protection to the mugger than to the muggee

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mugger - a person who attacks and robs someone in a public place

Etymology

Formed from 'mug' (as in 'to mug', meaning to attack and rob), plus the agentive suffix '-ee' (denoting the receiver of an action).
