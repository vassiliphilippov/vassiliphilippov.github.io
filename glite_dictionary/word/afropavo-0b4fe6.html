Afropavo

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfroʊˈpeɪvoʊ / (AmE), / ˌafrəʊˈpɑːvoʊ / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the pheasant family, represented by the Congo peafowl, native to Africa.
genus of birds including the Congo peafowl

Examples

The Afropavo is unique among peafowl as it is native to the Congo Basin.
Recent studies focus on the habitat preservation of the Afropavo.
Unlike its Asian relatives, the Afropavo is adapted to dense forests.

Etymology

From 'Afro-' (referring to Africa) + Latin 'pavo' (peafowl).
