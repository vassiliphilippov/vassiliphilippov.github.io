Sinn Fein

1. PROPN

/ sɪn ˈfeɪn / (AmE), / sɪn ˈfeɪn / (BrE)
an Irish republican political party founded in 1905, advocating for Irish independence and the unification of Ireland; historically associated with the political wing of the Irish Republican Army
Irish republican political party

Examples

The Sinn Fein leader gave a speech on Irish unity.
Many voters in Northern Ireland have historically supported Sinn Fein in elections.
The peace process involved negotiations with Sinn Fein representatives.

Etymology

From Irish 'Sinn Féin' meaning 'we ourselves'.
