chiffonier

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪˈfɑːniɚ / (AmE), / ʃɪˈfɒnieɪ / (BrE)
a tall, elegant chest of drawers, often used for storing clothes or linens
tall elegant chest of drawers

Examples

The antique chiffonier in the bedroom was beautifully carved and polished.
She kept her jewelry and scarves neatly arranged in the top drawers of the chiffonier.
The auctioneer pointed out the fine workmanship on the Victorian chiffonier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dresser - a chest of drawers, typically with a mirror
  • Synonym: bureau - a writing desk or a chest of drawers
  • Antonym: wardrobe - a tall piece of furniture for storing clothes, typically with doors and shelves

Etymology

From French 'chiffonnier', from 'chiffon' (rag) + '-ier' (agent noun suffix).
