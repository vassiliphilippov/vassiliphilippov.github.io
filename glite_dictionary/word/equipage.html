equipage

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkwəpɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɛkwɪpɪdʒ / (BrE)
A carriage or vehicle with wheels, especially one drawn by horses, often accompanied by attendants and used in historical contexts.
horse-drawn carriage or vehicle
archaic

Examples

The royal family arrived in a grand equipage pulled by four white horses.
In the 18th century, only the wealthy could afford an ornate equipage.
Crowds gathered to watch the procession of equipages through the cobblestone streets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carriage - a wheeled vehicle for people, usually horse-drawn

Etymology

From French 'équipage', from 'équiper' (to equip).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈkwɪpɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪpɪdʒ / (BrE)
the equipment and supplies that are used by a military force or expedition
military equipment and supplies

Examples

The army's equipage was transported in dozens of heavy wagons.
Much of the regiment's equipage was lost during the difficult crossing of the river.
His responsibilities included overseeing all military equipage before the march.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equipment - the necessary items for a particular purpose

Etymology

From French 'équipage', from 'équiper' (to equip).
