tranche

1. NOUN

/ trɑntʃ / (AmE), / trɑːnʃ / (BrE)
a portion or division of something, especially money or shares, often as part of a series of payments or allocations
a portion or installment, especially of money

Examples

The company released the first tranche of funds for the new project.
Each tranche of shares will be made available at different stages.
They received the final tranche of the grant after meeting all requirements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: installment - one of a series of payments made over time
  • Synonym: portion - a part of a whole, especially with respect to distribution

Etymology

From French 'tranche' meaning 'slice' or 'portion'.
