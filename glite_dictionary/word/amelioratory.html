amelioratory

1. ADJ

/ əˈmiːljərəˌtɔːri / (AmE), / əˌmiːlɪˈɒrətəri / (BrE)
intended to make something better or improve a situation
intended to improve or make better

Examples

The new policy included several amelioratory measures aimed at reducing poverty.
Researchers debated the effectiveness of amelioratory interventions in the school system.
Many support programs have an amelioratory effect on community well-being.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ameliorative - having the power to improve or make better
  • Antonym: worsening - making or becoming worse

Etymology

From Latin 'ameliorare' (to make better) + '-ory' (forming adjectives denoting purpose or tendency).
