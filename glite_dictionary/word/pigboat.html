pigboat

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡˌbəʊt / (BrE)
an early type of submarine, especially one used as a warship and usually armed with torpedoes
early submarine warship

Examples

The aging pigboat surfaced just before dawn, its crew exhausted after a long patrol.
In the early days of naval warfare, a pigboat was considered a daring and dangerous machine.
He wrote a memoir about serving aboard a pigboat during the First World War.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submarine - a submersible warship

Etymology

Originally US Navy slang from the early 20th century, referring to the cramped, unpleasant conditions aboard early submarines.
