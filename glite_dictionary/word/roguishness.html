roguishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊɡɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈrəʊɡɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of behaving in a mischievous or playfully dishonest way, often causing annoyance or discomfort to others
mischievous or playful dishonesty

Examples

Her roguishness made her both charming and frustrating to her teachers.
A sense of roguishness sparkled in his eyes as he planned his next prank.
Even as an adult, his roguishness often caused trouble at family gatherings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischief - playful misbehavior or troublemaking
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest or truthful

Etymology

From 'roguish' (mischievous, dishonest), from 'rogue', of uncertain origin, possibly related to Latin 'rogare' (to ask, beg).

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊɡɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈrəʊɡɪʃnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being mischievous or playfully dishonest, often in a charming or attractive way
mischievous or playful dishonesty

Examples

She looked at him with a roguishness in her eyes that made everyone laugh.
There was a certain roguishness to his grin that hinted at a hidden prank.
His roguishness often got him into trouble, but people found it hard to stay angry with him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischievousness - the quality of causing minor trouble in a playful way
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere

Etymology

From 'roguish' + '-ness'; 'rogue' from Old French 'rogue' (beggar, scoundrel).
