Pinus pungens

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.nəs ˈpʌn.dʒɛnz / (AmE), / ˈpʌɪ.nəs ˈpʌn.dʒɛnz / (BrE)
A small pine tree native to the eastern United States (Appalachians), characterized by two needle-like leaves per bundle, dark brown flaking bark, and cones with thorn-tipped scales.
a pine tree species native to the eastern U.S.

Examples

The Pinus pungens thrives on rocky slopes in the Appalachian Mountains.
Botanists studied the unusual thorn-tipped cones of the Pinus pungens specimen.
Fire has an important role in the regeneration of Pinus pungens forests.

Etymology

From Latin; Pinus meaning 'pine' and pungens meaning 'sharp','pointed', referring to its thorn-tipped cones.
