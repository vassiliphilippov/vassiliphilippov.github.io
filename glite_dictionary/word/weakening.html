weakening

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwiːkənɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of making something weaker or less strong
act of making something weaker

Examples

The constant rainfall resulted in a weakening of the bridge’s foundations.
Several factors contributed to the weakening of the country’s economy.
There has been a noticeable weakening in her voice since the illness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strengthening - the act of making something stronger

2. ADJ

/ ˈwikənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwiːkənɪŋ / (BrE)
causing someone or something to become less strong, healthy, or effective; leading to a loss of strength or vitality.
causing a loss of strength or vitality

Examples

The virus had a weakening effect on the patient's immune system.
After weeks without proper nutrition, he was in a weakening condition.
The harsh winter had a weakening impact on the herd's health.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strengthening - making something stronger or more powerful

3. NOUN

/ ˈwiːkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwiːkənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of becoming weaker or less strong
process of becoming weaker

Examples

There has been a noticeable weakening of the company's financial position this year.
The weakening of the bridge structure was caused by years of neglect.
Doctors are monitoring the weakening of her immune system due to the illness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strengthening - the process of becoming stronger
  • Synonym: deterioration - the process of becoming worse or declining in condition

Etymology

From the verb 'weaken' + '-ing'.
