Dominion

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmɪnjən / (AmE), / dəˈmɪnjən / (BrE)
(historical) One of the self-governing nations within the British Commonwealth, such as Canada or Australia, especially before becoming fully independent.
self-governing nation in the British Commonwealth (historical)
archaic british

Examples

Canada was once a Dominion within the British Empire, gaining increasing independence over time.
In the early 20th century, several countries were known as Dominions of the British Commonwealth.
The status of a Dominion implied a large degree of self-government, distinct from that of a colony.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: colony - country or area under the full or partial political control of another country

Etymology

From Latin dominionem (dominion, sovereignty), via Old French and Middle English.
