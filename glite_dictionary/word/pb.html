PB

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiː ˈbiː / (AmE), / ˌpiː ˈbiː / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,024 tebibytes or 2^50 bytes; a petabyte
unit of digital information (petabyte)

Examples

The new data center will have a storage capacity exceeding 10 PB by next year.
Researchers transferred more than 2 PB of genomic data for the study.
Cloud providers now offer customers the ability to store multiple PB of information securely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petabyte - a unit of information equal to 2^50 bytes

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'petabyte', from 'peta-' (denoting a factor of 10^15) and 'byte'.

2. NOUN

/ piː biː / (AmE), / piː biː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for petabyte, a unit of digital information equal to 1,000 terabytes or 10^15 bytes
abbreviation for petabyte (10^15 bytes)
informal

Examples

The new data center has a storage capacity of over 5 PB.
Cloud services nowadays can handle petabytes, or PB, of customer data.
Scientists generated almost 2 PB of astronomical data last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petabyte - unit of information equal to 10^15 bytes
  • Degree Lower: TB - abbreviation for terabyte, equal to 10^12 bytes
  • Degree Higher: EB - abbreviation for exabyte, equal to 10^18 bytes

Etymology

Abbreviation of petabyte, from Greek 'péta' (five) + byte.
