indifference

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɪfɚəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪfərəns / (BrE)
lack of interest, concern, or enthusiasm about something; the state of being indifferent.
lack of interest or concern

Examples

Her indifference to the suffering of others was alarming.
The manager’s indifference resulted in low staff morale and poor performance.
He tried to hide his excitement with a display of indifference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of feeling, emotion, or interest
  • Antonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval

Etymology

From Latin 'indifferentia', meaning 'lack of difference or distinction'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɪfrəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪfərəns / (BrE)
lack of interest, concern, or sympathy; absence of emotional reaction to something
lack of interest or concern

Examples

Despite the shocking news, she showed complete indifference to the situation.
His indifference towards his job was evident in his poorly done work.
Public indifference can allow injustice to persist unchecked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern
  • Antonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval

Etymology

From Latin 'indifferentia', from 'in-' (not) and 'differentia' (difference).

/ ɪnˈdɪfɚəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪfərəns / (BrE)
lack of interest, concern, or preference toward something; impartiality or unconcern.
lack of interest or concern

Examples

Her indifference to the outcome of the game surprised everyone who knew how competitive she usually is.
The company’s indifference toward customer complaints caused its reputation to suffer.
He tried to hide his excitement but could not maintain an air of indifference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of feeling or interest
  • Antonym: concern - interest or worry

Etymology

From Latin 'indifferentia' (impartiality, unconcern).

4. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɪf.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪf.ər.əns / (BrE)
the quality or state of not caring or showing a lack of concern about something
lack of interest or concern

Examples

His indifference to the suffering of others shocked his friends.
She tried to hide her excitement, but her face betrayed her apparent indifference.
Voters' indifference can have a significant impact on election results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern
  • Antonym: concern - worry or interest regarding something
  • Antonym: interest - the feeling of wanting to give attention or care to something

Etymology

From Latin 'indifferentia' (lack of difference, impartiality), from 'in-' (not) + 'differentia' (difference).
