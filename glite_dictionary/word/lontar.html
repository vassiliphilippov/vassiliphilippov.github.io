lontar

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊn.tɑr / (AmE), / ˈlɒn.tɑː / (BrE)
A tall tropical palm tree native to Africa and South and Southeast Asia, valued for its hard wood, sweet sap used for palm wine and sugar, and leaves used for thatching and weaving.
tall palm tree with useful wood, sap, and leaves

Examples

The villagers used the lontar leaves to weave traditional baskets and mats.
Sap from the lontar is often collected to make palm sugar or a fermented beverage.
A row of lontar trees lined the road, providing shade and materials for the local community.

Etymology

From Javanese, ultimately from Sanskrit 'latā' (creeper, vine).
