resinous

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛz.ɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛz.ɪ.nəs / (BrE)
having the characteristics of resin; sticky, viscous, and often aromatic like pitch or tar
having qualities of resin

Examples

The resinous scent of pine filled the forest after the rain.
The wood was covered with a resinous substance that made it feel sticky to the touch.
She noticed a resinous sheen on the antique violin, which protected the wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resin - a sticky, insoluble organic substance exuded by some trees and plants

Etymology

From Latin 'resina' (resin) + -ous (forming adjectives).
