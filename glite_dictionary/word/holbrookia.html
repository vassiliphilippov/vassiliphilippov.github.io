Holbrookia

1. NOUN

/ hoʊlˈbroʊkiə / (AmE), / hɒlˈbrəʊkiə / (BrE)
A genus of earless lizards native to North and Central America, distinguished by their lack of external ear openings.
genus of earless lizards

Examples

The Holbrookia lizard is well adapted to arid desert environments.
Biologists discovered several new species within the genus Holbrookia.
Unlike many lizards, members of Holbrookia lack external ear openings.

Etymology

Named after American zoologist John Edwards Holbrook (1794–1871).
