runcinate

1. ADJ

/ ˈrʌn.səˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈrʌnsɪneɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) having sharply incised margins with the lobes or teeth directed toward the base, as in dandelion leaves.
botanical term for a leaf with backward-pointing lobes
medical

Examples

The leaves of the dandelion are runcinate, with each lobe pointing toward the leaf base.
Many weedy plants have runcinate leaves that help them avoid grazing animals.
A botanist identified the species by its distinctively runcinate foliage.

Etymology

From Latin runcinatus, past participle of runcinare (to plane, to cut with a plane).
