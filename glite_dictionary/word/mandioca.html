mandioca

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæn.diˈoʊ.kə / (AmE), / ˌman.dɪˈəʊ.kə / (BrE)
Cassava, a plant with long, tuberous edible roots and soft brittle stems, used in cooking and to make products such as tapioca.
cassava plant with edible roots

Examples

The chef used mandioca flour to make traditional Brazilian bread.
Farmers in the region grow mandioca as a staple crop due to its resilience.
In some countries, mandioca roots are fermented to create local beverages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassava - tropical plant with edible starchy tuberous roots

Etymology

From Portuguese 'mandioca,' from Tupi 'mani' (name of a goddess) + 'oka' (house, plant).
