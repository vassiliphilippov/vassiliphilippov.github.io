Lech Walesa

1. PROPN

/ lɛk vəˈwɛnsə / (AmE), / lɛk vaˈwɛnsə / (BrE)
(born 1943) Polish labor leader and statesman, co-founder of Solidarity, and President of Poland from 1990 to 1995.
Polish labor leader and former president

Examples

Lech Walesa played a crucial role in the fall of communism in Poland.
Many people admire Lech Walesa for his leadership of the Solidarity movement.
As president, Lech Walesa helped guide Poland through its transition to democracy.
