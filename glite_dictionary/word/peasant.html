peasant

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛzənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛzənt / (BrE)
a person who works on the land, especially one who owns or rents a small piece of land in the country, usually with low social status and little money
rural agricultural laborer, country person

Examples

The medieval village was made up mostly of peasants who farmed the lord’s land.
After the revolution, many peasants received land to work for themselves.
She wrote a book about the daily lives of Russian peasants in the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmer - person who works the land or cultivates crops
  • Antonym: landowner - person who owns land, often employing peasants or tenants

Etymology

From Old French *païsant* (country-dweller), from *païs* (country), from Latin *pagus* (country district).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛzənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛznt / (BrE)
an uncouth or uncultured person; someone regarded as lacking refinement or manners (often used derogatorily)
an uncouth, unrefined person

Examples

He was called a peasant by his colleagues because of his rude behavior at dinner.
Don't be such a peasant; use your napkin properly.
Some critics dismissed the artist as a peasant who lacked sophistication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boor - a rude or unmannerly person
  • Synonym: lout - an uncouth or aggressive person
  • Antonym: gentleman - a courteous and refined man

Etymology

From Middle English *paisant* (country dweller), from Old French *païsant*, from *païs* (country), from Latin *pagus* (country district).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛzənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛzənt / (BrE)
a member of a traditional class of agricultural laborers or small farmers, especially in historical or pre-industrial Europe, usually of low social status and limited means
agricultural laborer of low status, esp. in Europe

Examples

During the Middle Ages, most of the rural population consisted of peasants who worked on their lord's land.
After the revolution, many peasants were granted small plots of land to farm for themselves.
The story follows the life of a young peasant struggling to survive in a harsh feudal society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serf - an agricultural laborer bound under the feudal system to work on his lord's estate
  • Antonym: landowner - a person who owns land, especially of estates in a rural context

Etymology

From Middle English 'paissaunt', from Old French 'païsant' (country dweller), from 'païs' (country), from Latin 'pagus' (country district).
