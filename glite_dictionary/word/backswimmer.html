backswimmer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌswɪmər / (AmE), / ˈbækˌswɪmə / (BrE)
A predatory aquatic insect of the family Notonectidae that swims on its back and can inflict a painful bite.
aquatic insect that swims on its back

Examples

A backswimmer moved swiftly across the surface of the pond, upside down.
The painful bite of a backswimmer can surprise swimmers who disturb it.
Backswimmers are fascinating to watch as they glide on their backs underwater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water boatman - an aquatic insect often confused with backswimmer but swims right side up

Etymology

From 'back' + 'swimmer', describing its habit of swimming upside down.
