Pholiota flammans

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈlaɪ.ə.tə ˈflæm.ənz / (AmE), / fəʊˈlɪ.ə.tə ˈflæm.ænz / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a bright yellow cap and stalk, both covered with fine scales.
a yellow-scaled mushroom species

Examples

We spotted several Pholiota flammans growing on the decaying logs in the forest.
The bright yellow scales on the cap helped us identify the fungus as Pholiota flammans.
Mycology students studied the unique features of Pholiota flammans during their field trip.

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Pholiota' from Greek 'pholis' (scale) and 'flammans' from Latin (flaming), referring to its bright color.
