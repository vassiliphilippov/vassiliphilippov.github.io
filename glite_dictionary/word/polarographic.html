polarographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpoʊlərəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌpəʊlərəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving polarography, a method of chemical analysis by measuring electric current.
relating to polarography
medical

Examples

The polarographic technique is widely used in clinical laboratories for the detection of metal ions.
Researchers measured the dissolved oxygen concentration with a polarographic sensor.
Advances in polarographic equipment have improved the accuracy of chemical analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polarography - electrochemical method for analyzing substances by measuring current as a function of voltage

Etymology

From 'polarography' + '-ic', polarography from Latin 'polaris' (of a pole) + Greek 'graphō' (to write).
