Veronica serpyllifolia

1. NOUN

/ vəˌrɑnɪkə sərˌpɪlɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / vəˌrɒnɪkə sɜːˌpɪlɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A perennial, low-growing herb with small, opposite leaves and racemes of blue flowers, found throughout Eurasia and the New World; known as thyme-leaved speedwell.
a perennial herb with blue flowers

Examples

Veronica serpyllifolia often carpets the damp meadows with its tiny blue blossoms in late spring.
Botanists surveyed the area and recorded a dense population of Veronica serpyllifolia along the riverbanks.
Gardeners sometimes appreciate Veronica serpyllifolia for its ground-covering ability and delicate flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyme-leaved speedwell - Common English name for Veronica serpyllifolia

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Veronica' from the genus name and 'serpyllifolia' meaning 'thyme-leaved' from Latin 'serpyllum' (wild thyme) and 'folium' (leaf).
