Viking

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪ.kɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.kɪŋ / (BrE)
a member of the Scandinavian people who raided and settled parts of Europe from the late 8th to early 11th centuries
Scandinavian raider from 8th–11th centuries

Examples

The Vikings were known for their longships and fearsome raids across Europe.
She is fascinated by the history of the Vikings and their exploration of the North Atlantic.
Archaeologists discovered a Viking settlement in Newfoundland.

Etymology

From Old Norse 'víkingr', meaning 'pirate' or 'sea-raider'.
