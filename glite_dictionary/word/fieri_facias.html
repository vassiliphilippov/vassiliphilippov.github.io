fieri facias

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪəri ˈfeɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌfɪəri ˈfæsiːəs / (BrE)
a writ ordering the seizure of a debtor's property to satisfy a judgment or debt
legal writ for seizure of debtor's property
legal

Examples

The court issued a fieri facias against the insolvent business to recover unpaid debts.
A fieri facias was served to seize the debtor's assets for auction.
Lawyers often apply for a fieri facias when other enforcement actions have failed.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'let it be made', an instruction to carry out a court order.
