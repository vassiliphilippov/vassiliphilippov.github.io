intermezzo

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈmɛtsoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɛtsəʊ / (BrE)
A short movement or piece played between the main sections of a longer musical work, such as a symphony or opera.
short musical movement between main sections

Examples

The composer included a beautiful intermezzo between the second and third movements of the symphony.
After the dramatic aria, the orchestra performed a gentle intermezzo to transition to the next act.
Each intermezzo in the suite brought a new mood to the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interlude - a short piece of music played between longer sections

Etymology

From Italian intermezzo (interval, interlude), from Latin intermedius (intermediate).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈmɛtsoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɛtsəʊ / (BrE)
A short piece of instrumental music performed between acts of a play or opera.
short musical piece between acts

Examples

The orchestra played a beautiful intermezzo while the stage crew prepared the next act.
Each act of the opera was separated by a lively intermezzo.
During the intermezzo, the audience remained seated, enjoying the music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entr'acte - a short performance or musical piece between acts

Etymology

From Italian 'intermezzo' (interval), from Latin 'intermedius' (lying between).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈmɛtsoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɛtsəʊ / (BrE)
A short musical or dramatic performance inserted between the acts of a longer composition, play, or event.
short performance between larger acts

Examples

The orchestra played an intermezzo while the stage was being changed for the next act.
A beautiful dance intermezzo added liveliness to the otherwise serious opera.
Between scenes, the audience enjoyed a short piano intermezzo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interlude - a short period or piece of entertainment between parts of a play or concert

Etymology

From Italian 'intermezzo' (interval, interlude), from Latin 'intermedius' (intermediate).
