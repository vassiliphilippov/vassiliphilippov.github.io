Rubus phoenicolasius

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəs ˌfiːnɪkoʊˈleɪsiəs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs ˌfiːnɪkəʊˈleɪsɪəs / (BrE)
A species of raspberry native to China and Japan, grown for its ornamental value and small, red, acidic fruits.
species of raspberry from China and Japan

Examples

The garden featured a thriving Rubus phoenicolasius along the fence, admired for its bright red fruit.
Botanists in the region are studying Rubus phoenicolasius for its potential as an ornamental shrub.
Some gardeners grow Rubus phoenicolasius both for its attractive flowers and its tart berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wineberry - common name for Rubus phoenicolasius

Etymology

From Latin: 'Rubus' (bramble) + 'phoenicolasius' from Greek 'phoinikos' (crimson) and 'lasios' (hairy), referring to the plant's reddish bristles.
