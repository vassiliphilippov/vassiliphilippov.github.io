bearable

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛr.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈbeə.rə.bəl / (BrE)
able to be endured or tolerated, even if unpleasant or difficult
able to be tolerated

Examples

The pain after the surgery was bearable with the help of medication.
The hot weather in August was just bearable thanks to the breeze.
His criticism was harsh but bearable because it was honest.

Synset Examples

sufferable punishment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unbearable - not able to be endured or tolerated
  • Synonym: tolerable - able to be tolerated; acceptable, though not desirable

Etymology

From 'bear' (to endure) + suffix '-able', from Latin '-abilis'.
