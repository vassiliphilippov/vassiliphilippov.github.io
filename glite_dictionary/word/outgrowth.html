outgrowth

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌɡroʊθ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtɡrəʊθ / (BrE)
A natural result or development that stems from something else.
natural result of development

Examples

The internet is an outgrowth of advances in computer technology.
Urban sprawl can be seen as an outgrowth of rapid population growth.
Her confidence was an outgrowth of years of hard work and persistence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition
  • Synonym: byproduct - something produced in addition to the main item
  • Antonym: cause - what brings something about

Etymology

Formed from 'out-' (outside) + 'growth', indicating something that has grown out from something else.

3. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌɡroʊθ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌɡrəʊθ / (BrE)
a natural projection or extension that develops from a part of an animal or plant, such as a bony process or shoot
a natural extension from a living organism

Examples

The cactus developed a new outgrowth on its main stem.
This bony outgrowth can be an indicator of arthritis in elderly patients.
The unusual outgrowth on the tree branch caught the botanist’s attention.

Related Terms
