deadness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdnəs / (AmE), / ˈdɛdnɪs / (BrE)
The state of having lost elasticity or responsiveness, especially in materials or objects.
lack of elasticity or responsiveness

Examples

The deadness of the old rubber band made it snap easily when stretched.
Players complained about the deadness of the court surface after the rain.
You could feel the deadness in the soccer ball after repeated use.

Synset Examples

he objected to the deadness of the tennis balls

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elasticity - the ability of an object or material to resume its normal shape after being stretched

Etymology

From the adjective 'dead' + noun suffix '-ness', indicating the state or quality of being dead or lifeless.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdɛd.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being dead or lifeless; lack of animation or vitality.
state of being lifeless or dead

Examples

The deadness of his eyes made her uneasy.
After years of neglect, the garden was filled with deadness and decay.
She couldn’t shake the feeling of deadness that hung over the deserted house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifelessness - the state of lacking life or vitality
  • Antonym: vitality - the state of being strong and full of life

Etymology

From Old English 'dead' + '-ness' (formative suffix denoting state or quality).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdnəs / (AmE), / ˈdɛdnəs / (BrE)
a lack of response or emotion in a person, or a lack of liveliness in something; unresponsiveness or dullness
lack of response or emotion

Examples

There was a strange deadness in his voice after hearing the bad news.
The deadness of the winter landscape gave the village a somber feeling.
After the traumatic event, she struggled with a feeling of emotional deadness.

Synset Examples

she began to recover from her numb unresponsiveness after the accident
in an instant all the deadness and withdrawal were wiped away

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liveliness - the quality of being lively, animated, or full of life
  • Synonym: numbness - the state of lacking sensation or feeling

Etymology

From 'dead' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the state or quality of being dead or lifeless.
