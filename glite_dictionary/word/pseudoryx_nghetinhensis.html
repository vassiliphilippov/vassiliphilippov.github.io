Pseudoryx nghetinhensis

1. NOUN

/ suːˈdoʊrɪks nɡɛtɪnˈhɛnsɪs / (AmE), / sjuːˈdəʊrɪks ŋɛtɪnhɛnˈsɪs / (BrE)
A rare, cow-like mammal native to the forests of Vietnam and Laos, known for its long horns and distinctive appearance; also called the saola.
rare mammal native to Vietnam and Laos

Examples

The discovery of the Pseudoryx nghetinhensis in the Annamite Range surprised the scientific community.
Researchers are working to protect the remaining Pseudoryx nghetinhensis population from habitat loss and poaching.
Very few people have seen a live Pseudoryx nghetinhensis in the wild due to its elusive nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saola - common name for Pseudoryx nghetinhensis, a rare Southeast Asian mammal

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudo-' (false), 'oryx' (a type of antelope), and Nghe Tinh, a region in Vietnam where it was discovered.
