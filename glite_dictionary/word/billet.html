billet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪlət / (AmE), / ˈbɪlɪt / (BrE)
a place where soldiers are lodged, especially in a private home or other non-military building
lodging for soldiers, often in private homes

Examples

During the war, families were required to provide a billet for soldiers stationed in their town.
The officer checked each billet to ensure the troops were comfortable.
After a long march, the soldiers were relieved to find their billet was a warm farmhouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarters - living accommodations, especially for military personnel

Etymology

From French *billet* (a short note), originally referring to a written order assigning lodging to soldiers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪlɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɪlɪt / (BrE)
a short written personal letter, especially one that is informal or secret
short personal letter

Examples

After reading her billet, he smiled and tucked it carefully into his pocket.
He sent a hurried billet to his friend before catching the train.
The lovers exchanged secret billets during the long winter evenings.

Synset Examples

drop me a line when you get there

Related Terms

  • Synonym: note - a brief written message or reminder
  • Degree Higher: epistle - a formal or lengthy letter

Etymology

From French 'billet' (note, ticket, letter), diminutive of 'bille' (document)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06638793-n

3. VERB

/ ˈbɪlɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɪlɪt / (BrE)
to assign lodging, especially for soldiers, typically in civilian houses
assign soldiers lodging

Examples

During the war, local families were asked to billet soldiers in their homes.
The officer decided to billet the entire regiment in nearby cottages for the winter.
Villagers objected to having troops billeted on them without any compensation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarter - to provide accommodation, especially for military personnel

Etymology

From French 'billet' (a short note or ticket, later an order for lodging); originally from Latin 'bulla' (a document, seal).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
