microcosmic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmaɪkrəˈkɑːzmɪk / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈkɒzmɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a microcosm; representing a miniature version of something much larger.
relating to a miniature representation

Examples

Sociologists often study the microcosmic dynamics of individual communities to understand broader social trends.
The novel captures the microcosmic struggles of a village as a reflection of larger societal issues.
His painting offers a microcosmic glimpse into urban life in the 21st century.

Synset Examples

the microcosmic world of business

Related Terms

  • Antonym: macrocosmic - relating to the macrocosm or the entire universe; opposite of microcosmic
  • Synonym: miniature - a much smaller copy or model of something

Etymology

From microcosm, itself from Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'kosmos' (world), plus the suffix -ic.
