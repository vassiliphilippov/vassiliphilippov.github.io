microscopic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmaɪ.krəˈskɑː.pɪk / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪ.krəˈskɒ.pɪk / (BrE)
So small as to be visible only with a microscope; relating to or using a microscope.
very small; seen with a microscope

Examples

The scientist discovered microscopic organisms in the water sample.
Dust particles are often microscopic and invisible to the naked eye.
Using a microscopic examination, they identified the bacteria causing the infection.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: macroscopic - large enough to be visible without a microscope
  • Synonym: microscopical - relating to or made with a microscope; synonym for 'microscopic'

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *microscopium* (microscope) + -ic.
