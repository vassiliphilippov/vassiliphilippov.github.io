retailing

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌteɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈriːteɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the business or activity of selling goods to the public, usually in shops or stores, for personal use, not for resale
selling goods directly to consumers

Examples

Careers in retailing require a good understanding of consumer behavior and market trends.
The company is expanding its retailing operations to include online sales channels.
Technological innovations have significantly changed the landscape of retailing in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wholesaling - the business of selling goods in large quantities to be retailed by others
  • Synonym: retail - to sell goods to the public in small quantities for use or consumption rather than for resale

Etymology

From 'retail' + -ing, where 'retail' comes from Old French 'retailler' (to cut off, pare, divide up), referring to the sale of small quantities.
