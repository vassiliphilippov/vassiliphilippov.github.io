Malpighiaceae

1. NOUN

/ mælˌpɪdʒiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / malˌpɪdʒɪˈiːsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of tropical shrubs or trees with distinctive paired glands and usually opposite leaves, including many species with ornamental or economic importance
a family of tropical shrubs or trees

Examples

The Malpighiaceae family includes several species prized for their bright yellow flowers.
Botanists have discovered new medicinal uses for plants within the Malpighiaceae family.
Many tropical gardens feature shrubs belonging to the Malpighiaceae due to their attractive appearance.

Etymology

From the genus name Malpighia, named after Italian physician Marcello Malpighi, combined with the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
