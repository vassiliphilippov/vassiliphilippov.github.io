select

1. ADJ

/ sɪˈlɛkt / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛkt / (BrE)
(of a group, event, etc.) chosen carefully for special quality or privilege; exclusive.
chosen for special quality or privilege

Examples

Only a select few were invited to attend the ceremony.
She belongs to a select group of scholars recognized for their achievements.
They dined at a select restaurant known for its exclusivity.

Synset Examples

the blue-ribbon event of the season

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusive - restricted to a particular person or group
  • Antonym: common - not special or exclusive; ordinary

Etymology

From Latin 'selectus', past participle of 'seligere' (to choose out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02350415-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ səˈlɛkt / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛkt / (BrE)
of superior quality; chosen as particularly fine or excellent among others
of superior quality; specially chosen

Examples

The chef used only select ingredients for the signature dish.
Our store carries a select range of luxury chocolates imported from Belgium.
These select wines are reserved for our most discerning customers.

Synset Examples

choice wines
prime beef
prize carnations
quality paper
select peaches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choice - of very good quality; selected as the best
  • Synonym: superior - higher in quality; excellent
  • Antonym: inferior - of lower quality; not as good

Etymology

From Latin 'selectus', past participle of 'seligere' (to select, choose out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02351005-s

3. VERB

/ səˈlɛkt / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛkt / (BrE)
to carefully choose someone or something from a group as being the best or most suitable
choose from a group

Examples

The committee will select the best candidate for the position next week.
Please select your preferred language from the list below.
She selected a blue dress for the party after trying on several options.

Synset Examples

Take any one of these cards
Choose a good husband for your daughter
She selected a pair of shoes from among the dozen the salesgirl had shown her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to choose - to pick someone or something among options
  • Antonym: reject - to refuse to accept or choose

Etymology

From Latin *selectus*, past participle of *seligere* (to choose out).
