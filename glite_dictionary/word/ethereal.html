ethereal

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈθɪriəl / (AmE), / ɪˈθɪəriəl / (BrE)
(of a substance) containing, relating to, or dissolved in ether, especially as used in chemistry or medicine.
containing or relating to ether
medical

Examples

In the experiment, an ethereal solution was used as a solvent for the reaction.
The laboratory stores various ethereal compounds in tightly sealed containers.
Because of its volatility, an ethereal mixture can evaporate quickly if left uncovered.

Synset Examples

ethereal solution

Etymology

From New Latin aetherialis, from Latin aether (ether).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02882364-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈθɪriəl / (AmE), / ɪˈθɪəriəl / (BrE)
Extremely delicate and light in a way that seems too perfect for this world; heavenly or spiritual.
heavenly; spiritual; delicate

Examples

The choir sang an ethereal hymn that seemed to float through the cathedral.
Her dress had an ethereal quality, shimmering like morning mist.
He gazed at the ethereal glow of the night sky, lost in wonder.

Synset Examples

celestial peace
ethereal melodies
the supernal happiness of a quiet death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celestial - pertaining to the sky or heavens

Etymology

From Latin 'aethereus' (of the upper air), from Greek 'aitherios'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01182889-s
Synset POS: s
