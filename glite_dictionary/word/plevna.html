Plevna

1. PROPN

/ ˈplɛvnə / (AmE), / ˈplɛvnə / (BrE)
A town in northern Bulgaria, known for the 1877 siege during the Russo-Turkish War when it was taken from the Turks by Russian forces.
town in Bulgaria, site of famous 1877 siege

Examples

The siege of Plevna was a turning point in the Russo-Turkish War.
Historians often visit Plevna to study the remains of its historic battlefield.
The fall of Plevna in 1877 had lasting effects on the balance of power in Eastern Europe.
