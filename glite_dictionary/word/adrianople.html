Adrianople

1. PROPN

/ ˌeɪdriəˈnoʊpəl / (AmE), / ˌeɪdrɪəˈnəʊpəl / (BrE)
A historic city in northwestern Turkey, formerly known as a Thracian town, rebuilt and renamed by the Roman Emperor Hadrian; now known as Edirne.
historic city in northwestern Turkey

Examples

The city of Adrianople played a significant role in the history of the Byzantine Empire.
After the Ottoman conquest, Adrianople became a major center of administration and culture.
Historians often refer to the Battle of Adrianople as a turning point in Roman history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Edirne - modern name for Adrianople, a city in northwestern Turkey

Etymology

From Latin Adrianopolis, after the Roman Emperor Hadrian.
