hydrazoite

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdræzoʊˌaɪt / (AmE), / hʌɪˈdræzəʊˌaɪt / (BrE)
a salt formed from hydrazoic acid by replacement of its hydrogen atom(s) with a metal or other positive ion
salt of hydrazoic acid

Examples

The laboratory synthesized a hydrazoite to study its reactivity with other salts.
Researchers must handle hydrazoite compounds with care due to their instability.
Each hydrazoite is derived from hydrazoic acid and typically contains a metal cation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrazoic acid - a highly explosive and poisonous acid with chemical formula HN3

Etymology

From 'hydrazoic' (referring to hydrazoic acid) + '-ite' (salt or derivative).
