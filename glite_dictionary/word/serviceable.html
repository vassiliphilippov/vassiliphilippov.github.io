serviceable

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɜːr.vɪ.sə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.vɪ.sə.bəl / (BrE)
able to be used for a practical purpose, though not necessarily attractive or perfect
functional or fit for use

Examples

The jacket was old but still serviceable for everyday chores.
We kept the serviceable chairs and donated the rest.
Although not stylish, her shoes were practical and serviceable.

Synset Examples

serviceable low-heeled shoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: functional - designed to be practical and useful
  • Antonym: ornamental - decorative rather than functional

Etymology

From Anglo-French 'servisable', from 'servir' (to serve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01841561-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɝː.vɪ.sə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.vɪ.sə.bəl / (BrE)
able to be used for a practical purpose, even if not perfect; fit for use.
fit to be used

Examples

The old car is still serviceable despite its age.
Although the coat was worn, it remained serviceable in cold weather.
This laptop isn’t fancy, but it’s perfectly serviceable for basic tasks.

Synset Examples

a serviceable kitchen gadget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: usable - able to be used
  • Antonym: unusable - not fit to be used

Etymology

From Old French 'serviceable', from 'servir' (to serve), from Latin 'servire'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02506333-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɜːrvɪsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪsəbəl / (BrE)
fit or ready for use; able to perform a function or withstand use over time
fit or ready for use; durable

Examples

The old truck is still serviceable despite its age.
She bought a serviceable winter coat that should last for years.
Most of the tools in the workshop are worn but serviceable.

Synset Examples

serviceable equipment
heavy serviceable fabrics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: usable - able to be used; fit for use
  • Antonym: unusable - not fit or ready for use

Etymology

From Old French 'servicable', from 'service' (help, use).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02131193-a
Synset POS: a
