furtively

1. ADV

/ ˈfɝːtɪvli / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that attempts to avoid notice or attention; secretly or stealthily.
in a secret or stealthy manner

Examples

He glanced furtively over his shoulder to make sure no one was watching.
She furtively slipped the note into his hand during the meeting.
The child furtively crept into the kitchen to grab a cookie before dinner.

Synset Examples

the soldiers were furtively crawling through the night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretly - without others knowing; in a secret way
  • Antonym: openly - in a way that is not secret; without concealment

Etymology

From 'furtive' + '-ly'; 'furtive' from Latin furtivus (stolen, secret), from furtum (theft).
