legatee

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛɡəˈti / (AmE), / ˌlɛɡəˈtiː / (BrE)
a person who receives money or property as stated in a will
person who inherits by will
legal

Examples

The legatee received a substantial inheritance upon the reading of the will.
As the primary legatee, she inherited the estate and all its assets.
Each legatee was notified in writing about their share of the property.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: testator - person who makes a will
  • Synonym: beneficiary - person who receives benefits from a will, insurance, or trust

Etymology

From Latin legatarius, from legatum (legacy).
