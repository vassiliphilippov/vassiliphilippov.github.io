roughness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌfnəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌfnəs / (BrE)
the quality of a surface or edge that is irregular, uneven, or not smooth
irregularity or unevenness of a surface

Examples

You can feel the roughness of the sandpaper when you run your hand over it.
The roughness of the road made the car vibrate.
Her hands showed the roughness from years of hard work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of being even and without roughness or bumps

Etymology

From Middle English 'roughnesse', from 'rough' + '-ness'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈrʌfnəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌfnəs / (BrE)

pitted surface texture from corrosion

Examples

The roughness of the metal increased as corrosion continued over the years.
Engineers measured the roughness of the pipe's inner surface to assess damage.
Corrosive chemicals can cause significant roughness on steel surfaces, affecting performance.

  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of having a flat, even surface
