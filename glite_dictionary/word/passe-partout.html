passe-partout

1. NOUN

/ pæs pɑːrˈtuː / (AmE), / pæs pɑːˈtuː / (BrE)
A border of gummed tape or cardboard used to mount or frame a picture or photograph.
mount for pictures using tape

Examples

The photograph was simply mounted with a passe-partout to keep costs down.
She carefully sealed the drawing inside a cardboard passe-partout before hanging it.
They sell frames and passe-partouts at the local art supply store.

Etymology

From French 'passe-partout', literally 'passes everywhere', referring to its versatility.

2. NOUN

/ pæs pɑːrˈtu / (AmE), / pɑːs pɑːˈtuː / (BrE)
a master key that can open a number of different locks; especially a key that opens every door in a building or establishment
master key for many locks

Examples

The caretaker carried a passe-partout that unlocked every office in the building.
After losing the original, the hotel manager used the passe-partout to enter the guests' rooms.
Only trusted employees are given access to the passe-partout for security reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: master key - a key designed to open several different locks

Etymology

From French passe-partout, literally 'passes everywhere'.
