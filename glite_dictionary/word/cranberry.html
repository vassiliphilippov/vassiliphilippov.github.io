cranberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrænˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈkrænˌbəri / (BrE)
a small, evergreen shrub of the genus Vaccinium that produces small, tart, red berries commonly used in cooking and juice-making
a shrub producing small, tart red berries

Examples

The cranberry thrives in acidic, boggy soil and is harvested in the fall.
Commercial growers often flood fields to collect cranberries during the harvest season.
You can plant a cranberry bush in your garden if you have the right conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - shrub of the genus Vaccinium bearing blue edible berries
  • Synonym: lingonberry - shrub of the genus Vaccinium bearing small red edible berries

Etymology

From Low German *kranebere*, literally 'crane berry,' named for the flower's resemblance to a crane's head.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrænˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈkrænˌbəri / (BrE)
a small, very tart red berry, commonly used to make sauces and juice
small, tart red berry used for food

Examples

She made a delicious sauce with fresh cranberry for the Thanksgiving dinner.
The juice from the cranberry is both tart and refreshing.
Dried cranberry is often added to salads for extra flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - a small, sweet blue-black berry
  • Degree Lower: currant - a small, tart berry, usually red or black

Etymology

From early modern Dutch 'kranebere' or German 'Kranbeere', literally 'crane berry'.
