Fagus pendula

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪɡəs pɛnˈdjuːlə / (AmE), / ˈfeɪɡəs pɛnˈdjuːlə / (BrE)
A cultivated variety of European beech tree (Fagus sylvatica) with pendulous, drooping branches.
variety of European beech with drooping branches

Examples

The graceful shape of the Fagus pendula makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
Many parks feature the Fagus pendula for its dramatic, sweeping branches.
The leaves of the Fagus pendula are similar to other beeches, but its weeping form makes it unique.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European beech - a species of beech native to Europe (Fagus sylvatica)

Etymology

From Latin: 'Fagus' meaning 'beech' and 'pendula' meaning 'hanging' or 'pendent'.
