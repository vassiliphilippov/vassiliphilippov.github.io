graphic

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
a picture or image produced by a computer, often used in digital media, websites, or design
computer-generated image or illustration

Examples

The website featured a colorful graphic on its homepage to attract visitors.
She created a detailed graphic to show the data trends in her presentation.
Many video games rely on high-quality graphics to enhance the player experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illustration - a picture illustrating a book, newspaper, etc.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03458640-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡrafɪk / (BrE)
describing something in vivid and explicit detail, especially involving sex or violence; giving a clear and often shocking depiction.
vividly explicit, often about sex or violence

Examples

The movie contains several graphic scenes of violence that may disturb viewers.
Her memoir provided a graphic account of her experiences during the war.
The documentary was criticized for its graphic depiction of real-life medical procedures.

Synset Examples

graphic sexual scenes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explicit - clearly and openly expressed, leaving no doubt about meaning, especially relating to sex or violence

Etymology

From Latin graphicus, from Greek graphikos (relating to drawing or writing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00944511-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to or presented by means of a graph or visual representation, especially in the context of data or statistics.
relating to graphs or visual presentations

Examples

The results were explained using a graphic chart that highlighted key trends.
Our report includes several graphic representations of sales growth over the past year.
A graphic display makes complex information easier to understand.

Synset Examples

a graphic presentation of the data

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graphical - relating to using or presented by a graph or graphics

Etymology

From Greek graphikos (pertaining to drawing or writing), from graphein (to write, draw).

4. ADJ

/ ˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡrafɪk / (BrE)
(of symbols, characters, etc.) written, drawn, or engraved, especially as opposed to spoken or conceptual forms.
written, drawn, or engraved (esp. of symbols)

Examples

The document uses several graphic signs to illustrate the instructions.
Computer interfaces rely on graphic icons for navigation.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pictorial - expressed or represented by pictures

Etymology

From Latin graphicus, from Greek graphikos (pertaining to drawing or writing).

5. ADJ

/ ˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡrafɪk / (BrE)
(of a description, account, or image) giving a clear and vividly realistic picture; strikingly lifelike or detailed.
vividly realistic or lifelike

Examples

The reporter gave a graphic account of the disaster, leaving the audience horrified.
Her graphic description of the scene made it easy to picture every detail.
The novel contains several graphic portrayals of battle that are not suitable for children.

Synset Examples

pictorial poetry and prose
graphic accounts of battle
a lifelike portrait
a vivid description

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vivid - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind
  • Synonym: lifelike - very similar to the person or thing represented
  • Antonym: dull - lacking vividness or interest

Etymology

From Greek graphikos (of or for writing or drawing), from graphein (to write).

Source: WordNet
