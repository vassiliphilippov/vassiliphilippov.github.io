exporter

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɔrtər / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɔːtə / (BrE)
a person or company that sells and sends goods to another country
person or business that sends goods abroad for sale

Examples

As an exporter of agricultural products, the company ships grain to several countries every year.
The nation's economy relies heavily on its role as an exporter of oil.
Many challenges face a small business trying to become an exporter for the first time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: importer - a person or company that brings goods into a country from abroad

Etymology

From export + -er, formed from Latin 'ex-' (out) + 'portare' (to carry).
