Lolo-Burmese

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊloʊˈbɜːrmiz / (AmE), / ˌləʊləʊˈbɜːmiːz / (BrE)
a branch of the Tibeto-Burman language family, spoken primarily in northern Myanmar and Yunnan, China
a Tibeto-Burman language group

Examples

Several ethnic groups in Yunnan speak Lolo-Burmese languages as their mother tongue.
Linguists study the structure of Lolo-Burmese to understand its relationship to other Asian languages.
The Lolo-Burmese languages form a major subgroup of the Tibeto-Burman family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tibeto-Burman - a large language family in Asia including Tibetan and Burmese

Etymology

From the names 'Lolo' (now often called Yi) and 'Burmese', referring to major groups in the language branch.
