queue

1. NOUN

/ kjuː / (AmE), / kjuː / (BrE)
a line of people, vehicles, or things waiting their turn to proceed or be attended to
a line of people or vehicles waiting

Examples

We had to wait in a long queue for tickets to the show.
The school children formed a queue at the cafeteria door.
Cars lined up in a queue at the traffic light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line - a group of people or vehicles arranged in a row, waiting

Etymology

From French 'queue' meaning 'tail', from Latin 'cauda'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ kjuː / (AmE), / kjuː / (BrE)
A braid of hair worn at the back of the head, especially as was traditional in some Asian cultures.
braid of hair at back of head

Examples

During the Qing dynasty, men were required to wear a queue as a sign of submission.
He carefully braided his long hair into a queue before the ceremony.
The soldier's queue hung neatly down his back, as tradition demanded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pigtail - a braid of hair, often one of two, hanging from the back or sides of the head

Etymology

From French *queue* (tail), from Latin *cauda* (tail), referencing the shape of the hairstyle.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ kju / (AmE), / kjuː / (BrE)
(of people) to form or join a line while waiting for something.
to form or join a line while waiting
british

Examples

We had to queue for over an hour to get tickets for the concert.
Dozens of fans started queueing outside the stadium early in the morning.
People queued patiently at the bus stop despite the rain.

Synset Examples

Customers lined up in front of the store

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to line up - to stand in a line
  • Degree Lower: wait - to stay where one is or delay action

Etymology

From French *queue* (tail), from Latin *cauda* (tail).

4. NOUN

/ kjuː / (AmE), / kjuː / (BrE)
(in computing) an ordered list of tasks, jobs, or messages waiting to be processed or transmitted, with items typically handled in the order they arrive.
computing: ordered list of tasks or messages

Examples

The print jobs were added to the queue and processed in order.
Before sending the file, make sure it enters the upload queue.
Messages are stored in a queue until the server is ready to handle them.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stack - (computing) a data structure in which the most recently added item is the first to be removed

Etymology

From French 'queue' (tail), indicating items lined up as in a tail.

Source: WordNet
