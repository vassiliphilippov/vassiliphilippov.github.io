Placodermi

1. NOUN

/ pləˈkɑːdərˌmaɪ / (AmE), / pləˈkɒdəmɪ / (BrE)
an extinct group of ancient fishes characterized by bony armor plates and primitive jaws, prominent during the Devonian period.
extinct armored prehistoric fishes

Examples

Fossils of Placodermi provide evidence for the early evolution of vertebrate jaws.
The Placodermi were among the dominant aquatic predators of the Devonian era.
Museum displays often feature impressive reconstructions of ancient Placodermi.

Etymology

From Greek 'plax, plakos' (plate, tablet) + 'derma' (skin).

2. NOUN

/ plækəˈdɜrmaɪ / (AmE), / plakəˈdɜːmɪ / (BrE)
an extinct group of prehistoric fishes characterized by bony plates covering the head and thorax, and possessing primitive jaws
extinct group of armored, jawed fishes

Examples

Fossils of Placodermi have been discovered in rocks dating back to the Devonian period.
Scientists consider Placodermi to be among the earliest vertebrates with true jaws.
The armor plates of Placodermi provided protection from predators in prehistoric seas.

Etymology

From Greek 'plax' (flat plate) and 'derma' (skin).
