Scorpaenoidea

1. NOUN

/ skɔːr.piˈæn.ɔɪ.di.ə / (AmE), / skɔː.pɪˈiː.nɔɪ.dɪə / (BrE)
A superfamily of spiny fish including scorpionfishes and gurnards, characterized by bony plates on their cheeks.
superfamily of spiny fishes

Examples

Many species within Scorpaenoidea possess venomous spines for defense against predators.
The colorful members of Scorpaenoidea are often found in tropical coral reefs.
Ichthyologists study the taxonomy and evolution of Scorpaenoidea to better understand marine biodiversity.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on genus Scorpaena (a type of scorpionfish) + -oidea (indicating superfamily).
