1. NOUN

/ lɛdʒ / (AmE), / lɛdʒ / (BrE)
a narrow, flat, projecting ridge of rock, especially on a mountain or under water
projecting ridge of rock

Examples

The climbers rested for a moment on a narrow ledge halfway up the cliff.
Schools of fish darted in and out of the rocky ledge beneath the water's surface.
A family of birds built their nest on a small ledge high above the ground.

Etymology

From Middle English legge, of uncertain origin; possibly related to Old English lecgan (to lay).
