Wilhelm Grimm

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɪl.hɛlm ˈɡrɪm / (AmE), / ˈvɪl.hɛlm ˈɡrɪm / (BrE)
(1786–1859) The younger of the two German brothers known for collecting and publishing folk and fairy tales.
German folklorist and fairy tale collector

Examples

Wilhelm Grimm and his brother compiled a famous collection of fairy tales now known worldwide.
The influence of Wilhelm Grimm can be seen in many of today's retellings of classic stories.
Many scholars study how Wilhelm Grimm and his brother preserved German folklore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jacob Grimm - the older Grimm brother, also a folklorist and linguist

Etymology

German personal name: Wilhelm from Germanic 'will' (will, desire) + 'helm' (helmet, protection).
