Umma Tameer-e-Nau

1. PROPN

/ ˈʊmə təˌmɪər eɪ ˈnaʊ / (AmE), / ˈʊmə təˌmɪə ˌeɪ ˈnaʊ / (BrE)
a Pakistani nongovernmental organization, founded by scientists, alleged to have supported terrorism by sharing information on chemical, biological, and nuclear warfare with extremist groups such as al-Qaeda and the Taliban
Pakistani NGO alleged to support terrorism

Examples

The activities of Umma Tameer-e-Nau came under international scrutiny after reports linked it to al-Qaeda.
Authorities investigated Umma Tameer-e-Nau for allegedly providing technical information to terrorist networks.
Despite its humanitarian claims, Umma Tameer-e-Nau has been accused of facilitating weapons development for militants.

Etymology

From Arabic 'umma' (community) and Urdu 'tameer-e-nau' (reconstruction).
