squire

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwaɪr / (AmE), / ˈskwaɪə / (BrE)
a young nobleman who acted as an attendant and apprentice to a knight before becoming a knight himself, especially in medieval times
young noble attendant to a knight

Examples

As a squire, he learned the arts of combat and chivalry from his master.
The young squire tended to his knight’s armor and horse before battle.
Every aspiring knight first served as a squire to gain experience and training.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: knight - a man awarded a nonhereditary title of honor, often serving as a mounted soldier in the Middle Ages
  • Degree Lower: page - a boy acting as a knight's attendant as the first stage of training for chivalry

Etymology

From Old French *esquier*, from Latin *scutarius* (shield-bearer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskwaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈskwaɪə / (BrE)
a man who accompanies or acts as an escort to a woman, especially at a social event
man who escorts a woman

Examples

He played the squire at the banquet, ensuring she was well attended all evening.
She arrived at the ball with a handsome squire by her side.
At the dance, every lady seemed to have a squire eager to escort her across the floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escort - a person who goes with someone to provide protection or as a companion

Etymology

From Old French *esquier*, from Latin *scutarius* (shield-bearer).

3. VERB

/ ˈskwaɪər / (AmE), / ˈskwaɪə / (BrE)
to attend to someone as a squire; to accompany or escort, especially in a courteous or attentive manner
act as a squire; escort or attend someone

Examples

He offered to squire her to the dance, making sure she arrived safely.
During the ceremony, young assistants squired the guests to their seats.
She was squired around the city by her attentive host.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escort - to accompany someone as a protector or guide
  • Synonym: to accompany - to go somewhere with someone as a companion

Etymology

From Old French *esquier* (shield-bearer), from Latin *scutarius* (shield-bearer), from *scutum* (shield).

4. NOUN

/ ˈskwaɪr / (AmE), / ˈskwaɪə / (BrE)
an English country gentleman, especially a landowner; often one who is the principal landholder in a rural area
English country gentleman or landowner
british

Examples

The old squire was respected by everyone in the village for his generosity.
After inheriting the estate, he became the new squire of the countryside.
The squire attended the parish meeting and listened to the concerns of his tenants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landowner - someone who owns land
  • Degree Higher: knight - a man awarded a non-hereditary title by a monarch or other political leader for service to the country

Etymology

From Old French *esquier* (shield-bearer), from Latin *scutarius* (shield-bearer), originally referring to an attendant to a knight.
