forward

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
moving toward a position ahead or in the direction that one is facing; situated at or toward the front.
moving toward the front or ahead

Examples

The forward movement of the troops was halted by the river.
She quickly stepped forward to accept the award.
Please take a forward seat near the stage if one is available.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rear - at or towards the back; the opposite of forward

Etymology

From Old English 'foreweard', from 'fore' (before, in front) + '-weard' (toward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01882654-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)

Examples

Related Terms
