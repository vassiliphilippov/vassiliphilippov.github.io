isomeric

1. ADJ

/ ˌaɪsəˈmɛrɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈmɛrɪk / (BrE)
relating to or exhibiting isomerism; having the same molecular formula but a different structure or arrangement of atoms.
relating to isomerism (chemistry, biology)

Examples

The compound has several isomeric forms, each with distinct physical properties.
Some sugars are isomeric with one another, sharing the same formula but differing in structure.
Modern analytical techniques help chemists distinguish between isomeric substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isomerism - the property of having the same molecular formula but different structure

Etymology

From Greek isomerēs (equal parts): iso- (equal) + meros (part).
