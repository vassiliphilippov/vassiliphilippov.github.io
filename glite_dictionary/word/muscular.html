muscular

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌs.kjʊ.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.lə / (BrE)
having or suggesting great physical power or force, especially in a way that is strong and energetic
powerful or forceful in strength or effect

Examples

The orchestra gave a muscular performance that left the audience in awe.
Her writing style is often described as muscular and direct, full of energy and force.
The new policy represents a more muscular approach to economic reform.

Synset Examples

the muscular and passionate Fifth Symphony

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerful - having great strength or force
  • Antonym: weak - lacking in strength or force

Etymology

From Latin musculāris, from musculus meaning 'muscle'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmʌs.kjələr / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.lə / (BrE)
having well-developed muscles or a strong, robust body build; relating to or characterized by strength and muscle development
having well-developed muscles

Examples

After months of training, his muscular physique impressed everyone at the gym.
The muscular dog easily pulled the heavy sled across the snow.
She admired the muscular legs of the marathon runners as they sprinted to the finish line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brawny - physically strong; muscular
  • Antonym: slender - gracefully thin; not muscular or large

Etymology

From Latin 'muscularis', from 'musculus' (muscle).
