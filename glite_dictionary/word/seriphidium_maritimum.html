Seriphidium maritimum

1. NOUN

/ sɛˌrɪfɪdiəm məˈrɪtɪməm / (AmE), / sɛˌrɪfɪdiəm məˈrɪtɪməm / (BrE)
A plant species of the genus Seriphidium found along the western and northern coasts of Europe, commonly known as sea wormwood.
plant of European coastal regions

Examples

Seriphidium maritimum is well adapted to the salty soils of the European coastline.
Botanists found a thriving population of Seriphidium maritimum near the tidal marshes.
The silvery leaves of Seriphidium maritimum help it retain moisture in harsh coastal environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea wormwood - common name for Seriphidium maritimum

Etymology

From Latin: 'seriphidium' (a type of wormwood) + 'maritimum' (of the sea/coast).
