Embothrium

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈbɒθriəm / (AmE), / ɛmˈbɒθriəm / (BrE)
A genus of South American evergreen shrubs or small trees with long, slender branches and showy clusters of flowers.
genus of South American evergreen shrubs or trees

Examples

The brilliant red flowers of the Embothrium make it a favorite among gardeners in temperate climates.
Scientists are studying the genetic diversity of wild Embothrium populations in Chile.
An Embothrium was planted in the botanical garden to showcase South American flora.

Etymology

From Greek embothrion (a socket), referring to the shape of the flower base.

2. NOUN

/ ɛmˈboʊθriəm / (AmE), / ɛmˈbɒθriəm / (BrE)
A small genus of South American evergreen shrubs or small trees, known for their long willowy branches and bright, showy flower clusters.
genus of South American evergreen shrubs or trees

Examples

The bright red flowers of Embothrium make it a striking addition to any garden.
Several species in the Embothrium genus are valued for their ornamental appeal.
Botanists discovered a new variety within the Embothrium genus in Chile.

Etymology

From New Latin, after Greek 'embothrion' (face of a cave), used as a botanical term.
