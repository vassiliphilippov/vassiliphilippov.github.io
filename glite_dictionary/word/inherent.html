inherent

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈhɛrənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪərənt / (BrE)
existing in something as a permanent, essential, or characteristic attribute.
existing as an essential attribute

Examples

Honesty is an inherent quality of his character.
There are risks inherent in almost every sport.
The difficulties were inherent in the design of the project.

Synset Examples

the Ptolemaic system with its built-in concept of periodicity
a constitutional inability to tell the truth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intrinsic - belonging naturally; essential
  • Antonym: extrinsic - not part of the essential nature of someone or something; coming or operating from outside

Etymology

From Latin inhaerens, present participle of inhaerēre (to be attached to, to stick in).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈhɪr.ənt, -ˈhɛr- / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪə.rənt / (BrE)
existing as a natural and inseparable quality or characteristic of something, often not immediately obvious
existing as a natural part of something

Examples

There are risks inherent in all forms of investment.
The desire to communicate is inherent in human beings.
Some limitations are inherent in the design of the system.

Synset Examples

shortcomings inherent in our approach
an underlying meaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intrinsic - belonging naturally; essential
  • Synonym: innate - inborn; natural, especially a quality or ability
  • Antonym: extrinsic - not inherent; coming from outside

Etymology

From Latin inhaerent-, present participle of inhaerere (to stick in, be inherent).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00945209-s
Synset POS: s
