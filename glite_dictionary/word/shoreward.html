shoreward

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɔr.wərd / (AmE), / ˈʃɔː.wəd / (BrE)
(of winds or movement) directed toward the shore from the sea; coming from the sea toward the land.
toward the shore (from the sea)

Examples

A shoreward breeze made sailing back to port easier.
The storm brought strong shoreward winds that battered the coastline.
As the tide changed, currents shifted in a shoreward direction.

Synset Examples

an inshore breeze
an onshore gale

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seaward - in the direction of the sea; toward the sea

Etymology

Formed from 'shore' + '-ward', a suffix meaning 'in the direction of'.
