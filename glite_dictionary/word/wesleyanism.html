Wesleyanism

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛz.li.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈwɛz.lɪ.ə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
The religious principles and practices taught by John Wesley, forming the foundation of the Methodist movement.
principles of Methodism founded by John Wesley

Examples

Many churches in the region are rooted in Wesleyanism and its emphasis on personal faith.
His studies focus on the impact of Wesleyanism in 18th-century British society.
She became interested in Wesleyanism after reading about John Wesley's teachings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Methodism - a Protestant movement founded on the teachings of John Wesley

Etymology

From the name John Wesley (1703–1791), founder of Methodism, plus -anism.
