cadent

1. ADJ

/ ˈkeɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈkeɪdənt / (BrE)
having a regular, rhythmical cadence or flow.
having a regular rhythm or cadence

Examples

The poem's lines are cadent, echoing the soothing rhythm of the waves.
He admired the cadent movement of the dancers as they glided across the stage.
Her speech had a cadent quality that held the audience's attention throughout.

Synset Examples

the cadenced crunch of marching feet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cadenced - having or marked by a rhythmic cadence

Etymology

From Latin cadere (to fall), through Italian cadente (falling).
