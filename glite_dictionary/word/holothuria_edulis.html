Holothuria edulis

1. NOUN

/ hoʊ.ləˈθjʊr.i.ə ˈɛd.jʊ.lɪs / (AmE), / hɒl.əˈθjʊə.ri.ə ˈɛd.jʊ.lɪs / (BrE)
a species of edible sea cucumber found in warm coastal waters from Australia to Asia, often used as food, especially in Chinese cuisine
an edible sea cucumber species

Examples

In many markets, Holothuria edulis is considered a delicacy and harvested for culinary uses.
The waters around Thailand are home to a thriving population of Holothuria edulis.
Chefs often rehydrate dried Holothuria edulis before adding it to gourmet soups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea cucumber - marine animal of the class Holothuroidea, related to Holothuria edulis

Etymology

From the genus name Holothuria (from Greek holothourion, a kind of water animal) and the Latin edulis, meaning 'edible'.
