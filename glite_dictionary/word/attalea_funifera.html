Attalea funifera

1. NOUN

/ əˈteɪliə fjuːˈnɪfərə / (AmE), / əˈteɪliːə fjuːˈnɪfərə / (BrE)
A Brazilian palm tree whose fibers are used in making ropes, mats, and brushes.
Brazilian palm used for fiber production

Examples

The fibers of Attalea funifera are highly valued for their strength and durability.
Local artisans weave mats and ropes using Attalea funifera fibers.
Research has focused on the sustainable harvesting of Attalea funifera in Brazil.

Etymology

Attalea from Neo-Latin (genus name), funifera from Latin funis (rope) + -fera (bearing).
