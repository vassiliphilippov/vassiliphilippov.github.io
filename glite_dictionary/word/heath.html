heath

1. NOUN

/ hiθ / (AmE), / hiːθ / (BrE)
a low-growing evergreen shrub of the family Ericaceae, with small bell-shaped pink or purple flowers, especially found in heathland or moorland areas
a small evergreen shrub with pink or purple flowers

Examples

The heath was covered with vibrant purple flowers in late summer.
Many insects and birds rely on heath for food and habitat.
She collected a sprig of heath and pressed it between the pages of her book.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heather - a similar low shrub of heathland, often with purple flowers

Etymology

Old English *hæth* (uncultivated land, heath); related to Dutch *heide* and German *Heide*.

2. NOUN

/ hiːθ / (AmE), / hiːθ / (BrE)
an area of open uncultivated land, typically with sandy soil and coarse grasses, heather, and low shrubs
open uncultivated land with low shrubs

Examples

The path crossed a wide heath dotted with wildflowers and heather.
In the novel, the protagonist wandered alone across the desolate heath.
Large areas of the English countryside were once covered by heath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moor - an area of open, uncultivated upland, typically covered with heather and coarse grasses

Etymology

Old English *hæth*, related to Dutch *heide* and German *Heide*.
