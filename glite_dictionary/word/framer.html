framer

1. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪmər / (AmE), / ˈfreɪmə / (BrE)
a person who makes frames, especially for pictures or photographs
person who makes picture frames

Examples

The framer carefully measured the painting before cutting the wood.
She took her diploma to a local framer to have it professionally mounted.
Each framer in the shop has a unique style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: picture framer - person who specializes in making picture frames

Etymology

From 'frame' (Middle English, from Old French 'fremier') + agent noun suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪ.mər / (AmE), / ˈfreɪ.mə / (BrE)
a person who drafts or formulates a new law, plan, or system, especially as part of a founding or constitution-making process
person who drafts a law or plan

Examples

The framers of the Constitution debated every detail of the new government.
She was recognized as one of the principal framers of the peace agreement.
Modern legislators often study the work of the original framers for inspiration.

Synset Examples

the framers of the Constitution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: founder - a person who establishes an institution or organization

Etymology

From the verb 'frame' (to shape, devise) + -er (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10128233-n
Synset POS: n
