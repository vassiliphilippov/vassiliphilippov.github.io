laughingstock

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæfɪŋˌstɑk / (AmE), / ˈlɑːfɪŋˌstɒk / (BrE)
A person or thing that is ridiculed or made fun of by others.
person or thing mocked by others

Examples

After his embarrassing mistake, Tom became the laughingstock of the office.
Their failed invention sadly turned them into a laughingstock in the tech community.
No one wants to be remembered as the laughingstock at their high school reunion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joke - a person or thing not taken seriously
  • Synonym: butt - the person who is the target of jokes or ridicule

Etymology

From 'laughing' + 'stock', with 'stock' meaning object or target; first recorded in the 16th century.
