Platanaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌplætəˈneɪsi.iː / (AmE), / ˌplatəˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of deciduous trees, coextensive with the genus Platanus, commonly known as plane trees
family of plane trees

Examples

The Platanaceae family includes all species of plane trees.
Many urban planners favor trees from the Platanaceae family for their hardiness.
Botanists studied the leaf structure characteristic of the Platanaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Platanus - a genus of large, deciduous trees commonly called plane trees or sycamores

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Platanus and the standard botanical suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ plæˌtænəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / plæˌtænəˈsiː.i / (BrE)
a small family of flowering plants consisting of the plane trees, including only the genus Platanus
a family of plane trees (Platanus)
medical

Examples

The Platanaceae are known for their distinctive exfoliating bark and lobed leaves.
Most members of the Platanaceae family are large, deciduous trees commonly found in temperate regions.
Botanists recently studied genetic diversity within the Platanaceae to learn more about their evolution.

Etymology

From New Latin Platanaceae, based on Platanus (genus of plane trees) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant family names).
