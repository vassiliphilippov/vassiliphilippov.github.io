seith

1. NOUN

/ seɪθ / (AmE), / seɪθ / (BrE)
a form of sorcery or magic practiced in Norse society during the Late Scandinavian Iron Age, associated particularly with rituals and prophecy.
Old Norse magical practice

Examples

The ancient Norse practiced seith to foresee events and influence fate.
Research into seith has revealed connections with shamanistic traditions.
Modern pagans sometimes revive seith rituals as part of their spiritual practice.

Synset Examples

Of all the reconstructed systems of archaic magickal practice, Seiðr seems to be one of the most misunderstood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorcery - the use of magic or supernatural powers
  • Synonym: witchcraft - the practice of magical skills, spells, and abilities

Etymology

From Old Norse seiðr (sorcery, magic).
