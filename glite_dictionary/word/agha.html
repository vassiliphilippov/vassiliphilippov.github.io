Agha

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːɡə / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡə / (BrE)
a title of respect for a civil or military leader, especially in Turkey and some other Islamic countries, used chiefly in historical contexts
title for Turkish civil or military leader

Examples

During the Ottoman era, the Agha was responsible for maintaining order in the barracks.
The local Agha offered guidance to villagers on many matters.
He was addressed as Agha out of respect for his leadership.

Etymology

From Turkish 'ağa', meaning 'chief' or 'master'.
