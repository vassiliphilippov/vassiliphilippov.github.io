Annona diversifolia

1. NOUN

/ əˈnoʊnə dɪˌvɜrsɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / əˈnəʊnə dɪˌvɜːsɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A tropical American tree grown in southern United States, known for its whitish pink-tinged fruit. Also called 'ilama.'
tropical tree with pink-tinged fruit

Examples

The Annona diversifolia is prized for its sweet, fragrant fruit in Central America.
Many gardeners in Florida cultivate Annona diversifolia for its ornamental and edible qualities.
Researchers studied the seeds of Annona diversifolia to explore their potential medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Annona' is a genus name, 'diversifolia' means 'with diverse leaves'.
