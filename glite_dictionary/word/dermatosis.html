dermatosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɜr.məˈtoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɜː.məˈtəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A disease of the skin, especially one involving lesions or eruptions and usually without inflammation.
non-inflammatory skin disease or disorder
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with dermatosis after developing persistent, non-inflamed skin lesions.
Research into rare forms of dermatosis has increased our understanding of skin disorders.
Some types of dermatosis can be treated with topical medications to relieve discomfort.

Etymology

From Greek 'derma' (skin) + '-osis' (condition).
