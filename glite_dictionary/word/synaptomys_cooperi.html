Synaptomys cooperi

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌnæptəˈmaɪz ˈkuːpəri / (AmE), / sɪˌnaptəˈmiːz ˈkuːpəri / (BrE)
A small North American rodent, commonly known as the southern bog lemming, that inhabits low bogs and meadows in northeastern and central United States and southern Canada.
species of North American lemming

Examples

The Synaptomys cooperi is known for its preference for moist, grassy habitats.
Researchers observed several Synaptomys cooperi near the edges of the bogs during their summer survey.
The population of Synaptomys cooperi has declined due to habitat loss in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: southern bog lemming - Common name for Synaptomys cooperi, a small rodent of North America

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, with Synaptomys from Greek 'synaptos' (fastened together) and 'mys' (mouse); species named for William Cooper, American naturalist.
