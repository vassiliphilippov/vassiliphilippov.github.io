documented

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɑːkjəˌmentɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɒkjʊˌmentɪd / (BrE)
supported by written evidence or official documents; having been recorded or proven with documentation
supported by documents; recorded

Examples

This is the first documented instance of this disease in the region.
Scientists examined the documented evidence before drawing conclusions.
The museum displayed documented artifacts dating back several centuries.

Synset Examples

the first documented case of shark attack in those waters

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undocumented - not supported by documents or lacking official documentation

Etymology

From 'document' (noun/verb) + -ed, forming the past participle used as an adjective.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00789846-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɑːkjəˌmɛntɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɒkjʊˌmɛntɪd / (BrE)
shown to be genuine, factual, or true by the provision of documents or reliable records; verified.
verified with documents or proof

Examples

The archaeological find is a documented artifact, proven authentic by several independent experts.
Only documented citizens are eligible for these government benefits.
His achievements are well documented in the historical records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verified - confirmed as true or accurate
  • Antonym: unsubstantiated - not supported or backed by evidence

Etymology

From 'document' (noun or verb) + '-ed' indicating possessing the qualities of being documented.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01119662-s
Synset POS: s
