defiled

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfaɪld / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪld / (BrE)
Morally corrupted, stained, or made impure.
morally stained or impure

Examples

The sacred site was considered defiled after the act of vandalism.
She felt defiled by the betrayal of her closest friend.
Many believed the temple was defiled by such impure rituals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pure - free from moral corruption or sin
  • Synonym: tainted - damaged or spoiled, especially in a moral sense

Etymology

From Middle English 'defoulen', from Old French 'defouler', based on Latin 'fullare' (to tread down).
