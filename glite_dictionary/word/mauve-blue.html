mauve-blue

1. ADJ

/ mɔv blu / (AmE), / məʊv bluː / (BrE)
Of a blue color tinged with mauve; a bluish-purple shade.
bluish-purple color

Examples

The artist chose a mauve-blue paint for the evening sky in her landscape.
She wore a mauve-blue dress that complemented her eyes.
The flowers bloomed in a striking mauve-blue shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavender - a pale purple color
  • Degree Lower: blue - a color between green and violet in the spectrum
  • Degree Higher: purple - a color intermediate between red and blue

Etymology

From 'mauve', a pale purple color (from French 'mauve', the mallow flower), and 'blue'.
