turret

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜːrət / (AmE), / ˈtʌrɪt / (BrE)
a small tower that projects vertically from the wall of a building, typically at a corner or angle, often seen in castles and fortifications
small tower on a building

Examples

The castle's stone turret offered a panoramic view of the surrounding countryside.
A narrow staircase led up to the turret where the guard kept watch.
Many Victorian houses feature a round turret at the corner, adding character to the building.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tower - a tall, narrow building or part of a building that rises high above the ground

Etymology

From Old French 'tourette' (small tower), diminutive of 'tour' (tower).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.ət / (AmE), / ˈtʌr.ɪt / (BrE)
A self-contained, usually rotating structure on a military vehicle, ship, or aircraft, housing guns or other weapons.
rotating weapons platform

Examples

The tank's turret rotated swiftly to aim at the approaching enemy vehicle.
Most modern warships are equipped with at least one heavy turret for their main armament.
The gunner climbed into the turret and prepared the weapon for firing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gun emplacement - a fixed position for mounting guns
  • Synonym: casemate - an armored gun position or room on a warship

Etymology

From Old French 'tourette' (small tower), diminutive of 'tour' (tower), influenced by its original meaning as a small tower.
