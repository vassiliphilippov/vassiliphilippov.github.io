Echinocactus

1. NOUN

/ ˌikɪnoʊˈkæktəs / (AmE), / ˌɛkɪnəʊˈkæktəs / (BrE)
A genus of cacti characterized by globular or cylindrical shapes, native to the southwestern United States through Brazil.
a genus of globular or cylindrical cacti

Examples

The Echinocactus species are prized for their distinctive barrel-like appearance.
A mature Echinocactus can live for decades in arid environments.
Botanists discovered a new variety within the Echinocactus genus in northern Mexico.

Etymology

From Greek 'ekhinos' (hedgehog, sea urchin) + 'cactus', referring to the spiny appearance.
