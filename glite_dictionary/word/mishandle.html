to mishandle

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈhændl̩ / (AmE), / mɪsˈhændl̩ / (BrE)
to deal with or manage something badly, causing problems or damage
manage badly; handle incorrectly

Examples

He completely mishandled the negotiations, leading to a breakdown in talks.
The fragile package was mishandled and arrived broken.
If you mishandle these chemicals, you could cause a dangerous reaction.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mismanage - to manage something badly or incompetently
  • Antonym: handle - to deal with or manage a situation, person, or thing

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (wrongly, badly) + 'handle'.

2. VERB

/ mɪsˈhændl / (AmE), / mɪsˈhændl / (BrE)
to deal with or manage something badly or incompetently
manage badly or incompetently

Examples

The company was accused of having mishandled customer data, resulting in a major security breach.
If you mishandle these chemicals, they could cause serious harm.
He admitted that he had mishandled the situation and apologized to everyone involved.

Synset Examples

The funds were mismanaged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mismanage - to manage something badly
  • Antonym: handle - to deal with or manage a situation or object

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (badly, wrongly) + 'handle' (to deal with).
