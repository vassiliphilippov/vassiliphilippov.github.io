Vaccinium vitis-idaea

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm ˈvaɪtɪs ɪˈdiːə / (AmE), / vækˈsɪniəm ˈvɪtɪs ɪˈdɛə / (BrE)
A low evergreen shrub native to high northern temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America, producing red edible berries; commonly known as lingonberry.
shrub with edible red berries (lingonberry)

Examples

The Vaccinium vitis-idaea thrives in the cool forests of Scandinavia, where its berries are harvested in late summer.
Jams made from Vaccinium vitis-idaea are popular accompaniments to meat dishes in Northern Europe.
Scientists are studying the antioxidant properties of the fruit produced by Vaccinium vitis-idaea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lingonberry - common English name for Vaccinium vitis-idaea

Etymology

From Latin *Vaccinium* (a kind of berry) and *vitis-idaea* (meaning 'vine of Mount Ida').
