mailing

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of sending letters, packages, or other items by mail
the sending of mail or letters

Examples

The mailing of the invitations took place last week.
She checked the mailing to ensure all addresses were correct.
The company offers a mailing service for customers overseas.

Synset Examples

the postmark indicates the time of mailing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: posting - the sending of letters or parcels by post

Etymology

From 'mail' (post) + '-ing', denoting the action or process.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00123158-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
a batch of mail, such as letters or advertisements, sent by a sender at one time
batch of mail sent at one time

Examples

The company prepared a mailing to announce its latest product launch.
We received a promotional mailing from the local gym this morning.
Each mailing included a personalized letter and a brochure.

Synset Examples

the candidate sent out three large mailings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mail - letters, parcels, or packages sent by post

Etymology

From the verb 'mail' (to send by post) + -ing, meaning 'the act or process of sending mail'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08480658-n
Synset POS: n
