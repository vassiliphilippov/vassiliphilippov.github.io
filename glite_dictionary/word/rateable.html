rateable

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.t̬ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.tə.bəl / (BrE)
(chiefly British) A property or asset that is assessed for local taxes (rates) by the government; something liable to be rated.
property assessed for local taxes
british

Examples

The council calculated how much revenue could be raised from all the rateables in the area.
Many rural villages had few rateables and struggled to maintain public services.
New developments increased the number of rateables on the town's tax roll.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxable - liable to be taxed

Etymology

From the verb 'rate' (to assess for taxation), combined with -able, meaning 'liable to be rated or assessed.'

2. ADJ

/ ˈreɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈreɪtəbl / (BrE)
(of property) liable to payment of locally assessed property taxes.
liable to local property taxes
british legal

Examples

The council determined that the new building is rateable under current law.
Only rateable properties are included in the local tax assessments.
Owners must ensure their rateable value is correctly registered with the authorities.

Synset Examples

the ratable value of property

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxable - liable to be taxed; subject to taxation

Etymology

From 'rate' (a local tax) + '-able'. Commonly used in British legal and property contexts.
