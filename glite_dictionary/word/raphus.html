Raphus

1. NOUN

/ ˈræfəs / (AmE), / ˈræfəs / (BrE)
A genus of extinct flightless birds, including the dodo, formerly found on the island of Mauritius.
genus of extinct birds (dodo)

Examples

The extinct dodo belongs to the genus Raphus.
Scientists study skeletal remains to learn more about Raphus and its evolutionary history.
Mauritius was once home to Raphus, better known as the dodo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dodo - an extinct flightless bird once native to Mauritius

Etymology

From modern Latin Raphus, the genus name given to the dodo.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræfəs / (AmE), / ˈræfəs / (BrE)
A genus of extinct flightless birds, including the dodo, formerly found in Mauritius.
genus of extinct birds (dodo)

Examples

The extinct dodo was classified under the genus Raphus.
Scientists debate the evolutionary relationships of birds in the genus Raphus.
The only known member of Raphus is the dodo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dodo - an extinct, flightless bird from Mauritius, member of genus Raphus

Etymology

From modern Latin Raphus, the scientific name assigned by taxonomists.
