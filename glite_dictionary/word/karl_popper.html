Karl Popper

1. PROPN

/ kɑrl ˈpɑːpər / (AmE), / kɑːl ˈpɒpə / (BrE)
(1902–1994) Austrian-born British philosopher known for his views on the philosophy of science, especially the idea that scientific theories can be tested but never conclusively proven true.
Austrian-British philosopher of science

Examples

Many scientists have been influenced by Karl Popper's philosophy of falsifiability.
The writings of Karl Popper are frequently cited in discussions on the scientific method.
Karl Popper proposed that every scientific theory must be open to testing and possible falsification.
