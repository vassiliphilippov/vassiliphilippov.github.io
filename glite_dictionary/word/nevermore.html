nevermore

1. ADV

/ ˌnɛvərˈmɔr / (AmE), / ˌnɛvəˈmɔː / (BrE)
(literary or archaic) At no time in the future; never again.
never again; at no future time (archaic/literary)
archaic

Examples

After that tragic day, he vowed nevermore to return to the old house.
Technology has rendered some professions obsolete, leaving them nevermore to be practiced.
In the cold silence of the hall, she whispered she would see him nevermore.

Synset Examples

Quoth the raven, nevermore!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: never - not ever; at no time

Etymology

Formed from 'never' + 'more', meaning 'never again'. Notably featured in Edgar Allan Poe's poem 'The Raven'.
