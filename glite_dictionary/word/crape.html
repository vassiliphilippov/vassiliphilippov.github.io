crape

1. NOUN

/ kreɪp / (AmE), / kreɪp / (BrE)
a soft, thin, lightweight fabric with a crinkled or wrinkled surface, often used for clothing and mourning dress
light crinkled fabric

Examples

Her dress was made of delicate black crape for the funeral ceremony.
Victorian women often wore crape as a symbol of mourning.
The shop displayed a variety of colorful crape fabrics for summer gowns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crepe - light crinkled fabric, variant spelling

Etymology

From French *crêpe*, ultimately from Latin *crispa* (curled, wrinkled).

2. VERB

/ kreɪp / (AmE), / kreɪp / (BrE)
To cover or drape with crape, especially as a sign of mourning.
cover with crape (mourning fabric)

Examples

During the Victorian era, it was common to crape doorways after a death in the family.
She carefully craped the portrait frame to honor her late father.
They spent the morning craping the parlor mirrors for the funeral.

Synset Examples

crape the mirror

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drape - to cover or adorn with cloth in loose folds

Etymology

From French *crêpe*, meaning 'crimped' or 'wrinkled', referring to the textured fabric.

3. NOUN

/ kreɪp / (AmE), / kreɪp / (BrE)
A very thin pancake, typically made from wheat flour, eggs, and milk, and served with sweet or savoury fillings.
a very thin pancake

Examples

She ordered a chocolate crape filled with strawberries for dessert.
The cafe specializes in savory crapes with cheese and ham.
He learned to make the perfect crape during his trip to Paris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crêpe - A very thin pancake; standard French spelling for the dish

Etymology

From French "crêpe", itself from Latin "crispa" (curled).

to curl into tight waves

Examples

Artists sometimes crape delicate fabrics to create decorative textures.
