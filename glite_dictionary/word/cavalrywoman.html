cavalrywoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈkævəlriˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈkævəlriˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A female soldier who serves in the cavalry, traditionally mounted on horseback.
female cavalry soldier

Examples

The museum exhibit featured the uniform of a nineteenth-century cavalrywoman.
As a skilled cavalrywoman, she led her unit through rough terrain with confidence.
Historical records mention several brave cavalrywomen who fought in the war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cavalryman - a male or unspecified-gender cavalry soldier

Etymology

Formed from 'cavalry' and 'woman', modeled after 'cavalryman'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkævəlriˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈkævəlriˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A female soldier who serves in a cavalry or armored unit, especially one operating motorized vehicles.
female soldier in a cavalry unit

Examples

The first cavalrywoman in her unit was celebrated for her exceptional skill and leadership.
During the parade, a cavalrywoman led the column of armored vehicles with pride.
As a cavalrywoman, she mastered both traditional cavalry tactics and modern armored warfare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cavalryman - a male soldier in a cavalry or armored unit

Etymology

From 'cavalry' + 'woman,' modeled after 'cavalryman.'
