Hinduism

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪn.duˌɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈhɪn.dʒuː.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A major religion and cultural tradition of South Asia, particularly India, characterized by a belief in reincarnation, a supreme being of many forms, a caste system, and the pursuit of spiritual liberation (moksha).
major religion of India; belief in reincarnation and caste

Examples

Many followers of Hinduism celebrate Diwali, the festival of lights.
He studied the sacred texts of Hinduism during his time in India.
Hinduism teaches the concept of reincarnation and karma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buddhism - Another major religion originating in India
  • Antonym: Christianity - World religion based on the life and teachings of Jesus Christ

Etymology

From Persian *Hindū* (an inhabitant of India) + English *-ism* (system, belief).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɪn.duˌɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈhɪn.djuːˌɪz.əm / (BrE)
A major world religion originating in South Asia, characterized by a belief in reincarnation, a multitude of gods and goddesses, and an adherence to a set of religious and social duties.
major religion of South Asia

Examples

Many festivals celebrated in India have their roots in Hinduism.
She decided to study Hinduism as part of her comparative religion course.
Rituals and traditions from Hinduism are observed in many South Asian countries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Buddhism - a major world religion founded by Siddhartha Gautama (the Buddha)
  • Antonym: Christianity - the religion based on the teachings of Jesus Christ

Etymology

From Hindi 'Hindu' + English '-ism', originally from Persian 'Hindu' (Indus River).
