Chasidic

1. ADJ

/ hɑːˈsɪdɪk / (AmE), / haˈsɪdɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the Jewish Hasidim, their members, or their religious beliefs and customs.
of or relating to the Jewish Hasidim

Examples

The Chasidic community gathered to celebrate the festival in their traditional style.
Many Chasidic men wear distinctive clothing, such as black hats and long coats.
She wrote her thesis on the spiritual teachings central to Chasidic Judaism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hasidic - Alternate spelling indicating the same concept, relating to the Jewish Hasidim

Etymology

From Hebrew ḥasīd (pious), via Yiddish khasidish.
