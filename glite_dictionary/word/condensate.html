condensate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.dənˌseɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dən.seɪt / (BrE)
A product formed when a gas or vapor cools and becomes a liquid or solid, especially in a scientific or industrial context.
product formed by condensation

Examples

The refinery collects water condensate from the overhead cooling systems.
Natural gas condensate can be separated out and processed for fuel.
During the experiment, a layer of liquid condensate formed inside the glass tube.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condensation - the process by which a gas or vapor becomes a liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'condensatus', past participle of 'condensare' (to condense).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.dənˌseɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dən.seɪt / (BrE)
atmospheric moisture, such as water droplets, that has condensed from vapor due to cooling
moisture formed by condensation

Examples

As the temperature dropped overnight, condensate formed on the inside of the windows.
The air conditioner removes condensate from the air to help keep the room dry.
Scientists collected condensate from the cold metal surface for chemical analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condensation - the process by which a gas or vapor changes into a liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'condensare' (to make thick or dense), influenced by 'condense'.
