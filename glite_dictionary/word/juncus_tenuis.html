Juncus tenuis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋkəs ˈtɛnjuɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkəs ˈtɛnjʊɪs / (BrE)
A species of tufted, wiry rush widely distributed in North America and other regions, commonly known as slender rush.
species of slender, wiry rush plant

Examples

The path was bordered on both sides by Juncus tenuis, giving it a green and wiry appearance.
Botanists often encounter Juncus tenuis in disturbed soils and compacted paths.
The field guide helped us identify the slender stems of Juncus tenuis along the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - a grass-like plant of wet places, genus Juncus

Etymology

From Latin: Genus name 'Juncus' (rush); species name 'tenuis' meaning 'slender, thin'.
