goosefoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡusˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈɡuːsfʊt / (BrE)
any of various annual herbs of the genus Chenopodium, typically with triangular or diamond-shaped leaves and small greenish flowers, often regarded as weeds.
plant of the genus Chenopodium

Examples

The leaves of the goosefoot plant are sometimes cooked as a leafy vegetable.
Farmers often find goosefoot growing wild along the edges of their fields.
Some species of goosefoot are used in traditional medicine for various remedies.

Etymology

Refers to the shape of the leaves, which resemble the foot of a goose.
