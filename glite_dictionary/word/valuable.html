valuable

1. ADJ

/ ˈvæljuəbəl / (AmE), / ˈvæljuːəbəl / (BrE)
worth a lot of money or having great material value, especially for use or exchange
worth a lot of money

Examples

The jeweler placed the valuable necklace in a secure display case.
She inherited a valuable painting from her grandmother.
These coins could become extremely valuable over time.

Synset Examples

a valuable diamond

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precious - of great value; not to be wasted or treated carelessly
  • Antonym: worthless - having no real value

Etymology

From Latin 'valere' (to be worth) via Old French 'valuable'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02510833-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈvæljəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈvæljʊəbl̩ / (BrE)
an object or objects, especially small items, that are worth a lot of money or are important for personal reasons
item of value, often valuable objects

Examples

Hotel guests are advised to keep their valuables in the safe provided in each room.
She lost all her valuables when her bag was stolen on the train.
Remember to pack your valuables in your carry-on luggage rather than your checked bag.

Synset Examples

all our valuables were stolen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valuables - personal possessions of value; plural form commonly used in same sense
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13392203-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈvæljəbəl / (AmE), / ˈvæljʊəbl / (BrE)
worth a lot or having great importance, usefulness, or merit.
of great worth or importance

Examples

She gave me some valuable advice before my interview.
Honesty is a valuable quality in a friend.
The documents are extremely valuable for understanding our history.

Synset Examples

a valuable friend
a good and worthful man

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: priceless - so precious that its value cannot be determined
  • Antonym: worthless - having no value or use
  • Synonym: useful - able to be used to achieve something; helpful

Etymology

From Latin 'valere' (to be strong, be worth), via Old French 'valuable'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02598053-s
Synset POS: s
