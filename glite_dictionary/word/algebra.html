algebra

1. NOUN

/ ˈældʒəbrə / (AmE), / ˈældʒɪbrə / (BrE)
the branch of mathematics in which letters and symbols are used to represent numbers and quantities in formulas and equations
branch of mathematics using symbols and formulas

Examples

Many students find algebra challenging at first, but it forms the basis for advanced mathematics.
She solved the problem using skills she learned in algebra class.
Understanding algebra is essential for pursuing science and engineering careers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arithmetic - the branch of mathematics dealing with the properties and manipulation of numbers

Etymology

From Arabic al-jabr (reunion of broken parts), introduced in the title of a 9th-century work by al-Khwarizmi.
