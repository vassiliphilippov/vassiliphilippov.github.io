Frans Hals

1. PROPN

/ frænz hælz / (AmE), / frænz hælz / (BrE)
(c. 1582–1666) A Dutch painter known for his lively and humorous portraits and genre scenes, prominent in the Dutch Golden Age.
Dutch painter known for lively portraits

Examples

Many art historians admire Frans Hals for his ability to capture spontaneous expressions.
The work of Frans Hals is featured in major museums across Europe.
Several of Frans Hals' paintings depict cheerful gatherings and show remarkable energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rembrandt - Dutch painter known for his portraits and use of light and shadow
