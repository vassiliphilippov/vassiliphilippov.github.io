Harkat-ul-Jihad al-Islami

1. PROPN

/ hɑrˈkɑːt ʊl dʒɪˈhɑːd æl ɪsˈlɑːmi / (AmE), / hɑːˈkɑːt ʊl dʒɪˈhɑːd æl ɪsˈlɑːmi / (BrE)
an Islamist militant group operating in Pakistan and Kashmir, known for seeking an Islamic government and close ties with the Taliban in Afghanistan
Islamist militant group in Pakistan and Kashmir

Examples

Harkat-ul-Jihad al-Islami has been designated as a terrorist organization by several countries.
Security forces in the region have launched operations against Harkat-ul-Jihad al-Islami militants.
Reports indicate that Harkat-ul-Jihad al-Islami has maintained links with other extremist groups.

Etymology

From Arabic, meaning 'Movement of Islamic Jihad'.
