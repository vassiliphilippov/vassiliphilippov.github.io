Limnodium spongia

1. NOUN

/ lɪmˈnoʊdiəm ˈspʌndʒiə / (AmE), / lɪmˈnəʊdɪəm ˈspʌndʒɪə / (BrE)
An American aquatic plant with roundish heart-shaped or kidney-shaped leaves, typically rooted in the muddy bottoms of ponds and ditches.
American aquatic plant with heart-shaped leaves

Examples

In the shallow pond, Limnodium spongia spread its lush, spongy leaves across the muddy bottom.
The botanist identified the plant as Limnodium spongia by its distinctive kidney-shaped leaves.
Many birds nest near areas where Limnodium spongia thrives in watery ditches.

Etymology

From Greek 'limne' (marsh, lake) and Latin 'spongia' (sponge), referring to its habitat and spongy texture.
