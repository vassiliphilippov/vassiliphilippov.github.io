Pollachius

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈlækiəs / (AmE), / pɒˈlakɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the cod family that includes species commonly known as pollack, found in the North Atlantic.
genus of fish (pollack) in the cod family

Examples

The species Pollachius virens is commonly known as saithe or coalfish in Europe.
Marine biologists conducted a study on several Pollachius populations in the North Atlantic.
The genus Pollachius includes economically important fish for commercial fishing industries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pollack - a North Atlantic fish of the cod family, member of the genus Pollachius

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the vernacular name pollack.

2. NOUN

/ poʊˈlæki.əs / (AmE), / pɒˈlæk.i.əs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the cod family, including species commonly known as pollock.
genus of fish including pollocks

Examples

The genus Pollachius includes important commercial fish species such as the Atlantic pollock.
Marine biologists identified the specimen as belonging to the genus Pollachius.
Fishermen rely on stocks of Pollachius for a significant part of their catch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pollock - a common name for fish in the genus Pollachius

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the species name Pollachius virens.
