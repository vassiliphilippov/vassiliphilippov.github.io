capriccio

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpriːtʃioʊ / (AmE), / kəˈprɪtʃiəʊ / (BrE)
an instrumental musical composition, typically lively and free in form, exhibiting improvisational style and not adhering to rules of any specific musical form
free-form lively musical composition

Examples

The pianist performed a breathtaking capriccio that left the audience in awe.
For her senior recital, Maria chose to play a capriccio composed by Tchaikovsky.
The composer's latest work is a capriccio full of unexpected tempo changes and improvisational passages.

Etymology

From Italian 'capriccio' meaning 'whim' or 'caprice'.
