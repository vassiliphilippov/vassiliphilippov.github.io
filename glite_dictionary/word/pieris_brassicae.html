Pieris brassicae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪˈɛrɪs ˌbræsɪˈkiː / (AmE), / ˌpɪərɪs ˌbrasɪˈkiː / (BrE)
a species of large white butterfly, commonly known as the cabbage white, native to Europe and Asia and noted for being a pest of cabbage and related plants
species of cabbage white butterfly

Examples

The larvae of Pieris brassicae are well known for damaging cabbage crops.
Gardeners often take precautions to protect their plants from Pieris brassicae.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Pieris brassicae across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage white - common name for Pieris brassicae and related species of white butterfly

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: Pieris (the genus name), from Greek mythology, and brassicae (genitive of Latin 'brassica', meaning cabbage).
