want

1. VERB

/ wɑnt / (AmE), / wɒnt / (BrE)
to be without or lack something that is needed or desired
to lack; to be without

Examples

In times of war, entire communities may want basic necessities.
This soup wants more salt to bring out its flavor.
If the plan wants clarity, it will confuse the team.

Synset Examples

want courtesy
want the strength to go on living
flood victims wanting food and shelter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lack - to be without or deficient in

Etymology

From Old Norse 'vanta' (to lack).
