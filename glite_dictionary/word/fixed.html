fixed

1. ADJ

/ fɪkst / (AmE), / fɪkst / (BrE)
firmly attached or positioned so that it cannot move or be changed easily
securely attached or set in place

Examples

The shelves are fixed to the wall for extra safety.
Each seat in the theater is fixed and cannot be rearranged.
He installed a fixed bracket to keep the gate steady.

Synset Examples

a fixed piece of wood
a fixed resistor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: movable - able to be moved or relocated

Etymology

From Latin 'fixus', past participle of 'figere' (to fasten).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01063099-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ fɪkst / (AmE), / fɪkst / (BrE)
firmly held in one place; not moving or changing
not moving; stationary

Examples

She stared at him with a fixed expression on her face.
The guard remained fixed at his post the entire night.
He sat in a fixed position, refusing to move.

Synset Examples

with eyes set in a fixed glassy stare
his bearded face already has a set hollow look
a face rigid with pain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stationary - not moving or not intended to be moved
  • Antonym: moving - in motion; not fixed in place

Etymology

From Latin 'fixus', past participle of 'figere' (to fasten).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01568996-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ fɪkst / (AmE), / fɪkst / (BrE)
(of a number, value, or quantity) remaining unchanged; not variable or fluctuating.
unchanging; not variable

Examples

A fixed interest rate does not change throughout the term of the loan.
In this experiment, we kept the temperature fixed while varying the pressure.
The company pays employees a fixed monthly salary regardless of hours worked.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: variable - (of a number, value, or quantity) able to change or vary
  • Synonym: constant - a value or quantity that does not change

Etymology

From Latin fixus (fastened), from figere (to fasten).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00743800-s
Synset POS: s

not able to be changed or moved

Examples

The terms of the contract are fixed and cannot be negotiated.
