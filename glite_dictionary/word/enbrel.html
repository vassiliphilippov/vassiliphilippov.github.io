Enbrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.brəl / (AmE), / ˈɛn.brəl / (BrE)
a trade name for a genetically engineered medication (etanercept) used to treat rheumatoid arthritis and other autoimmune conditions by inhibiting tumor necrosis factor (TNF)
trade name for a medication treating autoimmune disorders
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Enbrel to help manage her rheumatoid arthritis symptoms.
Many patients have experienced reduced joint pain after starting Enbrel injections.
You should discuss the potential side effects of Enbrel with your healthcare provider.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etanercept - the generic name for Enbrel, a biologic medication
