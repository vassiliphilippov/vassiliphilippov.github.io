deadbolt

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdˌboʊlt / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌbəʊlt / (BrE)
A strong, thick metal bar in a lock that is moved into place by turning a key or knob to secure a door.
strong metal bar in a lock

Examples

Make sure the deadbolt is locked before you leave the house.
He installed a new deadbolt to improve the security of his apartment.
The key turned smoothly in the deadbolt, securing the front door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: latch - a metal bar used to keep a door or gate closed, but usually less secure than a deadbolt
  • Synonym: lock - a fastening device operated by a key or combination

Etymology

From 'dead' (meaning unmoving) + 'bolt' (metal bar).
