estragon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛstrəˌgɒn / (AmE), / ˈɛstrəgən / (BrE)
an aromatic perennial herb (Artemisia dracunculus), especially cultivated for use as a culinary herb, also known as tarragon
aromatic culinary herb; tarragon

Examples

Fresh estragon leaves add a distinctive flavor to many French dishes.
She sprinkled chopped estragon over the roasted chicken before serving.
Gardeners often grow estragon for its fragrant leaves and culinary uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarragon - an aromatic herb also known as estragon; used in cooking

Etymology

From French 'estragon', from Italian 'estragone', altered from Medieval Latin 'tragonia', of Arabic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛstrəˌɡɒn / (AmE), / ˈɛstrəɡən / (BrE)
A culinary herb, the fresh leaves or leaves preserved in vinegar, used as a seasoning in cooking, especially French cuisine.
herb used as a seasoning

Examples

The recipe calls for a teaspoon of chopped estragon to enhance the flavor of the sauce.
She preserved estragon leaves in vinegar to use throughout the winter.
A classic Béarnaise sauce often includes fresh estragon for its distinctive taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarragon - A herb; another term for estragon, used as seasoning

Etymology

From French 'estragon', from Italian 'estragone', from Arabic 'tarkhūn'.
