caller

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔlɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːlə / (BrE)
a person who calls out, proclaims, or summons others in a loud voice
person who calls out loudly

Examples

The caller shouted across the playground to get the children's attention.
Each morning, the village caller would announce important news in the square.
During the festival, a caller summoned everyone to gather for the evening feast.

Synset Examples

the callers were mothers summoning their children home for dinner

Etymology

From call + -er, denoting someone who calls.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

Examples

8. NOUN

/ ˈkɔlər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːlə / (BrE)
in card games, a bettor who matches the bet and requests for a reveal of the players' hands
bettor who calls for a show of hands

Examples

When Tom was unsure, he became the caller and forced everyone to show their cards.
In high-stakes poker, the caller may risk losing more but also uncovers potential bluffs.
If no one else raises, the caller ends the betting round and prompts the final reveal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: raiser - a player who increases the bet in a card game
