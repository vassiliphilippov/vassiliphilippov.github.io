tercelet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜːrslət / (AmE), / ˈtɜːsəlɪt / (BrE)
a male hawk, especially a male peregrine or gyrfalcon
male of certain hawks

Examples

The falconer trained the tercelet to return at the sound of her whistle.
It was rare to see a wild tercelet hunting on its own in this region.
Renaissance nobles often prized the tercelet for its agility during falconry contests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiercel - male hawk, especially a male peregrine falcon

Etymology

From Old French 'tercelet', diminutive of 'tierce' (third), from the belief that one in three hatched birds is male.
