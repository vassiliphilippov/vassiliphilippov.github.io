premier

1. NOUN

/ prəˈmɪr / (AmE), / ˈprɛmiə / (BrE)
the head of government of certain countries, such as the United Kingdom; a prime minister.
head of government; prime minister

Examples

The new premier promised sweeping reforms during his first address to Parliament.
After the scandal, the premier faced mounting pressure to resign.
In many countries, the premier represents the government at international summits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prime minister - the head of government in parliamentary systems

Etymology

From French 'premier' (first), from Latin 'primarius' (principal, chief).

2. VERB

/ prɪˈmɪr / (AmE), / ˈprɛm.i.eə / (BrE)
(of a play, film, piece of music, etc.) to be performed, shown, or introduced to the public for the first time.
to be shown or performed for the first time

Examples

The director chose Paris as the city to premier his latest movie.
The musical premiered to sold-out audiences and received rave reviews.
