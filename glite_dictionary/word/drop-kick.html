to drop-kick

1. VERB

/ ˈdrɑp.kɪk / (AmE), / ˈdrɒp.kɪk / (BrE)
to kick a ball after it has been dropped and just as it touches the ground, especially in football or rugby.
kick a ball as it touches the ground

Examples

He expertly drop-kicked the ball through the goalposts.
The player decided to drop-kick for a field goal instead of passing.
She practiced how to drop-kick with precision during training.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: punt - to kick a ball after it is dropped, before it touches the ground

Etymology

From 'drop' + 'kick', referring to the action performed with the ball.

2. VERB

/ ˈdrɑp ˌkɪk / (AmE), / ˈdrɒp ˌkɪk / (BrE)
to kick a ball after letting it drop to the ground, especially to score points after a touchdown or in rugby
kick a ball after dropping it

Examples

The player managed to drop-kick the ball through the posts to score an extra point.
During the final moments of the game, he drop-kicked and secured the win for his team.
She practiced how to drop-kick perfectly for her first rugby match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punt - to kick the ball before it touches the ground

Etymology

From 'drop' + 'kick', referring to the technique of dropping the ball before kicking.
