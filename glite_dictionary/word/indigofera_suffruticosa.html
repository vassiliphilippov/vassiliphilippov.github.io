Indigofera suffruticosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈɡoʊfərə ˌsʌfruːtɪˈkoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈɡəʊfərə ˌsʌfruːtɪˈkəʊsə / (BrE)
a shrub native to the West Indies and South America, cultivated as a source of indigo dye
shrub used for indigo dye

Examples

Indigofera suffruticosa was widely cultivated by indigenous peoples for its dye-producing properties.
The leaves of Indigofera suffruticosa are processed to produce natural indigo dye.
Botanists study Indigofera suffruticosa to understand traditional methods of dye extraction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigo plant - any plant used as a source of indigo dye

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Indigofera' means 'indigo-bearing' and 'suffruticosa' means 'somewhat shrubby'.
