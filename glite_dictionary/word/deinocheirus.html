Deinocheirus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪnəˈkaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌdaɪnəʊˈkaɪərəs / (BrE)
a lightly built, medium-sized theropod dinosaur from the Late Cretaceous period, known for its long limbs and neck
a genus of lightly built theropod dinosaur

Examples

The fossil remains of Deinocheirus were first discovered in Mongolia in the 1960s.
Unlike many theropods, Deinocheirus had uniquely large arms and a duck-like bill.
Paleontologists were surprised by the unusual body structure of Deinocheirus.

Etymology

From Greek deinos (terrible) + cheir (hand), referring to its large forelimbs.

2. NOUN

/ daɪˌnəʊˈkaɪrəs / (AmE), / daɪˌnəʊˈkʌɪrəs / (BrE)
a genus of large, ornithomimid theropod dinosaurs known for their distinctive massive forelimbs, which lived during the Late Cretaceous period
genus of large ornithomimid dinosaurs

Examples

The fossilized remains of Deinocheirus were first discovered in Mongolia in 1965.
The massive arms of Deinocheirus have puzzled paleontologists for decades.
Unlike other ornithomimids, Deinocheirus possessed a unique hump on its back.

Etymology

From Greek 'deinos' (terrible) and 'cheir' (hand), referring to its huge forelimbs.
