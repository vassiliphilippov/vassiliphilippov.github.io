emaciated

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmeɪsiˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈmeɪsɪeɪtɪd / (BrE)
extremely thin, especially because of disease, hunger, or lack of food
abnormally thin or wasted in appearance

Examples

The refugee appeared emaciated, with his bones clearly visible beneath his skin.
After months of illness, the cat became so emaciated that it weighed barely two kilograms.
Doctors treated emaciated children suffering from severe malnutrition at the emergency clinic.

Synset Examples

a nightmare population of gaunt men and skeletal boys
eyes were haggard and cavernous
small pinched faces
kept life in his wasted frame only by grim concentration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaunt - extremely thin; haggard and drawn, as from hunger or weariness
  • Synonym: skeletal - very thin, like a skeleton
  • Antonym: robust - strong and healthy; vigorous

Etymology

From Latin 'emaciatus', past participle of 'emaciare' (to make lean).
