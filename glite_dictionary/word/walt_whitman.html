Walt Whitman

1. PROPN

/ wɔlt ˈwɪtmən / (AmE), / wɔːlt ˈwɪtmən / (BrE)
(1819–1892) United States poet known for celebrating the spirit and diversity of America, especially in his collection 'Leaves of Grass'.
American poet (1819–1892)

Examples

Many literary critics regard Walt Whitman as one of the most influential American poets.
The poems of Walt Whitman celebrate individuality and nature in innovative ways.
Walt Whitman's 'Leaves of Grass' broke new ground in poetic form and subject matter.
