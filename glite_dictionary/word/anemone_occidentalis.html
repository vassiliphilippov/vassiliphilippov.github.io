Anemone occidentalis

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛməni ɒkˌsɪdəntˈælɪs / (AmE), / əˈnɛməni ɒkˌsɪdəntˈælɪs / (BrE)
A species of herbaceous flowering plant native to western North America, also known as western pasqueflower.
herbaceous plant species of western North America

Examples

The Anemone occidentalis blooms early in the spring in the mountains of Washington.
Hikers in the Rockies often spot Anemone occidentalis along alpine trails.
Researchers studied the distribution of Anemone occidentalis across different elevations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: western pasqueflower - Common name for Anemone occidentalis

Etymology

Anemone from Greek anemōnē (windflower); occidentalis from Latin occidentalis (western).
