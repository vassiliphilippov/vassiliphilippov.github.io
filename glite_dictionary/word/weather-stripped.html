weather-stripped

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɛðər ˌstrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈwɛðə ˌstrɪpt / (BrE)
(of a building element, such as a door or window) having cracks or edges sealed with weather stripping to prevent drafts and improve insulation.
sealed with weather stripping

Examples

The old windows were finally weather-stripped before winter arrived.
After the house was weather-stripped, our heating bills went down noticeably.
It's important to keep your garage door weather-stripped to prevent cold drafts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: drafty - allowing air to pass through, not tightly sealed

Etymology

From 'weather stripping' (material used to seal joints against the weather) + '-ed' forming an adjective.
