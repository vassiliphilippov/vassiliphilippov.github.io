asthmatic

1. NOUN

/ æzˈmætɪk / (AmE), / azˈmatɪk / (BrE)
a person who suffers from asthma
person with asthma

Examples

The doctor specializes in treating children who are asthmatic.
As a lifelong asthmatic, she always carries her inhaler.
Several asthmatic patients attended the clinic for regular check-ups.

Synset Examples

she is a chronic asthmatic
the painful gasps of a dying asthmatic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asthma - a respiratory condition marked by spasms in the bronchi of the lungs

Etymology

From Greek asthma (panting) via Latin asthmaticus.

2. ADJ

/ æzˈmætɪk / (AmE), / æzˈmatɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by breathing with a wheezing or whistling sound, especially as a symptom of asthma.
relating to wheezing, whistling breathing
medical

Examples

His asthmatic breathing could be heard across the room during the allergy attack.
The doctor recognized the asthmatic sound as a sign that treatment was needed.
After running, she developed a harsh, asthmatic wheeze that made it hard to speak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheezing - breathing with a high-pitched whistling sound

Etymology

From Greek asthma (panting), via Latin asthmaticus.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01177592-s
Synset POS: s
