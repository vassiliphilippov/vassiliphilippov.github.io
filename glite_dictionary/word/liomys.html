Liomys

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ.əˌmɪs / (AmE), / ˈliː.əˌmɪs / (BrE)
a former genus of spiny pocket mice native to Central America and Mexico, now typically included in the genus Heteromys
genus of spiny pocket mice

Examples

The painted spiny pocket mouse was once classified under the genus Liomys.
Recent studies have merged Liomys species into the Heteromys genus based on genetic evidence.
Researchers collected data on the habitat preferences of Liomys in Mexico.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Heteromys - current genus including former Liomys species, spiny pocket mice

Etymology

From Greek leios (smooth) + mys (mouse), used in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ.əˌmɪs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.əˌmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of spiny pocket mice found in Central America and Mexico, including species such as the Panamanian and Mexican spiny pocket mouse.
a genus of spiny pocket mice

Examples

The painted spiny pocket mouse belongs to the genus Liomys.
Researchers have observed several species of Liomys in Mexico's dry forests.
The genus Liomys is characterized by stiff spines on its back.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, formed from Greek 'leios' (smooth) and 'mys' (mouse).
