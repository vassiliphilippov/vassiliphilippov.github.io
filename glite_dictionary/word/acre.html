Acre

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑː.kɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑː.kə / (BrE)
A town and port in northwestern Israel, on the eastern Mediterranean coast, known for its historical significance and ancient architecture.
historic town and port in Israel

Examples

The ancient city of Acre attracts thousands of tourists every year for its Crusader-era buildings.
Many important historical events took place in Acre over the centuries.
Trade flourished in Acre due to its strategic location on the Mediterranean coast.

Etymology

From Arabic 'ʿAkkā' (عكا), the local name for the city.
