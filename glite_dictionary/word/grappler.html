grappler

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræp.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡræp.lə / (BrE)
a tool with several hooks used for grasping and holding objects, often attached to a rope and thrown to catch or pull things
hooked tool for grabbing objects

Examples

The climber tossed the grappler over the ledge to secure his rope.
They used a grappler to retrieve the sunken equipment from the riverbed.
A skilled sailor can throw a grappler to catch onto distant objects with accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grappling hook - a device with hooks used for pulling or securing objects, often thrown attached to a rope

Etymology

From grapple, itself from Old French grappil (hook), with -er denoting an agent or tool.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡræp.lər / (AmE), / ˈɡræp.lə / (BrE)
a person who engages in wrestling or a style of combat where opponents try to throw each other to the ground
person who wrestles or grapples

Examples

As a skilled grappler, she excels at taking opponents to the mat.
The tournament featured both grapplers and strikers from around the world.
He trained for years to become a top-level grappler in Brazilian jiu-jitsu competitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrestler - a person who wrestles, especially professionally
  • Antonym: striker - a fighter whose style focuses on striking rather than grappling

Etymology

From 'grapple', meaning to seize or struggle, and the agent noun suffix '-er'.
