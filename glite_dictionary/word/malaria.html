malaria

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɛriə / (AmE), / məˈlɛːriə / (BrE)
A serious infectious disease caused by parasites transmitted by the bite of infected Anopheles mosquitoes, characterized by recurring fever and chills.
infectious disease spread by mosquitoes
medical

Examples

Many travelers take precautions to avoid malaria when visiting tropical regions.
Researchers are developing new drugs to treat malaria more effectively.
The spread of malaria has been reduced in some areas through mosquito control programs.

Etymology

From Italian 'malaria', from 'mal' (bad) + 'aria' (air), originally believed to be caused by bad air from marshes.
