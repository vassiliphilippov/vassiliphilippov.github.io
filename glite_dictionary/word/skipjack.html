skipjack

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪpˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈskɪpˌdʒæk / (BrE)
an animal that is able to right itself when on its back by flipping into the air with a clicking sound
animal that rights itself by flipping

Examples

The skipjack amazed scientists by leaping to its feet after being flipped onto its back.
When threatened, a skipjack can escape danger by flipping itself upright with a loud snap.
Researchers observed the skipjack performing its remarkable righting maneuver in captivity.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɪpˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈskɪp.dʒæk / (BrE)
a small oceanic tuna (Katsuwonus pelamis), found especially in the Pacific, valued as a food fish and known for traveling in large schools
a species of oceanic tuna

Examples

Fishermen caught several skipjack off the coast during their morning trip.
The canning factory processes tons of skipjack each year for export.
A school of skipjack passed under the boat as we sailed across the Pacific.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonito - type of fish similar in appearance and habit to skipjack
  • Degree Higher: tuna - large group of oceanic fish including skipjack

Etymology

From 'skip' + 'jack' (early use for various lively fish).

3. NOUN

/ ˈskɪpˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈskɪp.dʒæk / (BrE)
A medium-sized, tuna-like fish found in warm Atlantic and Pacific waters, often used as food but considered less valuable than true tuna.
medium-sized tuna-like food fish

Examples

Fresh skipjack is often used in sushi dishes throughout Japan.
The fishermen hauled in a large net full of skipjack early in the morning.
Canned skipjack is a common and affordable alternative to more expensive tuna.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuna - large, commercially valuable saltwater fish related to skipjack
