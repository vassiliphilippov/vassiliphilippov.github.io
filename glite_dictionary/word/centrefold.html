centrefold

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntərˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈsɛntəˌfəʊld / (BrE)
A fold-out page, especially in the center of a magazine, often featuring a large photograph or special content.
center spread of a magazine
british

Examples

The latest issue features a stunning centrefold of a wildlife photograph.
She carefully removed the centrefold to frame it as a poster.
Many collectors seek magazines that still have their original centrefold intact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centerfold - a central fold-out spread, especially in American English

Etymology

From 'center' + 'fold', indicating the folded pages at the center of a publication.
