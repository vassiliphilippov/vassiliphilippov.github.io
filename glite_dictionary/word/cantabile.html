cantabile

1. ADJ

/ kænˈtɑːbɪˌleɪ / (AmE), / kanˈtaːbɪleɪ / (BrE)
(in music) in a smooth, flowing, and songlike style.
smooth and songlike (musical)

Examples

The pianist performed the passage cantabile, drawing out each note with graceful phrasing.
She encouraged the choir to sing the melody cantabile for greater emotional impact.
His violin solo was marked by a beautifully cantabile tone that captivated the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legato - played or sung smoothly and connectedly
  • Antonym: staccato - with each note sharply separated from the others

Etymology

From Italian 'cantabile' meaning 'singable', from 'cantare' (to sing), ultimately from Latin 'cantare'.
