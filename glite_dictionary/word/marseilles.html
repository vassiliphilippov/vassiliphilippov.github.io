Marseilles

1. PROPN

/ mɑrˈseɪlz / (AmE), / mɑːˈseɪlz / (BrE)
a port city in southeastern France on the Mediterranean Sea, known for its history and as a major commercial center
port city in southeastern France

Examples

We spent a week exploring the vibrant neighborhoods of Marseilles.
The ferry from Marseilles to Corsica departs every morning.
She moved to Marseilles to work in the shipping industry.

Etymology

From French 'Marseille', the city’s modern French name; the former English spelling 'Marseilles' reflects the French plural form historically used in English.
