to Simonise

1. VERB

/ ˈsaɪməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪmənaɪz / (BrE)
to polish (a motor vehicle) with wax, especially using a proprietary brand such as Simoniz, to give a glossy finish
polish a vehicle with wax for shine

Examples

He decided to Simonise his car before taking it to the show.
After she Simonised the old motorcycle, it looked almost new.
They spent the morning Simonising their classic vehicles for the parade.

Synset Examples

The motorcycle has been Simonized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polish - to make smooth and shiny by rubbing

Etymology

From the proprietary name 'Simoniz', an American brand of car care products, first used in the early 20th century.
