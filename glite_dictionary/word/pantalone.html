Pantalone

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæn.təˈloʊ.neɪ / (AmE), / ˌpantəˈlɒneɪ / (BrE)
a stock character in the commedia dell'arte, typically depicted as a foolish, miserly old man
commedia dell'arte foolish old man character

Examples

In the play, the actor took on the role of Pantalone, delighting the audience with his bumbling antics.
The character Pantalone is often recognized by his hooked nose and red tights.
Modern performances of commedia dell'arte still feature Pantalone as the comically miserly old man.

Etymology

Italian, diminutive of 'pantaleone', a given name; from commedia dell'arte tradition.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpæn.təˈloʊni / (AmE), / ˌpantəˈləʊni / (BrE)
A stock character in Italian commedia dell'arte and modern pantomimes, depicted as an old, foolish man who is often the butt of jokes.
stock character; foolish old man (theatre)

Examples

In the play, Pantalone was constantly tricked by the younger characters.
The actor's exaggerated gestures brought the foolish Pantalone to life.
Modern pantomimes often feature a comedic Pantalone as the target of practical jokes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Harlequin - comic servant character in commedia dell'arte

Etymology

From Italian Pantalone, a traditional character in commedia dell'arte; origin uncertain, possibly from Saint Pantaleon.
