singular

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪŋɡjələr / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
(Grammar) Referring to a form of a word that denotes one person or thing, as opposed to more than one (plural).
grammatical number for one item

Examples

The word 'cat' is in the singular form, while 'cats' is plural.
Use the singular verb when the subject refers to one person or thing.
In French, nouns change endings to mark singular and plural forms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plural - grammatical form for more than one

Etymology

From Latin 'singularis', from 'singulus' meaning single.
