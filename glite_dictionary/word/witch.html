witch

1. NOUN

/ wɪtʃ / (AmE), / wɪtʃ / (BrE)
An old woman who is considered to be ugly and evil-looking, often believed to have supernatural powers.
ugly, evil-looking old woman

Examples

The children were terrified of the witch who lived at the end of the village.
People often accused lonely old women of being a witch in medieval times.
Her costume made her look like a classic witch from fairy tales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hag - an ugly, old woman, often associated with evil or witchcraft

Etymology

From Old English *wicce* (female magician or sorceress).

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ wɪtʃ / (AmE), / wɪtʃ / (BrE)
a woman thought to have magical powers, often believed to have made a pact with the devil or other evil forces
woman with magical powers, often evil

Examples

In old stories, villagers feared that a witch could curse their crops.
She dressed as a witch for Halloween, complete with a tall pointed hat.
During the witch hunts, many innocent women were accused of being a witch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wizard - a man thought to have magical powers
  • Synonym: sorceress - a woman thought to use magic, especially evil magic
  • Synonym: warlock - a man thought to have magical powers, often male counterpart to witch

Etymology

Old English *wicce* (female witch), related to *wicca* (male witch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09526414-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ wɪtʃ / (AmE), / wɪtʃ / (BrE)
A female believer or practitioner of Wicca, a modern pagan religion involving nature-based rituals and beliefs.
female Wiccan practitioner

Examples

She considers herself a witch and celebrates the rituals of Wicca.
The local witch led the group in a ceremony honoring the solstice.
Many young women today identify as a witch and practice modern pagan traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warlock - a male practitioner of witchcraft or Wicca
  • Synonym: wiccan - a person who follows the religion of Wicca

Etymology

Old English *wicce* (female sorceress), later applied to followers of Wicca in the 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-81968711-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ wɪtʃ / (AmE), / wɪtʃ / (BrE)
To cast a spell or put a hex on (someone or something); to bewitch.
cast a spell or hex

Examples

The villagers believed she had witched their crops after a poor harvest.
He claimed that someone had witched his cows, causing them to fall ill overnight.
Legends say the old well is witched and should be avoided at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bewitch - to cast a spell over; to enchant
  • Synonym: curse - to invoke supernatural harm; to put a hex on
  • Antonym: to bless - to confer or invoke divine favor upon someone or something

Etymology

From Old English *wicce* (female magician, sorceress); related to folklore traditions.

5. NOUN

/ wɪtʃ / (AmE), / wɪtʃ / (BrE)
A woman who is believed to have magical powers, especially one who practices sorcery or is associated with witchcraft.
woman believed to have magical powers

Examples

In the story, a wicked witch cast a spell on the entire village.
Many people in the Middle Ages feared their neighbors might be a witch.
The child dressed up as a witch for Halloween, complete with a pointy hat and broomstick.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wizard - a man who has magical powers, especially in stories
  • Synonym: sorceress - a female sorcerer; a woman who practices magic

Etymology

Old English *wicce* (female magician or sorceress).
