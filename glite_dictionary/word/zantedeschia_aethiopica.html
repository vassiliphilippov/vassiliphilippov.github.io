Zantedeschia aethiopica

1. NOUN

/ zæn.tɪˈdɛski.ə ˌiːθiˈɑpɪkə / (AmE), / zan.tɪˌdɛski.ə ˌiːθɪˈɒpɪkə / (BrE)
a South African plant with large arrow-shaped leaves and showy funnel-shaped white flowers, widely grown as an ornamental; also called arum lily or calla lily.
South African plant with white spathe; arum lily

Examples

The garden was filled with Zantedeschia aethiopica, their pure white flowers shining all summer.
She arranged several Zantedeschia aethiopica in a vase for the wedding decorations.
Botanists admire Zantedeschia aethiopica for its striking contrast between the white spathe and yellow spadix.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calla lily - another common name for Zantedeschia aethiopica
  • Synonym: arum lily - alternative common name for Zantedeschia aethiopica

Etymology

From modern Latin genus name, after Italian botanist Giovanni Zantedeschi (1773–1846), and species name from Latin 'aethiopicus', meaning 'Ethiopian' or 'African'.
