Prunus virginiana demissa

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs vɜrˌdʒɪneɪnə dɪˈmɪsə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs vɜːˌdʒɪneɪnə dɪˈmɪsə / (BrE)
A variety of chokecherry native to the western United States, known for its astringent edible fruit.
chokecherry variety of western U.S.

Examples

The Prunus virginiana demissa thrives in the foothills and canyons of the western United States.
Many foragers collect the fruit of Prunus virginiana demissa to make jams and jellies.
Birds feed on the berries of Prunus virginiana demissa throughout the late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chokecherry - a North American wild cherry with astringent fruit

Etymology

From the genus name Latin *Prunus* (plum) and species/variety epithets from Latin.
