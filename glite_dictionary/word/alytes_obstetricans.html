Alytes obstetricans

1. NOUN

/ əˈlaɪtiːz ɒbˌstɛt̬rɪˈkænz / (AmE), / əˈlaɪtiːz ɒbˌstɛt.rɪˈkænz / (BrE)
A European toad in which the male carries fertilized eggs wrapped around its hind legs until they hatch.
European toad species with egg-carrying males

Examples

The Alytes obstetricans is well known for the male's unusual role in caring for eggs.
Scientists observed a male Alytes obstetricans carrying eggs on its hind legs in the wild.
Habitat loss is threatening populations of Alytes obstetricans in certain parts of Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midwife toad - Common name for Alytes obstetricans

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Alytes' meaning 'one who leaps' and species name 'obstetricans' meaning 'obstetric', referring to its unique egg-carrying behavior.
