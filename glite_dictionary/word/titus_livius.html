Titus Livius

1. PROPN

/ ˈtaɪ.təs lɪˈvaɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.təs lɪˈvɪ.i.əs / (BrE)
(59 BC–AD 17) A Roman historian best known for his multi-volume work 'Ab Urbe Condita,' chronicling the history of Rome from its foundation.
Roman historian (59 BC–AD 17)

Examples

Titus Livius wrote extensively about the founding of Rome and its early republic.
Many details of the Second Punic War come to us through the works of Titus Livius.
Titus Livius, often referred to simply as Livy, influenced later historians with his monumental writings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Livy - English form of Titus Livius, Roman historian

Etymology

Latin; Titus Livius is the original Roman name.
