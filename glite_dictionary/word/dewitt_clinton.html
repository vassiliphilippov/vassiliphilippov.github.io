DeWitt Clinton

1. PROPN

/ dəˈwɪt ˈklɪntən / (AmE), / dəˈwɪt ˈklɪntən / (BrE)
(1769–1828) An American politician who served as governor of New York and strongly supported the construction of the Erie Canal.
American politician and governor of New York

Examples

DeWitt Clinton played a major role in the development of the Erie Canal, transforming New York's economy.
Many historians credit DeWitt Clinton with helping to shape the infrastructure of early nineteenth-century America.
A statue of DeWitt Clinton stands in New York City to honor his contributions as governor.
