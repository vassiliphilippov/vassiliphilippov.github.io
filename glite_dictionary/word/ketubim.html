Ketubim

1. NOUN

/ kɛˈtuːbɪm / (AmE), / kɛˈtjuːbɪm / (BrE)
the third section of the Hebrew Scriptures, consisting of various writings including Psalms, Proverbs, and others
third division of Hebrew Scriptures

Examples

Scholars often study the Ketubim to understand the historical context of ancient Israel.
The book of Psalms is included in the Ketubim section of the Hebrew Bible.
During their studies, students compared the laws found in the Torah with the wisdom literature of the Ketubim.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Torah - the first section of the Hebrew Scriptures
  • Degree Lower: Nevi'im - the second section of the Hebrew Scriptures

Etymology

From Hebrew כְּתוּבִים (kəthūvīm), meaning 'writings'.
