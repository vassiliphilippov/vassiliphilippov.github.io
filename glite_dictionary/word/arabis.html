Arabis

1. NOUN

/ ˈærəbɪs / (AmE), / ˈærəbɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the mustard family, known as rockcress, found in temperate regions of North America, Europe, and Asia.
genus of plants (rockcress)

Examples

Several species of Arabis thrive on rocky slopes and are popular garden plants.
The botanist identified the specimen as part of the Arabis genus.
Most Arabis species have delicate white or pink flowers in early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rockcress - any plant of the genus Arabis or related genera

Etymology

From Latin 'Arabis', meaning 'Arabian', originally referring to an Arabian plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈærəbɪs / (AmE), / ˈærəbɪs / (BrE)
A genus of annual to perennial woody herbs, commonly known as rockcress, native to temperate regions of North America, Europe, and Asia.
a genus of plants (rockcress)

Examples

Several species of Arabis thrive in rocky alpine gardens.
The Arabis genus includes both annual and perennial plants.
Gardeners value Arabis for its attractive white or pink flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rockcress - common name for plants in the genus Arabis

Etymology

From Latin 'arabis', from Greek 'arabís', meaning a plant from Arabia.
