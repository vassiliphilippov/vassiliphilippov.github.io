Ojibway

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈdʒɪb.weɪ / (AmE), / əʊˈdʒɪb.weɪ / (BrE)
A member of an Indigenous Algonquian people living mainly around the northern Great Lakes region, especially in Canada and the northern United States.
member of an Algonquian Indigenous people

Examples

Many Ojibway still maintain their traditional languages and customs today.
The Ojibway lived in the forests and near the lakes of North America.
She is proud of her heritage as an Ojibway woman.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chippewa - an alternative name for the Ojibway people, primarily in the United States

Etymology

From Ojibwa (Ojibwe), the people's own name for themselves.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈdʒɪb.weɪ / (AmE), / əʊˈdʒɪb.weɪ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Ojibwa people, chiefly in the regions of southern Canada and the northern United States.
Algonquian language of the Ojibwa people

Examples

She has studied the Ojibway language to understand indigenous Canadian cultures.
Many traditional stories are still told in Ojibway among elders in the community.
The dictionary contains over 6,000 words from Ojibway and their English translations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ojibwa - the people who speak the Ojibway language

Etymology

From Ojibwa (Oji·bwe), the name of the people and their language.
