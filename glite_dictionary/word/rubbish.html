rubbish

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌb.ɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrʌb.ɪʃ / (BrE)
nonsensical or worthless talk or writing; nonsense
nonsense; absurd words or ideas

Examples

Everything he said about ghosts was pure rubbish.
Don't listen to her; that's complete rubbish!
The theory was dismissed as rubbish by the scientists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - words or ideas that have no meaning or sense
  • Antonym: sense - meaningful or reasonable words, as opposed to nonsense
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌb.ɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrʌb.ɪʃ / (BrE)
waste material; things that are no longer wanted or are considered worthless and are to be thrown away.
waste or unwanted material
british

Examples

Please put all your rubbish in the bin before you leave.
The park was cleaned up and all the rubbish was collected by volunteers.
It's amazing how much rubbish people throw away every day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garbage - waste material, especially food waste; unwanted material to be disposed of
  • Synonym: trash - waste material; things that are thrown away

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *robous*, related to Old French *rebous* (refuse, garbage).

3. VERB

/ ˈrʌbɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrʌbɪʃ / (BrE)
to criticize someone or something severely, often in a dismissive or contemptuous way
criticize harshly or dismissively
british

Examples

The critics rubbished the new play for its lack of originality.
She felt discouraged when her colleagues rubbished her proposal at the meeting.
The idea was quickly rubbished by experts in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to criticize - to express disapproval of someone or something
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone or something

Etymology

From noun 'rubbish' (waste material); extension to verb sense common in British English.

nonsense; worthless statements or things

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms
