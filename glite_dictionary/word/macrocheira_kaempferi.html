Macrocheira kaempferi

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækroʊˈkaɪrə kaɪmˈfɛri / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˈkaɪərə kamˈfɛri / (BrE)
a very large deep-water Japanese crab, also known as the Japanese spider crab, distinguished by its long legs and found in the waters around Japan
Japanese spider crab species

Examples

The Macrocheira kaempferi is famous for having the longest leg span of any marine crab.
Visitors to the aquarium were amazed by the sheer size of the Macrocheira kaempferi.
Marine biologists study the Macrocheira kaempferi to better understand deep-sea ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek 'makros' (large) and 'cheir' (hand), referring to its long legs, and named after German naturalist Engelbert Kaempfer.
