visibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪzəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌvɪzəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being able to be seen; the degree to which something is perceptible to the eye, especially in terms of distance or clarity.
degree to which something can be seen

Examples

Fog can greatly reduce visibility on the road, making driving dangerous.
The mountain was barely within visibility due to the thick mist.
In business, increasing your online visibility can attract more customers.

Synset Examples

low visibility caused by fog

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invisibility - the quality of not being visible

Etymology

From Latin 'visibilitas', from 'visibilis' (that can be seen).

Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɪzəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌvɪzɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the degree to which someone or something attracts public attention or is noticed by the public
degree of public attention or notice

Examples

The company increased its visibility with a bold marketing campaign.
Activists are working to improve the visibility of the issue in the media.
Her visibility in the organization helped her advance to a leadership position.

Synset Examples

that candidate does not have sufficient visibility to win an election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prominence - the state of being important, famous, or easily noticed
  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or inconspicuous

Etymology

From Latin visibilitas, from visibilis (visible).

3. NOUN

/ ˌvɪzəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌvɪz.əˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the extent to which something allows a clear, unobstructed view
degree of unobstructed view

Examples

The car's new windshield greatly improved visibility for the driver.
Low visibility made it difficult to see through the security camera.
Cleaning the windows restored the visibility in the conference room.

Synset Examples

a windshield with good visibility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to see

Etymology

From Latin visibilis (that can be seen) + -ity.

Synset POS: n
