Mesantoin

1. NOUN

/ mɛˈsæn.tɔɪn / (AmE), / mɛˈsan.tɔɪn / (BrE)
A toxic anticonvulsant drug used to treat epilepsy when safer alternatives are ineffective.
anticonvulsant drug for epilepsy
medical

Examples

Doctors sometimes prescribe Mesantoin for epilepsy when other medications fail to control seizures.
Due to its toxicity, Mesantoin is usually considered only after other anticonvulsants have been tried.
She experienced side effects from Mesantoin and had to switch to a different epilepsy medication.
