pentlandite

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛntˌlænˌdaɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛntlənˌdaɪt / (BrE)
a yellowish mineral consisting of iron and nickel sulphide, which is the chief ore of nickel
iron-nickel sulphide mineral; main source of nickel

Examples

The mining company extracts large quantities of pentlandite to obtain nickel for industrial use.
Geologists identified the mineral sample as pentlandite based on its color and crystalline structure.
As the chief source of nickel, pentlandite is an important economic mineral worldwide.

Etymology

Named after Scottish chemist Joseph Barclay Pentland (1797–1873).
