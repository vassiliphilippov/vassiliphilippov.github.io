to dehumanise

1. VERB

/ diˈhjuːmənˌaɪz / (AmE), / diːˈhjuːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to deprive someone of human qualities, dignity, or individuality
deprive of human qualities
british

Examples

The harsh conditions of the prison system can dehumanise inmates.
Propaganda is often used to dehumanise opponents in times of war.
Years of neglect and abuse dehumanised the workers at the factory.

Synset Examples

Life in poverty has dehumanized them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dehumanize - American English spelling of 'dehumanise'—to deprive of human qualities
  • Antonym: to humanize - to make more humane or civilised

Etymology

From de- (expressing removal) + human + -ise, modelled after French déshumaniser.

2. VERB

/ diˈhjuməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / diˈhjuːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to deprive (a person or thing) of human qualities, making it mechanical or impersonal
make impersonal or mechanical; strip of human qualities

Examples

The company's strict policies have begun to dehumanise the workplace, reducing employees to mere numbers.
Some critics argue that modern technology can dehumanise personal interactions by making them automated and routine.
Repetitive factory jobs often dehumanise workers, stripping away their sense of individuality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mechanize - make (a process or activity) operate automatically or like a machine

Etymology

From de- (removal) + human + -ise (verb-forming suffix).
