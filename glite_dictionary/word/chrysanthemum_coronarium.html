Chrysanthemum coronarium

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˌsænθəˌmʌm kəˈroʊnəriəm / (AmE), / krɪˌsanθɪˌmʌm kəˈrəʊnərɪəm / (BrE)
A shrubby annual plant native to the Mediterranean region, known for its yellowish-white, daisy-like flowers.
Mediterranean annual plant with yellowish-white flowers

Examples

The Chrysanthemum coronarium adds a splash of color to Mediterranean gardens in spring.
Traditionally, Chrysanthemum coronarium was used in garlands due to its attractive flowers.
She identified the plant as Chrysanthemum coronarium by its bright yellowish-white petals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garland chrysanthemum - Another common name for Chrysanthemum coronarium

Etymology

From Latin: 'chrysanthemum' (from Greek 'chrysos' meaning gold and 'anthemon' meaning flower) and 'coronarium' (relating to crowns or garlands).
