meniscectomy

1. NOUN

/ mɛnɪsˈɛktəmi / (AmE), / mɛnɪsˈɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove all or part of a meniscus in the knee joint
removal of knee meniscus (surgical)
medical

Examples

After injuring his knee during the game, he required a meniscectomy to repair the damage.
The doctor explained that a meniscectomy could help relieve her persistent joint pain.
Recovery from a meniscectomy usually involves physical therapy to restore full function to the knee.

Etymology

From meniscus + -ectomy (Greek ektomē, ‘excision’).
