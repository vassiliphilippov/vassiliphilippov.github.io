contredanse

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːntrɪˌdæns / (AmE), / ˈkɒntrɪˌdɑːns / (BrE)
A lively folk dance for couples arranged in sets, often facing each other in lines.
type of folk dance for couples in sets or lines

Examples

The musicians played a lively contredanse as the couples moved across the floor.
She learned the steps of a classic contredanse for the historical reenactment.
During the festival, villagers gathered in the square to perform a traditional contredanse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: country dance - a traditional English folk dance similar in style and arrangement

Etymology

From French 'contredanse', itself an alteration of English 'country dance'.

2. VERB

/ ˌkɑːn.trəˈdæns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trə.dɑːns / (BrE)
to perform a contradance, a type of lively partnered country dance originally from France
to perform a contradance

Examples

The dancers began to contredanse as the orchestra played a lively tune.
At the historical reenactment, guests were invited to contredanse in period costume.
She learned how to contredanse during her summer in France.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contradance - to perform a contradance (alternate form/spelling)

Etymology

From French 'contredanse', itself from English 'country dance', reborrowed into English with the French spelling and pronunciation.
