Jamaican

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈmeɪkən / (AmE), / dʒəˈmeɪkən / (BrE)
a person who is a native or inhabitant of Jamaica
a person from Jamaica

Examples

My friend is a proud Jamaican who loves to share stories about his homeland.
The new restaurant is run by a talented Jamaican chef.
She met a group of Jamaicans during her trip to Kingston.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jamaica - an island country in the Caribbean Sea

Etymology

From Jamaica + -an, denoting origin or relation.

2. ADJ

/ dʒəˈmeɪkən / (AmE), / dʒəˈmeɪkən / (BrE)
of or relating to Jamaica, its people, language, or culture
of or relating to Jamaica or its people

Examples

The Jamaican flag has green, black, and gold colors.
She loves listening to Jamaican music, especially reggae.
Many tourists enjoy the beautiful Jamaican beaches every year.

Synset Examples

Jamaican rum
the Jamaican Prime Minister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jamaica - the Caribbean island country

Etymology

From Jamaica + -an (suffix forming adjectives and demonyms).
