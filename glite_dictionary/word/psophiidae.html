Psophiidae

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈfaɪɪˌdiː / (AmE), / səʊˈfɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds known as trumpeters, native to South American forests and recognized for their loud, distinctive calls.
a family of South American birds (trumpeters)

Examples

The Psophiidae are known for their melodious and resonant calls in the rainforests of South America.
Scientists are studying the behavioral patterns of the Psophiidae to understand their communication methods.
Many bird enthusiasts travel to the Amazon to observe members of the Psophiidae family in their natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trumpeter - a bird of the family Psophiidae, known for its loud call

Etymology

From New Latin Psophiidae, from Psophia (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ soʊˌfaɪˈɪdiː / (AmE), / səʊˌfɪˈɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical South American birds, known as trumpeters, with long legs and soft plumage, inhabiting forests.
tropical bird family (trumpeters)

Examples

The Psophiidae are native to the rainforests of the Amazon basin.
Zoologists have studied the unique vocalizations of birds in the Psophiidae family.
Unlike other ground-dwelling birds, Psophiidae use their calls to communicate over long distances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trumpeter - a bird of the genus Psophia, notable for its loud calls

Etymology

From New Latin Psophiidae, based on genus Psophia (trumpeter bird).
