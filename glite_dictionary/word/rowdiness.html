rowdiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊdi nəs / (AmE), / ˈraʊdinəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being noisy and disorderly, often involving rough or boisterous behavior
noisy and disorderly behavior

Examples

The rowdiness of the crowd forced the organizers to call for more security.
After several complaints about rowdiness, the bar owner decided to close earlier on weekends.
Teachers struggled to control the rowdiness in the classroom during the holiday party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rowdy - noisy and disorderly
  • Antonym: orderliness - the quality of being orderly

Etymology

From 'rowdy' + '-ness.'
