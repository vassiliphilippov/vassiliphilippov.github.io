Ceratosaurus

1. NOUN

/ sɛˌrætəˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / sɛˌrætəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of primitive, carnivorous saurischian dinosaurs characterized by a prominent horn on the nose, known from the Jurassic period.
genus of horned carnivorous dinosaurs

Examples

The Ceratosaurus is recognizable by the large horn on its snout.
Fossils of Ceratosaurus have been found in North America and Africa.
Scientists believe Ceratosaurus was a swift predator during the Late Jurassic period.

Etymology

From Greek 'keras' (horn) + 'sauros' (lizard).
