2. ADJ

/ haɪ ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / haɪ ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
(of elementary particles or processes) having energies of hundreds of thousands of electron volts or more; relating to or involving such high energies.
referring to particles or phenomena with very high energies

Examples

Researchers studied the behavior of high-energy protons in the particle accelerator.
The experiment requires a high-energy environment to observe rare particle interactions.
Detection of high-energy cosmic rays provides insights into astrophysical phenomena.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low-energy - referring to particles or phenomena with lower energies

3. ADJ

/ haɪˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / haɪˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
(of a substance or reaction) providing or producing a large amount of energy when undergoing a chemical change.
producing large amounts of energy (chemically)

Examples

The fuel used in rockets is a high-energy compound that releases a substantial amount of heat.
Researchers developed a high-energy battery to improve electric vehicle performance.
Explosives are considered high-energy materials due to their rapid release of energy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low-energy - producing or providing a small amount of energy when undergoing a chemical reaction
