Acer spicatum

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪsər spaɪˈkeɪtəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪsə spɪˈkɑːtəm / (BrE)
A small shrubby maple tree native to eastern North America, known for its scarlet autumn foliage.
shrubby maple tree of eastern North America

Examples

The leaves of Acer spicatum turn a brilliant red in the fall.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Acer spicatum in the Appalachian region.
Gardeners often plant Acer spicatum for its vibrant autumn color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain maple - another common name for Acer spicatum

Etymology

From Latin: 'Acer' meaning 'maple', 'spicatum' meaning 'spiked', referring to the arrangement of its flowers.
