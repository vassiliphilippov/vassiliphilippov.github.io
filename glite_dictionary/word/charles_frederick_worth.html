Charles Frederick Worth

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɑrlz ˈfrɛdrɪk wɜrθ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlz ˈfrɛdrɪk wɜːθ / (BrE)
(1825–1895) French couturier, originally from England, regarded as the founder of Parisian haute couture and known for introducing the bustle.
founder of Parisian haute couture

Examples

Charles Frederick Worth is considered the father of modern haute couture and transformed 19th-century fashion.
Many of the elaborate gowns created by Charles Frederick Worth are now displayed in fashion museums worldwide.
The introduction of the bustle in women's dresses was a defining achievement of Charles Frederick Worth's career.
