William Tell

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm tɛl / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm tɛl / (BrE)
a legendary Swiss folk hero of the early 14th century, famed for shooting an apple off his son's head with a crossbow as ordered by an Austrian governor.
legendary Swiss folk hero and archer

Examples

The legend of William Tell symbolizes resistance against tyranny in Swiss culture.
Many operas and books recount the exploits of William Tell and his famous crossbow shot.
Tourists in Switzerland often visit sites associated with William Tell.

Etymology

From German Wilhelm Tell, traditional name of the legendary Swiss hero.
