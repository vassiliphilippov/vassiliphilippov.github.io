polyphony

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪfəni / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪfəni / (BrE)
Music that combines several independent melodies played or sung at the same time.
music with two or more independent melodies

Examples

The choir performed a beautiful polyphony that echoed through the cathedral's arches.
Renaissance composers are well known for their use of polyphony in sacred music.
Learning to sing in polyphony can be challenging, as each vocal part is independent.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monophony - music with a single melodic line
  • Antonym: homophony - music where voices move together in harmony

Etymology

From Greek poluphōnia, from poly- (many) + phōnē (sound, voice).
