scramaseax

1. NOUN

/ skræməˌsiː.æks / (AmE), / skræməˌsiː.æks / (BrE)
A single-edged knife or short sword used by the Anglo-Saxons for fighting and hunting.
Anglo-Saxon fighting knife or sword

Examples

The museum displayed a beautifully preserved scramaseax that once belonged to an Anglo-Saxon warrior.
Historians believe the scramaseax was used for both combat and everyday tasks.
He admired the craftsmanship of the decorated hilt on the ancient scramaseax.

Etymology

From Old English *scramasax*, from Proto-Germanic *skrama- (skirmish, scratch) + *sahs* (knife).
