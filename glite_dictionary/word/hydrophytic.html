hydrophytic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈfɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəˈfɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of a plant) growing either wholly or partly in water or very wet conditions.
growing in water or wet conditions (of plants)

Examples

The lotus is a classic example of a hydrophytic plant found in ponds and lakes.
Certain hydrophytic species can survive in flooded wetlands for long periods.
Rice cultivation relies on the ability of the crop to grow in hydrophytic conditions.

Synset Examples

water lilies are hydrophytic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aquatic - (of plants or animals) living or growing in water
  • Synonym: hydrophyte - a plant that grows only in water or very wet conditions

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro' (water) + 'phytos' (plant).
