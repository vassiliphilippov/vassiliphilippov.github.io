Alstroemeria pelegrina

1. NOUN

/ ælˌstroʊˈmɪəriə pɛˈlɛɡrɪnə / (AmE), / alˌstrəʊˈmɪəriə pɛˈliːɡrɪnə / (BrE)
A species of Andean herb with clusters of showy, pinkish-purple, lily-like flowers.
Andean plant with pinkish-purple lily-like flowers

Examples

The garden featured a stunning bed of Alstroemeria pelegrina in full bloom.
Botanists studied the native habitat of Alstroemeria pelegrina along the Andean slopes.
She chose Alstroemeria pelegrina for its vibrant pinkish-purple flowers in her floral arrangement.

Etymology

From the genus name Alstroemeria, named after Swedish botanist C. Alströmer; species name from Latin 'pelegrina', meaning 'wanderer' or 'foreign'.
