pilocarpine

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪloʊˈkɑːrpiːn / (AmE), / ˌpɪləˈkɑːpiːn / (BrE)
a cholinergic alkaloid used chiefly in eyedrops to treat glaucoma and dry mouth
alkaloid used to treat glaucoma
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed pilocarpine eye drops to manage her glaucoma.
Some patients experience sweating as a side effect of pilocarpine.
Pilocarpine can also be used to treat dry mouth caused by radiation therapy.

Etymology

From Latinized International Scientific word 'Pilocarpus' (plant genus) + '-ine' (chemical suffix).
