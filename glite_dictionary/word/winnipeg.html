Winnipeg

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪnɪˌpɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈwɪnɪˌpɛɡ / (BrE)
A large lake in southern Manitoba, Canada.
large lake in Manitoba, Canada

Examples

Lake Winnipeg is one of the largest freshwater lakes in Canada.
Hundreds of bird species can be found around Winnipeg each year.
Fishermen travel to Winnipeg for its abundant walleye and other fish species.

Etymology

From Cree win-nipi (muddy water).

2. PROPN

/ ˈwɪnəˌpɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈwɪnɪpɛɡ / (BrE)
the capital and largest city of Manitoba, Canada, located in southern Manitoba and noted for its severe winters.
capital city of Manitoba, Canada

Examples

After moving to Winnipeg, she quickly adapted to the cold winter climate.
Winnipeg hosts several festivals throughout the year, attracting visitors from across Canada.
The flight from Toronto to Winnipeg takes about two and a half hours.

Etymology

Named after Lake Winnipeg; from Cree words for 'muddy water'.
