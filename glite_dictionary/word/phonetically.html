phonetically

1. ADV

/ fəˈnɛtɪkli / (AmE), / fəˈnɛtɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or is according to the sounds of speech; by means of phonetics.
in a way relating to speech sounds

Examples

The word is spelled differently but phonetically it sounds the same as in English.
Children who cannot read words phonetically may rely on memorizing their appearance.
Linguists analyze how speech is phonetically realized in various languages.

Synset Examples

phonetically realized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonetic - relating to speech sounds or pronunciation

Etymology

From phonetic + -ally, from Greek phōnētikos (vocal) + -ally (adverbial suffix).
