Charadrii

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrædriˌaɪ / (AmE), / kəˈrædriˌiː / (BrE)
A group of shorebirds that includes plovers, sandpipers, avocets, phalaropes, coursers, and stone curlews, classified as a suborder or clade in bird taxonomy.
a group of shorebirds (plovers, sandpipers, etc.)

Examples

Several species within the Charadrii are recognized for their long migratory journeys.
Researchers found that nesting patterns varied among different members of the Charadrii.
Field guides often list plovers and sandpipers under the suborder Charadrii.

Etymology

From modern Latin Charadrii, based on Ancient Greek kharadrios (a bird found in river valleys).
