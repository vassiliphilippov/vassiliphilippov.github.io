Numididae

1. NOUN

/ nuːˈmɪdəˌdiː / (AmE), / njuːˈmɪdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds that includes guinea fowl and their relatives, native to Africa and Madagascar.
family of birds including guinea fowl

Examples

The family Numididae includes several species of wild and domesticated guinea fowl.
Birdwatchers visiting Madagascar may spot rare members of the Numididae family.
Studies of Numididae provide insight into the evolutionary history of galliform birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guinea fowl - a bird of the family Numididae native to Africa

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Numidia, an ancient region in North Africa, with the zoological suffix -idae for animal families.

2. NOUN

/ nuːˈmɪdəˌdiː / (AmE), / njuːˈmɪdɪdiː / (BrE)
a subfamily of birds including the guinea fowl and related species, native to Africa and Madagascar
bird subfamily (guinea fowl etc.)

Examples

The Numididae are known for their distinct spotted plumage and social behavior among African aves.
Farmers in Madagascar sometimes keep species belonging to Numididae as domesticated poultry.
Ornithologists have recently discovered a new species within the Numididae subfamily.

Etymology

From New Latin Numididae, from Numida (type genus) + -idae (taxonomic suffix for families).
