antibacterial

1. ADJ

/ ˌæntiˌbækˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˌbækˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
destroying bacteria or preventing their growth.
inhibiting or destroying bacteria
medical

Examples

This soap contains antibacterial agents to help prevent the spread of germs.
She bought an antibacterial spray to clean the kitchen surfaces.
Certain fabrics are treated with antibacterial chemicals to reduce odor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - a substance that kills or inhibits the growth of microorganisms, especially bacteria
  • Degree Lower: bacterial - relating to or caused by bacteria
  • Degree Higher: bactericidal - able to kill bacteria

Etymology

From anti- (against) + bacterial (relating to bacteria).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæntibaɪˈtɛriəl / (AmE), / ˌantɪbaiˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
A substance or drug that destroys bacteria or inhibits their growth.
substance that kills or inhibits bacteria
medical

Examples

This cream contains a powerful antibacterial to help prevent infection in minor cuts.
Doctors often prescribe an antibacterial for certain types of skin infections.
The antibacterial in the handwash helps reduce the spread of germs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - a drug used to treat bacterial infections

Etymology

From anti- (against) + bacterial (relating to bacteria).

Synset POS: n
