Soren Kierkegaard

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɔːrən ˈkɪrkəˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈsɔːrən ˈkɪəkəˌɡɑːd / (BrE)
(1813–1855) Danish philosopher regarded as a founder of existentialism, known for his exploration of faith, ethics, and individuality.
Danish existentialist philosopher (1813–1855)

Examples

Many scholars credit Soren Kierkegaard as a pioneer of existentialist thought in Western philosophy.
In his writings, Soren Kierkegaard emphasized the importance of personal choice and responsibility.
The works of Soren Kierkegaard continue to influence theology and philosophy today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Friedrich Nietzsche - German philosopher, also considered a founder of existentialism

Etymology

From Danish Søren Kierkegaard, his given name and surname.
