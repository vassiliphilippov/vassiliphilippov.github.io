Haganah

1. PROPN

/ hɑːɡəˈnɑː / (AmE), / həˈɡɑːnə / (BrE)
the underground Jewish paramilitary organization in British Mandate Palestine from 1920 to 1948, which became the foundation of the Israeli Defense Forces
Jewish paramilitary group in British Mandate Palestine

Examples

The Haganah played a pivotal role in defending Jewish communities during the British Mandate period.
Many leaders of the Israeli Defense Forces began their military careers in the Haganah.
Historians often study the transition from the Haganah to the modern Israeli army.

Etymology

From Hebrew הַהֲגָנָה (HaHaganah) meaning 'the defense'.
