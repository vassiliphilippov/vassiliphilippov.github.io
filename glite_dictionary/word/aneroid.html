aneroid

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈænərɔɪd / (BrE)
a barometer that measures atmospheric pressure without the use of liquid
barometer without liquid

Examples

The ship's captain checked the aneroid before deciding to sail.
An aneroid is preferred over a mercury barometer for portable use.
My grandfather kept an old aneroid on his study wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barometer - an instrument measuring atmospheric pressure

Etymology

From French 'anéroïde', from Greek 'a-' (without) + 'neron' (water).

WordNet ID: oewn-02713459-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈænəˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈanərɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a measuring instrument) not using or containing any liquid, typically referring to barometers or similar devices.
not using liquid (esp. instruments)

Examples

The scientist preferred using an aneroid barometer for field work because it did not rely on mercury.
An aneroid gauge is commonly used to measure pressure in modern machinery.
Unlike mercury barometers, aneroid instruments are safer and more portable.

Synset Examples

aneroid barometer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liquid - a measuring device using liquid, especially a liquid barometer

Etymology

From French 'anéroïde', from Greek 'a-' (without) + 'neron' (water).
