extinct

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstɪŋkt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋkt / (BrE)
(of a volcano) permanently inactive, having stopped erupting and showing no signs of future activity.
permanently inactive (of volcano)

Examples

The scientists declared the volcano extinct after centuries without any eruptions.
Mount Slemish is classified as an extinct volcano by geologists.
Unlike active or dormant volcanoes, an extinct volcano is not expected to erupt again.

Synset Examples

an extinct volcano

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dormant - temporarily inactive (of a volcano)

Etymology

From Latin 'extinctus', past participle of 'extinguere' meaning 'to put out, quench'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00041273-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstɪŋkt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋkt / (BrE)
no longer in existence; having died out so that no individuals or examples remain
no longer existing; died out

Examples

The dodo is an extinct bird that once lived on the island of Mauritius.
Many fear that tigers could soon become extinct unless stronger conservation efforts are made.
That language is now extinct, with no native speakers left alive.

Synset Examples

an extinct species of fish
an extinct royal family
extinct laws and customs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alive - living; still existing or active
  • Degree Lower: endangered - at risk of extinction

Etymology

From Latin 'extinctus', past participle of 'extinguere' (to extinguish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00932405-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstɪŋkt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋkt / (BrE)
(of a fire, light, or similar) no longer burning or active; having gone out or grown cold.
no longer burning; gone out

Examples

He threw his extinct cigarette onto the ground before walking away.
The campfire was extinct by the time we woke up in the morning.
After hours of rain, the once raging blaze became extinct and safe to approach.

Synset Examples

threw his extinct cigarette into the stream
the fire is out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out - (of a fire or light) no longer burning or illuminated

Etymology

From Latin 'exstinctus', past participle of 'exstinguere' (to extinguish).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
