antigenic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tɪˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈdʒen.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting substances (antigens) that stimulate an immune response in the body
relating to antigens or immune response
medical

Examples

The scientist studied the antigenic properties of the virus to develop a new vaccine.
There are significant antigenic differences between strains of the same bacteria.
Some mutations can alter the antigenic profile of a pathogen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antigen - a substance which induces an immune response

Etymology

From 'antigen' + '-ic', forming an adjective relating to antigens.
