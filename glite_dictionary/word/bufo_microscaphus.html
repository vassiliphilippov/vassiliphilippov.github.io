Bufo microscaphus

1. NOUN

/ ˌbjuːfoʊ ˌmaɪ.krəˈskæf.əs / (AmE), / ˌbjuːfəʊ ˌmaɪ.krəˈskæf.əs / (BrE)
a small, uniformly warty toad found in washes and streams of semiarid regions in the southwestern United States
a toad species of the southwestern US

Examples

The Bufo microscaphus inhabits the rocky washes of Arizona and Utah.
Researchers observed several Bufo microscaphus in the streambed after a summer rainstorm.
Unlike other toads, Bufo microscaphus tends to stay close to permanent water sources.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Bufo' (the genus, Latin for toad) and Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'skaphos' (boat), indicating morphological features.
