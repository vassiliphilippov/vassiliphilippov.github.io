Gaviiformes

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæviˈɪfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌɡaviˈɪfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of large aquatic birds that includes loons and some extinct related species
order of aquatic birds including loons

Examples

The Gaviiformes are well known for their distinctive wailing calls across northern lakes.
Research on Gaviiformes has increased our understanding of aquatic bird evolution.
Fossils of extinct members of the Gaviiformes order have been discovered in ancient lakebeds.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on genus Gavia (loon) + -formes (having the form of).
