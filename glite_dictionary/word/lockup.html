lockup

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑkˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈlɒkˌʌp / (BrE)
the act of securing something by locking it to ensure its safety or protection
the act of locking for protection

Examples

Daily lockup of the tools is required to prevent theft.
The lockup of the building each night is part of the security guard's duties.
Make sure the lockup procedure is followed after closing the store.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: securing - the act of making something safe or protected
  • Antonym: unlocking - the act of opening something that was locked

Etymology

From 'lock' + 'up', indicating the act of making secure by locking.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːkˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈlɒk.ʌp / (BrE)
a room or building in a police station where people are kept under temporary detention; a small jail
temporary jail at a police station

Examples

After the arrest, the suspect was held in the lockup overnight.
The local lockup can only hold a few prisoners at a time.
He spent a few hours in the lockup before being released on bail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jail - a place where people are kept as punishment for a crime or while waiting for trial
  • Synonym: cell - a small room in a prison or police station where a prisoner is kept

Etymology

From 'lock up', meaning to secure by locking; used from the 18th century for places of confinement.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03690066-n
Synset POS: n
