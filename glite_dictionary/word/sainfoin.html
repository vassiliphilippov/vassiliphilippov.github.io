sainfoin

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪnˌfɔɪn / (AmE), / ˈseɪn.fɔɪn / (BrE)
a Eurasian perennial herb with pale pink flowers, cultivated as a forage crop and source of honey, especially in Britain and North America
leguminous plant used as forage

Examples

The farmer planted sainfoin to improve soil fertility and feed his livestock.
Bees are often attracted to fields of sainfoin because of its abundant nectar.
In some British meadows, sainfoin grows alongside other native wildflowers.

Etymology

From French 'sainfoin', from 'sain' (wholesome) + 'foin' (hay).
