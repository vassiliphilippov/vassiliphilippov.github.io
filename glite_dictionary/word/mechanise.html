to mechanise

1. VERB

/ ˈmek.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛk.ə.nʌɪz / (BrE)
to introduce machines or automatic devices into (a process, industry, etc.) to replace manual labor; to make mechanical
introduce machines; make mechanical

Examples

The company decided to mechanise its packaging process for greater efficiency.
Many farms have mechanised to reduce the need for manual labor.
The factory is mechanising its assembly line to boost production.

Synset Examples

mechanize the procedure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to automate - to use automatic equipment instead of manual labor

Etymology

From mechanism + -ise, modeled on French 'mécaniser' and Italian 'meccanizzare'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00480529-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈmɛkəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmiːkənaɪz / (BrE)
to equip (an army or military force) with motor vehicles, especially armored ones, to increase mobility and firepower
equip with military vehicles

Examples

After the reforms, the military began to mechanise its infantry divisions to improve their combat effectiveness.
Many nations rushed to mechanise their armies before the start of the Second World War.
Efforts to mechanise the cavalry met with resistance from some traditional officers.

Synset Examples

mechanize armies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to motorize - to equip with motor vehicles (more general)

Etymology

From Greek mēkhanē (machine), via French 'mécaniser' or directly from English 'mechanize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00481682-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈmɛkəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛkənaɪz / (BrE)
to make (an activity or process) automatic or routine, especially to the point of being monotonous
make automatic or routine; make monotonous

Examples

His daily routine had become so mechanised that he barely noticed the passage of time.
The new manager was determined not to mechanise the creative process.
Over time, long meetings can mechanise innovation by discouraging spontaneous thinking.

Synset Examples

If your work becomes too mechanized, change jobs!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to automatize - to make automatic, especially by using machines or automation
  • Antonym: to humanize - to make something less mechanical and more humane

Etymology

From Greek mēkhanē (machine) via French mécaniser.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00481500-v
Synset POS: v
