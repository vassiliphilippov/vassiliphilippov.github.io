Metrazol

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtrəˌzɔl / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrəzɒl / (BrE)
A drug formerly used as a circulatory and respiratory stimulant and, in larger doses, to induce convulsions in shock therapy; trademark name for pentylenetetrazol.
stimulant drug and shock therapy inducer
medical

Examples

Metrazol was once widely used in psychiatric hospitals for convulsive therapy.
Doctors discontinued the use of Metrazol after safer alternatives became available.
The severe reactions to Metrazol led to controversy over its medical use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pentylenetetrazol - the chemical name for Metrazol, a convulsant drug

Etymology

Trademark derived from chemical name (pentylenetetrazol).
