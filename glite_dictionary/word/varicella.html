varicella

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛr.əˈsɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˌvar.ɪˈsɛl.ə / (BrE)
An acute, highly contagious disease caused by the varicella-zoster virus, marked by a fever and a vesicular rash, commonly known as chickenpox.
medical term for chickenpox
medical

Examples

The hospital reported several new cases of varicella among school-aged children this week.
Vaccination has greatly reduced the incidence of varicella in many countries.
After exposure to varicella, symptoms usually appear within two weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chickenpox - common name for varicella, a contagious viral disease with a rash

Etymology

From New Latin, from Latin 'varius' (variegated) + '-ella' (diminutive suffix).
