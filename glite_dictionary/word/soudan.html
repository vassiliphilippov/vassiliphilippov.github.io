Soudan

1. PROPN

/ suːˈdæn / (AmE), / suːˈdæn / (BrE)
a historical region of northern Africa, lying south of the Sahara and Libyan deserts and extending from the Atlantic Ocean to the Red Sea.
historic region in northern Africa

Examples

In the 19th century, explorers traveled across the vast expanse of the Soudan in search of Timbuktu.
French colonial forces established control over large parts of the Soudan region.
The peoples of the Soudan developed diverse cultures and languages adapted to the Sahel environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sudan - country in northeastern Africa, named after the larger region

Etymology

From Arabic 'bilād as-sūdān' meaning 'land of the blacks'.

2. PROPN

/ suˈdɑn / (AmE), / suˈdɑːn / (BrE)
an alternative or archaic spelling of Sudan, a country in northeastern Africa bordering the Red Sea, which became independent in 1956.
alternative spelling of Sudan (country in Africa)
archaic

Examples

In the nineteenth century, explorers traveled deep into Soudan seeking the source of the Nile.
Historical documents often refer to present-day Sudan as Soudan.
The ancient kingdoms of Soudan thrived along the banks of the Nile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sudan - modern spelling for the country in northeastern Africa

Etymology

Variant from French 'Soudan', based on the Arabic 'bilād as-sūdān' (land of the blacks).
