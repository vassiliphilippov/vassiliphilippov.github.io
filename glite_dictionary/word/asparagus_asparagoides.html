Asparagus asparagoides

1. NOUN

/ əˈspærəɡəs əˌspærəˈɡɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / əˈspærəɡəs æˌspærəˈɡɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A fragile, twining plant native to South Africa, with bright green flattened stems and glossy foliage, often used as a floral decoration.
twining South African plant used in floral decoration

Examples

Asparagus asparagoides is often used in wedding bouquets for its attractive leaves.
Many florists value Asparagus asparagoides for its durability and elegant appearance.
In some countries, Asparagus asparagoides has become invasive outside its native range.

Etymology

Botanical Latin; genus name from Greek 'asparagos' (asparagus).
