deuterium

1. NOUN

/ duːˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / djuːˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
An isotope of hydrogen with one neutron in its nucleus, used in nuclear reactors and scientific research.
hydrogen isotope with one neutron

Examples

Heavy water contains a higher proportion of deuterium than ordinary water.
Scientists use deuterium in experiments to trace chemical reactions.
The fusion reaction in the sun involves both hydrogen and deuterium nuclei.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrogen - the lightest chemical element, atomic number 1

Etymology

From Greek 'deuteros' (second), referring to its status as a second form of hydrogen.
