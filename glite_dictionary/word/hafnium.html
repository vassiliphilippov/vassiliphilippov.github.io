hafnium

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæfniəm / (AmE), / ˈhæfniəm / (BrE)
a metallic chemical element (symbol Hf), silvery-gray in color, resembling zirconium and used especially in filaments and nuclear reactors
a metallic chemical element (Hf)

Examples

Hafnium is valued for its ability to absorb neutrons in nuclear reactors.
The laboratory ordered a new supply of hafnium for their research on advanced filaments.
Because of its high melting point, hafnium is used in specialized alloys and equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zirconium - a chemical element (Zr), similar to hafnium and found in the same minerals

Etymology

From New Latin 'Hafnia', the Latin name for Copenhagen, where it was discovered.
