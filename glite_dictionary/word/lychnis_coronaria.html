Lychnis coronaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪk.nɪs ˌkɔː.rəˈneɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈlɪk.nɪs ˌkɒ.rəˈneɪ.ə / (BrE)
an ornamental plant native to southeastern Europe, widely grown for its white woolly leaves and bright crimson flowers; also known as rose campion
a plant with woolly leaves and crimson flowers

Examples

Lychnis coronaria brightens up gardens in early summer with its vivid crimson blooms.
The silver foliage of Lychnis coronaria provides a lovely contrast in a flower border.
Many cottage gardens feature Lychnis coronaria for its low maintenance and striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rose campion - common name for Lychnis coronaria

Etymology

From Greek 'lychnis' (lamp, due to the leaf shape or flower color), and Latin 'coronaria' (used for garlands).
