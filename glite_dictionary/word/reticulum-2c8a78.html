reticulum

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɪkjələm / (AmE), / rɪˈtɪkjʊləm / (BrE)
A fine network, especially of cells, fibers, or blood vessels in the body.
fine network of cells or fibers
medical

Examples

The spleen contains a reticulum of fibers supporting its cellular structure.
Microscopic examination revealed a reticulum formed by delicate connective tissue.
The scientist studied the reticulum of blood vessels within the tissue sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: network - an interconnected system or group of things

Etymology

From Latin 'reticulum' (a little net), diminutive of 'rete' (net).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɪkjələm / (AmE), / rɪˈtɪkjələm / (BrE)
the second compartment of the stomach in ruminant animals, situated between the rumen and omasum, involved in the digestion process
second compartment of ruminant stomach
medical

Examples

The cow's reticulum helps break down fibrous plant material before further digestion.
A veterinarian examined the sheep's reticulum to check for foreign objects or disease.
Food passes from the rumen to the reticulum as part of the digestion process in ruminants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumen - the first compartment of a ruminant's stomach
  • Synonym: omasum - the third compartment of a ruminant's stomach
  • Synonym: abomasum - the fourth compartment of a ruminant's stomach

Etymology

From Latin reticulum (little net), referring to its net-like inner structure.
