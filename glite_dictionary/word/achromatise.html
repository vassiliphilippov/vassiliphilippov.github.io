to achromatise

1. VERB

/ əˈkroʊməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / əˈkrəʊmətaɪz / (BrE)
to remove color from something, especially to correct a lens so that it does not produce color distortions
remove color, especially from a lens

Examples

Optical engineers often achromatise lenses to reduce color fringing in images.
The company uses special coatings to achromatise its camera lenses.
It is possible to achromatise glass by combining different materials.

Synset Examples

achromatize the lenses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to achromatize - remove color from (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'chrōma' (color), influenced by 'achromatic'.
