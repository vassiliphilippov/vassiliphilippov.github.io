Aconitum lycoctonum

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɑnɪtəm laɪˌkɒktənəm / (AmE), / əˈkɒnɪtəm lɪˌkɒktənəm / (BrE)
A poisonous perennial herb native to Eurasia, with broad rounded leaves, yellow flowers, and a fibrous rootstock.
Eurasian poisonous herb with yellow flowers

Examples

The roots of Aconitum lycoctonum contain potent toxins, making the plant dangerous if ingested.
Researchers studied Aconitum lycoctonum to understand its effects on livestock in the region.
In some alpine meadows, Aconitum lycoctonum can be identified by its distinctive yellow flowers.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Aconitum' and species 'lycoctonum', meaning 'wolf-slayer' (from Greek λύκος 'lykos' – wolf and κτείνω 'kteino' – to kill).
