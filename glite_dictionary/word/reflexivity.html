reflexivity

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːflɛkˈsɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌriːflɛkˈsɪvəti / (BrE)
(logic and mathematics) The property of a relation whereby it holds between any element and itself.
the property of relating an element to itself

Examples

In mathematics, equality has the property of reflexivity because any number is equal to itself.
The reflexivity of the relation means every set is related to itself.
Understanding reflexivity is crucial when studying equivalence relations in logic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symmetry - property of a relation holding in both directions
  • Synonym: transitivity - property where relation holds between A and C if it holds between A and B and B and C

Etymology

From Latin 'reflexivus' (turned back).

2. NOUN

/ ˌriːflɛkˈsɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌriːflɛkˈsɪvɪti / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The relationship in which a reflexive pronoun refers back to its antecedent within the same clause or sentence.
relation of reflexive pronoun to antecedent

Examples

The reflexivity in the sentence 'She saw herself in the mirror' is displayed by the pronoun 'herself'.
Teachers often explain reflexivity using examples with subjects referring to themselves.
Understanding reflexivity is essential for studying sentence structure in English grammar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflexive pronoun - a pronoun that refers back to the subject (e.g. 'himself')

Etymology

From Latin reflexivus (bent back).
