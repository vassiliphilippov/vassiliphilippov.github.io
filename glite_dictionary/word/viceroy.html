viceroy

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪsɪˌrɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈvaɪsərɔɪ / (BrE)
a North American butterfly, Limenitis archippus, resembling the monarch butterfly but smaller and with a black line across its hind wings.
a monarch-like North American butterfly

Examples

A viceroy can be distinguished from a monarch by the black line crossing its hind wings.
The viceroy mimics the appearance of the monarch butterfly to deter predators.
We spotted a viceroy resting on a milkweed plant in the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monarch butterfly - large orange-and-black North American butterfly, Danaus plexippus

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪsˌrɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈvaɪs.rɔɪ / (BrE)
A governor of a country or province who rules as the representative of a king or sovereign.
governor ruling for a king or sovereign

Examples

The Spanish crown appointed a viceroy to administer its colonies in the New World.
During British rule, the viceroy of India lived in a grand palace in Delhi.
After the rebellion, the new viceroy enacted sweeping administrative reforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: governor - an official appointed to govern a region or colony

Etymology

From Latin 'vice-' (in place of) + Old French 'roi' (king).
