muscle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a person employed as a thug or bodyguard, often to use physical force or intimidation on behalf of someone
a thug or bodyguard; hired brute

Examples

The mafia boss always traveled with his trusted muscle to ensure his safety.
They sent in some muscle to scare the witnesses into silence.
Without his muscle around, the gangster seemed much less intimidating.

Synset Examples

the drug lord had his muscleman to protect him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscleman - a strongman or hired thug/bodyguard

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.əl / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.l̩ / (BrE)
A type of animal tissue consisting mainly of cells that contract to cause movement in parts of the body.
tissue that contracts to produce movement

Examples

He strained a muscle in his leg while running the marathon.
Flexing your muscle is a common way to show strength.
Athletes often train to build more muscle and improve performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tendon - a cord of strong tissue connecting muscle to bone
  • Synonym: ligament - a band of tissue connecting bones or supporting organs

Etymology

From Latin musculus (little mouse, muscle).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌsəl / (BrE)
A band or bundle of tissue in the body that has the ability to contract, producing movement or maintaining the position of parts of the body.
body tissue that contracts to produce movement

Examples

Regular exercise strengthens your muscle and improves overall health.
She strained a muscle while lifting heavy boxes.
The heart is a powerful muscle that pumps blood throughout the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tendon - cord of strong tissue connecting muscle to bone
  • Synonym: ligament - band of tissue connecting bones or holding joints together
  • Antonym: bone - rigid organ that forms part of the skeleton

Etymology

From Latin 'musculus' meaning 'little mouse', referring to the shape of some muscles when they contract.

5. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.əl / (BrE)
power, influence, or authority, especially when applied forcefully or coercively to achieve a goal.
power or influence (figurative)

Examples

The union used its muscle to negotiate a better contract for its members.
The company showed political muscle by persuading lawmakers to change the regulations.
They don’t have enough muscle in the organization to push that policy through.

Synset Examples

the senators used their muscle to get the party leader to resign

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clout - informal term for influence or power
  • Synonym: influence - the capacity to affect the behavior of others
  • Synonym: authority - the power or right to give orders or make decisions
  • Antonym: weakness - lack of strength or power

Etymology

From Latin 'musculus' (little mouse, muscle); figurative sense developed from the idea of physical strength.
