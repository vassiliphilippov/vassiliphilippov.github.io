northeastward

1. ADV

/ ˌnɔrθˈistwɚd / (AmE), / ˌnɔːθˈiːstwəd / (BrE)
towards the northeast; in a northeast direction
in a northeast direction

Examples

The river flows northeastward until it reaches the lake.
Migrating birds tend to travel northeastward in the spring.
The storm moved northeastward across the coast, bringing heavy rain.

Synset Examples

the river flows northeastward to the gulf

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: northward - in a north direction
  • Degree Lower: eastward - in an east direction

Etymology

From 'northeast' + 'ward' (directional suffix).

2. ADV

/ ˌnɔrθˈistwɚd / (AmE), / ˌnɔːθˈiːstwəd / (BrE)
Toward or in the direction of the northeast.
toward the northeast

Examples

The storm is expected to move northeastward along the coast tonight.
Migrating birds travel northeastward in the spring.
Our expedition continued northeastward toward the mountain range.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northwestward - toward the northwest
  • Antonym: southeastward - toward the southeast
  • Synonym: northeasterly - in a north-easterly direction

Etymology

Formed from 'northeast' + '-ward', denoting direction.

3. NOUN

/ ˌnɔrθˈistwərd / (AmE), / ˌnɔːθˈiːstwəd / (BrE)
The direction or point on the compass that is midway between north and east, at an angle of 45 degrees.
compass point between north and east

Examples

They traveled in a northeastward direction to reach the coast.
The ship anchored at a point due northeastward of the lighthouse.
Birds migrate along a northeastward route in the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northeast - the compass point between north and east (NE, 45°)
  • Degree Lower: north-northeast - the compass point between north and northeast (NNE, 22.5°)
  • Degree Higher: east-northeast - the compass point between north and east (ENE, 67.5°)

Etymology

From 'north' + 'east' + 'ward', indicating direction toward the northeast.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13854042-n
Synset POS: n
