proxy

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑksi / (AmE), / ˈprɒksi / (BrE)
a person who is authorized to act on behalf of another person, especially in matters such as voting or attending meetings
person authorized to act for another

Examples

You may appoint a proxy to vote at the meeting if you cannot attend yourself.
She acted as his proxy during the negotiations with the board.
A proxy must present proper identification in order to represent someone else.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agent - a person who acts on behalf of another
  • Antonym: principal - a person for whom another acts as an agent

Etymology

From Middle English 'procuracie', from Anglo-Norman 'procuracie', from Latin 'procuratio' (administration, management).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑksi / (AmE), / ˈprɒksi / (BrE)
a written authorization given by a shareholder allowing another person to vote on their behalf at a corporate meeting
authority to vote on behalf of someone else
legal

Examples

The shareholder was unable to attend the meeting, so she sent her proxy to vote in her place.
Each investor must submit a proxy form if they cannot participate in person.
The board accepted the proxy as valid for determining the outcome of the vote.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power of attorney - a legal document giving one person authority to act for another

Etymology

From Middle English 'procuracie', via Anglo-Norman, from Latin 'procuratio' (management, care).
