Rhizopogon

1. NOUN

/ raɪˈzɑpəˌɡɑn / (AmE), / raɪˈzɒpəˌɡɒn / (BrE)
A genus of fungi characterized by underground, tuber-like spore-producing bodies.
genus of fungi with tuber-like sporophores

Examples

Several species in the Rhizopogon genus form symbiotic relationships with pine trees.
The truffle-like appearance of Rhizopogon fungi makes them difficult to locate in the wild.
Researchers studied the soil to find Rhizopogon spores associated with forest roots.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhiza' (root) + 'pogon' (beard), referring to the appearance of the fungi.

2. NOUN

/ ˌraɪzoʊˈpoʊɡən / (AmE), / ˌrʌɪzəʊˈpəʊɡən / (BrE)
A genus of fungi that produce underground fruiting bodies resembling tubers.
a genus of subterranean fungi

Examples

The rare Rhizopogon species plays an important role in forest ecosystems.
Researchers discovered a new Rhizopogon growing beneath pine trees.
Many animals feed on the tuber-like sporophores of Rhizopogon fungi.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhiza' (root) + 'pogon' (beard), referring to the root-like appearance of some species.
