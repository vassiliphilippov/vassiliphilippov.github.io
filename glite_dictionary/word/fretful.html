fretful

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrɛtfəl / (AmE), / ˈfrɛtfʊl / (BrE)
often feeling or expressing worry, dissatisfaction, or complaint; irritable or restless
irritable, often complaining

Examples

The fretful baby cried all night and could not be comforted.
She grew fretful as the hours passed without hearing any news.
Travel delays can make even the calmest person feel fretful.

Synset Examples

a whiny child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Antonym: calm - free from agitation or worry

Etymology

From fret + -ful; 'fret' from Old English 'fretan' (to devour, eat away).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfrɛtfəl / (AmE), / ˈfrɛtfʊl / (BrE)
feeling or expressing distress, worry, or impatience; unable to relax or remain calm
nervous or unable to relax

Examples

The fretful baby cried all night, making it hard for her parents to sleep.
His fretful behavior during the exams worried his teachers.
After hours of waiting, she grew fretful and started pacing the room.

Synset Examples

a constant fretful stamping of hooves
a restless child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restless - unable to rest or relax; continuously moving or worried
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other emotions

Etymology

From Old English *fretan* (to devour, gnaw), later influenced by 'fret' (to be irritated).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02414962-s
Synset POS: s
