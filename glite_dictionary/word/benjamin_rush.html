Benjamin Rush

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɛndʒəmɪn rʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɛndʒəmɪn rʌʃ / (BrE)
(1745–1813) An American physician, educator, and Revolutionary leader, known for signing the Declaration of Independence and advancing medical and social reforms.
American physician and Revolutionary leader

Examples

Benjamin Rush was a prominent figure in the American Revolution and a signer of the Declaration of Independence.
The hospital was named after Benjamin Rush to honor his contributions to medicine.
Many historians have studied the life of Benjamin Rush and his influence on early American society.
