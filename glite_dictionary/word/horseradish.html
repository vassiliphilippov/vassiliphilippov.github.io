horseradish

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔːrˌsˌrædɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsˌrædɪʃ / (BrE)
a pungent root used as a seasoning, typically grated or ground to make a spicy condiment
pungent root used as seasoning

Examples

She added a spoonful of horseradish to the sauce for extra flavor.
The roast beef was served with a side of freshly grated horseradish.
Some people find horseradish too spicy for their taste.

Etymology

From horse (used to indicate large size or coarseness) + radish (from Latin radicula, diminutive of radix 'root').

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔːrsˌrædɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsˌrædɪʃ / (BrE)
A coarse plant of Eurasian origin, cultivated for its thick, pungent white root that is often grated and used as a condiment.
pungent root plant used as condiment

Examples

Grated horseradish adds a spicy kick to roast beef sandwiches.
She planted horseradish in her garden for homemade sauces.
Fresh horseradish has a much stronger flavor than the store-bought variety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wasabi - A green, pungent Japanese condiment sometimes called 'Japanese horseradish'

Etymology

From horse (implying large or coarse) + radish, first used in English in the 16th century.
