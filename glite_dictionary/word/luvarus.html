Luvarus

1. NOUN

/ luːˈvɛərəs / (AmE), / luːˈvɑːrəs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Luvaridae, a group of large marine fish known for their distinctive shape
genus of marine fish (Luvaridae)

Examples

The genus Luvarus is best known for its sole living species, the louvar.
Researchers studied the evolutionary history of Luvarus using fossil evidence.
Luvarus specimens are rare in museum collections due to their deep-sea habitat.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek 'louros', a kind of fish, used in scientific taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ luːˈvɛərəs / (AmE), / luːˈvɑːrəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Luvaridae, comprising certain species of large marine fish.
type genus of Luvaridae (marine fish)

Examples

The Luvarus genus includes species known as louvar, found in warm ocean waters.
Marine biologists recently discovered a juvenile Luvarus near the Mediterranean coast.
Fish of the genus Luvarus are notable for their unique body shape and large size.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Luvaridae - the family of marine fishes including Luvarus

Etymology

From modern Latin Luvarus, the genus name, based on a local or common name for the species.
