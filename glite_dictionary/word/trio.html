trio

1. NOUN

/ ˈtri.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈtriː.əʊ / (BrE)
A musical composition written for three performers or instruments.
musical work for three performers

Examples

The string trio performed an enchanting piece by Beethoven at the concert.
She composed a piano trio for her graduation recital.
Each member of the jazz trio brought a unique style to the performance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: duet - a musical composition for two performers
  • Degree Higher: quartet - a musical composition for four performers

Etymology

From Italian 'trio', from Latin 'tres' (three).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtriː.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈtriː.əʊ / (BrE)
A group of three people or things considered as a single unit, especially in music or performance.
group of three people or things

Examples

The comedy trio entertained the audience for over an hour.
She formed a jazz trio with two of her classmates.
They always worked together as an inseparable trio of friends.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: duo - a group of two people or things
  • Degree Higher: quartet - a group of four people or things

Etymology

From Italian 'trio', from Latin 'tres' (three).

group of three performers

Examples

Related Terms
