junker

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋkər / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkə / (BrE)
a person who collects or repairs junk, especially for resale or hobby purposes
person who collects or repairs junk

Examples

The junker spent hours at the scrapyard searching for parts.
Growing up, my uncle was a real junker, always fixing up old appliances he found.
They called her a junker because her garage was full of salvaged bicycles and radios.

Synset Examples

The junker spent hours at the scrapyard searching for parts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scavenger - a person who searches for and collects discarded items

Etymology

From 'junk' (discarded items, rubbish) + '-er' (agent suffix).
