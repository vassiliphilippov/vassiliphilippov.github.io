misapplication

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsˌæplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˌæplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of using something in an incorrect or inappropriate way
incorrect or inappropriate use

Examples

The misapplication of the drug can lead to serious health risks.
There was a misapplication of funds in the project, which caused an audit.
Her theory failed due to the misapplication of a key principle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misuse - improper or incorrect use of something
  • Antonym: proper use - correct or suitable use of something

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + application, first recorded in the early 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsˌæplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˌæplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the dishonest or improper use of funds or property entrusted to one's care, especially for personal gain
fraudulent use of entrusted funds/property
legal

Examples

The accountant was charged with the misapplication of company funds.
His misapplication of client assets led to a lengthy investigation.
The trust sued the fiduciary for the misapplication of property entrusted to him.

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'application', from Latin 'applicare' (to attach).
