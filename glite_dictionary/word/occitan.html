Occitan

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑksɪˌtæn / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪtæn / (BrE)
the group of Romance dialects, especially those spoken in medieval southern France, collectively known as Langue d'oc.
medieval dialects of southern France

Examples

In medieval poetry, Occitan was the language of the troubadours.
Many historical texts from southern France are written in Occitan.
He studied the grammar and vocabulary of Occitan for his research on medieval literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Provençal - a dialect of Occitan spoken in Provence
  • Degree Higher: French - the Romance language spoken in most of France

Etymology

From Latin 'occitanus', referring to the Occitania region where the language was spoken.
