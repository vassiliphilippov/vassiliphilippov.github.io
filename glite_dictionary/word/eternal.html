eternal

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈtɜr.nəl / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜː.nəl / (BrE)
Seeming to last forever, especially so long as to be tedious or tiresome; endless.
seeming to last forever; endlessly long

Examples

After what felt like an eternal wait, the doctor finally arrived with the results.
The meeting dragged on for an eternal two hours before it finally ended.
Standing in the eternal line, I wondered if I would ever get my coffee.

Synset Examples

endless debates
an endless conversation
the wait seemed eternal
eternal quarreling
an interminable sermon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endless - having or seeming to have no end
  • Synonym: interminable - endless or seeming to be endless (often used about something tedious)

Etymology

From Latin aeternalis, from aeternus (everlasting, eternal).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈtɜrnəl / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜːnəl / (BrE)
lasting or existing forever; without end or beginning
lasting forever; without end

Examples

Some philosophers claim that the soul is eternal and will never perish.
The conflict seemed eternal, stretching on with no resolution in sight.
Shakespeare's works are considered to have eternal relevance.

Synset Examples

the ageless themes of love and revenge
eternal truths
life everlasting
hell's perpetual fires
the unending bliss of heaven

Related Terms

  • Synonym: everlasting - lasting forever; perpetual
  • Synonym: perpetual - continuing forever or for a very long time without stopping
  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period; not permanent

Etymology

From Latin aeternalis, from aevum ('age, lifetime').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01759536-s
Synset POS: s
