to dispense

1. VERB

/ dɪˈspɛns / (AmE), / dɪˈspɛns / (BrE)
to prepare and give out (medications) to people, especially by a pharmacist or medical professional
to give out medications
medical

Examples

The pharmacist will dispense your prescription once it has been verified.
Doctors are not allowed to dispense medication directly to patients in some countries.
She was trained to dispense medicines safely and answer patients' questions.

Etymology

From Latin 'dispensare' (to distribute, administer), from 'dis-' (apart) + 'pensare' (to weigh).

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈpɛns / (AmE), / dɪsˈpɛns / (BrE)
to distribute or give out something, especially in portions or shares
give out or distribute (in portions)

Examples

The vending machine will dispense a can of soda when you insert a coin.
The pharmacist is responsible for dispensing medication to patients with valid prescriptions.
At the shelter, volunteers dispensed food and water to those in need after the storm.

Synset Examples

administer critical remarks to everyone present

deal a blow to someone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distribute - to give out among recipients
  • Synonym: to administer - to give or apply something, especially medicine or justice
  • Antonym: collect - to gather or bring together

Etymology

From Latin dispensare (to distribute, manage).

3. VERB

/ dɪsˈpɛns / (AmE), / dɪsˈpɛns / (BrE)
to give official permission for someone not to follow a rule, law, or usual requirement; to grant an exemption or dispensation
to grant an exemption from a rule

Examples

After much deliberation, the authority dispensed him from attending the mandatory meetings.
The bishop dispensed her from the usual fasting rules due to her illness.
Special circumstances may allow officials to dispense individuals from jury duty.

Synset Examples

I was dispensed from this terrible task

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exempt - to free someone from an obligation or rule
  • Antonym: to enforce - to make someone obey a rule or law

Etymology

From Latin dispensare (to distribute, manage), from dis- (apart) + pendere (to weigh).
