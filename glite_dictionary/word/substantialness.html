substantialness

1. NOUN

/ səbˈstænʃəl.nəs / (AmE), / səbˈstænʃəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being substantial; having substance, weight, or importance
quality of being substantial or having substance

Examples

The substantialness of his argument impressed the entire committee.
She appreciated the substantialness of the meal after a long day of work.
The substantialness of the evidence led to a swift verdict in the trial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substance - the quality of having a solid basis
  • Antonym: insubstantiality - the lack of substance or firmness

Etymology

From 'substantial' + '-ness', where 'substantial' ultimately derives from Latin 'substantia' (substance).
