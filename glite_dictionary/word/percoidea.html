Percoidea

1. NOUN

/ pɚˈkɔɪ.di.ə / (AmE), / pəˈkɔɪ.di.ə / (BrE)
A superfamily of fishes within the order Perciformes, encompassing many species, especially perches and their relatives.
superfamily of perch-like fishes

Examples

Many freshwater species belonging to the Percoidea are popular among anglers.
Recent studies have revised the classification of the Percoidea superfamily.
The diversity of Percoidea is most apparent in their fin and jaw structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Perciformes - the largest order of fishes, including perches and basses

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Percus ('perch') and the suffix -oidea (resembling or related to).

2. NOUN

/ pɚˈsɔɪdiə / (AmE), / pəˈsɔɪdɪə / (BrE)
a suborder of spiny-finned fishes, sometimes nearly or exactly equivalent to the order Perciformes in some classifications
a suborder of spiny-finned fishes

Examples

The Percoidea includes many economically important species of fish.
Taxonomists debate whether Percoidea should be a separate suborder or synonymous with Perciformes.
She specialized in the anatomy of Percoidea fishes during her doctoral studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Perciformes - the largest order of fishes, also known as perch-like fishes

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Perco-, from Latin 'perca' (perch) and -oidea (resembling form).
