renegade

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛnɪˌɡeɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛnɪɡeɪd / (BrE)
a person who deserts a party, cause, or set of principles, often becoming an outlaw or rebel
a rebel or outlaw; a deserter

Examples

The notorious renegade led his followers into the mountains after betraying his former allies.
She was branded a renegade for defying the group's traditions and joining their rivals.
As a young man, he became a renegade, turning his back on authority and living as an outlaw.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebel - a person who rises in opposition against authority
  • Antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to a party or cause

Etymology

From Spanish renegado, from Medieval Latin renegatus, past participle of renegare (to deny).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛnɪˌɡeɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛnɪɡeɪd / (BrE)
a person who deserts and betrays an organization, country, or set of principles; a traitor or defector.
a person who betrays or deserts a cause

Examples

Some considered him a renegade for joining the rival company.
The political party expelled the renegade who leaked confidential plans.
Once a respected member, she became a renegade by speaking against the organization's values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traitor - a person who betrays a friend, country, principle, etc.
  • Antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to a cause or organization

Etymology

From Spanish renegado, from Medieval Latin renegatus, past participle of renegare ('to deny').

having deserted a cause or principle

Examples

A renegade faction has withdrawn its support for the coalition government.

4. VERB

/ ˈrɛnɪˌgeɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛnɪgeɪd / (BrE)
to abandon or reject established customs, beliefs, or allegiances; to act as a traitor.
abandon established customs or beliefs

Examples

He chose to renegade against the traditions of his family and forge his own path.
Several artists decided to renegade from the strict conventions of their movement.
Faced with mounting pressure, she renegaded against the established rules and followed her beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defect - to leave one's allegiance or group in favor of another
  • Antonym: to conform - to comply with established customs or rules

Etymology

From Spanish 'renegado' (apostate), from Medieval Latin 'renegatus', past participle of 'renegare' (to deny).
