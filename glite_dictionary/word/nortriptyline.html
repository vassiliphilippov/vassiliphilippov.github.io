nortriptyline

1. NOUN

/ nɔrˈtrɪptɪˌlaɪn / (AmE), / nɔːˈtrɪptɪˌliːn / (BrE)
a tricyclic antidepressant drug used, often together with psychotherapy, to treat depression and certain mood disorders
tricyclic antidepressant drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed nortriptyline to help manage her chronic depression.
Some patients experience fewer side effects with nortriptyline compared to other antidepressants.
It is important not to mix nortriptyline with certain other medications without consulting your physician.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amitriptyline - another tricyclic antidepressant drug used to treat depression and other conditions

Etymology

From 'nort-' (referring to the removal of a methyl group) and 'amitriptyline', the parent compound.
