a fortiori

1. ADV

/ ˌɑ fɔrˈtiˌɔraɪ / (AmE), / ˌɑː fɔː.tiˈɔː.raɪ / (BrE)
used to express a conclusion for which there is stronger evidence than for a previously accepted one; with even greater reason or certainty.
with even greater reason or certainty

Examples

If the first proposal was rejected, a fortiori the second, weaker one will be as well.
If John cannot solve the problem, then a fortiori it is unlikely anyone else can.
She was upset about missing the meeting and, a fortiori, about not being informed beforehand.

Synset Examples

if you are wrong then, a fortiori, so am I

Etymology

Latin, literally 'from the stronger (argument)'.
