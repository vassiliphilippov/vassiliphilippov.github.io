to solicit

1. VERB

/ səˈlɪsɪt / (AmE), / səˈlɪsɪt / (BrE)
to ask someone for something earnestly or repeatedly; to try to obtain something from someone by persistent requests
request earnestly or persistently

Examples

The charity will solicit donations from local businesses to support its cause.
Teachers often solicit feedback from students to improve their lessons.
He has solicited signatures for the petition all week.

Synset Examples

Henry IV solicited the Pope for a divorce
My neighbor keeps soliciting money for different charities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: request - to ask for something politely or formally
  • Degree Lower: to beg - to ask someone earnestly or humbly for something

Etymology

From Latin sollicitare (to disturb, rouse, solicit), from sollicitus (anxious).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00784104-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ səˈlɪsɪt / (AmE), / səˈlɪsɪt / (BrE)
to approach someone, especially in a public place, with an offer of sexual services, usually for payment
offer sexual services for payment

Examples

The police arrested her for trying to solicit men outside the nightclub.
It is illegal to solicit for prostitution in this area.
He was fined for attempting to solicit clients on the street.

Synset Examples

he was solicited by a prostitute
The young man was caught soliciting in the park

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostitute - a person who offers sexual services for payment

Etymology

From Latin sollicitare (to disturb, to trouble).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00783699-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ səˈlɪs.ɪt / (AmE), / səˈlɪs.ɪt / (BrE)
to ask for or try to obtain something from someone, especially in a formal or persistent way
ask for or try to obtain something

Examples

Nonprofit organizations often solicit donations from the public to fund their activities.
The politician decided to solicit feedback from her constituents before making a decision.
It is not legal to solicit money on this property without a permit.

Synset Examples

She is too shy to solicit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: request - ask for something politely or formally
  • Synonym: petition - make a formal request for something
  • Antonym: offer - present or propose something for acceptance or rejection

Etymology

From Latin sollicitare (to disturb, rouse, entreat), from sollus (whole) + citare (to set in motion).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00755779-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ səˈlɪs.ɪt / (AmE), / səˈlɪs.ɪt / (BrE)
to approach someone with the intention of making amorous or sexual advances, often in a persistent or unwelcome way
make amorous or sexual advances towards

Examples

The lawyer was dismissed from the bar for attempting to solicit women clients inappropriately.
He was fined for trying to solicit passersby in the park late at night.
It is illegal to solicit strangers for sexual favors in most countries.

Synset Examples

John is courting Mary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court - to try to win the love or affection of someone, often with the intention of marriage
  • Synonym: proposition - to make a sexual advance or offer to someone

Etymology

From Latin 'sollicitare' (to disturb, agitate, solicit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02540158-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ səˈlɪsɪt / (AmE), / səˈlɪsɪt / (BrE)
to incite, persuade, or urge someone to commit an unlawful or disobedient act
urge to commit an illegal act

Examples

He was arrested for attempting to solicit others to break the law.
The official was accused of soliciting his staff to cover up the crime.
It is illegal to solicit someone to commit fraud.

Synset Examples

He was accused of soliciting his colleagues to destroy the documents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to incite - to encourage or stir up unlawful behavior
  • Synonym: to induce - to lead or move by persuasion or influence

Etymology

From Latin sollicītāre (to agitate, disturb).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00773178-v
Synset POS: v
