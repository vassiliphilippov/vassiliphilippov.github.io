lusterless

1. ADJ

/ ˈlʌstɚləs / (AmE), / ˈlʌstələs / (BrE)
lacking brightness, shine, or vitality; dull or uninspired.
lacking brightness or vitality

Examples

The once vibrant painting now appeared lusterless after years of neglect.
His lusterless eyes revealed his exhaustion.
The meeting was marred by a lusterless presentation from the main speaker.

Synset Examples

a dull lackluster life
a lusterless performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - lacking brightness, vividness, or excitement
  • Synonym: lackluster - lacking in vitality, force, or conviction; uninspired
  • Antonym: shiny - having a bright or glossy surface

Etymology

From 'luster' (from Latin 'lustrare', to make bright) + '-less' (without).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlʌstərˌlɪs / (AmE), / ˈlʌstəlɪs / (BrE)
having no shine, brightness, or gloss; dull or lacking luster
dull; lacking shine or brightness

Examples

Her lusterless hair hung limply over her shoulders, showing signs of fatigue.
After years of neglect, the painting had become faded and lusterless.
The actor's lusterless performance failed to excite the audience.

Synset Examples

staring with lackluster eyes
lusterless hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - lacking brightness, vividness, or sheen
  • Antonym: shiny - having a bright, glowing, or reflective surface
  • Synonym: lackluster - lacking in vitality, force, or luster

Etymology

Formed from 'luster' (from Latin 'lustrare', to illuminate) + '-less', a suffix meaning 'without'.
