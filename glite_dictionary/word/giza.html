Giza

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡiːzə / (AmE), / ˈɡiːzə / (BrE)
An ancient city in Egypt on the west bank of the Nile, famous as the site of the three Great Pyramids and the Sphinx.
ancient Egyptian city known for pyramids and the Sphinx

Examples

The Great Pyramid of Giza is one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
Many tourists travel to Giza to marvel at the Sphinx and the pyramids.
Archaeologists continue to study the ancient tombs and artifacts found in Giza.

Etymology

From Arabic الجيزة (al-Jīza), meaning 'the side' or 'the plateau'.
