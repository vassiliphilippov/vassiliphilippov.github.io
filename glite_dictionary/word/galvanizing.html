galvanizing

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡæl.vəˌnaɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡal.və.nʌɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
causing a person or group to become excited or motivated, often suddenly and intensely
causing excitement or motivation

Examples

The coach's galvanizing speech turned the team's attitude around.
Her galvanizing presence brought hope to the entire community.
The announcement had a galvanizing effect on the employees, who began working with renewed energy.

Synset Examples

gave an electric reading of the play
the new leader had a galvanic effect on morale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspiring - encouraging enthusiasm or creativity
  • Antonym: demotivating - making someone less enthusiastic or interested

Etymology

From galvanize, meaning to stimulate as if by electric shock, from Italian physiologist Luigi Galvani.
