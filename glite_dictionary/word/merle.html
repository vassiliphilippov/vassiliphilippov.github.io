merle

1. NOUN

/ mɜrl / (AmE), / mɜːl / (BrE)
A common black European thrush (Turdus merula), known for its melodious song; also called the Eurasian blackbird.
common black European thrush

Examples

A solitary merle perched on the garden fence, singing its morning song.
The merle is easily recognized by its glossy black plumage and yellow beak.
In spring, the mellow notes of the merle fill the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackbird - the Eurasian blackbird, Turdus merula

Etymology

From Old French 'merle', from Latin 'merula' (blackbird).
