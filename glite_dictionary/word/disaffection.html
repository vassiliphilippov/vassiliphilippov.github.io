disaffection

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.əˈfɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈfɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
a state of being dissatisfied or alienated, often resulting in a lack of loyalty or involvement
lack of satisfaction or alienation

Examples

Political disaffection often grows when citizens feel their voices are not being heard.
The manager tried to address employee disaffection before it affected productivity.
After the scandal, there was noticeable disaffection among formerly loyal supporters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alienation - a feeling of being isolated or estranged from a group or activity
  • Antonym: loyalty - a strong feeling of support or allegiance

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'affectio' (relation, disposition).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.əˈfɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈfɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
lack of loyalty or support for a government or established authority; the state of being disaffected.
lack of loyalty to authority

Examples

The general sensed growing disaffection among the ranks after the failed campaign.
Years of corruption led to widespread disaffection toward the ruling party.
Public disaffection was evident in the protests that swept the country.

Synset Examples

the widespread disaffection of the troops

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loyalty - strong feeling of support or allegiance
  • Degree Lower: dissatisfaction - a feeling of not being content or pleased
  • Degree Higher: rebellion - open resistance to authority, often involving violence

Etymology

From Latin 'disaffectio' via French 'désaffection' (loss of affection).
