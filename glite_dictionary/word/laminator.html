laminator

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæm.əˌneɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈlæm.ɪ.neɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person or device that makes laminates by bonding layers of material together, especially plastics
person or machine making laminates

Examples

The factory hired an experienced laminator to oversee the production of plastic sheets.
Each laminator must follow strict safety guidelines when operating the machinery.
After training, he became a skilled laminator in the furniture manufacturing industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laminate - material made by bonding layers together

Etymology

From 'laminate' (to layer) + '-or' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæmɪˌneɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlæmɪneɪtə / (BrE)
A machine used to apply a protective or decorative layer of plastic or other material over an object, especially paper.
machine for applying a protective layer

Examples

We used the laminator to protect the important documents from getting damaged.
The school purchased a new laminator for the teachers to use in the resource room.
After printing the posters, she ran them through the laminator for a glossy finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laminate - to cover with a thin layer of material
  • Synonym: lamination - the process of laminating

Etymology

From 'laminate', from Latin 'lamina' (layer), plus '-or', denoting an agent or device.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-92448669-n
Synset POS: n
