snakeroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈsneɪk.ruːt / (BrE)
any of various North American plants of the genus Liatris, characterized by racemes or panicles of small discoid flower heads
a North American plant of the genus Liatris

Examples

Native Americans used the roots of snakeroot for medicinal purposes.
The tall purple spikes of snakeroot add color to summer gardens.
In the wild, snakeroot thrives in prairies and open woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blazing star - another common name for plants of the genus Liatris

Etymology

From 'snake' + 'root', referring to the traditional belief that the plant could treat snakebites.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪkˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈsneɪk.ruːt / (BrE)
a North American plant of the genus Sanicula, with palmately compound leaves and small unisexual flowers in umbels, formerly believed to have medicinal properties
a plant of the genus Sanicula with reputed medicinal uses

Examples

Early settlers used snakeroot in herbal remedies, believing it could cure snakebites.
Clusters of pink-white snakeroot bloom in the woodland each spring.
The healer brewed a tea from snakeroot and wild herbs to treat fevers.

Etymology

Named for its historical use as a remedy for snakebite.
