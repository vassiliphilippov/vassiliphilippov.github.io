allegorizer

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɛɡəˌraɪzər / (AmE), / əˈlɛɡəraɪzə / (BrE)
a person who expresses ideas or communicates messages through allegories
one who communicates in allegories

Examples

As a skilled allegorizer, the author layered meaning into every line of his story.
The allegorizer turned simple fables into deeper social commentary.
Some critics dismiss his work, but others praise him as a modern allegorizer of complex themes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegorist - person who writes or interprets allegories

Etymology

From 'allegorize' (to interpret or express as allegory) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
