complin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmplɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪn / (BrE)
the last of the seven canonical hours in the Christian liturgy, said or sung just before retiring for the night
last canonical hour before retiring

Examples

The monks gathered in the chapel each night for complin before going to bed.
Visitors were welcome to join the community for Vespers and complin.
The peace of complin marked the formal end of the monastery's day.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'completorium' (completion), referring to the completion of the day's prayer.
