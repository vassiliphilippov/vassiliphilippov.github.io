acrid

1. ADJ

/ ˈækrɪd / (AmE), / ˈækrɪd / (BrE)
(of language or tone) harsh, bitter, or caustic; expressing criticism or hostility in an unpleasantly sharp way.
harsh or bitter in tone

Examples

Her acrid comments during the meeting offended several colleagues.
The review was acrid, sparing no criticism of the film's flaws.
He answered the question with an acrid tone that surprised everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

an acerbic tone piercing otherwise flowery prose
a barrage of acid comments
her acrid remarks make her many enemies
bitter words
blistering criticism
caustic jokes about political assassination, talk-show hosts and medical ethics
a sulfurous denunciation
a vitriolic critique

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caustic - (of remarks) severely critical or sarcastic
  • Synonym: acerbic - (of speech or tone) sharp and forthright
  • Antonym: gentle - (of tone or language) mild or kind

Etymology

From Latin acrid(us) 'sharp, pungent'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈækrɪd / (AmE), / ˈækrɪd / (BrE)
having a strong and unpleasantly sharp taste or smell; bitter or pungent.
strong, unpleasant taste or smell

Examples

The acrid smoke from the fire irritated my eyes.
She wrinkled her nose at the acrid odor coming from the laboratory.
The soup had an acrid aftertaste that lingered unpleasantly.

Synset Examples

the pungent taste of radishes
the acrid smell of burning rubber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pungent - having a strong, sharp taste or smell
  • Antonym: mild - not strong or sharp in taste or smell

Etymology

From Latin 'acer' or 'acris' (sharp, pungent).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02407331-s
Synset POS: s
