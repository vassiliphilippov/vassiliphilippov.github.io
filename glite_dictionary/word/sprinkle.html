sprinkle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋkəl / (BrE)
an act of scattering small drops of liquid or particles over a surface, often water
act of scattering drops or particles

Examples

The ceremony began with a gentle sprinkle of holy water on the congregation.
A sprinkle of rain was enough to refresh the garden in the morning.
There was a light sprinkle of water on the dusty floor after cleaning.

Synset Examples

baptized with a sprinkling of holy water
a sparge of warm water over the malt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - act of scattering liquid, especially water, in drops

Etymology

From Middle English sprenklen, frequentative of sprengen ‘to scatter’ (of Germanic origin).

2. VERB

/ ˈsprɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋkəl / (BrE)
to scatter drops of liquid lightly over a surface
to scatter liquid drops lightly

Examples

Remember to sprinkle the plants every morning during the summer.
She likes to sprinkle water on the floor before cleaning it.
If you sprinkle the cake with a little water, it won’t dry out so quickly.

Synset Examples

Sprinkle the lawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray - to send out liquid in very small drops
  • Antonym: to drench - to wet thoroughly

Etymology

From Middle English sprenklen, frequentative of sprengen, from Old English sprengan (to sprinkle, scatter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00229081-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsprɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋkəl / (BrE)
(of rain) to fall in small, light drops; to rain gently or lightly.
to rain lightly

Examples

It began to sprinkle just as we were leaving the house.
Only a few drops fell when it started to sprinkle in the afternoon.
Don't worry, it's just sprinkling; we won't get too wet.

Synset Examples

It has only sprinkled, but the roads are slick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drizzle - to rain lightly with very small drops
  • Antonym: to pour - to rain very heavily
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02763786-v
Synset POS: v

/ ˈsprɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋkəl / (BrE)
to scatter small drops or particles of a substance over a surface or area
scatter small drops or particles

Examples

Related Terms

5. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋkəl / (BrE)
a light rain falling in scattered locations rather than over a wide area
light, scattered rainfall

Examples

We only got a sprinkle of rain last night, not enough to wet the ground.
There was a brief sprinkle this morning before the sun came out.
Clouds passed overhead, leaving just a light sprinkle in the garden.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: shower - a brief fall of rain, sometimes heavier than a sprinkle
  • Synonym: drizzle - light rain falling in very fine drops, more persistent than a sprinkle

Etymology

From Middle English sprenklen, frequentative of sprengen (to scatter), from Old English sprengan.

Source: WordNet
