spam

1. VERB

/ spæm / (AmE), / spæm / (BrE)
To send unsolicited or unwanted messages, especially email, to a large number of recipients.
send unsolicited or junk email

Examples

Marketing companies often spam thousands of people with unsolicited advertisements.
Please do not spam the forum with repeated messages or irrelevant links.
Many users reported that their inboxes had been spammed overnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junk mail - unsolicited or unwanted email

Etymology

Originally from the brand name SPAM (a canned meat product); later used in computing after a Monty Python sketch referencing repetitive, unsolicited content.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01034587-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ spæm / (AmE), / spæm / (BrE)
unwanted email messages, usually of a commercial nature, sent to a large number of people
unwanted bulk email

Examples

My inbox is full of spam offering dubious investment opportunities.
It's important to filter out spam so you don't miss important messages.
He receives so much spam that it's difficult to find legitimate emails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junk mail - unsolicited or unwanted advertising material received by mail, email, or other means

Etymology

Originally a trademark for a canned meat product by Hormel Foods; later used in reference to unwanted messages, possibly from a Monty Python sketch where 'Spam' is repetitively mentioned.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06291000-n
Synset POS: n
