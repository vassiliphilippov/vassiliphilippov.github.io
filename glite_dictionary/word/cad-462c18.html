cad

1. NOUN

/ kæd / (AmE), / kæd / (BrE)
a man who behaves dishonorably, especially toward women
dishonorable man, esp. toward women

Examples

Everyone knew Charles was a cad after the terrible way he treated his fiancée.
He acted like a complete cad by not apologizing for his rude comments.
Although he seemed charming at first, his true cad nature was soon revealed.

Synset Examples

you dirty dog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Antonym: gentleman - a man of good manners and honorable conduct

Etymology

Originally a shortening of 'caddie', referring to an unskilled helper or errand boy in Scottish English; developed pejorative meaning in the 19th century.
