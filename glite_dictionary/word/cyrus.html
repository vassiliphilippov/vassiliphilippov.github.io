Cyrus

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈsaɪrəs / (BrE)
A Persian prince (died 401 BC) who was defeated by his brother Artaxerxes II in a struggle for the throne.
Persian prince, rival of Artaxerxes II

Examples

Cyrus led an army against Artaxerxes II but was killed at the Battle of Cunaxa.
Ancient historians wrote about the failed rebellion of Cyrus the Younger.
The rivalry between Cyrus and his brother shaped Persian history in the 5th century BC.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'Kῦρος' (Kȳros), from Old Persian 'Kūrush'.
