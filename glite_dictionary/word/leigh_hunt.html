Leigh Hunt

1. PROPN

/ liː hʌnt / (AmE), / liː hʌnt / (BrE)
(1784–1859) British writer and critic, known for his defense of Romantic poets such as Keats and Shelley.
British writer and Romantic critic

Examples

Leigh Hunt played a significant role in promoting the works of young Romantic poets in Britain.
The essays of Leigh Hunt are still admired for their wit and insight.
In English literature classes, Leigh Hunt is often discussed alongside his contemporaries Keats and Shelley.
