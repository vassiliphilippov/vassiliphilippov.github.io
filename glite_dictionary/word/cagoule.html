cagoule

1. NOUN

/ kəˈguːl / (AmE), / kəˈguːl / (BrE)
A lightweight, hooded waterproof jacket, often worn as protection against rain.
lightweight waterproof jacket
british

Examples

She quickly pulled her cagoule over her clothes when the rain started.
Many hikers carry a cagoule because it's easy to pack and keeps you dry.
It was windy and wet, so I put on my red cagoule before leaving the house.

Etymology

From French 'cagoule', meaning 'hood'.
