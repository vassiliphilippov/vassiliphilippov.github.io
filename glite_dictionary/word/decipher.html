to decipher

1. VERB

/ dɪˈsaɪfər / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪfə / (BrE)
to succeed in reading or understanding something that is difficult to make out or interpret, such as poor handwriting or a faded text
make sense of something hard to read

Examples

She struggled to decipher his messy handwriting on the birthday card.
It took me hours to decipher the notes from the old manuscript.
Can you decipher what this doctor wrote on the prescription?

Synset Examples

Can you decipher this letter?
The archeologist traced the hieroglyphs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interpret - to explain or understand the meaning of something
  • Synonym: to decode - to convert a coded message into intelligible language

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'decifrare', from Latin 'de-' (reverse) + 'cifra' (cipher).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00627767-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈsaɪfɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪfə / (BrE)
to convert a code or text written in code into ordinary language; to interpret or make sense of something difficult to understand
convert code or unclear text to plain language

Examples

It took the team several hours to decipher the encrypted message.
She worked hard to decipher his messy handwriting on the letter.
Historians are still trying to decipher the ancient script found on the ruins.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to encode - to put information into code
  • Synonym: to decode - to convert coded material or a message into a normal language

Etymology

From Medieval Latin decifrare, from de- (reversal) + cifra (cipher, zero).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00996499-v
Synset POS: v
