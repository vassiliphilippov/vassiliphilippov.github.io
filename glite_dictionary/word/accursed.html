accursed

1. ADJ

/ əˈkɝːsɪd, əˈkɝːst / (AmE), / əˈkɜːsɪd, əˈkɜːst / (BrE)
experiencing or marked by a curse; under a curse or doomed
under a curse; doomed

Examples

The villagers believed the old ruins were accursed and avoided them after dark.
He wandered the earth like an accursed soul, never finding peace.
Some said the treasure was accursed and brought misfortune to anyone who owned it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cursed - under a curse; condemned
  • Antonym: blessed - enjoying divine favor and happiness

Etymology

From Old English 'accursian', meaning 'to curse'.
