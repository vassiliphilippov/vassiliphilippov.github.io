Lonicera sempervirens

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈnɪsərə sɛmpərˈvaɪrənz / (AmE), / ləˈnɪsərə sɛmpəˈvʌɪərɛnz / (BrE)
An evergreen North American honeysuckle vine with coral-red or orange flowers, commonly called coral honeysuckle.
a coral-red flowering honeysuckle vine

Examples

The gardener planted Lonicera sempervirens along the fence to attract hummingbirds with its vibrant flowers.
We admired the bright blooms of Lonicera sempervirens as they climbed gracefully over the trellis.
Unlike some honeysuckles, Lonicera sempervirens is an evergreen vine that provides year-round foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral honeysuckle - common name for Lonicera sempervirens

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lonicera' named after botanist Adam Lonicer, 'sempervirens' meaning 'evergreen.'
