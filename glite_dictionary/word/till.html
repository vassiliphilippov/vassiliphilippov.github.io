till

1. NOUN

/ tɪl / (AmE), / tɪl / (BrE)
Unsorted and unstratified soil or sediment deposited directly by a glacier, typically containing a mixture of clay, sand, gravel, and boulders.
glacial deposit of mixed sediments

Examples

The field was covered in a thick layer of till left by the retreating glacier.
Farmers often struggle to plant crops in till because of its stony composition.
Geologists examined the till to learn more about the region's glacial history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moraine - an accumulation of glacial debris (soil and rock) carried and deposited by a glacier

Etymology

From Middle English, possibly from Old Norse *tjald* (covering, tent), in reference to covering the land, but exact origin uncertain.

2. NOUN

/ tɪl / (AmE), / tɪl / (BrE)
a drawer, box, or compartment for keeping money, especially in a shop or bank
drawer or box for holding cash

Examples

The shop assistant placed the notes carefully in the till after the customer paid.
At the end of the day, Sarah counted the money in the till to balance the accounts.
He unlocked the till to give the customer the correct change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cash register - a machine for recording sales and storing cash in shops
  • Synonym: safe - a strong container for storing valuables, especially money and documents

Etymology

From Old English *till* (compartment, compartment for money), influenced by Old Norse *till* (compartment, partitioned space).

3. VERB

/ tɪl / (AmE), / tɪl / (BrE)
to prepare land for crops by ploughing, harrowing, and fertilizing it
prepare land for crops

Examples

The farmer uses modern machinery to till the fields every spring.
Early settlers had to till the land by hand before they could plant crops.
After the flood, the community worked together to till their damaged soil.

Synset Examples

till the soil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cultivate - to prepare and use land for growing crops

Etymology

From Old English 'tilian' (to aim for, obtain, cultivate).

4. NOUN

/ tɪl / (AmE), / tɪl / (BrE)
(historical) A treasury or fund, especially one for public or government money.
treasury or fund for government money
archaic

Examples

The kingdom’s till was nearly empty after years of war.
Taxes were collected and deposited into the royal till.
He was accused of embezzling money from the state till.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treasury - a place where money is kept, especially by a government

Etymology

From Old English *till* (storage, compartment), ultimately from Proto-Germanic *tiljaną* (to aim, to prepare).
