focalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the confinement of an infection or disease process to a specific, limited area within the body.
confinement of infection to a limited area
medical

Examples

Doctors identified the focalization of the infection in the patient's left lung.
Early focalization of an abscess can help prevent its spread throughout the body.
The focalization of the disease made targeted treatment more effective.

Etymology

From Latin 'focus' (hearth, fireplace) + '-ization'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊ.kə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊ.kə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of bringing something into focus or concentrating attention on a particular point
process of bringing into focus

Examples

The focalization of the camera allowed the image to become much clearer.
Effective focalization is crucial in microscopy to observe minute details.
There was a sudden focalization of attention when the speaker entered the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - the center of interest or activity; the point at which rays of light converge

Etymology

From French focalisation, from 'focal' + '-ization'.
