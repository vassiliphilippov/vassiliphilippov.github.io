frisky

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrɪski / (AmE), / ˈfrɪski / (BrE)
full of energy and playful; lively and spirited, often in a way that is eager to play or move about.
lively and playful

Examples

The puppy was so frisky that it never seemed to sit still for a moment.
After her nap, Grandma felt surprisingly frisky and wanted to go for a walk.
The horses grew frisky in the cool morning air, galloping around the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: playful - fond of games and amusement; lively in spirit
  • Synonym: energetic - showing or involving great activity or vitality
  • Antonym: sedate - calm, quiet, or composed; not active or playful
