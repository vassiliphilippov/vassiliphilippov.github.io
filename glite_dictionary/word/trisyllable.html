trisyllable

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈsɪləbəl / (AmE), / traɪˈsɪləb(ə)l / (BrE)
A word consisting of three syllables.
word with three syllables

Examples

The word trisyllable itself is a good example because it has exactly three syllables.
Teachers often ask students to count the syllables in a trisyllable.
In poetry, a trisyllable can fit neatly into a single metrical foot.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: disyllable - a word with two syllables
  • Degree Higher: polysyllable - a word with more than three syllables
  • Degree Lower: monosyllable - a word with one syllable

Etymology

From Greek 'tri-' (three) + 'syllabē' (syllable).
