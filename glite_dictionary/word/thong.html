thong

1. NOUN

/ θɔŋ / (AmE), / θɒŋ / (BrE)
A narrow strip of leather, used especially for fastening, tying, or lashing things together.
narrow strip of leather for tying or lashing

Examples

The craftsman used a thong to secure the handle to the axe head.
She tied the bundle tightly with a leather thong.
Ancient sandals were often fastened with a simple thong.

Etymology

From Old English *þwong* (thong, strip of leather), related to Old Norse *þvengr* and German *Zwang*.
