to impair

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpɛr / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeə / (BrE)
to damage or weaken something, making it less effective or less good
to make something weaker or less good

Examples

Smoking can impair your lung function over time.
Too much alcohol will impair your ability to drive safely.
His vision was impaired by the heavy fog.

Synset Examples

nothing marred her beauty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damage - to cause physical harm to something
  • Antonym: to improve - to make something better

Etymology

From Old French *empeirier*, from Latin *impedīre* (to hinder).

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈpɛr / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeə / (BrE)
to make something weaker or less effective; to damage or diminish in quality, strength, or ability
make weaker or less effective

Examples

Lack of sleep can impair your memory and concentration.
Drinking alcohol may impair your ability to drive safely.
Her hearing was impaired after the accident.

Synset Examples

His vision was impaired

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damage - to harm or cause injury to something
  • Antonym: to improve - to make or become better

Etymology

From Old French *empeirer*, from Vulgar Latin *impejorare* (to make worse), from Latin *peior* (worse).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00263767-v
Synset POS: v
