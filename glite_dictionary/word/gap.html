gap

1. NOUN

/ ɡæp / (AmE), / ɡæp / (BrE)
a low place between hills or mountains, often forming a pass through a range
low pass between hills or mountains

Examples

The hikers crossed the mountain gap just before noon.
A cool breeze blew through the narrow gap in the hills.
Settlers used the gap as a route through the Appalachian Mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pass - a way through a range of mountains
