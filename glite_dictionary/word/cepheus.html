Cepheus

1. PROPN

/ ˈsiːfiəs / (AmE), / ˈsiːfiəs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the king of Ethiopia and husband of Cassiopeia.
king of Ethiopia in Greek mythology

Examples

According to the myth, Cepheus was forced to sacrifice his daughter Andromeda to appease the gods.
The tale of Cepheus and his family is a prominent legend in Greek mythology.
Some constellations are named after mythological figures like Cepheus and Cassiopeia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cassiopeia - wife of Cepheus in Greek mythology

2. PROPN

/ ˈsiːfiəs / (AmE), / ˈsiːfiəs / (BrE)
a faint constellation in the northern sky near Cassiopeia and the pole star, named after a king in Greek mythology.
constellation in the northern sky

Examples

The constellation Cepheus is best viewed during autumn evenings in the Northern Hemisphere.
Astronomers often use Cepheus as a reference point when locating nearby constellations.
Cepheus is named after a king from Greek mythology whose wife was Cassiopeia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cassiopeia - a nearby constellation in the northern sky

Etymology

From Latin Cepheus, from Greek Kēpheus, a mythological king of Ethiopia.
