ichthyolatry

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪkθiˈɑlətri / (AmE), / ˌɪkθiˈɒlətri / (BrE)
the worship of fish as divine beings or idols
worship of fish

Examples

Ancient tribes along the river practiced ichthyolatry, revering certain species as sacred.
The study examines ichthyolatry in the context of early Egyptian religion.
He wrote a thesis on ichthyolatry and its impact on local culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idolatry - the worship of idols or physical objects as gods

Etymology

From Greek 'ichthys' (fish) + 'latreia' (worship).
