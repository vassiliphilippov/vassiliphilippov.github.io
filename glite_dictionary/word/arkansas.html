Arkansas

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːrkənsɔː / (AmE), / ˈɑːkənsɔː / (BrE)
a major river in the south-central United States that flows from Colorado through Kansas, Oklahoma, and Arkansas, joining the Mississippi River.
river in the south-central United States

Examples

The Arkansas flows through several states before joining the Mississippi River.
Many towns along the Arkansas depend on the river for water and recreation.
Flooding along the Arkansas has impacted communities in Arkansas and Oklahoma.

Etymology

From a French spelling of a Quapaw word, named after the people living in the region.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɑːr.kən.sɔː / (AmE), / ˈɑː.kən.sɔː / (BrE)
A state in the south central region of the United States, which was one of the Confederate states during the American Civil War.
U.S. state in the south central region

Examples

She moved to Arkansas after graduating from college.
Arkansas is known for its natural hot springs and scenic mountains.
The history of Arkansas includes its role as a Confederate state during the Civil War.

Etymology

From a French interpretation of a Siouan name, possibly Quapaw, for the indigenous people.
