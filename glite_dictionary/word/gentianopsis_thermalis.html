Gentianopsis thermalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn(t)ʃi.əˈnɑp.sɪs θərˈmeɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn(t)ʃɪ.əˈnɒp.sɪs θəˈmɑː.lɪs / (BrE)
A small blue-flowered fringed gentian native to the Rocky Mountains of the western United States, often found near hot springs in Yellowstone National Park.
Rocky Mountain blue-flowered fringed gentian

Examples

We spotted a rare Gentianopsis thermalis blooming near the Yellowstone hot springs.
The delicate petals of the Gentianopsis thermalis are edged with a distinctive fringe.
Botanists visited the park specifically to study Gentianopsis thermalis populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fringed gentian - a group of gentian species with fringed petals

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'gentiana' (gentian); species epithet 'thermalis' from Latin meaning 'of hot springs'.
