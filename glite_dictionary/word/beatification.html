beatification

1. NOUN

/ biˌætɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / biˌatɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the action of making someone or something supremely blessed or extremely happy
the act of making supremely blessed or happy

Examples

The news of her recovery was a moment of pure beatification for her family.
His kindness to others brought a sense of beatification to everyone he met.
They experienced a state of beatification after being reunited with their loved ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blessing - something that brings happiness or benefit
  • Antonym: damnation - the act of condemning someone to eternal punishment or unhappiness

Etymology

From Medieval Latin beatificatio, from Latin beatus (blessed) + -ficatio (making).

2. NOUN

/ biˌædəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / biˌatɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) the official act by which the Pope declares that a deceased person has lived a holy life and is worthy of public veneration, marking the first step toward sainthood (canonization).
act of declaring someone blessed in Catholicism

Examples

The beatification ceremony attracted thousands of pilgrims from around the world.
Her life of charity and devotion led to her beatification by the Pope.
The Church marked the anniversary of his beatification with a special mass.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: canonization - the act of officially declaring someone a saint in the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Medieval Latin beatificatio, from Latin beatus (blessed) + facere (to make).
