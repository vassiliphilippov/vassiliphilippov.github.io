to sully

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌli / (AmE), / ˈsʌli / (BrE)
to damage or spoil the good quality or reputation of someone or something
damage reputation; tarnish

Examples

The scandal threatened to sully the company's image.
He refused to let gossip sully his good name.
Several false accusations were made in an effort to sully her reputation.

Synset Examples

The journalists have defamed me!
The article in the paper sullied my reputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defame - to harm the reputation of someone by making false statements
  • Synonym: tarnish - to make or become less valuable or respected
  • Antonym: honor - to regard with great respect; to keep reputation intact

Etymology

From Old French 'souiller' meaning 'to soil', from Latin 'sullāre'.

2. VERB

/ ˈsʌli / (AmE), / ˈsʌli / (BrE)
to make dirty or stained; to damage the purity or integrity of something, especially in a figurative sense
to soil or tarnish (literally or figuratively)

Examples

The scandal threatened to sully the company's good name.
Years of neglect had sullied the once-beautiful monument.
Accusations of cheating can easily sully an athlete's reputation.

Synset Examples

The silver was tarnished by the long exposure to the air
Her reputation was sullied after the affair with a married man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarnish - to lose or cause to lose luster or purity; to spoil
  • Synonym: soil - to make dirty or unclean
  • Antonym: to cleanse - to make clean or pure

Etymology

From Old French *souiller* (to soil), from Latin *suillare* (to make dirty).

3. VERB

/ ˈsʌli / (AmE), / ˈsʌli / (BrE)
to damage the purity or integrity of; place under suspicion or cast doubt upon
to tarnish or damage (reputation, purity)

Examples

Publishing false accusations can easily sully someone's reputation.
The scandal threatened to sully the company's image for years to come.
He refused to let jealousy sully his friendship with her.

Synset Examples

sully someone's reputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarnish - to damage or lessen the good quality or condition of something
  • Synonym: stain - to damage the reputation or purity of someone or something
  • Antonym: to cleanse - to make clean or pure, the opposite of sully

Etymology

From Old French *souiller* (to soil, make dirty), from Latin *suillare* (to soil), from *suilus* (of swine).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
