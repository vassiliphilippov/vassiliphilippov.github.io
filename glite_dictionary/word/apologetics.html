apologetics

1. NOUN

/ əˌpɑːləˈdʒɛtɪks / (AmE), / əˌpɒləˈdʒɛtɪks / (BrE)
the branch of theology concerned with the defense and rational justification of Christian beliefs and doctrines
theological defense of Christian beliefs

Examples

She specialized in apologetics and often debated critics of Christianity.
Many universities offer courses in apologetics to train future theologians.
His writings on apologetics are widely regarded as influential within the church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apology - a formal defense or justification, especially in a literary or philosophical context

Etymology

From Greek apologētikos (suitable for defense), from apologia (a speech in defense).
