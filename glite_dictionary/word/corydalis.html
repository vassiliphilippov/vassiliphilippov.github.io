corydalis

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˈrɪdəlɪs / (AmE), / kɒˈrɪdəlɪs / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Corydalis, known for its finely divided leaves and spurred tubular flowers.
genus of flowering plants

Examples

The garden features several varieties of corydalis that bloom in early spring.
She planted blue corydalis along the shaded pathway for a splash of color.
The delicate foliage of corydalis adds texture to woodland gardens.

Etymology

From New Latin Corydalis, from Greek korydalis (crested lark), referring to the flower's shape.
