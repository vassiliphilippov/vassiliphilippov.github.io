Corydalis

1. NOUN

/ kɔːrɪˈdeɪlɪs / (AmE), / kɒrɪˈdeɪlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of annual or perennial flowering herbs, native to the Northern Hemisphere and South Africa, known for their tubular flowers and divided leaves.
a genus of flowering plants

Examples

Several species of Corydalis are popular in shade gardens for their delicate, fern-like foliage.
The botanist collected samples of Corydalis growing in the Himalayan foothills.
You can find yellow and purple Corydalis blooming in the early spring.

Etymology

From Greek korydalís, meaning 'lark' or 'crested lark,' referring to the shape of the flower spur.
