polisher

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑlɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪʃə / (BrE)
a power tool or device used to smooth or shine surfaces by rubbing or buffing them
tool for buffing surfaces

Examples

The woodworker used a polisher to give the table a glossy finish.
After waxing the car, he went over it with an electric polisher to make it shine.
Industrial floors are often maintained with a heavy-duty floor polisher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffer - a device used for buffing surfaces to make them smooth or shiny
  • Synonym: sander - a power tool for smoothing surfaces by abrasion with sandpaper

Etymology

From the verb 'polish' + -er, denoting an agent or device.
