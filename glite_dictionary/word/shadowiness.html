shadowiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃædoʊinəs / (AmE), / ˈʃadəʊinəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being shadowy; relative darkness caused by something blocking light
state of being shadowy or dim

Examples

The shadowiness of the old alley made it seem mysterious at dusk.
Photographers often struggle with shadowiness when trying to capture clear images in uneven lighting.
The painter captured the shadowiness of the forest, where sunlight barely reached the ground.

Synset Examples

it is much cooler in the shade
there's too much shadiness to take good photographs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shade - relative darkness caused by light being blocked
  • Antonym: brightness - the state of being full of light

Etymology

From 'shadowy' + '-ness', forming a noun meaning 'the quality of being shadowy'.
