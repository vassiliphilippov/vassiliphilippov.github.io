hurdling

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɜrdlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɜːdlɪŋ / (BrE)
A race in which competitors run and jump over a series of obstacles called hurdles.
footrace with obstacles (hurdles)

Examples

She won the gold medal in the 100-meter hurdling event at the Olympics.
Many athletes find hurdling to be both physically demanding and technically challenging.
The school's new track team offers training sessions for hurdling throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprint - a short-distance running race at full speed

Etymology

From 'hurdle' (obstacle) + '-ing' (action or process).
