capriciously

1. ADV

/ kəˈprɪʃəsli / (AmE), / kəˈprɪʃəsli / (BrE)
In a manner that is unpredictable, subject to sudden or impulsive changes.
in an unpredictable, impulsive way

Examples

The CEO acted capriciously, making major decisions without warning or explanation.
Public opinion can shift capriciously in response to unexpected events.
The artist was known for changing her style capriciously from one project to the next.

Synset Examples

the weather has been freakishly variable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impulsively - in a way that is driven by sudden urges or desires
  • Antonym: predictably - in a way that can be anticipated or foreseen

Etymology

From Italian 'capriccio' (whim), with the suffix '-ly' forming the adverb.

2. ADV

/ kəˈprɪʃəsli / (AmE), / kəˈprɪʃəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows sudden, unpredictable changes or is governed by whim rather than reason.
in a whimsical or unpredictable way

Examples

The weather changed capriciously from sunshine to storm in a matter of minutes.
She decided capriciously to redecorate her entire house.
Resources were allocated capriciously, leaving some teams with much more support than others.

Synset Examples

there were Turk's head lilies and patches of iris, islands of brilliant blue set capriciously in the green sea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arbitrarily - in a random or unrestrained way
  • Antonym: predictably - in a way that can be predicted or foreseen easily

Etymology

From 'capricious' + '-ly'; 'capricious' from French 'capricieux', from Italian 'capriccio' (whim).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00282921-r
Synset POS: r
