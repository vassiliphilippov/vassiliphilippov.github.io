chemisorption

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛmɪˈsɔrpʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɛmɪˈsɔːpʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process in which a substance is adsorbed onto the surface of another through the formation of chemical bonds, typically irreversible
adsorption by chemical bonding
medical

Examples

The chemisorption of hydrogen on platinum is essential in many catalytic reactions.
Researchers studied the chemisorption of sulfur dioxide on metal oxide surfaces.
Because chemisorption involves stronger bonding, it is often irreversible compared to physisorption.

Synset Examples

chemisorption of gaseous nitrogen on iron catalysts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: physisorption - adsorption through physical (not chemical) forces

Etymology

From 'chem-' (chemical) + 'adsorption', modeled after 'physisorption'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɛmɪˈsɔːrpʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɛmɪˈsɔːpʃən / (BrE)
The adsorption of molecules onto a surface by means of chemical bonds, often irreversible, rather than by physical attraction.
adsorption by chemical bonding
medical

Examples

The chemisorption of hydrogen on platinum is an important step in catalysis.
Due to chemisorption, the process is often irreversible and involves strong chemical bonds.
Understanding chemisorption is crucial for designing better catalysts in industrial reactions.

Synset Examples

chemisorption of gaseous nitrogen on iron catalysts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: physisorption - adsorption by physical rather than chemical forces

Etymology

From 'chemi-' (relating to chemistry) + 'sorption' (absorption or adsorption).
