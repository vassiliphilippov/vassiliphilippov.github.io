1. NOUN

/ ˌæspɪˈlæps / (AmE), / ˌaspɪˈlæps / (BrE)

2. NOUN

/ ˌæspɪˈdɛlæps / (AmE), / ˌaspɪˈdɛlaps / (BrE)
A genus of venomous African coral snakes of the family Elapidae.
genus of African coral snakes

Examples

The Aspidelaps is distinguished by its distinctive banded coloration, similar to other coral snakes.
Researchers have documented several species within the genus Aspidelaps across southern Africa.
Venom from the Aspidelaps is potent, and medical attention should be sought immediately after a bite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral snake - a brightly colored, venomous snake of the family Elapidae

Etymology

From Greek 'aspis' (shield) and 'elaps' (a kind of snake), used in New Latin scientific naming.
