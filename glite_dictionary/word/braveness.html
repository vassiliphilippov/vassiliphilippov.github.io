braveness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪvnɪs / (AmE), / ˈbreɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being courageous and able to face danger, fear, or pain without flinching
quality of being brave

Examples

Her braveness in the face of adversity inspired everyone around her.
He showed remarkable braveness while rescuing the children from the burning building.
It takes great braveness to speak out against injustice, even when you are afraid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bravery - courageous behavior or character; quality of being brave
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of bravery; easily frightened; opposite of courage

Etymology

Formed from 'brave' + '-ness'; 'brave' from Old French 'brave' (splendid, valiant).
