pitcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃə / (BrE)
the amount of liquid that a pitcher holds or is able to hold
the quantity a pitcher holds

Examples

She brought a pitcher of lemonade to the table for everyone to share.
We drank an entire pitcher of water after our hike.
The party ran out of soda after the third pitcher was finished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jug - a large container for liquids, usually with a handle and spout

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃə / (BrE)
the player position on a baseball team responsible for throwing the ball to the batter
baseball player who throws to batter

Examples

After years of playing shortstop, she finally became the team's pitcher.
The pitcher threw a fastball that struck out the batter.
Every baseball team relies on its pitcher to control the pace of the game.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: catcher - the baseball player who catches pitches thrown by the pitcher

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃə / (BrE)
(Botany) A leaf modified to form a deep cavity or tube, often used by certain plants to trap insects.
specialized leaf of some carnivorous plants

Examples

The pitcher of the plant trapped several insects overnight.
Some carnivorous plants have evolved a pitcher to capture and digest prey.
The brightly colored pitcher attracts insects with the promise of nectar.

5. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃə / (BrE)
A container with a handle and spout, used for holding and pouring liquids.
liquid container with handle and spout

Examples

She filled the pitcher with lemonade before serving it to her guests.
The pitcher on the table was made of clear glass.
Please pour the milk from the pitcher into my cup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jug - a container used for holding and pouring liquids

Etymology

From Middle English *picher*, from Old French *pichier* (pot, jug), from Medieval Latin *bicarium* (drinking vessel).
