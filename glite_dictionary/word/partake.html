to partake

1. VERB

/ pɑrˈteɪk / (AmE), / pɑːˈteɪk / (BrE)
to have some of the qualities or attributes of something; to share in a characteristic
share in a quality or characteristic

Examples

Her paintings partake of both surrealism and impressionism, making them difficult to categorize.
The new architecture partakes of classical elements, yet remains thoroughly modern.
This tradition partakes of ancient rituals still practiced in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: share - to have a part or portion of something in common with others

Etymology

From Middle English 'partaken', from part + take.

2. VERB

/ pɑrˈteɪk / (AmE), / pɑːˈteɪk / (BrE)
to eat or drink something, especially as part of a group or together with others
to eat or drink (especially with others)

Examples

During the festival, everyone gathered to partake in the communal meal.
He politely declined to partake of the wine offered at dinner.
Guests are encouraged to partake in the refreshments provided.

Synset Examples

She didn't touch her food all night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to consume - to eat or drink something; to ingest

Etymology

From Middle English partaken, from part (share) + take.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01174998-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pɑrˈteɪk / (AmE), / pɑːˈteɪk / (BrE)
to have, give, or receive a share of something; to participate in or consume a portion of something
have a share or participate

Examples

Everyone was invited to partake in the celebration and enjoy the meal.
She politely declined to partake of dessert after dinner.
All employees are welcome to partake in the company's profit-sharing scheme.

Synset Examples

We shared the cake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: share - to have a portion of something with others
  • Synonym: to participate - to take part in something

Etymology

From Middle English partaken, from part + take, influenced by Latin partem capere (to take a part).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02300169-v
Synset POS: v
