Erodium moschatum

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈroʊdiəm mɒsˈkeɪtəm / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊdiəm mɒsˈkɑːtəm / (BrE)
A low-growing annual herb native to Europe, now also found in America, resembling alfilaria.
a low annual European herb; similar to alfilaria

Examples

The leaves of Erodium moschatum are often found carpeting disturbed ground in spring.
Gardeners sometimes confuse Erodium moschatum with alfilaria due to their similar appearance.
Erodium moschatum has spread widely in California, thriving in fields and along roadsides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alfilaria - a related species of Erodium, also known as common stork's-bill

Etymology

From Latin 'Erodium' (from Greek 'erōdios', meaning heron, due to the beak-like fruit) and 'moschatum' (from Latin 'moschatus', meaning musky, referring to its scent).
