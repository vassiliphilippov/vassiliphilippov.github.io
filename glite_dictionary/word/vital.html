vital

1. ADJ

/ ˈvaɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈvaɪtəl / (BrE)
essential to the existence or functioning of a living organism; necessary to sustain life.
essential for life or biological function

Examples

The heart is a vital organ necessary for human survival.
Certain injuries can damage vital tissues, threatening life itself.
Doctors carefully monitor vital signs like heart rate and respiration.

Synset Examples

vital organs
blood and other vital fluids
the loss of vital heat in shock
a vital spot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essential - absolutely necessary; extremely important
  • Synonym: critical - of great importance; essential
  • Antonym: lethal - causing death; deadly

Etymology

From Latin vitalis (of life), from vita (life).
