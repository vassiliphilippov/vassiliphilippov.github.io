indris

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.drɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪn.drɪs / (BrE)
a large, short-tailed lemur native to Madagascar, with thick, silky fur that is black, white, and fawn in color
large lemur of Madagascar

Examples

The indris are known for their loud, haunting calls that echo through the forests of Madagascar.
Researchers observed a group of indris leaping gracefully between the trees.
Because the indris is endangered, conservation efforts are crucial to its survival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemur - small to medium-sized primates endemic to Madagascar

Etymology

From French 'indri', from Malagasy 'endrina' (the local name for the animal).
