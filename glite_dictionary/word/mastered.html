mastered

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæstɚd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəd / (BrE)
Fully understood or learned so as to be able to use or explain it without difficulty.
fully understood or learned

Examples

After months of study, she finally had the material mastered.
The basics of chess are easily mastered by most children.
He has all the necessary skills mastered for the new job.

Synset Examples

had his algebra problems down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proficient - competent or skilled in doing or using something
  • Synonym: adept - very skilled or proficient at something
  • Antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From the verb 'master', which is from Old French 'maistre', from Latin 'magister' (chief, teacher).
