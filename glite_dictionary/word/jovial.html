jovial

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒoʊ.vi.əl / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊ.vi.əl / (BrE)
cheerful and friendly; full of high-spirited good humor or merriment
cheerful and friendly

Examples

The jovial host greeted every guest with a warm laugh and a story to tell.
Despite the rain, the team remained jovial and optimistic about their chances.
His jovial personality made him popular at work and among his friends.

Synset Examples

when hearts were young and gay
a poet could not but be gay, in such a jocund company
the jolly crowd at the reunion
jolly old Saint Nick
a jovial old gentleman
have a merry Christmas
peals of merry laughter
a mirthful laugh

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Antonym: gloomy - sad or lacking in cheer

Etymology

From Latin Iovis (Jupiter), referring to the jovial qualities attributed to those born under Jupiter's sign.
