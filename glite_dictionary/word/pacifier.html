pacifier

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
A thing that calms or soothes people or a situation.
something that calms or soothes

Examples

The new rule is just a pacifier to appease the angry employees.
Music can act as a pacifier during stressful times.
He offered a proposal as a political pacifier, but it failed to address the real issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soother - something that calms or reduces agitation

Etymology

From Latin 'pacificare' (to make peace), via French 'pacifier'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a person who tries to bring about peace or calm a conflict
someone who tries to bring peace

Examples

As a skilled pacifier, she was often called upon to mediate heated debates.
The community respected him as a pacifier during times of unrest.
History remembers certain leaders as pacifiers rather than conquerors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peacemaker - a person who brings about peace, especially by settling disputes
  • Antonym: agitator - a person who stirs up conflict or unrest

Etymology

From Latin 'pacificare' (to make peace), from 'pax' (peace).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
A rubber or plastic device for an infant to suck or bite on, used to calm or soothe.
soothing device for babies to suck

Examples

The baby stopped crying as soon as she was given her pacifier.
Please sterilize the pacifier before handing it to the infant.
He always keeps a spare pacifier in the diaper bag.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soother - another term for a pacifier (chiefly British)
  • Synonym: dummy - British term for a baby's pacifier

Etymology

From 'pacify' + '-er'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
