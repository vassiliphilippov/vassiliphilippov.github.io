obituary

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈbɪtʃuˌɛri / (AmE), / əˈbɪtjʊəri / (BrE)
a notice of a person's death, especially in a newspaper, typically including a brief biography of the deceased
death notice with short biography

Examples

The newspaper published an obituary for the famous writer who passed away last week.
Her obituary highlighted her achievements and contributions to the community.
Many friends learned of his passing after reading his obituary online.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death notice - a public announcement of a person's death, usually with basic facts

Etymology

From Medieval Latin obituary (record of death), from Latin obitus (death, a going to meet).

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈbɪtʃuˌɛri / (AmE), / əʊˈbɪtjʊəri / (BrE)
a written notice of a person's death, often with a brief account of their life and achievements, typically published in a newspaper or online.
notice of a person's death with life summary

Examples

The obituary in the newspaper paid tribute to her dedication as a teacher.
He wrote a moving obituary for his late colleague.
Many readers sent letters after reading the obituary of the renowned author.

Synset Examples

The obit highlighted his contributions to science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obit - informal or short form for obituary

Etymology

From Latin obitus (death, decease), from obīre (to go to meet, to die).
