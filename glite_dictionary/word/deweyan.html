Deweyan

1. ADJ

/ ˈduːiən / (AmE), / ˈdjuːiən / (BrE)
Relating to the ideas, philosophy, or methods of John Dewey, especially his approaches to education and pragmatism.
of or relating to John Dewey or his philosophy

Examples

Her teaching methods are strongly influenced by Deweyan principles of experiential learning.
The debate centered on the Deweyan concept of democracy in education.
Many educators embrace a Deweyan approach to fostering critical thinking in students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pragmatic - relating to dealing with things sensibly and realistically

Etymology

Formed from the name of John Dewey (1859–1952), American philosopher and educator, with the adjectival suffix -an.
