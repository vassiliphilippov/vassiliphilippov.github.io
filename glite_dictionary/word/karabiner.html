karabiner

1. NOUN

/ ˌkærəˈbiːnər / (AmE), / ˌkarəˈbiːnə / (BrE)
A strong, oblong metal ring with a spring-loaded gate, used in climbing to connect a rope to equipment, such as a piton, or to join two ropes.
metal clip used in climbing

Examples

Make sure the karabiner is securely fastened before you begin climbing.
He attached the safety rope to the anchor using a karabiner.
Several karabiners jingled on her climbing harness as she prepared her gear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carabiner - oblong metal clip used in climbing and safety applications

Etymology

From German 'Karabinerhaken' (hook for a carbine), relating to early use in firearms equipment.
