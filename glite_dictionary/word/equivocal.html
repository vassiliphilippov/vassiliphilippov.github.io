equivocal

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈkwɪvəkəl / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪvəkəl / (BrE)
open to more than one interpretation; ambiguous or uncertain, especially so as to be questionable or suspect
open to multiple interpretations; ambiguous

Examples

The evidence presented in court was highly equivocal, leading to much debate among the jurors.
Her equivocal answers made it difficult to determine where she truly stood on the issue.
Some passages in the contract were so equivocal that both parties interpreted them differently.

Synset Examples

aliens of equivocal loyalty
his conscience reproached him with the equivocal character of the union into which he had forced his son

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation

Etymology

From Late Latin 'aequivocus' (of doubtful meaning), from Latin 'aequus' (equal) + 'vocare' (to call).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈkwɪvəkəl / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪvəkəl / (BrE)
(of a sign or indication) uncertain or open to more than one interpretation; not definite or straightforward.
uncertain or ambiguous as a sign

Examples

The results of the clinical trial were equivocal and required further investigation.
His comments about the matter were equivocal, leaving everyone uncertain about his true opinion.
The X-ray findings were equivocal, so the doctor recommended additional tests.

Synset Examples

the evidence from bacteriologic analysis was equivocal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambiguous - having more than one possible interpretation
  • Antonym: definite - clearly stated or decided; not ambiguous

Etymology

From Late Latin 'aequivocus', from Latin 'aequus' (equal) + 'vocāre' (to call).

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈkwɪvəkəl / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪvəkəl / (BrE)
open to more than one interpretation; ambiguous or uncertain, often with the intention to mislead or conceal the truth.
ambiguous; open to multiple interpretations

Examples

His answer to the press was deliberately equivocal and left everyone confused about his true intentions.
The research produced equivocal results, leading to further debates among scientists.
She gave an equivocal reply, making it unclear whether she agreed with the proposal or not.

Synset Examples

an equivocal statement
the polling had a complex and equivocal (or ambiguous) message for potential female candidates
the officer's equivocal behavior increased the victim's uneasiness
popularity is an equivocal crown
an equivocal response to an embarrassing question

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation; unclear or inexact
  • Antonym: unequivocal - leaving no doubt; unambiguous

Etymology

From Latin 'aequivocus', from 'aequus' (equal) + 'vocare' (to call).
