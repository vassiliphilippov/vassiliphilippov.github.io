streptococcal

1. ADJ

/ ˌstrɛptəˈkɑkəl / (AmE), / ˌstrɛptəˈkɒkəl / (BrE)
of, relating to, or caused by streptococci (a group of bacteria, some of which cause diseases)
related to streptococci bacteria
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with a streptococcal throat infection.
A streptococcal rash is often seen in cases of scarlet fever.
Early treatment can help prevent complications from streptococcal diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: streptococcus - a genus of spherical bacteria occurring in chains, some species of which cause disease

Etymology

From 'streptococcus' (a type of spherical, chain-forming bacteria) plus the suffix '-al'.
