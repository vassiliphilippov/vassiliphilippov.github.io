nullity

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈnʌl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
an act, condition, or thing that is legally void or has no validity; the state of being null, especially in legal contexts
the state of being legally void
legal

Examples

The court declared the contract a nullity due to fraud.
Any law passed without proper authority is considered a nullity.
He argued that the proceedings amounted to a legal nullity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invalidity - the state of not being legally valid
  • Antonym: validity - the state of being legally valid

Etymology

From Latin nullitas (nothingness, nonexistence), from nullus (none, not any).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnʌl.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˈnʌl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The state of being null or nonexistent; nothingness.
state of nonexistence

Examples

Philosophers have long debated whether true nullity is possible in the universe.
The theory suggests that the concept of nullity underlies certain mathematical abstractions.
After years of neglect, the property had fallen into a state of almost complete nullity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nothingness - the state of not existing or being nothing
  • Antonym: existence - the state of being real or living

Etymology

From Latin nullitas (nothingness), from nullus (none, not any).
