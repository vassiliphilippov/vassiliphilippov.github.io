Melanthiaceae

1. NOUN

/ məˌlænθiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / mɛˌlænθɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants including genera such as Aletris, Narthecium, and Veratrum, sometimes formerly classified within the Liliaceae
a family of flowering plants

Examples

The Melanthiaceae family includes many plants found in temperate regions.
Botanists recently reclassified several genera under Melanthiaceae.
Toxic species of Melanthiaceae have been studied for their medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on Greek 'melas' (black) and the suffix '-aceae' denoting plant families.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛlænθiˈeɪsi.i / (AmE), / ˌmiːlanθiːˈeɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, formerly considered a subfamily of Liliaceae, including genera such as Aletris, Narthecium, and Veratrum
a family of flowering plants

Examples

The Melanthiaceae family contains several species known for their medicinal properties.
Botanists have reclassified some lilies as members of the Melanthiaceae.
Plants belonging to the Melanthiaceae are often found in temperate regions.

Etymology

From the genus name Melanthium, from Greek 'melas' (black) and 'anthos' (flower).
