abbacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈæbəsi / (AmE), / ˈæbəsi / (BrE)
the office or jurisdiction of an abbot
office of an abbot

Examples

The newly appointed monk was to assume the abbacy after the passing of the previous abbot.
During his abbacy, the monastery experienced significant growth and reform.
Records from the medieval period often chronicle disputes over the abbacy and its privileges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abbot - the male head of an abbey of monks

Etymology

From Medieval Latin abbatia (abbacy), from abbas (abbot).
