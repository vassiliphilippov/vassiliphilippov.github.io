Chaldaea

1. PROPN

/ kælˈdiːə / (AmE), / kalˈdiːə / (BrE)
an ancient region in southern Mesopotamia, situated between the lower Euphrates River and the Persian Gulf, notable for reaching its peak under Nebuchadnezzar II before being conquered by the Persians in 539 BC
ancient region in southern Mesopotamia

Examples

The ruins of Chaldaea offer a fascinating glimpse into ancient Mesopotamian civilization.
Historians believe that astronomy flourished in Chaldaea during the reign of Nebuchadnezzar II.
The Persian conquest marked the end of Chaldaea as an independent region.

Etymology

From Latin Chaldaea, from Greek KHALDAIA, referring to the ancient Chaldean region.

2. PROPN

/ kælˈdiːə / (AmE), / kalˈdiːə / (BrE)
an ancient kingdom in southern Mesopotamia, also known as Babylonia, which became prominent in the first millennium BC
ancient kingdom in southern Mesopotamia

Examples

Historians have uncovered many artifacts from ancient Chaldaea.
Babylon was the capital of Chaldaea during its greatest period.
The cultural achievements of Chaldaea influenced later civilizations in Mesopotamia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Babylonia - an ancient empire in Mesopotamia, of which Chaldaea became a part

Etymology

From Latin Chaldaea, from Greek Chaldaia, from Akkadian Kaldu (Chaldeans).
