comate

1. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊ.meɪt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.meɪt / (BrE)
(of a plant) bearing a tuft or crown, especially of leaves, branches, or bracts; crowned with a coma.
bearing a tuft or crown (botany)

Examples

The comate royal palms stood out against the horizon, their leafy crowns visible from afar.
Some species of grasses are comate, sporting tufts of hairlike bracts atop their stems.
Botanists described the new tree as comate due to its dense cluster of leaves at the top.

Synset Examples

comate royal palms
pineapples are comate

Etymology

From Latin coma (hair of the head, foliage).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.meɪt / (BrE)
(of seeds or fruits) having a tuft or tufts of hair, especially as found in some plants like cotton.
having a tuft of hair (botany)
medical

Examples

The cotton plant produces comate seeds covered in soft fibers.
Many grass species have comate structures that aid in dispersal by the wind.
The botanist examined the comate appendages under a microscope.

Synset Examples

comate (or comose) seeds
a comal tuft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comose - having a tuft of hairs (botanical)

Etymology

From Latin comatus 'having long hair', from coma 'hair'.

Synset POS: s
