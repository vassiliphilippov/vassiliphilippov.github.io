background

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbækɡraʊnd / (BrE)
a painted or constructed piece of scenery positioned at the back of a stage to serve as a setting for a play or performance
scenery at back of stage

Examples

The background was painted to resemble a forest.
Stagehands hurried to change the background between acts.
A new background was designed for the final scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backdrop - a painted cloth hung at the back of a theatre stage as part of the scenery
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

/ ˈbækˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbækɡraʊnd / (BrE)
the circumstances, experiences, or training that form a person's history or influence their character or abilities

Examples

Synset Examples

7. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbækɡraʊnd / (BrE)
(in science and technology) Extraneous signals, noise, or data that may interfere with or obscure the phenomenon being observed or measured.

Examples

Researchers must account for background noise when analyzing the data from the experiment.
Astronomers filter out background signals to focus on distant celestial objects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noise - random or unwanted signals that obscure a desired signal
