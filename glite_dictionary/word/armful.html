armful

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrmˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈɑːm.fʊl / (BrE)
as much as can be held or carried in the arms at one time.
as much as arms can carry

Examples

She carried an armful of books across the library.
He handed me an armful of firewood for the fireplace.
With an armful of groceries, he struggled to open the door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handful - the amount that can be held in one hand

Etymology

Formed from 'arm' + '-ful', a suffix indicating an amount that fills or can be held by the referenced part.
