Platanistidae

1. NOUN

/ pləˌtænɪˈstɪdiː / (AmE), / pləˌtanɪˈstɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of river dolphins found in freshwater habitats in South and Southeast Asia.
family of river dolphins

Examples

The family Platanistidae includes the endangered South Asian river dolphin.
Scientists study the habitat preferences of Platanistidae to develop better conservation strategies.
Unlike oceanic dolphins, members of Platanistidae are adapted to murky freshwater environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dolphin - an aquatic mammal of the family Delphinidae or related families

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on the genus Platanista, which is derived from Greek 'platanistēs' (a kind of fish).

2. NOUN

/ plə.təˈnɪs.tɪˌdi / (AmE), / plæt.əˈnɪs.tɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, freshwater dolphins known as river dolphins, found in South and Southeast Asia.
family of river dolphins

Examples

The endangered Ganges river dolphin belongs to the Platanistidae family.
Scientists have studied the unique sonar abilities of Platanistidae in murky rivers.
The Platanistidae are adapted to life in freshwater habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: river dolphin - common name for dolphins in the family Platanistidae

Etymology

From New Latin Platanista (a genus of river dolphins) + -idae (a zoological family suffix).
