beebalm

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːˌbɑm / (AmE), / ˈbiːˌbɑːm / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb of the mint family, with aromatic leaves and tubular flowers, often grown in gardens.
aromatic North American herb

Examples

Many gardeners plant beebalm to attract bees and butterflies to their yards.
The leaves of beebalm can be used to make a fragrant herbal tea.
Bright red beebalm blossoms stood out in the summer border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bergamot - A synonym in the context of North American mint plants (Monarda species)

Etymology

Named for attracting bees and used in herbal remedies (balm); from 'bee' + 'balm'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbiˌbɑm / (AmE), / ˈbiːˌbɑːm / (BrE)
A perennial aromatic herb of eastern North America, of the mint family, with variously colored tubular flowers in dense, showy heads.
North American aromatic herb with showy flowers

Examples

Native Americans have long used beebalm for its medicinal properties.
The garden was filled with colorful beebalm, attracting bees and butterflies alike.
She planted beebalm next to the tomatoes to help deter pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bergamot - another name for beebalm, especially Monarda didyma

Etymology

Refers to the plant's attractiveness to bees and its past use in soothing balms.
