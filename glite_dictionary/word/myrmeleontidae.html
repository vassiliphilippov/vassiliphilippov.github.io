Myrmeleontidae

1. NOUN

/ mɜrˌmiːliˈɒntɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌmɜːmɪliːˈɒntɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects known as antlions, characterized by their predatory larvae that dig conical pits to trap ants and other small insects.
family of insects known as antlions

Examples

The family Myrmeleontidae includes more than 2,000 species of antlions found worldwide.
Scientists study the behavior of Myrmeleontidae larvae to understand their unique hunting strategies.
The Myrmeleontidae are best known for their pit-building larvae that prey on ants and other small insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antlion - insect known for larval pits that trap ants

Etymology

From New Latin Myrmeleon (type genus, meaning 'ant-lion') + -idae (family suffix).
