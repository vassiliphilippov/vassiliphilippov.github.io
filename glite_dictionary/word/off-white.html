off-white

1. NOUN

/ ɔfˈwaɪt / (AmE), / ɒfˈwaɪt / (BrE)
a pale shade of white with a slight tint of another color; not pure white
pale white with a slight tint

Examples

The walls in the living room were painted an elegant off-white shade.
She chose an off-white dress for the summer party.
Many ceramics have a natural off-white color after firing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: white - the color of pure snow or milk

Etymology

Formed from 'off' (not entirely) + 'white'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɔfˈwaɪt / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈwaɪt / (BrE)
of a color that is close to but slightly different from pure white, often with a hint of gray, yellow, or beige.
nearly white color

Examples

The walls were painted an off-white shade to create a calm, neutral atmosphere.
She wore an elegant off-white dress to the summer wedding.
The furniture in the living room is mostly off-white, adding brightness to the space.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: white - the purest shade of white; the color of fresh snow
  • Degree Lower: cream - a pale yellowish-white color
