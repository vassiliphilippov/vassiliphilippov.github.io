Moehringia

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈhɪr.iːn.dʒə / (AmE), / məʊˈhɪər.ɪn.dʒɪə / (BrE)
A genus of low-growing herbs, known as sandworts, found in temperate and Arctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere, characterized by four-petaled flowers.
a genus of low-growing sandwort herbs

Examples

Moehringia species are often found in rocky alpine environments.
The botanist identified the plant as a member of the Moehringia genus due to its four-petaled flowers.
Unlike Arenaria, Moehringia typically has only four petals per blossom.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Arenaria - a related genus of sandworts with five-petaled flowers

Etymology

Named after Paul Möhring, an 18th-century German botanist.

2. NOUN

/ moʊˈrɪndʒiə / (AmE), / məʊˈrɪndʒɪə / (BrE)
A genus of low-growing herbs of the sandwort family, found in temperate and Arctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere, characterized by four-petaled flowers.
genus of sandwort plants with four-petaled flowers

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Moehringia genus during their Alpine field study.
Several Moehringia species are valued in rock gardens for their delicate appearance.
Unlike Arenaria, Moehringia flowers typically have four petals instead of five.

Etymology

Named after Paul Moehring, a German physician and botanist (1710–1792).
