Jute

1. NOUN

/ dʒuːt / (AmE), / dʒuːt / (BrE)
a member of an ancient Germanic people who settled in parts of England with the Angles and Saxons and became part of the Anglo-Saxon population
member of ancient Germanic people in England

Examples

The Jutes migrated from the Jutland peninsula to Britain in the 5th century.
Historians believe the Jutes settled mostly in Kent and the Isle of Wight.
Archaeological evidence shows distinct burial practices among the Jutes compared to the Angles and Saxons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Angle - member of a Germanic people who settled in England
  • Synonym: Saxon - member of a Germanic people who settled in England
  • Antonym: Briton - member of the Celtic people inhabiting Britain at the time of Roman occupation

Etymology

From Latin 'Juti', from Old English 'Iútan', referring to people from the Jutland peninsula.
