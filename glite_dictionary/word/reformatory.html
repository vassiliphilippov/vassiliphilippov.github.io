reformatory

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈfɔːrməˌtɔːri / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːmətəri / (BrE)
Tending or intended to reform, especially by improving character or behavior.
tending or intended to reform

Examples

The prison implemented reformatory policies to encourage inmate rehabilitation.
Some view strict curfews as a reformatory measure for troubled youth.
Her approach was both punitive and reformatory, focusing on changing behavior.

Synset Examples

reformative and rehabilitative agencies
reformatory punishment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reformative - having the intention or effect of reforming

Etymology

From reform +‎ -atory (influenced by Latin reformare 'to form again').

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈfɔrməˌtɔri / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːmətəri / (BrE)
a correctional institution for the detention, discipline, and training of young or first-time offenders
correctional institution for young offenders

Examples

After being caught stealing, the teenager was sent to a reformatory for rehabilitation.
Many reformatories focused on providing both discipline and education to young offenders.
He described his experiences at the reformatory in his autobiography.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juvenile detention center - facility for the detention of minors
  • Synonym: prison - building where people are legally held as punishment for a crime
  • Synonym: penitentiary - prison for people convicted of serious crimes

Etymology

From reform + -atory, indicating a place for reforming.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04076889-n
Synset POS: n
