dekalitre

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkəˌliːtər / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəlɪtə / (BrE)
A metric unit of volume equal to 10 liters.
metric unit equal to 10 liters

Examples

The tank can hold exactly five dekalitres of water.
Each batch of juice is packaged in containers of one dekalitre.
In scientific experiments, measurements are sometimes recorded in dekalitres instead of liters.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: liter - metric unit of capacity equal to 1,000 cubic centimeters
  • Degree Higher: hectoliter - metric unit of volume equal to 100 liters

Etymology

From French 'décalitre', from Greek 'deka' (ten) + French 'litre'.
