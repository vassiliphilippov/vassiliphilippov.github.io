moulin

1. NOUN

/ muːˈlæn / (AmE), / muːˈlæn / (BrE)
a vertical or nearly vertical shaft in a glacier, formed by surface water percolating down, through which water flows from the glacier's surface to its base
vertical shaft in a glacier for water

Examples

The icy river plunged, roaring, into the moulin's blue depths.
Glaciologists examined the moulin to understand how meltwater reached the base of the glacier.
A dramatic moulin opened up after a week of unusually warm weather.

Synset Examples

the icy river plunged, roaring, into the moulin's blue depths

Etymology

From French 'moulin' (mill), possibly from the shape and movement of the water resembling that of a mill.
