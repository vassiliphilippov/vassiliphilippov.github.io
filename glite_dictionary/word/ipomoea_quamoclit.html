Ipomoea quamoclit

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə kwæˈmɒklɪt / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə kwæˈmɒklɪt / (BrE)
A tropical American annual climbing plant with finely divided leaves and bright red or sometimes white tubular flowers, commonly known as cypress vine.
tropical climbing plant with red flowers; cypress vine

Examples

The Ipomoea quamoclit brings a vibrant splash of color to summer gardens with its red, star-shaped blooms.
Gardeners often plant Ipomoea quamoclit to attract hummingbirds and butterflies.
In warm climates, Ipomoea quamoclit can quickly cover trellises and fences with its delicate foliage and flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress vine - common name for Ipomoea quamoclit, tropical climbing plant with divided leaves and red flowers

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Ipomoea' and species name 'quamoclit', used in botanical nomenclature.
