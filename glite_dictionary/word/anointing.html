anointing

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɔɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈnɔɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of applying oil or an oily substance, especially as part of a religious or ceremonial ritual
the act of applying oil, often ceremonially

Examples

The priest performed the anointing of the sick during the ceremony.
In many traditions, anointing with oil symbolizes purification and blessing.
The anointing of kings was an important part of the coronation ritual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anoint - to apply oil, usually as a part of a religious or ceremonial ritual

Etymology

From Middle English 'anointen', from Old French 'enoindre', from Latin 'inungere' (to smear on).
