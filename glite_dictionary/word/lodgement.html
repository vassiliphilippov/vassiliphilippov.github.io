lodgement

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒmənt / (BrE)
the state or quality of being lodged or fixed, even temporarily, in a particular place
state of being lodged or fixed

Examples

The lodgement of dust in the gears caused the machine to malfunction.
A sudden lodgement of the ball in the gutter stopped the game.
We removed the lodgement of mud from the pipe to restore the water flow.

Synset Examples

the lodgment of the balloon in the tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lodgment - alternate spelling meaning state of being lodged

Etymology

From Old French *logement*, from *loger* (to lodge).
