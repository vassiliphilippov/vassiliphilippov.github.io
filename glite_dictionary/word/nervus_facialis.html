nervus facialis

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɜrvəs fəˈseɪliɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs fəˈseɪliːs / (BrE)
the seventh cranial nerve that controls the muscles of facial expression
the facial nerve; seventh cranial nerve
medical

Examples

Damage to the nervus facialis can result in facial paralysis.
The surgeon identified the nervus facialis during the procedure to avoid any injury.
Bell's palsy is a condition that affects the nervus facialis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facial nerve - the seventh cranial nerve controlling facial muscles

Etymology

Latin, meaning 'facial nerve' (nervus = nerve, facialis = of the face).
