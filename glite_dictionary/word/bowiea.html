Bowiea

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈwiːə / (AmE), / bəʊˈwiːə / (BrE)
a small genus of perennial bulbous plants native to tropical Africa, having deciduous, twining stems, sometimes classified in the family Hyacinthaceae.
a genus of African bulbous plants

Examples

Bowiea is best known for its species Bowiea volubilis, also called the climbing onion.
The unusual appearance of Bowiea makes it a favorite among succulent collectors.
Botanists debated whether to place Bowiea in Hyacinthaceae or Asparagaceae.

Etymology

Named after James Bowie, a British botanist (1789–1869).

2. NOUN

/ boʊˈwiːə / (AmE), / bəʊˈwiːə / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical African perennial bulbous herbs with twining stems, sometimes classified in the family Hyacinthaceae.
genus of tropical African bulbous herbs

Examples

The rare Bowiea plant is prized by collectors for its unusual appearance.
Some botanists debate whether Bowiea belongs in the family Hyacinthaceae or Asparagaceae.
The twining stems of Bowiea make it a distinctive species among African bulbous herbs.

Etymology

Named after James Bowie (1789–1869), English plant collector.
