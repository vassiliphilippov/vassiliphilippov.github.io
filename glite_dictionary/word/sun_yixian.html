Sun Yixian

1. PROPN

/ suːn jiːˈʃjɛn / (AmE), / suːn jiːˈʃjɛn / (BrE)
(1866–1925) Chinese statesman who founded the Kuomintang and led the revolution that overthrew the Manchu dynasty, becoming the first president of the Republic of China.
Chinese statesman, founder of modern China

Examples

Sun Yixian is widely regarded as the father of modern China for his revolutionary achievements.
The leadership of Sun Yixian helped to end thousands of years of imperial rule in China.
Many monuments in China commemorate Sun Yixian and his contributions to the country's development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sun Yat-sen - alternative name for Sun Yixian, Chinese revolutionary leader

Etymology

Mandarin Chinese 孫逸仙 (Sūn Yìxiān), also known as Sun Yat-sen.
