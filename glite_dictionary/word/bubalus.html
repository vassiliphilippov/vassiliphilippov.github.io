Bubalus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːbələs / (AmE), / ˈbjuːbələs / (BrE)
a genus of large bovids in the family Bovidae, including the water buffalo and related species
genus of water buffaloes

Examples

The Bubalus genus includes the well-known water buffalo.
Researchers studied wild populations of Bubalus in Southeast Asia.
Fossil remains of extinct species within Bubalus have been discovered in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bos - a genus of wild and domestic cattle

Etymology

From Latin 'bubalus' (wild ox, buffalo), itself from Greek 'boubalos' (a kind of African antelope or wild ox).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbjuː.bə.ləs / (AmE), / ˈbjuː.bə.ləs / (BrE)
a genus of large bovine mammals that includes the water buffalo and related species, sometimes classified under the genus Bos.
genus of water buffaloes

Examples

The domesticated water buffalo belongs to the genus Bubalus.
Researchers studied several species within the genus Bubalus across Asia.
Taxonomists debate whether Bubalus should be considered a separate genus from Bos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bos - genus that includes cattle and, in some classifications, water buffaloes

Etymology

From Latin *bubalus* (buffalo), from Greek *boubalos* (antelope or buffalo).
