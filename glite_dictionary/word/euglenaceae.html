Euglenaceae

1. NOUN

/ juːˌɡliːnəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / juːˌɡliːnəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of single-celled, typically green algae, including organisms such as Euglena, found mainly in freshwater environments.
family of green algae

Examples

Many species in the Euglenaceae are capable of both photosynthesis and movement using flagella.
Some freshwater ponds contain members of the Euglenaceae, visible under a microscope.
Research on Euglenaceae has contributed to understanding cell biology and photosynthesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Euglena - a genus of single-celled green algae in Euglenaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Euglena and the standard family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ juːˌɡliːnəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / juːˌɡliːnəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of mostly freshwater, flagellated, unicellular green algae, including the genus Euglena
a family of green algae

Examples

The Euglenaceae are found mainly in freshwater environments around the world.
Scientists have studied the role of Euglenaceae in aquatic ecosystems.
The distinctive shape and movement of Euglenaceae make them easy to recognize under a microscope.

Etymology

From Euglena (the type genus) + scientific family suffix -aceae.
