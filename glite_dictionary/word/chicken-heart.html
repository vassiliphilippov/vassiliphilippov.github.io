chicken-heart

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪk.ɪn hɑrt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.ɪn hɑːt / (BrE)
a person who is easily frightened; a coward
cowardly person
informal slang

Examples

No one wanted to be called a chicken-heart in front of the whole class.
Whenever there was trouble, Pete proved to be a chicken-heart and ran away.
She hated feeling like a chicken-heart when it came to taking risks.

Synset Examples

He called her a chicken-heart for avoiding the challenge.
She felt like a chicken-heart for backing down.
Don't be a chicken-heart; face your fears!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coward - a person who lacks courage
  • Antonym: brave - showing courage

Etymology

Formed from 'chicken' (a term for a coward) and 'heart,' referencing lack of courage.
