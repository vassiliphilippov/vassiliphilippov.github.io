Sayornis phoebe

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈɔrnɪs ˈfiːbi / (AmE), / saɪˈɔːnɪs ˈfiːbi / (BrE)
A small, plain-colored North American flycatcher species, also known as the Eastern Phoebe.
a small North American flycatcher bird

Examples

The Sayornis phoebe builds its nest on ledges and bridges near streams.
Birdwatchers often spot the Sayornis phoebe flicking its tail while perched.
You can identify the Sayornis phoebe by its distinctive 'phoebe' call.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eastern Phoebe - another common name for Sayornis phoebe

Etymology

From the genus name Sayornis (honoring Thomas Say) and specific epithet phoebe (from Greek myth, denoting brightness).
