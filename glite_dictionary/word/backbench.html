backbench

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌbɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˈbak.bɛntʃ / (BrE)
any of the seats in the House of Commons or other legislatures occupied by members who do not hold governmental office or opposition frontbench posts.
seat for ordinary member in parliament
british

Examples

She spent most of her career sitting on the backbench before becoming a minister.
The debate was lively, with several voices rising from the backbench.
Newly elected MPs usually start their parliamentary life on the backbench.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backbencher - a member of parliament who does not hold government office or sit on the front bench

Etymology

From 'back' + 'bench', referring to the benches at the back of parliamentary chambers.
