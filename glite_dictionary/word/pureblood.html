pureblood

1. ADJ

/ ˈpjʊrˌblʌd / (AmE), / ˈpjʊəˌblʌd / (BrE)
an animal having documented purebred ancestry, with a list of ancestors proving its breed purity
animal with documented purebred ancestry

Examples

The kennel only breeds pureblood dogs with excellent pedigrees.
Some horse shows require that all entrants be pureblood Arabians.
Having a pureblood lineage makes the animal more valuable to collectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purebred - an animal of unmixed breed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01910144-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊrˌblʌd / (AmE), / ˈpjʊəˌblʌd / (BrE)
An animal, especially a horse, of unmixed or pure lineage; a pedigreed animal whose ancestry is of the same breed.
animal of unmixed lineage (esp. horses)

Examples

The ranch specializes in breeding pureblood Arabian horses for international competitions.
After years of careful selection, they produced a prize-winning pureblood that was highly sought after.
Many breeders keep detailed records to ensure the lineage of each pureblood animal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoroughbred - a horse of pure breed, especially one of a breed developed for racing
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01326240-n
Synset POS: n
