Notropis cornutus

1. PROPN

/ noʊˈtroʊpɪs ˈkɔrnətəs / (AmE), / nəʊˈtrəʊpɪs ˈkɔːnətəs / (BrE)
A common species of freshwater fish found in North America, known as the common shiner.
species of North American freshwater fish

Examples

Many anglers are familiar with the Notropis cornutus as a bait fish in North American rivers.
Populations of Notropis cornutus often thrive in clear, fast-moving streams and small lakes.
Researchers observed the spawning behavior of the Notropis cornutus during early spring studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common shiner - another name for Notropis cornutus, a freshwater fish species

Etymology

From Greek 'noton' (back) + 'tropis' (keel), referring to the fish's body shape; 'cornutus' from Latin, meaning 'horned'.
