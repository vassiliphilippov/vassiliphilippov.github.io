neurotropism

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʊroʊˈtroʊpɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˈtrəʊpɪzəm / (BrE)
the tendency or affinity of an agent, such as a virus or cell, to target, affect, or be attracted to neural tissues
affinity for neural tissues
medical

Examples

The rabies virus exhibits a marked neurotropism, enabling it to infect brain tissue effectively.
Researchers study neurotropism to develop therapies that specifically target nervous system diseases.
Certain cancer cells show neurotropism, leading them to metastasize to the brain or spinal cord.

Etymology

From neuro- (relating to nerves) + -tropism (from Greek tropos, meaning 'turn').
