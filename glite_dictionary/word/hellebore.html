hellebore

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛl.ɪˌbɔr / (AmE), / ˈhɛl.ɪ.bɔː / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Helleborus, native to Eurasia, known for its leathery leaves and often poisonous qualities.
a Eurasian genus of flowering plants

Examples

The gardener planted several hellebore varieties to add winter color to the border.
Children should avoid touching wild hellebore because some species are toxic.
Hellebore blooms early in the spring, often pushing up through the last patches of snow.

Etymology

From Latin 'helleborus', from Ancient Greek 'helleboros'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlɪˌbɔr / (AmE), / ˈhɛlɪˌbɔː / (BrE)
A perennial herbaceous plant of the genus Helleborus, of the buttercup family, with thick, toxic rhizomes and mostly winter- or spring-blooming flowers.
toxic perennial herbaceous plant

Examples

The gardener wore gloves when handling the hellebore to protect her skin from its toxins.
Many hellebores bloom in late winter, adding color to otherwise bare gardens.
Some people plant hellebore under trees for early spring flowers and deer resistance.

Etymology

From Latin *Helleborus*, from Greek *helleboros*.
