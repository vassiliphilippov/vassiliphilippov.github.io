Nymphicus

1. NOUN

/ nɪmˈfɪkəs / (AmE), / nɪmˈfɪkəs / (BrE)
a genus of parrots in the family Psittacidae that includes the cockatiel (Nymphicus hollandicus)
genus of parrots; cockatiel genus

Examples

The cockatiel, the only member of the genus Nymphicus, is native to Australia.
Recent studies have clarified the evolutionary placement of Nymphicus within the parrot family.
Avian experts debate how closely Nymphicus is related to other cockatoos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockatiel - a small Australian parrot, member of Nymphicus

Etymology

From Greek nymphē (nymph, young woman), referencing the bird's delicate appearance.

2. NOUN

/ nɪmˈfaɪkəs / (AmE), / nɪmˈfɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of parrots in the family Psittacidae, containing only the cockatiel species.
genus of parrots including the cockatiel

Examples

The cockatiel is the only member of the genus Nymphicus.
Researchers discovered distinctive features that classify the bird within the Nymphicus genus.
Ornithologists often study Nymphicus to learn about parrot evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockatiel - an Australian parrot, the only species in the genus Nymphicus

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Nymphicus', based on 'nymph', referring to mythological spirits of nature.
