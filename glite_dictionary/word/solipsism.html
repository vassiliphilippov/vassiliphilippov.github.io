solipsism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑː.lɪpˌsɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈsɒ.lɪpˌsɪz.əm / (BrE)
(philosophy) the theory that only the self is sure to exist and knowledge of anything outside the mind is uncertain.
philosophical theory about self and reality

Examples

Some critics accuse extreme subjectivism of leading to solipsism.
The novel toys with the idea of solipsism, suggesting that reality may be merely a personal construct.
Her philosophical writings often address solipsism and the problem of other minds.

Etymology

From Latin 'solus' (alone) + 'ipse' (self).
