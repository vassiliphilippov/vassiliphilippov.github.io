alkahestic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæl.kəˈhɛs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌal.kəˈhɛs.tɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the alkahest, the hypothetical universal solvent believed by alchemists to be capable of dissolving any substance.
pertaining to the alkahest (universal solvent in alchemy)

Examples

Medieval writings are filled with alkahestic pursuits and legends of substances able to dissolve gold.
The alkahestic properties described in the text are purely speculative and lack scientific basis.
He devoted years to searching for an alkahestic fluid that could transmute metals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alkahest - the hypothetical universal solvent in alchemy

Etymology

From alkahest, a term introduced by alchemists (possibly from pseudo-Arabic roots), plus the suffix -ic.
