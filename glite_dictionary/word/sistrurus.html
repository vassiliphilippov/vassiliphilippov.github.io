Sistrurus

1. NOUN

/ sɪsˈtruːrəs / (AmE), / sɪsˈtrʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, venomous rattlesnakes native to North America, commonly known as pygmy rattlesnakes.
genus of small North American rattlesnakes

Examples

The Sistrurus genus includes several species of pygmy rattlesnakes found in the United States.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Sistrurus in southeastern forests.
Unlike larger rattlesnakes, the Sistrurus species are smaller and have a less prominent rattle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake - venomous American snake with a segmented rattle at the end of its tail

Etymology

From Greek 'sistron' (a kind of rattling instrument) and the suffix '-urus' (tail).
