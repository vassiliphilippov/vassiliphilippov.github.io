depressed

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈprɛst / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛst / (BrE)
(of a surface or structure) flattened downward or pressed in from above; having a dorsoventrally flattened shape.
flattened downward; dorsoventrally compressed

Examples

The shell of this species is depressed rather than domed.
In some beetles, the body is noticeably depressed to aid in burrowing.
The depressed discs can be seen clearly under the microscope.

Etymology

From Latin depressus, past participle of deprimere (to press down).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈprɛst / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛst / (BrE)
feeling very sad and without hope; filled with melancholy or despondency.
very sad and without hope

Examples

She felt depressed for weeks after losing her job.
He sounded depressed when he called last night.
The rainy weather made everyone in the office feel depressed and unmotivated.

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈprɛst / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛst / (BrE)
(of prices, markets, or economic conditions) lower than usual or previously; experiencing a decline.
lowered in value; in decline (economics)

Examples

The country's housing market remained depressed for several years after the crash.
During the recession, company profits were depressed compared to previous years.
Oil prices have been depressed by falling global demand.

Synset Examples

the market is depressed
prices are down

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elevated - higher than normal or previous level (opposite in value context)
  • Synonym: reduced - decreased in amount or value; made less

Etymology

From Latin 'depressus', past participle of 'deprimere' meaning 'to press down'.
