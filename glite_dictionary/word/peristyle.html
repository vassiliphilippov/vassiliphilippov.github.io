peristyle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛr.ɪˌstaɪl / (AmE), / ˈpɛr.ɪ.staɪl / (BrE)
A row of columns surrounding a building or enclosing a courtyard, especially in classical architecture.
colonnaded area around a building or court

Examples

The ancient Roman house featured a spacious peristyle filled with lush gardens.
Visitors admired the temple's impressive peristyle of marble columns.
A tranquil pool was situated at the center of the peristyle.

Etymology

From Greek peristylon, from peri- (around) + stylos (column).
