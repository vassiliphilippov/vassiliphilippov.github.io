Bouteloua gracilis

1. NOUN

/ buːtəˈluːə grəˈsɪlɪs / (AmE), / bjuːtəˈluːə ɡrəˈsɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of pasture grass native to western North America, commonly known as blue grama, important for grazing livestock.
species of pasture grass in North America

Examples

In Colorado, Bouteloua gracilis is a dominant species in the shortgrass prairie ecosystem.
Ranchers appreciate Bouteloua gracilis for its drought tolerance and nutritional value for cattle.
Researchers are studying the effects of climate change on Bouteloua gracilis populations.

Etymology

Bouteloua named after Spanish botanist Claudio Boutelou; gracilis from Latin meaning 'slender'.
