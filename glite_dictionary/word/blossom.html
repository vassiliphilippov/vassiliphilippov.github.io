blossom

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɑːsəm / (AmE), / ˈblɒsəm / (BrE)
a flower or mass of flowers, especially one of a fruit tree or other plant with showy or colorful parts
flower, especially of a tree or showy plant

Examples

The cherry trees are covered in pink blossom every spring.
A single white blossom appeared on the apple tree this morning.
The garden is full of the sweet scent of orange blossom in May.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flower - the reproductive structure in flowering plants; more general term

Etymology

Old English blōstm (flower, bloom), related to 'bloom'.

2. VERB

/ ˈblɑːsəm / (AmE), / ˈblɒsəm / (BrE)
to develop or flourish, especially into a promising or successful stage
develop or flourish successfully

Examples

After joining the art club, her confidence began to blossom.
The small startup quickly blossomed into a successful business.
Friendship often blossoms when people work together toward a common goal.

Synset Examples

Youth blossomed into maturity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flourish - to develop in a healthy or vigorous way
  • Antonym: to wither - to shrivel, fade, or decline

Etymology

From Old English blōstm, blōstma (flower, bloom).

3. NOUN

/ ˈblɑːsəm / (AmE), / ˈblɒsəm / (BrE)
a period of greatest prosperity or productivity; the peak or flourishing stage of something
peak period of prosperity or productivity

Examples

The city experienced its cultural blossom during the Renaissance.
The company is enjoying a blossom of innovation under its new leadership.
Her career reached its blossom in her thirties, when she published her best-known work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peak - the highest point or greatest level
  • Synonym: prime - the time of greatest strength or success
  • Antonym: decline - a period of decreasing prosperity or productivity

Etymology

From Old English blōstm (flower, bloom); the figurative sense developed by analogy with the flourishing of flowers.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈblɑːsəm / (AmE), / ˈblɒsəm / (BrE)
(of a tree or plant) to produce or yield flowers.
produce flowers (of a plant)

Examples

Every spring, the apple trees blossom with delicate white flowers.
The roses in the garden have just begun to blossom.
If you water your plants regularly, they will blossom beautifully.

Synset Examples

The cherry tree bloomed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloom - (of a plant) to produce flowers

Etymology
