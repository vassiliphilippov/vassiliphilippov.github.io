ambulatory

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmbjəˌleɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈæmbjʊlət(ə)ri / (BrE)
(architecture) a covered passageway for walking, typically found around the apse or cloister of a church or monastery.
covered walkway in church or cloister

Examples

The cathedral's ambulatory allowed visitors to walk behind the high altar.
Monks used the ambulatory for silent reflection and prayer.
The architect designed an elegant ambulatory around the central garden.

Synset Examples

it has an ambulatory and seven chapels

Etymology

From Medieval Latin ambulatorium, from Latin ambulare (to walk).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæmbjəˌleɪtɔri / (AmE), / ˈambjʊlət(ə)ri / (BrE)
able to walk; not confined to bed, especially used of patients in medical contexts.
able to walk about
medical

Examples

After surgery, the doctor determined the patient was ambulatory and could return home.
The clinic encourages ambulatory patients to participate in light exercise.
He was pleased to be ambulatory again after weeks with a broken leg.

Synset Examples

the patient is ambulatory

Etymology

From Latin *ambulatorius* (of or pertaining to walking), from *ambulare* (to walk).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01527104-s

3. ADJ

/ ˈæmbjəˌlɛːtɔri / (AmE), / ˈæmbjʊlət(ə)ri / (BrE)
relating to or adapted for walking; able to walk
adapted for walking

Examples

The hospital has an ambulatory wing designed for patients who can walk on their own.
Some birds have highly ambulatory legs adapted for foraging on the ground.
The cathedral featured an ambulatory passage allowing visitors to walk behind the main altar.

Synset Examples

an ambulatory corridor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mobile - able to move or be moved freely or easily

Etymology

From Latin 'ambulare' (to walk).
