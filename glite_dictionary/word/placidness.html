placidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæsɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈplæsɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being calm and undisturbed; tranquil calmness.
calm and peaceful state

Examples

The placidness of the lake in the early morning was truly breathtaking.
Her placidness during the crisis reassured everyone around her.
He admired the placidness of the countryside, far from the city's noise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calmness - the state of being free from agitation or strong emotion
  • Synonym: serenity - the state of being calm, peaceful, and untroubled
  • Antonym: agitation - a state of anxiety or nervous excitement

Etymology

From Latin placidus (calm, gentle) + -ness.
