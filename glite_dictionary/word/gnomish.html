gnomish

1. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊmɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊmɪʃ / (BrE)
resembling or characteristic of a gnome, especially in being small and often misshapen or fantastical
resembling or related to a gnome

Examples

The old man had a gnomish appearance, with a long beard and twinkling eyes.
Her gnomish features made her stand out among the other fairies in the story.
The artist drew a gnomish figure crouched beneath the roots of a tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gnome - a small imaginary being, often depicted as shriveled and misshapen, that lives underground

Etymology

From 'gnome' + '-ish', with 'gnome' from Renaissance Latin 'gnomus' (earth-dwelling spirit).
