ogress

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.ɡrɪs / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.ɡrɛs / (BrE)
(in folklore) a female ogre, especially one depicted as very large, fierce, or terrifying.
female ogre in folklore

Examples

The children in the story narrowly escaped from the wicked ogress who lived in the dark forest.
Legends often describe the ogress as being even more cunning than her male counterpart.
In the play, she wore a costume to become a terrifying ogress who chased villagers away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ogre - a man-eating giant or monster in folklore

Etymology

From French 'ogresse', feminine form of 'ogre'.
