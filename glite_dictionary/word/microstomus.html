Microstomus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.krəˈstoʊ.məs / (AmE), / ˌmɪ.krəˈstəʊ.məs / (BrE)
A genus of righteye flounders found in the North Pacific and Northeast Atlantic Oceans.
a genus of righteye flounders (fish)

Examples

The genus Microstomus includes several commercially important species of flatfish.
Scientists studied the distribution of Microstomus in northern Atlantic waters.
The common dab is one of the best-known members of the Microstomus genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'stoma' (mouth), referring to the small mouth of fishes in this genus.

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈkroʊstoʊməs / (AmE), / mʌɪˈkrɒstəʊməs / (BrE)
A genus of right-eye flounders in the family Pleuronectidae, native to the North Pacific and Northeast Atlantic Oceans.
genus of right-eye flounders

Examples

The genus Microstomus includes several species of commercially important flatfish.
Biologists studied the habitat preferences of Microstomus in the North Pacific.
You can find Microstomus species in both the North Atlantic and North Pacific Oceans.

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) and 'stoma' (mouth).
