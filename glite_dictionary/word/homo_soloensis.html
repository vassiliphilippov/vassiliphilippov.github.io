Homo soloensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊmoʊ soʊloʊˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməʊ səʊləʊˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
an extinct primitive hominid species from the late Pleistocene epoch, discovered in Java and formerly known as Javanthropus
extinct hominid species from Java

Examples

Fossils of Homo soloensis were discovered near the Solo River in Java.
Scientists debate whether Homo soloensis should be classified as a separate species or a subspecies of Homo erectus.
The skulls attributed to Homo soloensis exhibit characteristics intermediate between earlier hominids and modern humans.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Homo' meaning 'man' and 'soloensis' referring to the Solo River in Java, where remains were found.
