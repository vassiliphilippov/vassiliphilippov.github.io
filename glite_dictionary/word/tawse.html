tawse

1. NOUN

/ tɔz / (AmE), / tɔːz / (BrE)
a leather strap, often split at one end, formerly used for corporal punishment, especially in Scottish schools
leather strap for punishment
archaic british

Examples

The teacher used a tawse to discipline unruly students in the past.
He winced at the memory of the tawse landing sharply on his palm.
A tawse was displayed in the museum as a relic of old educational practices.

Etymology

From Scots, plural of 'taws', of uncertain origin, possibly from Old French 'tauser' (to handle or pull about).
