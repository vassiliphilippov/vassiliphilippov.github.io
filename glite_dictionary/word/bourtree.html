bourtree

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrˌtri / (AmE), / ˈbɔːˌtriː / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree (Sambucus nigra), native to Europe and Asia, with clusters of small white flowers and black berries used for wines and jellies
common elder shrub or tree (Sambucus nigra)

Examples

The old bourtree at the edge of the field is heavy with ripe berries in late summer.
Local villagers collect bourtree fruit every year to make homemade wine and preserves.
A flock of birds landed in the bourtree, attracted by the abundance of black berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elder - the common name for shrubs or small trees of the genus Sambucus

Etymology

From Scots 'bourtree', variant of 'elder tree', ultimately from Old English 'ellen' or 'eller'.
