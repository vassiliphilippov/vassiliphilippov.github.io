corrosiveness

1. NOUN

/ kəˈroʊsɪvnəs / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊsɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality or property of being able to cause deterioration or damage, especially by chemical action.
property of causing damage by chemical action

Examples

The corrosiveness of seawater poses a challenge for shipbuilders.
Scientists measured the corrosiveness of various cleaning agents in the lab.
When selecting materials, engineers must consider the corrosiveness of the environment.

Synset Examples

The corrosiveness of the acid damaged the metal quickly.
The corrosiveness of the solution requires careful handling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrosive - having the quality of corroding or causing corrosion
  • Antonym: inertness - the property of being chemically inactive

Etymology

From 'corrosive', which comes from Latin 'corrosivus', from 'corrodere' (to gnaw through, corrode) + '-ness' forming a noun.
