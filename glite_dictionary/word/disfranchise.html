to disfranchise

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (AmE), / dɪsˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (BrE)
to deprive someone of the right to vote in elections
deprive of the right to vote

Examples

The new law would disfranchise thousands of minority voters.
Women were disfranchised until the early twentieth century in many countries.
Campaigners protested against the plan to disfranchise prisoners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disenfranchise - to deprive of the right to vote

Etymology

From Old French 'desfranchir,' meaning to disfranchise or deprive of privileges; based on 'franchise' (freedom, right).
