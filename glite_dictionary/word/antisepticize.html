to antisepticize

1. VERB

/ ˌæn.tɪˈsɛp.tɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈsɛp.tɪ.saɪz / (BrE)
to disinfect or treat with an antiseptic to prevent infection
disinfect with an antiseptic
medical

Examples

Before surgery, the nurse will antisepticize the patient's skin to reduce the risk of infection.
Veterinarians commonly antisepticize wounds on animals before applying bandages.
The laboratory protocol requires staff to antisepticize their hands before handling samples.

Synset Examples

The animals were antisepticized by the veterinarian before the operation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disinfect - to clean something to destroy bacteria or viruses
  • Antonym: to contaminate - to make something impure by exposure to or addition of a poisonous or polluting substance

Etymology

From antiseptic + -ize, meaning to make antiseptic.
