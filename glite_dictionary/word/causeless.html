causeless

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔzləs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːzləs / (BrE)
Without a justifying cause or reason; unwarranted.
without cause or justification

Examples

The judge described the attack as a causeless act of violence.
They entered into a causeless argument that left everyone confused.
His sudden departure seemed completely causeless to his friends.

Synset Examples

a senseless, causeless murder
a causeless war that never had an aim
an apparently arbitrary and reasonless change

Related Terms

  • Antonym: justified - having or shown to have a sufficient legal or other reason
  • Synonym: reasonless - lacking a reason; irrational

Etymology

Formed from 'cause' + '-less' (lacking).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɔz.ləs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːz.ləs / (BrE)
without a cause or apparent cause; happening for no identifiable reason
without a cause or apparent cause

Examples

The rumors spread by the press created a causeless panic among the public.
He felt a causeless sense of dread as he walked into the room.
Some friendships end not because of conflict, but for seemingly causeless reasons.

Synset Examples

a causeless miracle
fortuitous encounters — strange accidents of fortune
we cannot regard artistic invention as … uncaused and unrelated to the times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncaused - not brought about by any cause
  • Antonym: caused - having a cause or reason

Etymology

Formed from 'cause' + the suffix '-less', meaning 'without'.
