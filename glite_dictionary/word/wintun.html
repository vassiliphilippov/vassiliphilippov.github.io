Wintun

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntuːn / (AmE), / ˈwɪntuːn / (BrE)
A Copehan language traditionally spoken by the Wintun people of northern California.
a Native American language of northern California

Examples

The traditional stories of the Wintun were originally told in the Wintun language.
Linguists have documented efforts to revive Wintun among the local communities.
She is studying Wintun to better understand the region's cultural history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wintun - a member of the Wintun people

Etymology

Name of the indigenous group self-identified as Wintʼu; used to designate both the people and their language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪn.tuːn / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.tuːn / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people inhabiting the Sacramento Valley in California.
member of a Native American group in California

Examples

The Wintun traditionally hunted and fished along the Sacramento River.
Several Wintun communities still preserve their native language and customs.
A descendant of the Wintun people shared stories of his ancestors' way of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pomo - another Native American people of northern California
  • Synonym: Maidu - another Native American group of northern California
