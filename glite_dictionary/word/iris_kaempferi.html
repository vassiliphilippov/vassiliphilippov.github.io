Iris kaempferi

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪs kaɪmˈfɜːrɪ / (AmE), / ˈaɪrɪs kaːmˈfɜːrɪ / (BrE)
a species of iris native to Japan, known for its large, showy flowers often cultivated in gardens
Japanese iris species with large flowers

Examples

The garden features a collection of Iris kaempferi, adding vibrant color in early summer.
Botanists are studying the hybridization of Iris kaempferi with other iris species.
Many flower enthusiasts prize Iris kaempferi for its elegant and dramatic blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a plant of the genus Iris, with sword-shaped leaves and showy flowers

Etymology

From the genus name Iris and the commemorative epithet kaempferi, after German botanist Engelbert Kaempfer.
