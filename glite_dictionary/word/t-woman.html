T-woman

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiː ˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈtiː ˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A female special law-enforcement agent of the United States Treasury.
female Treasury law-enforcement agent (US)
american

Examples

The undercover T-woman successfully infiltrated the counterfeiting ring.
As a T-woman, she specialized in investigating financial crimes for the Treasury Department.
The film depicted the challenges faced by a pioneering T-woman in the 1940s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: T-man - a male special law-enforcement agent of the United States Treasury
  • Synonym: G-man - a special law-enforcement agent of the US government, especially the FBI

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'G-man' (government man), using 'T' for 'Treasury' and 'woman' for the female agent.
