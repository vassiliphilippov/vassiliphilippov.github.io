solemnity

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɛm.nə.ti / (AmE), / səˈlɛm.nɪ.ti / (BrE)
a serious and dignified feeling or atmosphere; the quality of being solemn
serious and dignified feeling or atmosphere

Examples

The solemnity of the occasion was reflected in the silence of the crowd.
Her voice carried a solemnity that made everyone pause and listen carefully.
During the ceremony, there was a sense of solemnity in the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravity - seriousness and importance
  • Antonym: levity - lightness of manner or lacking seriousness

Etymology

From Old French 'solemnité', from Latin 'sollemnitas', from 'sollemnis' (customary, solemn).

2. NOUN

/ səˈlɛm.nə.t̬i / (AmE), / səˈlɛmn.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being serious, formal, and dignified
dignified seriousness

Examples

The solemnity of the ceremony moved everyone present to silence.
Her speech was delivered with an air of solemnity that emphasized the importance of the occasion.
Despite the festive decorations, there was a solemnity in the room as the announcement was made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravity - seriousness of manner or conduct
  • Antonym: levity - lack of seriousness

Etymology

From Old French 'solemnité', from Latin 'solemnitas' (ceremony, solemnity).
