Micropterus pseudoplites

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈkrɑptərəs ˌsuːdoʊˈplaɪtiːz / (AmE), / mɪˈkrɒptərəs ˌsjuːdəʊˈplaɪtiːz / (BrE)
A species of freshwater fish in the sunfish family, commonly known as the spotted bass, native to the central and southern United States.
species of freshwater fish (spotted bass)

Examples

Anglers often seek out Micropterus pseudoplites in rivers and reservoirs for sport fishing.
The Micropterus pseudoplites can be differentiated from other bass by its distinct spotted pattern.
Biologists studied the habitat preferences of Micropterus pseudoplites in the Ozark region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted bass - common name for Micropterus pseudoplites

Etymology

From Greek 'micros' (small), 'pteron' (fin), and 'pseudes' (false) + 'hoplites' (armed soldier), referring to fin structure and appearance.
