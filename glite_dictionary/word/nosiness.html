nosiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊzi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnəʊz.i.nəs / (BrE)
A tendency to be too interested in other people's affairs; inquisitiveness that is considered rude or intrusive.
offensive inquisitiveness

Examples

Her nosiness about her neighbors' lives made her unpopular on the street.
Some people mistake friendly concern for nosiness.
If you keep asking personal questions, your nosiness will upset your friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curiosity - a strong desire to know or learn something
  • Synonym: intrusiveness - the quality of being unwelcome or invasive
  • Antonym: discretion - the quality of behaving or speaking in such a way as to avoid causing offense
