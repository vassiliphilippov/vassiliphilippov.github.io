libertine

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪbərˌtin / (AmE), / ˈlɪb.ə.tiːn / (BrE)
a person, especially a man, who behaves without moral principles or a sense of responsibility, particularly in sexual matters
morally unrestrained person

Examples

His reputation as a libertine was well known throughout the city.
The novel's protagonist is a charming libertine who disregards society's rules.
She warned her daughter about falling for a notorious libertine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profligate - a person who is recklessly extravagant or wasteful in the use of resources; also dissolute
  • Antonym: puritan - a person with strict moral beliefs, especially about sexual behavior

Etymology

From Latin *libertinus* (freedman, from *liber* meaning 'free').

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪbərˌtin / (AmE), / ˈlɪbətiːn / (BrE)
Showing or characterized by a disregard for moral conventions, especially in sexual matters; unrestrained by conventional or moral rules.
unrestrained by moral convention

Examples

He was known for his libertine lifestyle, ignoring the strict codes of his family.
The author's libertine characters scandalized Victorian society.
Her libertine attitude shocked her more conventional friends.

Synset Examples

Congreve draws a debauched aristocratic society
deplorably dissipated and degraded
riotous living
fast women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debauched - indulging in or characterized by sensual pleasures to excess
  • Synonym: dissolute - lax in morals; licentious
  • Antonym: pure - free from moral fault or guilt; innocent

Etymology

From Latin 'libertinus' (freedman, libertine), from 'liber' (free).
