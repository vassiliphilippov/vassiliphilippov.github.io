gryphon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪfən / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪfən / (BrE)
a mythical creature with the head and wings of an eagle and the body of a lion
mythical creature, part eagle, part lion

Examples

The ancient tapestry depicts a fierce gryphon guarding a treasure hoard.
In legends, the gryphon was believed to be a powerful and noble guardian.
She chose a gryphon as the emblem for her family's crest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: griffin - mythical creature with the head and wings of an eagle and the body of a lion

Etymology

From Old French 'griffon', from Latin 'gryphus', from Greek 'gryps, gryph-' (curved, hook-nosed).
