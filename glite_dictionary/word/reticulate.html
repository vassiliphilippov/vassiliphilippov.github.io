reticulate

1. ADJ

/ rəˈtɪkjʊlət / (AmE), / rɪˈtɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
having a pattern or structure like a net; forming or resembling a network.
having a network-like structure

Examples

The leaf's reticulate veins create a beautiful, web-like design.
Many reptiles have reticulate patterns on their skin that help with camouflage.
A reticulate network of canals distributes water evenly across the agricultural fields.

Synset Examples

the reticulate veins of a leaf
a reticulated highway system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: networked - having the structure of a network
  • Synonym: reticulated - having an arrangement or appearance like a network

Etymology

From Latin reticulatus, past participle of reticulare (to form a network), from reticulum (a small net).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈtɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈtɪkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to divide or arrange (something) in the form of a network; to form into a net-like pattern
arrange or divide into a network

Examples

The veins in a leaf often reticulate to form complex patterns.
Over millions of years, rivers can reticulate across floodplains and create new landscapes.
The city planner sought to reticulate the streets to improve traffic flow.

Etymology

From Latin reticulatus, past participle of reticulare (to make like a net), from reticulum (small net).

/ rɪˈtɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈtɪkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(technical) To distribute or arrange something in the form of a network, especially for the supply of water, electricity, or other services.
to distribute or arrange in a network

Examples

The city decided to reticulate the new suburb with underground water pipes.
Engineers will soon reticulate the area to provide reliable electricity to every household.
Plans were drawn up to reticulate the entire region with a modern gas supply network.

Etymology

From Latin 'reticulatus' (netted), from 'reticulum' (net).
