muckhill

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌkˌhɪl / (AmE), / ˈmʌkˌhɪl / (BrE)
a heap or pile of dung or waste matter, especially on a farm
heap of dung or refuse

Examples

The farmer added the manure to the muckhill behind the barn.
We could smell the muckhill long before we saw the farm.
Rats had made their home in the old muckhill near the fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dunghill - a heap of dung; a similar concept

Etymology

From 'muck' (dung, dirt) + 'hill' (heap or mound), both of Old English origin.
