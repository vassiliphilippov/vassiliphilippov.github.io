voluptuousness

1. NOUN

/ vəˈlʌptʃuəsnəs / (AmE), / vəˈlʌptjuəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having a full, curvaceous, and attractive body, often associated with sensuality
fullness and sensual attractiveness of the body

Examples

The actress was celebrated for her voluptuousness and her charismatic screen presence.
Many Renaissance paintings depict female figures with striking voluptuousness.
He admired the voluptuousness of her figure, which evoked classic ideals of beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curvaceousness - the quality of having an attractively curved body

Etymology

From Latin 'voluptuosus' (full of pleasure), from 'voluptas' (pleasure).

2. NOUN

/ vəˈlʌp.tʃu.əs.nəs / (AmE), / vəˈlʌp.tjʊ.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being lush, abundant, and giving pleasure to the senses, especially in a rich and sensual way
pleasurable sensual abundance

Examples

The voluptuousness of the garden made it feel like a hidden paradise.
She admired the voluptuousness of the velvet drapes in the grand hall.
The painter tried to capture the voluptuousness of ripe fruit in his still life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensuality - the enjoyment, expression, or pursuit of physical, especially sexual, pleasure
  • Antonym: austerity - plainness and simplicity, often with absence of comfort or luxury

Etymology

From Latin 'voluptuosus' (full of pleasure), from 'voluptas' (pleasure).

3. NOUN

/ vəˈlʌptʃuəsnəs / (AmE), / vəˈlʌptjʊəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being sexually attractive and exciting, especially appealing to the senses or physical pleasure
sexual or sensual attractiveness

Examples

Her voluptuousness was celebrated in the art world for its bold depiction of feminine beauty.
The actress's voluptuousness captivated audiences in the glamorous new film.
People often confuse voluptuousness with superficiality, but it can also imply confidence and vitality.

Synset Examples

he thought she was really hot stuff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensuality - the enjoyment or pursuit of sensual pleasure
  • Antonym: chasteness - the quality of being pure or morally restrained in sexual matters

Etymology

From Latin voluptuosus (full of pleasure, voluptuous) via Old French voluptuos and Middle English.
