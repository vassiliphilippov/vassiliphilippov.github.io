ephebeia

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈfiːbiə / (AmE), / ɛˈfiːbiə / (BrE)
in ancient Greece, a state institution for training freeborn young men, usually aged 18 to 20, in military and civic duties.
ancient Greek youth training institution

Examples

The city-state required all eligible young men to participate in the ephebeia before becoming full citizens.
Archaeologists found epigraphic records describing the organization of the ephebeia in Hellenistic Athens.
The historian gave a lecture on how the ephebeia influenced civic identity in ancient Greece.

Synset Examples

This dissertation provides a new diachronic history of the Athenian ephebeia, a state-sponsored and -directed system of military training for ephebes.

Etymology

From Greek ἐφηβεία (ephēbeia), from ἔφηβος (ephēbos, 'youth of military age').
