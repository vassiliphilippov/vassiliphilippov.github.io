thawed

1. ADJ

/ θɔd / (AmE), / θɔːd / (BrE)
(of something previously frozen) no longer frozen; having been allowed to reach a temperature above freezing.
no longer frozen

Examples

Please keep the thawed chicken in the refrigerator until you are ready to cook it.
After hours in the sun, the once solid ice pack was completely thawed.
Make sure the berries are fully thawed before adding them to the dessert.

Synset Examples

the thawed ice was treacherous

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frozen - solidified by cold; turned into ice

Etymology

From the verb 'thaw', originating from Old English *thawian* (to melt).

2. ADJ

/ θɔd / (AmE), / θɔːd / (BrE)
(of something previously frozen) having become unfrozen; no longer in a frozen state.
no longer frozen; unfrozen

Examples

After several hours sitting on the counter, the thawed chicken was ready to be cooked.
The thawed river allowed the boats to set sail earlier than usual this year.
She walked carefully across the thawed field, which had turned soft and muddy.

Synset Examples

the thawed ground was muddy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frozen - in a solid state due to cold; having become ice
