3. NOUN

/ θroʊt / (AmE), / θrəʊt / (BrE)
The passage in the front part of the neck, below the chin and above the collarbone, that leads to the stomach and lungs.
passage in neck to stomach and lungs

Examples

She wore a scarf around her throat to keep warm during the winter.
He cleared his throat before beginning his speech.
Her throat was sore from coughing all night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neck - the part of the body connecting the head to the rest of the body

Etymology

Old English ‘throte’ (throat, neck), related to Dutch ‘strot’.
