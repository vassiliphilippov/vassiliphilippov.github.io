pekoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɛkəʊ / (BrE)
a high-quality grade of black tea made from young leaves and buds, often grown in India, Sri Lanka, and Java
high-quality grade of black tea

Examples

She always prefers a cup of pekoe in the afternoon instead of green tea.
The shop sells high-quality pekoe imported directly from Sri Lanka.
I could taste the fresh and delicate flavor characteristic of pekoe in this blend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orange pekoe - a specific grade of black tea made from young leaves

Etymology

From Chinese (Min Nan dialect), from 福建话 (Hokkien) 'pe̍h-ho', meaning 'white down' (referring to the fine hairs on young tea leaves).
