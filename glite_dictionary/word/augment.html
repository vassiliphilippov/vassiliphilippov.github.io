to augment

1. VERB

/ ɔgˈmɛnt / (AmE), / ɔːgˈmɛnt / (BrE)
to increase or make something increase in amount, size, or intensity
increase in amount or intensity

Examples

The city council voted to augment the budget for public safety.
As the tension in the room augmented, everyone grew more anxious.
Regular exercise can augment your strength and stamina over time.

Synset Examples

The pressure augmented

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - to become or make greater in size, amount, or degree
  • Antonym: to diminish - to make or become less

Etymology

From Latin augmentare (to increase), from augmentum (an increase).

2. VERB

/ ɔɡˈmɛnt / (AmE), / ɔːɡˈmɛnt / (BrE)
to make something greater by adding to it; to increase or enlarge.
to increase or make larger

Examples

They plan to augment the city budget by introducing new taxes.
Her responsibilities at work were augmented after the restructuring.
Using graphs and images can augment your presentation significantly.

Synset Examples

The recent speech of the president augmented tensions in the Near East

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - become or make greater in size, amount, or degree
  • Antonym: to diminish - to make or become less

Etymology

From Latin 'augmentare' (to increase), from 'augere' (to increase).
