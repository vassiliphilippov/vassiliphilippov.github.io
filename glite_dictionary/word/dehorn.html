to dehorn

1. VERB

/ diˈhɔrn / (AmE), / diˈhɔːn / (BrE)
to remove or prevent the growth of horns on an animal, typically livestock
remove or prevent animal horns

Examples

The farmer decided to dehorn the calves to make them easier to manage.
It is standard practice to dehorn cattle on many ranches for safety reasons.
Special tools are used to dehorn goats when they are young.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disbud - remove the horn buds from a young animal to prevent horn growth

Etymology

From prefix 'de-' (removal) + 'horn'; patterned after similar formations like 'defang'.

2. VERB

/ diˈhɔrn / (AmE), / diˈhɔːn / (BrE)
to remove the horns from (an animal, especially livestock)
remove the horns from an animal

Examples

It is common practice to dehorn young cattle to prevent injuries to other animals.
The veterinarian was called to dehorn several goats on the farm.
Farmers sometimes dehorn livestock for safety and management reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disbud - remove the buds that would grow into horns (in young animals)

Etymology

From de- (removal) + horn.
