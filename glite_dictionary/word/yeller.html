yeller

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈjɛlə / (BrE)
a person who communicates vocally in a very loud voice, especially by shouting
person who shouts loudly

Examples

The coach was known as a yeller, always directing the team with booming commands.
As a yeller, he could be heard all the way down the hallway.
Some parents discourage being a yeller at home, preferring a calm approach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shouter - someone who shouts or calls out loudly
  • Antonym: whisperer - someone who speaks in a very quiet voice

Etymology

From 'yell' (to shout) plus agentive suffix '-er'.
