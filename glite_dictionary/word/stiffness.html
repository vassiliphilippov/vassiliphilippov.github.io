stiffness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪfnəs / (AmE), / ˈstɪfnɪs / (BrE)
the condition of being unable to move easily or without pain, often due to injury, illness, or overuse
difficulty or pain in movement

Examples

After spending hours gardening, she felt a strong stiffness in her lower back.
The stiffness in his legs made it hard for him to climb the stairs.
Regular stretching can help reduce morning stiffness in your joints.

Synset Examples

he awoke with a painful stiffness in his neck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and inflexible
  • Antonym: flexibility - the quality of bending easily without breaking

Etymology

From Old English 'stif' (rigid, inflexible) + '-ness' (state or quality).

Examples

Related Terms
