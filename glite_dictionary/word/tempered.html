tempered

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛmpɚd / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəd / (BrE)
(of something) moderated or made less extreme by the addition of a counterbalancing element.
moderated by a balancing element

Examples

Her anger was tempered by her understanding of the situation.
The harsh rules were tempered with compassion for new members.
His criticism was tempered by sincere concern for the team's progress.

Synset Examples

criticism tempered with kindly sympathy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderated - become less extreme or intense

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'temper', from Old French 'temprer', from Latin 'temperare' (to moderate, mix, or blend).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɛmpɚd / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəd / (BrE)
(of metal, glass, etc.) made hard, flexible, or resilient, especially by being subjected to heat or chemical treatment.
hardened or toughened by treatment

Examples

The chef's knife is made from tempered steel, ensuring both strength and sharpness.
Many modern buildings use tempered glass for windows due to its safety qualities.
The engineer recommended installing tempered bolts to withstand higher stress levels.

Synset Examples

a sword of tempered steel
tempered glass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardened - made hard or tough, especially by processing
  • Antonym: annealed - (of metal or glass) made tough by controlled cooling

Etymology

From Old French *tempre*, from Latin *temperare* (to mix, moderate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01525661-a
Synset POS: a
