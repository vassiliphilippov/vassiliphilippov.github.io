Charolais

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑːrəlˌeɪ / (AmE), / ʃaːrəˌleɪ / (BrE)
a large breed of white or cream-colored cattle originating from France, valued for its meat
French breed of large white cattle

Examples

The farmer decided to raise Charolais because of their rapid weight gain and high-quality beef.
A herd of Charolais grazed peacefully in the lush French pastures.
Many ranchers crossbreed Charolais with other cattle to improve meat yield.

Etymology

From French Charolais, named after the Charolais region in France where the breed originated.
