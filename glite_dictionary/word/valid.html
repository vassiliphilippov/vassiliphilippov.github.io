valid

1. ADJ

/ ˈvælɪd / (AmE), / ˈvælɪd / (BrE)
based on sound reasoning or evidence; legally or officially acceptable or effective
legally or logically sound

Examples

The lawyer assured us that the contract was valid in all respects.
Only a valid argument will be accepted in the debate.
Her passport is no longer valid and needs to be renewed.

Synset Examples

a valid inference
a valid argument
a valid contract

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invalid - not valid; without legal or logical force

Etymology

From Latin 'validus' (strong, powerful), from 'valere' (to be strong).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02508521-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈvælɪd / (AmE), / ˈvælɪd / (BrE)
(of a document, ticket, contract, etc.) officially acceptable and still in force according to the law or rules.
legally acceptable; still in force

Examples

Your driver's license is valid for another year.
The coupon is only valid until the end of the month.
Is this parking permit still valid or has it expired?

Synset Examples

the license is still valid

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invalid - not legally or officially acceptable

Etymology

From Latin validus (strong, powerful), from valēre (to be strong).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00941928-s
Synset POS: s
