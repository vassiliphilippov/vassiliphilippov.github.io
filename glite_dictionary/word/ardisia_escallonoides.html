Ardisia escallonoides

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈdɪziə ɛˌskæləˈnɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ɑːˈdɪzɪə ɛˌskæləˈnɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A tropical American shrub or small tree with brown wood and dark berries, often found in coastal forests.
tropical American shrub or tree with dark berries

Examples

The Ardisia escallonoides is prized for its hard brown wood in tropical regions.
Berries of the Ardisia escallonoides provide food for birds in coastal forests.
Botanists studied the leaf arrangement of Ardisia escallonoides in its natural habitat.

Etymology

Ardisia from Greek 'ardis' (point or arrowhead); escallonoides refers to resemblance to the genus Escallonia.
