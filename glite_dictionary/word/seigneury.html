seigneury

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪn.jə.ri / (AmE), / ˈseɪn.jʊəri / (BrE)
the position, authority, or domain of a feudal lord, especially in the feudal system of France or French Canada
authority or domain of a feudal lord

Examples

The vast seigneury was passed down from generation to generation.
Under the old regime, peasants were obliged to pay dues to the seigneury.
After the revolution, the privileges of the seigneury were abolished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lordship - the authority or domain of a lord

Etymology

From Old French 'seigneurie' (lordship), from 'seigneur' (lord), ultimately from Latin 'senior' (elder).

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪn.jə.ri / (AmE), / ˈseɪn.jəri / (BrE)
an estate or domain under the control of a seigneur, especially in the feudal system
estate governed by a seigneur

Examples

The land was organized into a seigneury controlled by a powerful French noble.
Many rural communities in Quebec developed within the boundaries of a seigneury.
The history of the seigneury is central to understanding the region's colonial past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manor - the house or lands of a feudal lord

Etymology

From Old French seigneurie, from seigneur (lord).
