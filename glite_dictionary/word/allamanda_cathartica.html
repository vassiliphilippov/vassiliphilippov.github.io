Allamanda cathartica

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.əˈmændə kəˈθɑrtɪkə / (AmE), / ˌal.əˈmandə kəˈθɑːtɪkə / (BrE)
A vigorous evergreen climbing plant native to South America, with glossy leathery leaves and large golden yellow flowers.
South American climbing plant with yellow flowers

Examples

The garden was adorned with the vibrant flowers of Allamanda cathartica climbing over the trellis.
They planted Allamanda cathartica by the fence to add a tropical feel to their backyard.
The glossy leaves and bright blooms of Allamanda cathartica attract butterflies during the summer months.

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Allamanda' named after Swiss botanist F. Allamand, 'cathartica' from Greek 'kathartikos' meaning purgative.
