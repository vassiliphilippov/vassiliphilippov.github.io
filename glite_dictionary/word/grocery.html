grocery

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡroʊsəri / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊsəri / (BrE)
A shop or store where food and other household supplies are sold.
shop selling food and household items

Examples

She walked to the grocery to pick up some fresh vegetables for dinner.
The new grocery in town offers a wide variety of organic products.
He manages a small, family-owned grocery on the corner of Maple Street.

Synset Examples

the grocery store included a meat market

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grocery store - a shop that sells food and household goods
  • Synonym: supermarket - a large self-service shop selling foods and household goods

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *grosserie*, from Old French *grossier* (wholesaler), ultimately from Latin *grossus* (large).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡroʊsəri / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊsəri / (BrE)
an item of food or other goods sold by a grocer, usually referring to items bought for household use, especially foodstuffs
food and household items sold by a grocer

Examples

She stopped at the supermarket to pick up some groceries for dinner.
The rising cost of groceries has made it harder for many families to budget.
He carried the bags of groceries up three flights of stairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groceries - plural form meaning food and household goods bought at a store

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman or Old French *grossier* (wholesaler), from *gros* (wholesale).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
