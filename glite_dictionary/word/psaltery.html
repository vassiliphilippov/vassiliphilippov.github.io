psaltery

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔl.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.tə.ri / (BrE)
an ancient stringed musical instrument, typically with a trapezoidal sounding board, played by plucking the strings
ancient stringed musical instrument

Examples

The museum displayed a medieval psaltery alongside other ancient instruments.
She learned to play the psaltery as part of her study of early music.
The sweet tones of the psaltery filled the cathedral during the concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zither - a musical instrument with many strings stretched over a flat body
  • Synonym: lyre - an ancient stringed instrument known for its use in Classical antiquity

Etymology

From Latin *psalterium*, from Greek *psaltērion* (stringed instrument), from *psallein* (to pluck).
