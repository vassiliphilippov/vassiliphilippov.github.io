scanner

1. NOUN

/ ˈskænər / (AmE), / ˈskænə / (BrE)
a radar device with a rotating or oscillating dish that scans a broad area for objects or signals
rotating radar dish for scanning areas

Examples

The airport's new scanner can detect approaching aircraft within a fifty-mile radius.
The technician adjusted the angle of the scanner to improve radar coverage.
As the scanner rotated, it mapped the entire coastal area for any incoming vessels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radar - system using radio waves to detect objects
  • Synonym: transmitter - device that sends out signals
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04150064-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈskænər / (AmE), / ˈskænə / (BrE)
an electronic device that creates a digital image of a physical object or document for input to a computer
device that digitizes images or documents

Examples

I used the scanner to digitize all my old photographs.
Please place your document face down on the scanner before starting the process.
The new office scanner can handle double-sided printing and color images.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: printer - a device that transfers digital information onto paper
  • Synonym: copier - a machine that makes copies of documents or images

Etymology

From Latin 'scandere' (to climb), via 'scan'; modern sense from technological use in the 20th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈskænər / (AmE), / ˈskænə / (BrE)
a radio receiver that automatically scans a range of frequencies to detect signals, such as police or emergency communications
radio receiver that scans frequencies

Examples

The police department used a scanner to listen to different emergency frequencies throughout the city.
He bought a scanner so he could monitor weather alerts in his area.
With her scanner, she could tune into conversations between firefighters during the rescue operation.

Synset Examples

they used scanners to monitor police radio channels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radio receiver - a device for receiving radio signals

4. NOUN

/ ˈskænɚ / (AmE), / ˈskanə / (BrE)
a person who analyzes the rhythm and meter of poetry by examining syllables and stresses in verse
person analyzing poetic meter

Examples

The poetry professor is an expert scanner who can identify complex metrical patterns in ancient texts.
As a diligent scanner, she spent hours marking the stresses in every line of verse.
Every aspiring poet benefits from becoming a careful scanner to truly understand poetic structure.

Etymology

From Latin *scandere* (to climb), used metaphorically in classical verse analysis.
