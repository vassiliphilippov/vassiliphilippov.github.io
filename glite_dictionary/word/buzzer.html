buzzer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌzər / (AmE), / ˈbʌzə / (BrE)
A device that makes a buzzing sound, used to signal or alert people.
signaling device making a buzzing sound

Examples

Press the buzzer if you know the answer to the question.
The door buzzer sounded, letting me know my guest had arrived.
When the cookies were ready, the oven buzzer went off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell - A device that makes a ringing sound, used as a signal
  • Synonym: alarm - A device that gives a warning signal, such as a sound or light

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌzər / (AmE), / ˈbʌzə / (BrE)
A device, typically a push button, installed at a door that sounds a signal, such as a ring or buzz, when pressed to announce a visitor.
doorbell device making a ringing or buzzing sound

Examples

Press the buzzer if you need to be let into the building.
The delivery man rang the buzzer several times before someone answered.
I heard the buzzer and knew my friend had arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doorbell - a device at a door that rings or buzzes when pressed

Etymology

From 'buzz' + '-er', indicating something that buzzes.
