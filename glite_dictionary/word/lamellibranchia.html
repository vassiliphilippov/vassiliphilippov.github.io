Lamellibranchia

1. NOUN

/ ləˌmɛlɪˈbræŋkiə / (AmE), / ləˌmɛlɪˈbraŋkɪə / (BrE)
A former taxonomic class of mollusks, including oysters, clams, scallops, and mussels, characterized by having bivalve shells and gill structures in the form of lamellae.
class of bivalve mollusks

Examples

The fossil record shows that Lamellibranchia appeared in the early Paleozoic era.
Researchers studied the shell formation process in several species within Lamellibranchia.
The classification of Lamellibranchia has largely been replaced by the term Bivalvia in modern taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bivalvia - The modern taxonomic class including bivalve mollusks

Etymology

From Latin 'lamella' (thin plate) and Greek 'branchia' (gills), referring to the plate-like gills of these animals.

2. NOUN

/ ləˌmɛlɪˈbræŋkiə / (AmE), / læˌmɛlɪˈbraŋkɪə / (BrE)
A former class of mollusks that includes oysters, clams, scallops, and mussels, characterized by their two-part shells and gill structures.
class of bivalve mollusks (oysters, clams, etc.)

Examples

The Lamellibranchia possess two shells joined by a hinge and are commonly found in marine environments.
Oysters, clams, and mussels are all members of the Lamellibranchia.
Research into the fossil record of the Lamellibranchia has revealed much about ancient aquatic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bivalve - an aquatic mollusk with a hinged shell

Etymology

From Latin 'lamella' (thin plate) + Greek 'branchia' (gills).
