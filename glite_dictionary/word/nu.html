nu

1. NOUN

/ nuː / (AmE), / njuː / (BrE)
the 13th letter of the Greek alphabet (Ν, ν), used in mathematics and science to represent various values or constants
13th letter of the Greek alphabet

Examples

The symbol nu is often used in physics to represent frequency.
In statistical equations, nu can denote the degrees of freedom.
The Greek letter nu follows mu and precedes xi in the alphabet.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mu - the 12th letter of the Greek alphabet
  • Degree Higher: xi - the 14th letter of the Greek alphabet

Etymology

From Greek νῦ (ny), the thirteenth letter of the Greek alphabet.
