Liliopsida

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌliː.ɑpˈsaɪ.də / (AmE), / lɪˌlɪ.ɒpˈsɪ.də / (BrE)
A large class of flowering plants (monocots) whose seeds contain a single cotyledon, typically having parallel-veined leaves, including grasses, lilies, palms, and orchids.
class of monocot seed plants (incl. grasses, lilies, palms)

Examples

Many cereal crops, such as wheat and corn, belong to the Liliopsida.
The Liliopsida are characterized by having leaves with parallel veins.
Orchids, palms, and grasses are all part of the Liliopsida class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monocotyledon - a plant whose seed contains a single cotyledon (monocot)

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining 'Lilium' (lily) and Greek 'opsis' (appearance, view).
