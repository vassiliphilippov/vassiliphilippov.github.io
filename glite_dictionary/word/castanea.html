Castanea

1. PROPN

/ kæsˈteɪniə / (AmE), / kæsˈtiːniə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees and shrubs in the beech family, including chestnut and chinkapin species, known for edible nuts and distinctive foliage.
genus of chestnut trees and chinkapins

Examples

The Castanea genus includes the familiar sweet chestnut and several species of chinkapin.
Researchers are studying Castanea to develop blight-resistant chestnut trees.
Several members of Castanea are important for timber and nut production worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chestnut - deciduous tree producing edible nuts; member of Castanea genus

Etymology

From Latin 'castanea' (chestnut), originally from Ancient Greek 'kastanea'.

2. NOUN

/ kæsˈteɪniə / (AmE), / kasˈtiːnɪə / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous trees in the beech family that includes the chestnuts and chinkapins
genus of chestnut trees

Examples

Several species within the genus Castanea are valued for their edible nuts.
The American chestnut, once dominant in eastern forests, belongs to the Castanea genus.
Botanists studied the genetic diversity of Castanea after a fungal blight decimated populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chestnut - a tree producing edible brown nuts; member of Castanea

Etymology

From Latin 'castanea' (chestnut).
