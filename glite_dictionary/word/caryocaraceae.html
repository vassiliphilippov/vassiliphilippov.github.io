Caryocaraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæriːoʊˌkærəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / ˌkærɪəʊˌkærəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
A small family of tropical South American trees known for their large, edible seeds and sometimes used for timber.
family of tropical South American trees

Examples

The Caryocaraceae family includes several tree species with valuable nuts used in local cuisine.
Botanists are studying the Caryocaraceae for their potential in sustainable forestry.
Many members of the Caryocaraceae produce fruits that are important to wildlife in South America.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Caryocar.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɛəriˌoʊkɑːˈreɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌkæriəʊˌkɑːˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
a small family of tropical South American trees, known for producing large, edible fruits and valued hardwood.
family of tropical South American trees

Examples

The Caryocaraceae are native to the rainforests of South America.
Botanists study fruit development within the Caryocaraceae family.
Many valuable timber species belong to the Caryocaraceae.

Etymology

From the genus name Caryocar, itself from Greek 'karyon' (nut) and 'kara' (head).
