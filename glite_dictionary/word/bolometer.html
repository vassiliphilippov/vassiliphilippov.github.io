bolometer

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈlɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / bəˈlɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an extremely sensitive instrument for measuring heat (infrared) radiation
instrument for measuring heat radiation

Examples

The scientist used a bolometer to detect even the faintest traces of infrared radiation.
Modern astronomical observatories often rely on a bolometer for sensitive thermal measurements.
The accuracy of a bolometer makes it essential for measuring minute amounts of heat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiometer - instrument for measuring the intensity of radiation

Etymology

From Greek 'bolē' (a throw, ray) + '-meter' (measurer).
