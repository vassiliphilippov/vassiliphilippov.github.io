subterranean

1. ADJ

/ ˌsʌb.təˈreɪ.ni.ən / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.təˈreɪ.ni.ən / (BrE)
existing, occurring, or situated below the surface of the earth; underground.
underground; beneath the earth's surface

Examples

The explorers discovered a vast network of subterranean tunnels beneath the old cathedral.
Some animals, like moles, lead primarily subterranean lives.
Geologists study subterranean water reservoirs to understand regional groundwater supplies.

Synset Examples

subterranean passages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underground - situated or occurring beneath the surface of the ground

Etymology

From Latin subterraneus, from sub- (under) + terra (earth).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsʌb.təˈreɪ.ni.ən / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.təˈreɪ.ni.ən / (BrE)
Existing or operating beneath the surface; hidden or not openly revealed.
hidden; not openly revealed

Examples

The investigation uncovered subterranean forces influencing the outcome of the election.
She suspected there were subterranean motives behind his generosity.
Literature often explores the subterranean desires that drive human behavior.

Synset Examples

subterranean motives for murder
looked too closely for an ulterior purpose in all knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ulterior - not directly expressed or revealed; hidden
  • Antonym: overt - done or shown openly; not secret

Etymology

From Latin subterraneus, from sub- (under) and terra (earth).

WordNet ID: oewn-01712186-s
