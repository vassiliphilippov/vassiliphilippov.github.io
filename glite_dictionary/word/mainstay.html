mainstay

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪnˌsteɪ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnˌsteɪ / (BrE)
the most important or central support of something or someone; a main source of strength or stability
most important support; central source of strength

Examples

Education is the mainstay of a strong and prosperous society.
For years, she has been the financial mainstay of her family.
Tourism is the economic mainstay of the island.

Synset Examples

faith is his anchor
the keystone of campaign reform was the ban on soft money
he is the linchpin of this firm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linchpin - a person or thing vital to an organization or system
  • Synonym: cornerstone - an important quality or feature on which a particular thing depends or is based

Etymology

Originally a nautical term: from 'main' (principal) and 'stay' (a supporting rope or cable on a ship).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪnˌsteɪ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnsteɪ / (BrE)
the strong rope or wire that supports the mainmast of a sailing ship, running fore-and-aft to brace it
supporting rope or wire for a ship's mainmast

Examples

The mainstay kept the mainmast upright during the storm.
Without a properly secured mainstay, the ship’s rigging would be dangerously unstable.
Sailors checked the tension on the mainstay before heading out to sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forestay - the stay that supports the mast from the front

Etymology

From main (principal) + stay (a strong rope supporting a ship's mast), used in nautical contexts since the 17th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪnˌsteɪ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnsteɪ / (BrE)
a person or thing that plays an important or central support role in a group, organization, or system
an important supporter or key element

Examples

As the team's mainstay, she kept everyone motivated during difficult times.
Since his arrival, he has become a mainstay in the local music scene.
Reliable volunteers like Margaret are the mainstay of our charity organization.

Synset Examples

he is a pillar of the community

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pillar - a person who is a fundamental support of something
  • Synonym: linchpin - a person or thing vital to an enterprise or organization
  • Antonym: weakling - a person lacking strength or influence

Etymology

Originally a nautical term from 'main' + 'stay' (the stay that supports the mainmast). Figurative sense developed in the 17th century.
