mRNA

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm ɑːr ɛn ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛm ɑː ˈrɛn eɪ / (BrE)
a type of RNA that carries genetic information from DNA to the ribosome, where proteins are synthesized
RNA molecule carrying genetic information
medical

Examples

Scientists studied how mRNA vaccines stimulate the immune system to fight viruses.
The genetic code is first transcribed into mRNA before it is translated into a protein.
Recent breakthroughs in medicine involve the use of mRNA to develop new therapies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: messenger RNA - full term for mRNA, type of RNA that conveys genetic information for protein synthesis
  • Antonym: DNA - the molecule that holds genetic instructions in living organisms

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'messenger RNA', where RNA stands for 'ribonucleic acid'.
