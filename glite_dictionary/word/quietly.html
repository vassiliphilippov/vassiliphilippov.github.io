quietly

1. ADV

/ ˈkwaɪətli / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪətli / (BrE)
In a way that makes little or no noise; in a low or soft voice.
in a low or soft voice

Examples

She closed the door quietly so as not to wake anyone.
The teacher spoke quietly to the nervous student.
He tiptoed quietly across the room to avoid attention.

Synset Examples

speak softly but carry a big stick
she spoke quietly to the child
the radio was playing softly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: softly - in a gentle or quiet manner, with little noise
  • Antonym: loudly - in a way that produces a lot of noise

2. ADV

/ ˈkwaɪətli / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪətli / (BrE)
In a way that makes little or no noise; in a quiet manner.
in a quiet manner, with little or no sound

Examples

The children crept quietly into the room so as not to wake their parents.
She closed the door quietly behind her and tiptoed away.
He quietly read his book in the corner, lost in thought.

Synset Examples

the class was listening quietly and intently
she was crying quietly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silently - without any sound at all
  • Antonym: noisily - with a lot of noise or sound

Etymology

From 'quiet' + '-ly', the adverbial suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00231015-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈkwaɪətli / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪətli / (BrE)
In a way that is calm, with little or no noise, disturbance, or activity.
in a calm or noiseless manner

Examples

She closed the door quietly so she wouldn't wake the baby.
The students sat quietly during the exam, focused on their work.
He quietly slipped out of the meeting without anyone noticing.

Synset Examples

her hands rested quietly in her lap
the rock star was quietly led out the back door
sit here as quiet as you can

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noisily - in a loud or boisterous manner
  • Degree Higher: silently - with no sound at all

Etymology

From quiet + -ly, with 'quiet' from Latin quietus (rest, calm, quiet).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00231184-r
Synset POS: r

4. ADV

/ ˈkwaɪətli / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪətli / (BrE)
in a way that is calm, peaceful, or without noise or disturbance.
in a calm or peaceful way

Examples

She sat quietly by the window, enjoying the peaceful afternoon.
Please enter the room quietly so you don't disturb the meeting.
After the guests had left, the house became quietly calm.

Synset Examples

the streets are restfully sunny and still for the town is at mass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peacefully - in a peaceful or calm way
  • Antonym: noisily - in a noisy or loud way

Etymology

From 'quiet' + '-ly', with 'quiet' from Latin 'quietus' (at rest).
