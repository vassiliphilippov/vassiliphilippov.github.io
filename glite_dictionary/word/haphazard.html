haphazard

1. ADJ

/ hæpˈhæz.ərd / (AmE), / hæpˈhæz.əd / (BrE)
Done with no particular order or plan; marked by lack of organization or care.
lacking order or care

Examples

He arranged the books in a haphazard fashion on the shelf, without any logical order.
Their haphazard approach to planning led to many mistakes during the event.
Reports were kept in a haphazard manner, making it difficult to find important information.

Synset Examples

a most haphazard system of record keeping
slapdash work
slipshod spelling
sloppy workmanship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slapdash - done too hurriedly and carelessly
  • Antonym: orderly - arranged or organized in a systematic way

Etymology

From English 'hap' (chance) + 'hazard' (danger, risk), originally from Old French and Arabic roots.

2. ADJ

/ hæpˈhæzərd / (AmE), / hæpˈhæzəd / (BrE)
lacking any obvious organization or plan; dependent on chance rather than method or order.
lacking order or planning; random

Examples

Their haphazard approach to organizing the files made it nearly impossible to find anything quickly.
The city grew in a haphazard fashion, with little planning or regulation.
Decorations were hung in a haphazard way, giving the party a quirky and spontaneous feel.

Synset Examples

a haphazard plan of action
his judgment is rather hit-or-miss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: random - made or done without method or conscious decision
  • Antonym: systematic - done or acting according to a fixed plan or system

Etymology

From hap (chance, luck) + hazard (risk, danger); originally meaning 'chance event'.

Source: WordNet

3. ADV

/ hæpˈhæzɚd / (AmE), / hæpˈhæzəd / (BrE)
In a manner lacking any obvious principle or order; without care or planning.
done without order or planning

Examples

The books were piled haphazard on the desk, with no apparent order.
She threw her clothes haphazard into the suitcase, not bothering to fold them.
The paintings hung haphazard across the wall, creating an unplanned yet interesting display.

Synset Examples

the Prime Minister was wearing a grey suit and a white shirt with a soft collar, but his neck had become thinner and the collar stood away from it as if it had been bought haphazard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: randomly - without method or conscious decision
  • Antonym: systematically - using a method or system

Etymology

From hap (chance, luck) + hazard (danger, risk), indicating something left to chance.
