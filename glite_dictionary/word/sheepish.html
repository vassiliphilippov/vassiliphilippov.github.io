sheepish

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃipɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈʃiːpɪʃ / (BrE)
showing or feeling embarrassment, especially from shame or a lack of self-confidence
showing embarrassment or shame

Examples

He gave a sheepish grin when he realized his mistake.
She looked sheepish as she apologized for the confusion.
Tom felt sheepish after tripping over the rug in front of everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ashamed - feeling embarrassment or guilt because of one's actions
  • Antonym: confident - feeling or showing self-assurance

Etymology

From sheep + -ish; likening the shy or embarrassed demeanor to that of a sheep.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃiːpɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈʃiːpɪʃ / (BrE)
showing embarrassment, shyness, or a lack of self-confidence, often in a way that suggests meekness or timidity
embarrassed or shy in a timid way

Examples

He gave a sheepish grin after realizing his mistake in front of everyone.
She looked sheepish when asked about her lateness.
The student offered a sheepish apology for forgetting his homework.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bashful - reluctant to draw attention to oneself; shy
  • Antonym: confident - feeling or showing certainty; self-assured

Etymology

Formed in English from 'sheep' + '-ish', referring to sheep's docile or timid behavior.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
