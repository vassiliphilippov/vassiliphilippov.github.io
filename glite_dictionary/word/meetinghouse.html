meetinghouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːtɪŋˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈmiːtɪŋhaʊs / (BrE)
a building where religious assemblies are held, especially by Nonconformist groups such as Quakers.
building for religious assembly, especially Nonconformists

Examples

The old Quaker meetinghouse has stood in the village for over two centuries.
Members gathered at the meetinghouse every Sunday for services and community discussions.
Unlike an ornate cathedral, a meetinghouse is often simple and unadorned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: church - a building for Christian worship
  • Synonym: chapel - a small building for Christian worship, often attached to an institution

Etymology

From 'meeting' + 'house', originally used by early Protestant groups in England and America.
