solder

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːdər / (AmE), / ˈsɒldə / (BrE)
a mixture of metals, usually lead and tin, that melts easily and is used to join metal surfaces together
alloy for joining metals

Examples

He melted the solder and used it to connect the wires to the circuit board.
Make sure the solder flows smoothly into the joint for a strong connection.
The technician ran out of solder while repairing the broken metal frame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weld - to join pieces of metal by melting the surfaces together

Etymology

From Old French 'souder' (to weld), from Latin 'solidare' (to make solid).

WordNet ID: oewn-14742983-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈsɑːdɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɒldə / (BrE)
to join metal objects together by melting and applying solder
join metals with solder

Examples

The technician carefully soldered the wires to the circuit board.
You need to solder the copper pipes to prevent leaks.
She learned how to solder electronic parts as part of her engineering course.

Synset Examples

solder these two pipes together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weld - to join metal parts by heating until they melt and fuse together

Etymology

From Old French 'soldure', from Latin 'solidare' (to make solid).

Synset POS: v
