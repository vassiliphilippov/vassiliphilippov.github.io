lowland

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.lənd / (BrE)
An area of land that is low in elevation, especially in comparison to nearby highland regions; typically level or gently rolling country.
area of land at low elevation

Examples

The fertile lowland supported many thriving villages along the riverbanks.
Glaciers once covered both the lowland valleys and the higher mountains in this region.
Agriculture is a major activity in the country's lowland areas due to the rich soil.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: highland - area of high elevation land

Etymology

From Middle English lowe (low) + land, meaning land at a low elevation.

2. ADJ

/ ˈloʊˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌlænd / (BrE)
(of land) situated at a relatively low elevation or in a region that is level compared to surrounding areas.
relating to low or level land

Examples

The lowland areas are often more fertile than the uplands.
Many species thrive in the lowland forests of the region.
Farmers prefer the lowland fields because they are easier to cultivate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: highland - of land at a higher elevation or mountainous

Etymology

From Old English 'hlēow' (shelter, hill) + 'land'.
