frost

1. NOUN

/ frɑst / (AmE), / frɒst / (BrE)
A thin layer of tiny white ice crystals formed when water vapor in the air condenses and freezes, usually on surfaces outdoors.
white ice crystals formed outdoors

Examples

A thick layer of frost covered the grass in the early morning.
The windowpanes glistened with frost after the cold night.
Gardeners worry about frost damaging their plants in late spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ice - frozen water; solid state of water

Etymology

From Old English 'forst' or 'frost', related to 'freeze'.

/ frɑst / (AmE), / frɒst / (BrE)

Examples
