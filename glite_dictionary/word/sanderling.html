sanderling

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændərlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsændəlɪŋ / (BrE)
a small, pale sandpiper (Calidris alba) that breeds in the Arctic and migrates south along sandy coasts throughout much of the world
small sandpiper of Arctic coasts

Examples

Flocks of sanderlings ran back and forth with the waves along the beach.
The sanderling is easily recognized by its pale plumage and quick movements.
During migration, thousands of sanderlings can be seen on sandy shores worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandpiper - a type of wading bird with a slender bill, found along shorelines

Etymology

From 'sand' + diminutive suffix '-ling', meaning 'little sand (bird)'.
