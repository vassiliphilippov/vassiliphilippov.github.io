Scirpus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪr.pəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪə.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial grasslike plants, commonly known as bulrushes, found in wetlands and characterized by rhizomatous growth.
a genus of wetland plants (bulrushes)

Examples

The marsh was dominated by stands of Scirpus along the water's edge.
Botanists collected samples of Scirpus to study its ecological role in wetlands.
Many bird species use Scirpus as a nesting site due to its dense growth.

Etymology

From Latin *scirpus* (bulrush).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪr.pəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪə.pəs / (BrE)
a genus of perennial, grasslike, rhizomatous herbs commonly known as bulrushes or club-rushes, found in wetlands and marshy areas.
a genus of wetland grasslike plants

Examples

The marsh was dominated by thick stands of Scirpus plants.
Wildlife often finds food and shelter among Scirpus in the wetland.
Several species within the Scirpus genus are important for stabilizing soil in ponds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulrush - common name for plants in the genus Scirpus and related genera

Etymology

From Latin 'scirpus' (rush, bulrush).
