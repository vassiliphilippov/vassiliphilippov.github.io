xylometer

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈlɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / zaɪˈlɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the specific gravity of wood
instrument measuring wood's specific gravity

Examples

The laboratory technician used a xylometer to analyze the sample of pine.
Researchers employ a xylometer to compare densities of different wood species.
Accurate measurement was ensured by calibrating the xylometer before testing.

Etymology

From Greek 'xylon' (wood) and 'metron' (measure).
