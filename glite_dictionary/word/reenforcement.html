reenforcement

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːɪnˈfɔːrsmənt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈfɔːsmənt / (BrE)
the action of providing additional troops, equipment, or support to strengthen a military force or mission
strengthening a military force with additional support

Examples

The general ordered immediate reenforcement to the embattled division on the front line.
After several days of heavy fighting, reenforcement finally arrived to turn the tide of the battle.
Without timely reenforcement, the besieged soldiers were forced to retreat.

Synset Examples

they called for artillery support

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reinforcement - the act of strengthening with additional support, especially in the military

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'reinforcement', from French renforcer (to strengthen) + -ment.
