jest

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛst / (AmE), / dʒɛst / (BrE)
an activity, remark, or act characterized by good humor or playful joking
playful act or joke

Examples

His comment was made in jest, not to be taken seriously.
The friends exchanged stories and jest throughout the evening.
She played her prank in harmless jest, hoping to make everyone laugh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joke - something said or done to provoke laughter
  • Synonym: prank - a playful trick or practical joke
  • Antonym: earnest - seriousness or sincerity of manner or attitude

Etymology

From Old French 'geste' (narrative, tale, exploit) and Latin 'gesta' (deeds, exploits).

2. VERB

/ dʒɛst / (AmE), / dʒɛst / (BrE)
to say or do something in a joking or playful way; to act in a manner intended to amuse or tease
joke or act playfully

Examples

Even when the conversation turned serious, he continued to jest with his friends.
Please don't jest about such important matters; some people might misunderstand.
She jested that she was the queen of the world, making everyone laugh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joke - say something intended to make people laugh
  • Synonym: tease - make fun of or try to provoke in a playful way
  • Antonym: be serious - to not joke or act playfully

Etymology

From Middle English 'jest', from Old French 'geste' (narrative, jest), from Latin 'gesta' (deeds, acts).
