influent

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪnfluənt / (AmE), / ˈɪnflʊənt / (BrE)
(of water or another substance) flowing inward or into something, especially into a larger body of water.
flowing inward or into (esp. water)

Examples

The influent streams nourished the main river throughout the rainy season.
In some wetlands, influent water plays a vital role in maintaining groundwater levels.
Engineers measured the volume of influent entering the reservoir each day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: effluent - flowing out; outflowing substance, especially waste

Etymology

From Latin influent-, present participle of influere (to flow in).
