Chunga burmeisteri

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃʊŋɡə bɜrˈmaɪstəri / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌŋɡə bɜːˈmaɪstəri / (BrE)
A species of bird in the Cariamidae family, commonly known as the black-legged seriema, native to Argentina and adjacent areas.
species of Argentinian bird (black-legged seriema)

Examples

The Chunga burmeisteri is often found in dry savanna habitats in northern Argentina.
Researchers observed the nesting behavior of Chunga burmeisteri over several breeding seasons.
Unlike its relative the red-legged seriema, Chunga burmeisteri has predominantly black legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seriema - long-legged South American bird of the Cariamidae family

Etymology

Genus name 'Chunga' and species epithet honoring Hermann Burmeister, a German-Argentinian zoologist.
