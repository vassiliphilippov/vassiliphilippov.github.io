pettiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛt̬i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpet.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being concerned with small or trivial matters, often displaying a narrowness of mind or spirit
concern with trivial matters; narrowness of mind

Examples

Her pettiness made working with her difficult, as she constantly complained about inconsequential issues.
The pettiness of their argument surprised everyone at the meeting.
He learned to rise above pettiness and focus on more important goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: small-mindedness - the quality of showing a narrow or mean outlook
  • Antonym: magnanimity - the quality of being generous and not petty

Etymology

From petty + -ness, based on Old French petit (small, little).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛt̬.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpet.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being overly concerned with trivial matters or showing a lack of generosity in minor issues
concern with trivial matters; lack of generosity

Examples

Her refusal to forgive was a sign of pettiness rather than true offense.
He showed his pettiness by arguing over who should pay a few cents more.
Office politics are often plagued by pettiness and unnecessary rivalries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generosity - the quality of being kind and generous
  • Synonym: meanness - lack of generosity; stinginess

Etymology

From petty (from Old French 'petit', meaning 'small') + -ness (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛt̬.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpet.i.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being concerned with trivial matters, lacking generosity, or of little importance.
quality of being petty or trivial

Examples

Her pettiness made it difficult for her to maintain lasting friendships.
The pettiness of their argument surprised everyone at the meeting.
He wished people would stop focusing on pettiness and address the real issues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generosity - the quality of being kind and generous
  • Antonym: magnanimity - the quality of being generous and forgiving
  • Synonym: pettiness - the quality of being petty or trivial

Etymology

From petty + -ness.
