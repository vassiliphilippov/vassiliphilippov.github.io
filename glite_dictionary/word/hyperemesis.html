hyperemesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərˈɛməsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈrɛmɪsɪs / (BrE)
severe and excessive vomiting, especially as a medical condition
severe excessive vomiting (medical term)
medical

Examples

Pregnant women suffering from hyperemesis may require hospitalization for rehydration.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with hyperemesis after days of uncontrollable vomiting.
Persistent hyperemesis can lead to dehydration and weight loss.

Etymology

From Greek hyper- (over, excessive) + emesis (vomiting).
