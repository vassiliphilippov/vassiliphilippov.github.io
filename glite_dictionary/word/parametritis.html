parametritis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˌmɛˈtraɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌpærəˌmɛˈtraɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the connective tissue adjacent to the uterus
inflammation near the uterus
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with parametritis after presenting with pelvic pain and fever.
Untreated infections can sometimes lead to parametritis, requiring prompt medical intervention.
Her doctor explained that parametritis involves inflammation of tissues next to the uterus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metritis - inflammation of the uterus
  • Synonym: endometritis - inflammation of the uterine lining

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'para-' (beside, near) and Greek 'mētra' (womb, uterus).
