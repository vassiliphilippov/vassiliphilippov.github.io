Betula alleghaniensis

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtuːlə ˌælɪɡəˈheɪniːɛnsɪs / (AmE), / bɛˈtjuːlə ˌælɪɡəˈheɪniːɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a species of birch tree native to eastern North America, noted for its thin, shiny yellow or gray bark; also known as yellow birch
a species of birch tree (yellow birch)

Examples

The Betula alleghaniensis is prized for its hard, valuable timber in the furniture industry.
In autumn, the leaves of the Betula alleghaniensis turn a brilliant golden color.
Hikers in eastern forests often recognize the Betula alleghaniensis by its peeling yellow bark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow birch - common name for Betula alleghaniensis

Etymology

From Latin: 'Betula' (the birch genus) and 'alleghaniensis' (of the Allegheny Mountains).
