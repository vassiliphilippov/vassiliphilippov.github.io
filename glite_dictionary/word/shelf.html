shelf

1. NOUN

/ ʃɛlf / (AmE), / ʃɛlf / (BrE)
a flat, horizontal board or surface fixed to a wall or within a frame, used for supporting objects
flat support for holding items

Examples

She placed the books neatly on the top shelf above her desk.
The kitchen shelf was crowded with jars and spices.
He built a wooden shelf for his growing collection of trophies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ledge - a narrow horizontal surface projecting from a wall

Etymology

From Old English 'scylfe', meaning 'ledge' or 'shelf'.

2. NOUN

/ ʃɛlf / (AmE), / ʃɛlf / (BrE)
a flat, extended projection of rock, sand, or land, often forming a ridge on a mountain or under water
projecting ridge on mountain or underwater

Examples

The divers explored the coral shelf that jutted out from the shoreline.
Snow gathered on a narrow shelf high above the valley.
Scientists studied the continental shelf where the ocean floor drops steeply.

Etymology

From Old English 'scylfe' (ledge, shelf), of Germanic origin.

Synset POS: n
