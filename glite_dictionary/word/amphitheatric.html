amphitheatric

1. ADJ

/ ˌæmfəθiˈætrɪk / (AmE), / ˌamfɪθɪˈatrɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling an amphitheater; characteristic of an amphitheater in form, function, or arrangement.
relating to an amphitheater

Examples

The ancient city featured an amphitheatric arena where games were held.
Rows of seats rose in an amphitheatric arrangement around the stage.
The outdoor lecture took place in a natural amphitheatric hollow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphitheater - a round or oval open-air venue for performances or sports

Etymology

From 'amphitheater' + '-ic', ultimately from Greek 'amphitheatron'.
