quart

1. NOUN

/ kwɔrt / (AmE), / kwɔːt / (BrE)
A unit of liquid capacity equal to a quarter of a gallon, or 32 US fluid ounces.
unit of liquid capacity (US, 32 fl oz)

Examples

The recipe calls for a quart of milk, so make sure you have enough.
She bought a quart of ice cream to share with her friends.
A quart is equal to two pints in the United States measurement system.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gallon - unit of liquid capacity equal to four quarts
  • Degree Lower: pint - unit of liquid capacity equal to half a quart

Etymology

From Old French 'quarte' (quarter), from Latin 'quarta' (fourth part).

2. NOUN

/ kwɔrt / (AmE), / kwɔːt / (BrE)
A unit of liquid or dry capacity equal to a quarter of a gallon, or two pints; in the US dry measure, equal to 67.2 cubic inches.
US dry unit equal to 2 pints or 67.2 cubic inches

Examples

The recipe calls for one quart of flour.
He bought a quart of strawberries at the farmer’s market.
A US dry quart is slightly larger than a liquid quart.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gallon - A unit of capacity equal to four quarts.
  • Degree Lower: pint - A unit of capacity equal to half a quart.

Etymology

From Middle English *quarte*, from Old French *quarte*, from Latin *quartus* (fourth).

3. NOUN

/ kwɔrt / (AmE), / kwɔːt / (BrE)
A unit of liquid or dry capacity in the British imperial system equal to two pints or about 1.136 liters.
imperial unit of capacity (2 pints)
british

Examples

He bought a quart of milk from the corner shop.
The recipe calls for a quart of water to be added before boiling.
In the UK, a quart is slightly larger than in the US.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pint - an imperial unit of capacity, equal to half a quart
  • Degree Higher: gallon - an imperial unit of capacity, equal to four quarts

Etymology

From Old French 'quarte', from Latin 'quartus' meaning 'fourth.'
