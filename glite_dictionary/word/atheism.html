atheism

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪθiɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪθɪɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that God or gods do not exist, or a lack of belief in their existence
disbelief or lack of belief in God or gods

Examples

Her decision to embrace atheism surprised her religious family.
Public debates about atheism have become more common in recent years.
He wrote an essay defending atheism as a rational worldview.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agnosticism - the view that the existence of God or the supernatural is unknown or unknowable
  • Antonym: theism - belief in the existence of God or gods
  • Synonym: irreligion - absence or rejection of religion

Etymology

From Greek atheos (without god), from 'a-' (without) + 'theos' (god).
