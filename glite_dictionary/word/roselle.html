roselle

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈzɛl / (AmE), / rəʊˈzɛl / (BrE)
a tropical plant (Hibiscus sabdariffa) grown for its edible fleshy calyxes used in food and its fiber used in textiles
tropical plant grown for edible calyxes and fiber

Examples

The tart red calyxes of the roselle are used to make refreshing beverages and jams.
Farmers in tropical regions often cultivate roselle for both its edible and fibrous parts.
The roselle plant thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil.

Etymology

From French 'roselle', diminutive of 'rose' (rose), in reference to its resemblance.
