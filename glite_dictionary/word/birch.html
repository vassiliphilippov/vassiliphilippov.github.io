2. NOUN

/ bɝtʃ / (AmE), / bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
the hard, fine-grained wood of a birch tree, often used in furniture making, interior finishes, and plywood
wood from a birch tree

Examples

The cabinets were crafted from polished birch, giving the kitchen a warm, natural look.
He prefers to use birch for making musical instruments because of its resonant quality.
The chair was made of solid birch and was both sturdy and elegant.

3. NOUN

/ bɝːtʃ / (AmE), / bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A tree or shrub of the genus Betula, with thin, peeling bark, commonly found in temperate regions.
tree or shrub with peeling bark (Betula)

Examples

The birch trees along the river were just beginning to lose their leaves.
He used a piece of birch wood to craft a small box.
The pale, peeling bark of the birch made it easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alder - a tree or shrub of the genus Alnus, related to the birch
  • Antonym: oak - a type of large tree chiefly of the genus Quercus, different genus from birch

Etymology

From Old English *birc(e)*, from Proto-Germanic *berkō*.

4. VERB

/ bɝːtʃ / (AmE), / bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
to whip someone with a birch twig as a form of punishment
whip with a birch twig

Examples

In the past, teachers sometimes birched students as a form of discipline.
The headmaster threatened to birch any boy caught cheating on the exam.
After being caught stealing, he was publicly birched as punishment.

Etymology

From Middle English birche (noun), ultimately from Proto-Germanic *berkō; verb sense arises from use of birch rods in punishment.

a bundle of twigs from a birch tree, or a single twig, used as an instrument for punishing people by beating them, especially in former times
bundle of birch twigs used for punishment

Examples

In Victorian schools, the birch was often used to discipline unruly pupils.
The judge sentenced him to six strokes with the birch for his crime.
Stories of the birch as a punishment tool appear frequently in old English literature.

Related Terms
