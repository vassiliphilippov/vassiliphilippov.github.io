aperient

1. ADJ

/ əˈpɪriənt / (AmE), / əˈpɪəriənt / (BrE)
(of a medicine or substance) mildly laxative; causing a gentle evacuation of the bowels.
mildly laxative (medicine)
medical

Examples

This herbal tea has an aperient effect and can help relieve mild constipation.
Doctors sometimes prescribe aperient medicines for patients with irregular bowel movements.
The pharmacist recommended an aperient syrup for gentle overnight relief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laxative - a substance that promotes bowel movements

Etymology

From Latin 'aperiens', present participle of 'aperire' (to open).

2. NOUN

/ əˈpɪriənt / (AmE), / əˈpɪəriənt / (BrE)
A medicine that stimulates evacuation of the bowels; a mild laxative.
medicine for stimulating bowel movement
medical

Examples

After her surgery, the doctor prescribed an aperient to help restore regular bowel movements.
He asked the pharmacist for a mild aperient rather than a strong laxative.
The herbal remedy acted as a gentle aperient to ease his discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laxative - a medicine that promotes evacuation of the bowels

Etymology

From Latin 'aperiens', present participle of 'aperire' (to open).
