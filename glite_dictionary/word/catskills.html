Catskills

1. PROPN

/ ˈkætskɪlz / (AmE), / ˈkætskɪlz / (BrE)
a mountain range in southeastern New York State, west of the Hudson River, known for its scenic beauty and popular resort areas
mountain range in southeastern New York State

Examples

Many city dwellers escape to the Catskills during the summer for fresh air and hiking.
The Catskills have inspired generations of artists with their picturesque landscapes.
We rented a cabin in the Catskills for a peaceful weekend retreat.

Etymology

Derived from Dutch 'Kaaterskill' meaning 'Kaaters creek' or 'cat stream.'
