asparagus

1. NOUN

/ əˈspɛr.ə.ɡəs / (AmE), / əˈspær.ə.ɡəs / (BrE)
a tall plant with slender, edible green shoots that are widely eaten as a vegetable; also refers to the young shoots themselves.
edible plant with green shoots

Examples

Fresh asparagus is in season during the spring months.
She served grilled asparagus with lemon and olive oil.
The chef recommended steamed asparagus as a side dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asparagus spear - the edible young shoot of the asparagus plant

Etymology

From Latin asparagus, from Greek asparagos.

2. NOUN

/ əˈspærəɡəs / (AmE), / əˈspærəɡəs / (BrE)
A tall plant with slender, edible shoots that are commonly eaten as a vegetable.
edible plant with young shoots

Examples

We served steamed asparagus as a side dish with dinner.
During spring, fresh asparagus appears at local farmers' markets.
She planted asparagus in her garden for next year's harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vegetable - an edible plant or part of a plant used as food

Etymology

From Latin 'asparagus', from Greek 'asparagos', referring to the plant.

3. NOUN

/ əˈspærəɡəs / (AmE), / əˈspærəɡəs / (BrE)
A tall plant whose young edible shoots are eaten as a vegetable, commonly green or white in color.
edible vegetable shoots

Examples

She prepared a delicious salad using fresh asparagus from the market.
Grilled asparagus makes a perfect side dish for fish or chicken.
The chef garnished the plate with tender spears of asparagus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vegetable - an edible plant or part of a plant, not usually sweet

Etymology

From Latin 'asparagus', from Greek 'asparagos' (shoot, sprout).
