Armillaria ponderosa

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌmɪlɛəriə pɒn.dəˈroʊsə / (AmE), / ɑːˌmɪl.ə.rɪə pɒn.dəˈrəʊ.sə / (BrE)
a large species of edible white mushroom, developing brown stains as it ages, with white gills and a spicy aroma, collected commercially for cooking, especially in the Pacific Northwest
a large edible mushroom species

Examples

Chefs in the Pacific Northwest prize Armillaria ponderosa for its distinctive flavor in Asian-inspired dishes.
We found several clusters of Armillaria ponderosa while hiking in the damp forest.
The gills of Armillaria ponderosa are bright white when young but darken with age.

Etymology

From Latin: Armillaria, the genus name, and ponderosa meaning 'heavy.'
