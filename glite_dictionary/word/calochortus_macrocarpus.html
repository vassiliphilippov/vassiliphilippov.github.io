Calochortus macrocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈkɔrtəs ˌmækroʊˈkɑrpəs / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈkɔːtəs ˌmakrəʊˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
A North American species of mariposa lily with loose clusters of one to three lilac flowers atop stout, erect stems, native to arid regions east of the Cascade Mountains.
a species of mariposa lily with lilac flowers

Examples

Calochortus macrocarpus can be found in the dry hills and grasslands of eastern Oregon.
Gardeners admire Calochortus macrocarpus for its striking lilac blooms and ease of cultivation in arid climates.
The delicate flowers of Calochortus macrocarpus appear from late spring to early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mariposa lily - a North American plant of the genus Calochortus with showy tulip-like flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) + 'chortos' (grass), with the species name from Greek 'makros' (large) and 'karpos' (fruit).
