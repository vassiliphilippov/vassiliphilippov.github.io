deflexion

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the movement of the pointer or pen of a measuring instrument from its zero position, often indicating a measured quantity or change
movement of instrument's pointer from zero

Examples

The deflexion of the galvanometer needle indicated the presence of an electric current.
A sudden deflexion on the chart alarmed the technician monitoring the equipment.
Careful calibration ensures that each deflexion corresponds precisely to a known pressure value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deflection - alternative spelling or synonym meaning the same movement away from zero

Etymology

From Latin 'deflexio'—'a bending aside', from 'deflectere' (to bend away).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the state or property of being bent or deflected from a straight position
the state of being bent or deflected

Examples

The beam showed significant deflexion under the load during the test.
Engineers measured the deflexion of the bridge to ensure safety standards were met.
Repeated stress can cause progressive deflexion in metal structures over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deflection - the state of being bent or turned aside; alternative spelling
  • Antonym: straightness - the state of being straight, not bent or deflected

Etymology

From Latin 'deflexio', from 'deflectere' (to bend aside).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈflɛksɪən / (BrE)
the amount by which a propagating wave is bent as it passes through a medium or encounters an obstacle
amount a wave is bent

Examples

The deflexion of light by a prism is a fundamental concept in optics.
Engineers measured the deflexion of the radio waves as they passed through the atmosphere.
The deflexion observed in the experiment confirmed the presence of a magnetic field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deflection - the amount by which something is turned from a straight course or position

Etymology

From Latin deflexio (a bending aside), via deflect.
