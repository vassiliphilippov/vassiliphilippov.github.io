Eubryales

1. NOUN

/ juːˈbraɪəliːz / (AmE), / juːˈbrɪəliːz / (BrE)
an order of mosses characterized by perennial upright gametophores, stems lined with rows of leaves, and drooping capsules
an order of mosses

Examples

Many woodland soils are covered by various Eubryales species forming dense green mats.
Botanists often study Eubryales to understand the ecological role of mosses.
The drooping capsules are a distinctive feature of Eubryales among bryophytes.

Etymology

From New Latin *Eubryales*, from Greek *eu-* (good, well) + *bryon* (moss) + Latin plural suffix *-ales* (order).

2. NOUN

/ juːˈbraɪ.əˌliːz / (AmE), / juːˈbraɪ.əˌliːz / (BrE)
an order of mosses characterized by perennial, upright shoots (gametophores), stems with rows of leaves, and drooping capsules
an order of mosses with upright shoots and drooping capsules

Examples

The Eubryales include several moss species with distinctive drooping capsules.
Botanists often study the Eubryales to understand the diversity of mosses.
Features such as perennial stems help distinguish the Eubryales from other moss orders.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Eu-' (true) and 'Bryales' (a group of mosses).
