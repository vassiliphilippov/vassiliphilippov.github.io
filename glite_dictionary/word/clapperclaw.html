to clapperclaw

1. VERB

/ ˈklæpərˌklɔ / (AmE), / ˈklapəˌklɔː / (BrE)
to scratch, maul, or attack with the nails or claws
to scratch or maul with the nails
archaic

Examples

The furious cat began to clapperclaw its rival until both were covered in scratches.
During their quarrel, she threatened to clapperclaw anyone who came too close.
He clapperclawed his way out of the brambles, leaving torn sleeves and bleeding arms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scratch - to mark or damage a surface by scraping, rubbing, or tearing with claws or nails
  • Synonym: maul - to handle or treat roughly, especially so as to injure

Etymology

Probably from 'clapper' (tongue, chatterer) + 'claw'; first recorded in the 16th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈklæpərˌklɔ / (AmE), / ˈklæpəˌklɔː / (BrE)
to criticize or abuse someone harshly with words
to scold or abuse with words
archaic

Examples

She was so angry that she began to clapperclaw her rival in front of everyone.
The critics clapperclawed the new play, calling it a disaster.
He clapperclaws anyone who disagrees with his opinions.

Synset Examples

The actress abused the policeman who gave her a parking ticket
The angry mother shouted at the teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - to use insulting or offensive language towards someone
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From 'clapper' (tongue) + 'claw' (to scratch); originally meant 'to scratch or claw', later used for verbal attack.
