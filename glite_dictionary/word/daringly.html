daringly

1. ADV

/ ˈdɛrɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈdeərɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that shows courage, boldness, or originality; with a willingness to take risks.
in a bold or original manner

Examples

She stepped daringly onto the stage, ready to perform her new song.
The architect daringly combined modern and traditional elements in the building's design.
He spoke daringly, challenging the old ways of thinking in the meeting.

Synset Examples

daringly he took the first step

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boldly - in a confident and courageous way
  • Antonym: timidly - in a shy or hesitant way

Etymology

From 'daring' + adverbial suffix '-ly'. 'Daring' from Middle English 'dering', present participle of 'deren' (to dare).

2. ADV

/ ˈdɛrɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈdɛːrɪŋli / (BrE)
In a bold or adventurous manner; showing courage or a willingness to take risks.
in a bold or adventurous manner

Examples

She daringly volunteered to climb the mountain in spite of the warnings.
The actor daringly performed his own stunts without a safety net.
He daringly voiced his unpopular opinions during the heated meeting.

Synset Examples

daringly, he set out on a camping trip in East Africa

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boldly - in a brave or confident way
  • Antonym: timidly - in a shy or lacking confidence manner

Etymology

From 'daring' + '-ly', formed from the verb 'dare' (Old English 'durran').
