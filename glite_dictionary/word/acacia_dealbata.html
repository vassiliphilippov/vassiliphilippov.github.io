Acacia dealbata

1. NOUN

/ əˈkeɪʃə diːˈælbətə / (AmE), / əˈkeɪsɪə diːˈælbətə / (BrE)
An evergreen tree native to Australasia, notable for its white or silvery bark, young leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers; commonly known as the silver wattle.
evergreen Australasian tree with silvery bark and yellow flowers

Examples

The Acacia dealbata adds a splash of golden color to gardens each spring.
Botanists studied the unique bark texture of Acacia dealbata specimens in the region.
Many florists use branches of Acacia dealbata in their arrangements for its bright, fragrant flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silver wattle - Common name for Acacia dealbata, an evergreen Australasian tree with yellow flowers

Etymology

From the genus name 'Acacia,' from Greek 'akakia' (thorny Egyptian tree), and 'dealbata,' Latin for 'whitened' or 'whitewashed,' referring to the bark.
