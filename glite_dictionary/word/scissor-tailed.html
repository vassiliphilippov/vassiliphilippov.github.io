scissor-tailed

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪzər teɪld / (AmE), / ˈsɪzə teɪld / (BrE)
(of a bird) having a deeply forked tail resembling the blades of scissors.
having a deeply forked tail (of a bird)

Examples

The scissor-tailed flycatcher is known for its spectacular tail and graceful flight.
Many tropical birds are scissor-tailed, which helps them maneuver quickly in dense forests.
A scissor-tailed shape makes these birds easily recognizable even from a distance.

Synset Examples

scissor-tailed birds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fork-tailed - (of birds) having a divided or forked tail

Etymology

Formed from 'scissor' (cutting instrument) + 'tailed' (having a tail), referring to resemblance to open scissors.
