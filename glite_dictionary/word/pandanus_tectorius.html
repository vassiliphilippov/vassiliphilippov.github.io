Pandanus tectorius

1. NOUN

/ ˌpændənəs tɛkˈtɔriəs / (AmE), / ˌpandənəs tɛkˈtɔːrɪəs / (BrE)
A species of screw pine native to coastal regions of the Pacific Islands, also known as the Polynesian screw pine, cultivated for its edible fruit and fibrous leaves.
species of screw pine native to the Pacific

Examples

The leaves of the Pandanus tectorius are often used for weaving mats and baskets in Pacific cultures.
During our hike, we saw a tall Pandanus tectorius growing along the sandy shore.
The fruit of Pandanus tectorius is edible and valued as a traditional food source in many island communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screw pine - Any plant of the genus Pandanus with spiral leaves

Etymology

From modern Latin; 'Pandanus' from Malay 'pandan', and 'tectorius' from Latin meaning 'covering' or 'roofing', referring to its traditional use.
