strongbox

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔŋˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈstrɒŋˌbɒks / (BrE)
A strongly constructed box with a lock for keeping money or valuables safe.
lockable box for valuables

Examples

The old mansion contained a hidden strongbox filled with antique coins.
She kept her most important documents locked in a metal strongbox under her bed.
When the police arrived, they found the empty strongbox in the back room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safe - a strong, lockable container for valuables, often larger or built-in
  • Synonym: vault - a secure room or compartment for storing valuables, often in a bank

Etymology

From 'strong' + 'box', indicating a sturdy container.
