John Ruskin

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈrʌskɪn / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈrʌskɪn / (BrE)
(1819–1900) a British art critic, writer, and social thinker known for his works on art, architecture, and society.
British art critic and writer (1819–1900)

Examples

The ideas of John Ruskin greatly influenced the development of the Arts and Crafts movement.
Many Victorian artists were inspired by John Ruskin's lectures and writings.
During the exhibition, the museum displayed several letters written by John Ruskin to contemporary painters.
