Occam

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːkəm / (AmE), / ˈɒkəm / (BrE)
An English scholastic philosopher (c. 1285–1349), best known for formulating Occam's Razor, a principle advocating simplicity in theoretical explanations.
English scholastic philosopher, author of Occam's Razor

Examples

Many philosophers refer to Occam when discussing the principle of parsimony in scientific theories.
The logic expressed by Occam has had a lasting impact on modern scientific method.
Scholars still debate some of Occam's philosophical arguments centuries after his death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Occam's Razor - the principle that the simplest explanation is preferable

Etymology

From William of Ockham, named after the village of Ockham in Surrey, England.
