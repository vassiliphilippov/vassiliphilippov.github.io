marcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrtʃər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtʃə / (BrE)
a person who lives in or is native to a border district, especially historically in the Welsh or Scottish Marches of Britain
inhabitant of a border district

Examples

In medieval England, a marcher often held special privileges to defend and govern the borderlands.
The castle was ruled by a powerful marcher who protected the frontier from invasion.
Historians study the unique laws that applied to a marcher during the reign of Edward I.

Etymology

From Middle English marcher, from 'march' (border district), from Old French 'marche' (frontier).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrtʃər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtʃə / (BrE)
A person who marches, especially as part of a procession, demonstration, or parade.
person who marches in a group

Examples

Thousands of marchers filled the streets calling for climate action.
The parade's lead marcher carried the national flag.
Police escorted the group of marchers to ensure their safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demonstrator - person who takes part in a public protest or demonstration

Etymology

From the verb 'march', from French 'marcher' (to walk, to march).
