Kleenex

1. NOUN

/ ˈkliːnɛks / (AmE), / ˈkliːnɛks / (BrE)
A piece of soft, absorbent paper, typically in a rectangular shape, used as a disposable handkerchief for wiping the nose or face; originally a trademark.
disposable paper tissue

Examples

She handed him a Kleenex when he started sneezing.
Do you have a Kleenex? I think I have something in my eye.
He wiped his glasses with a Kleenex to clean the smudges off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tissue - a piece of soft thin paper used as a disposable handkerchief

Etymology

Original trademark owned by Kimberly-Clark; likely from 'clean' plus '-ex' as a brand suffix.
