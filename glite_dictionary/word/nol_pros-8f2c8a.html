to nol. pros.

1. VERB

/ ˌnoʊl ˈprɑs / (AmE), / ˌnɒl ˈprɒs / (BrE)
(law) To drop prosecution of a case by entering a nolle prosequi in the court records.
(law) drop prosecution formally
legal american

Examples

The prosecutor decided to nol. pros. the charges due to insufficient evidence.
After further review, the court agreed to nol. pros. the case.
The defense attorney argued for the state to nol. pros. the indictment.

Synset Examples

They nolle prossed the charge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to officially end a legal case without a decision on the merits

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'nolle prosequi', meaning 'to be unwilling to pursue'.
