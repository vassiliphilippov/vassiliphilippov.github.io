to entwine

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtwaɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtwaɪn / (BrE)
to twist or wind things together, or to become twisted together
twist or wind things together

Examples

She watched the vines entwine themselves around the old fence.
His fingers gently entwined with hers as they walked.
Ivy was entwining up the columns, giving the building a wild, overgrown look.

Synset Examples

intertwine the ribbons
Twine the threads into a rope
intertwined hearts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intertwine - to twist or be twisted together
  • Antonym: to untangle - to separate things that are twisted or knotted together

Etymology

From en- (to cause to) + twine (from Old English *twīn*, meaning thread, strand).

2. VERB

/ ɛnˈtwaɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtwaɪn / (BrE)
to twist or wind together; to tie or link closely together.
twist or wind together

Examples

The vines entwine around the old wooden fence in the garden.
Their fingers entwined as they walked down the path together.
Legends and history often entwine in ancient cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intertwine - to twist together; to become involved closely

Etymology

From en- (make) + twine, Middle English twin (to twist).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
