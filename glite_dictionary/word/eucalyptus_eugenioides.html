Eucalyptus eugenioides

1. NOUN

/ juːˌkliːptəs juːˌdʒiːniˈoʊɪdiːz / (AmE), / juːˌklɪptəs juːˌdʒiːniˈəʊɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of eucalyptus tree, commonly known as white stringybark, distinguished by its white wood and stringy bark.
a species of eucalyptus tree with white wood

Examples

The Eucalyptus eugenioides is native to eastern Australia and is valued for its white timber.
We planted a row of Eucalyptus eugenioides to provide shade along the driveway.
The bark of Eucalyptus eugenioides peels away in long, stringy strips, giving the tree its common name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white stringybark - common name for Eucalyptus eugenioides

Etymology

From Latinized New Latin genus 'Eucalyptus' and species name from resemblance to genus Eugenia.
