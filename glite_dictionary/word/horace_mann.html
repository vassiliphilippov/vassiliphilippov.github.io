Horace Mann

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɔrɪs mæn / (AmE), / ˈhɒrɪs mæn / (BrE)
(1796–1859) An American educator and reformer known for pioneering significant improvements in the public education system.
pioneering American educator and reformer

Examples

Horace Mann is often called the father of American public education for his work on school reform.
The legacy of Horace Mann continues to influence educational policy today.
Many schools and colleges across the United States are named after Horace Mann in honor of his contributions.
