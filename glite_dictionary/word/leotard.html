leotard

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːəˌtɑrd / (AmE), / ˈliːətɑːd / (BrE)
a close-fitting one-piece garment, usually made of stretchable fabric, that covers the torso and sometimes the arms and legs, worn especially by dancers and gymnasts
tight-fitting one-piece garment for dancers/gymnasts

Examples

The dancer wore a bright red leotard during her performance.
Gymnasts often change into their leotards before practice at the gym.
She bought a new leotard with long sleeves for her ballet class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unitard - a close-fitting one-piece garment covering the entire body except for the head, hands, and feet

Etymology

Named after Jules Léotard (1838–1870), a French acrobat who popularized the garment.

2. NOUN

/ ˈliːəˌtɑrd / (AmE), / ˈliːətɑːd / (BrE)
A close-fitting one-piece garment that covers the torso, often worn by dancers, gymnasts, or acrobats.
tight-fitting dance or athletic garment

Examples

The gymnast wore a sparkling leotard during her floor routine.
Ballet students are usually required to practice in a leotard and tights.
She packed several colorful leotards for the dance competition.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: unitard - a similar garment that also covers the legs and sometimes the arms

Etymology

Named after Jules Léotard (1838–1870), a French acrobat who popularized the garment.
