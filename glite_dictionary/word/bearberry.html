bearberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɛːbəri / (BrE)
A mainly evergreen subshrub of northern and Arctic regions, with small red berries, belonging to the genus Arctostaphylos.
evergreen subshrub with red berries of the North

Examples

The bearberry thrives in the cold climates of Canada and Scandinavia.
Bears are particularly fond of eating the berries produced by the bearberry shrub.
During our hike, we came across a dense patch of bearberry along the rocky slope.

Etymology

From 'bear' + 'berry', named because bears commonly eat its berries.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbeəˌbɛri / (BrE)
a small deciduous shrub found in southeastern and central United States, with leathery leaves and red berries, often eaten by bears
deciduous shrub with red berries

Examples

The leaves of the bearberry turn a beautiful shade of red in autumn.
Wildlife, including bears, often feed on the berries produced by the bearberry shrub.
Gardeners value bearberry for its hardiness and attractive appearance in natural landscaping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kinnikinnick - another name for bearberry, especially in North America

Etymology

Named for its berries being a food source for bears.
