strange

1. ADJ

/ streɪndʒ / (AmE), / streɪndʒ / (BrE)
unusual or surprising in a way that is slightly odd or hard to understand; out of the ordinary
unusual or unexpected; odd

Examples

It felt strange returning to her childhood home after so many years.
There's a strange noise coming from the attic that I can't identify.
His behavior grew increasingly strange after the incident.

Synset Examples

a strange exaltation that was indefinable
a strange fantastical mind
what a strange sense of humor she has

Related Terms

  • Synonym: odd - unusual or out of the ordinary; peculiar
  • Antonym: normal - conforming to the usual or expected standard

Etymology

From Old French 'estrange', from Latin 'extraneus' (foreign, external).

2. ADJ

/ streɪndʒ / (AmE), / streɪndʒ / (BrE)
not previously known or familiar; unfamiliar or new to one's experience
not known before; unfamiliar

Examples

We saw many strange faces at the party last night.
The city was full of strange streets and unknown landmarks.
Don't accept packages from strange people you meet at the airport.

Synset Examples

used many strange words
saw many strange faces in the crowd
don't let anyone unknown into the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unknown - not known or familiar
  • Antonym: familiar - well-known from long or close association

Etymology

From Old French estrange, from Latin extraneus (foreign).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00969942-s
Synset POS: s
