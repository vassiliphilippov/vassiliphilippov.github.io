to engross

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈɡroʊs / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡrəʊs / (BrE)
to absorb all the attention or interest of someone; to occupy someone completely
absorb all one's attention

Examples

The novel was so exciting that it engrossed her for hours.
He was engrossed in his work and didn't notice the time passing.
A good movie can easily engross an audience from start to finish.

Synset Examples

Her interest in butterflies absorbs her completely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absorb - to take in all the attention or interest
  • Antonym: to distract - to divert attention away

Etymology

From Latin 'ingrossare', from 'in-' (in) + 'grossus' (thick, coarse); originally meaning 'to write in large letters'.

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈɡroʊs / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡrəʊs / (BrE)
to occupy all the attention or interest of someone; to absorb completely
absorb the attention of; occupy fully

Examples

She was so engrossed in the novel that she missed her train stop.
The children became engrossed in their game and forgot about dinner.
He tends to engross himself in his work and ignore everything else.

Synset Examples

He immersed himself into his studies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absorb - to take up all the attention or interest of
  • Antonym: to distract - to divert the attention of someone

Etymology

From Old French *engrosser* ‘put in large letters’, from Latin *ingrossare* ‘make thick or large’.
