awkward

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔk.wɚd / (AmE), / ˈɔː.kwəd / (BrE)
Causing difficulty or inconvenience; not suitable or easy to deal with.
causing inconvenience or difficulty

Examples

They arrived at an awkward time, just as we were leaving for the airport.
It was an awkward location for a meeting, far from public transport.
Having the plumber over during dinner was a bit awkward.

Synset Examples

they arrived at an awkward time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inconvenient - causing trouble or difficulty
  • Antonym: convenient - fitting well with needs or plans

Etymology

From Middle English 'awkeward', from 'awk' (turned in the wrong direction) + '-ward'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00607783-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔkwərd / (AmE), / ˈɔːkwəd / (BrE)
lacking grace or skill in movement, manner, or performance; clumsy or inelegant.
lacking grace or skill; clumsy

Examples

He made an awkward attempt to jump over the fence and nearly fell.
Her awkward movements revealed she was new to dance class.
The speech ended with an awkward pause as he searched for the right words.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsy - lacking physical coordination or grace
  • Antonym: graceful - showing grace or elegance in movement

Etymology

From Middle English awakward (the wrong way round, clumsy), from awk (turned the wrong way, backwards) + -ward.
