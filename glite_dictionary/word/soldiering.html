soldiering

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊldʒərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊldʒərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of avoiding work or duty; shirking responsibilities
evasion of work or duty

Examples

The manager warned that soldiering would not be tolerated among the team members.
Instead of working, he was accused of soldiering and wasting company time.
Excessive soldiering can lower productivity and morale in any organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shirking - the act of avoiding work or responsibility

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊldʒərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊldʒərɪŋ / (BrE)
the skills and activities associated with being a soldier, especially as required for military life
skills and duties of a soldier

Examples

He spent years soldiering overseas and learned many valuable lessons on the field.
Basic training covers the fundamentals of soldiering for new recruits.
Good soldiering requires discipline, resilience, and teamwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: military service - service in the armed forces

Etymology

From 'soldier' + '-ing', denoting the action or practice of being a soldier.
