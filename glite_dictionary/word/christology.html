Christology

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˈstɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / kraɪˈstɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of Christian theology that deals with the person, nature, and role of Jesus Christ.
study of the nature of Christ

Examples

She specialized in Christology during her graduate studies at the seminary.
Debates in Christology have shaped Christian doctrine for centuries.
Modern Christology seeks to reinterpret Jesus' teachings in the context of contemporary issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theology - the study of the nature of God and religious beliefs
  • Degree Lower: soteriology - the study of religious doctrines of salvation

Etymology

From Greek 'Christos' (Christ) + 'logia' (study).

2. NOUN

/ kraɪˈstɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / kraɪˈstɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of Christian theology that studies the person, nature, and work of Jesus Christ
theological study of Christ

Examples

She wrote her thesis on early church Christology and its impact on Christian doctrine.
Modern debates in Christology often focus on the divinity and humanity of Jesus.
His lectures covered developments in Christology from the New Testament to the Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theology - the study of the nature of God and religious belief

Etymology

From Greek 'Christos' (Christ) + 'logia' (study, discourse).
