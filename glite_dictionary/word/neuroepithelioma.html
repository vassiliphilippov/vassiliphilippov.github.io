neuroepithelioma

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʊroʊˌɛpəˌθiːliˈoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˌɛpɪˌθiːlɪˈəʊmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor arising from the neuroepithelium, typically found in the central nervous system.
malignant tumor of neuroepithelium
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a rare type of neuroepithelioma in the brain.
Treatment options for neuroepithelioma may include surgery, radiation, and chemotherapy.
Researchers are studying the genetic causes of neuroepithelioma to develop better therapies.

Etymology

From neuro- (relating to nerves) + epithelium (tissue lining surfaces) + -oma (tumor).
