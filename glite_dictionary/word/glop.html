glop

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɑp / (AmE), / ɡlɒp / (BrE)
a sticky or viscous mass of shapeless, usually unpleasant material
sticky, shapeless unpleasant matter

Examples

She scraped the green glop off her plate and threw it away.
The blender malfunctioned and sprayed sticky glop all over the kitchen.
His shoes were covered in some kind of brown glop from the construction site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goo - soft, sticky or viscous substance
  • Synonym: gunk - any sticky or greasy substance
  • Synonym: sludge - thick, soft, wet mud or a similar viscous mixture

2. NOUN

/ ɡlɑp / (AmE), / ɡlɒp / (BrE)
writing, music, or other artistic work that is excessively sweet, sentimental, or lacking in seriousness or depth
overly sentimental writing or music
informal

Examples

The movie was nothing but predictable glop designed to make audiences cry.
Her latest novel is popular, but critics have dismissed it as sentimental glop.
I can't listen to that glop they call love songs on the radio these days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schmaltz - excessively sentimental music, art, or writing
  • Synonym: kitsch - art or writing considered to be in poor taste due to excessive sentimentality

Etymology

Imitative; originally used for messy or sticky substances, later extended metaphorically to sentimental art or writing.
