fenestral

1. ADJ

/ fəˈnɛstrəl / (AmE), / fəˈnɛstrəl / (BrE)
relating to or resembling a window or windows; of or pertaining to windows.
relating to windows

Examples

The building's fenestral design allows ample sunlight to flood the interior spaces.
Many cathedrals are noted for their elaborate fenestral decorations.
The fenestral openings above the door provided ventilation and light.

Etymology

From Latin 'fenestra' meaning 'window', with the adjective-forming suffix '-al'.

2. ADJ

/ fəˈnɛstrəl / (AmE), / fɪˈnɛstrəl / (BrE)
relating to or having a fenestra, especially in anatomical or biological contexts.
relating to a fenestra (opening)

Examples

The fenestral structure in the bone allows nerves to pass through.
Certain reptiles have a fenestral skull due to evolution.
The fenestral opening is clearly visible in the cross-section diagram.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fenestra - an opening or window-like structure in anatomy

Etymology

From Latin 'fenestra' (window) + '-al'.
