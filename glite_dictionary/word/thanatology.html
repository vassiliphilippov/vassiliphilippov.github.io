thanatology

1. NOUN

/ ˌθænəˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌθænəˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of death and the practices associated with it, particularly in its social and psychological aspects
the scientific study of death

Examples

The medical conference included a panel on thanatology and its role in hospice care.
As a graduate student, she chose to specialize in thanatology to help families cope with loss.
Recent advancements in thanatology have changed the way society approaches end-of-life issues.

Etymology

From Greek thanatos (death) + -logy (study of).
