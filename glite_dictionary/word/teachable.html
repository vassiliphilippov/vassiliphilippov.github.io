teachable

1. ADJ

/ ˈtiːtʃəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtiːtʃəbl / (BrE)
willing and able to learn; capable of being taught or instructed
able and willing to be taught

Examples

As a teachable student, she quickly absorbed new ideas and skills in the classroom.
The coach values athletes who are teachable because they respond well to feedback.
He has a teachable attitude that makes him a pleasure to work with on any project.

Synset Examples

docile pupils eager for instruction
teachable youngsters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: docile - ready to accept instruction; submissive
  • Antonym: unteachable - not able to be taught or unwilling to learn

Etymology

From English 'teach' + '-able', meaning capable of being taught.
