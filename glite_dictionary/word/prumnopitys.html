Prumnopitys

1. NOUN

/ prumˈnɑpɪtɪs / (AmE), / prʌmˈnɒpɪtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of mostly dioecious evergreen coniferous trees, with relatively soft, flat leaves, native to the Southern Hemisphere.
a genus of evergreen coniferous trees

Examples

The Prumnopitys species are valued for their durable timber and attractive foliage.
Some Prumnopitys trees can reach impressive heights in their native forests.
Botanists often compare Prumnopitys leaves to those of Podocarpus due to their similar appearance.

Etymology

From Greek prymnos (hindmost) and pitys (pine), referring to its pine-like appearance.

2. NOUN

/ prʌmˈnɒpɪtɪs / (AmE), / prʌmˈnɒpɪtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of mostly dioecious evergreen coniferous trees with softer leaves than those in Podocarpus, native to parts of the Southern Hemisphere.
a genus of evergreen coniferous trees

Examples

The Prumnopitys genus includes several slow-growing conifer species found in South America and Australasia.
Leaves of Prumnopitys trees are notably softer than those of most podocarps.
Researchers studied the seed dispersal mechanisms in various Prumnopitys species.

Etymology

From Greek prymnos (hindmost) and pitys (pine).
