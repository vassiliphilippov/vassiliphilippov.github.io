William Rufus

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm ˈruːfəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm ˈruːfəs / (BrE)
the second son of William the Conqueror, who was King of England from 1087 to 1100
King of England (1087–1100), son of William the Conqueror

Examples

Many historians debate whether William Rufus was assassinated or died in a hunting accident.
The reign of William Rufus was marked by conflict with the church and his nobles.
King William Rufus succeeded his father, William the Conqueror, in 1087.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: William II - another name for William Rufus, King of England (1087–1100)

Etymology

From Latin 'Rufus' meaning 'red', referring to his ruddy complexion or red hair.
