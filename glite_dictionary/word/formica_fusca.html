Formica fusca

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈmaɪkə ˈfʌskə / (AmE), / fɔːˈmaɪkə ˈfʌskə / (BrE)
a species of ant found in Europe and parts of Asia, often noted for being frequently enslaved by other ant species.
a species of ant often enslaved by others

Examples

The Formica fusca is commonly found in woodland environments across Europe.
Some species of ants, such as Formica fusca, are known to be enslaved by other ants.
Researchers observed that the Formica fusca colonies displayed complex social behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ant - a small insect living in large social colonies

Etymology

From Latin; 'Formica' meaning 'ant' and 'fusca' meaning 'dark' or 'dusky'.
