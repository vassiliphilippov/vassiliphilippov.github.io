orphanhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.fən.hʊd / (AmE), / ˈɔː.fən.hʊd / (BrE)
the condition or state of being an orphan; having lost both parents through death.
the state of being an orphan

Examples

Her sense of orphanhood made her independent at an early age.
After his parents died, the reality of orphanhood became painfully clear.
Literature often explores the theme of orphanhood and its impact on children's lives.

Synset Examples

his early orphanage shaped his character as an adult

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orphan - a child whose parents are dead

Etymology

From 'orphan' + suffix '-hood', from Old English '-hād' (state, condition).
