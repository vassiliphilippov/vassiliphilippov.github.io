valiant

1. ADJ

/ ˈvæljənt / (AmE), / ˈvælɪənt / (BrE)
showing courage or determination, especially in the face of great danger or difficulty.
showing courage; brave

Examples

Her valiant efforts in the competition inspired everyone around her.
Despite fierce opposition, he made a valiant stand for what he believed was right.
The firefighters made a valiant attempt to save those trapped inside the building.

Synset Examples

a valiant attempt to prevent the hijack
a valiant soldier

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brave - ready to face and endure danger or pain; showing courage
  • Antonym: cowardly - lacking courage; easily frightened

Etymology

From Old French *vaillant* (brave, worthy), from Latin *valentem* (being strong).
