nervus hypoglosus

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɜrvəs ˌhaɪpoʊˈɡloʊsəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs ˌhɪpəˈɡlɒsəs / (BrE)
the twelfth cranial nerve that supplies the muscles of the tongue
cranial nerve for tongue muscles
medical

Examples

Damage to the nervus hypoglosus can cause weakness in tongue movement.
During surgery, care must be taken not to injure the nervus hypoglosus.
The nervus hypoglosus plays a crucial role in speech and swallowing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypoglossal nerve - the twelfth cranial nerve supplying the tongue muscles

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'nerve below the tongue'.
