Penobscot

1. PROPN

/ pəˈnɑb.skɑt / (AmE), / pəˈnɒb.skɒt / (BrE)
a river in central Maine, United States, that flows into Penobscot Bay
river in central Maine, USA

Examples

The Penobscot is the largest river entirely within Maine, spanning nearly 350 miles.
Many towns along the Penobscot depend on the river for fishing and recreation.
The bridge over the Penobscot offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Etymology

From the name of the indigenous Penobscot people, itself from Eastern Abenaki 'Panawámpskek' (where the rocks widen out).

2. PROPN

/ pəˈnɑbˌskɑt / (AmE), / pəˈnɒbˌskɒt / (BrE)
A member of the Native American people belonging to the Algonquian language family, traditionally living in the Penobscot valley of northern Maine.
a Native American people of northern Maine

Examples

The Penobscot have maintained their cultural traditions for centuries in Maine.
She studied the history and language of the Penobscot during her anthropology course.
Many members of the Penobscot continue to advocate for their rights and preserve their heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Abnaki - another Native American group belonging to the same confederacy

Etymology

From the Penobscot word 'Pαnáwαhpskek' meaning 'place of the white rocks'.
