deferral

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɜrəl / (AmE), / dɪˈfɜːrəl / (BrE)
the action or state of delaying or postponing something; a situation where a decision or activity is put on hold
the act of delaying or postponing

Examples

The committee agreed to a deferral of the final decision until more data could be gathered.
University students may request a one-year deferral before starting their courses.
Due to unforeseen circumstances, the project was put into deferral status until next quarter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postponement - the action of delaying something
  • Synonym: abeyance - a state of temporary disuse or suspension

Etymology

From defer + -al, based on Latin 'differre' (to delay).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɜr.əl / (AmE), / dɪˈfɜː.rəl / (BrE)
the act of delaying or postponing something to a future time
the act of postponing something

Examples

The company announced the deferral of its new product launch until next year.
His request for a deferral of the payment was granted due to financial hardship.
University students may apply for a deferral if they are unable to attend exams due to illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: postponement - the action of postponing something; delay
  • Synonym: delay - an instance of postponing or slowing something
  • Antonym: acceleration - the process of speeding something up

Etymology

From the verb 'defer,' ultimately from Latin 'differre' (to delay, differ).
