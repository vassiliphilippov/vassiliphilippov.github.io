dispensableness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɛnsəbəlnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈspɛnsəbəlnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being able to be done without or not essential.
quality of being not essential

Examples

The dispensableness of certain luxury items becomes obvious during financial hardships.
She questioned the dispensableness of some tasks on the daily agenda.
Modern technology can increase the dispensableness of manual labor in many industries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispensable - able to be replaced or done without; not essential
  • Antonym: indispensability - the quality of being absolutely necessary or essential

Etymology

From dispensable + -ness.
