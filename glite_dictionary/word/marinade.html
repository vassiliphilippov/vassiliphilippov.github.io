marinade

1. NOUN

/ ˌmærɪˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˌmærɪˈneɪd / (BrE)
A mixture, usually of vinegar or wine, oil, and spices, in which food is soaked to give it flavor before cooking.
flavored liquid for soaking food before cooking

Examples

She prepared a tangy marinade with garlic and lemon for the chicken.
Let the beef sit in the marinade overnight to absorb all the flavors.
Before grilling, he soaked the vegetables in a spicy marinade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to marinate - to soak food in a marinade before cooking

Etymology

From French *mariner* (to pickle in brine), from Old French *marin* (marine).

2. VERB

/ ˌmærəˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˌmærɪˈneɪd / (BrE)
to soak food, especially meat or fish, in a marinade before cooking to add flavor or tenderize it
soak food in a marinade before cooking

Examples

It's best to marinade the chicken overnight to let the flavors develop.
The chef decided to marinade the beef in red wine and herbs before grilling it.
Many recipes recommend you marinade fish before baking to keep it moist.

Synset Examples

marinade herring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to marinate - to soak food in a seasoned liquid before cooking

Etymology

From French *mariner* (to pickle in brine), related to 'marinade', borrowed from Italian *marinare* (to pickle, marinate).
