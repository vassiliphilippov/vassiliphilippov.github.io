quinidine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪnəˌdiːn / (AmE), / ˈkwɪnɪdiːn / (BrE)
a drug used to treat certain types of irregular heartbeats (arrhythmias)
drug for treating heart arrhythmias
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed quinidine to regulate her abnormal heartbeat.
Patients taking quinidine often need regular monitoring for side effects.
Treatment of ventricular arrhythmias may involve medications such as quinidine.

Etymology

From quinine, with a scientific suffix. Related to Latin *quina* (cinchona tree).
