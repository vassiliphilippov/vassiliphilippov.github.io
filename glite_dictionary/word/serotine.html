serotine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛr.ə.taɪn / (AmE), / sɛˈrɒt.iːn / (BrE)
A common brown bat of Europe, belonging to the species Eptesicus serotinus.
a European brown bat species

Examples

The serotine is often seen hunting insects at dusk during the summer months.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of the serotine in rural France.
Unlike some other bats, the serotine emerges relatively late in the evening.

Etymology

From Latin serotinus meaning 'late', referring to its late evening activity.
