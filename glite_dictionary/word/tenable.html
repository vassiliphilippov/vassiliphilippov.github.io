tenable

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛnəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛnəbəl / (BrE)
(of a theory, argument, or position) able to be defended against criticism or attack; based on sound reasoning or evidence.
able to be defended or justified

Examples

The committee found his explanation barely tenable in light of the new evidence.
No tenable argument supported their position after the results were published.
She presented a tenable thesis that convinced even her harshest critics.

Synset Examples

well-founded suspicions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untenable - not able to be maintained against attack or objection
  • Synonym: defensible - able to be defended or justified

Etymology

From Old French 'tenir' (to hold) and Latin 'tenere' (to hold).
