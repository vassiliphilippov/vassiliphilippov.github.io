tracer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪsɚ / (AmE), / ˈtreɪsə / (BrE)
a radioactive substance introduced into the body to study metabolic or other biological processes, especially in radiology or medical research
radioactive substance for medical tracing
medical

Examples

A radioactive tracer was injected to help visualize the blood flow in the patient's heart.
Doctors used a tracer to monitor the spread of the drug throughout the body.
The PET scan relies on a tracer to highlight areas of abnormal cell activity.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪsər / (AmE), / ˈtreɪsə / (BrE)
a person employed to locate missing people or goods; an investigator who traces the whereabouts of something or someone
investigator who finds missing people or goods

Examples

The company hired a tracer to locate the stolen shipment.
After her uncle went missing, a tracer was brought in to follow the leads.
Insurance firms often work with a tracer when clients disappear without a trace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigator - a person whose job is to investigate and solve cases

3. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪsər / (AmE), / ˈtreɪsə / (BrE)
A type of bullet or shell that leaves a visible trail of smoke or light so its trajectory can be observed.
bullet or shell that leaves a visible trail

Examples

The soldiers loaded their rifles with tracer rounds for the night exercise.
Watching the path of the tracer helped the shooter adjust their aim.
A bright tracer streaked across the sky during the battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tracer bullet - bullet that leaves a luminous or smokey trail

Etymology

From 'trace' + -er, meaning something that traces or marks out a path.

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪsər / (AmE), / ˈtreɪsə / (BrE)
an instrument used to make copies or outlines by following the lines of an original drawing or pattern.
instrument for making tracings

Examples

The architect used a tracer to reproduce the complex pattern onto a new sheet.
Artists often rely on a tracer when working with intricate designs.
She carefully followed the lines of the old map using a tracer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stylus - instrument for drawing or tracing lines

Etymology

From trace + -er, meaning one who or that which traces.
