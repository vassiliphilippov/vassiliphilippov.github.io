abbe

1. NOUN

/ æˈbeɪ / (AmE), / ˈæbeɪ / (BrE)
a French abbot, especially one from the time before the French Revolution; used as a title for some clergymen in France
French abbot or cleric

Examples

The young abbe delivered an inspiring sermon to the congregation.
In 18th-century France, an abbe often played a significant role in the local community.
He befriended an elderly abbe who introduced him to Parisian society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abbot - the head of an abbey of monks

Etymology

From French abbé (abbot), from Latin abbas (abbot).
