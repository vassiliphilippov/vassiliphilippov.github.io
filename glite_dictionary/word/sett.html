sett

1. NOUN

/ sɛt / (AmE), / sɛt / (BrE)
a rectangular paving stone with a curved top, formerly used to surface roads
rectangular paving stone for roads

Examples

The old street was lined with setts instead of modern asphalt.
Workers carefully laid each sett to create a durable cobbled surface.
You can still see setts in some historic European city centers.

Etymology

From Old English *settan* (to set); related to 'set', referring to how the stones are set in place.
