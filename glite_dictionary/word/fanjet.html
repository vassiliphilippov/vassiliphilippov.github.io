fanjet

1. NOUN

/ ˈfænˌdʒɛt / (AmE), / ˈfanˌdʒɛt / (BrE)
An aircraft powered by a fanjet (turbofan) engine.
aircraft powered by a fanjet engine

Examples

The new fanjet offers improved efficiency compared to older propeller planes.
Engineers are testing a prototype fanjet designed for supersonic speeds.
A fanjet can fly at higher altitudes than most other commercial aircraft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbofan - a type of jet engine with a fan that provides additional thrust

Etymology

From 'fan' and 'jet', referring to the fan component of the jet engine.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfænˌdʒɛt / (AmE), / ˈfænˌdʒɛt / (BrE)
A jet engine in which a fan driven by a turbine supplies additional air to the burner, producing extra thrust; a type of turbofan engine.
type of jet engine with a turbine-driven fan

Examples

The new airliner uses a fanjet engine for greater fuel efficiency and quieter operation.
Maintenance on a fanjet requires specialized knowledge distinct from traditional turbojets.
Many commercial airplanes today are powered by advanced fanjets for smoother and more powerful flights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbofan - a jet engine similar to a fanjet, commonly used in modern aircraft

Etymology

From 'fan' + 'jet', indicating a jet engine with a turbine-driven fan.

WordNet ID: oewn-03326048-n
Synset POS: n
