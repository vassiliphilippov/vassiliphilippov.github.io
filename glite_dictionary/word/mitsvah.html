mitsvah

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪtsvə / (AmE), / ˈmɪtsvə / (BrE)
(Judaism) a good deed or religious commandment performed out of religious duty.
Jewish religious commandment or good deed

Examples

He considered helping the elderly neighbor a true mitsvah.
Donating to charity is an important mitsvah in many Jewish communities.
She performed a mitsvah by volunteering at the soup kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mitzvah - alternative spelling: Jewish commandment or good deed

Etymology

From Hebrew מִצְוָה (mitzvah), meaning 'commandment'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪtsvə / (AmE), / ˈmɪtsvə / (BrE)
(in Judaism) a commandment or religious duty prescribed by Jewish law.
Jewish religious commandment or duty

Examples

In the Jewish tradition, lighting the Sabbath candles is considered a mitsvah.
Helping those in need is a central mitsvah of the faith.
Each mitsvah serves to strengthen the connection between the community and God.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mitzvah - alternative spelling of 'mitsvah' (Jewish religious commandment)
  • Synonym: commandment - an order or law given by God in religious contexts

Etymology

From Hebrew מִצְוָה (mitzvah), meaning 'commandment'.
