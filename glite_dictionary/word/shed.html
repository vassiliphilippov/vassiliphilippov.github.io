shed

1. NOUN

/ ʃɛd / (AmE), / ʃɛd / (BrE)
a small, simple building, usually with a single story, used for storage or shelter, typically in a garden or yard
small outbuilding for storage or shelter

Examples

We keep our gardening tools in the shed behind the house.
During the storm, the cat hid in the old shed for shelter.
He built a wooden shed to store his bicycles and lawn equipment.

Etymology

From Old English 'scead' meaning 'separation' or 'cover'.
