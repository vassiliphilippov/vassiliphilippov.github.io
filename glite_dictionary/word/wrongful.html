wrongful

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɔŋ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈrɒŋ.fəl / (BrE)
(of an act) not fair, morally right, or legal; involving a violation of someone's rights or the law.
unfair or illegal; violating rights

Examples

The company was found liable for the wrongful termination of several employees.
She sued for damages after experiencing a wrongful eviction from her apartment.
A wrongful conviction can destroy an innocent person's life.

Synset Examples

wrongful death
a wrongful diversion of trust income

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegal - forbidden by law
  • Antonym: lawful - conforming to, permitted by, or recognized by law or rules

Etymology

From Middle English, from 'wrong' + '-ful'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɔŋfəl / (AmE), / ˈrɒŋfʊl / (BrE)
(of a person or action) having no legally valid claim or justification; not authorized by law.
not having a legal claim or right
legal

Examples

The court ruled that he was the wrongful heir to the property.
She received compensation for her wrongful dismissal from the company.
His wrongful claim to the land was successfully challenged in court.

Synset Examples

the wrongful heir to the throne

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lawful - in accordance with the law; having a legal right or claim

Etymology

From Old English 'wrang' (wrong) + '-ful'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01411393-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈrɔŋ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈrɒŋ.fəl / (BrE)
(of an action) not fair, just, or legal; unjust or illicit.
unjust or illegal

Examples

The company faced several lawsuits after its wrongful dismissal of employees.
He was arrested on a wrongful charge and later released when the truth came out.
Victims of wrongful imprisonment may be entitled to compensation.

Synset Examples

a wrongful act
a wrongful charge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unlawful - not conforming to, permitted by, or recognized by law or rules
  • Antonym: just - morally right and fair

Etymology

From Old English 'wrang' (wrong) + '-ful', meaning 'full of wrong'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01373936-s
Synset POS: s
