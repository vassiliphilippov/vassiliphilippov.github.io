Arianrhod

1. PROPN

/ ˌɛəriˌɑːnˈroʊd / (AmE), / ˌariːˈanrɒd / (BrE)
A Celtic goddess in Welsh mythology renowned for her beauty and known as the mother of Dylan and Lleu Llaw Gyffes.
Celtic/Welsh mythological goddess

Examples

The legend of Arianrhod plays a significant role in Welsh mythology.
Scholars have debated the symbolism of Arianrhod's silver wheel.
In many stories, Arianrhod is depicted as both beautiful and enigmatic.

Etymology

From Welsh mythology; Welsh 'arian' (silver) + 'rhod' (wheel).
