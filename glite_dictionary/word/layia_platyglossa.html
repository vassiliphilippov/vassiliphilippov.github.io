Layia platyglossa

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.i.ə ˌplæ.tiˈɡlɒs.ə / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.i.ə ˌplæt.ɪˈɡlɒs.ə / (BrE)
A species of annual plant native to California, known for its yellow flower heads tipped with white.
California annual plant with yellow-white flowers

Examples

The wildflower garden was filled with Layia platyglossa in full bloom during the spring.
California's grasslands turn vibrant yellow and white when Layia platyglossa carpets the hillsides.
Botanists study Layia platyglossa to understand how native species adapt to their environment.

Etymology

From Latinized botanical nomenclature: genus 'Layia', species 'platyglossa' (Greek platys 'broad' + glossa 'tongue').
