to denigrate

1. VERB

/ ˈdenɪˌɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛnɪɡreɪt / (BrE)
to unfairly criticize or defame someone, damaging their reputation
criticize or defame unfairly

Examples

The politician tried to denigrate his opponent’s character during the debate.
It is unfair to denigrate someone’s achievements without knowing the full story.
She was hurt by those who continued to denigrate her work despite its success.

Synset Examples

The journalists have defamed me!
The article in the paper sullied my reputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defame - damage the good reputation of someone; slander or libel
  • Antonym: praise - express warm approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin denigrare (to blacken), from de- (completely) + nigrare (to blacken).

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛnəˌɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛnɪɡreɪt / (BrE)
to criticize unfairly; to make something or someone seem less important or valuable
criticize unfairly; belittle

Examples

It is unfair to denigrate the efforts of those who work behind the scenes.
He often denigrated his colleagues to boost his own reputation.
Instead of helping, she chose to denigrate my idea in front of everyone.

Synset Examples

Don't belittle his influence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to belittle - to make someone or something seem unimportant
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin 'denigrare' (to blacken), from 'de-' (away, completely) + 'niger' (black).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00866139-v
Synset POS: v
