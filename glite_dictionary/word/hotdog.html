hotdog

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑt.dɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈhɒt.dɒɡ / (BrE)
A cooked frankfurter or sausage served in a sliced bun, often with condiments such as mustard, ketchup, and relish.
frankfurter in a bun

Examples

She ordered a hotdog with extra mustard from the street vendor.
At the baseball game, I always eat a hotdog and drink a soda.
Kids love making their own hotdog creations at birthday parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frankfurter - a type of cooked sausage
  • Synonym: sausage - ground meat seasoned and stuffed into a casing

Etymology

From 'hot' and 'dog', possibly referring to the shape and warmth of the sausage; popularized in the U.S. in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑt.dɔg / (AmE), / ˈhɒt.dɒg / (BrE)
A cooked sausage of finely minced beef or pork, usually smoked, and often served in a sliced bread roll, typically as a snack or fast food.
a cooked sausage served in a bread roll

Examples

He bought a hotdog from the street vendor for lunch.
The stadium was crowded with people eating hotdogs and cheering for their team.
Would you like mustard or ketchup on your hotdog?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frankfurter - a similar cooked sausage typically served hot
  • Degree Higher: sausage - a general term for a food made of ground meat in a casing
  • Synonym: bratwurst - a type of German sausage often grilled or fried

person who shows off with daring stunts
