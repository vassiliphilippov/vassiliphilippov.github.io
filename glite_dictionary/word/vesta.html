Vesta

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɛstə / (AmE), / ˈvɛstə / (BrE)
the brightest asteroid in the solar system, and the fourth to be discovered, located in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter
the brightest asteroid; fourth discovered

Examples

Vesta can sometimes be seen with the naked eye under very dark skies.
NASA's Dawn spacecraft studied Vesta up close in 2011.
Among asteroids, Vesta is known for its unusually bright surface.

Etymology

Named after Vesta, the Roman goddess of hearth and home.

2. PROPN

/ ˈvɛstə / (AmE), / ˈvɛstə / (BrE)
(in Roman mythology) the goddess of the hearth and its sacred fire, whose flame was tended by Vestal Virgins; counterpart of the Greek goddess Hestia.
Roman goddess of the hearth

Examples

The ancient Romans prayed to Vesta to protect their homes and families.
The sacred flame of Vesta was never allowed to go out in her temple.
As a counterpart to Hestia in Greek mythology, Vesta was revered throughout Rome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hestia - Greek goddess of the hearth and home

Etymology

From Latin Vesta, of uncertain origin, possibly related to the Greek Hestia.
