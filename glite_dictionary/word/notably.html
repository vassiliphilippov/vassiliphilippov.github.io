notably

1. ADV

/ ˈnoʊtəbli / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtəbli / (BrE)
to a remarkable or significant degree; especially or in particular.
to a remarkable degree; especially

Examples

The city is notably quieter in the early morning hours.
Several important figures were notably absent from the ceremony.
The new policy has notably improved public safety.

Synset Examples

she was unusually tall
Notably missing from the network's fall line-up are any half-hour scripted comedies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: especially - used to single out one person, thing, or situation over all others
  • Synonym: particularly - to a higher degree than is usual or average

Etymology

From Old French *notablement*, from Latin *notabilis* (notable).

2. ADV

/ ˈnoʊtəbli / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtəbli / (BrE)
Especially; in particular.
especially; in particular

Examples

The company has grown rapidly in recent years, notably in the European market.
Many athletes, notably marathon runners, follow strict dietary plans.
Tourism has increased, notably during the summer months.

Synset Examples

notably in the social sciences, the professors teach too much

Related Terms

  • Synonym: especially - in particular; to a distinctly greater extent

Etymology

From Middle French notable and -ly, ultimately from Latin notabilis (noteworthy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00140076-r
Synset POS: r
