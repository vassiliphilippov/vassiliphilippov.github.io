recrudescent

1. ADJ

/ ˌriːkruːˈdɛsənt / (AmE), / ˌriːkruːˈdɛsənt / (BrE)
(of something undesirable) breaking out or recurring again after a period of abatement or inactivity.
breaking out again after a pause

Examples

The region faced a recrudescent threat of conflict after several years of peace.
There was concern over the recrudescent violence in the town.
The disease appeared to be under control, but a recrudescent outbreak alarmed health officials.

Synset Examples

the patient presented with a case of recrudescent gastralgia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relapsing - (of a disease or condition) recurring after improvement
  • Synonym: resurgent - rising again into life or activity

Etymology

From Latin recrudescens, recrudescent-, present participle of recrudescere (to become raw again).
