microtome

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.kɹəˌtoʊm / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪ.krə.təʊm / (BrE)
a scientific instrument used to cut extremely thin slices of material for examination under a microscope
instrument for slicing thin sections for microscopy
medical

Examples

The laboratory technician used a microtome to prepare tissue samples for microscopic examination.
With a microtome, scientists can cut sections thin enough to view individual cell structures.
Proper alignment of the microtome blade is crucial for obtaining uniform slices.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ultramicrotome - an instrument for cutting ultra-thin slices for electron microscopy

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) and 'tomē' (cutting).
