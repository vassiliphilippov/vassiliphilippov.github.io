Scolopax

1. NOUN

/ skəˈloʊ.pæks / (AmE), / skəˈləʊ.paks / (BrE)
a genus of birds in the family Scolopacidae, comprising the Old World woodcocks
genus of Old World woodcocks

Examples

The genus Scolopax includes several species of woodcocks found across Europe and Asia.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Scolopax genus in Southeast Asia.
Unlike their American relatives, members of Scolopax are distinguished by their unique plumage patterns.

Etymology

From Latin *scolopax* (woodcock).
