mannequin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪˌkɪn / (AmE), / ˈmænɪkɪn / (BrE)
a woman who models clothes to display fashions, especially for designers or on catwalks
female fashion model

Examples

She started her career as a mannequin for a well-known Paris fashion house.
The aspiring mannequin walked confidently down the runway in the designer's latest collection.
Critics praised the mannequin for her elegant style and grace.

Synset Examples

she was too fat to be a mannequin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - person employed to wear or display clothing and pose for photographs

Etymology

From French *mannequin*, meaning a model or artist's jointed figure.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmænəˌkɪn / (AmE), / ˈmænɪkɪn / (BrE)
a life-size model of a human figure, used by tailors, dressmakers, and in shop displays to show clothing and accessories
life-size model for displaying clothes

Examples

The new dress looked stunning on the mannequin in the shop window.
He adjusted the jacket on the mannequin before the grand opening.
Several mannequins were arranged to model the store's spring collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dummy - a model of a human figure, especially used in place of a person

Etymology

From French 'mannequin,' from Middle Dutch 'manneken' (little man).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03723460-n
Synset POS: n
