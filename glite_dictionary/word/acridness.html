acridness

1. NOUN

/ ˈækrɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈækrɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having a strong, bitter, and pungent taste or smell
extreme bitterness or pungency

Examples

The acridness of the chemicals in the air made it hard to breathe.
She wrinkled her nose at the acridness of the burnt toast.
The acridness of the smoke lingered long after the fire was out.

Synset Examples

the acridity of alkali

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acidity - sharpness or sourness in taste or smell
  • Synonym: bitterness - having a sharp, unpleasant taste or smell

Etymology

From Latin 'acer' (sharp) via 'acrid'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈækrɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈækrɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being harshly or sharply disagreeable in language or tone
sharp disagreeableness in tone or language

Examples

Her response carried a surprising acridness that silenced the room.
The acridness of his remarks left everyone feeling uncomfortable.
Despite the acridness in their exchange, they managed to resolve the issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrimony - bitterness or sharpness in speech or behavior
  • Antonym: gentleness - the quality of being gentle or mild in language or tone

Etymology

From 'acrid' (from Latin 'acer' meaning sharp) + '-ness'.
