to interfere

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈfɪr / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈfɪə / (BrE)
to become involved in a situation so as to prevent or hinder a process or activity
to hinder or become involved in a situation

Examples

Please do not interfere with the ongoing investigation.
Loud noises from outside can interfere with my concentration when I study.
She didn't want to interfere in her friend's personal matters.

Synset Examples

Your talking interferes with my work!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to obstruct - to block or get in the way of something or someone
  • Antonym: assist - to help or facilitate a process or activity

Etymology

From Latin *interferre* (to strike between), from *inter-* (between) + *ferre* (to carry).

2. VERB

/ ˌɪn.tɚˈfɪr / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈfɪə / (BrE)
to become involved in a situation in a way that prevents things from happening as they would have otherwise, often causing disruption or hindrance
to get involved and hinder or obstruct

Examples

Please don't interfere with the investigation, as it could compromise the results.
The government promised not to interfere in private businesses.
Parents should avoid interfering too much in their children's friendships.

Synset Examples

Why did the U.S. not intervene earlier in WW II?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intervene - to get involved in a situation to help resolve it or alter its course
  • Synonym: to meddle - to involve oneself in other people's affairs without invitation or necessity
  • Antonym: assist - to help or support, as opposed to hindering

Etymology

From Latin inter- (between) + ferīre (to strike).
