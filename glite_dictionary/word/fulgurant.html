fulgurant

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʌlɡjərənt / (AmE), / ˈfʌlɡjʊərənt / (BrE)
Amazingly impressive or striking; flashing like lightning; dazzling.
dazzling, striking like lightning

Examples

The artist's fulgurant style captivated critics and audiences alike.
A fulgurant light streaked across the night sky, illuminating the entire valley.
She delivered a fulgurant performance that left the audience breathless.

Synset Examples

the skater's dazzling virtuosic leaps
these great best canvases still look as astonishing and as invitingly new as they did … when … his fulgurant popularity was in full growth
adventures related … in a style both vivid and fulgurous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dazzling - extremely impressive or beautiful; stunning
  • Synonym: astonishing - very surprising; difficult to believe
  • Antonym: ordinary - with no special or distinctive features; normal

Etymology

From Latin fulgūrāns, fulgūrantis, present participle of fulgūrāre (to flash with lightning), from fulgur (lightning).
