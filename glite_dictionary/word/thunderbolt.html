thunderbolt

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌndɚˌboʊlt / (AmE), / ˈθʌndəˌbəʊlt / (BrE)
a flash of lightning followed by a crash of thunder, typically regarded as a single event
a lightning flash with thunder

Examples

A sudden thunderbolt struck the tree, splintering it instantly.
We heard a thunderbolt in the distance as the storm approached.
The ancient myth described Zeus hurling a thunderbolt from the sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lightning - the flash of light in the sky during a thunderstorm

Etymology

From Old English 'thunorbolt' (thunder + bolt).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθʌndərˌboʊlt / (AmE), / ˈθʌndəbəʊlt / (BrE)
a sudden and unexpected event that causes great surprise or shock
shocking surprise; sudden shock

Examples

The announcement of his resignation was a real thunderbolt to the team.
Her sudden decision to move abroad came as a thunderbolt to her friends.
It was a thunderbolt when she revealed the family secret during dinner.

Synset Examples

news of the attack came like a bombshell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombshell - something that causes great surprise or shock
  • Synonym: shock - a sudden upsetting or surprising event

Etymology

From Old English 'þunorbolt', combining 'thunder' and 'bolt' (missile, shaft of lightning).

Synset POS: n
