Muscari

1. NOUN

/ mʌˈskɑːri / (AmE), / mjuːˈskɑːri / (BrE)
a genus of small, bulbous perennial plants with dense spikes of usually blue, urn-shaped flowers, commonly known as grape hyacinths
genus of plants; grape hyacinth

Examples

The Muscari in her garden produced a vivid display of blue blossoms in spring.
Botanists often confuse Muscari with some related genera due to their similar flower shapes.
Many gardeners plant Muscari bulbs along pathways for their charming, early spring color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grape hyacinth - a common name for plants in the genus Muscari

Etymology

From Italian 'muscario', from Latin 'muscare' (to perfume with musk), referencing the musky scent of some species.

2. NOUN

/ mʌˈskɛəri / (AmE), / mʊsˈkɑːri / (BrE)
A genus of small, bulbous perennial plants in the family Asparagaceae, commonly known as grape hyacinths, with compact clusters of usually blue, bell-shaped flowers.
genus of plants known as grape hyacinth

Examples

In the spring, Muscari blooms in vibrant blue clusters across the garden.
Many gardeners plant Muscari bulbs to add early color to flower beds.
The delicate, bell-shaped flowers of Muscari are often mistaken for small grapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grape hyacinth - common name for plants in the genus Muscari

Etymology

From modern Latin Muscari, from Greek 'moschos' (musk), referring to the scent of some species.
