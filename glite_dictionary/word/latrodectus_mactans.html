Latrodectus mactans

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæt.rəˈdɛk.təs mækˈtænz / (AmE), / ˌlæt.rəˈdɛk.təs mækˈtanz / (BrE)
The scientific name for the black widow, a venomous spider native to the Americas, with females distinguished by a black body and an hourglass-shaped red mark underneath the abdomen.
scientific name for the black widow spider

Examples

The bite of the Latrodectus mactans can cause severe reactions in humans if untreated.
Researchers captured several Latrodectus mactans specimens for their venom studies.
Female Latrodectus mactans spiders are easily recognized by the red hourglass marking on their abdomens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black widow - common name for Latrodectus mactans, a venomous spider

Etymology

From Latin: Latrodectus (spider genus), mactans (killing).
