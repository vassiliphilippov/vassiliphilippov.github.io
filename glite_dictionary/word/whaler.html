whaler

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈweɪlə / (BrE)
A person who works on a ship that hunts and kills whales for commercial purposes.
a person who hunts whales

Examples

He spent twenty years as a whaler in the icy waters of the Arctic.
The old whaler told stories of his adventures at sea.
As a young man, he dreamed of becoming a whaler and exploring distant oceans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whaleman - a person who hunts whales, especially in the past

Etymology

From whale + -er, denoting a person involved with whales.

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪlər / (AmE), / ˈweɪlə / (BrE)
a ship used for catching whales
ship used for hunting whales

Examples

The old whaler sailed into port after a long journey in the Arctic.
In the museum, there is a model of a nineteenth-century whaler.
Many historic whalers have been preserved as floating museums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whaleboat - a small boat used in whale hunting

Etymology

From 'whale' + '-er', originally used in the early 18th century.
