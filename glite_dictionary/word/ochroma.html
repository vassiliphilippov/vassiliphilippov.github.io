Ochroma

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈkroʊmə / (AmE), / əʊˈkrəʊmə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees in the mallow family, best known for the species balsa (Ochroma pyramidale), which produces very lightweight wood.
genus of trees including balsa

Examples

Ochroma is a tropical genus whose most famous species produces balsa wood.
The wood of trees from the genus Ochroma is prized for its lightness and strength.
Botanists classify balsa trees under the genus Ochroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsa - a tree of the genus Ochroma known for its very light wood

Etymology

From Greek *ōkhros* (pale yellow), reflecting the color of the wood.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈkroʊmə / (AmE), / əʊˈkrəʊmə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American trees in the mallow family, best known for its only species, the balsa tree (Ochroma pyramidale), valued for its lightweight wood
genus of tropical trees including balsa

Examples

The Ochroma genus is native to the tropical regions of the Americas.
Lightweight balsa wood comes from trees of the Ochroma genus.
Researchers studied growth patterns in Ochroma to improve sustainable wood harvesting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsa - a tropical American tree known for its very lightweight wood

Etymology

From Greek *ochrōma* (pallor, pale color), possibly referring to the pale wood of the balsa tree.
