drudge

1. NOUN

/ drʌdʒ / (AmE), / drʌdʒ / (BrE)
A person who works hard at dull, routine, or menial tasks.
person who does dull, hard work

Examples

He spent years as a drudge in the office, performing the most tedious tasks.
Janet refused to be the household drudge, always picking up after everyone else.
In the past, many factory workers were regarded as little more than drudges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toiler - a person who works hard for long hours, often at unloved tasks
  • Antonym: idler - a person who avoids work or activity

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'druggen' (to work hard, slave).

2. NOUN

/ drʌdʒ / (AmE), / drʌdʒ / (BrE)
a person who is required to do hard, menial, or tedious work, especially work that is boring or thankless
person doing dull or menial work

Examples

After years as an office drudge, she longed for a more creative career.
He felt like a drudge spending all day filing paperwork.
The factory drudges were often overlooked despite their hard work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toiler - a person who works very hard, especially at hard or boring tasks
  • Synonym: laborer - a person doing unskilled manual work
  • Synonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and forced to work

Etymology

From Middle English 'druge', of uncertain origin.

3. VERB

/ drʌdʒ / (AmE), / drʌdʒ / (BrE)

work hard at dull tasks

Examples

4. NOUN

/ drʌdʒ / (AmE), / drʌdʒ / (BrE)
a person who does long, tedious, or monotonous work, especially of a menial kind
person doing tedious work

Examples

Working as a drudge in the office left him feeling unfulfilled and tired.
She refused to become a drudge and looked for more meaningful work.
Although he was new at the company, he wasn't content to be just a drudge.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toiler - a person who works very hard
  • Synonym: grind - a person who works hard at tedious tasks
  • Antonym: idler - a person who avoids work or activity

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'druggen' (to work hard); origin uncertain.
