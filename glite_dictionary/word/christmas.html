Christmas

1. PROPN

/ ˈkrɪs·məs / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs·məs / (BrE)
The annual Christian festival celebrating the birth of Jesus Christ, held on December 25th.
Christian holiday on December 25

Examples

Every year, the family gathers to celebrate Christmas together.
Many people exchange gifts and decorate their homes for Christmas.
Church services are often held on Christmas to mark the birth of Jesus Christ.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Easter - important Christian festival celebrating the resurrection of Christ
  • Synonym: Christmas Eve - the evening or day before Christmas Day
  • Synonym: Boxing Day - the day after Christmas, observed as a holiday in the UK and other countries

Etymology

From Old English Cristes mæsse (Mass of Christ).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪs·məs / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.məs / (BrE)
The period in the Christian calendar extending from December 24 (Christmas Eve) to January 6 (Epiphany), traditionally celebrated as the season of Christmas.
Christian holiday period, Dec. 24–Jan. 6

Examples

The church holds special services every day during Christmas.
Many families exchange gifts throughout the entire Christmas period, not just on December 25th.
In some cultures, Christmas lasts until Epiphany on January 6.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christmastide - the season of Christmas extending from December 24 to January 6
  • Degree Lower: Advent - the period of preparation before Christmas
  • Degree Higher: Epiphany - the celebration ending the Christmas season on January 6

Etymology

From Old English Cristes mæsse (mass of Christ), referring to the church festival celebrating the birth of Christ.
