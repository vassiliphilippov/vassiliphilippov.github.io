doctrinally

1. ADV

/ ˈdɑktrənəli / (AmE), / ˈdɒktrɪnəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or is based on doctrine or a system of beliefs, especially in religion or philosophy
in terms of doctrine or belief systems

Examples

The church remains doctrinally conservative despite social changes.
He disagreed with her doctrinally, but respected her integrity.
The schools are doctrinally aligned with traditional teachings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctrinal - relating to doctrine or a set of beliefs

Etymology

From 'doctrinal' + '-ly'. 'Doctrinal' from Late Latin doctrinalis (of doctrine).
