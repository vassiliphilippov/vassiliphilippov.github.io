bankroll

1. VERB

/ ˈbæŋkˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈbaŋk.rəʊl / (BrE)
to provide money for a project, person, or organization; to finance
to finance; provide funds

Examples

Several private investors decided to bankroll the new tech startup.
The documentary was bankrolled by a well-known philanthropist.
Without someone to bankroll the operation, the project risked being canceled.

Synset Examples

Who will bankroll the restoration of the former East German economy?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finance - to provide funding for a person or enterprise
  • Synonym: fund - to provide the money for something

Etymology

From the noun 'bankroll' (roll of banknotes), meaning 'supply of money' (late 19th century, US English).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈbæŋk.rəʊl / (BrE)
a roll of paper money; the funds or financial resources available to a person, organization, or business
a roll of cash; available funds

Examples

He built up his bankroll before entering the high-stakes game.
The company relied on a large bankroll to fund its new project.
After losing several bets, his bankroll was nearly gone.

Synset Examples

he shot his roll on a bob-tailed nag

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funds - money set aside or available for a purpose
  • Synonym: capital - money available for investment or business

Synset POS: n
