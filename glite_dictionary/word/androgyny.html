androgyny

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdrɑːdʒəni / (AmE), / ænˈdrɒdʒəni / (BrE)
The quality or state of having both male and female characteristics, either physically or in appearance or behavior.
having both male and female characteristics

Examples

The model's androgyny made her stand out in the fashion industry.
Some plants display androgyny by producing both male and female flowers.
He admired the androgyny of her features, which defied traditional gender norms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: masculinity - qualities or attributes regarded as characteristic of men
  • Antonym: femininity - qualities or attributes regarded as characteristic of women

Etymology

From Greek 'andro-' (man) and 'gynē' (woman).
