quintillion

1. NOUN

/ kwɪnˈtɪljən / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtɪljən / (BrE)
the number 1,000,000,000,000,000,000 (a one followed by 18 zeros), chiefly in British English usage
number equal to 1 followed by 18 zeros (UK)
british

Examples

A quintillion grains of sand would not fill the Sahara Desert.
In British English, a quintillion is one followed by eighteen zeros.
No computer today could process a quintillion calculations per second.

Synset Examples

in England they call a quintillion a ‘trillion’

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: quintillion - the number 1,000,000,000,000,000,000,000 in American usage (a one followed by 18 zeros)
  • Degree Lower: quadrillion - the number 1,000,000,000,000,000 (a one followed by 15 zeros) in British usage

Etymology

From Latin 'quintus' (fifth) + '-illion', modeled after 'million' and 'billion'.

2. NOUN

/ kwɪnˈtɪljən / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtɪljən / (BrE)
A number represented by 1 followed by 18 zeros (1,000,000,000,000,000,000) in the United States.
a number equal to 1 followed by 18 zeros (US)

Examples

In terms of distance, a light year is about six quintillion miles.
Scientists estimate there are more than a quintillion bacteria on Earth.
The computer performed a quintillion calculations per second.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quadrillion - a number equal to 1 followed by 15 zeros (US)
  • Degree Higher: sextillion - a number equal to 1 followed by 21 zeros (US)

Etymology

From French 'quintillion', based on Latin 'quintus' (fifth) with '-illion' as in 'million'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
