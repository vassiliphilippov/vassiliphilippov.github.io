Angrecum

1. NOUN

/ ænˈɡriːkəm / (AmE), / ænˈɡriːkəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical Old World epiphytic orchids with large, often striking or unusual flowers.
genus of tropical orchids

Examples

The Angrecum genus is renowned for its star-shaped flowers and long nectar spurs.
Several species of Angrecum are prized by collectors for their unique appearance.
Botanists study Angrecum to understand adaptation in epiphytic orchids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the orchid family, Orchidaceae

Etymology

From French 'angraecum', which is from Latinized form of Malay 'angrek' (orchid).
