Eptesicus

1. NOUN

/ ɛpˈtisɪkəs / (AmE), / ɛpˈtiːsɪkəs / (BrE)
a genus of bats in the family Vespertilionidae, commonly known as house bats
genus of house bats

Examples

The Eptesicus genus includes the widely known big brown bat.
Researchers studied the habits of Eptesicus in urban environments.
Many species in the Eptesicus genus are adapted to roosting in buildings.

Etymology

From Greek 'epten' (to fly) and 'oikos' (house).

2. NOUN

/ ɛpˈtɛsɪkəs / (AmE), / ɛpˈtɛsɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of bats in the family Vespertilionidae, commonly known as house bats.
a genus of bats (house bats)

Examples

The Eptesicus genus includes bats that are often found roosting in buildings.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Eptesicus genus during their expedition.
Members of Eptesicus are commonly referred to as house bats due to their urban habitats.

Etymology

From Greek epten (to fly) and oikos (house), referring to their habit of living near dwellings.
