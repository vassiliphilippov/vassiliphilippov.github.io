proportion

1. NOUN

/ pɹəˈpɔɹʃən / (AmE), / prəˈpɔːʃən / (BrE)
the relationship of one part to another or to the whole, expressed as a quotient or fraction
relationship between part and whole (fraction)

Examples

A small proportion of the population voted in the last election.
The proportion of sugar to flour is important in this recipe.
Only a tiny proportion of the budget is spent on education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ratio - a comparison of two quantities by division

Etymology

From Latin proportio (comparative relation, analogy), from pro (for) + portio (portion).

/ prəˈpɔrʃən / (AmE), / prəˈpɔːʃən / (BrE)

harmonious relationship of parts within a whole

Examples

Many artists study the proportion of the human body to improve their figure drawings.
