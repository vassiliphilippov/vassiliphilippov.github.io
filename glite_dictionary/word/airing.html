airing

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
an occasion when a subject or issue is discussed openly and publicly, often to encourage debate or consideration of different views
public discussion of a subject

Examples

The issue received a thorough airing during the meeting, with everyone contributing their thoughts.
Let's give the proposal a fair airing before making any decisions.
The documentary ensured the subject got a much-needed public airing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discussion - the act of talking about something to reach a decision or exchange ideas
  • Synonym: debate - a formal discussion on a particular topic

Etymology

From the verb 'air', meaning 'to expose to air' or 'to make known', plus '-ing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeərɪŋ / (BrE)
a short walk or ride taken outdoors for fresh air or exercise, often for one's health
a short outdoor walk or ride

Examples

He took the dogs for an airing before dinner.
After work, she enjoys a brief airing in the park to clear her mind.
We went for an airing along the seafront to enjoy the breeze.

Synset Examples

he took the dogs for an airing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroll - a leisurely walk, usually taken for pleasure
  • Synonym: outing - a short trip taken for pleasure, especially a group excursion

Etymology

From 'air' + -ing, originally referring to exposure to fresh air.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of allowing fresh air to circulate in a space to remove stale or foul air
allowing fresh air in

Examples

After months of being closed, the cottage needed a thorough airing before anyone could stay there.
She opened all the windows to give the room an airing.
Leaving your clothes outside for an airing can help make them smell fresh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ventilation - the provision of fresh air to a room, building, etc.

Etymology

From the verb 'air' (to expose to the air) plus -ing.
