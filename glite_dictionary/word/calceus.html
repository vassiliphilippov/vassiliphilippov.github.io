calceus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.si.əs / (AmE), / ˈkæl.si.əs / (BrE)
A shoe covering the ankle, worn by ancient Romans as part of their traditional attire.
ankle-high Roman shoe

Examples

The senator put on his calceus before entering the forum.
Archaeologists discovered a remarkably preserved calceus in the ruins of Pompeii.
Unlike sandals, the calceus was closed and covered the Roman foot up to the ankle.

Etymology

From Latin 'calceus' (shoe).
