rostrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːstrəm / (AmE), / ˈrɒstrəm / (BrE)
a beaklike projection at the front of the head of certain insects, such as weevils
beaklike projection on insect head

Examples

The weevil uses its rostrum to bore into plants and lay its eggs.
Detailed drawings of the insect's rostrum reveal adaptations for piercing.
Some aquatic insects possess an elongated rostrum for sucking plant sap.

Etymology

From Latin 'rostrum' meaning 'beak, snout'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːstrəm / (AmE), / ˈrɒstrəm / (BrE)
A platform raised above the surrounding level, used to give prominence to the person standing or speaking on it.
raised platform for speakers or performers

Examples

The politician stepped onto the rostrum to address the enthusiastic crowd.
At the graduation ceremony, each student was called up to the rostrum to receive their diploma.
The winner stood proudly on the highest rostrum, holding her gold medal aloft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: podium - a small platform for a speaker, performer, or conductor
  • Synonym: dais - a low platform for a lectern, seats of honor, or a throne

Etymology

From Latin 'rostrum' (beak, platform), originally referring to a speaker's platform decorated with the prows (beaks) of ships in ancient Rome.
