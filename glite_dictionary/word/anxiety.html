anxiety

1. NOUN

/ æŋˈzaɪəti / (AmE), / æŋˈzaɪəti / (BrE)
A feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease, typically about an imminent event or something with an uncertain outcome.
a feeling of worry or unease

Examples

She felt a growing sense of anxiety before the important interview.
His anxiety about the future kept him awake at night.
Despite her anxiety, she managed to give a confident presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worry - state of being anxious or concerned about actual or potential problems
  • Antonym: calm - state of being free from anxiety or worry

Etymology

From Latin anxietas (anxiety, uneasiness), from anxius (troubled in mind).

2. NOUN

/ æŋˈzaɪəti / (AmE), / æŋˈzaɪəti / (BrE)
A persistent feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease, often occurring in mental disorders and sometimes with compulsive behaviors or panic attacks.
state of persistent worry or nervousness
medical

Examples

He sought therapy after his anxiety began interfering with his daily life.
Chronic anxiety can sometimes lead to panic attacks or physical symptoms.
Some people experience intense anxiety in social situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worry - A feeling of unease or concern about something
  • Antonym: calmness - A state of being free from agitation or strong emotion

Etymology

From Latin anxietas (anxiousness), from anxius (troubled in mind, uneasy).
