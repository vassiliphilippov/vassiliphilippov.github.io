Fraxinus excelsior

1. NOUN

/ ˌfræksɪnəs ɪkˈsɛlsiər / (AmE), / ˌfræksɪnəs ɪkˈsɛlsiə / (BrE)
a tall ash tree native to Europe and western Asia, with shiny dark green leaves above and pale, downy leaves beneath, commonly known as the common ash
European ash tree species

Examples

The ancient Fraxinus excelsior in the village square has stood for over three centuries.
Some gardens feature Fraxinus excelsior for its graceful appearance and shade.
Diseases such as ash dieback threaten populations of Fraxinus excelsior across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash - a type of tree from the genus Fraxinus

Etymology

From Latin: ‘fraxinus’ meaning 'ash tree' and ‘excelsior’ meaning 'higher' or 'taller'.
