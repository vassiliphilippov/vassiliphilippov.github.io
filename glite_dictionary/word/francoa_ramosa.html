Francoa ramosa

1. NOUN

/ frænˈkoʊ.ə rəˈmoʊ.sə / (AmE), / franˈkəʊ.ə rəˈməʊ.sə / (BrE)
A Chilean evergreen shrub noted for its delicate spikes of small white flowers, grown as an ornamental plant.
Chilean evergreen shrub with white flower spikes

Examples

The Francoa ramosa in her garden bloomed early, attracting many pollinators with its white flowers.
Gardeners prize Francoa ramosa for its long-lasting spikes of delicate blossoms.
You can find Francoa ramosa in many botanical gardens specializing in South American flora.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Francoa' is the genus named after Spanish botanist Francisco Franco, and 'ramosa' means 'branched'.
