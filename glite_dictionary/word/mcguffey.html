McGuffey

1. PROPN

/ məˈɡʌfi / (AmE), / məˈɡʌfi / (BrE)
William Holmes McGuffey (1800–1873), an American educator known for compiling the McGuffey Eclectic Readers, influential 19th-century school textbooks.
U.S. educator; compiler of McGuffey Readers
american american

Examples

McGuffey transformed American education with his widely used Eclectic Readers in the nineteenth century.
Many early schools relied on the textbooks compiled by McGuffey to teach reading and moral lessons.
The legacy of McGuffey is still evident in educational philosophy today.
