2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˈeɪ / (BrE)
A purine base found in DNA and RNA; it pairs with thymine in DNA and uracil in RNA.
purine base in DNA and RNA
medical

Examples

The base A pairs with thymine in the DNA double helix.
During transcription, A is replaced by uracil in the mRNA sequence.
A mutation replaced A with a different nucleotide at that position.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adenine - the full name for the purine base 'A' in DNA/RNA

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'adenine,' from Greek 'aden' (gland).

blood group with A antigen
medical

Examples

5. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˈeɪ / (BrE)
A nucleotide in DNA, also called deoxyadenylate or dAMP, which consists of a phosphate group, deoxyribose sugar, and the base adenine.
nucleotide in DNA (deoxyadenylate)
medical

Examples

In DNA, A always pairs with thymine (T) through two hydrogen bonds.
The sequence included several A nucleotides near the promoter region.
Mutations occasionally result from an incorrect A being incorporated during DNA replication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adenine - a purine base found in DNA and RNA
  • Degree Lower: T - thymine, a nucleotide in DNA
  • Degree Lower: G - guanine, a nucleotide in DNA
  • Degree Lower: C - cytosine, a nucleotide in DNA

Etymology

From the chemical abbreviation for 'adenine', the purine base contained in the nucleotide.
