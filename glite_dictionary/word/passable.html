passable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpæsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsəbəl / (BrE)
(of a road or route) able to be passed or traversed; not blocked or impassable.
able to be traversed or crossed

Examples

After the storm, only one lane of the highway was passable for traffic.
The hiking trail is usually passable in summer but can be blocked by snow in winter.
Due to heavy flooding, none of the roads were passable last night.

Synset Examples

the road is passable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impassable - not able to be passed or traversed

Etymology

From Old French passer (to pass) + -able, from Latin passabilis.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpæsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsəbəl / (BrE)
Good enough to be accepted or considered satisfactory, though not exceptional; adequate.
adequate; acceptable

Examples

The meal was passable, but nothing to write home about.
Her report was passable, though it lacked depth and detail.
The hotel offers passable service for the price you pay.

Synset Examples

more than adequate as a secretary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adequate - satisfactory or acceptable in quality or quantity
  • Synonym: acceptable - good enough to be accepted
  • Antonym: excellent - extremely good; outstanding

Etymology

From Old French 'passable', from passer (to pass), influenced by Latin 'passabilis'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02088076-s
Synset POS: s
