snarky

1. ADJ

/ ˈsnɑːrki / (AmE), / ˈsnɑːki / (BrE)
Disdainfully or ironically humorous in a way that is scornful or mocking; sarcastically critical.
sarcastically critical or mocking

Examples

Her snarky comments made it clear she wasn't impressed with the presentation.
He sent a snarky reply to the manager’s email, which got him in trouble.
The comedian's snarky humor divided the audience, with some finding it hilarious and others offended.

Synset Examples

his rebellion is the bitter, sardonic laughter of all great satirists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcastic - using irony to mock or convey contempt
  • Synonym: sardonic - grimly mocking or cynical

Etymology

Probably from 'snark' (to nag or find fault) + '-y'.
