unicycle

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuː.nɪˌsaɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪˌsaɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a vehicle with a single wheel that is propelled by pedals, typically ridden by balancing on top
one-wheeled pedal vehicle

Examples

She can ride a unicycle across the stage without losing her balance.
Learning to ride a unicycle takes a lot of practice and patience.
The street performer amazed everyone by juggling while balancing on a unicycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bicycle - a vehicle with two wheels that is powered by pedals
  • Synonym: tricycle - a vehicle with three wheels that is powered by pedals

Etymology

Formed from 'uni-' (one) and 'cycle' (wheel); patterned after 'bicycle' and 'tricycle'.

2. VERB

/ ˈjuː.nɪˌsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪ.saɪ.kəl / (BrE)
to ride a unicycle, a vehicle with a single wheel, typically for sport, entertainment, or exercise
ride a one-wheeled vehicle

Examples

She learned to unicycle in less than a week.
Do you know how to unicycle along that narrow path?
The performer will unicycle across the stage during the show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycle - ride a bicycle

Etymology

Formed from 'uni-' (one) and 'cycle', modeled after 'bicycle'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01939705-v
Synset POS: v
