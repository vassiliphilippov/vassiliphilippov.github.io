crispiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪsp.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkrɪsp.i.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being firm but easily broken, often used to describe foods that are pleasantly brittle or crunchy.
the state of being crispy or crunchy

Examples

The crispiness of the fried chicken made it especially delicious.
He admired the crispiness of the autumn leaves underfoot.
The chef tries to achieve just the right crispiness in the pizza crust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crisp - firm, dry, and easily broken
  • Antonym: sogginess - the quality of being wet and soft

Etymology

From 'crispy' + '-ness', where 'crispy' is from 'crisp' (origin: Latin 'crispus', meaning curled or wrinkled).
