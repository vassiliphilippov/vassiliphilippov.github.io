fiercely

1. ADV

/ ˈfɪrsli / (AmE), / ˈfɪəsli / (BrE)
In a strong, intense, or aggressive way; with great force or violence.
with intense or violent force

Examples

The wolves fiercely defended their territory from intruders.
She glared fiercely at anyone who dared disturb her concentration.
The debate became fiercely competitive as both sides refused to back down.

Synset Examples

silence broken by dogs barking ferociously
they fought fiercely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violently - with great force or intensity
  • Antonym: gently - in a mild or soft manner

Etymology

From 'fierce' + '-ly'; 'fierce' from Old French 'fers' (fierce, wild) and Latin 'ferus' (wild, untamed).

2. ADV

/ ˈfɪr.sli / (AmE), / ˈfɪəs.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows great intensity, anger, determination, or emotion.
in a very intense or passionate way

Examples

She was fiercely proud of her children and never hesitated to praise them.
The wind blew fiercely throughout the night, rattling the windows.
He fiercely defended his beliefs during the heated debate.

Synset Examples

she was fiercely proud of her children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intensely - with great intensity
  • Antonym: gently - in a mild or tender way

Etymology

From 'fierce' + '-ly'; 'fierce' from Old French *fiers*, from Latin *ferus* (wild).
