malacopterygian

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæləkɒpˈtɛrɪdʒiən / (AmE), / ˌmaləkɒpˈtɛrɪdʒɪən / (BrE)
any fish belonging to the superorder Malacopterygii, characterized by having soft, ray-like fins.
fish of the superorder Malacopterygii

Examples

The malacopterygian is distinguished by its soft fin rays and is common in many freshwater habitats.
Scientists discovered a new species of malacopterygian in the Amazon River basin.
Unlike the spiny-finned fish, a malacopterygian has flexible, segmented fins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teleost - a member of the largest group of bony fishes

Etymology

From New Latin Malacopterygii, from Greek malakos (soft) + pteryx (fin, wing).
