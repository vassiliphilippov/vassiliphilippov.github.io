substructure

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌstrʌktʃər / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
the supporting part of a building or structure that is below ground level, such as foundations or the lowest support
lowest supporting part of a structure

Examples

The engineers examined the substructure before starting work on the upper floors.
Damage to the substructure caused the entire building to settle unevenly.
Proper waterproofing is essential to protect the substructure from moisture.

Synset Examples

it was built on a base of solid rock
he stood at the foot of the tower

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superstructure - the part of a building or structure above its foundations or lowest supporting part

Etymology

From Latin 'substructura', from 'sub-' (under) + 'structura' (a building, structure).

2. NOUN

/ sʌbˌstrʌktʃɚ / (AmE), / sʌbˈstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
the underlying or supporting structure that forms the foundation of a system, organization, or physical construction
underlying framework or foundation

Examples

Financial transparency is an essential part of the substructure of any democratic institution.
The engineers examined the substructure before beginning work on the upper levels of the bridge.
A solid educational substructure enables students to tackle more advanced subjects later on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infrastructure - the basic physical and organizational structures needed for operation
  • Antonym: superstructure - the part of a building above its foundation or the framework built on top of a base

Etymology

From Latin 'substructura', from 'sub-' (under) + 'structura' (structure, building).
