apothegmatic

1. ADJ

/ əˌpɑθɪɡˈmætɪk / (AmE), / əˌpɒθɪɡˈmatɪk / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) terse and witty like a maxim; expressed in concise and memorable form.
terse and witty like a maxim

Examples

Her apothegmatic remarks often left the audience both amused and thoughtful.
The book is filled with apothegmatic statements that distill complex truths into a few words.
He has an apothegmatic style, preferring brief but memorable expressions over lengthy explanations.

Synset Examples

much given to apothegmatic instruction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphoristic - terse and pithy, like an aphorism

Etymology

From Greek 'apophthegmatikos', from 'apophthegma' (terse saying).

2. ADJ

/ əˌpɑːθɪɡˈmætɪk / (AmE), / əˌpɒθɪɡˈmatɪk / (BrE)
expressed in or characterized by concise and witty sayings or apothegms
terse or pithy in style

Examples

Her apothegmatic style made every sentence memorable and to the point.
The professor was known for his apothegmatic remarks during lectures.
Writers admired his apothegmatic observations on life and society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphoristic - expressed in aphorisms; terse and pithy

Etymology

From Greek apophthegmatikos, from apophthegma (apothegm, pithy saying).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02645872-a
Synset POS: a
