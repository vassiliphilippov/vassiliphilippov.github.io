Provençal

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌvɑːnˈsɑl / (AmE), / prɒˈvɒ̃səl / (BrE)
the dialects of the Occitan language spoken in Provence and other parts of southern France, especially in the medieval period
medieval dialect of southern France

Examples

Scholars study medieval poetry written in Provençal to understand culture in southern France.
The troubadours composed many of their songs in Provençal.
She specializes in the Provençal dialects that were spoken during the twelfth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Occitan - the Romance language group spoken in southern France, including Provençal

Etymology

From French 'provençal', from 'Provence', the region in southern France.
