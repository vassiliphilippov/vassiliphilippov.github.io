carouse

1. VERB

/ kəˈraʊz / (AmE), / kəˈraʊz / (BrE)
to drink and enjoy oneself noisily, especially in a group and typically with much alcohol
drink and celebrate boisterously, often with alcohol

Examples

They love to carouse at parties until the early morning hours.
After the victory, the team went out to carouse with their supporters.
If you spend every weekend carousing, you'll eventually need a break.

Synset Examples

They were out carousing last night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to celebrate - to observe or honor with festivities
  • Synonym: revel - to take part in lively and noisy festivities, often involving drinking

Etymology

From Middle French 'carouser', from German 'gar aus' ('completely out', a toast to empty the cup).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈraʊz / (AmE), / kəˈraʊz / (BrE)
an occasion of lively, noisy drinking and revelry; a merry drinking party
lively drinking party
archaic

Examples

The old tavern echoed with laughter during last night's carouse.
After the victory, the villagers held a merry carouse in the town square.
Their carouse lasted until dawn, with songs, dancing, and endless toasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacchanal - an occasion of wild and drunken revelry
  • Degree Lower: soiree - an evening party or gathering

Etymology

From French 'carousser' (to drink heavily), from German 'gar aus' (drink up entirely).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00512052-n
Synset POS: n
