ARDS

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ɑːr ˌdiː ˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ɑː ˌdiː ˈɛs / (BrE)
A severe condition in which the lungs become inflamed and unable to provide enough oxygen to the body's organs, often as a result of injury or infection.
acute respiratory distress syndrome; severe lung inflammation
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with ARDS after being exposed to inhaled toxins at the factory.
Intensive care is required to treat severe cases of ARDS caused by pneumonia.
Rapid intervention can improve the prognosis for people developing ARDS following trauma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acute respiratory distress syndrome - full form of ARDS, severe lung inflammation

Etymology

Acronym of Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome.
