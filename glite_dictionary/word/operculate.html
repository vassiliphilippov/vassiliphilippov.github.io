operculate

1. ADJ

/ oʊˈpɜːrkjʊlət / (AmE), / əʊˈpɜːkjʊlət / (BrE)
(of a plant or animal) having an operculum; possessing a lid or covering structure.
having an operculum (lid or covering)
medical

Examples

Many gastropods are described as operculate because they have a hard plate that closes the opening of their shells.
Certain mosses are operculate, releasing spores through a lid-like structure.
The operculate condition helps protect some aquatic snails from predators and desiccation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inoperculate - lacking an operculum (without a covering lid)
  • Synonym: operculum - a lid or covering structure found in plants and animals

Etymology

From Latin operculatus, from operculum meaning ‘cover’ or ‘lid’.
