starting

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɑrtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtɪŋ / (BrE)
Relating to or suitable for the beginning or start of an event, activity, or process.
relating to a beginning or start

Examples

She took her place in the starting position before the race began.
The starting point for the tour is the main entrance.
Check your starting time on the schedule before arriving.

Synset Examples

the starting point
hands in the starting position

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initial - existing or occurring at the beginning; first
  • Antonym: ending - relating to or occurring at the conclusion of something

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'start', from Middle English 'starten'.

/ ˈstɑr.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɑː.tɪŋ / (BrE)
(of eyes) bulging or protruding, especially as a result of fear or surprise.
bulging, protruding (of eyes, with fear)
archaic

Examples

She gazed at the ghost, her starting eyes wide with terror.
He stared at the scene with starting eyes, unable to move.
The child's starting eyes revealed her shock at the unexpected noise.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protruding - sticking out; projecting
