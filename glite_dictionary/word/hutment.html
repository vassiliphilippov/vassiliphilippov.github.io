hutment

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌtmənt / (AmE), / ˈhʌtmənt / (BrE)
A group or encampment of huts, especially for soldiers or laborers.
a group of huts, often military

Examples

The soldiers stayed in a temporary hutment near the base during training.
During the war, dozens of hutments were built to accommodate the influx of troops.
The workers lived in a crowded hutment on the outskirts of the construction site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barracks - a building or group of buildings used to house soldiers

Etymology

From 'hut' + '-ment', modelled after similar formations like 'tenement'.
