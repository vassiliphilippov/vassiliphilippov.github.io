Lychnis flos-cuculi

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪk.nɪs floʊs kuˈkuːliː / (AmE), / ˈlɪk.nɪs flɒs kjuːˈkjuːlaɪ / (BrE)
A common perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia, known for its usually pink flowers with ragged petals.
European pink-flowered perennial plant

Examples

The meadow was dotted with bright pink Lychnis flos-cuculi swaying in the breeze.
Gardeners often plant Lychnis flos-cuculi to attract butterflies and bees.
During late spring, the delicate Lychnis flos-cuculi adds a splash of color to wetlands and damp fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ragged robin - Common name for Lychnis flos-cuculi

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lychnis', an ancient plant name, and 'flos-cuculi', meaning 'cuckoo flower'.
