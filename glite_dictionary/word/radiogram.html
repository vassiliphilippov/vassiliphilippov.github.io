radiogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəˌɡræm / (BrE)
a photographic image produced on a radiosensitive surface by radiation other than visible light, especially by X-rays or gamma rays
image produced by X-rays or gamma rays
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the radiogram to check for any fractures in the patient's arm.
A radiogram of her lungs showed signs of pneumonia.
The dentist requested a radiogram to assess the condition of the teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiograph - an image produced by X-rays or similar radiation; synonym for radiogram
  • Synonym: X-ray - an image produced using X-rays (also the process)

Etymology

From radio- (radiation) + -gram (Greek grámma, 'something written or drawn').

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊɡram / (BrE)
a message sent by radio, especially one transmitted by wireless telegraphy
message sent by radio telegraphy

Examples

The ship received a radiogram with urgent instructions from the mainland.
During the war, families often waited anxiously for a radiogram from soldiers overseas.
He sent a radiogram to announce his safe arrival in New York.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telegram - a message sent by telegraph

Etymology

From radio- (radio) + -gram (something written or recorded); modeled after 'telegram'.
