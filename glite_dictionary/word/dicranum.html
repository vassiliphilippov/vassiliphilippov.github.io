Dicranum

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈkreɪnəm / (AmE), / dɪˈkrɑːnəm / (BrE)
A genus of mosses in the family Dicranaceae, commonly known as fork mosses, found in many temperate regions.
genus of mosses (Dicranaceae)

Examples

The forest floor was covered with patches of Dicranum thriving in the shade.
Researchers identified three species of Dicranum in the mountainous region.
The distinctive forked leaves help botanists distinguish Dicranum from similar moss genera.

Etymology

From Greek dikranon, meaning 'two-headed' or 'forked', referring to the forked appearance of the leaves.
