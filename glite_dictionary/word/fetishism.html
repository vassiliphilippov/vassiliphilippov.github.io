fetishism

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛtɪˌʃɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfɛtɪˌʃɪzəm / (BrE)
sexual arousal or gratification achieved by focusing on a fetish, typically an object or a specific body part that is not a sexual organ.
sexual attraction to objects or nonsexual body parts

Examples

Some people engage in fetishism involving shoes or leather objects.
He discussed his experiences with fetishism during a therapy session.
The psychologist explained that fetishism is one of many forms of sexual expression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paraphilia - atypical sexual interests, including fetishism

Etymology

From French fétichisme, from fétiche ('fetish').

belief in magical power of objects

He wrote an essay exploring the role of fetishism in early religious practices.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɛt̬.ɪˌʃɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈfet.ɪ.ʃɪ.zəm / (BrE)
Sexual arousal or gratification derived from handling or fantasizing about an object or a specific body part that is not typically considered sexual.
sexual arousal from a fetish object or body part

Examples

His interest in shoes bordered on fetishism, as he found them deeply arousing.
The psychologist discussed the psychological roots of fetishism in her lecture.
Instances of fetishism involving objects like leather or latex are often portrayed in popular media.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paraphilia - sexual interest or behavior outside cultural norms

Etymology

From French 'fétichisme', from 'fétiche' (fetish), ultimately from Portuguese 'feitiço' (charm, sorcery).
