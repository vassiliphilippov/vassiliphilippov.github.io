diarchy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪˌɑrki / (AmE), / daɪˈɑːki / (BrE)
a system of government in which power is shared by two joint rulers or authorities
government by two joint rulers

Examples

The kingdom established a diarchy to maintain balance between its rival factions.
In a diarchy, decision-making is often more complex than in a monarchy.
Ancient Sparta is sometimes cited as an example of a diarchy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monarchy - government by a single ruler, usually a king or queen
  • Synonym: duumvirate - a political office or government ruled by two people

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) + 'arkhē' (rule, government).
