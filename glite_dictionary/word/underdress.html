to underdress

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈdrɛs / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈdrɛs / (BrE)
to dress in a way that is less formal or less elaborate than is suitable for a particular occasion
wear less formal clothing than expected

Examples

It is easy to underdress for a formal dinner if you are unsure of the dress code.
Some students tend to underdress during warm weather, even for special assemblies.
He realized he had underdressed when everyone else arrived in suits and dresses.

Synset Examples

On Fridays, employees can underdress

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to overdress - to dress in a way that is more formal than necessary
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00045077-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌʌndɚˈdrɛs / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈdrɛs / (BrE)
to dress in clothing that does not provide enough warmth or protection against the weather
dress in insufficiently warm clothing

Examples

If you underdress for winter conditions, you might end up shivering all day.
He learned the hard way not to underdress during mountain expeditions.
Children often underdress in spring and catch colds when the weather suddenly turns chilly.

Synset Examples

She was underdressed for the hiking trip and suffered hypothermia

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to overdress - to dress in more clothing than necessary for warmth or formality
