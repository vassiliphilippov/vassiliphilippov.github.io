tranquillity

1. NOUN

/ træŋˈkwɪləti / (AmE), / træŋˈkwɪlɪti / (BrE)
A state of calmness and peace, free from stress or disturbance.
state of calmness or peace

Examples

She found tranquillity in the peaceful garden after a busy day.
Meditation helps many people achieve a sense of tranquillity.
The lake was a place of perfect tranquillity at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serenity - calmness and peacefulness
  • Antonym: agitation - a state of anxiety or nervous excitement

Etymology

From Latin tranquillitas, from tranquillus (calm, still).

2. NOUN

/ træŋˈkwɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / træŋˈkwɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
A state of calmness and peace, especially when free from disturbance or agitation.
state of calm or peace

Examples

The tranquillity of the lake was broken only by the sound of birds.
She sought tranquillity in the peaceful countryside after years of city life.
Meditation helps him achieve a sense of tranquillity amidst daily stress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calmness - the quality of being calm
  • Synonym: serenity - the state of being serene, calm, or tranquil
  • Antonym: turmoil - a state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty

Etymology

From Latin tranquillitas (calmness, stillness), from tranquillus (quiet, calm).

3. NOUN

state of calm and peace

Examples

After moving to the countryside, she finally found the tranquillity she had been seeking.
The garden was designed to promote a sense of tranquillity and relaxation.
He sat by the lake, enjoying the tranquillity of the quiet afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serenity - the state of being serene, calm, and untroubled
  • Antonym: agitation - a state of nervousness or unrest

Etymology

From Latin tranquillitas (calmness), from tranquillus (quiet, calm).
