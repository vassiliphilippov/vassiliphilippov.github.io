commencement

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɛnsmənt / (AmE), / kəˈmɛnsmənt / (BrE)
the beginning or start of something
the beginning or start

Examples

The commencement of the meeting was delayed due to technical problems.
Every journey has its commencement, no matter how long it may be.
The commencement of the new law brought significant changes to the industry.

Synset Examples

he was responsible for the beginning of negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - the act of beginning something
  • Antonym: end - the final part of something
  • Synonym: inception - the establishment or starting point of something

Etymology

From Old French 'comencement', from 'comencier' (to begin), from Latin 'cominitiare'.

3. NOUN

/ kəˈmɛnsmənt / (AmE), / kəˈmɛnsmənt / (BrE)
a ceremony at which academic degrees or diplomas are formally conferred upon graduates
graduation ceremony

Examples

The university's commencement will be held outdoors, with thousands of students receiving their degrees.
Family members traveled from all over the country to attend her commencement ceremony.
He delivered the keynote address at last year's commencement at Harvard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graduation - the act of receiving a degree or diploma at a ceremony

Etymology

From Old French *comencement*, from Latin *cominitiare* (to begin).
