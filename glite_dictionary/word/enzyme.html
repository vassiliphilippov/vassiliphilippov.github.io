enzyme

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnˌzaɪm / (AmE), / ˈɛn.zɑɪm / (BrE)
A complex protein produced by living cells that acts as a catalyst to speed up specific biochemical reactions.
protein catalyst in biochemical reactions
medical

Examples

Digestive enzymes help break down food into nutrients the body can absorb.
Each enzyme in the body is tailored to catalyze a specific chemical reaction.
Scientists studied how high temperatures affect the activity of this enzyme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalyst - a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself undergoing any permanent chemical change

Etymology

From Greek 'enzymos' (leavened), from 'en-' (in) + 'zyme' (leaven).
