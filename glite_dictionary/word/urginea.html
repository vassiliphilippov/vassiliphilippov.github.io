Urginea

1. NOUN

/ ərˈdʒɪniə / (AmE), / ɜːˈdʒɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of Mediterranean plants in the lily family, sometimes placed in the Hyacinthaceae family.
genus of Mediterranean lily-like plants

Examples

Several species of Urginea have been used for medicinal purposes since ancient times.
The botanical garden features a rare collection of Urginea plants in its Mediterranean section.
Researchers are studying the chemical compounds found in Urginea to develop new treatments.

Etymology

From New Latin; named after the ancient Greek city Urginia (modern-day El Arish).
