plectranthus

1. NOUN

/ plɛkˈtrænθəs / (AmE), / plɛkˈtranθəs / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Plectranthus, typically grown as an ornamental, often with aromatic foliage and attractive flowers.
ornamental plant of the genus Plectranthus

Examples

The plectranthus in her garden bloomed with clusters of pale purple flowers.
Gardeners often choose plectranthus for its attractive, aromatic leaves and low maintenance needs.
This variety of plectranthus is especially popular as a houseplant due to its hardiness.

Etymology

From Greek 'plektron' (something to strike with) and 'anthos' (flower); modern Latin botanical name.
