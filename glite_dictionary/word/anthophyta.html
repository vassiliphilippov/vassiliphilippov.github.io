Anthophyta

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθɑfɪtə / (AmE), / ænˈθɒfɪtə / (BrE)
A division or major group in botany comprising all flowering plants whose seeds develop within an ovary.
division of flowering plants

Examples

Scientists classify roses, daisies, and sunflowers as members of the Anthophyta.
The Anthophyta display a remarkable diversity in flower structure and form.
Evolutionary biologists study how Anthophyta came to dominate many terrestrial ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Angiosperm - a plant with seeds enclosed in an ovary (flowering plant)
  • Antonym: Gymnosperm - a plant with seeds not enclosed in an ovary (non-flowering plant)

Etymology

From Greek 'anthos' (flower) + 'phyton' (plant).
