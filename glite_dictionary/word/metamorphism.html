metamorphism

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˈmɔːrˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈmɔːfɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The process by which the structure or composition of rocks is changed by natural agencies such as heat, pressure, or the introduction of new chemical substances.
geological process altering rocks

Examples

Regional metamorphism often occurs when large tectonic plates collide and create intense pressure.
The scientist explained how heat and pressure contribute to metamorphism in the Earth's crust.
Contact metamorphism happens when rocks are altered due to proximity to hot magma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metamorphosis - change of form or structure

Etymology

From Greek metamorphōsis (transformation), from meta- (change) + morphē (form).
