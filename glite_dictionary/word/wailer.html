wailer

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈweɪlə / (BrE)
a person who emits long, loud, high-pitched cries, especially as a sign of mourning or grief
a person who cries loudly, especially in grief

Examples

The wailer could be heard from across the street as the funeral procession passed by.
Professional wailers were sometimes hired to mourn at traditional ceremonies.
Amidst the silence, a single wailer broke out in uncontrollable grief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mourner - a person who is at a funeral, often expressing grief
  • Synonym: wail - to utter a long, loud, high-pitched cry of sadness or pain

Etymology

From 'wail' + '-er', a suffix forming agent nouns.
