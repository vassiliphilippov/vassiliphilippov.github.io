dodecahedron

1. NOUN

/ ˌdoʊdəkəˈhiːdrən / (AmE), / ˌdəʊdɪkəˈhiːdrən / (BrE)
A solid figure with twelve flat faces, most commonly with regular pentagonal faces in geometry.
twelve-faced solid figure

Examples

The crystal took the form of a perfect dodecahedron, dazzling in the sunlight.
In geometry class, we learned how to calculate the volume of a dodecahedron.
The game piece was shaped like a dodecahedron with numbers on each face.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cube - polyhedron with six square faces
  • Degree Higher: icosahedron - polyhedron with twenty triangular faces

Etymology

From Greek dōdeka (twelve) + hédra (seat, base, face).
