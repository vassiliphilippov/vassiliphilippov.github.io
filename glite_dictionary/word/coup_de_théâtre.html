coup de théâtre

1. NOUN

/ kuː də teɪˈɑːtɹə / (AmE), / kuː də teɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A sudden, dramatic, and unexpected event or turn in a play or situation that creates a strong impression on the audience.
sudden dramatic turn in a play or event

Examples

The playwright introduced a coup de théâtre in the final act that left the audience speechless.
Her resignation was a political coup de théâtre nobody had anticipated.
The film’s unexpected ending served as a classic coup de théâtre that redefined the characters’ relationships.

Etymology

From French 'coup de théâtre' meaning 'a stroke of theatre'.

2. NOUN

/ kuː də teɪˈɑːtrə / (AmE), / kuː də teɪˈɑːtrə / (BrE)
a sudden and dramatic turn of events, especially in a play or narrative, that surprises the audience or characters
sudden dramatic event or twist

Examples

The play's ending delivered a stunning coup de théâtre that left the audience speechless.
A well-timed coup de théâtre can transform a predictable plot into a memorable experience.
Critics praised the director for orchestrating a powerful coup de théâtre in the final act.

Etymology

From French, literally 'stroke of theatre' or 'dramatic blow'.
