Reb

1. NOUN

/ rɛb / (AmE), / rɛb / (BrE)
(in the American Civil War) a term used by Union soldiers to refer to a Confederate soldier; short for 'rebel'.
Confederate soldier (Civil War, US, historical)
american archaic

Examples

During the battle, a Reb shouted across the field to his Union counterpart.
Many letters from the era mention the bravery of the Rebs in combat.
Historians still debate the daily life of a typical Reb during the Civil War.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Yank - Union soldier in the American Civil War
  • Synonym: rebel - One who resists authority or control; origin of 'Reb'

Etymology

Shortened from 'rebel', used especially by Union soldiers during the American Civil War.
