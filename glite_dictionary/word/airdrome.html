airdrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈeə.drəʊm / (BrE)
An airfield equipped with a control tower, hangars, and facilities for passengers and cargo.
airfield with facilities

Examples

The city recently upgraded its airdrome to handle international flights and larger passenger aircraft.
During the war, the airdrome was a key strategic location for air operations.
All cargo shipments must be inspected upon arrival at the airdrome before distribution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airport - a place where aircraft can take off and land, including runways and buildings

Etymology

From French 'aérodrome', from Greek 'aēr' (air) + 'dromos' (course, racecourse).
