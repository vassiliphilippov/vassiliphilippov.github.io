unobligated

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɑblɪˌɡeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɒblɪˌɡeɪtɪd / (BrE)
Not required or bound by obligation; free from duty or commitment.
not obligated; free from obligation

Examples

These funds remain unobligated and may be used for future projects.
She felt unobligated to attend the event, as there was no formal invitation.
The company had several unobligated employees available for urgent tasks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obligated - being bound or required to do something; under obligation
  • Synonym: required - necessary according to rules or regulations

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'obligated', from Latin 'obligare' (to bind).
