innovation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of introducing new ideas, devices, or methods
introduction of something new

Examples

The company's commitment to innovation led to the development of groundbreaking products.
Educational innovation has dramatically improved classroom learning in recent years.
She was recognized for her innovation in streamlining the production process.

Synset Examples

she looked forward to her initiation as an adult
the foundation of a new scientific society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invention - the creation of something that has never existed before
  • Antonym: tradition - a long-established custom or belief passed down through generations

Etymology

From Latin innovatio, from innovare (to renew, change).
