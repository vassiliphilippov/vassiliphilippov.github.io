hotbed

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑtˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈhɒtˌbɛd / (BrE)
A bed of earth, covered with glass and heated by fermenting manure, used to encourage the growth of plants.
glass-covered, heated plant bed

Examples

The seedlings thrived in the hotbed during the early spring months.
Gardeners built a hotbed to start their vegetables before transplanting them outdoors.
Using a hotbed can significantly advance the growing season for many plants.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑtˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈhɒtbɛd / (BrE)
a place or environment where something, especially something undesirable, develops quickly and strongly
place where rapid development occurs (often negative)

Examples

The internet quickly became a hotbed for rumors and conspiracy theories.
That nightclub was once a hotbed of criminal activity.
This area is considered a hotbed of innovation in the tech industry.

Synset Examples

it was a hotbed of vice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breeding ground - a place where something, especially something bad, develops or is fostered

Etymology

Originally referred to a bed of earth heated for forcing plants to grow; now used figuratively.
