Anglican

1. ADJ

/ ˈæŋɡlɪkən / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡlɪkən / (BrE)
Relating to the Anglican Church, its members, or its traditions.
of the Anglican Church

Examples

The Anglican tradition traces its roots back to the Reformation in England.
She attended an Anglican service every Sunday with her family.
The Anglican Church is known for its distinctive liturgy and history.

Synset Examples

an Anglican bishop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Episcopalian - Relating to the Episcopal Church, especially in the US

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Anglicanus', meaning 'English'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02966005-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡlɪkən / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡlɪkən / (BrE)
a member of the Church of England or of a church that is in communion with it, adhering to its doctrine and practices
follower of the Anglican Church

Examples

She was raised as an Anglican and attends services every Sunday.
Many Anglicans around the world celebrate traditional holidays in similar ways.
As an Anglican, he values both scripture and tradition in his faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Protestant - a member of any of the Western Christian churches separate from the Roman Catholic Church, following the principles of the Reformation

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Anglicanus, meaning 'English'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09977771-n
Synset POS: n
