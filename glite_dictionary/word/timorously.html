timorously

1. ADV

/ ˈtɪmərəsli / (AmE), / ˈtɪmərəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows fear or a lack of confidence; timidly or fearfully.
in a timid or fearful manner

Examples

She spoke timorously, afraid of how her idea would be received by the group.
The child timorously peeked around the door, unsure if he was allowed to enter.
He approached the unfamiliar dog timorously, keeping his distance at first.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: boldly - in a brave or confident manner
  • Synonym: timidly - in a fearful or shy manner

Etymology

From Latin 'timor' (fear) via 'timorous' + '-ly'.
