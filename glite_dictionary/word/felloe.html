felloe

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɛləʊ / (BrE)
the outer rim or a segment of the rim of a wheel, into which the ends of spokes are fitted
outer rim of a wheel for spokes

Examples

The carpenter carefully attached each felloe to form the wheel's rim.
Period craftsmen shaped the felloes before inserting the spokes.
Replacing a damaged felloe restored the strength of the wooden cart wheel.

Etymology

From Old French 'feloe', perhaps of Germanic origin; related to Old English 'felg(e)', 'felga' (rim of a wheel).
