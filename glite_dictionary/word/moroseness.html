moroseness

1. NOUN

/ məˈroʊsnəs / (AmE), / məˈrəʊsnəs / (BrE)
a sullen, gloomy, or resentful mood or disposition
sullen and gloomy disposition

Examples

Her moroseness made it difficult for others to approach her at the party.
He tried to shake off his moroseness and join the conversation with a smile.
The film's cinematic style reflects the moroseness of its main character.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the quality of being noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Synonym: sullenness - a gloomy or sulky mood

Etymology

From Latin morosus (peevish, fretful) via French morose.

2. NOUN

/ məˈroʊsnɪs / (AmE), / məˈrəʊsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being sullen, gloomy, or ill-tempered
sullen or gloomy temperament

Examples

His moroseness made it difficult for him to enjoy social gatherings.
There was a sense of moroseness in the room after the disappointing news.
She tried to shake off her moroseness and focus on something positive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melancholy - a deep, persistent sadness or gloomy mood
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the quality of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Latin morosus (peevish, fretful), from mos (manner, habit).
