Lecythidaceae

1. NOUN

/ lɛsɪθɪˈdeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / lɛsɪθɪˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of large tropical trees bearing large fruits with woody skins, including the Brazil nut and cannonball tree
family of tropical trees with large woody fruits

Examples

The Lecythidaceae include notable trees such as the Brazil nut and the cannonball tree.
Botanists study the Lecythidaceae for their unique fruit and ecological importance in tropical forests.
Fruits from species in the Lecythidaceae family have tough, woody shells that protect the seeds.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Lecythis.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɛsɪθɪˈdeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌlɛsɪθɪˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of large tropical trees that bear large fruits with woody skins, including the Brazil nut and related species
a family of tropical trees bearing woody fruits

Examples

Some of the world's largest fruits are produced by trees in the Lecythidaceae family.
Conservationists are studying Lecythidaceae species for their ecological importance in tropical rainforests.
The Brazil nut, perhaps the most famous member of the Lecythidaceae, is an important economic resource.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Lecythis + family suffix -aceae.
