polemist

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈlɛmɪst / (AmE), / pəˈlɛmɪst / (BrE)
a person who engages in controversial debate or argues strongly against others, especially in writing on theological or philosophical topics
one who engages in debate or controversy

Examples

The famous polemist published a scathing critique of his rival's interpretation.
Throughout history, religious debates have often been led by a determined polemist.
Her reputation as a skilled polemist made her articles both admired and controversial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polemical - relating to or involving strongly critical or controversial writing or speech
  • Antonym: apologist - a person who argues in defense of something, often opposite role to a polemist

Etymology

From Greek *polemistēs* (warrior, fighter), from *polemos* (war).
