Lonicera japonica

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈnɪsərə dʒəˈpoʊnɪkə / (AmE), / lɒˈnɪsərə dʒəˈpɒnɪkə / (BrE)
An Asiatic climbing or trailing honeysuckle with semi-evergreen leaves and sweetly scented white flowers that turn yellow with age, often considered invasive.
Asiatic honeysuckle with fragrant, white-yellow flowers

Examples

Lonicera japonica spreads easily in woodland areas, sometimes outcompeting native plants.
Gardeners are warned that Lonicera japonica can become invasive if not controlled properly.
The fragrant flowers of Lonicera japonica attract bees and butterflies to the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - Climbing shrub with sweetly scented flowers; the common name for plants of the genus Lonicera

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining Lonicera (the genus name, after botanist Adam Lonicer) and japonica (meaning 'of Japan').
