John Keats

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈkits / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈkiːts / (BrE)
(1795–1821) An English Romantic poet known for his vivid imagery and sensuous style, and for works such as 'Ode to a Nightingale'.
English Romantic poet (1795–1821)

Examples

Many students study the poetry of John Keats in literature classes.
The works of John Keats are celebrated for their beauty and emotional depth.
A portrait of John Keats hangs in the museum's Romantic poetry section.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Percy Bysshe Shelley - English Romantic poet (1792–1822) and contemporary of Keats
  • Synonym: Lord Byron - English poet (1788–1824), leading figure in the Romantic movement
