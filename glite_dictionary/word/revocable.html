revocable

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛvəkəbəl / (AmE), / ˈriːvəkəbl / (BrE)
capable of being withdrawn, cancelled, or annulled; able to be revoked.
able to be cancelled or withdrawn

Examples

The contract was signed under revocable terms, allowing either party to withdraw with notice.
They issued a revocable permit for the new construction project.
A revocable trust can be changed or cancelled by its creator at any time.

Synset Examples

a revocable order

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irrevocable - impossible to revoke or change

Etymology

From Latin revocabilis, from revocare (to recall, to revoke).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈvoʊkəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈvəʊkəbəl / (BrE)
Able to be revoked, canceled, or annulled; not permanent or irreversible.
capable of being revoked or canceled

Examples

The contract is revocable at any time by either party with written notice.
A revocable trust allows the settlor to make changes or terminate the trust as needed.
His license was revocable if he failed to meet certain conditions.

Synset Examples

a revocable order

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irrevocable - not able to be revoked or changed

Etymology

From Latin revocabilis, from revocare (to recall, revoke).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01764644-a
Synset POS: a
