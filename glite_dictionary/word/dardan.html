Dardan

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːrdən / (AmE), / ˈdɑːdən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of ancient Troy.
native of ancient Troy

Examples

According to legend, Aeneas was a famous Dardan who survived the fall of Troy.
The poet describes the bravery of the Dardans in battle.
Ancient texts often refer to the Dardan people as skilled warriors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Trojan - native or inhabitant of Troy

Etymology

From Latin Dardanus, a mythical ancestor of the Trojans, from Greek Dardanos.
