piccalilli

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪkəˈlɪli / (AmE), / ˌpɪkəˈlɪli / (BrE)
a relish made from chopped pickled vegetables, typically cucumbers, green peppers, and onions, often mixed with mustard and spices
a type of pickled vegetable relish

Examples

She served sandwiches with a generous helping of piccalilli on the side.
A jar of homemade piccalilli makes a great gift during the holidays.
The tangy flavor of piccalilli complements cold meats perfectly.

Etymology

Originated in the 18th century, possibly an alteration of 'pickle' and 'lily', first attested in English.
