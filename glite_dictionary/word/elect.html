elect

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɛkt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛkt / (BrE)
(after a title) having been chosen or elected to a position but not yet installed or officially in office.
elected, but not yet in office

Examples

He was introduced as the president elect at the ceremony.
The mayor elect met with city officials to prepare for her term.
After the results, she became the chairperson elect of the organization.

Synset Examples

the president elect

Etymology

From Latin 'electus', past participle of 'eligere' (to choose).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01297980-s
Synset POS: s

2. VERB

/ ɪˈlɛkt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛkt / (BrE)
to choose or decide to do something, especially by making a deliberate or formal choice
choose or decide to do

Examples

After much consideration, she elected to pursue a career in medicine.
You can elect to receive your statements electronically or by mail.
They elected not to participate in the final round of interviews.

Synset Examples

I elected to have my funds deposited automatically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to choose - to select from a number of possibilities
  • Antonym: decline - to refuse or choose not to do

Etymology

From Latin eligere (to pick out, choose).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00678783-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛkt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛkt / (BrE)
A group of people who are chosen or regarded as superior, privileged, or set apart for a special position or purpose.
privileged or chosen group

Examples

Only a handful of the elect were invited to attend the closed meeting.
The committee is seen as the elect who shape the university's policy.
He considered himself one of the elect destined for greatness.

Synset Examples

one of the elect who have power inside the government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elite - a group regarded as the best or most powerful in society

Etymology

From Latin *electus* (chosen), past participle of *eligere* (to pick out, choose).

/ ɪˈlɛkt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛkt / (BrE)
to choose someone for a position or office by voting
choose by voting

Examples

They elected him as their leader by a unanimous vote.
