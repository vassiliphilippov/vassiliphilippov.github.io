Trogium pulsatorium

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊdʒiəm ˌpʌlˈsætɔːriəm / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊdʒiəm ˌpʌlˈsətɔːriəm / (BrE)
A species of small wingless insect commonly known as the booklouse, found in old books, stored products, and damp environments.
species of booklouse insect

Examples

The archives were infested with Trogium pulsatorium, causing damage to the delicate old manuscripts.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Trogium pulsatorium in museum collections.
Proper humidity control can help prevent outbreaks of Trogium pulsatorium in libraries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booklouse - a small, wingless insect that feeds on mold and starchy materials, often in books

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Trogium' derived from Greek 'trōgion' (little gnawer), and 'pulsatorium' from Latin 'pulsare' (to strike or beat).
