to masculinize

1. VERB

/ ˈmæskjələˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmæskjʊlɪnaɪz / (BrE)
to cause a person, animal, or part of the body to develop male characteristics, often as a result of hormones
cause to develop male characteristics
medical

Examples

Hormone therapy can masculinize the voice and body shape during gender transition.
Certain medical conditions may masculinize women by increasing levels of androgens.
Scientists studied how prenatal exposure to testosterone could masculinize an animal’s behaviour.

Synset Examples

the drugs masculinized the teenage girl

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to feminize - to cause to develop female characteristics, often via hormones

Etymology

From Latin masculinus (male, masculine) + -ize.

2. VERB

/ ˈmæskjəˌlɪnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmæskjʊlɪnaɪz / (BrE)
to give a masculine appearance, qualities, or character to someone or something
make more masculine

Examples

During puberty, certain hormones masculinize the development of boys' bodies.
In the 1990s, some fashion trends masculinized women's clothing styles.
Artists sometimes masculinize female characters to challenge traditional gender roles.

Synset Examples

Fashion designers have masculinized women's looks in the 1990s

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to feminize - to give a feminine appearance, qualities, or character to

Etymology

Formed from 'masculine' and the suffix '-ize'.
