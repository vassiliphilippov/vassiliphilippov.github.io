bobble

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːbəl / (AmE), / ˈbɒbəl / (BrE)
a brief loss of control when a fielder momentarily mishandles a batted or thrown baseball but regains it quickly
momentary mishandling in baseball

Examples

The shortstop's bobble nearly cost the team a run, but he recovered just in time.
Despite a bobble at second base, the player managed to make the out.
His first bobble of the season happened during the championship game.

Synset Examples

the second baseman made a bobble but still had time to throw the runner out

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: error - a mistake made in fielding a ball in baseball
