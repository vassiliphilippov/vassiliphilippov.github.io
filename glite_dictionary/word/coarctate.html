coarctate

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈɑrk.teɪt / (AmE), / kəʊˈɑːk.teɪt / (BrE)
(of an insect pupa) enclosed within a rigid external case formed by the hardened skin of the last larval stage.
enclosed in a rigid case (pupa)

Examples

The fly undergoes a coarctate pupation, remaining within the last larval skin until adulthood.
Certain parasitic wasps develop a coarctate pupa inside their host.
Entomologists distinguish coarctate pupae from other types based on the presence of a rigid case.

Etymology

From Latin coarctatus, past participle of coarctare (to press together).
