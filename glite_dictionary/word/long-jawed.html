long-jawed

1. ADJ

/ lɔŋ dʒɔd / (AmE), / lɒŋ dʒɔːd / (BrE)
Having relatively long jaws, especially in comparison to similar species or individuals.
having relatively long jaws

Examples

The long-jawed spider is easily identified by its pronounced mandibles.
Paleontologists examined the long-jawed skull fossil to determine its feeding habits.
Certain long-jawed fish species can capture prey others can't reach.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short-jawed - having relatively short jaws
