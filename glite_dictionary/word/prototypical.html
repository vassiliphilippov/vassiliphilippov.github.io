prototypical

1. ADJ

/ ˌproʊtəˈtɪpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəˈtɪpɪkəl / (BrE)
representing or constituting the original or typical example of a particular kind; serving as a model for others of its type
characteristic of an original or typical example

Examples

The prototypical smartphone combines a touchscreen with internet connectivity and a sleek design.
She is considered the prototypical entrepreneur, always seeking new opportunities and innovations.
That building, with its glass facade and open-plan design, is prototypical of modern office architecture.

Synset Examples

archetypal patterns
she was the prototypal student activist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archetypal - very typical of a certain kind of person or thing

Etymology

From 'prototype' + '-ical', ultimately from Greek 'protos' (first) and 'typos' (model, impression).
