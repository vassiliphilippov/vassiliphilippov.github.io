Baldr

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɑːl.dər / (AmE), / ˈbɑːl.də / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, the god of light and peace, known for his beauty and gentle nature; the son of Odin and Frigg and husband of Nanna, killed by Hoth.
Norse god of light, peace, and beauty

Examples

In Norse legends, Baldr was celebrated for his radiant beauty and kindness.
The tragic death of Baldr marked the beginning of Ragnarok in the myths.
Statues depicting Baldr often show him with a peaceful and gentle expression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Baldur - Alternative spelling of Baldr, Norse god of light

Etymology

From Old Norse 'Baldr', meaning 'lord' or 'prince'.
