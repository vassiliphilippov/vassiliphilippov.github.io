unfocused

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfoʊkəst / (AmE), / ʌnˈfəʊkəst / (BrE)
not concentrated on a particular point or objective; lacking clear direction or focus
not concentrated; lacking clear focus

Examples

Her unfocused mind drifted from one thought to another during the lecture.
The company's unfocused strategy made it difficult to achieve consistent results.
His photos are often unfocused, making it hard to see the subject clearly.

Synset Examples

diversity … in our huge unfocused country

Related Terms

  • Antonym: focused - concentrated on one point or goal

Etymology

Formed by combining 'un-' (not) with 'focused' (from 'focus', Latin origin).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00544995-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfoʊkəst / (AmE), / ʌnˈfəʊkəst / (BrE)
(of an image or view) not brought into clear focus; blurred or indistinct.
not in focus; blurred

Examples

The photograph came out unfocused and too dark to see any details.
Her vision remained unfocused until she put on her glasses.
Because the lens was dirty, the entire scene looked slightly unfocused to everyone.

Synset Examples

at their edges, things were pretty much unfocused

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blurred - unclear or lacking sharpness
  • Antonym: focused - brought into clear focus; sharp

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'focused', from Latin 'focus' (hearth, fireplace; figuratively, point of convergence).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00786516-a
Synset POS: a
