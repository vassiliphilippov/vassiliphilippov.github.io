arsenate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrsəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈɑːsɪneɪt / (BrE)
A salt or ester of arsenic acid, typically containing the anion AsO4^3−.
salt or ester of arsenic acid
medical

Examples

The presence of arsenate in drinking water can pose significant health risks.
Some bacteria can use arsenate instead of phosphate for their metabolic processes.
He analyzed the soil samples for arsenate and other contaminants.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: arsenite - a salt or ester of arsenious acid

Etymology

From arsenic, based on the pattern of chemical nomenclature.
