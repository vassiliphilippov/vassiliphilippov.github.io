deciliter

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛsɪˌlitər / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪˌliːtə / (BrE)
A metric unit of volume equal to one tenth of a liter (0.1 liter).
metric unit, one tenth of a liter

Examples

The recipe calls for exactly one deciliter of cream.
Each serving contains about half a deciliter of orange juice.
Doctors measured the blood sample in deciliters for accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: liter - basic metric unit of volume
  • Degree Lower: centiliter - a metric unit equal to one hundredth of a liter

Etymology

From French 'décilitre', from 'déci-' (ten) + 'litre'.
