sac

1. NOUN

/ sæk / (AmE), / sæk / (BrE)
a hollow, bag-like structure in an animal or plant, often containing fluid or other material, such as a pollen sac or a moss capsule
a hollow, bag-like structure in organisms

Examples

The pollen sac opens to release grains for pollination.
A spider's egg sac can contain hundreds of eggs.
The doctor explained that the fluid-filled sac needed to be removed surgically.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capsule - a small container or sac in plants or animals

Etymology

From French 'sac', from Latin 'saccus' meaning 'bag'.

2. NOUN

/ sæk / (AmE), / sæk / (BrE)
an anatomical pouch or cavity in an animal that resembles a bag and holds fluids, air, or other substances
pouchlike anatomical structure
medical

Examples

The lungs are made up of millions of tiny air sacs called alveoli.
A jellylike sac inside the seed supplies nutrients to the developing plant.
The gallbladder acts as a storage sac for bile.

Etymology

From French 'sac' (bag), from Latin 'saccus'.

3. NOUN

/ sæk / (AmE), / sæk / (BrE)
a pouch or enclosed space in a living organism or within a structure, often containing fluid or air
a pouch or enclosed space, often in a living organism

Examples

The frog’s eggs develop inside a jelly-like sac for protection.
A pollen sac in the plant stores grains until they are released.
Fluid accumulated in the tiny sac beneath his eye.
