Sac

1. NOUN

/ sæk / (AmE), / sæk / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people formerly living in the Fox River valley and on the shores of Green Bay, Wisconsin.
Native American people of Wisconsin

Examples

The Sac people played a significant role in the history of the Midwest.
Artifacts from the Sac tribe can be found in local museums.
Many Sac descendants still live in the region today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fox - A Native American people historically associated with the Sac

Etymology

From French 'Sauk', from an Algonquian language (Sauk 'saak'), the self-designation of the people.
