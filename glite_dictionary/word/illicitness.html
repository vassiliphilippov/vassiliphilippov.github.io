illicitness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɪsɪtnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪsɪtnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being illegal or forbidden by law
the state of being illegal

Examples

The illicitness of the transaction made everyone nervous about getting caught.
Despite its illicitness, the practice continued for years without punishment.
He was drawn to the thrill and illicitness of the forbidden activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegality - the state of being illegal or unlawful
  • Antonym: legality - the quality of being legal or permissible by law

Etymology

From illicit + -ness, ultimately from Latin illicitus (not allowed).
