Cherokee

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛrəˌki / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛrəki / (BrE)
a member of an Indigenous people originally from the southeastern United States, especially the Appalachian Mountains, now chiefly living in Oklahoma
a member of an Indigenous people of the US

Examples

Many Cherokee attended the annual powwow to celebrate their heritage.
She is proud to be a descendant of the Cherokee.
The Cherokee were forcibly relocated to Oklahoma during the Trail of Tears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iroquoian - a group of Indigenous peoples and languages related to the Cherokee

Etymology

From Cherokee self-designation ᏣᎳᎩ (Tsalagi).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛrəˌki / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛrəki / (BrE)
the Iroquoian language spoken by the Cherokee people, primarily in the southeastern United States
Iroquoian language of the Cherokee

Examples

A small number of people still speak Cherokee as their first language.
He is learning Cherokee to connect with his ancestral heritage.
The Cherokee language uses its own unique syllabary for writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iroquoian - a family of North American indigenous languages, including Cherokee

Etymology

From Cherokee self-designation ᏣᎳᎩ (Tsalagi).
