adsorption

1. NOUN

/ ædˈsɔɹpʃən / (AmE), / ədˈsɔːpʃən / (BrE)
the process by which molecules of a gas or liquid accumulate on the surface of a solid or liquid to form a thin film
accumulation of molecules on a surface
medical

Examples

Activated carbon filters purify air through adsorption of impurities on their surface.
The dye removal from wastewater relies on the adsorption of color molecules onto clay particles.
At lower temperatures, the rate of adsorption increases due to decreased kinetic energy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absorption - the process in which a substance is taken up internally by another substance

Etymology

From Latin root 'ad-' (to) + 'sorbere' (to suck in), patterned after 'absorption'.
