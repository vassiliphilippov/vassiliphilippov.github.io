warren

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔr.ən / (AmE), / ˈwɒr.ən / (BrE)
A network of interconnected rabbit burrows where a colony of rabbits lives.
colony of rabbits; network of burrows

Examples

The warren was well hidden beneath a thicket and difficult for predators to find.
After the rain, several young rabbits were seen playing near the entrance to the warren.
Farmers often keep an eye out for signs of a warren in their fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burrow - a hole or tunnel dug by a small animal

Etymology

From Old English *warenne*, via Old French *warenne* (game park, preserve), of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔr.ən / (AmE), / ˈwɒr.ən / (BrE)
A network of interconnecting underground tunnels inhabited by rabbits.
a network of rabbit burrows

Examples

The rabbits disappeared into their warren as soon as they sensed danger.
A complex warren stretched beneath the field, housing dozens of rabbits.
Foxes sometimes try to dig into a warren to catch their prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burrow - a hole or tunnel dug by an animal for habitation

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *warenne* (game park), from Old French *garenne* (enclosure).

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɔɹ.ən / (AmE), / ˈwɒɹ.ən / (BrE)
An area or building that is crowded with many people living closely together, often in poor or cramped conditions.
overcrowded residential area

Examples

The city's old quarter is a labyrinth of narrow streets and warrens packed with families.
During the industrial revolution, many workers lived in dark, unhealthy warrens near the factories.
The refugees were forced to stay in makeshift warrens, with little space or privacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slum - a squalid and overcrowded urban street or district inhabited by very poor people

Etymology

Originally from Old English *wearm* (meaning 'burrow'), related to rabbit habitats. Later extended metaphorically to humans in crowded spaces.
