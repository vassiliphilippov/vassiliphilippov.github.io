connatural

1. ADJ

/ kəˈnætʃərəl / (AmE), / kəˈnatʃərəl / (BrE)
(of a quality or trait) existing naturally from birth; innate or inborn.
innate or inborn

Examples

Many philosophers believe that a connatural understanding of justice guides human morality.
She has a connatural talent for music that was evident from a young age.
The species’ connatural drive to survive has shaped its evolution.

Synset Examples

mankind's connatural sense of the good

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innate - inborn; naturally present at birth
  • Antonym: acquired - obtained or developed after birth

Etymology

From Late Latin connaturalis, from com- (together) + naturalis (by birth, natural).

belonging to or having the same nature or inherent qualities as something else; similar in nature or kind
similar in nature
archaic

Examples
