B-52

1. NOUN

/ bi ˈfɪfti tu / (AmE), / biː ˈfɪfti tjuː / (BrE)
a large United States military aircraft, officially named the Boeing B-52 Stratofortress, designed for long-range bombing missions
type of long-range U.S. military bomber aircraft

Examples

The B-52 flew over the parade as part of the military display.
During the Cold War, the B-52 was a crucial part of America's nuclear deterrent.
A retired B-52 is now on display at the air and space museum.

Etymology

The 'B-' prefix stands for 'bomber'; '52' is the model number assigned by the manufacturer, Boeing.
