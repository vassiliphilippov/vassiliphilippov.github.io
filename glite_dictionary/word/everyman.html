everyman

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛv.riˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈɛv.riˌman / (BrE)
an ordinary or typical person with whom most people can easily identify
an ordinary or typical person

Examples

The novel's hero is an everyman who struggles with everyday challenges.
As an everyman, he represents the hopes and fears of the general public.
Critics praised the actor for his convincing portrayal of an everyman caught in extraordinary circumstances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commoner - an ordinary person without rank or title
  • Synonym: nobody - an unimportant or insignificant person
  • Antonym: celebrity - a famous person

Etymology

From the character Everyman in a late 15th-century English morality play representing the common person.
