phenoplast

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːnəˌplæst / (AmE), / ˈfiːnəʊˌplɑːst / (BrE)
A type of synthetic resin formed by the polymerization of phenols with aldehydes, used chiefly as a thermosetting plastic.
a synthetic thermosetting resin

Examples

The electrical components were encased in phenoplast for insulation.
Manufacturers choose phenoplast for its resistance to heat and chemicals.
The suitcase handle is made from durable phenoplast material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aminoplast - a synthetic resin made by the polymerization of amino compounds with formaldehyde

Etymology

From 'pheno-' (relating to phenol) + 'plast' (moulded material).
