widely

1. ADV

/ ˈwaɪdli / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdli / (BrE)
over a large area, range, or extent; to a great degree or by many people
over a large area or by many people

Examples

Her research has been widely recognized in the scientific community.
The statue is widely admired for its beauty and craftsmanship.
The disease spread widely across the country.

Synset Examples

wandered wide through many lands
he traveled widely

Related Terms

  • Antonym: narrowly - in a limited or restricted manner

Etymology

From wide (Old English wīd) + -ly (adverbial suffix).

2. ADV

/ ˈwaɪdli / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdli / (BrE)
with a lot of space or distance between points or objects; far apart from each other
with much space or distance between

Examples

The houses in the village are widely spaced apart from one another.
At the zoo, the enclosures are widely separated to give animals more privacy.
The trees in this orchard are widely planted, allowing space for machinery to pass.

Synset Examples

widely separated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: narrowly - with small distance or space between points or objects
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00508875-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈwaɪdli / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdli / (BrE)
to a great degree; by or among a large number of people or over a large area.
to a great degree; over a wide range

Examples

The movie was widely praised by critics across the globe.
Her invention became widely adopted in the field of medicine.
The news spread widely through social media within hours of the announcement.

Synset Examples

her work is widely known

Related Terms

  • Antonym: narrowly - to a small extent or over a limited area

Etymology

From 'wide' + '-ly', where '-ly' is an adverbial suffix in Old and Middle English.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
