to cajole

1. VERB

/ kəˈdʒoʊl / (AmE), / kəˈdʒəʊl / (BrE)
to persuade someone to do something by gentle urging, flattery, or repeated appeals
persuade by flattery or gentle urging

Examples

She tried to cajole her brother into joining the trip by promising him his favorite dessert.
The sales representative cajoled the hesitant customer into making a purchase.
He spent all afternoon cajoling his colleagues to support his proposal.

Synset Examples

He palavered her into going along

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coax - to persuade someone gradually or gently
  • Antonym: to dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action

Etymology

From French cajoler (to coax or flatter).
