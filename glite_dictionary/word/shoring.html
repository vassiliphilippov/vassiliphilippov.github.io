shoring

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɔː.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of supporting a structure with props or shores to prevent collapse
act of supporting with props

Examples

The workers installed shoring to stabilize the trench during excavation.
Due to the building's age, extensive shoring was required before renovations could begin.
Emergency crews quickly put up shoring to prevent the wall from collapsing further.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - the act of bearing the weight of or strengthening

Etymology

From 'shore' (prop or support) + -ing, from Middle English 'shoren'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)
A beam, timber, or similar structural element placed against a building or excavation to provide temporary support and prevent collapse.
supporting beam or timber

Examples

The workers installed shoring to stabilize the wall during renovations.
Emergency crews used heavy shoring to prevent the building from collapsing.
Proper shoring is essential when working in deep trenches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prop - a rigid support placed against something to hold it up
  • Synonym: brace - a support placed to strengthen or hold up a structure

Etymology

From shore (to prop), from Middle English 'schore' (prop, support).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
