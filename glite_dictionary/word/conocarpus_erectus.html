Conocarpus erectus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnoʊˈkɑːrpəs ɪˈrɛktəs / (AmE), / ˌkɒnəʊˈkɑːpəs ɪˈrɛktəs / (BrE)
an evergreen tree or shrub of tropical America, known as buttonwood, with rounded fruit clusters and dense, hard wood
evergreen tree or shrub (buttonwood) of tropical America

Examples

The roots of the Conocarpus erectus stabilize coastal soils and protect against erosion.
Local artisans often use the dense wood of Conocarpus erectus for carving and construction.
The leaves of Conocarpus erectus remain green year-round in warm climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttonwood - another common name for Conocarpus erectus, especially in coastal contexts

Etymology

From Greek 'konos' (cone) + 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the button-like fruit shape; 'erectus' is Latin for 'upright'.
