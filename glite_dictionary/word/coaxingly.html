coaxingly

1. ADV

/ ˈkoʊksɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈkəʊksɪŋli / (BrE)
in a gentle and persuasive manner; in a way that attempts to influence someone to do something through friendliness or flattering.
in a gentle, persuasive manner

Examples

She spoke coaxingly to convince her son to eat his vegetables.
He smiled coaxingly as he asked for a small favor.
'Please, just this once,' she said coaxingly, hoping he would agree.

Synset Examples

‘Come here,’ she said coaxingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cajolingly - in a manner intended to persuade gently or with flattery

Etymology

From 'coax' + '-ing' + '-ly'; 'coax' likely from obsolete 'coax' (fool, simpleton).
