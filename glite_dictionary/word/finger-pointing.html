finger-pointing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡɚ ˌpɔɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡə ˌpɔɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of blaming someone or assigning responsibility, especially in a public or accusatory manner
the act of blaming others

Examples

There was a lot of finger-pointing after the project failed.
Instead of finger-pointing, the team should focus on finding solutions.
The committee urged members to avoid finger-pointing and work together.

Synset Examples

they want all the finger-pointing about intelligence failures to stop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blame - the act of holding someone responsible for a fault or wrong
  • Antonym: praise - the act of expressing approval or admiration

Etymology

From the literal act of pointing a finger at someone to show blame or accusation.
