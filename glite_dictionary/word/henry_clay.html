Henry Clay

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛnri kleɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri kleɪ / (BrE)
(1777–1852) A prominent United States politician and statesman known for his role in the Missouri Compromise and other important legislative efforts.
U.S. politician and statesman (1777–1852)

Examples

Henry Clay was instrumental in brokering the Missouri Compromise in 1820.
Many historians consider Henry Clay one of the most influential legislators in American history.
As Speaker of the House, Henry Clay was a key figure in shaping American domestic policy.
