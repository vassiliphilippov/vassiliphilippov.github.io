Nepalese

1. ADJ

/ nəˌpɑːˈliz / (AmE), / nɪˌpɑːˈliːz / (BrE)
relating to Nepal, its people, language, or culture
of Nepal or its people/culture/language

Examples

The museum has a remarkable collection of Nepalese art and artifacts.
She learned to cook traditional Nepalese dishes during her travels.
Several Nepalese climbers joined the expedition to Mount Everest.

Synset Examples

Nepalese troops massed at the border
Nepali mountains are among the highest in the world
the different Nepali words for ‘rice’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nepali - relating to Nepal, its people, or its language (variant form)

Etymology

From Nepal + -ese, a suffix denoting nationality or language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02983384-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ nəˈpɑːliz / (AmE), / nɛˈpɑːliːz / (BrE)
A person who is a native or inhabitant of Nepal.
person from Nepal

Examples

Many Nepalese live in the Kathmandu Valley.
The restaurant is owned and operated by a family of Nepalese.
She became friends with several Nepalese during her travels in Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nepali - native or inhabitant of Nepal; language of Nepal

Etymology

From Nepal + -ese, a suffix forming demonyms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09743480-n
Synset POS: n
