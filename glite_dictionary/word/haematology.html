haematology

1. NOUN

/ hiːˌmætəˈlɑːdʒi / (AmE), / hiːˌmætəˈlɒdʒi / (BrE)
The branch of medicine concerned with the study and treatment of diseases of the blood and blood-forming organs.
study of blood and its diseases
medical british

Examples

After studying haematology, she became an expert in diagnosing blood disorders.
The haematology department handles cases of anaemia, leukaemia, and clotting disorders.
Research in haematology has led to significant improvements in cancer treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hematology - study of blood and its diseases (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + -logy (study of).
