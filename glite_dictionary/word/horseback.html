horseback

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsbæk / (BrE)
A narrow ridge or crest of hills resembling the back of a horse.
a narrow ridge of hills

Examples

The hikers followed the horseback until they reached the summit.
A narrow horseback separated the two valleys below.
Clouds gathered above the horseback, casting shadows on the land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridge - a long, narrow elevated landform
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09327282-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsbæk / (BrE)
the back or upper part of a horse, especially as a place to sit when riding
the back of a horse

Examples

He climbed onto the horseback and rode away into the sunset.
The tribe traveled miles on horseback to reach the festival.
She lost her balance and slipped off the horseback while trying to adjust the saddle.

Etymology

Compound of 'horse' and 'back', referring to the back of a horse.

Source: WordNet

3. ADV

/ ˈhɔrsˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsbæk / (BrE)
on the back of a horse; riding a horse.
on the back of a horse

Examples

He traveled horseback across the countryside, enjoying the fresh air and open fields.
The messenger arrived horseback with urgent news from the capital.
In the old West, lawmen often chased outlaws horseback through rugged canyons.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ahorseback - on the back of a horse
  • Antonym: on foot - walking rather than riding

Etymology

From 'horse' + 'back', referring to the back of a horse.
