kitty

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪti / (AmE), / ˈkɪti / (BrE)
An informal term for a domestic cat, especially used by or to children.
informal term for a cat
informal

Examples

The little girl played with her kitty all afternoon.
We adopted a stray kitty from the animal shelter yesterday.
Can you feed the kitty while I'm away this weekend?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cat - a small domesticated carnivorous mammal, Felis catus

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪti / (AmE), / ˈkɪti / (BrE)
the sum of money collected by a group, especially in gambling or for a particular purpose
pooled money in games or groups

Examples

Each player put a dollar into the kitty at the start of the game.
At the end of the night, the winner took home the entire kitty.
We're collecting a kitty to pay for snacks during the tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pot - the total sum of money bet by all players in a game, especially poker

Probably from the diminutive of 'kit', meaning a collection or hoard.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɪti / (AmE), / ˈkɪti / (BrE)
A young domestic cat; a kitten.
young domestic cat

Examples

My daughter loves to play with the kitty we adopted last week.
The kitty curled up in my lap and purred contentedly.
Please don't let the kitty outside; she's too young to go out alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kitten - a young cat
  • Degree Higher: cat - a small domesticated mammal (Felis catus)

Etymology

Diminutive of 'kit' (kitten), probably from Middle English 'kitling'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈkɪti / (AmE), / ˈkɪti / (BrE)
the sum of money contributed by players or participants in a game or activity, to be won or used collectively
pooled money in a game or activity

Examples

All the players threw in a dollar to build the kitty before the game began.
She won the entire kitty after a tense round of poker.
If you fold, you can’t take any money out of the kitty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pot - the total sum of money bet by all the players in a game

Etymology

Probably from the diminutive of 'kit'; origin uncertain, but first recorded in mid-19th century gambling slang.

5. NOUN

/ ˈkɪti / (AmE), / ˈkɪti / (BrE)
A sum of money collected from a group of people for a particular purpose, typically to be used jointly or distributed as needed.
communal sum of money

Examples

Everyone chipped in to create a kitty for snacks during the trip.
At the end of the month, the roommates used the apartment kitty to buy cleaning supplies.
They added a few coins each day to their vacation kitty.

Synset Examples

They raised a little kitty for the charity event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pool - a collective sum of money, especially in a betting or card game context

Etymology

Probably from English children's slang, later extended to card games for the pool of money.
