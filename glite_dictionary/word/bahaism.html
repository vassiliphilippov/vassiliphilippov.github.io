Bahaism

1. NOUN

/ bəˈhaɪˌɪzəm / (AmE), / bəˈhaɪˌɪzəm / (BrE)
a religion founded in Iran in 1863, emphasizing the spiritual unity of all humankind and incorporating elements from Christianity and Islam
religion founded in Iran, 1863

Examples

Many people are unfamiliar with the core teachings of Bahaism.
She studied the influence of Bahaism on modern religious movements.
Public worship is not a feature of Bahaism; instead, prayers are often said in private.

Synset Examples

Bahaism has no public rituals or sacraments and praying is done in private

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bahá'í Faith - formal name for the Bahai religion

Etymology

From Persian 'Bahā’ī' (follower of Bahá'u'lláh) + English '-ism'.
