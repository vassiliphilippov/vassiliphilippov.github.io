to suggest

1. VERB

/ səgˈdʒɛst, səˈdʒɛst / (AmE), / səgˈdʒɛst, səˈdʒɛst / (BrE)
to put forward an idea or plan for consideration
propose an idea or plan

Examples

May I suggest a different approach to solve this problem?
She suggested having dinner at the new Italian restaurant.
The committee is suggesting several changes to the current policy.

Synset Examples

the senator proposed to abolish the sales tax

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to propose - to put forward a plan or suggestion
  • Synonym: to recommend - to suggest something as a good choice

Etymology

From Latin 'suggestus', past participle of 'suggerere' (to bring up, suggest).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00876925-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ səgˈdʒɛst, səˈdʒɛst / (AmE), / səgˈdʒɛst, səˈdʒɛst / (BrE)
to mention or indicate something as a possible fact or explanation without stating it directly
imply or indicate as a possibility

Examples

The data suggest that the new treatment may be effective in some patients.
The tone of her voice seemed to suggest a lack of interest in the project.
Several clues suggest that he was at the scene, though nothing is certain.

Synset Examples

The evidence suggests a need for more clarification

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imply - to suggest something without stating it directly
  • Antonym: state - to express something clearly and directly

Etymology

From Latin suggerere (to put under, bring up, suggest), from sub- (under) + gerere (to carry).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00932768-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ səɡˈdʒɛst, səɡˈʤɛst / (AmE), / səɡˈdʒɛst, səˈdʒest / (BrE)
to cause someone to think of something; to indirectly bring to mind or imply
call to mind; imply

Examples

The faint smile on her face suggested a hidden joy.
The dark clouds and distant thunder suggest an impending storm.
His tone of voice suggested that he was not pleased with the outcome.

Synset Examples

this remark evoked sadness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imply - to strongly suggest the truth or existence of something without explicitly stating it
  • Synonym: to evoke - to bring or recall a feeling, memory, or image to the mind
  • Antonym: state - to say or declare something directly

Etymology

From Latin suggerere (bring up, supply), from sub- (under) + gerere (bring, carry).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00932330-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ səgˈdʒɛst, səgˈdʒest / (AmE), / səˈdʒest, səgˈdʒest / (BrE)
to mention or introduce an idea, plan, or possibility indirectly; to imply or indicate without stating directly
imply or hint at something

Examples

The tone of his voice seemed to suggest that he was unhappy with the decision.
Her silence suggested that she disagreed, even though she said nothing outright.
The evidence suggests a possible connection between the two incidents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imply - to express something indirectly
  • Synonym: to indicate - to point out or suggest something indirectly
  • Antonym: state - to express in a direct form

Etymology

From Latin 'suggestus' (set under, brought up), past participle of 'suggerere'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00929401-v
Synset POS: v
