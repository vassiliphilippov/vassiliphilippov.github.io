Batrachoseps

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtrækəˌsɛps / (AmE), / bəˈtrækəˌsɛps / (BrE)
A genus of slender salamanders native to western North America, known for their elongated bodies and small limbs.
genus of slender salamanders

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species of Batrachoseps in the mountains of California.
Many Batrachoseps species thrive in moist, shaded woodland habitats.
The Batrachoseps are recognized by their exceptionally slender bodies and reduced limbs.

Etymology

From Greek 'batrachos' (frog) + 'seps' (lizard, newt).
