rectifiable

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛktɪˌfaɪəbəl / (AmE), / ˈrɛktɪˌfaɪəbl / (BrE)
capable of being corrected, repaired, or put right
able to be corrected or repaired

Examples

The mistake was unfortunate but completely rectifiable with a few changes.
Several damages from the storm were found to be rectifiable by the repair crew.
She assured him that any errors in the document were rectifiable before submission.

Synset Examples

reparable damage to the car
rectifiable wrongs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reparable - able to be repaired or mended
  • Antonym: irreparable - not able to be repaired or corrected

Etymology

From Latin 'rectificare' (to make right) + English suffix '-able'.
