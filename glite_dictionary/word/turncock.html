turncock

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrnˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnˌkɒk / (BrE)
a person whose job is to control the water supply by turning water mains on and off
person who controls water supply mains

Examples

The city's turncock arrived quickly to shut off the leaking main.
As a turncock, he was responsible for ensuring water supplies during emergencies.
The old turncock kept a detailed log of water main operations.

Etymology

From 'turn' + 'cock' (valve for controlling flow), originally referring to the device and then to the person operating it.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrnˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnˌkɒk / (BrE)
A valve or faucet operated by turning a handle to control the flow of liquid, especially water.
rotating valve or faucet

Examples

The old house still had a turncock near the main water pipe in the cellar.
A plumber came to replace the rusty turncock with a modern valve.
In an emergency, knowing how to operate the turncock is essential for shutting off the water supply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valve - device for controlling flow of a fluid
  • Synonym: faucet - a device for controlling the flow of liquid from a pipe

Etymology

From 'turn' (to rotate) and 'cock' (a tap or valve).
