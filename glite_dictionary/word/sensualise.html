to sensualise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛn.tʃu.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tjʊ.əˌlaɪz / (BrE)
To make something more focused on or influenced by sensual or carnal gratification, often diminishing its intellectual or moral qualities.
make more sensual or carnal

Examples

The director was criticized for trying to sensualise what was originally a profound story.
Some fear that modern advertising tends to sensualise traditional values for commercial gain.
He argued that contemporary art had begun to sensualise themes once considered sacred.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to debase - to lower in quality or value
  • Antonym: to spiritualise - make more spiritual or elevate spiritually

Etymology

From Latin 'sensualis' (endowed with feeling), via French and English formation with '-ise'.
