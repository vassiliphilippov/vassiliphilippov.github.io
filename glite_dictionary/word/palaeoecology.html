palaeoecology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊiˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpalɪəʊiˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The scientific study of interactions between organisms and their environments in the geological past.
study of ancient ecosystems

Examples

Research in palaeoecology helps reconstruct past environments by analyzing fossil evidence.
She specializes in palaeoecology, focusing on how ancient plants responded to climate change.
Advancements in palaeoecology have provided new insights into the extinction of prehistoric animals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ecology - study of interactions between organisms and their environment

Etymology

From Greek 'palaios' (ancient) + 'ecology'.
