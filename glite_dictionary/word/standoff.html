standoff

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈstændɒf / (BrE)
an act of resisting or repelling an attack, especially by successfully maintaining a defensive position
successful defensive stand or resistance

Examples

The soldiers held a standoff at the bridge, preventing enemy forces from advancing.
After several hours, the standoff between protesters and police ended without violence.
Their defensive strategy resulted in a standoff, stopping the opposition's attack cold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defense - the act of protecting against attack
  • Antonym: retreat - an act of withdrawing from an attack or confrontation

2. NOUN

/ ˈstændˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈstændɒf / (BrE)
a situation in a contest or competition where the score is tied and no winner is decided
a tied or undecided contest

Examples

The match ended in a tense standoff, with both teams unable to score in extra time.
After nine rounds of chess, the tournament finished in a standoff between the top two players.
The debate resulted in a standoff, as neither side could convince the other of their argument.

Synset Examples

the game ended in a draw
their record was 3 wins, 6 losses and a tie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draw - a contest that ends with neither side winning
  • Synonym: tie - a result in which two or more competitors finish with the same score
  • Antonym: win - to achieve victory in a contest

Synset POS: n
