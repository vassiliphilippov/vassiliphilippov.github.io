Megalobatrachus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəloʊbəˈtrækəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡələʊbəˈtrækəs / (BrE)
A former genus of giant salamanders, now usually included in the genus Cryptobranchus.
genus of giant salamanders (obsolete)

Examples

The Japanese giant salamander was once classified under the genus Megalobatrachus.
Recent studies have merged Megalobatrachus into the modern genus Cryptobranchus.
Specimens labeled as Megalobatrachus are often found in older zoological collections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cryptobranchus - genus of large aquatic salamanders (giant salamanders)

Etymology

From Greek 'megas' (large) + 'batrachos' (frog).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəloʊbəˈtreɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡələʊbəˈtrækəs / (BrE)
a genus of giant salamanders, sometimes classified within the genus Cryptobranchus.
genus of giant salamanders

Examples

The Japanese giant salamander is classified in the genus Megalobatrachus by some scientists.
Members of Megalobatrachus are known for their impressive size and aquatic habits.
Taxonomic debates sometimes place Megalobatrachus species in the genus Cryptobranchus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cryptobranchus - a genus of aquatic salamanders known as hellbenders

Etymology

From Greek 'megas' (large) and 'batrachos' (frog).
