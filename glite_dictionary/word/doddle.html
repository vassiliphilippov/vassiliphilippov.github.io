doddle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑː.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈdɒd.əl / (BrE)
A task or activity that is very easy to do.
something very easy to do
informal british

Examples

Finishing the exam was a doddle for her, as she had studied thoroughly.
Compared to last year's race, this marathon was an absolute doddle.
He said the repair job was a doddle, taking him just a few minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinch - something that is very easy to do
  • Synonym: piece of cake - something that is extremely easy to do
  • Antonym: challenge - something that is difficult to accomplish

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly British slang from early 20th century.
