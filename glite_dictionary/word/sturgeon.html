sturgeon

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɝːdʒən / (AmE), / ˈstɜːdʒən / (BrE)
A large primitive fish found mainly in northern temperate regions, valued for its flesh and roe (caviar).
large fish prized for flesh and roe

Examples

The chef prepared a delicate dish using fresh sturgeon caught earlier that day.
Caviar, made from the roe of the sturgeon, is considered a luxury delicacy worldwide.
Many species of sturgeon are now endangered due to overfishing and habitat loss.

Etymology

From Old French esturgeon, from Latin acipenser.
