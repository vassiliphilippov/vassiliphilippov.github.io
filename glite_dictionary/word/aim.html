aim

1. NOUN

/ eɪm / (AmE), / eɪm / (BrE)
the act of directing or pointing something at a target or object; the process of taking careful direction before shooting or throwing
the act of aiming at a target

Examples

He took aim and fired at the approaching deer.
You need steady hands and good aim to hit the bullseye.
The soldier's aim improved after hours of practice.

Synset Examples

he took aim and fired

Related Terms

  • Synonym: target - an object that is aimed at

Etymology

From Old French aimer (to estimate, appraise), from Latin aestimare (to estimate).

Synset POS: n
