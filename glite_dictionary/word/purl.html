purl

1. VERB

/ pɝl / (AmE), / pɜːl / (BrE)
to knit using a purl stitch, creating a raised loop on one side of the fabric
to knit with a purl stitch

Examples

She carefully purled each row to create a textured pattern in the scarf.
After learning how to knit, he decided to purl for the first time.
You must purl every second row to produce the classic ribbed effect.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: knit - to make fabric by interlocking yarn with needles

Etymology

From Middle English *pirlyng* or *purl*, possibly imitative of twisting motions.

Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ pɜrl / (AmE), / pɜːl / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to flow with a swirling or curling motion, often accompanied by a murmuring sound.
flow with a swirling motion (liquids)

Examples

The stream began to purl gently around the mossy stones.
Water purled over the pebbles, creating a soothing sound in the quiet forest.
As the brook purled through the meadow, birds sang nearby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eddy - move in a circular way (liquids or air)

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'pirlen' (to ripple), related to the murmuring sound of flowing water.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ pɝl / (AmE), / pɜːl / (BrE)
a fine thread made of twisted gold or silver wire, used for embroidery or decorative edging in clothing and textiles
decorative gold or silver wire thread

Examples

The elaborate gown was embroidered with purl along its sleeves and collar.
Artisans skillfully threaded purl through the fabric to create a shimmering pattern.
She purchased several spools of gold purl for her embroidery project.

Etymology

From Old French *purl* or *purle*, of uncertain origin, originally referring to a fringe or border.

5. NOUN

/ pɜrl / (AmE), / pɜːl / (BrE)
a basic knitting stitch made by passing the needle through the front of the stitch from the right-hand side, creating a raised, bumpy row
knitting stitch with raised, bumpy surface

Examples

She used the purl stitch to create a textured scarf.
Alternating rows of purl and knit stitches form a ribbed pattern.
Learning how to purl is essential for beginner knitters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: knit stitch - a basic knitting stitch opposite to purl, forming a smooth, flat surface

Etymology

From Middle English 'pirle', possibly from Old French 'purler', meaning to embroider or edge fabric.
