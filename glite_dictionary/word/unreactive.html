unreactive

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.riˈæk.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌn.riˈæk.tɪv / (BrE)
not tending to respond to stimulation; not reacting or showing a response.
not responsive; not reacting

Examples

The scientist worked with noble gases because they are generally unreactive.
Despite being insulted, she remained completely unreactive and calm.
Some chemicals are so unreactive that they do not combine with other substances easily.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reactive - tending to react to stimulus or changes

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'reactive'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnriˈæktɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌnriˈæktɪv / (BrE)
(of a substance) not tending to undergo a chemical reaction under given conditions.
not likely to react chemically

Examples

Noble gases like helium are highly unreactive due to their full outer electron shells.
The metal remained unreactive when exposed to acid, indicating good corrosion resistance.
Because this compound is unreactive, it can be safely used as a stabilizer in chemical mixtures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reactive - tending to react chemically
  • Synonym: inert - chemically inactive or very slow to react

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) and 'reactive', from Latin 're-' (again) + 'agere' (to do, act).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01935999-a
Synset POS: a
