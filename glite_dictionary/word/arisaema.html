Arisaema

1. NOUN

/ əˈraɪsiˌimə / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈsiːmə / (BrE)
A genus of tuberous or rhizomatous herbaceous perennial plants, often known as jack-in-the-pulpit, native to Asia and North America.
genus of perennial herbaceous plants

Examples

Many Arisaema species are cultivated in gardens for their unusual flowers.
The Arisaema produces a distinctive, hooded spathe that conceals its spadix.
Some Arisaema varieties thrive in shade and moist woodland environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, derived from Greek 'aris' (arum) and 'haima' (blood), referring to the red sap of some species.
