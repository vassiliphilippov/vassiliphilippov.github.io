entry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.tɹi / (AmE), / ˈɛn.tri / (BrE)
the act of going or coming into a place
the act of entering

Examples

Her dramatic entry at the party caught everyone's attention.
The thief made an entry through the back door.
Please wait for the signal before making your entry into the building.

Synset Examples

she made a grand entrance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entrance - the act of entering or an opening for entering
  • Antonym: exit - the act of leaving a place

Etymology

From Old French *entrée*, from Latin *intrata* (entrance), from *intrare* (to enter).
