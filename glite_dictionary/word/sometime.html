sometime

1. ADV

/ ˈsʌm.taɪm / (AmE), / ˈsʌm.taɪm / (BrE)
At an unspecified or unknown time, usually in the past or future.
at an unspecified time

Examples

Let's have coffee sometime next week.
He promised to call her sometime soon, but she never heard from him.
We should visit the museum sometime during the holidays.

Synset Examples

let's get together sometime
everything has to end sometime
It was to be printed sometime later

Related Terms

  • Synonym: someday - at an indefinite time in the future

Etymology

From Middle English 'sometime' (at some time); 'some' + 'time'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsʌm.taɪm / (AmE), / ˈsʌm.taɪm / (BrE)
formerly; having held a position or role in the past, but not now.
former; in the past

Examples

She was a sometime actress before becoming a director.
The sometime president made a rare public appearance.
He returned to visit his sometime colleagues at the university.

Synset Examples

erstwhile friend
our former glory
the once capital of the state
her quondam lover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: former - having previously filled a particular role or been a certain thing
  • Synonym: erstwhile - former; of a prior time
  • Antonym: current - presently existing or happening; opposite of former or past

Etymology

From Old English sum + time; used adjectivally since the 16th century to mean 'former'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01733798-s
Synset POS: s
