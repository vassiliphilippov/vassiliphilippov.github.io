Balaenoptera

1. NOUN

/ bəˌliːnoʊpˈtɛrə / (AmE), / bəˌliːnɒpˈtɜːrə / (BrE)
A genus of large whales in the family Balaenopteridae, including the blue whale and fin whale.
genus of large whales (rorquals)

Examples

The blue whale, the largest animal on earth, belongs to the genus Balaenoptera.
Marine biologists are studying several species within Balaenoptera to learn more about their migratory patterns.
The Balaenoptera genus includes both the fin whale and the minke whale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Balaenopteridae - the whale family containing rorquals

Etymology

From Latinized Greek 'balaena' (whale) + 'pteron' (fin, wing).

2. NOUN

/ bæˌliːəˈnɑptərə / (AmE), / bəˌliːənˈɒptərə / (BrE)
a genus of large baleen whales that includes the blue whale, fin whale, and others, belonging to the family Balaenopteridae
genus of large baleen whales

Examples

The blue whale, the largest animal on Earth, belongs to the genus Balaenoptera.
Researchers studied several species within Balaenoptera to better understand whale migration patterns.
Conservation efforts are focused on protecting endangered members of the Balaenoptera genus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Balaenopteridae - the family of rorqual whales, to which Balaenoptera belongs

Etymology

From Latin *balaena* (whale) + Ancient Greek *pteron* (fin, wing).
