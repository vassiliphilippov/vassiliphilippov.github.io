Karok

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæroʊk / (AmE), / ˈkærək / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people from the Klamath River valley in northern California.
member of a Native American people of California

Examples

The Karok have lived along the Klamath River for thousands of years.
A Karok elder spoke about traditional customs at the gathering.
Many Karok are actively involved in preserving their language and culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Karok - the language of the Karok people

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæˌrɒk / (AmE), / ˈkæˌrɒk / (BrE)
the Native American language traditionally spoken by the Karok people of northwestern California.
a Native American language of California

Examples

The Karok language is taught in some Californian schools to help preserve cultural heritage.
Scholars have been working to document the grammar and vocabulary of Karok.
He is fluent in both English and Karok.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Karok - a member of the Karok people

Etymology

From the self-designation of the Karok people, meaning 'upriver people' in their language.
