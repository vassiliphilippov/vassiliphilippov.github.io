tablet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæblət / (AmE), / ˈtæblɪt / (BrE)
a set of sheets of paper, fastened together at one edge, used for writing or drawing
pad of writing paper

Examples

She tore a sheet from her tablet and scribbled a quick note.
Please write your answers on the tablet provided.
The artist sketched the landscape on a small tablet of paper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notepad - a small pad of paper for writing notes
  • Synonym: pad - a block of paper sheets fastened together at one end
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14997692-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæblət / (AmE), / ˈtæblət / (BrE)
a small, flat, compressed piece or cake of a substance, such as soap or medicine, intended for individual use
a small flat compressed cake of a substance

Examples

She placed a tablet of soap in the bathroom dish before the guests arrived.
The doctor told me to dissolve a tablet in water and drink it once a day.
Each tablet contains the exact dosage required for your treatment.

Synset Examples

a tablet of soap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pill - a small round mass of medicinal substance for swallowing

Etymology

From Old French *tablete* (a small table or slab), diminutive of *table*; originally referring to a flat slab, later extended to small cakes.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04388270-n
Synset POS: n

portable touchscreen computer

Many students now bring a tablet to class instead of a laptop.

4. NOUN

/ ˈtæblət / (AmE), / ˈtæblɪt / (BrE)
A small, solid, round or oval dose of medicine intended to be swallowed.
a small, solid medicine dose

Examples

Take one tablet every morning with a glass of water.
The doctor prescribed a new tablet to help with her headaches.
He accidentally dropped the tablet on the floor and had to get a new one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pill - a small round mass of solid medicine to be swallowed
  • Synonym: capsule - a small container with medicine, usually gel-like and easy to swallow

Etymology

From Old French tablette, diminutive of table (table), from Latin tabula (a board, plank, writing tablet).

5. NOUN

/ ˈtæblət / (AmE), / ˈtæblət / (BrE)
a flat, usually rectangular slab of stone, wood, or other material, used for inscriptions or carvings
a slab for inscriptions or carvings

Examples

The museum displayed an ancient tablet inscribed with hieroglyphics.
A commemorative tablet was mounted on the wall to honor the hero.
He studied the stone tablet that marked the entrance to the temple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaque - a flat plate, typically of metal, stone, or wood, with an inscription or decoration

Etymology

From Old French 'tablete', diminutive of 'table' from Latin 'tabula' (a board or plank).
