Musca domestica

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskə doʊˈmɛstɪkə / (AmE), / ˈmʌskə dəʊˈmɛstɪkə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the common housefly, a widespread fly that often lives in human habitats and can spread disease
the common housefly (scientific name)

Examples

Musca domestica is known to transmit various diseases due to its habit of landing on waste and food.
Scientists studied the behavior of Musca domestica to develop better pest control methods.
The spread of Musca domestica increases during the summer months when temperatures rise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: housefly - a common fly found in homes worldwide

Etymology

From Latin: 'musca' meaning 'fly' and 'domestica' meaning 'domestic, of the household'.
