Calophyllum calaba

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈfɪləm kəˈlɑbə / (AmE), / ˌkæl.əˈfɪl.əm kəˈlɑː.bə / (BrE)
A West Indian tree with clusters of fragrant white flowers, producing durable timber and a resinous sap.
West Indian tree yielding timber and resin

Examples

The durable wood of the Calophyllum calaba is prized in tropical construction.
In some Caribbean regions, the resin of the Calophyllum calaba is used in traditional medicine.
The garden featured a blooming Calophyllum calaba with its characteristic white flowers.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name Calophyllum, from Greek kalos (beautiful) + phyllon (leaf).
