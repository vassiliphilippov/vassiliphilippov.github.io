geoduck

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡuː.iˌdʌk / (AmE), / ˈɡiː.əʊˌdʌk / (BrE)
a very large edible clam of the Pacific coast of North America, known for its long siphon and deep burrowing in sandy mud
a large Pacific coast edible clam

Examples

Fresh geoduck is considered a delicacy in many high-end seafood restaurants along the West Coast.
Divers harvested several large geoducks from the muddy bottom during the low tide.
The unique appearance of the geoduck, with its long siphon, fascinates marine biologists and chefs alike.

Etymology

From Chinook Jargon *gʷídəq*, referencing the clam species in the Pacific Northwest.
