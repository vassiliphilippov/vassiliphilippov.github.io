alphanumerics

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.fə.nuˈmɛr.ɪks / (AmE), / ˌæl.fə.njuːˈmɛr.ɪks / (BrE)
A set of characters consisting of letters and digits, sometimes including punctuation, used especially in computer systems and coding.
letters and digits (sometimes punctuation) as a set

Examples

Passwords must contain a mix of alphanumerics and symbols for security.
The license plate is made up of seven alphanumerics.
When filling out the form, use only alphanumerics to create your username.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alphanumeric - consisting of both letters and numbers

Etymology

From 'alpha' (alphabetic characters) and 'numeric' (numbers).
