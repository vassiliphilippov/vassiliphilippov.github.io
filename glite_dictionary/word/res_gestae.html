res gestae

1. NOUN

/ reɪz ˈʤɛstaɪ / (AmE), / reɪz ˈʤɛstaɪ / (BrE)
(in law) Facts and circumstances surrounding an act, especially those which are admissible as evidence in court because they help explain the principal event.
legal facts surrounding an act
legal

Examples

The court admitted the statement as part of the res gestae of the case.
Her testimony was considered relevant because it formed part of the res gestae.
The lawyer argued that the evidence fell within the res gestae exception to the hearsay rule.

Etymology

From Latin res gestae (things done).

2. NOUN

/ reɪs ˈdʒɛsti, reɪz ˈdʒɛstaɪ / (AmE), / reɪs ˈdʒɛstiː, rɛz ˈdʒɛstaɪ / (BrE)
(Law) Statements or facts so closely connected with an event that they are considered part of the event, and are therefore admissible as evidence, notwithstanding the hearsay rule.
legal doctrine; facts or remarks part of an event
legal

Examples

The witness's exclamation was admitted as res gestae and did not violate the hearsay rule.
Lawyers debated whether the statement fell within the res gestae exception during the trial.
The judge determined that these remarks comprised part of the res gestae of the incident.

Etymology

From Latin res gestae (things done).
