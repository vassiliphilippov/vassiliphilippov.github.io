aftershaft

1. NOUN

/ ˈæf.tɚˌʃæft / (AmE), / ˈɑːf.təˌʃɑːft / (BrE)
a small, secondary feather attached to the base of the main shaft of some feathers in certain birds
secondary feather at base of main bird feather

Examples

The ornithologist carefully examined the aftershaft to identify the bird species.
Some birds have a well-developed aftershaft that helps insulate their bodies.
You can see the tiny aftershaft beneath the main feather when the wing is spread.

Etymology

From 'after-' (meaning secondary or following) and 'shaft' (the main stem of a feather).
