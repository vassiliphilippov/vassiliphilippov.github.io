vernal

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɝː.nəl / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.nəl / (BrE)
Suggestive of youth, vigor, or freshness; relating to or characteristic of spring.
fresh, youthful, or springlike

Examples

Her vernal enthusiasm inspired everyone on the team.
The artist captured a vernal freshness in her paintings that reminded viewers of new beginnings.
His vernal spirit belied his advanced years.

Synset Examples

he is young for his age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: youthful - having qualities associated with young people
  • Antonym: aged - having much experience or age; the opposite of fresh or youthful

Etymology

From Latin 'vernalis', from 'ver' meaning 'spring'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɝːnəl / (AmE), / ˈvɜːnəl / (BrE)
Relating to, characteristic of, or occurring in the spring season.
of or relating to spring

Examples

The vernal breeze carried the scent of blooming flowers into the open window.
Many migratory birds return in response to vernal changes in temperature.
Gardeners often begin planting delicate seeds during the vernal months.

Synset Examples

the vernal equinox

Related Terms

  • Antonym: autumnal - relating to, or characteristic of, autumn

Etymology

From Latin vernalis, from ver (spring).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01257504-a
Synset POS: a
