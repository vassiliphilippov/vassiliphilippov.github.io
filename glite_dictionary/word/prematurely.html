prematurely

1. ADV

/ ˌpriː.məˈtʃʊr.li / (AmE), / ˌpriː.məˈtʃʊə.li / (BrE)
In a way that happens too soon or before the appropriate time.
too soon; before the right time

Examples

He left the meeting prematurely, missing the final decision.
If you judge prematurely, you might misunderstand the situation.
Her excitement caused her to announce the news prematurely.

Synset Examples

I spoke prematurely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: early - before the expected or usual time
  • Antonym: belatedly - later than expected or usual

Etymology

From 'premature' (from Latin praematurus: prae- 'before' + maturus 'ripe, mature') + -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˌpriː.məˈtʃʊr.li / (AmE), / ˌpriː.məˈtʃʊə.li / (BrE)
Before the expected or appropriate time; especially before the normal end of gestation in childbirth.
before the expected or normal time

Examples

The baby was delivered prematurely and required special care in the hospital.
She ended the meeting prematurely because of a sudden emergency.
Flowers that bloom prematurely can be damaged by late frosts.

Synset Examples

the child was born prematurely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: early - before the expected time
  • Antonym: late - after the expected time

Etymology

From Latin 'praematurus' (very early), from 'prae' (before) + 'maturus' (ripe, mature).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00431998-r
Synset POS: r
