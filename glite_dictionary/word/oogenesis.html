oogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.əˈdʒɛn.ə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.əˈdʒɛn.ɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
the process by which ova (egg cells) are developed in the female reproductive organs
development of egg cells
medical

Examples

The process of oogenesis begins before a female is born and continues into adulthood.
Researchers are studying oogenesis to understand fertility issues in mammals.
Hormonal imbalances can disrupt normal oogenesis and affect reproductive health.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spermatogenesis - formation of sperm cells

Etymology

From Greek 'ōon' (egg) + 'genesis' (origin, creation).
