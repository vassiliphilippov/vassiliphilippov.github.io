Artemisia gnaphalodes

1. NOUN

/ ɑɹtəˈmɪziə nɡæfəˈloʊdiːz / (AmE), / ɑːtəˈmɪziə ɡnafəˈləʊdiːz / (BrE)
a perennial cottony-white herb native to the southwestern United States, belonging to the genus Artemisia.
a cottony-white herb of the southwestern U.S.

Examples

The silver foliage of Artemisia gnaphalodes stands out in the arid landscape.
Gardeners in New Mexico appreciate Artemisia gnaphalodes for its drought tolerance.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Artemisia gnaphalodes in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Artemisia - a genus of aromatic plants including sagebrush and wormwood

Etymology

Artemisia: from Greek Artemis, name of the goddess; gnaphalodes: from Greek *gnaphalon* (lock of wool) and -odes (resembling).
