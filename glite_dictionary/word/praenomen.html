praenomen

1. NOUN

/ preɪˈnoʊmən / (AmE), / priːˈnəʊmɛn / (BrE)
the first personal name of a citizen in ancient Rome, preceding the nomen and cognomen
ancient Roman first personal name

Examples

In ancient Rome, the praenomen was usually chosen from a small group of traditional names.
The historian explained the significance of each part of a Roman's praenomen, nomen, and cognomen.
Marcus was a common praenomen among Roman citizens.

Etymology

From Latin praenōmen, from prae- (before) + nōmen (name).
