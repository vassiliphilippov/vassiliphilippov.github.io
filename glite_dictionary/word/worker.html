worker

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkər / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkə / (BrE)
a member of the working class, especially as considered in sociopolitical contexts, not necessarily currently employed
member of the working class

Examples

Many workers felt their rights were being ignored by the government.
The novel describes the struggles of workers during the industrial revolution.
Political parties often promise better conditions for workers before elections.

Synset Examples

workers of the world — unite!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proletarian - a member of the working class, especially in Marxist theory
  • Antonym: bourgeois - a member of the middle class, especially in Marxist theory
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkər / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkə / (BrE)
a person who takes action or gets things done, rather than just thinking or talking about them
person who gets things done

Examples

Sarah is a true worker; she always follows through on her promises.
We need a worker like John on this project to keep everything moving forward.
Instead of waiting for things to happen, he is a proactive worker who makes things happen.

Synset Examples

he's a principal actor in this affair
when you want something done, get a doer
he's a miracle worker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doer - a person who actively does things rather than just planning or thinking
  • Antonym: dreamer - a person who spends much time thinking or dreaming rather than taking action

Etymology

From Old English *weorc* (work) + *-er* (agent noun suffix).

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrkər / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkə / (BrE)
A sterile member of a colony of social insects, such as ants or bees, that forages for food and cares for the young.
sterile insect in social colony

Examples

In an ant colony, a worker gathers food and tends to the queen's eggs.
The hive depends on its workers to collect nectar and build honeycombs.
Unlike the queen or drones, the worker bee cannot reproduce but is vital to the colony's survival.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: queen - fertile female in a colony of social insects
  • Antonym: drone - male member of a colony of social insects, typically for mating
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02210101-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrkər / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkə / (BrE)
a person who is employed to do a particular job, especially one involving physical or routine tasks
person employed in a specific job

Examples

She is a reliable worker who always finishes her tasks on time.
The factory hired several new workers this month to increase production.
As a construction worker, he often starts his day at sunrise.

Synset Examples

he is a good worker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: employee - a person employed for wages or salary
  • Antonym: employer - a person or organization that hires workers

Etymology

From Old English *weorcan* (to work) + -er (agent noun suffix).
