cagy

1. ADJ

/ ˈkeɪɡi / (AmE), / ˈkeɪdʒi / (BrE)
very cautious and unwilling to give information or commit oneself, often in order to avoid trouble or gain an advantage
cautious; wary; secretive

Examples

He grew cagy when reporters asked about the details of the agreement.
Her cagy responses made it hard to know what she really thought.
As a cagy negotiator, he never revealed his strategy.

Synset Examples

a cagey avoidance of a definite answer
chary of the risks involved
a chary investor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cautious - being careful to avoid danger or risks
  • Synonym: chary - cautiously or suspiciously reluctant to do something
  • Antonym: reckless - not showing proper concern about the possible bad results of your actions

2. ADJ

/ ˈkeɪ.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.dʒi / (BrE)
Reluctant to give information or show one's feelings, often out of caution or shrewdness.
cautious or shrewd; evasive

Examples

She gave a cagy response when asked about her plans for the weekend.
The politician was always cagy when discussing controversial issues.
He's been cagy about his finances since the investigation began.

Synset Examples

a cagey lawyer
too clever to be sound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cagey - Reluctant to give information or show feelings out of caution

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'cagey', of uncertain origin, possibly related to 'cage' suggesting wariness or being on guard.
