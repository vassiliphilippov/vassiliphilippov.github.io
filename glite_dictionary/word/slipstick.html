slipstick

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪpˌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈslɪpˌstɪk / (BrE)
An analog device, also known as a slide rule, used for performing rapid mathematical calculations by sliding graduated scales relative to each other; now largely replaced by electronic calculators.
analog device for rapid calculation (slide rule)

Examples

Engineers once relied on the slipstick for complex calculations before electronic calculators became common.
He proudly demonstrated how to multiply large numbers with his old slipstick.
My grandfather taught me how to use a slipstick when I was a child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slide rule - an analog device for calculation using sliding scales

Etymology

A colloquial term for the 'slide rule,' originally referring to the sliding action of the instrument.
