Ferdinand

1. PROPN

/ ˌfɜrdnˌænd / (AmE), / ˈfɜːdɪnənd / (BrE)
The king of Castile and Aragon, who ruled jointly with his wife Isabella; their marriage united Spain and they supported the expedition of Columbus.
King of Spain, joint ruler with Isabella

Examples

Ferdinand and Isabella completed the Reconquista by capturing Granada in 1492.
As king, Ferdinand played a crucial role in the formation of a unified Spain.
The Spanish Inquisition was established during the reign of Ferdinand and Isabella.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Isabella - Queen of Castile, wife and co-ruler with Ferdinand

Etymology

From Spanish 'Fernando', from Gothic 'Frithunanths' (brave in peace).
