Maimonides

1. PROPN

/ maɪˈmɑːnɪˌdiz / (AmE), / maɪˈmɒnɪˌdiːz / (BrE)
(1135–1204) A Spanish-born Jewish philosopher, rabbi, and physician who is regarded as the greatest Jewish scholar of the Middle Ages and who codified Jewish law in the Mishneh Torah.
medieval Jewish philosopher and scholar

Examples

Maimonides had a profound influence on Jewish thought and law throughout the centuries.
Many students of philosophy study the writings of Maimonides in university courses.
The works of Maimonides were translated into several languages and shaped both Jewish and non-Jewish scholarship.

Etymology

From Greek Μαϊμωνίδης (Maimōnidēs), based on Hebrew name Moses ben Maimon.
