Scincella

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈsɛl.ə / (AmE), / sɪŋˈsɛl.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small, slender skinks in the family Scincidae, commonly found in Asia and North America.
genus of small skinks (lizards)

Examples

The forest floor was alive with small Scincella darting through the leaf litter.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Scincella in southern China.
Scincella skinks are known for their smooth, shiny scales and secretive habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skink - a type of lizard in the family Scincidae

Etymology

From Latinized form of the family name Scincidae, with the diminutive suffix -ella.

2. NOUN

/ skɪnˈsɛlə / (AmE), / skɪŋˈsɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of small skinks in the family Scincidae, including species found in Asia and North America.
a genus of small skinks (family Scincidae)

Examples

The forest floor was teeming with Scincella lizards darting among the leaves.
Researchers discovered a new species in the Scincella genus in northern Vietnam.
Many Scincella have smooth, shiny scales that help them move through dense undergrowth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skink - a lizard of the family Scincidae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, diminutive formed from 'Scincus', the genus name for skinks, itself from Greek 'skinkos'.
