Hydnum

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪd.nəm / (AmE), / ˈhɪd.nəm / (BrE)
a genus of fungi in the family Hydnaceae, known for their spiny or toothed fruiting bodies
genus of fungi (Hydnaceae)

Examples

The species Hydnum repandum is commonly known as the wood hedgehog or sweet tooth.
Many edible mushrooms belong to the genus Hydnum, valued by foragers.
Mycologists study Hydnum to learn more about toothed fungi and their ecological roles.

Etymology

From Greek hydnon (a truffle), due to the fungi's resemblance to truffles.
