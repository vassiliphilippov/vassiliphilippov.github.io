melt

1. NOUN

/ mɛlt / (AmE), / mɛlt / (BrE)
the process of changing from a solid to a liquid as a result of heating
process of becoming liquid by heat

Examples

The unexpected melt in the freezer ruined all the frozen food.
Spring brings a rapid melt of snow on the mountain slopes.
The power outage caused a refrigerator melt that created quite a mess.

Synset Examples

the power failure caused a refrigerator melt that was a disaster
the thawing of a frozen turkey takes several hours

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freezing - the process of changing from liquid to solid

Etymology

From Middle English 'melten', from Old English 'meltan' (to melt, dissolve).
