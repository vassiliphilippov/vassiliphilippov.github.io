bellyache

1. VERB

/ ˈbɛliˌeɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɛliˌeɪk / (BrE)
to complain, especially in a way that is excessive or annoying
to complain, often excessively
informal

Examples

He likes to bellyache about his job even though he has it pretty easy.
Stop bellyaching and do something about the situation if it bothers you so much.
She bellyached all afternoon about the long wait at the doctor's office.

Synset Examples

What was he hollering about?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grumble - to complain in a quiet or muted way
  • Synonym: moan - to complain, especially about something unimportant
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛliˌeɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɛliˌeɪk / (BrE)
A pain or ache in the stomach or abdominal area.
stomach or abdominal pain

Examples

After eating too much candy, she complained of a bellyache all evening.
Children often get a bellyache from running around after lunch.
He skipped dinner because he had a mild bellyache from lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stomachache - pain in the stomach or abdominal region
  • Antonym: headache - pain in the head

Etymology

From 'belly' (stomach, abdomen) + 'ache' (pain).
