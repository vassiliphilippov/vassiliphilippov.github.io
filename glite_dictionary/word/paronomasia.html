paronomasia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærənoʊˈmeɪʒiə / (AmE), / ˌparənəʊˈmeɪzɪə / (BrE)
the use of a pun or word play, especially for humorous or rhetorical effect
the use of a pun; word play

Examples

The poet’s clever use of paronomasia delighted the audience.
Writers often employ paronomasia to add wit and humor to their work.
His speech was full of paronomasia, making everyone laugh.

Synset Examples

I do it for the pun of it
his constant punning irritated her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pun - a joke exploiting different meanings of a word or similar-sounding words

Etymology

From Greek paronomasía (playing with words), from para- (beside) + onoma (name).
