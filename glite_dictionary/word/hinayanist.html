Hinayanist

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈnɑː.jə.nɪst / (AmE), / hɪˈnɑː.jə.nɪst / (BrE)
A follower or adherent of Hinayana Buddhism, a term used for certain early schools of Buddhism, especially as contrasted with Mahayana.
follower of Hinayana Buddhism

Examples

As a devoted Hinayanist, he emphasized personal spiritual development and discipline.
The teachings of a Hinayanist often differ from those of the Mahayana tradition.
She wrote her thesis on the philosophical differences between Mahayana and Hinayanist practices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Mahayana - one of the two major branches of Buddhism, contrasted with Hinayana

Etymology

From Sanskrit hīnayāna (lesser vehicle) + -ist.
