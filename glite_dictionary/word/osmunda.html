Osmunda

1. NOUN

/ ɑsˈmʌndə / (AmE), / ɒsˈmʌndə / (BrE)
A genus of ferns that is the type genus of the family Osmundaceae, including species such as the royal fern.
genus of ferns (Osmundaceae family)

Examples

The Osmunda genus includes several species of ferns found in wetlands.
Botanists studied the unique reproductive structures of Osmunda for insights into fern evolution.
The royal fern, a member of Osmunda, is valued for its ornamental foliage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Osmundaceae - the family of ferns to which Osmunda belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'Osmunda', used in botanical nomenclature; possibly from the name of a Saxon god of war.
