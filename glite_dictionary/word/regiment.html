regiment

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdʒəmənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʒɪmənt / (BrE)
a military unit that is smaller than a division and can be divided into several companies, squadrons, or batteries, typically commanded by a colonel
military unit smaller than a division

Examples

The army deployed an entire regiment to secure the border region.
His grandfather served in the Highland Light Infantry regiment during World War II.
Each new recruit was assigned to a specific regiment based on their skills and training.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: battalion - military unit typically forming part of a regiment
  • Degree Higher: division - large military unit usually consisting of several regiments or brigades

Etymology

From Latin 'regimentum' (rule, control) via Old French 'regiment' (government, control, military unit).

/ ˈrɛdʒəˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʒɪmənt / (BrE)
to assign or organize (soldiers) into a regiment; to form into a regiment as part of a military unit.

Examples

During wartime, it is common to regiment large groups of volunteers into structured units.
