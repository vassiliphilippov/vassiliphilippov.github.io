blackleg

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌlɛg / (AmE), / ˈblak.lɛg / (BrE)
a person who continues to work or is employed in place of others during a strike; a strikebreaker
person who works during a strike

Examples

Several workers accused him of being a blackleg when he refused to join the strike.
During the dispute, the company hired blacklegs to keep production going.
Calling someone a blackleg was considered a serious insult among the striking miners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scab - a person who works or is employed in place of others who are on strike; strikebreaker

Etymology

Originally British; possibly from the custom of labeling or shaming strikebreakers (19th century).

2. VERB

/ ˈblækˌlɛg / (AmE), / ˈblæk.lɛɡ / (BrE)
(of a person) to work or take the place of someone who is on strike, especially as a replacement worker.
work as a replacement during a strike
informal british

Examples

Workers refused to blackleg despite pressure from management.
He was criticized by the union for choosing to blackleg during the strike.
Few people were willing to blackleg, fearing backlash from their colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scab - a person who works or replaces others during a strike

Etymology

Originally British; related to 'blackleg' (noun) for a person who violates strike action.
