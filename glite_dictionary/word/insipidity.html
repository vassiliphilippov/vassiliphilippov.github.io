insipidity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.sɪˈpɪdəti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈpɪdəti / (BrE)
The quality of being dull, bland, or lacking any distinctive or interesting taste, flavor, or quality.
dullness; lack of distinctive taste or interest

Examples

The insipidity of the soup disappointed everyone at the dinner table.
Her speech was criticized for its insipidity and lack of passion.
Many found the film's insipidity made it forgettable despite a promising cast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insipid - lacking flavor; dull or uninteresting

Etymology

From Medieval Latin insipidus (tasteless, dull) + English -ity.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.səˈpɪd.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈpɪd.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
extreme dullness or lack of spirit, interest, or flavor; the state of being insipid.
lack of spirit or interest

Examples

The insipidity of the lecture made it hard to stay awake.
Critics complained about the insipidity of the new movie’s script.
She grew tired of the insipidity in his daily conversations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insipidness - the state or quality of being insipid

Etymology

From Late Latin insipiditas, from insipidus (tasteless).
