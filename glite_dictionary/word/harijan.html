Harijan

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːriˌdʒɑːn / (AmE), / ˈhɑːrɪˌdʒæn / (BrE)
(historical, sometimes offensive) a member of the lowest social and ritual caste in India, as formerly described by Mahatma Gandhi; now regarded as outdated and sometimes offensive.
member of lowest caste in India (historical)
offensive

Examples

The term Harijan was popularized by Gandhi to describe those considered untouchable in Indian society.
Many Harijans have fought for equality and recognition throughout India's history.
Today, the word Harijan is largely avoided in favor of less stigmatized terms like 'Dalit'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dalit - a term for members of the lowest caste in India

Etymology

Coined by Mahatma Gandhi from Hindi 'har' (God) + 'jan' (person), meaning 'people of God'.
