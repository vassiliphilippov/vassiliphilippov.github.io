Asclepias exaltata

1. NOUN

/ æsˈkliːpiəs ɪgˌzɒltˈeɪtə / (AmE), / æsˈkliːpiəs ɪgˌzæltˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A milkweed species native to the eastern United States, with leaves that resemble those of pokeweed.
a species of milkweed plant in eastern US

Examples

The Asclepias exaltata grows well in shaded woodlands and attracts many pollinators.
Gardeners often plant Asclepias exaltata to support monarch butterfly populations.
The leaves of Asclepias exaltata are easily mistaken for those of pokeweed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkweed - a group of plants in the genus Asclepias known for their milky sap

Etymology

From Latin, Asclepias named after Asclepius, the Greek god of medicine; exaltata meaning 'lofty' or 'exalted.'
