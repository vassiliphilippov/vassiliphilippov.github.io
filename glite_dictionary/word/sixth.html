sixth

1. ADJ

/ sɪksθ / (AmE), / sɪksθ / (BrE)
coming next after the fifth in a sequence; the ordinal number matching the number six
ordinal number after fifth

Examples

She finished in sixth place at the competition.
The class is learning about fractions, and today they discussed what a sixth of something is.
His birthday is on the 6th of March.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fifth - coming after the fourth in a sequence
  • Degree Higher: seventh - coming after the sixth in a sequence

Etymology

From Middle English 'sixte,' from Old English 'sixta,' from 'six' (six).

3. NOUN

/ sɪksθ / (AmE), / sɪksθ / (BrE)
a musical interval spanning six consecutive notes in a standard diatonic scale; the difference in pitch between the first and sixth note.
musical interval of six scale degrees

Examples

The melody leaps a sixth at the start of the chorus.
She struggled to sing the sixth because of its wide span.
Composers often use a sixth to create a distinctive emotional effect.

Related Terms

5. NOUN

/ sɪksθ / (AmE), / sɪksθ / (BrE)

Examples

This is the sixth time we've met this month.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: seventh - the position number seven in a sequence
