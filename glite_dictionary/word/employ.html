employ

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈplɔɪ / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɔɪ / (BrE)
the state of being employed or having a job, especially formal or literary
state of being employed

Examples

He had spent many years in the employ of the royal household.
After the company bankrupt, hundreds were left without employ.
The secret agent disappeared while in the employ of the government.

Synset Examples

they are looking for employment
he was in the employ of the city

Related Terms

  • Synonym: employment - the condition of having paid work
  • Antonym: unemployment - the state of not having a job

Etymology

From Old French *employer*, from Latin *implicare* (to involve, engage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13991539-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈplɔɪ / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɔɪ / (BrE)
to give work to someone and pay them for it; to hire for a job or position
hire or give work to

Examples

The company will employ 100 new workers this year.
She was employed as an accountant right after graduation.
Many businesses employ extra staff during the holiday season.

Synset Examples

They hired two new secretaries in the department
How many people has she employed?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hire - to give someone a job or position, usually temporarily
  • Antonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from their job; to fire

Etymology

From Old French *emploier* (to use, employ), from Latin *implicare* (to enfold, involve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02414542-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪmˈplɔɪ / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɔɪ / (BrE)
to make use of something for a particular purpose; to use or utilize
to use for a purpose

Examples

The scientist decided to employ a new technique to solve the problem.
Many artists employ bold colors to express emotion in their paintings.
We should employ our resources more efficiently to achieve better results.

Synset Examples

use your head!
we only use Spanish at home

This thinking was applied to many projects
How do you utilize this tool?
I apply this rule to get good results
use the plastic bags to store the food
He doesn't know how to use a computer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: use - to put into service or action
  • Synonym: to utilize - to make practical use of

Etymology

From Old French *employer*, from Latin *implicare* (to involve, entwine, employ).
