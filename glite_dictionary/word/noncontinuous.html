noncontinuous

1. ADJ

/ nɑn.kənˈtɪn.ju.əs / (AmE), / nɒn.kənˈtɪn.ju.əs / (BrE)
not continuing without interruption in time or space; not continuous
not continuous; having interruptions

Examples

The noncontinuous rainfall made it difficult to plan outdoor activities.
His education was noncontinuous due to frequent moves between cities.
A noncontinuous pattern appeared on the fabric, broken by lines and spaces.

Synset Examples

discontinuous applause
the landscape was a discontinuous mosaic of fields and forest areas
he received a somewhat haphazard and discontinuous schooling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discontinuous - not continuous; having breaks or interruptions
  • Antonym: continuous - without interruption; unbroken

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'non-' (not) and 'continuous' (from Latin 'continuus', unbroken).
