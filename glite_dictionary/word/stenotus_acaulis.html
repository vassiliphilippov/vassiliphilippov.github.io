Stenotus acaulis

1. NOUN

/ stoʊˈnoʊtəs əˈkaʊlɪs / (AmE), / stɛˈnəʊtəs əˈkaʊlɪs / (BrE)
A dark green, erect herb native to the northwestern United States and southwestern Canada, with stiff leaves in dense tufts and yellow flower heads; sometimes classified as belonging to the genus Haplopappus.
herbaceous plant native to NW US and SW Canada

Examples

Stenotus acaulis thrives in rocky, open areas across the western states, displaying clusters of bright yellow flowers.
The botanist identified the small tufts as Stenotus acaulis during her field survey in Montana.
Many gardeners appreciate Stenotus acaulis for its drought tolerance and sturdy foliage.

Etymology

From Greek 'stenos' (narrow) and Latin 'acaulis' (stemless), referring to plant features.
