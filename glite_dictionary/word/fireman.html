fireman

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəmən / (BrE)
A man employed to tend and maintain fires, especially one who manages the boiler fire on a steam locomotive, ship, or other steam-powered machine.
worker tending fires on steam machinery

Examples

The fireman shoveled coal into the locomotive's furnace to keep the train running.
Each fireman on the steamship worked long shifts in the engine room.
In the age of steam engines, being a fireman was a hot and demanding job.

Etymology

From 'fire' + 'man', originally denoting a man who tends a fire, later applied to steam engines.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəmæn / (BrE)
A man whose job is to put out fires and rescue people from dangerous situations, especially as a member of a fire department.
male firefighter

Examples

The fireman bravely entered the burning building to rescue the trapped family.
A retired fireman spoke to children at the local school about fire safety.
Several firemen worked together to control the wildfire in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firefighter - a person whose job is to put out fires

Etymology

From 'fire' + 'man', referring to the historical male-dominated role in firefighting.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚmən / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəmən / (BrE)
(Baseball) A relief pitcher, especially one called into a game in a difficult situation to protect a lead.
baseball relief pitcher

Examples

The team's veteran fireman was called in during the ninth inning to close out the game.
When the bases were loaded, the coach signaled for his best fireman to take the mound.
Many teams rely on a trusted fireman to rescue them from late-inning trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relief pitcher - a pitcher brought in to replace another, typically in later innings

Etymology

From 'fireman' (person fighting fires), likening coming into 'put out' a dangerous baseball situation.
