vacuousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvækjuəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈvakjʊəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being empty, especially of meaning, intelligence, or ideas
emptiness of thought or meaning

Examples

The vacuousness of the conversation made the meeting feel pointless and dull.
Critics often complain about the vacuousness of reality television shows.
Her speech revealed a surprising vacuousness of thought and conviction.

Synset Examples

the vacuousness of her face belied her feelings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emptiness - the state of containing nothing; lack of meaning or content
  • Antonym: depth - intellectual or emotional profundity, opposite of superficiality or emptiness

Etymology

From Latin vacuus (empty) via vacuous.
