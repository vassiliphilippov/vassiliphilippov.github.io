aerology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛərəˈlɑdʒi / (AmE), / ˌɛə.rəˈlɒdʒ.i / (BrE)
the branch of meteorology that deals with the study of the entire atmosphere, especially its upper layers
study of the total atmosphere
medical

Examples

Modern aerology uses advanced instruments to measure conditions in the upper atmosphere.
The research team specializes in aerology, focusing on weather patterns above the troposphere.
Courses in aerology are crucial for understanding global climate change.

Etymology

From Greek 'aēr' (air) + '-logia' (study).
