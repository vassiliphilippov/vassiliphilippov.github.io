Ipomoea leptophylla

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪpoʊˈmiːə lɛptoʊˈfɪlə / (AmE), / ˌɪpəˈməʊə lɛp.təˈfɪl.ə / (BrE)
A species of morning glory native to the western United States, known for its long roots and showy purple flowers.
North American morning glory species

Examples

The Ipomoea leptophylla thrives in the sandy soils of the Great Plains.
Gardeners admire Ipomoea leptophylla for its vibrant purple flowers and resilience to drought.
Researchers are studying Ipomoea leptophylla to understand more about deep-rooted prairie plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morning glory - common name for plants in the genus Ipomoea, known for their trumpet-shaped flowers

Etymology

From the genus name Ipomoea (Greek ipos, worm, and homoios, resembling) and Latin leptophylla (having thin leaves).
