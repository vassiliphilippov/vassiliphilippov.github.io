leavening

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛvənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛv.ən.ɪŋ / (BrE)
A substance added to dough or batter to produce fermentation, causing it to rise and become light.
substance that makes dough rise

Examples

Bakers often rely on leavening agents such as yeast to produce light, airy bread.
The recipe calls for a chemical leavening to make the cake rise quickly.
Without any leavening, the dough remained dense and flat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yeast - a common leavening agent used in baking
  • Synonym: baking powder - a chemical leavening agent used in baked goods
  • Antonym: unleavened - without leavening; not risen

Etymology

From 'leaven' (Middle English, from Old French 'levain', from Latin 'levamen' meaning 'alleviation, leaven').

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛvənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛvənɪŋ / (BrE)
a subtle influence that lightens, modifies, or enlivens something
subtle influence that enlivens or modifies

Examples

Her remarks brought a welcome leavening of wit to the otherwise tedious debate.
The director's playful approach provided a leavening to the serious subject matter.
A leavening of optimism can transform even the gloomiest report.

Synset Examples

his sermons benefited from a leavening of humor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influence - the capacity to have an effect on something
  • Antonym: burden - something that weighs down or makes heavier

Etymology

From Middle English leven, from Old French levain, from Latin levāmen (means of raising), from levāre (to raise).
