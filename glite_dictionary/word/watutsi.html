Watutsi

1. NOUN

/ wɑːˈtutsi / (AmE), / wəˈtʊtsi / (BrE)
A member of a Bantu-speaking ethnic group native to Rwanda and Burundi; more often known as Tutsi.
member of an ethnic group in Rwanda and Burundi

Examples

The Watutsi have a long history and cultural influence in the Great Lakes region of Africa.
Decades of conflict have deeply affected the lives of the Watutsi population.
Anthropologists have studied the traditions and social structures of the Watutsi people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tutsi - member of the Tutsi ethnic group in Rwanda and Burundi

Etymology

Variant of 'Tutsi', an indigenous name of the people.
