market

1. VERB

/ ˈmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
to make something commercial; to make available or promote for sale in a market
to make commercial or promote for sale

Examples

The company plans to market its new line of organic foods in Europe.
Some small towns have begun to market their local traditions to attract tourists.
She helped the farmers market their produce directly to consumers.

Synset Examples

Some Amish people have commercialized their way of life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commercialize - to make commercial in character; to exploit for profit

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
The collective system of securities exchanges where stocks, bonds, and other financial instruments are bought and sold.
system of securities exchanges

Examples

Many analysts believe the market will rebound after the recent downturn.
She follows the market closely to make informed investment decisions.
The market often reacts unpredictably to global events.

Synset Examples

the market always frustrates the small investor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock exchange - A place where stocks and other securities are traded

Etymology

From Old North French 'market', from Latin 'mercatus' (trade, market, fair).

Examples
