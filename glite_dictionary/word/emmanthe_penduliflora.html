Emmanthe penduliflora

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛmˈænθiː ˌpɛndjʊlˈflɔːrə / (AmE), / ˌɛmˈænθiː ˌpɛndjʊlˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A sticky herb found in arid or desert habitats of the southwestern United States, bearing drooping yellow flowers.
herb with drooping yellow flowers, southwestern U.S.
medical

Examples

Emmanthe penduliflora thrives in sandy soils of the southwestern deserts.
The pendulous yellow blossoms of Emmanthe penduliflora are often seen after rare desert rains.
Botanists studied Emmanthe penduliflora to understand its adaptation to arid conditions.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining the genus Emmanthe and species epithet penduliflora (meaning 'with hanging flowers').
