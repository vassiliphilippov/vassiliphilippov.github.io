to impel

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpɛl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛl / (BrE)
to urge or drive someone to take action, especially by strong moral pressure or necessity
to drive or urge to action

Examples

A sense of duty impelled her to volunteer for the difficult assignment.
His curiosity impelled him to ask more questions about the mysterious discovery.
The urgent message impelled the team to take immediate action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compel - to force someone to do something
  • Antonym: to restrain - to hold back or keep under control

Etymology

From Latin impellere, from in- (towards) + pellere (to drive).

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈpɛl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛl / (BrE)
to drive, force, or urge someone or something to move forward or to take action
drive or urge forward

Examples

Strong currents impel the boat down the river.
Her conscience impelled her to confess the truth.
The desire for justice impelled many people to join the protest.

Synset Examples

Steam propels this ship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to propel - to drive or push something forwards
  • Synonym: to compel - to force someone to do something

Etymology

From Latin impellere (to drive on), formed from in- (in, onwards) + pellere (to drive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01514338-v
Synset POS: v
