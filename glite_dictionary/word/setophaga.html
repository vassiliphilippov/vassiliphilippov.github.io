Setophaga

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈtɑf.ə.ɡə / (AmE), / sɛˈtɒf.ə.ɡə / (BrE)
A genus of small songbirds in the family Parulidae, commonly known as New World warblers, containing more than 30 species.
genus of New World warblers (birds)

Examples

The American redstart belongs to the genus Setophaga and is known for its striking plumage.
Many Setophaga species migrate long distances between North and South America.
Research on Setophaga has contributed to our understanding of bird migration patterns.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Parulidae - family of New World warblers

Etymology

From Greek 'setos' (bristle) and 'phagos' (eating), referring to warbler feeding habits.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛtəˈfeɪɡə / (AmE), / ˌsɛtəˈfeɪɡə / (BrE)
A genus of small songbirds in the New World warbler family Parulidae, containing species found throughout the Americas.
genus of New World warblers

Examples

The black-throated blue warbler is a member of the genus Setophaga.
Scientists have reclassified several warbler species under Setophaga based on genetic studies.
Many of the most colorful songbirds in North America belong to Setophaga.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'seta' (bristle) and 'phaga' (eating).
