Madeiras

1. PROPN

/ məˈdɛrəz / (AmE), / məˈdeərəz / (BrE)
a group of volcanic islands in the Atlantic Ocean, west of Morocco, forming an autonomous region of Portugal
volcanic islands in the Atlantic, part of Portugal

Examples

The Madeiras are known for their lush landscapes and mild climate.
Every year, thousands of tourists visit the Madeiras to enjoy hiking and wine tasting.
The autonomous government of the Madeiras maintains a distinct cultural identity within Portugal.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Madeira' meaning 'wood', named for the island's dense forests.
