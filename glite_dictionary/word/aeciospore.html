aeciospore

1. NOUN

/ iˈsiːi.oʊˌspɔr / (AmE), / iːˈsiːi.əʊˌspɔː / (BrE)
A spore produced by a rust fungus in its aecium stage.
spore produced in the aecium stage of rust fungi
medical

Examples

The aeciospore is an important reproductive unit in the life cycle of rust fungi.
After germination, the aeciospores infect the host plant's leaves.
Scientists observed the formation of aeciospores on the underside of the infected leaves.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: spore - a reproductive cell capable of developing into a new individual
  • Degree Higher: teliospore - thick-walled resting spore stage of rust fungi

Etymology

From Greek 'aix' (genitive 'aigos', goat) + '-spore,' because of their shape or association with rust fungi stages.
