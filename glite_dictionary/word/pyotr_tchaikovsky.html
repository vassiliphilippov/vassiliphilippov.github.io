Pyotr Tchaikovsky

1. PROPN

/ piˈjɔtər tʃaɪˈkɒfski / (AmE), / pɪˈɒtə tʃaɪˈkɒfski / (BrE)
(1840–1893) a Russian composer known for his expressive melodies, including ballets such as Swan Lake and The Nutcracker.
Russian composer (1840–1893)

Examples

The symphonies of Pyotr Tchaikovsky are celebrated for their emotional intensity and vivid melodies.
During the concert, the orchestra performed a suite from Pyotr Tchaikovsky's The Nutcracker.
Many ballet companies stage the works of Pyotr Tchaikovsky every holiday season.

Etymology

Anglicized form of Russian Пётр Ильи́ч Чайко́вский (Pyotr Ilyich Chaykovsky).
