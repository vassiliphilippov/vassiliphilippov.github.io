Lyginopteridales

1. NOUN

/ laɪˌdʒɪnəptəˈraɪliːz / (AmE), / lɪˌdʒɪnəptəˈrɪːliːz / (BrE)
an extinct order of fossil seed ferns that were gymnospermous trees or climbing plants from the Devonian period
order of extinct fossil seed ferns

Examples

Fossils of the Lyginopteridales provide important evidence about the evolution of seed plants.
Many of the Lyginopteridales had a climbing habit and flourished during the late Paleozoic era.
The structure of seeds in Lyginopteridales shows features intermediate between ferns and true gymnosperms.

Etymology

From New Latin Lyginopteridales, based on genus Lyginopteris + Greek pteris (fern) + -ales (order suffix).
