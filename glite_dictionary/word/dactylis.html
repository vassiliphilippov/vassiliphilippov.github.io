Dactylis

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæk.tɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈdæk.tɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
a genus of perennial grasses in the family Poaceae, commonly known as orchard grass or cocksfoot, characterized by tufted clumps and widely used as forage.
a genus of perennial grasses (orchard grass)

Examples

Dactylis is widely cultivated for use as livestock forage in temperate regions.
Botanists identified several clumps of Dactylis in the overgrown meadow.
The distinctive flower head of Dactylis helps distinguish it from other grasses.

Etymology

From Greek daktylos (finger), referring to the shape of the flower head.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdæk.tɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈdæk.tɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
a genus of perennial grasses in the family Gramineae, commonly known as orchard grass and typically with only one recognized species (Dactylis glomerata)
a genus of perennial grasses (orchard grass)

Examples

Dactylis is often used as a pasture grass due to its rapid growth and nutritional value.
Botanists studied the spread of Dactylis across various ecosystems and its ecological impact.
Farmers prefer Dactylis for hay production because of its resilience and high yield.

Etymology

From Greek daktylos (finger), referring to the finger-like clusters of the grass.
