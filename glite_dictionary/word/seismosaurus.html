Seismosaurus

1. NOUN

/ saɪzmoʊˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / saɪzməˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of very large, herbivorous dinosaurs from the Late Jurassic period, fossils of which were found in western North America
large herbivorous dinosaur genus

Examples

The fossilized bones of a Seismosaurus were discovered in New Mexico.
Paleontologists believe that Seismosaurus may have been among the longest dinosaurs ever to live.
The museum's exhibit on Seismosaurus attracts visitors interested in prehistoric life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Diplodocus - another large, long-necked dinosaur genus from the same period

Etymology

From Greek seismos (earthquake) + sauros (lizard); meaning 'earthquake lizard' due to its massive size.

2. PROPN

/ ˌsaɪzmoʊˈsɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˌsaɪzməʊˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A former genus of large herbivorous dinosaurs from the Cretaceous period, found in western North America, now generally classified under Diplodocus.
former dinosaur genus from North America

Examples

The skeleton of Seismosaurus exhibited at the museum fascinated visitors of all ages.
Paleontologists once believed Seismosaurus was among the longest dinosaurs ever discovered.
Recent studies suggest Seismosaurus should be reclassified as a species of Diplodocus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Diplodocus - a genus of large herbivorous dinosaurs related to Seismosaurus

Etymology

From Greek 'seismos' (earthquake) + 'sauros' (lizard); meaning 'earthquake lizard.'
