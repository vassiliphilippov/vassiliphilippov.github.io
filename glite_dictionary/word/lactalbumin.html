lactalbumin

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæktælˈbjumɪn / (AmE), / ˌlaktalˈbjuːmɪn / (BrE)
a type of albumin protein found in milk, especially in whey
milk protein (albumin)
medical

Examples

Whey is a rich source of lactalbumin in dairy products.
Some people are allergic to lactalbumin found in cow's milk.
Scientists studied the properties of lactalbumin during food processing experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albumin - a class of simple water-soluble proteins

Etymology

From Latin 'lac' (milk) and 'albumen' (egg white, albumin).
