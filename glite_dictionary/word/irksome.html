irksome

1. ADJ

/ ˈɝːk.səm / (AmE), / ˈɜːk.səm / (BrE)
causing annoyance, irritation, or boredom; tedious or wearisome.
annoying or tedious

Examples

Filling out endless forms can be an irksome chore.
He found his colleague's questions increasingly irksome during the meeting.
Waiting in the long line proved to be an irksome experience.

Synset Examples

a boring evening with uninteresting people
the deadening effect of some routine tasks
a dull play
his competent but dull performance
a ho-hum speaker who couldn't capture their attention
what an irksome task the writing of long letters is
tedious days on the train
the tiresome chirping of a cricket
other people's dreams are dreadfully wearisome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tedious - too long, slow, or dull; tiresome or monotonous
  • Synonym: wearisome - causing one to feel tired or bored
  • Antonym: entertaining - providing amusement or enjoyment

Etymology

From Middle English 'irk', from Old Norse 'yrkja' (to work, worry), plus '-some' (forming adjectives).
