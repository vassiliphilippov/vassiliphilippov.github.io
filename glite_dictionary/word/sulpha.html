sulpha

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlfə / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə / (BrE)
any of several synthetic antibacterial drugs that inhibit the growth of bacteria by interfering with the use of PABA (para-aminobenzoic acid)
synthetic antibacterial drug
medical british

Examples

After being diagnosed with a bacterial infection, the doctor prescribed a course of sulpha.
Some people experience allergic reactions to sulpha drugs and should inform their healthcare provider.
The use of sulpha paved the way for the development of more advanced antibiotics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfa - antibacterial drug; American variant spelling

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'sulfa', from 'sulfonamide', relating to the chemical structure.
