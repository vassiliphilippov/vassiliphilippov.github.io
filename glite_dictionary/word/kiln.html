kiln

1. NOUN

/ kɪln / (AmE), / kɪln / (BrE)
a furnace or oven for firing, drying, or hardening substances such as pottery, bricks, or ceramics
furnace for firing pottery or bricks

Examples

The pottery was placed in the kiln to be fired at a high temperature.
Brickmakers loaded the fresh bricks into the kiln for drying and hardening.
After glazing, each vase must be carefully baked in a kiln to finish the process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oven - a chamber used for baking, heating, or drying
  • Synonym: furnace - an enclosed structure for intense heating

Etymology

From Old English 'cyln', from Latin 'culina' (kitchen, cooking stove, kiln).
