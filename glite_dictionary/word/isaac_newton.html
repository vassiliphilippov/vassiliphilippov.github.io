Isaac Newton

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪzək ˈnuːtən / (AmE), / ˈaɪzək ˈnjuːtən / (BrE)
(1642–1726) An English mathematician, physicist, and astronomer, best known for formulating the law of gravitation and the three laws of motion.
English mathematician and physicist

Examples

Isaac Newton is considered one of the most influential scientists in history.
Many students learn about Isaac Newton's laws of motion in physics class.
The story of the apple falling supposedly inspired Isaac Newton's thinking about gravity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Galileo Galilei - Italian astronomer and physicist, an influential predecessor in physics
  • Synonym: Albert Einstein - German-born physicist, known for the theory of relativity
