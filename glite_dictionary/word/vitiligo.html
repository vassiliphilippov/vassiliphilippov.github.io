vitiligo

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪt̬.ɪˈlaɪ.ɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌvɪt.ɪˈlaɪ.ɡəʊ / (BrE)
A medical condition in which patches of skin lose their pigment, often bordered by darker skin.
loss of skin pigment in patches
medical

Examples

She was diagnosed with vitiligo after noticing several pale spots on her arms.
People with vitiligo often use sunscreen to protect the affected areas.
His vitiligo became more noticeable during the summer months.

Etymology

From Latin 'vitiligo' (a type of skin blemish or eruption).
