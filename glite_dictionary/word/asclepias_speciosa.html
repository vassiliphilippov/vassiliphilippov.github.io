Asclepias speciosa

1. NOUN

/ əˈskliːpiəs spəˈsiːoʊsə / (AmE), / əˈskliːpɪəs spəˈsiːəsə / (BrE)
A species of milkweed native to southern North America, known for its large star-shaped purple and pink flowers.
a milkweed plant of southern North America

Examples

Asclepias speciosa is often planted in gardens to attract monarch butterflies.
The large, star-shaped flowers of Asclepias speciosa make it a popular choice among native plant enthusiasts.
Scientists studied Asclepias speciosa to understand its role in supporting pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkweed - a plant of the genus Asclepias

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Asclepias' after Asclepius, the Greek god of medicine, and species 'speciosa' meaning 'showy' in Latin.
