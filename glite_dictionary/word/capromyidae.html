Capromyidae

1. NOUN

/ kæˌproʊˈmɪɪdiː / (AmE), / kæˌprəʊˈmɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, mainly herbivorous rodents native to the Caribbean, commonly known as hutias.
family of Caribbean rodents (hutias)

Examples

The Capromyidae are primarily distributed throughout the Caribbean islands.
Scientists study various species in the Capromyidae family to learn about island evolution.
Several members of the Capromyidae are threatened due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hutia - any rodent of the family Capromyidae, native to the Caribbean

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Capromys (from Greek kapros ‘boar’ + mys ‘mouse’).
