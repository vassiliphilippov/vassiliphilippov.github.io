Vaccinium caespitosum

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm sɛsˈpɪtəsəm / (AmE), / vækˈsɪniəm siːˈspɪtəsəm / (BrE)
A low-growing, tufted deciduous shrub native to northern and alpine regions of North America, with pink to coral-red flowers and sweet blue berries.
a low-growing North American shrub with blue berries

Examples

The berries of the Vaccinium caespitosum are sweet and enjoyed by many birds and mammals.
During our hike, we spotted several Vaccinium caespitosum shrubs growing along the mountain trail.
Botanists are interested in studying the pollination of Vaccinium caespitosum due to its unique alpine habitat.

Etymology

From Latin: Vaccinium (a genus of shrubs, originally applied to the bilberry) + caespitosum (clumped, tufted).
