packaging

1. NOUN

/ ˈpækɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpækɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the business or process of preparing and wrapping goods for transport, sale, or storage
business of preparing and wrapping goods

Examples

He started a company specializing in packaging for electronics manufacturers.
The packaging industry is growing rapidly due to increased demand for online shopping.
Consultants advised them on how to streamline their packaging operations.

Synset Examples

his business is packaging for transport

Related Terms

  • Synonym: packing - the action or process of packing things into packages

Etymology

From 'package' + '-ing', first used in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpækɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpækɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
material used to enclose or protect products for storage, transportation, or sale
material for wrapping or protecting goods

Examples

The company is working to reduce the amount of packaging used for its products.
Plastic packaging can be difficult to recycle and often ends up in landfills.
Many shoppers prefer eco-friendly packaging made from recycled materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrapping - material used to cover or enclose something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03877202-n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpækɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpækɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the way in which something, such as an idea, product, or person, is presented or promoted to the public
presentation or promotion of something

Examples

The company's clever packaging of its new service attracted many customers.
Politicians often focus on the packaging of their ideas to gain public support.
The success of the campaign was due to the creative packaging of a complex issue.

Synset Examples

the packaging of new ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: presentation - the way in which something is shown or arranged for others to see
  • Synonym: promotion - the act of raising awareness or encouraging acceptance of something

Etymology

From package + -ing, originally referring to wrapping goods, later extended metaphorically.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07261731-n
Synset POS: n
