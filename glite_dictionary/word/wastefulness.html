wastefulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪstfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈweɪstfʊlnəs / (BrE)
the quality or habit of using resources carelessly or inefficiently, resulting in unnecessary loss or expense
the quality of wasting resources

Examples

His constant wastefulness led to higher utility bills every month.
The report criticized the government's wastefulness with taxpayer money.
Many organizations want to reduce wastefulness by promoting recycling and energy efficiency.

Synset Examples

a life characterized by thriftlessness and waste
the wastefulness of missed opportunities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thriftlessness - the lack of care in managing resources or money, leading to waste
  • Antonym: frugality - the quality of being careful with resources, avoiding waste

Etymology

From 'wasteful' + '-ness'; ultimately from Middle English 'wasten', from Old North French 'waster'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪst.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈweɪst.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of using resources carelessly, extravagantly, or without purpose, resulting in unnecessary loss or expense
careless or excessive use of resources

Examples

His wastefulness with money quickly led him into debt.
The company's wastefulness was evident in their disregard for recycling and energy conservation.
Many governments are criticized for the wastefulness of certain public spending programs.

Synset Examples

if the effort brings no compensating gain, it is a waste
mindless dissipation of natural resources

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thriftiness - the quality of using resources carefully and not wastefully
  • Synonym: extravagance - the quality of spending or using resources excessively

Etymology

From 'wasteful' + '-ness', forming a noun to denote the quality of being wasteful.
