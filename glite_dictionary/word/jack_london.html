Jack London

1. PROPN

/ dʒæk ˈlʌn.dən / (AmE), / dʒæk ˈlʌn.dən / (BrE)
(1876–1916) An American writer famous for novels based on his experiences in the Klondike gold rush, such as 'The Call of the Wild.'
American novelist (1876–1916), author of adventure stories

Examples

Jack London's stories often focus on survival in harsh environments.
Many critics consider Jack London one of the foremost American adventure writers.
She wrote her thesis on the influence of Jack London on twentieth-century literature.
