chiasmatic

1. ADJ

/ kaɪˌæzˈmætɪk / (AmE), / kaɪˌæzˈmatɪk / (BrE)
relating to a chiasm, especially the crossing of nerve fibers in anatomy
relating to a chiasm (anatomy)
medical

Examples

The chiasmatic region is critical for the crossing of optic nerve fibers.
Tumors in the chiasmatic area can affect vision.
Researchers examined changes in the chiasmatic structure following surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chiasmal - relating to a chiasm

Etymology

From chiasm (Greek 'khiasma', a crossing) + -atic (suffix forming adjectives).
