mutation

1. NOUN

/ mjuˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / mjuːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a living organism with altered characteristics resulting from a change in its chromosomes, often passed to offspring
organism with genetic alteration

Examples

The scientist studied the effects of radiation on a mutation that displayed unusual traits.
A rare mutation in mice caused them to have white fur instead of brown.
The mutation exhibited resistance to certain diseases, making it valuable for research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutant - organism resulting from genetic mutation

Etymology

From Latin mutatio (a changing), from mutare (to change).

2. NOUN

/ mjuˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / mjuːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a permanent alteration in the genetic material of an organism, often resulting in a change of traits or characteristics
change in genetic material
medical

Examples

Radiation can cause a mutation in an organism’s DNA, leading to unexpected traits.
The scientist studied the mutation that made the bacteria resistant to antibiotics.
Some genetic disorders are the result of a single mutation in the genome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variation - a change or slight difference in condition, amount, or level
  • Antonym: stability - the quality of being unchanging or resistant to change

Etymology

From Latin mutatio (a changing), from mutare (to change).

3. NOUN

/ mjuˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / mjuːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a change or alteration in form or qualities, especially one that is significant or affects the characteristics of something
a change in form or qualities

Examples

The scientist observed a mutation in the bacteria after exposing them to radiation.
The sudden mutation of traditional customs shocked the older generation.
Some genetic diseases arise from a single mutation in the DNA sequence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modification - the act or process of changing something slightly
  • Antonym: stability - the state of being stable and unchanging

Etymology

From Latin 'mutatio' (a changing), from 'mutare' (to change).
