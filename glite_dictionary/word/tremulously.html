tremulously

1. ADV

/ ˈtrɛmjələsli / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmjʊləsli / (BrE)
in a way that shows nervousness, shakiness, or lacks confidence
in a shaky or nervous manner

Examples

Her voice quivered tremulously as she began her speech.
The candlelight flickered tremulously in the darkened room.
He waited tremulously for the test results, unable to sit still.

Synset Examples

the leaves rustled tremulously in the wind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremulous - shaking or quivering slightly; timid or nervous

Etymology

From Latin 'tremulus' (shaking, trembling) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
