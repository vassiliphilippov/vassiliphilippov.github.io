Physalis alkekengi

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈseɪlɪs ˌæl.kəˈkɛn.dʒaɪ / (AmE), / fɪˈseɪlɪs ˌæl.kɪˈkɛn.dʒiː / (BrE)
An Old World perennial plant cultivated for its ornamental, inflated, papery orange-red calyx, also known as the Chinese lantern plant.
ornamental plant with inflated orange-red calyx

Examples

The brilliant orange papery pods of Physalis alkekengi add a decorative touch to autumn arrangements.
Gardeners appreciate Physalis alkekengi for its unusual lantern-like calyxes.
After flowering, Physalis alkekengi develops bright orange husks that resemble tiny lanterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinese lantern plant - common English name for Physalis alkekengi

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Physalis' (from Greek physalis, meaning 'bladder') and the species name 'alkekengi' (from Arabic al-kakengi, from Persian kâkengî, a kind of plant).
