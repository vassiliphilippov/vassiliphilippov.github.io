wampum

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːmpəm / (AmE), / ˈwɒmpəm / (BrE)
(informal) Money or cash; an old-fashioned slang term for currency.
old-fashioned slang for money
informal slang

Examples

I don't have enough wampum to buy a new phone right now.
He hopes to earn some extra wampum working over the weekend.
They pooled their wampum to pay for the concert tickets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dough - slang for money
  • Synonym: bread - slang for money
  • Synonym: cash - money in the form of bills or coins

Etymology

From the Massachusett word *wampumpeag*, originally referring to shell beads used as currency by Native Americans.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːmpəm / (AmE), / ˈwɒmpəm / (BrE)
small cylindrical beads made from polished shells, traditionally used by certain Native American peoples as jewelry, ceremonial gifts, or currency
Native American shell beads for jewelry or currency

Examples

The chief wore a belt decorated with finely crafted wampum.
Early settlers sometimes traded goods in exchange for wampum from local tribes.
She admired the intricate patterns made with purple and white wampum shells.

Etymology

From the Massachusett (Eastern Algonquian) word *wampumpeag*, meaning 'white shell beads'.
