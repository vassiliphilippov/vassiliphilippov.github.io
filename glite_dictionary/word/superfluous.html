superfluous

1. ADJ

/ suˈpɝːfluəs / (AmE), / suˈpɜːfluəs / (BrE)
being more than is needed or serving no useful purpose; unnecessary
not needed; unnecessary

Examples

He packed his suitcase, removing any superfluous items to save space.
The report was full of superfluous details that distracted from the main findings.
Please keep your comments brief and avoid any superfluous information.

Synset Examples

otiose lines in a play
advice is wasted words
a pointless remark
a life essentially purposeless
senseless violence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redundant - not or no longer needed or useful; superfluous
  • Antonym: necessary - needed to be done, achieved, or present; essential

Etymology

From Latin superfluus (overflowing, unnecessary), from super- (over) + fluere (to flow).

2. ADJ

/ suˈpɜrfluəs / (AmE), / suːˈpɜːfluəs / (BrE)
unnecessary, especially through being more than enough; exceeding what is needed, desired, or required
unnecessary; more than enough

Examples

The report was filled with superfluous details that distracted from the main findings.
You can remove any superfluous words to make your writing clearer.
Buying new furniture seemed superfluous since the house was already fully furnished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redundant - not or no longer needed or useful; superfluous
  • Synonym: unnecessary - not needed or required
  • Antonym: essential - absolutely necessary; extremely important

Etymology

From Latin superfluus (overflowing, excessive), from super- (over) + fluere (to flow).
