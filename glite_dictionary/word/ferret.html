ferret

1. VERB

/ ˈfɛrət / (AmE), / ˈfɛrət / (BrE)
to hunt rabbits or other small animals by using ferrets to drive them from their burrows
to hunt using ferrets

Examples

They used trained dogs and a ferret to flush out rabbits from the field.
In the old days, hunters would often ferret for rabbits in rural England.
He learned how to ferret with his grandfather on weekends.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛrət / (AmE), / ˈfɛrɪt / (BrE)
a small domesticated mammal of the weasel family, typically an albino variety of the European polecat, bred to hunt rats and rabbits
domesticated mammal used for hunting rodents

Examples

A ferret can be trained to help catch rabbits on farms.
Their family keeps a white ferret as a lively pet.
The ferret squeezed through the burrow to flush out the rats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polecat - a wild European mammal related to the ferret

Etymology

From Old French *furet*, diminutive of *fur*, from Late Latin *furo* (a ferret).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɛrət / (AmE), / ˈfɛrət / (BrE)
a small carnivorous mammal of the weasel family, native to prairie regions of the United States and now nearly extinct in the wild
prairie mustelid mammal, nearly extinct

Examples

The black-footed ferret is one of the most endangered mammals in North America.
Researchers tracked the movements of several ferrets released into the wild.
Conservation programs are working hard to save the prairie ferret from extinction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polecat - a type of mustelid mammal related to the ferret

Etymology

From Old French *furet*, diminutive of *fur* (thief), from Latin *fur* (thief), likely in reference to its reputation for hunting rodents.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02446115-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈfɛr.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɛr.ɪt / (BrE)
to search for and discover information or something hidden, often with determination and persistence
search for and discover persistently

Examples

The journalist managed to ferret out some incriminating evidence.
It took hours to ferret through the documents for the right forms.
She always loves to ferret out family secrets during reunions.

Synset Examples

She ferreted out the truth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - to look for something
  • Synonym: hunt - to seek out or look for diligently

Etymology

From the noun 'ferret' (an animal used to drive out rabbits), from Old French 'furet', related to searching out.

5. VERB

/ ˈfɛrət / (AmE), / ˈfɛrət / (BrE)
to search for something persistently and often with great determination, especially to uncover information or objects that are hidden
search persistently; seek out

Examples

She managed to ferret out some old family photographs from the attic.
The journalist tried to ferret the truth out of the evasive politician.
He ferreted among the documents until he found the missing contract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunt - to search for or pursue something
  • Synonym: to harry - to persistently attack or harass

Etymology

From the practice of using a ferret (the animal) to flush out rabbits from their burrows.
