Hakka

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑkə / (AmE), / ˈhækə / (BrE)
a member of an ethnic Han Chinese group originating from northern China and now living mainly in southeastern China, Taiwan, and other regions
member of a Han Chinese ethnic group

Examples

Many Hakka settled in Taiwan during the Qing dynasty.
The Hakka community is known for preserving its unique dialect and traditions.
A large number of Hakka can also be found in Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Chinese (Mandarin) Kèjiā (客家), meaning 'guest families.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːkə / (AmE), / ˈhækə / (BrE)
a Chinese dialect spoken in southeastern China and by Hakka communities worldwide
Chinese dialect spoken by the Hakka

Examples

Many elderly people in the region still speak Hakka as their first language.
The film features characters switching between Mandarin and Hakka throughout the story.
He is studying Hakka to better communicate with his grandparents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cantonese - a major Chinese dialect spoken in Guangdong and Hong Kong
  • Synonym: Mandarin - the official language of China, also a major Chinese dialect

Etymology

From Chinese (Mandarin) Kèjiā (客家), meaning 'guest families.'
