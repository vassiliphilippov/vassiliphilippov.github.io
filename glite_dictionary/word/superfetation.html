superfetation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːpərfiˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəfiˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the fertilization of a second ovum during an existing pregnancy, resulting in two fetuses of different gestational ages in the uterus at the same time
fertilization during existing pregnancy
medical

Examples

Superfetation is extremely rare in humans but can occur in some animal species.
The veterinarian explained that the twins were the result of superfetation.
Scientists are studying the biological factors that make superfetation possible in certain mammals.

Synset Examples

superfetation is normal in some animal species

Etymology

From Latin superfetatio, from superfetare (to conceive in addition).
