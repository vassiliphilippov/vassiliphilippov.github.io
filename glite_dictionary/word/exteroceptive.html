exteroceptive

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛk.stə.roʊˈsɛp.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌɛk.stərəʊˈsɛp.tɪv / (BrE)
Relating to the perception of external stimuli by the senses, such as touch, sight, or hearing.
relating to external sensory perception

Examples

The study examined how exteroceptive signals influence human decision-making.
Infants rely heavily on exteroceptive cues such as sounds and lights to learn about their environment.
Damage to certain nerves can affect exteroceptive abilities like detecting temperature or pressure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interoceptive - relating to internal bodily perception (interoception)

Etymology

From exteroception, from Latin 'exterus' (outside) + 'ception' (perception).
