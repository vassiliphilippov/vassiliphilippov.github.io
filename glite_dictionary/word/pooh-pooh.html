to pooh-pooh

1. VERB

/ puː ˈpuː / (AmE), / puː ˈpuː / (BrE)
to express disdain or dismissive contempt for something as unworthy of consideration
dismiss with contempt or disdain

Examples

He tends to pooh-pooh any suggestion that doesn't align with his own ideas.
The critics pooh-poohed her latest novel, but it became a bestseller.
She is quick to pooh-pooh new technologies she doesn't understand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to refuse to consider or accept; to reject as unworthy
  • Synonym: ridicule - to mock or make fun of; to subject to contemptuous laughter

Etymology

Imitative of a dismissive sound or interjection used to express scorn, first recorded in the early 19th century.

2. VERB

/ ˌpuːˈpuː / (AmE), / ˌpuːˈpuː / (BrE)
to dismiss or reject an idea, suggestion, or claim with contempt or mockery
dismiss with contempt or disdain

Examples

The committee was quick to pooh-pooh his innovative suggestions for budget reform.
Many critics pooh-poohed the new art movement when it first appeared.
She tends to pooh-pooh anyone's concerns unless they fit her agenda.

Synset Examples

She spurned his advances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to decide that something or someone is not worth consideration
  • Antonym: to accept - to consent to receive or approve an idea or thing

Etymology

Imitative, from the exclamation 'pooh!', expressing disdain or contempt.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00798638-v
