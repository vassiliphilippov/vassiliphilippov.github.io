Gregor Mendel

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡreɪɡər ˈmɛndəl / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪɡɔː ˈmɛndl̩ / (BrE)
(1822–1884) Austrian monk and scientist whose experiments with pea plants established the basic laws of heredity, making him the founder of modern genetics.
Austrian founder of genetics

Examples

Gregor Mendel is often called the father of modern genetics due to his pioneering work with pea plants.
The principles discovered by Gregor Mendel are still taught in biology classes around the world.
Many scientific institutions honor Gregor Mendel for his monumental contributions to the understanding of heredity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genetics - the scientific study of heredity and variation in organisms
