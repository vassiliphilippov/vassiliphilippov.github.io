Aldrovanda

1. NOUN

/ ˌældroʊˈvændə / (AmE), / ˌaldrəˈvandə / (BrE)
A genus of carnivorous aquatic plants, best known for the waterwheel plant (Aldrovanda vesiculosa).
genus of carnivorous water plants

Examples

The rare Aldrovanda is prized among botanists for its unique aquatic trapping mechanism.
Several conservation projects aim to reintroduce Aldrovanda to wetland habitats across Europe.
The Aldrovanda requires clean, nutrient-poor water to thrive in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aldrovanda vesiculosa - the waterwheel plant, the only extant species of Aldrovanda

Etymology

Named after Italian naturalist Ulisse Aldrovandi (1522–1605).

2. NOUN

/ ˌældrəˈvændə / (AmE), / ˌældrəˈvandə / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic carnivorous plants consisting of a single species, the waterwheel plant.
genus of aquatic carnivorous plants

Examples

The rare Aldrovanda is an aquatic plant that captures small insects in its underwater traps.
Botanists studied the unique trapping mechanism of Aldrovanda in its natural habitat.
Unlike many other carnivorous plants, Aldrovanda lives entirely submerged in water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterwheel plant - the single species in the genus Aldrovanda, an aquatic carnivorous plant

Etymology

Named after Italian naturalist Ulisse Aldrovandi (1522–1605).
