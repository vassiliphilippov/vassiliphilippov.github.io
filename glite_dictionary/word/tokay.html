Tokay

1. NOUN

/ toʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / təʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
A sweet Hungarian dessert wine made from Tokay grapes, especially those affected by noble rot.
sweet Hungarian dessert wine

Examples

The restaurant offered a glass of Tokay to complement the dessert course.
She bought a rare bottle of Tokay during her trip to Hungary.
Collectors prize vintage Tokay for its rich flavor and historical significance.

Etymology

From Hungarian 'Tokaji,' meaning 'of Tokaj,' a region in Hungary where the wine is produced.

2. NOUN

/ toʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / təʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
a white wine grape originally grown in Hungary, used to make Tokaji wines; also, a sweet wine made from this grape
Hungarian wine grape; type of sweet wine

Examples

The dessert was accompanied by a glass of Tokay to complement its richness.
Some connoisseurs regard Hungarian Tokay as one of the world’s finest sweet wines.
He bought several bottles of Tokay during his trip to Budapest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muscat - a family of grapes and wines known for their sweet, aromatic flavor

Etymology

From Hungarian 'Tokaji', meaning 'of Tokaj', a region in Hungary famous for its wine.
