arteria vertebralis

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˌtɪriə vɜrˌtiːˈbrælɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪəriə ˌvɜːtɪˈbrɑːlɪs / (BrE)
The vertebral artery; the first branch of the subclavian artery, supplying blood to the posterior part of the brain and divided into four parts.
vertebral artery of the neck
medical

Examples

Damage to the arteria vertebralis can lead to reduced blood flow to the brainstem.
The surgeon carefully exposed the arteria vertebralis during the neck procedure.
A narrowing of the arteria vertebralis may cause symptoms of vertebrobasilar insufficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vertebral artery - major artery of the neck that supplies blood to the brain

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'vertebral artery'.
