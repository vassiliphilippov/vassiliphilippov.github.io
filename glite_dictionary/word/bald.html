bald

1. ADJ

/ bɔld / (AmE), / bɔːld / (BrE)
having little or no hair on the scalp
having little or no hair

Examples

He started to grow bald in his early thirties.
The bald man wore a hat to protect his head from the sun.
Her father has been completely bald since he turned fifty.

Synset Examples

a bald pate
a bald-headed gentleman

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hairy - having a lot of hair
  • Degree Lower: balding - losing hair, becoming bald

Etymology

From Middle English bald, balled, probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse baldr (white patch, bald spot).

/ bɔld / (AmE), / bɔːld / (BrE)
to lose hair on the head, especially so as to become bald
lose hair on one's head

Examples

He started to bald in his early thirties.
Some men bald gradually, while others maintain their hair for longer.
Doctors are researching why some people bald more quickly than others.

Synset Examples

He is balding already

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lose hair - to experience hair loss

3. ADJ

/ bɔld / (AmE), / bɔːld / (BrE)
(of a statement or lie) presented bluntly and without any attempt to disguise or conceal the truth or falsity.
blunt, undisguised, or unconcealed (esp. statement/lie)

Examples

He told a bald lie to everyone in the meeting, not even trying to hide the truth.
The report presented the bald facts without any attempt at sugarcoating.
Her explanation was a bald excuse that fooled no one.

Synset Examples

a barefaced lie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barefaced - open and shameless; with no attempt to hide
  • Synonym: blatant - completely obvious, conspicuous, or unsubtle

4. ADJ

/ bɔld / (AmE), / bɔːld / (BrE)
lacking the natural or usual covering, especially of hair, vegetation, or another surface layer.
lacking natural covering (hair, vegetation, etc.)

Examples

The bald patch in the garden never grew back after the harsh winter.
He reached the top of a bald hill and admired the view.
Decades of heavy foot traffic left a bald spot where no grass would grow.

Synset Examples

a bald spot on the lawn
bare hills

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bare - without covering or clothing; exposed
  • Antonym: hairy - covered with hair

Etymology

From Old English 'bald' (white, bald), related to Old Norse 'baldr' (bright).

Source: WordNet
