mezzo-rilievo

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtsoʊ rɪˈljeɪvoʊ / (AmE), / ˌmɛtsə rɪˈljɛːvəʊ / (BrE)
A sculptural relief in which figures are raised more than in low relief but less than in high relief; a medium-depth relief carving.
medium-depth sculptural relief

Examples

The museum's marble mezzo-rilievo depicts mythological scenes with a pleasing sense of depth.
The artist chose mezzo-rilievo for the frieze, striking a balance between subtlety and prominence.
Restoring the damaged mezzo-rilievo required exceptional skill to preserve its intricate details.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bas-relief - low sculptural relief with slight projection from the background
  • Degree Higher: alto-rilievo - high sculptural relief in which figures project strongly from the background

Etymology

From Italian 'mezzo rilievo', meaning 'half relief'.
