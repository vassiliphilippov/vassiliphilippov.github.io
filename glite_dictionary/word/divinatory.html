divinatory

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈvɪnəˌtɔri / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪnətəri / (BrE)
based primarily on guessing or surmise rather than on sufficient evidence; relating to conjecture.
based on conjecture or guesswork

Examples

The scientist dismissed the report as purely divinatory and unsupported by data.
Many early theories about the universe were largely divinatory in nature.
His divinatory explanations of the event lacked real proof.

Synset Examples

theories about the extinction of dinosaurs are still highly conjectural
the supposed reason for his absence
suppositious reconstructions of dead languages
hypothetical situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjectural - based on guesswork rather than firm evidence
  • Synonym: suppositious - based on assumption rather than fact
  • Synonym: hypothetical - based on a suggested idea or theory

Etymology

From Latin divinatio (foretelling, prophecy).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈvɪnəˌtɔri / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪnətəri / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of divination, prophecy, or the attempt to foretell the future
relating to divination or prophecy

Examples

The priest used divinatory tools to interpret the signs before the ceremony.
She consulted a tarot reader claiming to have divinatory abilities.
The ancient text is filled with divinatory wisdom said to predict future events.

Synset Examples

the high priest's divinatory pronouncement
mantic powers
a kind of sibylline book with ready and infallible answers to questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophetic - having the characteristics of a prophecy or prophet
  • Synonym: mantic - relating to prophecy or divination
  • Antonym: mundane - relating to the ordinary or common world, not supernatural

Etymology

From Latin divinatorius (prophetic, oracular), from divinari (to foresee, foretell).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
