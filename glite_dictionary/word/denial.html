denial

1. NOUN

/ dəˈnaɪ.əl / (AmE), / dɪˈnaɪ.əl / (BrE)
the act of refusing to agree to or grant something requested
refusal to grant a request

Examples

The manager's denial of our request for more time surprised everyone.
After repeated denials of their application, they decided to appeal.
The denial of service caused widespread frustration among users.

Synset Examples

it resulted in a complete denial of his privileges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refusal - the act of declining to accept or grant something

Etymology

From Old French *denial*, from Latin *denegare* (to deny).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈnaɪ.əl / (AmE), / dɪˈnaɪ.əl / (BrE)
the act of stating that something is not true or refusing to accept something claimed or believed
the act of saying something is not true

Examples

Her constant denial of the allegations only made people more suspicious.
His denial of the facts delayed the resolution of the problem.
The company issued a denial after rumors of a merger surfaced.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: admission - the act of confessing to or acknowledging something is true
  • Synonym: rejection - the act of dismissing or refusing a proposal, idea, or statement

Etymology

From Old French 'denial', from 'deny', from Latin 'denegare' (to deny).

4. NOUN

/ dɪˈnaɪ.əl / (AmE), / dɪˈnaɪ.əl / (BrE)
In psychiatry, a defense mechanism in which a person refuses to accept reality or painful facts.
refusal to accept reality (psychiatry)
medical

Examples

His continued denial of his addiction prevented him from seeking help.
The therapist explained that denial is a common reaction to trauma.
She was in complete denial about the seriousness of her mental health condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repression - Involuntary blocking of unpleasant feelings from conscious thought (psychiatric term)
  • Antonym: acceptance - Recognizing or acknowledging reality (psychiatric/psychological context)

Etymology

From Latin 'denegare' (to deny), via Old French 'denial' (refusal).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ dɪˈnaɪ.əl / (AmE), / dɪˈnaɪ.əl / (BrE)
the act of giving up one's own interests or desires for the sake of others; self-sacrifice
giving up one's interests for others

Examples

His daily denial of comfort for the sake of his family was remarkable.
Religious traditions often emphasize denial as a path to spiritual growth.
She practiced denial by always putting her children's needs before her own.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-denial - the act of deliberately denying oneself of pleasures or interests

Etymology

From Old French *denial* (refusal), from Latin *denegare* (to refuse).
