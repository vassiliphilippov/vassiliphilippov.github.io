Macrouridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækroʊˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌmakrɔːˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of deep-sea fish known as grenadiers, characterized by their elongated bodies and large heads.
family of deep-sea fish (grenadiers)

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species in the Macrouridae family during the deep-sea expedition.
Many Macrouridae possess elongated tails, making them easily recognizable among deep-sea creatures.
The diet of most Macrouridae consists mainly of invertebrates found on the ocean floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grenadier - a common name for fish in the family Macrouridae

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Macrouridae', based on Greek 'makros' (long) + 'oura' (tail).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.rəˈraʊ.ɹɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌmak.rɔːˈrʊ.ɪ.di / (BrE)
A family of deep-sea fish known as grenadiers or rattails, characterized by their elongated bodies and tapering tails.
family of deep-sea grenadier fish

Examples

The Macrouridae are commonly found in deep-sea environments around the world.
Scientists collected several specimens of Macrouridae during the oceanographic expedition.
Many species within the Macrouridae family are studied for their adaptation to deep-water pressures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grenadier - common name for fish of the family Macrouridae

Etymology

From Greek makros (long) + oura (tail), reflecting the fish’s long tail.
