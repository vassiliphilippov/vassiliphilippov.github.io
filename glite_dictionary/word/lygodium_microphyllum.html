Lygodium microphyllum

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈɡoʊdiəm ˌmaɪkrəˈfɪləm / (AmE), / laɪˈɡəʊdiəm ˌmaɪkrəˈfɪləm / (BrE)
A species of tropical climbing fern native to the Old World, now also found in Jamaica and Florida as an invasive plant.
species of tropical climbing fern

Examples

Lygodium microphyllum has become a problematic invasive species in parts of Florida.
Scientists are studying methods to control the spread of Lygodium microphyllum in natural reserves.
The fronds of Lygodium microphyllum can grow rapidly and smother native vegetation.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek lygos (flexible twig or wood) and Latinized species epithet microphyllum, from Greek mikros (small) + phyllon (leaf).
