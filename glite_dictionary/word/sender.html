sender

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛndə / (BrE)
a device or apparatus used to transmit radio or television signals
device transmitting radio or TV signals

Examples

The sender must be powerful enough to reach distant receivers.
Technicians checked the sender for any faults before the television broadcast began.
A new sender was installed atop the building to improve radio coverage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transmitter - a device that sends out radio, television, or other signals
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04479961-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛndər / (AmE), / ˈsɛndə / (BrE)
a person or organization that sends a message, letter, or parcel to someone
person or entity sending a message or item

Examples

Please write the sender's address clearly on the envelope.
The email bounced back because the sender's address was invalid.
In any communication, both the sender and the recipient play important roles.

Synset Examples

return to sender

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recipient - person or entity that receives something
  • Synonym: transmitter - a person or device that transmits something

Etymology

From Old English sendan (to send).
