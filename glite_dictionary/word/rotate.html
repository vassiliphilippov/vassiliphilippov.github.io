to rotate

1. VERB

/ ˈroʊteɪt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊteɪt / (BrE)
to exchange or assign (a role, position, or duty) in regular succession or turns
exchange roles or positions in turns

Examples

Each team member will rotate as leader throughout the project.
We rotate the night shift duties so no one has to work late all week.
The committee decided to rotate the chairperson every six months.

Synset Examples

We rotate the lead soprano every night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alternate - to take turns repeatedly

Etymology

From Latin 'rotatus', past participle of 'rotare' (to turn, revolve), from 'rota' (wheel).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02399036-v
Synset POS: v

6. VERB

/ ˈroʊ.teɪt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.teɪt / (BrE)
(of crops) to plant or grow in a fixed succession or order each season, especially to preserve soil quality or reduce pests.
to grow crops in succession

Examples

Farmers often rotate their crops to prevent the depletion of soil nutrients.
It is important to rotate wheat and legumes in the same field for sustainable agriculture.
If you rotate crops regularly, you can minimize pest problems and improve yields.
