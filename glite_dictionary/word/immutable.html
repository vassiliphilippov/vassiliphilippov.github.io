immutable

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmjuː.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuː.tjʊ.bəl / (BrE)
unchanging over time; unable to be changed
unchanging; unable to be altered

Examples

Scientists study the immutable laws of physics that govern our universe.
His beliefs were immutable, no matter how much evidence was presented.
Some say moral principles are immutable across cultures and time.

Synset Examples

the immutable laws of nature

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mutable - liable to change; changeable

Etymology

From Latin immutabilis, from in- (not) + mutabilis (able to be changed).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈmjutəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːtəbl / (BrE)
unable to be changed; unchanging over time or unable to be altered.
unchangeable; unable to be altered

Examples

Their core principles remain immutable despite changing trends.
The laws of physics are often considered immutable.
Her immutable attitude made compromise impossible.

Synset Examples

the view of that time was that all species were immutable, created by God

Related Terms

  • Antonym: changeable - able to be changed

Etymology

From Latin 'immutabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'mutabilis' (changeable).
