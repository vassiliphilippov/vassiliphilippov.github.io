Faustian

1. ADJ

/ ˈfaʊstiən / (AmE), / ˈfaʊstiən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Faust; insatiably ambitious, especially in pursuing knowledge or power at the expense of moral or spiritual values.
relating to reckless pursuit of power or knowledge

Examples

He made a Faustian bargain, sacrificing his integrity for career success.
The company's Faustian ambitions led it to cut corners and ignore ethical standards.
Critics argued that the scientist had followed a Faustian path in search of fame and discovery.

Synset Examples

a Faustian pact with the Devil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mephistophelian - cunning or devilish, alluding to Mephistopheles, the demon in the Faust legend

Etymology

From the legend of Faust, a character in German folklore who made a pact with the Devil.
