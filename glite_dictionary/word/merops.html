Merops

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛr.ɑps / (AmE), / ˈmɪər.ɒps / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Meropidae, comprising the bee-eaters, a group of brightly colored, insectivorous birds found in Africa, Eurasia, and Australia.
type genus of bee-eaters (Meropidae)

Examples

The genus Merops includes more than twenty species of bee-eaters.
Ornithologists recently discovered a new species within the Merops genus.
Many Merops species are known for their striking plumage and aerial acrobatics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee-eater - a brightly colored, insect-eating bird of the family Meropidae

Etymology

From Ancient Greek mēropos, a kind of bird.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛr.ɑps / (AmE), / ˈmɪə.rɒps / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored, insect-eating birds known as bee-eaters, belonging to the family Meropidae.
genus of bee-eater birds

Examples

The Merops genus includes several species of bee-eater found in Africa and Asia.
Members of the Merops genus are known for their vivid plumage and aerial insect hunting skills.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Merops genus in the rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Meropidae - the family including bee-eaters
  • Synonym: bee-eater - common name for birds of the genus Merops

Etymology

From Greek mēropos (“dividing the voice”), used by Aristotle for a kind of bird.
