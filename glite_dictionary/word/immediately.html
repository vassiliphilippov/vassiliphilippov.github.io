immediately

1. ADV

/ ɪˈmiːdiətli / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiətli / (BrE)
in a way that is directly or closely related; without anything coming between.
directly related; without anything intervening

Examples

The new law immediately affects property owners in the area.
Her decision immediately impacts the outcome of the negotiations.
This change immediately involves everyone in the department.

Synset Examples

this immediately concerns your future

Related Terms

  • Synonym: directly - without anything intervening; in a direct manner

Etymology

From Latin 'immediatus', from 'in-' (not) + 'mediatus' (having a middle).

2. ADV

/ ɪˈmiːdiətli / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiətli / (BrE)
very close to a particular place; directly next to or nearest to something
very close to a place; directly next to

Examples

The school is located immediately next to the library.
A small garden lay immediately behind the house.
There was a café immediately across the street from the station.

Synset Examples

he passed immediately behind her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: directly - without anything in between; immediately or straight

Etymology

From Latin 'immediatus' (without anything between), from 'in-' (not) + 'mediatus' (in the middle).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ɪˈmiːdiətli / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiətli / (BrE)
at once; without any delay or hesitation
at once; without delay

Examples

She called the doctor immediately when she felt ill.
If any problems occur, please report them immediately.
The fire alarm rang and everyone left the building immediately.

Synset Examples

he answered immediately
found an answer straightaway
an official accused of dishonesty should be suspended forthwith
Come here now!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instantly - at once; immediately
  • Synonym: straightaway - without delay; at once
  • Antonym: later - after a period of time; not at once

Etymology

From Latin 'immediatus' (without anything between), from 'in-' (not) + 'mediatus' (in the middle).
