lightship

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtʃɪp / (BrE)
a ship equipped with a light and other navigational aids, anchored at a location where building a permanent lighthouse is not feasible
ship serving as a floating lighthouse

Examples

The old lightship was decommissioned after a new lighthouse was built nearby.
A lightship once marked the dangerous reef to warn passing vessels.
Tourists can now visit a historic lightship that has been turned into a museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lighthouse - a tall structure with a powerful light to guide ships

Etymology

Formed from 'light' (as in lighthouse) + 'ship'.
