Biblical Elam

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɪb.lɪ.kəl ˈiː.lɑːm / (AmE), / ˈbɪb.lɪ.kəl ˈiː.læm / (BrE)
an ancient country in southwestern Asia, east of the Tigris River (in modern Iran), often mentioned in the Bible and known for its warlike people
ancient country east of the Tigris, in modern Iran

Examples

Many ancient texts refer to Biblical Elam as a major power in early Mesopotamia.
Archaeologists have discovered artifacts from Biblical Elam near modern-day Khuzestan in Iran.
The warriors of Biblical Elam are frequently mentioned in the Old Testament.

Etymology

Elam: from Hebrew ‘Êlām, appearing in the Bible; Biblical prefix denotes its scriptural context.
