Hirundo

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈrʌndoʊ / (AmE), / hɪˈrʌndəʊ / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Hirundinidae, comprising typical swallows.
a genus of typical swallows

Examples

The barn swallow, scientifically known as Hirundo rustica, is a widely distributed species.
Many ornithologists study the migration patterns of birds in the genus Hirundo.
Hirundo is the type genus that encompasses several common swallows found across Europe and Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallow - any small, slender-bodied songbird of the family Hirundinidae

Etymology

From Latin *hirundo* (swallow, the bird).

2. NOUN

/ hɪˈrʌndoʊ / (AmE), / hɪˈrʌnduː / (BrE)
a genus of birds in the swallow family Hirundinidae, which includes many of the typical swallows found worldwide
genus of swallows (birds)

Examples

The barn swallow is a well-known member of the genus Hirundo.
Scientists found several new species within the genus Hirundo in Africa.
Many species in the genus Hirundo are admired for their graceful flight and migratory behaviour.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hirundinidae - the swallow family of birds

Etymology

From Latin *Hirundo* (swallow).
