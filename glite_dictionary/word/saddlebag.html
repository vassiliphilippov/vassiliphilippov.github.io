saddlebag

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæd.əlˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈsæd.əl.bæɡ / (BrE)
a large bag, often one of a pair, designed to hang over or be attached to a saddle on a horse or motorcycle for carrying belongings
bag hung over a saddle

Examples

He strapped his tools into the saddlebag before heading onto the trail.
The old leather saddlebags were still sturdy after years of use.
She unbuckled the saddlebag and took out her lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pannier - a basket or bag, usually one of a pair, carried on the sides of a bicycle, motorcycle, or animal

Etymology

From 'saddle' + 'bag', referring to a bag designed for attachment to a saddle.
