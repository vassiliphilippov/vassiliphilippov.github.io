vivace

1. ADJ

/ viˈvɑːtʃeɪ / (AmE), / vɪˈvɑːtʃeɪ / (BrE)
(in music) played in a very fast and lively tempo.
very fast and lively (musical tempo)

Examples

The symphony's second movement is played vivace, adding energy and excitement to the piece.
Make sure the final section is performed vivace as indicated in the score.
The conductor asked the orchestra to start the next passage vivace, increasing the tempo dramatically.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: presto - (music) very fast tempo
  • Degree Lower: allegro - (music) fast and lively tempo

Etymology

From Italian 'vivace' meaning 'lively, brisk', from Latin 'vivax' (lively, long-lived).

2. ADV

/ viˈvɑː.tʃeɪ / (AmE), / vɪˈvɑː.tʃeɪ / (BrE)
(in music) in a lively and brisk manner; used as a direction to play with a spirited tempo.
in music, lively tempo

Examples

The final movement should be played vivace to capture the composer's intent.
The conductor signaled the orchestra to begin the piece vivace.
His favorite sections were marked vivace, demanding quick and energetic playing.

Synset Examples

play this section vivace!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegro - in music, a fast and lively tempo
  • Antonym: lento - in music, a slow tempo

Etymology

From Italian 'vivace' (lively), from Latin 'vivax' (full of life).
