Chenopodiales

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛnoʊˌpoʊdiˈeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌkiːnəˌpəʊdiˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants comprising species formerly placed in the group Centrospermae, including the goosefoot family and related families
order of flowering plants (botany)

Examples

Researchers have conducted genetic studies on several species within the Chenopodiales.
Many desert plants belong to the Chenopodiales due to their adaptation to saline soils.
Taxonomists recently reclassified some families from the Chenopodiales into new orders.

Etymology

From genus name Chenopodium (goosefoot) + Latin -ales (order).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkiːnəˌpoʊdiˈeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌkɛːnəˌpəʊdɪˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants, corresponding roughly to the former group Centrospermae, comprising families such as Chenopodiaceae.
an order of flowering plants

Examples

The order Chenopodiales includes plants that thrive in saline environments.
Researchers debated whether beetroot should be classified under the Chenopodiales order.
Taxonomists sometimes refer to the older group Centrospermae, which is approximately equivalent to Chenopodiales.

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from 'Chenopodiaceae' (the goosefoot family) with the botanical order suffix '-ales'.
