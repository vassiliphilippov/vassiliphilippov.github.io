glacé

1. ADJ

/ ɡlɑːˈseɪ / (AmE), / ɡlæˈseɪ / (BrE)
(of fruit or other food) preserved by coating with or absorbing sugar, especially used in confectionery and baking.
preserved or coated with sugar (especially fruit)

Examples

The cake was decorated with glacé cherries and almonds.
She bought a box of glacé fruits to give as a gift.
This recipe calls for glacé orange peel to add sweetness and texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candied - (of fruit or peel) preserved or glazed with sugar
  • Antonym: unsweetened - not containing added sugar

Etymology

From French 'glacé', past participle of 'glacer' meaning 'to freeze, to glaze'.
