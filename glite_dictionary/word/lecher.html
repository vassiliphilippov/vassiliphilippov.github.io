lecher

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɛtʃə / (BrE)
A man who shows excessive or offensive sexual desire.
man given to lewd sexual desires

Examples

Everyone at the party avoided the old lecher who wouldn't stop making inappropriate comments.
His reputation as a notorious lecher was well-known throughout the city.
She warned her friend to stay away from that lecher in the office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lech - to behave in a lascivious way; a licentious man
  • Antonym: prude - a person who is or claims to be easily shocked by matters relating to sex

Etymology

From Old French lechier (to lick, live in debauchery), from Late Latin lūdicāre (to play, sport).
