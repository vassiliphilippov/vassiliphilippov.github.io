to embolden

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈboʊldən / (AmE), / ɪmˈbəʊldən / (BrE)
to give someone the courage or confidence to do something
give someone confidence or courage

Examples

Her success stories embolden others to pursue their dreams.
The team was emboldened by their recent victory.
His words embolden me to speak up for myself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encourage - give support or confidence to; inspire with courage
  • Antonym: to discourage - make someone less confident or brave

Etymology

From Old French 'emboldir', from Latin 'imboldire', formed from 'em-' (cause to) + 'bold'.
