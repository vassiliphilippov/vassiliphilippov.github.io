college

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
A complex of buildings where an institution of higher education is housed.
buildings of a higher education institution

Examples

The university expanded its college to include new science laboratories and lecture halls.
The main library is located at the center of the college campus.
After the storm, several buildings in the college were in need of repairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: university - an institution of higher education offering degrees

Etymology

From Latin 'collegium' (community, society, guild).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
an institution of higher education where students study for degrees, often as part of a university
institution of higher education

Examples

After graduating from high school, she decided to apply to college in the city.
He spent four years at college studying engineering.
Many students live on college campuses during their first year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: university - an institution of higher education and research granting academic degrees

Etymology

From Latin 'collegium' (society, guild), from 'collega' (colleague).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08295245-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the faculty and students collectively belonging to a college
faculty and students of a college

Examples

The college voted overwhelmingly to approve the new curriculum.
She spoke passionately about the responsibilities of the college in society.
The traditions of the college have been passed down for generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: university - the faculty and students collectively belonging to a university

Etymology

From Latin collegium (society, association).
