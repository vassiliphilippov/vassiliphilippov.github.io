incensed

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɛnst / (AmE), / ɪnˈsenst / (BrE)
very angry, especially because of something unfair or wrong
very angry or outraged

Examples

She was incensed by the unfair decision made by her supervisor.
Many citizens were incensed at the corruption uncovered in the city council.
His incensed expression left no doubt about his feelings toward the policy.

Synset Examples

an indignant denial
incensed at the judges' unfairness
a look of outraged disbelief
umbrageous at the loss of their territory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outraged - feeling fierce anger or shock due to injustice
  • Synonym: indignant - feeling or showing anger because something is unfair
  • Antonym: calm - not feeling angry or upset

Etymology

From Latin 'incensus', past participle of 'incendere' (to set on fire).
