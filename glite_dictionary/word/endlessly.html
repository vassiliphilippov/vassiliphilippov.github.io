endlessly

1. ADV

/ ˈɛndləsli / (AmE), / ˈɛndləsli / (BrE)
In a way that has no end; without stopping or seeming to stop.
without end; indefinitely

Examples

She talked endlessly about her travels across Europe.
The river seemed to wind endlessly through the mountains.
He could stare at the stars endlessly, lost in thought.

Synset Examples

there are infinitely many possibilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infinitely - without limits or end
  • Synonym: ceaselessly - without pausing or stopping
  • Antonym: briefly - for a short time; not for long

Etymology

From 'endless' + '-ly', based on 'end' (from Old English 'endian').

2. ADV

/ ˈɛn(d)lɪsli / (AmE), / ˈɛn(d)lɪsli / (BrE)
In a way that continues without stopping; with unflagging resolve or seemingly without limit.
without stopping; infinitely

Examples

She talked endlessly about her travels through Europe.
The river seems to flow endlessly into the horizon, with no end in sight.
He worked endlessly to perfect his craft, never giving up despite setbacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eternally - lasting or existing forever; without end

Etymology

3. ADV

/ ˈɛndləsli / (AmE), / ˈɛndləsli / (BrE)
Seemingly having no limits or boundaries; extending without end, especially in space or time.
without bounds; with no end

Examples

The desert stretched endlessly in every direction, making them feel very small.
She talked endlessly about her travels, never seeming to run out of stories.
The road wound endlessly through the mountains, disappearing into the horizon.

Synset Examples

the Nubian desert stretched out before them endlessly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infinitely - having no limits; extremely large or great
  • Synonym: eternally - lasting or existing forever; without end
  • Antonym: briefly - for a short time; not lasting long

Etymology

From 'endless' + '-ly'; 'endless' from Old English 'endeleas' (having no end).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

4. ADV

/ ˈɛn.dləs.li / (AmE), / ˈɛn.dləs.li / (BrE)
in a way that seems to have no end; continuously and without stopping
without end; continuously

Examples

We waited endlessly for the train to arrive that night.
He could talk endlessly about his travels in South America.
The road seemed to stretch endlessly into the desert.

Synset Examples

a theological student with whom I argued interminably
her nagging went on endlessly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interminably - endlessly, without end or limit, especially in time
  • Synonym: forever - for all time; seemingly without end
  • Antonym: briefly - for a short time; not continuously

Etymology

From 'endless' + '-ly', in use since the 14th century, from Old English 'end(e)' and 'l¯eas' (without).
