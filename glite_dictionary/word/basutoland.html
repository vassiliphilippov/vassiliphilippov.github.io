Basutoland

1. PROPN

/ bəˈsuːtəˌlænd / (AmE), / bəˈsuːtəˌlænd / (BrE)
the former name of Lesotho, a landlocked constitutional monarchy in southern Africa that gained independence from the United Kingdom in 1966
former name of Lesotho, southern African country

Examples

During the colonial period, Basutoland was a British protectorate in southern Africa.
The mountainous landscape of Basutoland made it difficult for foreign powers to invade.
After achieving independence, Basutoland was renamed Lesotho in 1966.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lesotho - a landlocked country in southern Africa, formerly known as Basutoland

Etymology

From 'Basuto' (the people or language of Lesotho) and 'land'.
