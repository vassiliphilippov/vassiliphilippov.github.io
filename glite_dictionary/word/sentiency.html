sentiency

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsiənsi / (AmE), / ˈsɛntiənsi / (BrE)
The capacity to experience sensations or perceive the external world; the state of being sentient.
capacity for sensation or perception

Examples

Some philosophers debate whether animals possess sentiency similar to that of humans.
The sentiency of the patient was tested through various sensory stimuli.
New research explores the sentiency of artificial intelligence and its ability to perceive its environment.

Synset Examples

in the dark he had to depend on touch and on his senses of smell and hearing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentience - the ability to perceive or feel things; state of being sentient

Etymology

Formed from 'sentient' + '-cy' by analogy with 'sentience.'
