to predominate

1. VERB

/ prɪˈdoʊmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / prɪˈdɒmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to have greater numbers, strength, influence, or importance than others in a particular group or situation
to be greater in number or influence

Examples

In this region, pine trees predominate over other types of vegetation.
Women predominate in the nursing profession across many countries.
New technologies predominate the discussions at the annual industry conference.

Synset Examples

Money reigns supreme here
Hispanics predominate in this neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prevail - to prove more powerful or superior
  • Synonym: to dominate - to have a commanding influence on

Etymology

From Latin praedominari (to rule over), from prae- (before) + dominari (to rule).

2. VERB

/ prɪˈdoʊmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / prɪˈdɒmɪneɪt / (BrE)
(of an object or feature) to appear very large or occupy a commanding position; to dominate visually in a place or scene.
appear dominant or commanding in position

Examples

The golden dome predominates over the entire city skyline.
A sense of darkness predominated in the abandoned theater, making it feel eerie.
The towering oak tree predominates at the center of the garden, drawing everyone's attention.

Synset Examples

The huge sculpture predominates over the fountain
Large shadows loomed on the canyon wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dominate - to have a commanding position or control

Etymology

From Latin 'praedominari', from 'prae-' (before) + 'dominari' (to rule).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
