cosmopolitan

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːzməˈpɑːlɪtən / (AmE), / ˌkɒzməˈpɒlɪtən / (BrE)
a person who is comfortable and sophisticated in many different countries and cultures, often as a result of extensive travel or experience
sophisticated, well-travelled person

Examples

As a true cosmopolitan, she felt at home in cities from New York to Tokyo.
He became a cosmopolitan after years spent working and living abroad.
A cosmopolitan is usually open-minded and comfortable in diverse environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: globetrotter - a person who travels widely around the world
  • Antonym: provincial - a person limited in outlook, not worldly or sophisticated

Etymology

From Greek 'kosmopolitēs' (citizen of the world), from 'kosmos' (world) and 'polites' (citizen).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːzməˈpɑːlɪtən / (AmE), / ˌkɒzməˈpɒlɪtən / (BrE)
including people from many different countries; showing experience of or openness to different cultures; not limited to a narrow area or viewpoint.
international; familiar with many cultures

Examples

New York is known for being a truly cosmopolitan city, with residents from every continent.
Her cosmopolitan outlook helped her appreciate and adapt to cultures wherever she traveled.
The restaurant's cosmopolitan menu features dishes inspired by cuisines from around the world.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: provincial - narrow-minded or limited in outlook, especially from a limited or local perspective
  • Synonym: worldly - experienced and knowledgeable about the world

Etymology

From Greek kosmopolitēs meaning 'citizen of the world', from kosmos (world) + politēs (citizen).

3. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːzməˈpɑːlətən / (AmE), / ˌkɒzməˈpɒlɪtən / (BrE)
(of a plant, animal, or other organism) found in many different parts of the world; not restricted to a particular geographic region.
widely distributed around the world (esp. of organisms)

Examples

The dandelion is a cosmopolitan species, thriving on every continent except Antarctica.
Many bacteria are cosmopolitan, existing in diverse environmental conditions worldwide.
This weed's cosmopolitan distribution makes it difficult to control in any one location.

Synset Examples

a cosmopolitan herb
cosmopolitan in distribution

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endemic - restricted or native to a particular region

Etymology

From Greek kosmopolitēs (citizen of the world), from kosmos (world) + politēs (citizen).
