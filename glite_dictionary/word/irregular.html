irregular

/ ɪˈrɛɡjələr / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛɡjʊlə / (BrE)
(of soldiers or military forces) not belonging to the regular army; not part of the official or formal military forces.
not part of the official army

Examples

The rebels relied heavily on irregular troops to carry out their operations.
During the conflict, irregular forces launched a series of surprise attacks on the capital.
Much of the fighting in the region involved clashes between government forces and irregular militias.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From Latin 'irregularis' (not according to rule).

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈrɛɡjələr / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛɡjʊlə / (BrE)
(of a solid) not having measurable dimensions; requiring special methods, such as liquid displacement, to determine volume.
not having measurable dimensions (solid)

Examples

To find the volume of an irregular object, scientists often use water displacement.
A rock is an irregular solid, so its volume can't be calculated with a ruler.
Many natural materials are considered irregular because they lack clearly defined shapes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: regular - (of solids) having clear, measurable dimensions

Etymology

From Latin irregularis, from in- ‘not’ + regularis ‘regular’.

11. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɛɡjələr / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛɡjʊlə / (BrE)
a member of an armed force not belonging to a regular army, who engages in fighting using non-traditional tactics such as sabotage and harassment

Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin 'irregularis' (not according to rule), applied to military use in the 18th–19th centuries.
