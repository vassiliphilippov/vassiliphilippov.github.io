Cavia porcellus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.viə pɔːrˈsɛl.əs / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.vɪə pɔːˈsɛl.əs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the domestic guinea pig, a rodent species native to South America and commonly kept as a pet or used in research.
scientific name for the domestic guinea pig

Examples

The laboratory used Cavia porcellus in several behavioral experiments.
Many children learn about mammals by caring for a Cavia porcellus in school.
The domestic guinea pig, or Cavia porcellus, originated in the Andes of South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guinea pig - small domesticated cavy, common pet

Etymology

From Latin; 'Cavia' is the genus name, possibly derived from Portuguese 'cavia' (guinea pig), and 'porcellus' means 'little piglet'.
