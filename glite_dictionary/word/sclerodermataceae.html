Sclerodermataceae

1. NOUN

/ sklɛroʊˌdɜrməˈteɪsi.i / (AmE), / sklɪərəʊˌdɜːməˈteɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi with a single-layered peridium, including the earthballs, in the order Sclerodermatales
a family of fungi (earthballs)
medical

Examples

Several species in the Sclerodermataceae family are known for producing earthball fungi in forests.
Research on Sclerodermataceae helps mycologists better understand fungal diversity in temperate ecosystems.
The unique structure of the peridium distinguishes members of the Sclerodermataceae from puffballs and other fungi.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'sklēros' (hard) and 'derma' (skin), with botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
