sierra

1. NOUN

/ siˈɛrə / (AmE), / siˈɛərə / (BrE)
A Spanish mackerel (Scomberomorus sierra) found in the Pacific waters off western North America, prized as a game and food fish.
Pacific Spanish mackerel species

Examples

Fishermen often catch sierra along the coasts of Baja California.
The restaurant specializes in freshly grilled sierra for its seafood menu.
A school of sierra was spotted near the harbor early this morning.

Etymology

From Spanish 'sierra', meaning both 'mountain range' and the fish species.

2. NOUN

/ siˈɛrə / (AmE), / siˈɛərə / (BrE)
A range of mountains, especially one with jagged peaks and a rugged outline.
a mountain range with jagged peaks

Examples

The hikers planned their route across the sierra before sunset.
From the summit, the entire sierra spread out in a jagged line against the horizon.
Many rare plants and animals live only at certain elevations in the sierra.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain range - a line or group of mountains connected by high ground

Etymology

From Spanish 'sierra' meaning 'mountain range' or 'saw', derived from Latin 'serra' (saw), referring to the saw-toothed appearance of the peaks.
