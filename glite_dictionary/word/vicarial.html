vicarial

1. ADJ

/ vɪˈkɛriəl / (AmE), / vɪˈkeəriəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a vicar.
of or relating to a vicar

Examples

The church maintains a vicarial residence beside the parish hall.
Her vicarial duties included visiting the sick and organizing community events.
He wore a vicarial collar as part of his clerical attire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vicar - a representative or deputy of a bishop in the Church

Etymology

From Medieval Latin vicarialis, from vicarius (substitute, vicar).
