glaring

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡlerɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡleərɪŋ / (BrE)
shining with a very strong or dazzling light; intensely bright or blinding
shining intensely; very bright

Examples

The glaring sun forced us to shield our eyes as we walked outside.
The headlights were so glaring that I had to pull over until I could see clearly again.
Snow on the mountain reflected a glaring light that made it hard to keep my eyes open.

Synset Examples

the blazing sun
blinding headlights
dazzling snow
fulgent patterns of sunlight
the glaring sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dazzling - extremely bright and hard to look at
  • Synonym: blinding - making it difficult to see because of strong light
  • Antonym: dim - not shining brightly or clearly

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡlɛɹɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡleə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a fault or mistake) very obvious and easily noticed, particularly because it is bad or shocking.
extremely obvious or conspicuous, especially of faults

Examples

The journalist pointed out a glaring error in the official report.
There was a glaring inconsistency between his words and his actions.
Despite its glaring flaws, the project moved forward.

Synset Examples

a crying shame
an egregious lie
flagrant violation of human rights
a glaring error
gross ineptitude
gross injustice
rank treachery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egregious - outstandingly bad; shocking
  • Synonym: flagrant - noticeably wrong or offensive
  • Antonym: subtle - not obvious; difficult to detect or notice

Etymology

From the verb 'glare' (to shine intensely), figuratively extended to mean 'obviously noticeable'.
