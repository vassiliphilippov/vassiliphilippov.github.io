coronach

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrənæk / (AmE), / ˈkɒrənæk / (BrE)
a song or lament of mourning, especially one sung at a funeral as a tribute to the dead, particularly in Scottish or Irish tradition
funeral lament or dirge

Examples

The mourners gathered around the grave, their voices rising together in a solemn coronach.
As the bagpipes played a haunting coronach, the village paid their last respects.
He wrote a moving coronach to honor his late friend, reciting it at the memorial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirge - a mournful song in memory of the dead
  • Synonym: elegy - a poem or song expressing grief for someone deceased

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'coronach' or Irish 'coranach', meaning 'lament for the dead'.
