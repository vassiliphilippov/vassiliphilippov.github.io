scorer

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɔrər / (AmE), / ˈskɔːrə / (BrE)
an official responsible for recording the score during the progress of a game or sporting event
official who records scores in a game

Examples

The cricket scorer tallied the runs after every over.
Without an official scorer, mistakes in the score can easily occur during the match.
The scorer announced the final results once the game ended.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: referee - an official who enforces the rules in a game or match

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɔrər / (AmE), / ˈskɔːrə / (BrE)
a player who gains points, goals, or runs in a game or contest
person who scores in a game or contest

Examples

The team celebrated as their top scorer broke another record.
Last night's game saw an unexpected scorer net the winning goal.
Every basketball team relies on at least one consistent scorer to lead the offense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goal scorer - a player who scores a goal in a sport or game
  • Antonym: defender - a player whose role is to prevent the opposition from scoring

Etymology

From 'score' (to gain a point) plus '-er' (agent noun suffix).

/ ˈskɔɚɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɔːrə / (BrE)

logger who marks trees to be felled

As a scorer, she carefully marked each trunk for the cutting crew.
