adherence

1. NOUN

/ ədˈhɪrəns / (AmE), / ədˈhɪərəns / (BrE)
the act of supporting or sticking firmly to a particular set of beliefs, rules, organization, or cause
faithful support or attachment to something

Examples

Her adherence to the company's values made her a role model for new employees.
Strict adherence to the diet improved his health noticeably.
The organization expects complete adherence to its code of conduct from all members.

Synset Examples

attachment to a formal agenda
adherence to a fat-free diet
the adhesion of Seville was decisive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegiance - loyalty or commitment to a group or cause
  • Antonym: disloyalty - lack of allegiance or support

Etymology

From Latin 'adhaerentia', meaning 'a sticking to'.

2. NOUN

/ ədˈhɪrəns / (AmE), / ədˈhɪərəns / (BrE)
the property or state of sticking firmly to a surface or to each other, especially of substances or materials
the property of sticking or joining surfaces

Examples

The adherence of the sticker to the glass was impressive, lasting for weeks.
Proper adherence between the paint and the metal prevents peeling over time.
Medical researchers studied the adherence of the implant to surrounding tissue.

Synset Examples

the mutual adhesiveness of cells
a heated hydraulic press was required for adhesion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adhesion - the process or state of sticking together or attaching surfaces
  • Degree Lower: cohesion - the property of molecules sticking together within the same substance

Etymology

From Latin adhaerentia (a sticking to), from adhaerere (to stick to).
