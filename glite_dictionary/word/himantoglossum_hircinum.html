Himantoglossum hircinum

1. NOUN

/ hɪˌmæntəˈɡlɑsəm hɜrˈsaɪnəm / (AmE), / hɪˌmantəˈɡlɒsəm hɜːˈsɪnəm / (BrE)
A species of orchid commonly known as the lizard orchid, native to Europe and North Africa, recognized for its long, twisted labellum and strong scent.
lizard orchid species

Examples

The Himantoglossum hircinum is well known for its unusual, spiral-shaped flowers resembling lizards.
Botanists discovered a vibrant cluster of Himantoglossum hircinum in the limestone meadows of southern France.
The strong, goat-like fragrance of Himantoglossum hircinum helps attract pollinating insects.

Etymology

From Greek 'himantoglossum' (strap-tongued), referring to the shape of the flower, and Latin 'hircinum' (goat-like), relating to its smell.
