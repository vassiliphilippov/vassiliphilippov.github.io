Senna marilandica

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnə ˌmærɪˈlændɪkə / (AmE), / ˈsɛnə ˌmarɪˈlandɪkə / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb of the legume family, with leaves sometimes used medicinally; also known as wild senna.
North American perennial herb (wild senna)

Examples

The herbalist recommended Senna marilandica as a natural remedy for digestive issues.
In the summer, Senna marilandica produces bright yellow flowers attractive to pollinators.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Senna marilandica leaves in laboratory trials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild senna - Alternative common name for Senna marilandica

Etymology

Genus name from Arabic 'sanā', species name refers to Maryland, USA.
