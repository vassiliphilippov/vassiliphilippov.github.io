vitriolic

1. ADJ

/ ˌvɪtriˈɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌvɪtrɪˈɒlɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance, especially a strong acid) capable of burning or destroying tissue by chemical action; highly corrosive.
corrosive, capable of burning or destroying

Examples

The scientist warned that the vitriolic solution could damage skin upon contact.
Over time, the vitriolic acid ate through the metal container.
Laboratory safety protocols require special handling of vitriolic chemicals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrosive - having the quality of eating away or destroying by chemical action

Etymology

From Latin *vitriolicus*, from *vitriolum* (sulphate of iron, literally 'glassy').

2. ADJ

/ ˌvɪtriˈɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌvɪtrɪˈɒlɪk / (BrE)
(of speech, writing, or behavior) filled with bitter criticism or malice; harshly scathing.
filled with harsh, bitter criticism

Examples

Her vitriolic comments during the meeting shocked everyone in the room.
The review was so vitriolic that the director refused to speak to the critics again.
Political debates can become vitriolic when emotions run high.

Synset Examples

an acerbic tone piercing otherwise flowery prose
a barrage of acid comments
her acrid remarks make her many enemies
bitter words
blistering criticism
caustic jokes about political assassination, talk-show hosts and medical ethics
a sulfurous denunciation
a vitriolic critique

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caustic - harsh or severely critical
  • Synonym: acerbic - sharp and forthright (especially of speech)
  • Antonym: mild - gentle or not severe

Etymology

From Latin vitriolum (sulphuric acid), with reference to the corrosive power of vitriol.
