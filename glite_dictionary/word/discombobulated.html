discombobulated

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪskəmˈbɑbjəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəmˈbɒbjʊˌleɪtɪd / (BrE)
confused and disconcerted; having one's composure disrupted
confused; thrown into disorder
informal american

Examples

After missing her flight, she felt completely discombobulated in the busy airport.
The professor appeared discombobulated when the projector suddenly stopped working during his lecture.
He was so discombobulated by the sudden news that he could hardly respond.

Synset Examples

the hecklers pelted the discombobulated speaker with anything that came to hand
looked at each other dumbly, quite disconcerted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confused - unable to think clearly; bewildered
  • Antonym: composed - calm and in control of oneself
  • Synonym: disconcerted - disturbed or unsettled

Etymology

Probably a fanciful American coinage from 'discompose' or 'discomfort', blended and extended with playful suffixation (mid-19th century, US).
