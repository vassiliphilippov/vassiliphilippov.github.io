bluebell

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌbɛl / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌbɛl / (BrE)
a wildflower with large, bell-shaped bluish flowers, commonly found in moist prairies and fields in parts of North America
prairie wildflower with blue bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The bluebell blooms every spring, covering the field with a carpet of blue.
She spotted a cluster of bluebells growing near the stream.
The prairie was dotted with bluebell flowers swaying gently in the breeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harebell - a similar wildflower with blue, bell-shaped flowers

Etymology

From 'blue' (the color) + 'bell' (the shape of the flower), referring to the blue, bell-shaped flowers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌbɛl / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌbɛl / (BrE)
a plant with bell-shaped blue flowers, especially one sometimes placed in the genus Scilla or Hyacinthoides.
a flowering plant with blue bell-shaped flowers

Examples

In the spring, the bluebell woods are a beautiful sight in the countryside.
She picked a bluebell and placed it carefully in her sketchbook.
Bluebell flowers thrive in shady, damp areas under trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyacinth - a fragrant flowering plant, sometimes confused with bluebells

Etymology

From blue + bell, referring to the color and shape of the flower.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌbɛl / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌbɛl / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the Northern Hemisphere with slender stems and bell-shaped blue flowers.
a plant with blue bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The woods were carpeted with bluebells in the spring.
She picked a bouquet of wild bluebells during her walk.
In late April, the bluebell flowers bring a splash of color to the meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyacinth - a plant with fragrant bell-shaped flowers, sometimes confused with bluebells
  • Degree Lower: snowdrop - a plant with white bell-shaped flowers, related by form
