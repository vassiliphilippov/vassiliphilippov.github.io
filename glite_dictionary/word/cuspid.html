cuspid

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌs.pɪd / (AmE), / ˈkʌs.pɪd / (BrE)
one of the pointed conical teeth located between the incisors and premolars, also known as a canine tooth
pointed tooth between incisors and premolars
medical

Examples

The dentist noted that my left cuspid was impacted and might require extraction.
Children usually lose their primary cuspids around the age of ten.
A sharp cuspid helps animals tear their food efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canine tooth - the pointed tooth between incisors and premolars

Etymology

From Latin 'cuspis' (point, tip).
