Geococcyx californianus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːoʊˈkɑksiˌʌks ˌkælɪˌfɔrˈniːənəs / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəʊˈkɒksiˌʌks ˌkælɪˌfɔːˈniːənəs / (BrE)
A large, fast-running bird of the cuckoo family, native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, commonly known as the greater roadrunner.
greater roadrunner; fast terrestrial bird

Examples

The Geococcyx californianus is famously able to outrun many of its natural predators.
Researchers observed a Geococcyx californianus darting across the desert in search of food.
In southwestern folklore, the Geococcyx californianus is often associated with speed and agility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roadrunner - the common name for Geococcyx californianus, a fast-running bird of the American Southwest

Etymology

From Greek geo- (earth) + kokkux (cuckoo) and Latin californianus (of California).
