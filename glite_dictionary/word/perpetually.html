perpetually

1. ADV

/ pərˈpɛtʃuəli / (AmE), / pəˈpɛtʃuəli / (BrE)
In a way that continues forever or for a very long time without stopping.
continually; endlessly

Examples

She seems perpetually busy, never taking a moment to rest.
The old clock in the hallway ticks perpetually throughout the night.
The mountains are perpetually covered with snow, regardless of the season.

Synset Examples

rays … streaming perpetually from the sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eternally - in a manner that lasts forever
  • Antonym: temporarily - for a limited period of time

Etymology

From Latin 'perpetuus' (continuous, permanent) + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ pɚˈpɛtʃuəli / (AmE), / pəˈpɛtʃuəli / (BrE)
In a way that never ends or changes; constantly and repeatedly without interruption.
continually, without interruption

Examples

She seemed perpetually cheerful, regardless of the circumstances.
The machine ran perpetually for years without needing repairs.
He feels perpetually busy and rarely finds time to relax.

Synset Examples

the world is constantly changing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constantly - Continuously over a period of time; always
  • Antonym: occasionally - Happening from time to time, not regularly

Etymology

From Latin 'perpetuus' (continuous, perpetual) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00020735-r
Synset POS: r
