Erythrina lysistemon

1. NOUN

/ əˌrɪθrəˈnaɪ lɪˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ɛˌrɪθrɪnə lɪˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A small semi-evergreen tree native to South Africa, known for its dense clusters of bright scarlet flowers and red seeds.
South African tree with scarlet flowers

Examples

The Erythrina lysistemon adds a splash of color to many South African gardens each spring.
Gardeners prize the Erythrina lysistemon for its brilliant scarlet blossoms and modest size.
Birds are attracted to the Erythrina lysistemon because of its nectar-rich flowers.

Etymology

From genus name 'Erythrina', from Greek 'erythros' (red), referring to the flower color, and specific epithet 'lysistemon', from Greek 'lysis' (loosening) and 'stemon' (stamen), referring to the flower's structure.
