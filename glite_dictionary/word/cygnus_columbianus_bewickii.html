Cygnus columbianus bewickii

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡ.nəs ˌkoʊ.lʌmˈbiː.ə.nəs bɪˈwɪk.i / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.nəs ˌkɒ.ləmˈbiː.ə.nəs bɪˈwɪk.i / (BrE)
A Eurasian subspecies of tundra swan, smaller than the whooper swan, distinguished by its yellow and black bill.
Eurasian subspecies of tundra swan

Examples

The Cygnus columbianus bewickii migrates annually between the Arctic tundra and parts of western Europe.
Unlike the whooper swan, the Cygnus columbianus bewickii is notably smaller and has more black on its bill.
Birdwatchers in the UK eagerly await the arrival of Cygnus columbianus bewickii each winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tundra swan - a species of swan found in the Arctic and subarctic regions
  • Degree Higher: whooper swan - a large northern swan species, similar but larger than Bewick's swan

Etymology

Cygnus from Latin for 'swan'; specific and subspecific epithets honoring naturalist William Bewick.
