penurious

1. ADJ

/ pəˈnjʊriəs / (AmE), / pɪˈnjʊərɪəs / (BrE)
unwilling to spend money; extremely frugal or miserly
very unwilling to spend money

Examples

Despite his wealth, Mr. Jones remained penurious and rarely donated to charity.
Their penurious lifestyle meant they avoided even minor luxuries.
He was so penurious that he patched his clothes instead of buying new ones.

Synset Examples

parsimonious thrift relieved by few generous impulses
lived in a most penurious manner — denying himself every indulgence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsimonious - unwilling to spend money or use resources; stingy or frugal
  • Antonym: generous - willing to give money or help freely

Etymology

From Latin 'penuria' (want, scarcity), via Late Latin 'penuriosus'.

2. ADJ

/ pəˈnʊriəs / (AmE), / pɪˈnjʊəriəs / (BrE)
Extremely poor; lacking sufficient money to pay for basic needs or necessities.
extremely poor; lacking money for basics

Examples

The penurious family struggled to afford food and shelter.
He grew up in a penurious village where even the basics were scarce.
Although penurious, she always found a way to help those in need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destitute - lacking all basic resources; extremely poor
  • Synonym: impoverished - reduced to poverty; poor
  • Antonym: affluent - having a great deal of money; wealthy

Etymology

From Latin 'penuria' (want, scarcity).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02030912-s
Synset POS: s
