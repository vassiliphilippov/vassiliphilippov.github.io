Perdicinae

1. NOUN

/ pərˈdɪsɪni / (AmE), / pɜːˈdɪsɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of birds in the pheasant family (Phasianidae), comprising the Old World partridges and their relatives.
subfamily of Old World partridges

Examples

The Perdicinae are distributed across Europe, Asia, and Africa, inhabiting grasslands and forests.
Researchers studied genetic differences among several species within the Perdicinae subfamily.
Most members of the Perdicinae are ground-dwelling birds noted for their cryptic plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partridge - a medium-sized game bird of the Old World
  • Degree Higher: Phasianidae - family of birds including pheasants, partridges, and relatives

Etymology

From New Latin Perdicinae, based on Latin perdix (partridge).
