stability

1. NOUN

/ stəˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / stəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of being consistent and not likely to change or vary; the state of being steady or enduring.
state of being steady or unchanged

Examples

The country's economic stability attracted many foreign investors.
We need to ensure the stability of the structure before continuing construction.
Emotional stability is important for healthy relationships.

Synset Examples

early mariners relied on the constancy of the trade winds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: instability - the state of being likely to change, not stable
  • Synonym: constancy - the quality of being unchanging or unwavering

Etymology

From Latin stabilitas (firmness), from stabilis (firm, stable).

2. NOUN

/ stəˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / stəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being steady, firm, or unchanging; resistance to change or disturbance
the state of being steady and unchanging

Examples

The stability of the building was tested after the earthquake.
Political stability is crucial for economic growth.
She valued emotional stability in her relationships.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: instability - the state of being unstable or likely to change
  • Synonym: steadiness - the quality of being stable and not changing

Etymology

From Latin stabilitas, from stabilis (firm, steadfast).

3. NOUN

/ stəˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / stəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the condition of being steady and not likely to change or be disturbed; especially, a state of order and security in society or a system.
a state of steadiness or order, especially in society

Examples

Economic stability is essential for the long-term growth of any country.
After years of unrest, the new government promised peace and stability.
Social stability allows communities to thrive and develop.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: instability - the state of being likely to change or fail suddenly, lack of stability
  • Synonym: order - a state where rules or laws are obeyed and authority is respected

Etymology

From Latin stabilitas (firmness, stability), from stabilis (firm, stable).
