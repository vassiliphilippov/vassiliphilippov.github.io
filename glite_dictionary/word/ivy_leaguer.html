Ivy Leaguer

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪvi ˈliːɡər / (AmE), / ˌaɪvi ˈliːɡə / (BrE)
a student or graduate of an Ivy League university in the United States
student/graduate of an Ivy League school

Examples

He always dreamed of becoming an Ivy Leaguer and attending Harvard.
Many Ivy Leaguers pursue careers in finance or law after graduation.
She is proud to be an Ivy Leaguer and often attends university alumni events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ivy League - group of prestigious northeastern US universities

Etymology

From 'Ivy League', the group of eight prestigious northeastern US universities, and '-er' denoting a person.
