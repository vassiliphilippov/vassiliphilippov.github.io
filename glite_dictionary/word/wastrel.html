wastrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪstrəl / (AmE), / ˈweɪstrəl / (BrE)
A person who spends resources, especially money or time, wastefully or self-indulgently.
person who wastes resources

Examples

The inheritance was soon squandered by the family's only wastrel, who spent it on frivolous pleasures.
Society often looks down on a wastrel who refuses to take responsibility for their actions.
She feared her efforts would be wasted on a hopeless wastrel with no desire to change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spendthrift - a person who spends money in an extravagant, irresponsible way
  • Synonym: prodigal - a person who spends resources recklessly or wastefully
  • Antonym: miser - a person who hoards wealth and spends as little as possible

Etymology

From 'waste' plus the suffix '-rel', indicating a person associated with an action or trait.
