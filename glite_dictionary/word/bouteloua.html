Bouteloua

1. NOUN

/ buːtəˈloʊə / (AmE), / buːtəˈləʊə / (BrE)
a genus of grasses native to the Americas, known commonly as grama grasses and used as forage for livestock
a genus of forage grasses (grama)

Examples

Ranchers often plant Bouteloua to provide high-quality grazing for cattle.
Some species of Bouteloua are well adapted to dry climates and poor soils.
The lawn featured native grasses, including several varieties of Bouteloua.

Etymology

Named after Spanish botanists Claudio and Esteban Boutelou.

2. NOUN

/ buːtəˈluːə / (AmE), / buːtəˈluːə / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly North and South American grasses, commonly known as grama grasses, valued for forage
a genus of forage grasses

Examples

Several species in the genus Bouteloua are important for grazing livestock in the Americas.
Native prairie ecosystems often contain grasses from the Bouteloua genus, such as blue grama.
Researchers studied the drought tolerance of Bouteloua to improve forage crops.

Etymology

Named after Spanish botanists Claudio and Esteban Boutelou.
