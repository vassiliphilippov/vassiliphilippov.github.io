gypsyweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪpsiˌwid / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪpsiˌwiːd / (BrE)
A common hairy perennial plant native to Europe, with pale blue or lilac flowers arranged in small clusters.
European perennial plant with blue or lilac flowers

Examples

The meadow was dotted with blooming gypsyweed in the late spring.
Botanists often note the hairy stems of gypsyweed when identifying it in the wild.
Gardeners sometimes plant gypsyweed for its delicate blue flowers and hardy nature.
