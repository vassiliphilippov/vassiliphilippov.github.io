Appalachian

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈleɪtʃən / (AmE), / ˌapəˈlatʃɪən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Appalachia, a region in the eastern United States.
person from Appalachia (US region)

Examples

Many Appalachians have deep family roots in the region spanning several generations.
The festival celebrated the culture and traditions of the Appalachian people.
An Appalachian might be known for their distinctive music and storytelling.

Etymology

From Appalachia, the name of the mountain range derived from the Apalachee people of Florida.

2. ADJ

/ ˌæpəˈleɪtʃən / (AmE), / ˌæpəˈleɪtʃɪən / (BrE)
of or relating to the region of Appalachia in the eastern United States or its people, culture, or geology.
relating to Appalachia or its people/culture

Examples

Many Appalachian communities have preserved unique traditions and dialects over the centuries.
The Appalachian landscape is famous for its rolling hills and dense forests.
She studied the impact of coal mining on Appalachian towns.

Etymology

From Appalachia, the name of the mountain range, itself from Apalachee, a Native American people of Florida.

Synset POS: a
