tangram

1. NOUN

/ ˈtænˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈtæŋ.ɡræm / (BrE)
a Chinese puzzle consisting of a square cut into seven flat pieces that can be arranged to form various shapes and designs
Chinese seven-piece puzzle toy

Examples

My grandfather gave me a wooden tangram that I still enjoy solving on quiet evenings.
The teacher used a tangram to help the students explore geometric shapes and spatial reasoning.
They spent hours trying to arrange the tangram pieces to match the complex animal designs in the puzzle book.

Etymology

From Chinese 'tángrén' (Chinese person) + 'gram' (from Greek 'gramma', something written or drawn), referring to the puzzle's origin and visual nature.
