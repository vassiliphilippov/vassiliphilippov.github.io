fault

1. NOUN

/ fɔlt / (AmE), / fɔːlt / (BrE)
(in tennis and similar sports) a serve that is invalid because it does not land within the prescribed area or violates a rule.
invalid serve in tennis or similar sport

Examples

His third fault in a row cost him the set.
The umpire called the serve a fault because it landed outside the box.
After two faults, she was under pressure to get her next serve right.

Synset Examples

he served too many double faults

2. VERB

/ fɔlt / (AmE), / fɔːlt / (BrE)
to blame or criticize someone for a mistake or problem
blame or criticize

Examples

It's unfair to fault her for something that was beyond her control.
The coach refused to fault the team after their narrow loss.
Many people fault the government for the current economic crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blame - to consider responsible for a fault or wrong
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of someone or something

Etymology

From Old French 'faute' (lack, fault), from Latin 'fallere' (to deceive).
