afflatus

1. NOUN

/ əˈfleɪtəs / (AmE), / əˈfleɪtəs / (BrE)
a strong creative impulse or inspiration, especially as if divinely imparted
divine creative inspiration

Examples

Many poets throughout history have described feeling an afflatus when composing their greatest works.
The composer credited her symphony to a sudden afflatus that seized her one evening.
He waited for the afflatus that would guide his paintbrush and bring his vision to life.

Synset Examples

divine afflatus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspiration - the process of being mentally stimulated to do or feel something, especially to do something creative

Etymology

From Latin afflatus (a blowing or breathing on), from afflare (to blow on).
