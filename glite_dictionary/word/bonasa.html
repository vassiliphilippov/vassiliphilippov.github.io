Bonasa

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈneɪsə / (AmE), / bəˈnɑːsə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the grouse family, which includes the ruffed grouse and related species.
genus of birds (ruffed grouse)

Examples

The Bonasa genus is best known for the ruffed grouse, a bird native to North America.
Members of Bonasa inhabit temperate and boreal forests across the Northern Hemisphere.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of birds in the Bonasa genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruffed grouse - A species of grouse (Bonasa umbellus) found in North America

Etymology

From Latin *Bonasa*, the taxonomic name for this genus of grouse, based on classical nomenclature.
