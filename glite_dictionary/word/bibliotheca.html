bibliotheca

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɪbliəˈθikə / (AmE), / ˌbɪblɪəˈθiːkə / (BrE)
A collection or library of books, especially as a named or curated selection.
collection of books or library

Examples

The university's bibliotheca houses several rare manuscripts dating back to the 15th century.
She donated her private bibliotheca to the local museum for public benefit.
Researchers spent months cataloging each book in the ancient bibliotheca.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: library - a building or room containing a collection of books

Etymology

From Latin 'bibliotheca', from Greek 'bibliothēkē' (library, bookcase), from 'biblion' (book) + 'thēkē' (case).
