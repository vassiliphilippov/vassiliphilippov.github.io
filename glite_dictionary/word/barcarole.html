barcarole

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɑrkəˈroʊl / (AmE), / ˌbɑːkəˈrəʊl / (BrE)
a traditional Venetian song sung by gondoliers or a musical composition that imitates its style, typically characterized by a gentle rhythm reminiscent of rowing.
Venetian gondolier's song or imitation

Examples

The orchestra performed a beautiful barcarole inspired by the streets of Venice.
She hummed a gentle barcarole as they drifted across the lake.
The composer's new piece captures the lilting rhythm of a classic barcarole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serenade - a song or piece of music performed in the open air, often by a lover at night

Etymology

From Italian 'barcarola', from 'barca' (boat), referring to a boatman's song.
