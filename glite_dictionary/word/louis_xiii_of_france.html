Louis XIII of France

1. PROPN

/ luˈi ðə ˈθɜːrˌtinθ əv ˈfræns / (AmE), / luˈi ðə ˈθɜːˌtiːnθ əv ˈfrɑːns / (BrE)
King of France from 1610 to 1643, known for his reliance on Cardinal Richelieu as chief minister.
King of France (1610–1643)

Examples

Louis XIII of France ascended the throne at the age of nine after the assassination of his father.
Under Louis XIII of France, Cardinal Richelieu became one of the most powerful men in Europe.
The reign of Louis XIII of France was marked by the strengthening of royal authority.

Etymology

From French Louis XIII, 'Louis the Thirteenth.'
