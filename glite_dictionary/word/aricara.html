Aricara

1. NOUN

/ ˌærɪˈkɑːrə / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈkɑːrə / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people, historically living in the Dakotas west of the Missouri River.
member of a Native American people (Dakotas)

Examples

The Aricara were known for their earth lodges along the upper Missouri River.
An Aricara chief played a significant role in mediating peace between tribes.
The traditions of the Aricara continue to be passed down through generations.

Etymology

From the Native American Arikara language, also appearing in French and English records.

2. NOUN

/ əˈrɪkərə / (AmE), / əˈrɪkərə / (BrE)
the Caddoan language traditionally spoken by the Arikara people of North Dakota
a Caddoan Native American language

Examples

Linguists are working to document and revitalize the Aricara language.
She has studied several indigenous languages, including Aricara and Pawnee.
The dictionary contains many words from both English and Aricara.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Arikara - a member of the Arikara people

Etymology

From the name of the Arikara people, a Native American tribe of the northern Great Plains.
