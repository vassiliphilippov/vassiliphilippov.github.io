hospital

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːspɪtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈhɒspɪtəl / (BrE)
A large building or institution where people who are ill or injured are given medical treatment and care.
medical facility for treatment of patients

Examples

The ambulance took him straight to the hospital after the accident.
She works as a nurse in the city’s largest hospital.
After surgery, he spent several weeks recovering at the hospital.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: clinic - a smaller medical facility for diagnosis and treatment

Etymology

From Latin 'hospitale' (guest-house), from 'hospes' (guest).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːspɪtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈhɒspɪt(ə)l / (BrE)
a large building or institution where people who are ill or injured receive medical or surgical treatment and care
place for medical or surgical care

Examples

She was taken to the hospital after she broke her leg.
Many doctors and nurses work at the city's main hospital.
The new children's hospital opened last year to serve the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clinic - a place for outpatient medical treatment
  • Synonym: nursing home - a residential facility for long-term care of the elderly or disabled

Etymology

From Latin hospitāle (guest house), from hospes (guest).
