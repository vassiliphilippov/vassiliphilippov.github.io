to sweeten

1. VERB

/ ˈswiːtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈswiːtən / (BrE)
to make something sweeter in taste by adding sugar, honey, or another sweet substance
make sweeter in taste

Examples

You can sweeten your tea with a spoonful of honey.
She likes to sweeten her oatmeal with brown sugar.
The chef decided to sweeten the sauce to balance the sour flavors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar - to add sugar to food or drink
  • Antonym: bitter - having a sharp, unpleasant taste opposite to sweet

Etymology

From Old English 'swētan', derived from 'swēte' (sweet).

2. VERB

/ ˈswitən / (AmE), / ˈswiːt(ə)n / (BrE)
to make something sweeter in taste, or to make a situation or offer more pleasant or attractive
make sweeter or more attractive

Examples

She added honey to sweeten her tea.
They tried to sweeten the deal by offering free delivery.
A little sugar will sweeten the sauce and balance its acidity.

Synset Examples

sweeten a deal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enhance - to improve or make better
  • Antonym: to embitter - to make something more bitter or unpleasant

Etymology

From Old English swētan, related to 'sweet'.
