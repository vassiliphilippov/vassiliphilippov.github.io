petticoat

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛt̬iˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈpɛtɪˌkəʊt / (BrE)
A woman's light, loose undergarment hanging from the waist and worn under a skirt or dress.
woman's under-skirt undergarment

Examples

She wore a white petticoat beneath her summer dress.
The old-fashioned dress required several layers of petticoats for fullness.
After laundering, she ironed her petticoat carefully to keep it neat.

Etymology

From Middle English 'peticote', from Old French 'petite cote' meaning 'little coat'.
