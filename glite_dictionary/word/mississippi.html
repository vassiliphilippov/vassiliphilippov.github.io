Mississippi

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɪsɪˈsɪpi / (AmE), / ˌmɪsɪˈsɪpi / (BrE)
A state in the southeastern United States, located on the Gulf of Mexico, known for its history, culture, and as one of the Confederate States during the American Civil War.
a southeastern U.S. state
american

Examples

We spent our summer vacation traveling through Mississippi and exploring its historic towns.
Mississippi is known for its blues music and delicious Southern cuisine.
During the Civil War, Mississippi was a member of the Confederacy.

Etymology

From the French rendering of an Ojibwe word (*Misi-ziibi*) meaning ‘great river’.

2. PROPN

/ ˌmɪsɪˈsɪpi / (AmE), / ˌmɪsɪˈsɪpi / (BrE)
a major river in the United States, flowing from northern Minnesota south to the Gulf of Mexico, and the chief river of North America
major river in the United States

Examples

The Mississippi is known for its vital role in American history and commerce.
Many towns along the Mississippi rely on the river for transportation and trade.
Mark Twain wrote extensively about life on the Mississippi in his novels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Missouri River - a river in the United States, the longest tributary of the Mississippi
  • Degree Higher: Amazon - the largest river in South America by discharge volume

Etymology

From French 'Mississippi', from Ojibwa (Anishinaabe) 'Misi-ziibi' meaning 'Great River'.
