overexposure

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərɪkˈspoʊʒɚ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
Exposure of photographic film or a sensor to too much light, resulting in a loss of image detail.
excessive light exposure (photography)

Examples

The overexposure of the photograph made the details in the sky completely disappear.
He checked the camera settings to avoid overexposure on the next shot.
Improper lighting can easily result in overexposure when using sensitive film.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underexposure - exposure of film to too little light
  • Degree Lower: exposure - the process of exposing film to light

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessive) + 'exposure', from Latin 'exponere' (to set forth).

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvɚɪkˈspoʊʒɚ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
the act or condition of being exposed to something, such as an experience or influence, to an excessive or harmful degree
excessive exposure, often harmful

Examples

Repeated overexposure to the sun can damage your skin.
There is concern about children's overexposure to social media.
The overexposure to advertising made her immune to the messages.

Synset Examples

an overexposure to violence on television

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: exposure - the state of being exposed to contact with something

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessive) + 'exposure'.
