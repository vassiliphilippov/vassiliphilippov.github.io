ampul

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.puːl / (AmE), / ˈæm.pjuːl / (BrE)
A small sealed glass or plastic container used to hold a single dose of a drug, especially for injection.
sealed container for drug injection
medical

Examples

The nurse broke open the ampul before drawing the vaccine into the syringe.
Each medication dose is supplied in a separate ampul to ensure sterility.
Dispose of any unused ampuls according to hospital guidelines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ampoule - alternative spelling of ampul
  • Synonym: vial - small bottle for holding liquids, especially medicine

Etymology

From French 'ampoule', from Latin 'ampulla' (small flask).
