Meloidae

1. NOUN

/ məˈloʊɪˌdiː / (AmE), / mɛˈlɔɪɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of beetles known as blister beetles, whose members can secrete a toxic substance causing blisters on contact with skin.
a family of blister beetles

Examples

The Meloidae family contains many species capable of producing cantharidin.
Entomologists often study Meloidae because of their unique chemical defenses.
Several agricultural pests belong to the Meloidae, posing risks to crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blister beetle - a beetle from the Meloidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'mēloē' (a kind of beetle) + '-idae' (family suffix).
