to empanel

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈpænəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpanəl / (BrE)
to select or enlist people, especially jurors, from a list to form a panel
select or enlist (esp. jurors) for a panel
legal

Examples

The court will empanel a new jury for the upcoming trial.
It took several hours to empanel a panel of impartial jurors.
Before the proceedings can start, the judge must empanel the committee.

Synset Examples

empanel prospective jurors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impanel - to select or enroll jurors for a jury (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman empaneler, from Old French empaneler ('to enroll, list').

2. VERB

/ ɛmˈpænəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpæn(ə)l / (BrE)
to enroll or select people, especially jurors, onto an official list for duty
to enroll people, especially as jurors
legal

Examples

The court began to empanel a jury for the high-profile trial.
She was empanelled as a prospective juror in the district court.
It can take several hours to empanel enough people for a large case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impanel - alternative spelling with same meaning: to enroll as jurors

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'empaneler', from 'en-' (in) + 'panel' (list, piece of parchment).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02478126-v
Synset POS: v
