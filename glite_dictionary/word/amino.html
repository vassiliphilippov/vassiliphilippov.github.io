amino

1. NOUN

/ əˈmiːnoʊ / (AmE), / əˈmiːnəʊ / (BrE)
a chemical group consisting of one nitrogen atom bonded to two hydrogen atoms (–NH₂); commonly found in organic compounds such as amino acids
chemical group –NH₂

Examples

The amino group is a key component of all amino acids.
During the reaction, the amino radical can attach to various carbon skeletons.
Many synthetic drugs are designed to contain an amino functional group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amino group - the –NH₂ group in organic chemistry

Etymology

From Latin 'amīnum', from 'ammonia', relating to compounds derived from ammonia.

2. ADJ

/ əˈminoʊ / (AmE), / əˈmiːnəʊ / (BrE)
Relating to or containing an amino group (–NH₂), a group of atoms derived from ammonia found in organic compounds.
relating to the amino group (–NH₂)

Examples

The amino group is essential in the structure of proteins.
Some drugs contain amino compounds to enhance their effectiveness.
Scientists analyzed the amino content of the new substance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amino acid - an organic compound containing both a carboxyl and an amino group

Etymology

From Latin 'ammoniacum' (gum ammoniac), from which 'ammonia' is derived.
