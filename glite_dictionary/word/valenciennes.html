Valenciennes

1. PROPN

/ vælənˈsiːn / (AmE), / valɒ̃sjɛn / (BrE)
A town in northeastern France famous for its lace-making industry.
town in northeastern France, known for lace

Examples

The lace produced in Valenciennes was highly prized throughout Europe.
During her trip to France, she visited Valenciennes to learn about its history.
Many art lovers admire the museums found in Valenciennes.

2. NOUN

/ vəˌlɑːnsiˈɛn / (AmE), / væˌlɒnsiˈɛn / (BrE)
A type of fine bobbin lace, typically featuring floral patterns, originally made in Valenciennes, France.
type of fine bobbin lace

Examples

Her wedding dress was trimmed with delicate Valenciennes lace.
Antique dealers often seek out original Valenciennes for their collections.
The museum display included a beautiful example of eighteenth-century Valenciennes.

Etymology

Named after Valenciennes, a city in northern France where this lace was originally made.
