stingy

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɪndʒi / (AmE), / ˈstɪndʒi / (BrE)
small in quantity or quality; insufficient or meager.
insufficient or meager (amount, quality)

Examples

After the long hike, the stingy meal left everyone still hungry.
The lighting in the room was stingy, making it hard to read.
Supplies were stingy during the drought, so we had to ration carefully.

Synset Examples

meager resources
meager fare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meager - lacking in quantity or quality
  • Antonym: ample - enough or more than enough; plentiful

Etymology

From sting + -y, originally meaning 'sharp, stinging', later applied figuratively to meager giving.

2. ADJ

/ ˈstɪndʒi / (AmE), / ˈstɪndʒi / (BrE)
Unwilling to spend money, time, or resources; mean or miserly.
unwilling to spend; miserly

Examples

My landlord is so stingy that he refuses to turn on the heating in winter.
Despite being wealthy, she was surprisingly stingy with her donations.
Don't be stingy with your compliments when someone does a good job.

Synset Examples

she practices economy without being stingy
an ungenerous response to the appeal for funds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generous - willing to give or share; opposite of stingy
  • Synonym: mean - unwilling to spend money; similar to stingy
  • Synonym: miserly - showing extreme unwillingness to spend money

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps from Scots 'stinge' (to sting), or related to 'sting'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01116284-a
Synset POS: a
