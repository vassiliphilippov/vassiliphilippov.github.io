eyeful

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.fəl / (BrE)
a full or satisfying view of something, especially something remarkable or attractive
a full or satisfying view

Examples

She caught an eyeful of the stunning landscape from the balcony.
The celebrity's arrival gave the crowd quite an eyeful.
He turned just in time to get an eyeful of the fireworks exploding over the city.

Synset Examples

they wanted to see violence and they got an eyeful

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: glimpse - a brief or quick look

Etymology

Formed from 'eye' + 'ful', modeled after 'handful', mid-19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈaɪfʊl / (BrE)
a woman who is exceptionally attractive or striking in appearance, often drawing admiring attention
an exceptionally attractive woman
informal

Examples

Everyone at the party noticed the new employee; she was quite an eyeful.
The movie star stepped onto the red carpet and gave the paparazzi a real eyeful.
He described his date as an absolute eyeful in her evening gown.

Synset Examples

she was a statuesque redheaded eyeful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beauty - a beautiful woman, especially one admired for her looks
  • Antonym: plain Jane - an ordinary-looking woman

Etymology

Formed from 'eye' + '-ful', originally meaning 'a quantity sufficient to fill the eye'; later used for 'something or someone worth looking at' (1920s).
