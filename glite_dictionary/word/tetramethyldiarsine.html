tetramethyldiarsine

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˌmɛθiˌldaɪˈɑːrˌsiːn / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˌmɛθɪldiˈɑːsiːn / (BrE)
a poisonous, oily liquid with a garlicky odor, consisting of two cacodyl groups, that ignites spontaneously in dry air
poisonous, flammable oily chemical compound

Examples

Tetramethyldiarsine is highly toxic and must be handled with extreme care in the laboratory.
The garlicky smell of tetramethyldiarsine can be detected even at low concentrations.
Exposure to tetramethyldiarsine is dangerous due to its spontaneous combustion in dry air.

Etymology

From tetra- (four), methyl (a chemical group), and diarsine (containing two arsenic atoms).
