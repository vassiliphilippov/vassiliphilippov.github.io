to wet-nurse

1. VERB

/ wɛt nɜrs / (AmE), / wɛt nɜːs / (BrE)
to breastfeed and care for another person's baby as a wet nurse
breastfeed another's baby

Examples

In the past, it was common for young women to wet-nurse the children of wealthy families.
When her neighbor became ill, Maria offered to wet-nurse the newborn twins.
Some historical records mention women being paid to wet-nurse royal babies.

Synset Examples

The wetnurse suckled the infant
You cannot nurse your baby in public in some places

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nurse - to breastfeed or care for a baby

Etymology

Formed from 'wet' + 'nurse', with 'wet' referring to nursing with breast milk.
