to verbalize

1. VERB

/ ˈvɜːrbəlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɜːbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to express something in words, especially by speaking rather than writing or remaining silent
express in words or speech

Examples

It can be difficult for children to verbalize their feelings after a traumatic event.
She struggled to verbalize her thoughts during the interview.
The therapist encouraged the patient to verbalize his emotions.

Synset Examples

She talks a lot of nonsense
This depressed patient does not verbalize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulate - express an idea or feeling fluently and coherently
  • Antonym: silence - the state of not speaking or making noise

Etymology

From Latin 'verbum' (word) + English '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈvɜrbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɜːbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to convert (a noun or other part of speech) into a verb; to form a verb from another word
convert into a verb

Examples

Many English nouns have been verbalized over time, such as 'to process' from 'process'.
Linguists often verbalize new technology terms to describe emerging actions.
The word 'email' was quickly verbalized after its initial use as a noun.

Synset Examples

many English nouns have become verbalized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nominalize - to convert into a noun

Etymology

From 'verb' + '-alize', patterned after similar formations like 'realize'.

3. VERB

/ ˈvɝː.bə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.bə.laɪz / (BrE)
to express or articulate (thoughts, feelings, or ideas) in words; to put into words
express in words

Examples

It can be difficult for young children to verbalize their emotions.
She struggled to verbalize her disappointment during the meeting.
He finally verbalized what everyone had been thinking.

Synset Examples

She expressed her anger
He uttered a curse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulate - to express clearly in speech or writing
  • Synonym: express - to convey (a thought or feeling) in words or by gestures
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep (something) secret; not reveal or express

Etymology

From Latin 'verbum' (word) and the suffix '-ize', via French 'verbaliser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00942415-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈvɝbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɜːbəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to speak or write at length, often in a way that is unnecessarily wordy or verbose
to be verbose or overly wordy

Examples

The professor tends to verbalize during lectures, making it difficult for students to stay focused.
Try not to verbalize your thoughts unnecessarily when writing essays.
When asked a simple question, she would often verbalize for several minutes before giving an answer.

Synset Examples

This lawyer verbalizes and is rather tedious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramble - to talk or write at length in a confused or inconsequential way
  • Antonym: concise - using few words; not wordy

Etymology

From verb 'verbal' + -ize; influenced by Latin 'verbum' (word).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
