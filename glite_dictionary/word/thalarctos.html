Thalarctos

1. NOUN

/ θəˈlɑrk.toʊs / (AmE), / θəˈlɑːk.tɒs / (BrE)
A former or alternative genus name for polar bears, sometimes not distinguished from the genus Ursus in modern classifications.
genus of polar bears (taxonomy)

Examples

The species formerly classified as Thalarctos maritimus is now widely considered part of the genus Ursus.
Some older zoological texts refer to the polar bear under the genus Thalarctos.
Taxonomists debate whether Thalarctos should remain distinct from Ursus in classifying polar bears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ursus - the genus that includes bears, often encompassing polar bears in modern taxonomy

Etymology

From Greek 'thalassa' (sea) and 'arktos' (bear), referring to the polar bear's habitat.

2. NOUN

/ θəˈlɑrktəs / (AmE), / θəˈlɑːktɒs / (BrE)
A genus of bears including the polar bear, sometimes regarded as not distinct from the genus Ursus.
genus of bears including the polar bear

Examples

The polar bear is classified under the genus Thalarctos by some zoologists.
Older references to polar bears may list them as Thalarctos maritimus rather than Ursus maritimus.
There is debate among scientists about whether Thalarctos should be recognized as a separate genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ursus - genus that includes various bears, sometimes also polar bear

Etymology

From Greek thalassa (sea) + arktos (bear), referring to the polar bear's habitat.
