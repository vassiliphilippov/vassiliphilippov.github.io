fastness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæst.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfɑːst.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being firmly fixed in place or securely attached.
quality of being firmly fixed

Examples

The fastness of the anchor ensured the boat remained securely in place during the storm.
Architects tested the fastness of the foundation to guarantee the building would not shift.
She marveled at the fastness of the roots that bound the ancient tree to the earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stability - the state of being stable or firm
  • Antonym: instability - the state of being unstable or not firmly fixed

Etymology

From Old English fæstnes, from fæst (firm, fixed) + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæst.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfɑːst.nəs / (BrE)
a strongly fortified place, especially a secure refuge or stronghold against attack
strongly fortified place; stronghold

Examples

The rebels retreated to their mountain fastness to escape the advancing army.
Legends tell of a hidden fastness deep within the forest.
The castle served as an impregnable fastness during the long siege.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stronghold - a place that has been fortified to defend against attack
  • Synonym: fortress - a large strong building used for defending a place

Etymology

From Old English fæstness, from fæst (firm, fast) + -ness (noun suffix).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfæstnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɑːstnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being firm, stable, or resistant, especially against change, fading, or pressure
quality of being firm or resistant

Examples

The fastness of the dye ensures that the colors won't fade in sunlight.
We were impressed by the fastness of his resolve even under pressure.
Scientists measured the fastness of the new material to determine its durability.

Synset Examples

The fastness of her commitment was inspiring.
We admired the fastness of the river's current.
The fastness of the fabric surprised everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firmness - the quality of being solid and unyielding
  • Synonym: stability - the state of being stable and unchanging

Etymology

From Old English fæstnes (firmness, security), from fæst (firm).
