red-ragger

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd ˌræɡər / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌræɡə / (BrE)
a person who advocates radical change or is associated with extreme leftist political views
person with extreme left-wing views

Examples

The red-ragger's speeches always inspired the crowd.
He was labeled a red-ragger by his political opponents.
Many young activists admired the passion of the local red-ragger.

Synset Examples

The red-ragger's speeches always inspired the crowd.
The red-ragger led the protest with fierce determination.
He was known as a red-ragger in the activist community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radical - a person with extreme political or social views
  • Antonym: conservative - a person resistant to change and supportive of traditional values

Etymology

From 'red' (symbolizing left-wing politics) + 'ragger'; 'red' has long been associated with socialism or communism.
