Trajan

1. PROPN

/ ˈtreɪdʒən / (AmE), / ˈtreɪdʒən / (BrE)
(53–117) Roman Emperor noted for expanding the Roman Empire to its greatest extent and implementing major building projects.
Roman Emperor (53–117) known for expansion and building works

Examples

Trajan oversaw the construction of impressive monuments such as Trajan's Column in Rome.
Many historians regard Trajan as one of the greatest Roman emperors.
Under Trajan, the Roman Empire reached its maximum territorial extent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nerva - Roman Emperor who adopted Trajan as successor

Etymology

From Latin Traianus, Roman family name.
