hay

1. VERB

/ heɪ / (AmE), / heɪ / (BrE)
to cut and dry grass or other plants to make hay
to make hay from plants

Examples

Farmers usually hay in late June when the weather is dry.
They hayed the fields early this year to ensure a good supply of feed.
It's important to hay regularly to maintain proper livestock nutrition.

2. NOUN

/ heɪ / (AmE), / heɪ / (BrE)
grass that has been cut and dried for use as animal feed
dried grass for animal feed

Examples

The farmer spent all afternoon stacking hay in the barn.
Freshly cut hay filled the air with a sweet, earthy smell.
The cows munched contentedly on hay during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straw - the dried stalks of cereal plants after the grain has been removed, used as animal bedding or for thatching

Etymology

Old English hēg, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch hooi and German Heu.
