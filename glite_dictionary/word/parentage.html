parentage

1. NOUN

/ ˈper.ən.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpeə.rən.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
the identity and relationship of one's parents; the origin or descent of a person or animal
origin or descent; relationship to parents

Examples

The scientist studied the parentage of the rare plant species to understand its evolution.
Her royal parentage was confirmed by historical documents.
Questions about the child's parentage arose during the legal proceedings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestry - one's family or ancestral descent
  • Synonym: heritage - valued objects and qualities passed down from previous generations
  • Synonym: progeny - a descendant or the descendants of a person, animal, or plant

Etymology

From Middle French parentage, from parent (parent).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛr.ən.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpær.ɪn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
the origin or descent of a person, especially with regard to their parents or family lineage
the origin or family lineage of a person

Examples

Questions were raised about his parentage after the old family documents were discovered.
Her royal parentage was a source of both privilege and responsibility.
Some animals have their parentage traced for generations to maintain pure breeds.

Synset Examples

his entire lineage has been warriors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lineage - direct descent from an ancestor; ancestry or pedigree
  • Synonym: ancestry - one's family or ethnic descent
  • Synonym: descendance - being descended from a particular ancestor

Etymology

From Old French 'parentage' (family, lineage), from 'parent' (parent).
