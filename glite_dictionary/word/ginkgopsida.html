Ginkgopsida

1. NOUN

/ gɪŋˌgoʊpˈsaɪdə / (AmE), / gɪŋkˌɡɒpˈsɪdə / (BrE)
A class of gymnosperm plants that includes the ginkgo; used in some scientific classifications to refer to a distinct group separate from other gymnosperms.
class of gymnosperm plants (ginkgo)

Examples

The Ginkgopsida comprise only one extant species, the ginkgo tree.
Paleobotanists study fossils to understand the evolutionary history of Ginkgopsida.
Many modern classifications treat Ginkgopsida as a separate group distinct from conifers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ginkgo - a unique species of tree native to China, the only living member of its division

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Ginkgo' (the genus) and the Greek '-psida' (class).
