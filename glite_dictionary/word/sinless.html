sinless

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪnləs / (AmE), / ˈsɪnləs / (BrE)
Not having committed any sin; morally pure or innocent.
without sin; morally pure

Examples

She believed her newborn child to be truly sinless.
The painting depicted a sinless angel bathed in light.
He lived a sinless life devoted to kindness and charity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sinful - full of sin; morally wrong or wicked
  • Synonym: innocent - not guilty of a crime or offense; pure

Etymology

Formed from 'sin' (Old English 'synn') + '-less' (Old English '-lēas', meaning 'without').
