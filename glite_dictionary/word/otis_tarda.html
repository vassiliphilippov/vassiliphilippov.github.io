Otis tarda

1. PROPN

/ ˈoʊtɪs ˈtɑrdə / (AmE), / ˈəʊtɪs ˈtɑːdə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the great bustard, the largest European land bird, found across parts of Europe and Asia
scientific name of the great bustard

Examples

The Otis tarda is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and hunting.
Researchers spotted a flock of Otis tarda feeding in the open grasslands.
Conservationists are working to increase the population of Otis tarda in southern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great bustard - large, heavy-bodied bird native to Europe and Asia

Etymology

From Latin 'Otis' (a kind of bustard) and 'tarda' (slow), referring to the bird's gait.
