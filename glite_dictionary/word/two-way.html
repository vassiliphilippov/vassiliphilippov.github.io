two-way

1. ADJ

/ ˈtuˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈtuːweɪ / (BrE)
involving or allowing movement, communication, or interaction in two directions or between two parties
involving two directions or parties

Examples

Effective two-way communication is essential in any relationship.
The street became a two-way road after the construction was completed.
They signed a two-way agreement to share their research findings.

Synset Examples

a bipartite document
a two-way treaty

Related Terms

  • Antonym: one-way - involving only one direction or party
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00239629-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ tuˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈtuː weɪ / (BrE)
Involving or supported by both sides or parties; mutual or reciprocal.
mutual; involving both sides

Examples

The agreement was a two-way contract that required cooperation from both parties.
Their relationship was strictly two-way, with each person listening and responding equally.
This is a two-way street, meaning both sides must make concessions for it to work.

Synset Examples

a two-way treaty

Related Terms

  • Antonym: one-sided - involving only one side or party

Synset POS: s
