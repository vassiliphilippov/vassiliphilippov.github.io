Thamnophilus

1. NOUN

/ θæmˈnɒfɪləs / (AmE), / θamˈnɒfɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the antbird family (Thamnophilidae), commonly known as antshrikes, found mainly in Central and South America.
genus of antshrikes (birds)

Examples

The genus Thamnophilus includes several species commonly known as antshrikes.
Researchers observed a pair of Thamnophilus feeding in the dense undergrowth of the rainforest.
Birdwatchers in the Amazon often spot members of the Thamnophilus genus flitting among the thickets.

Etymology

From Greek 'thamnos' (bush) + 'philos' (loving), referring to the birds' habitat.

2. NOUN

/ θæmˈnɒfɪləs / (AmE), / θamˈnɒfɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American birds in the antbird family, Formicariidae, known as the typical antshrikes.
genus of antshrike birds

Examples

Several species belonging to the genus Thamnophilus are found throughout Central and South America.
The black-hooded antshrike is one of the many birds in the Thamnophilus genus.
Researchers studied the vocalizations of different Thamnophilus species in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

From Greek thamnos (bush) + philos (loving).
