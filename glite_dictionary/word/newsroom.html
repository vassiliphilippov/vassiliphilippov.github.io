newsroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːzˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzˌruːm / (BrE)
an office or area in a newspaper, news agency, or broadcasting station where news is gathered, written, and edited before publication or broadcast
office for journalistic news processing

Examples

She started her career as an intern in the newsroom of a major newspaper.
There was a sense of urgency in the newsroom when the breaking story came in.
Technological advances have dramatically changed how a newsroom operates in the digital age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: editorial office - office where editing and editorial decisions are made
  • Antonym: studio - room where broadcasting or recording occurs

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈnuːzˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzˌruːm / (BrE)
the department or staff in a newspaper or broadcasting organization responsible for gathering, writing, and editing news
department or staff producing news

Examples

Tension ran high in the newsroom as reporters worked to meet their deadlines.
She started her career as an intern in the newsroom of a local newspaper.
The entire newsroom celebrated when their story won a prestigious journalism award.

Synset Examples

every newspaper editor is criticized by the newsroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: editorial office - the office where editors work and coordinate newspaper content

Etymology

From 'news' + 'room', referring to the place or group producing news.

3. NOUN

/ ˈnuːzruːm / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzruːm / (BrE)
a room, especially in a library or club, where newspapers and periodicals are available to be read
room for reading newspapers and periodicals

Examples

She spent her afternoons quietly reading the latest magazines in the newsroom of the public library.
The club's newsroom was popular among members who wanted to keep up with current events.
A new newsroom was established to provide easy access to daily and weekly newspapers for visitors.

Etymology

From 'news' + 'room', referring to a space designated for news materials.
