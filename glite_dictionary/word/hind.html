hind

1. NOUN

/ haɪnd / (AmE), / haɪnd / (BrE)
an adult female deer, especially a red deer
adult female deer (esp. red deer)

Examples

We spotted a hind and her fawn grazing quietly in the meadow.
During autumn, a solitary hind is often a sign that her calf is nearby.
The forest ranger explained that the hind is usually more cautious than the stag.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stag - an adult male deer, especially a red deer

Etymology

From Old English hind, of Germanic origin, related to Dutch hinde and German Hinde.

2. ADJ

/ haɪnd / (AmE), / haɪnd / (BrE)
(of a part of an animal) situated at or nearer to the rear or back.
at or near the back (of an animal)

Examples

The horse injured its hind leg while running in the field.
Frogs use their powerful hind legs to leap long distances.
The veterinarian examined the dog's hind quarters for any signs of injury.

Synset Examples

back (or hind) legs
the hinder part of a carcass

Related Terms

  • Antonym: front - situated at or nearer to the front
  • Synonym: rear - the back part of something, especially an animal's body

Etymology

Old English 'hindan' meaning 'from behind'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ haɪnd / (AmE), / haɪnd / (BrE)
a type of fish in the sea bass family, often spotted, that resembles a grouper
a spotted fish resembling a grouper

Examples

We spotted a beautiful hind swimming near the coral reef.
The hind is known for its bright spots and resemblance to groupers.
Fishermen often catch hinds along with groupers in tropical waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grouper - large sea fish related to the hind

Etymology

From Old English *hind* (female deer); in this sense, probably influenced by visual similarity in spots.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02572115-n
Synset POS: n
