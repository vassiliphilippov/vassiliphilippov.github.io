murderousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːdɚrəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɜːdərəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being likely to murder, or having feelings of intense, violent hatred.
quality of being murderous

Examples

Her eyes glinted with a murderousness that made everyone step back in fear.
The novel explores the murderousness lurking beneath a seemingly peaceful community.
He was shocked by the sudden murderousness of her outburst.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murderous - having the desire or intent to kill

Etymology

From 'murderous' + '-ness', where 'murderous' derives from 'murder' (Old English 'mordor') plus the suffix '-ous'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrdərəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɜːdərəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being capable of, or inclined toward, murder or extreme violence
readiness to commit murder

Examples

Her eyes glinted with murderousness as she confronted her enemy.
The film portrayed the murderousness of the gang leader in chilling detail.
There was a hint of murderousness in his voice that made everyone uneasy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violence - behavior involving physical force intended to hurt or kill
  • Antonym: gentleness - the quality of being kind and mild; opposite of murderousness

Etymology

From 'murderous' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the quality or state.
