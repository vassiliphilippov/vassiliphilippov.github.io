Beaumontia grandiflora

1. NOUN

/ boʊˌmɔːnʃə ɡrænˈdɪflərə / (AmE), / bəʊˌmɔːntiə ɡrændɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
An evergreen woody climbing plant native to Asia, known for its large glossy leaves and fragrant white trumpet-shaped flowers.
climbing plant with white trumpet-shaped flowers

Examples

The garden featured a stunning Beaumontia grandiflora climbing over the pergola.
Hummingbirds were attracted to the fragrant blooms of the Beaumontia grandiflora near the gate.
She planted Beaumontia grandiflora for its lush foliage and showy white flowers.

Etymology

From genus name Beaumontia, dedicated to Diana Wentworth Beaumont, and Latin 'grandiflora' (large-flowered).
