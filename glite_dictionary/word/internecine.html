internecine

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntɚˈnɛsin / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈniːsaɪn / (BrE)
(of conflict) destructive to both sides; involving mutual slaughter or bloodshed.
destructive to both sides, especially in war

Examples

The internecine struggle within the party left it divided and weakened.
Many historians have written about the internecine wars that devastated the region for decades.
The internecine conflict caused massive casualties on both sides of the dispute.

Synset Examples

internecine war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civil war - a war between factions within the same country or group

Etymology

From Latin *internecinus* (deadly, destructive), from *internecare* (to kill).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.tɚˈniː.saɪn / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈniː.siːn / (BrE)
(of conflict) occurring within a group or organization; involving members of the same group fighting against each other.
conflict within a group or organization

Examples

The company's downfall was hastened by an internecine struggle between top executives.
Political parties often weaken themselves through internecine disputes that divide their members.
The merger talks broke down amid internecine competition among the directors.

Synset Examples

an internecine feud among proxy holders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domestic - existing or occurring inside a particular country; not foreign or international (of affairs or conflicts)

Etymology

From Latin *internecinus* (destructive), from *internecare* (to kill within, exterminate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00952437-s
Synset POS: s
