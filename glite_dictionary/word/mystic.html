mystic

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of mysticism or seeking direct communion with ultimate reality or the divine.
relating to mysticism or the mystical

Examples

The poem had a mystic quality that fascinated readers.
Her interests include mystic traditions from both East and West.
The mystic atmosphere of the old temple drew many visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystical - relating to mysticism or the supernatural

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɪstɪk / (BrE)
a person who believes in the existence of realities or knowledge beyond ordinary human comprehension, often through spiritual or religious experiences
person believing in supernatural or spiritual realities

Examples

The medieval mystic claimed to have visions of the divine.
As a practicing mystic, she spent years in meditation seeking enlightenment.
Many poets and philosophers have been influenced by the writings of a mystic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiritualist - a person who believes in communication with spirits
  • Antonym: skeptic - a person inclined to doubt or question accepted opinions

Etymology

From Greek mystikos (mystical), from mystēs (an initiate).
