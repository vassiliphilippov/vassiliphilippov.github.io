revived

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈvaɪvd / (AmE), / rɪˈvaɪvd / (BrE)
Having been given new life, energy, or strength; restored to an active or fresh condition.
given new life or strength

Examples

The revived tradition attracted many young participants this year.
Her revived interest in painting led her to enroll in art classes.
After some water and rest, the revived athlete returned to the race.

Synset Examples

stirred by revived hopes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restored - Returned to a former or original condition
  • Synonym: renewed - Made new or fresh again

Etymology

Past participle of 'revive,' from Latin 'revivere' (to live again).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈvaɪvd / (AmE), / rɪˈvaɪvd / (BrE)
restored to consciousness, life, or strength; brought back to an active or fresh state
restored to life or vigor

Examples

After a long rest, she felt revived and ready to continue the hike.
The community was revived by the arrival of new businesses and residents.
His revived interest in art led him to take painting classes again.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
