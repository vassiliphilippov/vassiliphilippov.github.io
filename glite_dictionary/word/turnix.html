Turnix

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrnɪks / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnɪks / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Turnicidae, comprising the buttonquails
genus of birds: buttonquails

Examples

The genus Turnix includes several species of small, ground-dwelling birds known as buttonquails.
Ornithologists recently discovered a new species within the Turnix genus.
Members of Turnix are characterized by their lack of a hind toe and their quail-like appearance.

Etymology

From New Latin Turnix, coined as a generic name in ornithology; possibly an irregular formation imitative of the Old French word for quail, 'turnix'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrnɪks / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnɪks / (BrE)
A genus of small terrestrial birds known as buttonquails, the type genus of the family Turnicidae.
genus of buttonquails

Examples

The Turnix genus includes several species of buttonquail found in Africa and Asia.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Turnix in northern Australia.
Birdwatchers spotted a rare Turnix near the wetlands during migration season.

Etymology

From the Latin 'Turnix', an old name for the buttonquail.
