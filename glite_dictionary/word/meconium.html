meconium

1. NOUN

/ məˈkoʊniəm / (AmE), / mɪˈkəʊniəm / (BrE)
the thick, dark green mucous material that forms the first feces of a newborn child or mammal
first feces of a newborn
medical

Examples

In the delivery room, the nurse immediately noted the presence of meconium in the infant’s diaper.
If meconium is passed before birth, it can indicate fetal distress.
The doctor explained that delayed passage of meconium may signal certain medical conditions in newborns.

Etymology

From Greek mēkōnion (poppy juice), by extension from its appearance.
