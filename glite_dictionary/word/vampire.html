vampire

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæm.paɪr / (AmE), / ˈvæm.paɪə / (BrE)
(in folklore) a dead person who rises from the grave at night to drink the blood of the living.
folkloric blood-drinking undead being

Examples

The legend of the vampire has inspired countless books and movies.
Villagers feared that a vampire was responsible for the mysterious deaths.
She dressed up as a vampire for Halloween, complete with fake fangs and a cloak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghoul - an evil spirit or phantom, especially one supposed to rob graves
  • Synonym: zombie - a corpse said to be revived by witchcraft, especially in certain African and Caribbean religions

Etymology

From French 'vampire', from German 'Vampir', from Serbian 'vampir'.
