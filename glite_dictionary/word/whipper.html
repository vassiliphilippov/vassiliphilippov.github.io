whipper

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪpər / (AmE), / ˈwɪpə / (BrE)
a person who administers punishment by whipping or using a switch
person who administers corporal punishment by whipping

Examples

The cruel whipper walked along the line, eyeing the prisoners with cold detachment.
In colonial times, a whipper was often employed to maintain discipline among convicts.
The infamous whipper became a figure of dread throughout the entire estate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punisher - person who inflicts punishment
  • Antonym: victim - person who is punished or harmed

Etymology

From 'whip' (to strike with a whip) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
