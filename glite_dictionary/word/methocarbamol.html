methocarbamol

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛθoʊˈkɑrbəmɔl / (AmE), / ˌmɛθəʊˈkɑːbəmɒl / (BrE)
a medication used as a muscle relaxant to treat skeletal muscle spasms and pain
muscle relaxant medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed methocarbamol to help manage his back spasms after the injury.
Patients taking methocarbamol should avoid operating heavy machinery due to possible drowsiness.
After a week of methocarbamol therapy, her muscle pain had decreased significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscle relaxant - drug that reduces muscle tension or spasm
  • Synonym: robaxin - trade name for methocarbamol
