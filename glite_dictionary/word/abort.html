abort

1. NOUN

/ əˈbɔrt / (AmE), / əˈbɔːt / (BrE)
an act of stopping a project or process before it is finished, especially deliberately or due to unforeseen problems
an act of terminating a process or project early

Examples

The mission had to face an unexpected abort due to technical failure.
Engineers discussed the possibility of an abort in case of complications during launch.
After reviewing the risks, the team initiated an abort to avoid potential disasters.

Synset Examples

I wasted a year of my life working on an abort
he sent a short message requesting an abort due to extreme winds in the area

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancellation - the act of deciding that an organized event will not happen
  • Synonym: termination - the act of ending something

Etymology

From Latin abortus, past participle of aboriri (to miscarry, to die away).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00035660-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ əˈbɔrt / (AmE), / əˈbɔːt / (BrE)
to terminate a pregnancy by undergoing an abortion
terminate a pregnancy

Examples

She decided to abort after learning about the medical risks involved.
The doctor explained the procedure to patients who wished to abort early in the pregnancy.
Some countries have strict laws on who can abort and under what circumstances.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to miscarry - to have a spontaneous abortion; lose a pregnancy naturally

Etymology

From Latin abortare (to miscarry), from aboriri (to miscarry, be lost).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00059670-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈbɔrt / (AmE), / əˈbɔːt / (BrE)
to stop a process, plan, or activity before it is completed, often deliberately or due to problems
terminate before completion

Examples

The engineers decided to abort the launch due to a technical malfunction.
You can abort the download if it is taking too long.
He had to abort the experiment after unexpected results appeared.

Synset Examples

abort the mission
abort the process running on my computer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to continue - to keep going without stopping
  • Synonym: cancel - to decide not to carry out an activity

Etymology

From Latin abortus, past participle of aboriri (to miscarry).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00354493-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ əˈbɔrt / (AmE), / əˈbɔːt / (BrE)
(of a process or development) to stop prematurely, especially before completion or maturity, often due to natural causes.
to cease development prematurely
medical

Examples

The experiment had to abort when the main system failed.
If the embryo does not implant properly, it may abort early in pregnancy.
Some species will abort their offspring in response to environmental stress.

Synset Examples

an aborting fetus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to miscarry - (of a pregnancy) to end before the fetus is viable

Etymology

From Latin abortare, from aboriri (to miscarry).

Source: WordNet
