Mollienesia

1. NOUN

/ mɒliˈniːziə / (AmE), / mɒliˈniːziə / (BrE)
A former genus of small, tropical freshwater fish commonly known as mollies, often kept in aquariums; now included in the genus Poecilia.
a former genus of tropical fish (mollies)

Examples

Many aquarium enthusiasts enjoy keeping Mollienesia for their vibrant colors and ease of care.
In older literature, the black molly was often classified under Mollienesia instead of Poecilia.
Some species formerly placed under Mollienesia are now widely bred in captivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molly - a small, tropical freshwater fish often kept in aquariums
