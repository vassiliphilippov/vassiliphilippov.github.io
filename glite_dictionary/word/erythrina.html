Erythrina

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌrɪˈθraɪnə / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈθriːnə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical shrubs or trees, commonly known as coral trees, noted for their usually red flowers
a genus of tropical shrubs or trees with red flowers

Examples

The beautiful red blossoms of the Erythrina attract hummingbirds to tropical gardens.
Many species within the Erythrina genus are cultivated for their striking appearance.
Botanists discovered a new variant of Erythrina in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' meaning 'red', referring to the typical flower color.
