dithyramb

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪθ.ɪˌræm / (AmE), / ˈdɪθ.ɪ.ræm / (BrE)
a passionate and often improvisational ancient Greek hymn, especially one sung in honor of Dionysus
ancient Greek passionate hymn

Examples

The choir performed a moving dithyramb to celebrate the festival of Dionysus.
Historians study the dithyramb as an important precursor to ancient Greek drama.
He read a poem structured like a dithyramb, full of exuberant praise and rhythm.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek διθύραμβος (dithurambos), denoting a choral hymn sung in honor of Dionysus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪθɪˌræm / (AmE), / ˈdɪθɪræm / (BrE)
a wildly enthusiastic or impassioned speech or piece of writing, often in praise of something or someone
wildly enthusiastic speech or writing

Examples

The critic's review turned into a lengthy dithyramb celebrating the author's genius.
His acceptance speech was less a statement of gratitude and more a dithyramb of self-praise.
She wrote a dithyramb about her favorite musician, describing them in exuberant terms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panegyric - a formal public speech or written verse, delivered in high praise of a person or thing
  • Antonym: tirade - a long, angry speech of criticism or accusation

Etymology

From Ancient Greek dithurambos, a type of choric hymn in praise of Dionysus.
