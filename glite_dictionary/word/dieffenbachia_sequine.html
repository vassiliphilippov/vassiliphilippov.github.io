Dieffenbachia sequine

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiː.fənˈbæki.ə siːˈkwiːn / (AmE), / ˌdiː.fənˈbæk.i.ə sɪˈkwiːn / (BrE)
A tropical evergreen plant with large, showy, dark green leaves, known for containing a toxin that can cause swelling of the tongue and throat.
tropical plant with poisonous leaves

Examples

The Dieffenbachia sequine is a popular choice for indoor decoration because of its striking foliage.
You should keep Dieffenbachia sequine out of reach of pets and children due to its toxic sap.
When handling Dieffenbachia sequine, it's advisable to wear gloves to avoid skin irritation.

Etymology

Named after Ernst Dieffenbach, a German physician and botanist; 'sequine' is from modern botanical Latin.
