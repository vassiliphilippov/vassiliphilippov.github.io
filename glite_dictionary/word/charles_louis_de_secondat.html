Charles Louis de Secondat

1. PROPN

/ ʃɑrl lwi də səˈkɔ̃da / (AmE), / ʃɑːl lwi də səˈkɒ̃da / (BrE)
(1689–1755) French political philosopher famous for advocating the separation of executive, legislative, and judicial powers.
French political philosopher (1689–1755)

Examples

Charles Louis de Secondat was highly influential in the development of modern political science.
Many scholars refer to Charles Louis de Secondat's theory of the separation of powers when discussing constitutional law.
The writings of Charles Louis de Secondat continue to shape democratic governments around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Montesquieu - Title used for Charles Louis de Secondat, famous as a political philosopher
