perishableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛrɪʃəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪʃəblnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being liable to decay, spoil, or destruction.
susceptibility to decay or spoilage

Examples

The perishableness of fresh produce requires careful storage and fast distribution.
Packaging has improved to reduce the perishableness of dairy products.
Scientists study the perishableness of organic materials to extend their shelf life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perishability - the quality of being perishable

Etymology

From 'perishable' (liable to perish) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).
