Nitella

1. NOUN

/ nɪˈtɛlə / (AmE), / nɪˈtɛlə / (BrE)
a genus of delicate, branching aquatic plants in the stonewort family, found in fresh water
genus of delicate aquatic stoneworts

Examples

The pond was full of Nitella, making the water appear green and feathery.
Researchers collected samples of Nitella to study its unique reproductive structures.
Many small aquatic animals depend on Nitella for shelter and food.

Etymology

From Latin, derived from 'nita', the type genus of stoneworts, plus the diminutive suffix '-ella'.

2. NOUN

/ naɪˈtɛlə / (AmE), / nɪˈtɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of delicate, branching freshwater green algae (stoneworts) found in still or slow-moving waters.
a genus of freshwater green algae

Examples

The pond was rich in aquatic plants such as Nitella and duckweed.
Researchers collected specimens of Nitella to study freshwater ecosystems.
Nitella often forms dense, green mats on the beds of slow-moving rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stonewort - a type of branched, freshwater green alga in the family Characeae

Etymology

From Latin, diminutive of 'nix' (snow), possibly referring to the whitish appearance of some species.
