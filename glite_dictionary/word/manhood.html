manhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈman.hʊd / (BrE)
the state or period of being a man rather than a boy; qualities traditionally associated with men such as courage or strength
state of being a man; manly qualities

Examples

He proved his manhood by facing the danger without fear.
The ceremony symbolized his transition into manhood.
Courage and honesty are traits often linked with manhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manliness - the quality of having characteristics traditionally associated with men
  • Antonym: womanhood - the state or period of being a woman; womanly qualities

Etymology

Formed from 'man' + '-hood', with '-hood' denoting state or condition.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmænˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈmæn.hʊd / (BrE)
the state or status of being a man, especially in terms of maturity or adulthood
the state of being a man

Examples

He reached manhood at eighteen and took on new responsibilities.
The novel explores the challenges of manhood in a changing society.
Many cultures have rites of passage that mark the transition into manhood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: womanhood - the state or status of being a woman

Etymology

From Old English 'mann' (man) + 'hād' (state, condition).
