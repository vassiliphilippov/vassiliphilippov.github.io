Falcatifolium

1. NOUN

/ fælˌkæ.tɪˈfoʊ.li.əm / (AmE), / fælˌkæt.ɪˈfəʊ.li.əm / (BrE)
a genus of tropical evergreen trees and shrubs with sickle-shaped leaves, native to Malaysia, the Philippines, New Guinea, and New Caledonia
genus of tropical evergreen trees and shrubs

Examples

The endangered Falcatifolium species are found in the rainforests of New Guinea.
Several Falcatifolium trees were studied for their unique sickle-shaped leaves.
Botanists discovered a new Falcatifolium shrub in the mountains of New Caledonia.

Etymology

From Latin 'falcatus' (sickle-shaped) and 'folium' (leaf), referring to the shape of the leaves.

2. NOUN

/ fælˌkætɪˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / falˌkatɪˈfəʊliəm / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen tropical trees and shrubs in the family Podocarpaceae, characterized by sickle-shaped leaves and native to regions from Malaysia and the Philippines to New Guinea and New Caledonia.
genus of tropical evergreens with sickle-shaped leaves

Examples

Botanists discovered a new species of Falcatifolium in the forests of New Guinea.
The sickle-shaped leaves are a distinctive feature of the Falcatifolium genus.
Several species of Falcatifolium are cultivated as ornamental trees in botanical gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dacrycarpus - a related genus of evergreen conifers in the family Podocarpaceae

Etymology

From Latin 'falcatus' (sickle-shaped) + 'folium' (leaf).
