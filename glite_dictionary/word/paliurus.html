Paliurus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪliˈjʊərəs / (AmE), / ˌpælɪˈjʊərəs / (BrE)
a genus of thorny Eurasian shrubs belonging to the buckthorn family, often with disc-shaped fruits
genus of thorny Eurasian shrubs

Examples

The Paliurus shrub is often used as a hedge due to its dense, thorny branches.
Botanists discovered a rare species of Paliurus near the riverbank.
In parts of Eurasia, Paliurus is valued for its medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Latin *Paliurus*, derived from Greek *paleiouris* (a kind of thorny shrub).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈlaɪˌjʊrəs / (AmE), / pəˈlaɪjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of thorny shrubs native to Eurasia, belonging to the buckthorn family (Rhamnaceae).
genus of thorny Eurasian shrubs

Examples

Botanists have identified several species within the genus Paliurus in Eastern Europe and Asia.
The dried fruits of Paliurus are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
Many gardens feature Paliurus shrubs as ornamental plants due to their distinctive thorns.

Etymology

From Latin *Paliurus*, from Greek *paliouros* (a thorny shrub).
