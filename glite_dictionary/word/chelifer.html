Chelifer

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛlɪfər / (AmE), / ˈkɛlɪfə / (BrE)
A genus of small arachnids in the order Chelonethida, commonly known as pseudoscorpions.
genus of pseudoscorpions

Examples

The species Chelifer cancroides is commonly found in houses and libraries.
Many Chelifer species prey on tiny insects and mites.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Chelifer in controlled laboratory conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pseudoscorpion - small arachnid resembling a scorpion but without a tail

Etymology

From Greek 'chele' (claw) and Latin 'fer' (bearing), meaning 'claw-bearer'.
