to extemporise

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɛmpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛmpərʌɪz / (BrE)
to perform or produce something, such as a speech or music, without prior preparation; to improvise
improvise; perform without preparation

Examples

She had to extemporise a response when she forgot her notes.
The actor decided to extemporise his lines during the rehearsal.
Facing an unexpected question, he chose to extemporise instead of reading from his script.

Synset Examples

he extemporized a speech at the wedding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improvise - to create or perform spontaneously or without preparation

Etymology

From Latin 'ex tempore' (on the spur of the moment).
