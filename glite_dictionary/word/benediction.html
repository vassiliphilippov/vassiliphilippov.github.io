benediction

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛnəˈdɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌbɛnɪˈdɪkʃən / (BrE)
A ceremonial prayer, especially at the end of a religious service, asking for divine blessing or protection.
prayer asking for divine blessing

Examples

The priest ended the service with a heartfelt benediction.
After the wedding ceremony, the minister offered a benediction to the couple.
We bowed our heads as the benediction was pronounced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blessing - a prayer or words asking for divine favor

Etymology

From Latin 'benedictio', from 'benedicere' (to bless), from 'bene' (well) + 'dicere' (to speak).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbɛnɪˈdɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌbɛnɪˈdɪkʃən / (BrE)
a prayer asking for God's blessing and protection, especially at the end of a religious service
prayer for divine blessing

Examples

At the conclusion of the ceremony, the priest gave a benediction to all attendees.
She bowed her head during the benediction, feeling a deep sense of peace.
The bishop delivered a special benediction before everyone left the church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blessing - a prayer asking for divine favor or protection
  • Antonym: malediction - a curse, a prayer for harm

Etymology

From Latin 'benedictio', from 'benedicere' meaning 'to bless'.
