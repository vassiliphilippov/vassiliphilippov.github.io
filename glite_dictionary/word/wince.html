wince

1. NOUN

/ wɪns / (AmE), / wɪns / (BrE)
an involuntary movement or slight grimace caused by sudden pain or distress
an involuntary grimace of pain or distress

Examples

She tried to hide her wince as the nurse inserted the needle.
A barely noticeable wince crossed his face when he heard the bad news.
The player gave a wince of pain after twisting his ankle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grimace - a facial expression showing pain or disgust

Etymology

From Middle English *wynsen* (to kick restively), probably from Old North French *guenchir* (to turn aside).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ wɪns / (AmE), / wɪns / (BrE)
to make a sudden facial expression showing pain, disgust, or dislike
make a facial expression of pain or dislike

Examples

She winced at the sour taste of the lemon.
Every time he mentioned the accident, she would wince visibly.
He winces whenever he hears nails scratching a chalkboard.

Synset Examples

She winced when she heard his pompous speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flinch - to make a quick, nervous movement as an instinctive reaction to fear, pain or surprise
  • Synonym: cringe - to react with discomfort or embarrassment; to shrink back as if in fear or disgust

Etymology

From Middle English 'wynsen', from Old North French 'wincier' (to recoil, draw back).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00008970-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ wɪns / (AmE), / wɪns / (BrE)
a sudden facial expression showing pain or discomfort
facial expression showing pain

Examples

A sharp wince crossed her face when she touched the hot pan.
His wince did not go unnoticed by the doctor during the examination.
With every injection, a small wince appeared on the child's face.

Etymology

From Middle English *wincen*, from Old North French *wincer* ('to kick, wince').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06892106-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ wɪns / (AmE), / wɪns / (BrE)
to make a sudden, slight movement back or away, often caused by pain or fear
sudden movement from pain or fear

Examples

He couldn't help but wince as the dentist poked at his sore tooth.
She winced at the sound of the car crash outside.
Whenever he hears harsh criticism, he tends to wince inwardly.

Synset Examples

she flinched when they showed the slaughtering of the calf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flinch - to make a quick, nervous movement as an instinctive reaction to fear, pain, or surprise

Etymology

From Middle English 'wynsen' (to kick restively), from Old North French 'wenchier' (to turn aside, kick).
