taunting

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɔːntɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of mocking, ridiculing, or provoking someone in a scornful or contemptuous manner
mocking or provoking act

Examples

The constant taunting from the opposing team made it hard to concentrate.
Despite the taunting, she remained calm and composed throughout the event.
His taunting was meant to provoke a reaction from his classmates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mockery - the act of mocking or making fun of someone or something
  • Synonym: insult - a disrespectful or scornful remark or action
  • Antonym: praise - the expression of approval or admiration

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɔntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɔːntɪŋ / (BrE)
expressing contempt, ridicule, or mockery, often intended to provoke or upset someone
expressing mockery or contempt

Examples

She ignored his taunting remarks and walked away.
The taunting laughter from the crowd only made her more determined.
He gave his opponent a taunting smile before the match began.

Synset Examples

derisive laughter
a jeering crowd
her mocking smile
taunting shouts of ‘coward’ and ‘sissy’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mocking - making fun of someone in a cruel or contemptuous way
  • Synonym: jeering - shouting or laughing at someone in a rude or mocking way

Etymology

From the verb 'taunt', likely from Middle French 'tanter' (to tempt, to provoke).
