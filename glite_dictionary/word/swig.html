swig

1. NOUN

/ swɪɡ / (AmE), / swɪɡ / (BrE)
a large and hurried swallow or drink, especially of an alcoholic beverage
a large swallow or drink

Examples

He took a long swig of water after finishing the race.
With a quick swig from his flask, she tried to calm her nerves.
She offered me a swig of her lemonade on the hot afternoon.

Synset Examples

he finished it at a single gulp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gulp - a large and hurried swallow

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly imitative or related to 'swig' (verb, to drink in large gulps) from 16th-century English.

2. VERB

/ swɪɡ / (AmE), / swɪɡ / (BrE)
to strike someone or something heavily, especially with a fist or bat
strike heavily with fist or bat

Examples

He raised his bat and swigged the ball with all his strength.
She swigged him on the arm before running away.
The boxer swigged his opponent right in the jaw.

Synset Examples

He slugged me so hard that I passed out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slug - to strike with a heavy blow, especially with the fist
  • Synonym: smack - to hit with a sharp blow, especially with the hand
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01418239-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ swɪɡ / (AmE), / swɪɡ / (BrE)
To drink a large amount of liquid quickly or in one swallow, especially greedily or in one draught.
drink quickly or greedily

Examples

He grabbed the bottle and took a long swig of water after his run.
She swigged her coffee before heading out the door.
They swigged beer and laughed loudly late into the night.

Synset Examples

The men gulped down their beers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gulp - to swallow hurriedly or greedily
  • Antonym: sip - to drink a small amount slowly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01204688-v
Synset POS: v
