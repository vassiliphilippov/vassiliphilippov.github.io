lackadaisical

1. ADJ

/ ˌlækəˈdeɪzɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌlakəˈdeɪzɪkəl / (BrE)
showing little spirit, energy, or enthusiasm; careless or lazy in attitude or manner
uninspired or lazy

Examples

After a lackadaisical performance at work, he was warned by his supervisor.
Her lackadaisical attitude toward her studies led to poor grades.
The team's lackadaisical defense allowed their opponents to score easily.

Synset Examples

a lackadaisical attempt
a languid mood
a languid wave of the hand
a hot languorous afternoon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: languid - showing a lack of energy or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: energetic - showing or involving great activity or vitality

Etymology

From 'lackaday', an archaic exclamation of regret (from 'alack the day'), plus the suffix -ical.

2. ADJ

/ ˌlæk.əˈdeɪ.zɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌlæk.əˈdeɪ.zɪ.kəl / (BrE)
showing a lack of energy, enthusiasm, or interest; idle or lazy, especially in a dreamy or indifferent way
lacking enthusiasm or energy; lazy

Examples

Her lackadaisical attitude towards her studies worried her parents.
The team played in a lackadaisical manner, showing little effort to win the match.
He gave a lackadaisical wave and wandered off without saying goodbye.

Synset Examples

she was annoyingly lackadaisical and impractical
a … lackadaisical, spiritless young man-about-town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: listless - lacking energy or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: energetic - showing or involving great activity or vitality

Etymology

Formed from 'lackaday', an old exclamation of regret, plus '-ical'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00295877-s
Synset POS: s
