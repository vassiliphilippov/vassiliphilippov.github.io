Parulidae

1. NOUN

/ pəˈruːlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / pəˈruːlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, colorful songbirds known as New World warblers, found mainly in the Americas.
family of New World warbler songbirds

Examples

Many birdwatchers travel to see rare members of the Parulidae during the spring migration.
The Parulidae exhibit a wide range of plumage colors, especially among males.
Researchers have recently documented new nesting behaviors in the Parulidae family.

Etymology

From New Latin Parulidae, based on the type genus Parula.
