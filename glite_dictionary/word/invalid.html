invalid

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvælɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈvælɪd / (BrE)
not legally or officially acceptable, or no longer valid, in effect, or usable.
not legally or officially valid

Examples

The contract was declared invalid by the court.
If your password is invalid, you won't be able to log in.
Her argument was rendered invalid by new evidence.

Synset Examples

the license is invalid

Related Terms

  • Antonym: valid - legally or officially acceptable or effective

Etymology

From Latin 'invalidus' meaning 'weak', from 'in-' (not) + 'validus' (strong, valid).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00941479-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌvælɪd / (AmE), / ˈɪnvəlɪd / (BrE)
a person made weak or disabled by chronic illness or injury, often requiring care
person disabled by illness or injury
archaic offensive

Examples

After his accident, he became an invalid and relied on his family for daily support.
Victorian novels often portray an invalid remaining in bed for years due to illness.
She was treated as an invalid despite wanting to resume her normal activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convalescent - a person recovering from an illness or operation
  • Antonym: healthy - in good health, not suffering from illness or injury

Etymology

From Latin 'invalidus' (weak, powerless), from 'in-' (not) + 'validus' (strong).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10233900-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈɪnvəˌleɪd / (AmE), / ˈɪnvəlɪd / (BrE)
to remove from active duty or service, typically due to illness, injury, or disability
retire due to disability or illness

Examples

After his injury, the army invalided him out of service.
Many firemen were invalided during the hazardous operation.
She was invalided home after a serious illness while abroad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retire - to leave one's job and stop working, usually because of age or health
  • Synonym: discharge - to officially make someone leave a job, especially for health reasons or at the end of service

Etymology

From Latin invalidus (weak, feeble), from in- (not) + validus (strong).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02409926-v
Synset POS: v

5. ADJ

/ ˈɪnvəˌlɪd / (AmE), / ˈɪnvəlɪd / (BrE)
Not legally or officially acceptable; having no cogency or force.
not legally or officially acceptable

Examples

The court found the contract to be invalid due to missing signatures.
If your password is invalid, you will not be able to access your account.
An invalid argument will not convince anyone, no matter how passionately it is presented.

Synset Examples

invalid reasoning
an invalid driver's license

Related Terms

  • Antonym: valid - legally or logically acceptable, having legal force

Etymology

From Latin *invalidus* (weak, not strong), from *in-* (not) + *validus* (strong, valid).
