bryophyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪ.əˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈbrʌɪ.əˌfaɪt / (BrE)
Any plant of the division Bryophyta, including mosses, liverworts, and hornworts, characterized by the absence of true vascular tissue.
non-vascular plant (moss/liverwort/hornwort)
medical

Examples

Scientists studied the bryophyte samples collected from the forest floor.
Unlike flowering plants, a bryophyte lacks true roots and stems.
Mosses are a common type of bryophyte found in moist environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moss - a small, non-vascular, green plant in damp or shady locations
  • Antonym: fern - a vascular, spore-producing plant, distinct from bryophytes

Etymology

From Greek bryon (moss) + phyton (plant).
