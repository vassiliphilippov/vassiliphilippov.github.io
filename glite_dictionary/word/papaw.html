papaw

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpɔ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːpɔː / (BrE)
A tropical fruit with yellow flesh, similar to the custard apple, commonly found in North America and parts of Asia.
tropical fruit with yellow flesh

Examples

The papaw is prized for its sweet, custard-like flavor and creamy texture.
She picked a ripe papaw from the tree in her backyard.
Many wild animals enjoy feasting on the fallen papaws in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pawpaw - a fruit with soft yellow flesh, also known as papaya or papaw

Etymology

Alteration of 'pawpaw,' possibly from Carib or Arawakan origin.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈpɔ / (AmE), / pəˈpɔː / (BrE)
A small tree native to eastern North America, Asimina triloba, that has oblong leaves and produces large, fleshy edible fruit.
North American fruit-bearing tree

Examples

The papaw tree grows wild along riverbanks in several states.
He picked a ripe papaw from the tree and ate it fresh.
In autumn, the leaves of the papaw turn a bright yellow before falling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pawpaw - alternative spelling and variant name for 'papaw'

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'pawpaw,' which itself is of Carib origin (from Carib 'papaya').
