eatage

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːtɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈiːtɪdʒ / (BrE)
Bulky food such as grass or hay provided for grazing or browsing animals, especially horses or cattle.
bulky feed for grazing animals

Examples

The field provides ample eatage for the cattle during summer months.
She assessed the eatage before deciding to bring more horses onto the pasture.
Poor eatage can affect livestock health throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forage - bulky food for animals, especially when taken by grazing
  • Synonym: fodder - coarse food for livestock consisting of plants or leaves and stalks

Etymology

From 'eat' + '-age', a noun suffix indicating related action or product.
