boustrophedonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌbaʊstrəfəˈdɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌbaʊstrəfəˈdɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of writing) written in alternate lines in opposite directions, as in some ancient scripts.
written in alternate directions

Examples

Ancient Greek inscriptions were sometimes written in a boustrophedonic manner, making them challenging to read.
The manuscript displayed boustrophedonic writing, with each line reversing direction from the one above.
Some early alphabets used the boustrophedonic style instead of the more familiar left-to-right or right-to-left formats.

Etymology

From ancient Greek boustrophēdon (turning like oxen in plowing), from bous (ox) + strephein (to turn).
