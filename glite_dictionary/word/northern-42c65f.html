northern

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɔrðərn / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən / (BrE)
of or characteristic of the region of the United States north of the Mason-Dixon line, especially with reference to its culture, politics, or geography.
relating to the northern U.S. region
american

Examples

Northern cities experienced rapid industrial growth in the early 20th century.
The debate often highlighted differences between Northern and Southern states on key issues.
Many Northern liberals supported civil rights reforms during the 1960s.

Synset Examples

Northern liberals
northern industry
northern cities

Related Terms

  • Antonym: southern - of or characteristic of the southern region of the United States
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01610335-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɔrðərn / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən / (BrE)
(of wind or weather) coming from the north.
coming from the north (wind/weather)

Examples

A strong northern wind brought cold air into the city.
The storm was intensified by a blast of northern air.
Weather forecasts predict northern gales later this evening.

Synset Examples

a northern snowstorm
the winds are northerly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: southern - coming from the south (wind/weather)
  • Synonym: northerly - coming from the north (wind/weather), often used interchangeably with 'northern'
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01605651-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈnɔrðərn / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən / (BrE)
located in or relating to the north or to the region or area lying to the north.
situated in the north or northern regions

Examples

The northern lights are visible in parts of Scandinavia during winter.
They moved to a northern city known for its cold climate.
The northern coast is famous for its rugged landscape.

Synset Examples

the Northern Hemisphere
northern autumn colors

Related Terms

  • Antonym: southern - situated in regions of the south

Etymology

From Old English 'norþerne', from 'norþ' (north) + '-ern' (suffix denoting direction or location).

4. ADJ

/ ˈnɔrðərn / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən / (BrE)
located in or toward the north; facing or directed toward the north.
situated in or facing north

Examples

The northern slopes of the mountain remain snow-covered throughout most of the year.
Many migratory birds travel to northern regions during the summer.
Our office is located on the northern edge of the city.

Synset Examples

the northern suburbs
going in a northerly direction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: southern - located in or toward the south

Etymology

From Middle English northerne, from north + -ern.
