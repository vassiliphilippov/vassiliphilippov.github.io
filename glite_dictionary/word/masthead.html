masthead

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstˌhɛd / (BrE)
the highest part of a ship's mast or a similar vertical structure
top of a ship's mast

Examples

The lookout climbed to the masthead to scan the horizon for approaching ships.
A flag was hoisted at the masthead as the ship left the harbor.
During the storm, the wind ripped the light from the masthead.

Etymology

From 'mast' (Old English mæst) + 'head' (Old English hēafod).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæstˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstˌhɛd / (BrE)
the name and details of a newspaper or magazine, typically printed at the top of the front page or editorial page
title and details of a publication

Examples

The newspaper changed its masthead after it was sold to new owners.
You can find the contact information on the masthead of the magazine.
Every issue features the masthead prominently at the top of the front page.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nameplate - the printed name of a newspaper on the front page

Etymology

Originally referred to the top of a ship's mast where flags were flown, later applied to the prominent position of a newspaper's title.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmæstˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstˌhɛd / (BrE)
The section of a newspaper or magazine, typically found on the editorial page, listing the publication's name and the names of the editorial staff and other important details.
staff and details listing in a publication

Examples

The editor's name appeared in the masthead along with the rest of the editorial team.
She checked the masthead to find contact details for submitting her article.
Every issue of the magazine includes a masthead listing its staff and contributors.

Etymology

Originally referred to the top of a ship's mast; in publishing, by analogy with prominence.

Source: WordNet
