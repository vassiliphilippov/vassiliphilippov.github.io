double-barrelled

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʌbl ˈbærəld / (AmE), / ˌdʌbl ˈbærəld / (BrE)
Having two purposes, aspects, or functions; twofold.
having two purposes or aspects

Examples

The committee devised a double-barrelled strategy to tackle both education and healthcare.
His double-barrelled proposal addressed efficiency and sustainability at the same time.
Her double-barrelled approach made the campaign both effective and memorable.

Synset Examples

our double-barreled desire to make things profitable as well as attractive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twofold - having two parts or aspects

Etymology

From 'double' + 'barrelled', originally referring to firearms with two barrels; later figurative.

2. ADJ

/ ˌdʌbəl ˈbɛrəld / (AmE), / ˌdʌbl ˈbærəld / (BrE)
having two barrels mounted side by side, especially referring to a firearm such as a shotgun
having two barrels side by side (esp. firearm)
british

Examples

He inherited a classic double-barrelled shotgun from his grandfather.
The hunter loaded the double-barrelled rifle before setting out at dawn.
Police discovered a double-barrelled weapon hidden under the suspect's floorboards.

Synset Examples

a double-barreled shotgun

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single-barrelled - having only one barrel (esp. referring to a firearm)

Etymology

Formed from 'double' + 'barrel', indicating two barrels; spelling 'barrelled' follows British usage.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00797832-a
Synset POS: a
