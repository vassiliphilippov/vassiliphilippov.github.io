Petroselinum crispum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛt.rəˌsiːlɪnəm ˈkrɪspəm / (AmE), / ˌpiːtrəʊˌsɛlɪnəm ˈkrɪspəm / (BrE)
an annual or biennial herb with aromatic, curly or flat leaves, commonly known as parsley, used in cooking and garnishing
a herb; scientific name for parsley

Examples

Petroselinum crispum is often grown in home gardens for its flavorful leaves.
The botanical name for common parsley is Petroselinum crispum.
Chefs frequently use Petroselinum crispum to add color and taste to dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsley - a common culinary herb; the common name for Petroselinum crispum

Etymology

From Latin 'petroselinum' (parsley), originally from Greek 'petroselinon', meaning 'rock celery'.
