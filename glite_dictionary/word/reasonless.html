reasonless

1. ADJ

/ ˈriːzənləs / (AmE), / ˈriːzənləs / (BrE)
Lacking the ability or capacity to think logically or rationally.
lacking capacity to reason

Examples

The movie portrayed a reasonless violence that shocked the audience.
Some believe that animals act out of reasonless instinct rather than logic.
He was attacked by a reasonless rage that he couldn't explain.

Synset Examples

a reasonless brute

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrational - not logical or reasonable
  • Antonym: reasonable - having sound judgment; fair and sensible

Etymology

From 'reason' + suffix '-less', meaning 'without reason'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈriːzənləs / (AmE), / ˈriːzənləs / (BrE)
lacking a rational or logical basis; without reason or justification.
without reason or justification

Examples

His sudden outburst of reasonless anger startled everyone at the meeting.
The villagers lived in fear of reasonless attacks by unknown raiders.
It was a reasonless decision that could not be justified by any logic.

Synset Examples

mindless violence
reasonless hostility
a senseless act

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senseless - lacking meaning, purpose, or justification
  • Synonym: irrational - not logical or reasonable

Etymology

From 'reason' + '-less' (without).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01951756-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈɹizənləs / (AmE), / ˈɹiːzənləs / (BrE)
having no justifying cause or explanation; without reason
without justifying cause or explanation

Examples

The policy changes seemed completely reasonless and confused the entire department.
He was upset by her reasonless refusal to speak to him any longer.
They protested against the reasonless destruction of the historic building.

Synset Examples

a senseless, causeless murder
a causeless war that never had an aim
an apparently arbitrary and reasonless change

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senseless - lacking meaning, purpose, or justification
  • Synonym: arbitrary - based on random choice or personal whim, rather than any reason
  • Antonym: justified - having a good reason or explanation

Etymology

Formed from 'reason' + '-less' (Old English '-lēas', meaning 'without').

Source: WordNet
