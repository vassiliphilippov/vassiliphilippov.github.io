Centranthus ruber

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtrænθəs ˈruːbər / (AmE), / sɛnˈtranthəs ˈruːbə / (BrE)
A perennial European plant with clusters of small fragrant crimson or white spurred flowers, commonly known as red valerian.
European plant with fragrant crimson or white flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with the bright blossoms of Centranthus ruber in late spring.
Many bees are attracted to Centranthus ruber due to its nectar-rich flowers.
You can often spot Centranthus ruber growing in rocky soils along Mediterranean hillsides.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Centranthus', a genus name, and 'ruber' meaning 'red'.
