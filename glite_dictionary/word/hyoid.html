hyoid

1. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪˌɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪɔɪd / (BrE)
relating to the hyoid bone in the neck, which supports the tongue
relating to the hyoid bone
medical

Examples

A fracture of the hyoid bone is rare but can occur in cases of strangulation.
The hyoid region is essential for movements involved in swallowing and speech.
Damage to the hyoid muscles may cause difficulty in breathing or speaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyoid bone - a U-shaped bone in the neck supporting the tongue

Etymology

From New Latin hyoides, from Greek hūoeidēs (shaped like the letter upsilon, υ).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪɔɪd / (BrE)
a U-shaped bone at the base of the tongue that supports the tongue muscles and is not directly attached to any other bone
U-shaped bone at the base of the tongue
medical

Examples

The hyoid bone is essential for speech and swallowing.
A fractured hyoid can indicate trauma to the neck area.
In humans, the hyoid is unique because it does not articulate directly with any other bone.

Etymology

From Greek hyoeidēs, from hyoeides (shaped like the letter upsilon, υ).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
