freehanded

1. ADJ

/ ˌfriːˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌfriːˈhændɪd / (BrE)
done by hand without the use of mechanical aids or devices, especially in drawing or writing.
done by hand, without aids

Examples

Her portrait was a freehanded sketch completed in just a few minutes.
The architect drew a freehanded diagram of the new building layout.
He preferred to create illustrations that had a spontaneous, freehanded quality.

Synset Examples

a freehand drawing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freehand - done by hand without mechanical aids, especially in drawing

Etymology

From 'freehand' (by hand, without guiding lines) + '-ed' adjective suffix.

2. ADJ

/ ˌfriːˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌfriːˈhændɪd / (BrE)
generous or giving freely; not sparing with one's resources or gifts
generous; giving freely

Examples

Her freehanded nature made her popular among her friends.
The company became known for its freehanded donations to various charities.
He was so freehanded with his praise that everyone felt encouraged.

Synset Examples

was a big tipper

a munificent gift

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generous - willing to give more of something, especially money, than is strictly necessary or expected
  • Antonym: stingy - unwilling to give or spend; ungenerous

Etymology

Formed from 'free' + 'handed', suggesting an open, unrestricted giving hand.
