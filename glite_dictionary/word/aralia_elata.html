Aralia elata

1. NOUN

/ əˈreɪliə iˈleɪtə / (AmE), / əˈreɪlɪə ɪˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A deciduous, clump-forming shrub or small tree native to Asia, sometimes found adventively in eastern North America.
an Asian deciduous shrub or small tree

Examples

The Aralia elata in our garden produces beautiful clusters of white flowers in late summer.
Gardeners in the United States sometimes plant Aralia elata for its attractive foliage and architectural form.
Although Aralia elata is native to Asia, it has established itself in parts of the eastern United States.

Etymology

From Latinized genus name 'Aralia' and species epithet 'elata', meaning 'tall' in Latin.
