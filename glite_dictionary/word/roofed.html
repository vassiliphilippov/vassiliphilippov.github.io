roofed

1. ADJ

/ ruːft / (AmE), / ruːft / (BrE)
having a roof, especially of a specified kind, often used in combination
having a roof or a specified kind of roof

Examples

The newly roofed stadium can now host events year-round.
We took shelter in a thatched-roofed cottage during the storm.
Many old barns in the area are red-roofed and well maintained.

Synset Examples

roofed picnic areas
a slate-roofed house
palmleaf-roofed huts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: roofless - without a roof
