slain

1. NOUN

/ sleɪn / (AmE), / sleɪn / (BrE)
people who have been killed, especially in battle or conflict.
people killed in battle

Examples

The memorial honors the slain of both world wars.
Families gathered to remember the slain after the battle ended.
Poets often write elegies for the slain who fell in wars long past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casualties - people injured or killed in a war or accident

Etymology

Past participle of the verb "slay," from Old English *slēan* (to strike, slay).

2. ADJ

/ sleɪn / (AmE), / sleɪn / (BrE)
killed, especially in battle or in a violent manner; used mainly in formal, literary, or historical contexts.
killed, especially in battle (formal/literary)

Examples

The old poem tells of slain heroes who fought bravely for their land.
Several slain soldiers were honored during the memorial ceremony.
He gazed solemnly at the slain beast lying at his feet.

Synset Examples

a picture of St. George and the slain dragon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slay - to kill violently, especially in battle

Etymology

Past participle of 'slay', from Old English 'slean' (to strike, slay).
