phantasmagorical

1. ADJ

/ ˌfæn.təz.məˈɡɔːr.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌfæn.təz.məˈɡɒr.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
having a bizarre or dreamlike appearance, with fantastic or surreal imagery and incongruous juxtapositions
having surreal, dreamlike imagery

Examples

The artist’s paintings are renowned for their phantasmagorical landscapes and vibrant colors.
Walking through the abandoned carnival, she felt trapped in a phantasmagorical world of distorted lights and eerie shadows.
The novel’s phantasmagorical scenes blur the boundaries between reality and imagination.

Synset Examples

a great concourse of phantasmagoric shadows
the incongruous imagery in surreal art and literature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surreal - having the qualities of surrealism; bizarre
  • Antonym: realistic - representing things accurately and as they are

Etymology

From French phantasmagorique, based on 'phantasmagoria', which comes from Greek phantasma (image, apparition).
