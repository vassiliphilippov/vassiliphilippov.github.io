luminary

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːməˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈluːmɪnəri / (BrE)
a person who inspires or influences others, especially one prominent in a particular sphere
influential or inspiring person

Examples

Several scientific luminaries attended the conference and gave inspiring talks.
She is considered a luminary in the field of medicine due to her groundbreaking research.
The awards ceremony attracted luminaries from the worlds of art, science, and politics.

Synset Examples

he was host to a large gathering of luminaries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebrity - a famous person, especially in entertainment or sport
  • Synonym: icon - a person or thing regarded as a representative symbol, especially one who is admired
  • Antonym: nobody - a person of no importance or influence

Etymology

From Latin lumen, lumin- (light) + -ary (suffix forming nouns and adjectives).
