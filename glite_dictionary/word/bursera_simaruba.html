Bursera simaruba

1. NOUN

/ bɜːrˈsɪərə sɪˈmɑːrəbə / (AmE), / bɜːˈsɪərə sɪˈmarʊbə / (BrE)
A tropical American tree that produces a reddish resin used in cements and varnishes; also known as gumbo-limbo.
tropical American tree yielding reddish resin

Examples

The resin from Bursera simaruba is commonly used in the production of varnishes.
Many gardeners in the tropics appreciate the fast growth of Bursera simaruba.
The striking peeling bark of Bursera simaruba has given it the nickname 'tourist tree.'
