to compactify

1. VERB

/ kəmˈpæk.təˌfaɪ / (AmE), / kəmˈpak.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make (something, especially data or a space) more compact; to reduce in size or increase efficiency by eliminating redundancy or unnecessary elements
make more compact; reduce size or redundancy

Examples

The software can compactify the data to optimize storage requirements.
Before transmission, it's best to compactify the data to minimize transfer time.
The algorithm was designed to compactify the data set without losing essential information.

Synset Examples

We need to compactify the data to save space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compress - to press or squeeze something so that it occupies less space, or to reduce digital data size
  • Antonym: to expand - to make larger or less compact

Etymology

From 'compact' + the verb-forming suffix '-ify', modeled on terms in mathematics and computing.
