resolving

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɑlvɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈzɒlvɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of breaking something down into distinct, clear-cut components for analysis or understanding.
breaking into clear-cut components

Examples

The resolving of complex issues is essential in scientific research.
Their resolving of the data allowed a better understanding of the underlying patterns.
Accurate resolving of musical sounds helps distinguish individual notes in a chord.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analysis - detailed examination of the elements of something
  • Antonym: synthesis - combining components to form a whole

Etymology

From Latin 'resolvere' (to loosen, release), via Old French and Middle English.
