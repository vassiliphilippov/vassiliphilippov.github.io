self-helpy

1. ADJ

/ sɛlfˈhɛlpi / (AmE), / sɛlfˈhɛlpi / (BrE)
having the qualities of or resembling self-help, especially in a superficial or overly earnest way; promoting personal improvement through self-help strategies
like or related to self-help methods
informal

Examples

His self-helpy advice sounded like it came straight from a motivational poster.
The book took a self-helpy approach to dealing with everyday problems.
She found the workshop too self-helpy and not practical enough for her needs.

Synset Examples

His self-helpy attitude inspired many around him.
The seminar focused on self-helpy techniques for success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-help - the use of one's own efforts to achieve things, especially to improve one's own situation

Etymology

Formed from 'self-help' + '-y', using the adjectival suffix to denote resemblance or characteristic quality.
