homiletical

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɑːməˈlɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌhɒmɪˈlɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of homiletics or the art of preaching and sermon writing
relating to preaching or sermon writing

Examples

The professor delivered a homiletical analysis of the biblical text, focusing on its message for modern audiences.
Her homiletical approach emphasized practical lessons drawn from scripture.
Critics praised the preacher's homiletical style for its clarity and persuasive power.

Synset Examples

homiletic speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homiletic - relating to homiletics or sermons

Etymology

From Greek homilētikos (of conversation or discourse), from homilia (conversation, sermon).

2. ADJ

/ ˌhɑːməˈlɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌhɒmɪˈlɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a homily or sermon.
of or relating to a homily or sermon

Examples

The priest's message had a distinctly homiletical tone that encouraged deep reflection.
Her book offers a homiletical approach to interpreting biblical stories for modern readers.
Despite its homiletical style, the lecture resonated with both religious and secular audiences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sermonic - relating to a sermon

Etymology

From Medieval Latin homileticus, from Greek homilētikos (of conversation or discourse), from homilia (discourse, sermon).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
