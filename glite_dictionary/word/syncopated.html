syncopated

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪŋ.kəˌpeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.kəʊ.peɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of music or rhythm) characterized by emphasizing beats or parts of beats that are normally unaccented, creating a displaced or unexpected rhythm.
with unexpected or off-beat rhythm

Examples

The band played a syncopated jazz tune that got everyone moving to the rhythm.
Her piano piece features a highly syncopated left-hand accompaniment.
Many pop songs today use a syncopated beat to add complexity and excitement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straight - (of rhythm) with evenly spaced, regularly accented beats

Etymology

From Medieval Latin syncopatus, past participle of syncopare (to cut short, to syncope).
