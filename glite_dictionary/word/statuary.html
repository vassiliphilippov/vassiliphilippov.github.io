statuary

1. ADJ

/ ˈstætʃuˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈstatjʊəri / (BrE)
of, relating to, or suitable for statues.
relating to statues

Examples

The garden featured numerous statuary columns designed to evoke an ancient atmosphere.
Her statuary grace made her the perfect model for the artist.
The hall was adorned with statuary marble, lending it an air of classical elegance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sculptural - relating to or resembling sculpture

Etymology

From Latin 'statuarius' (of or belonging to a statue).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstætʃuˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈstætʃuəri / (BrE)
statues collectively, especially as a group or type of artistic works
statues considered as a group

Examples

The museum's impressive statuary includes works from Ancient Greece and Rome.
The garden was adorned with classical statuary amid the blooming flowers.
New statuary was commissioned to honor the city's founders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statue - a carved or cast figure of a person or animal, especially one that is life-size or larger

Etymology

From Latin statua (statue) + -ary, denoting a collection or associated with.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
