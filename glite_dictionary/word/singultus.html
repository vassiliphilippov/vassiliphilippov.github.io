singultus

1. NOUN

/ sɪŋˈɡʌltəs / (AmE), / sɪŋˈɡʌltəs / (BrE)
(Medical) A reflex involving sudden, involuntary spasms of the diaphragm, typically producing the characteristic sound known as hiccups.
medical term for hiccups
medical

Examples

Persistent singultus may require medical evaluation if it lasts more than 48 hours.
The patient complained of frequent episodes of singultus after surgery.
Certain medications may induce singultus as a side effect.

Synset Examples

how do you cure the hiccups?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hiccup - a sharp, involuntary intake of air with a sound, commonly known as a 'hiccup'

Etymology

From Latin 'singultus' (a sob, hiccup, or gasp).
