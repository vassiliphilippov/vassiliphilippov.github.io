lofty

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔfti / (AmE), / ˈlɒfti / (BrE)
of high moral or intellectual value; elevated in character, ideals, or style
elevated in morals or intellect

Examples

She admired his lofty ideals and commitment to justice.
The novel is written in a lofty and poetic style that challenges readers.
Despite his lofty ambitions, he remained humble and approachable.

Synset Examples

an exalted ideal
argue in terms of high-flown ideals
a noble and lofty concept
a grand purpose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exalted - of high moral or intellectual value
  • Synonym: noble - having or showing fine personal qualities or high moral principles
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low view of one's importance

Etymology

From Old Norse 'lopt' (air, sky) + '-y'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɔfti / (AmE), / ˈlɒfti / (BrE)
having or showing great dignity, nobility, or elevated status; impressive in manner or appearance
of great dignity or elevated status

Examples

The king always carried himself with a lofty bearing, commanding respect from all who met him.
Her lofty ideals inspired those around her to strive for greatness.
The lofty pillars of the ancient temple gave the building a sense of grandeur and dignity.

Synset Examples

a gallant pageant
lofty ships
majestic cities
proud alpine peaks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: majestic - having or showing impressive beauty or dignity
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From Middle English 'lofte' (sky, air), from Old Norse 'lopt' (air, sky).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01288228-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈlɔfti / (AmE), / ˈlɒfti / (BrE)
Of great height; impressively tall, especially so as to stand out above others.
of great or impressive height

Examples

The lofty trees in the forest seemed to touch the sky.
From the balcony, she admired the lofty spires of the ancient castle.
The climbers gazed up at the lofty peaks ahead, feeling both awe and excitement.

Synset Examples

an eminent peak
lofty mountains
the soaring spires of the cathedral
towering icebergs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: towering - extremely high or tall, especially standing out above others
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of importance or height

Etymology

From Old Norse 'lopt' (air, sky) + English '-y'.
