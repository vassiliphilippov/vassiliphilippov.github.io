slave

1. NOUN

/ sleɪv / (AmE), / sleɪv / (BrE)
a person who is legally owned by another person and is forced to work for them without pay or freedom
person legally owned by another

Examples

The ancient Greeks and Romans often kept slaves in their households.
She was determined to escape a life as a slave and find her freedom.
In the 19th century, many slaves in America sought liberation through the Underground Railroad.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: servant - a person employed to perform domestic duties
  • Antonym: master - a person who owns a slave or has control over others

Etymology

From Old French esclave, from Medieval Latin sclavus (Slav), because Slavic peoples were often enslaved in early Europe.

2. NOUN

/ sleɪv / (AmE), / sleɪv / (BrE)
A person who is so strongly influenced or controlled by something or someone that they are unable to act independently.
person dominated by influence or person

Examples

He became a slave to his work, neglecting everything else in his life.
Some people are slaves to technology, unable to put their phones down.
She realized she was a slave to her own perfectionism.

Synset Examples

a slave to fashion
a slave to cocaine
his mother was his abject slave

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: servant - a person employed to perform domestic duties for others
  • Antonym: master - a person with control or power over others; the opposite of being dominated

Etymology

From Old French 'esclave', from Medieval Latin 'sclavus' (Slav, slave), originally referring to people from Eastern Europe enslaved in the Middle Ages.

3. NOUN

/ sleɪv / (AmE), / sleɪv / (BrE)
A person who works extremely hard or is excessively devoted to their work or a particular cause, often used figuratively.
person who works extremely hard (figurative)

Examples

Ever since the new manager started, I've been a slave to my desk, working late every night.
He became a slave to his job, rarely taking time off for himself.
Those students are slaves to their studies during the exam period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drudge - a person who does hard, menial, or tedious work
  • Synonym: toiler - a person who works very hard, especially at dull or difficult tasks

Etymology

From Old French esclave, from Medieval Latin sclavus (Slav, slave), originally referring to Slavs captured and sold as slaves in the Middle Ages.

4. VERB

/ sleɪv / (AmE), / sleɪv / (BrE)
to work very hard, especially for a long time and with little rest or reward
work very hard

Examples

She had to slave over the project for months to meet the deadline.
Many people slave away in the kitchen during the holidays to prepare elaborate meals.
He had been slaving at his desk all night to finish the report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toil - to work extremely hard or incessantly
  • Antonym: rest - to cease work or movement in order to relax

Etymology

From Old French esclave, from Medieval Latin sclavus (slave), originally referring to Slavs who were often enslaved in the Middle Ages.
