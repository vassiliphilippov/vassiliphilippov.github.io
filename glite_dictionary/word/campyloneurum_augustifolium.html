Campyloneurum augustifolium

1. NOUN

/ kæm.pɪ.loʊˈnɪə.rəm ɔːˌgʌs.tɪˈfoʊ.li.əm / (AmE), / kæm.pɪ.ləʊˈnɪə.rəm ɔːˌgʌs.tɪˈfəʊ.li.əm / (BrE)
A species of fern native to Florida, the West Indies, Mexico, and South America, distinguished by its shorter and narrower leaves compared to the Florida strap fern.
a species of fern with narrow leaves

Examples

The Campyloneurum augustifolium is often found growing in humid forests across South America.
Botanists studied the unique characteristics of Campyloneurum augustifolium for their research on fern diversity.
Unlike its relative, the Florida strap fern, Campyloneurum augustifolium has distinctly shorter and narrower leaves.

Etymology

From Greek 'kampylos' (curved) and Latin 'augustus' (narrow) + 'folium' (leaf).
