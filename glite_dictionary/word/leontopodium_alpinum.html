Leontopodium alpinum

1. NOUN

/ liˌɑːntəˈpoʊdiəm ælˈpaɪnəm / (AmE), / liˌɒntəˈpəʊdiəm ælˈpiːnəm / (BrE)
A small alpine perennial plant native to Europe, with woolly white leaves and star-shaped flower heads, commonly known as edelweiss.
alpine plant known as edelweiss

Examples

The rare Leontopodium alpinum flourishes on rocky slopes high in the Alps.
Many hikers climb just to catch a glimpse of the blooming Leontopodium alpinum.
The white, star-shaped flowers of Leontopodium alpinum have inspired legends and songs throughout Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edelweiss - common name for Leontopodium alpinum, an alpine plant with white, star-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'leōn' (lion) + 'podion' (little foot) and Latin 'alpinum' (of the Alps).
