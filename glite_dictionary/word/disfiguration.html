disfiguration

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.fɪˈɡjʊr.eɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.fɪˈɡjʊə.reɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of spoiling or damaging the appearance or surface of something; the condition of being disfigured
the act of damaging appearance

Examples

A large crack in the wall caused significant disfiguration of the mural.
The disfiguration of the statue after the accident was heartbreaking for local residents.
He spoke against the disfiguration of the natural landscape by new developments.

Synset Examples

the defacement of an Italian mosaic during the Turkish invasion
he objected to the dam's massive disfigurement of the landscape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defacement - the act of deliberately spoiling the appearance of something
  • Synonym: disfigurement - the state of being disfigured; the result of damaging appearance

Etymology

From Latin 'disfiguratio', from 'disfigurare' (to disfigure).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.fɪˈɡjʊr.eɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.fɪˈɡjʊə.reɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an appearance that has been spoiled, misshapen, or made less attractive, especially by injury or damage
spoiled or misshapen appearance

Examples

The accident left him with significant disfiguration on his arms and legs.
Modern reconstructive surgery can help reduce facial disfiguration after severe injuries.
The disfiguration of the statue upset art lovers throughout the city.

Synset Examples

there were distinguishing disfigurements on the suspect's back
suffering from facial disfiguration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disfigurement - the act of spoiling or injuring appearance

Etymology

From Latin 'disfiguratio', from 'dis-' (apart) + 'figurare' (to form, shape).
