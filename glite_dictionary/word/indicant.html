indicant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndɪkənt / (AmE), / ˈɪndɪkənt / (BrE)
a number or ratio derived from observed data that indicates change or trends, especially over time
indicator value from data

Examples

The unemployment rate is an important indicant of economic health.
Researchers tracked each indicant to measure the policy’s effectiveness.
Rising prices served as a key indicant of inflation over the past year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indicator - something that provides an indication or sign

Etymology

From Latin 'indicare' (to indicate) with suffix '-ant' forming nouns meaning an agent or thing that performs the action.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndɪkənt / (AmE), / ˈɪndɪkənt / (BrE)
something that serves to indicate or suggest; a sign, symptom, or indication.
sign or indicator

Examples

Fever is one common indicant of infection in patients.
Rising unemployment rates can be an indicant of economic distress.
Physical symptoms act as an indicant of underlying health issues.

Synset Examples

an indication of foul play
indications of strain
symptoms are the prime indicants of disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indicator - something that indicates
  • Synonym: symptom - a sign or indication of something, especially of disease

Etymology

From Latin indicans (present participle of indicare, meaning 'to indicate').

Synset POS: n
