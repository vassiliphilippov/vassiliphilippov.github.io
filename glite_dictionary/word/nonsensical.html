nonsensical

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈsɛnsɪkəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈsɛnsɪkəl / (BrE)
having no meaning that can be understood; making no sense
having no intelligible meaning

Examples

The theory he proposed sounds completely nonsensical to experts in the field.
She dismissed his nonsensical remarks with a wave of her hand.
The children invented a nonsensical language just for fun.

Synset Examples

nonsense syllables
a nonsensical jumble of words

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sensical - having a clear or understandable meaning
  • Synonym: meaningless - lacking meaning or significance

Etymology

Formed from 'nonsense' + suffix '-ical'.

2. ADJ

/ nɑnˈsɛnsɪkəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈsɛnsɪkəl / (BrE)
so unreasonable, illogical, or absurd as to be worthy of ridicule or lacking in sense
absurd or lacking sense

Examples

His explanation for missing work was completely nonsensical and no one believed him.
The film was criticized for its nonsensical plot that confused the audience.
She grew frustrated with his nonsensical arguments during the debate.

Synset Examples

the absurd excuse that the dog ate his homework
that's a cockeyed idea
ask a nonsensical question and get a nonsensical answer
a contribution so small as to be laughable
it is ludicrous to call a cottage a mansion
a preposterous attempt to turn back the pages of history
her conceited assumption of universal interest in her rather dull children was ridiculous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absurd - wildly unreasonable or illogical; ridiculous
  • Synonym: ridiculous - deserving or inviting derision or mockery; absurd
  • Antonym: sensible - done or chosen in accordance with wisdom or prudence; reasonable

Etymology

Formed from 'nonsense' + '-ical', patterned after 'sensical'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02581199-s
Synset POS: s
