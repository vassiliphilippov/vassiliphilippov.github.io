distressful

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstrɛs.fəl / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɛs.fəl / (BrE)
causing distress, worry, or anxiety; making someone feel upset or troubled
causing distress or worry

Examples

The news of the accident was especially distressful for her family.
She found the experience of losing her job to be deeply distressful.
Witnessing the aftermath of the disaster was a distressful sight for everyone involved.

Synset Examples

distressing (or disturbing) news
lived in heroic if something distressful isolation
a disturbing amount of crime
a revelation that was most perturbing
a new and troubling thought
in a particularly worrisome predicament
a worrying situation
a worrying time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distressing - causing distress or anxiety
  • Synonym: troubling - causing worry or concern
  • Antonym: comforting - making one feel less worried or distressed

Etymology

From distress + -ful, modelled after words like 'stressful'.
