piolet

1. NOUN

/ piː.oʊˈleɪ / (AmE), / piː.ɒˈleɪ / (BrE)
An ax with a pointed end, used by mountain climbers for cutting footholds in ice.
mountaineering ice ax

Examples

The climber gripped his piolet to carve a step into the glacier.
She packed her piolet, crampons, and rope for the ascent.
A sturdy piolet is essential for traversing steep, icy slopes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ice axe - an ax designed for use in climbing on ice

Etymology

From French 'piolet', diminutive of 'pieu' (stake, pick).
