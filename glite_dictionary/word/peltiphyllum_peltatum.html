Peltiphyllum peltatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛl.tɪˈfɪl.əm pɛlˈteɪ.təm / (AmE), / ˌpɛl.tɪˈfɪl.əm pɛlˈtɛɪ.təm / (BrE)
a rhizomatous perennial herb with large round peltate leaves and clusters of white to bright pink flowers, native to stream banks in the Sierra Nevada of California
perennial herb native to the Sierra Nevada

Examples

The Peltiphyllum peltatum blooms in early spring along mountain streams in California.
Gardeners admire Peltiphyllum peltatum for its striking foliage and adaptability to wet soils.
The large, shield-shaped leaves of Peltiphyllum peltatum create a dramatic effect in woodland gardens.

Etymology

From Greek 'peltē' (small shield) and 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to its leaf shape.
