duce

1. NOUN

/ ˈduː.tʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˈduː.tʃeɪ / (BrE)
the leader, especially as a title for Benito Mussolini, the Fascist leader of Italy
title for Mussolini; leader

Examples

Benito Mussolini was known as Duce during his rule over Italy.
The Italian Fascists pledged their loyalty to their duce.
Historians often debate the decisions made by the Duce in World War II.

Synset Examples

Mussolini was called Il Duce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: führer - title given to Adolf Hitler meaning 'leader' in German

Etymology

From Italian 'duce' (leader), from Latin 'dux' (leader, commander).
