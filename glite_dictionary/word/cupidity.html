cupidity

1. NOUN

/ kjuˈpɪdəti / (AmE), / kjuːˈpɪdɪti / (BrE)
extreme greed for money or possessions; an intense desire for material wealth.
extreme greed for wealth

Examples

The CEO's cupidity led him to make risky decisions that ultimately destroyed the company.
Stories of cupidity and ambition have shaped the course of history for centuries.
He looked at the treasure with barely concealed cupidity, unable to resist its lure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power
  • Antonym: generosity - willingness to give more than is necessary or expected

Etymology

From Latin cupīdō, cupīdinis (desire), from cupere (to desire).
