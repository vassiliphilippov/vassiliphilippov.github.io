reclassification

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːˌklæs.ɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌklɑː.sɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of classifying something again, usually by assigning it to a different category or group
the act of assigning to a new category

Examples

The reclassification of the property changed its tax status significantly.
A recent reclassification of several diseases was announced by the medical board.
His job was impacted by the reclassification of company roles and responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification - the act of arranging into categories

Etymology

From re- (again) + classification (the act of classifying).
