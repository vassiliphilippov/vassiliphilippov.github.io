Hydrangea petiolaris

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdreɪndʒiə ˌpɛtiəˈlɑrɪs / (AmE), / hʌɪˈdrandʒɪə ˌpɛtɪəˈlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A species of deciduous climbing plant with aerial roots and large, flat flower heads, cultivated as an ornamental.
climbing hydrangea species

Examples

The Hydrangea petiolaris climbed gracefully up the brick wall, displaying its showy white flower clusters.
Gardeners often choose Hydrangea petiolaris for shaded fences and trellises due to its vigorous growth.
In late spring, the Hydrangea petiolaris blooms, attracting pollinators to its large, flat flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climbing hydrangea - common name for Hydrangea petiolaris and closely related forms

Etymology

From genus name Hydrangea (Greek 'hydor' meaning water and 'angeion' meaning vessel) and species epithet 'petiolaris' (Latin, meaning 'having a petiole').
