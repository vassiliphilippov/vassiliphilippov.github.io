dongle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdɒŋɡəl / (BrE)
a small electronic device that connects to a computer to enable, protect, or unlock certain software or functions
electronic device enabling software or functions

Examples

The software won't work unless you plug the dongle into your computer's USB port.
Many companies use a dongle as a security measure to prevent unauthorized copying.
He lost the dongle, so he couldn't access the protected application.

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from English slang "dong" (referring to a device or pin) with a diminutive ending.
