mononucleate

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɑːnoʊˈnuːkliˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˈnjuːklɪeɪt / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having only one nucleus.
having one nucleus (biology)
medical

Examples

Most white blood cells in human blood are mononucleate in structure.
The scientist identified the sample as mononucleate under the microscope.
Unlike muscle fibers, which are multinucleate, these amoebae remain strictly mononucleate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: binucleate - having two nuclei
  • Antonym: multinucleate - having more than one nucleus

Etymology

From mono- (one) + Latin nucleus (kernel, nucleus) + -ate (having).
