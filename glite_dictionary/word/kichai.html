Kichai

1. NOUN

/ kiˈtʃaɪ / (AmE), / kiˈtʃaɪ / (BrE)
A member of a Native American Caddo people formerly living in north central Texas.
member of a Caddo Native American people

Examples

The Kichai were skilled traders who interacted with neighboring tribes in Texas.
Some descendants of the Kichai people still preserve their cultural heritage today.
Anthropologists have studied the language and traditions of the Kichai for decades.

Etymology

Originally from Caddoan languages, name transcribed into English.
