Adiantum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæd.iˈæn.təm / (AmE), / ˌæd.iˈæn.təm / (BrE)
a genus of ferns, commonly known as maidenhair ferns, found worldwide and known for their delicate, fan-shaped leaf segments
genus of maidenhair ferns

Examples

The delicate leaves of the Adiantum make it popular among houseplant enthusiasts.
Many species within the Adiantum genus are used in traditional medicine.
Adiantum ferns thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair fern - a common name for ferns in the genus Adiantum

Etymology

From Greek adianton, meaning 'unwetted,' referring to the plant's water-repellent leaves.
