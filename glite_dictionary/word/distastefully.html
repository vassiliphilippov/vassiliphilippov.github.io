distastefully

1. ADV

/ dɪsˈteɪstfəli / (AmE), / dɪsˈteɪstfəli / (BrE)
in a way that causes dislike or disgust; in an offensive or unpleasant manner
in an offensive or unpleasant manner

Examples

She spoke distastefully about the event, making her feelings clear to everyone.
The movie was criticized for its distastefully graphic scenes that upset many viewers.
He described the situation distastefully, focusing on all the negative details.

Synset Examples

a distastefully explicit report on the Royal couple's marital life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offensively - in a manner that causes offense or displeasure

Etymology

From 'distasteful' + '-ly', where 'distasteful' derives from 'distaste' (to dislike).

2. ADV

/ dɪsˈteɪstfəlli / (AmE), / dɪsˈteɪstfəli / (BrE)
in a manner that shows dislike or disgust; in a way that is offensive or unpleasant
in an offensive or unpleasant way

Examples

She looked distastefully at the messy kitchen before starting to clean.
He rejected the proposal distastefully, making his disapproval obvious to everyone in the room.
The critic wrote distastefully about the film's violence and lack of subtlety.

Synset Examples

the beggar was disgustingly filthy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgustingly - in a disgusting or offensive manner

Etymology

From 'distasteful' + '-ly', where 'distasteful' derives from 'distaste' + '-ful'.
