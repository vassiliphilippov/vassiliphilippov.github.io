manoeuvre

1. NOUN

/ məˈnuːvər / (AmE), / məˈnuːvə / (BrE)
an action or movement designed to avoid or outwit an opponent, especially in a competitive or tactical situation
a tactical action to evade an opponent
british

Examples

The player made a quick manoeuvre to dodge his defender and score.
Outflanking the enemy was a clever manoeuvre that changed the course of the battle.
Her sudden manoeuvre caught her opponent off guard during the chess match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maneuver - an action or movement designed to avoid an opponent (American spelling)

Etymology

From French 'manœuvre', from Italian 'manovra', from Latin 'manu operari' (to work by hand).
