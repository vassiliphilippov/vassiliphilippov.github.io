express

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsprɛs / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprɛs / (BrE)
a mail service or item of mail delivered quickly by a special, rapid system
quick mail delivery service

Examples

She sent the documents by express to ensure quick delivery.
The package arrived early because it was shipped via express.
I always use express when I need something delivered urgently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courier - a person or service delivering messages, packages, or mail

Etymology

From French *exprès* (special, intended for a purpose), from Latin *expressus* (clearly presented).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06276128-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsprɛs / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprɛs / (BrE)
a train or bus that travels quickly between destinations, stopping only at a few main stations or stops
fast train or bus with few stops

Examples

This station is not served by the express, only by local trains.
The express to Boston leaves at precisely 8:00 a.m. every morning.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: local - a train or bus that stops at all or most stations

Etymology

From Latin 'expressus', meaning 'distinctly presented'; noun sense developed from the idea of 'express' meaning rapid, direct transport.
