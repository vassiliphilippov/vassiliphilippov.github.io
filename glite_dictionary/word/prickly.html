prickly

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɪkli / (AmE), / ˈprɪkli / (BrE)
covered with or having sharp points such as thorns, spines, or barbs
covered with sharp points or thorns

Examples

The cactus has a prickly surface that can be painful to touch.
Hedgehogs are known for their prickly spines, which protect them from predators.
He avoided walking through the prickly bush to prevent scratching his legs.

Synset Examples

a horse with a short bristly mane
bristly shrubs
burred fruits
setaceous whiskers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny - covered with spines or thorns
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even or flat surface without bumps or projections

Etymology

From prick + -ly, meaning 'having pricks or points'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈprɪkli / (AmE), / ˈprɪkli / (BrE)
(of a person) easily irritated or offended; quick to take offense.
easily irritated or offended

Examples

Whenever he is tired, he becomes especially prickly and impatient with everyone around him.
The manager was prickly during the meeting and snapped at minor questions.
Her prickly reaction to criticism surprised her coworkers.

Synset Examples

bristly exchanges between the White House and the press
he became prickly and spiteful
witty and waspish about his colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Synonym: touchy - easily upset or offended
  • Antonym: even-tempered - not easily annoyed or angered
