transfusion

1. NOUN

/ trænsˈfjuːʒən / (AmE), / trænsˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
the act of pouring a liquid from one vessel or container into another
pouring liquid from one vessel to another

Examples

The careful transfusion of wine from one cask to another preserved its delicate flavor.
Ancient alchemists believed that the transfusion of liquids could change their properties.
Before storage, the transfusion of chemicals into clean bottles was essential to maintain purity.

Etymology

From Latin 'transfusio' (a pouring from one to another), from 'trans-' (across) + 'fundere' (to pour).

2. NOUN

/ trænsˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / trænsˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
the process of introducing blood or blood plasma into a vein or artery, usually as a medical treatment
introduction of blood into a vein or artery
medical

Examples

After surgery, the patient required a transfusion to replace lost blood.
Doctors performed a transfusion to treat the severe anemia.
The availability of safe transfusion services is vital in emergency situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood transfusion - the direct transfer of blood into a person's circulation

Etymology

From Latin 'transfusio' (a pouring from one vessel to another), from 'transfundere' (to pour from one to another).
