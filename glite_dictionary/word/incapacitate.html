to incapacitate

1. VERB

/ ˌɪn.kəˈpæs.ɪ.teɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kəˈpæs.ɪ.teɪt / (BrE)
to make someone or something unable to perform a normal function or action, especially by injury or other means
to make unable to act or function

Examples

The accident incapacitated him for several months.
The virus can incapacitate a computer system within minutes.
Strong sedatives were used to incapacitate the suspect before arrest.

Synset Examples

disable this command on your computer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disable - to make unable to function normally
  • Antonym: to enable - to make able or capable of doing something

Etymology

From Latin incapacitas (incapacity), from in- (not) + capacitas (capacity).

2. VERB

/ ˌɪnkəˈpæsɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˈpæsɪteɪt / (BrE)
to make someone unable to function or do something, especially by injuring them permanently or severely
render unable to function, often by injury

Examples

The accident incapacitated him for the rest of his life.
A serious injury can incapacitate a professional athlete, ending their career prematurely.
Doctors tried to save the patient's arm, but the damage was enough to incapacitate it permanently.

Synset Examples

He was disabled in a car accident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disable - to make unable to use a limb or normal function, especially by injury
  • Synonym: cripple - to cause severe and permanent injury, preventing normal function

Etymology

From Latin 'incapacitatus', past participle of 'incapacitare' (to make incapable), from 'in-' (not) + 'capacitas' (capacity).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
