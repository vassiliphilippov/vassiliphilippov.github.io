Parthenium argentatum

1. NOUN

/ pɑːrˈθiːniəm ˌɑːrdʒənˈteɪtəm / (AmE), / pɑːˈθiːnɪəm ˌɑːdʒənˈteɪtəm / (BrE)
a much-branched shrub with silvery leaves and small white flowers, native to Texas and northern Mexico, cultivated as a source of natural rubber
shrub native to Texas and northern Mexico, rubber source

Examples

Parthenium argentatum is being studied as a renewable source of natural rubber in the United States.
Farmers in northern Mexico have begun cultivating Parthenium argentatum for its economic value.
The leaves of Parthenium argentatum have a distinctive silvery appearance and small white flowers.

Etymology

From the genus name Parthenium (from Greek parthenion, a plant), and Latin argentatum 'silvery'.
