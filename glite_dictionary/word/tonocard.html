Tonocard

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊnəˌkɑrd / (AmE), / ˈtəʊnəkɑːd / (BrE)
A trademarked antiarrhythmic drug used to treat serious ventricular arrhythmias when other drugs have not been effective.
antiarrhythmic drug for ventricular arrhythmias
medical

Examples

The cardiologist prescribed Tonocard after other medications proved ineffective.
Patients taking Tonocard often require regular monitoring of their heart rhythm.
Serious side effects may occur with Tonocard, so it's reserved for severe cases.
