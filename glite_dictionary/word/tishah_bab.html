Tishah b'Ab

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɪʃə bəˈɑb / (AmE), / ˌtɪʃə bəˈab / (BrE)
(in Judaism) a major Jewish fast day commemorating the destruction of the First and Second Temples in Jerusalem.
Jewish fast day commemorating the destruction of the Temples

Examples

Jews around the world observe Tishah b'Ab by fasting and mourning the destruction of the ancient temples.
Many synagogues hold special services on Tishah b'Ab to remember the tragedies that befell the Jewish people.
The book of Lamentations is traditionally read on Tishah b'Ab as part of the commemoration.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Tish‘āh b’Āvā' (ninth of Av), referring to the date in the Hebrew calendar.
