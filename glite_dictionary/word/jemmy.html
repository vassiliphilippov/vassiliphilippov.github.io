jemmy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛmi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛmi / (BrE)
a short, sturdy crowbar, especially used for prying open doors or windows
short crowbar (esp. British)
british

Examples

The burglar used a jemmy to force open the window.
He kept a jemmy in his toolbox for difficult demolition jobs.
Police found a jemmy at the scene of the break-in.

Synset Examples

in Britain they call a jimmy a ‘jemmy’.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowbar - a heavy iron bar with a flattened end, used as a lever
  • Synonym: jimmy - American term for a jemmy: a short crowbar
