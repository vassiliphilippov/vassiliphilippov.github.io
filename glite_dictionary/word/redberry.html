redberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌbɛri / (BrE)
a small spiny evergreen shrub found in the western United States and Mexico, known for its minute flowers and bright red berries
a spiny shrub with bright red berries

Examples

The hillside was covered in redberry shrubs, their berries shining in the sunlight.
Wildlife often feeds on the redberry during the winter months.
Gardeners sometimes plant redberry as an ornamental shrub due to its vibrant fruit.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌbɛri / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb with compound leaves and clusters of small white flowers, followed by bright red oval, poisonous berries.
a North American herb with red poisonous berries

Examples

Children are advised not to eat the redberry because it is toxic.
The woodland trail was lined with redberry plants in early summer.
She recognized the redberry by its distinctive compound leaves and clusters of berries.
