John Dewey

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈduːi / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈdjuːi / (BrE)
(1859–1952) United States philosopher and educator known for his work in pragmatism and advocacy of progressive education.
American philosopher and educational reformer

Examples

John Dewey was a leading advocate for educational reform in the early 20th century.
The philosophy of John Dewey continues to influence teaching methods today.
Many universities have named buildings after John Dewey in recognition of his contributions.
