door

1. NOUN

/ dɔɹ / (AmE), / dɔː / (BrE)
A means or opportunity of access or escape.
means of access or opportunity

Examples

Education is often described as the door to a better life.
For many artists, social media has opened a door to new audiences.
Winning the competition was the door that led to her professional success.

Synset Examples

we closed the door to Haitian immigrants
education is the door to success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gateway - a means of entrance or access to a place or state
  • Antonym: barrier - something that blocks or prevents movement or access

Etymology

From Old English 'duru', related to German 'Tür' and Latin 'fores' (doorways).

2. NOUN

/ dɔr / (AmE), / dɔː / (BrE)
the space in a wall or partition that allows entry or exit from a room, building, or vehicle, often fitted with a movable barrier
space in a wall for entry or exit

Examples

She stood in the door, waiting for her guests to arrive.
The cat darted through the open door and disappeared outside.
Light spilled in from the door at the end of the hallway.

Synset Examples

he stuck his head in the doorway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doorway - an entranceway through a door

Etymology

From Old English duru (door, gate, entrance), related to Old High German turi, Latin fores.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03228735-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ dɔr / (AmE), / dɔː / (BrE)
a hinged, sliding, or revolving barrier at the entrance to a room, building, or vehicle, used for opening and closing the way in or out
an entry barrier for a room, building, or vehicle

Examples

He forgot to lock the door before leaving the house.
She opened the door quietly to avoid waking anyone.
Rain beat against the front door all night.

Synset Examples

he knocked on the door
he slammed the door as he left

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gate - a movable barrier in a fence or wall, typically giving access to a garden or field
  • Antonym: window - an opening in a wall fitted with glass to let in light and air

Etymology

From Old English 'duru', related to German 'Tür' and Latin 'fores'.
