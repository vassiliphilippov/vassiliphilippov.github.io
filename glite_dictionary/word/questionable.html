questionable

1. ADJ

/ ˈkwɛstʃənəbəl / (AmE), / ˈkwɛstʃ(ə)nəbəl / (BrE)
Open to doubt or challenge; able to be refuted or disputed.
able to be refuted or disputed

Examples

The scientist argued that the evidence for the new theory was questionable and could easily be disproven.
Many experts believe the company's claim is questionable and should be examined closely.
His argument relied on sources that are questionable at best.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undeniable - impossible to deny or refute
  • Synonym: refutable - able to be proven false or wrong

Etymology

From Latin 'quaestionabilis' (subject to inquiry).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkwɛs.tʃə.nə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkwɛs.tʃən.ə.bəl / (BrE)
not certain; likely to be doubted, or not completely honest, safe, or suitable.
open to doubt or suspicion

Examples

The company made several questionable decisions that led to financial losses.
He gave a questionable explanation for his absence from work.
Some people have a questionable reputation in the business community.

Synset Examples

questionable motives
a questionable reputation
a fire of questionable origin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dubious - hesitating or doubting; not to be relied upon
  • Synonym: uncertain - not able to be relied on; not known or definite
  • Antonym: certain - known for sure; established beyond doubt

Etymology

From Latin 'quaestionabilis' (subject to inquiry), from 'quaestio' (question).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01922610-a
Synset POS: a
