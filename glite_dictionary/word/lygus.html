Lygus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪɡəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of small plant-sucking bugs, some of which are agricultural pests.
genus of plant-sucking bugs

Examples

Lygus bugs are known to damage a variety of crops in North America.
Farmers often use insecticides to control Lygus populations in cotton fields.
The presence of Lygus can significantly reduce agricultural yields.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Lygus, a genus name used in entomology.
