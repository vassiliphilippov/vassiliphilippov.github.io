organ

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.ɡən / (AmE), / ˈɔː.ɡən / (BrE)
a musical instrument with one or more keyboards, in which air is forced through reeds by bellows to produce sound
reed keyboard instrument with bellows

Examples

She played the old organ in the church for Sunday services.
The antique organ in his living room still produces a beautiful sound.
He bought a small organ with foot pedals and bellows at the auction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmonium - a reed organ powered by hand- or foot-operated bellows

Etymology

From Latin 'organum', from Greek 'organon' (tool, instrument).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɡən / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
An electronic keyboard instrument that simulates the sound of a pipe organ, often used in popular and church music.
electronic keyboard imitating a pipe organ

Examples

The church band accompanied the choir with an electric organ for the concert.
She learned to play the organ before taking up the piano.
His favorite songs sound even better when performed on a classic Hammond organ.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synthesizer - electronic instrument producing a wide range of sounds
  • Degree Higher: pipe organ - large musical instrument with pipes, operated by a keyboard

4. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɡən / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
a large musical instrument in which sound is produced by air driven through pipes, controlled by one or more keyboards and pedals
large keyboard wind instrument

Examples

The church boasts a magnificent organ that fills the hall with music.
She learned to play the organ when she was a teenager.
The concert featured a solo performance on the grand organ.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmonium - a free-reed keyboard instrument that produces sound as air flows past vibrating reeds
  • Synonym: pipe organ - a specific type of organ using pipes to produce sound
  • Antonym: piano - a keyboard instrument that produces sound by hammers striking strings

Etymology

From Latin 'organum' (instrument, tool), from Greek 'organon'.

5. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɡən / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
a part of an animal's body composed of different tissues that performs a specific function, such as the heart or liver.
body part with a specific function

Examples

The human heart is a vital organ responsible for pumping blood throughout the body.
Damage to any major organ can have severe consequences for health.
The liver is the largest internal organ in the human body.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tissue - a group of similar cells carrying out a specific function
  • Degree Higher: system - a group of organs working together in the body

Etymology

From Latin organum (instrument, tool), from Greek organon (tool, organ).
