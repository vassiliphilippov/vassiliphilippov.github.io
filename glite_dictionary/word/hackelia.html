Hackelia

1. NOUN

/ həˈkiːliə / (AmE), / həˈkiːlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the borage family, commonly known as stickweed or beggar's lice, characterized by small, sticky fruits that adhere to fur or clothing.
a genus of plants known as stickweed or beggar's lice

Examples

The seeds of Hackelia easily attach to hikers' clothing when walking through the woods.
Many farmers consider Hackelia a nuisance weed because its burrs stick to animal fur.
Botanists conducted a survey to document the diversity of Hackelia species in the region.

Etymology

Named after Austrian botanist Josef Hackel.

2. NOUN

/ həˈkiːliə / (AmE), / hækˈiːliə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the borage family (Boraginaceae), commonly known as stickweed or beggar's lice, characterized by their clinging seeds.
a genus of plants known as stickweed or beggar's lice

Examples

The seeds of Hackelia readily attach to clothing and animal fur.
Researchers discovered a new species of Hackelia growing in the valley.
Many hikers recognize Hackelia by its sticky burs and blue flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stickweed - common name for plants of the genus Hackelia
  • Synonym: beggar's lice - another common name for Hackelia plants

Etymology

Named after Josef Hackel, a Czech botanist.
