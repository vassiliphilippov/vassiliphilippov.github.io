binomial

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈnoʊmiəl / (AmE), / bɪˈnəʊmiəl / (BrE)
consisting of or relating to two terms, especially in mathematics or language
consisting of two terms

Examples

The equation contains a binomial expression that must be expanded.
In statistics, the binomial distribution describes the number of successes in a series of trials.
She solved the problem by applying a binomial formula.

Synset Examples

binomial expression

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: monomial - consisting of a single term
  • Degree Higher: polynomial - consisting of multiple terms

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) and 'nomen' (name).

2. NOUN

/ baɪˈnoʊmiəl / (AmE), / baɪˈnəʊmiəl / (BrE)
(mathematics) an algebraic expression that consists of the sum or difference of two terms.
polynomial with two terms
medical

Examples

The expression binomial is used to describe polynomials with exactly two terms.
A common example of a binomial is x + y.
You can expand a binomial raised to a power using the binomial theorem.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: polynomial - an algebraic expression with more than one term
  • Degree Lower: monomial - an algebraic expression with a single term

Etymology

From Latin 'binomius', from 'bi-' (two) + 'nomen' (name, term).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06046036-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ baɪˈnoʊmiəl / (AmE), / baɪˈnəʊmiəl / (BrE)
having or characterized by two names, especially referring to the two-term naming system in biological classification (genus and species).
having two names, especially in taxonomy

Examples

The scientist used the binomial system to classify the newly discovered plant.
In binomial nomenclature, each species is given a two-part Latin name.
The textbook explained the importance of binomial names in biological classification.

Synset Examples

binomial nomenclature of bacteria

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binomial nomenclature - system of naming species with two terms, genus and species

Etymology

From Medieval Latin binomium, from Latin bi- (two) + nomen (name).
