comb-out

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊm aʊt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊm aʊt / (BrE)
the act of removing tangles from hair by using a comb
removing hair tangles with a comb

Examples

She gave her daughter a gentle comb-out after washing her hair.
The stylist offered a quick comb-out before finishing the haircut.
Regular comb-outs prevent knots and keep the hair smooth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detangle - to remove knots or tangles, especially from hair
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ koʊm aʊt / (AmE), / kəʊm aʊt / (BrE)
the careful process of removing or eliminating unwanted items or people from a group, organization, or collection
systematic removal of unwanted elements

Examples

The manager ordered a complete comb-out of inefficient procedures in the office.
After the scandal, the company underwent a thorough comb-out to restore public trust.
The library is planning a comb-out of outdated books next month.

Synset Examples

the department got a good comb-out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purge - removal of unwanted people or things
  • Synonym: culling - selective removal of inferior members from a group

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'comb out', originally referring to untangling hair, later used figuratively for thorough removal.
