Syngnathidae

1. NOUN

/ sɪŋˈnæθɪˌdi / (AmE), / sɪŋˈnæθɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of elongated, narrow-bodied fishes that includes pipefishes and seahorses, found in marine and estuarine waters.
family of fishes: pipefishes and seahorses

Examples

The family Syngnathidae includes both pipefishes and seahorses, known for their unique body shapes.
Researchers have studied the reproductive behavior of Syngnathidae across different habitats.
Many members of the Syngnathidae family have a remarkable ability to camouflage among seagrass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipefish - a slender, elongated fish of the family Syngnathidae
  • Synonym: seahorse - a marine fish of the genus Hippocampus, also in Syngnathidae

Etymology

From New Latin Syngnathus (type genus), from Greek syn- (together) + gnathos (jaw).

2. NOUN

/ sɪŋˈnæθɪˌdiː / (AmE), / sɪŋˈnaθɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, slender marine fishes that includes pipefishes and seahorses, characterized by elongated bodies and tubular snouts.
family of fishes including pipefishes and seahorses

Examples

The Syngnathidae family includes both pipefishes and seahorses, found in oceans worldwide.
Biologists are studying the reproductive habits of Syngnathidae to learn more about their unique parenting behaviors.
Many species within Syngnathidae exhibit remarkable camouflage to blend in with seagrass habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipefish - a small, elongated fish related to the seahorse
  • Synonym: seahorse - a small marine fish with a horselike head, in the family Syngnathidae

Etymology

From Greek syn- (together) + gnathos (jaw), referring to the fused jaws characteristic of these fishes.
