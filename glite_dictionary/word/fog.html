fog

1. NOUN

/ fɑg / (AmE), / fɒg / (BrE)
A state of mental confusion or lack of clarity that makes it difficult to think or understand clearly.
mental confusion; lack of clarity

Examples

After the accident, she was in a fog and couldn't remember what happened.
Trying to work through my fog of exhaustion, I struggled to concentrate on the task.
The medication left him in a mental fog for several hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - the state of being bewildered or unclear
  • Antonym: clarity - clearness in thought or expression

2. NOUN

/ fɑg / (AmE), / fɒg / (BrE)
A thick cloud of tiny water droplets suspended in the atmosphere near the Earth's surface, reducing visibility.
thick cloud of water droplets near ground

Examples

Driving through the fog this morning was slow and stressful.
A thick fog covered the city, making it hard to see more than a few meters ahead.
The airport delayed flights because of the dense fog on the runway.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mist - a fine spray of water droplets in the air, less dense than fog
  • Degree Lower: haze - atmospheric moisture or dust causing reduced visibility, less dense than fog or mist
  • Synonym: smog - a mixture of smoke and fog, especially in urban areas

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse 'fog' (mist, spray).

3. VERB

/ fɑɡ / (AmE), / fɒɡ / (BrE)
to make something less visible or more difficult to understand; to obscure or cloud

Examples

Her memory of that day is fogged by time and emotion.
The truth was fogged by conflicting reports in the media.

4. NOUN

/ fɑːɡ / (AmE), / fɒɡ / (BrE)
A thick cloud of tiny water droplets or other substances suspended in the atmosphere, reducing visibility to a significant degree.
thick cloud reducing visibility

Examples

A dense fog covered the city, making it difficult for drivers to see the road.
Flights were delayed because of the heavy fog at the airport this morning.
Walking through the early morning fog, she could barely make out the houses across the street.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mist - very fine droplets in the air, reducing visibility but less dense than fog
  • Degree Lower: haze - atmospheric obscurity caused by dust, smoke, or vapour, less dense than fog
  • Synonym: smog - a mixture of fog and smoke or pollutants
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear, when there is no fog

Etymology

From Old Norse *fog* (mist, spray), related to Old English *fog* (aftergrass).
