metacentric

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛtəˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
relating to or situated at the metacenter; having properties pertaining to the metacenter, especially in reference to stability in floating bodies or chromosome structure in biology.
relating to the metacenter

Examples

A ship with a high metacentric height is more stable but may roll uncomfortably.
The scientist described the chromosome as metacentric due to its centrally located centromere.
Engineers ran calculations to determine the vessel's metacentric stability in rough waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metacenter - the point of equilibrium stability in a floating body

Etymology

From 'metacenter' + '-ic', modeled on scientific adjective formation.

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɛtəˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
(of a chromosome) having two arms of equal length because the centromere is located in the middle.
(of a chromosome) with the centromere in the middle

Examples

Human chromosome 1 is metacentric, with its centromere positioned near the middle.
The researchers compared metacentric chromosomes to those that were acrocentric in structure.
A metacentric chromosome has two arms of approximately equal length.

Synset Examples

a metacentric chromosome

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acrocentric - (of a chromosome) having the centromere near one end, resulting in one short arm and one long arm
  • Antonym: submetacentric - (of a chromosome) having the centromere slightly off-center, creating arms of unequal length

Etymology

From Greek meta- (after, beyond) + kentron (center); originally in geometry and later used in biology.

Source: WordNet
