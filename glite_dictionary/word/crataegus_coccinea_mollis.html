Crataegus coccinea mollis

1. NOUN

/ krəˈtiːɡəs kɑkˈsɪniə ˈmɑlɪs / (AmE), / krəˈtiːɡəs kɒkˈsɪnɪə ˈmɒlɪs / (BrE)
An American red-fruited hawthorn with stems and leaves densely covered with short woolly hairs.
a type of American red-fruited hawthorn

Examples

The Crataegus coccinea mollis thrives in well-drained soils throughout the eastern United States.
Gardeners often choose Crataegus coccinea mollis for its attractive red fruit and woolly foliage.
Researchers are studying Crataegus coccinea mollis to better understand its resistance to certain plant diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - a thorny shrub or tree of the genus Crataegus

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Crataegus' (hawthorn), species 'coccinea' (red), variety 'mollis' (soft, referring to the hairiness).
