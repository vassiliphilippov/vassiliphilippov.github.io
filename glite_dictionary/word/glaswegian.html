Glaswegian

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɑːzˈwiːdʒiən / (AmE), / ɡlazˈwiːdʒiən / (BrE)
a person who comes from or lives in Glasgow, Scotland
person from Glasgow, Scotland

Examples

My grandfather was a proud Glaswegian who loved telling stories about his childhood in the city.
Many Glaswegians are known for their distinctive accent and sense of humor.
As a Glaswegian, she always supports the local football team.

Etymology

Formed from Glasgow + -wegian (patterned after 'Norwegian').

2. ADJ

/ ɡlæsˈwiːdʒən / (AmE), / ɡlazˈwiːdʒi.ən / (BrE)
relating to Glasgow or its inhabitants.
related to Glasgow or its people

Examples

The Glaswegian accent is distinctive among Scottish dialects.
She enjoyed the warm hospitality of her Glaswegian friends during her visit.
A Glaswegian restaurant opened in the city center, serving traditional Scottish fare.

Related Terms

Etymology
