Cynodon plectostachyum

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈnoʊdən plɛkˌtoʊˈstækiəm / (AmE), / sɪˈnəʊdən plɛkˌtəʊˈstækiəm / (BrE)
a perennial grass with stems 3 to 4 feet high, used particularly in Africa and India for pasture and hay
a tall perennial pasture grass

Examples

In many African countries, Cynodon plectostachyum is widely cultivated for its nutritious forage.
Farmers often prefer Cynodon plectostachyum because it thrives during dry seasons.
Studies have shown that livestock feeding on Cynodon plectostachyum generally gain more weight.

Etymology

From Greek *kynos* (of a dog) and *odon* (tooth), combined with Latinized species epithet from Greek roots meaning 'twisted spike'.
