styptic

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪptɪk / (AmE), / ˈstɪptɪk / (BrE)
a substance used to stop bleeding by causing contraction of body tissues or blood vessels
substance that stops bleeding
medical

Examples

The barber applied a styptic to stop the minor shaving cut from bleeding.
Alum is commonly used as a styptic to control surface bleeding after a shave.
Doctors may use a styptic during surgery to reduce bleeding from small blood vessels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astringent - a substance that contracts body tissues; often used to refer to the same action as a styptic

Etymology

From Greek 'stuptikos' (causing contraction), from 'styphein' (to contract).

2. ADJ

/ ˈstɪptɪk / (AmE), / ˈstɪptɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance) tending to check bleeding by contracting the tissues or blood vessels.
stops bleeding by contracting tissues
medical

Examples

The doctor applied a styptic powder to the wound to stop the bleeding.
Some shaving sticks contain styptic ingredients to soothe minor cuts.
A styptic pencil can be used to quickly control bleeding from small nicks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astringent - a substance that contracts body tissues; similar property

Etymology

From Greek *styptikos* (causing contraction), from *styphein* (to contract).
