bluegill

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluˌɡɪl / (AmE), / ˈbluːɡɪl / (BrE)
a small freshwater sunfish (Lepomis macrochirus) native to eastern and central North America, valued as a food and sport fish
a North American freshwater sunfish

Examples

The fisherman proudly displayed a large bluegill he caught at the lake.
Many children learn to fish by catching bluegill in local ponds.
Fried bluegill is a popular dish in the Midwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunfish - a freshwater fish of the family Centrarchidae
