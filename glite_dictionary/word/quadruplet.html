quadruplet

1. NOUN

/ kwɑːˈdrʊplət / (AmE), / kwɒˈdrʌplɪt / (BrE)
a set or group of four similar things considered as a unit
group of four similar things

Examples

The museum displayed a quadruplet of rare ancient vases.
For the challenge, each team needed to find a quadruplet of matching shells.
Scientists observed a quadruplet of stars orbiting each other in a distant galaxy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: triplet - a set of three similar things considered as a unit
  • Degree Higher: quintuplet - a set of five similar things considered as a unit

Etymology

From Latin 'quadruplus' (fourfold), influenced by 'triplet'.

2. NOUN

/ kwɑːˈdrʌplət / (AmE), / kwɒˈdrʌplɪt / (BrE)
one of four children born at the same time to the same mother
one of four siblings born together

Examples

The Smiths were thrilled when their quadruplet daughters all arrived healthy and strong.
Raising a quadruplet requires a lot of patience and organization from parents.
Each quadruplet developed a unique personality, despite their shared birthday.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: triplet - one of three children born at the same time
  • Degree Higher: quintuplet - one of five children born at the same time

Etymology

From Latin 'quadruplus' (fourfold) with the '-et' diminutive suffix.
