Gaius Cassius Longinus

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡaɪ.əs ˈkæʃ.i.əs lɒnˈdʒaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪ.əs ˈka.si.əs lɒŋˈɪ.nəs / (BrE)
(died 42 BC) A Roman senator and general, known as a leading instigator of the conspiracy to assassinate Julius Caesar.
Roman senator; led conspiracy against Caesar

Examples

Gaius Cassius Longinus played a central role in orchestrating the plot to assassinate Julius Caesar.
Historians often debate whether Gaius Cassius Longinus was driven by personal motives or political ideals.
The downfall of Gaius Cassius Longinus came at the Battle of Philippi in 42 BC.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Marcus Junius Brutus - Roman politician, co-conspirator in Julius Caesar's assassination
  • Antonym: Julius Caesar - Roman general and statesman, assassinated in 44 BC

Etymology

From Latin, the full Roman name of the historical figure.
