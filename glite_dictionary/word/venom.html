venom

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛnəm / (AmE), / ˈvɛnəm / (BrE)
intense bitterness or spite; a feeling of wanting others to suffer
intense spite or bitterness

Examples

There was so much venom in his words that it stunned everyone in the room.
She spoke with unexpected venom, revealing years of hidden resentment.
He directed his venom towards anyone who challenged his authority.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malice - the intention or desire to do evil; ill will
  • Antonym: benevolence - the quality of being well meaning; kindness

Etymology

From Latin 'venenum' (poison).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛnəm / (AmE), / ˈvɛnəm / (BrE)
a poisonous substance secreted by some snakes, spiders, scorpions, or other animals, usually injected by bite or sting
poisonous secretion of certain animals

Examples

The snake injects its venom into its prey to immobilize or kill it.
Scientists are studying the venom of certain spiders to develop new medicines.
Bee venom can cause serious allergic reactions in some people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison - a substance that causes harm or death when introduced into the body
  • Antonym: antivenom - a substance used to counteract the effects of venom

Etymology

From Old French venim, from Latin venenum (poison).
