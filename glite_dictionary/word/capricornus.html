Capricornus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæprɪˈkɔrnəs / (AmE), / ˌkæprɪˈkɔːnəs / (BrE)
a faint zodiacal constellation in the Southern Hemisphere, located between Sagittarius and Aquarius
zodiacal constellation between Sagittarius and Aquarius

Examples

The Capricornus constellation is best seen in the late summer months in the Southern Hemisphere.
Though Capricornus is a zodiacal constellation, it is not very bright compared to its neighbors.
Astronomers map Capricornus as the region of the sky between Sagittarius and Aquarius.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Sagittarius - zodiac constellation west of Capricornus
  • Degree Higher: Aquarius - zodiac constellation east of Capricornus

Etymology

From Latin 'capricornus' meaning 'horned goat', referring to the mythological creature represented by the constellation.
