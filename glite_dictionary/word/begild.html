to begild

1. VERB

/ bɪˈɡɪld / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɪld / (BrE)
to decorate with gold or as if with gold leaf or liquid gold
decorate with gold or gold-like material

Examples

The craftsmen begilded the temple doors with intricate patterns of gold leaf.
A subtle light seemed to begild the hills at sunset, giving them a luminous glow.
She hoped to begild the plain frame and make it worthy of the masterpiece within.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gild - to cover with a thin layer of gold

Etymology

From Old English 'begyldan', from 'be-' (about, around) + 'gyldan' (to gild).
