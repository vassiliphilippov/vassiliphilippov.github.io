barrage

1. NOUN

/ bəˈrɑːʒ / (AmE), / bəˈrɑːʒ / (BrE)
a rapid and overwhelming outpouring or stream of something, especially questions, comments, or criticism
an overwhelming outpouring (e.g., of questions)

Examples

Reporters launched a barrage of questions at the politician after the press conference.
He received a barrage of emails following the announcement.
The teacher was met with a barrage of complaints from concerned parents.

Synset Examples

a barrage of questions
a bombardment of mail complaining about his mistake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombardment - a continuous attack or outpouring, especially of questions or complaints

Etymology

From French 'barrage' (barrier, barrage), originally a military term.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07097633-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ bəˈrɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˈbærɑːʒ / (BrE)
a heavy and continuous artillery bombardment over a wide area, often intended to protect advancing troops or to saturate without aiming at a specific target
heavy artillery bombardment

Examples

The enemy troops advanced under the cover of a barrage from their own artillery.
The town endured a relentless barrage that lasted throughout the night.
Soldiers waited for the barrage to lift before moving forward toward the trenches.

Synset Examples

they laid down a barrage in front of the advancing troops
the shelling went on for hours without pausing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombardment - continuous attack with bombs or artillery fire
  • Antonym: ceasefire - an agreement to stop fighting temporarily

Etymology

From French 'barrage' (barrier), originally from 'barrer' (to bar).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00989949-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ bəˈrɑːʒ / (AmE), / bəˈrɑːʒ / (BrE)
to direct a continuous and overwhelming stream of questions, criticisms, or requests at someone
overwhelm with a stream of questions or requests

Examples

Journalists barraged the senator with questions about his recent statements.
The customer service hotline was barraged with complaints after the outage.
Fans barraged the author on social media, eager for updates on the next book.

Synset Examples

The speaker was barraged by an angry audience
The governor was bombarded with requests to grant a pardon to the convicted killer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombard - to attack someone persistently, especially with questions or requests

Etymology

From French 'barrage' (barrier), originally referring to a military artillery attack.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00865243-v
Synset POS: v
