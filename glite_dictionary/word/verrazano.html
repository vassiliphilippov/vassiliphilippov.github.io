Verrazano

1. PROPN

/ vəˈræzəˌnoʊ / (AmE), / vɛˈræzənɑːʊ / (BrE)
Giovanni da Verrazzano (c. 1485–1528), Florentine navigator who explored the eastern coast of North America.
Florentine navigator and explorer

Examples

The Verrazano Bridge in New York is named after the famous explorer.
Verrazano was the first European to enter New York Bay.
Many history books describe Verrazano's voyages along the Atlantic coast.

Etymology

From Italian 'Verrazzano', the name of the Italian explorer.
