breakage

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act or process of breaking something, or the state of being broken
the act or process of breaking

Examples

The company was compensated for any breakage during shipping.
Frequent breakage of glassware was a major concern in the laboratory.
They calculated the cost of breakage when setting the insurance premium.

Synset Examples

the breakage was unavoidable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - to separate into pieces as a result of a blow, shock, or strain

Etymology

From break + -age, a suffix used to form nouns indicating the result or process of an action.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkɪdʒ / (BrE)
compensation or reimbursement for goods that have been damaged during transit or use, especially in commercial transactions
compensation for damaged goods

Examples

The insurance policy covers breakage incurred during shipping.
Retailers often receive credit for breakage if goods arrive damaged.
Any breakage during delivery must be reported immediately for reimbursement.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13314535-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkɪdʒ / (BrE)
the amount or quantity of something that has been broken, especially in transport or handling
amount broken; broken pieces

Examples

The warehouse reported significant breakage during the delivery process.
We must account for some breakage when shipping fragile goods overseas.
The store kept a record of all breakage to assess inventory losses.

Synset Examples

the total breakage was huge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damage - physical harm that impairs value or function
  • Synonym: loss - the state of no longer having something or as much of something

Etymology

From 'break' + '-age', first used in early 18th century.
