finespun

1. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪnˌspʌn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnˌspʌn / (BrE)
Extremely delicate, subtle, or refined, often to an excessive or impractical degree; developed with excessive attention to fine detail.
overly subtle or delicately detailed

Examples

The philosopher introduced a finespun argument that few could follow.
She criticized the report for its finespun distinctions between similar concepts.
His finespun legal reasoning impressed the judges but confused the jury.

Synset Examples

finespun distinctions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subtle - not obvious; delicate or precise as to be difficult to analyze
  • Synonym: hair-splitting - making overly fine and unnecessary distinctions

Etymology

Formed from 'fine' + past participle of 'spin', suggesting something spun very finely.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪnˌspʌn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnˌspʌn / (BrE)
Extremely delicate, subtle, or elaborate in detail or reasoning, often to the point of being overly refined.
extremely delicate or subtle

Examples

The professor's finespun arguments impressed those who appreciated subtlety, but confused students new to the topic.
She offered a finespun analysis of the poem, revealing layers of meaning others had overlooked.
His finespun distinctions between similar concepts sometimes seemed unnecessary to his colleagues.

Synset Examples

the satire touches with finespun ridicule every kind of human pretense

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delicate - very fine in texture or structure; easily broken or damaged
  • Antonym: coarse - rough or loose in texture or grain; lacking delicacy

Etymology

From 'fine' (of high quality, delicate) + 'spun' (past participle of 'spin', associated with making delicate threads or fabrics).
