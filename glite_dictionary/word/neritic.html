neritic

1. ADJ

/ nəˈrɪtɪk / (AmE), / nɪˈrɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the region of shallow water near a seacoast, typically above the drop-off of the continental shelf.
of the shallow coastal sea region

Examples

Scientists collected neritic organisms along the continental shelf for analysis.
The diversity of species is particularly high in neritic zones due to abundant sunlight and nutrients.
Many commercially important fish live in neritic waters close to the shore.

Synset Examples

neritic fauna

Etymology

From Greek 'nēritēs' (sea snail), related to 'Nereus,' a sea god.

WordNet ID: oewn-00695710-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ nəˈrɪtɪk / (AmE), / nəˈrɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the shallow part of the sea near a coast, specifically the area over the continental shelf less than about 200 meters deep
relating to the shallow coastal sea

Examples

Many commercially important fish inhabit the neritic zone because of its abundance of nutrients.
Coral reefs are typically found in neritic waters where sunlight penetrates easily.
The neritic region supports a diverse array of marine plants and animals.

Synset Examples

neritic fauna
neritic oil

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pelagic - relating to the open sea (away from the coast and continental shelf)

Etymology

From Greek nēritikos, relating to the sea nymph Nereis.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02985381-a
Synset POS: a
