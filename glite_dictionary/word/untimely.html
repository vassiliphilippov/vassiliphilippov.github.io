untimely

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtaɪmli / (AmE), / ʌnˈtaɪmli / (BrE)
(of an action or event) occurring at a time that is inappropriate or inconvenient; badly timed.
occurring at a bad or inappropriate time

Examples

Her untimely comment disrupted the flow of the meeting.
The movie's release was seen as untimely given the current events.
An untimely interruption ruined his concentration.

Synset Examples

an ill-timed intervention
you think my intrusion unseasonable
an untimely remark
it was the wrong moment for a joke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ill-timed - badly or inappropriately timed
  • Antonym: timely - happening at the right or appropriate time

Etymology

From un- (not) + timely, formed by analogy with 'timely'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtaɪmli / (AmE), / ʌnˈtaɪmli / (BrE)
happening or done earlier than expected, especially in a way that is considered unfortunate or inappropriate
occurring before the expected time

Examples

His untimely death shocked the entire community.
The project came to an untimely end due to lack of funding.
Everyone felt the manager's resignation was untimely and disruptive to the team.

Synset Examples

illness led to his premature death
alcohol brought him to an untimely end

Related Terms

  • Synonym: premature - happening before the usual or expected time
  • Antonym: timely - occurring at a suitable or opportune time

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + timely; originally in Middle English as untimely meaning 'not occurring at the right time'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00818569-s
Synset POS: s
