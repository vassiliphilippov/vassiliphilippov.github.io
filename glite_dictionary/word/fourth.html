fourth

1. NOUN

/ fɔrθ / (AmE), / fɔːθ / (BrE)
the person or thing occupying position number four in a sequence; the ordinal number corresponding to four.
the position or item numbered four

Examples

She finished in fourth place in the marathon.
This is the fourth of five lectures in the series.
Their team was ranked fourth overall last year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: third - the person or thing in the third position
  • Degree Higher: fifth - the person or thing in the fifth position

Etymology

Formed by adding the ordinal suffix -th to 'four', from Old English 'feower'.

2. NOUN

/ fɔrθ / (AmE), / fɔːθ / (BrE)
In music, the interval between one note and another that is four scale degrees apart, including the starting note.
musical interval spanning four scale degrees

Examples

The cello plays a fourth above the violin in this passage.
In Western music, a perfect fourth spans five semitones.
She easily identified the fourth when practicing ear training.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fifth - the musical interval between one note and another five notes away from it
  • Degree Lower: third - the musical interval between one note and another three notes away from it

3. NOUN

/ fɔrθ / (AmE), / fɔːθ / (BrE)
Each of four equal parts into which something can be divided; one quarter.
one of four equal parts; quarter

Examples

She divided the cake into four fourths and gave one to each child.
A fourth of the total votes were cast by mail this year.
After spending a fourth of his savings, he decided to slow down on shopping.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarter - one of four equal parts

Etymology
