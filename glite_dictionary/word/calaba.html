calaba

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɑːbə / (AmE), / kəˈlɑːbə / (BrE)
A tropical West Indian tree (Calophyllum calaba) with racemes of fragrant white flowers, yielding durable timber and a resinous juice.
West Indian tree yielding timber and resin

Examples

The furniture was crafted from calaba wood, prized for its strength and beauty.
The resin extracted from the calaba tree has traditional medicinal uses in the Caribbean.
We admired the racemes of fragrant white flowers adorning the calaba in the botanical garden.

Etymology

From Spanish calaba, referring to the tree in the West Indies.
