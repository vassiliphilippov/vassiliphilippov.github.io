deciduous

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɪdʒuəs / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪdjuːəs / (BrE)
(of teeth, antlers, etc.) falling off or shed at a particular stage of development or after a period of growth.
shed at the end of a growth period

Examples

Most mammals have deciduous teeth that are replaced by permanent ones as they grow.
The deciduous antlers of deer are shed annually and regrown the next season.
Veterinarians check for retained deciduous teeth in young dogs as part of routine exams.

Synset Examples

deciduous teeth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permanent - (of teeth) meant to last or remain throughout life, not shed

Etymology

From Latin 'deciduus' (that which falls off), from 'decidere' (to fall down).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɪdʒuəs / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪdjuːəs / (BrE)
(of plants and trees) shedding leaves annually, especially at the end of the growing season.
shedding leaves annually

Examples

The deciduous trees in the park turn bright colors before losing their leaves each autumn.
Some species of oak are deciduous, while others remain green all year.
In the winter, the garden looks bare because most of the deciduous shrubs have lost their leaves.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: evergreen - (of plants) retaining green leaves throughout the year

Etymology

From Latin 'deciduus' (falling off), from 'decidere' (to fall off).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00917218-a
Synset POS: a
