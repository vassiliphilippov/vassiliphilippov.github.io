Caravaggism

1. NOUN

/ ˌkærəˈvɑːdʒɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌkærəˈvædʒɪzəm / (BrE)
A style of painting influenced by the techniques and subjects of Caravaggio, characterized by dramatic lighting and realistic depiction of scenes.
art style influenced by Caravaggio

Examples

Many painters across Europe adopted Caravaggism in the early 17th century.
The influence of Caravaggism can be seen in the dramatic use of light and shadow.
She wrote her thesis on the spread of Caravaggism in northern Europe.

Synset Examples

Caravaggism, a modern term used to describe the international artistic movement generated by Caravaggio's style, had a considerable life until the early 1630s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Baroque - art style with dramatic expression and rich detail

Etymology

From the name of Italian painter Caravaggio (1571–1610) + -ism.
