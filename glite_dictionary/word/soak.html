2. NOUN

/ soʊk / (AmE), / səʊk / (BrE)
an act of immersing something in liquid, especially to wash or soften it
an act of soaking for washing or softening

Examples

She gave her stained shirt a long soak before washing it.
The recipe recommends a quick soak of the beans overnight.
After a muddy hike, his boots needed a good soak in warm water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wash - an act of washing something
  • Degree Lower: rinse - to wash lightly, especially to remove soap

Etymology

From Old English *socian* (to soak), related to Old Norse *súga* (to suck).
