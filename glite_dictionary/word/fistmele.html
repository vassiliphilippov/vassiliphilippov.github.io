fistmele

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪstˌmiːl / (AmE), / ˈfɪstˌmiːl / (BrE)
The traditional measurement in archery of the proper distance between a bowstring and the handle when the bow is strung, approximately the breadth of a fist with the thumb extended (about seven inches).
archery measurement of bowstring distance

Examples

Traditional English longbows are often checked for the correct fistmele before use.
The archer measured the fistmele to ensure his bow was properly strung.
A gap wider than the standard fistmele could affect accuracy and power.

Etymology

From Old English 'fyst' (fist) and Old Norse 'māl' (measure).
