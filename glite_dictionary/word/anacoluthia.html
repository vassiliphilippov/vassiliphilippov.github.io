anacoluthia

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəkoʊˈluːθiə / (AmE), / ˌanəkəˈluːθɪə / (BrE)
A sudden break or change in the grammatical or syntactic structure of a sentence, resulting in a lack of expected sequence or agreement.
abrupt change in sentence structure

Examples

The essay contained an anacoluthia that confused some readers.
Writers sometimes use anacoluthia intentionally for stylistic effect.
In the middle of his speech, an unexpected anacoluthia interrupted the flow of ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anacoluthon - the use of anacoluthia; the actual grammatical inconsistency

Etymology

From Greek anacolouthia (inconsistency), from an- (not) + akolouthos (following).
