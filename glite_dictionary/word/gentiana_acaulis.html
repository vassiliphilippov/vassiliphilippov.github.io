Gentiana acaulis

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛnˈʃæn.i.ə əˈkaʊ.lɪs / (AmE), / dʒɛnˈtʃeɪ.ni.ə əˈkaʊ.lɪs / (BrE)
A low-growing alpine plant of the gentian family, cultivated for its bright blue, bell-shaped flowers and rosettes of glossy green leaves.
alpine plant with blue bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The Gentiana acaulis is prized by gardeners for its vibrant blue flowers and compact growth.
Many alpine gardens feature Gentiana acaulis due to its ability to thrive in rocky soil.
In spring, you can spot Gentiana acaulis blooming among the high mountain meadows.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Gentiana', named after King Gentius of Illyria; species 'acaulis' meaning 'stemless'.
