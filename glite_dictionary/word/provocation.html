provocation

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɑːvəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒvəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
an action or event that stimulates or encourages a certain response or action
something that incites a response

Examples

The teacher's question served as a provocation for lively debate in the classroom.
Sometimes a little provocation is all it takes to spark creativity among artists.
The mayor's speech acted as a provocation for the community to take action.

Synset Examples

the result was a provocation of vigorous investigation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulus - something that encourages a response or activity
  • Synonym: encouragement - the action of giving someone support, confidence, or hope

Etymology

From Latin provocatio (a challenge, incitement), from provocare (to call forth).

2. NOUN

/ ˌprɑːvəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒvəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
an action or statement that deliberately annoys or angers someone, often causing them to react
action that provokes anger or annoyance

Examples

His angry response was understandable given such blatant provocation.
She managed to remain calm in the face of constant provocation.
The slightest provocation can set him off into a rage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incitement - the act of urging someone to behave unlawfully or violently
  • Synonym: insult - a disrespectful or scornful remark or act

Etymology

From Latin provocatio (a challenge), from provocare (to call forth, challenge).

3. NOUN

/ ˌprɑː.vəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒ.vəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
An action, statement, or event that makes someone angry or prompts a response, especially deliberately.
act of provoking or inciting

Examples

His rude remarks were a clear provocation meant to start an argument.
The soldiers responded to the enemy's provocation with discipline.
She ignored the repeated provocation and remained calm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incitement - the act of provoking unlawful behavior or urging someone to behave unlawfully
  • Synonym: insult - an action or remark that causes offense or provokes someone
  • Antonym: calm - the state of being peaceful, not stirred to anger or action

Etymology

From Latin provocatio (a challenge), from provocare (to call forth, challenge).
