Acacia catechu

1. NOUN

/ əˈkeɪʃə ˈkætɪˌtʃu / (AmE), / əˈkeɪsɪə ˈkætɪˌtʃu / (BrE)
A species of spiny tree native to South and Southeast Asia, with twice-pinnate leaves, yellow flowers, and flat pods; the source of black catechu extract.
species of spiny tree; source of black catechu

Examples

The bark of Acacia catechu is used in traditional medicine and for tanning leather.
Researchers studied the chemical compounds found in Acacia catechu extracts.
Black catechu, obtained from Acacia catechu, is used as a dye and astringent.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Acacia with a Latinized form of Hindi 'khair' (the tree).
