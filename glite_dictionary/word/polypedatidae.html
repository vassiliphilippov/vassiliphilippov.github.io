Polypedatidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑlɪˈpɛdəˌtaɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈpiːdəˌtɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of frogs known as Old World tree frogs, found primarily in Asia and characterized by their arboreal lifestyle.
family of Old World tree frogs

Examples

The Polypedatidae are commonly found in forests throughout Southeast Asia.
Scientists study the Polypedatidae to better understand arboreal adaptations in frogs.
Members of the Polypedatidae exhibit a remarkable variety of vocalizations.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus Polypedates + family suffix -idae.
