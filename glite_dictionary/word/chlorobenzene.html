chlorobenzene

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔroʊˈbɛnziːn / (AmE), / ˌklɔːrəʊˈbenziːn / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile, flammable liquid with an almond-like odor, used as a solvent and in making phenol, DDT, and other compounds
volatile liquid chemical compound
medical

Examples

Chlorobenzene is often used as a solvent in organic chemistry laboratories.
Researchers detected traces of chlorobenzene in the river near the chemical plant.
The manufacture of DDT involves the use of chlorobenzene as a starting material.

Etymology

From 'chloro-' (relating to chlorine) and 'benzene', indicating a benzene molecule substituted with chlorine.
