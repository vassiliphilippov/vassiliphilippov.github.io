whitecup

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌkʌp / (BrE)
a prostrate woody South American herb with white tubular flowers, often tinged with blue or rose
South American herb with white tubular flowers

Examples

The gardener planted a whitecup near the pond for its delicate blossoms.
During their hike, they spotted several whitecups growing among the rocks.
The whitecup blooms in early spring, brightening the landscape with its pale flowers.
