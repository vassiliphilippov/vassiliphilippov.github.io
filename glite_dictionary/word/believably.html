believably

1. ADV

/ bəˈlivəbli / (AmE), / bɪˈliːvəbli / (BrE)
In a way that can be believed; in a believable manner.
in a believable manner

Examples

The actor portrayed the character believably, making the audience empathize with his struggles.
Her explanation was so believably detailed that everyone accepted it without question.
It is important to write dialogue believably to keep readers engaged in the story.

Synset Examples

he acted believably sincere

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unbelievably - in an unbelievable manner
  • Synonym: convincingly - in a convincing manner

Etymology

From 'believable' + '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00246100-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ bəˈliːvəbli / (AmE), / bɪˈliːvəbli / (BrE)
In a manner that is easy to believe; in a way that appears credible or plausible.
in a believable manner

Examples

The story was believably told, making everyone question whether it was real.
She believably portrayed the character of a grieving mother in the film.
His excuse was believably detailed, so no one doubted his explanation.

Synset Examples

he talked plausibly before the committee
he will probably win the election

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incredibly - in a manner that is impossible or hard to believe
  • Synonym: plausibly - in a credible or reasonable manner

Etymology

From 'believable' + '-ly', with 'believable' from 'believe' + '-able'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00297385-r
Synset POS: r
