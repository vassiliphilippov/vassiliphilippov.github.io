organon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.ɡəˌnɑn / (AmE), / ˈɔː.ɡə.nɒn / (BrE)
a systematic set of principles or methods used in philosophical or scientific investigation; an instrument or framework for acquiring knowledge
system for acquiring knowledge

Examples

Aristotle's organon profoundly influenced Western approaches to logic and reasoning.
The scientific method is often considered an organon of modern research.
In his essay, the philosopher proposed a new organon for ethical inquiry.

Etymology

From Greek órganon (instrument, tool), adopted in New Latin for philosophical works.
