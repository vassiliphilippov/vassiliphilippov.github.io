undischarged

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɪsˈtʃɑrdʒd / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪsˈtʃɑːdʒd / (BrE)
(of ammunition or explosive devices) not yet detonated, fired, or expended; still capable of exploding.
not yet detonated or fired (esp. ammunition)

Examples

The police found several undischarged shells at the scene of the accident.
It is dangerous to handle undischarged firecrackers without proper safety precautions.
After the battle, the soldiers carefully collected any undischarged ammunition left on the ground.

Synset Examples

undischarged ammunition
an unexploded bomb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unexploded - (of a bomb or shell) not having exploded

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈdɪstʃɑrdʒd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈdɪstʃɑːdʒd / (BrE)
(of a debt or obligation) not yet paid, settled, or fulfilled.
not yet paid or settled

Examples

There are still several undischarged debts in the company's accounts.
The trustee must report any undischarged liabilities to the court.
He was declared bankrupt due to a number of undischarged obligations.

Synset Examples

outstanding bills
undischarged debts
the amount still outstanding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outstanding - (of money) remaining to be paid, unsettled
  • Antonym: paid - (of a debt or bill) having been settled

Etymology

Formed by the prefix un- (not) + 'discharged' (released from an obligation), from Old French 'descharger'.
