Trachurus trachurus

1. NOUN

/ trəˈkjʊrəs trəˈkjʊrəs / (AmE), / trəˈkjʊərəs trəˈkjʊərəs / (BrE)
A large, elongated, compressed food fish of the Atlantic waters of Europe, commonly known as the Atlantic horse mackerel.
Atlantic horse mackerel fish species

Examples

Fishermen in Portugal often catch Trachurus trachurus for both local markets and export.
The distinct silver body and elongated shape help identify Trachurus trachurus in European waters.
Studies have shown that Trachurus trachurus populations are declining due to overfishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse mackerel - common name for Trachurus trachurus

Etymology

From Greek 'trachys' (rough) + 'oura' (tail), referring to the rough tail of the fish.
