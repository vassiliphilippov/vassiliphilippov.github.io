oddments

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑdˌmɛnts / (AmE), / ˈɒdmənts / (BrE)
small items or pieces left over from a larger set or collection; miscellaneous remnants.
miscellaneous leftover items

Examples

She kept a box of oddments for crafting in the closet.
The drawer was full of oddments like buttons, ribbons, and scraps of paper.
After sewing, there were several oddments of fabric left over.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remnants - small parts left after the main part is gone
  • Synonym: odds and ends - a group of miscellaneous small items

Etymology

From 'odd' (strange or remaining) + 'ment' (suffix forming nouns).
