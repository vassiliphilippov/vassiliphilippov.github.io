Capparis mitchellii

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpærɪs ˌmɪtʃəˈlaɪ / (AmE), / kəˈpærɪs ˌmɪtʃɪˈliː / (BrE)
a small Australian tree that bears edible dark purple fruit, commonly known as the wild orange
Australian tree with edible purple fruit

Examples

The fruit of the Capparis mitchellii is a traditional food among some Australian Indigenous communities.
Botanists studied the unique adaptations of the Capparis mitchellii to arid climates.
The jam made from Capparis mitchellii fruit is highly prized in parts of Australia.

Etymology

Named for Thomas Mitchell, British explorer, and from Latinized genus Capparis.
