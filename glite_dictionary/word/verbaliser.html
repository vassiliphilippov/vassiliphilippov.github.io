verbaliser

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜːrbəlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈvɜːbəlaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who expresses ideas in words, especially by speaking, or who tends to speak a lot
person who expresses ideas in words

Examples

As a skilled verbaliser, she captivated audiences with her inspiring speeches.
Among the group, he's known as a natural verbaliser who rarely runs out of things to say.
The company's spokesperson is a talented verbaliser, always clear and persuasive.

Synset Examples

the speaker at commencement
an utterer of useful maxims

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speaker - a person who speaks to an audience
  • Synonym: orator - a skilled public speaker
  • Antonym: listener - a person who listens

Etymology

From 'verbal' + '-iser', after the verb 'to verbalise'.
