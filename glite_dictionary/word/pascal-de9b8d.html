pascal

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæskəl / (AmE), / ˈpæskæl / (BrE)
the SI unit of pressure, equal to one newton per square meter
SI unit of pressure

Examples

Atmospheric pressure at sea level is approximately 101,325 pascals.
The instrument recorded a sudden drop of 50 pascals during the experiment.
This tire is inflated to 250,000 pascals for optimal performance.

Etymology

Named after Blaise Pascal, French mathematician and physicist (1623–1662).
