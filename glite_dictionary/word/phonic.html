2. ADJ

/ ˈfɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to the method of teaching reading that stresses the use of sound-symbol correspondence (phonics).
relating to phonics in teaching reading

Examples

The new curriculum places a strong emphasis on phonic awareness for early readers.
Teachers use phonic exercises to help children learn to decode unfamiliar words.
Recent research has highlighted the benefits of phonic instruction in primary schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonics - a method of teaching reading based on sound-letter correspondence

Etymology

From Greek phōnē (sound, voice), via French phonie.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03111095-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to speech sounds or the production of sound, especially by the human voice
relating to speech sounds

Examples

The teacher used phonic methods to help students learn to read.
Some languages have complicated phonic systems.
The program emphasizes phonic awareness for early readers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonetic - relating to the sounds of speech or their transcription

Etymology

From Greek phōnikos (pertaining to sound or voice), from phōnē (voice, sound).
