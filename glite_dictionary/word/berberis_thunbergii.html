Berberis thunbergii

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɜːrˈbɛrɪs θʌnˈbɜːrɡiː / (AmE), / ˌbɜːˈbɛərɪs θʌnˈbɜːɡiː / (BrE)
A compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, widely cultivated for its ornamental value and used especially for hedges; it produces persistent red berries.
species of ornamental shrub, Japanese barberry

Examples

The hedge was made of Berberis thunbergii to provide year-round color and privacy.
Gardeners appreciate Berberis thunbergii for its vibrant fall foliage and hardiness.
You can identify Berberis thunbergii by its sharp thorns and bright red berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese barberry - Common English name for Berberis thunbergii

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Berberis' and specific epithet honoring Swedish botanist Carl Peter Thunberg.
