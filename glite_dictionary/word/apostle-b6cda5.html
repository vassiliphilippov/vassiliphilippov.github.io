apostle

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑsəl / (AmE), / əˈpɒsl̩ / (BrE)
an important early teacher of Christianity, especially one of the twelve disciples chosen by Jesus, or a Christian missionary to a people
early Christian teacher or missionary

Examples

St. Paul is often called the thirteenth apostle because of his influence on early Christianity.
The twelve apostles spread the teachings of Jesus throughout the Roman Empire.
She devoted her life to missionary work and was known as an apostle of compassion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disciple - a follower or student of a teacher, especially Jesus

Etymology

From Greek apostolos (messenger, person sent forth).

2. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːsəl / (AmE), / əˈpɒsl̩ / (BrE)
A passionate early supporter and promoter of a particular cause or reform.
ardent early supporter of a cause

Examples

She became an apostle of environmental awareness, speaking at every possible event.
He is widely recognized as an apostle of free-market capitalism in the early twentieth century.
The scientist was an apostle of vaccination when few others supported the idea.

Synset Examples

an apostle of revolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advocate - a person who supports or recommends a particular cause
  • Antonym: opponent - someone who is against or resists a cause or proposal

Etymology

From Greek apostolos (messenger, envoy), originally referring to one sent forth.
