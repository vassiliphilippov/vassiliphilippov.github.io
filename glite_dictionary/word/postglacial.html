postglacial

1. ADJ

/ ˌpoʊstˈɡleɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstˈɡleɪsɪəl / (BrE)
relating to or occurring during the period following a glacial epoch, especially after the last Ice Age.
after a glacial period

Examples

The melting of ice sheets marked the beginning of the postglacial era.
Scientists study postglacial changes in plant and animal life to understand climate history.
Many current river valleys were shaped during postglacial times as the landscape adjusted to warmer conditions.

Etymology

From Latin 'post-' (after) and 'glaciālis' (icy, frosty).
