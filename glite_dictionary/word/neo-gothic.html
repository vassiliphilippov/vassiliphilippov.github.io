neo-Gothic

1. ADJ

/ ˌnioʊˈɡɑθɪk / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈɡɒθɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting a style of architecture that revived and adapted elements of Gothic architecture, especially in the 19th and early 20th centuries.
revived Gothic architectural style

Examples

The museum was built in a neo-Gothic style, complete with pointed arches and ornate stone carvings.
Many 19th-century churches in America feature neo-Gothic architectural elements.
She admired the neo-Gothic spires and stained glass windows of the restored cathedral.

Synset Examples

In England, the centre of the Gothic revival movement, Gothic revival was intertwined with deeply philosophical movements associated with a re-awakening of High Church or Anglo-Catholic belief (and by the Catholic convert Augustus Welby Pugin) concerned by the growth of religious nonconformism.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Gothic - style of architecture distinguished by pointed arches and ribbed vaults, developed in the medieval period

Etymology

Formed from 'neo-' (Greek, meaning 'new') and 'Gothic' (a European architectural style).
