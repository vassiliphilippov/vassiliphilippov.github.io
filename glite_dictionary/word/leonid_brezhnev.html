Leonid Brezhnev

1. PROPN

/ liˈoʊnɪd ˈbrɛʒnɛf / (AmE), / lɪˈəʊnɪd ˈbrɛʒnɛf / (BrE)
(1906–1982) Soviet statesman who served as the leader of the Soviet Union from 1964 to 1982.
Leader of the Soviet Union, 1964–1982

Examples

Leonid Brezhnev played a pivotal role in shaping Soviet policies during the Cold War era.
During Leonid Brezhnev's leadership, the Soviet Union experienced a period known as the Era of Stagnation.
Many people remember Leonid Brezhnev for his iconic eyebrows and lengthy speeches.
