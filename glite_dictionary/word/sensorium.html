sensorium

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈsɔriəm / (AmE), / sɛnˈsɔːriəm / (BrE)
the parts of the brain that receive and process sensory information, making conscious perception of the world possible
part of the brain for sensory perception
medical

Examples

Damage to the sensorium can lead to loss of conscious perception of sights or sounds.
The neurologist examined the patient's sensorium to evaluate his awareness of his surroundings.
Certain drugs can alter the sensorium, affecting one's sense of reality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensory cortex - the brain region involved in processing sensory input

Etymology

From Latin 'sensorium' (seat of sensation), from 'sentire' (to perceive, feel).
