Vicia cracca

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʃiə ˈkrækə / (AmE), / ˈvɪsɪə ˈkraka / (BrE)
A common perennial climbing plant of temperate regions in Eurasia and North America, known for its dense, elongated clusters of bluish-purple flowers; also called tufted vetch.
a perennial climbing plant with dense blue-purple flowers

Examples

We spotted Vicia cracca growing along the edge of the field, its purple flowers trailing in the grass.
Gardeners often plant Vicia cracca to improve soil by fixing nitrogen naturally.
The dense clusters of Vicia cracca are a favorite among wild bees in the summertime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tufted vetch - Common name for Vicia cracca

Etymology

From Latin: 'vicia' (vetch), 'cracca' (climbing vetch).
