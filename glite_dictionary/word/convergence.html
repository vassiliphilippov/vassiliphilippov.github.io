convergence

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɜrdʒəns / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
the property of a mathematical series or sequence approaching a specific value as more terms are added
mathematical series approaching a finite limit

Examples

The convergence of the series means that its sum approaches a fixed number.
Mathematicians studied the convergence of infinite sequences in their research.
Tests can determine the convergence or divergence of a mathematical series.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divergence - the process of moving away from a limit or becoming infinitely large (mathematics)

Etymology

From Latin convergere (to incline together) via French convergence.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈvɝːdʒəns / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
the process by which different ideas, theories, or phenomena become more similar or move towards a common point or agreement
becoming more similar; finding common ground

Examples

Recent scientific discoveries show a remarkable convergence between genetics and environmental research.
There is increasing convergence in the approaches used by economists and sociologists.
The convergence of digital and traditional media is changing the way information is consumed.

Synset Examples

there was no overlap between their proposals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divergence - the process by which things become different or move apart

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'convergentia', from Latin 'convergere' (to come together).
