vehement

1. ADJ

/ ˈviːəmənt / (AmE), / ˈviːəmənt / (BrE)
Showing strong feeling; forceful, passionate, or intense.
forceful or passionate; intense

Examples

She made a vehement protest against the unfair decision.
His vehement anger startled everyone in the room.
The senator spoke in a vehement tone during the debate.

Synset Examples

vehement deluges of rain
vehement clapping
a vehement defense

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passionate - showing or caused by strong feelings or a strong belief
  • Antonym: mild - not severe, intense, or strong

Etymology

From Latin 'vehementem' (impetuous, violent).

2. ADJ

/ ˈviəmənt / (AmE), / ˈviː.ɪ.mənt / (BrE)
showing strong and often forceful emotion or conviction; marked by great intensity of feeling
showing intense emotion or conviction

Examples

She gave a vehement denial when accused of the mistake.
The debate was marked by vehement disagreements between the two sides.
His vehement objection to the plan surprised everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

fierce loyalty
in a tearing rage
vehement dislike
violent passions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passionate - showing strong feelings or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: mild - gentle in manner or degree; not intense

Etymology

From Latin 'vehemens', meaning violent or impetuous.
