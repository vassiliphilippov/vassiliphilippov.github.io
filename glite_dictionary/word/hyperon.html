hyperon

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪpəˌrɑn / (AmE), / ˈhaɪpərɒn / (BrE)
An unstable subatomic particle belonging to the baryon family, with a mass greater than that of a neutron and not classified as a nucleon.
unstable baryon heavier than a neutron

Examples

The discovery of the hyperon expanded our understanding of subatomic particles beyond the proton and neutron.
Physicists observed the rapid decay of a hyperon in the particle accelerator experiments.
Unlike nucleons, a hyperon is not found in ordinary atomic nuclei.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baryon - a subatomic particle made up of three quarks, including protons and neutrons
  • Antonym: nucleon - a proton or neutron, the components of atomic nuclei

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper' (over, beyond) + '-on', a suffix used in naming subatomic particles.
