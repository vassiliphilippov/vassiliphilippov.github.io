anorthography

1. NOUN

/ ˌænɔːrˈθɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌanɔːˈθɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
Loss of the ability to write or to express thoughts in writing, usually as a result of brain damage.
loss of writing ability due to brain damage
medical

Examples

After her stroke, the patient exhibited anorthography and could no longer write her name legibly.
The neurologist diagnosed anorthography based on the patient's inability to write coherent sentences.
Researchers study anorthography to better understand how brain lesions affect language and writing.

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (not) + 'ortho-' (correct) + 'graphy' (writing).
