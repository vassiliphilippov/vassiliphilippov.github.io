successor

1. NOUN

/ səkˈsɛsɚ / (AmE), / səkˈsɛsə / (BrE)
a person who takes over a job, position, or title after someone else
a person who comes next in a role or position

Examples

After the CEO retired, Jane became his successor.
The king's successor was formally crowned the following month.
Every manager hopes their successor will respect their achievements.

Synset Examples

he was President Lincoln's successor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: predecessor - a person who held a job or position before someone else

Etymology

From Latin 'successor' (a follower), from 'succedere' (to come after).

2. NOUN

/ səkˈsɛsər / (AmE), / səkˈsɛsə / (BrE)
a person who takes over a position, title, or office after someone else
person who replaces another in a position

Examples

After the CEO retired, her successor continued many of her initiatives.
Queen Elizabeth II became the successor to the British throne in 1952.
Each president hopes that their successor will uphold important traditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: predecessor - a person who held a position before someone else
  • Synonym: heir - a person legally entitled to inherit property or title

Etymology

From Latin 'successor', from 'succedere' meaning 'to come after'.

3. NOUN

/ səkˈsɛsɚ / (AmE), / səkˈsɛsə / (BrE)
a person or thing that comes after and takes the place of another in a position, office, or role.
person or thing that replaces another

Examples

After the CEO retired, her successor took over the company’s leadership.
The new phone model is faster than its successor.
Every king must eventually be replaced by a successor when the time comes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: predecessor - a person who held a position or office before another

Etymology

From Latin 'successor', from 'succedere' meaning 'to follow after'.
