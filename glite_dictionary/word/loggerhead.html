loggerhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑɡərˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡəˌhɛd / (BrE)
an insulting term for a person regarded as stupid or lacking intelligence
stupid person (insult)

Examples

Only a real loggerhead would forget their own birthday.
She called him a loggerhead after he missed the obvious answer.
Those loggerheads have been bickering all day over nothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blockhead - a person regarded as very stupid
  • Synonym: dunce - a slow-witted or stupid person

Etymology

Probably from 'logger' (block of wood) + 'head', referring metaphorically to a blockhead.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːɡərˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡəhɛd / (BrE)
A very large marine turtle with a broad head, found in warm seas, known for its powerful jaws and carnivorous diet.
large sea turtle with a broad head

Examples

The loggerhead is easily recognized by its massive head and reddish-brown shell.
Scientists tagged a young loggerhead to study its migration patterns across the Atlantic.
Many loggerhead turtles come ashore each year to lay their eggs on sandy beaches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea turtle - Any of the marine turtles that inhabit warm and temperate seas

Etymology

Possibly from the English words 'logger' (an agent noun from 'log') + 'head', referring to its large head.
