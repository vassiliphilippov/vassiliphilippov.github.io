Phallaceae

1. NOUN

/ fæˈleɪsiˌi / (AmE), / fæˈleɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi in the order Phallales, commonly known as the true stinkhorns, characterized by their foul odor and distinct fruiting bodies.
family of fungi (true stinkhorns)

Examples

Several species within the Phallaceae family are known for their unpleasant odor.
The Phallaceae can often be found growing in wooded areas and gardens.
Mycology students studied the unique structures of the Phallaceae during their laboratory session.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Phallaceae, based on Greek phallos (phallus) due to the shape of the fungi.
