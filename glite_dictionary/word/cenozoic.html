Cenozoic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛn.əˈzoʊ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌsiː.nəˈzəʊ.ɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to the most recent geological era, following the Mesozoic, which began about 66 million years ago and continues to the present day
relating to the Cenozoic era

Examples

The Cenozoic period is known for the rise and dominance of mammals.
Many fossils from the Cenozoic era have provided insights into the evolution of birds.
Geologists study Cenozoic rock formations to understand recent changes to Earth's surface.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Mesozoic - relating to the geological era before the Cenozoic

Etymology

From Greek *kainos* (new) + *zoion* (animal), introduced in the 19th century to denote the era of 'new animals'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛn.əˈzoʊ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌsiː.nəˈzəʊ.ɪk / (BrE)
the current geological era, beginning about 66 million years ago, characterized by the development of mammals and birds
the current geological era

Examples

The extinction of the dinosaurs marked the beginning of the Cenozoic era.
Many modern mammals evolved during the Cenozoic period.
Geological evidence from the Cenozoic provides insight into how Earth's climate has changed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Mesozoic - the geological era before the Cenozoic
  • Degree Lower: Paleozoic - the geological era before the Mesozoic

Etymology

From Greek kainos (new) + zōē (life).
