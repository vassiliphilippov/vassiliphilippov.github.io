uninvited

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈvaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈvaɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) not having been invited or asked to attend or participate.
not having been invited

Examples

Several uninvited guests arrived at the party, causing some awkwardness for the host.
She was upset when she found her ex-boyfriend on the list of uninvited attendees.
The uninvited media presence made the private ceremony uncomfortable for the family.

Synset Examples

uninvited guests

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invited - having received an invitation

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' + 'invited', past participle of 'invite'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02539440-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈvaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈvaɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a thought, action, or person) not welcome, especially when arriving or occurring unexpectedly or without request.
not welcome or not invited

Examples

She apologized for her uninvited comment during the meeting.
He struggled with uninvited thoughts that disrupted his concentration.
Uninvited guests showed up at the party, causing some confusion.

Synset Examples

uninvited thoughts
uninvited advances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unwelcome - not gladly received; unwanted
  • Antonym: welcome - received gladly or willingly

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'invited' (from Latin 'invitare', to invite).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02551202-s
Synset POS: s
