authority

1. NOUN

/ əˈθɔr.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒr.ə.ti / (BrE)
a book or other written work that is recognized as the most reliable or authoritative source on a subject
an authoritative written work or source

Examples

This biography is considered the authority on 19th-century Russian literature.
Her research paper cited the leading authority on ancient Greek philosophy.
In legal matters, his textbook has become the standard authority for students.

Synset Examples

this book is the final authority on the life of Milton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reference - a book or source used for information

Etymology

From Old French autorite, from Latin auctoritas (influence, command), from auctor (originator, promoter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06423235-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ əˈθɔrəti / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒrəti / (BrE)
the power or right to give orders, make decisions, or enforce obedience
the power to command or decide

Examples

The manager has the authority to approve all budget requests in her department.
After the storm, local authority imposed a curfew to keep residents safe.
Parents sometimes struggle when children question their authority.

Synset Examples

he has the authority to issue warrants
deputies are given authorization to make arrests
a place of potency in the state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power - the ability to direct or influence behavior or events
  • Antonym: subordination - the condition of being under the authority or control of another

Etymology

From Old French 'autorité', from Latin 'auctoritas' (influence, command).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05203850-n
Synset POS: n

official permission or approval

Examples

4. NOUN

/ əˈθɔr.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒr.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
(usually plural) a group of people or an organization who have the power to make decisions and enforce laws or rules.

Examples

The authorities closed the road due to hazardous weather conditions.

Synset Examples

the authorities have issued a curfew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: government - the group of people who govern a country or state

5. NOUN

/ əˈθɔrəti / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒrəti / (BrE)
a person who is recognized as an expert in a particular subject, whose opinions or advice are highly respected and regarded as definitive
expert whose opinion is respected

Examples

Dr. Martin is an authority on medieval literature and often lectures at international conferences.
The museum consulted an authority in ancient art to verify the authenticity of the sculptures.
You should ask an authority in finance if you want reliable investment advice.

Synset Examples

he is an authority on corporate law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expert - a person with a high level of knowledge or skill in a particular area
  • Antonym: novice - a person who is new to and inexperienced in a job or situation

Etymology

From Old French 'autorite', from Latin 'auctoritas' (influence, command, opinion), from 'auctor' (originator, promoter).
