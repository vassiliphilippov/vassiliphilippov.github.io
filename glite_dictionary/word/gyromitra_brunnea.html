Gyromitra brunnea

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒaɪrəˈmaɪtrə bruːnˈniːə / (AmE), / ˌdʒɪərəˈmaɪtrə bruːnˈniːə / (BrE)
A species of false morel mushroom with a deep brownish-red, irregularly shaped cap and a white stalk, commonly found in North America.
species of false morel mushroom

Examples

The distinctive cap of the Gyromitra brunnea makes it easy to identify in the wild.
Mushroom hunters are warned not to eat Gyromitra brunnea due to its potential toxicity.
Researchers collected samples of Gyromitra brunnea for a study on its chemical properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false morel - type of poisonous mushroom resembling true morels

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Gyromitra' and species epithet 'brunnea' meaning brown.
