unparliamentary

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˌpɑɹləˈmɛn.tɛɹ.i / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌpɑː.lɪˈmen.tər.i / (BrE)
(of language or behavior) not suitable for use in parliament, especially because it is rude or abusive.
unsuitable for parliament; rude or abusive

Examples

The speaker asked him to withdraw his unparliamentary remarks.
Accusing another member of lying is considered unparliamentary language in most assemblies.
She was reprimanded for using unparliamentary words during the heated debate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: parliamentary - relating to or suitable for parliament

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' (not) to 'parliamentary'.
