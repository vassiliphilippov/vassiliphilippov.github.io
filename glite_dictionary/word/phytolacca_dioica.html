Phytolacca dioica

1. NOUN

/ faɪtəˈlækə daɪˈoʊɪkə / (AmE), / ˌfɪtələˈkə dɪˈəʊɪkə / (BrE)
A fast-growing evergreen tree native to South America, known for its broad, water-rich trunk and dark green oval leaves.
South American evergreen tree species

Examples

The park was shaded by a giant Phytolacca dioica that dominated the landscape.
Botanists studied the unique trunk structure of Phytolacca dioica in its native habitat.
The broad canopy of Phytolacca dioica provides shelter for many species of birds.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'phyto-' (plant), 'lacca' (from Hindi 'lakh' for shellac), and 'dioica' (botanical term for plants with male and female flowers on separate individuals, from Greek 'dioikos').
