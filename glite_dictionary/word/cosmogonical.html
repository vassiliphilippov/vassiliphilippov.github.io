cosmogonical

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑz.məˈɡɑ.nɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒz.məˈɡɒ.nɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to the origin and development of the universe, especially as studied in the scientific branch of cosmogony
relating to the origin of the universe

Examples

Many ancient myths offer cosmogonical explanations for how the world came into being.
The scientist presented a new cosmogonical model during the astronomy symposium.
Debates about the Big Bang often revolve around cosmogonical questions regarding the universe's origin.

Synset Examples

cosmologic science
cosmological redshift
cosmogonic theories of the origin of the universe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmological - relating to the science of the origin and development of the universe as a whole

Etymology

From Greek kosmogonia (creation of the world), from kosmos (world) + gonos (birth, origin).
