sterling

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɝː.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɜː.lɪŋ / (BrE)
the standard British currency, especially the pound as the basic monetary unit of the UK
British currency, especially the pound
british

Examples

The value of the sterling fell sharply after the announcement.
International investors closely follow the movements of sterling against other major currencies.
He converted his euros to sterling before traveling to London.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pound sterling - the principal unit of British currency

Etymology

From Old English *sterlina* (silver penny), possibly relating to 'star', a type of coin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈstɝːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɜːlɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or their work or qualities) of the highest standard; excellent.
of the highest quality; excellent

Examples

She is known for her sterling leadership and unwavering integrity.
His sterling efforts on the project earned him a well-deserved promotion.
The team delivered a sterling performance that impressed everyone in the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excellent - of exceptional quality; extremely good
  • Antonym: inferior - of lower quality or less value
