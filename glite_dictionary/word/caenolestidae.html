Caenolestidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːnoʊˈlɛstəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌsiːnəʊˈlɛstɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, shrew-like marsupials native to southern South America.
family of South American marsupials

Examples

The Caenolestidae are sometimes referred to as shrew opossums because of their resemblance to true shrews.
Members of the Caenolestidae family are rarely seen due to their elusive nature.
Scientists have discovered new species within the Caenolestidae in the Andes mountains.

Etymology

From New Latin Caenolestidae, based on genus name Caenolestes, from Greek kainos (new) + lestes (robber, marauder).
