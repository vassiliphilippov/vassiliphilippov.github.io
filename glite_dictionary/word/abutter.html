abutter

1. NOUN

/ əˈbʌtər / (AmE), / əˈbʌtə / (BrE)
a person or entity that owns property immediately adjacent to another property
owner of adjacent property
legal

Examples

The new zoning law requires each abutter to be notified before construction begins.
The abutter objected to the proposed fence along the property line.
At the meeting, several abutters expressed concerns about increased traffic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neighbor - person living near or next to another

Etymology

From 'abut' (to be adjacent to) + '-er' (agent suffix)
