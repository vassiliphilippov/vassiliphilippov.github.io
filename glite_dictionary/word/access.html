access

1. NOUN

/ ˈæksɛs / (AmE), / ˈæksɛs / (BrE)
the right or opportunity to enter a place or use something
the right to enter or use

Examples

Only authorized personnel have access to the secure laboratory.
We need to improve wheelchair access to the building.
The new system provides remote access to company files.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entry - the action of coming or going into a place

Etymology

From Latin 'accessus' (a coming to, approach), from 'accedere' (to approach).
