tribromoethanol

1. NOUN

/ ˌtraɪˌbroʊmoʊˈɛθənɒl / (AmE), / ˌtraɪˌbrəʊməʊˈiːθənɒl / (BrE)
a chemical compound used as an intravenous anesthetic, particularly in laboratory animal research
intravenous anesthetic compound
medical

Examples

The researchers administered tribromoethanol to the mice before beginning the procedure.
Because tribromoethanol is fast-acting, it is often chosen for short-term anesthesia in animal studies.
Handling tribromoethanol requires proper safety precautions due to its chemical properties.

Etymology

Formed from 'tri-' (three), 'bromo' (bromine), and 'ethanol' (alcohol), indicating three bromine atoms in an ethanol molecule.
