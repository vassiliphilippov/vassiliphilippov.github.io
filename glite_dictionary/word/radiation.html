radiation

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
treatment of disease, especially cancer, using controlled exposure to radioactive substances or radiation.
use of radiation to treat disease
medical

Examples

The patient underwent radiation to shrink the tumor before surgery.
Advances in radiation have improved survival rates for many cancer patients.
She experienced side effects from the radiation used to treat her lymphoma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiotherapy - treatment of disease using radiation

Etymology

From Latin radiatio (shining, radiation), from radius (ray, beam).
