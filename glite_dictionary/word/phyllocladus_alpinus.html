Phyllocladus alpinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪləʊˈklædəs ælˈpaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌfɪləkˈlædəs alˈpiːnəs / (BrE)
a small shrubby celery pine native to New Zealand, with scale-like leaves and often growing in alpine or subalpine regions
small shrubby celery pine of New Zealand

Examples

The Phyllocladus alpinus is commonly found in the mountainous regions of New Zealand.
Botanists studied the adaptations of Phyllocladus alpinus to harsh alpine environments.
Many conservation efforts aim to protect Phyllocladus alpinus from habitat loss.

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllo-' (leaf) + 'cladus' (branch) and Latin 'alpinus' (alpine, of high mountains).
