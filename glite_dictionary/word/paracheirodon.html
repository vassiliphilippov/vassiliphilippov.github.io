Paracheirodon

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˌkaɪrəˈdɑːn / (AmE), / ˌparəkʌɪrəˈdɒn / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater fish in the family Characidae, which includes species such as the neon tetra.
genus of small freshwater fish

Examples

The neon tetra is one of the most popular aquarium fish from the Paracheirodon genus.
Several Paracheirodon species are highly prized for their vibrant colors in the aquarist community.
Scientists recently described a new species belonging to the Paracheirodon genus from the Amazon basin.

Etymology

From Greek 'para' (beside), 'cheir' (hand), and 'odous' (tooth), referencing the characteristic teeth of these fishes.

2. NOUN

/ pæɹəˌkaɪroʊˈdɑn / (AmE), / parəˌkaɪərəʊˈdɒn / (BrE)
A genus of small, colorful freshwater fish in the family Characidae, native to South America, including the neon tetra.
genus of small South American freshwater fish

Examples

Many aquarium enthusiasts keep Paracheirodon species, such as the neon tetra, for their striking colors.
The genus Paracheirodon is native to the rivers of South America, especially the Amazon basin.
Scientists have discovered new species within the Paracheirodon genus in remote rainforest streams.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Characidae - a family of freshwater fish that includes tetras and allies
  • Degree Lower: neon tetra - a small, brightly colored fish in the genus Paracheirodon

Etymology

From Greek 'para' (beside, near) + 'Cheirodon' (an existing fish genus).
