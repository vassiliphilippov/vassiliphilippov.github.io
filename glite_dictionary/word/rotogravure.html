rotogravure

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊtəɡrəˈvjʊr / (AmE), / ˌrəʊtəɡrəˈvjʊə / (BrE)
printed material (such as magazines or illustrations) produced by a rotary intaglio printing process; also, the printing process itself characterized by engraving images onto a cylinder.
printed material or process using rotary intaglio printing

Examples

The magazine's glossy pages were printed using rotogravure to ensure vibrant images.
Collectors often seek out vintage rotogravure prints from early 20th-century newspapers.
Advances in rotogravure technology have improved the quality of color reproduction in high-volume printing.

Etymology

From Italian 'rotogravura', combining Latin 'rota' (wheel) and French 'gravure' (engraving).

2. NOUN

/ ˌroʊtəɡrəˈvjʊr / (AmE), / ˌrəʊtəɡrəˈvjʊə / (BrE)
a printing process in which photographs or other images are transferred onto a plate or cylinder to produce high-quality prints, typically used for magazines and packaging.
photogravure printing process

Examples

The magazine was printed using the rotogravure process, resulting in vivid, high-quality images.
Many packaging labels are produced by rotogravure to achieve precise and consistent color reproduction.
He specialized in rotogravure printing for art books and luxury publications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photogravure - an intaglio printmaking or photo-mechanical process

Etymology

From Italian 'rotogravura', combining 'rotare' (to rotate) and 'gravura' (engraving), referring to the rotating cylinder used in the process.

3. NOUN

/ ˌroʊtəɡrəˈvjʊr / (AmE), / ˌrəʊtəɡrəˈvjʊə / (BrE)
a printing process in which images are transferred from a photogravure plate onto a rotary press cylinder and then onto paper, often used for high-quality magazines and packaging
printing process using rotary press and photogravure plate

Examples

The magazine was printed using rotogravure to ensure high-quality images and vibrant colors.
Advances in rotogravure have made packaging designs more colorful and detailed.
He worked at a printing press that specialized in rotogravure for luxury catalogs.

Etymology

From Italian *rotogravura*: 'roto-' (from 'rotary') + 'gravura' ('engraving').
