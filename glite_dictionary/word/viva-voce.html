viva-voce

1. ADJ

/ ˌviːvəˈvoʊtʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˌviːvəˈvəʊtʃeɪ / (BrE)
spoken or expressed orally rather than in writing
expressed orally; spoken

Examples

The candidate was required to give a viva-voce answer during the examination.
The committee decided to conduct a viva-voce interview instead of a written test.
His promotion was announced in a viva-voce report at the meeting.

Synset Examples

a viva-voce report
the film had good word-of-mouth publicity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oral - spoken rather than written

Etymology

From Medieval Latin viva voce ‘with the living voice’.
