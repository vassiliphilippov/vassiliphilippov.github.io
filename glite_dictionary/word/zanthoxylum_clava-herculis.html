Zanthoxylum clava-herculis

1. NOUN

/ zænˈθɒksɪləm ˌkleɪvə hɜːrˈkjuːlɪs / (AmE), / zanˈθɒksɪləm ˌklɑːvə hɜːˈkjuːlɪs / (BrE)
a densely spiny shrub or small tree of the southeastern United States and West Indies, often grown as an ornamental
spiny ornamental shrub/tree of SE US and West Indies

Examples

The Zanthoxylum clava-herculis is sometimes called the Hercules' club due to its spiny trunk.
Gardeners in the southern states prize Zanthoxylum clava-herculis for its unique appearance and resistance to pests.
In some areas, Zanthoxylum clava-herculis is cultivated primarily as an ornamental plant.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Zanthoxylum' from Greek 'xanthos' (yellow) + 'xylon' (wood), species name referencing Hercules' club.
