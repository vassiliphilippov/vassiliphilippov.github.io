recruiter

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkruːtər / (AmE), / rɪˈkruːtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to find and enlist new employees, members, or participants for an organization or group
person who finds and hires members or employees

Examples

The recruiter contacted several candidates for the new engineering positions.
Many military recruiters visit high schools to discuss career opportunities.
During the job fair, I spoke with a recruiter from a major tech company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headhunter - a person who recruits candidates for high-level jobs
  • Degree Higher: employer - a person or organization that hires employees
  • Antonym: applicant - a person who applies for a job

Etymology

From recruit + -er, ultimately from French 'recruter' meaning 'to enlist new soldiers'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈkruːtər / (AmE), / rɪˈkruːtə / (BrE)
an official whose job is to enlist people for military service
person who enlists for military service

Examples

The recruiter explained the benefits of joining the army to the high school students.
After passing the entrance exam, she received a call from a military recruiter.
A recruiter visited our college campus to discuss opportunities in the air force.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enlistment officer - an official whose job involves enlisting personnel, especially for military service

Etymology

From 'recruit' (to enlist), based on French 'recruter' and 'recrue' (fresh supply of troops).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
