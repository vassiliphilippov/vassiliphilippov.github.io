cesarean

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈzɛriən / (AmE), / sɪˈzɛːrɪən / (BrE)
the surgical delivery of a baby by cutting through the abdominal wall and uterus
surgical birth by abdominal incision
medical

Examples

After many hours of labor, she had to undergo a cesarean to safely deliver her child.
The doctor explained the risks and benefits of a cesarean compared to natural childbirth.
Recent years have seen an increase in elective cesarean deliveries worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cesarean section - alternative term for surgical delivery of a baby

Etymology

From Latin 'Caesareus', relating to Caesar, from the belief that Julius Caesar was born by this method.

2. ADJ

/ səˈzɛriən / (AmE), / sɪˈzɛːrɪən / (BrE)
Relating to childbirth by surgical operation through an incision in the abdominal wall and uterus.
relating to surgical childbirth delivery
medical

Examples

The doctors recommended a cesarean delivery to ensure the safety of both mother and child.
She recovered quickly after her cesarean birth.
A cesarean procedure is sometimes planned in advance for medical reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cesarean section - surgical delivery of a baby via abdominal incision

Etymology

From the Latin 'caesareus,' relating to the 'Caesarean section,' named by association with Julius Caesar, who was (mythically) said to have been born this way.

Synset POS: a
