Euphorbia exigua

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔrbiə ɛkˈsiɡwiə / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪə ɛkˈsɪɡwiə / (BrE)
A species of small annual spurge native to Europe, often found as a weed and sometimes present in northeastern United States.
species of small annual spurge

Examples

The fields were dotted with Euphorbia exigua, thriving among the taller grasses.
Some gardeners consider Euphorbia exigua to be an invasive weed in their flower beds.
While hiking, she identified several rare plants, including Euphorbia exigua.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurge - any plant of the genus Euphorbia

Etymology

From Latin: 'Euphorbia' (genus named after Euphorbus, Greek physician) and 'exigua' meaning 'small' or 'meagre'.
