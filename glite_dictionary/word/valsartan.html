valsartan

1. NOUN

/ vælˈsɑːrtən / (AmE), / vælˈsɑːtæn / (BrE)
a drug that blocks the action of angiotensin II, used to treat high blood pressure and heart failure
angiotensin II inhibitor drug for high blood pressure
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed valsartan to help control her blood pressure.
After switching to valsartan, he noticed fewer side effects than with his previous medication.
Patients with heart failure may benefit from treatment with valsartan.
