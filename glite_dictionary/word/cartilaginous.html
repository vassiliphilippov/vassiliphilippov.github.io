cartilaginous

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːr.tɪˈlædʒ.ə.nəs / (AmE), / ˌkɑː.tɪˈlædʒ.ɪ.nəs / (BrE)
consisting of, containing, or relating to cartilage.
relating to cartilage

Examples

Sharks are classified as cartilaginous fish because their skeletons are made of cartilage instead of bone.
The doctor explained that the cartilaginous tissue in the knee can be damaged by injury.
Mammalian ears often contain a cartilaginous framework that supports their shape.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bony - consisting of or resembling bone

Etymology

From Latin cartilagineus (‘of cartilage’), from cartilago (‘cartilage’).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːrtɪˈlædʒɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌkɑːtɪˈlædʒɪnəs / (BrE)
difficult to chew; having the texture or characteristics of cartilage.
difficult to chew; like cartilage

Examples

The steak was so cartilaginous that I struggled to eat more than a few bites.
Some fish, like sharks and rays, have cartilaginous skeletons instead of bones.
He complained that the chicken was too cartilaginous for his liking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough - difficult to chew or cut
  • Antonym: tender - easy to chew or cut (usually describing meat)

Etymology

From Latin 'cartilagineus', from 'cartilago' (cartilage).
