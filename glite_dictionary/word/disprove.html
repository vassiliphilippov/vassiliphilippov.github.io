to disprove

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈpruv / (AmE), / dɪsˈpruːv / (BrE)
to show that something is not true; to prove false
prove something false

Examples

She managed to disprove the theory after months of research.
The lawyer tried to disprove the witness's testimony during the trial.
New evidence may disprove what we previously believed about the event.

Synset Examples

The physicist disproved his colleagues' theories

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to prove - to show that something is true by providing evidence

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (opposite) + 'probare' (to test, prove).
