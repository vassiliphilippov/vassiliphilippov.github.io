fore

1. ADJ

/ fɔr / (AmE), / fɔː / (BrE)
situated at or towards the front, especially the bow, of a vessel.
situated at or near the front (of a ship)

Examples

The fore deck was crowded with passengers eager to see the coastline.
Crew members moved quickly to the fore section of the ship during the emergency drill.
Heavy waves splashed over the fore end of the boat as the storm intensified.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aft - situated at or towards the rear of a vessel

Etymology

From Old English 'foran', meaning 'at the front'.

2. NOUN

/ fɔr / (AmE), / fɔː / (BrE)
the front part of a vessel or aircraft
front part of a ship or aircraft

Examples

Crew members gathered at the fore as the ship left the harbor.
The cargo was moved from the fore to balance the vessel.
He stood at the fore of the aircraft, ready for landing.

Synset Examples

he pointed the bow of the boat toward the finish line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bow - the front part of a ship
  • Antonym: aft - the rear part of a ship or aircraft

Etymology

From Old English 'foran' (before, in front).

3. ADV

/ fɔr / (AmE), / fɔː / (BrE)
toward or near the front, especially of a ship or aircraft.
toward the front (nautical/aviation)

Examples

The sailors moved fore to secure the anchor at the bow.
In case of emergency, passengers may be directed fore toward the cockpit.
The captain ordered the crew to go fore and check the navigation lights.

Synset Examples

the captain went fore (or forward) to check the instruments

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aft - toward the rear (of a ship or aircraft)

Etymology

From Old English 'foran' meaning 'before, in front'.
