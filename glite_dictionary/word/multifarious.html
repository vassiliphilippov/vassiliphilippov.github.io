multifarious

1. ADJ

/ ˌmʌl.təˈfer.i.əs / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪˈfeə.ri.əs / (BrE)
having many varied parts, aspects, or types; diverse or numerous in kind
having many varied parts or aspects

Examples

She has multifarious interests, ranging from painting to astrophysics.
The city's multifarious noises create a unique atmosphere day and night.
His library is filled with books on multifarious topics and disciplines.

Synset Examples

a many-sided subject
a multifaceted undertaking
multifarious interests
the multifarious noise of a great city
a miscellaneous crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diverse - showing a great deal of variety; very different
  • Synonym: varied - characterized by variety; having many differences
  • Antonym: uniform - not changing in form or character; remaining the same in all cases

Etymology

From Latin 'multifarius' (of many kinds), from 'multi-' (many) + 'facies' (face, form).
