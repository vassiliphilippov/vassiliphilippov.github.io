garishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛrɪʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡɛː.rɪʃ.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being excessively bright, flashy, or showy, especially in color or decoration
excessive brightness or showiness

Examples

She was criticized for the garishness of her outfit at the ceremony.
The garishness of the neon signs made the street hard to look at at night.
Some people love decorating with garishness, while others prefer a more minimalist style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaudiness - the quality of being too bright or ornate
  • Antonym: subtlety - the quality of being delicate or understated

Etymology

From 'garish' (probably from Middle English 'gare', meaning 'to stare') + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛrɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːrɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being excessively bright, flashy, or tastelessly showy.
tasteless showiness

Examples

The garishness of the neon signs overwhelmed the small street.
She disliked the garishness of the decorations at the party.
Critics condemned the garishness of the new building's facade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaudiness - the quality of being too brightly colored or ornamented
  • Antonym: subtlety - the quality of being understated or delicately complex; the opposite of garishness

Etymology

From 'garish' (early 16th century, probably from obsolete Middle English 'gare' meaning 'to stare') + '-ness'.
