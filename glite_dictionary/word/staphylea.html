Staphylea

1. PROPN

/ stæfəˈliːə / (AmE), / stæfəˈliːə / (BrE)
a genus of small trees or shrubs in the family Staphylaceae, commonly known as bladdernuts, native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere
genus of small trees or shrubs (bladdernuts)

Examples

The Staphylea genus is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Staphylea genus.
The fruit of Staphylea plants is distinctive, giving the plant its common name, bladdernut.

Etymology

From Greek staphylē (a bunch of grapes), referring to the shape of the plant's fruit clusters.
