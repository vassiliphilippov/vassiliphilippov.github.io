modernness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑdərnnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɒd.ən.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being modern, up-to-date, or characteristic of the present time
the state of being modern or current

Examples

The modernness of the new library building impressed the entire community.
She admired the modernness of his approach to solving traditional problems.
Many tourists are attracted by the modernness and vibrancy of the city.

Synset Examples

a shopping mall would instill a spirit of modernity into this village

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modernity - the quality or condition of being modern
  • Antonym: antiquity - the ancient past, or the quality of being ancient or old-fashioned

Etymology

From "modern" + suffix "-ness" (forming abstract nouns).
