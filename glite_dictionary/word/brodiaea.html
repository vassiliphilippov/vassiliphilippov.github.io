brodiaea

1. NOUN

/ broʊˈdiːə / (AmE), / brəʊˈdiːə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial plants native to western North America, with grass-like leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers.
a plant genus with grasslike leaves and blue or purple flowers

Examples

In the spring, the hills were dotted with blooming brodiaea.
The delicate blue flowers of the brodiaea attracted several species of pollinators.
Many gardeners value brodiaea for its elegant, grass-like foliage and vibrant flower clusters.

Etymology

Named after Scottish botanist James Brodie (1744–1824).
