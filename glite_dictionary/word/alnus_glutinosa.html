Alnus glutinosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈælnəs ɡluːˈtɪnəsə / (AmE), / ˈal.nəs ɡluːˈtɪn.ə.sə / (BrE)
a species of alder tree native to Europe and western Asia, with dark bark and rounded leaves, often found in wetlands
species of alder tree (black alder)

Examples

The Alnus glutinosa is commonly found along riverbanks and wetlands in Europe.
Botanists studied the Alnus glutinosa for its ability to improve soil fertility.
We planted an Alnus glutinosa in our garden to attract more wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black alder - common name for Alnus glutinosa
  • Degree Higher: alder - any tree of the genus Alnus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Alnus' (alder) and 'glutinosa' (sticky, referring to the leaves).
