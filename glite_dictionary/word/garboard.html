garboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːbɔːd / (BrE)
the first plank or wale laid next to the keel of a wooden ship, forming the lowest part of the hull
plank next to the keel of a ship

Examples

The garboard is crucial to the strength and integrity of the ship's hull.
During restoration, the shipwright replaced both the keel and the garboard.
Water seeped in where the garboard plank had rotted away.

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'garboord', from 'garen' (to gather, connect) + 'boord' (board, side of a ship).
