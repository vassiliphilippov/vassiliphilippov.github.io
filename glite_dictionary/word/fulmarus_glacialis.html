Fulmarus glacialis

1. NOUN

/ fʊlˈmɛərəs ɡleɪˈsiːəlɪs / (AmE), / fʊlˈmɑːrəs ɡlæˈsiːˌælɪs / (BrE)
a heavy, short-tailed oceanic bird of polar regions, commonly known as the northern fulmar
northern fulmar species of oceanic bird

Examples

A Fulmarus glacialis glided steadily just above the icy waves, searching for food.
Researchers observed that the nesting sites of Fulmarus glacialis are often located on steep coastal cliffs.
The migration patterns of Fulmarus glacialis take it across both the North Atlantic and the Arctic oceans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern fulmar - common name for Fulmarus glacialis

Etymology

From Latin: Fulmarus (genus name, from Old Norse 'fúll' meaning foul and 'már' meaning gull) and glacialis (Latin for 'icy', relating to its northern habitat).
