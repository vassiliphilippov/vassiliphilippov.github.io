despondently

1. ADV

/ dɪˈspɑndəntli / (AmE), / dɪˈspɒndəntli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a loss of hope or confidence; in a dejected or discouraged manner.
in a hopeless or dejected manner

Examples

She sighed despondently when she realized her efforts had gone unnoticed.
He stared despondently at the test results, unable to hide his disappointment.
After losing the match, the team walked off the field despondently.

Synset Examples

‘Why can't you understand?’ she asked despairingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despairingly - in a way that shows despair or loss of hope
  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a happy or optimistic manner

Etymology

From "despondent" + "-ly"; ultimately from Latin "despondere" (to give up, lose hope).
