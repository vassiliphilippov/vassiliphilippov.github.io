Amur

1. PROPN

/ əˈmʊr / (AmE), / əˈmjʊə / (BrE)
a major river in eastern Asia that forms part of the border between northeastern China and Russia, flowing into the Sea of Okhotsk
major river between China and Russia

Examples

The Amur River is one of the longest in Asia, marking the border between Russia and China.
Ships navigate the Amur during the ice-free months to transport goods.
Wildlife conservation efforts along the Amur have helped protect endangered species such as the Amur tiger.

Etymology

From Russian 'Амур' (Amur), likely from a local Tungusic or Mongolic language.
