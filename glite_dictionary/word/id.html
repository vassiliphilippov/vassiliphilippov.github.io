I.D.

1. NOUN

/ aɪ di / (AmE), / aɪ diː / (BrE)
an official card or badge that shows a person's identity, especially for proof of age or employment
identification card or badge

Examples

The security guard checked my I.D. before letting me enter the building.
You must show your I.D. if you want to buy alcohol here.
She forgot her I.D. at home and couldn't access the office.

Synset Examples

you had to show your ID in order to get in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: identification card - card that proves someone's identity
  • Synonym: passport - official document certifying identity and citizenship

Etymology

Abbreviation of identification, from identify.
