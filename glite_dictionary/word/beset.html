to beset

1. VERB

/ bɪˈsɛt / (AmE), / bɪˈsɛt / (BrE)
to decorate or cover something lavishly, especially with jewels or ornaments
decorate or cover lavishly

Examples

The queen's crown was beset with sparkling diamonds and rubies.
The artist beset the frame with tiny pearls and crystals.
Her velvet cloak was beset with gold embroidery and gemstones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adorn - to make more attractive by adding decoration
  • Synonym: to embellish - to decorate by adding details or features

Etymology

From Old English 'besettan' (to set about, surround).

2. VERB

/ bɪˈsɛt / (AmE), / bɪˈsɛt / (BrE)
to attack or trouble persistently from all directions; to assail or hem in on every side
attack or trouble from all sides

Examples

The small village was beset by enemy forces for weeks.
Throughout her career, she was beset by rumors and criticism.
We found ourselves beset with challenges at every stage of the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surround - to encircle on all sides
  • Synonym: to harass - to trouble or attack persistently

Etymology

From Old English *besettan* (to surround, besiege), from be- (around) + settan (to set).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01129126-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ bɪˈsɛt / (AmE), / bɪˈsɛt / (BrE)
To trouble or harass persistently; to annoy continually or chronically.
to harass or trouble persistently

Examples

Journalists are often beset by deadlines and constant demands for new stories.
The project was beset with unexpected technical issues from the very beginning.
He felt beset by doubts after receiving conflicting advice from his colleagues.

Synset Examples

He is known to harry his staff when he is overworked
This man harasses his female co-workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harass - to annoy or trouble someone repeatedly
  • Synonym: to harry - to persistently attack or trouble

Etymology

Old English 'besettan', from 'be-' (about) + 'settan' (to set).
