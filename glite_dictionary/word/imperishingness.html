imperishingness

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛrɪʃɪŋnɪs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛrɪʃɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being incapable of decaying, perishing, or being destroyed.
quality of not decaying or perishing

Examples

The ancient monument stands as a symbol of the imperishingness of human creativity.
Many materials are valued for their imperishingness in harsh environments.
She admired the imperishingness of their friendship despite the passage of time.

Synset Examples

he advertised the imperishability of the product

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperishability - the quality of being incapable of perishing or decay
  • Antonym: decay - the process of deteriorating or breaking down

Etymology

From 'imperishing' (not perishing) + '-ness' (noun suffix).
