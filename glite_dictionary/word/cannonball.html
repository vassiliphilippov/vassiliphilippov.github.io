cannonball

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.ənˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈkæn.ənˌbɔːl / (BrE)
A solid, round metal ball that was fired from a cannon, especially in the past.
solid projectile fired from a cannon

Examples

The museum displayed a cannonball that was found on the old battlefield.
During the re-enactment, the soldiers loaded a cannonball into the cannon.
A single cannonball can weigh several kilograms depending on the size of the cannon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: projectile - an object that is thrown or fired forwards, especially as a weapon

Etymology

Formed from 'cannon' + 'ball', referring to its use and shape.
