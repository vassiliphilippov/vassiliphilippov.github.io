greenling

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrinlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnlɪŋ / (BrE)
a food fish native to the northern Pacific Ocean, especially of the genus Hexagrammos, valued for its edible flesh
a northern Pacific food fish

Examples

Fishermen in Alaska often catch greenling for commercial and recreational purposes.
The chef prepared a dish using freshly caught greenling from the market.
Many coastal communities rely on greenling as an important source of seafood.
