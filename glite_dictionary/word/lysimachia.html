Lysimachia

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌsɪˈmeɪkiə / (AmE), / lɪˌsɪˈmækɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants, commonly known as loosestrife, typically found in damp or swampy areas and often having yellow flowers.
genus of marsh plants (loosestrife)

Examples

Lysimachia thrives in moist meadows and along the edges of ponds.
Gardeners often plant Lysimachia for its bright yellow blooms and rapid spread.
Some species of Lysimachia can become invasive if not properly managed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loosestrife - common name for plants in the Lysimachia genus

Etymology

Latin, from Greek Lysimakheia, named after Lysimachus (350–281 BC), a Macedonian king believed to have discovered the plant’s medicinal uses.

2. NOUN

/ lɪsɪˈmækiə / (AmE), / lɪsɪˈmækiə / (BrE)
a genus of plants in the primrose family, typically found in damp or swampy areas and often having yellow flowers; commonly known as loosestrife
a genus of flowering plants (loosestrife)

Examples

Some species of Lysimachia can spread rapidly in wet garden beds.
The yellow flowers of Lysimachia brighten many marshy areas in summer.
Botanists have identified over a hundred species within the genus Lysimachia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loosestrife - common name for plants in the genus Lysimachia and related genera

Etymology

From Latin *Lysimachia*, from Ancient Greek *Λυσιμαχία* (Lysimakhía), named after Lysimachus, a companion of Alexander the Great.
