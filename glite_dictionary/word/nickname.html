nickname

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkˌneɪm / (AmE), / ˈnɪk.neɪm / (BrE)
an informal or descriptive name given to a place or thing, often reflecting its characteristics or reputation
informal name for a place or thing

Examples

The city's nickname is 'The Windy City' because of its breezy weather.
The spacecraft earned the nickname 'Tin Can' among its designers.
She learned that 'Big Apple' is New York's most famous nickname.

Synset Examples

the nickname for the U.S. Constitution is ‘Old Ironsides’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epithet - a descriptive phrase or nickname expressing a quality of a person or thing

Etymology

From Middle English *ekename* ('an additional name'), influenced by the use of 'an ekename' misheard as 'a nickname'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkˌneɪm / (AmE), / ˈnɪk.neɪm / (BrE)
a familiar or informal name given to a person, often a shortened or altered version of their real name
familiar or informal name for a person

Examples

Everyone called him by his nickname, which was much easier to remember than his full name.
She disliked her childhood nickname and insisted on being called by her proper name.
After his heroic act, he earned the nickname 'Champ' among his friends.

Synset Examples

Joe's mother would not use his nickname and always called him Joseph
Henry's nickname was Slim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alias - another name used instead of the real one

Etymology

From Middle English 'ekename' (an additional name), influenced by the misdivision of 'an ekename' as 'a nekename'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06349063-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈnɪkˌneɪm / (AmE), / ˈnɪk.neɪm / (BrE)
to give a nickname to someone or something; to call by a familiar or informal name instead of the real name
give a nickname to

Examples

Everyone at school nicknamed him 'Speedy' because he ran so fast.
They affectionately nicknamed the city 'The Big Apple'.
The press quickly nicknamed the new leader 'Iron Lady'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dub - to give a name or nickname to someone or something

Etymology

From Middle English 'nikename', a misdivision of 'an ekename' (an additional name), from Old English 'ecename' ('eke' meaning 'additional').
