Cichorium intybus

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkɔriəm ɪnˈtaɪbəs / (AmE), / sɪˈkɔːrɪəm ɪnˈtɪbəs / (BrE)
A perennial plant with blue flower heads, cultivated for its edible leaves and root, also known as chicory.
perennial plant; chicory

Examples

Cichorium intybus is often used as a coffee substitute in many regions.
Farmers rotate crops with Cichorium intybus to improve soil health.
The edible leaves of Cichorium intybus are popular in Mediterranean salads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicory - common name for Cichorium intybus, a plant cultivated for its root and leaves

Etymology

From Latin *Cichorium* (chicory) + *intybus*, Latinized from Greek *intybos*.
