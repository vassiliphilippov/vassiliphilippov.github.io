clerisy

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɛrəsi / (AmE), / ˈklɛrɪsi / (BrE)
the educated or intellectual elite of a society
the intellectual elite

Examples

The university was once dominated by a powerful clerisy that shaped academic culture.
Many writers criticized the detachment of the clerisy from everyday social concerns.
He joined the clerisy after years of scholarly work and publishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligentsia - the intellectual elite of a society, especially as a social class

Etymology

From German 'Klerisei' (clergy) via Latin 'clericia'.
