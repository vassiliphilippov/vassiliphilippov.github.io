leaching

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈliːtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which a substance is removed from a solid material by percolating liquid, especially water, through it
removal of substances by percolating liquid

Examples

Excessive rainfall can cause nutrient leaching from the soil, reducing plant growth.
The leaching of contaminants from waste dumps poses a risk to groundwater quality.
Industrial processes often involve leaching to separate metals from their ores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extraction - the act of obtaining something from a mixture or compound by chemical or physical means

Etymology

From the verb 'leach', from Middle English 'lechen', likely related to Old English 'leccan' (to moisten).
