capsulate

1. VERB

/ ˈkæpsjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæpsjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to enclose or surround something in a capsule or similar protective covering, especially in scientific or medical contexts
enclose in a capsule
medical

Examples

Scientists capsulate the medication to ensure it dissolves only in the intestine.
The seeds are capsulated to protect them from harsh environmental conditions.
Technicians will capsulate the sample before sending it to the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encapsulate - to enclose in or as if in a capsule

Etymology

From Latin 'capsula' (small box) + '-ate', forming a verb meaning to make into a capsule.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkæpsjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkapsjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(of seeds or spores) enclosed in a capsule; having a capsule enclosing the structure.
enclosed in a capsule (botany)
medical

Examples

Certain mushrooms produce capsulate spores that are protected from environmental stress.
The capsulate seeds are released only when the capsule matures and splits open.
Under the microscope, the capsulate structure of the pollen grains is clearly visible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encapsulated - enclosed in a capsule or cover

Etymology

From Latin 'capsula' (little box, capsule), with English suffix '-ate'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
