Poulenc

1. PROPN

/ puːˈlɛŋk / (AmE), / puːˈlɛŋk / (BrE)
(Francis Poulenc) (1899–1963), French pianist and composer, noted for his contributions to 20th-century classical music.
French composer and pianist (1899–1963)

Examples

Many music critics consider Poulenc's choral works to be his finest compositions.
The orchestra performed a piece by Poulenc at last night's concert.
As a pianist, Poulenc was acclaimed for his expressive playing style.

Etymology

French surname, used for Francis Poulenc, the composer.
