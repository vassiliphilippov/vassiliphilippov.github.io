cookhouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊkˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈkʊk.haʊs / (BrE)
a building or area on a ship or in a camp where food is prepared and cooked, especially for a group of people
kitchen area on a ship or camp

Examples

Each morning, the crew gathered in the cookhouse for breakfast before starting their work.
The smell of fresh bread drifted from the cookhouse across the ship's deck.
Due to the storm, everyone was forced to eat in the cookhouse instead of on the upper deck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: galley - the kitchen on a ship or aircraft

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʊkˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈkʊkˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a small building or shelter, separate from the main structure, used for cooking, especially outdoors or at camps or worksites
outdoor or detached shelter for cooking

Examples

The workers gathered in the cookhouse for their midday meal.
During the summer camp, meals were prepared in an old wooden cookhouse.
The army set up a temporary cookhouse near the field for the soldiers.

Synset Examples

the circus used a tent as their cookhouse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kitchen - a room or area where food is prepared and cooked indoors

Etymology

From 'cook' (to prepare food by heating) + 'house' (building for a specific purpose).
