hierarchy

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪəˌrɑːrki / (AmE), / ˈhaɪərɑːki / (BrE)
a system in which people or groups are ranked one above the other according to status or authority, especially within an organization or society
system of ranked authority or status

Examples

The military is known for its strict hierarchy and clear chain of command.
Many companies have a corporate hierarchy that defines each employee's responsibilities and authority.
Breaking down the traditional hierarchy can encourage more open communication within an organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pecking order - the informal ranking of people according to status in a group
  • Antonym: egalitarianism - belief in the equality of all people

Etymology

From Greek 'hierarkhia' (rule of a high priest), from 'hieros' (sacred) + 'archein' (to rule).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.əˌrɑr.ki / (AmE), / ˈhaɪəˌrɑː.ki / (BrE)
a system in which people or things are arranged according to their importance or status
system of ranked order or authority

Examples

Many companies have a rigid hierarchy in which authority flows from the top down.
The Catholic Church has maintained a complex hierarchy for centuries.
She challenged the traditional hierarchy of the organization, advocating for a flatter structure.

Synset Examples

put honesty first in her hierarchy of values

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pecking order - the informal hierarchy in a group
  • Antonym: equality - the state of being equal in status or rights

Etymology

From Greek hierarkhia, from hierarkhēs 'sacred ruler', from hieros 'sacred' + arkhē 'rule'.
