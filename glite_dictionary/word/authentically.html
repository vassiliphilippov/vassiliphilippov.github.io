authentically

1. ADV

/ ɔːˈθɛn.tɪ.kli / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɛn.tɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a genuine or authentic manner; in a way that shows something is real, true, or done with authority.
in a genuine or authentic way

Examples

The restaurant serves dishes prepared authentically, following traditional family recipes.
She speaks Italian authentically, after living there for many years.
The story was told authentically, capturing the culture and spirit of the time.

Synset Examples

it is authentically British

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genuinely - in a real and sincere way
  • Antonym: falsely - in a way that is not genuine or true

Etymology

Formed by adding the adverbial suffix -ly to "authentic," from Greek "authentikos" (original, genuine).

2. ADV

/ əˈθɛn.tɪ.kli / (AmE), / ɔːˈθen.tɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a way that is real, true, or done in accordance with the original or genuine manner; genuinely and with authority.
in a genuine or original manner

Examples

The restaurant is known for its authentically Italian dishes prepared by chefs from Rome.
The play was authentically performed in the original Shakespearean English, captivating the audience.
She wanted to live authentically, embracing her true self without pretending for others.

Synset Examples

it is authentically British

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genuinely - in a sincere, true, or authentic manner
  • Antonym: falsely - in a manner that is not genuine or real

Etymology

From authentic + -ally, ultimately from Greek 'authentikos' (genuine).
