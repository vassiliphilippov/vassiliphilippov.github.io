Macrozoarces americanus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækroʊzoʊˈɑːrsiz əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊzəʊˈɑːsiːz əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (BrE)
A species of marine fish known as the Atlantic wolfish, found along the northeastern coast of North America.
species of Atlantic wolfish

Examples

The Macrozoarces americanus is an important predator in its cold Atlantic habitat.
Scientists observed a decline in Macrozoarces americanus populations due to environmental changes.
Fishermen occasionally catch Macrozoarces americanus off the coast of Maine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atlantic wolffish - common name for Macrozoarces americanus, a large marine fish of the North Atlantic

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) + zoarces (a genus of fish), with the species name meaning 'American'.
