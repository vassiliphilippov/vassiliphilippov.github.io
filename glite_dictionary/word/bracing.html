bracing

1. ADJ

/ ˈbreɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
(of air, weather, etc.) making you feel invigorated and full of energy.
invigorating; giving energy, esp. air or weather

Examples

A walk along the cliffs in the bracing wind left us feeling alive and alert.
She found the bracing sea breeze invigorating after her long illness.
The bracing air of the highlands quickly cleared his mind.

Synset Examples

the bracing mountain air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invigorating - making you feel more lively and energetic
  • Synonym: refreshing - pleasantly fresh or cool, making you feel revived
  • Antonym: enervating - causing you to feel drained or lacking in energy

Etymology

From the verb 'brace' (to make stronger or firmer), itself from Old French 'bracier' (to embrace, clasp), from Latin 'bracchium' (arm).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
a structural component used to strengthen or stiffen a framework or structure
structural support for stiffening frameworks

Examples

The engineer recommended adding extra bracing to stabilize the roof during the renovation.
Steel bracing was installed to reinforce the building's frame against earthquakes.
Without proper bracing, the wooden structure would be too weak to support the upper floors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strut - a structural component designed to resist compression
  • Synonym: support - a structural element that bears weight or holds up an object

Etymology

From brace + -ing, relating to the action or result of bracing.

Synset POS: n
