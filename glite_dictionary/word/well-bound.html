well-bound

1. ADJ

/ wɛlˈbaʊnd / (AmE), / wɛlˈbaʊnd / (BrE)
(of a book) having a strong and attractive binding.
having a sturdy, attractive bookbinding

Examples

The collector admired the well-bound volume for its elegant leather cover.
Every library desires a shelf full of well-bound books that can withstand frequent handling.
She chose a well-bound edition of her favorite novel as a gift.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: poorly bound - (of a book) having a weak or unattractive binding
