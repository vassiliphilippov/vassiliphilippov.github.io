to decamp

1. VERB

/ dɪˈkæmp / (AmE), / dɪˈkæmp / (BrE)
to leave a place suddenly or secretly, often to avoid detection or capture
leave suddenly or secretly

Examples

The thieves decamped with the stolen jewels before the police arrived.
As soon as the sun rose, the soldiers decamped from their temporary base.
Realizing they had been discovered, the spies quickly decamped from the safe house.

Synset Examples

The hikers decamped before dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depart - to leave, especially in a formal or planned way
  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from a place or danger quickly

Etymology

From French 'décamper', from 'de-' (from) + 'camper' (to camp).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈkæmp / (AmE), / dɪˈkamp / (BrE)
to leave a place suddenly and secretly, often taking something or someone with you, especially to escape trouble or avoid being noticed
leave suddenly and secretly

Examples

Realizing they had been discovered, the criminals decided to decamp before the police arrived.
She watched in disbelief as her business partner decamped with all the company’s resources.
The guests began to decamp quietly after the argument at the dinner table.

Synset Examples

The thief made off with our silver
the accountant absconded with the cash from the safe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abscond - to leave hurriedly and secretly, often to avoid arrest or prosecution
  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger

Etymology

From French 'décamper', from 'de-' (from) + 'camp' (camp), originally military slang for breaking up camp suddenly.

3. VERB

/ dɪˈkæmp / (AmE), / dɪˈkamp / (BrE)
to leave a place suddenly and secretly, especially to avoid trouble or escape from something
leave suddenly and secretly

Examples

After the meeting, the CEO decided to decamp before any questions could be asked.
The thieves managed to decamp with the valuables before the police arrived.
When the campfire was discovered, the group had already decamped into the forest.

Synset Examples

She persuaded him to decamp
skip town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • Antonym: to linger - to stay somewhere longer than necessary

Etymology

From French 'décamper', from 'dé-' (away) + 'camper' (to camp), meaning to break camp.
