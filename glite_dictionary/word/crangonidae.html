Crangonidae

1. NOUN

/ krænˈɡoʊnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kræŋˈɡɒnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized shrimps found in marine and estuarine environments, including species commonly known as sand shrimps.
family of marine shrimps

Examples

Several species within the Crangonidae family play a crucial role in coastal food webs.
The Crangonidae are often studied for their adaptability to different salinities.
Fishermen sometimes catch Crangonidae while trawling for other seafood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrimp - small crustacean with an elongated body and a primarily swimming mode of locomotion

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus *Crangon* + suffix *-idae*, used for animal family names in taxonomy.
