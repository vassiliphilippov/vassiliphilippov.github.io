hours

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊɚz / (AmE), / ˈaʊəz / (BrE)
A long or indefinite period of time; used to emphasize that something lasts for a considerable time.
a long or indefinite period of time

Examples

They waited for hours before receiving any news.
She spent hours looking for her lost keys.
It felt like hours had passed during the meeting.

Synset Examples

they talked for hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ages - a very long time; ages used in a similar indefinite sense

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊɚz / (AmE), / ˈaʊəz / (BrE)
the amount of time someone is scheduled to work or actually works, typically measured in periods of sixty minutes
time period assigned for work

Examples

The company offers flexible hours to accommodate working parents.
She often stays late and puts in extra hours at the office.
During the summer, my hours are shorter than in winter.

Synset Examples

they work long hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shift - a period of work time, especially when work is divided into periods throughout a day
