pandurate

1. ADJ

/ ˈpændjʊˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈpændjʊərət / (BrE)
(of a leaf) shaped like a fiddle, with rounded ends and a narrowed, constricted center.
fiddle-shaped (botanical)

Examples

The botanist noted that the leaf was pandurate, narrowing in the middle like a fiddle.
Some species exhibit pandurate leaves, which help in identifying them.
The pandurate form of the leaf distinguishes it from other similar plants.

Etymology

From Latin 'panduratus', from 'pandura' (a musical instrument, a kind of lute).
