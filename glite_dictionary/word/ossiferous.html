ossiferous

1. ADJ

/ ɑˈsɪfərəs / (AmE), / ɒˈsɪfərəs / (BrE)
(of rock, soil, or sediment) containing bones, especially fossilized bones.
containing bones, especially fossils

Examples

The paleontologists were excited to discover an ossiferous layer of rock in the canyon.
Several ossiferous caves in the region have yielded ancient animal fossils.
This ossiferous deposit is believed to have formed during the early Pleistocene epoch.

Synset Examples

ossiferous caves

Etymology

From Latin ossifer (bone-bearing), from os (bone) + -fer (bearing), with English suffix -ous.
