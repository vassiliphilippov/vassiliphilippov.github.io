horror

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɒrə / (BrE)
a strong feeling of fear, disgust, or intense aversion.
intense fear or aversion

Examples

She recoiled in horror upon seeing the gruesome scene.
The idea of cruelty to animals filled him with horror.
After reading the report, she could not hide her horror at the findings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terror - extreme fear
  • Antonym: delight - great pleasure, opposite of aversion or horror

Etymology

From Latin horrere (to shudder, to bristle with fear).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrər / (AmE), / ˈhɒr.ər / (BrE)
a person or thing that causes feelings of intense fear, shock, or revulsion; something or someone that is very unpleasant or shocking
something or someone causing horror

Examples

The old abandoned hospital is a real horror at night.
The new film was a horror that many viewers found deeply disturbing.
He called the house a horror after seeing its ruined interior.

Synset Examples

the painting that others found so beautiful was a horror to him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monstrosity - something especially large and ugly; something very unsightly or frightening
  • Antonym: delight - something that causes great pleasure or joy (opposite in emotional effect)

Etymology

From Latin 'horror', meaning 'trembling, shuddering'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrər / (AmE), / ˈhɒrə / (BrE)
A feeling of intense and profound fear, shock, or disgust.
intense fear or shock

Examples

She froze in horror as the shadow moved across the wall.
The film was filled with scenes of unspeakable horror.
He recalled with horror the events of that terrible night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terror - extreme fear
  • Antonym: delight - great pleasure and joy

Etymology

From Latin 'horror' (a trembling, shuddering, dread), from 'horrēre' (to bristle, shudder).
