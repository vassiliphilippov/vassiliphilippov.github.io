polyptoton

1. NOUN

/ pəˌlɪptəˈtɑn / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪpˈtəʊtɒn / (BrE)
the rhetorical repetition of a word in different grammatical forms or inflections within a sentence
repetition of a word in varied forms

Examples

The poet uses polyptoton to emphasize the different forms of love through words like 'love', 'loved', and 'loving'.
Shakespeare famously employed polyptoton in his line, 'Who shall watch the watchmen?'
In her essay, she analyzed the clever polyptoton found in ancient Roman speeches.

Synset Examples

polyptoton such as ‘my own heart's heart’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anaphora - repetition of a word or phrase at the start of successive clauses
  • Synonym: epistrophe - repetition of a word at the end of successive phrases or sentences

Etymology

From Greek polyptōton, from poly- (many) + ptōsis (case, grammatical inflection).
