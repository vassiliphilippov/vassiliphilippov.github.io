onstage

1. ADV

/ ˈɑnˌsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
on or onto a stage; taking place or appearing as part of a performance
appearing or taking place on a stage

Examples

The actor waited nervously before stepping onstage for his first scene.
She has always felt most comfortable when she is onstage, in front of an audience.
A new set was brought onstage during the intermission.

Synset Examples

it was time for her to go onstage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: offstage - away from the stage; not visible to the audience
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00261207-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑnˌsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
Situated or taking place on the part of a stage visible to the audience.
on the visible part of a stage

Examples

He waited nervously before making his onstage entrance.
The actors practiced their onstage movements during rehearsal.
Only authorized personnel are allowed to stand on the onstage area during the performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: offstage - situated or taking place in the area of a stage not visible to the audience
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01642195-a
Synset POS: a
