roundup

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊn.dʌp / (AmE), / ˈraʊn.dʌp / (BrE)
an act of gathering together people, especially suspects or criminals, by the police in a systematic operation
police gathering of suspects

Examples

The police carried out a roundup of suspected gang members early this morning.
A roundup of illegal immigrants led to several arrests in the city.
There was a massive roundup after the bank robbery, with dozens of people taken in for questioning.

Synset Examples

a mass roundup of suspects

2. NOUN

/ ˈraʊnˌdʌp / (AmE), / ˈraʊndʌp / (BrE)
a summary or review of events or information, especially presented in a concise form
a summary review of events or items

Examples

Today's paper included a roundup of the week's most important news stories.
The magazine features a roundup of the best restaurants in the city.
The report ended with a roundup of this month's key economic statistics.

Synset Examples

a news roundup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summary - a brief statement of the main points of something
  • Synonym: review - a formal assessment or examination of something with the possibility or intention of instituting change if necessary

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'round up', originally meaning to gather things or people together.

3. NOUN

/ ˈraʊnd.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈraʊnd.ʌp / (BrE)
an act of gathering together livestock, such as cattle or horses, so they can be counted, branded, or sold
gathering of livestock for counting or sale

Examples

Every spring, the ranchers organize a roundup to bring in all the cattle from the open range.
The annual roundup is an important event for the local farming community.
During the roundup, skilled cowboys ride out to gather strays from the hills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drive - an organized movement of livestock from one location to another

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'round up', meaning to gather together.
