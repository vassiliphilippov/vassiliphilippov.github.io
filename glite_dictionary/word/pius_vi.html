Pius VI

1. PROPN

/ ˌpaɪ.əs sɪksθ / (AmE), / ˌpaɪ.əs sɪksθ / (BrE)
(1717–1799) Italian pope from 1775 to 1799, noted for his papacy during the French Revolution and his imprisonment by French forces.
Pope from 1775 to 1799; imprisoned by French

Examples

During his reign, Pius VI faced significant challenges from the French Revolution.
The fall of the Papal States marked the capture of Pius VI by Napoleon's troops.
Historians remember Pius VI for his resistance to secular reforms in late 18th-century Europe.

Etymology

From Latin 'Pius', papal name meaning 'pious' or 'devout'; sequential papal numbering.
