impassiveness

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpæsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɑːsɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality of not showing any emotion or feelings; lack of emotional response.
absence of emotional reaction

Examples

Despite the chaos in the courtroom, the judge maintained an air of impassiveness.
Her impassiveness made it difficult for others to know what she was truly thinking.
The soldier’s impassiveness under fire impressed his commanding officers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of interest or emotion
  • Antonym: emotion - strong feeling such as love, hate, or anger
  • Synonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern

Etymology

From Latin 'impassibilis' (incapable of feeling pain), via French and Late Latin forms; related to 'impassive'.
