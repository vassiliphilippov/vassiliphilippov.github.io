Guru

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʊru / (AmE), / ˈɡʊəruː / (BrE)
(in Sikhism) any of the first ten spiritual leaders who established and shaped the Sikh religion from the late 15th to the early 18th century.
Sikh spiritual leader; one of the first ten leaders of Sikhism

Examples

Sikhs pay great respect to each Guru and their teachings.
The third Guru of Sikhism, Guru Amar Das, introduced important reforms.
Pilgrims visit shrines dedicated to every Guru in Sikh tradition.

Etymology

From Sanskrit गुरु (guru, 'teacher, spiritual guide').
