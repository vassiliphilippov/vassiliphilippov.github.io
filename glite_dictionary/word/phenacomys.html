Phenacomys

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnækəˌmɪs / (AmE), / fɛˈnækəˌmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents (voles) native to North America, commonly known as bog lemmings.
genus of North American voles

Examples

The bog lemming, belonging to the genus Phenacomys, is found in wet, grassy habitats.
Researchers observed several Phenacomys species during their survey of North American rodents.
Unlike other voles, members of Phenacomys are adapted to moist, boggy environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vole - a small burrowing rodent

Etymology

From Greek phainein (to appear) + komys (hair), referring to the animal's appearance.
