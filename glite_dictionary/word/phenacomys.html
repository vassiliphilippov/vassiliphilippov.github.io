phenacomys

1. NOUN

/ fɛˈnæk.əˌmɪs / (AmE), / fɛˈnæk.əˌmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, vole-like rodents found in cold forested regions of Canada and the western United States, also known as bog lemmings.
genus of small North American rodents

Examples

The northern bog lemming, a species of phenacomys, thrives in cold, wet habitats.
Researchers observed several phenacomyses in the subalpine forest during the study.
Unlike true voles, phenacomys species often have softer fur and a stubbier tail.

Etymology

From New Latin Phenacomys, genus name constructed from Greek 'phenax' (deceiver) and 'mys' (mouse).
