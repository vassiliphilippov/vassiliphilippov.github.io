cold-bloodedly

1. ADV

/ ˌkoʊldˈblʌdɪdli / (AmE), / ˌkəʊldˈblʌdɪdli / (BrE)
in an unemotional and deliberately cruel or callous manner; without pity or remorse
with deliberate cruelty and lack of emotion

Examples

The villain cold-bloodedly carried out his plan without hesitation or regret.
She cold-bloodedly dismissed her former friends when they were no longer useful to her.
The report detailed how the animals were cold-bloodedly slaughtered for profit.

Synset Examples

he cold-bloodedly planned the murder of his boss

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: coldly - in an unemotional or unfriendly manner

Etymology

Formed from 'cold-blooded' + '-ly', where 'cold-blooded' refers metaphorically to lack of emotion.
