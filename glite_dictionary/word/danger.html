danger

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈdeɪndʒə / (BrE)
a person, thing, or situation that is likely to cause harm, injury, or loss
something that may cause harm or injury

Examples

There are many dangers associated with swimming in that river.
He warned us of the danger posed by falling rocks.
Ignoring the traffic signals put her in serious danger.

Synset Examples

he feared the dangers of traveling by air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazard - something that can cause harm or danger
  • Synonym: risk - a situation involving exposure to danger
  • Antonym: safety - the condition of being protected from danger

Etymology

From Old French 'dangier', from Latin 'dominarium' (power, authority), later meaning 'power to hurt'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪndʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪndʒə / (BrE)
A place or situation where one is at risk of harm, injury, or death.
dangerous place or situation

Examples

The hikers finally reached a clearing and were out of danger.
As the volcano erupted, the residents quickly left the zone of danger.
You must not enter the construction site because it is full of danger.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: safety - the condition of being protected from or unlikely to cause danger
  • Synonym: hazard - a potential source of danger

Etymology

From Old French *dangier* (power, authority, peril), from Latin *dominium* (ownership, power).
