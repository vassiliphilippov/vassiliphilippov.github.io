rathole

1. NOUN

/ ˈrætˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈræt.həʊl / (BrE)
a hole in a wall or floor made by rats; also, any small hole made by rodents.
a hole made by rats

Examples

The old basement was full of ratholes and needed urgent repairs.
We found a rathole under the kitchen sink, so we set a trap nearby.
The inspector pointed out several ratholes in the storeroom wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burrow - a hole or tunnel dug by an animal, especially a small mammal

Etymology

From 'rat' + 'hole', meaning a hole made by rats.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrætˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈrætˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a very small, dirty, and uncomfortable room, especially one considered unpleasant to live or work in
a small, dirty, uncomfortable room

Examples

After losing his job, he was forced to live in a rathole above a noisy bar.
The office they gave me was a complete rathole with no windows or ventilation.
She refused to stay another night in that filthy rathole of a hotel room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hovel - a small, squalid, unpleasant dwelling
  • Antonym: palace - a large, impressive, and comfortable residence

Etymology

From 'rat' + 'hole', likening a cramped, unclean space to a hole where a rat might live.
