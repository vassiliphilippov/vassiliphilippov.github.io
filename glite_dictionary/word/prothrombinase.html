prothrombinase

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈθrɑːmbəˌneɪs / (AmE), / prəʊˈθrɒmbɪneɪs / (BrE)
an enzyme complex that catalyzes the conversion of prothrombin to thrombin during blood coagulation, requiring calcium ions and other coagulation factors
enzyme complex in blood clotting
medical

Examples

Prothrombinase plays a critical role in the final steps of the blood coagulation cascade.
A deficiency in prothrombinase activity can result in impaired blood clotting and excessive bleeding.
The formation of prothrombinase on platelets is essential for rapid thrombin generation during wound healing.

Etymology

From 'prothrombin' (a coagulation protein) + '-ase' (enzyme suffix).
