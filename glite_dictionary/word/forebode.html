to forebode

1. VERB

/ fɔrˈboʊd / (AmE), / fɔːˈbəʊd / (BrE)
to predict or indicate a future event, especially something unpleasant or bad
predict, especially of something bad

Examples

The dark clouds forebode an approaching storm.
Some critics forebode a decline in the housing market next year.
The eerie silence seemed to forebode trouble for the expedition.

Synset Examples

Call the outcome of an election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to foretell - to predict or tell something before it happens
  • Synonym: to predict - to state what will happen in the future
  • Synonym: to prophesy - to say that a specified thing will happen in the future

Etymology

From Old English 'forbodian', from 'for-' (prefix meaning 'before') + 'bodian' (to announce).
