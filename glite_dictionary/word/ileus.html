ileus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪli.əs / (AmE), / ˈɪli.əs / (BrE)
A blockage of the intestine, especially the ileum, that stops the contents of the intestine from passing to the lower bowel.
intestinal blockage, especially of the ileum
medical

Examples

After surgery, some patients may develop ileus and require medical intervention.
Prolonged vomiting and abdominal pain can be symptoms of ileus.
The doctor ordered an X-ray to diagnose the suspected ileus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intestinal obstruction - blockage that prevents food or liquid from passing through the intestines

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek eileos (intestinal obstruction).
