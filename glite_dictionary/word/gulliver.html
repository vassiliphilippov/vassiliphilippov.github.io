Gulliver

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌlɪvər / (AmE), / ˈɡʌlɪvə / (BrE)
the fictional Englishman who is the protagonist of Jonathan Swift's satirical novel 'Gulliver's Travels', known for his voyages to fantastical lands such as Lilliput
fictional traveler in 'Gulliver's Travels'

Examples

Gulliver encountered many strange people and customs on his journey through Lilliput and Brobdingnag.
The story of Gulliver has inspired countless adaptations in theater and film.
In the novel, Gulliver is both an observer and a satirical commentator on human society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lilliputian - a tiny person, from the inhabitants of Lilliput in 'Gulliver's Travels'

Etymology

From the name of Lemuel Gulliver, the main character in Jonathan Swift's 1726 novel 'Gulliver's Travels'.
