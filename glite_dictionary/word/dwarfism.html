dwarfism

1. NOUN

/ ˈdwɔrˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdwɔːfɪzəm / (BrE)
a medical or genetic condition resulting in abnormally short stature or growth.
condition causing short stature
medical

Examples

Some forms of dwarfism are caused by genetic mutations that affect bone growth.
He has a rare type of dwarfism that results in shorter limbs but a normal torso.
The charity supports families affected by dwarfism and promotes awareness about the condition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gigantism - a condition causing excessive growth in stature

Etymology

From 'dwarf' + '-ism', modeled on medical term formation.
