Fragaria

1. NOUN

/ frəˈɡɛəriə / (AmE), / frəˈɡɛəriə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the rose family, commonly known as strawberries, which produce the edible red fruit.
genus of strawberry plants

Examples

Several species of Fragaria are cultivated for their delicious fruit.
The wild Fragaria plants grow naturally in forest clearings.
Researchers are studying the genetics of Fragaria to improve crop yields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strawberry - the sweet, soft red fruit of Fragaria species

Etymology

From Latin *fragum* (strawberry), referring to the plant's fragrance.

2. NOUN

/ frəˈɡɛriə / (AmE), / frəˈɡɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the rose family, comprising the species commonly known as strawberries.
genus of strawberry plants

Examples

Wild species belonging to the Fragaria genus can be found across many continents.
Botanists are studying the diversity within Fragaria to improve strawberry cultivation.
The cultivated garden strawberry is a hybrid descendant of two Fragaria species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strawberry - the sweet, red fruit of plants of genus Fragaria

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Fragaria, from Latin fragum (strawberry).
