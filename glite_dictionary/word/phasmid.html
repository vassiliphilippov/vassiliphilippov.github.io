phasmid

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæzmɪd / (AmE), / ˈfæzmɪd / (BrE)
A large, mostly tropical insect of the order Phasmatodea, typically with a cylindrical or flattened body and long legs, including walking sticks and leaf insects.
tropical insect; walking stick or leaf insect

Examples

The phasmid remains almost invisible among the branches due to its leaf-like appearance.
Several species of phasmids are popular in the pet trade for their unique looks.
Researchers discovered a new phasmid species in the tropical rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walking stick - an insect that resembles a stick, member of Phasmatodea
  • Synonym: leaf insect - an insect that resembles a leaf, member of Phasmatodea

Etymology

From Modern Latin Phasmida, from Greek phasma (apparition, phantom), referring to their camouflage abilities.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæz.mɪd / (AmE), / ˈfaz.mɪd / (BrE)
A large, mostly tropical insect of the order Phasmatodea, with a cylindrical or flattened body and long, strong legs, often resembling sticks or leaves; includes walking sticks and leaf insects.
insect of the order Phasmatodea (walking sticks, leaf insects)

Examples

Some phasmids can remain motionless for hours to avoid being detected by predators.
The phasmid on the branch was so well camouflaged that it was almost invisible.
Many phasmids are herbivorous and feed on the leaves of trees and shrubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walking stick - insect of the family Phasmatidae, known for its stick-like appearance
  • Synonym: leaf insect - insect of the family Phylliidae, resembles a leaf

Etymology

From modern Latin Phasmida, from Greek 'phasma' (apparition, phantom) due to their camouflage.
