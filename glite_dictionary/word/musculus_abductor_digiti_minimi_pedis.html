musculus abductor digiti minimi pedis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskjələs æbˈdʌktər ˈdɪdʒaɪ ˈmɪnɪmaɪ ˈpɛdɪs / (AmE), / ˈmʌskjʊlʊs æbˈdʌktə ˈdɪdʒɪtʌɪ ˈmɪnɪmaɪ ˈpɛdɪs / (BrE)
a muscle of the foot that abducts the little toe away from the other toes
muscle that abducts the little toe
medical

Examples

The musculus abductor digiti minimi pedis is crucial in allowing the little toe to move outward from the rest of the foot.
Injuries to the musculus abductor digiti minimi pedis may cause pain on the outer side of the foot.
Strengthening the musculus abductor digiti minimi pedis can help improve balance and foot stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abductor digiti minimi - muscle that abducts the little toe or little finger

Etymology

From Latin: musculus (muscle), abductor (one that leads away), digiti minimi (of the little finger/toe), pedis (of the foot).
