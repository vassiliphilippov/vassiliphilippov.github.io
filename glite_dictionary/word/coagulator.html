coagulator

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈæɡjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / kəʊˈæɡjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
A substance or device that causes or produces coagulation, especially in a liquid.
agent that causes coagulation

Examples

The coagulator is used in cheese-making to help curdle the milk and separate the whey.
Blood banks use a chemical coagulator to ensure that stored samples do not bleed uncontrollably.
The surgeon employed an electrical coagulator during the operation to stop excessive bleeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coagulant - a substance that causes coagulation

Etymology

From Latin 'coagulare' (to curdle, to clot) with English agent noun suffix '-or'.
