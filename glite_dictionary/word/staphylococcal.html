staphylococcal

1. ADJ

/ stæfɨlɵˈkɑkəl / (AmE), / stæfɪlɵˈkɒkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or caused by staphylococcus bacteria.
related to staphylococcus bacteria
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a staphylococcal infection after surgery.
Doctors are concerned about the rise in staphylococcal antibiotic resistance in hospitals.
A staphylococcal rash often appears as red, swollen, and sometimes pus-filled bumps.

Synset Examples

a staphylococcal infection

Related Terms

  • Antonym: streptococcal - relating to streptococcus bacteria

Etymology

From modern Latin Staphylococcus, from Greek 'staphyle' (a bunch of grapes) + 'kokkos' (berry), referring to the shape of the bacteria, with English adjectival suffix '-al'.
