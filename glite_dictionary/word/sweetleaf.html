sweetleaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈswitˌlif / (AmE), / ˈswiːtˌliːf / (BrE)
A small yellowwood tree native to the southern United States, known for its small fragrant white flowers and leaves and bark that yield a yellow dye.
southern U.S. yellowwood tree with fragrant flowers

Examples

The sweetleaf is prized both for its beautiful blossoms and its use in natural dyes.
Botanists studied the sweetleaf to better understand its fragrant flowers and dye properties.
We found a grove of sweetleaf trees near the river in southern Georgia.
