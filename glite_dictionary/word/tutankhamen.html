Tutankhamen

1. PROPN

/ ˌtuːtənˈkɑːmən / (AmE), / ˌtjuːtənˈkɑːmən / (BrE)
An Egyptian pharaoh who reigned around 1358 BC, famous for his nearly intact tomb discovered in 1922.
Egyptian pharaoh around 1358 BC

Examples

The treasures found in Tutankhamen's tomb revealed much about ancient Egyptian society.
Scholars continue to study Tutankhamen's reign to understand its historical significance.
Howard Carter's discovery of Tutankhamen in 1922 was one of archaeology's greatest moments.

Etymology

From ancient Egyptian Djehutymose, Hellenized as Tutankhamen, meaning 'living image of Amun'.
