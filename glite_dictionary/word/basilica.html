basilica

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsɪlɪkə / (AmE), / bəˈsɪlɪkə / (BrE)
an ancient Roman building used for public administration, legal proceedings, and other civic functions
ancient Roman public building

Examples

The ancient Roman basilica served as a law court and meeting hall for citizens.
Much of the city's public life centered on the impressive basilica near the forum.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of a large basilica dating back to the first century B.C.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cathedral - a principal church, typically where a bishop's seat is located

Etymology

From Latin 'basilica', from Greek 'basilikē' (royal hall).

2. NOUN

/ bəˈsɪlɪkə / (AmE), / bəˈsɪlɪkə / (BrE)
A large, important church building, originally of early Christian or Roman design, or a Roman Catholic church given special ceremonial rights by the Pope.
large important church, often with special status

Examples

The ancient basilica attracted tourists from all over the world.
Saint Peter's basilica is one of the most famous churches in Rome.
The church was granted the status of basilica by the Pope.

Synset Examples

the church was raised to the rank of basilica

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cathedral - a principal church of a diocese, containing the bishop's seat

Etymology

From Latin basilica (public building), from Greek basilikē (royal hall).
