sidestep

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈsaɪd.stɛp / (BrE)
a step taken to one side, especially to avoid something or as part of a movement in activities such as boxing or dancing
a step taken to one side

Examples

The fighter made a quick sidestep to dodge the incoming punch.
She added a graceful sidestep to her dance routine.
With a swift sidestep, he avoided the puddle on the sidewalk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dodge - a sudden evasive movement; to avoid by quick movement
  • Antonym: advance - a forward movement

Etymology

Formed from 'side' + 'step', combining the ideas of lateral movement and stepping.

2. VERB

/ ˈsaɪdˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌstɛp / (BrE)
to avoid answering a question or dealing directly with a problem, duty, or issue, often deliberately
avoid answering or dealing with a problem

Examples

He tried to sidestep the reporter's difficult question during the interview.
Politicians often sidestep controversial topics to avoid offending voters.
When asked about the budget cut, the manager sidestepped the issue and spoke about future opportunities.

Synset Examples

He dodged the issue
she skirted the problem
They tend to evade their responsibilities
he evaded the questions skillfully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dodge - to avoid (something) in a clever or dishonest way
  • Synonym: to evade - to avoid or escape from (someone or something)
  • Antonym: address - to deal with or discuss something directly

Etymology

From noun 'side' + verb 'step'; originally referred to a lateral step to avoid something.

3. VERB

/ ˈsaɪdˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈsaɪd.stɛp / (BrE)
to avoid dealing with or answering something, especially a difficult question or issue, by ignoring or evading it
avoid dealing with something, often a question

Examples

When asked about the company's financial troubles, the CEO chose to sidestep the difficult question.
Politicians often sidestep difficult questions during interviews to avoid controversy.
Instead of giving a direct reply, she cleverly sidestepped the difficult question.

Synset Examples

She managed to sidestep the difficult questions effortlessly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evade - to avoid giving a direct answer or escape from a duty or question
  • Antonym: to confront - to face up to and deal with something, such as a problem or question

Etymology

From 'side' + 'step', originally referring to a physical movement to the side, later used figuratively for avoidance.
