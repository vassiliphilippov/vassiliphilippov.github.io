keratonosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛrətoʊˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɛrətəˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
Any abnormal condition affecting the outer layer of the skin (epidermis).
abnormal skin condition (epidermis)
medical

Examples

The dermatologist diagnosed her with keratonosis after examining the thickened patches on her arms.
Certain forms of keratonosis may require ongoing treatment to manage the symptoms.
Patients with chronic keratonosis are often advised to avoid excessive sun exposure.

Etymology

From Greek 'keras' (horn) and 'nosis' (disease).
