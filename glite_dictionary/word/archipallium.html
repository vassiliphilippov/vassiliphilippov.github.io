archipallium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrkɪˈpæliəm / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈpæliəm / (BrE)
the part of the cerebrum in the brain responsible for olfaction; also called the olfactory cortex.
olfactory cortex of the brain
medical

Examples

The archipallium is primarily associated with the sense of smell in vertebrates.
Evolutionarily, the archipallium represents one of the oldest regions of the brain.
Damage to the archipallium can impair olfactory processing in some animals.

Etymology

From Greek 'arkhi' (chief, primitive) + 'pallium' (cloak, mantle); refers to the phylogenetically oldest part of the cerebral cortex.
