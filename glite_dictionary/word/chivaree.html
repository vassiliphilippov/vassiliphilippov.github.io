chivaree

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪvəˈriː / (AmE), / ʃɪvəˈriː / (BrE)
A noisy celebration or mock serenade, typically involving the banging of pans and kettles, given to a newly married couple.
noisy mock serenade for newlyweds
archaic

Examples

The village gathered in front of the house to give the couple a loud chivaree after their wedding.
The newlyweds were surprised by a traditional chivaree late that night.
Our grandparents often told stories about participating in a chivaree with pots and pans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serenade - a musical performance in the open air, especially one for a lover

Etymology

From French dialect charivari (uproar, mock serenade).
