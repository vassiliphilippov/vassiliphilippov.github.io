to bacterise

1. VERB

/ ˈbæktəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈbæktəˌraɪz / (BrE)
to subject (a substance) to the action of bacteria, often to bring about decomposition or fermentation
subject to action of bacteria

Examples

The laboratory bacterised the sample to observe its decomposition process.
Certain foods are intentionally bacterised during the fermentation process to develop unique flavors.
She is researching new ways to bacterise agricultural waste for efficient recycling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferment - to undergo or cause to undergo fermentation

Etymology

From 'bacteria' + '-ise', following the pattern of similar verbs.
