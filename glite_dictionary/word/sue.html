to sue

1. VERB

/ suː / (AmE), / suː / (BrE)
to start legal proceedings against a person or organization, especially by bringing a case to court
start legal proceedings in court
legal

Examples

After the accident, she decided to sue the company for negligence.
The actor plans to sue several publications for defamation of character.
They are suing their landlord for failing to make necessary repairs.

Synset Examples

He was warned that the district attorney would process him
She actioned the company for discrimination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prosecute - to officially charge someone with a crime in a court of law
  • Antonym: to defend - to argue or act in support of someone in court against legal claims

Etymology

From Old French *suer* (to follow, pursue), from Latin *sequi* (to follow).
