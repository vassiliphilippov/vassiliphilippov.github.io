sub-interval

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌɪn.tɚ.vəl / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌɪn.tə.vəl / (BrE)
An interval that is entirely contained within another interval.
interval within another interval

Examples

In this proof, we consider each sub-interval separately to evaluate the function's behavior.
The real line can be partitioned into several sub-intervals for detailed analysis.
Choose a sub-interval of [0, 1] where the function is continuous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interval - a range between two values, especially in mathematics

Etymology

Formed from 'sub-' (under, below) and 'interval'.
