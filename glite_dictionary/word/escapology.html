escapology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛs.kəˈpɑ.lə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌɛs.kəˈpɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
the study and practice of escaping from physical restraints or confinement, often as a form of entertainment
study/practice of escaping confinement

Examples

The art of escapology requires years of training and practice to master.
Famous magicians have used escapology in their performances to thrill audiences.
She gave a lecture on the history and techniques of escapology at the local theater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape artist - a person skilled in escaping from restraints, often as entertainment

Etymology

From escape + -ology, modeled on similar scientific word formations.
