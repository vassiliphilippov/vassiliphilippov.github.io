Catherine II

1. PROPN

/ ˌkæθərˈin ðə ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˌkæθərˈiːn ðə ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
(1729–1796) Empress of Russia, known as Catherine the Great, who expanded and modernized the Russian Empire during her reign.
empress of Russia (1729–1796)

Examples

Catherine II implemented reforms that greatly advanced Russian society and culture.
Historians debate the political strategies that made Catherine II a powerful female ruler.
The reign of Catherine II is often called Russia's Golden Age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Catherine the Great - another name for Catherine II of Russia
