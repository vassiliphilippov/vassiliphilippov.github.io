booming

1. ADJ

/ ˈbuːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuːmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) deep and resonant; loud and echoing.
deep, resonant, and loud (voice or sound)

Examples

His booming voice could be heard across the crowded hall.
The booming laughter filled the empty auditorium.
A booming sound echoed from the distant cliffs as thunder rumbled overhead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonant - (of a sound) deep, clear, and continuing to sound or ring
  • Antonym: muffled - (of a sound) not loud because of being obstructed in some way

2. ADJ

/ ˈbuːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuːmɪŋ / (BrE)
Undergoing rapid growth, success, or profitability; very lively and expanding quickly.
growing rapidly and successfully

Examples

The tech sector is currently booming thanks to increased investment and innovation.
Tourism in the city was booming throughout the summer months.
They moved to a booming town where new businesses were opening every week.

Synset Examples

flourishing businesses
a palmy time for stockbrokers

doing a roaring trade
a thriving tourist center

the burgeoning administration
the burgeoning population

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flourishing - growing or developing successfully
  • Synonym: thriving - growing or developing vigorously
  • Antonym: declining - becoming less successful or weaker

Etymology

From the verb 'boom', meaning 'to prosper or flourish', originally imitative of a deep resonant sound.
