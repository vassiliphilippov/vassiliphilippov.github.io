discombobulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪskəmˌbɑbəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəmˌbɒbjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a state of confusion or disorientation, often accompanied by embarrassment
confused or disoriented state

Examples

After getting lost in the city, I felt a sudden discombobulation that made it hard to focus.
His unexpected question caused momentary discombobulation among the interviewees.
There was a palpable sense of discombobulation in the room after the strange announcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - a feeling of being bewildered or unclear
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear, in particular

Etymology

From American English 'discombobulate', a humorous formation possibly based on 'discompose' or 'confound'.
