Triticum dicoccum dicoccoides

1. NOUN

/ ˌtraɪtɪkəm daɪˈkɒkəm daɪˌkɒkɔɪˈdiːz / (AmE), / ˌtrɪtɪkəm dɪˈkɒkəm dɪˌkɒkɔɪˈdiːz / (BrE)
a wild variety of emmer wheat native to the Middle East, considered the ancestor of cultivated wheat varieties
wild ancestor of cultivated wheat

Examples

Triticum dicoccum dicoccoides is thought to be the direct wild ancestor of modern wheat varieties.
Archaeobotanists discovered remains of Triticum dicoccum dicoccoides in ancient settlements in the Levant.
Research into Triticum dicoccum dicoccoides offers insights into early agricultural practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emmer - a type of ancient wheat, also known as farro, cultivated since ancient times

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Triticum' (wheat), species 'dicoccum' (two-grained), with 'dicoccoides' indicating resemblance to dicoccum.
