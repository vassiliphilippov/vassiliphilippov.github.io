to discontinue

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.kənˈtɪn.ju / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.kənˈtɪn.juː / (BrE)
to come to or be at an end; to cease or stop happening or existing
cease; stop happening or existing

Examples

The medication will discontinue if your symptoms improve.
Funding for the program will discontinue by the end of the year.
Their weekly meetings are set to discontinue after this month.

Synset Examples

the support from our sponsoring agency will discontinue after March 31

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cease - to come or bring to an end; to stop
  • Synonym: stop - to cease to happen or exist
  • Antonym: to continue - to keep happening or existing without stopping

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'descontinuer', from Late Latin 'discontinuare'.

2. VERB

/ ˌdɪskənˈtɪn.ju / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈtɪn.juː / (BrE)
to stop doing or providing something; to end a state, activity, or service
stop doing or providing something

Examples

The company decided to discontinue their line of sports shoes due to low sales.
You should not discontinue your medication without consulting your doctor.
They announced plans to discontinue the bus service after next month.

Synset Examples

Quit teasing your little brother

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to continue - to keep happening, existing, or doing something without stopping
  • Synonym: to cease - to stop doing something
  • Synonym: to suspend - to temporarily stop something from continuing

Etymology

From Late Latin 'discontinuare' (to interrupt or break off), from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'continuare' (to join together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02686624-v
Synset POS: v
