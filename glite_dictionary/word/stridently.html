stridently

1. ADV

/ ˈstraɪdəntli / (AmE), / ˈstraɪdəntli / (BrE)
In a way that is loud, harsh, and unpleasant to listen to; in a forcefully assertive or strident manner.
in a loud and harsh manner

Examples

The school bell rang stridently, startling everyone in the hallway.
She spoke stridently against the new policy during the meeting.
The alarm sounded stridently, demanding immediate attention.

Synset Examples

the cheap clock ticked stridently

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harshly - in a harsh or severe manner

Etymology

Formed from 'strident' + '-ly'. 'Strident' from Latin 'strident-', present participle of 'stridere' (to make a harsh sound).
