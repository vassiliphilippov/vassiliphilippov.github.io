versification

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɜrsəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɜːsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the arrangement of words and lines in poetry according to metrical and rhythmic patterns; the art or practice of composing verse.
metrical composition of poetry

Examples

The poet's skillful versification gave the poem a musical quality.
Classical versification often requires strict adherence to specific metrical patterns.
Her unique approach to versification set her poetry apart from that of her contemporaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosody - the patterns of rhythm and sound used in poetry

Etymology

From Latin versificatio (the making of verses), from versificare (to compose verses).

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɜrsəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɜːsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the adaptation of a text, especially prose, into metrical or verse form; the act or result of arranging words according to the rules of meter in poetry
metrical adaptation of text

Examples

The versification of Shakespeare's plays into poetic format is a challenging task.
Her versification of the old legend breathed new life into the story.
The professor discussed the versification of biblical passages during the Renaissance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paraphrase - a restatement of a text in different words, often to clarify meaning

Etymology

From Latin 'versificatio', from 'versificare' (to compose verse).

3. NOUN

/ ˌvɝːsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɜːsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the art or practice of composing verse, especially with regard to metrical structure and poetic technique
the art of writing verse

Examples

Her versification displayed a deep understanding of classical poetic forms.
The professor gave a lecture on the history of versification in English literature.
His success as a poet was largely due to his skillful versification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poetry - literary work in which special intensity is given to the expression of feelings and ideas by the use of distinctive style and rhythm
  • Antonym: prose - written or spoken language in its ordinary form, without metrical structure

Etymology

From Latin versificatio (the making of verse), from versificare (to make verses).
