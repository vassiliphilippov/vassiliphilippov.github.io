cacuminal

1. ADJ

/ kəˈkjuːmɪnəl / (AmE), / kəˈkjuːmɪnəl / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) pronounced with the tip of the tongue turned back toward the hard palate.
pronounced with tongue tip turned back

Examples

In some Indian languages, the cacuminal consonants are distinctly different from other retroflex sounds.
The linguist explained how a cacuminal articulation changes the quality of the spoken consonant.
Students had to practice producing cacuminal sounds as part of the phonetics course.

Etymology

From Latin cacūmen (peak, point, top).
