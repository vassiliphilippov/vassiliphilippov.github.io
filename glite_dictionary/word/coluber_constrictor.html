Coluber constrictor

1. NOUN

/ kəˈluːbɚ kənˈstrɪktɚ / (AmE), / kəˈluːbə kənˈstrɪktə / (BrE)
A species of large, slender, non-venomous snake native to the eastern United States, commonly known as the black racer.
species of non-venomous snake (black racer)

Examples

The Coluber constrictor can move rapidly through fields and forests to hunt its prey.
Wildlife enthusiasts often spot the Coluber constrictor basking in the sun on rocks or logs.
Unlike venomous snakes, the Coluber constrictor subdues its food by quickly overpowering it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black racer - common name for Coluber constrictor, a non-venomous snake

Etymology

From Latin: Coluber meaning 'snake' and constrictor meaning 'constrictor' or 'one that constricts'.
