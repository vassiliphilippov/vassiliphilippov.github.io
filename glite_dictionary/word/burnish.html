burnish

1. VERB

/ ˈbɝː.nɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɜː.nɪʃ / (BrE)
to polish a surface by rubbing, especially to make it shiny
to polish and make shiny

Examples

After hours of work, she managed to burnish the silver candlestick until it gleamed.
The craftsman would carefully burnish the wooden table to achieve a glassy finish.
He used a soft cloth to burnish his shoes before the interview.

Synset Examples

buff the wooden floors
buff my shoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polish - to make the surface of something smooth and shiny by rubbing it
  • Antonym: tarnish - to make or become less bright or shiny

Etymology

From Old French burnir, from Frankish *brunjan (to make brown, polish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɜrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnɪʃ / (BrE)
a smooth and shiny quality, especially on a surface as a result of polishing or rubbing
smooth, shiny surface quality

Examples

The marble floor had lost its burnish after years of heavy use.
A fresh burnish on the silverware gave it a mirror-like gleam.
The artist admired the burnish of the finished bronze sculpture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheen - a soft, smooth shine on a surface
  • Antonym: matte - a surface without shine or gloss

Etymology

From Old French *burnir* (to polish, shine), from Frankish *brunjan* (to make brown, polish).

Synset POS: n
