monsoon

1. NOUN

/ mɑnˈsun / (AmE), / mɒnˈsuːn / (BrE)
A seasonal wind in southern Asia that reverses direction at different times of the year, often bringing heavy rains.
seasonal reversing wind, often with rain

Examples

The monsoon typically brings months of heavy rain to the region every year.
Sailors had to plan their journeys according to the direction of the monsoon winds.
During the winter monsoon, the wind changes direction and brings dry weather.

Etymology

From Arabic mausim (season), via Portuguese monção.

2. NOUN

/ mɑnˈsun / (AmE), / mɒnˈsuːn / (BrE)
A seasonal prevailing wind in southern Asia, which blows from the southwest in summer and usually brings heavy rain, and from the northeast in winter.
seasonal wind in southern Asia

Examples

The monsoon brought heavy rains that flooded the entire village.
Farmers eagerly await the arrival of the monsoon to begin planting their crops.
After a long dry spell, the monsoon finally arrived, cooling the air and reviving the land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rainy season - the period of heavy rain associated with the monsoon

Etymology

From Portuguese 'monção', from Arabic 'mawsim' (season).

3. NOUN

/ mɑnˈsun / (AmE), / mɒnˈsuːn / (BrE)
A seasonal wind in southern Asia that brings heavy rains, especially during the rainy season when the southwestern wind blows.
seasonal wind bringing heavy rain

Examples

The arrival of the monsoon every June transforms the landscape with lush greenery.
Farmers depend on the monsoon to irrigate their crops each year.
Heavy flooding was reported after the start of the monsoon season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rainy season - the period of the year characterized by heavy rainfall

Etymology

From Portuguese *monção*, from Arabic *mawsim* (season).

4. NOUN

/ mɑnˈsun / (AmE), / mɒnˈsuːn / (BrE)
a season when heavy rains fall in hot Asian countries or the wind that brings these rains, occurring especially in South and Southeast Asia
season of heavy rain (esp. Asia)

Examples

Every year, farmers rely on the monsoon to irrigate their crops.
Travel can be difficult during the monsoon due to flooding and landslides.
The city prepared emergency shelters ahead of the approaching monsoon.

Synset Examples

They planned their trip around the monsoon to avoid flooding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rainy season - the time of year when most annual rainfall occurs

Etymology

From Arabic 'mawsim' (season), through Portuguese 'monção'.
