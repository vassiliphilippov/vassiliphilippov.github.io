Fouquieriaceae

1. NOUN

/ fuːˌkiːriːˈeɪsiːˌaɪ / (AmE), / fuːˌkwɪəriːˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a small family of spiny shrubs or trees native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, including the ocotillo and related plants
family of spiny shrubs or trees

Examples

The Fouquieriaceae are known for their drought-resistant adaptations in desert environments.
Many species in the Fouquieriaceae family have striking red flowers that attract hummingbirds.
Researchers studied the distribution of Fouquieriaceae across arid regions of North America.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after French physician and botanist Pierre Fouquier (1776–1850), with -aceae forming family names in botanical Latin.
