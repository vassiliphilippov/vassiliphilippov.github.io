mannerism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmænərɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a habitual or characteristic way of behaving, speaking, or moving that is distinctive to an individual
distinctive personal habit or behavior

Examples

She had a nervous mannerism of tapping her fingers on the table.
His unique mannerism made him instantly recognizable to his fans.
People often imitate the mannerisms of their favorite celebrities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habit - a regular tendency or practice, especially one that is hard to give up
  • Synonym: idiosyncrasy - a characteristic, habit, or mannerism that is peculiar to an individual

Etymology

From Italian *manierismo*, from *maniera* (manner).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmænəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmænərɪzəm / (BrE)
a deliberate or exaggerated way of behaving, speaking, or performing, especially one intended to impress or conceal one's true feelings
deliberate or exaggerated behavior

Examples

Her constant use of grand gestures was merely a mannerism to impress her audience.
He adopted a theatrical mannerism whenever he spoke at public events.
The actor's exaggerated mannerism in the role made the character seem less believable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affectation - an artificial or pretentious behavior or speech

Etymology

From Italian *manierismo* (excessive use of style or manner), from *maniera* (manner).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00757016-n
Synset POS: n
