collateral

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / kəˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
Additional but secondary; serving to support or reinforce something else.
additional, secondary; auxiliary

Examples

The investigation revealed a number of collateral issues that needed attention.
In some medical cases, collateral blood vessels can provide an alternative pathway for circulation.
The project offered several collateral benefits beyond its initial goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxiliary - providing additional help or support; supplementary
  • Antonym: primary - main; of chief importance

Etymology

From Medieval Latin collateral 'side by side,' from Latin col- (with, together) + lateralis (lateral, of the side).

2. ADJ

/ kəˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / kəˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
Situated or running side by side; parallel in position but not directly connected.
side by side; parallel in position

Examples

The surgeon carefully avoided the collateral nerves during the procedure.
Several collateral channels allow water to flow beside the main river.
You can see collateral ridges running alongside the main mountain chain.

Synset Examples

collateral ridges of mountains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parallel - extending in the same direction and at a constant distance apart; not meeting

Etymology

Occurring as a secondary or accompanying result, especially in addition to something more important or direct.
accompanying or secondary result

Examples

The new policy had several collateral effects that were not anticipated by lawmakers.
Strict security measures were implemented, with some collateral inconvenience to regular employees.

5. ADJ

/ kəˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / kəˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
(of a person or relative) descended from the same ancestor but through a different line of descent, such as cousins or siblings rather than direct descendants.
related by descent from a common ancestor via a different line

Examples

The inheritance passed to a collateral branch of the family after the lineal heirs died out.
Aunt Clara is a collateral relative rather than a direct ancestor.
In matters of succession, collateral kin often play an important role when there are no direct descendants.

Synset Examples

cousins are collateral relatives
an indirect descendant of the Stuarts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lineal - being in the direct line of descent from an ancestor

Etymology

From Medieval Latin collateral(is), from Latin col- (together with) + lateralis (lateral, to the side).

6. NOUN

/ kəˈlæt̬ɚəl / (AmE), / kəˈlatərəl / (BrE)
property or other asset pledged as security for the repayment of a loan, to be forfeited in the event of a default.
security for a loan

Examples

The bank required him to put up his house as collateral for the loan.
She used her company shares as collateral to secure additional funding.
Without sufficient collateral, the lender will not approve your application.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: security - property or assets pledged to guarantee repayment of a loan

Etymology

From Medieval Latin collateralis (accompanying), from Latin com- (together) + lateralis (of the side).

Synset POS: n
