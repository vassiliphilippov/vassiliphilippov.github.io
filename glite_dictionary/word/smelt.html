smelt

1. NOUN

/ smɛlt / (AmE), / smɛlt / (BrE)
a small silvery fish found in cold waters, known for migrating between salt and fresh water
a small migratory silvery fish

Examples

Every spring, millions of smelt travel upstream to spawn in freshwater rivers.
Fishermen gathered at the icy riverbank, hoping for a good catch of smelt.
Grilled smelt is considered a delicacy in many northern regions.

Etymology

From Middle English smelte, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old Norse smjolti (a kind of fish).

2. NOUN

/ smɛlt / (AmE), / smɛlt / (BrE)
a small, silvery marine or freshwater fish of northern waters, related to the trout and valued as food
small silvery fish of northern waters

Examples

Every spring, local fishermen catch smelt in large numbers from the river.
The chef prepared a delicious dish featuring grilled smelt and seasonal vegetables.
During the festival, children enjoyed watching the nets fill with shimmering smelt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trout - a common freshwater fish related to smelt

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *smelt* or Low German *smelt*, referring to the fish.

3. VERB

/ smɛlt / (AmE), / smɛlt / (BrE)
to extract (a metal) from its ore by heating and melting
extract metal from ore by heating

Examples

The factory used ancient techniques to smelt copper from mined ore.
Ironworkers smelt the metal at extremely high temperatures to remove impurities.
Early civilizations learned to smelt various metals, which revolutionized their technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melt - to change from solid to liquid by heating

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'smelten', meaning to melt or fuse, related to the process of melting ore for extraction.
