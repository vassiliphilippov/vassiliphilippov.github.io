goggles

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑɡəlz / (AmE), / ˈɡɒɡlz / (BrE)
Tight-fitting spectacles worn to protect the eyes from dust, water, chemicals, or injury.
protective eyewear

Examples

The chemist wore goggles to protect her eyes while mixing the chemicals.
Swimmers often use goggles to see clearly underwater.
Welding without suitable goggles can be very dangerous for your eyesight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectacles - glasses for aiding vision

Etymology

From 'goggle', meaning 'to stare', influenced by the sense of eye coverings.
