Tringa

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪŋɡə / (AmE), / ˈtrɪŋɡə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Scolopacidae, including the shanks and tattlers.
genus of birds (shanks and tattlers)

Examples

The Tringa genus includes several species of waders found worldwide.
Many birdwatchers are keen to observe members of the Tringa genus during migration.
Some species within the Tringa genus are known for their distinctive calls.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, originating from the Ancient Greek 'trungas', a kind of wading bird.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪŋɡə / (AmE), / ˈtrɪŋɡə / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds in the family Scolopacidae, which includes the shanks and the tattlers.
a genus of wading birds

Examples

Several species within the genus Tringa migrate thousands of kilometers every year.
The common greenshank is classified under Tringa due to its distinctive features.
Ornithologists often study members of Tringa to understand wader behavior.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Tringa, from Ancient Greek tringa (a type of wading bird described by Aristotle).
