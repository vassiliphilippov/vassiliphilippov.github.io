to unkennel

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈkɛnəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɛnəl / (BrE)
to release (an animal) from a kennel or confinement
release from a kennel or confinement

Examples

Every morning, I unkennel the dogs so they can go outside and exercise.
The shelter staff unkenneled the puppies before mealtime.
Please remember to unkennel your pets as soon as you arrive at home.

Synset Examples

It's time to unkennel the puppies and let them play.
When we unkenneled the cat, it immediately ran for the window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kennel - a small shelter for a dog or other domestic animal

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'kennel,' meaning to take out of a kennel.
