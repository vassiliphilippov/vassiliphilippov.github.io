perigonal

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛrɪˈɡoʊnəl / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈɡəʊnəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a perigone, especially in flowers without a differentiated calyx and corolla
relating to a perigone (botany)

Examples

The scientist studied the perigonal structures of various lily species.
The flower's perigonal tube was particularly conspicuous under magnification.
Certain monocots have perigonal leaves that are not differentiated into sepals and petals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perigone - the outer envelope or perianth of a flower, especially when sepals and petals are not distinguishable

Etymology

From neo-Latin 'perigonium' (perigone), from Greek 'peri-' (around) + 'gonos' (seed, offspring).
