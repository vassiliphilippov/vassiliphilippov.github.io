butterfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small, slippery, scaleless fish found in northern Atlantic coastal waters, valued as a food fish
small Atlantic food fish

Examples

The chef pan-fried the butterfish and served it with a lemon sauce.
Fishermen often catch butterfish along the northeastern coast during the summer months.
The butterfish is prized for its tender, mild-tasting flesh.

Examples

/ ˈbʌtərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A small marine fish with a short, flattened body and weak spines, commonly found in coastal waters.
small, flattened marine fish

Examples
