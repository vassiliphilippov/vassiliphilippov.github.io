reactionary

1. NOUN

/ riˈækʃəˌneri / (AmE), / riˈakʃənəri / (BrE)
A person who strongly opposes social or political progress or reform and favors a return to a previous state of affairs.
person opposing progress or reform

Examples

The new law was opposed by every major reactionary in parliament.
As a reactionary, he criticized all attempts to modernize the education system.
Some historians label the movement's leaders as reactionary forces resisting change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservative - person favoring traditional views and values
  • Antonym: progressive - person advocating progress, reform, or change

Etymology

From French 'réactionnaire', from 'réaction' (reaction).

2. ADJ

/ riˈækʃəˌneri / (AmE), / riˈækʃənəri / (BrE)
(of a person or their views) opposing political or social progress or reform; favoring a return to a previous state.
opposing political or social reform

Examples

His reactionary ideas were out of step with the changing times.
The government was criticized for its reactionary policies against social change.
Some members of the board held reactionary attitudes toward educational reform.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservative - favoring traditional views and values
  • Antonym: progressive - favoring social reform or new ideas

Etymology

From French réactionnaire, from réaction (reaction), after révolutionnaire (revolutionary).
