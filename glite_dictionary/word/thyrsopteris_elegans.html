Thyrsopteris elegans

1. NOUN

/ θɪrˈsɒptərɪs ɪˈlɛɡənz / (AmE), / θɜːˈsɒptərɪs ɪˈlɛɡənz / (BrE)
A species of terrestrial tree fern native to South America, known for its elegant fronds and unique appearance.
a South American tree fern species

Examples

The fronds of Thyrsopteris elegans are distinctive among South American ferns.
Botanists studied Thyrsopteris elegans to understand its adaptation to its environment.
In the botanical garden, Thyrsopteris elegans attracts visitors with its graceful appearance.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'thyrsos' (a staff tipped with ivy or pine cone) and 'pteris' (fern); species epithet 'elegans' is Latin for 'elegant'.
