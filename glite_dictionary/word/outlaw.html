outlaw

1. ADJ

/ ˈaʊt.lɔː / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.lɑː / (BrE)
Disobedient to or defiant of law; not observing legal restrictions or authority.
disobedient to or defiant of law

Examples

The region became a hideout for outlaw gangs escaping the authorities.
He was famous for his outlaw ways, never caring about rules or regulations.
During the wild west era, many towns struggled to control outlaw elements.

Synset Examples

lawless bands roaming the plains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lawless - not governed by or obedient to laws
  • Antonym: legal - conforming to the law

Etymology

From Old English 'ūtlaga', influenced by Old Norse 'útlagi' (outlawed person).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01399338-s
Synset POS: s

2. VERB

/ ˈaʊtˌlɔ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌlɔː / (BrE)
to make something illegal by law; to ban officially
to declare illegal by law

Examples

The government decided to outlaw the use of certain toxic pesticides.
Many countries have outlawed discrimination based on race or gender.
Environmental activists are calling for the city to outlaw single-use plastics.

Synset Examples

Marijuana is criminalized in the U.S.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to legalize - to make something legal
  • Synonym: ban - to forbid or prohibit by law or authority

Etymology

From Old English 'ūtlaga', influenced by Scandinavian languages, originally meaning 'outlawed person'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02486512-v
Synset POS: v

3. ADJ

/ ˈaʊtˌlɑʊ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.lɑː / (BrE)
(of an action or activity) contrary to or forbidden by law; illegal.
forbidden by law; illegal

Examples

The workers declared an outlaw strike to protest new regulations.
Any outlaw activity at the site will be prosecuted immediately.
The government cracked down on the outlaw sale of endangered animals.

Synset Examples

an illegitimate seizure of power
illicit trade
an outlaw strike
unlawful measures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegal - forbidden by law; not legal
  • Antonym: lawful - permitted by law; legal

Etymology

From Old English *ūtlagu* ('out of the law'), via Old Norse *útlagi* ('outlawed person').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01406764-s
Synset POS: s

4. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌlɔ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.lɔː / (BrE)
a person who has committed a crime or has been legally declared outside the protection of the law
person declared a criminal

Examples

The notorious outlaw was finally captured after years of eluding the authorities.
In the past, an outlaw could be hunted without legal consequence.
Legends of the Wild West often feature a heroic outlaw fighting for justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminal - a person who has committed a crime
  • Antonym: law-abiding citizen - a person who obeys the law

Etymology

From Old Norse *útlagi* (outlawed person), from *ut* (out) + *log* (law).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09997190-n
Synset POS: n
