manometer

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑmətər / (AmE), / məˈnɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used for measuring and comparing the pressure of gases or liquids
instrument for measuring pressure
medical

Examples

The scientist used a manometer to measure the pressure difference inside the chamber.
A mercury manometer is commonly used in laboratory experiments.
Before starting the reaction, the technician checked the manometer for any fluctuations in gas pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barometer - instrument measuring atmospheric pressure

Etymology

From Greek 'manos' (thin, sparse) + 'metron' (measure).
