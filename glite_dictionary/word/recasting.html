recasting

1. NOUN

/ ˌriˈkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of expressing something differently, especially by altering the form or wording of a statement or phrase
expressing something in a different form

Examples

The editor suggested a recasting of the sentence for greater clarity.
During the meeting, he offered a recasting of her proposal to make it more concise.
Teachers often use recasting to help students understand grammatical structures better.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paraphrasing - rewording something to achieve the same meaning

Etymology

From 'recast' (verb: to cast again or differently) + '-ing'.
