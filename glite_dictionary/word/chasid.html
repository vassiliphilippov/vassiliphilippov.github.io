Chasid

1. NOUN

/ xɑˈsid / (AmE), / xaˈsid / (BrE)
a member of a Jewish sect that strictly observes Orthodox Judaism, especially as part of Hasidic tradition
member of Hasidic Jewish sect

Examples

At the festival, a Chasid led the prayers before the crowd.
He spoke with a Chasid about the traditions of Orthodox Judaism.
Several young Chasidim gathered in the synagogue to celebrate the holiday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hasid - alternate spelling for Chasid

Etymology

From Hebrew ḥasīd (pious, devout person).
