sorption

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔːrʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɔːpʃən / (BrE)
the process by which one substance takes up or holds another, either by absorption or adsorption
uptake of one substance by another

Examples

The efficiency of the filter depends on the sorption properties of the material.
Scientists studied the sorption of contaminants by different types of soil.
The process of sorption plays a crucial role in removing pollutants from water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorption - the process by which one substance is taken into and distributed throughout another
  • Synonym: adsorption - the process in which a substance adheres to the surface of another

Etymology

From Latin 'sorbere' (to suck in), modeled after 'absorption' and 'adsorption'.
