Stanleya pinnata

1. NOUN

/ stænˈliːə pɪˈneɪtə / (AmE), / stanˈliːə pɪˈneɪtə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to the southwestern United States, with blue-green, deeply divided leaves and tall spikes of yellow flowers.
perennial plant of the southwestern US

Examples

The Stanleya pinnata is admired for its showy yellow plumes that brighten arid landscapes.
Botanists observed that Stanleya pinnata thrives in alkaline soils of the desert.
Gardeners often cultivate Stanleya pinnata for its drought resistance and striking appearance.

Etymology

Named after Edward Stanley, a British naturalist; 'pinnata' means 'feathered' or 'pinnate' in Latin, describing the leaves.
