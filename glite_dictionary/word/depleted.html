depleted

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈplitɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈpliːtɪd / (BrE)
no longer having enough of the necessary or desired amount; reduced in quantity, power, or value
reduced or diminished in quantity

Examples

After months of drought, the village's water supply was depleted and needed urgent replenishment.
His energy was depleted after running the marathon in the intense heat.
The depleted inventory meant the store was unable to meet customers' demands.

Synset Examples

supplies are low
our funds are depleted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhausted - completely used up or drained of resources
  • Antonym: replenished - restored or refilled to a previous level

Etymology

From the verb 'deplete', from Latin 'deplētus', past participle of 'deplēre' (to empty).
