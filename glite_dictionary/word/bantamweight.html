bantamweight

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæn.təmˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈbæn.təm.weɪt / (BrE)
A weight division in boxing and some other combat sports, for competitors heavier than flyweight but lighter than featherweight.
weight class in combat sports

Examples

He won the bantamweight title after a close match last night.
Several promising fighters have moved up from bantamweight to featherweight this season.
The bantamweight division is known for its speed and agility.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: flyweight - a lighter weight class below bantamweight
  • Degree Higher: featherweight - the weight class above bantamweight

Etymology

From 'bantam', a small breed of chicken, and 'weight', reflecting the lightness of the class.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæn.təmˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈbæn.təmˌweɪt / (BrE)
In boxing and other combat sports, a competitor in a weight class typically with an upper limit of 118 or 119 pounds (54 kg), depending on the organization's rules.
a boxer of the bantamweight class

Examples

The bantamweight champion defended his title in a thrilling match last night.
She started her career as a bantamweight before moving up a division.
Many young boxers aim to compete as bantamweights due to the intense competition in that class.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: featherweight - a slightly heavier weight class above bantamweight in boxing
  • Degree Lower: flyweight - a lighter weight class below bantamweight in boxing

Etymology

From 'bantam,' a small breed of chicken, and 'weight,' referring to the class based on competitors' weight.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbæn.təmˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈbæn.təm.weɪt / (BrE)
a weight class in boxing and some other combat sports for competitors weighing generally between 115 and 118 pounds (or, in some contexts, up to 126 pounds)
a boxer in a specific light weight class

Examples

He won the national title as a bantamweight in 2019.
The bantamweight fighters displayed incredible speed and agility in the ring.
She moved up from flyweight to compete as a bantamweight this season.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: featherweight - weight class just above bantamweight in boxing
  • Degree Lower: flyweight - weight class just below bantamweight in boxing

Etymology

Named after the bantam, a small breed of chicken, + 'weight', first used in reference to lighter boxers in the late 19th century.
