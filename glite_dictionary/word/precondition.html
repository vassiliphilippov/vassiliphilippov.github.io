precondition

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriː.kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌpriː.kənˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
an assumption or condition that must be met before something else can occur or be valid
necessary prior condition or assumption

Examples

The agreement set the withdrawal of troops as a precondition for holding further talks.
Adequate funding is a basic precondition for the success of this project.
Trust is often a precondition for effective teamwork in any organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condition - a state that must exist for something else to happen
  • Synonym: requirement - something that is needed or demanded
  • Antonym: consequence - something that follows as a result

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + condition (from Latin conditio).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpriːkənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
an assumption or condition that must be true or fulfilled before something else can occur or be done
a necessary prior condition or assumption

Examples

One precondition for entry is that you must be over 18 years old.
Trust is often a precondition for successful teamwork in organizations.
Meeting the deadline was a precondition for receiving the grant funding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condition - a situation that must exist before something else happens
  • Synonym: assumption - something accepted as true without proof

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'pre-' (before) + 'condition' (a state required for something else).

3. NOUN

/ ˌpriːkənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a condition that must be fulfilled before something else can happen or be done
a prerequisite condition

Examples

A good education is often a precondition for achieving success in many careers.
He set honesty as a precondition for continuing the negotiation.
Registration is a precondition for attending the conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prerequisite - a thing that is required as a prior condition for something else to happen

Etymology

From Latin 'prae-' (before) + 'condition'.

4. VERB

/ ˌpriːkənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
to prepare something in advance by putting it into a required condition
prepare in advance; set necessary condition

Examples

You should precondition the laboratory equipment before starting the experiment.
The software installation script will precondition the environment to meet all requirements.
They preconditioned the building to ensure it was safe for occupancy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prepare - to make ready beforehand for a specific purpose

Etymology

From pre- (before) + condition, modeled after the noun 'precondition'.
