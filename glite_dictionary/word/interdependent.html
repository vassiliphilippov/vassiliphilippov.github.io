interdependent

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərdɪˈpɛndənt / (AmE), / ˌɪntədɪˈpɛndənt / (BrE)
(of two or more things) depending on each other; mutually reliant.
mutually dependent

Examples

The economy and environment are interdependent, with each influencing the other's well-being.
The two countries have developed an interdependent relationship through years of trade and cooperation.
In a healthy ecosystem, plants and animals are interdependent, relying on each other to survive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: independent - not dependent on anything else
  • Degree Lower: dependent - relying on someone or something else

Etymology

From Latin 'inter-' (between, among) + 'dependent', from 'dependere' (to hang from).
