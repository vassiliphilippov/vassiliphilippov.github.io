Chrysobalanus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkraɪsəˈbælənəs / (AmE), / ˌkrɪsəʊˈbæɫənəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical evergreen shrubs or small trees, known as coco plums, valued for their edible fruit and ornamental use.
genus of tropical plants (coco plums)

Examples

Chrysobalanus is commonly found in coastal regions where it thrives in sandy soils.
The fruit of Chrysobalanus is edible and often used to make jams in the Caribbean.
Botanists identified the specimen as a member of the Chrysobalanus genus due to its distinctive leaves.

Etymology

From Greek chrysos (gold) + balanos (acorn), referring to the golden color of some fruits.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkraɪsoʊbəˈleɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌkrɪsəʊbəˈleɪnəs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical trees and shrubs in the family Chrysobalanaceae, commonly known as coco plums
genus of coco plum plants

Examples

The Chrysobalanus genus is native to tropical regions of the Americas and Africa.
Researchers have found medicinal compounds in the bark of Chrysobalanus species.
The fruit of Chrysobalanus is often called coco plum and is enjoyed both raw and cooked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coco plum - an edible fruit produced by plants of the genus Chrysobalanus

Etymology

From Greek *chrysos* (gold) + *balanos* (acorn); refers to the golden color of some fruits.
