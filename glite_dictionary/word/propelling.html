propelling

1. ADJ

/ prəˈpɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / prəˈpɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
Tending to move or drive something forward.
causing movement forward

Examples

The engineer described the propelling mechanism of the new engine in great detail.
A strong propelling force pushed the rocket into orbit.
The propelling current made it difficult for the swimmers to return to shore.

Synset Examples

propellant fuel for submarines
the faster a jet plane goes the greater its propulsive efficiency
universities … the seats of propulsive thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propulsive - having the power to move or push forward
  • Antonym: pulling - drawing or hauling toward oneself or itself

Etymology

From 'propel' + '-ing'. 'Propel' from Latin propellere (to drive forward).
