accommodative

1. ADJ

/ əˈkɑməˌdeɪtɪv / (AmE), / əˈkɒməˌdeɪtɪv / (BrE)
willing to adjust to the needs or wishes of others; helpful in creating a harmonious adaptation
willing to adapt or help

Examples

The manager took an accommodative stance to ensure everyone was satisfied with the schedule.
Her accommodative attitude made the transition much easier for the new employees.
Policies that are accommodative of different needs foster a more inclusive environment.

Synset Examples

the warden was always accommodating in allowing visitors in
made a special effort to be accommodating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accommodating - willing to help or do what someone else wants

Etymology

From accommodate + -ive; modeled after related words like 'accommodating'.

2. ADJ

/ əˈkɑːməˌdeɪtɪv / (AmE), / əˈkɒməˌdeɪtɪv / (BrE)
Willing or able to adapt to different conditions or bring about reconciliation; tending to bring people or ideas into harmony.
adaptable or reconciling in nature

Examples

The manager took an accommodative approach to resolving the team's conflict.
Central banks may adopt a more accommodative policy during periods of economic slowdown.
Her accommodative attitude made it easier for everyone to reach an agreement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexible - willing to change or adapt easily
  • Antonym: rigid - unwilling to change or adapt

Etymology

From accommodate + -ive, on the pattern of adjectives indicating capacity for action.

3. ADJ

/ əˈkɑːməˌdeɪtɪv / (AmE), / əˈkɒməˌdeɪtɪv / (BrE)
willing to adjust to differences or modify one’s position in order to reach agreement or compromise
willing to adjust or compromise

Examples

She is known for her accommodative approach during negotiations, always seeking a solution that works for everyone.
The manager's accommodative attitude helped to ease tensions within the team.
In international relations, an accommodative policy can prevent unnecessary conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexible - willing to change or adapt; easily adjustable
  • Antonym: inflexible - unwilling to change or adapt

Etymology

From accommodate + -ive; following the pattern of adjectives meaning 'tending to...'
