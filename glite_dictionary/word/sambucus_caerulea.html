Sambucus caerulea

1. NOUN

/ sæmˈbjuːkəs siːəˈruːliə / (AmE), / sæmˈbjuːkəs siːəˈruːlɪə / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree native to western North America, with white flowers and blue berries, known as blue elderberry
western North American shrub with blue berries

Examples

The Sambucus caerulea in our garden produces clusters of beautiful blue berries every summer.
Wildlife often feed on the fruit of Sambucus caerulea during the late summer months.
Many people collect berries from Sambucus caerulea to make jams and homemade wines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue elderberry - common name for Sambucus caerulea

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sambucus' (elder tree) and 'caerulea' (blue), referring to the blue coloration of the berries.
