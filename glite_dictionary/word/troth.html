troth

1. NOUN

/ troʊθ / (AmE), / trəʊθ / (BrE)
a mutual promise or pledge, especially one of faithfulness in marriage; truth or loyalty, especially as pledged in engagement
mutual promise to marry; pledged faithfulness
archaic

Examples

He gave her his troth before the wedding ceremony.
They plighted their troth beneath the ancient oak tree.
In olden times, to break one's troth was a serious matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betrothal - the act of becoming engaged to marry

Etymology

From Middle English *troth*, a variant of 'truth', from Old English *trēowþ* (faith, loyalty).

2. NOUN

/ troʊθ / (AmE), / trəʊθ / (BrE)
a solemn promise or pledge of loyalty or faithfulness, especially one made in marriage or betrothal
solemn pledge of fidelity
archaic

Examples

He gave her his troth before the assembled guests.
In ancient times, knights would swear troth to their lords.
She pledged her troth in a quiet and heartfelt ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pledge - a formal promise or undertaking
  • Synonym: oath - a solemn promise, often invoking a divine witness

Etymology

From Old English 'trēowþ' (faith, loyalty), related to 'truth'.
