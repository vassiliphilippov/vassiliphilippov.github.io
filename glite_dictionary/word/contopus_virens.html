Contopus virens

1. NOUN

/ kɒnˈtoʊpəs ˈvaɪrɛnz / (AmE), / kɒnˈtəʊpʊs ˈvɪərɛnz / (BrE)
a small, olive-colored woodland flycatcher native to eastern North America, commonly known as the Eastern Wood-Pewee
a North American woodland flycatcher (Eastern Wood-Pewee)

Examples

During migration, birdwatchers often hope to spot the elusive Contopus virens among the treetops.
The call of the Contopus virens is a familiar sound in eastern North American forests.
Researchers banded several Contopus virens to study their migratory patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eastern Wood-Pewee - common English name for Contopus virens

Etymology

From Latin; 'Contopus' combines Greek 'kontos' (pole) and 'pous' (foot), 'virens' means 'green' in Latin.
