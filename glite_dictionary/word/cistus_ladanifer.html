Cistus ladanifer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstəs ləˈdænɪfɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəs ləˈdanɪfə / (BrE)
A Mediterranean shrub with white flowers and sticky stems and leaves, which yield a fragrant oleoresin used in perfumes, especially as a fixative.
Mediterranean shrub yielding fragrant resin

Examples

Cistus ladanifer is cultivated in Mediterranean gardens for its attractive flowers and aromatic resin.
The oleoresin extracted from Cistus ladanifer is prized in the perfume industry for its rich, warm scent.
Botanists often study Cistus ladanifer for its ecological role in dry, sunny habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rockrose - A common name for shrubs of the genus Cistus

Etymology

From Latin; 'Cistus' from Greek 'kistos' (shrub), 'ladanifer' from Latin 'ladanum' (resin) and 'ferre' (to bear).
