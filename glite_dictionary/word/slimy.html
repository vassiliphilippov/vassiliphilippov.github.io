slimy

1. ADJ

/ ˈslaɪmi / (AmE), / ˈslaɪmi / (BrE)
covered with or resembling slime; having a wet, sticky, or slippery texture like slime
covered with or like slime

Examples

The rocks by the river were covered in a slimy layer of algae.
She recoiled at the slimy feel of the fish in her hands.
A slimy substance oozed from the bottom of the old bucket.

Synset Examples

a slimy substance covered the rocks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slippery - difficult to hold or stand on because it is smooth, wet, or slimy
  • Synonym: sticky - having the quality of adhering or sticking to a surface
  • Antonym: dry - free from moisture or liquid; not wet

Etymology

From Old English slīmig (slimy), from slīm (slime) + -y.

2. ADJ

/ ˈslaɪmi / (AmE), / ˈslaɪmi / (BrE)
(of a person or behavior) morally disgusting, dishonest, or repellent.
morally disgusting or repellent

Examples

Her slimy tactics in the office made her unpopular with her coworkers.
He played the role of a slimy villain who tricked everyone around him.
Nobody trusted the slimy salesman who twisted the truth to make a sale.

Synset Examples

would do something as despicable as murder
ugly crimes
the vile development of slavery appalled them
a slimy little liar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonest - not honest; likely to lie or cheat
  • Synonym: vile - extremely unpleasant or morally bad
  • Antonym: upstanding - honest and respectable in character
