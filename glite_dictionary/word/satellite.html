satellite

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæt̬.əl.aɪt / (AmE), / ˈsæt.əl.aɪt / (BrE)
A celestial body that orbits a planet or star, especially a natural one such as a moon.
celestial body orbiting a planet or star

Examples

Jupiter has many satellites that can be observed through a telescope.
The Earth's only natural satellite is the Moon.
Astronomers discovered a new satellite orbiting Saturn last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moon - The natural satellite of the Earth, or by extension, any natural satellite of a planet

Etymology

From Latin satellitēs (attendant or companion).

/ ˈsætəlˌaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsæt.əl.aɪt / (BrE)
to broadcast or transmit (a signal, program, etc.) by using an artificial satellite
broadcast via satellite

Examples

They plan to satellite the news feed to remote regions.
After recording the interview, the station satellited it to their affiliates worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcast - to transmit (a program or signal) by radio or television

Etymology

From the noun 'satellite', referring to artificial satellites used for communication.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsæt̬.əl.aɪt / (AmE), / ˈsæt.əl.aɪt / (BrE)
An artificial object placed in orbit around the Earth, the moon, or another celestial body, especially for communication or research purposes.
man-made object in orbit

Examples

The communication satellite was launched successfully into orbit last night.
Scientists use a weather satellite to monitor changes in the Earth's atmosphere.
A television signal is transmitted via satellite to homes around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probe - an unmanned exploratory spacecraft

Etymology

From Latin 'satelles' (attendant, companion), originally meaning a follower or escort.
