Hylocichla fuscescens

1. NOUN

/ haɪloʊˈsɪklə fʌsˈsɛsəns / (AmE), / hɪləʊˈsɪklə fʌsˈsɛsəns / (BrE)
A tawny brown North American thrush, commonly known as the wood thrush, noted for its distinctive song.
species of North American thrush

Examples

The Hylocichla fuscescens is easily recognized by its flutelike song in eastern woodlands.
Ornithologists studied the migration patterns of Hylocichla fuscescens across North America.
Spotting a Hylocichla fuscescens in the forest is a rare treat for birdwatchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood thrush - common name for Hylocichla fuscescens, a North American thrush
  • Degree Lower: Turdus migratorius - American robin, a related North American thrush

Etymology

From Ancient Greek hūlē (wood) + kikhle (thrush); fuscescens from Latin, meaning 'darkening'.
