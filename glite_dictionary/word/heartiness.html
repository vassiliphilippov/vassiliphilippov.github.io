heartiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːrtinəs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtinɪs / (BrE)
vigorous strength and enthusiasm, especially of body or mind
vigorous strength or enthusiasm

Examples

Her heartiness made everyone feel welcome during the long hike.
The heartiness of his laughter could be heard throughout the house.
Despite her age, she approached every task with remarkable heartiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigor - physical strength and good health
  • Antonym: languor - state of tiredness or inertia

Etymology

From 'hearty' + '-ness'; 'hearty' from Middle English 'herti', from 'heart'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑr.ti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhɑː.ti.nɪs / (BrE)
sincere enthusiasm, warmth, or wholeheartedness in manner or feeling.
sincere warmth or enthusiasm

Examples

She thanked him with heartiness in her voice, making him smile.
The heartiness of his laughter filled the whole room.
Despite the formality of the meeting, there was a surprising heartiness in everyone's greetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cordiality - warm and friendly quality
  • Antonym: insincerity - lack of sincerity or genuineness

Etymology

From 'hearty' (Middle English 'herti'), meaning sincere or warm, + '-ness' suffix indicating a state or quality.
