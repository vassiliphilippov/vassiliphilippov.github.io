meekly

1. ADV

/ ˈmikli / (AmE), / ˈmiːkli / (BrE)
In a quiet, gentle, and submissive manner; showing humility or a lack of self-assertion.
in a humble, submissive way

Examples

She meekly accepted the criticism without protest.
He meekly followed the instructions given to him by his teacher.
The dog meekly waited by the door, hoping to be let inside.

Synset Examples

he humbly lowered his head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humbly - in a way that shows modesty or humility
  • Antonym: proudly - in a way that shows pride or self-importance

Etymology

From 'meek' + '-ly', where 'meek' comes from Old Norse 'mjǫk' (gentle, mild).

2. ADV

/ ˈmikli / (AmE), / ˈmiːkli / (BrE)
in a submissive, gentle, or humble manner
in a submissive or gentle way

Examples

She spoke meekly, barely above a whisper, in front of the large audience.
He meekly accepted the criticism without protest.
The dog meekly followed its owner after being scolded.

Synset Examples

meekly bowed to his wishes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submissively - in a way that is ready to conform to the authority or will of others
  • Antonym: boldly - in a confident and courageous way; not meekly

Etymology

From 'meek' + '-ly', where 'meek' comes from Old Norse *mjūkr* (soft, gentle).
