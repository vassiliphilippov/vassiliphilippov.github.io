gynobase

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡaɪnəˌbeɪs / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪnəʊbeɪs / (BrE)
the enlarged part of a flower's receptacle that supports the pistil
enlarged floral base supporting pistil

Examples

In some members of the mustard family, the gynobase is clearly visible beneath the ovary.
The botanist described how the gynobase supports both carpels in the flower.
A prominent gynobase distinguishes certain flower species from closely related ones.

Etymology

From Greek 'gynē' (woman, female) and 'basis' (base).
