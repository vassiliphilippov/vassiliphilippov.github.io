amazed

1. ADJ

/ əˈmeɪzd / (AmE), / əˈmeɪzd / (BrE)
filled with sudden and great surprise or wonder; extremely surprised
greatly surprised or filled with wonder

Examples

She was amazed by the generosity of strangers who helped her during the storm.
The children looked amazed when the magician made the rabbit disappear.
I am still amazed that we finished the project ahead of schedule.

Synset Examples

an amazed audience gave the magician a standing ovation
I stood enthralled, astonished by the vastness and majesty of the cathedral
astounded viewers wept at the pictures from the Oklahoma City bombing
stood in stunned silence
stunned scientists found not one but at least three viruses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonished - very surprised or impressed; amazed
  • Synonym: stunned - so shocked or surprised that one is temporarily unable to react
  • Antonym: unimpressed - not feeling admiration, respect, or interest

Etymology

From the verb 'amaze,' originating in Middle English, from Old English 'amasian' (to confuse or bewilder).
