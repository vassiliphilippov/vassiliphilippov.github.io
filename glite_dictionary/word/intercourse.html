intercourse

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪntərˌkɔrs / (AmE), / ˈɪntəkɔːs / (BrE)
sexual activity involving the penetration of the penis into the vagina, typically culminating in orgasm; also used more broadly for sexual relations between individuals.
sexual activity involving penetration

Examples

The doctor explained that unprotected intercourse increases the risk of sexually transmitted infections.
They decided to abstain from intercourse until they were married.
Talking openly about intercourse can help promote healthy attitudes towards sex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copulation - the act of sexual union between two individuals
  • Synonym: coitus - sexual intercourse, especially the physical union between a man and a woman
  • Antonym: abstinence - the practice of restraining oneself from sexual activity

Etymology

From Latin 'intercursus' (a running between, intercourse), from 'intercursus', past participle of 'intercurrere' (to run between).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪntərkɔrs / (AmE), / ˈɪntəkɔːs / (BrE)
communication or dealings between individuals or groups, especially in a formal or social context
communication or dealings (formal/archaic)
archaic

Examples

Years ago, polite intercourse between neighbors was considered essential for a good community.
There was little intercourse between the rival factions outside of official meetings.
In academic circles, international intercourse has promoted the exchange of groundbreaking ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conversation - informal talk, especially about opinions or feelings
  • Synonym: dialogue - discussion between two or more people

Etymology

From Latin 'intercursus' (a running between), via Old French 'entrecours'.
