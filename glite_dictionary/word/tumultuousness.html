tumultuousness

1. NOUN

/ tuːˈmʌltʃuəsnəs / (AmE), / tjuːˈmʌltjʊəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being full of commotion, noise, or confusion; turbulence or disorder
state of commotion or disorder

Examples

The tumultuousness of the crowd made it hard to hear the announcement.
He was overwhelmed by the tumultuousness that erupted after the decision was announced.
Despite the tumultuousness of their surroundings, she remained remarkably focused.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turmoil - a state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty
  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being free from agitation or strong emotion

Etymology

From Latin tumultus (uproar, commotion) + English -ous + -ness.
