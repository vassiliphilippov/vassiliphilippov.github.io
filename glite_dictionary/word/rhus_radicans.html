Rhus radicans

1. NOUN

/ ɹʌs ˈrædɪkənz / (AmE), / ɹʌs ˈrædɪkənz / (BrE)
A climbing plant native to eastern and central North America, known for its three-part leaves and white berries, which produces an oil that causes skin irritation upon contact.
climbing plant causing skin rash (poison ivy)
medical

Examples

Rhus radicans is notorious for causing allergic reactions in people who brush against its leaves.
The white berries produced by Rhus radicans are a distinctive feature in its natural habitat.
Hikers are advised to learn how to identify Rhus radicans to avoid unpleasant skin rashes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison ivy - common name for Rhus radicans, a plant causing skin irritation

Etymology

From Latin 'rhus' (sumac) and 'radicans' (rooting), referring to the plant's growth habit.
