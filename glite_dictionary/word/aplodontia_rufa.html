Aplodontia rufa

1. NOUN

/ ˌæp.ləˈdɒn.ʃə ˈruː.fə / (AmE), / ˌæp.ləˈdɒn.tɪ.ə ˈruː.fə / (BrE)
a bulky, nocturnal, burrowing rodent found in upland areas along the Pacific coast of North America; the most primitive living rodent, also known as the mountain beaver
a burrowing rodent of the Pacific coast (mountain beaver)

Examples

The Aplodontia rufa is often called the mountain beaver, despite not being a true beaver.
Researchers studied the burrowing habits of Aplodontia rufa in the dense forests of Washington State.
Sightings of Aplodontia rufa are rare due to its nocturnal and secretive nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain beaver - another common name for Aplodontia rufa

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name 'Aplodontia' (meaning 'simple tooth') + species epithet 'rufa' (Latin for 'reddish').
