Brassica oleracea capitata

1. NOUN

/ brəˈsɪkə oʊˌlɛriˈeɪʃə kæˈpɪtə tə / (AmE), / brəˈsɪkə əʊˌlɛəriˈeɪʃə kəˈpɪtə tə / (BrE)
A cultivated plant of the cabbage family with a short thick stalk and a large, compact head of edible, usually green leaves; commonly known as cabbage.
cultivated cabbage plant species

Examples

The farmer grew rows of Brassica oleracea capitata to harvest fresh cabbage for the market.
Researchers study Brassica oleracea capitata for its high nutritional value and resistance to pests.
In the kitchen, Brassica oleracea capitata is often shredded and added to salads and coleslaws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage - a common edible leafy vegetable

Etymology

From modern Latin: genus name Brassica (cabbage) + species oleracea (vegetable) + varietal epithet capitata (headed).
