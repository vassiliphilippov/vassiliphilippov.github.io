periclase

1. NOUN

/ ˈper.əˌkleɪs / (AmE), / ˈper.ɪ.kleɪz / (BrE)
A white crystalline mineral consisting of magnesium oxide, occurring naturally and used as a source of magnesium.
white mineral; magnesium source

Examples

The geologist identified the white mineral as periclase under the microscope.
Refractory bricks often contain periclase for its high melting point and durability.
Naturally occurring periclase is an important source of magnesium in industrial processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnesia - powdered magnesium oxide; a related chemical substance
  • Degree Lower: magnesite - a mineral source of magnesium carbonate, related mineral

Etymology

From Greek 'peri' (around) + 'klasis' (breaking), referring to its cleavage.
