sinuosity

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪnjuˈɑsəti / (AmE), / ˌsɪnjuˈɒsəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being winding or curving, especially in reference to roads, streams, or paths
the quality of being winding or curving

Examples

The sinuosity of the river made navigation slow and challenging for the small boats.
Engineers studied the sinuosity of the path to improve traffic flow and safety.
He was fascinated by the natural sinuosity of old country roads winding through the landscape.

Synset Examples

he hated the sinuosity of mountain roads

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curvature - the fact of being curved or bent
  • Antonym: straightness - the condition of being straight, without bends or curves

Etymology

From Latin 'sinuositas', from 'sinuosus' (full of bends).
