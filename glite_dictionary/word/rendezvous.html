rendezvous

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːn.deɪˌvuː / (AmE), / ˈrɒn.deɪˌvuː / (BrE)
a meeting arranged between two people, especially for a romantic purpose
meeting, often romantic

Examples

They agreed to a secret rendezvous at the park after sundown.
Her heart raced as she prepared for the rendezvous with her long-distance partner.
Their regular Friday night rendezvous became a cherished routine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meeting - an assembly of people for a particular purpose

Etymology

From French 'rendez-vous', meaning 'present yourselves'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːn.deɪˌvuː / (AmE), / ˈrɒn.deɪˌvuː / (BrE)
a meeting arranged to occur at a specific time and place, often secretly or for a particular purpose
arranged meeting at a set time and place

Examples

We agreed to meet at our usual rendezvous in the park.
The spy arrived early at the rendezvous point, waiting nervously.
Their secret rendezvous was discovered by the authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appointment - a prearranged meeting, typically formal
  • Synonym: meeting - an act of people coming together by arrangement

Etymology

From French 'rendez-vous', literally 'present yourselves' (imperative plural of se rendre + vous).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01234113-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːn.deɪˌvuː / (AmE), / ˈrɒn.deɪˌvuː / (BrE)
a place where people have arranged to meet
a place for meeting

Examples

We agreed to meet at our usual rendezvous before the concert.
The old café became the secret rendezvous for the two friends.
Police surrounded the rendezvous to apprehend the suspects.

Synset Examples

he was waiting for them at the rendezvous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meeting place - a place where people gather for a meeting or social occasion
  • Synonym: destination - the place to which someone is going or directed

Etymology

From French 'rendez-vous', meaning 'present yourselves'.

4. VERB

/ ˈrɑːndɪˌvuː / (AmE), / ˈrɒndɪvuː / (BrE)
to meet at a prearranged place and time; to come together at an agreed location
meet at a prearranged place/time

Examples

The expedition will rendezvous at base camp before continuing their journey.
We agreed to rendezvous at the café at noon.
The ships rendezvoused just off the coast before launching the joint operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meet - to come together with; to encounter (as persons or things)

Etymology

From French 'rendez-vous', meaning 'present yourselves' (imperative of 'se rendre', to go, and 'vous', you).
