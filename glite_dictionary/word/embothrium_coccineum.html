Embothrium coccineum

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈbɒθriəm kɒkˈsɪniəm / (AmE), / ɛmˈbɒθriəm kɒkˈsɪniəm / (BrE)
a South American tree with showy scarlet-crimson flowers, commonly known as the Chilean firebush
South American tree with scarlet flowers

Examples

The gardens featured a beautiful Embothrium coccineum in full bloom near the entrance.
Botanists admire Embothrium coccineum for its striking scarlet flowers and glossy green leaves.
Many visitors take photos when the Embothrium coccineum bursts into color each spring.

Etymology

From Greek 'embothrion' (a socket or plug) and Latin 'coccineus' (scarlet).
