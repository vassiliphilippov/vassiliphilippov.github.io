postern

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstərn / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstən / (BrE)
a small gate or door in the rear part of a fort, castle, or large building, often used for secret or private entry or exit
small rear gate in a castle or fort

Examples

The guards quietly opened the postern to allow the messengers to slip out unnoticed.
Historians discovered an old postern hidden behind the crumbling stone wall of the castle.
During the siege, supplies were smuggled in through the postern at night.

Etymology

From Old French 'posterne', from Latin 'posterus' meaning 'coming after, later'.
