Etruscology

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌtrʌskoʊˈlɑdʒi / (AmE), / ɪˌtrʌskəˈlɒdʒi / (BrE)
the study of Etruscan civilization, especially its culture, artifacts, and language
study of Etruscan civilization

Examples

She developed a deep interest in Etruscology after visiting ancient sites in Italy.
Leading universities offer advanced courses in Etruscology for archaeology students.
His research in Etruscology has shed new light on Etruscan religious practices.

Synset Examples

Half a century ago, however, Massimo Pallottino, the father figure of modern ‘Etruscology’, demolished this thesis once and for all, and today it is accepted that the origins of the Etruscan city states must be found in the internal social transformations that took place among the indigenous late prehistoric societies of Etruria in the two or three centuries preceding the emergence of the city states.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archaeology - the study of human history and prehistory through excavation of sites and analysis of artifacts
  • Synonym: Etruscan - relating to the ancient Etruscan civilization of Italy

Etymology

From Etruscan (ancient Italian people) + -logy (the study of).
