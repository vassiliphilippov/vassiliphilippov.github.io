Cimmerian

1. ADJ

/ sɪˈmɪriən / (AmE), / sɪˈmɪəriən / (BrE)
intensely dark and gloomy; enveloped in perpetual darkness
intensely dark; gloomy
archaic

Examples

A Cimmerian fog covered the abandoned village, obscuring the moon and stars.
The novel's protagonist wandered through a Cimmerian landscape, desperate for the faintest glimmer of hope.
The basement was so Cimmerian that even the flashlight seemed swallowed by the darkness.

Synset Examples

the Cimmerian gloom … a darkness that could be felt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dark - with little or no light; gloomy
  • Synonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, especially as to appear depressing or frightening
  • Antonym: bright - giving out or reflecting much light; shining

Etymology

From Latin Cimmerii, an ancient people in classical mythology said to dwell in perpetual darkness.
