jug

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌɡ / (AmE), / dʒʌɡ / (BrE)
a large container with a narrow mouth, typically used for holding and pouring liquids
large container for liquids, narrow mouth

Examples

She poured fresh lemonade from the jug into our glasses.
The old ceramic jug sat on the kitchen shelf as decoration.
He filled the plastic jug with water before heading out on the hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitcher - a container for holding and pouring liquids, typically with a handle and spout

Etymology

From Middle English 'jugge', of uncertain origin; possibly from Old English or related to similar words in other Germanic languages.

2. NOUN

/ dʒʌɡ / (AmE), / dʒʌɡ / (BrE)
the amount of liquid or other substance that a jug can hold
the amount a jug holds

Examples

We bought a jug of lemonade for the picnic.
The recipe calls for a jug of fresh milk.
He drank nearly a whole jug of water after the run.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottle - the quantity contained in a bottle
  • Synonym: glass - the quantity contained in a glass

Etymology

Probably from Late Latin *cupa* (tub, cask, barrel), via Middle English *jugge* (drinking vessel).

3. VERB

/ dʒʌɡ / (AmE), / dʒʌɡ / (BrE)
(of meat or game) to stew in an earthenware jug, typically with wine and other ingredients.
to stew meat in a jug

Examples

The chef will jug the hare with a rich red wine sauce for tonight's special.
Traditionally, people would jug rabbit to tenderize the meat and enhance the flavor.
Many old English cookbooks include instructions for how to jug game birds or small animals.

Synset Examples
