turnstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrnˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnˌstəʊn / (BrE)
a migratory shorebird of the plover family that searches for food by turning over stones, typically found along coastal areas
a migratory shorebird of the plover family

Examples

The turnstone is often seen along rocky shorelines flipping over pebbles in search of insects.
Every autumn, flocks of turnstones migrate to warmer regions along the coast.
A turnstone was spotted at the estuary, identified by its distinctive black and white plumage.

Etymology

From 'turn' + 'stone', referring to the bird's habit of turning over stones while foraging.
