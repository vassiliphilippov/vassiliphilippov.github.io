coagulated

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈæɡjəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / kəʊˈæɡjʊˌleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a liquid, especially blood or milk) changed into a thick or solid mass.
changed from liquid to solid mass

Examples

The blood on the bandage was already coagulated and turning dark brown.
Heat caused the protein in the milk to become coagulated.
After an hour, the sauce had coagulated into a jelly-like consistency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coagulate - to change from a liquid to a solid or semi-solid state
  • Antonym: liquefied - made or become liquid
  • Synonym: solidified - made or become hard or solid

Etymology

From Latin 'coagulatus', past participle of 'coagulare' (to curdle, collect together).

2. ADJ

/ koʊˈæɡjəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / kəʊˈæɡjʊleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a liquid) having been changed into a soft, semisolid, or solid mass by a chemical or physical process.
changed from liquid to thickened mass

Examples

The nurse carefully cleaned away the coagulated blood from the wound.
Over time, the stagnant water became coagulated with algae and debris.
The scientist observed the coagulated sample under the microscope.

Synset Examples

coagulated blood
curdled milk
grumous blood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curdled - (of milk or cream) changed into curds and whey; lumpy or thickened

Etymology

From Latin 'coagulatus', past participle of 'coagulare' (to curdle, to clot).
