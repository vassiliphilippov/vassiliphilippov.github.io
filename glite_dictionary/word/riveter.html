riveter

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪvətər / (AmE), / ˈrɪvɪtə / (BrE)
a worker who fastens pieces of metal together by inserting and hammering rivets
worker who fastens metals with rivets

Examples

The factory hired a skilled riveter to assemble the steel beams.
As a riveter, she worked on shipbuilding during the war.
The job required every riveter to wear safety equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: welder - worker who fuses metal parts by welding

Etymology

From 'rivet' (a metal pin or bolt) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

WordNet ID: oewn-10553098-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪvətər / (AmE), / ˈrɪvɪtə / (BrE)
a machine used for driving rivets into materials to fasten them together
machine for driving rivets

Examples

The factory installed a new riveter to speed up the assembly line.
A pneumatic riveter makes joining metal plates much more efficient.
He trained the workers on how to use the automatic riveter safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rivet - a short metal pin or bolt used for fastening

Etymology

From 'rivet' + '-er', denoting a machine or person that drives rivets.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04102671-n
Synset POS: n
