distinctly

1. ADV

/ dɪˈstɪŋktli / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋktli / (BrE)
in a way that is clear and unmistakable; to a noticeable or marked degree
clearly; noticeably; to a marked degree

Examples

The two species are distinctly different in their appearance and behavior.
His voice was distinctly heard over the noise of the crowd.
The weather turned distinctly colder after the sun went down.

Synset Examples

urbanization in Spain is distinctly correlated with a fall in reproductive rate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clearly - in a clear manner; without doubt or confusion
  • Antonym: vaguely - in an unclear or indistinct way

Etymology

From Latin 'distinctus' (separated, distinguished), past participle of 'distinguere'.

2. ADV

/ dɪˈstɪŋktli / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋktli / (BrE)
in a clear or definite way; with unmistakable clarity or precision
clearly; unmistakably

Examples

It was distinctly stated in the contract that payment was due by the end of the month.
She could distinctly hear voices coming from the next room, even though the door was closed.
The mountain peak was distinctly visible against the bright morning sky.

Synset Examples

it's distinctly possible
I could clearly see myself in his situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clearly - in a clear and easy to understand manner
  • Antonym: vaguely - not clearly or fully explained or expressed

Etymology

From Latin 'distinctus' (separated, distinguished), past participle of 'distinguere' (to separate, distinguish).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ dɪˈstɪŋktli / (AmE), / dɪsˈtɪŋktli / (BrE)
in a way that is clearly noticeable or markedly different; clearly and unmistakably
clearly; in a noticeable way

Examples

Her voice was distinctly louder than everyone else's in the room.
This painting is distinctly different from his earlier works.
He spoke distinctly so that everyone could understand his message.

Synset Examples

the subtleties of this distinctly British occasion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clearly - in a clear way; without doubt
  • Antonym: vaguely - in a way that is not clear, distinct, or definite

Etymology

From 'distinct' + '-ly', with 'distinct' from Latin 'distinctus' (distinguished).
