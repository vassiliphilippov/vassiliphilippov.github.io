cubism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuː.bɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.bɪ.zəm / (BrE)
an early 20th-century artistic movement, particularly in France, that depicted subjects using geometric shapes and planes
early 20th-century art movement using geometric forms

Examples

Pablo Picasso is considered one of the leading figures in cubism.
The exhibition explores the origins and development of cubism in early twentieth-century France.
Many artists were influenced by cubism and began experimenting with geometric abstraction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fauvism - an early 20th-century art movement characterized by vivid colors and bold brushwork
  • Synonym: impressionism - an art movement focusing on capturing light and immediate impression, rather than realism

Etymology

From French 'cubisme', based on 'cube' (a geometric shape) and the suffix '-ism'.
