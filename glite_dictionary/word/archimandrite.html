archimandrite

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkɪˈmændraɪt / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈmændraɪt / (BrE)
a senior priest or superior in charge of a large monastery or group of monasteries, especially in the Eastern Orthodox Church
superior of an Orthodox monastery

Examples

The archimandrite welcomed the visiting monks to the abbey with a formal blessing.
After many years of service, he was appointed archimandrite of the renowned monastery.
The duties of an archimandrite include overseeing the spiritual and administrative life of the monastery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abbot - the superior of an abbey of monks, typically in Western Christianity

Etymology

From Greek archimandritēs, from archi- (chief) + mandra (enclosure, monastery).
