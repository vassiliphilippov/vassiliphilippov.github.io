sharkskin

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑːrkˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːkskɪn / (BrE)
a smooth, crisp fabric with a soft sheen, often made from wool, acetate, rayon, or blends, used especially for suits and jackets
smooth, shiny fabric for clothing

Examples

He wore a tailored sharkskin suit to the awards ceremony.
The sharkskin fabric caught the light and shimmered subtly as she moved.
Classic sharkskin is often favored for its sleek, professional look.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the appearance of actual shark skin.
