decasyllable

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛkəˈsɪləbəl / (AmE), / ˌdɛkəˈsɪləbəl / (BrE)
A line of verse consisting of ten syllables.
verse line of ten syllables

Examples

The poet chose to write each stanza in a decasyllable to achieve a rhythmic consistency.
A classic Shakespearean sonnet is composed mainly in lines of decasyllable.
In English poetry, the decasyllable is closely associated with iambic pentameter.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: octosyllable - a verse line consisting of eight syllables
  • Degree Higher: hendecasyllable - a verse line consisting of eleven syllables

Etymology

From Greek deka (ten) + syllabē (syllable).
