Geothlypis trichas

1. NOUN

/ dʒiːˈɑːθlɪpɪs ˈtraɪkəs / (AmE), / dʒiːˈɒθlɪpɪs ˈtrɪkəs / (BrE)
a species of small American songbird in the warbler family, commonly known as the common yellowthroat, found in North America
species of American warbler

Examples

Birdwatchers were delighted to spot a Geothlypis trichas in the wetland area yesterday.
The distinctive song of the Geothlypis trichas is often heard in marshy habitats.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Geothlypis trichas over several years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common yellowthroat - the common English name for Geothlypis trichas, a species of American warbler

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, with 'Geothlypis' derived from Greek 'ge' (earth) and 'thlypis' (an unknown bird), and 'trichas' from Greek 'trikhas' (a thrush-like bird).
