calibre

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləbɚ / (AmE), / ˈkælɪbə / (BrE)
the quality or level of someone’s ability, skill, or accomplishment; the degree of excellence or worth
degree of excellence or quality

Examples

The university only accepts students of the highest calibre.
Her colleagues were impressed by the sheer calibre of her work.
We need engineers of this calibre to lead such important projects.

Synset Examples

the quality of students has risen
an executive of low caliber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quality - the standard of something as measured against other things
  • Synonym: merit - the quality of being particularly good or worthy
  • Antonym: mediocrity - the quality of being average or not very good

Etymology

From French calibre (quality, measure), originally referring to the diameter of a gun barrel.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.ɪ.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæl.ɪ.bə / (BrE)
The internal diameter of a tube, gun barrel, or similar cylindrical object, typically measured in millimeters or inches.
internal diameter of a tube or gun barrel

Examples

The rifle has a calibre of 7.62 millimeters.
When choosing ammunition, make sure it matches the calibre of your firearm.
Many hunting rifles are available in different calibres to suit various game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gauge - standard measure of the diameter of a tube or wire

Etymology

From French 'calibre', probably from Italian 'calibro', of uncertain origin.
