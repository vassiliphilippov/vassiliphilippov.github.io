Lechanorales

1. NOUN

/ lɛkəˌnɔːrˈeɪliːz / (AmE), / lɛkəˌnɔːˈrɑːliːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of lichens characterized by the production of apothecia (fruiting bodies); used in some scientific classification systems.
an order of lichens producing apothecia

Examples

Many crustose lichens belong to the Lechanorales and share similar reproductive structures.
Recent studies have led to the reclassification of some species within the Lechanorales.
The Lechanorales are noted for producing apothecia, distinguishing them from other lichen orders.

Etymology

From New Latin Lechanora (a genus of lichens) + -ales (a standard taxonomic suffix).
