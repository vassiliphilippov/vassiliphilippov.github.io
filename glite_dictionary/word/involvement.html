involvement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɑlvmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒlvmənt / (BrE)
a feeling of concern with or curiosity about someone or something; interest or engagement in an activity or subject
interest or engagement (noun)

Examples

Her involvement in community projects shows her genuine concern for others.
The teacher praised his involvement in classroom discussions.
A deep involvement with music can enrich a person's life.

Synset Examples

an interest in music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interest - the feeling of wanting to know or learn about something

Etymology

From Latin 'involvere' (to roll in, involve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05690773-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɑːlvmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒlvmənt / (BrE)
the act of taking part in or being engaged with activities, especially within a group
participation in activities or groups

Examples

Her involvement in the local community theater helped her make many friends.
Parents' involvement in their children's education is considered highly beneficial.
The company's involvement in environmental projects has improved its public image.

Synset Examples

the teacher tried to increase his students' engagement in class activities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: participation - the act of taking part in something
  • Antonym: exclusion - the act of being left out or not allowed to take part

Etymology

From involve + -ment, originally from Latin 'involvere' (to roll in).
