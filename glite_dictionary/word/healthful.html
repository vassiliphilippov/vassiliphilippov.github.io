healthful

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɛlθfl̩ / (AmE), / ˈhɛlθfʊl / (BrE)
(of conditions or environments) free from filth and pathogens; promoting or conducive to good health.
free from filth and pathogens

Examples

The kitchen must be kept healthful to prevent the spread of disease.
They ensured the water supply was always healthful for the entire village.
Maintaining a healthful environment is essential in a hospital.

Synset Examples

sanitary conditions for preparing food
a sanitary washroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanitary - free from dirt and harmful microorganisms

Etymology

From Middle English 'helthful', from 'health' + '-ful'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02120256-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɛlθ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈhɛlθ.fəl / (BrE)
promoting or conducive to good health of the body or mind; beneficial to health
promoting good health

Examples

Eating a healthful diet can reduce your risk of chronic disease.
The mountain air is known for its healthful properties.
After a week at the spa, she felt rejuvenated by the healthful environment.

Synset Examples

a healthful climate
a healthful environment
healthful nutrition
healthful sleep
Dickens's relatively healthful exuberance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: healthy - in good health; showing health
  • Antonym: unhealthy - harmful to health or not in good health

Etymology

Formed from 'health' + '-ful' (full of); in use since the 15th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01168307-a
