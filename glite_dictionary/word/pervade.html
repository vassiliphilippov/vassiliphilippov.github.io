to pervade

1. VERB

/ pərˈveɪd / (AmE), / pəˈveɪd / (BrE)
(of a feeling, influence, or quality) to spread through and be perceived in every part of a place or thing.
spread through every part of something

Examples

A sense of excitement seemed to pervade the entire stadium before the big game.
The smell of fresh paint pervaded the newly renovated office.
Questions about the future pervade every discussion these days.

Synset Examples

An atmosphere of distrust has permeated this administration
music penetrated the entire building
His campaign was riddled with accusations and personal attacks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to permeate - to spread throughout something; to pass through
  • Synonym: to invade - to enter into a place forcefully or spread into it

Etymology

From Latin pervadere, from per- (through) + vadere (to go).
