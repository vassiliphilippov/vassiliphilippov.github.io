imputrescible

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪm.pjuːˈtrɛs.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pjuːˈtrɛs.ɪ.bəl / (BrE)
Not subject to decay or putrefaction; incapable of rotting.
incapable of decaying

Examples

The archaeologists marveled at the imputrescible nature of the ancient artifacts.
Certain synthetic materials are valued for being almost entirely imputrescible under normal environmental conditions.
Gold is often described as imputrescible because it does not tarnish or corrode over time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: putrescible - liable to decay or putrefy
  • Antonym: decay - to undergo decomposition

Etymology

From French 'imputrescible', from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'putrescere' (to rot).
