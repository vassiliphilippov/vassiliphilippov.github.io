to decapitate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈkæpɪteɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈkapɪteɪt / (BrE)
to cut off the head of a person or animal
cut off the head

Examples

During the Revolution, many prisoners were decapitated by guillotine.
The myth says that Perseus decapitated Medusa with a single stroke.
If a snake is decapitated, its body may still move for a short time.

Synset Examples

the French King was beheaded during the Revolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to behead - to cut off someone's head

Etymology

From Late Latin decapitatus, past participle of decapitare (to behead), from Latin de- (off) + caput (head).
