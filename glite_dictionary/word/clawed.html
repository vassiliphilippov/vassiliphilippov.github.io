clawed

1. ADJ

/ klɔd / (AmE), / klɔːd / (BrE)
(of animals) having claws or talons.
having claws or talons

Examples

The clawed feet of the eagle help it grasp its prey tightly.
Some dinosaurs, like the velociraptor, were known for their sharp, clawed hands.
The cat extended its clawed paw toward the scratching post.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clawless - having no claws or talons

2. ADJ

/ klɔd / (AmE), / klɔːd / (BrE)
having claws or resembling claws; used to describe animals, limbs, or objects possessing or resembling claws.
having or resembling claws

Examples

The clawed paws of the tiger gripped the ground as it prepared to pounce.
Many crustaceans have clawed appendages for defense and feeding.
He noticed the clawed tracks in the mud near the riverbank.

Synset Examples

sharp-clawed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clawless - lacking claws

Etymology

From 'claw' + '-ed', forming the adjective.
