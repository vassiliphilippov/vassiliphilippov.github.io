to recrudesce

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.kruːˈdɛs / (AmE), / ˌriː.kruːˈdɛs / (BrE)
(of something undesirable) to break out or occur again after a period of dormancy.
break out or recur again (undesirable events)

Examples

Tensions in the region may recrudesce if diplomatic efforts fail.
The disease seemed to disappear, only to recrudesce a few months later.
Old resentments can recrudesce in times of crisis.

Synset Examples

Report the news as it develops
These political movements recrudesce from time to time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recur - happen again; occur repeatedly
  • Antonym: to abate - to become less intense or widespread

Etymology

From Latin 'recrudesco' meaning 'to become raw again'.

2. VERB

/ ˌriːkruːˈdɛs / (AmE), / ˌriːkruːˈdɛs / (BrE)
(of a disease, condition, or symptom) to break out or appear again after a period of dormancy or remission.
to break out or reappear (esp. medical)
medical

Examples

If the infection recrudesces, stronger antibiotics may be necessary.
Symptoms of malaria can recrudesce even after initial treatment appears successful.
Her eczema might recrudesce in response to stress or allergens.

Synset Examples

He broke out in hives
My skin breaks out when I eat strawberries
Such boils tend to recrudesce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recur - to happen again; to return
  • Antonym: to abate - to become less severe or widespread

Etymology

From Latin 'recrudescere' meaning 'to become raw again'.
