Samolus parviflorus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæmoʊləs ˌpɑːrvəˈflɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌsæmələs ˌpɑːvɪˈflɔːrəs / (BrE)
A species of water pimpernel native to North America, commonly found in wetlands.
North American water pimpernel plant

Examples

Samolus parviflorus is often found growing along the edges of marshes and ponds.
Botanists recently discovered a large patch of Samolus parviflorus in the nature reserve.
The delicate white flowers of Samolus parviflorus are characteristic of the water pimpernel family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water pimpernel - a common name for Samolus species, small flowering plant of wet habitats

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Samolus', species epithet 'parviflorus' meaning 'small-flowered'.
