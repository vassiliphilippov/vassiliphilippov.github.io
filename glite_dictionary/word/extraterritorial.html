extraterritorial

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛkstrəˌtɛrɪˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ˌɛkstrəˌtɛrɪˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
(of a law, right, or privilege) existing or taking effect outside the established territorial boundaries or jurisdiction.
outside territorial limits or jurisdiction

Examples

The embassy staff have extraterritorial status under international law.
He was prosecuted under the country’s extraterritorial anti-corruption legislation.
They discussed the impact of extraterritorial policies on global trade agreements.

Synset Examples

fishing in extraterritorial waters
enjoying exterritorial privileges and rights

Related Terms

  • Antonym: territorial - relating to territory or territorial rights

Etymology

From Latin extra (outside) + territorial (of territory), meaning "outside the territory".
