alizarine

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪzəriːn / (AmE), / əˈlɪzəriːn / (BrE)
An orange-red crystalline compound used in making red pigments and in dyeing fabrics.
orange-red dye and pigment compound

Examples

The bright red of the fabric was achieved using alizarine as a dye.
Artists favored alizarine for its vivid hue and permanence in oil paints.
The laboratory synthesized alizarine to test its suitability for industrial dyeing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alizarin - another spelling of alizarine, same compound

Etymology

From French 'alizarine', from 'alizari' (madder), ultimately from Arabic 'al-‘uṣāra' (juice).
