Sherpa

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɜːr.pə / (AmE), / ˈʃɜː.pə / (BrE)
A member of a Himalayan ethnic group native to Nepal and Tibet, renowned for their expertise in mountaineering and guiding climbers in high-altitude expeditions.
member of a Himalayan mountaineering people

Examples

Many climbers hire a Sherpa to guide them to the summit of Mount Everest.
The Sherpas carried heavy equipment and set up camps for the expedition.
As a skilled Sherpa, Pasang has climbed several of the world's highest peaks.

Etymology

From Tibetan 'shar pa', meaning 'eastern people'.
