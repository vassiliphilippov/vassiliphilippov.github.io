drogue

1. NOUN

/ droʊɡ / (AmE), / drəʊɡ / (BrE)
a small parachute used to slow down or stabilize a rapidly moving object, such as an aircraft or a vehicle
parachute for decelerating an object

Examples

The pilot deployed the drogue to slow the jet after landing.
Racing cars sometimes use a drogue to help decelerate quickly at the finish line.
The spacecraft released a drogue to stabilize its descent before landing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parachute - a device for slowing the motion of an object through the air

2. NOUN

/ droʊɡ / (AmE), / drəʊɡ / (BrE)
A device, typically a canvas-covered frame, that floats behind a vessel to act as a drag or anchor, preventing drifting or helping to maintain course in the wind.
floating device to prevent drifting

Examples

In heavy storms, the crew deployed a drogue to keep the boat's bow facing into the wind.
The fishing vessel stayed in position because a canvas drogue trailed behind, slowing its drift.
Many sailors carry a drogue as a precaution during long ocean passages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea anchor - device used to stabilize or slow a boat in the water

Etymology

From French 'drogue', possibly meaning 'drag' or 'trailing object', adopted into nautical use.

3. NOUN

/ droʊɡ / (AmE), / drəʊɡ / (BrE)
a funnel-shaped device that is towed behind an aircraft and used as a target for gunnery practice or missile testing
funnel-shaped aerial target towed by aircraft

Examples

During training, the fighter pilot aimed at the drogue to demonstrate his accuracy.
A drogue is attached to a cable and towed behind a plane for gunnery practice.
New pilots must successfully hit the drogue before moving on to live-fire exercises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: target - an object aimed at in shooting practice or attacks
  • Synonym: tow target - an object towed behind an aircraft for use in target practice

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from French 'drogue' (drag, trawl).

4. NOUN

/ droʊɡ / (AmE), / drəʊɡ / (BrE)
a truncated cloth cone mounted on a pole or mast, used especially at airports to show the direction and strength of the wind.
wind direction cone at airports

Examples

The drogue at the end of the runway pointed sharply to the west, indicating a strong wind.
As the storm approached, the airport staff checked the drogue to monitor changing wind directions.
Pilots are trained to observe the drogue before takeoff and landing for safety reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windsock - a fabric cone used at airports to indicate wind direction and speed

Etymology

Perhaps from French ‘drogue’ (drug, trash), in reference to a drag or hindrance.
