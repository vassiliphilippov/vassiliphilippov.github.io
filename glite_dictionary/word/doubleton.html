doubleton

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌb.əl.tən / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl.tən / (BrE)
In bridge and other card games, a holding of exactly two cards in a particular suit in a player's hand.
two cards in a suit (cards)

Examples

She found herself with a doubleton in hearts, which made her lead more challenging.
If you hold a doubleton in spades, you might consider ruffing early in the play.
In bridge strategy, the presence of a doubleton can influence your discard choices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: singleton - a holding of only one card in a suit (cards)
  • Antonym: void - a lack of any cards in a suit (cards)

Etymology

From 'double', meaning two, and the suffix '-ton'.
