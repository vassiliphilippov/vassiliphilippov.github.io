Surnia

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɝːniə / (AmE), / ˈsɜːnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of owls in the family Strigidae, commonly known as hawk owls, found mainly in northern forested regions.
a genus of hawk owls

Examples

The Surnia genus includes the well-known northern hawk owl.
Researchers studied the hunting behavior of Surnia in the wild.
The Surnia species is noted for its diurnal activity unlike most owls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk owl - a species of owl within the genus Surnia

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Surnia, coined for this taxonomic genus.

2. PROPN

/ ˈsɜrniə / (AmE), / ˈsɜːnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of hawk-like owls, notably including the northern hawk-owl.
a genus of hawk-like owls

Examples

The northern hawk-owl, classified under Surnia, is known for its diurnal hunting habits.
Biologists studied the unique features of the Surnia genus.
Surnia species are found across boreal forests in the Northern Hemisphere.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin *Surnia*, the scientific name assigned by taxonomists.
