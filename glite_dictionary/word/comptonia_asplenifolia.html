Comptonia asplenifolia

1. NOUN

/ kɑmpˈtoʊniə æsˌplɛnɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / kɒmpˈtəʊniə æsˌplɛnɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub of eastern North America with sweet-scented, fern-like leaves and small white flowers, also known as sweetfern.
deciduous shrub with fern-like leaves, sweetfern

Examples

The aromatic leaves of Comptonia asplenifolia are often used in herbal teas by locals.
Botanists studied the habitat requirements of Comptonia asplenifolia in New England forests.
When hiking in eastern Canada, you may encounter Comptonia asplenifolia growing along sandy trails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetfern - common name for Comptonia asplenifolia, a shrub with aromatic leaves

Etymology

From genus name 'Comptonia' and species epithet 'asplenifolia', referring to the resemblance of its leaves to those of the fern genus Asplenium.
