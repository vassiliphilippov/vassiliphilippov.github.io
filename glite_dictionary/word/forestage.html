forestage

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
the part of a theater stage that projects in front of the curtain and orchestra pit
front part of theater stage

Examples

The actor delivered his monologue from the forestage, engaging the audience more intimately.
During the musical, several scenes were performed on the forestage while the crew changed sets behind the curtain.
The director instructed the dancers to assemble on the forestage for the opening number.

Etymology

From 'fore-' (meaning in front) + 'stage'.
