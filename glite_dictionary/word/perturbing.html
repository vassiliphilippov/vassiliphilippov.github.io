perturbing

1. ADJ

/ pərˈtɝːbɪŋ / (AmE), / pəˈtɜːbɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing anxiety, worry, or distress.
causing anxiety or distress

Examples

The increase in crime rates is particularly perturbing to city residents.
She found the lack of communication from the hospital perturbing.
There was something perturbing about his sudden disappearance without explanation.

Synset Examples

distressing (or disturbing) news
lived in heroic if something distressful isolation
a disturbing amount of crime
a revelation that was most perturbing
a new and troubling thought
in a particularly worrisome predicament
a worrying situation
a worrying time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturbing - causing worry or upset
  • Antonym: comforting - providing comfort; alleviating worry or distress

Etymology

From Latin perturbare ('disturb, confuse'), from per- ('through') + turbare ('to disturb').
