unsaddled

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsædld / (AmE), / ʌnˈsædld / (BrE)
(of a horse or other animal) not having a saddle on its back.
without a saddle (especially of a horse)

Examples

The horses stood unsaddled in the stable after a long day's ride.
He returned the unsaddled mare to the paddock to let her rest.
We watched as the riders carefully left their mounts unsaddled by the fence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: saddled - having a saddle on its back (especially of a horse)

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'saddled' (having a saddle).
