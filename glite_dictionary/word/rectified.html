rectified

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛk.tɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.tɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
put right; corrected or made accurate after being wrong or faulty
corrected; put right

Examples

The error in the financial report was quickly rectified by the accountant.
Steps were taken to ensure that the situation was rectified as soon as possible.
Once the faulty wiring was rectified, the lights worked perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrected - made free from error or fault
  • Synonym: improved - made better; enhanced in quality or value
  • Antonym: faulty - having faults; imperfect or incorrect

Etymology

From Latin 'rectificare' (to make right), from 'rectus' (right) + 'facere' (to make).
