anorchism

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɔrˌkɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈnɔːkɪzəm / (BrE)
A congenital or acquired absence of one or both testes.
absence of testes (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with anorchism after a thorough examination.
Infertility can be a consequence of anorchism in some individuals.
Patients with anorchism may require hormone replacement therapy.

Etymology

From Greek an- (without) + orchis (testicle) + -ism (condition).
