lugubriously

1. ADV

/ luˈɡuːbriəsli / (AmE), / luˈɡjuːbriəsli / (BrE)
in a sorrowful, gloomy, or mournful manner
in a gloomy or sorrowful way

Examples

He spoke lugubriously about the company's uncertain future.
The old man lugubriously recounted stories of his lost youth.
Rain pattered lugubriously against the window as she sat alone.

Synset Examples

his long face lugubriously reflecting a hidden and unexpressed compassion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomily - in a dark, sad, or pessimistic manner
  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a happy or optimistic manner

Etymology

From Latin 'lugubris' (mournful) via 'lugubrious' + '-ly'.
