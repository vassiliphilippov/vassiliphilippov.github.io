threadbare

1. ADJ

/ ˈθrɛdˌbɛr / (AmE), / ˈθrɛd.beə / (BrE)
(of an idea, remark, or phrase) used so often that it is no longer interesting or effective; unoriginal or stale from overuse.
overused or stale (idea, phrase)

Examples

His speech was filled with threadbare clichés that failed to inspire the audience.
Journalists criticized the candidate for using the same threadbare slogans at every rally.
The plot of the movie was so threadbare that viewers guessed every twist in advance.

Synset Examples

his remarks were trite and commonplace
hackneyed phrases
a stock answer
repeating threadbare jokes
parroting some timeworn axiom
the trite metaphor ‘hard as nails’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trite - lacking originality or freshness; dull on account of overuse
  • Synonym: hackneyed - (of a phrase or idea) lacking significance through having been overused
  • Antonym: original - not derived from something else; fresh and inventive

Etymology

From thread + bare, originally referring to fabric worn thin; figurative sense attested from the 16th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈθrɛdˌbɛr / (AmE), / ˈθrɛd.bɛə / (BrE)
(of cloth, clothing, or furnishings) having the nap worn away, with threads showing through due to age or use; shabby or worn-out.
worn so thin that threads show through

Examples

After years of use, the living room sofa had become threadbare and uncomfortable.
He wore a threadbare coat that barely kept out the cold winds.
The hotel carpets were so threadbare that you could see the floor underneath.

Synset Examples

threadbare rugs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shabby - in poor condition through long use or lack of care
  • Synonym: worn - damaged and thin because of continuous use
  • Antonym: new - not existing before; recently made

Etymology

From 'thread' + 'bare', originally meaning 'bare of thread'.
