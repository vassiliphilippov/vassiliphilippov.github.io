resurvey

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːˈsɜːr.veɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsɜː.veɪ / (BrE)
a new survey or examination, especially one carried out to update or verify previous results
a new or repeated survey or study

Examples

The committee decided to conduct a resurvey of the area to confirm the initial findings.
A resurvey of public opinion was necessary after the policy changes.
The engineer ordered a resurvey because the original measurements were unclear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: survey - an examination or study of something in order to gather information

Etymology

From re- (again) + survey (to examine or inspect).
