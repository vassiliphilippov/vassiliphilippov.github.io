combustion

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈbʌs.tʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈbʌs.tʃən / (BrE)
A chemical process in which a substance reacts rapidly with oxygen and gives off heat, and usually light.
rapid chemical reaction with oxygen producing heat

Examples

The engine relies on combustion of fuel to generate power.
Incomplete combustion can produce harmful gases such as carbon monoxide.
Spontaneous combustion can occur when certain materials heat up without an external flame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burning - The process of being on fire or consuming by fire
  • Synonym: oxidation - A chemical reaction in which a substance combines with oxygen

Etymology

From Latin combustio (a burning), from comburere (to burn up).

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈbʌs.tʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈbʌs.tʃən / (BrE)
The process of burning, especially a chemical reaction that produces heat and light.
the act or process of burning

Examples

During combustion, fuels react with oxygen to release energy in the form of heat and light.
Proper ventilation is important to ensure safe combustion in a fireplace.
The engine's power comes from the combustion of gasoline within its cylinders.

Synset Examples

the burning of leaves was prohibited by a town ordinance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burning - the process or action of being on fire or burned
  • Antonym: extinguishing - the process of putting out a fire

Etymology

From Latin combustio, from comburere (to burn up).
