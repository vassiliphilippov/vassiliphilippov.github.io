cefuroxime

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈfjʊərəˌksaɪm / (AmE), / sɛfˈjʊərəksʌɪm / (BrE)
a broad-spectrum cephalosporin antibiotic used to treat infections of the lungs, throat, ears, urinary tract, or meninges
a cephalosporin antibiotic drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed cefuroxime to treat her severe sinus infection.
Many patients respond well to cefuroxime for respiratory tract infections.
Allergic reactions to cefuroxime are rare but should be reported immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalosporin - a group of broad-spectrum antibiotics derived from Acremonium

Etymology

From 'cephalosporin' + 'uroxime,' relating to its chemical structure.
