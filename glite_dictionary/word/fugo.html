Fugo

1. NOUN

/ fuːˈɡoʊ / (AmE), / fuːˈɡəʊ / (BrE)
a bomb carried by a balloon, specifically referring to those launched by Japan during World War II to be carried by wind currents to distant targets
Japanese WWII balloon bomb

Examples

During World War II, Japan launched hundreds of Fugo bombs across the Pacific toward North America.
A Fugo was discovered in Oregon decades after the war had ended.
The history exhibit featured a reconstructed Fugo balloon as a reminder of the little-known attacks.

Synset Examples

In World War II the Japanese launched balloon bombs against North America

Etymology

From Japanese 風船爆弾 (fūsen bakudan), shortened to 'fūgo', meaning 'balloon bomb'.
