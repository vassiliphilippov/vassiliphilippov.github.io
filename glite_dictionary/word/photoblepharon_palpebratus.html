Photoblepharon palpebratus

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtəˈblɛfərɒn ˌpælpəˈbreɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəˈblɛfərɒn ˌpalpɪˈbreɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of deep-sea fish known for having a light-producing organ beneath each eye.
deep-sea fish with light organs below its eyes

Examples

The Photoblepharon palpebratus is often called the flashlight fish due to its glowing eye patches.
Marine biologists studied how Photoblepharon palpebratus uses its bioluminescence in the dark ocean.
A diver was thrilled to spot a rare Photoblepharon palpebratus during the deep-sea expedition.

Etymology

From Greek 'phōs' (light) + 'blepharon' (eyelid) and Latin 'palpebratus' (having eyelids).
