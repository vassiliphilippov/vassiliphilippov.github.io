cheroot

1. NOUN

/ tʃəˈrut / (AmE), / tʃəˈruːt / (BrE)
A cigar with both ends cut flat, typically cylindrical and often unsophisticated in appearance.
a cigar with both ends cut flat

Examples

He leisurely puffed on a cheroot while reading his newspaper on the porch.
The vendor offered hand-rolled cheroots at the bustling market in Myanmar.
After dinner, he enjoyed a cheroot instead of his usual pipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cigar - a cylinder of tobacco rolled for smoking

Etymology

From Tamil 'curuttu' (roll), via Portuguese 'cheruto'.
