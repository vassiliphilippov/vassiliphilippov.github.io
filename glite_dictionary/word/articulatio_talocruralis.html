articulatio talocruralis

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌtɪkjəˌleɪʃioʊ ˌtæloʊˈkruːrəlɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪkjʊˌleɪʃɪəʊ ˌtæləʊˈkruːrəlɪs / (BrE)
the anatomical name for the ankle joint, where the tibia and fibula meet the talus bone
ankle joint (anatomical term)
medical

Examples

During surgery, the doctor examined the articulatio talocruralis for signs of injury.
The range of motion in the articulatio talocruralis is critical for walking and running.
Damage to the articulatio talocruralis can result in chronic ankle instability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ankle joint - the joint connecting the foot with the leg

Etymology

From Latin: 'articulatio' (joint) and 'talocruralis' (relating to the ankle).
