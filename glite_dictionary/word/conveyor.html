conveyor

1. NOUN

/ kənˈveɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / kənˈveɪ.ə / (BrE)
A mechanical device consisting of a continuous moving belt that transports materials or objects from one place to another, especially in a factory or industrial setting.
a moving belt for transporting objects

Examples

The factory uses a conveyor to move boxes from one end of the line to the other.
Workers stood next to the conveyor sorting the fresh produce as it passed by.
A jam on the conveyor halted production until the problem was fixed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conveyor belt - a continuous moving band used for transporting objects

Etymology

From convey + -or, meaning 'one that conveys'.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈveɪ.ər / (AmE), / kənˈveɪ.ə / (BrE)
a person who carries or transmits something, such as information or objects, from one place or person to another
a person who carries or transmits something

Examples

As the conveyor of the news, she felt a sense of responsibility toward the community.
He acted as a conveyor of secret information during the negotiations.
In the ceremony, the priest was seen as the conveyor of blessings to the people.

Synset Examples

the conveyer of good tidings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: messenger - a person who carries messages or information

Etymology

From convey + -or, patterned after similar agent nouns formed from verbs.

Synset POS: n
