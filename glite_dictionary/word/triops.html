Triops

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.ɑps / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.ɒps / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater crustaceans with three eyes, including a distinctive median eye, belonging to the family Triopidae.
genus of three-eyed freshwater crustaceans

Examples

The Triops in the aquarium fascinated the children with its three eyes and rapid movements.
Scientists studied the life cycle of Triops in temporary desert pools.
We observed several Triops swimming at the bottom of the pond during our field trip.

Etymology

From Greek 'tri-' meaning 'three' and 'ops' meaning 'eye'.
