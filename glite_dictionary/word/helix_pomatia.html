Helix pomatia

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːlɪks poʊˈmeɪtiə / (AmE), / ˈhiːlɪks pəˈmeɪtɪə / (BrE)
a large edible land snail native to Europe, often used in cooking, especially in French cuisine (also called the Roman snail or Burgundy snail)
large edible European land snail

Examples

In many French restaurants, Helix pomatia is the species used to prepare escargot.
Conservationists are concerned about declining Helix pomatia populations due to overharvesting.
The chef explained how to properly clean and cook Helix pomatia for a traditional dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escargot - a dish of cooked edible snails, especially in French cuisine

Etymology

From Latin: 'helix' meaning spiral and 'pomatia', specific epithet of uncertain origin.
