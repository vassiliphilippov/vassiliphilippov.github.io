arouser

1. NOUN

/ əˈraʊzər / (AmE), / əˈraʊzə / (BrE)
a person who wakes others from sleep.
person who wakes others from sleep

Examples

The arouser made his rounds early each morning in the old boarding school.
In the past, an arouser would go from room to room to wake up factory workers.
He took pride in his work as the designated arouser on the night shift.

Etymology

From the verb 'arouse' (to wake up or stimulate), with the agentive suffix '-er'.
