Halimodendron

1. NOUN

/ hæləˌmoʊˈdɛndrən / (AmE), / hælɪməʊˈdɛndrən / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the pea family (Fabaceae), consisting of a single species commonly known as the salt tree, native to central Asia.
a genus of flowering plants (salt tree)

Examples

Halimodendron is well adapted to saline soils in its native habitat.
Botanists have studied Halimodendron for its unique salt tolerance.
The Halimodendron shrub produces fragrant pink flowers in the spring.

Etymology

From Greek 'halimos' (salt) and 'dendron' (tree), referring to its salt-tolerant nature.
