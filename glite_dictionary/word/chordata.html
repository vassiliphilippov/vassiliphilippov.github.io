Chordata

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˈdeɪtə / (AmE), / kɔːˈdeɪtə / (BrE)
a large phylum of animals that includes all species possessing a notochord at some stage of development, including vertebrates such as mammals, birds, fishes, and reptiles
phylum of animals with a notochord

Examples

The phylum Chordata includes animals such as fish, birds, and mammals.
All members of Chordata possess a notochord at some stage in their life cycle.
Biologists classify humans as part of the Chordata because we have a backbone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vertebrate - an animal with a backbone, member of Chordata
  • Antonym: invertebrate - an animal lacking a backbone, not a chordate

Etymology

From Latin 'chorda' (cord, string) referring to the notochord, with '-ata' indicating possession.
