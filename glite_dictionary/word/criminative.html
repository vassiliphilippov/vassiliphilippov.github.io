criminative

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪməˌneɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmɪnətɪv / (BrE)
(of a statement, fact, or act) tending to charge with or suggest guilt or blame.
suggesting guilt or blame

Examples

The detective presented criminative evidence that pointed directly to the suspect.
His silence during questioning was viewed as criminative by the jury.
The report contained several criminative statements implicating the accused.

Synset Examples

incriminatory testimony

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incriminatory - tending to show or imply guilt
  • Antonym: exculpatory - tending to clear from alleged fault or guilt

Etymology

From Late Latin *criminativus* (pertaining to accusation or guilt), from Latin *criminari* (to accuse).
