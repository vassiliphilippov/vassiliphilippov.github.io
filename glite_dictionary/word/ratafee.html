ratafee

1. NOUN

/ ˌrætəˈfi / (AmE), / ˈrætəfiː / (BrE)
A sweet liqueur made from wine and brandy, flavored with plum, peach, or apricot kernels, and bitter almonds.
sweet liqueur flavored with fruit kernels and almonds

Examples

She poured a small glass of ratafee after dinner as a special treat.
The recipe calls for a splash of ratafee to enhance the dessert's flavor.
At the tasting, the rich aroma of ratafee stood out among the liqueurs.

Etymology

From French ratafia, referring to a type of liqueur.
