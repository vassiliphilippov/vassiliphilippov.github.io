Megalosauridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛgəloʊˈsɔrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌmɛgələʊˈsɔːrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, carnivorous theropod dinosaurs that lived during the Jurassic and early Cretaceous periods.
family of large Jurassic theropod dinosaurs

Examples

Scientists believe Megalosauridae were among the earliest large predatory dinosaurs of the Jurassic period.
Fossils attributed to the Megalosauridae have been found in Europe, Africa, and South America.
The Megalosauridae are closely related to other large theropods like allosaurids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Megalosaurus - a genus of large carnivorous Jurassic dinosaurs

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Megalosaurus (type genus) + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəloʊˈsɔrɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡələʊˈsɔːrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large, carnivorous theropod dinosaurs that lived during the Jurassic and early Cretaceous periods, known as megalosaurs
family of large carnivorous dinosaurs (megalosaurs)

Examples

Fossils of the Megalosauridae have been discovered in Europe, Africa, and South America.
The Megalosauridae were among the earliest large theropod dinosaurs.
Researchers debate the evolutionary relationships within the Megalosauridae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Megalosaurus - type genus of Megalosauridae

Etymology

From New Latin: 'megalosaurus' (type genus, from Greek 'megas' meaning 'large' and 'sauros' meaning 'lizard') + '-idae' (family suffix).
