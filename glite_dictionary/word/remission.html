remission

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
a payment of money sent to someone in another place, especially as part of an official or regular arrangement
a money payment sent to another place

Examples

International workers often send a remission each month to support their families back home.
The remission of funds to his relatives overseas was handled by specialized remittance companies.
The government closely monitors every remission to prevent illegal transfers.

Etymology

From Latin remissio (sending back, release), from remittere (to send back).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
(Law) The act of referring a legal case to another court or authority for decision.
legal referral of a case to another court
legal

Examples

The judge granted a remission of the case to the appellate court for further consideration.
Requests for remission are sometimes made when jurisdictional issues arise.
The defense sought remission so that a higher court could hear the matter anew.

Etymology

From Latin remissio (a sending back, forgiveness), from remittere (to send back).

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of absolving or forgiving a sin, debt, or offense, especially as formally declared by a priest in a religious context
formal absolution or forgiveness (esp. religious)

Examples

The priest granted remission of sins during the sacrament of penance.
Many people sought remission through confession and prayer.
The doctrine teaches that remission of guilt is possible for all who repent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolution - formal release from guilt or punishment, especially by a priest
  • Synonym: forgiveness - the act of forgiving or being forgiven

Etymology

From Latin *remissio* (sending back, forgiveness), from *remittere* (send back, remit).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ rəˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
a period during which the symptoms of a disease decrease or disappear; a reduction in intensity or severity
temporary reduction or disappearance of symptoms
medical

Examples

After six months of treatment, the patient's leukemia was in remission.
She hopes her migraines will go into remission after the new medication.
Periods of remission can give patients hope for a better quality of life.

Synset Examples

his cancer is in remission

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relapse - a return of symptoms after a period of improvement

Etymology

From Latin 'remissio' (relaxation, diminishing); from 'remittere' (to send back, relax).
