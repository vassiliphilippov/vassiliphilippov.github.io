gyroscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪrəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪ.rə.skəʊp / (BrE)
a device consisting of a spinning wheel or disk mounted in such a way that its axis can turn freely in any direction, used to maintain balance or measure rotation
device for maintaining balance or measuring rotation

Examples

The airplane relies on a gyroscope to keep its orientation stable during flight.
By spinning rapidly, the gyroscope helps to balance bicycles and navigation systems.
He demonstrated how the gyroscope resists changes in direction, making it useful for scientific experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gyrostabilizer - device using a gyroscope to keep something stable

Etymology

From Greek 'gyros' (circle) and 'skopos' (observer).
