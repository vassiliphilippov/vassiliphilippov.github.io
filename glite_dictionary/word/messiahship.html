messiahship

1. NOUN

/ məˈsaɪ.əˌʃɪp / (AmE), / mɪˈsaɪ.əʃɪp / (BrE)
the state or position of being a messiah, especially in a religious context
the position of being a messiah

Examples

The question of Jesus' messiahship has been debated by scholars for centuries.
Claims of messiahship have sparked major religious movements throughout history.
He was reluctant to make any public statement about his alleged messiahship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: messiah - the promised deliverer or savior in certain religions

Etymology

Formed from 'messiah' + suffix '-ship', denoting state or office.
