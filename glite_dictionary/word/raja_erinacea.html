Raja erinacea

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdʒə ɛrɪˈneɪsɪə / (AmE), / ˈrɑːdʒə ɛrɪˈneɪsiə / (BrE)
a species of small skate found in the inshore waters of North America, reaching up to 21 inches in length, especially abundant in summer
a species of small North American skate

Examples

The Raja erinacea is commonly found along the sandy bottoms of the Atlantic coast.
Marine biologists frequently study Raja erinacea to learn about skate population dynamics.
Unlike larger rays, Raja erinacea rarely grows longer than 21 inches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skate - a type of cartilaginous fish related to rays

Etymology

From Latin: *Raja* meaning 'ray' (the fish), *erinacea* from Latin for 'like a hedgehog', possibly referring to texture or appearance.
