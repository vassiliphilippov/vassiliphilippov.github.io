doyly

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɪ.li / (AmE), / ˈdɔɪ.li / (BrE)
A small ornamental mat, usually of lace or linen, placed under a dish or bowl for decoration or to protect the surface beneath.
ornamental mat placed under a dish

Examples

She placed a delicate doyly on the tray before setting down the teapot.
Each dessert was served on a china plate with a lace doyly underneath.
The antique shop displayed silverware neatly arranged on embroidered doylies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doily - A small ornamental mat used with dishes

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'doily', from the name of a 17th-century London draper, Doily or Doyley, who made popular a type of lightweight fabric.
