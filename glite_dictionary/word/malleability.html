malleability

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæliəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌmalɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of a material that allows it to be shaped or formed by hammering or pressure without breaking or cracking
ability to be shaped without breaking

Examples

Gold is valued for its exceptional malleability, making it easy to shape into jewelry.
The malleability of copper allows it to be formed into thin sheets and wires.
Scientists tested the malleability of the new metal alloy under high pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ductility - the ability of a substance to be drawn into thin wires

Etymology

From Medieval Latin malleabilitas, from Latin malleus (hammer).
