blamed

1. ADJ

/ bleɪmd / (AmE), / bleɪmd / (BrE)
(informal, attributive) Used as an intensifier before a noun, often to express annoyance or emphasis.
used as an intensifier, showing annoyance
informal american

Examples

I don't want to hear another blamed excuse about why he's late.
That blamed alarm clock didn’t go off again this morning.
It was the coldest blamed winter we've had in years.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damned - used informally as an intensifier before nouns
  • Synonym: blasted - used as an informal intensifier expressing annoyance

Etymology

From past participle of 'blame', used as an intensifying adjective in American English since the 19th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbleɪmd / (AmE), / ˈbleɪmd / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly before nouns) Used as a mild expletive to intensify the force of a statement, similar to 'damned' or 'blasted'.
used as a mild intensifier before nouns
informal

Examples

He missed the blamed train again and had to wait another hour.
She couldn't find the blamed keys anywhere despite searching all morning.
It was a blamed shame that the concert was cancelled at the last minute.
