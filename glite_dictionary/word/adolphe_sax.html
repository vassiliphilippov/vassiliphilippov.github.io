Adolphe Sax

1. PROPN

/ əˈdɑlf ˈsæks / (AmE), / aˈdɔlf ˈsæks / (BrE)
(1814–1894) A Belgian musical instrument maker who invented the saxophone.
inventor of the saxophone

Examples

The saxophone was invented by Adolphe Sax in the 19th century.
Belgian inventor Adolphe Sax changed the course of modern music.
Many musicians appreciate the lasting impact of Adolphe Sax on jazz and classical music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saxophone - a woodwind instrument invented by Adolphe Sax
