Sequoia sempervirens

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkwɔɪ.ə sɛm.pərˈvaɪ.rɛnz / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɔɪ.ə sɛm.pəˈvaɪ.rɛnz / (BrE)
a tall evergreen tree native to the coastal foothills of the western United States, known for its great height and longevity; also called the coast redwood
coast redwood tree species

Examples

The towering Sequoia sempervirens in this park are over a thousand years old.
Scientists study Sequoia sempervirens to understand ancient forest ecosystems.
Fog drifting through the Sequoia sempervirens groves creates a mystical atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redwood - common name for Sequoia sempervirens and similar trees

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Sequoia' (genus name, after Cherokee Chief Sequoyah) + Latin 'sempervirens' (evergreen, always green).
