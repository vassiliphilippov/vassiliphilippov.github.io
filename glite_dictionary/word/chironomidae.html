Chironomidae

1. NOUN

/ kaɪrəˈnɑmɪdiː / (AmE), / kʌɪrəˈnɒmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of non-biting midges, small flies often found near water, whose larvae are important in aquatic ecosystems.
family of non-biting midges (flies)

Examples

The larvae of Chironomidae play a vital role in freshwater food webs.
Scientists collected samples of Chironomidae to study water quality in the lake.
The presence of Chironomidae indicates healthy aquatic environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midge - a small two-winged fly of the order Diptera

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'cheir' (hand) and 'nomos' (law), used for taxonomic naming.

2. NOUN

/ kaɪrəˈnɒmɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kaɪrəˈnɒmɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of non-biting midges in the order Diptera, whose larvae are aquatic and adults resemble mosquitoes
family of non-biting midges

Examples

Larvae of the Chironomidae are commonly known as bloodworms due to their red color.
Scientists studied the community of Chironomidae in the freshwater lake.
Many species in the Chironomidae family serve as indicators of water quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midge - small, delicate fly resembling a mosquito
  • Antonym: mosquito - type of biting fly, often confused with midges

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Chironomus plus -idae (a zoological family ending).
