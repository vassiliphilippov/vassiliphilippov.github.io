dumbfounding

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʌmˈfaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdʌmˈfaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
Astonishing or bewildering; causing someone to be speechless with surprise or amazement.
astonishing; bewildering

Examples

The magician's final trick was so dumbfounding that the audience sat in stunned silence.
Her dumbfounding discovery changed the course of the investigation entirely.
It was a dumbfounding coincidence to meet an old friend in such a remote place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonishing - causing a feeling of great surprise or wonder
  • Synonym: bewildering - extremely confusing or difficult to understand
  • Antonym: mundane - lacking interest or excitement; dull

Etymology

From the verb 'dumbfound', which combines 'dumb' (speechless) and 'found', an alteration of 'confound'.
