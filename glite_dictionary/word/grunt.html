grunt

1. NOUN

/ ɡrʌnt / (AmE), / ɡrʌnt / (BrE)
a medium-sized tropical marine fish, noted for making grunting sounds when caught, and often used as food
a tropical marine food fish that grunts

Examples

Fishermen prize the grunt for its tasty, white flesh and distinctive flavor.
The boat's catch included several snapper and a large grunt.
When pulled from the water, the grunt emits a curious, croaking sound.

2. NOUN

/ ɡrʌnt / (AmE), / ɡrʌnt / (BrE)
an unskilled or low-ranking soldier, especially in the infantry, or a worker who does basic or routine tasks
unskilled soldier or worker
informal

Examples

After the merger, he started as a grunt in the mailroom before working his way up.
The army relies on grunts to carry out its most dangerous missions.
Being a grunt meant long hours and little recognition, but he took pride in his work.

Synset Examples

infantrymen in Vietnam were called ‘grunts’
he went from grunt to chairman in six years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laborer - a person doing unskilled manual work
  • Antonym: boss - a person in charge of workers

Etymology

Originally imitative of a low, guttural sound; extension to 'low-ranking soldier' dates from the Vietnam War era.

3. VERB

/ ɡrʌnt / (AmE), / ɡrʌnt / (BrE)
to make a short, low, guttural sound, typically of effort, discomfort, or disapproval
make a low animal-like sound

Examples

The wrestler grunted as he lifted the heavy weights above his head.
She grunted in frustration when her computer froze again.
He grunted a response and turned back to his book.

Synset Examples

He grunted his reluctant approval

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groan - make a long, low sound expressing pain or discomfort

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound itself.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01045103-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ɡrʌnt / (AmE), / ɡrʌnt / (BrE)
a short, low, rough sound, especially one made by a pig or hog
a short, low, rough noise (as from hogs)

Examples

The pig let out a loud grunt as it rooted around in the mud.
She answered his question with nothing but a grunt of annoyance.
The farmer recognized the familiar grunt of his hogs at feeding time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snort - a sudden, forceful noise made through the nose

Etymology

Imitative of the sound itself; related to Middle English 'grunten'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
