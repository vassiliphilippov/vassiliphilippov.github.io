pueblo

1. NOUN

/ ˈpwɛbloʊ / (AmE), / ˈpjʊəbləʊ / (BrE)
A communal village built by Native Americans in the southwestern United States, typically consisting of multistoried adobe or stone buildings.
communal Native American village in the Southwest US

Examples

The ancient pueblo was built on the side of a cliff overlooking the valley.
Many tourists visit the old pueblo communities to learn about their history and architecture.
Some families have lived in the same pueblo for generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adobe - sun-dried brick used especially for building in the Southwest US

Etymology

From Spanish 'pueblo' (people, village), from Latin 'populus' (people).
