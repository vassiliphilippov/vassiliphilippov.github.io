nutrient

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.tri.ənt / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.tri.ənt / (BrE)
A substance that provides essential nourishment for the growth and maintenance of living organisms, especially plants.
substance essential for growth or nourishment

Examples

Plants absorb nutrients from the soil through their roots.
This fertilizer is rich in essential nutrients for healthy plant growth.
A lack of certain nutrients can stunt the development of crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutriment - a substance that nourishes; food
  • Antonym: toxin - a poisonous substance produced by living cells or organisms

Etymology

From Latin 'nutrientem', present participle of 'nutrire' (to nourish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnuːtriənt / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrɪənt / (BrE)
a substance that provides nourishment essential for growth and the maintenance of life
substance providing nourishment

Examples

Fruits and vegetables are an excellent source of nutrients.
The food label lists the nutrients contained in each serving.
A balanced diet provides all the nutrients your body needs to stay healthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutriment - a substance that provides nourishment

Etymology

From Latin 'nutrientem', present participle of 'nutrīre' (to nourish).
