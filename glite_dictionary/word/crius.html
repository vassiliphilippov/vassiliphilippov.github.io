Crius

1. PROPN

/ ˈkraɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈkraɪ.əs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) one of the Titans, father of Perses, Astraios, and Pallas.
Titan from Greek mythology

Examples

In ancient myths, Crius was counted among the original twelve Titans.
Few stories directly feature Crius, but he is significant as a father to other deities.
Scholars debate the role Crius played in the Titanomachy.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Κριός (Kriós), the name of a Titan.
