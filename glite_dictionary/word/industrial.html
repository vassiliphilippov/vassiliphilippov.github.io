industrial

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdʌstriəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌstriəl / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by a highly developed system of industries, especially manufacturing.
relating to highly developed industries

Examples

Germany is known as an industrial nation with a strong manufacturing sector.
The industrial areas of the city are filled with factories and warehouses.
Japan underwent rapid industrial development after World War II.

Synset Examples

the industrial revolution
an industrial nation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agricultural - relating to farming rather than industry
  • Synonym: manufacturing - involving the production of goods in large quantities, especially in factories

Etymology

From Latin *industria* (diligence, activity) via French *industriel*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01304559-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdʌstriəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌstriəl / (BrE)
(of goods or materials) designed to be durable and suitable for heavy use, especially in commercial or manufacturing settings.
designed for heavy or commercial use

Examples

The hotel installed industrial carpeting to withstand the constant foot traffic in the lobby.
He chose industrial shelving for his garage because it could support heavy boxes.
Their kitchen is equipped with industrial appliances built for frequent, demanding use.

Synset Examples

industrial carpeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commercial - designed for business or trade use
  • Antonym: domestic - intended for home use

Etymology

From Latin industria (diligence, activity), used in English since the 19th century for things related to industry.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01192191-s
Synset POS: s
