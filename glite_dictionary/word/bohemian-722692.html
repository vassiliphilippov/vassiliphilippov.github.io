bohemian

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈhiːmiən / (AmE), / bəʊˈhiːmiən / (BrE)
a person, especially an artist or writer, who leads an unconventional, nonconformist lifestyle, often in pursuit of artistic or literary goals
unconventional artist or writer

Examples

She has always considered herself a bohemian, preferring art galleries and poetry readings to more traditional pursuits.
The city was once home to a lively community of bohemians known for their creative spirit and disregard for convention.
His bohemian lifestyle attracted both admiration and criticism from his more conventional peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonconformist - a person whose behavior or views do not conform to prevailing ideas or practices
  • Antonym: conformist - a person who conforms to accepted behavior or established practices

Etymology

From French 'bohémien', originally meaning 'Gypsy,' based on the belief that Gypsies came from Bohemia in central Europe. Later used for unconventional artists in 19th-century France.

2. ADJ

/ boʊˈhiːmiən / (AmE), / bəʊˈhiːmiən / (BrE)
Unconventional, especially in appearance or lifestyle; not following accepted standards of behavior or dress.
unconventional in appearance or lifestyle

Examples

She has always lived a bohemian lifestyle, dressing in bright clothes and traveling wherever she pleased.
Their apartment decor was distinctly bohemian, filled with art, books, and mismatched furniture.
He admired her bohemian spirit and refusal to follow society's expectations.

Synset Examples

a bohemian life style

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unconventional - not conforming to accepted customs or practices
  • Antonym: conventional - following accepted customs and proprieties

Etymology

From French 'bohémien', originally meaning 'gypsy', because it was believed that Romani people came from Bohemia.
