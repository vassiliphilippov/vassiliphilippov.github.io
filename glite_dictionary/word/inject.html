to inject

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to introduce a new quality, idea, or element into something
introduce something new

Examples

His enthusiasm injected much-needed energy into the team.
The director injected humor into an otherwise serious film.
She injects fresh ideas into every project she works on.

Synset Examples

He injected new life into the performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to introduce - to bring something into use or operation for the first time

Etymology

From Latin 'injectus', past participle of 'inicere' (to throw in).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00187413-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to force a fluid or gas into something, typically by means of a needle or similar device
force fluid or gas into something

Examples

The technician will inject dye into your bloodstream for the scan.
Doctors often inject vaccines directly into a muscle.
They had to inject hydrogen into the balloon to make it float.

Synset Examples

inject hydrogen into the balloon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insert - to put something into something else

Etymology

From Latin injectus, past participle of inicere 'to throw in'.

medical
