isochronal

1. ADJ

/ ˌaɪsəˈkroʊnəl / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈkrəʊnəl / (BrE)
Occurring at equal intervals of time; having equal duration or timing.
equal in duration or interval

Examples

The scientist noted that the two pendulums were isochronal throughout the experiment.
These chemical reactions proceed in an isochronal fashion under controlled conditions.
The engineer designed the system to produce isochronal pulses every second.

Synset Examples

the oscillations were isochronal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isochronous - occurring at equal intervals of time
  • Antonym: anisochronous - not occurring at equal intervals of time

Etymology

From Greek 'iso-' (equal) + 'chronos' (time).
