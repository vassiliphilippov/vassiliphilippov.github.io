to preassemble

1. VERB

/ priˈæsəmˌbel / (AmE), / ˌpriːəˈsɛmb(ə)l / (BrE)
to manufacture or put together the parts of something, especially a building, in advance so that they can be quickly assembled later
manufacture parts for quick assembly

Examples

The company decided to preassemble wall panels at the factory to speed up the construction process.
Many furniture manufacturers now preassemble major components to simplify home assembly for customers.
By choosing to preassemble the roof trusses, builders reduced labor costs and saved valuable time on site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assemble - to fit together the parts of something

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'pre-' (before) and 'assemble'.
