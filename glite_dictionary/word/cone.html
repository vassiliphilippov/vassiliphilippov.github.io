cone

1. NOUN

/ koʊn / (AmE), / kəʊn / (BrE)
a solid or hollow shape that has a circular base and sides that slope up to a single point at the top
a shape with a circular base and tapering sides

Examples

The ice cream was served in a cone rather than a cup.
A traffic cone blocked the entrance to the parking lot.
In math class, we learned how to calculate the volume of a cone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cylinder - a solid with straight sides and a circular base and top

Etymology

From Latin conus, from Greek kōnos (pine cone, cone).

2. NOUN

/ koʊn / (AmE), / kəʊn / (BrE)
a type of photoreceptor cell in the retina of the eye that is sensitive to bright light and enables color vision
retinal cell for color vision
medical

Examples

The human retina contains three types of cone cells, each sensitive to different wavelengths of light.
Damage to the cones in the retina can result in color blindness.
During the day, cones are primarily responsible for our ability to see colors clearly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rod - a type of retinal photoreceptor cell sensitive to low light and peripheral vision

Etymology

From Greek kōnos (cone), referring to the cone-like shape of the cell.

3. NOUN

/ koʊn / (AmE), / kəʊn / (BrE)
an object or artifact that is shaped like a cone, with a circular base tapering smoothly to a point.
object shaped like a cone

Examples

The children played with a brightly colored cone at the playground.
After the parade, orange cones blocked off several streets for cleanup.
She placed a small cone of incense on the altar before the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cylinder - a solid object with straight sides and circular ends
  • Antonym: sphere - a perfectly round geometrical object in three-dimensional space

Etymology

From Latin 'conus', from Greek 'kōnos' (cone, pine cone).

4. NOUN

/ koʊn / (AmE), / kəʊn / (BrE)
a seed- or spore-producing structure in certain plants, typically consisting of overlapping scales or bracts arranged around a central axis, as found in pine trees and some ferns
seed-bearing structure in plants

Examples

The pine tree dropped a large cone covered in sticky resin onto the forest floor.
Each cone contains numerous seeds that are released when it matures.
Some species of spruce develop a different type of cone for pollen and seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strobilus - technical term for a cone-like reproductive structure in plants

Etymology

From Latin conus (cone), from Greek kōnos (cone, pine cone).
