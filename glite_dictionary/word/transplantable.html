transplantable

1. ADJ

/ trænˈsplæntəbl̩ / (AmE), / trænˈsplɑːntəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be moved from one place or organism to another, especially in medical or horticultural contexts.
able to be transplanted

Examples

The doctor confirmed that the organ was transplantable and suitable for the patient.
Roses are best transplantable in early spring when they are still dormant.
The success of the surgery depends on finding a transplantable kidney.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nontransplantable - not able to be transplanted

Etymology

From 'transplant' (to move from one place to another) + '-able'.
