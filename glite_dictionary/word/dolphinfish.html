dolphinfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊlfɪnˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɒlfɪnˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large, slender fish found in warm seas, valued as food and for sport fishing, especially around Hawaii; also known as mahi-mahi.
large food and game fish of warm seas

Examples

Fishermen in Hawaii often catch dolphinfish for both sport and food.
The chef prepared a delicious grilled dolphinfish with tropical salsa.
Dolphinfish are known for their vibrant coloring and swift swimming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahi-mahi - another name for the dolphinfish, especially in Hawaiian and culinary context

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑlfɪnˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɒlfɪnˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
The lean, edible flesh of a saltwater fish found in warm waters, especially around Hawaii, typically eaten as food.
edible lean flesh of a warm-water fish

Examples

Fresh dolphinfish is often grilled or baked in Hawaiian cuisine.
The chef recommended the dolphinfish fillet, served with tropical salsa.
They caught a large dolphinfish during their deep-sea fishing trip.

Related Terms
