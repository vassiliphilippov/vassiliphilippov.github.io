radicalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈrædɪkəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈrædɪkəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the beliefs or actions of people who advocate thorough or complete political or social reform, often through revolutionary change.
belief in revolutionary political or social change

Examples

His support for radicalism led him to participate in street protests demanding change.
In the early 20th century, radicalism inspired many political movements throughout Europe.
Some critics argued that radicalism posed a threat to the country's stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extremism - beliefs or actions that are far outside the usual or accepted political views
  • Antonym: conservatism - the political philosophy that favors traditional views and values and is cautious about change

Etymology

From French radicalisme, from Latin radix (root), originally referring to going to the root of a matter.
