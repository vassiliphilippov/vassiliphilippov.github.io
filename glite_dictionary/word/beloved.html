beloved

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈlʌvɪd, bɪˈlʌvd / (AmE), / bɪˈlʌvɪd, bɪˈlʌvd / (BrE)
dearly loved; greatly cherished
dearly loved

Examples

She wore a necklace given to her by her beloved grandmother.
The city mourned the loss of its beloved leader.
He returned home to his beloved family after a long journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherished - greatly loved and valued
  • Antonym: hated - intensely disliked

Etymology

From Middle English biloved, past participle of 'belove' (to love greatly).

2. NOUN

/ bəˈlʌvɪd / (AmE), / bɪˈlʌvɪd / (BrE)
a person who is dearly loved, often used as a term of endearment
dearly loved person

Examples

He wrote a letter to his beloved before leaving for the war.
She will always cherish the memories of her late beloved.
At the ceremony, she spoke about her beloved with great affection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darling - a person who is much loved or cherished
  • Synonym: sweetheart - a person who is loved by another

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'belove', from be- + love.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09869006-n
Synset POS: n
