flatus-relieving

1. ADJ

/ ˈfleɪtəs rɪˈliːvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfleɪtəs rɪˈliːvɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a medicine or treatment) relieving or expelling gas from the alimentary tract, especially to reduce bloating, colic, or discomfort.
relieving gas in the digestive tract
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a flatus-relieving medication to ease her abdominal discomfort.
Some herbal teas have flatus-relieving properties that can help with bloating and flatulence.
He chose a flatus-relieving remedy to soothe his baby's colic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carminative - a substance that helps expel gas from the intestines

Etymology

From Latin 'flatus' (a blowing, breath, or wind) + English 'relieving'.
