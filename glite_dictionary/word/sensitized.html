sensitized

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪzd / (BrE)
Having developed an allergy or an unusual or excessive susceptibility, especially to a specific substance or factor.
having an allergy or excessive susceptibility
medical

Examples

After years of exposure to the chemical, she became sensitized and developed a severe rash.
The laboratory mice were sensitized to the allergen for experimental purposes.
Some people are sensitized to peanuts and must avoid them completely.

Synset Examples

allergic children
hypersensitive to pollen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allergic - having an allergy to a particular substance
  • Antonym: immune - protected or exempt from a particular disease or the effect of something

Etymology

From 'sensitize' (to make sensitive) + -ed; ultimately from Latin 'sentire' (to feel).
