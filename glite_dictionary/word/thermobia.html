Thermobia

1. NOUN

/ θərˈmoʊ.bi.ə / (AmE), / θəˈməʊ.bi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of primitive insects in the family Lepismatidae, which includes species such as the firebrat.
genus of primitive insects (Lepismatidae)

Examples

The common firebrat, Thermobia domestica, is often found in warm, damp areas of houses.
Scientists studied the diet of several Thermobia species to understand their role in decomposing household materials.
Unlike silverfish, members of the genus Thermobia are more tolerant of high temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lepismatidae - family of wingless insects including silverfish and firebrats

Etymology

From Greek 'thermo-' (heat) and 'bios' (life), referring to the warm environments favored by some species.

2. NOUN

/ θərˈmoʊbiə / (AmE), / θɜːˈməʊbiə / (BrE)
A genus of small, wingless insects in the family Lepismatidae, which includes the firebrat.
a genus of silverfish insects

Examples

The Thermobia genus is commonly found in warm, moist areas such as bakeries and boiler rooms.
Scientists have studied Thermobia to understand the evolutionary history of silverfish.
A well-known species of Thermobia is the firebrat, which prefers environments above 32°C.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Lepismatidae - family of primitive, wingless insects including silverfish and firebrats
  • Synonym: firebrat - common name for Thermobia domestica, a species in the genus Thermobia

Etymology

From Greek 'thermos' (warm, heat) and 'bios' (life), referring to their preference for warm habitats.
