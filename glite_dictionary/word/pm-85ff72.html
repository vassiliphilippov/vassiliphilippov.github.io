P.M.

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiː ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌpiː ˈɛm / (BrE)
The period of time from noon until midnight, used to indicate time in the 12-hour clock system.
time between noon and midnight

Examples

The meeting is scheduled for 3:00 P.M. in the conference room.
Please submit your assignments before 11:59 P.M. tonight to be considered on time.
Most stores close by 8 P.M. on weekdays.

Synset Examples

AM to PM

Related Terms

  • Antonym: A.M. - the period between midnight and noon

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'post meridiem' meaning 'after midday'.

2. ADV

/ ˌpiː ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌpiː ˈem / (BrE)
The period of time from noon until midnight; used to indicate time after midday.
time after noon and before midnight

Examples

The concert will start at 7 P.M. sharp.
My flight departs at 3 P.M. tomorrow afternoon.
She usually finishes work by 6 P.M. each day.

Synset Examples

let's meet at 8 P.M.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: A.M. - the period from midnight to noon

Etymology

From Latin 'post meridiem' meaning 'after midday'.
