overture

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌtʃʊr / (AmE), / ˈəʊvətjʊə / (BrE)
a tentative proposal or approach made to someone to elicit their response or begin discussions
a tentative proposal or approach

Examples

He made an overture to the new neighbors, hoping to break the ice.
The company's overtures to acquire its rival were swiftly declined.
Despite several overtures for peace, the negotiations failed to produce an agreement.

Synset Examples

she rejected his advances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proposal - a suggestion or offer put forward for consideration
  • Antonym: rejection - the act of refusing a suggestion, offer, or overture

Etymology

From French *ouverture* (opening), from Latin *apertura* (an opening).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vɚ.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.və.tjʊə / (BrE)
a piece of orchestral music played at the beginning of an opera, oratorio, or other extended musical work
orchestral introduction to an opera or oratorio

Examples

The orchestra performed the stirring overture before the curtain rose on the opera.
Each act of the oratorio began with a brief overture setting the mood.
Tchaikovsky's 1812 Overture is famous for its dramatic cannon fire and bells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prelude - a short piece of music played as an introduction to a larger piece
  • Antonym: finale - the last part of a musical or dramatic work

Etymology

From French overture, from ouvrir 'to open', ultimately from Latin aperire.

3. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɚˌtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvətjʊə / (BrE)
an event, action, or proposal that introduces or precedes something more important; a preliminary step or introduction
preliminary event or introduction

Examples

The welcome speech was the overture to an evening of celebration.
The peace talks began with an overture from the new ambassador.
The first chapter serves as an overture to the themes developed in the novel.

Synset Examples

training is a necessary preliminary to employment
drinks were the overture to dinner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prelude - an action or event serving as an introduction to something more important
  • Synonym: preliminary - an action or event done in preparation for something more important
  • Antonym: epilogue - a section or speech at the end of a book or play serving as a comment or conclusion

Etymology

From Old French *overture* (opening), from Latin *aperīre* (to open).
