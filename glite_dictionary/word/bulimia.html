bulimia

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlɪmiə / (AmE), / bjuˈlɪmɪə / (BrE)
An abnormal and excessive hunger or appetite, especially when medically caused, such as by brain disorders.
pathological excessive hunger
medical

Examples

After the accident, she developed bulimia due to damage to the hunger-regulating centers in her brain.
Persistent, uncontrollable eating episodes may indicate an underlying medical issue such as bulimia.
Doctors warned that brain lesions in the patient were responsible for his severe bulimia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anorexia - lack or loss of appetite for food

Etymology

From Greek boulīmia, from bous (ox) + limos (hunger), meaning 'ravenous hunger'.

2. NOUN

/ bjuˈlɪmiə / (AmE), / bjuˈlɪmɪə / (BrE)
a serious eating disorder characterized by episodes of excessive eating (binges) followed by attempts to avoid weight gain, such as vomiting, fasting, or excessive exercise
an eating disorder involving binge eating and compensatory behaviors
medical

Examples

Her struggle with bulimia began in her late teens, affecting her physical and emotional health.
Many support groups offer help to those suffering from bulimia and related eating disorders.
The doctor explained the long-term health risks associated with bulimia to her family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anorexia - an eating disorder characterized by an abnormally low body weight and intense fear of gaining weight

Etymology

From Greek boulimia, from bous (ox) + limos (hunger), meaning 'extreme hunger'.
