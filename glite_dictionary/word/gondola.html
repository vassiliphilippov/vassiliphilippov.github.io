gondola

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑndələ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒndələ / (BrE)
a compartment suspended from an airship that carries crew, cargo, and other equipment
airship compartment

Examples

The crew operated the controls from the gondola of the giant airship.
After the flight, engineers inspected the gondola to ensure there was no damage.
Supplies and passengers were loaded into the gondola before takeoff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: car - compartment for passengers (in a cable car or balloon)

Etymology

From Italian 'gondola', originally referring to a type of boat.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑndələ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒndələ / (BrE)
a low, flat-bottomed railroad freight car with fixed sides and no roof, used for transporting bulk materials
open-topped railroad freight car

Examples

The train included a gondola filled with scrap metal headed for recycling.
Workers loaded gravel into the open-topped gondola at the quarry.
After unloading coal from the gondola, the crew prepared it for its next shipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hopper car - a railroad freight car used for carrying loose bulk materials, usually with a sloping floor and doors for unloading

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑndələ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒndələ / (BrE)
a long, narrow, flat-bottomed boat with a high prow and stern, traditionally used on the canals of Venice and propelled by a person standing at the stern and rowing with a single oar
Venetian canal boat

Examples

The couple hired a gondola to tour the canals of Venice at sunset.
A skilled oarsman expertly maneuvered the gondola through the narrow waterways.
Tourists lined up along the pier, waiting for their turn to board a traditional gondola.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barque - a boat with three or more masts, typically used for sailing

Etymology

From Italian 'gondola', likely of Venetian origin.
