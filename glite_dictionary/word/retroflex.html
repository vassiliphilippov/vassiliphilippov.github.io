retroflex

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛtrəˌflɛks / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəˌflɛks / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) pronounced with the tip of the tongue curled back toward the hard palate.
pronounced with the tongue tip turned back

Examples

In Hindi, the letter 'ट' represents a retroflex sound distinct from the English 't'.
Some Indian and Australian Aboriginal languages use many retroflex consonants in their phonetic inventory.
The teacher demonstrated how to produce a retroflex stop by curling the tongue back.

Etymology

From Latin retroflexus, from retro- (back) + flexus (bent).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛtroʊˌflɛks / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrə(ʊ)flɛks / (BrE)
bent or curved backward; turned back upon itself.
bent or curved backward

Examples

The plant's retroflex leaves helped it collect more water from the air.
Some birds possess a retroflex claw that curves distinctively backward from the toe.
A retroflex tongue position is essential when pronouncing certain consonants in Hindi.

Etymology

From Latin retroflexus, past participle of retroflectere ('to bend backward'), from retro ('backward') + flectere ('to bend').

3. VERB

/ ˈrɛtroʊˌflɛks / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌflɛks / (BrE)
(of a consonant) to pronounce or articulate with the tongue curled back towards the palate.
to pronounce with tongue curled back

Examples

Many speakers of South Asian languages retroflex certain consonants that English does not.
The linguist demonstrated how to retroflex the 't' sound for students.
Children learning Hindi often learn to retroflex consonants from an early age.

Synset Examples

/ ˈrɛtroʊˌflɛks / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌflɛks / (BrE)
to bend or turn something backward or back upon itself, especially in medical or scientific contexts
bend or turn backward

Examples

In some surgical procedures, the doctor must retroflex the tip of the endoscope to see certain areas clearly.
The plant's leaves can retroflex in response to environmental stress.
This muscle allows the tongue to retroflex during speech articulation.

From Latin 'retro-' (back) + 'flexus' (bent, past participle of 'flectere' to bend).
