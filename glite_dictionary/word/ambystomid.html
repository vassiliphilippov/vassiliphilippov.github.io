ambystomid

1. NOUN

/ æmˈbɪstəˌmɪd / (AmE), / amˈbɪstəˌmɪd / (BrE)
A small to moderate-sized terrestrial or semiaquatic salamander of the family Ambystomatidae, native to the New World.
a New World salamander (Ambystomatidae)

Examples

The tiger salamander is a well-known species of ambystomid in North America.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of several ambystomid populations in the wetlands.
The ambystomid larvae develop in ponds before becoming terrestrial adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mole salamander - salamander of the family Ambystomatidae
  • Degree Higher: salamander - an amphibian with a lizard-like appearance

Etymology

From New Latin Ambystoma (the type genus) + -id (denoting family membership in zoological taxonomy).
