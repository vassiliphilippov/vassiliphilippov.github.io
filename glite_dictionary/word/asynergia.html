asynergia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæ.sɪˈnɝː.dʒi.ə / (AmE), / ˌæ.sɪˈnɜː.dʒi.ə / (BrE)
absence of coordination between organs or body parts that normally function together harmoniously, often as a result of neurological disease
lack of bodily coordination (medical)
medical

Examples

The neurologist diagnosed the patient with asynergia after observing his uncoordinated limb movements.
Damage to the cerebellum can result in asynergia and difficulty walking.
Asynergia often makes everyday tasks, like buttoning a shirt or writing, extremely challenging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ataxia - loss of control of body movements

Etymology

From Greek a-, meaning 'not', and synergia, meaning 'working together'.
