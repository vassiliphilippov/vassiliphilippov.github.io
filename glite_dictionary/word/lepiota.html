lepiota

1. NOUN

/ liˈpaɪ.ətə / (AmE), / lɛˈpiː.ətə / (BrE)
any fungus of the genus Lepiota, a group of gilled mushrooms with scaly caps.
a genus of gilled mushrooms

Examples

Some species of lepiota are considered poisonous and should not be eaten.
The forest floor was dotted with lepiotas after the rain.
The mycologist collected a lepiota sample for closer examination.

Etymology

From New Latin Lepiota, from Greek lepis (scale) + -ota (resembling), referring to their scaly caps.
