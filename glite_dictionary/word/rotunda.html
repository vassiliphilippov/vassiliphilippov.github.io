rotunda

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈtʌndə / (AmE), / rəˈtʌndə / (BrE)
a large, round building or room, often with a dome, typically used for public gatherings or as a notable architectural feature
circular building with a dome

Examples

The state capitol building is famous for its magnificent rotunda and marble columns.
Visitors gathered under the spacious rotunda to admire the murals on the ceiling.
The art museum's central rotunda provides natural light and a sense of grandeur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dome - a rounded vault forming the roof of a building or structure

Etymology

From Italian 'rotonda', from Latin 'rotundus' meaning 'round'.
