tetra

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛtrə / (AmE), / ˈtɛtrə / (BrE)
any of various small, brightly colored tropical freshwater fishes of the family Characidae, popular in home aquariums.
small tropical freshwater fish

Examples

A school of neon tetras adds vibrant color to the aquarium.
Many beginner aquarists choose tetras because they are easy to care for.
The tetra is known for its small size and striking coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: characin - fish from the family Characidae, including tetras

Etymology

From New Latin Tetra-, from Greek tetra- (four), referring to the fish's four unpaired fins.
