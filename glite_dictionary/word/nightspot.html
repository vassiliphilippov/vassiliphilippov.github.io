nightspot

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪt.spɑt / (AmE), / ˈnaɪt.spɒt / (BrE)
A place open late at night offering entertainment such as music, dancing, food, and drinks.
late-night entertainment venue

Examples

We went to a trendy nightspot downtown after the concert.
That nightspot is famous for its live jazz performances every Friday.
Many tourists look for a lively nightspot to enjoy the city's nightlife.

Synset Examples

don't expect a good meal at a cabaret
the gossip columnist got his information by visiting nightclubs every night
he played the drums at a jazz club

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightclub - a club offering late-night entertainment, music, dancing, and drinks

Etymology

From "night" + "spot," meaning a place or location, first used in the early 20th century.
