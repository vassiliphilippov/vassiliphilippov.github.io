sing-kwa

1. NOUN

/ sɪŋˈkwɑ / (AmE), / sɪŋˈkwɑː / (BrE)
A type of loofah plant widely cultivated in the tropics, especially in Pakistan, often used as a vegetable or for its fibrous sponge.
a tropical loofah plant, used as vegetable or sponge

Examples

Many Asian dishes include slices of sing-kwa cooked with garlic and other vegetables.
The sing-kwa is also valued for its fibrous interior, which can be dried and used as a natural sponge.
Farmers in Pakistan grow sing-kwa both for culinary uses and for making bath sponges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loofah - a climbing plant whose dried fibrous fruit is used as a sponge
