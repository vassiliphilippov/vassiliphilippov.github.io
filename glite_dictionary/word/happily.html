happily

1. ADV

/ ˈhæpəli / (AmE), / ˈhæpɪli / (BrE)
In a joyous or cheerful manner; with happiness.
in a joyous manner

Examples

They laughed happily as they played in the garden.
She accepted the invitation happily, eager to see her friends again.
The children ran happily down the hill into their mother's arms.

Synset Examples

they shouted happily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joyfully - in a manner full of joy
  • Antonym: sadly - in a sorrowful or unhappy manner

Etymology

From 'happy' + '-ly', where 'happy' is from Middle English, from Old Norse 'happ' meaning 'good luck'.

2. ADV

/ ˈhæpɪli / (AmE), / ˈhæpɪli / (BrE)
in a way that is fortunate or brings a favorable outcome, especially unexpectedly
in a fortunate or lucky way

Examples

Happily, he arrived at the airport just in time for his flight.
Happily, we avoided the worst of the storm on our drive home.
The lost wallet was happily returned by an honest passerby.

Synset Examples

happily he was not injured

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortunately - in a way that is fortunate or lucky
  • Antonym: unfortunately - in a way that is not lucky or fortunate

Etymology

From Middle English happili (by chance, by luck, by fortune), from happy + -ly.
