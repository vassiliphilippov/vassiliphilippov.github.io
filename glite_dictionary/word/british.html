British

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɪt̬.ɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪt.ɪʃ / (BrE)
of or relating to Great Britain, its people, or its culture.
relating to Great Britain or its people

Examples

She enjoys listening to classic British rock bands from the 1960s.
The new ambassador is a British diplomat with years of experience.
We tasted a variety of traditional British dishes during our trip.

Synset Examples

his wife is British

Related Terms

  • Synonym: English - relating to England, its people, or its language

Etymology

From Briton + -ish, referring to the people of Britain.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03033691-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪtɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtɪʃ / (BrE)
the people of Great Britain collectively, especially considered as a nation or ethnic group.
people of Great Britain

Examples

The British took great pride in their naval tradition during the 19th century.
Many British fought alongside their allies during World War II.
Traditional afternoon tea is an old custom enjoyed by the British.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: English - the people of England
  • Synonym: Scots - the people of Scotland
  • Synonym: Welsh - the people of Wales

Etymology

From Briton + -ish; related to Old English Bryttisc (relating to the Britons).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09751958-n
Synset POS: n
