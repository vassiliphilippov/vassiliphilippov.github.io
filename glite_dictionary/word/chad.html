Chad

1. NOUN

/ tʃæd / (AmE), / tʃad / (BrE)
a group of Afroasiatic tonal languages, mostly with two tones, spoken in regions west and south of Lake Chad in north central Africa
group of Afroasiatic languages of the Lake Chad region

Examples

Linguists have studied the structure of different Chad languages for decades.
The Chad languages are mainly tonal and belong to the Afroasiatic family.
Ethnographers traveled to communities where Chad languages are still widely spoken.

Etymology

Named after the Lake Chad region where these languages are spoken.

2. PROPN

/ tʃæd / (AmE), / tʃad / (BrE)
a large lake in north-central Africa, mainly located in Chad and partly in Nigeria, Cameroon, and Niger, fed primarily by the Shari River.
large lake in north-central Africa

Examples

Lake Chad has shrunk significantly over the last century due to climate change and water use.
Many fishing communities rely on the resources provided by Chad.
The Shari River is the main water source feeding Lake Chad.

/ tʃæd / (AmE), / tʃad / (BrE)
