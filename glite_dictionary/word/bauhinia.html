Bauhinia

1. NOUN

/ baʊˈhɪniə / (AmE), / bɔːˈhɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical flowering plants known as orchid trees, with showy, often fragrant flowers, native to Asia, Africa, and the Americas.
a genus of tropical orchid trees

Examples

The Bauhinia tree is celebrated for its spectacular purple blossoms in spring.
Gardeners often plant Bauhinia for its ornamental value in tropical landscapes.
Hong Kong adopted the Bauhinia flower as a symbol on its regional flag.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid tree - a common name for Bauhinia species

Etymology

Named after Swiss botanists Johann and Gaspard Bauhin.

2. NOUN

/ baʊˈhɪniə / (AmE), / bɔːˈhɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical flowering trees and shrubs, commonly known as orchid trees, characterized by bilobed leaves and colorful, orchid-like flowers.
genus of tropical orchid trees or shrubs

Examples

The Bauhinia in our garden blooms with striking pink flowers every spring.
Many city streets are lined with Bauhinia trees for their attractive foliage and blossoms.
The leaves of the Bauhinia are often shaped like a butterfly or a heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid tree - a common name for trees in the genus Bauhinia

Etymology

Named after Swiss botanists Johann and Gaspard Bauhin.
