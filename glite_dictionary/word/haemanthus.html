Haemanthus

1. NOUN

/ hiˈmænθəs / (AmE), / hiˈmænθəs / (BrE)
a genus of African flowering plants in the amaryllis family, known as blood lilies and recognized for their bulbous roots and striking red or white flowers
genus of African blood lilies

Examples

Several species of Haemanthus are popular in ornamental gardens for their vibrant blooms.
The Haemanthus genus includes both evergreen and deciduous types adapted to different environments.
Botanists classified the blood lily under the genus Haemanthus due to its unique floral structure.

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + anthos (flower), referring to the blood-red flowers of some species.
