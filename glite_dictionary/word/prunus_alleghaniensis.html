Prunus alleghaniensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruː.nəs ˌæ.lə.ˌgeɪ.niˈɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈpruː.nəs ˌæl.ɪ.ˌɡæ.niˈen.sɪs / (BrE)
A species of wild plum native to the northeastern United States, bearing dark purple fruits with yellow flesh.
wild plum species of northeastern U.S.

Examples

The Prunus alleghaniensis produces small, edible fruits valued by both wildlife and people.
Hikers in Pennsylvania sometimes come across Prunus alleghaniensis growing wild along forest edges.
Due to habitat loss, some populations of Prunus alleghaniensis are considered rare in the wild.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Prunus' (plum), 'alleghaniensis' (of the Allegheny region).
