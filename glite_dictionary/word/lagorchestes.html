Lagorchestes

1. NOUN

/ ləˌɡɔrˈkɛstiːz / (AmE), / ləˌɡɔːˈkɛstiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small macropod marsupials known as hare wallabies, native to Australia.
genus of hare wallabies

Examples

The genus Lagorchestes includes several species of hare wallabies found in Australia.
Researchers are studying the habitat preferences of Lagorchestes to aid conservation efforts.
One member of Lagorchestes, the spectacled hare-wallaby, is recognized by its distinctive facial markings.

Etymology

From Greek 'lagos' (hare) and 'orchestes' (dancer).

2. NOUN

/ ləˌɡɔrˈkɛstiːz / (AmE), / ləˌɡɔːˈkɛstiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small, hare-like wallabies found in Australia, known as hare wallabies.
genus of hare wallabies

Examples

The endangered Lagorchestes species are primarily found in remote areas of Australia.
Scientists are studying Lagorchestes populations to help preserve their natural habitats.
Several attempts have been made to reintroduce Lagorchestes into regions where they once thrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hare wallaby - a small kangaroo-like marsupial native to Australia

Etymology

From Greek 'lagos' (hare) + 'orchestes' (dancer), referring to the animal's leaping movements.
