Pseudotsuga

1. NOUN

/ sjuːˌdoʊtˈsuːɡə / (AmE), / sjuːˌdəʊtˈsuːɡə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen coniferous trees commonly known as Douglas firs, closely related to the genera Larix and Cathaya, found mainly in North America and Asia.
genus of Douglas fir trees

Examples

The forest was dominated by ancient Pseudotsuga towering above the undergrowth.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Pseudotsuga in different climates.
Many lumber products are made from the durable wood of Pseudotsuga.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Douglas fir - common name for trees of the genus Pseudotsuga

Etymology

From Greek pseudēs (false) + tsouga, Japanese name for hemlock, referring to the mistaken identity of the tree.

2. NOUN

/ suː.dɑːtˈsuː.gə / (AmE), / sjuː.dɒtˈsuː.ɡə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen coniferous trees in the pine family, which includes the Douglas fir.
genus of trees including Douglas fir

Examples

The Pseudotsuga genus is best known for its Douglas fir species, widely used in timber production.
Forests dominated by Pseudotsuga are an important habitat for wildlife in western North America.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Pseudotsuga seedlings under different soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Douglas fir - a species of tree in the Pseudotsuga genus
  • Degree Lower: Larix - a related genus, larches
  • Degree Lower: Cathaya - a related conifer genus

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudes' (false) + 'tsuga' (Japanese for hemlock), indicating resemblance to hemlock trees.
