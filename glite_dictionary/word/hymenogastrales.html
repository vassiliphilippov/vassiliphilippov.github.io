Hymenogastrales

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪmɪnəˌɡæˈstreɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌhɪmɛnəʊˌɡæˈstreɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi in the class Gasteromycetes, characterized by a distinct basidiocarp with a fleshy or waxy gleba
order of fungi with fleshy or waxy basidiocarp

Examples

Many species within the Hymenogastrales produce mushrooms with a waxy or gelatinous texture.
Mycologists have debated the classification of the Hymenogastrales for decades.
The identification of new genera in the Hymenogastrales has expanded our understanding of fungal diversity.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek hymēn (membrane) + gastr- (stomach) + -ales (order suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪmɪnoʊˌɡæstəˈreɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌhɪmɪnəʊˌɡɑːstəˈreɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi in the class Gasteromycetes, characterized by a basidiocarp with a fleshy or waxy gleba
order of fungi in Gasteromycetes

Examples

The Hymenogastrales include several genera of fungi with subterranean fruiting bodies.
Mycologists often debate the precise classification of the Hymenogastrales.
Species within Hymenogastrales are found in a variety of forest ecosystems worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Gasteromycetes - a class of fungi including puffballs and related forms

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Hymenogaster' (a genus of fungi) and '-ales' (order suffix).
