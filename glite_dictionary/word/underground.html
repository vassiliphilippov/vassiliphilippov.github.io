underground

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndərˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
located or taking place beneath the surface of the ground
beneath the ground's surface

Examples

The workers spent hours repairing the underground pipes.
London has an extensive network of underground tunnels.
They stored the vegetables in an underground cellar to keep them cool.

Synset Examples

belowground storage areas
underground caverns

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aboveground - located above the ground

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌndərˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈʌndəɡraʊnd / (BrE)
a secret organization working to resist or overthrow a government or occupying power, often operating covertly
secret resistance group

Examples

During the war, the French underground played a crucial role in sabotaging enemy operations.
She joined the underground to fight against the oppressive regime.
The underground distributed leaflets urging people to resist the occupation forces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resistance - organized opposition to an occupying force or government
  • Synonym: insurgency - an organized rebellion against authority
Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.dɚˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dəˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
an electric railway system operating below the surface of the ground, especially in a city; known as the subway in American English
city railway running below ground
british

Examples

We took the underground to get across London during rush hour.
The underground was especially crowded this morning due to train delays.
Tourists often use the underground to reach famous attractions in the city.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subway - underground city railway (AmE)

(of activities or organizations) conducted in secret, especially in order to avoid detection by authorities or opponents.
secret; hidden from authorities

Examples

The group operated an underground newspaper to spread dissenting views.
Many artists were involved in the underground music scene during the 1970s.
She was a member of the underground resistance during the occupation.
