inheritress

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhɛrɪtrəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪtrɪs / (BrE)
a female who inherits property, title, or rights; a female heir
female heir
archaic

Examples

As the only inheritress, she became the owner of the entire estate.
The novel's protagonist is an inheritress of great fortune and responsibility.
Upon her father's death, she was recognized as the rightful inheritress of the title.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heir - a person legally entitled to the property or rank of another on that person's death

Etymology

Formed from 'inherit' with the feminine suffix '-ess.'
