Drosophyllum lusitanicum

1. NOUN

/ droʊˈsɑːfɪləm luːsɪˈteɪnɪkəm / (AmE), / drɒˈsɒfɪləm ljuːsɪˈteɪnɪkəm / (BrE)
a perennial carnivorous plant native to Portugal, southern Spain, and Morocco, with glandular leaves that trap and digest insects
carnivorous plant of southwestern Europe and Morocco

Examples

Drosophyllum lusitanicum thrives in dry, sandy soils and can survive long periods of drought.
Unlike most carnivorous plants, Drosophyllum lusitanicum prefers full sunlight and arid conditions.
Botanists are studying Drosophyllum lusitanicum to understand how its sticky leaves digest insects.

Etymology

From Greek drósos (dew) and phýllon (leaf), Latinized; lusitanicum from Latin Lusitania (ancient name for Portugal).
