dispute

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspjut / (AmE), / dɪˈspjuːt / (BrE)
A serious argument or disagreement, especially one that lasts for a long time or between groups or organizations.
serious argument or disagreement

Examples

The two companies are involved in a legal dispute over the rights to the patent.
His contract renewal has become the subject of much dispute among the board members.
The border dispute between the neighboring countries has lasted for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument, typically a protracted one
  • Antonym: agreement - harmony or accordance in opinion or feeling

Etymology

From Old French *disputer*, from Latin *disputare* (to discuss, examine).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈspjut / (AmE), / dɪˈspjuːt / (BrE)
A disagreement or argument, especially about something important.
a disagreement or argument

Examples

The neighbors had a long-standing dispute over the boundary line between their properties.
The union entered into a dispute with management over pay and working conditions.
Their bitter dispute eventually led to a lawsuit.

Synset Examples

he had a dispute with his wife
there were irreconcilable differences
the familiar conflict between Republicans and Democrats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argument - an exchange of diverging or opposite views, often heated
  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or clash
  • Antonym: agreement - harmony or consensus in opinion or feeling

Etymology

From Old French *desputer* and Latin *disputare* (to discuss, examine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07196595-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ dɪˈspjut / (AmE), / dɪˈspjuːt / (BrE)
to argue or have a disagreement about something; to question whether something is true or valid
to argue or disagree

Examples

The neighbors continue to dispute the boundary between their properties.
Historians often dispute who was responsible for key decisions during the war.
The company plans to dispute the charges in court.

Synset Examples

We quarreled over the question as to who discovered America
These two fellows are always scrapping over something

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to argue - to express opposite views in a discussion or fight
  • Antonym: to agree - to share the same opinion or come to a mutual decision

Etymology

From Old French *disputer*, from Latin *disputare* (to discuss, argue).

4. VERB

/ dɪˈspjut / (AmE), / dɪˈspjuːt / (BrE)
to question the truth or validity of something; to argue against or express doubt about a statement, claim, or decision
question or argue against something

Examples

Several scientists dispute the findings of the published report.
You are free to dispute any charges you believe are incorrect.
He does not dispute that mistakes were made, but argues they were unintentional.

Synset Examples

She challenged his claims

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge - to question the validity or authority of something
  • Antonym: to accept - to agree to or not dispute something

Etymology

From Latin 'disputare' (to discuss, to argue).
