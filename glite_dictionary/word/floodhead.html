floodhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʌdˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈflʌdˌhɛd / (BrE)
the leading wave or mass of water moving at the front of a flood
front wall of water in a flood

Examples

The floodhead crashed against the riverbank, uprooting several trees in its path.
Residents were warned to evacuate before the floodhead reached their community.
We watched anxiously as the floodhead surged toward the low-lying fields.

Synset Examples

we were lucky to be safe when the floodheads hit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floodwave - a large wave or surge of water during a flood
