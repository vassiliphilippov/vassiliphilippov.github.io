matrix

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.trɪks / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.trɪks / (BrE)
the material or substance in an organism in which tissue cells are embedded
substance in which tissue cells are embedded

Examples

Collagen fibers are found within the matrix of connective tissue.
The extracellular matrix provides structural support to surrounding cells.
Bone strength depends in large part on the composition of its matrix.

Etymology

From Latin matrix (breeding female, womb), from mater (mother).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.trɪks / (AmE), / ˈmætrɪks / (BrE)
A mold or form used in the production of phonograph records, printing type, or other objects with a relief surface.
mold for records or printing type

Examples

The master matrix is used to press hundreds of identical phonograph records.
Type is cast from a matrix to ensure precise letter shapes and sizes.
Each printing plate was made by pouring metal into a matrix created by the engraver.
