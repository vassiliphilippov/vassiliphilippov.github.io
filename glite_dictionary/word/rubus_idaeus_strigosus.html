Rubus idaeus strigosus

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəs aɪˈdeɪ.i.əs strɪˈɡoʊsəs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs ɪˈdaɪ.əs strɪˈɡəʊsəs / (BrE)
a North American variety of the red raspberry plant, recognized for its edible red fruit
North American red raspberry variety

Examples

Many wild Rubus idaeus strigosus bushes can be found growing along forest edges in Canada.
The fruit of Rubus idaeus strigosus is prized for its sweet flavor and is commonly used in jams.
Botanists differentiate Rubus idaeus strigosus from its European relative based on its leaf and stem characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red raspberry - the European or common red raspberry plant

Etymology

From Latin: Rubus (bramble), idaeus (of Mount Ida), strigosus (having stiff hairs).
