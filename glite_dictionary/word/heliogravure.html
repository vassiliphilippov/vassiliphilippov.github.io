heliogravure

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːlioʊˈɡrævjʊr / (AmE), / ˌhiːliəʊˈɡrævjʊə / (BrE)
an intaglio printmaking process in which a photographic image is transferred to a metal plate and then printed; also, a print made by this process
intaglio printmaking process using photographs

Examples

The artist created a stunning heliogravure of the city skyline.
Collectors often prize antique heliogravure prints for their detail and tone.
He studied traditional heliogravure techniques at an art institute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photogravure - an intaglio printmaking process that uses photographic techniques

Etymology

From French 'héliogravure', from Greek 'hēlios' (sun) and French 'gravure' (engraving).
