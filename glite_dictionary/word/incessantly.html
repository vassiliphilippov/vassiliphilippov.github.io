incessantly

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈsɛsəntli / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛsəntli / (BrE)
without stopping; in a continual or unending manner.
without stopping; continually

Examples

She talked incessantly during the long train ride, never pausing for a moment.
The rain poured incessantly throughout the night, causing the streets to flood.
He worked incessantly to complete the project before the deadline.

Synset Examples

dance inspires him ceaselessly to strive higher and higher toward the shining pinnacle of perfection that is the goal of every artiste

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceaselessly - without stopping or pausing; continually
  • Synonym: constantly - happening all the time or very often
  • Antonym: intermittently - occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous

Etymology

From Latin 'incessans' (unceasing), from 'in-' (not) + 'cessare' (to cease).

2. ADV

/ ɪnˈsɛs.ənt.li / (AmE), / ɪnˈses.ənt.li / (BrE)
in a way that continues without stopping; constantly or ceaselessly
without stopping; ceaselessly

Examples

The rain fell incessantly throughout the night, flooding the streets and fields.
She was incessantly checking her phone for new messages while waiting at the airport.
The workers complained about the machines running incessantly, making it difficult to concentrate.

Synset Examples

the world is constantly changing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constantly - happening all the time or very often over a period of time
  • Synonym: continually - repeatedly, frequently, or without interruption
  • Antonym: occasionally - not happening regularly or all the time

Etymology

From Latin 'incessans, incessantis' (not ceasing, continuous) + English '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00020735-r
Synset POS: r
