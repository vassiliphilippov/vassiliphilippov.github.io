Amun

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑː.mʊn / (AmE), / ˈɑː.mʊn / (BrE)
an ancient Egyptian god of air and breath, worshipped especially at Thebes, often considered a primeval deity.
ancient Egyptian god of air and breath

Examples

The temple at Karnak was dedicated to Amun, the chief deity of Thebes.
In ancient mythology, Amun was often merged with the sun god Ra to become Amun-Ra.
Many pharaohs considered themselves sons of Amun and built monuments in his honor.

Etymology

From ancient Egyptian ‘Imn (Amun, Amen), attested in Greek as Ammon.
