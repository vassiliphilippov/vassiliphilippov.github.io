grappling

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræplɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡræplɪŋ / (BrE)
A sport or form of hand-to-hand combat in which unarmed opponents try to seize, hold, or throw each other down, typically without striking.
a sport of hand-to-hand wrestling

Examples

He began training in grappling to improve his self-defense skills.
Many martial arts tournaments now include grappling events alongside striking disciplines.
Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu is a popular form of grappling practiced worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrestling - a sport or physical contest in which two unarmed individuals grapple and try to pin each other to the mat

Etymology

From the verb 'grapple', from Old French 'grapil' meaning 'hook', referring to seizing or holding.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡræplɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡraplɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of engaging in close hand-to-hand combat, often involving holding or wrestling with an opponent
close hand-to-hand combat or wrestling

Examples

The two opponents engaged in intense grappling throughout the match.
He has years of experience in various forms of grappling, including judo and Brazilian jiu-jitsu.
After several minutes of grappling, the fighters were finally separated by the referee.

Synset Examples

they had a fierce wrestle
we watched his grappling and wrestling with the bully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrestling - the sport or activity of grappling with an opponent
  • Antonym: boxing - a combat sport involving striking rather than grappling

Etymology

From grapple + -ing; ultimately from Old French *grapiller* (to seize).
