overage

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊvərˌeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvərɪdʒ / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) too old to be useful, suitable, or allowed for a particular purpose.
too old to be useful or suitable

Examples

Some teams are disqualified for including overage players in the tournament.
The factory replaced all its overage equipment with modern machinery.
Because he was considered overage, he was no longer eligible for the youth program.

Synset Examples

He left the house … for the support of twelve superannuated wool carders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superannuated - too old for work or use; retired because of age
  • Antonym: underage - too young to be allowed by law or rules

Etymology

Formed from 'over' (beyond) and 'age', first recorded in early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvərɪdʒ / (BrE)
a surplus amount of money or goods that is present and exceeds what was expected or recorded
excess of money or merchandise

Examples

The accountant found an overage in the cash register at the end of the day.
Inventory overage can indicate errors in stocking or record-keeping.
They were pleased to discover a small overage after completing the year-end inventory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shortage - an amount by which something is less than expected or needed
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
