Certhiidae

1. NOUN

/ sərˈθɪɪˌdiː / (AmE), / səˈθɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, slender, tree-climbing birds commonly known as creepers, found in Eurasia and North America.
family of creeper birds

Examples

Many ornithologists specialize in studying Certhiidae due to their unique climbing behavior.
The Certhiidae are known for their brown plumage and slender curved bills.
Several species within the Certhiidae family inhabit temperate forests around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creeper - common name for small birds in the Certhiidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Certhiidae, based on the genus Certhia, from Ancient Greek kerthios, a type of small bird.
