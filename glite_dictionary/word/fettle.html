fettle

1. VERB

/ ˈfɛtəl / (AmE), / ˈfɛtəl / (BrE)
To remove mold marks or sand from a casting, especially in foundry work.
remove mold marks or sand from metal casting

Examples

The worker carefully fettled the iron piece to smooth out rough edges.
After casting, each part must be fettled to remove any excess sand.
She fettled the moldings before sending them off for inspection.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛtəl / (AmE), / ˈfɛtəl / (BrE)
a state or condition of health, fitness, or form, especially good health
state of health or fitness

Examples

After a good night's sleep, she woke up in fettle and ready for the day.
The team is in fine fettle this season, winning most of their matches.
Regular exercise helps keep you in peak fettle.

  • Synonym: form - physical or mental state, especially in comparison to normal or optimal state

Etymology

Probably from Old English 'fetel' (belt, girdle), referring to one's bearing or readiness.
