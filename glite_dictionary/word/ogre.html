ogre

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊɡər / (AmE), / ˈəʊɡə / (BrE)
(in folklore) a large, hideous, man-eating giant, often found in fairy tales and legends.
man-eating giant from folklore

Examples

The fairytale described an ogre who lived under the bridge and terrified travelers.
Children were warned not to stray into the woods for fear of being caught by an ogre.
The movie's main villain is a towering ogre who eats anyone who comes near his lair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - a being of great size and strength in legends and folklore
  • Synonym: troll - a mythical, cave-dwelling being often depicted as large and ugly

Etymology

From French 'ogre', possibly via Italian 'orco' (demon, monster), ultimately from Latin 'Orcus' (god of the underworld).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊɡər / (AmE), / ˈəʊɡə / (BrE)
a person regarded as cruel, wicked, and inhuman; someone who inspires fear or terror due to their behavior.
cruel or wicked person

Examples

Despite his reputation as an ogre, the manager was surprisingly fair to his staff.
The children described their strict teacher as an ogre after he gave them extra homework.
She realized her father was not the ogre she had imagined, but a caring and misunderstood man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monster - a cruel or inhuman person; a wicked or frightening being
  • Synonym: tyrant - a cruel and oppressive ruler
  • Antonym: angel - a kind or benevolent person

Etymology

From French 'ogre', possibly from Italian 'orco' (man-eating giant), ultimately from Latin 'Orcus' (god of the underworld).

3. NOUN

/ ˈoʊɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊɡə / (BrE)
a very grumpy, unpleasant, or frightening person, often used figuratively
a very grumpy or unpleasant person

Examples

When he hasn't had his coffee, he can be a real ogre in the mornings.
No one likes working with an ogre who constantly criticizes everything.
She turned into an ogre when things didn't go according to her plan.

Synset Examples

He’s an ogre when he doesn't get enough sleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tyrant - a cruel and oppressive person
  • Antonym: angel - a kind and pleasant person
