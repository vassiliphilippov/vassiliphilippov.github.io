anapaestic

1. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈpistɪk / (AmE), / ˌanəˈpiːstɪk / (BrE)
(of a metrical foot) consisting of two short or unstressed syllables followed by one long or stressed syllable.
poetic metrical foot: two short, one long

Examples

The poet chose an anapaestic rhythm to create a light, galloping effect in the verse.
Lines written in anapaestic meter often sound playful and energetic.
Many children’s poems use anapaestic foot for a sing-song quality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dactylic - (of a metrical foot) one long or stressed syllable followed by two short or unstressed syllables

Etymology

From Greek anapaistos, meaning ‘struck back’ or ‘reversed’, referring to its reversed dactylic structure.
