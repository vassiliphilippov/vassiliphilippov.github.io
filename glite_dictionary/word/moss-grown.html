moss-grown

1. ADJ

/ mɑs ˈgroʊn / (AmE), / mɒs ˈgrəʊn / (BrE)
Covered or overgrown with moss.
covered with moss

Examples

The ancient stone wall was moss-grown and crumbling in places.
A narrow moss-grown path wound its way through the dense forest.
Rain had left the roof tiles slippery and moss-grown after years of neglect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mossy - covered with or resembling moss

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɑs ˌɡroʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɒs ˌɡrəʊn / (BrE)
(pejorative) Old-fashioned or outdated; no longer current or fashionable.
old-fashioned; outdated (pejorative)

Examples

The committee was full of moss-grown attitudes toward technology and change.
Her moss-grown opinions did not resonate with the younger generation.
The novel criticizes the moss-grown traditions that still dominate the village.

Synset Examples

moss-grown ideas about family life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiquated - old-fashioned; outmoded
  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times

Etymology

Formed from "moss" (a type of small plant) + "grown" (past participle of "grow"), figuratively referring to something neglected or antiquated as if it had grown moss.
