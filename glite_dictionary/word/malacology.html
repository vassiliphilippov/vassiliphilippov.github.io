malacology

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæləˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌmaləˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of zoology concerned with the study of mollusks
study of mollusks in zoology

Examples

She decided to specialize in malacology after developing an interest in sea snails.
The museum's collection is valuable for researchers in malacology and marine biology.
Advances in malacology have greatly increased our understanding of mollusk diversity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: zoology - the scientific study of animals
  • Degree Lower: conchology - the branch of zoology dealing with shells of mollusks

Etymology

From French 'malacologie', from Greek 'malakos' (soft) + '-logy' (study of).
