Daubentonia

1. PROPN

/ dɔːbɛnˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / dɔːbɛnˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of lemur-like primates, represented by the aye-aye, and the only genus in the family Daubentoniidae.
genus of primates (aye-aye)

Examples

The Daubentonia is notable for its unusual method of finding food.
Scientists have studied Daubentonia to understand the evolution of primates in Madagascar.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the endangered Daubentonia population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aye-aye - a lemur-like primate of Madagascar, the only species in the genus Daubentonia

Etymology

Named after the French naturalist Louis-Jean-Marie Daubenton (+ New Latin suffix -ia).

2. NOUN

/ dɔːbɛnˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / dɔːbɛnˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of lemurs comprising the aye-aye, native to Madagascar, and serving as the type genus of the family Daubentoniidae.
genus of lemurs (aye-aye)

Examples

The Daubentonia is best known for its only extant species, the aye-aye.
Scientists have classified the aye-aye within the genus Daubentonia.
Evidence suggests that members of the Daubentonia genus are unique to Madagascar.

Etymology

Named after French naturalist Louis-Jean-Marie Daubenton (1716–1800).
