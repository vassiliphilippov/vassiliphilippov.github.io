feature

1. VERB

/ ˈfiːtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈfiːtʃə / (BrE)
to have or include as a prominent part or special attraction
to include as a prominent part

Examples

The new museum features interactive exhibits for children and adults alike.
Tonight's concert will feature several up-and-coming artists from the local area.
The film festival features works by directors from around the world.

Synset Examples

This restaurant features the most famous chefs in France

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to include - to contain as part of the whole
  • Antonym: to exclude - to not include something
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈfiːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈfiːtʃə / (BrE)
a distinctive part of someone's face, such as the eyes, nose, mouth, or chin.
part of a person's face

Examples

Her delicate features gave her a striking appearance.
The artist expertly captured the features of his subject's face.
Strong features often run in their family.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lineament - a distinctive shape, contour, or line, especially of the face
  • Synonym: aspect - the appearance of a person's face expressing a particular emotion

Etymology

From Old French 'faiture', from Latin 'factura' (a making, formation).
