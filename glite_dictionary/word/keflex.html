Keflex

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːˌflɛks / (AmE), / ˈkiːˌflɛks / (BrE)
A trademarked brand name for the antibiotic cephalexin, used to treat mild to moderately severe bacterial infections of the skin, ears, throat, lungs, or urinary tract.
antibiotic brand (cephalexin)
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed Keflex to treat my sinus infection.
The pharmacist explained that Keflex should be taken with a full glass of water.
Some patients may experience mild side effects while taking Keflex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalexin - a cephalosporin antibiotic used to treat bacterial infections
