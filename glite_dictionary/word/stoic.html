stoic

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˈstəʊ.ɪk / (BrE)
a person who shows little or no emotion, especially when enduring pain or hardship
person who is indifferent to emotions

Examples

Despite the chaos around him, the stoic remained calm and collected.
She faced the difficult news like a true stoic, without shedding a tear.
The stoic in our group always handles stress better than anyone else.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emotionless - lacking emotion or feeling
  • Antonym: emotional - showing strong feelings

Etymology

From Greek 'Stōïkos', relating to the Stoic school of philosophy founded by Zeno in Athens.

2. ADJ

/ ˈstoʊɪk / (AmE), / ˈstəʊɪk / (BrE)
enduring pain or hardship without showing feelings or complaint; appearing unaffected by pleasure or pain
showing no visible reaction to hardship or pain

Examples

Despite the bad news, she remained stoic and did not shed a single tear.
He showed stoic endurance while facing the difficult challenges at work.
Her stoic patience inspired her friends during the long wait for results.

Synset Examples

stoic courage
stoic patience
a stoical sufferer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impassive - showing no emotion; not affected by pain or pleasure
  • Antonym: emotional - showing strong feelings or emotions

Etymology

From Greek 'Stōikos', relating to the Stoics, a school of philosophy advocating self-control and fortitude.
