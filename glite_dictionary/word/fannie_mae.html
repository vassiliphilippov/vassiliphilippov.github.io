Fannie Mae

1. PROPN

/ ˈfæni ˌmeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfæni ˌmeɪ / (BrE)
a federally chartered corporation in the United States that buys and guarantees mortgages, providing liquidity to the mortgage market
US government-sponsored mortgage corporation
american

Examples

Fannie Mae was established to expand the flow of mortgage money to homebuyers in the United States.
The financial crisis of 2008 had a significant impact on Fannie Mae's operations and stability.
Many lenders sell their home loans to Fannie Mae to free up capital for new mortgages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Freddie Mac - a US government-sponsored corporation that buys and securitizes mortgages

Etymology

From the acronym of Federal National Mortgage Association (FNMA), with the phonetic rendering 'Fannie Mae'.
