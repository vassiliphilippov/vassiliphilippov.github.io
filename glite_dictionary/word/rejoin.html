to rejoin

1. VERB

/ riˈdʒɔɪn / (AmE), / riˈdʒɔɪn / (BrE)
to join something or someone again after being separated.
to join again after separation

Examples

He plans to rejoin the team after recovering from his injury.
After years abroad, she finally decided to rejoin her family.
The rivers rejoin downstream to form a single larger flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: join - to connect or bring together
  • Antonym: leave - to go away from or cease to be part of something

Etymology

From re- (again) + join, from Latin iungere (to join).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈdʒɔɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɔɪn / (BrE)
to reply or respond quickly, especially in a sharp or witty manner
respond quickly or sharply

Examples

“That isn't fair,” she rejoined with a hint of irritation in her voice.
He paused for a moment, then rejoined with a clever remark.
When challenged, the professor rejoined confidently, leaving no room for doubt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retort - to reply in a quick, sharp, or witty way

Etymology

From Old French *rejoindre* (to answer back), from Latin *re-* (again) + *iungere* (to join).
