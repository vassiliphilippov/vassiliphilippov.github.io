Allium carinatum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæli.əm ˌkɛr.ɪˈneɪ.təm / (AmE), / ˈæli.əm ˌkær.ɪˈneɪ.təm / (BrE)
A Eurasian bulbous plant of the onion genus, known for its small purple or pink flowers and edible bulb.
a Eurasian bulbous plant of the onion genus

Examples

The ornamental Allium carinatum adds a touch of color to many European gardens.
She planted Allium carinatum in her backyard for its decorative flowers and culinary use.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Allium carinatum in different soil types.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Allium' (garlic, onion), species 'carinatum' (keeled), referring to the shape of its parts.
