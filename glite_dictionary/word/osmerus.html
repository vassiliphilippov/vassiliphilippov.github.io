Osmerus

1. NOUN

/ ɑsˈmɛrəs / (AmE), / ɒsˈmɛːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of small fish in the family Osmeridae, commonly known as smelts, found in northern waters.
genus of smelt fish

Examples

The genus Osmerus includes several species of smelts found in cold northern waters.
Scientists collected samples of Osmerus from the river for their ecological study.
Fishermen often catch Osmerus smelts during the spring spawning season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smelt - a small silvery fish found in northern waters, related to Osmerus

Etymology

From Greek 'osméros', meaning 'smelling' (referring to the distinctive odor of smelts).

2. NOUN

/ ɑːsˈmɛrəs / (AmE), / ɒsˈmɛərəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the smelt family Osmeridae, comprising small fish found in northern waters.
type genus of smelt fish (Osmeridae)

Examples

The genus Osmerus is well known for its importance in northern fisheries.
Members of Osmerus are typically small, silvery fish found in cold-water habitats.
Researchers studied the reproductive habits of several Osmerus species in Europe.

Etymology

From scientific Latin Osmerus, the genus name for smelt.
