whirl

1. NOUN

/ wɝl / (AmE), / wɜːl / (BrE)
a rapidly rotating or spiraling shape or movement
spinning or spiraling shape

Examples

The dancer's skirts spun out in a colorful whirl on stage.
A sudden whirl of autumn leaves blew across the empty street.
In the photograph, her hair appeared to be caught in a whirl of motion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eddy - a circular movement of water, air, or smoke
  • Synonym: swirl - a twisting or spiraling shape or movement
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13900751-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ wɜrl / (AmE), / wɜːl / (BrE)
to cause something to spin or move rapidly in a circular motion
cause to spin or rotate

Examples

She used her fingers to whirl the pencil on the table.
The magician whirled the coins in his palm before making them disappear.
You can whirl the spinner to start the game.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spin - to turn around and around quickly
  • Synonym: twirl - to turn something repeatedly around or quickly in a circle

Etymology

From Middle English 'whirlen', frequentative of 'whir', of imitative origin.
