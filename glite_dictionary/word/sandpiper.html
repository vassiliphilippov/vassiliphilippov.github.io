sandpiper

1. NOUN

/ ˈsænˌdaɪpər / (AmE), / ˈsændˌpaɪpə / (BrE)
A small or medium-sized wading bird with a slender bill and a piping call, found chiefly along shorelines and closely related to plovers.
small or medium wading bird

Examples

A group of sandpipers darted along the shoreline, searching for food in the wet sand.
The sandpiper is easily recognized by its piping call and slender bill.
During migration, large flocks of sandpipers can be seen resting on mudflats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plover - wading bird closely related to sandpipers

Etymology

From sand + piper, referring to its habitat and piping call.
