char

1. NOUN

/ tʃɑr / (AmE), / tʃɑː / (BrE)
a small, trout-like freshwater fish of the genus Salvelinus, found chiefly in cold northern waters
a small trout-like freshwater fish

Examples

The Arctic char is prized for its delicate flavor and pink flesh.
Several species of char inhabit the cold, clear lakes of Scandinavia.
He caught a beautiful char while fishing in the Scottish Highlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trout - a common name for fish of the family Salmonidae

Etymology

From Old Norse ‘tjár’, related to Icelandic ‘tjara’; ultimate origin uncertain.
