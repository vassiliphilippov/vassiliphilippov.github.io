bawler

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔlɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlə / (BrE)
a person who shouts or speaks in a loud, harsh voice
person who shouts loudly

Examples

The bawler at the back of the crowd was disturbing everyone with his constant yelling.
Don't be such a bawler; we're inside a library.
Even as a child, he was a notorious bawler at family gatherings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shouter - someone who speaks or calls out loudly
  • Antonym: whisperer - someone who speaks in a very soft or low voice

Etymology

From 'bawl' (to shout loudly) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔlɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlə / (BrE)
A person or animal who cries or weeps loudly and noisily.
a person who cries loudly

Examples

During the movie, the toddler became such a bawler that they had to leave early.
Everybody in the ward could hear the bawler in the next room.
He earned a reputation as a bawler every time things didn't go his way.

Synset Examples

their new baby is a real bawler

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crybaby - a person, especially a child, who cries or complains easily or often

Etymology

From 'bawl' (to cry loudly) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
