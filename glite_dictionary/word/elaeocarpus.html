Elaeocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌliː.oʊˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / ɪˌliː.əʊˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical and subtropical trees and shrubs, belonging to the family Elaeocarpaceae, known for their ornamental flowers and fruits
a genus of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The Elaeocarpus genus includes species native to Southeast Asia and Australia.
Some Elaeocarpus trees are cultivated for their attractive blue seeds.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Elaeocarpus genus in the tropical rainforest.

Etymology

From Greek elaia (olive) + karpos (fruit), referring to the appearance of some species' fruits.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌli.oʊˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / ɪˌliː.əʊˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical trees and shrubs that is the type genus of the family Elaeocarpaceae, characterized by evergreen leaves and often ornamental fruit
genus of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

Several species within the Elaeocarpus genus are valued for their decorative foliage and attractive berries.
Botanists collected samples of Elaeocarpus leaves in the rainforest for further study.
The distinctive seeds of Elaeocarpus trees are often used in traditional jewelry in some cultures.

Etymology

From Greek elaia (olive) + karpos (fruit), referring to the fruit of some species resembling olives.
