carthorse

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrt.hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈkɑːt.hɔːs / (BrE)
a large, strong horse bred or used for pulling carts, wagons, or other heavy loads
a horse used for pulling carts

Examples

The old carthorse calmly pulled the heavy wagon down the muddy lane.
For generations, farmers relied on the strength of a sturdy carthorse for their daily work.
She admired the power and patience of the carthorse as it hauled the timber from the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draft horse - a large, heavy horse bred for hard labor such as pulling heavy loads
  • Antonym: racehorse - a horse bred and trained for racing

Etymology

Compound of 'cart' and 'horse'.
