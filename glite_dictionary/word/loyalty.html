loyalty

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔɪəlti / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪəlti / (BrE)
a strong feeling of support or allegiance to someone or something, or the quality of being loyal
strong feeling of support or allegiance

Examples

Her loyalty to her friends was clear in everything she did.
Many employees value loyalty to their company above higher pay elsewhere.
The team's loyalty to their coach never wavered, even in tough times.

Synset Examples

his long commitment to public service
they felt no loyalty to a losing team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faithfulness - the quality of being faithful or loyal
  • Antonym: betrayal - the act of being disloyal or treacherous to someone or something

Etymology

From Old French *loiauté*, from Latin *legalitas* (legality, faithfulness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔɪəlti / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪəlti / (BrE)
the quality of being faithful and devoted to a person, organization, or cause
faithfulness or allegiance

Examples

Her loyalty to her friends was never in doubt, even during difficult times.
Employees are expected to show loyalty to the company and its values.
He demonstrated his loyalty by supporting the team through every loss and victory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faithfulness - the quality of being faithful
  • Antonym: betrayal - the act of being disloyal or unfaithful

Etymology

From Old French 'loiauté', based on Latin 'legalis' (legal).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɔɪəlti / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪəlti / (BrE)
the quality of being faithful and devoted to a person, organization, cause, or duty
faithfulness or devotion

Examples

She valued her friend's loyalty above all other qualities.
The team's loyalty to their coach was unwavering despite the losses.
He showed great loyalty to his company, staying even during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegiance - loyalty or commitment to a group or cause
  • Synonym: fidelity - faithfulness to a person, cause, or belief
  • Antonym: betrayal - the act of being disloyal or treacherous

Etymology

From Old French 'loiauté', from 'loial' (loyal).
