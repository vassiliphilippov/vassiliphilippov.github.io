-looking

1. ADJ

/ ˈlʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
appearing to be as specified; used with adjectives or nouns in combinations to describe someone's or something's appearance.
appearing in a certain way (combining form)

Examples

The child wore a happy-looking hat decorated with bright feathers.
We met several serious-looking officials at the entrance.
Don't trust that suspicious-looking package lying in the hallway.

Synset Examples

left their clothes dirty looking
a most disagreeable looking character
angry-looking
liquid-looking
severe-looking policemen on noble horses
fine-sounding phrases
taken in by high-sounding talk

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'look' + the suffix '-ing', used as a combining form.
