hearth

1. NOUN

/ hɑrθ / (AmE), / hɑːθ / (BrE)
A brick or stone-lined area in a home at the base of a fireplace, where a fire is made.
area at base of a fireplace

Examples

On cold winter evenings, the family gathered around the hearth to enjoy its warmth.
She cleaned the ashes from the hearth before lighting a new fire.
A decorative rug was placed in front of the hearth to add color to the room.

Synset Examples

the fireplace was so large you could walk inside it
he laid a fire in the hearth and lit it
the hearth was black with the charcoal of many fires

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fireplace - a structure for holding a domestic fire, typically framed in brick or stone

Etymology

From Old English *heorþ* (hearth, fireplace).

2. NOUN

/ hɑrθ / (AmE), / hɑːθ / (BrE)
(literary) The floor of a fireplace, or, figuratively, a person's home and family life.
the home or family life (figurative)

Examples

After a long journey, he was finally back at his own hearth and among his family.
Writers often speak of the hearth as the heart of the home, a place of warmth and comfort.
Many soldiers yearned for the comfort of their own hearth during the war.

Synset Examples

driven from hearth and home
fighting in defense of their firesides

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fireside - the part of a room near the fireplace, often used figuratively for home life

Etymology

Old English *heorþ*; related to Dutch *haard* and German *Herd*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03512385-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ hɑːrθ / (AmE), / hɑːθ / (BrE)
the floor or area in front of a fireplace, usually made of stone or brick, extending out into a room
area in front of a fireplace

Examples

They placed the kettle on the hearth to keep it warm.
Children gathered around the hearth to listen to stories on cold evenings.
Ashes from last night's fire still covered the hearth.

Synset Examples

they sat on the hearth and warmed themselves before the fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fireplace - an open space in a wall for a fire in a room, often with a chimney

Etymology

From Old English heorð, related to Old High German herd (hearth, fireplace).
