galop

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlɑp / (AmE), / ˈɡælɒp / (BrE)
A lively piece of music composed to accompany the galop, a spirited ballroom dance in duple time.
lively dance music in duple time

Examples

The orchestra played a lively galop to close the evening's ball.
Johann Strauss II composed several famous galops in the 19th century.
The dancers requested a fast-paced galop after the waltz set.

Etymology

From French 'galop' (gallop), named after the fast pace of the dance.
