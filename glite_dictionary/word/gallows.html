gallows

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæloʊz / (AmE), / ˈɡaləʊz / (BrE)
a wooden structure used for executing people by hanging
structure for execution by hanging

Examples

The condemned man stood silently beneath the gallows as the crowd watched.
In the old days, criminals were executed on the town gallows.
The gallows cast a long shadow over the empty courtyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scaffold - a raised platform used for public executions

Etymology

From Old English *galga* (gallows, cross), related to Old Norse *galgi*.
