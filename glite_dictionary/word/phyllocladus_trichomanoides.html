Phyllocladus trichomanoides

1. NOUN

/ fɪl.oʊˈkleɪ.dəs traɪˌkoʊ.məˈnɔɪ.diːz / (AmE), / fɪl.əʊˈklæd.əs trɪˌkɒ.məˈnɔɪ.diːz / (BrE)
A species of medium-tall evergreen tree native to New Zealand, also known as the celery pine.
a New Zealand evergreen tree (celery pine)

Examples

The forest was dominated by Phyllocladus trichomanoides and other native conifers.
Botanists recorded several mature Phyllocladus trichomanoides in the protected reserve.
The wood of Phyllocladus trichomanoides is valued for its durability and pale color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celery pine - common name for Phyllocladus trichomanoides

Etymology

From Greek phyllon (leaf) + klados (branch), and Latin trichomanes (a kind of fern), with botanical Latin endings.
