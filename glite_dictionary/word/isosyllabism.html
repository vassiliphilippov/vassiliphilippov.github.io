isosyllabism

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsoʊˈsɪləbɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊˈsɪləbɪzəm / (BrE)
The practice or quality of using verse lines with the same number of syllables.
use of lines with equal syllables in verse

Examples

Classical French poetry often exhibits isosyllabism, requiring an exact count of syllables per line.
Some languages, like Serbian, favor isosyllabism in their traditional verse forms.
The poet abandoned isosyllabism to experiment with more irregular, free verse structures.

Synset Examples

But it is likewise true that — especially in the context of the eighteenth century — anapestic meter seemed somewhat flippant and irreverent in comparison with the staid isosyllabism of iambic pentameter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isosyllabic - Having lines or syllables equal in number, esp. in verse

Etymology

From iso- (equal) + syllable, with -ism; based on Greek roots.
