Mirabilis multiflora

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈræbɪlɪs ˌmʌltɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / mɪˈræbɪlɪs ˌmʌltɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A species of wildflower with deep pink tubular flowers that bloom in the evening, native to sandy and desert regions of southern California, southern Colorado, and Mexico.
species of wildflower with pink evening-blooming flowers

Examples

The Mirabilis multiflora adds a splash of color to the desert landscape each spring.
Botanists often study Mirabilis multiflora because of its unique evening blooming habits.
We spotted several Mirabilis multiflora growing along the sandy trail in New Mexico.

Etymology

From Latin: 'mirabilis' meaning 'wonderful' and 'multiflora' meaning 'many-flowered'.
