pre-Socratic

1. NOUN

/ pri səˈkrætɪk / (AmE), / pri səˈkrætɪk / (BrE)
any philosopher who lived before Socrates, especially those whose ideas influenced early Greek philosophy.
ancient philosopher before Socrates

Examples

Thales is often considered the first significant pre-Socratic thinker in Western philosophy.
The ideas of pre-Socratic philosophers laid the groundwork for later scientific inquiry.
A course on ancient philosophy typically begins with the pre-Socratics and their views of nature.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Socratic - relating to Socrates or his philosophy

Etymology

From prefix 'pre-' (before) and 'Socratic' (relating to Socrates, c. 470–399 BC).

2. ADJ

/ ˌpriː səˈkrætɪk / (AmE), / ˌpriː səˈkrætɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the period or philosophers before Socrates in ancient Greek history.
before Socrates; early Greek philosophy

Examples

The pre-Socratic philosophers laid the foundations for Western thought.
Much of pre-Socratic philosophy survives only in fragments and quotations.
Her research focuses on pre-Socratic theories of the natural world.

Etymology

From Greek 'pre' (before) and 'Socrates', an ancient Greek philosopher.
