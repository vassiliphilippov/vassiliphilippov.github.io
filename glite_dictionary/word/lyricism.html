lyricism

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪrɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪrɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
the quality of being suitable for singing, especially in expressing emotion through words or music
suitability for singing; emotional expressiveness

Examples

The lyricism of the poem made it feel as if it could be sung.
Many composers value lyricism in operatic arias above technical complexity.
Her writing is celebrated for its musical lyricism and emotional depth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poetry - literature in verse; poems collectively

Etymology

From French 'lyricisme', from 'lyrique' (lyric); ultimately from Greek 'lyrikos'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪrɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪrɪsɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
unrestrained and exaggerated enthusiasm, especially in expression or feeling
exaggerated enthusiasm or expression

Examples

The author's lyricism was evident in his passionate descriptions of childhood.
Her acceptance speech was marked by a lyricism that moved the entire audience.
There was a certain lyricism in the way he spoke about his dreams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment or interest

Etymology

From French 'lyricisme', ultimately from Greek 'lyrikos' (of the lyre).
