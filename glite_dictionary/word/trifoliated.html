trifoliated

1. ADJ

/ traɪˈfoʊliˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / traɪˈfəʊliˌeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a leaf) having three leaflets.
having three leaflets (botany)

Examples

The plant's trifoliated leaves help distinguish it from other species in the garden.
Clover is a common example of a trifoliated plant.
Many legumes have characteristic trifoliated foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trifoliate - (of a leaf) having three leaflets

Etymology

From Latin 'tri-' (three) and 'folium' (leaf).
