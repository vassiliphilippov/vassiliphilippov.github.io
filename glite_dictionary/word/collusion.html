collusion

1. NOUN

/ kəˈluʒən / (AmE), / kəˈluːʒən / (BrE)
secret or illegal cooperation or conspiracy, especially in order to cheat or deceive others.
secret cooperation for deceitful purposes

Examples

The companies were accused of collusion to fix prices in the marketplace.
There was clear evidence of collusion between the witness and the defendant.
Investigators uncovered collusion among several officials to manipulate the election results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conspiracy - a secret plan by a group to do something unlawful or harmful

Etymology

From Latin 'collusio' (a colluding), from 'colludere' (to play together, to collude).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈluʒən / (AmE), / kəˈluːʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
Secret or illegal cooperation or conspiracy, especially in order to cheat or deceive others.
secret or illegal cooperation

Examples

The company was accused of collusion with suppliers to fix prices.
There was evidence of collusion between the two firms during the bidding process.
Detectives uncovered collusion among several officials to commit fraud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conspiracy - a secret plan by a group to do something unlawful or harmful
  • Synonym: complicity - the state of being involved with others in an illegal activity

Etymology

From Latin collūsiō (a playing together, collusion), from collūdō (to play together, conspire), from com- (together) + lūdō (to play).
