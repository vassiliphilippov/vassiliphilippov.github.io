Lolium multiflorum

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈliːəm ˌmʌltɪˈflɔːrəm / (AmE), / ləʊˈliːəm ˌmʌltɪˈflɒrəm / (BrE)
A species of European grass, commonly known as Italian ryegrass, widely used for hay, turf, and as green manure.
species of European grass (Italian ryegrass)

Examples

Farmers often plant Lolium multiflorum for its rapid growth and high-quality hay production.
The golf course was seeded with Lolium multiflorum to create a lush, green turf.
Because of its adaptability, Lolium multiflorum is frequently used as a cover crop in rotation systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Italian ryegrass - Common name for Lolium multiflorum, a grass species used for hay, turf, and green manure

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lolium' (genus of ryegrass) and 'multiflorum' (many-flowered).
