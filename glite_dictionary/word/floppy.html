floppy

1. ADJ

/ ˈflɑːpi / (AmE), / ˈflɒpi / (BrE)
soft and hanging down loosely, often in a way that appears limp or flexible.
hanging loosely; limp

Examples

The dog's floppy ears bounced as it ran.
She wore a large, floppy hat to protect herself from the sun.
After being washed, the old stuffed rabbit became even more floppy.

Synset Examples

a spaniel with floppy ears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limp - not stiff or firm; hanging loosely

Etymology

Perhaps from 'flop' + '-y', patterned after similar adjectives ending in '-y'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02412942-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈflɑːpi / (AmE), / ˈflɒpi / (BrE)
a flat, flexible magnetic disk enclosed in a square or rectangular plastic shell, used for storing computer data, especially in the late 20th century
magnetic computer data storage disk

Examples

He found a box of old floppies in the back of the closet.
Many vintage computers still use a floppy to boot the operating system.
I saved my school project on a floppy and brought it to class.

Synset Examples

floppy disks are noted for their relatively slow speed and small capacity and low price

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floppy disk - the full form of 'floppy', a magnetic storage disk

Etymology

Shortened from 'floppy disk', referring to the flexible nature of early disks.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03214631-n
Synset POS: n
