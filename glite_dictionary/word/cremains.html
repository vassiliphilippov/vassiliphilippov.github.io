cremains

1. NOUN

/ krəˈmeɪnz / (AmE), / krɪˈmeɪnz / (BrE)
the ashes that remain after a dead body has been cremated
ashes after cremation

Examples

The family decided to scatter the cremains in their loved one's favorite place.
An urn was chosen to hold the cremains after the ceremony.
The law allows people to keep the cremains at home or in a memorial garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ashes - the powdery residue left after the combustion of a substance, especially a human body after cremation

Etymology

Blend of 'cremated' and 'remains', first used in the mid-20th century.
