second-rate

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛkəndˈreɪt / (AmE), / ˌsɛkəndˈreɪt / (BrE)
not of the best quality; moderate or inferior in standard or value
moderate or inferior in quality

Examples

The hotel was rather second-rate compared to the others we visited.
She refused to settle for a second-rate education for her children.
Despite its second-rate reputation, the restaurant was surprisingly busy.

Synset Examples

they improved the quality from mediocre to above average

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediocre - of only average quality; not very good
  • Synonym: inferior - lower in quality or value; less important
  • Antonym: first-rate - of the highest quality
  • Degree Lower: third-rate - of very poor quality

Etymology

From the practice of ranking people or things in 'rates,' with 'second-rate' signifying the next class below the best.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛkəndˈreɪt / (AmE), / ˌsɛkəndˈreɪt / (BrE)
of inferior or mediocre quality; not of the best standard
of inferior quality or standard

Examples

The critics dismissed the movie as a second-rate imitation of a classic film.
Despite its central location, the restaurant offered only second-rate service and food.
He refused to settle for a second-rate education when better options were available.

Synset Examples

Her performance was good, but not second-rate.
The hotel was second-rate at best, with poor service.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: first-rate - of the highest quality or standard
  • Degree Lower: third-rate - of very poor quality or standard
  • Synonym: mediocre - of only average or moderate quality

Etymology

From the ranking of goods, ships, or people into first, second, etc., as a measure of quality; 'second' plus 'rate'.
