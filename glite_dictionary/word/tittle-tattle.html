tittle-tattle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪt.əlˌtæt.əl / (AmE), / ˈtɪt.əlˌtæt.əl / (BrE)
casual or trivial chatter, often consisting of gossip or idle talk
gossip or trivial chat

Examples

The party was filled with harmless tittle-tattle about neighbors and old friends.
She avoided the office tittle-tattle, preferring to focus on her work.
Their afternoon tea always included plenty of tittle-tattle and laughter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - casual conversation or reports about other people, especially about their private affairs
  • Synonym: chatter - informal, light conversation; talk rapidly in a trivial way

Etymology

Reduplication of 'tattle', likely imitative in origin, dating from the late 16th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈtɪtəl ˌtætl̩ / (AmE), / ˈtɪtəl ˌtætl / (BrE)
to talk rapidly and incessantly about trivial or unimportant matters; to gossip or chatter aimlessly.
talk about trivial or trivial matters incessantly

Examples

The children tittle-tattled about their classmates all through recess.
Please stop tittle-tattling and tell me something important.
He tittle-tattles every time he visits the office, spreading the latest rumors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - talk about other people, often involving details that are not confirmed as true
  • Synonym: chatter - talk rapidly about trivial matters

Etymology

Reduplication of 'tittle', an old word meaning small mark or insignificant thing, imitating the sound or triviality of idle talk.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01038676-v
Synset POS: v
