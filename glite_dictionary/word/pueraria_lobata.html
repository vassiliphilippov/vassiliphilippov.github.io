Pueraria lobata

1. NOUN

/ puˈɛəriə ləˈbeɪtə / (AmE), / pjʊəˈrɑːrɪə ləˈbɑːtə / (BrE)
a fast-growing perennial vine native to eastern Asia, known for its invasive growth and tuberous roots; also called kudzu
invasive Asian climbing vine; kudzu

Examples

Pueraria lobata has become a notorious invasive species in the southern United States.
Farmers once planted Pueraria lobata to control soil erosion and provide livestock fodder.
The rapid spread of Pueraria lobata can displace native plant species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kudzu - common name for Pueraria lobata

Etymology

From the genus 'Pueraria', named after Swiss botanist Marc Nicolas Puerari, and Latin 'lobata' meaning lobed.
