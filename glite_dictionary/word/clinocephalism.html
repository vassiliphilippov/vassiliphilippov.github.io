clinocephalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌklaɪnəˈsɛfəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌklaɪnəʊˈsɛfəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A congenital condition in which the top of the head is abnormally depressed or concave instead of convex.
congenital defect with sunken top of head
medical

Examples

The pediatrician diagnosed the infant with clinocephalism after observing the unusual shape of her skull.
Researchers are studying genetic factors that may contribute to the development of clinocephalism in newborns.
Early intervention may help manage some of the complications associated with clinocephalism.

Etymology

From Greek 'klino-' (to slope or lean) and 'kephalē' (head).
