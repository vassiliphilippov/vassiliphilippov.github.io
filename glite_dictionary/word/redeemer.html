Redeemer

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdimər / (AmE), / rɪˈdiːmə / (BrE)
(in Christianity) Jesus Christ, regarded as the one who saves or delivers believers from sin.
Jesus Christ as savior in Christianity

Examples

Many Christians believe that Jesus is the Redeemer who brings salvation to humanity.
Hymns were sung in praise of the Redeemer during the Sunday service.
The stained glass depicted the Redeemer with outstretched arms blessing the congregation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Savior - a person who saves someone or something from danger; especially Jesus Christ

Etymology

From Latin 'redemptor', from 'redimere' meaning 'to buy back, redeem'.
