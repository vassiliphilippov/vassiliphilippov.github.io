Galeorhinus zyopterus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡeɪliəˈraɪnəs zaɪˈɒptərəs / (AmE), / ˌɡeɪliəˈraɪnəs zaɪˈɒptərəs / (BrE)
A species of Pacific shark valued for its fins, often used in Chinese cuisine, and for its vitamin A–rich liver.
species of Pacific shark

Examples

The Galeorhinus zyopterus is prized for its fins in some Asian cuisines.
Researchers have studied the Galeorhinus zyopterus for its high vitamin A content in the liver.
Due to overfishing, the population of Galeorhinus zyopterus has declined in recent years.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Galeorhinus' meaning 'shark nose' and 'zyopterus' referring to the fin structure.
