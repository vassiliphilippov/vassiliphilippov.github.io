Salvinia

1. NOUN

/ sælˈvɪniə / (AmE), / sælˈvɪniə / (BrE)
a genus of floating aquatic ferns found in freshwater environments, especially known for their rapid growth and sometimes invasive nature
genus of aquatic floating ferns

Examples

The pond was almost entirely covered by Salvinia after only a month.
Scientists are studying how Salvinia absorbs pollutants from contaminated water.
Some species of Salvinia can become invasive and disrupt local ecosystems.

Etymology

Named after Anton Maria Salvini (1633–1729), Italian scientist.

2. NOUN

/ sælˈvɪniə / (AmE), / salˈvɪnɪə / (BrE)
a genus of floating aquatic ferns in the family Salviniaceae, commonly called water ferns, found in tropical and subtropical regions
genus of floating water ferns

Examples

The invasive Salvinia has caused significant ecological changes in many freshwater habitats.
Researchers studied the growth habits of Salvinia under different environmental conditions.
A dense mat of Salvinia covered the surface of the pond, blocking sunlight from reaching other plants.

Etymology

Named after Anton Maria Salvini (1633–1729), Italian scientist.
