Domitian

1. PROPN

/ doʊˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / dəʊˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
(AD 51–96) Roman emperor, son of Vespasian and brother of Titus, who ruled from 81 to 96 and was known for his autocratic rule and eventual assassination.
Roman emperor (ruled 81–96)

Examples

Domitian ordered extensive construction projects, including the completion of the Colosseum in Rome.
The reign of Domitian was marked by increasing autocracy and paranoia among the ruling elite.
Historians debate whether Domitian was a cruel tyrant or an effective administrator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vespasian - Roman emperor, father of Domitian
  • Synonym: Titus - Roman emperor, brother of Domitian

Etymology

From Latin 'Domitianus', the emperor's name.

2. PROPN

/ doʊˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / dəˈmɪʃ(i)ən / (BrE)
Emperor of Rome from AD 81 to 96, son of Vespasian and brother of Titus, known for his autocratic rule and eventual assassination.
Roman emperor (AD 81–96)

Examples

The reign of Domitian was marked by increasing autocracy and suspicion.
Many historians debate whether Domitian was a cruel tyrant or a competent administrator.
Sculptures of Domitian can still be found in the ruins of ancient Rome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vespasian - Roman emperor, father of Domitian
  • Synonym: Titus - Roman emperor, brother of Domitian

Etymology

From Latin 'Domitianus', the cognomen of the emperor.
