caesarian

1. NOUN

/ siˈzɛriən / (AmE), / siˈzɛːriən / (BrE)
a surgical operation in which a baby is delivered through an incision in the mother's abdomen and uterus
surgical delivery of a baby
medical

Examples

After complications during labor, the doctors performed a caesarian to safely deliver the baby.
Her recovery from the caesarian took a few weeks, but both mother and child were healthy.
In some cases, a planned caesarian is recommended for medical reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caesarean section - the surgical operation to deliver a baby through the abdomen and uterus
  • Antonym: natural birth - childbirth through the vaginal canal without surgery

Etymology

From the belief that Julius Caesar was born by this method; from Latin 'Caesareus'.

2. ADJ

/ sɪˈzɛriən / (AmE), / siˈzɛːrɪən / (BrE)
Relating to a Caesarean section, a surgical operation for delivering a baby through an incision in the abdomen and uterus.
relating to Caesarean (abdominal) delivery
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a caesarian delivery due to complications during labor.
She was born by caesarian section after an unexpected medical emergency.
Many hospitals have seen an increase in caesarian births in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caesarean section - a surgical operation for delivering a baby through the abdomen

Etymology

From Latin 'Caesareus', traditionally linked to the birth of Julius Caesar by this procedure, though this is historically debated.
