Yucca carnerosana

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʌkə kɑːrˌnɛroʊˈsænə / (AmE), / ˈjʌkə kɑːˌnɛərəʊˈsɑːnə / (BrE)
a tree-like species of yucca native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, with sword-shaped leaves and white flowers
a tree-like yucca species with sword-shaped leaves

Examples

The Yucca carnerosana stands tall in the arid landscapes of northern Mexico.
Gardeners prize Yucca carnerosana for its dramatic, sword-shaped foliage and towering white flower spikes.
During spring, the flowers of the Yucca carnerosana attract numerous pollinators to the desert garden.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: yucca - a genus of perennial shrubs and trees in the family Asparagaceae

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Yucca' and the specific epithet 'carnerosana', likely referring to Sierra de Carneros in Mexico.
