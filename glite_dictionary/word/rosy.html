rosy

1. ADJ

/ ˈroʊzi / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzi / (BrE)
having a color resembling a pink or red blush; tinted with pink or red, especially suggesting health or vitality
pinkish or blush-colored

Examples

Her cheeks looked especially rosy after a brisk walk in the cold morning air.
The sky turned a rosy hue as the sun began to set over the hills.
The baby's rosy complexion indicated good health.

Synset Examples

blushful mists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pink - a pale red color; the color between red and white
  • Synonym: ruddy - having a healthy, red complexion or color

Etymology

From rose + -y, indicating resemblance to the color of a rose.

2. ADJ

/ ˈroʊzi / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzi / (BrE)
(of a situation or prospect) likely to be successful or positive; showing or expecting optimism.
showing optimism or positivity

Examples

Despite the economic downturn, the CEO painted a rosy picture of the company's future.
Her parents have a rosy outlook on life and believe things will always get better.
It would be unwise to assume such a rosy scenario without considering the risks.

Synset Examples

a rosy future
looked at the world through rose-colored glasses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optimistic - hopeful and confident about the future
  • Antonym: pessimistic - expecting the worst possible outcome

Etymology

From rose + -y, referring to the color of a rose and metaphorically to a bright outlook.
