pacificism

1. NOUN

/ pəˈsɪfɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that all international disputes should be settled by peaceful means such as arbitration instead of war or violence
belief in settling disputes peacefully

Examples

Her unwavering belief in pacificism led her to campaign for diplomatic solutions to every conflict.
The organization promotes pacificism as the only ethical stance in international relations.
Many students studied the history of pacificism during their course on peace studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pacifism - the belief in opposition to war and violence; rejection of war on principle

Etymology

Formed from 'pacific' (peaceful) + '-ism', modeled after 'pacifism'.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈsɪfɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪsɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The belief or doctrine that all forms of violence, including war, are unjustifiable under any circumstances.
doctrine opposing all violence

Examples

Many adherents of pacificism reject both military service and self-defense.
His commitment to pacificism led him to protest all forms of violence, even in self-defense.
The philosophy of pacificism differs from pacifism in its stricter opposition to any violent action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pacifism - the belief that war and violence are unjustifiable

Etymology

From Latin 'pacificus' (making peace) + '-ism'. Distinct from 'pacifism', emphasizing an absolute opposition to violence.
