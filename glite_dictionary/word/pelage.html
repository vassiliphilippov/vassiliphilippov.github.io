pelage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɛlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the coat of hair, wool, or fur covering the body of an animal
animal's coat of hair, wool, or fur

Examples

The arctic fox’s pelage changes color with the seasons to provide better camouflage.
Scientists studied the lion’s pelage to determine its age and health.
Some mammals shed their pelage in warmer months for comfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fur - the thick coat of soft hair covering certain animals

Etymology

From French 'pelage', from 'poil' (hair), related to Latin 'pilus' (hair).
