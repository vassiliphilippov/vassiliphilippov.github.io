lota

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊtə / (AmE), / ˈləʊtə / (BrE)
A freshwater fish of the cod family, commonly known as the burbot, found in northern Europe, Asia, and North America.
a freshwater fish; burbot

Examples

The small lota is often found in cold, deep rivers and lakes.
Fishermen in northern Canada prize the lota for its delicate flavor.
Despite its ugly appearance, the lota provides valuable nutrition in rural areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burbot - a freshwater fish of the cod family

Etymology

From Neo-Latin *Lota* (genus name), possibly via Medieval Latin from a Germanic root.

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.tə / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.tə / (BrE)
a small, round water vessel traditionally used in South Asia for storing or pouring water, typically made of brass, copper, or plastic
South Asian water vessel

Examples

She filled the lota with fresh water from the well every morning.
A brass lota is often used in Hindu religious rituals and daily hygiene.
During the trek, he carried a small lota for drinking and washing.

Etymology

From Hindi 'lōṭā' and Urdu 'لوٹا' (lōṭā), meaning 'rounded water vessel'.
