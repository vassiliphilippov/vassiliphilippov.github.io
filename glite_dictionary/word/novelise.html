to novelise

1. VERB

/ ˈnɑːvəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnɒvəlaɪz / (BrE)
to convert (a story, event, film, etc.) into the form or style of a novel
to turn into a novel
british

Examples

The film was later novelised by a well-known author.
After years of research, she decided to novelise the life of the famous explorer.
Several popular TV series have been novelised for fans who prefer reading.

Synset Examples

The author novelized the historical event

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to novelize - to convert into a novel (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'novel' + '-ise' (British spelling of '-ize'), meaning to make into a novel.
