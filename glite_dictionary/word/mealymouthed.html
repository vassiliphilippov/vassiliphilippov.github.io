mealymouthed

1. ADJ

/ ˈmiːliˌmaʊðd / (AmE), / ˈmiːliˌmaʊðd / (BrE)
hesitant to express facts or opinions plainly, often out of timidity, insincerity, or a desire to avoid offense
hesitant or insincere in stating views

Examples

The spokesperson gave a mealymouthed response to the difficult question.
Instead of being mealymouthed, try giving us your honest opinion.
Her mealymouthed excuses only made her seem less trustworthy.

Synset Examples

a mealymouthed politician

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straightforward - direct and honest in expressing opinions or facts
  • Synonym: evasive - tending to avoid stating things clearly; avoiding directness

Etymology

From 'mealy' (soft, effusively insipid) + 'mouthed', originally implying words as soft or evasive as meal (ground grain).
