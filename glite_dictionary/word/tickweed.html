tickweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪkˌwid / (AmE), / ˈtɪkˌwiːd / (BrE)
Any of numerous plants of the genus Coreopsis, noted for their showy, usually yellow, daisylike flowers blooming over extended periods, native to North and South America.
a plant of the genus Coreopsis with yellow flowers

Examples

The field was full of wild tickweed swaying in the summer breeze.
Gardeners often plant tickweed for its long-lasting, bright yellow blooms.
After the rain, the tickweed along the roadside burst into vibrant color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coreopsis - genus of flowering plants with daisy-like yellow flowers
