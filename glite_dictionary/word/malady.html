malady

1. NOUN

/ ˈmælədi / (AmE), / ˈmalədi / (BrE)
A disease or disorder of the body or mind; an illness.
disease or disorder

Examples

Medical researchers are searching for a cure to this mysterious malady.
Many in the village suffered from the same chronic malady.
Mental health is as important as physical health in combating any malady.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disease - a disorder of structure or function affecting an organism
  • Synonym: illness - a state of poor health or sickness

Etymology

From Old French malady, from Latin malatia (sickness), from male (badly) + habitus (having).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæl.ə.di / (AmE), / ˈmælədi / (BrE)
a serious problem or condition, especially one that is unwholesome or difficult to remedy, often used in a figurative sense
serious or desperate condition/problem

Examples

The economic malady has left many out of work and uncertain about their future.
Corruption has been described as a malady affecting all levels of government.
Poverty remains a persistent malady in many regions of the world.

Synset Examples

what maladies afflict our nation?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disease - an illness that affects a person, animal, or plant
  • Synonym: illness - a disease or period of being ill
  • Antonym: cure - a treatment that ends a disease or condition

Etymology

From Old French 'maladie', from Latin 'male habitus' (in bad condition).
