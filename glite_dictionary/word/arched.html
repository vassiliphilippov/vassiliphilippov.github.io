arched

1. ADJ

/ ɑrtʃt / (AmE), / ɑːtʃt / (BrE)
having a shape or structure that curves like an arch.
curved like an arch

Examples

The old bridge had an arched design that was both elegant and strong.
She admired the arched windows in the historic building.
The cat stretched its back until it was perfectly arched.

Synset Examples

an arched ceiling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curved - having a smoothly bending shape
  • Antonym: straight - having no curves or bends

Etymology

From 'arch' + -ed, meaning 'shaped like an arch.'

2. ADJ

/ ɑrtʃt / (AmE), / ɑːtʃt / (BrE)
having a curved structure or shape like an arch; constructed with or in the form of an arch or arches.
having a curved shape like an arch

Examples

The cathedral's arched ceilings drew everyone's gaze upward.
They passed through an arched doorway into the main hall.
Sunlight filtered through the arched windows, casting patterns on the floor.

Synset Examples

an arched passageway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curved - having a smoothly bending shape, not straight
  • Antonym: straight - not having any curves or bends
