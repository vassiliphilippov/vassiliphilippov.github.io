defoliate

1. ADJ

/ diˈfoʊliˌeɪt / (AmE), / diˈfəʊlieɪt / (BrE)
Deprived of leaves, especially as a result of a natural process or deliberate action.
deprived of leaves

Examples

A region can become defoliate following a severe drought.

2. VERB

/ dɪˈfoʊliˌeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈfəʊlɪeɪt / (BrE)
to remove or cause the leaves to fall from a plant, tree, or area, especially by the use of chemicals.
remove leaves from a plant or tree

Examples

Farmers may defoliate entire fields to prepare for mechanical harvesting.
The chemical was used to defoliate forests during the war.
Some insects can defoliate trees completely, leaving them vulnerable.

Related Terms

From Latin *de-* (removal) + *folium* (leaf).
