to speciate

1. VERB

/ ˈspiːʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈspiːsɪˌeɪt / (BrE)
(of a population or organism) to evolve into a new species or to undergo the process of formation of new species.
evolve into a new species
medical

Examples

Under isolated conditions, some populations can speciate rapidly to exploit new ecological niches.
If the lake dries up, the fish may speciate, adapting to different local ponds.
Biologists track how small genetic differences can cause groups to speciate over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evolve - to develop gradually, especially from a simple to a more complex form

Etymology

From the word 'species' + -ate, modeled on the pattern of Latin-derived verbs.
