Gentiana saponaria

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛnˈʃæn.i.ə ˌsæp.əˈnɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / dʒɛnˈʃeɪ.ni.ə ˌsæp.əˈnɑː.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to wet woodlands of North America, with erect stems and leaves and flower buds resembling soapwort.
perennial herb native to North America

Examples

Gentiana saponaria is often found growing in moist, shaded regions across eastern North America.
The leaves and flower buds of Gentiana saponaria closely resemble those of soapwort.
Gardeners appreciate Gentiana saponaria for its attractive blue flowers and adaptability to woodland gardens.

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'gentiana' (gentian); species name 'saponaria' from Latin referring to soapwort-like characteristics.
