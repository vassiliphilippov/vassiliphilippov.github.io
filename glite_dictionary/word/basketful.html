basketful

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæskətˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈbɑːskɪtˌfʊl / (BrE)
An amount that fills a basket or as much as a basket can hold.
the amount that fills a basket

Examples

She brought a basketful of apples from the orchard.
After the picnic, there was still a basketful of sandwiches left over.
He carried a basketful of laundry down to the basement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handful - as much as can be held in one hand
  • Synonym: armful - as much as can be carried in the arms

Etymology

Formed from 'basket' + 'ful' (Old English -full, denoting 'full of').
