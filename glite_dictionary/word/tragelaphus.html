Tragelaphus

1. NOUN

/ træˈdʒɛl.ə.fəs / (AmE), / trəˈɡiː.lə.fəs / (BrE)
A genus of African antelopes that includes animals such as kudus, bongos, nyalas, and bushbucks.
genus of African antelopes

Examples

The bongo is a striking member of the genus Tragelaphus.
Researchers observed several Tragelaphus species grazing in the savanna.
The kudu, classified as Tragelaphus strepsiceros, is known for its impressive spiral horns.

Etymology

From Greek 'tragos' (goat) + 'elaphos' (deer), used in scientific taxonomy.

2. NOUN

A genus of African antelopes that includes species such as kudus, bongos, nyalas, and bushbucks.
genus of African antelopes

Examples

The Tragelaphus genus includes some of Africa's most striking antelopes, such as the greater kudu and bongo.
Researchers discovered new behavioral traits in female Tragelaphus during the rainy season.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect several endangered species within the Tragelaphus genus.

Etymology

From Greek *tragos* (goat) and *elaphos* (deer). Used in scientific taxonomy.
