veracious

1. ADJ

/ vəˈreɪʃəs / (AmE), / vəˈreɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of a person) habitually speaking the truth; truthful and honest.
habitually truthful and honest

Examples

Everyone respected her as a veracious reporter who never distorted the facts.
The committee needed a veracious account of the incident to guide their decision.
Despite rumors, his veracious nature was never questioned by his friends.

Synset Examples

a veracious witness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truthful - tending to tell the truth; honest
  • Antonym: deceitful - dishonest; likely to lie or mislead

Etymology

From Latin 'vērāx' (truthful), from 'vērus' (true).

2. ADJ

/ vəˈreɪʃəs / (AmE), / vəˈreɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of a statement, account, etc.) accurate and truthful; not containing or affected by falsehood.
accurate and truthful

Examples

The reporter provided a veracious account of the events as they unfolded.
Historians praised his work for its veracious treatment of the facts.
We rely on veracious data to make informed decisions.

Synset Examples

a veracious account

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truthful - telling or expressing the truth
  • Synonym: accurate - correct and without any mistakes
  • Antonym: false - not true; incorrect

Etymology

From Latin 'verax' (truthful), from 'verus' (true).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00023315-s
Synset POS: s
