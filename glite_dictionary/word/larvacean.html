larvacean

1. NOUN

/ lɑrˈveɪsiən / (AmE), / lɑːˈveɪsɪən / (BrE)
a small planktonic marine animal of the class Larvacea, related to tunicates, which retains larval features as an adult
small marine animal of class Larvacea

Examples

The ocean was teeming with larvaceans filtering tiny particles from the water.
Scientists study larvaceans to understand how marine food webs function.
Each larvacean builds a delicate, mucous house to capture food.

Etymology

From New Latin Larvacea, the class name, itself from 'larva' (the juvenile form of an animal), alluding to the retention of larval traits.
