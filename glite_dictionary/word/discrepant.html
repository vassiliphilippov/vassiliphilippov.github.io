discrepant

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɛpənt / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɛpənt / (BrE)
not in agreement with other facts or circumstances; differing or inconsistent.
differing; not in agreement

Examples

His testimony was discrepant with the evidence presented by the prosecution.
The results of the two experiments are discrepant and require further investigation.
A discrepant report may indicate errors in data collection or analysis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consistent - acting or done in the same way over time; compatible
  • Synonym: inconsistent - not staying the same throughout; incompatible

Etymology

From Latin 'discrepans,' present participle of 'discrepare' (to differ, sound differently).

differing; not in agreement

Examples

If the figures are discrepant, we need to check for calculation errors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: congruent - in agreement or harmony

From Latin 'discrepans', present participle of 'discrepare' (to differ in sound, to disagree).
