countdown

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊnˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊnt.daʊn / (BrE)
a count backward from a specified number to zero to indicate the time remaining before a significant event occurs, such as a rocket launch
counting down to an event

Examples

The countdown to the rocket launch began at ten seconds.
Everyone watched the screen as the countdown ticked down to zero.
The New Year's Eve celebration includes a countdown to midnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launch - the act of sending off, especially of a rocket or spacecraft
  • Synonym: timer - device or event for measuring or indicating an interval of time
  • Degree Lower: build-up - the period of preparation leading to an event

Etymology

Formed from 'count' + 'down' in reference to the process of counting numbers in reverse order.
