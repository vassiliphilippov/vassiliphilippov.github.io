Knossos

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɑːsəs / (AmE), / ˈnɒsɒs / (BrE)
an ancient city on the island of Crete, known as the center of Minoan civilization flourishing from about 2000 BC to 1400 BC
ancient Minoan city on Crete

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered magnificent frescoes in Knossos during recent excavations.
The palace at Knossos is famously linked to the legend of the Minotaur and the labyrinth.
During the Bronze Age, Knossos was the most prominent city in Crete.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Κνωσός (Knōsós), the name of the city in classical sources.
