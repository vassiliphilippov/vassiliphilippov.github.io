Conuropsis

1. NOUN

/ kəˌnʊrˈɑpsɪs / (AmE), / kɒˌnjuːˈrɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of parrots in the family Psittacidae that includes certain extinct North American parrots, notably the Carolina parakeet.
genus of North American parrots

Examples

The extinct Carolina parakeet belonged to the genus Conuropsis.
Scientists have studied Conuropsis fossils to understand the bird's disappearance.
Conuropsis is noted for being the only parrot genus once native to the eastern United States.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'conurus' (a former genus of parakeet) and Greek 'opsis' (appearance).

2. NOUN

/ kəˌnʊˈrɑːpsɪs / (AmE), / kɒˌnjuːˈrɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of parrots in the family Psittacidae, formerly containing the Carolina parakeet, now extinct.
genus of parrots; includes extinct Carolina parakeet

Examples

The extinct Carolina parakeet was once classified in the genus Conuropsis.
Researchers have studied fossil remains attributed to Conuropsis to learn more about its habitat.
Conuropsis was unique among parrot genera native to North America.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Psittacidae - the family of parrots

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'kónuros' (parakeet) and '-opsis' (appearance).
