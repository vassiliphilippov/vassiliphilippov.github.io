boneshaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊnˌʃeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈbəʊnˌʃeɪkə / (BrE)
an old, dilapidated, and uncomfortable wheeled vehicle, especially a bicycle or car
uncomfortable old vehicle

Examples

The bus we rode into town was a real boneshaker, rattling us the entire way.
He still owns the ancient boneshaker his grandfather used to ride to work.
We laughed as we rode the old boneshaker down the bumpy country lane.

Synset Examples

that old bike without rubber tires was a real boneshaker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jalopy - an old, dilapidated car

Etymology

From bone + shaker, originally used in the 19th century for the first bicycles with rigid frames and no tires, which gave a rough ride.
