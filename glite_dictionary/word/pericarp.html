pericarp

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛrɪˌkɑrp / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪkɑːp / (BrE)
the part of a fruit formed from the wall of the ripened ovary of a flower, sometimes modified into distinct layers
the wall of a ripened fruit ovary
medical

Examples

The pericarp of the peach consists of a fleshy layer that we eat.
In some fruits, the pericarp develops into a hard shell protecting the seeds.
Botanists study how the structure of the pericarp affects seed dispersal.

Etymology

From Greek 'perikarpion', from 'peri-' (around) + 'karpos' (fruit).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛr.ɪˌkɑrp / (AmE), / ˈpɛr.ɪˌkɑːp / (BrE)
the part of a fruit formed from the ovary wall, which encloses the seed or seeds
outer layer of a fruit surrounding the seed
medical

Examples

The orange pericarp is thick and leathery, protecting the juicy segments inside.
After pollination, the ovary develops into the pericarp surrounding the seeds.
Botanists study the structure of the pericarp in various fruit species.

Synset Examples

The seed is protected by the pericarp of the fruit.

Etymology

From Greek perikarpos, from peri- (around) + karpos (fruit).
