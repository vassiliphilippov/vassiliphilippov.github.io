Succos

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːkəs / (AmE), / ˈsʊkəs / (BrE)
a major Jewish festival, also known as Sukkot, beginning on the 15th of Tishri and commemorating the Israelites' shelter during their 40 years in the wilderness
Jewish festival commemorating Israelites' shelter in wilderness

Examples

Every year, they build a Succos booth in their backyard to celebrate the holiday.
During Succos, families gather in temporary shelters to eat and pray together.
The school planned a field trip to the synagogue's Succos celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sukkot - a major Jewish harvest festival; also called Feast of Tabernacles

Etymology

From Hebrew סֻכּוֹת (Sukkot), meaning 'booths' or 'huts'.
