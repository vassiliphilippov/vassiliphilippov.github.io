Pecten

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɛktən / (AmE), / ˈpɛktən / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Pectinidae, comprising various sea and bay scallops.
a genus of sea and bay scallops

Examples

The genus Pecten includes several widely distributed scallop species.
Scientists studied the shell structure of Pecten for clues about mollusk evolution.
Several commercial fisheries harvest members of the Pecten genus for their flavorful meat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallop - a marine bivalve mollusk with a fan-shaped shell
  • Degree Higher: Pectinidae - the biological family containing the scallops

Etymology

From Latin pecten (comb), referring to the scallop's shell shape.
