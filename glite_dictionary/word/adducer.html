adducer

1. NOUN

/ əˈduːsər / (AmE), / əˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
a person who offers evidence, an example, or a reason in discussion or argument
person who presents evidence or reasons

Examples

In the debate, the adducer presented several compelling statistics to support her position.
As an adducer of facts, he strengthened the argument with well-chosen examples.
The court appreciated the work of the adducer who introduced new evidence to the case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adduce - to offer as evidence or proof

Etymology

From Latin adducere (to bring to); formed from the verb 'adduce' plus suffix '-er'.
