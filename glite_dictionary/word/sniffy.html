sniffy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsnɪfi / (AmE), / ˈsnɪfi / (BrE)
having or showing a disdainfully superior or contemptuous attitude; haughty or scornful.
disdainfully superior; haughty
informal

Examples

The reviewer was rather sniffy about the new restaurant's décor.
She gave a sniffy glance at my outfit before turning away.
They were too sniffy to join in with the rest of the group.

Synset Examples

some economists are disdainful of their colleagues in other social disciplines
haughty aristocrats
his lordly manners were offensive
walked with a prideful swagger
very sniffy about breaches of etiquette
his mother eyed my clothes with a supercilious air
a more swaggering mood than usual

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haughty - arrogantly superior and disdainful
  • Synonym: supercilious - behaving or looking as though one thinks one is superior to others
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance
