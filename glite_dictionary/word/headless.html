headless

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɛdləs / (AmE), / ˈhɛdləs / (BrE)
Not having a head, or formed without a head.
lacking a head

Examples

The legend speaks of a headless horseman who roams the countryside at night.
Some types of screws are deliberately made headless to be hidden when installed.
She discovered a headless statue in the ancient ruins.

Synset Examples

the headless horseman
brads are headless nails

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beheaded - having had the head cut off
  • Antonym: headed - having a head

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English 'hēafodlēas' ('hēafod' meaning 'head' + 'lēas' meaning 'without').

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɛdləs / (AmE), / ˈhɛdlɪs / (BrE)
Acting or done without intelligence or sense; lacking direction or rational thought.
lacking intelligence or sense

Examples

The project turned into a headless rush without any real planning.
He made a headless decision that cost the company thousands of dollars.
Their headless behavior during the emergency only made things worse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senseless - lacking sense or meaning; foolish
  • Synonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment

Etymology

From head + -less, meaning 'without a head', extended metaphorically to mean 'without sense or direction'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01339790-s
Synset POS: s
