crooner

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruːnər / (AmE), / ˈkruːnə / (BrE)
a singer, typically male, who performs sentimental popular songs in a soft, smooth voice
a singer of soft, sentimental songs

Examples

The famous crooner serenaded the audience with his classic love songs.
In the 1940s, a crooner like Bing Crosby captured the hearts of millions.
She prefers listening to old crooner records on rainy evenings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: singer - a person who sings

Etymology

From the verb 'croon,' meaning to sing softly or hum; from Middle Dutch 'kronen' (to lament, hum).
