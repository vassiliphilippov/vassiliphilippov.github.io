kilolitre

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪləˌliːtər / (AmE), / ˈkɪləliːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of volume equal to 1,000 litres
metric volume unit (1,000 litres)

Examples

The new storage tank can hold up to fifty kilolitres of water.
The annual rainfall in the region is measured in kilolitres per hectare.
Each truck delivered exactly one kilolitre of milk to the factory.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: liter - basic metric unit of volume
  • Degree Lower: millilitre - a metric unit equal to one thousandth of a litre
  • Degree Higher: megalitre - a metric unit of volume equal to one million litres

Etymology

From French *kilolitre*, from kilo- (thousand) + litre.
