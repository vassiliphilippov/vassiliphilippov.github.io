Costia necatrix

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɒstiə nəˈkeɪtrɪks / (AmE), / ˌkɒstiə nɪˈkeɪtrɪks / (BrE)
a flagellate protozoan parasite that causes the often fatal fish disease costiasis
protozoan parasite of fish
medical

Examples

The presence of Costia necatrix in the aquarium led to an outbreak of costiasis among the fish.
Veterinarians identified Costia necatrix as the cause of the rapid decline in the carp population.
Routine screening for Costia necatrix can help prevent deadly infections in ornamental fish farms.
