John Wesley

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈwɛsli / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈwɛsli / (BrE)
(1703–1791) English clergyman and theologian, best known as the founder of Methodism.
founder of Methodism

Examples

John Wesley traveled extensively throughout Britain, preaching and spreading his religious message.
Many Christians today are influenced by the teachings of John Wesley and the Methodist church.
The anniversary of John Wesley's birth is commemorated by Methodists worldwide.
