to venesect

1. VERB

/ ˈvɛnɪˌsɛkt / (AmE), / ˈviːnɪˌsɛkt / (BrE)
to cut open a vein, especially for medical purposes such as drawing blood; to practice venesection
perform venesection (cut a vein medically)
medical archaic

Examples

In the 18th century, doctors would often venesect patients to treat various ailments.
The physician decided to venesect the patient to relieve his symptoms.
Some historians criticize how frequently patients were venesected for minor conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to phlebotomize - to let blood by cutting a vein
  • Synonym: to bleed - to draw blood as a medical treatment

Etymology

From Latin vena (vein) + secare (to cut).
