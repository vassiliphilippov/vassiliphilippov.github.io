Myxophyceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪksoʊfaɪˈsiːiː / (AmE), / ˌmɪksəʊfʌɪˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a former classification for a group of blue-green algae, now known as Cyanophyceae or cyanobacteria
former term for blue-green algae

Examples

The classification of Myxophyceae has been largely replaced by the modern term cyanobacteria.
Early botanists grouped blue-green algae under the class Myxophyceae.
The characteristics attributed to Myxophyceae included their pigment content and slimy appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyanophyceae - group of blue-green algae, now called cyanobacteria

Etymology

From Greek myxa (slime, mucus) + phykos (seaweed, algae); refers to their slimy texture.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪksoʊfaɪˈsiːiː / (AmE), / ˌmɪksə(ʊ)fʌɪˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a former scientific name for a group of blue-green algae, now classified as Cyanophyceae or cyanobacteria.
former name for blue-green algae group

Examples

The term Myxophyceae is no longer in use among modern biologists.
Many older scientific texts refer to blue-green algae as Myxophyceae.
Studies on Myxophyceae contributed to the understanding of cyanobacteria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyanophyceae - a group of blue-green algae, now known as cyanobacteria

Etymology

From Greek myxa (mucus) + phykos (seaweed); scientific Latin formation.
