Brevoortia

1. NOUN

/ brɪˈvɔːrʃiə / (AmE), / brɪˈvɔːtɪə / (BrE)
A genus of small, oily fish commonly known as menhaden, found in coastal waters of North America.
genus of fish; menhaden

Examples

Many commercial fisheries target Brevoortia for use as bait and fish oil.
Scientists studied the spawning habits of Brevoortia along the Atlantic coast.
The population of Brevoortia has significantly declined due to overfishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menhaden - A common name for fish of the genus Brevoortia.

Etymology

Named after American naturalist James Carson Brevoort (1818–1887).
