realisation

1. NOUN

/ riˌæləˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of selling assets, such as stocks or property, in order to convert them into cash, or the money obtained from such a sale
sale of assets to obtain cash

Examples

The bankrupt company's properties were sold off in a realisation to pay creditors.
After the realisation of his shares, he invested the money in real estate.
The administrator handled the realisation of the estate efficiently, ensuring all debts were settled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquidation - the process of liquidating a business or assets
  • Synonym: sale - the act of selling something

Etymology

From Latin 'realis' (actual) via French 'réalisation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌriːələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of making something real or giving it the appearance of reality; the process by which something becomes real or is perceived as real.
act of making real or appearing real
british

Examples

The director's realisation of the fantasy world made the film truly immersive.
Artists strive for the realisation of their visions on canvas.
The stage set contributed greatly to the realisation of the play's dreamlike atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realization - The act of making real or appearing real (American spelling)

Etymology

From French 'réalisation', from 'réaliser' (to make real), from Latin 'realis'.

5. NOUN

/ ˌriːəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of completing or enriching a piece of music that has been left sparsely notated by the composer, typically by adding harmonies or instrumental parts
completion of a sparsely notated piece of music

Examples

The realisation of the Baroque sonata required a skilled harpsichordist to interpret the figured bass.
Her realisation of the incomplete score brought new depth to the composer's intentions.
The quartet performed a modern realisation of the unfinished chamber piece.

Etymology

From French 'réalisation', from 'réaliser' (to realize), based on Latin 'realis' (real).

6. NOUN

/ ˌriːələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a musical composition that has been completed or enriched by someone other than the original composer, often based on a fragment or sketch
completion of a musical composition by another

Examples

The Mozart Requiem is well known for its realisation by Franz Xaver Süssmayr.
This edition contains a modern realisation of the composer's incomplete sketches.
The realisation of this Baroque piece was based on surviving manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completion - the act or process of finishing something

Etymology

From French 'réalisation', from 'réaliser' (to realize), ultimately from Late Latin 'realis' (actual).
