spritz

1. NOUN

/ sprɪts / (AmE), / sprɪts / (BrE)
A quick, light squirt or spray of liquid, especially carbonated water.
a quick squirt of liquid

Examples

She gave the plants a spritz of water to keep them fresh.
The bartender finished the cocktail with a spritz of soda.
A quick spritz of perfume filled the room with fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray - liquid dispersed in fine drops
  • Degree Lower: mist - tiny droplets of liquid dispersed in the air

Etymology

From German 'spritzen' (to squirt or spray).

2. VERB

/ sprɪts / (AmE), / sprɪts / (BrE)
to lightly spray or squirt a liquid onto something, usually in small jets or mist
to spray or squirt liquid lightly

Examples

She likes to spritz her plants with water every morning to keep them fresh.
Before ironing, he always spritzes a little starch on his shirts.
After a long day, she spritzed her face with a refreshing mist.

Synset Examples

Spritz the lawn with water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray - to send out liquid in small drops over an area

Etymology

From German 'spritzen' (to squirt, to splash).

3. VERB

/ sprɪts / (AmE), / sprɪts / (BrE)
to spray or squirt a small amount of liquid quickly and lightly
to spray liquid quickly

Examples

She likes to spritz her plants with water every morning.
The bartender spritzed a bit of lemon over the cocktail before serving it.
I usually spritz perfume on my wrists before going out.

Synset Examples

spritz water on a surface

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray - to send out liquid in tiny drops
  • Synonym: squirt - to force a liquid out in a thin fast stream

Etymology

German origin, from 'spritzen' meaning 'to squirt'.
