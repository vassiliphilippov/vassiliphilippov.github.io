geomorphologic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒiː.oʊˌmɔːr.fəˈlɑː.dʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əʊˌmɔː.fəˈlɒ.dʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to the form and processes of the Earth's surface or its geological structure, especially as studied in geomorphology.
relating to Earth's surface structure

Examples

The geomorphologic characteristics of the region reveal its glacial history.
Researchers published a study on the geomorphologic changes caused by river flooding.
Coastal management strategies must consider the geomorphologic features of the shoreline.

Synset Examples

geomorphological features of the Black Hills
morphological features of granite
structural effects of folding and faulting of the earth's surface

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geomorphological - relating to geomorphology; variant form often used interchangeably

Etymology

From geo- (Earth) + morphology (study of form), based on geomorphology.
