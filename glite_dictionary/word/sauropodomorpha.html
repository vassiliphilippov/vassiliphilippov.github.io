Sauropodomorpha

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɔːrəˌpɒdəˈmɔːrfə / (AmE), / ˌsɔːrəˌpɒdəˈmɔːfə / (BrE)
a suborder of gigantic herbivorous dinosaurs characterized by long necks, small heads, and massive bodies, including both prosauropods and sauropods such as Diplodocus and Apatosaurus
suborder of large long-necked dinosaurs

Examples

The Sauropodomorpha include some of the largest animals ever to walk the Earth.
Recent fossil discoveries have provided new insights into Sauropodomorpha evolution.
Paleontologists study Sauropodomorpha to understand the diversity of prehistoric herbivorous dinosaurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sauropod - Large, long-necked herbivorous dinosaur

Etymology

From Greek 'sauros' (lizard) + 'pous, podos' (foot) + 'morphē' (form, shape).
