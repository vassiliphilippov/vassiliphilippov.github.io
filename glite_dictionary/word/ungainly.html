ungainly

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡeɪnli / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡeɪnli / (BrE)
Moving or appearing in a way that is awkward and lacks grace.
awkward or lacking grace

Examples

The tall boy's ungainly walk made him stand out in the crowd.
She tried to dance, but her movements were too ungainly for the waltz.
Despite his ungainly appearance, the dog was friendly and playful.

Synset Examples

a gawky lad with long ungainly legs
clumsy fingers
what an ungainly creature a giraffe is
heaved his unwieldy figure out of his chair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkward - lacking grace or ease of movement
  • Antonym: graceful - having or showing grace or elegance

Etymology

From un- (not) + obsolete gainly (graceful), from Old English 'gegn' (direct, straight).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡeɪnli / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡeɪnli / (BrE)
difficult to handle or manage, especially because of an awkward or unwieldy shape or movement.
awkward or difficult to handle

Examples

The package was so ungainly that it barely fit through the door.
She found the large, ungainly suitcase a struggle to drag up the stairs.
Balancing the ungainly box proved harder than he had expected.

Synset Examples

an awkward bundle to carry
a load of bunglesome paraphernalia
clumsy wooden shoes
the cello, a rather ungainly instrument for a girl

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkward - lacking grace or ease in movement or form
  • Antonym: graceful - showing grace or elegance, especially in movement or form
  • Synonym: clumsy - lacking coordination or physical skill; awkward in movement or shape

Etymology

From un- (not) + gainly (attractive, graceful), from obsolete gainly (useful, graceful), probably from Old Norse gegn (straight, direct).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02574753-s
Synset POS: s
