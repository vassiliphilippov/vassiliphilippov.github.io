licitness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪs.ɪt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪs.ɪt.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being strictly in accordance with the law
quality of being lawful
legal

Examples

The licitness of the new policy was confirmed by the legal department after a thorough review.
Questions regarding the licitness of the transaction were raised during the audit.
Judges must consider both the fairness and licitness of any proceedings in their court.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illegality - the state of being illegal or not allowed by law
  • Synonym: lawfulness - the state of being in accordance with the law

Etymology

From Latin 'licitus' (lawful) + English '-ness'.
