Scombresocidae

1. NOUN

/ skɑmˌbɹəˈsɑsɪˌdi / (AmE), / skɒmˌbɹɪˈsɒsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of fish that includes sauries, belonging to the order Beloniformes.
family of fish including sauries

Examples

The Scombresocidae family is best known for its slender, fast-swimming sauries.
Marine biologists classified several species of sauries within Scombresocidae.
The evolutionary traits of Scombresocidae separate them from related fish families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Belonidae - family of fish known as needlefish

Etymology

From New Latin Scombresocidae, based on genus Scomberesox plus family suffix -idae.

2. NOUN

/ skɒm-briˈsɑsɪˌdi / (AmE), / skɒm-brɪˈsəʊsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fish to which sauries belong, characterized by elongated bodies and beak-like jaws.
fish family including the sauries

Examples

The Scombresocidae family includes several species of fast-swimming marine fish.
Sauries are part of the Scombresocidae and are known for their slender bodies.
Marine biologists are studying the migration patterns of fish in the Scombresocidae.

Etymology

From New Latin Scombresocidae, based on genus Scomberesox (type genus).
