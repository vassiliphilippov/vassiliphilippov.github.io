embouchure

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːm.buːˈʃʊr / (AmE), / ˌɒm.buːˈʃʊə / (BrE)
The opening in a wind instrument into which the player blows directly.
mouth opening of a wind instrument

Examples

The flutist carefully shaped her lips around the embouchure of the instrument.
A cracked embouchure can greatly affect the sound quality of a trumpet.
The saxophone's embouchure is designed for comfortable and effective airflow.

Etymology

From French embouchure (mouth of a musical instrument).
