to euthanatize

1. VERB

/ juːˈθænəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / juːˈθænətaɪz / (BrE)
to intentionally cause the painless death of a person or animal, especially to relieve suffering
cause a painless death intentionally
medical

Examples

The veterinarian decided it was most humane to euthanatize the suffering animal.
In rare medical cases, doctors may have to euthanatize a patient at the family's request.
Many animal shelters are forced to euthanatize stray pets due to overcrowding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to euthanize - to intentionally cause the painless death of a person or animal

Etymology

Formed from 'euthanasia' (from Greek 'eu' meaning good and 'thanatos' meaning death) and the English verb-forming suffix '-ize'.
