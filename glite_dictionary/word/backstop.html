backstop

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌstɑp / (AmE), / ˈbækstɒp / (BrE)
A fence, screen, or barrier behind the home plate in baseball, designed to prevent the ball from leaving the playing area.
barrier behind home plate in baseball

Examples

The foul ball bounced off the backstop and rolled back onto the field.
A tall chain-link backstop protected spectators seated behind home plate.
The team installed a new backstop to ensure safety during games.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌstɑp / (AmE), / ˈbækˌstɒp / (BrE)
a precaution or safeguard put in place to prevent failure or address emergencies if other measures do not work
precaution or safeguard for emergencies

Examples

The government created a financial backstop to reassure the markets during the crisis.
She served as a backstop for the team, ready to help if anything went wrong.
The policy was intended as a backstop in case the primary plan failed.

Synset Examples

he acted as a backstop in case anything went wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safeguard - something that protects against possible danger or trouble
  • Synonym: contingency - a possible emergency or unexpected event; something done to prepare for this

Etymology

Originally referring to a screen behind a baseball catcher (late 19th century), later extended metaphorically to mean a safeguard.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌstɑp / (AmE), / ˈbækˌstɒp / (BrE)
(Baseball) The catcher, the player who positions behind home plate to catch pitches not hit by the batter.
the catcher (baseball position)

Examples

The team's starting backstop was sidelined with a shoulder injury.
As a skilled backstop, he prevented many wild pitches from reaching the back fence.
The rookie backstop showed impressive reflexes behind home plate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catcher - the player who catches pitches in baseball

Etymology

Originally referring to the screen or fence behind home plate in baseball; later extended by metonymy to mean the catcher.

4. VERB

/ ˈbækˌstɑp / (AmE), / ˈbækstɒp / (BrE)
to provide support, protection, or a safety net; to act as a backup in case the primary system or plan fails
act as support or backup

Examples

The government agreed to backstop the banks if the crisis worsens.
She backstopped the team by double-checking all the calculations.
A reserve fund was created to backstop emergency repairs in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to give assistance or help to someone or something
  • Synonym: bolster - to support or strengthen
