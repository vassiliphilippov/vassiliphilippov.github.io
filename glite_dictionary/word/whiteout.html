whiteout

1. VERB

/ ˈwaɪt.aʊt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt.aʊt / (BrE)
to cover written or printed errors with a white liquid correction fluid
cover errors with correction fluid

Examples

She quickly whiteouted the mistake on the report before submitting it.
You can whiteout any typos using a correction pen.
After realizing the error, I whited out the extra digit.

Synset Examples

white-out the typo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erase - remove marks or written material by rubbing or scraping
  • Synonym: to cross out - draw a line through written words to remove them

Etymology

From the brand name White-Out, first marketed in the 1960s in the US.

2. VERB

/ ˈwaɪtˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌaʊt / (BrE)
to lose daylight visibility due to heavy fog, snow, or rain, making the landscape appear featureless and white
lose visibility in white conditions

Examples

The hikers were suddenly whited out as the storm intensified on the mountainside.
If the road whites out during your drive, pull over and wait for conditions to improve.
The plane had to turn back when the runway whited out in heavy snow.

Etymology

From 'white' + 'out'; originally from North American English describing blizzard conditions.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00547478-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪt.aʊt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt.aʊt / (BrE)
a weather condition in polar or snowy regions where clouds, fog, and snow blend to create a uniform white appearance, making it difficult to see objects or the horizon
arctic condition with uniform whiteness, obscuring vision

Examples

The explorers were caught in a whiteout and had to stop until it cleared.
During the whiteout, it was impossible to distinguish the ground from the sky.
Driving became dangerous as a sudden whiteout reduced visibility to near zero.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blizzard - a severe snowstorm with high winds and low visibility
  • Synonym: fog - a thick cloud of tiny water droplets in the atmosphere, obscuring visibility
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11529226-n
Synset POS: n
