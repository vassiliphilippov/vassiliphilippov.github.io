ravishment

1. NOUN

/ ˈrævɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈravɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the crime of forcing someone to submit to sexual intercourse against their will; rape
rape; sexual assault by force
legal archaic

Examples

The law once used the term ravishment to describe what is now called rape.
Historical accounts often reference the ravishment of individuals during times of war.
The accused stood trial for the alleged ravishment of his neighbor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rape - the crime of forcing another person to have sexual intercourse against their will

Etymology

From Old French 'ravissement', from Latin 'rapere' (to seize, carry off).
