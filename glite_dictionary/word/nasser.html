Nasser

1. PROPN

/ ˈnæsər / (AmE), / ˈnasə / (BrE)
a large artificial lake in southern Egypt and northern Sudan, created by the construction of the Aswan High Dam on the Nile River
large artificial lake in Egypt and Sudan

Examples

The construction of the Aswan High Dam led to the creation of Nasser.
Many Nile crocodiles can be found in the waters of Nasser.
The villages near Nasser were relocated due to the flooding caused by the dam.

Etymology

Named after Gamal Abdel Nasser, former President of Egypt.

2. PROPN

/ nɑːˈsɜr / (AmE), / ˈnæsə / (BrE)
Gamal Abdel Nasser (1918–1970), Egyptian statesman and President who nationalized the Suez Canal and was a leading figure in Arab nationalism.
Egyptian president and statesman (1918–1970)

Examples

Nasser was a key architect of the modern Egyptian state.
The nationalization of the Suez Canal by Nasser changed Middle Eastern politics.
Many Arabs admired Nasser for his stance against colonial powers.
