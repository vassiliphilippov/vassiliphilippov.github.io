tinea barbae

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪniə ˈbɑːrbi / (AmE), / ˈtɪniə ˈbɑːˌbiː / (BrE)
A fungal infection of the beard area of the face and neck, usually affecting men.
fungal infection of the beard area
medical

Examples

Barbers used to encounter tinea barbae frequently before the introduction of modern hygiene practices.
Only men who grow facial hair are typically affected by tinea barbae.
A dermatologist diagnosed the red, scaly rash as tinea barbae and prescribed antifungal medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ringworm - fungal infection of the skin, usually presenting as a ring-shaped rash

Etymology

From Latin tinea (worm, ringworm) + barbae (of the beard).
