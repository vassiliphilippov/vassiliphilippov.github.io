rachis

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.kɪs / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.kɪs / (BrE)
the main axis or shaft of a compound leaf or a compound inflorescence in plants
main axis of a compound leaf or inflorescence

Examples

The feather's central rachis provides support for its barbs and barbules.
Many ferns have a long rachis from which the leaflets grow symmetrically.
The rachis of the flower cluster was thick and sturdy, holding numerous blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axis - central line about which a structure is arranged

Etymology

From Greek rhakhis (spine, backbone).

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.kɪs / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.kɪs / (BrE)
the spine or vertebral column, forming the axis of the skeleton and protecting the spinal cord
the vertebral column; spine

Examples

The X-ray showed that the rachis was undamaged after the accident.
In anatomy class, we learned the structure of the human rachis.
Some diseases can affect the flexibility of the rachis, leading to limited movement.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
