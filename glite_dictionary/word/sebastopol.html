Sebastopol

1. PROPN

/ səˈbæstəˌpoʊl / (AmE), / sɪˈbæstəpɒl / (BrE)
A city and major port in southern Ukraine, located on the Black Sea, known for its strategic military importance and historical events.
city in southern Ukraine on the Black Sea

Examples

The Russian navy has maintained a base in Sebastopol for over a century.
During the Crimean War, Sebastopol was the site of a famous siege.
She visited Sebastopol to explore its historic fortifications and museums.

Etymology

From Russian Sevastopol, possibly from Greek 'Sebastos' (venerable, august) and 'polis' (city).
