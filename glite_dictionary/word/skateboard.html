skateboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈskeɪtˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈskeɪtˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a flat board mounted on wheels, ridden while standing or crouching, and propelled by pushing with one foot
a board with wheels for riding

Examples

He learned how to ride a skateboard when he was ten years old.
The skateboard rolled down the hill after she lost her balance.
Many teenagers spend hours practicing tricks on their skateboards at the park.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: longboard - a longer type of skateboard for cruising or downhill racing

Etymology

From 'skate' (as in roller skate) + 'board', first used in the 1960s.

2. VERB

/ ˈskeɪtˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈskeɪtˌbɔːd / (BrE)
to ride on a flat board with small wheels attached, known as a skateboard
ride on a skateboard

Examples

He loves to skateboard around the park after school.
Many teenagers skateboard on the city streets during the summer.
She learned to skateboard when she was eight years old.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skate - move or glide on skates (noun/verb)

Etymology

Formed from 'skate' + 'board' (referring to a board used for skating).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01941654-v
Synset POS: v
