incommodious

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkəˈmoʊdiəs / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˈməʊdiəs / (BrE)
(of a room, building, or space) uncomfortably or inconveniently small or cramped.
uncomfortably small or cramped

Examples

We found the incommodious apartment difficult to live in for a family of four.
The guests complained about the incommodious seating arrangements at the event.
Despite the hotel's charm, the rooms were rather incommodious for a long stay.

Synset Examples

incommodious hotel accommodations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spacious - (of a room or space) having ample space
  • Synonym: cramped - uncomfortably small or restricted

Etymology

From Latin 'in' (not) + 'commodus' (convenient, fitting).
