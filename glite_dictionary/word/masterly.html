masterly

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæstərli / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəli / (BrE)
showing or done with great skill; demonstrating supreme mastery.
showing great skill

Examples

The pianist gave a masterly performance that captivated the entire audience.
Her masterly handling of the negotiations impressed both sides.
This biography is a masterly work, blending extensive research with engaging storytelling.

Synset Examples

a consummate artist
consummate skill
a masterful speaker
masterful technique
a masterly performance of the sonata
a virtuoso performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masterful - showing skill or dominance; having the qualities of a master
  • Antonym: amateurish - lacking professional skill or expertise

Etymology

From Middle English 'maisterly', from 'maister' (master) + '-ly'.
