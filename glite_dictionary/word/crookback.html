crookback

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrʊkˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈkrʊkˌbæk / (BrE)
a person whose back is hunched due to abnormal curvature of the upper spine, especially as a result of kyphosis; an old-fashioned or literary term for a hunchback.
person with a hunched back
archaic

Examples

The cruel children laughed at the poor crookback who shuffled down the street.
In the play, the villain was described as a wicked crookback feared by all.
Legends tell of a wise crookback who lived alone in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunchback - a person whose back is hunched due to abnormal spinal curvature

Etymology

From 'crook' (a bend) + 'back', referring to the curvature of the spine.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkrʊkˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈkrʊkˌbak / (BrE)
having a curved or hunched back, especially as a result of kyphosis; suffering from an abnormal spinal curvature.
person with a hunched back
archaic

Examples

The old man was a crookback who walked with great difficulty.
Legends often featured a crookback living at the edge of town.
He was cruelly mocked as a crookback in his youth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunchback - a person with a hunched or rounded back

Etymology

From 'crook' (bend) plus 'back'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
