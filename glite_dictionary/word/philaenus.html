Philaenus

1. NOUN

/ fəˈleɪ.nəs / (AmE), / fɪˈleɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of insects in the family Cercopidae, known commonly as froghoppers or spittlebugs.
a genus of froghopper insects

Examples

The most widespread species in the genus Philaenus is Philaenus spumarius, known for creating foam nests.
Researchers collected several Philaenus specimens from fields in southern Europe.
Philaenus insects play a role in the spread of plant pathogens like Xylella fastidiosa.

Etymology

From Greek 'philos' (loving) + 'ainos' (praise, story), used as a scientific genus name.

2. NOUN

/ fɪˈleɪənəs / (AmE), / fɪˈliːənəs / (BrE)
A genus of froghopper insects in the family Cercopidae, commonly known as spittlebugs.
genus of froghopper insects (spittlebugs)

Examples

The species Philaenus spumarius is a common spittlebug found in many gardens.
Researchers studied several members of the genus Philaenus for their role in transmitting plant diseases.
The larvae of Philaenus produce characteristic frothy masses known as spittle.

Etymology

From New Latin, used as a genus name in scientific classification.
