wassailer

1. NOUN

/ wəˈseɪlər / (AmE), / ˈwɒseɪlə / (BrE)
a person who enjoys noisy celebration and often excessive drinking, especially during festivities
person who enjoys festive, noisy drinking

Examples

During the festival, every wassailer in the village gathered at the tavern to celebrate with song and drink.
The lively wassailer led the crowd in a boisterous chorus of holiday tunes.
As a notorious wassailer, he was always the life of every party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reveller - a person who is enjoying themselves in a lively and noisy way

Etymology

From Middle English 'wassail' (to carouse, drink to health) + '-er'. Originally from Old Norse 'ves heill' meaning 'be well'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːˌseɪlər / (AmE), / ˈwɒs.əɪlər / (BrE)
a person who drinks to someone's health or to the success of an undertaking, especially as part of a festive tradition
someone who drinks to health or success
archaic

Examples

Every Christmas, a group of wassailers visited our village singing and toasting good fortune.
In the old tales, the king would welcome the wassailers into his hall during the winter festival.
As wassailers, they roamed from house to house, bringing cheer and raising their cups in celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reveller - person enjoying themselves in a lively and noisy way, especially with drinking

Etymology

From 'wassail', from Old Norse 'ves heill' (be healthy), used as a drinking toast.
