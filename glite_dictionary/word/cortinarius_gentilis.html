Cortinarius gentilis

1. NOUN

/ kɔːrˌtɪnˈɛəriəs ˈdʒɛntɪlɪs / (AmE), / kɔːˌtɪnˈɑːrɪəs ˈdʒɛntɪlɪs / (BrE)
a species of poisonous mushroom with a bright yellow-brown cap and a long cinnamon-colored stalk, found in temperate forests
a poisonous mushroom species

Examples

It is important to avoid collecting Cortinarius gentilis because it is highly poisonous.
The forest floor was dotted with Cortinarius gentilis beneath the pine trees.
Experts warn that Cortinarius gentilis can be mistaken for edible mushrooms by inexperienced foragers.

Etymology

From the genus name Cortinarius (from Latin 'cortina' meaning curtain) and the Latin specific epithet 'gentilis' (of a clan or race).
