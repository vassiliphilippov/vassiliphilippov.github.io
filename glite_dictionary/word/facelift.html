facelift

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪsˌlɪft / (AmE), / ˈfeɪslɪft / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove wrinkles and other signs of aging from the face, typically by tightening the skin and removing excess tissue
surgical procedure to tighten facial skin

Examples

She decided to have a facelift to reduce the wrinkles around her eyes and mouth.
After his facelift, he looked ten years younger and more refreshed.
Many celebrities openly admit to getting a facelift to maintain a youthful appearance.

Synset Examples

some actresses have more than one face lift

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhytidectomy - medical term for a facelift (the same cosmetic surgery)

Etymology

From the phrase 'face lift', referring to lifting the face; first used in early 20th century cosmetic surgery.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪslɪft / (AmE), / ˈfeɪslɪft / (BrE)
a renovation or improvement intended to make the outward appearance of something, especially a building, more attractive without making major structural changes
superficial renovation or improvement

Examples

The historic theater received a much-needed facelift to restore its original charm.
Our office building is getting a facelift with a fresh coat of paint and new windows.
The company hoped that a product facelift would boost sales without changing its core features.

Synset Examples

give your home a facelift
more than a facelift, the new model marks a fundamental change of direction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renovation - the act of making changes and repairs to improve something
  • Antonym: overhaul - a thorough examination or repair, especially with significant changes

Etymology

Metaphoric extension from 'facelift', a surgical procedure to improve the appearance of the face.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪsˌlɪft / (AmE), / ˈfeɪslɪft / (BrE)
a renovation or set of improvements that refreshes or enhances the outward appearance of something, especially a building, without making major structural changes
cosmetic improvement to appearance, esp. of buildings

Examples

After a quick facelift, the old café looked modern and welcoming again.
The company hopes that a major facelift of its software will attract new users.
The library is set to receive a much-needed facelift next spring.

Synset Examples

give your home a facelift
more than a facelift, the new model marks a fundamental change of direction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renovation - the process of repairing or improving a building
  • Degree Higher: overhaul - a thorough examination and repair, often involving more substantial changes

Etymology

Modeled on the literal sense of 'face-lift' (cosmetic surgery), figuratively extended to buildings, products, etc. from the mid-20th century.

Source: WordNet
