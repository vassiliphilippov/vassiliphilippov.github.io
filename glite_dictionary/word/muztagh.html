Muztagh

1. PROPN

/ ˈmuːzˌtɑːɡ / (AmE), / ˈmʌztɑːɡ / (BrE)
a mountain peak in the Kunlun Shan range in China near the Indian border, with an elevation of 23,891 feet (7,282 meters)
mountain peak in Kunlun Shan, China

Examples

The expedition prepared for weeks to ascend Muztagh in the challenging weather conditions of western China.
From the summit of Muztagh, climbers can see vast stretches of the Kunlun Shan Mountain Range.
Scientists study the glaciers on Muztagh to better understand climate change in the region.

Etymology

From Turkic languages, meaning 'ice mountain'.
