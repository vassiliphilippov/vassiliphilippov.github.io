switching

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɪtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswɪtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of changing from one thing, position, or option to another
the act of changing from one thing to another

Examples

Her sudden switching of careers surprised everyone in her family.
Effective switching between tasks can improve productivity at work.
The switching of political allegiances is common during election campaigns.

Synset Examples

his switch on abortion cost him the election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: change - the process of becoming different or making something different

Etymology

Derived from the verb 'switch', from Middle English 'swichen' (to move suddenly).
