declassified

1. ADJ

/ ˌdiːˈklæsɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈklæsɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(of documents or information) having had a confidential or secret status officially removed, making it accessible to the public.
made public after being secret

Examples

The government released several declassified files related to the investigation.
Researchers analyzed the declassified documents to learn more about the incident.
After years of secrecy, the declassified report was finally made available to the public.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: classified - officially designated as secret or confidential

Etymology

From de- (removal) + classify, modeled after the verb 'to declassify' (from 'declassify,' itself from 'de-' + 'classify').
