weedy

1. ADJ

/ ˈwidi / (AmE), / ˈwiːdi / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) very thin and physically weak in appearance; lacking flesh or muscle.
very thin and weak-looking

Examples

As a teenager he was rather weedy and lacked confidence in sports.
The weedy boy struggled to lift the heavy backpack.
She described herself as a weedy child who preferred books to outdoor games.

Synset Examples

a child with skinny freckled legs
a long scrawny neck
pale bony hands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrawny - very thin and bony
  • Synonym: skinny - very thin, often unattractively so
  • Antonym: robust - strong and healthy
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00993331-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈwiːdi / (AmE), / ˈwiːdi / (BrE)
full of weeds or resembling weeds, especially with many wild, unwanted plants growing densely
full of or resembling weeds

Examples

The abandoned lot became weedy after just one summer.
She followed a weedy trail through the neglected garden.
The fields grew weedy and wild when no one maintained them.

Synset Examples

a weedy path
weedy plants that take over a garden

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overgrown - covered with plants that have grown in an uncontrolled way

Etymology

From 'weed' (an undesirable plant) + '-y' (adjective suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02550461-a
Synset POS: a
