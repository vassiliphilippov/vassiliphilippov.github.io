noradrenaline

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɔːr.əˈdrɛn.əl.ɪn / (AmE), / ˌnɒr.əˈdrɛn.ə.lɪn / (BrE)
a hormone and neurotransmitter that is released by nerve cells and the adrenal medulla, playing a role in the body's response to stress
hormone and neurotransmitter in stress response
medical

Examples

The body releases noradrenaline during stressful situations to prepare for action.
Low levels of noradrenaline may be linked to certain mood disorders.
Doctors measured the patient's noradrenaline levels to diagnose the condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: norepinephrine - the American English term for noradrenaline
  • Synonym: adrenaline - a hormone similar to noradrenaline involved in the fight-or-flight response

Etymology

From 'nor-' (indicating a chemical compound lacking a methyl group) + 'adrenaline'.
