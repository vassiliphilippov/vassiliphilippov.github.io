uptake

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌteɪk / (AmE), / ˈʌpteɪk / (BrE)
the process of taking food, liquid, or other substances into the body, especially through the mouth
process of taking substances into the body

Examples

The body's uptake of vitamins depends on various dietary factors.
Doctors monitor the uptake of nutrients in patients with digestive disorders.
Certain medications can affect the uptake of calcium in the bones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorption - the process by which substances are taken into tissues or cells

Etymology

From 'up' + 'take', first used in scientific contexts in the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌteɪk / (AmE), / ˈʌpteɪk / (BrE)
the process or act of absorbing, taking in, or consuming a substance or resource
process of taking in or absorbing

Examples

The plant's uptake of nutrients is essential for healthy growth.
Improved uptake of vaccines can lead to better public health outcomes.
The study measured the uptake of new technology among older adults.

Synset Examples

they developed paper napkins with a greater uptake of liquids

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorption - the process of taking in or being absorbed

Etymology

From the phrase 'take up', influenced by related verbs 'take' and 'up'.
