inadvisable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.ədˈvaɪ.zə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ədˈvaɪ.zə.bəl / (BrE)
not recommended or sensible; likely to have undesirable or negative consequences
not recommended; unwise

Examples

It would be inadvisable to travel during the storm.
Investing all your savings in one stock is generally considered inadvisable.
She realized it was inadvisable to confront him when he was so angry.

Synset Examples

an unnecessary and inadvisable action

Related Terms

  • Antonym: advisable - recommended; sensible; appropriate for action or decision

Etymology

From in- (not) + advisable, meaning not advisable.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.ədˈvaɪ.zə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ədˈvaɪ.zə.bəl / (BrE)
not recommended; unwise or not sensible to do in a given situation
not recommended; unwise to do

Examples

It would be inadvisable to travel alone at night in this area.
Wearing heavy coats in the summer heat is generally considered inadvisable.
Taking sides in their argument proved to be inadvisable for everyone involved.

Synset Examples

running on the ice is inadvisable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: advisable - wise or sensible to do; recommended

Etymology

From 'in-' (not) + 'advisable', from advise + -able.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00068376-a
Synset POS: a
