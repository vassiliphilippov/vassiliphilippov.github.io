Daboecia cantabrica

1. NOUN

/ dəˈbiːʃiə kænˈtæbrɪkə / (AmE), / dəˈbiːsɪə kænˈtæbrɪkə / (BrE)
A low, spreading evergreen shrub native to western Europe, with flowers ranging in color from white to rose-purple.
evergreen shrub of western Europe

Examples

The garden border was lined with Daboecia cantabrica, displaying its vibrant purple blossoms in spring.
She planted Daboecia cantabrica to add evergreen texture and color to her rockery.
In its native habitat, Daboecia cantabrica thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after St. Daboecus, an Irish saint; 'cantabrica' refers to Cantabria, a region in Spain.
