forecaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrkæstər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːkɑːstə / (BrE)
a person who predicts future events or trends, especially using expertise or scientific methods
person who predicts the future

Examples

The weather forecaster predicted heavy rain for the weekend.
Many people rely on a financial forecaster for investment advice.
The forecaster explained how climate models are used to predict future temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predictor - a person or thing that predicts something

Etymology

From 'forecast' (verb: to predict), derived from Old English 'fore-' (before) + 'cast' (to throw, to calculate).
