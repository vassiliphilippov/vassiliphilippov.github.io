Lathyrus pratensis

1. NOUN

/ ləˈθaɪərəs prəˈtɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ləˈθaɪərəs praˈtɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a scrambling perennial Eurasian wild pea with yellowish flowers and compressed seed pods, cultivated for forage
a yellow-flowered Eurasian wild pea

Examples

Lathyrus pratensis is often found in meadows and grassy places across Europe.
The bright yellow flowers of Lathyrus pratensis appear from May to August.
Farmers sometimes grow Lathyrus pratensis as a forage crop for livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meadow vetchling - common name for Lathyrus pratensis

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lathyrus' (the genus name for wild peas) and 'pratensis' (meadow-dwelling).
