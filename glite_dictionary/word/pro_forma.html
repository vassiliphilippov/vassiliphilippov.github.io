pro forma

1. ADJ

/ proʊ ˈfɔrmə / (AmE), / prəʊ ˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
done or produced as a matter of form or for the sake of formality, rather than for a substantive purpose
done as a formality or matter of form

Examples

The board approved the pro forma budget before reviewing the final figures.
It was a pro forma meeting, lasting only ten minutes with no decisions made.
His apology sounded pro forma rather than sincere.

Synset Examples

a one-candidate pro forma election

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: formal - done in accordance with rules or convention

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'for form's sake'.
