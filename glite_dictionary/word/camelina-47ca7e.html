Camelina

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæməˈliːnə / (AmE), / ˌkæməˈliːnə / (BrE)
a genus of annual and biennial herbs native to regions from the Mediterranean to central Asia, including plants commonly called false flax
genus of Mediterranean and Asian herbs

Examples

Researchers are studying Camelina as a potential source of biofuel due to its oil-rich seeds.
The Camelina plants thrived in the dry, rocky soil of the Mediterranean climate.
Farmers have begun to grow Camelina as an alternative crop for improving soil health.

Etymology

From Latin Camelina, a type of plant, possibly from Greek kamēlinos (hemp-like).
