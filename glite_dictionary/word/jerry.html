Jerry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛri / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛri / (BrE)
An offensive slang term referring to a person of German descent, especially during wartime.
offensive term for a German person
offensive slang british

Examples

During the war, British soldiers often referred to German troops as Jerries.
The old film used the term Jerry to describe enemy soldiers, reflecting the prejudices of its time.
Calling someone a Jerry is now considered highly offensive and inappropriate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kraut - offensive slang term for a German person
  • Synonym: Hun - offensive slang term for a German person, especially during WWI

Etymology

Originated as British wartime slang, possibly from German given names or 'German'.
