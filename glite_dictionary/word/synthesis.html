synthesis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnθəsɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪnθəsɪs / (BrE)
the process of producing a chemical compound by combining simpler substances
process of creating a chemical compound

Examples

The synthesis of aspirin in the laboratory requires several steps.
Modern pharmaceuticals often rely on the synthesis of complex organic molecules.
Researchers developed an efficient synthesis for the new antibiotic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decomposition - the process in which a chemical compound is broken down into simpler compounds or elements

Etymology

From Greek synthesis (a putting together), from syn- (together) + tithenai (to put).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnθəsɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪnθəsɪs / (BrE)
In logic and philosophy, reasoning from general principles to specific cases or from causes to effects.
reasoning from general to particular

Examples

The philosopher explained the process of synthesis as moving from broad theories to individual examples.
In scientific reasoning, synthesis allows researchers to predict outcomes based on established principles.
Her argument relied on synthesis to derive concrete conclusions from abstract laws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deduction - logical reasoning from general to specific

Etymology

From Greek *synthesis* (a putting together), from *synthithemi* (to put together).

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnθəsɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪnθəsɪs / (BrE)
the combination of separate ideas or elements to form a unified whole
combining elements into a whole

Examples

Her essay provided a thoughtful synthesis of the main points from several sources.
The conference encouraged the synthesis of ideas from different scientific disciplines.
Successful innovation often depends on the synthesis of old concepts and new technologies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: analysis - the separation of a whole into its component parts

Etymology

From Greek *synthesis* (putting together), from *syn-* (together) + *thesis* (placing).
