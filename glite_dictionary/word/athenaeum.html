athenaeum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæθəˈniːəm / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈniːəm / (BrE)
a literary or scientific association or club, often devoted to the promotion of learning and the arts
literary or scientific association

Examples

The city's old athenaeum still hosts weekly lectures on science and literature.
She became a member of the local athenaeum to connect with fellow writers and scholars.
Founded in the 19th century, the athenaeum played a vital role in community education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academy - a society or institution for the advancement of art, literature, or science
  • Synonym: club - an organization dedicated to a particular interest or activity

Etymology

From Latin 'Athenaeum', name of a school in Rome, ultimately from Greek 'Athēnaion', temple of Athena.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæθəˈniːəm / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈniːəm / (BrE)
a building or institution dedicated to literary or scientific study, reading, or learning, often providing reading materials for public use
institution or building for reading or study

Examples

The town's athenaeum hosts regular book clubs and lectures for the community.
She visited the historic athenaeum to conduct research for her thesis.
Many rare manuscripts are housed in the city's main athenaeum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: library - a place where books and other materials are kept for reading, study, or reference

Etymology

From Latin 'Athenaeum', originally named after a temple of Athena in ancient Rome used for literary and scientific pursuits.
