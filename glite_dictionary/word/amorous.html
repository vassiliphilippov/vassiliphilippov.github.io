amorous

1. ADJ

/ ˈæmərəs / (AmE), / ˈamərəs / (BrE)
showing, feeling, or relating to sexual desire or romantic love
showing sexual or romantic love

Examples

He sent her amorous messages that left little doubt about his intentions.
The play features an amorous couple whose relationship is central to the plot.
She responded with an amorous glance that made his heart race.

Synset Examples

her amatory affairs
amorous glances
a romantic adventure
a romantic moonlight ride

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romantic - emotionally loving or affectionate in a sentimental way
  • Antonym: platonic - relating to a close relationship that is not sexual

Etymology

From Old French *amoureux* (loving), from Medieval Latin *amorosus*, from Latin *amor* (love).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæmərəs / (AmE), / ˈamərəs / (BrE)
showing, feeling, or relating to romantic or sexual love
showing or feeling romantic love

Examples

He gave her an amorous glance across the crowded room.
After dinner, the couple was in an amorous mood, holding hands and laughing.
The poem describes the amorous adventures of a young man in Paris.

Synset Examples

feeling amorous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romantic - characterized by or expressing romance or love
  • Synonym: passionate - showing or caused by strong feelings or a strong belief
  • Antonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned

Etymology

From Old French *amoureux*, from Latin *amor* (love).
