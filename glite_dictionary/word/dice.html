2. VERB

/ daɪs / (AmE), / daɪs / (BrE)
to cut food into small cubes, typically as part of food preparation
cut into small cubes (food)

Examples

Carefully dice the onions before adding them to the pan.
The recipe instructs you to dice the potatoes into half-inch pieces.
She diced the tomatoes for the salsa.

Synset Examples

cube the cheese

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cube - to cut into cubes

Etymology

From Old French 'déis', plural of 'dé' (die, playing cube), referring to small cubes; extended to cubes of food.

3. NOUN

/ daɪs / (AmE), / daɪs / (BrE)
A small cube with its six faces marked with spots from 1 to 6, used in games of chance to generate random numbers.
small cube used in games of chance

Examples

He rolled the dice to see who would go first in the game.
A pair of loaded dice was discovered at the gambling table.
The game requires you to throw two dice and move your piece accordingly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - singular form for one cube used in games of chance

Etymology

From Old French *dé*, from Latin *datum* (something given, a throw), past participle of *dare* (to give).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03195713-n
Synset POS: n
