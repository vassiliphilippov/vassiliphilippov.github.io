analytical

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.əˈlɪ.t̬ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌæn.əˈlɪ.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to or using logical reasoning and analysis, especially by breaking a whole into its parts to understand it better.
relating to analysis or logical reasoning

Examples

Her analytical skills make her an excellent scientist.
The detective took an analytical approach to solving the mystery.
He impressed the interviewers with his analytical thinking.

Synset Examples

an analytic experiment
an analytic approach
a keenly analytic man
analytical reasoning
an analytical mind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synthetic - involving putting together parts or elements to form a whole; opposite of analytical

Etymology

From Greek analytikos, from analyein (to analyze).

2. ADJ

/ əˌnælɪˈtɪkəl / (AmE), / əˌnælɪˈtɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a proposition) necessarily true by virtue of its meaning, independent of fact or experience.
necessarily true by meaning, not by fact

Examples

In philosophy, an analytical proposition is one that is true by definition alone.
The statement 'All bachelors are unmarried' is analytical because the predicate is contained in the subject.
Distinguishing between analytical and synthetic judgments was central to Kant's philosophy.

Synset Examples

‘all spinsters are unmarried’ is an analytic proposition

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synthetic - (of a proposition) not necessarily true by meaning, but by how it relates to experience or fact

Etymology

From Greek analytikos (skilled in analysis), via Medieval Latin analyticus and French analytique.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00111664-a
Synset POS: a
