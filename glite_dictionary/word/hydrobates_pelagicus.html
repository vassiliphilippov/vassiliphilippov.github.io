Hydrobates pelagicus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑːbeɪts pɛˈlɑːdʒɪkəs / (AmE), / hʌɪˈdrəʊbeɪts pɛˈladʒɪkəs / (BrE)
A small, sooty-black petrel with white markings, found in the northern Atlantic and Mediterranean regions; also known as the European storm petrel.
species of storm petrel (bird)

Examples

The Hydrobates pelagicus is often seen gliding low over the waves during rough weather.
During their breeding season, Hydrobates pelagicus nests in crevices along remote sea cliffs.
Researchers tracked the migration routes of the elusive Hydrobates pelagicus to study its feeding habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European storm petrel - Common English name for Hydrobates pelagicus

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro-' (water) and 'bates' (one who treads); pelagicus from Greek 'pelagos' (sea).
