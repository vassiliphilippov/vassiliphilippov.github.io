viosterol

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈɑstərɒl / (AmE), / vaɪˈɒstərɒl / (BrE)
a preparation of vitamin D used to prevent and treat rickets, formerly derived by irradiating ergosterol
a vitamin D preparation formerly used against rickets
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed viosterol supplements to treat the child's rickets.
Before modern vitamin D tablets, viosterol was widely used in medicine.
Researchers once produced viosterol by irradiating yeast-derived ergosterol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin D - a group of fat-soluble vitamins important for bone health
  • Degree Lower: ergosterol - a sterol precursor irradiated to produce viosterol

Etymology

From Latin *vīum* (life) + Greek *stear* (fat), reflecting its role and source.
