trichloride

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈklɔːr.aɪd / (AmE), / traɪˈklɔːr.aɪd / (BrE)
a compound containing three chlorine atoms in each molecule.
compound with three chlorine atoms

Examples

Phosphorus trichloride is widely used in the chemical industry.
The reaction produced a trichloride as one of its main products.
Always handle trichloride compounds with appropriate safety equipment.

Etymology

From tri- (three) + chloride (compound of chlorine).
