Mercurialis perennis

1. NOUN

/ mərˌkjʊəriˈælɪs pəˈrɛnɪs / (AmE), / mɜːˌkjʊəriˈælɪs pəˈrɛnɪs / (BrE)
A European perennial plant of the spurge family, with greenish flowers, commonly found in woodland and known as dog's mercury.
European woodland plant with greenish flowers

Examples

Mercurialis perennis grows abundantly in shady European woodlands.
The toxic properties of Mercurialis perennis can be harmful if ingested by livestock.
In early spring, Mercurialis perennis produces clusters of small greenish flowers.

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Mercurialis' said to be named after Mercury, the Roman god; 'perennis' meaning 'perennial'.
