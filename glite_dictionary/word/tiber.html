Tiber

1. PROPN

/ ˈtaɪbər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪbə / (BrE)
a river in central Italy that flows through Rome and empties into the Tyrrhenian Sea
river in central Italy, through Rome

Examples

The Tiber has played a vital role in the history of Rome.
Many ancient Roman monuments are situated along the banks of the Tiber.
During the flood season, the Tiber sometimes overflows its banks in Rome.

Etymology

From Latin Tiberis, the ancient Roman name for the river.
