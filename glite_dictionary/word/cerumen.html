cerumen

1. NOUN

/ səˈrumən / (AmE), / sɪˈruːmən / (BrE)
the soft yellowish waxy substance secreted by glands in the ear canal, commonly known as earwax
earwax; waxy substance in ear canal
medical

Examples

Excess cerumen can sometimes block the ear canal and affect hearing.
The nurse carefully removed the impacted cerumen from the patient's ear.
Regular cleaning is not recommended as cerumen protects the ear from dust and bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earwax - common name for the waxy substance produced in the ear canal

Etymology

From Latin 'cerumen' (wax), related to 'cera' (wax).
