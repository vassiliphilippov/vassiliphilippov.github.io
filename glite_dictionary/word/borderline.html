borderline

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔːrdərˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdəˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a line marking the boundary between two places, areas, or countries
a boundary line

Examples

The river acts as a natural borderline between the two countries.
They planted trees along the borderline to clearly mark their property.
The dispute was about where the exact borderline lay between the two provinces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundary - a real or imaginary line that marks the edge of something
  • Synonym: border - the edge or boundary of something, or the dividing line between two areas

Etymology

From 'border' + 'line', first recorded in the early 19th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɔrdərˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdəlaɪn / (BrE)
of questionable quality or very close to failing or being unacceptable; barely adequate or acceptable
barely adequate; of questionable quality

Examples

Her performance was considered borderline and failed to impress the judges.
The candidate's qualifications are borderline for this position, so we're not sure she'll be selected.
Some students received borderline grades, putting their graduation in jeopardy.

Synset Examples

borderline grades
marginal writing ability

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marginal - of minimal quality or barely acceptable
  • Antonym: excellent - of extremely high quality; outstanding

Etymology

From 'border' + 'line,' originally referring to something lying on a dividing line or boundary.
