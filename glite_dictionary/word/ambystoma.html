Ambystoma

1. NOUN

/ æmˈbɪstəˌmə / (AmE), / æmˈbɪstəˌmə / (BrE)
A genus of salamanders commonly known as mole salamanders, native to North America.
genus of mole salamanders

Examples

The axolotl, a member of the genus Ambystoma, is well known for its remarkable regenerative abilities.
Researchers are studying Ambystoma species to understand their adaptation to various aquatic environments.
Many Ambystoma salamanders spend much of their lives buried underground.

Etymology

From New Latin Ambystoma, the taxonomic name.

2. NOUN

/ æmˈbɪstəˌmə / (AmE), / æmˈbɪstəmə / (BrE)
A genus of mole salamanders in the family Ambystomatidae, including species such as the axolotl and tiger salamander.
a genus of mole salamanders

Examples

The Ambystoma genus includes the well-known axolotl, a unique paedomorphic salamander.
Many North American salamanders belong to the Ambystoma genus, such as the spotted salamander.
Scientists are studying Ambystoma species to understand limb regeneration.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, introduced as a scientific name; possibly from Greek 'amphistomos' (with a mouth at both ends), alluding to larval forms.
