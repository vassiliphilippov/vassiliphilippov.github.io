Thermopsis

1. NOUN

/ θərˈmɑːp.sɪs / (AmE), / θɜːˈmɒp.sɪs / (BrE)
A genus of showy perennial herbs, commonly known as bush peas, native to North America and Asia, characterized by rhizomatous growth.
genus of showy bush peas (plants)

Examples

Thermopsis plants are valued in gardens for their vibrant yellow flowers.
Botanists identified a new species of Thermopsis in the mountainous region.
The leaves of Thermopsis resemble those of the common pea.

Etymology

From Greek 'thermē' (heat) and 'opsis' (appearance), referring to the plant's showy nature.

2. NOUN

/ θərˈmɑːp.sɪs / (AmE), / θəˈmɒp.sɪs / (BrE)
a genus of American and Asian showy, rhizomatous, leguminous herbs commonly known as bush peas
genus of bush pea plants

Examples

The vibrant yellow flowers of Thermopsis make it a striking addition to gardens.
Many species of Thermopsis are found in the western United States.
Botanists studied the rhizomes of Thermopsis to understand its growth patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lupin - a similar leguminous plant often confused with Thermopsis

Etymology

From Greek thermos (heat) and -opsis (appearance), referring to the plant's resemblance to lupins.
