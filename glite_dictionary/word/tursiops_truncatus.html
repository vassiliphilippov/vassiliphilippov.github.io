Tursiops truncatus

1. NOUN

/ tɝˈsaɪɒps trʌŋˈkeɪtəs / (AmE), / tʊəˈsaɪɒps trʌŋˈkeɪtəs / (BrE)
the common bottlenose dolphin, a widely known marine mammal found in the northern Atlantic and Mediterranean, often kept in captivity and trained for performances
common bottlenose dolphin (marine mammal)

Examples

The Tursiops truncatus is one of the most studied marine mammals due to its intelligence and adaptability.
Many aquariums feature Tursiops truncatus in educational and entertainment programs.
Researchers have discovered complex communication abilities among wild Tursiops truncatus groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottlenose dolphin - a common, intelligent dolphin species known for its bottle-shaped snout and playful behavior

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Tursiops' (from 'tursio', a kind of dolphin) + species name 'truncatus' (meaning 'truncated' or 'shortened').
