leptorrhinic

1. ADJ

/ ˌlɛptəˈraɪnɪk / (AmE), / ˌlɛptəˈrɪnɪk / (BrE)
having a long, narrow nose, especially used in anthropological or medical contexts.
having a long, narrow nose
medical

Examples

The anthropologist described the skull as leptorrhinic due to its distinct nasal proportions.
Certain populations are more likely to exhibit a leptorrhinic nasal type.
A leptorrhinic nose is characterized by longer and narrower nasal openings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: platyrrhine - having a broad, flat nose

Etymology

From Greek leptos (thin, slender) + rhis, rhinos (nose) + -ic.
