critical

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
of decisive importance; involving or constituting a turning point or crisis.
decisive or turning-point (situation, moment)

Examples

We are approaching a critical moment in the peace negotiations.
The patient's condition became critical overnight, requiring immediate attention.
Making the right decision at this critical juncture could save the company.

Synset Examples

a critical point in the campaign
the critical test

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crucial - extremely important or decisive
  • Antonym: insignificant - not important or decisive

Etymology

From Greek kritikos (able to discern, judge), via Latin criticus.

7. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪdɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a value or state) relating to the point at which a property or phenomenon undergoes a sudden or significant change, especially having the necessary mass to sustain a nuclear chain reaction.
at a threshold where change occurs suddenly

Examples

When the uranium sample reached its critical mass, it could sustain a chain reaction.
Water reaches its critical temperature at 100°C under standard atmospheric pressure.
A nuclear reactor must stay below the critical point to operate safely.

Synset Examples

a critical temperature of water is 100 degrees C — its boiling point at standard atmospheric pressure
critical mass
go critical

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threshold - the point or level at which something begins or changes
  • Antonym: subcritical - below the threshold for a chain reaction or abrupt change
  • Degree Higher: supercritical - above the threshold for a chain reaction or abrupt change

Etymology

From Greek *kritikos* (able to discern or judge), later technical use in science.

Source: WordNet
