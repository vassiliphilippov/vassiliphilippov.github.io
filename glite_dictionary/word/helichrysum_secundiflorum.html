Helichrysum secundiflorum

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːlɪˈkraɪsəm sɪˌkʌndɪˈflɔːrəm / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪˈkrɪsəm sɪˌkʌndɪˈflɒrəm / (BrE)
A shrub with white woolly branches and leaves, bearing fragrant flowers in long sprays; often used for dried floral arrangements and sometimes classified in the genus Helichrysum.
a shrub with woolly leaves and fragrant flowers

Examples

Helichrysum secundiflorum is often prized by gardeners for its fragrant, woolly flowers.
Dried arrangements made with Helichrysum secundiflorum can last for months without losing their scent.
The silver foliage of Helichrysum secundiflorum contrasts beautifully with its bright yellow flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'helios' (sun) and 'chrysos' (gold), referring to the flower's appearance; 'secundiflorum' from Latin, meaning 'with flowers on one side.'
