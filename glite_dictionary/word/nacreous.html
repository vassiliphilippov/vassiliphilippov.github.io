nacreous

1. ADJ

/ ˈneɪkriəs / (AmE), / ˈneɪkriəs / (BrE)
having a shiny, iridescent surface like that of mother-of-pearl; pearly.
resembling mother-of-pearl; pearly

Examples

The nacreous lining of the shell shimmered in the sunlight, reflecting subtle colors.
She admired the nacreous clouds spreading across the evening sky.
The artist used nacreous paints to give the fish scales a natural, luminous finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pearly - having the qualities or appearance of a pearl
  • Synonym: iridescent - showing luminous colors that change when seen from different angles

Etymology

From Latin nacreus (of mother-of-pearl), from French nacre (mother-of-pearl).

2. ADJ

/ ˈneɪ.kri.əs / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.krɪ.əs / (BrE)
having a lustrous, iridescent quality like that of mother-of-pearl
having a pearly, iridescent appearance

Examples

The shell's nacreous interior shimmered in the sunlight, displaying subtle pinks and greens.
She admired the nacreous sheen on the delicate pendant.
High in the sky, nacreous clouds glowed with shifting, pastel colors after sunset.

Synset Examples

an iridescent oil slick
nacreous (or pearlescent) clouds looking like mother-of-pearl
a milky opalescent (or opaline) luster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iridescent - showing luminous colors that seem to change when seen from different angles
  • Synonym: pearlescent - having the gleaming, lustrous appearance of pearl

Etymology

From Latin 'nacre' (mother-of-pearl), via French 'nacre'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00282886-s
Synset POS: s
