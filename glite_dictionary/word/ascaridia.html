Ascaridia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæskəˈrɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌaskəˈrɪdɪə / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic roundworms, typically found in the intestines of birds, characterized by the presence of a preanal sucker.
a genus of parasitic roundworms
medical

Examples

Veterinarians often encounter Ascaridia infections in poultry, leading to digestive issues and poor growth.
Symptoms of Ascaridia infestation in birds may include lethargy and loss of appetite.
The presence of a preanal sucker distinguishes Ascaridia from other roundworm genera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roundworm - parasitic or free-living worm of the phylum Nematoda

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'askaris' (intestinal worm).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæskəˈrɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌaskəˈrɪdɪə / (BrE)
A genus of roundworms, characterized by possessing a preanal sucker, often parasitic in birds and sometimes other animals.

Examples

Certain species within the Ascaridia genus can cause health issues in poultry.
Researchers are studying the life cycle of Ascaridia to develop better treatments.
