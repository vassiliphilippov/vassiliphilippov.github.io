daftly

1. ADV

/ ˈdæftli / (AmE), / ˈdɑːftli / (BrE)
In a silly, foolish, or mildly insane manner.
in a foolish or silly manner

Examples

He daftly attempted to balance a chair on his head during the party.
She daftly insisted on wearing sandals in the snow.
The characters in the play behaved daftly to provoke laughter from the audience.

Synset Examples

the old lady is beginning to behave quite dottily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolishly - in a way that shows a lack of good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: sensibly - in a way that shows good sense or judgment

Etymology

From 'daft' (Middle English, foolish, stupid) + -ly (forming adverbs).
