Trialeurodes

1. NOUN

/ traɪəˌlɔːroʊˈdiːz / (AmE), / traɪəˌlʊərəˈdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small whiteflies in the family Aleyrodidae, some species of which are important agricultural pests.
genus of whiteflies (insects)

Examples

Trialeurodes vaporariorum, the greenhouse whitefly, is a common pest in many parts of the world.
Scientists are studying the life cycle of Trialeurodes to develop better pest management strategies.
Infestations of Trialeurodes can severely damage crop yields in greenhouses.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining tri- (three) and Aleyrodes (a related genus of whitefly).

2. NOUN

/ traɪˌæljʊˈroʊdiːz / (AmE), / traɪˌæljuːˈrəʊdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small whiteflies in the family Aleyrodidae, some of which are agricultural pests.
genus of small whiteflies

Examples

The greenhouse whitefly belongs to the genus Trialeurodes, which contains several pest species.
Researchers found that Trialeurodes infestations caused significant damage to tomato plants.
Integrated pest management strategies often target members of the genus Trialeurodes to protect crops.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining elements of Greek 'tri-' (three), 'aleuron' (flour), and typical taxonomic suffix '-odes'.
