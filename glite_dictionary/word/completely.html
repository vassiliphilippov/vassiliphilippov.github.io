completely

1. ADV

/ kəmˈplitli / (AmE), / kəmˈpliːtli / (BrE)
In every way or as a whole; entirely, with nothing lacking.
entirely, fully, with nothing lacking

Examples

She forgot to completely lock the door before leaving for work.
The house was completely destroyed by the fire, leaving nothing behind.
I completely agree with your opinion on this matter.

Synset Examples

he had filled out the form completely
the apartment was completely furnished

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entirely - to the full or whole extent, without exception or exclusion
  • Antonym: partially - in part; not totally

Etymology

From 'complete' + suffix '-ly', modeled on similar adverbs.

2. ADV

/ kəmˈpliːtli / (AmE), / kəmˈpliːtli / (BrE)
To the full extent; totally; in every way or part.
totally; in every way or part

Examples

The instructions were completely clear and easy to follow.
She completely forgot about the meeting and missed it.
After the renovations, the house looked completely different from before.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entirely - To the full or entire extent, wholly
  • Antonym: partially - Only in part; to some degree or in some parts

Etymology

Formed from 'complete' plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'. 'Complete' from Latin 'completus' (filled up).
