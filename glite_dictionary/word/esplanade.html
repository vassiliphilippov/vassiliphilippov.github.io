esplanade

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛspləˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˌɛspləˈnɑːd / (BrE)
a long, open, level area, usually beside the sea, designed for walking or strolling
open walkway by the sea

Examples

We took a leisurely stroll along the esplanade at sunset.
The city renovated the esplanade to attract more visitors to the waterfront.
Joggers and cyclists share the esplanade every morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promenade - a paved public walk, especially one along the seafront at a resort
  • Synonym: boardwalk - a wooden walkway along a beach or waterfront

Etymology

From French 'esplanade', from Italian 'spianata' (level stretch), from 'spianare' (to make level).
