penult

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.ʌlt / (AmE), / ˈpiː.nʌlt / (BrE)
the second-to-last syllable in a word, especially as used in the study of word stress or pronunciation
the next-to-last syllable in a word

Examples

The stress in the word 'banana' falls on the penult.
In certain languages, accent always goes on the penult.
If you remove the penult, the word's rhythm changes significantly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ultima - the last syllable of a word
  • Degree Lower: antepenult - the third-to-last syllable of a word

Etymology

From Latin *paenultima* (the almost last), from *paene* (almost) + *ultima* (last).
