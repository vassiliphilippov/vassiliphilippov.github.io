sixth-former

1. NOUN

/ sɪksθ ˈfɔrmər / (AmE), / sɪksθ ˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
a student in the sixth form of a British secondary school, typically aged 16 to 18
student aged 16–18 in British sixth form
british

Examples

After her exams, the sixth-former applied to several top universities.
As a sixth-former, he took on more responsibilities within the school.
The science club is open to any sixth-former interested in research projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sixth form - the final two years of secondary education in Britain

Etymology

From 'sixth form', denoting the final two years of secondary education in Britain, plus the agent suffix '-er'.
