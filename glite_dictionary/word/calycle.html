calycle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkælɪkəl / (BrE)
a small cup-shaped structure found in plants and animals, such as the cavity in a coral containing a polyp or a formation resembling a cup in tissues like taste buds or the optic cup
small cup-shaped biological structure

Examples

The coral's calycle protects the soft polyp inside its hard structure.
Researchers examined the calycles located at the base of each taste bud under the microscope.
Each polyp lives in a separate calycle within the colony, providing it with stability.

Etymology

From Greek 'kalyx' (cup, husk), with diminutive suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkælɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkælɪkəl / (BrE)
a small group of bracts resembling a calyx, especially as found beneath the flowers of plants such as carnations or hibiscus
group of bracts resembling a calyx

Examples

The calycle of the carnation is composed of several green bracts that protect the flower.
In some species, the calycle can be mistaken for the flower's calyx due to its similar appearance.
Botanists examined the calycle structure beneath the hibiscus blooms for research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calyx - the outermost whorl of a flower, typically made up of sepals

Etymology

From French 'calycle', diminutive of 'calyxe' (calyx), from Greek 'kalyx' (case, husk).
