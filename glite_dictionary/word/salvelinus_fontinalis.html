Salvelinus fontinalis

1. NOUN

/ sæl.vəˈlaɪ.nəs fɒn.tɪˈnæl.ɪs / (AmE), / sæl.vəˈliː.nəs fɒn.tɪˈnæl.ɪs / (BrE)
a species of North American freshwater trout, commonly known as the brook trout, also introduced to European waters.
North American freshwater trout species

Examples

The Salvelinus fontinalis is highly prized by anglers for its fighting spirit and beautiful coloration.
Conservationists are working to restore native habitats for Salvelinus fontinalis in Appalachian streams.
In Europe, Salvelinus fontinalis has been introduced to certain mountain lakes where it competes with local fish species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brook trout - common name for Salvelinus fontinalis

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Salvelinus' and species name 'fontinalis', meaning 'of springs or fountains'.
