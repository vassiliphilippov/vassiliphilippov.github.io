theosophism

1. NOUN

/ θiˈɑːsəˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / θɪˈɒsəˌfɪzəm / (BrE)
belief in or adherence to the principles of theosophy, a system of mystical and occult philosophy.
belief in theosophy

Examples

His study of theosophism greatly influenced his philosophical writings.
Adherents to theosophism believe in a universal spiritual wisdom beyond organized religion.
The history of theosophism in the West dates back to the late 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theosophy - a system of mystical and occult philosophy seeking direct knowledge of the divine

Etymology

From theosophy + -ism; theosophy from Greek theos (god) + sophia (wisdom).
