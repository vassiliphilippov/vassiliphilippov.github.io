kavakava

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːvəˈkɑːvə / (AmE), / ˌkɑːvəˈkɑːvə / (BrE)
An alcoholic beverage made from the aromatic roots of the kava shrub, traditionally consumed in the South Pacific islands.
alcoholic drink from kava shrub roots

Examples

Many tourists try kavakava at traditional ceremonies in Fiji.
The taste of kavakava is earthy and slightly bitter, with numbing effects on the mouth.
Villagers sat in a circle, sharing bowls of freshly prepared kavakava.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kava - the plant whose roots are used to make kavakava

Etymology

From Tongan or other Polynesian languages, directly from 'kava' (the plant and its drink).
