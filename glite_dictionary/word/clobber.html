clobber

1. VERB

/ ˈklɑːbər / (AmE), / ˈklɒbə / (BrE)
to defeat thoroughly and decisively in a competition or fight
defeat thoroughly (informal)
informal

Examples

The home team managed to clobber their rivals with a final score of 8–1.
If we train hard enough, we can clobber them in the finals next week.
She absolutely clobbered her opponent in the chess tournament.

Synset Examples

We licked the other team on Sunday!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrash - defeat soundly in a contest
  • Synonym: to trounce - defeat decisively
  • Antonym: to lose - to be defeated in a competition or fight

/ ˈklɑbɚ / (AmE), / ˈklɒbə / (BrE)

informal

Examples

If you don't duck, he's going to clobber you with that bat.
The champion clobbered his opponent in the final round of the match.
They were clobbering the punching bag during their intense workout.
