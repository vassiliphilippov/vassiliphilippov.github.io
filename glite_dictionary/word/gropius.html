Gropius

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡroʊpiəs / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊpiəs / (BrE)
(Walter) Gropius (1883–1969), German-born American architect, founder of the Bauhaus School, influential in modern architecture.
German-born American architect, Bauhaus founder

Examples

The vision of Gropius helped shape the principles of modern architecture.
Many students at the Bauhaus were directly influenced by Gropius's innovative teaching methods.
Gropius designed several iconic buildings during his career in both Germany and the United States.
