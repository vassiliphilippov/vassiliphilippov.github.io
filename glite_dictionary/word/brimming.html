brimming

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
full to the point of overflowing; filled with a particular quality or emotion
full to overflowing; abundantly filled

Examples

The basket was brimming with freshly picked apples from the orchard.
She looked at him with eyes brimming with tears of happiness.
The hall was brimming with excitement as everyone waited for the announcement.

Synset Examples

a brimful cup
I am brimful of chowder
a child brimming over with curiosity
eyes brimming with tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overflowing - so full that the contents go over the top
  • Antonym: empty - containing nothing; not filled

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'brim', meaning to fill to the brim.
