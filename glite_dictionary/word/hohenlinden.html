Hohenlinden

1. PROPN

/ ˌhoʊənˈlɪndən / (AmE), / ˌhəʊənˈlɪndən / (BrE)
the site of a significant battle during the Napoleonic Wars in 1800, where French forces defeated the Austrians
site of 1800 Napoleonic Wars battle

Examples

The Battle of Hohenlinden was a decisive French victory during the Napoleonic Wars.
Poets such as Thomas Campbell have written about Hohenlinden in their work.
Historians often cite Hohenlinden as a turning point in the campaign against Austria.

Etymology

German place name, meaning 'high linden (tree)'.
