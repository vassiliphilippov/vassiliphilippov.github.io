to variegate

1. VERB

/ ˈver.i.əˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈveə.ri.ɪˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
to change the appearance of something, especially by marking it with different colors; to diversify in external appearance
to mark with different colors

Examples

Gardeners often variegate leaves to create more visually interesting plants.
The artist decided to variegate the background with splashes of red and gold.
Natural minerals sometimes variegate rocks, giving them intricate patterns and colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diversify - to make varied or more diverse
  • Antonym: monochrome - having or consisting of one color

Etymology

From Latin 'variegatus', past participle of 'variegare' (to make various), from 'varius' (various) + '-agare' (to make).

to make something more diverse or varied, often by introducing different colors, elements, or features
make diverse or varied

Examples

Gardeners often variegate their flower beds with plants of different colors and heights.
Adding new ingredients can variegate the traditional recipe and make it more interesting.
