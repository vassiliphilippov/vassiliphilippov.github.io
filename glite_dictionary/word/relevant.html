relevant

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛl.ə.vənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛl.ɪ.vənt / (BrE)
closely connected with or appropriate to what is being done or considered.
closely connected; applicable

Examples

Only send documents that are relevant to the application process.
Her comments were not relevant to the main topic of the meeting.
The judge asked the lawyer to provide relevant evidence.

Synset Examples

the scientist corresponds with colleagues in order to learn about matters relevant to her own research

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irrelevant - not connected with or relevant to something
  • Synonym: pertinent - relevant or applicable to a particular matter

Etymology

From Medieval Latin relevantem, present participle of relevare (to raise up).
