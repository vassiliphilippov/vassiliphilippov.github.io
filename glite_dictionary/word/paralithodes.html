Paralithodes

1. NOUN

/ ˌpær.ə.lɪˈθoʊ.diːz / (AmE), / ˌpar.ə.lɪˈθəʊ.diːz / (BrE)
a genus of large king crabs in the family Lithodidae, which includes some commercially important species.
genus of large king crabs

Examples

The red king crab, classified under Paralithodes, is highly prized for its meat.
Marine biologists studied several species within the genus Paralithodes to better understand their distribution.
Commercial fisheries often target Paralithodes species for their lucrative seafood market.

Etymology

From Greek 'para' (beside) and 'lithos' (stone), referencing their habitat and relation within Lithodidae.
