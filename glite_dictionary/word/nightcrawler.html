nightcrawler

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtˌkrɔlər / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtˌkrɑːlə / (BrE)
A large, terrestrial earthworm that burrows in soil and often comes to the surface at night or during wet conditions, commonly used as bait by anglers.
large earthworm used as bait

Examples

After the rain, we found a nightcrawler on the driveway.
He used a nightcrawler as bait to catch the fish.
Gardeners appreciate the nightcrawler for its ability to aerate the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthworm - a worm that lives in the soil
