depravity

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpræv.ə.ti / (AmE), / dɪˈpræv.ə.ti / (BrE)
moral corruption or wickedness; an act or practice of great immorality or vice
moral corruption; wickedness

Examples

She was appalled by the utter depravity revealed during the investigation.
Many novels depict the depravity of humanity during times of crisis.
The judge condemned the crime as an act of unimaginable depravity.

Synset Examples

the various turpitudes of modern society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corruption - dishonest or immoral behavior
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Late Latin 'depravitās', from Latin 'depravare' (to pervert).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈprævəti / (AmE), / dɪˈprævəti / (BrE)
Moral corruption; a state or act of wickedness or immorality.
moral corruption or wickedness

Examples

The novel explores the depravity that can arise when power is unchecked.
Many were shocked by the depravity described in the police reports.
The city’s history is filled with tales of both great achievement and great depravity.

Synset Examples

the luxury and corruption among the upper classes
moral degeneracy followed intellectual degeneration
its brothels, its opium parlors, its depravity
Rome had fallen into moral putrefaction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corruption - dishonest or immoral behavior, especially by those in power
  • Synonym: wickedness - the quality of being evil or morally wrong
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Late Latin 'depravitās' (perversion, corruption), from Latin 'depravare' (to distort, corrupt).
