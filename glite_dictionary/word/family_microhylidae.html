family Microhylidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊˈhɪlɪdi / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈhɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of frogs known as narrow-mouthed toads and sheep frogs, some of which are burrowing or arboreal, found in various regions worldwide.
a family of narrow-mouthed toads and sheep frogs

Examples

Species in the family Microhylidae can be found in both tropical and temperate regions.
Researchers discovered a new frog species belonging to the family Microhylidae in Papua New Guinea.
The family Microhylidae is known for its diversity in habitat preferences, with some species living in trees and others burrowing underground.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, created from Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'hyle' (wood) + taxonomic suffix '-idae'.
