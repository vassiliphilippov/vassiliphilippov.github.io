cleared

1. ADJ

/ klɪrd / (AmE), / klɪəd / (BrE)
having been declared free from blame, suspicion, or guilt
free from blame or guilt

Examples

After months of investigation, the mayor was cleared of all corruption charges.
The athlete felt relieved when his name was finally cleared by the committee.
She was cleared after new evidence proved her innocence.

Synset Examples

is absolved from all blame
was now clear of the charge of cowardice
his official honor is vindicated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vindicated - cleared of blame or suspicion
  • Synonym: absolved - freed from blame or responsibility
  • Antonym: accused - charged with a crime or fault

2. ADJ

/ ˈklɪrd / (AmE), / ˈklɪəd / (BrE)
(of an area or space) having been made free from objects, obstructions, or unwanted material, especially vegetation, debris, or fixtures.
free from objects or obstructions

Examples

The rescue team quickly made their way along the cleared path.
Farmers used the cleared field to plant their crops early in the season.
After the storm, most streets were still blocked, but a few had already been cleared.

Synset Examples

cleared land
cleared streets free of fallen trees and debris
a cleared passage through the underbrush
played poker on the cleared dining room table

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncleared - not free from objects or obstructions
