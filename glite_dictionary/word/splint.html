splint

1. NOUN

/ splɪnt / (AmE), / splɪnt / (BrE)
a small, thin strip of wood, often used for lighting fires or in crafts.
a thin strip of wood

Examples

He used a splint to transfer the flame from the candle to the fireplace.
The basket was woven from thin splints of willow.
Carefully breaking off a splint, she lit the campfire with it.

Synset Examples

he lit the fire with a burning splint

2. NOUN

/ splɪnt / (AmE), / splɪnt / (BrE)
a rigid or flexible device applied to a limb or part of the body to prevent movement and protect it, especially after injury or surgery
device for immobilizing body part
medical

Examples

The nurse used a splint to keep his fractured arm stable.
After the accident, a splint was applied to her leg until further treatment could be done.
Children sometimes need a splint to support their injured fingers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cast - a hard protective shell for immobilizing a broken bone

Etymology

From Middle English 'splente', meaning metal plate, from Old French 'esplente'.

3. VERB

/ splɪnt / (AmE), / splɪnt / (BrE)
to support or immobilize (a body part, especially a broken bone) with a splint
to support with a splint

Examples

The nurse carefully splinted the patient's fractured wrist.
You should splint the injured leg before moving the person to prevent further damage.
They splinted his finger using a small piece of wood and medical tape.

Synset Examples

splint a broken finger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to immobilize - to prevent from moving, especially in a medical context
  • Synonym: set - to put a broken bone into position for healing
