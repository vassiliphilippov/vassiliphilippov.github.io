Nycticorax nycticorax

1. NOUN

/ nɪk.tɪˈkoʊ.ræks nɪk.tɪˈkoʊ.ræks / (AmE), / nɪk.tɪˈkɔː.raks nɪk.tɪˈkɔː.raks / (BrE)
A species of night heron found widely in both the Old and New Worlds, known as the black-crowned night heron.
a species of night heron

Examples

The Nycticorax nycticorax is recognizable by its black crown and back contrasting with its grey wings.
Researchers observed a group of Nycticorax nycticorax feeding along the wetland at dusk.
Unlike other herons, the Nycticorax nycticorax is most active during the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: night heron - a medium-sized heron of the genus Nycticorax

Etymology

From Greek ‘nyktos’ (night) and ‘korax’ (raven), relating to the heron's nocturnal habits and dark coloring.
