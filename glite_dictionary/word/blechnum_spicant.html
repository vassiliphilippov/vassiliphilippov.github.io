Blechnum spicant

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɛk.nəm ˈspaɪ.kənt / (AmE), / ˈblɛk.nəm ˈspɪk.ənt / (BrE)
A species of fern native to Europe and western North America, with erect fronds, often cultivated and known as deer fern.
a species of fern; deer fern

Examples

Botanists often study Blechnum spicant because of its distinctive erect fronds and adaptability to different environments.
Gardeners sometimes plant Blechnum spicant in shady areas for its graceful, evergreen foliage.
In the wild, deer are known to browse on Blechnum spicant throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deer fern - Common name for Blechnum spicant, a fern species native to Europe and North America

Etymology

From Latin: 'Blechnum' meaning fern, and 'spicant' meaning spiked.
