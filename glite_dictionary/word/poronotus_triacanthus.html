Poronotus triacanthus

1. NOUN

/ pɔːrəˈnoʊtəs traɪəˈkænθəs / (AmE), / pɒrəˈnəʊtəs traɪəˈkanθəs / (BrE)
A small food fish found along the Atlantic coast, commonly known as the butterfish.
small food fish of the Atlantic coast

Examples

Fishermen along the eastern seaboard often catch Poronotus triacanthus during the summer months.
The mild flavor of Poronotus triacanthus makes it popular in a variety of seafood dishes.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of Poronotus triacanthus in the Atlantic Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfish - a small marine fish with a slippery, buttery texture

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name Poronotus and species name triacanthus (meaning 'three-spined').
