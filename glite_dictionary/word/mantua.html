mantua

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæntʃuːə, mænˈtuːə / (AmE), / ˈmæntjʊə, mænˈtjuːə / (BrE)
A loose-fitting gown with flowing lines, fashionable in the 17th and 18th centuries, usually worn by women.
loose gown of the 17th/18th centuries

Examples

The museum displayed a beautifully embroidered mantua from the early 1700s.
She wore a replica mantua to the historical reenactment ball.
Fashion in the 18th century often featured the elaborate mantua as evening attire.

Etymology

From French *mantu*, possibly named after the city of Mantua in Italy.
