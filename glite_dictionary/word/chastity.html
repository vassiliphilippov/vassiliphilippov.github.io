chastity

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæs.tə.ti / (AmE), / ˈtʃæs.tɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of refraining from sexual activity, especially outside of marriage or as a virtue.
sexual purity or abstinence

Examples

She took a vow of chastity as part of her religious commitment.
The novel explores the theme of chastity in a conservative society.
Historically, chastity was often considered a virtue, especially for women.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celibacy - the state of abstaining from marriage and sexual relations
  • Antonym: promiscuity - the practice of frequent, casual sexual relations with different partners

Etymology

From Latin 'castitas', from 'castus' meaning pure, innocent.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæstɪti / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːstɪti / (BrE)
the practice of abstaining from sexual activity, typically for reasons of religion or morality
abstinence from sexual activity

Examples

She took a vow of chastity and devoted herself to religious life.
The knight was admired for his chastity and moral integrity.
Throughout history, many cultures have placed a high value on chastity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celibacy - the state of abstaining from marriage and sexual relations, often for religious reasons
  • Antonym: lust - strong sexual desire; often seen as the opposite of chastity

Etymology

From Latin 'castitas' (purity, chasteness), from 'castus' (pure, chaste).
