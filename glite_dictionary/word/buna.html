Buna

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːnə / (AmE), / ˈbuːnə / (BrE)
A type of synthetic rubber produced by polymerizing butadiene, often blended with styrene or acrylonitrile to enhance its properties.
type of synthetic rubber

Examples

Tires are often made using Buna because of its durability and resistance to wear.
The factory increased production of Buna to meet demand for synthetic rubber during the war.
Scientists discovered that adding certain chemicals improved the flexibility of Buna.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synthetic rubber - a man-made elastic material, often used in manufacturing and industry

Etymology

From German, derived from 'butadiene' and 'Natrium' (sodium, an agent used in its production).
