trepid

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrɛpɪd / (AmE), / ˈtrɛpɪd / (BrE)
timid or nervous; showing a lack of confidence or courage
timid; showing nervousness
archaic

Examples

He gave a trepid glance around the unfamiliar room before stepping inside.
His trepid reply betrayed his fear of making a mistake.
The trepid child clung to her mother on the first day of school.

Synset Examples

timorous little mouse
in a timorous tone
cast fearful glances at the large dog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened
  • Synonym: fearful - feeling afraid; showing fear or anxiety
  • Antonym: bold - showing willingness to take risks; confident

Etymology

From Latin 'trepidus' (alarmed, agitated).
