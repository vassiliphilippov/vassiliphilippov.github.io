rigorous

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪɡ.ər.əs / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡ.ər.əs / (BrE)
extremely thorough, strict, or accurate; allowing no deviation from a standard
extremely strict or precise

Examples

The school is known for its rigorous academic standards and challenging coursework.
A rigorous inspection was carried out to ensure all safety regulations were met.
She followed a rigorous training schedule to prepare for the competition.

Synset Examples

rigorous application of the law
a strict vegetarian

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strict - demanding complete obedience or accuracy
  • Antonym: lax - not strict or severe; relaxed

Etymology

From Latin 'rigorosus' (full of hardness, strictness), from 'rigor' (stiffness, severity).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɪɡərəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡərəs / (BrE)
Demanding strict attention to rules, procedures, or standards; thorough and accurate.
strict and thorough

Examples

The school has a rigorous admissions process that only admits the most qualified students.
She followed a rigorous training schedule to prepare for the championship.
Only those who meet the rigorous safety requirements are allowed to work in the laboratory.

Synset Examples

rigorous discipline
tight security
stringent safety measures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stringent - strict and precise, especially regarding rules or standards
  • Antonym: lax - showing little strictness or attention to rules

Etymology

From Latin rigorosus (harsh, severe), from rigor (stiffness, strictness).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00714525-s
Synset POS: s
