Baptisia lactea

1. NOUN

/ bæpˈtɪʃiə ˈlæk.ti.ə / (AmE), / bapˈtɪsɪə ˈlak.tɪə / (BrE)
A species of wild indigo, an erect or spreading herb with racemes of creamy white flowers, native to the eastern United States.
a plant species with white racemes, US native

Examples

Baptisia lactea is often found growing in open woodlands throughout the eastern United States.
Gardeners prize Baptisia lactea for its attractive white flowers and hardy nature.
The creamy racemes of Baptisia lactea bloom from late spring into early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild indigo - a common name for various species in the genus Baptisia

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Baptisia' from Greek 'baptisis' (dipping), and 'lactea' from Latin 'lacteus' (milky, referring to the flower color).
