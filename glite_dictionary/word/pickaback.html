pickaback

1. ADV

/ ˈpɪkəˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpɪkəbæk / (BrE)
on the back or shoulders, typically referring to carrying someone in this manner
on the back or shoulders (carrying)

Examples

He carried his little brother pickaback all the way home.
The child loves to ride pickaback when she gets tired during walks.
She gave her friend a pickaback ride through the festival crowd.

Synset Examples

she carried her child piggyback

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piggyback - on someone's back or shoulders

Etymology

Alteration of earlier 'pick pack', influenced by 'back' and perhaps 'piggyback'.
