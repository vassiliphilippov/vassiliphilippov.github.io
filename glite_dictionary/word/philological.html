philological

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɪləˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌfɪləˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the study of language in historical, literary, or linguistic contexts; concerning philology.
relating to philology or language study

Examples

The professor gave a philological analysis of the ancient manuscript.
Her research focuses on philological methods to trace the origins of words.
A philological approach can reveal hidden meanings in historical texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linguistic - relating to language or linguistics
  • Synonym: literary - concerning literature or writing

Etymology

From Greek philologikos (fond of learning or literature), from philología (love of learning).
