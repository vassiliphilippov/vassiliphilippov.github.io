enjoyment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒɔɪmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɔɪmənt / (BrE)
(Law) The exercise of the legal right to use and benefit from property.
legal right to use property
legal

Examples

The tenant has the right to enjoyment of the property as stated in the lease.
Any interference with the enjoyment of your land can lead to legal action.
She claimed quiet enjoyment of her apartment despite the ongoing construction next door.

Synset Examples

we were given the use of his boat

Etymology

From Old French *enjoir* (to enjoy), from Latin *gaudere* (to rejoice).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒɔɪmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɔɪmənt / (BrE)
the state or feeling of taking pleasure in something; the pleasure experienced when having a good time
pleasure or satisfaction

Examples

She could barely contain her enjoyment as the concert began.
Reading is one of my greatest sources of enjoyment.
They watched the children playing with obvious enjoyment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleasure - a feeling of happy satisfaction and enjoyment
  • Antonym: misery - a state of great unhappiness or discomfort

Etymology

From Old French 'enjoir' (to enjoy), from Latin 'gaudere' (to rejoice).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒɔɪmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɔɪmənt / (BrE)
the state or process of deriving pleasure or satisfaction from something
state of taking pleasure; satisfaction

Examples

She gained great enjoyment from reading her favorite novels.
The children's laughter was a clear sign of their enjoyment at the party.
People find enjoyment in different hobbies, from painting to hiking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleasure - a feeling of happy satisfaction and enjoyment
  • Antonym: misery - a state of great suffering and unhappiness

Etymology

From Old French 'enjoir' (to enjoy), based on Latin 'gaudere' (to rejoice).
