decriminalisation

1. NOUN

/ diˌkrɪmɪnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌkrɪmɪnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something legal that was previously illegal, especially through changes in the law
making something legal by law

Examples

The decriminalisation of cannabis has been a contentious issue in many countries.
Advocates argue that decriminalisation could reduce harm and free up police resources.
Since the decriminalisation of certain offenses, there has been a decrease in related arrests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legalisation - the process of making something legal

Etymology

From de- (removal) + criminal + -isation (forming nouns denoting the process of making or becoming).
