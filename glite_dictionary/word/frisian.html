Frisian

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriʒ.i.ən / (AmE), / ˈfrɪz.i.ən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Friesland or Frisia, a region in the northern Netherlands and northwestern Germany
native or inhabitant of Friesland or Frisia

Examples

There is a strong sense of identity among the Frisians of the northern Netherlands.
Many Frisians are proud of their unique language and traditions.
The Frisian people have a history dating back over a thousand years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dutch - a native or inhabitant of the Netherlands
  • Synonym: German - a native or inhabitant of Germany

Etymology

From Latin Frisii (the Frisians), referring to the Germanic people of the region.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfriːʒən / (AmE), / ˈfrɪziən / (BrE)
a West Germanic language spoken in Friesland in the Netherlands and parts of Germany, closely related to English.
West Germanic language of Friesland

Examples

Many linguists study Frisian because of its close similarity to English.
The Frisian language is still spoken by thousands of people in the Netherlands and Germany.
She learned to speak Frisian during her stay in Friesland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dutch - the West Germanic language of the Netherlands
  • Synonym: English - the West Germanic language spoken in the UK and elsewhere

Etymology

From Latin Frisius ('Frisian'), from Frisii, a Germanic tribe.
