Choeronycteris

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌɛrəˌnɪkˈtɛrɪs / (AmE), / kəʊˌɛrəˌnɪkˈtɛrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of bats in the family Phyllostomatidae, including the Mexican long-tongued bat.
a genus of bats

Examples

Choeronycteris is best known for its single species, the Mexican long-tongued bat.
Researchers found a colony of Choeronycteris living in the cave system.
The unique feeding behavior of Choeronycteris has drawn interest from mammalogists.

Etymology

From Greek 'choiros' (pig) and 'nycteris' (bat).

2. NOUN

/ kioʊˌrɒnɪkˈtɪərɪs / (AmE), / kɪəˌrɒnɪkˈtɪərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of bats in the family Phyllostomatidae, native to Central and North America, commonly known as the Mexican long-tongued bat.
a genus of leaf-nosed bats

Examples

The Choeronycteris genus includes the Mexican long-tongued bat, known for its unusually long tongue.
Researchers studied Choeronycteris populations to better understand their important role as pollinators.
Only one living species, Choeronycteris mexicana, belongs to this genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'choiros' (pig) and 'nyktēros' (bat), reflecting features of the bat's nose and nocturnal habits.
