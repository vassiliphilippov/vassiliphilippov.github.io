bloodletting

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌlɛt̬ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌlɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or practice of withdrawing blood from a patient for therapeutic purposes, especially as an ancient medical treatment to cure or prevent illness
historical medical procedure of removing blood

Examples

In medieval times, bloodletting was often performed to balance the body's humors.
The physician recommended bloodletting as a remedy, reflecting the beliefs of his era.
Many historic illustrations depict doctors using leeches for bloodletting.

Etymology

From Middle English 'bloodletting', from 'blood' + 'letting' (letting out).

2. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌlɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌlɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
Indiscriminate or large-scale killing or slaughter, especially in a violent or political context.
indiscriminate or large-scale killing

Examples

The bloodletting that followed the coup shocked the entire nation.
Rival factions called for an end to the cycle of bloodletting in the region.
Historians still debate the causes behind the sudden bloodletting during the uprising.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloodbath - an event involving the violent killing of many people
  • Synonym: massacre - the killing of many people, especially those who cannot defend themselves
  • Synonym: bloodshed - the killing or wounding of people, typically in large numbers

Etymology

Originally from the medical practice of 'letting' (releasing) blood for healing, now also used figuratively for mass killing.
