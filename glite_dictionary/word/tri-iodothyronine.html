tri-iodothyronine

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌaɪ.ə.doʊˈθaɪ.rəˌnin / (AmE), / traɪˌaɪ.ə.dəʊˈθaɪ.rəˌniːn / (BrE)
a thyroid hormone containing three iodine atoms, important for regulating metabolism in the body
thyroid hormone with three iodine atoms
medical

Examples

The doctor measured the patient's tri-iodothyronine levels to assess thyroid function.
Tri-iodothyronine is sometimes referred to by its abbreviation, T3, in medical literature.
A deficiency in tri-iodothyronine can lead to symptoms of hypothyroidism.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: thyroxine - a thyroid hormone with four iodine atoms
  • Degree Lower: thyronine - a basic structure for iodinated thyroid hormones

Etymology

From 'tri-' (three) + 'iodo-' (relating to iodine) + 'thyronine' (a hormone of the thyroid gland).
