unfavorable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfeɪvərəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪvərəbl / (BrE)
(of conditions, especially weather or winds) tending to hinder or oppose progress or success.
hindering or opposing conditions

Examples

The hikers delayed their trip because of the unfavorable weather conditions.
Strong unfavorable winds made flying difficult for the small aircraft.
Farmers worried that the season’s unfavorable climate would impact their crops.

Synset Examples

unfavorable winds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: favorable - (of conditions) advantageous; making something more likely to succeed

Etymology

From un- (not) + favorable, from Latin favorabilis (pleasing, indulgent).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfeɪvərəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪvərəbl / (BrE)
causing or showing conditions or opinions that are not likely to lead to success, approval, or advantage; not supportive or beneficial
not supportive or beneficial

Examples

The jury was influenced by the unfavorable publicity surrounding the case.
Their proposal met with an unfavorable response from the committee.
We had to postpone the match due to unfavorable weather conditions.

Synset Examples

a disadvantageous outcome
a well-known study from the 1970's showed that gender stereotyping placed women in a disadvantageous position
made an unfavorable impression

Related Terms

  • Antonym: advantageous - involving or creating favorable circumstances
  • Synonym: hostile - actively opposed or unfriendly
  • Synonym: disadvantageous - causing disadvantage; unfavorable

Etymology

From un- (not) + favorable, which comes from Latin favorabilis (pleasing, favored).

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfeɪvərəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪvərəbl / (BrE)
not giving approval, support, or a positive result; not helpful or suitable; showing or expressing disapproval.
not approving or beneficial

Examples

The team was forced to play under unfavorable conditions during the final match.
Her proposal received unfavorable comments from several members of the committee.
Economic forecasts for the coming year remain unfavorable according to analysts.

Synset Examples

unfavorable conditions
an unfavorable comparison
unfavorable comments
unfavorable impression

Related Terms

  • Antonym: favorable - giving approval or support; beneficial
  • Synonym: negative - not optimistic or approving; expressing refusal or denial

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'favorable', from Latin 'favorabilis'.
