to anthropomorphise

1. VERB

/ ˌænθrəpəˈmɔrfaɪz / (AmE), / ˌænθrəpəˈmɔːfaɪz / (BrE)
to attribute human characteristics or behavior to animals, objects, or other non-human things
attribute human traits to non-humans
british

Examples

Writers often anthropomorphise animals in children's stories to make them more relatable.
The robot was deliberately anthropomorphised to appear friendly and approachable.
It is common to anthropomorphise the weather by saying it is 'angry' or 'kind.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anthropomorphize - assign human traits to non-humans (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'anthrōpos' (human) + 'morphē' (form), via French and Latin influences.
