perishable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛr.ɪʃ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛr.ɪʃ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of an object or substance) likely to decay, deteriorate, or spoil, especially in a short time.
liable to decay or spoil

Examples

Fruit and vegetables are perishable goods that need to be stored carefully.
You should keep all perishable food items in the refrigerator to prevent spoilage.
Flowers are highly perishable and should be delivered quickly to retain their freshness.

Synset Examples

this minute and perishable planet
perishable foods such as butter and fruit

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperishable - not subject to decay, spoilage, or destruction
  • Degree Lower: durable - able to withstand wear, pressure, or damage; lasting

Etymology

From Latin perire (to perish) + -able.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛɹ.ɪ.ʃə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛɹ.ɪ.ʃə.bəl / (BrE)
A food or item, especially one that decays, spoils, or loses quality quickly and must be kept cool or used shortly after purchase.
item that spoils or decays quickly

Examples

The grocery store places all the perishables in refrigerated sections to keep them fresh.
During the summer, it's important to transport perishables in a cooler to prevent spoilage.
Airlines often restrict the shipping of perishables on long international flights.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonperishable - food that does not spoil or decay quickly

Etymology

From Old French *perissable*, from Latin *perire* (to perish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07953584-n
Synset POS: n
