indeterminateness

1. NOUN

/ ɪn.dəˈtɝː.mɪ.nət.nəs / (AmE), / ɪn.dɪˈtɜː.mɪ.nət.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being vague, unclear, or not precisely defined.
quality of being vague or undefined

Examples

The indeterminateness of his answer left everyone confused about his true intentions.
Modern art often embraces indeterminateness to challenge the viewer's perceptions.
Legal language sometimes suffers from indeterminateness, making certain laws difficult to interpret.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indeterminacy - the quality of being uncertain or not precisely fixed
  • Antonym: definiteness - the quality of being clearly defined or distinct

Etymology

From Latin 'indeterminatus' (not determined) + English suffix '-ness'.
