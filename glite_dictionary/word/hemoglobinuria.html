hemoglobinuria

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmoʊˌɡloʊbəˈnjʊriə / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊˌɡləʊbɪˈnjʊəriə / (BrE)
The presence of hemoglobin in the urine, usually due to the breakdown of red blood cells.
hemoglobin in urine (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected hemoglobinuria after noticing the patient's dark-colored urine.
Persistent hemoglobinuria may indicate underlying hemolytic anemia or kidney problems.
Routine tests revealed traces of hemoglobinuria in the athlete following intense physical activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hematuria - the presence of blood in the urine

Etymology

From hemoglobin + -uria; hemoglobin from Greek 'haima' (blood) and Latin 'globus' (globe), -uria from Greek 'ouron' (urine).
