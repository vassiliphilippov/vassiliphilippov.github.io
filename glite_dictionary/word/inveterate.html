inveterate

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɛt̬.ɚ.ət / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛt.ər.ət / (BrE)
Having a particular habit, activity, or interest that is long-established and unlikely to change; habitual.
habitual; long-established

Examples

He is an inveterate gambler who never misses a night at the casino.
She has been an inveterate reader since childhood, seldom seen without a book.
Despite his promise to quit, Mark remained an inveterate smoker.

Synset Examples

a chronic smoker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habitual - done constantly or as a habit
  • Antonym: occasional - occurring, appearing, or done infrequently and irregularly

Etymology

From Latin 'inveteratus' (made old), past participle of 'inveterare' (to grow old).
