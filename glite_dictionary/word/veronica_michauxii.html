Veronica michauxii

1. NOUN

/ vəˈrɑnɪkə mɪˈʃoʊiː / (AmE), / vəˈrɒnɪkə mɪˈʃaʊiː / (BrE)
A species of plant found in wet places in Eurasia and America, belonging to the speedwell genus.
a plant species of wet habitats in Eurasia and America

Examples

Veronica michauxii often grows along streams and in wet meadows across northern regions.
The blue flowers of Veronica michauxii are a common sight in mountain wetlands.
Botanists study Veronica michauxii to understand its adaptation to moist environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speedwell - any plant of the genus Veronica, especially those with small blue or purple flowers

Etymology

Named after French botanist André Michaux (1746–1802).
