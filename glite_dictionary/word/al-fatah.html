al-Fatah

1. PROPN

/ æl fəˈtɑː / (AmE), / æl fəˈtɑː / (BrE)
a Palestinian political and military organization, founded by Yasser Arafat in 1958, that aimed to create a Palestinian state and was active in organizing armed resistance and political activities
Palestinian political and military organization

Examples

al-Fatah was a central faction within the Palestine Liberation Organization during the late 20th century.
Membership in al-Fatah has historically been significant in Palestinian political life.
al-Fatah leaders participated in several peace negotiations with Israel.

Synset Examples

al-Fatah carried out numerous acts of international terrorism in western Europe and the Middle East in the 1970s

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fatah - alternative transliteration of al-Fatah, Palestinian political and military organization

Etymology

From Arabic 'al-Fatḥ' meaning 'the conquest' or 'the opening.'
