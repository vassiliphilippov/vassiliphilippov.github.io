debarment

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈbɑrmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈbɑːmənt / (BrE)
the act or state of being officially prevented from doing something or from entering a place, especially as a legal or formal punishment
official exclusion or prohibition

Examples

After the investigation, the company faced debarment from all government contracts for five years.
His debarment from the club resulted from repeated violations of its rules.
The university's policy includes debarment as a penalty for academic dishonesty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusion - the act of keeping someone out or preventing them from participating
  • Antonym: admittance - the act of allowing someone to enter or join

Etymology

From 'debar' + '-ment', with 'debar' from Old French 'desbarer', from 'des-' (expressing removal) + 'barre' (bar).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈbɑrmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈbɑːmənt / (BrE)
the act of officially preventing someone from doing something, especially by legal means; exclusion, often from a position, privilege, or activity
official exclusion or prohibition
legal

Examples

After his conviction, the court ordered his debarment from practicing law.
The company's unethical actions resulted in debarment from government contracts.
Her debarment from entering the competition came as a shock to her supporters.

Synset Examples

they achieved his debarment from holding public office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusion - the act of preventing someone from entering or participating
  • Antonym: admission - the act of allowing someone to enter or participate

Etymology

From 'debar' + '-ment'; 'debar' from Old French 'desbarer' (to exclude from entry).
