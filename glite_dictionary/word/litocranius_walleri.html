Litocranius walleri

1. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪtoʊˈkreɪniəs ˈwɒləri / (AmE), / ˌlɪtəʊˈkrɑːnɪəs ˈwɒləri / (BrE)
A slender antelope of East Africa, known for its long neck and backward-curving horns, commonly called the gerenuk.
East African antelope (gerenuk)

Examples

During the safari, we were lucky to spot a Litocranius walleri feeding on leaves high in the trees.
The Litocranius walleri is well adapted to browsing for food at heights other antelopes cannot reach.
Researchers observed a group of Litocranius walleri displaying their characteristic upright posture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gerenuk - Common English name for Litocranius walleri, an antelope of East Africa

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name from Greek *litos* (plain, slender) + *kranion* (skull); species named after Gerald Waller, English naturalist.
