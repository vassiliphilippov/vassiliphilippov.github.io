Nymphaea lotus

1. NOUN

/ nɪmˈfiːə ˈloʊtəs / (AmE), / nɪmˈfiːə ˈləʊtəs / (BrE)
A species of water lily native to Egypt and southeastern Africa, known as the white Egyptian lotus and considered sacred in ancient Egypt.
white Egyptian lotus plant

Examples

The ancient Egyptians often depicted the Nymphaea lotus flower in their art and religious ceremonies.
In the palace gardens, a pond filled with Nymphaea lotus added a serene beauty to the landscape.
Botanists study the Nymphaea lotus for its unique ecological role in freshwater habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lily - aquatic flowering plant of the family Nymphaeaceae

Etymology

From Latin genus name Nymphaea (water lily) and species name lotus, referencing its resemblance to the lotus.
