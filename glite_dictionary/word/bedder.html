bedder

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛdər / (AmE), / ˈbɛdə / (BrE)
an ornamental plant grown especially for planting in a flowerbed, often to create decorative patterns or color displays
ornamental plant for flowerbeds

Examples

The gardener selected several bedders to brighten up the front yard.
Marigolds make excellent bedders for adding vibrant color to summer borders.
Every spring, the park is replanted with new bedders to create intricate floral patterns.

Etymology

From 'bed' (as in flowerbed) + -er (agent noun suffix).
