zonule

1. NOUN

/ ˈzoʊ.njul / (AmE), / ˈzəʊ.njuːl / (BrE)
a small beltlike zone, especially referring to anatomical structures such as bands or belts in tissues
small beltlike anatomical zone
medical

Examples

The zonule of Zinn supports the lens of the eye in humans.
Certain parasites attach to the intestinal wall using a zonule.
The scientist described a zonule located between two muscle groups during the dissection.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: zone - an area or stretch of land having a particular characteristic

Etymology

From Latin 'zonula', diminutive of 'zona' (belt, zone).
