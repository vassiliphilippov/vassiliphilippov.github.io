Trondheimsfjord

1. PROPN

/ ˈtrɒn.heɪmzˌfjɔrd / (AmE), / ˈtrɒn.dəmsˌfjɔːd / (BrE)
a long, narrow inlet of the Norwegian Sea in central Norway, notable for its length and scenic landscapes
fjord in central Norway

Examples

The Trondheimsfjord is renowned for its beautiful coastal scenery and rich marine life.
A ferry ride across the Trondheimsfjord offers stunning views of surrounding mountains and woodlands.
Many tourists visit the Trondheimsfjord region to experience Norway's picturesque landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fjord - a long, deep, narrow body of water that reaches far inland, typically formed by glacial activity

Etymology

From Norwegian 'Trondheimsfjord', meaning 'fjord of Trondheim'—Trondheim being the nearby city.
