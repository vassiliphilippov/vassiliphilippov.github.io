trembler

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛmblər / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmblə / (BrE)
a person who shakes or quivers, typically from fear or nervousness
person who shakes from fear or nerves

Examples

The child was such a trembler during thunderstorms that she would hide under the bed.
He was a notorious trembler in the face of danger, unable to control his nerves.
As the horror movie reached its climax, the audience was filled with gasps and more than one trembler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, typically as a result of emotion or cold

Etymology

From 'tremble' (to shake involuntarily) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
