pennate

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛneɪt / (BrE)
having wings that are feathered, like those of a bird
having feathered wings

Examples

The pennate structure of the bird allows for efficient flight.
Some fossilized creatures display distinctly pennate wings.
Scientists studied the pennate appendages of ancient insects to understand their evolution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: apterous - having no wings

Etymology

From Latin 'pennatus' (feathered), from 'penna' (feather).
