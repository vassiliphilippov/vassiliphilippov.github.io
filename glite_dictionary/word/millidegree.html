millidegree

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪlɪdɪˈɡri / (AmE), / ˌmɪlɪdɪˈɡriː / (BrE)
A unit of temperature equal to one-thousandth of a degree.
one-thousandth of a degree (temperature unit)

Examples

Modern scientific instruments can measure temperature changes as small as one millidegree.
The experiment required adjustments within a single millidegree to ensure accuracy.
Precision up to several millidegrees is necessary for this type of calibration.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: degree - a unit of measurement for temperature or angles

Etymology

From milli- (thousandth) and degree, following the SI prefix system.
