commemorative

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmɛmɚˌeɪtɪv / (AmE), / kəˈmɛm(ə)rətɪv / (BrE)
intended to honor or remember a person, event, or occasion, especially by being created or done as a tribute.
honoring or celebrating someone or something

Examples

A commemorative stamp was issued to celebrate the anniversary of the event.
The museum unveiled a commemorative plaque in honor of the artist.
He collected several commemorative coins from the Olympic Games.

Synset Examples

a commemorative plaque

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memorial - something, especially a structure, established to remind people of a person or event

Etymology

From Latin *commemorativus*, from *commemorare* (to remember).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmɛmɚətɪv / (AmE), / kəˈmɛmərətɪv / (BrE)
An object, especially a coin or postage stamp, issued to mark an event or honor a person.
object made to mark an event or honor a person

Examples

The royal wedding was celebrated with a special commemorative issued by the postal service.
Collectors eagerly sought the limited-edition commemoratives released for the anniversary.
He displayed his prized commemorative on a shelf in his study.

Etymology

From Latin 'commemorare' (to remember together) via 'commemorativus' (commemorative).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09274595-n
Synset POS: n
