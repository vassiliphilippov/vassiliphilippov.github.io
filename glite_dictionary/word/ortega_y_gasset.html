Ortega y Gasset

1. PROPN

/ ɔɹˈteɪgə i ɡəˈset / (AmE), / ɔːˈteɪɡə i ɡæˈset / (BrE)
(1883–1955) Spanish philosopher known for advocating leadership by an intellectual elite and for his works on society and culture.
Spanish philosopher (1883–1955)

Examples

Ortega y Gasset argued that mass society threatened individuality and true culture.
The philosophy of Ortega y Gasset continues to be studied in universities worldwide.
Many of Ortega y Gasset's ideas about elites remain influential in social theory.
