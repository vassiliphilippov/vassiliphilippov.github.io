Nemophila menziesii

1. NOUN

/ ˌniː.moʊˈfaɪ.lə mɛnˈziːzi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌniː.məˈfɪl.ə menˈziːzi.aɪ / (BrE)
a delicate annual plant native to California, with blue flowers marked with dark spots, commonly known as baby blue eyes
California annual with blue-spotted flowers

Examples

The garden's border was brightened by the gentle blooms of Nemophila menziesii.
Many hikers delight in the fields of Nemophila menziesii that bloom each spring in California.
Botanists often refer to Nemophila menziesii as 'baby blue eyes' due to its striking flower color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baby blue eyes - common name for Nemophila menziesii, a California wildflower with blue flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'nemos' (wood, grove) + 'philos' (loving) and named after Archibald Menzies, Scottish botanist.
