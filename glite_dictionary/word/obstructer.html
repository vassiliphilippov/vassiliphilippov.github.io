obstructer

1. NOUN

/ əbˈstrʌktɚ / (AmE), / əbˈstrʌktə / (BrE)
a person who deliberately prevents or hinders progress or action by others
person who hinders action or progress

Examples

The obstructer at the meeting kept raising unnecessary objections to delay the vote.
She was accused of being an obstructer to progress in the organization.
Despite having an obstructer on the team, the project moved forward successfully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstructor - someone who obstructs; alternate spelling
  • Antonym: facilitator - someone who makes a process easier or helps progress

Etymology

From the verb 'obstruct' + '-er' suffix, indicating agent noun.

2. NOUN

/ əbˈstrʌktər / (AmE), / əbˈstrʌktə / (BrE)
a person or thing that obstructs; any structure or object that makes progress difficult
something that blocks or hinders progress

Examples

The fallen tree acted as an obstructer on the hiking trail.
During the renovation, the workers removed every obstructer from the hallway.
A malfunctioning vehicle became an obstructer in the busy intersection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstructor - a person or thing that obstructs
  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks or impedes progress

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'obstruct' + agent suffix '-er'.
