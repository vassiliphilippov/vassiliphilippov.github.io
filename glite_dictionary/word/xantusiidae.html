Xantusiidae

1. NOUN

/ zænˌtuːsiˈɪdiː / (AmE), / zanˌtuːsiˈɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small, secretive lizards known as night lizards, native to the southwestern United States, Mexico, and Central America
family of night lizards

Examples

The Xantusiidae are known for their secretive habits and preference for hiding under rocks or logs.
Researchers found several new species within the Xantusiidae family during the expedition.
Unlike many other lizards, members of the Xantusiidae family give birth to live young.

Etymology

From New Latin, after the type genus Xantusia.
