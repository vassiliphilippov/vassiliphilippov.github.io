Trinidad

1. PROPN

/ ˈtrɪnɪˌdæd / (AmE), / ˈtrɪnɪdæd / (BrE)
an island in the southern Caribbean, just off the northeastern coast of Venezuela, forming part of the nation of Trinidad and Tobago.
Caribbean island off Venezuela

Examples

Many tourists visit Trinidad each year for its beaches and vibrant culture.
The capital city of Trinidad is Port of Spain.
Trinidad and Tobago gained independence from Britain in 1962, with Trinidad being the larger of the two islands.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Trinidad', meaning 'Trinity'.
