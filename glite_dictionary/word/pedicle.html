pedicle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛdɪkəl / (BrE)
a small stalk that bears a single flower in a cluster; the ultimate branch of a flower stalk (peduncle) in plants
stalk bearing a single flower in a cluster

Examples

The pedicle supports each individual blossom in the inflorescence.
If the pedicle is cut, the flower will no longer receive nutrients from the main stem.
In many plants, the length of the pedicle determines how far the flower projects from the cluster.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: peduncle - the main stalk supporting a cluster of flowers or fruit

Etymology

From Latin pediculus (little foot or stalk), diminutive of pes (foot).
