smith

1. NOUN

/ smɪθ / (AmE), / smɪθ / (BrE)
a person who works metal, especially by hammering it when it is hot and malleable
person who works metal by hammering

Examples

The smith skillfully shaped the horseshoe over the glowing coals.
In many old villages, the local smith was a central figure in the community.
He apprenticed with a master smith to learn the art of metalworking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blacksmith - a person who makes and repairs things in iron by hand
  • Synonym: silversmith - a person who makes items from silver
  • Synonym: goldsmith - a person who makes items from gold

Etymology

Old English *smið*, related to Old Norse *smiðr* and German *Schmied*, meaning 'craftsman' or 'metalworker'.
