tribute

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪb.juːt / (AmE), / ˈtrɪb.juːt / (BrE)
a payment made forcibly, especially to gangsters or a criminal organization, in exchange for protection or to avoid harm
payment extorted as protection money

Examples

The shopkeepers had to pay a weekly tribute to the local gang or risk being attacked.
Refusing to pay tribute often resulted in threats or violence from the racketeers.
Throughout the decade, many restaurants were forced to offer a secret tribute to avoid trouble.

Synset Examples

every store in the neighborhood had to pay him protection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protection money - money paid for protection by criminals

Etymology

From Latin 'tributum' (thing contributed or paid), originally payment to a ruler or state.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪb.juːt / (AmE), / ˈtrɪb.juːt / (BrE)
something given, said, or done to show respect or admiration for someone or something
gift or act to show respect or admiration

Examples

The concert was organized as a tribute to the legendary musician.
He wrote a heartfelt tribute in memory of his late mentor.
Their victory is a tribute to hard work and teamwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homage - special honor or respect shown publicly
  • Antonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval based on perceived faults

Etymology

From Latin 'tributum' (a tax, offering), from 'tribuere' (to grant, allot).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06709958-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪbˌjut / (AmE), / ˈtrɪb.juːt / (BrE)
A payment made by one nation or ruler to another, especially as a sign of dependence or for protection, often under threat of force.
payment by one nation for protection

Examples

The smaller kingdom agreed to pay an annual tribute to its more powerful neighbor.
Failure to deliver the required tribute often resulted in military action.
Throughout history, invading empires demanded tribute from conquered territories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: levy - a sum of money collected as a tax or payment
  • Synonym: tax - a compulsory contribution to state revenue

Etymology

From Latin tribūtum (a tribute), from tribuere (to assign, allot).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01124708-n
Synset POS: n
