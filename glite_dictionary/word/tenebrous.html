tenebrous

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛn.ə.brəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.ɪ.brəs / (BrE)
Dark, shadowy, or obscure; full of darkness or gloom.
dark and gloomy

Examples

The tenebrous sky signaled the approach of a violent storm.
She described the haunted house as a tenebrous place with shadows lurking in every corner.
A tenebrous forest surrounded the old castle, making it even more mysterious.

Synset Examples

a tenebrous cave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, causing a feeling of depression
  • Synonym: murky - dark and difficult to see through
  • Antonym: bright - full of light or shining strongly

Etymology

From Latin 'tenebrosus' (dark, gloomy), from 'tenebrae' (darkness).
