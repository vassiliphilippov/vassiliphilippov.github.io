to conceptualise

1. VERB

/ kənˈsɛptʃuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kənˈsɛptjʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
to form an idea or concept of something in the mind; to visualize or invent mentally
form an idea or concept
british

Examples

It can be difficult to conceptualise the scale of the universe.
The artist managed to conceptualise complex emotions in her paintings.
Researchers must conceptualise new theories before testing them.

Synset Examples

He conceived of a robot that would help paralyzed patients
This library was well conceived

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conceive - form a mental idea or plan; imagine
  • Synonym: to imagine - form a picture or idea in the mind
  • Antonym: to realise - become fully aware of something as a fact

Etymology

From concept +‎ -ualise, after French conceptualiser.
