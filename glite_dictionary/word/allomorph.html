allomorph

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.əˌmɔrf / (AmE), / ˈal.əˌmɔːf / (BrE)
any of several different crystalline forms in which the same chemical compound can exist
different crystalline form of a compound

Examples

Calcium carbonate can exist as the allomorph calcite or as aragonite.
The mineral displayed a rare allomorph not often seen in nature.
Scientists studied the structure of each allomorph to understand its properties.

Synset Examples

calcium carbonate occurs in the allomorphs calcite and aragonite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polymorph - a substance with multiple crystalline forms

Etymology

From Greek allo- (other) + morphē (form).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæləˌmɔrf / (AmE), / ˈæləmɔːf / (BrE)
any of the variant phonological forms of a morpheme occurring in specific contexts within a language
variant form of a morpheme

Examples

In the words 'cats' and 'dogs', the final sounds are different allomorphs of the plural morpheme.
The prefix 'in-' in 'impossible' and 'irregular' demonstrates how allomorphs adapt to their phonetic environment.
Linguists study how allomorphs of a morpheme appear in various grammatical contexts.

Synset Examples

the final sounds of ‘bets’ and ‘beds’ and ‘horses’ and ‘oxen’ are allomorphs of the English plural morpheme

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morpheme - the smallest grammatical unit in a language

Etymology

From Greek 'allo-' (other) + 'morph' (form).

Synset POS: n
