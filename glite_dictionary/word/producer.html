producer

1. NOUN

/ prəˈdusər / (AmE), / prəˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
an organism, person, or thing that produces or creates something; especially one that generates or manufactures goods, substances, or outcomes
something that produces or creates

Examples

Maine is a leading producer of potatoes in the United States.
The new factory is a significant producer of solar panels for export.
This species of algae is an important producer in the aquatic ecosystem.

Synset Examples

Maine is a leading producer of potatoes
this microorganism is a producer of disease

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consumer - an organism or person that consumes goods or services

Etymology

From Latin 'producere' (to bring forth, produce).

2. NOUN

/ prəˈdusər / (AmE), / prəˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
a person who finds financing for and supervises the making and presentation of a show, film, or program
person who oversees making of a show or film

Examples

After years of struggle, she finally convinced a producer to back her film project.
The Broadway musical had a new producer who brought fresh ideas to the production.
Every TV show needs a skilled producer to manage the budget and oversee the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: director - person who supervises the creative aspects of a play, film, or TV program

Etymology

From Latin producere (to bring forth), via French produire.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10499603-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ prəˈdusər / (AmE), / prəˈdjuːsər / (BrE)
a person, company, or country that makes goods for sale, especially in large quantities
someone or something that manufactures goods

Examples

As a leading producer of textiles, the country exports goods worldwide.
The region became a major producer of wine after the new vineyards opened.
Farmers are the primary producers of food in our economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manufacturer - a person or company that makes goods for sale on a large scale

Etymology

From Latin producere (to bring forth), via French produire.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10311977-n
Synset POS: n
