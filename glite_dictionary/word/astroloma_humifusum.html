Astroloma humifusum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəˈloʊmə ˌhjuːməˈfjuːsəm / (AmE), / ˌæstrəˈlɒmə ˌhjuːmɪˈfjuːsəm / (BrE)
a small prostrate or ascending shrub native to Australia, with scarlet flowers and succulent fruit resembling cranberries; also known as native cranberry.
Australian shrub with red flowers and fruit

Examples

The Astroloma humifusum blooms in early spring, displaying its striking scarlet flowers across the heathland.
Local children enjoy picking the sweet fruit of Astroloma humifusum, often called native cranberry.
Gardeners value Astroloma humifusum for its low, spreading habit and ability to thrive in sandy soils.

Etymology

From Greek 'astron' (star) + 'loma' (border or fringe) for the genus; species name from Latin 'humifusus' (spreading over the ground).
