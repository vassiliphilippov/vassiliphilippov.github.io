neophyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈniː.əˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈniː.əʊ.faɪt / (BrE)
a plant species that is newly established in an area where it was not previously found
a newly established plant species

Examples

Many invasive weeds are classified as neophytes in Europe.
The park's ecologists monitor neophyte species to track ecological changes.
Some neophytes spread rapidly and disrupt native habitats.

Etymology

From Greek neophytos (newly planted), from 'neos' (new) + 'phyton' (plant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈniːəˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈniːəfaɪt / (BrE)
a person who is new to a subject, skill, or belief, especially a recent convert to a religion or doctrine
a person new to a subject or belief

Examples

The church welcomed the neophyte with open arms after his conversion.
As a neophyte, she was eager to learn the rituals of the faith.
The class was composed mostly of neophytes just beginning their spiritual journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to or inexperienced in a field
  • Antonym: veteran - a person with long experience in a field

Etymology

From Greek 'neophytos' (newly planted), from 'neos' (new) + 'phytos' (planted).

3. NOUN

/ ˈniːəˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈniː.ə.faɪt / (BrE)
a person who is new to a subject, skill, or activity; a beginner
beginner; person new to something

Examples

As a neophyte in the world of coding, she asked many questions during her first week.
The club welcomes both neophyte and experienced gardeners alike.
Though once a neophyte entrepreneur, he quickly mastered the basics of running a business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to or inexperienced in a field or situation
  • Antonym: veteran - a person with long experience in a particular field

Etymology

From Greek neophytos (newly planted), via Late Latin neophytus.
