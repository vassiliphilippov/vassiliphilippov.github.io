agenesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌædʒəˈniːʒə / (AmE), / ˌaɡɪˈniːziə / (BrE)
The absence or failure of development of an organ or body part; imperfect or nondevelopment of a part.
failure of development of a body part
medical

Examples

Congenital agenesia of the kidney is a rare but well-documented birth defect.
The patient showed signs of agenesia affecting several facial bones.
Genetic studies have identified factors that contribute to the risk of limb agenesia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agenesis - the absence of an organ due to its failure to develop

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) and 'genesis' (origin, creation).
