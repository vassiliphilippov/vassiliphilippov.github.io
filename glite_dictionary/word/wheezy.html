wheezy

1. ADJ

/ ˈwiːzi / (AmE), / ˈwiːzi / (BrE)
Making or characterized by a whistling or rattling sound in the chest when breathing, typically as a result of illness or obstruction in the air passages.
breathing with a whistling sound

Examples

After running up the stairs, his breathing became wheezy and labored.
Her wheezy cough worried the doctor, who suggested further tests.
The old accordion played a wheezy tune as the musician pressed its keys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asthmatic - related to or suffering from asthma (often involving wheezy breathing)
  • Antonym: healthy - free from illness (contrasting with wheezy)

Etymology

From 'wheeze' (to breathe with a whistling sound) + -y (adjective suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈwiːzi / (AmE), / ˈwiːzi / (BrE)
having a tone or sound characterized by a high-pitched, breathy, or coarse quality, like a wheeze; reminiscent of the sound of a reed instrument.
having a breathy or coarse tone, like a reed instrument

Examples

The old accordion produced a wheezy melody that filled the room with charm.
After years of use, the organ's wheezy notes had become its most distinctive feature.
His wheezy voice gave the song a haunting, nostalgic quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspy - having a harsh or grating sound

Etymology

From 'wheeze' + '-y', denoting something having qualities of a wheeze.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01928038-s
Synset POS: s
