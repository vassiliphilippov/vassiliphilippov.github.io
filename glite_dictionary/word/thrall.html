thrall

1. NOUN

/ θrɔl / (AmE), / θrɑːl / (BrE)
a person who is held in bondage or slavery; a captive or serf
person held in bondage or slavery

Examples

He spent years as a thrall in a distant land, longing for freedom.
The conquerors reduced the villagers to thralls and forced them to labor.
Legends spoke of heroes breaking the chains of thrall and reclaiming their freedom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and forced to obey them
  • Antonym: master - a person who has control or ownership over someone in bondage

Etymology

From Old Norse 'þræll' (slave, serf).

2. NOUN

/ θrɔl / (AmE), / θrɑːl / (BrE)
the state of being completely controlled or dominated by another person or by a powerful influence
state of complete control or domination

Examples

Many people have fallen into the thrall of social media, unable to disconnect.
She escaped the thrall of her controlling partner after many years.
The novel explores a world in the thrall of a tyrant king.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bondage - the state of being a slave or under control of someone else
  • Antonym: freedom - the state of being free, not under the control of another

Etymology

From Old Norse 'þræl' meaning 'slave'.
