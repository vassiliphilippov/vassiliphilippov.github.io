Chauna torquata

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊnə tɔrˈkwɑːtə / (AmE), / ˈkaʊnə tɔːˈkwɑːtə / (BrE)
A large bird species, the largest crested screamer, native to southern Brazil and Argentina.
large South American screamer bird

Examples

The wetlands of Argentina are home to the magnificent Chauna torquata.
Conservationists study the Chauna torquata to monitor the health of marsh ecosystems.
The Chauna torquata is known for its loud call and striking black neck ring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screamer - large, loud-voiced South American bird in the family Anhimidae
  • Synonym: southern screamer - common English name for Chauna torquata

Etymology

From Latin: 'Chauna' (the genus name), and 'torquata' meaning 'collared'.
