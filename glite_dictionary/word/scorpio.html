Scorpio

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɔːrpioʊ / (AmE), / ˈskɔːpiəʊ / (BrE)
A person born between October 23 and November 21, when the sun is in the astrological sign Scorpio.
a person born under the sign Scorpio

Examples

He's a true Scorpio, passionate and determined.
Many Scorpios are known for their intense loyalty.
As a Scorpio, she values honesty in relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scorpion - the eighth astrological sign of the zodiac
  • Degree Higher: Sagittarius - a person born under the sign Sagittarius (next sign in the zodiac)
  • Degree Lower: Libra - a person born under the sign Libra (previous sign in the zodiac)

Etymology

From Latin 'Scorpio' meaning 'scorpion'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈskɔr.pi.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈskɔː.pi.əʊ / (BrE)
A large zodiac constellation located between Libra and Sagittarius, also representing the eighth sign of the zodiac.
zodiac constellation and sign

Examples

The constellation Scorpio is prominent in the southern sky during summer.
Many people born under Scorpio believe they share certain personality traits.
Scorpio lies between Libra and Sagittarius in the zodiac.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scorpius - the astronomical constellation corresponding to the zodiac sign Scorpio
  • Degree Lower: Libra - zodiac constellation preceding Scorpio
  • Degree Higher: Sagittarius - zodiac constellation following Scorpio

Etymology

From Latin 'Scorpio' meaning 'scorpion', referencing the shape of the constellation.

3. NOUN

/ ˈskɔr.pi.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈskɔː.pi.əʊ / (BrE)
The eighth sign of the zodiac, covering approximately October 23 to November 21, traditionally symbolized by a scorpion.
eighth sign of the zodiac

Examples

My birthday is in late October, so my zodiac sign is Scorpio.
People born under Scorpio are said to be passionate and determined.
She read her daily horoscope for Scorpio in the newspaper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zodiac - an imaginary belt in the sky through which the sun, moon, and planets move, divided into twelve signs

Etymology

Latin Scorpiō, meaning 'scorpion', referring to the constellation's shape.
