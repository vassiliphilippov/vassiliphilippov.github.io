extensor

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɛnsər / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnsə / (BrE)
a muscle that causes a limb or other part of the body to straighten or extend when it contracts
muscle for straightening a limb
medical

Examples

The extensor muscles in your arm help you straighten your elbow after bending it.
Injuries to the extensor tendons can limit a person's ability to extend their fingers.
Physical therapy often targets the extensor group to improve joint movement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flexor - a muscle that bends a limb or body part

Etymology

From Latin 'extendere' (to stretch out), with the agent suffix '-or'.
