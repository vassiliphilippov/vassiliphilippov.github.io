Mucorales

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːkɔːˈreɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌmjuːkɔːˈrɑːliːz / (BrE)
an order of mostly saprophytic fungi, including species that grow on decaying organic matter and some pathogenic forms
order of mostly saprophytic fungi
medical

Examples

Several species of Mucorales are commonly found on decaying fruits and vegetables.
The rapid growth of Mucorales in culture helped confirm the diagnosis of fungal infection.
Researchers study Mucorales to better understand their impact on immunocompromised patients.

Etymology

From New Latin, from genus name Mucor + -ales (denoting an order in taxonomy).
