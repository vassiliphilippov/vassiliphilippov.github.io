Hytrin

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪtrɪn / (AmE), / ˈhaɪtrɪn / (BrE)
A trade name for an antihypertensive drug used to treat high blood pressure.
brand of blood pressure medication
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed Hytrin to help control my high blood pressure.
Patients taking Hytrin should be monitored for side effects such as dizziness.
The pharmacy switched my medication from Hytrin to its generic equivalent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terazosin - the generic name for the drug sold as Hytrin
