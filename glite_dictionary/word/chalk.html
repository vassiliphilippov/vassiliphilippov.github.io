chalk

1. NOUN

/ tʃɔk / (AmE), / tʃɔːk / (BrE)
a soft, white, or colored substance in the form of a stick used for writing or drawing on blackboards or other flat surfaces
stick for writing or drawing on blackboards

Examples

The teacher wrote the lesson on the board using a piece of chalk.
She bought colored chalk for her sidewalk art project.
Please clean the chalk marks off the blackboard after class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crayon - a stick of colored wax used for drawing

Etymology

From Old English cealc, from Latin calx (limestone).

5. NOUN

/ tʃɑk / (AmE), / tʃɔːk / (BrE)
a soft, white, porous sedimentary rock, a form of limestone composed mainly of calcite, used for writing or drawing and in various industrial processes
soft, white, porous limestone

Examples

The cliffs were made of white chalk that gleamed in the sunlight.
She drew diagrams on the blackboard using a piece of chalk.
Farmers noticed that the soil contained a high proportion of chalk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limestone - a sedimentary rock consisting mainly of calcium carbonate

Etymology

From Old English 'cealc', from Latin 'calx' (limestone), from Greek 'khalix' (small stone, pebble).
