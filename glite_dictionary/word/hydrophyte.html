hydrophyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrəˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪdrə(ʊ)fʌɪt / (BrE)
a plant that grows wholly or partly in water or in very wet conditions, either rooted in the mud or floating
aquatic or water-adapted plant
medical

Examples

The lotus is a well-known example of a hydrophyte that thrives in shallow lakes.
Many hydrophytes have adapted leaves that repel excess water and aid in floating.
A hydrophyte typically requires saturated soil or standing water to survive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: xerophyte - a plant adapted to dry environments
  • Antonym: mesophyte - a plant adapted to moderate water conditions

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro-' (water) + 'phyton' (plant).
