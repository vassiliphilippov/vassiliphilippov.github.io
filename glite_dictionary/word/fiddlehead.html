fiddlehead

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪd.əlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈfɪd.əlˌhɛd / (BrE)
The young, coiled frond of a fern, especially of certain species eaten as a vegetable.
young, coiled fern frond (often edible)

Examples

In spring, hikers often search for fiddleheads to cook in salads and soups.
The curled shape of a fiddlehead resembles the ornament on a violin.
Restaurants feature fiddlehead ferns as a seasonal delicacy in early May.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a nonflowering vascular plant with feathery or leafy fronds

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the scroll at the top of a fiddle (violin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪdəlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈfɪd(ə)lhɛd / (BrE)
a young, coiled frond of certain ferns, especially of northern temperate regions, often resembling the head of a fiddle and sometimes eaten as a vegetable
young, coiled fern frond

Examples

The chef sautéed fiddlehead ferns with garlic and olive oil for a seasonal side dish.
In spring, foragers search forest floors for fresh fiddleheads to harvest and eat.
The curled fiddlehead is prized both for its flavor and its unique appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a non-flowering vascular plant with feathery fronds

Etymology

From the resemblance to the scroll at the end of a violin (fiddle) neck.
