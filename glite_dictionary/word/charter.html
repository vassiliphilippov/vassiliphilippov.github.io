charter

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃɑːrtər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtə / (BrE)
to hire or lease a vehicle, vessel, or service for a specified period of time
hire or lease a vehicle or service

Examples

We decided to charter a bus for the weekend trip to the mountains.
The company charters private jets for business executives.
They chartered a yacht to explore the Caribbean islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rent - to pay for the temporary use of something
  • Synonym: lease - to grant or take the temporary possession of property or services for payment

Etymology

From Old French 'chartre' (charter), from Latin 'chartula', diminutive of 'charta' (paper, document).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02466042-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈtʃɑːrtər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtə / (BrE)
to formally grant a charter or official permission to organize or operate, especially to a company, city, or institution.
to grant an official charter

Examples

The king decided to charter the new university, giving it the right to award degrees.
In 1837, the state legislature chartered the city as an official municipality.
The organization is hoping the government will charter it to operate as a nonprofit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: license - to grant official permission to someone or something

Etymology

From Old French *charte*, from Latin *charta* (paper, document).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02452557-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈtʃɑːrtər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtə / (BrE)
to hire or lease a vehicle, vessel, or aircraft for private use under a contract for a specified period
hire under contract, esp. vehicle or vessel

Examples

They chartered a boat for a week to explore the islands in comfort.

4. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːrtər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtə / (BrE)
an official document that formally creates an organization, such as a company, and defines its rights and purposes
official document creating an organization

Examples

The university received its charter from the state in 1852.
A company must present its charter when registering legally.
The new city operated under a special charter granted by parliament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constitution - a set of fundamental principles or established precedents according to which an organization is governed
  • Synonym: deed - a legal document regarding the ownership or rights of property

Etymology

From Old French *charte*, from Latin *charta* (paper, document).

5. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑr.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑː.tə / (BrE)
a contract for the exclusive hire or lease of a vehicle, such as a boat, aircraft, or bus, usually for a specified period
contract for exclusive hire of a vehicle

Examples

We signed a charter to hire the yacht for a weeklong cruise.
The company arranged a charter for a private jet to fly the executives to the meeting.
They organized a charter with the bus company for the school field trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lease - a contract granting use of property during a specified time for a specified payment
  • Synonym: rent - the act of paying for the use of something (as an apartment or car)

Etymology

From Old French 'chartre' (charter, document), from Latin 'charta' (paper, document).
