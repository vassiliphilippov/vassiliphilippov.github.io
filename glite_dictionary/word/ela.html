ELA

1. PROPN

/ ˈiːˌɛlˌeɪ / (AmE), / ˈiːˌɛlˌeɪ / (BrE)
a far-left revolutionary group that operated in Greece from 1971 to 1995, opposing the military junta and advocating anti-capitalist and anti-imperialist positions
Greek far-left revolutionary group (1971–1995)

Examples

The Greek authorities launched a major crackdown on ELA after a series of attacks in the 1980s.
Historians classify ELA as one of the most active groups during Greece's years of political unrest.
Members of ELA justified their actions as resistance against capitalist and imperialist influences.

Etymology

Acronym for Greek Επαναστατικός Λαϊκός Αγώνας (Epanastatikós Laïkós Agónas, 'Revolutionary People's Struggle').
