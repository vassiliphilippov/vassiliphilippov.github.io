diffusor

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfjuːsər / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːsə / (BrE)
A device or baffle used to distribute sound waves evenly within a space to reduce echoes and improve acoustic quality.
device that diffuses sound waves

Examples

The new recording studio features a diffusor panel to improve the room's acoustics.
Installing a diffusor on the wall helped to reduce unwanted echoes during performances.
Architects used a wooden diffusor to create a balanced sound environment in the auditorium.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sound absorber - material or device that absorbs rather than diffuses sound waves

Etymology

From Latin 'diffundere' meaning 'to pour out' or 'spread out'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈfjuzər / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːzə / (BrE)
an optical device that spreads light from a lamp evenly across an area
device that spreads light evenly

Examples

The photographer attached a diffusor to soften the harsh light from the flash.
A good diffusor helps eliminate unwanted shadows in studio lighting setups.
We installed a diffusor over the ceiling light to create a more pleasant atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diffuser - alternative spelling of 'diffusor', also distributes light evenly

Etymology

From Latin 'diffundere' (to spread out) via diffusion.
