theurgy

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiːɝːdʒi / (AmE), / ˈθjʊə.dʒi / (BrE)
the practice of rituals believed to invoke the action or presence of divine or supernatural powers, especially to achieve spiritual enlightenment or transformation
rituals for invoking divine intervention

Examples

The ancient priest claimed that theurgy allowed humans to communicate directly with the gods.
Some mystical traditions place a strong emphasis on theurgy as a means to achieve spiritual transformation.
College courses on magic and mysticism sometimes explore the theories behind theurgy.

Etymology

From late Latin 'theurgia', from Greek 'theourgia' (miracle-working), from 'theos' (god) + 'ergon' (work).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθiːɝrdʒi / (AmE), / ˈθjʊəˌdʒi / (BrE)
the practice of rituals or magic intended to invoke the action or presence of benevolent spiritual beings, especially as practiced by Neoplatonists
ritual magic invoking benevolent spirits

Examples

The ancient philosophers believed that theurgy could unite the soul with the divine.
Some modern esoteric groups have revived the practice of theurgy in their rituals.
Neoplatonic texts describe how theurgy differs from sorcery or darker forms of magic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magic - the use of mysterious or supernatural powers
  • Antonym: necromancy - the supposed practice of communicating with the dead to predict the future

Etymology

From Greek theourgia, from theos (god) + -urgia (work).
