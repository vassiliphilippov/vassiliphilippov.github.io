Asphodelus

1. NOUN

/ æsˈfoʊdələs / (AmE), / æsˈfɒdələs / (BrE)
a small genus of tall annual or perennial plants with grasslike leaves and dense clusters of flowers, native to the Mediterranean region and the Himalayas
genus of Mediterranean and Himalayan flowering plants

Examples

The genus Asphodelus includes several striking species commonly found in Mediterranean gardens.
Botanists have classified Asphodelus as part of the asphodel family, Asphodelaceae.
Researchers collected samples of Asphodelus from different regions for genetic analysis.

Etymology

From Latin 'asphodelus', from Greek 'asphodelos', meaning a kind of lily.

2. NOUN

/ æsˈfoʊdələs / (AmE), / æsˈfɒdɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of tall, striking annual or perennial plants with grass-like leaves and flowers in dense clusters, native to the Mediterranean and Himalayas.
genus of Mediterranean and Himalayan plants

Examples

Asphodelus species are often cultivated in gardens for their ornamental flowers.
The scientist identified the plant as a member of the genus Asphodelus.
Many wildflowers of the Asphodelus genus thrive in rocky Mediterranean soils.

Etymology

From Greek asphodelos (asphodel), the classical name for these plants.
