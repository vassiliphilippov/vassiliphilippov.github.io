rudd

1. NOUN

/ rʌd / (AmE), / rʌd / (BrE)
A European freshwater fish (Scardinius erythrophthalmus) resembling the roach, typically with red fins and a deep body.
European freshwater fish resembling a roach

Examples

The lake is home to many species, including perch, pike, and rudd.
He caught a large rudd while fishing near the riverbank.
The rudd can be recognized by its distinctive red fins and silvery scales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roach - a common European freshwater fish similar in appearance to the rudd

Etymology

From Middle English rudde (redness), referring to the fish's red fins.
