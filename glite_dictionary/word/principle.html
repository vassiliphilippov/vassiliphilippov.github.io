principle

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪpl / (BrE)
a fundamental truth or law that explains how something works, especially in science, technology, or law
fundamental rule or law

Examples

The principle of conservation of energy is fundamental in physics.
She explained the basic principles behind engine operation to the students.
Understanding the principle of supply and demand is important in economics.

Synset Examples

the rationale for capital punishment
the principles of internal-combustion engines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: law - a statement describing a consistent natural phenomenon
  • Synonym: theory - a system of ideas explaining something

Etymology

From Latin 'principium' (origin, beginning).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsəpl / (BrE)
a fundamental truth or law that serves as the foundation for a system of belief, reasoning, or behavior
fundamental truth or general law

Examples

The company's core principle is to treat all customers with respect.
He refused to compromise on his principle of honesty, even when it was difficult.
Scientific experiments are designed to test the basic principle behind a theory.

Synset Examples

their principles of composition characterized all their works

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rule - an explicit or understood regulation or principle governing conduct or procedure
  • Synonym: doctrine - a belief or set of beliefs taught by a church, political party, or other group
  • Antonym: exception - a case to which a general rule does not apply

Etymology

From Latin principium (beginning, foundation), from princeps (first, chief).
