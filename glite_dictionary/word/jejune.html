jejune

1. ADJ

/ dʒɪˈdʒun / (AmE), / dʒɪˈdʒuːn / (BrE)
lacking interest, significance, or impact; dull or uninspiring.
lacking interest or significance

Examples

Many critics agreed that the movie's plot was jejune and lacked emotional depth.
His presentation was surprisingly jejune, failing to engage the audience at all.
Although the novel received publicity, readers found the story rather jejune and uninspired.

Synset Examples

an insipid personality
jejune novel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insipid - lacking flavor, excitement, or interest
  • Antonym: interesting - arousing curiosity or attention; engaging

Etymology

From Latin 'jejunus' (fasting, meager, hungry).

naive or immature; superficial

Examples

Many critics dismissed the novel's themes as jejune and lacking depth.
His jejune comments revealed a fundamental misunderstanding of the issue.
She found their jejune humor more irritating than amusing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mature - showing the mental and emotional qualities of an adult

Etymology
