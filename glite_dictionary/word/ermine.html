ermine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrmɪn / (AmE), / ˈɜːmɪn / (BrE)
a small mammal of the weasel family, especially when in its white winter coat; also, its white fur, used historically for trimming garments.
small weasel-like mammal in white winter coat

Examples

In winter, the ermine blends in perfectly with the snow-covered landscape.
The royal robe was trimmed with luxurious ermine.
We spotted an ermine darting across the snowy field during our hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoat - a small mammal related to the weasel, whose white-coat phase is called ermine

Etymology

From Old French *ermyne*, from Medieval Latin *hereminius*, probably of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɝː.mɪn / (AmE), / ˈɜː.mɪn / (BrE)
the expensive white fur of the ermine, especially used for trimming ceremonial or royal garments
white fur from the ermine

Examples

The king's robe was lined with ermine to signify his royal status.
Judges in the United Kingdom traditionally wear robes trimmed with ermine.
A cloak of ermine was once considered a symbol of great prestige.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mink - the soft, valuable fur of the mink

Etymology

From Old French *ermine*, from Medieval Latin *herminius* (weasel).
