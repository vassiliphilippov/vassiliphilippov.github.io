Ophioglossales

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊfioʊˌɡlɑːˈseɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌəʊfiəʊˌɡlɒˈseɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of ferns that includes the family Ophioglossaceae, known as adder's-tongue ferns
order of ferns (Ophioglossaceae and relatives)

Examples

The Ophioglossales include unusual ferns like the adder's-tongue and moonwort.
Botanists classify these species within the order Ophioglossales.
Research has shown that Ophioglossales are among the most ancient lineages of modern ferns.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek ophis (snake) + glōssa (tongue) + -ales (order)
