Coerebidae

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈɛrəˌbɪdiː / (AmE), / kəʊˈɛrɪˌbiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, mainly tropical American birds commonly known as honeycreepers.
family of tropical honeycreeper birds

Examples

Many species within the Coerebidae family are brightly colored and feed on nectar.
The bananaquit is one of the most widely recognized members of the Coerebidae.
Research on the Coerebidae has shed light on avian evolution in the Caribbean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeycreeper - a small songbird of the tropical Americas, especially of the Coerebidae family

Etymology

From New Latin *Coerebidae*, from the genus name *Coereba*.

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈɛr.ɪbɪˌdi / (AmE), / kəʊˈɛrɪbɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, colorful passerine birds known as the honeycreepers, native mainly to the Americas.
family of birds: the honeycreepers

Examples

The Coerebidae are well known for their vibrant plumage and specialized feeding habits.
Many ornithologists study the unique adaptations of the Coerebidae family.
The bananaquit is one of the most widespread species in the Coerebidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeycreeper - a small songbird belonging to the family Coerebidae

Etymology

From New Latin Coerebidae, based on the type genus Coereba.
