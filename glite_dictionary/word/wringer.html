wringer

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋə / (BrE)
a device consisting of two rollers between which wet clothes are squeezed to remove water, formerly used as a clothes dryer
device for squeezing water from clothes

Examples

My grandmother used a wringer to dry the laundry before electric dryers were common.
The old washing machine had a built-in wringer on top for squeezing out excess water.
You must be careful not to get your fingers caught in the wringer when using it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mangle - a machine with rollers for pressing or smoothening cloth, sometimes used for drying as well
