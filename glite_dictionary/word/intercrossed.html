intercrossed

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈkrɔst / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈkrɒst / (BrE)
(of animals or plants) produced by crossbreeding between different individuals, breeds, or varieties.
produced by crossbreeding

Examples

The breeder introduced several intercrossed varieties to increase the genetic diversity of the plants.
Many of the laboratory mice used in the experiment were intercrossed to study inherited traits.
The field contained rows of intercrossed corn, resulting from generations of controlled breeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hybrid - an animal or plant produced by crossbreeding

Etymology

From inter- (between) + cross (breed); related to 'intercross.'
