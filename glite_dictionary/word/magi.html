Magi

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.dʒaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.dʒaɪ / (BrE)
the wise men from the East who, according to the New Testament, visited the infant Jesus and brought gifts of gold, frankincense, and myrrh
wise men who visited Jesus after his birth

Examples

The story of the Magi bringing gifts to the newborn Jesus is told in the Gospel of Matthew.
Many Christmas carols mention the Magi who followed a star to Bethlehem.
In traditional nativity scenes, the Magi are often depicted bearing gold, frankincense, and myrrh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wise men - another name for the Magi who visited Jesus
  • Synonym: king - term sometimes used interchangeably, as in 'Three Kings'

Etymology

From Latin magi (plural of magus), from Old Persian maguš (magician, wise man).
