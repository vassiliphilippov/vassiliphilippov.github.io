Homarus capensis

1. NOUN

/ həʊˈmɑːrəs kəˈpɛnsɪs / (AmE), / həʊˈmɑːrəs kəˈpɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A small species of lobster found in southern Africa, also known as the Cape lobster.
Cape lobster species from southern Africa

Examples

The rare Homarus capensis is a unique species of lobster found only along the southern African coast.
Marine biologists studied the habitat of Homarus capensis to understand its adaptation to local waters.
Unlike its northern relatives, Homarus capensis remains relatively small throughout its life.

Etymology

From Latin: genus Homarus (lobster) + species name capensis, meaning 'of the Cape'.
