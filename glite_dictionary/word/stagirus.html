Stagirus

1. PROPN

/ stəˈdʒaɪrəs / (AmE), / stəˈdʒaɪrəs / (BrE)
An ancient town in northern Greece, known as the birthplace of the philosopher Aristotle.
ancient Greek town, birthplace of Aristotle

Examples

Aristotle was born in Stagirus in 384 BCE.
The ruins of Stagirus can be found in modern-day northern Greece.
Many scholars visit Stagirus to learn more about Aristotle's early life.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Στάγειρα (Stágeira), the original name of the town.
