Alexander Borodin

1. PROPN

/ ˌælɪɡˈzændər ˌbɔːrəˈdin / (AmE), / ˌælɪɡˈzandə ˌbɒrəˈdiːn / (BrE)
(1833–1887) A Russian composer and chemist, known for his symphonies, chamber music, and contributions to the nationalist style in Russian classical music.
Russian composer and chemist (1833–1887)

Examples

The music of Alexander Borodin continues to inspire classical composers today.

Alexander Borodin was also a respected chemist in addition to his achievements in music.
