pricker

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪkər / (AmE), / ˈprɪkə / (BrE)
a pointed hand tool used for making small holes, especially for brads or small screws
tool for making small holes

Examples

He used a pricker to mark the spots where each screw would go.
Before starting the stitching, she made tiny holes with a pricker.
A pricker is an essential tool for detailed woodwork projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awl - a small pointed tool for piercing holes, especially in leather

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪkər / (AmE), / ˈprɪkə / (BrE)
a small, sharp-pointed projection resembling a spike on a stem or leaf, especially on a plant or shrub
small plant spike or thorn

Examples

The rose bush was covered in prickers that scratched my arm as I walked past.
Carefully remove any pricker from the stem before arranging the flowers in a vase.
The children learned to avoid the prickers when playing near the blackberry bush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorn - a stiff, sharp-pointed, woody projection on a plant
  • Synonym: spine - a stiff, sharp-pointed structure on a plant or animal
