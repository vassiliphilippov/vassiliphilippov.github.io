politic

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːlətɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪtɪk / (BrE)
behaving in a way that is smooth, agreeable, and courteous, especially to achieve advantage or avoid offence
smoothly agreeable and courteous

Examples

It would not be politic to challenge your boss in front of the whole team.
He offered a politic reply to the sensitive question, avoiding controversy.
She remained politic throughout the negotiations, winning respect from all parties.

Synset Examples

he was too politic to quarrel with so important a personage
the manager pacified the customer with a smooth apology for the error

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diplomatic - tactful and able to deal with people in a sensitive way
  • Antonym: tactless - lacking sensitivity in dealing with others

Etymology

From Latin politicus (of citizens or the state), via Old French politique.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːlətɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of an action or attitude) showing good judgment or shrewdness, especially in dealing with sensitive situations; prudent or tactful.
shrewd or prudent; tactful

Examples

It was politic of her to avoid criticism by addressing the main concerns directly.
His politic response helped ease tensions during the heated discussion.
Choosing to stay silent was the most politic action he could take in that situation.

Synset Examples

it is neither polite nor politic to get into other people's quarrels
a politic decision
a politic manager
a politic old scoundrel
a shrewd and politic reply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudent - acting with or showing care and thought for the future
  • Antonym: tactless - showing a lack of sensitivity in dealing with others

Etymology

From French *politique*, from Latin *politicus* (of citizens or the state), from Greek *politikós*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01818685-a
Synset POS: a
