Will H. Hays

1. PROPN

/ wɪl eɪtʃ heɪz / (AmE), / wɪl eɪtʃ heɪz / (BrE)
(1879–1954) United States lawyer and politician who created the 'Hays Code,' regulating moral content in U.S. films from 1930 to 1966.
U.S. film code creator (1879–1954)

Examples

Will H. Hays played a pivotal role in shaping the early standards of American cinema.
The influence of Will H. Hays was evident in Hollywood productions throughout the mid-20th century.
Many filmmakers found the Will H. Hays code to be both restrictive and challenging.
