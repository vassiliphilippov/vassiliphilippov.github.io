subclinical

1. ADJ

/ sʌbˈklɪnɪkəl / (AmE), / sʌbˈklɪnɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to a stage of a disease in which symptoms are not yet present or observable.
not showing noticeable disease symptoms
medical

Examples

The infection was subclinical, so the patient showed no outward signs of illness.
Researchers often study subclinical cases to understand the disease before symptoms appear.
Many people carry a subclinical virus without ever becoming aware of it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clinical - relating to the observation and treatment of actual patients

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under) + 'clinical' (from Greek 'klinē,' meaning bed).
