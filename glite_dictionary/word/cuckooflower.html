cuckooflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊkoʊˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈkʊkəʊˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a wild plant of the cress family, with small pale pink or white flowers, native to Europe and North America
wild cress with pale flowers

Examples

A field of cuckooflower bloomed early this spring, covering the meadow in pale pink and white.
The cuckooflower is often found growing beside streams and damp grasslands.
Many gardeners appreciate the delicate appearance of the cuckooflower in wildflower gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady's smock - another common name for the cuckooflower

Etymology

Named for its association with the time of year when cuckoos are heard in spring.
