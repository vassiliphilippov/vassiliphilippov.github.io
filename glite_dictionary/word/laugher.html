laugher

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæfər / (AmE), / ˈlɑːfə / (BrE)
an event or contest that is easily won, typically with little difficulty or challenge for the victor
easy victory or contest
informal

Examples

The football match turned into a laugher when one team scored six goals in the first half.
What was expected to be a close contest ended up being a laugher.
Their surprise victory last week was a laugher, with the final score being 20–2.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walkover - a victory that is won very easily, often because the opponent withdraws or is much weaker
  • Synonym: rout - a decisive defeat in a contest or game

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæfər / (AmE), / ˈlɑːfə / (BrE)
a person who is laughing or who tends to laugh easily
person who laughs or laughs easily

Examples

Megan is such a laugher; she finds humor in almost any situation.
At the comedy show, every laugher in the crowd was doubled over with joy.
He's known as an easy laugher who brings light to the workplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laugh - to make the spontaneous sounds and movements of the face and body that are the instinctive expressions of lively amusement

Etymology

From 'laugh' + '-er', denoting someone who laughs.
