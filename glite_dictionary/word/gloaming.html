gloaming

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡloʊmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡləʊmɪŋ / (BrE)
The period of twilight just after sunset and before nightfall.
twilight after sunset
archaic

Examples

The fields looked magical in the soft gloaming of evening.
We took a quiet walk through the woods as the gloaming settled in.
In the gloaming, the outlines of the mountains became faint and mysterious.

Synset Examples

he loved the twilight
they finished before the fall of night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twilight - the soft light from the sky when the sun is below the horizon

Etymology

From Old English *glōmung* (twilight), related to 'glōwan' (to glow).
