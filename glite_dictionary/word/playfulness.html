playfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪ.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpleɪ.f(ə)l.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being light-hearted or ready to engage in fun or amusements
quality of being playful or fun-loving

Examples

Her playfulness made everyone in the group feel at ease.
The kitten’s playfulness was evident as it chased the ball of yarn around the room.
He brought a sense of playfulness to the usually serious meetings.

Synset Examples

her playfulness surprised me
he was fun to be with

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious
  • Synonym: mischievousness - the quality of enjoying mischief or playful teasing

Etymology

From play + -fulness, formed from the noun 'play' and the suffix '-ful' denoting quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈpleɪfʊlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being light-hearted, fun-loving, or inclined to engage in enjoyable or amusing activities
quality of being playful or fun-loving

Examples

The playfulness of the puppy brought smiles to everyone in the room.
Her playfulness made the meeting much more enjoyable than usual.
There was a sense of playfulness in his voice as he teased his friend.

Synset Examples

I do it for the fun of it
he is fun to have around

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fun - enjoyment or amusement
  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious or earnest

Etymology

From 'playful' + '-ness', with 'play' from Old English 'plegian' (to exercise, frolic).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪ.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpleɪ.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being light-hearted, fun-loving, or full of playful spirit
the quality of being playful or fun-loving

Examples

The playfulness in her voice made everyone around her smile.
Children often express their playfulness through laughter and creative games.
He admired the playfulness of the puppy as it chased its tail.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious rather than playful or light-hearted
  • Synonym: mischief - a fondness for causing playful trouble or teasing

Etymology

From 'playful' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the state or quality of being playful.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07527278-n
Synset POS: n
