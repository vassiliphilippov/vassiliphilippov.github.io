mop

1. NOUN

/ mɑp / (AmE), / mɒp / (BrE)
a cleaning implement consisting of absorbent material attached to a handle, used for washing floors
floor-cleaning tool with absorbent head

Examples

She dipped the mop into the soapy water before cleaning the kitchen floor.
After the spill, he grabbed a mop to soak up the juice.
Every evening, the janitor uses a fresh mop to clean the hallways.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: broom - a long-handled brush used for sweeping floors

Etymology

From Middle English mopp (rag, napkin), probably of Low German origin.

2. VERB

/ mɑp / (AmE), / mɒp / (BrE)
to clean or wipe a surface using a mop or a similar implement
clean or wipe with a mop

Examples

He had to mop the kitchen floor after the party.
She gently mopped his brow as he lay in bed, exhausted.
I watched as the janitor mopped up the spilled water in the hallway.

Synset Examples

Mop the hallway now
He mopped her forehead with a towel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wipe - to rub a surface to clean or dry it
  • Synonym: scrub - to clean something by rubbing it hard, often with a brush

Etymology

From Middle English moppe (bundle of coarse cloth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01395993-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ mɑp / (AmE), / mɒp / (BrE)
(archaic or literary) To make a sad or sulky face, often by thrusting out one's lower lip.
make a sad or sulky face
archaic

Examples

When scolded, the boy began to mop and whimper softly in the corner.
She would mop in disappointment whenever she was denied a treat.
He likes to mop and act aggrieved to get sympathy from his friends.

Synset Examples

mop and mow
The girl pouted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pout - push one's lips or lower lip forward to show annoyance or sadness
