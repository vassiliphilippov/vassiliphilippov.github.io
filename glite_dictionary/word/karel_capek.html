Karel Capek

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɑːrəl ˈtʃɑːpɛk / (AmE), / ˈkɑːrəl ˈtʃapɛk / (BrE)
(1890–1938) Czech writer who introduced the word 'robot' into the English language.
Czech writer, coined 'robot'

Examples

Karel Capek is credited with bringing the word 'robot' into popular use through his play R.U.R.
The novels of Karel Capek explore themes of science, technology, and society.
Many science fiction enthusiasts regard Karel Capek as a pioneer of the genre.
