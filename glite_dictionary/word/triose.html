triose

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.oʊs / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.əʊs / (BrE)
a simple sugar (monosaccharide) containing three carbon atoms in each molecule
a monosaccharide with three carbons
medical

Examples

Glyceraldehyde is a well-known example of a triose found in metabolic pathways.
The breakdown of glucose involves the formation of two trioses during glycolysis.
Each triose molecule consists of three carbon atoms, making it the simplest monosaccharide.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tetrose - a monosaccharide with four carbon atoms
  • Degree Higher: pentose - a monosaccharide with five carbon atoms

Etymology

From Greek 'treis' (three) and 'ose' (sugar suffix).
