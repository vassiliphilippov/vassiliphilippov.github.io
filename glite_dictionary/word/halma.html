halma

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæl.mə / (AmE), / ˈhæl.mə / (BrE)
a board game in which players move pieces across a checkered board to occupy the opponent's starting area
board game moving pieces to opponent's base

Examples

We spent the evening playing halma with the whole family.
He quickly learned the rules of halma and soon became an expert.
Although less popular today, halma inspired other board games like Chinese checkers.

Etymology

From Greek halma (a leap, jump), referencing the jumping moves in the game.
