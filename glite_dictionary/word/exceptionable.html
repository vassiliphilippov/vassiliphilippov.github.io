exceptionable

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsɛpʃənəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛpʃənəbl̩ / (BrE)
liable to objection; likely to cause offense or disapproval
liable to objection or disapproval

Examples

The committee found several exceptionable clauses in the proposed contract.
His remarks were considered highly exceptionable by many of his colleagues.
The critic argued that the film's portrayal of the issue was exceptionable and insensitive.

Synset Examples

a thoroughly unpleasant highly exceptionable piece of writing
found the politician's views objectionable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: objectionable - arousing disapproval or opposition
  • Antonym: unexceptionable - not open to objection, entirely acceptable

Etymology

From Middle French exceptionable, from Latin exceptio (exemption, objection) + -able.
