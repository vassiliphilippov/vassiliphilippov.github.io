Momus

1. PROPN

/ ˈmoʊ.məs / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.məs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the god of blame, criticism, and mockery; often personified as a figure of satire or ridicule.
Greek god of blame and mockery

Examples

In the story, Momus satirized the gods for their flaws.
Writers have invoked Momus to represent the spirit of satire and criticism.
The tradition of poking fun at authority can be traced back to Momus in Greek myths.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Μῶμος (Mômos), meaning 'blame' or 'reproach.'
