reissue

1. VERB

/ riˈɪʃu / (AmE), / riˈɪʃuː / (BrE)
to publish, distribute, or issue something again, especially in a revised or new form
publish or issue again

Examples

The publisher decided to reissue the classic novel with a new introduction.
You can request the bank to reissue your lost credit card.
The software company plans to reissue the update to fix several bugs.

Synset Examples

if you forget your password, it can be changed and reissued

Related Terms

  • Synonym: issue - to formally give out or publish

Etymology

From re- (again) + issue (to send out, publish), from Middle French 'issuer'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02485290-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ riˈɪʃu / (AmE), / riˈɪʃuː / (BrE)
a publication or recording, such as a book or music album, that is released again without changes, usually after its initial release
a publication or recording issued again

Examples

The publisher decided to reissue the classic novel due to renewed interest.
Her favorite album was only available as a reissue with bonus tracks.
Collectors eagerly awaited the reissue of the rare comic book.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edition - a particular version or release of a publication
  • Antonym: original - the first or initial publication or recording

Etymology

From re- (again) + issue (from Latin 'exire' to go out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06280961-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ riˈɪʃu / (AmE), / riˈɪsju / (BrE)
to print or issue a book, record, or other publication again, especially in a revised or updated form
to publish or print again

Examples

The publisher decided to reissue the novel with a new introduction and cover art.
They plan to reissue the band's classic album on vinyl next year.
Many out-of-print books are now reissued through digital platforms.

Synset Examples

They never reprinted the famous treatise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to republish - to publish again; to issue a new edition
  • Synonym: reprint - to print again, especially a new edition of a book or publication

Etymology

From re- (again) + issue, from Old French 'issuer' (to go out, come out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01739979-v
Synset POS: v
