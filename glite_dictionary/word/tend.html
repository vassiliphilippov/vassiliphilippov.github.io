to tend

1. VERB

/ tɛnd / (AmE), / tɛnd / (BrE)
to care for or look after someone or something
care for; look after

Examples

Every morning, she tends the garden carefully before breakfast.
The nurse tended the patient during his recovery in the hospital.
He tends to his elderly father's needs with great dedication.

Synset Examples

She tends to the children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to care for - to look after or provide for the needs of someone or something
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for properly

Etymology

From Middle English tenden, from Old French tendre, from Latin tendere (to stretch, strive toward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02555544-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tɛnd / (AmE), / tɛnd / (BrE)
to manage or look after something, especially as part of one's job
to manage or look after

Examples

She has tended the bookstore on the corner for over twenty years.
During the summer, college students often tend local cafes for extra income.
It's his duty to tend the information desk during the afternoon shift.

Synset Examples

tend a store

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to manage - to be in charge of; direct or supervise
  • Synonym: to oversee - to supervise or watch over a process or job

Etymology

From Latin 'tendere' (to stretch, extend, direct).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02443912-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ tɛnd / (AmE), / tɛnd / (BrE)
to regularly behave in a particular way or have a general tendency; to be likely to do something
to usually behave in a certain way

Examples

I tend to wake up early, even on weekends.
Children tend to imitate the behavior of adults around them.
Old wooden houses tend to require frequent maintenance.

Synset Examples

She tends to be nervous before her lectures
These dresses run small
He inclined to corpulence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incline - to have a tendency or preference towards something
  • Synonym: be likely - to have a high chance or probability to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'tendere' (to stretch, incline).
