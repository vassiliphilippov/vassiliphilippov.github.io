interruption

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntəˈrʌpʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈrʌpʃən / (BrE)
an act of stopping the continuous progress of an activity or process; a period when something is halted or delayed
act of stopping or breaking continuity

Examples

The unexpected interruption in the power supply lasted for over two hours.
We apologize for the interruption and will resume our program shortly.
He grew frustrated with the repeated interruption of his work by phone calls.

Synset Examples

it was presented without commercial breaks
there was a gap in his account

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pause - a temporary stop in an activity or speech
  • Antonym: continuation - the act of carrying on or resuming something without interruption

Etymology

From Latin 'interruptio', from 'interrumpere' (to break apart).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntəˈrʌpʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈrʌpʃən / (BrE)
an event or occurrence that unexpectedly stops or breaks the continuity of something in progress
unexpected disruption or break

Examples

We apologized for the interruption and resumed the meeting as soon as possible.
The movie was enjoyable except for the constant interruption by phone calls.
A fire alarm caused an unexpected interruption during the final exam.

Synset Examples

the telephone is an annoying interruption
there was a break in the action when a player was hurt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disruption - disturbance or interruption of a process or activity
  • Antonym: continuation - the act of continuing or resuming something

Etymology

From Latin interruptio (a breaking off) from interrumpere (to break apart).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪntəˈrʌpʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈrʌpʃən / (BrE)
a period when something stops or is stopped temporarily
a temporary pause or break

Examples

The power outage caused a brief interruption in the meeting.
There was an interruption in the broadcast due to technical difficulties.
Without any interruption, the ceremony proceeded smoothly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pause - a brief stop in an activity or speech
  • Antonym: continuation - the act of continuing after a pause or interruption

Etymology

From Latin interruptio(n-) ‘a breaking off’
