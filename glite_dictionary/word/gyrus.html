gyrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪərəs / (BrE)
An outwardly curving fold or ridge on the surface of the brain, separating adjacent grooves (sulci).
a fold or ridge on the brain's surface
medical

Examples

The precentral gyrus plays a critical role in voluntary motor control.
Damage to the left temporal gyrus can impair language comprehension.
Researchers mapped the activation of each gyrus during memory tasks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sulcus - a groove or furrow on the surface of the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'gyrus' (circle, ring), from Greek 'gyros' (circle, ring).
