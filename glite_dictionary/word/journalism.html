journalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝː.nə.lɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜː.nə.lɪ.zəm / (BrE)
newspapers and magazines regarded collectively as an institution or profession
the press; newspapers and magazines collectively

Examples

Many critics argue that journalism plays a vital role in maintaining a free society.
She decided to pursue a career in journalism after graduating from university.
The impact of digital media on traditional journalism has been profound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the press - the collective body of journalists and news publications

Etymology

From French 'journalisme', from 'journal' (daily record, newspaper).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːnəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːnəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the profession or activity of gathering, writing, editing, and presenting news and information for media such as newspapers, magazines, television, or online platforms
the profession of reporting news

Examples

She decided to pursue a career in journalism after graduating from university.
Good journalism requires accuracy, impartiality, and a strong sense of ethics.
The internet has dramatically changed the landscape of journalism in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reporting - the act of telling or writing about news events for publication or broadcast
  • Synonym: journalist - a person who practices journalism

Etymology

From French *journalisme*, from *journal* (daily record, newspaper), from Latin *diurnalis* (daily).
