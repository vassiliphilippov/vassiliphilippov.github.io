strangulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌstræŋɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstraŋɡjʊˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The condition of breathing being stopped by compressing the airway, typically by external pressure on the neck.
stopping breathing by throat compression
medical

Examples

The victim died from strangulation caused by pressure on the neck.
Medical reports concluded that there was evidence of strangulation rather than accidental choking.
Prompt medical attention is critical in cases of attempted strangulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffocation - death or unconsciousness caused by lack of air to breathe
  • Synonym: asphyxiation - state or process of being deprived of oxygen, leading to unconsciousness or death

Etymology

From Latin strangulatio (a choking), from strangulare (to choke).

2. NOUN

/ ˌstræŋɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstræŋɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of killing or injuring someone by squeezing or constricting the throat, preventing breathing
act of suffocating by constricting the throat

Examples

The coroner determined that the victim died of strangulation.
Signs of strangulation were evident around his neck.
The detective suspected strangulation due to the bruising patterns.

Synset Examples

no evidence that the choking was done by the accused

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choking - the act of blocking the windpipe or restricting breathing
  • Synonym: suffocation - death caused by lack of air or inability to breathe

Etymology

From Latin 'strangulatio', from 'strangulare' (to strangle).

3. NOUN

/ ˌstræŋɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstræŋɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(Pathology) the constriction or compression of a body part, especially a blood vessel or organ, so as to cut off the flow of blood or other fluid.
constriction cutting off blood flow (medical)
medical

Examples

The surgeon quickly relieved the strangulation of the intestine to restore normal blood flow.
Untreated hernias can sometimes lead to strangulation of the affected tissue, requiring emergency surgery.
Strangulation of a limb can result in serious tissue damage if not addressed promptly.

Synset Examples

strangulation of the intestine

Etymology

From Latin strangulatio, from strangulare (to strangle).
