Ontario

1. PROPN

/ ɑnˈtɛrioʊ / (AmE), / ɒnˈtɛəriəʊ / (BrE)
a large province in central Canada, known for its prosperity and industrialization, with Toronto as its capital.
province in central Canada

Examples

Many immigrants choose to settle in Ontario because of its diverse job opportunities.
Ontario experiences all four seasons, with hot summers and cold, snowy winters.
Toronto, the largest city in Ontario, is a major financial center in Canada.

Etymology

From Canadian French, named after Lake Ontario; Iroquoian origin, possibly meaning 'beautiful lake'.

2. PROPN

/ ɑnˈtɛrioʊ / (AmE), / ɒnˈtɪəriəʊ / (BrE)
the smallest of the Great Lakes, located on the border between Canada and the United States
the smallest Great Lake

Examples

Lake Ontario serves as the outlet to the Atlantic Ocean via the Saint Lawrence River.
Many cities, including Toronto, are located on the shores of Ontario.
The water levels in Ontario are monitored to help manage regional flooding.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Lake Superior - the largest of the Great Lakes
  • Degree Higher: Lake Erie - a larger Great Lake lying to the west of Lake Ontario

Etymology

From Iroquoian, perhaps meaning 'lake of shining waters'.
