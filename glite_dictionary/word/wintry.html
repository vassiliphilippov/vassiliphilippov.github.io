wintry

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪntri / (AmE), / ˈwɪntri / (BrE)
Characteristic of or relating to winter; cold, bleak, or typical of winter.
relating to or typical of winter

Examples

We walked through the wintry landscape, bundled up against the biting wind.
The wintry sunlight barely warmed the frozen ground.
Her cheeks were red from the wintry air as she entered the house.

Synset Examples

suffered severe wintry weather
brown wintry grasses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wintery - Variant spelling of wintry, less common
  • Antonym: summery - Characteristic of or relating to summer

Etymology

From Old English 'wintrig' (of winter), from 'winter' + '-y'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɪntri / (AmE), / ˈwɪntri / (BrE)
(of a manner, expression, or atmosphere) lacking warmth or friendliness; cold or unfriendly.
cold or unfriendly in manner

Examples

She greeted him with a wintry smile that revealed her true feelings.
His wintry tone made it clear he was not pleased with the news.
Despite her wintry manner, she agreed to help with the project.

Synset Examples

a frigid greeting
got a frosty reception
a frozen look on their faces
a glacial handshake
icy stare
wintry smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frosty - cold or unfriendly in manner
  • Synonym: icy - unfriendly or showing a lack of warmth
  • Antonym: cordial - warm and friendly in manner

Etymology

From Old English 'wintrig' (of or pertaining to winter); figurative sense developed in Modern English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01261336-s
Synset POS: s
