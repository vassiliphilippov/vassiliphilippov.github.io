Gavialidae

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈvaɪələˌdi / (AmE), / ɡəˈvaɪəˌlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, long-snouted crocodilian reptiles comprising the gavials or gharials, native to parts of South Asia.
family of long-snouted crocodilians

Examples

The Gavialidae family includes only a few surviving species such as the gharial.
Researchers are studying the evolutionary history of Gavialidae in river systems.
Unlike other crocodilians, Gavialidae have slender snouts suited for catching fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gharial - a large crocodilian with a long narrow snout from South Asia

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Gavialis', the type genus, from 'gavial' (a kind of crocodilian) and '-idae', a zoological family suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ɡəˌvaɪəˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / ɡəˌvaɪəˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, long-snouted crocodilian reptiles, commonly known as gharials and false gharials, native to parts of Asia.
family of crocodilian reptiles (gharials)

Examples

The Gavialidae are distinguished by their extremely long, narrow snouts adapted for catching fish.
Scientists recently conducted a survey of Gavialidae populations along the riverbanks of India.
Unlike other crocodilians, members of the Gavialidae rarely leave the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocodile - a large reptile related to gavials, with a broad snout
  • Synonym: Gavialis - the genus of the true gharial

Etymology

From New Latin 'Gavialidae', from 'Gavialis' (the type genus) + '-idae' (family suffix).
