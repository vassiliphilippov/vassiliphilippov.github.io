ragweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈræɡˌwid / (AmE), / ˈræɡˌwiːd / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Ambrosia, native to North America and Europe, known for its yellowish flowers and for producing pollen that causes allergic reactions; sometimes considered a troublesome weed and toxic to cattle if eaten in large amounts.
allergy-causing weed; toxic to cattle

Examples

Many people suffer from allergies caused by ragweed pollen in late summer.
Farmers must take care to keep ragweed out of their pastures, as it can be harmful to livestock.
She identified the tall, yellow-flowered plant in her garden as ragweed.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræɡˌwid / (AmE), / ˈræɡ.wiːd / (BrE)
A North American plant of the genus Ambrosia, known for producing pollen that causes allergic reactions such as hay fever.
a plant causing hay fever, genus Ambrosia

Examples

Every autumn, ragweed pollen triggers allergies for millions of people.
The fields behind her house were filled with ragweed and other wild plants.
Doctors advise allergy sufferers to avoid ragweed during peak pollen season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ambrosia - the genus to which ragweed plants belong
  • Degree Higher: hay fever - an allergy caused by pollen from plants like ragweed
