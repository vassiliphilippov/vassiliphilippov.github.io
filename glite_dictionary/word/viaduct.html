viaduct

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪ.əˌdʌkt / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.ədʌkt / (BrE)
A long bridge, typically supported by a series of arches or spans, used to carry a road or railway over a valley, gorge, or other low ground.
long bridge with arches for road or railway

Examples

The train crossed the viaduct high above the river, offering breathtaking views of the valley below.
Workers spent two years restoring the historic stone viaduct outside the city.
A new viaduct was constructed to replace the old railway bridge that had collapsed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bridge - structure built to span physical obstacles for transportation

Etymology

From Latin 'via' (road) and 'ductus' (led, conducted).
