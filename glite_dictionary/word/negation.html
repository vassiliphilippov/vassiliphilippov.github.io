negation

1. NOUN

/ nɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / nɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
a negative statement, or the act of denying or refusing a statement or proposition
the act of denying or a negative statement

Examples

His response was a clear negation of the accusations made against him.
The concept of negation is fundamental in formal logic and mathematics.
She interpreted his silence as a subtle negation of her proposal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denial - the statement that something is not true
  • Antonym: affirmation - a statement that something is true; the opposite of negation

Etymology

From Latin 'negatio' (denial), from 'negare' (to deny).

2. NOUN

/ nɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / nɪˈɡeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(Logic) A statement or proposition that is true if and only if another specified proposition is false.
logical proposition in opposition

Examples

In formal logic, the negation of a true statement is always false.
The symbol '~' is commonly used to represent negation in mathematical expressions.
Every proposition has a uniquely corresponding negation in classical logic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: affirmation - the assertion that something is true

Etymology

From Latin negatio (denial), from negare (to deny).

3. NOUN

/ nɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / nɪˈɡeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of stating that something is not true or the process of denying something; denial or contradiction of a statement or proposition
the act of denying or contradicting

Examples

The politician's negation of the allegations failed to convince the public.
In grammar, negation is expressed by words like 'not' or 'never'.
His constant negation of others' ideas made collaboration difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denial - the act of refusing to accept the truth of a statement
  • Antonym: affirmation - the act of stating that something is true; assertion

Etymology

From Latin negatio (denial), from negare (to deny).
