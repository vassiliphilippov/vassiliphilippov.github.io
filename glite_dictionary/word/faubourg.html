faubourg

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈbʊərɡ / (AmE), / foˈbʊəɡ / (BrE)
a district or suburb, especially one outside the original city limits (especially in New Orleans, often used in place names)
district or suburb, esp. in New Orleans

Examples

Many Creole families once lived in the faubourg that stretched downriver from the French Quarter.
She rented an apartment in the lively faubourg known for its jazz clubs.
Several historic homes are located in the faubourgs surrounding downtown New Orleans.

Synset Examples

in Faubourg Sainte-Marie

Etymology

From French *faubourg* (suburb), from Old French *forsbourg*, from Latin *foris* (outside) + *burgus* (town).
