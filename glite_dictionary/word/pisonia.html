Pisonia

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈsoʊniə / (AmE), / pɪˈsəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of often thorny tropical trees, shrubs, and some vines native mainly to the Americas.
genus of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The seeds of the Pisonia tree are notorious for sticking to birds' feathers.
Many island ecosystems depend on the presence of Pisonia forests for native bird habitats.
Botanists discovered several rare species within the Pisonia genus during their expedition.

Etymology

Named after Willem Piso, a 17th-century Dutch physician and naturalist.

2. NOUN

/ pɪˈsoʊniə / (AmE), / pɪˈsəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of often thorny tropical trees, shrubs, and some vines, mainly native to the Americas.
genus of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The Pisonia trees are known for their sticky seeds that can trap small birds.
Researchers discovered several new species within the Pisonia genus in the rainforest.
Many Pisonia plants thrive in coastal and subtropical environments.

Etymology

Named after Willem Piso (1611–1678), Dutch physician and naturalist.
