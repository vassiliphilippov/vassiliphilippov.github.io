vena basivertebralis

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə ˌbæz.i.vɜr.təˈbreɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈveɪ.nə ˌbæz.i.vɜː.tɪˈbreɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
a vein that drains the spongy tissue inside the vertebrae and empties into the anterior internal vertebral venous plexus
vein in the vertebrae draining spongy tissue
medical

Examples

The vena basivertebralis plays a critical role in draining blood from the interior of each vertebral body.
During spinal surgery, the surgeon carefully avoided injuring the vena basivertebralis.
Blockage of the venae basivertebrales can contribute to certain spinal vascular conditions.

Etymology

From Latin: vena (vein) + basi- (base) + vertebralis (of the vertebra).
