Alcea rosea

1. NOUN

/ ælˈsiːə ˈroʊsiə / (AmE), / ælˈsiːə ˈrəʊziə / (BrE)
a tall garden plant with terminal racemes of showy white, pink, or purple flowers, commonly known as the hollyhock
tall flowering plant; hollyhock

Examples

Alcea rosea adds a beautiful vertical accent to traditional cottage gardens.
Many gardeners grow Alcea rosea for its tall spikes of colorful blossoms.
The village flower show awarded first prize to an impressive specimen of Alcea rosea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hollyhock - common name for Alcea rosea, a tall garden plant with showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'alcea' (mallow) and 'rosea' (rosy, pink).
