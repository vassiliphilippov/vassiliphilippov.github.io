maternal

1. ADJ

/ məˈtɜrnəl / (AmE), / məˈtɜːnəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a mother, especially in showing care, kindness, or affection.
characteristic of a mother

Examples

She showed a maternal concern for the well-being of her students.
His aunt's maternal instincts made her the family's go-to for comfort.
The nurse offered maternal care and support to the frightened children.

Synset Examples

warm maternal affection for her guest

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paternal - characteristic of a father

Etymology

From Latin maternus (of a mother), from mater (mother).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01739133-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ məˈtɝː.nəl / (AmE), / məˈtɜː.nəl / (BrE)
Relating to or inherited from the mother's side of the family.
on the mother's side

Examples

She inherited her maternal grandfather's blue eyes.
His maternal relatives live in Spain.
The recipe was passed down from her maternal great-aunt.

Synset Examples

my maternal grandmother

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paternal - relating to the father's side

Etymology

From Latin 'maternus', from 'mater' meaning 'mother'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01979414-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ məˈtɝː.nəl / (AmE), / məˈtɜː.nəl / (BrE)
relating to or derived from one's mother
relating to one's mother

Examples

Her maternal instincts made her attentive to every need of her child.
The disease can be passed on through maternal genes.
He inherited his maternal grandmother's blue eyes.

Synset Examples

maternal genes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paternal - relating to or derived from one's father

Etymology

From Latin mater (mother) via Late Latin maternalis.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02770139-a
Synset POS: a
