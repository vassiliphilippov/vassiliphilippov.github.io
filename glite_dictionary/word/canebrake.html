canebrake

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪnˌbreɪk / (AmE), / ˈkeɪnˌbreɪk / (BrE)
A dense thicket or growth of cane, especially of giant cane, typically found in wet or low-lying areas.
dense growth of cane plants

Examples

A group of birds flew out of the canebrake as we approached the riverbank.
The rare rabbit is found only in isolated canebrakes across the region.
Hikers avoided the dense canebrake because it was nearly impossible to pass through.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thicket - a dense group of bushes or trees

Etymology

Formed from 'cane' (from Old French 'cane', from Latin 'canna') + 'brake' (thicket).
