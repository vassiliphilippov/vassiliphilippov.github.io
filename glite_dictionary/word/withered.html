withered

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪðərd / (AmE), / ˈwɪðəd / (BrE)
lean and wrinkled, often showing signs of age, illness, or lack of vitality due to shrinkage or drying out.
lean and wrinkled, shrunken

Examples

The withered leaves crunched beneath his feet as he walked through the garden.
Her withered hands trembled as she poured the tea for her guests.
After months without rain, the crops appeared withered and lifeless.

Synset Examples

the old woman's shriveled skin
he looked shriveled and ill
a shrunken old man
a lanky scarecrow of a man with withered face and lantern jaws
he did well despite his withered arm
a wizened little man with frizzy grey hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrivelled - lean and wrinkled from shrinkage, especially from dryness or age
  • Synonym: wizen - to become dry, shrunken, and wrinkled, often due to aging

Etymology

From Middle English 'witheren', probably from a Germanic root meaning 'dry up'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɪðərd / (AmE), / ˈwɪðəd / (BrE)
(especially of plants or vegetation) dry and shriveled, having lost moisture or freshness.
dry and shriveled (especially of plants)

Examples

After weeks without rain, the once-green fields were now covered in withered grass.
The old bouquet sat in the vase, its withered petals crumbling at a touch.
They could see withered vines clinging to the abandoned garden wall.

Synset Examples

dried-up grass
the desert was edged with sere vegetation
shriveled leaves on the unwatered seedlings
withered vines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dried-up - having lost all moisture, especially through heat or lack of water
  • Synonym: sere - (of plants) dry or withered
  • Antonym: fresh - (of plants) not dried or withered, retaining moisture and life

Etymology

From Middle English 'witheren', of uncertain origin.
