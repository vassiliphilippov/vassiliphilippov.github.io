Prunus caroliniana

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs ˌkærəˌlaɪˈniːə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs ˌkærəˌləˈnaɪə / (BrE)
A small evergreen tree native to the southern United States, known for its dark glossy leaves and clusters of small white flowers.
evergreen tree of the southern U.S.

Examples

The Prunus caroliniana is often planted as an ornamental tree in southern gardens.
Wildlife, especially birds, are attracted to the berries of the Prunus caroliniana.
The Prunus caroliniana blooms in early spring, producing fragrant white flowers.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Prunus' (the genus name for plum and cherry trees) and 'caroliniana' meaning 'of Carolina'.
