obviously

1. ADV

/ ˈɑːb.vi.əs.li / (AmE), / ˈɒb.vi.əs.li / (BrE)
In a way that is easily perceived or understood; clearly or plainly.
clearly; plainly; without doubt

Examples

The mistake was obviously a result of miscommunication.
He obviously cares about his family very much.
She was obviously upset by the outcome of the meeting.

Synset Examples

the answer is obviously wrong
she was in bed and evidently in great pain
he was manifestly too important to leave off the guest list
it is all patently nonsense
she has apparently been living here for some time
I thought he owned the property, but apparently not
You are plainly wrong
he is plain stubborn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clearly - In a clear manner; without doubt or confusion
  • Synonym: apparently - As far as one knows or can see; seemingly
  • Synonym: evidently - In a way that is clearly seen or understood
  • Antonym: subtly - In a way that is not obvious; delicately or faintly

Etymology

From 'obvious' + '-ly'; 'obvious' from Latin 'obvius' (in the way, easily seen).
