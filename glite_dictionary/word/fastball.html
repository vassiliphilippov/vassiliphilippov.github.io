fastball

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæstˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈfɑːstˌbɔːl / (BrE)
(in baseball) a pitch thrown at maximum velocity, typically straight and fast.
a high-velocity baseball pitch

Examples

The pitcher threw a fastball right down the middle for a strike.
She struggled to hit his blazing fastball during practice.
The batter anticipated a curve, but got a fastball instead.

Synset Examples

he swung late on the fastball
he showed batters nothing but smoke

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curveball - a baseball pitch with a curving motion
  • Antonym: slider - a baseball pitch that slides laterally as it approaches the plate
  • Antonym: changeup - a slow baseball pitch intended to deceive the batter
