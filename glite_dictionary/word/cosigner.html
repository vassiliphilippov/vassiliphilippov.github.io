cosigner

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈsaɪnər / (AmE), / kəʊˈsaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who signs a document in addition to the principal signer, typically to guarantee a loan or obligation
person signing with another as guarantee

Examples

My father agreed to be a cosigner on my student loan application.
If you miss a payment, your cosigner will be responsible for the debt.
The bank requires a cosigner to ensure the loan is repaid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guarantor - a person who gives a guarantee, especially for a loan

Etymology

Formed from 'co-' (together) and 'signer' (one who signs).

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈsaɪnər / (AmE), / kəʊˈsaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who signs a document jointly with another, often to guarantee payment or performance if the primary signer fails to do so
joint signer of a document

Examples

The bank required a cosigner before approving his student loan application.
Her father agreed to be a cosigner on her apartment lease to help her get approved.
Without a cosigner, it can be difficult to qualify for certain types of credit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guarantor - person who guarantees something, especially a loan

Etymology

From co- (joint, together) + signer, from Latin signāre (to sign).
