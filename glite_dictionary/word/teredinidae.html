Teredinidae

1. NOUN

/ təˌrɛdəˈnɪdiː / (AmE), / təˌriːdɪˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine mollusks known as shipworms that bore into and destroy submerged wood, such as piers and ships.
family of shipworm mollusks
medical

Examples

Marine engineers are concerned about the destructive effects of Teredinidae on wooden structures.
The Teredinidae family includes several notorious species that bore into ships and piers.
Researchers studied the wood-boring habits of Teredinidae in tropical harbors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipworm - Common name for species in the Teredinidae family, wood-boring mollusk

Etymology

From New Latin Teredinidae, from Teredo (the type genus) + -idae (a zoological family suffix).
