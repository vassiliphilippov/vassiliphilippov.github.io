confirmatory

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfɜːrməˌtɔri / (AmE), / kənˈfɜːmətəri / (BrE)
serving to confirm, support, or corroborate something as true or valid
serving to confirm or corroborate

Examples

The judge required confirmatory evidence before making a ruling.
They ordered a confirmatory test to verify the diagnosis.
His report offered confirmatory data that supported the scientists' hypothesis.

Synset Examples

collateral evidence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corroborative - supporting or confirming with evidence
  • Antonym: refutatory - serving to refute or disprove

Etymology

From Late Latin confirmatorius, from confirmare (to make firm, strengthen).
