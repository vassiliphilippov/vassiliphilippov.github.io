Atlantic

1. PROPN

/ ətˈlæntɪk / (AmE), / ətˈlæntɪk / (BrE)
the world's second largest ocean, lying between the Americas to the west and Europe and Africa to the east
the second largest ocean, between America, Europe, and Africa

Examples

Ships often cross the Atlantic from Europe to North America.
The storm originated somewhere over the Atlantic last week.
He spent months sailing solo across the Atlantic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Pacific - the largest and deepest ocean, located between Asia/Australia and the Americas

Etymology

From Greek Atlantis, meaning 'island of Atlas.'

2. ADJ

/ ətˈlæntɪk / (AmE), / ətˈlæntɪk / (BrE)
relating to or bordering the Atlantic Ocean
relating to the Atlantic Ocean

Examples

The Atlantic coastline is known for its scenic beauty and rugged cliffs.
Many storms originate over the Atlantic Ocean during hurricane season.
Several countries have important Atlantic ports used for international trade.

Synset Examples

Atlantic currents

Etymology

From Latin 'Atlanticus', from 'Atlas', a mythological figure thought to support the heavens.
