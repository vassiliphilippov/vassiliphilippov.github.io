Camelus bactrianus

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmiːləs bækˈtraɪənəs / (AmE), / kəˈmiːləs bækˈtraɪənəs / (BrE)
A two-humped camel native to the cold deserts of central Asia, known as the Bactrian camel.
two-humped camel of central Asia

Examples

The Camelus bactrianus is well-adapted to survive in the harsh climates of Central Asian deserts.
Scientists studied the dietary habits of Camelus bactrianus to learn how it copes with extreme temperatures.
A caravan of Camelus bactrianus made its way slowly across the snow-covered steppes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bactrian camel - common name for Camelus bactrianus
  • Degree Lower: camel - large, long-necked mammal adapted to desert life
  • Antonym: Camelus dromedarius - single-humped camel (dromedary)

Etymology

From Latin 'Camelus' (camel) and 'Bactrianus' (of Bactria, an ancient region in central Asia).
