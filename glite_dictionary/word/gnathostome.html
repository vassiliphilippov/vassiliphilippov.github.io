gnathostome

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæθəˌstoʊm / (AmE), / ˈnæθəstəʊm / (BrE)
a vertebrate animal possessing true jaws, belonging to the group Gnathostomata
jawed vertebrate animal

Examples

Sharks are among the earliest known gnathostomes in the fossil record.
All modern mammals, birds, reptiles, and most fish are classified as gnathostomes.
The evolution of jaws in early gnathostomes represented a major step in vertebrate history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agnathan - jawless vertebrate animal

Etymology

From Greek 'gnathos' (jaw) + 'stoma' (mouth).
