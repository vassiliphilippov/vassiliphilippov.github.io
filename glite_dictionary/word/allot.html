to allot

1. VERB

/ əˈlɑt / (AmE), / əˈlɒt / (BrE)
to give a share of something for a particular purpose or to a particular person or group
to assign or distribute (something)

Examples

Each team was allotted five minutes to present their ideas.
The government will allot funds to rebuild the schools damaged by the storm.
She allotted specific tasks to each member of the group.

Synset Examples

grant a privilege

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to allocate - to distribute or assign resources or duties for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Old French *aloter*, from *a-* (to) + *loter* (to divide by lots).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02260076-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈlɑt / (AmE), / əˈlɒt / (BrE)
to give a share of something to someone or to assign resources, duties, or time to people or things
give or assign a share

Examples

Each student was allotted a seat number before the exam began.
The committee will allot funds to different projects based on their needs.
They allotted only ten minutes to each speaker at the conference.

Synset Examples

We were assigned new uniforms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to allocate - to distribute resources or duties for a specific purpose
  • Synonym: to assign - to give someone a particular task or share
  • Antonym: to withhold - to refuse to give something that is due or desired

Etymology

From Old French 'aloter', from 'a-' (to) + 'loter' (to divide by lots).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02233480-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈlɑt / (AmE), / əˈlɒt / (BrE)
to give or assign a share or portion of something to someone or something, especially by dividing or distributing according to a plan
to assign or give a portion

Examples

The teacher decided to allot extra time for the final project.
Each group will be allotted a different section of the report to complete.
We must carefully allot our resources to ensure everyone has enough.

  • Synonym: to assign - to give someone a particular task or responsibility
  • Antonym: to withhold - to refuse to give something that is due or desired by another

Etymology

From Old French 'aloter', from 'a-' (to) + 'loter' (to divide by lots).
