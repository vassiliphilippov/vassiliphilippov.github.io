moiety

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔɪ.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈmɔɪ.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
each of two approximately equal parts into which something can be divided.
one of two equal (or nearly equal) parts

Examples

Under the agreement, each partner received a moiety of the profits.
In medieval times, a kingdom might be divided into two moieties for administrative purposes.
He donated a moiety of his estate to charity.

Etymology

From Old French 'moitié', from Latin 'medietas' meaning 'middle' or 'half'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɔɪ.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˈmɔɪ.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
either of two social or kinship groups into which a tribe is divided, especially in some Indigenous cultures.
one of two subdivisions of a tribe

Examples

The tribe was traditionally divided into two moieties, each with its own totems and customs.
Members of the Eagle moiety were required to marry members of the Wolf moiety.
Anthropologists studied how each moiety played a distinct role in ceremonial life.

Etymology

From Old French *moitié* (half), from Latin *medietas* (half, middle).
