to screak

1. VERB

/ skɹik / (AmE), / skɹiːk / (BrE)
to make or utter a harsh, abrupt, and high-pitched sound; to scream in a shrill manner
emit a harsh, abrupt, shrill sound

Examples

The rusty gate began to screak every time the wind blew against it.
She screaked in surprise when the cold water splashed on her back.
In the silent night, a distant owl screaked from the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screech - give a loud, harsh, piercing cry

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound, related to 'screech'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01050811-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ skrik / (AmE), / skriːk / (BrE)
to make a high-pitched, harsh, screeching noise
emit a high-pitched, screeching sound

Examples

We could hear the rusty gate screak as the wind blew it back and forth.
Her old bicycle would screak loudly every time she hit the brakes.
The hinges of the cellar door screaked open, echoing through the empty house.

Synset Examples

The door creaked when I opened it slowly
My car engine makes a whining noise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screech - to make a loud, harsh, piercing cry or sound
  • Synonym: creak - to make a prolonged, squeaking or grating sound

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, akin to "screech" and "creak".

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02176108-v
Synset POS: v
