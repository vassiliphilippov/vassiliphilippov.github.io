somesthesia

1. NOUN

/ səˈmiːsθiə / (AmE), / səʊˈmiːsθiə / (BrE)
the faculty of perceiving bodily sensations such as touch, position, and internal organ feelings
sense of bodily sensation
medical

Examples

The patient had impaired somesthesia after the stroke, making it hard to detect touch on her skin.
Advanced neurologists may assess somesthesia to evaluate nerve function and response to stimuli.
Pilots sometimes rely on their somesthesia when instruments are unavailable or malfunctioning.

Synset Examples

he relied on somesthesia to warn him of pressure changes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proprioception - the sense of the position and movement of one's own body parts
  • Synonym: tactile sense - the sense of touch

Etymology

From Greek 'sōma' (body) + 'aisthēsis' (perception).

2. NOUN

/ soʊmɛsˈθiːʒə / (AmE), / səʊmɛsˈθiːzɪə / (BrE)
the faculty by which bodily sensations, such as touch, pressure, pain, temperature, and position, are perceived
sense of bodily perception
medical

Examples

Damage to the nervous system can disrupt somesthesia and lead to abnormal sensations.
The doctor's research focused on disorders affecting somesthesia in elderly patients.
Yoga can increase your awareness of somesthesia through mindful movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proprioception - the sense of the relative position of one's own body parts

Etymology

From Greek sōma (body) + aisthēsis (perception).
