toxemia

1. NOUN

/ tɑkˈsiːmiə / (AmE), / tɒkˈsiːmiə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which toxins produced by bacteria enter the bloodstream, leading to blood poisoning.
blood poisoning by bacterial toxins
medical

Examples

The patient developed toxemia following a severe bacterial infection.
Doctors monitored her closely for any signs of toxemia during her recovery.
Early recognition of toxemia is vital for effective treatment and patient survival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sepsis - a serious medical condition caused by the body's response to infection in the bloodstream

Etymology

From Greek 'toxikon' (poison) + 'haima' (blood); originally modern Latin 'toxaemia'.

2. NOUN

/ tɑkˈsimiə / (AmE), / tɒkˈsiːmiə / (BrE)
An abnormal medical condition occurring during pregnancy, characterized by high blood pressure, swelling, and protein in the urine.
pregnancy condition with high blood pressure, swelling, and protein in urine
medical

Examples

The doctor closely monitored her for signs of toxemia during the last trimester of pregnancy.
Swelling and high blood pressure can be early indicators of toxemia.
Untreated toxemia can pose risks to both the mother and the baby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preeclampsia - A more common term for the same pregnancy complication as toxemia

Etymology

From Greek ‘toxikon’ (poisoned arrow) + ‘-emia’ (condition of the blood).
