Levisticum

1. NOUN

/ lɛˈvɪstɪkəm / (AmE), / lɪˈvɪstɪkəm / (BrE)
a genus of aromatic European herbs with umbels of yellow flowers, including the lovage plant
genus of aromatic European herbs

Examples

Levisticum is commonly grown in herb gardens for its flavorful leaves and seeds.
Botanists have identified several medicinal uses for plants in the Levisticum genus.
The best-known species in the Levisticum genus is Levisticum officinale, also called lovage.

Etymology

From Latin 'levisticum', referring to lovage.

2. NOUN

/ lɛˈvɪstɪkəm / (AmE), / lɪˈvɪstɪkəm / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic European herbs in the family Apiaceae, known for yellow flowers, including the lovage plant.
genus of aromatic European herbs

Examples

Levisticum is commonly found growing wild in temperate regions of Europe.
The garden was filled with aromatic herbs, including several varieties of Levisticum.
Botanists study Levisticum for its culinary and medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lovage - an aromatic herb, Levisticum officinale, used in cooking

Etymology

From Latin 'levisticum', the classical name for lovage.
