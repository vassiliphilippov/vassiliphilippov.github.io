Dendrolagus

1. NOUN

/ dɛnˈdroʊˌleɪɡəs / (AmE), / dɛnˈdrɒləɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of marsupials known as tree kangaroos, native to New Guinea, Australia, and nearby islands, characterized by their arboreal lifestyle.
genus of tree-dwelling kangaroos

Examples

Researchers in Papua New Guinea recently discovered a previously unknown species of Dendrolagus.
Unlike ground-dwelling kangaroos, Dendrolagus species have strong forelimbs for climbing trees.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitats of Dendrolagus populations threatened by deforestation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree kangaroo - common name for species of Dendrolagus, arboreal marsupials from Australasia

Etymology

From Greek dendron (tree) + lagos (hare).

2. NOUN

/ dɛnˌdroʊˈleɪɡəs / (AmE), / dɛnˌdrəʊˈleɪɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of marsupials known as tree-kangaroos, native to New Guinea, northeastern Australia, and nearby islands; members of this genus are adapted for arboreal living.
genus of tree-kangaroos

Examples

The Dendrolagus genus includes several species of tree-dwelling kangaroos native to Australasia.
Researchers observed a Dendrolagus carefully climbing among the dense rainforest branches.
Conservationists are working to protect habitats vital for endangered Dendrolagus populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree-kangaroo - a marsupial of the genus Dendrolagus adapted for life in trees

Etymology

From Greek dendron (tree) + lagos (hare, rabbit).
