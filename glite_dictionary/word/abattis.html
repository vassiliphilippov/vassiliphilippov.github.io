abattis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæb.əˈtiː / (AmE), / ˈæb.ə.tiː / (BrE)
A defense barrier made of felled or live trees with sharpened branches or barbed wire pointing outward toward the enemy.
barrier of felled trees for defense

Examples

The soldiers constructed an abattis along the ridge to slow the advancing troops.
During the siege, defenders relied on a thick abattis to protect their encampment.
Engineers reinforced the abattis with additional barbed wire to strengthen the defensive line.

Etymology

From French 'abattis', from 'abattre' meaning 'to fell'.
