deuce

1. NOUN

/ dus / (AmE), / djuːs / (BrE)
a playing card with two spots, one of the four cards in a deck with that value
playing card with two spots

Examples

He drew a deuce of hearts from the deck.
If you bet on a deuce, you're hoping for a card with two spots.
The deuce is considered a low card in many games.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: two - playing card with two spots

Etymology

From Old French deus, from Latin duo (two).

4. NOUN

/ dus / (AmE), / djuːs / (BrE)
(in tennis or table tennis) a score when both players have 40 points or the same score in a game, making it necessary to win two consecutive points to win the game.
tie score in tennis or table tennis requiring two-point win

Examples

After a long rally, the score returned to deuce once again.
If you reach deuce in tennis, you must win two points in a row to win the game.
The match had several intense moments when the score was at deuce.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: advantage - when a player has won the point after deuce and only needs one more point to win the game

Etymology

From French 'deux' meaning 'two', referring to needing two consecutive points to win after a tie at 40-all.
