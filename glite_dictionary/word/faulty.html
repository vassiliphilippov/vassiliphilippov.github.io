faulty

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔlti / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlti / (BrE)
having faults or defects; not working or made correctly
having a defect; not working properly

Examples

The technician quickly identified the faulty wire in the system.
We had to recall the product due to several faulty parts.
She complained about the faulty brakes on her new bike.

Synset Examples

I returned the appliance because it was defective

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defective - having a fault or defect
  • Synonym: broken - not functioning; damaged
  • Antonym: perfect - without any flaws or defects

Etymology

From Old French 'faute' (fault) and Middle English 'fault', influenced by 'fault.'

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɔlti / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlti / (BrE)
containing mistakes or defects; not working correctly or not following rules or standards.
containing errors; defective

Examples

The machine has been recalled because it was found to be faulty and unsafe.
She returned the faulty charger that failed to power her laptop.
His argument was dismissed for being based on faulty reasoning.

Synset Examples

he submitted a faulty report
an incorrect transcription
the wrong side of the road

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defective - having a fault or defect; imperfect
  • Antonym: flawless - having no defects or faults

Etymology

From Old French 'faute' (fault, deficiency).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00023894-s
Synset POS: s
