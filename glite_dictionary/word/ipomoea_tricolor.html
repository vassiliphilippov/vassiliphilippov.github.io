Ipomoea tricolor

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə ˈtraɪ.kəl.ɔɹ / (AmE), / ˌɪ.pəˈmiː.ə ˈtrɪ.kə.lɔː / (BrE)
A species of climbing plant native to Central America, known for its bright sky-blue flowers; commonly cultivated as the morning glory.
species of morning glory plant

Examples

She planted Ipomoea tricolor along the garden fence for its striking blue blooms.
The vibrant flowers of Ipomoea tricolor open each morning and close by afternoon.
Many gardeners favor Ipomoea tricolor for its rapid growth and beautiful appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morning glory - common name for several flowering plants in the genus Ipomoea

Etymology

From Latinized Greek: 'Ipomoea' from Greek 'ips' (worm) and 'homoios' (similar), referring to its twining habit; 'tricolor' meaning 'three-colored' in Latin.
