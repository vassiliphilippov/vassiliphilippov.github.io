eugenic

1. ADJ

/ juˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / juːˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or causing the improvement of the genetic quality of offspring.
promoting genetic improvement of offspring

Examples

Some breeding programs are designed to have a strictly eugenic effect on animal populations.
In the early twentieth century, several governments adopted eugenic policies that are now widely condemned.
The scientist argued that eugenic interventions could improve future generations' health outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dysgenic - causing a deterioration of genetic quality

Etymology

From Greek eugenēs (well-born), from eu (good) + genos (birth, kind).
