fretty

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrɛti / (AmE), / ˈfrɛti / (BrE)
feeling anxious or fretful; showing unnecessary worry or concern
anxious, fretful

Examples

She became increasingly fretty about the possibility of missing her train.
It's normal to feel a bit fretty before a big exam, but try to relax.
His fretty demeanor made others feel uneasy during the meeting.

Synset Examples

Being fretty often leads to unnecessary stress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxious - experiencing worry or unease
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or anxiety

Etymology

Related to 'fret', meaning to worry, from Old English 'fretan' (to devour, eat up).
