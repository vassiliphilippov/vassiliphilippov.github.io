to dwindle

1. VERB

/ ˈdwɪndl̩ / (AmE), / ˈdwɪndl̩ / (BrE)
to gradually become smaller, weaker, or less in number or amount
become gradually smaller or less

Examples

As the hours passed, our hopes began to dwindle.
The crowd started large but quickly dwindled as the rain intensified.
Her enthusiasm for the project has dwindled over time.

Synset Examples

Her savings dwindled down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decrease - to become or make less in size, amount, or degree
  • Synonym: to diminish - to make or become less
  • Antonym: increase - to become or make greater in size, amount, or degree

Etymology

Perhaps from Old English *dwīnan* (to waste away) + diminutive suffix -le.
