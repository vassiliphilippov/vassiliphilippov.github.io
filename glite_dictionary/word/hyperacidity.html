hyperacidity

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərəˈsɪdəti / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpərəˈsɪdəti / (BrE)
An excessive level of acidity, especially in the stomach.
excessive acidity, esp. in the stomach
medical

Examples

The patient complained of severe hyperacidity after eating spicy food.
Frequent episodes of hyperacidity can lead to discomfort and heartburn.
Doctors may prescribe antacids to treat symptoms caused by hyperacidity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: acidity - the quality or state of being acid

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (over, excessive) + 'acidity'.
