double-crosser

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbəlˌkrɑsər / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəlˈkrɒsə / (BrE)
a person who betrays someone by pretending loyalty but acting against them; a betrayer or deceiver
person who betrays or deceives

Examples

No one wants to be friends with a double-crosser who cannot be trusted.
The spy was revealed as a double-crosser after selling secrets to the enemy.
He always seemed loyal, but turned out to be a double-crosser when it mattered most.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betrayer - a person who betrays another
  • Synonym: traitor - a person who is not loyal or is treacherous

Etymology

Formed from the phrasal verb 'double-cross,' meaning to betray, with 'double' implying duplicity and 'cross' suggesting a crossing or betrayal.
