to embrocate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛm.broʊ.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛm.brəʊ.keɪt / (BrE)
to apply oil or ointment to a part of the body, especially for healing or as part of a religious ceremony
apply oil or ointment to the body

Examples

The nurse gently embrocated his aching shoulder with a soothing balm.
During the ceremony, the priest embrocated the child as part of the ritual.
She was instructed to embrocate the injured area twice a day with special oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anoint - to smear or rub with oil, especially as part of a religious ceremony

Etymology

From Latin 'embrocare' (to moisten), from Greek 'embrochē' (a lotion).
