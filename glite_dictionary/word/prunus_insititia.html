Prunus insititia

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs ɪnˈsɪtiə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs ɪnˈsɪtiə / (BrE)
A species of small wild or semi-domesticated Eurasian plum bearing small, oval fruit in clusters, commonly known as the damson.
species of Eurasian plum (damson)

Examples

The jam was made from the fruit of the Prunus insititia tree growing in their orchard.
Botanists often study Prunus insititia due to its hardiness compared to other plums.
The small, tart plums of Prunus insititia are prized for making preserves.

Etymology

From Latin: 'prunus' (plum) and 'insititia' (grafted, planted).
