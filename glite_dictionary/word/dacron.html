Dacron

1. NOUN

/ ˈdækrɑn / (AmE), / ˈdak.rɒn / (BrE)
A trademarked type of strong, wrinkle-resistant polyester fiber, used especially in making fabrics.
a trademarked polyester fiber used in fabrics

Examples

The tent was made of Dacron fabric, which is both durable and lightweight.
Many sails today are constructed from Dacron because of its resistance to stretching.
He wore a blend of cotton and Dacron to stay cool and comfortable on hot days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyester - a synthetic resin used to make fibers and fabrics

Etymology

Trade name, coined by the textile company DuPont in the 1950s.
