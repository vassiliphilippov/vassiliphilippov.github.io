to reflect

1. VERB

/ rɪˈflɛkt / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛkt / (BrE)
to think deeply or carefully about something, especially when considering or analyzing it
think deeply or carefully

Examples

After the meeting, she took time to reflect on what had been discussed.
He likes to reflect before making important decisions.
As I listened to the old song, I couldn't help but reflect on my childhood.

Synset Examples

I mulled over the events of the afternoon
philosophers have speculated on the question of God for thousands of years
The scientist must stop to observe and start to excogitate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ponder - to think about something carefully, especially before making a decision
  • Synonym: to contemplate - to look thoughtfully for a long time at; to think deeply about something
  • Synonym: to speculate - to form a theory or conjecture about a subject without firm evidence

Etymology

From Latin reflectere, meaning 'to bend back'; later extended to mental turning back or deep consideration.
