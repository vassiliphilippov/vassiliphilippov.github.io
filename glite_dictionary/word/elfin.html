elfin

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛl.fɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛl.fɪn / (BrE)
small and delicate, typically in a charming, fairy-like way.
small and delicate; fairy-like

Examples

She moved with an elfin grace that enchanted the audience.
The garden was filled with elfin flowers that looked almost magical in the morning light.
His elfin features made him stand out in the crowded room.

Synset Examples

she was an elfin creature — graceful and delicate
obsessed by things elfin and small

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairy-like - having qualities or appearance of a fairy; delicate and magical

Etymology

From 'elf' + '-in', used poetically to describe things resembling elves; earliest usage in 18th century English.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛlfɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛlfɪn / (BrE)
Playfully mischievous, usually in a good-natured and charming way; resembling a fairy or elf in spirit or behavior.
playfully mischievous; fairy-like

Examples

She gave him an elfin smile as she handed back the keys.
His elfin humor kept the children laughing all afternoon.
The actor's elfin charm made him perfect for the whimsical role.

Synset Examples

perpetrated a practical joke with elfin delight
elvish tricks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impish - playfully mischievous in a way that is similar to 'elfin'
  • Synonym: mischievous - causing or showing a fondness for causing trouble in a playful way

Etymology

From elf + -in (diminutive suffix), modeled on similar Germanic forms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02129841-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈɛl.fɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛl.fɪn / (BrE)
Strange and otherworldly in a way that is reminiscent of an elf; charmingly mysterious or delicate.
suggestive of an elf; strange and otherworldly

Examples

There was an elfin glow to her smile that intrigued everyone in the room.
The forest was cloaked in an elfin mist, making it feel truly enchanted.
His elfin features gave him a mysterious and captivating appearance.

Synset Examples

thunderbolts quivered with elfin flares of heat lightning
the fey quality was there, the ability to see the moon at midday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fey - having an otherworldly, magical, or fairy-like quality
  • Synonym: fairy-like - resembling or characteristic of a fairy; delicate or magical

Etymology

From elf + -in, patterned after similar formations like sylph/sylphin.

Source: WordNet
