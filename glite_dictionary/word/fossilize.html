to fossilize

1. VERB

/ ˈfɑː.sə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɒs.ɪ.laɪz / (BrE)
to change or be changed into a fossil by natural processes over a long period
become or make into a fossil

Examples

Over millions of years, dinosaur bones can fossilize under layers of sediment.
The plant material did not fossilize because conditions were not ideal.
Scientists believe the footprints fossilized quickly due to the volcanic ash.

Synset Examples

The little animals fossilized and are now embedded in the limestone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preserve - to keep something in its original state or in good condition

Etymology

From French *fossiliser*, from Latin *fossilis* (dug up).

2. VERB

/ ˈfɑːsəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɒsɪlʌɪz / (BrE)
(of a person, attitude, or institution) to become rigid or inflexible in thinking, unable to adapt or change.
become rigid or inflexible mentally

Examples

Over time, some leaders fossilize and resist any suggestion of reform.
If your views fossilize, it's difficult to accept new ideas or perspectives.
The education system risks becoming outdated if policies fossilize and are never reviewed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ossify - to become rigid or inflexible in habits or opinions

Etymology

From fossil + -ize, meaning to turn into a fossil; figurative use from the idea of becoming hardened and unchanging.

3. VERB

/ ˈfɑːsəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɒsɪlaɪz / (BrE)
(of ideas, attitudes, or thinking) to become outdated, rigid, or incapable of change.
to become outdated or inflexible (of ideas)

Examples

Over time, some scholars fossilize in their thinking and resist any new theories.
If leaders fossilize in their approaches, organizations can quickly lose their edge.
She cautioned against allowing our policies to fossilize and hinder innovation.

Synset Examples

They fear that traditional practices may fossilize in modern society.
If you don't adapt, you'll fossilize in your thinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ossify - to become rigid or fixed in position or attitude; to harden

Etymology

From fossil + -ize, modeled on fossilization (related to the process of turning organic material into fossils).
