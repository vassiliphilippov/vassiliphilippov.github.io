accolade

1. NOUN

/ ˈækəˌleɪd / (AmE), / ˈækəleɪd / (BrE)
An award or tangible symbol given as a mark of approval, honor, or distinction.
award or symbol of distinction

Examples

Receiving the highest accolade from the academy was the highlight of her career.
The scientist was presented with an accolade in recognition of his groundbreaking research.
This prestigious accolade is awarded annually to an artist who shows extraordinary creativity.

Synset Examples

an award for bravery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: award - A prize or other mark of recognition given for achievement
  • Synonym: honor - A mark of distinction or recognition
  • Antonym: disgrace - Loss of reputation or respect

Etymology

From French 'accolade', from Provençal 'acolada', originally meaning the ceremonial embrace at knighthood.
