Kachinic

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætʃɪˈnɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatʃɪˈnɪk / (BrE)
a subgroup of Tibeto-Burman languages spoken in northern Myanmar (Burma), and parts of China and India
subgroup of Tibeto-Burman languages

Examples

The Kachinic languages are spoken by many communities in northern Myanmar.
Researchers are documenting the grammar of several Kachinic dialects.
Linguists classify Jinghpaw as part of the Kachinic subgroup.

Etymology

From Kachin, the name of an ethnic group in northern Myanmar (Burma), plus the suffix -ic, meaning 'related to'.
