chive

1. NOUN

/ tʃaɪv / (AmE), / tʃaɪv / (BrE)
A small perennial herb (Allium schoenoprasum) with hollow, cylindrical leaves used as a seasoning in cooking.
herb used for seasoning

Examples

She sprinkled some fresh chive on the soup for extra flavor.
The recipe calls for chopped chive as a garnish.
Growing chive in the garden is easy and rewarding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallion - a type of long, thin onion with a mild flavor
  • Synonym: garlic - bulbous plant with a strong taste and smell, related to chives
  • Synonym: onion - common bulb vegetable, related to chives

Etymology

From Old French 'cive', from Latin 'cepula', diminutive of 'cepa' (onion).
