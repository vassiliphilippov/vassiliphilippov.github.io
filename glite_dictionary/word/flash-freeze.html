to flash-freeze

1. VERB

/ flæʃˈfriz / (AmE), / flæʃˈfriːz / (BrE)
to freeze food or another item very rapidly, typically to preserve its quality, flavor, and texture
to freeze very quickly to preserve quality

Examples

Fishermen often flash-freeze their catch right on the boat to maintain freshness.
Vegetables are usually flash-frozen soon after harvest for optimal nutrition.
The chef decided to flash-freeze the berries for use in desserts later.

Synset Examples

quick-freeze the shrimp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to quick-freeze - to freeze food quickly to preserve quality
