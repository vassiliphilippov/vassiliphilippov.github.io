carboxyl

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈbɑksəl / (AmE), / kɑːˈbɒksɪl / (BrE)
the univalent group –COOH, present in and characteristic of organic acids
the –COOH group in organic acids
medical

Examples

A carboxyl group is responsible for the acidic properties of many organic compounds.
Fatty acids all contain at least one carboxyl group in their structure.
The presence of a carboxyl group classifies a molecule as a carboxylic acid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carboxylic acid - organic acid containing one or more carboxyl groups

Etymology

From French 'carboxyle', from 'carb(o)-' (carbon) + 'ox(y)' (oxygen) + '-yl' (chemical suffix).

2. ADJ

/ kɑrˈbɑksɪl / (AmE), / kɑːˈbɒksɪl / (BrE)
relating to or containing the carboxyl group (–COOH) or carboxyl radical in its structure.
of or containing a carboxyl group
medical

Examples

A carboxyl group consists of a carbon atom double-bonded to an oxygen atom and single-bonded to a hydroxyl group.
Many amino acids have a carboxyl functional group in their structure.
Adding a carboxyl group to the molecule increased its acidity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carboxylic - relating to carboxylic acids, containing the carboxyl group

Etymology

From French *carboxyle*, from *carbone* (carbon) and *oxygène* (oxygen), referring to its components.
