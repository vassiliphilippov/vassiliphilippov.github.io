Borodino

1. PROPN

/ ˌbɔrəˈdiːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˌbɒrəˈdiːnəʊ / (BrE)
A village in western Russia, west of Moscow, known as the site of the Battle of Borodino in 1812, where Napoleon's army defeated the Russians but suffered heavy losses.
historic battle site near Moscow (1812)

Examples

The Battle of Borodino marked a turning point in Napoleon's Russian campaign.
Many historians consider Borodino to be one of the bloodiest single-day battles in history.
Tolstoy depicted Borodino vividly in his novel 'War and Peace'.
