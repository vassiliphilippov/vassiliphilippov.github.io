misappropriation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪs.əˌproʊ.priˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.əˌprəʊ.priˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of dishonestly or wrongfully taking or using money or property that has been entrusted to one, especially for personal use
wrongful use of entrusted property or funds
legal

Examples

The accountant was charged with misappropriation of company funds.
New regulations aim to reduce the misappropriation of public resources.
Evidence of misappropriation led to an internal investigation at the charity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embezzlement - theft or misappropriation of funds placed in one's trust

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + appropriation, from Latin appropriare 'to make one's own'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪs.əˌproʊ.priˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.əˌprəʊ.priˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of dishonestly or wrongfully taking something, especially money or ideas, for one's own use
wrongful or dishonest taking of something

Examples

The auditor discovered the misappropriation of company funds during the annual review.
Her article was a clear misappropriation of data from previous research.
He was accused of misappropriation after spending public money on personal expenses.

Synset Examples

his explanation was a misappropriation of sociological theory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embezzlement - the act of stealing money one is trusted with
  • Synonym: appropriation - the act of taking something for one's own use, often without permission
  • Antonym: donation - the act of giving something freely

Etymology

From late Latin misappropriatus, past participle of misappropriare (to wrongfully allocate).
