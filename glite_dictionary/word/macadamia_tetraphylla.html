Macadamia tetraphylla

1. NOUN

/ məkəˈdeɪmiə tɛtəˈfaɪlə / (AmE), / məkəˈdeɪmɪə tɛtəˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A species of macadamia tree native to eastern Australia, characterized by bushy growth and pink to purple blossoms, cultivated for its edible nuts.
a species of macadamia nut tree

Examples

The plantation recently added hundreds of Macadamia tetraphylla trees to increase nut production.
Unlike its relative M. integrifolia, Macadamia tetraphylla produces nuts with a slightly rougher shell.
Gardeners admire the ornamental value of Macadamia tetraphylla for its beautiful pinkish flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macadamia - nut-bearing tree of the genus Macadamia

Etymology

Genus name from John Macadam, a Scottish-Australian chemist; species from Greek 'tetra-' (four) and 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to its four-parted leaves.
