alkaline

1. ADJ

/ ˈæl.kəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.laɪn / (BrE)
having the properties of or containing an alkali; having a pH greater than 7.
having a pH greater than 7

Examples

The water in this lake is alkaline and not safe to drink without treatment.
Certain plants thrive better in alkaline soil conditions.
Household cleaning products often contain alkaline substances to break down grease.

Synset Examples

alkaline soils derived from chalk or limestone

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acidic - having a pH less than 7; containing acid

Etymology

From French 'alcalin', from 'alcali', ultimately from Arabic 'al-qaly' (ashes of saltwort).
