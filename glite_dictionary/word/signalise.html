to signalise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪɡnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to provide (a road, intersection, etc.) with traffic signals
provide with traffic signals
british

Examples

The city decided to signalise the dangerous intersection to improve safety.
Plans are underway to signalise the routes leading to the new shopping centre.
After complaints from drivers, the council voted to signalise the busy roundabout.

Synset Examples

signalize a busy intersection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to signalize - to provide with traffic signals (US spelling)

Etymology

From signal + -ise, chiefly British spelling of 'signalize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02301945-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪɡnəlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something conspicuous or significant; to draw special attention to something
make something conspicuous or notable
british

Examples

The organization decided to signalise the anniversary with a special celebration.
He hoped his generous donation would signalise his commitment to the cause.
The award signalised her achievements in the field of science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to signalize - make conspicuous or noteworthy; US spelling
  • Synonym: highlight - to emphasize or make prominent
  • Antonym: obscure - to make less visible or noticeable

Etymology

From Latin 'signalis', from 'signum' (sign), related to the word 'signal'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00924409-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsɪɡ.nə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.nə.laɪz / (BrE)
to communicate or indicate something by means of signals or signs, especially silently and non-verbally
communicate by signals or signs

Examples

She signalised her agreement with a subtle nod.
The students signalised to the teacher that they needed help without speaking.
He signalises when to stop by raising his hand.

Synset Examples

He signed his disapproval with a dismissive hand gesture
The diner signaled the waiters to bring the menu

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal - to communicate information by making a signal or gesture
  • Synonym: gesture - to use bodily movements to express or emphasize something

Etymology

From signal + -ise, modeled on the French 'signaliser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01041202-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈsɪɡnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to point out or make something clearly noticeable or prominent; to indicate with emphasis
make clearly noticeable or prominent

Examples

Her courage was signalised in the official report for all to see.
The author signalises the theme of loss repeatedly throughout the novel.
It is important to signalise the main changes in the policy document.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highlight - to emphasize or make prominent
  • Antonym: obscure - to make unclear or hide from view

Etymology

From French 'signaliser', from 'signal' (sign); related to Latin 'signum' (mark, sign).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
