humanisation

1. NOUN

/ hjuːˌmænəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / hjuːˌmanɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something more human in character or nature
the act of making more human

Examples

The humanisation of healthcare has become a priority in modern hospitals.
Many animated films rely on the humanisation of animals to create relatable characters.
Efforts toward the humanisation of technology focus on making devices more intuitive and user-friendly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dehumanisation - the process of depriving of human qualities
  • Synonym: to humanize - to make something more human

Etymology

From 'humanise' + '-ation', based on Latin 'humanus' (human).
