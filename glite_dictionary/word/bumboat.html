bumboat

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌmˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈbʌmbəʊt / (BrE)
a small boat used to ferry supplies and goods for sale to ships anchored offshore
boat supplying ships at anchor

Examples

The bumboat approached the anchored ship, carrying fresh fruit and other necessities.
Several sailors eagerly waited for the bumboat to arrive with supplies from shore.
She earned her living running a bumboat, selling food and newspapers to the crew.

Etymology

Possibly from Dutch 'boomschuit' (tree boat or canoe), adapted in English nautical slang.
