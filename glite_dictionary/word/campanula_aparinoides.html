Campanula aparinoides

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæmpəˈnuːlə æˌpæraɪˈnoʊɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌkampəˈnjuːlə æˌpærɪˈnəʊɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of bellflower native to marshes in eastern North America, known for its lance-shaped linear leaves and small whitish flowers.
a marsh bellflower of eastern North America

Examples

The rare Campanula aparinoides blooms each summer in the wetlands near the lake.
Botanists discovered a patch of Campanula aparinoides with delicate white flowers.
Due to habitat loss, Campanula aparinoides has become less common in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellflower - any plant of the genus Campanula, with bell-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Campanula (little bell), species name aparinoides, meaning ‘resembling Galium aparine’.
