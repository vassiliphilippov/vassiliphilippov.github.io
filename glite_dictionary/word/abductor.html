abductor

1. NOUN

/ æbˈdʌktər / (AmE), / æbˈdʌktə / (BrE)
a muscle that moves a part of the body away from the median axis or middle line
muscle that moves body part away from the midline
medical

Examples

The abductor muscles of the hip help you move your leg to the side.
After his injury, the patient's abductor was weaker and required physical therapy.
The surgeon repaired damage to the vocal cord abductor to improve breathing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adductor - a muscle that moves a body part toward the median line

Etymology

From Latin abductor, from abducere meaning 'to lead away'.

2. NOUN

/ æbˈdʌktɚ / (AmE), / əbˈdʌktə / (BrE)
A person who unlawfully seizes and detains someone, often for ransom or other demands.
person who kidnaps another unlawfully

Examples

The abductor demanded a large sum of money from the victim's family.
Police caught the abductor just as he was trying to escape.
The child was safely rescued after being held by her abductor for three days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kidnapper - person who seizes and holds a victim for ransom
  • Antonym: victim - person who is harmed or killed by another

Etymology

From Latin 'abducere' meaning 'to lead away'.
