pleonastic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpliːəˈnæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌpliːəˈnæstɪk / (BrE)
(of language) using more words than necessary, especially when the same idea is repeated with different terms; redundant.
using unnecessary words; redundant

Examples

The phrase 'free gift' is a pleonastic expression because 'gift' already implies that it is free.
His writing style is often criticized for being pleonastic and overly wordy.
Avoiding pleonastic phrases can help make your arguments clearer and more effective.

Synset Examples

‘a true fact’ and ‘a free gift’ are pleonastic expressions
the phrase ‘a beginner who has just started’ is tautological
at the risk of being redundant I return to my original proposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tautological - using unnecessary repetition or redundancy in language
  • Synonym: redundant - exceeding what is necessary; repetitive
  • Antonym: concise - giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words

Etymology

From Greek pleonastikos ('excessive, redundant'), from pleonazein ('to be excessive'), based on pleon ('more').
