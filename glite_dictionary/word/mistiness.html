mistiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪstɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪstɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being misty, as caused by haze, mist, or vapor
state of being misty or hazy

Examples

The mistiness of the early morning made it hard to see the path ahead.
Looking out over the lake, a gentle mistiness blurred the distant shoreline.
Photographers often appreciate the soft mistiness that foggy weather brings to a landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloudiness - the state of being cloudy or filled with clouds
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear, transparency or lucidity

Etymology

From 'misty' + '-ness', denoting the quality or state of being misty.
