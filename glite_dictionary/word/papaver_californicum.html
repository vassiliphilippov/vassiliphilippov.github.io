Papaver californicum

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpeɪvər ˌkæləˈfɔrnɪkəm / (AmE), / pəˈpeɪvə ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkəm / (BrE)
A showy annual plant native to California, known for its red flowers.
a California poppy with red flowers

Examples

The vibrant blooms of Papaver californicum add striking color to spring gardens in California.
Botanists documented Papaver californicum growing wild along the coastal hills.
Many gardeners choose to plant Papaver californicum for its remarkable red flowers and easy care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: California poppy - a species of poppy native to California (Eschscholzia californica)

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Papaver' (poppy) and specific epithet 'californicum', referring to California.
