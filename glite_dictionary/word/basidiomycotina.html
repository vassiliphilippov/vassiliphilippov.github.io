Basidiomycotina

1. NOUN

/ bəˌsɪdi.oʊmaɪˈkoʊtɪnə / (AmE), / bəˌsɪdi.əʊmaɪˈkəʊtɪnə / (BrE)
a large subdivision of fungi whose spores develop on a structure called a basidium, including mushrooms, toadstools, puffballs, smuts, and rusts; formerly considered a division of the fungal kingdom in certain classification systems
subdivision of fungi with basidium-born spores
medical

Examples

The Basidiomycotina include many common mushrooms and toadstools found in forests around the world.
Researchers study the Basidiomycotina to learn more about fungal diversity and evolution.
Puffballs, which belong to the Basidiomycotina, are known for releasing spores when compressed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungus - organisms including yeasts, molds, and mushrooms

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'basidium' (spore-bearing structure) and Greek 'mykes' (fungus).

2. NOUN

/ bəˌsɪdioʊmaɪˈkoʊtɪnə / (AmE), / bæsɪdɪˌəʊmaɪˈkəʊtɪnə / (BrE)
A former subdivision of fungi characterized by producing spores on a basidium, including mushrooms, puffballs, smuts, rusts, and bracket fungi.
fungal subdivision with spores on basidia
medical

Examples

Many of the familiar mushrooms belong to the Basidiomycotina.
The classification of Basidiomycotina has changed with advances in molecular biology.
Researchers study Basidiomycotina to better understand fungal diversity.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'basidium' (spore-bearing structure) and Greek 'mykēs' (fungus), with the taxonomic suffix '-ina'.
