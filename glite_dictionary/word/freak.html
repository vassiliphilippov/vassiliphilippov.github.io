freak

1. VERB

/ friːk / (AmE), / friːk / (BrE)
(informal) To react very strongly or lose control of one's emotions, especially because of fear, shock, or anxiety.
to lose control emotionally (informal)
informal

Examples

He started to freak when he realized he had lost his passport at the airport.
She tried to stay calm but began to freak as the deadline approached.
Don’t freak; we’ll find a solution if we work together.

Synset Examples

When he saw the accident, he freaked out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panic - to suddenly feel intense fear or anxiety
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ frik / (AmE), / friːk / (BrE)
A person who is extremely enthusiastic or obsessed with a particular activity, subject, or interest.
person obsessively devoted to something

Examples

He’s a real freak when it comes to mountain biking—he rides almost every day.
As a comic book freak, she owns thousands of rare issues from around the world.
My brother’s always been a fitness freak, spending hours in the gym every week.

Synset Examples

a golf addict
a car nut
a bodybuilding freak
a news junkie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fanatic - A person filled with excessive zeal, especially for an extreme cause or activity.
  • Synonym: addict - A person who is unable to stop doing or using something, especially something harmful.
  • Synonym: junkie - A person with a compulsive habit or obsession, often used informally.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09788499-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ friːk / (AmE), / friːk / (BrE)
a person or animal that is extremely unusual in appearance, behavior, or physical form, sometimes as a result of deformity
unusually abnormal or deformed person or animal

Examples

The child was cruelly called a freak because of his unusual birthmark.
In the 19th century, people with rare medical conditions were sometimes displayed as freaks in traveling shows.
She felt like a freak in high school because she was so much taller than everyone else.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oddity - an unusual or peculiar person or thing
  • Antonym: normal - conforming to a standard; usual, typical, or expected

Etymology

Probably from English dialect 'freak' (sudden change), of uncertain origin.
