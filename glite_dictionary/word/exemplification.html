exemplification

1. NOUN

/ ɪgˌzɛmpləfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪgˌzɛmplɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of showing something by giving an example; a typical example or illustration of something
showing by example; illustration

Examples

Her approach serves as a powerful exemplification of effective leadership in the workplace.
The novel offers a perfect exemplification of the theme of sacrifice.
His speech included the exemplification of principles through real-life case studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illustration - an example that helps make something clear
  • Synonym: demonstration - an act of showing how something is done or proves a point

Etymology

From Latin 'exemplificatio' (illustration by example), from 'exemplificare' (to illustrate by example).

2. NOUN

/ ɪɡˌzɛm.plə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzɛmplɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a person or thing that serves as a typical example or model; the act of illustrating by example
typical example or illustration

Examples

Her research project is a brilliant exemplification of critical thinking and innovative methodology.
The courthouse is an exemplification of modernist architectural design.
His generosity was seen as an exemplification of true community spirit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: example - something that is representative of all such things in a group
  • Synonym: prototype - an original model on which something is patterned
  • Antonym: counterexample - an instance that disproves a generalization

Etymology

From Medieval Latin exemplificatio (illustration by example), from Latin exemplificare (to illustrate by example).
