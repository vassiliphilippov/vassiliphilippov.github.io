Helvellaceae

1. NOUN

/ hɛlˌvɛləˈsiː.i / (AmE), / hɛlˌvɛləˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of fungi known as false morels or lorchels, some of which are edible and others poisonous
family of fungi (false morels/lorchels)

Examples

The Helvellaceae family includes both edible and highly toxic species of mushrooms.
Mycologists studied new species within the Helvellaceae discovered in the forested region.
Some members of the Helvellaceae resemble true morels, making identification important for foragers.

Etymology

From the genus name Helvella, from Latin helvella (an herb, possibly a kind of fungus), with the family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ hɛlˌvɛl.iˈeɪ.siˌi / (AmE), / hɛlˌvɛl.iˈeɪ.siˌi / (BrE)
a family of ascomycete fungi known as false morels or lorchels, including species that are both edible and poisonous
family of fungi including false morels
medical

Examples

The Helvellaceae include species valued by some for their culinary uses.
Research into the Helvellaceae has revealed the presence of toxic compounds in certain varieties.
Mushroom collectors should be cautious with members of the Helvellaceae family due to potential poisoning risks.

Etymology

From genus name Helvella + Latin suffix -aceae (family).
