Rio Grande

1. PROPN

/ ˌrioʊ ˈɡrænd, ˌriːoʊ ˈɡrɑːnd / (AmE), / ˌriːəʊ ˈɡrænd, ˌriːəʊ ˈɡrɑːnd / (BrE)
a large river forming part of the boundary between the United States and Mexico, flowing southeast into the Gulf of Mexico
river forming US-Mexico border

Examples

The Rio Grande serves as a natural border between Texas and Mexico for hundreds of miles.
Many species of birds can be found along the banks of the Rio Grande.
Farmers in New Mexico rely on water from the Rio Grande for irrigation.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Río Grande', meaning 'big river'.
