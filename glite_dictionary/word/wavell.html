Wavell

1. PROPN

/ ˈweɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈweɪvəl / (BrE)
(1883–1950) British field marshal who commanded Allied forces in North Africa during World War II, defeating the Italians before being overcome by German forces.
British field marshal in WWII North Africa

Examples

Field Marshal Wavell played a vital role in the early Allied campaigns in North Africa.
After his victories against the Italians, Wavell was reassigned to the Middle East.
Many historians study Wavell's strategies for insights into World War II leadership.
