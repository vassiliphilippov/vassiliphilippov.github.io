Ligustrum ovalifolium

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈɡʌstrəm oʊˌvæləˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / lɪˈɡʌstrəm əʊˌvælɪˈfəʊlɪəm / (BrE)
A semi-evergreen shrub native to Japan, commonly used for hedges, with malodorous flowers and foliage that tends to stay green year-round.
Japanese shrub often used for hedges

Examples

The dense foliage of Ligustrum ovalifolium makes it a popular choice for garden hedges.
Gardeners often prefer Ligustrum ovalifolium because it stays green through most winters.
The flowers of Ligustrum ovalifolium can have a strong, unpleasant scent in midsummer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privet - any shrub of the genus Ligustrum, often used for hedges

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ligustrum' (privet) and 'ovalifolium' (oval-leaved).
