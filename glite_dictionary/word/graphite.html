graphite

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡræfaɪt / (BrE)
a soft, black, crystalline form of carbon that is used as a lubricant and as a moderator in nuclear reactors, and is commonly found in pencils
a soft, black form of carbon used in pencils and industry

Examples

Most pencils contain a core of graphite for writing and drawing.
The graphite in the reactor acted as a moderator to slow down neutrons.
Applying graphite powder to the lock made the key turn smoothly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon - chemical element with symbol C

Etymology

From Greek *graphein* (to write), referring to its use in writing.
