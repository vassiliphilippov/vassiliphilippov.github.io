to colorize

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌlərˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkʌl.ə.raɪz / (BrE)
to add color to a black-and-white photograph, film, or image, often using special techniques or software
add color to black-and-white images

Examples

Many filmmakers use modern technology to colorize old black-and-white movies.
She learned how to colorize photographs using digital tools for her art project.
The software allows users to quickly colorize scanned hand-drawn sketches.

Synset Examples

The child colored the drawings
Fall colored the trees
colorize black and white film

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color - to give or add color to something
  • Antonym: monochromatize - to render or represent in a single color

Etymology

From 'color' + '-ize', following standard verb formation in English.
