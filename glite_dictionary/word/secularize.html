to secularize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛkjələrˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛkjʊləraɪz / (BrE)
to transfer something from religious or church ownership, use, or control to civil or non-religious authority
transfer from religious to civil control

Examples

The government decided to secularize several church-owned schools during the reform.
Many monasteries were secularized in the eighteenth century, transferring their land to the state.
The movement aimed to secularize health care, taking hospitals out of religious control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nationalize - transfer from private or other ownership to state control

Etymology

From Medieval Latin secularizare, from Latin saecularis (worldly, temporal).

2. VERB

/ ˈsɛkjələˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛkjʊləraɪz / (BrE)
make something non-religious or remove it from religious control or influence
make non-religious; remove religious influence

Examples

The new government set out to secularize the nation's education system.
Many historic churches have been secularized and turned into museums.
Reforms in the country aimed to secularize its laws and institutions.

Synset Examples

Ataturk secularized Turkey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deconsecrate - to remove the sacred character of something, especially a religious building

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'saecularizare', from Latin 'saecularis' (worldly).

Synset POS: v
