frigidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪdʒɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈfrɪdʒɪdnɪs / (BrE)
A lack of warmth, affection, or enthusiasm, especially in behavior or attitude.
absence of warmth or enthusiasm

Examples

The frigidness in her voice made it clear she was upset.
His frigidness toward guests made social gatherings uncomfortable.
A certain frigidness had developed between the old friends after the argument.

Synset Examples

a distressing coldness of tone and manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coldness - lack of warmth or friendliness in manner or feeling
  • Antonym: warmth - friendly or enthusiastic behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'frigidus' meaning 'cold'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪdʒ.ɪd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfrɪdʒ.ɪd.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being very cold; lack of warmth or heat
the state of being very cold

Examples

The frigidness of the wind made it impossible to stay outside for long.
She shivered at the frigidness in the room after the heater broke.
The frigidness of the Arctic night can be life-threatening without proper clothing.

Synset Examples

the coldness made our breath visible
come in out of the cold
cold is a vasoconstrictor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coldness - the state or quality of being cold
  • Antonym: warmth - the quality or state of being warm

Etymology

From Latin 'frigidus' (cold), via 'frigid' + '-ness'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪdʒɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈfrɪdʒɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The condition of being sexually unresponsive or unable to achieve orgasm, especially in women.
sexual unresponsiveness, esp. in women

Examples

The therapist discussed her client's frigidness and its impact on her marriage.
Some older medical texts attribute frigidness to psychological factors rather than physical causes.
Open communication may help couples address issues of frigidness in their relationship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frigidity - sexual unresponsiveness (especially in women)

Etymology

From Latin 'frigidus' (cold) via English 'frigid', plus the noun-forming suffix '-ness'.
