peri

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛri / (AmE), / ˈpɛəri / (BrE)
(in Persian folklore) a supernatural being descended from fallen angels and excluded from paradise until penance is done.
supernatural being in Persian folklore

Examples

Legends tell of the peri longing to return to paradise after centuries of exile.
She was enchanted by a story about a peri who aids a human prince.
In Persian art, the graceful peri is often depicted with wings and flowing robes.

Etymology

From Persian پری (parī, peri), meaning 'fairy' or 'supernatural being'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛri / (AmE), / ˈpɜːri / (BrE)
(in Persian mythology) a mythical being or fairy of great beauty and grace, sometimes used poetically to refer to a beautiful and graceful girl.
beautiful supernatural being or graceful girl

Examples

In ancient tales, a peri was said to bring fortune to those she favored.
Her delicate features and grace made everyone compare her to a peri.
He wrote a poem about a peri who danced in the moonlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairy - a small imaginary being of human form with magical powers

Etymology

From Persian 'peri' (fairy, supernatural being).
