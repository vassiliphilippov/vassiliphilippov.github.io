Cacalia lutea

1. NOUN

/ kəˈkeɪliə ˈluːtiə / (AmE), / kəˈkeɪliə ˈluːtɪə / (BrE)
a tropical African annual plant with scarlet, tassel-shaped flower heads; sometimes formerly placed in the genus Cacalia.
tropical African annual with scarlet tassel-shaped flowers

Examples

The garden featured a rare Cacalia lutea with its distinctive scarlet tassel-shaped blooms.
Some botanists debate whether Cacalia lutea should remain in its current genus.
In tropical African regions, Cacalia lutea is often admired for its vibrant flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin, Cacalia (a former genus of plants) + Latin lutea (yellow).
