Lonicera morrowii

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈnɪsərə məˈroʊ.i.aɪ / (AmE), / ləˈnɪsərə məˈrɒwi.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of deciduous honeysuckle shrub native to Japan, with grey foliage and paired white flowers that turn yellow as they age.
deciduous honeysuckle shrub from Japan

Examples

The garden was overtaken by invasive Lonicera morrowii shrubs crowding out native plants.
Researchers studied the pollination patterns of Lonicera morrowii in its introduced range.
Each spring, the Lonicera morrowii produces fragrant white blossoms that fade to yellow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bush honeysuckle - a common name for several species of deciduous honeysuckle shrubs

Etymology

From the genus name Lonicera, honoring botanist Adam Lonicer, and the species epithet 'morrowii' after American plant collector George Ernest Morrow.
