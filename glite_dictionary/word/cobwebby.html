cobwebby

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑbˌwɛbi / (AmE), / ˈkɒbˌwɛbi / (BrE)
covered with or resembling cobwebs; thin, delicate, and often tangled like a cobweb
covered with or like cobwebs

Examples

The abandoned house had cobwebby corners and dust everywhere.
Her attic was full of cobwebby old furniture and forgotten memories.
A cobwebby veil hung across the window, glinting in the morning light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spidery - thin and delicate like a spider's web

Etymology

Formed from 'cobweb' + '-y', meaning 'like' or 'characteristic of'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑbˌwɛbi / (AmE), / ˈkɒbˌwɛbi / (BrE)
very thin, light, and sometimes almost transparent, like a cobweb; delicate and filmy.
thin and delicate like a cobweb

Examples

The curtains in the old house were cobwebby and let the morning light filter through.
She wore a cobwebby scarf that looked weightless around her shoulders.
A cobwebby layer of dust covered the forgotten mirror in the attic.

Synset Examples

a hat with a diaphanous veil
filmy wings of a moth
gauzy clouds of dandelion down
gossamer cobwebs
sheer silk stockings
transparent chiffon
vaporous silks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diaphanous - very light, thin, and almost transparent
  • Antonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent or translucent

Etymology

From 'cobweb' (a web spun by a spider) + '-y' (adjectival suffix).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
