Telugu

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛlʊɡu / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪɡuː / (BrE)
a Dravidian language spoken chiefly in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana, southeastern India
Dravidian language of southeastern India

Examples

She is fluent in Telugu as well as English.
The film was originally released in Telugu before being dubbed into other languages.
He learned to write poems in Telugu during his childhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kannada - related Dravidian language spoken in southwestern India

Etymology

From Telugu తెలుగూ (Telugu), the native term for the language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛlʊˌgu / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌɡuː / (BrE)
A member of a people living mainly in southeastern India (primarily Andhra Pradesh) who speak the Telugu language.
a person from the Telugu-speaking people of India

Examples

Many Telugu have migrated to cities throughout India for work and education.
Telugu have a rich tradition of classical dance and music in Andhra Pradesh.
As a Telugu, he grew up speaking both Telugu and English at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Telugu - the Dravidian language spoken by the Telugu people

Etymology

From Telugu తెలుగు (Telugu), the self-designation of the people and their language.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɛlɪˌɡu / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪɡuː / (BrE)
A Dravidian language spoken mainly in the Indian states of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana.
Dravidian language of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana

Examples

She is fluent in both Hindi and Telugu.
Telugu is one of the official languages of the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh.
They watched a popular Telugu film together last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kannada - A related Dravidian language spoken in Karnataka, India
  • Synonym: Tamil - A major Dravidian language spoken in southern India and Sri Lanka

Etymology

From Telugu *Telugu*, the native name for the language.
