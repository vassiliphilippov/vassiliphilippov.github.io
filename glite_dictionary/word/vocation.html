vocation

1. NOUN

/ voʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / vəʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
A group or community of people engaged in the same type of profession or occupation.
group of people in the same profession

Examples

The medical vocation has a long tradition of public service.
Teaching is a vocation that attracts people who want to make a difference.
He found companionship in the vocation he shared with other scientists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profession - a paid occupation, especially one that requires advanced education or training

Etymology

From Latin vocatio (a calling), from vocare (to call).

2. NOUN

/ voʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / vəʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a strong feeling of suitability for a particular career or occupation, especially one to which a person is specially drawn or trained
a person's occupation or calling

Examples

After years of study, she finally found her true vocation as a teacher.
He feels that nursing is more than just a job—it's his vocation.
Many people struggle to discover their vocation in life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profession - a paid occupation, especially one that requires advanced education and training
  • Antonym: avocation - a hobby or minor occupation, something done in addition to one's main job

Etymology

From Latin 'vocatio' (a calling), from 'vocare' (to call).
