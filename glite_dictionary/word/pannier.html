pannier

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæniər / (AmE), / ˈpæniə / (BrE)
a large basket, typically one of a pair, carried by an animal such as a horse or donkey, or sometimes by a person, used for transporting goods
large basket carried by animal or person

Examples

The donkey was loaded with heavy panniers filled with vegetables for the market.
She attached the pannier to her bicycle to carry groceries home.
Traders used panniers to transport goods across the mountain paths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basket - a container used to hold or carry things, often made from interwoven materials

Etymology

From Old French *panier* (basket), from Latin *panarium* (bread basket).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæniɚ / (AmE), / ˈpæniə / (BrE)
one of a pair of bags or containers attached to each side of the rear of a bicycle or motorcycle to carry goods
bag or container hung on a bicycle or motorcycle

Examples

He strapped a pannier onto each side of his bicycle before heading out for the trip.
The pannier was filled with groceries after her ride to the market.
Touring cyclists often use panniers to carry their camping equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saddlebag - a bag carried over the back of a saddle, especially on a horse or bicycle

Etymology

From Old French *pannier* (basket), from Latin *panarium* (bread basket).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpæniər / (AmE), / ˈpæniə / (BrE)
A framework of small hoops worn under a skirt to extend and add fullness over the hips, especially in eighteenth-century women's fashion.
framework for expanding skirts at the hips

Examples

The dress included a pannier to enhance its silhouette for the masquerade ball.
In the eighteenth century, wearing a pannier was common among European aristocratic women.
Curators carefully reconstructed the antique gown by adding a replica pannier beneath the skirt.

Etymology

From French 'pannier' (basket), from Latin 'panarium' (bread basket).
