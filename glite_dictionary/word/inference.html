inference

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.fɚ.əns / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fər.əns / (BrE)
the act or process of reaching a conclusion based on evidence and reasoning, rather than from direct observation
drawing a conclusion from evidence

Examples

Based on the evidence found at the scene, the detective made an inference about the suspect's motive.
Her inference from his silence was that he disagreed with the proposal.
Scientific research often relies on inference rather than direct observation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deduction - a conclusion reached by logical reasoning from given premises
  • Degree Lower: speculation - forming a theory or conjecture without firm evidence
  • Synonym: conclusion - a judgement or decision reached after consideration

Etymology

From Latin inferre (to bring in) via Medieval Latin inferentia (conclusion).
