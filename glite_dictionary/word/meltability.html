meltability

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛltəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌmɛltəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being able to be melted, especially when referring to a substance's ease or capacity to turn from solid to liquid, usually when heated.
capacity to be melted

Examples

The meltability of different cheeses varies depending on their fat and moisture content.
Researchers tested the meltability of the new polymer under high temperatures.
For the perfect pizza, you want mozzarella with excellent meltability.

Synset Examples

It was demonstrated that high homogenization pressures resulted in poor stretchability and meltability of the cheese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fusibility - the ability of a material to be fused or melted

Etymology

From 'melt' + '-ability', modeled after words like 'solubility'.
