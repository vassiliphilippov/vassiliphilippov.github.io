Solanum tuberosum

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈleɪnəm tuːbəˈroʊsəm / (AmE), / səʊˈleɪnəm tjuːbəˈrəʊsəm / (BrE)
the scientific name for the potato, a South American plant cultivated widely for its edible starchy tubers
scientific name for potato

Examples

Solanum tuberosum is the botanical name for the plant we commonly call the potato.
Researchers study the genetics of Solanum tuberosum to improve crop yields.
The poisonous vines of Solanum tuberosum contrast with its edible tubers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potato - a starchy tuberous crop, edible root of the plant Solanum tuberosum

Etymology

From Latin: ‘Solanum’ (nightshade genus) and ‘tuberosum’ (tuberous, knobby).
