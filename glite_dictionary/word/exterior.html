exterior

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɪriɚ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
the outer side or surface of something; the outside part or appearance
outer side or surface

Examples

The exterior of the building was recently renovated to give it a modern appearance.
Despite its plain exterior, the house is beautifully decorated inside.
Rain had stained the exterior walls over the years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interior - the inside part or inner surface of something

Etymology

From Latin 'exter' (outward, outside).

Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstɪriɚ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
situated in or suitable for the outside of a building or outdoors
suitable for use outdoors

Examples

The new house features an exterior paint that is resistant to harsh weather.
We need to replace the exterior lights around the garage and driveway.
This chair is made from materials suitable for exterior use.

Synset Examples

an exterior scene
exterior grade plywood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interior - situated inside or relating to the inside of something, especially a building

Etymology

From Latin 'exterior' (comparative of 'ex' meaning 'out of, from').

3. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɪriɚ / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
the outside part or surface of something; the region that is outside of something
outside part; outer surface

Examples

The exterior of the building was renovated last year to improve its appearance.
Despite its plain exterior, the house is beautifully decorated inside.
The car's exterior looked spotless after it was washed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interior - the inside part or inner surface of something

Etymology

From Latin 'exterior' (comparative of 'exter', meaning 'outward').
