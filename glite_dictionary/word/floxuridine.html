floxuridine

1. NOUN

/ floʊkˈsjʊrɪdiːn / (AmE), / flɒkˈsjʊərɪdiːn / (BrE)
an antineoplastic drug used in chemotherapy to treat certain types of cancer, sometimes causing side effects such as hair loss
cancer chemotherapy drug
medical

Examples

The patient was prescribed floxuridine as part of her cancer treatment regimen.
Doctors monitor blood counts closely when administering floxuridine due to its potential side effects.
Hair loss is a common side effect of floxuridine therapy.

Etymology

From 'fluorouracil' (a related drug) + 'uridine' (a nucleoside), indicating its chemical structure.
