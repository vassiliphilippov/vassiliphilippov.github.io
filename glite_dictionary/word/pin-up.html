pin-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈpɪnˌʌp / (BrE)
a person, especially a woman, who is photographed for glamorous or sexually attractive pictures intended for public display
glamorous model in pictures for display

Examples

During World War II, many soldiers kept pin-up posters in their lockers.
The actress became famous after her pin-up photos were published in a popular magazine.
She was once a pin-up girl whose pictures adorned countless bedroom walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - a person employed to display clothes or pose for artists and photographers

Etymology

From the idea of pictures being 'pinned up' on walls; first popularized in the 1940s.
