antelope

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.t̬əˌloʊp / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪ.ləʊp / (BrE)
a graceful, fast-running mammal of the family Bovidae, found mainly in Africa and Asia, with long legs and upward-curving horns
graceful, horned mammal of Africa/Asia

Examples

We spotted a herd of antelope grazing on the plains at sunrise.
The cheetah chased the antelope across the savanna, moving with incredible speed.
Many species of antelope are threatened by habitat loss and poaching.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gazelle - a small, graceful antelope

Etymology

From Greek 'antholops', a word used by Eustathius for a fabulous animal.
