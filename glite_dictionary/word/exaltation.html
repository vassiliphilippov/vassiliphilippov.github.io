exaltation

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˌzɑlˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzɑːlˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a collective noun for a group of larks, especially when they are flying together overhead.
group of larks (collective noun)

Examples

As dawn broke, an exaltation of larks rose above the meadow, their song filling the air.
We paused on our walk to admire an exaltation of larks soaring in perfect harmony overhead.
In her poem, she beautifully described the sky alive with an exaltation of larks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flock - a group of birds of one kind feeding, resting, or travelling together

Etymology

From Latin 'exaltatio' via Old French, meaning 'elevation' or 'lifting up'.

2. NOUN

/ ɛɡˌzɑlˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzɔːlˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
(Astrology) The position of a planet in the zodiac where it is believed to exert its strongest influence.
maximum planetary influence in astrology

Examples

According to astrologers, the exaltation of the Sun is in Aries.
Venus reaches its exaltation in the sign of Pisces.
Many believe that planetary exaltation enhances positive traits in a birth chart.

Etymology

From Latin exaltatio (a raising up), from exaltare (to raise up).

3. NOUN

/ ɛɡˌzɔlˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzɔːlˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of elevating someone to a very high or divine status, especially to that of a god
elevation to divine status

Examples

The ancient ceremony included the exaltation of the ruler to godhood.
Some myths feature the exaltation of mortals, transforming them into deities.
His heroic deeds led to the exaltation of his name throughout the land.

Etymology

From Latin exaltatio (elevation, lifting up), from ex- (out, up) + altus (high).

4. NOUN

/ ɛɡˌzɔlˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɛɡˌzɔːlˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A feeling or state of intense happiness, joy, or powerful emotion.
state of intense happiness or joy

Examples

She felt a sense of exaltation after winning the championship.
The choir’s performance filled the audience with exaltation.
In moments of exaltation, he forgot all his worries.

Synset Examples

listening to sweet music in a perfect rapture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecstasy - a state of overwhelming emotion or joy

  • Antonym: depression - a state of extreme sadness

Etymology

From Latin exaltatio (raising up), from ex (out, up) + altus (high).
