breathless

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɛθləs / (AmE), / ˈbrɛθlɪs / (BrE)
so exciting, remarkable, or intense as to make someone unable to breathe normally; causing great excitement or suspense
exciting or suspenseful to the point of stopping breath

Examples

The movie's final scene was so breathless that the audience sat silent in awe.
She told a breathless story that had everyone on the edge of their seats.
We embarked on a breathless journey through the winding mountain roads.

Synset Examples

a breathless flight
breathtaking adventure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breathtaking - astonishing or excitingly impressive

Etymology

From Middle English 'brethel', from 'breath' + '-less'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbrɛθləs / (AmE), / ˈbrɛθləs / (BrE)
unable to breathe easily or normally, often because of exertion, excitement, or strong emotion; gasping for breath
unable to breathe easily or normally

Examples

She was breathless after climbing the steep stairs to the top floor.
His breathless excitement was obvious as he recounted the story.
The runners were breathless at the finish line, gasping for air.

Synset Examples

breathless at thought of what I had done
breathless from running
followed the match with breathless interest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winded - short of breath, usually after exertion
  • Synonym: panting - breathing quickly and heavily
  • Antonym: calm - not excited or agitated, often with steady breathing

Etymology

Formed from 'breath' + suffix '-less' (without).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00268737-a
Synset POS: a
