azoturia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæz.oʊˈtjʊr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌaz.əˈtjʊə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by an abnormally high amount of urea in the urine.
excess urea in urine (medical)
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the horse with azoturia after observing dark-colored urine and muscle stiffness.
Patients suffering from azoturia may present symptoms of muscle weakness and excessive urination.
Early detection of azoturia is essential for effective treatment and prevention of complications.

Etymology

From French 'azote' (nitrogen) + '-uria' (urine condition).
