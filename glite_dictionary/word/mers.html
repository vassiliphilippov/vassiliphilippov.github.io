MERS

1. NOUN

/ mɜrz / (AmE), / mɜːz / (BrE)
a viral respiratory disease caused by the Middle East respiratory syndrome coronavirus (MERS-CoV), often transmitted from camels to humans, first identified in 2012 in Saudi Arabia
viral disease caused by MERS-CoV
medical

Examples

The outbreak of MERS in South Korea in 2015 led to widespread public concern.
Researchers continue to study how MERS spreads between camels and humans.
Doctors are trained to recognize the symptoms of MERS in travelers from affected regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: SARS - a viral respiratory disease caused by SARS-CoV
  • Synonym: COVID-19 - a viral disease caused by SARS-CoV-2

Etymology

Acronym from Middle East Respiratory Syndrome.
