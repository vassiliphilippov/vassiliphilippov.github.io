Laridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈlarɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of seabirds that includes gulls and terns
family of seabirds: gulls and terns

Examples

The Laridae family comprises both gulls and terns found worldwide along coasts and inland waters.
Members of the Laridae are known for their adaptability and intelligence.
Ornithologists study the evolutionary history of Laridae to understand seabird diversification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gull - a seabird of the family Laridae
  • Synonym: tern - a seabird closely related to gulls, in family Laridae

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Laridae', from Latin 'larus' (a gull).
