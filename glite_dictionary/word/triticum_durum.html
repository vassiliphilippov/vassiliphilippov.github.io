Triticum durum

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪtɪkəm ˈdʊrəm / (AmE), / ˈtrɪtɪkəm ˈdjʊərəm / (BrE)
a species of wheat with hard, dark-colored kernels high in gluten, used mainly for making bread and pasta
wheat variety used for bread and pasta

Examples

Durum pasta is made exclusively from Triticum durum because of its high gluten content.
Many farmers in Canada cultivate Triticum durum for both domestic use and export.
The bread's texture was perfect, thanks to the use of Triticum durum flour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: durum wheat - common name for Triticum durum

Etymology

From Latin, with *Triticum* meaning 'wheat' and *durum* meaning 'hard'.
