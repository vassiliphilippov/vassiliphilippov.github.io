unemployment

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnɪmˈplɔɪmənt / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪmˈplɔɪmənt / (BrE)
The state of not having a paid job, especially when actively seeking work.
state of being without work

Examples

Rising unemployment has become a major concern for the government in recent years.
The company closed several factories, resulting in increased unemployment in the region.
Graduates often worry about facing unemployment after finishing university.

Synset Examples

unemployment is a serious social evil
the rate of unemployment is an indicator of the health of an economy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: employment - the state of having paid work
  • Synonym: joblessness - the condition of being without a job; synonym for unemployment

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'employment', first used in English in the early 19th century.
