Agnus Dei

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːɡ.nəs ˈdeɪ.i / (AmE), / ˌæɡ.nəs ˈdeɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
A liturgical prayer addressed to Christ as the Lamb of God, usually recited or sung during the Roman Catholic Mass and other Christian services.
Christian liturgical prayer 'Lamb of God'

Examples

The choir sang the Agnus Dei with remarkable clarity during the Easter service.
At the end of Mass, the congregation joined silently in the Agnus Dei.
Composers throughout history have set the Agnus Dei to music for choral performances.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'Lamb of God'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæɡnəs ˈdeɪ.i / (AmE), / ˌaɡnəs ˈdeɪ.i / (BrE)
A representation of a lamb, often with a halo and flag, used as a symbol of Jesus Christ in Christian art and worship.
lamb symbol of Christ

Examples

The altar was adorned with an Agnus Dei carved in white marble.
In medieval paintings, the Agnus Dei is often shown holding a banner.
The choir sang a beautiful version of the Agnus Dei during the service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lamb of God - another name for Agnus Dei, referring to Christ in Christian theology

Etymology

From Latin 'Agnus Dei' meaning 'Lamb of God'.
