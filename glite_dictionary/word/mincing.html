mincing

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪnsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɪnsɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person’s manner, speech, or movement) affectedly delicate or refined, often in a way that seems artificial.
affectedly delicate or refined

Examples

He spoke in a mincing voice that made everyone uneasy.
She walked with a mincing step, pretending to be more refined than she was.
His mincing manners amused some guests but irritated others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affected - pretending to have qualities not genuinely felt
  • Antonym: natural - not artificial or affected in manner

Etymology

From the verb 'mince', meaning 'to walk or speak in an affectedly dainty way', from Old French 'mincier'.
