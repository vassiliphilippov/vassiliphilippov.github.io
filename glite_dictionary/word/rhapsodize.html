to rhapsodize

1. VERB

/ ˈræp.səˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræp.səˌdaɪz / (BrE)
to speak or write about something with great enthusiasm and delight
speak with great enthusiasm

Examples

During her speech, she began to rhapsodize about the beauty of the countryside.
He would often rhapsodize over classical music, sharing his passion with anyone who would listen.
Critics rhapsodized about the director's latest film, calling it a masterpiece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enthuse - to speak or express excitement about something
  • Synonym: gush - to speak with excessive enthusiasm

Etymology

From French 'rhapsodiser', from Greek 'rhapsōidos' (reciter of epic poetry).

2. VERB

/ ˈræp.səˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræp.sə.daɪz / (BrE)
to recite or perform a rhapsody, especially in the style of ancient poets
recite or perform a rhapsody

Examples

The poet would often rhapsodize before an eager crowd in the town square.
At the festival, he was asked to rhapsodize selections from Homer’s Odyssey.
She can rhapsodize ancient verses with absolute conviction and emotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recite - to repeat aloud from memory, especially publicly

Etymology

From French *rhapsodiser*, from Greek *rhapsōidein* (to recite epic poems).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00956169-v
Synset POS: v
