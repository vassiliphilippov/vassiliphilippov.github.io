aground

1. ADJ

/ əˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / əˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
(of a ship, boat, etc.) resting on or stuck on the bottom of a body of water and unable to move; stranded in shallow water.
stuck on the bottom (of a ship/boat)

Examples

The fishing boat ran aground during the storm and couldn't be freed until morning.
Several ships went aground on the rocky shoreline after the tide went out.
If we aren't careful with the map, we could end up aground before sunrise.

Synset Examples

a ship aground offshore
a boat aground on the beach waiting for the tide to lift it

Etymology

From Middle English agrounde, from a- (on) + ground (the land or bottom).

2. ADV

/ əˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / əˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
(of a ship or boat) resting on or stuck on the bottom of a body of water and unable to move.
stuck with bottom on the ground (nautical)

Examples

The fishing boat ran aground on a sandbar during the storm.
Several ships went aground after the tide went out unexpectedly.
The captain managed to steer clear of the rocks so the ferry did not go aground.

Synset Examples

he ran the ship aground

Related Terms

  • Antonym: afloat - floating or not grounded (of a vessel)

Etymology

From Middle English 'a ground' (on the ground), with 'a-' meaning 'on' or 'in'.
