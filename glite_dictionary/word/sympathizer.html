sympathizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəˌθaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəθaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who supports or agrees with the feelings or views of a group, cause, or individual, especially without being an active participant
person who supports or agrees with a cause

Examples

He was known as a sympathizer of the reform movement and often spoke in its favor.
During the war, many suspected sympathizers were detained by authorities.
She became a vocal sympathizer for animal rights causes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supporter - a person who actively supports a person, cause, or idea
  • Antonym: opponent - a person who disagrees with or resists a cause or idea

Etymology

From Greek sympatheia (fellow feeling) via French sympathiser (to sympathize) and English -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəˌθaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəθaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who shows compassion or expresses support and understanding for someone else's misfortune
person who offers compassion or support

Examples

She became a trusted sympathizer after hearing about his loss.
Many sympathizers attended the vigil to support the grieving family.
The organization offers help to sympathizers who wish to express their condolences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supporter - a person who supports another or a cause
  • Antonym: opponent - a person who disagrees with or opposes someone or something

Etymology

From Greek sympatheia (fellow feeling) via Late Latin sympathia and English sympathize + -er.
