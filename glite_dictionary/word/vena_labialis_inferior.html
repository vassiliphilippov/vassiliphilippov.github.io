vena labialis inferior

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə læˈbiː.ə.lɪs ɪnˈfɪr.i.ɔr / (AmE), / ˈveɪ.nə læˈbɪə.lɪs ɪnˈfɪə.rɪ.ɔː / (BrE)
a branch of the facial vein that drains blood from the lower lip
vein draining the lower lip
medical

Examples

The vena labialis inferior plays an important role in draining blood from the lower lip region.
In surgical procedures involving the mouth, care must be taken to avoid damaging the vena labialis inferior.
Swelling in the lower lip can sometimes be traced to issues with the vena labialis inferior.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein), 'labialis' (of the lip), 'inferior' (lower).
