Otus scops

1. PROPN

/ ˈoʊtəs ˈskɑps / (AmE), / ˈəʊtəs ˈskɒps / (BrE)
The scientific name for the European scops owl, a small owl species native to southern Europe and parts of Asia and Africa.
scientific name of the European scops owl

Examples

The Otus scops is known for its distinctive whistling call during the breeding season.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot an Otus scops in the Mediterranean forest.
Unlike many larger owls, the Otus scops prefers to nest in tree hollows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European scops owl - a small migratory owl native to Europe, Asia, and Africa

Etymology

From Latin: Otus (a genus of owls), scops (a kind of small owl).
