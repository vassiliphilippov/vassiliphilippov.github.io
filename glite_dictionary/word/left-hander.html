left-hander

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛftˈhændər / (AmE), / ˌlɛftˈhandə / (BrE)
A baseball pitcher who throws the ball with the left hand.
baseball pitcher throwing with left hand

Examples

The team signed a veteran left-hander to strengthen their pitching rotation.
As a left-hander, she had an advantage against right-handed batters.
The coach prefers to use a left-hander in late-inning situations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: right-hander - a baseball pitcher who throws the ball with the right hand

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɛftˈhændər / (AmE), / ˌlɛftˈhændə / (BrE)
a person who uses their left hand more skillfully than their right, especially for writing or throwing
person who is left-handed

Examples

My brother is a left-hander and always finds it tricky to use regular scissors.
It's rare to see a professional golfer who is a left-hander.
Being a left-hander, she had to adapt to a world designed for right-handed people.

Synset Examples

their pitcher was a southpaw

Related Terms

  • Antonym: right-hander - a person who uses the right hand more skillfully than the left
  • Synonym: southpaw - informal term for a left-handed person
