Pteridium esculentum

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈrɪdiəm ˌɛskjuˈlɛntəm / (AmE), / tɛˈrɪdɪəm ˌɛskjʊˈlɛntəm / (BrE)
A species of fern native to southeastern Asia, not hardy in cold temperate regions.
fern native to southeastern Asia

Examples

The Pteridium esculentum thrives in the mild, moist climates of southeastern Asia.
Botanists study Pteridium esculentum for its adaptability and ecological importance.
Unlike other ferns, Pteridium esculentum cannot survive harsh winters.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pteridium' (a genus of ferns) and 'esculentum' (edible), referring to the plant's edibility in some cultures.
