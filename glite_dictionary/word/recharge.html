to recharge

1. VERB

/ riˈtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈriːtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to load or fill something again, especially something that can be used up or emptied, such as a battery or weapon
to load or fill again

Examples

After hours of use, it's important to recharge your phone to avoid running out of power.
The soldiers had to recharge their rifles during the long battle.
She carefully began to recharge the printer with new paper.

Synset Examples

She reloaded the gun carefully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reload - to load something again, especially a weapon or program

Etymology

From re- (again) + charge (from Old French charger, from Late Latin carricare 'to load a wagon').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01493229-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌriːˈtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to restore electrical energy to (a battery or other device) by connecting it to a power source
restore electrical energy to

Examples

It's important to recharge your phone overnight so you have enough battery during the day.
After a few hours of use, the electric scooter needed to be recharged.
She plugged in her laptop to recharge it before her next meeting.

Synset Examples

recharge a battery

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discharge - to release stored electrical energy from a battery or device

Etymology

From re- (again) + charge, first used in the context of batteries in the late 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00519368-v
Synset POS: v
