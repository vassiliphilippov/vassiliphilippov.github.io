interlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪntərˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈɪntəlɒk / (BrE)
a safety device in a vehicle that prevents the engine from starting unless certain conditions are met
vehicle safety device preventing engine start

Examples

Many modern cars are equipped with an interlock that requires the brake pedal to be pressed before starting.
The mechanic explained how the faulty interlock was preventing the engine from turning over.
Authorities required him to install an interlock after his conviction for drunk driving.

Synset Examples

car thieves know how to bypass the ignition interlock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignition interlock - a device fitted to vehicle ignitions to prevent starting unless a breathalyzer test is passed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03584960-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪntərˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈɪntəlɒk / (BrE)
the act or process of interlocking or becoming joined together so that movement is restricted or coordinated
the act of joining or meshing tightly

Examples

The gears rely on a precise interlock to function smoothly within the machine.
The police maintained an interlock of their shields to control the protesters.
A strong interlock between the puzzle pieces made the structure stable.

Synset Examples

an interlocking of arms by the police held the crowd in check

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesh - the process of fitting or joining together closely
  • Antonym: disengagement - the act of separating or unmeshing parts

Etymology

From inter- (between) + lock, meaning to fasten together.

Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈlɑk / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈlɒk / (BrE)
(of two or more things) to fit or become joined together so that movement in one part is constrained by the others; to become meshed or engaged with each other.
to fit or mesh together

Examples

The two puzzle pieces interlock perfectly, making it impossible to separate them easily.
Their fingers interlocked as they walked down the street together.
Gears in a machine must interlock smoothly to ensure proper operation.

Synset Examples

They were locked in embrace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to engage - to become involved or cause to fit together
  • Antonym: to detach - to disconnect or separate

Etymology

From Latin inter- (between) + locāre (to place).
