Te

1. NOUN

/ ti / (AmE), / tiÀê / (BrE)
the chemical element tellurium; a brittle silver-white metalloid used in alloys and as a semiconductor
chemical element (tellurium)

Examples

The mineral contains traces of Te and other rare elements.
Pure Te is brittle and conducts electricity moderately well.
Silicon is often mixed with Te to improve its electrical properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tellurium - chemical element with atomic number 52

Etymology

From Latin 'tellus' meaning 'earth'; named in the late 18th century.
