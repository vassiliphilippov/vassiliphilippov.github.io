zydeco

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪ.dəˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈzaɪ.dɪˌkəʊ / (BrE)
a style of music from southern Louisiana that blends French Creole, Caribbean, and blues influences, often featuring accordion and washboard
music blending French Creole, Caribbean, and blues

Examples

She learned to dance to the lively rhythms of zydeco at the local festival.
The band played traditional zydeco music, complete with accordion and washboard.
Many radio stations in Louisiana feature zydeco alongside jazz and blues.

Etymology

From Louisiana French *les haricots* (the beans), mistaken as 'zydeco' in English; associated with Creole dance music.
