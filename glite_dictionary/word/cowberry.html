cowberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈkaʊbəri / (BrE)
a low, evergreen shrub of northern temperate regions producing small, red, edible berries; also, the berry itself
evergreen shrub with edible red berries

Examples

Wild cowberry bushes grow abundantly in Scandinavian forests.
She added fresh cowberries to the jam for a tart flavor.
The cowberry thrives in cold, acidic soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lingonberry - another name for cowberry, especially used in Scandinavia

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈkaʊbəri / (BrE)
A small, tart, red berry similar to the American cranberry, produced by a low-growing shrub native to northern and alpine regions.
small tart red berry similar to the cranberry

Examples

Wild cowberry grows in abundance across Scandinavian forests.
The jam was made from fresh cowberries picked this morning.
She prefers the sour taste of cowberry preserves over sweeter fruit spreads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranberry - a larger, tart red berry from North America
