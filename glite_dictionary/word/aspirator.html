aspirator

1. NOUN

/ ˈæspəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈaspɪreɪtə / (BrE)
a device that creates a suction by drawing air or gas through a liquid, typically used in laboratories for filtration or vacuum applications
device for creating suction by drawing gas through liquid

Examples

The chemistry lab used an aspirator to filter the solution efficiently.
He connected the aspirator to the water tap to create a vacuum for his experiment.
Modern laboratories often replace the traditional aspirator with an electric vacuum pump.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuum pump - a device that removes gas molecules from a sealed volume to create a vacuum

Etymology

From Latin 'aspirare' (to breathe upon, to breathe).
