Ustilago

1. NOUN

/ juːˈstɪl.əˌɡoʊ / (AmE), / juːˈstɪl.ə.ɡəʊ / (BrE)
a genus of fungi comprising the loose smuts, known for causing diseases in cereal crops
genus of fungi causing smut diseases
medical

Examples

Ustilago is responsible for smut infections in many cereal crops.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Ustilago genus affecting maize.
Control measures for Ustilago are essential in modern agriculture.

Etymology

From Latin *ustilare* (to burn), referencing the burnt appearance of infected plants.

2. NOUN

/ ˌjuːstɪˈleɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌjuːstɪˈleɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Ustilaginaceae, comprising the loose smuts that infect grasses and cereals.
genus of fungi (loose smuts)

Examples

Many species in the genus Ustilago are known to cause significant crop losses in agriculture.
The life cycle of Ustilago includes both sexual and asexual stages.
Researchers are studying Ustilago to develop resistant strains of wheat and corn.

Etymology

From Latin ustilare (to burn), referring to the scorched appearance of infected plants.
