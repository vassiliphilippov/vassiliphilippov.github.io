Antonio Gaudí

1. PROPN

/ ænˌtoʊni.oʊ gaʊˈdi / (AmE), / anˌtɒni.əʊ gaʊˈdiː / (BrE)
(1852–1926) a Spanish architect renowned for his distinctive and influential Art Nouveau architectural style, especially in Barcelona.
Spanish Art Nouveau architect (1852–1926)

Examples

The works of Antonio Gaudí attract millions of visitors to Barcelona every year.
Many architecture students study the unique style of Antonio Gaudí as part of their curriculum.
The Sagrada Família, designed by Antonio Gaudí, remains an iconic landmark in Spain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: architect - a person who designs buildings and oversees their construction
