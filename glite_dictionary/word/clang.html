clang

1. VERB

/ klæŋ / (AmE), / klæŋ / (BrE)
to make or cause to make a loud, resonant, metallic sound
make a loud metallic sound

Examples

The church bells clang loudly every Sunday morning.
As the heavy iron gate closed, it clanged shut behind us.
Tools clanged together on the factory floor, creating a harsh echo.

Synset Examples

clanging metal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clank - to make a dull, metallic sound, usually less resonant than clang
  • Synonym: rattle - to make quick, short, sharp sounds in rapid succession

Etymology

Imitative origin, representing the sound itself.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02178559-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ klæŋ / (AmE), / klæŋ / (BrE)
a loud, resonant, and often repeated metallic sound
loud metallic sound

Examples

The clang of the factory bell signaled the end of a shift.
He was awakened by the clang of dishes in the kitchen.
The clang of metal hitting metal echoed through the empty warehouse.

Synset Examples

he could hear the clang of distant bells

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clank - a loud, sharp sound like pieces of metal striking together
  • Synonym: ring - a clear resonant sound produced by striking metal
  • Degree Lower: clatter - a loud, rattling noise, typically of hard objects striking together

Etymology

Imitative; from the sound itself or related to Latin 'clangere' (to resound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07394744-n
Synset POS: n
