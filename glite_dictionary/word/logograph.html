logograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑɡəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a written character that represents a whole word or phrase, rather than a sound
character representing a word or phrase

Examples

A Chinese character is often a logograph representing a single word.
The number ‘2’ is a logograph for the word 'two'.
Many ancient scripts, such as Egyptian hieroglyphs, used logographs to convey meaning directly.

Synset Examples

‘7’ is a logogram that is pronounced ‘seven’ in English and ‘nanatsu’ in Japanese

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logogram - a symbol that represents a word

Etymology

From Greek logos (word) + -graph (writing).
