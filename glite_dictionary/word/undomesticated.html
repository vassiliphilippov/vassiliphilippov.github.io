undomesticated

1. ADJ

/ ʌn.dəˈmɛs.tɪ.keɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ʌn.dəˈmes.tɪ.keɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
Not accustomed to or suited for home life; lacking domestic habits or tendencies.
not suited for home life

Examples

Even after moving in together, he remained undomesticated and rarely helped with household chores.
Some wild animals remain undomesticated, even after years in captivity.
Her undomesticated attitude clashed with her partner's desire for a tidy, orderly home.

Synset Examples

after years of marriage he remained essentially undomesticated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: domesticated - accustomed to or adapted for home life or tame environment

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌndoʊˈmɛstɪˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəʊˈmɛstɪkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
not tamed or bred for human use; existing in a wild or natural state.
not tamed; wild

Examples

A few undomesticated horses still roam the plains.
The forest is home to many undomesticated animals rarely seen by humans.
Scientists are studying the differences between undomesticated and domesticated plant species.

Synset Examples

a few undomesticated horses left

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild - living or growing in a natural state; not domesticated
  • Antonym: domesticated - tamed and kept by humans

Etymology

From un- (not) + domesticated, from Latin domus (house).
