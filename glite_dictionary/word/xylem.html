xylem

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪləm / (AmE), / ˈzaɪləm / (BrE)
The vascular tissue in plants that transports water and minerals from the roots and forms the woody part of stems.
water-conducting plant tissue
medical

Examples

The oak tree's xylem carries water from its roots to its leaves.
During periods of drought, a plant's xylem can become blocked by air bubbles.
Botanists study the xylem to understand how trees grow taller each year.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: phloem - plant tissue that conducts food from leaves

Etymology

From Greek xylon (wood).
