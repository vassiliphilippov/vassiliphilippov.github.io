incorporative

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkɔrpərətɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɔːpərətɪv / (BrE)
(of a state or organization) expanding by taking over and integrating adjacent areas or entities.
expanding by territorial incorporation

Examples

The incorporative nature of the empire led to its vast territorial expanse.
Historians describe the kingdom as incorporative due to its absorption of neighboring regions.
Some governments pursue incorporative strategies to extend their influence over surrounding territories.

Synset Examples

the Russian Empire was a typical incorporative state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expansionist - favoring or practicing the expansion of territory or influence

Etymology

From Latin 'incorporare' (to combine into one body) + English suffix '-ive'.
