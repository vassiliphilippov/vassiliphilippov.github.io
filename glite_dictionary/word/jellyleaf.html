jellyleaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛliˌliːf / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛliˌliːf / (BrE)
A tropical and subtropical herb used as forage and for its medicinal, demulcent properties; also valued for its strong bast fiber, and considered an aggressive weed in some regions.
tropical/subtropical herb with medicinal and fiber uses

Examples

Local farmers use jellyleaf as a source of fiber that is even stronger than jute.
Herbalists in the region value jellyleaf for its soothing, demulcent properties.
In some climates, jellyleaf can become an aggressive weed that invades agricultural fields.
