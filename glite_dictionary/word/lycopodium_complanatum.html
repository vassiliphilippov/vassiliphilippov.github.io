Lycopodium complanatum

1. NOUN

/ laɪ.kəˈpoʊdi.əm ˌkɑm.pləˈneɪ.təm / (AmE), / lɪ.kəˈpəʊdɪ.əm ˌkɒm.pləˈneɪ.təm / (BrE)
A species of club moss with creeping stems, commonly known as Creeping Jenny or Northern Running-pine, found in northern temperate regions.
a species of club moss (plant)

Examples

Lycopodium complanatum is commonly found in pine forests across northern Europe and North America.
The dried stems of Lycopodium complanatum were once used in traditional medicine.
Botanists often refer to Lycopodium complanatum as Creeping Jenny when discussing club moss identification.

Etymology

From Greek *lycos* (wolf) and *podion* (little foot) plus Latin *complanatum* (flattened), referring to the plant's appearance.
