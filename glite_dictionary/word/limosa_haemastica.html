Limosa haemastica

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈmoʊsə hiːˈmæstɪkə / (AmE), / lɪˈməʊsə hɪˈmæstɪkə / (BrE)
A species of godwit native to the Americas, known as the Hudsonian godwit, recognized for its long bill and migratory behavior.
a species of New World godwit

Examples

The migration of the Limosa haemastica from North America to South America is one of the longest for any bird.
Conservationists are tracking Limosa haemastica populations to study the impacts of habitat loss.
The distinctive long, slightly upturned bill helps Limosa haemastica probe for food in muddy wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: godwit - a large, long-billed wading bird

Etymology

From Latin: Limosa (a genus of godwits) and haemastica, from Greek 'haimastikos' (bloody), referring to chest coloration.
