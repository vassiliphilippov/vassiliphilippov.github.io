totalitarian

1. ADJ

/ toʊˌtæləˈtɛriən / (AmE), / təʊˌtælɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
Relating to a political system in which the state holds total authority and controls all aspects of public and private life.
relating to total state control

Examples

The government enacted totalitarian measures to maintain absolute control over the population.
Critics warned that such policies could lead to a totalitarian regime.
Her novel depicted life under a totalitarian state, where even private thoughts were regulated.

Synset Examples

totalitarian theory and practice
operating in a totalistic fashion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritarian - enforcing strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom
  • Antonym: democratic - relating to a form of government where people have power

Etymology

From Italian *totalitario*, from *totalità* (totality), modeled on *total* + *-arian*.

2. NOUN

/ toʊˌtæləˈtɛriən / (AmE), / təʊˌtælɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates totalitarian principles or a totalitarian government
supporter of totalitarian principles

Examples

As a committed totalitarian, he believed the government should control every aspect of society.
The documentary explores the mindset of totalitarians who value state power above individual rights.
She accused her rival of being a totalitarian who suppresses all dissent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritarian - a person favoring strict obedience to authority, often at the expense of personal freedom
  • Antonym: democrat - supporter of democracy, government by the people

Etymology

From Italian totalitario (total), influenced by Latin 'totalis' and English '-arian'.

3. ADJ

/ toʊˌtæləˈtɛriən / (AmE), / təʊˌtælɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
Relating to a political system in which the government has total authority and seeks to control every aspect of public and private life.
relating to absolute, centralized government control

Examples

The country was ruled by a totalitarian government that suppressed all opposition.
In a totalitarian state, citizens have little freedom and are constantly monitored.
The novel explores the dangers of totalitarian regimes and the loss of individual rights.

Synset Examples

a totalitarian regime crushes all autonomous institutions in its drive to seize the human soul

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritarian - favoring or enforcing strict obedience to authority, especially that of the government, at the expense of personal freedom
  • Antonym: democratic - characterized by the principle of political or social equality for all

Etymology

From Italian totalitario (total), from Latin totus (whole, entire), used in 20th-century political theory.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00722079-s
Synset POS: s
