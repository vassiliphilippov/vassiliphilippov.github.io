childish

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃaɪldɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪldɪʃ / (BrE)
(of an adult or their behavior) showing a lack of maturity; behaving in a way more typical of a child than an adult.
showing a lack of maturity

Examples

His childish outburst embarrassed everyone in the meeting.
She finds it frustrating when her colleagues resort to childish name-calling during arguments.
Despite being nearly thirty, he sometimes displays childish jealousy over small things.

Synset Examples

childish tantrums
infantile behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immature - not fully developed or grown; lacking maturity
  • Antonym: adult - showing the qualities of a responsible grown person

Etymology

From Old English cildisc, from cild (child) + -ish (adjectival suffix).
