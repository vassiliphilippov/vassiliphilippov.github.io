to unhear

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈhɪr / (AmE), / ʌnˈhɪə / (BrE)
to try or wish to forget something one has heard, especially something disturbing or unwelcome
try or wish to forget something heard
informal

Examples

I wish I could unhear what they said about me in that meeting.
Once you learn the secret, it's impossible to unhear it.
She tried to unhear the insult, but it lingered in her mind.

Synset Examples

It’s hard to unhear the gossip that circulates here.
Once he said it, I couldn't unhear those words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to forget - to be unable to remember

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'hear', modelled after similar constructions like 'unsee'.
