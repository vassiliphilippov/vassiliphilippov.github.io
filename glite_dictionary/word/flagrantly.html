flagrantly

1. ADV

/ ˈfleɪɡrəntli / (AmE), / ˈfleɪɡrəntli / (BrE)
In a way that is shockingly noticeable or obvious, especially in an offensive or immoral manner.
in a shockingly obvious or offensive way

Examples

The company was flagrantly ignoring environmental regulations in its operations.
He flagrantly broke the rules in front of everyone, knowing there would be consequences.
The politician flagrantly lied to the public about his involvement.

Synset Examples

he is flagrantly disregarding the law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blatantly - in an open and unashamedly obvious manner
  • Antonym: covertly - in a manner that is not openly acknowledged or displayed

Etymology

From the adjective 'flagrant', from Latin flagrans, flagrant- (burning, blazing).
