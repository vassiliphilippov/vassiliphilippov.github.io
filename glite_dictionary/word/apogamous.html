apogamous

1. ADJ

/ əˈpɑɡəməs / (AmE), / əˈpɒɡəməs / (BrE)
(of plants) relating to or denoting the development of an embryo without fertilization.
embryo development without fertilization
medical

Examples

Many ferns are known to reproduce through apogamous processes, avoiding the need for fertilization.
The researcher observed that the plant's apogamous development led to viable offspring even in the absence of pollen.
Certain species exhibit apogamous reproduction as an adaptation to unstable environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parthenogenetic - reproducing from an unfertilized egg, especially in animals

Etymology

From Greek 'apo-' (away from) + 'gamos' (marriage, union).
