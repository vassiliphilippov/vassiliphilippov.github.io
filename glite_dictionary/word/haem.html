haem

1. NOUN

/ hiːm / (AmE), / hiːm / (BrE)
a complex red organic compound containing iron, found in hemoglobin and other proteins, to which oxygen binds
iron-containing pigment in hemoglobin
medical british

Examples

Iron is an essential component of haem in red blood cells.
Disorders affecting haem synthesis can lead to anemia.
The structure of haem allows it to bind oxygen efficiently in the bloodstream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heme - iron-containing pigment in hemoglobin (US spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' meaning 'blood'.
