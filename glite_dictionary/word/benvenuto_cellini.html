Benvenuto Cellini

1. PROPN

/ ˌbɛn.vəˈnu.toʊ tʃɛˈli.ni / (AmE), / ˌbɛn.veˈnuː.tə tʃɛˈliː.ni / (BrE)
(1500–1571) An Italian sculptor and goldsmith of the Renaissance, famous for his technical skill and artistic works, including the sculpture Perseus with the Head of Medusa.
Italian sculptor and goldsmith (1500–1571)

Examples

Many art historians consider Benvenuto Cellini one of the greatest sculptors of the Italian Renaissance.
The bronze statue of Perseus by Benvenuto Cellini draws crowds in Florence's Piazza della Signoria.
In his autobiography, Benvenuto Cellini vividly describes his adventures as a goldsmith and artist.

Etymology

Italian personal name; Benvenuto means 'welcome' in Italian. Surname from Italian family name Cellini.
