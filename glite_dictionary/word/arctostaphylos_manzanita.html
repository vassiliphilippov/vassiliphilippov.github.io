Arctostaphylos manzanita

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrktoʊˌstæfɪloʊs mænzəˈniːtə / (AmE), / ˌɑːktəʊˌstæfɪlɒs mænzəˈniːtə / (BrE)
A species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to California, characterized by smooth bark, twisting branches, white or pink drooping flowers, and red berrylike fruits.
a Californian shrub or small tree with red fruits

Examples

Several Arctostaphylos manzanita shrubs grew along the trail, their red bark gleaming in the sunlight.
The garden featured a beautiful Arctostaphylos manzanita with clusters of pinkish flowers in early spring.
Wildlife is attracted to Arctostaphylos manzanita because of its nutritious berries.

Etymology

From Greek arktos (bear) + staphyle (bunch of grapes) and Spanish manzanita (little apple).
