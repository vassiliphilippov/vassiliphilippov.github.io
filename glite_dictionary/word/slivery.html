slivery

1. ADJ

/ ˈslɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈslɪvəri / (BrE)
resembling or made up of thin, narrow, sharp fragments, especially of wood
resembling thin, sharp fragments

Examples

The old barn had a slivery wooden floor that pricked anyone who walked barefoot.
She avoided touching the slivery branches piled in the yard.
After hours of sanding, the door no longer felt rough and slivery.

Synset Examples

a rough splintery floor of old pine boards

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splintery - having or likely to produce sharp splinters

Etymology

From 'sliver', meaning a thin, narrow piece or fragment.
