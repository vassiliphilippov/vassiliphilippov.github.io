remedial

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈmidiəl / (AmE), / rɪˈmiːdiəl / (BrE)
Intended to correct or improve something, especially skills or knowledge that are below the expected level.
intended to rectify or improve

Examples

The school offers remedial classes in mathematics for students who are struggling.
She attended a remedial reading program to improve her literacy skills.
There are special remedial measures in place to help employees meet company standards.

Synset Examples

a remedial reading course
remedial education

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrective - intended to correct or improve faults or deficiencies

Etymology

From Late Latin remedialis, from Latin remedium (remedy).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈmidiəl / (AmE), / rɪˈmiːdiəl / (BrE)
intended to cure or restore health; providing a remedy for a disease or medical condition
curative; intended to restore health

Examples

The doctor recommended remedial exercises to help strengthen her injured knee.
After months of remedial treatment, his health gradually improved.
Certain plants have long been valued for their remedial properties in traditional medicine.

Synset Examples

curative powers of herbal remedies
her gentle healing hand
remedial surgery
a sanative environment of mountains and fresh air
a therapeutic agent
therapeutic diets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therapeutic - having healing or curative properties
  • Synonym: curative - able to cure disease or restore health
  • Antonym: harmful - causing injury or damage; the opposite of curative or beneficial

Etymology

From Latin remedium (cure, remedy) + -al.
