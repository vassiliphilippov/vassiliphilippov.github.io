exile

1. VERB

/ ˈɛɡˌzaɪl / (AmE), / ˈɛksaɪl / (BrE)
to force someone to leave their country, especially for political reasons or as a punishment
to expel someone from their country

Examples

The dissident was exiled from his homeland after criticizing the regime.
They threatened to exile anyone who opposed their rule.
She was exiled to a remote island for her political beliefs.

Synset Examples

The poet was exiled because he signed a letter protesting the government's actions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to banish - to send someone away from a place as an official punishment
  • Synonym: to expel - to officially force someone to leave a place or organization

Etymology

From Latin 'exilium' (banishment), from 'exul' (banished person).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛɡ.zaɪl, ˈɛk.saɪl / (AmE), / ˈɛɡ.zaɪl, ˈɛk.saɪl / (BrE)
a person who chooses to live away from their native country or home, often for political or personal reasons
person living away from home or country

Examples

After the war, the writer spent many years as an exile in Paris.
Famous exiles often form tight-knit communities in foreign cities.
He lived as an exile by choice, never returning to his homeland.

Synset Examples

American expatriates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expatriate - a person living outside their native country
  • Synonym: refugee - a person forced to leave their country to escape danger or persecution

Etymology

From Latin exilium (banishment), from exul (an exile).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛɡ.zaɪl, ˈɛk.saɪl / (AmE), / ˈɛksaɪl, ˈɛɡzaɪl / (BrE)
the state or act of being expelled from one's native country, typically as a punishment or for political reasons
forced removal from one's native country

Examples

After the revolution, he faced exile and was forced to live abroad for years.
Many artists produced their most influential work while in exile.
The threat of exile was used to silence political dissent.

Synset Examples

men in exile dream of hope
his deportation to a penal colony
the expatriation of wealthy farmers
the sentence was one of transportation for life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banishment - the act of ordering someone to leave and stay away from a place
  • Synonym: deportation - the act of expelling a foreigner from a country
  • Synonym: expatriation - the act of banishing a person from their native country

Etymology

From Latin exilium (banishment), from exul (an exile).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈɛɡˌzaɪl / (AmE), / ˈɛksaɪl / (BrE)
a person who has been forced to leave their home or country by authority and is not allowed to return
person expelled from country

Examples

The celebrated writer lived as an exile in Paris for twenty years.
Many political exiles dreamed of returning home when the regime changed.
After speaking out against the government, she became an exile in a foreign land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refugee - a person who has been forced to flee their country because of persecution, war, or violence

Etymology

From Latin exilium (banishment), from exul (banished person).
