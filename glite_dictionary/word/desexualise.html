to desexualise

1. VERB

/ diˈsɛkʃuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈsɛksjʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
to redirect or eliminate sexual feelings, impulses, or characteristics
redirect or remove sexual impulses/traits

Examples

Some therapies attempt to desexualise aggressive impulses by channeling them into creative activities.
The artwork was desexualised to be more appropriate for a family audience.
Over time, certain dances have been desexualised to focus on their cultural significance rather than sensual expression.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to sexualise - to make sexual or give a sexual character to something

Etymology

From de- (removal) + sexual + -ise (verb-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01957534-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ diˈsɛkʃuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈsɛksjʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make infertile or incapable of reproduction, especially by removing or suppressing sexual organs or functions
make infertile; remove sexual ability

Examples

The treatment is intended to desexualise certain insects to control their population.
In history, some oppressive policies sought to desexualise specific groups through forced procedures.
Doctors sometimes desexualise animals to prevent them from breeding uncontrollably.

Synset Examples

in some countries, people with genetically transmissible disabilities are sterilized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sterilize - to make unable to reproduce, commonly via medical procedures

Etymology

From de- (expressing removal) + sexual + -ise (verb forming suffix).

Synset POS: v
