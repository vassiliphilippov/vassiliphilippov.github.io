politics

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑlətɪks / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪtɪks / (BrE)
Activities or relationships involving the use of power and influence within a group or organization, often including maneuvering or intrigue to gain authority.
maneuvering for power or influence

Examples

Politics in the workplace can sometimes create tension between colleagues.
She was tired of the politics involved in getting a promotion at her company.
The committee's decision was influenced more by politics than by actual merit.

Synset Examples

office politics is often counterproductive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: office politics - activities in a workplace to gain advantage or authority

Etymology

From Greek politikē (art of government), from politikos (of citizens or the state).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːlətɪks / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪtɪks / (BrE)
the activities, actions, and policies used to gain and hold power in a government or to influence government processes
activities related to government or power

Examples

She has always had a keen interest in politics, following elections and debates closely.
Economic issues often shape the politics of a nation for decades.
University students discussed international politics at the weekly forum.

Synset Examples

unemployment dominated the politics of the inter-war years
government agencies multiplied beyond the control of representative politics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: government - the group of people with the authority to govern a country or state; a particular administration or regime

Etymology

From Greek politiká (affairs of the city); related to polites (citizen).
