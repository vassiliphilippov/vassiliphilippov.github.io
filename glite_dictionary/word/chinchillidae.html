Chinchillidae

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪn.tʃɪˈlɪ.dieɪ / (AmE), / tʃɪn.tʃɪˈlɪ.dɪ.iː / (BrE)
A family of small, bushy-tailed, burrowing rodents native to South America, including chinchillas and their relatives.
family of South American rodents

Examples

Scientists are studying the evolutionary traits of Chinchillidae to better understand their adaptation to harsh environments.
The Chinchillidae family includes both wild and domesticated species prized for their soft fur.
Many members of Chinchillidae make extensive burrows in the arid regions of the Andes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chinchilla - a small S. American rodent with soft grey fur

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'chinchilla', the type genus, plus the standard zoological suffix '-idae' for animal families.
