tale

1. NOUN

/ teɪl / (AmE), / teɪl / (BrE)
a lie, especially a trivial or harmless one, often told by a child
a trivial or harmless lie

Examples

As a child, she often made up tales to avoid getting into trouble.
He was caught telling tales about why he hadn’t done his homework.
Don’t believe every tale he tells you – he likes to exaggerate.

Synset Examples

he told a fib about eating his spinach
how can I stop my child from telling stories?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fib - a small or trivial lie
  • Synonym: story - an account, real or invented, told to entertain or deceive
  • Antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true

2. NOUN

/ teɪl / (AmE), / teɪl / (BrE)
a narrative or story describing a series of events, often imaginative or fictional, presented in written, spoken, or other media
narrative; story of events

Examples

He told a fascinating tale about his adventures in South America.
The movie is based on a classic fairy tale.
She wrote a tale of mystery and suspense that captivated her readers.

Synset Examples

his narrative was interesting
Disney's stories entertain adults as well as children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: story - an account of imaginary or real people and events told for entertainment
  • Synonym: narrative - a spoken or written account of connected events; a story

Etymology

From Old English talu (story, tale, account).
