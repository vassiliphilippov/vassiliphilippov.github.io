crenation

1. NOUN

/ krɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / krɪˈneɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a series of rounded projections or notches along the edge of an object, such as a leaf, shell, or a shriveled cell
round notches or projections on an edge

Examples

The crenation of the leaf margin gives it a distinctive, wavy appearance.
When red blood cells are placed in a hypertonic solution, they undergo crenation.
The shell exhibited clear crenation along its outer edge, making it easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crenulate - having finely scalloped or notched edges

Etymology

From Latin 'crenatus' (notched).
