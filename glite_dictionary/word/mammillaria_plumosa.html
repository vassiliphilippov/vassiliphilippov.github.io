Mammillaria plumosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæməˈlɛəriə pluːˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌmamɪˈlɛːrɪə pluːˈməʊsə / (BrE)
A small, tuberculate cactus with white, feathery spines, native to northeastern Mexico.
a cactus with feathery spines from Mexico

Examples

The Mammillaria plumosa is prized in gardens for its unique feathery appearance.
Botanists discovered a new colony of Mammillaria plumosa in the wilds of northeastern Mexico.
With proper care, Mammillaria plumosa can thrive indoors as an attractive houseplant.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Mammillaria' refers to the nipple-like tubercles, 'plumosa' means 'feathery'.
