Euphausiacea

1. NOUN

/ juːˌfɔziˈeɪsiə / (AmE), / juːˌfɔːziˈeɪsiə / (BrE)
an order of small, often bioluminescent crustaceans, commonly called krill, that are a key component of marine plankton
order of small marine crustaceans (krill)
medical

Examples

The diet of baleen whales largely consists of Euphausiacea.
Researchers study Euphausiacea to understand their crucial role in the ocean food web.
Many species within Euphausiacea are known for their natural bioluminescence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: krill - small, shrimp-like marine crustacean, part of Euphausiacea

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Euphausia + -acea (order suffix).

2. NOUN

/ juːfəˌsiːəˈsiːə / (AmE), / juːfəʊˌsiːɪˈeɪsiə / (BrE)
an order of small, commonly luminescent crustaceans, including krill, that form a major element of marine plankton
order of crustaceans including krill

Examples

Many species within the Euphausiacea are bioluminescent, producing light in the deep sea.
The Euphausiacea play a crucial role in the ocean food web as a primary diet of whales.
Researchers collected samples of Euphausiacea to study changes in plankton populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: krill - small shrimp-like marine crustaceans

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Euphausia + -acea (order suffix in taxonomy).
