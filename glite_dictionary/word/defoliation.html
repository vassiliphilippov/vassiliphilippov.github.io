defoliation

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌfoʊliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌfəʊliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of causing leaves to fall off trees or plants, especially by the use of chemicals.
removal of leaves from plants

Examples

The government banned the use of defoliation chemicals due to environmental concerns.
Heavy defoliation can weaken trees and reduce agricultural yields.
The region suffered severe defoliation after the pest infestation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defoliate - to remove leaves from a plant or tree, especially by chemical means

Etymology

From Late Latin defoliare, from de- (removal) + folium (leaf).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˌfoʊliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌfəʊliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the loss or removal of leaves from a plant or tree, either naturally or by artificial means
loss or removal of leaves

Examples

The forest suffered extensive defoliation after the caterpillar infestation.
Chemical defoliation was used to clear vast areas of vegetation during the war.
Repeated defoliation can weaken plants and make them more vulnerable to disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defoliate - to remove leaves from a plant or tree

Etymology

From Latin 'de-' (removal) + 'folium' (leaf).
