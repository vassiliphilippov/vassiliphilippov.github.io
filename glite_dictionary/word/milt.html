milt

1. NOUN

/ mɪlt / (AmE), / mɪlt / (BrE)
The seminal fluid of male fish, which contains sperm; also, the reproductive glands producing this fluid.
seminal fluid of male fish

Examples

The chef prepared the salmon milt delicately, serving it with fresh herbs and lemon.
During spawning season, the river was filled with milt released by the fish.
Scientists collected samples of milt to study the breeding habits of herring.

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; in biological sense, attested since the 15th century.

2. NOUN

/ mɪlt / (AmE), / mɪlt / (BrE)
the seminal fluid or sperm-filled reproductive gland of a male fish, often with a creamy texture
fish sperm or sperm-filled gland

Examples

Fried milt is considered a delicacy in some coastal regions.
The fisherman collected the salmon's milt for breeding purposes.
Some sushi dishes use milt because of its creamy texture and mild flavor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: roe - eggs or egg masses of fish

Etymology

From Middle English *milte*, of Scandinavian origin (compare Norwegian *milt*).
