Nasua narica

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪsu.ə nəˈriːkə / (AmE), / ˈnasjʊ.ə nəˈriːkə / (BrE)
A species of coati, an omnivorous mammal found in Central and South America, known for its long snout and ringed tail.
species of coati (mammal)

Examples

The Nasua narica can often be seen foraging for fruit in the forests of Costa Rica.
Researchers studied the social behavior of Nasua narica in its natural habitat.
A group of Nasua narica was spotted near the riverbank, searching for insects and eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coati - common name for the genus Nasua, small omnivorous mammals

Etymology

From Latin; 'Nasua' from Latin 'nasus' (nose), and 'narica' also derived from Latin, referencing the nostrils.
