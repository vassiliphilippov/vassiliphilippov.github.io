lowercase

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊərˌkeɪs / (AmE), / ˈləʊəkeɪs / (BrE)
(of a letter) written in small form, not in capital or upper-case form.
written in small, not capital, letters

Examples

Please fill out the form using only lowercase letters.
The password is case-sensitive, so be sure to use lowercase characters.
Her handwriting mixes lowercase and uppercase letters for emphasis.

Synset Examples

lowercase letters: a and b and c etc.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uppercase - written in capital letters

Etymology

From the practice in printing of keeping smaller letters in the lower case (drawer) of a compositor's type case.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01470213-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊərˌkeɪs / (AmE), / ˈləʊəˌkeɪs / (BrE)
the small form of letters in the alphabet, as opposed to capital (uppercase) letters, used in writing and printing
small letters in the alphabet

Examples

Please write your name in lowercase letters only.
The password must include both lowercase and uppercase characters.
Children first learn to recognize lowercase letters in school.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uppercase - capital letters in the alphabet

Etymology

Refers to the lower section of a printer's type case, where small letters were stored.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06837807-n
Synset POS: n
