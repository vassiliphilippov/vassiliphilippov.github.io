aciduric

1. ADJ

/ ˌæsɪˈdʊrɪk / (AmE), / ˌasɪˈdjʊərɪk / (BrE)
(of an organism, especially certain bacteria) able to grow or thrive in an acidic environment.
able to grow in acidic environments
medical

Examples

Certain aciduric bacteria are responsible for the spoilage of acidic foods like fruit juices.
The aciduric nature of these microorganisms allows them to survive in low pH environments.
Researchers are studying aciduric strains to develop new probiotics for the gut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acidophilic - (of organisms) thriving in acidic environments

Etymology

From Latin 'acidus' (sour) + '-uric' (relating to urine or uric acid).
