trice

1. VERB

/ traɪs / (AmE), / traɪs / (BrE)
to hoist up or in and lash or secure with a small rope, especially on a ship
hoist and secure with a rope (nautical)

Examples

The sailors triced up the sail before the storm arrived.
He quickly triced the rope to secure the loose barrel on deck.
You must trice the canvas properly to keep it out of the way.

Etymology

Probably related to Middle Dutch *trîsen* (to hoist).

2. NOUN

/ traɪs / (AmE), / traɪs / (BrE)
a very short period of time; an instant
a very brief moment

Examples

She finished the puzzle in a trice, surprising everyone.
I'll be with you in a trice, so please wait right here.
The answer came to him in a trice, before anyone else could even react.

Synset Examples

if I had the chance, I'd do it in a flash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moment - a very brief period of time
  • Synonym: instant - an extremely short period of time

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *trisen* (to pull), perhaps from Middle Dutch *trīsen* (to hoist, pull up)

3. VERB

/ traɪs / (AmE), / traɪs / (BrE)
to raise, pull, or haul up with a rope or line, especially by means of a sudden movement
raise or pull up with a line

Examples

The sailors had to trice up the sails before the storm hit.
She quickly triced the curtain to let more sunlight into the room.
He told his crew to trice up the side ladder for safety.

Synset Examples

trice a window shade

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch 'trisen', meaning 'to hoist'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
