to procrastinate

1. VERB

/ proʊˈkræs.təˌneɪt / (AmE), / prəˈkræs.tɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to delay or postpone doing something, especially out of habitual carelessness or laziness
delay doing something unnecessarily

Examples

If you procrastinate on writing your paper, you might be up all night before the deadline.
She tends to procrastinate when faced with difficult decisions.
Many students procrastinate by cleaning their rooms instead of studying.

Synset Examples

He procrastinated the matter until it was almost too late

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - to put off something to a later time
  • Antonym: to hasten - to do something quickly; to hurry

Etymology

From Latin procrastinare, from pro- (forward) + crastinus (of tomorrow).

2. VERB

/ proʊˈkræstəˌneɪt / (AmE), / prəˈkræstɪneɪt / (BrE)
to delay or postpone doing something that should be done, often unnecessarily or habitually
delay or postpone an action

Examples

I tend to procrastinate when faced with difficult decisions.
She procrastinated all week and finished her assignment at the last minute.
If you procrastinate too much, important tasks may never get done.

Synset Examples

He did not want to write the letter and procrastinated for days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - to make something happen later than planned or expected
  • Antonym: advance - to move something forward to an earlier time

Etymology

From Latin procrastinare, from pro- (forward) + crastinus (of tomorrow), from cras (tomorrow).
