purgative

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrɡətɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɜːɡətɪv / (BrE)
A medicine or substance that causes evacuation of the bowels; a laxative.
medicine that stimulates bowel evacuation
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a purgative to relieve his constipation.
Some traditional remedies used herbs as natural purgatives.
After taking the purgative, she felt much better.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laxative - a medicine that induces the evacuation of the bowels
  • Antonym: constipating - causing constipation or inhibiting bowel movements

Etymology

From Latin purgativus (cleansing), from purgare (to cleanse).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɝːɡətɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɜːɡətɪv / (BrE)
(of a medicine or substance) having a strong laxative effect; tending to cause evacuation of the bowels.
strongly laxative (adj.)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a purgative medicine to quickly relieve the patient’s severe constipation.
This herbal tea is considered purgative and should be taken only under supervision.
Because of its purgative properties, the compound is used in certain medical treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laxative - a substance that promotes evacuation of the bowels

Etymology

From Latin 'purgativus', from 'purgare' (to cleanse, to purge).
