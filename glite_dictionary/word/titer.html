titer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtiːtə / (BrE)
the concentration of a solution, especially as determined by titration
concentration of a solution by titration

Examples

The titer of antibodies in the blood can indicate immunity to a disease.
Scientists measured the titer of each chemical sample to ensure accuracy.
After dilution, the titer of the solution was lower than expected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titre - British spelling for concentration of a solution (by titration)

Etymology

From French *titre* (standard of purity), from Latin *titulus* (title).
