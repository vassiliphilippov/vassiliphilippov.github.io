mahimahi

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːhiˈmɑːhi / (AmE), / ˌmɑːhiˈmɑːhi / (BrE)
The lean flesh of a saltwater fish, especially found in warm waters, valued as food, particularly in Hawaiian cuisine.
lean flesh of a warm-water fish; food delicacy

Examples

Many seafood restaurants offer grilled mahimahi as a healthy choice on their menus.
He ordered a mahimahi sandwich with a side of tropical salsa.
Freshly caught mahimahi is enjoyed in various Hawaiian dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dolphinfish - a large, colorful saltwater fish also called mahi-mahi

Etymology

From Hawaiian *mahimahi* (a type of fish).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːhiˈmɑːhi / (AmE), / ˌmɑːhiˈmɑːhi / (BrE)
A large, slender fish (Coryphaena hippurus), valued as food and game, found in warm seas, especially around Hawaii.
large fish found in warm seas, prized as food

Examples

Grilled mahimahi is a popular dish in many Hawaiian restaurants.
Fishermen spotted a school of mahimahi off the coast this morning.
She ordered fresh mahimahi tacos with a side of mango salsa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dolphinfish - another name for the same species, Coryphaena hippurus

Etymology

From Hawaiian 'mahimahi' meaning 'very strong'.
