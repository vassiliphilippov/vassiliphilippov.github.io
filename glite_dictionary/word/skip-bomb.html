to skip-bomb

1. VERB

/ skɪp bɑm / (AmE), / skɪp bɒm / (BrE)
to attack a target by releasing bombs from an aircraft at a low altitude so that they bounce or skip along the surface before exploding, typically used against ships.
bomb by bouncing bombs along a surface

Examples

The air force planned to skip-bomb the enemy ships hiding in the bay.
During the war, pilots learned how to skip-bomb targets with impressive precision.
They skip-bombed oil tankers along the coast to disrupt supply lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bomb - to attack a place or object with bombs

Etymology

Formed from 'skip' (to bounce) and 'bomb'; refers to the bombing technique used especially in World War II.
