thought

1. NOUN

/ θɔt / (AmE), / θɔːt / (BrE)
The process of using the mind to consider or reflect on something carefully; mental activity or contemplation.
careful consideration; contemplation

Examples

She paused for a moment of thought before answering the question.
His actions showed a complete lack of thought for others' feelings.
After much thought, they decided to move to another city.

Synset Examples

thinking always made him frown
she paused for thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflection - serious thought or consideration
  • Antonym: daydream - a series of pleasant thoughts that distract one's attention

Etymology

From Old English 'þōht', related to 'think'.

2. NOUN

/ θɔt / (AmE), / θɔːt / (BrE)
an idea or opinion produced by thinking, or the content of what someone is considering in their mind
an idea or consideration

Examples

The sudden thought of moving to another city made her nervous.
He shared a thought that changed the direction of the meeting.
One thought kept him awake all night: did he lock the door?

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idea - a formulated thought or opinion

Etymology

Examples

Buddhist thought continues to inspire millions of followers worldwide.

4. NOUN

/ θɔt / (AmE), / θɔːt / (BrE)
a personal belief or opinion that is not necessarily based on proof or certainty
personal belief or opinion

Examples

I'd like to hear your thoughts on this proposal.
Her thought was that we should wait for more information before deciding.
That was just my thought, but I'm open to other suggestions.

Synset Examples

my opinion differs from yours
I am not of your persuasion
what are your thoughts on Haiti?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opinion - a view or judgment formed about something
  • Synonym: conviction - a firmly held belief or opinion
  • Antonym: certainty - something that is definitely true or known

Etymology

From Old English 'þoht' (thought, conception), from 'þencan' (to think).
