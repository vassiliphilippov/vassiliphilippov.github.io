to disengage

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.ɪnˈɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪnˈɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
to release or separate something from what holds, connects, or entangles it
release or separate from something

Examples

She tried to disengage her arm from the man's grip.
The commander ordered the troops to disengage from the front line.
You must disengage the safety catch before starting the engine.

Synset Examples

I want to disengage myself from his influence
disengage the gears

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to engage - to cause to become involved or attached

Etymology

From French *désengager*, from dés- (dis-) + engager (to engage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01514012-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
to become free or released from an attachment or connection; to separate or stop being involved
become free or separated

Examples

When the lever is pulled, the safety mechanism will disengage automatically.
The gears disengage when the car is shifted into neutral.
She learned to disengage her emotions from the stressful situation.

Synset Examples

in neutral, the gears disengage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detach - to separate from something to which it is attached
  • Antonym: to engage - to occupy, attract, or involve; to attach

Etymology

From French 'désengager', based on Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'engage' (to involve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00220702-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌdɪsənˈɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
to free something from an obstruction or difficulty, or to release it from attachment to something else
free from obstruction or attachment

Examples

The engineer had to disengage the safety lock before opening the machine.
She disengaged the gears to stop the motor smoothly.
Before moving the furniture, we must disengage all the cables from the wall.

Synset Examples

free a path across the cluttered floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free - to make or become free; to release
  • Synonym: release - to let go or set free from confinement or attachment
  • Antonym: to engage - to cause to be attached, involved, or occupied

Etymology

From French 'désengager', from 'dés-' (dis-) + 'engager' (to engage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01478583-v
Synset POS: v
