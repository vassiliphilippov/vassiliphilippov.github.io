Manet

1. PROPN

/ mæˈneɪ / (AmE), / mæˈneɪ / (BrE)
Édouard Manet (1832–1883), a French painter whose innovative style greatly influenced the Impressionist movement.
French painter, influenced Impressionism

Examples

The bold brushwork of Manet transformed nineteenth-century art.
Many critics at first rejected Manet's unconventional approach to painting.
In Paris, we visited an exhibition devoted to Manet and his contemporaries.
