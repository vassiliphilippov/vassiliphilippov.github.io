butterwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəˌwɔːt / (BrE)
A bog-dwelling carnivorous plant of the genus Pinguicula, with broad sticky leaves that trap insects and typically showy flowers.
carnivorous bog plant of the genus Pinguicula

Examples

The butterwort catches insects with its sticky leaves to supplement its nutrient intake.
Some species of butterwort have flowers ranging from pale yellow to deep purple.
We found a rare butterwort during our hike through the wetland reserve.

Etymology

From 'butter' + 'wort' (an old word for plant or herb), possibly from the greasy feel of its leaves.
