ceylonite

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈloʊnaɪt / (AmE), / sɪˈləʊnaɪt / (BrE)
A dark-colored variety of spinel mineral containing iron, typically black or dark brown in appearance.
dark iron-rich spinel mineral

Examples

A rare specimen of ceylonite was displayed at the museum's mineralogy section.
Geologists identified the black crystal as ceylonite due to its high iron content.
The gem dealer specialized in spinels, including unusual types like ceylonite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinel - a hard glassy mineral consisting of an oxide of magnesium and aluminum

Etymology

From Ceylon (former name of Sri Lanka), where the mineral was first found, plus the mineral suffix -ite.
