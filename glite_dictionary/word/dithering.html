dithering

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪðɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɪðərɪŋ / (BrE)
The process in digital imaging of creating the illusion of intermediate colors by arranging tiny dots of different colors in patterns.
color-blending technique using dot patterns

Examples

Modern printers use dithering to produce smooth gradients from limited color palettes.
The photograph appeared less pixelated after applying advanced dithering algorithms.
Without dithering, images can show ugly color banding on screens with low color depth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halftoning - a printing technique that simulates continuous tone by varying dot size or spacing

Etymology

From 'dither' (to tremble or quiver); used in digital graphics to describe pixel patterning that simulates a smooth gradient.
