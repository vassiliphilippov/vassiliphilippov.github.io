to ensnarl

1. VERB

/ ɛnˈsnɑːrl / (AmE), / ɪnˈsnɑːl / (BrE)
to entangle or catch, as if in a net or trap
to entangle or trap

Examples

The fisherman managed to ensnarl several fish in his net.
Her long hair would often ensnarl itself in the bristles of her brush.
The legal dispute could ensnarl the negotiations for months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entangle - to twist together or complicate

Etymology

From prefix 'en-' (make or put in) + 'snarl' (to entangle).
