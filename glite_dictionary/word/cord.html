cord

1. NOUN

/ kɔrd / (AmE), / kɔːd / (BrE)
A durable fabric with vertical ribs, usually made of cotton, used especially for making trousers and jackets.
fabric with vertical ribs

Examples

My new trousers are made from a soft brown cord that's perfect for autumn.
The tailor recommended cord as a sturdy option for workwear pants.
That classic jacket, cut from fine cord, has remained stylish for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corduroy - a heavy cotton fabric with raised ribs (cords)

2. NOUN

/ kɔrd / (AmE), / kɔːd / (BrE)
a unit for measuring cut firewood, equal to 128 cubic feet (about 3.62 cubic metres)
unit for measuring firewood

Examples

We ordered a cord of firewood to last through the winter.
The truck delivered three cords of wood to our cabin yesterday.
He stacked a full cord neatly beside the shed for easy access.

3. NOUN

/ kɔrd / (AmE), / kɔːd / (BrE)
a thin, flexible, insulated wire used to conduct electricity in household appliances or electronics
flexible electrical wire for household use

Examples

The lamp stopped working because its cord was damaged.
Please unplug the cord from the wall before moving the toaster.
She wrapped the long extension cord around a hook to keep it organized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cable - a thick, strong wire or group of wires covered in plastic, used to carry electricity or signals

5. NOUN

/ kɔrd / (AmE), / kɔːd / (BrE)
a thin, flexible length of twisted fibers or threads, used for tying, fastening, or connecting things
thin rope made of twisted fibers

Examples

He used a cord to tie the package securely.
The lamp wouldn't work because the cord was damaged.
She wore a small silver charm on a leather cord around her neck.

Synset Examples

the bundle was tied with a cord

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rope - a strong, thick line made of twisted fibers or threads
  • Degree Lower: string - a thin piece of cord used for tying or fastening

Etymology

From Latin 'chorda' (string, rope), from Greek 'khordē' (gut, string).
