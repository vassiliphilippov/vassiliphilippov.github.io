hypothesis

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɑθəsɪs / (AmE), / haɪˈpɒθəsɪs / (BrE)
an idea or explanation formed on the basis of incomplete evidence, serving as a starting point for further investigation or argument
idea based on limited evidence

Examples

The scientist presented a hypothesis to explain the strange experimental results.
Her hypothesis was later tested and proven true by further research.
We need more data to support or refute this hypothesis before we draw any conclusions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: theory - a system of ideas intended to explain something, usually supported by evidence
  • Degree Lower: guess - an estimate or conjecture made without sufficient information

Etymology

From Greek 'hupothesis' (foundation, supposition), from 'hupo-' (under) + 'thesis' (placing, proposition).

2. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɑːθəsɪs / (AmE), / haɪˈpɒθɪsɪs / (BrE)
a theory or explanation that is proposed as a starting point for further investigation, but has not yet been proved
proposed explanation, not yet proven

Examples

The scientist formulated a hypothesis to explain the unexpected results of the experiment.
Before conducting any analysis, you should state your main hypothesis clearly.
Her hypothesis was later confirmed by a series of independent studies.

Synset Examples

a scientific hypothesis that survives experimental testing becomes a scientific theory
he proposed a fresh theory of alkalis that later was accepted in chemical practices

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: theory - an explanation based on substantiated evidence and reasoning
  • Degree Lower: assumption - a thing that is accepted as true or as certain without proof
  • Synonym: conjecture - an opinion or conclusion formed on the basis of incomplete information

Etymology

From Greek 'hypothesis' (foundation, supposition), from 'hypo-' (under) + 'thesis' (placing).

3. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɑːθəsɪs / (AmE), / haɪˈpɒθəsɪs / (BrE)
An idea or explanation for something that is based on known facts but has not yet been proved.
proposed explanation; unproven idea

Examples

The scientist proposed a new hypothesis to explain the observed chemical reaction.
Before testing, the team developed a hypothesis based on previous experiments.
If the hypothesis is incorrect, the data will not support it.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: theory - A system of ideas intended to explain something, generally supported by evidence
  • Degree Lower: conjecture - An opinion or idea formed without sufficient evidence or proof

Etymology

From Greek hupothesis (foundation, supposition), from hupo- (under) + thesis (placing, proposition).
