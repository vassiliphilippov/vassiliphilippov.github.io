Myrrhis odorata

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪrɪs oʊˈdɔrətə / (AmE), / ˈmɪrɪs əʊˈdɔːrətə / (BrE)
A European herb with soft, fern-like leaves and white flowers, commonly known as sweet cicely.
European herb with fern-like leaves

Examples

Gardeners often plant Myrrhis odorata for its sweetly scented leaves and delicate white flowers.
The leaves of Myrrhis odorata can be used to flavor desserts and liqueurs.
A patch of Myrrhis odorata thrived in the shaded corner of the herb garden.

Etymology

From Latin 'Myrrhis' (the genus name) and 'odorata' meaning 'fragrant'.
