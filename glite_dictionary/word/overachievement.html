overachievement

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərəˈtʃivmənt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərəˈtʃiːvmənt / (BrE)
the act or result of achieving more or performing better than expected, especially in comparison with predicted abilities or standards
achievement beyond expected levels

Examples

The student’s overachievement surprised her teachers and classmates alike.
This year’s sales figures are a clear example of overachievement by the marketing team.
He was praised for his consistent overachievement despite limited resources.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underachievement - performance below expected ability or standards

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (beyond) + 'achievement'.
