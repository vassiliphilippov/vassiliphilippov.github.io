re-echo

1. NOUN

/ ˌriˈɛkoʊ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɛkəʊ / (BrE)
a repeated or secondary echo; an echo that follows another echo
a second or repeated echo

Examples

The re-echo of the footsteps made the hall seem endless.
A faint re-echo drifted back across the valley as the thunder faded.
The cave was so deep that every shout produced a persistent re-echo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - a sound reflected off a surface and heard again

Etymology

From re- (again) + echo, patterned on the verb form 'to re-echo'.
