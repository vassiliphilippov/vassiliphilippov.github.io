filoselle

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪləˈsɛl / (AmE), / ˌfɪləˈsɛl / (BrE)
a soft, loosely twisted silk thread used for embroidery and decorative purposes
soft silk embroidery thread

Examples

She chose a pale green filoselle to stitch the delicate leaves onto the fabric.
The antique sampler was embroidered with faded pink filoselle threads.
For her tapestry, she ordered several shades of filoselle from the specialist shop.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: silk - fine, strong, soft fiber produced by silkworms
  • Synonym: floss - loosely twisted thread used in embroidery

Etymology

From French 'filoselle', a diminutive of 'fil' (thread), originally from Latin 'filum'.
