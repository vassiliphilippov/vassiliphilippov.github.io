Nothosaurus

1. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊθoʊˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌnɒθəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of extinct marine reptiles from the Triassic period, belonging to the order Nothosauria
a genus of extinct Triassic marine reptiles

Examples

The fossils of Nothosaurus have been found in rocks dating back to the Triassic period.
Paleontologists study Nothosaurus to understand the evolution of marine reptiles.
A complete skeleton of Nothosaurus was discovered in Central Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nothosauria - an order of extinct marine reptiles

Etymology

From Greek nothos (false) + sauros (lizard).
