incense

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnsɛns / (AmE), / ˈɪnsɛns / (BrE)
a substance burned to produce a pleasant or fragrant smell, often used in religious ceremonies or for its aromatic effect
substance burned for fragrance

Examples

The sweet aroma of incense filled the temple during the ceremony.
She lit a stick of incense to create a calming atmosphere in her room.
Shops selling herbs and incense lined the narrow street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell

Etymology

From Latin 'incensum' (something burned), from 'incendere' (to set on fire).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14943145-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈɪnˌsɛns / (AmE), / ˈɪnsɛns / (BrE)
to perfume or make fragrant with incense, especially as part of a religious ceremony
to perfume with incense

Examples

The priest began to incense the altar with slow, deliberate movements.
They traditionally incense the temple before the evening prayers.
She watched as the acolyte incensed the room, filling it with fragrant smoke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfume - to make something smell pleasant, especially by applying a pleasant-smelling substance
  • Synonym: to fumigate - to apply smoke or fumes to an area, especially for purification

Etymology

From Latin 'incensare' (to burn), from 'incensum' (incense, that which is burned).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02131151-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌsɛns / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sens / (BrE)
a substance that is burned to produce a pleasant smell, often used in religious ceremonies or for fragrance.
fragrant substance burned for scent

Examples

The sweet aroma of incense wafted through the temple, creating a peaceful atmosphere.
She bought some jasmine incense to freshen the air in her apartment.
During the ceremony, the priest lit incense and let its smoke fill the room.

Synset Examples

incense filled the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell
  • Antonym: odor - a distinctive smell, especially an unpleasant one

Etymology

From Latin 'incensum' (that which is burnt), from 'incendere' (to set on fire).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05722692-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ɪnˈsɛns / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛns / (BrE)
to make someone extremely angry; to enrage
make very angry

Examples

The unfair accusation seemed to incense her even more.
His arrogant attitude can incense his colleagues at times.
It would incense the public if the truth were revealed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enrage - to make very angry
  • Synonym: to infuriate - to make someone extremely angry
  • Antonym: calm - to make less angry or agitated

Etymology

From Latin 'incensare', frequentative of 'incendere' (to set on fire).

Source: WordNet
