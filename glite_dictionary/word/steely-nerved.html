steely-nerved

1. ADJ

/ ˈstiːli nɝvd / (AmE), / ˈstiːli nɜːvd / (BrE)
having great courage, determination, and self-control, especially in difficult situations
showing strong courage and composure

Examples

The steely-nerved firefighter entered the burning building without hesitation.
Even in the face of mounting pressure, the CEO remained steely-nerved.
Her steely-nerved response impressed everyone in the courtroom.

Synset Examples

Only a steely-nerved individual could handle that crisis.
She remained steely-nerved during the intense negotiations.
His steely-nerved resolve made him a great leader.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courageous - having or showing courage
  • Synonym: resolute - admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering

Etymology

Formed from 'steely' (resembling steel in firmness) and 'nerve' (courage or composure).
