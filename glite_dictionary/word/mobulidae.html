Mobulidae

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈbjuːlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / məʊˈbjuːlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large rays lacking venomous spines, including manta rays.
family of large rays (mantas, etc.)

Examples

Scientists observed a Mobulidae gliding gracefully near the coral reef.
The Mobulidae are known for their large size and lack of venomous spines.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect members of the Mobulidae family from overfishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manta ray - a large ray belonging to the Mobulidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Mobulidae, based on the genus Mobula + -idae (denoting family in zoological taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ moʊˈbjuːlɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / məʊˈbjuːlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large rays lacking venomous spines, including mantas and devil rays.
family of large rays (mantas, devil rays)

Examples

The Mobulidae family includes the giant manta ray, one of the ocean's most iconic creatures.
Researchers are studying the migratory patterns of Mobulidae to better protect these vulnerable rays.
Unlike their relatives, Mobulidae rays do not possess venomous spines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantas - large rays belonging to Mobulidae, known as manta rays

Etymology

From Modern Latin Mobulidae, based on Mobula (genus name for mantas and devil rays).
