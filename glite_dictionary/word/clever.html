clever

1. ADJ

/ ˈklɛvɚ / (AmE), / ˈklɛvə / (BrE)
quick to invent or apply new ideas and solutions, especially with skill or ingenuity.
inventive and skillful

Examples

The engineer designed a clever device that saves energy effortlessly.
She came up with a clever solution to a difficult puzzle.
His clever use of recycled materials impressed the judges.

Synset Examples

a clever gadget
the cunning maneuvers leading to his success
an ingenious solution to the problem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingenious - very clever and inventive
  • Antonym: stupid - lacking intelligence; foolish
  • Synonym: resourceful - good at finding ways to solve problems

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'cliver' (nimble, skillful).

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈklɛvɚ / (AmE), / ˈklɛvə / (BrE)
quick to understand, learn, and devise or apply ideas; intelligent and resourceful.
mentally quick and resourceful

Examples

She is so clever that she always finds solutions to the hardest problems.
Only a clever student could have figured out that riddle so quickly.
The clever fox managed to escape from the trap.

Synset Examples

an apt pupil
you are a clever man … you reason well and your wit is bold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligent - having or showing intelligence; clever; smart
  • Antonym: stupid - lacking intelligence or common sense

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *cliver* (nimble, skillful), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01337910-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈklɛvɚ / (AmE), / ˈklɛv.ə / (BrE)
showing skillful, shrewd, or crafty behavior, especially in achieving one's goals or self-interest
shrewd or crafty in achieving goals

Examples

The clever negotiator managed to secure a better deal than anyone expected.
Her clever handling of the situation minimized the risks for the company.
He was too clever to fall for their obvious tricks.

Synset Examples

a cagey lawyer
too clever to be sound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cagey - cautious or shrewd, especially in dealing with others
  • Synonym: crafty - clever, especially in a deceptive or underhand way
  • Antonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or shrewdness; easily deceived

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'cliver' (nimble, skillful), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
