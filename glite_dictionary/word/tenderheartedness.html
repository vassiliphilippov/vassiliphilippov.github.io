tenderheartedness

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛndɚˈhɑrtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌtɛndəhˈhɑːtɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being kind, gentle, and easily moved by the feelings or suffering of others
compassionate and gentle nature

Examples

Her tenderheartedness made her the first to offer help to those in distress.
The novel explores the tenderheartedness of its main characters in difficult times.
Despite his gruff exterior, he was known for his deep tenderheartedness toward animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassion - sympathetic concern for others' suffering
  • Antonym: hardheartedness - lack of sympathy or compassion

Etymology

Formed from 'tenderhearted' + '-ness', with 'tender' from Middle English via Old French, ultimately Latin 'tener' (soft, delicate).
