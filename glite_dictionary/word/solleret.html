solleret

1. NOUN

/ sɑːˈlɛrɪt / (AmE), / sɒlˈɛrɪt / (BrE)
A piece of armor consisting of protective plates for the foot, often featuring mail with a solid toe and heel, used in medieval times.
armored plate for the foot (medieval)
archaic

Examples

The knight's sollerets clanked loudly with each step he took across the stone floor.
Restorers studied the distinctive shape of the sollerets before repairing the ancient suit of armor.
A full set of plate armor includes helmets, gauntlets, and sollerets to protect the feet.

Etymology

From French ‘solleret’, diminutive of ‘soulier’ (shoe).
