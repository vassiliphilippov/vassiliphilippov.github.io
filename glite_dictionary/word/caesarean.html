caesarean

1. ADJ

/ siˈzɛriən / (AmE), / siˈzɛːrɪən / (BrE)
relating to the surgical delivery of a baby through an incision in the mother's abdomen and uterus.
relating to surgical abdominal delivery
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a caesarean delivery due to complications during labor.
A caesarean birth can be scheduled or performed as an emergency.
Her recovery after the caesarean operation was smooth and uncomplicated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caesarean section - surgical procedure to deliver a baby through abdominal incision

Etymology

From Latin 'Caesareus', supposedly from the birth of Julius Caesar by this method, though this is disputed.

2. NOUN

/ siˈzɛəriən / (AmE), / siˈzɛəriən / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a baby is delivered through an incision made in the mother's abdominal wall and uterus
surgical delivery of a baby
medical

Examples

After a long labor, the doctors recommended a caesarean to ensure the safety of mother and child.
The hospital has a specialized team for performing caesarean deliveries.
Her first child was born by caesarean, but her second was a natural delivery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cesarean - alternate spelling of caesarean (surgical birth)

Etymology

From Latin 'caesareus', associated with the belief that Julius Caesar was born by this method.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00186627-n
Synset POS: n
