chasteness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæstnəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːstnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being simple, pure, or without ornamentation
simplicity or purity of style

Examples

The chasteness of the furnishings gave the house an understated elegance.
Many admired the chasteness of the poem's language, free from excessive imagery.
The designer preferred chasteness in her work, avoiding unnecessary embellishment.

Synset Examples

the room was simply decorated with great restraint

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simplicity - the quality of being simple or uncomplicated
  • Antonym: ornamentation - the use of decorative details in design

Etymology

From Latin 'castus' (pure, chaste) via Old French 'chaste' and Middle English.
