faddy

1. ADJ

/ ˈfædi / (AmE), / ˈfædi / (BrE)
(of ideas, styles, etc.) intensely fashionable for a short time; characteristic of a passing fad.
fashionable for a short time

Examples

That style of hat is quite faddy and probably won't last until next season.
The restaurant's menu is a bit too faddy for my traditional tastes.
Fashion is often faddy, with trends changing every few months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trendy - very fashionable and up to date
  • Antonym: timeless - not affected by the passage of time or changes in fashion

Etymology

From 'fad' (a short-lived fashion or craze) + '-y' (adjectival suffix).
