mockingbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑkɪŋˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈmɒkɪŋˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a long-tailed, gray-and-white songbird of the southern United States, noted for its ability to mimic the songs of other birds
songbird able to mimic other birds

Examples

A mockingbird sat on the fence, imitating the calls of nearby sparrows.
The distinctive song of the mockingbird filled the warm summer air.
We watched a mockingbird repeat the tunes of at least three different species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrasher - a songbird related to the mockingbird

Etymology

Formed from 'mocking' (imitating) + 'bird'.
