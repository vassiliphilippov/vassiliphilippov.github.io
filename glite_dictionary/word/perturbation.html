perturbation

1. NOUN

/ pərˌtɜrˈbeɪʃən / (AmE), / pəːˌtɜːˈbeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Physics) A secondary influence or disturbance that causes a system to deviate slightly from its normal or original state.
minor influence causing deviation in a system

Examples

The orbit of the satellite was affected by a small perturbation from the moon's gravity.
Scientists studied the perturbation of the system caused by the external electromagnetic field.
A minor perturbation in the atmosphere caused unexpected weather changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturbance - an interruption of a settled condition; a disturbance in a system

Etymology

From Latin perturbatio (confusion, disturbance), from perturbare (to disturb).
