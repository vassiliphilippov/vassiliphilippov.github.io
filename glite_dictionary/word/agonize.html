to agonize

1. VERB

/ ˈæɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to cause someone to suffer intense mental or emotional pain; to distress greatly
cause intense mental distress

Examples

The constant uncertainty began to agonize her, making each day harder than the last.
He did not mean to agonize his friends by withholding the truth, but he wanted to protect them.
The decision to leave her family behind continued to agonize her long after she moved away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torture - to inflict severe mental or physical pain
  • Antonym: to soothe - to calm or reduce distress

Etymology

From Greek agōnizesthai (to contend, struggle), from agōn (contest).

2. VERB

/ ˈæɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to experience intense mental or physical pain or anguish; to suffer agony
suffer intense pain or anguish

Examples

She would agonize for days over making even the smallest decision.
Many parents agonize about whether they are raising their children properly.
He agonized through the long night, unable to sleep because of his worries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suffer - to experience pain or distress
  • Synonym: worry - to feel anxious or troubled
  • Antonym: to rejoice - to feel or show great joy

Etymology

From Greek agonizesthai (to contend for a prize), from agōn (contest).
