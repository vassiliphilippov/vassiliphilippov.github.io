IgM

1. NOUN

/ aɪ dʒiː ˈɛm / (AmE), / aɪ dʒiː ˈɛm / (BrE)
a class of immunoglobulins that is involved in the early stages of immune response, especially fighting blood infections and triggering production of immunoglobulin G
a type of antibody in the immune system
medical

Examples

Doctors test for IgM antibodies to determine if an infection is recent.
A high level of IgM in the blood may indicate an acute infection.
IgM is the first antibody produced by the body in response to a new infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: IgG - a class of immunoglobulins providing the majority of antibody-based immunity
  • Synonym: antibody - a protein produced by immune system to fight pathogens

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'immunoglobulin M'.
