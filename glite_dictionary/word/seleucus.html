Seleucus

1. PROPN

/ sɪˈluːkəs / (AmE), / sɪˈljuːkəs / (BrE)
A Macedonian general who accompanied Alexander the Great into Asia and founded the Seleucid dynasty, ruling much of Asia Minor until 65 BC.
founder of the Seleucid dynasty, Macedonian general

Examples

Seleucus played a pivotal role in the partitioning of Alexander the Great's empire.
The Seleucid Empire was named after its founder, Seleucus.
Ancient historians often debated the strategies used by Seleucus during his campaigns.

Etymology

From Greek Σελεύκος (Seleukos), a personal name.
