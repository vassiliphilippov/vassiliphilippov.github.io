pianistic

1. ADJ

/ piː.əˈnɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / piː.əˈnɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to the piano or characteristic of piano playing; skilled at or adapted for the piano
relating to piano or piano playing

Examples

Her pianistic technique impressed the entire audience during the recital.
The composer’s work is noted for its challenging pianistic passages.
He possesses remarkable pianistic abilities for someone his age.

Synset Examples

pianistic abilities

Etymology

From piano + -istic, modeled after similar adjective formations.

2. ADJ

/ pi.əˈnɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / pi.əˈnɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to the piano or to the technique of playing the piano
relating to the piano

Examples

The composer's later works displayed a highly pianistic approach, exploiting the instrument's full range.
Her pianistic skills were evident during the concert's difficult passages.
This piece is famous for its intricate pianistic effects and technical demands.

Etymology

From 'piano' + '-istic'.
