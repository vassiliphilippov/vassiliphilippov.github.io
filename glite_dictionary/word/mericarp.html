mericarp

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛrɪˌkɑrp / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪˌkɑːp / (BrE)
(Botany) One of the segments into which a split fruit (schizocarp) separates at maturity, each typically containing a single seed.
segment of a split fruit, usually one seed

Examples

Each mericarp contains a single seed and separates from its partner at maturity.
The scientist examined the mericarp to understand how seeds disperse from the plant.
In fennel, the fruit splits into two mericarps when ripe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schizocarp - a dry fruit that splits into single-seeded parts when ripe

Etymology

From Greek meros (part) + karpos (fruit).
