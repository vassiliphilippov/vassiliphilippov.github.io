Brama raii

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːmə ˈraɪ.i / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːmə ˈraɪ.i / (BrE)
a deep-bodied, sooty-black pelagic spiny-finned fish found in the northern Atlantic and northern Pacific, valued as food
species of edible spiny-finned fish

Examples

Fishermen reported catching several Brama raii off the coast this season.
The chef prepared a delicious dish using freshly caught Brama raii.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of Brama raii in the northern Pacific.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name Brama and specific epithet raii, honoring the naturalist Ray.
