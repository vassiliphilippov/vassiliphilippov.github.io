naming

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈneɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of identifying someone or something by name
the act of giving a name

Examples

The teacher asked for the naming of all the countries in South America.
His job involved the naming of new pharmaceutical products.
She hesitated during the naming of each person in the photograph.

Synset Examples

the part he failed was the naming of state capitals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: designation - the act of choosing a name or title for someone or something
  • Synonym: identification - the act of recognizing and naming someone or something
  • Antonym: unnaming - the act of removing or withholding a name

Etymology

From the verb 'name' + -ing, Middle English 'namen' from Old English 'namian'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈneɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈneɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of appointing someone to a position or role, especially one that is not filled by election
the act of appointing to a position

Examples

The committee's naming of a new chair was met with broad approval.
His sudden naming as director surprised many of his colleagues.
The naming of members to the advisory board is based on their areas of expertise.

Synset Examples

the appointment had to be approved by the whole committee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appointment - the act of assigning a job or position to someone

Etymology

From name (verb) + -ing, denoting the action of naming or appointing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00164600-n
Synset POS: n
