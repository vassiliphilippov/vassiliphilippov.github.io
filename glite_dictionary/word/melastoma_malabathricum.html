Melastoma malabathricum

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈstoʊmə ˌmæləbəˈθrɪkəm / (AmE), / ˌmɛləˈstəʊmə ˌmæləbəˈθrɪkəm / (BrE)
An evergreen spreading shrub of India and southeastern Asia, belonging to the Melastomaceae family, characterized by large purple flowers.
evergreen shrub of Asia with purple flowers

Examples

The garden featured a rare Melastoma malabathricum with its vibrant purple blooms.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Melastoma malabathricum found in Southeast Asia.
Botanists identified the spreading shrub as Melastoma malabathricum due to its distinctive flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Melastoma' from Greek 'melas' (black) and 'stoma' (mouth), referring to staining properties of the fruit.
