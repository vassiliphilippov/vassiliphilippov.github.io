hydrocephalus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrə(ʊ)ˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which excess cerebrospinal fluid accumulates in the ventricles of the brain, often leading to increased pressure and swelling, especially in infants.
condition involving excess brain fluid
medical

Examples

The child's enlarged head was diagnosed as a sign of hydrocephalus.
Modern surgical techniques can greatly improve outcomes for patients with hydrocephalus.
Infants with untreated hydrocephalus may experience rapid growth of the head and neurological problems.

Etymology

From Greek hydro- (water) + kephalē (head).
