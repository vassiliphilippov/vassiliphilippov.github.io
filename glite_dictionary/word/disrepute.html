disrepute

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.rɪˈpjut / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.rɪˈpjuːt / (BrE)
the state of being regarded with disfavor or low esteem; a bad reputation
state of having a bad reputation

Examples

The company fell into disrepute after the corruption scandal was revealed.
Following the incident, his name was dragged into disrepute.
The school worked hard to recover from years of disrepute in the community.

Synset Examples

your actions will bring discredit to your name
because of the scandal, the school has fallen into disrepute

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgrace - loss of respect and honor; shame
  • Antonym: repute - the state of having a good reputation

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'reputare' (to think over, reckon).
