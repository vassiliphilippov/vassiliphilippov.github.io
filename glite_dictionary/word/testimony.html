testimony

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛstəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / ˈtɛstɪməni / (BrE)
A formal written or spoken statement, especially one given in a court of law, offering evidence or proof about a fact.
formal statement as evidence

Examples

The witness gave her testimony before the judge and jury during the trial.
His testimony was crucial in proving the defendant's innocence.
According to his testimony, he was at home on the night of the incident.

Synset Examples

according to his own testimony he can't do it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evidence - information or objects that support the truth of something, especially in court
  • Antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true

Etymology

From Latin testimonium (evidence, witness), from testis (witness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛstəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / ˈtɛstɪməni / (BrE)
a formal written or spoken statement, especially one made under oath in a court of law
statement made under oath

Examples

The witness gave her testimony calmly before the entire court.
His testimony was crucial in convicting the defendant of the crime.
The jury listened intently to the testimony of each expert called to the stand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evidence - the available body of facts or information indicating whether a belief or proposition is true

Etymology

From Latin 'testimonium' (evidence, witness), from 'testis' (witness).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɛstəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / ˈtɛstɪməni / (BrE)
something that shows or proves that a particular fact is true or exists; evidence of a fact or quality.
evidence or proof of something

Examples

Her constant support is a clear testimony to their close friendship.
The award is testimony to his years of hard work and dedication.
Every achievement stands as testimony to her perseverance and talent.

Synset Examples

his effort was testimony to his devotion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evidence - facts or information that show something is true
  • Synonym: proof - evidence or argument establishing a fact or the truth of a statement

Etymology

From Latin 'testimonium' (evidence, proof), from 'testis' (witness).
