communication

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of conveying information, ideas, or feelings to others, especially by speaking, writing, or using some other medium
the act of conveying information

Examples

Clear communication is essential for effective teamwork in any organization.
The company improved its internal communication with regular staff meetings.
He waited for official communication from the embassy before making travel plans.

Synset Examples

they could not act without official communication from Moscow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correspondence - written communication, especially letters exchanged

Etymology

From Latin communicatio (a sharing), from communicare (to share).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06262268-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ kəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a message or information that is conveyed from one person or group to another
a message conveyed between people or groups

Examples

The manager issued a communication to all staff regarding the new policy.
Please read this important communication from the human resources department.
They received an urgent communication from their overseas partners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: message - a verbal, written, or recorded communication sent to or left for a recipient

Etymology

From Latin communicatio (a sharing, imparting), from communicare (to share).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00033319-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌkəˌmjuː.nəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kəˌmjuː.nɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a means of connecting people or places, such as a passage, channel, or system that allows access or the exchange of goods, people, or information
means of connection or access

Examples

The engineer designed an underground tunnel to serve as communication between the two buildings.
During the blackout, the city lost all communication with the outside world.
A narrow staircase provided communication from the cellar to the kitchen.

Synset Examples

how many lines of communication can there be among four people?
a secret passageway provided communication between the two rooms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: channel - a route or means of communication or access

Etymology

From Latin communicatio (sharing, imparting).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
