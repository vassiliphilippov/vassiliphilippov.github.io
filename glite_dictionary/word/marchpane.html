marchpane

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrtʃˌpeɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtʃˌpeɪn / (BrE)
A sweet confection made of almond paste and sugar, sometimes with egg whites, popular in historical English cooking.
almond paste confection
archaic

Examples

At the banquet, silver plates were stacked high with marchpane shaped into tiny castles.
She learned to craft exquisite marchpane roses for the queen's table.
The recipe called for ground almonds, sugar, and rose water to create the perfect marchpane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marzipan - a confection of almond paste and sugar, commonly used today

Etymology

From Italian 'marzapane' (marzipan); possibly via Old French 'marcepain'.
