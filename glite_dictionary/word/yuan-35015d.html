yuan

1. NOUN

/ juˈɑn / (AmE), / juˈæn / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in China, equal to 10 jiao or 100 fen
Chinese currency unit

Examples

She exchanged her dollars for Chinese yuan before traveling to Beijing.
The price of the souvenir was 20 yuan at the local market.
Many international stores now accept payment in yuan as well as other major currencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renminbi - the official currency of the People's Republic of China
  • Antonym: yen - the basic unit of money in Japan

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese 元 (yuán), meaning 'origin' or 'unit'.
