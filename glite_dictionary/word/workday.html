workday

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːk.deɪ / (BrE)
A day on which work is usually done, especially a day that is not a weekend or holiday.
a day when work is done

Examples

My typical workday starts at 8 a.m. and ends at 5 p.m.
He prefers to leave early to avoid traffic on his workday.
Many people find it hard to exercise after a long workday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weekday - a day of the week except Saturday or Sunday
  • Antonym: weekend - Saturday and Sunday, when most people do not work
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːk.deɪ / (BrE)
the amount of time in a day that an employee is required or expected to work, usually for a set wage
required daily working time

Examples

Her workday typically starts at 9 a.m. and ends at 5 p.m.
Due to new regulations, the standard workday has been reduced to seven hours.
During the busy season, his workday often extends into the evening.

Synset Examples

they work an 8-hour day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shift - a scheduled period of work within a workday

Etymology

From 'work' + 'day', signifying a day devoted to labor.
