Empirin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmpərɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛmpərɪn / (BrE)
A former trademark for a commercial preparation of aspirin, used as a pain reliever and to reduce fever or inflammation.
trademarked aspirin-based pain reliever

Examples

My grandmother used to take Empirin whenever she had a headache or muscle pain.
The doctor recommended switching from Empirin to a different type of pain reliever for her condition.
Although Empirin is less common now, it was once a popular over-the-counter medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspirin - a common painkiller, antipyretic, and anti-inflammatory drug
