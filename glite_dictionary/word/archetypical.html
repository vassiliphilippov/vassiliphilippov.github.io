archetypical

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːrkɪˈtɪpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈtɪpɪkəl / (BrE)
very typical of a certain kind of person or thing; representing an original model or type after which others are patterned.
characteristic of an original model

Examples

The story contains archetypical themes of good versus evil.
Many fairy tales feature archetypical characters like the wicked stepmother.
He played the archetypical hero, brave and selfless throughout the novel.

Synset Examples

archetypal patterns
she was the prototypal student activist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archetypal - very typical of an original type; same as 'archetypical'
  • Synonym: prototypical - having the nature of a prototype; serving as a typical example

Etymology

From Greek 'arkhetupon' (original pattern) via Medieval Latin 'archetypicus'.
