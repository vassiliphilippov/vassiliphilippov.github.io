Hystricidae

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈstrɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / hɪˈstrɪsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large Old World rodents known as Old World porcupines, characterized by spiny coats for protection.
family of Old World porcupines

Examples

The Hystricidae are distinguished from their New World counterparts by differences in skull structure and quill arrangement.
Members of the Hystricidae family inhabit Africa, southern Europe, and parts of Asia.
Unlike the North American porcupines, Hystricidae have shorter, stouter bodies and larger quills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porcupine - spiny-coated rodent of family Hystricidae or Erethizontidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'hystríx' (porcupine) + '-idae' (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
