Anthocerotaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθoʊˌsɛrəˈteɪsi.i / (AmE), / ˌanθəʊˌsɛrəˈtiːsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of hornworts, small non-vascular plants found in damp places, characterized by a horn-shaped spore-producing structure
a family of hornwort plants

Examples

Anthocerotaceae are commonly found growing on moist soils in temperate regions.
Scientists have studied the reproductive cycle of several Anthocerotaceae species in the laboratory.
The distinguishing feature of Anthocerotaceae is their horn-shaped sporophyte.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hornwort - a group of non-vascular plants with horn-shaped sporophytes

Etymology

From the genus name Anthoceros (Greek anthos 'flower' + keras 'horn') and the family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌænθoʊˌsɛrəˈteɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌanθəʊˌsɛrəˈteɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of non-vascular plants known as hornworts, typically found in damp habitats.
family of hornworts (plants)

Examples

The Anthocerotaceae are often studied for their simple structure and evolutionary significance.
Hornworts belonging to the Anthocerotaceae family can be found in many moist tropical and temperate environments.
Researchers discovered several new species within the Anthocerotaceae during their fieldwork.

Etymology

From Greek anthos (flower) + keras, kerat- (horn), referring to the hornlike shape of the sporophyte.
