to depend

1. VERB

/ dɪˈpɛnd / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛnd / (BrE)
to trust or have confidence in someone or something; to rely on for help or support
trust or rely on someone or something

Examples

You can always depend on Sarah to keep her promises.
During tough times, I depend on my friends for emotional support.
The success of this project will depend on everybody working together.

Synset Examples

you can count on me to help you any time
Look to your friends for support
You can bet on that!
Depend on your family in times of crisis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rely - to depend on with full trust or confidence
  • Synonym: to count on - to trust someone to do something or be reliable
  • Antonym: distrust - to doubt the honesty or reliability of someone

Etymology

From Latin 'dependere' (to hang down, be dependent on), from 'de-' (down) + 'pendere' (to hang).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈpɛnd / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛnd / (BrE)
to be determined or decided by something else; to be contingent upon conditions or circumstances
to be contingent on other factors

Examples

Whether we go to the beach tomorrow will depend on the weather forecast.
Success in this job can depend on your ability to communicate clearly.
The final choice may depend on who is available at the time.

Synset Examples

That depends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hinge (on) - to depend entirely on; to be determined by
  • Synonym: rely (on) - to depend on with confidence or trust
  • Antonym: be independent (of) - not depending on something or someone else

Etymology

From Latin dependere, from de- ‘down’ + pendere ‘to hang’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02670312-v
Synset POS: v
