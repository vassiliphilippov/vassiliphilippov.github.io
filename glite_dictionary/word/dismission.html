dismission

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / dɪsˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of officially dismissing someone from a position or employment, especially through a formal notice
official dismissal from a job

Examples

After his sudden dismission, he struggled to find new employment.
The company issued a formal letter of dismission to its employees.
Her dismission from the board came as a shock to everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dismissal - the act of removing someone from employment

Etymology

From Latin 'dimissio' (dismissal, sending away), from 'dimittere' (to send away).

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˈmɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of dismissing someone from their job or position; termination of employment
termination of employment

Examples

The company announced the dismission of several employees due to restructuring.
His sudden dismission left many colleagues shocked and uncertain.
During the meeting, the manager explained the reasons for each dismission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dismissal - the act of removing someone from employment; being fired
  • Antonym: appointment - the act of assigning someone to a position or job

Etymology

From Latin 'dismissionem', meaning 'a sending away, dismissal'.
