Jaghatai

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑːɡəˈtaɪ / (AmE), / dʒaɡəˈtʌɪ / (BrE)
a Turkic literary language used in medieval Central Asia, named after one of the sons of Genghis Khan
Turkic literary language of medieval Central Asia

Examples

Much classical Central Asian poetry was written in Jaghatai.
The Jaghatai language served as a literary standard for many centuries.
Modern Uzbek has roots in Jaghatai, which influenced its vocabulary and grammar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkic - a branch of the Altaic language family

Etymology

From Chaghatai, the name of a son of Genghis Khan, after whom the language was named.
