psilosis

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / saɪˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
A chronic disorder affecting nutrient absorption, characterized by foul-smelling diarrhea and emaciation, occurring in both children and adults in tropical and non-tropical regions.
malabsorption disorder causing diarrhea and emaciation
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with psilosis after months of unexplained weight loss and severe diarrhea.
In children, early recognition of psilosis can prevent serious complications related to malnutrition.
Doctors in both tropical and non-tropical regions report cases of psilosis among adults and children.

Etymology

From Greek psilōsis (becoming bald or bare), later applied to loss or wasting in a medical context.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / saɪˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical term for the loss or shedding of hair, especially as a symptom of disease.
loss or shedding of hair (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with psilosis after several months of unexplained hair loss.
Chronic stress is sometimes associated with psilosis in both men and women.
Some nutritional deficiencies can result in temporary psilosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alopecia - medical term for hair loss

Etymology

From Greek psilōsis (becoming bare), from psilos (bare, bald).
