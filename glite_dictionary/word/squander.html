to squander

1. VERB

/ ˈskwɑːndɚ / (AmE), / ˈskwɒndə / (BrE)
to waste something, especially money or time, in a reckless or foolish manner
waste recklessly, especially money or time

Examples

He managed to squander his entire inheritance in less than two years.
Don't squander your opportunities by being careless.
She regretted the way she had squandered her money during her youth.

Synset Examples

waste not, want not

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waste - to use or expend carelessly, extravagantly, or to no purpose
  • Antonym: save - to keep safe or to avoid spending or using something unnecessarily

Etymology

Probably from obsolete English "squander" (to scatter), origin uncertain, possibly related to "squander" meaning to scatter abroad.

2. VERB

/ ˈskwɑndɚ / (AmE), / ˈskwɒndə / (BrE)
to spend or use something wastefully or recklessly, especially money, opportunities, or resources
spend or use wastefully

Examples

If you squander your money on unnecessary things, you might regret it later.
She squandered her chance to study abroad by missing the application deadline.
Despite his talent, he squanders every opportunity that comes his way.

Synset Examples

He wasted his inheritance on his insincere friends
You squandered the opportunity to get an advanced degree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waste - to use carelessly, extravagantly, or to no purpose
  • Antonym: conserve - to protect from loss, waste, or harm; to use sparingly

Etymology

Probably from obsolete English 'squander' (to scatter), of uncertain origin.
