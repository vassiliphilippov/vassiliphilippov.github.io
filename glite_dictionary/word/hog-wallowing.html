to hog-wallowing

1. VERB

/ hɑɡ ˈwɑloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / hɒɡ ˈwɒləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to roll or lie about in mud, especially in a messy or carefree way.
rolling or lying in mud messily

Examples

After the rainstorm, the children were hog-wallowing in the muddy backyard.
The football players ended up hog-wallowing after sliding through the wet field.
The piglets spent the afternoon hog-wallowing under the farmer's watchful eye.

Synset Examples

They found the pigs hog-wallowing in the muddy pen.
The kids were hog-wallowing in the backyard after the storm.
He spent the day hog-wallowing in the mud after the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallow - to roll about in mud or water, often for pleasure

Etymology

From 'hog' (pig) + 'wallow' (to roll about in mud).
