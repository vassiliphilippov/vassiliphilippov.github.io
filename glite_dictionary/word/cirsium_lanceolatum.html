Cirsium lanceolatum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜːr.si.əm ˌlæn.si.oʊˈleɪ.təm / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.si.əm ˌlan.si.əˈleɪ.təm / (BrE)
A European thistle with large heads and prickly leaves, often considered a weed and widely naturalized in the United States.
European thistle species; prickly weed

Examples

Cirsium lanceolatum commonly invades pastures, reducing their value for grazing livestock.
Gardeners often struggle to remove Cirsium lanceolatum due to its deep, spreading roots.
The prickly leaves of Cirsium lanceolatum make it easily distinguishable from other weeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creeping thistle - Common name for Cirsium lanceolatum
  • Degree Higher: thistle - Prickly plants of the family Asteraceae

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cirsium' (a genus of thistles), 'lanceolatum' (lance-shaped).
