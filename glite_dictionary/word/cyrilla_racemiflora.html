Cyrilla racemiflora

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈrɪlə reɪˌsiːmɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪlə rəˌsiːmɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States, the West Indies, and Brazil, grown for its slender clusters of white flowers and orange to crimson foliage
flowering shrub or small tree species

Examples

The garden's wetland area features a beautiful Cyrilla racemiflora in full bloom.
Many landscapers appreciate Cyrilla racemiflora for its vivid autumn foliage and delicate white flowers.
Cyrilla racemiflora thrives in moist, acidic soils common to southern coastal regions.

Etymology

From genus name Cyrilla, named after the French botanist Cyrille Laporte, and Latin racemiflora meaning 'with raceme flowers.'
