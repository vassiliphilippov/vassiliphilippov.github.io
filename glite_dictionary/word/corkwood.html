corkwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrkˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈkɔːkˌwʊd / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree or shrub of damp areas in the southeastern United States, noted for its exceptionally light wood
light-wooded tree/shrub from SE US

Examples

The extremely light wood of the corkwood makes it valuable for certain craft uses.
We spotted a rare corkwood growing near the wetlands during our hike.
Many birds nest in the branches of the corkwood tree.
