hallmark

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔlˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈhɔːlˌmɑːk / (BrE)
a distinctive feature or characteristic that is typical of a person, place, or thing
distinctive feature or characteristic

Examples

Attention to detail is the hallmark of a good craftsman.
Honesty was the hallmark of her leadership style.
The hallmark of the brand is its simple, elegant design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trait - a distinguishing quality or characteristic
  • Synonym: feature - a distinctive attribute or aspect

Etymology

Originally from the mark stamped at Goldsmiths’ Hall in London, certifying gold and silver articles.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔlˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈhɔːlˌmɑːk / (BrE)
an official mark stamped on articles of gold, silver, or platinum to certify their origin and purity; more generally, a mark indicating authenticity or origin
official mark certifying origin or authenticity

Examples

The silver teapot bears a hallmark that guarantees its authenticity.
Every genuine product from the company has a unique hallmark stamped on it.
She checked the jewelry for a hallmark before agreeing to buy it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stamp - an official mark indicating authenticity or approval
  • Synonym: brand - a mark indicating ownership or origin

Etymology

From Goldsmiths' Hall in London, where gold and silver articles were tested and marked for purity.
