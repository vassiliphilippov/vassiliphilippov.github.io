Turnicidae

1. NOUN

/ tɜːrˈnɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / tɜːˈnɪsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small Old World birds, known as buttonquails, which resemble true quail but are not closely related to them.
family of small Old World birds: buttonquails

Examples

Members of the Turnicidae family are often mistaken for true quail due to their similar appearance.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Turnicidae in Southeast Asia.
Unlike quail, Turnicidae exhibit unusual breeding habits where females compete for mates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttonquail - small ground-dwelling bird of the family Turnicidae
  • Antonym: quail - small, short-tailed game bird; superficially similar but unrelated to Turnicidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Turnix.

2. NOUN

/ tɜrˈnɪsɪˌdi / (AmE), / tɜːˈnɪsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of small Old World birds, known as buttonquails, that resemble but are not closely related to true quails
family of birds known as buttonquails

Examples

The Turnicidae are often mistaken for true quails due to their similar appearance.
Scientists have found that Turnicidae are more closely related to shorebirds than to quails.
Members of the Turnicidae family are distributed across Africa, Asia, and Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttonquail - small ground-dwelling bird of the family Turnicidae
  • Antonym: quail - small game bird, not closely related to buttonquails

Etymology

From modern Latin Turnicidae, from Turnix (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
