scampi

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæm.pi / (AmE), / ˈskam.pi / (BrE)
A dish of large shrimp sautéed in oil or butter and garlic, often served as an appetizer or main course.
shrimp dish sautéed in garlic butter

Examples

I ordered scampi with a side of pasta at the restaurant.
The chef prepared the scampi with fresh garlic and parsley.
She loves making scampi at home for special occasions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrimp scampi - dish of shrimp cooked in garlic butter sauce

Etymology

From Italian 'scampi', plural of 'scampo', a type of edible lobster.
