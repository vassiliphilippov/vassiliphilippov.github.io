Uncompahgre Peak

1. PROPN

/ ˌʌnkəmˈpɑːɡreɪ piːk / (AmE), / ˌʌnkəmˈpɑːɡreɪ piːk / (BrE)
the highest mountain in the San Juan Mountains of southwestern Colorado, reaching 14,309 feet (4,361 m) in elevation
mountain peak in Colorado, USA

Examples

Climbing Uncompahgre Peak is a favorite challenge for experienced hikers visiting southwestern Colorado.
The sunrise over Uncompahgre Peak was truly breathtaking and well worth the early start.
From a distance, the distinctive summit of Uncompahgre Peak dominates the skyline of the San Juan Mountains.

Etymology

Derived from the Uncompahgre Ute term meaning 'hot water spring,' adopted as the mountain's name.
