south-southwest

1. ADV

/ ˌsaʊθ ˌsaʊθˈwɛst / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθ ˌsaʊθˈwɛst / (BrE)
toward, to, or in the direction midway between south and southwest
in a direction between south and southwest

Examples

They set sail south-southwest to avoid the storm.
The road curves gently south-southwest after the village.
The birds migrate south-southwest in early autumn.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: south - the cardinal direction to the right of east and to the left of west
  • Degree Higher: southwest - the direction halfway between south and west

2. NOUN

/ ˌsaʊθ saʊθˈwɛst / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθ saʊθˈwɛst / (BrE)
The compass point midway between south and southwest, at an angle of 202°30′ clockwise from due north.
compass point between south and southwest

Examples

The wind shifted and began blowing from the south-southwest early in the evening.
Sailors adjusted their course to steer toward the south-southwest as directed.
The weather report indicated strong gusts coming from the south-southwest tomorrow.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: south - the direction opposite to north on a compass
  • Degree Higher: southwest - the compass point midway between south and west

Etymology

Formed by combining 'south' and 'southwest'.
