grimness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪmnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪmnəs / (BrE)
the harsh or severe quality of something that is difficult or unpleasant to endure
harsh or severe quality; difficulty to endure

Examples

The grimness of the landscape made travel in winter a real challenge.
He was struck by the grimness of her expression as she delivered the bad news.
Despite the grimness of the situation, the team remained determined to succeed.

Synset Examples

the asperity of northern winters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asperity - harshness or severity, especially of temper or conditions
  • Synonym: severity - the fact or condition of being severe

Etymology

From Middle English grimnesse, from Old English grimnes (severity, fierceness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪmnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪmnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being grim or ghastly, often causing fear, horror, or distress
quality of being grim or ghastly

Examples

The grimness of the abandoned hospital sent chills down my spine.
Despite the grimness of the situation, she tried to remain hopeful.
The artist captured the grimness of war in his haunting paintings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severity - the quality of being very harsh or strict
  • Antonym: gentleness - the quality of being gentle and kind

Etymology

From 'grim' + '-ness', with 'grim' from Old English *grimm* (fierce, cruel).
