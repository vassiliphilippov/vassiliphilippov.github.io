tenebrious

1. ADJ

/ təˈniːbriəs / (AmE), / tɪˈniːbriəs / (BrE)
Dark and gloomy; obscure or shadowy.
dark and gloomy

Examples

The forest was especially tenebrious at dusk, cloaked in thick shadows.
His tenebrious paintings evoke a sense of melancholy and mystery.
We avoided the tenebrious alley that looked particularly unsafe after sunset.

Synset Examples

a tenebrous cave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, especially so as to appear depressing or frightening
  • Antonym: bright - giving out or reflecting a lot of light; shining

Etymology

From Latin 'tenebrosus' (dark, gloomy), from 'tenebrae' (darkness).
