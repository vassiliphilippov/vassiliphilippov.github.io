edification

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the instruction or improvement of a person morally or intellectually
improvement of morals or intellect

Examples

He listened to the lecture for his own edification and self-improvement.
The museum visit was not only enjoyable but also provided great edification.
Books are a source of both entertainment and edification for many people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: education - the process of receiving or giving systematic instruction
  • Synonym: instruction - the act of teaching or providing knowledge
  • Antonym: corruption - the act of becoming morally depraved or losing integrity

Etymology

From Latin 'aedificatio' (building, edification), from 'aedificare' (to build).
