hurry

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɜːri / (AmE), / ˈhʌri / (BrE)
a state of rushed activity, often resulting in carelessness or haste
state of rushed activity or haste

Examples

In her hurry to finish the assignment, she made several mistakes.
They left in such a hurry that they forgot their tickets.
There's no hurry; take your time and do it properly.

Synset Examples

in his haste to leave he forgot his book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haste - excessive speed or urgency

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɜri / (AmE), / ˈhʌri / (BrE)
A state of urgency or a need to act quickly.
state of urgency; rush

Examples

I'm sorry, I'm in a hurry and can't talk now.
She left the house in such a hurry that she forgot her keys.
There's no need to be in a hurry; we have plenty of time.

Synset Examples

in a hurry to lock the door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - A sudden movement or surge, often due to urgency
  • Antonym: delay - A period of waiting; lack of speed

Etymology

Probably from the Middle English hurre (to move with haste) or from the hurry-scurry of the 16th century.
