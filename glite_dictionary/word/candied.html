candied

1. ADJ

/ ˈkændid / (AmE), / ˈkændid / (BrE)
(of fruit, nuts, etc.) preserved by being coated with or soaked in sugar.
fruit or nuts preserved with sugar

Examples

The bakery sells candied orange peel during the holidays.
She decorated the cake with candied violets for a beautiful finish.
Recipes often use candied ginger to add a sweet and spicy flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crystallized - (of fruit, etc.) preserved by coating with sugar crystals

Etymology

From candy (verb), from Old French *sucre candi* (candied sugar), from Arabic *qand* (cane sugar).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkændid / (AmE), / ˈkændid / (BrE)
(of fruit, peel, nuts, etc.) coated or encrusted with sugar or syrup, often as a method of preservation.
coated with sugar or syrup

Examples

The bakery sells delicious candied orange peel dipped in chocolate.
She garnished the cake with candied ginger for extra flavor.
They enjoyed candied almonds at the festival.

Synset Examples

candied grapefruit peel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar-coated - covered with or containing a layer of sugar

Etymology

From the verb 'candy', from Old French 'sucre candi', from Arabic 'qandī' (made of sugar).
