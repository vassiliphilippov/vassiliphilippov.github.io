flamenco

1. NOUN

/ fləˈmɛŋkoʊ / (AmE), / fləˈmeŋkəʊ / (BrE)
a style of Spanish music, often featuring guitar, singing, and dancing, characterized by lively rhythms and expressive melodies
Spanish dance music and song style

Examples

The guitarist played a haunting flamenco tune that captivated the entire audience.
She has studied traditional flamenco for years and now performs at festivals across Europe.
The film opened with a scene of passionate flamenco dancing accompanied by vibrant guitar music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumba - a style of lively dance music of Cuban origin
  • Synonym: tango - a style of ballroom dance and music from Argentina

Etymology

Spanish, from Old Spanish flamenco, literally 'Flemish' (thought to relate to a style or group in Spain).

2. NOUN

/ fləˈmɛŋkoʊ / (AmE), / fləˈmɛŋkəʊ / (BrE)
A passionate and rhythmic style of dance and music originating from Andalusia in southern Spain, characterized by expressive movements, clapping, and stamping of feet.
Andalusian dance and music style

Examples

The dancers captivated the audience with their energetic flamenco performance.
She has been studying flamenco since she was a child and hopes to perform in Spain someday.
The rhythmic clapping and footwork are essential elements of traditional flamenco.

Etymology

Spanish, of uncertain origin, possibly from Old Spanish ‘flamenco’ (Flemish), referring to the migration of Flemish people, but later associated with Andalusian Gypsies.
