semibreve

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛm.i.briːv / (AmE), / ˈsɛ.mɪˌbriːv / (BrE)
a musical note with the longest duration in common use, equal to four beats in common time; also known as a whole note
a musical note equal to four beats
british

Examples

The choir held the semibreve at the end of the song for four full beats.
In this exercise, every bar starts with a semibreve.
She struggled to keep the semibreve perfectly in time during the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whole note - a musical note equal in duration to four beats; American term for semibreve

Etymology

From Latin 'semi-' (half) + 'brevis' (short), referring to its value in relation to the medieval 'breve'.
