Pinctada margaritifera

1. NOUN

/ pɪŋkˈtɑːdə ˌmɑːrɡərɪˈtɪfərə / (AmE), / pɪŋkˈtɑːdə ˌmɑːɡərɪˈtɪfərə / (BrE)
a tropical marine bivalve mollusc, known as the black-lipped pearl oyster, found chiefly off eastern Asia and the Pacific coast of North and Central America, and a major source of pearls
black-lipped pearl oyster species

Examples

The Pinctada margaritifera is cultivated for its beautiful black pearls.
Scientists are studying how environmental changes affect Pinctada margaritifera populations.
The native people of Polynesia have long harvested Pinctada margaritifera for pearls and shells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oyster - a marine bivalve mollusc with a rough, irregular shell
  • Degree Higher: pearl - a smooth, round bead formed inside the shell of some molluscs

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Pinctada' and species epithet 'margaritifera' meaning 'pearl-bearing'.
