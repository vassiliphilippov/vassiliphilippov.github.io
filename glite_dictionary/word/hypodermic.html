hypodermic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪpəˈdɜrmɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈdɜːmɪk / (BrE)
relating to the region immediately beneath the skin; or intended for injection beneath the skin.
under the skin; relating to injections
medical

Examples

The nurse used a hypodermic needle to administer the vaccine.
A hypodermic injection delivers medication directly beneath the skin.
He carried a hypodermic syringe in his medical kit for emergencies.

Synset Examples

hypodermic needle
subcutaneous implant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subcutaneous - situated or applied under the skin

Etymology

From Greek 'hypo-' (under) + 'derma' (skin).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.pəˈdɝː.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəˈdɜː.mɪk / (BrE)
A medical syringe fitted with a hollow needle, used for injecting substances under the skin.
syringe with needle for injections
medical

Examples

The nurse sterilized the hypodermic before administering the vaccine.
He reached into his bag for a hypodermic, preparing the injection with care.
Some hospitals now use disposable hypodermics to prevent contamination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syringe - a tube with a nozzle and piston for injecting or withdrawing fluids

Etymology

From Greek 'hypo-' (under) + 'derma' (skin).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03560842-n
Synset POS: n
