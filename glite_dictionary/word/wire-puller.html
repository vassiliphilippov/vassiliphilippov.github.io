wire-puller

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪr ˌpʊlɚ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪə ˌpʊlə / (BrE)
a person who uses secret or indirect influence to control or manipulate others, especially for personal gain
person using secret influence for control

Examples

Many believe a powerful wire-puller is behind the recent political changes.
Despite appearing uninvolved, she was actually the main wire-puller in the organization.
He became known as a skilled wire-puller who shaped decisions from the shadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: string-puller - one who manipulates events or people behind the scenes
  • Synonym: puppet master - person who controls others in a covert way

Etymology

From 'wire' (as in puppet strings) + 'puller', referring to someone who controls events from behind the scenes.
