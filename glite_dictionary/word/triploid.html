triploid

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrɪp.lɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtrɪp.lɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having three complete sets of chromosomes.
having three sets of chromosomes
medical

Examples

The scientist observed a triploid plant that exhibited unusual growth patterns.
Some fish species can be deliberately bred to be triploid for aquaculture purposes.
Most human triploid embryos do not develop to term due to genetic imbalance.

Synset Examples

human triploid fetuses are usually spontaneously aborted

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: diploid - having two complete sets of chromosomes
  • Degree Higher: tetraploid - having four complete sets of chromosomes

Etymology

From Greek 'tri-' (three) + 'ploid', as in 'haploid' or 'diploid', itself from Greek 'ploos' (fold).
