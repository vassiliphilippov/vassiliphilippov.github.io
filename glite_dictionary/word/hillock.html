hillock

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪlək / (AmE), / ˈhɪlək / (BrE)
A small natural hill; a slight elevation of the ground.
small natural hill

Examples

A lone hillock rose from the middle of the otherwise flat plain.
Children ran to the top of the grassy hillock to fly their kites.
The cottage was hidden behind a wooded hillock near the stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mound - a rounded mass projecting above a surface; a small hill

Etymology

From Middle English hillok, diminutive of 'hill'.
