republishing

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːˈpʌblɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈpʌblɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of publishing something again, especially a book, article, or other material that has already been published.
the act of publishing again

Examples

The republishing of classic novels introduces them to new generations of readers.
After resolving copyright issues, the author approved the republishing of her early essays.
The company announced the republishing of its entire magazine archive online.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: to publish - to prepare and issue a book, article, or other material for public sale or distribution

Etymology

From prefix 're-' (again) + 'publishing' (from 'publish').
