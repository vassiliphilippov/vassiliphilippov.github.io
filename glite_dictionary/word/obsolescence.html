obsolescence

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑb.səˈlɛs.əns / (AmE), / ˌɒb.səˈlɛs.əns / (BrE)
The process of becoming obsolete or outdated, especially as a result of technological or social change.
process of becoming obsolete or outdated

Examples

The rapid pace of technology can lead to the obsolescence of electronic devices within a few years.
Many factories faced obsolescence as new, more efficient methods of production were introduced.
Planned obsolescence encourages consumers to replace products more frequently than is truly necessary.

Synset Examples

a policy of planned obsolescence

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modernization - the process of adapting something to modern needs or habits
  • Synonym: decay - gradual decline or deterioration

Etymology

From Latin 'obsolescere' (to fall into disuse).
