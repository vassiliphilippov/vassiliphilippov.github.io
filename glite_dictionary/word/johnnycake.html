johnnycake

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑniˌkeɪk / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒniˌkeɪk / (BrE)
A type of cornbread, usually cooked pancake-style on a griddle, chiefly found in New England and parts of the United States.
cornbread pancake, New England

Examples

We enjoyed fresh johnnycake with maple syrup at the breakfast table.
The chef served a traditional johnnycake alongside grilled fish at the festival.
In some New England towns, johnnycake is still a beloved breakfast staple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornbread - bread made primarily from cornmeal

Etymology

Probably from 'John' (generic name) + 'cake', 18th-century American English; influenced by earlier terms like 'journey cake'.
