marguerite

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑrɡjəˈrit / (AmE), / ˌmɑːɡjəˈriːt / (BrE)
a tall Eurasian perennial plant with white daisy-like flowers, often naturalized and cultivated in gardens
a type of daisy-like flowering plant

Examples

The garden was filled with blooming marguerites that swayed in the breeze.
She picked a single marguerite and placed it in a small vase on the table.
Marguerites are often used in wildflower bouquets for their simple, classic beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxeye daisy - a common white daisy-like flower, also known as marguerite

Etymology

From French marguerite, meaning 'daisy', from Latin margarita (pearl).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɑrɡjəˈrit / (AmE), / ˌmɑːɡjʊˈriːt / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the daisy family, native to the Canary Islands, with pale yellow or white daisy-like flowers, often included in the genus Chrysanthemum.
a daisy-like perennial flower

Examples

A single marguerite brought cheer to the windowsill with its delicate yellow petals.
She planted marguerites along the garden path to brighten up the borders.
The florist recommended marguerite for a long-lasting and pretty bouquet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daisy - a common name for various plants with white petals and a yellow center

Etymology

From French 'marguerite', meaning 'daisy', from Latin 'margarita' (pearl).
