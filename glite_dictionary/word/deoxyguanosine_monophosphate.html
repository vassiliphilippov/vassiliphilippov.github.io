deoxyguanosine monophosphate

1. NOUN

/ diˌɑksiˌɡwɑnəˌsin ˌmɑnoʊˌfɑsˌfeɪt / (AmE), / diˌɒksiˌɡwɑːnəˌsiːn ˌmɒnəˌfæsˌfeɪt / (BrE)
a nucleotide that is one of the four basic building blocks of DNA, consisting of a phosphate group, the sugar deoxyribose, and the base guanine
a nucleotide building block of DNA
medical

Examples

Scientists often study deoxyguanosine monophosphate to understand how DNA replicates in cells.
The structure of deoxyguanosine monophosphate includes a phosphate group, deoxyribose sugar, and the guanine base.
Mutations involving deoxyguanosine monophosphate can lead to errors in genetic information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deoxyadenosine monophosphate - nucleotide building block of DNA with adenine base
  • Synonym: deoxycytidine monophosphate - nucleotide building block of DNA with cytosine base
  • Synonym: deoxythymidine monophosphate - nucleotide building block of DNA with thymine base

Etymology

From 'deoxy-' (lacking oxygen), 'guanosine' (nucleoside containing guanine), and 'monophosphate' (one phosphate group).
