acrobatics

1. NOUN

/ ˌækrəˈbætɪks / (AmE), / ˌakrəˈbatɪks / (BrE)
the performance of skilled gymnastic or physical feats, especially those involving agility, balance, and coordination, as practiced by acrobats
skilled gymnastic feats or stunts

Examples

The circus amazed the audience with breathtaking acrobatics performed high above the ground.
She trained for years to perfect her acrobatics before joining the dance company.
The film's action scenes feature impressive acrobatics and daring stunts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gymnastics - the practice or sport of exercises and performances requiring physical strength, flexibility, and coordination

Etymology

From 'acrobat' (from French, ultimately from Greek 'akrobatos' meaning 'walking on tiptoe') with suffix '-ics' indicating a field of study or practice.

2. NOUN

/ ˌækrəˈbætɪks / (AmE), / ˌækrəˈbætɪks / (BrE)
The performance of complex or daring stunts in flight, especially while piloting an aircraft.
aerial stunts in aircraft

Examples

The pilot impressed the crowd with his spectacular acrobatics during the air show.
Modern fighter jets are designed to handle extreme acrobatics with ease.
Learning acrobatics requires exceptional skill and concentration from every pilot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerobatics - the practice of flying maneuvers involving aircraft attitudes not used in normal flight

Etymology

From Greek akrobatikos (walking on tiptoe), originally referring to gymnastics.

Source: WordNet
