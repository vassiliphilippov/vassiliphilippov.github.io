aztreonam

1. NOUN

/ æzˈtriːəˌnæm / (AmE), / azˈtrɪəˌnæm / (BrE)
a synthetic antibiotic used to treat severe bacterial infections, especially in cases where other antibiotics cannot be used
synthetic antibiotic for severe infections
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed aztreonam for the patient's serious lung infection.
Some patients with penicillin allergies can safely use aztreonam as an alternative.
Hospitals often reserve aztreonam for treating complicated, drug-resistant infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - a substance used to kill or inhibit the growth of bacteria
