hydra

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrə / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪdrə / (BrE)
A persistent problem or obstacle with many aspects, making it difficult to solve or eliminate.
persistent or multifaceted problem

Examples

The new regulations created a hydra of legal challenges that the company struggled to resolve.
Corruption in the city has proved to be a hydra, with new issues emerging as soon as old ones are addressed.
Dealing with misinformation online can feel like battling a hydra, as false claims multiply despite every effort to stop them.

Synset Examples

we may be facing a hydra that defies any easy solution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: problem - a matter or situation regarded as unwelcome or harmful and needing to be dealt with
  • Synonym: obstacle - a thing that blocks one's way or prevents progress

Etymology

From Greek myth: Hydra, a many-headed serpent; from Greek 'Húdra' (water serpent).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrə / (AmE), / ˈhɪdrə / (BrE)
a small freshwater animal of the genus Hydra, with a tubular body and tentacles, known for its regenerative ability
small freshwater polyp with tentacles

Examples

The hydra can reproduce asexually by budding off new individuals.
Under the microscope, the hydra's tentacles extend gracefully to catch prey.
Scientists study the remarkable regenerative powers of the hydra in laboratory experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyp - a simple aquatic animal with a tubular body and tentacles

Etymology

From Latin Hydra, from Greek Hudra (the water serpent in mythology), later used for the animal genus.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdrə / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪdrə / (BrE)
A persistent or complex problem that seems to multiply or grow worse as attempts are made to resolve it.
problem that multiplies when addressed

Examples

The new regulations turned the tax code into a hydra of complexity.
Trying to fix the software bug only created a hydra of new errors.
The political scandal became a hydra, producing new controversies at every turn.

Synset Examples

The project became a hydra, with issues sprouting everywhere.
Every time we solved one problem, another hydra appeared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quagmire - A complicated, awkward, or hazardous situation
  • Synonym: Pandora's box - A source of many unforeseen troubles

Etymology

From Greek mythology: the Hydra, a many-headed serpent defeated by Hercules, which regrew two heads for each cut off.
