unparented

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈpɛrən.tɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈpɛə.rən.tɪd / (BrE)
having no parent or parents, or not cared for by parental figures or surrogates
without parents or parental care

Examples

After the earthquake, many children were suddenly unparented and depended on aid organizations for survival.
The story explores the emotional struggles of unparented youth in inner cities.
She dedicated her career to supporting unparented children around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orphaned - having lost one's parents through death

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'parent', meaning not having parents.
