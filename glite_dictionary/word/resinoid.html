resinoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛz.ə.nɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛz.ɪ.nɔɪd / (BrE)
a synthetic plastic material made by incorporating natural or synthetic resins
plastic material containing resins

Examples

The manufacturer used a resinoid to improve the durability of the finished product.
Scientists developed a new resinoid that resists high temperatures.
Each sample of resinoid was analyzed for its chemical stability.

Etymology

From 'resin' (natural or synthetic adhesive substance) + '-oid' (having the form or likeness of).
