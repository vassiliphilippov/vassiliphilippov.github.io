monistic

1. ADJ

/ moʊˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / məˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of monism, the philosophical doctrine that reality is ultimately composed of a single substance or principle.
relating to monism (single substance philosophy)

Examples

The monistic view opposes the idea that mind and body are fundamentally different kinds of things.
In law, a monistic framework treats international and national law as a single system.
Her monistic interpretation of the universe influenced much of her philosophical writing.

Synset Examples

the monistic school would regard national law and international law as an integrated whole

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dualistic - relating to dualism, the doctrine that reality consists of two distinct principles
  • Synonym: monism - the doctrine that only one kind of substance or principle constitutes reality

Etymology

From 'monism' + '-ic', 'monism' from Greek 'monos' (single, alone).
