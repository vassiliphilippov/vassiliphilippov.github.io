actinomycin

1. NOUN

/ ˌæktɪnoʊˈmaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌæktɪnəʊˈmʌɪsɪn / (BrE)
any of a group of red antibiotics produced by soil bacteria, used especially in research and cancer treatment
red antibiotic from soil bacteria
medical

Examples

Researchers studied the effects of actinomycin on cancer cells in the laboratory.
Treatment with actinomycin was effective in stopping bacterial growth.
The doctor explained that actinomycin is sometimes used in chemotherapy regimens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - a substance that destroys or inhibits the growth of microorganisms

Etymology

From actino- (Greek aktis, stem aktin-, meaning 'ray') + -mycin (from mykes, Greek for 'fungus'; used in antibiotics names).
