neigh

1. NOUN

/ neɪ / (AmE), / neɪ / (BrE)
the loud, high-pitched sound made by a horse
the sound made by a horse

Examples

The neigh of a horse echoed across the fields.
She recognized the familiar neigh coming from the stable.
At dawn, a loud neigh awakened the entire farm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whinny - a gentle or soft neigh; a sound made by a horse

Etymology

From Middle English 'neighen', from Old English 'hnægan'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07401916-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ neɪ / (AmE), / neɪ / (BrE)
(of a horse) to make its characteristic high-pitched sound.
horse makes its characteristic sound

Examples

The horse began to neigh loudly as it approached the stable.
Early in the morning, I heard several horses neighing in the field.
She smiled as the foal neighed playfully at its mother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whinny - to make a gentle, high-pitched neighing sound (of a horse)

Etymology

From Old English *hnǣgan*, of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01061615-v
Synset POS: v
