arrival

1. NOUN

/ əˈraɪvəl / (AmE), / əˈraɪvəl / (BrE)
the act of coming to a place, especially at the end of a journey
the act of coming to a place

Examples

We were excited about her arrival at the airport last night.
The arrival of spring is always a welcome change after winter.
Please notify us upon your arrival at the hotel.

Synset Examples

they awaited her arrival

Related Terms

  • Antonym: departure - the act of leaving a place
  • Synonym: coming - the act of arriving at a place

Etymology

From Old French *arriver* (to reach land), from Vulgar Latin *arripāre*, from Latin *ad* (to) + *rīpa* (shore).

2. NOUN

/ əˈraɪvəl / (AmE), / əˈraɪvəl / (BrE)
the achievement of a goal or objective, especially after considerable effort or anticipation
achievement of a goal or objective

Examples

Her arrival as a leading expert in the field was the result of years of dedication.
The company's arrival at international prominence was marked by its groundbreaking new product.
Winning the award signified his arrival in the world of literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attainment - the act of achieving something
  • Antonym: failure - lack of success in achieving a goal

Etymology

From Old French *arriver* (to arrive), from Latin *ad* (to) + *rīpare* (to the shore).

3. NOUN

/ əˈraɪvəl / (AmE), / əˈraɪvəl / (BrE)
a person who arrives at a place, especially one who has just come to join a group or community
a person who has just arrived

Examples

The party was excited to meet the latest arrival from abroad.
At the airport, friends greeted each arrival with flowers and smiles.
As a recent arrival, she still felt unfamiliar with the local customs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newcomer - a person who has recently arrived in a place or group
  • Antonym: old-timer - a person who has been in a place or group for a long time

Etymology

From Old French arriver (to arrive), ultimately from Latin ad- (to) + ripa (shore).
