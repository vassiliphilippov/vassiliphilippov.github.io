Ardisia crenata

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈdɪzi.ə krəˈneɪtə / (AmE), / ɑːˈdɪzɪə krɛˈnɑːtə / (BrE)
a shrub native to East and South Asia, bearing glossy leaves and clusters of bright red berries, commonly known as coral berry or Christmas berry
Asian shrub with red berries

Examples

The Ardisia crenata in her garden produces clusters of bright red berries each winter.
Ardisia crenata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fruit.
They cautioned that Ardisia crenata can spread rapidly and become invasive in some regions.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ardisia' is the genus name, and 'crenata' means 'scalloped', referring to the leaf edges.
