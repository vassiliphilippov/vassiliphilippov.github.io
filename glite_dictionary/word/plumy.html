plumy

1. ADJ

/ ˈpluːmi / (AmE), / ˈpluːmi / (BrE)
having many soft feathers or feather-like tufts; resembling a plume.
resembling a plume; feathery

Examples

The bird's plumy crest shimmered in the sunlight.
She drew a plumy pattern in the sand with a stick.
The dancer's costume featured a plumy headpiece that swayed with her movements.

Synset Examples

the dog's plumy tail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feathery - covered with or resembling feathers

Etymology

From Latin 'pluma' (feather) + English '-y' (having the quality of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpluːmi / (AmE), / ˈpluːmi / (BrE)
having or covered with plumes; resembling or abounding in feathers or plumes
having or covered with plumes

Examples

The bird's plumy crest caught the sunlight.
She wore a plumy hat to the festival parade.
The actor's costume was ornate, decorated with a plumy collar reminiscent of the Renaissance.

Synset Examples

white-plumed egrets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plumed - having feathers or plumes

Etymology

Formed from 'plume' (feather) with the suffix '-y', meaning 'having the quality of'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈpluːmi / (AmE), / ˈpluːmi / (BrE)
decorated with feathers or plumes; having the appearance of being covered in soft, feathery tufts
adorned with feathers or plumes

Examples

The dancer wore a plumy headdress that shimmered under the lights.
Her costume was adorned with plumy decorations along the sleeves and collar.
A rare bird with a plumy tail landed on the branch outside my window.

Synset Examples

a feathered hat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feathery - resembling or covered with feathers

Etymology

From 'plume' (Latin pluma, 'feather') + English adjective suffix '-y'.
