Anagasta

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈɡæstə / (AmE), / ˌanəˈɡastə / (BrE)
a genus of small moths whose larvae are commonly known as flour moths, pests of stored grains and flour
genus of flour moths

Examples

The larvae of the Anagasta moth are a major pest in flour mills.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Anagasta to find better pest control methods.
Proper storage can help prevent Anagasta infestations in grain warehouses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flour moth - a moth whose larvae feed on flour and grain products

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, used in scientific taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈɡæstə / (AmE), / ˌanəˈɡastə / (BrE)
A genus of moths whose larvae are known as flour moths.
genus of moths including flour moths

Examples

The Anagasta genus includes several species that infest stored grains and flour.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Anagasta moths to develop better pest control methods.
The larvae of Anagasta are a common problem in flour mills worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flour moth - a moth whose larvae are pests of stored grain and flour (Ephestia kuehniella)

Etymology

From New Latin, used in taxonomy for this genus of moths.
