grape

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪp / (AmE), / ɡreɪp / (BrE)
a small, round, smooth-skinned edible berry, typically growing in clusters on woody vines of the genus Vitis, used as fruit and for making wine
a small edible berry from a vine

Examples

She picked a grape from the bunch and popped it into her mouth.
The vineyard is famous for its grape varieties used in red wine.
A single grape rolled off the table and onto the floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raisin - a dried grape

Etymology

From Old French 'grape', meaning bunch of grapes or hook, possibly related to Old Provençal 'grapa' (hook).

2. NOUN

/ ɡreɪp / (AmE), / ɡreɪp / (BrE)
a cluster of small metal balls fired together from a cannon, used historically to produce a spread of shot against troops or ships
cluster of projectiles fired from a cannon

Examples

The cannon was loaded with grape to repel the advancing infantry.
The sailors braced themselves as grape tore across the deck.
During the battle, grape caused heavy casualties among the enemy ranks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grapeshot - a type of ammunition consisting of a cluster of small balls for cannons

Etymology

From the resemblance of grouped projectiles to a bunch of grapes.

3. NOUN

/ ɡreɪp / (AmE), / ɡreɪp / (BrE)
A small, round, smooth-skinned fruit, typically green or purple, which grows in clusters on vines of the genus Vitis.
a small, round fruit, often in clusters

Examples

She picked a ripe grape from the bunch and popped it in her mouth.
Vineyards in this region produce some of the finest grape varieties in the world.
A single grape contains many antioxidants and nutrients beneficial to health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raisin - a dried grape

Etymology

From Old French 'graipe', from Latin 'grappus' meaning hook (from the clusters' grape hooks).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07774656-n
Synset POS: n
