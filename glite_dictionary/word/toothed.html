toothed

1. ADJ

/ tuːθt / (AmE), / tuːθt / (BrE)
having teeth, especially of a particular number or type; often used in compounds.
having teeth; toothed

Examples

The toothed edge of the saw makes it ideal for cutting wood.
Some prehistoric fish had heavily toothed jaws for catching prey.
The leaf is easily recognized by its toothed margins.

Synset Examples

saw-toothed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: toothless - lacking teeth

Etymology

Formed from the noun 'tooth' with the suffix '-ed.'

2. ADJ

/ ˈtuːθt / (AmE), / ˈtuːθt / (BrE)
having a notched or serrated edge with projections resembling the teeth of a saw, typically pointing toward the tip.
having a notched, saw-like edge

Examples

The leaves of the plant were toothed, making them easy to distinguish from other species.
Many fish have toothed jaws that help them capture their prey.
The toothed edge of the tool was ideal for cutting through thick material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serrated - having a jagged or saw-like edge
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even or unbroken surface without notches or projections

3. ADJ

/ ˈtuːθt / (AmE), / ˈtuːθt / (BrE)
having an irregularly notched or jagged margin, as if cut or worn by teeth; serrated or indented, especially at the edge.
having a jagged or notched margin

Examples

The leaves of this plant are toothed along their edges, creating a jagged outline.
Many prehistoric sharks had toothed jaws perfect for tearing through flesh.
The old saw had a toothed blade designed for cutting thick wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serrated - having a notched or sawlike edge
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even, unbroken edge or surface

Etymology

From tooth + -ed, meaning 'having teeth' or 'tooth-like projections'.
