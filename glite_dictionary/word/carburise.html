to carburise

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːrbjəraɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbjʊraɪz / (BrE)
to combine or infuse a material, especially metal, with carbon, usually by heating
infuse with carbon (metallurgy)

Examples

Engineers carburise the steel to enhance its surface hardness for industrial applications.
The process will carburise the metal, making it more resistant to wear.
Before being used in machine parts, the component is often carburised for added durability.

Synset Examples

carburize metal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carburize - unite metal with carbon (US spelling)

Etymology

From French *carburer* (to combine with carbon) + -ise; related to 'carbure' for carbide.
