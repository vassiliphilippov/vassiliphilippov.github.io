naff

1. ADJ

/ næf / (AmE), / næf / (BrE)
unfashionable, of poor quality, or in bad taste
unfashionable or of poor quality
informal british slang

Examples

Everyone thought his naff jokes were embarrassing and out of date.
She wore a naff dress that clashed with her shoes.
The hotel room was clean, but the curtains looked a bit naff.

Synset Examples

The decor in that restaurant is quite naff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tacky - showing poor taste and quality
  • Antonym: stylish - fashionable and elegant

Etymology

British slang origin, possibly from Polari, a form of cant slang used in Britain.
