disarray

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.əˈreɪ / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈreɪ / (BrE)
A state of mental confusion or lack of clear and orderly thought or behavior.
mental confusion or disorder

Examples

After losing his job, he fell into a state of emotional disarray.
Her mind was in complete disarray after hearing the unexpected news.
The team's sudden change in strategy left the players in mental disarray.

Synset Examples

a confusion of impressions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - a lack of clarity or certainty in thought or behavior
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From Old French desareer (to throw into disorder), from des- (dis-) + areer (to put in order).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.əˈreɪ / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈreɪ / (BrE)
A state of untidiness or disorder, especially in clothing or appearance.
untidiness or disorder

Examples

After the long journey, her hair was in complete disarray.
The sudden announcement threw the staff into disarray.
His tie was askew and his suit in disarray after the hectic morning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: order - neat and organized condition
  • Synonym: mess - a dirty or untidy state

Etymology

From Old French desarei (disorder), from des- (dis-) + areer (to put in order).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04904781-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˌdɪsəˈreɪ / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈreɪ / (BrE)
to bring disorder or confusion to something; to make untidy or disorganized.
to bring disorder to

Examples

The sudden storm disarrayed the carefully planned outdoor event.
Her unexpected arrival completely disarrayed his schedule for the day.
A wild wind had disarrayed the papers scattered across the desk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disorder - to make untidy or chaotic; to disturb order
  • Antonym: to organize - to arrange or put in order

Etymology

From Old French 'desareier', from 'des-' (dis-) + 'areier' (to put in order).
