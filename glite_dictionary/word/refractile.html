refractile

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈfræk.taɪl / (AmE), / rɪˈfræk.taɪl / (BrE)
able to refract or bend light; relating to refraction.
capable of refracting light

Examples

The doctor noted several refractile bodies under the microscope.
Diamonds are highly refractile, which contributes to their brilliance.
His research focused on the refractile properties of various crystalline materials.

Synset Examples

the refractive characteristics of the eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refractive - of or relating to refraction

Etymology

From Latin refractus (broken back, bent) + English -ile (having the quality of).
