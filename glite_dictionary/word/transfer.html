transfer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræns.fɚ / (AmE), / ˈtræns.fə / (BrE)
a person who moves from one place, position, or institution to another, especially for work or study
person transferred to another place or position

Examples

The college welcomed several transfers from other universities this semester.
As a transfer, she had to adjust to a new academic environment.
Our team gained a talented player who was a transfer from another club.

Synset Examples

2. NOUN

/ ˈtræns.fɚ / (AmE), / ˈtræns.fə / (BrE)
a ticket issued to a passenger that allows them to continue their journey on a different vehicle without paying a new fare
ticket for changing vehicles on a journey

Examples

You must ask the driver for a transfer if you plan to switch to another bus.
The transfer is valid for 90 minutes after your first ride begins.
I lost my transfer and had to pay for a second subway trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ticket - a printed pass allowing entry or travel

Etymology

From Latin transferre, meaning to carry across.
