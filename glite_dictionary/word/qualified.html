qualified

1. ADJ

/ ˈkwɑləˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkwɒlɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(of a statement, approval, or feeling) not complete or absolute; limited or restricted in some way.
limited or restricted; not absolute

Examples

The board gave only a qualified endorsement to the new policy.
Her acceptance of the offer was qualified by certain conditions she wanted to discuss.
He expressed qualified support for the proposal, noting some areas of concern.

Synset Examples

gave only qualified approval

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unqualified - not limited or restricted; absolute

Etymology

From Latin qualificare (to make of a certain quality), via French qualifier.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01919581-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈkwɑː.ləˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkwɒ.lɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
having the necessary skills, experience, or education to do a particular job or activity
having met the necessary requirements

Examples

Only qualified candidates will be considered for the manager position.
She is highly qualified to lead the research project thanks to her experience.
Some applicants were not qualified because they lacked the necessary certifications.

Synset Examples

many qualified applicants for the job

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unqualified - not having the necessary qualifications or requirements

Etymology

From late Latin qualificare (to make of a certain quality), via Old French qualifier.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01917434-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈkwɑləˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkwɒlɪfaɪd / (BrE)
limited, modified, or restricted in some way; not complete or unconditional.
limited or conditional; not absolute

Examples

His approval was a qualified endorsement, as he still had some reservations about the proposal.
The offer comes with several qualified conditions that must be met before it is finalized.
She gave a qualified yes, agreeing only if certain requirements were fulfilled.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unqualified - not limited or restricted in any way; absolute

Etymology

From Latin qualificare (to attribute a quality).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00558400-s
Synset POS: s
