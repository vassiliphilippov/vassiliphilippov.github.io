campaign

1. NOUN

/ kæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / kæmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
an organized series of activities or events to achieve the goal of being elected to a public office
political race for public office

Examples

She volunteered for his campaign for mayor last year.
The presidential campaign attracted national attention and huge media coverage.
Donations poured in to support his campaign for city council.

Synset Examples

I managed his campaign for governor
he is raising money for a Senate run

Related Terms

  • Synonym: election - a formal group decision-making process by which a population chooses an individual to hold public office

Etymology

From French 'campagne', from Italian 'campagna', from Latin 'campus' (field), originally referring to military operations in the field.

2. NOUN

/ kæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / kæmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
an overland expedition by hunters, especially for big-game hunting in Africa
overland hunting expedition (esp. Africa)

Examples

The group set out on a campaign across the savannah to hunt big game.
Their African campaign was filled with both peril and excitement.
During the nineteenth century, wealthy Europeans often embarked on a campaign to collect trophies.

Etymology

From French 'campagne', from Italian 'campagna', from Latin 'campānia' (level country).

3. NOUN

/ kæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / kamˈpeɪn / (BrE)
a series of planned activities or operations intended to achieve a particular goal, especially within a certain time and area
a series of organized actions to reach a goal

Examples

The marketing team launched a new campaign to attract younger customers.
During the election, she led an intensive campaign across several states.
The organization is running a campaign to raise awareness about climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: operation - an organized activity involving many people, especially in a military context
  • Synonym: initiative - an act or strategy intended to resolve a difficulty or improve a situation

Etymology

From French *campagne* (military expedition in the field), from Italian *campagna*, from Latin *campus* (field).
