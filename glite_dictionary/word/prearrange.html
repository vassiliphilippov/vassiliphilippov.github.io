to prearrange

1. VERB

/ ˌpriː.əˈreɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˌpriː.əˈreɪndʒ / (BrE)
to arrange or organize (something) in advance or before it is needed
arrange in advance

Examples

We need to prearrange transportation before the conference starts.
All the meetings were prearranged to avoid scheduling conflicts.
She prefers to prearrange her travel plans weeks in advance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arrange - to put things in a neat, attractive, or required order

Etymology

From prefix 'pre-' (meaning 'before') + 'arrange'.
