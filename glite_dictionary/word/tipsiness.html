tipsiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪpsinəs / (AmE), / ˈtɪpsinɪs / (BrE)
A temporary state of slight drunkenness caused by consuming alcohol.
slight drunkenness from alcohol

Examples

Her tipsiness was obvious as she giggled at every joke during dinner.
After two glasses of wine, a pleasant tipsiness set in and everyone relaxed.
He blamed his tipsiness for forgetting his keys at the bar.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of being sober, not affected by alcohol
  • Degree Higher: drunkenness - the state of being drunk or intoxicated
  • Synonym: tipsy - slightly drunk

Etymology

From 'tipsy' (slightly drunk) + '-ness'. 'Tipsy' possibly from tip (to tilt or unsteady) + -sy.
