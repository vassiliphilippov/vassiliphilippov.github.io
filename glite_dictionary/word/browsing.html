browsing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbraʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of feeding by continually nibbling on leaves, twigs, or other vegetation, especially by animals such as deer or goats
feeding by continual nibbling

Examples

Deer were observed browsing along the forest edge, nibbling on fresh shoots.
Heavy browsing by goats can prevent young trees from growing in the area.
The botanist studied the effects of browsing on the diversity of plant species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grazing - feeding on grass in fields, typically by cattle or sheep

2. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbraʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of looking through written material or goods in a casual or superficial way, without a specific aim.
casual or superficial reading or looking

Examples

She spent the afternoon browsing through the bookstore without buying anything.
He enjoys browsing magazines while waiting for his appointment.
A little casual browsing often leads to unexpected discoveries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scanning - looking over quickly or superficially
  • Antonym: perusing - reading carefully or thoroughly

Etymology

From the verb 'browse', from Old French 'broster' (to sprout, bud), later used figuratively for reading or looking through.
