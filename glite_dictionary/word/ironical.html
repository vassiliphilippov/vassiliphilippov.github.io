ironical

1. ADJ

/ aɪˈrɑnɪkəl / (AmE), / aɪˈrɒnɪkəl / (BrE)
characterized by irony; showing or expressing irony, especially when the outcome is the opposite of what was expected
characterized by irony; showing irony

Examples

It was ironical that their attempt to avoid trouble only led to more of it.
She gave an ironical smile when she heard about the so-called victory.
The ironical twist in the story left readers surprised and amused.

Synset Examples

madness, an ironic fate for such a clear thinker
it was ironical that the well-planned scheme failed so completely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ironic - characterized by irony; showing or expressing irony

Etymology

From irony + -ical, modelled after the formation of "ironical" from "irony".

2. ADJ

/ aɪˈrɑnɪkəl / (AmE), / aɪˈrɒnɪkəl / (BrE)
using or characterized by irony, especially in a humorous or mocking way.
humorously ironic or mocking

Examples

She gave him an ironical smile when he apologized for being late.
The ironical tone of the article made the criticism less direct but more effective.
His speech was so ironical that many people failed to realize he was joking.

Synset Examples

dry humor
an ironic remark often conveys an intended meaning obliquely
an ironic novel
an ironical smile
with a wry Scottish wit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ironic - using irony; characterized by irony
  • Synonym: sarcastic - using irony to mock or convey contempt
  • Antonym: sincere - genuine, not ironic or mocking

Etymology

Variant of 'ironic', formed from 'irony' + '-ical'.
