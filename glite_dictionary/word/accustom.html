to accustom

1. VERB

/ əˈkʌstəm / (AmE), / əˈkʌstəm / (BrE)
to make someone or yourself familiar with something so that it seems normal or usual
make familiar with something

Examples

It took several weeks to accustom myself to the new climate.
Parents often accustom their children to a fixed bedtime routine.
She tried to accustom her dog to car rides by taking him out every weekend.

Synset Examples

She became habituated to the background music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to habituate - to make someone used to something through repeated exposure
  • Antonym: unaccustom - to stop being accustomed to something; to make unfamiliar

Etymology

From Old French 'acostumer', based on 'costume' (custom, habit), from Latin 'consuetudo'.
