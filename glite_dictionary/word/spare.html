spare

1. NOUN

/ spɛr / (AmE), / spɛə / (BrE)
A score in bowling when all ten pins are knocked down with two consecutive rolls in a single frame.
bowling score: all ten pins down in two rolls

Examples

After missing the first pin, he was relieved to get a spare on his second try.
A spare is worth more points than an open frame in bowling.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: strike - A score in bowling when all ten pins are knocked down with the first roll in a frame
