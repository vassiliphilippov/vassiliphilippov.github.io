predestinarian

1. NOUN

/ priˌdɛstəˈnɛriən / (AmE), / priˌdiːstɪˈneəriən / (BrE)
a person who believes that all events, especially regarding salvation, are determined in advance by a divine power and cannot be changed by human actions
one who believes fate is predetermined

Examples

As a predestinarian, she believed that her life's course was set long before she was born.
The debate between free will and predestinarian thought has divided theologians for centuries.
He was accused of being a predestinarian because of his strong belief in divine preordination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fatalist - a person who believes that all events are inevitable and humans are powerless to change them

Etymology

From Medieval Latin praedestinationarius, from Latin praedestinatio (predestination).

2. ADJ

/ prɪˌdɛstəˈnɛriən / (AmE), / priˌdɛstɪˈneəriən / (BrE)
Relating to or believing in the doctrine of predestination, especially in theology.
relating to belief in predestination

Examples

His predestinarian beliefs shaped his view of salvation and free will.
The church split due to disagreements over predestinarian theology.
Some critics find predestinarian doctrines at odds with moral responsibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predestination - the doctrine that all events, especially salvation, are predetermined by God

Etymology

From 'predestination' + '-arian' (suffix denoting adherents).
