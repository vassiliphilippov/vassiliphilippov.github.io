Sigurd

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɪɡərd / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡɜːd / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, a legendary hero known for slaying the dragon Fafnir and acquiring a cursed treasure, later killed by Brynhild.
Norse legendary hero; dragon slayer

Examples

The legend of Sigurd slaying the dragon Fafnir is a central tale in Norse mythology.
In Wagner's opera cycle, the character of Sigurd is mirrored by Siegfried.
Scholars often debate the origins of the Sigurd story and its variations across Germanic cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siegfried - German counterpart and parallel of the Norse hero Sigurd in the Nibelungenlied legend

Etymology

From Old Norse *Sigurðr*, composed of 'sigr' (victory) and 'varðr' (guardian).
