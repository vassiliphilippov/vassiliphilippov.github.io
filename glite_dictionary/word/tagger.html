tagger

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæɡər / (AmE), / ˈtæɡə / (BrE)
a computer program that assigns grammatical labels to the words or other constituents within text
program that labels grammar in text

Examples

The tagger automatically labeled each word in the sentence with its correct part of speech.
Researchers improved the accuracy of their tagger for analyzing social media texts.
When training a new language model, it is important to select a reliable tagger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parser - a computer program that analyzes the grammatical structure of sentences
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæɡər / (AmE), / ˈtæɡə / (BrE)
a person who appends, attaches, or joins one thing to another, often by adding labels or supplementary material
person who attaches or adds to something

Examples

The anonymous tagger added several footnotes to clarify the original manuscript.
During the survey, each sample was recorded by a designated tagger to ensure accuracy.
A content tagger helps organize large databases by attaching relevant labels to each item.

Synset Examples

a theory that was simply added on by some anonymous tagger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attacher - a person who joins or fixes something to another thing

Etymology

From 'tag' (to attach or append) + agent suffix '-er.'

3. NOUN

/ ˈtæɡər / (AmE), / ˈtæɡə / (BrE)
a person or system that assigns grammatical labels or categories to words in a text, especially in computational linguistics or language processing
person who assigns grammatical labels

Examples

The linguist trained a tagger to automatically identify parts of speech in English sentences.
Many natural language processing tools include a built-in tagger for text analysis.
After running the document through a tagger, each word was labeled with its grammatical category.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annotator - a person who adds explanatory notes or labels to data, such as text or images

Etymology

From 'tag' (to attach a label) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10708471-n
Synset POS: n
