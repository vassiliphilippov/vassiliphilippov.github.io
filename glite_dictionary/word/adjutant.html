adjutant

1. NOUN

/ ˈædʒətənt / (AmE), / ˈadʒʊt(ə)nt / (BrE)
a large stork native to India, known for its tall stature and stately, military-like walk.
large Indian stork with stately walk

Examples

The adjutant is easily recognized by its elongated neck and upright posture.
Birdwatchers marveled at the adjutant as it strode across the wetlands.
The adjutant plays an important role in the ecosystem by feeding on carrion and waste.

2. NOUN

/ ˈædʒədənt / (AmE), / ˈadʒʊtənt / (BrE)
an officer in the armed forces who acts as an administrative assistant to a more senior officer
military assistant officer

Examples

The adjutant delivered the message to the commanding officer without delay.
As adjutant, his responsibilities included organizing the daily schedule for the colonel.
She was promoted to adjutant after several years of exemplary service in the army.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aide-de-camp - an officer acting as a confidential assistant to a senior officer

Etymology

From French 'adjudant', from Latin 'adiutant-', present participle of 'adiuvare' (to help).
