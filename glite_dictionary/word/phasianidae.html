Phasianidae

1. NOUN

/ feɪˈzɪænɪˌdiː / (AmE), / feɪˈzɪænɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of birds including pheasants, quails, and partridges, characterized by strong legs and often colourful plumage
the bird family of pheasants, quails, and partridges

Examples

The Phasianidae family includes many ground-dwelling birds such as pheasants and partridges.
Many species within the Phasianidae are prized by hunters for their game qualities.
Researchers are studying the evolution of the Phasianidae family to understand their diverse behaviors.

Etymology

From New Latin Phasianidae, from Phasianus (pheasant genus) + -idae (family suffix in zoology).

2. NOUN

/ feɪˈzɪ.nɪˌdi / (AmE), / feɪˈzɪ.nɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds that includes pheasants, quails, partridges, and related species.
family of birds including pheasants and quails

Examples

The Phasianidae family includes many colorful and ground-dwelling birds.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of several Phasianidae species across Asia.
Quails and partridges are well-known members of the Phasianidae family.

Etymology

From New Latin Phasianidae, from genus name Phasianus (pheasant), from Greek phasianos.
