to commercialize

1. VERB

/ kəˈmɝːʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kəˈmɜːʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something available for sale to the public or operated primarily for financial profit, often losing its original character
make commercial; turn into a business

Examples

Many companies aim to commercialize new medical technologies as quickly as possible.
There is a debate about whether art loses value when it is commercialized.
The festival organizers plan to commercialize the event by selling merchandise and sponsorships.

Synset Examples

Some Amish people have commercialized their way of life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to monetize - to convert into or express in the form of money
  • Synonym: to privatize - to transfer from public or government control to private ownership

Etymology

From 'commercial' + '-ize', influenced by French 'commercialiser'.

2. VERB

/ kəˈmɝː.ʃə.l̩.aɪz / (AmE), / kəˈmɜː.ʃəl.aɪz / (BrE)
to make something available or operated for profit, often by emphasizing financial gain at the expense of other values
make available for profit, often at cost to quality

Examples

Some critics argue that reality television has commercialized entertainment at the expense of creativity.
The festival has become too commercialized in recent years, losing its original spirit.
Several companies are trying to commercialize new sustainable energy technologies.

Synset Examples

The hotel owners are commercializing the beaches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to monetize - to convert into or express in the form of money

Etymology

From commercial + -ize; based on 'commerce' from Latin 'commercium' (trade).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01167041-v
Synset POS: v
