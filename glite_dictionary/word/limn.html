to limn

1. VERB

/ lɪm / (AmE), / lɪm / (BrE)
to depict or describe in painting or words; to portray, especially by drawing or painting
depict or portray, especially in art
archaic

Examples

The artist was commissioned to limn the royal family's likenesses.
The author managed to limn the city's atmosphere in exquisite detail.
She skillfully limned the gentle curve of his smile on canvas.

Synset Examples

Goya wanted to portray his mistress, the Duchess of Alba

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to portray - to depict or represent visually
  • Synonym: to depict - to represent by a picture or other visual means

Etymology

From Middle English limnen, from Anglo-French limner, from luminer 'to illuminate (a manuscript)', from Latin luminare 'to illuminate'.

3. VERB

/ lɪm / (AmE), / lɪm / (BrE)
to depict, portray, or describe in art or words, often in detail or with vividness
depict or describe in detail

Examples

The author masterfully limns the troubled emotions of her characters.
With only a few lines, the illustrator managed to limn a portrait of the dancer's grace.
Historians often limn the events of the era in vivid detail.

Synset Examples

She can limn a portrait in just a few strokes.
He loves to limn the landscapes he encounters during travels.
The artist will limn the scene with great detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depict - to represent or show in the form of a picture or description
  • Synonym: to describe - to give an account of in words

Etymology

From Middle English *limnen*, from Old French *enluminer* (to illuminate, adorn), from Latin *illuminare* (to light up, illuminate).
