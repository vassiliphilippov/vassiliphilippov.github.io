splashdown

1. NOUN

/ ˈsplæʃˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈsplæʃˌdaʊn / (BrE)
the landing of a spacecraft in the sea at the end of its flight
spacecraft landing in water

Examples

The capsule made a perfect splashdown in the Pacific Ocean.
After re-entering Earth's atmosphere, the astronauts prepared for splashdown.
News agencies broadcasted the live splashdown of the returning spacecraft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touchdown - the moment when an aircraft or spacecraft lands

Etymology

Formed from 'splash' + 'down', modeled after 'touchdown'.
