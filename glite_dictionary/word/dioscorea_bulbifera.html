Dioscorea bulbifera

1. NOUN

/ di.ɑːsˈkɔːri.ə bʌlˈbɪfərə / (AmE), / dɪ.ɒsˈkɔːri.ə bʌlˈbɪfərə / (BrE)
A species of yam native to tropical Africa and Asia, cultivated for its large, edible tubers.
species of yam cultivated for its tubers

Examples

Dioscorea bulbifera is often called the air potato because of the aerial tubers it produces.
Farmers in Southeast Asia sometimes grow Dioscorea bulbifera for both food and traditional medicine.
Researchers study Dioscorea bulbifera to understand its nutritional value and potential health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yam - edible starchy tuber of plants in the genus Dioscorea

Etymology

From Latin, genus name 'Dioscorea' (after the Greek physician Dioscorides) and Latin 'bulbifera' meaning 'bulb-bearing'.
