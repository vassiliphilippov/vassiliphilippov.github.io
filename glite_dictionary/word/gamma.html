2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæmə / (AmE), / ˈɡæmə / (BrE)
A unit of magnetic field strength equal to one-hundred-thousandth of an oersted, used chiefly in geophysics and related sciences.
unit of magnetic field strength

Examples

The Earth's magnetic field is often measured in gamma units by geophysicists.
A sudden increase of several hundred gamma indicated a geomagnetic storm.
Modern magnetometers can detect variations as small as one gamma.

Etymology

From the Greek letter gamma (Γ, γ), used as a symbol in scientific notation.
