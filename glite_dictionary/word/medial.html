medial

1. ADJ

/ ˈmiːdiəl / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiəl / (BrE)
situated in or relating to the middle; extending toward the midline of something, especially the body
relating to the middle or midline

Examples

The medial aspect of the knee is closest to the other knee.
In linguistics, a medial sound occurs in the middle of a word.
During surgery, the doctor made an incision along the medial line of the abdomen.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lateral - of, at, or toward the side

Etymology

From Latin 'medialis', from 'medius' meaning 'middle'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmiːdiəl / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiəl / (BrE)
relating to or situated toward the middle, especially referring to a plane dividing an animal or body into right and left halves
toward the middle; dividing right and left sides
medical

Examples

The medial plane divides the body into equal right and left halves.
The surgeon made an incision along the medial line of the abdomen.
In anatomy, structures closer to the medial axis are said to be 'medial' to those farther away.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lateral - relating to the side, especially farther from the midline

Etymology

From Latin 'medialis' (middle, of the middle).
