engrossed

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɡroʊst / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡrəʊst / (BrE)
so interested or involved in something that you give it all your attention
completely absorbed; giving full attention

Examples

She was so engrossed in her book that she didn't hear the phone ring.
The children sat engrossed in the movie, their eyes glued to the screen.
He became engrossed in his work and lost track of time.

Synset Examples

that engrossed look of rapt delight
enwrapped in dreams
so intent on this fantastic … narrative that she hardly stirred
rapt with wonder
wrapped in thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorbed - deeply involved or preoccupied
  • Antonym: distracted - unable to concentrate because one's mind is preoccupied

Etymology

From Middle English 'engrosen' (to write in large letters, engross), from Anglo-French 'engrosser', from 'en-' + 'gros' (large, great).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɡroʊst / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡrəʊst / (BrE)
(of a document) written formally in a large, clear, and official script, especially for legal use.
written in a formal, clear legal script
legal

Examples

The lawyer handed over the engrossed copy of the contract for the clients to sign.
An engrossed deed was preserved in the county archives as an official record.
During the ceremony, the engrossed charter was displayed for all attendees to admire.

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Middle English 'engrossen' (to write in large letters), from Old French 'en-' + 'gros' (large).
