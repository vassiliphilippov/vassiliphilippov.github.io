lateral

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
(in American football) a pass to a teammate that travels sideways or backward, rather than forward.
a sideways or backward pass (sports)

Examples

The quarterback threw a risky lateral to his running back.
The team lost possession after the lateral was fumbled.
A perfectly timed lateral helped the player avoid being tackled.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forward pass - an American football pass thrown forward toward the opponent's goal line

Etymology

From Latin 'lateralis' (of the side).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈlat.ər.əl / (BrE)
situated at, on, or directed toward the side; relating to the sides of something rather than the center or middle.
at or toward the side

Examples

The doctor observed a lateral movement in the patient's knee joint during the examination.
Branches growing in a lateral direction gave the tree a broad, sprawling shape.
The team made a lateral pass to avoid the opponent in the game.

Synset Examples

the lateral branches of a tree
shot out sidelong boughs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: medial - relating to the middle; opposite of lateral
  • Synonym: sideways - toward or from the side

Etymology

From Latin lateralis, from latus (side).

3. ADJ

/ ˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
situated on, toward, or from the side of something, especially away from the midline of the body or an organ
toward or on the side; away from the midline
medical

Examples

The fracture is located on the lateral aspect of the forearm.
In anatomy, the ears are lateral to the nose.
The MRI scan revealed damage to the lateral meniscus of the knee.

Synset Examples

lateral lemniscus

Related Terms

  • Antonym: medial - situated near the midline of the body or organ

Etymology

From Latin 'lateralis' (of or belonging to the side), from 'latus' (side).
