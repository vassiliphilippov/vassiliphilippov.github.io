discussion

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈskʌʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈskʌʃən / (BrE)
An act of talking about something with others and exchanging ideas or opinions.
exchange of views or conversation

Examples

The team held a discussion to plan their next strategy for the project.
After a lengthy discussion, they reached a unanimous decision.
We had a lively discussion about climate change during dinner.

Synset Examples

we had a good discussion
we had a word or two about it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debate - formal discussion on a particular topic
  • Synonym: conversation - informal talk between people

Etymology

From Latin 'discussio' (examination, investigation), from 'discutere' (to examine, strike asunder).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈskʌʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈskʌʃən / (BrE)
a detailed or extended consideration of a particular topic, often involving exchange of ideas or arguments
detailed consideration or exchange of ideas

Examples

The professor led a lively discussion about climate change with her students.
After reading the article, we had a thorough discussion on its main arguments.
The report includes an in-depth discussion of the economic impacts of the policy.

Synset Examples

the book contains an excellent discussion of modal logic
his treatment of the race question is badly biased

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debate - formal argument or exchange of opposing views
  • Degree Lower: conversation - informal spoken exchange of ideas or information

Etymology

From Latin 'discussio' (examination), from 'discutere' (to shake apart).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈskʌʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈskʌʃən / (BrE)
the act of talking about something with another person or a group to exchange ideas or reach a decision
conversation about a topic

Examples

The team held a discussion about the upcoming project to clarify everyone's roles.
After a lengthy discussion, they finally reached a compromise on the issue.
There will be a discussion following the lecture where students can ask questions.

Synset Examples

Parasitism is often overlooked in discussions of biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debate - formal discussion in which opposing arguments are put forward
  • Degree Lower: conversation - informal exchange of ideas by spoken words

Etymology

From Latin 'discussio' (examination, investigation), from 'discutere' (to examine, shake apart).
