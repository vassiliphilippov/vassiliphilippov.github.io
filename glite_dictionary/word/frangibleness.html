frangibleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrændʒəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfran.dʒɪ.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being easily broken, damaged, or destroyed; fragility.
quality of being easily broken

Examples

The frangibleness of the glass vase made it unsuitable for use in a busy household.
Scientists tested the frangibleness of the material under various pressure conditions.
Due to its frangibleness, the sculpture must be handled with great care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged
  • Antonym: robustness - the quality of being strong and unlikely to break or fail

Etymology

From Latin 'frangibilis' (breakable) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting quality or state).
