stumble

1. VERB

/ ˈstʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈstʌmbəl / (BrE)
to trip or lose balance while walking or running, often nearly falling
trip or nearly fall while moving

Examples

Be careful not to stumble on the uneven pavement.
She stumbled in the dark hallway but caught herself before falling.
He stumbles over his words when he's nervous.

Synset Examples

She stumbled over the tree root

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trip - to catch one's foot and almost fall
  • Degree Higher: fall - to drop down to a lower position, typically by accident

Etymology

From Middle English 'stumblen', probably of Scandinavian origin, related to Old Norse 'stumbra' (to stumble).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01904401-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈstʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈstʌmbəl / (BrE)
An act or instance of walking or moving unsteadily, often because of losing balance temporarily.
an unsteady movement or trip

Examples

His sudden stumble almost made him fall down the stairs.
After a long run, she finished with a tired stumble over the finish line.
The actor recovered gracefully from his stumble during the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trip - to catch one's foot and almost fall

Etymology

From Middle English 'stoumblen', probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian 'stumla'.
