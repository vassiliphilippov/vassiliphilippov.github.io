streptolysin

1. NOUN

/ strɛpˈtoʊlɪsɪn / (AmE), / strɛpˈtɒlɪsɪn / (BrE)
a hemolytic toxin produced by certain strains of streptococcus bacteria, capable of breaking down red blood cells
hemolytic toxin from streptococcus bacteria
medical

Examples

The laboratory detected streptolysin in the patient's blood culture, confirming a streptococcal infection.
Streptolysin is known to damage red blood cells during severe cases of strep throat.
Researchers are studying how streptolysin contributes to the bacteria's ability to cause disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemolysin - a substance that causes the destruction of red blood cells

Etymology

From 'strepto-' (twisted chain, referring to bacteria) + 'lysin' (dissolver, from Greek 'lysis')
