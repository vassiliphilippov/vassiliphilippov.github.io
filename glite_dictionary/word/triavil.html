Triavil

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪəˌvɪl / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.ə.vɪl / (BrE)
A trademarked medication combining a tranquilizer and antidepressant, sometimes used as an antiemetic for adults.
trade name for a tranquilizer and antidepressant

Examples

Her doctor prescribed Triavil to help manage her symptoms of depression and anxiety.
Some patients experienced drowsiness as a side effect of Triavil.
Although Triavil is sometimes used as an antiemetic, it is primarily known as a psychiatric medication.
