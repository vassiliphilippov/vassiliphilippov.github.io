to assuage

1. VERB

/ əˈsweɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈsweɪdʒ / (BrE)
to make someone less angry or hostile; to gain someone's goodwill by calming or appeasing them
to calm or appease someone's anger

Examples

She tried to assuage his anger with a sincere apology and a calm explanation.
The company offered a refund to assuage dissatisfied customers.
His kind words did little to assuage her disappointment after the defeat.

Synset Examples

She managed to mollify the angry customer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mollify - to soothe or calm someone's feelings
  • Synonym: to appease - to calm or pacify someone, often by acceding to their demands
  • Antonym: to aggravate - to make a situation worse or more intense

Etymology

From Old French assouagier, from Vulgar Latin *assuaviare, from Latin suavis (sweet).

2. VERB

/ əˈsweɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈsweɪdʒ / (BrE)
to satisfy a desire or need, such as thirst; to relieve or quench
to satisfy or quench (thirst, desire)

Examples

The cool lemonade assuaged her thirst after a long, hot walk.
He hoped the clear springwater would assuage his parched throat.
No amount of soda could assuage his thirst after running the marathon.

Synset Examples

The cold water quenched his thirst

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to quench - to satisfy (thirst) by drinking; to put out

Etymology

From Old French *assuager*, from Latin *assuaviare*, from *ad* (to) + *suavis* (sweet).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01206641-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈsweɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈsweɪdʒ / (BrE)
to make an unpleasant feeling or pain less severe; to relieve or ease
make pain or feelings less severe

Examples

The nurse gave him medicine to assuage his pain after surgery.
Cold compresses can help assuage the discomfort of a minor burn.
This ointment should assuage the irritation caused by insect bites.

Synset Examples

This pill will relieve your headaches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relieve - to reduce or remove pain or discomfort
  • Synonym: to alleviate - to make pain or a problem less severe
  • Antonym: to exacerbate - to make something worse, especially pain or a problem

Etymology

From Latin 'assuaviare' (to make sweet, to soften), from 'ad' (to) + 'suavis' (sweet).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
