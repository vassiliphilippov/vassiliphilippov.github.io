Falco peregrinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌfælkoʊ pəˈrɛɡrɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌfalkəʊ pəˈrɛɡrɪnəs / (BrE)
a species of falcon, also known as the peregrine falcon, widely distributed and known for its speed; formerly used in falconry
species of falcon (peregrine)

Examples

The Falco peregrinus is often considered the fastest bird in the world when in a hunting dive.
Conservation efforts have helped restore Falco peregrinus populations in many regions.
In medieval times, Falco peregrinus was highly prized for use in falconry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peregrine falcon - common name for Falco peregrinus, a species of falcon noted for speed

Etymology

From Latin: falco (falcon) + peregrinus (foreigner, wanderer).
