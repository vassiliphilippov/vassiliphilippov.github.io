Araucaria cunninghamii

1. NOUN

/ ˌæ.rəˈker.i.ə ˌkʌn.ɪŋˈhæm.i.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌa.rɔːˈkeə.ri.ə ˌkʌn.ɪŋˈhæm.i.aɪ / (BrE)
a species of pine native to Australia and New Guinea, valued for its light, even-textured wood; commonly known as the hoop pine
Australian and New Guinean pine tree species

Examples

The timber of the Araucaria cunninghamii is prized for furniture and construction.
In Queensland, plantations of Araucaria cunninghamii are an important resource.
Many parks in Australia feature mature Araucaria cunninghamii as ornamental trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoop pine - common name for Araucaria cunninghamii, an evergreen tree

Etymology

Genus name from Araucano (Arauco, a region in Chile); species named for Allan Cunningham, English botanist.
