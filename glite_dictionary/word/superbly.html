superbly

1. ADV

/ səˈpɝːbli / (AmE), / səˈpɜːbli / (BrE)
In an excellent or outstanding manner; extremely well.
in an excellent manner

Examples

The chef prepared the meal superbly, impressing everyone at the table.
She handled the situation superbly, showing great composure under pressure.
The play was superbly acted, with every character perfectly cast.

Synset Examples

her voice is superbly disciplined
the colors changed wondrously slowly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excellently - in an excellent manner
  • Antonym: badly - in a poor or inferior manner

Etymology

From 'superb' + '-ly'; 'superb' from Latin 'superbus' (proud, splendid).
