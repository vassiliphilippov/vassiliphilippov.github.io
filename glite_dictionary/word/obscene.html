obscene

1. ADJ

/ əbˈsin / (AmE), / əbˈsiːn / (BrE)
offensive or indecent, especially in a way that is sexually suggestive or contrary to accepted standards of morality
sexually suggestive or indecent

Examples

The magazine was banned for publishing obscene images considered offensive by authorities.
She received a series of obscene phone calls that made her feel uncomfortable.
Some people found his jokes obscene, while others thought they were just harmless fun.

Synset Examples

lewd pictures
obscene telephone calls
salacious limericks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lewd - crude and offensive in a sexual way
  • Synonym: salacious - having or conveying undue or inappropriate interest in sexual matters
  • Antonym: decent - conforming to accepted standards of morality or respectability

Etymology

From Latin obscēnus (foul, repulsive).

2. ADJ

/ əbˈsin / (AmE), / əbˈsiːn / (BrE)
offensive or indecent, especially because it is designed to provoke sexual desire or indecency
sexually offensive or indecent

Examples

The committee found the film's content to be obscene and banned it from theaters.
Some critics argued that the performance was obscene and not suitable for broadcast.
Her artwork was labeled as obscene due to its explicit imagery.

  • Synonym: lewd - crude and offensive in a sexual way
  • Antonym: decent - conforming with acceptable standards; not obscene

Etymology

4. ADJ

/ əbˈsin / (AmE), / əbˈsiːn / (BrE)
(of an amount, quality, or degree) shockingly or unreasonably excessive.
shockingly excessive or extreme (amount/degree)

Examples

The CEO received an obscene bonus last year, far surpassing what most employees made.
Some luxury homes are listed for obscene amounts that only the super-rich can afford.
Critics called the corporation's profits obscene during economic downturns.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
