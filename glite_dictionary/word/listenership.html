listenership

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪsənərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈlɪsənəʃɪp / (BrE)
the group of people who listen to a particular radio program, station, or broadcaster
collective audience of radio listeners

Examples

The morning talk show has steadily increased its listenership over the past year.
Due to the engaging content, the station's listenership doubled during the summer months.
Advertisers are keen to target programs with a large listenership.

Synset Examples

In 2012, the share of radio listenership on mobile phones has increased by 10%, as compared to 2009.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audience - the assembled spectators or listeners at an event such as a play, movie, or concert
  • Synonym: viewership - audience of viewers, especially of television

Etymology

From 'listener' + '-ship', modelled after forms like 'readership'.
