dustbin

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌstˌbɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʌs(t)bɪn / (BrE)
a container for holding household rubbish or waste until it is collected
container for holding rubbish
british

Examples

Please remember to put the empty bottles into the dustbin before you leave.
The dustbin was overflowing, so it needed to be taken out.
She found her old notebook in the dustbin while taking out the trash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trash can - a container for waste, especially in American English
  • Synonym: garbage can - another North American term for waste container

Etymology

From 'dust' + 'bin', originally referring to bins for household ash and dust in the 19th century.
