drafting

1. NOUN

/ ˈdræftɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɑːftɪŋ / (BrE)
the skill or technique of drawing detailed technical plans or blueprints, especially for engineering or architecture
drawing technical blueprints or plans

Examples

She took a course in drafting before applying to architecture school.
Modern drafting is often done with computer-aided design software instead of paper and pencils.
His job at the engineering firm primarily involves drafting building plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: technical drawing - producing precise diagrams or plans using standard conventions

Etymology

From draft (variant of draught), meaning a preliminary version; the sense evolved to denote plan drawing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdræftɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɑːftɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of creating detailed drawings, diagrams, or plans, especially as an artistic or technical skill
creating artistic or technical drawings

Examples

She spent the afternoon drafting detailed plans for the new building.
His career in drafting began with sketching simple diagrams in high school.
The architect's drafting was precise and beautifully arranged on the blueprints.

Synset Examples

he learned drawing from his father

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawing - the act or process of producing a picture with lines

Etymology

From 'draft' (variant of 'draught'), ultimately from Old English 'dragan' (to draw, drag).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdræftɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɑːftɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of preparing a preliminary version of a piece of writing, usually to be revised and improved later
writing a preliminary version

Examples

The novelist spent weeks drafting the first chapters of her new book.
Good drafting is essential before submitting any important report.
During drafting, try not to worry about making everything perfect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: editing - the process of making changes and corrections to improve a text
  • Synonym: revision - the process of making changes to an earlier draft to improve it
  • Degree Higher: finalizing - the process of completing and approving a final version

Etymology

From 'draft' (noun, meaning a preliminary version), ultimately from Middle English 'draught' (drawing, pull), from Old English 'dræht'.
