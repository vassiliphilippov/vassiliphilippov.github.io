equity

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkwəti / (AmE), / ˈɛkwɪti / (BrE)
the value of a property after deducting the amount of any claims, such as mortgages, against it
value of property minus what is owed

Examples

She used the equity in her home to secure a loan for her business.
After years of mortgage payments, their equity in the house had grown significantly.
The bank calculates the equity by subtracting the remaining mortgage from the property's market value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asset - something valuable owned, especially property

Etymology

From Old French equité, from Latin aequitas (evenness, fairness; equality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛkwəti / (AmE), / ˈɛkwɪti / (BrE)
the value of shares issued by a company or the ownership interest held by shareholders in a corporation
ownership interest in a corporation

Examples

Investors were eager to increase their equity in the growing tech company.
The startup raised funds by selling equity to several venture capitalists.
She owns 40% equity in her family business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock - shares representing ownership in a company
  • Antonym: debt - money owed by an entity, contrasted with ownership interest

Etymology

From Old French 'equité', from Latin 'aequitas' (evenness, fairness).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛkwɪti / (AmE), / ˈɛkwɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being fair or just; adherence to rules or standards of fairness
fairness; just treatment

Examples

All employees should be treated with equity in the workplace.
The new policy was praised for promoting equity among students.
She demanded equity in the division of the inheritance.

Synset Examples

the judge recognized the fairness of my claim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairness - the quality of being fair and reasonable
  • Synonym: justice - the quality of being just, impartial, or fair
  • Antonym: inequity - lack of fairness or justice

Etymology

From Old French equité, from Latin aequitas (evenness, fairness).
