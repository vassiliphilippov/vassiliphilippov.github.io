Equetus pulcher

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈkwiːtəs ˈpʊlkər / (AmE), / ɛˈkwiːtəs ˈpʊlkə / (BrE)
A species of tropical drumfish, also known as the spotted drum, found in the western Atlantic Ocean.
a species of tropical drumfish

Examples

The juvenile Equetus pulcher is known for its striking black and white stripes.
Divers often spot Equetus pulcher swimming near coral reefs in the Caribbean.
Marine biologists study Equetus pulcher to learn more about tropical fish biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted drum - common name for Equetus pulcher

Etymology

From Latin: 'Equetus' is the fish genus; 'pulcher' means 'beautiful'.
