Collinsia

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪnziə / (AmE), / kɒˈlɪnziə / (BrE)
A genus of hardy annual herbs native to the western United States, known for their showy, often blue or purple flowers.
genus of western US annual herbs

Examples

Several species of Collinsia can be found growing wild in California’s woodlands.
Gardeners appreciate Collinsia for its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.
The blue blossoms of Collinsia attract bees and other pollinators throughout the spring.

Etymology

Named after British botanist Zacchaeus Collins (1764–1831).
