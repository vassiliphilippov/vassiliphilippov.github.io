Michelson

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɪkəlsən / (AmE), / ˈmɪkəlsən / (BrE)
(1852–1931) United States physicist, born in Germany, known for collaborating with Morley in the Michelson-Morley experiment.
U.S. physicist, co-conducted the Michelson-Morley experiment

Examples

Albert Michelson became the first American to win a Nobel Prize in science.
The famous Michelson-Morley experiment, led by Michelson and Morley, challenged the theory of the ether.
Many physics textbooks describe Michelson's contributions to the measurement of the speed of light.
