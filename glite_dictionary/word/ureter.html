ureter

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈritər / (AmE), / jʊəˈriːtə / (BrE)
either of the pair of muscular tubes that carry urine from the kidneys to the urinary bladder in humans and other vertebrates
tube carrying urine from kidney to bladder
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained how the ureter could sometimes become blocked by kidney stones.
Urine travels down each ureter on its way from the kidneys to the bladder.
A tumor near the ureter may cause pain and difficulty with urination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: urethra - the tube that carries urine from the bladder to the outside of the body

Etymology

From Greek ouretēr (urinary duct), from ouron (urine).
