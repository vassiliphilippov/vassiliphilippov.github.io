whoremaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈhɔːˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
A man who is a customer of prostitutes; an habitual patron of brothels or prostitutes, now chiefly archaic.
customer of prostitutes (archaic)
archaic offensive

Examples

In Elizabethan drama, the whoremaster was often portrayed as a figure of vice.
He was scorned as a notorious whoremaster throughout the city.
The old court records mention fines levied against any known whoremaster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: john - a man who is a prostitute's customer (slang)

Etymology

From Middle English 'hore' (prostitute) + 'master' (one who controls or manages).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrˌmæstɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
an archaic and offensive term for a man who procures prostitutes; a pimp
man who procures prostitutes; pimp
archaic offensive

Examples

In several old plays, the villain is described as a notorious whoremaster.
He was labeled a whoremaster by the authorities and banished from the city.
The term whoremaster now sounds antiquated and is rarely used in modern English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pimp - a person, especially a man, who controls prostitutes and arranges clients for them, taking a portion of their earnings

Etymology

From Middle English whoremastre, combining 'whore' and 'master'.
