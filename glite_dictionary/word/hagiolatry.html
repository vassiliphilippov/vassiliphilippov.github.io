hagiolatry

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæɡiˈɑlətri / (AmE), / ˌhæɡiˈɒlətri / (BrE)
the worship or veneration of saints
worship of saints

Examples

Some critics accused the church of promoting hagiolatry along with its other traditions.
The Reformation challenged the hagiolatry that had become common in medieval Europe.
His studies focus on the origins and development of hagiolatry in early Christianity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idolatry - the worship of idols

Etymology

From Greek hagios (holy) + -latry (worship).
