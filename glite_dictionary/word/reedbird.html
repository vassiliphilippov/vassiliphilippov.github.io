reedbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːdbɝːd / (AmE), / ˈriːdbɜːd / (BrE)
A small European warbler that breeds among reeds and sedges and winters in Africa.
small European warbler

Examples

The reedbird is well known for its melodious song during the breeding season.
Ornithologists spotted a flock of reedbirds nesting by the riverbank.
Each autumn, thousands of reedbirds migrate south to spend the winter in Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedge warbler - a small brown and cream European warbler often called 'reedbird'

2. NOUN

/ ˈridˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈriːdˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A migratory American songbird, especially the bobolink, often found in marshy or reedy areas.
migratory American songbird

Examples

We spotted a reedbird perched quietly among the cattails at dawn.
The song of the reedbird fills the marshes every spring as it migrates north.
Scientists tracked the movements of the reedbird to understand its migration patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bobolink - a migratory North American songbird (Dolichonyx oryzivorus)

Etymology

From 'reed' (the tall grass) + 'bird'.
