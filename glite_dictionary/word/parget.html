parget

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːr.dʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.dʒɪt / (BrE)
Plaster or a similar material used to coat the exterior of walls or line the inside of chimneys, especially in historical buildings.
plaster for coating walls or lining chimneys

Examples

The historic cottage featured intricate parget designs on its exterior walls.
Restorers carefully reapplied parget to the old chimney to preserve its original look.
Much of the medieval town's charm comes from the decorative parget seen on many buildings.

Etymology

From Old French 'pargeter' (to coat with plaster), related to 'parjetter' (to throw about).

2. VERB

/ ˈpɑrdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɑːdʒɪt / (BrE)
to apply ornamental plaster or stucco, especially as decoration on walls or ceilings
apply ornamental plaster

Examples

They hired skilled artisans to parget the ceiling with intricate floral designs.
In the restoration project, workers carefully pargeted the 17th-century façade.
She specializes in pargeting historic buildings to preserve their original charm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stucco - to coat or decorate with a fine plaster

Etymology

From Old French *pargeter* (to throw about, plaster), related to *parjir* (to adorn), ultimately from Latin *pingere* (to paint).
