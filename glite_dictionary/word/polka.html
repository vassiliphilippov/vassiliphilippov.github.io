polka

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊlkə / (AmE), / ˈpɒlkə / (BrE)
a lively Central European dance in quick triple time, characterized by three steps and a hop
fast Bohemian dance with steps and a hop

Examples

The band played a lively polka that got everyone on their feet.
At the festival, couples joined in a traditional polka competition.
He learned to polka from his grandparents, who grew up dancing it in Bohemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waltz - a ballroom dance in triple time performed in closed position

Etymology

From Czech 'polka', meaning 'Polish woman', reflecting its origins in 19th-century Bohemia.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊl.kə / (AmE), / ˈpɒl.kə / (BrE)
a lively dance of Bohemian origin, or the music performed for such a dance, characterized by a quick tempo in duple time.
a lively dance or its music

Examples

The band played a lively polka that had everyone on the dance floor.
My grandmother enjoys listening to traditional polka music at family gatherings.
They learned to dance the polka at a folk festival last summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waltz - a ballroom dance in triple time
  • Synonym: tango - a ballroom dance of Latin American origin

Etymology

From Czech polka (a dance), probably from polka (Polish woman, feminization of Polák ‘Pole’), but possibly from Czech půlka (‘half’, referring to the half-step used in the dance).

Synset POS: n
