gaberdine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæbərˌdin / (AmE), / ˈɡæbədɪn / (BrE)
a long, loose outer garment or coverall, often reaching down to the ankles
long, loose outer garment

Examples

The traveler wore a gaberdine to protect himself from the harsh weather.
In the play, the actor appeared onstage dressed in a ragged gaberdine.
He buttoned his gaberdine before stepping out into the rain.

Etymology

From Old French *gabardine*, probably from Spanish *gabardina* (a type of cloak).
