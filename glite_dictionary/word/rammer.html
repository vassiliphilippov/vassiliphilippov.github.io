rammer

1. NOUN

/ ˈræmər / (AmE), / ˈræmə / (BrE)
a tool used for driving or forcing something into place by repeated blows or pressure
tool for driving or forcing objects

Examples

The construction crew used a rammer to pack the soil tightly.
He bought a new rammer for his paving business.
A heavy rammer is required to drive fence posts into the ground.

Etymology

From 'ram' (to forcefully drive) + '-er' (agent suffix), dating back to the 17th century.
