following

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɑloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
coming immediately after something in time or order
coming next in sequence

Examples

We met again on the following morning to discuss the issue.
In the following chapters, the author explores different theories in detail.
Please answer the following questions to complete the survey.

Synset Examples

the following day
next in line
the next president
the next item on the list

Related Terms

  • Synonym: next - coming immediately after, especially in time, order, or place
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00128838-s
Synset POS: s

/ ˈfɑloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
about to be mentioned or specified; coming next in order or in a list
about to be mentioned or specified

Examples

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɑloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
(of wind or current) moving in the same direction as a vehicle or traveler, aiding progress.
moving in the same direction as movement

Examples

We were lucky to have a following wind all the way across the Atlantic.
With a following current, the boat picked up speed.
The pilot reported a following breeze, making the landing easier.

Synset Examples

a following wind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: headwind - a wind blowing in the opposite direction to movement
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00998712-s
Synset POS: s

/ ˈfɑloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
coming or going after something or someone in the same direction; moving immediately behind.
coming or going after; moving behind

Examples

5. NOUN

/ ˈfɑloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
A group of people who support, admire, or enthusiastically follow a particular person, group, or idea.
group of supporters or admirers

Examples

The singer has a large following in Europe and Asia.
Her blog quickly gained a devoted following among tech enthusiasts.
Despite being new to the scene, he managed to build a small but loyal following.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fan - an enthusiastic admirer or supporter
  • Synonym: supporter - someone who supports a person, group, or cause

Etymology

From the verb 'follow', ultimately from Old English 'folgian'.
