atrociousness

1. NOUN

/ əˈtroʊʃəsnəs / (AmE), / əˈtrəʊʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being shockingly cruel, brutal, or inhumane
the state of being shockingly cruel or brutal

Examples

The atrociousness of the crime shocked the entire community.
Historians still discuss the atrociousness exhibited during the conflict.
No one could ignore the sheer atrociousness of his actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrocity - an extremely wicked or cruel act

Etymology

From atrocious + -ness, dating to at least the early 19th century.
