to demobilise

1. VERB

/ dɪˈmoʊ.bəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈməʊ.bɪ.laɪz / (BrE)
to release (someone) from military service and return them to civilian life
release from military service

Examples

After the war, the government began to demobilise thousands of soldiers.
Many veterans were demobilised and returned to civilian jobs.
Plans are in place to demobilise the army once the peace agreement is signed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to mobilise - to prepare and organize troops for active service

Etymology

From French démobiliser, from dé- (reverse) + mobiliser (to mobilize).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈməʊbɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to release someone from military service and return them to civilian life
retire from military service
british

Examples

After the war, thousands of soldiers were demobilised and returned to their hometowns.
The government decided to demobilise most of the army following the peace agreement.
He was demobilised in 1946 and soon found work as a teacher.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to mobilise - to prepare and organize troops for service
  • Synonym: to demobilize - American English spelling: to retire from military service

Etymology

From French 'démobiliser', based on 'mobiliser' (to mobilize).
