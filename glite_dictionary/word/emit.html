to emit

1. VERB

/ ɪˈmɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪt / (BrE)
to produce and release a sound or utterance; to express audibly
produce or release a sound

Examples

She couldn't help but emit a small gasp when she saw the accident.
The machine suddenly emits a loud buzzing sound every hour.
During the speech, some audience members emitted disapproving murmurs.

Synset Examples

She let out a big heavy sigh
He uttered strange sounds that nobody could understand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utter - to express audibly; to say or pronounce
  • Antonym: to suppress - to prevent the expression of a sound or feeling

Etymology

From Latin 'emittere', from 'e-' (out) + 'mittere' (send).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈmɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪt / (BrE)
to send out or release (a gas, odor, sound, or light) into the surrounding area
send out (gas, odor, sound, or light)

Examples

Some flowers emit a pleasant fragrance in the evening.
The factory was found to emit dangerous gases into the atmosphere.
When heated, the substance begins to emit smoke and an unpleasant odor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - to allow something to be set free or emitted
  • Antonym: to absorb - to take in or soak up a substance, often used as an antonym to 'emit' for gases or light

Etymology

From Latin emittere, from ex- (out) + mittere (send).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00105087-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪˈmɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪt / (BrE)
to send out or give off something such as light, heat, sound, gas, or radiation
send out light, sound, heat, etc.

Examples

Many plants emit oxygen during the process of photosynthesis.
The factory was fined because it emitted toxic gases into the atmosphere.
Stars emit light and heat as part of the process of nuclear fusion.

Synset Examples

The ozone layer blocks some harmful rays which the sun emits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - to let go or set free; to allow something to escape

Etymology

From Latin emettere (to send out), from ex- (out) + mittere (to send).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02773266-v
Synset POS: v
