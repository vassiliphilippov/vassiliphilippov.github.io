intralobular

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.trəˈloʊ.bjələr / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈləʊ.bjʊ.lə / (BrE)
Located or occurring within a lobe or lobule, especially in a gland or organ.
within a lobe or lobule (medical)
medical

Examples

The biopsy revealed an intralobular pattern of fibrosis in the tissue sample.
In the liver, intralobular ducts play an important role in bile transport.
Pathologists noted intralobular inflammation as a key sign of the disease.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interlobular - situated between lobes or lobules

Etymology

From Latin intra- (within) + lobulus (small lobe).
