Rhapis excelsa

1. NOUN

/ ˈræpɪs ɛkˈsɛlsə / (AmE), / ˈræpɪs ɛkˈsɛlsə / (BrE)
A small, graceful species of palm with slender, reedlike stems and fibrous leaf bases, commonly grown as an ornamental plant indoors and outdoors.
a species of small ornamental palm

Examples

The Rhapis excelsa is often used as a decorative plant in offices and homes.
Botanists admire the slender stems and lush green leaves of Rhapis excelsa.
Because it thrives in low light, Rhapis excelsa is a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Rhapis' is the genus name from Greek 'rhapís' (rod, stick), and 'excelsa' means 'lofty' or 'tall' in Latin.
