Cleridae

1. NOUN

/ klɛˈraɪˌdiː / (AmE), / klɪəˈrɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of beetles, commonly known as checkered beetles, that prey on other insects.
family of predatory beetles

Examples

Some species in the Cleridae family help control agricultural pests by feeding on harmful insects.
The bright colors and patterns of Cleridae make them easy to distinguish from other beetles.
Entomologists collected several Cleridae specimens during their field research in the forest.

Etymology

From New Latin Cleridae, based on the genus Clerus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈklɛrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of beetles, commonly known as checkered beetles, that prey on other insects
family of predatory beetles (checkered beetles)

Examples

Many species within the Cleridae family are brightly colored and found on flowers.
The Cleridae are known for feeding on wood-boring insect larvae.
Entomologists often study Cleridae beetles to control pest populations in forests.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Cleridae', based on the genus name 'Clerus' (Greek 'klēros', meaning 'lot' or 'allotment').
