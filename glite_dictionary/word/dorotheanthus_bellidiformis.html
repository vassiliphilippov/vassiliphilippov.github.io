Dorotheanthus bellidiformis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɔːrəˈθiːænθəs ˌbɛlɪˈdɪfɔːrmɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɒrəˈθiːænθəs ˌbɛlɪˈdɪfɔːmɪs / (BrE)
A low-growing, showy succulent annual native to South Africa, with white, pink, red, or orange flowers and spatulate, crystal-like leaves.
a flowering succulent annual of South Africa

Examples

Gardeners appreciate Dorotheanthus bellidiformis for its dazzling display of brightly colored blooms.
Dorotheanthus bellidiformis thrives best in sunny rock gardens with well-drained soil.
During the spring, the beds were filled with blooming Dorotheanthus bellidiformis, attracting visitors to the botanical garden.

Etymology

From Greek *doros* (gift), *theanthos* (divine flower), and Latin *bellidiformis* (daisy-like in shape).
