dodger

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑdʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɒdʒə / (BrE)
A small, oval-shaped cake of corn bread, usually baked or fried, especially in Southern U.S. cuisine.
small southern corn bread cake
american

Examples

Corn dodger is a traditional side dish at many southern barbecues.
She fried a batch of golden dodgers for breakfast every Sunday.
The recipe calls for baking each dodger until crisp on the outside but soft inside.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːdʒər / (AmE), / ˈdɒdʒə / (BrE)
a person who avoids an obligation or engages in deception, often by clever or dishonest means
shifty or deceptive person

Examples

The tax dodger was finally caught by the authorities after years of evasion.
Everyone knew him as a dodger who was always looking for a way around the rules.
She's no dodger, and always fulfills her responsibilities honestly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - a person who behaves dishonestly to gain an advantage
  • Antonym: honest person - a person who is truthful and does not deceive

Etymology

From 'dodge' (to evade), with '-er' suffix forming agent nouns.
