bucolic

1. NOUN

/ bjuˈkɑlɪk / (AmE), / bjuːˈkɒlɪk / (BrE)
a short poem describing rural or pastoral life; a pastoral poem
a pastoral poem

Examples

The poet's latest bucolic evokes the simplicity of country living.
She recited a charming bucolic at the poetry reading last night.
In his bucolic, the fields and shepherds become symbols of peace and contentment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastoral - a work of literature portraying idealized rural life

Etymology

From Latin *bucolicus*, from Greek *boukolikos* (pastoral, relating to herdsmen).
