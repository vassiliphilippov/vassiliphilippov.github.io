cyberpunk

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪbərˌpʌŋk / (AmE), / ˈsaɪbəˌpʌŋk / (BrE)
a genre of science fiction set in a dystopian future, typically featuring advanced technology, cybernetics, and oppressive societal structures
science fiction genre with futuristic, high-tech dystopias

Examples

The novel is a classic of the cyberpunk genre, set in a neon-lit city ruled by powerful corporations.
Many popular video games incorporate cyberpunk themes and aesthetics, featuring hackers and artificial intelligence.
The film's gritty, high-tech world is a perfect example of cyberpunk storytelling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dystopia - an imagined society characterized by great suffering or injustice
  • Synonym: science fiction - genre involving imaginative concepts such as futuristic science and technology

Etymology

Blend of 'cybernetics' and 'punk', first used in the 1980s.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪbərˌpʌŋk / (AmE), / ˈsaɪbəˌpʌŋk / (BrE)
a writer of science fiction whose work is set in a dystopian future dominated by computer technology and lawless subcultures
science fiction writer in a tech-dominated dystopia

Examples

William Gibson is regarded as a leading cyberpunk for his influential vision of the digital future.
As a cyberpunk, she explores the darker sides of technology and society in her novels.
Many readers appreciate the unique worlds that a talented cyberpunk can create.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: science fiction writer - author who writes works of science fiction

Etymology

Blend of 'cybernetics' and 'punk', popularized in the 1980s to describe a new style of science fiction.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
