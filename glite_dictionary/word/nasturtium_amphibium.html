Nasturtium amphibium

1. NOUN

/ næˈstɝːʃəm æmˈfɪbiəm / (AmE), / næˈstɜːʃəm æmˈfɪbiəm / (BrE)
A perennial herb found along streams and riversides throughout Europe, sometimes classified under the genus Nasturtium.
a European perennial water-side herb

Examples

Nasturtium amphibium grows best in moist soils near streams or ponds.
Botanists recently debated whether Nasturtium amphibium should remain in this genus or be reclassified.
The yellow flowers of Nasturtium amphibium are easily recognized in the European countryside.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Nasturtium' from Latin nasturtium (watercress), 'amphibium' from Greek 'amphibios' (living a double life, amphibious).
