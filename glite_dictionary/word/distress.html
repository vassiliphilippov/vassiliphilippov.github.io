distress

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrɛs / (AmE), / dɪˈstres / (BrE)
the legal seizure and holding of property to compel payment of a debt or the fulfillment of an obligation
seizure of property for debt payment (legal)
legal

Examples

Under the law of landlord and tenant, distress was used to recover unpaid rent.
The creditor used distress to take possession of the debtor's assets.
Although distress is less common today, it once played a major role in enforcing claims.

Synset Examples

Originally distress was a landlord's remedy against a tenant for unpaid rents or property damage but now the landlord is given a landlord's lien

Etymology

From Old French destresce, from Latin districtus (past participle of distringere, to stretch apart, compel).
