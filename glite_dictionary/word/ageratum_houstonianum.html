Ageratum houstonianum

1. NOUN

/ ˌædʒəˈreɪtəm ˌhuːstənˈiːənəm / (AmE), / ˌadʒəˈreɪtəm ˌhjuːstənˈiːənəm / (BrE)
A small tender herbaceous plant, often grown for its fluffy, brushlike blue to lavender flowers.
ornamental herb with blue or lavender blooms

Examples

Gardeners often choose Ageratum houstonianum for its long-lasting blue blooms.
We planted Ageratum houstonianum along the border to add a splash of color to the garden.
The fluffy flowers of Ageratum houstonianum attract butterflies throughout the summer.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'ageratos' (ageless) and species named after Scottish botanist William Houston.
