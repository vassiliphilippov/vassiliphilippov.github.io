isolation

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of separating someone or something from others; the state of being alone or kept apart.
act of separating or being alone

Examples

Many patients experienced a sense of isolation during the long hospital stay.
His isolation from the group made it difficult for him to adjust.
The research lab ensures the isolation of sensitive samples to prevent contamination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seclusion - the state of being private and away from other people
  • Antonym: integration - the process of combining or incorporating into a whole

Etymology

From French *isolation*, from 'isoler' (to isolate), from Italian *isolare*, from 'isola' (island), from Latin *insula* (island).

2. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(Psychiatry) A defense mechanism in which the memory of an unacceptable act or impulse is separated from the emotion originally associated with it.
psychiatric defense mechanism separating feelings from memory
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited isolation by recalling the incident without any visible emotional reaction.
In therapy, understanding isolation can help address unresolved trauma.
Freud described isolation as a way people separate disturbing thoughts from their feelings.

Etymology

From French 'isolation,' from 'isolé' (isolated), from Italian 'isolato,' from Latin 'insula' (island).
