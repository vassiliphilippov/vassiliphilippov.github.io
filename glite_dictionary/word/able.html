able

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪbl / (BrE)
having the skills, qualities, or qualifications needed to do something well
having necessary skills or qualifications

Examples

She is an able leader who manages her team with skill and confidence.
The school is fortunate to have many able teachers on staff.
Although young, he proved himself an able organizer of the event.

Synset Examples

able teachers
a capable administrator
children as young as 14 can be extremely capable and dependable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capable - having the ability, fitness, or quality necessary to do or achieve a specified thing

Etymology

From Latin habilis (easily handled, apt) via Old French able.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00512273-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈeɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.bəl / (BrE)
physically strong and healthy; fit for duty or service, especially used to describe someone capable of physical work or military service
physically strong and healthy

Examples

Only able crew members were assigned to work in the engine room.
During the war, every able man was expected to serve his country.
The navy promoted him to able seaman after completing his training.

Synset Examples

an able seaman
every able-bodied young man served in the army

Related Terms

  • Synonym: able-bodied - physically strong and healthy; not disabled

Etymology

From Latin habilis (able, capable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01021031-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈeɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.bəl / (BrE)
having the necessary skill, means, or opportunity to do something (usually followed by 'to')
having the skill or means to do something

Examples

Despite his injury, he was able to finish the race.
Will you be able to attend the meeting tomorrow?
She is able to speak three different languages fluently.

Synset Examples

able to swim
she was able to program her computer
we were at last able to buy a car
able to get a grant for the project

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unable - lacking the skill, means, or opportunity to do something
  • Synonym: capable - having the ability, fitness, or quality necessary to do or achieve a specified thing

Etymology

From Latin habilis (easily handled, apt, skillful), through Old French able.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00001740-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADJ

/ ˈeɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.bəl / (BrE)
having the power, skill, means, or opportunity to do something; having the necessary physical or mental capacity
having the capacity to do something

Examples

Despite his injury, he was still able to finish the race.
Children are often able to learn new languages very quickly.
Thanks to the new software, we are now able to track our expenses more efficiently.

Synset Examples

able to learn
human beings are able to walk on two feet
Superman is able to leap tall buildings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capable - having the ability or qualities necessary to do something
  • Antonym: unable - lacking the power, skill, or means to do something

Etymology

From Latin habilis (easily handled, apt), later through Old French able.

Source: WordNet
