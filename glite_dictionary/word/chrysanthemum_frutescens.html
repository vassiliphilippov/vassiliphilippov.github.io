Chrysanthemum frutescens

1. NOUN

/ krɪˌsænθəməm fruːˈtɛsɛnz / (AmE), / krɪˌsænθəməm fruːˈtɛsɛnz / (BrE)
A perennial subshrub native to the Canary Islands, often cultivated for its pale yellow daisylike flowers; also known as the marguerite daisy.
a Canary Islands subshrub with pale yellow daisy flowers

Examples

The Chrysanthemum frutescens is a popular choice for patio planters due to its abundant blooms.
She planted several Chrysanthemum frutescens varieties along the garden border.
Botanists often debate whether Chrysanthemum frutescens should be placed in the genus Chrysanthemum or Argyranthemum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marguerite daisy - Common name for Chrysanthemum frutescens

Etymology

From Greek *chrysos* (gold) and *anthemon* (flower) for 'Chrysanthemum'; Latin *frutescens* (becoming shrubby).
