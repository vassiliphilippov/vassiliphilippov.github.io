to evaporate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈvæpəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈvapəreɪt / (BrE)
to remove or lose liquid by converting it into vapor, leaving a more concentrated substance behind
remove liquid by vaporization

Examples

To make condensed milk, you must evaporate most of the water from the milk.
The solution was heated to evaporate the excess solvent, leaving crystals behind.
If you evaporate water from the mixture, the flavor will become more intense.

Synset Examples

evaporate milk

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to condense - to make (a gas) become liquid or solid

Etymology

From Latin *evaporare*, from *e-* (out) + *vapor* (steam, vapor).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00367511-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪˈvæpəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈvapəreɪt / (BrE)
to cause a liquid to change into a vapor, especially by applying heat
change a liquid to vapor

Examples

The sun can evaporate water from the surface of a lake.
Scientists evaporate the solvent to isolate the solid compound.
Applying heat will quickly evaporate the liquid in the container.

Synset Examples

The chemist evaporated the water

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to condense - to change from a vapor to a liquid
  • Synonym: to vaporize - to turn into vapor

Etymology

From Latin 'evaporare' (to disperse as vapor), from 'e-' (out) + 'vapor' (steam).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00577047-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪˈvæpəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈvapəreɪt / (BrE)
(of an emotion, feeling, or condition) to gradually disappear or become less strong until it is gone
gradually disappear or fade away

Examples

As the years passed, her enthusiasm for the project began to evaporate.
Their excitement quickly evaporated when they heard the bad news.
Doubt can evaporate in the face of clear evidence.

Synset Examples

her resistance melted under his charm
her hopes evaporated after years of waiting for her fiance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dissipate - to disappear or cause to disappear
  • Synonym: to disappear - to cease to be visible or exist

Etymology

From Latin 'evaporare' (to disperse in vapor), from 'e-' (out) + 'vapor' (steam).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00224354-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ɪˈvæpəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈvapəreɪt / (BrE)
to change from a liquid or solid state into vapor, especially as a result of heating
change into vapor; turn into gas

Examples

If you leave a glass of water in the sun, it will evaporate quickly.
After the rain, the puddles on the street began to evaporate.
Heat from the stove caused the soup to evaporate over time.

Synset Examples

The water evaporated in front of our eyes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to condense - change from a gas to a liquid
  • Synonym: to vaporize - be converted into vapor

Etymology

From Latin 'evaporare', from 'e-' (out) + 'vapor' (steam).
