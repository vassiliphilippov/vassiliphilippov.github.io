magistracy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmædʒɪstrəsi / (AmE), / ˈmædʒɪstrəsi / (BrE)
the office, position, or authority of a magistrate.
the office of a magistrate

Examples

After years of service, she was elected to the magistracy of the city.
The magistracy requires a deep understanding of local laws and procedures.
Corruption within the magistracy can undermine public trust in the justice system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magistrate - a civil officer who administers the law

Etymology

From Latin magistratus (magistrate, office of magistrate), from magister (master).
