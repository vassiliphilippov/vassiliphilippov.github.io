Formica

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈmaɪkə / (AmE), / fɔːˈmʌɪkə / (BrE)
The genus of ants that is the type of the family Formicidae, including many common wood ants and mound ants.
genus of ants (wood and mound ants)

Examples

The Formica genus is known for its elaborate mound structures built by worker ants.
Scientists have discovered a new species within the Formica genus in the northern forests.
Many Formica ants play important roles in ecosystem engineering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ant - a small insect of the family Formicidae

Etymology

From Latin 'formica' meaning 'ant'.

2. NOUN

/ fɔːrˈmaɪkə / (AmE), / fɔːˈmʌɪkə / (BrE)
A type of durable plastic laminate, often used as a surface for countertops and tables.
plastic laminate used for surfaces

Examples

The kitchen countertops were finished with Formica for easy cleaning and durability.
He chose a dark woodgrain Formica for the new office desk.
Many retro diners feature tables topped with brightly colored Formica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laminate - a material made by bonding together layers of material

Etymology

Trademark, said to be a blend of 'for' and 'mica' (a mineral once used for electrical insulation).

3. NOUN

/ fɔrˈmaɪkə / (AmE), / fɔːˈmɪkə / (BrE)
The type genus of the ant family (Formicidae), comprising common wood ants and related species.
type genus of the ant family (Formicidae)

Examples

Many scientists have studied the behavior of Formica species in European forests.
The most widespread wood ants belong to the genus Formica.
Several Formica ants help control outbreaks of forest pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ant - a small insect of the family Formicidae

Etymology

From Latin *formica* (ant).
