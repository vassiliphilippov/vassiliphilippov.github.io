parader

1. NOUN

/ pəˈreɪdər / (AmE), / pəˈreɪdə / (BrE)
a person who walks with a regular or stately step, often to display or attract attention
person who walks with a stately step

Examples

Each year, hundreds of paraders march through the main street during the festival.
The parader led the procession with confidence and grace.
Spectators applauded as the parader passed by in a colorful costume.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parade - to march or walk in a formal or ostentatious way

Etymology

From 'parade' (to march or walk ostentatiously) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
