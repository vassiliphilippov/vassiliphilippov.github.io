undermanned

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɚˈmænd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈmænd / (BrE)
not having enough workers, assistants, or staff to do a job properly; short-handed or understaffed.
lacking enough workers or staff

Examples

The hospital was undermanned during the night shift, making it difficult to care for all patients.
Due to holidays, the restaurant found itself undermanned and struggling to meet customer demands.
Many factories became undermanned during the pandemic, disrupting production.

Synset Examples

they're rather short-handed at the moment
overcrowded and understaffed hospitals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: understaffed - lacking enough staff or personnel
  • Synonym: short-handed - having fewer helpers or workers than needed

Etymology

From 'under' (not enough) + 'manned' (provided with staff), first used in the early 20th century.
