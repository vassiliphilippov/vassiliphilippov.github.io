samurai

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæmʊˌraɪ / (AmE), / ˈsæmʊˌraɪ / (BrE)
A member of the military aristocracy of feudal Japan, renowned for their code of honor and martial skill.
member of feudal Japanese military class

Examples

The samurai were bound by a code of honor known as bushido.
Many films depict the life and struggles of a samurai in feudal Japan.
The armor of a samurai is a symbol of Japanese history and craftsmanship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warrior - a person engaged or experienced in warfare
  • Synonym: knight - medieval European mounted warrior

Etymology

From Japanese 侍 (samurai), originally meaning 'one who serves'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑː.məˌraɪ / (AmE), / ˈsæm.ʊ.raɪ / (BrE)
A member of the Japanese feudal military aristocracy, known as a warrior class, especially prominent from the 12th to the 19th century.
Japanese feudal warrior

Examples

The samurai were bound by a strict code of honor known as bushido.
In movies, a lone samurai often defends his village against invaders.
Many samurai wore elaborate armor and carried a pair of swords.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warrior - a person engaged or experienced in warfare

Etymology

From Japanese 侍 (samurai), meaning 'warrior' or 'one who serves'.
