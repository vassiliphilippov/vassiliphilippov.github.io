Exacum affine

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˈsækəm əˈfaɪn / (AmE), / ɛkˈsækəm əˈfiːn / (BrE)
A species of perennial plant cultivated especially as a houseplant, noted for its fragrant bluish to dark lavender flowers.
a houseplant with fragrant bluish flowers

Examples

The Exacum affine blooms beautifully on my windowsill every spring.
Many plant enthusiasts choose Exacum affine for its low maintenance and lovely scent.
The florist recommended Exacum affine as an attractive and fragrant houseplant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Persian violet - another common name for Exacum affine

Etymology

From the genus name 'Exacum' and species epithet 'affine,' both derived from Latin botanical nomenclature.
