Patrick

1. PROPN

/ ˈpætrɪk / (AmE), / ˈpætrɪk / (BrE)
(also Saint Patrick) The patron saint of Ireland, a 5th-century missionary credited with bringing Christianity to Ireland.
patron saint of Ireland

Examples

Every year, Irish people celebrate Patrick on March 17th with parades and festivities.
The story of Patrick driving the snakes out of Ireland is a popular legend.
Pilgrims visit sites in Ireland associated with Patrick's missionary work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Patrick - the patron saint of Ireland, 5th-century Christian missionary

Etymology

From Latin Patricius, meaning 'nobleman'; also known as Saint Patrick.
