to endear

1. VERB

/ ɛnˈdɪr / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪə / (BrE)
to cause (someone or something) to be loved or liked; to make dear or attractive
cause to be loved or liked

Examples

Her kindness quickly endeared her to everyone in the office.
The puppy's playful antics endeared it to the children.
His honesty endeared him to his friends and colleagues.

Synset Examples

This behavior endeared her to me

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to alienate - to cause (someone) to feel isolated or estranged

Etymology

From Old French *endier* (to make dear), from Latin *carus* (dear).
