to reforge

1. VERB

/ riˈfɔrdʒ / (AmE), / riˈfɔːdʒ / (BrE)
to make or form something again, especially by melting and reshaping; to reshape or reform anew, often used figuratively
make or shape again; reshape anew

Examples

The sculptor decided to reforge the statue after it was damaged in the storm.
After the scandal, the company had to reforge its public image to regain trust.
Traditions can weaken over time, but communities often strive to reforge their sense of unity.

Synset Examples

She had to recast her image to please the electorate in her home state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remake - to make something again or differently
  • Synonym: to reshape - to shape or form again or differently

Etymology

From re- (again) + forge (from Old French *forger*, from Latin *fabricare*, to make or shape).
