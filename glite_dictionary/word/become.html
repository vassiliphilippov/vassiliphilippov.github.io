to become

1. VERB

/ bɪˈkʌm / (AmE), / bɪˈkʌm / (BrE)
to come into existence or begin to be; to start to exist
come into existence

Examples

Scientists study how new species become over time.
As night falls, shadows become in the corners of the room.
New traditions often become from small, repeated acts.

Synset Examples

What becomes has duration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arise - to come into being or to originate
  • Antonym: to cease - to come to an end, to stop existing

Etymology

From Old English *becuman* (to come, arrive), from be- + cuman (to come).

WordNet ID: oewn-02629610-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bɪˈkʌm / (AmE), / bɪˈkʌm / (BrE)
to start to be, or begin to show the qualities or state of; to enter into a specified condition
to begin to be; enter a new state

Examples

When the sun set, the sky became a deep shade of purple.
After years of training, she became a skilled dancer.
If you leave the bread out too long, it will become stale.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get - to become or come to be, used informally
  • Synonym: turn - to become or change into a different state

Etymology

From Old English becuman (to come to, arrive at, happen), from be- + cuman (to come).

Examples

4. VERB

/ bɪˈkʌm / (AmE), / bɪˈkʌm / (BrE)
to make someone or something look attractive or suitable; to suit or enhance the appearance of
to enhance the appearance of; to suit

Examples

That color really becomes you; it makes your eyes stand out.
The elegant gown became her, highlighting her graceful posture.
This old-fashioned style does not become him at all.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suit - to be right or appropriate for someone

Etymology

From Old English becuman, from be- (about) + cuman (to come).
