pricket

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪkɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɪkɪt / (BrE)
a sharp metal spike or holder on which a candle is placed, typically used in candlesticks or candelabra.
spike for holding a candle

Examples

The ancient church was lit by candles held upright on a sturdy pricket.
She polished the brass pricket until it gleamed in the candlelight.
A single taper burned on a pricket on the mantelpiece, casting long shadows across the room.

Etymology

From Middle English 'priket' (pointed instrument), related to 'prick' (a point or spike).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪk.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɪk.ɪt / (BrE)
A male deer in its second year, typically with unbranched antlers.
male deer in its second year

Examples

The hunter spotted a pricket grazing at the edge of the forest.
In autumn, a pricket can be identified by its single-pointed antlers.
The park ranger explained that the young stag was a pricket before growing branching antlers.

Etymology

From Middle English 'priket', from Old French, diminutive of 'prik' (spike, point).
