Aegiceras majus

1. NOUN

/ iːˌdʒɪˈsɛrəs ˈmeɪdʒəs / (AmE), / iːˌdʒɪˈsɛːrəs ˈmeɪdʒəs / (BrE)
an Australian tree species resembling the black mangrove, found in mangrove ecosystems.
Australian mangrove-like tree species

Examples

The coastal wetlands were populated with Aegiceras majus thriving near the water's edge.
Researchers studied the adaptation strategies of Aegiceras majus in saline environments.
Locals often refer to Aegiceras majus as the Australian mangrove due to its similarity to other mangrove species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black mangrove - a tropical tree found in the West Indies and Florida, known for salt excretion roots

Etymology

From the Latinized Greek 'aix' (goat) + 'keras' (horn), referring to the shape of its fruit, and Latin 'majus' meaning larger.
