Artois

1. PROPN

/ ɑːrˈtwɑː / (AmE), / ɑːˈtwɑː / (BrE)
a former province in northern France near the English Channel, situated between Picardy and Flanders
former province of northern France

Examples

The region of Artois played a strategic role during several European wars.
Many architectural styles in Artois reflect its rich and varied history.
After the French Revolution, Artois was dissolved as a province and integrated into Pas-de-Calais.

Etymology

From French 'Artois', derived from Latin 'Atrebatum', relating to the Atrebates, a Belgic tribe.
