Shivaism

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃiːvəˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈʃiːvəɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A major sect of Hinduism in which Shiva is worshipped as the supreme god.
Hindu sect worshipping Shiva

Examples

Shivaism emphasizes the worship of Shiva as the Supreme Being in Hinduism.
Many ancient temples in South India are closely associated with the traditions of Shivaism.
He became interested in Hindu philosophy, especially Shivaism and its teachings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shaivism - alternate spelling; sect of Hinduism devoted to Shiva

Etymology

From Shiva, the Hindu god, and the suffix -ism, denoting a religious movement or belief.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃiːvəˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvəɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the worship of Shiva, one of the principal deities of Hinduism, as the supreme god
worship of Shiva in Hinduism

Examples

Many devotees practice Shivaism as their main religious path, focusing on the worship of Shiva.
Shivaism has a significant influence on art and culture in southern India.
The temples dedicated to Shiva are important centers for followers of Shivaism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shaivism - alternate spelling; worship of Shiva as a principal Hindu deity

Etymology

From Shiva (the Hindu god) + -ism (doctrine or practice).
