cabbage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæb.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkæb.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
a round vegetable with thick green or purple leaves that are eaten raw or cooked, belonging to the species Brassica oleracea and including several cultivated varieties
a leafy vegetable, Brassica oleracea

Examples

We planted several cabbages in our vegetable garden this spring.
Shredded cabbage is a main ingredient in traditional coleslaw.
You can steam, boil, or stir-fry cabbage for a healthy side dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lettuce - a type of leafy green vegetable often used in salads

Etymology

From Old French 'caboche' (head), possibly from Latin 'caput' (head).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæb.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkæb.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
A round vegetable with thick, green or purple leaves that are eaten as food, belonging to the species Brassica oleracea.
edible leafy vegetable

Examples

She shredded some cabbage to add to the salad.
The farmer grew rows of cabbage in his vegetable garden.
Steamed cabbage is a traditional side dish in many cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lettuce - a leafy green vegetable often used in salads
  • Synonym: kale - a type of leafy green Brassica oleracea similar to cabbage

Etymology

From Old French *caboche* (head), of uncertain origin.
