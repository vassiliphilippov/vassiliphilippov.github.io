to vitalize

1. VERB

/ ˈvaɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvʌɪtəlʌɪz / (BrE)
to give life, energy, or vitality to; make more lively or vigorous
give life or energy to

Examples

The new leadership managed to vitalize the struggling company.
Fresh air and exercise can vitalize both body and mind.
The community center was vitalized by the introduction of new programs.

Synset Examples

The eggs are vitalized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to energize - to give energy or vitality to
  • Synonym: to invigorate - to fill with life or energy; energize
  • Antonym: to deaden - to make less lively, intense, or sensitive

Etymology

From Latin 'vita' (life) + English suffix '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈvaɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvaɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something more lively, energetic, or vigorous; to give life or animation to
make more lively or energetic

Examples

Sunlight and fresh air can vitalize both your mood and your energy levels.
The speaker's passion seemed to vitalize her audience, sparking a lively discussion.
Regular exercise can vitalize your body and mind, keeping you feeling youthful.

Synset Examples

The treatment at the spa vitalized the old man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invigorate - to give strength or energy to; energize
  • Synonym: to strengthen - to make stronger or more powerful
  • Antonym: to deaden - to make less lively, intense, or sensitive
