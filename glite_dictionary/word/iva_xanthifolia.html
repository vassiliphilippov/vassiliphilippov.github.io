Iva xanthifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪvə zænθɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌaɪvə zænθɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A tall annual plant, also known as marsh elder, found in central North America, notable for causing contact dermatitis and producing pollen that often triggers hay fever.
tall North American marsh elder plant

Examples

Many people experience severe hay fever in the summer due to the pollen from Iva xanthifolia.
The invasive Iva xanthifolia thrives in moist, fertile soils near rivers.
Some individuals may develop contact dermatitis after touching the leaves of Iva xanthifolia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh elder - another term for Iva xanthifolia or closely related species

Etymology

From genus name Iva and species epithet xanthifolia, Latinized from Greek xanthos (yellow) and phyllon (leaf).
