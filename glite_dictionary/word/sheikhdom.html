sheikhdom

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪkˌdəm / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪk.dəm / (BrE)
A country, territory, or domain ruled by a sheikh.
territory ruled by a sheikh

Examples

The oil-rich sheikhdom has seen rapid economic growth in recent decades.
He traveled through several sheikhdoms on his way across the Arabian Peninsula.
The agreement was signed by the leaders of each sheikhdom in the region.

Etymology

From 'sheikh' (Arabic for 'chief, elder') + English suffix '-dom' (denoting domain or jurisdiction).
