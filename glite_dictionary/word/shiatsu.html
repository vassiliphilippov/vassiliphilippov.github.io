shiatsu

1. NOUN

/ ʃiˈɑtsu / (AmE), / ʃiˈætsu / (BrE)
A form of Japanese bodywork in which pressure is applied with the fingers to specific points on the body to relieve pain and treat various conditions.
Japanese pressure-based bodywork therapy

Examples

After a stressful week, she booked a shiatsu session to help her relax.
Some people find shiatsu helpful for back pain and tension headaches.
The clinic offers shiatsu as part of its holistic treatment options.

Etymology

From Japanese 'shi' (finger) + 'atsu' (pressure).
