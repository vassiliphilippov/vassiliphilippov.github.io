Pseudemys scripta

1. NOUN

/ suːˈdɛ.mɪs ˈskrɪp.tə / (AmE), / sjuːˈdɛ.mɪs ˈskrɪp.tə / (BrE)
A species of freshwater turtle, also known as the red-eared slider, native to the United States and South America, frequently kept as a pet and often raised commercially.
species of freshwater turtle, the red-eared slider

Examples

The Pseudemys scripta is one of the most popular turtles sold in pet stores worldwide.
Scientists have studied the migration patterns of Pseudemys scripta in river basins.
Many families enjoy keeping a Pseudemys scripta in their home aquariums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-eared slider - common name for Pseudemys scripta, a freshwater turtle kept as a pet

Etymology

From Greek pseudes (false) and emys (turtle), and Latin scripta (written/marked), referring to the markings.
