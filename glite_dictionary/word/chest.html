chest

1. NOUN

/ tʃɛst / (AmE), / tʃɛst / (BrE)
A large, sturdy box or piece of furniture with drawers, used for storing clothes or other items.
furniture with drawers for storing clothes

Examples

She kept her winter sweaters in an old wooden chest at the foot of the bed.
The antique chest in the hallway was passed down from her grandmother.
He opened the chest to look for clean shirts before getting ready for work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dresser - a piece of furniture with drawers for storing clothes
  • Synonym: cabinet - a piece of furniture with shelves or drawers, used for storage

Etymology

From Old English 'cest', from Latin 'cista' (box, chest).

2. NOUN

/ tʃɛst / (AmE), / tʃɛst / (BrE)
the front part of the human body between the neck and the abdomen, containing the heart and lungs; the corresponding part in other animals
front part of the torso

Examples

She put her hand on her chest to feel her heartbeat.
The doctor listened to his chest with a stethoscope.
He suffered a pain in his chest after running up the stairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorax - medical term for the chest (body part)
  • Degree Lower: abdomen - part of the body below the chest
  • Degree Higher: neck - body part above the chest

Etymology

From Old English cest (box, chest), later used for the body part.
