Euthynnus

1. NOUN

/ juːˈθɪnəs / (AmE), / juːˈθɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the mackerel family (Scombridae), which includes several species of tuna-like fish.
genus of tuna-like fish

Examples

The genus Euthynnus contains several important species for commercial fishing.
Marine biologists studied the migratory patterns of Euthynnus in the Atlantic Ocean.
One species of Euthynnus is commonly known as the little tunny.

Etymology

From Greek euthynō (to straighten), used in scientific Latin classification.

2. NOUN

/ juːˈθɪn.əs / (AmE), / juːˈθɪn.əs / (BrE)
A genus of tuna fishes in the family Scombridae, which includes several smaller species commonly known as little tunny or kawakawa.
a genus of tuna fishes

Examples

Several species in the genus Euthynnus are important to commercial and sport fisheries.
The little tunny, classified as Euthynnus alletteratus, is widely distributed in warm seas.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of fish in the Euthynnus genus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Scombridae - the family of mackerels and tunas

Etymology

From Greek 'eu' (good) + 'thynnos' (tuna), referring to a type of tuna.
