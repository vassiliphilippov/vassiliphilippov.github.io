greasy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡriːsi, ˈɡrisi / (AmE), / ˈɡriːzi, ˈɡriːsi / (BrE)
Covered with or marked by grease or oil; smeared or soiled with a fatty or oily substance.
covered with grease or oil

Examples

His hands were greasy after fixing the car engine.
The tools in the workshop are always greasy from regular use.
She wiped the greasy fingerprints off the kitchen counter.

Synset Examples

greasy coveralls
get rid of rubbish and oily rags

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oily - covered in or resembling oil; containing oil

Etymology

From Middle English 'grese' (grease) + -y.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡrisi / (AmE), / ˈɡriːzi / (BrE)
containing or covered with an excessive amount of grease or oil, especially making something feel slippery or oily to the touch
covered with or full of grease or oil

Examples

The fries were so greasy that my fingers became shiny after eating them.
She wiped the greasy residue off the kitchen counter with a paper towel.
After working on the car, his hands were covered in greasy oil.

Synset Examples

greasy hamburgers
oily fried potatoes
oleaginous seeds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oily - containing or covered with oil
  • Antonym: dry - not containing moisture or oil

Etymology

From Middle English 'grese' (grease) + '-y', from Old French 'graisse' (grease).
