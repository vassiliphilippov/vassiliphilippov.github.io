Phalaris canariensis

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɛərɪs kəˌnɛəriˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / fəˈlɑːrɪs kəˌneəriːˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A Mediterranean grass species, also known as canary grass, whose seeds are commonly used as feed for caged birds.
species of grass used for bird feed

Examples

Many pet owners buy Phalaris canariensis seeds to feed their caged birds.
Fields of Phalaris canariensis are cultivated in Mediterranean regions for the pet trade.
The nutritious seeds of Phalaris canariensis make it popular among bird enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canary grass - alternate common name for Phalaris canariensis

Etymology

From Latin; Phalaris (the genus name) and canariensis (meaning 'from the Canary Islands').
