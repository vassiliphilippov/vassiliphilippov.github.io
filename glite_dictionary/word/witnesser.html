witnesser

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtnɪsər / (AmE), / ˈwɪtnɪsə / (BrE)
a person who observes an event, especially a crime or accident, and reports what happened
person who sees and reports an event

Examples

The witnesser described exactly what happened during the accident.
As the only witnesser of the incident, her testimony was crucial to the investigation.
Each witnesser was asked to write down their account of the events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witness - a person who sees an event, typically a crime or accident, take place

Etymology

Formed from 'witness' + '-er', where 'witness' comes from Old English 'witnes', meaning 'testimony' or 'attestation'.
