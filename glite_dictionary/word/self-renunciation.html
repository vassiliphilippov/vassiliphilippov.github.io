self-renunciation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf rɪˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf rɪˌnʌnsɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of giving up one's own interests, desires, or needs for the sake of others.
giving up one's own interests for others

Examples

Her life was characterized by complete self-renunciation in service to her community.
True leadership often requires acts of self-renunciation for the greater good.
He was admired for his self-renunciation and unwavering generosity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-sacrifice - the act of giving up one's own interests or comfort for others

Etymology

From 'self' + 'renunciation' (from Latin renuntiatio: refusal, rejection).
