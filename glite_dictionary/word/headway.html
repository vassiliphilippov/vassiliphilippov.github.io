headway

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.weɪ / (BrE)
The vertical space between the surface and an overhead structure, allowing for unobstructed passage beneath.
vertical clearance space under something

Examples

The tunnel had enough headway for trucks to pass safely underneath.
Low headway on the bridge restricted the height of vehicles allowed.
Mind the headway when entering the garage with your van.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.weɪ / (BrE)
forward movement, especially in the face of obstacles; progress toward a goal
forward movement or progress

Examples

The researchers are finally making significant headway in their efforts to develop a new vaccine.
Despite the heavy traffic, we managed to make good headway and arrived on time.
The team found it difficult to gain any headway against the experienced competitors.

Synset Examples

the ship made little headway against the gale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progress - forward or onward movement toward a goal
  • Antonym: setback - a reversal or check in progress

Etymology

From head (front) + way (path, progress), originally referring to a ship's movement through water.
