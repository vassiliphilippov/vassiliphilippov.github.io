Icelandic

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈslæn.dɪk / (AmE), / aɪsˈlæn.dɪk / (BrE)
The Scandinavian language that is the official language of Iceland.
language of Iceland

Examples

Many young people in Iceland are fluent in both Icelandic and English.
She is studying Icelandic at university because of her interest in Nordic languages.
The novel was written in Icelandic and later translated into several other languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Danish - Scandinavian language spoken in Denmark
  • Synonym: Norwegian - Scandinavian language spoken in Norway
  • Synonym: Swedish - Scandinavian language spoken in Sweden

Etymology

From Iceland + -ic, indicating belonging to Iceland or its language.

3. NOUN

/ aɪˈslændɪk / (AmE), / aɪsˈlændɪk / (BrE)
the official language of Iceland, belonging to the North Germanic branch of the Scandinavian languages
language of Iceland

Examples

She is fluent in Icelandic after living in Reykjavík for several years.
Many ancient Norse texts are preserved in the Icelandic language.
The poem was originally written in Icelandic and later translated into English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Danish - North Germanic language spoken in Denmark
  • Synonym: Norwegian - North Germanic language spoken in Norway
  • Synonym: Swedish - North Germanic language spoken in Sweden

Etymology

From Iceland, the country name, + -ic (forming language adjectives/nouns).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06966923-n
Synset POS: n
