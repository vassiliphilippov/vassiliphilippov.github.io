institution

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnstəˈtuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
an organization founded for a specific purpose, especially one of public, educational, charitable, or similar character
organized group for a specific purpose

Examples

Harvard University is a well-known institution of higher learning.
The new law affected every financial institution in the country.
A local charitable institution provided meals for the homeless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organization - a group with a particular purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'institutio' (arrangement, instruction), from 'instituere' (to establish).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnstɪˈtuʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
an established organization, especially one occupying a building or group of buildings, for the promotion of a particular cause or activity
organization with physical premises

Examples

The new art institution in town attracts visitors from across the country.
She spent her career working at a charitable institution helping the homeless.
The hospital is an important institution in the community, providing essential services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organization - an organized group of people with a particular purpose

Etymology

From Latin institutio (arrangement; instruction), from instituere (to establish).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.stəˈtu.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃən / (BrE)
a hospital for people with mental illnesses or intellectual disabilities, especially one providing long-term care
hospital for mental illness or disability

Examples

After his diagnosis, he was admitted to an institution for treatment and care.
Many former patients of the old institution recalled the harsh conditions they experienced.
The government has reformed the mental health system, closing many large institutions in favor of community care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asylum - an institution for the care of people with mental illness

Etymology

From Latin institutio(n-), from instituere ‘set up, establish’.

4. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.stɪˈtu.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃən / (BrE)
A custom, practice, or system that has existed for a long time and is an important feature of a particular society or group.
long-established custom or practice

Examples

The institution of marriage has been central to many cultures around the world.
A free press is considered an institution of a democratic society.
Over the years, the annual parade became a beloved institution in the community.

Synset Examples

the institution of marriage
the institution of slavery
he had become an institution in the theater

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tradition - a custom or belief passed down within a group
  • Antonym: innovation - a new idea, method, or device

Etymology

From Latin institutio (arrangement, instruction), from instituere (to set up, establish).
