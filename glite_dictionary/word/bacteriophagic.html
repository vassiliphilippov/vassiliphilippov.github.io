bacteriophagic

1. ADJ

/ bækˌtɪri.oʊˈfeɪdʒɪk / (AmE), / bækˌtɪəri.əˈfeɪdʒɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of bacteriophages (viruses that infect and destroy bacteria).
relating to bacteriophages
medical

Examples

The researchers studied the bacteriophagic activity in wastewater samples to assess viral presence.
Certain antibiotics work in combination with bacteriophagic therapies to combat resistant bacterial infections.
Advances in bacteriophagic technology are opening new possibilities for treating bacterial diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacteriophage - a virus that infects and destroys bacteria

Etymology

From 'bacteriophage' (a virus that destroys bacteria) + '-ic' (forming adjectives).
