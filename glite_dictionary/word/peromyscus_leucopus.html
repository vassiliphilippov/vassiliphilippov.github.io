Peromyscus leucopus

1. PROPN

/ pəˈroʊmɪskəs luˈkoʊpəs / (AmE), / pəˈrəʊmɪskəs luˈkəʊpəs / (BrE)
A species of small North American woodland mouse with white feet and underparts, commonly known as the white-footed mouse.
North American woodland mouse with white feet

Examples

Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Peromyscus leucopus in different forest types.
Lyme disease is commonly associated with populations of Peromyscus leucopus in certain regions.
The Peromyscus leucopus quickly adapted to changes in the woodland environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white-footed mouse - common English name for Peromyscus leucopus

Etymology

From Greek *perōmyskos* (pouched mouse) and Latin *leucopus* (white-footed).
