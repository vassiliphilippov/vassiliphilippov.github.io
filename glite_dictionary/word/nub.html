nub

1. NOUN

/ nʌb / (AmE), / nʌb / (BrE)
a small lump, knob, or protuberance; a small rounded piece projecting from a surface
small lump or protuberance

Examples

The statue had a nub where the arm used to be.
He felt a small nub on the surface of the fabric.
Over time, a nub of metal formed from repeated use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knob - a rounded lump or handle on a surface
  • Synonym: protuberance - a thing that protrudes from a surface

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly related to nubbin, meaning a small lump or knob.

2. NOUN

/ nʌb / (AmE), / nʌb / (BrE)
a small lump or piece, especially one remaining after the rest has been used or worn away
small lump or leftover piece

Examples

He tossed the nub of chalk into the bin after finishing the lesson.
At the bottom of the box was only a nub of soap left.
She chewed on the nub of her pencil as she thought about the answer.

Synset Examples

a nub of coal
a stub of a pencil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stub - the short end left after the rest has been used
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09397558-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ nʌb / (AmE), / nʌb / (BrE)
the central or most important point or part of something, especially an argument or discussion
the essential or central point

Examples

The nub of the debate was whether the law should be changed.
We finally reached the nub of the issue after hours of discussion.
She explained the nub of her argument in a single sentence.

Synset Examples

the gist of the prosecutor's argument
the heart and soul of the Republican Party
the nub of the story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crux - the most important or decisive point
  • Synonym: essence - the intrinsic nature or indispensable quality
  • Antonym: periphery - the outer limits or edge, less important part

Etymology

Origin unknown; possibly from a dialect word meaning a small lump or protuberance.
