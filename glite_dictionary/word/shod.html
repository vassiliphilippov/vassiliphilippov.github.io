shod

1. ADJ

/ ʃɑd / (AmE), / ʃɒd / (BrE)
(of members of certain religious orders) wearing shoes, as opposed to being barefoot or wearing sandals.
wearing shoes (esp. in religious orders)

Examples

The shod Carmelites permitted footwear in contrast to their unshod brethren.
Traditionally, the shod friars were distinguished by their leather shoes.
In some orders, only the shod monks are allowed to perform outdoor work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unshod - not wearing shoes, especially in reference to religious orders

Etymology

From Old English *sceod*, past participle of 'shoe' (to provide with shoes).

2. ADJ

/ ʃɑd / (AmE), / ʃɒd / (BrE)
wearing footwear or having shoes fitted, especially animals with horseshoes.
wearing footwear or fitted with shoes

Examples

The children were shod in sturdy boots for the muddy walk.
The stable hands always made sure the horses were properly shod.
He entered the temple barefoot, while everyone else was shod.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unshod - not wearing footgear or not fitted with shoes

Etymology

Old past participle of 'shoe', from Old English 'scōd'.
