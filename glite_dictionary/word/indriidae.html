Indriidae

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdrɪiˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdrɪiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of lemurs native to Madagascar, including the indris, sifakas, and woolly lemurs.
family of lemurs

Examples

The species belonging to the Indriidae family are known for their powerful leaping abilities.
Conservationists have focused efforts on protecting the Indriidae because of habitat loss in Madagascar.
Scientists study the vocalizations of Indriidae to better understand their social structures.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Indriidae', based on the genus 'Indri' + 'idae' (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdriːɪdiː / (AmE), / ɪnˈdrɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of lemurs found in Madagascar, including the indri and sifakas.
family of lemurs (Indriidae)
medical

Examples

The Indriidae are best known for their loud calls and distinctive appearance.
Unlike other lemur families, Indriidae includes some of the largest living lemurs.
Biologists study the social structure of Indriidae to understand primate evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemur - a primate native to Madagascar; member of Lemuroidea

Etymology

From New Latin Indriidae, based on the type genus Indri.
