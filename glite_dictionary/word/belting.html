belting

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛltɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɛltɪŋ / (BrE)
a strong and flexible material, typically leather, fabric, or synthetic, used for making belts and mechanical belts
material used for making belts

Examples

The factory ordered several rolls of belting for the new conveyor system.
Heavy-duty leather belting is commonly used in industrial machinery.
Cotton belting is sometimes preferred for its flexibility and strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belt - a band of material worn around the waist or used in machinery

Etymology

Formed from 'belt' + '-ing', denoting material.
