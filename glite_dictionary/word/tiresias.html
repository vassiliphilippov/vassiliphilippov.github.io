Tiresias

1. PROPN

/ taɪˈriːsiəs / (AmE), / taɪˈriːsiəs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a blind prophet of Thebes, renowned for his wisdom, who revealed to Oedipus that he had killed his father and married his mother.
blind prophet in Greek mythology

Examples

The character of Tiresias appears as a wise seer in many Greek tragedies.
In the story, Tiresias reveals Oedipus’s dark secret, changing the course of the tale.
Scholars often reference Tiresias when discussing prophecy and blindness in literature.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Τειρεσίας (Teiresías).
