Cnemidophorus exsanguis

1. NOUN

/ nɛmɪˌdɒfəˌrəs ɛkˈsæŋɡwɪs / (AmE), / nɛmɪˌdɒfəˌrəs ɛkˈsæŋɡwɪs / (BrE)
A species of lizard found in the southwestern United States and Mexico, characterized by longitudinal stripes with light spots; also known as the little striped whiptail.
a striped whiptail lizard species

Examples

The Cnemidophorus exsanguis darts quickly across the rocky desert in search of insects.
Researchers observed that Cnemidophorus exsanguis is active primarily during the warmer parts of the day.
Conservationists are studying the habitat requirements of Cnemidophorus exsanguis in the American Southwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: little striped whiptail - common name for Cnemidophorus exsanguis, a small striped lizard of the US and Mexico

Etymology

From the genus name Cnemidophorus (Greek knēmē 'shin, leg' + phoros 'bearing') and Latin exsanguis ('bloodless').
