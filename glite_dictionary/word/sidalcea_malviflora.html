Sidalcea malviflora

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈdælsiə ˌmælvɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / sɪˈdalsɪə ˌmalvɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
a perennial purple-flowered wild mallow native to western North America, sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant
perennial wild mallow of western North America

Examples

Gardeners appreciate the vibrant blooms of Sidalcea malviflora in spring.
Wild stands of Sidalcea malviflora can be found along the Pacific coast.
The purple flowers of Sidalcea malviflora attract pollinators to native gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - plant of the Malvaceae family with characteristic flowers

Etymology

From genus name Sidalcea and species epithet from Latin 'malva' (mallow) + 'flora' (flowered).
