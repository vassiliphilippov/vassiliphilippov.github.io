to approve

1. VERB

/ əˈpruv / (AmE), / əˈpruːv / (BrE)
to officially agree to or accept something; to give consent or sanction to something
give official consent or sanction

Examples

The board will not approve the project until all requirements are met.
Many parents did not approve of the new school policy.
The government must approve the proposed changes before they take effect.

Synset Examples

I approve of his educational policies

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reject - to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with something
  • Synonym: to endorse - to declare one's public approval or support of something

Etymology

From Latin 'approbare' (to assent to), from 'ad-' (to) + 'probare' (to test, prove).

2. VERB

/ əˈpruv / (AmE), / əˈpruːv / (BrE)
to believe that something or someone is good or acceptable; to officially agree to or endorse
to accept or think well of

Examples

The committee will approve the new regulations next week if there are no objections.
Her parents did not approve of her decision to move abroad.
I fully approve of your plan to start your own business.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to disapprove - to think that something is bad or wrong; to not approve
  • Synonym: to endorse - to express formal support or approval for something

Etymology

From Latin 'approbare' (to assent to, to prove), from 'ad-' (to) + 'probare' (to test, to prove).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00675902-v
Synset POS: v
