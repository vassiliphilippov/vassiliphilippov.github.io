essence

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛsəns / (AmE), / ˈɛsəns / (BrE)
the central or most important quality that gives something its character, especially the main idea or meaning of a speech or literary work
central meaning or main idea

Examples

The essence of her argument was that everyone deserves equal opportunity.
After reading the lengthy report, I finally grasped the essence of its message.
He summarized the essence of the novel in just a few sentences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gist - the main point or essence of a matter
  • Synonym: substance - the essential meaning or main part of something
  • Antonym: superficiality - lack of depth or substance; opposite of essence

Etymology

From Latin essentia (being, essence), from esse (to be).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛsəns / (AmE), / ˈɛsəns / (BrE)
a concentrated substance obtained from a plant, drug, or other natural source, possessing its main properties in a high degree
concentrated plant or drug extract

Examples

The chef added a drop of vanilla essence to the batter.
A few drops of peppermint essence can soothe an upset stomach.
Lavender essence is popular in aromatherapy for its calming effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extract - a preparation containing the active ingredient of a substance in concentrated form

Etymology

From Latin essentia (being, essence), based on esse (to be).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛsəns / (AmE), / ˈɛsəns / (BrE)
A fragrant liquid or preparation used as a toiletry, often for its pleasant scent.
a fragrant toiletry or perfume

Examples

She dabbed a few drops of essence on her wrists before the party.
This floral essence leaves a delicate scent on your skin all day.
He purchased a gift set that included soap, lotion, and a lavender essence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfume - a fragrant liquid typically used to give a pleasant smell to one's body
  • Synonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell, especially from a liquid used as a toiletry
  • Degree Lower: deodorant - a substance applied to the body to prevent or mask body odor

Etymology

From Latin essentia (being, essence), later used for scented extracts and perfumes.

4. NOUN

/ ˈɛsəns / (AmE), / ˈɛsəns / (BrE)
the most important or central part of an idea, experience, or argument; the core or fundamental quality.
the most important or central part

Examples

The essence of his argument can be summed up in a single sentence.
The artist captured the essence of the landscape in her painting.
We must not lose sight of the essence of the problem amid all the details.

Synset Examples

the gist of the prosecutor's argument
the heart and soul of the Republican Party
the nub of the story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: core - the central or most important part of something
  • Synonym: gist - the main or essential part of a matter
  • Antonym: detail - a small part or minor feature, as opposed to the central idea

Etymology

From Latin 'essentia' (being, essence), from 'esse' (to be).
