Centaurea

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtɔːriə / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɔːriə / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae, including knapweeds and star thistles
genus of knapweed and star thistle plants

Examples

The invasive species Centaurea has become a problem in several North American ecosystems.
Many gardeners appreciate Centaurea for its attractive blue or purple flowers.
Pollinators such as bees are often drawn to Centaurea growing in wildflower meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knapweed - common name for plants of the genus Centaurea
  • Synonym: star thistle - common term for several Centaurea species with spiny flower heads

Etymology

From Latin *centaurea*, from Greek *kentauros* (centaur), referring to a mythical use of the plant.

2. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtɔriə / (AmE), / sɛnˈtjʊəriə / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, commonly known as knapweed or star thistle
plant genus; knapweed or star thistle

Examples

Several species in the Centaurea genus are considered invasive in some regions.
The purple flowers of Centaurea attract a variety of pollinators to the garden.
Botanists use the term Centaurea to refer collectively to more than 500 species of knapweed and star thistle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knapweed - common name for plants of the genus Centaurea
  • Synonym: star thistle - another common name for plants of the genus Centaurea

Etymology

From Latin Centaurea, from Greek kentauros (centaur), referring to the mythical being Chiron, who was said to use the plant for healing.
