about-face

1. NOUN

/ əˌbaʊt ˈfeɪs / (AmE), / əˌbaʊt ˈfeɪs / (BrE)
A sudden or complete change of direction, opinion, or policy; originally, an act of turning 180 degrees, especially in a military formation.
a 180-degree turn; complete change

Examples

The soldiers performed an about-face and marched back toward the barracks.
After much debate, the company made an about-face on its environmental policy.
The politician's sudden about-face surprised both his supporters and critics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: about-turn - A complete change of direction or opinion (chiefly British)

Etymology

From the military command 'about face,' meaning to turn and face the opposite direction.

2. NOUN

/ əˈbaʊt ˌfeɪs / (AmE), / əˌbaʊt ˈfeɪs / (BrE)
a complete change of opinion, attitude, or course of action to the opposite one
sudden reversal of opinion or policy

Examples

After months of resistance, the company made an about-face on its environmental policies.
His sudden about-face on the issue surprised everyone at the meeting.
The politician's about-face was seen as an attempt to win back voters.

Synset Examples

an about-face on foreign policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reversal - a change to the opposite direction, position, or course of action
  • Synonym: U-turn - a sudden change to the opposite direction or opinion
  • Antonym: consistency - steadfast adherence to the same principles or course

Etymology

Originally from a military command meaning 'turn to face the opposite direction.'
