tragus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪɡəs / (AmE), / ˈtreɪɡəs / (BrE)
a small cartilaginous projection in front of the external opening of the ear
cartilaginous flap of the ear
medical

Examples

The piercing was placed through the tragus of her left ear.
Swelling of the tragus can indicate an ear infection.
He winced in pain when the doctor touched his tragus during the examination.

Etymology

From Greek *tragos* (he-goat), referring to the tuft of hair sometimes present on the tragus resembling a goat's beard.
