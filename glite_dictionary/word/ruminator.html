ruminator

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːməˌneɪtər / (AmE), / ˈruːmɪneɪtə / (BrE)
a person who thinks deeply and often quietly about things; a reflective thinker
a reflective thinker

Examples

As a natural ruminator, she often spends hours lost in thought about life's big questions.
Unlike the impulsive members of the team, he is a ruminator who weighs every decision carefully.
Great philosophers are often ruminators who prefer contemplation to action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thinker - a person who thinks deeply about things
  • Synonym: to ruminate - to think deeply about something

Etymology

From Latin 'ruminator', from 'ruminare' (to chew the cud, to meditate), related to 'rumen' (throat, gullet).
