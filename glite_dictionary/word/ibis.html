Ibis

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪbɪs / (AmE), / ˈaɪbɪs / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds in the family Threskiornithidae, known for their long legs and down-curved bills.
a genus of wading birds

Examples

The Ibis genus includes several species found in wetlands around the world.
Many art pieces from Ancient Egypt depict the Ibis as a sacred animal.
Researchers observed a flock of Ibis feeding in the marshlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ibis - a wading bird with a long down-curved bill

Etymology

From Latin 'ibis', from Ancient Greek 'ἶβις' (ibis), referring to the sacred ibis in Egypt.
