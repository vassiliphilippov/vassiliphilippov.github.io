ibis

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪbɪs / (AmE), / ˈaɪbɪs / (BrE)
A large wading bird found in warm regions, known for its long, slender, downward-curving bill.
large wading bird with curved bill

Examples

The white ibis waded gracefully through the shallow water in search of food.
An ibis can often be identified by its distinctive curved bill and long legs.
Several ibises gathered along the riverbank at sunset.

Etymology

From Latin *ibis*, from Greek *ibis*.
