vulgar

1. ADJ

/ ˈvʌlɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡə / (BrE)
Of or relating to the common people or the masses, often as opposed to the elite or refined classes.
pertaining to the common people

Examples

His ideas were considered too vulgar for the scholarly audience of the time.
The novel's protagonist comes from a vulgar background but aspires to join the upper class.
Some critics dismissed the poet's work as being too vulgar to be considered true art.

Synset Examples

the common people in those days suffered greatly
behavior that branded him as common
his square plebeian nose
a vulgar and objectionable person
the unwashed masses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plebeian - belonging to the common people

Etymology

From Latin 'vulgaris' (of the common people), from 'vulgus' (the common people).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvʌlɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡə / (BrE)
lacking refinement, sophistication, or good taste; coarse or crude in manners or style.
lacking refinement or taste

Examples

The lavish decorations were criticized as vulgar by many art critics.
Some celebrities are known for their vulgar displays of wealth and taste.
His jokes were not just rude, but rather vulgar and in poor taste.

Synset Examples

he had coarse manners but a first-rate mind
behavior that branded him as common
an untutored and uncouth human being
an uncouth soldier — a real tough guy
appealing to the vulgar taste for violence
the vulgar display of the newly rich

Related Terms

  • Antonym: refined - having or showing well-bred feeling, taste, etc.
  • Synonym: coarse - lacking delicacy or refinement; rough or crude

Etymology

From Latin 'vulgāris' (common, of the people), from 'vulgus' (the common people).

4. ADJ

/ ˈvʌlɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡə / (BrE)
(of language or terms) characteristic of or appropriate to ordinary, everyday speech, rather than formal or technical language.
relating to common or everyday speech

Examples

The biologist explained the species' scientific and vulgar names to the students.
Shakespeare often included dialogue in the vulgar tongue to appeal to a wider audience.
While the official term is 'canine,' the vulgar form is simply 'dog.'

Synset Examples

common parlance
a vernacular term

the vulgar tongue of the masses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vernacular - the language spoken by ordinary people in a particular region
  • Synonym: colloquial - used in ordinary or familiar conversation; not formal or literary
  • Antonym: formal - marked by strict or official style, as opposed to everyday speech

Etymology

From Latin 'vulgaris' meaning 'of the common people'.
