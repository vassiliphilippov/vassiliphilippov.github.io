infrigidation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfrɪdʒɪˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnfrɪdʒɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of cooling or freezing something, especially food, in order to preserve it
process of cooling or freezing for preservation

Examples

Proper infrigidation of perishable foods is essential to prevent spoilage.
The laboratory uses advanced infrigidation systems to store sensitive samples.
New guidelines recommend infrigidation within two hours of preparing cooked meats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refrigeration - the process of keeping something cold to preserve it

Etymology

From Latin 'infrigidare' (to make cold) + English suffix '-ation'.
