Manteodea

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæntiˈoʊdiə / (AmE), / ˌmantiˈəʊdiə / (BrE)
An order of insects comprising the mantises, previously considered a suborder of Orthoptera in older classifications.
order of insects containing mantises

Examples

The Manteodea are known for their predatory habits and distinctive front legs.
Scientists often study the Manteodea to learn more about insect camouflage.
Insect taxonomy has changed over time, but Manteodea remains a distinct order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantis - a predatory insect of the order Manteodea

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Greek mantis (seer, prophet) + -odea (indicative of an order).
