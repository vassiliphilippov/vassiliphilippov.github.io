Allium tuberosum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm tuːˈbɛroʊsəm / (AmE), / ˈæliəm tjuːˈbɛrəsəm / (BrE)
a species of perennial plant native to eastern Asia, also known as garlic chives, with flat leaves and white flowers, often used as a culinary herb.
a plant species, garlic chives

Examples

The chef garnished the dish with freshly chopped Allium tuberosum for a mild garlicky flavor.
In her garden, she grows Allium tuberosum alongside other herbs for both the flowers and leaves.
Allium tuberosum is often used in Asian cuisine to add a subtle onion and garlic taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garlic chives - common name for Allium tuberosum, a culinary herb
  • Degree Lower: Allium schoenoprasum - a related species known as chives

Etymology

From Latin; 'allium' (garlic) and 'tuberosum' (tuberous), referring to the plant's swollen rootstock.
