mongrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋɡrəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋɡrəl / (BrE)
A dog of mixed or indeterminate breed, often regarded as inferior.
dog of mixed breed

Examples

The family adopted a mongrel from the animal shelter.
Her favorite companion was a loyal mongrel with a patchy coat.
Some owners prefer a mongrel for its unique personality and hardiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutt - an informal synonym meaning a dog of mixed breed
  • Antonym: purebred - a dog of known and unmixed ancestry

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'mongrel', of unknown origin; possibly related to 'mong' (to mix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋɡrəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋɡrəl / (BrE)
A derogatory term for something of mixed origin that is irregular, inferior, or not genuine.
derogatory term for irregular or mixed origin thing
offensive

Examples

Some critics dismissed the painting as a mongrel of several conflicting styles.
The chef's new menu is a mongrel of cuisines, lacking a clear identity.
He considered the dialect a mongrel and preferred the traditional language instead.

Synset Examples

the architecture was a kind of bastard suggesting Gothic but not true Gothic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hybrid - an animal or thing resulting from a mixture of different things
  • Antonym: purebred - of pure ancestry or origin

Etymology

From Middle English 'mongrel', perhaps from an unrecorded Old English root meaning 'mix'.
