chaos

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.ɑs / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.ɒs / (BrE)
A state of complete confusion and lack of order.
complete confusion or disorder

Examples

The sudden power outage threw the entire city into chaos.
After the announcement, there was chaos in the office as everyone tried to understand what was happening.
Her plans fell into chaos when her computer crashed before the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disorder - lack of order or organization
  • Synonym: confusion - a state of being bewildered or unclear
  • Antonym: order - an organized or harmonious state

Etymology

From Ancient Greek khaos (vast chasm, void).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.ɑs / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.ɒs / (BrE)
(in mythology and literature) the formless, empty, and disordered state believed to have existed before the creation of the universe.
primordial formless state before the cosmos

Examples

According to Greek mythology, the universe began as chaos before order emerged.
Many ancient myths describe chaos as a boundless void preceding the birth of gods and the world.
Poets often refer to chaos when depicting the great emptiness before creation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cosmos - the universe seen as an ordered system

Etymology

From Greek *khaos* (vast chasm, void).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.ɑs / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.ɒs / (BrE)
(Physics) A dynamical system whose behavior is extremely sensitive to initial conditions, making long-term prediction impossible in practice.
sensitive dynamical system (physics)

Examples

In physics, chaos refers to systems where tiny changes in the starting state lead to unpredictable outcomes.
The weather is often cited as a classic example of chaos in nature.
Scientists use mathematical models to study the chaos present in complex systems like the brain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaos theory - The mathematical study of chaotic systems

Etymology

From Greek khaos (abyss, empty space); scientific sense developed in the 20th century with chaos theory.
