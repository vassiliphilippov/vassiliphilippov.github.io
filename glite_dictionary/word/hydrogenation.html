hydrogenation

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdrɑdʒəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / haɪˌdrɒdʒɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a chemical process in which hydrogen is added to another compound, especially to convert unsaturated fats or oils into saturated ones.
chemical process adding hydrogen to compounds

Examples

Hydrogenation is commonly used in the food industry to create margarine from vegetable oils.
The process of hydrogenation can increase the shelf life of certain products.
Complete hydrogenation of fats results in products that are solid at room temperature.

Synset Examples

food producers use hydrogenation to keep fat from becoming rancid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hydrogenate - to treat with hydrogen; to add hydrogen to a compound
  • Antonym: dehydrogenation - removal of hydrogen from a compound, often the reverse process

Etymology

From hydrogen + -ation, reflecting the process of adding hydrogen.
