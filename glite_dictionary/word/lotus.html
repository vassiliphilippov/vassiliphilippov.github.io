Lotus

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊtəs / (AmE), / ˈləʊtəs / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the pea family (Fabaceae), consisting of annual or perennial herbs or subshrubs, many bearing yellow or pink flowers.
plant genus in the pea family

Examples

The Lotus genus includes species found in both Europe and Asia.
Botanists are studying several wild Lotus varieties for their environmental resilience.
Some Lotus species are popular in gardens for their colorful flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'lotus', from Greek 'lotos', meaning various plants or trees, especially those bearing edible fruit.
