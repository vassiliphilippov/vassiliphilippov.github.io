lotus

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.təs / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.təs / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Lotus, consisting of annual or perennial herbs or subshrubs, known for their showy flowers; sometimes also refers to other water plants with similar appearance.
a plant of the genus Lotus

Examples

The lotus is revered in many cultures for its beauty and symbolism.
She planted several lotuses in the garden pond.
The leaves of the lotus are often broad and water-repellent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lily - an aquatic plant with large floating leaves and flowers, sometimes confused with lotus

Etymology

From Greek 'lōtos', referring to several plants, especially those with edible or sacred attributes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.t̬əs / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.təs / (BrE)
A water plant native to eastern Asia, widely cultivated for its large pink or white flowers and often associated with spiritual symbolism.
aquatic plant with large showy flowers

Examples

The garden pond was filled with blooming lotus flowers in the summer.
A lotus is often used as a symbol of purity and spiritual enlightenment in many Asian cultures.
She photographed the delicate pink lotus rising above the still water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lily - an aquatic plant with large, typically round floating leaves and white or pink flowers

Etymology

From Greek *lōtos*, used for various plants, especially the sacred lotus.

3. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.təs / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.təs / (BrE)
A white water lily (Nymphaea lotus) native to Egypt and southeastern Africa, regarded as sacred by ancient Egyptians.
a white water lily of Egypt, Nymphaea lotus

Examples

The ancient Egyptians considered the lotus to be a symbol of creation and rebirth.
A single lotus flower floated gracefully on the surface of the pond.
Artists often depict the goddess Isis holding a white lotus in Egyptian art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lily - an aquatic plant with large floating leaves and showy flowers

Etymology

From Greek lōtos, referring to several plants, including water lilies.
