Ukrainian

1. NOUN

/ juˈkreɪniən / (AmE), / juˈkreɪniən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Ukraine.
person from Ukraine

Examples

She met a Ukrainian who shared stories about life in Kyiv.
The restaurant is owned by a proud Ukrainian who moved here ten years ago.
Many Ukrainians celebrate Independence Day with traditional foods and music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ukrainian - the language of Ukraine

Etymology

From Ukraine, the name of the country, plus the suffix -ian.

2. NOUN

/ juˈkreɪniən / (AmE), / juːˈkreɪniən / (BrE)
the Slavic language that is the official language of Ukraine
Slavic language of Ukraine

Examples

She speaks fluent Ukrainian with her grandparents.
The novel was originally written in Ukrainian, then translated into English.
Learning Ukrainian can help you better understand the culture of Ukraine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Russian - the Slavic language spoken mainly in Russia and neighboring countries
  • Synonym: Ukrainian (adj) - of or relating to Ukraine, its people, or language

Etymology

From Ukraine + -ian, denoting origin or association.

relating to Ukraine, its people, or its culture

Examples
