chase

1. NOUN

/ tʃeɪs / (AmE), / tʃeɪs / (BrE)
A rectangular metal frame used in letterpress printing to hold together pages or columns of type for printing.
metal frame for type in printing

Examples

The printer carefully locked the type into the chase before placing it on the press.
Antique letterpress machines often come with an original chase.
You must ensure the chase is square before tightening the quoins.

2. NOUN

/ tʃeɪs / (AmE), / tʃeɪs / (BrE)
an act of pursuing someone or something in order to catch or overtake them
an act of pursuit or hunting

Examples

The police began a high-speed chase after the robbery suspect fled the scene.
Fans watched the thrilling chase as the detective raced down the alley.
After an hour-long chase, the firefighters finally caught the stray dog.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pursuit - the act of pursuing someone or something
  • Antonym: flight - the act of fleeing or running away (opposite action)

Etymology

From Old French 'chacier' (to hunt, pursue), from Vulgar Latin '*captiare' (to catch).

4. VERB

/ tʃeɪs / (AmE), / tʃeɪs / (BrE)
(of a workman) to cut a furrow or groove into a surface, such as a column, usually to embed wiring or pipes or for decorative purposes.
cut a groove or furrow (esp. in masonry)

Examples

The stonemason carefully chased a groove into the marble column for the decorative inlay.
Electricians sometimes chase channels into walls to lay hidden cables.
Before installing the pipe, workers chased a narrow furrow along the base of the pillar.

Etymology

From Old French *chacier* (to hunt, pursue), later extended in English to denote tooling or carving grooves.
