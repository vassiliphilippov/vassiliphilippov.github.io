Hypsiglena

1. NOUN

/ hɪp.sɪˈɡliː.nə / (AmE), / hɪp.sɪˈɡliː.nə / (BrE)
A genus of small, nocturnal colubrid snakes commonly known as night snakes, found in North and Central America.
a genus of night snakes

Examples

The desert is home to several snake species, including the elusive Hypsiglena.
Researchers discovered a new subspecies of Hypsiglena in the arid valleys.
While hiking at night, we spotted a small Hypsiglena near the trail.

Etymology

From Greek 'hypsi-' meaning 'high' and 'glēnē' meaning 'socket' or 'cavity'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpsɪˈɡliːnə / (AmE), / ˌhɪpsɪˈɡliːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small, nocturnal snakes native to North and Central America, commonly known as night snakes.
a genus of night snakes

Examples

The Hypsiglena are elusive, preferring to hunt at night in arid regions.
Several species within Hypsiglena are found across the southwestern United States.
Researchers observed a juvenile Hypsiglena near the rocky outcrops last spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: night snake - a common name for snakes in the genus Hypsiglena

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Greek 'hypsi-' (high) and the snake name 'glene', referring to a characteristic of the genus.
