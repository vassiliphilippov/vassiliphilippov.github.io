Pascha

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːskə / (AmE), / ˈpaskə / (BrE)
A name for the Jewish festival of Passover, commemorating the Israelites' liberation from slavery in Egypt; sometimes also used for Easter in Christian tradition.
the Jewish feast of Passover
archaic

Examples

In some ancient texts, the celebration of Pascha is described in detail.
The date of Pascha was carefully calculated by Jewish scholars each year.
Early Christians often referred to Easter using the term Pascha, linking it to the Passover festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Passover - the major Jewish spring festival commemorating the liberation of the Israelites from Egyptian slavery

Etymology

From Latin 'Pascha', from Greek 'Πάσχα' (Páskha), from Aramaic and Hebrew 'pesakh' (Passover).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːskə / (AmE), / ˈpæskə / (BrE)
The Christian festival of Easter, especially as celebrated in Eastern Orthodox and some Eastern Christian traditions.
Christian festival of Easter

Examples

The Orthodox Church celebrates Pascha according to the Julian calendar.
Many traditions are observed during Pascha, including midnight liturgies and festive meals.
She baked a special bread for Pascha as part of her family's annual celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Easter - Christian festival celebrating the resurrection of Jesus

Etymology

From Latin 'pascha', from Greek 'páskha', from Hebrew 'pesach' (Passover).
