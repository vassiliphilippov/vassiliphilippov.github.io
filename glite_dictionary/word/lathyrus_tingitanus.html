Lathyrus tingitanus

1. NOUN

/ ləˈθaɪrəs tɪnˈdʒɪtənəs / (AmE), / ləˈθaɪrəs tɪnˈdʒɪtənəs / (BrE)
A North African annual plant, related to the sweet pea, with showy but odorless flowers.
North African plant with showy, odorless flowers

Examples

The vibrant flowers of Lathyrus tingitanus add color to Mediterranean gardens.
Botanists often compare Lathyrus tingitanus with the common sweet pea because of their similar appearance.
While Lathyrus tingitanus has striking petals, it lacks the fragrance of its relatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet pea - a commonly grown flowering plant related to Lathyrus tingitanus

Etymology

From Latin genus name Lathyrus and species name tingitanus, referring to Tangier in North Africa.
