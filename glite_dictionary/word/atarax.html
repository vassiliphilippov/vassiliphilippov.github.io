Atarax

1. NOUN

/ ˈæt̬.əˌræks / (AmE), / ˈæt.ə.ræks / (BrE)
A trademarked drug name for hydroxyzine, used as a tranquilizer to treat anxiety and motion sickness.
a tranquilizer drug (hydroxyzine)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Atarax to help manage her anxiety before surgery.
Some people use Atarax to prevent nausea during long car trips.
After taking Atarax, he felt much calmer and less restless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydroxyzine - a medication used to treat anxiety and allergies; the generic name for Atarax
