to disambiguate

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.æmˈbɪɡ.juː.eɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.æmˈbɪɡ.juː.eɪt / (BrE)
to state something clearly by removing ambiguity or uncertainty
make clear by removing ambiguity

Examples

The editor asked the author to disambiguate the meaning of the phrase.
Advanced search features can help disambiguate similar terms in a database.
We need to disambiguate these instructions so everyone understands them clearly.

Synset Examples

Can you disambiguate this statement?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clarify - to make something easier to understand; remove confusion
  • Antonym: obscure - to make something unclear or difficult to understand

Etymology

Formed from the prefix dis- (expressing removal) and 'ambiguate' (from Latin ambiguus, meaning 'uncertain').
