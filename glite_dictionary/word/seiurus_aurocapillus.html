Seiurus aurocapillus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈjʊr.əs ɔːr.oʊ.kəˈpɪl.əs / (AmE), / saɪˈjʊər.əs ɔː.rəʊ.kəˈpɪl.əs / (BrE)
A species of American warbler, also known as the ovenbird, noted for building a dome-shaped nest on the ground.
American warbler species (ovenbird)

Examples

The distinctive call of the Seiurus aurocapillus echoes across North American forests every spring.
Biologists noted the Seiurus aurocapillus building its nest in a sheltered spot on the forest floor.
Birdwatchers hope to spot a Seiurus aurocapillus during their hike in the woodland preserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ovenbird - American warbler with orange crown and dome-shaped ground nest

Etymology

From Latin; 'Seiurus' (a genus of North American warblers) and 'aurocapillus' meaning 'golden-haired', referring to its orange crown.
