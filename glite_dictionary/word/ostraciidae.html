Ostraciidae

1. NOUN

/ ɒsˌtreɪsiˈaɪədi / (AmE), / ɒsˌtræsɪˈiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, box-shaped tropical marine fishes commonly known as boxfishes, characterized by their rigid, bony carapaces.
family of box-shaped tropical fishes
medical

Examples

Members of the Ostraciidae family are easily recognized by their box-like bodies and bright coloration.
Marine biologists often study Ostraciidae to understand the evolutionary advantages of their protective armor.
Aquariums sometimes showcase Ostraciidae because of their unique shape and fascinating appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxfish - common name for fishes of the Ostraciidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'ostrakon' (shell) + '-idae' (family suffix in zoological classification).
