Fundulus majalis

1. NOUN

/ fʌnˈduː.ləs məˈdʒaɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / fʌnˈdjuː.ləs məˈdʒeɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
A black-barred fish found in bays and coastal marshes along the Atlantic and Gulf Coast of the United States.
a coastal North American killifish species

Examples

The Fundulus majalis inhabits shallow waters and is often seen darting among marsh grasses.
Marine biologists studied the diet of Fundulus majalis along the Gulf Coast.
Because of its unique appearance, Fundulus majalis is easily distinguished from other killifish species.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Fundulus' meaning 'bottom' (referring to the fish's habit), and 'majalis' meaning 'of or pertaining to May.'
