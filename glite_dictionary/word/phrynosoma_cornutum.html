Phrynosoma cornutum

1. NOUN

/ frɪˌnoʊˈsoʊmə kɔːrˈnuːtəm / (AmE), / frɪˌnəʊˈsəʊmə kɔːˈnjuːtəm / (BrE)
a species of horned lizard native to arid and semiarid regions of the southern United States, especially Texas, known for its distinctive horns and spiny body
a North American horned lizard species

Examples

The Phrynosoma cornutum blends into desert landscapes thanks to its sandy coloration.
Wildlife researchers are tracking the population of Phrynosoma cornutum in western Texas.
Many Texans know the Phrynosoma cornutum as the state reptile and a symbol of local heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Texas horned lizard - common name for Phrynosoma cornutum

Etymology

From Greek phrynos (toad) + soma (body) and Latin cornutum (horned).
