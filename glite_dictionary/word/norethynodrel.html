norethynodrel

1. NOUN

/ nɔːˌrɛθ.ɪˈnoʊ.drɛl / (AmE), / nɔːˌrɛθ.ɪˈnəʊ.drɛl / (BrE)
a synthetic progestogen used in oral contraceptives and for treatment of menstrual disorders and abnormal uterine bleeding
synthetic progestogen used in contraceptives and menstrual therapy
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed norethynodrel to help regulate her menstrual cycle.
Early oral contraceptives often contained norethynodrel as the active hormonal ingredient.
Research has shown norethynodrel can effectively treat abnormal uterine bleeding.

Etymology

From 'nor-' + 'ethynyl' + 'hydroxyprogesterone' (components indicating chemical structure).
