convincingly

1. ADV

/ kənˈvɪnsɪŋli / (AmE), / kənˈvɪnsɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that makes someone believe that something is true or valid.
in a believable or persuasive manner

Examples

She spoke convincingly about her plans for the future during the interview.
The defendant explained his alibi so convincingly that the jury began to doubt the prosecution's case.
If you want to win the debate, you must argue your point convincingly.

Synset Examples

he argued convincingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persuasively - in a way that is able to persuade someone to do or believe something
  • Antonym: unconvincingly - in a way that does not make someone believe that something is true or valid

Etymology

From 'convincing' + '-ly'.
