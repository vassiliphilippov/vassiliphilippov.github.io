Picea breweriana

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈsiːə bruːˈɛriːˌænə / (AmE), / paɪˈsiːə bruːˈɛəriːˌanə / (BrE)
a medium-sized species of spruce native to California and Oregon, distinguished by its pendulous branches
a species of spruce tree with pendulous branches

Examples

The Picea breweriana is well known for its graceful, drooping branches.
Conservationists are working to protect the limited range of Picea breweriana in the wild.
Botanists admire the unique silhouette of Picea breweriana among North American spruce species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spruce - a type of evergreen tree of the genus Picea

Etymology

From Latin; genus 'Picea' (spruce) and species named after botanist William Henry Brewer.
