necropolis

1. NOUN

/ nəˈkrɑːpəlɪs / (AmE), / nɛˈkrɒpəlɪs / (BrE)
a large ancient cemetery or burial ground, often associated with a specific city or civilization
large ancient cemetery or burial ground

Examples

The archaeologists uncovered a vast necropolis dating back to the Roman Empire.
Many ancient cities had a necropolis located on the outskirts for funeral rites and burials.
Tourists visit the Egyptian necropolis of Saqqara to see its remarkable tombs and pyramids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cemetery - a place where dead people are buried
  • Synonym: graveyard - an area of land, often near a church, used for burying the dead

Etymology

From Greek nekropolis, meaning 'city of the dead', from nekros (dead body) + polis (city).
