uranyl

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊr.ə.nɪl / (AmE), / ˈjʊə.rə.nɪl / (BrE)
a bivalent chemical ion or radical (UO₂) of uranium, which forms salts with acids
bivalent uranium ion (UO₂)
medical

Examples

The bright yellow color of uranyl compounds makes them useful in porcelain glazing.
Scientists detected uranyl ions in the water sample near the old mine.
In the laboratory, uranyl nitrate was added to begin the precipitation reaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uranate - a salt containing the uranate ion
  • Synonym: uranium - a radioactive chemical element (U)

Etymology

From French 'uranyle', from 'uranium' + '-yl' (chemical suffix).
