Lonas inodora

1. NOUN

/ loʊˌnæs ɪˈnoʊdərə / (AmE), / ˈlɒnəs ɪˈnɔːdərə / (BrE)
A shrub native to the southwestern Mediterranean region, known for its yellow, daisylike flowers.
Mediterranean shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

The gardens featured a rare specimen of Lonas inodora in full bloom.
Botanists studied the adaptations of Lonas inodora to dry Mediterranean climates.
The distinctive yellow flowers of Lonas inodora made it popular among horticulturists.

Etymology

Latin binomial: genus name 'Lonas' and species 'inodora' meaning 'odorless'.
