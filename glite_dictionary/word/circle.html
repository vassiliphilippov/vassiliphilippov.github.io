2. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.kəl / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kəl / (BrE)
A simple closed shape in which all points are equally distant from a fixed central point; the plane figure bounded by such a curve.
a round plane figure, all points equidistant from the center

Examples

The children sat in a circle and played a game together.
Draw a circle with a compass to get a perfect shape.
A ring is shaped like a circle.

Synset Examples

he calculated the circumference of the circle

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ellipse - a regular oval shape, a closed curve on a plane
  • Degree Higher: sphere - a perfectly round geometrical 3D object, like the shape of a round ball
  • Antonym: square - a plane figure with four equal straight sides and four right angles

Etymology

From Latin circulus (small ring), diminutive of circus (ring, circle).
