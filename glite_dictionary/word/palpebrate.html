palpebrate

1. VERB

/ ˈpæl.pəˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈpal.pɪ.breɪt / (BrE)
To wink or blink, especially repeatedly.
blink or wink repeatedly

Examples

The patient began to palpebrate rapidly as the bright light shone in his eyes.
Some reptiles palpebrate to clear debris from their eyes.
He would nervously palpebrate whenever asked a difficult question.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blink - to shut and open the eyes quickly
  • Synonym: wink - to close and open one eye quickly, often as a signal

Etymology

From Latin 'palpebra' (eyelid).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpæl.pəˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈpal.pɪˌbreɪt / (BrE)
Having eyelids.
having eyelids

Examples

Most mammals are palpebrate creatures, unlike some snakes and fish.
The study compared palpebrate and non-palpebrate species to understand evolutionary adaptations.
Certain amphibians develop palpebrate features during metamorphosis.

Etymology

From Latin 'palpebra' meaning 'eyelid'.
