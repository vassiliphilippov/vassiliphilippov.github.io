Cahita

1. NOUN

/ kɑˈhitə / (AmE), / kɑˈhiːtə / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people of the Taracahitian group from central Mexico
member of Taracahitian people of Mexico

Examples

The Cahita were known for their unique agricultural practices in central Mexico.
Anthropologists have studied the traditional crafts of the Cahita people.
Members of the Cahita community continue to preserve their language and customs.

2. NOUN

/ kɑːˈhiːtə / (AmE), / kəˈhiːtə / (BrE)
the Uto-Aztecan language spoken by the Cahita people in northwestern Mexico
a Uto-Aztecan language of Mexico

Examples

The Cahita language is still spoken in some rural communities in Sonora and Sinaloa.
Linguists have studied the structure of Cahita to understand its relationship to other Uto-Aztecan languages.
Efforts are underway to preserve the Cahita language and teach it to younger generations.
