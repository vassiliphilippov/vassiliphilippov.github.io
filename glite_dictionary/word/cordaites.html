Cordaites

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˈdaɪtiːz / (AmE), / kɔːˈdaɪtiːz / (BrE)
an extinct genus of tall Paleozoic trees that superficially resembled modern screw pines and were structurally intermediate between cycads and conifers
extinct genus of Paleozoic trees

Examples

Fossils of Cordaites show that these trees grew in swampy environments during the late Paleozoic era.
The leaves of Cordaites were long and strap-shaped, resembling those of some modern plants.
Paleobotanists study Cordaites to understand the evolution of conifers and other seed plants.

Etymology

From the genus name, honoring the French botanist Augustin Pyramus de Candolle (Cordait being a Latinized form of his name).

2. NOUN

/ kɔrˈdaɪtiːz / (AmE), / kɔːˈdaɪtiːz / (BrE)
an extinct genus of tall Paleozoic trees that superficially resembled screw pines and were structurally intermediate between cycads and conifers
extinct genus of tall Paleozoic trees

Examples

Fossils of Cordaites have been found in many Paleozoic rock formations around the world.
The structure of Cordaites leaves offers important clues about plant evolution.
Scientists believe that Cordaites may be an ancestor of modern conifers.

Etymology

Named after French botanist Auguste Cordait.
