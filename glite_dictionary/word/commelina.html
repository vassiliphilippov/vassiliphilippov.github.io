commelina

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɛlɪnə / (AmE), / kəˈmiːlɪnə / (BrE)
Any plant belonging to the genus Commelina, commonly known as dayflowers, which have delicate blue or purple flowers.
a genus of flowering plants (dayflowers)

Examples

The garden featured several species of commelina with vibrant blue petals.
Researchers studied how commelina adapts to different soil types.
Many commelina plants bloom early in the morning, closing by midday.

Etymology

Named after Dutch botanists Jan and Caspar Commelin.
