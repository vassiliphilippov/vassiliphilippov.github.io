idyllic

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈdɪlɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈdɪlɪk / (BrE)
extremely pleasant, peaceful, or picturesque in a way that suggests a perfect, idealized setting.
extremely pleasant and picturesque

Examples

They spent an idyllic summer in the countryside, away from the noise of the city.
The cottage was set in an idyllic landscape of rolling hills and wildflowers.
She remembered her childhood as an idyllic time filled with laughter and sunlight.

Synset Examples

an idyllic spot for a picnic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ideal - satisfying one's conception of what is perfect; most suitable
  • Synonym: paradisiacal - resembling or befitting paradise; idyllic
  • Antonym: harsh - unpleasantly rough or jarring to the senses

Etymology

From Greek eidyllion (short poem or little picture), via Latin idylicus and French idyllique.

2. ADJ

/ aɪˈdɪlɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈdɪlɪk / (BrE)
(of a place or time) extremely happy, peaceful, or picturesque; like an idyll.
charmingly simple and peaceful

Examples

They spent an idyllic afternoon picnicking by the river.
The cottage is set in an idyllic location surrounded by wildflowers.
She described her childhood as idyllic, filled with joy and freedom.

Synset Examples

his idyllic life in Tahiti

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastoral - (of a work of art) portraying an idealized version of country life
  • Synonym: serene - calm, peaceful, and untroubled; tranquil

Etymology

From Greek eidyllion (short poem or little picture), via Latin idylicus and French idyllique.

Synset POS: s
