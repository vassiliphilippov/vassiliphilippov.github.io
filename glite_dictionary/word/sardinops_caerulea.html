Sardinops caerulea

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈdaɪnɑps siˈruliə / (AmE), / sɑːˈdaɪnɒps siːˈruːlɪə / (BrE)
a species of small pilchard found off the Pacific coast of North America, commonly known as the Pacific sardine
species of pilchard (Pacific sardine)

Examples

Commercial fishing of Sardinops caerulea was once a major industry along the California coast.
Recent studies show the population of Sardinops caerulea is recovering after years of decline.
The diet of many marine birds includes Sardinops caerulea as a primary food source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sardine - small, oily fish commonly used as food

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Sardinops' + species 'caerulea' (meaning 'blue').
