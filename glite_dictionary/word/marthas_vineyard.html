Martha's Vineyard

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑːrθəz ˈvɪnjɚd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːθəz ˈvɪnjəd / (BrE)
an island off the coast of Massachusetts, known as a summer resort destination.
island resort off Massachusetts

Examples

Many celebrities spend their summers on Martha's Vineyard enjoying the beaches and restaurants.
The ferry to Martha's Vineyard gets very crowded during the tourist season.
Historic lighthouses are one of the many attractions on Martha's Vineyard.

Etymology

Named after Martha, daughter of English explorer Bartholomew Gosnold, and the presence of wild grapes ('vineyard').
