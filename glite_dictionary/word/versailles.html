Versailles

1. PROPN

/ vɛrˈsaɪ / (AmE), / vɛəˈsaɪ / (BrE)
a city in north-central France near Paris, famous for the Palace of Versailles, which was built by Louis XIV in the 17th century and is known for its historical significance and grandeur.
city in France; site of famous royal palace

Examples

The Palace of Versailles attracts millions of visitors from around the world every year.
After the French Revolution, many royal furnishings from Versailles were sold or lost.
The Treaty of Versailles was signed in the famous Hall of Mirrors, ending World War I.

Etymology

From French Versailles, the name of the city, likely from Old French verser (to turn the soil).

2. PROPN

/ vərˈsaɪ / (AmE), / vɛəˈsaɪ / (BrE)
a grand palace and former royal residence built in the 17th century for Louis XIV, located southwest of Paris near the city of Versailles
grand palace near Paris, royal residence

Examples

The Hall of Mirrors in Versailles is famous for its breathtaking beauty and historical significance.
During their trip to France, they spent an entire day exploring Versailles and its gardens.
Many treaties, including the Treaty of Versailles, were signed at this magnificent palace.

Etymology

From French Versailles, the location of the palace, itself of uncertain origin.
