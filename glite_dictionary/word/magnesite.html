magnesite

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡnəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæɡnɪsaɪt / (BrE)
a white mineral composed of magnesium carbonate, used as a source of magnesium and in making refractory materials
white mineral; source of magnesium

Examples

The factory processes magnesite to produce magnesium metal for commercial use.
Deposits of magnesite are commonly found in ultramafic rocks.
Engineers selected magnesite bricks for lining the high-temperature furnaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnesium - a chemical element, symbol Mg, essential for life

Etymology

From magnesium + -ite, denoting a mineral.
