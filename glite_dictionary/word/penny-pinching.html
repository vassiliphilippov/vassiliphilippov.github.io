penny-pinching

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛni ˌpɪntʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɛni ˌpɪntʃɪŋ / (BrE)
unwilling to spend or give much money; very reluctant to part with money
unwilling to spend money

Examples

Her penny-pinching attitude meant she would never buy anything she didn't absolutely need.
Everyone avoided dining out with his penny-pinching uncle, who never wanted to split the bill fairly.
The company’s penny-pinching policies frustrated employees who needed better equipment to work efficiently.

Synset Examples

our cheeseparing administration
very close (or near) with his money
a penny-pinching miserly old man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miserly - unwilling to spend money; stingy
  • Antonym: generous - willing to give or spend freely; unselfish

Etymology

From 'penny' (a small amount of money) + 'pinching' (suggesting stinginess).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛni ˌpɪntʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɛni ˌpɪntʃɪŋ / (BrE)
extreme unwillingness to spend money; excessive frugality or stinginess
unwillingness to spend money

Examples

Her penny-pinching meant she always looked for the best deals before buying anything.
Despite his wealth, his penny-pinching often frustrated his friends and family.
Their penny-pinching approach made the company profitable, but morale was very low.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stinginess - unwillingness to give or spend; mean
  • Antonym: generosity - willingness to give or share; liberality

Etymology

From 'penny' (a small amount of money) and 'pinching' (holding tightly, suggesting a reluctance to let go).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04900720-n
Synset POS: n
