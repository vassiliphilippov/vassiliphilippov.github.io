ashen

1. ADJ

/ ˈæʃən / (AmE), / ˈæʃən / (BrE)
made of or resembling the wood of the ash tree
made of ash wood

Examples

The cabinet was crafted from ashen boards, giving it a pale and elegant look.
They admired the ashen beams supporting the old cottage's roof.
A pair of ashen chairs stood in the corner, their grain visible in the soft light.

Etymology

From 'ash' (the tree) + '-en' (adjectival suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæʃən / (AmE), / ˈaʃən / (BrE)
pale and gray in color, especially because of illness, shock, or strong emotion
pale and grayish from shock or illness

Examples

She appeared ashen after hearing the unexpected news.
His face turned ashen when he saw the accident scene.
The patient's ashen complexion worried the nurse.

Synset Examples

the invalid's blanched cheeks
tried to speak with bloodless lips
a face livid with shock
lips … livid with the hue of death
lips white with terror
a face white with rage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pale - having a lighter color than usual, as from illness or fear
  • Antonym: florid - having a red or flushed complexion

Etymology

From Old English 'æscen,' meaning 'of ash,' from 'æsc' (ash tree, ash).
