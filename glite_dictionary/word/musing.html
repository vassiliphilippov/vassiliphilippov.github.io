musing

1. ADJ

/ ˈmjuzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmjuːzɪŋ / (BrE)
deeply or seriously thoughtful; engaged in reflective contemplation
deeply thoughtful or contemplative

Examples

She sat by the window, her musing expression lost in the passing clouds.
His musing mood made him a quiet and attentive listener to others.
With a musing look, the professor considered the question before answering.

Synset Examples

Byron lives on not only in his poetry, but also in his creation of the ‘Byronic hero’ - the persona of a brooding melancholy young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflective - thoughtful, especially about past experiences or ideas
  • Synonym: brooding - deeply or moodily thoughtful

Etymology

From verb 'muse', from Old French *muser* (to ponder, wonder).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmju.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmjuː.zɪŋ / (BrE)
a period of deep or thoughtful reflection; a calm, prolonged consideration or contemplation
deep or thoughtful reflection

Examples

She sat by the window, lost in her musing about the future.
His musing on the meaning of life led him to write poetry.
The professor's musing was interrupted by a knock at the door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflection - serious thought or consideration
  • Degree Lower: daydream - a series of pleasant thoughts that distract from the present
  • Degree Higher: meditation - the action or practice of meditating; deep focus or contemplation

Etymology

From verb 'muse,' from Old French *muser* (to ponder); related to Latin *musa* (muse, source of inspiration).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05793513-n
Synset POS: n
