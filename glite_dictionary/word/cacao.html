cacao

1. NOUN

/ kəˈkaʊ / (AmE), / kəˈkaʊ / (BrE)
a tropical American tree (Theobroma cacao) that produces seeds (cacao beans) used to make cocoa and chocolate
tropical tree producing cacao beans

Examples

The cacao tree is native to Central and South America.
Farmers carefully harvest the pods from the cacao each season.
Many chocolate products are made from the seeds of the cacao plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cocoa - powder made from roasted and ground cacao seeds

Etymology

From Spanish 'cacao', from Nahuatl 'cacahuatl'.
