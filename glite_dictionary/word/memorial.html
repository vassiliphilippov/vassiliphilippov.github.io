memorial

1. NOUN

/ məˈmɔːriəl / (AmE), / mɪˈmɔːriəl / (BrE)
a structure, such as a statue or monument, built to remind people of a person or event
structure commemorating a person or event

Examples

The city unveiled a new memorial to honor the fallen soldiers.
Tourists visit the memorial every year to pay their respects.
A stone memorial was placed in the park to commemorate the historic event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monument - a statue, building, or structure erected to commemorate a famous or notable person or event

Etymology

From Latin 'memorialis' (belonging to memory), from 'memoria' (memory).

2. NOUN

/ məˈmɔɹ.i.əl / (AmE), / mɪˈmɔː.ri.əl / (BrE)
a written statement of facts, often accompanying a petition submitted to an authority, especially to request action or decision
formal written statement for an authority

Examples

The citizens presented a memorial to Parliament asking for urgent reforms.
The lawyer drafted a detailed memorial in support of the client's case.
A memorial was submitted to the council, outlining the local community's concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petition - a formal written request to an authority

Etymology

From Latin memoriale (record, reminder), from memoria (memory).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06525615-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ məˈmɔriəl / (AmE), / mɪˈmɔːriəl / (BrE)
an official recognition or statement in honor of meritorious service, especially in a formal context
formal recognition of service

Examples

The organization awarded him a memorial in recognition of his years of dedicated service.
A special memorial was issued to honor her contributions to the company.
He received a memorial from the committee, praising his meritorious achievements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commendation - formal praise or award for merit or service

Etymology

From Latin memoriale (record, memory), from memoria (memory).
