to refreshen

1. VERB

/ rɪˈfrɛʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
to make fresh or lively again; to restore freshness
make fresh again

Examples

Opening the windows helped to refreshen the musty air in the room.
She splashed cold water on her face to refreshen herself after a long walk.
The hotel staff quickly refreshened the linens before the new guests arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refresh - to make fresh or restore strength; similar meaning

Etymology

Formed from 'refresh', itself from Old French 'refreschir', based on Latin 'frescus' (fresh).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈfrɛʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɛʃən / (BrE)
to make or become fresh again; to restore freshness or vitality
make or become fresh again

Examples

A quick shower helped him refreshen before the evening meeting.
Open windows to refreshen the air in the house after painting.
After hours of study, she took a walk to refreshen her mind.

Synset Examples

She freshened up after the tennis game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to freshen - to make fresh or fresher

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 're-' to 'freshen', from 'fresh'.
