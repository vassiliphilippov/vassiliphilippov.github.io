Argyrol

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːr.dʒɪˌrɒl / (AmE), / ˈɑː.dʒɪ.rɒl / (BrE)
a trademarked antiseptic preparation consisting of a protein-silver compound, formerly used in medicine
trademarked antiseptic of protein-silver compound
medical

Examples

The physician applied Argyrol to the wound as an antiseptic.
Before antibiotics, Argyrol was commonly used in treating eye infections.
She found an old bottle of Argyrol in her grandfather's medical kit.

Etymology

Formed from Greek 'argyros' (silver) and '-ol' (as in chemical compounds).
