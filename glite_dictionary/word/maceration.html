maceration

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæsəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmasəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of becoming extremely lean, typically as a result of starvation or disease
extreme leanness from starvation or illness
medical

Examples

Months of illness had left his body in a state of maceration.
The doctor noted the maceration visible in patients suffering from severe malnutrition.
Historical accounts describe the maceration suffered by prisoners in the camp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emaciation - the state of being abnormally thin or weak, especially because of illness or lack of food

Etymology

From Latin 'maceratio' (a making thin or lean), from 'macerare' (to make soft, thin).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæs.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmæs.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The process of softening or breaking up a substance by soaking or steeping in a liquid.
softening by soaking

Examples

The maceration of the fruit in sugar created a delicious syrup.
After several hours of maceration, the herbs released their full flavor into the oil.
Excessive water can lead to the maceration of delicate plant roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infusion - the process of steeping something in liquid to extract flavor or healing properties
  • Antonym: dehydration - the process of removing moisture or water

Etymology

From Latin maceratio (a soaking), from macerare (to soften by soaking).
