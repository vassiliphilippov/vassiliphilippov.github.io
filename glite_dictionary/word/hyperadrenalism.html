hyperadrenalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərəˈdrɛnəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpərəˈdriːnəlɪzəm / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the adrenal glands produce an excessive amount of hormones, often resulting in symptoms such as obesity.
excessive activity of the adrenal glands
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with hyperadrenalism after experiencing rapid weight gain and high blood pressure.
Prolonged hyperadrenalism can lead to complications such as diabetes and cardiovascular disease.
Doctors measured hormone levels to confirm the presence of hyperadrenalism in the patient.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypoadrenalism - insufficient activity of the adrenal glands

Etymology

From hyper- (excessive) + adrenal (relating to adrenal glands) + -ism (condition).
