Sir Thomas Wyatt

1. PROPN

/ sər ˈtɑːməs ˈwaɪət / (AmE), / sə ˈtɒməs ˈwaɪət / (BrE)
(1503–1542) An English poet credited with introducing the sonnet form into English literature.
English poet, introduced sonnet to English

Examples

Sir Thomas Wyatt is often credited with popularizing the Italian sonnet in English poetry.
Scholars have studied the influence of Sir Thomas Wyatt on later poets such as Shakespeare.
The works of Sir Thomas Wyatt marked a turning point in English Renaissance literature.
