bereaved

1. NOUN

/ bəˈrivd / (AmE), / bɪˈriːvd / (BrE)
a person who has suffered the death of someone they loved
person grieving a loved one's death

Examples

The bereaved gathered together to share memories and support each other after the funeral.
Counselors were available to help the bereaved cope with their loss.
He reached out to the bereaved family to express his deepest condolences.

Synset Examples

the bereaved do not always need to be taken care of

Etymology

Past participle of 'bereave', from Old English 'bereafian' (to deprive).

2. ADJ

/ bəˈriːvd / (AmE), / bɪˈriːvd / (BrE)
Suffering the loss of a loved one through death; deeply saddened or sorrowful because of such loss.
sorrowful due to loss, especially death

Examples

After the funeral, friends gathered to support the bereaved family.
Counselors are trained to help the bereaved cope with their grief.
The bereaved widow struggled to adjust to life without her husband.

Synset Examples

bereft of hope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grieving - feeling intense sorrow, especially due to loss
  • Synonym: mournful - feeling or expressing sadness, especially because of someone's death
  • Antonym: joyful - feeling, expressing, or causing great happiness

Etymology

From Old English 'bereafian' (to deprive, rob), related to 'reave' (to plunder).
