greybeard

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪˌbɪrd / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪˌbɪəd / (BrE)
an old man, especially one with a grey beard, often used to imply wisdom or experience
old man, usually with a grey beard

Examples

The respected greybeard shared stories from his youth around the fire.
Many saw him as a wise greybeard who offered guidance to the community.
The novel's protagonist seeks advice from an old greybeard in the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: old-timer - an old man, especially one with long experience
  • Antonym: youngster - a young person, especially a child or adolescent

Etymology

From 'grey' (color) + 'beard', originally describing the beard's color typical of old age.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪˌbɪrd / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪˌbɪːd / (BrE)
A stoneware drinking jug with a long neck, typically decorated with a caricature of Cardinal Bellarmine, used in the 17th century.
17th-century stoneware jug with a long neck

Examples

The museum displayed a rare greybeard from the 17th century in its ceramics collection.
Collectors highly value a greybeard that retains its original glaze and decoration.
She recognized the greybeard by the bearded face molded on its neck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellarmine - Another name for a greybeard jug, historically based on Cardinal Bellarmine

Etymology

From the resemblance of the jug’s decoration, often bearded, to an old man's grey beard; also called 'Bellarmine' after Cardinal Bellarmine.
