tricot

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrikoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtriːkɒt / (BrE)
A soft, stretchy knitted fabric, typically made from wool, cotton, or synthetic fibers, used especially in clothing and lingerie.
soft, stretchy knitted fabric

Examples

This dress is made of tricot, which makes it comfortable and flexible.
The lingerie collection features delicate lace and smooth tricot fabric.
Manufacturers prefer tricot for sportswear because of its breathability and stretch.

Etymology

From French tricot (knitted fabric), from tricoter (to knit).
