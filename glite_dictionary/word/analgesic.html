analgesic

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.əlˈdʒi.sɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.əlˈdʒiː.zɪk / (BrE)
a drug or substance used to relieve pain without causing loss of consciousness
pain-relieving drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an analgesic to manage her post-surgery pain.
You can purchase some analgesics over the counter for headaches.
Certain analgesic medications are not suitable for children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: painkiller - a drug that relieves pain

Etymology

From Greek an- (without) + algēsis (sense of pain).

2. ADJ

/ ˌæn.əlˈdʒi.sɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.əlˈdʒiː.zɪk / (BrE)
(of a drug or substance) capable of relieving pain.
relieving pain (as a drug)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an analgesic cream to ease the patient's joint pain.
Some plants are known for their analgesic properties and are used in herbal medicine.
After surgery, she was given an analgesic medication to manage her discomfort.

Synset Examples
