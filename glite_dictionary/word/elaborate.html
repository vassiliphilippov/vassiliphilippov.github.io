elaborate

1. VERB

/ əˈlæbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlæbəreɪt / (BrE)
to produce something from basic elements or sources; to develop or refine into a more complex product
develop or produce something complex

Examples

Honeybees elaborate wax from the sugars they consume.
The plant cells elaborate complex compounds from simple nutrients.
Certain enzymes elaborate specific molecules within the cell.

Synset Examples

The bee elaborates honey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to synthesize - to combine elements to form a more complex product

Etymology

From Latin elaborare (to work out, produce by labor).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01628380-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪˈlæbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlæbəreɪt / (BrE)
to add more details to something; to explain or describe something in a more thorough or comprehensive way, especially in writing or speaking
add more detail or expand on an idea

Examples

The professor asked her to elaborate on her argument during the presentation.
Can you elaborate a bit more on your plan so everyone understands?
He elaborated his theory with numerous examples and case studies.

Synset Examples

She elaborated on the main ideas in her dissertation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expand - to make something larger or more detailed in discussion or writing
  • Antonym: to condense - to make something shorter or more concise by removing unnecessary details

Etymology

From Latin elaboratus, past participle of elaborare (to work out, elaborate), from e- (out) + laborare (to work).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00957633-v
Synset POS: v
