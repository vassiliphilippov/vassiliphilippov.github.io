quixotic

1. ADJ

/ kwɪkˈsɑtɪk / (AmE), / kwɪkˈsɒtɪk / (BrE)
extremely idealistic, unrealistic, and impractical, especially in pursuit of ideals or perfection.
unrealistically idealistic or impractical

Examples

His quixotic plan to start a utopian community quickly ran into practical difficulties.
She admired his quixotic quest to end world hunger, even if it seemed impossible.
Attempting to solve all the world's problems alone is a rather quixotic ambition.

Synset Examples

as quixotic as a restoration of medieval knighthood
a romantic disregard for money
a wild-eyed dream of a world state

Related Terms

  • Antonym: realistic - having a sensible and practical idea of what can be achieved or expected
  • Synonym: romantic - characterized by or suggestive of an idealized view of reality; often impractical

Etymology

From Don Quixote, the title character in the Spanish novel by Cervantes (1605–15), noted for his idealism and impracticality.
