pothole

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːtˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɒthəʊl / (BrE)
A hole in the surface of a road, typically caused by wear, weather, or traffic.
hole in a road surface

Examples

Be careful not to drive over that pothole or you might damage your tire.
After the harsh winter, the streets were full of potholes.
The city sent a crew to repair the dangerous pothole in front of my house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crater - a large hole in the ground caused by a violent explosion or impact

Etymology

Formed from 'pot' + 'hole', originally referring to a pit where potters dug for clay in British English.
