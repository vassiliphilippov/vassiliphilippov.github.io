Klyuchevskaya Sopka

1. PROPN

/ kljuːˌtʃɛfˈskaɪə ˈsɒpkə / (AmE), / klʲuːˌtʃɛfˈskaɪə ˈsɒpkə / (BrE)
an inactive volcano located on the Kamchatka Peninsula in Russia, known for its large size and historical eruptions
inactive volcano in Kamchatka, Russia

Examples

Scientists have long studied Klyuchevskaya Sopka because of its remarkable volcanic activity.
Tourists visit the Kamchatka Peninsula to see the impressive silhouette of Klyuchevskaya Sopka on the horizon.
In 1946, Klyuchevskaya Sopka last erupted, adding to its dramatic geological history.

Etymology

From Russian Ключевская Сопка (Klyuchevskaya Sopka), meaning 'Klyuchev's Hill'.
