to confine

1. VERB

/ kənˈfaɪn / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪn / (BrE)
to keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits or boundaries; to enclose or imprison.
restrict or keep within limits

Examples

The prisoners are confined to their cells for 23 hours a day.
He felt confined in the cramped office space with no windows.
The disease was successfully confined to one region of the country.

Synset Examples

darkness enclosed him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrain - to prevent someone or something from moving freely
  • Antonym: release - to set free from confinement or restraint

Etymology

From Latin 'confinare' (to border, limit), from 'con-' (together) and 'finis' (boundary).

5. VERB

/ kənˈfaɪn / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪn / (BrE)
to keep someone or something within limits or restrict their movement, often by depriving freedom or placing in confinement
restrict or imprison; keep within limits

Examples

The authorities decided to confine the suspect in a secure cell for questioning.
During the outbreak, the government had to confine travelers to quarantine facilities.
Parents sometimes confine their children to their rooms as a punishment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imprison - to put someone in prison or keep them somewhere against their will
  • Antonym: release - to set someone free from confinement

Etymology

From Latin 'confinare' (to border, limit), from 'con-' (together) + 'finis' (end, boundary).
