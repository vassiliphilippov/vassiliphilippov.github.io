Symphoricarpos

1. NOUN

/ sɪmˌfɔrɪˈkɑrpoʊs / (AmE), / sɪmˌfɒrɪˈkɑːpɒs / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous shrubs, commonly known as snowberries, native to North America, Central America, and China
genus of shrubs known as snowberries

Examples

Many species in the genus Symphoricarpos are valued for their ornamental white berries.
Symphoricarpos thrives in a variety of soil conditions across North America.
Gardeners often plant Symphoricarpos hedges to attract birds to their yards.

Etymology

From Greek symphorein (to bear together) and karpos (fruit), referring to the clustered berries.

2. NOUN

The Symphoricarpos genus includes the well-known snowberry, popular for its striking white fruit.
