Belemnoidea

1. NOUN

/ bəˌlɛmˈnɔɪdiə / (AmE), / bɪˌlɛmˈnɔɪdiə / (BrE)
An order of extinct dibranchiate cephalopods related to modern spirulas, known primarily from fossil records.
order of extinct cephalopods

Examples

Fossils of Belemnoidea provide important insights into ancient marine ecosystems.
The Belemnoidea were once abundant but disappeared by the end of the Cretaceous period.
Paleontologists have studied Belemnoidea to understand the evolution of modern cephalopods.

Etymology

From Greek 'belemnon' (dart, javelin) + '-oidea' (resembling form).

2. NOUN

/ bɛlˌɛmˈnɔɪdiə / (AmE), / bəlɛmˈnɔɪdiə / (BrE)
an order of extinct dibranchiate cephalopods related to modern spirulas, known chiefly from fossil remains
order of extinct cephalopods

Examples

Fossils from the Belemnoidea group provide important insights into ancient marine life.
Scientists recently discovered a new species belonging to the Belemnoidea in Jurassic rock strata.
Unlike modern squids, members of the Belemnoidea had a hard internal skeleton.

Etymology

From Greek *belemnon* (dart, javelin) + modern Latin taxonomy suffix -oidea, referring to the dart-like fossil shape.
