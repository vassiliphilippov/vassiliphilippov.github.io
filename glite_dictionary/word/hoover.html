Hoover

1. NOUN

/ ˈhuːvər / (AmE), / ˈhuːvə / (BrE)
(in British English) a vacuum cleaner, or any similar cleaning device, often used as a generic term for vacuum cleaners.
vacuum cleaner (British)
british

Examples

She used the Hoover to clean the carpets every Saturday morning.
The Hoover broke down, so they had to sweep the floor by hand.
Do you want to borrow my Hoover for your apartment?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuum cleaner - an electrical device for cleaning floors by suction

Etymology

Originally a trademark from the Hoover Company, named after its founder William Henry Hoover.
