to debase

1. VERB

/ dɪˈbeɪs / (AmE), / dɪˈbeɪs / (BrE)
To reduce the value of something, especially a coin, by adding metal of lower value to its composition.
lower the value of coins by adding base metal

Examples

The government decided to debase the currency to increase its silver reserves.
In ancient times, rulers would often debase coins to finance wars.
Collectors value coins made before the government began to debase their metal content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devalue - to reduce the value of; especially of currency

Etymology

From Old French *debaser*, from de- (down) + base (low, from Latin *bassus*, short or low).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈbeɪs / (AmE), / dɪˈbeɪs / (BrE)
to reduce the quality or purity of something, especially by adding inferior or foreign substances
reduce quality by adding inferior substances

Examples

The manufacturer was fined for trying to debase the product with cheaper ingredients.
Some unscrupulous dealers debase precious metals by mixing them with less valuable metals.
Historically, governments have debased their currency by reducing the amount of precious metal in coins.

Synset Examples

adulterate liquor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adulterate - to make something poorer in quality by adding another substance
  • Antonym: to purify - to remove impurities from something and make it pure

Etymology

From Old French 'debacier' (to lower in status), from Latin 'bassus' (low, short).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00488684-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈbeɪs / (AmE), / dɪˈbeɪs / (BrE)
to lower in moral quality or corrupt someone's character, especially through indulgence or sensuality
to corrupt morally or lower in character

Examples

Exposure to greed and dishonesty can debase even the most honorable individuals.
Some critics argued that the new media would debase the morals of young people.
He feared that luxury and excess might debase the character of the nation.

Synset Examples

debauch the young people with wine and women
Socrates was accused of corrupting young men
Do school counselors subvert young children?
corrupt the morals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debauch - lead into moral corruption or excessive indulgence
  • Synonym: corrupt - cause to become morally depraved
  • Antonym: uplift - improve the moral quality or status of someone

Etymology

From Old French *debacier* or Latin *bassus* (low, short), with influence from French *débaser* (to lower).
