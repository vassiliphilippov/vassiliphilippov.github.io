dolor

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊlɚ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊlə / (BrE)
painful sorrow or grief, especially of a profound or poetic nature
poetic/literary term for deep sorrow
archaic

Examples

In the poem, the protagonist is overwhelmed by dolor after the loss of his beloved.
Her eyes revealed an unfathomable dolor that words could not express.
The valley was said to be haunted by echoes of ancient dolor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorrow - a feeling of deep distress caused by loss or disappointment
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness

Etymology

From Latin 'dolor' (pain, grief), used poetically in English.
