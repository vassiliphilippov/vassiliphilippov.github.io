marsupial

1. ADJ

/ mɑrˈsuːpiəl / (AmE), / mɑːˈsuːpiəl / (BrE)
relating to or belonging to marsupials, a group of mammals that carry and nurture their young in a pouch.
relating to marsupials (pouched mammals)

Examples

The koala is a marsupial species native to Australia.
Scientists studied the marsupial reproductive system in detail.
A kangaroo is a well-known marsupial animal.

Synset Examples

marsupial animals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsupial - a mammal of the group Marsupialia, such as a kangaroo or opossum

Etymology

From modern Latin Marsupialia, from Latin marsupium (pouch).

2. NOUN

/ mɑrˈsuːpiəl / (AmE), / mɑːˈsuːpiəl / (BrE)
A mammal whose females carry and feed their young in a pouch on the abdomen.
mammal with a pouch for young

Examples

The kangaroo is perhaps the most famous marsupial native to Australia.
Most marsupials give birth to tiny, undeveloped young that crawl into their mother's pouch.
The koala, another well-known marsupial, spends much of its life in eucalyptus trees.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: placental - mammal whose young develop inside the womb with a placenta
  • Antonym: monotreme - egg-laying mammal such as the platypus

Etymology

From modern Latin Marsupialia, from Latin marsupium (pouch).
