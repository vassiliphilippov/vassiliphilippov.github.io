Tapirus terrestris

1. NOUN

/ təˈpaɪrəs tɛˈrɛstrɪs / (AmE), / təˈpaɪrəs tɛˈrɛstrɪs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the South American tapir, a large browsing mammal native to South and Central America
South American tapir (animal species)
medical

Examples

The Tapirus terrestris is easily recognized by its distinctive snout and robust body.
Researchers are studying Tapirus terrestris populations to assess their risk of extinction.
Habitat loss is a major threat to the survival of Tapirus terrestris in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapir - a large, heavy-bodied, hoofed mammal of tropical America and Southeast Asia

Etymology

From Latin: 'tapirus' (tapir, from Tupi 'tapira'); 'terrestris' meaning 'of the earth' or 'terrestrial'.
