prosecutor

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.sɪˌkjuː.tər / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.ɪ.kjuː.tər / (BrE)
a lawyer who represents the government and brings charges against a person accused of a crime in court
government lawyer for criminal cases

Examples

The prosecutor presented new evidence that was crucial to the trial.
After several weeks, the prosecutor finally rested her case against the defendant.
Local prosecutors are calling for stricter penalties for repeat offenders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defense attorney - a lawyer who defends an accused person in court
  • Synonym: district attorney - a public official who represents the government in legal proceedings in a specific district

Etymology

From Latin prosequutor, from prosequi (to pursue).
