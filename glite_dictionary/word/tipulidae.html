Tipulidae

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈpjuːlɪdiː / (AmE), / tɪˈpjuːlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, long-legged insects commonly known as crane flies, characterized by slender bodies and delicate wings.
family of insects known as crane flies

Examples

The Tipulidae are easily recognized by their long legs and slender bodies.
Scientists studied the life cycle of several Tipulidae species found in wetlands.
Gardeners often mistake Tipulidae for giant mosquitoes, but they do not bite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crane fly - large, long-legged fly of the family Tipulidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Tipula' (the type genus) + '-idae', a zoological suffix denoting a family.

2. NOUN

/ tɪˈpjuːlɪdiː / (AmE), / tɪˈpjuːlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of long-legged flies known as crane flies, typically resembling large mosquitoes and found worldwide.
a family of flies called crane flies

Examples

The Tipulidae are often mistaken for giant mosquitoes, but they do not bite humans.
Many species within the Tipulidae family play a crucial role in wetland ecosystems.
Entomologists collected specimens of Tipulidae during their spring field survey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crane fly - a common name for insects of the family Tipulidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Tipulidae, based on the genus Tipula, the type genus for this family.
