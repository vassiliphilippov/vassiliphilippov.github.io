Ceratopetalum gummiferum

1. NOUN

/ sɛˌrætəˌpɛtələm ˌɡʌmɪˈfɜrəm / (AmE), / sɛˌrætəˌpɛtələm ˌɡʌmɪˈfɜːrəm / (BrE)
an Australian tree or shrub with red flowers, often used in Christmas decorations
Australian plant with red flowers, used at Christmas

Examples

The Ceratopetalum gummiferum is commonly known as the New South Wales Christmas bush.
Florists use Ceratopetalum gummiferum branches to make festive holiday arrangements.
In Australia, Ceratopetalum gummiferum adds a bright splash of color to Christmas celebrations.

Etymology

From Greek keras (horn), petalon (petal), and Latin gummifer (gum-bearing); named for its botanical characteristics.
