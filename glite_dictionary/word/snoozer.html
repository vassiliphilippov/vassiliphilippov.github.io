snoozer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnuːzər / (AmE), / ˈsnuːzə / (BrE)
a lecture, event, or experience that is very boring
something boring or tedious
informal

Examples

The meeting turned out to be a total snoozer with no interesting updates.
Despite the hype, the film was a snoozer and failed to capture my attention.
Everyone agreed that last night's lecture was such a snoozer they struggled to stay awake.

Synset Examples

He called the lecture a snoozer and decided to skip it.
That game was a real snoozer; I expected more excitement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bore - a person or thing that is dull or uninteresting

Etymology

From "snooze" (to sleep lightly), suggesting something so dull it induces sleep.
