Haldea striatula

1. NOUN

/ hælˈdiːə straɪˈætjələ / (AmE), / hælˈdiːə striˈætjʊlə / (BrE)
A small reddish-grey snake native to eastern North America, sometimes classified in the genus Haldea.
small reddish-grey North American snake

Examples

The Haldea striatula can often be found under logs and leaf litter in forests.
Scientists disagree whether Haldea striatula should be placed in the Haldea or Storeria genus.
While not venomous, the Haldea striatula plays an important role in controlling insect populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown snake - A common name for Haldea striatula and related snakes

Etymology

Genus name from Greek; species name from Latin 'striatulus' meaning faintly striated.
