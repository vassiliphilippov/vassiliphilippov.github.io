aguacate

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːɡwəˈkɑːteɪ / (AmE), / ˌɑːɡwəˈkɑːteɪ / (BrE)
A pear-shaped tropical fruit with green or blackish skin and rich yellowish pulp surrounding a large seed, also known as an avocado.
pear-shaped tropical fruit; avocado

Examples

The chef used fresh aguacate to prepare a creamy guacamole.
In some markets, you may hear aguacate instead of avocado for the same fruit.
Ripe aguacate is a delicious addition to salads and sandwiches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avocado - A pear-shaped fruit with green or blackish skin and yellowish pulp; another name for aguacate

Etymology

From Spanish 'aguacate,' from Nahuatl 'āhuacatl' (avocado).
