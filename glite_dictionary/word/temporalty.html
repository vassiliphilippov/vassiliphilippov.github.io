temporalty

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpɚˌælti / (AmE), / tɛmˈpɒːlti / (BrE)
The worldly or secular possessions and property belonging to a religious institution, especially a church.
worldly possessions of a church
archaic

Examples

The bishop managed the temporalty of the diocese with great care.
Much of the monastery's temporalty was confiscated by the crown during the Reformation.
Laymen were often appointed to administer the temporalty belonging to church lands.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spirituality - the spiritual authority or possessions of the church

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *temporalitas* (secular possessions).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərəlti / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəlti / (BrE)
In Christianity, the lay members of a religious community who do not carry priestly or clerical responsibilities.
lay members of a religious community

Examples

The temporalty participated in the procession alongside the clergy.
It was the duty of the temporalty to support the church in matters outside spiritual affairs.
In medieval times, disputes sometimes arose between the clergy and the temporalty over land ownership.

Etymology

From Latin 'temporalitas' (secular possessions or state), ultimately from 'temporalis' (temporal).
