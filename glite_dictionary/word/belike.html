belike

1. ADV

/ bɪˈlaɪk / (AmE), / bɪˈlaɪk / (BrE)
With considerable certainty; probably; most likely.
probably; most likely (archaic)
archaic

Examples

He will belike arrive before sundown, as he always does.
The king, belike, never learned of the matter himself.
If the storm persists, our journey will belike be delayed.

Synset Examples

He is probably out of the country
in all likelihood we are headed for war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probably - likely to happen or be true; with some degree of certainty
  • Degree Lower: possibly - with a chance of happening, but less certain than 'probably'

Etymology

From Middle English, literally 'by like' (by likelihood).
