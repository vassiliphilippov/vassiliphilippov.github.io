gasbag

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæsˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈɡasˌbaɡ / (BrE)
the envelope or bag that contains gas in a balloon or airship, providing lift
the bag holding gas in a balloon/airship

Examples

If the gasbag develops a leak, the balloon could descend rapidly.
The Zeppelin's massive gasbag was filled with hydrogen.
Technicians carefully inspected the gasbag for any signs of wear before the flight.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæsˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈɡasˌbaɡ / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who talks a lot, especially about trivial or uninteresting topics
person who talks a lot, usually boringly
informal

Examples

Uncle Jim can be a real gasbag at family gatherings, going on about politics for hours.
Everyone tries to avoid sitting next to the office gasbag at lunch.
He didn’t want to come across as a gasbag during the meeting, so he kept his comments brief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windbag - a person who talks too much, especially in a boring way

Etymology

From 'gas' (talk in a trivial way) plus 'bag', figuratively referring to someone full of hot air.
