tempting

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛmptɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɛmptɪŋ / (BrE)
attractive or inviting, especially in a way that makes you want to have or do something, even if you should not
attractive; inviting; appealing

Examples

The smell of freshly baked bread was so tempting that I couldn't resist.
She found the offer from the rival company extremely tempting.
It's tempting to eat chocolate late at night, even though I know I shouldn't.

Synset Examples

a tantalizing aroma
a tempting repast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tantalizing - teasing or tormenting by making something desirable available but out of reach
  • Antonym: unappealing - not attractive or inviting

Etymology

From Latin 'temptare' (to handle, test, try), via Old French 'tempter'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɛmptɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɛmptɪŋ / (BrE)
attractive or appealing, making you want to have or do something, although you may not think it is right or wise
attractive or appealing; hard to resist

Examples

The chocolate cake looked tempting even though I was on a diet.
She received a tempting offer for her old car.
It's tempting to stay up late, but I have an early meeting tomorrow.

Synset Examples

an alluring prospect
her alluring smile
the voice was low and beguiling
difficult to say no to an enticing advertisement
a tempting invitation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alluring - powerfully attractive or charming
  • Synonym: enticing - attracting by arousing hope or desire; alluring
  • Antonym: unappealing - not attractive or inviting

Etymology

From Middle English tempten, from Old French tempter, from Latin temptare (to try, test).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02104760-s
Synset POS: s
