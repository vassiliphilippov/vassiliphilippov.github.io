Triostium

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈɑstiəm / (AmE), / traɪˈɒstiəm / (BrE)
A genus of herbs found in Asia and North America, commonly known as feverroot.
genus of Asiatic and North American herbs

Examples

Triostium is commonly called feverroot because of its traditional medicinal uses.
Botanists have identified several species within the Triostium genus in North American woodlands.
The leaves of Triostium were historically used to reduce fever.
