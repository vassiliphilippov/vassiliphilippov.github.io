Hindu

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪn.du / (AmE), / ˈhɪn.duː / (BrE)
A person who follows or practices Hinduism.
follower of Hinduism

Examples

Hindu temples can be found throughout India and many parts of the world.
She was raised as a Hindu and celebrates Diwali every year.
The festival attracted both Hindu and non-Hindu participants from various countries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Muslim - a person who follows Islam

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Hindū', referring to someone from the Indus region or a follower of Hinduism.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɪn.du / (AmE), / ˈhɪn.duː / (BrE)
of or relating to Hinduism or its followers.
relating to Hinduism or its followers

Examples

She wore traditional Hindu attire during the festival.
The Hindu temple in our town is open to everyone.
Many Hindu rituals involve elaborate ceremonies and music.

Synset Examples

the Hindu faith

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hinduism - the major world religion originating in India

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Sindhu' (river, especially the Indus), via Persian and Urdu 'Hindu' (referring to people of the Indus region).

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɪn.du / (AmE), / ˈhɪn.duː / (BrE)
(historical) A native or inhabitant of Hindustan or India, especially before the partition and in non-religious contexts.
native or inhabitant of Hindustan (India)

Examples

During the colonial era, many Hindus migrated to work in other parts of the British Empire.
She traced her ancestry to a long line of Hindus living near the Ganges.
Old census records classified residents of the region as Hindus regardless of their religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indian - a native or inhabitant of India

Etymology

From Persian 'Hindū', meaning a person from the land beyond the Indus River, ultimately from Sanskrit 'Sindhu' (Indus River).
