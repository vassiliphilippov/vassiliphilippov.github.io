naut mi

1. NOUN

/ nɔt maɪ / (AmE), / nɔːt maɪ / (BrE)
a former British unit of length, also known as a nautical mile, equal to 6,080 feet (1,853.184 meters), used primarily in maritime and aviation contexts
a former British nautical mile (6,080 ft)

Examples

The ship covered more than one naut mi every four minutes under full sail.
Early British charts measured distances in naut mi rather than in statute miles.
For international standards, the modern nautical mile has replaced the British naut mi.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: statute mile - a standard land mile equal to 5,280 feet (1,609.344 meters)
  • Synonym: nautical mile - a unit of length used in navigation, currently standardized as exactly 1,852 meters

Etymology

Short form of 'nautical mile', from Latin 'nauticus' (of ships) and English 'mile'.
