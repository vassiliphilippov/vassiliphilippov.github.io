stump

1. NOUN

/ stʌmp / (AmE), / stʌmp / (BrE)
a platform raised above the surrounding ground to give prominence to the speaker, especially in political contexts
raised speaker's platform, esp. political

Examples

The candidate climbed the stump and began addressing the cheering crowd.
Town squares often had a wooden stump for political speeches.
He was known for his passionate oratory from the stump.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platform - a raised level surface on which people or things can stand

Etymology

From the political practice in 19th-century America of speakers standing on tree stumps to address crowds.

/ stʌmp / (AmE), / stʌmp / (BrE)
to remove tree stumps from a piece of land, often to prepare it for cultivation or construction
remove tree stumps from land

Examples

It took several days to stump the entire property using heavy machinery.
After logging, they decided to stump the area to clear it for development.

3. NOUN

/ stʌmp / (AmE), / stʌmp / (BrE)
the part of a limb or tooth that remains after the rest has been removed
part left after removal of limb or tooth

Examples

After the surgery, the patient had difficulty adjusting to the stump of his leg.
The dentist explained that the stump of the tooth needed to be removed to avoid infection.
Care must be taken to clean the stump daily to prevent any complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: residue - what is left after a part is removed

Etymology

Probably from Middle Low German or Middle Dutch *stump(e)*.

4. NOUN

/ stʌmp / (AmE), / stʌmp / (BrE)
(in cricket) any of the three upright wooden posts which together with the bails form the wicket.
upright post forming wicket in cricket

Examples

The bowler aimed directly at the stump to try and get the batsman out.
After hitting the stump, the bails fell, signaling the batsman was bowled.
He set up the stumps before the start of the cricket match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wicket - set of three stumps and two bails used in cricket

Examples

8. VERB

/ stʌmp / (AmE), / stʌmp / (BrE)
to confuse or puzzle someone so that they are unable to answer or solve something
to puzzle or confound someone

Examples

The complex math question stumped all of the students in the class.
His unexpected response stumped the interviewer for a moment.
Some crossword clues can really stump even experienced solvers.

Synset Examples

This problem stumped her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baffle - to totally confuse or frustrate someone
  • Synonym: puzzle - to cause confusion in someone's mind
  • Antonym: to enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding

Etymology

Originally from Old English *stump* (tree trunk); figurative use for perplexing originates in the 19th century.
