omnivorousness

1. NOUN

/ ɑmˈnɪvɚəsnəs / (AmE), / ɒmˈnɪvərəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of eating both animal and plant matter.
the state of eating both plants and animals

Examples

The omnivorousness of brown bears allows them to thrive in a wide range of habitats.
Human omnivorousness has contributed to our ability to adapt to many different environments.
Some biologists study the omnivorousness of certain fish species to understand their ecological impact.

Synset Examples

They are not the only creatures that will resort to this, but it is certainly their omnivorousness that makes them such an amazing trash patrol.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: herbivorousness - the quality or state of eating only plant matter
  • Antonym: carnivorousness - the quality or state of eating only animal matter

Etymology

From Latin 'omnivorus' (all-eating) + '-ness'.
