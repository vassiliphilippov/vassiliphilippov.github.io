undoer

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈduːər / (AmE), / ʌnˈduːə / (BrE)
a person who undoes, unfastens, unwraps, or opens something
person who undoes or opens things

Examples

The magician was a skilled undoer of knots and locks.
As a notorious undoer of carefully wrapped gifts, she always ruined the surprise.
Children can be surprisingly talented undoers of shoe laces and ribbons.

Synset Examples

children are talented undoers of their shoelaces

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfastener - a person or thing that unfastens
  • Synonym: opener - a person or device that opens something

2. NOUN

/ ənˈduːər / (AmE), / ʌnˈduːə / (BrE)
a person, especially a seducer, who brings about the downfall or ruin of someone, often a woman.
someone who causes another's downfall, especially a seducer

Examples

In the old novel, the young woman's life was forever changed by her undoer.
Some stories warn of the undoer who entices with charm but leaves only sorrow.
She realized, too late, the true intentions of her so-called undoer.

Synset Examples

she awoke in the arms of her cruel undoer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruiner - a person who brings about ruin or destruction
  • Antonym: savior - a person who saves someone from harm or danger

Etymology

From 'undo' + '-er', meaning one who undoes or brings to ruin.

3. NOUN

/ ʌnˈduːər / (AmE), / ʌnˈduːə / (BrE)
a person or thing that destroys, ruins, or undoes something
someone who destroys or ruins things

Examples

His own impatience proved to be his undoer in the competition.
The villain in the novel acted as the undoer of the peaceful community.
Jealousy can be the undoer of even the strongest friendships.

Synset Examples

a destroyer of the environment
jealousy was his undoer
uprooters of gravestones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destroyer - a person or thing that destroys something
  • Antonym: creator - a person who makes or brings something into existence

Etymology

From 'undo' + '-er', where 'undo' is from Old English 'undōn' (to unfasten, reverse) and '-er' denotes an agent noun.
