disjuncture

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈdʒʌŋktʃər / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒʌŋktʃə / (BrE)
A separation or disconnection between two or more things; a state of being disconnected.
state of being disconnected

Examples

There is a clear disjuncture between what the company promises and what it delivers.
The report pointed out the disjuncture between theory and practice in the educational system.
Modern society often creates a disjuncture between personal values and public behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disjunction - the act or state of being disconnected or separated
  • Antonym: connection - the state of being connected

Etymology

From Latin 'disjunctus', past participle of 'disjungere' (to separate).
