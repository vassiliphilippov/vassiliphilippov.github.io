to troubleshoot

1. VERB

/ ˈtrʌbəlˌʃuːt / (AmE), / ˈtrʌbəlˌʃuːt / (BrE)
To identify and solve problems, especially of a technical or operational nature.
identify and solve problems

Examples

The technician was called in to troubleshoot the network issue before the deadline.
He spent the entire afternoon troubleshooting the printer until it finally worked.
Our IT department regularly troubleshoots problems with the office computers.

Synset Examples

He is known to be good at trouble-shooting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diagnose - to identify the cause of a problem
  • Synonym: repair - to fix something that is broken
  • Antonym: cause - the reason something happens (opposite of solution)

Etymology

From 'trouble' + 'shoot', originally from the practice of American railroad workers who repaired faults ('trouble') along lines, often called out to 'shoot' a trouble.

2. VERB

/ ˈtrʌbəlˌʃut / (AmE), / ˈtrʌbəlˌʃuːt / (BrE)
To analyze and solve problems, especially technical or mechanical faults.
analyze and solve problems

Examples

The technician was called in to troubleshoot the server issue right away.
If you can't connect to the internet, try troubleshooting your network settings first.
She often troubleshoots complex software problems for her colleagues.

Synset Examples

He is known to be good at trouble-shooting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diagnose - identify the nature of a problem or fault
  • Synonym: repair - fix something that is broken or malfunctioning

Etymology

Formed from 'trouble' + 'shoot', originally an engineering term from the early 20th century US.
