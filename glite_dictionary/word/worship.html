worship

1. VERB

/ ˈwɜːrʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈwɜːʃɪp / (BrE)
to take part in a religious ceremony, especially as part of organized religion
attend religious services

Examples

Every Sunday, the family worships at their local church.
Many people worship in different ways, some alone and others in large congregations.
They worship together during special religious holidays and festivals.

Synset Examples

They worship in the traditional manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pray - to speak to God or a deity, especially in order to give thanks or ask for help

Etymology

From Old English weorþscipe (worthiness, honor, respect).

WordNet ID: oewn-02619310-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈwɜːrʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈwɜːʃɪp / (BrE)
to show respect and devotion to a deity or religious figure, often through prayer or ritual
show devotion to a deity

Examples

Many communities worship the sun in religious ceremonies.
She worships at the temple every weekend to honor her faith.
They worshipped together during the festival, singing hymns and offering prayers.

Synset Examples

Many Hindus worship Shiva

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adore - to love and respect (someone or something) deeply
  • Antonym: to blaspheme - to speak irreverently about a deity or sacred things

Etymology

From Old English weorðscipe, meaning 'worthiness, respect, reverence'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01782907-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈwɜːʃɪp / (BrE)
the activity of showing reverence and adoration for a deity; religious devotion or ceremonies
religious devotion or ceremonies

Examples

The church holds regular worship services every Sunday morning.
For many, music is an important part of worship.
Freedom of worship is a fundamental human right.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adoration - deep love and respect, especially for a deity
  • Synonym: veneration - great respect or reverence, often in a religious context
  • Antonym: blasphemy - the act or offense of speaking sacrilegiously about God or sacred things

Etymology

From Old English weorþscipe (‘worthiness, respect, reverence’), from weorþ (‘worthy’) + -scipe (‘-ship’).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01030597-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈwɜːrʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈwɜːʃɪp / (BrE)
to love, admire, or respect someone or something greatly, often to an excessive or uncritical degree
admire or love greatly, often excessively

Examples

As a child, she used to worship her older brother and wanted to be just like him.
Some people worship celebrities as if they were gods.
He worships the ground his favorite teacher walks on.

Synset Examples

Many teenagers idolized the Beatles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to idolize - to admire or love someone very much or too much
  • Synonym: to adore - to love and respect someone deeply

Etymology

From Old English weorþscipe (worthiness, honor, respect).

5. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈwɜːʃɪp / (BrE)
a feeling of deep love, admiration, and respect, often towards a person or deity
deep love and admiration

Examples

His worship of the artist bordered on obsession.
The fans expressed their worship for the actor by cheering loudly at every appearance.
Her worship of her mentor was evident in the way she spoke about him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adoration - deep love and respect
  • Synonym: reverence - profound respect and awe
  • Antonym: disdain - the feeling of not respecting someone or something

Etymology

From Old English 'weorþscipe' (worthiness, honor, respect), from 'weorþ' (worthy) + 'scipe' (ship, condition).
