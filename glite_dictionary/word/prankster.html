prankster

1. NOUN

/ ˈpræŋk.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈpræŋk.stə / (BrE)
a person who plays practical jokes or tricks on others
person who plays practical jokes

Examples

The notorious prankster filled the teacher's desk with balloons as a harmless joke.
Everyone knew Mike was the office prankster because of his elaborate April Fools’ Day tricks.
After the fire alarm incident, the prankster finally admitted his involvement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joker - a person who jokes or plays tricks
  • Synonym: trickster - a person who deceives or plays tricks

Etymology

From 'prank' (joke or trick) + '-ster' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpræŋk.stər / (AmE), / ˈpræŋk.stə / (BrE)
A person who plays tricks or practical jokes on others for amusement.
person who plays practical jokes

Examples

The class prankster replaced the teacher’s pen with a disappearing ink one.
She has a reputation as a bit of a prankster at family gatherings.
Even in the office, there’s always one prankster who keeps everyone laughing.

Synset Examples

Everyone knew him as Scarlem, the prankster of the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joker - a person who jokes or makes fun
  • Synonym: trickster - a person who deceives or plays tricks

Etymology

From 'prank' (a trick or practical joke) + '-ster' (denoting a person associated with an activity).
