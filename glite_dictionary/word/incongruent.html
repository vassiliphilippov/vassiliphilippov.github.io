incongruent

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnˈkɑŋɡruənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnˈkɒŋɡruənt / (BrE)
not in agreement or harmony; not matching or congruent, especially in form, structure, or content.
not matching or not in agreement

Examples

The results of the two experiments were incongruent, raising questions about their validity.
His behavior was incongruent with his stated beliefs, making people doubt his sincerity.
In geometry, two shapes are incongruent if they cannot be superimposed exactly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: congruent - in agreement or harmony; matching in shape or size

Etymology

From Latin incongruens, incongruent- (not fitting), from in- (not) + congruens, present participle of congruere (to agree, to come together).
