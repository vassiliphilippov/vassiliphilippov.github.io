gudgeon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌdʒən / (AmE), / ˈɡʌdʒən / (BrE)
a small slender European freshwater fish (Gobio gobio) often used as bait by anglers
small European freshwater bait fish

Examples

The fisherman used a gudgeon as live bait to attract larger fish.
In many European rivers, the gudgeon is plentiful and easy to catch.
Children often try to catch gudgeons with simple rods near the riverbank.

Etymology

From Old French 'goujon', from Latin 'gobio' (gudgeon, a kind of fish), from Greek 'kōbios'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌdʒən / (AmE), / ˈɡʌdʒən / (BrE)
A small spiny-finned fish of coastal or brackish waters, with a large head, elongated tapering body, and ventral fins modified as a sucker.
small fish with sucker-like fins

Examples

While exploring the tidal pools, we spotted a gudgeon resting quietly on the rocks.
The gudgeon uses its sucker-like fins to cling to underwater surfaces in fast-flowing streams.
Fishermen often find the elusive gudgeon in the brackish waters near the river's mouth.

Etymology

From Old French *goujon*, from Latin *gobio* (gudgeon, fish).
