Primaxin

1. NOUN

/ praɪˈmæksɪn / (AmE), / praɪˈmæksɪn / (BrE)
A brand name for a combination antibiotic (imipenem/cilastatin) used to treat serious infections, typically administered by injection.
antibiotic drug, trade name
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Primaxin to treat the patient's severe bacterial infection.
After other antibiotics failed, she was given Primaxin intravenously.
Some hospitals reserve Primaxin for cases involving resistant bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imipenem - a broad-spectrum beta-lactam antibiotic, one component of Primaxin
  • Synonym: cilastatin - an inhibitor of renal dehydropeptidase, one component of Primaxin
