anthropometry

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθrəˈpɑːmətri / (AmE), / ˌænθrəˈpɒmɪtri / (BrE)
the scientific measurement and study of the human body and its parts and capacities
measurement of the human body
medical

Examples

The field of anthropometry plays a crucial role in designing ergonomic furniture for offices.
Researchers used anthropometry to collect data on the body shapes of athletes.
Advances in anthropometry have helped improve the fit and safety of protective equipment.

Etymology

From Greek anthrōpos (human) and metron (measure).
