justiciary

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈstɪʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / dʒʌˈstɪʃəri / (BrE)
(historical) A high judicial officer, especially in Scotland or England, with authority over legal matters; a judge having jurisdiction over justice.
historical high judicial officer
archaic

Examples

The ancient justiciary was responsible for maintaining order across the realm.
Records show that the Scottish justiciary held significant power during the Middle Ages.
He traced his lineage to a twelfth-century justiciary of England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judge - a public official who decides cases in a court of law

Etymology

From Medieval Latin justiciarius (judge), from Latin justitia (justice).

jurisdiction of a justiciar
legal archaic

Examples

Few records remain of the extent of his justiciary and its functioning.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'justiciarius' (a judge), related to justice.
