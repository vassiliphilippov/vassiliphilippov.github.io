isophene

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsəˌfiːn / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəˌfiːn / (BrE)
A line on a map connecting places where a specific biological event, such as plant flowering, occurs at the same time.
line marking simultaneous biological events

Examples

The researchers drew an isophene across the map to indicate where the cherry trees began to bloom simultaneously.
Climate change can cause shifts in the position of the isophene for various flowering plants each year.
Mapping the isophenes for different species helps ecologists understand patterns in plant development.

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) + 'phainein' (to show).
