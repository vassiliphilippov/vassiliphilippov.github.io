1 Maccabees

1. PROPN

/ wʌn ˈmækəˌbiz / (AmE), / wʌn ˈmækəˌbiːz / (BrE)
an Apocryphal book recounting the Jewish revolt against Seleucid rule, focusing on Judas Maccabaeus and events under Antiochus IV Epiphanes
an Apocryphal book about the Jewish revolt

Examples

In Bible study, we discussed the historical significance of 1 Maccabees for Jewish resistance.
Many scholars analyze 1 Maccabees to understand the origins of Hanukkah.
The narrative in 1 Maccabees focuses on the leadership of Judas Maccabaeus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: 2 Maccabees - another Apocryphal book about the Maccabean revolt

Etymology

From Latin 'Machabaeorum liber primus', after Judas Maccabaeus, the book's central figure.
