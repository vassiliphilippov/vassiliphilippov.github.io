sniff

1. NOUN

/ snɪf / (AmE), / snɪf / (BrE)
an act of breathing in through the nose, especially to detect a smell
an act of inhaling through the nose

Examples

He took a quick sniff of the milk to check if it was sour.
With a loud sniff, she tried to clear her blocked nose.
The dog gave the stranger a cautious sniff before wagging its tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sniffle - an act or sound of sniffing repeatedly, usually because of a runny nose

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made when drawing air through the nose.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00884817-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ snɪf / (AmE), / snɪf / (BrE)
to perceive a smell by drawing air through the nose, typically audibly or with effort
inhale through the nose to smell

Examples

He paused to sniff the fresh bread before taking a bite.
The dog began to sniff around the door, searching for the source of the scent.
She leaned over to sniff the flowers in the vase.

Synset Examples

sniff the perfume

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smell - to perceive an odor by means of the nose

Etymology

Imitative of the sound of inhaling sharply through the nose; first recorded in the 14th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02129497-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ snɪf / (AmE), / snɪf / (BrE)
to inhale air audibly through the nose, especially as a result of cold, emotion, or to detect a smell
inhale audibly through the nose

Examples

She tried to sniff quietly during the movie, but her cold made it difficult.
The dog began to sniff around the kitchen looking for scraps.
He would often sniff loudly when he was upset or about to cry.

Synset Examples

the sick student was sniffling in the back row

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sniffle - to repeatedly sniff, especially due to a cold
  • Degree Higher: snort - to forcefully expel air through the nose, often making a harsh sound

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of sniffing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00007549-v
Synset POS: v
