devilment

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəlmənt / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlmənt / (BrE)
reckless or mischievous behavior intended to cause discomfort or annoyance to others
reckless mischievous behavior

Examples

His childish devilment constantly got him into trouble at school.
There was a look of devilment in her eyes as she planned the prank.
The party was filled with laughter and a hint of devilment among friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischief - playful misbehavior or troublemaking
  • Synonym: mischievousness - the quality of causing or showing a fondness for causing trouble in a playful way
  • Antonym: goodness - the quality of being morally good or virtuous

Etymology

Formed from 'devil' + '-ment', indicating the state or result of being devilish.
