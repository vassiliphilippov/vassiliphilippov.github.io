conceited

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsitɪd / (AmE), / kənˈsiːtɪd / (BrE)
Having an excessively high opinion of oneself; arrogantly proud.
excessively self-important or arrogant

Examples

Despite his accomplishments, Mark never became conceited or boastful.
Everyone found her conceited attitude off-putting at the party.
The conceited actor expected special treatment wherever he went.

Synset Examples

a conceited fool
an attitude of self-conceited arrogance
an egotistical disregard of others
so swollen by victory that he was unfit for normal duty
growing ever more swollen-headed and arbitrary
vain about her clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vain - having or showing an excessively high opinion of one's appearance, abilities, or worth
  • Synonym: arrogant - having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities
  • Antonym: modest - having a humble estimate of one's own importance

Etymology

From conceit + -ed, meaning 'filled with conceit' (pride).
