to resorb

1. VERB

/ rɪˈzɔrb / (AmE), / rɪˈzɔːb / (BrE)
(of a cell, tissue, or structure) to absorb (something) again or to undergo absorption, especially in a biological or medical context.
to absorb again (biological/medical)
medical

Examples

The body can resorb a small blood clot without medical intervention.
As the bone heals, it will gradually resorb the graft material.
Some plants can resorb nutrients from their leaves before they fall off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absorb - to take in or soak up (liquid, energy, etc.)
  • Antonym: to secrete - to produce and discharge (a substance)

Etymology

From Latin 'resorbere', formed from 're-' (again) + 'sorbere' (to suck in).
