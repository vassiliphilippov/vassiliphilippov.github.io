Halichoeres

1. NOUN

/ hælɪˈkɔːrˌiːz / (AmE), / hælɪˈkɔːəriːz / (BrE)
a genus of fish in the wrasse family (Labridae), containing about 81 species found in tropical and subtropical marine waters
genus of wrasses (marine fish)

Examples

The coral reefs are home to several species of Halichoeres.
Scientists discovered a new member of the Halichoeres genus in Indonesian waters.
Juveniles of Halichoeres often display striking color patterns that change as they mature.

Etymology

From Greek 'halios' (of the sea) + 'choiros' (pig), referring to the fish's snout.
