brutalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌbruːtəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbruːtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of treating someone with extreme cruelty or violence
treating someone with extreme cruelty

Examples

The brutalisation of prisoners during the conflict shocked the international community.
Exposure to constant violence can lead to the brutalisation of children.
The documentary explored the brutalisation of society during wartime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brutality - extreme cruelty or savagery

Etymology

From 'brutal' (from Latin 'brutalis', meaning animal-like or savage) + '-isation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbruːtələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbruːtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the condition or process of being treated in a cruel, violent, or savage manner
the state of being treated with cruelty or violence

Examples

The brutalisation of civilians during the conflict shocked the international community.
Years of exposure to violence resulted in the brutalisation of the prison population.
Some critics argue that media can contribute to the brutalisation of society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brutality - cruel and violent treatment

Etymology

From 'brutal' + '-isation', based on the Latin 'brutalis' (bestial), via French.

3. NOUN

/ ˌbruːtəlʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbruːtələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making someone cruel or insensitive, often by exposing them to violence or harsh conditions
process of making someone cruel

Examples

Some critics argue that the brutalisation of society can be traced to exposure to violent media.
Decades of war have led to the brutalisation of entire communities.
The film powerfully depicts the brutalisation experienced by children in abusive homes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dehumanization - the process of depriving a person or group of positive human qualities

Etymology

From brutal + -isation, patterned after French 'brutalisation'.
