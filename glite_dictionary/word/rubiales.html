Rubiales

1. NOUN

/ ruːˈbaɪəˌliːz / (AmE), / ruːˈbaɪəliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants in the subclass Asteridae, characterized by opposite leaves and an inferior compound ovary; includes families such as Rubiaceae.
order of flowering plants in Asteridae

Examples

The family Rubiaceae is part of the botanical order Rubiales.
Recent studies have led some botanists to reclassify certain members traditionally placed in Rubiales.
Rubiales includes plants with opposite leaves and inferior ovaries, making it distinct from related orders.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the plant family Rubiaceae, which is included in this order.

2. NOUN

/ ruːˈbaɪəˌliːz / (AmE), / ruːˈbaɪəˌliːz / (BrE)
An order of dicotyledonous flowering plants in the subclass Asteridae, characterized by opposite leaves and an inferior compound ovary; now largely obsolete in modern taxonomy.
an order of flowering plants (obsolete taxonomy)

Examples

The family Rubiaceae was once classified within the order Rubiales.
Botanists have restructured the classification and many plants formerly in Rubiales are now placed elsewhere.
The order Rubiales included groups such as madder and bedstraw before the taxonomic revision.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Rubiales', deriving from 'Rubia', a genus in the order, plus the taxonomic suffix '-ales'.
