Treponemataceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrɛpəˌniːməˈteɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˌtrɛpəˌniːməˈtasɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of small spirochete bacteria, some of which are parasitic or cause diseases in humans and animals
family of spirochete bacteria
medical

Examples

The bacterial family Treponemataceae includes organisms responsible for diseases like syphilis and yaws.
Researchers studied the genetic diversity within the Treponemataceae to understand their pathogenicity.
Certain species in the Treponemataceae family are known to be harmful to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spirochete - type of bacteria with a spiral shape

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Treponema and family suffix -aceae.
