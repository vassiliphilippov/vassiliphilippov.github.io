Geraint

1. PROPN

/ dʒəˈraɪnt / (AmE), / ɡɛˈraɪnt / (BrE)
A knight of the Round Table in Arthurian legend, known for his bravery and loyalty.
Arthurian knight of the Round Table

Examples

Geraint was renowned for his prowess in battle and his unwavering loyalty to King Arthur.
The tale of Geraint and Enid remains one of the most celebrated romances of Arthurian legend.
Many scholars have written about Geraint's role among the knights of the Round Table.

Etymology

From Welsh personal name 'Geraint', possibly from Latin 'Gerontius'.
