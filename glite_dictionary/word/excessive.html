excessive

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsɛsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛsɪv / (BrE)
more than is necessary, reasonable, or acceptable; too much in amount or degree
more than is reasonable or necessary

Examples

The company was criticized for its excessive price increases this year.
He used an excessive amount of salt in the recipe, ruining the flavor.
Parents are worried about their children's excessive screen time on phones and tablets.

Synset Examples

excessive charges
a book of inordinate length
his dress stops just short of undue elegance
unreasonable demands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inordinate - unusually or disproportionately large; excessive
  • Synonym: undue - more than is appropriate or warranted; excessive
  • Synonym: unreasonable - not guided by or based on good sense; excessive
  • Antonym: moderate - kept within reasonable or proper limits; not excessive

Etymology

From Latin 'excessivus', from 'excedere' (to go beyond).

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsɛsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛsɪv / (BrE)
greater than what seems reasonable or necessary; unrestrained, especially with regard to feelings or actions.
greater than reasonable or necessary

Examples

His excessive enthusiasm sometimes makes others uncomfortable.
The report criticized the company for its excessive use of resources.
She found his praise a bit excessive and insincere.

Synset Examples

extravagant praise
exuberant compliments
overweening ambition
overweening greed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: moderate - kept within reasonable or proper limits
  • Synonym: extravagant - lacking restraint in spending or behavior
  • Synonym: exaggerated - represented as greater than is true or reasonable

Etymology

From Latin 'excessivus', from 'excedere' (to exceed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02008064-s
Synset POS: s
