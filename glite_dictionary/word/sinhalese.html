Sinhalese

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɪnhəˈliz / (AmE), / ˌsɪn(h)əˈliːz / (BrE)
relating to the Sinhalese people or their culture, language, or history
of the Sinhalese people or culture

Examples

Traditional Sinhalese music is often played during Buddhist festivals.
Many Sinhalese customs are influenced by centuries of history and tradition.
She specializes in Sinhalese art and teaches it at the university.

Synset Examples

The Sinhalese are the main ethnic group of Sri Lanka

Etymology

From Sanskrit Sinhala (lion) + -ese (denoting language or people).

of or relating to the Sinhalese language or dialects.
relating to the Sinhalese language

Examples

The film included subtitles in both English and Sinhalese.

  • Synonym: Sinhala - language spoken by the Sinhalese people

3. NOUN

/ sɪn.həˈliz / (AmE), / sɪn.həˈliːz / (BrE)
a member of the largest ethnic group native to Sri Lanka, known for their own language and culture
a person from the largest ethnic group of Sri Lanka

Examples

The Sinhalese have a rich tradition of dance and music.
Many Sinhalese people speak both Sinhala and English fluently.
The capital city, Colombo, is home to a large Sinhalese community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Tamil - a member of the other major ethnic group native to Sri Lanka and southern India

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Siṃhala' (Sri Lanka) + English '-ese' (denoting origin).

Source: WordNet

Indic language of Sri Lanka

Examples

She is fluent in Sinhalese as well as English.
Many books in Sri Lanka are published in Sinhalese and Tamil.

Related Terms
