nostrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑstrəm / (AmE), / ˈnɒstrəm / (BrE)
A medicine or remedy, especially one that is not considered effective or whose ingredients are not disclosed; a hypothetical cure for all diseases.
ineffective or secret remedy; cure-all

Examples

Many consumers wasted money on the nostrum that claimed to cure all ailments.
During the 19th century, traveling salesmen often sold a nostrum with dubious medical benefits.
Some politicians offer a simple nostrum for complex social issues, but such solutions rarely work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panacea - a solution or remedy for all problems or diseases

Etymology

From Latin 'nostrum' ('our'), short for 'remedium nostrum' ('our remedy').

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːstrəm / (AmE), / ˈnɒstrəm / (BrE)
A medicine, especially one that is not considered effective, that is prepared by an unqualified person and sold with dubious claims.
ineffective or questionable medicine

Examples

The company sold a nostrum promising to cure everything from headaches to heart disease.
Many patent medicines in the 19th century turned out to be nothing more than a nostrum.
He became wealthy by marketing his signature nostrum, despite lacking any medical training.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: panacea - a remedy for all diseases or difficulties
  • Synonym: quack remedy - an unproven or fraudulent medicine

Etymology

From Latin 'nostrum' meaning 'ours', short for 'remedium nostrum' (our remedy).
