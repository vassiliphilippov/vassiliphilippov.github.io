New Brunswick

1. PROPN

/ nuː ˈbrʌnzwɪk / (AmE), / njuː ˈbrʌnzwɪk / (BrE)
a province in southeastern Canada, located on the Atlantic coast and bordered by Quebec, Nova Scotia, and the U.S. state of Maine.
province in southeastern Canada

Examples

She moved to New Brunswick to attend university.
The forests of New Brunswick are home to many wildlife species.
Tourists often visit New Brunswick for its beautiful coastline and seafood.

Etymology

Named after the British royal house of Brunswick-Lüneburg.

2. PROPN

/ nuː ˈbrʌnzwɪk / (AmE), / njuː ˈbrʌnzwɪk / (BrE)
A city in central New Jersey, United States, known for being a university town and a major center for healthcare and pharmaceuticals.
city in central New Jersey, USA

Examples

He moved to New Brunswick to attend Rutgers University.
New Brunswick is often called the Hub City because of its central location and significance.
Many major healthcare companies have headquarters in New Brunswick.

Etymology

Named after the city of Brunswick in Germany, from 'Braunschweig'.
