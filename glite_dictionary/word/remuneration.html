remuneration

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌmjunəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌmjuːnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
money paid for work or services; compensation or reward given to someone for their efforts or losses.
money paid for work or services

Examples

Most employees expect fair remuneration for their contributions to the company.
The package includes not just salary, but also additional remuneration such as bonuses and benefits.
There was disagreement over the amount of remuneration due after the contract was completed.

Synset Examples

adequate remuneration for his work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensation - money received as payment for work or as recompense for loss
  • Synonym: salary - fixed regular payment for employment
  • Antonym: deduction - an amount subtracted from earnings; not paid to the worker

Etymology

From Latin 'remuneratio' (reward, recompense), from 'remunerari' (to reward).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˌmjunəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌmjuːnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
money or other compensation paid for work or services; payment or reward for employment or services rendered.
payment or reward for work

Examples

The company offered a competitive remuneration package to attract the best candidates.
Employees are satisfied with the remuneration they receive for their hard work.
Changes in legislation affected the minimum remuneration employers were required to pay.

Synset Examples

wages were paid by cash
he wasted his pay on drink
they saved a quarter of all their earnings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salary - fixed regular payment, typically paid monthly, for employment
  • Synonym: wages - regular payment for work, typically paid hourly or weekly
  • Synonym: compensation - something, typically money, awarded to someone in recognition of loss, suffering, or work

Etymology

From Latin 'remuneratio', from 'remunerari' (to reward).
