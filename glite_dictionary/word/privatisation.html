privatisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpraɪvətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɪvətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of transferring an industry, service, or asset from government ownership or control to private ownership or control
transfer from public to private ownership

Examples

The government announced the privatisation of the railways last year.
Opponents argue that privatisation can lead to job losses and reduced public services.
After privatisation, the company was able to raise much-needed capital from private investors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nationalisation - the transfer of private assets into public ownership

Etymology

From 'private' plus '-isation', modeled after French 'privatisation'.
