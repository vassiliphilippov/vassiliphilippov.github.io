raster

1. NOUN

/ ˈræstər / (AmE), / ˈrɑːstə / (BrE)
a rectangular pattern of parallel lines, especially those that guide the electron beam on a television screen or computer monitor
rectangular pattern of scanning lines on screens

Examples

On old televisions, the raster was sometimes visible as faint horizontal lines on the screen.
The graphics card controls how the raster is drawn to display images smoothly.
Modern displays use LED technology but still organize light into a raster pattern for image rendering.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vector - a graphical representation using lines and curves defined mathematically rather than a grid of pixels

Etymology

From German 'Raster' (a grid), from Latin 'rastrum' (rake).
