to coact

1. VERB

/ koʊˈækt / (AmE), / kəʊˈækt / (BrE)
(of people or organisms) to act together; to cooperate in action or effect.
act together; cooperate

Examples

In certain ecosystems, different species coact to maintain environmental balance.
The two scientists coacted on a series of experiments investigating climate change.
For the project to succeed, all departments must coact efficiently and share information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cooperate - to work or act together for a common purpose or benefit

Etymology

From Latin coactare, frequentative of cogere (to drive together).
