Ornithischia

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˌnɪˈθɪskiə / (AmE), / ɔːˌnɪˈθɪskɪə / (BrE)
A major group of extinct herbivorous dinosaurs characterized by a bird-like pelvic structure, including armored, horned, and duck-billed dinosaurs.
group of bird-hipped extinct dinosaurs

Examples

The Ornithischia included many well-known dinosaurs, such as Stegosaurus and Triceratops.
Scientists have studied the pelvic structure unique to Ornithischia to trace their evolutionary history.
Fossils of Ornithischia are often found alongside evidence of rich prehistoric plant life.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'ornis, ornithos' (bird) + 'ischion' (hip joint).

2. NOUN

/ ɔːrnɪˈθɪskiə / (AmE), / ɔːnɪˈθɪskɪə / (BrE)
an order of extinct terrestrial dinosaurs characterized by a bird-like pelvis, including armored, boneheaded, horned, and duck-billed dinosaurs.
order of herbivorous dinosaurs with bird-like pelvis

Examples

The Ornithischia included diverse dinosaurs such as Triceratops and Stegosaurus.
Unlike the Saurischia, the Ornithischia had a pelvis more similar to that of birds.
Many recent discoveries have provided new insights into the evolution of Ornithischia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Saurischia - order of dinosaurs with lizard-like hips

Etymology

From Greek 'ornith-' (bird) + 'ischion' (hip joint), referring to the bird-like shape of the pelvis.
