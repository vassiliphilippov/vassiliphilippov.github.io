wicked

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪkɪd / (AmE), / ˈwɪkɪd / (BrE)
Morally wrong and deliberately committing evil or unrighteous acts.
morally wrong; evil

Examples

The judge sentenced the wicked criminal to life in prison for his crimes.
She believed that only a truly wicked person could commit such acts of cruelty.
Throughout the novel, the wicked queen is feared for her ruthless deeds.

Synset Examples

a sinful person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evil - profoundly immoral or wicked
  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Old English wicced (wicked, evil), from wiccian (to practice sorcery).
