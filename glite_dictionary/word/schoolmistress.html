schoolmistress

1. NOUN

/ ˈskuːlˌmɪstrəs / (AmE), / ˈskuːlˌmɪstrɪs / (BrE)
A female schoolteacher, especially one who is regarded as strict or authoritative.
female (often strict) schoolteacher

Examples

The new schoolmistress was known for enforcing strict discipline in her classroom.
In Victorian England, a schoolmistress often lived on the premises of the school.
The schoolmistress assigned extra homework to students who were late.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: schoolmaster - a male schoolteacher, often strict

Etymology

From 'school' + 'mistress', with 'mistress' from Old French 'maistresse', feminine of 'maistre' (master).
