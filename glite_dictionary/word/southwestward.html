southwestward

1. ADV

/ ˌsaʊθˈwɛstwərd / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθˈwɛstwəd / (BrE)
toward or in the direction of the southwest
in a direction toward the southwest

Examples

The birds began to fly southwestward as autumn approached.
The storm moved southwestward across the countryside, bringing heavy rain.
They traveled southwestward for two days before reaching the city.

2. ADV

/ ˌsaʊθˈwɛstwərd / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθˈwɛstwəd / (BrE)
toward or in the direction of the southwest.
toward the southwest

Examples

The birds migrate southwestward every autumn to escape the colder weather.
A southwestward wind pushed the sailboats across the lake.
The road curves southwestward through the rolling hills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: southwest - the direction midway between south and west
  • Antonym: northeastward - toward or in the direction of the northeast

Etymology

From 'southwest' + 'ward' (directional suffix).

3. NOUN

/ ˌsaʊθˈwɛstwərd / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθˈwɛstwəd / (BrE)
the direction or point on the compass that is midway between south and west, corresponding to 225 degrees
direction between south and west (225°)

Examples

The hikers continued in a southwestward direction to reach the campsite before nightfall.
A steady southwestward breeze cooled the city all afternoon.
The storm was moving southwestward across the region, bringing heavy rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: southwest - the direction halfway between south and west, or 225 degrees on a compass

Etymology

From 'southwest' (direction) and '-ward' (directional suffix), indicating 'toward the southwest'.
