Sirenidae

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈrɛnɪdiː / (AmE), / saɪˈrɛnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic, eel-like salamanders, commonly known as sirens, found mainly in North America.
family of aquatic salamanders (sirens)

Examples

The Sirenidae family includes species with reduced limbs and external gills.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Sirenidae in southeastern wetlands.
Members of Sirenidae are unique among amphibians for their eel-like appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sirens - common name for amphibians of the Sirenidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Sirena', the type genus, after the legendary sirens.
