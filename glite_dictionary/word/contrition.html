contrition

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtrɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˈtrɪʃən / (BrE)
deep regret and sorrow for having committed a wrongdoing, especially in a religious sense
deep remorse for wrongdoing

Examples

He expressed genuine contrition for his past mistakes during the hearing.
The priest spoke about the importance of contrition and forgiveness in his sermon.
After realizing the harm he had caused, she felt a deep sense of contrition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remorse - a feeling of deep regret for a wrong committed
  • Antonym: impenitence - lack of regret or remorse for one's wrongdoing

Etymology

From Latin contritio (grinding down, bruising, remorse), from conterere (to grind down).
