I Corinthians

1. PROPN

/ ˌfɜrst kəˈrɪnθiənz / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst kəˈrɪnθiənz / (BrE)
the first book of Corinthians; a book of the New Testament containing the first epistle from Saint Paul to the church at Corinth
a New Testament book; first epistle to the Corinthians

Examples

Many Christians find comfort in passages from I Corinthians.
The verse about love in I Corinthians 13 is often read at weddings.
She referenced I Corinthians in her sermon about unity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: II Corinthians - the second epistle to the Corinthians in the New Testament

Etymology

From Latin Corinthus, the name of the Greek city, and the Roman numeral I indicating the first epistle.
