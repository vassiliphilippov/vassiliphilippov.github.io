nutgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌtˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈnʌtˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a widely distributed perennial sedge with small, edible nutlike tubers, often considered a weed
a sedge with edible tubers, often a weed

Examples

The farmer struggled to remove nutgrass from his fields each spring.
Some wild animals feed on the tubers of nutgrass for nutrition.
Gardeners often mistake nutgrass for regular grass due to its similar appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedge - a grass-like plant of the family Cyperaceae
