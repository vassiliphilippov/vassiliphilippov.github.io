videodisc

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪdioʊˌdɪsk / (AmE), / ˈvɪdɪəʊˌdɪsk / (BrE)
an optical disc that stores video recordings, such as movies, which can be played on a computer or television
optical disc for video recordings

Examples

He watched the documentary on a videodisc because the streaming service was unavailable.
Classic films are sometimes preserved on videodiscs in university libraries.
The teacher used a videodisc to show educational material to the class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DVD - a type of digital videodisc for storing high-capacity data including movies

Etymology

Formed from 'video' (from Latin 'videre', to see) and 'disc' (from Latin 'discus').
