confection

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / kənˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
a sweet food, especially one rich in sugar such as candy or a pastry
sweet food rich in sugar

Examples

The bakery offers an array of brightly colored confections in the display case.
She couldn't resist sampling every chocolate confection at the festival.
This delicate fruit tart is considered a classic French confection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet - a small piece of candy or other sweet food
  • Synonym: candy - a sweet food made with sugar or syrup
  • Synonym: pastry - a baked good made from dough and often sweetened

Etymology

From Latin 'confectio' (making, preparing), from 'conficere' (to make, prepare).

2. VERB

/ kənˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / kənˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
to make into a confection; to prepare or mix (especially in the context of medicine or sweets)
make into a confection

Examples

The pharmacist will confection the remedy according to the traditional recipe.
Artisans confection elaborate sweets for the holiday festival.
Some herbalists still confection medicinal syrups by hand.

Synset Examples

This medicine is home-confected

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prepare - to make something ready for use or consideration
  • Synonym: mix - to combine or blend ingredients

Etymology

From Latin 'confectio', from 'conficere' (to make, prepare).

3. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / kənˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of preparing something by mixing a variety of ingredients, especially in making medicines, drinks, or soups
the act of mixing ingredients

Examples

The careful confection of herbal remedies was once a respected skill among apothecaries.
His duties included the confection of traditional soups for the royal kitchen.
In medieval times, the confection of complex medicines required expertise and access to rare ingredients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compounding - the act of mixing or combining ingredients, especially in pharmacy

Etymology

From Latin 'confectio' (a putting together, making up), from 'conficere' (to prepare, make, accomplish).
