Ephestia

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛstiə / (AmE), / ɪˈfiːstiə / (BrE)
A genus of small moths whose larvae spin silky tunnels and feed on stored food products, often considered pests.
genus of moths that infest stored food

Examples

The larvae of Ephestia are notorious for contaminating stored grains.
Food warehouses must take measures to prevent Ephestia infestations.
We found evidence of Ephestia in the damaged flour sacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moth - an insect related to the butterfly, typically nocturnal

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'Ephesia', referring to Ephesus, an ancient city in Asia Minor.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛstiə / (AmE), / ɛˈfɛstɪə / (BrE)
A genus of small moths whose larvae spin silken tunnels and feed on stored food products, commonly considered pests.
genus of small moths pestering stored food

Examples

The warehouse was infested with Ephestia larvae, contaminating much of the stored grain.
Proper sealing of food storage can help prevent an outbreak of Ephestia moths.
Scientists identified the pest as belonging to the genus Ephestia after examining the larvae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moth - an insect related to butterflies, typically nocturnal

Etymology

From New Latin, named after Ephestia, a figure from Greek mythology.
