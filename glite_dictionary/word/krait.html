krait

1. NOUN

/ kraɪt / (AmE), / kraɪt / (BrE)
a brightly colored, venomous but typically nonaggressive snake found in southeastern Asia and the Malay peninsula, belonging to the genus Bungarus.
venomous Asian snake

Examples

The krait is known for its potent venom but rarely attacks humans unless provoked.
While hiking in Thailand, we spotted a colorful krait near the edge of a rice field.
Despite its fearsome reputation, the krait is usually not aggressive towards people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobra - a highly venomous snake found in Asia and Africa, known for its hood

Etymology

From Hindi 'karait', referring to this group of snakes.
