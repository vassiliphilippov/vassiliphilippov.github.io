hominid

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːmɪnɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɒmɪnɪd / (BrE)
a member of the biological family Hominidae, which includes humans, great apes, and their ancestors
member of the Hominidae family (humans and great apes)

Examples

Some scientists study ancient hominid fossils to learn about human evolution.
The gorilla is considered a hominid, along with orangutans and chimpanzees.
Recent discoveries have revealed new species of extinct hominids in Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primate - an order of mammals including humans, apes, and monkeys

Etymology

From Latin *homin-, homo* (man, human being) + -id (used in taxonomy).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɑː.mə.nɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɒ.mɪ.nɪd / (BrE)
characteristic of or relating to the family Hominidae, which includes humans and their extinct relatives.
relating to the family Hominidae

Examples

The fossil remains display several hominid characteristics, such as upright posture and reduced canine teeth.
Recent discoveries have provided new insight into hominid evolution during the Pleistocene era.
Scientists debated whether the jawbone belonged to a modern human or a more ancient hominid species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hominine - relating to the subfamily Homininae, which includes humans and their closest relatives

Etymology

From Latin 'homo' (man) + '-id' (resembling), used in 20th-century taxonomy.
