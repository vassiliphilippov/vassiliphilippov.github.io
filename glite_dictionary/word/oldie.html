oldie

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊldi / (AmE), / ˈəʊldi / (BrE)
a song that was popular in the past, especially one considered a classic or nostalgic
a formerly popular song

Examples

The radio station only plays oldies from the 60s and 70s.
Every Saturday night, my parents dance to their favorite oldie.
He requested an oldie at the party, and everyone started singing along.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classic - a song widely recognized as outstanding of its kind and of lasting worth
  • Synonym: hit - a song that was very successful and popular when released

Etymology

From 'old' with the diminutive suffix '-ie', used to denote something from the past with affection or nostalgia.
