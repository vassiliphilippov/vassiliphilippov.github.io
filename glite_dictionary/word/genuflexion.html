genuflexion

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn.juˈflɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒen.juˈflek.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of bending one or both knees as a sign of worship or deep respect
kneeling in worship or respect

Examples

The congregation performed a genuflexion before the altar as a sign of reverence.
With a deep genuflexion, the knight honored his monarch.
Traditional Catholic services often include the ritual of genuflexion when entering the pews.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genuflection - the act of bending the knee in worship or respect

Etymology

From Latin genuflexio (bending of the knee), from genu (knee) + flectere (to bend).
