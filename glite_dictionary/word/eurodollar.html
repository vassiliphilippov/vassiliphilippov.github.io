Eurodollar

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊroʊˌdɑlər / (AmE), / ˈjʊərəʊˌdɒlə / (BrE)
A United States dollar deposited in a bank outside the United States, especially in Europe, and used as an international currency to finance trade and investment.
US dollar held in banks outside the US, mainly Europe

Examples

The bank invested heavily in Eurodollars to take advantage of favorable interest rates.
Many multinational companies use Eurodollar accounts to facilitate international trade transactions.
Changes in US monetary policy can significantly impact the Eurodollar market.

Etymology

Formed from 'Euro-' (referring to Europe) + 'dollar', first used in financial contexts in the mid-20th century.
