Chiococca alba

1. NOUN

/ ki.oʊˈkɑː.kə ˈæl.bə / (AmE), / ki.əˈkɒk.ə ˈæl.bə / (BrE)
An evergreen climbing shrub native to southern Florida and the West Indies, grown for its clusters of fragrant white to creamy flowers followed by round, white, succulent berries.
evergreen climbing shrub with white berries

Examples

The Chiococca alba in my backyard climbs along the fence and produces small, fragrant white flowers.
Gardeners in subtropical regions often plant Chiococca alba for its attractive white berries.
Wildlife in southern Florida feed on the succulent fruit of Chiococca alba during the summer months.

Etymology

From Latin: 'chiococca' (snowberry), from Greek 'khiōn' (snow) and 'kokkos' (berry); 'alba' meaning 'white' in Latin.
