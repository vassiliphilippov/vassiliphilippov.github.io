Penstemon cyananthus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnstɪmən saɪˈænənˌθəs / (AmE), / ˌpɛnstɪmən sʌɪˈanənθəs / (BrE)
a species of erect perennial plant with blue-violet flowers arranged in rings near the stem tips, native to Idaho, Utah, and Wyoming
a North American plant with blue-violet flowers

Examples

The wild slopes of Wyoming were dotted with blooming Penstemon cyananthus in early summer.
Gardeners value Penstemon cyananthus for its showy blue-violet flowers and upright habit.
We spotted a cluster of Penstemon cyananthus growing along the Idaho hiking trail.

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name 'Penstemon' plus species Latin 'cyananthus' (blue-flowered), from Greek 'kyanos' (blue) and 'anthos' (flower).
