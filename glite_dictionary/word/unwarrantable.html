unwarrantable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɔɹəntəbl̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɒɹəntəbl / (BrE)
(of an action or statement) not able to be justified, defended, or explained; lacking justification or authority.
not able to be justified or defended

Examples

Such unwarrantable interference in personal affairs was met with outrage.
The company's actions were considered unwarrantable by the regulatory board.
Her accusations were found to be completely unwarrantable after a thorough investigation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: justifiable - able to be justified; defensible
  • Synonym: unjustifiable - impossible to justify; not defensible

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'warrantable' (able to be justified); 'warrantable' from Middle French 'garantir' (to guarantee).
