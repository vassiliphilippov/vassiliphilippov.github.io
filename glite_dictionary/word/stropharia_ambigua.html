Stropharia ambigua

1. NOUN

/ stroʊˈfɛəriə æmˈbɪɡjuə / (AmE), / strəʊˈfɛəriə æmˈbɪɡjuə / (BrE)
A species of gilled mushroom distinguished by a long stalk and a slimy yellow cap with fragments of a broken veil; its gills turn from white to dark brown as it matures.
a species of gilled mushroom with a slimy yellow cap

Examples

The bright yellow, sticky cap of the Stropharia ambigua makes it easy to distinguish from other mushrooms in the forest.
When foraging in damp woods, she spotted a cluster of Stropharia ambigua growing among the fallen leaves.
The gills of Stropharia ambigua transition from white to dark brown as the spores mature and are released.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Stropharia' (Greek strophos, band or belt), and species name 'ambigua' (Latin for doubtful or ambiguous).
