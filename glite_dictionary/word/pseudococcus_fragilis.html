Pseudococcus fragilis

1. NOUN

/ sjuːdoʊˈkɑːkəs frəˈdʒɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˈkɒkəs frəˈdʒɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of mealybug that is particularly destructive to citrus plants.
a citrus-damaging mealybug species

Examples

The infestation of Pseudococcus fragilis devastated the orange groves last summer.
Agricultural experts are developing new methods to control Pseudococcus fragilis populations in citrus orchards.
Early detection of Pseudococcus fragilis is crucial to prevent major crop losses.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek pseudo- (‘false’) and kokkos (‘berry’), and Latin fragilis (‘fragile’).
