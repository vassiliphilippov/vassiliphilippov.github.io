watchdog

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑtʃˌdɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃˌdɒɡ / (BrE)
a dog that has been trained to guard a building or property against intruders
dog trained to guard property

Examples

The factory hired a watchdog to protect the premises after hours.
A fierce watchdog barked loudly whenever anyone approached the gate.
They trained their new puppy to become an effective watchdog.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guard dog - dog trained to protect people or property

Etymology

From 'watch' (to observe) + 'dog', first used in 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑtʃˌdɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃˌdɒɡ / (BrE)
a person or organization that monitors and acts as a guardian against theft, illegal activity, or waste, especially by keeping an eye on others' conduct
guardian against illegal practices or waste

Examples

The consumer rights group acts as a watchdog to prevent companies from exploiting customers.
The government appointed an independent watchdog to ensure transparency in financial transactions.
Many believe the free press should serve as a watchdog over public officials.

Synset Examples

she is the global watchdog for human rights abuses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guardian - a protector or defender
  • Synonym: overseer - someone who supervises others
  • Antonym: offender - a person who commits an illegal act

Etymology

Originally referring to a guard dog; figurative sense for human or organizational guardians dates to the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
