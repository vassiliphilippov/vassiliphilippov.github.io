Phalaenopsis amabilis

1. NOUN

/ fəˌliːnˈɑːpsɪs əˈmæbɪlɪs / (AmE), / fəˌliːnˈɒpsɪs əˈmæbɪlɪs / (BrE)
a species of orchid with large fleshy leaves and fragrant pink-and-white flowers marked with red spots, commonly known as the moon orchid
species of fragrant, pink-and-white orchid

Examples

The Phalaenopsis amabilis is often displayed as an ornamental plant because of its stunning blooms.
Many collectors prize the Phalaenopsis amabilis for its ease of care and long-lasting flowers.
In her conservatory, she nurtures a thriving Phalaenopsis amabilis on a shaded shelf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moon orchid - common name for Phalaenopsis amabilis

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Phalaenopsis, from Greek 'phalaina' (moth) + 'opsis' (appearance), and species name amabilis meaning 'lovely' in Latin.
