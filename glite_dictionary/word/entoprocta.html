Entoprocta

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛntoʊˈprɑktə / (AmE), / ˌɛntəʊˈprɒktə / (BrE)
a small phylum of aquatic, mainly marine, invertebrate animals distinguished by their characteristic tentacled crown and sometimes regarded as a subphylum of Bryozoa.
a phylum of small aquatic invertebrates

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species of Entoprocta living on the underside of rocks.
The Entoprocta are often studied to understand evolutionary relationships among aquatic invertebrates.
Unlike Bryozoa, Entoprocta have their anus inside the tentacular crown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bryozoa - phylum of aquatic colonial invertebrates, sometimes called moss animals

Etymology

From Greek 'entos' (within) + 'proktos' (anus), referring to the location of the anus inside the ring of tentacles.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəˈprɑktə / (AmE), / ˌɛntəˈprɒktə / (BrE)
a phylum of small, aquatic invertebrate animals with a crown of tentacles surrounding the mouth and anus, sometimes considered a subphylum of Bryozoa
a phylum of aquatic invertebrates
medical

Examples

Members of the Entoprocta phylum are found in both marine and freshwater environments.
Scientists once debated whether Entoprocta should be a separate phylum or a subphylum of Bryozoa.
The feeding mechanism of Entoprocta involves using a tentacular crown to capture food particles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bryozoa - a phylum of aquatic invertebrate animals, also known as moss animals

Etymology

From Greek 'ento-', meaning 'within', and 'proktos', meaning 'anus'.
