Dyirbal

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪrbəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪəbəl / (BrE)
A language of Australian Aboriginal people, spoken in northeast Queensland.
Australian Aboriginal language of northeast Queensland

Examples

The Dyirbal language is known for its complex system of noun classification.
Linguists have documented traditional stories told in Dyirbal.
She is studying Dyirbal as part of her research on Australian Aboriginal languages.

Etymology

From the language's own name, Dyirbal.
