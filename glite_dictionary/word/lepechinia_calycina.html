Lepechinia calycina

1. NOUN

/ lɛpəˈkɪniə ˌkælɪˈsaɪnə / (AmE), / lɛpɪˈkɪnɪə ˌkælɪˈsaɪnə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to California, characterized by woolly stems and leaves and large white flowers; commonly known as pitcher sage.
California plant with woolly stems and white flowers

Examples

Lepechinia calycina thrives in the coastal scrub habitats of California's foothills.
Botanists often refer to Lepechinia calycina as pitcher sage due to its distinctive flowers.
Gardeners appreciate Lepechinia calycina for its aromatic foliage and showy white blooms.

Etymology

From genus name Lepechinia, honoring Ivan Lepechin, a Russian botanist, and species name calycina, from Greek 'kalyx' meaning 'calyx'.
