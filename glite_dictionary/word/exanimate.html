exanimate

1. ADJ

/ ɛɡˈzænɪmət / (AmE), / ɛɡˈzænɪmət / (BrE)
Devoid of life; lifeless or spiritless.
lifeless; without spirit or life

Examples

The exanimate form of the deer lay silent in the morning light.
Her exanimate face showed no signs of emotion or awareness.
The battlefield was filled with exanimate bodies after the fierce fight.

Synset Examples

a lifeless body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifeless - lacking life or energy
  • Synonym: dead - no longer alive
  • Antonym: animate - having life; alive or lively

Etymology

From Latin exanimatus, past participle of exanimare ('to deprive of life').
