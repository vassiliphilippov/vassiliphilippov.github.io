Podocarpus dacrydioides

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdəˈkɑːrpəs ˌdækriˌdiˈoʊdiːz / (AmE), / ˌpɒdəˈkɑːpəs ˌdækrɪˌdɪˈəʊdiːz / (BrE)
a large evergreen tree native to New Zealand, valued for its light and easily worked wood
New Zealand evergreen tree, useful wood

Examples

The timber from Podocarpus dacrydioides was historically used in house construction throughout New Zealand.
Many conservationists advocate planting Podocarpus dacrydioides to restore native forests.
The durable wood of Podocarpus dacrydioides is prized for making furniture and cabinetry.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Podocarpus' (footed fruit) and species epithet from Greek 'dakrydion' (little tear) and 'eidos' (form), referring to the fruit shape.
