Caesalpinia ferrea

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːzælˈpɪniə fəˈriːə / (AmE), / ˌsiːzælˈpɪniə fəˈriːə / (BrE)
A species of thornless tropical tree native to South America, known for yielding heavy, durable wood.
thornless tropical tree yielding heavy wood

Examples

The wood from Caesalpinia ferrea is prized for its strength and durability in construction.
Many urban parks in Brazil feature the distinctive Caesalpinia ferrea as an ornamental shade tree.
Researchers studied the growth rate of Caesalpinia ferrea in different soil conditions.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Caesalpinia' honours Italian botanist Andrea Cesalpino, and species 'ferrea' means 'iron-like' in Latin, referring to the wood's hardness.
