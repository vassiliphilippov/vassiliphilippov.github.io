clerical

1. ADJ

/ ˈklɛrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈklɛrɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to office work, such as routine administrative tasks performed in an office or by office staff.
relating to office work or office staff

Examples

She applied for a clerical position at a local law firm.
Most of the clerical work can be completed on a computer nowadays.
The company hired more staff to handle the increasing clerical duties.

Synset Examples

clerical skills
a clerical job
the clerical staff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administrative - relating to the running of a business, organization, etc.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin clericalis (of a cleric or clerk), via Old French clérical.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02566999-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈklɛrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈklɛrɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the clergy or the office and duties of religious leaders in Christian churches
relating to the clergy

Examples

He wore a clerical collar as a symbol of his faith.
The church held a conference to discuss clerical responsibilities and reforms.
There are strict codes of conduct for those in clerical positions.

Synset Examples

clerical collar

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters

Etymology

From Medieval Latin clericalis, from clericus (clerk, clergyman).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02708648-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈklɛrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈklɛrɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to clerks or office work, especially routine administrative or record-keeping tasks
relating to clerks or office work

Examples

The company has several clerical positions available in the human resources department.
She was responsible for various clerical duties such as filing and data entry.
A clerical error in the report caused some confusion during the meeting.

Synset Examples

clerical work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administrative - relating to administration or management
  • Antonym: manual - relating to work done by hand (as antonym to office work)

Etymology

From cleric, originally referring to members of the clergy, later applied to office workers due to their role in writing and record-keeping.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02708768-a
Synset POS: a
