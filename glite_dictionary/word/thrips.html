Thrips

1. NOUN

/ θrɪps / (AmE), / θrɪps / (BrE)
A genus of very small insects, the type genus of the family Thripidae, which includes many species known commonly as thrips.
genus of tiny insects (thrips)

Examples

Several species within the genus Thrips are known to cause damage to crops worldwide.
Researchers collected specimens of Thrips from different agricultural fields for analysis.
The type genus Thrips has been extensively studied due to its economic impact.

Etymology

From Greek *thrīps* (woodworm, wood louse, insect).
