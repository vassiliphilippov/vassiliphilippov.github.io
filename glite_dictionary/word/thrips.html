thrips

1. NOUN

/ θɹɪps / (AmE), / θɹɪps / (BrE)
A small insect with slender, feathery wings, many species of which feed on plant sap and can cause damage to crops and plants.
tiny plant-sucking insect

Examples

The leaves were damaged by a large number of thrips feeding on their sap.
A gardener may use natural predators to control thrips infestations in greenhouses.
When a single thrips is found, it can quickly multiply and harm plants.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Thrips', genus name, from Greek 'thríps' (woodworm, wood louse).
