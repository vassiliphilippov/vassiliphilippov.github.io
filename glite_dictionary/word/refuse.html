refuse

1. VERB

/ rəˈfjuz / (AmE), / rɪˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to not allow someone to have or receive something that they want or need
not allow someone to have something

Examples

His parents refuse him sweets before dinner.
The club refused her entry because she was underage.
The teacher often refuses students extensions on their homework assignments.

Synset Examples

She denies me every pleasure
he denies her her weekly allowance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deny - to refuse to give or grant something to someone
  • Antonym: grant - to give or allow someone what they ask for
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02217607-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈfjuz / (AmE), / rɪˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to indicate or show unwillingness to do something; to decline to accept or agree to a request, offer, or invitation
decline to do something or accept

Examples

She refused to answer any of their questions about the incident.
Although invited to the meeting, he politely refused.
They refuse to participate in activities that go against their beliefs.

Synset Examples

he declined to join the group on a hike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decline - to politely refuse an invitation or offer
  • Antonym: to accept - to consent to receive or undertake something offered

Etymology

From Old French refuser, from Latin refundere (to pour back).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00799092-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rɪˈfjus / (AmE), / rɪˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to say or show that you do not accept or believe something as true or correct
decline to accept as true

Examples

The committee refused to believe the allegations without evidence.
Despite the evidence, she still refused to accept his explanation.
Many people refuse to admit that they have made a mistake.

Synset Examples

He refused to accept what she said

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to accept - to believe or agree that something is true
  • Synonym: to deny - to state that something is not true

Etymology

From Old French *refuser*, from Latin *refusare* (to refuse, reject).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02242120-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ rɪˈfjuz / (AmE), / rɪˈfjuːz / (BrE)
(of a body) to reject a transplanted organ or tissue by an immune response.
reject transplanted organ or tissue
medical

Examples

If the patient's immune system refuses the new heart, another transplant may be necessary.
Doctors were concerned that her body would refuse the donor kidney after the procedure.
Sometimes patients refuse a graft, leading to complications that require immediate attention.

Synset Examples

His body rejected the liver of the donor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - (of a body) to not accept a transplanted organ or tissue

7. NOUN

/ rɪˈfjuz / (AmE), / rɪˈfjuːz / (BrE)
food waste or scraps that are discarded, especially from a kitchen
discarded food waste

Examples

The kitchen generates a lot of refuse every day, much of which can be composted.
After dinner, he collected the refuse and took it out to the bin.
Proper disposal of food refuse helps prevent attracting pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garbage - waste material, especially household waste
  • Synonym: food waste - discarded food or food scraps
  • Degree Higher: compost - decayed organic material used as fertilizer

Etymology

From Old French *refuser* (to refuse), ultimately from Latin *refusare* (to refuse, reject).
