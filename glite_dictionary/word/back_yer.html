back yer

1. NOUN

/ bæk jɪr / (AmE), / bak jɪə / (BrE)
the Cyrillic letter Ъ/ъ, used in modern languages to indicate a hard (non-palatalized) consonant
the Cyrillic letter Ъ/ъ

Examples

The Russian word 'мать' uses the back yer at the end in older orthography.
Some Slavic languages retain the back yer as an important orthographic symbol.
When learning Cyrillic, understanding the function of the back yer is essential.

Synset Examples

The final yer appears to be a back yer, but several corrections to this letter have been made.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soft yer - the Cyrillic letter Ь/ь, denoting a palatalized consonant
