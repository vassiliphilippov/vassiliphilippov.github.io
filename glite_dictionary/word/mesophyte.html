mesophyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛs.əˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmiː.səʊ.faɪt / (BrE)
a plant that grows in environments with a moderate or average supply of water, neither too wet nor too dry
plant in moderate water conditions

Examples

The lush, green leaves of the mesophyte indicate it is well-suited to average moisture levels.
Unlike a cactus, a mesophyte cannot survive prolonged droughts.
Most temperate forest trees are considered mesophytes because they thrive in environments with moderate rainfall.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: xerophyte - plant adapted to dry environments
  • Antonym: hydrophyte - plant adapted to very wet or aquatic environments

Etymology

From Greek 'mesos' (middle) + 'phyton' (plant).
