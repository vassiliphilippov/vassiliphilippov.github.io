crumbliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrʌm.bli.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkrʌm.bli.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being easily crumbled or broken into small fragments
tendency to crumble easily

Examples

The crumbliness of the pastry made it difficult to serve without making a mess.
Some cheeses are prized specifically for their rich flavor and delightful crumbliness.
If a cake has too much crumbliness, it may fall apart when you try to slice it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brittleness - the quality of being easily broken, snapped, or cracked

Etymology

From 'crumbly' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being crumbly.
