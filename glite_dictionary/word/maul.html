maul

1. VERB

/ mɔl / (AmE), / mɔːl / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to injure someone badly by beating, scratching, or tearing.
injure badly by beating or tearing

Examples

The bear mauled the camper before running into the woods.
The journalist was mauled by angry fans after the controversial match.
Rescuers found the hiker mauled and barely conscious after the animal attack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mangle - to injure very severely by cutting, tearing, or crushing
  • Synonym: attack - to take aggressive action against (someone or something)

Etymology

From Middle English 'maulen', from Old French 'maillier' (to pound, beat), from Latin 'malleus' (hammer).

2. VERB

/ mɔl / (AmE), / mɔːl / (BrE)
to split wood using a heavy hammer (maul) and wedges
split wood with a heavy hammer and wedges

Examples

He spent the afternoon mauling firewood for the winter.
The workers mauled large logs into usable pieces using wedges and a sledge.
Before we could light the fireplace, we had to maul some fresh wood outside.

Etymology

From Old French mail, from Latin malleus (hammer).

3. NOUN

/ mɔl / (AmE), / mɔːl / (BrE)
a large, heavy hammer with a long handle, used for driving stakes or wedges into the ground
heavy long-handled hammer

Examples

The worker selected a maul to drive the stake deep into the ground.
He swung the maul with both hands to split the thick log.
A heavy maul is needed for this type of construction work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sledgehammer - a large, heavy hammer used for such work as breaking rocks or driving in posts

Etymology

From Old French *mail*, from Latin *malleus* (hammer).

4. VERB

/ mɑl / (AmE), / mɔːl / (BrE)
to injure, damage, or spoil something by rough or violent handling
to damage or injure by rough handling

Examples

The bear tried to maul the hiker, leaving him badly injured.
The packaging was so poorly handled that it arrived looking mauled and torn.
Untrained movers can easily maul your furniture during a hurried relocation.

Synset Examples

The dog began to maul the toy aggressively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear - to pull apart or damage by force
