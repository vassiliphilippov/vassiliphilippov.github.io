musculus triceps brachii

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskjələs ˈtraɪsɛps ˈbræki.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈmʌskjʊləs ˈtraɪsɛps ˈbræki.aɪ / (BrE)
the large muscle at the back of the upper arm, having three points of origin, that is responsible for extending the forearm.
muscle at back of upper arm
medical

Examples

The musculus triceps brachii is essential for straightening the elbow joint.
Injury to the musculus triceps brachii can severely limit arm extension.
During a push-up, the musculus triceps brachii contracts forcefully to lift the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triceps - the muscle running along the back of the upper arm

Etymology

Latin; from 'musculus' (muscle), 'triceps' (three-headed), and 'brachii' (of the arm).
