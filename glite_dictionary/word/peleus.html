Peleus

1. PROPN

/ ˈpiːliəs / (AmE), / ˈpiːliːəs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a king of the Myrmidons and the father of Achilles.
king of the Myrmidons; father of Achilles

Examples

In Greek mythology, Peleus is celebrated as the father of the hero Achilles.
The wedding of Peleus and Thetis is a famous story in ancient literature.
Many ancient texts recount the adventures of Peleus and his encounters with the gods.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Πηλεύς (Pēleus), a mythological king.
