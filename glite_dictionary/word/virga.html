virga

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrɡə / (AmE), / ˈvɜːɡə / (BrE)
wisps of precipitation, such as rain or snow, that evaporate before reaching the ground, typically seen as streaks descending from clouds
precipitation that evaporates before reaching ground

Examples

The weather forecast mentioned virga as a possible explanation for the cloud streaks.
From the airplane window, we could see virga trailing beneath the storm clouds.
Virga can be mistaken for rain on the horizon, even though it never reaches the ground.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: precipitation - any form of water, such as rain or snow, that falls from clouds and reaches the ground

Etymology

From Latin 'virga' (rod, twig, stripe), referring to the streak-like appearance.
