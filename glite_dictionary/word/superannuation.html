superannuation

1. NOUN

/ suː.pɚˌæn.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / suː.pərˌæn.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of retiring or being retired from employment because of reaching a certain age, often with a pension
retirement due to age, often with a pension

Examples

He reluctantly accepted his superannuation and left the company after forty years of service.
Policies regarding superannuation vary from one organization to another, especially concerning pension benefits.
She is concerned about how her superannuation will affect her future financial security.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retirement - the state of leaving one's job and ceasing to work

Etymology

From Latin superannuatus (outdated by age), from super- (above) + annus (year).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsuːpərˌænjuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərˌanjʊˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
A regular payment made to a retired employee, usually from a pension fund or retirement plan.
regular pension payment to a retiree

Examples

After 30 years at the company, she relies on her superannuation for a steady income.
The government is considering raising the minimum superannuation contributions for employees.
He checked his balance online to see how much his superannuation had grown over the past year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pension - a regular payment to retirees or disabled people, usually from a former employer or the state

Etymology

From Latin 'superannuatus' (out of date, retired), from 'super-' (above) + 'annus' (year).

the state of being obsolete or no longer current
the state of being obsolete
archaic

Examples

Many traditions have fallen into superannuation and are no longer practiced today.
The museum displayed machines that had long suffered superannuation.
To avoid superannuation, the company must continuously innovate its products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsolescence - the process of becoming outdated or no longer used

Etymology

From Latin superannuatus (too old), from super- (over) + annus (year).
