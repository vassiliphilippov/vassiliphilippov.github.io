crested

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɛstɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrɛstɪd / (BrE)
(of a shield or coat of arms) bearing a crest or heraldic device.
bearing a heraldic crest

Examples

The knight entered the hall with a crested shield, displaying his family's emblem proudly.
Their ancient armor remained crested, signifying noble lineage through generations.
At the ceremony, each banner was crested with the colors and devices of its house.

Etymology

From crest + -ed, denoting possession or presence of a crest.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkrɛstɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrɛstɪd / (BrE)
(of a knight's helmet) having a decorative plume or feathered ornament on top.
decorated with a plume (helmet)

Examples

The knight rode into battle wearing a crested helmet that gleamed in the sunlight.
Only nobles could afford to have crested helmets specially designed for tournaments.
The museum displayed a crested helmet from the medieval period, complete with its original plume.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plumed - adorned with a feather or feathers, often as decoration

Etymology

From Middle English, from crest + -ed, meaning having a crest.

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˈkrɛstɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrɛstɪd / (BrE)
(of a bird or animal) having a tuft, crest, or plume on the head, often ornamental; sometimes used of plants with a prominent ridge or tuft.
having a crest or tuft on the head

Examples

The crested duck floated gracefully on the pond, its head tuft standing out.
We spotted a crested oriole in the forest, easily identified by its prominent plume.
Many gardeners admire the crested iris for its unique ridged petals.

Synset Examples

golden crested
crested iris
crested oriole
tufted duck
tufted loosestrife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tufted - having a bunch or cluster of small parts, especially on the head

Etymology

From crest + -ed, forming an adjective meaning 'having a crest.'

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
