mortal

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɔr.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.tl̩ / (BrE)
(of living things) subject to death; not able to live forever.
subject to death; not immortal

Examples

Unlike gods, all humans are mortal and must eventually face death.
The myth tells of a mortal hero who challenged the gods.
Plants, animals, and people are all mortal beings.

Synset Examples

mortal beings

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immortal - not subject to death; living forever

Etymology

From Latin mortalis (subject to death), from mors, mort- (death).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01562007-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɔrtəl / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtəl / (BrE)
(of hostility or conflict) persistent and deadly; showing absolute enmity or opposition likely to end in death.
deadly; showing absolute enmity

Examples

He considered his rival a mortal enemy and would never forgive him.
Their feud had become a mortal conflict, with neither side willing to compromise.
She vowed mortal vengeance after the betrayal.

Synset Examples

mortal enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadly - causing or able to cause death
  • Synonym: lethal - sufficient to cause death
  • Synonym: venomous - showing strong hostility

Etymology

From Latin 'mortalis', from 'mors' (death).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01511671-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɔrtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtl̩ / (BrE)
(of a sin) causing the loss of divine grace or resulting in spiritual death.
causing loss of divine grace (theology)

Examples

According to Catholic doctrine, a mortal sin must be confessed to receive forgiveness.
He worried that missing Mass would be considered a mortal offense by the Church.
Committing a mortal sin, according to some beliefs, puts the soul in grave danger.

Synset Examples

the seven deadly sins

Related Terms

  • Antonym: venial - a sin that does not result in a complete separation from God

Etymology

From Latin 'mortalis' (subject to death), from 'mors' (death).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01725926-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈmɔrt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtəl / (BrE)
(of a thing) causing or capable of causing death; fatal.
fatal; causing death

Examples

The wound he suffered in battle was mortal and could not be treated.
Doctors warned that without treatment, the infection could prove mortal.
They faced mortal danger as the building began to collapse around them.

Synset Examples

a deadly enemy
mortal combat
a mortal illness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadly - causing or able to cause death
  • Synonym: fatal - resulting in death
  • Antonym: harmless - not able to cause injury, not dangerous

Etymology

From Latin mortalis (subject to death), from mors (death).
