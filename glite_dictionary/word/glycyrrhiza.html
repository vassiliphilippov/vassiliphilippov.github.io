Glycyrrhiza

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɪˈsɪrəˌhaɪzə / (AmE), / ɡlɪˌsɪrɪˈhaɪzə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial Eurasian herbs known for their sticky roots, including plants such as licorice.
genus of sticky Eurasian herbs

Examples

The Glycyrrhiza genus includes several species commonly used in traditional medicine.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Glycyrrhiza plants, particularly their anti-inflammatory effects.
Licorice, which comes from Glycyrrhiza glabra, is widely used as a flavoring and herbal remedy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: licorice - sweet root of Glycyrrhiza glabra; also a confection

Etymology

From Greek glykyrrhiza, from glykys (sweet) + rhiza (root), referring to the sweet root of licorice.

2. NOUN

/ ɡlaɪˌsɪˈraɪzə / (AmE), / ɡlɪˌsɪˈrʌɪzə / (BrE)
a genus of sticky perennial Eurasian herbs, including the licorice plant, known for their sweet-tasting roots
genus of herbs; includes licorice

Examples

The most well-known member of the Glycyrrhiza genus is the licorice plant, prized for its sweet root.
Scientists study Glycyrrhiza species to learn more about their medicinal properties.
Several Glycyrrhiza herbs grow wild in Eurasian meadows and riverbanks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: licorice - a sweet-tasting root from plants in the genus Glycyrrhiza

Etymology

From Greek glykyrrhiza, meaning 'sweet root' (glykys 'sweet' + rhiza 'root').
