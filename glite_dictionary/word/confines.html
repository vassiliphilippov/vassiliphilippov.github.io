confines

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnˌfaɪnz / (AmE), / ˈkɒnˌfaɪnz / (BrE)
the borders or limits that define the extent of an area or a subject
the limits or boundaries of something

Examples

The prisoners were not allowed to leave the confines of the prison.
Her research remained within the confines of classical music studies.
They felt trapped by the strict confines of tradition.

Synset Examples

he stayed within the confines of the city

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundaries - lines or limits indicating the edges of an area
  • Synonym: limits - points or lines beyond which something does not extend
  • Synonym: extent - the area covered by something

Etymology

From Latin 'confinis' (bordering on, adjacent), from 'con-' (together) + 'finis' (end, boundary).
