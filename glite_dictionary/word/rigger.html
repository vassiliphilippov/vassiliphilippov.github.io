rigger

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡə / (BrE)
a person who fits the rigging on a ship, such as sails, masts, and ropes
person who rigs ships

Examples

The experienced rigger prepared the sails for the ship's long voyage.
As a rigger, she was responsible for ensuring all ropes and pulleys were secure.
Every rigger must understand the complex system of masts and rigging aboard a tall ship.

Etymology

From 'rig', relating to equipping a vessel, plus the agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡə / (BrE)
A long, slender, pointed sable brush used by artists, especially for painting fine lines and details.
a slender, pointed artist's brush

Examples

The artist used a rigger to add delicate lines to the painting.
A rigger is perfect for painting fine branches and calligraphy in watercolor.
She kept her finest rigger brush carefully stored to prevent damage.

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡə / (BrE)
a sailing vessel with a specified type of rigging, often used with a descriptive term (e.g. 'square rigger')
a vessel with a specified rigging type

Examples

The museum features a beautifully restored rigger from the 19th century.
They spotted a three-masted rigger sailing across the bay.
As a child, he dreamed of working aboard a grand rigger on the open seas.

Synset Examples

a square rigger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: square rigger - a sailing ship with square-rigged sails on all masts

Etymology

From 'rig' (equipment or arrangement of sails) + -er (one that is or does). Nautical usage dates to the 18th century.

4. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡə / (BrE)
a person who works on an oil rig, especially handling equipment and machinery used in drilling for oil or gas
oil rig worker

Examples

After years as a rigger, he knew every safety protocol on the oil platform.
The company hired more riggers to keep up with the increased drilling schedule.
Life as a rigger can be tough, with long hours and challenging conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roughneck - worker on a drilling rig, especially one who handles drilling equipment

Etymology

Originally from 'rig' (to equip a ship), later extended to refer to workers handling equipment on oil rigs.
