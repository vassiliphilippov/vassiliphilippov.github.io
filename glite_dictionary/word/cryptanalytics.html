cryptanalytics

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptəˈnælɪtɪks / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəˈnælɪtɪks / (BrE)
the study and practice of analyzing and deciphering codes, ciphers, and cryptograms
analysis of codes and ciphers
medical

Examples

Advances in cryptanalytics have made some old ciphers obsolete in modern security systems.
He pursued a doctorate focusing on cryptanalytics and its application in data protection.
The field of cryptanalytics requires strong mathematical and analytical skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryptanalysis - the study of deciphering encrypted messages without knowing the key

Etymology

From 'crypt-' (Greek kryptós, hidden) and 'analytics', derived from 'analysis' (Greek analusis, breaking up).
