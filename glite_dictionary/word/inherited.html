inherited

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈhɛrɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a characteristic, disease, or trait) passed from parents to offspring through genes; received by heredity.
passed down by heredity

Examples

Many forms of cancer have an inherited component that increases risk.
The boy was born with an inherited blood disorder.
Her remarkable musical talent is believed to be inherited from her parents.

Synset Examples

an inherited disease
familial traits
genetically transmitted features

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congenital - (of a disease or condition) present from birth
  • Antonym: acquired - (of a condition or trait) developed after birth, not inherited

Etymology

From Latin 'inhereditare', from 'in-' (in) + 'hereditare' (to inherit), from 'heres' (heir).
