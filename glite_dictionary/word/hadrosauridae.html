Hadrosauridae

1. NOUN

/ hædrəˈsɔːrɪdiː / (AmE), / hædrəˈsɔːrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, herbivorous dinosaurs known as duck-billed dinosaurs, which lived during the Upper Cretaceous period.
family of duck-billed dinosaurs

Examples

Fossils of Hadrosauridae have been discovered on several continents, shedding light on their wide distribution.
Some members of Hadrosauridae had elaborate crests that may have been used for communication.
Scientists believe that Hadrosauridae traveled in herds and were among the most common herbivores of their time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hadrosaur - a dinosaur of the family Hadrosauridae; duck-billed dinosaur

Etymology

From New Latin Hadrosaurus (a genus name) + -idae (a zoological family suffix), from Greek hadros (thick, bulky) + sauros (lizard).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhædrəˌsɔrɪˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌhædrəˌsɔːrɪˈdiː / (BrE)
a family of duck-billed dinosaurs from the Upper Cretaceous period, known for their broad, flat snouts
family of duck-billed dinosaurs

Examples

The discovery of Hadrosauridae fossils has greatly improved our understanding of dinosaur evolution.
Scientists believe that members of the Hadrosauridae family were highly adapted herbivores.
Many museums display reconstructed skeletons of Hadrosauridae species such as Parasaurolophus and Edmontosaurus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hadrosaur - a member of the Hadrosauridae family; duck-billed dinosaur

Etymology

From New Latin Hadrosaurus (genus name), from Greek 'hadros' (thick, bulky) + 'sauros' (lizard), with '-idae' as a zoological family suffix.
