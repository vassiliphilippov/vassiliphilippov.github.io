bailiff

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪlɪf / (AmE), / ˈbeɪlɪf / (BrE)
an officer of the court who is responsible for executing court orders, serving writs, and maintaining order in courtrooms
court officer responsible for court orders and security
legal

Examples

The bailiff announced that the court was in session and asked everyone to rise.
When the witness became unruly, the bailiff escorted him out of the courtroom.
It is the bailiff's duty to serve legal documents and make arrests when necessary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marshal - an officer who maintains order in court

Etymology

From Old French *baillif* (administrator), from *baillier* (to govern or administer).
