uncongenial

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kənˈdʒiː.ni.əl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈdʒiː.ni.əl / (BrE)
(of plant stock or scions) incapable of being successfully grafted together due to incompatibility.
incapable of being grafted (plants)

Examples

These two varieties are uncongenial and cannot be grafted successfully.
The failure of the graft was due to uncongenial stock and scion.
Because the rootstock and scion were uncongenial, the tree did not thrive.

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'congenial' (agreeable, compatible), meaning 'not compatible'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnkənˈdʒiniəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈdʒiːniəl / (BrE)
(of a place, situation, or environment) not suitable or favorable for living, growth, or comfort; unpleasant or inhospitable.
unpleasant, inhospitable environment

Examples

The explorers struggled to survive in the uncongenial climate of the Arctic.
Plants rarely thrive in such uncongenial soil conditions.
His creativity flourished despite the otherwise uncongenial atmosphere at work.

Synset Examples

a hostile climate
an uncongenial atmosphere
an uncongenial soil
the unfriendly environment at high altitudes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhospitable - not friendly or suitable for living or staying in
  • Synonym: hostile - unfriendly or antagonistic environment or conditions
  • Antonym: congenial - pleasant and suitable; friendly and agreeable

Etymology

From un- (not) + congenial, where congenial is from Latin congenialis (of the same kind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01246247-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌnkənˈdʒiːniəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈdʒiːniəl / (BrE)
(of a person, place, or thing) not pleasant or suitable; not compatible with one's tastes, needs, or disposition.
not pleasant or suitable

Examples

She found the atmosphere at the new office uncongenial and difficult to adjust to.
His uncongenial attitude made it hard for him to make friends in the group.
Many people left the company because the working conditions were uncongenial to their needs.

Synset Examples

the uncongenial roommates were always fighting
the task was uncongenial to one sensitive to rebuffs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: congenial - pleasant or agreeable; suitable to one's nature
  • Synonym: unsuitable - not appropriate or fitting for a particular situation or person

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'congenial' (pleasant, agreeable); 'congenial' from Latin 'congenialis' (of the same kind, agreeable).

Source: WordNet
