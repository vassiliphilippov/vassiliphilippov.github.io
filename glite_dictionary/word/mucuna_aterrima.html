Mucuna aterrima

1. NOUN

/ muˈkuːnə əˈtɛrɪmə / (AmE), / mjuːˈkuːnə əˈtɛrɪmə / (BrE)
A species of annual woody vine native to Asia, with long clusters of purplish flowers and densely hairy pods, cultivated in warm regions as green manure and for grazing.
annual vine used as green manure and forage

Examples

Mucuna aterrima is often cultivated as a cover crop to improve soil fertility in tropical regions.
Farmers in southern states use Mucuna aterrima as green manure and for grazing livestock.
The distinctive hairy pods of Mucuna aterrima can cause skin irritation when handled without gloves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: velvet bean - common name for Mucuna species, including Mucuna aterrima

Etymology

From Latin genus name Mucuna and species name aterrima meaning 'very black', referring to the seed color.
