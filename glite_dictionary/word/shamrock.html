shamrock

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæmˌrɑk / (AmE), / ˈʃæm.rɒk / (BrE)
A small Eurasian plant with three-part leaves, typically regarded as a symbol of Ireland.
three-leafed plant, Irish symbol

Examples

The shamrock is often associated with St. Patrick's Day celebrations in Ireland.
She wore a green shamrock pin on her jacket for good luck.
Farmers in Europe sometimes plant shamrock to improve soil quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clover - A plant with trifoliate leaves, related to the shamrock

Etymology

From Irish seamróg, diminutive of seamar (clover).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃæm.rɑk / (AmE), / ˈʃæm.rɒk / (BrE)
a small plant, typically clover, with three round leaves, especially regarded as the national emblem of Ireland.
three-leafed clover; Irish symbol

Examples

A green shamrock decorated every table at the St. Patrick's Day celebration.
She wore a shamrock pin on her jacket to show her Irish heritage.
The pasture was covered in creeping shamrocks and wildflowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clover - plant with three-lobed leaves, genus Trifolium

Etymology

From Irish 'seamróg', diminutive of 'seamair' (clover).
