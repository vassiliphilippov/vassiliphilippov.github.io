Baruch de Spinoza

1. PROPN

/ bəˈruːk də spɪˈnoʊzə / (AmE), / bəˈruːx də spɪˈnəʊzə / (BrE)
(1632–1677) A Dutch philosopher known for his development of a pantheistic system of philosophy and major contributions to rationalism.
Dutch philosopher and rationalist (1632–1677)

Examples

Baruch de Spinoza challenged traditional religious beliefs with his philosophical writings.
Many scholars regard Baruch de Spinoza as a central figure in the history of Western philosophy.
The major works of Baruch de Spinoza remain influential in modern philosophical thought.

Etymology

From Baruch (Hebrew for ‘blessed’) and de Spinoza (family name of Portuguese origin).
