Papuan

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑː.pju.ən / (AmE), / ˈpæp.ju.ən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Papua New Guinea or New Guinea
a person from Papua New Guinea or New Guinea

Examples

The Papuan was explaining local customs to the visiting researchers.
Many Papuans retain traditional ways of life deep in the highlands.
She developed a deep respect for Papuan culture during her travels.

Etymology

From 'Papua', the name of the region, with the suffix '-an' denoting belonging or origin.

/ ˈpæpjuən / (AmE), / ˈpapjʊən / (BrE)
of or relating to Papua, or its people or language

Examples

Many Papuan traditions have been preserved by local communities.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɑpjuən / (AmE), / ˈpæpjʊən / (BrE)
any of the indigenous languages spoken in Papua New Guinea, New Britain, or the Solomon Islands that are not part of the Malayo-Polynesian language family.
indigenous language of Papua New Guinea region

Examples

Linguists have identified more than 800 distinct Papuan languages in Papua New Guinea alone.
The grammar of a typical Papuan language can differ significantly from that of neighboring Austronesian tongues.
Many Papuan languages are spoken by relatively small communities in remote areas.

Etymology

From Papua, a region in New Guinea, with English suffix -an.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06953877-n
Synset POS: n
