Erigeron glaucous

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrəˈdʒiːrɒn ˈɡlɔːkəs / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈdʒɪərɒn ˈɡlɔːkəs / (BrE)
A slightly succulent perennial plant native to coastal bluffs from Oregon to southern California, with basal leaves, hairy sticky stems, and solitary flower heads with narrow pink or lavender rays.
plant native to U.S. West Coast bluffs

Examples

The Erigeron glaucous flourishes along the windy bluffs of California’s coastline.
Botanists admire the resilient nature of Erigeron glaucous in its native habitat.
Gardeners value Erigeron glaucous for its delicate lavender flower heads and drought tolerance.

Etymology

From Greek: ‘Erigeron’ meaning 'early old man', referencing the whitish down of some species, and Latin ‘glaucus’ meaning 'blue-green'.
