imbibition

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.bəˈbɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɪm.bɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
(Chemistry, biology) The process by which a solid or gel absorbs a liquid, often causing swelling.
absorption of liquid by a solid or gel
medical

Examples

The imbibition of water by seeds is essential for germination.
Gelatin increases in size through imbibition when placed in water.
In some materials, imbibition causes notable swelling due to water intake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorption - the process of taking in a substance

Etymology

From Latin 'imbibere' meaning 'to drink in'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˌbɪˈbɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪmˌbɪˈbɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or act of absorbing or taking in liquid, especially by living tissues or materials; also, the act of drinking
the act of absorbing or drinking liquids

Examples

The imbibition of water is essential for seed germination in plants.
Rapid imbibition of fluids can sometimes lead to discomfort or bloating.
During the tasting, the imbibition of various wines was carefully paced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorption - the process by which one thing absorbs or is absorbed by another
  • Synonym: drinking - the act of consuming liquid, especially alcoholic drink

Etymology

From Latin imbibitio(n-) from 'imbibere' (to drink in).
