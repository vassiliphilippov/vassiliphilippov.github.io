2. ADJ

/ əˈslænt / (AmE), / əˈslɑːnt / (BrE)
at an angle; in a slanted or oblique direction
in a slanted or oblique direction

Examples

The picture on the wall hung aslant, drawing everyone's attention.
Sunlight came aslant through the blinds, casting striped shadows on the floor.
He glanced at her aslant, unsure of what to say next.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slanted - not straight or level; at an angle
  • Antonym: straight - not bending or slanting; vertical or horizontal

Etymology

From Old Norse aslant (sloping, sideways), related to 'slant'.

3. ADV

/ əˈslænt / (AmE), / əˈslɑːnt / (BrE)
At a slant or in a slanting direction; across or over at an angle.
at a slant; slantwise

Examples

The painter placed the ladder aslant against the wall to reach the ceiling.
A beam of sunlight fell aslant through the dusty window, illuminating the old books.
Her hat sat aslant on her head, giving her a jaunty appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagonally - in a straight line at an angle across something
  • Antonym: straight - in a direct line without bending or curving

Etymology

From Middle English, from a- (prefix, meaning 'on' or 'in') + slant.
