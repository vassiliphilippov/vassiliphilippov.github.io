Arvicola amphibius

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈvɪkələ æmˈfɪbiəs / (AmE), / ɑːˈvɪkələ æmˈfɪbiəs / (BrE)
a large Eurasian vole species, commonly known as the European water vole, found near water in Europe and parts of Asia
species of large Eurasian vole

Examples

The Arvicola amphibius population has declined dramatically due to habitat loss.
Naturalists observed Arvicola amphibius constructing burrows along the riverbank.
Predators such as the mink threaten the survival of Arvicola amphibius in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water vole - common name for Arvicola amphibius, a large Eurasian vole

Etymology

From Latin: 'arvicola' meaning 'field-dweller' and 'amphibius' meaning 'living both on land and in water'.
