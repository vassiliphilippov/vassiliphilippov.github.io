nihil obstat

1. NOUN

/ ˌniːhɪl ˈɑːbstæt / (AmE), / ˌniːhɪl ˈɒbstæt / (BrE)
an official statement that a book or other document contains nothing objectionable, especially in regard to Catholic doctrine or morals
official declaration of no objection

Examples

Before publication, the manuscript received the Church's nihil obstat and was deemed free of doctrinal error.
The bishop granted a nihil obstat after carefully reviewing the theological content of the text.
A nihil obstat does not imply endorsement, only that nothing in the work is objectionable to faith or morals.

Etymology

From Latin nihil obstat (nothing hinders/stands in the way).
