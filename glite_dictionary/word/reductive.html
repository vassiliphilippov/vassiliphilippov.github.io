reductive

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
tending to simplify or diminish something, often so much that important details or complexities are overlooked.
over-simplifying or diminishing

Examples

Her analysis was criticized for being too reductive and missing the nuances of the argument.
It is a reductive explanation that fails to address the complexity of the problem.
Many historians warn against making reductive generalizations about the past.

Synset Examples

their views of life were reductive and depreciatory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simplistic - treating complex issues as simpler than they really are
  • Antonym: expansive - covering a wide scope; open to including more detail

Etymology

From Latin 'reduct-', past participle stem of 'reducere' (to lead back), plus '-ive'.
