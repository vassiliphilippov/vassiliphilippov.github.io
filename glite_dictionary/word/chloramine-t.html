chloramine-T

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔːrəˌmin ˈtiː / (AmE), / ˌklɔːrəˌmiːn ˈtiː / (BrE)
a compound containing chlorine and nitrogen, used as an antiseptic and disinfectant, especially in medical settings
antiseptic compound containing chlorine and nitrogen
medical

Examples

The surgeon used chloramine-T to disinfect the wound before stitching it closed.
Laboratory technicians often rely on chloramine-T as an effective disinfectant.
Some water treatment plants add chloramine-T to control bacterial growth.

Etymology

From 'chloramine' (combining 'chlorine' and 'amine') plus 'T' for toluenesulfonamide, describing its chemical structure.
