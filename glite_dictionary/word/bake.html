to bake

1. VERB

/ beɪk / (AmE), / beɪk / (BrE)
to feel very hot or become overheated, especially because of strong heat or sunlight
become very hot in sunlight or heat

Examples

After an hour on the beach, we were all baking in the midday sun.
The city baked under a relentless heatwave for days.
If you don't find some shade soon, you'll be baking out here.

Synset Examples

The town was broiling in the sun
the tourists were baking in the heat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broil - to be exposed to or undergo great heat
  • Antonym: cool - to become or make less hot
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02761440-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ beɪk / (AmE), / beɪk / (BrE)
to cook food by heating it in an oven or on heated surfaces without direct exposure to a flame
cook food by oven heat

Examples

I like to bake fresh bread on weekends when I have extra time.
She plans to bake a chocolate cake for his birthday party.
The smell of potatoes baking in the oven filled the kitchen.

Synset Examples

bake the potatoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roast - to cook food, especially meat, using dry heat in an oven or over a fire
  • Antonym: boil - to cook food by immersing it in boiling water
  • Antonym: grill - to cook food using direct heat from below or above

Etymology

From Old English 'bacan' (to bake), related to Old Norse 'baka' and German 'backen'.

Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ beɪk / (AmE), / beɪk / (BrE)
(Of the sun or another natural force) to heat or dry by exposure, especially so that something becomes very hot.
heat or dry by natural force

Examples

The desert sun can bake the earth until cracks appear.
After hours without shade, we were baked by the relentless heat.
His skin was baking as he worked in the midday sun.

Synset Examples

The sun broils the valley in the summer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broil - to become very hot or scorched by heat
  • Antonym: cool - to make or become less hot
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00545216-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ beɪk / (AmE), / beɪk / (BrE)
to cook food by dry heat, especially in an oven
cook with dry heat in an oven

Examples

I love to bake fresh bread on Sundays.
She decided to bake a chocolate cake for his birthday.
They baked the cookies until they turned golden brown.

Synset Examples

bake a cake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roast - to cook food, especially meat, by dry heat in an oven or over a fire
  • Antonym: boil - to cook food by immersing it in boiling water or liquid

Etymology

Old English *bacan* (to bake, bake bread), from Proto-Germanic *bakaną*.
