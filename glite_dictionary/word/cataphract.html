cataphract

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætəˌfrækt / (AmE), / ˈkatəfrakt / (BrE)
A suit of armor that protects the entire body, especially as used by ancient heavy cavalry.
full-body armor, often for cavalry

Examples

The ancient Persians deployed elite cataphracts on the battlefield to break enemy lines.
Each cataphract was covered in scale armor from head to toe, along with his horse.
Historians debate how effective cataphracts were against Roman legionnaires.

Etymology

From Greek kataphraktos (clad in armor, protected), from kata- (down) + phraktos (enclosed).
