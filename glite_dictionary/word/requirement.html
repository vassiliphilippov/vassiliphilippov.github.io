requirement

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkwaɪərmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈkwaɪəmənt / (BrE)
a thing that is needed or demanded; something that is indispensable for a particular purpose or situation
thing that is needed or demanded

Examples

A passport is a basic requirement for international travel.
We met all the requirements to join the scholarship program.
Safety is an important requirement for this construction site.

Synset Examples

food and shelter are necessities of life
the essentials of the good life
allow farmers to buy their requirements under favorable conditions
a place where the requisites of water fuel and fodder can be obtained

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necessity - something that is essential or indispensable
  • Synonym: essential - an absolutely necessary thing
  • Antonym: luxury - something desirable but not essential

Etymology

From require + -ment, ultimately from Latin requirere (to seek, ask for).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈkwaɪərmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈkwaɪəmənt / (BrE)
A thing that is needed or demanded, especially as a necessary activity or obligation.
something needed or demanded

Examples

Meeting every requirement of the new safety regulations took months of planning and effort.
One requirement for the job is a willingness to travel frequently.
The course has a writing requirement that all students must fulfill.

Synset Examples

the requirements of his work affected his health
there were many demands on his time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demand - an insistent and peremptory request, made as if by right
  • Synonym: obligation - something one is bound to do by duty or contract

Etymology

From require + -ment, from Old French requerre and Latin requirere (seek, ask for).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05901258-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈkwaɪərmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈkwaɪəmənt / (BrE)
a thing that is needed or demanded as a necessary condition
thing that is needed or demanded

Examples

Good communication skills are a requirement for this job.
A passport is a requirement for international travel.
They failed to meet the minimum requirement for admission to the university.

Synset Examples

Latin was a prerequisite for admission

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prerequisite - thing required as a prior condition
  • Synonym: condition - something required as part of an agreement or circumstance
  • Antonym: option - something that may be chosen instead of being required

Etymology

From require + -ment, based on Old French and Latin roots.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05901034-n
Synset POS: n
