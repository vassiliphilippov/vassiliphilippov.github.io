progeria

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈdʒɪəriə / (AmE), / prəʊˈdʒɪəriə / (BrE)
a rare genetic disorder in children characterized by symptoms of rapid aging, such as grey hair, wrinkled skin, and a stooped posture
rare genetic disorder causing premature aging in children
medical

Examples

The young boy was diagnosed with progeria after doctors noticed signs of premature aging.
Scientists are researching progeria to better understand the mechanisms of aging.
Children with progeria often exhibit joint stiffness and growth failure.

Etymology

From Greek 'pro-' (before) + 'gēras' (old age).
