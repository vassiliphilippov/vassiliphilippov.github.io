abetter

1. NOUN

/ əˈbɛtər / (AmE), / əˈbɛtə / (BrE)
a person who helps, encourages, or incites another, especially to commit a crime or wrongdoing
one who assists or encourages another, esp. in crime
legal

Examples

The court found her guilty as an abetter in the fraud scheme.
An abetter may be held legally responsible even if they did not commit the main crime.
He was accused of being an abetter rather than the principal offender.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abettor - alternative spelling of abetter
  • Synonym: accomplice - a person who helps another commit a crime
  • Synonym: instigator - a person who initiates or incites wrongdoing

Etymology

From the verb abet, from Old French abeter (to incite), from a- (to) + beter (to bait).
