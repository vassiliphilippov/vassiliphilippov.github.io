Euphorbia heterophylla

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔrbi.ə ˌhɛtərəˈfaɪlə / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪə ˌhɛtərəˈfʌɪlə / (BrE)
a species of flowering plant in the spurge family, commonly known as the showy poinsettia, found from the southern United States to Peru
a species of flowering plant; showy poinsettia

Examples

Euphorbia heterophylla is commonly found in agricultural fields throughout Florida.
The leaves of Euphorbia heterophylla can vary significantly in shape even on the same plant.
Gardeners appreciate Euphorbia heterophylla for its bright, attractive bracts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showy poinsettia - common name for Euphorbia heterophylla

Etymology

From the genus name Euphorbia, after Greek physician Euphorbus, and the species epithet 'heterophylla,' meaning 'different-leaved' from Greek 'heteros' (different) and 'phyllon' (leaf).
