Mesocricetus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛsəˈkrɪsətəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛsəʊˈkrɪsɛtəs / (BrE)
A genus of rodents in the hamster family, including the golden hamster and its relatives.
genus of golden hamsters

Examples

The golden hamster, known scientifically as Mesocricetus auratus, is a popular pet around the world.
Researchers studied the behavior of several Mesocricetus species in their natural habitats.
Our laboratory stocks only Mesocricetus rodents for genetic experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hamster - small rodent often kept as a pet

Etymology

From Greek 'mesos' (middle) + 'cricetus' (hamster).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛsəˈkrɪsɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌmiːsəˈkrɪsɪtəs / (BrE)
a genus of rodents in the hamster subfamily Cricetinae, including the golden hamster and its relatives
genus of hamsters, includes golden hamster

Examples

The golden hamster, or Mesocricetus auratus, is a popular pet around the world.
Several species in the genus Mesocricetus are used in laboratory research.
Wild Mesocricetus populations are found in parts of southeastern Europe and western Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hamster - rodent in the subfamily Cricetinae, often kept as a pet

Etymology

From Greek 'mesos' (middle) and 'kricetos' (hamster).
