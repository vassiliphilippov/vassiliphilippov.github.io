Conradina glabra

1. NOUN

/ kɒn.rəˈdiː.nə ˈɡlæb.rə / (AmE), / kɒn.rəˈdiː.nə ˈɡlæb.rə / (BrE)
A small, aromatic shrub native to the Apalachicola River area in the southeastern United States, bearing pinkish flowers and classified as a threatened species.
an aromatic shrub of the Apalachicola area (US)

Examples

Scientists are working to protect the endangered Conradina glabra from habitat loss and invasive species.
The leaves of Conradina glabra release a strong scent when crushed.
Botanists documented the remaining Conradina glabra populations along the Apalachicola River.

Etymology

Genus name from the surname Conrad; species from Latin 'glabra' meaning 'smooth' or 'hairless'.
