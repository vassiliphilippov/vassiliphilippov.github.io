stinkpot

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋkˌpɑt / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkˌpɒt / (BrE)
a small North American freshwater turtle (Sternotherus odoratus) known for releasing a strong musky odor when disturbed
small musky North American turtle

Examples

The stinkpot is a common sight in the shallow lakes and ponds of North America.
When threatened, the stinkpot releases a pungent odor as a defense mechanism.
Many people are surprised by the strong smell a stinkpot can produce.

Etymology

From 'stink' + 'pot', referencing the turtle's odor.
