quadrature

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑː.drə.tʃər / (AmE), / ˈkwɒd.rə.tjʊər / (BrE)
the mathematical process of constructing a square with an area equal to that of a given figure
constructing a square with equal area to a given figure

Examples

The ancient Greeks studied quadrature as they tried to square the circle.
In geometry, quadrature involves finding a square with the same area as a given curvilinear figure.
Archimedes worked on the quadrature of the parabola using a geometric approach.

Etymology

From Latin 'quadratura', from 'quadrare' (to make square).
