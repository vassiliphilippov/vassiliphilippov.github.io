Halimodendron halodendron

1. NOUN

/ ˌhælɪmoʊˈdɛndrɒn ˌhælɪˈdɛndrɒn / (AmE), / ˌhalɪməˈdɛndrɒn ˌhalɪˈdɛndrɒn / (BrE)
A spiny shrub native to the Caspian salt plains and Siberia, with silvery, downy young leaves and mildly fragrant pink-purple flowers.
spiny shrub of Caspian salt plains and Siberia

Examples

The dry landscape was dotted with Halimodendron halodendron, their pink blooms standing out against the sand.
Botanists surveyed the Halimodendron halodendron population on the Caspian salt plains for conservation efforts.
The silvery foliage of Halimodendron halodendron is prized in some gardens for its ornamental beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea shrub - a shrub in the pea family; similar in appearance or function

Etymology

From Greek halimos (salt) + dendron (tree), referring to its salt-tolerant nature.
