glutamic oxalacetic transaminase

1. NOUN

/ ɡluˈtæmɪk ˌɑksəˌlæsɪtɪk trænsˈæmɪˌneɪs / (AmE), / ɡluˈtæmɪk ˌɒksələˌsɪtɪk trænsˈæmɪˌneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme found in the liver and other tissues that catalyzes the transfer of amino groups, commonly measured to assess liver function
an enzyme measured in liver function tests
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a blood test to check the level of glutamic oxalacetic transaminase for possible liver damage.
Elevated glutamic oxalacetic transaminase is often an indicator of hepatitis or other liver conditions.
Scientists measured glutamic oxalacetic transaminase activity to assess the effect of the new drug on liver function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspartate aminotransferase - another name for glutamic oxalacetic transaminase, referring to the same enzyme
  • Degree Lower: alanine aminotransferase - a related enzyme involved in transamination, also used in liver function tests

Etymology

From scientific root words: glutamic (from glutamic acid), oxalacetic (from oxaloacetic acid), and transaminase (enzyme that transfers amino groups).
