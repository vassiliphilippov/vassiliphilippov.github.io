mendicity

1. NOUN

/ mɛnˈdɪsəti / (AmE), / mɛnˈdɪsɪti / (BrE)
the condition or practice of begging; the state of being a beggar
the state of being a beggar

Examples

After losing his job and home, he found himself in a state of mendicity.
In the 19th century, laws against mendicity were strictly enforced in some cities.
Her research focused on the causes and consequences of urban mendicity.

Synset Examples

they were reduced to mendicancy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: begging - the act of asking people for money or food

Etymology

From Late Latin mendicitas (begging, poverty), from mendicus (beggar).
