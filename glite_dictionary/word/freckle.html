freckle

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛk.əl / (AmE), / ˈfrɛk.əl / (BrE)
A small, light brown spot on the skin, often becoming more pronounced from exposure to sunlight.
small brownish skin spot

Examples

She has a cute freckle on the tip of her nose.
After a day in the sun, more freckles appeared on his cheeks.
His face was dotted with freckles from childhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blemish - a small mark or flaw on the skin

Etymology

From Middle English 'freken' or 'freckel', probably from Old Norse 'freknur' (freckles).

2. VERB

/ ˈfrɛkəl / (AmE), / ˈfrɛkəl / (BrE)
to mark or become marked with small brown spots on the skin, known as freckles
to become or make marked with freckles

Examples

The summer sun caused her arms to freckle more than usual.
His fair skin tends to freckle when exposed to sunlight.
As a child, her nose would quickly freckle after a day outside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speckle - to mark with small spots or patches of color

Etymology

From Middle English 'freken' (to sprinkle with freckles), from 'freken' (freckle), of Scandinavian origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00510938-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈfrɛkəl / (AmE), / ˈfrɛkəl / (BrE)
(of skin) to develop small brownish spots as a result of exposure to the sun.
develop small brownish spots on skin

Examples

My arms tend to freckle after just a few hours in the sun.
Some people freckle more easily than others.
If you don't use sunscreen, your nose might freckle during the summer.

Synset Examples

I freckle easily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot - to mark with small round marks (used in broader sense)

Etymology

From Middle English 'frekel' (freckle), probably from Old Norse 'freknǫttr'.
