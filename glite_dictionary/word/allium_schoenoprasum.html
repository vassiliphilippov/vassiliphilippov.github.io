Allium schoenoprasum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm ˌskoʊnəˈpreɪsəm / (AmE), / ˈæliəm ˌskiːnəˈprɑːsəm / (BrE)
A species of perennial plant with hollow cylindrical leaves, commonly known as chive, used for seasoning foods.
species of chive used for seasoning

Examples

The chef garnished the soup with freshly chopped Allium schoenoprasum to add a mild onion flavor.
Many gardeners cultivate Allium schoenoprasum for its hardiness and usefulness in cooking.
The fine green leaves of Allium schoenoprasum are a nutritious addition to salads and omelets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chive - common name for Allium schoenoprasum, a culinary herb

Etymology

From Latin: allium (garlic) + schoenoprasum (from Greek skhoinos 'rush' + prason 'leek').
