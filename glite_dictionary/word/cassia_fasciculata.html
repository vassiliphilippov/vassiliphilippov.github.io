Cassia fasciculata

1. PROPN

/ ˌkæsiə fæˌsɪkjʊˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˌkæsiə fæsɪkjuˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A tropical American plant with somewhat sensitive leaflets, sometimes classified in the genus Cassia.
a species of tropical American plant

Examples

Cassia fasciculata is often found growing wild along roadsides in southern states.
The yellow flowers of Cassia fasciculata attract many pollinating insects.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Cassia fasciculata as an ornamental plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Senna fasciculata - Alternative botanical classification for Cassia fasciculata

Etymology

From Latin: cassia (cinnamon-like plant) and fasciculata (clustered), botanical Latin name.
