furuncle

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjʊr.ʌŋ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈfjʊə.rʌŋ.kəl / (BrE)
A painful boil with a hard core filled with pus, usually caused by bacterial infection of a hair follicle.
painful pus-filled boil
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the swollen lump on his leg as a furuncle and prescribed antibiotics.
A furuncle can be quite painful if left untreated, sometimes requiring medical intervention.
After a week of discomfort, the furuncle finally drained and began to heal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boil - a painful, pus-filled bump on the skin

Etymology

From Latin 'furunculus', meaning a petty thief or boil.
