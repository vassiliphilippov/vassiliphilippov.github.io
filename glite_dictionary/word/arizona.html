Arizona

1. PROPN

/ ˌɛrɪˈzoʊnə / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈzəʊnə / (BrE)
A state in the southwestern United States, known for the Grand Canyon and its desert landscape.
southwestern U.S. state

Examples

I have always dreamed of hiking through Arizona's beautiful national parks.
The climate in Arizona can be extremely hot during the summer.
Many people visit Arizona each year to see the Grand Canyon.

Etymology

Name possibly derived from Spanish *Arizonac*, a place name of uncertain meaning.

2. NOUN

/ əˈrɪzoʊnə / (AmE), / əˈrɪzəʊnə / (BrE)
A genus of snakes in the Colubridae family, commonly known as glossy snakes, native to North America.
a genus of North American glossy snakes

Examples

The Arizona snakes are known for their shiny, smooth scales.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Arizona genus in the desert.
The Arizona genus is distinguished by its elongated body and subtle coloration.

Etymology

Named after the U.S. state of Arizona, where the snakes are found.
