egoist

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːɡoʊɪst / (AmE), / ˈiːɡəʊɪst / (BrE)
a person who is excessively conceited or self-centered
a conceited, self-centered person

Examples

Many people found the celebrity to be an egoist who always talked about himself.
John's reputation as an egoist made it hard for him to maintain close friendships.
She accused him of being an egoist who never considered anyone else's feelings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egotist - a person who is excessively self-absorbed or boastful
  • Antonym: altruist - a person who is unselfishly concerned for the welfare of others

Etymology

From French *égoïste*, derived from Latin *ego* (I).

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːɡoʊɪst / (AmE), / ˈɛɡəʊɪst / (BrE)
a person who is self-centered and shows little concern for others
self-centered person

Examples

The company suffered under the leadership of an egoist who cared only about his own success.
It's difficult to have a deep relationship with an egoist, as everything revolves around them.
During the meeting, he was criticized for behaving like an egoist and ignoring the team's ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egotist - a person who is excessively conceited or self-absorbed
  • Antonym: altruist - a person who has an unselfish concern for the welfare of others

Etymology

From French égoïste, from Latin ego (I) plus -ist (person who practices).
