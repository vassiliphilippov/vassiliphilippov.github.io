Lepus californicus

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːpəs ˌkælɪˈfɔrnɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈliːpəs ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkəs / (BrE)
a species of jackrabbit native to grasslands and open areas of the western United States, characterized by large black-tipped ears and a black-streaked tail
species of jackrabbit in western US

Examples

The Lepus californicus can often be seen bounding through the deserts of the American Southwest.
Researchers tracked the movements of Lepus californicus during a population study in Nevada.
With its striking ears and speed, Lepus californicus easily escapes most predators in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jackrabbit - a large North American hare with long legs and ears

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lepus' meaning hare, 'californicus' meaning of California.
