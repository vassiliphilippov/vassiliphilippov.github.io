Clintonia

1. NOUN

/ klɪnˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / klɪnˈtəʊnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbaceous plants in the lily family, native to North America and eastern Asia, known for their broad leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers.
a genus of plants in the lily family

Examples

Clintonia species are commonly found in cool, shady woodlands across North America.
The delicate blue berries of Clintonia make it a recognizable forest plant.
Botanists debate whether Clintonia should be placed in the lily family or the Convallariaceae family.

Etymology

Named after DeWitt Clinton (1769–1828), American politician and naturalist.
