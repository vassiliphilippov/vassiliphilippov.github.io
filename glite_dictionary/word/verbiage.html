verbiage

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜr.bi.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvəː.bɪ.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
the use of too many words, or of more words than are needed to express an idea; excessive wordiness
excessive wordiness

Examples

The report was full of unnecessary verbiage that made it difficult to understand.
The contract's legal verbiage confused the client.
Try to avoid too much verbiage and keep your answer concise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wordiness - using more words than necessary

Etymology

From French 'verbiage', from 'verbe' (word), from Latin 'verbum' (word).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɜːr.bi.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.bɪ.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
the manner or style in which something is expressed in words, especially with regard to excessive or complicated wording
manner of expression in words

Examples

The legal document was filled with confusing verbiage that made it hard to understand.
She asked her colleague to reduce the verbiage in his report and keep it direct.
His explanation was lost in unnecessary verbiage, making a simple idea seem complex.

Synset Examples

use concise military verbiage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wording - the choice and arrangement of words in speech or writing

Etymology

From French 'verbiage' (wordiness), from 'verbe' (word), from Latin 'verbum' (word).
