belt-fed

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛlt fɛd / (AmE), / ˈbɛlt fɛd / (BrE)
(of a firearm) supplied with ammunition by a linked belt rather than individual magazines or clips.
supplied with ammunition by a belt

Examples

The machine gun was belt-fed, allowing for sustained bursts of fire during the battle.
Many military weapons remain belt-fed to maximize their rate of fire and reduce reload times.
He demonstrated how to properly load a belt-fed firearm at the training course.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: magazine-fed - supplied with ammunition by a magazine
