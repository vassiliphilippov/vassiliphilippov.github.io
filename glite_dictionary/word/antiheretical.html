antiheretical

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ti.həˈrɛt.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.həˈrɛt.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Opposed to heresy or to heretical beliefs; against doctrines considered to deviate from established religious teachings.
opposed to heresy

Examples

The council issued several antiheretical decrees to preserve orthodox doctrine.
In the Middle Ages, antiheretical campaigns were common throughout Europe.
His writings reveal a strongly antiheretical stance against new sects.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heretical - holding beliefs contrary to orthodox religious doctrine

Etymology

From anti- (against) + heretical (from Greek 'hairetikos', able to choose).
