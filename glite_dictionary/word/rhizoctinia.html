rhizoctinia

1. NOUN

/ raɪˌzoʊkˈtɪniə / (AmE), / raɪzˌɒkˈtɪniə / (BrE)
any fungus now or formerly classified in the form genus Rhizoctinia, often including plant pathogens.
fungus of the genus Rhizoctinia
medical

Examples

The agricultural researcher identified rhizoctinia as the cause of the crop disease.
Certain soil conditions favor the growth of rhizoctinia in greenhouses.
Treatments to control rhizoctinia are essential for protecting plant roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungus - a group of spore-producing organisms feeding on organic matter

Etymology

Derived from the genus name Rhizoctinia, itself from Greek 'rhiza' (root) and ‘ktinein’ (to kill).
