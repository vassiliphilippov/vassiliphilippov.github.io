Congridae

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˈɡraɪdi / (AmE), / kɒŋˈɡriːdiː / (BrE)
a family of elongated, mostly marine fish including conger eels and garden eels
family of conger and garden eels

Examples

The Congridae family includes many species found in tropical and temperate seas.
Researchers studied the distribution of Congridae along the ocean floor.
Some members of the Congridae are important to commercial fisheries.

Etymology

From modern Latin Congridae, from Conger (the type genus) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ kɑŋˈɡrɪdiː / (AmE), / kɒŋˈɡrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of elongated marine fishes that includes conger eels and garden eels
family of conger and garden eels

Examples

The Congridae are known for their elongated bodies and burrowing behavior.
Marine biologists often study members of the Congridae family to understand eel migration patterns.
Some species within the Congridae are prized catches for commercial fisheries.

Etymology

From New Latin *Congridae*, based on *Conger* (type genus) and Latin suffix *-idae* (denoting families).
