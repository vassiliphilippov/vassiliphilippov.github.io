nanotechnology

1. NOUN

/ ˌnænoʊtɛkˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌnanəʊtɛkˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of technology that deals with the design, production, and use of structures, devices, and systems by manipulating atoms and molecules at nanoscale (typically less than 100 nanometers).
engineering at the nanoscale

Examples

Nanotechnology is revolutionizing the field of medicine by enabling targeted drug delivery systems.
The company specializes in nanotechnology products used in electronics and materials science.
Many universities now offer courses and degrees focused on nanotechnology and its applications.

Etymology

From nano- (meaning ‘one billionth’) + technology.
