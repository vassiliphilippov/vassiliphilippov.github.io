barrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæriɚ / (AmE), / ˈbæriə / (BrE)
A circumstance or condition that prevents progress or makes it difficult to achieve something.
obstacle to progress or achievement

Examples

Language differences can be a barrier to effective communication.
Poverty remains a significant barrier to education for many children worldwide.
He is determined to overcome every barrier standing in his way.

Synset Examples

intolerance is a barrier to understanding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress or prevents movement or action
  • Synonym: hindrance - a thing that provides resistance or delay to something or someone
  • Antonym: aid - something that helps or assists progress

Etymology

From Old French *barriere* ‘obstacle’, from *barre* ‘bar’.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæriɚ / (AmE), / ˈbæriə / (BrE)
An object or structure that prevents movement, progress, or access, or keeps things separate by obstructing passage or view.
obstacle that blocks or separates

Examples

The tall fence acted as a barrier between the two neighboring gardens.
Fog can sometimes serve as a natural barrier to visibility on the road.
A language barrier made communication difficult for the new arrivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks one's way or hinders progress
  • Synonym: hindrance - a thing that provides resistance, delay, or obstruction to something or someone
  • Antonym: gateway - a means of access or entry

Etymology

From Old French 'barriere', from Vulgar Latin 'barraria', from 'barra' (bar).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09237488-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbæriər / (AmE), / ˈbæriə / (BrE)
A structure or object that prevents or restricts movement or access from one place to another.
something that blocks movement or access

Examples

A security barrier was erected around the festival grounds.
Tall hedges form a natural barrier between the houses.
The language difference proved to be a barrier to communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks or hinders progress
  • Synonym: obstruction - something that obstructs or blocks a passage
  • Antonym: gateway - an opening or entrance, especially when allowing passage through a barrier

Etymology

From Middle French *barriere*, from Old French *barre* (bar).
