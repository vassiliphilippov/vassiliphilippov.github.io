geomorphological

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒiː.əˌmɔr.fəˈlɑː.dʒɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əʊˌmɔː.fəˈlɒdʒ.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to the study of the physical features of the earth's surface and their relation to its geological structures
relating to the earth's physical landforms
medical

Examples

The researcher conducted a survey of the geomorphological changes along the riverbanks.
Understanding geomorphological processes is essential for predicting erosion patterns.
The geomorphological diversity of the region attracts many geologists and students.

Synset Examples

geomorphological features of the Black Hills
morphological features of granite
structural effects of folding and faulting of the earth's surface

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morphological - relating to the form or structure of something

Etymology

From 'geomorphology' (the study of landforms) + '-ical' (adjectival suffix). 'Geomorphology' derives from Greek 'geo-' (earth), 'morphē' (form), and '-logia' (study).
