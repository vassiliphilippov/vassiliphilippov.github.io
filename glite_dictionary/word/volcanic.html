volcanic

1. ADJ

/ vəˈkænɪk / (AmE), / vɒlˈkænɪk / (BrE)
(of rock) relating to or formed from magma erupted onto or near the earth's surface, as in basalt, andesite, or rhyolite.
formed from erupted magma (rock)

Examples

The island is largely composed of volcanic rock formed by ancient eruptions.
Obsidian is a type of volcanic glass often used for tools and ornaments.
Geologists collected samples of volcanic basalt from the mountain slope.

Synset Examples

volcanic rock includes the volcanic glass obsidian

Related Terms

  • Synonym: igneous - (of rocks) formed by solidification of molten magma
  • Antonym: plutonic - (of rock) formed by solidification of magma deep underground

Etymology

From Latin 'Volcanus' (Vulcan), the Roman god of fire.

2. ADJ

/ vɑlˈkænɪk / (AmE), / vɒlˈkænɪk / (BrE)
(of a person or feeling) liable to erupt suddenly or violently; explosively unstable.
prone to explosive outbursts (fig.)

Examples

She had a volcanic temper that frightened everyone in the office.
His volcanic personality often made social gatherings unpredictable.
Even small annoyances could trigger his volcanic outbursts.

Synset Examples

a volcanic temper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosive - likely to erupt with violence or suddenness (personality/feeling)
  • Antonym: placid - not easily upset or excited; calm in temperament

Etymology

From Latin 'volcanus', relating to Vulcan, Roman god of fire.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02301492-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ vɑlˈkænɪk / (AmE), / vɒlˈkænɪk / (BrE)
relating to or produced by a volcano or volcanoes; consisting of material ejected from volcanoes.
relating to volcanoes or volcanic material

Examples

The island was formed from volcanic eruptions millions of years ago.
Scientists measured the levels of volcanic ash in the atmosphere after the eruption.
The landscape is dotted with volcanic cones and craters.

Synset Examples

volcanic steam
volcanic islands such as Iceland
a volcanic cone is a conical mountain or hill built up of material from volcanic eruptions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: igneous - (of rock) formed by solidification from molten state, especially relating to volcanoes

Etymology

From Latin volcanicus, from Vulcanus (the Roman god of fire and volcanoes).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
