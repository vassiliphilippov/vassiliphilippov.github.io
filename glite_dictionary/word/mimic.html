mimic

1. VERB

/ ˈmɪmɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɪmɪk / (BrE)
to imitate someone or their manner, especially in order to entertain or mock
imitate, especially for effect

Examples

She loved to mimic her teacher's voice to make her classmates laugh.
The comedian perfectly mimicked famous politicians during the show.
Children often mimic the gestures and expressions of adults around them.

Synset Examples

The actor mimicked the President very accurately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imitate - to copy the behavior or actions of someone
  • Synonym: mock - to imitate for the purpose of ridicule

Etymology

From Greek mīmikos (imitative), from mimos (imitator, actor).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01747412-v
Synset POS: v

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɪmɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɪmɪk / (BrE)
imitating or resembling something, especially in order to appear like it; constituting an imitation.
imitating or resembling something

Examples

The performance featured dazzling mimic battles to create a sense of historical realism.
Some insects have developed mimic coloration to resemble dangerous species and avoid predators.
The museum displayed mimic artifacts created to give visitors a lifelike sense of the originals.

Synset Examples

the mimic warfare of the opera stage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitative - tending to imitate or copy something
  • Antonym: authentic - genuine; not a copy or imitation

Etymology

From Greek mīmikos (imitative, mimicking), via Latin mimicus.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01219652-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪmɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɪmɪk / (BrE)
a person who imitates the actions, appearance, or speech of others, especially for entertainment
person who imitates others

Examples

The talented mimic captured the prime minister's peculiar gestures perfectly.
She is a gifted mimic who can imitate almost any accent.
At the party, the mimic had everyone laughing with his impressions of famous actors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impersonator - a person who imitates someone else, especially for entertainment
  • Synonym: imitator - a person who copies the behavior or actions of others

Etymology

From Greek mimikos (imitative), from mimos (mime, actor).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10338821-n
Synset POS: n
