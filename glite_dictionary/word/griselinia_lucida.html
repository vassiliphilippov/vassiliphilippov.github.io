Griselinia lucida

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪˌsɛlɪˈniːə ˈluːsɪdə / (AmE), / ɡrɪˌsɛlɪˈniːə ˈluːsɪdə / (BrE)
A South American shrub or small tree with long, shiny evergreen leaves and panicles of green or yellow flowers.
a South American evergreen shrub or tree

Examples

The garden was home to a striking Griselinia lucida with glossy leaves and delicate flowers.
Botanists admire Griselinia lucida for its resilience and year-round greenery.
We planted a Griselinia lucida near the patio to provide natural shade and color.

Etymology

From genus name Griselinia, named after Italian botanist Francesco Griselini, and Latin lucida meaning 'shining' or 'bright'.
