palaeoclimatology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪl.i.oʊˌklaɪ.məˈtɑː.lə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌpal.i.əʊˌklaɪ.məˈtɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
The scientific study of the climate of past geological ages based on evidence from rocks, fossils, and other natural records.
study of ancient climates

Examples

Research in palaeoclimatology helps scientists understand how Earth's climate has changed over millions of years.
She is writing her thesis on palaeoclimatology and its implications for modern climate change.
Advances in palaeoclimatology have provided new insights into ancient ice ages and warming periods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paleoclimatology - the American or alternative spelling for palaeoclimatology

Etymology

From Greek 'palaios' (ancient) + 'clima' (region, climate) + '-logy' (study of).
