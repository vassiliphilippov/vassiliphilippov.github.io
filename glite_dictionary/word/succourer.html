succourer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌkɚər / (AmE), / ˈsʌkərə / (BrE)
a person who gives help or assistance to someone in need, distress, or difficulty
someone who gives help or aid
archaic

Examples

During the famine, the villagers saw her as their succourer in a time of great need.
In ancient tales, a mysterious succourer would often appear to help lost travelers.
The charity worker became a succourer for countless people facing hardship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helper - a person who helps someone else
  • Synonym: rescuer - a person who saves someone from a dangerous situation
  • Antonym: oppressor - someone who keeps others in hardship, usually unjustly

Etymology

From Old French *socorreur*, from Late Latin *succurator*, from Latin *succurrere* (to help, to run to help).
