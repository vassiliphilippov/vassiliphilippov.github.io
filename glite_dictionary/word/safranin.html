safranin

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæfrənɪn / (AmE), / ˈsæfrənɪn / (BrE)
any of a class of chiefly red synthetic dyes used especially in biological staining and microscopy
red organic dye used in science

Examples

The histologist used safranin to stain the cartilage cells for microscopic examination.
Some bacteria are identified by their ability to retain safranin in differential staining techniques.
Because of its vivid color, safranin is popular in various laboratory staining protocols.

Etymology

From French 'safranine', from 'safran' (saffron).
