Pilularia

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈluːləriə / (AmE), / pɪˈljuːləriə / (BrE)
a small genus of aquatic ferns known as pillworts, characterized by slender, grass-like leaves and small, round sporocarps
a genus of aquatic ferns (pillworts)

Examples

Pilularia is commonly found in shallow ponds and marshes across Europe and North America.
Scientists identified a new species in the genus Pilularia during their field study.
The delicate structure of Pilularia makes it sensitive to environmental changes in wetland ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pillwort - an aquatic fern of the genus Pilularia

Etymology

From Latin pilularia (resembling a small pill), referring to the round sporocarps.

2. NOUN

/ pɪˈluːləriə / (AmE), / pɪˈljʊələriə / (BrE)
A genus of small aquatic ferns known as pillworts, characterized by thread-like leaves and pill-shaped sporocarps.
a genus of aquatic ferns (pillworts)

Examples

The Pilularia genus is commonly found in shallow ponds and wetlands.
Botanists studied several species within the Pilularia genus to understand their ecological roles.
The thread-like leaves of Pilularia make it easily distinguishable from other ferns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - A member of a group of non-flowering vascular plants

Etymology

From Latin 'pilula' (small ball or pill) + '-aria' (suffix forming taxonomic names), referring to the spherical sporocarps.
