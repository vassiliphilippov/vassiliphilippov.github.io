senioritis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːniəˈraɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌsiːniəˈraɪtɪs / (BrE)
A decline in motivation or effort by students in their final year of high school or college, often due to anticipation of graduation.
loss of motivation in senior year
informal american

Examples

Many students experience senioritis as graduation approaches and their motivation drops.
My brother's grades slipped in his final semester because of senioritis.
The school counselor gave tips to help students fight senioritis in their last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burnout - a state of physical or mental exhaustion caused by prolonged stress

Etymology

From 'senior' (final-year student) + the medical suffix '-itis' (inflammation).
