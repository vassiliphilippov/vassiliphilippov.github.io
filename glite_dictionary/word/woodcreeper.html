woodcreeper

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌkriːpər / (AmE), / ˈwʊdˌkriːpə / (BrE)
any of several Central and South American birds of the subfamily Dendrocolaptinae, with a curved bill and stiffened tail feathers, that use their tails for support while climbing tree trunks and feeding much like woodpeckers
Central/South American climbing bird

Examples

The woodcreeper uses its stiff tail feathers to support itself as it climbs tree trunks in search of insects.
We spotted a woodcreeper darting along a branch during our hike in the Amazon rainforest.
Many species of woodcreeper have uniquely shaped, curved bills adapted to extracting prey from bark.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌkriːpər / (AmE), / ˈwʊdˌkriːpə / (BrE)
Any of various South and Central American birds of the subfamily Dendrocolaptinae, known for their curved bills and stiff tail feathers, which help them climb tree trunks in a manner similar to woodpeckers.
a climbing bird of Central and South America

Examples

The woodcreeper uses its stiff tail feathers to support itself as it climbs tree trunks.
We spotted a woodcreeper searching for insects in the dense rainforest undergrowth.
Unlike woodpeckers, the woodcreeper relies more on its curved bill to probe under bark.

Etymology

From 'wood' + 'creeper', referring to their habit of climbing trees in forests.
