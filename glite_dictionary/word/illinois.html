Illinois

1. PROPN

/ ɪlɪˈnɔɪ / (AmE), / ɪlɪˈnɔɪ / (BrE)
a state in the midwestern region of the United States, known for its largest city, Chicago, and its diverse landscape
a midwestern U.S. state

Examples

He grew up in Illinois before moving to New York for college.
The city of Chicago is the largest in Illinois.
Many tourists visit Illinois to see its beautiful state parks.

Etymology

From French, named after the Illinois people, a Native American group; ultimately from Algonquian.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪləˈnɔɪ / (AmE), / ˌɪlɪˈnɔɪ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken historically by the Illinois and Miami peoples
Algonquian language of Illinois and Miami peoples

Examples

Linguists have worked to reconstruct the Illinois language from historical documents.
Few people speak Illinois today, but efforts to revive it are ongoing.
The Illinois language is closely related to Miami and other Algonquian languages.

Etymology

From French, adaptation of indigenous self-designation in Algonquian.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪləˈnɔɪ / (AmE), / ˌɪlɪˈnɔɪ / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people formerly inhabiting Illinois and regions to the west.
member of a Native American people

Examples

The Illinois played a significant role in the fur trade along the Mississippi River.
She studied the customs of the Illinois while researching Indigenous peoples of the Midwest.
Many descendants of the Illinois now live in Oklahoma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Illiniwek - the confederation of tribes collectively known as the Illinois

Etymology

From French, from Algonquian (Miami-Illinois) 'ilinweewa', meaning 'the people'.
