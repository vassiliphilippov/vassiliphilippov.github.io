Henry VI

1. PROPN

/ ˌhɛnri sɪksθ / (AmE), / ˌhɛnri sɪksθ / (BrE)
(1421–1471) King of England from 1422 to 1461 and again from 1470 to 1471, known for his role in the Wars of the Roses and eventual murder in the Tower of London.
King of England (1422–1461, 1470–1471)

Examples

Henry VI became king as an infant after his father's death in 1422.
Many plays by Shakespeare dramatize the turbulent reign of Henry VI.
The capture and murder of Henry VI marked a turning point in English history.
