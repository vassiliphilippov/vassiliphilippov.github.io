Garamycin

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡærəˈmaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌɡærəˈmaɪsɪn / (BrE)
A trademarked name for gentamicin, an antibiotic used to treat various bacterial infections, especially of the urinary tract.
trademarked antibiotic (gentamicin)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Garamycin to treat her urinary tract infection.
Allergic reactions to Garamycin are rare but possible.
Hospital staff administered Garamycin intravenously to control the infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentamicin - an antibiotic used to treat bacterial infections
