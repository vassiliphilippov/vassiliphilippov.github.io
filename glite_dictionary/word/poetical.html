poetical

1. ADJ

/ poʊˈɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / pəʊˈɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of poetry or poets; having qualities appropriate to poetry.
relating to poetry

Examples

Her writing has a distinctly poetical quality that sets it apart from others.
The architecture of the old cathedral has a poetical beauty.
He delivered a poetical speech that captivated the entire audience.

Synset Examples

poetic works
a poetic romance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poetic - relating to or characteristic of poetry

Etymology

From late Middle English, from Old French *poetique* or Latin *poeticus*, with the English adjectival suffix -al.

2. ADJ

/ poʊˈɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / pəʊˈɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
having qualities that are characteristic of poetry; suitable for or resembling poetry in style or feeling.
resembling or suitable for poetry

Examples

The author's poetical language brought the landscape vividly to life.
Many enjoyed the poetical atmosphere of the old garden.
Her poetical descriptions made every moment seem magical.

Synset Examples

poetic diction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poetic - having the qualities or form of poetry

Etymology

Formed from 'poet', on the pattern of 'poetical', from Late Latin 'poeticus' (poetical).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02025355-s
Synset POS: s
