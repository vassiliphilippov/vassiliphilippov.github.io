rubberneck

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌbərˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈrʌbənɛk / (BrE)
a person who stares or gawks inquisitively, especially at the scene of an accident or something unusual
person who gawks inquisitively
informal american

Examples

A group of rubbernecks gathered at the scene of the accident, blocking traffic.
Tourists are often seen as rubbernecks when they stare at New York's skyscrapers.
The police had to shoo away the rubbernecks so emergency workers could do their job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onlooker - a person who watches something without taking part
  • Synonym: bystander - a person present at an event but not involved

Etymology

From 'rubber' (elastic material) + 'neck', referring to the idea of craning one's neck to look at something out of curiosity. American English, late 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌbərˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈrʌbənɛk / (BrE)
an overly curious person, especially a tourist, who stares at sights or events of interest
an overly curious onlooker or tourist
informal american

Examples

The bus was filled with rubbernecks eager to see every famous landmark in the city.
Local drivers complained that rubbernecks slowed down traffic near the accident.
As a typical rubberneck, he snapped photos at every stop on the tour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sightseer - a person who visits interesting places, especially as a tourist

Etymology

From 'rubber' (implying flexibility) + 'neck', referring to craning one's neck to look.

3. VERB

/ ˈrʌbərˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈrʌbəˌnɛk / (BrE)
To look or stare with great curiosity, especially at something unusual or interesting, often in a way that slows progress or is considered intrusive.
stare curiously or intrusively

Examples

Several tourists stopped to rubberneck at the car crash by the roadside.
Please don't rubberneck when passing the construction site; it's unsafe.
Drivers tend to rubberneck during major accidents, causing further traffic delays.

Synset Examples

The cars slowed down and the drivers rubbernecked after the accident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gawk - to stare openly and stupidly
  • Synonym: to ogle - to stare at in a lecherous manner
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention

Etymology

From 'rubber' (implying flexibility) + 'neck', suggesting craning one's neck to look.
