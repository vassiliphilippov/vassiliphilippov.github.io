faultfinding

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔltˌfaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːltˌfaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
inclined to criticize others, especially on moral or personal grounds
inclined to criticize others

Examples

His faultfinding attitude made it difficult for others to enjoy working with him.
She tried to ignore her colleague's faultfinding remarks during the meeting.
A good leader avoids being faultfinding and instead encourages positive change.

Synset Examples

a counselor tries not to be faultfinding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critical - inclined to find fault or express disapproval
  • Antonym: tolerant - showing willingness to allow the existence of opinions or behavior that one does not necessarily agree with

Etymology

From 'fault' + 'finding', originally meaning the act of finding faults, later used adjectivally.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɔltˌfaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːltˌfaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
always finding or pointing out mistakes, especially in a critical or negative way
habitually critical; tending to find fault

Examples

The manager's faultfinding attitude made it difficult for the team to stay motivated.
Her faultfinding comments often discouraged those around her from sharing their ideas.
Despite his success, he had to endure the faultfinding remarks of his critics.

Synset Examples

a captious pedant
an excessively demanding and faultfinding tutor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critical - inclined to judge severely and find faults
  • Antonym: forgiving - ready to overlook faults and not criticize
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00650889-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɔltˌfaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːltˌfaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
the habit of continually finding small or unjustified faults or defects in others or things; persistent petty criticism
habit of persistent, petty criticism

Examples

Her constant faultfinding made it difficult to enjoy working with her.
The manager's faultfinding discouraged new ideas within the team.
He was criticized for his faultfinding attitude rather than offering constructive feedback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval or the analysis and judgment of works

Etymology

From 'fault' + 'finding'; attested since the 17th century.
