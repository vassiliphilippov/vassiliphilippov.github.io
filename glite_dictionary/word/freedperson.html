freedperson

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriːdˌpɜːrsən / (AmE), / ˈfriːdˌpɜːsən / (BrE)
A person who has been released from slavery.
formerly enslaved person

Examples

After the war, many freedpeople established new communities in the South.
The rights of freedpersons were often denied despite legal emancipation.
Historians study the lives of freedpeople to understand the transition from slavery to freedom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freedman - a man who has been freed from slavery
  • Antonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and forced to obey them

Etymology

Formed from 'freed' (past participle of 'free') + 'person'. Modeled after 'freedman' and 'freedwoman'.
