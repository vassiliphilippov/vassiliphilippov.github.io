Trachipterus arcticus

1. NOUN

/ trəˌkɪptərəs ɑrˈktɪkəs / (AmE), / trəˌkɪptərəs ɑːkˈtɪkəs / (BrE)
A species of deep-sea ribbonfish found in northern Atlantic waters, known for its long, slender body and silvery appearance.
a species of deep-sea ribbonfish

Examples

The Trachipterus arcticus is often found at great depths in the northern Atlantic Ocean.
Researchers recently documented a sighting of Trachipterus arcticus near the coast of Norway.
With its ribbon-like body, the Trachipterus arcticus is easily distinguished from other deep-sea fish.

Etymology

From Greek *trachys* (rough) and *pteron* (fin), Latin *arcticus* (northern, of the Arctic).
