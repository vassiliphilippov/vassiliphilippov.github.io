mirage

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈrɑʒ / (AmE), / mɪˈrɑːʒ / (BrE)
an idea, hope, or wish that seems real or possible but is in fact illusory or impossible to achieve
something illusory or unattainable

Examples

For many, the promise of easy wealth is just a mirage that always remains out of reach.
His hopes for a perfect life turned out to be nothing more than a mirage.
Freedom seemed like a distant mirage as they struggled under the oppressive regime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - a deceptive appearance or impression
  • Synonym: chimera - a hope or dream that is extremely unlikely or impossible
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From French 'mirage', from 'mirer' (to look at), from Latin 'mirari' (to wonder at).

2. NOUN

/ məˈrɑʒ / (AmE), / mɪˈrɑːʒ / (BrE)
An optical illusion caused by atmospheric conditions, especially the appearance of a sheet of water in a desert or on a hot road, caused by the refraction of light from the sky by heated air.
optical illusion caused by atmospheric refraction

Examples

Driving through the desert, we saw a shimmering mirage that looked like a lake in the distance.
The oasis turned out to be nothing more than a mirage created by the heat.
After hiking for hours, the exhausted travelers kept chasing a mirage that was never actually there.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - something that deceives by producing a false or misleading impression

Etymology

From French 'mirage', from 'mirer' (to look at), from Latin 'mirari' (to wonder at).
