tuber

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːbər / (AmE), / ˈtjuːbə / (BrE)
a thick, fleshy, usually underground stem or root that stores food and serves in reproduction, as in the potato
fleshy underground stem or root

Examples

The potato is a common type of tuber consumed worldwide.
During the harvest, farmers unearthed a large tuber hidden beneath the soil.
Some plants use their tuber to survive through harsh winters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhizome - a continuously growing horizontal underground stem
  • Synonym: bulb - an underground storage organ with fleshy leaves or leaf bases

Etymology

From Latin tuber (a swelling, lump, or bump).
