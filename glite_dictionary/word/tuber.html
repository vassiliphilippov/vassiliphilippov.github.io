Tuber

1. PROPN

/ ˈtuːbər / (AmE), / ˈtjuːbə / (BrE)
a genus of fungi in the family Tuberaceae, whose fruiting bodies are known as truffles
a fungus genus including truffles

Examples

The black Tuber species is highly sought after by chefs for its aromatic qualities.
Scientists discovered a new Tuber species in the forests of northern Italy.
Many species of Tuber form symbiotic relationships with tree roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truffle - an edible subterranean fungus, a fruiting body of the genus Tuber

Etymology

From Latin *tuber* (lump, swelling, truffle).
