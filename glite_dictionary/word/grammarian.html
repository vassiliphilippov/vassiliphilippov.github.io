grammarian

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈmɛriən / (AmE), / ɡrəˈmeəriən / (BrE)
a person who specializes in the study or teaching of grammar and syntax
expert in grammar and syntax

Examples

As a grammarian, she was often consulted about complex language rules.
The grammarian corrected the common errors found in students' writing.
Modern technology has changed the role of the traditional grammarian in education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linguist - a person skilled in languages or the scientific study of language

Etymology

From Latin 'grammaticus', from Greek 'grammatikos' (pertaining to letters or grammar).
