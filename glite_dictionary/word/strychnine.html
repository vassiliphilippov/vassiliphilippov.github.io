strychnine

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪkˌnaɪn / (AmE), / ˈstrɪkniːn / (BrE)
a highly poisonous, bitter alkaloid obtained chiefly from the seeds of the nux vomica tree, formerly used as a stimulant and in medicine
a poisonous plant alkaloid

Examples

The presence of strychnine in the water source posed a serious health risk to the community.
He was hospitalized after accidentally ingesting a small amount of strychnine.
Laboratory tests confirmed that the substance was indeed strychnine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison - a substance that causes harm or death when introduced to the body

Etymology

From French *strychnine*, from New Latin *Strychnos* (the genus name of the nux vomica plant).
