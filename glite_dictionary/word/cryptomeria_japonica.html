Cryptomeria japonica

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˈmɪriə dʒəˈpɑnɪkə / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəˈmɪəriə dʒəˈpɒnɪkə / (BrE)
a tall evergreen tree native to Japan and China, valued for its soft, durable wood
Japanese evergreen tree with soft wood

Examples

The ancient temple was surrounded by towering Cryptomeria japonica trees.
Furniture made from Cryptomeria japonica wood is prized for its lightness and durability.
Gardeners in temperate regions often plant Cryptomeria japonica for its ornamental value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese cedar - a common name for Cryptomeria japonica

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'meros' (part) and Latin 'japonica' (of Japan), referring to its discovery in Japan.
