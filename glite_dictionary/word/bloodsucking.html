bloodsucking

1. ADJ

/ ˈblʌdˌsʌkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌsʌkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an animal or insect) drawing blood from the body of another living being in order to feed.
feeding by drawing blood

Examples

Mosquitoes are well-known bloodsucking insects that can spread disease.
He was bitten by several bloodsucking leeches while swimming in the lake.
Some bats are bloodsucking mammals that feed on livestock.

Synset Examples

a plague of bloodsucking insects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hematophagous - feeding on blood

2. ADJ

/ ˈblʌdˌsʌkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌsʌkɪŋ / (BrE)
Figurative: (of a person, company, etc.) exploiting or living off others in a parasitic way; Literal: (of an animal) feeding by sucking blood.
exploiting others; feeding by sucking blood

Examples

The landlord was accused of being a bloodsucking profiteer during the housing crisis.
Some companies have bloodsucking practices that exploit their employees.
A bloodsucking insect can transmit several diseases to humans.

Synset Examples

a wealthy class parasitic upon the labor of the masses
parasitic vines that strangle the trees
bloodsucking blackmailer
his indolent leechlike existence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parasitic - living as a parasite; exploitive
  • Synonym: leechlike - resembling or characteristic of a leech; exploiting others
  • Antonym: generous - showing kindness and willingness to give

Etymology

Formed from 'blood' + 'suck' + '-ing', literal and figurative use dates to the 17th century.
