neobehaviorism

1. NOUN

/ niː.oʊ.bɪˈheɪ.vjəˌrɪz.əm / (AmE), / niː.əʊ.bɪˈheɪ.vjə.rɪz.əm / (BrE)
a school of psychology that evolved from behaviorism, emphasizing experimental research and laboratory study of both observable behavior and internal processes such as thoughts and feelings
psychology school derived from behaviorism

Examples

Many modern psychologists have built upon neobehaviorism to incorporate studies of mental processes.
The rise of neobehaviorism marked a major shift in the focus of psychological research.
Skinner’s theories are often associated with neobehaviorism due to their experimental approach.

Synset Examples

Behaviorism underwent a remarkable development from the less sophisticated formulations of Watson to the brilliant neobehaviorism of Skinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: behaviorism - school of psychology focused on observable behavior

Etymology

From neo- (new) and behaviorism, indicating a new form of behaviorist psychology.
