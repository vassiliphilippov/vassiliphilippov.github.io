to commence

1. VERB

/ kəˈmɛns / (AmE), / kəˈmɛns / (BrE)
to begin; to start doing something, especially in a formal or official way
to begin; to start

Examples

The ceremony will commence at 10 a.m. sharp.
They commenced construction on the new bridge last month.
Once everyone was seated, the lecture commenced without delay.

Synset Examples

We began working at dawn
Who will start?
Get working as soon as the sun rises!
The first tourists began to arrive in Cambodia
He began early in the day
Let's get down to work now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to begin - to start doing something
  • Synonym: start - to begin doing or using something
  • Antonym: finish - to bring to an end or completion

Etymology

From Old French 'comencier', from Latin 'cominitiare' (to initiate) and 'cominitium' (beginning).

3. VERB

/ kəˈmɛns / (AmE), / kəˈmɛns / (BrE)
to begin or start something; to set in motion an action or process
to begin; to start

Examples

The ceremony will commence at noon, so please arrive on time.
They decided to commence construction of the new bridge next year.
She commenced her speech with a warm welcome to the audience.

Synset Examples

The U.S. started a war in the Middle East
The Iraqis began hostilities
begin a new chapter in your life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to begin - to do the first part of something; to start
  • Synonym: start - to begin to happen or exist, or to make something begin
  • Antonym: end - to bring or come to a conclusion

Etymology

From Old French 'comencier', from Latin 'cominitiare' (to initiate).
