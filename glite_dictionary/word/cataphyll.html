cataphyll

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬əˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈkatəˌfɪl / (BrE)
A reduced or rudimentary leaf, often appearing early in plant development, such as a cotyledon or scale leaf.
rudimentary or early-stage leaf in plants
medical

Examples

In many seedlings, the cataphyll serves as the plant's first protective leaf.
The cataphylls at the base of the stem eventually dry up and fall away.
Unlike true leaves, a cataphyll typically lacks chlorophyll and is not involved in photosynthesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotyledon - the first leaf or one of the first pair of leaves produced by the embryo of a seed plant

Etymology

From Greek kata- (down) + phyllon (leaf).
