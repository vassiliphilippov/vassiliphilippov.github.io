photosynthesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊˈsɪnθəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˈsɪnθəsɪs / (BrE)
The process by which green plants and some other organisms use sunlight to synthesize nutrients from carbon dioxide and water.
process in plants making food using sunlight

Examples

Photosynthesis is essential for life on Earth because it provides oxygen and food for many organisms.
Without photosynthesis, plants would not be able to create the energy they need to survive.
Scientists study photosynthesis to better understand how plants convert sunlight into chemical energy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chemosynthesis - the synthesis of organic compounds by bacteria or other living organisms using energy derived from reactions involving inorganic chemicals

Etymology

From Greek 'phōs' (light) + 'synthesis' (putting together).
