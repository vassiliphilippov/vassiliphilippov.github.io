pettifogger

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛtiˌfɑɡər / (AmE), / ˈpɛtifɒɡə / (BrE)
A person who quibbles over trivial matters or raises petty objections, especially in a legal context.
person who makes petty objections

Examples

The lawyer was dismissed as a pettifogger who wasted everyone's time with endless technicalities.
He had a reputation as a notorious pettifogger, always finding faults in every proposal.
Instead of addressing the main issue, she acted like a pettifogger and argued over minor details.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quibbler - a person who raises trivial objections or focuses on minor details

Etymology

From 'petty' (small) + 'fogger' (cheat, low-ranking lawyer), originally from Dutch *fokken* ('to swindle').

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛtiˌfɑɡər / (AmE), / ˈpɛtifɒɡə / (BrE)
a lawyer or politician who uses underhanded, unscrupulous, or unethical methods, especially in minor or petty matters
unscrupulous or unethical lawyer

Examples

The court was fed up with the pettifogger who constantly found loopholes and delayed proceedings.
He refused to be represented by a pettifogger who would stoop to any tactic to win.
Critics accused the politician of behaving like a pettifogger, twisting the facts for personal gain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shyster - dishonest lawyer or person using dubious methods
  • Antonym: honest lawyer - a lawyer acting with integrity and ethics

Etymology

From petty (of little importance) + fogger (a peddler or second-rate lawyer, from Middle Dutch 'fugger')
