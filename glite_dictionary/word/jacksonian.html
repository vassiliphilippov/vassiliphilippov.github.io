Jacksonian

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒækˈsoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌdʒækˈsəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to Andrew Jackson, his presidency, or his concept of popular democracy in the United States
relating to Andrew Jackson or his presidency

Examples

The senator supported Jacksonian policies during the debate on economic reform.
Historically, the Jacksonian era marked a shift toward greater democracy for the common man.
Many historians have analyzed the influence of Jacksonian ideals on American political traditions.

Etymology

From the name Andrew Jackson (7th U.S. President) + -ian (forming adjectives and nouns).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒækˈsoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌdʒækˈsəʊniən / (BrE)
a follower of Andrew Jackson or his political principles, particularly those associated with American democracy in the early 19th century
follower of Andrew Jackson

Examples

Many southern farmers identified as Jacksonians during the 1830s.
The candidate positioned himself as a modern Jacksonian in his campaign rhetoric.
Historians debate the impact that Jacksonians had on American democracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jeffersonian - a follower of Thomas Jefferson or his ideas

Etymology

From the name Andrew Jackson (1767–1845), 7th President of the United States, with suffix -ian forming nouns for followers or advocates.
