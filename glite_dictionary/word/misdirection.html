misdirection

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsdəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪsdɪˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
careless or inefficient management of resources, people, or efforts
careless or poor management

Examples

The company's failure was blamed on the misdirection of its limited resources.
Years of financial misdirection left the organization in debt.
Her efforts were hampered by the misdirection of the project team.

Synset Examples

he accomplished little due to the mismanagement of his energies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mismanagement - ineffective, careless, or corrupt management

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + direction, meaning guidance or management.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsdəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪsdɪˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
an incorrect instruction or guidance given to a jury by a judge, especially one that may affect the verdict
incorrect legal instruction to a jury
legal

Examples

The defense appealed the case on the grounds of misdirection by the trial judge.
Justice was compromised due to misdirection affecting the jury’s verdict.
The court found that misdirection had occurred during the summing up to the jury.

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + direction; legal use since the 18th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07184394-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsdəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪsdɪˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act or instance of giving wrong or misleading directions or instructions
giving wrong or misleading directions

Examples

The tourist became lost after following the misdirection given by a passerby.
Due to misdirection from the coach, the players ran the wrong play on the field.
The project's failure was partly due to misdirection from inexperienced supervisors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: guidance - advice or information aimed at resolving a problem or difficulty
  • Synonym: to mislead - to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression

Etymology

Formed from 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'direction'.

4. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsdəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪsdɪˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
The act of distracting someone or drawing their attention away from something important.
the act of distracting or diverting attention

Examples

The magician used clever misdirection to make the coin vanish without the audience noticing.
Her skillful misdirection prevented anyone from guessing her true intentions.
During the heist, misdirection played a crucial role in keeping the guards occupied.

Synset Examples

conjurers are experts at misdirection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distraction - something that draws attention away

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + direction.
