wryly

1. ADV

/ ˈraɪli / (AmE), / ˈraɪli / (BrE)
In a wry manner; expressing dry, especially mocking, humor or irony.
in a wry or ironic manner

Examples

He smiled wryly as he recounted the mishap to his friends.
"So much for best-laid plans," she remarked wryly after the event was canceled.
The author wryly noted the absurdity of modern office life in his book.

Synset Examples

‘I see,’ he commented wryly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ironically - in an ironic manner

Etymology

From "wry" + "-ly", where "wry" comes from Old English *wrīgian* (to turn, bend).
