Canna indica

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænə ɪnˈdikə / (AmE), / ˈkanə ɪnˈdiːkə / (BrE)
A species of canna plant cultivated particularly for its edible rootstock, which is used to produce arrowroot starch.
plant grown for edible rootstock/arrowroot starch

Examples

The root of Canna indica is processed to make arrowroot starch in several countries.
Farmers harvested Canna indica because it's both ornamental and useful for its edible rhizomes.
Some local cuisines use Canna indica as a gluten-free alternative in baking.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'Indian canna', with 'canna' from Greek 'kánna' (reed) and 'indica' meaning 'of India'.
