soleus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊliəs / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlɪəs / (BrE)
a broad, flat muscle in the calf of the leg, situated beneath the gastrocnemius muscle, involved in standing and walking
muscle in the calf of the leg
medical

Examples

The soleus is vital for maintaining balance while standing.
Injuries to the soleus can cause persistent calf pain in athletes.
During running, both the gastrocnemius and soleus muscles are heavily engaged.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gastrocnemius - the chief muscle of the calf of the leg, forming its bulk

Etymology

From Latin 'solea' (sandal), referencing its flat shape.
