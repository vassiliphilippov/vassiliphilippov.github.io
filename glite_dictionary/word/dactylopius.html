Dactylopius

1. NOUN

/ dækˈtɪləpiəs / (AmE), / dækˈtɪləpiəs / (BrE)
a genus of scale insects in the family Dactylopiidae, known for species used to produce cochineal dye
genus of scale insects (cochineal producers)

Examples

The insect genus Dactylopius is widely known for its role in producing cochineal dye.
Researchers discovered several new species within the Dactylopius genus in South America.
Cochineal, a natural red pigment, is derived from female Dactylopius insects.

Etymology

From New Latin Dactylopius, based on Greek daktylos (finger) + pisos (fat, slime), possibly referring to the insects’ appearance.

2. NOUN

/ dækˈtɪloʊpiəs / (AmE), / dækˈtɪləʊpiəs / (BrE)
A genus of scale insects in the family Dactylopiidae, including species such as the cochineal.
genus of scale insects (Dactylopiidae)

Examples

The genus Dactylopius includes insects that are valuable for producing natural dyes.
Scientists studied various species within the Dactylopius genus to understand their ecological roles.
The best-known member of Dactylopius is the cochineal insect, which is used for carmine dye.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Dactylopius', based on Greek 'daktylos' (finger) and 'ops' (appearance).
