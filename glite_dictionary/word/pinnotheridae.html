Pinnotheridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪnəˈθɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌpɪnəˈθɛrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of tiny, soft-bodied crabs, many of which live as commensals with mollusks or other marine animals.
family of tiny soft-bodied crabs

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Pinnotheridae family living inside clam shells.
The soft carapace of most Pinnotheridae makes them difficult to spot in their hosts.
Marine biologists often study Pinnotheridae to understand symbiotic relationships in the ocean.

Etymology

From New Latin *Pinnotheridae*, based on the type genus *Pinnotheres*.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɪnəˈθɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌpɪnəˈθɛrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of tiny, soft-bodied crabs that often live symbiotically inside other marine animals such as mollusks and echinoderms.
family of small, soft-bodied crabs
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Pinnotheridae family living inside local oysters.
Members of the Pinnotheridae are often referred to as pea crabs due to their small size.
The soft shells of Pinnotheridae help them adapt to life inside other marine animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea crab - a member of the Pinnotheridae family known for living inside bivalves

Etymology

From New Latin, from genus name Pinnotheres, itself from Greek 'pínna' (a mussel) + 'thēras' (hunter).
