cryptologist

1. NOUN

/ krɪpˈtɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / krɪpˈtɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person skilled in the study and analysis of codes, ciphers, and cryptograms
expert in codes and cryptography

Examples

Alan Turing was a brilliant cryptologist during World War II.
The government hired a cryptologist to work on decoding intercepted messages.
She became fascinated with puzzles and eventually pursued a career as a cryptologist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: codebreaker - someone who solves or deciphers codes
  • Synonym: cryptanalyst - an expert in analyzing and breaking codes and ciphers

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden) + -logist (one who studies).
