principality

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɪnsɪˈpæləti / (AmE), / ˌprɪnsɪˈpælɪti / (BrE)
A territory ruled by a prince, especially as a sovereign state.
territory ruled by a prince

Examples

The small European principality attracts thousands of tourists each year.
Monaco is a well-known principality on the Mediterranean coast.
In the Middle Ages, several principalities emerged following the breakdown of larger kingdoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duchy - territory ruled by a duke or duchess
  • Degree Higher: kingdom - a country, state, or territory ruled by a king or queen

Etymology

From Old French *principalité*, from Latin *principalitas* (first place, primacy).
