downfall

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌfɔl / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌfɔːl / (BrE)
A sudden loss of power, status, strength, or success.
sudden decline or collapse

Examples

The scandal led to the unexpected downfall of the once-popular politician.
His arrogance ultimately caused his downfall within the company.
The documentary explored the rapid downfall of the country's economic system.

Synset Examples

the fall of the House of Hapsburg

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - a sudden complete failure or breakdown
  • Synonym: demise - an end or death, especially of something powerful
  • Antonym: rise - an increase or upward movement in status or importance

Etymology

From Middle English 'downfal', from down + fall.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌfɔl / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.fɔːl / (BrE)
a sudden loss of position, power, or reputation; failure that leads to ruin or destruction
a sudden loss of position or reputation

Examples

His arrogance was ultimately the cause of his downfall.
The company's downfall shocked investors across the country.
After years at the top, a single mistake led to her sudden downfall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - the complete destruction of something; collapse or downfall
  • Synonym: collapse - a sudden failure or breakdown; loss of power, health, or success
  • Antonym: success - the accomplishment of aims or attainment of fame, wealth, or power

Etymology

From Old English 'dūn' (down) + 'feallan' (to fall).
