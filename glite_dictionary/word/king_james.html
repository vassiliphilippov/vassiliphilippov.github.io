King James

1. PROPN

/ kɪŋ ˈdʒeɪmz / (AmE), / kɪŋ ˈdʒeɪmz / (BrE)
the first Stuart king of England and Ireland from 1603 to 1625 and of Scotland from 1567 to 1625, known for asserting the divine right of kings and commissioning the King James Bible
Stuart king of England, Scotland, and Ireland (1566–1625)

Examples

King James succeeded Elizabeth I and united the crowns of England and Scotland.
The King James Version of the Bible was commissioned by him in 1604.
Historians debate whether King James effectively managed relations with Parliament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: James I - the same historical monarch referred to by full regnal number
