sympatric

1. ADJ

/ sɪmˈpætrɪk / (AmE), / sɪmˈpætrɪk / (BrE)
(of species or populations) occurring in the same or overlapping geographic areas, especially without physical barriers preventing interbreeding.
occupying the same geographical area (biology)
medical

Examples

Several closely related bird species are sympatric in this forest, sharing the same habitat.
Recent studies have shown that sympatric speciation can occur without geographic isolation.
The two frogs are sympatric, often found breeding in the same ponds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: allopatric - occurring in separate or non-overlapping geographic areas (biology)

Etymology

From Greek 'sympatris' (from 'syn' = together + 'patra' = native land).
