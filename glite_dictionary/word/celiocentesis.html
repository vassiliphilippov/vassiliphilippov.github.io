celiocentesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːli.oʊˈsɛntəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsiːli.əʊˈsɛntɪsɪs / (BrE)
a medical procedure involving the removal of fluid from the abdomen using a needle or trocar
removal of abdominal fluid by puncture
medical

Examples

The doctor performed a celiocentesis to relieve the patient's abdominal discomfort.
A celiocentesis is often required when unexplained abdominal swelling occurs.
After the celiocentesis, the collected fluid was sent to the laboratory for analysis.

Etymology

From Greek 'koilia' (belly) + 'kentēsis' (pricking).
