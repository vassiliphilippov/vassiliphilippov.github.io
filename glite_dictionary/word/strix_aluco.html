Strix aluco

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪks ˈæluˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈstrɪks ˈaluːkəʊ / (BrE)
A reddish-brown European owl with a round head and black eyes, also known as the tawny owl.
a European tawny owl species

Examples

The Strix aluco is often heard hooting at night in European woodlands.
Researchers tracked the movements of several Strix aluco individuals during their breeding season.
With its distinctive call, the Strix aluco is a familiar sound throughout rural Britain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tawny owl - common name for Strix aluco

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Strix' means 'owl' and 'aluco' refers to the tawny owl.
