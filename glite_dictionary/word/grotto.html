grotto

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡrɒtəʊ / (BrE)
a small picturesque cave, especially one with attractive or artificial features
small picturesque cave

Examples

The hidden grotto sparkled with crystals and cool, clear water.
Tourists flocked to see the famous sea grotto on the island's coast.
In the palace gardens, there was an artificial grotto decorated with shells and statues.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cave - a large natural underground chamber
  • Degree Higher: cavern - a large cave or a large chamber in a cave

Etymology

From Italian 'grotta', from Latin 'crypta' (vault, cave).
