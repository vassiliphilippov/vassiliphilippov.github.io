surtax

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜːrˌtæks / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.tæks / (BrE)
An additional tax imposed on certain types of income or transactions that have already been taxed.
additional tax levied on taxed income

Examples

The government decided to impose a surtax on luxury goods to increase revenue.
Residents earning above a certain threshold must pay an additional surtax each year.
Critics argue that the new surtax will disproportionately affect middle-income families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax - a compulsory financial charge imposed by a government

Etymology

From French surtaxe, from sur- (over) + taxe (tax).

2. VERB

/ ˈsɝː.tæks / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.tæks / (BrE)
to impose an additional tax on something, especially above existing taxes
impose an extra tax

Examples

The government decided to surtax imports of luxury vehicles to curb excessive spending.
If the new law passes, companies may be surtaxed on their record profits this year.
Some politicians propose to surtax sugary drinks as a public health measure.

Synset Examples

surtax luxury items that cost more than $1,000

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax - to impose a compulsory financial charge
  • Antonym: untax - to remove or exempt from tax

Etymology

From Latin 'sur-' (over) and 'taxa' (tax), via French 'surtaxe'.

Synset POS: v
