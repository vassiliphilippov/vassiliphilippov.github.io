yuga

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːɡə / (AmE), / ˈjuːɡə / (BrE)
(in Hindu philosophy) one of the four epochs or ages that make up a cycle in the history of the world.
epoch in Hindu cosmology

Examples

According to ancient texts, the Kali yuga is the present era marked by decline in righteousness.
Each yuga is said to be progressively shorter and less virtuous than the previous one.
The concept of a yuga plays a central role in Hindu cosmology and mythology.

Synset Examples

Like Summer, Spring, Winter and Autumn, each yuga involves stages or gradual changes which the earth and the consciousness of mankind goes through as a whole.

Etymology

From Sanskrit युग (yuga), meaning 'age' or 'era'.
