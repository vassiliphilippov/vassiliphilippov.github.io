carrion

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæriən / (AmE), / ˈkæriən / (BrE)
the decaying flesh of dead animals, especially when regarded as unfit for human consumption
decaying flesh of dead animals

Examples

Vultures often feed on carrion found alongside roads or in open fields.
The smell of carrion attracted a host of scavengers to the site.
Certain beetles are specialized in breaking down carrion in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carcass - the dead body of an animal

Etymology

From Old North French *caroigne*, from Latin *carō* (flesh).
