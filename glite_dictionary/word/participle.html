participle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrtɪˌsɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.tɪ.sɪ.pəl / (BrE)
a verb form used as an adjective or to form compound verb tenses, such as 'walking' in 'walking down the street'
a verb form used as an adjective or in verb tenses

Examples

The past participle of 'eat' is 'eaten'.
She found the participle forms confusing when learning English grammar.
A sentence may use a participle phrase to add more detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gerund - a noun form derived from a verb ending in -ing
  • Antonym: infinitive - the base form of a verb, often preceded by 'to'

Etymology

From Latin 'participium' (literally 'a sharing', used for participles due to their sharing characteristics of verb and adjective).
