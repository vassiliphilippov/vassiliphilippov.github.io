cantaloupe

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.təˌloʊp / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tə.luːp / (BrE)
A round melon with a thick, rough, tan rind and sweet orange flesh.
type of sweet orange-fleshed melon

Examples

She cut a fresh cantaloupe into slices for the fruit salad.
A ripe cantaloupe has a distinct sweet aroma and orange flesh.
For breakfast, he enjoyed yogurt topped with diced cantaloupe and berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melon - a large, round fruit with sweet, juicy flesh and a hard rind
  • Degree Lower: honeydew - a type of sweet, pale-fleshed melon

Etymology

From French *cantaloup*, named after Cantalupo in Italy where the melon was cultivated.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæntəˌloʊp / (AmE), / ˈkæntəˌləʊp / (BrE)
A round or oval melon with a hard, rough rind and sweet, yellowish-orange flesh.
a type of sweet melon

Examples

She sliced a ripe cantaloupe and served it for breakfast.
The fresh cantaloupe added a sweet, juicy flavor to the fruit salad.
He bought a cantaloupe from the market to make a summer smoothie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melon - a large, round fruit with sweet, juicy flesh and a hard rind

Etymology

From French 'cantaloup', named after Cantalupo, a former papal estate near Rome where this melon was first grown in Europe.
