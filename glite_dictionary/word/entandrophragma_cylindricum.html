Entandrophragma cylindricum

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˌtændroʊˈfræɡmə sɪˌlɪndrɪkəm / (AmE), / ɛnˌtændrəˈfræɡmə sɪˌlɪndrɪkəm / (BrE)
An African tree species with lightweight, cedar-scented wood that ranges in color from pink to reddish brown; also known as sapele.
African tree, cedar-scented wood

Examples

The wood of the Entandrophragma cylindricum is prized for making high-quality furniture.
Experts identified the fallen log as Entandrophragma cylindricum by its cedar-like aroma and reddish color.
Many guitars feature bodies made from Entandrophragma cylindricum due to its excellent acoustic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sapele - common name for Entandrophragma cylindricum, the African timber tree

Etymology

From Greek: ent-, andro- (man), phragma (fence), and Latin cylindricum (cylindrical), referring to the tree's physical features.
