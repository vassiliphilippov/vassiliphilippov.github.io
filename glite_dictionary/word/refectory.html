refectory

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈfɛktəri / (AmE), / rɪˈfɛktəri / (BrE)
a large dining hall, especially in a monastery, college, or other institution, where people eat together
communal dining hall, especially in a monastery

Examples

The monks gathered in the refectory each evening for supper.
Visitors were invited to join the students in the college refectory for lunch.
During the tour, we admired the beautiful stained glass windows in the refectory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dining hall - a large room where meals are served to groups of people

Etymology

From Latin refectorium (place to refresh oneself), from reficere (to restore).
