supplemental

1. ADJ

/ ˌsʌpləˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌsʌplɪˈmɛntl̩ / (BrE)
added to complete something, make up for a lack, or enhance the original
added to complete or make up for a lack

Examples

The company offers supplemental insurance to cover medical expenses not paid by the basic plan.
Students can attend supplemental classes for extra help in difficult subjects.
She received a supplemental income from her freelance work.

Synset Examples

produced supplementary volumes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplementary - added to provide what is lacking or needed

Etymology

From Latin supplementum (something added) via supplement with the suffix -al forming the adjective.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00050099-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌsʌpləˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌsʌplɪˈmɛntəl / (BrE)
serving as an additional support; functioning to supplement or reinforce something else
serving as additional support or supplement

Examples

The company offers supplemental insurance plans for added peace of mind.
Many students attend supplemental classes to improve their grades.
The team needed supplemental funding to complete the project.

Synset Examples

the main library and its auxiliary branches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxiliary - providing additional help or support

Etymology

From Latin 'supplementum' (that which fills up or completes), via Late Latin 'supplementalis'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01860057-s
Synset POS: s
