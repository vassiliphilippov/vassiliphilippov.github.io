blackpoll

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ˈblakˌpəʊl / (BrE)
A North American warbler (Setophaga striata) with a distinctive black cap and white cheeks, common during migration.
a North American warbler bird

Examples

Every autumn, huge flocks of blackpoll fly south from Canada to South America.
The blackpoll can be identified by its black cap and striking white cheeks.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare blackpoll in the park this spring.
