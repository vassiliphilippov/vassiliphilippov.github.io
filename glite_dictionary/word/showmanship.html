showmanship

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊ mənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊ mənˌʃɪp / (BrE)
the skill or ability to present or perform something, especially in entertainment, in a way that attracts and impresses an audience
skill in presenting or performing attractively

Examples

Her showmanship captivated the audience from the very first moment.
The magician's showmanship turned a simple trick into a spectacular performance.
Effective showmanship can make even an ordinary act seem extraordinary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagecraft - skill in theatrical production and presentation

Etymology

Formed from 'show' + '-manship', patterned after 'craftsmanship'.
