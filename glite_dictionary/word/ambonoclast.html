ambonoclast

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.bə.nəˌklæst / (AmE), / ˈæm.bə.nəˌklɑːst / (BrE)
a person who challenges or opposes established beliefs or traditions
person who challenges established beliefs

Examples

As an ambonoclast, he often questioned the validity of traditional customs at every gathering.
The committee was surprised by the ambonoclast's willingness to challenge long-held assumptions.
In academic circles, being an ambonoclast can foster new ways of thinking.

Synset Examples

Being an ambonoclast, she challenged every old belief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iconoclast - a person who attacks cherished beliefs or institutions

Etymology

From Greek 'ambōn' (pulpit) + '-clast' (breaker), originally referring to one who breaks or opposes the pulpit or what it stands for.
