bride

1. NOUN

/ braɪd / (AmE), / braɪd / (BrE)
a woman who has recently been married
woman recently married

Examples

The bride looked radiant as she greeted the guests at the reception.
After the ceremony, the bride and groom shared their first dance together.
Everyone admired the beautiful dress worn by the bride.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: groom - a man who has recently been married

Etymology

From Old English 'bryd', related to Old High German 'brūt' (bride).

2. NOUN

/ braɪd / (AmE), / braɪd / (BrE)
a woman on her wedding day or just before and during the marriage ceremony
woman at her wedding ceremony

Examples

The bride wore a beautiful white gown as she walked down the aisle.
After the ceremony, the bride and groom shared a joyful dance.
Friends and family gathered to celebrate the bride's special day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: groom - a man on his wedding day or just before and during the marriage ceremony

Etymology

From Old English *bryd* (bride, betrothed woman).
