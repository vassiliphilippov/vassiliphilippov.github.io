sumpsimus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmp.sɪ.məs / (AmE), / ˈsʌmp.sɪ.məs / (BrE)
A correct word or expression that is used instead of a previously common but incorrect one, especially when stubbornly or pedantically insisted upon.
correct expression replacing a wrong one
archaic

Examples

The editor was quick to correct the old mumpsimus with the proper sumpsimus.
Linguists enjoy pointing out the sumpsimus to those who stick to popular but flawed phrases.
Despite popular resistance, the grammatical sumpsimus eventually replaced the widespread mistake.

Synset Examples

he preferred his erroneous but pleasing mumpsimus to the correct sumpsimus

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mumpsimus - a stubborn use of an incorrect expression in place of the correct one

Etymology

From Latin 'sumpsimus' (we have taken), originally from a story about a priest misreading the Latin Mass.
