bowing

1. ADJ

/ ˈboʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
overly respectful or excessively deferential in manner or attitude
excessively deferential

Examples

His bowing attitude towards the manager irritated his colleagues.
She offered a bowing apology that seemed insincere and exaggerated.
They disliked his bowing manners, which bordered on subservience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority or will of others
  • Synonym: servile - having or showing an excessive willingness to serve or please others
  • Antonym: assertive - having or showing a confident and forceful personality

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of bending the head or upper body forward as a sign of respect, greeting, submission, or shame
act of bending forward as greeting or respect

Examples

At the end of the concert, the musicians finished with a graceful bowing to the audience.
In Japanese culture, bowing is a common way to show respect or greet someone.
She was criticized for her constant bowing to her superiors, which some saw as excessive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curtsy - a respectful bow made by women, bending the knees
  • Synonym: salute - a gesture of respect or greeting, especially in a military context
  • Antonym: standing upright - to stand in a straight, vertical position without bowing

Etymology

From the verb 'bow', which comes from Old English 'bugan' (to bend) and the suffix '-ing'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈboʊ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the technique or manner of using the bow to play a stringed instrument, especially in terms of control and expression
technique of using a bow on stringed instruments

Examples

The cellist's bowing brought a rich and expressive tone to the performance.
Her teacher praised her for improving her bowing technique during practice.
Mastering smooth bowing is essential for every aspiring violinist.

Synset Examples

the violinist's bowing was excellent

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pizzicato - the technique of plucking the strings of a string instrument

Etymology

From 'bow' (the tool used to play stringed instruments) + '-ing' (noun-forming suffix).
