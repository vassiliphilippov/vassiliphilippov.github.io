lampshade

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmpˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈlampʃeɪd / (BrE)
a decorative or protective cover for a lamp, designed to shield the bulb from direct view and diffuse the light
decorative cover for a lamp's bulb

Examples

She changed the lampshade to one with a floral pattern to match the curtains.
The lampshade softened the harsh glare of the light bulb in the study.
During the renovation, they replaced every old lampshade with a new, modern design.

Etymology

Compound of 'lamp' and 'shade', first recorded in the 19th century.
