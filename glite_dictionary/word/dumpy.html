dumpy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʌmpi / (AmE), / ˈdʌmpi / (BrE)
(of a place or thing) unattractive and dirty, resembling a garbage dump.
unattractive, dirty; like a garbage dump

Examples

The apartment was so dumpy that I didn't want to take off my shoes.
We stayed in some dumpy hotel outside the city center.
She moved out of her dumpy old flat as soon as she could afford better.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirty - covered or marked with an unclean substance
  • Synonym: messy - untidy or disordered
  • Antonym: tidy - neat, clean, and in order (opposite meaning)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02725783-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʌmpi / (AmE), / ˈdʌmpi / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) short and somewhat fat or thick-set in appearance.
short and plump; thick-set

Examples

The dumpy little cottage sat at the end of the lane, surrounded by wildflowers.
She felt self-conscious about her dumpy figure in the group photo.
A dumpy gray pigeon hopped along the window ledge, searching for crumbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stocky - broad and sturdily built
  • Antonym: slender - gracefully thin

Etymology

Probably from "dump," meaning a thick, heavy piece of something, with "-y" forming the adjective.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00990698-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈdʌmpi / (AmE), / ˈdʌmpi / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) short and thick or stout, often in a way that appears unattractive or clumsy.
short and thick-set

Examples

The dumpy little house sat at the end of the lane, surrounded by overgrown bushes.
She was a dumpy woman with a friendly smile and twinkling eyes.
The dumpy legs of the pony made it look awkward but endearing.

Synset Examples

some people seem born to be square and chunky
a dumpy little dumpling of a woman
dachshunds are long lowset dogs with drooping ears
a little church with a squat tower
a squatty red smokestack
a stumpy ungainly figure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squat - short and thick-set in form or stature
  • Synonym: stumpy - short and thick or broad, usually in a way that lacks grace
  • Antonym: lean - thin, especially in a healthy or attractive way

Etymology

Probably from the noun 'dump', meaning 'a short, thick piece', of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
