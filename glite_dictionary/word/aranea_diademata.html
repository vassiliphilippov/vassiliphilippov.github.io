Aranea diademata

1. NOUN

/ əˈreɪniə ˌdaɪəˈmætə / (AmE), / əˈreɪnɪə ˌdaɪəˈmɑːtə / (BrE)
A common European garden spider, recognizable by the white cross-shaped marking on its back.
European garden spider species

Examples

The Aranea diademata is often seen spinning orb-shaped webs in late summer.
Biologists observed an Aranea diademata constructing its intricate web in the garden.
Children are often fascinated by the cross pattern on the back of the Aranea diademata.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European garden spider - Common name for Aranea diademata, a spider found in Europe

Etymology

From Latin: Aranea ('spider') and diademata ('adorned with a diadem, or crown'), referring to its markings.
