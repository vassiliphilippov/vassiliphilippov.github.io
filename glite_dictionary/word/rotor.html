rotor

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊtər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtə / (BrE)
the rotating bar inside a distributor that directs high-voltage current to the spark plugs of an internal combustion engine
revolving bar in a distributor (auto)

Examples

The mechanic replaced the faulty rotor in the car's distributor to restore proper ignition timing.
If the rotor is worn, the engine may misfire or fail to start entirely.
Regular inspection of the distributor rotor can help prevent unexpected engine problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distributor - device that distributes electrical current to engine cylinders

Etymology

From Latin 'rotor', a form from 'rotare' meaning 'to rotate'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊtər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtə / (BrE)
a rotating part of a mechanical device, especially an assembly of rotating blades or vanes, such as those found on helicopters and jet engines
rotating part of a machine or engine

Examples

The main rotor of the helicopter began spinning as the engine started.
Every wind turbine has a large rotor that generates electricity as it turns.
Engineers inspected the rotor blades for signs of wear and damage.

Synset Examples

there are horizontal rotors on a helicopter or compressor rotors in a jet engine

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stator - the stationary part of a rotary system against which the rotor moves

Etymology

From Latin rota (wheel).

3. NOUN

/ ˈroʊ.tər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.tə / (BrE)
the rotating part of the armature in a motor or generator that turns inside a stationary part (the stator) to produce or use electrical energy
rotating part of a motor or generator

Examples

The rotor spins inside the stator to generate electricity in the wind turbine.
If the rotor is misaligned, the motor will run inefficiently and may overheat.
Engineers inspected the rotor for cracks after the machine malfunctioned.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stator - the stationary part of an electric motor or generator

Etymology

From Latin rota (wheel) via modern technical use.
