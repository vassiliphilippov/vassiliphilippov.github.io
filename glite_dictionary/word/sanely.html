sanely

1. ADV

/ ˈseɪnli / (AmE), / ˈseɪnli / (BrE)
In a sane or rational manner.
in a rational way

Examples

She spoke sanely about the options during the meeting, despite the stress involved.
It is important to respond sanely even when faced with difficult challenges.
After some rest, he was able to think sanely about the situation.

Synset Examples

she is behaving rather sanely these days even though we know she is schizophrenic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irrationally - in an illogical or unreasonable manner
  • Synonym: sensibly - in a sensible, practical, or reasonable manner

Etymology

From 'sane' + '-ly', following standard English adverb formation.

2. ADV

/ ˈseɪnli / (AmE), / ˈseɪnli / (BrE)
in a reasonable, intelligent, or rational manner
in a rational or sensible way

Examples

She calmly and sanely explained her side of the story during the argument.
It is important to respond sanely when faced with unexpected challenges.
He addressed the concerns sanely, refusing to let panic dictate his actions.

Synset Examples

he acted sensibly in the crisis
speak more sanely about these affairs
acted quite reasonably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensibly - in a sensible or reasonable way
  • Antonym: irrationally - in an illogical or unreasonable manner

Etymology

From 'sane' + '-ly'; 'sane' from Latin 'sanus' (healthy, sound).
