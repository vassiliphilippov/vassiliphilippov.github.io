episodic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛpɪˈsɑdɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈsɒdɪk / (BrE)
occurring or appearing at irregular or infrequent intervals; not continuous or regular
happening at irregular intervals

Examples

His interest in the project was episodic, with enthusiasm coming and going over the months.
She experiences episodic bouts of anxiety rather than feeling anxious all the time.
The region is subject to episodic droughts, making agriculture challenging.

Synset Examples

episodic in his affections
occasional headaches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: occasional - happening from time to time, not regular
  • Synonym: sporadic - occurring at irregular intervals or only in a few places
  • Antonym: continuous - happening without interruption or break

Etymology

From Greek 'epeisodikos', from 'epeisodion' (episode).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛpɪˈsɑdɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈsɒdɪk / (BrE)
(of something) limited in duration to a single episode; relating to or characteristic of a single episode.
relating to a single episode or event

Examples

The historian preferred an episodic account, focusing on individual reigns rather than a continuous narrative.
Their podcast takes an episodic approach, with each episode telling a self-contained story.
Some TV shows are highly episodic, allowing viewers to enjoy episodes out of order.

Synset Examples

an account concerned primarily with episodic events such as the succession of rulers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: event-based - based on or relating to specific events or episodes
  • Antonym: continuous - without interruption or episodes, unbroken

Etymology

From Greek 'epeisodikos', from 'epeisodion' meaning 'episode'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01761270-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌɛpɪˈsɑdɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈsɒdɪk / (BrE)
(of writing or narration) consisting of a series of separate episodes or incidents rather than following a continuous story.
composed of separate episodes (writing)

Examples

The novel has an episodic structure, with each chapter focusing on a different character.
Her memoirs are episodic, recounting events from different periods of her life without a strict chronological order.
The show's episodic format allows viewers to enjoy individual stories without needing to follow every episode.

Synset Examples

the book is episodic and the incidents don't always hang together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serial - published or broadcast in parts or episodes

Etymology

From Greek 'epeisodikos', from 'epeisodion' meaning 'episode'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02491286-s
Synset POS: s
