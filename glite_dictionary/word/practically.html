practically

1. ADV

/ ˈpræktɪkli / (AmE), / ˈpræktɪkli / (BrE)
For all practical purposes; almost but not completely.
almost; for all practical purposes

Examples

Practically everyone at the meeting agreed with the proposal.
There were practically no seats left in the theater by the time we arrived.
It's practically impossible to finish this project in one day.

Synset Examples

much the same thing happened every time
practically everything in Hinduism is the manifestation of a god

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almost - very nearly, but not quite
  • Antonym: entirely - completely or fully

Etymology

From practical + -ly, modeled on similar adverbs in English.

2. ADV

/ ˈpræktɪkli / (AmE), / ˈpræktɪkli / (BrE)
Almost; nearly, but not completely.
almost; nearly

Examples

The store was practically empty when I arrived.
She has practically finished her research for the project.
It's practically impossible to park here during rush hour.

Synset Examples

practically the first thing I saw when I got off the train
he was practically the only guest at the party
there was practically no garden at all

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almost - not quite; very nearly
  • Synonym: nearly - close to; almost

Etymology

From practical + -ly, after practical (from Medieval Latin practicus, from Greek praktikos) + -ly adverbial suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00054282-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈpræk.tɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˈpræk.tɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a practical manner; with regard to actual use or practice rather than theory.
in a practical manner

Examples

He approached the project practically, focusing on workable solutions instead of abstract ideas.
Teachers appreciate students who can think practically when solving real-world problems.
The repairs were done practically, using available materials and straightforward methods.

Synset Examples

practically orientated institutions such as business schools
a brilliant man but so practically inept that he needed help to cross the road safely

Related Terms

  • Antonym: theoretically - in a theoretical manner, as opposed to practically

Etymology

From practical + -ly, formed in English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00054050-r
Synset POS: r
