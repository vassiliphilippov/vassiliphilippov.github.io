to allege

1. VERB

/ əˈlɛdʒ / (AmE), / əˈlɛdʒ / (BrE)
to state or claim that something is true, especially without having proof
claim or assert (often without proof)

Examples

The lawyer alleged that the company had violated environmental laws.
It was alleged that the documents were forged, but no evidence was produced.
Several witnesses are alleging that they saw the suspect near the scene of the crime.

Synset Examples

He alleged that he was the victim of a crime
He said it was too late to intervene in the war
The registrar says that I owe the school money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assert - to state a fact or belief confidently and forcefully
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true

Etymology

From Old French 'aleguer', from Latin 'allegare' (to adduce in evidence).
