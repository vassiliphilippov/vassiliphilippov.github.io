indelibly

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈdɛləbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛləbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be removed, forgotten, or erased.
in a way that cannot be erased

Examples

The memory of her kindness remained indelibly in his mind for years.
That moment was indelibly marked in history as a turning point.
The experience was indelibly etched into the fabric of the community.

Synset Examples

this tradition has left its mark indelibly upon the social, political, educational and industrial fabric of this country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permanently - in a way that lasts or remains unchanged indefinitely

Etymology

From 'indelible' + '-ly'; 'indelible' from Latin 'indelebilis' (that cannot be destroyed).
