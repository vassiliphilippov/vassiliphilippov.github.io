Aaron Copland

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛrən ˈkoʊplənd / (AmE), / ˈærən ˈkəʊplənd / (BrE)
(1900–1990) a United States composer who developed a distinctly American style of classical music.
American composer, pioneer of American classical music

Examples

Aaron Copland composed "Appalachian Spring," which became an iconic piece of American music.
The works of Aaron Copland are often performed by major orchestras around the world.
Many students study Aaron Copland as a central figure in twentieth-century American music.
