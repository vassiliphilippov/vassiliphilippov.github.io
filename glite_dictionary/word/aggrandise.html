to aggrandise

1. VERB

/ əˈɡrænˌdaɪz / (AmE), / əˈɡræn.daɪz / (BrE)
to make something appear greater, more important, or more impressive than it really is, often by adding details or exaggerating
to make something seem greater or more impressive

Examples

She tended to aggrandise her achievements whenever she spoke about her work.
The author chose to aggrandise the hero's deeds to make the story more captivating.
Politicians sometimes aggrandise their role in historical events for personal gain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exaggerate - to make something seem larger or more important than it is

Etymology

From French 'agrandir' (to enlarge), from Latin 'grandis' (large, great).
