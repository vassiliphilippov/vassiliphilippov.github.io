transcendence

1. NOUN

/ trænˈsɛndəns / (AmE), / trænˈsɛndəns / (BrE)
The state of existing above or beyond the physical world or ordinary experience, often referring to a higher or spiritual realm.
existence beyond material experience

Examples

Many philosophers have debated whether true transcendence is attainable by human beings.
The novel explores themes of spiritual transcendence and the search for higher meaning.
Some religions teach that meditation can lead to a state of transcendence beyond the physical world.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immanence - the state of being present within and throughout the material world

Etymology

From Latin transcendere (to climb over, surpass), from trans- (across) + scandere (to climb).

2. NOUN

/ trænˈsɛndəns / (AmE), / trænˈsɛndəns / (BrE)
The state of being or going beyond ordinary limits; surpassing or excelling beyond what is usual or expected.
the state of surpassing usual limits

Examples

Many spiritual traditions emphasize the pursuit of transcendence as a path to enlightenment.
Her achievements in science revealed a kind of intellectual transcendence rarely seen in her field.
The novel explores the transcendence of the human spirit in the face of adversity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immanence - the state of being inherent or existing within
  • Synonym: excellence - the quality of being outstanding or extremely good

Etymology

From Latin 'transcendentia', from 'transcendere' (to climb over, surpass).
