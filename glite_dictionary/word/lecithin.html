lecithin

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛsəθɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɛsɪθɪn / (BrE)
a yellowish fatty substance found in animal and plant tissues, important for the metabolism of fats and often used as an emulsifier in food products
a fatty substance used as an emulsifier
medical

Examples

Soybeans are a common source of lecithin used in processed foods.
The nutrition label listed lecithin as an ingredient to keep the mixture stable.
Egg yolks are naturally rich in lecithin, which helps bind ingredients in mayonnaise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emulsifier - a substance that helps mix liquids that do not usually combine

Etymology

From Greek lekithos (egg yolk), from which it was first isolated.
