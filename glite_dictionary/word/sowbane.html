sowbane

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊˌbeɪn / (AmE), / ˈsəʊˌbeɪn / (BrE)
a herb, especially black goosefoot, said to be poisonous or fatal to swine
herb poisonous to swine

Examples

Farmers avoided planting sowbane near pig pens to protect their livestock.
The leaves of sowbane can be toxic if ingested by swine.
Some herbalists once believed that sowbane was useful in controlling wild pig populations.

Etymology

Formed from 'sow' (female pig) + 'bane' (something causing death or destruction); used historically to refer to plants harmful to pigs.
