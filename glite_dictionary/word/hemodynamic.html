hemodynamic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhiːmoʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (BrE)
relating to the flow of blood or the forces involved in blood circulation within the body
relating to blood flow or circulation
medical

Examples

The patient required careful monitoring of hemodynamic stability during the surgery.
Improving hemodynamic function is essential in treating patients with heart failure.
Certain drugs are used to manage hemodynamic responses during anesthesia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemodynamics - the study of blood flow and forces in the circulatory system

Etymology

From 'hemo-' (relating to blood) + 'dynamic' (relating to force or movement).
