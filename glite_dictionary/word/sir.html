Sir

1. NOUN

/ sɝː / (AmE), / sɜː / (BrE)
a title of respect used before the given name of a knight or baronet.
title for knight or baronet

Examples

He was knighted and became Sir David Attenborough.
We were introduced to Sir Charles at the ceremony.
After receiving the honor, he preferred to be addressed as Sir John.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Dame - title of respect for a woman, equivalent to 'Sir' for men

Etymology

From Old French *sire*, from Latin *senior* (older, elder).
