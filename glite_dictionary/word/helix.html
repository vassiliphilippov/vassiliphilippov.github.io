Helix

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːlɪks / (AmE), / ˈhiːlɪks / (BrE)
A genus of land snails, including the edible snail and other species typical of the family Helicidae.
genus of land snails

Examples

The common garden snail belongs to the genus Helix.
Scientists observed the reproductive habits of several Helix species in the laboratory.
Many edible land snails, such as Helix pomatia, are considered delicacies in French cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snail - a small, slow-moving mollusc with a spiral shell

Etymology

From Latin 'helix', from Greek 'helix' (spiral, coil), referring to the spiral shape of the snail's shell.
