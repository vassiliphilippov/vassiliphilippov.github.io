winger

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪŋɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋə / (BrE)
a player positioned on the wing of the field or rink, especially in sports such as football, hockey, or rugby, whose primary role is to attack and provide width to the team's play
player positioned on the wing in sports

Examples

The team’s new winger scored two goals in his debut match.
As a winger, she often uses her speed to outrun defenders down the sideline.
He started his career as a central midfielder but now plays primarily as a winger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forward - attacking player in team sports
  • Antonym: defender - player whose main role is to prevent the opposition from scoring

Etymology

From 'wing' + '-er', referring to the side positions in a team.
