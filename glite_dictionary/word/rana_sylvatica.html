Rana sylvatica

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪ.nə sɪlˈvæt.ɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˌreɪ.nə sɪlˈvat.ɪ.kə / (BrE)
A species of small, light-brown frog found widely in moist North American woodlands, especially among spruce trees; commonly known as the wood frog.
species of North American wood frog

Examples

The Rana sylvatica is remarkable for its ability to survive being frozen during winter.
Biologists observed several Rana sylvatica near the edge of the woodland pond.
Unlike other frogs, Rana sylvatica can thrive in colder northern forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood frog - common name for Rana sylvatica, a woodland frog species

Etymology

From Latin: rana (frog) + sylvatica (of the woods).
