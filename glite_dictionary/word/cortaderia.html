Cortaderia

1. NOUN

/ kɔr.təˈdɪə.ri.ə / (AmE), / ˌkɔː.təˈdɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tall ornamental grasses native to South America, New Zealand, and New Guinea, including pampas grass.
genus of tall ornamental grasses

Examples

The landscaper suggested planting Cortaderia to add height and texture to the garden.
Many gardeners prize Cortaderia for its large plumes and dramatic appearance.
Invasive Cortaderia species can spread quickly and dominate local ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pampas grass - a tall grass of the genus Cortaderia with feathery plumes, grown as an ornamental

Etymology

From Spanish 'cortadera', meaning 'cutter', due to the sharp leaf edges.
