combative

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈbæt̬.ɪv / (AmE), / kəmˈbæt.ɪv / (BrE)
having a tendency to fight or argue; eager to engage in conflict or competition.
eager or ready to fight

Examples

Despite his young age, the new manager adopted a combative approach to negotiations.
She grew combative when defending her beliefs during the debate.
Their combative attitude made teamwork difficult.

Synset Examples

bellicose young officers
a combative impulse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellicose - willing to fight; aggressively hostile
  • Antonym: peaceful - not willing to fight; calm and non-violent

Etymology

From French *combatif*, from *combattre* (to fight).

2. ADJ

/ kəmˈbætɪv / (AmE), / kəmˈbætɪv / (BrE)
eager or ready to argue or fight; showing a readiness to engage in dispute or conflict, especially in argument.
eager to argue or fight

Examples

Her combative personality often led to heated discussions with her colleagues.
The politician took a combative stance during the televised debate.
His combative attitude made compromise difficult in team meetings.

Synset Examples

a dialectical and agonistic approach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argumentative - given to expressing or engaging in arguments
  • Antonym: peaceful - not wanting or involving conflict or argument

Etymology

From French 'combatif', from 'combattre' meaning 'to fight'.

3. ADJ

/ kəmˈbæt̬ɪv / (AmE), / kəmˈbætɪv / (BrE)
having or showing a tendency to argue, fight, or be ready to engage in conflict; eager to dispute or oppose.
eager to argue or fight; ready for conflict

Examples

Her combative attitude made negotiations challenging for everyone involved.
During the debate, he adopted a combative tone toward his opponent.
The lawyer was known for his combative approach in court.

Synset Examples

a style described as abrasive and contentious
a disputatious lawyer
a litigious and acrimonious spirit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contentious - likely to cause or engage in argument or conflict
  • Antonym: peaceful - not inclined to fight or argue; calm

Etymology

From French 'combatif', from Latin 'combattere' (to fight).
