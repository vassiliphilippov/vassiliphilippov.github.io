Rhinoptera

1. NOUN

/ raɪˈnɑptərə / (AmE), / raɪˈnɒptərə / (BrE)
a genus of rays in the family Myliobatidae, commonly known as cownose rays, found in warm temperate and tropical waters
genus of cownose rays

Examples

The Rhinoptera genus includes several species of cownose rays found along the Atlantic coast.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of Rhinoptera to better understand their role in the ecosystem.
Fishermen occasionally encounter Rhinoptera while trawling in warm coastal waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cownose ray - a common name for species in the genus Rhinoptera

Etymology

From Greek 'rhino-' (nose) and 'pteron' (wing), referring to the shape of the rays.

A genus of rays in the family Myliobatidae, commonly known as cownose rays, found in warm temperate and tropical waters.
genus of cownose rays

Examples

The Rhinoptera are often seen swimming in large schools along the coast.
Several species within the genus Rhinoptera are important to marine biodiversity.
Marine biologists studied the migration patterns of Rhinoptera in the Gulf of Mexico.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhinos' (nose) and 'pteron' (wing), referring to the ray's snout and wing-like fins.
