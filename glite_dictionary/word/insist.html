to insist

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈsɪst / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪst / (BrE)
to state or demand firmly and refuse to change one's mind or position
state or demand firmly, refuse to yield

Examples

If you insist on coming with us, you must be ready to leave early.
She insisted that the mistake be corrected before publication.
No matter how much we argued, he insisted on his original plan.

Synset Examples

I must insist!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persist - to continue firmly in an opinion or course of action
  • Antonym: yield - to give way to arguments, demands, or pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'insistere', meaning 'to persist' or 'stand upon'.

WordNet ID: oewn-00820635-v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈsɪst / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪst / (BrE)
to demand or beg something firmly and persistently, especially when others do not agree or are reluctant
demand or beg persistently

Examples

She insisted that the team follow her directions, repeating her request until everyone agreed.
If you insist on coming with us, you must be ready for a long day.
He insisted tirelessly that the board reconsider its decision.

Synset Examples

I importune you to help them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully
  • Degree Lower: request - to politely or formally ask for something
  • Degree Higher: to implore - to beg someone desperately to do something
  • Antonym: yield - to cease to demand, to give way

Etymology

From Latin 'insistere' (to persist, press upon), from 'in-' (upon) + 'sistere' (to stand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00779976-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈsɪst / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪst / (BrE)
to state or demand firmly and assertively, especially when others disagree or resist
state or demand firmly

Examples

She insisted that she was right, even when everyone else disagreed.
The manager insisted on seeing proof before approving the expense.
Despite his apologies, they insisted he take responsibility for the mistake.

Synset Examples

The letter asserts a free society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assert - to state something clearly and firmly
  • Synonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully
  • Antonym: to concede - to admit something is true or valid after first denying or resisting it

Etymology

From Latin 'insistere' (stand upon, persist), from 'in-' (upon) + 'sistere' (to stand).

Source: WordNet
