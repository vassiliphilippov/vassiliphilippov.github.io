richweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃˌwid / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃˌwiːd / (BrE)
A strong-scented perennial plant with serrated pointed leaves and loose clusters of yellowish flowers, native to the eastern United States.
perennial plant of eastern U.S.

Examples

The forest floor was covered in richweed, its yellowish flowers standing out among the greenery.
Gardeners appreciate richweed for its ability to attract pollinators in shady areas.
Botanists surveyed the area and noted a healthy population of richweed near the riverbank.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃˌwid / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃˌwiːd / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Pilea, characterized by drooping green flower clusters and smooth, translucent stems and leaves.
plant of the genus Pilea

Examples

The forest floor was covered in richweed, thriving in the dappled sunlight.
Gardeners often plant richweed for its attractive, translucent stems and lush green foliage.
When hiking in moist woodlands, you may encounter clusters of richweed near streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilea - a genus of typically herbaceous plants, including clearweed and richweed
