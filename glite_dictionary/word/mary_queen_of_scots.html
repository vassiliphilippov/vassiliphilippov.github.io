Mary Queen of Scots

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛri kwiːn əv skɑts / (AmE), / ˈmɛːri kwiːn əv skɒts / (BrE)
(1542–1587) the queen of Scotland from 1542 to 1567, famous for her turbulent life, forced abdication, imprisonment in England, and execution for alleged involvement in plots against Elizabeth I.
Scottish queen (1542–1587), executed in England

Examples

Historians continue to debate the true motives behind the execution of Mary Queen of Scots.
The film portrays Mary Queen of Scots' dramatic escape from Loch Leven Castle.
Many letters written by Mary Queen of Scots have survived and provide insight into her character.
