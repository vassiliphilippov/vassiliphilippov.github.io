Penstemon linarioides

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.stə.mɒn lɪˌnɛr.iˈoʊ.diːz / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.stɪ.mən lɪˌnaː.rɪˈəʊ.diːz / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant with small narrow leaves and blue-violet flowers in long open clusters, native to parts of Utah, Colorado, New Mexico, and Arizona.
species of flowering plant in western US

Examples

Several hikers stopped to admire the striking blue-violet flowers of the Penstemon linarioides along the trail.
The Penstemon linarioides thrives in rocky soils across regions of Colorado and New Mexico.
Gardeners appreciate Penstemon linarioides for its drought tolerance and delicate floral clusters.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Penstemon - genus of flowering plants commonly called beardtongues

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Penstemon' and species 'linarioides' meaning 'resembling Linaria'.
