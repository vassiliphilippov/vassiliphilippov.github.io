ferromagnet

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛroʊˌmæɡnət / (AmE), / ˈfɛrəʊˌmæɡnət / (BrE)
a material that exhibits strong magnetic properties, especially one that retains magnetization after an external magnetic field is removed
material with strong magnetic properties

Examples

Iron is a classic example of a ferromagnet due to its strong magnetic behavior.
Nickel and cobalt are also well-known ferromagnets used in various applications.
When a ferromagnet is magnetized, its magnetic domains align in the same direction.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: paramagnet - material with weak, non-permanent magnetic properties
  • Antonym: antiferromagnet - material in which adjacent atoms have opposite spins, canceling magnetism

Etymology

From Latin 'ferrum' (iron) and Ancient Greek 'magnetis' (magnet).
