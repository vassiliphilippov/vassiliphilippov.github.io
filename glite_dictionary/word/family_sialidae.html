family Sialidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæməli saɪˈælɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌfæmɪli saɪˈælɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic insects in the order Megaloptera, commonly known as alderflies.
a family of aquatic insects (alderflies)

Examples

The family Sialidae includes insects commonly referred to as alderflies.
Larvae of the family Sialidae are aquatic and are often found in slow-moving streams.
Entomologists have studied the geographic distribution of species within the family Sialidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alderfly - a common name for insects in the family Sialidae

Etymology

From New Latin Sialidae, based on genus Sialis, from Greek sialis (a kind of fly).
