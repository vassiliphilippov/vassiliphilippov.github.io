uprising

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌraɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌp.raɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
an act of resistance or rebellion against an authority or government, often involving organized force
rebellion or organized resistance

Examples

The government quickly suppressed the uprising in the capital before it spread to other regions.
A popular uprising led to significant political changes in the country.
The documentary explores the causes behind the workers' uprising of 1912.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebellion - an act of violent or open resistance to an established government or ruler
  • Synonym: revolt - an attempt to overthrow the authority of a government
  • Degree Higher: revolution - a forcible overthrow of a government or social order for a new system

Etymology

From the verb 'up' + 'rising', first used in this sense in the 16th century.
