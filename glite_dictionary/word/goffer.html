goffer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːfər / (AmE), / ˈɡɒfə / (BrE)
an iron tool used for pressing pleats and ridges into fabric, especially lace
tool for pressing pleats in fabric

Examples

The lace-maker used a goffer to create delicate ridges in the collar.
Old-fashioned garments sometimes required a goffer for finishing touches on the fabric.
Restorers borrowed a museum's antique goffer to replicate Victorian-style pleats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iron - a tool used for pressing clothes

Etymology

From French 'gaufrer' (to emboss or crimp), related to 'gaufre' (waffle).

2. VERB

/ ˈɡɑːfər / (AmE), / ˈɡɒfə / (BrE)
to press or shape fabric, especially lace or trim, into wavy or fluted patterns using a heated iron
press fabric into wavy folds with heat

Examples

The seamstress skillfully goffered the lace collar to create an elegant, ruffled effect.
You must goffer the edges of the napkins before displaying them at the table.
She goffered the trim of the dress to give it a delicate, decorative finish.

Synset Examples

goffer the trim of the dress

Etymology

From French 'gaufrer', meaning to emboss or press, from 'gaufre' (waffle).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡɒfər / (AmE), / ˈɡɒfə / (BrE)
an ornamental frill or border made by pressing fabric into pleats or ridges, often for decorative purposes
ornamental pleated frill or edging

Examples

The maid carefully ironed each goffer on the dress’s collar to make it crisp.
Her Victorian blouse was adorned with a delicate white goffer around the neckline.
Old-fashioned bonnets often featured a wide goffer as decoration.

Etymology

From French *gaufrer* (to emboss, crimp), from *gaufre* (honeycomb).
