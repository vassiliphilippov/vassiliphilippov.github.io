to rase

1. VERB

/ reɪz / (AmE), / reɪz / (BrE)
to tear down or demolish a building or structure completely, making it level with the ground
demolish or level to the ground

Examples

The old fortress was rased to make way for new urban development.
The conquerors rased the town, leaving not a single building standing.
Plans were announced to rase the abandoned factories along the river.

Synset Examples

The building was levelled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to raze - to completely destroy; to level to the ground; a variant spelling in modern English
  • Synonym: to demolish - to pull or knock down a building
  • Antonym: construct - to build or put up (especially a building)

Etymology

From Old French 'raser', from Latin 'rasare', frequentative of 'radere' (to scrape, shave).
