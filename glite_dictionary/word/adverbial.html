adverbial

1. NOUN

/ ædˈvɝːbiəl / (AmE), / ədˈvɜːbiəl / (BrE)
a word or group of words that functions as an adverb, modifying a verb, adjective, or other adverb
word or phrase acting as an adverb

Examples

The phrase 'in the morning' is an adverbial in the sentence 'She left in the morning.'
In English grammar, an adverbial can be a single word, a phrase, or even a clause.
He used an adverbial to give more detail about when the event occurred.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adverb - a word that modifies a verb, adjective, or other adverb

Etymology

From Latin 'adverbialis', from 'adverbium' (adverb).
