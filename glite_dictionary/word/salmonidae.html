Salmonidae

1. NOUN

/ sælˈmɒnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / sælˈmɒnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of fish that includes salmon, trout, and related species, typically found in cold, freshwater and some marine environments
family of fish including salmon and trout

Examples

The family Salmonidae includes several commercially important species prized by anglers and chefs alike.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Salmonidae across different river systems in North America.
Climate change poses a significant threat to the habitats of many Salmonidae species.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Salmo (salmon) and the suffix -idae used for animal family names.

2. NOUN

/ sælˈmɒnɪˌdi / (AmE), / sælˈmɒnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of fish that includes salmon, trout, and related species, typically found in cold fresh and saltwater habitats.
the family of salmon and trout fishes

Examples

The Salmonidae family contains some of the most economically important fish species in the world.
Many species within the Salmonidae are prized both for sport and for their nutritional value.
Research on Salmonidae migration patterns has contributed to advances in ecology and conservation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmon - a large edible fish of the Salmonidae family
  • Synonym: trout - a common name for freshwater fish in the Salmonidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus name 'Salmo' (salmon) with the suffix '-idae' used in zoological family names.
