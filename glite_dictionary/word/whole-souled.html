whole-souled

1. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊlˌsoʊld / (AmE), / ˈhəʊlˌsəʊld / (BrE)
showing unconditional and enthusiastic devotion or sincerity
unconditionally devoted; sincere

Examples

She gave her whole-souled support to the project, inspiring everyone around her.
His whole-souled enthusiasm for teaching made a lasting impact on his students.
The novel describes a whole-souled friendship that endures through hardship and time.

Synset Examples

heart-whole friendship
gave wholehearted support to her candidacy
wholehearted commitment
demonstrated his whole-souled allegiance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wholehearted - showing complete sincerity and commitment

Etymology

Formed from 'whole' (entire, complete) + 'soul' (spirit, emotional nature).
