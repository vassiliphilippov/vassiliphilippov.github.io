regicide

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdʒɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒɪsʌɪd / (BrE)
A person who kills or is responsible for the killing of a king.
killer of a king

Examples

The infamous regicide was executed shortly after the king's death.
Some historians still debate whether Brutus should be considered a regicide or a liberator.
After the regicide, the country was thrown into political turmoil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parricide - person who kills a parent
  • Synonym: patricide - person who kills their father
  • Degree Higher: king - male ruler of an independent state

Etymology

From Latin 'regis' (of a king) + '-cida' (killer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdʒɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʒɪsʌɪd / (BrE)
the act of killing a king or monarch
the killing of a king

Examples

The conspirators committed regicide in their attempt to seize the throne.
Historically, regicide was often met with severe punishment and public outrage.
Many plays and novels have explored the consequences of regicide and its impact on society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patricide - the act of killing one's father
  • Synonym: matricide - the act of killing one's mother
  • Degree Lower: king - male sovereign ruler of a nation or territory

Etymology

From Latin 'rex' (king) + 'cida' (killer from 'caedere', to kill).
