Jasminum officinale

1. NOUN

/ ˌjæzˌmɪnəm əˌfɪʃiˈnɑliː / (AmE), / ˌjæzˌmɪnəm ɒˌfɪsɪˈnɑːliː / (BrE)
a climbing deciduous shrub with fragrant white or yellow or red flowers, commonly known as common jasmine, used in perfume and to flavor tea
species of jasmine plant with fragrant flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with the sweet scent of Jasminum officinale in full bloom.
Many perfumes use extracts from Jasminum officinale to achieve their distinctive aromas.
They added dried Jasminum officinale flowers to the tea for extra fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jasmine - any plant of the genus Jasminum, especially those with fragrant flowers

Etymology

From Latin: Jasminum (jasmine) + officinale (used in medicine/pharmacy).
