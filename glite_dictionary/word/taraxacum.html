Taraxacum

1. NOUN

/ təˈræksəkəm / (AmE), / təˈræksəkəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae, commonly known as dandelions.
genus of plants including dandelions

Examples

Many botanists specialize in the study of Taraxacum due to its worldwide distribution.
The most familiar species of Taraxacum is known as the common dandelion.
Seeds of Taraxacum are easily dispersed by the wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandelion - a common plant with yellow flowers of the genus Taraxacum

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Taraxacum', believed to derive from Arabic 'tarakhshaqūn' (a kind of plant).
