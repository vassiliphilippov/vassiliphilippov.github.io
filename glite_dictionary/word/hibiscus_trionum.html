Hibiscus trionum

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈbɪskəs traɪˈoʊnəm / (AmE), / hɪˈbɪskəs traɪˈɒnəm / (BrE)
an annual weedy herb with short-lived yellow flowers with purple centers, native to the Old World tropics and naturalized in North America
a weedy herb with yellow, purple-eyed flowers

Examples

The garden was overrun with Hibiscus trionum, their distinctive yellow flowers bright against the greenery.
Farmers often consider Hibiscus trionum a weed due to its rapid spread in cultivated fields.
A botanist identified the plant as Hibiscus trionum because of its purple-centered blossoms.

Etymology

From Latin; 'hibiscus' from Greek hibískos (a kind of mallow), 'trionum' relates to its historical association with the number three.
