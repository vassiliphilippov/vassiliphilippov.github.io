undraped

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈdreɪpt / (AmE), / ʌnˈdreɪpt / (BrE)
not covered with drapes, curtains, clothing, or other coverings; exposed or bare.
not covered with drapes or coverings

Examples

The room was filled with sunlight from the undraped windows.
An undraped statue stood on a pedestal in the center of the gallery.
She felt exposed standing before the undraped window at night.

Synset Examples

undraped windows

Related Terms

  • Antonym: draped - covered with drapes, curtains, or coverings
  • Synonym: bare - not covered or clothed; naked or exposed

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) and draped (from drape, to cover with cloth).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈdreɪpt / (AmE), / ʌnˈdreɪpt / (BrE)
not covered with drapery, clothing, or any kind of fabric; uncovered or naked, often used to describe statues or artistic representations.
not covered; uncovered or naked

Examples

The artist preferred to sculpt undraped figures, believing they revealed true human form.
The undraped statue in the center of the gallery attracted many curious visitors.
In ancient art, gods and heroes were often depicted undraped to symbolize purity and strength.

Synset Examples

the undraped statue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naked - not wearing any clothes; bare
  • Antonym: covered - having a covering or being draped

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'draped' (covered with cloth or drapery).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
