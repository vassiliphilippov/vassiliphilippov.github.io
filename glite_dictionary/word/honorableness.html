honorableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːnərəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈɒnərəbəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being deserving of honor or respect; the state of having high moral standards
quality of deserving honor or respect

Examples

Her honorableness was evident in the way she treated everyone with fairness and dignity.
The judge was known for his honorableness throughout his long and respected career.
Acts of honorableness are valued in every culture and society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honor - high respect or esteem; a sense of what is morally right
  • Antonym: dishonor - loss or lack of respect or reputation; disgrace

Etymology

Formed from 'honorable' + '-ness', with 'honorable' from Old French honorable and Latin honorabilis.
