askance

1. ADV

/ əˈskæns / (AmE), / əˈskɑːns / (BrE)
with an attitude or look of suspicion, disapproval, or distrust
with suspicion or disapproval

Examples

The manager glanced askance at my request, clearly not convinced.
She viewed the newcomer's odd behavior askance.
He reacted askance when told about the sudden change in plans.

Synset Examples

he looked askance at the offer

Etymology

From Italian a scancio (aslant, askew).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00273330-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ əˈskæns / (AmE), / əˈskɑːns / (BrE)
with a look or glance that expresses doubt, suspicion, or disapproval; to the side
with doubt or suspicion; to the side

Examples

She looked askance at the stranger who entered the meeting late.
The teacher regarded his explanation askance, clearly doubting its truth.
Whenever he mentioned his grand plans, his friends glanced askance at each other.

Synset Examples

her eyes with their misted askance look
sidelong glances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suspiciously - in a way that shows distrust or doubt
  • Antonym: admiringly - in a way that shows admiration (contrasts the negative implication of 'askance')

Etymology

Probably from Old Italian scansare (to slant), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00766264-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADV

/ əˈskæns / (AmE), / əˈskɑːns / (BrE)
With a look or glance that is sideways or oblique, often suggesting suspicion or disapproval.
sideways or suspicious glance

Examples

The teacher looked askance at the student who arrived late for the third time that week.
Whenever he hears gossip, he tends to glance askance at those spreading the rumors.
Her parents viewed his unusual career choice askance but let her make her own decision.

Synset Examples

did not quite turn all the way back but looked askance at me with her dark eyes

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps from Old Italian 'a scancio' (slanting).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00273182-r
Synset POS: r
