Fyodor Dostoyevsky

1. PROPN

/ ˈfjɔdər ˌdɔstoʊˈjɛfski / (AmE), / ˈfjɔːdɔː ˌdɒstɔɪˈjɛfski / (BrE)
(1821–1881) A Russian novelist noted for his exploration of human psychology and suffering, and for his works combining profound insight with humor.
Russian novelist known for psychological insight

Examples

Fyodor Dostoyevsky is best known for novels such as 'Crime and Punishment' and 'The Brothers Karamazov'.
Many scholars have analyzed the existential themes present in Fyodor Dostoyevsky's works.
I was deeply moved by the depth of human emotion in Fyodor Dostoyevsky's writing.

Etymology

Transliteration of Russian Фёдор Достоевский (Fyodor Dostoyevskiy).
