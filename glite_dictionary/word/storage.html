storage

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɔrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈstɔːrɪdʒ / (BrE)
A device or component in a computer system used to store electronic data or information.
computer memory device

Examples

The new laptop has a solid-state storage device for faster performance.
Data can be transferred from your phone to cloud storage safely and securely.
Upgrading the storage capacity of the server will allow it to hold more files.

Synset Examples

a memory and the CPU form the central part of a computer to which peripherals are attached

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memory - component of a computer that stores data temporarily or permanently

Etymology

From store + -age, meaning the act of storing or a place for storing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03749767-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɔːrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈstɔːrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a place or facility where goods, materials, or equipment are kept until needed; a depository for goods
place where goods are kept

Examples

The company uses a secure storage facility to keep its inventory safe.
After moving, they put all their extra furniture into storage.
Seasonal decorations are kept in storage until they are needed.

Synset Examples

storehouses were built close to the docks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warehouse - a large building for storing goods
  • Synonym: depository - a place where things are stored safely

Etymology

From the verb 'store' + -age, modelled on similar French formations.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈstɔrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈstɔːrɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act of keeping or placing things in a particular space or container so that they can be used later
the act of keeping or storing things

Examples

We rented a unit for extra storage during our move.
Proper storage of food helps prevent spoilage.
This hard drive has enough storage for all your documents and photos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: store - to put things away for future use; to keep something in a place
  • Antonym: retrieval - the act of getting something back from storage

Etymology

From Old French 'estorage' (a store), from 'estorer' (to store).

6. NOUN

/ ˈstɔrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈstɔːrɪdʒ / (BrE)
The business or activity of keeping goods and materials in a place for future use or distribution.
commercial activity of keeping goods

Examples

The company offers storage facilities for both personal and commercial clients.
Due to limited space, they had to use an external storage service for their products.
She works in storage and handles inventory management for the warehouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warehouse - a large building for storing goods
  • Synonym: logistics - the organization of moving and storing goods

Etymology

From Old French *estorage*, from *estor* (to store), ultimately from Latin *instaurare* (to renew, restore).
