plumping

1. ADJ

/ ˈplʌmpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplʌmpɪŋ / (BrE)
very large or exceptional in size for its kind; substantial or considerable.
very large; substantial

Examples

The bill passed with a plumping majority in parliament.
She received a plumping pay raise after her promotion.
It was a plumping sum for such a small job.

Synset Examples

won by a plumping majority

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substantial - considerable in amount or size
  • Synonym: massive - exceptionally large or heavy

Etymology

From the verb "plump" meaning to become or make plump; used figuratively for substantial amounts or sizes.
