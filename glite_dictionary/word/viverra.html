Viverra

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈvɛrə / (AmE), / vɪˈvɛrə / (BrE)
a genus of mammals in the family Viverridae, which includes the civets
genus of mammals in Viverridae (civets)

Examples

The genus Viverra is characterized by its slender body and long tail.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Viverra genus in Southeast Asia.
Many members of Viverra are known for their musky scent used in marking territory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Viverridae - the family of mammals that includes civets and genets

2. NOUN

/ vɪˈvɛrə / (AmE), / vɪˈvɛrə / (BrE)
A genus of carnivorous mammals in the family Viverridae, including certain civets native to Asia and Africa.
genus of civets (Viverridae family)

Examples

The large Indian civet belongs to the genus Viverra and is found throughout South and Southeast Asia.
Species in the genus Viverra are known for their distinctive body markings and scent glands.
Zoologists have identified several members of the Viverra genus living in tropical forests.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Viverridae - the family of mammals including civets and genets

Etymology

From Latin 'viverra' meaning 'ferret'.
