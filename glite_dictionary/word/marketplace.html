marketplace

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkɪtˌpleɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪtˌpleɪs / (BrE)
the world or sphere of commercial activity where goods and services are bought and sold
sphere of buying and selling activities

Examples

Competition in the marketplace drives innovation and better prices for consumers.
In today's global marketplace, companies must adapt quickly to changing trends.
The book explores how technology is transforming the traditional marketplace.

Synset Examples

without competition there would be no market
they were driven from the marketplace

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: market - an actual place or event where people gather to buy and sell goods

Etymology

From Middle English market place, meaning ‘public square where a market was held’.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkətˌpleɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪtˌpleɪs / (BrE)
an open area or building in a town where people gather to buy and sell goods, especially regularly or in large numbers
place where goods are bought and sold

Examples

Every Saturday, the town’s marketplace is filled with stalls selling fresh produce and handmade crafts.
The old marketplace has been renovated and now hosts a lively farmers’ market.
Tourists often gather in the central marketplace to sample local foods and buy souvenirs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market - an open space where a market is held; the act of buying and selling

Etymology

From Middle English 'market place', combining 'market' and 'place'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03727779-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrkɪtˌpleɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪtˌpleɪs / (BrE)
the realm of commercial activity where goods and services are bought and sold, often involving competition between businesses
the realm of buying and selling goods and services

Examples

In the digital marketplace, companies fiercely compete for consumers' attention and loyalty.
New technologies are rapidly transforming the global marketplace in unexpected ways.
Regulations are necessary to ensure fairness in the marketplace.

Synset Examples

without competition there would be no market
they were driven from the marketplace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market - an area or arena in which commercial dealings are conducted

Etymology

From 'market' (from Latin mercatus, trade, market) + 'place'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01099197-n
Synset POS: n
