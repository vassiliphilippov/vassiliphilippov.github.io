to occidentalize

1. VERB

/ ˌɑːk.sɪˈden.təl.aɪz / (AmE), / ˌɒk.sɪˈden.təl.aɪz / (BrE)
to make (someone or something) adopt or resemble the customs, values, or characteristics of Western (especially European or American) culture
make western in character

Examples

Rulers in the 19th century attempted to occidentalize their societies.
Some traditional practices disappeared as the country began to occidentalize.
The school's curriculum was occidentalized to align with Western educational standards.

Synset Examples

The country was Westernized after it opened up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to westernize - to make western in character or customs

Etymology

From Latin 'occidens' (the west) + '-ize'.
