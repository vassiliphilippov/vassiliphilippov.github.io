wollastonite

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːləs.təˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɒl.ə.stəˌnaɪt / (BrE)
a white or greyish mineral made of calcium silicate, commonly found in metamorphic limestone and used as an industrial raw material
a white or greyish calcium silicate mineral

Examples

The ceramic industry uses wollastonite because of its high heat resistance.
Thin veins of wollastonite were discovered within the metamorphic rocks.
Geologists identified the white mineral as wollastonite during their field survey.

Etymology

Named after English chemist and mineralogist William Hyde Wollaston (1766–1828).
