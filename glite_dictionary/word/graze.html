graze

1. VERB

/ ɡreɪz / (AmE), / ɡreɪz / (BrE)
to break or scrape the skin slightly, typically causing a minor wound
scrape or break the skin slightly

Examples

She grazed her knee when she fell off the bike.
The bullet grazed his arm, leaving a shallow wound.
Children often graze their elbows while playing outside.

Synset Examples

She was grazed by the stray bullet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scratch - to cut or damage the surface of the skin or something lightly

2. VERB

/ ɡreɪz / (AmE), / ɡreɪz / (BrE)
to allow animals to feed on grass in a field, pasture, or meadow
allow animals to feed on grass

Examples

Farmers often graze their cattle in open pastures during the summer months.
The shepherd moved the flock to a new field to graze.
After the rain, the land became lush and ready for the animals to graze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasture - land covered with grass used for grazing
  • Synonym: browse - to eat leaves, twigs, or other high-growing vegetation

Etymology

From Middle English *grasen*, from Old English *grasian* (to feed on grass), from *græs* (grass).
