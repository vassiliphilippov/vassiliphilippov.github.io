daring

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdeə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
radically new or original, especially in a way that takes risks or challenges conventional ideas
radically new or original

Examples

The architect is famous for his daring designs that break all conventions.
It took a daring approach to reinvent the traditional play format.
Their daring ideas have transformed the tech industry.

Synset Examples

an avant-garde theater piece

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innovative - featuring new methods; advanced and original
  • Antonym: conservative - adhering to traditional styles or ideas; opposed to change
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01691548-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdeərɪŋ / (BrE)
the willingness to take risks or face danger; boldness or courage
willingness to take risks; boldness

Examples

Her daring in the face of adversity inspired everyone around her.
The explorer's daring led him into uncharted territories.
It takes a certain amount of daring to start your own company.

Synset Examples

the proposal required great boldness
the plan required great hardiness of heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boldness - willingness to take risks; confidence
  • Synonym: courage - the ability to confront fear or danger
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

From Middle English *deringe*, from Old English *dearr* (to dare) + *-ing*.

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˈdɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdeərɪŋ / (BrE)
Willing to take risks; showing a readiness to do dangerous or unusual things.
willing to take risks

Examples

The climber made a daring ascent of the nearly vertical cliff.
Her daring decision to start a business at a young age impressed her friends.
The film features a daring rescue scene that left audiences breathless.

Synset Examples

audacious visions of the total conquest of space
an audacious interpretation of two Jacobean dramas
the most daring of contemporary fiction writers
a venturesome investor
a venturous spirit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audacious - showing willingness to take bold risks
  • Antonym: timid - lacking courage or confidence

Etymology

Present participle of 'dare,' from Old English *dearr* (I dare).
