moorage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʊrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a place where a boat or ship can be secured, typically with a line or anchor
a place to secure a boat or ship

Examples

We reserved a moorage for our sailboat at the marina.
The lack of available moorage during the busy season can be frustrating for boaters.
After a long day at sea, the crew found a secure moorage near the harbor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: berth - a place for a ship to stay in a port
  • Synonym: anchorage - a place suitable for a ship to anchor

Etymology

From moor (to secure a boat) + -age (noun-forming suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʊrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːrɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act of securing a vessel with ropes, especially when it arrives at a dock or port
securing a vessel with ropes

Examples

The moorage of the ship was completed just before the storm began.
Vessels require secure moorage to prevent drifting overnight at the marina.
Improper moorage can lead to damage during rough weather conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: berthing - the act of providing or securing a berth for a vessel
  • Synonym: anchorage - the act of anchoring a vessel

Etymology

From 'moor' (to anchor or tie a vessel) + '-age' forming a noun for the act or fee.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00052966-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmʊrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a charge or fee paid for securing a boat or ship to a dock, buoy, or mooring location
fee for securing a boat or ship

Examples

The moorage for our sailboat at the marina is paid monthly.
They raised the moorage rates this season due to increased maintenance costs.
If you don't pay your moorage on time, you could lose your reserved dock space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: docking fee - charge for securing a boat at a dock

Etymology

From 'moor' (to secure a boat) + '-age' (denoting an action or fee).
