wing-nut

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪŋ ˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋ ˌnʌt / (BrE)
A deciduous tree of the genus Pterocarya, native to regions from the Caucasus to southeastern Asia, or its small, winged, nutlike fruit.
tree of genus Pterocarya or its winged fruit

Examples

The wing-nut is prized for its rapid growth and attractive foliage in landscaping.
Each wing-nut tree produces clusters of small nuts, each with wing-like structures that aid in wind dispersal.
Botanists collected samples of the wing-nut's distinctive fruit during their research trip in Asia.

Etymology

Formed from 'wing' and 'nut', describing the fruit's shape.
