makeover

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪkˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkəʊvə / (BrE)
an overall beauty treatment or change in appearance, typically involving hair, makeup, and clothing, intended to improve how someone looks
total change or improvement in appearance

Examples

After her makeover, everyone commented on how confident and stylish she appeared.
The TV show featured dramatic makeovers for people wanting a fresh start.
He decided to get a complete makeover before the big job interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revamp - to give a new and improved form, structure, or appearance to something

Etymology

From the phrase 'make over', meaning to change or transform, joined as a compound noun in the mid-20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪkˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkˌəʊvə / (BrE)
A complete transformation or renovation of something, especially to improve its appearance or function.
complete transformation or renovation

Examples

The old hotel received a stunning makeover and now looks completely modern.
After her room's makeover, she barely recognized it.
The company is planning a makeover of its brand image next year.

Synset Examples

the blighted neighborhood underwent a total makeover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renovation - the action of restoring something to a good state of repair
  • Synonym: restoration - the process of returning something to its original condition
  • Synonym: transformation - a thorough or dramatic change in form or appearance

Etymology

Formed from 'make' + 'over', indicating an act of making something over or anew.
