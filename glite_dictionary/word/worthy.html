worthy

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɝːði / (AmE), / ˈwɜːði / (BrE)
having qualities or abilities that merit attention, recognition, or a specified action
deserving of recognition or attention

Examples

She received an award for her worthy contributions to the community.
That was a worthy effort, even if it did not succeed.
We only support worthy causes that truly help those in need.

Synset Examples

behavior worthy of reprobation
a fact worthy of attention

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deserving - having qualities that merit something, especially reward or punishment
  • Antonym: unworthy - not deserving; lacking merit or value

Etymology

From Old English *weorþig*, from *weorþ* ('worthy'), related to worth.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01024736-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrði / (AmE), / ˈwɜːði / (BrE)
a person who is important, respected, or honorable, often used with humorous or ironic intent
an important or respected person

Examples

The mayor invited several local worthies to speak at the anniversary event.
At the banquet, all the town’s worthies gathered to celebrate the new hospital.
Some of the region’s most celebrated worthies attended the launch of the new library.

Synset Examples

he told his story to some conservative worthies
local worthies rarely challenged the chief constable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dignitary - a person considered to be important because of high rank or office
  • Synonym: notable - a famous or important person

Etymology

From Old English worthig (‘valuable, honorable’), from worth (‘worthy, deserving’).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10812146-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈwɝːði / (AmE), / ˈwɜːði / (BrE)
considered to have the qualities or value that make someone suitable to be chosen, especially as a spouse.
having qualities making one suitable (esp. as a spouse)

Examples

She was considered a worthy match for the prince by both families.
The tradition is to find a worthy partner who will uphold family values.
He searched for years to meet someone truly worthy of sharing his life.

Synset Examples

the parents found the girl suitable for their son

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suitable - appropriate; right for a particular person or purpose
  • Synonym: eligible - having the right qualifications; suitable to be chosen

Etymology

From Old English *weorþig* (valuable, deserving).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00855376-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈwɝːði / (AmE), / ˈwɜːði / (BrE)
deserving respect, attention, or admiration because of having value, merit, or honorable qualities.
deserving respect or admiration

Examples

She dedicated her time to a worthy cause, helping people in need.
Only worthy candidates will be considered for the award.
He proved himself to be a worthy opponent in the championship match.

Synset Examples

a worthy fellow
a worthy cause

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deserving - having qualities worthy of reward or praise
  • Antonym: unworthy - not deserving respect, admiration, or value

Etymology

From Old English weorþig (valuable, honorable).

Source: WordNet
