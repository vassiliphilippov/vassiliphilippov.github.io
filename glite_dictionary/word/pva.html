PVA

1. NOUN

/ ˌpi ˌvi ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌpiː ˌviː ˈeɪ / (BrE)
a synthetic resin (polyvinyl acetate) used chiefly in paints, adhesives, and coatings
a synthetic resin used in paints and adhesives

Examples

The craftsman used PVA to bond the wood pieces together securely.
Most school glues contain PVA as the main ingredient.
The walls were primed with a coat of PVA before painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyvinyl acetate - the full chemical name of PVA, a synthetic resin
  • Synonym: glue - a common adhesive substance; often contains PVA

Etymology

Abbreviation of polyvinyl acetate, from poly- (many) + vinyl + acetate.
