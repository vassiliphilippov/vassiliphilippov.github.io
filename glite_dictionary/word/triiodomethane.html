triiodomethane

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌaɪədoʊˈmɛθeɪn / (AmE), / traɪˌaɪədəʊˈmiːθeɪn / (BrE)
A yellow crystalline compound with a strong odor, sometimes used as an antiseptic.
yellow crystalline antiseptic compound
medical

Examples

The doctor applied triiodomethane to the wound to prevent infection.
In chemistry class, we studied the structure and properties of triiodomethane.
Pure triiodomethane has a distinctive yellow color and a strong medicinal odor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iodoform - An alternative name for triiodomethane, used as an antiseptic

Etymology

From tri- (three) + iodo- (relating to iodine) + methane (the simplest alkane).
