handhold

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈhændˌhəʊld / (BrE)
a part of an object or structure designed to be held by the hand for support or stability
something to hold onto for support

Examples

She reached out for the handhold as the bus lurched forward.
The climbing wall is covered with brightly colored handholds and footholds.
Make sure every ladder has a secure handhold to prevent accidents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foothold - a place where a person's foot can be placed to support them, especially while climbing

Etymology

From 'hand' + 'hold', meaning something for the hand to hold.
