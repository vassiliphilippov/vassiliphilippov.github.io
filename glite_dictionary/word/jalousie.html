jalousie

1. NOUN

/ ˈʒæləsi / (AmE), / ˈʒæləsi / (BrE)
A blind or shutter with horizontal angled slats that allow light and air to pass through but exclude direct sun and rain.
shutter with angled slats

Examples

The old mansion still had its original jalousies on every window.
She closed the jalousie to keep out the midday sun but let in the breeze.
Maintenance workers painted the wooden jalousies to preserve them from weather damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: louver - a set of angled slats for admitting air and light but keeping out rain

Etymology

From French 'jalousie', meaning 'jealousy', originally referring to slatted blinds that allowed a person to look out without being seen.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæləsi / (AmE), / ˈʒæləsi / (BrE)
A window made with parallel glass or wooden louvers that can be adjusted to control airflow and light.
window with adjustable glass louvers

Examples

The old house featured a jalousie on the kitchen window that let in a pleasant breeze.
He repaired the broken slats on the jalousie to improve ventilation.
Many tropical homes use jalousie windows to keep cool in hot climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: louver - a set of angled slats for regulating airflow or light

Etymology

From French 'jalousie' (shutter, jealousy), perhaps due to the idea of concealing from view.
