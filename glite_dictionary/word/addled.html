addled

1. ADJ

/ ˈæd.əld / (AmE), / ˈæd.əld / (BrE)
(of eggs) no longer fit to be eaten; rotten or spoiled.
(of eggs) spoiled, rotten

Examples

The farmer found several addled eggs in the nest during the morning check.
An addled egg gives off a distinctive odor and should not be eaten.
She quickly discarded the addled egg before cooking the rest.

Synset Examples

an addled egg

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *adel* (liquid filth, urine).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæd.əld / (AmE), / ˈæd.əld / (BrE)
confused and unable to think clearly; muddled, especially of someone's mind or thoughts
confused or muddled in mind

Examples

After staying up all night, his addled mind struggled to focus on the exam.
She gave him an addled look, clearly confused by the instructions.
The medication left me feeling addled for most of the afternoon.

Synset Examples

muddleheaded ideas
your addled little brain
woolly thinking
woolly-headed ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muddled - confused or mixed up in mind; not clear
  • Antonym: lucid - expressed clearly; easy to understand; clear-headed

Etymology

From Middle English 'adled', meaning 'empty of offspring,' later generalized to 'spoiled' or 'confused', probably from Old English 'adlian' (to become sick).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
