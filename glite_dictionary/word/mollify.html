to mollify

1. VERB

/ ˈmɑː.ləˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɒl.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make something less severe, angry, or harsh, often by calming or conciliating; to make more acceptable or suitable
make less severe or more acceptable

Examples

She tried to mollify her angry colleague by offering an apology.
The CEO’s statement did little to mollify public criticism about the company’s actions.
Adding a touch of honey can mollify the sharpness of the lemon juice in the recipe.

Synset Examples

she tempered her criticism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appease - to calm or pacify, usually by giving in to demands
  • Antonym: to aggravate - to make worse or more severe

Etymology

From Latin mollificare, from mollis (soft) + facere (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈmɑːlɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɒlɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make someone less angry or upset; to calm or soothe someone's feelings
to calm or appease someone

Examples

The manager tried to mollify the frustrated employees with promises of change.
Her soothing words helped mollify his irritation after the disagreement.
He brought flowers in an attempt to mollify his girlfriend after their argument.

Synset Examples

She managed to mollify the angry customer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appease - to pacify or placate, especially by acceding to demands
  • Antonym: anger - a strong feeling of annoyance or hostility

Etymology

From Latin mollificare, from mollis (soft) + -ficare (to make).

3. VERB

/ ˈmɑː.ləˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɒl.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make something less severe, rigid, or intense; to soften or appease.
to soften or appease

Examples

The company tried to mollify angry customers with a public apology.
Nothing could mollify his stubborn determination during the negotiations.
A gentle breeze can sometimes mollify the harshness of a cold winter night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to soften - to make or become less hard or severe
  • Antonym: to harden - to make or become more rigid or severe

Etymology

From Latin mollificare (to make soft), from mollis (soft) + facere (to make).
