first-rate

1. ADV

/ ˌfɝstˈreɪt / (AmE), / ˌfɜːstˈreɪt / (BrE)
(adverb) In a very good or excellent manner; extremely well.
very well; excellently

Examples

Despite her injury, she performed first-rate in the finals.
He was feeling anything but first-rate after the long journey.
The team worked first-rate to finish the project on time.

Synset Examples

she doesn't feel first-rate today

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superbly - in an excellent or impressive way
  • Antonym: poorly - in a bad or unsatisfactory way

Etymology

From 'first' + 'rate,' originally referring to the highest class of ships in the British Navy.

2. ADJ

/ ˌfɝstˈreɪt / (AmE), / ˌfɜːstˈreɪt / (BrE)
Of the highest quality; excellent.
of the highest quality

Examples

The hotel offered first-rate service and amenities to all its guests.
He is a first-rate pianist who has won several international competitions.
The museum's first-rate collection attracts art lovers from around the world.

Synset Examples

an ace reporter
a crack shot
a first-rate golfer
a super party
played top-notch tennis
an athlete in tiptop condition
she is absolutely tops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: top-notch - of the highest quality; excellent
  • Synonym: superb - of very high quality; excellent
  • Antonym: second-rate - of poor or inferior quality

Etymology

From the former British naval rating system, where 'first rate' referred to the most powerful class of warships.
