grudging

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡrʌdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
Given or done unwillingly or with reluctance.
unwilling or reluctant

Examples

She offered a grudging apology after being confronted about her mistake.
His grudging admiration became obvious when he praised the team's efforts.
They gave grudging approval to the new plan, despite their initial reservations.

Synset Examples

gave grudging consent
grudging acceptance of his opponent's victory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reluctant - unwilling or disinclined to do something
  • Antonym: willing - acting or ready to act gladly or without reluctance

Etymology

From the verb 'grudge' + -ing, first used in the 17th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡrʌdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
reluctant or unwilling to give, grant, or allow something; given or done unwillingly
reluctant or unwilling

Examples

She gave a grudging apology after being confronted by her manager.
The company offered a grudging pay increase to its employees.
He made a grudging donation to the charity, showing little enthusiasm.

Synset Examples

a niggardly tip

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generous - willing to give or share; opposite of grudging

Etymology

From 'grudge' (Middle English gruggen, probably of Scandinavian origin) + -ing.
