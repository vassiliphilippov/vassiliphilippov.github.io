pieta

1. NOUN

/ piˈeɪtə / (AmE), / piˈeɪtə / (BrE)
A representation, especially in art, of the Virgin Mary mourning over the dead body of Jesus Christ.
artistic depiction of Mary mourning Jesus

Examples

Michelangelo's pieta in St. Peter's Basilica is one of the most famous sculptures in the world.
The museum's newest exhibit includes a breathtaking pieta carved from marble.
The artist decided to paint a modern pieta, reimagining the traditional subject in a contemporary setting.

Etymology

From Italian 'pietà' meaning 'pity' or 'compassion', from Latin 'pietas'.
