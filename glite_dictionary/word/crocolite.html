crocolite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊkəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkrɒkəlaɪt / (BrE)
A rare lead chromate mineral that forms bright orange crystals, also known as crocoite.
a rare bright orange mineral

Examples

The museum displayed a stunning specimen of crocolite with vivid orange crystals.
Geologists found traces of crocolite in the old lead mine.
He collects rare minerals, but crocolite is one of his most prized finds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocoite - another name for crocolite

Etymology

From Greek krokos (saffron) for its color, plus -ite.
