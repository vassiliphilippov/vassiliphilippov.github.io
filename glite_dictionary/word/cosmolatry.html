cosmolatry

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːzˈmɑːlətri / (AmE), / ˌkɒzˈmɒlətri / (BrE)
the worship of the cosmos or the universe as a divine entity
worship of the cosmos

Examples

Ancient philosophers sometimes promoted cosmolatry alongside traditional religion.
The priest taught that cosmolatry was misguided, insisting on the worship of a single deity instead.
Her poetry reflected a kind of cosmolatry, revering the universe as a living presence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idolatry - the worship of idols
  • Degree Lower: cosmology - the science of the origin and development of the universe

Etymology

From Greek 'kosmos' (world, universe) and '-latry' (worship).
