woody

1. ADJ

/ ˈwʊdi / (AmE), / ˈwʊdi / (BrE)
made of, containing, or resembling wood; having the qualities or characteristics of wood
made of or like wood

Examples

The botanist explained that oaks and pines are examples of woody plants.
Over time, the shrub developed a thick, woody stem at its base.
The wine had a slightly woody flavor that reminded me of oak barrels.

Synset Examples

woody plants
perennial herbs with woody stems
a woody taste

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wooden - made of wood
  • Antonym: herbaceous - (of plants) not woody; having soft stems

Etymology

From Old English wudu (wood) + -y (adjective suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈwʊdi / (AmE), / ˈwʊdi / (BrE)
(of an area) having many trees; covered with or resembling woodland.
covered with trees; woodland-like

Examples

The air felt cool and fresh in the woody hills above the town.
They decided to camp in a woody area for more shade and privacy.
Spring flowers bloom early in the woody corners of the old park.

Synset Examples

an arboreous landscape
violets in woodsy shady spots
a woody area near the highway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forest - large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth
  • Antonym: treeless - lacking trees; without trees

Etymology

From Old English wudu (wood) + -y (adjectival suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02583630-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈwʊdi / (AmE), / ˈwʊdi / (BrE)
having a hard structure like wood, usually as a result of the deposition of lignin in the cell walls
having a hard, wood-like structure

Examples

The plant develops a woody stem after several years of growth.
Certain shrubs remain herbaceous, while others become woody as they mature.
With age, the root system turns woody and difficult to cut through.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: herbaceous - having the characteristics of an herb; not woody
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
