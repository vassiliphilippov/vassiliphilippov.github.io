Asclepias purpurascens

1. NOUN

/ əˈskliːpiəs ˌpɜːrpjəˈræskənz / (AmE), / əˈskliːpiəs ˌpɜːpjʊˈræskɛnz / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to eastern North America, known for its pink-purple flowers; also called purple milkweed.
a perennial North American plant with pink-purple flowers

Examples

The Asclepias purpurascens in our garden attracted dozens of butterflies in early summer.
Conservationists are working to restore populations of Asclepias purpurascens across its native range.
She identified the wildflower as Asclepias purpurascens by its distinctive pink-purple blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purple milkweed - another common name for Asclepias purpurascens

Etymology

From Greek 'Asklēpias', the genus name honoring Asclepius, the god of medicine, and Latin 'purpurascens' meaning 'turning purple'.
