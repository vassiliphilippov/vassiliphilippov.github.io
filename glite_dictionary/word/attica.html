Attica

1. PROPN

/ ˈætɪkə / (AmE), / ˈætɪkə / (BrE)
A historical region of ancient Greece that included Athens and its surrounding territory, noted for its use of the Ionic dialect.
ancient region of Greece including Athens

Examples

The city of Athens was located in Attica, an important cultural center of the ancient world.
Many ancient Greek philosophers were born or lived in Attica.
The architecture of Attica is renowned for its classical beauty and influence.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀττική (Attikē), the name of the region.
