to outwit

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈwɪt / (AmE), / aʊtˈwɪt / (BrE)
to defeat or get the better of someone by being more clever or cunning than they are
defeat by cleverness or cunning

Examples

The chess champion managed to outwit all his opponents in the tournament.
She tried to outwit her brother in every board game they played together.
It takes quick thinking to outwit a skilled detective.

Synset Examples

I beat the traffic
She outfoxed her competitors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outsmart - to defeat someone by being more intelligent or clever
  • Synonym: to outfox - to defeat someone through cunning or cleverness

Etymology

From prefix 'out-' (meaning surpass) + 'wit' (intelligence, cleverness), first recorded in the 17th century.
