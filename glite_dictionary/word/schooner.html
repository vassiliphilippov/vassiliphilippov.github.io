schooner

1. NOUN

/ ˈskuːnər / (AmE), / ˈskuːnə / (BrE)
a sailing ship with two or more masts, typically with the foremast shorter than the mainmast, used especially in the past
type of sailing vessel

Examples

The old schooner sailed gracefully into the harbor at sunset.
During their expedition, they traveled up the coast in a sturdy schooner.
He gazed at the model schooner displayed in the maritime museum.

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch 'schoener', from 'schoon' (beautiful) + '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskuːnər / (AmE), / ˈskuːnə / (BrE)
a large glass used for serving beer, especially in Australia and the UK
large beer glass
british

Examples

He ordered a schooner of pale ale at the pub.
In Australia, a schooner is smaller than a pint but larger than a middy.
She raised her schooner in a toast to celebrate the promotion.

Etymology

Adapted from the original meaning of 'schooner' (a type of ship), possibly referring to the large size of the glass resembling the spaciousness of the vessel.
