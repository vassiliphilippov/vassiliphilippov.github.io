Polypedates

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɒlɪpəˈdeɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪpəˈdeɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of tree frogs belonging to the family Polypedatidae, found mainly in South and Southeast Asia.
genus of tree frogs

Examples

The Polypedates genus includes several species of tree frogs known for their distinctive mating calls.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Polypedates genus in the rainforests of Borneo.
Members of Polypedates are commonly found breeding in temporary pools during the monsoon season.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'pedates' (footed).
