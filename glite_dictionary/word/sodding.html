sodding

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɑdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɒdɪŋ / (BrE)
(especially British, informal) Used as an intensifier to express annoyance or emphasis, often with mild irritation; sometimes considered mildly vulgar.
intensifier expressing annoyance or emphasis
informal vulgar british

Examples

I can’t believe the sodding bus is late again!
Turn off that sodding alarm, it’s driving me mad.
He forgot to bring the sodding tickets to the concert.

Etymology

From British slang 'sod', a mild expletive, originally referring to a sodomite (offensive), now generalized as a mild term of annoyance.
