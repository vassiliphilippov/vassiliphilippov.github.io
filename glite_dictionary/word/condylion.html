condylion

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdɪliˌɑn / (AmE), / kɒnˈdɪlɪən / (BrE)
(Anatomy) The craniometric point at the tip of the mandibular condyle, used as a reference in measurements of the jaw.
anatomical point at tip of jaw condyle
medical

Examples

The surgeon marked the condylion to ensure accurate placement of the jaw implant.
In anthropological studies, measurements from the condylion are used to evaluate mandibular size.
Precise identification of the condylion is important in craniometric analysis and dental research.

Etymology

From Greek kondylos (knuckle, joint) + -ion (diminutive suffix).
