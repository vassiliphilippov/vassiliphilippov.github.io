removability

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌmuːvəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / rɪˌmuːvəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality or condition of being able to be dismissed or removed from a position, especially from office.
state of being able to be removed from office

Examples

The new constitution increased the removability of government officials to ensure accountability.
Questions were raised about the removability of directors under the current corporate bylaws.
He argued for the removability of judges who violated ethical standards.

Synset Examples

The ‘democratic revolution’ emphasized the delegation of authority and the removability of officials, precisely because, as we shall see, neither delegation nor removability were much recognized in actual institutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: removal - the act of removing someone or something

Etymology

From 'removable' + '-ity', based on Latin '-bilis' and '-itas' (quality, condition).
