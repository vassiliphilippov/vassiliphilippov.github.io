nonresistance

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑn.rɪˈzɪs.təns / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.rɪˈzɪs.təns / (BrE)
the principle or practice of not using physical force or violence, even in self-defense or when confronted by aggression
refusal to use violence, even in self-defense

Examples

The community practiced nonresistance, refusing to defend themselves with violence even when threatened.
Religious groups advocating nonresistance often cite moral or spiritual reasons for their stance.
His strong belief in nonresistance shaped his response to conflict and injustice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pacifism - the belief that any form of violence or war is unjustifiable
  • Synonym: nonviolence - the practice of refusing to use violence

Etymology

From non- (not) + resistance (from Latin resistere 'to withstand').
