legalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːɡəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
Strict adherence to the letter of the law or to prescribed rules, often at the expense of the spirit or intention of those laws.
strict adherence to laws or rules

Examples

The judge criticized the defendant's reliance on legalism to circumvent the true intent of the contract.
Some religious groups have been accused of practicing legalism at the expense of compassion.
Debates over tax law often expose the limits of legalism in complex societal issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: literalism - adherence to the explicit meaning of laws or texts
  • Antonym: antinomianism - the rejection of the binding force of laws or rules

Etymology

From Latin 'legalis' (legal) + '-ism' (practice or doctrine).
