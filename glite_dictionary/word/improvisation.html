improvisation

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˌprɑːvəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪmˌprɒvɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
something that is created or done spontaneously, without planning, especially as an immediate solution to a problem.
spontaneous, unplanned solution or creation

Examples

When the tent poles broke, Tom's improvisation with sticks kept the shelter standing overnight.
The chef's improvisation in the kitchen saved the dinner after an ingredient went missing.
Their sound system failed, and a quick improvisation using a megaphone saved the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ad-lib - something said or done without preparation
  • Antonym: preparation - the action or process of making ready or being made ready

Etymology

From French 'improvisation', from Latin 'improvisus' (unforeseen).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪmprəvəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪmˌprɒvɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of creating or performing something spontaneously or without preparation, especially in music, speech, or writing
spontaneous or unprepared creation or performance

Examples

The actor's improvisation on stage delighted the audience with its creativity.
Jazz musicians often rely on improvisation during live performances.
Her speech was an impressive improvisation after the original notes were lost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extemporization - the act of composing or performing without preparation
  • Antonym: preparation - the act of preparing ahead rather than acting spontaneously

Etymology

From French 'improvisation', from Latin 'improvisus' (unforeseen).
