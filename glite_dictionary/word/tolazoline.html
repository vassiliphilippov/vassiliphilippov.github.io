tolazoline

1. NOUN

/ toʊˈlæzəˌliːn / (AmE), / təʊˈlæzəliːn / (BrE)
a drug that acts as a vasodilator, used to treat spasms of peripheral blood vessels, such as in acrocyanosis.
vasodilator drug for blood vessel spasms
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed tolazoline to alleviate her peripheral vascular spasms.
When other medications failed, tolazoline proved effective in improving blood flow to his extremities.
Pharmacists must be cautious when dispensing tolazoline due to its potential side effects.
