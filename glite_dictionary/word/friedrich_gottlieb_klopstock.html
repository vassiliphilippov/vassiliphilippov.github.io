Friedrich Gottlieb Klopstock

1. PROPN

/ ˈfriːdrɪk ˈɡɒtliːb ˈklɔpˌstɒk / (AmE), / ˈfriːdrɪç ˈɡɔtliːp ˈklɔpstɔk / (BrE)
(1724–1803) A German poet celebrated for his epic and lyrical poetry, especially 'The Messiah', which greatly influenced German literature.
German poet (1724–1803)

Examples

Friedrich Gottlieb Klopstock is best known for his epic poem 'The Messiah'.
The influence of Friedrich Gottlieb Klopstock can be seen in later German Romantic literature.
In his lecture, the professor discussed the style of Friedrich Gottlieb Klopstock and his impact on 18th-century poetry.
