Erythrina caffra

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθrɪnə ˈkæfrə / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrɪnə ˈkafrə / (BrE)
a small, semi-evergreen tree native to eastern South Africa, with orange-scarlet flowers, coral-red seeds, and light soft wood used for fence posts or shingles
South African tree with scarlet flowers and red seeds

Examples

The Erythrina caffra is often planted in parks for its spectacular springtime display of flowers.
Farmers use the wood of Erythrina caffra for lightweight fence posts.
The bright red seeds of the Erythrina caffra are sometimes used to make decorative jewelry.

Etymology

From Greek *erythros* (red), referring to the flower color; 'caffra' relates to 'Kaffraria,' a historical region in South Africa.
