Gymnogyps californianus

1. NOUN

/ d͡ʒɪmˈnɑd͡ʒɪps kəˌlɪfɔrˈniːənəs / (AmE), / d͡ʒɪmˈnɒd͡ʒɪps kəˌlɪfəˈniːənəs / (BrE)
A large North American vulture, commonly known as the California condor, with mainly black plumage and nearly extinct in the wild.
California condor species

Examples

The Gymnogyps californianus is now primarily found in protected areas of California and Arizona.
Conservationists are working tirelessly to save the Gymnogyps californianus from extinction.
With a wingspan of almost three meters, the Gymnogyps californianus is North America's largest flying bird.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: California condor - Common name for Gymnogyps californianus, a large vulture species native to North America

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) + 'gyps' (vulture) and Latin 'californianus' (of California).
