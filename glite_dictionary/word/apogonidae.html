Apogonidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈgɑːnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌapəˈɡɒnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of brightly colored marine fishes, commonly called cardinalfishes, that incubate their eggs in the mouth.
family of brightly colored marine fishes

Examples

The Apogonidae are well known for their unusual breeding behavior, with males incubating the eggs in their mouths.
Many species in the Apogonidae family feature striking red and yellow coloration.
Aquarists often admire Apogonidae for their vivid colors and peaceful nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardinalfish - a common name for fishes of the family Apogonidae

Etymology

From New Latin Apogonidae, based on Apogon (a genus name) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈɡɑnɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌapəˈɡɒnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of brightly colored marine fishes, known as cardinalfishes, many of which incubate their eggs in their mouths.
a family of bright marine fishes (cardinalfishes)

Examples

Biologists studied the reproductive behavior of Apogonidae along the coral reefs.
Several species in the Apogonidae family are popular in home aquariums for their vibrant colors.
The Apogonidae exhibit unusual parental care by incubating their eggs inside the male's mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardinalfish - a brightly colored marine fish in the Apogonidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Apogonidae, from the type genus Apogon.
