unconstructive

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnkənˈstrʌktɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈstrʌktɪv / (BrE)
Not helpful or useful; failing to produce a positive result or improvement.
not helpful or productive

Examples

His comments during the meeting were completely unconstructive and did not help solve the problem.
Giving unconstructive criticism can lower morale and hinder progress.
The discussion became unconstructive when participants started blaming each other.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: constructive - serving a useful purpose; tending to build up

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'constructive' (helpful, serving a useful purpose).
