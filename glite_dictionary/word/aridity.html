aridity

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪdəti / (AmE), / əˈrɪdəti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being very dry, especially because of a lack of rainfall.
extreme dryness or lack of moisture

Examples

The aridity of the desert makes it difficult for most plants to survive.
Long periods of aridity can lead to devastating droughts and crop failures.
Scientists study aridity to understand changes in global climate patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dryness - the state of being dry
  • Antonym: humidity - the amount of moisture in the air; opposite of dryness

Etymology

From Latin 'ariditas', from 'aridus' (dry).

2. NOUN

/ əˈrɪdəti / (AmE), / əˈrɪdɪti / (BrE)
the quality of yielding nothing of value; lack of interest, excitement, or meaningful content
lack of value or interest

Examples

Some critics have noted the aridity of the novel's dialogue, finding it uninspiring and bland.
The intellectual aridity of the debate left many participants feeling disappointed and uninterested.
He was frustrated by the aridity of her responses, which offered no new ideas or insights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dryness - the quality of having no moisture; literal sense of aridity
  • Synonym: barrenness - the state of being unproductive or unfruitful
  • Antonym: richness - the quality of having great value or interest

Etymology

From Latin ariditas, from aridus (dry).
