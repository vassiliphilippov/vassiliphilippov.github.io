Neptune

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɛp.tuːn / (AmE), / ˈnɛp.tjuːn / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the god of the sea, equivalent to the Greek god Poseidon.
Roman god of the sea

Examples

In Roman mythology, Neptune was believed to control storms and earthquakes at sea.
Many ancient temples were dedicated to Neptune, where sailors prayed for safe journeys.
The artist depicted Neptune with his trident, emerging from the waves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Poseidon - Greek god of the sea

Etymology

From Latin 'Neptūnus', the Roman god of the sea.

2. PROPN

/ ˈnɛp.tun / (AmE), / ˈnɛp.tjuːn / (BrE)
the eighth planet from the Sun in the solar system, a large gaseous planet with a distinct blue color and faint rings of ice particles
the eighth planet from the Sun

Examples

Astronomers study Neptune to understand the outer solar system.
It takes Neptune about 165 Earth years to orbit the Sun.
Voyager 2 was the first spacecraft to make a close approach to Neptune.

Synset Examples

the existence of Neptune was predicted from perturbations in the orbit of Uranus and it was then identified in 1846

Etymology

Named after the Roman god of the sea, Neptune; discovered in 1846.
