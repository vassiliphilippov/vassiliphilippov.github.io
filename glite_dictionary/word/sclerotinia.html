Sclerotinia

1. NOUN

/ sklɛrəˈtɪniə / (AmE), / sklɪərəˈtɪnɪə / (BrE)
a genus of ascomycetous fungi that includes several species known as destructive plant pathogens
genus of plant-pathogenic fungi

Examples

Several species within the Sclerotinia genus are responsible for major crop diseases.
Researchers are developing new methods to control Sclerotinia infections in commercial agriculture.
The presence of Sclerotinia in soil can devastate a wide range of vegetable plants.

Etymology

From Greek 'skleros' (hard), referring to the hard sclerotia produced by these fungi.
