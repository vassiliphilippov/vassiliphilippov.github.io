Phthirius pubis

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪriəs ˈpjuːbɪs / (AmE), / ˈθɪriəs ˈpjuːbɪs / (BrE)
A species of louse that infests the pubic hair and other coarse body hair of humans, causing itching and irritation.
species of louse infesting human pubic hair
medical

Examples

Phthirius pubis is most commonly spread through close physical contact between people.
Treatment for an infestation of Phthirius pubis usually involves medicated shampoos.
Unlike head lice, Phthirius pubis prefers coarse hair found in the pubic region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pubic louse - common name of Phthirius pubis, a louse infesting human pubic hair

Etymology

From modern Latin, with 'Phthirius' from Greek 'phthir' (louse) and 'pubis' (pubic region).
