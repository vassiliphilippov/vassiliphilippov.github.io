bottleneck

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑt̬lˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈbɒt(ə)lˌnɛk / (BrE)
the narrow part of a bottle near its top, through which the contents are poured out.
narrow part at bottle's top

Examples

Carefully pour the wine through the bottleneck to avoid spilling.
Sediment sometimes collects around the bottleneck of an old bottle.
The design of the bottleneck makes it easy to control the flow of liquid.

Etymology

Formed from 'bottle' + 'neck', referring to its similarity to a human neck.

2. VERB

/ ˈbɑt.l̩ˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈbɒt.l̩.nɛk / (BrE)
(of a road, process, etc.) to become narrow or constricted, creating a delay or restriction.
to become narrow or constricted

Examples

Traffic tends to bottleneck here during rush hour, causing long delays for commuters.
At one point, the hiking trail bottlenecks between two cliffs, forcing everyone into single file.
As production increases, the assembly line may bottleneck near the packaging station.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narrow - become or make less wide

Etymology

From the noun 'bottleneck', referring to the narrow neck of a bottle.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɑtəlnɛk / (AmE), / ˈbɒt(ə)lnɛk / (BrE)
A narrow section of a road, pipe, or other channel where flow is constricted, causing a reduction in movement or capacity.
a narrowing that restricts flow

Examples

There is a bottleneck on the highway where the lanes merge into one.
A bottleneck formed in the river, slowing the water's flow considerably.
The old bridge creates a bottleneck for traffic during rush hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constriction - a narrowing or tightening that restricts flow
  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks or impedes progress

Etymology

Originally from the literal neck of a bottle, which is narrower than the body and restricts flow.

point of congestion or blockage

Her indecision created a bottleneck in the project, causing significant delays.
