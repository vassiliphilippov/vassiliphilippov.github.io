ringdove

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋˌdʌv / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋ.dʌv / (BrE)
A large, grayish Eurasian dove with a black ring around its neck, commonly found in woodland and often kept in captivity.
a Eurasian dove with a black neck ring

Examples

A ringdove perched quietly on the branch, its black neck band clearly visible.
Many people keep the ringdove as a pet because of its gentle nature.
The soft cooing of the ringdove was heard throughout the ancient forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood pigeon - a large, mainly gray pigeon native to Europe and western Asia, also called ringdove

Etymology

From 'ring' + 'dove', referring to the distinctive black ring or band on the bird's neck.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋˌdʌv / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋˌdʌv / (BrE)
A Eurasian pigeon (Columba palumbus) with distinctive white patches on its wings and neck.
a Eurasian pigeon with white markings

Examples

A flock of ringdoves perched in the oak tree, cooing softly in the morning light.
The distinctive white collar identifies the ringdove among other local pigeons.
Farmers sometimes find ringdoves feeding on seeds in their fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood pigeon - another common name for Columba palumbus

Etymology

From 'ring' (referring to the white ring on its neck) and 'dove' (the bird).
