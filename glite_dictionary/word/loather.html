loather

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊðər / (AmE), / ˈləʊðə / (BrE)
a person who feels intense dislike or hatred for someone or something
a person who hates or loathes

Examples

She became a loather of injustice after witnessing the unfair treatment of her coworkers.
The passionate loather of spicy food avoided the new restaurant entirely.
A loather of violence, he always preferred peaceful solutions to conflicts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hater - a person who greatly dislikes a specified person or thing
  • Antonym: admirer - a person who has a particular regard for someone or something

Etymology

From the verb 'loathe' + '-er', denoting someone who performs the action.
