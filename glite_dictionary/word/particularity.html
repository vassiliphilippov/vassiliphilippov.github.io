particularity

1. NOUN

/ pərˌtɪkjəˈlærəti / (AmE), / pəˌtɪkjuˈlærəti / (BrE)
the quality of being specific or relating to a particular case or instance
quality of being specific; uniqueness

Examples

The particularity of his argument made it especially persuasive to the committee.
Every culture has its own particularity that sets it apart from others.
Her writing style is marked by an attention to particularity and concrete detail.

Synset Examples

the particularity of human situations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generality - the quality of being general rather than specific

Etymology

From Late Latin particularitas, from Latin particularis (particular).
