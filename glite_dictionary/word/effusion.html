effusion

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
an unrestrained or heartfelt expression of emotion or feelings
unrestrained expression of emotion

Examples

Her passionate letter was an effusion of gratitude and relief.
During the speech, he broke into an emotional effusion that moved the audience.
The poem is filled with an effusion of love and longing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outpouring - a sudden, strong expression of feeling

Etymology

From Latin 'effusio' (a pouring out), from 'effundere' (to pour out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
the act or process of a liquid or gas flowing out under pressure, especially through a small opening
liquid or gas flowing under pressure

Examples

The effusion of gas through the membrane can be measured using specialized equipment.
Doctors detected a pleural effusion in the patient's chest X-ray.
The experiment demonstrated the effusion of air from the high-pressure container.

Etymology

From Latin 'effusio' (a pouring out), from 'effundere' (to pour out).
