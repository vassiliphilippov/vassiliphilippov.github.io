underslung

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɚˈslʌŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈslʌŋ / (BrE)
having a lower part that projects or is suspended below the upper part, often giving a hanging or drooping appearance.
having a lower part projecting below the upper

Examples

The new model features an underslung exhaust system for improved aerodynamics.
He noticed the bulldog’s distinctive underslung jaw as it played with the children.
The old railway carriage had an underslung frame beneath the passenger compartment.

Synset Examples

an underhung jaw
undershot bulldog

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overhung - having an upper part projecting beyond the lower

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌndərˈslʌŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈslʌŋ / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or structure) having a low center of gravity or constructed low to the ground.
built low to the ground

Examples

The sports car features an underslung chassis for improved handling.
The truck's underslung exhaust system helps maximize cargo space above.
Its underslung design allows the machine to pass under low bridges easily.

Etymology

From under- (below) + slung (past participle of sling).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01210369-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɚˈslʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəsˌlʌŋ / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or its components) having parts, especially the body or springs, supported from above or hung below an axle or frame.
supported from above (in vehicles)

Examples

The truck's underslung chassis gives it a lower center of gravity for better stability.
Some vintage cars feature an underslung suspension design to permit a sleeker profile.
Their trailers have an underslung spare tire for easy access and weight distribution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overslung - having springs or body supported above the axle

Etymology

From 'under-' (beneath) and 'slung', past participle of 'sling'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02360709-s
Synset POS: s
