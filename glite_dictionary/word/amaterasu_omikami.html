Amaterasu Omikami

1. PROPN

/ ˌɑːməˌtɛrɑːsu oʊˈmikɑːmi / (AmE), / ˌæmətəˌrɑːsu əʊˈmiːkɑːmi / (BrE)
the central goddess of Shinto, personifying the sun and regarded as the ancestral deity of the Japanese imperial family
sun goddess in Shinto and imperial ancestor

Examples

The legend of Amaterasu Omikami is central to Japanese mythology and Shinto tradition.
Japanese emperors are said to be descendants of Amaterasu Omikami.
Many shrines throughout Japan are dedicated to the worship of Amaterasu Omikami.

Etymology

From Japanese 天照大神 (Amaterasu Ōmikami), meaning 'Great Divinity Illuminating Heaven.'
