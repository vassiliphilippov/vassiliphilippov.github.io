morass

1. NOUN

/ məˈræs / (AmE), / məˈrɑːs / (BrE)
An area of soft, wet, low-lying ground that is difficult to walk on because it sinks underfoot; a bog or marsh.
soft, wet, sinking ground; bog

Examples

The hikers struggled to cross the morass before reaching solid ground.
After heavy rain, the field turned into a muddy morass that trapped vehicles.
Mist hovered over the morass at dawn, adding to its eerie beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bog - an area of wet, spongy ground; similar type of wetland
  • Synonym: marsh - low-lying wetland usually dominated by grasses
  • Synonym: swamp - an area of low, wet, spongy land with woody plants

Etymology

From Dutch *moeras* (marsh), from Old French *marais*, from Vulgar Latin *mariscus* (marsh).

2. NOUN

/ məˈræs / (AmE), / məˈrɑːs / (BrE)
a complicated or confused situation that is difficult to escape from or deal with
complicated or confused situation

Examples

Without clear plans, the team fell into a morass of indecision and delays.
The law was so complex that only experts could navigate the morass of regulations.
She struggled to make sense of the bureaucratic morass after her father's passing.

Synset Examples

The project turned into a morass of confusion.
He found himself in a morass of paperwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quagmire - a difficult or precarious situation; a swampy area
  • Synonym: predicament - a difficult, unpleasant, or embarrassing situation
  • Synonym: tangle - a confused mass or complicated situation

Etymology

From Dutch 'moeras' (swamp), from Old French 'maresche', from Vulgar Latin '*marisca'.
