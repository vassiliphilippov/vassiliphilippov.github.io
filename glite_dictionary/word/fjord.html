fjord

1. NOUN

/ fiˈɔrd / (AmE), / fjɔːd / (BrE)
A long, narrow inlet of the sea between high cliffs or steep slopes, especially one formed by glacial action, commonly found in Norway.
long narrow sea inlet between cliffs

Examples

The ship sailed quietly into the deep fjord surrounded by towering cliffs.
Norway is famous for its breathtaking fjords that attract tourists from around the world.
We spent our vacation hiking along the edge of a beautiful fjord.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound - a large sea or ocean inlet larger than a bay, deeper than a bight, and wider than a fjord

Etymology

From Norwegian 'fjord', rooted in Old Norse 'fjǫrðr' meaning 'inlet, estuary'.
