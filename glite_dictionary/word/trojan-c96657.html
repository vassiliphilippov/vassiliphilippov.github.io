trojan

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊdʒən / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊdʒən / (BrE)
A type of malicious computer program that appears legitimate or desirable but actually contains harmful code.
malicious program disguised as legitimate

Examples

The technician discovered that the trojan had compromised the security of the entire system.
After clicking on the suspicious link, her computer was infected by a dangerous trojan.
You should always check software sources to avoid accidentally installing a trojan.

Synset Examples

the contents of a trojan can be a virus or a worm
when he downloaded the free game, it turned out to be a trojan horse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virus - a malicious computer program that replicates itself
  • Synonym: worm - a type of self-replicating malware

Etymology

Shortened from 'Trojan horse', based on the ancient Greek story of deception.
