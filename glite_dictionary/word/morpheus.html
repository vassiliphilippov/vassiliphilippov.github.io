Morpheus

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɔr.fi.əs / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.fi.əs / (BrE)
the Roman god of dreams who shapes and personifies the dreams of mortals
Roman god of dreams

Examples

In the poem, Morpheus gently descends upon the weary traveler, sending him into a peaceful sleep.
The painter portrayed Morpheus as a winged figure hovering above a dreamer’s bed.
According to legend, Morpheus can appear in any form within one’s dreams.

Etymology

From Latin 'Morpheus', from Greek 'Morpheús', meaning 'the fashioner' or 'shaper'.
