Tellima grandiflora

1. NOUN

/ təˈlaɪmə ɡrænˈdɪflɔrə / (AmE), / tɛˈlɪmə ɡranˈdɪflərə / (BrE)
a perennial plant with clumping growth, mostly basal leaves, and cream or pale pink fringed flowers in long racemes, native to western North America
a western North American perennial plant

Examples

Tellima grandiflora is often grown in shade gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate flower spikes.
The woodland trail was dotted with patches of Tellima grandiflora in bloom each spring.
Gardeners appreciate how Tellima grandiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Etymology

From the genus name Tellima, an anagram of Mitella, and Latin grandiflora (large-flowered).
