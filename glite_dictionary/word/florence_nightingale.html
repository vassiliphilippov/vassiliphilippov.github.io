Florence Nightingale

1. PROPN

/ ˈflɔːrəns ˈnaɪtənˌɡeɪl / (AmE), / ˈflɒrəns ˈnaɪtɪnɡeɪl / (BrE)
(1820–1910) An English nurse known for her pioneering work in nursing during the Crimean War and for laying the foundations of professional nursing.
pioneering English nurse (1820–1910)

Examples

Florence Nightingale is often regarded as the founder of modern nursing.
Many hospitals have wards named after Florence Nightingale in honor of her achievements.
The story of Florence Nightingale inspired countless individuals to enter the nursing profession.

2. PROPN

/ ˈflɔːrəns ˈnaɪtɪnˌɡeɪl / (AmE), / ˈflɒrəns ˈnaɪtɪnɡeɪl / (BrE)
(1820–1910) The British nurse and social reformer known as the founder of modern nursing, often used figuratively to refer to a compassionate caregiver.
founder of modern nursing; compassionate caregiver

Examples

Many considered her the Florence Nightingale of her community because of her tireless volunteer work.
After volunteering during the crisis, he was nicknamed the Florence Nightingale of the shelter.
Her calm presence in the ward reminded everyone of Florence Nightingale.

Synset Examples

Her dedication to nursing earned her the title of Florence Nightingale.
She was praised as the Florence Nightingale of the hospital.
His compassion for patients made him a modern Florence Nightingale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nurse - a person trained to care for the sick or infirm

Etymology

Named after Florence Nightingale (1820–1910), British social reformer and founder of modern nursing.
