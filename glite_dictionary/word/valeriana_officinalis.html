Valeriana officinalis

1. NOUN

/ vəˌlɪriːˈænə ˌɒfɪsɪˈnɑːlɪs / (AmE), / vəˌlɪəriːˈɑːnɪə ˌɒfɪsɪˈneɪlɪs / (BrE)
A tall perennial plant of the valerian family, known for its fragrant flowers and rhizomes used in herbal medicine.
species of valerian plant used medicinally
medical

Examples

Valeriana officinalis has long been valued for its sedative properties in traditional medicine.
The gardener cultivated Valeriana officinalis for both its attractive blooms and medicinal roots.
Supplements containing extracts from Valeriana officinalis are often marketed as sleep aids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valerian - common name for Valeriana officinalis, a plant used in herbal remedies

Etymology

From Latin: 'Valeriana' (the genus), possibly from personal name Valerius; 'officinalis' means 'of the storeroom', used for plants with medicinal use.
