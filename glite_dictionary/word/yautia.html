yautia

1. NOUN

/ jaʊˈtiːə / (AmE), / jaʊˈtiːə / (BrE)
A tropical American plant with starchy, edible tubers that are cooked and eaten like yams or potatoes.
tropical plant with edible tubers

Examples

She prepared a delicious stew using diced yautia and other root vegetables.
In the market, I saw several varieties of yautia next to the yams and cassava.
The recipe calls for boiled yautia, which gives the dish a creamy texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malanga - another name for yautia; often used interchangeably
  • Synonym: taro - another tropical root vegetable, similar to yautia but botanically distinct

Etymology

From Spanish 'yautía', of Taíno origin.
