os zygomaticum

1. NOUN

/ oʊz zaɪˌɡoʊˈmætɪkəm / (AmE), / ɒs zaɪˌɡɒmɪˈtʃuːm / (BrE)
the bone under the eye that forms the prominence of the cheek; the cheekbone
cheekbone; bone under the eye
medical

Examples

The os zygomaticum is often referred to as the cheekbone in everyday language.
Injuries to the os zygomaticum can affect the shape of the face.
The surgeon repaired the fractured os zygomaticum after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zygomatic bone - the bone that forms the prominence of the cheek
  • Degree Lower: maxilla - upper jawbone, adjacent facial bone

Etymology

From Latin os (bone) + zygomaticum (of the yoke, relating to the cheekbone).
