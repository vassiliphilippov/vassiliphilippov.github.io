cripple

1. VERB

/ ˈkrɪp.əl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪp.əl / (BrE)
to cause serious damage to someone or something, making them weak or unable to function effectively
render ineffective or powerless

Examples

The severe storm crippled the city’s power supply for days.
New regulations could cripple small businesses if they are not carefully implemented.
Fear of failure can sometimes cripple a person’s ability to try new things.

Synset Examples

This measure crippled our efforts
Their behavior stultified the boss's hard work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to paralyze - to make unable to move or function
  • Synonym: handicap - to put at a disadvantage or render less efficient
  • Antonym: to enable - to provide with the means or ability to do something

Etymology

From Old English 'cryppel', related to 'creopan' (to creep).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪpəl / (BrE)
(offensive) A person who is unable to walk properly because of injury or disability to the legs or back.
person unable to walk normally (offensive)
offensive archaic

Examples

The old term cripple is now considered highly offensive and should be avoided.
In the past, society often isolated anyone labeled a cripple.
He rejected the label cripple and preferred to be known by his name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invalid - a person made weak or disabled by illness or injury
  • Antonym: able-bodied - not disabled; having no injury or impairment

Etymology

Old English *cripel*, related to *creopan* (to creep).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09998419-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈkrɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪpəl / (BrE)
to cause someone to lose the use of a limb, especially a leg, often resulting in permanent disability
deprive of the use of a limb
offensive

Examples

The accident crippled her for life, leaving her unable to walk without assistance.
A severe stroke can cripple a person's ability to speak clearly or move their limbs.
His right arm was crippled in the crash and never fully recovered.

Synset Examples

The accident has crippled her for life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disable - to make unable to use part of the body; to incapacitate
  • Synonym: to paralyze - to make unable to move or feel part or all of the body
  • Antonym: to heal - to restore to health or normal function

Etymology

From Old English *cryppelian* (to cripple), related to *crypel* (cripple, nomad); see also Old Norse *krœppr* (crooked).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00091399-v
Synset POS: v
