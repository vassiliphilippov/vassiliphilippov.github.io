Solidago

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑːlɪˈdeɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌsɒlɪˈdeɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, commonly known as goldenrods, with bright yellow flowers.
genus of goldenrod plants

Examples

The meadows were filled with bright yellow Solidago in late summer.
Many beekeepers plant Solidago to attract bees and support honey production.
Botanists are studying the distribution of Solidago species across North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldenrod - common name for plants of the genus Solidago

Etymology

From Latin 'Solidago', meaning 'to make whole', referring to the plant's medicinal properties.

2. NOUN

/ səˈlaɪdəˌɡoʊ / (AmE), / sɒˈlɪdəɡəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly North American plants in the daisy family, known as goldenrods, with small yellow flowers clustered on tall stems.
genus of goldenrod plants

Examples

The bright yellow flowers of Solidago attract bees and butterflies in late summer.
Some people believe extracts from Solidago can be used as herbal remedies.
Solidago species are widespread across fields and meadows in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldenrod - common name for plants of genus Solidago

Etymology

From Latin 'solidago', from 'solidare' (to make whole), referring to its supposed healing properties.
