pensionable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛnʃənəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpɛnʃənəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of a job, person, or employment) eligible to receive a pension, especially after reaching a certain age or meeting specific conditions.
entitled to receive a pension

Examples

After thirty years of service, Jane became a pensionable employee and started planning her retirement.
Only pensionable service is counted towards your retirement benefits.
He asked if his new job was pensionable before accepting the offer.

Synset Examples

a pensionable employee

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-pensionable - not eligible to receive a pension

Etymology

From 'pension' + '-able', patterned after similar English adjectives.
