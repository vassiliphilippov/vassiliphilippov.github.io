combo

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːm.boʊ / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.bəʊ / (BrE)
a small band of musicians, especially one playing jazz or popular music
small jazz/pop band

Examples

The trio formed a jazz combo that played in local nightclubs every weekend.
She joined a combo specializing in blues and swing music.
Their new combo features talented musicians from both the university and the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: band - a group of musicians who play together, especially pop or rock music

Etymology

Shortened form of 'combination'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmboʊ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmbəʊ / (BrE)
a combination of different things, especially in games, food, or music, often chosen for their complementary qualities.
combination of different elements
informal

Examples

The new menu includes a combo of fries, a burger, and a drink.
She pulled off a stunning combo in the video game, earning extra points.
Their band is a unique combo of jazz and electronic music styles.

Synset Examples

This game features a combo of puzzles and action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combination - a joining or mixing of different things

Etymology

Shortened from 'combination', first used in American English in the mid-20th century.
