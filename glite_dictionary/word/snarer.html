snarer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnɛɹɚ / (AmE), / ˈsnɛːrə / (BrE)
a person who sets traps to catch birds or small animals
someone who sets animal snares

Examples

The snarer set his traps along the forest edge, hoping for a good catch before dawn.
Villagers sometimes hire a skilled snarer to deal with pests threatening their crops.
He learned the secrets of an expert snarer from his grandfather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trapper - a person who traps wild animals, usually for their fur

Etymology

From 'snare' (a trap) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
