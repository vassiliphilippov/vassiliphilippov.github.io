whither

1. ADV

/ ˈwɪðər / (AmE), / ˈwɪðə / (BrE)
(archaic) To what place or to which place; to where something leads or goes.
to what place; to where (archaic)
archaic

Examples

She asked herself whither this path might ultimately lead.
In her letter, she questioned whither the nation was heading.
The poet mused on whither the winds would carry his message.

Synset Examples

She wondered whither her dreams would take her next.
Whither we travel next remains to be decided.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: where - at or in what place

Etymology

From Old English *hwider* (to where), related to 'where' and 'whether'.
