naturalized

1. ADJ

/ ˈnætʃəɹəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)ɹəˌlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a plant or animal) introduced from another region and able to survive and reproduce without human assistance.
introduced species surviving without cultivation

Examples

Many naturalized plants have adapted so well that they seem native to the area.
The park is home to several naturalized bird species originally from Asia.
After years of cultivation, the flowers became fully naturalized in local meadows.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: native - originating in a particular place, not introduced
  • Degree Lower: exotic - originating in a different place, not native

Etymology

From naturalize + -ed; ultimately from Latin 'natura' (nature).

2. ADJ

/ ˈnætʃ.ər.ə.laɪzd / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ.ər.əl.aɪzd / (BrE)
(of plants) planted in a way that appears wild or as if growing naturally in the area.
planted to appear wild or natural

Examples

The garden was designed with naturalized tulips and irises along the winding paths.
She preferred the look of naturalized wildflowers over neat, formal beds.
The old estate's lawns are dotted with naturalized patches of bluebells each spring.

Synset Examples

drifts of naturalized daffodils

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: wild - growing or living in a natural environment, not cultivated
  • Degree Lower: cultivated - planted and grown by humans, especially for decorative or practical purposes

Etymology

From 'naturalize', from Middle French 'naturaliser', meaning to make natural.
