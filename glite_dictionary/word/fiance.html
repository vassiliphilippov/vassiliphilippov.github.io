fiance

1. NOUN

/ fiˈɑnseɪ / (AmE), / fiˈɒnseɪ / (BrE)
a man to whom someone is engaged to be married
man engaged to be married

Examples

She introduced us to her fiance at the party last night.
After the proposal, Mark proudly called himself her fiance.
His fiance is helping him plan the wedding.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fiancee - a woman engaged to be married
  • Degree Higher: husband - a married man (after marriage)

Etymology

From French fiancé (engaged man), past participle of 'fiancer' (to betroth).
