sinoper

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnəpər / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəpə / (BrE)
a red ochre pigment formerly used by artists and painters, especially in classical and medieval times.
red ochre pigment

Examples

The artist mixed sinoper with oil to achieve the rich red tones in her painting.
Early Renaissance frescoes often employed sinoper for preparatory sketches known as sinopia.
Restorers identified traces of sinoper beneath the layers of modern paint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ochre - an earthy pigment containing ferric oxide, typically with clay, varying from light yellow to brown or red

Etymology

From Latin *sinopis*, from Greek *sinōpis* (red earth from Sinope, an ancient city in Asia Minor).
