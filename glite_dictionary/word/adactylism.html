adactylism

1. NOUN

/ əˈdæktɪˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈdæktɪlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A congenital condition characterized by the absence of fingers and/or toes.
absence of fingers and/or toes
medical

Examples

The geneticist specialized in rare conditions such as adactylism.
Babies born with adactylism may require special medical care and adaptive tools.
Scientists are studying the causes and possible treatments for adactylism.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'daktylos' (finger, toe) + '-ism'.
