purse-proud

1. ADJ

/ pɝs praʊd / (AmE), / pɜːs praʊd / (BrE)
proud or arrogant because of one's wealth, especially when lacking other merits
proud due to wealth alone

Examples

Her purse-proud attitude alienated friends who valued kindness over riches.
He became purse-proud after inheriting a fortune, boasting about his wealth at every opportunity.
The novelist described the merchant as purse-proud, caring little for culture or learning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogant - having or revealing an exaggerated sense of one's own importance
  • Antonym: modest - having a humble view of one's own importance

Etymology

Formed from 'purse' (moneybag) + 'proud'; dating from the 17th century.
