gecko

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡɛkəʊ / (BrE)
A small, chiefly tropical lizard that is typically nocturnal and insect-eating, often with immovable eyelids, and is harmless to humans.
a small tropical nocturnal lizard

Examples

As night fell, a gecko scurried across the wall, searching for insects.
We spotted a tiny gecko clinging to the windowpane in the tropical heat.
The gecko is harmless and even helps control pests in the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard - a type of reptile with four legs and a long tail

Etymology

From Malay 'gekok', imitative of its call.
