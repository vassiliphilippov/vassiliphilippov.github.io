babe

1. NOUN

/ beɪb / (AmE), / beɪb / (BrE)
a very young child, especially one who is not yet able to walk or talk; a baby
very young child; baby

Examples

She wrapped the babe tightly in a soft blanket.
In the story, a lost babe was found sleeping under a tree.
The mother sang gently to her babe as she rocked him to sleep.

Synset Examples

the baby began to cry again
she held the baby in her arms
it sounds simple, but when you have your own baby it is all so different

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baby - a very young child, especially one newly or recently born

Etymology

From Middle English babes, variant of babe, from Old French baban.
