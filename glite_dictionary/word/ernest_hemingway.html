Ernest Hemingway

1. PROPN

/ ˈɜrnəst ˈhɛmɪŋweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɜːnɪst ˈhɛmɪŋweɪ / (BrE)
(1899–1961) An American writer celebrated for his novels and short stories, and winner of the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1954.
American writer and Nobel laureate

Examples

Many critics consider Ernest Hemingway one of the most influential writers of the twentieth century.
In college, we studied Ernest Hemingway's distinctive writing style in depth.
The Old Man and the Sea, written by Ernest Hemingway, is a classic of American literature.
