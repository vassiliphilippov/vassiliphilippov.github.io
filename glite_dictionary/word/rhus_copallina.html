Rhus copallina

1. NOUN

/ ɹʊs koʊpəˈlaɪnə / (AmE), / ɹʊs kɒpəˈliːnə / (BrE)
A common nonpoisonous shrub of eastern North America, also known as winged sumac, with compound leaves, green panicle-shaped flowers, and red berries.
a nonpoisonous shrub of eastern North America

Examples

The Rhus copallina in our backyard attracts a wide variety of birds in the fall.
We identified the shrub as Rhus copallina by its distinctive winged leaf stalks.
Native Americans historically used parts of Rhus copallina for medicinal purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winged sumac - another common name for Rhus copallina
  • Antonym: poison sumac - a poisonous shrub of the genus Rhus

Etymology

From Latin genus name Rhus (sumac) and species epithet copallina, referring to its resinous properties.
