Crustacea

1. NOUN

/ krʌˈsteɪʃiə / (AmE), / krʌˈsteɪsɪə / (BrE)
a large class of mainly aquatic arthropods that includes crabs, lobsters, shrimps, and barnacles
class of aquatic arthropods (crabs, lobsters, etc.)

Examples

Marine biologists study Crustacea to understand the diversity of ocean life.
Several species within Crustacea are harvested for human consumption, such as shrimp and lobster.
The fossil record of Crustacea helps scientists trace evolutionary changes in aquatic environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arthropod - an invertebrate animal with an exoskeleton and jointed limbs

Etymology

From Latin 'crustacea', plural of 'crustaceus' meaning 'having a crust or shell'.

2. NOUN

/ krʌˈsteɪʃiə / (AmE), / krʌsˈteɪsiə / (BrE)
a large class of arthropods that includes lobsters, crabs, shrimps, barnacles, and related animals, usually living in aquatic environments
class of aquatic arthropods (crabs, lobsters, etc.)

Examples

Many Crustacea play vital roles in aquatic food chains.
Scientists have discovered new species within the Crustacea class.
The fossil record of Crustacea dates back hundreds of millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crustacean - an individual animal belonging to the class Crustacea

Etymology

From Latin *crusta* (shell) + -acea (indicating group or class).
