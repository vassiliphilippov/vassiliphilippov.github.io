thermistor

1. NOUN

/ θərˈmɪstər / (AmE), / θəˈmɪstə / (BrE)
A semiconductor device whose electrical resistance changes significantly with temperature, used for measuring or controlling temperature in electronic circuits.
temperature-sensitive resistor

Examples

The circuit uses a thermistor to automatically adjust the fan speed based on the temperature.
He replaced the faulty thermistor in his digital thermometer to restore its accuracy.
Modern cars often feature a thermistor to monitor engine temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resistor - an electrical component that limits or regulates the flow of electrical current in a circuit

Etymology

Blend of 'thermal' and 'resistor'.
