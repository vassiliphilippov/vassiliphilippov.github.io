airway

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈeə.weɪ / (BrE)
A passage in the body through which air flows to and from the lungs, such as the nose, throat, or windpipe.
passage for air in the body

Examples

If the airway becomes blocked, the patient may not be able to breathe.
The doctor checked the child's airway after the accident to ensure it was clear.
Swelling in the throat can obstruct the airway and lead to serious complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windpipe - the main tube (trachea) through which air passes to and from the lungs

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈeə.weɪ / (BrE)
a duct or passage used to provide ventilation, especially in mines
duct for ventilation, esp. in mines

Examples

The miners constructed a new airway to improve airflow throughout the tunnel.
Dust built up quickly until the airway was cleared and ventilation was restored.
Proper maintenance of the airway is essential for the safety of workers underground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ventilation shaft - a shaft used for air circulation in underground spaces

4. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈeə.weɪ / (BrE)
A designated route in the sky that airplanes follow when flying from one airport to another.
route for airplanes in the sky

Examples

Pilots must stay within the airway assigned to their flight plan.
The new airway reduces congestion between major airports.
Each airway is clearly defined on aeronautical charts.
