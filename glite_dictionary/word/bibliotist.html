bibliotist

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪbliətɪst / (AmE), / ˈbɪblɪətɪst / (BrE)
A person who practices bibliotics, the scientific study of handwriting in books, especially to determine authorship.
person who studies handwriting in books

Examples

The expert bibliotist authenticated the signature in the rare manuscript.
She hoped to become a leading bibliotist at the national library.
As a bibliotist, he was often consulted in cases of disputed authorship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bibliotics - the scientific study of handwriting in books

Etymology

From 'bibliotics' (study of handwriting in books), from Greek 'biblion' (book) + '-ist'.
