smokescreen

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmoʊkˌskrin / (AmE), / ˈsməʊkˌskriːn / (BrE)
A cloud of smoke created to hide military movements, vehicles, or positions from the enemy.
military cloud concealing movements

Examples

The smokescreen allowed the troops to advance undetected across the field.
During the exercise, a smokescreen was deployed to conceal the tank maneuvers.
The army used a dense smokescreen to obscure their withdrawal from enemy sight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - something that conceals or provides protection
  • Synonym: camouflage - concealment by means of disguise

2. NOUN

/ ˈsmoʊkˌskrin / (AmE), / ˈsməʊkˌskriːn / (BrE)
an action or statement used to conceal the truth or obscure someone’s real intentions
something used to hide the truth

Examples

The company's promise of new jobs was just a smokescreen to win public approval.
Politicians often use economic statistics as a smokescreen to distract from real issues.
Her explanation for being late was a clever smokescreen that hid the actual reason.

Synset Examples

requesting new powers of surveillance is just a smokescreen to hide their failures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretext - a false reason given to hide the real purpose
  • Synonym: cover - something that serves to conceal or protect
  • Antonym: revelation - the act of making something evident or clear

Etymology

From 'smoke screen', originally referring to literal smoke used to conceal military operations.
