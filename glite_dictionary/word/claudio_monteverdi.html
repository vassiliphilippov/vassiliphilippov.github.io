Claudio Monteverdi

1. PROPN

/ ˈklaʊdi.oʊ ˌmɒntəˈvɛrdi / (AmE), / ˈklaʊdiəʊ ˌmɒntɪˈvɛːdi / (BrE)
(1567–1643) An Italian composer whose work marks the transition from the Renaissance to the Baroque period in music.
Italian composer (1567–1643)

Examples

Many musicologists consider Claudio Monteverdi the father of opera for his pioneering works in the genre.
The madrigals of Claudio Monteverdi are often performed by early music ensembles today.
Scholars praise Claudio Monteverdi for blending Renaissance polyphony with new Baroque styles.
