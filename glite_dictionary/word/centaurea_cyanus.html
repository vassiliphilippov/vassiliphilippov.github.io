Centaurea cyanus

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtɔriə saɪˈænəs / (AmE), / sɛntaʊˈriːə saɪˈanəs / (BrE)
an annual Eurasian plant known for its bright blue, purple, pink, or white flowers, often grown in gardens and sometimes called cornflower
a Eurasian plant with colorful flowers

Examples

The vibrant blue Centaurea cyanus bloomed all over the wildflower meadow last summer.
Gardeners often plant Centaurea cyanus for its attractive flowers that attract bees and butterflies.
A bouquet of Centaurea cyanus brightened the dining table with vivid shades of blue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornflower - common name for Centaurea cyanus, a blue-flowered plant

Etymology

From Latin genus name Centaurea and species epithet cyanus, from Greek kuanos (dark blue).
