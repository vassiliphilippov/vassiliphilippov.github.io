polite

1. ADJ

/ pəˈlaɪt / (AmE), / pəˈlaɪt / (BrE)
having or showing good manners and respect for other people
having good manners; respectful

Examples

It's important to be polite when meeting new people.
She always speaks in a polite and respectful tone.
He thanked the waiter in a polite manner for his service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courteous - polite and respectful in manner
  • Antonym: impolite - not having or showing good manners; rude
  • Antonym: rude - offensively impolite or ill-mannered

Etymology

From Latin politus (polished, refined), past participle of polire (to polish).

Examples

3. ADJ

/ pəˈlaɪt / (AmE), / pəˈlaɪt / (BrE)
having or showing refinement in taste and manners; cultured or genteel.
refined in manners or taste

Examples

She grew up in a polite household where everyone valued good manners and elegance.
The art gallery attracted a polite crowd with an appreciation for classical music and fine wine.
He addressed the guests with polite conversation, displaying his refined upbringing.

Synset Examples

cultivated speech
cultured Bostonians
cultured tastes
a genteel old lady
polite society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cultured - having refined taste and manners
  • Synonym: genteel - polite, refined, or respectable, often in an affected manner
  • Antonym: coarse - lacking refinement or delicacy

Etymology

From Latin politus (polished, refined), past participle of polire (to polish).
