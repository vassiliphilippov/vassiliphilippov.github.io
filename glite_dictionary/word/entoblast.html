entoblast

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛntoʊˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈɛntəʊˌblɑːst / (BrE)
the innermost germ layer of an embryo, which develops into the lining of the digestive and respiratory systems
innermost germ layer of an embryo

Examples

The entoblast gives rise to the epithelial lining of the digestive tract.
During embryonic development, the entoblast differentiates into several vital internal organs.
Scientists study the entoblast to better understand organ formation in early embryos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endoderm - the inner germ layer in animal embryos

Etymology

From Greek 'entos' (within) + 'blastós' (germ, sprout).
