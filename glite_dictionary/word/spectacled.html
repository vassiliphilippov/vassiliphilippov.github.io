spectacled

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɛktəkəld / (AmE), / ˈspɛktək(ə)ld / (BrE)
Wearing eyeglasses or having the face adorned with spectacles.
wearing eyeglasses

Examples

The spectacled professor adjusted his glasses before speaking to the class.
A spectacled child sat quietly in the front row, reading a book.
The detective, spectacled and serious, examined the clues carefully.

Synset Examples

a bespectacled grandmother
the monocled gentleman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bespectacled - wearing glasses or eyeglasses

Etymology

From spectacle (eyeglasses) + -ed (adjectival suffix).
