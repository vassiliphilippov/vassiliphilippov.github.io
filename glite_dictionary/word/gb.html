GB

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːˈbiː / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːˈbiː / (BrE)
a unit of digital information storage equal to 1,073,741,824 bytes (2^30 bytes); abbreviation for gigabyte
unit of digital information (gigabyte)

Examples

This hard drive has a capacity of 500 GB.
The new smartphone comes with 128 GB of storage.
Please make sure your computer has at least 8 GB of RAM.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gigabyte - a unit of digital information storage equivalent to 1,073,741,824 bytes

Etymology

Initialism of 'gigabyte', from 'giga-' meaning billion and 'byte'.

4. NOUN

/ dʒiː biː / (AmE), / dʒiː biː / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,000,000,000 bytes or 1,000 megabytes, commonly used for measuring data storage
unit of data storage (1,000,000,000 bytes)

Examples

My phone has 128 GB of storage for photos, videos, and apps.
The software update requires at least 2 GB of free space to install.
Cloud services often offer several GB of free storage before you need to pay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gigabyte - a unit of digital information equal to 1,000,000,000 bytes
  • Degree Lower: MB - a unit of digital information equal to 1,000,000 bytes
  • Degree Higher: TB - a unit of digital information equal to 1,000,000,000,000 bytes

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'gigabyte,' from 'giga-' (Greek for 'giant') + 'byte.'

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
