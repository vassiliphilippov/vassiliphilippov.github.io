Gb

1. NOUN

/ dʒiː biː / (AmE), / dʒiː biː / (BrE)
An abbreviation for gilbert, a unit of magnetomotive force equal to 0.7958 ampere-turns.
unit of magnetomotive force (gilbert)

Examples

The electromagnet generates a flux equivalent to fifty Gb.
The specification called for a magnetomotive force of 100 Gb.
When measuring the coil, the engineer noted the Gb value exceeded expectations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gilbert - a unit of magnetomotive force equal to 0.7958 ampere-turns

Etymology

From the name of William Gilbert (1544–1603), English physician and physicist.

2. NOUN

/ dʒiː biː / (AmE), / dʒiː biː / (BrE)
abbreviation for gigabit, a unit of information equal to 1,000,000,000 (10^9) bits
gigabit (1,000,000,000 bits, abbrev.)

Examples

The new fiber optic service offers speeds up to 1 Gb per second.
Downloading a movie might take only a few seconds with a 5 Gb connection.
Network devices today often support data transfers measured in Gb rather than megabits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gigabit - a unit of digital information equal to 1,000,000,000 bits
  • Degree Higher: Gbps - gigabits per second, a rate of data transfer

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'gigabit', from 'giga-' meaning 'billion' and 'bit', a basic unit of information.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13650593-n
Synset POS: n
