centralization

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of concentrating control or authority under a single, central body or organization
the act of consolidating control centrally

Examples

The centralization of decision-making often leads to greater efficiency in large organizations.
Many believe the centralization of government power threatens local autonomy and democracy.
The company's recent centralization of its IT services has reduced costs and improved security.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decentralization - the process of distributing or dispersing functions away from a central authority

Etymology

From French centralisation, from Latin centralis (central) + -ization.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntrələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsentrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of bringing activities, power, or organization under a single authority or central point.
concentration of control in one place

Examples

The centralization of decision-making helped the company respond more efficiently to market changes.
Many argue that too much centralization can stifle creativity and local initiative.
Recent reforms have reversed years of centralization in government administration.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decentralization - the process of dispersing power or functions away from a central authority

Etymology

From French 'centralisation', based on 'central'.

Synset POS: n
