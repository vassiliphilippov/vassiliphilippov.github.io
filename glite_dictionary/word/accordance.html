accordance

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɔrdəns / (AmE), / əˈkɔːdəns / (BrE)
the act of granting or bestowing rights or privileges to someone, especially in a formal or legal context
granting of rights or privileges

Examples

Citizenship was granted in accordance with the new laws.
The accordance of voting rights marked a significant milestone in the country's development.
He protested the accordance of special privileges to certain groups.

Synset Examples

the accordance to Canada of rights of access

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grant - to give or bestow something formally, especially rights or privileges

Etymology

From Old French 'acorder' (to agree) and Medieval Latin 'accordantia', from Latin 'accordare' (to bring into agreement).

2. NOUN

/ əˈkɔrdəns / (AmE), / əˈkɔːdəns / (BrE)
agreement or conformity with a rule, opinion, or request; concurrence or harmony.
agreement; conformity

Examples

The policy was changed in accordance with recent government regulations.
The teams acted in full accordance to ensure a successful outcome.
He completed the task in accordance with the manager's instructions.

Synset Examples

we are in accord with your proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreement - a situation in which people have the same opinion; harmony
  • Antonym: disagreement - a lack of consensus or harmony; difference of opinion

Etymology

From Old French *acordance*, from *acorder* (to agree), from Latin *accordare* (to bring into agreement).
