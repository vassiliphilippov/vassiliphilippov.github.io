rimose

1. ADJ

/ ˈraɪmoʊs / (AmE), / ˈrɪməʊs / (BrE)
(of a surface) marked by a network of cracks or small crevices, especially in bark, rocks, or fungi.
surface covered with cracks or crevices

Examples

The old stone wall was rimose after years of exposure to wind and rain.
Many lichen species develop a rimose thallus as they age.
The rimose bark of the oak tree makes it easy to identify.

Synset Examples

a tree with rimose bark

Etymology

From Latin rimōsus (full of cracks), from rima (crack, fissure).
