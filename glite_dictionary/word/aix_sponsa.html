Aix sponsa

1. NOUN

/ aɪks ˈspɑnsə / (AmE), / aɪks ˈspɒnsə / (BrE)
A species of brightly colored North American duck that nests in hollow trees, commonly known as the wood duck.
showy North American duck species

Examples

Birdwatchers often hope to spot an Aix sponsa in wetland habitats during spring migration.
The brilliant plumage of the male Aix sponsa makes it one of North America's most attractive ducks.
Conservationists monitor Aix sponsa populations as an indicator of healthy forested wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood duck - common name for Aix sponsa, a colourful North American duck

Etymology

From Latin, Aix (genus name for ducks) and sponsa (bride), referring to the bird's striking appearance.
