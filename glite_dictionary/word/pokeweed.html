pokeweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊkˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈpəʊkˌwiːd / (BrE)
A tall, coarse perennial plant of the genus Phytolacca, native to North America, with dark purple berries that are toxic if eaten.
perennial plant with toxic berries

Examples

Children are sometimes warned not to eat the berries of the pokeweed plant.
In late summer, the stems of pokeweed turn a bright red color.
Wildlife often feed on pokeweed, even though its berries are poisonous to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phytolacca - the plant genus to which pokeweed belongs

Etymology

From American English 'poke' (from Virginia Algonquian) + 'weed'.
