Chasidism

1. NOUN

/ xɑˈsidɪzəm / (AmE), / xɑˈsɪdɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A Jewish religious movement within Orthodox Judaism, emphasizing mysticism, spiritual devotion, and strict religious observance.
mystical movement in Orthodox Judaism

Examples

Many aspects of Chasidism focus on joyful worship and close-knit community life.
Scholars have studied the history and spread of Chasidism throughout Eastern Europe.
The spiritual leader delivered a sermon on the core values of Chasidism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hasidic - relating to Chasidism or its followers

Etymology

From Hebrew ḥasidut (piety, holiness), based on ḥasid (pious).
