Oreamnos americanus

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈriːæmnəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈriːamnɒs əˌmerɪˈkeɪnəs / (BrE)
a sure-footed mammal native to the mountainous regions of northwestern North America, commonly known as the mountain goat
mountain goat of northwestern North America

Examples

The wild Oreamnos americanus is well-adapted to climbing steep cliffs and rugged terrain.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Oreamnos americanus in the northern Rockies.
Conservation efforts have helped the population of Oreamnos americanus to recover in some protected areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain goat - common name for Oreamnos americanus

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name from Greek oros (mountain) + amnos (lamb), species name meaning 'American'.
