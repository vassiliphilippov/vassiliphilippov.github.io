wizen

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪzən / (AmE), / ˈwɪz(ə)n / (BrE)
Having a dry, shriveled, and wrinkled appearance, especially as a result of aging or illness.
shriveled or wrinkled from age or illness

Examples

After years in the sun, her hands became wizened and thin.
The wizened face of the old gardener revealed decades of hard work.
His wizened features gave him a look of both wisdom and weariness.

Synset Examples

the old woman's shriveled skin
he looked shriveled and ill
a shrunken old man
a lanky scarecrow of a man with withered face and lantern jaws
he did well despite his withered arm
a wizened little man with frizzy grey hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shrivel - to become dry and wrinkled, often from loss of moisture
  • Synonym: to wither - to become dry and shriveled, especially from loss of moisture

Etymology

From Old English *wisnian* (to dry up, shrink), related to German *verwelken*.
