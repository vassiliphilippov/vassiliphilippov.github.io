bastardized

1. ADJ

/ ˈbæstɚˌdaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈbɑːstədaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a form, style, or version) altered from an original or pure state, often by combining elements from different sources in a way regarded as of lower quality or less authentic.
mixed from multiple sources or styles, often inferior

Examples

Critics argue that the new translation is a bastardized version of the original masterpiece.
The recipe has become bastardized over time, losing its authentic flavors and techniques.
Many modern jazz songs are seen as bastardized blends of various musical traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hybrid - something that is made by combining two or more different things

Etymology

From 'bastard' (illegitimate child) + '-ize', meaning to change to an inferior form.
