peril

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛrəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛrəl / (BrE)
Serious and immediate danger; a situation involving risk or exposure to harm.
serious and immediate danger

Examples

Hikers faced great peril when they ventured into the stormy mountains.
The company ignored the early warnings at its own peril.
Many ancient explorers braved unknown seas despite the peril that awaited them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: danger - the possibility of suffering harm or injury
  • Antonym: safety - the condition of being protected from danger or risk

Etymology

From Old French 'peril', from Latin 'periculum' (danger, risk).
