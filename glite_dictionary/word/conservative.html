Conservative

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɝː.və.tɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsɜː.və.tɪv / (BrE)
a member or supporter of a Conservative Party, especially the Conservative Party in the United Kingdom
member of a Conservative Party

Examples

Her father was a lifelong Conservative, deeply involved in local politics.
The Conservative argued against the proposed tax increase in Parliament.
At the conference, Conservatives discussed strategies for the upcoming election.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Labourite - a member or supporter of the Labour Party (UK)
  • Antonym: Liberal - a member or supporter of a Liberal Party

Etymology

From conservative (adjective), used as a name for members of conservative political groups since the 19th century.
