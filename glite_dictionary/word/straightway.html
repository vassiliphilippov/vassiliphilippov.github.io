straightway

1. ADV

/ ˈstreɪtˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtˌweɪ / (BrE)
immediately; without delay; at once.
immediately, at once
archaic

Examples

When the signal was given, they left straightway for the next town.
The rain began to pour, and straightway everyone ran for cover.
He straightway realized his mistake and apologized to her.

Synset Examples

straightway the clouds began to scatter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediately - at once; without delay
  • Synonym: instantly - immediately, at once
  • Antonym: eventually - in the end, after a delay

Etymology

From Middle English 'strete wey', literally 'straight way'.

2. ADV

/ ˈstreɪtˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈstreɪt.weɪ / (BrE)
immediately; without delay or deviation from a course or action
immediately; directly
archaic

Examples

He left the meeting straightway, not waiting to say goodbye to anyone.
The message was so urgent that she delivered it straightway to the commander.
Realizing his error, he corrected it straightway without hesitation.

Synset Examples

plunged straightway to the rocks below

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediately - without any delay
  • Antonym: indirectly - not in a direct way or manner

Etymology

From Old English 'streteweġ', meaning 'straight way' or 'direct path'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00469534-r
Synset POS: r
