somnambulism

1. NOUN

/ sɑmˈnæm.bjəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / sɒmˈnæm.bjʊ.lɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the act or condition of walking while asleep; sleepwalking
walking while asleep
medical

Examples

Cases of somnambulism are more common in children than in adults.
He sought treatment after his episodes of somnambulism became dangerous.
The doctor explained that somnambulism can be triggered by stress or lack of sleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleepwalking - the act of walking while asleep

Etymology

From Latin somnus (sleep) + ambulare (to walk).
