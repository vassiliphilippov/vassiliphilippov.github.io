to rove

1. VERB

/ roʊv / (AmE), / rəʊv / (BrE)
to travel or move about aimlessly or without a fixed destination, often in search of something
move about aimlessly; wander

Examples

They spent months roving across Europe, never staying in one town for long.
The wolves rove through the forest in search of prey during the night.
As a young man, he roved from job to job, never settling in one place.

Synset Examples

The gypsies roamed the woods
roving vagabonds
the wandering Jew
The cattle roam across the prairie
the laborers drift from one town to the next
They rolled from town to town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wander - to walk or move in a leisurely, casual, or aimless way
  • Synonym: to roam - to move about or travel aimlessly or unsystematically, especially over a wide area
  • Antonym: settle - to stay in one place and not move around

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'roven' (to wander, shoot as in archery), origin uncertain.
