uniovular

1. ADJ

/ ˌjuːniˈɑːvjələr / (AmE), / ˌjuːniˈɒvjʊlə / (BrE)
(of an ovary or organism) having a single ovule; producing or originating from a single ovule.
having a single ovule
medical

Examples

Identical twins are often described as uniovular because they develop from a single ovum.
Some species of plants produce uniovular ovaries, resulting in seeds that are genetically similar.
The scientist focused her research on the differences between uniovular and biovular twin development.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: biovular - having two ovules

Etymology

From Latin 'uni-' (one) + 'ovulum' (small egg, ovule).
