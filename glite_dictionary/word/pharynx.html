pharynx

1. NOUN

/ ˈfærɪŋks / (AmE), / ˈfærɪŋks / (BrE)
the muscular tube in the throat that connects the mouth and nasal passages to the esophagus and larynx
throat tube connecting mouth and esophagus
medical

Examples

Food passes from the mouth to the pharynx before entering the esophagus.
A sore pharynx can make swallowing very painful.
The doctor examined the patient's pharynx for signs of infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throat - the front part of the neck, including the pharynx and surrounding structures

Etymology

From Greek pharynx (throat, windpipe).
