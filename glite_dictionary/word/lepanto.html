Lepanto

1. PROPN

/ lɪˈpɑːn.toʊ / (AmE), / lɛˈpæn.təʊ / (BrE)
a naval battle in 1571 in which Turkish sea power was destroyed by a league of Christian nations organized by the Pope
naval battle between Christian and Turkish forces, 1571

Examples

The Battle of Lepanto marked a turning point in Mediterranean naval history.
Many poets and historians have written about the significance of Lepanto.
At Lepanto, a coalition fleet achieved a decisive victory over the Ottoman navy.

Etymology

From the Italian 'Lepanto', referring to the Greek town Naupaktos near which the battle took place.
