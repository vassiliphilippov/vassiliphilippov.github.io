impenitent

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛnɪtənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛnɪtənt / (BrE)
Not feeling shame or regret about one's actions; not remorseful or repentant.
not feeling regret or remorse

Examples

Despite his conviction, he remained impenitent and refused to apologize for his actions.
The judge was troubled by the defendant’s impenitent attitude throughout the trial.
She gave an impenitent shrug when confronted about her dishonest behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unrepentant - not feeling regret or remorse for wrongdoings
  • Antonym: remorseful - feeling or expressing regret or guilt for wrongdoing

Etymology

From Latin impenitens, from in- (not) + paenitens (repenting).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛnətənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛnɪtənt / (BrE)
Not feeling or expressing remorse or regret for wrongdoing; unrepentant.
not feeling remorse; unrepentant

Examples

Despite being found guilty, the defendant remained impenitent in court.
Her impenitent attitude shocked those who expected an apology.
He spoke with an impenitent tone, showing no sign of regret for his actions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: penitent - feeling or showing sorrow and regret for having done wrong
  • Antonym: remorseful - feeling deep regret or guilt

Etymology

From Latin 'impenitens', from 'in-' (not) + 'paenitere' (to feel regret).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01748158-a
Synset POS: a
