rye

1. NOUN

/ raɪ / (AmE), / raɪ / (BrE)
A type of whiskey distilled from rye grain, or from a mixture of rye and malted barley.
whiskey made from rye grain

Examples

He poured himself a glass of rye after dinner.
The bar specializes in craft cocktails using rye as the base spirit.
Canadian rye is known for its smooth flavor and spicy notes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiskey - a distilled alcoholic beverage made from fermented grain mash

Etymology

From Old English 'ryge', referring originally to the cereal grain, and by extension its use in alcohol production.

2. NOUN

/ raɪ / (AmE), / raɪ / (BrE)
a hardy annual cereal grass (Secale cereale) widely cultivated for its grain, which is used for making bread and as animal fodder, especially in northern Europe and North America
a hardy cereal grain used for bread and fodder

Examples

Rye bread is a staple food in many Eastern European countries.
The farmer planted fields of rye to improve the soil for next season's crops.
She prefers rye over wheat when baking her own bread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheat - a common cereal grain used in bread and pasta
  • Synonym: barley - a cereal grain used for food and brewing
  • Synonym: oats - a cereal grain commonly used for animal feed and as food

Etymology

From Old English 'ryge', related to Dutch 'rogge' and German 'Roggen'.

3. NOUN

/ raɪ / (AmE), / raɪ / (BrE)
the seed of a cereal grass used for food and to make flour, bread, and alcoholic drinks
seed of a cereal grass

Examples

Farmers harvested a bumper crop of rye this year, which was used to make bread and whiskey.
Freshly baked rye bread has a distinctive flavor compared to bread made from wheat.
The distillery specializes in spirits distilled from rye rather than corn or barley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheat - seed of another major cereal grass, used for flour and bread
  • Synonym: barley - another cereal grain similar to rye, used for food and beverages

Etymology

From Old English *ryge*, related to German *Roggen* and Dutch *rogge*.
