Connaraceae

1. NOUN

/ kəˌnærəˈsiːi / (AmE), / kɒˌnærəˈsiːi / (BrE)
A family of mostly tropical climbing shrubs or small trees, closely related to Leguminosae.
a family of tropical woody plants

Examples

The Connaraceae include various woody climbers found in tropical forests.
Botanists have classified several species from Africa and Asia within the Connaraceae family.
Research on Connaraceae has increased our understanding of tropical plant diversity.

Etymology

From the genus name Connarus + the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ kəˌnɛr.əˈsiː.i / (AmE), / kɒn.əˈreɪ.siː.iː / (BrE)
a family of mostly tropical climbing shrubs or small trees, closely related to the Leguminosae, found chiefly in Africa and Asia
family of tropical climbing shrubs or small trees

Examples

The Connaraceae are prominent in many tropical forests around the world.
Several species within the Connaraceae family are used in traditional medicine.
Botanists often compare Connaraceae to Leguminosae due to certain morphological similarities.

Etymology

From the genus name Connarus combined with the standard botanical suffix -aceae (family).
