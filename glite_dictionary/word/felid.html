felid

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːlɪd / (AmE), / ˈfiːlɪd / (BrE)
any member of the cat family (Felidae), including both wild and domestic species
member of the cat family

Examples

The tiger is the largest living felid and a formidable predator.
Many felids possess excellent night vision, aiding their hunting activities.
The cheetah is the only felid that cannot fully retract its claws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cat - a small domesticated carnivorous mammal
  • Antonym: canid - any member of the dog family (Canidae)

Etymology

From Latin 'felis' meaning 'cat', with zoological suffix '-id' denoting family.
