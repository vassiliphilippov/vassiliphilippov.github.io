alertness

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɝːtnəs / (AmE), / əˈlɜːtnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being attentive and quick to notice things
state of being attentive and aware

Examples

Her alertness during the night shift helped prevent a serious accident.
Drivers need to maintain alertness at all times, especially in poor weather conditions.
Regular exercise is said to improve mental alertness throughout the day.

Synset Examples

wakefulness, watchfulness, and bellicosity make a good hunter
vigilance is especially susceptible to fatigue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigilance - the action or state of keeping careful watch for danger or difficulties
  • Antonym: inattention - lack of attention; distraction

Etymology

From 'alert' + '-ness', with 'alert' from Italian 'all'erta' (on the lookout).

2. NOUN

/ əˈlɝːtnəs / (AmE), / əˈlɜːtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being quick to notice things and respond; lively attentiveness
lively attentiveness; quick to notice

Examples

The driver maintained his alertness throughout the long, tiring journey.
Early morning exercise helps increase mental alertness for the rest of the day.
Because of her alertness, she quickly spotted the error in the report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigilance - the action or state of keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties
  • Antonym: drowsiness - a state of being sleepy or lethargic

Etymology

From 'alert' + '-ness', where 'alert' is from Italian 'all'erta' (to the watchtower).

3. NOUN

/ əˈlɝːtnəs / (AmE), / əˈlɜːtnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being ready to notice or respond quickly to possible danger or problems
state of being ready to respond

Examples

His alertness during the emergency helped prevent a serious accident.
Good alertness is essential for air traffic controllers to ensure safety.
Lack of sleep can severely impair your alertness throughout the day.

Synset Examples

alerting was indicated by the desynchronization of the EEG

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigilance - the action or state of keeping careful watch for danger or difficulties

Etymology

From 'alert' (from Italian *all'erta*, on the watch), with '-ness' forming a noun.
