Rutaceae

1. NOUN

/ ruːˈteɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ruːˈtæsiː / (BrE)
a large family of dicotyledonous plants, the rue or citrus family, characterized by flowers divided into four or five parts and often having a strong scent; includes citrus fruits and ornamental rue.
the rue or citrus plant family

Examples

Plants in the Rutaceae family include oranges, lemons, and grapefruits.
Many members of the Rutaceae are cultivated for their aromatic fruits and flowers.
The strong scent typical of the Rutaceae is often used to identify members of this plant family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citrus - plants in the Rutaceae family, such as oranges and lemons

Etymology

From Latin 'ruta' (rue) + botanical family suffix '-aceae'.

2. NOUN

/ ruːˈteɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ruːˈteɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, often aromatic, typically with flowers divided into four or five parts; includes citrus fruits such as oranges and lemons
the rue or citrus plant family

Examples

The Rutaceae family includes such well-known members as lemons, oranges, and grapefruits.
Botanists often study the unique scent and petal arrangement found in Rutaceae.
Many ornamental garden plants, as well as edible citrus fruits, belong to the Rutaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citrus - any fruit or plant of the Citrus genus, widely cultivated members of Rutaceae
  • Antonym: Geraniaceae - a different family of dicotyledonous plants in the order Geraniales

Etymology

From Latin 'ruta' (rue) + scientific botanical suffix '-aceae'.
