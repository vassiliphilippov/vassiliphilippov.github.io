lemanderin

1. NOUN

/ lɛm.ənˈdɛr.ɪn / (AmE), / lɛː.mənˈdɛr.ɪn / (BrE)
A hybrid fruit that is a cross between a mandarin orange and a lemon, noted for its very acidic flesh and orange-colored peel.
citrus hybrid of mandarin and lemon

Examples

The farmers introduced a new variety of lemanderin to the market this season.
A lemanderin combines the tangy flavor of a lemon with the sweetness of a mandarin orange.
She made a refreshing drink using the juice of freshly picked lemanderins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon - a yellow citrus fruit with acidic flesh
  • Synonym: mandarin - a small citrus fruit with an easy-to-peel skin

Etymology

Blend of 'lemon' and 'mandarin', referring to its parent fruits.
