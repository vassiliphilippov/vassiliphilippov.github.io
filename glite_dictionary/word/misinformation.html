misinformation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪs.ɪn.fərˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.ɪn.fəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
false or inaccurate information, especially that which is spread unintentionally
false or inaccurate information

Examples

The article was full of misinformation about the vaccine's effectiveness.
Social media can be a major source of misinformation during elections.
She apologized for spreading misinformation without checking the facts first.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disinformation - false information deliberately spread to deceive
  • Degree Lower: information - facts provided or learned about something

Etymology

From mis- (wrong, incorrect) + information; modelled on Latin roots.
