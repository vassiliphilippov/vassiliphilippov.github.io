absent

1. VERB

/ ˈæb.sənt / (AmE), / ˈæb.sənt / (BrE)
to deliberately stay away from a place or event, especially when expected to be present
deliberately stay away

Examples

He deliberately absented himself from the meeting to avoid confrontation.
If you absent yourself from class, you must provide a valid reason.
She absented herself for several days due to illness.

Synset Examples

He absented himself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stay away - to not be present in a place

Etymology

From Latin 'absentare', meaning 'to keep away'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00422488-v
Synset POS: v

2. ADJ

/ ˈæb.sənt / (AmE), / ˈæb.sənt / (BrE)
not present, existing, or available; nonexistent or missing.
not present or nonexistent

Examples

His name was on the list, but he was absent from the meeting.
The necessary evidence is completely absent in this case.
Due to the storm, any signs of activity were absent from the streets.

Synset Examples

the thumb is absent
her appetite was lacking

Related Terms

  • Antonym: present - existing or available
  • Synonym: lacking - not present or not available

Etymology

From Latin absentem, present participle of abesse (to be away).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00931073-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈæb.sənt / (AmE), / ˈæb.sənt / (BrE)
not paying attention to what is happening because one's mind is on other things; lost in thought; preoccupied
lost in thought; preoccupied

Examples

She gave him an absent nod, clearly thinking of something else.
His absent expression suggested he wasn't listening to the conversation at all.
During meetings, she often wears an absent look when her mind drifts elsewhere.

Synset Examples

an absent stare
an absentminded professor
the scatty glancing quality of a hyperactive but unfocused intelligence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distracted - unable to concentrate; preoccupied
  • Antonym: attentive - paying close attention
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00166433-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈæb.sənt / (AmE), / ˈæb.sənt / (BrE)
not present in a place or situation where one is expected or required to be
not present; missing

Examples

Two students were absent from class yesterday due to illness.
Please explain why you were absent from the meeting this morning.
Her name was called, but she remained absent from the roll call.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: present - being in a particular place; not absent
  • Synonym: missing - not present where expected, unable to be found

Etymology

From Latin 'absens', present participle of 'abesse' (to be away).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01853600-a
Synset POS: a
