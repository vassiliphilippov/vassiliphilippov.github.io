pixy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪksi / (AmE), / ˈpɪksi / (BrE)
a creeping evergreen shrub with narrow overlapping leaves and early white star-shaped flowers, native to the pine barrens of New Jersey and the Carolinas
creeping evergreen shrub of U.S. pine barrens

Examples

The pixy thrives in the sandy soil of the New Jersey pine barrens.
Each spring, the pixy produces delicate white, star-shaped flowers that carpet the forest floor.
Conservationists are working to preserve the habitat of the rare pixy shrub.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪksi / (AmE), / ˈpɪksi / (BrE)
In folklore, a small mischievous fairy or sprite, especially one from Devon and Cornwall in England.
mischievous fairy in folklore

Examples

The children believed a pixy had hidden their toys in the garden.
According to legend, a pixy can lead travelers astray on misty nights.
The old tales warn of the tricks a mischievous pixy might play on farmers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairy - a small imaginary being of human form with magical powers
  • Synonym: imp - a small, mischievous supernatural being

Etymology

From Devon dialect, possibly of Celtic origin; related to 'pisky' in Cornish folklore.
