wort

1. NOUN

/ wɜrt / (AmE), / wɜːt / (BrE)
A plant, especially one forming part of a compound name for various herbs, such as 'liverwort' or 'milkwort'.
suffix for various plant names

Examples

The herbalist identified the wort in the field as a type of milkwort.
Many English plant names, such as lungwort or spiderwort, include the word wort.
In medieval times, a wort was commonly used in remedies for various ailments.

Etymology

Old English 'wyrt' meaning plant, root, or herb; related to German 'Wurz' (root).

2. NOUN

/ wɝt / (AmE), / wɜːt / (BrE)
a sweet liquid extracted from malted grains, used in brewing before fermentation occurs
unfermented liquid in brewing

Examples

The brewer carefully monitored the temperature as the wort cooled before adding the yeast.
After the grains are mashed, the resulting wort is transferred to the fermentation vessel.
A cloudy wort can affect the clarity of the finished beer.

Etymology

From Old English 'wyrt' (plant, herb), later specialized in brewing; related to German 'Würze' (wort, spice).
