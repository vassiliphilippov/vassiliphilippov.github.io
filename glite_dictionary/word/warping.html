warping

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːr.pɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɔː.pɪŋ / (BrE)
A process or effect by which something, especially a person's morals or mind, is distorted or twisted from its natural state.
moral or mental distortion

Examples

Many consider the warping of values by propaganda a serious threat to society.
His childhood trauma led to a subtle warping of his views on trust and loyalty.
The novel explores the warping of the human mind under extreme pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distortion - the action of distorting or the state of being distorted
  • Synonym: corruption - moral perversion; depravity

Etymology

From the verb 'warp', Old English 'weorpan' (to throw, turn, or distort).
