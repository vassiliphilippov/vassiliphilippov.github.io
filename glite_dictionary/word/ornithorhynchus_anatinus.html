Ornithorhynchus anatinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrnɪθoʊˈrɪŋkəs ˌænəˈtaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌɔːnɪθəˈrɪŋkəs ˌanəˈtʌɪnəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the platypus, a small aquatic monotreme native to Australia and Tasmania, known for its broad bill, webbed feet, and dense fur.
scientific name for the platypus

Examples

The Ornithorhynchus anatinus is easily recognized by its duck-bill and webbed feet.
Scientists studied the habitat of Ornithorhynchus anatinus in Tasmanian rivers.
Because of its unique features, Ornithorhynchus anatinus has long fascinated biologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platypus - a small, egg-laying aquatic mammal found in Australia, with a duck-like bill

Etymology

From Greek 'ornitho-' (bird), 'rhynchos' (snout), and Latin 'anatinus' (duck-like).
