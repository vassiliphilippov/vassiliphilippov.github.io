compost

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːm.poʊst / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.pɒst / (BrE)
A mixture of decaying plant matter and manure used to fertilize and improve the soil.
decayed organic material for fertilizer

Examples

The gardener spread compost over the flower beds to enrich the soil.
Kitchen scraps and yard waste can be turned into compost for your garden.
Many farmers use compost as a natural alternative to chemical fertilizers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertilizer - a substance added to soil to make plants grow more successfully

Etymology

From Old French 'composte', from Latin 'compositus' (put together).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑm.poʊst / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.pəʊst / (BrE)
to convert (organic matter) into compost, typically by allowing it to decompose under controlled conditions
to convert material into compost

Examples

Many households now compost their food scraps to reduce waste.
Gardeners often compost leaves and grass clippings in the autumn.
You should compost organic debris instead of throwing it away.

Synset Examples

compost organic debris

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decompose - to rot or break down into simpler forms, especially of organic matter
