Tsushima

1. PROPN

/ tsuːˈʃiːmə / (AmE), / tsuːˈʃiːmə / (BrE)
A decisive naval battle in the Russo-Japanese War (1905), in which the Japanese fleet defeated the Russian fleet in the Korean Strait.
naval battle (Russo-Japanese War, 1905)

Examples

The Battle of Tsushima marked a turning point in naval warfare history.
Many historians regard Tsushima as the moment Japan emerged as a major world power.
The Russian Baltic Fleet suffered a devastating defeat at Tsushima in 1905.

Etymology

Named after Tsushima Island, near where the battle took place, Japan.
