Thanatos

1. NOUN

/ ˈθæn.əˌtɑs / (AmE), / ˈθæn.ətɒs / (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis) the unconscious drive towards death and self-destruction.
psychoanalytic death drive

Examples

Some Freudian analysts argue that Thanatos explains seemingly self-destructive behavior in humans.
The concept of Thanatos contrasts sharply with Eros, the drive for life and creativity.
Literary critics have explored themes of Thanatos in twentieth-century poetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death drive - Freudian concept for the instinctual drive toward death and self-destruction
  • Antonym: Eros - (psychoanalysis) the life drive, contrasting Thanatos

Etymology

From Greek Thanatos (Θάνατος), personification of death in Greek mythology.

2. PROPN

/ ˈθænəˌtɒs / (AmE), / ˈθænətɒs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the personification of death, son of Nyx.
Greek mythological figure, personification of death

Examples

In Greek mythology, Thanatos was the spirit who guided souls to the underworld.
Freud borrowed the term Thanatos to describe the drive towards death and destruction.
Many ancient texts personify death as Thanatos, a shadowy and inevitable figure.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Θάνατος (Thánatos), meaning 'death'.
