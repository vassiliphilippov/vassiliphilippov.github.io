Quassia amara

1. NOUN

/ kwɑːsiə əˈmɑːrə / (AmE), / ˈkwæsɪə əˈmɑːrə / (BrE)
A South American shrub or small tree with bright scarlet flowers, known for its fine-grained yellowish wood and as a source of the bitter drug quassia.
South American shrub yielding bitter quassia

Examples

Extracts from Quassia amara are often used as a natural insecticide in organic farming.
The bright scarlet flowers of Quassia amara make it an attractive ornamental plant for gardens.
Traditional herbalists use the bark and wood of Quassia amara to prepare bitter tonics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quassia - the bitter drug obtained from Quassia amara wood

Etymology

From the genus name Quassia, named after Graman Quassi, an 18th-century Surinamese healer; amara from Latin 'bitter'.
