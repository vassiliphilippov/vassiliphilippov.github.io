sulphur

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlfər / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə / (BrE)
a yellow chemical element (symbol S), found abundantly in nature, odorless and tasteless, used in industry and occurring in minerals and volcanic regions
a yellow chemical element; nonmetal

Examples

Elemental sulphur is often found near hot springs and volcanic craters.
They added sulphur to the soil to improve its fertility.
The distinctive yellow crystals of sulphur are easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfur - Alternative spelling (US) of 'sulphur'

Etymology

From Latin 'sulphur' (brimstone, sulfur).

2. VERB

/ ˈsʌlfər / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə / (BrE)
to treat or expose (something), especially food, to sulphur or sulphur dioxide, typically in order to preserve it or prevent discoloration
treat or preserve with sulphur

Examples

Many commercial dried apricots are sulphured to maintain their orange color.
It is common to sulphur grapes before drying them into raisins.
After you sulphur the fruit, be sure to let it air out before storage.

Synset Examples

These dried fruits are sulphured

Etymology

From Latin 'sulphur', meaning brimstone. The verb originated from the noun referring to the chemical element.
