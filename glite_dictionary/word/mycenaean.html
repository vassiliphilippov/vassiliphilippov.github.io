Mycenaean

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪsɪˈniːən / (AmE), / ˌmaɪsɪˈniːən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of ancient Mycenae, an important center of Greek civilization in the late Bronze Age.
person from ancient Mycenae

Examples

Archaeologists discovered the tomb of a wealthy Mycenaean dating back to the 14th century BC.
The Mycenaean was known for his skill in warfare and his elaborate palatial culture.
Many treasures were found in the graves of Mycenaeans near the ancient citadel.

Etymology

From Mycenae, the name of the ancient Greek city, plus the suffix -an.

2. ADJ

/ ˌmaɪsɪˈniən / (AmE), / ˌmaɪsɪˈniːən / (BrE)
relating to ancient Mycenae or its inhabitants, language, or culture, especially of the late Bronze Age in Greece (circa 1600–1100 BCE)
of ancient Mycenae, its people, or culture

Examples

The Mycenaean palace at Pylos revealed impressive architectural features.
Many Mycenaean artifacts have been discovered in archaeological excavations across Greece.
Some ancient Greek myths were shaped during the Mycenaean period.

Synset Examples

Mycenaean bronzes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Minoan - relating to ancient Crete or the Minoan civilization
  • Antonym: modern Greek - relating to contemporary Greek language, people, or culture, in contrast to ancient periods

Etymology

From Mycenae, an ancient city in Greece.
