impressive

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɛsɪv / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛsɪv / (BrE)
evoking admiration, awe, or respect; making a strong or vivid impression
making a strong or vivid impression

Examples

The team delivered an impressive performance that amazed everyone in the audience.
Her impressive résumé helped her secure the job easily.
The architect designed an impressive building that dominates the city skyline.

Synset Examples

an impressive ceremony

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unimpressive - not making a strong or vivid impression
  • Synonym: remarkable - worthy of attention; striking

Etymology

From Medieval Latin impressivus (capable of making an impression), from Latin impressus (pressed into).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɛsɪv / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛsɪv / (BrE)
evoking admiration or wonder, especially by being particularly notable, effective, or skillful
evoking admiration or wonder

Examples

Her speech was truly impressive, leaving the entire audience in awe.
The athlete's impressive performance broke several records at the tournament.
The building's architecture is impressive, combining both modern and classical elements.

Synset Examples

gave an impressive performance as Othello
a telling gesture

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unimpressive - not evoking admiration or interest
  • Synonym: remarkable - worthy of being noticed due to being unusually good or surprising

Etymology

From Latin impressivus (‘capable of being pressed into or impressed’), from imprimere (‘to impress, stamp’).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00838831-s
Synset POS: s
