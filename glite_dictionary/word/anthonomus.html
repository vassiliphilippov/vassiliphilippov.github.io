Anthonomus

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθəˈnoʊməs / (AmE), / ˌanθəˈnəʊməs / (BrE)
A genus of weevils, some species of which are serious pests of cultivated plants.
genus of plant-damaging weevils

Examples

The Anthonomus weevil is notorious for damaging cotton crops.
Farmers monitor fields for signs of Anthonomus infestation during the growing season.
Several species within the genus Anthonomus are considered major agricultural pests.

Etymology

From Greek anthos (flower) and nomos (law, custom), used in New Latin taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˌænθəˈnoʊməs / (AmE), / ˌænθəˈnəʊməs / (BrE)
a genus of weevils that are destructive to cultivated plants, including important pests such as the boll weevil.
a genus of destructive weevils

Examples

The Anthonomus genus includes pests that threaten cotton and other crops.
Farmers monitor their fields for signs of Anthonomus infestations each season.
The most notorious species within Anthonomus is the boll weevil, a major cotton pest.

Etymology

From Greek 'anthos' (flower) + 'nomos' (law, custom), referring to the weevils' association with plants.
