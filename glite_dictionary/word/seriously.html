seriously

1. ADV

/ ˈsɪriəsli / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəsli / (BrE)
To a severe or serious degree; very or extremely.
to a severe or serious degree

Examples

He was seriously injured in the car accident and needed immediate surgery.
Many buildings were seriously damaged during the earthquake last night.
If you seriously want this job, you should prepare thoroughly for the interview.

Synset Examples

fingers so badly frozen they had to be amputated
badly injured
a severely impaired heart
is gravely ill
was seriously ill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: badly - to a great or serious degree
  • Antonym: mildly - in a mild or gentle degree

Etymology

Formed from 'serious' + '-ly', dating to the 16th century.

2. ADV

/ ˈsɪriəsli / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəsli / (BrE)
In a serious or earnest manner; with seriousness and not joking.
in a serious manner

Examples

She spoke seriously about her plans for the future.
Are you seriously telling me you’ve never been abroad?
He looked at me seriously before answering the question.

Synset Examples

talking earnestly with his son
she started studying snakes in earnest
a play dealing seriously with the question of divorce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earnestly - in an earnest or sincere manner
  • Antonym: jokingly - in a joking or playful manner

Etymology

From 'serious' + '-ly'. 'Serious' from Latin 'serius' (earnest, grave).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00166233-r
Synset POS: r
