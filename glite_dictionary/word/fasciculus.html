fasciculus

1. NOUN

/ fəˈsɪkjələs / (AmE), / fəˈsɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
A bundle of fibers, especially nerve fibers, in the body.
a bundle of nerve fibers
medical

Examples

The spinal cord contains several important fasciculi that transmit sensory information.
Damage to a fasciculus in the brain may result in loss of function.
Doctors examined the fasciculus to understand the patient's neurological disorder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fascicle - a small bundle of muscle or nerve fibers

Etymology

From Latin 'fasciculus', diminutive of 'fascis' (bundle).
