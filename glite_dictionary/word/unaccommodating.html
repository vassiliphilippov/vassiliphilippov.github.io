unaccommodating

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈkɑː.mə.deɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈkɒ.mə.deɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
not willing to help or do favors for others; not obliging or cooperative
not willing to help or oblige

Examples

The manager was unaccommodating when we asked for a late checkout.
He is known for being unaccommodating to special requests from customers.
Her unaccommodating attitude made teamwork very difficult.

Synset Examples

the unaccommodating bus driver pulled out while she was banging on the door

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accommodating - willing to help; obliging
  • Synonym: unhelpful - not helping; not useful

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'accommodating' (from Latin accommodāre, to make fit or suitable).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈkɑː.məˌdeɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈkɒm.əˌdeɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
not willing to help or provide assistance; not cooperative or helpful
not helpful or cooperative

Examples

The hotel staff were surprisingly unaccommodating about our request for a late check-out.
She found her new boss to be rather unaccommodating when it came to flexible hours.
Mountain climbing can be dangerous due to the unaccommodating environment.

Synset Examples

rudely unaccommodating to the customers
The conditions in mountain regions are often harsh and unaccommodating

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accommodating - helpful and willing to do what someone else wants
  • Synonym: helpful - giving or ready to give help

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not', and 'accommodating', from Latin accommodāre (to fit, adapt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01201731-s
Synset POS: s
