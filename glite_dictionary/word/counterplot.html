counterplot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌplɑt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌplɒt / (BrE)
A secret scheme or plan intended to thwart or undermine another plot.
plan to thwart another plot

Examples

The detective launched a counterplot to foil the villain's scheme.
Unbeknownst to the conspirators, a counterplot was already in motion.
The story became more intriguing when a new counterplot emerged among the rivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - a secret plan to achieve a goal, especially illegally or dishonestly
  • Synonym: scheme - a secret or cunning plan

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) and 'plot' (scheme or plan).

2. VERB

/ ˈkaʊn.tɚ.plɑːt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.tə.plɒt / (BrE)
to devise or enact a plot in response to another plot, especially as a form of opposition or retaliation
devise a plot in response to another

Examples

The villain counterplotted against the hero’s plan to save the city.
In response to the scheme, she quickly counterplotted to outwit her rivals.
They spent the night counterplotting after discovering their enemy’s intentions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - to make a secret plan, typically to achieve something illicit

Etymology

From 'counter-' (against) + 'plot' (a secret plan or scheme).

Synset POS: v
