Hadean

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪdiən / (AmE), / ˈheɪdiən / (BrE)
the earliest eon in Earth's history, from the formation of the planet about 4.6 billion years ago to about 4 billion years ago, before the appearance of the oldest known rocks or evidence of life
the earliest eon in Earth's history

Examples

The Hadean eon was characterized by intense meteorite bombardment and a molten surface.
Scientists study zircon crystals to learn about the conditions on Earth during the Hadean.
No rocks older than the Hadean eon have yet been discovered on Earth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Archean - the eon following the Hadean, marked by the formation of the Earth's crust

Etymology

From Hades, the Greek god of the underworld, alluding to the eon's harsh conditions, plus -an.

2. ADJ

/ heɪˈdiː.ən / (AmE), / həˈdiː.ən / (BrE)
of or relating to Hades or Tartarus, or to the earliest eon in Earth's history.
relating to Hades or the earliest geologic eon

Examples

The planet's surface during the Hadean era was scorching and inhospitable.
The novel described the underworld in dark, Hadean terms.
Geologists study rocks dating back to the Hadean period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infernal - relating to hell or the underworld

Etymology

From Hades, the Greek god of the underworld, with -an forming an adjective.
