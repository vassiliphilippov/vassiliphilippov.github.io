Baccharis halimifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækərɪs ˌhælɪmɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈbakərɪs ˌhalɪmɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A North American and Caribbean shrub found in salt marshes, with fruit surrounded by white, plumelike hairy tufts.
shrub of salt marshes; white, plumelike fruit tufts

Examples

Baccharis halimifolia is often used in landscaping due to its tolerance of salty soils.
The seeds of Baccharis halimifolia are dispersed by wind thanks to their fluffy tufts.
Conservationists monitor Baccharis halimifolia populations in coastal wetlands.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'bakcharis' (a fragrant plant); species epithet from Latin 'halimifolia' meaning 'with leaves like halimus' (a salt-tolerant plant).
