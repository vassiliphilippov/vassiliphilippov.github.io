tabour

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪ.bər / (AmE), / ˈteɪ.bə / (BrE)
a small drum with one head, traditionally made of soft calfskin, used especially in folk and historical music
small one-headed drum

Examples

The musician played the tabour while a companion danced around the fire.
Historically, a tabour was often used to accompany a pipe in folk music.
He crafted a tabour with a single calfskin head for the medieval festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tabor - a small drum, especially of medieval origin

Etymology

From Old French 'tabour', from Persian 'tabīr' (small drum).
