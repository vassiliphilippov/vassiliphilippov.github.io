Hygrophoraceae

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌɡrɑfəˈreɪsiaɪ / (AmE), / haɪˌɡrɒfəˈriːsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi in the order Agaricales, typically having gills with a clean, waxy appearance
a family of fungi with waxy gills
medical

Examples

The Hygrophoraceae are well known to mycologists for their brightly colored mushrooms.
Recent studies have reclassified some species previously assigned to the Hygrophoraceae.
Members of the Hygrophoraceae often grow in grasslands and woodlands worldwide.

Etymology

From New Latin Hygrophorus (a fungal genus) + -aceae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ haɪˌɡrɑfəˈreɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / haɪˌɡrɒfəˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi in the order Agaricales, characterized by mushrooms with gills that have a clean, waxy appearance
family of fungi with waxy gills

Examples

Several species in the Hygrophoraceae family are easily identified by their waxy gills.
The forest was rich with mushrooms belonging to the Hygrophoraceae.
Research has shown that members of the Hygrophoraceae play a role in forest ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Agaricaceae - a related family of fungi in the same order

Etymology

From the genus name Hygrophorus, from Greek 'hygros' (wet) + '-phoros' (bearing), with standard scientific suffix '-aceae' for plant/fungal families.
