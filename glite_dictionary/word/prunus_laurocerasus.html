Prunus laurocerasus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpruːnəs ˌloʊroʊˈsɛrəs / (AmE), / ˌpruːnəs ˌlɔːrəˈsɛərəs / (BrE)
A Eurasian evergreen shrub or small tree with glossy leaves, clusters of white flowers, and fruit, often cultivated for hedges and yielding oil similar to bitter almond oil.
Eurasian evergreen shrub; cherry laurel

Examples

The tall Prunus laurocerasus hedge provided privacy from the street.
Gardeners often use Prunus laurocerasus in landscape designs for its dense foliage.
The fragrant white flowers of Prunus laurocerasus attract pollinators in the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherry laurel - Common name for Prunus laurocerasus, an evergreen shrub or small tree

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Prunus' (plum, cherry) and species 'laurocerasus' (laurel cherry), referring to its laurel-like leaves.
