Quercus ellipsoidalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs ɛˌlɪpsoɪˈdeɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ɛˌlɪpsɔɪˈdeɪlɪs / (BrE)
A small to medium deciduous oak tree native to east central North America, known for its leaves with sharply pointed lobes.
a North American deciduous oak tree species

Examples

The Quercus ellipsoidalis thrives in sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands.
Botanists studied the unique acorn shape of Quercus ellipsoidalis during their field research.
In the fall, the leaves of Quercus ellipsoidalis turn a vivid red, adding color to the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern pin oak - another common name for Quercus ellipsoidalis
  • Degree Higher: oak - a tree of the genus Quercus, typically having lobed leaves and acorns

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' meaning oak; 'ellipsoidalis' referring to the ellipsoid shape of its acorns.
