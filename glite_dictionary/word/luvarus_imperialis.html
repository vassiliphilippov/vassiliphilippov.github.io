Luvarus imperialis

1. NOUN

/ luːˈvɑːrəs ɪmˌpɪəriˈælɪs / (AmE), / luːˈvɑːrəs ɪmˌpɪərɪˈælɪs / (BrE)
A large, silvery marine fish found in warm seas worldwide, resembling a whale and feeding on plankton; also known as the luvar.
species of large, silvery marine fish

Examples

The rare Luvarus imperialis is occasionally caught by deep-sea fishermen in tropical waters.
Marine biologists were thrilled to spot a juvenile Luvarus imperialis near the reef.
The Luvarus imperialis is known for its distinctive, whale-like shape and silvery color.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, the taxonomy for this fish species.
