branchiopod

1. ADJ

/ ˈbræŋki̯əˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈbræŋkɪəˌpɒd / (BrE)
of, relating to, or characteristic of the subclass Branchiopoda, a group of small freshwater crustaceans.
relating to Branchiopoda (aquatic crustaceans)

Examples

The branchiopod species thrive in temporary pools formed during the rainy season.
Scientists are studying the branchiopod adaptations that allow these creatures to survive in extreme environments.
A branchiopod crustacean, such as a fairy shrimp, was discovered in the pond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branchiopod - a small freshwater crustacean of the subclass Branchiopoda

Etymology

From Greek *branchia* (gills) + *pous, podos* (foot); refers to animals with gill-like appendages.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrænkiˌoʊpɑd / (AmE), / ˈbraŋkɪəˌpɒd / (BrE)
an aquatic crustacean with a carapace and many pairs of leaflike appendages used for swimming, breathing, and feeding, found in both fresh and salt water
type of aquatic crustacean
medical

Examples

The freshwater lakes are home to several rare species of branchiopod.
Tiny branchiopods filter nutrients from the water with their leaflike limbs.
Scientists studied the unique swimming adaptations of the branchiopod under a microscope.

Etymology

From Greek *branchia* (gills) and *pous, podos* (foot), meaning 'gill-foot'.
