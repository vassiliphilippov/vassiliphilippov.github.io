Eucalyptus globulus

1. NOUN

/ juːˈkæləptəs gloʊˈbjuːləs / (AmE), / juːˈkælɪptəs ˈglɒbjʊləs / (BrE)
A tall, fast-growing tree native to Australia, known for its bluish young leaves and medicinally valuable oil.
species of eucalyptus tree

Examples

The leaves of the Eucalyptus globulus are often harvested for their essential oils.
Farmers in Australia plant Eucalyptus globulus for timber and windbreaks.
Researchers have studied Eucalyptus globulus for its antiseptic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eucalyptus - type of tall aromatic tree; genus containing Eucalyptus globulus

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Eucalyptus', from Greek 'eu-' (well) + 'kalyptos' (covered), and species 'globulus' (small sphere), referring to the flower bud shape.
