fils

1. NOUN

/ fɪls / (AmE), / fɪls / (BrE)
a fractional monetary unit used in Bahrain, Iraq, Jordan, and Kuwait, equal to one thousandth of a dinar
fractional unit of currency in several Arab countries

Examples

In Kuwait, a small bottle of water costs 250 fils.
The parking meter requires payment in fils, not dinars.
She received ten fils in change after her purchase.

Etymology

From Arabic فلس (fils), meaning 'money' or 'coin'.

2. NOUN

/ fɪls / (AmE), / fɪls / (BrE)
A monetary unit of several Middle Eastern countries, equal to one thousandth of the dinar in some countries or a subunit of other currencies like the Yemeni rial.
Middle Eastern monetary unit

Examples

The price was 500 fils for a loaf of bread in the market.
He exchanged his dollars for a few Yemeni fils and some rials.
Coins of 100 fils are commonly used in Yemen for small transactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirham - currency unit in several Arab states, including the United Arab Emirates and Morocco

Etymology

From Arabic فِلْس (fils), ultimately from Latin follis (bag, purse, coin).
