epiphora

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪfərə / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪfərə / (BrE)
The repetition of a word or phrase at the end of successive clauses, sentences, or verses for emphasis or artistic effect.
repetition at sentence or verse ends

Examples

The poem makes effective use of epiphora to emphasize its central message.
Writers often use epiphora to create rhythm and reinforce key ideas in their work.
In her speech, the politician employed epiphora by ending each sentence with the words 'for the people.'

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anaphora - the repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses or sentences

Etymology

From Greek epiphora (a bringing to, repetition), from epi- (upon) + pherein (to bring).
