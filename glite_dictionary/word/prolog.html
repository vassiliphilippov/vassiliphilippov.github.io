Prolog

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊ.lɑːɡ / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.lɒɡ / (BrE)
a high-level programming language developed for artificial intelligence and computational linguistics, especially for solving problems involving natural language processing and symbolic reasoning
logic programming language for AI

Examples

Many artificial intelligence courses use Prolog to teach logic programming concepts.
The chatbot was developed using Prolog due to its strength in handling symbolic reasoning.
Some software engineers prefer Prolog when working on projects involving language parsing.

Etymology

From French 'programmation en logique' (programming in logic), contracted to 'Prolog' in the 1970s.
