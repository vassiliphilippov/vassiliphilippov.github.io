neurohypophysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʊroʊhaɪˈpɑfəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊhaɪˈpɒfəsɪs / (BrE)
the posterior lobe of the pituitary gland, which releases hormones produced by the hypothalamus into the bloodstream
posterior lobe of the pituitary gland
medical

Examples

Damage to the neurohypophysis can result in the disruption of hormone release.
The neurohypophysis stores and releases oxytocin and vasopressin produced by the hypothalamus.
Magnetic resonance imaging revealed an abnormality in the patient's neurohypophysis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adenohypophysis - the anterior lobe of the pituitary gland

Etymology

From Greek 'neuron' (nerve) and 'hypophysis' (pituitary gland).
