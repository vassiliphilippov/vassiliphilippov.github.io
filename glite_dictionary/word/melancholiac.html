melancholiac

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛl.ənˈkoʊ.li.æk / (AmE), / ˌmɛl.ənˈkəʊ.li.æk / (BrE)
a person who is habitually affected by deep sadness or melancholia
person affected by melancholia

Examples

The melancholiac often withdrew from social gatherings, preferring solitude to company.
As a diagnosed melancholiac, he wrote poetry filled with sorrow and longing.
Some believed that the melancholiac saw the world more clearly, albeit through a veil of sadness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melancholic - a person suffering from melancholia
  • Antonym: optimist - a person who tends to be hopeful and confident about the future

Etymology

From Greek melankholiakos, from melankholia (melancholy).
