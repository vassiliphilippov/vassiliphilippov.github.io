pimozide

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈmoʊzaɪd / (AmE), / pɪˈməʊzaɪd / (BrE)
a drug used to treat certain mental and behavioral disorders, particularly Tourette's syndrome and schizophrenia
antipsychotic medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed pimozide to help control the patient's tics.
Patients taking pimozide should have their heart rhythm monitored regularly.
He experienced fewer outbursts after starting treatment with pimozide.

Etymology

Formed from chemical nomenclature; not of general linguistic interest.
