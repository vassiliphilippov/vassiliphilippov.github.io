Abies balsamea

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.bi.iːz bælˈseɪ.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.bi.iːz bælˈseɪ.mɪ.ə / (BrE)
A medium-sized fir tree native to northeastern North America, known for its fragrant leaves and use as pulpwood and Christmas trees.
a North American fir tree with fragrant leaves

Examples

The Abies balsamea is often chosen as a Christmas tree for its pleasant scent and appearance.
Foresters harvest Abies balsamea for its valuable pulpwood used in the paper industry.
When you crush the needles of Abies balsamea, they release a distinctive balsam aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsam fir - another name for Abies balsamea
  • Degree Lower: fir - a type of evergreen tree, genus Abies

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Abies' (fir) and species name 'balsamea' (balsam-like).
