reefy

1. ADJ

/ ˈriːfi / (AmE), / ˈriːfi / (BrE)
(of an area of water) full of submerged reefs, sandbanks, or shoals.
full of submerged reefs or sandbanks

Examples

The sailors avoided the reefy waters to prevent damaging the boat.
A reefy coastline can be hazardous for inexperienced mariners.
The map clearly marked all reefy sections along the channel.

Synset Examples

reefy shallows
shoaly waters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoaly - full of shoals (shallow places in a body of water)

Etymology

From 'reef' (a ridge of rock or sand in the sea) + -y (adjective suffix).
