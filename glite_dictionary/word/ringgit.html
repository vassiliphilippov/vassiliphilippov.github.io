ringgit

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋɡɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋɡɪt / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Malaysia, equal to 100 sen
Malaysian currency unit

Examples

The price of the souvenir was twenty-five ringgit at the local market.
She exchanged her dollars for ringgit before arriving in Kuala Lumpur.
The hotel listed its rates in both US dollars and ringgit.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sen - a subunit of currency in Malaysia, equal to 1/100 of a ringgit
  • Synonym: baht - the basic unit of currency in Thailand

Etymology

From Malay 'ringgit' (jagged), originally referring to the serrated edges of silver Spanish dollars used in the region.
