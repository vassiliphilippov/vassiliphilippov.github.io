cross-sectional

1. ADJ

/ krɑs ˈsɛkʃənəl / (AmE), / krɒs ˈsɛkʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or representing a cross section or a cut made across something, especially to show internal structure.
relating to a cross section

Examples

The engineer examined the cross-sectional area of the beam to assess its strength.
A cross-sectional image of the organ reveals its internal structure.
They took a cross-sectional slice of the tree to study its rings.

Synset Examples

a cross-sectional slice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sectional - relating to a section; made up of sections
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ ˌkrɔsˈsɛkʃənl / (AmE), / ˌkrɒsˈsɛkʃən(ə)l / (BrE)
relating to or representing a plane cut at right angles to the length of something, especially to show its internal features or structure.
relating to a cross section; at right angles to length

Examples

The MRI provided a detailed cross-sectional image of the patient's brain.
Engineers analyzed the cross-sectional area of the beam to assess its strength.
The textbook includes several cross-sectional diagrams of the heart.

Synset Examples

cross-section views of the neck

Related Terms

  • Antonym: longitudinal - relating to lengthwise sections; running along the length rather than across

Etymology

Formed from 'cross section' + '-al' suffix.
