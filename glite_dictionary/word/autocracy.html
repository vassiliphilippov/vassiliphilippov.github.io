autocracy

1. NOUN

/ ɔˈtɑkrəsi / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒkrəsi / (BrE)
A political system in which one person possesses unlimited power and authority over others.
government by a single ruler with total power

Examples

The country was ruled by an autocracy for several decades before the revolution.
An autocracy often suppresses opposition and controls all aspects of public life.
Many historians have studied the decline of the Russian autocracy in the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictatorship - a form of government in which one person or a small group has absolute power
  • Antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population or all eligible members

Etymology

From Greek autokratia, from autos (self) + kratos (power, rule).

2. NOUN

/ ɔˈtɑkrəsi / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒkrəsi / (BrE)
A system of government or political theory in which one individual holds unlimited power and authority.
government by one with absolute power

Examples

The country was ruled as an autocracy for decades, with little tolerance for dissent.
Some philosophers defended autocracy as the most efficient form of government.
He wrote extensively about the dangers of autocracy and the erosion of individual rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictatorship - a form of government in which a single person or party wields absolute control
  • Antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population or all eligible members, typically through elected representatives

Etymology

From Greek autokrateia, from autos (self) + kratos (power, rule).
