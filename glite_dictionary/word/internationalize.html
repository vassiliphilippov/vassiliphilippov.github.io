to internationalize

1. VERB

/ ɪn.tɚˈnæʃ.ə.nəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪn.təˈnæʃ.ə.nə.laɪz / (BrE)
to make something international in character, scope, or involvement
make international in character or scope

Examples

The university plans to internationalize its curriculum to attract more overseas students.
Many companies seek to internationalize their operations in order to reach a global market.
The organization decided to internationalize its annual conference by including participants from over 40 countries.

Synset Examples

We internationalized the committee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to globalize - to make something worldwide in scope or application

Etymology

From international + -ize, originating in the late 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00410044-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌɪntɚˈnæʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃənaɪz / (BrE)
to place (something) under the control or regulation of an international body or authority
put under international control

Examples

The United Nations sought to internationalize the management of the Suez Canal.
There were proposals to internationalize air traffic regulations after the crisis.
Attempts to internationalize disputed territories have often met strong resistance from national authorities.

Synset Examples

internationalize trade of certain drugs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to nationalize - to bring under the control of a national government

Etymology

From 'international' + '-ize', formed in English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02446551-v
Synset POS: v
