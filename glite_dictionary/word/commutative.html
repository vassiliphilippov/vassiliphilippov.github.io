commutative

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmjuː.t̬ə.t̬ɪv / (AmE), / kəˈmjuː.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a mathematical operation) having a property such that the order of the elements does not affect the result; for example, a × b = b × a.
property of order-independence in math operations

Examples

Multiplication of real numbers is a commutative operation because the order does not change the result.
Matrix multiplication is generally not commutative, so AB may not equal BA.
In elementary arithmetic, both addition and multiplication are commutative operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: associative - property of an operation where grouping of elements does not affect the result
  • Antonym: noncommutative - not commutative; order of operation affects result

Etymology

From Latin 'commutare' (to exchange, interchange).
