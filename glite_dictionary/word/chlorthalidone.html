chlorthalidone

1. NOUN

/ klɔrˈθælɪˌdoʊn / (AmE), / klɔːˈθælɪdəʊn / (BrE)
a diuretic drug used to treat high blood pressure and conditions causing fluid retention
a diuretic used for hypertension
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed chlorthalidone to help lower his blood pressure.
Patients taking chlorthalidone should have their kidney function monitored regularly.
She experienced increased urination after starting chlorthalidone for her hypertension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrochlorothiazide - another commonly used thiazide diuretic for hypertension
