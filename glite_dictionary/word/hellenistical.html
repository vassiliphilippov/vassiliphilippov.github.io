Hellenistical

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɛlɪˈnɪstɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪˈnɪstɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of ancient Greek civilization, especially during the period following the conquests of Alexander the Great.
of or relating to classical Greek civilization

Examples

Her Hellenistical studies focused particularly on the influence of Greek philosophy on Roman thinkers.
The architecture of the building drew heavily on Hellenistical principles and motifs.
He wrote several articles examining the Hellenistical elements in early Christian art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hellenistic - relating to Greek history, culture, or art after Alexander the Great

Etymology

From 'Hellenistic', from Greek 'Hellēnistikos' (of the Greeks, Hellenistic) + English adjectival suffix '-al'.
