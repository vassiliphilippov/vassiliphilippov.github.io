to reconsider

1. VERB

/ ˌriːkənˈsɪdər / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈsɪdə / (BrE)
to consider something again, especially with the possibility of changing a previous decision or opinion
consider again, possibly change decision

Examples

After new evidence was presented, the committee agreed to reconsider the proposal.
The manager decided to reconsider her earlier decision after receiving additional feedback.
You should reconsider your stance if there are valid arguments on the other side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - examine or assess again
  • Synonym: rethink - think about again, often with the intent of changing a previous decision
  • Antonym: to decide - make a final choice, especially as an antonym for reconsidering

Etymology

From re- (again) + consider (from Latin considerare, to look at closely).

2. VERB

/ ˌriːkənˈsɪdər / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈsɪdə / (BrE)
to think about something again, especially with the possibility of changing a decision or opinion
consider again, often to change a decision

Examples

After hearing all the facts, the committee agreed to reconsider the proposal.
You should reconsider your stance before making a final decision.
She decided to reconsider after receiving new information.

Synset Examples

Won't you reconsider your decision?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to consider - to think carefully about something

Etymology

From re- (again) + consider; first appeared in English in the early 17th century.

Source: WordNet
