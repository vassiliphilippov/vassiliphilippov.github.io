philologue

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪləˌlɔg / (AmE), / ˈfɪləˌlɒg / (BrE)
A scholar specializing in the study of classical languages and literature, or in philology more broadly.
scholar of classical languages/literature

Examples

The renowned philologue devoted his life to analyzing ancient Greek manuscripts.
As a philologue, she could read texts in Latin, Greek, and Sanskrit with ease.
During the conference, a philologue presented new findings on Homeric poetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philologist - A person who studies language in historical texts (philology)

Etymology

From French 'philologue', from Greek 'philologos' (fond of learning or literature).
