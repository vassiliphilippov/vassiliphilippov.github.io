Panicum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈpænɪkəm / (BrE)
A large genus of grasses, commonly known as panic grass, found in warm regions and cultivated for fodder or as ornamentals.
genus of grasses (panic grass)

Examples

Many farmers use Panicum as animal fodder due to its high nutritional value.
The fields were dominated by different species of Panicum during the rainy season.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Panicum thriving in the wetland reserve.

Etymology

From Latin *panicum* (panic grass, millet), originally from 'panus' (ear of millet).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpænɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈpænɪkəm / (BrE)
a genus of grasses, commonly known as panic grasses, found in warm regions and including several species important as forage or cereal crops
a genus of grass (panic grass)

Examples

Several species within the genus Panicum are cultivated as grain crops in tropical regions.
The botanist identified the grass as belonging to the Panicum genus because of its distinctive seed head.
Farmers often grow Panicum for livestock feed due to its high nutritional value.

Etymology

From Latin panīcum (panic grass, millet).
