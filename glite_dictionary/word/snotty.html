snotty

1. ADJ

/ ˈsnɑti / (AmE), / ˈsnɒti / (BrE)
Dirty or soiled with nasal mucus; covered with or containing nasal discharge.
dirty with nasal mucus

Examples

The child came home with a snotty nose after playing outside.
She handed her snotty tissue to her mother for help.
Please wash your face, it's all snotty from your cold.

Synset Examples

a snotty nose
a house full of snot-nosed kids

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sniveling - crying or sniffing in a way that produces mucus

Etymology

From 'snot' (nasal mucus) + '-y' (forming adjectives).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsnɑti / (AmE), / ˈsnɒti / (BrE)
(informal) behaving as if you are more important or better than other people; conceited or arrogant.
arrogant or conceited (informal)
informal

Examples

She found his snotty attitude unbearable during the meeting.
The snotty receptionist barely acknowledged us when we walked in.
Don't be so snotty just because you got a better grade.

Synset Examples

a snotty little scion of a degenerate family
they're snobs — stuck-up and uppity and persnickety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snobbish - behaving as if you are superior to others; snobbish
  • Antonym: modest - having a humble opinion of oneself; not arrogant

Etymology

Originally from 'snot' (nasal mucus); figuratively extended to mean conceited or arrogant in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01896449-s
Synset POS: s
