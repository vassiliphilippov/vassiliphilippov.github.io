tagasaste

1. NOUN

/ təˈɡɑːsəsteɪ / (AmE), / təˈɡæsəsti / (BrE)
A shrub native to the Canary Islands, with bristle-tipped oblanceolate leaves, often used as cattle fodder.
fodder shrub of Canary Islands

Examples

The farmer planted tagasaste to feed his cattle during the dry season.
Tagasaste thrives in well-drained soils and provides excellent green fodder.
Researchers studied the impact of tagasaste on livestock nutrition in arid regions.

Etymology

From Spanish 'tagasaste', of Guanche origin.
