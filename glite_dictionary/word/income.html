income

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌkʌm / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌkʌm / (BrE)
Money received, especially on a regular basis, for work or through investments.
money received regularly

Examples

Her yearly income has increased steadily due to promotions at work.
Many retirees depend on their savings as their primary source of income.
The family struggled to cover their expenses with only one income.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revenue - total income, especially of a company or government
  • Antonym: expenditure - money spent or paid out

Etymology

From Middle English 'income', meaning 'entrance, arrival', later evolved to mean 'money received'.
