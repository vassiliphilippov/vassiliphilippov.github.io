oligomenorrhea

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɪɡoʊˌmɛnəˈriə / (AmE), / ɒˌlɪɡəʊˌmɛnəˈriːə / (BrE)
A condition characterized by abnormally light or infrequent menstrual periods.
infrequent menstrual periods (medical)
medical

Examples

Women experiencing oligomenorrhea may have fewer than nine menstrual periods per year.
The doctor diagnosed her with oligomenorrhea after reviewing her menstrual history.
Hormonal imbalances are a common cause of oligomenorrhea in adolescents.

Etymology

From Greek oligos (few) + mēn (month) + rhoia (flow).
