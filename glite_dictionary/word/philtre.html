philtre

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪltər / (AmE), / ˈfɪltə / (BrE)
A drink supposed to have magical properties, especially one that causes the person who drinks it to fall in love with someone.
magical love potion

Examples

In the old legend, the witch concocted a powerful philtre to make the prince fall in love with her.
He claimed that the mysterious philtre would ensure undying affection from anyone who drank it.
The fairy tale revolved around a philtre that bound two strangers together in irresistible love.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potion - a liquid with supposed magical properties
  • Synonym: elixir - a magical or medicinal potion

Etymology

From Old French 'philtre', from Latin 'philtrum', from Greek 'philtron' (love charm).
