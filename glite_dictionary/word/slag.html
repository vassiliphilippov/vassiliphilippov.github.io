slag

1. VERB

/ slæɡ / (AmE), / slæɡ / (BrE)
to convert or reduce (an ore, metal, or material) into slag, especially as a result of smelting or refining processes
convert into slag (metallurgy)

Examples

The furnace is designed to slag impurities during the steel-making process.
Improper temperatures can fail to fully slag the metal, leaving contaminants behind.
To purify the ore, the workers slagged it in the blast furnace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smelt - extract (metal) from its ore by a process involving heating and melting

Etymology

From Middle Low German *slagge* (metal dross), related to German *Schlacke*.

2. NOUN

/ slæɡ / (AmE), / slæɡ / (BrE)
a layer of stony waste material that forms on the surface of molten metals during smelting or refining processes
waste material on molten metals

Examples

During the steelmaking process, slag is removed from the surface of the molten metal.
The black mound behind the factory was made up of slag from decades of smelting.
New techniques allow for recycling of slag into construction materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dross - waste material separated from metals during smelting

Etymology

From Middle Low German *slagge*, related to 'strike', referring to matter struck off during smelting.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
