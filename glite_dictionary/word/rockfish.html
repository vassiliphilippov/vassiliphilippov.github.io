rockfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑkˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrɒkˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a marine fish that lives among rocks along the northern coasts of Europe and America, valued for food.
marine food fish living among rocks

Examples

Fishermen often catch rockfish near the rocky shores of Maine.
The restaurant served grilled rockfish with a lemon butter sauce.
Overfishing has led to a decline in rockfish populations in some areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bass - a common edible fish, sometimes called rock bass

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑkˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrɒkˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A marine food and game fish, often having dark longitudinal stripes, that migrates upriver to spawn, sometimes placed in the genus Morone.
a striped marine food and game fish

Examples

Fishermen often seek rockfish for its delicious, flaky meat.
Each spring, the rockfish migrates upriver to spawn.
We spotted several large rockfish swimming near the reef.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped bass - a large, predatory, striped game fish also called a rockfish

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɑkˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrɒkˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
the lean, edible flesh of any of various market fish that are caught among rocks, valued for food
edible flesh of certain fish caught among rocks

Examples

The chef recommended grilled rockfish for its delicate flavor and firm texture.
We bought a pound of fresh rockfish at the seafood market for dinner.
Rockfish is a popular choice in many coastal restaurants due to its lean flesh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapper - a popular market fish whose flesh is eaten

Etymology

From 'rock' + 'fish', indicating fish that live among rocks.
