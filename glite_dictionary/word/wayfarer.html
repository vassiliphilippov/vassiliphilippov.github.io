wayfarer

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪˌfɛrər / (AmE), / ˈweɪfə.rə / (BrE)
a person who travels on foot from place to place, especially over long distances
person who travels on foot

Examples

The old inn provided shelter to every wayfarer passing through the village.
Dressed in worn boots and carrying a satchel, the wayfarer continued along the dusty road.
Legends tell of a mysterious wayfarer who helped those in need during his travels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traveler - a person who travels or journeys
  • Synonym: pedestrian - a person walking, especially in a town or city

Etymology

From Middle English weyfarer, from way + fare (to travel).

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪˌfɛrər / (AmE), / ˈweɪfəərə / (BrE)
a person who travels from place to place, especially on foot
person who travels, especially on foot

Examples

During his journeys, the wayfarer often relied on the kindness of strangers for shelter.
Every wayfarer has a story to tell about the roads they have traveled.
The old inn provided a warm fire and a meal for any wayfarer passing through the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traveler - a person who is traveling or who often travels

Etymology

From Middle English, from way (road, path) + fare (to travel).
