vermilion

1. ADJ

/ vərˈmɪl.jən / (AmE), / vəˈmɪl.i.ən / (BrE)
of a vivid red or reddish-orange color
vivid red or reddish-orange (adj.)

Examples

The artist chose a vermilion paint for the sunset in her landscape.
A vermilion scarf added a bold splash of color to his outfit.
Her lips were painted a striking vermilion that caught everyone's attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarlet - bright red color with a slightly orange tinge
  • Degree Lower: crimson - rich deep red color inclining to purple

Etymology

From Latin 'vermiculus' (little worm), referring to a dye produced from insects.

2. NOUN

/ vərˈmɪliən / (AmE), / vəˈmɪliən / (BrE)
A bright red or scarlet color, sometimes tinged with orange.
vivid red or orange-red color

Examples

The artist used a striking shade of vermilion to highlight the sunset in her painting.
She wore a vermilion dress that captivated everyone's attention at the party.
Traditional Chinese temples are often painted in vibrant vermilion hues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarlet - a brilliant red color with a slight orange tinge
  • Degree Lower: crimson - a deep red color inclining to purple

Etymology

From Latin 'vermiculus' (little worm), referring to the insect used to produce the original pigment.
