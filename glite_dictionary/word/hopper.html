hopper

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːpər / (AmE), / ˈhɒpə / (BrE)
(Baseball) A hit that travels along the ground, usually bouncing one or more times before being fielded.
baseball hit that bounces along the ground

Examples

The shortstop fielded the hopper and threw to first base for the out.
He hit a slow hopper to third that gave the runner time to reach home.
The batter managed only a weak hopper that barely left the infield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grounder - a baseball hit that travels along the ground

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːpər / (AmE), / ˈhɒpə / (BrE)
a funnel-shaped container that receives and dispenses materials, allowing them to pass by gravity into another receptacle below
funnel-shaped container for dispensing materials

Examples

The grain poured from the hopper into a waiting truck below.
Please fill the hopper with coffee beans before brewing.
The machine jams if the hopper becomes overloaded with material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chute - a sloping channel or slide for conveying things to a lower level

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *hoppen* (to leap), referencing an action or device for jumping or dropping, later applied to containers that drop contents.

5. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːpər / (AmE), / ˈhɒpə / (BrE)
a small plant-eating insect, especially in its immature stage, with long hind legs adapted for jumping
plant-eating insect that jumps

Examples

The field was filled with hoppers leaping from blade to blade.
A tiny green hopper landed on my arm while I sat in the garden.
Birds often feed on hoppers during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grasshopper - a plant-eating insect with long hind legs for jumping

Etymology
