veiled

1. ADJ

/ veɪld / (AmE), / veɪld / (BrE)
partially hidden, covered, or not expressed openly; concealed or disguised
concealed or covered; not openly shown

Examples

The mayor issued a veiled threat to those who opposed his plans.
Her meaning was clear despite her veiled hints.
The painting showed a veiled woman sitting by the river.

Synset Examples

a veiled dancer
a veiled hat
veiled threats
veiled insults

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overt - done or shown openly; not concealed
  • Antonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail, leaving no room for confusion or doubt
  • Synonym: covert - not openly acknowledged or displayed; secret

Etymology

From 'veil', from Old French 'veil', from Latin 'velum' (covering, curtain), plus suffix '-ed'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈveɪld / (AmE), / ˈveɪld / (BrE)
not expressed or presented clearly; muted, blurred, or partially concealed
muted or unclear; partially concealed

Examples

The mountains were shrouded in a veiled mist that softened their outlines.
Her words carried a veiled threat that made everyone uneasy.
Distant, veiled music could be heard through the thick walls.

Synset Examples

veiled sounds
the image is veiled or foggy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clear - easily perceived or understood; not veiled or obscure
