Donatist

1. ADJ

/ ˈdoʊnətɪst / (AmE), / ˈdɒnətɪst / (BrE)
of or relating to Donatism, a Christian sect in North Africa in the 4th and 5th centuries known for its strict views on church purity and sacraments
relating to Donatism (Christian sect)

Examples

The council addressed disputes between the mainstream church and the Donatist community.
Scholars have long debated the impact of Donatist beliefs on early Christianity.
Only members of the Donatist sect were admitted to this particular ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Donatism - the doctrines and practices of the Donatists, a Christian sect

Etymology

From Donatus, a 4th-century Berber bishop, leader of the movement + -ist.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊ.nə.tɪst / (AmE), / ˈdɒn.ə.tɪst / (BrE)
A member of a Christian sect in North Africa that broke away from the main church over issues of the validity of sacraments administered by priests and bishops who had lapsed during persecution, active from the 4th to 6th centuries CE.
member of an early Christian sect in North Africa

Examples

Augustine wrote extensively against the Donatist position in his theological writings.
The Donatists insisted that the church must be composed only of saints, not sinners.
Many conflicts between Catholics and Donatists occurred in Roman North Africa.

Etymology

From Latin Donatista, named after Donatus, a 4th-century bishop and leader of the movement.
