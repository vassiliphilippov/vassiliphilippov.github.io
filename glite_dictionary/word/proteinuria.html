proteinuria

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtiːnˈjʊriə / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtiːnˈjʊəriə / (BrE)
the presence of abnormal amounts of protein, especially albumin, in the urine, typically as a sign of kidney disease
excess protein in the urine (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered tests to determine the cause of proteinuria in the patient.
Persistent proteinuria may indicate chronic kidney damage and needs further investigation.
Mild proteinuria can sometimes occur after intense exercise or with fever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albuminuria - the presence of albumin in the urine
  • Degree Lower: hematuria - the presence of blood in the urine

Etymology

From protein + Greek ouron (urine) + -ia (condition).
