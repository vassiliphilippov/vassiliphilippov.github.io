impregnation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪmˌpreɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmˌpreɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which male and female gametes unite to initiate the development of a new organism, such as fertilization of an egg in animals or of an ovule in plants
process of fertilization; union of gametes
medical

Examples

The impregnation of the egg marks the beginning of a new life cycle.
Successful impregnation in flowering plants often depends on the activity of pollinators.
Some infertility treatments assist with the impregnation process in humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertilization - the process of a sperm cell uniting with an ovum
  • Synonym: conception - the process of becoming pregnant by fertilization

Etymology

From Latin 'impregnatio', from 'impregnare' (to fertilize).
