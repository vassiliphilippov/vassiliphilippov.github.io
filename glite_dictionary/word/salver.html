salver

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæl.vər / (AmE), / ˈsæv.ə / (BrE)
a flat tray, typically made of silver, used for serving food or drinks
large tray for serving, often silver

Examples

The waiter carried the drinks into the room on a polished salver.
An ornate salver was placed in the center of the table, holding fresh fruit.
She presented the letter on a small silver salver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tray - a flat, shallow container for carrying or holding items

Etymology

From French salve (presentation tray), from Spanish salva, of uncertain origin.
