to sellotape

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛləˌteɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.ə.teɪp / (BrE)
to fasten or attach something with Sellotape (transparent adhesive tape)
attach with adhesive tape
british informal

Examples

She sellotaped the scraps of paper together to stop them scattering.
Please sellotape the label onto the box before sending it.
He sellotapes his timetable to the fridge so everyone can see it.

Synset Examples

The hotel manager scotch taped a note to the guest's door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tape - to fasten or attach with tape
  • Antonym: unstick - to detach or remove something that is stuck

Etymology

From Sellotape, a trademarked British brand of adhesive tape, first marketed in the 1930s.
