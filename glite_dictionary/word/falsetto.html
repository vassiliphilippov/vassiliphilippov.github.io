falsetto

1. NOUN

/ fɔlˈsɛtoʊ / (AmE), / fɔːlˈsɛtəʊ / (BrE)
a method of voice production used by male singers, which allows them to sing notes higher than their normal range in an artificial register
artificially high male singing voice

Examples

He surprised the audience by hitting the high notes in falsetto.
Many pop singers use falsetto to add emotion to their songs.
The lead singer switched to falsetto for the chorus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: head voice - the higher register of the singing voice, distinct from chest voice and falsetto

Etymology

From Italian 'falsetto', diminutive of 'falso' (false), from Latin 'falsus'.

2. ADJ

/ fɔlˈsɛtoʊ / (AmE), / fɔːlˈsɛtəʊ / (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) artificially high and above the normal vocal range, especially in singing.
artificially high (voice or sound)

Examples

He spoke in a falsetto voice during the performance, which made everyone laugh.
The singer hit a remarkable falsetto note during the chorus of the song.
Her laughter suddenly turned into a high falsetto that echoed across the room.

Synset Examples

a falsetto voice

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tenor - the highest adult male singing voice
  • Degree Higher: soprano - the highest singing voice for females or boys

Etymology

From Italian 'falsetto' (diminutive of 'falso', meaning 'false').
