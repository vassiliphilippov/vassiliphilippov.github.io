japan

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpæn / (AmE), / dʒəˈpan / (BrE)
Lacquerware decorated and finished in the Japanese style, typically with a hard, glossy black varnish.
Japanese-style lacquerware

Examples

The antique japan on display gleamed with an unforgettable dark luster.
Collectors are always seeking rare pieces of japan from the 18th century.
Her grandmother owned a chest decorated in traditional japan style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacquerware - Objects coated with lacquer, especially decorative items

Etymology

From Japan, the country, whose lacquer technique inspired the name.
