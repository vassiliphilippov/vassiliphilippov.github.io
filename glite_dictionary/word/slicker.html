slicker

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪkər / (AmE), / ˈslɪkə / (BrE)
a raincoat, especially one made of cotton fabric treated with oil or pigment to make it waterproof.
waterproof raincoat, often treated cotton

Examples

She wore her yellow slicker as the storm rolled in.
The fisherman pulled on his old slicker before heading out to sea.
Children splashed through puddles in bright slickers after the downpour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raincoat - a waterproof coat worn to protect against rain

2. NOUN

/ ˈslɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈslɪkə / (BrE)
a person who is smooth, suave, and stylish, especially in behavior and dress
stylish, suave person

Examples

The party was full of slickers who seemed to care more about style than substance.
Everyone knew him as the office slicker, always impeccably dressed and polite.
She mistrusted the new manager, thinking he was just another slicker with a charming smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandy - a man who places particular importance upon physical appearance and style, often in a showy way
  • Synonym: fop - a man overly concerned with his appearance and clothes
  • Antonym: boor - a rude or unmannerly person

Etymology

Formed from 'slick' (smooth, glossy) + '-er' (agent noun suffix), referring to one who is slick in manners or appearance.
