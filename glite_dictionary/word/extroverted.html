extroverted

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛkstroʊˈvɜrtɪd / (AmE), / ˌɛkstrəʊˈvɜːtɪd / (BrE)
outgoing and socially confident; concerned more with external things or social interaction than with inner thoughts or feelings
outgoing, socially confident

Examples

She has always been extroverted, easily making friends wherever she goes.
The company values extroverted employees who can comfortably engage with clients.
Although he's extroverted at parties, he still enjoys quiet time to himself.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: introverted - shy and preferring solitude or small groups

Etymology

From 'extrovert' + '-ed'; 'extrovert' from Latin 'extra' (outside) + 'vertere' (to turn).

3. ADJ

/ ˈɛkstrəˌvɜrtɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛkstrəʊˌvɜːtɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) outgoing and socially confident; at ease in talking to others.
outgoing and socially confident

Examples

Despite being new at work, Jessica was so extroverted that she quickly made friends with everyone.
His extroverted nature made parties much more enjoyable for everyone around him.
People often assume that actors are naturally extroverted, but that's not always the case.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: introverted - shy and reserved; tending to turn inward socially
  • Synonym: outgoing - friendly and socially confident

Etymology

From 'extrovert' (from Latin 'extra' meaning 'outside' + 'vertere' meaning 'to turn') plus the suffix '-ed'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02266005-s
Synset POS: s
