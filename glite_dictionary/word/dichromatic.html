dichromatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪ.kroʊˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.krəʊˈmæt.ɪk / (BrE)
Having or exhibiting two colors; relating to or characterized by dichromatism, especially in vision or pigmentation.
having or showing two colors

Examples

Certain animals have dichromatic vision, meaning they can only perceive two primary colors.
The bird's feathers display a striking dichromatic pattern of blue and yellow.
Researchers studied the effects of dichromatic pigments on plant growth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: trichromatic - having or involving three colors, especially in vision

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) + 'chrōma' (color) + '-ic'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪ.kroʊˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.krəʊˈmæt.ɪk / (BrE)
Having or showing two colors.
having two colors

Examples

The artist created a dichromatic painting using only shades of blue and yellow.
Some species of birds display dichromatic plumage during the mating season.
We chose dichromatic curtains to match the two-tone theme of the room.

Synset Examples

a bicolor flower
a bicolored postage stamp

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monochromatic - consisting of one color
  • Degree Higher: trichromatic - having or using three colors
  • Synonym: bicolor - having two different colors

Etymology

From Greek di- (two) + khrōmatikos (of color).
