neuroleptic

1. NOUN

/ nʊˌroʊˈlɛptɪk / (AmE), / njʊəˌrəʊˈlɛptɪk / (BrE)
a drug that reduces nervous tension and is used mainly to treat psychotic disorders such as schizophrenia
drug for treating psychotic disorders
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with schizophrenia, he was prescribed a neuroleptic to help manage his symptoms.
Some neuroleptic medications can cause drowsiness or other side effects.
The doctor decided to change his neuroleptic because the previous one was not effective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antipsychotic - drug used to treat psychotic conditions

Etymology

From Greek neuron (nerve) + lepsis (a taking hold of).
