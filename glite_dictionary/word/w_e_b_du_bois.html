W. E. B. Du Bois

1. PROPN

/ ˌdjuː ˈbɔɪs / (AmE), / ˌdjuː ˈbɔɪs / (BrE)
(1868–1963) United States civil rights leader, scholar, and political activist who campaigned for equality for Black Americans.
American civil rights leader and activist
american

Examples

W. E. B. Du Bois was a founding member of the NAACP and a prominent advocate for African American rights.
Many historians regard W. E. B. Du Bois as a pioneering scholar in sociology and civil rights.
The writings of W. E. B. Du Bois continue to inspire movements for social justice today.
