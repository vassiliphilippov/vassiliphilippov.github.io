Vitus Bering

1. PROPN

/ ˈvaɪtəs ˈbɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈviːtəs ˈbɛərɪŋ / (BrE)
(1681–1741) A Danish explorer who sailed for Russia, explored the northern Pacific Ocean, and discovered the Bering Strait.
Danish explorer; discovered Bering Strait

Examples

Vitus Bering led two major expeditions to explore the waters north of Russia.
The Bering Strait, named after Vitus Bering, separates Asia and North America.
Many historians credit Vitus Bering with opening up the Russian Far East to European exploration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: navigator - A person who explores or directs a ship's course
