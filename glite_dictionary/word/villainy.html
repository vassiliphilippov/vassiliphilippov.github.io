villainy

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪləni / (AmE), / ˈvɪləni / (BrE)
Wicked or criminal behavior; the quality of being morally wrong or evil.
wicked or criminal behavior

Examples

The story is filled with villainy and betrayal around every corner.
Her unrivaled villainy made her the most feared character in the series.
Despite his charm, he was capable of shocking acts of villainy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wickedness - the quality of being morally evil or wrong
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Old French *villenie*, from *vilain* (peasant, villain).
