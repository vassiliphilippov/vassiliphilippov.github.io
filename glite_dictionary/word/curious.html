curious

1. ADJ

/ ˈkjʊriəs / (AmE), / ˈkjʊəriəs / (BrE)
eager to know or learn something; showing a desire to investigate or find out more, especially about things that do not directly concern oneself
eager to know or learn; inquisitive

Examples

The curious toddler kept asking questions about everything she saw in the park.
He grew curious about his family's history and decided to do some research.
Many people stopped to take a curious look at the new sculpture in the town square.

Synset Examples

a curious child is a teacher's delight
a trap door that made me curious
curious investigators
traffic was slowed by curious rubberneckers
curious about the neighbor's doings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inquisitive - showing curiosity; eager to know or ask questions
  • Antonym: indifferent - lacking interest or concern

Etymology

From Latin 'curiosus' (careful, inquisitive), from 'cura' (care).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkjʊriəs / (AmE), / ˈkjʊəriəs / (BrE)
strange or unusual; beyond what is normal or expected.
strange; unusual

Examples

Her new painting has a curious texture that draws attention.
There's something curious about the way he always speaks in riddles.
A curious silence settled over the city after the event.

Synset Examples

a curious hybrid accent
her speech has a funny twang
they have some funny ideas about war
had an odd name
the peculiar aromatic odor of cloves
something definitely queer about this town
what a rum fellow
singular behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: odd - strange or unusual, different from what is normal
  • Synonym: peculiar - different to what is normal or expected; strange
  • Antonym: ordinary - with nothing unusual; normal

Etymology

From Latin 'curiosus' (careful, inquisitive), but this sense developed as 'strange, odd' via use in English by extension.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00971199-s
Synset POS: s
