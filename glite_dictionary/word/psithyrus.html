Psithyrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪθɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪθɪrəs / (BrE)
a genus of large bees, closely related to bumblebees, which lack pollen-collecting structures and a worker caste, and are parasitic on other bees
a parasitic genus of large bees

Examples

Psithyrus species invade bumblebee nests to lay their eggs and do not have a worker caste.
Unlike true bumblebees, Psithyrus bees lack the structures needed to collect pollen.
The life cycle of Psithyrus relies entirely on exploiting the resources of other bumblebee colonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bumblebee - a large, hairy bee of the genus Bombus that collects pollen and lives in colonies with workers

Etymology

From New Latin Psithyrus, former genus name, now classified as a subgenus of Bombus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪθɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪθɪrəs / (BrE)
A genus of large bees that resemble bumblebees but lack pollen-collecting structures and do not have a worker caste.
genus of bees resembling bumblebees

Examples

The Psithyrus bee infiltrates the nests of other bumblebees to lay its eggs.
Researchers observed that Psithyrus species do not collect pollen themselves.
Unlike true bumblebees, Psithyrus lacks a worker caste and relies on hosts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bumblebee - large, hairy bee known for its buzzing sound

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name used in taxonomy.
