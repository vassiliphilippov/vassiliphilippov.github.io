unsymmetrical

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnsɪˈmɛtrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnsɪˈmɛtrɪkəl / (BrE)
having parts that are not identical on both sides of a central line; lacking symmetry.
not symmetrical; lacking symmetry

Examples

The architect designed an unsymmetrical building to create a more dynamic skyline.
Her painting features an unsymmetrical arrangement of shapes and colors.
The table legs were unsymmetrical, causing it to wobble on the floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asymmetrical - not symmetrical; lacking symmetry
  • Antonym: symmetrical - having symmetry; identical on both sides

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' (not) to 'symmetrical', from Greek 'symmetria' (agreement in dimensions, proportion).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.sɪˈmɛtrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.sɪˈmɛtrɪk(ə)l / (BrE)
not having symmetry; lacking balance or evenness in form, design, or arrangement.
not having symmetry

Examples

The artist intentionally created an unsymmetrical pattern to challenge traditional design concepts.
Her face was noticeably unsymmetrical, with one eye higher than the other.
The solution resulted in an unsymmetrical molecule, which affected its chemical properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asymmetrical - not symmetrical; lacking symmetry
  • Antonym: symmetrical - having symmetry; same shape and size on both sides of a dividing line

Etymology

From un- (not) + symmetrical, from Greek summetria (measured together).

Source: WordNet
