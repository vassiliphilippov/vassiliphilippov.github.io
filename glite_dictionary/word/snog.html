to snog

1. VERB

/ snɑg / (AmE), / snɒg / (BrE)
to kiss and cuddle passionately, especially in a romantic or sexual way
kiss and cuddle passionately
informal british

Examples

They were caught snogging behind the school gym after class.
I can't believe you snogged your crush at the party last night!
The couple spent the evening snogging on the sofa, oblivious to everyone else.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

Probably of British origin, first recorded in early 20th century; origin unknown.
