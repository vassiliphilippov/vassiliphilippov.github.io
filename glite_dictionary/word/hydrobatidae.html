Hydrobatidae

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdroʊˈbætɪˌdi / (AmE), / haɪˌdrəʊˈbætɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small seabirds known as storm petrels, found mostly over oceans and characterized by their fluttering flight close to the water's surface.
family of seabirds: storm petrels

Examples

The family Hydrobatidae includes all species of storm petrels found worldwide.
Members of the Hydrobatidae are noted for their distinctive flight pattern just above ocean waves.
Researchers studied the breeding behavior of Hydrobatidae off the coast of New Zealand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm petrel - small seabird of the Hydrobatidae family, found mostly at sea

Etymology

From New Latin Hydrobatidae, from Greek hydōr (water) + bates (one that treads); referring to birds that appear to walk on water.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈbætɪdiˌi / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəʊˈbatɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small seabirds known as storm petrels, found mainly in open ocean environments.
family of storm petrels (seabirds)

Examples

The Hydrobatidae are known for their graceful flight over the open seas.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of Hydrobatidae along the Atlantic coast.
Several species within Hydrobatidae nest on isolated oceanic islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm petrel - a small seabird in the family Hydrobatidae

Etymology

From New Latin Hydrobatidae, based on Greek 'hydor' (water) and 'bates' (one who treads).
