indefinability

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dɪˌfaɪ.nəˈbɪl.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˌfaɪ.nəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible to define or describe precisely.
quality of being impossible to define

Examples

The indefinability of beauty makes it challenging to establish universal standards for art.
Philosophers have long debated the indefinability of concepts such as truth or justice.
Her music has an air of indefinability, blending several genres in unpredictable ways.

Synset Examples

It discusses the indefinability of death in terms of a not entirely clear gloss that death is neither definable through equality nor through inequality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indefinable - impossible to define or describe exactly
  • Antonym: definability - quality of being able to be defined

Etymology

From 'indefinable' + '-ity', following the pattern of Latin-derived nouns of quality.
