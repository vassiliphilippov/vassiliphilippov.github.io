ataractic

1. NOUN

/ ˌætəˈræktɪk / (AmE), / ˌatəˈraktɪk / (BrE)
A drug used to reduce anxiety, stress, or tension without impairing mental clarity.
anti-anxiety drug with no mental dulling
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an ataractic to help manage her anxiety symptoms.
After taking the ataractic, he felt noticeably calmer but remained alert and focused.
Some patients prefer an ataractic because it eases tension without making them drowsy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tranquilizer - a drug used to reduce anxiety or tension
  • Synonym: anxiolytic - a drug that relieves anxiety

Etymology

From Greek *ataraktos* (not disturbed).
