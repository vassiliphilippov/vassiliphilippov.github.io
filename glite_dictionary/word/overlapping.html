overlapping

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈlæpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈlæpɪŋ / (BrE)
the action or result of one thing covering a part of another, especially in a pattern where elements partially cover each other, as with tiles or shingles
partial covering of one thing by another

Examples

The overlapping of the tiles prevents rain from entering the house.
She admired the overlapping patterns in the mosaic floor.
Good roofing relies on proper overlapping to ensure water runs off effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlap - to partly cover the edge or surface of something
