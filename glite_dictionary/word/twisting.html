twisting

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtwɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of distorting or misrepresenting something so that it appears to mean something unintended
distorting or misrepresenting meaning

Examples

The lawyer accused the reporter of twisting his words to create a misleading headline.
Her twisting of the facts made it hard to know what really happened.
Political debates often involve the twisting of opponents' statements to gain an advantage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distortion - the act of twisting or altering something out of its true, natural, or original state
  • Synonym: misrepresentation - the action of giving a false or misleading account of something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00752872-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈtwɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtwɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of rotating or turning something rapidly around an axis
rapid rotational movement

Examples

The twisting of the rope caused it to fray and eventually snap.
After much twisting of the lid, the jar finally opened.
The mechanic checked the engine's crankshaft for any unusual twisting during operation.

Synset Examples

he gave the crank a spin
it broke off after much twisting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotation - the action of turning around an axis
  • Synonym: spinning - the act of turning around rapidly

Etymology

From the verb 'twist', of Middle English origin, probably from Old Norse 'tvísta' (to twist).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00344075-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈtwɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtwɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
having many turns, bends, or curves; winding or tortuous.
full of turns or bends

Examples

The twisting path through the forest challenged even the most experienced hikers.
Drivers must be cautious on the twisting roads along the cliffside.
A twisting river ran through the valley, creating scenic views at every turn.

Synset Examples

a tortuous road up the mountain
winding roads are full of surprises
had to steer the car down a twisty track

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tortuous - having many twists and turns; winding
  • Synonym: winding - repeatedly turning or bending
  • Antonym: straight - without bends or curves

Etymology

From the verb 'twist' + -ing, used as an adjective.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
