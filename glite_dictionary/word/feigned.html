feigned

1. ADJ

/ feɪnd / (AmE), / feɪnd / (BrE)
Pretended; not genuine; insincerely made to appear true or real.
pretended; insincerely made to appear real

Examples

Her feigned interest in the conversation was obvious to everyone.
He offered a feigned apology, hoping to avoid further trouble.
The child gave a feigned yawn to convince his mother he was tired.

Synset Examples

feigned sympathy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: genuine - Actually being what something is said to be; real, not false or pretended
  • Synonym: fake - Not real; made to look like something else in order to deceive

Etymology

From Old French 'feindre' (to pretend, feign), from Latin 'fingere' (to shape, invent, feign).
