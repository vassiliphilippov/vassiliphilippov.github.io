floss

1. NOUN

/ flɑs / (AmE), / flɒs / (BrE)
a soft, loosely twisted thread used especially in embroidery and similar needlework.
soft thread used in embroidery

Examples

She carefully selected a blue floss to embroider the delicate petals.
The pattern requires three different colors of floss for the flowers and leaves.
He bought a set of silk floss for his cross-stitch project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thread - thin strand of fiber used in sewing

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'floccus' (tuft of wool).

2. VERB

/ flɑs / (AmE), / flɒs / (BrE)
to clean between one's teeth using dental floss
clean teeth using dental floss

Examples

It's important to floss every day to prevent gum disease.
She always reminds her kids to floss after brushing their teeth.
He flossed carefully to remove food stuck between his teeth.

Synset Examples

floss your teeth after every meal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush - to clean teeth with a toothbrush

3. NOUN

/ flɑs / (AmE), / flɒs / (BrE)
A soft thread used to clean between the teeth as part of dental hygiene.
thread for cleaning between teeth

Examples

The dentist recommended using floss daily to prevent cavities.
She reached for the floss after finishing her meal.
It's important to choose a good quality floss for sensitive gums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dental floss - thread for cleaning between teeth
