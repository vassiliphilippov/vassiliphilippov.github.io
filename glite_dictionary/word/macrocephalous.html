macrocephalous

1. ADJ

/ ˌmækrəˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
Having an abnormally large head and brain.
having an abnormally large head
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the infant was macrocephalous and would need further tests.
Some plants in the family are classified as macrocephalous due to their unusually large flower heads.
The syndrome is characterized by macrocephalous features and delayed physical development.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: microcephalous - having an abnormally small head

Etymology

From Greek makro- (large) + kephalē (head) + -ous (adjective suffix).
