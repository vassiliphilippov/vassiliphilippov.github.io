to skulk

1. VERB

/ skʌlk / (AmE), / skʌlk / (BrE)
to avoid responsibilities or duties, especially by moving stealthily or by pretending to be ill or absent
avoid duties or responsibilities stealthily

Examples

He tried to skulk out of his chores by pretending to have a headache.
Several employees attempted to skulk away before the meeting started.
You can't just skulk around the office hoping no one will assign you work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shirk - to avoid or neglect a duty or responsibility
  • Synonym: to malinger - to pretend to be ill to avoid work or duty
  • Antonym: to confront - to face and deal with responsibilities directly

Etymology

From Middle English skulken, of Scandinavian origin; related to Danish skulke (to lie in hiding).

2. VERB

/ skʌlk / (AmE), / skʌlk / (BrE)
to move stealthily or keep out of sight, usually with a sinister or cowardly motive
move stealthily or lurk

Examples

The thief tried to skulk in the shadows, hoping no one would notice him.
She watched a cat skulk around the garden, ready to pounce on a bird.
He was seen skulking near the back entrance late at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lurk - to remain hidden so as to wait in ambush

Etymology

From Middle English 'skulken', probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse 'skulka' (to lurk).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02646137-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ skʌlk / (AmE), / skʌlk / (BrE)
to move stealthily or furtively, often with a sense of guilt or avoidance
move furtively or stealthily

Examples

The cat skulked behind the sofa, waiting for the right moment to pounce.
Late at night, he skulked through the alleyways to avoid being seen.
She was caught skulking around the office when everyone else had left.

Synset Examples

The lonely man skulks down the main street all day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sneak - to move in a stealthy or secretive way
  • Synonym: to lurk - to remain hidden so as to wait in ambush

Etymology

Probably from Middle English skulken, of Scandinavian origin; compare Danish skulke (to shirk, malinger).
