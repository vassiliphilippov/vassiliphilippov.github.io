point-blank

1. ADJ

/ pɔɪnt ˈblæŋk / (AmE), / pɔɪnt ˈblaŋk / (BrE)
(of a shot, weapon, or range) very close to the target, so that little or no aiming adjustment is needed.
very close to the target (range)

Examples

The marksman fired a point-blank shot at the target.
He was standing at point-blank range when the gun was discharged.
The robber threatened them with a point-blank weapon.

Synset Examples

point-blank range
a point-blank shot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: close-range - near to the target or object

Etymology

From French 'point blanc,' referring to a white spot as an aiming mark.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɔɪnt ˈblæŋk / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪnt ˈblæŋk / (BrE)
(of speech or manner) direct and straightforward; without attempt to soften or hide the truth.
direct and blunt in speech or manner

Examples

She asked him a point-blank question about his intentions.
I appreciate your point-blank honesty, even when the truth is hard to hear.
His point-blank refusal to cooperate surprised everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

blunt talking and straight shooting
a blunt New England farmer
I gave them my candid opinion
forthright criticism
a forthright approach to the problem
tell me what you think — and you may just as well be frank
it is possible to be outspoken without being rude
plainspoken and to the point
a point-blank accusation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunt - direct in speech or manner, often without concern for politeness
  • Synonym: forthright - (of a person or their manner or speech) direct and outspoken
  • Antonym: subtle - not obvious, indirect or delicate in approach

Etymology

Originally from shooting terminology, from 'point' + 'blank' (white spot in a target); later extended figuratively to speech.
