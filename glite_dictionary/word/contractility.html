contractility

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.trækˈtɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.trækˈtɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the ability or quality of muscle fibers or other living tissue to contract or shrink
ability to contract (muscles, tissue)
medical

Examples

The contractility of the heart muscle is vital for effective blood circulation.
Certain medications can enhance the contractility of smooth muscle tissues.
A reduction in contractility may indicate underlying heart disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contraction - the process of becoming smaller or shorter

Etymology

From Latin contrahere (to draw together) and the suffix -ility (quality or state).
