autochthonal

1. ADJ

/ ɔːˈtɑːkθənəl / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒkθənəl / (BrE)
Originating in the place where found; indigenous or native to a particular area.
native or indigenous to an area

Examples

The autochthonal traditions of the island remain vibrant despite outside influences.
Geologists studied autochthonal rock formations that had not been transported by erosion.
The autochthonal flora provides evidence of the region's unique evolutionary history.

Synset Examples

the autochthonal fauna of Australia includes the kangaroo
autochthonous rocks and people and folktales
endemic folkways
the Ainu are indigenous to the northernmost islands of Japan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autochthonous - Native or formed in the place where found; indigenous.
  • Synonym: indigenous - Originating or occurring naturally in a particular place; native.
  • Antonym: allochthonous - Originating in a place other than where it is found; not native.

Etymology

From Greek 'autokhthōn' (sprung from the land itself), via Latin 'autochthōnes'.
