zoning

1. NOUN

/ ˈzoʊ.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈzəʊ.nɪŋ / (BrE)
the system or process of dividing an area, especially a city, into zones for particular purposes such as residence, business, or manufacturing
system of dividing an area into zones for use

Examples

The city council held a public meeting to discuss new zoning regulations for the downtown area.
Strict zoning laws prevent factories from being built near residential neighborhoods.
The developer applied for a zoning change in order to construct a shopping center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rezoning - changing existing zoning classifications for a section
  • Synonym: land use - manner in which land is used or designated

Etymology

From zone + -ing, first used in the context of urban planning in the early 20th century.
