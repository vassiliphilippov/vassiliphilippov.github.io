Salvelinus

1. NOUN

/ sæl.vəˈlaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / sal.vɛˈliː.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the salmon family that includes the chars and brook trout.
genus of freshwater fish (chars, brook trout)

Examples

The Salvelinus genus includes both the brook trout and the Arctic char.
Researchers studied several species within the Salvelinus group to assess water quality.
Anglers prize Salvelinus for their beauty and sporting challenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brook trout - a common North American freshwater fish, member of the Salvelinus genus
  • Synonym: char - any of several species in the Salvelinus genus, cold-water fish

Etymology

From scientific Latin Salvelinus, adopted as a genus name in zoological classification.
