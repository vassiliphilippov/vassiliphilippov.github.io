Hermann Goering

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛrmən ˈɡøːrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛːmæn ˈɡɜːrɪŋ / (BrE)
(1893–1946) German political and military leader in Nazi Germany, founder of the Gestapo, and a key figure in mobilizing Germany for war.
Nazi German leader (1893–1946); founded Gestapo

Examples

Hermann Goering was one of Adolf Hitler's closest associates during the Third Reich.
The Nuremberg Trials convicted Hermann Goering for his role in war crimes.
Historians often debate the extent of Hermann Goering's influence over Nazi Germany's military strategy.
