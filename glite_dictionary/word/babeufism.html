Babeufism

1. NOUN

/ bəˈbœfɪzəm / (AmE), / bəˈbɜːfɪzəm / (BrE)
A form of collectivist political theory advocating communal ownership and equality, as proposed by François-Noël Babeuf.
collectivist doctrine by François-Noël Babeuf

Examples

Her writing explores the principles of Babeufism and its influence on later socialist movements.
Debates about Babeufism often arise in discussions of revolutionary France.
The historian argued that Babeufism was a precursor to modern communism.

Synset Examples

But Herzen's attack on its advocacy of terror that frees by ‘despotism’, on its Babeufism, was quite explicit.

Etymology

From the name of François-Noël Babeuf (1760–1797), French political agitator, with English suffix -ism.
