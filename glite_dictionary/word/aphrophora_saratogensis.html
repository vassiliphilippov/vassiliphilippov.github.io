Aphrophora saratogensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfrəˈfoʊrə səˌrætəˈdʒɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌæfrəˈfɔːrə səˌrætəˈdʒɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of spittlebug that feeds on pine trees in the northern United States.
species of spittlebug feeding on pines

Examples

The larvae of Aphrophora saratogensis create frothy masses on pine needles during the summer.
Researchers observed that Aphrophora saratogensis was common in the coniferous forests of the region.
Damage caused by Aphrophora saratogensis can affect the growth of young pine trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spittlebug - an insect whose nymphs produce frothy secretions on plants
