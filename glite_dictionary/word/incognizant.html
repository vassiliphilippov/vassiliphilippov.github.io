incognizant

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkɑɡnɪzənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒɡnɪzənt / (BrE)
not aware of something; lacking knowledge or awareness (often followed by 'of')
not aware; lacking awareness

Examples

She walked through the noisy construction site, seemingly incognizant of the danger around her.
Many people remain incognizant of how their actions affect the environment.
He was completely incognizant of the impression he made on others.

Synset Examples

seemed unaware of the scrutiny
unaware of the danger they were in
unaware of the newborn hope
the most unaware person I've known

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unaware - not knowing or realizing something
  • Antonym: aware - having knowledge or perception of a situation or fact

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'in-' (not) + 'cognizant', from Latin 'cognoscere' (to know).
