assignation

1. NOUN

/ əˌsaɪ.nəˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / əˌsɪɡ.nəˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a secret meeting, especially one arranged between lovers
secret meeting between lovers

Examples

Rumors spread quickly after their assignation was discovered by a nosy neighbor.
The lovers arranged a secret assignation at midnight near the old bridge.
Their assignation was brief, but it would be remembered for a lifetime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rendezvous - a meeting at an agreed time and place, especially one that is secret or private

Etymology

From Latin assignatio (an appointment), via French assignation.

2. NOUN

/ əˌsaɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌsaɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of distributing something by allotting or apportioning, often according to a plan or system
the act of distributing or allotting

Examples

The assignation of responsibilities ensured that every team member had a clear role.
After a complex assignation of seats, each delegate knew where to sit.
Budget assignation is carried out by an independent committee each year.

Synset Examples

the apportionment of seats in the House of Representatives is based on the relative population of each state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allocation - the process of giving out resources or duties
  • Synonym: apportionment - the action of dividing and distributing according to a plan

Etymology

From Latin assignatio (an assigning, allotment), from assignare (to assign).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
