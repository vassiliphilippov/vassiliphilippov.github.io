Seconal

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkəˌnɔl / (AmE), / ˈsiːkənɔːl / (BrE)
A trademarked barbiturate drug used as a sedative and to treat convulsions; commonly administered as sodium secobarbital.
barbiturate sedative drug (trade name)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Seconal to help the patient sleep before surgery.
Some medical facilities no longer use Seconal due to its potential for dependence and overdose.
The pharmacist explained the risks associated with taking Seconal for extended periods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secobarbital - the chemical name for the drug known as Seconal

Etymology

Trade name, from shortened 'secobarbital' and ending similar to 'phenobarbital'.
