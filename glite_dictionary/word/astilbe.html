Astilbe

1. NOUN

/ əˈstɪl.bi / (AmE), / əˈstɪl.bi / (BrE)
a genus of herbaceous perennial plants, chiefly native to Asia, with fern-like foliage and feathery plumes of flowers, often grown as ornamentals
genus of perennial, ornamental plants

Examples

The shady corner of the garden was brightened by a cluster of Astilbe in full bloom.
Gardeners often choose Astilbe for its elegant, feathery flower plumes and shade tolerance.
Several species in the Astilbe genus are popular in perennial borders and woodland gardens.

Etymology

From New Latin, coined by botanist Carl Ludwig Blume, from Greek 'a-' (not) + 'stilbe' (brightness), referring to the plant's dull leaves.
