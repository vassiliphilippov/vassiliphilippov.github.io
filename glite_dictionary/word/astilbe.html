astilbe

1. NOUN

/ əˈstɪl.bi / (AmE), / əˈstɪl.bi / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the genus Astilbe, with compound leaves and large, showy clusters of tiny, colorful flowers.
perennial plant with feathery flowers

Examples

The shaded garden bed was filled with blooming astilbe in shades of pink and white.
She planted astilbe along the path to add texture and vibrant color to the border.
In early summer, the feathery plumes of astilbe stand out among the foliage plants.

Etymology

From New Latin Astilbe, coined by Scottish botanist George Don.
