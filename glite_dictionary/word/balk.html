balk

1. NOUN

/ bɔk / (AmE), / bɔːk / (BrE)
the area on a billiard table behind the balkline, from which the cue ball is played under certain rules
area behind the balkline on a billiard table

Examples

He carefully placed the cue ball in the balk before making his shot.
The rules specify that after a foul, play must resume from the balk.
She practiced her break shots starting from the balk area.

Synset Examples

a player with ball in hand must play from the balk

2. NOUN

/ bɑk / (AmE), / bɔːk / (BrE)
An obstacle or hindrance that interferes with or delays progress.
obstacle or hindrance

Examples

The negotiations hit a balk when neither side would compromise.
Outdated regulations remain a significant balk to innovation in the industry.
Every entrepreneur faces a balk at some point on their journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks one's way or prevents progress

Etymology

From Old English 'balca' (ridge, bank), related to Old Norse 'balkr' (beam).

3. NOUN

/ bɔk / (AmE), / bɔːk / (BrE)
one of several parallel sloping beams that support a roof, especially in a timber-framed building
sloping roof support beam

Examples

The ancient barn still had its original balks supporting the heavy thatched roof.
Carpenters carefully adjusted each balk to ensure the rafters would align correctly.
A beam broke during the storm, but the remaining balks kept the roof standing.

Etymology

From Old English *balc* (ridge, bank; beam), related to Old High German *balco* (beam).

4. VERB

/ bɑk / (AmE), / bɔːk / (BrE)
to hesitate or refuse to accept an idea or undertake an action; to be unwilling to comply or proceed
hesitate or refuse to comply

Examples

The board will likely balk at the proposal if the costs are too high.
Many employees balk at working overtime during the holidays.
She started to sign the contract, but balked when she read the fine print.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to comply - to act in accordance with a wish or command
  • Synonym: refuse - to indicate or show that one is not willing to do something

Etymology

From Old English 'balca' meaning a ridge or barrier; developed metaphorically to mean 'hinder' or 'refuse'.

5. NOUN

/ bɔk / (AmE), / bɔːk / (BrE)
In baseball, an illegal motion made by the pitcher while runners are on base, which allows all runners to advance one base.
illegal baseball pitching motion

Examples

The umpire called a balk, allowing the runner on third base to score.
After the pitcher's balk, the crowd erupted into boos from the stands.
The manager argued with the umpire about whether it was really a balk or just a hesitation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitching - throwing the ball to the batter in baseball
