packrat

1. NOUN

/ ˈpækˌræt / (AmE), / ˈpakˌrat / (BrE)
A small North American rodent of the genus Neotoma, known for its bushy tail and habit of collecting and hoarding objects.
a hoarding North American rodent

Examples

A packrat will often fill its nest with twigs, leaves, and bits of human trash.
Researchers studied the diet of the packrat by analyzing what it collected over time.
The packrats in the desert are known for their impressive nests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodrat - another name for the same group of rodents, genus Neotoma

2. NOUN

/ ˈpækˌræt / (AmE), / ˈpakˌrat / (BrE)
a person who hoards or collects large quantities of unnecessary or useless items, often unwilling to throw things away
person who hoards useless items
american

Examples

My grandfather is a real packrat—his garage is stacked with old magazines and broken tools.
Being a packrat makes it hard for her to throw anything away, even empty jars.
After years of being a packrat, he finally decided to declutter his house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoarder - a person who accumulates things and has difficulty discarding them
  • Antonym: minimalist - a person who prefers simplicity and avoids clutter

Etymology

Originally American English, from the name of the North American pack rat, a rodent that collects various objects.
