Wahhabi

1. NOUN

/ wɑːˈhɑːbi / (AmE), / wəˈhɑːbi / (BrE)
a member of a strictly orthodox Sunni Muslim sect originating in Saudi Arabia, advocating the purification of Islamic beliefs and rejecting religious innovations introduced after the 3rd century of Islam
member of strict Sunni Muslim sect

Examples

Osama bin Laden was said to be a Wahhabi Muslim by many sources.
The Wahhabi movement has had a significant influence on Saudi Arabian society.
He studied Wahhabi teachings while living in Riyadh.

Synset Examples

Osama bin Laden is said to be a Wahhabi Muslim

Etymology

From Arabic Wahhābī (وَهَّابِي), from the name of Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahhab, founder of the movement.

2. NOUN

/ wɑːˈhɑːbi / (AmE), / wəˈhɑːbi / (BrE)
A member of a strictly orthodox Sunni Muslim sect originating in Saudi Arabia that advocates a return to the early traditions of Islam and rejects innovations introduced after the 3rd century of Islam.
member of a strict Sunni Muslim sect

Examples

He was raised as a Wahhabi and followed strict religious practices.
Some scholars consider Wahhabi beliefs to be very conservative within Sunni Islam.
The influence of the Wahhabis spread beyond Saudi Arabia in the twentieth century.

Synset Examples

Osama bin Laden is said to be a Wahhabi Muslim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Salafi - a member of an Islamic movement advocating a return to the traditions of the 'salaf' (the first three generations of Muslims)

Etymology

From Arabic Wahhābī, from Muḥammad ibn ʿAbd al-Wahhāb, founder of the sect.
