metaphase

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtəˌfeɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛtəˌfeɪz / (BrE)
the stage of cell division, following prophase, in which chromosomes align at the cell's equatorial plate before being separated into daughter cells
stage in cell division when chromosomes align
medical

Examples

During metaphase, the chromosomes line up along the center of the cell.
Scientists observed abnormal metaphase figures under the microscope.
Problems during metaphase can lead to genetic mutations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: anaphase - stage of mitosis when chromosomes separate
  • Degree Lower: prophase - first stage of mitosis when chromosomes condense

Etymology

From Greek 'meta-' (after, beyond) + 'phase' (stage).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtəˌfeɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛtəˌfeɪz / (BrE)
The stage in cell division, particularly in mitosis and meiosis, during which the chromosomes align in the center of the cell before being separated into each of the two daughter cells.
stage of cell division when chromosomes align
medical

Examples

During metaphase, the chromosomes line up across the cell's equator.
Researchers studied the behavior of spindle fibers during metaphase in meiosis.
The accuracy of chromosome separation in metaphase is critical for healthy cell division.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: anaphase - the stage following metaphase, when chromosomes separate
  • Degree Lower: prophase - the stage before metaphase, when chromosomes condense

Etymology

From Greek 'meta' (after, beyond) + 'phase' (stage).
