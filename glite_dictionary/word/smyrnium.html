Smyrnium

1. NOUN

/ smɪrˈniːəm / (AmE), / smɪˈnɪː.ri.əm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the parsley family, including Alexanders (Smyrnium olusatrum), traditionally used as a culinary herb.
plant genus including Alexanders

Examples

Smyrnium is often found growing wild near coastal regions in Europe.
Botanists have identified three species within the genus Smyrnium.
The leaves of Smyrnium were once commonly used in salads and soups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alexanders - a biennial plant (Smyrnium olusatrum) formerly cultivated as a vegetable

Etymology

From Latin 'smyrnium', from Greek 'smyrnion', a plant associated with myrrh.

2. NOUN

/ smɪrˈniːəm / (AmE), / smɪˈnɪniəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the parsley family (Apiaceae), native to Eurasia, including species such as Alexanders.
a genus of Eurasian plants (Apiaceae)

Examples

Several species within the genus Smyrnium are valued for their edible leaves and stems.
The most commonly known member of Smyrnium is Alexanders, a herb once popular as a vegetable.
Botanists identified the plant as belonging to the genus Smyrnium after careful examination of its flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'smyrnium', from Greek 'smyrnion', possibly related to 'smyrna' (myrrh), referring to the plant's aromatic scent.
