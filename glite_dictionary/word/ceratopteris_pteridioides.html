Ceratopteris pteridioides

1. NOUN

/ sɛrəˌtɑptərɪs ptɛrɪdiˈɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / sɛrəˌtɒptərɪs ptɛrɪdiˈɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
an aquatic fern of tropical America, often cultivated in aquariums
aquatic fern used in aquariums

Examples

Many aquarists prefer to grow Ceratopteris pteridioides because it purifies the water and provides hiding places for fish.
The aquarium was decorated with lush green Ceratopteris pteridioides fronds swaying gently in the current.
In tropical regions, Ceratopteris pteridioides can be found growing wild in slow-moving streams and ponds.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus Ceratopteris + species epithet from Greek 'pteridion', a diminutive of 'pteris' (fern).
