corporation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔr.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɔː.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A large company or group of companies that is legally authorized to act as a single entity and recognized as such in law.
a legally recognized business entity

Examples

The multinational corporation expanded its operations into several new countries this year.
She works as an accountant for a large corporation based in New York.
Many people invest in stocks of a corporation expecting long-term growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: company - a business organization selling goods or services
  • Antonym: partnership - a business owned by two or more people

Etymology

From Latin 'corporatio', from 'corporare' (to form into a body).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɔːpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
(slang) A large, protruding belly; a paunch.
slang for a large, protruding belly
slang

Examples

After years of good food and little exercise, he developed quite a corporation.
He tried to hide his corporation behind the table during dinner.
Joking about his growing corporation, he promised to start dieting next week.

Etymology

Playful transfer from 'corporation' (business body) to 'body' as in a belly; originally humorous.
