venae pudendum

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːnaɪ pjuːˈdɛndəm / (AmE), / ˈviːniː pjuːˈdɛndəm / (BrE)
a vein or group of veins located in the external genital area (pudendum) of the body, especially in women.
vein in the external genital area
medical

Examples

The surgeon identified the venae pudendum during the procedure to prevent excessive bleeding.
Anatomical illustrations label the venae pudendum to help students locate important blood vessels in the pelvic region.
Certain conditions can affect the venae pudendum, causing discomfort or swelling in the area.

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'pudendum' (the external genital organs, especially of the female).
