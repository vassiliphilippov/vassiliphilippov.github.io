Agnatha

1. NOUN

/ æɡˈneɪθə / (AmE), / æɡˈneɪθə / (BrE)
A superclass of eel-shaped vertebrates that lack jaws and pelvic fins, including lampreys, hagfishes, and some extinct forms.
jawless vertebrate superclass

Examples

The lamprey is an example of an animal in the Agnatha superclass.
Scientists study Agnatha to understand the early evolution of vertebrates.
Unlike most modern fish, members of the Agnatha lack jaws and paired fins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamprey - a jawless fish in the superclass Agnatha
  • Synonym: hagfish - a jawless eel-like fish in Agnatha

Etymology

From Greek a- (without) + gnathos (jaw).

2. NOUN

/ ˈægnəθə / (AmE), / ˈægnəθə / (BrE)
A superclass of jawless, eel-shaped vertebrates that includes lampreys, hagfishes, and their extinct relatives.
jawless vertebrate superclass

Examples

Lampreys and hagfishes belong to the Agnatha superclass, which is characterized by the absence of jaws.
Unlike most fish, members of the Agnatha lack both jaws and pelvic fins.
Fossil evidence has revealed a wide diversity of extinct Agnatha species from the Paleozoic era.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'gnathos' (jaw).
