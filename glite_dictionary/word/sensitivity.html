sensitivity

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛn.səˈtɪv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌsɛnsɪˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the ability to perceive and respond to the feelings and emotions of oneself and others; emotional awareness or responsiveness
emotional awareness or responsiveness

Examples

Her sensitivity to other people's emotions makes her a great listener.
Good managers often demonstrate sensitivity when handling employee concerns.
He showed remarkable sensitivity in dealing with his friend's grief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empathy - the ability to understand and share the feelings of others
  • Antonym: insensitivity - lack of concern or awareness for others' feelings

Etymology

From Latin 'sensitivus' (capable of sensation), from 'sentire' (to feel).

medical

Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insensitivity - lack of responsiveness to stimuli

5. NOUN

/ ˌsɛn.səˈtɪv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌsɛnsɪˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the degree to which an organism or cell is susceptible to infection or harm by a pathogen or substance
degree of susceptibility to pathogens
medical

Examples

Some bacteria have developed a low sensitivity to antibiotics, making infections harder to treat.
The patient's high sensitivity to pollen led to severe allergic reactions during spring.
Scientists test the sensitivity of crops to various plant viruses in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resistance - the ability of an organism to withstand or fend off a pathogen

Etymology

From sensitive + -ity, ultimately from Latin sensibilis (capable of perceiving) + -itas.
