ESR

1. NOUN

/ ˌiː ˌɛs ˈɑːr / (AmE), / ˌiː ˌɛs ˈɑː / (BrE)
a technique in which microwaves are absorbed by paramagnetic substances to study their electronic structure, also known as electron spin resonance
electron spin resonance spectroscopy

Examples

Scientists used ESR to investigate the molecular structure of the sample.
The ESR spectrum revealed details about the material's unpaired electrons.
Routine ESR measurements are critical in identifying free radicals in chemistry research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: EPR - electron paramagnetic resonance, another name for ESR

Etymology

Abbreviation of electron spin resonance.

2. NOUN

/ i ɛs ɑr / (AmE), / i ɛs ɑː / (BrE)
abbreviation for erythrocyte sedimentation rate; a blood test measuring how quickly red blood cells settle in a test tube, used to detect inflammation.
blood test for inflammation (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered an ESR to check for signs of inflammation.
A high ESR may indicate an underlying disease such as infection or autoimmune disorder.
Her ESR was elevated, prompting further diagnostic tests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erythrocyte sedimentation rate - the full term for ESR; a blood test indicating inflammation

Etymology

Acronym from erythrocyte sedimentation rate.
