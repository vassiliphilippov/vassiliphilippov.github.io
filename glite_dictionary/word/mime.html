mime

1. NOUN

/ maɪm / (AmE), / maɪm / (BrE)
a theatrical performance in which meaning is conveyed through gestures and body movements without spoken words
performance using gestures, not speech

Examples

The actor performed a brilliant mime that captivated the entire audience.
Children laughed while watching the street artist's mime in the park.
At the festival, a group presented a classic mime piece about being trapped in an invisible box.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pantomime - a dramatic entertainment in which performers express meaning through gestures without speech

Etymology

From Greek mimos (imitator, actor).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00551053-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ maɪm / (AmE), / maɪm / (BrE)
an actor who performs and conveys stories or emotions entirely through gestures and facial expressions, without spoken words
actor communicating by gesture without speech

Examples

The mime entertained the crowd with silent comedy in the town square.
As a skilled mime, she could convey a wide range of emotions with just a look and a gesture.
Children watched the mime pretend to be trapped inside an invisible box.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pantomime - a type of performance or performer using only gesture and movement

Etymology

From Latin mimus, from Greek mimos (imitator, actor).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10338550-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ maɪm / (AmE), / maɪm / (BrE)
to imitate or copy the actions or manner of someone, often for entertainment or satirical effect
imitate a person or manner

Examples

The comedian mimed the boss’s walk to get a laugh from the audience.
She mimes her sister’s mannerisms so well that everyone instantly recognizes the impression.
During the skit, he mimed the famous singer’s gestures with exaggerated flair.

Synset Examples

The actor mimicked the President very accurately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mimic - to imitate closely, especially for amusement
  • Synonym: to impersonate - pretend to be another person as entertainment or fraud

Etymology

From Latin 'mimus' (actor, mimic), via French 'mime'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01747412-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ maɪm / (AmE), / maɪm / (BrE)
to act out or express something using gestures and movements without speaking
act using gestures without speech

Examples

The children mimed washing their hands for the play.
He mimed opening a heavy door during his performance.
She mimed drinking from an invisible cup to amuse the audience.

Synset Examples

The acting students mimed eating an apple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pantomime - to express meaning through gestures accompanied by music, often without speech

Etymology

From French *mime*, from Latin *mimus* (imitator, actor, mimic).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01728062-v
Synset POS: v
