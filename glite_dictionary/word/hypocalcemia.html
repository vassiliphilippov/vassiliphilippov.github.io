hypocalcemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpoʊkælˈsiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊkalˈsiːmɪə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which there is an abnormally low level of calcium in the blood
abnormally low blood calcium
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with hypocalcemia after reviewing her blood test results.
Muscle cramps are a common symptom of hypocalcemia.
Proper management of hypocalcemia often includes calcium and vitamin D supplementation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypercalcemia - abnormally high calcium level in the blood

Etymology

From New Latin, from hypo- (under) + calcemia (calcium in the blood).
