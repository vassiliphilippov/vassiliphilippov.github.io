Dido

1. PROPN

/ ˈdaɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɪdəʊ / (BrE)
(in Roman mythology) a princess of Tyre who became the founder and queen of Carthage, noted for her tragic love affair with Aeneas as told by Virgil.
queen of Carthage in Roman mythology

Examples

In the Aeneid, Dido is portrayed as a tragic figure driven to despair by love.
The legend of Dido founding Carthage fascinated many classical historians.
Opera composers, including Purcell, have made Dido's death the subject of famous works.

Etymology

From Latin Dīdō, possibly from Phoenician.
