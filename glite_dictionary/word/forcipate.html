forcipate

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔr.səˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.sɪ.peɪt / (BrE)
having a shape resembling forceps; deeply forked or divided.
shaped like forceps; deeply forked

Examples

The insect's forcipate mandibles are adapted for grasping prey.
Some leaves have a distinct forcipate appearance, almost as if pinched at the tip.
The fossil displays a forcipate structure, indicating it was once part of a predatory animal.

Etymology

From Latin 'forceps' (a pair of tongs).
