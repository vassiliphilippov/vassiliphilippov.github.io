Neptunism

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛp.tuˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈnɛp.tjuːˌnɪz.əm / (BrE)
An obsolete theory in geology that all rocks of the earth's crust were formed by the action of water.
obsolete geological theory about rock formation by water
archaic

Examples

The theory of Neptunism was widely accepted among geologists in the late 18th century.
Abraham Werner was a major proponent of Neptunism in geological circles.
Modern geology has replaced Neptunism with the understanding that many rocks form through volcanic activity.

Synset Examples

Werner, with his Neptunism theory, argued that all rocks are the precipitates and crystallized minerals of an ocean that once covered the earth in the not too distant past.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Plutonism - the theory that rocks formed from volcanic activity

Etymology

From Neptune, Roman god of the sea, referring to the role of water in the theory, plus -ism.
