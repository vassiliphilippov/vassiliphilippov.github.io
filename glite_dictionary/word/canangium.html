Canangium

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnæn.dʒi.əm / (AmE), / kəˈnæn.dʒi.əm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees native to Malaysia, known for their aromatic flowers.
genus of Malayan aromatic trees

Examples

The Canangium genus includes trees prized for their fragrant blossoms.
Many perfumes use extracts derived from Canangium flowers as a key ingredient.
Botanists studied the distribution of Canangium species across Southeast Asia.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈnæn.dʒi.əm / (AmE), / kəˈnæn.dʒi.əm / (BrE)
a genus of trees native to Malaysia, known for fragrant flowers and used in perfumery.
a genus of Malayan trees

Examples

The Canangium genus includes trees with highly aromatic blossoms prized in the fragrance industry.
Research into Canangium species has led to new uses in traditional medicine.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Canangium genus in the forests of Malaysia.

Etymology

From Malay 'kenanga', the local name for the tree, combined with Latin genus naming conventions.
