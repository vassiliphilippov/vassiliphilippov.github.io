to double crochet

1. VERB

/ ˈdʌbl kroʊˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl krəʊˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
to create a crochet stitch by making two yarn overs and drawing the yarn through the loops, forming a taller stitch than a single crochet.
make a stitch twice yarned in crochet

Examples

After you turn your work, double crochet into the next stitch.
She double crocheted a blanket for her nephew's birthday.
They taught me how to double crochet during the craft workshop.

Etymology

From 'double' (meaning twice as much) + 'crochet', from French 'crochet' (small hook).
