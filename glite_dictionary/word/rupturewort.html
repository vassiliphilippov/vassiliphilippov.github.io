rupturewort

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌptʃərˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈrʌptʃəˌwɜːt / (BrE)
a small, low-growing Old World herb (Herniaria glabra), often used as ground cover, once believed to cure hernias and ruptures
an Old World ground cover herb

Examples

The gardener recommended planting rupturewort in areas where grass had difficulty growing.
In ancient herbal medicine, rupturewort was valued for its supposed healing properties.
The carpet of rupturewort created a dense, green cover along the rocky path.

Etymology

From rupture + wort, 'wort' being an old word for plant or herb.
