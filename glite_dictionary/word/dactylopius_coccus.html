Dactylopius coccus

1. NOUN

/ dækˈtɪl.ə.pi.əs ˈkɒk.əs / (AmE), / dækˈtɪl.ə.pi.əs ˈkɒk.əs / (BrE)
a species of Mexican red scale insect that feeds on cacti and is the source of cochineal, a natural red dye
Mexican insect; source of red dye cochineal

Examples

The brilliant red coloring came from Dactylopius coccus collected on prickly pear cacti.
Many artists in the Renaissance preferred the pigment derived from Dactylopius coccus.
Farmers in Mexico have cultivated Dactylopius coccus for centuries to produce cochineal dye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cochineal - a natural red dye made from insects

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek dáktylos (finger) and pión (fat); coccus from Greek kokkos (berry, seed), referring to shape and form.
