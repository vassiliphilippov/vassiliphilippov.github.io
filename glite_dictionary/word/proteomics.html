proteomics

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtiː.ɑː.mɪks / (AmE), / prəʊˈtiː.ə.mɪks / (BrE)
the branch of genetics and molecular biology concerned with the study of the full set of proteins produced by an organism's genome
the study of all proteins produced by a genome
medical

Examples

She is conducting research in proteomics to discover how diseases affect protein expression.
Advances in proteomics have allowed scientists to analyze thousands of proteins simultaneously.
The field of proteomics has become essential for understanding complex biological systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genomics - the study of genomes and their functions

Etymology

From protein + -omics, patterned after genomics.
