Masticophis lateralis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæstɪˈkoʊfɪs læˈtɛrəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌmæstɪˈkəʊfɪs læˈtɛrəlɪs / (BrE)
A species of slender whipsnake native to scrublands and rocky hillsides in western North America, known for its fast movements and long, thin body.
species of slender whipsnake

Examples

The Masticophis lateralis can often be seen darting through rocky outcrops in search of prey.
Biologists collected data on the habitat preferences of Masticophis lateralis in California.
The distinctive coloration makes the Masticophis lateralis easy to identify among other snakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whipsnake - any of several fast-moving, slender snakes

Etymology

From Greek *mastix* (whip) + *ophis* (snake), and Latin *lateralis* (of the side).
