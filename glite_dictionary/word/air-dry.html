air-dry

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛrˌdraɪ / (AmE), / ˈeəˌdraɪ / (BrE)
(of a material) not giving off moisture when exposed to air; maintaining a stable moisture content under normal atmospheric conditions.
not emitting moisture to the air

Examples

Make sure the air-dry bricks are used in the foundation to prevent moisture problems.
This type of air-dry clay is popular for children’s crafts because it hardens without baking.
The wood must be completely air-dry before it can be used in furniture making.

Etymology

Formed from the words 'air' and 'dry', denoting a state achieved by exposure to air rather than artificial means.
