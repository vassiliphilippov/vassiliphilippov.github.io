to caponize

1. VERB

/ ˈkeɪpəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkæpənaɪz / (BrE)
to castrate a rooster so that it becomes a capon, usually to improve the quality of its meat
castrate a rooster (to make a capon)

Examples

Farmers sometimes caponize young roosters to improve the tenderness of their meat.
It is illegal in some countries to caponize chickens for commercial purposes.
The veterinarian showed the students how to safely caponize poultry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castrate - to remove the testicles of a male animal

Etymology

From 'capon', ultimately from Latin 'caponem' (capon), with the suffix '-ize' meaning 'to make or become'.
