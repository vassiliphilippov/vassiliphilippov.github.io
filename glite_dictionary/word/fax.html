fax

1. NOUN

/ fæks / (AmE), / fæks / (BrE)
a machine that sends and receives printed documents electronically over a telephone line or radio signal
electronic document-sending machine

Examples

He sent the contract by fax to the main office.
The report arrived on the fax machine this morning.
Many businesses still use a fax for urgent document transmission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facsimile - an exact copy, especially of written or printed material

Etymology

Shortened form of facsimile, from Latin 'fac simile' (make similar).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03321050-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ fæks / (AmE), / fæks / (BrE)
to send (a document or image) by a facsimile machine over a telephone line
send by facsimile machine

Examples

Could you fax these documents to the lawyer before noon?
She faxed the signed contract to the client as soon as she received it.
We are faxing the latest updates to all branch offices this afternoon.

Synset Examples

Can you fax me the report right away?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: email - send electronic mail; communicate digitally
  • Synonym: mail - send by post; transmit a letter or document

Etymology

Shortened from 'facsimile', referring to the transmission technology, with 'fax' first used in this sense in the mid-20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01009703-v
Synset POS: v
