epicardium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈkɑːrdiəm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈkɑːdiəm / (BrE)
the inner layer of the pericardium that directly covers the surface of the heart
inner layer of the heart's pericardium
medical

Examples

The epicardium plays a crucial role in protecting the heart and supporting its blood vessels.
During heart surgery, doctors must carefully avoid damaging the epicardium.
Inflammation of the epicardium can lead to serious cardiac complications.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pericardium - the membrane enclosing the heart

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek epi- (upon) + kardia (heart).
