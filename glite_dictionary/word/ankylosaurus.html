Ankylosaurus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋkɪloʊˈsɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˌaŋkɪləʊˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of large, armored herbivorous dinosaurs that lived during the Cretaceous period, known for their bony plates and club-like tail
armored herbivorous dinosaur genus

Examples

The fossils of Ankylosaurus have been found in North America, revealing its distinctive bony armor.
Children are often fascinated by the spiked tail of the Ankylosaurus when learning about dinosaurs.
Paleontologists believe Ankylosaurus used its clubbed tail to defend itself against predators.

Etymology

From Greek ankylos (bent, crooked) and sauros (lizard).
