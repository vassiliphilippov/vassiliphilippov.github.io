ankylosaurus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋ.kə.ləˈsɔːr.əs / (AmE), / ˌaŋ.kɪ.ləˈsɔː.rəs / (BrE)
a large, herbivorous dinosaur of the late Cretaceous period with a heavily armored body and club-like tail.
armored dinosaur with club tail

Examples

The ankylosaurus used its clubbed tail to defend itself against predators.
Many paleontologists study the armor plating of the ankylosaurus to learn about dinosaur defense mechanisms.
Children at the museum were fascinated by the life-size model of an ankylosaurus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stegosaurus - herbivorous dinosaur with bony plates along its back

Etymology

From Greek ankylos (fused, curved) + sauros (lizard).
