frotteur

1. NOUN

/ frɑːˈtɝː / (AmE), / frɒˈtɜː / (BrE)
a person who derives sexual pleasure from rubbing against another person, often in a crowded public place, typically without consent
person who gains sexual pleasure by rubbing against others
medical

Examples

The police arrested the frotteur after several complaints from subway passengers.
A psychiatric evaluation determined that the man was a habitual frotteur.
Experts warn that a frotteur may repeatedly target crowded areas to avoid detection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frotteurism - sexual arousal from rubbing against non-consenting persons

Etymology

From French 'frotteur' meaning 'rubber' or 'one who rubs', from 'frotter' (to rub).
