Platanus racemosa

1. NOUN

/ pləˈteɪnəs ˌræsɪˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / pləˈteɪnəs ˌrasɪˈməʊsə / (BrE)
A species of tall tree native to Baja California, with deciduous bark, large palmately lobed leaves, and ball-shaped flower clusters.
species of tall tree native to Baja California

Examples

The Platanus racemosa is commonly known as the California sycamore.
Botanists study the unique bark patterns of Platanus racemosa in arid regions.
Many parks in southern California feature Platanus racemosa along walking trails.

Etymology

From Latin; Platanus is the plane tree genus, racemosa refers to 'clustered' or 'in bunches', describing the flower clusters.
