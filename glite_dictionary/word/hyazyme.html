Hyazyme

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪəˌzaɪm / (AmE), / ˈhaɪəˌzaɪm / (BrE)
A trade name for an enzyme (hyaluronidase) used to split hyaluronic acid, reducing viscosity and increasing tissue permeability.
enzyme trade name (hyaluronidase)
medical

Examples

The doctor administered Hyazyme to enhance the absorption of the injected medication.
Hyazyme is sometimes used to reduce swelling in certain medical treatments.
Patients with allergies to Hyazyme should notify their healthcare provider before the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyaluronidase - an enzyme that breaks down hyaluronic acid
