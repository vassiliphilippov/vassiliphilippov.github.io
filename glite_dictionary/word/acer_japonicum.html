Acer japonicum

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪsər dʒæˈpɒnɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪsə dʒəˈpɒnɪkəm / (BrE)
A species of maple tree native to Japan, known for its deeply incised leaves that turn bright red in autumn.
Japanese maple tree species

Examples

The Acer japonicum in our garden displays stunning red foliage every autumn.
Many horticulturists appreciate Acer japonicum for its ornamental beauty.
The leaves of Acer japonicum are deeply lobed and very decorative.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maple - A tree or shrub with lobed leaves, often with brilliant autumn colors

Etymology

From Latin: 'acer' (maple) and 'japonicum' (of Japan).
