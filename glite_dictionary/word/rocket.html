rocket

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɒkɪt / (BrE)
a firework that is propelled high into the air, often producing a visual or audible display
firework sent high into the air

Examples

The children watched as the rocket exploded in a shower of colorful sparks.
We lit a rocket to celebrate New Year's Eve.
A single rocket shot into the night sky, marking the start of the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firework - an explosive device for producing sights and sounds, used in celebrations
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04240011-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɒkɪt / (BrE)
A vehicle or device propelled by a rocket engine, typically used for space travel, military purposes, or scientific exploration.
vehicle propelled by a rocket engine

Examples

The rocket soared into the sky, leaving a trail of smoke behind.
NASA launched a new rocket carrying supplies to the International Space Station.
The museum displays an early rocket used in space exploration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: missile - an object forcibly propelled at a target, especially a weapon

Etymology

From Italian 'rocchetto' (bobbin, spool), referring to its cylindrical shape; later applied to the engine-powered vehicle.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04106523-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈrɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɒkɪt / (BrE)
to propel or launch using a rocket engine; to send upward swiftly by means of a rocket
propel using a rocket

Examples

The engineers plan to rocket the new satellite into orbit next month.
They hope to rocket supplies to the stranded crew as soon as possible.
With a burst of flame, the rescue capsule was rocketed into the sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launch - to send something into the air or space

Etymology

From Italian *rocchetta* (bobbin), later applied to a projectile; later generalized for rocket engines.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01244317-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɒkɪt / (BrE)
A type of engine or vehicle that carries its own fuel and is propelled by the force of gases released from burning that fuel.
engine or vehicle driven by reaction propulsion

Examples

The scientists launched a rocket to study the atmosphere.
The rocket burned through its fuel in less than two minutes.
Space travel became possible with the invention of the rocket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: missile - an object propelled to a target, sometimes using rocket propulsion

Etymology

From Italian 'rocchetto' (bobbin, small spindle), via French 'roquette' in the 17th century, referring to the cylindrical shape.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04106269-n
Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ ˈrɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɒkɪt / (BrE)
(of an amount, price, etc.) to increase very quickly and suddenly.
increase rapidly and suddenly

Examples

After the announcement of the new product, sales rocketed within a few weeks.
Unemployment rates rocketed during the economic crisis.
The temperature rocketed to record highs this summer.

Synset Examples

prices skyrocketed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to soar - to increase rapidly, especially in price or amount
  • Antonym: plummet - to fall or drop straight down at high speed (opposite in direction)

Etymology

From the noun 'rocket' (projectile), used metaphorically to describe something rising rapidly.

6. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɒkɪt / (BrE)
A firework or device that rises into the air, propelled by combustion, often emitting a trail of bright light, or a device used to send a line or harpoon to a distant point, especially in rescue operations.
firework or device for sending a line

Examples

The children watched excitedly as the rocket soared into the night sky, trailing sparks behind it.
A rocket was fired from the shore to send a lifesaving line to the stranded ship.
He held the rocket carefully, making sure to secure it before lighting the fuse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firework - an explosive device giving a display of light and noise

Etymology

From Italian *rocchetto* (bobbin), from *rocca* (distaff), probably in reference to its cylindrical shape; the word later referred to projectile devices.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07277721-n
Synset POS: n

7. NOUN

/ ˈrɑː.kɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɒk.ɪt / (BrE)
a leafy green European plant with a peppery flavor, often grown as a salad crop; also known as arugula, particularly in American English.
a peppery salad green plant
british

Examples

I like to add fresh rocket to my salads for an extra kick of flavor.
The chef garnished the pizza with a handful of rocket leaves.
She grows rocket in her garden and picks it when it's still young and tender.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arugula - North American term for the same plant (Eruca vesicaria)

Etymology

From Italian 'rocchetta', diminutive of 'rocca' (the plant), via French 'roquette'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11907006-n
Synset POS: n
