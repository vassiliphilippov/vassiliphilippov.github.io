inimitably

1. ADV

/ ɪˈnɪmɪtəbli / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪmɪtəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is impossible to imitate; uniquely or exceptionally.
uniquely, in an impossible-to-imitate way

Examples

She delivered the speech inimitably, captivating everyone in the audience.
His desserts are prepared inimitably and cannot be found anywhere else.
The artist combined colors inimitably, creating a style all his own.

Synset Examples

he has an inimitably verbose style

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniquely - in a way that is the only one of its kind
  • Antonym: imitably - in a way that can be imitated

Etymology

From Latin inimitabilis ('that cannot be imitated'), from in- ('not') + imitari ('to imitate').
