to congregate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑŋɡrɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡrɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to come together in a group, usually for a particular purpose
gather into a group

Examples

After the fire alarm sounded, employees congregated in the parking lot.
Birds often congregate by the lake during migration season.
Protesters congregated outside the government building to voice their concerns.

Synset Examples

The crowds congregated in front of the Vatican on Christmas Eve

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to come together or assemble in one place
  • Antonym: to disperse - to break up and move in different directions

Etymology

From Latin congregare (to collect into a flock), from com- (together) + gregare (to gather, from grex 'flock').
