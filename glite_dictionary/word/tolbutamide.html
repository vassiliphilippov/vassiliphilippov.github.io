tolbutamide

1. NOUN

/ toʊlˈbjuːtəˌmaɪd / (AmE), / tɒlˈbjuːtəməɪd / (BrE)
a sulfonylurea drug used orally to lower blood sugar in the treatment of type 2 diabetes mellitus
oral antidiabetic medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed tolbutamide to help control her blood sugar levels.
Patients taking tolbutamide should monitor for signs of low blood sugar.
Recent guidelines recommend alternatives to tolbutamide for new patients with type 2 diabetes.

Etymology

Formed from elements of 'toluene', 'butyl', and 'amide', referring to its chemical structure.
