xenotransplant

1. NOUN

/ ˌzinoʊˈtrænsplænt / (AmE), / ˌziːnəʊˈtrænsplɑːnt / (BrE)
A surgical procedure in which tissue or whole organs are transplanted from one species to another.
transplantation across species
medical

Examples

The medical team performed a xenotransplant using a pig heart to treat the patient.
Researchers are studying the risks involved in xenotransplant procedures for organ failure.
Ethical debates often arise concerning the use of animal organs for xenotransplant purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transplant - to transfer an organ or tissue from one body to another

Etymology

From Greek xenos (stranger, foreigner) + English transplant.
