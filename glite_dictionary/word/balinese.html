Balinese

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːlɪˈniːz / (AmE), / ˌbæliˈniːz / (BrE)
the Austronesian language spoken by the people of Bali, Indonesia
language of the people of Bali, Indonesia

Examples

Many residents of Bali primarily communicate in Balinese at home and in daily life.
He decided to study Balinese to better understand the island's rich cultural heritage.
The Balinese language uses its own unique script, distinct from Indonesian.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Bali - Indonesian island, home of the Balinese people

Etymology

From Bali (the Indonesian island) + -nese (forming demonyms and language names).
