lozenge

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːzɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɒzɪndʒ / (BrE)
A small, typically medicated tablet intended to dissolve slowly in the mouth to soothe or treat mouth or throat ailments.
medicated tablet for mouth or throat
medical

Examples

He took a lozenge to soothe his sore throat during the meeting.
The pharmacist recommended a honey-flavored lozenge for my cough.
Please suck the lozenge slowly until it dissolves completely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastille - a small, often flavored tablet for oral use, similar in function to a lozenge
  • Synonym: tablet - a compressed, solid dose of medication, usually swallowed

Etymology

From Old French 'losenge' (diamond shape), later applied to the medicinal tablet because of its shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɑzəndʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɒzɪndʒ / (BrE)
a small, usually sweet tablet or candy, often medicated, that is dissolved slowly in the mouth to soothe irritation, especially of the throat
medicated or sweet tablet dissolved in the mouth

Examples

She took a lozenge to soothe her sore throat before the presentation.
The pharmacist recommended a honey-flavored lozenge for my cough.
He always carries a pack of lozenges in his bag during cold season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cough drop - a medicated lozenge used to soothe sore throats or suppress coughing
  • Synonym: pastille - a small flavored candy, often medicated, that dissolves in the mouth

Etymology

From Old French *losenge* (diamond shape, rhombus), later applied to tablet form.
