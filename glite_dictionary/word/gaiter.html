gaiter

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a cloth or leather covering for the lower leg and ankle, worn over the shoe to protect against mud or water
protective leg covering for ankle and instep

Examples

The hiker wore gaiters to keep mud and snow out of his boots.
Her gaiters protected her legs from burrs while walking through the tall grass.
Hunters often use waterproof gaiters during wet weather.

Etymology

From French 'guêtre,' meaning leg covering, of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪtə / (BrE)
A cloth or leather covering for the lower leg, from the knee to the ankle, often worn over shoes or boots for protection or as part of a uniform.
cloth/leather leg covering (knee to ankle)

Examples

The hikers wore gaiters to keep mud and pebbles out of their boots.
During the parade, soldiers marched in polished boots and black gaiters.
He strapped on his gaiters before heading into the snowy forest.

Etymology

From French *guêtre* (leg covering), possibly of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a type of shoe or covering that fits over the ankle and sometimes the lower leg, often with elastic sides for a snug fit
ankle-covering shoe or garment

Examples

He wore a pair of leather gaiters over his boots for extra protection during the hike.
The vintage shop displayed several styles of gaiters popular in the early twentieth century.
Modern gaiters with elastic sides are both practical and stylish for urban wear.

Etymology

From French *guêtre* (protective leg covering), likely of Germanic origin.
