to nab

1. VERB

/ næb / (AmE), / næb / (BrE)
(In baseball) to catch a base runner by tagging them and thereby get them out.
to tag a base runner out (baseball)

Examples

The shortstop managed to nab the runner before he could reach second base.
The catcher quickly threw the ball to third and nabbed the base runner.
She was known for her ability to nab opponents who tried to steal bases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tag out - to put a base runner out by touching them with the ball or glove holding the ball

2. VERB

/ næb / (AmE), / næb / (BrE)
to catch or seize someone, especially to arrest them suddenly or unexpectedly
catch or arrest someone suddenly
informal

Examples

The police managed to nab the thief as he tried to escape through the window.
She was nabbed for shoplifting by the store security guard.
After hours of investigation, detectives finally nabbed the prime suspect.

Synset Examples

the police nabbed the suspected criminals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrest - to take someone into custody, especially by legal authority
  • Antonym: release - to set someone free from custody

Etymology

Perhaps an alteration of 'knab', from a Scandinavian source related to Norwegian 'nabbe' (to seize).

3. VERB

/ næb / (AmE), / næb / (BrE)
to catch or seize someone or something suddenly, especially in order to stop or arrest them
seize or catch suddenly
informal

Examples

The police managed to nab the thief as he tried to escape through the window.
She tried to nab the last piece of cake before anyone noticed.
The security guard nabbed a shoplifter near the exit yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catch - to capture or seize someone or something
  • Synonym: grab - to take hold of something quickly or suddenly
  • Antonym: release - to set free or let go

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in the late 17th century, perhaps imitative.
