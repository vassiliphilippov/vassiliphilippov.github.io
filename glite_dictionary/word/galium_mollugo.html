Galium mollugo

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪliəm məˈluːɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡalɪəm mɒˈluːɡəʊ / (BrE)
A Eurasian herb with loose clusters of small white flowers, commonly known as hedge bedstraw, naturalized in North America.
a Eurasian herb with small white flowers

Examples

Galium mollugo often grows along roadsides and in meadows throughout Europe.
Botanists observed the spread of Galium mollugo into fields across North America.
The delicate white flowers of Galium mollugo make it easy to identify in the wild.

Etymology

From Latin: 'galium' (milky, from the milk-coagulating property) and 'mollugo' (soft herb).
