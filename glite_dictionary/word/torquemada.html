Torquemada

1. PROPN

/ ˌtɔr.kəˈmɑː.də / (AmE), / ˌtɔː.kɪˈmɑː.də / (BrE)
Tomás de Torquemada (1420–1498), the Spanish Dominican friar who served as the first Grand Inquisitor during the Spanish Inquisition and was responsible for the persecution and execution of thousands.
Spanish Grand Inquisitor (1420–1498)

Examples

Many historians view Torquemada as a symbol of religious intolerance.
The name Torquemada remains associated with the brutality of the Spanish Inquisition.
Art and literature often depict Torquemada as the embodiment of fanaticism.

Etymology

Spanish surname; Tomás de Torquemada, from the town name Torquemada in Spain.
