Clathrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæθrəs / (AmE), / ˈklæθrəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Clathraceae, known for their latticed, cage-like fruiting bodies.
genus of cage-like fungi

Examples

Specimens of Clathrus are often found in forested areas after heavy rain.
The strange, cage-like structure of Clathrus mushrooms is unmistakable to mycologists.
Some species in the Clathrus genus emit a strong odor to attract insects for spore dispersal.

Etymology

From Greek klathron (lattice, cage).
