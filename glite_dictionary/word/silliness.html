silliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪli.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being silly or lacking in good sense; foolishness or absurdity
foolish or absurd behavior

Examples

His silliness during the meeting made everyone laugh despite the serious topic.
Sometimes a little silliness is all it takes to brighten a dull day.
The teacher appreciated the children's silliness as it showed their creativity.

Synset Examples

the crowd laughed at the absurdity of the clown's behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious or earnest

Etymology

From silly + -ness, with "silly" originating from Old English *sælig* (happy, fortunate) later shifting to 'foolish.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlinəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪlinəs / (BrE)
behavior or quality marked by lack of seriousness, showing impulsive or scatterbrained actions
lack of seriousness; foolish behavior

Examples

His constant silliness made it hard for the group to focus on their work.
There was a lot of silliness at the party, with people playing games and telling jokes.
She was known for her spontaneous silliness that always made her friends laugh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgement; folly
  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious, not playful or silly

Etymology

From 'silly' (Middle English 'sely', meaning happy, harmless, feeble-minded) + '-ness'.
