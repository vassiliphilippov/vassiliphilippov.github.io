chlorpromazine

1. NOUN

/ klɔrˈproʊməˌziːn / (AmE), / klɔːˈprəʊməziːn / (BrE)
a drug used as an antipsychotic, sedative, and tranquilizer, especially in the treatment of schizophrenia and other mental disorders
antipsychotic and sedative drug
medical

Examples

The psychiatrist prescribed chlorpromazine to help manage her hallucinations and agitation.
Side effects of chlorpromazine can include drowsiness and dry mouth.
Before surgery, the patient received a dose of chlorpromazine as a tranquilizer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thorazine - trade name for chlorpromazine

Etymology

From chloro- (relating to chlorine), and promethazine (another phenothiazine derivative).
