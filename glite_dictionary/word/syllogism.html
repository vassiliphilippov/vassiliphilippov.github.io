syllogism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪl.əˌdʒɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.ə.dʒɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A form of reasoning in which a conclusion is drawn from two given or assumed propositions (premises); especially in deductive logic, an argument consisting of a major and a minor premise and a conclusion.
argument based on two premises

Examples

The philosopher explained the syllogism as a fundamental tool for logical reasoning.
Every classic syllogism has a major premise, a minor premise, and a conclusion.
By constructing a syllogism, she demonstrated that all humans are mortal.

Etymology

From Greek 'syllogismos' (inference, conclusion), from 'syllogizesthai' (to infer), from 'syn-' (together) + 'logizesthai' (to reason).
