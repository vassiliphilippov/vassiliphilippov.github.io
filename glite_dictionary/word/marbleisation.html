marbleisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːrbələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɑːbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of acquiring a texture or appearance resembling that of marble
texture or appearance like marble
british

Examples

The artist achieved remarkable marbleisation on the sculpture's surface.
This wallpaper features a subtle marbleisation that adds elegance to any room.
During metamorphism, the rock undergoes marbleisation and takes on a crystalline pattern similar to true marble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marbling - the process or effect of marking with a streaked or mottled pattern, especially like marble

Etymology

From 'marble' + '-isation', based on the pattern of creating nouns denoting the process of becoming or resembling something, especially used in British English spelling.
