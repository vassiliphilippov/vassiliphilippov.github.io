to collogue

1. VERB

/ kəˈloʊɡ / (AmE), / kəˈləʊɡ / (BrE)
to confer or plot secretly, especially in order to scheme or plan something covertly
confer or plot secretly
archaic

Examples

The two men would collogue under cover of darkness, planning their next move.
Historians believe certain nobles would secretly collogue against the king.
Rumors spread that the merchants had collogued to set unfair prices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conspire - to make secret plans jointly to commit an unlawful or harmful act
  • Antonym: to consult - to seek advice or discussion, typically openly and for legitimate purposes

Etymology

Probably from Scottish, related to French *colloquer* (to talk together).
