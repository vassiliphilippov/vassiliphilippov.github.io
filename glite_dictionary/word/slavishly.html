slavishly

1. ADV

/ ˈsleɪvɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈsleɪvɪʃli / (BrE)
In a way that shows excessive obedience, servility, or uncritical imitation.
in an excessively servile or obedient way

Examples

He slavishly followed every suggestion his mentor made, never questioning her advice.
Some designers slavishly copy the work of others instead of developing their own style.
She slavishly adhered to the outdated rules, even when they no longer made sense.

Synset Examples

his followers slavishly believed in his new diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obediently - in an obedient way
  • Antonym: independently - in an independent manner

Etymology

From 'slavish' + '-ly', with 'slavish' from 'slave'.
