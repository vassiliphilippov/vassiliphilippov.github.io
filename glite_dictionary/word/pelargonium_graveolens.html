Pelargonium graveolens

1. NOUN

/ pɛlɑːrˈgoʊniəm ɡrəˈviːələnz / (AmE), / pɛlɑːˈɡəʊniəm ɡræviːˈɒlɛnz / (BrE)
A species of southern African geranium known for its fragrant lobed leaves and pink flowers, often called rose geranium.
a fragrant species of geranium

Examples

The essential oil of Pelargonium graveolens is prized for its rose-like scent in perfumery.
Gardeners often cultivate Pelargonium graveolens for its aromatic leaves and attractive flowers.
Research shows that Pelargonium graveolens can be used as a natural mosquito repellent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geranium - a genus of flowering plants with lobed leaves and symmetrical flowers

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Pelargonium with Latin graveolens (strong-smelling).
