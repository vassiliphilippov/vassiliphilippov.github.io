chilly

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɪli / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪli / (BrE)
noticeably or unpleasantly cold; uncomfortably cool
noticeably cold; uncomfortably cool

Examples

It's a chilly morning, so remember to wear your jacket.
The room felt chilly after we turned off the heater.
Even in summer, the water in the lake is surprisingly chilly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold - having a low temperature, especially compared to the air or to what is normal
  • Antonym: warm - at a comfortably high temperature, not cold

Etymology

From chill + -y, Middle English 'chille' referring to coldness.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɪli / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪli / (BrE)
(of a person, action, or atmosphere) showing a lack of friendliness or warmth in feeling.
unfriendly or distant in feeling

Examples

Her chilly reply made it clear she was not interested in conversation.
Despite his chilly manner, he actually cared deeply about his team.
The board's chilly reception discouraged further discussion of the proposal.

Synset Examples

a chilly greeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold - lacking affection or emotional warmth
  • Antonym: warm - showing affection, enthusiasm, or friendliness

3. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɪli / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪli / (BrE)
showing little or no warmth or emotion; emotionally cold or distant.
emotionally cold or distant

Examples

The manager gave me a chilly reception when I arrived late to the meeting.
Her response to the bad news was surprisingly chilly, showing no sign of sympathy.
He was known for his chilly demeanor, rarely expressing any warmth or affection.

Synset Examples

a female form in marble — a chilly but ideal medium for depicting abstract virtues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold - lacking warmth or emotion
  • Antonym: warm - showing friendliness, enthusiasm, or affection

Etymology

From chill + -y, Middle English, ultimately from Old English 'ciele' (cold, chill).

4. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪli / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪli / (BrE)
A very hot, finely tapering pepper known for its special pungency; an alternate spelling of 'chili'.
a hot, pungent pepper

Examples

She added a finely sliced chilly to the curry for extra heat.
The market stall sold dried chilly peppers of all shapes and sizes.
A single chilly can make a whole pot of stew spicy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili - a hot pepper of the species Capsicum, also spelled 'chilli'
  • Degree Lower: bell pepper - a mild, sweet pepper of the species Capsicum annuum
  • Degree Higher: habanero - an extremely hot variety of chili pepper

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'chili', from Nahuatl 'chīlli' via Spanish 'chile'.
