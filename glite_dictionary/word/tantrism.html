Tantrism

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæn.trɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈtan.trɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A religious and philosophical tradition emphasizing the unity of self and the universe, often involving rituals, mantras, and meditation, and influential in Hinduism, Buddhism, and especially Tibetan Buddhism.
Indian religious and philosophical tradition

Examples

Many Western scholars have studied Tantrism to understand its influence on Buddhist traditions.
Practices associated with Tantrism include the use of mantras and visualizations for spiritual development.
The rituals of Tantrism are often kept secret and are initiated only to advanced practitioners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tantra - A scripture or doctrine central to Tantrism

Etymology

From Sanskrit tantra (loom, framework, doctrine) + -ism.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæntrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈtæntrɪzəm / (BrE)
A movement within Buddhism and Hinduism emphasizing mystical practices, rituals, and esoteric teachings.
mystical movement in Buddhism and Hinduism

Examples

Many scholars study Tantrism to understand its rituals and philosophical perspectives.
His interest in Buddhist Tantrism led him to travel through Tibet and Nepal.
The practice of Tantrism often involves secret mantras and symbolic gestures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tantra - sacred texts or doctrines forming the basis of Tantrism

Etymology

From Sanskrit *tantra* (loom, framework, doctrine) + English suffix -ism.
