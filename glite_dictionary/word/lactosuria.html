lactosuria

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæk.toʊˈsjʊr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌlæk.təʊˈsjʊə.ri.ə / (BrE)
the presence of lactose in the urine, which may occur during pregnancy or lactation
presence of lactose in the urine
medical

Examples

Routine screening revealed lactosuria in the pregnant patient.
The condition of lactosuria is most often seen during late pregnancy or breastfeeding.
Doctors may check for lactosuria if unusual symptoms are reported during lactation.

Etymology

From Latin 'lactis' (milk) and 'uria' (urine condition), modeled on medical terminology.
