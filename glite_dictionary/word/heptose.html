heptose

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛpˌtoʊs / (AmE), / ˈhɛptəʊs / (BrE)
a monosaccharide sugar containing seven carbon atoms per molecule
seven-carbon monosaccharide

Examples

Rare in nature, heptose sugars are sometimes found in bacterial cell walls.
The scientist isolated a heptose from the sample using chromatography.
Understanding the structure of a heptose is important for biochemistry students.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hexose - a monosaccharide with six carbon atoms
  • Degree Higher: octose - a monosaccharide with eight carbon atoms

Etymology

From Greek 'hepta-' (seven) + '-ose' (a chemical suffix for sugars).
