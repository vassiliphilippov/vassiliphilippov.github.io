amalgam

1. NOUN

/ əˈmælɡəm / (AmE), / əˈmalɡəm / (BrE)
an alloy of mercury with another metal, especially silver, commonly used as a dental filling material
alloy of mercury with another metal, especially for dental fillings
medical

Examples

The dentist used an amalgam to fill the cavity in my tooth.
Many dental fillings were once made from a silver amalgam.
Mercury amalgam has been widely used in both dentistry and chemistry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alloy - a mixture of metals

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'amalgama', from Greek 'malagma' (softening agent).

2. NOUN

/ əˈmælɡəm / (AmE), / əˈmalɡəm / (BrE)
a mixture or blend of different elements, particularly ideas, styles, or components
mixture or blend of elements

Examples

The new novel is an amalgam of science fiction and detective genres.
Her painting style is an amalgam of abstraction and realism.
Modern jazz is often seen as an amalgam of musical traditions from around the world.

Synset Examples

his theory is an amalgam of earlier ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blend - a mixture of different things that have been combined
  • Synonym: mixture - a combination of different things

Etymology

From Medieval Latin amalgama (alloy of mercury), from Arabic al-malgham.
