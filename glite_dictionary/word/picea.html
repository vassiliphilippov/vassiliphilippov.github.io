Picea

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.si.ə / (AmE), / ˈpiː.si.ə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees known as spruces, found in temperate and Arctic regions and widely used for timber and ornament.
genus of evergreen spruces

Examples

The Siberian Picea forests are a crucial part of the taiga ecosystem.
Several species of Picea are commonly planted as Christmas trees.
Botanists collected samples from a mature Picea in the high-altitude region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spruce - evergreen tree of the genus Picea

Etymology

From Latin *Picea* (spruce), from *pix* (pitch, resin).

2. NOUN

/ paɪˈsiːə / (AmE), / pɪˈsiːə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen coniferous trees commonly known as spruces, native to temperate and Arctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of spruce trees

Examples

The Picea genus includes many species of spruce found across Europe, Asia, and North America.
Foresters often plant Picea trees for timber and reforestation projects.
Botanists have identified more than 30 different Picea species worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spruce - common name for trees in the genus Picea

Etymology

From Latin Picea (the name of the spruce tree).
