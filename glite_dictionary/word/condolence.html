condolence

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdoʊləns / (AmE), / kənˈdəʊləns / (BrE)
an expression of sympathy, especially on the occasion of a death
expression of sympathy for someone's loss

Examples

She sent a heartfelt condolence card to her friend after the funeral.
Many people offered their condolences to the family during the memorial service.
I would like to express my deepest condolences for your loss.

Synset Examples

they sent their condolences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sympathy - feelings of pity and sorrow for someone else's misfortune

Etymology

From Latin condolēre (to suffer together), from con- (together) + dolere (to grieve).
