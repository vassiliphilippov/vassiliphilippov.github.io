trente-et-quarante

1. NOUN

/ trɑn(t)ˌeɪ ˈkɑːrɑːnt / (AmE), / tʁɑ̃t.e.ka.ʁɑ̃t / (BrE)
a gambling card game of French origin, played with two rows of cards, in which players bet on the color of cards or which row's total will be closer to thirty-one
French card game involving betting on totals

Examples

He spent hours at the casino playing trente-et-quarante with seasoned gamblers.
The rules of trente-et-quarante are easy to learn but require strategy to master.
In 19th-century Europe, trente-et-quarante was a favorite pastime among aristocrats.

Etymology

French, meaning 'thirty and forty,' from the point-count ranges used in the game.
