to re-equip

1. VERB

/ ˌriˈɪkwɪp / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɪkwɪp / (BrE)
to provide with new or additional equipment, especially after removing old or outdated equipment
equip again; provide new equipment

Examples

After the renovation, the company decided to re-equip the entire factory with modern machinery.
The school will re-equip its science labs next year to meet new safety standards.
Due to outdated computers, the IT department had to re-equip every workstation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to equip - to supply with necessary items for a particular purpose

Etymology

Formed from the prefix re- (again) and the verb 'equip' (to provide with equipment), from French 'équiper'.
