contraction

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / kənˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of becoming smaller or more compressed
process of becoming smaller or compressed

Examples

The contraction of the metal caused it to crack in the cold.
During exercise, muscle contraction increases blood flow.
Economic downturns often lead to the contraction of the job market.

Synset Examples

the contraction of a gas on cooling

Related Terms

  • Antonym: expansion - the process of becoming larger or more extensive

Etymology

From Latin contractio (a drawing together), from contrahere (to draw together, shorten).

4. NOUN

/ kənˈtræk.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˈtræk.ʃən / (BrE)
(physiology) The tightening or shortening of a muscle or muscle fiber, resulting in movement or tension within a part of the body.
shortening or tightening of a muscle

Examples

The contraction of the heart muscle pumps blood throughout the body.
During exercise, repeated contraction and relaxation of muscles enable movement.
Labor pains are caused by the contraction of uterine muscles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relaxation - the process of a muscle returning to its normal length after contraction

Etymology

From Latin 'contractio' (a drawing together), from 'contrahere' (to draw together).
