flagship

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæɡˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈflæɡʃɪp / (BrE)
the ship in a fleet that carries the commanding officer and flies their distinguishing flag
commander's ship in a fleet

Examples

The admiral transferred to the flagship before the battle began.
Most of the important meetings were held on the fleet's flagship.
HMS Victory served as Admiral Nelson's flagship at the Battle of Trafalgar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command ship - a ship from which a commander exercises control

Etymology

From 'flag' (banner or ensign) + 'ship', first used in English in the 17th century for the ship from which a fleet is commanded.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflæɡˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈflæɡʃɪp / (BrE)
the most important or prestigious product, company, or publication in a group
most important member of a group

Examples

This car is the automaker's flagship model, offering the latest features and technology.
Their flagship store on Fifth Avenue attracts thousands of visitors each year.
The company launched its flagship smartphone to set a new industry standard.

Synset Examples

it is their flagship newspaper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showpiece - something that is the best or most outstanding example of its kind
  • Antonym: subsidiary - a company controlled by another (contrasting with the leading one)

Etymology

Originally referred to the ship carrying the commanding officer's flag in a fleet.
