tantrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæntrəm / (AmE), / ˈtæntrəm / (BrE)
an uncontrolled outburst of anger and frustration, typically in a young child
a sudden outburst of anger

Examples

When her mother refused to buy her the toy, the child threw a tantrum in the store.
Despite being an adult, he sometimes has a tantrum when things don't go his way.
The teacher remained calm while the toddler had a tantrum during class.

Synset Examples

he had a fit
she threw a tantrum
he made a scene

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fit - a sudden attack of uncontrollable emotion, especially anger
  • Antonym: calm - a state of being free from anger or upset

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in the early 18th century.
