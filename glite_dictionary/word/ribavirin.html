ribavirin

1. NOUN

/ ˌraɪbəˈvaɪrɪn / (AmE), / ˌraɪbəˈvʌɪrɪn / (BrE)
an antiviral drug used to treat serious viral infections, sometimes administered by inhalation or other methods
antiviral drug for serious viral infections
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed ribavirin as part of the treatment for the viral infection.
Some patients receive ribavirin through inhalation to help combat severe respiratory illnesses.
Studies have shown that ribavirin can be effective against certain types of viral infections.

Etymology

From ribose (a type of sugar) + vir- (from 'virus') + -in (chemical suffix).
