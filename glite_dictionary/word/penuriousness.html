penuriousness

1. NOUN

/ pəˈnʊriəsnəs / (AmE), / pɪˈnjʊəriəsnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of having very little money; extreme poverty or stinginess
the state of being very poor or miserly

Examples

Her penuriousness was evident in her reluctance to spend even on necessities.
The penuriousness of the times forced many families to seek assistance.
Despite his penuriousness, he donated generously to charity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor
  • Antonym: affluence - the state of having a great deal of money; wealth

Etymology

From Latin 'penuria' (want, scarcity) via the adjective 'penurious'.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈnʊri.əs.nəs / (AmE), / pɪˈnjʊəri.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being extremely unwilling to spend money; extreme stinginess
extreme reluctance to spend money

Examples

His penuriousness was legendary—he would never spend a cent unless absolutely necessary.
The family's penuriousness made even simple pleasures seem extravagant.
Despite his wealth, his penuriousness prevented him from helping those in need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stinginess - the quality of being unwilling to give or spend; meanness with money
  • Antonym: generosity - the quality of being generous and giving freely

Etymology

From Latin 'penuriosus' (needy, poor), via penurious + -ness.
