Cymatiidae

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌmætiˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / sɪˌmatiˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large predatory sea snails, known as tritons, belonging to the class Gastropoda
family of predatory sea snails (tritons)

Examples

The Cymatiidae are primarily found in warm, tropical oceans around the world.
Many members of the Cymatiidae family play an important role as predators in marine ecosystems.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Cymatiidae while exploring coral reefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triton - large predatory marine snail of the family Cymatiidae

Etymology

From New Latin Cymatiidae, based on genus Cymatium, a group of sea snails.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˌmætiˈaɪdi / (AmE), / sɪˌmætiˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of predatory sea snails, commonly known as tritons, belonging to the class Gastropoda
family of predatory sea snails (tritons)

Examples

The Cymatiidae are known for their beautifully sculpted shells found in tropical and temperate seas.
Researchers are studying the feeding behavior of the Cymatiidae to better understand marine ecosystems.
Collectors prize shells of the Cymatiidae for their ornate and distinctive appearances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triton - a large marine snail with a spiral shell, common name for members of family Cymatiidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Cymatium + -idae (a zoological family suffix).
