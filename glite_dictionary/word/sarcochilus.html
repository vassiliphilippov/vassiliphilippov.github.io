Sarcochilus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑːrkəˈkaɪləs / (AmE), / ˌsɑːkəˈkɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of small epiphytic or lithophytic orchids native to Australia, Polynesia, and Southeast Asia, known for clustered foliage and colorful arching racemes of flowers.
a genus of orchids with clustered foliage and arching flowers

Examples

The Sarcochilus orchids are prized for their vibrant colors and ease of cultivation.
She specializes in propagating rare species of Sarcochilus in her greenhouse.
Botanists have documented a new variety of Sarcochilus in the rainforests of Queensland.

Etymology

From Greek 'sarx' (flesh) and 'cheilos' (lip), referring to the flower's thick lip.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɑːrkəˈkaɪləs / (AmE), / ˌsɑːkəˈkaɪləs / (BrE)
a genus of diminutive epiphytic or lithophytic orchids with short-stemmed clumped foliage and arching sprays of colorful flowers, native to Australia, Polynesia, and southeastern Asia.
a genus of small epiphytic orchids

Examples

The Sarcochilus thrives in humid Australian forests where it clings to rocks and tree branches.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Sarcochilus genus in southeastern Asia.
Many orchid enthusiasts admire the vibrant blooms of Sarcochilus in their collections.

Etymology

From Greek *sarx* (flesh) + *cheilos* (lip), referring to the fleshy lip of the flower.
