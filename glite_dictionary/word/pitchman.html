pitchman

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtʃˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃˌman / (BrE)
A person who aggressively promotes or sells products, typically using fast, persuasive talk, often in public places or on television.
an aggressive salesman; showy product promoter
american

Examples

The pitchman dazzled the crowd with his energetic demonstration of the new kitchen gadget.
Late-night infomercials often feature a pitchman promising extraordinary results from simple products.
He started his career as a pitchman at county fairs, selling miracle cleaners to curious onlookers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salesperson - a person whose job is to sell products or services
  • Synonym: huckster - a seller who uses showy or aggressive methods

Etymology

From 'pitch' (selling talk or promotion) + 'man', arising in American English in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtʃ.mæn / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃ.mæn / (BrE)
a man who travels around, especially at fairs or markets, selling goods by attracting attention and delivering a persuasive sales talk
man selling goods publicly, often at fairs

Examples

The pitchman drew a crowd as he demonstrated his new kitchen gadget at the fair.
People stopped to watch the pitchman enthusiastically promote his wares on the busy street corner.
For years, he worked as a carnival pitchman, captivating audiences with his lively patter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawker - person who travels about selling goods
  • Synonym: salesman - person whose job is selling things
  • Antonym: customer - person who buys goods or services

Etymology

From 'pitch' (to promote), combined with 'man'.
