Hippocrepis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpoʊˈkrɛpɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈkriːpɪs / (BrE)
a genus of Old World herbs or subshrubs in the legume family, commonly known as horseshoe vetch
genus of Old World herbs; horseshoe vetch

Examples

The flowers of Hippocrepis are a delicate yellow, attracting many pollinators in spring.
Botanists studied Hippocrepis species to understand their adaptation to dry soil conditions.
Conservation efforts have focused on protecting the rare habitats of Hippocrepis in southern Europe.

Etymology

From Greek hippos (horse) + krepis (shoe), referring to the shape of the seed pods.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɪp.oʊˈkrɛp.ɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɪp.əʊˈkrɛp.ɪs / (BrE)
a genus of Old World herbs or subshrubs in the pea family, including the horseshoe vetch
genus of Old World herbs (horseshoe vetch)

Examples

The botanist identified the plant as Hippocrepis, common in Mediterranean regions.
Several species within the Hippocrepis genus are cultivated for their attractive yellow flowers.
Horseshoe vetch, known scientifically as Hippocrepis comosa, is important for certain butterflies.

Etymology

From Greek hippokrépis (horseshoe-shaped, from hippos 'horse' + krepis 'shoe').
