Languedoc-Roussillon

1. PROPN

/ ˌlænɡəˌdɑk ˌruːsiˈjoʊn / (AmE), / ˌlɒ̃ɡdɔk ˌrusijɔ̃ / (BrE)
a former administrative region in south-central France, known for its medieval Occitan dialect and Mediterranean coastline
former region of south-central France

Examples

She spent a summer in Languedoc-Roussillon, exploring its medieval towns and vineyards.
The wines produced in Languedoc-Roussillon are famous worldwide for their variety and quality.
Many tourists are drawn to Languedoc-Roussillon for its beautiful Mediterranean beaches.

Etymology

From French 'Languedoc', referring to the Occitan language ('langue d'oc'), and 'Roussillon', a historic province.
