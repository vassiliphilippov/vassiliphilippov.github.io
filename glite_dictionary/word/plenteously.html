plenteously

1. ADV

/ ˈplɛn.ti.əs.li / (AmE), / ˈplɛn.tɪ.əs.li / (BrE)
In a bountiful or abundant manner; with great plenty.
in a bountiful or abundant manner

Examples

Rains fell plenteously through the night, saturating the fields.
The orchard produced fruit plenteously this season, enough for the whole village.
She gave plenteously to charity, helping hundreds of families in need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plentifully - in a plentiful manner; abundant

Etymology

From 'plenteous' + '-ly', with 'plenteous' deriving from Old French 'plenteus', from Latin 'plentiosus'.
