oxlip

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑks.lɪp / (AmE), / ˈɒks.lɪp / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant of the primrose family, with clusters of yellow flowers on one side of the stem.
a Eurasian primrose with yellow flowers

Examples

In the woods, the oxlip blooms in early spring among other wildflowers.
The countryside was dotted with oxlips swaying gently in the breeze.
She admired the delicate yellow petals of the oxlip during her walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primrose - a plant with pale yellow flowers, closely related to the oxlip
  • Synonym: cowslip - a similar Eurasian plant in the primrose family

Etymology

From Middle English *oxslippe*, likely from 'ox' + 'slip', referring to its growing in pastures grazed by oxen.
