to demagnetize

1. VERB

/ diˈmæɡnəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / diˈmæɡnɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to remove or reduce the magnetic properties of something
remove magnetic properties

Examples

You need to demagnetize the screwdriver before using it on sensitive electronics.
The technician demagnetized the old television screen to fix the color distortion.
Modern devices may include a feature to automatically demagnetize their hard drives.

Synset Examples

demagnetize the iron shavings
they degaussed the ship

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to magnetize - give magnetic properties to; make magnetic
  • Synonym: to degauss - remove unwanted magnetism from a device

Etymology

From de- (removal of) + magnetize.

2. VERB

/ diˈmæɡnəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / diˈmaɡnɪtʌɪz / (BrE)
to erase data from a magnetic storage device by removing its magnetic properties
erase data from magnetic storage device

Examples

Before selling the old hard drive, the technician decided to demagnetize it to ensure no data remained.
You should demagnetize magnetic tapes before disposal to prevent sensitive information leaks.
The IT department regularly demagnetizes outdated storage disks for security reasons.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to magnetize - make magnetic; impart magnetic properties

Etymology

From de- (removal) + magnetize; related to magnet and magnetic.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01000807-v
Synset POS: v
