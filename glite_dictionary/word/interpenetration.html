interpenetration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˌpɛnɪˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˌpɛnɪˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which two or more things penetrate, diffuse, or permeate each other mutually.
mutual penetration or diffusion

Examples

The interpenetration of cultures can lead to the emergence of entirely new traditions.
Some philosophers discuss the interpenetration of mind and body in consciousness.
The artists explored the interpenetration of colors in their abstract paintings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penetration - the act of piercing or passing into or through something

Etymology

From Latin 'inter-' (between, mutually) + 'penetratio' (penetration).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˌpɛnəˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˌpɛnɪˈtreɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the action or process of penetrating or passing through each other or being penetrated mutually
mutual penetration or mingling

Examples

The interpenetration of cultures can lead to new forms of artistic expression.
Their ideas achieved a fascinating interpenetration, enriching the conversation for everyone involved.
In some philosophical systems, the interpenetration of mind and matter is a central theme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penetration - the act or process of entering or piercing something

Etymology

Formed from 'inter-' (between) and 'penetration', ultimately from Latin *inter* (between) + *penetratio* (a piercing).
