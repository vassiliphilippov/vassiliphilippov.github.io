human chorionic gonadotropin

1. NOUN

/ ˌhjuːmən kɔːˈriːɒnɪk gəˌnædəˈtroʊpɪn / (AmE), / ˌhjuːmən kɒːˈriːɒnɪk gəˌnædəˈtrəʊpɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the placenta early in pregnancy, used as the basis for certain pregnancy tests
hormone detected in pregnancy tests
medical

Examples

The level of human chorionic gonadotropin rises rapidly during the first trimester of pregnancy.
Doctors use human chorionic gonadotropin measurements to confirm pregnancy and monitor its progress.
A positive result on the test indicates the presence of human chorionic gonadotropin in the urine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hCG - abbreviation of human chorionic gonadotropin

Etymology

From Greek 'chorion' (membrane) + 'gonad' (reproductive organ) + 'tropin' (stimulating effect).
