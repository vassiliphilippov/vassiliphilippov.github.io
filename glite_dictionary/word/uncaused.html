uncaused

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkɔzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɔːzd / (BrE)
having no cause or apparent cause; not produced by something else
having no cause or apparent cause

Examples

Some philosophers argue that the universe is an uncaused event.
The phenomenon appeared entirely uncaused and unexpected to the scientists.
He struggled to explain the seemingly uncaused emotions that surfaced.

Synset Examples

a causeless miracle
fortuitous encounters — strange accidents of fortune
we cannot regard artistic invention as … uncaused and unrelated to the times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: causeless - without a cause; lacking a reason or explanation
  • Antonym: caused - brought about by a cause; having a cause

Etymology

From un- (not) + caused (from cause, ultimately from Latin causa, meaning 'reason' or 'cause').
