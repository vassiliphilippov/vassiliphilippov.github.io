inspissation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌspɪˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌspɪˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming thickened, especially through the removal of water
thickening by dehydration

Examples

The inspissation of mucus in the airways can complicate respiratory diseases.
Evaporation led to the gradual inspissation of the syrup in the pan.
Medical researchers studied the inspissation of fluids under various laboratory conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thickening - the process or result of making or becoming thick or thicker

Etymology

From Latin 'inspissare' (to thicken), from 'in-' (into) + 'spissus' (thick).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnspəˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnspɪˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming thickened or of causing a fluid to become thick or viscous
process of thickening

Examples

The inspissation of the solution was achieved by gentle heating.
Drought conditions led to the inspissation of clay deposits in the valley.
In certain diseases, mucus inspissation can block airways and complicate breathing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thickening - the process of becoming thicker

Etymology

From Latin 'inspissare' (to thicken), from 'in-' (in) + 'spissus' (thick, dense).
