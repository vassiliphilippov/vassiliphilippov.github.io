Anemone

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛməni / (AmE), / əˈnɛməni / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs with tuberous roots and showy flowers, found in temperate regions of the Northern and Southern Hemispheres.
genus of perennial flowering herbs

Examples

The garden was full of blooming Anemone, adding vibrant color in early spring.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Anemone genus in the mountain region.
Many gardeners choose Anemone for their hardiness and striking flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windflower - another name for plants of the genus Anemone

Etymology

From Latin 'anemone', from Ancient Greek 'anemōnē' (windflower).
