anemone

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛməni / (AmE), / əˈnɛməni / (BrE)
a woodland plant of the genus Anemone, known for its showy flowers and finely divided leaves.
a flowering woodland plant of the genus Anemone

Examples

A cluster of anemone bloomed in the shade beneath the old oak tree.
She added purple anemones to her bouquet for a splash of color.
Botanists admire the whorled leaves and delicate petals of the anemone.

Etymology

From Latin anemone, from Greek anemōnē (windflower).

2. NOUN

/ əˈnɛməni / (AmE), / əˈnɛməni / (BrE)
A sea-dwelling animal that resembles a flower, with a ring of tentacles around its mouth, related to corals but having no hard skeleton.
marine animal with ring of tentacles

Examples

The clownfish darted among the swaying tentacles of the anemone.
Unlike corals, an anemone does not form a hard skeleton.
An anemone attaches itself to rocks or coral reefs in shallow seas.

Etymology

From Greek anemōnē (windflower).
