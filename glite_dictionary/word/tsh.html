TSH

1. NOUN

/ ti ɛs eɪtʃ / (AmE), / tiː ɛs eɪtʃ / (BrE)
an anterior pituitary hormone that stimulates the activity and function of the thyroid gland
a hormone stimulating the thyroid gland
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a blood test to check my TSH levels.
High TSH can indicate an underactive thyroid gland.
She was diagnosed with hypothyroidism based on her abnormal TSH readings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyroid-stimulating hormone - the full term for the hormone abbreviated as TSH

Etymology

Abbreviation of thyroid-stimulating hormone.
