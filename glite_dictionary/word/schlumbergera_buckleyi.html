Schlumbergera buckleyi

1. NOUN

/ ʃlʌmˈbɜrɡərə bʌkˈliːaɪ / (AmE), / ʃlʌmˈbɜːɡərə bʌkˈliːaɪ / (BrE)
A species of epiphytic cactus native to Brazil, widely grown as a houseplant for its jointed flat stems and rose-purple, winter-blooming flowers.
species of Brazilian epiphytic cactus; houseplant with winter blooms

Examples

My grandmother's Schlumbergera buckleyi blooms beautifully every December by the window.
The vibrant flowers of Schlumbergera buckleyi make it a popular choice among indoor gardeners.
We learned how to propagate Schlumbergera buckleyi from cuttings in our botany class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christmas cactus - common name for Schlumbergera buckleyi

Etymology

Genus named after Frédéric Schlumberger, a French cactus collector; species name from Samuel Buckley, American botanist.
