to perambulating

1. VERB

/ pəˈræmbjəˌleɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / pəˈræmbjəleɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
walking or strolling around, especially in a leisurely or aimless way
walking around; strolling

Examples

The couple were perambulating through the quiet park at sunset.
She spent the morning perambulating the halls of the old museum, lost in thought.
Groups of students could be seen perambulating around campus between classes.

Synset Examples

perambulating nursemaids with their charges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strolling - walking in a slow, relaxed way

Etymology

From Latin 'perambulare', meaning 'to walk through'.
