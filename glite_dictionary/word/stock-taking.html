stock-taking

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑkˌteɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌteɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making a detailed list of merchandise or supplies on hand, especially in a business or store
checking and listing inventory or supplies

Examples

The shop closed early for its annual stock-taking to count all goods on the shelves.
Regular stock-taking helps managers keep track of inventory levels and avoid shortages.
After a thorough stock-taking, they discovered several items had gone missing.

Synset Examples

an inventory may be necessary to see if anything is missing
they held an inventory every month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inventory - a complete list of items such as merchandise or supplies on hand

Etymology

From 'stock' (inventory) + 'taking' (the act of taking account); used since the 18th century in business.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɑkˌteɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌteɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of carefully reviewing and assessing a situation, position, or outlook, often to make decisions or changes.
reappraisal or review of a situation

Examples

After the failed project, the team conducted a thorough stock-taking to identify areas for improvement.
The end of the year is a good time for personal stock-taking and setting new goals.
A period of stock-taking followed the unexpected results of the election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - a formal assessment or examination of something
  • Synonym: assessment - the act of evaluating or appraising a situation or individual

Etymology

Originally from 'stock' (inventory) + 'taking', referring to counting goods in a store, later used figuratively for assessment.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05755760-n
Synset POS: n
