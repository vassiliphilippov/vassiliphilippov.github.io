Dag Hammarskjold

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɑːɡ ˌhɑːmərˈʃoʊld / (AmE), / ˈdɑːɡ ˌhaməˈʃœld / (BrE)
(1905–1961) Swedish diplomat who served as Secretary-General of the United Nations and played a major role in expanding its peacekeeping operations.
Swedish UN Secretary-General (1905–1961)

Examples

Dag Hammarskjold was widely respected for his dedication to international peace and cooperation.
The legacy of Dag Hammarskjold continues to influence the work of the United Nations today.
Many historians credit Dag Hammarskjold with shaping modern peacekeeping methods.
