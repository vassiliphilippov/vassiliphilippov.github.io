shoveler

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌvələr / (AmE), / ˈʃʌvələ / (BrE)
a person whose job is to move, dig, or clear material using a shovel
person who shovels for work

Examples

The shoveler worked tirelessly to clear the driveway after the heavy snowfall.
He was employed as a shoveler in the coal mines during the winter.
Each shoveler on the construction site wore protective gear while moving gravel.

Synset Examples

a shoveler of coal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digger - a person who digs, especially as an occupation
  • Synonym: loader - someone who loads materials, often with a shovel

Etymology

From 'shovel' + '-er', meaning one who shovels.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌvələr / (AmE), / ˈʃʌvələ / (BrE)
A freshwater duck found in the Northern Hemisphere, known for its broad, flat bill used for sifting food from the water.
freshwater duck with a broad bill

Examples

Among the various ducks at the lake, the shoveler is easily recognized by its distinctive bill.
I watched a pair of shovelers filter food from the surface of the pond.
The northern shoveler is a common sight in wetlands during migration season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duck - a waterbird with a broad blunt bill, short legs, webbed feet, and a waddling gait
