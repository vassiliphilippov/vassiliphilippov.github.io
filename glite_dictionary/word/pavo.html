Pavo

1. PROPN

/ ˈpeɪvoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːvəʊ / (BrE)
A small constellation in the southern sky, located between Tucana and Ara.
southern constellation

Examples

Astronomers often study the stars in Pavo for their unusual patterns.
Pavo is best seen from the Southern Hemisphere during the summer months.
The constellation Pavo was first catalogued by Dutch navigators in the late 16th century.

Etymology

From Latin 'pavo' meaning 'peacock', named for the bird's resemblance in the star pattern.
