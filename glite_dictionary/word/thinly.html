thinly

1. ADV

/ ˈθɪnli / (AmE), / ˈθɪnli / (BrE)
in a way that forms or covers only a small quantity or extent; sparsely or in a thin layer.
sparsely; in a thin layer

Examples

Spread the butter thinly on the toast for a lighter flavor.
The garden was thinly planted, allowing sunlight to reach the soil.
She sliced the vegetables thinly for the salad.

Synset Examples

spread the margarine thinly over the meat
apply paint lightly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thickly - in a thick or dense manner; opposite of thinly

2. ADV

/ ˈθɪnli / (AmE), / ˈθɪnli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows little force, sincerity, or enthusiasm.
without sincerity or enthusiasm

Examples

She thinly disguised her disappointment when she heard the news.
He thinly smiled at the joke but remained otherwise silent.
The apology was offered thinly and failed to convince anyone.

Synset Examples

smiled thinly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faintly - in a way that is not strong or clear
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00480070-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈθɪnli / (AmE), / ˈθɪnli / (BrE)
in a way that is spread out sparsely or with wide gaps; not thickly or densely
sparsely; not thickly or densely

Examples

The grass was thinly scattered across the hillside, leaving patches of bare earth.
A few houses were thinly spaced along the long country road.
The sauce should be spread thinly over the pizza to avoid sogginess.

Synset Examples

thinly overgrown mountainside

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thickly - in a dense or closely packed manner

Etymology

From the adjective 'thin' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.
