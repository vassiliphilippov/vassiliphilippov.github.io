Hymenaea

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈmiːniə / (AmE), / hɪˈmiːniə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American trees in the family Fabaceae, known for producing valuable timber and resin
genus of tropical American timber trees

Examples

The wood of Hymenaea is highly prized for its strength and durability.
Several species in the Hymenaea genus produce copal resin, used in varnishes.
Researchers discovered a new species of Hymenaea in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

From Greek Hymenaios (Hymen), the Greek god of marriage, reflecting the double leaflets resembling a pair.
