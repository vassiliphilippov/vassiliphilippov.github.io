Cladrastis kentukea

1. NOUN

/ kləˈdræstɪs kɛnˈtuːkiə / (AmE), / kləˈdrastɪs kɛnˈtjuːkiə / (BrE)
A small, round-topped deciduous tree native to North America, known for its showy white flower clusters and hardwood that yields a yellow dye; also called the American yellowwood.
species of deciduous tree, American yellowwood

Examples

The garden featured a beautiful Cladrastis kentukea that bloomed every spring with cascades of white flowers.
Carpenters value Cladrastis kentukea for its durable yellow timber, which is used in fine woodworking.
Botanists often refer to Cladrastis kentukea as American yellowwood because of the color of its heartwood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellowwood - common name for Cladrastis kentukea

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Cladrastis with 'kentukea', referring to Kentucky, USA, where the tree is found.
