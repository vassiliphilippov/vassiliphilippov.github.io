Kahoolawe

1. PROPN

/ kɑːˌhoʊ.oʊˈlɑːveɪ / (AmE), / kɑːˌhəʊ.əʊˈlɑːveɪ / (BrE)
An island of the Hawaiian archipelago, located in south-central Hawaii, known for its dry climate and past military use.
island in south-central Hawaii

Examples

The state has been working to restore Kahoolawe after years of military training damaged the island's ecosystem.
Tourists are not permitted to freely visit Kahoolawe due to ongoing restoration efforts.
The history of Kahoolawe is significant to Native Hawaiians and environmentalists alike.

Etymology

From Hawaiian Kahoʻolawe, meaning 'the carrying away' or 'the removal.'

2. PROPN

/ kɑːˌhoʊ.oʊˈlɑːveɪ / (AmE), / kɑːˌhəʊ.əˈlɑːveɪ / (BrE)
An island in south-central Hawaii, known for being the smallest of the eight main volcanic islands and for its historical significance.
island in south-central Hawaii

Examples

The island of Kahoolawe was once used as a military training ground.
Environmentalists have worked to restore Kahoolawe's natural habitat since the 1990s.
Few people have visited Kahoolawe due to its remote location and limited access.

Etymology

From Hawaiian Kahoʻolawe, the native name for the island.
