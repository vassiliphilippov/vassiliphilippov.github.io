doubtful

1. ADJ

/ ˈdaʊtfəl / (AmE), / ˈdaʊt.fəl / (BrE)
feeling or showing uncertainty or a lack of conviction about something; unsettled in mind or opinion
uncertain or unsettled in mind

Examples

She gave me a doubtful look, unsure whether to believe my story.
He sounded doubtful about whether the project would succeed under current conditions.
Even after the explanation, I'm still doubtful about the results.

Synset Examples

drew a few tentative conclusions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: certain - sure, confident, without doubt
  • Synonym: unsure - not certain or confident

Etymology

From Old French douteus, from Latin dubitābilis (doubtful), from dubitāre (to doubt).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdaʊtfəl / (AmE), / ˈdaʊtfəl / (BrE)
Not sure or certain; feeling or causing doubt or uncertainty.
uncertain; causing or feeling doubt

Examples

She was doubtful that her idea would be accepted by the team.
It seems doubtful that the project will be finished on time.
He gave a doubtful look when asked about the missing report.

Synset Examples

they were doubtful that the cord would hold
it was doubtful whether she would be admitted
dubious about agreeing to go

Related Terms

  • Antonym: certain - having no doubt; sure
  • Synonym: uncertain - not able to be relied on; not known or definite

Etymology

From Old French douter (to doubt) + -ful (full of).

3. ADJ

/ ˈdaʊtfəl / (AmE), / ˈdaʊtfʊl / (BrE)
Open to doubt or suspicion; considered unlikely or uncertain to be true or genuine.
open to doubt or suspicion

Examples

Many investors are doubtful about the accuracy of the company's financial statements.
His explanation for returning home so late sounded doubtful to his parents.
The authenticity of the painting remains doubtful despite further investigation.

Synset Examples

the candidate's doubtful past
he has a dubious record indeed
what one found uncertain the other found dubious or downright false
it was more than dubitable whether the friend was as influential as she thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dubious - hesitating or doubting; not to be relied upon
  • Antonym: certain - free from doubt or confusion

Etymology

From Old French *douteux*, from Latin *dubitābilis* (doubtful).
