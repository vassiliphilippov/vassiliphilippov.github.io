Hypodermatidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpoʊˌdɜːrməˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊˌdɜːməˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large parasitic flies, commonly known as warble flies, whose larvae infest the skin or tissue of mammals
family of parasitic warble flies

Examples

Cattle farmers often have to deal with infestations caused by Hypodermatidae larvae under the animals' skin.
The life cycle of Hypodermatidae involves the larvae migrating through the host's tissues.
Veterinarians are trained to recognize the symptoms of Hypodermatidae infections in livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warble fly - a large fly whose larvae develop beneath the skin of mammals

Etymology

From New Latin Hypodermatidae, formed from genus name Hypoderma and suffix -idae (denoting animal families).

2. NOUN

/ haɪpoʊˌdɜrməˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / hʌɪˌpə(ʊ)dɜːməˈtɪdɪiː / (BrE)
A family of large flies (warble flies) whose larvae are parasitic on mammals, causing lumps or warbles under the skin.
family of parasitic warble flies

Examples

The larvae of Hypodermatidae can cause significant damage to the hides of cattle.
Farmers are concerned about infestations of Hypodermatidae in their livestock.
Veterinarians monitor for symptoms of Hypodermatidae activity, such as lumps beneath the skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warble fly - large fly whose larvae develop under the skin of mammals

Etymology

From Greek hypo- (under) + derma (skin), with Latin taxonomic suffix -idae for animal families.
