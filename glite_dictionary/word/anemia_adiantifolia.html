Anemia adiantifolia

1. NOUN

/ əˈniːmiə ˌædiˌæntɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / əˈniːmiə ˌædiˌæntɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A fern native to Florida, the West Indies, and Central America, characterized by a rhizome densely covered in brown hairs.
fern native to Florida and Central America

Examples

The Anemia adiantifolia thrives in moist, shaded areas of Florida's woodlands.
Botanists were excited to find Anemia adiantifolia growing along the forest trail in Central America.
The rhizomes of Anemia adiantifolia are noted for their dense covering of brown hairs.

Etymology

From the genus name Anemia and species epithet adiantifolia, referring to the fern's resemblance to Adiantum (maidenhair fern) and its leafy appearance.
