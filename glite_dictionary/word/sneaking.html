sneaking

1. ADJ

/ ˈsniːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsniːkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a feeling or suspicion) not openly acknowledged or expressed; kept secret or hidden.
secret or hidden (feeling/suspicion)

Examples

She had a sneaking suspicion that he wasn't telling the truth.
I've always had a sneaking admiration for people who take risks.
He felt a sneaking hope that things might work out after all.

Synset Examples

a sneaking suspicion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secret - kept hidden from knowledge or view; concealed
  • Antonym: open - not hidden or secret; expressed or done publicly

Etymology

Present participle of 'sneak', meaning to move or behave secretly.
