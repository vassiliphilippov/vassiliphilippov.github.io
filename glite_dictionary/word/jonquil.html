jonquil

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑːŋ.kwəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒŋ.kwɪl / (BrE)
A widely cultivated Mediterranean plant of the daffodil family, with fragrant yellow or white clustered flowers and slender leaves.
a fragrant Mediterranean plant with clustered flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with the sweet scent of jonquil blossoms in the spring.
She picked a bouquet of jonquils to decorate the dining table.
Many people mistake a jonquil for a daffodil due to their similar appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daffodil - a spring flower with a trumpet-shaped central corona, typically yellow or white

Etymology

From French jonquille, from Spanish junquillo, diminutive of junco (reed), from Latin juncus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑːŋ.kwɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒŋ.kwɪl / (BrE)
A Mediterranean plant of the daffodil family, with fragrant yellow or white flowers and long, slender leaves.
a type of yellow-flowered daffodil

Examples

A single jonquil bloomed early in the corner of the garden.
She picked a bouquet of fresh jonquils to brighten the kitchen table.
The delicate scent of jonquil filled the room, announcing the arrival of spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daffodil - a spring-flowering bulbous plant with bright yellow flowers

Etymology

From French jonquille, from Spanish junquillo (diminutive of junco, 'rush'), referencing the rush-like leaves.
