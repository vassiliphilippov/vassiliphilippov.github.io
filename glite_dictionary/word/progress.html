progress

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑɡrɛs / (AmE), / ˈprəʊɡrɛs / (BrE)
forward or onward movement toward a destination or goal; advancement or development.
forward movement; advancement

Examples

The progress of the train was delayed by heavy snowfall on the tracks.
Over the years, scientists have made remarkable progress in cancer research.
We monitored the progress of the project every week to ensure deadlines were met.

Synset Examples

he listened for the progress of the troops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advance - forward movement or development
  • Antonym: regress - a return to a previous, less advanced state

Etymology

From Latin 'progressus', from 'progredi' (to go forward).

/ prəˈɡrɛs / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛs / (BrE)
to develop or improve over a period of time; to advance toward a better or more complete state
develop or advance positively

Examples

Scientists hope the research will progress rapidly and lead to a cure.
