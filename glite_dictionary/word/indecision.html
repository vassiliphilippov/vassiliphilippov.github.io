indecision

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈsɪʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
the inability to make a decision quickly because you are uncertain about what to do
uncertainty in decision-making

Examples

Her indecision made it hard for the team to know which direction to take.
Moments of indecision can cost you valuable opportunities in business.
He struggled with indecision before finally choosing a university.

Synset Examples

his indecision was only momentary but the opportunity was lost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hesitation - the act of pausing or worrying before saying or doing something
  • Antonym: decisiveness - the ability to make decisions quickly and confidently

Etymology

From Latin 'indecisio', from 'in-' (not) + 'decisio' (a cutting off, decision).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
the state of being unable to make choices quickly or confidently; lack of decisiveness or firmness in decision-making
inability to decide quickly or firmly

Examples

Her indecision was obvious as she stared at the menu for several minutes.
The company's progress was slowed by months of indecision among the leadership team.
He regretted his indecision after missing several important opportunities.

Synset Examples

the king's incurable indecisiveness caused turmoil in his court

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decisiveness - the quality of being able to make decisions quickly and confidently

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'decision' (from Latin 'decisio', cutting off).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04874097-n
Synset POS: n
