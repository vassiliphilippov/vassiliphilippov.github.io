basket

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæskɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɑːskɪt / (BrE)
an amount or quantity that fills a basket, used as a unit of measurement for items such as fruit or laundry
quantity filling a basket (unit of measurement)

Examples

We bought a basket of peaches at the market.
She carried a basket of laundry up the stairs.
The farmer delivered two baskets of fresh eggs to the kitchen.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæskət / (AmE), / ˈbɑːskɪt / (BrE)
a container, typically made by weaving together thin strips of material and often with handles, used for carrying or holding things
woven container with handles

Examples

She filled the basket with fresh apples from the orchard.
Please put your dirty clothes in the laundry basket.
A picnic basket is perfect for carrying food to the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hamper - large basket with a lid, often for laundry

Etymology

From Old French 'basket', probably of Celtic origin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbæskɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɑːskɪt / (BrE)
(in basketball) a horizontal circular metal hoop with an attached net, through which players try to throw the ball to score points.
hoop and net in basketball

Examples

He scored 30 points by shooting the ball through the basket.
The basket was mounted 10 feet above the gym floor.
After his impressive slam dunk, the basket shook for a few seconds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoop - a circular band, especially one supporting a net in sports
  • Synonym: goal - in sports, a point scored by putting the ball in the designated area

4. NOUN

/ ˈbæs.kɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.skɪt / (BrE)
a score in basketball made by throwing the ball through the hoop
score in basketball

Examples

She made an incredible basket just before the buzzer sounded.
The crowd cheered every time their team scored a basket.
He missed several shots before finally sinking a basket in the second half.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goal - a score made in various sports by sending the ball into a particular area
  • Antonym: miss - an unsuccessful attempt to score in basketball
