ornery

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔrnəri / (AmE), / ˈɔːnəri / (BrE)
easily annoyed or difficult to deal with; having a bad-tempered or combative disposition.
bad-tempered or difficult to deal with

Examples

My grandfather can be downright ornery when he doesn't get his morning coffee.
The ornery cat hissed at anyone who tried to come near.
Despite her ornery attitude, her friends knew she had a kind heart.

Synset Examples

a cantankerous and venomous-tongued old lady

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cantankerous - bad-tempered, argumentative, and uncooperative
  • Antonym: amiable - having or displaying a friendly and pleasant manner

Etymology

Alteration of 'ordinary' in American regional usage.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔrnəri / (AmE), / ˈɔːnəri / (BrE)
bad-tempered, stubborn, or difficult to deal with.
irritable or difficult in behavior
american

Examples

My grandfather can be quite ornery when things don't go his way.
The children grew ornery after missing their nap.
She's ornery in the mornings until she has her coffee.

Synset Examples

He's been ornery all day for no reason.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or provoked
  • Antonym: good-natured - pleasant and easy to get along with

Etymology

Variant of American English 'ordinary', used dialectally in the 19th century to mean 'commonplace' and then 'bad-tempered'.
