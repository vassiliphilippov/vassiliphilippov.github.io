prowess

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraʊ.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈpraʊ.ɪs / (BrE)
great skill or ability in a particular activity or field, especially as a result of training or experience.
exceptional skill or expertise

Examples

The young athlete showed remarkable prowess on the soccer field during the final match.
Her prowess in mathematics impressed both her teachers and her classmates.
He was admired for his prowess as a violinist, captivating audiences around the world.

Synset Examples

the art of conversation
it's quite an art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skill - the ability to do something well
  • Synonym: competence - the ability to do something successfully or efficiently
  • Antonym: incompetence - lack of ability to do something successfully

Etymology

From Old French 'prouesse', from 'prou' meaning 'valiant, brave'.
