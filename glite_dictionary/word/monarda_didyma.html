Monarda didyma

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈnɑːrdə dɪˈdɪmə / (AmE), / məˈnɑːdə dɪˈdɪmə / (BrE)
A perennial aromatic herb of eastern North America with dense showy heads of variously colored tubular flowers; also known as bee balm or bergamot.
aromatic North American herb; bee balm

Examples

The garden was filled with the vibrant reds of Monarda didyma attracting bees and butterflies.
Herbalists prize Monarda didyma for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties.
Monarda didyma blooms throughout the summer, adding color and fragrance to the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee balm - common name for Monarda didyma
  • Synonym: bergamot - another common name for Monarda didyma (not to be confused with the citrus)

Etymology

From the genus name 'Monarda', named after Spanish botanist Nicolás Monardes, and species name 'didyma' from Latin, meaning 'in pairs'.
