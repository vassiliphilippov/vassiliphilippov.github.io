Paris quadrifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛərɪs ˌkwɑdrɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌpærɪs ˌkwɒdrɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A European woodland plant with a whorl of four broad leaves and a single greenish flower, related to the trilliums and reputed to be poisonous.
European woodland plant with four broad leaves

Examples

The shady undergrowth was dotted with Paris quadrifolia, its distinctive four leaves catching the light.
Gardeners are advised not to plant Paris quadrifolia in areas accessible to pets due to its toxicity.
In botanical studies, Paris quadrifolia is often compared to the better-known trilliums.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Paris' and species 'quadrifolia' (meaning 'four-leaved').
