curt

1. ADJ

/ kɝːt / (AmE), / kɜːt / (BrE)
using very few words in a way that seems rude or unfriendly; brief and to the point, sometimes abruptly so.
brief, abrupt in speech or manner

Examples

Her curt reply ended the conversation abruptly.
He gave a curt nod and walked away without another word.
The manager's curt instructions left little room for questions.

Synset Examples

a crisp retort
a response so curt as to be almost rude
the laconic reply: ‘yes’
short and terse and easy to understand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terse - using few words, concise, and sometimes abrupt
  • Antonym: long-winded - using more words than necessary, lengthy and verbose

Etymology

From Latin "curtus" (shortened, cut short).

2. ADJ

/ kɝːt / (AmE), / kɜːt / (BrE)
rudely brief or abrupt in speech or manner
rudely brief; abrupt

Examples

Her curt response made it clear she didn't want to continue the conversation.
The manager was surprisingly curt with his employees during the meeting.
I received a curt email declining my request without any explanation.

Synset Examples

try to cultivate a less brusque manner
a curt reply
the salesgirl was very short with him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brusque - abrupt or offhand in speech or manner
  • Antonym: polite - having or showing good manners or respect

Etymology

From Latin curtus (shortened, cut short) via Old French court.
