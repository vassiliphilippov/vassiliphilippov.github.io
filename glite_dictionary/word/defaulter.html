defaulter

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɔltər / (AmE), / dɪˈfɔːltə / (BrE)
a person who fails to meet a financial obligation, such as paying a debt or fulfilling a contract
person who fails to pay a debt

Examples

The bank sent several reminders before finally taking legal action against the defaulter.
As a defaulter, he faced heavy penalties and restrictions on future loans.
If you are a defaulter on your mortgage, the lender may seize your property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debtor - a person who owes money
  • Antonym: creditor - a person or organization to whom money is owed

Etymology

From 'default' (failure to fulfill an obligation) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɔltər / (AmE), / dɪˈfɔːltə / (BrE)
a person who fails to appear in court as required by law
someone who does not appear in court as required
legal

Examples

The court issued a warrant for the defaulter who failed to appear at the hearing.
Repeatedly being a defaulter may lead to additional legal penalties.
Authorities are actively seeking the defaulter after their unexplained absence from court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absconder - person who evades justice or leaves unlawfully to avoid prosecution

Etymology

From default, from Old French 'defaute', from Vulgar Latin *defallita (from Latin 'fallere', to fail or deceive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10019203-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɔltər / (AmE), / dɪˈfɔːltə / (BrE)
a contestant or team who fails to compete in a match or forfeits by not appearing or by violating the rules
one who forfeits a match

Examples

The referee declared him a defaulter when he failed to show up for the match.
After missing three games, the team was labeled as defaulters in the tournament records.
In some sports, a defaulter is automatically disqualified from further competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forfeit - to lose or give up something, especially as a penalty

Etymology

From 'default' (fail to fulfill an obligation) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
