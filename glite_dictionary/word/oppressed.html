oppressed

1. ADJ

/ əˈprɛst / (AmE), / əˈprɛst / (BrE)
feeling weighed down, burdened, or distressed mentally or emotionally
mentally or emotionally burdened

Examples

She felt oppressed by the constant pressure to succeed in her career.
After weeks of worrying, he became oppressed by his own negative thoughts.
Many students feel oppressed during stressful exam periods.

Synset Examples

laden with grief
oppressed by a sense of failure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burdened - feeling weighed down or carrying a heavy psychological load
  • Antonym: untroubled - not burdened or worried mentally

Etymology

From Latin 'opprimere' (to press against, to overwhelm).
