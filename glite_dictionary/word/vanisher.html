vanisher

1. NOUN

/ ˈvænɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈvanɪʃə / (BrE)
a person who suddenly disappears, especially in a mysterious or inexplicable way
person who disappears

Examples

The magician was known as the world’s greatest vanisher, amazing audiences everywhere.
After the accident, he became a recluse, almost a vanisher in his own city.
Detectives dubbed the criminal a modern-day vanisher because she left no trace behind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disappearer - a person who disappears

Etymology

From 'vanish' (from Old French *esvanir*), plus the agentive suffix '-er'.
