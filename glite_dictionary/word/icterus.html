Icterus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪktərəs / (AmE), / ˈɪktərəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Icteridae, which includes New World orioles.
genus of New World orioles (birds)

Examples

The genus Icterus includes several species commonly known as New World orioles.
Scientists discovered a new member of the Icterus genus in Central America.
The plumage patterns of Icterus species are known for their striking yellow and black coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oriole - a brightly coloured bird of the genus Icterus

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek ikteros (a yellow bird), referring to the birds' plumage.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪktərəs / (AmE), / ˈɪktərəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Icteridae, commonly known as New World orioles.
genus of New World orioles (birds)

Examples

The Baltimore oriole is a well-known member of the genus Icterus.
Many species within Icterus are recognized for their bright yellow and black plumage.
Scientists recently discovered a new bird in the Icterus genus in Central America.

Etymology

From Greek *ikteros* (jaundice), because the birds' yellow color was thought to cure jaundice.
