wolf

1. NOUN

/ wʊlf / (AmE), / wʊlf / (BrE)
a person who is aggressively predatory or rapacious, especially toward others for personal gain
predatory or rapacious person

Examples

Many considered the greedy businessman a wolf who exploited his employees.
She warned her friend that the charming investor was actually a wolf in disguise.
Don't be fooled by his nice words; beneath the surface, he's a real wolf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predator - a person or animal that ruthlessly exploits others
  • Antonym: prey - a person who is the target or victim of a predator
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09864997-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ wʊlf / (AmE), / wʊlf / (BrE)
a large wild carnivorous mammal of the dog family, native to North America and Eurasia, that usually hunts in packs
large wild pack-hunting canine

Examples

The wolf prowled silently through the snowy forest, searching for prey.
A pack of wolves howled at the moon late into the night.
Unlike the domestic dog, the wild wolf relies on cooperation to hunt large animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog - domesticated carnivorous mammal related to the wolf
  • Degree Lower: fox - small carnivorous mammal related to dogs and wolves

Etymology

Old English 'wulf', from Proto-Germanic '*wulfaz', ultimately from Proto-Indo-European '*wĺ̥kʷos' (wolf).

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ wʊlf / (AmE), / wʊlf / (BrE)
to eat food quickly and greedily
eat quickly and greedily
informal

Examples

She wolfed down her breakfast before rushing out the door.
After the long hike, we wolfed our sandwiches in minutes.
He was so hungry he wolfed the entire meal without saying a word.

Synset Examples

The teenager wolfed down the pizza

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gobble - to eat quickly and noisily
  • Synonym: to devour - to eat hungrily or quickly

Etymology

Metaphorically derived from the noun 'wolf', referring to the animal's reputation for voracious eating.

4. NOUN

/ wʊlf / (AmE), / wʊlf / (BrE)
A man who is aggressive or predatory in making romantic or sexual advances toward women.
aggressive man making amorous advances

Examples

He's known as a wolf at parties, always flirting with every woman he meets.
In the workplace, a wolf like him can make female colleagues uncomfortable.
She warned her friend to stay away from that wolf at the bar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: womanizer - a man who engages in numerous casual sexual affairs with women
  • Antonym: gentleman - a man who treats women with respect and courtesy

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'wolf' (the animal), likening predatory behavior to the hunting habits of wolves.
