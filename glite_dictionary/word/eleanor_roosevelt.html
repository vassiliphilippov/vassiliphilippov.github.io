Eleanor Roosevelt

1. PROPN

/ ˌɛl.ə.nɔr ˈroʊ.zəˌvɛlt / (AmE), / ˌɛl.ə.nɔː ˈruː.zə.vɛlt / (BrE)
(1884–1962) American politician, diplomat, and activist; wife of Franklin D. Roosevelt, renowned for her advocacy of human rights and social justice.
American activist and First Lady (1884–1962)

Examples

Eleanor Roosevelt played a pivotal role in drafting the Universal Declaration of Human Rights.
Many historians regard Eleanor Roosevelt as one of the most influential First Ladies of the United States.
Eleanor Roosevelt traveled widely to promote civil rights and humanitarian causes throughout her life.

Related Terms
