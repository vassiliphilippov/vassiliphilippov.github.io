to vegetize

1. VERB

/ ˈvɛdʒɪtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɛdʒɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to make (a meal or diet) vegetarian or based on plant foods; to replace animal products with plant-based alternatives in food
make meals vegetarian; adopt a plant-based diet

Examples

I'm trying to vegetize my weekly menu by using more beans and grains instead of meat.
Many restaurants now offer ways to vegetize traditional favorites like burgers and tacos.
After watching the documentary, she decided to vegetize her meals and cut out animal products.

Synset Examples

She plans to vegetize her meals this month.
I need to vegetize my diet and eat healthier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veganize - to make vegan or remove all animal-derived products from
  • Antonym: to animalize - to make more animal-based or to add animal products to

Etymology

Formed from 'vegetarian' or 'vegetable' + suffix '-ize', following the English pattern of forming verbs meaning 'to make or become.'
