Scorpaenidae

1. NOUN

/ skɔrˈpiːnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / skɔːˈpiːnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine fish including scorpionfishes, rockfishes, and lionfishes, known for venomous spines and distinctive appearances
family of venomous marine fish

Examples

The family Scorpaenidae contains some of the most venomous fish in the world.
Researchers observed several species within the Scorpaenidae family along the coral reefs.
Many aquariums display members of Scorpaenidae for their striking coloration and unique shapes.

2. NOUN

/ skɔrˈpiːnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / skɔːˈpiːnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of venomous marine fishes that includes scorpionfishes, rockfishes, and lionfishes, characterized by spiny fins.
family of venomous marine fishes

Examples

The lionfish, a member of the Scorpaenidae, is known for its striking appearance and venomous spines.
Marine biologists often study the Scorpaenidae due to their unique adaptations and diverse habitats.
Many species within the Scorpaenidae family exhibit excellent camouflage among coral reefs.

Etymology

From New Latin Scorpaena (a genus of fish) + '-idae' (family suffix in zoological classification).
