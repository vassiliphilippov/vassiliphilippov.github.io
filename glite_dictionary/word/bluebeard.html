Bluebeard

1. PROPN

/ ˈbluːˌbɪrd / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌbɪəd / (BrE)
A character in a French fairy tale, depicted as a wealthy and violent man who kills his wives for disobedience.
legendary wife-murderer from fairy tales

Examples

The story of Bluebeard has inspired fear and curiosity for generations of readers.
Many adaptations portray Bluebeard as a symbol of hidden dangers in marriage.
She compared the criminal to Bluebeard because of his dark and violent past.

Etymology

From French 'Barbe bleue', title of the fairy tale by Charles Perrault (1697).
