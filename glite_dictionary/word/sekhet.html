Sekhet

1. PROPN

/ sɛˈkɛt / (AmE), / ˈsɛkɛt / (BrE)
An ancient Egyptian goddess depicted with the head of a lioness, representing the destructive power of the sun.
lion-headed Egyptian goddess

Examples

In ancient Egyptian mythology, Sekhet was associated with both healing and destruction.
Priests prayed to Sekhet for protection from plagues and misfortune.
The temple honors Sekhet, the fierce lioness goddess of the sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sekhmet - Egyptian lion-headed goddess of war and destruction

Etymology

From Egyptian Sḫt (Sekhet), the name of the goddess.
