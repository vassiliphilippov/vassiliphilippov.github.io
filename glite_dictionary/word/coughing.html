coughing

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɒfɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of suddenly forcing air out of the lungs with a loud sound, often to clear the airways or as a symptom of illness
act of forcefully expelling air from the lungs

Examples

The patient's persistent coughing kept him awake all night.
Sudden coughing fits are often a sign of respiratory infection.
Her coughing became worse as the cold progressed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cough - to expel air from the lungs suddenly with a sharp sound

Etymology

From 'cough' + -ing, where 'cough' is from Middle English 'coffen'.
