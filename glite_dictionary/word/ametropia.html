ametropia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈtroʊpiə / (AmE), / ˌæmɛˈtrəʊpiə / (BrE)
a condition in which the eye does not properly focus light on the retina, resulting in blurred vision, as seen in astigmatism or myopia
improper focusing of the eye; vision defect
medical

Examples

Patients with ametropia may require corrective lenses to achieve clear vision.
Regular eye exams can help detect ametropia early and prevent complications.
The study focused on the causes and treatments of ametropia in children.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: emmetropia - normal refractive condition of the eye

Etymology

From Greek ametros (without measure) + -opia (eye condition).
