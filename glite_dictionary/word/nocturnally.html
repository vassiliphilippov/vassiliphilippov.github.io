nocturnally

1. ADV

/ nɑkˈtɝːnəli / (AmE), / nɒkˈtɜːnəli / (BrE)
In a manner occurring, active, or done during the night.
at night; during the night

Examples

Owls are known to hunt nocturnally, using their keen sense of hearing to locate prey in the dark.
Many flowers open and release their scent nocturnally to attract nighttime pollinators.
Some mammals, such as bats, feed nocturnally to avoid predators and competition.

Synset Examples

nocturnally active bird

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diurnally - during the day; in the daytime

Etymology

From 'nocturnal' + '-ly'; 'nocturnal' from Latin 'nocturnalis' (of the night), from 'nox' (night).
