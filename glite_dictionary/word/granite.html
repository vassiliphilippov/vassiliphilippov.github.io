granite

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræn.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡræn.ɪt / (BrE)
A very hard, coarse-grained, crystalline igneous rock composed mainly of quartz, feldspar, and mica, often used in building and monuments.
hard crystalline igneous rock

Examples

The kitchen countertops were made of polished granite.
Ancient statues carved from granite have survived for centuries.
They chose a granite headstone for its durability and elegant appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marble - a metamorphic rock used in sculpture and building
  • Degree Lower: basalt - a dark, fine-grained volcanic rock

Etymology

From French 'granit', from Italian 'granito', from 'grano' meaning grain, describing its grainy texture.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡræn.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡran.ɪt / (BrE)
Great firmness, strength, or unyielding character, likened to the hard rock granite.
unyielding firmness or strength (figurative)

Examples

Her resolve showed a granite that inspired everyone on the team.
With granite in his voice, he refused to back down.
Years of hardship had given her a granite will.

Synset Examples

a man of granite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stone - great strength or enduring quality (figurative)

Etymology

From French 'granit', from Italian 'granito' (grained), from 'grano' (grain).
