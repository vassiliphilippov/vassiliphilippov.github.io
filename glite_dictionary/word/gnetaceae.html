Gnetaceae

1. NOUN

/ nɛˈtæsiˌi / (AmE), / nɛˈtæsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of gymnosperm plants characterized by small unisexual flowers and fleshy or winged fruit, sometimes including the genera Ephedra, Welwitschia, and Gnetum.
a family of gymnosperm plants

Examples

The Gnetaceae are unique among gymnosperms for their vessel-bearing wood.
Research on Gnetaceae has provided insights into the evolution of flowering plants.
Some scientists classify Welwitschia within the Gnetaceae family due to certain morphological traits.

Etymology

From New Latin, from genus Gnetum + suffix -aceae (denoting plant families).
