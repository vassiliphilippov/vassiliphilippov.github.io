unassailable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈseɪ.lə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈseɪ.lə.bəl / (BrE)
Impossible to attack, question, or defeat; not able to be tampered with or disproven.
impossible to attack or challenge

Examples

After years of research, her theory was considered unassailable by the scientific community.
The castle's high walls made it practically unassailable during medieval times.
He believed his financial position was unassailable, safe from any market downturns.

Synset Examples

an impregnable fortress
fortifications that made the frontier inviolable
a secure telephone connection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impregnable - unable to be captured or broken into; strong and secure
  • Antonym: vulnerable - capable of being wounded or attacked; susceptible

Etymology

From un- (not) + assailable, from Old French *assaillir* (to assault).

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈseɪ.lə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈseɪ.lə.bəl / (BrE)
impossible to question, attack, or defeat; without flaws or loopholes.
impossible to attack or refute

Examples

Her position in the debate was unassailable because her evidence was thorough and compelling.
The company built an unassailable legal case that left their rivals with no chance for appeal.
His reputation for honesty was unassailable, making him the obvious choice for the job.

Synset Examples

an ironclad contract
a watertight alibi
a bulletproof argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invulnerable - impossible to harm or damage
  • Synonym: indisputable - unable to be denied or challenged
  • Antonym: vulnerable - open to attack or criticism

Etymology

From un- (not) + assailable, from Old French assaillable, from assaillir (to assail).
