to Europeanise

1. VERB

/ jʊˌroʊˈpiːəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / jʊəˌrəʊˈpiːənaɪz / (BrE)
To denationalize and bring (a country or region) under the influence, policies, or supervision of European institutions or agencies, especially those associated with the European Union.
bring under European institution influence

Examples

The government sought to Europeanise its legal system to align with EU standards.
After the treaty, the administration began to Europeanise the management of its borders.
Efforts to Europeanise the territory met resistance from nationalist groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to Europeanize - To bring under European influence (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'Europe' + '-anise', modeled after the US spelling 'Europeanize'.

2. VERB

/ jʊˌroʊˈpiːəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / jʊəˌrəʊˈpiːənaɪz / (BrE)
to make something more European in customs, character, or ideas
make more European in character or ideas
british

Examples

The government sought to Europeanise local regulations to align them with EU standards.
Educators are debating whether to Europeanise the curriculum in secondary schools.
Some fear that globalization might Europeanise traditional cultural festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to Europeanize - make (continental) European in customs, character, or ideas (American spelling)

Etymology

From European + -ise (British spelling of '-ize').
