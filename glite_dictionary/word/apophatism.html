apophatism

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑː.fəˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / əˈpɒ.fəˌtɪz.əm / (BrE)
The belief or doctrine in theology that God or the divine is unknowable and can only be described by stating what God is not, rather than what God is.
the doctrine of describing God by negation

Examples

Some theologians embrace apophatism, arguing that God cannot be defined by human concepts.
Medieval Christian mystics often used apophatism to express divine transcendence.
The doctrine of apophatism focuses on what God is not, rather than attempting to describe God's essence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cataphatism - the doctrine that God can be described in positive terms

Etymology

From Greek 'apophatikos' (negative, denying), from 'apophasis' (denial, negation).
