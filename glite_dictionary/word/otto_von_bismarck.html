Otto von Bismarck

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑtoʊ vɑn ˈbɪzˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈɒtəʊ vɒn ˈbɪzmɑːk / (BrE)
(1815–1898) A German statesman who, as Chancellor, led the unification of Germany and served as its first Chancellor.
German statesman; unified Germany

Examples

Otto von Bismarck played a crucial role in shaping modern Germany through his policies and leadership.
The unification of Germany would not have been possible without Otto von Bismarck's diplomatic skills.
Historians often debate the long-term effects of Otto von Bismarck's domestic and foreign policies.
