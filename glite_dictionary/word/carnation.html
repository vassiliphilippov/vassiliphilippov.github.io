carnation

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / kɑːˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
A pink or reddish-pink color, resembling the hue of the carnation flower.
a pink or reddish-pink color

Examples

The designer chose a carnation shade for the bridesmaids’ dresses.
A soft carnation glow filled the sky at sunrise.
She painted the baby's room a gentle carnation to create a warm atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pink - a pale red color

Etymology

From Latin 'carnatio' (fleshiness), referring to the flesh-colored hue of the original flower.

2. NOUN

/ kɑrˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / kɑːˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a garden plant with sweetly scented, usually double flowers in a variety of colors, especially pink, red, or white; widely cultivated as an ornamental.
a brightly colored, fragrant garden flower

Examples

She placed a single carnation in the vase on the table.
The bouquet was filled with pink and white carnations.
Many people choose carnations for Mother's Day because of their lovely scent.

Etymology

From Latin 'carnatio' meaning 'flesh' (referring to the flower’s pinkish color).
