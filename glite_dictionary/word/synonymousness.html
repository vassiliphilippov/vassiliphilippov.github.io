synonymousness

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnɑnɪməsnəs / (AmE), / sɪˈnɒnɪməsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being synonymous; the condition in which two or more words have the same or nearly the same meaning in a particular context.
the quality of being synonymous

Examples

The synonymousness of these technical terms often causes confusion among students.
It's important to recognize that total synonymousness between words is rare in any language.
The debate focused on the synonymousness of the phrases 'due to' and 'because of'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synonymy - the semantic relationship of words with the same or nearly the same meaning

Etymology

From synonymous + -ness; ultimately from Greek syn- (together) + onoma (name).
