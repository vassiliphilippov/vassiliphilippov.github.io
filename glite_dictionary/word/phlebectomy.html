phlebectomy

1. NOUN

/ fləˈbɛk.tə.mi / (AmE), / flɪˈbɛk.tə.mi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure for the removal of all or part of a vein, typically performed to treat severe varicose veins
surgical removal of a vein
medical

Examples

The surgeon recommended a phlebectomy to alleviate the patient's painful varicose veins.
After undergoing phlebectomy, she noticed a significant improvement in both appearance and discomfort.
A minimally invasive phlebectomy allows for quick recovery and reduced scarring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vein - a blood vessel that carries blood toward the heart

Etymology

From Greek 'phleps, phleb-' (vein) + '-ectomy' (removal by cutting).
