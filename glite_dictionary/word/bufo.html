Bufo

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːfoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbjuːfəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of toads, comprising numerous species commonly known as true toads.
genus of true toads

Examples

The common European Bufo is often found in gardens during the spring.
Scientists studied the toxin-producing glands of several Bufo species.
The American Bufo is known for its distinctive croaking call.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toad - a tailless amphibian with a warty skin

Etymology

From Latin 'bufo', meaning 'toad'. Used in scientific taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːfoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbjuːfəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of toads, commonly known as true toads, found in many parts of the world and forming the type genus of the family Bufonidae.
genus of true toads

Examples

The Bufo genus includes many familiar species of toads found in gardens and forests.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Bufo populations across Europe.
Common features of Bufo include warty skin and the secretion of toxins as a defense mechanism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toad - a tailless amphibian related to frogs, typically with dry, bumpy skin

Etymology

From Latin *bufo* meaning 'toad'.
