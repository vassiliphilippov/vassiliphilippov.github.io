phenobarbital

1. NOUN

/ ˌfiːnoʊˈbɑːrbɪtɑl / (AmE), / ˌfiːnəʊˈbɑːbɪtɒl / (BrE)
a long-acting barbiturate drug used as a sedative and to control seizures
a barbiturate sedative drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed phenobarbital to help manage her epilepsy.
Because of its sedative effects, phenobarbital must be taken exactly as directed by a physician.
In some cases, phenobarbital is used to prevent seizures in newborns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbiturate - a class of drugs that act as central nervous system depressants

Etymology

From 'pheno-' (chemical prefix) + 'barbital' (a type of barbiturate), coined in early 20th century.
