Paprilus alepidotus

1. NOUN

/ pəˈprɪləs æˌlɛpɪˈdoʊtəs / (AmE), / pəˈprɪləs æˌlɛpɪˈdəʊtəs / (BrE)
A species of butterfish found in Atlantic waters, ranging from Chesapeake Bay to Argentina, growing up to about a foot long.
a species of Atlantic butterfish

Examples

The waters off Brazil are home to the Paprilus alepidotus, a commonly caught butterfish.
Marine biologists studied the diet of Paprilus alepidotus in the Chesapeake Bay region.
Anglers sometimes mistake Paprilus alepidotus for similar fish due to its silvery appearance.
