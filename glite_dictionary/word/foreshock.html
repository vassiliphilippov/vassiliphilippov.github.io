foreshock

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌʃɑk / (AmE), / ˈfɔːʃɒk / (BrE)
a minor tremor that occurs before a larger earthquake, often serving as a warning sign
small tremor before an earthquake

Examples

Scientists detected a foreshock just hours before the major quake struck the region.
The residents felt a mild foreshock that served as a warning for the larger earthquake.
Many earthquakes are preceded by a foreshock, but not all tremors signal a bigger event.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aftershock - a smaller earthquake that follows the main shock

Etymology

From 'fore-' (before) and 'shock' (seismic shock).
