to infuse

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈfjuz / (AmE), / ɪnˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to introduce a liquid or substance, especially a drug or nutrient, directly into a vein for medical purposes
inject a substance into a vein
medical

Examples

The nurse will infuse the medication slowly into your bloodstream.
Doctors chose to infuse saline solution to rehydrate the patient.
In emergencies, glucose is often infused intravenously to stabilize blood sugar levels.

Synset Examples

Some physiologists infuses sugar solutions into the veins of animals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inject - to force a liquid into a body, especially with a syringe

Etymology

From Latin infusum, past participle of infundere, meaning 'to pour in'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00086350-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈfjuz / (AmE), / ɪnˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to allow an ingredient to soak in a liquid in order to extract its flavor or cleanse it
soak to extract flavor

Examples

Let the herbs infuse in hot water for several minutes before drinking.
You should infuse the tea leaves overnight for the best flavor.
Chefs often infuse oil with garlic to add extra taste to their dishes.

Synset Examples

steep the blossoms in oil
steep the fruit in alcohol

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steep - let sit in liquid to extract flavor (culinary)

Etymology

From Latin infundere (to pour in).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00328172-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈfjuz / (AmE), / ɪnˈfjuːz / (BrE)
(of a substance) to become steeped in liquid so as to extract flavor or other properties; to undergo the process of infusion.
(of a substance) to become infused in liquid

Examples

Leave the tea bag in the cup and let the flavor infuse for a few minutes.
The chamomile flowers are infusing gently in the hot water.
Allowing the herbs to infuse longer will produce a stronger taste.

Synset Examples

the mint tea is infusing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steep - soak in liquid to extract flavor or soften

Etymology

From Latin infundere (to pour in), from in- (into) + fundere (to pour).

5. VERB

/ ɪnˈfjuz / (AmE), / ɪnˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to fill or permeate something with a particular quality or feeling
fill or permeate with a quality

Examples

Her words infuse the team with a sense of hope and determination.
The light infused the room with warmth and comfort.
A spirit of generosity infused every aspect of the celebration.

Synset Examples

The heavy traffic tinctures the air with carbon monoxide

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imbue - to inspire or permeate with a feeling or quality
  • Synonym: fill - to make full or occupy completely
  • Antonym: drain - to cause a quality or feeling to leave or diminish

Etymology

From Latin 'infundere' meaning 'to pour in' or 'into.'
