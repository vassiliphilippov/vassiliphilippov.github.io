exterminable

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstɜrmɪnəbəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːmɪnəbl / (BrE)
Capable of being completely destroyed or wiped out; able to be exterminated.
able to be exterminated or wiped out

Examples

Some invasive species are difficult to eradicate, but others are exterminable with persistent effort.
The pests were thought to be exterminable, yet they reappeared every season.
He wondered if such evil was truly exterminable, or if it would persist in some form.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inexterminable - incapable of being exterminated or destroyed

Etymology

Formed from Latin 'exterminare' (to drive out) + English suffix '-able'.
