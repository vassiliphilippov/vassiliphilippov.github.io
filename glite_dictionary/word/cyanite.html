cyanite

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsaɪənaɪt / (BrE)
A grey or greenish-blue mineral consisting of crystalline aluminum silicate, found in metamorphic rocks and used as a refractory material.
a mineral; aluminum silicate crystal

Examples

Geologists discovered large deposits of cyanite in the mountain range.
The high melting point of cyanite makes it valuable for use in refractory products.
A specimen of cyanite showed a brilliant blue color under the light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kyanite - the same mineral, alternative spelling

Etymology

From Greek 'kyanos' (dark blue), via French 'cyanite.'
