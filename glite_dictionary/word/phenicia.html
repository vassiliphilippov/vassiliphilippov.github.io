Phenicia

1. PROPN

/ fəˈnɪʃə / (AmE), / fɪˈnɪʃɪə / (BrE)
an ancient maritime country consisting of a collection of city-states at the eastern end of the Mediterranean, known for its seafaring, trade, and the creation of the Phoenician alphabet
ancient country in eastern Mediterranean

Examples

The traders of Phenicia were renowned for their skills in navigation and shipbuilding.
Artifacts from Phenicia have been discovered throughout the Mediterranean region.
Many historians believe that Phenicia played a crucial role in the spread of the alphabet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phoenicia - ancient civilization on the eastern Mediterranean, often spelled 'Phoenicia'

Etymology

From Latin 'Phoenicia', from Greek 'Phoiníkē' (Φοινίκη).
