imperceptibility

1. NOUN

/ ɪm.pɚˌsɛp.təˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ɪm.pəˌsɛp.təˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible or very difficult to notice or perceive by the mind or senses
quality of being impossible to perceive

Examples

The imperceptibility of the change made it impossible for anyone to notice at first.
He marveled at the imperceptibility of the artist's brushstrokes, which blended seamlessly into the canvas.
The imperceptibility of the sound meant only the most sensitive instruments could detect it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperceptible - impossible or very difficult to perceive
  • Antonym: perceptibility - the quality of being able to be perceived

Etymology

From Latin 'imperceptibilis' (not perceptible); formed by adding the suffix '-ity' to 'imperceptible'.
