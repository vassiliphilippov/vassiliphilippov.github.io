Nyctimene

1. PROPN

/ nɪkˈtɪmɪni / (AmE), / nɪkˈtɪməniː / (BrE)
A genus of East Indian fruit bats known for their tubular nostrils.
genus of East Indian fruit bats

Examples

The Nyctimene are easily recognized by their unique tubular nostrils.
Researchers encountered several Nyctimene specimens while studying fruit bats in Papua New Guinea.
Unlike many other bat genera, Nyctimene feed mainly on fruit rather than insects.

Etymology

From Greek 'nyx' (night) + 'timēnē' (honor), referring to nocturnal habits.

2. NOUN

/ nɪkˈtɪmɪni / (AmE), / nɪkˈtɪməni / (BrE)
A genus of East Indian fruit bats known for their tubular nostrils, belonging to the family Pteropodidae.
genus of East Indian fruit bats

Examples

The Nyctimene are easily recognized by their distinctive tubular nostrils.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Nyctimene genus in Papua New Guinea.
Unlike other fruit bats, Nyctimene use their unique noses to help with their sense of smell.

Etymology

From Greek nykt- (night) and mēnē (moon), referring to nocturnal habits.
