Rodolia cardinalis

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈdoʊliə kɑːrˈdɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / rəˈdəʊliə kɑːˈdɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
a species of ladybird beetle native to Australia, introduced elsewhere to control scale insects
species of ladybird beetle

Examples

Rodolia cardinalis was introduced to California in the 19th century to control cottony cushion scale.
Farmers often rely on Rodolia cardinalis as a natural alternative to chemical pesticides.
The success of Rodolia cardinalis demonstrates the benefits of biological pest control.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Rodolia' and species epithet 'cardinalis' (meaning cardinal, red in color).
