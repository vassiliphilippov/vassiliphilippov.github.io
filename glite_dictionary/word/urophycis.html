Urophycis

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊəroʊˈfaɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəʊˈfɪsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of hakes in the family Phycidae, comprising several species found in the Atlantic Ocean.
genus of hakes (fish)

Examples

The Atlantic hake belongs to the genus Urophycis, which includes several related species.
Scientists discovered a new species within Urophycis along the continental shelf.
Commercial fisheries sometimes target Urophycis due to its abundance in certain regions.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'oura' (tail) and 'phykis,' related to hakes.
