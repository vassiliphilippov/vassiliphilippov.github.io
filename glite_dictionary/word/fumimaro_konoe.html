Fumimaro Konoe

1. PROPN

/ fuːmiˈmɑːroʊ koʊˈnoʊeɪ / (AmE), / fuːmiˈmɑːrəʊ kəˈnəʊeɪ / (BrE)
(1891–1945) Japanese statesman who set Japan's expansionist policies and formed an alliance with Germany and Italy during World War II.
Japanese statesman and wartime prime minister

Examples

Fumimaro Konoe played a key role in Japan's foreign policy decisions before World War II.
The leadership of Fumimaro Konoe greatly influenced Japan's alliance with Germany and Italy.
Historians often debate the motivations behind the actions of Fumimaro Konoe during the late 1930s.
