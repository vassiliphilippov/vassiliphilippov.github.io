organiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɡəˌnaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡənaɪzə / (BrE)
a small, electronic handheld device used to manage personal information such as appointments, contacts, and notes
handheld electronic personal information device
british

Examples

He used his organiser to schedule meetings and set reminders throughout the day.
Many people relied on a organiser before smartphones became widely available.
My old electronic organiser still has all my contacts and important dates saved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: personal digital assistant - a handheld device that functions as a personal information manager
  • Degree Higher: smartphone - a mobile phone with advanced computing capability

Etymology

From 'organise' + '-er', denoting something that organises. The device was named for its function of organizing information.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɡəˌnaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡənaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who arranges and coordinates an activity, event, or enterprise and ensures it runs smoothly
person who arranges and coordinates activities
british

Examples

The organiser of the festival handled every detail with precision.
Our school trip wouldn't have happened without a dedicated organiser.
As the event's organiser, she ensured everything ran smoothly.

Synset Examples

she was the organizer of the meeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organizer - a person who arranges and coordinates an activity or event (American spelling)
  • Synonym: manager - a person responsible for controlling or administering an organization or group of staff

Etymology

From organize + -er, influenced by French organiser and English organize.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔːr.ɡə.naɪ.zər / (AmE), / ˈɔː.ɡə.naɪ.zə / (BrE)
a person who recruits or enlists workers to join a union
person who recruits workers for a union

Examples

The organiser visited several factories to encourage workers to join the union.
As a union organiser, she played a key role in improving labor rights.
Each team met with the organiser to discuss membership and workplace concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: union representative - person elected to represent union members

Etymology

From organize + -er, meaning one who organizes.
