to expound

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈspaʊnd / (AmE), / ɪkˈspaʊnd / (BrE)
to explain something in detail or to present and clarify an idea, theory, or principle thoroughly, often in a learned or formal way
to explain in detail or at length

Examples

During the lecture, the professor expounded the theory in great detail.
She was invited to expound on her views during the conference.
In his latest book, the author expounds the importance of ethical leadership.

Synset Examples

She elaborated on the main ideas in her dissertation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elaborate - to add more detail or information to something
  • Antonym: to summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points

Etymology

From Latin 'expoundere', meaning 'to set forth, explain'.

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈspaʊnd / (AmE), / ɪkˈspaʊnd / (BrE)
to explain or state something in detail
explain in detail

Examples

During the meeting, she expounded her views on the company's new policy.
The professor expounded on the philosophy of science for over an hour.
He expounded the main principles of his argument in clear, simple language.

Synset Examples

set forth one's reasons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explain - make something clear by describing it in detail
  • Synonym: outline - give a summary of the main points

Etymology

From Latin 'expoundere', from 'ex-' (out) + 'ponere' (to put).
