obstruction

1. NOUN

/ əbˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / əbˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
the state of being blocked or having a passage filled so that movement or flow is prevented
state of being blocked or filled

Examples

A serious obstruction in the artery requires immediate medical attention.
The drainpipe suffered from an obstruction caused by accumulated leaves and debris.
After the accident, the doctors treated the patient for a bowel obstruction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blockage - the state of being blocked
  • Antonym: clearance - the removal of something that blocks

Etymology

From Latin 'obstructio', from 'obstruere' (to build against, block up).

2. NOUN

/ əbˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / əbˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
something that blocks progress or makes it difficult to achieve something, especially an intangible barrier or hindrance.
intangible barrier to progress

Examples

The complex regulations created a serious obstruction to starting new businesses.
His constant negativity became an obstruction to the team's progress.
Lack of funding is the main obstruction to expanding the program.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks or stands in the way of progress
  • Synonym: impediment - something that makes progress difficult
  • Antonym: aid - something that helps progress

From Latin obstruere (to block up).

3. NOUN

/ əbˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / əbˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
the act of getting in someone's way or preventing progress, movement, or activity
the act of blocking or hindering

Examples

The fallen tree created an obstruction on the road, causing traffic to back up for miles.
He was penalized for obstruction after deliberately stepping in front of his opponent during the game.
Her plans met with unexpected obstruction from the board, delaying the project for months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blockage - something that blocks a passage or flow
  • Synonym: impediment - something that hinders or prevents progress
  • Antonym: assistance - the act of helping or aiding someone

Etymology

From Latin obstructio (a blocking up), from obstruere (to block up).
