Sanskrit

1. NOUN

/ ˈsænˌskrɪt / (AmE), / ˈsanskɹɪt / (BrE)
an ancient Indic language of India, the classical language of Hinduism and the language in which many religious, philosophical, and literary texts are written; now used mainly in religious contexts
classical ancient language of India

Examples

Many ancient Hindu texts are composed in Sanskrit.
She studied Sanskrit for several years to better understand Indian philosophy.
The Vedas were originally written in Sanskrit, making translation a complex task.

Etymology

From Sanskrit saṃskṛta (adorned, cultivated, purified), from sam (together) + kṛta (made, done).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsænˌskrɪt / (AmE), / ˈsænˌskrɪt / (BrE)
an ancient Indo-European language of India, the classical language of Hinduism and of most ancient Indian literature, now used mainly in religious contexts
ancient Indian classical language

Examples

Many classical Indian texts were composed in Sanskrit.
He studied Sanskrit to better understand early Hindu scriptures.
The ceremony was conducted entirely in Sanskrit, as is traditional.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pali - a Middle Indo-Aryan language closely related to Sanskrit, used in early Buddhist scriptures

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'saṃskṛta', meaning 'adorned' or 'refined'.
