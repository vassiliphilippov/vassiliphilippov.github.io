Cygnus cygnus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡ.nəs ˈsɪɡ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.nəs ˈsɪɡ.nəs / (BrE)
a large Old World swan species, known for its loud trumpeting call, commonly called the whooper swan
large swan with a loud trumpet call

Examples

The migration patterns of the Cygnus cygnus have fascinated ornithologists for decades.
We spotted a family of Cygnus cygnus on the lake, their calls echoing across the water.
The distinctive trumpet call of Cygnus cygnus can be heard in northern Europe during their breeding season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whooper swan - common name for Cygnus cygnus

Etymology

From Latin: 'cygnus' means 'swan'.
