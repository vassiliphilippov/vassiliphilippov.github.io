timer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪmər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmə / (BrE)
(in sports) an official responsible for keeping track of the time elapsed during a game, race, or event.
sports official who tracks elapsed time

Examples

The timer announced that only two minutes remained in the match.
After a technical issue, the timer had to reset the clock.
The race began as soon as the timer blew the starting whistle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timekeeper - an official who records the time in a sports event
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪmɚ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmə / (BrE)
A device that automatically activates or deactivates a mechanism at a set time or after a preset interval.
device controlling time-based activation

Examples

Please set the timer for 20 minutes before putting the cake in the oven.
The lights in the garden are connected to a timer so they turn on automatically at dusk.
She used a timer to remind herself to take breaks while working.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alarm - a device that gives a warning at a set time
  • Synonym: stopwatch - a device used to measure the exact time elapsed

Etymology

From 'time' + '-er', indicating something that times or measures time.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04445641-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪmər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmə / (BrE)
A device that measures a set period of time and indicates when that period has ended, often by sounding an alarm or turning something off.
device for measuring a time interval

Examples

Please set the timer for 20 minutes so the cake doesn't burn.
The kitchen timer beeped loudly, telling us that the pasta was ready.
He bought a new digital timer to help him manage his workouts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stopwatch - a handheld timepiece designed to measure the amount of time elapsed
  • Synonym: alarm clock - a clock that sounds an alarm at a set time

Etymology

From 'time' + '-er', denoting an agent or device related to time.
