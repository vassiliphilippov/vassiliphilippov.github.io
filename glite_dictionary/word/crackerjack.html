crackerjack

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræk.ɚˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈkræk.əˌdʒæk / (BrE)
A person or thing of exceptional quality or ability; something or someone excellent.
something or someone excellent
informal american

Examples

She's a real crackerjack at solving difficult math problems.
His new book is a crackerjack—I couldn't put it down.
That restaurant is a crackerjack in terms of both food and service.

Synset Examples

the bike was a jimdandy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ace - someone who excels at a particular skill or activity
  • Antonym: dud - a person or thing that is unsuccessful or of very poor quality

Etymology

Originally American English, perhaps from combination of 'cracker' (something striking) and 'jack' (man or lad, representing excellence).
