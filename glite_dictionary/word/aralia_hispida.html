Aralia hispida

1. NOUN

/ əˈreɪliə hɪˈspɪdə / (AmE), / əˈreɪlɪə hɪˈspɪdə / (BrE)
A bristly herb native to eastern and central North America, known for its black fruit and medicinal bark.
bristly North American herb with black fruit

Examples

The roots of Aralia hispida have been used in traditional medicine for generations.
Aralia hispida grows well in dry, sandy soils across the eastern United States.
Researchers collected samples of Aralia hispida to study its medicinal properties.

Etymology

From the genus name Aralia, with the species epithet 'hispida' from Latin meaning 'bristly'.
