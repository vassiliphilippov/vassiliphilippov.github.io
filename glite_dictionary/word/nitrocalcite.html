nitrocalcite

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪtroʊˈkælsaɪt / (AmE), / ˌnɪtrəʊˈkælsaɪt / (BrE)
A mineral form of calcium nitrate, typically found as colorless or white crystalline masses in certain natural deposits.
mineral form of calcium nitrate

Examples

The presence of nitrocalcite in the cave resulted from the decomposition of bat guano.
Researchers analyzed samples of nitrocalcite they collected in arid regions.
Humidity can cause nitrocalcite crystals to dissolve over time.

Etymology

From 'nitro-' (referring to nitrate) and 'calcite' (a form of calcium carbonate), indicating a nitrate-bearing mineral related to calcite.
