Ceratophyllum

1. PROPN

/ sɛrəˈtɑfɨləm / (AmE), / sɛrəˈtɒfɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of submerged, free-floating aquatic plants (hornworts) found in ponds and slow-moving water, constituting the family Ceratophyllaceae.
genus of aquatic hornwort plants

Examples

Many aquarium enthusiasts use Ceratophyllum as an oxygenating plant in their tanks.
The pond was filled with Ceratophyllum, providing shelter for small fish and insects.
Biologists studied the growth patterns of Ceratophyllum in various freshwater environments.

Etymology

From Greek keras (horn) + phyllon (leaf), referring to the plant's horn-shaped leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛrəˈtɒfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌsɛrəˈtɒfɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic flowering plants, commonly known as hornworts, forming the family Ceratophyllaceae.
genus of aquatic plants; hornworts

Examples

Ceratophyllum is often found floating freely in freshwater ponds and lakes.
Many aquarists use Ceratophyllum to help control algae in their aquariums.
The horn-shaped leaves of Ceratophyllum make it easy to identify among aquatic plants.

Etymology

From Greek keras (horn) + phyllon (leaf), referring to the plant's horn-shaped leaves.
