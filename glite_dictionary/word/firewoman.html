firewoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A female pitcher in baseball who enters the game after it has started, typically in a relief role.
female baseball relief pitcher

Examples

After the starter struggled, the team brought in their ace firewoman to finish the seventh inning.
The rookie firewoman quickly gained a reputation for shutting down late-game rallies.
As a firewoman, her role was crucial in securing tight victories for the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relief pitcher - a baseball pitcher who enters the game after it has started

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a female member of a fire department whose job is to extinguish fires
female firefighter

Examples

The city celebrated its first firewoman after she completed her training.
As a firewoman, she responded quickly to the emergency call downtown.
The interview featured a firewoman discussing her experiences on the job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fireman - a male firefighter
  • Synonym: firefighter - a person whose job is to extinguish fires, regardless of gender

Etymology

Formed from 'fireman' by analogy, with 'woman' replacing 'man'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman employed to tend fires, especially on a coal-fired train or steamship.
female who tends fires (on trains or ships)

Examples

She worked as a firewoman on the steam locomotive, shoveling coal into the engine.
The firewoman ensured the boiler maintained enough pressure throughout the journey.
During the war, many women took up roles as firewomen on ships and trains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fireman - a person, especially a man, who tends fires on a train or ship

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'fireman', using 'woman' as the feminine equivalent.
