analytic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.əˈlɪt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌan.əˈlɪt.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a method, especially in mathematics) using algebra and calculus to solve problems rather than geometric or numerical methods.
using algebra/calculus to solve problems

Examples

The analytic method allows us to solve these equations systematically.
In analytic geometry, problems are approached using analytic techniques rather than purely geometric constructions.
His expertise lies in analytic number theory, which relies heavily on calculus.

Synset Examples

analytic statics

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synthetic - (of a mathematical method) proceeding by combining separate elements; opposed to analytic in mathematics

Etymology

From Greek analytikos, from analyein (to analyze).

2. ADJ

/ ˌæn.əˈlɪt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.əˈlɪt.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or using logical reasoning, especially by breaking a complex whole into its parts to understand it better
using analysis or logical reasoning

Examples

She has an analytic mind that helps her solve complicated problems efficiently.
The report takes an analytic approach to understanding economic trends.
His analytic skills were crucial for dissecting the data and drawing conclusions.

Synset Examples

an analytic experiment
an analytic approach
a keenly analytic man
analytical reasoning
an analytical mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analytical - relating to or skilled in analysis
  • Antonym: synthetic - relating to synthesis, putting elements together

Etymology

From Greek analytikos (‘pertaining to analysis’), from analuein (‘to unloose, to analyze’).

3. ADJ

/ əˈnælɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌan.əˈlɪt.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a language or construction) expressing grammatical categories by using two or more words rather than by inflection.
expressed with separate words, not inflection

Examples

Chinese is considered an analytic language because it uses word order rather than endings to convey meaning.
In analytic languages, tense is often indicated with auxiliary verbs.
English has become more analytic over time, relying less on inflectional endings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synthetic - (of a language) expressing grammatical categories by inflection rather than by separate words

Etymology

From Greek analytikos, from analuein (to unloose).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00112226-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADJ

/ ˌæn.əˈlɪt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌan.əˈlɪt.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a statement or proposition) necessarily true by virtue of its terms, independent of fact or experience.
true by definition, independent of fact

Examples

According to Kant, the statement 'All bachelors are unmarried men' is analytic.
In logic, an analytic judgment is true by definition and does not rely on empirical evidence.
The distinction between analytic and synthetic statements is fundamental in philosophy.

Synset Examples

‘all spinsters are unmarried’ is an analytic proposition

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synthetic - (of a proposition) not analytically true, but requiring empirical verification

Etymology

From Greek analytikos ('skilled in analysis'), via Latin analyticus.

WordNet ID: oewn-00111664-a
