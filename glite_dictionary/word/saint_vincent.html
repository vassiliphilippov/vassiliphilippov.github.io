Saint Vincent

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈvɪnsənt / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈvɪnsənt / (BrE)
a Caribbean island, the largest of Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, located in the center of the Windward Islands.
largest island of Saint Vincent and the Grenadines

Examples

Tourists often visit Saint Vincent for its beautiful beaches and volcanic landscapes.
After the hurricane, aid was quickly sent to Saint Vincent to support recovery efforts.
Bananas are a major export crop from Saint Vincent, contributing to the local economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Vincent and the Grenadines - an island country in the Caribbean, including Saint Vincent and the Grenadines islands

Etymology

Named after Saint Vincent of Saragossa by Christopher Columbus, who sighted the island on his feast day in 1498.
