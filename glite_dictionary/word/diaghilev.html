Diaghilev

1. PROPN

/ diˈɑɡɪlɛv / (AmE), / diˈɑːɡɪlɛv / (BrE)
Sergei Diaghilev (1872–1929), Russian ballet impresario who founded the Ballets Russes and introduced Russian ballet to Western Europe.
Russian ballet impresario (1872–1929)
american british

Examples

Diaghilev revolutionized the world of ballet by bringing Russian productions to Paris and London.
Many famous artists collaborated with Diaghilev, including Picasso and Stravinsky.
The influence of Diaghilev can still be seen in contemporary ballet performances.

Etymology

Russian surname Диагилев (Diaghilev).
