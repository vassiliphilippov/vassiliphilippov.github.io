five-needled

1. ADJ

/ faɪvˈnidəld / (AmE), / faɪvˈniːdl̩d / (BrE)
(of conifers) having clusters of five needles per bundle.
having five needles per bundle (of conifers)

Examples

White pine is a classic example of a five-needled tree species.
The five-needled pines are easily identified by their needle groupings.
Botanists studied the five-needled conifers to understand their evolutionary history.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: three-needled - (of conifers) having three needles per bundle
  • Degree Lower: two-needled - (of conifers) having two needles per bundle
  • Synonym: five-leaved - (of conifers) having five leaves per bundle
