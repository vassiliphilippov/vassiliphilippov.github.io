beating

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
an act of defeating or outdoing someone, especially in a competition or contest
the act of defeating or outdoing

Examples

Our team suffered a heavy beating in the final match.
The chess champion gave his opponent a thorough beating.
After last week's beating, they trained even harder for the next game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeat - an instance of beating someone in a contest or battle
  • Synonym: victory - the act of winning or prevailing
  • Antonym: loss - being beaten in a competition

Etymology

From the verb 'beat', from Old English 'bēatan' (to strike).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbiːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
an act of striking a person repeatedly as a form of punishment or violence
act of striking repeatedly as punishment

Examples

The principal condemned the beating of students as an unacceptable form of discipline.
After admitting his mistake, he feared his father would give him a harsh beating.
Reports of beatings in the prison led to a public outcry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrashing - a severe beating with repeated blows, often as punishment
  • Synonym: whipping - the act of beating with a whip as punishment

Etymology

From the verb 'beat', which comes from Old English 'bēatan' (to strike, inflict blows).
