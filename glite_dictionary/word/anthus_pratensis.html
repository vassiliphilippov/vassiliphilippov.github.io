Anthus pratensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈænθəs prəˈtɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈænθəs præˈtɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the meadow pipit, a small brown bird common in northern and central Europe and Asia.
scientific name of the meadow pipit (bird)

Examples

The Anthus pratensis is often seen fluttering low over open fields in the summer.
Ornithologists study the migratory patterns of Anthus pratensis across Europe.
I spotted an Anthus pratensis while hiking in the Scottish Highlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meadow pipit - a small brown songbird found in European and Asian grasslands

Etymology

From Latin; 'Anthus' denotes a kind of small bird, and 'pratensis' means 'of the meadow'.
