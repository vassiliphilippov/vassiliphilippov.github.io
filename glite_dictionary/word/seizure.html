seizure

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːʒər / (AmE), / ˈsiːʒə / (BrE)
the act of taking possession of property or goods by legal process, usually by authority or court order
confiscation by legal authority
legal

Examples

The court authorized the seizure of assets connected to the fraud case.
After failing to pay his debts, John faced the seizure of his property by the authorities.
The customs officers ordered the immediate seizure of the illegally imported goods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confiscation - the act of officially taking something away, especially as a penalty

Etymology

From Old French *seisir* (to seize) and Medieval Latin *sacire*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈsiːʒə / (BrE)
the act of taking possession of property by force or due to legal authority, especially from its owner
forcible taking of property
legal

Examples

The police carried out the seizure of the suspect's assets following the investigation.
During the raid, officials ordered the seizure of all illegal documents found on the premises.
The landlord's seizure of the property was later challenged in court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confiscation - the act of taking or seizing someone's property with authority, often as a penalty
  • Synonym: forfeiture - loss or giving up of property as punishment or penalty

Etymology

From Old French 'seisir' (to take possession), from Medieval Latin 'sacīre'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsiʒər / (AmE), / ˈsiːʒə / (BrE)
the act of taking control of a person, property, or place by force or legal authority
act of taking by force or authority

Examples

The police made the seizure of the fugitive early this morning.
Unlawful seizure of property is a violation of civil rights.
The army's seizure of the city was swift and unexpected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capture - the act of catching or taking control of someone or something
  • Antonym: release - the act of setting someone or something free

Etymology

From seize + -ure, modeled after measure, from Old French seisir (to take possession of).

4. NOUN

/ ˈsiːʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈsiːʒə / (BrE)
A sudden attack or recurrence of an illness, especially a sudden episode of abnormal electrical activity in the brain, such as in epilepsy.
sudden attack of illness
medical

Examples

The patient experienced a seizure after missing his medication.
During the test, she suddenly had a seizure and fell to the floor.
People with epilepsy are at risk of repeated seizures.

Synset Examples

he suffered an epileptic seizure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convulsion - a violent, involuntary contraction of the muscles

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'seisir', from Old French 'seisir' (to seize).
