meerschaum

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪrˌʃɑm / (AmE), / ˈmɪəʃəm / (BrE)
a tobacco pipe whose bowl is made from meerschaum, a light, porous, white mineral.
pipe with bowl made from meerschaum mineral

Examples

He proudly displayed his new meerschaum on the mantelpiece.
She enjoyed smoking her favorite blend in a carved meerschaum.
The antique shop had several meerschaum pipes with intricate designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipe - smoking instrument, typically with a bowl and stem

Etymology

From German 'Meerschaum', literally 'sea foam', referring to the mineral's appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪrʃəm / (AmE), / ˈmɪəʃɔːm / (BrE)
a soft, white, clay-like mineral, often used to make tobacco pipes
white clay-like mineral; used for pipes

Examples

He admired the intricately carved meerschaum pipe in the antique shop.
The distinctive white appearance of meerschaum makes it popular for ornamental objects.
A century-old meerschaum was passed down through the family as a treasured heirloom.

Etymology

From German 'Meerschaum', meaning 'sea foam', due to its frothy appearance.
