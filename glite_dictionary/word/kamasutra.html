Kamasutra

1. NOUN

/ kɑːməˈsuːtrə / (AmE), / kɑːməˈsuːtrə / (BrE)
an ancient Sanskrit text that sets out rules for sensual pleasure, love, and marriage, especially in accordance with traditional Hindu teachings
ancient Sanskrit treatise on love and sensual pleasure

Examples

The Kamasutra is often cited as the world's most famous ancient text on sexual practices.
He studied the Kamasutra to better understand the historical perspectives on love and relationships.
References to the Kamasutra can be found in discussions about Indian culture and literature.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Kāmasūtra', literally 'Aphorisms on Love', from 'kāma' (desire) + 'sūtra' (thread, guideline).
