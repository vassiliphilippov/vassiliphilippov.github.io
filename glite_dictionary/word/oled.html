OLED

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊˌlɛd / (AmE), / ˈəʊˌlɛd / (BrE)
a type of self-emitting diode used for display screens, which produces light when an electrical current is applied and does not require backlighting
self-emitting display diode

Examples

The new smartphone features a vibrant OLED display for enhanced color and contrast.
Many modern televisions now use OLED panels for their superior picture quality.
Thanks to its thinness and flexibility, OLED technology is popular in wearable devices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: LED - a light-emitting diode

Etymology

Acronym for 'organic light-emitting diode'.
