minstrelsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnstrəlsi / (AmE), / ˈmɪnstrəlsi / (BrE)
A company or troupe of minstrels, typically musicians or performers who entertain in a group.
troupe of minstrels

Examples

The minstrelsy traveled from village to village performing songs and tales.
A famous minstrelsy was invited to entertain at the royal court.
The town square was alive with music when the minstrelsy played their lively tunes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minstrel - a medieval singer or musician, especially one who sang or recited poetry

Etymology

From Middle English minstrelsie, from Anglo-Norman menestralsie, related to minstrel (from Old French ménestrel).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnstrəlsi / (AmE), / ˈmɪnstrəlsi / (BrE)
the art, performance, or practice of minstrels, especially as practiced by medieval singers or musicians; also, the works or songs of minstrels
art or music of medieval minstrels

Examples

The tradition of minstrelsy flourished in the courts of medieval Europe.
He dedicated his life to the study and preservation of Scottish minstrelsy.
Echoes of ancient minstrelsy can still be heard in modern folk music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minstrel - a medieval singer, musician, or poet who entertained people

Etymology

From Middle English minstralsie, from Old French menestrel (minstrel) + -sie (suffix forming nouns).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnstrəlsi / (AmE), / ˈmɪn.strəl.si / (BrE)
the art or practice of minstrels, especially the singing of ballads or the collective body of songs sung by minstrels
the singing of ballads by minstrels

Examples

The ancient tradition of minstrelsy was an important part of medieval court entertainment.
He studied the art of minstrelsy to learn how minstrels composed and performed their ballads.
The museum exhibit features manuscripts and instruments relating to the history of minstrelsy in Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballad - a narrative poem or song
  • Synonym: minstrel - a medieval poet and musician

Etymology

From Middle English minstralsie, from Old French menestralsie, related to 'minstrel' (a medieval singer or musician).
