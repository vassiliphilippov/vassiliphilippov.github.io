lunch

1. NOUN

/ lʌntʃ / (AmE), / lʌntʃ / (BrE)
a meal eaten in the middle of the day
midday meal

Examples

We usually have lunch together at noon in the cafeteria.
She packed a sandwich and fruit for her lunch today.
Business meetings are often scheduled over lunch to save time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinner - the main meal of the day, often eaten in the evening
  • Antonym: breakfast - the first meal of the day, eaten in the morning
  • Degree Lower: snack - a small amount of food eaten between meals

Etymology

Shortened from 'luncheon', of uncertain origin, possibly from Old English or Old French.

2. VERB

/ lʌntʃ / (AmE), / lʌntʃ / (BrE)
to provide a midday meal for someone
provide a midday meal for someone

Examples

She always lunches her colleagues when they visit from out of town.
The company lunched its clients at a fashionable restaurant.
He enjoys lunching us on Sundays after church.

Synset Examples

She lunched us well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dine - to eat dinner or have a main meal

Etymology

From luncheon, meaning a light meal; first used as a verb in the 19th century.

3. VERB

/ lʌntʃ / (AmE), / lʌntʃ / (BrE)
to eat lunch; to take the midday meal.
to eat lunch

Examples

We usually lunch together at noon during the week.
She prefers to lunch outdoors when the weather is nice.
They lunched at a small café near the river yesterday.

Synset Examples

At what time are you lunching?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dine - to eat dinner or have a meal
  • Degree Lower: breakfast - to eat breakfast
  • Degree Higher: dinner - to eat dinner

Etymology

From the noun 'lunch', itself a shortened form of 'luncheon' (18th century).
