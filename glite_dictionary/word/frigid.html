frigid

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈfrɪdʒɪd / (BrE)
(of a person, especially a woman) showing or feeling little or no sexual desire or response.
showing little sexual desire

Examples

Despite his affection, she remained frigid and distant during their relationship.
The novel depicts a frigid character who seems incapable of intimacy.
Calling someone frigid is now seen as outdated and insensitive.

Synset Examples

was cold to his advances
a frigid woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold - emotionally or sexually unresponsive

Etymology

From Latin 'frigidus' (cold), from 'frigere' (to be cold).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfrɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈfrɪdʒɪd / (BrE)
lacking warmth or friendliness; cold and unwelcoming in manner or attitude
emotionally cold or unfriendly

Examples

She gave me a frigid glance and walked away without speaking.
Despite his sincere apology, her response remained frigid and distant.
The frigid tone of the meeting made everyone uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cold - lacking affection or warmth of feeling
