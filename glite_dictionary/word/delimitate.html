to delimitate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈlɪməˌteɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪmɪteɪt / (BrE)
to determine and establish the limits or boundaries of something; to define precisely the essential qualities of something
set limits; define boundaries

Examples

Scientists must delimitate the key characteristics that make each species unique.
It is difficult to delimitate the boundaries between art and science.
The committee was tasked to delimitate the project’s scope before beginning any work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to define - state or describe exactly the nature, scope, or meaning of
  • Synonym: to demarcate - set the boundaries or limits of

Etymology

From Latin delimitatus, past participle of delimitare, meaning to mark out the limits.

2. VERB

/ dɪˈlɪməˌteɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪmɪteɪt / (BrE)
to set, mark, or draw the boundaries or limits of something
mark boundaries or limits

Examples

The architects delimitated the area where the new park would be built.
It is important to delimitate responsibilities clearly within the team.
The treaty delimitated the border between the two countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to delineate - to describe or portray something precisely, or to outline or define

Etymology

From Latin 'dēlīmitāre', from 'de-' (from, away) + 'līmitāre' (to limit).

Synset POS: v
