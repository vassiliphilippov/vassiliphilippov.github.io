fineness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being very narrow or thin; extreme slenderness or delicacy.
quality of being very narrow or thin

Examples

The fineness of the thread made it ideal for delicate embroidery.
She noticed the fineness of the artist's brush strokes up close.
They admired the fineness of the model's features in the sculpted statue.

Synset Examples

he marvelled at the fineness of her hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thinness - the quality of having little thickness; being thin

Etymology

Derived from 'fine' + the suffix '-ness', meaning the state or quality of being fine.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪn.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfaɪn.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being very fine in texture, detail, or grain.
very fine texture or quality

Examples

The fineness of the flour makes it perfect for delicate pastries.
He admired the fineness of the artist’s brushwork in the painting.
The jeweler assessed the fineness of the gold under a magnifying glass.

Synset Examples

the fineness of the sand on the beach

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coarseness - the quality of being rough or not fine in texture

Etymology

From Old French 'finesse' (delicacy), from 'fin' (end, fine).
