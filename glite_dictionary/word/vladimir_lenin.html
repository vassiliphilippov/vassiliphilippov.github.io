Vladimir Lenin

1. PROPN

/ vlɑːˈdɪmɪr ˈlɛnɪn / (AmE), / vləˈdɪmɪə ˈlɛnɪn / (BrE)
(1870–1924) Russian revolutionary leader, founder of the Bolsheviks, leader of the Russian Revolution, and first head of the USSR.
Russian revolutionary leader and first head of USSR

Examples

Vladimir Lenin played a pivotal role in shaping 20th-century Russian history as the leader of the Bolshevik Revolution.
Many scholars have studied the influence of Vladimir Lenin on modern communist states.
A statue of Vladimir Lenin still stands in some cities of the former Soviet Union.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Leninism - political theory and practice based on Lenin's work

Etymology

Vladimir is a common Russian forename; Lenin was adopted as a pseudonym, possibly derived from the Lena River, Russia.
