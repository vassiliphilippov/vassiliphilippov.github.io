octameter

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˈtæmɪtər / (AmE), / ɒkˈtæmɪtə / (BrE)
A line of verse consisting of eight metrical feet.
verse line with eight metrical feet

Examples

The poet experimented with octameter to create an unusually long and rhythmic line.
It is rare to find English verses written entirely in octameter.
The rhythmic structure of octameter can be challenging to sustain throughout a poem.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tetrameter - a line of verse with four metrical feet
  • Degree Lower: pentameter - a line of verse with five metrical feet
  • Degree Lower: hexameter - a line of verse with six metrical feet

Etymology

From Latin 'octo' (eight) + Greek 'metron' (measure).
