self-evident

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlfˈɛv.ə.dənt / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈɛv.ɪ.dənt / (BrE)
Clear or obvious without needing any proof or explanation.
obvious; requiring no proof

Examples

It is self-evident that hard work leads to success.
The solution to the problem was self-evident once she explained it.
Some basic human rights are considered self-evident truths.

Synset Examples

an axiomatic truth
we hold these truths to be self-evident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear, self-evident
  • Antonym: uncertain - not able to be relied on; not known or definite

Etymology

Formed from 'self' (referring to itself) and 'evident' (obvious, clear). Used since the early 18th century.
