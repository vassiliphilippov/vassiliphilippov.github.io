alehouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪlˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈeɪl.haʊs / (BrE)
A tavern or public house where ale is sold and consumed, especially in former times.
tavern selling ale
archaic

Examples

The weary travelers stopped at a local alehouse to rest and have a drink.
Many old English villages still have buildings that once served as alehouses.
He told tales of adventure over mugs of ale in the dimly lit alehouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tavern - an establishment for the sale of beer and other drinks
  • Synonym: inn - a pub, typically one in the country, in some cases providing accommodation
  • Synonym: pub - a public house; a place where alcoholic drinks are served

Etymology

From Middle English alehous, from Old English ealu (ale) + hūs (house).
