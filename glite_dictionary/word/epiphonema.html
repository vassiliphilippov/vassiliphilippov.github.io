epiphonema

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈfəʊnəmə / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈfəʊniːmə / (BrE)
A memorable or emphatic sentence, often an exclamation or succinct summary, that expresses or sums up what has previously been said.
memorable summarizing or exclamatory sentence

Examples

The speech ended with an epiphonema that left the audience in stunned silence.
Authors often use an epiphonema to deliver a powerful final thought to their readers.
Her novel concludes with an epiphonema encapsulating the central themes of the story.

Synset Examples

In Chaucer's The Reeve's Tale, the Reeve drives home his story of a cheating Miller by summarizing the tale that he has told in an epiphonema.

Etymology

From Latin epiphonema, from Greek epiphonēma (exclamation, emphatic phrase), from epiphōnein (to exclaim).
