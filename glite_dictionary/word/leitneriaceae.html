Leitneriaceae

1. NOUN

/ laɪtˌnɪəriˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / laɪtˌnɪəriˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a small family of flowering plants, coextensive with the genus Leitneria, sometimes placed in its own distinct order.
a family of flowering plants (Leitneria)

Examples

The Leitneriaceae are known for their unique wood anatomy among flowering plants.
Researchers have had difficulty classifying Leitneriaceae due to its distinctive botanical features.
The rare trees in the Leitneriaceae family are native to parts of the southern United States.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Leitneria, named after E.F. Leitner, a 19th-century German naturalist, plus the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
