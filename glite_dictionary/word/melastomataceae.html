Melastomataceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˌstoʊməˈteɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌmɛləsˌtəʊməˈteɪsiː / (BrE)
a large family of tropical and subtropical flowering plants, including trees, shrubs, and herbs, many cultivated as ornamentals
a family of tropical flowering plants

Examples

Many species in the Melastomataceae family are prized for their vibrant flowers in botanical gardens.
Researchers discovered a new shrub belonging to the Melastomataceae in the Amazon rainforest.
The Melastomataceae includes both small herbs and large ornamental trees.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Melastoma, from Greek 'melas' (black) and 'stoma' (mouth), referring to the staining properties of the fruits.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛl.əˌstoʊ.məˈteɪ.si.i / (AmE), / ˌmɛl.əˌstəʊ.məˈtiː.si.iː / (BrE)
A family of tropical trees, shrubs, and herbs of the order Myrtales, many of which are grown as ornamental plants.
family of tropical trees, shrubs, and herbs

Examples

Many species in the Melastomataceae family are prized for their vibrant purple flowers.
The Melastomataceae are commonly found in tropical regions around the world.
Botanists study the Melastomataceae to understand plant biodiversity in rainforests.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Melastomataceae, based on the type genus Melastoma + -aceae (family suffix).
