appendaged

1. ADJ

/ əˈpɛndɪdʒd / (AmE), / əˈpɛndɪdʒd / (BrE)
having one or more appendages (such as limbs, feelers, or projections) attached to a main body.
having an appendage or appendages

Examples

The insect's appendaged legs allow it to cling securely to branches.
Some sea creatures are highly appendaged, with arms and tentacles radiating from their bodies.
The fossil displayed a strangely appendaged structure protruding from its side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appendage - a projecting part attached to a main body

Etymology

From 'appendage' + '-ed' suffix, formed in analogy with other adjectives.
