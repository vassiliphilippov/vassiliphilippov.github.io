Qaddafi

1. PROPN

/ kəˈdɑːfi / (AmE), / kaˈdæfi / (BrE)
Muammar Qaddafi (1942–2011), Libyan leader who ruled from 1969 to 2011 after seizing power in a coup and establishing a socialist state based on his interpretation of Islamic principles.
Libyan leader (1942–2011), ruled 1969–2011

Examples

During his rule, Qaddafi was known for his controversial political philosophy and leadership style.
The death of Qaddafi marked a turning point in Libya's modern history.
Many international organizations imposed sanctions on Qaddafi's government during his time in power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaddafi - Alternative transliteration of 'Qaddafi', Libyan leader 1969–2011

Etymology

From the Arabic surname القذافي (al-Qaddāfī).
