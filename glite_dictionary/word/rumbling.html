rumbling

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌm.blɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌm.blɪŋ / (BrE)
a loud, low, dull, continuous noise; a deep sound that continues for a while, often produced by thunder, machinery, or internal bodily processes
loud, low, continuous noise

Examples

The rumbling of the distant train could be heard throughout the night.
Suddenly, a deep rumbling came from beneath the ground, shaking the windows.
He woke up to the rumbling of his empty stomach.

Synset Examples

they heard the rumbling of thunder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roar - a loud, deep, long sound
  • Degree Lower: hum - a low, steady, continuous sound

Etymology

From the verb 'rumble', which comes from Middle English 'rumbelen', probably of imitative origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrʌm.blɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌm.blɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a sound) continuous, deep, and low-pitched, often with a throbbing quality.
continuous low-pitched sound

Examples

A rumbling noise came from the old furnace in the basement.
The rumbling thunder made the windows shake during the storm.
He felt a rumbling sensation in his stomach before lunch.

Synset Examples

the rumbling rolling sound of thunder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thunderous - very loud and powerful sound like thunder
  • Synonym: roaring - a loud, deep, and continuous sound
  • Antonym: silent - completely without sound

Etymology

From verb 'rumble', which is probably of imitative origin.
