to crucify

1. VERB

/ ˈkruːsəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkruːsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to kill someone by nailing or fastening them to a cross, typically as a form of execution
execute by nailing to a cross

Examples

The Romans would sometimes crucify criminals as a public warning.
Historians debate the exact location where Jesus was crucified.
In ancient times, authorities used to crucify perceived enemies to maintain order.

Synset Examples

Jesus Christ was crucified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to execute - to carry out a sentence of death on someone
  • Antonym: hang - to kill by hanging as a form of execution

Etymology

From Latin crucifigere, from crux (cross) + figere (to fix, fasten).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkruːsəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkruːsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to treat someone with extreme cruelty or severity, especially in punishment or criticism
treat with extreme cruelty or severity

Examples

The media was quick to crucify the politician after the scandal broke.
She felt her classmates would crucify her if she made another mistake.
Critics tend to crucify the film for its lack of originality.

Synset Examples

The children tormented the stuttering teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torment - to cause severe suffering or agony
  • Synonym: to punish - to subject to punishment for an offence
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin 'crucifigere' (to fasten to a cross): 'crux' (cross) + 'figere' (to fix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01806967-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈkruːsəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkruːsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to criticize someone severely or violently, especially in public
criticize harshly; attack verbally

Examples

The media crucified the actor for his insensitive comments.
The manager was crucified by his team after the failed project.
She felt the public would crucify her if the truth ever came out.

Synset Examples

The press savaged the new President
The critics crucified the author for plagiarizing a famous passage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savage - to attack or criticize fiercely
  • Synonym: to lambaste - to criticize harshly
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin 'crucificare', from 'crux' (cross) + 'facere' (to make).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
