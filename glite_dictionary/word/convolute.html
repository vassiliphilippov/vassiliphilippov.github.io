convolute

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑn.vəˌlut / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.və.luːt / (BrE)
(of leaves, petals, etc.) rolled or coiled upon themselves lengthwise, especially before opening.
rolled upon itself lengthwise (botany)

Examples

The flower has convolute petals that unroll as they bloom.
In some plants, the young leaves are convolute before they expand.
The convolute structure helps protect the delicate inner tissues.

Synset Examples

a convolute petal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rolled - formed into a cylindrical shape by turning over itself
  • Antonym: straight - not curved or bent

Etymology

From Latin 'convolutus', past participle of 'convolvere' meaning 'to roll together'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːnvəˌluːt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnvəˌluːt / (BrE)
to twist, wind, or coil together, especially in a complex way
to twist or coil together

Examples

The vines began to convolute around the old wooden fence as spring progressed.
Her long hair would often convolute into knots if not carefully brushed every day.
The artist let the wires convolute in intricate patterns across the sculpture's surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entwine - to twist or wind round together; interweave
  • Synonym: to intertwine - to twist or twine together

Etymology

From Latin 'convolutus', past participle of 'convolvere' (to roll together).
