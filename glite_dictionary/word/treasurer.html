treasurer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛʒərər / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒ(ə)rə / (BrE)
an official responsible for collecting, managing, and disbursing funds, especially for an organization, company, or government
person managing funds for an organization

Examples

The club elected a new treasurer to manage its finances for the year.
As company treasurer, she ensures all financial records are accurate and up to date.
Every non-profit organization must have a treasurer to oversee donations and expenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cashier - person responsible for receiving and paying out money, usually in a shop or bank

Etymology

From Old French 'tresorier', from 'tresor' (treasure), from Latin 'thēsaurus'.
