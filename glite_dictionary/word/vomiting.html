vomiting

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑːmɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈvɒmɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
The reflex act of ejecting the contents of the stomach through the mouth.
ejecting stomach contents through mouth
medical

Examples

The patient was experiencing frequent vomiting after eating the spoiled food.
Signs of dehydration can occur if vomiting continues for several hours.
Morning sickness in pregnancy often includes nausea and vomiting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emesis - medical term for vomiting
  • Degree Lower: nausea - feeling of sickness with an inclination to vomit

Etymology

From Latin *vomitare*, frequentative of *vomere* (to vomit).
