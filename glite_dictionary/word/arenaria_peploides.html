Arenaria peploides

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrəˈnɛəriə ˌpɛpˈlɔɪˌdiz / (AmE), / ˌɛrəˈnɑːriə ˌpiːplɔɪˈdiːz / (BrE)
A perennial succulent herb of coastal regions, with small solitary flowers, commonly known as sea sandwort.
perennial coastal succulent herb

Examples

The creeping stems of Arenaria peploides help stabilize sandy shorelines.
The flowers of Arenaria peploides are small and grow singly in leaf axils.
Botanists surveyed the coastal dunes for populations of Arenaria peploides.

Etymology

From Latin: 'arenaria' (relating to sand), 'peploides' from Greek 'peplos' (covering, cloak) with '-oides' (likeness).
