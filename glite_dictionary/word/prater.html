prater

1. NOUN

/ ˈpreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈpreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who talks at length in a foolish or tiresome way, especially in an obnoxious or boastful manner.
obnoxious, foolishly talkative person

Examples

Nobody at the meeting had patience for the endless ramblings of the prater.
Every group has at least one prater who monopolizes the conversation with nonsense.
She rolled her eyes as the self-important prater launched into another pointless story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatterbox - a person who talks a lot, especially about trivial things
  • Synonym: prate - to talk foolishly or tediously about something
  • Antonym: listener - a person who listens rather than talks

Etymology

From the verb 'prate', from Middle Dutch praten (to chatter, talk foolishly).
