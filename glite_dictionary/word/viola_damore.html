viola d'amore

1. NOUN

/ viˈoʊlə dəˈmɔːr / (AmE), / viˈəʊlə dɑːˈmɔː / (BrE)
A stringed musical instrument of the viol family, typically with six or seven playing strings and additional sympathetic strings, and played with a bow.
stringed instrument of the viol family

Examples

The musician played a beautiful solo on the viola d'amore during the concert.
Her collection includes both a baroque violin and a viola d'amore.
Many composers wrote chamber works featuring the viola d'amore in the eighteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viol - any of a family of stringed instruments played with a bow

Etymology

From Italian 'viola d'amore' meaning 'viola of love'.
