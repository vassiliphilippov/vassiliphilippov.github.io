plasterwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæstərˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstəwɜːk / (BrE)
A surface or decorative feature made of hardened plaster, such as found on walls or ceilings.
surface or decoration of hardened plaster

Examples

The ornate plasterwork on the ceiling was restored to its original beauty.
After years of neglect, the plasterwork in the hallway began to crumble.
Crumbling plasterwork revealed the old brick beneath the walls.

Synset Examples

there were cracks in the plaster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaster - material used for coating walls and ceilings

Etymology

From 'plaster' (building material) + 'work' (physical labor or creation).
