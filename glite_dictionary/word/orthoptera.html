Orthoptera

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈθɑptərə / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒptərə / (BrE)
an order of insects that includes grasshoppers, locusts, and crickets, characterized by straight wings and jumping hind legs
order of insects including grasshoppers and crickets

Examples

Orthoptera includes familiar insects such as grasshoppers and crickets.
Scientists study the Orthoptera to learn about insect behavior and adaptation.
In warm summer evenings, the sound of the Orthoptera can be heard in the fields.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek orthos (straight) + pteron (wing).
