blockbuster

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɑkˌbʌstər / (AmE), / ˈblɒkˌbʌstə / (BrE)
a film, book, play, or other work that is extremely popular and commercially successful
hugely popular and successful work

Examples

The latest superhero movie was a blockbuster, earning millions worldwide.
Every summer, studios hope to release a blockbuster that draws large audiences.
Her novel became an instant blockbuster and topped bestseller lists for months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit - a successful and popular entertainment work
  • Antonym: flop - an unsuccessful entertainment work

Etymology

Originally U.S. slang (1940s) for a devastating bomb; by the 1950s, applied metaphorically to anything with great impact, especially films.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblɑkˌbʌstər / (AmE), / ˈblɒkˌbʌstə / (BrE)
a very large bomb designed to destroy an entire city block or extensive area
a large bomb for demolishing large areas

Examples

The city was devastated after a blockbuster was dropped during the air raid.
Historians noted the use of the blockbuster as a turning point in aerial warfare.
The enormous crater left by the blockbuster was visible for years after the bombing.

Etymology

Originally used during World War II for bombs capable of destroying a city block ('block' + 'buster').
