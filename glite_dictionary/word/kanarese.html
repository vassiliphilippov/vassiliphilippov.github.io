Kanarese

1. NOUN

/ ˌkænəˈriːz / (AmE), / ˌkanəˈriːz / (BrE)
a Dravidian language spoken mainly in the state of Karnataka in southern India, also known as Kannada
Dravidian language of southern India

Examples

Many historical manuscripts are written in Kanarese.
He studied both Hindi and Kanarese during his time in India.
The film was dubbed into several languages, including Kanarese and Tamil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kannada - the principal Dravidian language of Karnataka, India

Etymology

From Kannada *kannaḍa (name of the language or people), with influence from English word formation patterns.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnəˈriːz / (AmE), / ˌkanəˈriːz / (BrE)
a member of a Kannada-speaking people primarily inhabiting the Kanara region of southern India
member of a Kannada-speaking group in southern India

Examples

Many Kanarese migrated to urban centers in search of better opportunities.
The traditional dress of the Kanarese is vibrant and distinctive.
As a Kanarese, she is fluent in both Kannada and English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kannadiga - a native or inhabitant of Karnataka who speaks Kannada

Etymology

From Kannada (the language) + -ese (forming names of peoples/languages); Kanara is a region in southern India.
