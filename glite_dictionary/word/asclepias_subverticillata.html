Asclepias subverticillata

1. NOUN

/ æsˈkliːpiəs ˌsʌbvərˌtɪsɪˈleɪtə / (AmE), / æsˈkliːpɪəs ˌsʌbvɜːtɪsɪˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A species of milkweed native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, known to be poisonous to livestock.
Southwestern U.S. and Mexican milkweed species

Examples

Asclepias subverticillata is commonly found in arid regions of New Mexico and Arizona.
Farmers are cautious of Asclepias subverticillata because it is toxic to cattle and sheep.
The delicate white flowers of Asclepias subverticillata attract various pollinators in the late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkweed - a group of plants in the genus Asclepias

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Asclepias' from Greek Asklepiás (after Asclepius, the god of medicine); species epithet 'subverticillata' meaning 'somewhat whorled'.
