drawer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔr / (AmE), / ˈdrɔː.ə / (BrE)
A box-shaped compartment in a piece of furniture, designed to slide in and out for storing things.
sliding compartment in furniture

Examples

She opened the top drawer to look for her notebook.
Please keep your keys in the middle drawer so you don't lose them.
The desk has three spacious drawers for organizing office supplies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabinet - A piece of furniture with shelves or drawers for storage
  • Antonym: shelf - A flat, horizontal surface used for storing or displaying items, often in furniture

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔːr / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːə / (BrE)
the person who writes and signs a check or draft, instructing a bank or another party to pay money to someone else
person who writes a check or draft
legal

Examples

The drawer must endorse the check before it can be deposited.
If the drawer does not have sufficient funds, the check may bounce.
Legally, the drawer is responsible for the validity of the payment instrument.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: drawee - person or bank ordered to pay on a check or draft
  • Antonym: payee - person to whom a check or draft is made payable

Etymology

From draw + -er, referring to the person who draws (writes out) a financial instrument.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10052249-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔːər / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːə / (BrE)
an artist who is skilled at drawing pictures
person skilled at drawing

Examples

As a talented drawer, she filled notebooks with intricate sketches from her travels.
The magazine featured a drawer known for his expressive portraits.
Though primarily a painter, he was also an accomplished drawer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artist - a person who creates art, especially paintings or drawings

Etymology

From the verb 'draw' (to produce a picture) + -er (agent noun suffix).
