distaste

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈteɪst / (AmE), / dɪsˈteɪst / (BrE)
A feeling of dislike or aversion towards something.
feeling of dislike or aversion

Examples

She expressed her distaste for the idea of working weekends.
His distaste for spicy food was evident at the dinner table.
There was a clear distaste in her voice when she spoke of the incident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aversion - a strong feeling of dislike or opposition
  • Synonym: disgust - a strong sense of dislike or revulsion
  • Antonym: fondness - a feeling of affection or liking

Etymology

From Middle French 'distaste' (dislike), from 'des-' (expressing reversal) + 'taste' (to perceive flavor).
