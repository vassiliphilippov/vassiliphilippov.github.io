galbanum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæl.bə.nəm / (AmE), / ˈɡæl.bə.nəm / (BrE)
a bitter, aromatic gum resin obtained from certain plants, used in perfumes and incense, and resembling asafetida
bitter aromatic gum resin

Examples

The perfume was enriched by a hint of galbanum in its composition.
Some incense blends rely on galbanum for their unique aroma.
Historically, galbanum was prized for its medicinal qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asafetida - a pungent gum resin used in medicine and cookery

Etymology

From Latin galbanum, from Greek *galbanon*, from Semitic origins.
