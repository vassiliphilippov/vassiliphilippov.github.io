macaroni

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækəˈroʊni / (AmE), / ˌmækəˈrəʊni / (BrE)
A type of dry pasta shaped like slender tubes, commonly used in various dishes.
tubular pasta

Examples

She made a delicious cheese sauce to go with the macaroni.
I prefer macaroni to spaghetti in my pasta salad.
The casserole contained chicken, vegetables, and plenty of macaroni.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasta - A type of food made from wheat and water, typically shaped and dried

Etymology

From Italian 'maccheroni', plural of 'maccherone'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmækəˈroʊni / (AmE), / ˌmækəˈrəʊni / (BrE)
a fashionable young man in 18th-century Britain who adopted extravagant Continental European manners and styles
18th-century fashionable British dandy
archaic british

Examples

In the 1770s, being called a macaroni implied you dressed in the height of absurd fashion.
The painting depicts a young macaroni with elaborate wigs and colorful attire.
Satirical cartoons often made fun of the extravagant lifestyle of a macaroni.

Synset Examples

Yankee Doodle stuck a feather in his cap and called it macaroni

Etymology

From Italian 'maccherone', later used in 18th-century England to mock young men with Continental tastes.
