savageness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsævɪdʒnəs / (AmE), / ˈsævɪdʒnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being wild, fierce, or untamed.
state of being wild or fierce

Examples

The savageness of the storm left the small village in ruins.
Her writing captured the savageness of life on the open frontier.
Many were shocked by the savageness of the debate between the two candidates.

Synset Examples

the coastline is littered with testaments to the savageness of the waters
a craving for barbaric splendor, for savagery and color and the throb of drums

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savagery - the quality of being fierce or brutal
  • Synonym: ferocity - the state of being ferocious
  • Antonym: gentleness - the quality of being gentle or tame

Etymology

Formed from 'savage' + '-ness', following the common English noun suffix pattern.
