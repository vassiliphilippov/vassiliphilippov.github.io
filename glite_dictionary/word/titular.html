titular

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪtʃələr / (AmE), / ˈtɪtjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to or denoting a legal title to something, especially ownership or rights held in name only
relating to a legal title
legal

Examples

The company maintained titular ownership of the property until the contract was finalized.
She held titular rights to the land, but had no power to sell it.
Although his name was on the deed, his titular claim was challenged in court.

Synset Examples

titulary rights

Etymology

From Latin 'titularis' (of or belonging to a title), from 'titulus' (title).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02827279-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɪtʃələr / (AmE), / ˈtɪtjʊlə / (BrE)
having a title of rank or nobility, often without the associated power or authority.
holding a title of nobility

Examples

Although the prince was titular head of state, real power lay with the elected officials.
She was a titular duchess, inheriting the title but not the lands.
Several titular nobles attended the ceremony, but few wielded actual influence.

Synset Examples

titular dignitaries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nominal - existing in name only, not in reality

Etymology

From Latin 'titulus' (title, inscription).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02826782-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈtɪtʃələr / (AmE), / ˈtɪtjʊlə / (BrE)
(of a role or position) existing in name or title only, without real authority or power.
holding a position in name only

Examples

Although he was the titular president, the board made all the important decisions.
She is the titular leader of the group, but her influence is limited.
The titular head of the company attends ceremonies but does not manage daily operations.

Synset Examples

the nominal (or titular) head of his party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nominal - existing in name only, not in reality
  • Antonym: actual - existing in fact, real or genuine

Etymology

From Latin 'titulus' (title).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01047056-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈtɪtʃələr / (AmE), / ˈtɪtjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to the title of a book, play, film, or other work of art; appearing in or taken from the title itself.
relating to the title of a work

Examples

The actor was perfectly cast in the titular role of the new stage production.
The novel's titular character faces moral dilemmas throughout the story.
Her analysis focused on the titular theme present in the film's opening scenes.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: title role - the role named in the title of a play or film

5. ADJ

/ ˈtɪtjələr / (AmE), / ˈtɪtjʊlə / (BrE)
having a formal title or designation, but often without the associated powers or duties.
having a title, often without real authority

Examples

He is the titular head of the committee, but decisions are made by others.
Although she holds the titular position of president, her influence is limited.
The country has a titular monarch who performs mainly ceremonial duties.

Synset Examples

of titular rank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nominal - existing in name only; not actual or real

Etymology

From Latin 'titularis' (of a title), from 'titulus' (title).
