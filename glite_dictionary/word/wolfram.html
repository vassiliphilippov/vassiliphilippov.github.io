wolfram

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊlˌfræm / (AmE), / ˈwʊlfrəm / (BrE)
a heavy, hard, grey-white metallic element, chiefly used in electrical applications; also known as tungsten (chemical symbol W)
metallic element (tungsten)

Examples

Spain is one of the world's largest producers of wolfram, which is vital for the electronics industry.
Light bulb filaments were traditionally made of wolfram because of its high melting point.
The mineral scheelite is an important source of wolfram.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tungsten - hard, heavy metallic element; another name for wolfram

Etymology

From German Wolfram, name for certain ores containing the element.
