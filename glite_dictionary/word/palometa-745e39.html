palometa

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæloʊˈmeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌpaləˈmiːtə / (BrE)
a small marine fish, similar to the Florida pompano, commonly found in the West Indies and prized as a food fish
small West Indian food fish

Examples

Fishermen in the Caribbean often catch palometa for both sport and food.
The grilled palometa at the local market was a local delicacy.
Due to its resemblance to the pompano, the palometa is sometimes mistaken for other similar fish.

Etymology

From Spanish 'palometa', meaning 'little dove', used as a name for certain fish species.
