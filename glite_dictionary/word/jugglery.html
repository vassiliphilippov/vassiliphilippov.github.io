jugglery

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌɡləri / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡləri / (BrE)
deceptive or artful trickery used to achieve a particular result; clever manipulation intended to mislead.
deceptive trickery; clever manipulation

Examples

Many investors lost money as a result of the financial jugglery practiced by dishonest executives.
The lawyer accused the opposing side of legal jugglery to confuse the jury.
Political jugglery often makes it hard to understand the real issues.

Synset Examples

the senator's tax program was mere jugglery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trickery - the practice of deception or cunning manipulation
  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving someone
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and free from deceit

Etymology

From French 'jonglerie', from 'jongleur' (juggler), ultimately from Latin 'ioculāri' (to jest, joke).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌɡ.lɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡ.ləri / (BrE)
the act or performance of juggling or deceptive tricks, especially as entertainment
performance of juggling tricks

Examples

The crowd was amazed by the magician's clever jugglery on stage.
His jugglery with five balls was the highlight of the evening.
Street performers often combine comedy with impressive jugglery acts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juggling - the skill of keeping several objects in motion in the air

Etymology

From Middle French 'jonglerie', from Old French, from 'jongleur' (juggler, minstrel), from Latin 'ioculātor' (joker).
