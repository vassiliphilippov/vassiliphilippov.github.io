Glechoma

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɪˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A small genus of creeping perennial herbs in the mint family, commonly known as ground ivy.
creeping herb genus (ground ivy)

Examples

Glechoma is often used as a ground cover in shady gardens.
The leaves of Glechoma have a distinctive scalloped edge and minty aroma.
Botanists observed the rapid spread of Glechoma in the forest understory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground ivy - common name for Glechoma hederacea, a creeping plant in the mint family

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name, based on Greek *glechon* (a kind of mint).

2. NOUN

/ ɡləˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A small genus of creeping perennial herbs in the mint family, including ground ivy.
genus of creeping herbs (mint family)

Examples

Glechoma is commonly found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
Gardeners often regard Glechoma as a persistent weed due to its rapid spread.
The most well-known member of Glechoma is ground ivy, valued for its medicinal uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground ivy - a creeping perennial plant of the mint family, Glechoma hederacea

Etymology

From Greek *glechon* (pennyroyal) + -oma (indicating result or body).
