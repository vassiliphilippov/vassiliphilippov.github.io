transexual

1. NOUN

/ trænˈsɛksjuəl / (AmE), / trænˈsɛksjʊəl / (BrE)
a person whose gender identity is entirely with the opposite sex, often undergoing medical treatment to physically change their sex
person identifying as the opposite sex

Examples

As a transexual, she faced many challenges in her life and career.
The documentary highlights the experiences of transexual individuals during the 1980s.
Medical support is crucial for transexual people undergoing gender reassignment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transgender - person whose gender identity differs from their birth sex

Etymology

From 'trans-' (across, beyond) + 'sexual', formed in mid-20th century.

2. NOUN

/ trænˈsɛkʃuəl / (AmE), / trænˈsɛksjʊəl / (BrE)
a person who has undergone or is undergoing a medical procedure to change their sex from male to female or vice versa
person who has had a sex change operation

Examples

After her surgery, she openly identified as a transexual and advocated for the community.
Doctors and support groups helped the transexual adjust to life after the operation.
He wrote a memoir about his experiences as a transexual navigating societal acceptance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transgender - a person whose gender identity differs from the sex they were assigned at birth

Etymology

From Latin 'trans-' (across) + 'sexual'. First used in English in the mid-20th century.
