enamelware

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnæməlˌwɛr / (AmE), / ɪˈnæməlˌweə / (BrE)
household goods, especially kitchen utensils, made of metal coated with a layer of enamel for a smooth, durable finish
enameled household/kitchen utensils

Examples

The vintage enamelware kettle added charm to their farmhouse kitchen.
She prefers to use enamelware because it is both sturdy and easy to clean.
Many campers choose enamelware for its light weight and durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cookware - pots, pans, and other items used for cooking

Etymology

From 'enamel' (a glassy coating) + 'ware' (objects of a specified type).
