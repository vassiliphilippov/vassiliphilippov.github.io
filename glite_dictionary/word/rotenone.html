rotenone

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊtəˌnoʊn / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtənəʊn / (BrE)
a naturally occurring, white crystalline substance used as an insecticide and piscicide, especially in home gardens; extracted from the roots of certain plants such as derris and cube, and known for its low toxicity to mammals
a plant-derived insecticide
medical

Examples

The gardener used rotenone to control the aphid infestation on her tomato plants.
Rotenone is extracted from the roots of certain tropical plants and used as a natural insecticide.
Because of its low toxicity to humans, rotenone is preferred in organic gardening for pest control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insecticide - a substance used for killing insects
  • Synonym: pesticide - a substance used to destroy pests

Etymology

From Latin 'Rōtēnus' (a plant genus name) and chemical suffix '-one'.
