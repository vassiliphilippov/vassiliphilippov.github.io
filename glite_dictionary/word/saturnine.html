saturnine

1. ADJ

/ ˈsæt̬.ɚ.naɪn / (AmE), / ˈsat.ə.nʌɪn / (BrE)
(of a person or their manner) slow and gloomy; having a moody or sullen temperament.
gloomy or sullen in mood or appearance

Examples

He had a saturnine expression that made people hesitant to approach him.
The old mansion added to the saturnine atmosphere of the novel.
Despite his saturnine demeanor, he was kind-hearted once you got to know him.

Synset Examples

a dark scowl
the proverbially dour New England Puritan
a glum, hopeless shrug
he sat in moody silence
a morose and unsociable manner
a saturnine, almost misanthropic young genius
a sour temper
a sullen crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morose - having a sullen and gloomy disposition
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Latin 'Saturninus', relating to Saturn, considered in astrology to influence gloominess.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsæt.ərˌnaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsat.jʊə.naɪn / (BrE)
(of a person or their manner) bitter or scornful; showing a gloomy, sullen, or morose temperament.
bitter, scornful, or gloomy in manner

Examples

His saturnine expression made people hesitate to approach him at the party.
Jane was intimidated by her boss's saturnine demeanor during meetings.
Despite the saturnine mood in the office, the team managed to complete their project on time.

Synset Examples

the face was saturnine and swarthy, and the sensual lips … twisted with disdain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomy - having a dark or sullen mood or appearance
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From Latin Saturninus, from Saturnus (the Roman god Saturn), associated in astrology with gloom and melancholy.
