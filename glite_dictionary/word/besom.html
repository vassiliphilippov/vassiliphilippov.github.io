besom

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːzəm / (AmE), / ˈbiːzəm / (BrE)
a broom made of twigs tied together on a long handle
broom made of tied twigs
archaic

Examples

The old witch swept her cottage with a besom made of birch twigs.
He leaned a besom against the garden wall after tidying up the leaves.
In the story, the besom was said to have magical powers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broom - a cleaning tool with bristles and a long handle

Etymology

From Old English *besma* (broom, brush).
