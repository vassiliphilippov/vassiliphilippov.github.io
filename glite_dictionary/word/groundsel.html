groundsel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndˌsəl / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊn(d)s(ə)l / (BrE)
A common Eurasian weed with small yellow flowers, often found in gardens and waste places.
a common Eurasian weed with yellow flowers

Examples

The garden was overrun with groundsel after the wet spring.
She carefully pulled every groundsel from between the paving stones.
Groundsel is known for spreading quickly and choking out other plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ragwort - weed with similar yellow flowers, sometimes confused with groundsel

Etymology

From Old English *grundeswelge*, meaning 'ground swallower', referring to its invasive growth.
