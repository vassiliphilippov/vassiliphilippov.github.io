obstructive

1. ADJ

/ əbˈstrʌktɪv / (AmE), / əbˈstrʌktɪv / (BrE)
causing a blockage or hindrance; preventing movement or progress.
causing obstruction; preventing movement

Examples

The fallen tree had an obstructive effect on traffic, causing delays throughout the city.
He grew impatient with her obstructive behavior during the meeting.
An obstructive mass in the pipe slowed the water flow considerably.

Synset Examples

the clogging crowds of revelers overflowing into the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blocking - causing something to be blocked or stopped
  • Antonym: unobstructed - not blocked, free from hindrance

Etymology

From Latin 'obstructus' (blocked up) + '-ive'.
