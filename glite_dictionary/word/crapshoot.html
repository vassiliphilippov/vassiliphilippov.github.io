crapshoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræpˌʃuːt / (AmE), / ˈkræpˌʃuːt / (BrE)
an activity or venture in which the outcome is highly uncertain and unpredictable, often depending largely on chance
risky and uncertain venture
informal american

Examples

Getting funding for a new tech startup can be a total crapshoot.
Whether or not your application is approved sometimes feels like a crapshoot.
Predicting the winner of the election turned out to be a crapshoot this year.

Synset Examples

getting admitted to the college of your choice has become a crapshoot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gamble - an action involving significant risk and chance
  • Antonym: sure thing - something certain to succeed or happen

Etymology

Originally American slang, from the dice game "craps" plus "shoot" (to throw dice).
