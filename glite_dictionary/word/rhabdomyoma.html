rhabdomyoma

1. NOUN

/ ræb.doʊ.maɪˈoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ræb.dəʊ.maɪˈəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A benign tumor composed of striated muscle tissue.
benign tumor of striated muscle
medical

Examples

The biopsy confirmed that the mass was a rhabdomyoma rather than a malignant tumor.
Pediatric rhabdomyoma is a rare condition found primarily in the heart.
Surgical removal is often curative for a rhabdomyoma in most cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myoma - a benign tumor made up of muscle tissue
  • Antonym: sarcoma - a malignant tumor of connective or other non-epithelial tissue

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'rhabdos' (rod), 'mys' (muscle), and '-oma' (tumor).
