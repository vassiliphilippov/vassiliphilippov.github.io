wasabi

1. NOUN

/ wəˈsɑːbi / (AmE), / wəˈsɑːbi / (BrE)
a Japanese plant of the mustard family with a thick, pungent green root, used as a spice in Japanese cuisine
Japanese spicy green plant root

Examples

Fresh wasabi has a delicate flavor that is quite different from the paste often served with sushi.
The chef grated wasabi root right at the table for extra spiciness.
Many restaurants use horseradish as a substitute for real wasabi.

Etymology

From Japanese ワサビ (wasabi).

2. NOUN

/ wəˈsɑbi / (AmE), / wəˈsɑːbi / (BrE)
a pungent green paste made from the root of a Japanese plant, commonly used as a condiment in Japanese cuisine, especially with sushi and sashimi
pungent Japanese green condiment

Examples

She added a small amount of wasabi to her sushi for an extra kick.
The chef mixed soy sauce with wasabi to create a flavorful dip.
Some people find the taste of wasabi too intense for their liking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseradish - a pungent root used as a condiment, related to wasabi in taste

Etymology

From Japanese ワサビ (wasabi), referring to the plant Wasabia japonica.
