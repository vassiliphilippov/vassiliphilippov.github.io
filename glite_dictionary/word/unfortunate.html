unfortunate

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈfɔrtʃənət / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɔːtʃənət / (BrE)
a person who suffers from bad luck or misfortune
person who suffers misfortune

Examples

Local charities provide support to unfortunates living on the streets.
The community organized a fundraiser for the unfortunates affected by the disaster.
She devoted her life to helping unfortunates in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victim - a person harmed or affected by an event or action
  • Antonym: fortunate - a person who is lucky or enjoys good fortune

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'infortunatus', from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'fortunatus' (fortunate, lucky).

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɔrtʃənət / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɔːtʃənət / (BrE)
(of an action or occurrence) unsuitable, inappropriate, or giving cause for regret.
unsuitable or regrettable

Examples

It was an unfortunate decision to cancel the event at the last minute.
His unfortunate remark caused tension among the team members.
Choosing that color for the walls was an unfortunate mistake.

Synset Examples

an unfortunate choice of words
an unfortunate speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regrettable - causing regret; undesirable
  • Antonym: fortunate - bringing good luck; favorable

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'un-' (not) + 'fortunate', from Latin 'fortuna' (fortune).

4. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɔɹtʃənət / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɔːtʃənət / (BrE)
Not likely to bring good luck; indicating or suggesting bad luck or an unfavorable outcome.
not likely to bring good luck

Examples

The timing of the announcement was especially unfortunate given the political climate.
Many sailors considered it unfortunate to set sail on a Friday.
He took her silence as an unfortunate omen before the contest.

Related Terms
