usury

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːʒəri / (AmE), / ˈjuːʒəri / (BrE)
the practice of lending money at unreasonably high or illegal rates of interest
lending at illegal or excessive interest rates
legal

Examples

Charging usury was forbidden by law in the medieval period.
The bank was accused of practicing usury by setting such high interest rates.
Legislation was introduced to protect consumers from usury and unfair lending.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loan sharking - the practice of lending money at extremely high interest rates, often illegally

Etymology

From Latin 'usura' (interest, use), from 'usus' (use).

2. NOUN

/ ˈjuːʒəri / (AmE), / ˈjuːzəri / (BrE)
the practice of lending money at unreasonably high rates of interest, often considered illegal or unethical
lending money at excessive interest

Examples

Laws were enacted to prevent usury and protect borrowers from exploitation.
He was accused of usury after charging a 50% interest rate on personal loans.
Throughout history, religious authorities have denounced usury as immoral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loan sharking - the practice of lending money at extremely high interest rates, often illegally
  • Degree Lower: interest - charge for the use of borrowed money

Etymology

From Latin usuria (interest, usury), from usura (use, enjoyment, interest).
