astronomic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæstrəˈnɑmɪk / (AmE), / ˌastrəˈnɒmɪk / (BrE)
relating to or belonging to the science of astronomy.
relating to astronomy

Examples

The observatory purchased a powerful astronomic telescope for research purposes.
She is fascinated by astronomic phenomena like black holes and pulsars.
The book contains detailed illustrations of astronomic instruments used throughout history.

Synset Examples

astronomic telescope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astronomical - relating to astronomy (variant, more common form)

Etymology

From Latin astronomicus, from Greek astronomikos, relating to the stars.

2. ADJ

/ ˌæstrəˈnɑːmɪk / (AmE), / ˌastrəˈnɒmɪk / (BrE)
extremely large; immense beyond ordinary comprehension.
extremely large; immense

Examples

The company faced an astronomic loss after the failed product launch.
Scientists discovered an astronomic number of stars in the new galaxy.
The cost of rebuilding the city was simply astronomic and shocked everyone involved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astronomical - extremely large; enormous in extent or degree
  • Synonym: gigantic - very large; enormous
  • Antonym: tiny - very small; diminutive

Etymology

From astronomy + -ic, patterned after 'astronomical.'
