pimp

1. NOUN

/ pɪmp / (AmE), / pɪmp / (BrE)
a person, especially a man, who controls prostitutes and arranges clients for them, taking part of their earnings
person who manages prostitutes
offensive vulgar

Examples

The police arrested the pimp during a raid on the city center.
He acted as a pimp, taking money from the women he managed.
The movie depicted the harsh life of a pimp in the 1970s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ponce - British term for a man who lives off the earnings of prostitutes

Etymology

Probably from Middle French *pimper* (to dress elegantly, attract attention).

2. VERB

/ pɪmp / (AmE), / pɪmp / (BrE)
to arrange or procure sexual partners for others, often for profit.
arrange sexual partners for others

Examples

He was arrested for trying to pimp young women on the streets.
Authorities claim he would pimp for a large prostitution ring.
She discovered that her so-called friend was attempting to pimp her to his clients.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: prostitute - a person who engages in sexual activity for payment

Etymology

Probably from German *Pimpf* (boy, young man), or from French *pimpant* (alluring).
