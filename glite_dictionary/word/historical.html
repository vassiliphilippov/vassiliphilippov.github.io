historical

1. ADJ

/ hɪˈstɔrɪkəl / (AmE), / hɪˈstɒrɪkəl / (BrE)
connected with the past or with the study of history; relating to events, people, or things that existed or happened in the past.
connected with the past or history

Examples

The museum features historical artifacts from ancient civilizations.
She wrote a historical novel set in the Victorian era.
The council is working to preserve several historical buildings downtown.

Synset Examples

historic victories
historical (or historic) times
a historical character

Related Terms

  • Synonym: historic - famous or important in history, or likely to be so

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'historicus', from Greek 'historikos' ('historical').

WordNet ID: oewn-01734292-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ hɪˈstɔrɪkəl / (AmE), / hɪˈstɒrɪkəl / (BrE)
having actually existed, lived, or taken place in the real world, as opposed to being mythical or legendary
real, not mythical; actually existed

Examples

Some scholars debate whether King Arthur was a historical figure or purely legendary.
The documentary explores the historical Jesus, separating fact from myth.
Evidence for a historical Troy was found during archaeological excavations.

Synset Examples

the historical Jesus
doubt that a historical Camelot ever existed
actual historical events

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legendary - relating to legends, not real or historical
  • Antonym: mythical - existing only in myths, not actually historical

Etymology

From Latin 'historicus', from Greek 'historikos' (of history).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01940876-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ hɪˈstɔrɪkəl / (AmE), / hɪˈstɒrɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the development or evolution of something, especially language, over time; diachronic.
concerned with change over time (esp. in language)

Examples

The historical study of languages reveals fascinating patterns of change over centuries.
His research focused on the historical development of English syntax.
The historical approach in linguistics contrasts with purely descriptive methods.

Synset Examples

diachronic linguistics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diachronic - concerned with the way in which something, especially language, develops over time

Etymology

From Latin 'historicus', from Greek 'historikos' (historical, of history).
