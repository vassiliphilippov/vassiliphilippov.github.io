blaze

1. NOUN

/ bleɪz / (AmE), / bleɪz / (BrE)
a bright light within the field of vision, especially one much brighter than the eyes are accustomed to
a very bright light

Examples

The blaze from the headlights made it hard to see the road.
A sudden blaze of lightning lit up the night sky.
The blaze of the afternoon sun forced them to shade their eyes.

Synset Examples

a glare of sunlight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glare - a strong, harsh, dazzling light
  • Synonym: flash - a sudden brief burst of bright light

2. NOUN

/ bleɪz / (AmE), / bleɪz / (BrE)
a very strong, bright flame or fire
strong, bright flame or fire

Examples

Firefighters struggled to control the blaze that engulfed the building.
A blaze lit up the night sky from the campfire.
The forest was consumed by a massive blaze after the lightning storm.

Synset Examples

the blaze spread rapidly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flame - a hot glowing body of ignited gas produced by something on fire
  • Degree Higher: conflagration - a large destructive fire

Etymology

Perhaps from Old English *blæse* (torch, flame), related to *blæstan* (to blow, blare).

Examples

8. VERB

/ bleɪz / (AmE), / bleɪz / (BrE)
to burn with a bright and intense flame
burn brightly and intensely

Examples

The campfire continued to blaze long into the night, warming everyone gathered around.
Dozens of candles blazed in the old cathedral, casting dancing shadows on the walls.
The wildfire blazed across the hillside, threatening homes and forcing people to evacuate.

Synset Examples

The summer sun alone can cause a pine to blaze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flame - to burn with a visible flame
  • Antonym: smolder - to burn slowly with no flame

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'blase' (torch, flame).
