Fagus purpurea

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪɡəs pɜrˈpjʊəriə / (AmE), / ˈfeɪɡəs pɜːˈpjʊəriə / (BrE)
A cultivated variety of the European beech tree distinguished by its shining purple or copper-colored leaves.
variety of beech with purple leaves

Examples

The leaves of the Fagus purpurea turn a brilliant metallic purple in the spring.
Many parks feature a majestic Fagus purpurea as a focal point of their landscape design.
Gardeners prize the Fagus purpurea for its striking coloration and hardy nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European beech - A common species of beech native to Europe

Etymology

From Latin 'Fagus' (beech) and 'purpurea' (purple), referring to the tree’s striking leaf color.
