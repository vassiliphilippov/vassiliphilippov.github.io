limber

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪmbə / (BrE)
a channel or gutter on either side of a ship's keelson that carries bilge water into the pump well
ship's channel for bilge water

Examples

The limbers must be kept clear to allow bilge water to flow freely.
Sailors regularly checked the limbers for blockages during long voyages.
A build-up of debris in the limbers can lead to flooding below deck.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪmbə / (BrE)
a two-wheeled horse-drawn vehicle used for towing a field gun or caisson, especially in historical military contexts
two-wheeled gun carriage vehicle

Examples

The artillerymen quickly attached the cannon to the limber for transport across the battlefield.
A restored limber is on display at the military history museum.
During the reenactment, horses pulled the limber and caisson as part of the procession.

Etymology

From earlier 'limber', likely of obscure origin, possibly related to obsolete 'limber' meaning cart shaft or pole.
