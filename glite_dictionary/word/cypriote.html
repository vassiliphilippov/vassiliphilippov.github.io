Cypriote

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪpriˌoʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɪprɪəʊt / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Cyprus
native or resident of Cyprus

Examples

She met a Cypriote artist at the exhibition.
Many Cypriotes speak both Greek and Turkish fluently.
The Cypriote community has maintained its traditions for generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cypriot - a native or inhabitant of Cyprus

Etymology

From French 'Cypriote', from Medieval Latin 'Cypriota', ultimately from Cyprus.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪpriˌoʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɪpriˌəʊt / (BrE)
Relating to Cyprus, its people, or its culture.
of Cyprus, its people, or culture

Examples

The museum houses several Cypriote artifacts from the Bronze Age.
He wrote a book about Cypriote traditions and festivals.
Many Cypriote communities celebrate religious holidays with unique rituals.

Synset Examples

Cypriot expatriates
Cypriote monasteries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cypriot - of or relating to Cyprus or its people or culture

Etymology

From French *Cypriote*, from Italian *cipriota*, from Latin *Cyprius* (of Cyprus).

Synset POS: a
