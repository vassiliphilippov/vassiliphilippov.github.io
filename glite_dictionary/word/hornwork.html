hornwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrnˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈhɔːnwɜːk / (BrE)
a fortification structure in the trace italienne system, consisting of two demi-bastions connected by a curtain wall, built to extend and strengthen defenses in a particular direction.
defensive fortification with two demi-bastions

Examples

The engineers decided to construct a hornwork to bolster the city's western wall.
The old castle's hornwork remains visible, showcasing the advanced military architecture of its time.
During the siege, the defenders retreated to the hornwork for a better vantage point against attackers.

Synset Examples

The hornwork was used to extend the fortified area in a particular direction to prevent the enemy occupying an area of high ground or simply strengthen the overall fortifications in the expected direction of attack.

Etymology

Horn + work; the 'horns' refer to the projecting demi-bastions resembling horns.
