slant

1. NOUN

/ slænt / (AmE), / slɑːnt / (BrE)
the degree to which a surface or line deviates from a horizontal plane; an incline or slope
degree of deviation from the horizontal

Examples

The house was built on a slant, making the floors uneven.
Please check the slant of the ramp to ensure it meets accessibility standards.
The roads had a noticeable slant due to the hilly terrain.

Synset Examples

the roof had a steep pitch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slope - the degree of inclination of a surface
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05076368-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ slænt / (AmE), / slɑːnt / (BrE)
to present information or a situation in a way that shows a particular bias or point of view
present with a bias

Examples

The newspaper slanted the story to favor one political candidate over the other.
It's difficult to find media sources that don't slant their coverage in some way.
He slants his reports to appeal to a younger audience.

Synset Examples

He biased his presentation so as to please the share holders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bias - to give a settled and often prejudiced outlook to something; to influence unfairly
  • Synonym: to distort - to change the way something is presented, often making it inaccurate
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00682404-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ slænt / (AmE), / slɑːnt / (BrE)
a particular point of view, bias, or personal perspective, especially in presenting information or events
a biased viewpoint or angle

Examples

The newspaper article had a clear slant favoring one political party.
It's important to recognize the slant in news coverage from different sources.
Her report offered a fresh slant on the city's economic problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bias - an inclination or prejudice for or against something
  • Synonym: angle - a particular viewpoint or perspective

Etymology

From Middle English slenten (to slip or slide), of Scandinavian origin.

4. VERB

/ slænt / (AmE), / slɑːnt / (BrE)
to lie or move in a sloping or oblique direction
lie or move at a slope

Examples

A bright beam of sunlight slanted through the window in the early morning.
The roof slants sharply to allow rainwater to drain away.
Her handwriting slanted across the page at a steep angle.

Synset Examples

A scar slanted across his face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slope - to be inclined from a horizontal or vertical line
  • Synonym: tilt - to move or cause to move into a sloping position
  • Antonym: level - to make or become level or even

Etymology

From Middle English *slaunten*, possibly of Scandinavian origin.

5. VERB

/ slænt / (AmE), / slɑːnt / (BrE)
to incline or bend from a vertical or straight position
incline or bend from vertical

Examples

The old tree slanted dangerously after the storm.
She slanted the painting so it would catch the light better.
He slanted forward to hear what the speaker was saying.

Synset Examples

She leaned over the banister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lean - to incline or bend from a straight position
  • Antonym: upright - in a vertical position, not slanted

Etymology

From Old Norse *slanta* (to slope), possibly related to Swedish *slant* (oblique); sense of 'to incline' dates from the 19th century.

6. VERB

/ slænt / (AmE), / slɑːnt / (BrE)
to slope or lean in a particular direction rather than being level or straight
to slope; to tilt at an angle

Examples

During the earthquake, the building began to slant dangerously to one side.
The rays of the setting sun slant through the windows in the late afternoon.
As the old bookshelf ages, it starts to slant forward due to the weight of the books.

Synset Examples

The tower is tilting
The ceiling is slanting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tilt - to move or cause to move into a sloping position
  • Synonym: lean - to incline or bend from a vertical position
