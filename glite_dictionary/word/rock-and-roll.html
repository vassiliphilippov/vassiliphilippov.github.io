rock-and-roll

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɑk.ənˈroʊl / (AmE), / ˌrɒk.ənˈrəʊl / (BrE)
a style of popular music that originated in the United States in the 1950s, characterized by a strong rhythm, simple melodies, and a blend of rhythm and blues with country music.
1950s popular music genre

Examples

Many early bands helped shape the sound of rock-and-roll in the 1950s.
Elvis Presley is often called the King of rock-and-roll.
The energetic beats and lively performances made rock-and-roll a cultural phenomenon.

Synset Examples

rock is a generic term for the range of styles that evolved out of rock'n'roll.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rock music - a broad genre of popular music that evolved from rock-and-roll

Etymology

From 'rock' and 'roll', both terms associated with rhythm and movement in earlier African-American music slang.
