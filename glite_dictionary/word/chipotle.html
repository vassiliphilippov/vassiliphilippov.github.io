chipotle

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈpoʊt.leɪ / (AmE), / tʃɪˈpəʊt.leɪ / (BrE)
A ripe jalapeño pepper that has been dried and smoked, used in cooking for its distinctive flavor.
dried, smoked jalapeño pepper

Examples

The recipe calls for one dried chipotle to add a smoky kick to the sauce.
She blended a chipotle pepper into the marinade for extra heat.
Smoky chipotle flavors are popular in modern Mexican dishes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: jalapeño - a medium-sized chili pepper with mild to moderate heat

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'chipotle', from Nahuatl 'chīlpoctli' (smoked chili).
