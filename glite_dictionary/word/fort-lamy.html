Fort-Lamy

1. PROPN

/ fɔrt ˈlæmi / (AmE), / fɔːt ˈlæmi / (BrE)
the former name of N'Djamena, the capital and largest city of Chad, located in the southwest on the Shari River
former name of Chad's capital city

Examples

The city of Fort-Lamy was renamed N'Djamena in 1973.
French colonial maps still feature Fort-Lamy as the capital of Chad.
Many historical events took place in Fort-Lamy before it changed its name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: N'Djamena - current name of the capital city of Chad, formerly Fort-Lamy

Etymology

Named after French army officer Amédée-François Lamy; renamed to N'Djamena in 1973.
