semiofficial

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmioʊˈfɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiəˈfɪʃəl / (BrE)
Having partial or limited official authority or status; not fully official, but sanctioned or recognized to some extent by an authority.
having limited official authority

Examples

The company issued a semiofficial statement before the government released an official response.
News agencies often rely on semiofficial sources for early information about diplomatic meetings.
Her appointment was only semiofficial, so she did not have full decision-making authority.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: official - having formal authority or sanction

Etymology

From semi- (meaning 'half' or 'partially') + official.
