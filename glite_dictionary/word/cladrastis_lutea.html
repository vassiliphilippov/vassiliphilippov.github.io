Cladrastis lutea

1. NOUN

/ kləˈdræstɪs ˈluːtiə / (AmE), / kləˈdræstɪs ˈluːtiə / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to eastern North America, known for its rounded crown, showy clusters of white flowers, and hardwood that yields a yellow dye.
ornamental North American tree with yellow-dye wood

Examples

The Cladrastis lutea in our garden blooms with fragrant white flowers every spring.
Historically, wood from the Cladrastis lutea was used to produce a bright yellow dye.
Botanists admire Cladrastis lutea for its attractive foliage and ornamental value.

Etymology

From the genus name Cladrastis and Latin lutea meaning 'yellow', referring to the tree's yellow wood.
