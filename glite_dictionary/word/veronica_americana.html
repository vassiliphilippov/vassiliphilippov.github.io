Veronica americana

1. NOUN

/ vəˌrɑnɪkə əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (AmE), / vəˌrɒnɪkə əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant native to western North America and northeastern Asia, with prostrate stems and dense clusters of pale violet to lilac flowers.
species of flowering plant native to North America and Asia

Examples

Veronica americana is often found growing along stream banks in western North America.
Gardeners value Veronica americana for its attractive lilac flowers and ground-covering stems.
Researchers are studying the ecological role of Veronica americana in supporting native pollinators.

Etymology

From the genus name Veronica and Latin americana, meaning 'of America'.
