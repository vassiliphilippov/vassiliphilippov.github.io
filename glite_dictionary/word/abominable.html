abominable

1. ADJ

/ əˈbɑm.ə.nə.bəl / (AmE), / əˈbɒm.ɪ.nə.bəl / (BrE)
extremely bad, unpleasant, or detestable; deserving strong dislike or condemnation
extremely detestable or unpleasant

Examples

The prisoners suffered under abominable conditions for months.
She considered lying an abominable habit she could never tolerate.
The food at that restaurant was abominable, and I can't recommend it to anyone.

Synset Examples

abominable treatment of prisoners
detestable vices
execrable crimes
consequences odious to those you govern

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detestable - deserving intense dislike
  • Synonym: odious - extremely unpleasant; repulsive
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

From Latin 'abominabilis', from 'abominari' (to detest as a bad omen).

2. ADJ

/ əˈbɑmɪnəbəl / (AmE), / əˈbɒmɪnəbl̩ / (BrE)
Causing moral revulsion; exceptionally bad or unpleasant.
exceptionally bad or unpleasant

Examples

The food at that restaurant was absolutely abominable and I would never eat there again.
He apologized for his abominable behavior at the meeting last night.
The living conditions in the camp were abominable and shocked the inspectors.

Synset Examples

atrocious taste
abominable workmanship
an awful voice
dreadful manners
a painful performance
terrible handwriting
an unspeakable odor came sweeping into the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrocious - exceptionally bad; horrifyingly wicked
  • Synonym: dreadful - extremely bad or serious
  • Antonym: excellent - extremely good; outstanding

Etymology

From Latin abominabilis 'detestable', from abominari 'to shun as a bad omen'.
