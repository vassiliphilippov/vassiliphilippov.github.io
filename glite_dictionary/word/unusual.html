unusual

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈjuʒuəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈjuːʒuəl / (BrE)
not commonly occurring, rare, or out of the ordinary; not usual.
not common; out of the ordinary

Examples

It is unusual to see snow in this region during the summer months.
Her unusual hairstyle attracted a lot of attention at the party.
Finding an empty seat on the train during rush hour is quite unusual.

Synset Examples

two-career families are no longer unusual

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rare - not occurring very often, uncommon
  • Antonym: common - occurring frequently, widespread

Etymology

From un- (not) + usual, ultimately from Latin 'usualis'.

not common, rare, or unexpected; out of the ordinary.
not common or ordinary

Examples

It was highly unusual for him to arrive late to the meeting.
The museum displayed several unusual artifacts from ancient times.
She wore an unusual necklace that attracted everyone's attention.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ordinary - common or normal; not unusual
  • Synonym: rare - not occurring very often; uncommon
  • Synonym: abnormal - not normal or typical; deviating from the norm

Etymology

From un- (not) + usual, ultimately from Latin 'usualis'.
