heterologous

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɛtəˈrɑləɡəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛtəˈrɒləɡəs / (BrE)
(of tissue, cells, or molecules) derived from a different but related species; not homologous.
from a different species (biology/medicine)
medical

Examples

The scientist used a heterologous enzyme source in the experiment.
A heterologous transplant may be rejected more quickly than a homologous one.
Therapies with heterologous antibodies have advanced in treating certain viral infections.

Synset Examples

a heterologous graft

Related Terms

  • Antonym: homologous - originating from the same species; corresponding in structure

Etymology

From Greek heteros (other, different) + logos (relation, proportion), by analogy with 'homologous'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌhɛtəˈrɑləɡəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛtəˈrɒləɡəs / (BrE)
having a different structure or origin, especially relating to tissue, organs, or species that are not evolutionarily related
having a different structure or origin

Examples

The scientist transplanted heterologous tissue from one species into another for the experiment.
Researchers observed different immune responses to heterologous virus strains.
A heterologous bone graft was used because suitable tissue from the same species was unavailable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: homologous - having a similar structure or evolutionary origin, especially in biology

Etymology

From Greek heteros (different) + logos (relation, proportion) + -ous.
