ultracentrifuge

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌltrəsɛntrɪˌfjudʒ / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈsɛntrɪˌfjuːdʒ / (BrE)
a high-speed centrifuge used to separate and analyze large molecules in substances such as proteins and polymers, by spinning samples at very high speeds
high-speed centrifuge for large molecules

Examples

Scientists use an ultracentrifuge to isolate different components of a cell based on their density.
The laboratory purchased a new ultracentrifuge capable of reaching extremely high rotational speeds.
Analysis of protein complexes often requires the use of an ultracentrifuge for precise separation.

Etymology

From 'ultra-' (extreme) + 'centrifuge' (from French centrifuger, from Latin centrum + fugare 'to make flee').

2. VERB

/ ˌʌltrəsɛntrəˌfjuːdʒ / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈsɛntrɪfjuːdʒ / (BrE)
to subject (something) to the process of separation by means of an ultracentrifuge.
separate using an ultracentrifuge
medical

Examples

After collecting the cell lysate, scientists ultracentrifuge it to separate the proteins from other components.
The samples were ultracentrifuged for two hours at 100,000g to pellet the membranes.
We will ultracentrifuge the virus suspension to purify the viral particles before analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centrifuge - to subject to the action of a centrifuge

Etymology

Formed from 'ultra-' (extremely) + 'centrifuge', after the name of the instrument.
