Pistacia

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈsteɪʃ(i)ə / (AmE), / pɪˈsteɪsɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering trees and shrubs in the family Anacardiaceae, including species such as the pistachio.
genus of trees including pistachio

Examples

The genus Pistacia includes several species cultivated for their nuts and ornament.
Botanists classify the pistachio tree as Pistacia vera, native to Central Asia.
Many members of the Pistacia genus are tolerant to drought and poor soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pistachio - the edible seed or the tree Pistacia vera

Etymology

From Latin 'pistacia', from Ancient Greek 'πιστάκια' (pistákia), referring to the pistachio nut.

2. NOUN

/ pɪˈsteɪʃə / (AmE), / pɪˈstɑːsɪə / (BrE)
a genus of trees in the family Anacardiaceae, including the pistachio, characterized by drupaceous fruit and compound leaves
genus of trees including pistachio

Examples

The genus Pistacia includes several species grown for their seeds and resin.
Researchers studied the biodiversity within the Pistacia genus to understand their adaptation to arid climates.
Commercial orchards often cultivate members of the Pistacia genus for culinary use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pistachio - a tree of the species Pistacia vera known for its edible seeds

Etymology

From Latin Pistacia, from Greek pistákia (pistachio tree).
