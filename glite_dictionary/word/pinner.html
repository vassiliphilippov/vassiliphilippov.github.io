pinner

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnər / (AmE), / ˈpɪnə / (BrE)
A woman's cap with two long flaps that are pinned on, especially worn in the 17th and 18th centuries.
woman's cap with pinned flaps

Examples

The museum displayed an elegant pinner from the colonial era.
She wore a white pinner as part of her traditional costume.
In portraits of the 1700s, women are often shown with a pinner on their heads.

Etymology

From 'pin' + '-er', so called because it was fastened with pins.
