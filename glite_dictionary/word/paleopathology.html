paleopathology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊpæˈθɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpaliəʊpæˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The study of diseases in ancient times, especially as revealed by examination of fossils or mummified remains.
study of ancient diseases via fossils
medical

Examples

Paleopathology has provided insights into how tuberculosis affected ancient populations.
The field of paleopathology combines archaeology and medical research to trace disease origins.
He specializes in paleopathology, analyzing skeletal remains for signs of historical illnesses.

Etymology

From Greek 'palaios' (ancient) and 'pathologia' (study of disease).
