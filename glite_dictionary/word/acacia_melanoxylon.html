Acacia melanoxylon

1. NOUN

/ əˈkeɪʃə ˌmɛləˈnɒksɪˌlɒn / (AmE), / əˈkeɪsɪə ˌmɛləˈnɒksɪlɒn / (BrE)
A tall Australian tree of the acacia genus, valued for its hard, dark timber known as blackwood.
species of Australian acacia tree

Examples

The furniture was made from beautiful Acacia melanoxylon harvested in Tasmania.
Botanists study the growth patterns of Acacia melanoxylon in different Australian regions.
Many musical instruments are crafted out of Acacia melanoxylon due to its acoustic qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackwood - the valuable dark timber of Acacia melanoxylon

Etymology

Acacia from Greek 'akakia' (thorny Egyptian tree); melanoxylon from Greek 'melas' (black) + 'xylon' (wood).
