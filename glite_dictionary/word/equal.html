equal

1. ADJ

/ ˈiːkwəl / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəl / (BrE)
having the same quantity, value, or measure as something else
of the same quantity, value, or measure

Examples

Both teams were equal in skill, making the competition very close.
Employees expect to receive equal pay for the same work.
The two companies are almost equal in size and market share.

Synset Examples

on equal terms
all men are equal before the law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equivalent - having the same value, function, or meaning
  • Synonym: identical - exactly the same
  • Antonym: unequal - not equal in quantity, value, or measure

Etymology

From Latin aequalis (uniform, identical), from aequus (even, level).

Examples
