unreplaceable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈpleɪ.sə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈpleɪ.sə.bəl / (BrE)
Impossible to replace; unable to be substituted or restored if lost or damaged.
impossible to replace

Examples

The lost painting was truly unreplaceable due to its historical significance.
Family heirlooms have unreplaceable sentimental value for many people.
His unreplaceable contribution to the project was acknowledged by everyone.

Synset Examples

irreplaceable antiques

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irreplaceable - impossible to replace

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' and 'replaceable', meaning 'able to be replaced'.
