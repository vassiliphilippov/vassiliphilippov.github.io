rightful

1. ADJ

/ ˈraɪtfəl / (AmE), / ˈraɪtf(ə)l / (BrE)
Having a legally or morally established claim to something; justly entitled by law or right.
legally or morally entitled

Examples

After years of dispute, the court recognized him as the rightful owner of the property.
She is the rightful heir to the family estate.
The crown was restored to its rightful monarch after the rebellion.

Synset Examples

the true and lawful king
the rightful heir

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lawful - conforming to the law; legally permitted
  • Antonym: illegitimate - not authorized by law; not in accordance with accepted standards or rules

Etymology

From Old English riht (right, just, lawful) + -ful (full of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈraɪtfəl / (AmE), / ˈraɪtfʊl / (BrE)
legally or morally correct; having a justified or lawful claim to something
legally or morally justified

Examples

She finally received her rightful share of the property after a long legal battle.
He is the rightful owner of the painting, as proven by the documents.
The crown was returned to its rightful place in the national museum.

Synset Examples

a rightful inheritance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legitimate - conforming to the law or rules; lawful
  • Antonym: unlawful - not conforming to or permitted by law or rules

Etymology

From Middle English rightful, from right + -ful; 'right' from Old English riht (just, proper).
