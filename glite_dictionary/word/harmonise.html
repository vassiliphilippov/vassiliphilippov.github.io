to harmonise

1. VERB

/ ˈhɑːrməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhɑːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to bring musical sounds into harmony or agreement, especially by singing or playing notes that complement each other
to bring musical sounds into harmony
british

Examples

The choir members harmonise beautifully during their performances.
Can you harmonise with the melody on this part of the song?
She loves to harmonise while her sister plays the piano.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harmonize - American spelling of 'harmonise', meaning to bring musical sounds into harmony

Etymology

From Greek harmonia (agreement, concord); via Old French harmonie and late Latin harmonia.
