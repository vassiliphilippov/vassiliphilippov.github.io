Casuaridae

1. NOUN

/ kæz.juˈɛr.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / kæz.juˈær.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, flightless birds native to Australasia, including cassowaries and emus.
family of large, flightless birds (cassowaries, emus)

Examples

The Casuaridae are known for their large size and powerful legs.
Researchers have studied the evolutionary history of the Casuaridae to better understand bird diversification.
Both cassowaries and emus are members of the Casuaridae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassowary - a large, flightless bird native to New Guinea and northeastern Australia

Etymology

From New Latin Casuaridae, after 'casuarius' (cassowary).
