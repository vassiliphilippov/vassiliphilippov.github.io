parliamentarian

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːrləmenˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ˌpɑːlɪəmənˈteəriən / (BrE)
a member of a parliament, especially the House of Commons in the United Kingdom
member of a parliament, esp. in the UK
british

Examples

The parliamentarian raised several important issues during the debate.
As a new parliamentarian, she hopes to bring change to her constituency.
Several parliamentarians abstained from voting on the controversial bill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legislator - a person who makes laws, a lawmaker
  • Synonym: senator - a member of a senate, especially in some other countries

Etymology

From French *parlementaire*, from *parlement* (parliament).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːrləmən'tɛriən / (AmE), / ˌpɑːləmən'teəriən / (BrE)
an expert in the rules and procedures of a parliament or other deliberative assembly
expert in parliamentary procedure

Examples

The parliamentarian advised the chair on the proper procedure for the vote.
As a certified parliamentarian, she ensured that all debates followed the correct rules.
When confusion arose, the parliamentarian clarified the assembly's bylaws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chairperson - the presiding officer of a meeting or assembly
  • Synonym: legislator - a person who makes laws

Etymology

From parliament + -arian, indicating a person associated with parliament.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10420008-n
Synset POS: n
