abactinal

1. ADJ

/ æbˈæktɪnəl / (AmE), / æbˈæktɪnəl / (BrE)
(of radiate animals) located on the surface or end opposite to that on which the mouth is situated.
opposite to the mouth (in radiate animals)
medical

Examples

The sea star's abactinal surface features numerous spines and granules.
Researchers examined the abactinal plates to study differences among starfish species.
The abactinal side is typically exposed to predators and the environment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: actinal - located on the surface where the mouth is situated (in radiate animals)

Etymology

From Latin 'ab' (away from) + Greek 'aktis' (ray).
