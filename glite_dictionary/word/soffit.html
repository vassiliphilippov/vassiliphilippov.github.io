soffit

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑː.fɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɒf.ɪt / (BrE)
the underside of a structural part of a building, such as an arch, overhang, or beam
underside of a building part

Examples

The electrician ran wires through the soffit above the porch.
Moisture damage was found in the soffit beneath the roof eaves.
He painted the soffits to match the exterior walls of the house.

Etymology

From French soffite, from Italian soffitto (ceiling), from Latin suffictus (fixed underneath).
