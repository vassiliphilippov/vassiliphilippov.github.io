Telescopium

1. PROPN

/ təˌlɛsˈkoʊpiəm / (AmE), / tɛlɪˈskəʊpiəm / (BrE)
a small constellation in the southern sky, located near Ara, visible in the Southern Hemisphere.
constellation in the southern sky

Examples

The constellation Telescopium was first introduced by the astronomer Nicolas-Louis de Lacaille.
During the summer months, Telescopium is best seen from the Southern Hemisphere.
Many stargazers enjoy locating Telescopium when mapping out southern constellations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ara - a constellation near Telescopium in the southern sky

Etymology

From Latin 'telescopium', meaning 'telescope'; named in the 18th century to honor the invention of the telescope.
