twinberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɪnˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈtwɪnˌbɛri / (BrE)
A creeping woody plant of eastern North America with shiny evergreen leaves and scarlet berries.
evergreen North American plant with scarlet berries

Examples

The woods were dotted with twinberry plants, their shiny leaves glistening in the sun.
Gardeners sometimes plant twinberry for its attractive scarlet berries and evergreen foliage.
We identified the creeping shrub as twinberry thanks to its distinctive red fruit.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtwɪnˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈtwɪnˌbɛri / (BrE)
A shrubby honeysuckle native to western North America, having purple flowers and paired berries.
a shrubby honeysuckle of western North America

Examples

The twinberry is easily identified by its pairs of shiny dark berries.
Gardeners in the Pacific Northwest often plant twinberry to attract hummingbirds.
The berries of the twinberry are usually not eaten by humans but valued by wildlife.

Etymology

Named for its paired (twin) berries.
