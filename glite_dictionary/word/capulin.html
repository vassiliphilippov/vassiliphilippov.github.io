capulin

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæpʊˈliːn / (AmE), / ˌkæpʊˈliːn / (BrE)
A small, dark cherry native to Mexico and Central America, also known as the Mexican black cherry.
Mexican black cherry fruit

Examples

Farmers in the region grow both capulin and traditional sweet cherries.
The juice from the ripe capulin is commonly used in desserts and beverages.
We sampled a delicious jam made from fresh capulin picked earlier that morning.

Etymology

From Spanish 'capulín', from Nahuatl 'capolcuahuitl' (cherry tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæpʊˈlin / (AmE), / ˌkapʊˈliːn / (BrE)
A Mexican black cherry tree (Prunus salicifolia) bearing small, edible, dark purple or black fruit.
Mexican black cherry tree with edible fruit

Examples

The fruit of the capulin is often made into jams and jellies in rural Mexico.
We planted a capulin tree in our backyard for its delicious cherries.
The capulin blossoms attract bees during the spring season.

Etymology

From Spanish capulín, from Nahuatl capolin (the tree and its fruit).
