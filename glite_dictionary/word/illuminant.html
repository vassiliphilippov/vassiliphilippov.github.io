illuminant

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈluːmɪnənt / (AmE), / ɪˈluːmɪnənt / (BrE)
a substance or device that serves as a source of light
something that gives off light

Examples

In scientific photography, choosing the right illuminant is crucial for accurate color reproduction.
The museum installed a new illuminant to better showcase the paintings.
Some types of illuminant are preferred in laboratories for their pure white light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light source - a device or object that emits light

Etymology

From Latin 'illuminare' (to light up).
