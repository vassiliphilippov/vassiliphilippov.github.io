to circularize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɜːrkjəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkjʊləˌraɪz / (BrE)
to distribute circulars or notices to many people
distribute circulars or notices

Examples

The company decided to circularize all its clients with information about the new product line.
Candidates often circularize voters in the district ahead of an election.
We plan to circularize our newsletter to local businesses next week.

Etymology

From 'circular' (a printed letter or advertisement) + '-ize' (verb-forming suffix).

3. VERB

/ ˈsɜrkjələˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkjʊləraɪz / (BrE)
to distribute letters or printed notices, especially in order to canvass or communicate with a number of people.
to send letters to canvass or inform

Examples

The charity decided to circularize all its supporters to raise funds for the new project.
Several companies circularized households in the area with information about their services.
During election season, it is common for candidates to circularize voters with promotional letters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mailshot - a distribution of promotional letters or leaflets to many people

Etymology

From 'circular', referring to a printed letter sent to many people, plus the verb-forming suffix '-ize'.

Examples

Political groups often circularize petitions to gather public support for their campaigns.
