Ephemeroptera

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌfiməˈrɑptərə / (AmE), / ɪˌfiːməˈrɒptərə / (BrE)
an order of aquatic insects, commonly known as mayflies, characterized by delicate bodies, two or three long tail filaments, and short adult lifespans
order of insects, mayflies

Examples

The fossil record of the Ephemeroptera dates back to the Carboniferous period.
Adult Ephemeroptera are known for their extremely brief lifespan, often just a single day.
Students studied the life cycle of Ephemeroptera in the freshwater habitat laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayfly - a common name for insects of the order Ephemeroptera

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'ephemeros' (short-lived) + 'pteron' (wing).
