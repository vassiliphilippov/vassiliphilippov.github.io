Hassidic

1. ADJ

/ hɑːˈsɪdɪk / (AmE), / həˈsɪdɪk / (BrE)
relating to the Hasidim, a Jewish religious group noted for its traditional practices and mystical beliefs
of or relating to the Hasidim sect of Judaism

Examples

The Hassidic community gathers for prayer every Friday evening.
He studied the teachings of several Hassidic masters during his travels in Eastern Europe.
Many Hassidic traditions emphasize joy and spiritual connection in worship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hasidic - alternate spelling of 'Hassidic', relating to the Hasidim sect of Judaism

Etymology

From Hebrew ḥasīd (pious), via Yiddish khasidish (of the Hasidim).
