dockhand

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːkˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈdɒkˌhænd / (BrE)
a person who loads and unloads ships at a dock or port
person who loads/unloads ships at a dock

Examples

My uncle worked as a dockhand in New York Harbor for over twenty years.
The dockhand skillfully guided the crane to unload the heavy cargo from the ship.
Many dockhands start their shift early in the morning to meet the arriving vessels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stevedore - a person employed to load and unload cargo from ships

Etymology

Formed from 'dock' (harbor structure) + 'hand' (worker).
