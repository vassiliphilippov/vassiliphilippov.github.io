flashback

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈflæʃˌbak / (BrE)
A scene in a novel, film, or play set in an earlier time than the main story, used to provide background or context.
scene set in earlier time (literature, film)

Examples

The movie opens with a flashback to the hero's childhood.
Through several flashbacks, the author reveals the character's troubled past.
A sudden flashback interrupts the action, transporting the viewer to an earlier battle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: foreshadowing - a warning or indication of a future event in a story

Etymology

From the noun 'flash' + 'back', first used in early 20th-century English literature.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈflæʃˌbak / (BrE)
a sudden and vivid memory or recurrence of a past experience, especially one brought on by hallucinogenic drugs or trauma
sudden, vivid re-experience of a past event

Examples

The war veteran suffered a terrifying flashback that made him relive the battlefield.
After taking the drug, some users experienced a flashback several months later.
Hearing the song gave her a vivid flashback to her childhood summers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memory - the faculty by which the mind stores and remembers information
  • Synonym: reminiscence - a recollection of a past event
  • Antonym: amnesia - partial or total loss of memory

Etymology

From 'flash' + 'back', originally referring to a sudden vivid scene in film or narrative.
