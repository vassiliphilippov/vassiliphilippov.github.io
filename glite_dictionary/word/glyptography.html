glyptography

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɪpˈtɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ɡlɪpˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the art or technique of carving or engraving, especially on stones
art of carving or engraving (on stone)

Examples

The ancient ring displayed exquisite glyptography depicting scenes from mythology.
She studied glyptography to better understand the art of ancient gemstone carvings.
Modern artists rarely practice glyptography due to the skill and patience it requires.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - the process or art of cutting designs into a hard surface
  • Synonym: sculpture - the art of shaping figures or designs in the round or in relief

Etymology

From Greek glyphein (to carve) + -graphy (writing, representing).

2. NOUN

/ ɡlɪpˈtɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ɡlɪpˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the art or practice of carving or engraving on precious stones
engraving on precious stones

Examples

The museum displayed ancient glyptography on Roman gemstones.
He studied glyptography to understand the techniques used by master gem engravers.
Experts in glyptography can often identify forgeries by subtle details in the carving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glyptic - relating to carving or engraving, especially on gems
  • Synonym: intaglio - a design engraved into a material, especially a gemstone

Etymology

From Greek 'glyphein' (to carve) + '-graphy' (writing, recording).
