erythrolysin

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθrəˈlaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrəˈlaɪsɪn / (BrE)
A substance that causes the destruction of red blood cells and releases their hemoglobin.
substance destroying red blood cells
medical

Examples

The laboratory tested the blood sample for the presence of erythrolysin to diagnose the patient's condition.
Some bacterial toxins act as erythrolysin, leading to the destruction of red blood cells.
Exposure to erythrolysin can cause hemolytic anemia in susceptible individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemolysin - a substance that destroys red blood cells; synonym in medical usage

Etymology

From Greek erythros (red) + lysis (loosening, dissolution) + -in (chemical suffix).
