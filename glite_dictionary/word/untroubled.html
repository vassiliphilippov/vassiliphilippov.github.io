untroubled

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtrʌbəld / (AmE), / ʌnˈtrʌbəld / (BrE)
not affected by worries, problems, or disturbances; calm or peaceful.
not affected by worries or disturbances

Examples

After the long hike, she enjoyed an untroubled night of deep sleep.
Despite the chaos around him, his expression remained untroubled.
The lake was perfectly still, reflecting an untroubled sky above.

Synset Examples

seemed untroubled by doubts of any kind
untroubled sleep
a kind untroubled face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions
  • Antonym: disturbed - showing signs of worry or agitation; troubled

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'troubled' (disturbed, worried).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtrʌbəld / (AmE), / ʌnˈtrʌbəld / (BrE)
feeling or showing no signs of worry, fear, or doubt; calm and at ease
not worried or disturbed

Examples

She looked untroubled by the criticism and continued her work confidently.
His sleep was deep and untroubled, free from nightmares or worries.
Despite the chaos around her, she remained untroubled and composed throughout the meeting.

Synset Examples

he was secure that nothing will be held against him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions
  • Antonym: anxious - experiencing worry, unease, or nervousness

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'troubled' (from Old French 'troubler', from Vulgar Latin 'turbulare')
