catapultian

1. ADJ

/ ˌkætəˈpʌltiən / (AmE), / ˌkætəˈpʌltɪən / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling a catapult.
of or like a catapult

Examples

The physicist described the catapultian motion of the released object.
Her ideas took off with catapultian force, surprising everyone in the room.
The invention gave their project a catapultian momentum, propelling it ahead of competitors.

Synset Examples

a catapultic kind of action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catapultic - relating to a catapult

Etymology

From 'catapult' + English adjectival suffix '-ian'.
