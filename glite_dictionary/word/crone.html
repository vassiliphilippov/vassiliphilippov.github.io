crone

1. NOUN

/ kroʊn / (AmE), / krəʊn / (BrE)
an old woman, especially one who is thin and ugly, often depicted as sinister or witch-like
an ugly old woman, often sinister

Examples

The fairy tale featured a wicked crone living alone in the woods.
She refused to believe she would ever become the bitter old crone her neighbors described.
In the play, the actress transformed herself into a convincing crone with prosthetic makeup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hag - an unattractive, often evil old woman
  • Synonym: witch - a woman with magical powers, usually evil in legends
  • Antonym: beauty - a beautiful or attractive person

Etymology

From Middle English *carogne* (dead flesh, old woman), from Anglo-French, related to Old North French *caroigne*.
