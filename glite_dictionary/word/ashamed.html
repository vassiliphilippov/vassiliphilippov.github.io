ashamed

1. ADJ

/ əˈʃeɪmd / (AmE), / əˈʃeɪmd / (BrE)
feeling embarrassed, guilty, or remorseful about something one has done or failed to do, or about one's circumstances
feeling shame or guilt

Examples

She was ashamed to admit her mistake in front of everyone.
He felt ashamed of his behavior at the party last night.
Many people are ashamed to ask for help when they need it.

Synset Examples

are you ashamed for having lied?
felt ashamed of my torn coat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proud - feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction as a result of achievements or qualities
  • Synonym: embarrassed - feeling awkward or self-conscious

Etymology

From Old English āscamian (to be ashamed), from scamu (shame).
