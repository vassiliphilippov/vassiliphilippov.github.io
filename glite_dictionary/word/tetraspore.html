tetraspore

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛtrəˌspɔr / (AmE), / ˈtɛtrəˌspɔː / (BrE)
one of the four asexual spores produced within a sporangium, especially in certain algae and fungi
asexual spore produced in fours
medical

Examples

In red algae, each tetraspore develops into a new plant.
The scientist observed the release of tetraspores from the sporangium under the microscope.
Formation of a tetraspore is a key part of the reproductive cycle in some seaweeds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: spore - a reproductive cell capable of developing into a new individual

Etymology

From Greek 'tetra-' meaning 'four' and 'spora' meaning 'seed, spore'.
