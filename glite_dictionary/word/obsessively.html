obsessively

1. ADV

/ əbˈsɛsɪvli / (AmE), / əbˈsɛsɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner showing excessive preoccupation or compulsive concern with something.
in a compulsive, excessive manner

Examples

She checked her email obsessively throughout the day, unable to relax.
He cleaned the house obsessively every weekend, making sure everything was spotless.
After losing her keys once, she began to obsessively double-check her bag before leaving.

Synset Examples

he cleaned his shoes compulsively after every walk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compulsively - in a way driven by an irresistible urge, especially against one's conscious wishes

Etymology

From 'obsessive' + '-ly'. 'Obsessive' from Latin 'obsessus' (besieged).
