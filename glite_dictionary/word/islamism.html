Islamism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪz.læˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪzˈlæm.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The religious system or civilization of Islam, or, more recently, a political movement advocating the implementation of Islamic principles in government and society.
movement or belief system based on Islam

Examples

Some political groups promote Islamism as a basis for government.
The rise of Islamism in certain countries has led to significant policy changes.
He is known for his academic work on the development of Islamism in the 20th century.

Synset Examples

Islam is a complete way of life, not a Sunday religion
the term Muhammadanism is offensive to Muslims who believe that Allah, not Muhammad, founded their religion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Islam - the religion founded by Muhammad, with the Quran as its holy book

Etymology

Formed from 'Islam' + '-ism', denoting a system of belief.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈslæmɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪzˈlɑːmɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A political and social movement seeking to implement Islamic values and law in all aspects of society, often advocating a return to what is seen as fundamental principles of Islam.
movement for implementing Islamic values in society

Examples

Many political parties in the region base their platform on Islamism and seek to impose Sharia law.
The rise of Islamism has significantly influenced contemporary debates on secularism in various countries.
Scholars study Islamism to understand its impact on modern Middle Eastern politics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Islam - The religion founded on the teachings of Muhammad
  • Antonym: secularism - The principle of separating religion from governmental institutions

Etymology

Formed from 'Islam' + '-ism', modeled on similar terms for movements or ideologies.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08112993-n
Synset POS: n
