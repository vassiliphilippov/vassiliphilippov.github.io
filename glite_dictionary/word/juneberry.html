juneberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒunˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːnˌbɛri / (BrE)
A North American tree or shrub with showy white flowers and edible blue-black or purplish fruit.
North American tree or shrub with edible fruit

Examples

We picked a basket of juneberries during our hike in the woods.
The juneberry tree is in full bloom, covered in clusters of white flowers.
Birds love to feast on the ripe juneberries each summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serviceberry - another name for juneberry, a tree or shrub with edible fruit
  • Synonym: shadbush - another name for juneberry, a North American shrub

Etymology

Named for the characteristic ripening month of its fruit, June, and the word 'berry.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːnˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːnˌbɛri / (BrE)
A small edible purple or red berry, produced by various species of the genus Amelanchier, commonly found in North America.
edible berry from Amelanchier plants

Examples

The children climbed the hill to gather juneberries for their breakfast.
She baked a pie using fresh juneberries picked from her garden.
Birds flock to the shrubs each summer to feast on the ripe juneberries.

Etymology

Named for the month June, when the berries typically ripen, and 'berry.'
