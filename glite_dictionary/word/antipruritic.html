antipruritic

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.pruːˈrɪ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌænti.pruːˈrɪtɪk / (BrE)
A substance or drug used to relieve or prevent itching.
a drug that relieves itching
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antipruritic to help reduce the patient's skin irritation.
Applying an antipruritic cream provided instant relief from the mosquito bites.
Many over-the-counter antipruritics are available for treating mild cases of itching.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antihistamine - a drug that counteracts the effects of histamine, often used to relieve allergic reactions including itching

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + pruritus (itch) from Latin.
