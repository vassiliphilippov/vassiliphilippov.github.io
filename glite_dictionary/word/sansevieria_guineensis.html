Sansevieria guineensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæn.səˈvɪəri.ə ˌɡwiːˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsan.sɪˈvɪəri.ə ˌɡwiːˈnɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
A tropical African plant of the genus Sansevieria, also known as bowstring hemp, valued for its strong fibers and ornamental use.
tropical African plant, bowstring hemp

Examples

The leaves of Sansevieria guineensis are often used to make strong cords and ropes.
Botanists studied Sansevieria guineensis for its drought-resistant qualities in arid landscapes.
Gardeners prize Sansevieria guineensis for its low-maintenance care and striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowstring hemp - fiber-yielding plant, also a common name for Sansevieria guineensis

Etymology

Sansevieria: named after Raimondo di Sangro, Prince of San Severo; guineensis: Latin, meaning 'of Guinea'.
