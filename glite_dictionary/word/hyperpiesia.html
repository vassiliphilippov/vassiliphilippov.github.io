hyperpiesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.pər.paɪˈiː.zi.ə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pə.paɪˈiː.zi.ə / (BrE)
persistent and abnormal high blood pressure for which no specific cause can be identified
persistent high blood pressure of unknown cause
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with hyperpiesia after several tests found no secondary cause for her elevated blood pressure.
Long-term hyperpiesia can damage the heart, kidneys, and blood vessels if not managed effectively.
Researchers are studying potential genetic factors that may contribute to the development of hyperpiesia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypertension - abnormally high blood pressure, of any cause

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (over) + 'piesis' (pressure).
