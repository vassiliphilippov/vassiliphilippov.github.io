arteria axillaris

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌtɪriə ækˈsɪlərɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪərɪə ækˈsɪlərɪs / (BrE)
the portion of the main artery of the arm that passes through the armpit, connecting the subclavian artery above with the brachial artery below.
artery of the arm in the armpit area
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully isolated the arteria axillaris during the shoulder procedure.
Injury to the arteria axillaris can cause significant bleeding and complicate trauma cases.
The arteria axillaris is commonly examined when assessing arm blood supply.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axillary artery - the main artery that runs through the armpit

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery) + 'axillaris' (of the armpit, from 'axilla').
