involuntary

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnˈvɑːlənˌtɛri / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒl.ən.tər.i / (BrE)
(of actions, movements, or processes) done without conscious control, especially those regulated by the autonomic nervous system.
done without conscious control

Examples

Sneezing is often an involuntary reaction to dust or allergens.
His leg gave an involuntary twitch as he fell asleep.
The doctor explained the difference between involuntary and voluntary muscles.

Synset Examples

involuntary muscles
gave an involuntary start

Related Terms

  • Antonym: voluntary - done by one's own free will or with conscious control

Etymology

From Latin involuntarius, from in- (not) + voluntarius (voluntary, of one's free will).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɑːlənˌtɛri / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒləntri / (BrE)
done without conscious control or against one's will; not subject to deliberate choice.
not done by choice or under control

Examples

Her involuntary gasp surprised everyone in the silent auditorium.
Muscle spasms are often involuntary and cannot be easily prevented.
His involuntary laughter broke the tension in the room.

Synset Examples

involuntary manslaughter
involuntary servitude
an involuntary shudder
It (becoming a hero) was involuntary. They sank my boat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: voluntary - done by choice or under one's own control

Etymology

From Latin involuntārius, from in- (not) + voluntārius (voluntary).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02531409-a
Synset POS: a
