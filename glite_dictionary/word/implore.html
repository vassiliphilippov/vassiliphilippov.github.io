to implore

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈplɔr / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɔː / (BrE)
to ask someone earnestly or desperately to do or not do something
ask earnestly; beg

Examples

She implored him to stay and listen to her explanation.
The villagers implored the government for help after the flood.
He implores his friends not to make the same mistake he did.

Synset Examples

I beg you to stop!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beg - to ask for something earnestly or desperately
  • Synonym: to entreat - to ask someone earnestly or anxiously to do something

Etymology

From Latin implorare (to beg for), from in- (towards) + plorare (to cry out).
