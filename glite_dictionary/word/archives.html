archives

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrkaɪvz / (AmE), / ˈɑːkaɪvz / (BrE)
A collection of historical documents or records providing information about a place, institution, or group of people.
collection of historical records

Examples

The university maintains extensive archives of student newspapers dating back to the 19th century.
Researchers spent weeks combing through the national archives for evidence.
Many historical documents are preserved in the city archives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - an account kept in writing or some other permanent form
  • Degree Lower: library - a building or room containing a collection of books and periodicals

Etymology

From French 'archives', from Latin 'archivum', from Greek 'arkheion' (public office, town hall).
