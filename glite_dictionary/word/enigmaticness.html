enigmaticness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛnɪɡˈmætɪknəs / (AmE), / ˌɛnɪɡˈmatɪknəs / (BrE)
The quality of being mysterious, difficult to understand, or puzzling.
state of being mysterious or puzzling

Examples

The enigmaticness of the painting left the critics fascinated and perplexed.
She admired the enigmaticness in his smile, always wondering what he was truly thinking.
The novel's plot is driven by the enigmaticness of its main character.

Synset Examples

I was overwhelmed by the enigmaticness and overall feeling of falling into an abyss of comforting silhouette dreamscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystery - that which is difficult or impossible to understand or explain
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From 'enigmatic' + '-ness', with 'enigmatic' from Greek 'ainigma' meaning 'riddle'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛnɪɡˈmætɪknəs / (AmE), / ˌɛnɪɡˈmatɪknɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being enigmatic; having a puzzling, mysterious, or difficult-to-understand character.
the quality of being enigmatic

Examples

The enigmaticness of the painting left viewers pondering its deeper meaning for hours.
She admired his enigmaticness, finding his mysterious behavior both frustrating and intriguing.
The enigmaticness of the novel's main character is what makes the story so compelling.

Synset Examples

She conveys Jesus's magnetism and charm, as well as how his enigmaticness must have stymied those closest to him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystery - something that is difficult or impossible to understand or explain
  • Synonym: opacity - the quality of being difficult to understand
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From 'enigmatic' + '-ness', with 'enigmatic' from Greek ainigma (riddle).
