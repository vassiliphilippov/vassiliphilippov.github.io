ureterocele

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈriːtərəˌsiːl / (AmE), / jʊˈriːtərəˌsiːl / (BrE)
A swelling or bulging of the end of a ureter into the bladder, which may obstruct the flow of urine.
swelling of ureter in bladder
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with a ureterocele after noticing symptoms of urinary obstruction.
Surgical intervention may be required if a ureterocele is causing a blockage in the bladder.
Ultrasound revealed a ureterocele at the point where the ureter enters the bladder.

Etymology

From 'ureter' (the duct by which urine passes from the kidney to the bladder) and '-cele' from Greek kēlē (hernia, swelling).
