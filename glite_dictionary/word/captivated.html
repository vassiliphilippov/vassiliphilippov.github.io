captivated

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæp.tɪ.veɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tɪ.veɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
very interested or attracted by someone or something; having one's attention held completely.
deeply attracted or fascinated

Examples

She was captivated by the beauty of the sunset over the ocean.
The children listened, captivated, to the magician's every word.
He became captivated with her charm and intelligence after their first meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entranced - filled with wonder and delight, spellbound
  • Antonym: bored - feeling weary and uninterested

Etymology

Past participle of 'captivate', from Latin 'captivare' (to capture).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkæp.təˌveɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tɪ.veɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
experiencing intense interest and delight; fascinated or charmed
filled with wonder and delight

Examples

The audience was captivated by the magician's performance from beginning to end.
She stood captivated by the beauty of the setting sun over the mountains.
The novel's plot captivated readers around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enchanted - delighted or charmed; filled with great pleasure
  • Antonym: bored - feeling weary and uninterested

Etymology

From the verb 'captivate', from Latin 'captivare' (to capture, to enthrall).
