commiserative

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmɪz.əˌreɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / kəˈmɪz.ər.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
Feeling or expressing sympathy for someone else's misfortune; sympathetic.
expressing sympathy; sympathetic

Examples

She gave him a commiserative look as he told his sad story.
The teacher's commiserative tone comforted the students after their disappointing results.
He offered a commiserative word to his grieving friend.

Synset Examples

made commiserative clicking sounds with his tongue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sympathetic - feeling, showing, or expressing sympathy
  • Antonym: unsympathetic - not feeling, showing, or expressing sympathy

Etymology

From Latin 'commiserari' (to commiserate, feel pity).
