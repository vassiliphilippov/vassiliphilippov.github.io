unlikeable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlaɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈlaɪkəbl / (BrE)
(of a person or character) difficult or impossible to like; tending to evoke dislike or antipathy.
difficult or impossible to like

Examples

The main character was so unlikeable that readers struggled to sympathize with his struggles.
Despite her talent, she came across as rather unlikeable in interviews.
Some villains are intentionally written to be extremely unlikeable to heighten the story's conflict.

Synset Examples

all the characters were peculiarly unsympathetic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: likeable - pleasant and easy to like
  • Synonym: unpleasant - not enjoyable or agreeable; disagreeable

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'likeable' (able to be liked).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlaɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈlaɪkəbəl / (BrE)
Not easy to like; arousing dislike or a lack of affection.
difficult or impossible to like

Examples

The main character is so unlikeable that it's hard to root for him.
Her constant complaints made her increasingly unlikeable to her colleagues.
Despite his talents, his rude behavior rendered him unlikeable to many fans.

Synset Examples

a disagreeable and unlikable old woman

Related Terms

  • Antonym: likeable - pleasant and easy to like
  • Synonym: unpleasant - not pleasant or enjoyable

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'likeable' (pleasant or easy to like).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01464602-s
Synset POS: s
