election

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of choosing or selecting someone or something, especially through deliberate decision
the act of choosing or selecting

Examples

Her election of medicine as a career surprised her family.
The election of a leader involves careful consideration and debate.
His election to the committee was based on his expertise.

Synset Examples

her election of medicine as a profession

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selection - the act of choosing or selecting

Etymology

From Latin electio (choice, selection), from eligere (to choose).

2. NOUN

/ əˈlɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
A formal process in which people vote to choose someone for a public office or position.
process of voting for a position

Examples

Voter turnout in the recent election was higher than expected.
She was declared the winner of the mayoral election by a narrow margin.
Many international observers monitored the election to ensure fairness.

Synset Examples

the results of the election will be announced tonight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vote - an act of expressing a choice or decision in an election
  • Synonym: referendum - a general vote by the electorate on a single political question
  • Antonym: appointment - the act of assigning someone to a position without an election

Etymology

From Latin 'electionem' (choice, selection), from 'eligere' (to choose).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
(in theology) The predestination of certain individuals by God to receive divine mercy and salvation, especially as conceived in Calvinist doctrine.
theological predestination of some to salvation

Examples

Calvinist theology emphasizes the doctrine of election, where God chooses some individuals for salvation.
The concept of election raised debates about free will and divine justice within the church.
Many believers found comfort in the idea of their election as a sign of God's mercy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predestination - the doctrine that all events, especially salvation, are preordained by God

Etymology

From Latin electio (a choosing, selection), from eligere (to choose out).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

the fact of being chosen for a position, especially by vote; the state of having been elected
status of having been elected

Examples

they celebrated his election

Related Terms

From Latin 'electionem' (a choosing out), from 'eligere' (to choose out).
