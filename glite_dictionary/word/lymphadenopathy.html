lymphadenopathy

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪmfˌædəˈnɑpəθi / (AmE), / ˌlɪmfˌædɪˈnɒpəθi / (BrE)
Abnormal enlargement of the lymph nodes, usually as a result of disease.
enlargement of the lymph nodes
medical

Examples

The doctor found lymphadenopathy in the patient's neck during the examination.
Persistent lymphadenopathy may be a sign of infection or an immune disorder.
After several tests, the cause of his lymphadenopathy was determined to be a viral illness.

Etymology

From Greek 'lymph' (referring to lymph), 'adēn' (gland), and 'pathos' (suffering or disease).
