dirt

1. NOUN

/ dɝt / (AmE), / dɜːt / (BrE)
the top layer of the earth's surface, made up of soil, humus, and disintegrated rock
soil; earth's surface layer

Examples

The children spent the afternoon playing in the dirt behind the house.
Plants need good dirt to grow healthy and strong.
His shoes were covered in dirt after the hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soil - the material on the earth's surface in which plants grow
  • Synonym: earth - the substance of the land surface; soil

Etymology

From Old Norse 'drit' (excrement, dirt).

3. ADJ

/ dɝt / (AmE), / dɜːt / (BrE)
(of roads) not paved, leveled, or drained, making them unsuitable for travel in all weather; unpaved.
(of roads) unpaved and rough

Examples

The cabin can only be reached by a long dirt road through the forest.
After heavy rain, the dirt track became slippery and difficult to drive on.
Many rural communities are connected by narrow dirt roads that are hard to travel in winter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paved - (of roads) covered with a hard surface; made suitable for all weather travel

5. NOUN

/ dɝt / (AmE), / dɜːt / (BrE)
any substance or matter that makes something unclean or soiled
something that makes things dirty

Examples

You'll need to wash the table to remove all the dirt before setting the plates.
His shirt was covered in dirt after working in the garage all day.
Too much dirt on your hands can spread germs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filth - extremely dirty matter; foul or offensive waste
  • Antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean (absence of dirt)

Etymology

From Old Norse *drit* (excrement), later broadened in English sense.
