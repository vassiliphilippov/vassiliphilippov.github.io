Luscinia

1. NOUN

/ luːˈsɪniə / (AmE), / luːˈsɪniə / (BrE)
a genus of songbirds in the thrush family, including the nightingale and related species
genus of songbirds including nightingales

Examples

The genus Luscinia includes several species of melodious songbirds.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of Luscinia across Europe and Asia.
The common nightingale belongs to the genus Luscinia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightingale - a small songbird known for its powerful and beautiful song

Etymology

From Latin lusciniola, meaning 'nightingale.'

2. NOUN

/ luːˈsɪniə / (AmE), / luːˈsɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of small passerine birds known as nightingales, found in Eurasia and Africa.
genus of nightingales (birds)

Examples

The common nightingale belongs to the genus Luscinia.
Several species within Luscinia are admired for their melodious song.
Ornithologists frequently study members of the Luscinia genus in Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightingale - a small bird known for its powerful song

Etymology

From Latin *luscinia* (nightingale).
