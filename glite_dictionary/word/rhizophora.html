Rhizophora

1. NOUN

/ raɪˈzɑːfərə / (AmE), / raɪˈzɒfərə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees and shrubs, including mangroves, found in coastal and tidal areas.
genus of tropical trees/shrubs (mangroves)

Examples

The Rhizophora species play a crucial role in stabilizing coastal shorelines.
Scientists are studying how Rhizophora adaptations help mangroves survive in saline environments.
Several birds nest in the branches of Rhizophora trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mangrove - any tree or shrub growing in tidal coastal swamps, especially of the family Rhizophoraceae

Etymology

From Greek 'rhiza' (root) + 'phoros' (bearing), referring to the plant's root-bearing characteristic.
