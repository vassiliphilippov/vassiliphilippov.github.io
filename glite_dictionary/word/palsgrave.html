palsgrave

1. NOUN

/ ˈpælzˌɡreɪv / (AmE), / ˈpalzɡreɪv / (BrE)
(historical) The lord of a palatinate who exercised sovereign powers over his lands, especially in the Holy Roman Empire during the Middle Ages.
medieval lord of a palatinate
archaic

Examples

During the Middle Ages, a palsgrave held significant administrative authority over his territory.
The title of palsgrave was especially influential in regions of the Holy Roman Empire.
A palsgrave would often act as both judge and ruler in his palatinate.

Etymology

From German 'Pfalzgraf', from Middle High German 'pfalzgrāve', originally meaning count of the palace.
