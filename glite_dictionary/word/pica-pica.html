pica-pica

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːkəˈpiːkə / (AmE), / ˌpiːkəˈpiːkə / (BrE)
A stinging herb of tropical America, known for causing skin irritation.
stinging herb of tropical America

Examples

The leaves of the pica-pica can cause severe itching if touched with bare hands.
Farmers in the region are cautious about removing pica-pica from their fields.
There are folk remedies used to relieve the irritation caused by pica-pica exposure.

Etymology

From Spanish 'pica-pica', meaning 'itch-itch', referring to the plant's irritant effect.
