reorientation

1. NOUN

/ ˌriˌɔriənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɔːriənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of changing the direction, focus, or set of beliefs, often by adopting a new approach or perspective
a change in direction or beliefs

Examples

After the merger, the company underwent a major reorientation of its business strategy.
Her time abroad prompted a personal reorientation of her values and priorities.
The education system is in need of a reorientation to meet the needs of modern society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orientation - the determination of the relative position or direction

Etymology

From re- (again) + orientation, from Latin 'orientare' (to arrange, direct to the east).

2. NOUN

/ ˌriːˌɔriənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɔːriənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of changing the direction or focus of something; a shift in orientation
the act of changing direction or orientation

Examples

After the company's merger, a reorientation of its core strategy was necessary.
The therapy program began with a reorientation to help patients adjust to their new environment.
Political changes in the region led to a reorientation of foreign policy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: orientation - the process of aligning or positioning; the adjustment or direction of something

Etymology

From re- (again) + orientation, following the pattern in French réorientation.
