upfront

1. ADJ

/ ʌpˈfrʌnt / (AmE), / ʌpˈfrʌnt / (BrE)
(of a person) honest, direct, and open, especially about intentions or feelings.
frank and honest
informal

Examples

She was completely upfront about the risks involved in the project.
I appreciate when people are upfront with their feedback instead of sugarcoating it.
He's always upfront about his mistakes and takes responsibility for them.

Synset Examples

he was upfront about his intentions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frank - open, honest, and direct in speech or writing
  • Synonym: candid - truthful and straightforward; frank
  • Antonym: guarded - careful not to give too much information; cautious
