Caesalpinia bonduc

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːzælˈpɪniə ˈbɑndək / (AmE), / ˌsiːzælˈpɪniə ˈbɒndʊk / (BrE)
a tropical tree with large prickly pods whose brown seeds are used for jewelry and rosaries
tropical tree with prickly pods and bead-like seeds

Examples

The seeds of Caesalpinia bonduc are highly valued for making traditional necklaces and rosaries.
In some cultures, the pods of Caesalpinia bonduc are believed to have medicinal properties.
Botanists often collect Caesalpinia bonduc specimens for research and conservation efforts.

Etymology

Named after the Italian botanist Andrea Cesalpino; from Latinized form of his surname and the species name 'bonduc' of Arabic origin.
