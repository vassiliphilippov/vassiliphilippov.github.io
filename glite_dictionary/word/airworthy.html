airworthy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛrˌwɝːði / (AmE), / ˈeəˌwɜːði / (BrE)
(of an aircraft) fit and safe to fly according to official standards.
fit and safe to fly (of aircraft)

Examples

The mechanic certified that the damaged plane was no longer airworthy.
Before each flight, the crew ensures that the helicopter remains airworthy.
Strict regulations exist to keep every commercial jet airworthy at all times.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unairworthy - not fit or safe to fly (of an aircraft)

Etymology

Formed from 'air' and 'worthy', following the pattern of terms like 'seaworthy'.
