delicatessen

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛlɪkəˈtɛsən / (AmE), / ˌdɛlɪkəˈtɛsən / (BrE)
Ready-to-eat foods, such as cooked meats, cheeses, salads, and other prepared items, typically sold in a store or section of a store specializing in such products.
ready-to-eat food products

Examples

The store offers a wide variety of delicatessen for lunch and dinner.
She stopped by the market to pick up some delicatessen for the picnic.
His refrigerator was stocked with cheeses and meats from the delicatessen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deli - shop or counter selling ready-to-eat foods

Etymology

From German 'Delikatessen', plural of 'Delikatesse' meaning delicacy, from French 'délicatesse'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛlɪkəˈtɛsən / (AmE), / ˌdɛlɪkəˈtɛsən / (BrE)
A shop that sells ready-to-eat foods such as cooked meats, cheeses, salads, and other prepared items.
shop selling ready-to-eat foods

Examples

He stopped by the delicatessen to buy some sandwiches for lunch.
Their local delicatessen offers a variety of imported cheeses and cured meats.
She works at a small delicatessen downtown that specializes in Mediterranean dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deli - short form of 'delicatessen', shop selling ready-to-eat foods

Etymology

German Delikatessen (delicacies), from French délicatesse.
