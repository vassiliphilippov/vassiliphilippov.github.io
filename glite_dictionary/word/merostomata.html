Merostomata

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛrəˈstɑmətə / (AmE), / ˌmɪərəˈstɒmətə / (BrE)
a subclass or group of arthropods that includes the horseshoe crabs (Xiphosura) and extinct sea scorpions (Eurypterida), used in some zoological classifications.
group of arthropods including horseshoe crabs and sea scorpions

Examples

The Merostomata are known for their hard exoskeletons and jointed limbs.
Horseshoe crabs belong to the order Xiphosura, within the Merostomata.
Fossils of the extinct Eurypterida are important for studying the evolution of Merostomata.

Etymology

From Greek meros (part) + stoma (mouth), referring to segmented mouthparts.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛrəˈstɑmətə / (AmE), / ˌmɛərəˈstɒmətə / (BrE)
a subclass of arthropods, historically used in some classifications to include horseshoe crabs (Xiphosura) and extinct sea scorpions (Eurypterida)
a subclass of arthropods (horseshoe crabs, sea scorpions)

Examples

Many paleontologists study the fossils of Merostomata to understand arthropod evolution.
The subclass Merostomata includes both extinct and extant species, such as horseshoe crabs.
Taxonomists sometimes debate the classification of Merostomata in the animal kingdom.

Etymology

From Greek 'meros' (part, thigh) + 'stoma' (mouth).
