crude

1. ADJ

/ kruːd / (AmE), / kruːd / (BrE)
(of substances) in a natural or raw state; not yet processed or refined.
in a raw, unrefined state

Examples

The refinery turns crude oil into gasoline and other useful products.
The miners transported crude ores to the factory for further processing.
This region has vast reserves of crude petroleum underground.

Synset Examples

unrefined ore
crude oil

Related Terms

  • Antonym: refined - (of a substance) processed to remove impurities or unwanted elements

Etymology

From Latin crudus (raw, unripe, rough, harsh).

3. NOUN

/ kruːd / (AmE), / kruːd / (BrE)
A dark, unrefined oil consisting mainly of hydrocarbons, extracted from the earth and used to produce fuels and other products.
unrefined petroleum oil

Examples

The price of crude rose sharply after the announcement of new sanctions.
Vast tankers transport crude from oil-producing countries to refineries around the world.
Much of the world's energy supply is still dependent on crude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crude oil - unrefined petroleum as extracted from the ground
