twenty

1. NUM

/ ˈtwɛnti / (AmE), / ˈtwɛnti / (BrE)
The number equivalent to the product of four and five; one more than nineteen (20).
number; 20

Examples

Her father turned twenty last month and celebrated with his friends.
There are twenty students in our English class this semester.
He bought twenty apples at the market for the baking contest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nineteen - number; one less than twenty (19)
  • Degree Higher: twenty-one - number; one more than twenty (21)

Etymology

From Old English 'twēntig', equivalent to 'twēgen' (two) + 'tig' (group of ten).

3. NUM

/ ˈtwɛnti / (AmE), / ˈtwɛnti / (BrE)
The number 20; the cardinal number equal to the sum of nineteen and one.
the number 20

Examples

There are twenty students in my class this year.
She turned twenty years old last month.
They bought twenty tickets for the charity raffle.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nineteen - the number 19; one less than twenty
  • Degree Higher: twenty-one - the number 21; one more than twenty

Etymology

From Old English 'twēntig,' meaning 'two tens'.
