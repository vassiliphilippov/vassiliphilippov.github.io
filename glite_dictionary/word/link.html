link

1. NOUN

/ lɪŋk / (AmE), / lɪŋk / (BrE)
a ring or loop, typically made of metal, that is used to join or connect things together, especially as part of a chain or structural fastening
a metal ring or connector

Examples

The metal link broke, causing the chain to fall apart.
Each link in the chain is carefully welded for strength.
She used a small link to fasten the two wires together.

Synset Examples

the walls are held together with metal links placed in the wet mortar during construction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chain - a series of connected links, typically metal

Etymology

From Old Norse 'hlenkr' (chain, ring).
