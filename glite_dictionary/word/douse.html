to douse

1. VERB

/ daʊs / (AmE), / daʊs / (BrE)
to plunge or dip something briefly into a liquid, typically to wet, coat, or saturate it
quickly immerse in liquid

Examples

She doused her hands in cold water to soothe the burn.
Before painting, he doused the brush in primer.
The chef quickly doused the vegetables in boiling water to blanch them.

Synset Examples

dip the garment into the cleaning solution
dip the brush into the paint

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dip - to put something quickly into a liquid and then take it out
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ daʊs / (AmE), / daʊs / (BrE)
to plunge or dip something into a liquid, especially briefly or suddenly
immerse briefly in liquid

Examples

She quickly doused the cloth in cold water before wiping the table.
The chef doused the vegetables in boiling water to blanch them.
He doused his feet in the stream to cool off after the hike.

Synset Examples

He dipped into the pool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to immerse - to dip or submerge completely in a liquid
  • Synonym: to submerge - to put or sink below the surface of a liquid
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to remove from being dipped or submerged

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly related to Old Norse 'dūsa' (to doze), though sense and form are obscure.

3. VERB

/ daʊs / (AmE), / daʊs / (BrE)
to make someone or something completely wet by pouring liquid over them; to soak thoroughly
to wet or soak thoroughly

Examples

The firefighter doused the burning logs with water until the flames were out.
She accidentally doused her clothes with juice while pouring a drink.
They doused the campfire to make sure it was completely out before leaving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soak - to make something very wet by immersing it in liquid
  • Antonym: dry - to remove moisture or become free from moisture

5. VERB

/ daʊs / (AmE), / daʊs / (BrE)
to pour liquid over someone or something; to drench or thoroughly soak with liquid
pour liquid on; drench

Examples

The children laughed as they doused each other with water during the hot summer day.
He quickly doused the flames with a bucket of sand before they could spread.
She doused his shirt in water to help cool him down after the race.

Synset Examples

souse water on his hot face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soak - make completely wet by immersion or drenching
  • Synonym: to saturate - to soak thoroughly; cause to become thoroughly soaked with liquid
  • Antonym: dry - to remove moisture from; to make or become dry

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly related to the obsolete English 'dowse' (to strike, plunge), or to Dutch 'duisen' (to plunge).

/ daʊs / (AmE), / daʊs / (BrE)
(Nautical) To lower or remove (a sail, flag, etc.) quickly; to take down swiftly.
lower or remove something quickly (esp. a sail)

Examples

The crew had to douse the mainsail as the storm intensified.
She quickly doused the flag before the rain started.
They worked together to douse the spinnaker when the wind shifted suddenly.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Perhaps an alteration of 'dowse', possibly related to obsolete English 'dous' (to strike).
