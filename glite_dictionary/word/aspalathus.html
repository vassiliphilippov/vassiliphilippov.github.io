Aspalathus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæspəˈleɪθəs / (AmE), / ˌaspəˈleɪθəs / (BrE)
A genus of South African heath-like shrubs in the pea family, many of which have small, needle-like leaves.
genus of South African shrubs

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the Aspalathus genus in the Cape region.
Some herbal teas are made from plants belonging to Aspalathus.
The unique flowers of Aspalathus attract a variety of pollinators in South Africa.

Etymology

From Greek aspalathos, a name for a spiny shrub.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæspəˈlæθəs / (AmE), / ˌaspəˈlaθəs / (BrE)
A genus of South African heathlike shrubs in the family Fabaceae.
genus of South African shrubs

Examples

Rooibos tea is made from the leaves of the Aspalathus linearis shrub.
Several species within the genus Aspalathus are known for their needle-like leaves and brightly colored flowers.
Botanists studied the distribution of Aspalathus across the Western Cape region.

Etymology

From Greek aspalathos, the ancient name for a type of spiny shrub.
