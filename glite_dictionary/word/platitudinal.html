platitudinal

1. ADJ

/ ˌplætɪˈtjuːdnəl / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈtjuːdɪnəl / (BrE)
(of a remark, sentiment, or style) dull, tiresome, and commonplace, especially while claiming to be original or significant.
dull and commonplace, claiming significance

Examples

The candidate's speech was disappointingly platitudinal, offering little in the way of innovative ideas.
Her platitudinal comments during the meeting failed to impress her colleagues.
Despite its platitudinal tone, the essay was graded highly for its structure.

Synset Examples

bromidic sermons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trite - lacking originality or freshness
  • Antonym: original - new or inventive, not copied or commonplace

Etymology

From 'platitude' (trite remark) + '-al' (forming adjectives).
