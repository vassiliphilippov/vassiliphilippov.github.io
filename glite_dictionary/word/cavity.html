cavity

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈkæv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
(anatomy) A natural hollow or sinus within the body, such as the mouth or chest.
natural hollow in the body
medical

Examples

The chest cavity protects vital organs such as the heart and lungs.
Fluid can accumulate in the abdominal cavity after surgery.
The human skull contains several air-filled cavities known as sinuses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sinus - a cavity or hollow space in bone or other tissue

Etymology

From Latin cavitas (holiness, emptiness), from cavus (hollow).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkævəti / (AmE), / ˈkævəti / (BrE)
A soft, decayed area in a tooth caused by progressive tooth decay.
decayed area in a tooth
medical

Examples

The dentist found a cavity in my molar during the check-up.
If left untreated, a cavity can cause severe tooth pain and infection.
He needed a filling to repair the cavity in his tooth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caries - decay and crumbling of a tooth or bone

Etymology

From Latin *cavitas* (hollowness), from *cavus* (hollow).

4. NOUN

/ ˈkævəti / (AmE), / ˈkævəti / (BrE)
a hollow space within a solid object or body, often naturally formed or occurring
a hollow space inside something

Examples

The dentist discovered a small cavity between my molars during the check-up.
Birds often build their nests in a tree cavity for protection.
The archaeologists studied the ancient cavity believed to be a storage chamber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hole - an opening or hollow space in a solid body or surface
  • Antonym: solid - having no cavities or hollow spaces inside; completely filled

Etymology

From Latin 'cavitas' (hollowness), from 'cavus' (hollow).
