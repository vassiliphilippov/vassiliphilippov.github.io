pentylenetetrazol

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛntɪˌlaɪnəˈtɛtrəzɒl / (AmE), / ˌpɛntɪˌliːnəˈtɛtrəzɒl / (BrE)
a drug used as a circulatory and respiratory stimulant, also formerly used to induce convulsions in shock therapy
circulatory and respiratory stimulant drug
medical

Examples

Researchers tested pentylenetetrazol as a stimulant for patients with respiratory depression.
Historically, pentylenetetrazol was used to induce convulsions during shock therapy for mental illness.
The hospital no longer uses pentylenetetrazol due to the risks associated with its side effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metrazol - a brand name for pentylenetetrazol used as a stimulant and convulsant

Etymology

From pentylen(e) + tetrazol(e), indicating its chemical structure.
