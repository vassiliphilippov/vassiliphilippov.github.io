cytogeneticist

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪtoʊdʒəˈnɛtɪsɪst / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪtə(ʊ)dʒɪˈnɛtɪsɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who specializes in the study of chromosomes and cell structures related to heredity
specialist in chromosomes and heredity
medical

Examples

The cytogeneticist analyzed the patient's chromosomes for potential genetic abnormalities.
As a cytogeneticist, she often collaborated with oncologists to study cancer cells.
The conference brought together leading cytogeneticists from around the world to share their research.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: geneticist - a scientist who studies genetics

Etymology

From 'cyto-' (cell), 'genetic', and '-ist' (specialist).
