symposiarch

1. NOUN

/ sɪmˈpoʊziˌɑrk / (AmE), / sɪmˈpəʊziˌɑːk / (BrE)
the person who presides over a banquet, proposing toasts and introducing speakers
toastmaster or host at a banquet

Examples

The symposiarch signaled for everyone to raise their glasses in a toast.
As symposiarch, she introduced each speaker with a witty anecdote.
Traditionally, the symposiarch ensured the smooth flow of conversation and festivities at the dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toastmaster - the person who introduces speakers at a banquet

Etymology

From Greek symposiarchēs, from symposion (drinking party) + archēs (ruler).
