Dracunculus

1. NOUN

/ drəˈkʌŋkjələs / (AmE), / drəˈkʌŋkjʊləs / (BrE)
A genus of tuberous herbaceous perennial plants in the arum family, commonly known as dragon arum.
genus of tuberous perennials (dragon arums)

Examples

The distinctive appearance of the Dracunculus makes it a favorite among exotic plant collectors.
We planted a Dracunculus in the shade garden to add some dramatic flair.
Botanists are studying the pollination methods of the Dracunculus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragon arum - a common name for plants of the genus Dracunculus

Etymology

From Latin 'dracunculus' (a little dragon), referring to the appearance of the plant's flower.

genus of herbaceous plants (dragon arum)

Examples

The Dracunculus is notable for its striking appearance and foul odor.
Gardeners sometimes plant Dracunculus for its unique flowers despite its pungent smell.

Related Terms

From Latin *dracunculus* (little dragon), referring to the plant's serpent-like spadix.
