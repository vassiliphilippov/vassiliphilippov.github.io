cypre

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪpri / (AmE), / ˈsɪpri / (BrE)
A large tropical American tree of the genus Cordia, grown for its abundant creamy white flowers and valuable wood.
tropical American tree (Cordia genus)

Examples

The craftsman used cypre wood to make elegant furniture.
In the spring, the cypre tree blooms with beautiful creamy white flowers.
Farmers planted rows of cypre along the boundary of their land for shade and timber.
