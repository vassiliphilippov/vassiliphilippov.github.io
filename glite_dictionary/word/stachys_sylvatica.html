Stachys sylvatica

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæ.kɪs sɪlˈvæ.tɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈstæ.kɪs sɪlˈvæ.tɪ.kə / (BrE)
a foul-smelling perennial herb native to Eurasia, with a green creeping rhizome, commonly known as hedge woundwort
a Eurasian perennial herb (hedge woundwort)

Examples

Stachys sylvatica is often found growing in damp woodlands across Europe.
The leaves of Stachys sylvatica give off a strong, unpleasant odor when crushed.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Stachys sylvatica for its medicinal properties and ornamental value.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Stachys' (meaning 'an ear of grain'), and species epithet 'sylvatica' (meaning 'of the woods').
