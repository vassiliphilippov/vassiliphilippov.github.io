cheekily

1. ADV

/ ˈtʃiːkɪli / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːkɪli / (BrE)
In a boldly rude or disrespectful manner; in a way that shows a lack of respect or is amusingly impudent.
in a bold or impudent way

Examples

He cheekily asked the teacher for more time on the assignment.
She cheekily winked at the guard as she walked past.
The boy cheekily took another cookie when his mother wasn't looking.

Synset Examples

brashly, she asked for a rebate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boldly - in a confident and courageous way
  • Antonym: respectfully - in a polite or reverent manner

Etymology

From 'cheeky' + '-ly', where 'cheeky' comes from 'cheek' (impudence, insolence) plus the suffix '-y'.
