ester

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛstɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛstə / (BrE)
an organic compound produced by the reaction between an acid and an alcohol, with the elimination of water, often characterized by a distinctive fragrance
organic compound from acid and alcohol

Examples

The aroma of many fruits is due to the presence of an ester in their chemical makeup.
She explained that an ester is formed when an acid reacts with an alcohol, releasing water.
Ethyl acetate is a common ester used as a solvent in nail polish removers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amide - organic compound with a carbonyl group linked to a nitrogen atom

Etymology

From German 'Ester,' of uncertain origin, introduced by chemist Leopold Gmelin in 1848.
