verifiable

1. ADJ

/ ˌvɛrɪˈfaɪəbəl / (AmE), / ˌvɛrɪˈfaɪəbl / (BrE)
capable of being proven true or false by experiment, observation, or investigation
able to be proven or confirmed

Examples

Scientific claims should be verifiable through observation and experimentation.
The data provided by the researcher was fully verifiable by independent experts.
We need verifiable evidence before making any accusations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falsifiable - able to be proven false by evidence or experiment
  • Antonym: unverifiable - not able to be confirmed or proven true

Etymology

From Medieval Latin verifiabilis, from Latin verificare (to verify) + -abilis (able).

2. ADJ

/ ˌvɛrəˈfaɪəbəl / (AmE), / ˌvɛərɪˈfaɪəbl / (BrE)
able to be proven to be true or correct by checking relevant facts or evidence
able to be proven or checked

Examples

The scientist presented only verifiable data to support her claims.
Your story is interesting, but do you have any verifiable evidence?
All financial transactions are recorded in a verifiable way for transparency.

Synset Examples

a verifiable account of the incident

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unverifiable - not able to be verified or proved true
  • Synonym: provable - able to be proved or demonstrated as true

Etymology

From Medieval Latin verificabilis, from Latin verficare (to verify) + -abilis (-able)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01619937-s
Synset POS: s
