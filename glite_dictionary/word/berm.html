berm

1. NOUN

/ bɝm / (AmE), / bɜːm / (BrE)
a narrow ledge, shelf, or raised strip of land, typically at the top or bottom of a slope, often used to control water flow or provide support
narrow ledge or shelf at a slope

Examples

A berm was built along the riverbank to prevent flooding during heavy rains.
Hikers often stop to rest on the berm at the top of the trail.
The construction crew added a small berm to direct runoff away from the road.

Etymology

From Dutch 'berm' (edge of a ditch or fortification).

2. NOUN

/ bɝm / (AmE), / bɜːm / (BrE)
a narrow strip of land or unpaved shoulder along the edge of a road, often used for stopping vehicles or providing drainage
narrow strip of land at roadside

Examples

The driver pulled over onto the berm to check his tires.
Wildflowers were growing all along the berm beside the highway.
Cyclists often rest on the grassy berm at the side of the country road.

Synset Examples

the car pulled off onto the shoulder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoulder - the strip of land bordering a roadway, for emergencies or breakdowns

Etymology

From Dutch 'berm' (edge, bank, a raised path), used in English since the 18th century, originally for military earthworks.
