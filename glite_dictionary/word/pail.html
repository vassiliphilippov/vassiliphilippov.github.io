pail

1. NOUN

/ peɪl / (AmE), / peɪl / (BrE)
the amount that a pail can hold
quantity a pail can contain

Examples

She brought in a pail of fresh milk from the barn.
The children took turns filling a pail with sand at the beach.
We need a pail of clean water to mop the floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bucket - quantity a bucket can contain

Etymology

Probably from Old French paelle (pan, bucket), from Latin patella (small pan).

2. NOUN

/ peɪl / (AmE), / peɪl / (BrE)
A round, usually metal or plastic container with high sides and a handle, used for carrying liquids or other materials; a bucket.
a round container with a handle; a bucket

Examples

She filled the pail with water from the well.
The children carried sand in a pail to build their sandcastle.
Please rinse the mop in the pail before using it again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bucket - an open container with a handle, typically used for carrying liquids or other substances

Etymology

From Old French 'paelle' (pan, bucket), from Latin 'patella' (pan, small dish).
