butterweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚˌwid / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəˌwiːd / (BrE)
A North American plant of the daisy family, with yellow flowers, commonly found in fields and open areas.
a yellow-flowered North American plant
american

Examples

The field was covered in bright butterweed during the spring.
We carefully pulled the butterweed from the garden beds to protect other plants.
Farmers often consider butterweed to be a troublesome weed in their pastures.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚˌwid / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəˌwiːd / (BrE)
any of several yellow-flowered plants of the genus Packera, commonly found in North America; sometimes classified in the genus Senecio.
a yellow-flowered plant of genus Packera

Examples

Fields were covered with blooming butterweed in the early spring.
The bright yellow flowers of butterweed are easy to spot along the roadside.
Farmers often consider butterweed a nuisance weed among their crops.
