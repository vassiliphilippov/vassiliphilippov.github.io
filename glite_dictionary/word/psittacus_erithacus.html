Psittacus erithacus

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈtæk.əs ˌɛr.ɪˈθeɪ.kəs / (AmE), / sɪˈtæk.əs ˌɛr.ɪˈθɑː.kəs / (BrE)
A species of African grey parrot, known for its intelligence and ability to mimic speech, native to equatorial Africa.
African grey parrot species

Examples

The Psittacus erithacus is highly regarded for its remarkable ability to mimic human speech.
Many experts consider the Psittacus erithacus to be among the most intelligent of all bird species.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect wild populations of Psittacus erithacus in Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African grey parrot - Common name for Psittacus erithacus, a grey parrot species with red tail

Etymology

From Latin: 'Psittacus' meaning 'parrot', and 'erithacus' (red), referring to the bird's red tail.
