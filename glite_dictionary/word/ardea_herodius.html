Ardea herodius

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈdiːə həˈroʊdi.əs / (AmE), / ɑːˈdiːə hɛˈrəʊdi.əs / (BrE)
A large American heron with bluish-grey plumage, commonly known as the great blue heron.
large American heron species

Examples

The Ardea herodius is often seen wading in shallow waters hunting for fish.
Naturalists observed an Ardea herodius nesting near the riverbank this spring.
Field guides frequently list Ardea herodius as the great blue heron in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great blue heron - common English name for Ardea herodius
  • Degree Higher: heron - any of various long-legged wading birds of the family Ardeidae

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ardea' meaning 'heron' and 'herodius' an ancient term for heron.
