actinoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.təˌnɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈaktɪnɔɪd / (BrE)
any of a series of radioactive chemical elements with atomic numbers from 89 through 103, including actinium and the actinides.
series of radioactive elements (atomic numbers 89–103)
medical

Examples

Scientists study the properties of actinoids to develop new types of nuclear fuel.
Plutonium is one of the most well-known actinoids used in the nuclear industry.
The periodic table places all actinoids in a distinct row below the main body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actinide - any of the 15 chemical elements from actinium to lawrencium (atomic numbers 89–103)

Etymology

Formed from 'actinium' (the first element in the series) + '-oid' (resembling or like).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæk.təˌnɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈæk.tɪnɔɪd / (BrE)
having a radial or star-shaped form, especially in biological structures
having a radial or star-like shape

Examples

The starfish has an actinoid body structure, with arms radiating from a central disc.
Many flowers exhibit an actinoid symmetry, making their petals appear like rays.
Scientists compared the actinoid forms of ancient fossils to modern sea urchins.

Synset Examples

starfish are actinoid — that is, they are radially symmetrical

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radial - having parts arranged like rays or radii from a common center

Etymology

From Greek aktis, aktinos (ray) + -oid (like, resembling).
