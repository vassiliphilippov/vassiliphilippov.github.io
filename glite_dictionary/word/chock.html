chock

1. VERB

/ tʃɑk / (AmE), / tʃɒk / (BrE)
to secure or prevent movement by placing a wedge or block (a chock) against something, especially a wheel
to secure with a wedge or block

Examples

The mechanic made sure to chock the wheels before beginning repairs on the truck.
Always chock the aircraft's tires after parking it on the runway.
She chocked the wagon firmly to prevent it from rolling down the hill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to obstruct or stop movement by placing an object

2. VERB

/ tʃɑk / (AmE), / tʃɒk / (BrE)
to secure or support (a vehicle, boat, or object) with wedges or blocks to prevent movement.
support or secure with chocks

Examples

They carefully chocked the wheels to stop the trailer from rolling.
Make sure you chock the aircraft before leaving it unattended on the tarmac.
The crew chocked the boat after pulling it out of the water.

Synset Examples

chock the boat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to prevent the movement of something, usually by placing an object in its way

Etymology

Probably from Old French choque (block, log), related to the verb chouquer (to block).

3. NOUN

/ tʃɑk / (AmE), / tʃɒk / (BrE)
a block or wedge, usually of wood, placed against a wheel or heavy object to prevent it from moving or rolling
block or wedge to prevent movement

Examples

The pilot placed a chock under the airplane's wheel before leaving the runway.
He used a wooden chock to keep the heavy cart from rolling down the hill.
Without a chock, the trailer could easily move when parked on a slope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wedge - a piece of material with a sloping edge used to hold an object in place or split something

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle French choque (block, stump) or Old French choc (shock, blow).

Source: WordNet
