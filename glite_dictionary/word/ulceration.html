ulceration

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌl.səˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌl.səˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of forming an ulcer or of becoming ulcerated
formation of an ulcer
medical

Examples

The chronic wound showed signs of ulceration after several weeks without improvement.
Ulceration of the gastric lining can cause significant pain and bleeding.
Poor circulation in the legs may lead to ulceration of the skin over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ulcer - an open sore on the body or a break in the skin or mucous membrane

Etymology

From Latin 'ulceratio', from 'ulcerare' (to ulcerate), from 'ulcus' (sore).

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌl.səˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌl.sərˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a localized area of tissue loss with inflammation, often forming an open sore on the skin or a mucous membrane
formation of an open sore (medical)
medical

Examples

The persistent ulceration on his leg required frequent medical attention.
Chronic ulceration of the mouth can make eating difficult.
Early treatment can reduce the risk of skin ulceration in diabetic patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ulcer - an open sore on the skin or mucous membrane

Etymology

From Late Latin ulceratio(n-), from Latin ulcus (sore).
