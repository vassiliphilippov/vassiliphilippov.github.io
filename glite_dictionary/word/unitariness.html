unitariness

1. NOUN

/ juːˈnɪt̬.ər.i.nɪs / (AmE), / juːˈnɪt.ər.i.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unified or forming a single entity.
the state of being unified or one

Examples

The philosopher questioned whether total unitariness in society is actually achievable.
The building’s design aimed to express unitariness rather than fragmentation.
Critics have argued that political unitariness can suppress regional identities.

Synset Examples

Modernist thinking tended to see the unitariness of groups and societies as equivalent to that of individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unity - the state of being joined as a whole
  • Antonym: division - the act or process of separating into parts

Etymology

From 'unitary' (from Latin 'unitarius', from 'unitas' meaning oneness) + suffix '-ness'.
