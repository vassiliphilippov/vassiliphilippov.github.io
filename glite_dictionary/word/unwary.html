unwary

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɛri / (AmE), / ʌnˈweəri / (BrE)
not careful or watchful about possible dangers or deception; easily surprised or tricked.
not alert to danger or trickery

Examples

Unscrupulous salespeople often take advantage of unwary customers who do not read the fine print.
The path through the forest was full of hidden traps for the unwary.
An unwary investor might be tempted by offers that seem too good to be true.

Synset Examples

the shrieks of unwary animals taken by surprise
some thieves prey especially on unwary travelers
seduce the unwary reader into easy acquiescence

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wary - careful to avoid danger or trouble
  • Synonym: cautious - careful to avoid potential problems or dangers

Etymology

From un- (not) + wary (cautious; from Old English warig, careful).
