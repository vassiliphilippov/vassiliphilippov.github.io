slave-driver

1. NOUN

/ ˈsleɪvˌdraɪvər / (AmE), / ˈsleɪvˌdraɪvə / (BrE)
a person who forces others to work very hard, usually in a harsh or demanding way
person who drives others to work excessively hard

Examples

My new supervisor is a total slave-driver who never lets us take a break.
The students complained that their coach was something of a slave-driver during practice.
Nobody likes working for a slave-driver who constantly demands overtime.

Synset Examples

She felt her manager was a slave-driver, demanding too much.
The boss is a real slave-driver, pushing everyone to their limits.
Being a slave-driver can lead to high employee turnover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taskmaster - a person who imposes hard or continuous work
  • Synonym: martinet - a strict disciplinarian, especially in the armed forces

Etymology

Originally referred to a person who drove slaves to work; now used figuratively.
