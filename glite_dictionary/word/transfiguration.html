transfiguration

1. NOUN

/ ˌtræns.fɪˈɡjʊr.eɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtræns.fɪˈɡjʊə.reɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a complete change of form or appearance into a more beautiful or spiritual state, often so as to exalt or glorify
the act of changing to a more exalted form

Examples

The painting depicted the transfiguration of the ordinary man into a legendary hero.
Her kindness brought about a remarkable transfiguration in the lives of everyone around her.
The sunrise cast the mountain in golden light, a natural transfiguration that left the hikers speechless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transformation - a thorough or dramatic change in form or appearance

Etymology

From Latin transfiguratio, from transfigurare (to change the shape of).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtræns.fɪˈɡjʊr.eɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtræns.fɪˈɡjʊə.rə.ʃən / (BrE)
a striking or significant change in appearance, form, or nature, often suggesting an improvement or elevation
a dramatic change in appearance or nature

Examples

The artist's mural brought about a transfiguration of the once dull city wall.
Her face underwent a remarkable transfiguration when she heard the good news.
The charity hopes for a transfiguration of the neighborhood through community projects.

Synset Examples

the metamorphosis of the old house into something new and exciting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transformation - a thorough or dramatic change in form or appearance
  • Synonym: metamorphosis - a change of the form or nature of a thing into a completely different one

Etymology

From Latin transfiguratio, from transfigurare (to change shape, transform).
