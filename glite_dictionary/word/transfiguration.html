Transfiguration

1. NOUN

/ ˌtræns.fɪˈɡjʊr.ə.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtræns.fɪˈɡjʊə.rə.ʃən / (BrE)
(in Christianity) A church festival commemorating the Transfiguration of Jesus.
Christian festival commemorating Jesus' Transfiguration

Examples

The Transfiguration is celebrated on August 6th by many Christian denominations.
The church hosted a special service for the Transfiguration this year.
During the Transfiguration festival, the story of Jesus' transformation is read aloud to the congregation.

Etymology

From Latin transfiguratio (change of shape or form), from transfigurare (to transform).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtrænsfəˈɡjʊreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænsfɪˈɡjʊəreɪʃən / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) the event where Jesus was suddenly transformed and radiated divine light before his disciples.
New Testament event: radiance of Jesus

Examples

The Transfiguration is described in the Gospels as a moment when Christ's face shone like the sun.
Many artists have tried to capture the brilliance of the Transfiguration in their paintings.
The church celebrated the Feast of the Transfiguration with a special mass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Metamorphosis - a complete change of form or nature

Etymology

From Latin 'transfiguratio', from 'transfigurare' (to change the shape of).
