suasible

1. ADJ

/ ˈswɑːzəbəl / (AmE), / ˈswɑːzɪbəl / (BrE)
(rare) Capable of being persuaded; easily influenced by argument.
susceptible to persuasion
archaic

Examples

The young prince was so suasible that his advisors often managed to sway his decisions.
Because she was suasible, her friends convinced her to try new experiences.
The villain targeted suasible minds in his plot to manipulate the kingdom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persuadable - easily persuaded; susceptible to persuasion
  • Antonym: incorrigible - impervious to persuasion or correction

Etymology

From Late Latin suasibilis (persuadable), from Latin suadere (to advise, persuade).
