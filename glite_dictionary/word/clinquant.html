clinquant

1. ADJ

/ ˈklɪŋkənt / (AmE), / ˈklɪŋkənt / (BrE)
Glittering with gold or silver; sparkling or tinsel-like in appearance, especially in a way that is showy or superficial.
glittering with gold or silver

Examples

The ballroom was adorned with clinquant decorations that dazzled the guests.
Beneath the clinquant surface, the costume was actually quite simple and plain.
She wore a clinquant dress that shimmered in the candlelight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tinsel - thin strips of glittering material used as decoration

Etymology

From French 'clinquant', from 'clinquer' (to clink or glitter).
