mendicancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.dɪ.kən.si / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.dɪ.kən.si / (BrE)
the condition or practice of begging for money, food, or other necessities
the state of being a beggar

Examples

After losing his job, he feared he would fall into mendicancy if he couldn't find work soon.
The novelist documented the harsh realities of urban mendicancy in his latest book.
Centuries ago, mendicancy was often regarded as a legitimate form of religious devotion.

Synset Examples

they were reduced to mendicancy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: begging - the act of asking for money or help as a beggar
  • Antonym: wealth - an abundance of valuable resources or material possessions

Etymology

From Latin 'mendicantia', from 'mendicans' meaning 'begging'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛndɪˌkænsi / (AmE), / ˈmɛndɪkənsi / (BrE)
the practice of begging for money or food, especially in public places
the act of begging for money or food

Examples

The city's new policy aimed to reduce mendicancy in busy downtown areas.
Historical records show that mendicancy was common during periods of economic hardship.
She dedicated her research to studying the causes and effects of urban mendicancy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: begging - the act of asking for money or food as charity, especially in the street

Etymology

From Latin mendicantia, from mendicans (begging).
