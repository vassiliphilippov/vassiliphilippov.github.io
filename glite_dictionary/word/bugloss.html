bugloss

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌɡˌlɔs / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡlɒs / (BrE)
A perennial or biennial herb, often cultivated for its delicate usually blue flowers, belonging to the borage family.
a herb with blue flowers, borage family

Examples

The gardener planted bugloss near the fence to attract bees and other pollinators.
The striking blue flowers of bugloss make it a favorite among wildflower enthusiasts.
Some varieties of bugloss thrive in dry, rocky soils and require little maintenance.

Etymology

From Greek 'bouglōssos', meaning 'ox tongue', referring to the rough texture of the leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌɡˌlɔs / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡˌlɒs / (BrE)
A European plant with spiny, tongue-shaped leaves and yellow or blue flowers, commonly found as a weed and naturalized in parts of the United States.
a European weed with spiny leaves and yellow or blue flowers

Examples

The field was covered with bugloss growing wild among the other weeds.
Gardeners sometimes struggle to remove bugloss because of its deep roots and spiny leaves.
In early summer, you can spot bugloss by its distinctive yellow flowers.

Etymology

From Greek bouglōssos, from bous (ox) + glōssa (tongue), referring to the shape of the leaves.
