flexible

1. ADJ

/ ˈflɛksəbəl / (AmE), / ˈflɛksɪbl / (BrE)
able to change or be adapted easily in response to different circumstances or needs.
capable of being changed or adapted

Examples

Our company offers flexible working hours to accommodate employees' personal schedules.
Thanks to a flexible approach, the plan could be adjusted to meet new requirements.
This ticket allows for flexible travel dates within a three-month period.

Synset Examples

flexible schedules

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rigid - unable or unwilling to change or adapt

Etymology

From Latin flexibilis, from flectere (to bend).

2. ADJ

/ ˈflɛksəbəl / (AmE), / ˈflɛksəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to bend easily without breaking and return to its original shape; capable of being bent or flexed repeatedly without breaking.
able to bend without breaking

Examples

The plastic ruler is flexible enough to bend without snapping.
This hose is made from a flexible material that returns to its shape after being twisted.
Yoga helps you become more flexible by stretching your muscles and joints.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rigid - not able to be bent or changed; stiff
  • Synonym: pliable - easily bent; flexible

Etymology

From Latin 'flexibilis' (that can be bent), from 'flectere' (to bend).
