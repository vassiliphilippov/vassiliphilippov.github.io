freewheel

1. VERB

/ ˈfriˌwil / (AmE), / ˈfriːwiːl / (BrE)
to live in a relaxed, unhurried, or sometimes irresponsible way, without worrying about rules or consequences
live freely or irresponsibly

Examples

After college, she decided to freewheel for a while before starting her career.
He spent his twenties freewheeling across Europe, taking odd jobs and enjoying life.
Some worry that if they freewheel too long, it will be hard to settle down later.

Synset Examples

My son drifted around for years in California before going to law school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drift - to move or be carried along without purpose; to live aimlessly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02623391-v

2. NOUN

/ ˈfriˌwil / (AmE), / ˈfriːwiːl / (BrE)
a device, especially on the rear wheel of a bicycle, that allows the wheel to turn freely when the pedals are not being turned
device allowing wheels to spin freely

Examples

The bicycle's freewheel lets you coast downhill without pedaling.
After replacing the freewheel, the bike rode much more smoothly.
A stuck freewheel can prevent the rear wheel from spinning properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coaster brake - a type of brake integrated into the hub of a bicycle that's activated by pedaling backward
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03398597-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈfriˌwil / (AmE), / ˈfriːwiːl / (BrE)
to coast in a vehicle, especially a bicycle, without using power from the pedals or engine, often relying on a freewheel mechanism
coast in a vehicle without pedaling or power

Examples

He let the bicycle freewheel down the hill, enjoying the smooth ride.
When the engine failed, the car freewheeled quietly to a stop.
She loves freewheeling along country roads without pedaling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coast - move easily without using power

Etymology

From 'free' + 'wheel', referring to the mechanical device allowing wheels to turn freely.
