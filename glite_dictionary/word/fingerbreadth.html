fingerbreadth

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡərˌbrɛdθ / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəˌbrɛdθ / (BrE)
The width of a finger, used as an informal or historical unit of linear measurement.
the width of a finger as a unit of measure

Examples

The crack in the wall was barely a fingerbreadth wide.
He measured the gap by placing his thumb beside it and found it was just a fingerbreadth.
The fabric was torn open by a fingerbreadth along its edge.

Etymology

Formed from 'finger' + 'breadth', literally meaning the width of a finger.
