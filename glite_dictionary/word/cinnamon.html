cinnamon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnəmən / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəmən / (BrE)
An aromatic spice made from the dried bark of certain tropical trees, used to flavor foods and drinks.
aromatic spice from tree bark

Examples

She sprinkled some cinnamon on her oatmeal for extra flavor.
The smell of cinnamon filled the kitchen as the buns baked.
A dash of cinnamon can enhance the taste of your coffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassia - a similar but distinct spice obtained from different species

Etymology

From Greek 'kinnamōmon' via Latin and Old French 'cinnamome'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnəmən / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəmən / (BrE)
A tropical Asian tree (genus Cinnamomum) with aromatic yellowish-brown bark, which is the source of the spice cinnamon.
tropical tree; source of cinnamon spice

Examples

The cinnamon in her backyard blooms with fragrant leaves each spring.
Farmers carefully strip the bark from the cinnamon to harvest the spice.
The island is famous for its vast cinnamon plantations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinnamon - A sweet spice made from the bark of the cinnamon tree

Etymology

From Greek kinnamōmon, via Latin cinnamomum.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnəmən / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəmɪn / (BrE)
A spice made from the dried aromatic bark of the Ceylon cinnamon tree, used in cooking as rolled strips or ground powder.
aromatic spice from cinnamon tree bark

Examples

She sprinkled a pinch of cinnamon over her morning oatmeal for extra flavor.
The recipe calls for ground cinnamon and sugar to be mixed together.
A warm cinnamon roll is perfect with a cup of coffee.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cassia - a spice similar to cinnamon but from a different tree species

Etymology

From Old French *cinnamome*, from Latin *cinnamomum*, from Greek *kinnamōmon*, from a Semitic source related to Hebrew *qinnāmōn*.
