finback

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪnˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈfɪnˌbæk / (BrE)
A large baleen whale, distinguished by a flat head and deep grooves along the throat, found in the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
a large baleen whale species

Examples

The finback is the second largest whale species after the blue whale.
Scientists tracked a pod of finbacks migrating along the coast of California.
The distinctive dorsal fin makes the finback easy to identify in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fin whale - another common name for the same whale species

Etymology

Named for the prominent dorsal fin on its back.
