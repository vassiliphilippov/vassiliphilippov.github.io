to possess

1. VERB

/ pəˈzɛs / (AmE), / pəˈzɛs / (BrE)
(of an emotion, idea, or impulse) to take control of or strongly influence someone; to dominate or overwhelm (a person’s mind, spirit, or actions).
(of emotion/idea) to control or dominate someone

Examples

A sudden fear possessed him as he stepped onto the stage.
What possessed you to make such a reckless decision?
During the heated debate, anger seemed to possess every participant.

Synset Examples

What possessed you to buy this house?
A terrible rage possessed her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overwhelm - to overpower completely in mind or feeling
  • Synonym: control - to exercise restraining or directing influence over

Etymology

From Latin possessus, past participle of possidēre (to have, hold, possess).

2. VERB

/ pəˈzɛs / (AmE), / pəˈzɛs / (BrE)
to have (a quality, knowledge, skill, or attribute) as one's own
have (a quality or knowledge)

Examples

She possesses exceptional musical talent and dedication.
This candidate possesses the experience needed for the position.
Few people possess the patience required to master such a complex skill.

Synset Examples

he possesses great knowledge about the Middle East

Related Terms

  • Synonym: have - to own or hold something
  • Antonym: lack - to not have or be without something

Etymology

From Latin possessus, past participle of possidēre (to have, own).

3. VERB

/ pəˈzɛs / (AmE), / pəˈzɛs / (BrE)
to have or own something, especially something valuable or important
to have or own something

Examples

The wealthy collector possessed several rare and valuable paintings.
He possesses a deep knowledge of classical music and history.
Despite her young age, she possessed remarkable confidence and independence.

Synset Examples

He owns three houses in Florida
How many cars does she have?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: own - to have something as one's property
  • Synonym: have - to hold or possess something
  • Antonym: lack - to be without or not have something

Etymology

From Latin possess-, past participle of possidēre (to have, hold, occupy).
