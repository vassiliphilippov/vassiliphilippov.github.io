Tuileries

1. PROPN

/ twiːˈlɛriz / (AmE), / tɥi.lə.ʁi / (BrE)
the royal palace in Paris built for Catherine de Medici in 1564, largely destroyed in 1871, with only the formal gardens remaining today
former royal palace and gardens in Paris

Examples

The Tuileries were once the center of royal life in Paris before being destroyed in 1871.
While visiting Paris, we strolled through the beautiful gardens of the Tuileries.
Artists often find inspiration among the statues and fountains in the Tuileries.

Etymology

French, from 'tuilerie' meaning 'tile factory', as the palace was built on the site of former tileworks.

2. PROPN

/ twiˈlɛriz / (AmE), / twiˈlɛəriːz / (BrE)
a large formal garden in Paris situated between the Louvre and the Place de la Concorde
formal gardens in Paris

Examples

The Tuileries are a popular place for locals and tourists to stroll and relax.
Monet often painted scenes of the Tuileries in different seasons.
The Louvre Museum is separated from the Place de la Concorde by the Tuileries.

Etymology

From French 'Tuileries', referring to the tile factories (tuileries) that were previously on the site.
