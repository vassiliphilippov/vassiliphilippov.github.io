efficiency

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / ɪˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
The ratio of the output to the input of a system, often used to measure how well a process or machine converts resources into useful results.
ratio of output to input in a system

Examples

The new engine's efficiency makes it much cheaper to run than previous models.
Improving the efficiency of the factory increased production without additional costs.
Energy efficiency is an important factor when evaluating new appliances.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inefficiency - lack of efficiency; the state of not achieving maximum productivity

Etymology

From Latin efficientia (power, efficiency), from efficere (to accomplish).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / ɪˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
The ability to accomplish a job with the minimum expenditure of time and effort; skillfulness in avoiding waste.
skillfulness in avoiding waste

Examples

The new software greatly improved the team's efficiency at completing tasks.
She admired his efficiency in organizing meetings and appointments.
Automating routine tasks can increase efficiency and reduce costs.

Synset Examples

she did the work with great efficiency

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inefficiency - lack of ability to avoid wasted time and effort
  • Synonym: effectiveness - the degree to which something is successful in producing a desired result

Etymology

From Latin efficientia (power to accomplish), from efficere (accomplish, effect).
