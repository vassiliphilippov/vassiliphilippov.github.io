Huang He

1. PROPN

/ hwɑŋ hɤ / (AmE), / hwæŋ hɜː / (BrE)
a major river in northern China, also known as the Yellow River, flowing eastwards to the Yellow Sea and noted for its yellow silt
major river in northern China (Yellow River)

Examples

The Huang He is often called the cradle of Chinese civilization.
After heavy rains, the Huang He frequently floods its surrounding plains.
Many ancient cities were founded along the banks of the Huang He.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yellow River - another name for the Huang He river in China

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese 'Huáng Hé', meaning 'Yellow River'.
