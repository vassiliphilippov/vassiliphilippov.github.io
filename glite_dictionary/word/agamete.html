agamete

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡæmiːt / (AmE), / əˈɡæmiːt / (BrE)
a cell that reproduces asexually without fusion with another cell
asexual reproductive cell
medical

Examples

Under certain conditions, the algae produce agametes rather than gametes.
Some protozoa multiply by releasing agametes into their environment.
The study focuses on the development of agamete forms in simple multicellular organisms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gamete - a mature sexual reproductive cell (sperm or egg)

Etymology

From Greek a- (not) + gamete (wife), indicating absence of sexual union.
