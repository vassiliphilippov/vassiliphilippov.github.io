endless

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛndləs / (AmE), / ˈɛndləs / (BrE)
So many or so numerous as to seem to have no limit or end.
too numerous to have an end

Examples

She gazed at the endless rows of books in the library.
There seemed to be endless possibilities for their summer vacation.
We waited in an endless line at the amusement park.

Synset Examples

endless waves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countless - too many to be counted
  • Synonym: limitless - without any limits in amount or number
  • Antonym: finite - having limits; not endless

Etymology

From Middle English 'endeles', from 'end(e)' + '-less'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈɛndləs / (AmE), / ˈɛndləs / (BrE)
seeming to have no end; continuing for a very long time and often in a way that is tiresome or annoying
seeming to have no end; very long

Examples

After hours of endless meetings, everyone was exhausted and frustrated.
The desert seemed to stretch in an endless expanse of sand and heat.
Their endless arguments about politics made dinner uncomfortable for everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eternal - lasting or seeming to last forever; endless in duration
  • Antonym: brief - of short duration; not long-lasting

Etymology

having no known beginning or end; seeming to last forever
having no beginning or end

Examples
