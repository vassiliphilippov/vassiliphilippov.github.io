semisweet

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˈswit / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈswiːt / (BrE)
(of food, especially chocolate) having a taste that is somewhat sweet but not excessively so, often with a slight bitterness.
partly sweet; mildly sweet flavor

Examples

I prefer semisweet chocolate chips when baking cookies, as they are not too sugary.
The dessert had a semisweet flavor that balanced perfectly between bitter and sweet.
She chose a semisweet red wine to accompany the meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bittersweet - having a mixture of bitterness and sweetness
  • Degree Higher: sweet - having a sugary taste
  • Degree Lower: bitter - having a sharp, pungent taste; not sweet

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'semi-' (meaning 'half' or 'partly') and 'sweet'.
