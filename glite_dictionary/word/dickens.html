dickens

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪkɪnz / (AmE), / ˈdɪkɪnz / (BrE)
(used in exclamations) an expression of surprise, confusion, or annoyance, often as a mild euphemism for 'devil'.
expression of surprise or confusion
informal archaic

Examples

What the dickens is going on here?
Where the dickens did you find that hat?
He asked, 'Who the dickens are you?' in utter astonishment.

Synset Examples

what the devil
the deuce with it
the dickens you say

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devil - an exclamation used in surprise or anger (e.g. 'what the devil')
  • Synonym: deuce - a mild expression of annoyance, surprise, or horror

Etymology

Possibly a euphemism for 'devil', origin uncertain; recorded from the 16th century.
