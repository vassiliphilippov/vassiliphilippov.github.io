tuppeny

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʌpəni / (AmE), / ˈtʌpəni / (BrE)
Of very little value or importance; trivial or insignificant.
of little value or importance
british

Examples

His tuppeny opinions meant nothing to the board of directors.
It was just a tuppeny gift, but she cherished it regardless of its value.
Don't waste time on their tuppeny complaints—they aren't important.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worthless - having no value; completely without merit or use
  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance; insignificant

Etymology

From 'tuppence' (two pence), formed similarly to 'penny' to mean of the value of two pence; extended to mean insignificant.
