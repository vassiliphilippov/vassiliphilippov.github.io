Manichaeism

1. NOUN

/ ˌmænɪˈkiːɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌmanɪˈkiːɪzəm / (BrE)
an ancient religion founded by the prophet Mani in the third century AD, combining elements of Zoroastrianism, Babylonian folklore, Buddhism, and Christianity, and centering on the dualistic struggle between light and darkness
ancient dualistic religion founded by Mani

Examples

The spread of Manichaeism had a significant impact on religious thought in the Roman Empire.
Historians study Manichaeism to understand the blending of ancient religious traditions.
Few followers of Manichaeism remained after the movement largely disappeared by the 11th century.

Etymology

From Late Latin Manichaeismus, from Manichaeus (Mani, the founder) + -ism.
