semipublic

1. ADJ

/ sɛmiˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / sɛmiˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
partly but not fully open or accessible to the public; having some but not all qualities of a public institution or space
partly public in nature or access

Examples

The council meets in a semipublic session that members of the press can sometimes attend.
Their residence is situated in a semipublic courtyard, accessible only to a select group of neighbors.
He made the announcement at a semipublic event, rather than an open public forum.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: public - open or accessible to all; not private
  • Degree Lower: private - not open to or controlled by the public

Etymology

From semi- (meaning 'half, partly') + public.
