Centaurium scilloides

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtɔriəm sɪˈloʊlaɪdz / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɔːriəm sɪˈlɔɪliːdz / (BrE)
A tufted perennial plant native to western Europe and the Azores, noted for its bright pink to white flowers.
species of flowering plant

Examples

The wild meadows in the Azores are often dotted with Centaurium scilloides during spring.
Botanists have noted a decline in Centaurium scilloides populations due to habitat loss.
The small, star-shaped blooms of Centaurium scilloides attract various pollinators.

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'Centaurium', said to be from Centaur Chiron; species name from Latinized form of 'Scilla'.
