postbag

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstˌbæɡ / (BrE)
a large bag used by a postal worker to carry letters and parcels; especially in British English, known as a mailbag in American English.
postal worker's large bag for mail
british

Examples

The postman slung the postbag over his shoulder as he began his route.
She handed the heavy postbag to her colleague at the start of the shift.
Letters spilled out of the cracked leather postbag after years of loyal service.

Synset Examples

in Britain they call a mailbag a ‘postbag’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mailbag - a large bag used by postal workers to carry mail (American English equivalent)

Etymology

Formed from 'post' (meaning mail) and 'bag'.
