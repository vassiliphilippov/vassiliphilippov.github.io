WD-40

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbəlju di ˈfɔrti / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəljuː diː ˈfɔːti / (BrE)
a proprietary brand of penetrating oil and water-displacing spray, commonly used as a lubricant and rust remover
brand of oil and water-displacing spray

Examples

He used WD-40 to loosen the rusty bolt on the gate.
Whenever the hinges squeak, I just spray some WD-40 on them.
You should keep a can of WD-40 in your toolbox for general repairs.

Synset Examples

Just spray some WD-40 on that squeaky door.
I fixed the bike chain with a little WD-40.

Etymology

The name stands for 'Water Displacement, 40th formula.'
