heartleaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrtˌlif / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtˌliːf / (BrE)
a low-growing evergreen perennial plant with mottled green and silvery-grey heart-shaped leaves, native to eastern North America
low-growing plant with heart-shaped leaves

Examples

The heartleaf thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments.
Gardeners appreciate the distinctive foliage of the heartleaf for its decorative value.
She planted several heartleaf specimens along the garden path for ground cover.

Etymology

From ‘heart’ + ‘leaf’, referring to the shape of the leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrtˌlif / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtˌliːf / (BrE)
A wild ginger plant of North America with persistent, heart-shaped, pungent leaves, found from West Virginia to Alabama.
wild ginger plant with heart-shaped leaves

Examples

The forest floor was blanketed with heartleaf growing in the shadows of tall trees.
She identified the distinctive heartleaf by its persistent, pungent foliage.
Botanists discovered a rare species of heartleaf in northern Alabama.
