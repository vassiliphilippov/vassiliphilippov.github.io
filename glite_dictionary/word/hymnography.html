hymnography

1. NOUN

/ hɪmˈnɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / hɪmˈnɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the art or study of composing hymns, especially for use in religious worship
composition and study of hymns

Examples

She devoted her career to the study of hymnography in the Orthodox Christian tradition.
Courses on hymnography explore the development and literary structure of sacred songs.
The cathedral's archive includes rare manuscripts related to medieval hymnography.

Synset Examples

As windows into the Kingdom, iconography and hymnography reveal the glory and majesty of God.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hymnody - the singing or composing of hymns; the collective body of hymns

Etymology

From Greek hymnographia, from hymnos (hymn) + -graphia (writing).
