to seep

1. VERB

/ siːp / (AmE), / siːp / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) to pass slowly or leak through small openings or pores.
pass or leak slowly through small openings

Examples

Water began to seep through the cracks in the basement walls after the heavy rain.
The news of their engagement slowly seeped into the community over the weekend.
Oil can seep from old machinery and contaminate the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leak - (of a liquid or gas) to escape through a hole or crack
  • Synonym: to percolate - (of a liquid) to filter gradually through a porous surface

Etymology

From Middle English 'sepen', probably from Old Norse 'sīpa' (to soak, drip).
