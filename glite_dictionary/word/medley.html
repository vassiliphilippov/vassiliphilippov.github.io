medley

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdli / (AmE), / ˈmɛdli / (BrE)
A musical composition made up of a series of songs or other pieces combined from different sources.
musical composition of combined songs

Examples

The band's encore featured a medley of their greatest hits.
She arranged a medley of Broadway songs for the school concert.
The orchestra performed a medley combining classical and pop tunes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potpourri - a mixture or medley, especially of music or literature

Etymology

From Middle English medlee, from Anglo-French medlee (mixture), from Old French meslee, from mesler (to mix).
