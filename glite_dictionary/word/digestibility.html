digestibility

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌdʒɛstəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / dɪˌdʒɛstəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality of being easy to digest by the stomach or body.
ease with which something is digested

Examples

The digestibility of this type of protein is very high compared to others.
Doctors often recommend foods with greater digestibility for patients recovering from illness.
Cooking methods can significantly affect the digestibility of certain vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indigestibility - the quality of being difficult or impossible to digest

Etymology

From digest + -ibility, after Latin digerere (to separate, divide, digest) and -ibilis (able to be).
