tantaliser

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæn.tə.laɪ.zər / (AmE), / ˈtan.tə.laɪ.zə / (BrE)
a person who teases or torments by offering something desirable and then keeping it out of reach
one who teases by withholding something desirable

Examples

The cruel tantaliser waved the treat just out of the dog's reach.
She accused him of being a tantaliser after he showed her the surprise but wouldn't reveal it.
History remembers the myth of Tantalus, the greatest tantaliser of all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teaser - a person who makes fun of or provokes others in a playful way

Etymology

From the verb 'tantalize', deriving from the Greek mythological character Tantalus, who was punished by always being kept from what he desired.
