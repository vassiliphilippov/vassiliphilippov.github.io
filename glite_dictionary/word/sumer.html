Sumer

1. PROPN

/ ˈsuːmər / (AmE), / ˈsuːmə / (BrE)
an ancient region in southern Mesopotamia, in present-day Iraq, known as the site of the earliest Sumerian civilization and the development of city-states during the third millennium BC
ancient region in southern Mesopotamia

Examples

Many scholars consider Sumer to be the birthplace of writing and early urban civilization.
The ruins of ancient Sumer attract archaeologists from around the world keen to study early city life.
In the third millennium BC, Sumer was a collection of independent city-states such as Ur and Uruk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mesopotamia - ancient region between the Tigris and Euphrates rivers

Etymology

From Akkadian Šumeru, referring to the ancient region in southern Mesopotamia.
