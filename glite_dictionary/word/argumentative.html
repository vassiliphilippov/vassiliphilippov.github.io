argumentative

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːrɡjəˈmentətɪv / (AmE), / ˌɑːɡjəˈmentətɪv / (BrE)
tending to argue or likely to start arguments; given to expressing or causing disagreement or debate.
tending to argue; disputatious

Examples

His argumentative nature often led to heated discussions with his coworkers.
During the meeting, she became unusually argumentative about the proposed changes.
The teacher described him as bright but a little too argumentative in class.

Synset Examples

an argumentative discourse
argumentative to the point of being cantankerous
an intelligent but argumentative child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrelsome - habitually inclined to quarrel or argue
  • Antonym: peaceable - inclined to avoid argument or conflict

Etymology

From Medieval Latin argumentativus, from Latin argumentari (to argue).
