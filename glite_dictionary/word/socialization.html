socialization

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsəʊʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of placing industries or services under state ownership or control according to socialist principles
placing under socialist or state control

Examples

The government announced the socialization of electricity production as part of its economic reforms.
During the 20th century, several countries underwent the socialization of their healthcare systems.
The socialization of industries was a major policy objective of the new administration.

Synset Examples

the socialization of medical services

Etymology

From 'socialize' + '-ation'; 'socialize' from French 'socialiser', ultimately from Latin 'socius' meaning 'companion'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsəʊʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the activity of spending time with others for enjoyment and companionship
activity of social interaction

Examples

Weekly socialization events help new employees become more comfortable in the company.
The club organizes regular socialization opportunities for members to interact outside work.
Parents often worry about their children's socialization when homeschooling.

Synset Examples

there was too much socialization with the enlisted men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: socialising - taking part in friendly gatherings; spending time socially

Etymology

From social + -ization, based on Latin socialis (of companionship or society).

3. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsəʊʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which individuals learn and adopt the behaviors and norms of their society or group
the process of learning societal norms

Examples

Early socialization plays a key role in shaping a child's values and identity.
The socialization of new employees helps them adjust to the company culture.
Different societies have various methods of socialization for children and young adults.

Synset Examples

the socialization of children to the norms of their culture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acculturation - the process of adopting the cultural traits or social patterns of another group
  • Synonym: indoctrination - the process of teaching a person to accept a set of beliefs uncritically

Etymology

From French 'socialisation', from Latin 'socialis' (of companionship, social).
