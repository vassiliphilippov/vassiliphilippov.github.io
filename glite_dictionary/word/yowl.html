yowl

1. VERB

/ jaʊl / (AmE), / jaʊl / (BrE)
to make a loud, long, and mournful cry or wail, especially like a cat or dog
make a loud, mournful cry

Examples

The stray cat began to yowl outside my window in the middle of the night.
Coyotes sometimes yowl when they search for their pack.
The baby woke up and started to yowl until its mother comforted it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: howl - to make a long loud wailing cry

Etymology

Possibly imitative of the sound itself.

2. NOUN

/ jaʊl / (AmE), / jaʊl / (BrE)
a loud, wailing cry or utterance, especially one resembling the sound made by an animal
loud, wailing animal-like cry

Examples

The cat let out a piercing yowl when it saw the dog.
Suddenly, a yowl echoed through the dark alley, startling everyone.
Her yowl of pain could be heard throughout the neighborhood.

Synset Examples

his bellow filled the hallway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: howl - a long, loud, doleful cry uttered by an animal or person
  • Antonym: whisper - a soft or low utterance made with the breath, not the vocal cords

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, mimicking the sound itself.

3. VERB

/ jaʊl / (AmE), / jaʊl / (BrE)
(of an animal or person) to make a loud, long, mournful cry or wailing noise.
make a loud, wailing cry

Examples

The injured cat began to yowl in pain, waking everyone in the house.
The wind was so strong it seemed to yowl through the trees at night.
Coyotes often yowl when communicating with others in their pack.

Synset Examples

The coyotes were howling in the desert

Related Terms

  • Synonym: howl - make a long, loud, mournful cry (as a dog or wolf)
  • Degree Lower: whimper - make a series of low, feeble sounds expressing fear or pain
  • Synonym: wail - make a prolonged high-pitched cry of pain, grief, or anger

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound itself; akin to howl.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01049253-v
Synset POS: v
