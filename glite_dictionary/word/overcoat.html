overcoat

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɚˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌkəʊt / (BrE)
A final or additional layer of paint, varnish, or similar substance applied for protection or finish.
final protective layer (paint, varnish)

Examples

The furniture was finished with an overcoat of clear varnish for added durability.
For best results, let the primer dry before applying the final overcoat.
After several base layers, a glossy overcoat was brushed on to complete the look.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undercoat - a preparatory or base layer of paint or coating applied before the overcoat

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɚˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌkəʊt / (BrE)
A long, heavy coat worn over other clothes, typically for warmth in cold weather.
long heavy winter coat

Examples

He put on his overcoat before stepping outside into the cold.
She left her overcoat hanging by the door.
During winter, a thick overcoat is essential for staying warm.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: coat - an outer garment worn for warmth or fashion

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (above, on top of) and 'coat', meaning a garment worn over other clothing.
