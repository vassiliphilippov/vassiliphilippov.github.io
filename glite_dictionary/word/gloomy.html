gloomy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡluːmi / (AmE), / ˈɡluːmi / (BrE)
causing or likely to cause feelings of sadness or depression; depressing in appearance or atmosphere.
depressing; causing sadness

Examples

The gloomy weather made everyone feel a little down as they went about their day.
She looked out at the gloomy landscape, missing the warmth of summer.
After days of gloomy news, everyone was longing for something cheerful.

Synset Examples

a blue day
the dark days of the war
a week of rainy depressing weather
a disconsolate winter landscape
the first dismal dispiriting days of November
a dark gloomy day
grim rainy weather

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheerful - having a happy or positive mood; opposite of gloomy
  • Synonym: dreary - dull, bleak, and depressing; similar to gloomy

Etymology

From Middle English gloume (darkness, twilight) + -y.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡluːmi / (AmE), / ˈɡluːmi / (BrE)
feeling or showing sadness and a lack of hope; melancholic or despondent.
feeling sad or hopeless

Examples

After hearing the bad news, she felt gloomy all day.
The weather was so gloomy that it seemed to match his mood.
Despite the gloomy forecast, we decided to go to the park anyway.

Synset Examples

gloomy at the thought of what he had to face
gloomy predictions
a gloomy silence
took a grim view of the economy
the darkening mood
lonely and blue in a strange city
depressed by the loss of his job
a dispirited and resigned expression on her face
downcast after his defeat
feeling discouraged and downhearted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depressed - feeling very sad and without hope
  • Antonym: cheerful - happy and showing positive emotions

Etymology

From Middle English *gloume* (darkness) and *-y*, ultimately from Old English *glōm* (twilight).

3. ADJ

/ ˈɡluː.mi / (AmE), / ˈɡluː.mi / (BrE)
Dark or poorly lit, especially so as to appear depressing or frightening.
dark and depressing (of places)

Examples

We walked through the gloomy corridor, barely able to see ahead of us.
The gloomy attic was filled with old furniture and cobwebs.
On winter afternoons, the living room becomes quite gloomy without any lamps on.

Synset Examples

the gloomy forest
the glooming interior of an old inn
‘gloomful’ is archaic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bright - full of light; shining strongly

Etymology

From gloom + -y; 'gloom' from Middle English 'glom', possibly of Scandinavian origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00276630-s
Synset POS: s
