correctness

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛkt.nəs / (AmE), / kəˈrɛkt.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being in accordance with fact, truth, or accepted standards
the quality of being correct or accurate

Examples

Scientists must ensure the correctness of their data before publishing any results.
The correctness of his answer was never in doubt during the competition.
We questioned the correctness of the procedure used in the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accuracy - being correct or precise
  • Antonym: incorrectness - the state of being incorrect
  • Synonym: precision - exactness or accuracy

Etymology

From correct +‎ -ness; ultimately from Latin 'correctus' (set right).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛktnəs / (AmE), / kəˈrɛktnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of behaving or speaking in a way that conforms to accepted social conventions or expectations
conformity to social expectations

Examples

The teacher emphasized the importance of correctness in both language and manners.
Her correctness at the formal dinner was noticed and praised by everyone.
He valued social correctness and always followed proper etiquette.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propriety - the state of conforming to conventional standards of behavior or morals
  • Antonym: incorrectness - the state of not conforming to standards or expectations

Etymology

From correct + -ness, denoting the quality or state of being correct.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04906378-n
Synset POS: n
