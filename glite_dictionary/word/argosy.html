argosy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrɡəsi / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡəsi / (BrE)
a large merchant ship, especially one of a fleet; also, a fleet of such ships
large merchant ship or fleet
archaic

Examples

In the sixteenth century, an argosy laden with spices returned from the East.
Traders watched as the great argosies sailed into the harbor at dawn.
Shakespeare often used the word argosy to evoke the majesty of sea commerce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merchantman - a merchant ship
  • Synonym: fleet - a group of ships sailing together

Etymology

From Italian 'ragusea', a ship from Ragusa (now Dubrovnik).
