charily

1. ADV

/ ˈtʃɛrɪli / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛːrɪli / (BrE)
In a cautious or wary manner; with great care or prudence.
with caution or wariness

Examples

She approached the subject charily, afraid of stirring up old arguments.
Investors acted charily during the period of economic uncertainty.
He charily accepted the offer, aware of potential risks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warily - in a cautious or watchful manner
  • Antonym: recklessly - without care or caution

Etymology

From English 'chary' (careful, cautious) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix). 'Chary' from Old English 'cearu' (care, sorrow).
