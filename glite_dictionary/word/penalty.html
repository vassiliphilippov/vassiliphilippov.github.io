penalty

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnəlti / (AmE), / ˈpɛnəlti / (BrE)
a sum of money that must be paid as a punishment for not fulfilling a contract or agreement
payment for breaking a contract

Examples

If you cancel your subscription early, you may have to pay a penalty.
The company faced a heavy penalty for breaking its contract.
Failure to deliver the goods on time will result in a financial penalty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fine - a sum of money exacted as a penalty by a court of law or other authority

Etymology

From Middle French *penalite*, from Latin *poenalis* (punitive).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnəlti / (AmE), / ˈpɛnəlti / (BrE)
a disadvantage or painful consequence that results from an action or condition, especially as a result of breaking a rule or making a mistake
disadvantage or painful consequence

Examples

Neglecting your studies now will eventually bring its own penalty.
He suffered the penalty for arriving late to the important meeting.
Every choice we make can carry a hidden penalty if we are not careful.

Synset Examples

neglected his health and paid the penalty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punishment - a penalty imposed for doing something wrong, especially legally or morally

Etymology

From Old French pene (“pain, punishment”) and Medieval Latin 'penalitās', from Latin 'poena' (punishment, penalty).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnəlti / (AmE), / ˈpɛnəlti / (BrE)
the act of punishing or the punishment imposed for breaking a law, rule, or contract
punishment for breaking a rule or law

Examples

If you fail to return the books on time, you will have to pay a penalty.
Breaking the rules can result in a severe penalty.
The company faced a financial penalty for violating the contract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punishment - the act of penalizing someone for a wrong or crime
  • Antonym: reward - something given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement; the opposite of penalty

Etymology

From Medieval Latin poenalitās, from Latin poena (penalty, punishment).

4. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnəlti / (AmE), / ˈpɛnəlti / (BrE)
(in games and sports) a punishment or disadvantage imposed on a player or team for breaking the rules.
punishment in sports for rule violation

Examples

The referee imposed a penalty on the team for delay of game.
She received a two-minute penalty for tripping an opponent in the hockey match.
A handball in the box often results in a penalty against the defending team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foul - an act that breaks the rules in a sport or game
  • Antonym: reward - something given in recognition of achievement or good behavior in games or sports

Etymology

From late Latin *poenalitās*, from Latin *poena* (penalty, punishment).
