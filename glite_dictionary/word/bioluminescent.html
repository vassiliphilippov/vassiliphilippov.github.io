bioluminescent

1. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊ.luː.mɪˈnɛs.ənt / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.əʊ.ljuː.mɪˈnɛs.ənt / (BrE)
(of living organisms) able to produce and emit light through a natural chemical process.
emitting light (in living organisms)

Examples

Certain jellyfish are bioluminescent and glow beautifully in the dark depths of the ocean.
The bioluminescent properties of fireflies have inspired scientific research in biotechnology.
Visitors marveled at the bioluminescent algae sparkling along the shoreline at night.

Synset Examples

fireflies are bioluminescent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphorescent - emitting light without noticeable heat, especially not produced by biological organisms

Etymology

From bio- (life) + luminescent, from Latin lumen (light).
