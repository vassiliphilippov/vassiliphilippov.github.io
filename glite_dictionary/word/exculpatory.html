exculpatory

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈskʌlpəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪkˈskʌlpətəri / (BrE)
(of evidence or statements) showing or tending to show that someone is not guilty of wrongdoing.
tending to clear from blame or guilt
legal

Examples

The defense attorney argued that the emails were exculpatory and proved her client's innocence.
Newly discovered exculpatory evidence led to the overturning of the conviction.
The prosecutor is required to disclose all exculpatory information to the defense.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inculpatory - (of evidence) tending to incriminate or establish guilt

Etymology

From Latin exculpatus (freed from blame), from ex- (out) + culpa (fault, blame).
