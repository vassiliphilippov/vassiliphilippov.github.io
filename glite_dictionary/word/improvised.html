improvised

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪm.prəˌvaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈɪm.prə.vaɪzd / (BrE)
created or done using whatever materials are at hand, rather than being carefully planned or prepared in advance.
made using what's available

Examples

They set up an improvised shelter after the storm destroyed their house.
The chef made an improvised meal with just a few leftover ingredients.
An improvised stage was built for the outdoor concert.

Synset Examples

crossed the river on improvised bridges
the survivors used jury-rigged fishing gear
the rock served as a makeshift hammer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: makeshift - serving as a temporary substitute, using available materials
  • Synonym: jury-rigged - constructed or arranged in a makeshift way, especially with only the tools and materials at hand

Etymology

From the verb 'improvise', ultimately from French 'improviser', from Italian 'improvvisare', from Latin 'improvisus' (unforeseen).
