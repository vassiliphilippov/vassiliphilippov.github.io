Portuguese

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɔrtʃəˈgiz / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtʃʊˈgiːz / (BrE)
of or relating to Portugal, its people, or their language
of Portugal or its people/language

Examples

The restaurant serves authentic Portuguese cuisine.
She bought a bottle of fine Portuguese wine for the party.
We admired the beautiful Portuguese tiles in the old church.

Synset Examples

Portuguese wines

Etymology

From Portuguese 'português', from Latin 'Portus Cale', the name of an ancient settlement and port in what is now Portugal.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02970553-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɔːrtʃəˈɡiz / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtʃuˈɡiːz / (BrE)
the Romance language spoken in Portugal, Brazil, and other Portuguese-speaking regions
Romance language of Portugal, Brazil

Examples

She learned to speak Portuguese fluently after moving to Brazil.
The book has been translated from Portuguese into several languages.
Many people in Mozambique also speak Portuguese as an official language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish - the Romance language spoken in Spain and much of Latin America
  • Synonym: French - the Romance language spoken in France and other countries

Etymology

From Portuguese 'português', from 'Portugal' + '-ês' (denoting language or people).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06979090-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌpɔrtʃəˈɡiz / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtʃʊˈɡiːz / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Portugal.
person from Portugal

Examples

Many Portuguese celebrate traditional festivals that date back centuries.
When visiting Lisbon, you will meet many friendly Portuguese locals.
The museum featured art created by famous Portuguese from different eras.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Portuguese - the language spoken in Portugal and Brazil

Etymology

From French *portugais*, from Portuguese *português* 'of Portugal'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09747351-n
Synset POS: n
