Araucaria bidwillii

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrəˈkɛəriə bɪdˈwɪli / (AmE), / ˌærəˈkeəriə bɪdˈwɪli / (BrE)
A large Australian coniferous tree, the bunya-bunya, known for its edible seeds resembling roasted chestnuts.
Australian conifer with edible seeds

Examples

The Araucaria bidwillii is commonly known as the bunya-bunya tree in Australia.
Aboriginal Australians have long harvested seeds from the Araucaria bidwillii for food.
The Araucaria bidwillii produces large cones filled with seeds prized for their nutty flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunya-bunya - Common name for Araucaria bidwillii

Etymology

Genus name from Araucanía (a region of Chile); species named after botanist John Carne Bidwill.
