heavenward

1. ADV

/ ˈhɛvənˌwɚd / (AmE), / ˈhɛvənwəd / (BrE)
toward heaven or the sky; directed or moving upwards, especially in a spiritual or literal sense.
toward heaven or the sky

Examples

The rocket soared heavenward, leaving a trail of flame behind.
She cast her gaze heavenward and whispered a prayer.
In the painting, the angels appear to be rising heavenward.

Synset Examples

the soul in its heavenward flight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upward - toward a higher place or position

Etymology

Formed from 'heaven' + '-ward', a suffix meaning 'in the direction of'.

2. ADV

/ ˈhɛvənˌwɝd / (AmE), / ˈhɛvənwəd / (BrE)
toward heaven or the sky; upwards, especially in a spiritual or literal sense.
toward heaven or upward

Examples

The priest lifted his arms heavenward in prayer.
The smoke from the incense curled heavenward above the altar.
She gazed heavenward, lost in thought as the stars began to appear.

Synset Examples

he pointed heavenward

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upward - toward a higher place or position
  • Antonym: downward - toward a lower place or position

Etymology

Formed from 'heaven' + 'ward', a suffix meaning 'toward'.
