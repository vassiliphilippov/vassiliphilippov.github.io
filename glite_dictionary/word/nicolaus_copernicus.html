Nicolaus Copernicus

1. PROPN

/ nɪˌkoʊləs koʊˈpɜrnɪkəs / (AmE), / nɪˌkəʊləs kɒˈpɜːnɪkəs / (BrE)
(1473–1543) A Polish astronomer who developed the heliocentric model of the solar system, placing the sun at its center.
Polish astronomer, heliocentric model pioneer

Examples

Nicolaus Copernicus revolutionized astronomy by suggesting that the Earth revolves around the sun.
Many scientists today regard Nicolaus Copernicus as the father of modern astronomy.
The publication of Nicolaus Copernicus's work in 1543 marked a turning point in scientific thought.

Etymology

Latinized form of the original Polish name Mikołaj Kopernik.
