ameboid

1. ADJ

/ əˈmiː.bɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈmiː.bɔɪd / (BrE)
resembling an amoeba, especially in having a variable, irregular shape or movement.
resembling an amoeba in shape or movement

Examples

The cell exhibited an ameboid movement, changing its shape as it moved forward.
Certain white blood cells are known for their ameboid behavior, allowing them to engulf pathogens.
Under the microscope, the ameboid structure of the protist became clearly visible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amoeboid - variant spelling of ameboid, resembling an amoeba

Etymology

From 'amoeba' + '-oid' (resembling).
