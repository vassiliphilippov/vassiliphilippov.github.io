Cordyline terminalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔr.dəˈlaɪn ˌtɝː.məˈnæl.ɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɔː.dɪˈlaɪn ˌtɜː.mɪˈneɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
A tropical shrub with tufts of long leaves, used for thatching and clothing, and thick sweet roots that are eaten as food.
tropical shrub with edible roots and long leaves

Examples

The leaves of Cordyline terminalis are traditionally used for thatching roofs in Pacific islands.
In many regions, people cook the sweet roots of Cordyline terminalis for food.
Gardeners prize Cordyline terminalis as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Cordyline' from Greek 'kordyle' (club); 'terminalis' meaning 'at the end'.
