centrosymmetric

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛntroʊsɪˈmɛtrɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəʊsɪˈmɛtrɪk / (BrE)
Having a symmetrical arrangement of parts around a central point.
symmetrical about a central point

Examples

The crystal structure is centrosymmetric, making it easier to predict its properties.
Many molecules in nature display centrosymmetric arrangements.
The scientist noted the centrosymmetric pattern when examining the organism under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symmetrical - having similarity in size, shape, and position on opposite sides of a dividing line or plane
  • Antonym: asymmetrical - having two sides or halves that are not the same or identical

Etymology

From centro- (center) + symmetric, referring to symmetry about a center.
