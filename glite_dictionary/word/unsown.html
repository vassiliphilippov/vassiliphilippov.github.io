unsown

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsoʊn / (AmE), / ʌnˈsəʊn / (BrE)
(of land) not having seeds planted; not sown with crops or plants.
not having seeds planted

Examples

After the storm, large stretches of unsown fields stretched across the landscape.
The farmland remained unsown due to a shortage of seeds last spring.
They decided to leave one plot unsown to allow the soil to recover.

Synset Examples

farmland still unsown

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sown - (of land) planted with seeds or crops

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) and 'sown', past participle of 'sow'.
