plastron

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæstrɒn / (AmE), / ˈplæstrɒn / (BrE)
an ornamental cloth panel, often decorated, forming the front of a woman's bodice or shirt
ornamental front panel of a woman's bodice or shirt

Examples

The elegant gown featured a lace plastron that drew attention to her neckline.
Her blouse was adorned with a silk plastron fastened by a vintage brooch.
Fashion designers revived the decorative plastron in their latest collection.

Etymology

From French 'plastron', originally meaning a breastplate, from Italian 'piastrone' (large plate).

4. NOUN

/ ˈplæs.trɑn / (AmE), / ˈplɑːs.trɒn / (BrE)
(zoology) the part of a turtle's shell that forms its underside, protecting the lower part of the body.
underside of a turtle's shell

Examples

The turtle's plastron is yellowish and protects its belly from predators.
Inspecting the plastron can help identify the species of a tortoise.
Damage to the plastron may affect a turtle's ability to move safely.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carapace - the upper part of a turtle's shell

Etymology

From French *plastron* (breastplate), from Italian *piastrone*, from *piastra* (plate).

5. NOUN

/ ˈplæstrɑn / (AmE), / ˈplæstrɒn / (BrE)
A metal breastplate worn beneath a coat of mail, especially as part of historical armor.
metal breastplate worn under mail

Examples

The knight wore a plastron under his mail to protect himself in battle.
During the museum tour, the guide pointed out a medieval plastron used by soldiers.
Historians believe the plastron was essential for absorbing the force of sword strikes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breastplate - a piece of armor covering the chest
  • Synonym: cuirass - a piece of armor consisting of a breastplate and backplate

Etymology

From French 'plastron', from Italian 'piastrone', augmentative of 'piastra' (plate).
