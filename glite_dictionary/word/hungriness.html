hungriness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌŋɡrinəs / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡrɪnəs / (BrE)
the state or condition of needing or desiring food; hunger
state of needing food; hunger

Examples

After skipping breakfast, his hungriness distracted him from his work all morning.
The sight of the delicious meal only intensified her hungriness.
Children suffering from hungriness cannot concentrate well in class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunger - the physical sensation or need for food

Etymology

From 'hungry' + '-ness', forming a noun denoting a state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌŋɡri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡri.nəs / (BrE)
a state of being hungry or having a strong, unfulfilled desire or need
state of being hungry or needy

Examples

The hungriness in his eyes revealed his longing for success.
After skipping lunch, she felt an intense hungriness by mid-afternoon.
There was a hungriness in the team to prove themselves after last season's defeat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunger - a feeling of discomfort caused by lack of food; also figurative for longing or desire
  • Antonym: fullness - the state of being full, especially after eating

Etymology

Formed from 'hungry' + '-ness', following common English noun formation.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhʌŋɡri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡri.nəs / (BrE)
a strong and intense desire for something, not necessarily food or drink
intense desire or longing

Examples

After months of setbacks, her hungriness for success only grew stronger.
The hungriness for recognition drove him to work late into the night.
There was a visible hungriness in his eyes as he spoke about his dreams.

Synset Examples

a thirst for knowledge
hunger for affection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunger - strong desire or craving for something, especially food
  • Synonym: thirst - strong desire for something, especially a nonphysical craving
  • Antonym: satisfaction - fulfillment of one's wishes, expectations, or needs

Etymology

Formed from 'hungry' + '-ness', following the pattern of noun formation in English.
