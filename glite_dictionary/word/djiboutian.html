Djiboutian

1. ADJ

/ dʒɪˈbuːʃən / (AmE), / dʒɪˈbuːtiən / (BrE)
relating to Djibouti, its people, or its culture
of or relating to Djibouti or its people

Examples

The Djiboutian coastline is famous for its beautiful beaches and coral reefs.
Many Djiboutian traditions have roots in the region's diverse cultural history.
She enjoyed tasting authentic Djiboutian cuisine during her travels.

Synset Examples

Djiboutian landscape
Djiboutian merchants
a Djiboutian storyteller

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Djibouti - a country in East Africa on the Red Sea

Etymology

From Djibouti (name of the country) + English adjectival suffix -an.

2. NOUN

/ dʒɪˈbuːtiən / (AmE), / dʒɪˈbuːtiən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Djibouti
person from Djibouti

Examples

There are many Djiboutian communities living abroad.
The Djiboutian greeted us warmly when we arrived in the capital.
As a proud Djiboutian, she celebrates her country's independence every year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Djibouti - a country in East Africa

Etymology

From Djibouti, the country name, with the suffix -an forming demonyms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09719521-n
Synset POS: n
