juvenile

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒuːvəˌnaɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːvənaɪl / (BrE)
relating to, characteristic of, or suitable for children or young people.
of or relating to young people

Examples

The movie was criticized for its juvenile humor and simple storyline.
Many juvenile fashions are designed with bright colors and playful patterns.
She specializes in treating juvenile diabetes at the children's hospital.

Synset Examples

juvenile diabetes
juvenile fashions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adult - relating to mature or fully grown persons; opposite of juvenile

Etymology

From Latin juvenilis (youthful), from juvenis (young).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02904232-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːvəˌnaɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːvənaɪl / (BrE)
a young person who is not yet fully grown or developed, especially one below the age at which ordinary criminal prosecution is possible
young person not fully developed

Examples

The police questioned the juvenile about the incident in the park.
Libraries often have a special section for juveniles containing age-appropriate books.
As a juvenile, he was tried in family court rather than criminal court.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adult - a fully grown and developed person

Etymology

From Latin juvenilis, from juvenis (young).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09645219-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈdʒuːvəˌnaɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːvənaɪl / (BrE)
(of a person, action, or behavior) immature; displaying or suggesting a lack of maturity.
immature or childish

Examples

He lost respect for his colleague after hearing his juvenile remarks at the meeting.
She found his sense of humor too juvenile for her taste.
Their constant arguing seemed rather juvenile considering their age.

Synset Examples

adolescent insecurity
jejune responses to our problems
their behavior was juvenile
puerile jokes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: childish - like a child; immature
  • Antonym: mature - behaving in an adult, responsible way

Etymology

From Latin juvenilis, from juvenis (young).

4. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːvəˌnaɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːvənaɪl / (BrE)
a young person who is not yet fully developed or mature, especially one below the age at which they become legally responsible for their actions
a young person not fully grown or mature

Examples

The court treats juvenile offenders differently from adults.
Many juveniles enjoy spending time at the community center after school.
The new policy aims to reduce juvenile crime in urban areas.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adult - a fully grown or mature person
  • Synonym: minor - a person under the age of legal responsibility
  • Degree Lower: child - a young human being below the age of puberty

Etymology

From Latin juvenilis, from juvenis meaning 'young'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09645219-n
Synset POS: n
