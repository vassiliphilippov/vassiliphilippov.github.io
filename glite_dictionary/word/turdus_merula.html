Turdus merula

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.dəs məˈruː.lə / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.dəs mɛˈruː.lə / (BrE)
The scientific name for the common blackbird, a widespread European thrush with all-black plumage in males and brownish tones in females.
scientific name for the common blackbird

Examples

The song of the Turdus merula is a familiar sound in English gardens.
Researchers studied Turdus merula populations to understand urban bird adaptation.
I spotted a male Turdus merula perched on the garden fence this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common blackbird - a common European thrush with all-black plumage in males

Etymology

From Latin: turdus (thrush) + merula (blackbird).
