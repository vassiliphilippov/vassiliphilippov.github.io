Richmondena

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɪtʃ.mənˈdiː.nə / (AmE), / ˌrɪtʃ.mənˈdiː.nə / (BrE)
A genus of North American birds in the cardinal family, now usually included in the genus Cardinalis.
a genus of cardinals (birds)

Examples

The genus Richmondena was once used to classify the northern cardinal.
Recent studies have merged Richmondena into the genus Cardinalis.
Ornithologists debated the distinguishing features of birds in Richmondena.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cardinalis - the genus now commonly used for cardinals
  • Synonym: cardinal - a North American bird with a crested head and bright plumage

Etymology

Named after Richmond, Virginia, where the type specimen was found, with Neo-Latin -ena.
