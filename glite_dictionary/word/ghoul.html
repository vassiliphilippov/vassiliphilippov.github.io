ghoul

1. NOUN

/ ɡul / (AmE), / ɡuːl / (BrE)
an evil spirit or ghost, especially one associated with graveyards or consuming the dead
an evil spirit or ghost

Examples

Legends say a ghoul haunts the old cemetery at midnight.
The horror film depicted a monstrous ghoul crawling out of a grave to frighten the villagers.
As a child, she was terrified of the idea of a ghoul lurking under her bed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person appearing to the living
  • Synonym: demon - an evil supernatural being or spirit

Etymology

From Arabic *ghūl* (a demon or monster of graveyards).

2. NOUN

/ ɡuːl / (AmE), / ɡuːl / (BrE)
a person who exhumes bodies from graves, especially to sell them for anatomical dissection
person who steals corpses from graves

Examples

In the 19th century, a ghoul could earn a living by supplying medical schools with cadavers.
Rumors spread that a ghoul was stealing bodies from the churchyard at night.
The police caught the notorious ghoul as he was leaving a freshly disturbed grave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grave robber - a person who steals valuables or corpses from graves

Etymology

From Arabic *ghūl* (evil spirit; demon), later extended in English to grave robbers.
