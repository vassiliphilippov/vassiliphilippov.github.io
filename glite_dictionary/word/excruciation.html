excruciation

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌskruːʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌskruːʃɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
A state of intense or acute pain or suffering.
acute pain or suffering

Examples

The patient’s excruciation was evident from his pained expression and labored breathing.
She endured hours of excruciation before the medication finally took effect.
His memoir describes the emotional excruciation he felt after the loss of his partner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agony - extreme physical or mental suffering
  • Synonym: torment - severe physical or mental suffering

Etymology

From Latin 'excruciatus', past participle of 'excruciare' (to torture, to torment), from 'crux' (cross).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˌskruːʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌskruːʃiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the infliction of extremely intense pain or suffering; severe torture or agony
the infliction of extreme pain or suffering

Examples

The excruciation of the victim was evident in his anguished cries.
Medieval punishments often involved the deliberate excruciation of prisoners.
She described the chronic pain as a kind of daily excruciation she could barely endure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torture - the action of inflicting severe pain as punishment or to force someone to do or say something
  • Synonym: agony - extreme physical or mental suffering

Etymology

From Latin excruciatus, past participle of excruciare (to torment, to torture).
