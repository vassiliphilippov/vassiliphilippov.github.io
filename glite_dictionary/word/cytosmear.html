cytosmear

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪtoʊˌsmɪr / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪtəʊˌsmɪə / (BrE)
a thin layer of tissue or blood spread on a glass slide and stained for cytological examination under a microscope
thin specimen layer for cytologic exam
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the cytosmear to look for any abnormal cells.
A cytosmear is commonly used in screening for cervical cancer.
After preparing the cytosmear, the technician stained it to highlight cellular features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smear - a sample spread on a slide for microscopic examination

Etymology

From 'cyto-' (relating to cells) + 'smear'.
