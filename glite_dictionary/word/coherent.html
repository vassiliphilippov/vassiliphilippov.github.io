coherent

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈhɪrənt / (AmE), / kəʊˈhɪərənt / (BrE)
(physics) Of waves: having a constant phase relationship with each other.
(physics) waves with constant phase relation

Examples

The laser produces coherent light, which is essential for the interference experiment.
Radio telescopes use signals that are coherent to improve image clarity and detail.
When the waves are coherent, they can produce a stable interference pattern.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incoherent - (physics) waves not having a constant phase relation

Etymology

From Latin 'cohaerent-', present participle of 'cohaerēre' (to stick together).
