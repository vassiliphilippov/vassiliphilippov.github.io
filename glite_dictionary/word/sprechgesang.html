sprechgesang

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃprɛkxgəˌzɑŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃprɛkxgəˌzaŋ / (BrE)
a style of dramatic vocalization in music that lies between singing and speaking, often used in opera or modern music
musical vocalization between singing and speaking

Examples

The composer used sprechgesang to create a haunting atmosphere in the performance.
Her mastery of sprechgesang impressed both critics and audiences at the modern opera.
Many avant-garde works feature passages of sprechgesang for dramatic effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprechstimme - a style of vocalization between speaking and singing, but closer to speaking

Etymology

From German Sprechgesang, from sprechen ('to speak') + Gesang ('song, singing').
