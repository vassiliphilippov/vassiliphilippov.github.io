communism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mjuˌnɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.juːˌnɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A political and economic system in which all property is owned collectively, and each person works and is paid according to their abilities and needs, aiming for a classless society.
political system of collective ownership

Examples

Many countries in the 20th century experimented with communism as an alternative to capitalism.
The ideals of communism include the abolition of private property and a classless society.
He studied the history of communism in Eastern Europe for his thesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: socialism - a political and economic system advocating collective or government ownership of the means of production
  • Antonym: capitalism - an economic system based on private ownership and free markets

Etymology

From French communisme, from commun (common) + -isme (-ism), influenced by Latin communis (common, shared).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mjəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkɒ.mjʊˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
A political and economic system in which all property is publicly owned and each person works and is paid according to their abilities and needs, typically seeking the abolition of private ownership.
system of collective ownership and abolition of private property

Examples

The rise of communism in Eastern Europe had a major impact on global politics.
He spent years studying the theories and practice of communism.
Critics of communism argue that it limits individual freedom and economic growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: socialism - a political and economic theory advocating collective or governmental ownership and administration of production and distribution
  • Antonym: capitalism - an economic system based on private ownership and free market competition

Etymology

From French communisme, from commun (common) + -isme (-ism), influenced by Latin communis (common, shared).
