to embiggen

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈbɪɡən / (AmE), / ɛmˈbɪɡən / (BrE)
to make or become bigger, especially in an informal or humorous context; to enlarge or increase in amount, size, or scope
make or become bigger (informal)
informal slang

Examples

The new project aims to embiggen our influence in the community.
Leaders discussed ways to embiggen the organization’s reach.
Social media campaigns can embiggen your chances of being noticed.

Synset Examples

The new policy will embiggen our chances for success.
We need to embiggen our outreach efforts for better results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enlarge - make something bigger
  • Synonym: to expand - increase in size or scope
  • Antonym: shrink - make smaller

Etymology

Coined in 1996 for The Simpsons TV show, possibly by blending 'enlarge' and 'big'.
