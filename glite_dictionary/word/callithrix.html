Callithrix

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪθ.rɪks / (AmE), / kəˈlɪθ.rɪks / (BrE)
The genus of small New World monkeys known as true marmosets, native to South America.
genus of true marmosets (monkeys)

Examples

The Callithrix genus includes several species of small, agile marmosets.
Researchers observed the social behavior of Callithrix in their natural rainforest habitat.
Members of the Callithrix genus are known for their claw-like nails and rapid movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marmoset - a small New World monkey of the family Callithricidae

Etymology

From Greek kallos (beauty) + thrix (hair), referring to the marmoset's appearance.
