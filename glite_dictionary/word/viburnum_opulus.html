Viburnum opulus

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈbɜrnəm ˈɑpjələs / (AmE), / vɪˈbɜːnəm ˈɒpjʊləs / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to the Old World, bearing clusters of white flowers and bright red berries, commonly known as guelder rose.
a shrub with white flowers and red berries

Examples

The Viburnum opulus in the garden blooms beautifully each spring.
Wildlife are often attracted to the bright red berries of Viburnum opulus.
Gardeners plant Viburnum opulus for its ornamental flowers and autumn color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guelder rose - another common name for Viburnum opulus

Etymology

From modern Latin; 'Viburnum' is the genus name, 'opulus' from Latin meaning 'maple' (referring to leaf resemblance).
