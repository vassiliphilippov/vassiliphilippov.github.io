Chloris gayana

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊrɪs gaɪˈænə / (AmE), / ˈklɔːrɪs ɡaɪˈænə / (BrE)
a perennial grass native to South Africa, widely cultivated as forage grass in dry regions
perennial forage grass native to South Africa

Examples

Chloris gayana is often planted by farmers in arid regions to provide pasture for livestock.
As a drought-tolerant species, Chloris gayana can thrive where other grasses fail.
Researchers study Chloris gayana for its potential to improve forage yields in semi-arid climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhodes grass - common name for Chloris gayana

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Chloris' and species epithet 'gayana', honoring French botanist Claude Gay.
