waggoner

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæɡənər / (AmE), / ˈwɒɡənə / (BrE)
a person who drives or manages a wagon, especially as an occupation
driver of a wagon

Examples

The waggoner guided his team of horses steadily down the dusty road.
In the old days, a skilled waggoner was essential for transporting goods across the countryside.
The waggoner stopped to rest and feed the horses at noon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teamster - a person who drives a team of draft animals or a truck

Etymology

From Middle English 'waggonere', from 'wagon' + '-er'.
