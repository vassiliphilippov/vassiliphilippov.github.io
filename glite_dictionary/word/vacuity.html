vacuity

1. NOUN

/ vəˈkjuːəti / (AmE), / vəˈkjuːɪti / (BrE)
the state of being empty; absence of matter or substance
emptiness; absence of substance

Examples

After the explosion, only a sense of vacuity remained in the vast chamber.
His comments were marked by a profound vacuity that left listeners unimpressed.
Scientists tried to measure the vacuity inside the sealed container.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: void - complete emptiness; containing no matter

Etymology

From Latin vacuitas (emptiness), from vacuus (empty).

2. NOUN

/ vəˈkjuː.ə.ti / (AmE), / vəˈkjuː.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
A space or area that is completely empty of matter; emptiness or void.
emptiness; void

Examples

The vacuity of outer space makes it impossible for sound to travel there.
Scientists studied the vacuity left behind after the experiment.
His eyes seemed to reflect a complete vacuity of emotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: void - an empty space; a vacuum
  • Synonym: emptiness - the state of containing nothing
  • Antonym: fullness - the state of being full

Etymology

From Latin 'vacuitas' (emptiness), from 'vacuus' (empty).

3. NOUN

/ vəˈkjuːɪti / (AmE), / vəˈkjuːɪti / (BrE)
the state of being empty of meaning, ideas, or content; emptiness or lack of substance
emptiness of meaning or ideas

Examples

Many critics have commented on the vacuity of the politician's speech.
She was frustrated by the vacuity of popular television programs.
His eyes betrayed a certain vacuity as he listened without understanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emptiness - the state of containing nothing
  • Antonym: depth - profoundness, richness in ideas or meaning (opposite)

Etymology

From Latin 'vacuus' (empty) via Late Latin 'vacuītas' (emptiness).
