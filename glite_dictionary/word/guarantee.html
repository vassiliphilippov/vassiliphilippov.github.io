guarantee

1. NOUN

/ ˌgærənˈti / (AmE), / ˌɡærənˈtiː / (BrE)
a written promise that a product or service will meet certain standards or that compensation will be provided if it does not
written assurance for a product or service

Examples

The refrigerator comes with a one-year guarantee against mechanical defects.
She requested a written guarantee before making the purchase.
Always check if the product has a guarantee for quality and performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warranty - a written guarantee, often for repairs or replacement

Etymology

From Old French garantie, from garantir (to protect), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06698201-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˌɡærənˈti / (AmE), / ˌɡærənˈtiː / (BrE)
to formally assure the quality, accuracy, or condition of something, promising repair or replacement if necessary
formally assure quality or condition

Examples

We guarantee all our products against defects for two years.
The company cannot guarantee the accuracy of the information provided.
He guaranteed that if the item broke, they would replace it for free.

Synset Examples

The dealer warrants all the cars he sells
I warrant this information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warrant - to provide a formal assurance that something is true or of good quality
  • Synonym: to certify - to formally declare something is true or meets a standard

Etymology

From Old French 'garantir', from 'garant' (protector, warrantor), ultimately of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00893457-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˌɡɛrənˈti / (AmE), / ˌɡærənˈtiː / (BrE)
a formal promise or collateral agreement to fulfill an obligation or pay a debt if the original debtor fails to do so
collateral promise to cover someone else's debt
legal

Examples

The bank required a guarantee from a third party before approving the loan.
If the borrower defaults, the guarantee ensures the lender will be repaid.
He acted as a guarantee for his friend's business loan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warranty - a written assurance that a product or service will meet certain specifications
  • Synonym: security - collateral pledged to guarantee repayment of a loan

Etymology

From Old French garantie (protection), from garantir (to protect), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13374019-n
Synset POS: n

Etymology
