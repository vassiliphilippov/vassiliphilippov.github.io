to undrape

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈdreɪp / (AmE), / ʌnˈdreɪp / (BrE)
to remove drapery or coverings from something
remove drapery or coverings

Examples

The stagehands quickly undraped the window to let more light in.
She undraped the statue, revealing it to the eager audience.
He carefully undraped the old portrait, exposing it for restoration.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: drape - to cover or decorate with cloth or drapery
  • Synonym: to uncover - to remove a covering from something

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'drape', meaning to remove what has been draped.
