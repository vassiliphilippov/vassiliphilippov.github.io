gamification

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡeɪ.mə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡeɪ.mɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the application of game-design elements and principles in non-game contexts to increase engagement and motivation
adding game elements to increase engagement

Examples

Many companies use gamification to motivate employees and make work more enjoyable.
The app relies on gamification strategies to encourage daily exercise.
Educators have found that gamification can help students stay focused and enthusiastic about learning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gamify - to apply game principles to a process or activity

Etymology

From English 'game' + '-ification', patterned after other words like 'gamify', first used in the early 21st century.
