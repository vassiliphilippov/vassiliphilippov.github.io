leviathan

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈvaɪəθən / (AmE), / lɪˈvaɪəθən / (BrE)
something that is the largest or most massive of its kind; a giant or colossal entity.
something extremely large or massive

Examples

The new cruise ship is a leviathan in the world of ocean liners.
Their company became a leviathan in the tech industry, dwarfing its competitors.
The mountain loomed before us, a real leviathan of rock and snow.

Synset Examples

it was a leviathan among redwoods
they were assigned the leviathan of textbooks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - an extremely large person, animal, or thing
  • Synonym: colossus - a person or thing of enormous size, importance, or ability

Etymology

From Late Latin leviathan, from Hebrew livyāthān (sea monster, serpent).

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈvaɪəθən / (AmE), / lɪˈvaɪəθən / (BrE)
A large, monstrous sea creature mentioned in the Old Testament, often used symbolically to represent evil or great power.
monstrous sea creature from the Bible

Examples

Scholars have long debated the true nature of the leviathan described in ancient texts.
The sailors feared the appearance of a leviathan beneath the stormy waves.
In literature, a leviathan often symbolizes uncontrollable power or menace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monster - a large, frightening imaginary creature

Etymology

From Hebrew *liwyāṯān* (twisted, coiled), used in the Old Testament.

very large or powerful entity or organization

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colossus - A person or thing of enormous size, importance, or ability
