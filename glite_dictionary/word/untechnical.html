untechnical

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtɛknɪkəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɛknɪkəl / (BrE)
not involving, requiring, or showing special knowledge, skill, or language of a particular field, especially in the applied sciences or arts.
not technical; lacking technical detail

Examples

The guide is written in untechnical language, making it accessible to a general audience.
Although she is an expert, she prefers to explain things in an untechnical way.
His review was surprisingly untechnical, focusing more on user experience than engineering details.

Synset Examples

nontechnical aspects of the job
nontechnical training
an untechnical reader
in clear effective nontechnical language

Related Terms

  • Antonym: technical - relating to a particular subject, art, or craft, or its techniques
  • Synonym: nontechnical - not involving technical details or knowledge

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'technical'.
