eldritch

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛl.drɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈɛl.drɪtʃ / (BrE)
strange or unnatural, often in a way that is frightening or suggests supernatural influences
weird or eerie, suggesting the supernatural

Examples

An eldritch mist crept silently through the abandoned graveyard at midnight.
The house was filled with an eldritch glow that made everyone uneasy.
She heard an eldritch cry in the distance that chilled her to the bone.

Synset Examples

an eldritch screech
the three weird sisters
stumps … had uncanny shapes as of monstrous creatures
an unearthly light
he could hear the unearthly scream of some curlew piercing the din

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weird - strange or mysterious, especially in an unsettling way
  • Synonym: uncanny - strange or mysterious, especially in a way that is unsettling
  • Antonym: ordinary - with no special or supernatural qualities; normal

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *eldritch*, of uncertain origin, possibly related to 'elf' + 'riche' (realm, kingdom).
