fleshy

1. ADJ

/ ˈfleʃi / (AmE), / ˈfleʃi / (BrE)
having a large amount of flesh; plump or thick in body or in a part of the body, especially in a way suggesting ample or soft tissue.
having much flesh; plump or thick

Examples

She had fleshy arms that shook slightly as she waved goodbye.
The fleshy part of his cheeks turned red with embarrassment.
Despite his fleshy build, he moved with surprising agility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fat - having a large amount of excess flesh or body fat
  • Antonym: skinny - very thin, lacking flesh
  • Synonym: plump - slightly fat in a pleasant or attractive way

Etymology

From Middle English fleschi, from Old English flǣscig (full of flesh).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfleʃi / (AmE), / ˈfleʃi / (BrE)
having much flesh; of, relating to, or resembling flesh, especially in being soft or thick.
having much flesh; like flesh

Examples

The cactus stores water in its fleshy stems.
He has fleshy cheeks that turn red when he laughs.
The fruit has a thick, fleshy interior under its tough skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscular - having well-developed muscles
  • Antonym: lean - having little flesh or fat

Etymology

From Old English 'flǣsclic' (flesh-like), from 'flǣsc' (flesh).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
