sideshow

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌʃəʊ / (BrE)
an event or activity that is incidental or of little importance compared to the main event
minor or subordinate event

Examples

Compared to the budget discussion, the argument over coffee supplies was just a sideshow.
Many viewed the scandal as a political sideshow distracting from real issues.
The media focused on a sideshow rather than the primary goals of the summit.

Synset Examples

instruction is not an educational sideshow

Related Terms

  • Antonym: main event - the central or most important occurrence or activity

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdʃəʊ / (BrE)
a small show or entertainment that is part of a larger event, especially at a fair or circus
minor show at a larger event

Examples

The sideshow featured acrobats and clowns performing in a tent beside the main circus arena.
At the festival, there were several sideshows that attracted large crowds throughout the day.
While the main attraction was the concert, the magic act in the sideshow drew plenty of curious visitors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: main event - the principal attraction or activity at an event
