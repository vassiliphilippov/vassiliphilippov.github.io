orchestra

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrkəstrə / (AmE), / ˈɔːkɪstrə / (BrE)
the seats on the main floor of a theater, usually closest to the stage
main floor seating in a theater

Examples

We bought tickets in the orchestra for a better view of the musical.
Seats in the orchestra tend to be more expensive than those in the balcony.
The orchestra filled up quickly as theatergoers arrived early for the show.

Etymology

From Greek *orkhēstra* (the space in front of a stage for chorus in ancient theaters).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔrkəstrə / (AmE), / ˈɔːkɪstrə / (BrE)
A large group of musicians who play various instruments together, typically including string, woodwind, brass, and percussion sections.
large group of musicians playing together

Examples

The orchestra tuned their instruments before the concert started.
She has played violin in the city orchestra for ten years.
A world-famous conductor led the orchestra through a complex symphony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: band - a group of musicians who play music together, typically without strings

Etymology

From Italian 'orchestra', from Greek 'orkhēstra' (the area in front of a stage for chorus and dancers).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
