introductory

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.trəˈdʌk.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈdʌk.tə.ri / (BrE)
serving as an introduction or to begin something; preliminary.
serving as an introduction

Examples

She took an introductory course to learn the basics of French.
The guide gave a brief introductory speech before the tour began.
This book serves as an introductory text for beginners in the field.

Synset Examples

began the slide show with some introductory remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preliminary - coming before the main part as an introduction
  • Antonym: concluding - ending or bringing something to a close

Etymology

From Latin 'introductorius', from 'introduct-' (brought in), from 'intro' (inward) + 'ducere' (to lead).

3. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.trəˈdʌk.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈdʌk.təri / (BrE)
serving as an introduction or providing a beginning section to a subject, book, or event
serving as an introduction

Examples

The professor gave an introductory lecture on the history of art.
New employees must complete an introductory training program before starting work.
Her introductory remarks set the tone for the entire conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preliminary - preceding or preparing for something more important
  • Antonym: concluding - serving to bring something to an end or close

Etymology

From Latin 'introductorius', from 'introductus' (introduced) + '-ory'.

Synset POS: s
