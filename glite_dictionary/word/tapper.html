tapper

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæpər / (AmE), / ˈtæpə / (BrE)
a worker whose job is to use a tap to cut screw threads inside holes in metal or other materials
worker who cuts screw threads with a tap

Examples

The experienced tapper ensured each hole had precise threads for the assembly.
As a tapper, he worked with various tools to shape metal components in the workshop.
Every tapper must pay close attention to detail to achieve a flawless finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: machinist - a person skilled in operating machine tools

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæpər / (AmE), / ˈtæpə / (BrE)
a dancer who creates rhythms by striking the floor with metal taps attached to their shoes
a dancer who performs tap dancing

Examples

The talented tapper captivated the audience with her energetic footwork.
He started training as a tapper at the age of seven.
The show features a famous tapper from New York's Broadway scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tap dancer - a person who dances in the tap dance style

Etymology

From 'tap' + agent suffix '-er', indicating one who taps (as in tap dancing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10710644-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtæpər / (AmE), / ˈtæpə / (BrE)
a person who draws liquor from kegs or casks, especially a tavern keeper who serves drinks
tavern keeper who taps kegs

Examples

The local tapper was known for his generous pours and friendly stories.
In old English villages, the tapper played a central role in the community's nightly gatherings.
He worked as a tapper, drawing ale from the casks to serve thirsty patrons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bartender - a person who serves drinks at a bar
  • Synonym: innkeeper - a person who manages or owns an inn

Etymology

From 'tap' (to draw liquid) + '-er' (agent suffix); Middle English roots related to drawing drink from casks.

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈtæpər / (AmE), / ˈtæpə / (BrE)
a person who repeatedly strikes a surface lightly, often with their fingers or an object
person who taps a surface repeatedly

Examples

The constant noise from the tapper at the next desk was a source of distraction.
She noticed a tapper in the audience, nervously drumming his fingers on his chair.
Teachers often ask habitual tappers to stop during exams.

Related Terms

From 'tap' (to strike lightly) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

5. NOUN

/ ˈtæpər / (AmE), / ˈtæpə / (BrE)
a person who intercepts telephone or telegraph communications by secretly attaching a device to the line
person who wiretaps phone or telegraph lines

Examples

The police arrested the tapper after discovering unauthorized devices on the telephone line.
During the investigation, it was revealed that a tapper had recorded the politician's private conversations.
Security experts warn companies to check for a tapper if they suspect corporate espionage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wiretapper - a person who intercepts communications by attaching a listening device to a wire

Etymology

From 'tap' (to attach a device for listening secretly) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
