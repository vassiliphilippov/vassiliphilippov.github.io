sceptred

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛptɚd / (AmE), / ˈsɛptəd / (BrE)
Invested with royal or official authority, especially as symbolized by the possession of a scepter.
holding royal or official authority

Examples

The sceptred monarch addressed her subjects from the grand balcony.
In Shakespeare, England is often described as this sceptred isle.
He dreamt of ruling, sceptred and revered by his people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sovereign - possessing supreme power or authority
  • Antonym: powerless - lacking power or authority

Etymology

From 'sceptre' (royal staff) + -ed (forming adjectives).
