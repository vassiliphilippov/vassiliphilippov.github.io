Podiceps cristatus

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈdaɪsɛps krɪsˈtɑːtəs / (AmE), / pəʊˈdɪsɛps krɪsˈtɑːtəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the great crested grebe, a large water bird of the Old World with distinctive black ear tufts
scientific name for the great crested grebe

Examples

The Podiceps cristatus breeds on freshwater lakes throughout Europe and Asia.
Birdwatchers were delighted to spot a pair of Podiceps cristatus displaying their elaborate courtship ritual.
The black ear tufts are a distinctive feature of the Podiceps cristatus during the breeding season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great crested grebe - large Old World water bird with a distinctive black double crest

Etymology

From Latin: 'podiceps' meaning 'rump-footed' (referring to the position of the legs), and 'cristatus' meaning 'crested'.
