hypethral

1. ADJ

/ haɪˈpiːθrəl / (AmE), / hɪˈpiːθrəl / (BrE)
(of a building or structure) having no roof, or an area that is partly or entirely open to the sky, especially as seen in classical architecture.
open to the sky (esp. in architecture)

Examples

The ancient temple's hypethral courtyard allowed sunlight to pour directly onto the altar.
Many classical sanctuaries were designed as hypethral spaces to create a connection with the sky.
The hypethral layout of the monument distinguishes it from fully roofed buildings of the period.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: covered - having a roof or overhead covering

Etymology

From Greek hupo- (under) + aithēr (air, sky), meaning 'under the open air.'
