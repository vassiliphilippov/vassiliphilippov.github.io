triamcinolone

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌæmˈsɪnəˌloʊn / (AmE), / traɪˌamˈsɪnələʊn / (BrE)
a synthetic corticosteroid drug used to reduce inflammation and treat various medical conditions such as allergies, skin disorders, and arthritis
synthetic corticosteroid drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed triamcinolone to help manage her severe asthma symptoms.
After applying triamcinolone cream, the patient's rash began to subside.
Triamcinolone injections are sometimes used to treat joint inflammation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corticosteroid - a class of steroid hormones used to reduce inflammation

Etymology

From 'tri-' (three) and 'amcinolone', a steroid nucleus; developed as a modified corticosteroid.
