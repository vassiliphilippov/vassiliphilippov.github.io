stilboestrol

1. NOUN

/ stɪlˈboʊɪsˌtrɒl / (AmE), / stɪlˈbəʊɪstrɒl / (BrE)
a synthetic, potent estrogen used medically and formerly in livestock and poultry feed
synthetic estrogen used in medicine
medical

Examples

The use of stilboestrol in animal feed was banned in many countries due to health concerns.
Doctors once prescribed stilboestrol to prevent miscarriages, but its use has been discontinued.
Researchers studied the effects of stilboestrol exposure on reproductive health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diethylstilbestrol - a specific form of stilboestrol, used as an estrogen

Etymology

From chemical name 'stilbene' + 'oestrol', denoting its structure as a synthetic estrogen.
