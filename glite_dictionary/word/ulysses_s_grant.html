Ulysses S. Grant

1. PROPN

/ juːˈlɪsiːz ɛs ˈɡrænt / (AmE), / juːˈlɪsiːz ɛs ˈɡrɑːnt / (BrE)
(1822–1885) The 18th President of the United States and the commanding general of the Union armies during the American Civil War.
18th U.S. president; Union Civil War general

Examples

Ulysses S. Grant led the Union Army to victory in the American Civil War.
The presidency of Ulysses S. Grant was marked by efforts to stabilize the postwar nation.
Many historians study the leadership style of Ulysses S. Grant.
