instillation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.stəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of introducing a liquid drop by drop, especially into a body part or cavity
drop-by-drop introduction of liquid

Examples

The instillation of eye drops should be done carefully to avoid contamination.
Regular instillation of medication was necessary to treat the patient's ear infection.
The nurse explained the instillation procedure before beginning the treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injection - the act of forcing liquid into something, usually with a syringe

Etymology

From Latin 'instillatio', from 'instillare' (to drip in).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.stəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A liquid substance that is administered drop by drop, especially for medical purposes.
liquid given drop by drop
medical

Examples

The nurse administered an instillation of antibiotic drops into the patient’s ear.
An instillation of saline solution can help clear the sinuses.
Doctors recommend using only a prescribed instillation for eye infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to instill - to introduce a substance drop by drop

Etymology

From Latin instillatio, from instillare (to drip into).
