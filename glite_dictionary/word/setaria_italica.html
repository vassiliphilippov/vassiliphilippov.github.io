Setaria italica

1. NOUN

/ səˈtɛəriə ɪˈtælɪkə / (AmE), / sɛˈtɑːrɪə ɪˈtælɪkə / (BrE)
A coarse, drought-resistant annual grass cultivated for its grain, hay, and forage, known as foxtail millet, grown mainly in Europe and Asia.
species of millet (foxtail millet)

Examples

Setaria italica is widely cultivated in Asia for its grain, known as foxtail millet.
Farmers prefer Setaria italica because it tolerates drought and poor soil conditions.
The hay produced from Setaria italica is used to feed livestock during the dry season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxtail millet - common name for Setaria italica, a species of millet

Etymology

From Latin: Setaria (genus name, from 'seta' meaning bristle) and italica ('Italian').
