to formularise

1. VERB

/ ˈfɔːrmjələˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmjʊləˌraɪz / (BrE)
to express or state something in the form of a formula or according to a fixed set of rules
to express as a formula
british

Examples

Scientists often formularise complex phenomena for easier analysis and prediction.
The committee sought to formularise the new procedures into a standardized document.
It can be challenging to formularise the emotional aspects of leadership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to formulate - to create, invent, or devise something methodically or systematically
  • Synonym: to formularize - to express as or reduce to a formula; American spelling of 'formularise'

Etymology

From 'formula' + '-ise', modelled after 'formulate'. Chiefly British spelling of 'formularize'.
