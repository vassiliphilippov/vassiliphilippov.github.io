haemangioma

1. NOUN

/ hiːˌmændʒiˈoʊmə / (AmE), / hiːˌmandʒiˈəʊmə / (BrE)
A benign tumor made up of a mass of blood vessels, often appearing as a birthmark.
benign tumor of blood vessels
medical british

Examples

The doctor explained that the red mark on the baby's skin was a haemangioma and would likely fade with age.
Surgical removal of a haemangioma is considered only if it causes complications or disfigurement.
Many parents worry when their child develops a haemangioma, but most are harmless and disappear over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemangioma - benign tumor of blood vessels (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'angeion' (vessel) + '-oma' (tumor).
