admirer

1. NOUN

/ ədˈmaɪɚ / (AmE), / ədˈmaɪərə / (BrE)
a person who admires someone or something, often showing respect, approval, or affection
someone who admires another

Examples

He received a letter from an anonymous admirer who praised his recent performance.
The actress has many devoted admirers around the world.
She blushed when she realized her secret admirer had sent her flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fan - a person who has a strong interest in or admiration for someone or something
  • Antonym: critic - a person who judges or finds fault; someone who is often negative

Etymology

From admire + -er, dating to the late 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ədˈmaɪɚ / (AmE), / ədˈmaɪərə / (BrE)
a person who is attracted to someone or expresses affection, respect, or romantic interest for them
a person expressing attraction or respect

Examples

He became her most devoted admirer after seeing her perform on stage.
She received flowers from an anonymous admirer every Valentine’s Day.
Many admirers gathered outside the theater hoping to catch a glimpse of the actress.

Synset Examples

she had many admirers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fan - a person who has a strong interest in or admiration for a particular person or thing
  • Antonym: critic - a person who expresses disapproval of or finds fault with someone or something

Etymology

From 'admire' + '-er', meaning 'one who admires'; from Latin 'admirari' (to wonder at).
