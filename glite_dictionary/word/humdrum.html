humdrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌmˌdrʌm / (AmE), / ˈhʌmdrʌm / (BrE)
a state of boredom or monotony caused by a lack of variety or excitement
monotony; routine lack of variety

Examples

The humdrum of daily commuter trains left him feeling restless and uninspired.
She longed to escape the humdrum of her repetitive office job.
Travel offers a break from the humdrum of everyday life.

Synset Examples

he had never grown accustomed to the monotony of his work
he was sick of the humdrum of his fellow prisoners
he hated the sameness of the food the college served

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monotony - lack of variety and interest; tedious repetition
  • Antonym: excitement - a feeling of great enthusiasm and eagerness

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly imitative of a monotonous sound.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhʌmˌdrʌm / (AmE), / ˈhʌm.drʌm / (BrE)
lacking excitement or variety; dull and monotonous.
dull; lacking excitement

Examples

She found her office work humdrum after years of travel and adventure.
Their daily routine had become so humdrum that they longed for change.
Cooking the same meals every night can be a bit humdrum.

Synset Examples

an unglamorous job greasing engines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boring - not interesting; tedious
  • Antonym: exciting - causing great enthusiasm and eagerness

Etymology

Originated in the late 16th century, possibly imitative or from the obsolete term 'hum' (to drone) and 'drum' (monotonous sound).
