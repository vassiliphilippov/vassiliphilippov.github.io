Myricaria germanica

1. NOUN

/ maɪˌrɪkəriə dʒərˈmænɪkə / (AmE), / mɪˌrɪkəriə dʒɜːˈmænɪkə / (BrE)
a Eurasian shrub of the tamarisk family, with narrow leaves and pinkish flowers, growing in sandy or gravelly riverbanks
a Eurasian shrub of the tamarisk family

Examples

The Myricaria germanica thrives along riverbeds with sandy soils in Central Europe.
Botanists are concerned about the declining populations of Myricaria germanica due to habitat loss.
We identified several Myricaria germanica shrubs growing near the banks of the Danube.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Myricaria', and the species name 'germanica', meaning 'of Germany' in Latin.
