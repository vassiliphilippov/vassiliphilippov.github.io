Vangueria

1. NOUN

/ væŋˈɡwɪəriə / (AmE), / væŋˈɡwɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical African and Asiatic trees and shrubs known for their one-seeded fruit.
genus of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

Many species within the Vangueria genus are valued for their edible fruit.
The botanist discovered a new variety of Vangueria in East Africa.
Wild animals in the savanna often feed on the ripe fruit of Vangueria trees.

Etymology

From modern Latin, named after Pietro Paolo Vangelisti, an Italian botanist.

2. NOUN

/ vænˈɡwɪəriə / (AmE), / væŋˈɡwɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical African and Asian trees and shrubs in the madder family, known for their one-seeded fruits.
genus of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The Vangueria genus includes several species valued for their edible fruit.
Botanists recently discovered a new species of Vangueria in East Africa.
Farmers in some tropical regions cultivate Vangueria for its drought-resistant qualities.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after Dutch explorer Vangoer, with suffix -ia denoting genus.
