Halichoeres bivittatus

1. NOUN

/ hælɪˈkɔəˌriːz baɪvɪˈteɪtəs / (AmE), / hælɪˈkɔːəriːz baɪvɪˈteɪtəs / (BrE)
A small species of wrasse found in the tropical Atlantic Ocean, known commonly as the slippery dick.
species of tropical Atlantic wrasse

Examples

Divers often spot the Halichoeres bivittatus darting among rocks and coral reefs in the Caribbean.
The Halichoeres bivittatus is distinguished by its two prominent stripes along its body.
Marine biologists conducted a study on the feeding habits of Halichoeres bivittatus.

Etymology

From Greek 'halio' (sea) and 'choiros' (pig), with Latin species name 'bivittatus' meaning 'two-banded'.
