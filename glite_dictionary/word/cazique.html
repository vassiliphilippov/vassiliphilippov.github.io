cazique

1. NOUN

/ kəˈzik / (AmE), / kəˈziːk / (BrE)
A type of black-and-red or black-and-yellow oriole found in the American tropics.
a tropical American oriole

Examples

The cazique can often be spotted in the dense rainforests of Central America.
Many birdwatchers travel to see the vibrant colors of the cazique during migration season.
Researchers observed a cazique building its elaborate nest high in the canopy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oriole - a colorful passerine bird of the family Oriolidae

Etymology

From Spanish 'cacique' (chief), originally applied to certain birds due to their prominent appearance.
