Tatary

1. PROPN

/ ˈtætəri / (AmE), / ˈtætəri / (BrE)
A vast historical region in Europe and Asia under Mongol control during the 13th and 14th centuries, also known as Tartary.
historic region in Eurasia under Mongol rule

Examples

The armies marched eastward across Tatary, conquering new lands with each passing month.
In medieval maps, Tatary was depicted as a mysterious and largely unknown territory.
Many European travelers wrote tales of their journeys through Tatary during the Middle Ages.

Synset Examples

under Genghis Khan Tartary extended as far east as the Pacific Ocean

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tartary - alternate spelling of Tatary, historic region under Mongol rule

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Tartaria', referring to lands inhabited by the Tatars.
