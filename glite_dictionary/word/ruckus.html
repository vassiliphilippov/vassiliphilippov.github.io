ruckus

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌkəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌkəs / (BrE)
a noisy commotion or disturbance, often involving confusion or conflict
noisy commotion or disturbance
informal

Examples

The children caused a ruckus in the living room, laughing and shouting late into the night.
When the alarm sounded, there was a ruckus in the hallway as everyone tried to leave at once.
Neighbors complained about the ruckus coming from the apartment next door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uproar - a state of noisy excitement or confusion
  • Synonym: commotion - a state of confused and noisy disturbance
  • Antonym: calm - absence of noise, excitement, or disturbance

Etymology

Origin unknown, first recorded in 19th-century America.
