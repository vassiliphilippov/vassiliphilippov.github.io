Chamaea

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmiːə / (AmE), / kəˈmiːə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Paridae, consisting of the wrentit.
a bird genus containing the wrentit

Examples

The Chamaea is unique as it contains only one living species, the wrentit.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a member of the genus Chamaea in California.
Taxonomists debated the precise classification of Chamaea within the family Paridae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrentit - small bird of western North America, the only member of the genus Chamaea

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'chamai' (on the ground).
