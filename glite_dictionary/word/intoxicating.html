intoxicating

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɑksɪˌkeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɒksɪˌkeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely exciting or exhilarating, as if producing the effects of alcohol or a narcotic.
extremely exciting or exhilarating

Examples

The view from the mountaintop was so intoxicating that she could hardly breathe.
His intoxicating laughter filled the entire room with energy and joy.
There was an intoxicating sense of freedom as they raced along the coastline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrilling - causing excitement or exhilaration
  • Antonym: boring - not interesting or exciting

Etymology

From 'intoxicate', ultimately from Latin 'toxicum' (poison).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɑksɪˌkeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɒksɪkeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a substance or drink) causing intoxication; making someone drunk or affected by alcohol or drugs.
causing intoxication; making drunk

Examples

This intoxicating beverage should be consumed responsibly.
Beer and wine are both examples of intoxicating drinks.
Consuming too much of an intoxicating substance can impair your judgment.

Synset Examples

an intoxicating drink

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alcoholic - containing alcohol; causing intoxication

Etymology

From Latin intoxicare (to poison), from in- (into) + toxicum (poison).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
