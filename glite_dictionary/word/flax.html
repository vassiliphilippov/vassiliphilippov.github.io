flax

1. NOUN

/ flæks / (AmE), / flæks / (BrE)
the fiber of the flax plant, used to make linen thread and fabric
fiber from the flax plant for linen

Examples

The curtain was woven from pure flax and hung beautifully in the sunlight.
Ancient Egyptians valued flax for making linen garments and burial shrouds.
She prefers flax fabrics for their coolness and breathability in summer clothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linen - fabric made from flax fibers

Etymology

Old English *fleax*, related to Old High German *flahs* and Latin *linum* (flax, linen).

2. NOUN

/ flæks / (AmE), / flæks / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Linum, cultivated for its slender stems and seeds, used for making linen and linseed oil.
plant grown for fiber and seeds

Examples

The flax fields turn blue when the delicate flowers are in bloom.
They harvested the flax to make linen for clothing and sails.
Flax seeds are often added to foods for their nutritional benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linen - cloth made from the fiber of the flax plant

Etymology

Old English *fleax*, from Proto-Germanic *flahsaz*.
