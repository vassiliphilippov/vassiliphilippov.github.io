Cyclopteridae

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌklɑːptəˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / sɪˌklɒptəˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine fishes, commonly known as lumpfishes, characterized by a globular body and a suction disc formed from their pelvic fins
family of lumpfishes (marine fishes)

Examples

The family Cyclopteridae includes several species adapted to cold northern waters.
Members of the Cyclopteridae are easily recognized by their unique suction discs.
Marine biologists are studying the reproductive habits of Cyclopteridae in the North Atlantic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumpfish - a common fish in the Cyclopteridae family

Etymology

From New Latin Cyclopterus (type genus), from Greek kyklos (circle) + pteron (fin) + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ saɪˌklɑːptəˈraɪdiː / (AmE), / sɪˌklɒptəˈriːdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes commonly known as lumpfishes or lumpsuckers, characterized by their rounded bodies and modified pelvic fins that act as suction discs.
family of lumpfishes
medical

Examples

The Cyclopteridae are well known for their unusual, rounded bodies and adhesive pelvic fins.
Researchers are studying the behavior of Cyclopteridae in cold northern waters.
Many eggs of the Cyclopteridae are harvested as caviar substitutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumpfish - a common member of the Cyclopteridae family

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklos' (circle) and 'pteron' (fin), referring to the shape of the fish's fins.
