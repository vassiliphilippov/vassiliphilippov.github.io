Aster linosyris

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstər lɪnoʊˈsaɪrɪs / (AmE), / ˈæstə lɪnəʊˈsaɪrɪs / (BrE)
An early-flowering perennial plant of southern and southeastern Europe, with yellow flower heads resembling those of goldenrod.
perennial plant with yellow flowers, native to Europe

Examples

The Aster linosyris blooms early in the summer and brightens meadows with its yellow flower heads.
Gardeners in southern Europe often cultivate Aster linosyris for its resemblance to goldenrod.
Botanists noted that Aster linosyris thrives in dry, sunny locations across its native range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldenrod - a plant with clusters of small yellow flowers, similar in appearance

Etymology

From Latin; 'Aster' meaning 'star', and 'linosyris' referring to similarity with flax (Linum) and Syrian plants (Syris).
