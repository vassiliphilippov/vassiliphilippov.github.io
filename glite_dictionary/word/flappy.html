flappy

1. ADJ

/ ˈflæpi / (AmE), / ˈflæpi / (BrE)
having loose parts that flutter or move up and down, especially in the wind or with movement
tending to flap; fluttering

Examples

The puppy's flappy ears bounced with every step he took.
She wore a flappy hat that waved in the strong seaside breeze.
The costume had flappy sleeves that made it look even more dramatic when she danced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flapping - moving up and down or fluttering repeatedly
  • Antonym: rigid - not able to move or bend easily; stiff

Etymology

From 'flap' + '-y', denoting tendency or quality.
