Thysanoptera

1. NOUN

/ θaɪsəˈnɑptərə / (AmE), / θaɪsəˈnɒptərə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of tiny, slender insects known as thrips, distinguished by fringed wings.
order of insects: thrips

Examples

Scientists discovered several new species belonging to the order Thysanoptera.
Many agricultural pests fall under Thysanoptera due to their plant-feeding habits.
The fringed wings are a key feature of insects in Thysanoptera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrips - tiny, slender insects in the order Thysanoptera

Etymology

From Greek 'thysanos' (fringe) + 'pteron' (wing), referring to their fringed wings.
