gung ho

1. ADJ

/ ɡʌŋ ˈhoʊ / (AmE), / ɡʌŋ ˈhəʊ / (BrE)
very enthusiastic and eager, often to the point of being overly zealous or energetic
very enthusiastic and eager

Examples

The new manager is so gung ho about the project that she works late every night.
Some team members think he is a bit too gung ho when it comes to change.
She's always gung ho about volunteering for extra work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiastic - showing intense and eager enjoyment or interest
  • Synonym: zealous - showing great energy or enthusiasm in pursuit of an objective

Etymology

From Chinese 工合 (gōng hé), short for 工業合作社 (gōng yè hé zuò shè, 'industrial cooperative'), adopted as a motto by U.S. Marines in World War II.

2. ADJ

/ ɡʌŋ ˈhoʊ / (AmE), / ɡʌŋ ˈhəʊ / (BrE)
extremely enthusiastic and eager, especially about doing something
extremely enthusiastic and eager
informal

Examples

The whole staff became gung ho about launching the new program.
He's always gung ho when it comes to trying out the latest technology.
They were so gung ho to volunteer that they signed up first.

Synset Examples

The team was gung ho to take on the challenge.
She was gung ho about starting the new project immediately.
His gung ho attitude inspired everyone to work harder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiastic - showing intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval
  • Synonym: eager - wanting to do or have something very much

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese 工合 (gōng hé), short for 工业合作社 (Gōngyè Hézuòshè, 'Industrial Cooperative'), adopted in English during World War II by U.S. Marines to mean 'work together' or 'enthusiastic.'
