volunteer

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɑːlənˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌvɒlənˈtɪə / (BrE)
a person who freely offers to join the military or enlist for service rather than being conscripted or drafted
person who freely joins the military

Examples

Many men served as volunteers during the war, choosing to fight for their country.
The army is seeking volunteers for a new peacekeeping mission.
As a volunteer, he was motivated by a sense of duty rather than obligation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conscript - a person who is compulsorily enlisted for military service

Etymology

From Latin voluntarius (of one's free will), via Old French voluntaire.

WordNet ID: oewn-10778999-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˌvɑːlənˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌvɒlənˈtɪə / (BrE)
to offer to do something or give help willingly without being asked or forced
offer to do something willingly

Examples

She volunteered to organize the community clean-up event.
No one asked him, but he volunteered to take notes during the meeting.
After hearing about the shortage, several students volunteered to help in the cafeteria.

Synset Examples

She volunteered to drive the old lady home
I offered to help with the dishes but the hostess would not hear of it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offer - to present or express willingness to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'voluntarius' (of free will, voluntary).

5. NOUN

/ ˌvɑːlənˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌvɒlənˈtɪə / (BrE)
a person who freely offers to take part in an activity or perform a service, especially without being paid
person who does unpaid work willingly

Examples

Every Saturday, the hospital relies on at least one volunteer to help with patient care.
As a volunteer, she read stories to children at the local library.
The charity is seeking more volunteers for its upcoming fundraising event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voluntary worker - a person who does work without pay, especially for a charity or community organization
  • Antonym: employee - a person who works for pay

Etymology

From Latin 'voluntarius' (of one's free will), via Old French and Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10778819-n
Synset POS: n

6. VERB

/ ˌvɑːlənˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌvɒlənˈtɪə / (BrE)
to offer information, help, or services willingly and without being asked or forced
offer willingly without being asked

Examples

She volunteered her opinion even though no one had asked.
During the meeting, he volunteered the details of the project willingly.
The suspect volunteered important information to the police.

Synset Examples

He volunteered the information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offer - to present or give something for someone to accept or reject
  • Antonym: to withhold - to refuse to give something that is expected or desired

Etymology

From Latin voluntarius (of one's free will), from voluntas (will, wish).
