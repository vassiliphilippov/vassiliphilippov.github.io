galactocele

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlæk.təˌsiːl / (AmE), / ɡəˈlæk.təˌsiːl / (BrE)
a cystic swelling in the mammary gland containing milk or a milky fluid, usually caused by blockage of a milk duct
a milk-filled cyst in the breast
medical

Examples

The ultrasound revealed a galactocele in the patient's right breast.
A galactocele may develop during breastfeeding if a duct becomes blocked.
After aspiration, the contents of the galactocele were found to be milky.

Etymology

From Greek 'galakt-' (milk) + '-cele' (tumor, hernia).
