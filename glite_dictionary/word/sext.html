sext

1. NOUN

/ sɛkst / (AmE), / sɛkst / (BrE)
the fourth of the seven canonical hours, traditionally observed around noon in Christian liturgy
canonical hour at noon
archaic

Examples

The monks gathered in the chapel to chant sext at midday.
After the service of sext, the community would take their main meal.
Historical monasteries strictly observed the daily prayers, including sext around noon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: none - the ninth canonical hour of the Divine Office
  • Degree Lower: terce - the third canonical hour, observed about midmorning

Etymology

From Latin 'sext' (the sixth hour), referring to the sixth hour after sunrise in the Roman system.

2. NOUN

/ sɛkst / (AmE), / sɛkst / (BrE)
a message, typically sent by text, email, or other digital means, that contains sexually explicit content.
sexually explicit digital message
informal

Examples

She was embarrassed when her private sext was accidentally sent to the wrong person.
The celebrity's leaked sext caused a major scandal in the media.
Teenagers should be aware of the risks involved in sending a sext.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexting - the act of sending sexually explicit messages or images, especially by mobile phone

Etymology

Blend of 'sex' and 'text' (message).
