deadeye

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌaɪ / (BrE)
(nautical) A round, flat hardwood disk with holes and a grooved edge, used on sailing ships to tighten and hold the shrouds (ropes or wires supporting the mast).
round wooden fitting used on sailing ships

Examples

The sailors replaced the old deadeye to ensure the shroud was properly tensioned.
Restoring the antique ship involved carving new deadeyes from hardwood.
Each deadeye was carefully set in place to help stabilize the mast.

Etymology

From 'dead' + 'eye', referring to the object's resemblance to a staring eye.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌaɪ / (BrE)
a person who is an expert at shooting; an exceptionally accurate marksman
expert marksman; very accurate shooter

Examples

With his reputation as a deadeye, he never missed a single target at the shooting range.
They called her the team's deadeye after she landed three consecutive bullseyes.
If you want to win the competition, you'll need a real deadeye on your side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharpshooter - a person highly skilled at shooting
  • Synonym: marksman - a person skilled at shooting
  • Antonym: novice - a person new to a skill or activity; not skilled

Etymology

Formed from 'dead' + 'eye', referring to precise aim.
