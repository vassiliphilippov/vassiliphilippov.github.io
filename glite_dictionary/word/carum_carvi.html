Carum carvi

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛəɹəm ˈkɑːrvaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːrəm ˈkɑːvaɪ / (BrE)
the scientific name for the caraway plant, a Eurasian plant with small white flowers that produces the caraway seed used as a spice
the caraway plant (scientific name)

Examples

Carum carvi is cultivated in many parts of Europe for its flavorful seeds.
The essential oil extracted from Carum carvi is valued in both cooking and medicine.
When researching herbal remedies, she learned about the benefits of Carum carvi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caraway - the common name for Carum carvi, a plant producing aromatic seeds used as a spice

Etymology

From Latin 'carum' (caraway), of Greek origin via Arabic 'karawya'.
