trapezium

1. NOUN

/ trəˈpiːziəm / (AmE), / trəˈpiːziəm / (BrE)
A quadrilateral with no sides parallel to each other, especially in British usage.
quadrilateral with no parallel sides

Examples

In British mathematics, a trapezium has no parallel sides at all.
The student drew a trapezium and labeled each of its angles.
Unlike a parallelogram, a trapezium does not have any parallel sides.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: trapezoid - a quadrilateral with exactly one pair of parallel sides

Etymology

From Greek trapezion (little table), diminutive of trapeza (table).

2. NOUN

/ trəˈpiziəm / (AmE), / trəˈpiːzɪəm / (BrE)
the carpal bone in the wrist, located on the thumb side and articulating with the first and second metacarpal bones
wrist bone at base of thumb
medical

Examples

The surgeon repaired the fracture to the trapezium after the cycling accident.
Pain near the base of the thumb may indicate an issue with the trapezium bone.
Arthritis can sometimes develop at the joint where the trapezium meets the metacarpal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trapezoid - the carpal bone next to the trapezium in the wrist

Etymology

From Greek *trapézion* (little table), referring to its geometric shape.
