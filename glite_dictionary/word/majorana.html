Majorana

1. NOUN

/ məˈdʒɔːrənə / (AmE), / məˈdʒɔːrənə / (BrE)
a small genus of aromatic herbs in the mint family, sometimes included in the genus Origanum, such as sweet marjoram
genus of aromatic herbs (botany)

Examples

The genus Majorana is known for its aromatic leaves, commonly used in cooking and herbal remedies.
Some botanists classify sweet marjoram under Majorana rather than Origanum.
Many gardeners grow Majorana species for their fragrant foliage and culinary value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Origanum - a genus of aromatic herbs including oregano and marjoram

Etymology

From Latin *Majorana*, name for marjoram, from Greek *amarakon* (marjoram).
