sceptical

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɛptɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈskɛptɪkəl / (BrE)
doubting or questioning the truth of generally accepted beliefs or claims, especially in matters of religion.
doubting accepted beliefs, especially religious ones

Examples

She remains sceptical about the existence of miracles despite what she has heard.
The committee took a sceptical approach to the extraordinary claims presented.
Many historians are sceptical of ancient legends that lack solid evidence.

Synset Examples

a skeptical approach to the nature of miracles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeptical - Alternative spelling, chiefly American English
  • Antonym: credulous - willing to believe or trust too readily

Etymology

From Greek *skeptikos* (thoughtful, inquiring), via Latin *scepticus* and late Middle English.

2. ADJ

/ ˈskɛptɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈskɛptɪkəl / (BrE)
Having or expressing doubt about something; not easily convinced or accepting things as true without proof.
doubtful; not easily convinced
british

Examples

Many scientists remain sceptical about the effectiveness of the new treatment.
Her tone was sceptical when she asked if the plan was truly workable.
He gave me a sceptical look when I explained my theory.

Synset Examples

a skeptical attitude
a skeptical listener

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeptical - doubtful; inclined to question or doubt claims
  • Antonym: convinced - completely certain about something

Etymology

From Greek *skeptikos* (inquiring, reflective), via Latin *scepticus* and French *sceptique*.
