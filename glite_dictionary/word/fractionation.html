fractionation

1. NOUN

/ ˌfræk.ʃəˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfræk.ʃəˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of separating a mixture into its individual components, often using heat
separation of mixture into components, often with heat

Examples

The oil refinery uses fractionation to separate crude oil into gasoline, diesel, and other products.
During chemical analysis, fractionation helps isolate compounds based on their boiling points.
The laboratory employed fractionation techniques to purify the protein samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distillation - the process of purifying a liquid by heating and cooling

Etymology

From Latin 'fractio' (a breaking), influenced by 'fraction'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfræk.ʃəˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfræk.ʃəˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of separating a mixture into its component parts or fractions, especially by physical or chemical means
separation into component parts

Examples

The laboratory used fractionation to isolate different components of the sample.
Petroleum fractionation is critical for producing fuels such as gasoline and diesel.
Through careful fractionation, the researchers purified each substance for further analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separation - the act of moving or being moved apart

Etymology

From Latin 'fractio' (a breaking), with '-ation' denoting an action or process.
