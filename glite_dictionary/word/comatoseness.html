comatoseness

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈmɑːtəsnəs / (AmE), / kəˈməʊtəsnəs / (BrE)
The state of being in a coma; deep and prolonged unconsciousness typically caused by disease or injury.
state of being in a coma
medical

Examples

The patient's comatoseness lasted for several weeks after the accident.
Doctors closely monitored the degree of comatoseness throughout her treatment.
Researchers studied the factors contributing to prolonged comatoseness in brain injury cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coma - deep state of prolonged unconsciousness

Etymology

From 'comatose' + '-ness'; 'comatose' from Greek 'kōma' (deep sleep).
