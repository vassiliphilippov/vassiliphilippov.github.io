Bucephala islandica

1. NOUN

/ ˌbjuːsəˈfæl.ə aɪˈlændɪkə / (AmE), / ˌbjuːsɪˈfɑːlə ʌɪˈlandɪkə / (BrE)
A species of North American goldeneye; a diving duck found mainly in northern forests and lakes.
a North American diving duck species

Examples

The Bucephala islandica is known for its striking black-and-white plumage and bright yellow eyes.
Birdwatchers traveled to the lake hoping to catch a glimpse of Bucephala islandica during migration season.
Unlike other ducks, Bucephala islandica prefers breeding in forested regions near cold, clear lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Barrow's goldeneye - A common English name for Bucephala islandica, a species of diving duck

Etymology

From New Latin: Bucephala (duck genus) + islandica (of Iceland), referring to the species' type locality.
