no-good

1. ADJ

/ ˌnoʊˈɡʊd / (AmE), / ˌnəʊˈɡʊd / (BrE)
Having little or no value or use; worthless or useless.
worthless; without merit or value

Examples

Many people thought the abandoned building was a no-good place for troublemakers to hang out.
She called her ex-boyfriend a no-good liar after learning the truth.
That no-good scam artist took everyone's money and disappeared.

Synset Examples

a sorry horse
a sorry excuse
a lazy no-count, good-for-nothing goldbrick
the car was a no-good piece of junk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worthless - having no value or use
  • Antonym: valuable - having worth or usefulness

2. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊˌɡʊd / (AmE), / ˌnəʊˈɡʊd / (BrE)
(of a check) not valid or accepted, especially because there are insufficient funds in the account.
worthless or invalid (of a check)

Examples

The bank called to inform me that my last payment was a no-good check.
She was embarrassed when the store rejected her no-good check at the register.
After writing a no-good check, he had to quickly deposit more money to cover it.

Synset Examples

a rubber check
a no-good check

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worthless - having no value, use, or importance
  • Antonym: valid - legally or officially acceptable
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01132700-s
Synset POS: s
