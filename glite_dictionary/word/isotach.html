isotach

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsoʊˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəʊˌtæk / (BrE)
A line on a chart or map joining points where the wind speed is the same.
line marking equal wind speed

Examples

The weather map clearly showed an isotach passing through the central region, indicating steady wind speed.
Forecasters used the isotach lines to analyze jet stream dynamics and predict storms.
Pilots studied the isotach patterns to identify areas of potential turbulence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isotherm - a line joining points of equal temperature
  • Synonym: isobar - a line joining points of equal atmospheric pressure

Etymology

From Greek 'iso-' (equal) and 'tachys' (swift).
