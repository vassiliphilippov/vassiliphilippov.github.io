perceptiveness

1. NOUN

/ pərˈsɛptɪvnəs / (AmE), / pəˈsɛptɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having keen insight or the ability to notice and understand things quickly and accurately
keen insight or understanding

Examples

The teacher praised her perceptiveness in identifying problems that others overlooked.
His perceptiveness helped him notice subtle changes in his friend's mood.
With remarkable perceptiveness, she understood the underlying issues almost instantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insight - the ability to understand people and situations clearly
  • Synonym: intuition - the ability to understand something immediately, without conscious reasoning
  • Antonym: insensitivity - lack of perceptiveness; not noticing or caring about others' feelings

Etymology

From 'perceptive' + '-ness'; 'perceptive' from Latin 'percipere' (to perceive).

4. NOUN

/ pərˈsɛptɪvnəs / (AmE), / pəˈsɛptɪvnəs / (BrE)
the ability to notice and understand things quickly and accurately, especially subtle details or emotions
keen insight and understanding

Examples

Her perceptiveness impressed everyone at the meeting, as she noticed problems no one else saw.
He is known for his perceptiveness when it comes to reading people's intentions.
The teacher appreciated her student's perceptiveness in understanding classmates' feelings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intuition - the ability to understand something immediately, without conscious reasoning
  • Antonym: obliviousness - the state of being unaware of what's happening
