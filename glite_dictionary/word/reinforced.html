reinforced

1. ADJ

/ ˌriː.ɪnˈfɔrst / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪnˈfɔːst / (BrE)
strengthened or made more durable by adding extra material or support
strengthened by added support

Examples

The walls were reinforced with steel beams to ensure stability during earthquakes.
She wore a reinforced helmet for extra protection while cycling.
The reinforced plastic was designed to withstand extreme temperatures.

Synset Examples

reinforced concrete contains steel bars or metal netting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strengthened - made stronger
  • Antonym: weakened - made weaker

2. ADJ

/ ˌriːɪnˈfɔrst / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈfɔːst / (BrE)
(of a soap or cleaning agent) having a substance such as an abrasive or filler added to make it more effective.
containing added substance for increased cleaning

Examples

This reinforced cleanser removes tough stains more easily than regular soaps.
Many industrial detergents are reinforced with abrasives for deeper cleaning.
The reinforced soap left my kitchen surfaces spotless.

Synset Examples

the built liquid detergents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortified - having added substances for extra strength or effectiveness

Etymology

From reinforce + -ed; reinforce from Old French renforcer, from Latin reforcāre (to strengthen).
