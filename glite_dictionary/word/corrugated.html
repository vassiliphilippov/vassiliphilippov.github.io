corrugated

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔrəˌɡeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəɡeɪtɪd / (BrE)
shaped into parallel ridges and grooves, typically to provide strength or texture
shaped with parallel ridges and grooves

Examples

The corrugated metal roof helped the rainwater flow off quickly.
We packed the dishes in corrugated cardboard to keep them safe during the move.
The artist sculpted a corrugated surface to create an interesting texture.

Synset Examples

the surface of the ocean was rippled and corrugated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ribbed - having a surface marked by ridges
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and flat surface, without ridges or grooves

Etymology

From Latin 'corrugatus', past participle of 'corrugare' (to wrinkle, to crease).
