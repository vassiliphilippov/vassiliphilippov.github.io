ill-breeding

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪlˈbriːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɪlˈbriːdɪŋ / (BrE)
Lack of good manners or courtesy, especially as a result of ignorance or poor upbringing.
lack of good manners; impoliteness

Examples

His ill-breeding was evident in the way he spoke to the waitstaff.
She could not forgive his ill-breeding, no matter how intelligent he was.
Such displays of ill-breeding were frowned upon in their social circle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudeness - disrespect or lack of manners
  • Antonym: good breeding - polite and well-mannered behavior

Etymology

From 'ill-' meaning 'bad' and 'breeding' in the sense of upbringing or education.
