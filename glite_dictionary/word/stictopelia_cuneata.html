Stictopelia cuneata

1. NOUN

/ stɪk.təˈpiː.li.ə kjuːˈniː.ə.tə / (AmE), / stɪk.təˈpiː.li.ə kjuːˈneɪ.tə / (BrE)
A small Australian dove with a slender body, known as the diamond dove, commonly found in arid and semi-arid regions.
species of small Australian dove

Examples

The Stictopelia cuneata is admired for the white spots on its wings.
Birdwatchers often travel to Australia to observe the elusive Stictopelia cuneata in its natural habitat.
The gentle calls of the Stictopelia cuneata can be heard at dawn in the outback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diamond dove - common name for Stictopelia cuneata

Etymology

From Greek *stiktos* (spotted) + *peleia* (dove), and Latin *cuneata* (wedge-shaped).
