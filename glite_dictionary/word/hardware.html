hardware

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌwɛə / (BrE)
heavy military equipment and weaponry, such as tanks and missiles, used by armed forces
military weaponry and equipment

Examples

The government increased its spending on hardware such as tanks and missile systems.
Modern warfare relies heavily on advanced hardware to maintain superiority on the battlefield.
Negotiations stalled as both sides showed off their latest military hardware.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːrdˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌwɛə / (BrE)
tools, implements, or equipment, especially those made of metal, used to carry out practical tasks.
metal tools and implements

Examples

He keeps all the household hardware in the toolbox in the garage.
The shop sells gardening hardware such as shovels, rakes, and hoes.
Old-fashioned hardware like cast iron pans is often more durable than modern alternatives.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: software - programs and operating information used by a computer

Etymology

From hard + ware; 'ware' from Old English 'waru' (goods, merchandise).

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrˌdwɛr / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌwɛə / (BrE)
The physical parts of a computer system, such as the mechanical, magnetic, electronic, and electrical components, as opposed to the software.
physical components of a computer

Examples

Many companies invest heavily in the latest hardware to keep their computer systems up to date.
Without reliable hardware, even the best software cannot function properly.
He upgraded his computer’s hardware to improve its performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: software - programs and operating information used by a computer

Etymology

From hard + ware, originally referring to metal goods, later applied to computers in the mid-20th century.

Source: WordNet
