homophobia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊməˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌhɒməˈfəʊbiə / (BrE)
prejudice, fear, or dislike of homosexual people or homosexuality.
prejudice against homosexuality or homosexual people

Examples

Many activists continue to fight against homophobia in schools and workplaces.
The film addresses themes of homophobia and acceptance in society.
She spoke out about the homophobia she experienced growing up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heterosexism - discrimination in favor of heterosexual and against homosexual people
  • Antonym: tolerance - acceptance of different views or practices, including those related to sexuality

Etymology

From Greek 'homo-' (same) and 'phobia' (fear).
