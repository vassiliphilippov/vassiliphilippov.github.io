centerfold

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntɚˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈsɛntəfəʊld / (BrE)
the middle spread of a magazine, especially featuring a large photograph, map, or similar item, often as a foldout
magazine's middle foldout page

Examples

The magazine featured a stunning centerfold of a tropical beach.
He carefully removed the centerfold from the vintage issue to frame it.
The centerfold included a detailed map of the National Park.

Etymology

Formed from 'center' + 'fold', referring to the central folded pages of a publication.
