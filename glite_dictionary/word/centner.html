centner

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnt.nɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnt.nə / (BrE)
a unit of weight equal to 100 kilograms, used chiefly in some European countries.
unit of weight (100 kilograms)

Examples

The shipment weighed five centners and required special handling.
Wheat was sold by the centner in several Eastern European markets.
Farmers recorded their potato harvest in centners per hectare.

Etymology

From German 'Zentner', from Latin 'centenarius' (containing a hundred).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnt.nɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnt.nə / (BrE)
A unit of weight used in some European countries, equal to 50 kilograms.
unit of weight (50 kg) in parts of Europe

Examples

The shipment weighed three centners and was carefully recorded upon arrival.
Farmers sold their wheat in centners, which made calculations easier at the local co-op.
He was surprised to learn that a centner equals exactly 50 kilograms.

Etymology

From German 'Zentner', ultimately from Latin 'centenarius' (of a hundred), relating to hundredweight.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnt.nɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnt.nə / (BrE)
A unit of weight used in the United States, equal to 100 pounds (approximately 45.36 kilograms).
unit of weight equal to 100 pounds

Examples

The farmer sold the grain by the centner to the local mill.
One centner of coal is roughly 45 kilograms in metric terms.
Wheat prices are often quoted per centner in some trade reports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hundredweight - a unit of weight equivalent to 100 pounds in the US or 112 pounds in the UK

Etymology

From Latin 'centenarius' (containing a hundred), via German 'Zentner'.
