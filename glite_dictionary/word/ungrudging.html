ungrudging

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡrʌdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡrʌdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a feeling, action, or quality) given freely and without envy, reluctance, or resentment.
given freely, without envy or reluctance

Examples

She expressed her ungrudging admiration for his achievements.
Their ungrudging support made a real difference to the project's success.
He offered his time and effort in an ungrudging manner.

Synset Examples

ungrudging admiration

Related Terms

  • Antonym: grudging - reluctant or resentful in giving or allowing

Etymology

From un- (not) + grudging (from grudge), meaning not showing reluctance or resentment.
