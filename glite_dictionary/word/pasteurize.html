to pasteurize

1. VERB

/ ˈpæs.tʃəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpɑːs.tjə.raɪz / (BrE)
to heat food or drink, especially milk, to a specific temperature for a set period of time in order to kill harmful microorganisms without significantly changing the taste or quality
heat to kill microorganisms (esp. food/drink)

Examples

Most milk sold in stores has been pasteurized to ensure it is safe to drink.
Some artisanal cheeses are made from unpasteurized milk.
The company pasteurizes all its juices to extend their shelf life.

Synset Examples

pasteurize milk

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: to sterilize - make free from all living microorganisms
  • Degree Lower: to sanitize - make clean and hygienic by removing bacteria

Etymology

From French pasteuriser, named after Louis Pasteur (1822–1895), the French scientist who developed the process.
