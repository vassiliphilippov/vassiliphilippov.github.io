Anzac

1. NOUN

/ ˈænzæk / (AmE), / ˈænzæk / (BrE)
A member of the Australian and New Zealand Army Corps, especially one who fought in World War I.
Australian or New Zealand WWI soldier

Examples

My great-grandfather was an Anzac at Gallipoli during World War I.
On Anzac Day, people commemorate the bravery of every Anzac who served.
The legend of the Anzac is an important part of Australian and New Zealand history.

Etymology

Acronym from Australian and New Zealand Army Corps.
