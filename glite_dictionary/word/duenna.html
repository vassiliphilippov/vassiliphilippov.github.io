duenna

1. NOUN

/ duˈɛnə / (AmE), / djʊˈenə / (BrE)
An older woman acting as a chaperone, especially one who accompanies a young woman to supervise and ensure proper behavior.
female chaperone or escort

Examples

The strict duenna never left the young woman alone with her suitor.
In the play, the duenna constantly supervises the heroine's every move.
She felt stifled by the presence of the ever-watchful duenna during social outings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaperone - a person, typically a woman, who accompanies and supervises young people

Etymology

From Spanish 'dueña' meaning 'lady, mistress, chaperone'.
