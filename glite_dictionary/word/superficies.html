superficies

1. NOUN

/ suː.pɚˈfɪʃ.iˌiz / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈfɪʃ.iːz / (BrE)
the outer surface or face of an object or area, especially as distinguished from its interior
outer surface of an object or area

Examples

The superficies of the ancient statue was marred by centuries of weathering.
Pollen accumulated on the superficies of the pond, forming a yellow film.
Examining the superficies revealed tiny cracks not visible to the naked eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surface - the outside part or uppermost layer of something

Etymology

From Latin superficies (surface), from super- (above) + facies (face).

2. NOUN

/ suːˈpɪʃiˌiz / (AmE), / suːˈpɪsiːiːz / (BrE)
the outward or superficial appearance of something, as opposed to its deeper or more substantial nature
outward or superficial appearance

Examples

The superficies of the painting was smooth and polished, hiding the rough sketch underneath.
She was tired of dealing with the superficies of people's personalities and longed to understand their true motives.
Despite the ornate superficies of the proposal, there was little substance behind it.

Synset Examples

the audience was held by the substance of the play rather than by the superficies of the production

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surface - the outside layer or top part of something
  • Antonym: substance - the essential nature or most important part of something

Etymology

From Latin superficies (surface, outward appearance), from super- (above) + facies (face).
