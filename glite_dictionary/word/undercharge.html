undercharge

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndərˌtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an amount charged that is less than what is appropriate or required
insufficient charge; too little charged

Examples

The accountant discovered an undercharge in last month's invoices.
Customers benefited from an undercharge due to the pricing error.
After reviewing the bill, the clerk noticed an undercharge for the service.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overcharge - an excessive charge; charging more than is appropriate
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04514904-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to charge someone less than the correct or expected amount for goods or services
charge too little

Examples

The cashier accidentally undercharged me for my groceries, so I went back to pay the difference.
If we undercharge our clients, we risk losing money on each job.
Staff discovered that the restaurant system had undercharged customers for several months.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overcharge - to charge someone more than the correct or expected amount

Etymology

From the prefix 'under-' (meaning 'below' or 'insufficient') + 'charge'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02324270-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
a price that is lower than the correct or expected amount
price lower than expected

Examples

The cashier gave me an undercharge by mistake, so I pointed it out and paid the difference.
Every undercharge in the accounts must be investigated to find its cause.
When an undercharge occurs, the business may lose money on the sale.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overcharge - a price that is higher than the correct amount

Etymology

From under- (meaning 'below') + charge (meaning 'price to be paid').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13328707-n
Synset POS: n
