obscurantist

1. NOUN

/ əbˈskjɜːrəntɪst / (AmE), / əbˈskjʊərəntɪst / (BrE)
a person who deliberately prevents the facts or full details of something from becoming known, often to obscure the truth or hinder understanding
person who deliberately obscures information

Examples

The politician was accused of being an obscurantist who often evaded direct questions.
As an obscurantist, she preferred to shroud her research in jargon that few could understand.
Critics argued that the committee's obscurantist approach only served to confuse the public further.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscurer - someone who makes something difficult to see or understand

Etymology

From French *obscurantisme*, from Latin *obscurare* (to darken), related to *obscurus* (dark).
