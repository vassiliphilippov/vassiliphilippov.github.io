engineer

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.dʒəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
A person who designs, builds, or maintains engines, machines, or structures, using scientific knowledge to solve practical problems.
person using science to solve problems

Examples

She trained as an engineer to work in the renewable energy sector.
The engineer fixed the problem with impressive speed and precision.
As an engineer, he specializes in designing safer bridges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: architect - a person who designs buildings
  • Degree Lower: technician - a person skilled in technical work or equipment

Etymology

From Latin 'ingeniator' (one who devises), from 'ingenium' (cleverness, invention).

2. VERB

/ ˌɛn.dʒəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
to plan and direct the details of a complex project, event, or scheme, often with careful skill or ingenuity
plan and direct a complex undertaking

Examples

She skillfully engineered the company's expansion into new markets.
The government engineered a solution to the economic crisis through careful negotiation.
They engineered the whole event to appear spontaneous, though it was meticulously planned.

Synset Examples

he masterminded the robbery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastermind - to plan and direct an elaborate or complex project

Etymology

From Old French *engigneor* (constructor), from Latin *ingeniator* (contriver, deviser), from *ingenium* (skill, talent).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.dʒəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
a person who operates a railway locomotive; a train driver, especially in American English.
operator of a railway locomotive
american

Examples

The engineer leaned out of the locomotive's window to check the tracks ahead.
After twenty years as a engineer, she knew every mile of the route by heart.
The engineer blew the whistle as the train approached the station.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: driver - person who drives a vehicle, especially a train
  • Antonym: conductor - railway employee responsible for the train but not operating it

Etymology

From Latin 'ingeniator' (contriver), via Middle French 'engignier'; adopted in railway context in 19th century America.

5. VERB

to design or create (a system, process, or device) in a careful and technical way
design or create carefully

Examples

The company hired experts to engineer a new security system for their offices.
We need to engineer a system that minimizes downtime and maximizes efficiency.
After months of planning, the team finally engineered a system capable of handling millions of users.

Synset Examples

They must engineer a more efficient system for production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: design - to create or plan something with a specific function or intention
  • Synonym: devise - to plan or invent by careful thought

Etymology

From Middle English 'engineour', from Old French, from Late Latin 'ingeniator' (one who creates or devises).
