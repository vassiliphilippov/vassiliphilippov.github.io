superscription

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːpərˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
an inscription or writing placed above or on the top of something, especially above the main text or object
inscription written above something

Examples

The ancient coin had a superscription that revealed its origin.
In medieval manuscripts, a superscription often indicated the chapter's theme.
The superscription above the painting explained its historical significance.

Etymology

From Latin 'superscriptio', from 'superscribere' (to write above).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pərˈskrɪp.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈskrɪp.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of writing or printing a heading, inscription, or address, especially on an envelope, book, or coin
the act of inscribing or addressing

Examples

The superscription on the envelope clearly displayed the recipient's name and address.
Ancient Roman coins often featured a superscription indicating the emperor's name.
The superscription on the book's title page was written in elegant calligraphy.

Etymology

From Latin superscriptio, from superscribere meaning 'to write above'.
