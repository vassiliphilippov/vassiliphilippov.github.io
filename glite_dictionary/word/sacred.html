sacred

1. ADJ

/ ˈseɪkrɪd / (AmE), / ˈseɪkrɪd / (BrE)
regarded with great respect or reverence; considered worthy of honor or dedication
worthy of respect or reverence

Examples

Many cultures consider the family unit to be sacred and deserving of utmost respect.
The ancient tree was treated as sacred by the villagers, who never cut its branches.
He believes that freedom of speech is a sacred right that should never be infringed.

Synset Examples

saw motherhood as woman's sacred calling

Related Terms

  • Antonym: profane - not sacred or religious; showing disrespect for sacred things

Etymology

From Latin sacer (holy, consecrated).

2. ADJ

/ ˈseɪ.krɪd / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.krɪd / (BrE)
regarded with great respect and reverence, especially because of a connection with religion
regarded with religious reverence

Examples

The mountain is considered sacred by the local tribe and is not open to tourists.
Many people believe that the temple's grounds are sacred and should be treated with respect.
He removed his shoes before entering the sacred shrine.

Synset Examples

the sacred name of Jesus
Jerusalem's hallowed soil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holy - dedicated or consecrated to God or a religious purpose
  • Antonym: profane - not sacred or religious; showing disrespect toward sacred things

Etymology

From Latin 'sacrare' (to consecrate, make holy), from 'sacer' (holy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02062014-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈseɪ.krɪd / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.krɪd / (BrE)
devoted exclusively to a particular use, purpose, or person
devoted exclusively to something

Examples

The quiet room was sacred to her writing and no one else entered.
Every Sunday morning, an hour was sacred to family breakfast.
In the museum, one gallery is sacred to works by local artists.

Synset Examples

a fund sacred to charity
a morning hour sacred to study
a private office sacred to the President

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dedicated - exclusively allocated or set aside for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: profane - not devoted to a special purpose; ordinary or secular

Etymology

From Latin sacer (holy, dedicated).

4. ADJ

/ ˈseɪ.krɪd / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.krɪd / (BrE)
Connected with religion or dedicated to a religious purpose and so deserving veneration.
related to religion or worship

Examples

Pilgrims gathered to read sacred scriptures in the ancient temple.
Only members of the clergy were allowed to touch the sacred objects on the altar.
Chanting and music played an important role in sacred ceremonies around the world.

Synset Examples

sacred texts
sacred rites
sacred music

Related Terms

  • Antonym: profane - not connected with religion or sacred matters; secular
  • Synonym: holy - dedicated or consecrated to God or a religious purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'sacrare' (to make holy), from 'sacer' (sacred, holy).
