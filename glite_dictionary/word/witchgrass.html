witchgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtʃˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A North American grass with slender, bushy flower clusters, commonly found as a weed in cultivated fields.
a North American weedy grass

Examples

Witchgrass quickly took over the edges of the newly planted cornfield.
Farmers often struggle to eradicate witchgrass from their land.
In late summer, the brushy heads of witchgrass become especially noticeable along rural roads.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtʃˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A European grass, Elymus repens, that spreads quickly by creeping rhizomes and is considered a weed in North America.
a fast-spreading weedy grass

Examples

The invasive witchgrass quickly took over the edges of the farmer's field.
Gardeners often struggle to remove persistent witchgrass from their flowerbeds.
After a wet spring, patches of witchgrass appeared along the country lane.
