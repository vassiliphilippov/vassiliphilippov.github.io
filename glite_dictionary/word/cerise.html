cerise

1. NOUN

/ səˈris / (AmE), / səˈriːz / (BrE)
a deep red color resembling that of ripe cherries
deep red color

Examples

She chose a cerise dress for the evening gala.
The walls were painted a vibrant shade of cerise.
He picked a bouquet of cerise tulips for the centerpiece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crimson - a strong, deep red color
  • Degree Higher: scarlet - a brilliant red color

Etymology

From French 'cerise' meaning 'cherry', from Latin 'cerasum'.

2. ADJ

/ səˈris / (AmE), / səˈriːz / (BrE)
of a bright to deep red-pink colour, resembling that of a cherry.
cherry-red colour

Examples

She wore a stunning dress in a vibrant cerise shade to the party.
The artist chose cerise paint to capture the lively atmosphere of the scene.
A bouquet of cerise roses brightened the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherry-red - a bright red colour like that of a cherry

Etymology

From French 'cerise' (cherry), from Latin 'cerasum'.
