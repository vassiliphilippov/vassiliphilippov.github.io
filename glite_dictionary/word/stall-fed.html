stall-fed

1. ADJ

/ stɔl-fɛd / (AmE), / stɔːl-fɛd / (BrE)
(of livestock) kept and fed in a stall, usually to fatten them for market.
livestock fattened in a stall

Examples

The cattle were stall-fed to ensure they gained enough weight before auction.
Many farmers choose the stall-fed method for greater control over the animals' diet.
She prefers to buy beef that is pasture-raised rather than stall-fed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pasture-fed - (of livestock) fed mainly on grass in a pasture
