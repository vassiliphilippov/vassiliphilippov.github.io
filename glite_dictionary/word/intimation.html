intimation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.t̬əˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tɪˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an indirect or subtle suggestion or hint
indirect suggestion or hint

Examples

The manager gave no intimation of his plans to retire at the end of the year.
Her smile was an intimation that she approved of the idea.
Without any intimation of danger, the hikers continued down the trail.

Synset Examples

not a breath of scandal ever touched her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hint - a slight or indirect indication or suggestion
  • Synonym: implication - something suggested but not stated directly
  • Synonym: suggestion - an idea or plan put forward for consideration

Etymology

From Latin intimatio, from intimare (to make known).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntɪˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a slight suggestion; an indirect or subtle indication or hint of something
a slight suggestion or hint

Examples

She gave no intimation of her true feelings during the meeting.
There was an intimation in his tone that something was wrong.
The first intimation of trouble arrived in an urgent phone call.

Synset Examples

he had no inkling what was about to happen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hint - a slight or indirect suggestion or clue
  • Synonym: implication - a suggestion that is not made directly
  • Antonym: overt - done or shown openly

Etymology

From Latin intimatio, from intimare ('to make known') via French 'intimation'.
