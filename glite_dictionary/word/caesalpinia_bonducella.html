Caesalpinia bonducella

1. NOUN

/ siːˌzælˈpɪniə bɒnˈdʌsələ / (AmE), / siːˌsælˈpɪniə bɒnˈdʌsələ / (BrE)
a tropical tree with large prickly seed pods that resemble beans, whose seeds are used for jewelry and rosaries
tropical tree with prickly pods, seeds used in jewelry

Examples

The seeds of the Caesalpinia bonducella are often used in making traditional jewelry in many cultures.
Farmers cultivate Caesalpinia bonducella for its medicinal properties and decorative seeds.
We spotted a Caesalpinia bonducella with distinctive prickly pods during our walk in the botanical garden.

Etymology

Caesalpinia: named after Italian botanist Andrea Cesalpino; bonducella: from New Latin, of uncertain origin.
