frisk

1. NOUN

/ frɪsk / (AmE), / frɪsk / (BrE)
a quick search of a person's outer clothing for concealed weapons or illegal drugs, especially by the police
search of a person for concealed items

Examples

After stopping the suspect, the officer performed a quick frisk for weapons.
The airport security staff conducted a frisk before allowing him through the checkpoint.
A routine frisk uncovered a concealed knife in his jacket pocket.

Synset Examples

he gave the suspect a quick frisk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pat-down - a search of a person's outer clothing for hidden objects
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00947876-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ frɪsk / (AmE), / frɪsk / (BrE)
to play or move in a lively, energetic, or playful way, especially with skipping or jumping
move or play energetically and playfully

Examples

The puppies frisk around the yard every morning.
Children love to frisk about in the park during recess.
Lambs would sometimes frisk in the fields at sunrise.

Synset Examples

The children frolicked in the garden
the gamboling lambs in the meadows
The toddlers romped in the playroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolic - to play and move about cheerfully, excitedly, or energetically
  • Synonym: romp - to play or move in a carefree, lively way

Etymology

From Middle English 'frisken', likely of Scandinavian origin; related to Old Norse 'frisky' (fresh, brisk).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01887709-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ frɪsk / (AmE), / frɪsk / (BrE)
(of a person, especially a police officer) to search someone for concealed items, especially weapons or drugs, by quickly running the hands over their clothing and through their pockets.
search a person for concealed items by patting clothing

Examples

The security guard frisked each visitor before letting them enter the building.
She felt nervous as the officer began to frisk her at the airport checkpoint.
If you refuse to be frisked, you may not be allowed to board the plane.

Synset Examples

The police frisked everyone at the airport

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - to look carefully for something or through something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01320883-v
Synset POS: v
