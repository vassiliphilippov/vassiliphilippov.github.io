Ephippiorhynchus

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌfɪpioʊˈrɪŋkəs / (AmE), / ɪˌfɪpɪəˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
A genus of large storks found in tropical Africa and Asia, known for their striking appearance and large bills.
genus of large storks

Examples

The Ephippiorhynchus genus includes some of the most visually striking storks in Africa.
Researchers observed several Ephippiorhynchus individuals nesting near the Zambezi River.
Birdwatchers traveled to Uganda hoping to spot an Ephippiorhynchus in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saddlebill - a large stork of the genus Ephippiorhynchus

Etymology

From Greek ephippion (saddle) + rhynchos (beak), referring to the bill's shape.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌfɪpiəˈrɪŋkəs / (AmE), / ɪˌfɪpiəˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
A genus of large wading birds in the stork family, including the saddle-billed storks.
a genus of large storks

Examples

The Ephippiorhynchus genus is easily recognized by the distinctive shape of the birds' bills.
Biologists studying wetlands often observe members of Ephippiorhynchus in their natural habitat.
The saddle-billed stork, classified under Ephippiorhynchus, is iconic for its colorful beak.

Etymology

From Greek 'ephippion' (saddle) and 'rhynchos' (beak), referring to the bird's saddle-shaped bill.
