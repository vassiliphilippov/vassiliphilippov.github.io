Kosciusko

1. PROPN

/ kɑːsˈtʃʌskoʊ / (AmE), / kɒsˈtʃʌskəʊ / (BrE)
Tadeusz Kościuszko (1746–1817), a Polish patriot and military leader who fought for American independence in the Revolutionary War.
Polish patriot and American Revolutionary leader

Examples

Kosciusko is remembered as a hero in both Poland and the United States for his role in the fight for independence.
Many American towns and landmarks are named after Kosciusko in honor of his contributions to the Revolutionary War.
During his service, Kosciusko engineered fortifications that proved vital to the American victory at Saratoga.

Etymology

Transliteration of Polish surname Kościuszko, as borne by Tadeusz Kościuszko.
