crucifix

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruːsəˌfɪks / (AmE), / ˈkruːsɪfɪks / (BrE)
a gymnastic exercise performed on the rings in which the gymnast supports their body with both arms extended horizontally
horizontal-arm rings exercise in gymnastics

Examples

The gymnast held a perfect crucifix for several seconds during his rings routine.
Performing the crucifix requires exceptional shoulder and core strength.
She trained for months before she could master the crucifix on the rings.

Etymology

From Latin 'crucifixus' (crucified), originally referring to a representation of Christ on the cross; the exercise is named for the resemblance to this posture.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkruː.səˌfɪks / (AmE), / ˈkruː.sɪ.fɪks / (BrE)
A representation of a cross with a figure of Jesus Christ on it, used especially as a symbol in Christian churches and devotion.
cross with figure of Jesus

Examples

A large crucifix hung on the wall above the altar in the old church.
He wore a silver crucifix around his neck as a sign of his faith.
The artist carefully carved a wooden crucifix for the local parish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross - an upright post with a transverse bar, used especially as a Christian symbol
  • Synonym: crucifixion - the execution of a person by nailing or binding them to a cross

Etymology

From Latin crucifixus (fixed to a cross), from cruci (cross) + fixus (fixed, fastened).
