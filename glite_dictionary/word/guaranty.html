guaranty

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡærənˌti / (AmE), / ˈɡærənti / (BrE)
a legal promise in which a person or organization agrees to be responsible for another's debt or obligation if that person fails to pay or perform
promise to answer for another's debt or obligation
legal

Examples

The bank required a guaranty before approving the loan to the company.
Her father signed a guaranty to help her secure the apartment lease.
Without a guaranty, lenders are less likely to provide credit to new businesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guarantee - an assurance that certain conditions will be fulfilled

Etymology

From Old French 'garantie' (protection), from garantir (to guarantee), of Germanic origin.
