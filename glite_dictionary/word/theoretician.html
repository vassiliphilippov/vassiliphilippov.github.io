theoretician

1. NOUN

/ ˌθiːərəˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌθɪəriˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
a person who develops or deals mainly with theories rather than practical applications, especially in science or art
person who develops theories

Examples

Many regard Einstein as the greatest theoretician of the twentieth century.
The artist is both a skilled painter and a respected theoretician in the field.
While some focus on experimental research, she considers herself primarily a theoretician.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theorist - a person concerned with the theoretical aspects of a subject
  • Antonym: practitioner - a person actively engaged in an art, discipline, or profession, especially as opposed to theory

Etymology

From theory + -ician (patterned after 'physician', from Latin -icianus, indicating a practitioner).
