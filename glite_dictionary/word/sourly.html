sourly

1. ADV

/ ˈsaʊrli / (AmE), / ˈsaʊəli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows resentment, bitterness, or displeasure.
in a resentful or bitter manner

Examples

She answered sourly, making it clear she disagreed with the decision.
He looked sourly at the success of his rival, unable to hide his jealousy.
The waiter sourly informed us that the kitchen was closed.

Synset Examples

he complained sourly that the new rules only benefitted the managers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitterly - in a bitter or resentful manner
  • Antonym: sweetly - in a pleasant or agreeable manner

Etymology

From "sour" (Old English *sūr*) + "-ly".
