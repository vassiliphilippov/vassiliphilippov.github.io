Chahta language

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːtə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language of the Choctaw people, a member of the Muskhogean language family spoken in the southeastern United States
the language of the Choctaw people

Examples

The Chahta language is still spoken by some Choctaw communities in Mississippi and Oklahoma.
He is studying the grammar of the Chahta language for his linguistics thesis.
Efforts are being made to preserve the Chahta language among younger generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Choctaw language - the language spoken by the Choctaw people

Etymology

Chahta is the self-designation (autonym) of the Choctaw people.
