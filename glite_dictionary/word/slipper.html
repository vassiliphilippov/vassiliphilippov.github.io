slipper

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪpər / (AmE), / ˈslɪpə / (BrE)
A light, low shoe that is easy to put on and take off, usually worn indoors for comfort.
indoor slip-on shoe

Examples

He padded quietly across the kitchen floor in his slippers.
She bought a new pair of fluffy slippers to wear around the house.
Please leave your muddy boots at the door and put on some slippers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoe - outer covering for the foot, typically made of leather, having a sturdy sole and not reaching above the ankle
  • Antonym: boot - type of footwear that covers the foot and ankle and sometimes extends up the leg

Etymology

From Middle English slipar, from slip (to slide) + -er.
