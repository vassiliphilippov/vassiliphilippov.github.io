easygoing

1. ADJ

/ ˌiziˈɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌiːziˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
relaxed and unhurried; proceeding at a slow or gentle pace.
relaxed and unhurried

Examples

We enjoyed an easygoing stroll through the park before dinner.
The class had an easygoing start, allowing students to settle in.
Their journey proceeded at an easygoing pace, with frequent stops to admire the scenery.

Synset Examples

an easy walk around the block
at a leisurely (or easygoing) pace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leisurely - unhurried and relaxed in pace or manner
  • Antonym: hurried - done quickly or with urgency

Etymology

Formed from 'easy' + 'going'; earliest use in the mid-19th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˌiziˈgoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌiːziˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
not demanding or stressful; easily managed, requiring little effort or hardship
not demanding or stressful

Examples

He enjoyed his easygoing job that allowed him plenty of time to relax.
Their lifestyle was so easygoing that every day felt like a vacation.
She preferred the easygoing routine of freelancing over the stresses of full-time work.

Synset Examples

what a cushy job!
the easygoing life of a parttime consultant
a soft job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cushy - (of a task or job) undemanding and easy
  • Synonym: soft - requiring little effort; not strenuous (of a job, assignment, etc.)

3. ADJ

/ ˌiːziˈɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌiːziˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
relaxed and unconcerned in manner or attitude; not easily upset or worried
relaxed and unconcerned

Examples

Despite the stressful environment, she remained easygoing and cheerful throughout the project.
His easygoing nature made him popular among his colleagues and friends.
They appreciated having an easygoing boss who understood their personal needs.

Synset Examples

an easygoing teacher who allowed extra time for assignments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laid-back - relaxed and easygoing in manner
  • Antonym: uptight - anxious or tense in manner

Etymology

From 'easy' (adj.) + 'going' (present participle of 'go').
