Copepoda

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈpɛpədə / (AmE), / kəʊˈpɛpədə / (BrE)
A large group of minute planktonic or parasitic crustaceans found in marine and freshwater habitats.
minute aquatic crustaceans group

Examples

Many species within the Copepoda are important components of marine food webs.
The diversity of Copepoda can be found in both saltwater and freshwater environments.
Scientists study Copepoda to better understand aquatic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copepod - any member of the group Copepoda

Etymology

From Modern Latin, from Greek kōpē (oar) + pous, pod- (foot).

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈpɛpədə / (AmE), / kəʊˈpɛpədə / (BrE)
A subclass of small, usually planktonic or parasitic crustaceans found in both freshwater and marine habitats.
subclass of small planktonic or parasitic crustaceans

Examples

Marine scientists study Copepoda to monitor changes in ocean ecosystems.
Many species within the Copepoda subclass are vital to aquatic food chains.
Some Copepoda are parasitic and can affect the health of fish populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copepod - an individual member of the subclass Copepoda

Etymology

From Greek 'kōpē' (oar) + 'pous' (foot), referring to their oar-like swimming limbs.
