decasyllabic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɛk.ə.sɪˈlæb.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɛk.ə.sɪˈlæb.ɪk / (BrE)
having or consisting of ten syllables, especially in a line of verse
consisting of ten syllables

Examples

The poet crafted a beautiful decasyllabic line for the final stanza.
English heroic verse is often written in decasyllabic meter.
He counted each syllable to ensure the poem was strictly decasyllabic.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hendecasyllabic - having or consisting of eleven syllables
  • Degree Lower: octosyllabic - having or consisting of eight syllables

Etymology

From Late Latin decasyllabus, from Greek deka (ten) + syllabē (syllable).
