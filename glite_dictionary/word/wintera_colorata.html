Wintera colorata

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntərə koʊˈlɒrə.tə / (AmE), / ˈwɪntərə kəˈlɒrətə / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub or small tree with foliage blotched red and yellow, producing small black fruits; native to New Zealand, also known as horopito
New Zealand evergreen shrub or small tree

Examples

The leaves of Wintera colorata are often used as a spice in traditional Maori cuisine.
Botanists studied the medical properties of Wintera colorata for its unique chemical compounds.
A cluster of Wintera colorata stood out among the green ferns in the forest.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Wintera' after Johann Winter, a 16th-century German herbalist; 'colorata' meaning 'colored' in Latin.
