refrain

1. VERB

/ rɪˈfreɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈfreɪn / (BrE)
to deliberately avoid doing something, especially something tempting or undesirable
deliberately avoid doing something

Examples

Please refrain from smoking in this area.
He refrained from making any negative comments during the meeting.
It's difficult to refrain from eating chocolate when it's right in front of you.

Synset Examples

He refrained from hitting him back
she could not forbear weeping

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to forbear - to restrain oneself from doing something

Etymology

From Old French 'refrener', from Latin 'refrenare' (to hold back, check), from 're-' (back) + 'frenum' (bridle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02731589-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈfreɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈfreɪn / (BrE)
the part of a song that is repeated after each verse, often sung by a group of singers with or after the soloist
repeated part of a song

Examples

Everyone joined in during the refrain, filling the hall with music.
The soloist sang the verse, and the choir joined for the refrain.
After each stanza, the refrain brought a sense of unity to the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chorus - the part of a song repeated after each verse; refrain

Etymology

From Old French *refrain* (repetition), from refraindre (to repeat), from Latin *refringere* (to break up, repeat).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07064217-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ rɪˈfreɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈfreɪn / (BrE)
to deliberately avoid doing or consuming something; to abstain
deliberately avoid doing something

Examples

Please refrain from smoking inside the building.
He decided to refrain from eating dessert after dinner.
I try to refrain from criticizing others unless absolutely necessary.

Synset Examples

I abstain from alcohol

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abstain - to deliberately avoid doing or consuming something, especially for moral or health reasons

Etymology

From Old French *refraindre*, from Latin *refrēnāre* ('to hold back, restrain'), from *re-* (again) + *frēnāre* ('to bridle').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01198570-v
Synset POS: v
