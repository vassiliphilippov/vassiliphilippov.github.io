broadax

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊˌdæks / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊˌdaks / (BrE)
a large ax with a broad cutting blade, used especially for shaping timber
a large ax with a broad blade

Examples

The craftsman used a broadax to hew the logs for the cabin walls.
A broadax is essential for traditional timber framing techniques.
He collected antique tools, including a rare nineteenth-century broadax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axe - a tool with a heavy bladed head used for chopping

Etymology

From 'broad' + 'ax', describing the wide blade of the tool.
