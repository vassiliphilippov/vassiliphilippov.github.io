astray

1. ADV

/ əˈstreɪ / (AmE), / əˈstreɪ / (BrE)
away from the correct path or direction; into error or wrongdoing
off the right path; into error

Examples

The hiker went astray after missing the trail marker.
His unconventional ideas led some colleagues astray from standard practice.
Important messages can go astray in such a busy office.

Synset Examples

he was led astray

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amiss - in a mistaken or wrong way
  • Synonym: off-course - not on the correct route or plan

Etymology

From Old French *estraier* (to stray), based on Vulgar Latin *estraiare* (to wander out of the road).

2. ADV

/ əˈstreɪ / (AmE), / əˈstreɪ / (BrE)
away from the correct path, direction, or intended target; in the wrong direction or position
in the wrong direction or position

Examples

The ball went astray and landed in the neighbor's yard.
Occasionally, an email may go astray and never arrive at its destination.
During the delivery, one of the packages went astray and was lost for a week.

Synset Examples

the arrow went wide of the mark
a bullet went astray and killed a bystander

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wide - far from the intended target, especially of a shot or aim

Etymology

From Old French 'estraier' (to stray), from Vulgar Latin *estragare.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00498056-r
Synset POS: r
