ablution

1. NOUN

/ əˈbluːʃən / (AmE), / əˈbluːʃən / (BrE)
the ritual washing of a priest's hands or sacred vessels, especially in religious ceremonies
ritual washing in religious practice

Examples

The priest performed the ablution before handling the sacred vessels.
During Mass, the ablution is an important symbolic act of purification.
The ornate basin was used exclusively for ritual ablution in the old cathedral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purification - the removal of ritual uncleanliness; making pure

Etymology

From Latin ablutio (a washing), from abluere (to wash away), from ab- (away) + lavere (to wash).
