bedrock

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛdˌrɑk / (AmE), / ˈbɛd.rɒk / (BrE)
the solid rock layer that lies beneath soil and loose surface materials
solid rock under soil

Examples

The drill could not penetrate the bedrock beneath the construction site.
Over millions of years, rivers have eroded the soil, exposing the bedrock below.
The stability of the building depends on its foundation being anchored to bedrock.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: subsoil - the layer of soil directly beneath the topsoil

Etymology

From 'bed' + 'rock', indicating the foundational rock layer.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛdˌrɑk / (AmE), / ˈbɛd.rɒk / (BrE)
the basic principles or foundations on which something is based
basic principles or foundation

Examples

Trust is the bedrock of any strong relationship.
Honesty forms the bedrock of her teaching approach.
The constitution is the bedrock of the country’s legal system.

Synset Examples

first you must learn the fundamentals
let's get down to basics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foundation - the basis or groundwork of anything
  • Synonym: fundamentals - essential or primary principles

Etymology

Originally a geological term; "bed" + "rock", referring to the solid rock beneath soil.
