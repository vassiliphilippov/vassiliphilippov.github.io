John Chrysostom

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈkrɪsəˌstɑm / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈkrɪsəstəʊm / (BrE)
(c. 347–407) an early Christian Church Father, renowned preacher, and Archbishop of Constantinople, recognized as a saint and Doctor of the Church.
early Christian preacher and archbishop; saint

Examples

Many sermons by John Chrysostom are still studied by theologians today.
John Chrysostom served as Archbishop of Constantinople in the late fourth century.
The eloquence of John Chrysostom earned him the title 'golden-mouthed' among his contemporaries.

Etymology

From Greek Chrysostomos, meaning 'golden-mouthed', referring to his eloquence in preaching.
