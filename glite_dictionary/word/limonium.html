Limonium

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈmoʊniəm / (AmE), / lɪˈməʊniəm / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants commonly known as sea lavender, found in coastal regions and grown for their ornamental value
genus of plants known as sea lavender

Examples

The florist included stems of Limonium in the bouquet for their delicate purple hue.
Limonium thrives in saline soils along the coastline, making it popular for beach gardens.
Scientists are studying Limonium for its ability to tolerate high salt levels in the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea lavender - common name for plants in the genus Limonium

Etymology

From Latin, based on Greek leimon (meadow), referencing its typical habitat.

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈmoʊniəm / (AmE), / lɪˈməʊniəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants commonly known as sea lavender, widely found in coastal regions.
genus of sea lavenders (plants)

Examples

The salt marsh was covered in blooming Limonium during the summer months.
Botanists identified several species within the Limonium genus along the coastline.
Dried Limonium is often used in floral arrangements for its delicate purple flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea lavender - common name for plants in the genus Limonium

Etymology

From Latin 'limonium', from Ancient Greek 'leimon' (meadow).
