corvee

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˈveɪ / (AmE), / kɔːˈveɪ / (BrE)
a system of unpaid, forced labor imposed by a government or lord, often for public works, as a form of tax or obligation, especially in historical contexts
unpaid forced labor required by authority

Examples

Under the old regime, peasants often had to perform corvee for the maintenance of roads and bridges.
Many societies have used corvee as a means to carry out large construction projects without wages.
The requirement to serve in the corvee was deeply resented by those who had to fulfill it.

Etymology

From French corvée (a day's unpaid labor), from Medieval Latin corrogata (demand for work).
