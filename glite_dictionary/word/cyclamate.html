cyclamate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪkləˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪkləmeɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of cyclamic acid, used especially as an artificial sweetener
artificial sweetener; cyclamic acid salt/ester

Examples

The government banned the use of cyclamate in soft drinks due to health concerns.
Manufacturers often add cyclamate to products as a low-calorie sweetener.
Some sugar substitutes, such as cyclamate, are not approved for use in certain countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sucralose - a synthetic sweetener used as a sugar substitute
  • Synonym: saccharin - another artificial sweetener with a similar purpose
  • Synonym: aspartame - artificial sweetener used in foods and drinks

Etymology

From cyclamic (acid) + -ate, denoting a salt or ester.
