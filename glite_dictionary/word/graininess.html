graininess

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪni.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪni.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of having a rough or granular texture, or being composed of relatively large particles
rough or granular texture

Examples

The graininess of the sandpaper made it perfect for smoothing rough wood surfaces.
She noticed the graininess of the photograph, which gave it an old-fashioned look.
The soup's unpleasant graininess was due to improperly dissolved flour.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of having a smooth, even surface

Etymology

From 'grain' + '-iness', modeled after words like 'happiness'.
