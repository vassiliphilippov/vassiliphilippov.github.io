Wilsonia pusilla

1. NOUN

/ wɪlˈsoʊniə pjuˈsɪlə / (AmE), / wɪlˈsəʊniə pjuˈsɪlə / (BrE)
a small North American wood warbler with yellow plumage and a distinctive black crown, commonly known as Wilson's warbler
a small yellow songbird with a black crown

Examples

We spotted a Wilsonia pusilla flitting among the branches on our morning walk.
The vibrant yellow color of the Wilsonia pusilla makes it easy to recognize in the wild.
During migration season, many birdwatchers hope to catch a glimpse of a Wilsonia pusilla.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wilson's warbler - common name for Wilsonia pusilla, a small yellow warbler

Etymology

From the genus name Wilsonia, honoring American ornithologist Alexander Wilson, and the Latin 'pusilla' meaning 'small, little.'
