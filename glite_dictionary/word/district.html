district

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪstrɪkt / (AmE), / ˈdɪstrɪkt / (BrE)
an area of a country, city, or town that is marked off for administrative, governmental, or other official purposes
administrative area or region

Examples

The school is located in the financial district of the city.
Each district has its own local council and administrative offices.
The city was divided into several districts for the purpose of organizing emergency services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: region - a large area of land, usually without precise boundaries
  • Synonym: zone - an area that is different from others in some way, often for particular use or administration

Etymology

From Latin 'districtus,' past participle of 'distringere' (to draw apart).

2. VERB

/ ˈdɪstrɪkt / (AmE), / ˈdɪstrɪkt / (BrE)
to divide (an area) into districts, especially for the purpose of regulating housing or land use
divide into districts for regulation

Examples

The city council decided to district the suburb to control the types of housing built in each area.
After the new policy, the town was districted to separate residential and commercial zones.
Urban planners are currently districting the city to improve access to public transportation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zone - to divide into or assign to zones, especially for land-use purposes

Etymology

From Latin 'districtus', past participle of 'distringere' (to draw apart).
