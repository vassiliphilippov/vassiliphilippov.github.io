cefadroxil

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛfəˈdrɑksɪl / (AmE), / ˌsɛfəˈdrɒksɪl / (BrE)
a broad-spectrum cephalosporin antibiotic used to treat a variety of bacterial infections
a cephalosporin antibiotic
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed cefadroxil to treat the patient's severe throat infection.
Allergic reactions to cefadroxil are rare but should be reported to your healthcare provider immediately.
Certain strains of bacteria have developed resistance to cefadroxil over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalexin - another cephalosporin antibiotic
  • Synonym: antibiotic - a medicine that inhibits the growth of or destroys microorganisms

Etymology

From 'cephalosporin' + chemical suffix '-droxil', modeled after chemical naming conventions.
