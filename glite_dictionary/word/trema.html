Trema

1. NOUN

/ ˈtriːmə / (AmE), / ˈtriːmə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees in the family Ulmaceae, native to tropical regions of America, Africa, and Asia.
genus of evergreen trees in Ulmaceae

Examples

The Trema tree is found throughout tropical regions, providing shade and habitat for local wildlife.
Scientists documented the rapid growth of young Trema saplings in the rainforest.
Some traditional medicines utilize bark from the Trema for treating minor ailments.

Etymology

From New Latin Trema, the group's scientific name.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtriːmə / (AmE), / ˈtriːmə / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen trees in the family Ulmaceae, native to tropical regions of America, Africa, and Asia.
genus of tropical evergreen trees

Examples

Botanists have identified several species within the Trema genus throughout Southeast Asia.
The Trema tree is known for its rapid growth in tropical climates.
Researchers are studying the ecological impact of Trema species on native forests.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'trēma' (hole), referring to the plant's pitted stone fruit.
