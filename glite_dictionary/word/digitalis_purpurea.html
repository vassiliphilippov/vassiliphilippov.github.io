Digitalis purpurea

1. NOUN

/ dɪdʒɪˈteɪlɪs pɜrˈpjʊriə / (AmE), / dɪdʒɪˈteɪlɪs pjʊəˈrɪə / (BrE)
A tall European plant with clusters of tubular pink-purple flowers, whose leaves yield the drug digitalis and are poisonous to livestock.
a European plant with pink-purple flowers, source of digitalis
medical

Examples

The leaves of Digitalis purpurea are used to make an important heart medication.
Gardeners often warn that Digitalis purpurea is toxic if ingested by pets or livestock.
In early summer, Digitalis purpurea brightens woodland edges with its towering purple spikes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxglove - common name for Digitalis purpurea, a plant with tubular flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'digitalis', meaning 'of a finger' (referring to the flower shape), and 'purpurea', meaning 'purple'.
