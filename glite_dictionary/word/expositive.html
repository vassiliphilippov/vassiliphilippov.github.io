expositive

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈspɑːzɪtɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɒzɪtɪv / (BrE)
serving to explain or expound a subject; intended to present information or clarify meaning
serving to explain or clarify

Examples

The professor gave an expositive lecture that clarified complex theories for the students.
Her expositive essays are valued for their clarity and depth of explanation.
An expositive summary at the start helped readers understand the research findings.

Synset Examples

clean expository writing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expository - serving to explain or describe something

Etymology

From Latin expositivus, from exponere (to expound).
