Vancouver

1. PROPN

/ vænˈkuvər / (AmE), / vænˈkuːvə / (BrE)
a city in southwestern Washington State, USA, located on the Columbia River across from Portland, Oregon.
city in Washington State, USA

Examples

I grew up in Vancouver, just across the river from Portland.
The weather in Vancouver tends to be mild compared to other parts of Washington.
Many people commute from Vancouver to work in Portland every day.

Etymology

Named after British explorer George Vancouver.

2. PROPN

/ vænˈkuːvɚ / (AmE), / vænˈkuːvə / (BrE)
a port city in southwestern British Columbia, Canada, situated on the Pacific Ocean and known as the country's chief Pacific port and third largest city
major city in western Canada

Examples

Many people choose to move to Vancouver because of its mild climate and beautiful scenery.
The 2010 Winter Olympics were held in Vancouver and nearby Whistler.
Vancouver is known for its diverse culinary scene and vibrant arts community.

Etymology

Named after British naval officer George Vancouver (1757–1798).
