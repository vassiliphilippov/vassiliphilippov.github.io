idealised

1. ADJ

/ aɪˈdiəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / aɪˈdɪəlaɪzd / (BrE)
regarded or represented as perfect or better than reality; exalted to an ideal perfection or excellence
regarded as perfect or better than reality
british

Examples

In the novel, the countryside is presented in an idealised manner, far removed from reality.
Many paintings from that era depict women in an idealised form, emphasizing beauty over accuracy.
His idealised vision of family life did not match the complex reality he faced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealized - regarded or represented as perfect or better than reality (US spelling)
  • Antonym: realistic - representing things as they really are

Etymology

From 'ideal' + '-ise' (British spelling), meaning 'to regard as ideal'.
