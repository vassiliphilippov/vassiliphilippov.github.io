printmaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪntˌmeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɪntˌmeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the art or process of creating images by printing, especially on paper, using techniques such as woodcut, etching, lithography, or screen printing
art of making printed images

Examples

He took a course in printmaking at the local art school.
Modern printmaking includes techniques such as etching and lithography.
Her passion for printmaking began after she saw an exhibit of Japanese woodcuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - incising a design onto a hard, flat surface
  • Antonym: painting - art of applying pigment to a surface
