Ecballium

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˈbæliəm / (AmE), / ɛkˈbaliəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the gourd family, known for its fruit that forcibly ejects its seeds when ripe; commonly called the squirting cucumber.
genus of plants known as squirting cucumber

Examples

The fruit of the Ecballium plant bursts open to scatter seeds when fully ripe.
Ecballium is often studied for its unique seed dispersal mechanism in botany classes.
Gardeners sometimes avoid planting Ecballium because its exploding fruit can be messy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirting cucumber - plant whose fruit ejects seeds when ripe

Etymology

From Greek ekballein (to throw out), referring to the plant's method of seed dispersal.

2. NOUN

/ ɛkˈbæliəm / (AmE), / ɛkˈbæliəm / (BrE)
a genus of Mediterranean plants in the gourd family, known for the exploding or squirting cucumber, whose fruit forcibly ejects seeds when ripe
genus of plants known as exploding cucumber

Examples

The fruit of the Ecballium bursts open to scatter its seeds forcefully.
Ecballium is often referred to as the exploding cucumber because of its unique seed dispersal.
Gardeners rarely cultivate Ecballium due to its unusual and messy seed ejection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirting cucumber - a plant species whose fruit ejects seeds forcefully

Etymology

From Greek *ekbállō* (to throw out), referring to the way the fruit ejects its seeds.
