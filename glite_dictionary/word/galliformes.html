Galliformes

1. NOUN

/ ɡæl.əˈfɔr.miːz / (AmE), / ɡæl.ɪˈfɔː.miːz / (BrE)
an order of heavy-bodied ground-feeding birds that includes chickens, turkeys, pheasants, quails, and related species
order of heavy-bodied ground-feeding birds

Examples

Many domesticated birds, such as the Galliformes chicken and turkey, are important sources of food worldwide.
Researchers observed several species of Galliformes in their natural habitats during the spring survey.
The gamekeeper specialized in managing populations of Galliformes on the estate.

Etymology

From Latin gallus (rooster) and -formes (having the form of), indicating 'fowl-shaped'.
