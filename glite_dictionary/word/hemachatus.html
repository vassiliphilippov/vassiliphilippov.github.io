Hemachatus

1. NOUN

/ hɛˈmækətəs / (AmE), / hɪˈmækətəs / (BrE)
A genus of venomous snakes known as rinkhals, native to southern Africa, notable for their ability to spit venom.
a genus of venomous African snakes

Examples

The Hemachatus is well known for its ability to feign death when threatened.
Researchers are studying the venom composition of the Hemachatus for potential medical applications.
The Hemachatus inhabits grasslands and savannas across southern Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rinkhals - a venomous African snake belonging to the genus Hemachatus

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) and khatus (spined, formed as a zoological taxon name).

2. NOUN

/ hɛˈmɑː.kə.təs / (AmE), / hɛˈmak.ə.təs / (BrE)
A genus of venomous snakes in the family Elapidae, comprising the ringhals or ring-necked spitting cobra native to southern Africa.
genus of African spitting cobra

Examples

The Hemachatus genus is known for its ability to spit venom when threatened.
Researchers observed a Hemachatus specimen displaying its characteristic hood.
The only species within Hemachatus is commonly known as the ringhals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ringhals - a highly venomous spitting cobra native to southern Africa

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) and 'chaitē' (mane), referencing the snake's distinctive neck pattern.
