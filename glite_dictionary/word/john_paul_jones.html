John Paul Jones

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn pɔl dʒoʊnz / (AmE), / dʒɒn pɔːl dʒəʊnz / (BrE)
(1747–1792) American naval commander during the American Revolution, noted for his leadership and naval victories against the British.
American naval commander (1747–1792)

Examples

Many historians regard John Paul Jones as a founding father of the United States Navy.
The daring tactics of John Paul Jones inspired future naval officers around the world.
Visitors to the naval museum admired the portrait of John Paul Jones.
