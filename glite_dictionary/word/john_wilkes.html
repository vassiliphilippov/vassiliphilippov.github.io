John Wilkes

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn wɪlks / (AmE), / dʒɒn wɪlks / (BrE)
(1727–1797) An English reformer known for publishing attacks on King George III and supporting the rights of American colonists.
English reformer, 18th century politician

Examples

John Wilkes became a symbol of the struggle for freedom of the press in 18th-century England.
Many historians credit John Wilkes with inspiring later democratic movements.
The life of John Wilkes illustrates the power of outspoken criticism in shaping political reform.
