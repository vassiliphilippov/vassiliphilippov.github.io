thinking

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of using the mind to consider or reason about something carefully
the process of considering something with the mind

Examples

After much thinking, she decided to accept the new job offer.
His thinking on the issue has changed over the years.
Deep thinking is required before making a major life decision.

Synset Examples

thinking always made him frown
she paused for thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thought - idea or opinion produced by thinking or occurring suddenly in the mind
  • Synonym: contemplation - the action of looking thoughtfully at something for a long time

Etymology

Present participle of 'think', from Old English 'þencan'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05778923-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person, animal, or being) having the ability to reason and use intellect.
able to reason; intelligent

Examples

Humans are considered thinking beings because of their ability to reason and solve problems.
As a thinking individual, she weighed the pros and cons before making a decision.
We often refer to dolphins as thinking animals due to their complex behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rational - able to think logically and make decisions based on reason
  • Antonym: unthinking - not showing thought or reason; lacking consideration

Etymology

Present participle of 'think', from Old English 'þencan' (to think).

Synset POS: s
