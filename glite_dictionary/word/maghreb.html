Maghreb

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡrɛb / (AmE), / ˈmaɡ.rɛb / (BrE)
the region of northwest Africa that includes the Atlas Mountains and the coastal areas of Morocco, Algeria, and Tunisia
region of northwest Africa (Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia)

Examples

The cuisine of the Maghreb features spices and flavors unique to North Africa.
Many ancient trade routes passed through the Maghreb region.
She traveled across the Maghreb to study its diverse cultures and landscapes.

Etymology

From Arabic 'al-Maghrib' meaning 'the west' or 'place of sunset'.
