rigid

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɪdʒɪd / (BrE)
not able to be bent or changed in shape; stiff and inflexible.
not able to be bent; stiff

Examples

The engineer chose a rigid material for the bridge to ensure it would not bend under heavy loads.
This plastic pipe is so rigid that it can't be coiled.
Her back remained rigid as she sat perfectly upright in her chair.

Synset Examples

a rigid strip of metal
a table made of rigid plastic
a palace guardsman stiff as a poker
stiff hair
a stiff neck

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flexible - able to bend easily without breaking
  • Synonym: stiff - not easily bent or changed in shape; rigid

Etymology

From Latin 'rigidus' (stiff, hard), from 'rigēre' (to be stiff).

5. ADJ

/ ˈrɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɪdʒɪd / (BrE)
(of an airship) having a shape maintained by a stiff, unyielding framework rather than by internal gas pressure alone.
of an airship, with a stiff structure

Examples

The Hindenburg was a famous rigid airship powered by hydrogen gas.
Unlike blimps, rigid airships contain an internal framework to maintain their shape.
The museum displays a model of a rigid dirigible from the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonrigid - an airship without a rigid internal structure
  • Degree Lower: semi-rigid - an airship with a partial rigid framework

Etymology

From Latin rigidus (stiff, hard), from rigere (to be stiff).
