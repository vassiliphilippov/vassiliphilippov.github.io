continual

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtɪn.ju.əl / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.ju.əl / (BrE)
happening repeatedly and frequently, with only brief interruptions, or occurring without interruption.
repeated or uninterrupted

Examples

The continual noise from the construction site made it difficult to concentrate.
She was frustrated by the continual interruptions during her meeting.
Our team faces continual challenges, but we always find a way to move forward.

Synset Examples

‘continual’ is often used interchangeably with ‘continuous’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: continuous - occurring without any interruption or break

Etymology

From Old French 'continuel,' from Latin 'continuus' (uninterrupted, continuous).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00598545-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ kənˈtɪn.ju.əl / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.ju.əl / (BrE)
recurring regularly or frequently over a period of time, often with brief interruptions in between
recurring frequently; intermittent

Examples

The continual barking of the dog made it hard for anyone to concentrate.
Her continual questions during the meeting became distracting.
Flooding in the area is a continual problem during the rainy season.

Synset Examples

the continual banging of the shutters

Related Terms

  • Antonym: continuous - without interruption or break

Etymology

From Latin 'continualis', from 'continuus' (uninterrupted).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00594915-a
Synset POS: a
