Virginia

1. PROPN

/ vərˈdʒɪniə / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪnɪə / (BrE)
A state in the eastern United States, known as one of the original thirteen colonies and a former Confederate state during the American Civil War.
a state in the eastern United States

Examples

She grew up in Virginia before moving to New York for college.
We spent our vacation exploring the historical sites of Virginia.
The Blue Ridge Mountains are one of the most beautiful parts of Virginia.

Etymology

Named after Queen Elizabeth I of England, known as the 'Virgin Queen'.

2. PROPN

/ vərˈdʒɪn.jə / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪn.i.ə / (BrE)
One of the original British colonies in North America that later became one of the United States, with its colonial capitals at Jamestown and Williamsburg.
former British colony in North America

Examples

The colony of Virginia played a central role in the founding of the United States.
Many early settlers traveled to Virginia seeking land and new opportunities.
Jamestown was the first capital of colonial Virginia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maryland - adjacent British colony in North America, also became a U.S. state
  • Synonym: Jamestown - first permanent English settlement and capital of colonial Virginia

Etymology

Named in honor of Queen Elizabeth I of England, the 'Virgin Queen'.
