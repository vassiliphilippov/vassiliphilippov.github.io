cryostat

1. NOUN

/ ˈkraɪ.əˌstæt / (AmE), / ˈkraɪ.əʊ.stæt / (BrE)
A device used to maintain very low temperatures, often for scientific or medical purposes.
device for maintaining very low temperatures

Examples

The laboratory uses a cryostat to keep tissue samples at ultra-low temperatures.
A malfunction in the cryostat caused the temperature to rise unexpectedly.
Modern medical imaging devices often require a cryostat for sample preparation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermostat - a device that regulates temperature

Etymology

From Greek 'kryos' (icy cold, frost) + 'stat' (standing, regulating).
