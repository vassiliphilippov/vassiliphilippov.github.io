theogony

1. NOUN

/ θiˈɑɡəni / (AmE), / θiˈɒɡəni / (BrE)
the account or study of the origin and genealogy of the gods, especially in mythology
origin and genealogy of the gods

Examples

Hesiod's theogony remains an important source on ancient Greek mythology.
Scholars often compare the theogony of different cultures to understand their beliefs about creation.
The professor delivered a lecture explaining the theogony depicted in early epic poetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmogony - the study of the origin of the universe

Etymology

From Greek theogonia, from theos (god) + -gonia (generation, origin).
