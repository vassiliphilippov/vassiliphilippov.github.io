lookup

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʊkˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈlʊkʌp / (BrE)
an act or process of finding or retrieving specific information, especially by searching in a database, list, or table
an act of retrieving information

Examples

The lookup function quickly retrieved the requested customer data.
Database performance can be improved by optimizing lookup operations.
Her programmer wrote a routine for efficient lookup of keywords.

Synset Examples

they wrote a program to do a table lookup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - the act of looking for information or an item

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'look up', used as a noun since mid-20th century, especially in computing.
