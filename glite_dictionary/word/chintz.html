chintz

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪnts / (AmE), / tʃɪnts / (BrE)
a brightly colored, glazed cotton fabric, often with floral patterns, used especially for upholstery and curtains
brightly printed glazed cotton fabric

Examples

The living room was decorated with chintz curtains featuring bright floral designs.
She recovered the old armchair with a stylish chintz fabric.
Large cushions covered in chintz gave the room a cheerful, vintage feel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calico - plain-woven textile made from unbleached cotton

Etymology

From Hindi *chīnt* (spotted cloth), from Sanskrit *chitra* (variegated, bright).
