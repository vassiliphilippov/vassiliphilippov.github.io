slavery

1. NOUN

/ ˈsleɪvəɹi / (AmE), / ˈsleɪvəri / (BrE)
the system or practice of owning slaves and forcing them to work without pay or rights
the practice of owning slaves

Examples

The abolition of slavery was a turning point in world history.
He studied the impact of slavery on economic development in the southern states.
Many countries still struggle with the legacy of slavery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servitude - the state of being subject to someone more powerful; similar to slavery but broader
  • Antonym: freedom - the state of being free; opposite of slavery

Etymology

From slave + -ry, ultimately from Medieval Latin sclavus (slave).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsleɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈsleɪvəri / (BrE)
the condition of being entirely under the control of another person; a situation in which a person is forced to obey someone else and lacks personal freedom
the state of being controlled by another

Examples

After years in slavery, he finally managed to escape and live freely.
Many activists fought tirelessly to abolish slavery in the nineteenth century.
She described her overwhelming workload as a form of modern slavery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servitude - the state of being a servant or completely subject to someone more powerful
  • Antonym: freedom - the state of being free rather than under the control of another

Etymology

From Old French *esclav* (slave), via Medieval Latin *sclavus* (slave), originally from the ethnonym *Slav*.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsleɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈsleɪvəri / (BrE)
very hard work done under harsh conditions for little or no pay, comparable to being treated as a slave
work done under harsh, exploitative conditions

Examples

Many factory workers endured slavery for years before labor reforms improved their conditions.
The documentary exposed the modern forms of slavery still present in some industries.
He compared his exhausting schedule to slavery, though he knew it wasn't truly the same.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servitude - the condition of being a servant or subject, often with limited freedom
  • Antonym: freedom - the state of being free, not under the control or power of another

Etymology

From slave + -ery, patterned after words signifying conditions or systems (e.g., peasantry).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
