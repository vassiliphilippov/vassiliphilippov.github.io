celandine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlənˌdaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsiːləndaɪn / (BrE)
A North American annual plant, typically with yellow or orange flowers, that grows chiefly on wet, acidic soil.
North American plant with yellow or orange flowers

Examples

The wet meadow was dotted with blooming celandine in early spring.
She identified the bright yellow flowers as celandine during the nature walk.
Celandine thrives in areas with acidic, moist soil.

Etymology

From Latin 'chelidonium', from Greek 'khelidōn' meaning 'swallow (bird)'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlənˌdaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsiːləndiːn / (BrE)
A perennial herb with branched woody stems and bright yellow flowers, commonly found in Europe and Asia.
a perennial herb with yellow flowers

Examples

We found a patch of celandine blooming beside the stream in early spring.
Gardeners often appreciate how easily celandine spreads in shady areas.
The bright yellow flowers of celandine add color to woodland walks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greater celandine - a related species, Chelidonium majus, also known as greater celandine
  • Synonym: lesser celandine - a related species, Ficaria verna, also known as lesser celandine

Etymology

From Latin *chelīdonia*, from Greek *khelidṓn* (swallow, the bird), as the plant was said to flower when swallows arrived.
