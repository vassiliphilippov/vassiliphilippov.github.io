Phoca vitulina

1. PROPN

/ ˌfoʊkə vɪˈtuːlɪnə / (AmE), / ˌfəʊkə vɪˈtjuːlɪnə / (BrE)
A small spotted seal found in the coastal waters of the Northern Hemisphere, commonly known as the harbor seal.
harbor seal species

Examples

The Phoca vitulina is frequently seen basking on rocks along the coast.
Marine biologists study the behavior of Phoca vitulina in their natural habitats.
Sightings of Phoca vitulina increase during the breeding season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harbor seal - A common name for Phoca vitulina

Etymology

From Latin: 'phoca' meaning 'seal', 'vitulina' meaning 'of a calf' (from resemblance to a calf's eyes).
