square-rigger

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɛr ˌrɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈskwɛː ˌrɪɡə / (BrE)
a sailing ship with the principal sails set on horizontal yards hung across the mast
type of sailing ship with square-rigged sails

Examples

The old square-rigger glided gracefully into the harbor.
During the 1800s, many cargo vessels were square-riggers.
We toured a restored square-rigger at the maritime museum.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: schooner - a sailing ship with two or more masts, typically with the foremast smaller than the mainmast

Etymology

From 'square-rigged' (having square sails) + 'rig' (the arrangement of a ship's sails and masts).
