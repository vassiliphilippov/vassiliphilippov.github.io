Delichon urbica

1. NOUN

/ dɛˈlaɪkən ɜrˈbiːkə / (AmE), / dɛˈlɪkɒn ˈɜːbɪkə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the house martin, a small European bird in the swallow family that nests under the eaves of buildings
European house martin (bird species)

Examples

Every spring, Delichon urbica returns to the same nesting sites under the eaves.
Scientists monitor Delichon urbica populations to study changes in the environment.
The white rump and forked tail make Delichon urbica easy to identify in flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: house martin - common small European swallow, Delichon urbica

Etymology

From Greek 'delichon' (swallow) and Latin 'urbica' (of the city).
