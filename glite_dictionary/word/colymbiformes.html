Colymbiformes

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪmbɪˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / kəˌlɪmbɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
An order of aquatic diving birds known as grebes, characterized by lobed toes and excellent swimming abilities.
order of aquatic diving birds (grebes)

Examples

The Colymbiformes are adapted to life on the water, with unique lobed feet.
Taxonomists once classified all grebes within the order Colymbiformes.
Ornithological studies of Colymbiformes have revealed fascinating diving behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grebe - a freshwater diving bird of the family Podicipedidae

Etymology

From Modern Latin, combining Greek 'kolymbos' (diver) and Latin '-formes' (having the form of).
