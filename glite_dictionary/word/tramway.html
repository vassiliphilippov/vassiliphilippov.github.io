tramway

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræmˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈtræm.weɪ / (BrE)
a set of rails or tracks on which trams or streetcars travel
tracks for trams or streetcars

Examples

The old tramway was removed to make space for a new road.
A narrow tramway runs through the city center, connecting several districts.
Construction workers repaired the damaged tramway after last night's storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: track - a set of rails for trains or trams

Etymology

From 'tram' (a type of rail vehicle) + 'way' (path or road).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtræmˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈtræm.weɪ / (BrE)
A system for transporting passengers or goods in carriers suspended from cables that are supported by towers.
aerial cable transport system

Examples

The mountain tramway gives visitors breathtaking views as it carries them to the summit.
A new tramway was installed to transport goods across the valley.
She was excited to ride the tramway over the snowy peaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cable car - a vehicle that hangs from and is moved by a cable

Etymology

From English 'tram' (a shaft or bar), and 'way' (a path or route).
