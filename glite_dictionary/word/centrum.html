centrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.trəm / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.trəm / (BrE)
the thick, central part of a vertebra, forming its main body and bearing most of its weight.
main body of a vertebra
medical

Examples

The intervertebral disc sits between each centrum, providing cushioning and flexibility to the spine.
The fossilized centrum revealed important details about the dinosaur's vertebral structure.
In the anatomy lecture, the professor highlighted the role of the centrum in supporting the spinal column.

Etymology

From Latin centrum, from Ancient Greek kentron (center, point).
