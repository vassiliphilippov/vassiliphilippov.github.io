reciprocity

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌsɪˈprɑːsəti / (AmE), / rɪˌsɪˈprɒsɪti / (BrE)
the practice or state of exchanging things with others for mutual benefit, especially in terms of privileges, actions, or influence
mutual exchange or influence

Examples

The agreement between the two nations was based on reciprocity, with each granting trading rights to the other.
She believed in reciprocity and always returned favors when she could.
Academic partnerships rely heavily on reciprocity to ensure mutual benefit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutuality - the sharing of a feeling or action between two or more parties
  • Antonym: one-sidedness - lack of mutual exchange or involvement from only one side

Etymology

From Latin reciprocus (moving back and forth) via reciprocatio.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˌsɪˈprɑːsəti / (AmE), / rɪˌsɪˈprɒsəti / (BrE)
the practice of exchanging things with others for mutual benefit, especially privileges granted by one country or organization to another
mutual exchange of privileges or benefits

Examples

The treaty was based on reciprocity, with each country lowering tariffs for the other.
Academic cooperation between the universities relies on the principle of reciprocity.
Without reciprocity, trade agreements are often considered unfair by the parties involved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutualism - exchange or cooperation for mutual benefit
  • Antonym: one-sidedness - lack of mutual benefit or exchange

Etymology

From Latin reciprocus (returning the same way), influenced by the suffix -ity.
