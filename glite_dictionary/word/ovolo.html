ovolo

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvəˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvələʊ / (BrE)
A convex molding with a cross section that forms a quarter circle or ellipse, used especially in architecture.
convex architectural molding

Examples

The ornate ceiling featured a prominent ovolo running along the cornice.
Restorers carefully matched the original ovolo moldings during the renovation.
In classical architecture, the ovolo is often used as a decorative element above doors and windows.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cavetto - a concave molding with a cross section like a quarter circle

Etymology

From Italian 'ovolo', diminutive of 'uovo' (egg), from Latin 'ovum' (egg), suggesting the rounded shape.
