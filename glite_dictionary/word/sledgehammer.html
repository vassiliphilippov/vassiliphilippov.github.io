sledgehammer

1. VERB

/ ˈslɛdʒˌhæmər / (AmE), / ˈslɛdʒˌhamə / (BrE)
to hit or strike forcefully with a sledgehammer.
strike with a sledgehammer

Examples

The workers sledgehammered the old wall to make room for the new extension.
He sledgehammered the frozen ground to break it apart.
After hours of effort, they finally sledgehammered the boulder into pieces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hammer - to hit forcefully with a hammer
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈslɛdʒˌhæmər / (AmE), / ˈslɛdʒˌhamə / (BrE)
a large, heavy hammer with a long handle, used for tasks such as driving stakes or breaking stones
a heavy long-handled hammer

Examples

He used a sledgehammer to break down the old concrete wall.
The workers swung the sledgehammer with all their strength.
It takes skill and caution to use a sledgehammer safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maul - a heavy hammer or mallet

Etymology

From sledge (heavy hammer) + hammer; 'sledge' from Old English 'slecg' (hammer).
