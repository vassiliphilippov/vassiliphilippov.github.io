ram

1. NOUN

/ ræm / (AmE), / ræm / (BrE)
a heavy tool or device used to drive or force something in by impact
tool for forcing or driving something by impact

Examples

The engineer used a ram to drive the steel post deep into the ground.
Make sure the ram is properly positioned before applying force to the pile.
The construction crew operated a hydraulic ram to demolish the wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hammer - a tool with a heavy head used for breaking things or driving in nails

Etymology

From Old English 'ramm', related to Old Norse 'rammr' (strong), referencing the forceful action.
