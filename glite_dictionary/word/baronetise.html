to baronetise

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑːrənˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbærənətaɪz / (BrE)
to confer the rank or title of baronet upon someone
make someone a baronet
british

Examples

After years of public service, he was finally baronetised by the crown.
The king chose to baronetise several loyal supporters who had funded his campaign.
It was rare for someone outside the nobility to be baronetised in those days.

Synset Examples

He was baronetized for his loyalty to the country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knight - confer the rank of knight upon someone

Etymology

From baronet + -ise; patterned after similar titles (e.g. knight, knighted).
