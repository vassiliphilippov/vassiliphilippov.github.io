Amelanchier

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmɪˈlæŋkiər / (AmE), / ˌæmɪˈlænkiə / (BrE)
A genus of North American deciduous trees or shrubs, typically bearing small edible berries and white flowers; commonly known as serviceberry, shadbush, or Juneberry.
genus of North American deciduous trees or shrubs

Examples

The Amelanchier in our yard produces sweet berries every spring.
Gardeners often plant Amelanchier for its attractive blossoms and edible fruit.
Several species of Amelanchier are valued for landscaping and wildlife habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serviceberry - North American tree or shrub of the genus Amelanchier
  • Synonym: shadbush - Another common name for Amelanchier plants
  • Synonym: Juneberry - Another common name for various Amelanchier species

Etymology

From French *amelanchier*, meaning serviceberry tree.
