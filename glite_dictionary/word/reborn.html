reborn

1. ADJ

/ riˈbɔrn / (AmE), / riˈbɔːn / (BrE)
Having undergone a spiritual renewal or conversion, especially in a religious sense.
spiritually renewed or converted

Examples

After attending the retreat, she felt entirely reborn and committed to her faith.
He described himself as a reborn Christian after his transformative experience.
Many people seek a sense of purpose through becoming reborn in their spiritual lives.

Synset Examples

a born-again Christian

Related Terms

  • Synonym: born-again - having undergone a spiritual rebirth or radical religious conversion

2. ADJ

/ riˈbɔrn / (AmE), / riˈbɔːn / (BrE)
restored to a state of renewed existence, strength, or importance; revived or given new life
restored to new life or vigor

Examples

After years of neglect, the historic theater has been reborn as a vibrant cultural center.
The city felt reborn after the successful completion of urban renewal projects.
Her passion for painting was reborn after she enrolled in an art class.

Synset Examples

a revitalized economy
a revitalized inner-city neighborhood
Berlin has been reborn after probably the most intense period of construction since the post-war period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revived - given new strength or energy; brought back to life or consciousness
  • Synonym: restored - brought back to a former or original state
  • Antonym: stagnant - showing no activity or development; dull and inactive

Etymology

From re- (again) + born, past participle of 'bear' (to be born).
