to amass

1. VERB

/ əˈmæs / (AmE), / əˈmæs / (BrE)
to collect or gather a large amount of something over a period of time
collect or gather in large quantities

Examples

Over the years, she managed to amass a fortune through wise investments.
Dust and clutter amass quickly if you don’t clean regularly.
The company sought to amass enough evidence to support its claim.

Synset Examples

Journals are accumulating in my office
The work keeps piling up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accumulate - to gather or collect, often in gradual degrees
  • Synonym: gather - to bring together or collect from different places
  • Antonym: to disperse - to scatter or spread widely

Etymology

From Latin 'amassare', from 'massa' (mass, lump).

2. VERB

/ əˈmæs / (AmE), / əˈmæs / (BrE)
to gather together or accumulate a large amount or number of things, especially over a period of time
to gather or accumulate in large amounts

Examples

Over the years, he managed to amass a remarkable collection of rare coins.
They amassed significant wealth through wise investments and careful planning.
The researcher is amassing valuable data for her groundbreaking study.

Synset Examples

I am accumulating evidence for the man's unfaithfulness to his wife
She is amassing a lot of data for her thesis
She rolled up a small fortune

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accumulate - to gather or collect, especially over time
  • Synonym: collect - to bring or gather things together
  • Antonym: to disperse - to distribute or spread over a wide area; to scatter

Etymology

From Old French *amasser* (to collect), from Vulgar Latin *ammassāre* (to gather into a mass).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
