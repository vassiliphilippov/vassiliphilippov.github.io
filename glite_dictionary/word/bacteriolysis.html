bacteriolysis

1. NOUN

/ bækˌtɪriˈɑləsɪs / (AmE), / bækˌtɪəriˈɒləsɪs / (BrE)
the destruction or dissolution of bacterial cells, especially by the action of a specific agent such as an antibody or enzyme
destruction of bacteria
medical

Examples

The scientist studied bacteriolysis to better understand the mechanism of antibacterial agents.
Penicillin induces bacteriolysis in susceptible bacterial strains, leading to cell destruction.
An immune response may result in bacteriolysis through the action of specific antibodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lysis - the disintegration of a cell by disruption of its membrane

Etymology

From New Latin: 'bacterium' + Greek 'lysis' (loosening, dissolving).
