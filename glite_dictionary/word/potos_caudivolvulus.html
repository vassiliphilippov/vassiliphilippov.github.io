Potos caudivolvulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊtəs ˌkɔːdɪˈvɒlvjələs / (AmE), / ˈpəʊtɒs ˌkɔːdɪˈvɒlvjʊləs / (BrE)
An arboreal fruit-eating mammal native to tropical America, known for its long prehensile tail; also called the kinkajou.
arboreal mammal of tropical America (kinkajou)

Examples

The Potos caudivolvulus uses its prehensile tail to swing gracefully through the rainforest canopy.
Although rarely seen, the Potos caudivolvulus plays a key role in dispersing seeds in its habitat.
Scientists observed a Potos caudivolvulus feeding on ripe bananas during their field study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kinkajou - another name for Potos caudivolvulus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Potos' (genus name) and 'caudivolvulus' meaning 'with a twining tail.'
