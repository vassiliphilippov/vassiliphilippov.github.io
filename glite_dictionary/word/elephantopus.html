Elephantopus

1. NOUN

/ ɛlɪˈfæntəpəs / (AmE), / ɛlɪˈfæntəpʊs / (BrE)
a genus of perennial herbs native to America, with broad basal leaves and small flower heads, commonly known as elephantsfoot.
genus of perennial American herbs

Examples

The species Elephantopus tomentosus is commonly found in open woodlands across the southeastern United States.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Elephantopus plants in the laboratory.
Gardeners appreciate Elephantopus for its unique foliage and hardiness in native plant gardens.

Etymology

From Greek 'elephas' (elephant) and 'pous' (foot), referring to the shape of the leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛləˈfæntəˌpʊs / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪˈfæntəˌpʊs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs native to the Americas, belonging to the daisy family, known for their basal leaves and small flower heads.
a genus of American perennial herbs

Examples

Species in the Elephantopus genus are commonly used in traditional medicine.
The botanist identified the plant as Elephantopus scaber after examining its leaves.
Many Elephantopus species thrive in open, sunny fields across the Americas.

Etymology

From Greek 'elephas', 'elephantos' (elephant) and 'pous', 'podos' (foot), referring to the shape of the plant's leaves.
