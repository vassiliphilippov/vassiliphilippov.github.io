cankerweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæŋkərˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈkaŋkəˌwiːd / (BrE)
A herbaceous plant native to northeastern North America, with drooping clusters of yellowish-white flowers; sometimes classified in the genus Prenanthes.
herb with drooping yellowish-white flowers

Examples

The fields were filled with cankerweed blooming in late summer alongside other wildflowers.
Some traditional remedies use cankerweed for its purported medicinal properties.
Gardeners often mistake cankerweed for a weed due to its vigorous growth.
