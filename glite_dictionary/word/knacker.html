knacker

1. NOUN

/ ˈnækər / (AmE), / ˈnækə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to buy and slaughter old or injured horses, usually for their by-products such as meat, hides, or glue
person who slaughters old horses

Examples

The old mare was sold to the knacker after she could no longer work.
Many rural communities had a local knacker who dealt with unfit animals.
After years of faithful service, the horse met its end at the hands of a knacker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slaughterer - a person who kills animals for food or other products

Etymology

Probably from knacker (slang for 'harness-maker' or 'horse slaughterer'), of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnækər / (AmE), / ˈnakə / (BrE)
a person who buys old buildings or ships to dismantle them and recover usable materials
person who salvages materials from old structures

Examples

The knacker arrived to survey the old warehouse before starting the demolition.
After the ship ran aground, a knacker was hired to break it up for parts.
Several knackers made their living dismantling derelict structures in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salvager - a person who salvages things, especially from wrecks

Etymology

From obsolete English 'knack' (to strike or knock), or possibly from Dutch 'knakken' (to break).
