color-blind

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌlər ˌblaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə ˌblaɪnd / (BrE)
Unable to distinguish certain colors, especially red and green, due to a deficiency in the color-sensing cells of the eyes.
unable to distinguish certain colors

Examples

Because he is color-blind, he finds it difficult to tell the difference between red and green traffic lights.
Being color-blind hasn't stopped her from becoming a talented artist.
Many tests can determine if a child is color-blind at an early age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color vision deficiency - inability to see certain colors

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌlərˌblaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈkʌləˌblaɪnd / (BrE)
not influenced by racial prejudice; treating all people equally, regardless of race
unprejudiced regarding race

Examples

The organization prides itself on having a color-blind approach to hiring.
She believes that adopting a color-blind attitude helps create a more inclusive environment.
Their admissions policy is explicitly color-blind to promote equal opportunity for everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impartial - treating all people and groups equally, without bias
  • Antonym: prejudiced - having or showing a dislike or distrust that is derived from prejudice
