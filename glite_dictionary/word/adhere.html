to adhere

1. VERB

/ ədˈhɪr / (AmE), / ədˈhɪə / (BrE)
To continue to behave according to a particular rule, plan, or set of beliefs without deviation.
to follow a rule or plan strictly

Examples

Despite several setbacks, the committee continued to adhere to its original timeline.
It is important that all members adhere to the agreed-upon rules during the competition.
She promised to adhere to her training schedule no matter what happened.

Synset Examples

They adhered to their plan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to comply - to act in accordance with rules or requests
  • Antonym: deviate - to move away from a plan or standard

Etymology

From Latin 'adhaerēre', meaning to stick to.

6. VERB

/ ədˈhɪr / (AmE), / ədˈhɪə / (BrE)
to stick firmly to a surface or substance, or to remain attached and resist separation
stick or hold fast

Examples

The paint will only last if it adheres properly to the surface.
Glue helps the wallpaper adhere to the wall for many years.
The sticker adhered so well that it was hard to remove.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cling - stick tightly or firmly to something
  • Synonym: to cohere - stick together, especially as parts of the same substance
  • Antonym: to detach - to separate or remove from something else
  • Antonym: to loosen - to make less firmly attached; to release from being tight or stuck

Etymology

From Latin *adhaerēre* (to stick to), from *ad-* (to) + *haerēre* (to stick).
