ranch

1. VERB

/ rænʧ / (AmE), / rɑːnʧ / (BrE)
to manage or run a ranch, especially one used for raising cattle, horses, or other livestock
manage or run a ranch

Examples

After moving to Montana, they decided to ranch instead of working office jobs.
He has been ranching in Texas for over a decade and loves the open land.
Many families in the region have ranched cattle for generations.

Synset Examples

Her husband is ranching in Arizona

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farm - to work on or manage a farm
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ræntʃ / (AmE), / rɑːntʃ / (BrE)
a large farm, especially in North America or Australia, where cattle or other animals are bred and raised
large livestock farm

Examples

They own a huge ranch in Texas where they raise hundreds of cattle.
The children enjoyed riding horses during their stay at the ranch.
He left the city to manage his family's ranch in Montana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farm - an area of land used for growing crops or raising animals

Etymology

From Spanish 'rancho' meaning 'group of people who eat together' or 'hut', adapted in American English for cattle farms.
