disorientation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsˌɔriɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˌɔːriɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a state of confusion about time, place, or identity, often leading to uncertainty about direction or action
a state of confusion about location or direction

Examples

The sudden loss of power in the tunnel led to momentary disorientation among the passengers.
After waking from anesthesia, she experienced disorientation and needed help remembering where she was.
The map helped reduce his disorientation in the unfamiliar city.

Synset Examples

his disorientation was the result of inattention

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - a state of being unclear or uncertain
  • Antonym: orientation - the determination of the relative position of something or someone

Etymology

From French 'désorientation', based on 'orienter' (to orient, to position).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsˌɔriənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˌɔːriənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a state in which someone experiences a wild delusion, especially one induced by a hallucinogenic drug
wild delusion, often drug-induced

Examples

The patient suffered from disorientation after ingesting the hallucinogenic mushrooms.
During the experiment, some participants reported vivid disorientation and altered perceptions of reality.
Prolonged use of the drug can lead to episodes of disorientation characterized by wild delusions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallucination - an experience involving the apparent perception of something not present
  • Synonym: delusion - a false belief or judgement about external reality

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart) + orientare (to arrange, direct to the east).
