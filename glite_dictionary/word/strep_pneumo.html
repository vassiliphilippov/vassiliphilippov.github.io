Strep pneumo

1. NOUN

/ strɛp ˈnuːmoʊ / (AmE), / strɛp ˈnjuːməʊ / (BrE)
an informal abbreviation for Streptococcus pneumoniae, a type of bacteria that can cause pneumonia and other respiratory infections
abbreviation for Streptococcus pneumoniae bacterium
medical informal

Examples

The doctor ordered a blood test to check for Strep pneumo infection.
Vaccination against Strep pneumo is recommended for young children and older adults.
Many cases of bacterial pneumonia are caused by Strep pneumo.

Synset Examples

Doctors often test for Strep pneumo in pneumonia cases.
Vaccination can help prevent infections caused by Strep pneumo.
Strep pneumo can lead to serious respiratory infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Streptococcus pneumoniae - the full name of the bacterium that causes pneumonia

Etymology

Shortened from 'Streptococcus pneumoniae', with 'Strep' from the genus name and 'pneumo' from 'pneumonia'.
