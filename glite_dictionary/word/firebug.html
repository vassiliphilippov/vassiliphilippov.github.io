firebug

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌbʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəbʌɡ / (BrE)
a small, brightly colored bug, especially Pyrrhocoris apterus, often found in warm regions and known for its vivid red and black markings
brightly colored insect, especially Pyrrhocoris apterus

Examples

The garden was full of firebugs crawling on the sunny pavement.
A firebug can be recognized by its striking red and black body.
Children often collect firebugs because of their vivid colors.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌbʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌbʌɡ / (BrE)
a person who illegally sets fire to property; an arsonist
person who sets fires illegally
informal

Examples

The abandoned warehouse was destroyed by a firebug last night.
Police are searching for the firebug responsible for a string of arson attacks in the neighborhood.
As a child, he was fascinated by flames, but as an adult, he became a notorious firebug.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsonist - a person who deliberately sets fire to property

Etymology

From 'fire' + 'bug', with 'bug' used in the sense of 'person obsessed with'; dates to late 19th century U.S. slang.
