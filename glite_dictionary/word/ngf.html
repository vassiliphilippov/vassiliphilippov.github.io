NGF

1. NOUN

/ ɛn dʒi ɛf / (AmE), / ɛn dʒi ɛf / (BrE)
a protein that promotes the growth and survival of nerve cells, especially in the peripheral nervous system
protein involved in nerve cell growth
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered that NGF plays a critical role in repairing damaged nerves.
Low levels of NGF have been linked to certain neurodegenerative diseases.
The study examined how NGF affects sensory neuron development in mice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve growth factor - protein involved in nerve cell growth and survival

Etymology

Abbreviation of nerve growth factor.
