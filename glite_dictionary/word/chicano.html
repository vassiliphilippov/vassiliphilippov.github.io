Chicano

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈkɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / tʃɪˈkɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
a person living in the United States who has Mexican descent, often referring to those embracing a distinct Mexican-American identity
person of Mexican descent in the US

Examples

Her father identifies as a proud Chicano and has always celebrated Mexican culture.
Several Chicano artists contributed to the mural project in East Los Angeles.
The Chicano movement played a key role in civil rights history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mexican American - an American citizen of Mexican descent

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'chicano', shortened form of 'mexicano' (Mexican).
