Pitymys pinetorum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtɪˌmɪs paɪnəˌtɔrəm / (AmE), / ˈpɪtɪmɪs ˌpɪnɪˈtɔːrəm / (BrE)
A species of short-tailed, glossy-furred, burrowing vole native to the eastern United States, commonly known as the woodland vole.
a vole species of eastern U.S.

Examples

The Pitymys pinetorum is often found in moist woodland areas where it builds extensive burrow systems.
Researchers observed the feeding habits of Pitymys pinetorum during the summer months.
Farmers sometimes consider Pitymys pinetorum a pest due to its impact on root crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodland vole - common name for Pitymys pinetorum

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Pitymys' and species name 'pinetorum', meaning 'of the pines'.
