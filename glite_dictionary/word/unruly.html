unruly

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈruːli / (AmE), / ʌnˈruːli / (BrE)
noisy or difficult to control; lacking discipline or restraint
difficult to control; lacking discipline

Examples

The unruly children refused to listen to their teacher and kept shouting.
Police struggled to control the unruly crowd after the concert ended.
His unruly hair stuck out in every direction despite his attempts to comb it.

Synset Examples

a boisterous crowd
a social gathering that became rambunctious and out of hand
a robustious group of teenagers
beneath the rumbustious surface of his paintings is sympathy for the vulnerability of ordinary human beings
an unruly class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disorderly - not behaving in a controlled or calm way
  • Synonym: boisterous - noisy, energetic, and rough
  • Antonym: obedient - doing what one is told; willing to obey

Etymology

From un- (not) + Middle English 'reule' (rule), from Old French 'riule', from Latin 'regula' (rule).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈruːli / (AmE), / ʌnˈruːli / (BrE)
difficult to control or discipline; disorderly or uncontrollable.
difficult to control; disorderly

Examples

The unruly crowd refused to disperse despite police efforts.
She struggled to tame her unruly hair before the meeting.
The teacher found the class particularly unruly after lunch.

Synset Examples

the little boy's parents think he is spirited, but his teacher finds him unruly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disorderly - lacking organization or control; behaving in a disruptive way
  • Antonym: manageable - able to be controlled or handled easily

Etymology

From un- (not) + obsolete Middle English reuly (regulated, ruled), from Old French reule (rule).

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈruːli / (AmE), / ʌnˈruːli / (BrE)
difficult to control or discipline; unwilling to submit to authority or rules.
difficult to control; disobedient

Examples

The unruly children disrupted the classroom with their constant talking and refusal to follow directions.
Police were called to break up the unruly crowd after the match ended in a draw.
His unruly hair refused to stay in place no matter how much gel he used.

Synset Examples

unruly teenagers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disobedient - refusing to obey rules or authority
  • Antonym: orderly - well-behaved and obedient to rules or authority

Etymology

From un- (not) + obsolete 'ruly', from Middle English 'reule' (rule).
