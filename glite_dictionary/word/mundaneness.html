mundaneness

1. NOUN

/ mʌnˈdeɪnnəs / (AmE), / mʌnˈdeɪnnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being ordinary, unremarkable, or lacking excitement
quality of being ordinary or unremarkable

Examples

She longed to escape the mundaneness of her daily routine and experience something new.
The novel explores the mundaneness of suburban life with sharp detail and wit.
Sometimes, a little mundaneness can bring comfort and stability during uncertain times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ordinariness - the quality of being ordinary
  • Synonym: commonplaceness - the state of being commonplace
  • Antonym: remarkableness - the quality of being remarkable

Etymology

From 'mundane', from Latin 'mundanus' (of the world, worldly).

2. NOUN

/ mənˈdeɪnnəs / (AmE), / mʌnˈdeɪnnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being mundane; ordinariness or lack of excitement.
the state of being ordinary or unremarkable

Examples

Some people long to escape the mundaneness of everyday life by traveling to exotic places.
He was frustrated by the mundaneness of his daily routine at the office.
Despite the mundaneness of the chores, she found a sense of peace in repetition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ordinary - with no special or distinctive features; normal
  • Antonym: excitement - the state of being emotionally aroused or stirred up

Etymology

From Latin 'mundanus' (of the world); 'mundane' + '-ness'.
