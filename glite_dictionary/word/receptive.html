receptive

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
willing to listen to or accept new ideas, suggestions, or influences.
willing to accept ideas or suggestions

Examples

She is receptive to constructive criticism and welcomes suggestions for improvement.
The manager was receptive to our proposals for streamlining the workflow.
Children are especially receptive to learning new languages at a young age.

Synset Examples

receptive to the proposals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas
  • Antonym: resistant - unwilling to accept new ideas or suggestions

Etymology

From Latin 'receptivus', from 'receptus' (received).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01992330-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
willing to consider or accept new suggestions, arguments, or ideas; open-minded or responsive to change.
open and willing to consider new ideas

Examples

The manager was receptive to suggestions from her team about improving workflows.
Children are especially receptive to learning new languages at a young age.
He found his audience surprisingly receptive to his innovative ideas.

Synset Examples

receptive to reason and the logic of facts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas, unprejudiced
  • Antonym: resistant - unwilling to accept new ideas or change

Etymology

From Latin receptivus (fit for receiving) from recipere (to receive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01777965-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ rɪˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
(of a nerve fiber or impulse) carrying signals from the body's periphery toward the central nervous system.
carrying signals toward the central nervous system
medical

Examples

An afferent neuron is also known as a receptive neuron in medical literature.
The receptive fibers transmit sensory information to the brain for processing.
Damage to receptive nerves can reduce sensations like pain and temperature.

Synset Examples

sensory neurons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afferent - (of nerves or impulses) conducting signals toward the central nervous system
  • Antonym: efferent - (of nerves or impulses) carrying signals away from the central nervous system

Etymology

From Latin recipere (to receive), via Late Latin receptivus (fit for receiving).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00334509-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ rɪˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
Able to absorb liquid or take up a substance, especially without repelling it.
Able to absorb liquid

Examples

The filter has a receptive surface that allows rapid absorption of water.
This type of fabric is especially receptive to dye, resulting in brighter colors.
The artist prefers paper that is highly receptive to ink for detailed drawings.

Synset Examples

the paper is ink-receptive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorbent - able to soak up liquid easily
  • Antonym: repellent - resisting or preventing absorption

Etymology

From Latin 'receptivus' (fit for receiving), from 'recipere' (to receive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00007227-s
Synset POS: s
