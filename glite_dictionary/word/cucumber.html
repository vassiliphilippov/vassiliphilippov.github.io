cucumber

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːkʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈkjuːkʌmbə / (BrE)
A long, green-skinned fruit with crisp, white flesh, typically eaten raw as a vegetable or used for pickling.
edible green-skinned fruit used as a vegetable

Examples

She sliced a fresh cucumber for the salad.
The cucumber plants grew quickly in the summer heat.
A cold cucumber sandwich is perfect for a light lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melon - sweet fleshy fruit of the gourd family, related plant

Etymology

From Middle English 'cucumer', from Old French 'cocombre', from Latin 'cucumis'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkjuː.kʌm.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.kʌm.bə / (BrE)
A long, green-skinned, edible fruit with watery flesh, eaten raw or pickled, produced by a climbing plant of the gourd family.
a long, green edible fruit

Examples

He sliced a fresh cucumber for the salad.
The cucumber plant climbs a trellis in our garden every summer.
She prefers her sandwiches with thin cucumber slices for a crisp texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gherkin - a small variety of cucumber, often pickled

Etymology

From Old French *cocombre*, from Latin *cucumis* (cucumber).
