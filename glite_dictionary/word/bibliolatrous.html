bibliolatrous

1. ADJ

/ ˌbɪbliˈɑlətrəs / (AmE), / ˌbɪblɪˈɒlətrəs / (BrE)
Showing excessive reverence or worship for the Bible.
excessively revering the Bible

Examples

Some critics view the group's bibliolatrous beliefs as a barrier to open discussion and change.
He was described as bibliolatrous for his literal interpretation of every biblical passage.
A bibliolatrous approach can sometimes hinder broader religious understanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bibliolatry - excessive reverence for the Bible

Etymology

From 'bibliolatry', 19th century, combining 'biblio-' (book, the Bible) and '-latry' (worship from Greek 'latreia').
