picayune

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɪkiˈjun / (AmE), / ˌpɪkəˈjuːn / (BrE)
(informal) Of little value or importance; petty or trivial.
of little value; petty
informal

Examples

He spent hours arguing over the most picayune details of the contract.
Her complaints about the service were so picayune that no one took them seriously.
Let's not get bogged down with picayune issues when there are bigger problems to solve.

Synset Examples

a fiddling sum of money
a footling gesture
our worries are lilliputian compared with those of countries that are at war
a little (or small) matter
a dispute over niggling details
limited to petty enterprises
piffling efforts
giving a police officer a free meal may be against the law, but it seems to be a picayune infraction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
  • Synonym: petty - of minor importance; trivial
  • Antonym: significant - important, having meaning or influence

Etymology

From French 'picaillon', a small coin, later used in American English for something trivial.
