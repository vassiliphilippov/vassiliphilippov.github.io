Rudolf Hess

1. PROPN

/ ˈruːdɑlf hɛs / (AmE), / ˈruːdɒlf hɛs / (BrE)
A German Nazi leader (1894–1987) who, in 1941, flew solo to Scotland in an attempt to negotiate peace with Great Britain but was imprisoned for life.
Nazi leader who flew to Scotland in 1941

Examples

Rudolf Hess was imprisoned in Spandau Prison after the Second World War.
Historians still debate the reasons behind Rudolf Hess's solo flight to Scotland in 1941.
The trial of Rudolf Hess at Nuremberg lasted for months.

2. PROPN

/ ˈruːdɑlf hɛs / (AmE), / ˈruːdɒlf hɛs / (BrE)
(1894–1987) A German Nazi leader and close associate of Adolf Hitler, known for his 1941 solo flight to Scotland in an attempt to negotiate peace with Britain, for which he was imprisoned for life.
German Nazi leader, 1941 Scotland flight

Examples

Rudolf Hess shocked the world when he secretly flew to Scotland in 1941.
The trial at Nuremberg found Rudolf Hess guilty and sentenced him to life imprisonment.
Historians continue to debate the motives behind Rudolf Hess's solitary flight during World War II.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Adolf Hitler - Dictator of Nazi Germany, leader of the Nazi Party
  • Synonym: Heinrich Himmler - Senior Nazi leader; head of the SS
