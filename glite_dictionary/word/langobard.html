Langobard

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæŋɡoʊˌbɑrd / (AmE), / ˈlæŋɡəbɑːd / (BrE)
a member of a Germanic people who invaded and settled in northern Italy in the 6th century
member of a Germanic people in early medieval Italy

Examples

The Langobards played a crucial role in the history of medieval Italy.
Archaeologists have uncovered artifacts believed to belong to the Langobard people.
Many northern Italian traditions can trace their roots to the rule of the Langobards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lombard - member of a Germanic people who settled in northern Italy

Etymology

From Latin Langobardus, from Proto-Germanic *Langabardaz meaning 'long beard.'
