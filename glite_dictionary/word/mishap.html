mishap

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsˌhæp / (AmE), / ˈmɪshæp / (BrE)
an unlucky accident or a small piece of bad luck; a minor misfortune
an unlucky accident; minor misfortune

Examples

She arrived late to the interview due to a minor mishap with her car.
There was a mishap in the kitchen, but fortunately nobody was hurt.
During the picnic, a small mishap occurred when someone spilled the drinks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accident - an unexpected and undesirable event
  • Antonym: fortune - chance or luck as an external force

Etymology

From Old French *meshap* (misfortune), from *mes* (badly) + *hap* (chance, luck), from Old Norse *happ* (good luck).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsˌhæp / (AmE), / ˈmɪshap / (BrE)
an unlucky accident or minor misfortune, often unexpected and not serious
unlucky accident or misfortune

Examples

There was a small mishap in the kitchen, but thankfully nobody was hurt.
The hikers managed to complete their journey without any serious mishap.
He laughed off the mishap and continued with his speech.

Synset Examples

if I didn't have bad luck, I wouldn't have any luck at all

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accident - an unexpected and undesirable event
  • Degree Higher: disaster - a sudden event causing great damage or suffering
  • Antonym: fortune - good luck; success or prosperity

Etymology

From Middle French *meshap* (bad luck), from *mes-* (wrongly) + *hap* (chance, luck).
