Sardinian

1. ADJ

/ sɑrˈdɪniən / (AmE), / sɑːˈdɪniən / (BrE)
of or relating to Sardinia, its people, or their language.
relating to Sardinia or its people/language

Examples

The Sardinian coast is famous for its beautiful beaches.
Many Sardinian traditions are still preserved in rural villages.
He is proud of his Sardinian heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sardinian - the Romance language spoken in Sardinia

Etymology

From Latin 'Sardinius', relating to Sardinia.

2. NOUN

/ sɑrˈdɪniən / (AmE), / sɑːˈdɪniən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Sardinia, an island region of Italy
a person from Sardinia

Examples

The ancient Sardinians developed unique traditions on their Mediterranean island.
My grandfather is a proud Sardinian who still speaks the regional dialect.
Many Sardinians celebrate traditional festivals that date back centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Italian - a native or inhabitant of Italy

Etymology

From Latin Sardiniānus, meaning 'of or pertaining to Sardinia'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10571713-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ sɑrˈdɪniən / (AmE), / sɑːˈdɪniən / (BrE)
the Romance language spoken in Sardinia, sometimes considered a distinct language from Italian, with many Spanish loanwords
Romance language of Sardinia

Examples

Many people in central Sardinia still speak Sardinian as their first language.
Some linguists argue that Sardinian should be classified as a separate language rather than as an Italian dialect.
The influence of Spanish can be heard in many words of modern Sardinian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Italian - the Romance language of Italy, related to Sardinian
  • Synonym: Spanish - the Romance language of Spain, a source of loanwords in Sardinian

Etymology

From Sardinia (island in the Mediterranean) + -ian.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06977342-n
Synset POS: n
