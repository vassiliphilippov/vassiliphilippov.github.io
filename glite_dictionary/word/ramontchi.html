ramontchi

1. NOUN

/ rəˈmɒntʃi / (AmE), / rəˈmɒntʃi / (BrE)
A small shrubby tree native to Madagascar, cultivated in tropical regions for its deep red, acidic, plum-like fruits and as a hedge plant.
tropical tree grown for its small plum-like fruits

Examples

The ramontchi is often planted as a natural fence in tropical gardens.
Children enjoy picking the tart fruit of the ramontchi tree when it ripens each summer.
Local farmers cultivate ramontchi for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
