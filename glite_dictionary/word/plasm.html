plasm

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæzəm / (AmE), / ˈplɑːzəm / (BrE)
the clear, cell-free fluid part of blood or lymph in which blood cells are suspended
cell-free fluid in blood or lymph
medical

Examples

The researchers extracted plasm from the blood samples for further analysis.
Diseases like leukemia can alter the composition of the plasm in a patient's bloodstream.
The technician noted that the plasm appeared unusually cloudy, indicating possible infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plasma - the liquid component of blood that holds cells in suspension

Etymology

From Greek 'plasma' (something molded, formed).

2. NOUN

/ ˈplæzəm / (AmE), / ˈplaz(ə)m / (BrE)
the protoplasm within germ cells that contains the chromosomes and genes; the living substance from which hereditary material is transmitted
protoplasm in germ cells carrying genetic material
medical

Examples

The hereditary characteristics are passed down through the plasm of germ cells.
Researchers studied the plasm to learn more about gene transmission.
Many classic genetics experiments focus on differences in the plasm among various organisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protoplasm - the living substance of a cell

Etymology

From Greek plasmos (something molded), via Late Latin plasma (something formed or molded).
