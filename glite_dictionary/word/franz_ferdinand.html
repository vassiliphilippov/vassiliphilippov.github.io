Franz Ferdinand

1. PROPN

/ fræns ˈfɜrdɪnænd / (AmE), / frants ˈfɜːdɪnænd / (BrE)
Archduke of Austria whose assassination in 1914 triggered the outbreak of World War I.
Austrian archduke assassinated in 1914

Examples

Franz Ferdinand was assassinated in Sarajevo in 1914, an event that sparked World War I.
Many historians believe that the death of Franz Ferdinand dramatically altered the course of the twentieth century.
Franz Ferdinand, heir to the Austro-Hungarian throne, was a key figure in early twentieth-century European politics.
