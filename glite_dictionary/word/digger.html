digger

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡə / (BrE)
a machine used for excavating earth or other material, typically with a scoop or bucket
excavating machine

Examples

The construction site was busy with a large digger clearing soil.
They rented a digger to help install the new swimming pool in the backyard.
Before building the road, workers used a digger to remove the old foundations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excavator - a large machine for digging and moving earth
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to dig, especially earth or soil
person who digs as a job

Examples

After the storm, several diggers were hired to clear the debris from the streets.
The digger spent hours creating trenches for the new water pipes.
During the construction project, a skilled digger is essential for making progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excavator - a person or machine that digs or removes earth

Etymology

From the verb 'dig' + -er suffix, denoting a person who digs.
