Indus

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪn.dəs / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dəs / (BrE)
a major river in South Asia, originating in Tibet and flowing through India and Pakistan to the Arabian Sea
major river in South Asia

Examples

The Indus River was vital to one of the world’s earliest civilizations.
Much of Pakistan’s agriculture relies on water from the Indus.
Archaeologists have discovered ancient cities along the banks of the Indus.

Synset Examples

the valley of the Indus was the site of an early civilization

Etymology

From Latin Indus, from Ancient Greek Ἰνδός (Indós), from Sanskrit सिन्धु (Sindhu), meaning ‘river’ or the Indus River.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɪn.dəs / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dəs / (BrE)
a faint constellation in the southern sky, located near Telescopium and Tucana
southern sky constellation

Examples

The constellation Indus is best observed during the late summer months in the southern hemisphere.
Early astronomers charted Indus along with other faint southern constellations.
Through my telescope, I tried to spot the main stars that form Indus.

Etymology

Latin, meaning 'the Indian', named in the 16th century after a figure representing a native of the East Indies or the Americas.
