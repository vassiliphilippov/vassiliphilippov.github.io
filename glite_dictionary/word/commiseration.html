commiseration

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmɪzəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌmɪzəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an expression or feeling of sympathy for someone who is experiencing distress or sorrow
expression of sympathy for another's grief

Examples

She offered her commiseration to the family after hearing about their loss.
Messages of commiseration poured in from friends and colleagues worldwide.
He expressed his commiseration for the team after their disappointing defeat.

Synset Examples

they sent their condolences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condolence - an expression of sympathy, especially on the occasion of a death

Etymology

From Latin commiseratio, from commiserari (to pity).

2. NOUN

/ kəˌmɪzəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌmɪzəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of sympathy and sorrow for the misfortunes or suffering of others.
feeling of sympathy for others' misfortunes

Examples

She offered her commiseration to the family after hearing the sad news.
Messages of commiseration poured in from colleagues and friends.
His genuine commiseration made her feel less alone in her hardship.

Synset Examples

the blind are too often objects of pity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sympathy - feelings of pity and sorrow for someone else's misfortune
  • Synonym: pity - the feeling of sorrow and compassion caused by the suffering of others
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest, concern, or sympathy

Etymology

From Latin commiseratio (sympathy), from commiserari (to pity).
