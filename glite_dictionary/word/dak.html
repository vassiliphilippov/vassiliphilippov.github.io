dak

1. NOUN

/ dɑk / (AmE), / dɑːk / (BrE)
An East Indian tree (Butea monosperma) known for its bright vermilion, velvet-textured flowers and its yellow dye.
East Indian tree with bright vermilion flowers

Examples

The brilliant red flowers of the dak tree bloom in spring across central India.
Traditional artisans use dye extracted from the dak for coloring fabrics.
Birds are often seen feeding on the nectar of the dak blossoms.

Etymology

From Hindi ḍhāk, referring to the tree Butea monosperma.
