Lythraceae

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈθreɪsiˌi / (AmE), / lɪˈθreɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants including herbs, shrubs, and small trees, typically with pink or purple flowers
a family of flowering plants
medical

Examples

Many ornamental garden plants belong to the Lythraceae family.
The Lythraceae are known for their showy, brightly colored blossoms.
Botanists study members of the Lythraceae for their unique floral structures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: purslane family - Portulacaceae, a different family of flowering plants sometimes confused with Lythraceae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Lythrum, from Greek lythron (gore, possibly referring to color).
