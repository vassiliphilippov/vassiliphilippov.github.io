Hygieia

1. PROPN

/ haɪˈdʒiːə / (AmE), / hɪˈdʒiːə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the goddess of health, cleanliness, and hygiene; daughter of Aesculapius and sister of Panacea.
Greek goddess of health and hygiene

Examples

Many ancient temples were dedicated to Hygieia, reflecting her importance in promoting health and cleanliness.
In Greek mythology, Hygieia was often depicted holding a serpent and a bowl.
The word 'hygiene' is derived from the name of the goddess Hygieia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Panacea - Greek goddess of universal remedy, sister of Hygieia
  • Synonym: Aesculapius - Greek god of medicine, father of Hygieia

Etymology

From Greek Ὑγιεία (Hygieía), personification of health, related to the word for 'health' (ὑγίεια).
