Orectolobus barbatus

1. NOUN

/ ɔːrɛkˈtɒləbəs bɑːrˈbeɪtəs / (AmE), / ɒrɛkˈtɒləbəs bɑːˈbeɪtəs / (BrE)
a species of shark of the western Pacific, known for its flattened body, mottled skin, and distinctive tasseled appearance around the mouth
a shark species with tassels around the mouth

Examples

Divers were excited to spot an Orectolobus barbatus resting among the coral reefs.
The Orectolobus barbatus uses its tassels to camouflage itself on the ocean floor.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of Orectolobus barbatus in coastal Australian waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tasseled wobbegong - common name for Orectolobus barbatus

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Orectolobus' (meaning 'stretched lobe'), species name 'barbatus' (meaning 'bearded').
