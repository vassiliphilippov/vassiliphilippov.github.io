pasteurellosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæs.tʃə.rɛˈloʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌpæs.tjʊə.rɛˈləʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
an infectious disease, mainly affecting animals and sometimes humans, characterized by pneumonia or blood infection caused by bacteria of the genus Pasteurella.
infectious disease caused by Pasteurella bacteria
medical

Examples

Veterinarians are concerned about the spread of pasteurellosis among livestock in the region.
Symptoms of pasteurellosis in rabbits include nasal discharge, respiratory distress, and lethargy.
The diagnosis of pasteurellosis was confirmed by isolating Pasteurella bacteria from the patient's blood.

Etymology

From Pasteurella (the genus name, after French chemist Louis Pasteur) + -osis (denoting a disease).
