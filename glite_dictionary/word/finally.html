finally

1. ADV

/ ˈfaɪ.nə.li / (AmE), / ˈfaɪ.nəli / (BrE)
after a long time or delay; at the end of a process or series of events
after a long time or delay

Examples

After months of searching, he finally found a suitable apartment.
They finally reached the summit just before sunset.
She finally admitted that she was wrong.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eventually - after a long time, especially after many difficulties

Etymology

From Latin finalis (of or relating to an end), via Old French and Middle English.

2. ADV

/ ˈfaɪnəli / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnəli / (BrE)
At the end of a process, series, or period of time; eventually or ultimately.
at the end; after a long process

Examples

After hours of discussion, they finally reached a decision that satisfied everyone.
The book was difficult to finish, but I finally completed it last night.
Spring finally arrived after a long and harsh winter.

Synset Examples

ultimately he had to give in
at long last the winter was over

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultimately - in the end, after a long process
  • Synonym: eventually - in the end, especially after a delay
  • Antonym: initially - at the beginning

Etymology

From Latin finalis (of an end), from finis (end).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00048441-r
Synset POS: r
