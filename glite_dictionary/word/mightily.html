mightily

1. ADV

/ ˈmaɪ.t̬ɪ.li / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.tɪ.li / (BrE)
To a great degree; very much; with great force or intensity.
to a great degree; very much

Examples

She was mightily relieved to hear the good news after a stressful week.
Despite being underestimated, he worked mightily to prove everyone wrong.

2. ADV

/ ˈmaɪ.t̬ɪ.li / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.tɪ.li / (BrE)
in a powerful or vigorous way; with great strength or effort
in a powerful or vigorous way

Examples

He worked mightily to finish the project ahead of schedule.
The river flowed mightily after the heavy rains.
She argued mightily for her team's proposal during the meeting.

Synset Examples

he strove mightily to achieve a better position in life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerfully - in a strong or forceful manner
  • Antonym: feebly - in a weak or lacking strength manner

Etymology

From Middle English 'mightily', from 'might', meaning strength or power, plus adverbial -ly.
