Hecate

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛk.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈhɛk.əti / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a goddess associated with magic, witchcraft, the night, and later the underworld, often depicted as a protector of witches.
Greek goddess of magic and the underworld

Examples

In Shakespeare's play, Hecate is portrayed as the mistress of witches.
Worshippers would leave offerings for Hecate at crossroads to seek her favor.
Many ancient spells invoked the powerful name of Hecate for protection and guidance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Persephone - Greek goddess of the underworld, associated with Hecate

Etymology

From Greek Hekátē (Ἑκάτη), name of the goddess.
