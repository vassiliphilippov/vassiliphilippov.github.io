ethnocentric

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛθ.noʊˈsɛn.trɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛθ.nəʊˈsɛn.trɪk / (BrE)
having or based on the attitude that one's own ethnic group is superior or central to others
regarding one's own ethnic group as central

Examples

The documentary criticizes the ethnocentric viewpoints that shape much of mainstream history.
Her analysis was considered ethnocentric, as it focused only on Western cultural norms.
It is important to recognize and move beyond ethnocentric biases when studying world cultures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: xenocentric - preferring or valuing cultures other than one's own
  • Synonym: chauvinistic - showing excessive or prejudiced support for one's own cause, group, or sex
  • Synonym: nationalistic - having strong identification with one's own nation

Etymology

From Greek 'ethnos' (nation, people) + 'centric' (centered).
