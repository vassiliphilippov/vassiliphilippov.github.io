steak

1. NOUN

/ steɪk / (AmE), / steɪk / (BrE)
A thick slice of meat, especially beef, or a large fish, typically cut for grilling or frying.
thick slice of meat or fish

Examples

He ordered a juicy steak cooked medium rare for dinner.
The chef recommends grilling the steak over a high flame for best results.
She prefers salmon steak to red meat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beefsteak - a steak cut from beef
  • Degree Lower: fillet - a boneless cut of meat or fish

Etymology

From Old Norse *steik* (roast meat), related to the verb *steikja* (to roast).
