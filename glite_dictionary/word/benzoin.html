benzoin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnzoʊɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɛnzɔɪn / (BrE)
a balsamic resin obtained from certain trees, used especially in medicine for treating skin irritation and in perfumery
a gum resin used in medicine and perfumery
medical

Examples

The nurse applied a small amount of benzoin to the wound to protect the skin.
Traditional ointments often contain benzoin for its soothing properties.
Benzoin is sometimes used as a fixative in making perfumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storax - a similar balsamic resin sometimes used as a substitute for benzoin

Etymology

From French *benjoin*, from Arabic *lubān jāwi* (frankincense of Java).
