humane

1. ADJ

/ hjuˈmeɪn / (AmE), / hjʊˈmeɪn / (BrE)
showing evidence of moral and intellectual advancement; characterized by compassion, sympathy, or consideration for others
showing compassion and consideration

Examples

The shelter uses humane methods to care for animals in need.
We need a more humane approach to prison reform.
Her humane attitude inspired others to treat everyone with greater respect.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inhumane - lacking compassion or kindness
  • Synonym: compassionate - showing sympathy and concern for others

Etymology

From Latin humanus (human, kindly).

2. ADJ

/ hjuˈmeɪn / (AmE), / hjʊˈmeɪn / (BrE)
showing compassion or kindness, especially toward people or animals in order to alleviate suffering.
compassionate; alleviating suffering

Examples

The organization works hard to ensure that animals are treated in a humane manner.
Many activists campaign for more humane treatment of prisoners.
Putting down injured wildlife in a humane way is sometimes necessary to prevent further suffering.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inhumane - lacking compassion or kindness; cruel
  • Synonym: compassionate - showing sympathy and concern for others' suffering

Etymology

From Latin humanus (human, kind, humane).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01264939-a
Synset POS: a
