babirusa

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːbəˈruːsə / (AmE), / ˌbæbɪˈruːsə / (BrE)
A large, wild pig native to Indonesia, notable for its long, curved canine teeth that grow upward through the snout.
Indonesian wild pig with curved tusks

Examples

The babirusa is easily identified by its extraordinary tusks that curl back toward its forehead.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered babirusa in Sulawesi.
A babirusa can sometimes be seen in specialized exhibits at large zoos.

Etymology

From Malay 'babi rusa' meaning 'deer pig'.
