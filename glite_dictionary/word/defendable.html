defendable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfɛndəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛndəbəl / (BrE)
Able to be defended against attack, criticism, or challenge.
able to be defended

Examples

The city was situated on a hill, making it highly defendable during ancient times.
She did not think her actions were defendable in any way.
The lawyer argued that his client’s position was both reasonable and defendable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defensible - able to be defended against attack or challenge
  • Antonym: indefensible - impossible to defend against attack or challenge

Etymology

From Latin 'defendere' (to ward off, defend) with English '-able' suffix.
