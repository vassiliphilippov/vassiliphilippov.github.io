unresolved

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnrɪˈzɑlvd / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈzɒlvd / (BrE)
(of a problem, conflict, or question) not settled, solved, or concluded.
not settled or solved

Examples

The issue of funding for the new building remains unresolved.
There were several unresolved questions after the meeting ended.
The mystery surrounding her disappearance is still unresolved.

Synset Examples

many crimes remain unsolved
many problems remain unresolved

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solved - settled or resolved (especially of a problem)

Etymology

From un- (not) + resolved, from Latin resolvere (to loosen, solve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02274920-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈzɑlvd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈzɒlvd / (BrE)
(of a problem, question, or situation) not yet settled or brought to a conclusion; open to further discussion or action.
not settled or concluded

Examples

The dispute between the two companies remains unresolved after months of negotiation.
There are still unresolved issues that need to be discussed at tomorrow's meeting.
The cause of the mysterious disappearance is still unresolved by the investigators.

Synset Examples

an open question
our position on this bill is still undecided
our lawsuit is still undetermined

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resolved - settled or brought to a conclusion
  • Synonym: settled - concluded; arranged or decided

Etymology

Formed from prefix un- (not) and resolved, from Latin resolvere (to loosen, resolve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02138201-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈzɑːlvd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈzɒlvd / (BrE)
(of a musical sound or chord) characterized by dissonance and lacking a final harmonic resolution.
musical: dissonant or unresolved

Examples

The symphony's opening features an unresolved chord that creates tension and expectation.
Some jazz pieces deliberately leave progressions unresolved to evoke a sense of ambiguity.
The unresolved dissonance at the end leaves listeners wanting a clear conclusion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissonant - lacking harmony; clashing in sound, especially in music
  • Antonym: resolved - (in music) having harmonic resolution; not dissonant
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01168105-s
Synset POS: s
