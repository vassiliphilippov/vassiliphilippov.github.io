aestivation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛstəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌiːstɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
(Botany) The arrangement of sepals and petals in a flower bud before it opens.
arrangement of floral parts in a bud

Examples

The type of aestivation can help botanists identify different species of plants.
Scientists study aestivation to understand how flower buds develop before blooming.
Certain plants display a unique form of aestivation that influences their pollination strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vernation - the arrangement of young leaves within a bud

Etymology

From Latin aestivatio (summer residence), from aestivare (to spend the summer), referring to a period of dormancy or arrangement during summer.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛstəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌiːstɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
(Zoology) A state of inactivity or dormancy in animals during hot or dry periods, characterized by slowed metabolism and reduced activity.
summer dormancy in animals
medical

Examples

Many snails enter aestivation to survive dry, hot summers.
The desert frog’s aestivation can last for several months without rainfall.
Some fish exhibit aestivation as an adaptation to drought conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hibernation - Dormancy during winter in animals

Etymology

From Latin aestivatio ('summering'), from aestivare ('to spend the summer'), from aestas ('summer').
