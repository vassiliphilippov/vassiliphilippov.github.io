vena saphena

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪnə səˈfiːnə / (AmE), / ˌviːnə səˈfiːnə / (BrE)
Either of two major superficial veins in the leg, responsible for draining blood from the foot.
major superficial vein of the leg
medical

Examples

The surgeon located the vena saphena during the procedure to ensure proper blood flow.
Varicose veins often affect the vena saphena, causing swelling and discomfort.
An ultrasound can help visualize the vena saphena in patients with suspected venous insufficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great saphenous vein - the larger of the two saphenous veins in the leg
  • Synonym: small saphenous vein - the smaller of the two saphenous veins in the leg

Etymology

From Latin 'vena saphena', where 'vena' means 'vein' and 'saphena' means 'clearly visible'.
