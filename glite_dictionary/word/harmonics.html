harmonics

1. NOUN

/ hɑrˈmɑnɪks / (AmE), / hɑːˈmɒnɪks / (BrE)
the branch of music or physics concerned with the study of musical sounds, especially their overtones and structure
the study of musical sound and overtones

Examples

She is taking a course in harmonics to better understand the science of music.
The textbook covers basic concepts in harmonics and acoustics.
Musicians often study harmonics to improve the quality of their sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acoustics - the science of sound, including production, transmission, and effects

Etymology

From Greek *harmonikos* (musical, harmonious) via Latin *harmonica*.
