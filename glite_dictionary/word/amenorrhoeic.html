amenorrhoeic

1. ADJ

/ əˌmɛn.əˈri.ɪk / (AmE), / əˌmɛn.əˈriː.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by amenorrhea; having an abnormal absence of menstrual periods for reasons other than pregnancy
affected by absence of menstruation
medical

Examples

The doctor noted that the patient was amenorrhoeic for over six months.
Certain eating disorders can result in women becoming amenorrhoeic.
The amenorrhoeic state may be temporary or persistent depending on the underlying cause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amenorrhea - absence of menstrual periods

Etymology

From 'amenorrhea' (absence of menstruation) + suffix '-ic', from Greek 'a-' (not) + 'men' (month) + 'rhoia' (flow).
