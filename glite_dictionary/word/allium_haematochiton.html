Allium haematochiton

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm ˌhiːmətoʊˈkaɪtən / (AmE), / ˈæliəm ˌhiːmətəʊˈkaɪtən / (BrE)
A species of wild onion with white to deep red outer tunic, native to California.
species of wild onion native to California

Examples

The foothills in spring are dotted with blooming Allium haematochiton.
Many botanists study Allium haematochiton for its unique coloration and adaptation to dry climates.
We found several clusters of Allium haematochiton along the California hiking trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild onion - a general term for uncultivated onion plants

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Allium' (onion) and Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'chiton' (tunic), referring to the reddish outer covering.
