Augustan

1. ADJ

/ ɔˈɡʌstən / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡʌstən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the period of the Roman Emperor Augustus, especially in terms of its art, literature, or culture
of the era of Roman Emperor Augustus

Examples

The poet's style was distinctly Augustan, mirroring the values of ancient Rome.
Many masterpieces of literature emerged during the Augustan Age.
Her interest in Augustan architecture was sparked by her studies of Roman history.

Synset Examples

the Augustan Age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classical - relating to the culture of ancient Greece and Rome

Etymology

From Augustus, the title of the first Roman emperor (Latin Augustus, meaning 'venerable').
