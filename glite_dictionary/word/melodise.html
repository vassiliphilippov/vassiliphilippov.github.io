to melodise

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛləˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛlədaɪz / (BrE)
to add or supply a melody to (a piece of music or lyrics)
add or supply a melody to something
british

Examples

The composer decided to melodise the simple lyrics to make a catchy song.
She carefully melodised the poem, turning it into a beautiful ballad.
It's challenging to melodise a speech intended for spoken delivery rather than singing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to melodize - to add or supply a melody to (American spelling)

Etymology

From melody + -ise (British spelling), compare American English 'melodize'.
