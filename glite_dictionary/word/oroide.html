oroide

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔroʊˌaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɔɪd / (BrE)
an alloy of copper, tin, and zinc, used especially to imitate gold in jewelry and decorative items
alloy imitating gold in jewelry

Examples

The counterfeit coins were made of oroide, giving them a deceptively golden appearance.
Many costume jewelry pieces from the 19th century were crafted from oroide.
Although it looks like real gold, this brooch is actually made of oroide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brass - a yellow alloy of copper and zinc, sometimes used in imitation gold jewelry

Etymology

From French 'oroïde', from Spanish 'oro' (gold) + Greek '-eidos' (form, appearance).
