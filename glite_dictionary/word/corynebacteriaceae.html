Corynebacteriaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrɪnɪˌbæktəˌriːəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪniˌbæktəˌriːəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
A large family of mostly Gram-positive, aerobic, nonmotile rod-shaped bacteria of the order Eubacteriales.
family of rod-shaped bacteria
medical

Examples

The physician identified the presence of Corynebacteriaceae in the patient's wound culture.
Species within the Corynebacteriaceae family are commonly found in soil and water.
Researchers are studying antibiotic resistance among various members of the Corynebacteriaceae family.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Corynebacterium (a genus) with -aceae, a standard suffix for bacterial families.

2. NOUN

/ kɔˌrɪnəbæktɪriˈeɪsiaɪ / (AmE), / kɒˌrɪnɪbækˌtɪəriˈeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
a family of mostly Gram-positive, aerobic, nonmotile rod-shaped bacteria in the order Eubacteriales
family of rod-shaped, Gram-positive bacteria
medical

Examples

The Corynebacteriaceae family includes several medically important bacteria.
Researchers studied the morphology of Corynebacteriaceae under the microscope.
Members of the Corynebacteriaceae are known for their rod-shaped appearance and nonmotility.

Etymology

From the genus Corynebacterium, from Greek korynē (club) + bacterium, with -aceae as a standard suffix for bacterial families.
