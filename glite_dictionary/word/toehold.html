toehold

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈtəʊˌhəʊld / (BrE)
A small support or ledge just large enough for the toe to rest on, used especially in climbing.
small foothold for climbing

Examples

The climber searched for a toehold on the sheer rock face.
She found a narrow toehold that helped her pull herself up.
Without a proper toehold, making progress up the wall was nearly impossible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foothold - a secure position from which further progress can be made, especially in climbing

Etymology

Formed from 'toe' + 'hold'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈtəʊˌhəʊld / (BrE)
A wrestling hold in which the opponent's toe is grasped and the leg is twisted to apply pressure to the joints.
wrestling hold twisting the opponent's toe and leg

Examples

The wrestler secured a toehold and forced his opponent to submit.
Applying a toehold can be an effective way to immobilize an opponent in a match.
She avoided the toehold by quickly shifting her leg out of reach.

Etymology

From 'toe' + 'hold', describing the part of the body gripped and the nature of the move.

/ ˈtoʊˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈtəʊˌhəʊld / (BrE)
a small or initial position that provides an opportunity for future advancement or progress
initial position for future progress

Examples

After months of searching, the company finally gained a toehold in the international market.
His internship gave him a toehold in the competitive tech industry.
The new product gave the brand a crucial toehold in the luxury sector.

Synset Examples

his father gave him a toehold in the oil business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foothold - a secure position from which progress can be made

Etymology

Formed from 'toe' + 'hold', originally referring to a small foothold for one's toe; later used metaphorically.
