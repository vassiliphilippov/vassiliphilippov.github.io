capricious

1. ADJ

/ kəˈprɪʃəs / (AmE), / kəˈprɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior; determined by chance or impulse rather than by reason
unpredictably changeable; impulsive

Examples

The CEO's capricious decisions caused uncertainty among employees.
Her capricious moods made it difficult to predict her reactions.
Weather in this region can be capricious, changing from sun to storm in an hour.

Synset Examples

a capricious refusal
authoritarian rulers are frequently capricious
the victim of whimsical persecutions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whimsical - playfully fanciful or unpredictable; similar meaning
  • Synonym: impulsive - acting suddenly without forethought; similar meaning
  • Antonym: steadfast - resolutely firm and unwavering; opposite meaning
  • Synonym: fickle - changing frequently, especially regarding loyalties; similar meaning

Etymology

From French capricieux, from Italian capriccioso, from capriccio (whim).

2. ADJ

/ kəˈprɪʃəs / (AmE), / kəˈprɪʃəs / (BrE)
subject to sudden and unpredictable changes, especially in mood or behavior
given to sudden changes

Examples

The manager's capricious decisions made it difficult for the team to plan ahead.
The capricious weather ruined our picnic with sudden rain showers.
Her capricious mood changes surprised everyone at the party.

Synset Examples

a capricious summer breeze
freakish weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fickle - likely to change frequently, especially regarding one's loyalties or affections
  • Antonym: stable - not likely to change; steady or firmly established

Etymology

From French capricieux, from Italian capriccioso, from capriccio (whim).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01848219-s
Synset POS: s
