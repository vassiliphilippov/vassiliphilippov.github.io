enamel

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnæməl / (AmE), / ɪˈnæməl / (BrE)
the hard, white, outer layer that covers and protects the crown of a tooth
hard outer layer of a tooth
medical

Examples

Brushing your teeth helps to keep the enamel strong and free from decay.
Certain acidic foods and drinks can erode tooth enamel over time.
Dentists recommend fluoride toothpaste to protect enamel and prevent cavities.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dentin - the dense tissue forming the bulk of a tooth beneath the enamel

Etymology

From Old French 'esmail', from Frankish *smalt (melting); influenced by Germanic roots.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈnæməl / (AmE), / ɪˈnaməl / (BrE)
a type of paint that dries to form a hard, glossy surface
paint with a hard, shiny finish

Examples

The kitchen cabinets were coated in a layer of white enamel that made them easy to clean.
After painting the model airplane with blue enamel, he left it to dry overnight.
You should use enamel paint for surfaces that need a tough, glossy finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varnish - a liquid that forms a hard, shiny surface when dry
  • Synonym: lacquer - a clear or colored wood finish that dries by solvent evaporation

Etymology

From Old French *esmail*, from Frankish *smalt* (melted glass), related to the process of fusing glass to a surface.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03289797-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈnæməl / (AmE), / ɪˈnæməl / (BrE)
any smooth, glossy coating that resembles a ceramic glaze, often used for protection or decoration on metal, glass, or pottery.
a smooth, glossy coating or finish

Examples

The antique box was covered with a layer of brilliant blue enamel.
She applied a special enamel to protect the metal from rusting.
The artist chose to use a glassy enamel finish on the ceramic vase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glaze - a vitreous coating applied to pottery or tiles

Etymology

From Old French *esmail*, from Frankish *smalt*, related to the German *Email* ('enamel'), ultimately of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03289654-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ɪˈnæməl / (AmE), / ɪˈnæməl / (BrE)
to coat or decorate (a surface) with enamel or a glossy substance.
to coat with enamel

Examples

The artist will enamel the jewelry to add a glossy, colorful finish.
Some antique dishes were beautifully enameled with intricate designs.
She learned how to enamel metal charms in her crafts class.

Etymology

From Old French *esmaillier* (to smelt, enamel), from *esmail* (enamel).

5. NOUN

/ ɪˈnæməl / (AmE), / ɪˈnæməl / (BrE)
a colored, glassy substance fused onto the surface of metal, glass, or pottery for decoration or protection
decorative/protective glassy coating

Examples

The artist applied a layer of enamel to the metal vase for a brilliant finish.
Ancient pottery is often decorated with colorful enamel designs.
The jeweler used blue enamel to add detail to the brooch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glaze - a vitreous coating applied to ceramics for decoration or waterproofing

Etymology

From Old French *esmail*, from Frankish *smalt* (smelted, fused).
