Chamaeleonidae

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmiːliˈɒnɪdiː / (AmE), / kəˌmiːliˈəʊnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of Old World lizards known as chameleons, characterized by their ability to change color and move their eyes independently.
family of chameleons (lizards)

Examples

Chamaeleonidae are known for their unique ability to change skin color.
Researchers have discovered new species within the Chamaeleonidae family.
The eyes of Chamaeleonidae can move independently, giving them a wide field of vision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chameleon - A lizard able to change its color for camouflage, from the family Chamaeleonidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'khamaileōn' (chameleon) and the suffix '-idae' used for animal family names.
