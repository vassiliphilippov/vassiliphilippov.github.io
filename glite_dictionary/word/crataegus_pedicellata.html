Crataegus pedicellata

1. NOUN

/ krəˈtiːɡəs ˌpɛdɪˌsɛˈleɪtə / (AmE), / krəˈtiːɡəs ˌpɛdɪˌsɛˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A species of American hawthorn with dense clusters of pink-red flowers and red fruit.
American hawthorn species with red fruit

Examples

The Crataegus pedicellata blooms in late spring, displaying its vibrant pink-red flowers.
Gardeners often plant Crataegus pedicellata for its beautiful clusters of red fruit.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Crataegus pedicellata berries.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Crataegus', meaning hawthorn, and 'pedicellata', meaning 'with small stalks'.
