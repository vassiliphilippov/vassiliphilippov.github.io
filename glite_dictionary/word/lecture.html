lecture

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛktʃər / (AmE), / ˈlɛktʃə / (BrE)
a talk given to an audience, especially to teach people about a particular subject, usually at a university or in a public meeting
a formal public talk or teaching session

Examples

The professor delivered an engaging lecture on the history of science.
We attended a lecture about climate change at the town hall last night.
Before the exam, the students reviewed the notes from last week's lecture.

Synset Examples

he attended a lecture on telecommunications

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speech - a formal address delivered to an audience
  • Degree Lower: seminar - a meeting for discussion or training

Etymology

From Latin 'lectura' (a reading), from 'legere' (to read).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07255209-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɛk.tʃə / (BrE)
an educational talk to an audience, especially to students in a university or college
an educational talk to students

Examples

The professor gave a fascinating lecture on ancient Greek philosophy.
Attendance at each lecture is mandatory for all students in the course.
She prepared carefully for her lecture to ensure it was engaging and informative.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seminar - a small group meeting for discussion or study
  • Synonym: workshop - an interactive, practical class
  • Synonym: speech - a public talk given to an audience

Etymology

From Latin 'lectura' (a reading), from 'legere' (to read).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00894541-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈlɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɛktʃə / (BrE)
to criticize or scold someone in a long and serious way, often giving advice or moral instruction
scold or criticize sternly

Examples

His father lectured him about responsibility after he broke the window.
She lectured her team for arriving late to the meeting.
The coach lectured the players on the importance of discipline during practice.

Synset Examples

The mother scolded the child for entering a stranger's car
The deputy ragged the Prime Minister
The customer dressed down the waiter for bringing cold soup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to angrily criticize someone, especially a child; to rebuke
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin 'lectura' (a reading), later associated with giving admonishing talks.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00826456-v
Synset POS: v

5. NOUN

/ ˈlɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɛktʃə / (BrE)
a long and serious talk given to someone in order to criticize or warn them, often as a form of discipline
a long scolding or rebuke

Examples

After he came home late, his mother gave him a lecture on responsibility.
No one wants to sit through another lecture about keeping their room tidy.
He received a stern lecture from his boss after missing the meeting.

Synset Examples

a good lecture was my father's idea of discipline
the teacher gave him a talking to

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scolding - an angry rebuke or reprimand
  • Synonym: reprimand - an official rebuke or expression of disapproval

Etymology

From Latin *lectura* (a reading), sense of scolding developed from instructive talks.
