Colinus

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈlaɪnəs / (AmE), / kɒˈliːnəs / (BrE)
A genus of small American quails known as bobwhites, native to North and Central America.
genus of bobwhite quails

Examples

The genus Colinus includes several species of bobwhites found in the Americas.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of birds in the Colinus genus.
The population decline of Colinus species has raised conservation concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bobwhite - a North American quail often called 'bobwhite'

Etymology

From Latin, originally a Neo-Latin name for the genus of bobwhite quails.

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈlaɪnəs / (AmE), / kɒˈliːnəs / (BrE)
A genus of New World quails, commonly known as bobwhites, native to the Americas.
genus of bobwhite quails

Examples

The genus Colinus includes several species, such as the Northern bobwhite.
Biologists study Colinus to understand quail behavior in their native habitats.
The distinctive call of Colinus species can be heard at dawn in the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bobwhite - a small North American quail with a distinctive whistling call

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Colinus, based on French ‘colin’ meaning 'quail'.
