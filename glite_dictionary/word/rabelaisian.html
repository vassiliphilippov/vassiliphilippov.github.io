Rabelaisian

1. ADJ

/ ˌræbəˈleɪziən / (AmE), / ˌrabəˈleɪzɪən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of François Rabelais or his works, especially marked by earthy humor, bawdiness, and exuberance.
characteristic of François Rabelais; bawdy, exuberant

Examples

The novel's Rabelaisian humor was both rowdy and insightful, echoing the spirit of Renaissance satire.
Critics praised the play for its Rabelaisian characters and unrestrained wit.
Some found the film's Rabelaisian energy and coarse jokes excessive, while others called it brilliant.

Synset Examples

Rabelaisian characters

Etymology

From the name of François Rabelais (c. 1494–1553), French Renaissance writer.
