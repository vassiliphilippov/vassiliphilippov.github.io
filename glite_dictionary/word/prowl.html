prowl

1. NOUN

/ praʊl / (AmE), / praʊl / (BrE)
an act of moving about stealthily or searching for something, especially as if hunting prey
an act of stealthy moving or searching

Examples

The tiger set off on a prowl through the dense jungle.
She went out for a midnight prowl around the city.
After hearing noises outside, he did a quick prowl around the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stalk - the act of stealthily pursuing prey
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00292969-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ praʊl / (AmE), / praʊl / (BrE)
to move about stealthily or restlessly, especially with the intent of finding something or for no apparent reason
move stealthily or aimlessly

Examples

The cat began to prowl around the empty house as darkness fell.
Security guards prowl the building at night looking for anything unusual.
He would often prowl the streets late at night with no particular destination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to loiter - stand or wait around without apparent purpose
  • Synonym: to wander - move about aimlessly or without fixed course

Etymology

Probably of Middle English origin, related to Old French *proler* (to go about in search of plunder).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02645559-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ praʊl / (AmE), / praʊl / (BrE)
to move stealthily or restlessly, especially in search of prey or something to steal
move stealthily, like a predator

Examples

The cat likes to prowl the garden at night, searching for mice.
Police officers continued to prowl the neighborhood after a series of break-ins.
He would prowl the halls, looking for any sign of trouble.

Synset Examples

The suspicious stranger prowls the streets of the town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creep - to move slowly and carefully in order to avoid being heard or noticed
  • Synonym: to skulk - to move stealthily or furtively
  • Degree Lower: to roam - to wander or travel around with no fixed purpose

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *prowlen*, of unknown origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01922297-v
Synset POS: v
