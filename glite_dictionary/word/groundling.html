groundling

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndlɪŋ / (BrE)
in Elizabethan theatre, a person who stood in the cheap area in front of the stage to watch plays
theatre-goer in the standing section (Elizabethan)

Examples

During Shakespeare's time, a groundling paid a penny to watch plays while standing near the stage.
The groundlings were often lively, loudly reacting to the actors' performances.
She described the experience of being a groundling at the modern reconstruction of the Globe Theatre.

Etymology

From 'ground' + '-ling' (a person associated with ground).
