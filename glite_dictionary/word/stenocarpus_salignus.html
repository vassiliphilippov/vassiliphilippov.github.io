Stenocarpus salignus

1. PROPN

/ ˌstiːnoʊˈkɑːrpəs səˈlɪɡnəs / (AmE), / ˌstiːnəʊˈkɑːpəs səˈlɪɡnəs / (BrE)
A tree or tall shrub native to Australia, having shiny leaves, umbels of fragrant creamy-white flowers, and yielding hard, heavy reddish wood.
Australian tree with fragrant white flowers

Examples

The botanical garden features a mature Stenocarpus salignus with clusters of creamy blossoms.
Timber from the Stenocarpus salignus is prized for its strength and reddish hue.
Wildlife often shelters in the spreading branches of the Stenocarpus salignus during the summer months.

Etymology

From Greek *stenos* (narrow) + *karpos* (fruit), and Latin *salignus* (of willow), referring to narrow fruit and willow-like leaves.
