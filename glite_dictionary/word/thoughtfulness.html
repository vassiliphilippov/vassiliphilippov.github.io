thoughtfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɔt.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈθɔːt.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being kind and considerate of others' needs and feelings.
kind and considerate regard for others

Examples

His thoughtfulness was evident in the little gifts he brought for everyone.
She was touched by the thoughtfulness of her friends during her illness.
A moment of thoughtfulness can make someone's entire day better.

Synset Examples

he showed no consideration for her feelings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consideration - careful thought, especially in making decisions or taking action
  • Antonym: thoughtlessness - lack of consideration or care for others

Etymology

From 'thoughtful' + '-ness', modeled after the English word-formation for noun of state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθɔt.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈθɔːt.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being considerate towards others, or an act that shows careful regard for someone's needs or feelings.
considerate act or attitude

Examples

Her thoughtfulness in bringing soup when I was sick meant a great deal to me.
He thanked her for her thoughtfulness in remembering his birthday.
The thoughtfulness of his gesture made her smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consideration - careful thought, especially for the needs and feelings of others
  • Antonym: thoughtlessness - lack of consideration for others; carelessness

Etymology

From 'thoughtful' + '-ness'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈθɔt.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈθɔːt.f(ʊ)l.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of careful and deep consideration or reflection
careful, deep consideration

Examples

He sat in thoughtfulness, staring out of the window for several minutes.
She welcomed his thoughtfulness when making important decisions.
The painter’s thoughtfulness is evident in every brushstroke of the portrait.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflection - careful consideration or thought
  • Antonym: absentmindedness - preoccupation so as to be unaware of one's surroundings

Etymology

From 'thoughtful' + '-ness', with 'thoughtful' from 'thought' + '-ful'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈθɔt.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈθɔːt.f(ə)l.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of considering things carefully and deliberately before taking action.
carefulness in considering before acting

Examples

Her thoughtfulness was evident in every choice she made during the project.
He admired her thoughtfulness before making important decisions.
Taking a moment for thoughtfulness can prevent many mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carelessness - lack of attention or caution; not thinking before acting
  • Synonym: prudence - the quality of being prudent; cautiousness

Etymology

From 'thoughtful' + '-ness', where 'thoughtful' is derived from 'thought' and Old English 'ful' (full of).
