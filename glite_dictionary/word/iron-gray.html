iron-gray

1. ADJ

/ ˈaɪərn ˌɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈaɪən ˌɡreɪ / (BrE)
of a grey color resembling that of iron, typically a dark or steely shade of grey
dark grey, iron-like color

Examples

Her iron-gray hair revealed her many years of experience.
He wore a suit of iron-gray wool that matched the stormy sky.
The old horse's coat had turned a distinctive iron-gray with age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray - the color gray (neutral color between black and white)

Etymology

From iron (the metal) + gray, referring to the typical color of unpolished iron.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərn ˌɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈaɪən ˌɡreɪ / (BrE)
a gray color resembling that of freshly broken cast iron, often with a bluish or metallic tinge.
gray color like broken cast iron

Examples

The horse's coat was a beautiful iron-gray that shimmered in the sunlight.
His beard had turned the shade of iron-gray with age.
She painted the fence an iron-gray to give it a modern, industrial look.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gray - a color between black and white
  • Degree Higher: silver - a shiny grayish-white color like silver metal
