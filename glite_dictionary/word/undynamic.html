undynamic

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.daɪˈnæm.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌʌn.daɪˈnæm.ɪk / (BrE)
lacking energy, force, or vitality; not dynamic
not energetic or forceful

Examples

The company's undynamic leadership failed to inspire innovation in the team.
Her approach to teaching was considered too undynamic by her students.
Critics described the performance as undynamic and lacking excitement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dynamic - characterized by constant change, activity, or progress
  • Degree Higher: energetic - showing or involving great activity or vitality
  • Degree Lower: lethargic - lacking energy or enthusiasm

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix un- (not) to dynamic.
