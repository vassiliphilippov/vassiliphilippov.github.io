Capet

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæp.eɪ / (AmE), / ˈkæp.eɪ / (BrE)
Hugh Capet (941–996), King of France elected in 987, founder of the Capetian dynasty.
founder of the Capetian dynasty, King of France

Examples

The reign of Capet marked the beginning of stable French monarchy.
It was Capet who established his family's dominance over the French crown.
Historians consider Capet a pivotal figure in medieval European history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hugh Capet - King of France (941–996), founder of Capetian dynasty

Etymology

From Old French Capet, derived from a nickname, possibly relating to a cape or hood worn.
