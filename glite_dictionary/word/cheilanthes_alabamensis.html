Cheilanthes alabamensis

1. NOUN

/ kiˈlænˌθiːz ˌæləˈbæmɛnsɪs / (AmE), / kiˈlanˌθiːz ˌaləˈbamənsɪs / (BrE)
A species of fern native to the southeastern United States, northern Mexico, and Jamaica.
species of fern in southeastern US and beyond

Examples

The shaded rock ledges were covered with Cheilanthes alabamensis.
Botanists identified the rare Cheilanthes alabamensis in the limestone outcrops of the region.
Cheilanthes alabamensis is well adapted to dry, rocky habitats in the southeast.

Etymology

From Latin; Cheilanthes is the genus name, and alabamensis refers to Alabama, the region where the species was first described.
