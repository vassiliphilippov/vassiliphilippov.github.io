Fraxinus velutina

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræksɪnəs vəˈluːtɪnə / (AmE), / ˈfræksɪnəs vɛˈluːtɪnə / (BrE)
A species of ash tree native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, typically small and shrubby.
species of ash tree native to SW US and NW Mexico

Examples

The Fraxinus velutina thrives in the arid climates of Arizona and New Mexico.
Botanists studied the Fraxinus velutina to understand its adaptation to dry environments.
The landscaping project featured several Fraxinus velutina along the southern boundary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash - a type of tree in the genus Fraxinus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Fraxinus' meaning 'ash', 'velutina' meaning 'velvety', referring to the texture of its leaves or twigs.
