forging

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of shaping metal by heating it and hammering or pressing it into a desired form
shaping metal by hammering when hot

Examples

The blacksmith demonstrated forging by crafting a horseshoe from a piece of iron.
Modern forging techniques allow for the creation of strong and durable parts.
The company specializes in the forging of custom metal components for the automotive industry.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: casting - shaping metal by pouring it into a mold

Etymology

From the verb 'forge', ultimately from Latin 'fabricare' (to make, construct).
