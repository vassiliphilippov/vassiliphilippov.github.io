Oenanthe

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈɛnænθi / (AmE), / əʊˈɛnænθi / (BrE)
A genus of small insectivorous passerine birds known as wheatears, found mainly in the Old World.
genus of birds (wheatears)

Examples

The Northern Oenanthe is known for its distinctive plumage and energetic movements.
Members of the genus Oenanthe are commonly found in stony, open country across Europe and Asia.
Many species within Oenanthe exhibit striking patterns of black, white, and orange.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheatear - a small songbird of the genus Oenanthe

Etymology

From Greek oinanthe, originally referring to a plant, but adopted as a genus name in ornithology.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈnænθi / (AmE), / əʊˈnænθi / (BrE)
A genus of poisonous water-loving plants, commonly known as water dropworts, found mainly in Europe and Asia.
genus of poisonous water dropworts

Examples

Some species of Oenanthe are highly poisonous and can be mistaken for edible plants.
The botanist collected samples of Oenanthe from the riverbank for further study.
Farmers are warned to keep livestock away from areas where Oenanthe grows.

Etymology

From Greek *oinanthē*, from *oinos* (wine) and *anthos* (flower).

3. NOUN

/ oʊˈnænθi / (AmE), / əʊˈnænθi / (BrE)
A genus of poisonous herbaceous plants in the family Apiaceae, commonly known as water dropworts.
genus of poisonous water dropworts

Examples

The Oenanthe genus includes several dangerously toxic water dropwort species found in wetlands.
Botanists conducted a field study on Oenanthe to better understand its chemical properties.
Ingesting plants from the Oenanthe genus can cause severe poisoning in both humans and animals.

Etymology

From Latin Oenanthe, from Ancient Greek oinánthē (wild vine, wild celery), from oînos (wine) + ánthos (flower).
