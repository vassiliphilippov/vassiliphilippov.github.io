bus

1. NOUN

/ bʌs / (AmE), / bʌs / (BrE)
(informal) An old car that is unreliable or in poor condition.
old, unreliable car (informal)
informal

Examples

He bought an old bus for a few hundred dollars just to get around town.
That bus barely made it up the hill before it started smoking.
After years of use, my father's old bus finally broke down for good.

Synset Examples

2. NOUN

/ bʌs / (AmE), / bʌs / (BrE)
a large motor vehicle designed to carry many passengers, especially along a fixed route as part of a public transport system
large passenger vehicle for public transport

Examples

The children waited patiently for the bus at the corner every morning.
He prefers to take the bus rather than drive his car to work.
A new electric bus was added to the city’s fleet this year.

Synset Examples

he always rode the bus to work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coach - a large motor vehicle for conveying passengers, especially over long distances
  • Degree Lower: minibus - a small bus for fewer passengers
  • Degree Higher: double-decker - a bus with two levels for passengers

Etymology

Shortened from 'omnibus', from Latin 'omnibus' meaning 'for all'.

5. NOUN

/ bʌs / (AmE), / bʌs / (BrE)
An electrical conductor that serves as a common connection for several circuits, used to transmit signals, power, or data within a system.
common electrical/data conductor

Examples

The motherboard has a bus that connects the processor to memory and other components.
If the bus is overloaded, it can cause data transmission errors in the system.
Technicians traced the fault to a break in the main power bus.

Synset Examples

the busbar in this computer can transmit data either way between any two components of the system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: busbar - a thick strip of copper or aluminum in switchboards and electrical substations for distributing power

Etymology

Shortened from 'omnibus', but in electronics possibly via analogy with the concept of shared travel or connection.

Source: WordNet

6. NOUN

/ bʌs / (AmE), / bʌs / (BrE)
a system or network topology in which all components are connected to a single communication channel (busbar), allowing data to be transmitted between them.
network system with shared communication channel

Examples

Many older local area networks used a bus to connect all computers and devices.
If a single cable in a bus topology fails, the entire network may be affected.
Network engineers debated whether to upgrade the old bus system to a more modern topology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: star topology - network topology where each node is connected to a central hub
  • Antonym: ring topology - network topology where nodes are connected in a closed loop
  • Antonym: tree topology - network topology with hierarchical connections

Etymology

From 'busbar', used in electrical engineering; shortened form commonly used in computing and electronics.
