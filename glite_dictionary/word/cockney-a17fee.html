cockney

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑkni / (AmE), / ˈkɒkni / (BrE)
Characteristic of or relating to Cockneys, their accent, or their dialect, especially that of East London.
relating to Cockneys or their dialect
british

Examples

Many actors struggle to master the distinctive cockney accent for period dramas.
The film features a group of thieves with colorful cockney expressions.
She loves listening to old songs sung in the traditional cockney style.

Synset Examples

cockney vowels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cockney - a native of East London or the distinctive dialect/accent spoken there

Etymology

From Cockney (noun), originally Middle English 'cockenay' meaning 'milksop,' later referring to natives of East London.
