stodge

1. NOUN

/ stɑdʒ / (AmE), / stɒdʒ / (BrE)
Food that is heavy, filling, and often starchy, typically lacking subtle flavor or refinement.
heavy, filling and starchy food

Examples

After a long walk in the cold, we craved some stodge to warm us up.
British school dinners are notorious for serving up bland stodge.
I'm trying to cut down on stodge and eat more fresh vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stodgy - (of food) heavy and hard to digest

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly related to 'stodgy'.
