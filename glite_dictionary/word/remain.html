to remain

1. VERB

/ rɪˈmeɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪn / (BrE)
to stay in a particular place and not leave it
stay in a place; not leave

Examples

You must remain in your seats until the end of the performance.
Despite the alarm, she chose to remain in the building.
Only a few students remained in the classroom after the bell rang.

Synset Examples

We are staying in Detroit; we are not moving to Cincinnati
Stay put in the corner here!
Stick around and you will learn something!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay - to continue to be in a place; not leave
  • Antonym: leave - to go away from a place

Etymology

From Latin remanēre (to stay behind), from re- (back) + manēre (to remain).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈmeɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪn / (BrE)
to continue to be in the same place, position, or situation without leaving or changing
continue in a place or position

Examples

She decided to remain at the company despite the merger.
Only a few trees remain after the fire.
You can remain in the waiting room until you are called.

Synset Examples

She continued as deputy mayor for another year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay - to continue to be in the same place or position
  • Antonym: leave - to go away from a place

Etymology

From Latin remanēre, from re- (back) + manēre (to stay).

3. VERB

/ rɪˈmeɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪn / (BrE)
to continue to exist or stay in the same state, especially when other people or things have gone, been dealt with, or changed
to continue to exist or be left

Examples

After the fire, only the old stone chimney remained standing.
Despite the changes in the company, several important issues still remain unresolved.
Only two pieces of cake remain on the plate.

Synset Examples

There remains the question of who pulled the trigger
Carter remains the only President in recent history under whose Presidency the U.S. did not fight a war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persist - to continue to exist, especially for longer than expected
  • Antonym: to disappear - to cease to be visible or present

Etymology

From Latin 'remanere' (to stay behind), from 're-' (again) + 'manere' (to remain).

4. VERB

/ rɪˈmeɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪn / (BrE)
to continue to be in the same state or condition; to stay unchanged or still in a particular place or situation
to stay unchanged or in the same state

Examples

Despite the rain, the children remained outside and continued playing.
Please remain seated until the train has come to a complete stop.
Her opinions remain unchanged after all these years.

Synset Examples

The dress remained wet after repeated attempts to dry it
rest assured
stay alone
He remained unmoved by her tears
The bad weather continued for another week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay - to remain in the same place or state
  • Synonym: to continue - to persist in an activity or state without stopping
  • Antonym: change - to become different, to undergo alteration

Etymology

From Latin remanēre (to stay behind, remain), from re- (again) + manēre (to stay).
