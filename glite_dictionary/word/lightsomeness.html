lightsomeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtsəmnəs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtsəmnɪs / (BrE)
a feeling of cheerfulness or light-heartedness, especially when free from trouble or worry
feeling of cheerful light-heartedness

Examples

Her lightsomeness made every difficult day seem a little brighter.
There was a lightsomeness in his manner after hearing the good news.
Traveling with friends often brings a welcome sense of lightsomeness to life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buoyancy - the ability or tendency to be cheerful and optimistic
  • Synonym: gaiety - a state of joyful exuberance or lightheartedness
  • Antonym: sadness - the condition or quality of being sad

Etymology

Formed from "lightsome" (cheerful, lively) + "-ness" (denoting a state or quality).

/ ˈlaɪtsəmnəs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtsəmnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being lighthearted and carefree; a cheerful or frivolous attitude
lighthearted, cheerful quality

Examples

The lightsomeness in her laughter brightened the entire room.
He approached each day with a natural lightsomeness that made him a joy to be around.
Despite the stress of exams, their lightsomeness helped ease the tension among friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lightheartedness - the quality of being cheerful and carefree
  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality of being solemn or serious

Etymology

Formed from 'lightsome' + '-ness'; 'lightsome' from Middle English, meaning lively or cheerful.
