to pussyfoot

1. VERB

/ ˈpʊsiˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈpʊsiˌfʊt / (BrE)
to move in a quiet or cautious way, especially to avoid being noticed
move quietly or cautiously

Examples

He tried to pussyfoot around the office without anyone noticing his late arrival.
Stop pussyfooting and tell us what you really think.
She pussyfooted past the sleeping dog to avoid waking it up.

Synset Examples

… stead of sneaking around spying on the neighbor's house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sneak - move stealthily
  • Synonym: creep - move slowly and carefully to avoid being noticed

Etymology

Originated in early 20th century America, from the cautious way a cat (pussy) walks.
