Allied

1. ADJ

/ ˈælaɪd / (AmE), / ˈælaɪd / (BrE)
Of or relating to the countries that fought together against the Central Powers in World War I.
relating to the Allies in World War I

Examples

The Allied troops launched a coordinated attack on the front lines.
An Allied victory in the battle shifted the momentum of the war.
During World War I, Britain and France were key members of the Allied forces.

Synset Examples

an allied offensive
the Allied powers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Allies - The countries allied against the Central Powers in World War I

Etymology

From 'allied', the past participle of 'ally', originally from Latin 'alligare' (to bind to).

2. ADJ

/ ˈælaɪd / (AmE), / ˈælaɪd / (BrE)
of or relating to the countries that fought together against the Axis powers in World War II; denoting their armed forces or actions.
relating to the Allies in World War II

Examples

The Allied powers launched a major offensive in 1944.
An Allied victory in Europe marked the end of the war there.
He wrote a book on Allied military strategy during World War II.

Synset Examples

an Allied victory
the Allied armies

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Axis - Countries opposed to the Allies during World War II
