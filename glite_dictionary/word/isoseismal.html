isoseismal

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsoʊˈsaɪzməl / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊˈsaɪzməl / (BrE)
A line drawn on a map connecting points where an earthquake's intensity was felt at the same level.
line on map of equal earthquake intensity
medical

Examples

The isoseismal lines showed that the city center experienced the strongest shaking during the earthquake.
Scientists use isoseismal maps to study how an earthquake's intensity varies across a region.
After each major quake, researchers carefully draw isoseismal lines to analyze the patterns of ground motion.

Synset Examples

Because of local variations in the ground conditions, isoseismals will generally separate zones of broadly similar felt intensity, while containing areas of both higher and lower degrees of shaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contour - Line connecting points of equal value on a map

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) and 'seismos' (earthquake).
