destined

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛstɪnd / (AmE), / ˈdɛstɪnd / (BrE)
heading for or intended to arrive at a particular place or achieve a specific purpose
heading for a place or purpose

Examples

The train is destined for Chicago and will arrive this evening.
She always seemed destined for greatness, even as a child.
The package is destined for Canada, so it may take a few days.

Synset Examples

children bound for school
a flight destined for New York

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bound - heading for a particular place; intending to go to

Etymology

From Latin destinare (to determine, appoint, destine) via Old French destiner.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɛstənd / (AmE), / ˈdɛstɪnd / (BrE)
certain to do or experience something, especially as if decided by fate; intended or predetermined.
certain or fated to happen

Examples

She always felt she was destined to become an artist.
Their meeting seemed destined, as if arranged by fate itself.
The old theater was destined for demolition despite public protest.

Synset Examples

bound to happen
an old house destined to be demolished
he is destined to be famous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doomed - certain to fail or suffer; fated for a negative outcome
  • Synonym: predestined - decided or determined in advance, especially by fate or divine will

Etymology

From Latin 'destinare' (to determine, appoint).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00341524-s
Synset POS: s
