bulrush

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlˌrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.rʌʃ / (BrE)
a tall marsh plant with cylindrical seed heads that burst when mature, shedding down; its long flat leaves are used for making mats and chair seats.
a tall marsh plant with cylindrical seed heads

Examples

During the nature walk, we spotted several bulrushes growing along the edge of the lake.
Artisans sometimes use dried bulrush leaves to weave sturdy baskets and mats.
The tall, slender stems of the bulrush swayed gently in the afternoon breeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cattail - a marsh plant with a similar cylindrical seed head, especially in North America

Etymology

From Middle English bulrish, of uncertain origin, possibly influenced by 'bull' and 'rush'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlˌrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.rʌʃ / (BrE)
a tall plant with soft stems that grows in wet areas, especially marshes and riverbanks, found in Eurasia, Australia, New Zealand, and North America
tall marsh plant with soft stems

Examples

Dense clusters of bulrushes bordered the edge of the pond, providing shelter for frogs and birds.
The wetland restoration project included planting native bulrush to stabilize the riverbanks.
In early spring, new shoots of bulrushes begin to appear alongside the reeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reed - tall, slender-leaved plant of wetlands

Etymology

From Middle English bulrysche, influenced by the word 'bull', referring to its large size, and 'rush', the type of plant.
