Ute

1. NOUN

/ juːt / (AmE), / juːt / (BrE)
a member of an Indigenous people native to Utah, Colorado, and New Mexico in the western United States.
member of an Indigenous people (US)

Examples

The Ute have a rich cultural heritage stretching back many centuries.
Descendants of the Ute people still live in Colorado and Utah today.
Several important legal battles have been fought to protect Ute lands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shoshone - a member of another Indigenous people of the western United States

Etymology

From the Ute people's own word for themselves, 'Núuchi-u', through Spanish 'Yuta'.

2. NOUN

/ juːt / (AmE), / juːt / (BrE)
the native language of the Ute people, belonging to the Shoshonean branch of the Uto-Aztecan language family
Shoshonean language of the Ute people

Examples

The Ute language is considered endangered, with only a few remaining fluent speakers.
She is studying Ute linguistics at the university.
Many traditional stories are still told in Ute among elders in the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ute - a member of the Ute people, indigenous to the western United States

Etymology

From the name of the Ute people, possibly from their own language.
