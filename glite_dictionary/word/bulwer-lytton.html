Bulwer-Lytton

1. PROPN

/ ˌbʊlwər ˈlɪtən / (AmE), / ˌbʊlwə ˈlɪtən / (BrE)
(1803–1873) An English novelist and playwright known for his historical romances and for phrases such as 'the pen is mightier than the sword.'
English writer of historical romances

Examples

Many Victorian readers admired Bulwer-Lytton for his imaginative and dramatic novels.
The annual bad writing contest is named after Bulwer-Lytton.
Bulwer-Lytton coined the popular phrase 'the pen is mightier than the sword.'
