Remilegia australis

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛməˈliːdʒiə ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌrɛməˈliːɡiə ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (BrE)
a large blue remora of the Pacific Ocean that attaches itself to whales and dolphins
a species of large Pacific remora

Examples

The Remilegia australis is often seen clinging to blue whales in the Pacific Ocean.
Researchers studied how Remilegia australis interacts with its mammal hosts.
A diver spotted a Remilegia australis attached to a dolphin during the survey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remora - a type of fish that attaches to other marine animals

Etymology

From New Latin, with genus 'Remilegia' and species 'australis' meaning southern.
