restriction

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈstrɪkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪkʃən / (BrE)
an act of limiting or controlling something, especially by rules or regulations
an act of limiting or controlling

Examples

There is a new restriction on outdoor activities due to the pandemic.
The company placed a restriction on employee internet usage during work hours.
Travelers should be aware of any restriction before entering a foreign country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limitation - an act or instance of limiting
  • Antonym: freedom - the state of being free, without restriction or restraint

Etymology

From Latin 'restrictio', from 'restringere' (to bind back, restrain).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈstrɪkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪkʃən / (BrE)
the action of limiting or controlling something within certain limits, sometimes by force
the act of limiting or controlling

Examples

The government imposed a restriction on the import of foreign cars.
Strict restriction of access to the laboratory is necessary for safety.
There was a restriction on the number of people who could attend the meeting.

Synset Examples

the restriction of the infection to a focal area

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limitation - the act of limiting or the state of being limited
  • Antonym: freedom - the state of being free, not restricted or limited

Etymology

From Latin 'restrictio', from 'restringere' meaning 'to bind fast, confine'.

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈstrɪk.ʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪk.ʃən / (BrE)
a rule or principle that limits what someone can do or how something can be done
a limiting rule or principle

Examples

Due to health concerns, the city imposed a restriction on outdoor gatherings.
There are no age restrictions for entering the exhibition.
Employees must follow every safety restriction imposed by the company.

Synset Examples

I am willing to accept certain restrictions on my movements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limitation - a restriction or limit placed on something
  • Antonym: freedom - the state of not being restricted; liberty

Etymology

From Latin 'restrictio' (a fastening, binding), from 'restringere' (to bind back, restrain).
