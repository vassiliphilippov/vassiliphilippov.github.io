giveaway

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪvəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡɪvəweɪ / (BrE)
a television or radio program in which contestants compete to win prizes or awards, often by answering questions or performing tasks
contest program for awards

Examples

She was thrilled to participate in the giveaway and hoped to win a new car.
The television network is launching a new giveaway where contestants can compete for cash prizes.
Many listeners tuned in to the radio giveaway for a chance to answer trivia questions and win.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: game show - a television program in which people play games or answer questions to win prizes
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06633568-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪv.əˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡɪv.əˌweɪ / (BrE)
a situation in which public land or resources are given to a private group, often at low cost or as a special benefit
gift of public property for private gain

Examples

The new mining law is criticized as a giveaway of federal lands to corporations.
Many activists protested what they saw as a giveaway of water rights to private companies.
The subsidies amounted to a huge giveaway to the oil industry.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪvəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡɪvəˌweɪ / (BrE)
Something that inadvertently reveals information that was meant to be kept secret.
unintentional disclosure or revealing clue

Examples

Her nervous laughter was a giveaway that she was hiding something.
The poker player's glance at his chips was a clear giveaway.
The muddy footprints were the giveaway that someone had sneaked into the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clue - a piece of evidence or information used in the detection of a crime or solving of a mystery
  • Antonym: secret - something kept hidden or unknown from others
