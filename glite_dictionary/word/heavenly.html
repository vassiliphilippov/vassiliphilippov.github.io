heavenly

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɛvənli / (AmE), / ˈhɛvənli / (BrE)
of or relating to the sky or outer space, especially as observed in astronomy
relating to the sky or outer space

Examples

Astronomers have discovered a new heavenly body beyond Pluto.
The telescope revealed thousands of bright heavenly objects in the night sky.
Ancient civilizations often created myths to explain heavenly phenomena such as comets and eclipses.

Synset Examples

celestial map
a heavenly body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celestial - relating to the sky or outer space, as in 'celestial body'

Etymology

From Old English 'heofonlic', from 'heofon' (heaven) + '-lic' (-ly, like).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɛvənli / (AmE), / ˈhɛvənli / (BrE)
of or belonging to heaven or god; related to the divine or celestial realm.
relating to heaven or the divine

Examples

Many believe that heavenly rewards await the righteous after death.
The choir's voices filled the cathedral with a heavenly sound.
She gazed at the night sky, marveling at its heavenly beauty.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: earthly - relating to the physical world or material existence

Etymology

From Old English heofonlic, from heofon (heaven) + -lic (-ly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01182518-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈhɛvənli / (AmE), / ˈhɛvənli / (BrE)
relating to or inhabiting heaven; belonging to or coming from the divine realm.
of or relating to heaven or divinity

Examples

The choir sang a heavenly melody that seemed to fill the cathedral with light.
Many ancient cultures believed in heavenly beings who watched over humans.
The painting depicted a heavenly host of angels surrounding the throne.

Synset Examples

celestial beings
heavenly hosts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celestial - relating to the sky or heavens, often used as a synonym to 'heavenly' in religious or poetic contexts
