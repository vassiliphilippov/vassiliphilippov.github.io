punkah

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌŋkə / (AmE), / ˈpʌŋkə / (BrE)
a large, ceiling-suspended fan made of canvas stretched on a frame, used especially in India to circulate air in a room
ceiling-suspended canvas fan used in India

Examples

The colonial officials sat beneath a slowly swaying punkah as the afternoon heat intensified.
A servant known as a punkah-wallah was tasked with pulling the rope to keep the punkah moving.
The vintage mansion was restored, complete with original punkahs in the main hall.

Etymology

From Hindi 'pankhā', from 'pankh' (feather, wing).
