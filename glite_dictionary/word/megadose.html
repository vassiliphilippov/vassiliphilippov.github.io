megadose

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛɡəˌdoʊs / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡəˌdəʊs / (BrE)
an exceptionally large dose of a substance, especially a drug or vitamin, often exceeding the recommended amount
an unusually large dose, esp. of vitamins or drugs

Examples

Some people believe taking a megadose of vitamin C can prevent colds, though experts are skeptical.
Doctors warn that a megadose of certain supplements could have harmful side effects.
A single megadose of antibiotics may be prescribed in some emergency cases.

Synset Examples

Vitamin C megadoses are claimed by alternative medicine advocates including Matthias Rath and Patrick Holford to have preventative and curative effects on diseases such as cancer and AIDS, but the available scientific evidence does not support these claims.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overdose - a dose of a drug that is larger than is safe or normal

Etymology

From 'mega-' (meaning large or great) and 'dose'.
