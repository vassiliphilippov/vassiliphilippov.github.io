Hyalophora

1. NOUN

/ haɪ.əˈlɑː.fə.rə / (AmE), / haɪ.əˈlɒf.ər.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large American silkworm moths, known for their striking appearance and economic importance.
genus of American silkworm moths

Examples

The Hyalophora genus includes the impressive Cecropia moth, one of North America's largest moths.
Scientists often study Hyalophora to understand silkworm development and silk production.
A Hyalophora caterpillar can be identified by its bright green color and distinctive blue and yellow tubercles.

Etymology

From Greek 'hyalos' (glass) + 'phoros' (bearing), possibly referring to transparent parts in the moth's wings.

2. NOUN

/ haɪ.əˈlɑː.fɚ.ə / (AmE), / haɪ.əˈlɒ.fə.rə / (BrE)
a genus of large American silkworm moths known for their striking appearance, including the cecropia moth
genus of American silkworm moths

Examples

The Hyalophora are among the largest moths native to North America.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Hyalophora genus last year.
The colorful patterns on the wings help Hyalophora moths avoid predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cecropia moth - a large North American moth of the Hyalophora genus

Etymology

From New Latin Hyalophora, from Greek 'hyalos' (glass) + 'phoros' (bearing), referring to the glass-like appearance of some species' wings.
