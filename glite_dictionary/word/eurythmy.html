eurythmy

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈrɪθmi / (AmE), / jʊəˈrɪθmi / (BrE)
A system of harmonious bodily movement and gesture used to express the rhythm and meaning of music or speech, often as a form of artistic performance or as an educational method.
expressive movement interpreting music or speech

Examples

The students practiced eurythmy to better understand musical rhythms through coordinated movement.
Many Waldorf schools include eurythmy as part of their artistic curriculum.
Her performance in eurythmy captivated the audience with graceful gestures and expressive motion.

Etymology

From Greek 'eurythmia' meaning 'good rhythm' or 'harmonious arrangement.'
