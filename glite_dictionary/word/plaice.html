plaice

1. NOUN

/ pleɪs / (AmE), / pleɪs / (BrE)
The flesh of a large European flatfish, especially when used as food.
flesh of a European flatfish (food)

Examples

I ordered grilled plaice with lemon and herbs at the seaside restaurant.
The market had fresh plaice alongside other fish like sole and cod.
She prefers plaice to haddock because of its delicate flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sole - a similar type of flatfish used for food

Etymology

From Old French 'plais', from Medieval Latin 'platessa', meaning 'flatfish'.

2. NOUN

/ pleɪs / (AmE), / pleɪs / (BrE)
a large European flatfish with a diamond-shaped body and orange spots, commonly used as food
a type of European flatfish

Examples

The fisherman brought in a fresh plaice for tonight's dinner.
Grilled plaice with lemon is a popular dish in many European countries.
She ordered battered plaice and chips at the seaside café.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flounder - a type of flatfish closely related to plaice

Etymology

From Old French *plaïs* or Middle Dutch *plec* (flatfish).
