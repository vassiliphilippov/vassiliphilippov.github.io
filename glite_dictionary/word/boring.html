boring

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔːrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of drilling a hole or tunnel into something, especially using a tool or machine.
drilling or making a hole

Examples

The workers began boring through the rock to create the tunnel.
Advances in technology have made oil well boring much more efficient.
The process of boring requires specialized equipment and careful planning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drilling - the act of making holes in something with a drill

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɔrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)
not interesting; making you feel tired and impatient
not interesting; tedious

Examples

He found the task boring and tried to finish it as quickly as possible.
She refuses to watch boring movies that don't capture her attention.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of drilling a hole in the earth, especially in search of oil or other resources
drilling a hole in the earth

Examples

The boring of the oil well took several weeks.
Advances in technology have made boring for petroleum much more efficient.
Geologists supervised the boring to ensure accurate core samples were collected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drilling - the act of making a hole, especially in the ground

Etymology

From 'bore' (to make a hole by drilling) + -ing, forming a noun of action.
