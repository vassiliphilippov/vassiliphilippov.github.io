to solarize

1. VERB

/ ˈsoʊləˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɒləraɪz / (BrE)
to reverse some tones in a photographic negative or print and create pronounced outlines by briefly exposing it to light during development
to reverse tones in photography by light exposure

Examples

The photographer decided to solarize the image to give it a surreal, high-contrast effect.
Old film prints were sometimes solarized accidentally when exposed to light during development.
In the darkroom, she carefully solarized the portrait to emphasize the subject’s facial features.

Etymology

From French 'solariser', from 'solaire' (solar), relating to the sun and light exposure.

2. VERB

/ ˈsoʊləraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊləraɪz / (BrE)
to expose (photographic film or paper) to an excessive amount of sunlight, often resulting in undesirable effects; to overexpose to sunlight
overexpose to sunlight (photography)

Examples

If you solarize the film, the picture will lose definition and contrast.
The negatives were accidentally solarized after hours of sunlight exposure in the lab.
Be careful not to solarize old photographs when scanning them near a window.

Synset Examples

be careful not to solarize the photographic film

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overexpose - to expose film to too much light

Etymology

From French 'solariser', derived from 'solaire' (solar), from Latin 'sol' (sun).

Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ ˈsoʊləˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊləraɪz / (BrE)
(of photographic film or image) to become overexposed, often resulting in the reversal or distortion of tones.
to become overexposed (photography)

Examples

Vintage photographic negatives can solarize if exposed to intense light during development.
The image appeared to solarize unexpectedly, creating a surreal effect on the final print.
If the film is left out in the sun, it may solarize and lose its clarity.

Synset Examples

The film solarized

Etymology

From 'solar', referring to the effect of sunlight on photographic material, plus '-ize'.

4. VERB

/ ˈsoʊ.ləˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.ləˌraɪz / (BrE)
to expose something to sunlight or to affect something by exposure to the sun's rays
expose to sunlight or its effects

Examples

If you solarize the seeds before planting, they may germinate faster.
The artist solarized the prints by leaving them out in direct sunlight for several hours.
Leaving the fabric outside too long can solarize its colors and cause fading.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to insolate - expose to the sun's rays

Etymology

Formed from 'solar' (from Latin sol, meaning 'sun') + '-ize'.
