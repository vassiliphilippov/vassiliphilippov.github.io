confluence

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnfluəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒnfluəns / (BrE)
the place where two or more rivers or streams flow together and become one
the junction of rivers

Examples

The city was founded at the confluence of two major rivers.
After heavy rainfall, the confluence became a dramatic sight as the waters surged.
Several villages are situated near the confluence of the river and its tributary.

Synset Examples

Pittsburgh is located at the confluence of the Allegheny and Monongahela rivers

Etymology

From Latin confluere (to flow together), from com- (together) + fluere (to flow).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnfluəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒnfluəns / (BrE)
An act or process of coming together, especially of people or groups.
coming together of people or groups

Examples

The annual festival is a confluence of musicians, artists, and fans from around the world.
There was a remarkable confluence of different cultures at the international conference.
The confluence of ideas during the discussion led to a creative solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gathering - an assembly or meeting of people
  • Antonym: dispersal - the act of spreading people or things widely apart

Etymology

From Latin 'confluentia', from 'confluere' (to flow together).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnfluəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒnfluəns / (BrE)
the act or process of two or more streams, rivers, or things flowing together and joining at one point
a flowing together or meeting point

Examples

The city developed at the confluence of two major rivers.
There was a remarkable confluence of ideas at the international conference.
The confluence of factors led to a significant change in the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junction - a place where things join
  • Antonym: divergence - the process of separating or moving apart

Etymology

From Latin *confluentia* (a flowing together), from *com-* (together) + *fluere* (to flow).
