Nicandra physaloides

1. NOUN

/ naɪˈkændrə ˌfaɪsəˈlɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / nɪˈkændrə ˌfɪsəˈlɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A coarse South American herb cultivated for its blue-and-white flowers and bladderlike fruit containing a dry berry; also known as the apple of Peru.
South American herb with bladderlike fruit

Examples

The garden featured Nicandra physaloides among several other unusual flowering plants.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Nicandra physaloides in traditional South American remedies.
I planted Nicandra physaloides for its distinctive blue-and-white blossoms and interesting fruits.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining the genus name Nicandra (after Greek poet Nicander) and physaloides (resembling Physalis).
