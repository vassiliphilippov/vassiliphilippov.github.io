paniculate

1. ADJ

/ pəˈnɪkjʊlət / (AmE), / pəˈnɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
(of a plant or part) having a panicle or arranged in a panicle-like cluster.
arranged in a panicle (botany)

Examples

The paniculate inflorescence gives the plant a graceful appearance.
Many grasses have paniculate flower clusters that help with wind pollination.
Some species in this family are characterized by their paniculate arrangement of fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panicled - having a panicle; arranged in panicles

Etymology

From Latin 'panicula' (tuft, panicle) with English '-ate' forming adjectives.
