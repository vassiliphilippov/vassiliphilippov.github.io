escapade

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛskəˌpeɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛskəpeɪd / (BrE)
an exciting, daring, or mischievous adventure, especially one that is carefree or unconventional
carefree or daring adventure

Examples

His latest escapade got him into trouble with his parents.
They laughed as they recalled their college escapades in Europe.
The children planned a secret escapade in the attic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adventure - an unusual and exciting, typically hazardous, experience or activity

Etymology

From French 'escapade' (a prank, flight), from Spanish 'escapada' (escape, prank), related to 'escape'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛskəˌpeɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛskəˌpɑːd / (BrE)
an exciting, adventurous, or somewhat risky act, especially one that is unconventional or daring
an adventurous or daring act

Examples

The children's latest escapade involved sneaking out to camp in the backyard all night.
They were notorious in college for every wild escapade you could imagine.
Her daring escapade in a foreign city became the talk of her friends for months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adventure - an unusual and exciting or daring experience
  • Synonym: exploit - a bold or daring feat
  • Antonym: misadventure - an unfortunate incident, similar to an escapade but with negative outcome

Etymology

From French 'escapade', from Spanish 'escapada', from 'escapar' (to escape).
