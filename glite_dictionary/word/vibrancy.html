vibrancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪ.brən.si / (AmE), / ˈvʌɪ.brən.si / (BrE)
the quality of being full of energy, brightness, or resonance, especially referring to a loud deep sound
quality of being resonant or lively

Examples

The vibrancy of her voice filled the concert hall, capturing everyone's attention.
Many artists are drawn to cities because of the vibrancy of their cultural life.
The room lost its vibrancy after everyone left, becoming silent and dull.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonance - the quality in a sound of being deep, full, and reverberating

Etymology

From 'vibrant' + '-cy', ultimately from Latin 'vibrāre' (to shake).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪ.brən.si / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.brən.si / (BrE)
the quality of being full of energy and life; liveliness or vitality, especially as applied to a place such as a city
quality of being lively and energetic

Examples

The vibrancy of the city attracts young professionals from all over the country.
You can feel the vibrancy in the arts and music scenes downtown.
Many tourists are drawn to the vibrancy of Barcelona's nightlife.

Synset Examples

The city's vibrancy makes Jerusalem a unique destination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liveliness - full of life and energy
  • Antonym: dullness - lacking energy or excitement

Etymology

From vibrant + -cy, modeled after 'brilliancy'. 'Vibrant' from Latin 'vibrāre' (to move quickly to and fro).
