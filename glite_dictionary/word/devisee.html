devisee

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌvaɪˈzi / (AmE), / dɪˌvaɪˈziː / (BrE)
a person to whom property, especially real estate, is left by a will
person receiving property by will
legal

Examples

The property was transferred to the devisee after the will was executed.
Each devisee must be clearly identified in the document to avoid disputes.
As the sole devisee, she inherited the entire estate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legatee - a person who receives a legacy (personal property) by will

Etymology

From Old French 'deviser' (to divide, distribute), from Latin 'divisus', past participle of 'dividere' (to divide).
