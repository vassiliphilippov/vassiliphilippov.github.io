tollon

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.lən / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.lən / (BrE)
An ornamental evergreen treelike shrub native to the Pacific coast of the United States, known for its large white flowers and red berrylike fruits; sometimes classified in the genus Photinia.
Pacific coast evergreen shrub with white flowers and red fruits

Examples

The hillside was dotted with tollon, their red berries bright against the dark green leaves.
Gardeners often choose tollon for its ornamental value and resistance to coastal winds.
Clusters of tollon added color to the winter landscape along the Pacific highways.
