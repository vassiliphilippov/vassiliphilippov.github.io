Panjabi

1. NOUN

/ pʌnˈdʒɑːbi / (AmE), / pʌnˈdʒɑːbi / (BrE)
A member of the people native to Punjab, a region in northern India and Pakistan.
person from Punjab region

Examples

Her grandmother is a proud Panjabi who still follows many traditional customs.
Many Panjabis have settled abroad and formed vibrant communities.
The festival was organized by the local Panjabi association.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Punjabi - A member of the people from Punjab (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From Persian پنجاب‎ (panjāb), meaning 'five rivers', referring to the region.

2. NOUN

/ pʌnˈdʒɑːbi / (AmE), / pʌnˈdʒɑːbi / (BrE)
the Indo-Aryan language spoken by people in the Punjab region of Pakistan and India
the language of Punjab, India and Pakistan

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Panjabi.
Many songs in Bollywood are influenced by Panjabi music and lyrics.
I am learning to write in Panjabi so I can communicate with my grandparents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hindi - an Indo-Aryan language spoken in northern India
  • Synonym: Urdu - an Indo-Aryan language spoken in Pakistan and India

Etymology

From Hindi and Urdu 'Punjabi', meaning 'of Punjab', itself from Persian 'panj' (five) and 'ab' (water), referring to the land of five rivers.
