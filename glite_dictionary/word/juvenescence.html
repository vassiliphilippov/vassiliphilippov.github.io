juvenescence

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒuːvəˈnɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːvəˈnɛsəns / (BrE)
the process of becoming young or youthful, or the period during which this happens
process of becoming youthful

Examples

The garden's juvenescence each spring fills me with hope.
Recent scientific research has focused on cellular juvenescence and ways to slow aging.
There was a sense of juvenescence in the community after the influx of young families.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: senescence - the process of aging or growing old

Etymology

From Latin 'juvenescere' (to grow young), from 'juvenis' (young).
