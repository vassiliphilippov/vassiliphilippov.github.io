camouflage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæməˌflɑʒ / (AmE), / ˈkaməˌflɑːʒ / (BrE)
a device or method used to conceal something or deceive by making it blend in with its surroundings or by disguising its true nature
device or method for concealment or disguise

Examples

The company used clever camouflage to hide its true intentions from competitors.
Her apparent indifference was merely a camouflage for her nervousness.
The spy's methods relied heavily on camouflage and deception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disguise - something that conceals the identity or true nature
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make something known or visible

Etymology

From French 'camouflage' (disguise, concealment).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæm.əˌflɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˈkæm.ə.flɑːʒ / (BrE)
A fabric with a pattern of splotches in green, brown, black, and tan, designed to make the wearer difficult to see against natural backgrounds.
patterned fabric for concealment

Examples

Military uniforms are often made of camouflage to blend in with the natural environment.
She wore a camouflage jacket while hiking in the forest.
The store sells camouflage fabric for hunting and outdoor activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concealment - the action of hiding or preventing disclosure

Etymology

From French *camouflage* (disguise or concealment), from *camoufler* (to disguise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02948034-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæməˌflɑʒ / (AmE), / ˈkæməˌflɑːʒ / (BrE)
the act of hiding or disguising the appearance or identity of something, especially to blend in with the surroundings
the act of concealing by disguise

Examples

The tiger's stripes act as camouflage in the tall grass, making it hard to spot.
Soldiers wore uniforms with camouflage to blend into the forest during training.
Some insects use natural camouflage to evade predators and hide on tree bark.

Synset Examples

he is a master of disguise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disguise - the act of concealing or altering appearance to mislead

Etymology

From French 'camoufler' meaning 'to disguise'; influenced by Italian 'camuffare.'

4. VERB

/ ˈkæməˌflɑʒ / (AmE), / ˈkæməˌflɑːʒ / (BrE)
to disguise or conceal by making someone or something blend in with the surroundings, especially to avoid detection
to disguise by blending in

Examples

Animals often camouflage themselves to avoid predators in the wild.
She camouflaged the stain on the wall with a new painting.

5. NOUN

/ ˈkæməˌflɑʒ / (AmE), / ˈkæməflɑːʒ / (BrE)
The act of concealing the true nature of something by giving it a deceptive appearance; a means or outward semblance used to mislead or disguise.
the use of deceptive appearance to conceal

Examples

The company’s friendly public image was just a camouflage for its unethical business practices.
Her cheerful attitude was a camouflage hiding her deep insecurity.
Under the camouflage of official paperwork, the money was quietly transferred abroad.
