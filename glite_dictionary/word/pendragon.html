pendragon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnˌdræɡən / (AmE), / ˈpɛnˌdræɡən / (BrE)
the chief warrior or war leader of the ancient Britons, especially as a title used for legendary British kings such as King Arthur's father.
chief war leader of the ancient Britons

Examples

According to legend, Uther was granted the title of pendragon after demonstrating his prowess in battle.
The ancient tales speak of the pendragon uniting the Britons against invading forces.
In the story, only the true pendragon could wield the mythical sword.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chieftain - the leader of a clan or tribe
  • Synonym: king - a male monarch or ruler of a kingdom

Etymology

From Welsh 'pen' (head, chief) + 'dragon' (dragon, leader), used as a title for war leaders.
