glasnost

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɑːz.nɒst / (AmE), / ˈɡlæz.nɒst / (BrE)
a policy introduced in the Soviet Union in the 1980s allowing greater openness and freedom of information and discussion of social and political issues
Soviet policy of openness (1980s)

Examples

The introduction of glasnost led to greater transparency in Soviet society.
Many credit glasnost with contributing to the end of the Cold War.
Under glasnost, censorship was relaxed and the media became more open.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perestroika - the policy of economic and governmental restructuring in the Soviet Union

Etymology

From Russian гласность (glasnost), meaning 'publicity' or 'openness'.
