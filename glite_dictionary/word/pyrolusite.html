pyrolusite

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪrəˈluːˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˌpɪərəˈluːsaɪt / (BrE)
a black or dark gray mineral consisting mainly of manganese dioxide, used as an important source of manganese
a manganese dioxide mineral

Examples

The ore samples were rich in pyrolusite, making them valuable for industrial use.
Large deposits of pyrolusite have been discovered in several parts of the world.
Chemists use pyrolusite as a primary source for extracting pure manganese.

Etymology

From German 'Pyrolusit', from Greek 'pyr' (fire) + 'lysis' (a loosening), referring to its property of releasing oxygen when heated.
