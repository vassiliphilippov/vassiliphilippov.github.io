reticulocyte

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɪkjəloʊˌsaɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈtɪkjʊləˌsaɪt / (BrE)
an immature red blood cell containing a network of fine filaments or granules, typically present in the blood before becoming fully mature
immature red blood cell
medical

Examples

A higher count of reticulocytes in the blood can indicate that the body is producing more red blood cells.
Doctors often measure the reticulocyte count to assess bone marrow function.
A reticulocyte appears different from a mature red blood cell under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: erythrocyte - a mature red blood cell

Etymology

From Latin 'reticulum' (net) + Greek 'kytos' (cell).
