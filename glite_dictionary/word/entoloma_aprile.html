Entoloma aprile

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛntoʊˈloʊmə əˈprɪleɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛntəˈləʊmə əˈprɪleɪ / (BrE)
A species of spring-fruiting agaric mushroom with a dark brown conical cap, found in woodland environments.
a spring-fruiting agaric mushroom species

Examples

The Entoloma aprile can often be seen growing in clusters in early spring.
Mycologists collected specimens of Entoloma aprile from the woodland floor in April.
The dark brown cap is a distinguishing feature of Entoloma aprile among other spring mushrooms.

Etymology

From modern Latin: genus name Entoloma + specific Latin epithet aprile (from 'April'), referring to its spring fruiting season.
