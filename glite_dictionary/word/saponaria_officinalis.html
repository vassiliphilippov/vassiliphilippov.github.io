Saponaria officinalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæpəˈnɛəriə ˌɑfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌsapəˈnɛːrɪə ˌɒfᵻˈsɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
A plant of European origin with pink or white flowers, whose leaves and roots produce a soap-like substance when bruised; also known as common soapwort.
European plant with soap-producing leaves; common soapwort

Examples

The leaves of Saponaria officinalis have traditionally been used as a natural soap.
Botanists study Saponaria officinalis for its cleansing properties and attractive flowers.
She planted Saponaria officinalis in her garden to enjoy its delicate pink blooms.

Etymology

From Latin *sapo* (soap) + *-aria* (connected with) and *officinalis* (used in medicine).
