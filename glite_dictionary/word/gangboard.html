gangboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæŋˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈɡæŋˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a temporary platform or bridge used to board or leave a ship at dockside
temporary platform for boarding a ship

Examples

The sailors laid down the gangboard to allow the passengers to disembark safely.
During heavy cargo loading, the gangboard provided easy access between the dock and the ship.
Before departure, the crew removed the gangboard and secured it below deck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangplank - a movable plank used as a ramp to board or leave a ship

Etymology

From 'gang', meaning to go or walk (from Old English 'gangan'), and 'board', referring to a plank or platform.
