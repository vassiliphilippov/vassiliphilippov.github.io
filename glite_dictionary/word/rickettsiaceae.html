Rickettsiaceae

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌkɛtsiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / rɪˌkɛtsiˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of microorganisms resembling bacteria, found in arthropods and capable of causing disease in vertebrates.
family of bacteria-like microorganisms
medical

Examples

The Rickettsiaceae family includes organisms responsible for several serious infectious diseases.
Researchers studied how Rickettsiaceae infect arthropods and are transmitted to vertebrates.
Accurate identification of Rickettsiaceae is crucial in diagnosing certain fevers and rashes.

Etymology

From the genus name Rickettsia, named after American pathologist Howard Taylor Ricketts, plus the family suffix -aceae.
