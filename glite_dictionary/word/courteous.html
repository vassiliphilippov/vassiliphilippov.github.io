courteous

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɝː.t̬i.əs / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.ti.əs / (BrE)
polite, respectful, and considerate in manner or behavior.
polite and respectful

Examples

The staff at the hotel were extremely courteous and helpful during our stay.
It is always important to be courteous when interacting with customers.
Her courteous response defused the tense situation.

Synset Examples

a nice gesture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polite - having or showing behavior that is respectful and considerate
  • Antonym: rude - offensively impolite or ill-mannered

Etymology

From Old French 'courtois', from court (court), influenced by Latin 'cohors' (court, yard).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɝː.t̬i.əs / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.ti.əs / (BrE)
Polite, respectful, and considerate in manner; showing good manners toward others.
polite and considerate in manner

Examples

Despite the rush, the receptionist remained courteous to every guest.
He was always courteous in his emails, no matter how stressful the situation.
The manager appreciated her courteous response to customer complaints.

Synset Examples

if a man be gracious and courteous to strangers it shows he is a citizen of the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polite - having or showing behavior that is respectful and considerate of other people
  • Antonym: rude - offensively impolite or ill-mannered

Etymology

From Old French 'courtois' (having courtly manners), from 'court' (court).
