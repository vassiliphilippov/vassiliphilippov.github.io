to belong

1. VERB

/ bəˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / bɪˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to be in the right place or situation as a part or adjunct; to be a member or part of something.
be a part of; fit in

Examples

I'm not sure this painting belongs in a modern art gallery.
She felt like she didn't belong in her new school at first.
Do these keys belong to you?

Synset Examples

the uniform looks like it belonged to a museum collection
These pages don't belong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fit - to be suitable or right in a particular situation
  • Antonym: to exclude - to prevent someone or something from being part of a group or place

Etymology

From Middle English 'belongen', from be- (prefix) + 'longen' (to belong), from Old English 'langian'.
