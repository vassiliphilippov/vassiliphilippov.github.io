Kokka

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈkɑː / (AmE), / kəʊˈkɑː / (BrE)
Relating to the branch of Shinto that was recognized as the state religion of Japan.
of state Shinto in Japan

Examples

The Kokka ideology played a significant role in the Meiji period reforms.
Some shrines were reclassified as Kokka institutions by government decree.
Scholars debate the influence of Kokka Shinto on modern Japanese society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shinto - the indigenous religion of Japan involving worship of kami

Etymology

From Japanese 国家 (Kokka), meaning 'nation' or 'state'.

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈkɑː / (AmE), / kəʊˈkɑː / (BrE)
the branch of Shintoism that was recognized and promoted as the official state religion of Japan, especially during the Meiji period and until the end of World War II
State Shinto of Japan

Examples

During the Meiji era, Kokka was institutionalized to strengthen national identity.
The practices associated with Kokka were separated from government affairs after World War II.
Historians often study the influence of Kokka on Japanese society and politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shinto - the indigenous spirituality of Japan involving the worship of kami (gods or spirits)

Etymology

From Japanese 国家神道 (Kokka Shintō), where 国家 (kokka) means 'nation' or 'state' and 神道 (shintō) means 'way of the gods'.
