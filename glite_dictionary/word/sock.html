sock

1. NOUN

/ sɑk / (AmE), / sɒk / (BrE)
a tubular fabric device, often cone-shaped and mounted on a pole, used for indicating wind direction, especially at airports
a fabric device for showing wind direction

Examples

The airport's sock pointed steadily to the north, indicating strong winds.
Pilots often check the sock before takeoff to judge the wind conditions.
During the storm, the sock flapped violently atop the control tower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windsock - a fabric cone for showing wind direction

2. NOUN

/ sɑk / (AmE), / sɒk / (BrE)
A piece of clothing made of soft material that covers your foot and the lower part of your leg, usually worn inside a shoe.
cloth covering for the foot

Examples

He put on a clean sock before stepping into his shoes.
A hole in her sock made her toes cold all day.
Children often lose one sock from a pair in the laundry.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: stocking - a garment covering the foot and leg, usually longer and thinner than a sock

Etymology

From Middle English socke, from Old English socc, from Latin soccus (a light, low-heeled shoe).

3. VERB

/ sɑk / (AmE), / sɒk / (BrE)
to hit someone or something very hard
hit hard
informal

Examples

He socked his opponent right in the jaw during the fight.
If you don't back off, I'm going to sock you!
She socked the punching bag with all her strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punch - to hit someone or something with your fist
  • Degree Lower: slap - to hit with an open hand
