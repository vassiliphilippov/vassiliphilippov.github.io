crevalle jack

1. NOUN

/ krəˈvæl i dʒæk / (AmE), / krəˈvæl i dʒæk / (BrE)
a large, silvery fish of the western Atlantic and Gulf of Mexico (Caranx hippos), valued as a game fish and sometimes called common jack or black-tailed trevally.
a large Atlantic game fish (Caranx hippos)

Examples

Fishermen in Florida often catch crevalle jack near the shore during the summer months.
The crevalle jack is known for its powerful fight when hooked by anglers.
A large school of crevalle jack was spotted feeding near the mouth of the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jack - any of various fish in the family Carangidae
  • Synonym: trevally - any fish of the genus Caranx, including crevalle jack

Etymology

From French 'cravalle,' of uncertain origin.
