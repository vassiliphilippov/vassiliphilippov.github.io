rumbustious

1. ADJ

/ rʌmˈbʌs.tʃəs / (AmE), / rʌmˈbʌs.tɪ.əs / (BrE)
loud, lively, and lacking in restraint or discipline; boisterous or unruly.
noisy and lacking restraint or discipline

Examples

The rumbustious children ran around the playground, laughing and shouting without a care.
The party grew more rumbustious as the night went on, with loud music and dancing.
Behind his rumbustious manner lies a surprisingly gentle and thoughtful personality.

Synset Examples

a boisterous crowd
a social gathering that became rambunctious and out of hand
a robustious group of teenagers
beneath the rumbustious surface of his paintings is sympathy for the vulnerability of ordinary human beings
an unruly class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boisterous - noisy, energetic, and cheerful; rowdy
  • Synonym: rambunctious - uncontrollably exuberant; boisterous
  • Synonym: unruly - disorderly or disruptive and not amenable to discipline or control

Etymology

Alteration of 'robustious', probably with influence from 'rumbustious' (Scottish and Irish English variants).
