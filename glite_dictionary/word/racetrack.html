racetrack

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪsˌtræk / (AmE), / ˈreɪs.træk / (BrE)
A track or course where races are held, typically for horses, cars, or runners.
track for races (horses, cars, etc.)

Examples

The crowd cheered as the horses thundered down the racetrack toward the finish line.
After months of training, she finally competed at the city’s main racetrack.
The new racetrack will host its first Formula 1 race next year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: track - a prepared course for racing or athletics

Etymology

Compound formed from 'race' and 'track'.
