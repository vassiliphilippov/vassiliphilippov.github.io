stumbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈstʌmblər / (AmE), / ˈstʌmblə / (BrE)
a person who frequently makes mistakes or errors, especially through lack of skill or competence
someone prone to making mistakes

Examples

The new employee turned out to be a stumbler who struggled with even basic tasks.
In the world of chess, a single stumbler can change the outcome of a tournament.
She feared being seen as a stumbler during her first public presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bungler - a person who does things clumsily or ineptly
  • Antonym: expert - a person with a high level of skill or competence

Etymology

From 'stumble' + '-er', denoting a person who stumbles or errs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstʌmblər / (AmE), / ˈstʌmblə / (BrE)
a person who trips or nearly falls while walking or running
person who trips or nearly falls

Examples

As he clumsily shuffled through the forest, he was the very image of a stumbler.
The marathon's rocky path turned even seasoned athletes into stumblers.
Every school trip seems to have at least one stumbler trailing behind the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stumble - to trip or momentarily lose one's balance
  • Synonym: walker - a person who walks, especially for exercise or sport
  • Synonym: runner - a person who runs, especially as a sport or for exercise

Etymology

From 'stumble' (Middle English stumblen), + -er agent noun suffix.
