sixtieth

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪkstiəθ / (AmE), / ˈsɪkstiɪθ / (BrE)
the ordinal number matching the number 60 in a sequence; being number sixty in order or position
ordinal number for sixty

Examples

He finished in sixtieth place in the marathon.
Today marks the sixtieth anniversary of the school's founding.
Only a sixtieth of the students passed the exam this year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sixty - the number 60
  • Degree Higher: sixty-first - the ordinal number for 61

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪkstiəθ / (AmE), / ˈsɪkstiɪθ / (BrE)
the ordinal number representing the position sixty in a sequence; 60th.
the ordinal number 60th

Examples

This is the sixtieth anniversary of the school's founding.
Her name appeared sixtieth on the list of candidates.
He finished in sixtieth place in the marathon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fiftieth - the ordinal number 50th
  • Degree Higher: seventieth - the ordinal number 70th

Etymology

Formed from 'sixty' (from Old English 'sixtig') + ordinal suffix '-th'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪkstiəθ / (AmE), / ˈsɪkstiɪθ / (BrE)
Each of the sixty equal parts into which something can be divided.
one part out of sixty equal parts

Examples

Each sixtieth of the pie was carefully measured for accuracy.
A minute is one sixtieth of an hour.
He divided the inheritance into sixtieths so that everyone could receive an equal share.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sixty - the number 60
  • Degree Higher: sixty-first - one part in sixty-one equal parts
  • Degree Lower: fiftieth - one part in fifty equal parts

Etymology

From Middle English 'sixtieth', from Old English 'sixtigōða', formed from 'sixty' + '-th' (ordinal suffix).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
