desynchronization

1. NOUN

/ diˌsɪŋkrənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌsɪŋkrənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the loss or absence of synchronization; a state in which events or processes do not occur at the same time or in a coordinated manner
loss of synchronization

Examples

The doctor noticed a desynchronization in the patient's brain wave patterns during the test.
Technical failures can cause a desynchronization between audio and video signals in recordings.
Jet lag is often due to the desynchronization of the body's internal clock with local time.

Synset Examples

the stimulus produced a desynchronizing of the brain waves

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synchronization - the state in which events or processes occur at the same time

Etymology

From de- (expressing reversal) + synchronization, from Greek syn- (together) and chronos (time).
