Insecta

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɛktə / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛktə / (BrE)
The class of animals comprising insects, which makes up the vast majority of animal species.
the class of animals comprising insects

Examples

Many scientists study Insecta to better understand biodiversity.
Insecta includes familiar creatures such as ants, bees, and butterflies.
The largest animal class by species is Insecta, encompassing over a million described types.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insect - a small animal with six legs and generally one or two pairs of wings

Etymology

From Latin *insectum* ('insect'), with 'Insecta' as the formal class name in scientific taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɛktə / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛktə / (BrE)
the class of animals comprising insects, characterized by a three-part body, compound eyes, and six legs
the class of insects

Examples

The class Insecta contains more species than all other animal groups combined.
Biologists study Insecta to understand the diversity of terrestrial life.
Beetles, butterflies, and flies are all part of the class Insecta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insect - a small arthropod animal with six legs and generally one or two pairs of wings

Etymology

From Latin *insectum* (insect), used as the modern scientific name for the class.
