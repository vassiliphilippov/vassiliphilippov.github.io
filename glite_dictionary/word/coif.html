coif

1. NOUN

/ kɔɪf / (AmE), / kɔɪf / (BrE)
A close-fitting cap covering the top, back, and sides of the head, worn by nuns under a veil, by soldiers under a hood of mail, or formerly by British sergeants-at-law.
close-fitting cap worn by nuns, soldiers, or in law

Examples

The nun adjusted her coif before putting on her veil.
Medieval knights often wore a mail coif for head protection under their helmets.
The traditional attire of British sergeants-at-law included a distinctive white coif.

Etymology

From Old French *coife* (a cap), from Late Latin *cofea* (head covering).

2. NOUN

/ kɔɪf / (AmE), / kɔɪf / (BrE)
a style or arrangement of the hair, especially a woman's hair
hair arrangement or style

Examples

The bride chose an elegant coif for her wedding day, adorned with delicate flowers.
After hours at the salon, her new coif turned heads at the party.
He complimented her on her stylish coif before they left for the dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hairstyle - the way in which someone's hair is cut and arranged
  • Synonym: hairdo - a style of arranging the hair, especially women's hair

Etymology

From Old French 'coife' (a cap, headdress), from Late Latin 'cofea' (helmet, head covering).
