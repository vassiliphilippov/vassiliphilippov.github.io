Lemnaceae

1. NOUN

/ lɛmˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / lɛmˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of small, free-floating aquatic plants, commonly known as duckweeds, often found in freshwater environments
family of small aquatic duckweeds

Examples

Researchers study Lemnaceae because these plants are important for wastewater treatment.
Many ponds are covered with Lemnaceae, forming a green mat on the surface.
The rapid growth of Lemnaceae makes them useful in ecological research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duckweed - a small, free-floating aquatic plant of the family Lemnaceae

Etymology

From New Latin Lemnaceae, based on the type genus Lemna.
