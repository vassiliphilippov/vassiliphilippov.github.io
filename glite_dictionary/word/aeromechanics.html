aeromechanics

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛroʊməˈkænɪks / (AmE), / ˌeərəʊmɪˈkænɪks / (BrE)
The branch of mechanics that studies the motion of gases, especially air, and their effects on bodies moving through them.
study of gases in motion and their effects

Examples

The principles of aeromechanics are essential in the design of modern aircraft.
He published several papers in the field of aeromechanics during his time as a research engineer.
Understanding aeromechanics helps improve the efficiency of wind turbine blades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerodynamics - study of the motion of air and its interaction with solid objects

Etymology

From aero- (relating to air) + mechanics.
