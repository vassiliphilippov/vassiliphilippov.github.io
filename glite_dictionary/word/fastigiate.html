fastigiate

1. ADJ

/ fəˈstɪdʒiət / (AmE), / fəˈstɪdʒɪət / (BrE)
(of a plant, especially a tree) having branches that are erect and close to the trunk, giving the appearance of a single column.
columnar plant growth; erect branches

Examples

The fastigiate form of the oak tree is often planted along city streets for its upright growth.
Some cypress species exhibit a fastigiate habit, making them ideal for narrow spaces.
Gardeners often choose fastigiate cultivars to create dramatic vertical accents in their landscapes.

Etymology

From Latin fastigiatus, meaning 'pointed' or 'sloping upwards'.
