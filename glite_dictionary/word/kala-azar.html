kala-azar

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːlə əˈzɑːr / (AmE), / ˌkɑːlə əˈzɑː / (BrE)
A severe infectious disease caused by protozoan parasites, affecting the internal organs, especially the liver and spleen; also known as visceral leishmaniasis.
severe disease caused by parasites (visceral leishmaniasis)
medical

Examples

In some rural areas, kala-azar remains a significant public health issue.
The patient was diagnosed with kala-azar after showing persistent fever and weight loss.
Early detection of kala-azar is crucial to prevent serious complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leishmaniasis - disease caused by Leishmania parasites

Etymology

From Hindi 'kālā-āzār', meaning 'black sickness'.
