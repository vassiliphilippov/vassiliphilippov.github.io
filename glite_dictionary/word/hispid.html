hispid

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɪspɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɪspɪd / (BrE)
(of animals or plants) having stiff, coarse hairs or bristles on the surface.
having stiff or bristly hairs

Examples

The leaves of the nettle are hispid and can irritate the skin on contact.
Many species of caterpillars are identified by their hispid bodies covered with bristles.
The botanist examined the hispid stems of the plant under a microscope.

Synset Examples

plants with hispid stems

Etymology

From Latin hispidus (bristly, rough).
