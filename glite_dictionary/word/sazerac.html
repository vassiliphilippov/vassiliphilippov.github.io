Sazerac

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæzəˌræk / (AmE), / ˈsæzərak / (BrE)
A cocktail made with rye whiskey or bourbon, bitters, sugar, Pernod (or absinthe), and lemon peel, served over ice.
cocktail with whiskey, bitters, and Pernod

Examples

He ordered a Sazerac at the famous New Orleans bar.
The Sazerac is known for its unique blend of bitters and rye whiskey.
Have you ever tried making a Sazerac at home?

Etymology

Named after Sazerac de Forge et Fils, a brand of cognac originally used in the drink.
