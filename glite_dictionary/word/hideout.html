hideout

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdaʊt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdaʊt / (BrE)
a place where someone can hide or stay secretly, especially one used by criminals or outlaws to avoid being found
a secret hiding place, often for criminals

Examples

The police finally discovered the gang's remote hideout in the mountains.
He kept a secret hideout in the woods where he could be alone.
The thieves returned to their hideout after the robbery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lair - a place where a wild animal lives or hides
  • Synonym: den - a secluded room or private place, also used for animal's resting place or hiding

Etymology

From 'hide' + 'out', originating in America in the early 20th century.
