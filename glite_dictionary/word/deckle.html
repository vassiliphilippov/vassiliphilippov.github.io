deckle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəl / (BrE)
A frame used in papermaking to shape pulp into sheets of paper.
papermaking frame for forming sheets

Examples

The artisan lifted the deckle carefully to reveal a freshly formed sheet of paper.
Handmade paper often has rough edges created by the deckle.
He explained how the deckle is crucial in determining the size and shape of each sheet.

Etymology

From German 'Deckel' (lid, cover).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəl / (BrE)
the rough, feathered edge left on handmade paper, or artificially produced on machine-made paper to imitate this effect
rough edge of handmade or imitation paper

Examples

The artist preferred using paper with a deckle to give his prints a handcrafted touch.
Collectors often value the deckle on limited edition books as a mark of authenticity.
She trimmed the deckle edges for a cleaner, modern look in her scrapbook.

Etymology

From German 'Deckel' meaning 'cover' or 'lid', from Middle High German 'decke'.
