Amanita

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈniːtə / (AmE), / ˌaməˈniːtə / (BrE)
A genus of widely distributed mushrooms that have white spores and are mostly poisonous, including some of the most notorious toxic species.
genus of mostly poisonous mushrooms

Examples

The death cap, a highly toxic species, belongs to the genus Amanita.
Many collectors avoid picking Amanita mushrooms because of their reputation for being poisonous.
The red and white fly agaric is perhaps the most recognizable Amanita species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - a plant of the fungus family, especially an edible or poisonous one

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek amanitai (a kind of fungus).

2. NOUN

/ əˈmæ.nɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌæ.məˈniː.tə / (BrE)
A genus of widely distributed mushrooms, many of which have white spores and are highly poisonous, with few exceptions.
genus of poisonous mushrooms

Examples

The Amanita genus includes some of the deadliest mushrooms in the world.
Most mushrooms in the Amanita group are not safe to eat and should be avoided by foragers.
Mycologists often warn beginners about mistaking edible fungi for a poisonous Amanita.

Etymology

From Latin 'Amanita', from Greek 'amanitai' (a kind of fungus).
