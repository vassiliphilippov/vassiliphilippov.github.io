genus Paeonia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs piːˈoʊniə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs piːˈəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous or shrubby plants, commonly called peonies, known for their large, showy flowers.
genus of flowering plants (peonies)

Examples

The genus Paeonia includes both herbaceous species and woody shrubs.
Botanists have identified several hybrids within the genus Paeonia.
Peonies from the genus Paeonia are popular in ornamental gardens worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peony - a plant of the genus Paeonia, known for its large, showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'Paeonia', the name of the peony plant, from Ancient Greek 'Paiōnia'.
