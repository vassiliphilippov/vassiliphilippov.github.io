envy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.vi / (AmE), / ˈɛn.vi / (BrE)
a feeling of discontent or resentful longing aroused by someone else's possessions, qualities, or luck
resentful desire for what someone else has

Examples

She couldn't hide her envy when she saw her friend's new car.
Their success was the object of envy among their competitors.
He tried to mask his envy behind a polite smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jealousy - resentment against a rival or against someone enjoying success or advantage
  • Degree Lower: admiration - a feeling of respect and approval
  • Degree Higher: covetousness - intense desire to possess something that belongs to someone else

Etymology

From Latin invidia (envy, jealousy), from invidere (to look against, to envy).

2. VERB

/ ˈɛn.vi / (AmE), / ˈɛn.vi / (BrE)
to feel a desire to have what someone else has; to be envious of someone or something
to desire what someone else has

Examples

She couldn't help but envy her friend's beautiful singing voice.
Many people envy those who travel the world for a living.
He secretly envied his colleague's confidence and success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to covet - to desire something belonging to others
  • Antonym: to admire - to respect or regard with approval rather than envy

Etymology

From Latin invidere, literally 'to look at with envy'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.vi / (AmE), / ˈɛn.vi / (BrE)
a feeling of discontent or resentful longing aroused by someone else's possessions, qualities, or luck; in religious context, one of the seven deadly sins.
resentful desire for another's qualities or possessions

Examples

Her envy was evident when she saw her colleague receive the promotion.
Consumed by envy, he could not celebrate his friend's achievements.
In many religions, envy is considered to be a serious sin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jealousy - resentment or suspicion due to rivalry or fear of losing something
  • Antonym: admiration - a feeling of respect and warm approval

Etymology

From Latin invidia (envy), from invidere (to look at with malice).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈɛn.vi / (AmE), / ˈɛn.vi / (BrE)
to feel envious of someone or their achievements; to wish you had what someone else has
feel envious of someone

Examples

She couldn't help but envy her friend's confidence and success.
Many people envy celebrities for their glamorous lifestyles, but don't see their struggles.
He secretly envied his colleague's quick promotion at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to admire - to regard someone or something with respect or warm approval
  • Antonym: to resent - to feel bitter or angry about someone's advantages or success

Etymology

From Old French 'envier', from Latin 'invidere' (to look at with malice, to envy).
