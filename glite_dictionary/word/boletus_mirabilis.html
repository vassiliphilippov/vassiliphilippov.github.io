Boletus mirabilis

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈliːtəs mɪˈræbɪlɪs / (AmE), / bəʊˈliːtəs mɪˈræbɪlɪs / (BrE)
an edible species of mushroom with a dark brown convex cap, yellow to greenish pores beneath, and a reddish stalk, eaten when young and fresh
an edible species of mushroom with a dark brown cap

Examples

The Boletus mirabilis is highly prized by foragers for its excellent flavor when cooked fresh.
She found a Boletus mirabilis in the forest and carefully identified it before harvesting.
Some mycologists study the Boletus mirabilis for its unusual coloration and culinary value.

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Boletus' meaning a type of mushroom and 'mirabilis' meaning wonderful or remarkable.
