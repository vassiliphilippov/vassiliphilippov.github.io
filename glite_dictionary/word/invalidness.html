invalidness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvælɪdnɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈvælɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being invalid, especially in logic, where a conclusion does not logically follow from the premises.
state of being invalid (in logic)

Examples

The lawyer argued that the contract's invalidness stemmed from a missing signature.
Her claim was dismissed due to the invalidness of the supporting evidence.
In logic, one must recognize the invalidness of arguments that have faulty reasoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invalidity - the quality or state of being invalid
