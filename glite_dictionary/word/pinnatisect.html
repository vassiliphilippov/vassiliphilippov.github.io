pinnatisect

1. ADJ

/ pɪˈnætəˌsɛkt / (AmE), / pɪˈnatɪsɛkt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) deeply divided in a pinnate pattern almost to the midrib, but not forming separate leaflets.
leaf deeply divided almost to midrib
medical

Examples

The fern's pinnatisect leaves give it a delicate, lacy appearance in the garden.
Some daisies have pinnatisect foliage that almost looks like individual strands.
Botanists identified the plant by its pinnatisect structure, with divisions reaching nearly to the midrib.

Etymology

From Latin 'pinnatus' (feathered) + 'sectus' (cut).
