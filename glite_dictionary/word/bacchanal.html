bacchanal

1. NOUN

/ ˌbækəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˌbakəˈnal / (BrE)
a wild and drunken celebration, often marked by excessive drinking and sexual activity
wild, drunken revelry

Examples

The ancient Romans were known to hold secret bacchanals in honor of their wine god.
The film depicts a lavish bacchanal where all social rules are cast aside.
News of the politician's attendance at a wild bacchanal shocked the public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orgy - a wild party, especially one involving excessive drinking and sexual activity

Etymology

From Latin Bacchanalia, festivals of Bacchus, the Roman god of wine.

2. ADJ

/ ˌbækəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˌbækəˈnæl / (BrE)
characterized by riotously drunken revelry or wild, uncontrolled festivities
drunken, riotous celebration

Examples

The party turned into a bacchanal celebration that lasted until dawn.
Revelers poured into the streets for a bacchanal night of music, dancing, and drinking.
Critics described the festival as more of a bacchanal than a cultural event.

Synset Examples

a night of bacchanalian revelry
carousing bands of drunken soldiers
orgiastic festivity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orgiastic - relating to unrestrained, wild festivity or celebration
  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol; moderate or restrained in celebration

Etymology

From Latin Bacchanalia (festival of Bacchus, the Roman god of wine)

3. NOUN

/ ˌbækəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˌbækəˈnal / (BrE)
A person who participates in noisy, drunken revelry; especially a devotee of Bacchus in ancient times.
drunken reveler; devotee of Bacchus

Examples

The festival drew many bacchanals who danced and sang until dawn.
During the Roman era, a true bacchanal would worship Bacchus with wild abandon.
He had a reputation as a bacchanal, always the last to leave the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reveller - a person who enjoys lively, noisy festivities
  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol; not drunk

Etymology

From Latin 'bacchanalis', from 'Bacchus', the Roman god of wine.

4. NOUN

/ ˌbækəˈnæl / (AmE), / ˌbakəˈnal / (BrE)
a person who participates in wild drinking parties or revelry
one who revels in drunken festivities

Examples

The notorious bacchanal stumbled out of the tavern at dawn, singing loudly.
During the festival, every bacchanal in town seemed to be indulging to excess.
He was known as a bacchanal, always at the center of every wild party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reveller - a person who is enjoying themselves in a lively and noisy way, especially with drinking and dancing
  • Antonym: teetotaler - a person who never drinks alcohol

Etymology

From Latin 'Bacchanalis', relating to Bacchus, the Roman god of wine.
