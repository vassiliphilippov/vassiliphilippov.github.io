bandaged

1. ADJ

/ ˈbændʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈbændɪdʒd / (BrE)
covered or wrapped with a bandage, especially to protect a wound or injury
covered with a bandage

Examples

The doctor carefully examined the bandaged arm, making sure the wound was healing properly.
After the accident, his head remained bandaged for several days.
She felt reassured when her injured foot was finally bandaged by the nurse.

Synset Examples

the bandaged wound on the back of his head
an injury bound in fresh gauze

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unbandaged - not covered with a bandage

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'bandage', from French 'bandage', from 'bande' (a strip, band).
