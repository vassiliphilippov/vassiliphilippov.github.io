Naiadaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪ.əˈdeɪ.si.i / (AmE), / ˌnaɪ.əˈdeɪ.si.i / (BrE)
A family of aquatic plants with narrow leaves and small flowers, typically found in fresh water.
family of aquatic plants

Examples

The Naiadaceae family includes several species that thrive in slow-moving streams and lakes.
Researchers studied the ecological role of Naiadaceae in maintaining freshwater habitats.
Some members of the Naiadaceae are used as indicators of water quality in environmental studies.

Etymology

From modern Latin Naiadaceae, based on Greek Naiad ('water nymph'), referencing the aquatic habit.
