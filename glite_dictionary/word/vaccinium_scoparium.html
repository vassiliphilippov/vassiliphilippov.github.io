Vaccinium scoparium

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm skoʊˈpɛriəm / (AmE), / vækˈsɪniəm skəˈpɑːriəm / (BrE)
A low-growing shrub of northwestern North America, producing small red edible berries; commonly known as grouse whortleberry or littleleaf huckleberry.
shrub with red berries of NW North America

Examples

Hikers in the Rockies sometimes snack on the berries of Vaccinium scoparium along the trail.
Vaccinium scoparium is an important food source for grouse and other wildlife in its native habitats.
Botanists study Vaccinium scoparium to understand its adaptation to subalpine environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huckleberry - edible berry-producing shrubs in the genus Vaccinium

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Vaccinium' (whortleberry family) and species epithet 'scoparium' meaning 'broom-like.'
