Frigg

1. PROPN

/ frɪɡ / (AmE), / frɪɡ / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, the goddess of the heavens and of married love, wife of Odin.
Norse goddess of the heavens and love

Examples

According to legend, Frigg weaves the clouds in the sky as she rules over the heavens.
The Vikings believed that Frigg was the protector of marriage and motherhood.
In many myths, Frigg is depicted alongside Odin, her powerful husband.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Freya - Norse goddess associated with love, beauty, and fertility
  • Synonym: Odin - chief god in Norse mythology, husband of Frigg

Etymology

From Old Norse 'Frigg', related to Proto-Germanic *Frijjō, associated with love and affection.
