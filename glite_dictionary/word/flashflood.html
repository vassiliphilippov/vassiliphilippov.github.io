flashflood

1. NOUN

/ flæʃ.flʌd / (AmE), / flæʃ.flʌd / (BrE)
a sudden, severe flood that occurs within a short period, usually as a result of heavy rain
sudden, intense local flood

Examples

The heavy rains last night caused a dangerous flashflood in the mountain valley.
Emergency services warned residents to evacuate due to the risk of a flashflood.
A sudden flashflood swept away several cars parked near the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - an overflow of water onto normally dry land

Etymology

From 'flash' (sudden) + 'flood' (overflow of water).
