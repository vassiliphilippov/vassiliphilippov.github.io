Acer circinatum

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪsər sərˈsaɪnətəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪsə sɜːˈsaɪnətəm / (BrE)
A small maple tree native to northwestern North America, having spreading stems that often root at the tips and form dense thickets.
species of maple tree native to northwestern North America

Examples

We spotted an Acer circinatum growing along the riverbank during our hike.
The garden was planted with several Acer circinatum to provide beautiful autumn color.
Acer circinatum is valued for its graceful, arching branches and delicate leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vine maple - common name for Acer circinatum

Etymology

From Latin; 'Acer' (maple) and 'circinatum' (rounded, circular), referring to the leaf shape.
