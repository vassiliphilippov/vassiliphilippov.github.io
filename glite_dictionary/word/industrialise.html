to industrialise

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈdʌstriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌstriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to organize the production of something as an industry, especially by introducing large-scale manufacturing methods
organize production into an industry
british

Examples

The government has made efforts to industrialise agriculture over the past decade.
Many developing nations seek to industrialise their economies to boost growth.
Plans to industrialise the region have met with both support and resistance.

Synset Examples

The Chinese industrialized textile production

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to industrialize - organize production into an industry (US spelling)

Etymology

From industry + -ise, modelled after French industrialiser.

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈdʌstriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌstriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to develop industry in a country, region, or economy; to undergo a process of becoming industrial
develop industry; become industrial
british

Examples

The government invested heavily to help the country industrialise after decades of agricultural dependence.
During the late 19th century, European nations began to rapidly industrialise.
Many developing countries are seeking ways to industrialise their economies and reduce poverty.

Synset Examples

The nations of South East Asia will quickly industrialize and catch up with the West

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to industrialize - American English spelling of 'industrialise'
  • Antonym: deindustrialise - reduce or eliminate industrial activity in an area

Etymology

From 'industry' + '-alise', patterned after 'industrial'.
