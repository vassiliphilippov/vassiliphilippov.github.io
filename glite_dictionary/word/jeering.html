jeering

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
mocking remarks or laughter showing contempt or scorn
mocking or scornful remarks

Examples

The speaker tried to continue despite the loud jeering from the crowd.
A chorus of jeering greeted the unpopular referee’s decision.
Her performance was met not with applause, but with outright jeering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mockery - ridicule or derision
  • Antonym: applause - praise shown by clapping, opposite of jeering

Etymology

From the verb 'jeer' (to mock), likely of imitative origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
Making rude and mocking remarks; expressing scorn or ridicule, especially loudly or publicly.
mocking or scornful; ridiculing

Examples

The fans erupted into jeering laughter when the referee made an unpopular call.
A jeering crowd gathered outside, shouting insults at the players.
His speech was interrupted by jeering remarks from the opposition.

Synset Examples

derisive laughter
a jeering crowd
her mocking smile
taunting shouts of ‘coward’ and ‘sissy’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mocking - making fun of someone or something in a cruel way
  • Synonym: taunting - intended to provoke or mock by insulting

Etymology

Present participle of 'jeer', likely from Middle English gere, of uncertain origin.
