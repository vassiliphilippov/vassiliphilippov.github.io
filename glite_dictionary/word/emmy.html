Emmy

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛmi / (AmE), / ˈɛmi / (BrE)
An annual award presented by the Academy of Television Arts and Sciences to recognize outstanding achievement in television.
annual television industry award

Examples

She was thrilled to win an Emmy for her role in the acclaimed series.
The ceremony will honor the year's best with various Emmy awards.
He has been nominated for an Emmy three times but has never won.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oscar - annual award for achievements in film
  • Synonym: Grammy - annual award for achievements in music
  • Synonym: Tony - annual award for achievements in theatre

Etymology

Named after 'immy', a nickname for an early television camera tube (the image orthicon).
