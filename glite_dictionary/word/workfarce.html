workfarce

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrkˌfɑrs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkˌfɑːs / (BrE)
an absurdly ineffective or ridiculous situation, especially in a work or business context
absurdly ineffective work situation
informal

Examples

The project review was a workfarce—no one took the agenda seriously.
After hours of pointless discussion, the strategy session ended up a complete workfarce.
Many employees see the annual budget meeting as a workfarce that achieves nothing.

Synset Examples

The meeting turned into a complete workfarce with no decisions made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farce - a ridiculous or absurd event or situation

Etymology

Blend of 'work' and 'farce'.
