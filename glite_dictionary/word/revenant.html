revenant

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛvənənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛvənənt / (BrE)
(of a person, spirit, or being) returning, especially from the dead or after a long absence.
returning, especially from the dead

Examples

The legend tells of a revenant soldier who haunts the old battlefield.
Some cultures fear the influence of a revenant spirit returning to the living world.
He swore he saw a revenant figure walking through the mist at midnight.

Synset Examples

a revenant ghost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: returned - having come back, especially after a long absence

Etymology

From French revenant, present participle of revenir (to return), from Latin revenīre.

WordNet ID: oewn-00595913-s

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛvənənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛvənənt / (BrE)
A person who returns from the dead, often featured in folklore or supernatural stories.
someone who returns from the dead

Examples

In the old tale, a revenant haunted the village, seeking justice for past wrongs.
The film features a chilling scene where the revenant appears at the stroke of midnight.
Many cultures have legends of a revenant returning to visit the living.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghost - The spirit of a dead person appearing to the living
  • Synonym: zombie - A corpse said to be revived by magic

Etymology

From French 'revenant' (returning), from revenir (to return), from Latin 're-' (again) + 'venire' (to come).

Synset POS: n
