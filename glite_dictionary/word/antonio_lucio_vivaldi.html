Antonio Lucio Vivaldi

1. PROPN

/ ænˌtoʊnioʊ luˈtʃioʊ viˈvɑldi / (AmE), / anˌtɔːniəʊ luˈtʃiːəʊ vɪˈvældi / (BrE)
(1678–1741) Italian Baroque composer and violinist, renowned for his concertos, especially 'The Four Seasons'.
Italian Baroque composer and violinist

Examples

Antonio Lucio Vivaldi was one of the most influential composers of the Baroque era.
Many orchestras regularly perform works by Antonio Lucio Vivaldi, such as his famous violin concertos.
As a violinist, Antonio Lucio Vivaldi captivated audiences across Europe with his virtuosic skill.
