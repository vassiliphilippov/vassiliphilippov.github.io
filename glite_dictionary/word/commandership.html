commandership

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmændərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːndəʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, office, or authority of a commander
position or office of a commander

Examples

His commandership of the battalion lasted three years.
She was honored for her exceptional commandership during the mission.
After achieving commandership, he began to reorganize the unit's structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command - to have authority over; to lead or direct

Etymology

From 'commander' + '-ship', denoting office or position.
