furor

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjʊrɔr / (AmE), / ˈfjʊərɔː / (BrE)
A sudden and intense public interest in something, often accompanied by excitement or enthusiasm.
sudden intense public excitement

Examples

The arrival of the new smartphone caused a furor among tech enthusiasts.
There was a furor over the designer's latest collection during fashion week.
The movie's unexpected success sparked a furor in the entertainment industry.

Synset Examples

he always follows the latest fads
it was all the rage that season

Related Terms

  • Synonym: craze - a widespread and short-lived enthusiasm for something
  • Synonym: fad - a style or activity that suddenly becomes popular but usually does not last long
  • Synonym: rage - something that is currently very popular or fashionable

Etymology

From Latin 'furor' (frenzy, madness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfjʊr ɔr, ˈfjʊr ər / (AmE), / ˈfjʊə rɔː / (BrE)
a sudden and intense outburst of excitement, anger, or protest, often among a crowd or in public
sudden public outburst or uproar

Examples

The scandal caused a furor across the entire nation, with people demanding answers.
There was a furor in the audience when the decision was announced unexpectedly.
His statement sparked a brief furor before the meeting returned to order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uproar - a loud and impassioned noise or disturbance
  • Synonym: commotion - a state of confused and noisy disturbance
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peace and quiet, without disturbance

Etymology

From Latin furor (rage, fury).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00555032-n
Synset POS: n
