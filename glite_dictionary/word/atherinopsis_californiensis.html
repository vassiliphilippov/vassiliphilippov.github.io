Atherinopsis californiensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæθəraɪˈnɑpsɪs ˌkæləˌfɔrniˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌæθərɪˈnɒpsɪs ˌkælɪˌfɔːniˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a relatively large species of silverside fish found along the Pacific coast of North America, also called jacksmelt
a large Pacific coast silverside fish

Examples

The Atherinopsis californiensis is often caught by anglers along the southern California coast.
Researchers studied the breeding patterns of Atherinopsis californiensis in various estuarine environments.
Local markets sometimes sell fresh Atherinopsis californiensis, commonly known as jacksmelt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jacksmelt - common name for Atherinopsis californiensis, a coastal fish species

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: 'Atherinopsis' (genus of silversides) + 'californiensis' (from California).
