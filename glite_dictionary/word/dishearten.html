to dishearten

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈhɑːrtn̩ / (AmE), / dɪsˈhɑːtn̩ / (BrE)
to cause someone to lose hope, enthusiasm, or confidence
make someone lose hope or enthusiasm

Examples

The news of the project's cancellation really disheartened the team.
Don't let one failure dishearten you from trying again.
The harsh criticism was enough to dishearten even the most dedicated student.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to discourage - cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: to encourage - give someone support, confidence, or hope

Etymology

From Old French 'desherter', from des- (expressing reversal) + 'herte' (heart).
