cataphatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkætəˈfætɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈfatɪk / (BrE)
(of religious belief or theology) expressing the nature of God through positive or affirmative statements, rather than by negation.
affirmatively describing God in theology

Examples

The cataphatic approach seeks to describe God through positive attributes and affirmations.
Many theologians debate whether a cataphatic or apophatic theology offers a truer understanding of the divine.
Her writings are known for their cataphatic emphasis, focusing on what can be affirmed about God.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: apophatic - describing God by negation, focusing on what God is not; opposite of cataphatic

Etymology

From Greek kataphatikos (affirmative), from kataphasis (affirmation).
