cohesive

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈhiːsɪv / (AmE), / kəʊˈhiːsɪv / (BrE)
forming a united whole; causing elements to stick or work together effectively
forming a united whole; sticking together

Examples

The group became more cohesive after they spent a weekend working together on the project.
A cohesive argument is well-structured and logically connected.
The team was praised for its cohesive approach to solving problems.

Synset Examples

a cohesive agent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adhesive - able to stick fast to a surface or object; sticky

Etymology

From Latin cohaesivus, from cohaerere (to stick together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00053804-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ koʊˈhiːsɪv / (AmE), / kəʊˈhiːsɪv / (BrE)
Forming a united whole; characterized by or causing cohesion.
forming a united whole

Examples

The new team worked together as a truly cohesive unit.
Effective communication helped make the community more cohesive.
Her argument was clear, logical, and highly cohesive.

Synset Examples

a cohesive organization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coherent - logically connected; consistent or making sense as a whole
  • Antonym: fragmented - broken into pieces; not forming a unified whole

Etymology

From Latin cohaesus, past participle of cohaerēre (to cling together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02486522-s
Synset POS: s
