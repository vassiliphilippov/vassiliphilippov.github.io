Trichodesmium

1. NOUN

/ trɪˌkoʊˈdɛzmiəm / (AmE), / trɪˌkəʊˈdɛzmɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of filamentous blue-green algae (cyanobacteria) found chiefly in tropical and subtropical ocean waters, known for forming large colonies and sometimes causing algal blooms.
genus of filamentous blue-green algae

Examples

The bloom of Trichodesmium discolored the surface of the tropical sea.
Scientists collected samples of Trichodesmium to study its ecological impact.
Large colonies of Trichodesmium are sometimes visible from boats as yellowish streaks.

Etymology

From Greek 'trichos' (hair) + 'desmos' (band), referring to its hair-like filaments.
