Phalangida

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlæŋɪdə / (AmE), / fəˈlæŋɪdə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of arachnids commonly known as harvestmen, characterized by a compact body and long legs.
order of arachnids (harvestmen)

Examples

The order Phalangida is popularly known as harvestmen or daddy longlegs.
Most species in Phalangida are harmless and commonly found in gardens and forests.
Researchers studying Phalangida have catalogued thousands of species worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvestman - a common name for members of Phalangida, arachnids with long legs

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek phalanx (line of soldiers, referring to the legs).
