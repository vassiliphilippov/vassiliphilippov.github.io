Polypodium scouleri

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊlɪˈpoʊdiəm ˈskaʊləri / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈpəʊdiəm ˈskaʊləri / (BrE)
A species of stiff, leathery-leaved fern from western North America, with ovate fronds divided to the midrib.
a fern species native to western North America

Examples

Polypodium scouleri is commonly found growing on coastal rocks and cliffs in California.
The tough, leathery leaves make Polypodium scouleri well adapted to its harsh environment.
Botanists often study Polypodium scouleri to understand plant adaptation to saline conditions.

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus name Polypodium, species named after John Scouler, Scottish naturalist.
