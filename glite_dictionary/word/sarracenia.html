Sarracenia

1. NOUN

/ sæɹ.əˈsiː.ni.ə / (AmE), / sær.əˈsiː.ni.ə / (BrE)
A genus of North American pitcher plants, commonly called trumpet pitchers, characterized by tubular, pitcher-shaped leaves that trap insects.
genus of North American pitcher plants

Examples

The Sarracenia is native to the wetlands of North America.
Botanists study how Sarracenia traps and digests insects in its tubular leaves.
Several species of Sarracenia are cultivated as ornamental plants for their striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitcher plant - a carnivorous plant with a deep cavity filled with digestive liquid

Etymology

Named after Michel Sarrazin, a French physician and naturalist.
