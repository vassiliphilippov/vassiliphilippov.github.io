nil

1. NOUN

/ nɪl / (AmE), / nɪl / (BrE)
no amount; nothing; zero, especially as a score in sports or in formal language.
nothing; zero (esp. in sports)

Examples

The final score was two to nil in favor of the home team.
Interest in the project has dropped to almost nil in recent months.
With nil resources left, they had to abandon the expedition.

Synset Examples

it looked like nothing I had ever seen before
reduced to nil all the work we had done
we racked up a pathetic goose egg
it was all for naught
I didn't hear zilch about it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zero - the number 0; nothing
  • Synonym: nothing - not anything; no single thing

Etymology

From Latin *nihil* (nothing).
