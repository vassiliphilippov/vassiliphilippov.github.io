ethician

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈθɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈθɪʃən / (BrE)
a philosopher who specializes in the study of ethics; an expert in moral philosophy.
philosopher specializing in ethics

Examples

As an ethician, she often speaks at conferences about bioethical dilemmas in medicine.
The debate was led by a renowned ethician who discussed the moral implications of artificial intelligence.
Many universities now offer programs for aspiring ethicians interested in business ethics and corporate responsibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethicist - an expert in ethics; one who studies moral philosophy

Etymology

From 'ethic' + '-ian', modeled after similar terms like 'physician'.
