crunch

1. VERB

/ krʌntʃ / (AmE), / krʌntʃ / (BrE)
to chew or crush with the teeth, making a grinding or crackling sound
chew noisily with a cracking sound

Examples

She likes to crunch apples while watching TV.
He crunched on the ice cubes absentmindedly during the meeting.
The dog was crunching a bone in the backyard.

Synset Examples

The children crunched the celery sticks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: munch - to chew steadily or vigorously, especially with a loud noise
  • Synonym: grind - to crush or break into small pieces, especially between two hard surfaces

2. NOUN

/ krʌntʃ / (AmE), / krʌntʃ / (BrE)
A sound made when something is crushed or chewed noisily.
noisy crushing or chewing sound

Examples

The only thing he could hear was the crunch of dry leaves beneath his boots.
She bit into an apple, enjoying the satisfying crunch.
There was a loud crunch as the car drove over the gravel.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crackle - a series of small, sharp sounds
  • Synonym: rustle - a soft, muffled crackling sound, like leaves or paper

Etymology
