ricochet

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪkəˌʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɪkəʃeɪ / (BrE)
a shot or projectile that rebounds off a surface at an angle rather than embedding or stopping
glancing rebound of a projectile

Examples

The bullet struck the wall and made a loud ricochet before embedding in the ceiling.
We heard a ricochet as the stone skipped off the sidewalk.
The ricochet of the puck caught the goalie off guard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebound - the action of bouncing back after hitting a surface

Etymology

From French 'ricochet', of uncertain origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈrɪkəˌʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɪkəʃeɪ / (BrE)
(of a projectile or object) to bounce off a surface after striking it, especially at an angle.
bounce off after striking a surface

Examples

The bullet ricocheted off the metal door and hit the wall.
He threw a stone, and it ricocheted across the surface of the lake.
Shrapnel ricocheted around the room after the explosion.

Synset Examples

The rubber ball bounced
These particles do not resile but they unite after they collide

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebound - to spring back after hitting something
  • Synonym: to deflect - to cause to change direction after hitting something

Etymology

From French 'ricochet' (the skipping of a stone), possibly from 'ricocheter' (to skip, as a stone across water).
