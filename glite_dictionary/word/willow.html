willow

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
a machine with revolving spikes used for opening and cleaning raw textile fibers in the textile industry
machine for cleaning raw textile fibers

Examples

The old mill still uses a willow to clean wool before spinning.
A willow effectively removes dust and seeds from the raw cotton.
Workers fed flax into the willow as part of the traditional linen-making process.

Etymology

From the resemblance of the machine's action to that of a willow branch 'willowing' (moving back and forth to separate fibers).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A deciduous tree or shrub of the genus Salix, typically having narrow leaves and slender branches, often found near water.
deciduous tree or shrub (genus Salix)

Examples

We sat in the shade of a large willow by the river.
The branches of the willow tree swept gracefully over the pond.
In spring, the willow produces soft, silvery catkins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osier - a type of willow with flexible branches used for basketry

Etymology

Old English 'welig', related to Middle Low German 'wilge' and Old High German 'wilga' (willow).
