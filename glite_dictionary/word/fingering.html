fingering

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of touching or handling something with one's fingers
touching with the fingers

Examples

The detective noticed the fingering of the drawer’s handle by the suspect.
Repeated fingering of the artwork can damage its fragile surface.
He was caught fingering the merchandise in the shop, raising suspicions of theft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touching - the act of making physical contact with something

Etymology

From 'finger' (Old English 'fingor') + '-ing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡərɪŋ / (BrE)
the arrangement or technique of placing the fingers on the keys, strings, or holes of a musical instrument to produce particular notes or passages
method of placing fingers on instruments

Examples

Proper fingering is important for playing the piano quickly and accurately.
The sheet music shows recommended fingering for beginners.
Changing the fingering made the guitar chord easier to play.

Etymology

From the verb 'finger' + -ing, in the musical sense since the 18th century.
