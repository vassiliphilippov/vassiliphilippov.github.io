hair-splitting

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrˌsplɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈheəˌsplɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making overly fine or trivial distinctions, especially in argument or analysis
making overly fine distinctions

Examples

The meeting deteriorated into hair-splitting about insignificant points.
I wish people would stop hair-splitting and focus on the real issues.
Her hair-splitting remarks frustrated everyone trying to move forward.

Synset Examples

We don’t have time for hair-splitting arguments.
His hair-splitting analysis confused the main issue.
They got into a hair-splitting debate over minor details.
The lawyer argued hair-splittingly over contract terms.
She explained the rules hair-splittingly for clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitpicking - giving too much attention to unimportant details

Etymology

From the metaphor of splitting a hair, something extremely thin, to emphasize excessive attention to minute details.
