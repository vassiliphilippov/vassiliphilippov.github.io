silverpoint

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvərˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvəˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
a drawing made with a silver-tipped instrument on specially prepared paper or parchment, especially popular during the 15th and 16th centuries
drawing made with silver-tipped instrument

Examples

The museum displayed a rare silverpoint by Leonardo da Vinci.
Artists prized silverpoint for its fine lines and delicate shading.
She experimented with silverpoint before moving on to other drawing techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawing - a picture or diagram made with a pencil, pen, or crayon

Etymology

From 'silver' (the metal) + 'point' (referring to the pointed instrument used).
