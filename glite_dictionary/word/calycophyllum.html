Calycophyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪkoʊˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌkælɪkəʊˈfɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of medium to large tropical American trees with shiny reddish-brown, shredding bark.
genus of tropical American trees

Examples

The bark of the Calycophyllum tree is known for its distinctive reddish hue and peeling texture.
Botanists identified the specimen as a Calycophyllum based on its large leaves and robust trunk.
Several species in the Calycophyllum genus are valued for their hardwood in Central and South America.

Etymology

From Greek kalux (calyx) + phyllon (leaf), referring to the leaf-like calyx lobes.
