riflebird

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪfəlˌbɜrd / (AmE), / ˈraɪfəlˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A velvety black Australian bird of paradise, notable for its green and purple iridescence on the head and tail.
Australian bird of paradise with iridescent coloring

Examples

The male riflebird performed its elaborate dance to attract a mate.
We spotted a riflebird in the rainforest, its feathers gleaming with iridescence.
The vivid colors on the riflebird's head make it a favorite among birdwatchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bird of paradise - any of various birds of the family Paradisaeidae

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of the male's black plumage to the uniform of a British rifleman.
