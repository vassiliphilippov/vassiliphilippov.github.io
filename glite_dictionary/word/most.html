most

1. DET

/ moʊst / (AmE), / məʊst / (BrE)
The largest number of; the greatest in quantity or amount, especially when referring to countable items.
greatest in number or amount

Examples

Most people arrived early to get good seats for the show.
He has the most books of anyone on the team.
Most children enjoy playing outside during the summer.

Synset Examples

who has the most apples?
most people like eggs
most fishes have fins

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fewest - smallest number of; superlative of 'few'

Etymology

From Old English māst (greatest in amount), related to Old High German meist.

2. ADV

/ moʊst / (AmE), / məʊst / (BrE)
(formal) Used as an adverb to mean 'very', especially to add emphasis to an adjective or adverb.
very; to a great degree (adverb)

Examples

It was a most delightful evening spent in good company.
She gave me a most helpful answer to my question.
The news was most unexpected and left everyone surprised.

Synset Examples

a most welcome relief

Related Terms

  • Synonym: very - used as an adverb to mean 'to a high degree'

3. ADV

/ moʊst / (AmE), / məʊst / (BrE)
used to form the superlative degree of adjectives and adverbs, indicating the greatest in amount, degree, or extent.
greatest in amount or degree (superlative form)

Examples

This was the most challenging exam I have ever taken.
She is the most talented artist in her class.
Mount Everest is the most famous mountain in the world.

Synset Examples

the king cobra is the most dangerous snake

Related Terms

  • Antonym: least - smallest in amount or degree (superlative form)

Etymology

From Old English māst, akin to German meist (most).

Source: WordNet

4. ADJ

/ moʊst / (AmE), / məʊst / (BrE)
the greatest in amount, extent, or degree; the superlative form of 'much' used with uncountable nouns or after 'the' to show something is at the highest level compared to others
superlative of 'much'; greatest amount or degree

Examples

Of all the teams in the league, she scored the most goals this season.
Whoever has the most patience will succeed in this tricky game.
He invested the most money in the project and therefore took the biggest risk.

Synset Examples

made the most money he could
what attracts the most attention?
made the most of a bad deal

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: much - large amount or extent (used with uncountable nouns)
  • Degree Lower: more - comparative of 'much', a greater amount or degree

Etymology

From Old English māst, superlative of micel (much).
