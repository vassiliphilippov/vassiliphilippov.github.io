Cyprinidae

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈprɪnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / sɪˈprɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of freshwater fish, including carps, minnows, and their relatives
family of freshwater fish (carp, minnow, etc.)

Examples

Many species in the Cyprinidae family are popular in freshwater aquariums.
The common carp belongs to the Cyprinidae, which is the largest family of freshwater fish.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Cyprinidae in the rivers of Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From New Latin Cyprinidae, from Cyprinus (the carp genus) + -idae (family suffix).
