tradecraft

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪdˌkræft / (AmE), / ˈtreɪdˌkrɑːft / (BrE)
the skills and techniques acquired through practical experience in a profession, especially those used in espionage or intelligence work
skills and techniques in a profession, especially espionage

Examples

The agency regularly updates its tradecraft to keep up with new security threats.
He admired the old spy's impeccable tradecraft during covert operations.
Mastering tradecraft takes years of hands-on experience and training.

Synset Examples

instructional designers are trained in something that might be called tradecraft
the CIA chief of station accepted responsibility for his agents' failures of tradecraft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expertise - specialized skill or knowledge in a particular field
  • Antonym: amateurism - lack of skill or formal training in a field

Etymology

Formed from 'trade' (profession) + 'craft' (skill), reflecting the specialized skills required in certain trades.
