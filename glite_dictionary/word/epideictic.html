epideictic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛpɪˈdaɪktɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdaɪktɪk / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) intended chiefly for display or ceremonial purposes, rather than for persuasion or instruction.
intended for rhetorical display

Examples

The professor discussed the influence of epideictic rhetoric in classical oratory.
The ceremony featured several epideictic speeches celebrating the community's achievements.
His style was distinctly epideictic, focusing more on elaborate language than on convincing the audience.

Synset Examples

epideictic orations

Etymology

From Greek *epideiktikos* (‘fit for display or demonstration’), from *epideiknynai* (‘to show, display’).
