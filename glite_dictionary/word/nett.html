nett

1. ADJ

/ nɛt / (AmE), / nɛt / (BrE)
(chiefly British) remaining after all deductions have been made; final or ultimate amount.
remaining after deductions
british

Examples

Their nett income was much lower after taxes and other expenses were deducted.
The company reported a nett loss for the quarter despite high sales figures.
You should always check the nett weight of a product, not just the gross weight.

Synset Examples

net profit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: net - remaining after all deductions; standard spelling, especially in American English

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'net' from Old French 'net' (clean, neat), originally from Latin 'nitidus' (clean, bright).
