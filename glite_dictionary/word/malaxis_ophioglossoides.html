Malaxis ophioglossoides

1. NOUN

/ məˈlæksɪs oʊˌfaɪəɡlɒˈsɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / məˈlæksɪs əʊˌfʌɪəɡlɒˈsɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A North American orchid species having a solitary leaf and threadlike-petaled flowers.
North American orchid species

Examples

The tiny flowers of Malaxis ophioglossoides can be difficult to spot in the wild.
Many botanists are excited to find Malaxis ophioglossoides growing in remote forest areas.
In some regions, Malaxis ophioglossoides is considered quite rare and protected.

Etymology

From Latin genus name Malaxis and species epithet ophioglossoides, meaning 'resembling Ophioglossum' (a genus of ferns).
