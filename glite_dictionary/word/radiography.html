radiography

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdiˈɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the process of producing images of the inside of objects or the body using X-rays or similar forms of radiation
production of internal images using radiation
medical

Examples

Modern hospitals rely heavily on radiography to help diagnose broken bones and other internal injuries.
She decided to specialize in radiography after becoming fascinated with medical imaging technology.
Advances in digital radiography have greatly improved the clarity and accuracy of diagnostic images.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to x-ray - a type of electromagnetic radiation used in radiography

Etymology

From radio- (radiation) + -graphy (from Greek -graphia, 'writing')

2. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdiˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌreɪdɪˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the process of creating images of the interior of objects using radiation other than visible light, especially for medical or scientific purposes
imaging using radiation other than visible light
medical

Examples

Modern medicine relies heavily on radiography to diagnose and monitor a wide range of conditions.
Advancements in radiography have greatly improved our ability to detect bone fractures.
She specialized in veterinary radiography to help diagnose illnesses in animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photography - the art or practice of taking and processing photographs

Etymology

From radio- (relating to radiation) + -graphy (process of recording), influenced by French radiographie.
