petrous

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛtɹəs / (AmE), / ˈpɛtɹəs / (BrE)
(of bone, especially the temporal bone) extremely hard and dense, resembling stone in texture and hardness.
very hard, stone-like (esp. bone)
medical

Examples

The petrous part of the temporal bone protects delicate structures within the inner ear.
Scientists studied the petrous bone to extract ancient DNA due to its density.
A fracture extending into the petrous temporal bone can cause serious complications.

Etymology

From Latin petrosus (stony), from petra (rock).
