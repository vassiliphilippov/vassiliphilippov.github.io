to confuse

1. VERB

/ kənˈfjuːz / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to make someone unable to think clearly or to mix up their understanding of something
make unable to think clearly

Examples

The complex instructions confuse many first-time users.
She didn't mean to confuse you with all those details.
The conflicting reports only served to confuse the investigation.

Synset Examples

These questions confuse even the experts
This question completely threw me
This question befuddled even the teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to perplex - to make someone confused or puzzled
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make something easier to understand

Etymology

From Latin 'confundere' (to mix together, confuse).

2. VERB

/ kənˈfjuz / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to think wrongly that one person or thing is another; to mistake one thing for another
to mistake one thing for another

Examples

I always confuse her name with her sister's.
Some people confuse Austria with Australia.
Don't confuse kindness with weakness.

Synset Examples

you are confusing me with the other candidate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mix up - to mistake one thing or person for another

Etymology

From Latin confusus, past participle of confundere (to mingle together, confuse).

3. VERB

/ kənˈfjuz / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to make something unclear, indistinct, or difficult to understand
make unclear or indistinct

Examples

The technical jargon only served to confuse the audience further.
The fog began to confuse the outlines of the distant mountains.
His contradictory statements confuse everyone trying to follow the argument.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to clarify - make clear or easier to understand
  • Synonym: obscure - make difficult to see or understand

Etymology

From Latin 'confundere' (to pour together, confuse), via Old French 'confus'.

4. VERB

/ kənˈfjuz / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to mix up elements so that their order is not clear or logical; to assemble things without order or sense
mix up elements without order

Examples

If you confuse the files, it will be impossible to find anything.
Please don't confuse the puzzle pieces by mixing them all together.
She accidentally confused the ingredients while preparing the recipe.

Synset Examples

She jumbles the words when she is supposed to write a sentence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jumble - to mix things together without order
  • Antonym: order - to arrange things neatly or methodically; opposite of confuse

Etymology

From Latin 'confundere', meaning 'to mingle together'.
