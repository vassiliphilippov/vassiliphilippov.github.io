Amundsen

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːm.ʌn.sən / (AmE), / ˈam.ʊn.sən / (BrE)
Roald Amundsen (1872–1928), Norwegian explorer, first to traverse the Northwest Passage and to reach the South Pole.
Norwegian polar explorer (1872–1928)

Examples

Amundsen led the first successful expedition to the South Pole in 1911.
Many historians consider Amundsen one of the greatest polar explorers of all time.
The Arctic voyage undertaken by Amundsen remains a remarkable feat in the history of exploration.

Etymology

From the Norwegian surname 'Amundsen', meaning 'son of Amund'.
