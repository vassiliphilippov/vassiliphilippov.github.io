hound

1. NOUN

/ haʊnd / (AmE), / haʊnd / (BrE)
A dog of a breed used for hunting, typically characterized by large drooping ears and a keen sense of smell or sight.
a hunting dog breed

Examples

The hunter released his hound to track the scent of the deer.
A pack of hounds followed the trail through the dense forest.
Their family has bred hounds for generations to help with hunting.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dog - domesticated animal, common pet, Canis familiaris
  • Synonym: retriever - a breed of dog used for retrieving game

Etymology

From Old English *hund* (dog; hound), related to German *Hund* and Dutch *hond*.

2. VERB

/ haʊnd / (AmE), / haʊnd / (BrE)
to pursue or harass someone persistently and relentlessly
to pursue relentlessly

Examples

The paparazzi continued to hound the actor despite requests for privacy.
She felt hounded by debt collectors day and night.
The police were accused of hounding the innocent man for years.

Synset Examples

The hunters traced the deer into the woods
the detectives hounded the suspect until they found him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harass - to subject someone to repeated or persistent attacks or pressure
  • Synonym: to persecute - to treat someone cruelly and unfairly, especially because of their beliefs
  • Antonym: to leave alone - to stop bothering or pursuing someone

Etymology

From Old English 'hund' (dog), via figurative use of hunting dogs' relentless pursuit.
