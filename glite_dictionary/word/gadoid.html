gadoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡædɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡadɔɪd / (BrE)
A fish of the family Gadidae, such as cod or haddock, characterized by soft fins.
fish of the family Gadidae

Examples

The North Atlantic gadoid stocks have been declining for decades due to overfishing.
Many commercially important fishes, such as cod and haddock, are classified as gadoids.
Research on gadoid species helps scientists understand the impact of environmental changes on marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - a type of large marine fish of the family Gadidae
  • Synonym: haddock - a species of gadoid fish closely related to cod

Etymology

From modern Latin Gadidae (the cod family), from Gadus (genus name for cod).
