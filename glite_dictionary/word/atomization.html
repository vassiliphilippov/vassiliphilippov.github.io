atomization

1. NOUN

/ ˌædəməˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌætəməˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of breaking something into very fine particles or droplets
breaking into fine particles or droplets

Examples

The atomization of fuel improves combustion efficiency in modern engines.
Spraying paint involves the atomization of liquid into tiny droplets.
The process of atomization is crucial in the manufacture of metal powders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispersion - the act of distributing things over a wide area
  • Antonym: aggregation - the formation of a number of things into a cluster

Etymology

From atom + -ization, reflecting the process of making into atoms or tiny particles.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæɾəmɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌatəməˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of annihilating or destroying something by breaking it down into atoms or tiny particles.
breaking into atoms; annihilation

Examples

The atomization of the material resulted in its complete destruction at the molecular level.
Nuclear explosions produce atomization effects, dispersing matter into its smallest components.
Some industrial processes aim for total atomization to create fine powders or sprays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annihilation - complete destruction or obliteration

Etymology

From atom + -ization, derived from the Greek 'atomos' (indivisible) and the suffix '-ization' indicating a process.
