gillyflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪliˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈɡɪlɪˌflaʊə / (BrE)
any of several Old World plants, especially species of the genus Matthiola or Dianthus, cultivated for their fragrant, brightly colored flowers.
fragrant flowering plant, often a stock or carnation

Examples

She picked a gillyflower from the garden and placed it in her hair.
The ancient walled gardens were full of bright gillyflowers in spring.
In old poems, the gillyflower is often praised for its sweet scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnation - a double-flowered cultivated variety of clove pink, Dianthus caryophyllus

Etymology

From Middle English gilofre, from Old French giroflée, from Greek karyophyllon (clove), due to the clove-like scent of the flower.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪliˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈɡɪlɪˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant with fragrant, usually double flowers, often pink to reddish-purple, cultivated in many varieties and colors.
fragrant garden flower; stock or carnation

Examples

She planted a row of gillyflowers along the garden path for their color and scent.
The scent of gillyflowers filled the warm summer air by the porch.
He admired the vibrant gillyflower blooms in various shades of pink and red.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock - a garden plant with similar fragrant flowers
  • Synonym: carnation - a related flower sometimes called gillyflower

Etymology

From Old French 'giroflée', from Greek 'karyophyllon' (clove), referring to the spicy scent.
