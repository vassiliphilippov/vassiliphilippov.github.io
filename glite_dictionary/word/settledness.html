settledness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtəldnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛtəldnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being settled, fixed, or established in a place, position, or condition.
state of being settled or established

Examples

Her settledness in the new city gave her great peace of mind.
Over time, the village gained a sense of settledness that attracted more residents.
Despite years of travel, he never found the settledness he was searching for.

Synset Examples

Whatever his settledness into place, Stern knows from his own history, from the Pennsylvania seasons, that there can be no settledness without the emergence into the scene of radical diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stability - the state of being firmly fixed or not likely to change
  • Antonym: unsettledness - the state of lacking stability or security
