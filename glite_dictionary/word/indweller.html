indweller

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌdwɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌdwɛlə / (BrE)
a person or being who lives in or inhabits a particular place
person who inhabits a place

Examples

The forest has long been considered the home of mysterious indwellers by local villagers.
Each indweller played a unique role in the small island's community life.
It is said that the cave's only indweller has never been seen by outsiders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhabitant - a person who lives in a particular place

Etymology

From Middle English indweller, from in + dweller.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌdwɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌdwɛlə / (BrE)
a spirit, force, or principle that resides within a person, place, or thing, especially in religious or philosophical contexts
one who or that which dwells within

Examples

Some theologians believe that the Holy Spirit is the indweller of each believer.
The poet wrote about an indweller that guides human actions from within.
Mystics spoke of an inner indweller shaping the soul's journey.

Synset Examples

the Holy Spirit is the indweller of the church and its members

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhabitant - a person or animal that lives in a place

Etymology

From the verb 'indwell' (to dwell within) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13519416-n
Synset POS: n
