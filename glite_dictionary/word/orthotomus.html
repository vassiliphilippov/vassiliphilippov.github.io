Orthotomus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrˈθɑːtəˌməs / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒtəməs / (BrE)
A genus of small Asian birds known as tailorbirds, noted for their habit of sewing leaves together to build their nests.
genus of tailorbirds

Examples

The Orthotomus species are remarkably skilled at weaving intricate nests from leaves.
Researchers observed an Orthotomus building its distinctive nest in the garden.
Many birdwatchers travel to Southeast Asia hoping to spot a rare Orthotomus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tailorbird - a small Asian bird that stitches leaves to make its nest

Etymology

From Greek 'orthos' (straight) and 'tomos' (cutting), referring to their nest-building behavior.

2. NOUN

/ ɔrˈθɑtəməs / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒtəməs / (BrE)
A genus of small passerine birds known as tailorbirds, found in tropical Asia and noted for their habit of stitching leaves to build nests.
genus of tailorbirds in tropical Asia

Examples

The genus Orthotomus is best known for its intricate nest-building techniques.
Several species within Orthotomus can be found throughout the forests of Southeast Asia.
Researchers observed the nesting habits of Orthotomus during their fieldwork in Malaysia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tailorbird - any small bird of the genus Orthotomus, known for sewing leaves together to form nests

Etymology

From Greek 'orthos' (straight) and 'tomos' (cutting), referring to the bird's stitching behavior.
