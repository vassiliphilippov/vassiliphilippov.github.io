azo

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪzoʊ / (AmE), / ˈeɪzəʊ / (BrE)
(of a chemical compound) relating to or containing the azo group (–N=N–).
relating to the azo group in chemistry
medical

Examples

The bright red color comes from an azo dye.
Many synthetic fibers are colored using azo compounds.
This azo linkage can be reduced to form new chemical structures.

Etymology

From French 'azote' (nitrogen), based on Greek 'azotos' (lifeless), referring to nitrogen compounds.
