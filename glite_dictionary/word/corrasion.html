corrasion

1. NOUN

/ kəˈreɪʒən / (AmE), / kəˈreɪʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process of erosion caused by the mechanical wearing away of rock or soil by friction, typically due to particles carried by wind, water, or ice
erosion by friction or abrasion

Examples

The river's pebbles cause intense corrasion of the rocks along its bed.
Wind-blown sand can lead to significant corrasion on exposed rock surfaces in deserts.
Glacial movement often results in corrasion, grinding down the valley floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abrasion - the process of scraping or wearing away at something
  • Antonym: corrosion - gradual destruction of materials, usually metals, by chemical reaction

Etymology

From Latin 'corradere' (to scrape together), via Late Latin 'corrasio' (scraping off).
