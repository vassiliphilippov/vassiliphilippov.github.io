firsthand

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɝstˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈfɜːstˌhænd / (BrE)
(of information or experience) received directly from the original source; not through an intermediary.
direct from original source

Examples

She gave a firsthand account of the events she witnessed.
I prefer to receive firsthand information rather than rely on rumors.
Traveling abroad offers the opportunity to gain firsthand experience of different cultures.

Synset Examples

firsthand information

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secondhand - acquired from others rather than directly from the original source

Etymology

From 'first' + 'hand', indicating the first or original hand (person) involved.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01858740-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADV

/ ˈfɝstˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈfɜːstˌhænd / (BrE)
In a direct way, from the original source or personal experience.
directly, from the original source

Examples

She experienced the excitement of the concert firsthand.
He wanted to see the results firsthand before making a decision.
Journalists report news firsthand to ensure accuracy.

Synset Examples

I heard this story firsthand

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secondhand - not directly from the original source; indirect

Etymology

Formed from 'first' + 'hand', originally referring to goods acquired directly from the producer rather than through intermediaries.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00342127-r
Synset POS: r
