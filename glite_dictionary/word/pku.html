PKU

1. NOUN

/ piː keɪ ˈjuː / (AmE), / piː keɪ ˈjuː / (BrE)
a genetic disorder in which the body cannot metabolize phenylalanine, leading to its accumulation and potentially causing intellectual disability if untreated.
genetic disorder affecting metabolism
medical

Examples

Babies are routinely screened for PKU shortly after birth.
A strict low-protein diet is essential for children diagnosed with PKU.
Left untreated, PKU can lead to severe intellectual disabilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenylketonuria - a genetic metabolic disorder causing a buildup of phenylalanine

Etymology

Acronym of phenylketonuria.
