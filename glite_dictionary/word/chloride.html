chloride

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔrˌaɪd / (AmE), / ˈklɔːr.aɪd / (BrE)
a compound consisting of chlorine combined with another element or group, especially a salt of hydrochloric acid containing the chloride ion (Cl−)
salt or compound of chlorine (Cl−)

Examples

The doctor checked my blood chloride levels during the routine examination.
Common table salt is actually sodium chloride.
Many swimming pools use chloride compounds to keep the water clean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorine - a chemical element with symbol Cl, a greenish-yellow gas

Etymology

From French *chlorure* (chloride), from Greek *chloros* (green-yellow), referencing elemental chlorine.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɔːr.aɪd / (AmE), / ˈklɔː.raɪd / (BrE)
a compound in which chlorine is combined with another element or group, especially a salt of hydrochloric acid
a compound containing chlorine

Examples

Table salt is made from sodium chloride, which dissolves easily in water.
Doctors often check for the level of chloride in a patient's blood.
Many industrial processes require the use of chloride compounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorine - a chemical element with symbol Cl, a greenish-yellow gas

Etymology

From French *chlorure* (chloride), from Greek *chloros* (greenish-yellow).
