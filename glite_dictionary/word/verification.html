verification

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɛrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Law) An affidavit attached to a statement confirming the truth of that statement.
legal affidavit confirming truth
legal

Examples

The lawyer filed a verification to confirm the accuracy of the client's statement.
A signed verification is required to support this legal document in court.
The court will not accept the claim without a proper verification attached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affidavit - a written statement confirmed by oath, used as evidence in court

Etymology

From Latin verificatio, from verificare ('to make true').

additional proof or evidence that confirms something is correct or true
additional proof or confirmation

Examples

The scientist requested verification of the results before publishing them.
We need independent verification of her story to ensure its accuracy.
The new software update required user verification before installation.

Synset Examples

fossils provided further confirmation of the evolutionary theory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confirmation - proof that something is true or correct

Etymology

From Medieval Latin verificatio (proof of truth), from Latin verus (true) + facere (to make).
