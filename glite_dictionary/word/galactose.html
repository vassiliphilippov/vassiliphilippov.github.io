galactose

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlækˌtoʊs / (AmE), / ɡəˈlaktəʊs / (BrE)
a simple sugar that is a component of lactose and is found in milk and other dairy products
a simple sugar found in milk
medical

Examples

Galactose is produced in the body by the breakdown of lactose from milk.
Some people have difficulty metabolizing galactose, leading to a rare genetic disorder.
The structure of galactose is very similar to that of glucose, differing only at one carbon atom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glucose - a simple sugar and important energy source
  • Synonym: fructose - a simple sugar found in fruit

Etymology

From Greek gala (milk) + -ose (sugar suffix).
