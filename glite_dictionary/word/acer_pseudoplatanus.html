Acer pseudoplatanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪsər ˌsuːdoʊˈplætənəs / (AmE), / ˈeɪsə ˌsjuːdəʊˈplætənəs / (BrE)
A Eurasian maple tree with pale grey bark that peels in flakes and leaves with five ovate lobes that turn yellow in autumn; commonly known as the sycamore maple.
species of Eurasian maple tree

Examples

The Acer pseudoplatanus is often planted in parks for its attractive foliage and shade.
In autumn, the leaves of Acer pseudoplatanus turn a brilliant yellow before falling.
The pale, flaky bark of Acer pseudoplatanus distinguishes it from other maple species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycamore maple - common name for Acer pseudoplatanus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Acer' meaning maple and 'pseudoplatanus' meaning false plane tree.
