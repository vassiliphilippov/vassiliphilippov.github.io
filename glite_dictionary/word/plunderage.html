plunderage

1. NOUN

/ ˈplʌndərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈplʌndərɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act of stealing goods, especially the embezzlement or theft of cargo from a ship
theft of goods, especially from a ship

Examples

The crew was charged with plunderage after goods mysteriously disappeared from the ship’s hold.
Insurance policies often exclude losses caused by plunderage during shipping.
Suspicions of plunderage grew when several valuable items were missing on arrival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plundering - the act of stealing goods, usually by force or in war
  • Synonym: embezzlement - theft or misappropriation of funds or property under one's control
  • Antonym: restoration - the return of stolen goods to their rightful owner

Etymology

Formed from 'plunder' (rob, steal) + suffix '-age' (denoting action or result of an action).
