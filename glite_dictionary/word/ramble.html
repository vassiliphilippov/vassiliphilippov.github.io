ramble

1. NOUN

/ ˈræm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈræm.bəl / (BrE)
a walk taken for pleasure, typically without a definite route or purpose, often in the countryside
leisurely, aimless walk

Examples

We enjoyed a peaceful ramble through the woods on Sunday afternoon.
Their afternoon ramble took them over winding hills and along hidden streams.
She set off on a solitary ramble without any destination in mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amble - a relaxed, easy walk; stroll
  • Degree Higher: hike - a long walk, usually in the countryside or wilderness
  • Synonym: stroll - walk in a leisurely way, especially for pleasure

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *rammelen* (to wander about).

2. VERB

/ ˈræm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈræm.bəl / (BrE)
to walk or travel aimlessly or without a fixed route, especially for pleasure or in search of something
walk or travel aimlessly

Examples

During their vacation, they would often ramble through the countryside for hours.
Deer sometimes ramble into the outskirts of the city in search of food.
He likes to ramble around markets, discovering small shops and new foods.

Synset Examples

The gypsies roamed the woods
roving vagabonds
the wandering Jew
The cattle roam across the prairie
the laborers drift from one town to the next
They rolled from town to town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wander - move about without a fixed course or aim
  • Synonym: to roam - move aimlessly or freely over an area
  • Antonym: stride - walk with long, decisive steps in a specified direction

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *ramblen*, of uncertain origin, perhaps linked to 'ram' (to wander).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01885173-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈræm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈræm.bəl / (BrE)
to talk or write at length in a confused or aimless way
talk or write in an aimless way

Examples

He tends to ramble when telling stories, often losing his main point.
The professor began to ramble after being asked an unrelated question.
Her letter rambled for pages without ever making her request clear.

Synset Examples

This novel rambles on and jogs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wander - move or talk without a clear direction or purpose
  • Synonym: meander - speak or write aimlessly or without structure
  • Antonym: focus - to pay particular attention or be concise

Etymology

From Middle English *ramblen* (to wander aimlessly), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
