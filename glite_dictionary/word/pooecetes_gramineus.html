Pooecetes gramineus

1. NOUN

/ poʊ.iˈsiːtiːz ɡræˈmɪniəs / (AmE), / pəʊ.iˈsiːtiːz ɡræˈmɪniəs / (BrE)
A common North American finch known as the vesper sparrow, noted for its melodious evening song.
North American vesper sparrow

Examples

The Pooecetes gramineus is often spotted in grasslands across North America during spring and summer.
Birdwatchers are drawn to the melodious song of the Pooecetes gramineus at dusk.
Unlike many sparrows, the Pooecetes gramineus prefers to nest on the ground among tall grasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vesper sparrow - Common name for Pooecetes gramineus

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Pooecetes' from Greek 'poikilos' (variegated) and 'gramineus' from Latin (grassy or of grass).
