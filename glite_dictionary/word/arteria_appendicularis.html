arteria appendicularis

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪəriə əˌpɛndɪˈkjʊlərɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪərɪə əˌpɛndɪˈkjʊlərɪs / (BrE)
the branch of the ileocolic artery that supplies blood to the vermiform appendix
artery supplying the appendix
medical

Examples

The arteria appendicularis is often considered during appendectomy procedures to prevent excessive bleeding.
Damage to the arteria appendicularis can result in ischemia of the appendix.
Surgeons carefully locate the arteria appendicularis before removing the vermiform appendix.

Etymology

From Latin, 'arteria' meaning artery and 'appendicularis' relating to the appendix.
