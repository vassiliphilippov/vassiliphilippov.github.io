lengthways

1. ADV

/ ˈlɛŋθˌweɪz / (AmE), / ˈlɛŋθ.weɪz / (BrE)
in the direction of the length of something; lengthwise.
in the direction of length

Examples

She sliced the cucumber lengthways to make thin strips.
Lay the fabric lengthways to ensure the pattern matches correctly.
For this recipe, cut the bread lengthways and then toast it.

Synset Examples

He cut the paper lengthwise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lengthwise - in the direction of the length of something

Etymology

Formed from 'length' + 'ways', by analogy with 'sideways'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00390113-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈlɛŋkθˌweɪz / (AmE), / ˈlɛŋθ.weɪz / (BrE)
In the direction of the length; along the longest side of something.
in the direction of the length

Examples

She sliced the cucumber lengthways to make thin strips.
Lay the planks lengthways along the wall for a neater effect.
The fabric should be folded lengthways before cutting.

Synset Examples

the lengthwise dimension

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lengthwise - in the direction of the length (adjective/adverb)

Etymology

Formed from 'length' + 'ways', modeled on the pattern of 'sideways' and similar adverbs.
