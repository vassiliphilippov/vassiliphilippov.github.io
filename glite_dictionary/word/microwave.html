microwave

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.kroʊˌweɪv / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.krəʊˌweɪv / (BrE)
An electric kitchen appliance that cooks or heats food quickly by using electromagnetic waves.
kitchen appliance using electromagnetic waves

Examples

She used the microwave to reheat her lunch at work.
Please clean the microwave after using it to avoid unpleasant smells.
The popcorn was ready in just two minutes in the microwave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: microwave oven - Kitchen appliance that cooks by microwave radiation

Etymology

From micro- (meaning small) and wave, referring to the use of microwaves to cook food.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkroʊˌweɪv / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌweɪv / (BrE)
an electromagnetic wave with a wavelength between infrared and radio waves, used in radar, microwave ovens, and for transmitting data
type of electromagnetic wave

Examples

The communication system relies on microwaves to transmit data over long distances.
Radar technology works by sending out microwaves that bounce off objects to determine their location.
Most satellite television signals are carried via microwaves between the dish and the broadcasting satellite.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: radio wave - an electromagnetic wave longer than microwaves, used in radio communication
  • Degree Lower: infrared - an electromagnetic radiation with shorter wavelength than microwaves, just beyond visible light

Etymology

From micro- (small) + wave, referring to the short wavelength compared to other radio waves.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11502712-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈmaɪ.kroʊˌweɪv / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.krəʊˌweɪv / (BrE)
to cook or heat (food) in a microwave oven
cook or heat in a microwave oven

Examples

I usually microwave my lunch at the office instead of eating out.
You can microwave popcorn in just two minutes for a quick snack.
She microwaved the soup because she didn't have time to cook on the stove.

Synset Examples

You can microwave the leftovers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reheat - to heat food again after it has been cooked and cooled
  • Antonym: to bake - to cook food by dry heat in an oven

Etymology

From the noun 'microwave' (a type of electromagnetic wave) and the appliance's name.
