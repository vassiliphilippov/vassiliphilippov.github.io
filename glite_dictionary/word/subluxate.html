to subluxate

1. VERB

/ sʌbˈlʌkseɪt / (AmE), / sʌbˈlʌkseɪt / (BrE)
to cause a joint or bone to be partially dislocated or moved out of its normal position
partially dislocate a joint or bone
medical

Examples

The patient managed to subluxate her shoulder during the workout.
Improper landing can subluxate the ankle, leading to pain and limited movement.
The doctor explained that to subluxate a vertebra means it is only partially out of alignment.

Synset Examples

subluxate the hip

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: to dislocate - to move a bone out of its normal position in a joint

Etymology

From Latin sub- (under, slightly) + luxatus (dislocated), past participle of luxare (to dislocate).
