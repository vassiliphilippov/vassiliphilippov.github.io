Paleo-American

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊ əˈmɛrɪkən / (AmE), / ˌpeɪlɪəʊ əˈmɛrɪkən / (BrE)
a member of the earliest human populations inhabiting North and South America during the late Pleistocene epoch
early human inhabitant of the Americas

Examples

Archaeologists discovered tools believed to be made by Paleo-Americans over 13,000 years ago.
Some scientists debate how the first Paleo-Americans migrated to the continent.
Artifacts from Paleo-American cultures provide vital clues about early life in the Americas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paleo-Indian - a member of one of the earliest peoples of North America

Etymology

From 'Paleo-', meaning ancient, and 'American', referring to the continents of North and South America.
