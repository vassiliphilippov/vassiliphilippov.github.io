feasibleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːzəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfiːzəbᵊlnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being feasible; the extent to which something is possible or doable
quality of being feasible

Examples

The feasibleness of the proposal was questioned by several committee members.
Engineers debated the feasibleness of building a bridge in that location.
She carefully considered the feasibleness of launching a new product in such a competitive market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feasibility - the state or degree of being easily or conveniently done
  • Antonym: impossibility - the state of being impossible to do

Etymology

From feasible, based on Old French 'faisible', from Latin 'facere' (to do, make), plus English '-ness'.
