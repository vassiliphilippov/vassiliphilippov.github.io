abuse

1. NOUN

/ əˈbjus / (AmE), / əˈbjuːs / (BrE)
rude or insulting language intended to offend or hurt someone
offensive or insulting language

Examples

After the referee made a controversial call, the crowd shouted abuse from the stands.
She endured years of verbal abuse from her classmates before transferring schools.
No one deserves to be the target of abuse, regardless of the circumstances.

Synset Examples

when a student made a stupid mistake he spared them no abuse
they yelled insults at the visiting team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - a disrespectful or scornful remark or act
  • Antonym: praise - express approval or admiration of someone or something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06728162-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ əˈbjus / (AmE), / əˈbjuːz / (BrE)
to use something in a wrong, improper, or excessive way, especially substances like drugs or alcohol
use something wrongly or excessively

Examples

Doctors warn that young people should never abuse prescription medicines.
He began to abuse alcohol after losing his job.
Some athletes abuse steroids to enhance performance, despite the risks.

Synset Examples

Her husband often abuses alcohol
while she was pregnant, she abused drugs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misuse - use something in the wrong way or for the wrong purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'abusus', past participle of 'abuti' meaning 'to misuse'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00203918-v
Synset POS: v

/ əˈbjuːz / (AmE), / əˈbjuːz / (BrE)
