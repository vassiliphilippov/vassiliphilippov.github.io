fete

1. VERB

/ feɪt / (AmE), / feɪt / (BrE)
to honor or entertain (someone) lavishly, especially by holding a celebration or party
to honor with a celebration or party

Examples

The company decided to fete its retiring CEO with a grand dinner and awards ceremony.
They feted the Nobel laureate upon his return home from Stockholm.
Every year, the city fetes local heroes with parades and speeches.

Synset Examples

They were feting the patriarch of the family
After the exam, the students were celebrating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to celebrate - to engage in festivities in honor of something
  • Synonym: honor - to show respect or pay tribute to someone

Etymology

From French 'fêter' (to celebrate), from 'fête' (festival, celebration).

2. NOUN

/ feɪt / (AmE), / feɪt / (BrE)
a public event or festival, often featuring organized entertainment, performances, and activities, typically to raise money for a cause
public event or festival with entertainment
british

Examples

The village fete draws hundreds of visitors every summer for music, games, and food stalls.
We volunteered at the school fete to help raise funds for new playground equipment.
A jazz band performed at the annual church fete, delighting the local community.

Synset Examples

a drama festival

Related Terms

  • Synonym: festival - a series of performances or events, usually organized around a theme or celebration

Etymology

From French fête (festival, party), from Latin festa (festival, holiday).

3. NOUN

/ feɪt / (AmE), / feɪt / (BrE)
An elaborate party, often held outdoors, typically for celebration or fundraising.
elaborate outdoor party or celebration

Examples

Every summer, the village holds a fete with games and food stalls on the green.
The charity fete attracted hundreds of visitors and raised a substantial amount for local causes.
We spent the afternoon enjoying the music and activities at the annual school fete.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: festival - a series of performances or festivities, often annual
  • Synonym: gala - a festive occasion, especially with entertainment
  • Synonym: banquet - a large formal meal for many people

Etymology

From French 'fête' (festival, party), from Latin 'festum' (feast).
