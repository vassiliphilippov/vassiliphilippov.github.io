Littorina

1. NOUN

/ lɪtəˈriːnə / (AmE), / lɪtəˈriːnə / (BrE)
a genus of small marine snails, commonly known as periwinkles, found mainly on rocky seashores
genus of small marine snails (periwinkles)

Examples

The rocky shore was covered in Littorina grazing on algae.
Researchers studied the shell patterns of different Littorina species.
Many seabirds feed on Littorina as a primary food source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periwinkle - common name for small marine snails of this genus

Etymology

From Latin 'littoralis' (of the seashore), referring to the habitat.

2. PROPN

/ ˌlɪtəˈraɪnə / (AmE), / ˌlɪtəˈrʌɪnə / (BrE)
A genus of small marine snails known as periwinkles, forming the type genus of the family Littorinidae.
genus of small marine snails (periwinkles)

Examples

Researchers collected several specimens of Littorina from the rocky shoreline.
The shell of Littorina species is often used to study marine ecology.
Littorina snails are commonly found grazing on algae in tidal pools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periwinkle - a small, edible marine snail

Etymology

From Latin 'littorina', diminutive of 'littoralis' (pertaining to the seashore).
