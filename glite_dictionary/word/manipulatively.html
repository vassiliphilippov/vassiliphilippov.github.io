manipulatively

1. ADV

/ məˈnɪpjəˌleɪtɪvli / (AmE), / məˈnɪpjʊlətɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner that seeks to influence or control others, often in a clever or devious way.
in a manipulative way

Examples

She spoke manipulatively to get what she wanted from the team.
The ad campaign was designed manipulatively to influence public opinion.
He manipulatively twisted her words during the debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deviously - in a deceitful or underhanded way
  • Antonym: honestly - in a sincere or truthful way

Etymology

From 'manipulative' + '-ly'. 'Manipulative' from French 'manipuler', from Latin 'manipulus' (handful).
