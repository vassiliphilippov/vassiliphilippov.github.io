Bangiaceae

1. NOUN

/ bænˈdʒiːəsi.iː / (AmE), / baŋˈɡiːəsɪ.iː / (BrE)
A family of red algae in the division Rhodophyta, including several important seaweeds.
family of red algae

Examples

The Bangiaceae family includes economically significant seaweeds used in food production.
Researchers identified several new species within the Bangiaceae during their marine survey.
Members of the Bangiaceae are commonly found in intertidal zones around the world.

Etymology

From New Latin Bangiaceae, based on the type genus Bangia.

2. NOUN

/ bæn.dʒiˈeɪ.siˌi / (AmE), / baŋ.giˈeɪ.sɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of red algae in the division Rhodophyta, comprising marine algae commonly found in temperate and tropical seas
family of red algae

Examples

The Bangiaceae family includes several important edible seaweeds such as nori.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Bangiaceae on the rocky shores of Japan.
Several members of the Bangiaceae can thrive in both warm and cold marine environments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Rhodophyta - division of red algae
  • Degree Lower: Bangia - a genus of red algae in Bangiaceae

Etymology

From the genus Bangia + Latin family suffix -aceae, used in botanical nomenclature.
