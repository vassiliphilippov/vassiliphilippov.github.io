Tuscarora

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʌskəˈrɔrə / (AmE), / ˌtʌskəˈrɔːrə / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally from North Carolina, later part of the Iroquois Confederacy in New York State
a member of a Native American people

Examples

The Tuscarora played a significant role in the history of the Iroquois Confederacy.
After migrating north, many Tuscarora settled in upstate New York.
James traces his ancestry back to the Tuscarora people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iroquois - a group of historically powerful Native American peoples of northeastern North America

Etymology

From the Tuscarora word *Ska:rò:rəʼ* meaning 'hemp gatherers', referring to the indigenous name for the people.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtʌskəˈrɔːrə / (AmE), / ˌtʌskəˈrɔːrə / (BrE)
the Iroquoian language spoken by the Tuscarora people, native to North America
Iroquoian language of the Tuscarora people

Examples

The Tuscarora language is considered endangered, with only a few native speakers remaining.
He is studying Tuscarora as part of his research into Iroquoian languages.
Efforts are being made to revitalize Tuscarora in the local community.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Iroquoian - a family of North American Indian languages

Etymology

From the name of the Tuscarora people, of Iroquoian origin.
