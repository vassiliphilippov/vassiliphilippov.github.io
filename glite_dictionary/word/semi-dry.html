semi-dry

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˈdraɪ / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈdraɪ / (BrE)
Moderately dry; not completely dry but not wet either.
somewhat dry; moderately dry

Examples

After the rain, the pavement was only semi-dry and still a bit slippery.
The winemaker described the rosé as semi-dry with a hint of sweetness.
Make sure the mop is semi-dry before you use it on the wooden floor.

Synset Examples

swabbing left the deck semi-dry but still slippery

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dry - free from moisture or liquid; not wet
  • Degree Lower: moist - slightly wet; damp

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'semi-' (from Latin, meaning 'half' or 'partially') and 'dry'.
