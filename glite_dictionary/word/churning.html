churning

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɜrnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a liquid) moving with strong agitation or turbulence; violently stirred up.
violently agitated (of liquid)

Examples

The boat rocked dangerously in the churning sea.
After the heavy rain, brown water rushed through the churning river.
Clouds of silt rose from the churning water near the waterfall.

Synset Examples

the river's roiling current
turbulent rapids

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbulent - (of liquid, air, etc.) moving unsteadily or violently
  • Antonym: calm - (of water) still, not moving or agitated

Etymology

From the verb 'churn', originally Old English 'cyrnan' (to churn, to stir).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɝːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
moving with or produced by vigorous or violent agitation, especially in a swirling or turbulent way
moving with violent agitation

Examples

The waves crashed against the rocks, sending churning foam into the air.
She looked down at the churning river below the old bridge.
The protesters marched through the churning crowd in the square.

Synset Examples

winds whipped the piled leaves into churning masses
a car stuck in the churned-up mud

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion; not controlled or calm
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions; tranquil

Etymology

Present participle of 'churn', from Old English *cyrin* (a churn).
