Ostariophysi

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.stə.riˈɑː.fə.saɪ / (AmE), / ˌɒ.stə.riˈɒ.fɪ.saɪ / (BrE)
A superorder of bony fishes that includes groups such as Cypriniformes (carps and minnows) and Siluriformes (catfish).
superorder of bony fishes

Examples

Many freshwater fish species belong to the Ostariophysi superorder, including well-known carps and catfish.
Scientists have studied the Ostariophysi for their unique Weberian apparatus, which enhances their hearing ability.
The diversity of the Ostariophysi makes them a significant group for ecological research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cypriniformes - order including carps and minnows
  • Synonym: Siluriformes - order including catfish

Etymology

From New Latin Ostariophysi, from Greek osteon (bone) + physa (bladder), relating to the Weberian apparatus in these fish.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.stɛəri.oʊˈfaɪ.saɪ / (AmE), / ˌɒ.stər.i.əˈfaɪ.saɪ / (BrE)
a large group (formerly an order, now often a superorder) of freshwater fish including the carps and catfishes, sometimes considered to comprise the orders Cypriniformes and Siluriformes
group of freshwater fish including carps and catfishes

Examples

The Ostariophysi is a major group of freshwater fishes encompassing species like carps and catfishes.
Researchers debated whether Ostariophysi should be classified as an order or a superorder within fish taxonomy.
Many aquarium hobbyists keep fish belonging to the Ostariophysi due to their diversity and adaptability.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'ostarion' (little bone) and 'physa' (bladder), referring to a related anatomical feature.
