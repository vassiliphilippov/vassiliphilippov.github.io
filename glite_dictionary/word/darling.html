darling

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑrlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɑːlɪŋ / (BrE)
A person who is dearly loved or cherished; used as a term of endearment.
a beloved person; term of endearment

Examples

She called her husband darling as she said goodbye.
Our little darling won first prize at the recital.
Come here, darling, and let me help you with that.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dear - a loved or cherished person; term of endearment

Etymology

From Middle English derling, from Old English dēorling, diminutive of dēore (dear).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑrlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɑːlɪŋ / (BrE)
a person who is very much loved or cherished, often used as a term of endearment
a loved or cherished person

Examples

She called her husband her darling in front of everyone.
Good night, darling, I'll see you in the morning.
Their youngest child is the darling of the family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dear - a person who is loved or regarded with affection

Etymology

From Middle English derling, from Old English dēorling (dear one), from dēore (dear) + -ling (diminutive).

3. ADJ

/ ˈdɑrlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɑːlɪŋ / (BrE)
dearly loved; cherished or regarded with great affection.
dearly loved; cherished

Examples

She looked at her darling son with pride and love.
This old house is my darling retreat from the busy world.
He presented the award to his darling wife, thanking her for years of support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beloved - dearly loved; much adored
  • Synonym: adored - deeply loved and admired
  • Antonym: hated - regarded with intense dislike

Etymology

From Middle English derlinge, from Old English dēorling (darling, favorite), from dēore (dear) + -ling (diminutive suffix).
