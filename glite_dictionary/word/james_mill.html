James Mill

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪmz mɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz mɪl / (BrE)
(1773–1836) A Scottish philosopher, historian, and economist known for promoting Bentham's utilitarianism and as the father of John Stuart Mill.
Scottish philosopher and father of John Stuart Mill

Examples

The ideas of James Mill heavily influenced nineteenth-century British political thought.
James Mill was a crucial figure in the development of utilitarian philosophy.
Historians often compare the works of James Mill with those of his son, John Stuart Mill.
