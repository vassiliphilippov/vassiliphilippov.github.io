dense

1. ADJ

/ dɛns / (AmE), / dɛns / (BrE)
(of a substance) permitting little or no light to pass through because of thickness or concentration; difficult to see through.
not letting light through; thick or concentrated

Examples

The dense fog made it impossible to see the road ahead.
Thick, dense smoke billowed out of the burning building.
The forest became so dense that barely any sunlight reached the ground.

Synset Examples

dense smoke
heavy fog
impenetrable gloom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent

Etymology

From Latin 'densus' (thick, dense).

/ dɛns / (AmE), / dɛns / (BrE)

Examples
