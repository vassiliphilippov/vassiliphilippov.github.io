superinfection

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːpərɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
an infection that develops while a patient is already being treated for another infection, often due to a different pathogen
infection occurring during treatment for another infection
medical

Examples

After several days on antibiotics, a superinfection emerged, complicating her recovery.
Doctors are wary of superinfection in patients who have prolonged hospital stays.
The patient developed a fungal superinfection while receiving antibacterial therapy.

Etymology

From Latin super- (above, over) + infection.
