deductive

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
relating to or involving the process of reasoning from general principles to reach specific conclusions.
relating to logical deduction

Examples

She used deductive logic to solve the complex problem.
In mathematics, many proofs are based on deductive reasoning.
A deductive approach starts with a general theory and tests it through observation.

Synset Examples

deductive reasoning

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inductive - relating to reasoning from specific instances to general principles

Etymology

From Latin 'deductivus', from 'deducere' (to lead down).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02721030-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
involving reasoning or conclusions that proceed from general principles to specific instances
reasoning from general to specific

Examples

The scientist used a deductive approach to test her hypothesis based on established laws.
Philosophers often rely on deductive reasoning to draw logical conclusions.
A deductive argument proceeds from general premises to reach a specific result.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inductive - involving reasoning from specific cases to general principles

Etymology

From Latin 'deductivus', from 'deducere' (to lead down, derive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01299595-a
Synset POS: a
