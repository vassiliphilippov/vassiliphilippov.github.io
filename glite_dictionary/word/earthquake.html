earthquake

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrθˌkweɪk / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌkweɪk / (BrE)
A sudden shaking of the Earth's surface caused by underground movement, often resulting in damage.
sudden shaking of the Earth's surface

Examples

The city was devastated by a powerful earthquake last year.
Small earthquakes are common in this region, but most go unnoticed.
Scientists use seismographs to detect the vibrations caused by an earthquake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temblor - another term for earthquake
  • Synonym: tremor - a small or minor earthquake

Etymology

From earth + quake, meaning to tremble. 'Quake' is from Old English 'cwacian' (to tremble).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɝːθˌkweɪk / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌkweɪk / (BrE)
a sudden and major disruption or upheaval, especially in a social, political, or organizational context
major disruption or upheaval

Examples

The CEO's resignation created an earthquake within the organization.
Her shocking revelation was an earthquake in the world of politics.
The policy change sent an earthquake through the tight-knit community.

Synset Examples

selling the company caused an earthquake among the employees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upheaval - a violent or sudden change or disruption
  • Antonym: calm - a period or state of tranquility; the absence of disturbance

Etymology

From Old English 'eorþquāce', literally 'earth' + 'quake'.
