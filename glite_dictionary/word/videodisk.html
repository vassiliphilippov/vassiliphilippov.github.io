videodisk

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪdioʊˌdɪsk / (AmE), / ˈvɪdiəʊˌdɪsk / (BrE)
A digital recording, especially of a movie, stored on an optical disk and playable on a computer or television.
optical disk for video playback

Examples

She bought the movie on videodisk so she could watch it anytime.
The library has an extensive collection of educational materials available on videodisk.
Some older computers require special drives to read a videodisk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DVD - A type of optical disk used for storing video and other data
  • Degree Higher: Blu-ray - A high-definition optical disk format for storing video
  • Antonym: videotape - Magnetic tape for recording and playing video

Etymology

Formed from 'video' (from Latin 'videre', to see) + 'disk'.
