gemsbok

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛmzbɑk / (AmE), / ˈɡɛmzbɒk / (BrE)
a large antelope native to southern Africa, characterized by a distinct black band along its flanks
large southern African antelope

Examples

The gemsbok is well-adapted to survive in arid, desert environments.
During the safari, we spotted a herd of gemsbok grazing on the open plains.
The striking black-and-white markings of the gemsbok make it easy to identify from a distance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oryx - large antelopes with long straight horns, native to Africa and Arabia

Etymology

From Afrikaans, from Dutch 'gems' (chamois) + 'bok' (buck, goat).
