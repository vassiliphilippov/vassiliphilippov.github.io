Sturnella

1. NOUN

/ stɜːrˈnɛlə / (AmE), / stɜːˈnɛlə / (BrE)
a genus of songbirds in the family Icteridae, which includes the meadowlarks of the Americas
a bird genus including meadowlarks

Examples

The genus Sturnella comprises several species widely known as meadowlarks.
Scientists recently reviewed the distribution of Sturnella across North and South America.
Birdwatchers observed a flock belonging to the Sturnella genus in the grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meadowlark - a bird species within the genus Sturnella

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, diminutive of Latin 'sturnus' meaning 'starling'.
