lawmaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈlɔːˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a person who makes or enacts laws, especially as a member of a legislative body
person who makes or enacts laws

Examples

The lawmaker introduced a new bill to reform education policy.
Several lawmakers attended the public hearing on climate change.
As a lawmaker, she is known for her commitment to social justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legislator - a person who makes laws, especially as a member of a legislative body

Etymology

From 'law' + 'maker'; modelled on the pattern of agent noun formation in English.
