promenade

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɑː.məˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˌprɒm.əˈnɑːd / (BrE)
a leisurely walk, especially one taken in a public place for pleasure or display
leisurely walk in a public place

Examples

They decided to take a promenade along the river after dinner.
The old-fashioned promenade was a highlight of the seaside town's charm.
Every evening, crowds enjoyed a leisurely promenade in the city park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroll - a slow, relaxed walk

Etymology

From French 'promenade' (a walk for pleasure), from 'promener' (to walk).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑməˌneɪd / (AmE), / ˌprɒməˈnɑːd / (BrE)
a formal dance or ball held for a school class at the end of the academic year, especially in high schools in the United States
formal school dance or ball
american

Examples

The seniors spent weeks preparing for the annual promenade at their high school.
Emma wore a beautiful gown to the promenade this year.
Tickets for the promenade went on sale at the beginning of May.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prom - informal abbreviation for 'promenade', meaning a formal dance or ball for students

Etymology

From French 'promenade' (a walk), later applied in the U.S. to formal school dances in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
