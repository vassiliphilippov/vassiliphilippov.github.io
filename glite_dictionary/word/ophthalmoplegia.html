ophthalmoplegia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑf.θæl.məˈpli.dʒə / (AmE), / ˌɒf.θæl.məˈpliː.dʒi.ə / (BrE)
paralysis of the muscles that control movement of the eye, usually due to nerve damage or disease.
paralysis of the eye muscles
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with ophthalmoplegia after experiencing double vision and difficulty moving her eyes.
Chronic diabetes can sometimes lead to ophthalmoplegia due to nerve damage.
Several neurological conditions may result in ophthalmoplegia, affecting the ability to look in different directions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paralysis - loss of ability to move a body part
  • Degree Lower: paresis - partial loss of voluntary movement or weakness

Etymology

From Greek *ophthalmos* (eye) + *plēgia* (paralysis).
