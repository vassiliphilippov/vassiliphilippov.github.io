heartfelt

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrtˌfɛlt / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtfɛlt / (BrE)
(of a feeling or expression) deeply and sincerely felt; genuine and earnest.
sincere; deeply felt

Examples

We offered our heartfelt thanks to everyone who supported us during that difficult time.
Her heartfelt condolences brought comfort to the grieving family.
The teacher's heartfelt words inspired the entire class.

Synset Examples

one's dearest wish
devout wishes for their success
heartfelt condolences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sincere - genuine, showing true feelings
  • Antonym: insincere - not genuine or heartfelt; pretending

Etymology

From heart + felt, implying a feeling experienced deeply within the heart.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrtˌfɛlt / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtfɛlt / (BrE)
(of a feeling or expression) deeply and sincerely felt; genuine.
sincerely and deeply felt

Examples

She offered her heartfelt thanks to everyone who supported her during a difficult time.
Please accept my heartfelt sympathy for your loss.
His apology was so heartfelt that everyone could see he truly meant it.

Synset Examples

His congradulations were heartfelt and sincere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sincere - genuine; free from pretense or deceit
  • Antonym: insincere - not expressing genuine feelings; pretending to feel something

Etymology

Formed from 'heart' + 'felt', referring to feelings perceived as coming from the heart.
