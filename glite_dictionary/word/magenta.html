magenta

1. ADJ

/ məˈdʒɛntə / (AmE), / məˈdʒɛntə / (BrE)
of a deep purplish-red color.
deep purplish-red color

Examples

She wore a magenta dress that stood out at the party.
The artist added a bold splash of magenta to the canvas for contrast.
The sunset filled the sky with vibrant shades of magenta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crimson - deep red color, close to magenta
  • Degree Higher: purple - color with more blue than magenta, near magenta on the spectrum
  • Degree Lower: pink - pale red color, lighter than magenta
  • Antonym: green - color opposite to magenta on the color wheel

Etymology

From Magenta, the name of a town in northern Italy, where the dye was first discovered.

2. NOUN

/ məˈdʒɛntə / (AmE), / məˈdʒɛntə / (BrE)
a deep purplish-red color, one of the primary colors in printing and photography
a purplish-red color

Examples

The artist chose magenta for her bold new painting to create a striking effect.
She wore a magenta dress that stood out in the crowd.
Printers often use cyan, yellow, and magenta as primary colors in color mixing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crimson - a deep red color with a hint of blue or purple
  • Degree Lower: scarlet - a bright red with a slightly orange tinge
  • Degree Higher: purple - a color intermediate between red and blue

Etymology

Named after the town of Magenta in Italy, commemorating the Battle of Magenta in 1859, around the time the dye was discovered.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04971763-n
Synset POS: n
