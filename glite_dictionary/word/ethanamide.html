ethanamide

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːθəˈneɪmaɪd / (AmE), / ˌiːθəˈneɪmaɪd / (BrE)
A colorless solid amide derived from acetic acid, used as a solvent and in the synthesis of organic compounds.
amide of acetic acid; organic solvent
medical

Examples

The laboratory used ethanamide as a solvent during several chemical reactions.
Exposure to ethanamide should be minimized in industrial settings due to potential health risks.
You might encounter ethanamide in organic synthesis procedures in advanced chemistry classes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetamide - another name for ethanamide

Etymology

From 'ethan-' (from ethane) + 'amide', indicating its origin from acetic acid.
