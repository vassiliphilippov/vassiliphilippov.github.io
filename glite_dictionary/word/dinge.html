dinge

1. NOUN

/ dɪndʒ / (AmE), / dɪndʒ / (BrE)
A dirty or discolored appearance, especially from accumulated dirt or age.
dirty or discolored appearance

Examples

Years of smoke had left a permanent dinge on the ceiling.
The white curtains lost their brightness and took on a yellowish dinge.
No amount of scrubbing could remove the dinge from the old floors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stain - a dirty mark that is difficult to remove
  • Antonym: spotless - completely clean, without any dirt
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ dɪndʒ / (AmE), / dɪndʒ / (BrE)
to make something dirty or dull in appearance; to make dingy
make dirty or dull

Examples

Prolonged neglect will dinge the fabric, making it look old and worn.
Smoke from the fire began to dinge the once-white ceiling tiles.
If you wash clothes with muddy items, it can dinge your whites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirty - to make unclean
  • Antonym: to brighten - to make brighter or cleaner

Etymology

From 'dingy', likely via back-formation.
