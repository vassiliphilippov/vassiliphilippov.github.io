Salix pendulina

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælɪks pɛnˈdjuːlɪnə / (AmE), / ˈsælɪks pɛnˈdjuːlɪnə / (BrE)
A hybrid species of willow that typically does not have strongly weeping branches.
a hybrid species of willow

Examples

The garden featured several Salix pendulina trees along the pond's edge.
Botanists often study Salix pendulina for its unique hybrid characteristics.
Unlike some willows, Salix pendulina does not have pronounced weeping branches.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Salix' (willow) and 'pendulina' (little hanging), referring to its growth habit.
