awareness

1. NOUN

/ əˈwɛrnəs / (AmE), / əˈweənəs / (BrE)
knowledge or perception of a situation or fact; the state of being conscious of something.
knowledge or consciousness of something

Examples

Her awareness of local issues made her an informed voter.
The training is designed to increase awareness of workplace safety.
He showed little awareness of the impact of his actions.

Synset Examples

he had no awareness of his mistakes
his sudden consciousness of the problem he faced
their intelligence and general knowingness was impressive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consciousness - the state of being awake and aware of one's surroundings
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From aware + -ness, dating from the mid-19th century.

2. NOUN

/ əˈwɛr.nəs / (AmE), / əˈweə.nəs / (BrE)
a state of conscious perception, especially at a basic or undifferentiated level; the condition of being aware
basic state of conscious perception

Examples

The sound of the alarm suddenly entered his awareness as he woke up.
Meditation can heighten your awareness of the present moment.
After the accident, her awareness of her surroundings slowly returned.

Synset Examples

the crash intruded on his awareness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consciousness - the state of being aware of and able to think about one's own existence and environment

Etymology

From 'aware' + '-ness', formed in English.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
