poker

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊkə / (BrE)
a metal rod with a handle, used for stirring or adjusting a fire
rod for stirring a fire

Examples

She used the poker to rearrange the burning logs in the fireplace.
The old brass poker was hanging next to the hearth.
Be careful not to touch the hot end of the poker when tending the fire.

Etymology

From poke (to prod or jab) + -er.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊkə / (BrE)
A card game in which players bet on the value of their hands, aiming to win the pot by holding the highest-ranking hand or by convincing others to fold.
card game involving betting and hand ranking

Examples

He invited his friends over for a night of poker and snacks.
Many professional poker players travel the world to compete in tournaments.
Learning to read your opponent is a crucial skill in poker.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blackjack - a card game where players try to get 21 points
  • Antonym: bridge - a trick-taking card game for four players
  • Synonym: gamble - to play games of chance for money

Etymology

Perhaps from French *poque*, a card game similar to poker, or from German *pochen* (to brag or to knock), referring to the bluffing aspect of the game.
