spathe

1. NOUN

/ speɪθ / (AmE), / speɪθ / (BrE)
a large, often showy bract that surrounds or subtends an inflorescence, especially a spadix, in certain plants.
large bract enclosing a flower cluster

Examples

The white spathe of the peace lily stood out among the green leaves.
Calla lilies are known for their impressive spathe that surrounds the flower.
The plant’s spathe unfurled, revealing a cluster of tiny blossoms inside.

Etymology

From Greek 'spathē' (broad blade), via Latin 'spatha'.
