salter

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔltər / (AmE), / ˈsɔːltə / (BrE)
a person who makes, sells, or deals in salt
person who makes or sells salt

Examples

The medieval salter was an important figure in the town's economy.
Her ancestors were salters who supplied salt to local markets.
A salter could achieve wealth and status through his trade.

Etymology

From Middle English saltere, from Old French saltier, from Latin sal (salt).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔltər / (AmE), / ˈsɔːltə / (BrE)
a person who salts meat, fish, or other foods to preserve them
person who salts foods to preserve them

Examples

The salter prepared barrels of fish for shipping to distant markets.
In the past, a skilled salter was essential for providing food during the winter months.
She learned the trade of a salter from her father, working long hours curing meat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preserver - person who preserves foods by various methods

Etymology

From Middle English salter, from Old English s(e)alt (salt) + -er (agent suffix).
