invertase

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɝːteɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːteɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the breakdown of sucrose into glucose and fructose
enzyme that splits sucrose into glucose and fructose
medical

Examples

Bakeries often use invertase to improve the sweetness and texture of confectioneries.
The action of invertase converts sucrose into glucose and fructose during digestion.
Researchers extracted invertase from yeast cells for use in various food applications.

Etymology

From 'invert' (inverting sugar, i.e., sucrose) + '-ase' (enzyme suffix).
