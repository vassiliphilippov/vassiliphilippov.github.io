alidad

1. NOUN

/ ˈælɪˌdæd / (AmE), / ˈælɪˌdæd / (BrE)
A surveying instrument part consisting of the upper movable part of a theodolite, including the telescope and its attachments, used to measure angles.
movable part of a theodolite (surveying)

Examples

The alidad must be carefully aligned to measure the correct horizontal angle.
After replacing the alidad, the surveyor recalibrated the theodolite for accurate readings.
Early astronomers also relied on the alidad to track celestial movements.

Etymology

From Arabic al-‘iḍāda (the revolving radius of an instrument).

2. NOUN

/ ˈælɪˌdæd / (AmE), / ˈælɪˌdæd / (BrE)
A surveying instrument used with a plane table, consisting of a straightedge with sights, for drawing lines of sight to distant objects and measuring angles.
surveying instrument for drawing lines of sight

Examples

The surveyor carefully adjusted the alidad to align with the distant church steeple.
Using the alidad, the engineers traced accurate lines on the map.
The alidad is an essential tool for traditional plane table surveying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alidade - alternative spelling of alidad, a surveying instrument

Etymology

From Arabic al-‘idāda (the revolving rule), via Medieval Latin alhidada.
