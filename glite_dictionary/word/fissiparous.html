fissiparous

1. ADJ

/ fɪˈsɪpəˌrəs / (AmE), / fɪˈsɪpərəs / (BrE)
Reproducing or dividing by fission; capable of reproduction by splitting into two or more parts.
reproducing by fission
medical

Examples

Many single-celled organisms are fissiparous, multiplying simply by splitting in two.
Some bacteria exhibit fissiparous reproduction under favorable environmental conditions.
The fissiparous nature of certain protozoans allows for rapid population growth.

Etymology

From Latin 'fissiparus', from 'fissus' (cleft, split) + 'parere' (to produce).

2. ADJ

/ fɪˈsɪpərəs / (AmE), / fɪˈsɪpərəs / (BrE)
Tending to break up or promote division into separate parts, groups, or entities, especially in relation to political or social organization.
tending to promote division or separation

Examples

The party's fissiparous tendencies became clear during the heated debate over policy.
Some feared that the fissiparous nature of the coalition would lead to its collapse.
Critics accused the leader of fostering a fissiparous atmosphere within the organization.

Synset Examples

a breakaway faction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divisive - tending to cause disagreement or hostility between people
  • Antonym: unifying - causing people or things to be joined or brought together

Etymology

From Latin 'fissiparus', from 'fissus' (split) + 'parere' (to bring forth).
