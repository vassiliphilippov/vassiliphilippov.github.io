undeviating

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.dɪˈvieɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈviː.eɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
not turning aside or deviating from a course; continuing in a straight or constant direction.
not deviating; straight or constant

Examples

She followed an undeviating route through the city, never pausing or changing her path.
The project succeeded thanks to his undeviating commitment to the original plan.
Trains on this line move with undeviating precision from station to station.

Synset Examples

some people see evolution as an undeviating upward march from simple organisms to the very complex
a straight and narrow tree-lined road unswerving across the lowlands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unswerving - not turning or changing direction; steady and constant
  • Antonym: deviating - departing from an established course or norm

Etymology

From un- (not) + deviating (from Latin 'deviatus', past participle of 'deviare', to turn aside).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈdiːvieɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈdiːvieɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Not changing or varying; steady and consistent, especially in values or principles.
steady and constant; unchanging

Examples

His undeviating commitment to honesty won him great respect.
She followed the plan with undeviating precision, never straying from the guidelines.
The soldier swore undeviating loyalty to his country.

Synset Examples

undeviating loyalty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constant - remaining the same over a period of time, unchanging
  • Synonym: unwavering - steady or resolute, not changing or faltering
  • Synonym: steady - firmly fixed, supported, or balanced; not shaking or moving
  • Antonym: fickle - changing frequently, especially in loyalty or affections

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'deviate' (to turn aside from), meaning not turning aside.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00728164-s
Synset POS: s
