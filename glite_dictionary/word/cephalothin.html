cephalothin

1. NOUN

/ sɛfəˈloʊθɪn / (AmE), / sɛfəˈlɒθɪn / (BrE)
a semisynthetic antibiotic of the cephalosporin group, used to treat bacterial infections
a cephalosporin antibiotic
medical

Examples

Cephalothin is administered intravenously to fight severe bacterial infections.
The use of cephalothin has declined due to newer cephalosporins with broader spectrums.
Doctors may prescribe cephalothin when treating infections caused by penicillin-resistant bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalosporin - a group of antibiotics derived from Acremonium fungi

Etymology

Formed from 'cephalo-' (referring to cephalosporin antibiotics) and '-thin', found in some antibiotic names.
