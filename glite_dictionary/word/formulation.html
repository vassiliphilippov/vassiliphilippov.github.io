formulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɔrmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɔːmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a substance that is prepared according to a specific formula, often for medical, chemical, or technical use
substance prepared by a formula

Examples

The pharmacist recommended a new formulation for treating allergies.
This sunscreen uses a water-resistant formulation ideal for outdoor activities.
Researchers tested the formulation to ensure it was safe and effective.

Synset Examples

the physician prescribed a commercial preparation of the medicine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preparation - a substance made by combining ingredients

Etymology

From Latin 'formula' (a small form, rule) + English suffix '-ation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɔːrmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɔːmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of creating or developing an idea, plan, or explanation and expressing it in a clear or systematic way
the act of devising or expressing an idea

Examples

The formulation of the theory took years of careful research and experimentation.
Her formulation of the solution demonstrated a deep understanding of the problem.
Successful project management relies on the clear formulation of goals and strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invention - the act of creating something new or original
  • Synonym: conceptualization - the act of forming an idea or concept of something

Etymology

From Latin 'formulatio', from 'formulare' (to form, shape).

3. NOUN

/ ˌfɔr.mjəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɔː.mjʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the way in which something is expressed or put into words; the style of expression
manner of expressing something

Examples

Her careful formulation of the proposal impressed the entire team.
The success of the ad campaign depended on the clever formulation of its main message.
He worked hard to find just the right formulation for his apology.

Synset Examples

he suggested a better formulation
his manner of expression showed how much he cared

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expression - the act of putting thoughts or feelings into words
  • Synonym: phraseology - a particular way of expressing something in words

Etymology

From Latin 'formulatio', from 'formulare' (to form, shape).
