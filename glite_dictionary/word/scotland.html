Scotland

1. PROPN

/ ˈskɑt.lənd / (AmE), / ˈskɒt.lənd / (BrE)
a country in the northern part of the island of Great Britain, which is part of the United Kingdom
country in northern Great Britain, UK

Examples

She spent her summer vacation traveling across Scotland and visiting the Highlands.
Many traditional festivals in Scotland are celebrated with music, dance, and kilts.
The national animal of Scotland is the unicorn, symbolizing purity and strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: England - country in southern Great Britain, part of the United Kingdom
  • Synonym: Wales - country in western Great Britain, part of the United Kingdom
  • Synonym: Northern Ireland - country in the north-east of the island of Ireland, part of the United Kingdom

Etymology

From Old English 'Scotland', meaning 'land of the Scots'; ultimately from Latin 'Scoti', a people of Gaelic origin.
