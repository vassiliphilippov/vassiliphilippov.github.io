made

1. ADJ

/ meɪd / (AmE), / meɪd / (BrE)
(of a bed) having the sheets, blankets, and covers arranged neatly in place.
describing a bed with covers arranged

Examples

She was pleased to find a made bed waiting for her at the hotel.
He always leaves his bed perfectly made before going to work.
A made bed instantly makes the whole room look tidier.

Synset Examples

a neatly made bed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unmade - of a bed, not having its covers arranged
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01472305-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ meɪd / (AmE), / meɪd / (BrE)
having achieved success, security, or a permanent position, often through effort or recognition
successful; permanently established

Examples

After closing the biggest deal of the year, he felt like a made man.
Once she received tenure at the university, her future was made.
With this promotion, you're a made person in the company.

Synset Examples

now I am a made man forever

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secure - safe and not likely to change or fail
  • Synonym: established - having gained permanent acceptance or recognition
  • Antonym: insecure - not confident, assured, or safe
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02340278-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ meɪd / (AmE), / meɪd / (BrE)
produced by a manufacturing process; manufactured rather than natural or handmade.
produced by a manufacturing process

Examples

Most of the furniture in this store is made rather than handmade.
She prefers natural fibers over made materials in clothing.
We checked the labels to see if the toys were made or handcrafted.

Synset Examples

bought some made goods at the local store: rope and nails

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manufactured - produced by machines or industrial process
  • Antonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature, not made by humans
  • Antonym: handmade - made by hand, not by machine

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'make', from Old English 'macian'.

WordNet ID: oewn-00676050-s
