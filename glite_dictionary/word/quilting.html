quilting

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪl.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪl.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of stitching together layers of fabric and a filling, often to create a decorative pattern or design
stitching layers of fabric to form a design

Examples

She spent hours working on the quilting for her new bedspread.
The museum featured exhibits on traditional quilting techniques from around the world.
He admired the intricate patterns created by the quilting on the blanket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quilt - a bed covering made of layers of fabric stitched together

Etymology

From 'quilt', from Middle English quilte, from Anglo-French cuilte, from Latin culcita (mattress, cushion).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪltɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪltɪŋ / (BrE)
Material used for making a quilt, or fabric that has been stitched with two layers enclosing a soft filling.
quilt-making material or stitched fabric

Examples

She chose a floral quilting for her new bedspread.
The jacket's quilting gives it extra warmth and a distinctive texture.
Many artisans prefer cotton quilting for its durability and comfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quilt - a warm bed covering made of padding enclosed between layers of fabric and kept in place by lines of stitching

Etymology

From 'quilt', derived from Latin *culcita* (a mattress or cushion).
