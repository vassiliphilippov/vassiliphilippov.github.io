extravert

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.strəˌvɝt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.strəˌvɜːt / (BrE)
(psychology) a person whose attention is focused more on external activities and social interaction rather than on internal thoughts and feelings.
outgoing, socially oriented person (psychology)
medical

Examples

Although she was naturally an extravert, she still enjoyed silent moments alone with a good book.
The team needed an extravert to lead the group discussions and motivate others.
People often assume that being an extravert means you are always outgoing and talkative.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: introvert - a person whose attention is focused on internal thoughts and feelings
  • Synonym: extrovert - alternative spelling: a person concerned with external things and social interaction

Etymology

From Latin 'extra' (outside) + 'vertere' (to turn); related to the concept introduced by Carl Jung in psychology.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛk.strə.vɜrt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.strə.vɜːt / (BrE)
(of a person) outgoing and socially confident; concerned more with the external world than with inner thoughts and feelings.
outgoing, socially confident person

Examples

She is such an extravert that she loves meeting new people at every opportunity.
His extravert tendencies make him the center of attention at parties.
Because she is an extravert, she feels energized by being around large groups.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: introvert - a person who is more focused on internal thoughts and feelings
  • Synonym: extrovert - alternative, now standard, spelling of 'extravert'

Etymology

From Latin 'extra' (outside) + 'vertere' (to turn); popularized by psychologist Carl Jung.
