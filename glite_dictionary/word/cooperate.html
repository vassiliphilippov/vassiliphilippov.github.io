to cooperate

1. VERB

/ koʊˈɑpəˌreɪt / (AmE), / kəʊˈɒpəreɪt / (BrE)
to work together with others in order to achieve a common goal or purpose
work together towards a shared goal

Examples

If all departments cooperate, the project should be completed ahead of schedule.
Nations must cooperate to address global environmental challenges.
The twins cooperated to build their model airplane together.

Synset Examples

The soprano and the pianist did not get together very well
We joined forces with another research group

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to collaborate - to work jointly on an activity, especially to produce or create something
  • Antonym: to compete - to strive against others for an objective

Etymology

From Latin cooperari, from co- (together) + operari (to work).
