pentoxide

1. NOUN

/ pɛnˈtɑksaɪd / (AmE), / pɛnˈtɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound in which five atoms of oxygen are combined with another element in the molecule
oxide with five oxygen atoms

Examples

Vanadium pentoxide is used as a catalyst in some industrial processes.
Phosphorus pentoxide is well known for its powerful drying properties.
Laboratory accidents can occur if pentoxide compounds are mishandled.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trioxide - oxide containing three oxygen atoms
  • Degree Lower: dioxide - oxide containing two oxygen atoms

Etymology

From Greek 'penta-' (five) and 'oxide' (a compound of oxygen).
