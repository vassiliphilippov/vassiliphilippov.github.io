uneasy

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈizi / (AmE), / ʌnˈiːzi / (BrE)
feeling anxious or uncomfortable; not relaxed or confident, often due to worry or uncertainty
anxious or uncomfortable

Examples

He gave an uneasy smile when asked about the missing files.
The quiet in the office made everyone feel uneasy.
She felt uneasy about walking home alone after dark.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxious - feeling worried or uneasy about something
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From un- (not) + easy; first recorded in the 16th century.
