Euglenophyceae

1. NOUN

/ juːˌgliːnəˈfaɪsiː / (AmE), / juːˌgliːnəˈfiːsiː / (BrE)
a class of mostly freshwater, flagellate protists belonging to the division Euglenophyta, commonly known as euglenoids
a class of flagellate protists (euglenoids)
medical

Examples

Scientists study the Euglenophyceae to better understand the evolution of photosynthetic organisms.
Members of the Euglenophyceae are commonly found in freshwater environments worldwide.
The green pigment in Euglenophyceae allows them to produce their own food through photosynthesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: euglenoid - a protist of the class Euglenophyceae

Etymology

From New Latin, from Euglena (a genus name) + -phyceae (a suffix for algae classes, from Greek 'phykos' meaning 'seaweed')

2. NOUN

/ juˌɡlinəˈfaɪsii / (AmE), / juˌɡliːnəˈfɪsɪiː / (BrE)
a class of mostly unicellular, flagellated algae, coextensive with the division Euglenophyta, found commonly in freshwater habitats
class of unicellular, flagellated algae
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, scientists noted that several members of Euglenophyceae were present in the sample.
The class Euglenophyceae includes both photosynthetic and non-photosynthetic species.
Research on Euglenophyceae has advanced our understanding of flagellated algae.

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Euglena (a genus) + Greek phyke (seaweed).
