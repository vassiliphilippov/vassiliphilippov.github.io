tenderfoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛndɚˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈtɛndəˌfʊt / (BrE)
a person who is new to or inexperienced at a particular activity, especially outdoor or frontier life
inexperienced or novice person

Examples

As a tenderfoot, she struggled to set up her tent for the first time.
It's easy to spot a tenderfoot on their first hiking trip.
The more experienced campers helped the tenderfoot build his first campfire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
  • Synonym: greenhorn - a person who is new to or inexperienced at something
  • Antonym: veteran - a person with long experience in a particular activity or field

Etymology

American English, originally 19th century Western US; literally 'tender' (delicate) + 'foot' (referring to sensitive feet of newcomers on rough terrain).
