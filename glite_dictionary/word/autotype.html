autotype

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːtoʊˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəʊˌtaɪp / (BrE)
a process for producing permanent prints using a carbon pigment; also, a print produced by this process
permanent print process using carbon pigment

Examples

The museum displayed several autotype prints from the 19th century.
He specialized in making autotype reproductions of artwork for preservation.
An autotype offers greater resistance to fading than many other photographic processes.

Etymology

From Greek auto- (self) + type (impression, model).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔːt̬oʊˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəʊtaɪp / (BrE)
an exact copy or reproduction of something, especially in printing or photography
exact copy or reproduction

Examples

The laboratory produced an autotype of the historic document for archival purposes.
She admired the autotype of the 19th-century photograph displayed in the museum.
An autotype ensures the original work can be studied without risking damage to it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facsimile - an exact copy, especially of written or printed material
  • Synonym: reproduction - a copy of a work of art, especially one made by the original artist or under their supervision
  • Antonym: original - the first or primary version from which copies are made

Etymology

From Greek 'auto-' (self) + 'type' (impression, form).
