oestrogen

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛstrədʒən / (AmE), / ˈiːstrədʒən / (BrE)
A group of steroid hormones which promote the development and maintenance of female characteristics in the body, primarily secreted by the ovaries.
female steroid sex hormone
medical british

Examples

The doctor explained that low levels of oestrogen could cause some of her symptoms.
During menopause, oestrogen production in the ovaries declines significantly.
Many contraceptive pills contain synthetic oestrogen to regulate hormones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estrogen - American English spelling of oestrogen

Etymology

From German Östrogen, from Latin oestrus (frenzy; a reference to the estrus cycle).
