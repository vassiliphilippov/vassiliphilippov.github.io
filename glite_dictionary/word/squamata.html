Squamata

1. NOUN

/ skwəˈmɑːtə / (AmE), / skwəˈmɑːtə / (BrE)
a large order of reptiles that includes snakes, lizards, and related species, characterized by their scaled skin and movable quadrate bones
order of reptiles: snakes and lizards

Examples

The Squamata are the largest order of modern reptiles, including both lizards and snakes.
Scientists study Squamata fossils to understand reptilian evolution.
Almost all living snakes and lizards belong to the Squamata order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard - a reptile with a long body and tail, four legs, and movable eyelids
  • Synonym: snake - a legless, elongated reptile

Etymology

From Latin 'squamatus' meaning 'scaly', from 'squama' (scale).
