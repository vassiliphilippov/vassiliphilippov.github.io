wage

1. NOUN

/ weɪdʒ / (AmE), / weɪdʒ / (BrE)
a regular payment, typically paid on a weekly or daily basis, made by an employer to an employee, especially for work that is hourly or piecework, as opposed to salary
payment for work, usually hourly or daily

Examples

He struggled to support his family on a low wage.
Most employees receive their wage at the end of each week.
The company decided to increase the minimum wage for all workers.

Synset Examples

wages were paid by cash
he wasted his pay on drink
they saved a quarter of all their earnings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salary - a fixed regular payment, typically paid monthly or biweekly but often expressed annually
  • Degree Lower: fee - a payment made to a professional person for services
  • Synonym: earnings - money obtained in return for labor or services

Etymology

From Old French ‘gage’ (a pledge, a payment), from Frankish origin.

2. VERB

/ weɪdʒ / (AmE), / weɪdʒ / (BrE)
to carry on or engage in (a war, battle, or campaign)
carry on a war, battle, or campaign

Examples

The country decided to wage war against its neighboring state.
Activists vowed to wage a campaign for social justice.
For years, the community has waged a battle against drug abuse.

Synset Examples

Napoleon and Hitler waged war against all of Europe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight - to take part in a violent struggle or battle
  • Antonym: to cease - to stop, especially referring to fighting or war

Etymology

From Old French 'wagier' (to pledge, to guarantee), from Frankish 'wadja' (pledge, security).

Synset POS: v
