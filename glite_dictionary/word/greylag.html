greylag

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪˌlæɡ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪlæɡ / (BrE)
a common large grey wild goose of Europe, regarded as the ancestor of many domestic goose breeds
a species of wild grey goose in Europe

Examples

The greylag is often seen grazing in the marshlands during spring.
Farmers have domesticated the greylag for centuries due to its adaptability.
We spotted a flock of greylags flying low over the lake.

Etymology

From 'grey' + 'lag', possibly because it lags behind other geese when migrating.
