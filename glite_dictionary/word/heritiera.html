Heritiera

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛrɪˈtɪriə / (AmE), / ˌhɛrɪˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
A small genus of large tropical timber trees native to eastern Asia, Australasia, and tropical Africa, known for their prominent buttress roots.
genus of tropical timber trees

Examples

Some species of Heritiera are important sources of timber in Southeast Asia.
The roots of Heritiera trees form impressive buttresses along riverbanks.
Conservationists are studying the distribution of Heritiera in tropical forests.

Etymology

From the name of the French botanist Charles Louis L'Héritier de Brutelle, after whom the genus was named.

2. NOUN

/ hɛˈrɪtiˌɛrə / (AmE), / hɛˈrɪtiːərə / (BrE)
A small genus of timber trees, native to eastern Asia, Australasia, and tropical Africa, known for producing large buttresses at the base of the trunk.
a genus of tropical timber trees

Examples

The mangrove forests in Southeast Asia include several species of Heritiera.
Timber from Heritiera trees is valued for its strength and durability.
Botanists surveyed a rare Heritiera population in tropical Africa.
