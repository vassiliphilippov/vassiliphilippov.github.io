plausible

1. ADJ

/ ˈplɔːzəbəl / (AmE), / ˈplɔːzəbl / (BrE)
Seeming reasonable or probable; appearing worthy of belief, especially when open to doubt or suspicion.
seeming reasonable or believable

Examples

She gave a plausible explanation for why she was late to the meeting.
Although his story sounded plausible, the detective remained suspicious.
The alibi was certainly plausible, but further investigation was needed.

Synset Examples

a plausible excuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: credible - able to be believed; convincing
  • Antonym: improbable - not likely to be true or to happen

Etymology

From Latin plausibilis (worthy of applause), from plaudere (to applaud).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01803966-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈplɔːzəbəl / (AmE), / ˈplɔːzəbəl / (BrE)
(of a person or argument) given to presenting seemingly reasonable or valid arguments that are actually misleading or false.
seemingly reasonable but misleading

Examples

She was a plausible liar who could charm anyone into believing her stories.
His plausible explanation cleverly disguised the true facts of the case.
The con artist's plausible manner made people trust him too easily.

Synset Examples

a plausible liar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specious - superficially plausible, but actually wrong or misleading

Etymology

From Latin plausibilis (worthy of applause), from plaudere (to applaud).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02190367-s
Synset POS: s
