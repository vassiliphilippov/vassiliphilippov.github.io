nonadaptive

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.əˈdæptɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.əˈdæptɪv / (BrE)
(of a trait or condition) not contributing to adjustment or adaptation to one's environment or circumstances.
not aiding adjustment or adaptation

Examples

The nonadaptive characteristics of the species made survival in the new environment difficult.
Some psychological disorders can result from nonadaptive behaviors that prevent effective coping.
Genetic mutations are often nonadaptive if they do not improve an organism's fitness.

Synset Examples

dysfunctional behavior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adaptive - having the ability to adjust or adapt to different conditions or environments

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'non-' (not) + 'adaptive', from Latin 'adaptare' (to fit to).
