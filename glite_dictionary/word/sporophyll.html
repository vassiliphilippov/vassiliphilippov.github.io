sporophyll

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɔːrəˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈspɒrəfɪl / (BrE)
A leaf or leaf-like organ that bears sporangia, found especially in ferns, mosses, and some seed plants.
leaf that bears sporangia in plants

Examples

In ferns, the sporophyll is often distinct from the sterile leaves.
Some club mosses produce cones composed of closely packed sporophylls.
The arrangement of sporophylls can help botanists identify different plant species.

Etymology

From New Latin *sporophyllum*, from Greek *spora* (seed) + *phyllon* (leaf).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɔrəˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈspɒrəˌfɪl / (BrE)
a leaf, often found in ferns and mosses, that bears sporangia (spore-producing structures)
leaf bearing sporangia in ferns or mosses

Examples

The fern produces sporophylls that carry clusters of sporangia on their undersides.
In some mosses, the sporophyll is modified to maximize spore dispersal.
Botanists examined the structure of the sporophyll to better understand the plant's reproductive cycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frond - the leaf or leaflike part of a palm, fern, or similar plant

Etymology

From Greek 'spora' (seed, sowing) + 'phyllon' (leaf).
