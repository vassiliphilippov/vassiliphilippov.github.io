omission

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / əˈmɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
something that has been left out or excluded.
something left out or excluded

Examples

The report contained a significant omission in the financial data.
Her omission of his name from the acknowledgments was noticed by many readers.
There was an omission in the list that caused confusion.

Synset Examples

she searched the table for omissions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inclusion - something that is included or contained

Etymology

From Latin omissio(n-), from omittere (to let go, neglect).

2. NOUN

/ əˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / əˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of leaving out sounds or words in speech or writing
the act of leaving out words or sounds

Examples

The omission of a consonant at the end of a word is common in casual speech.
Typographical omission can change the meaning of a sentence entirely.
The dialect is characterized by the omission of certain vowel sounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deletion - the removal of sounds or words in linguistic context
  • Antonym: inclusion - the act of including something

Etymology

From Latin 'omissio', from 'omittere' meaning 'to let go, neglect'.

/ əˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / əˈmɪʃən / (BrE)

Examples

By omission or by design, some facts were left out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusion - the act of deliberately not including something
  • Antonym: inclusion - the act of including something

Etymology

From Latin 'omissio', from 'omittere' meaning 'to let go, omit'.

4. NOUN

/ əˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / əˈmɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a mistake made by failing to do something, especially through neglect or oversight
a mistake from neglect or failure to act

Examples

The report identified the omission of important safety checks as a major flaw.
His failure to mention her contribution was not deliberate, but it was still a serious omission.
Accidental omission of details can lead to misunderstandings in official documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: error - a mistake or incorrect action
  • Antonym: commission - the act of doing something, often contrasted with omission in the legal or moral context

Etymology

From Latin omissio(n-), from omittere (to leave out).
