Centaur

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛn.tɔr / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tɔː / (BrE)
a prominent constellation in the Southern Hemisphere near the Southern Cross
constellation in the Southern Hemisphere

Examples

The Southern Hemisphere sky features the bright constellation Centaur near the Southern Cross.
He used a telescope to observe the stars within Centaur during the summer night.
Centaur is one of the largest and most easily recognizable constellations in the southern sky.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Crux - constellation known as the Southern Cross

Etymology

From Latin 'Centaurus', named after the mythological creature.
