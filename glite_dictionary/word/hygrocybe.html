Hygrocybe

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈɡroʊsaɪbi / (AmE), / haɪˈɡrɒsɪbi / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored fungi, commonly known as waxcaps, belonging to the family Hygrophoraceae.
genus of fungi (waxcaps)

Examples

Several species of Hygrocybe can be found in old, unimproved grasslands.
The bright red cap is characteristic of many Hygrocybe mushrooms.
Mycologists often search for Hygrocybe during autumn surveys.

Etymology

From Greek 'hygros' (moist) + 'kybe' (head), referring to their damp habitats and cap shape.

2. NOUN

/ haɪˈɡrɑːsɪbi / (AmE), / haɪˈɡrɒsɪbi / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored fungi in the family Hygrophoraceae, commonly known as waxcaps.
genus of fungi known as waxcaps

Examples

Several species in the Hygrocybe genus are prized for their vibrant red and yellow caps.
Mycologists discovered a new Hygrocybe species in the old woodland last autumn.
The Hygrocybe mushrooms are often indicators of undisturbed grassland habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waxcap - common name for mushrooms of the Hygrocybe genus

Etymology

From Greek hygros (moist) + kybe (head), referring to the moist cap of the mushroom.
