netkeeper

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛtˌkiːpər / (AmE), / ˈnɛtˌkiːpə / (BrE)
the player on an ice hockey, soccer, or lacrosse team who defends the goal and attempts to prevent the opposing team from scoring
goal-defending player in team sports

Examples

The team's netkeeper made an incredible save in the final seconds of the game.
After the injury, the backup netkeeper had to step in for the rest of the match.
She trained hard all summer to become the starting netkeeper for her lacrosse team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goalkeeper - the player who defends the goal in sports such as soccer or hockey
  • Synonym: goaltender - the player responsible for protecting the goal in hockey

Etymology

Formed from 'net' (goal frame) and 'keeper' (one who keeps or guards).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɛtˌkiːpər / (AmE), / ˈnɛtˌkiːpə / (BrE)
a player in soccer or hockey who is responsible for protecting the goal and preventing the opposing team from scoring
player who protects the goal in sports

Examples

The team's netkeeper made several impressive saves in the first half.
When the regular netkeeper was injured, the coach had to find a substitute quickly.
Both the players and the netkeeper celebrated after stopping the crucial penalty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goalkeeper - player who guards the goal in sports

Etymology

Formed from 'net' (referring to the goal structure) and 'keeper' (one who guards).
