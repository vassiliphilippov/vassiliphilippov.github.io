impalpability

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpæl.pəˈbɪl.ɪ.t̬i / (AmE), / ɪmˌpal.pəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of being unable to be felt by touch; the state of being intangible or difficult to perceive physically.
the state of being intangible or not felt by touch

Examples

The impalpability of the mist made it seem otherworldly.
Despite his fame, there was an impalpability to his true personality.
The story's emotional power lay in its impalpability and subtlety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intangibility - the quality of being intangible or not able to be touched
  • Antonym: tangibility - the quality of being able to be touched or felt

Etymology

From Late Latin impalpabilis (that cannot be touched), from Latin in- (not) + palpabilis (touchable).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpælpəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpælpəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality of being difficult or impossible to comprehend or grasp mentally.
the state of being hard to comprehend

Examples

The impalpability of the concept made it difficult for scientists to agree on a definition.
She admired the poet's skill in capturing the impalpability of human emotions in his verses.
The impalpability of his reasoning left the audience confused and unsettled.

Synset Examples

The nebulous impalpability of ideas and trends was preferred at all times to the brute empiricism of humanity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intangibility - the quality of being unable to be touched or grasped
  • Antonym: comprehensibility - the quality of being able to be understood

Etymology

From im- (not) + palpable (able to be felt), from Latin palpabilis.
