Polanisia graveolens

1. NOUN

/ poʊləˈnɪziə ɡræviˈoʊlɛnz / (AmE), / pɒləˈnɪziə ɡræviˈəʊlɛnz / (BrE)
A strong-scented herb common in the southern United States, covered with glandular hairs; known for its distinctive odor.
a strong-scented herb of the southern US

Examples

The fields were dotted with the distinctively aromatic Polanisia graveolens during the summer months.
Botanists easily identified Polanisia graveolens by its strong scent and glandular hairs.
I came across a patch of Polanisia graveolens on my hike through the southern plains.

Etymology

From genus Polanisia (ultimately Greek 'polys' many + 'anisos' unequal), with species name 'graveolens' from Latin, meaning 'strong-smelling'.
