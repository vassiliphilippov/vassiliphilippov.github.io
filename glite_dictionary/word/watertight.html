watertight

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɔtərˌtaɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtətaɪt / (BrE)
not allowing water to pass in or out; impermeable by water.
impermeable to water

Examples

Make sure the boat's hull is completely watertight before taking it out on the lake.
They installed new watertight seals on the windows to prevent leaks during storms.
The document's agreement is as watertight as a submarine, leaving no room for error.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterproof - impervious to water
  • Antonym: leaky - allowing water to pass through

Etymology

Formed from 'water' and 'tight' (impermeable).

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɔtərˌtaɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtətaɪt / (BrE)
(of an argument, case, plan, or alibi) so well put together that it contains no errors, flaws, or loopholes; impossible to disprove or question.
flawless; impossible to disprove or question

Examples

She presented a watertight argument that convinced the entire jury.
The company made sure its contract was watertight to prevent legal challenges.
His alibi was so watertight that the police could not find any inconsistencies.

Synset Examples

an ironclad contract
a watertight alibi
a bulletproof argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airtight - impossible to disprove or question; flawless
  • Synonym: bulletproof - completely safe from flaws; impossible to attack successfully
  • Synonym: ironclad - so strong or firm as to be unbreakable; not able to be contradicted
