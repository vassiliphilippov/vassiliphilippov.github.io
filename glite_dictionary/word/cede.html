to cede

1. VERB

/ sid / (AmE), / siːd / (BrE)
to give control or ownership of something, especially land or rights, to another person, country, or group, usually by formal agreement
formally give up control or ownership

Examples

After the war, the country was forced to cede part of its territory to its neighbor.
The company agreed to cede control of its assets to a government regulator.
He refused to cede his authority, even when the board demanded it.

Synset Examples

The squatters had to surrender the building after the police moved in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surrender - to give up possession or control, often under compulsion
  • Synonym: to relinquish - to voluntarily cease to keep or claim something
  • Antonym: to retain - to continue to have or keep possession

Etymology

From Latin cedere (to go, yield).

2. VERB

/ siːd / (AmE), / siːd / (BrE)
to give up power, land, or rights to another person or country, especially by formal agreement
surrender or give up (rights, land, power)

Examples

The country was forced to cede large portions of its territory after the war.
He agreed to cede control of the project to his colleague.
After long negotiations, they finally ceded the disputed land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surrender - to give up possession or control to another
  • Synonym: to relinquish - to voluntarily give up or let go of something
  • Antonym: to retain - to keep possession of something

Etymology

From Latin cedere (to yield, go away).
