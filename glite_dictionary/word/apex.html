apex

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.pɛks / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.pɛks / (BrE)
The highest point or tip of something, especially one forming an angle or a peak.
the highest point of something

Examples

At the apex of the mountain, the air was thin and cold.
The company reached the apex of its success in the early 2000s.
A pyramid's apex is directly above its base's center.

Synset Examples

at the peak of the pyramid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summit - the highest point of a hill or mountain
  • Antonym: nadir - the lowest point

Etymology

From Latin apex (summit, tip, extreme end).

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.pɛks / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.pɛks / (BrE)
the point on the celestial sphere toward which the sun and solar system appear to be moving relative to the fixed stars
direction of the sun's motion in astronomy

Examples

Astronomers have determined the exact coordinates of the solar apex using advanced telescopes.
The sun is moving toward the constellation Hercules, in the direction of the apex.
Knowledge of the apex helps scientists understand the motion of our solar system through space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vertex - the highest point; the top or apex

Etymology

From Latin apex (summit, tip, extremity).
