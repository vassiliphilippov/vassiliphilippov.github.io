cask

1. NOUN

/ kæsk / (AmE), / kɑːsk / (BrE)
an amount of liquid or other substance that fills a cask; the quantity a cask contains
the quantity a cask will hold

Examples

The brewery delivered a cask of ale to the pub every week.
They imported a cask of olive oil straight from Italy.
A full cask of wine was enough for the wedding celebration.

Etymology

From Middle French *casque* (cask, helmet), from Spanish *casco* (helmet, cask).

2. NOUN

/ kæsk / (AmE), / kɑːsk / (BrE)
a large, barrel-shaped container for storing liquids, typically alcoholic drinks such as wine or whisky
large container for liquids

Examples

The winery stores its finest wine in an oak cask for several years before bottling.
A large cask of ale was brought into the banquet hall for the festivities.
Workers carefully sealed each cask to prevent the whisky from evaporating during aging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrel - a cylindrical container similar to a cask, usually made of wood, for storing liquids

Etymology

From Middle French *casque*, from Latin *cascum* (vessel, container).
