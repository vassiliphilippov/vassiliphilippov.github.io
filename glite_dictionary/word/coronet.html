coronet

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrəˈnɛt / (AmE), / ˌkɒr.əˈnɛt / (BrE)
a small crown worn by nobles or members of the aristocracy, signifying rank below that of a sovereign
small crown for nobles

Examples

The young baron wore a coronet at the royal ceremony.
Each coronet was uniquely decorated with family emblems and jewels.
The duchess's coronet sparkled in the candlelight as she entered the ballroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crown - an ornamental headdress worn by a monarch

Etymology

From Old French coronete, from Latin corona (crown).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɹəˌnɛt / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.ə.nɛt / (BrE)
the upper border of a horse's hoof where the skin of the pastern meets the horn of the hoof
upper margin of a horse's hoof

Examples

Injuries to the coronet can affect hoof growth and overall health.
The veterinarian examined the coronet for signs of infection.
Proper care of the coronet is essential for maintaining a healthy hoof.

Etymology

From Old French 'coronete', diminutive of 'corone' (crown), from Latin 'corona'.
