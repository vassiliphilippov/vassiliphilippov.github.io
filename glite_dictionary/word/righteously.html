righteously

1. ADV

/ ˈraɪ.tʃəs.li / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.tʃəs.li / (BrE)
In a way that is morally right or justifiable; in a righteous manner.
in a morally right or justifiable way

Examples

She righteously defended her friend's reputation in front of everyone.
He was righteously angry when he saw the injustice.
The judge righteously upheld the principles of fairness in his decision.

Synset Examples

righteously indignant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: justly - in a just or fair manner

Etymology

From 'righteous' + adverbial suffix '-ly'. 'Righteous' from Old English *rihtwīs* (just, upright).
