possession

1. NOUN

/ pəˈzɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / pəˈzɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
the state of having or controlling something, especially property or goods
the state of having something

Examples

She fought for the possession of her late husband's estate in court.
The museum has several rare coins in its possession.
After the divorce, the possession of the house was granted to Mark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ownership - the state or fact of possessing something
  • Antonym: dispossession - the act of depriving someone of land or property

Etymology

From Latin possessio (occupation, possession), from possidere (to possess).

3. NOUN

/ pəˈzɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / pəˈzɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
a territory or area that is controlled or governed by a ruling state, often located outside the state's main borders
territory governed by another state

Examples

The Caribbean island was a possession of France until the early twentieth century.
Several Pacific islands remain under the possession of foreign nations.
The treaty transferred the territory into British possession.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colony - a territory under the immediate political control of a distant state
  • Synonym: protectorate - a dependent territory that has local autonomy but is under the protection of another country

Etymology

From Latin possessio (act of possessing, occupation), from possidere (to have, to hold).
