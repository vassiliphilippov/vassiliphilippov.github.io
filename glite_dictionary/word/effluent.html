effluent

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛfluənt / (AmE), / ˈɛflʊənt / (BrE)
liquid waste or sewage discharged into a river or the sea.
liquid waste or sewage

Examples

The factory was fined for releasing untreated effluent into the river.
Strict regulations control the amount of effluent industries can discharge to protect the environment.
Upgrades to the plant reduced the pollution in the effluent before it reached the sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sewage - waste water and excrement conveyed in sewers

Etymology

From Latin effluere (to flow out), from ex- (out) + fluere (to flow).
