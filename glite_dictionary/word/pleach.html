to pleach

1. VERB

/ pliːtʃ / (AmE), / pliːtʃ / (BrE)
to interlace or weave together the branches or shoots of trees or shrubs, especially to form a hedge or arbor
interlace shoots or branches

Examples

Gardeners often pleach hornbeam trees to create a living fence.
They spent the afternoon learning how to pleach the new hedge along the path.
The arbor was beautifully pleached, forming a shaded walkway in the garden.

Synset Examples

pleach a hedge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plait - to braid or interweave strands together

Etymology

From Old French *plechier* (to plait, interweave), from Latin *plectere* (to plait, braid).

2. VERB

/ pliːtʃ / (AmE), / pliːtʃ / (BrE)
to interweave or braid together, especially branches or twigs, to form a hedge or other structure
interweave or braid together

Examples

The gardener expertly pleached the branches to create a living archway.
In the old village, hedges were often pleached to mark property boundaries.
They spent the afternoon pleaching willow branches into intricate patterns.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: braid - to weave together strips or strands

Etymology

From Old French *plechier*, from Latin *plectere* (to plait, to weave).
