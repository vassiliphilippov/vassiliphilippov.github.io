Lyrurus

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈrʊrəs / (AmE), / lɪˈrʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the grouse family, including the black grouse and closely related species.
genus of birds including black grouse

Examples

The genus Lyrurus includes the well-known black grouse of Europe and Asia.
Scientists studying Lyrurus observed their unique courtship displays during the spring.
Members of the genus Lyrurus are known for the lyre-shaped tails of the males.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black grouse - a large grouse of Eurasia with black plumage in males

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'lyra' (lyre) and 'oura' (tail), referring to the lyre-shaped tail feathers.

2. NOUN

/ laɪˈruːrəs / (AmE), / laɪˈruːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of grouse in the family Phasianidae, known for including the black grouse and the Caucasian grouse.
a genus of grouse birds

Examples

The black grouse is classified under the genus Lyrurus.
Two species are recognized in the genus Lyrurus, native to Eurasia.
Many ornithologists study the behavior of birds in the Lyrurus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black grouse - a large bird in the grouse family, found in Eurasia

Etymology

From New Latin, probably from Greek 'lyra' (lyre), referencing the shape of the male bird's tail.
