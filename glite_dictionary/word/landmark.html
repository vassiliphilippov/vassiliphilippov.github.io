landmark

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈlændˌmɑːk / (BrE)
a mark, such as a stone or post, used to show the boundary of a piece of land
boundary mark for land

Examples

In the 18th century, a landmark was placed at the edge of the field to settle disputes.
The old oak tree served as a natural landmark marking their property line.
Surveyors inspected each landmark to ensure the boundaries were correctly established.

Etymology

Formed from 'land' + 'mark', originally referring to marks or features on the landscape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈlændˌmɑːk / (BrE)
an event or development that marks an important stage or turning point in history or progress
important turning-point or event

Examples

The Supreme Court decision was a landmark in civil rights history.
Her election as the first female president was a landmark event for the country.
The discovery marked a landmark in medical research.

Synset Examples

the agreement was a watershed in the history of both nations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watershed - a critical turning point or dividing line in events
  • Synonym: milestone - an important event marking a significant stage or development

Etymology

Originally referred to a physical mark on the land; later extended figuratively to important events or developments.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈlɑːndˌmɑːk / (BrE)
a prominent object or feature in a particular area that serves as a point of reference or helps people identify their location.
prominent feature used as a reference point

Examples

The old oak tree served as a useful landmark for lost hikers in the forest.
The Eiffel Tower is perhaps the most famous landmark in Paris.
Please turn left at the gas station; it's a well-known landmark in this neighborhood.

Synset Examples

the church steeple provided a convenient landmark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monument - a structure erected to commemorate persons or events

Etymology

From Middle English 'landmerk', from Old English 'landmearc' (land boundary).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈlænˌdmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈlɑːn(d)mɑːk / (BrE)
an anatomical structure used as a reference point in locating other anatomical structures or for taking measurements, especially in surgery or anatomy
anatomical reference point
medical

Examples

The surgeon used the bony landmark to determine the site of the incision.
Prominent anatomical landmarks help clinicians identify underlying structures during examination.
Measurement of limb length is often based on specific landmarks such as joints or protuberances.
