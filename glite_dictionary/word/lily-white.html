lily-white

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪliˌwaɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪliˌwaɪt / (BrE)
(of an organization or group) restricted to or composed entirely of white people, often in reference to policies of racial exclusion.
composed only of white people
offensive archaic

Examples

The exclusive club had a strict lily-white membership policy for decades.
Many neighborhoods were once deliberately kept lily-white through discriminatory laws.
The politician faced criticism for supporting lily-white organizations.

Synset Examples

under segregation there were even white restrooms and white drinking fountains
a lily-white movement which would expel Negroes from the organization

Etymology

From the image of the white lily flower, symbolizing pure whiteness; figuratively used due to associations with the color white.
