variform

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛrɪˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈvɛərɪˌfɔːm / (BrE)
having various forms or shapes; varying in form.
having various forms or shapes

Examples

The artist creates variform sculptures that captivate viewers with their diversity.
The festival included variform performances ranging from dance to spoken word.
The leaves of the plant are variform, some being round while others have jagged edges.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uniform - remaining the same in all forms or appearances
  • Synonym: multiform - having many forms; of diverse shapes

Etymology

From Latin varius (various) + -form (form, shape).
