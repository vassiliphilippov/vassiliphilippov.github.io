to personate

1. VERB

/ ˈpɜrsəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsəneɪt / (BrE)
to pretend to be another person, especially in order to deceive or to perform in a role
pretend to be another person

Examples

The actor managed to personate the famous painter with remarkable accuracy.
He attempted to personate a police officer to gain entry into the building.
During the play, she would personate several characters, each with a distinct voice.

Synset Examples

She posed as the Czar's daughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impersonate - to pretend to be another person
  • Synonym: masquerade - to pretend to be someone you are not

Etymology

From Latin 'personatus' (masked, disguised), from 'persona' (character, person).

2. VERB

/ ˈpɜːrsəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsəneɪt / (BrE)
(archaic) To attribute human qualities to something; to personify.
to attribute human qualities (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In ancient myths, poets would often personate the sun as a mighty chariot driver.
The novelist chose to personate nature, giving the forest desires and moods.
Many cultures personate abstract concepts such as Justice or Death in art and storytelling.

Synset Examples

The Greeks personated their gods ridiculous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to personify - to attribute human qualities to something

Etymology

From Latin 'personatus', past participle of 'personare' (to sound through a mask, to personify).
