Gregory of Nazianzus

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡrɛɡəri əv næziˈænzəs / (AmE), / ˈɡrɛɡəri əv næziˈænzəs / (BrE)
(c. 329–c. 391) An early Christian theologian and archbishop regarded as a Church Father, esteemed for his writings and efforts against heresies; recognized as a saint and Doctor of the Church in Roman Catholic tradition.
early Christian theologian and Church Father

Examples

Gregory of Nazianzus played a pivotal role in shaping early Christian theology.
The writings of Gregory of Nazianzus are still studied in Christian seminaries today.
Many recognize Gregory of Nazianzus as a defender of Trinitarian doctrine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Basil the Great - a notable Church Father and close associate of Gregory of Nazianzus
  • Synonym: Athanasius of Alexandria - another influential early Church Father who combated heresies
