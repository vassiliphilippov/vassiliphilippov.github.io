simpleton

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəltən / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəltən / (BrE)
a person who is not very intelligent or lacks common sense; a foolish or gullible person
foolish or unintelligent person

Examples

Only a simpleton would believe such an outrageous story without asking any questions.
He was no simpleton and saw through their attempt to deceive him.
They treated her like a simpleton, even though she understood more than they realized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person who acts unwisely; synonym for 'simpleton'
  • Antonym: genius - a person of very great intelligence; antonym of 'simpleton'

Etymology

Formed from 'simple' with the diminutive suffix '-ton,' first recorded in the 17th century.
