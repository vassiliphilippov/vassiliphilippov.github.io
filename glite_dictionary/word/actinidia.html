Actinidia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæk.tɪˈnɪd.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌæk.tɪˈnɪd.i.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small woody vines native to Asia, known for bearing many-seeded fruit such as the kiwifruit.
genus of Asiatic woody vines

Examples

Actinidia plants are widely cultivated in temperate regions for their edible fruit.
Botanists have identified over forty distinct species within the Actinidia genus.
The best-known member of the Actinidia genus is the kiwifruit vine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kiwifruit - the edible fruit of some Actinidia species

Etymology

From Greek 'aktis' (ray) and '-idia', referring to the ray-like arrangement of parts in the flower.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæktəˈnɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌaktɪˈnɪdɪə / (BrE)
a genus of small woody vines native to Asia, known for bearing many-seeded fruit such as the kiwifruit
genus of woody Asian vines, includes kiwifruit

Examples

The kiwifruit belongs to the genus Actinidia, which is native to East Asia.
Botanists are studying several species within the Actinidia genus for their unique fruit characteristics.
Some ornamental gardens feature vines of the genus Actinidia for their attractive foliage and edible fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kiwifruit - the fruit produced by plants of genus Actinidia, especially Actinidia deliciosa

Etymology

From Greek 'aktis' (ray) for the radiating styles of the flower.
