shirtfront

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɜrtˌfrʌnt / (AmE), / ˈʃɜːtˌfrʌnt / (BrE)
the front part of a shirt, especially the area not covered by a jacket or coat
front part of a shirt

Examples

He noticed a stain on his shirtfront just before the meeting.
The waiter wore a crisp white shirtfront under his tuxedo jacket.
She pinned a decorative brooch to her shirtfront for a touch of elegance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɝːt.frʌnt / (AmE), / ˈʃɜːt.frʌnt / (BrE)
a detachable, often starched piece of cloth simulating the front of a man's shirt, intended to be worn with formal dress
detachable front part of a man's formal shirt

Examples

He wore a stiff shirtfront with his tuxedo to the gala.
The shirtfront was meticulously ironed and perfectly white for the evening event.
In the 19th century, gentlemen often used a shirtfront for formal occasions.
