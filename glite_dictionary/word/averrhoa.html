Averrhoa

1. NOUN

/ ˌævərˈhoʊ.ə / (AmE), / ˌævəˈrəʊ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of trees native to the East Indies, including the carambola; characterized by pinnate leaves.
genus of trees including carambola

Examples

The Averrhoa genus includes the tree that produces starfruit.
Botanists studied several species within the Averrhoa group for their unique leaves and fruit.
Averrhoa trees have long been cultivated in tropical regions for their edible fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carambola - tropical fruit tree also known as starfruit

Etymology

Named after the Arab philosopher and scientist Averroës (Ibn Rushd, 1126–1198).

2. NOUN

/ ˌævərˈhoʊə / (AmE), / ˌævəˈrəʊə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees, native to the East Indies, with pinnate leaves; includes the carambola (star fruit).
genus of tropical trees; includes star fruit

Examples

The Averrhoa genus is best known for producing the star-shaped fruit called carambola.
Botanists studied several rare species within the Averrhoa group native to Southeast Asia.
The most commercially important member of Averrhoa is the tree yielding star fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carambola - a tropical fruit tree known as star fruit

Etymology

Named after Averroes (Ibn Rushd), a 12th-century Andalusian philosopher and physician.
