sidewall

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌwɔl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌwɔːl / (BrE)
a wall that forms the side of a structure, such as a building, vehicle, or container
side wall of a structure

Examples

The basketball bounced off the sidewall and rolled back onto the court.
Inspect the sidewall of your tire regularly for any signs of damage.
Moss was growing along the sidewall of the old stone building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wall - vertical structure that encloses or divides an area

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌwɔl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪd.wɔːl / (BrE)
the part of a tire that forms the side between the tread and the wheel rim
the side of a tire between tread and rim

Examples

The sidewall of the tire was damaged after hitting the curb.
Classic cars often feature white sidewalls for a distinctive look.
Check the sidewall for information about the tire's size and recommended pressure.

Synset Examples

the car had white sidewalls

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tread - the part of a tire that touches the road
