fiduciary

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈduːʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / fɪˈdjuːʃiːəri / (BrE)
a person who has the legal and ethical responsibility to manage someone else’s assets or interests on their behalf
person managing assets for another
legal

Examples

As a fiduciary, she must always act in her clients’ best interests.
A fiduciary who violates trust can face serious legal consequences.
Banks often serve as a fiduciary when managing investment funds.

Synset Examples

it is illegal for a fiduciary to misappropriate money for personal gain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trustee - person or entity that holds assets for the benefit of another
  • Antonym: beneficiary - person for whose benefit assets are managed

Etymology

From Latin fiduciarius, from fiducia (trust, confidence), from fidere (to trust).

2. ADJ

/ fɪˈduːʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / fɪˈdjuːʃɪəri / (BrE)
relating to a relationship of trust, especially when one person is responsible for managing assets for another's benefit
related to a legal trust or trust-based duty
legal

Examples

The company has a fiduciary duty to act in the best interests of its shareholders.
He acted in a fiduciary capacity when managing her financial affairs.
Banks are required to adhere to strict fiduciary standards to protect clients' money.

Synset Examples

a fiduciary contract
in a fiduciary capacity
fiducial power

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trustee - a person who holds property or assets for the benefit of another

Etymology

From Latin fiduciarius (entrusted), from fiducia (trust).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02859273-a
Synset POS: a
