klutz

1. NOUN

/ klʌts / (AmE), / klʌts / (BrE)
A clumsy or awkward person.
a clumsy or awkward person

Examples

Don't let Tom carry the cake—he's such a klutz!
I tripped over my own feet like a real klutz during the dance recital.
Even as a child, she was a klutz and always got bumps and bruises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bungler - a person who habitually makes mistakes or is clumsy
  • Antonym: graceful person - a person who moves smoothly and elegantly

Etymology

From Yiddish *klots* (wooden block, clumsy person), of German origin.
