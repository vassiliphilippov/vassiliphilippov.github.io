Monochamus

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑːkəməs / (AmE), / məˈnɒkəməs / (BrE)
A genus of large longhorn beetles known as sawyer beetles, especially found in pine forests and known for boring into wood.
genus of sawyer beetles

Examples

Monochamus larvae are known for boring into pine trees and causing damage to timber.
The forestry experts identified the beetle as a species within the Monochamus genus.
Some species of Monochamus act as vectors for the pinewood nematode, threatening forest health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sawyer beetle - large longhorn beetle that bores into wood
  • Degree Higher: longhorn beetle - beetle of the family Cerambycidae with long antennae

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) and 'chaimos' (gape, opening), referencing the beetle's mandibles.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɒnəˈkeɪməs / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈkeɪməs / (BrE)
a genus of large longhorn beetles, commonly known as sawyer beetles, whose larvae bore into wood, especially pine trees
a genus of longhorn beetles (sawyer beetles)

Examples

The larvae of the Monochamus beetle can severely damage pine trees by boring into the wood.
Forestry experts monitor Monochamus populations to prevent outbreaks of pine wilt disease.
Several species within the Monochamus genus are considered major pests in North America and Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sawyer beetle - common name for beetles of the genus Monochamus

Etymology

From Greek monos (single) + khamus (gape, opening), used as a genus name in taxonomy.
