psychological

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪkəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the mind, emotions, or mental processes, rather than the physical body
relating to the mind or emotions

Examples

The patient was offered psychological support to help cope with the trauma.
The film explores the psychological effects of isolation on the human mind.
Employers are paying more attention to the psychological well-being of their staff.

Synset Examples

give psychological support
psychological warfare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mental - relating to the mind
  • Antonym: physical - relating to the body as opposed to the mind

Etymology

From Greek 'psychē' (soul, mind) and '-logical' (relating to logic or study).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪ.kəˈlɑ.dʒɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌsaɪ.kəˈlɒ.dʒɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to the mind, mental processes, or the science of psychology
relating to the mind or psychology

Examples

The athletes undergo psychological testing before every major competition.
Her struggles were not physical but psychological in nature.
He wrote several books on psychological theories related to learning.

Synset Examples

psychological theories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mental - relating to the mind
  • Synonym: emotional - relating to emotions
  • Antonym: physical - relating to the body

Etymology

From Greek 'psyche' (soul, mind) and '-logical' from 'logos' (word, reason); via French 'psychologique'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02917107-a
Synset POS: a
