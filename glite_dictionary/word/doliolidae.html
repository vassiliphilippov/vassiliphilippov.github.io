Doliolidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌdoʊliˈɑːlɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌdəʊlɪˈɒlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, barrel-shaped oceanic tunicates found in marine waters, known for their complex life cycles and transparent, gelatinous bodies.
family of oceanic tunicates

Examples

Marine biologists study Doliolidae to understand the diversity of oceanic plankton.
The transparent bodies of Doliolidae make them difficult to spot in the open sea.
Doliolidae play a critical role in the ocean's food web as filter feeders.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Doliolum, from Latin 'doliolum' (a small cask or barrel).
