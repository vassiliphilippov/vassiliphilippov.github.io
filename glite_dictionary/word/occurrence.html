occurrence

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɝːəns / (AmE), / əˈkʌrəns / (BrE)
an event or incident; something that happens or takes place
event; something that happens

Examples

The rare occurrence of a lunar eclipse fascinated astronomers around the world.
Flooding has become a common occurrence in this region during the rainy season.
Each occurrence of the error was carefully documented by the technicians.

Synset Examples

a disease of frequent occurrence
the occurrence (or presence) of life on other planets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: event - something that happens, especially an important occurrence
  • Synonym: incident - a distinct event, especially one of minor importance

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'occurrentia', from Latin 'occurrere' (to happen, come to mind).

2. NOUN

/ əˈkɝː.əns / (AmE), / əˈkʌr.əns / (BrE)
an event or incident, especially one that happens unexpectedly or is notable
an event that happens

Examples

The sudden occurrence of thunderstorms surprised the hikers.
Heart disease is a common occurrence among older adults.
Such an occurrence is rare in this part of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: event - something that happens, especially something important
  • Synonym: incident - an individual occurrence or event

Etymology

From Latin 'occurrere' (to happen, to occur) via Medieval Latin 'occurrentia'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07298313-n
Synset POS: n
