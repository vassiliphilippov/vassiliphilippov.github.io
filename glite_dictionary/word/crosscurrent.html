crosscurrent

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌkɝrənt / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌkʌrənt / (BrE)
A current of water moving at an angle to or intersecting another current, often causing turbulence in a river or the sea.
a current flowing across another

Examples

The small boat struggled against the crosscurrent near the river's mouth.
Strong crosscurrents made swimming across the channel extremely dangerous.
Divers must always be cautious of unexpected crosscurrents while exploring coral reefs.

Etymology

From 'cross' (to go across) + 'current' (flow of water or air).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌkɝrənt / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌkʌrənt / (BrE)
a tendency, action, or influence that opposes or runs counter to the prevailing direction or main activity, especially within a group or movement
opposing tendency or influence

Examples

There were significant crosscurrents within the company, making agreement difficult.
The debate was marked by unexpected crosscurrents that challenged the main arguments.
Political crosscurrents disrupted the conference, leading to heated discussions.

Synset Examples

political crosscurrents disrupted the conference

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undercurrent - a hidden or underlying tendency or influence often opposed to the apparent

Etymology

Formed from 'cross' (across) and 'current' (a flow of water or air), extending the literal meaning to figurative use.
