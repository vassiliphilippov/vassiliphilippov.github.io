handwheel

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌwil / (AmE), / ˈhændwiːl / (BrE)
A wheel designed to be turned by hand, typically to operate a machine or device.
wheel turned by hand to operate a device

Examples

Turn the handwheel clockwise to raise the platform.
The operator used the handwheel to adjust the flow of water.
Make sure the handwheel is securely fastened before use.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌwil / (AmE), / ˈhændˌwiːl / (BrE)
a wheel with a rim designed to be grasped by hand and turned to operate or adjust a machine or device
wheel for manual control of a device

Examples

The operator turned the handwheel to adjust the position of the table.
Be sure to tighten the handwheel before starting the machine.
Some old valves are still opened and closed manually with a large handwheel.

  • Synonym: knob - a rounded handle for turning or pulling something
