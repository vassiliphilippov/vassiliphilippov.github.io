Paspalum dilatatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæspələm daɪˈleɪtətəm / (AmE), / ˌpaspaləm dɪˈlɑːtatəm / (BrE)
a tall, tufted perennial grass native to tropical America and widely grown as pasture and forage grass, especially in the southern United States
a perennial tropical American pasture grass

Examples

The ranchers prefer to plant Paspalum dilatatum for its resilience in humid conditions.
Extensive fields of Paspalum dilatatum were visible throughout the southern states.
Livestock graze on Paspalum dilatatum due to its nutritious forage value.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Paspalum' is the genus name, and 'dilatatum' means 'spread out' or 'expanded.'
