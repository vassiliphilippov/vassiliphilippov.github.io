pachydermous

1. ADJ

/ ˌpæk.ɪˈdɜːr.məs / (AmE), / ˌpak.ɪˈdɜː.məs / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of pachyderms (thick-skinned animals such as elephants or rhinoceroses)
relating to pachyderms (thick-skinned animals)

Examples

Her research focused on the pachydermous animals of Africa, particularly elephants and hippos.
The pachydermous hide of a rhinoceros provides natural protection against predators.
His pachydermous attitude made him seem emotionally thick-skinned and unresponsive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pachydermatous - having thick skin; relating to pachyderms

Etymology

From Greek 'pachys' (thick) + 'derma' (skin), via 'pachyderm' + '-ous'.
