Rosa eglanteria

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzə ˌɛɡlænˈtɪriə / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzə ˌɛɡlænˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
A Eurasian species of wild rose with prickly stems, fragrant leaves, bright pink flowers, and scarlet hips; also known as sweet briar.
species of wild rose (sweet briar)

Examples

The garden featured a beautiful Rosa eglanteria hedge, famous for its fragrant leaves.
She planted Rosa eglanteria to attract birds with its scarlet hips in autumn.
Many poets have referred to the delicate scent of Rosa eglanteria in their verses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetbriar - common name for Rosa eglanteria

Etymology

From Latin 'rosa' (rose) and 'eglanteria', from Old French 'eglentier' (sweet briar).
