longbowman

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋˌboʊmən / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋbəʊmən / (BrE)
a medieval English archer who used a longbow, especially as a soldier in warfare
medieval English archer using a longbow

Examples

The Welsh army deployed several skilled longbowmen at the front lines.
A single longbowman could shoot arrows with remarkable distance and accuracy.
English longbowmen played a crucial role at the Battle of Agincourt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archer - a person who shoots with a bow and arrow

Etymology

From longbow + man; referring to a person (man) who used the longbow.
