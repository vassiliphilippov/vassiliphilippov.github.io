cutpurse

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌtˌpɜrs / (AmE), / ˈkʌtˌpɜːs / (BrE)
a thief who steals from the pockets or purses of others in public places, especially in historical contexts
historical pickpocket or purse thief
archaic

Examples

In Shakespeare's time, a skilled cutpurse could disappear into the crowd unnoticed.
The notorious cutpurse was finally apprehended at the marketplace by the city guard.
Old London streets were once infamous for gangs of cutpurses preying on unwary travelers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickpocket - a person who steals from people's pockets or bags in public places

Etymology

From Middle English, literally 'one who cuts purses' (with a knife or blade) to steal money.
