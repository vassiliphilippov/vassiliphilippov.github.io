prang

1. NOUN

/ præŋ / (AmE), / præŋ / (BrE)
a minor crash or accident involving a car or plane
minor crash (car or plane)
british informal

Examples

There was a small prang at the intersection, but luckily no one was hurt.
The pilot managed to walk away from the prang with only a few scratches.
After the prang, they exchanged insurance details and drove off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crash - a collision causing damage, typically of vehicles or aircraft

Etymology

Originally British slang, possibly from RAF (Royal Air Force) slang of World War II; origin uncertain.

2. VERB

/ præŋ / (AmE), / præŋ / (BrE)
(informal, British) To crash a vehicle, especially a car, typically in a minor accident.
to crash a vehicle (informal, British)
informal british

Examples

He pranged his dad's car while trying to park last night.
If you prang the company van, you’ll have to pay for the repairs.
She was relieved no one was injured after she pranged into a lamppost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crash - to collide violently with an obstacle (especially a vehicle)

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly World War I RAF slang, first attested in the early 20th century.
