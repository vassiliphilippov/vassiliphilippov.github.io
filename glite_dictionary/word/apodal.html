apodal

1. ADJ

/ ˈæp.ə.dəl / (AmE), / ˈap.ə.dəl / (BrE)
(of animals) having no feet or limbs; footless.
lacking feet or limbs

Examples

Many species of snakes are apodal and have adapted to move without limbs.
The apodal body structure of eels makes them excellent swimmers in narrow spaces.
Some amphibians have evolved from having legs to being completely apodal in adulthood.

Synset Examples

eels are apodal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: footless - lacking feet; having no feet

Etymology

From Greek apous, apod- (footless), from a- (without) + pous (foot).
