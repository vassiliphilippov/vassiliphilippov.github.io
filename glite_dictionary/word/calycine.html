calycine

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæl.ɪ.saɪn / (AmE), / ˈkæl.ɪ.siːn / (BrE)
relating to or resembling a calyx, the outermost whorl of a flower, typically consisting of sepals.
relating to a calyx (flower part)

Examples

The calycine structure is easily visible in this species of wildflower.
Under the microscope, the calycine segments appear slightly hairy.
The calycine tube is unusually elongated in some tropical plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calyx - the outermost whorl of a flower, usually green and protecting the petals

Etymology

From Latin 'calyx' (cup, calyx) plus '-ine' (forming adjectives).
