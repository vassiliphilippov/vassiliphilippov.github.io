Lycopodiaceae

1. NOUN

/ laɪkəˌpoʊdiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / laɪkəˌpɒdiˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of spore-producing vascular plants, commonly known as clubmosses, belonging to the order Lycopodiales.
a family of clubmosses (plants)

Examples

The Lycopodiaceae family includes several species known commonly as clubmosses.
Members of the Lycopodiaceae can be found in temperate and tropical regions around the world.
Botanists have studied the reproductive cycles of Lycopodiaceae for insights into plant evolution.

Etymology

From the genus Lycopodium + Latin -aceae, a standard botanical suffix for plant family names.

family of clubmosses (plants)

Examples

The Lycopodiaceae are often referred to as clubmosses due to their resemblance to true mosses.
Scientists study the Lycopodiaceae to learn more about the evolution of vascular plants.
The Lycopodiaceae have been used historically in traditional medicine and rituals.

Related Terms

Etymology

From New Latin Lycopodium (a representative genus) + -aceae, the standard suffix for plant family names.
