to archaize

1. VERB

/ ˈɑːrkeɪˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɪˌaɪz / (BrE)
to give something an archaic or old-fashioned appearance or character
make archaic in appearance or style

Examples

The artist tried to archaize the building's design by using medieval motifs.
This novelist tends to archaize his prose to evoke a sense of history.
Their crafts deliberately archaize objects to make them appear centuries old.

Synset Examples

archaized craftwork

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to modernize - to give a modern character or appearance to

Etymology

From Greek archaizein (to imitate the ancients), from archaios (ancient).
