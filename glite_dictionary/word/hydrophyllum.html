Hydrophyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈfɪliəm / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəˈfɪlɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of North American herbaceous plants in the borage family, commonly known as waterleafs, typically found in moist woodlands.
genus of North American waterleaf plants

Examples

The woodland floor was covered in patches of Hydrophyllum during the spring bloom.
Many native gardens include Hydrophyllum for its attractive foliage and flowers.
Scientists studied several species of Hydrophyllum to understand their ecological roles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterleaf - common name for plants of the genus Hydrophyllum

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro' (water) + 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to its moist habitat and leafy appearance.
