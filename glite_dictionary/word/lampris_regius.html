Lampris regius

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmprɪs ˈriːdʒi.əs / (AmE), / ˈlamprɪs ˈriːdʒɪəs / (BrE)
a large, brightly colored elliptical deep-sea fish found in the Atlantic, Pacific, and Mediterranean, also known as the opah
a large, brightly colored deep-sea fish (opah)

Examples

The Lampris regius is famous for its vibrant colors and disc-shaped body.
Scientists recently observed a Lampris regius in the deep waters off the California coast.
Chefs prize the meat of the Lampris regius for its rich, distinctive flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opah - common name for Lampris regius, a large, colorful deep-sea fish

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining the genus 'Lampris' and species 'regius', meaning 'royal'.
