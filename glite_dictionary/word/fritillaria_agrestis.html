Fritillaria agrestis

1. PROPN

/ frɪˌtɪləriə əˈɡrɛstɪs / (AmE), / frɪˌtɪlɪˈɛəriə əˈɡrɛstɪs / (BrE)
A malodorous plant native to California, with bell-shaped flowers, commonly found as a weed in grainfields.
malodorous California plant; grainfield weed

Examples

Fritillaria agrestis is known for its strong, unpleasant odor that deters grazing animals.
Botanists often find Fritillaria agrestis thriving in disturbed soil near California’s grainfields.
Farmers consider Fritillaria agrestis a persistent weed among their crops.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Fritillaria', the genus name, refers to a dice box, and 'agrestis' means 'of the fields'.
