ebony

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛbəni / (AmE), / ˈɛbəni / (BrE)
Very dark black in color.
jet-black color

Examples

She wore an ebony gown that shimmered under the lights.
His ebony hair contrasted sharply with his pale skin.
The cat's fur was so ebony that it seemed to absorb all the light in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jet-black - completely black, like jet
  • Degree Lower: black - having the darkest color; like the night sky

Etymology

From Latin 'ebenus', from Greek 'ebenos', referring to the ebony tree and its dark wood.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛbəni / (AmE), / ˈɛbəni / (BrE)
A dense, hard, dark-colored wood from the ebony tree, valued for use in furniture, musical instruments, and decorative items.
hard dark wood from the ebony tree

Examples

The piano keys were originally made from ebony and ivory.
The craftsman carved a statue from a solid block of ebony.
Her desk was an antique, made of polished ebony wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - hard reddish-brown wood used for high-quality furniture

Etymology

From Latin *hebeninus*, from Greek *ebenos* (ebony tree).

4. NOUN

/ ˈɛbəni / (AmE), / ˈɛbəni / (BrE)
A tropical tree of southern Asia with hard, dark-colored heartwood used in making furniture and decorative objects.
tropical tree with hard, dark wood

Examples

The craftsman carved an intricate design from a block of ebony.
Furniture made of ebony is prized for its beauty and durability.
Some musical instruments, like piano keys, were traditionally made from ebony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rosewood - type of tropical hardwood used for furniture

Etymology

From Latin *hebenus*, from Greek *ébenos*, meaning 'ebony tree'.
