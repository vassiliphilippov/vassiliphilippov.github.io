nastiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæs.ti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnɑːs.ti.nəs / (BrE)
a condition or quality of being extremely dirty, foul, or disgusting, especially due to filth or refuse
state of being dirty or foul

Examples

The nastiness of the abandoned building made it hard to breathe inside.
Years of neglect had led to a level of nastiness in the alley that disgusted everyone.
The flood left behind a nastiness that took days to clean up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filthiness - the quality of being very dirty or filthy

Etymology

From 'nasty' + '-ness', originally from Dutch 'nastig' (dirty, nasty).

/ ˈnæs.ti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnɑː.sti.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being very unpleasant or offensive
unpleasant or offensive quality

Examples

Despite the nastiness of his cold, he still managed to attend the event.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ˈnæs.ti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnɑːs.ti.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being nasty, especially in a malicious, spiteful, or unpleasant manner.
the quality of being nasty or spiteful

Examples

The nastiness in his comments upset everyone at the meeting.
She was shocked by the nastiness of the online messages she received.
Political debates can sometimes descend into personal nastiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malice - the intention or desire to do evil; ill will
  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate

Etymology

From 'nasty' + '-ness', forming a noun denoting the state or quality.
