shoaly

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃoʊli / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊli / (BrE)
(of an area of water) having many shoals, sandbanks, or submerged reefs.
full of shoals or sandbanks

Examples

We avoided the shoaly inlet because the boat might run aground.
The fisherman warned us about the shoaly parts near the coast.
Exploring shoaly waters can be dangerous without proper navigation charts.

Synset Examples

reefy shallows
shoaly waters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shallow - not deep (of water or similar areas)

Etymology

From "shoal" (a shallow place in a body of water) + "-y" (forming adjectives).
