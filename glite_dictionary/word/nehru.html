Nehru

1. PROPN

/ ˈneɪruː / (AmE), / ˈneɪruː / (BrE)
(1889–1964) Indian statesman, the first Prime Minister of India (1947–1964), and a leading figure in the movement for Indian independence.
Indian prime minister, independence leader

Examples

Nehru played a key role in shaping modern India's government after independence.
At the United Nations, Nehru delivered speeches advocating for world peace.
The policies introduced by Nehru emphasized education and industrialization.

Etymology

From the Hindi name Nehru, derived from the Sanskrit 'nahar' (canal), originally a family name.
