Heteranthera

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛtərəˈnθɪrə / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈnθɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic or semi-aquatic plants in the pickerelweed family (Pontederiaceae), commonly known as mud plantains.
genus of aquatic mud plantains

Examples

The shallow pond was filled with blooming Heteranthera during the summer months.
Botanists discovered a rare species of Heteranthera in the flooded wetlands of the region.
Many aquarists choose to plant Heteranthera in their freshwater tanks for its attractive foliage.

Etymology

From Greek 'heteros' (different) + 'anthera' (anther), referring to the flowers' differing stamens.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛtəˈrænθərə / (AmE), / ˌhɛtəˈrænθərə / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic or marsh plants in the water hyacinth family (Pontederiaceae), commonly known as mud plantains, found in tropical and subtropical regions.
a genus of aquatic plants (mud plantains)

Examples

Heteranthera species are often found in shallow, nutrient-rich freshwater habitats.
Botanists have recently identified a new species within the Heteranthera genus in South America.
The floating leaves of Heteranthera help stabilize the muddy banks where these plants thrive.

Etymology

From Greek 'heteros' (different) and 'anthera' (anther), referring to diverse stamen structures.
