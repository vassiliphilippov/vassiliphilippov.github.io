to entrust

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtrʌst / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrʌst / (BrE)
to give someone the responsibility for something or someone, or put something into their care
give responsibility or care to someone

Examples

We decided to entrust the keys to our neighbor while we were on holiday.
She felt confident enough to entrust her children to the new babysitter.
The company will entrust you with confidential information after your training.

Synset Examples

He left the decision to his deputy
leave your child in the nurse's care

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delegate - to give a task or responsibility to another person
  • Synonym: trust - to place confidence in someone or something

Etymology

From Old French 'en-' (in) + 'truster' (to trust).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈtrʌst / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrʌst / (BrE)
to give someone the responsibility for doing something or for taking care of someone or something
give someone responsibility for something

Examples

She decided to entrust her car keys to her best friend while she was on vacation.
Hospitals often entrust important medical equipment to senior staff only.
The company entrusted the new manager with leading the expansion project.

Synset Examples

The messenger was entrusted with the general's secret
I commit my soul to God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delegate - to give a particular job, duty, right, etc. to someone else
  • Synonym: to assign - to give someone a task to do

Etymology

From Old French *enstruster*, from Latin *intrustare* (to assign, to confide), from *in-* (in) + *trustare* (to trust).

Synset POS: v
