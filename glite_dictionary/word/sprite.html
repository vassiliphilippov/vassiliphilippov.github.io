sprite

1. NOUN

/ spraɪt / (AmE), / spraɪt / (BrE)
(in folklore) a small, playful being with magical powers, usually depicted as human in form.
mythical small magical being

Examples

In the story, a mischievous sprite led the lost travelers out of the forest.
Legends say that sprites live among the flowers and help them bloom.
Children dressed as sprites for the village festival, with wings and sparkling costumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairy - a small imaginary being of human form with magical powers

Etymology

From Middle English *sprit*, a variant of *spirit*, from Latin *spiritus* (breath, spirit).

2. NOUN

/ spraɪt / (AmE), / spraɪt / (BrE)
a brief, luminous flash of red or pink light that appears high above thunderstorm clouds, caused by atmospheric electricity, often rising to altitudes up to 60 miles
flashes of red light above thunderstorms

Examples

Scientists observed several sprites lighting up the sky above the thunderstorm last night.
Photographs of sprites often show them as bright red flashes far above lightning clouds.
Research aircraft are sometimes used to study sprites and other high-altitude lightning phenomena.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elf - a similar atmospheric optical phenomenon, appearing as rapidly expanding disk-shaped glows above thunderstorms
