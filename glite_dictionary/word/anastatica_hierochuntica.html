Anastatica hierochuntica

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈstætɪkə ˌhaɪ.ərəˈkʌntɪkə / (AmE), / ˌænəˈstatɪkə ˌhaɪərəˈkʌntɪkə / (BrE)
A small, grey desert plant native to Asia, known for its ability to curl up when dry and reopen when exposed to moisture; also called the Rose of Jericho.
desert plant that curls up when dry

Examples

Anastatica hierochuntica is often called the Rose of Jericho due to its extraordinary ability to survive drought.
Desert travelers once carried Anastatica hierochuntica as a symbol of hope and renewal.
When watered after a long dry period, Anastatica hierochuntica quickly unfolds its branches and resumes growth.

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus name Anastatica from Greek 'anastasis' (resurrection) and species name hierochuntica referring to Jericho.
