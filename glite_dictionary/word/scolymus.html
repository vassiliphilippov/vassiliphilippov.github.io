Scolymus

1. NOUN

/ skoʊˈlaɪməs / (AmE), / skɒˈlɪməs / (BrE)
A small genus of thistle-like herbs native to the Mediterranean region, known for their spiny leaves and yellow flowers.
genus of Mediterranean thistle-like herbs

Examples

The ancient Greeks used the roots of Scolymus as a vegetable.
A field survey revealed that Scolymus species are common in Mediterranean grasslands.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the genus Scolymus.

Etymology

From Greek *skolymos* (a kind of thistle).
