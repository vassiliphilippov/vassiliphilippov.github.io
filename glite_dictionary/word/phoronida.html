Phoronida

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈrɒnɪdə / (AmE), / fəˈrɒnɪdə / (BrE)
A small phylum of wormlike marine animals that live in tubes and have a crown of tentacles around the mouth.
a phylum of wormlike marine animals

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species in the phylum Phoronida during the marine expedition.
Members of Phoronida are commonly called horseshoe worms due to their tentacled lophophore.
The biology textbook included a section on the anatomy of Phoronida.

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Greek 'phorōn' (bearing); named for the genus Phoronis.

2. NOUN

/ fəˈrɑːnɪdə / (AmE), / fəˈrɒnɪdə / (BrE)
a small phylum of wormlike marine animals characterized by a tubular body and a crown of tentacles, known as lophophores
phylum of wormlike marine animals

Examples

Scientists have discovered new species within the Phoronida along the Atlantic coast.
The Phoronida construct tubular burrows in muddy or sandy substrates in shallow seas.
Members of Phoronida are filter feeders, using their lophophores to capture plankton.

Etymology

From the genus name Phoronis (named after Greek mythological figure Phoronis) + -ida, a zoological suffix.
