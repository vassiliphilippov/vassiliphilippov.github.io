lepidopterology

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪˌdɑptəˈrɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌlepɪˌdɒptəˈrɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of entomology that studies butterflies and moths (Lepidoptera)
study of butterflies and moths
medical

Examples

She developed a keen interest in lepidopterology after observing butterflies in her garden.
His extensive research in lepidopterology contributed valuable knowledge about endangered moth species.
Many museums employ experts in lepidopterology to curate their butterfly and moth collections.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: entomology - the scientific study of insects
  • Synonym: butterfly - an insect of the order Lepidoptera, typically with brightly colored wings
  • Synonym: moth - an insect related to butterflies, often nocturnal with feathery antennae

Etymology

From Greek 'lepis' (scale) and 'pteron' (wing), referring to scaly wings of butterflies and moths, plus '-logy' (study of).
