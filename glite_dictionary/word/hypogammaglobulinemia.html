hypogammaglobulinemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpoʊˌɡæməˌɡlɑbjuːlɪˈmiːə / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊˌɡæməˌɡlɒbjʊlɪˈmiːə / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by an abnormally low concentration of gamma globulin in the blood, increasing the risk of infection
abnormally low gamma globulin in the blood
medical

Examples

The diagnosis of hypogammaglobulinemia explained her recurring infections over the past year.
Children with hypogammaglobulinemia often require regular infusions of immunoglobulin.
Doctors routinely check for hypogammaglobulinemia in patients presenting with unexplained immune deficiency.

Etymology

From Greek hypo- (under) + gamma globulin (a type of protein) + -emia (relating to blood).
