po-faced

1. ADJ

/ poʊˈfeɪst / (AmE), / pəʊˈfeɪst / (BrE)
Having a humorless, serious, or disapproving expression or manner; not showing amusement.
humorless or disapproving in expression
british

Examples

Everyone at the table was joking, but Mark remained po-faced throughout dinner.
The manager's po-faced reaction made the lighthearted comment fall flat.
She spoke in a po-faced tone, taking the issue far more seriously than anyone else.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solemn - serious or grave in appearance or mood
  • Antonym: lighthearted - cheerful and carefree, showing amusement or lack of seriousness

Etymology

Origin uncertain; British colloquial, attested from the 1920s, possibly from 'po' as an abbreviation of 'poker', suggesting a rigid expression.
