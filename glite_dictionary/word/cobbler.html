cobbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːblɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒblə / (BrE)
A tall, sweetened iced drink made with wine or liquor and fruit.
sweetened iced alcoholic drink with fruit

Examples

The bartender recommended a refreshing cobbler garnished with slices of orange and lemon.
She enjoyed a sherry cobbler on a hot summer afternoon.
Many Victorian recipes for cobbler included a variety of seasonal fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cocktail - an alcoholic drink consisting of a spirit or spirits mixed with other ingredients

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑblɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒblə / (BrE)
A person whose job is to make or repair shoes.
person who repairs or makes shoes

Examples

The cobbler skillfully repaired my favorite pair of leather boots.
She took her worn-out shoes to the cobbler down the street.
Years ago, every village had its own cobbler who was known by everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoemaker - person who makes shoes

Etymology

From Middle English *cobeler*, of uncertain origin, possibly from cobble (to mend).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑblɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒblə / (BrE)
a baked fruit dessert with a thick, biscuit-like topping, typically without a bottom crust
fruit dessert with biscuit topping

Examples

She baked a delicious peach cobbler for dessert last night.
The restaurant's apple cobbler came with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.
Blueberry cobbler is a summer favorite in many Southern homes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crumble - a baked fruit dessert with a crumbly flour-butter topping

Etymology

Possibly from the archaic sense 'clumsy work' due to its rough, rustic appearance.
