mumps

1. NOUN

/ mʌmps / (AmE), / mʌmps / (BrE)
an acute infectious viral disease causing fever and swelling of the parotid glands, typically affecting children
infectious disease with swollen glands
medical

Examples

Cases of mumps have declined since the introduction of the vaccine.
The doctor diagnosed the child with mumps after noticing the swollen glands.
After recovering from mumps, she felt tired for several weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measles - an infectious disease causing fever and a red rash
  • Synonym: rubella - a contagious viral disease, also known as German measles

Etymology

Probably from obsolete Dutch *momp* (grimace), referring to facial swelling.
