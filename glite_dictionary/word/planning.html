planning

1. NOUN

/ ˈplænɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplænɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of thinking about and arranging what you intend to do in advance, especially in anticipation of possible events or needs
process of thinking about future actions

Examples

Effective planning can help reduce stress when unexpected events occur.
Her planning skills were evident in the flawless organization of the event.
Financial planning is essential for a comfortable retirement.

Synset Examples

his planning for retirement was hindered by several uncertainties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preparation - the action or process of making ready for use or consideration

Etymology

From 'plan' (French 'plan' – plot, scheme) plus the gerund suffix '-ing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplænɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplænɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making detailed arrangements or decisions about how to achieve something
the process of making plans

Examples

Planning a successful event requires attention to detail and clear communication.
Careful planning was essential for the team's efficient performance.
She enjoys the planning phase of every new project.

Synset Examples

the planning was more fun than the trip itself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preparation - the action of making ready or being made ready for use or consideration
  • Antonym: improvisation - the act of creating or performing without prior preparation

Etymology

From the verb 'plan' + -ing, first used in this sense in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01146646-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈplæn.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplæn.ɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of making detailed arrangements or decisions in advance for achieving a particular goal or completing a project.
process of making plans or arrangements

Examples

Careful planning is essential for a successful event.
He spent weeks on the planning of the new library.
Urban planning helps shape the future development of cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preparation - the action or process of making ready beforehand
  • Antonym: improvisation - the act of making or doing something with no prior planning

Etymology

From the verb 'plan' + -ing suffix, first used in the early 19th century.

Source: WordNet
