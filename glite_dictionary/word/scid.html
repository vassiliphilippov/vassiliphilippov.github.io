SCID

1. NOUN

/ ɛs si ˈaɪ di / (AmE), / ɛs si ˈaɪ di / (BrE)
a rare inherited disorder of the immune system in which severely impaired T cell function leaves sufferers extremely susceptible to infections; untreated, it is usually fatal in childhood.
rare inherited immune deficiency disease
medical

Examples

The newborn was diagnosed with SCID after showing recurrent infections and poor growth.
Due to SCID, the child's immune system could not fight off common viruses.
Early detection and treatment of SCID can greatly improve outcomes for affected infants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severe combined immunodeficiency - the full form of SCID; a rare congenital disorder harming the immune system

Etymology

Acronym of Severe Combined Immunodeficiency.
