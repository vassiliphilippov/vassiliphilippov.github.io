Andropogon gerardii

1. NOUN

/ ˌændrəˈpoʊɡɑn dʒəˈrɑrdiː / (AmE), / ˌændrəˈpəʊɡɒn dʒəˈrɑːdiː / (BrE)
A tall perennial grass with smooth, bluish leaf sheaths, grown for hay and common in prairies of the United States.
species of tall North American prairie grass

Examples

Andropogon gerardii is a dominant species in the tallgrass prairies of the Midwest.
Farmers often harvest Andropogon gerardii for high-quality hay.
Restoration projects use Andropogon gerardii to help rebuild native grassland ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: big bluestem - another common name for Andropogon gerardii

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name 'Andropogon' (from Greek 'anēr' (man) + 'pōgōn' (beard)), and species named after French botanist Louis Gérard.
