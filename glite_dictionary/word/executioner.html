executioner

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.sɪˈkju.ʃə.nɚ / (AmE), / ˌɛk.sɪˈkjuː.ʃən.ə / (BrE)
an official who carries out the death sentence on a condemned person
official who performs executions

Examples

The executioner prepared the gallows as the crowd gathered in the square.
During the Middle Ages, the executioner was both feared and shunned by the local people.
Modern justice systems no longer rely on a single executioner for capital punishment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hangman - an official who hangs condemned prisoners
  • Synonym: execution - the act of carrying out a death sentence

Etymology

From execute + -ion + -er; ultimately from Latin exsequi (to carry out, follow to the end).
