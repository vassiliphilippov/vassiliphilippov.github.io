dearie

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪri / (AmE), / ˈdɪəri / (BrE)
A term of endearment for someone you love or are fond of.
a person who is loved or cherished

Examples

Come here, dearie, and let me give you a hug.
The old woman smiled at the child and called her dearie.
Don't worry, dearie, everything will be just fine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dear - a term of endearment for a loved one

Etymology

Diminutive form of 'dear', from Old English 'dēore' (precious, valuable).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪri / (AmE), / ˈdɪəri / (BrE)
A person who is loved or regarded with affection; a term of endearment.
affectionate term for a loved person

Examples

Oh, dearie, you look so tired after your long journey.
Let me help you with your coat, my dearie.
The old shopkeeper smiled and said, "Thank you, dearie."

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darling - a person who is very dear or beloved
  • Synonym: sweetheart - a person loved by another
  • Synonym: dear - a loved or cherished person

Etymology

Diminutive form of 'dear', from Old English 'dēore' (precious, valuable, beloved).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10011405-n
Synset POS: n
