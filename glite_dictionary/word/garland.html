garland

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑɹ.lənd / (AmE), / ˈɡɑː.lənd / (BrE)
A circular band of flowers or foliage woven together and used as a decoration.
circular arrangement of flowers or leaves

Examples

A garland of roses hung above the doorway to welcome guests.
She wore a delicate garland made of wildflowers around her head.
The winner was honored with a garland at the end of the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wreath - a circular arrangement of flowers or leaves for decoration or memorial purpose

Etymology

From Old French garlande, of unknown origin; possibly related to Old Provençal garlanda.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrlənd / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːlənd / (BrE)
a collection or anthology of short literary pieces, such as poems or ballads
anthology of literary pieces

Examples

The new garland featured works by prominent modern poets.
She contributed a short story to an annual garland of children's fiction.
His essay was included in a garland celebrating regional writers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthology - a published collection of poems or other pieces of writing

Etymology

From Old French *garlande*, likely of Frankish origin; the extension to literary collections comes from the metaphor of gathering works together.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06604903-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈɡɑːrlənd / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːlənd / (BrE)
to decorate or adorn something with a garland of flowers or leaves
adorn with a garland

Examples

The villagers garland the temple every spring during the festival.
They garlanded his portrait with fresh flowers for the ceremony.
Children were busy garlanding the entrance before the guests arrived.

Synset Examples

They garlanded the statue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - to make something look more attractive by adding things to it

Etymology

From Old French 'garlande' (a wreath of flowers), of Germanic origin.
