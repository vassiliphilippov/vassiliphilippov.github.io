shakiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪkinəs / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkɪnɪs / (BrE)
a quality or state of moving or trembling in an unsteady way
unsteady or trembling motion

Examples

The shakiness of her hands made it difficult to pour the tea.
There was a noticeable shakiness in the old bridge as we walked across.
He tried to hide the shakiness in his voice during the speech.

Synset Examples

the shaking of his fingers as he lit his pipe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremor - a slight shaking movement
  • Antonym: steadiness - firm, steady motion or state

Etymology

Formed from 'shaky' + suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪkinəs / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkinɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unstable, insecure, or lacking firmness.
instability; lack of firmness

Examples

The shakiness of the ladder made me nervous as I climbed it.
Investors worried about the shakiness of the financial markets.
Her voice had a slight shakiness when she spoke in public.

Synset Examples

the shakiness of the present regime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instability - lack of stability; tendency to change or fail
  • Antonym: steadiness - the quality of being firm or stable

Etymology

Formed from 'shaky' + '-ness', where 'shaky' is from 'shake'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
