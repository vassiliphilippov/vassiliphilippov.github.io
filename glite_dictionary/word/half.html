half

1. ADJ

/ hæf / (AmE), / hɑːf / (BrE)
Partly or not fully; used before a noun to mean incomplete or not whole.
incomplete; not whole

Examples

She offered only a half apology for her mistake.
The report was just a half effort and left out many important details.
He gave a half answer, avoiding the main point of the question.

Synset Examples

gave me a half smile
he did only a half job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partial - Not complete; existing only in part
  • Antonym: whole - Complete; entire

Etymology

From Old English healf, of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. ADV

/ hæf / (AmE), / hɑːf / (BrE)
Partially; to the extent of one half.
partially, to the extent of a half

Examples

She was half asleep during the lecture and missed most of the discussion.
The painting was half finished when the artist decided to take a break.
His face was half hidden in the shadows of the room.

Synset Examples

he was half hidden by the bushes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partly - to some extent, but not completely

Etymology

From Old English 'healf' meaning 'side, part, half'.

3. NOUN

/ hæf / (AmE), / hɑːf / (BrE)
either of two equal parts into which something can be divided; one part when something is divided into two equal portions.
one of two equal parts

Examples

We cut the cake in half so everyone could share equally.
She waited for half an hour before he arrived.
One half of the document was missing after the fire.

Synset Examples

half a loaf
half an hour
a century and one half

Related Terms

  • Antonym: whole - something complete, not divided; all of something

Etymology

From Old English 'healf', of Germanic origin.

5. ADJ

/ hæf / (AmE), / hɑːf / (BrE)
(of brothers, sisters, etc.) related by one parent only, not both.
sharing only one parent (as in siblings)

Examples

Although they have different fathers, Tim and Jake are half brothers.
She found out she had a half sister living in another city.
Even though they are half siblings, they were raised together and are very close.

Synset Examples

a half brother
half sister

Related Terms

  • Antonym: full - having both parents in common (as in full siblings)

Etymology

From Old English 'healf', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'half' and German 'halb'.

6. NOUN

/ hæf / (AmE), / hɑːf / (BrE)
Either of the two equal periods into which some games or performances are divided, separated by an interval.
one of two periods in a game or show

Examples

The team scored twice in the first half of the match.
There will be a short break between each half of the performance.
The players returned to the field for the second half after the interval.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quarter - one of four equal periods in a game, especially in some sports

Etymology

Old English 'healf', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'half' and German 'halb'.
