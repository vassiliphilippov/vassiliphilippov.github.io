profitably

1. ADV

/ ˈprɑːfɪtəbli / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪtəbli / (BrE)
in a way that produces profit or advantage; so as to yield financial or other benefits
in a way that yields profit or benefit

Examples

The company has operated profitably since its merger with the larger firm.
She invested her inheritance profitably in a range of new technology companies.
If managed profitably, the small farm could support an entire family.

Synset Examples

they worked together productively for two years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: productively - in a way that produces results or is effective

Etymology

Formed from 'profitable' + '-ly', from Old French *profitable* and Latin *proficere* (to make progress).
