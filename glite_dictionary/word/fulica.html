Fulica

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːlɪkə / (AmE), / ˈfjuːlɪkə / (BrE)
A genus of birds known as coots, which are medium-sized water birds of the rail family, found worldwide.
genus of birds, coots

Examples

The genus Fulica includes several species of coots distributed throughout the world.
Ornithologists studied the habits of Fulica in marshland habitats.
The American coot, known scientifically as Fulica americana, is commonly found in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coot - a medium-sized water bird of the genus Fulica

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Latin fulica (coot).
