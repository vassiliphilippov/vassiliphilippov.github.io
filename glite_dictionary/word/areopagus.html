Areopagus

1. PROPN

/ ˌɛəriˈɑpəɡəs / (AmE), / ˌeərɪˈɒpəɡəs / (BrE)
the highest governmental council in ancient Athens, which later became a principal judicial court
high council and court of ancient Athens

Examples

The Areopagus played a crucial role in maintaining justice in ancient Athens.
After his speech at the Areopagus, Paul gained both followers and critics.
The authority of the Areopagus shifted over centuries, evolving from a council to a judicial body.

Etymology

From Greek Areios pagos, literally 'hill of Ares', where the council met.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæriˈɑːpəɡəs / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈɒpəɡəs / (BrE)
a hill in Athens, west of the Acropolis, that was the meeting place of the highest governmental council and later a judicial court in ancient Athens
hill and council site in ancient Athens

Examples

The ancient council met on the Areopagus to decide matters of justice and governance.
Visitors to Athens can still see the remains of the Areopagus today.
Philosophers like Socrates once defended themselves before the court of the Areopagus.

Etymology

From Greek Areios pagos, meaning 'hill of Ares.'
