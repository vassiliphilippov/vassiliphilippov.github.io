macro

1. ADJ

/ ˈmækˌroʊ / (AmE), / ˈmæk.rəʊ / (BrE)
Very large in scale, scope, or capability; relating to the overall or most general aspects of something.
large-scale or broad in scope

Examples

The company needed to take a macro approach to address the industry-wide challenges.
Her research analyzes environmental problems at both the micro and macro levels.
A macro perspective can help leaders understand the broader trends affecting their business.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: micro - extremely small in scale or scope

Etymology

From Greek makros (long, large, great).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01391862-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈmækroʊ / (AmE), / ˈmakrəʊ / (BrE)
a single instruction in a computer program that automatically expands to carry out a set of instructions
computer command that expands into a set of instructions

Examples

He created a macro to automate repetitive spreadsheet tasks at work.
You can assign a macro to this button to speed up the formatting process.
The software allows users to record and edit macros for more efficient workflows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: script - a set of commands that automate tasks in a computer program

Etymology

Shortened from 'macroinstruction', from Greek 'makros' (long, large), referring to its function of representing several instructions.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06599062-n
Synset POS: n
