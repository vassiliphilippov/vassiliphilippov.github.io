Cucumis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːkəmɪs / (AmE), / ˈkjuːkəmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the gourd family that includes cucumbers and muskmelons.
genus of cucumbers and muskmelons

Examples

The Cucumis genus comprises both cucumbers and several types of melons.
Researchers are studying disease resistance in Cucumis species.
Many fruits in the Cucumis group are cultivated for consumption worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cucumber - a long, green edible fruit of Cucumis sativus
  • Synonym: melon - edible fruit of various plants in the genus Cucumis

Etymology

From Latin *cucumis* (cucumber).
