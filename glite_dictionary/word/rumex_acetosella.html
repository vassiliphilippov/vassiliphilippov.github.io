Rumex acetosella

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːmɛks əˌsɛtəˈsɛlə / (AmE), / ˈruːmɛks əˌsiːtəˈsiːlə / (BrE)
A small Eurasian plant with acid-tasting arrow-shaped leaves, commonly found in dry areas.
a plant with acid-tasting leaves

Examples

The leaves of Rumex acetosella add a tangy flavor to salads and soups.
Rumex acetosella often thrives in sandy, well-drained soils where few other plants grow.
Wildlife, especially rabbits, are known to feed on the leaves of Rumex acetosella.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheep's sorrel - common name for Rumex acetosella
  • Degree Higher: sorrel - any plant of the genus Rumex with acidic leaves

Etymology

From Latin: *Rumex* (the plant genus), *acetosella* (diminutive of 'acetosa', sour).
