outrageously

1. ADV

/ ˌaʊtˈreɪdʒəsli / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈreɪdʒəsli / (BrE)
to an extremely excessive, shocking, or extravagant degree
to an excessive or shocking degree

Examples

The hotel charged an outrageously high price for room service.
She dressed outrageously for the costume party, turning every head in the room.
It was outrageously unfair to blame him for something he didn't do.

Synset Examples

atrociously expensive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extremely - to a very great degree
  • Antonym: moderately - to a limited or average degree

Etymology

From 'outrageous' + '-ly', where 'outrageous' derives from Old French 'outrage' (excess, insult).

2. ADV

/ aʊtˈreɪdʒəsli / (AmE), / aʊtˈreɪdʒəsli / (BrE)
In a very shocking, offensive, or extreme manner.
in a shocking or offensive manner

Examples

She outrageously insulted the entire committee during the meeting.
The prices at that restaurant are outrageously high for such mediocre food.
He outrageously claimed credit for work he didn't do.

Synset Examples

he behaved outrageously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shockingly - in a shocking manner
  • Degree Lower: mildly - in a gentle or moderate manner

Etymology

From 'outrageous' + -ly, ultimately from Old French 'outrage' (excess, insult) and the suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00118415-r
Synset POS: r
