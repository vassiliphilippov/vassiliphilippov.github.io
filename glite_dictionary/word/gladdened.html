to gladdened

1. VERB

/ ˈɡlædən̩d / (AmE), / ˈɡlædnd / (BrE)
made joyful; filled with gladness or pleasure.
made joyful; filled with gladness

Examples

She was gladdened by the unexpected arrival of her old friends.
The news of their success gladdened everyone in the office.
His heart was gladdened by the cheerful atmosphere at the festival.

Synset Examples

the sun and the wind on his back made him feel exhilarated — happy to be alive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gladden - to make someone glad or joyful
  • Antonym: to sadden - to make or become sad

Etymology

From the verb 'gladden', derived from Old English 'glæd' (bright, cheerful), influenced by 'glad'.
