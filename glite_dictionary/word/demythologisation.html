demythologisation

1. NOUN

/ diːˌmɪθəˌlɑdʒɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌmɪθəˌlɒdʒɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the reinterpretation of myths or religious messages in rational or non-supernatural terms
reinterpretation of myths rationally

Examples

The theologian argued that demythologisation is necessary to make ancient scriptures relevant to modern audiences.
Many scholars debate the limits and value of demythologisation in interpreting sacred texts.
His approach to demythologisation sought to remove supernatural elements from the traditional narrative.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demythologization - the American English spelling of 'demythologisation'

Etymology

Formed from de- + mythologise + -ation; modelled after German Entmythologisierung (from Rudolf Bultmann's theological work).
