bassarisk

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæsəˌrɪsk / (AmE), / ˈbasərɪsk / (BrE)
A raccoon-like mammal native to Mexico and the southwestern United States, with a long bushy tail marked by black and white rings.
raccoon-like mammal of North America

Examples

The bassarisk is well known for its agility and nocturnal habits.
We spotted a bassarisk while hiking through the canyon in Arizona.
A bassarisk may use its distinctive tail rings to communicate with others in its territory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ringtail - another common name for the bassarisk, a small carnivorous mammal native to North America

Etymology

From New Latin *Bassariscus*, the genus name, derived from Greek *bassaris* (fox) with a Latin diminutive suffix.
