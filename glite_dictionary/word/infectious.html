infectious

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfɛkʃəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛkʃəs / (BrE)
relating to or caused by infection, especially by microorganisms such as bacteria or viruses; able to be transmitted to others through infection.
caused by or relating to infection

Examples

The doctor explained that the flu is an infectious disease caused by a virus.
Patients with infectious conditions are treated in separate hospital wards.
Proper handwashing can help prevent the spread of infectious illnesses.

Synset Examples

infectious hospital
infectious disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contagious - able to be transmitted from one person to another, especially through direct contact

Etymology

From Medieval Latin infectiosus, from Latin infectio (infection).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02843955-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfɛkʃəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛkʃəs / (BrE)
(Of a disease or agent) caused by infection or capable of causing infection or transmitting disease.
able to cause or spread infection

Examples

The flu is highly infectious and can spread rapidly in schools.
Some bacteria are not infectious to humans.
Hepatitis B is an infectious disease transmitted through blood and bodily fluids.

Synset Examples

viruses and other infective agents
a carrier remains infective without himself showing signs of the disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contagious - (of a disease) spread by direct or indirect contact
  • Antonym: noninfectious - (of a disease or agent) not capable of causing infection

Etymology

From Medieval Latin infectious, from Latin infectus (to stain, dye, infect).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02122026-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfɛkʃəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛkʃəs / (BrE)
(of a disease or emotion) able to be passed easily from one person to another by infection or through influence.
easily spread; contagious

Examples

His enthusiasm was so infectious that everyone joined in the project with excitement.
Laughter can be infectious in a room full of friends.
The flu is highly infectious and spreads quickly in crowded places.

Synset Examples

fear is exceedingly infectious: children catch it from their elders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contagious - (of a disease) able to be transmitted, especially by direct contact
  • Antonym: noninfectious - not able to be transmitted by infection

Etymology

From Medieval Latin infectiosus, from Latin infectio (infection).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01305746-a
Synset POS: a
