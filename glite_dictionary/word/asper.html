asper

1. NOUN

/ ˈæspər / (AmE), / ˈæspə / (BrE)
a former silver coin and monetary unit used in Turkey and the Ottoman Empire, equal to one twentieth of a kurus.
old Turkish monetary unit
archaic

Examples

The merchant demanded payment in gold or aspers.
Historians discovered several aspers buried in the ancient market site.
Before the adoption of modern currency, the asper was widely used throughout the Ottoman Empire.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin asprum, from Turkish akçe (white coin), influenced by Old French aspre (rough coin).
