Epipaleolithic

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛp.i.pæ.li.oʊˈlɪθ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛp.i.pæl.i.əˈlɪθ.ɪk / (BrE)
The middle part of the Stone Age, dating from about 15,000 years ago, marked by the transition from hunting and gathering to more settled forms of living.
middle part of the Stone Age

Examples

Archaeologists discovered Epipaleolithic tools at the ancient site.
The Epipaleolithic period saw important advances in human culture and technology.
New evidence has redefined the boundaries of the Epipaleolithic in the Levant.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Paleolithic - the early phase of the Stone Age
  • Degree Higher: Mesolithic - the later part of the Stone Age following the Epipaleolithic

Etymology

From Greek 'epi-' (upon, after) + 'Paleolithic'.
