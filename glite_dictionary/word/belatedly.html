belatedly

1. ADV

/ bɪˈleɪtɪdli / (AmE), / bɪˈleɪtɪdli / (BrE)
Later than expected or usual; after the appropriate or expected time.
later than expected or usual

Examples

He belatedly realized he had forgotten her birthday and sent an apology.
The announcement was belatedly posted on the company's website.
She belatedly finished her assignment after the deadline had passed.

Synset Examples

the train arrived late
we awoke late
the children came late to school
notice came so tardily that we almost missed the deadline
I belatedly wished her a happy birthday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: late - after the expected, proper, or usual time
  • Antonym: promptly - at the expected or proper time; on time

Etymology

From 'belated' (late or delayed), formed from 'be-' + 'late'.
