authoritarian

1. ADJ

/ əˌθɔr.əˈtɛr.i.ən / (AmE), / ɔːˌθɒr.ɪˈteə.ri.ən / (BrE)
expecting or demanding strict obedience to authority, often at the expense of personal freedom
demanding strict obedience

Examples

The authoritarian manager expected his staff to follow every order without question.
Her authoritarian teaching style left little room for student creativity or input.
They grew up in a highly authoritarian household where speaking out was discouraged.

Synset Examples

the timid child of authoritarian parents
insufferably overbearing behavior toward the waiter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictatorial - having characteristics of a dictator; expecting total obedience
  • Antonym: permissive - allowing much freedom; not strict or controlling

Etymology

From authority + -arian; ultimately from Latin 'auctoritas' (authority).

2. NOUN

/ əˌθɔr.əˈtɛr.i.ən / (AmE), / ɔːˌθɒr.ɪˈteə.ri.ən / (BrE)
a person who favors or enforces strict obedience to authority, especially that of the government, at the expense of personal freedom; a tyrannical or dictatorial person
person enforcing strict obedience to authority

Examples

The company's new manager is an authoritarian who expects total compliance from all employees.
Many people resisted the authoritarian who tried to suppress free speech in the town.
She accused her father of being an authoritarian whenever he laid down strict house rules.

Synset Examples

my boss is a dictator who makes everyone work overtime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictator - a ruler with total power, typically one who has obtained power by force
  • Antonym: democrat - a person who supports democracy or democratic forms of government

Etymology

From authority + -arian. The adjective is earlier (19th c.); the noun is a later extension.

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ əˌθɔr.əˈtɛr.i.ən / (AmE), / ɔːˌθɒr.ɪˈteə.ri.ən / (BrE)
Favouring or enforcing strict obedience to authority, often at the expense of personal freedom; characteristic of an absolute ruler or government.
enforcing strict obedience to authority

Examples

The country was governed by an authoritarian regime for over two decades.
His authoritarian style left little room for dissent among employees.
Many people fled the authoritarian government in search of freedom.

Synset Examples

an authoritarian regime
autocratic government
despotic rulers
a dictatorial rule that lasted for the duration of the war
a tyrannical government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autocratic - relating to a ruler with absolute power
  • Antonym: democratic - relating to a government by the people

Etymology

From authority + -an, from Latin auctoritas (authority).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00721128-s
Synset POS: s
