Prometheus

1. PROPN

/ prəˈmiːθiəs / (AmE), / prəˈmiːθiəs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a Titan who stole fire from the gods and gave it to humans, for which he was punished by Zeus.
Titan who gave fire to humans in Greek myth

Examples

The story of Prometheus is often seen as a symbol of defiance and progress.
Artists through the centuries have found inspiration in the myth of Prometheus.
Some consider Prometheus a hero for giving humanity knowledge and fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titan - one of the primordial deities in Greek mythology
  • Antonym: Zeus - the king of the gods in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Προμηθεύς (Promētheús), meaning 'forethought'.
