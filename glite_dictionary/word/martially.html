martially

1. ADV

/ ˈmɑːrʃəli / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃəli / (BrE)
In a manner characteristic of or suitable for war, soldiers, or military life.
in a military or warlike manner

Examples

He marched martially across the parade ground, head held high and back straight.
The general spoke martially to his troops before the battle, inspiring them with his commanding tone.
Flags waved martially in the wind as the soldiers prepared for the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: martial - relating to war or military life

Etymology

From 'martial' + '-ly', with 'martial' from Latin 'martialis' (of Mars, god of war).
