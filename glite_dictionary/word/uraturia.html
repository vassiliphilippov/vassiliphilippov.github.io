uraturia

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊrəˈtʊriə / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəˈtjʊəriə / (BrE)
The presence of abnormally large amounts of uric acid in the urine, often associated with conditions such as gout.
excess uric acid in urine (medical)
medical

Examples

The diagnosis of uraturia can indicate an increased risk of developing gout.
Routine tests showed he had uraturia after complaining of joint pain.
Doctors monitor uraturia in patients prone to kidney stones and metabolic disorders.

Etymology

From New Latin 'ura-' (urine) + Greek 'ouron' (urine) + '-uria' (condition of the urine).
