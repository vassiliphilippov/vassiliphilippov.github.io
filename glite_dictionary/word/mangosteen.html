mangosteen

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæŋɡəˌstin / (AmE), / ˈmaŋɡəstiːn / (BrE)
a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, known for its thick, leathery leaves and sweet, edible purple fruit
tropical tree and fruit from Southeast Asia

Examples

The mangosteen is often called the 'queen of fruits' for its delicious, sweet flavor.
They bought several ripe mangosteens at the market during their trip to Thailand.
A mature mangosteen tree can bear hundreds of fruit every year.

Etymology

From Malay *manggis*, perhaps via Dutch *mangistan*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæŋɡəˌstiːn / (AmE), / ˈmæŋɡəstiːn / (BrE)
A round, tropical fruit with thick purple rind and juicy, sweet white flesh, often said to taste like a blend of peach and pineapple.
tropical fruit with sweet, juicy flesh

Examples

A single mangosteen contains juicy segments with a delicate, sweet flavor.
Many travelers to Southeast Asia seek out fresh mangosteen in local markets.
The chef topped the dessert with slices of ripe mangosteen for a tropical touch.

Etymology

From Malay 'manggis' via Portuguese 'mangostão'.
