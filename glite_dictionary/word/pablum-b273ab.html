pablum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæbləm / (AmE), / ˈpabləm / (BrE)
ideas, writing, or statements that are bland, simplistic, and lacking in intellectual substance or originality; worthless or oversimplified ideas.
worthless or oversimplified ideas

Examples

The report was criticized as mere pablum that failed to offer real solutions.
His speech amounted to little more than political pablum for the masses.
Many viewers found the new show was just entertainment pablum with nothing thought-provoking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drivel - silly or senseless talk or ideas
  • Synonym: banality - something that lacks originality or is boring

Etymology

Originally a trademark of a baby food product, from Latin 'pabulum' (food, fodder).
