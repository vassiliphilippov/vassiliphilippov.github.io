centering

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛntərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of focusing attention or energy on a particular object, activity, or thought
the act of focusing attention or energy

Examples

Meditation often begins with the centering of one's thoughts and calming the mind.
Athletes use centering techniques to prepare mentally before competitions.
Her centering on positive goals helped her overcome many challenges.

Synset Examples

the focus of activity shifted to molecular biology
he had no direction in his life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - the center of interest or activity; concentrated attention

Etymology

From the verb 'center' + -ing, meaning to focus or direct toward a central point.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛntərɪŋ / (BrE)
In American football, the act of putting the ball into play by snapping it between the center's legs to a back, usually the quarterback.
putting football in play by snapping
american

Examples

The team's new center practiced centering every afternoon to ensure accurate snaps.
A bad centering can disrupt the entire offensive play in football.
He was penalized for an illegal centering during the first quarter.

Synset Examples

the quarterback fumbled the snap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap - the act of quickly passing the football from the center to the quarterback
  • Synonym: hiking - another term for centering, passing the football to a back

Etymology

From the verb 'center' + -ing; in football, refers to the position and action of the 'center' player.

Synset POS: n
