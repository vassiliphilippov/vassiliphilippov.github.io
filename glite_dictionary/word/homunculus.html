homunculus

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈmʌŋkjələs / (AmE), / hɒˈmʌŋkjʊləs / (BrE)
a tiny, fully formed human supposed to be present in sperm or eggs according to the outdated theory of preformation in biology
tiny fully formed human, in old theory

Examples

In the 17th century, some scientists believed that a homunculus existed inside every sperm cell.
The idea of the homunculus was eventually replaced by more accurate theories of reproduction.
A homunculus was once depicted in anatomical illustrations as a miniature person curled up in a cell.

Etymology

From Latin 'homunculus', diminutive of 'homo' (man).

2. NOUN

/ hoʊˈmʌŋkjələs / (AmE), / həˈmʌŋkjʊləs / (BrE)
a very small human or a miniature person, typically one who is otherwise normal in appearance
very small human or person

Examples

In the ancient tale, an alchemist claims to have created a homunculus in his laboratory.
The play features a mysterious homunculus who observes the world from behind the scenes.
She referred to her tiny figurine as a homunculus, marveling at its lifelike details.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dwarf - a person of unusually small stature, often due to a medical condition
  • Antonym: giant - a person of unusually great size or stature

Etymology

From Latin homunculus, diminutive of homo (man).
