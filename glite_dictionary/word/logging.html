logging

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or business of cutting down trees for timber
cutting down trees for timber

Examples

Logging in the rainforest has led to significant habitat loss for many species.
New laws were introduced to regulate commercial logging operations.
The company specializes in sustainable logging to protect the environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deforestation - the removal of a large area of trees

Etymology

From the verb 'log' (to cut down trees for timber) plus '-ing'.
