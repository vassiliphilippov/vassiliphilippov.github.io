vigor

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡə / (BrE)
a quality in writing or art marked by imagination, liveliness, and intensity of style
lively, imaginative style in writing or art

Examples

The author infuses her prose with such vigor that every page feels electric.
His speech was praised for its vigor and originality.
Critics admired the vigor of her writing, noting how it brought the story to life.

Synset Examples

his writing conveys great energy
a remarkable muscularity of style

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energy - vigorous and lively quality, especially in writing or art
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of lively or imaginative style in creative works

Etymology

From Latin vigor (liveliness, activity), via Old French vigueur.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡə / (BrE)
physical or mental strength, energy, or force; active power or intensity.
physical or mental energy, strength

Examples

She tackled the new project with remarkable vigor and enthusiasm.
Daily exercise can help you maintain your vigor as you age.
The campaign was conducted with much vigor and determination.

Synset Examples

he plays tennis with great energy
he's full of zip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energy - the strength and vitality required for sustained activity
  • Synonym: vitality - the state of being strong and active; energy
  • Antonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin 'vigor' (liveliness, activity), from 'vigēre' (to be lively, flourish).

3. NOUN

/ ˈvɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡə / (BrE)
Physical or mental energy, strength, or force; active power or vitality.
Physical or mental strength; vitality

Examples

The elderly man surprised everyone with his vigor during the hike.
She tackled each new project with impressive vigor and determination.
Regular exercise can help restore youthful vigor to both body and mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energy - The capacity for vigorous activity; lively and forceful exertion of power
  • Synonym: vitality - Physical or mental liveliness; the capacity to live and grow
  • Antonym: lethargy - A lack of energy and enthusiasm; sluggishness

Etymology

From Latin 'vigor' meaning liveliness or activity.
