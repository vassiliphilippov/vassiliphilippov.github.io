Begonia heracleifolia

1. NOUN

/ bəˈɡoʊniə həˌrækliəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / bɪˈɡəʊniə hɛˌrækliəˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A species of rhizomatous begonia with pointed, star-shaped lobed leaves and pink flowers, native to Mexico.
species of begonia with star-shaped leaves

Examples

The Begonia heracleifolia is admired for its unique, star-shaped leaves and delicate pink flowers.
She grows Begonia heracleifolia in her greenhouse because of its ornamental foliage.
Gardeners recommend Begonia heracleifolia for shady spots due to its attractive appearance and easy care.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: begonia - a genus of flowering plants in the family Begoniaceae

Etymology

From Latin; 'Begonia' named after Michel Bégon, French patron of botany; 'heracleifolia' from 'Heracleum' (hogweed) + 'folia' (leaves), referring to Heracleum-like leaves.
