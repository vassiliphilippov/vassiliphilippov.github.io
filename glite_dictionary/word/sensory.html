sensory

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛn.sə.ri / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sər.i / (BrE)
relating to sensation or the physical senses; transmitted or perceived by the senses.
relating to the physical senses

Examples

The painting provides a rich sensory experience, engaging sight, touch, and even smell.
Our sensory nerves help us detect heat, cold, pain, and other stimuli.
The chef created a sensory menu designed to stimulate all five senses.

Synset Examples

sensory experience
sensory channels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensual - relating to physical, especially sexual, pleasure
  • Antonym: motor - relating to movement (opposite of sensory, in physiology)

Etymology

From Latin 'sensorius', from 'sensus' (sense).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛn.sə.ri / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.səri / (BrE)
(of nerve fibers or impulses) carrying signals from sensory organs and tissues toward the central nervous system.
relating to nerves carrying signals toward the brain
medical

Examples

The sensory neurons transmit signals from the skin to the spinal cord.
Certain injuries can damage sensory nerve fibers, affecting the sense of touch.
Research focuses on how sensory information is processed by the brain.

Synset Examples

sensory neurons

Related Terms

  • Antonym: motor - (of a nerve fiber or impulse) carrying signals away from the central nervous system to muscles or glands

Etymology

From Latin sensorius (of sensation), from sentire (to feel).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00334509-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɛn.sə.ri / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.səri / (BrE)
relating to sensation or the physical senses; transmitted or perceived by the senses.
relating to the senses or sensation

Examples

The sensory cortex plays a crucial role in processing touch and other bodily sensations.
Bright colors and loud sounds can sometimes overwhelm a child's sensory system.
Many animals have specialized sensory organs for detecting heat, light, or movement.

Synset Examples

the sensory cortex
sensory organs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptual - relating to the ability to perceive or the process of perception
  • Antonym: motor - relating to movement rather than sensation

Etymology

From Latin sensorius (of feeling), from sensus (sense).
