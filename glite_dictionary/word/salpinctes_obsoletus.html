Salpinctes obsoletus

1. NOUN

/ sælˈpɪŋktiːz ˌɑbsoʊˈlɛtəs / (AmE), / sælˈpɪŋktiːz ˌɒbsəˈliːtəs / (BrE)
A species of wren commonly known as the rock wren, native to the badlands and mesa regions of western United States and Mexico.
species of wren (rock wren) in western North America

Examples

The Salpinctes obsoletus is often observed hopping among rocks in arid regions.
Birdwatchers in Arizona frequently spot Salpinctes obsoletus nesting in crevices of the mesas.
The call of Salpinctes obsoletus echoes through canyons during the early morning hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rock wren - common name for Salpinctes obsoletus

Etymology

From Greek 'salpinktēs' (trumpeter) and Latin 'obsoletus' (worn out).
