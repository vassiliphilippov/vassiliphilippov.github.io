nester

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛstər / (AmE), / ˈnɛstə / (BrE)
a bird that has built or is building a nest
bird that builds a nest

Examples

Every spring, the oak tree becomes home to several nesters.
The documentary captured the behavior of the nester as it gathered twigs and leaves.
Scientists observed a rare nester constructing its home in the wetland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brooder - a bird sitting on eggs to hatch them

Etymology

From nest + -er, denoting one who nests.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɛstər / (AmE), / ˈnɛstə / (BrE)
a person who settles lawfully on government land with the intent to acquire title to it
person settling on government land for ownership

Examples

The homestead laws allowed a nester to settle on public land and eventually gain ownership.
Many nesters built cabins and started farms on the western frontier.
As a nester, she hoped her claim would soon become permanent.

Etymology

From 'nest' (home or dwelling place) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
