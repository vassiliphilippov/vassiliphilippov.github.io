brigade

1. NOUN

/ brɪˈɡeɪd / (AmE), / brɪˈɡeɪd / (BrE)
an army unit that is smaller than a division and typically consists of several battalions
army unit smaller than a division

Examples

The 5th Infantry brigade was deployed to the border in response to the crisis.
Each brigade is made up of several battalions commanded by a brigadier.
After the ceremony, the brigade marched proudly past the reviewing stand.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: division - a large military unit normally consisting of several brigades
  • Degree Lower: battalion - a military unit typically composing part of a brigade

Etymology

From French 'brigade', based on Italian 'brigata' (company, group).

2. VERB

/ brɪˈɡeɪd / (AmE), / brɪˈɡeɪd / (BrE)
to form people or things into a brigade; to unite or organize as a brigade
organize into a brigade

Examples

The commander decided to brigade the new recruits for more efficient training.
During the emergency response, volunteers were quickly brigaded to tackle different tasks.
The event organizers brigaded the participants by region to improve coordination.

Etymology

From French 'brigade', from Italian 'brigata' (company, crew, troop).
