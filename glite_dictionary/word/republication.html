republication

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːˌpʌblɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌpʌblɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of publishing something again, especially a book, article, or other work that was previously published
the act of publishing again

Examples

The republication of the classic novel introduced it to a new generation of readers.
After several years out of print, the republication delighted fans and scholars alike.
She hoped the republication of her research would spark renewed interest in the subject.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: publication - the act of making something publicly known or available, particularly through print or broadcast

Etymology

From re- (again) + publication.

2. NOUN

/ ˌriːˌpʌblɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌpʌblɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
an act of publishing something again, or something that has been published again, especially a new edition of a literary or other work
the act or result of publishing again

Examples

The republication of the classic novel introduced it to a new generation of readers.
Due to popular demand, the magazine announced the republication of its special issue.
He wrote a new foreword for the republication of his earlier work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reissue - a new issue or edition of a printed work

Etymology

From re- (again) + publication, meaning the act of publishing again.
