shield

1. NOUN

/ ʃild / (AmE), / ʃiːld / (BrE)
the hard protective outer covering of certain animals, such as arthropods and turtles
hard protective animal covering

Examples

The turtle's shield protects it from most predators in the wild.
Some insects have a tough shield called an exoskeleton that helps them survive harsh environments.
Biologists examined the shield of the arthropod under a microscope for structural differences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carapace - hard upper shell of turtles, crustaceans, and some other animals

Etymology

From Old English *scield* (shield, protector).

2. NOUN

/ ʃild / (AmE), / ʃiːld / (BrE)
A broad piece of armor carried on the arm as a protection against blows or missiles.
protective armor for intercepting blows

Examples

The knight raised his shield just in time to deflect the enemy's sword.
Ancient warriors often decorated their shields with symbols representing their clans.
She studied the museum exhibit showcasing a medieval shield made of iron and wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buckler - a small round shield held by a handle or worn on the forearm

Etymology

From Old English *scield*, related to Old Norse *skjöldr*, German *Schild* (shield).

3. VERB

/ ʃild / (AmE), / ʃiːld / (BrE)
to protect someone or something from danger, harm, or unpleasant situations
protect from harm or danger

Examples

The umbrella helped shield us from the heavy rain during the parade.
She used her hand to shield her eyes from the bright sunlight.
The thick walls shield residents from the noise outside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to protect - keep safe from harm or injury
  • Antonym: to expose - leave unprotected or reveal to danger

Etymology

From Old English *scieldan* (to protect) related to the noun 'shield'.

4. NOUN

/ ʃild / (AmE), / ʃiːld / (BrE)
a broad piece of metal, wood, or another suitable material, held by hand or worn on the arm, used as a protection against blows or missiles; more generally, any protective covering or structure
protective covering or structure

Examples

The soldiers raised their shields to protect themselves from the arrows.
A sunscreen acts as a shield against harmful UV rays.
Emergency workers erected a temporary shield around the damaged reactor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrier - a structure that blocks or protects
  • Synonym: armor - metal covering worn for protection
  • Antonym: to expose - leave unprotected or without cover

Etymology

From Old English 'scield', related to Old Norse 'skjǫldr', German 'Schild', all meaning 'shield'.
