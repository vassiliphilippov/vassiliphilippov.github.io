teacup

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈtiːkʌp / (BrE)
An amount equal to the quantity that a teacup can contain, used as a unit of measure in cooking and other contexts.
amount a teacup holds

Examples

Add a teacup of sugar to the mixture and stir well.
He poured half a teacup of milk into the batter.
The recipe calls for one teacup of chopped nuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cup - unit of liquid measurement equal to 8 fluid ounces (US)

Etymology

From 'tea' and 'cup', referring to a cup for drinking tea.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtiː.kʌp / (AmE), / ˈtiː.kʌp / (BrE)
A small cup with a handle, typically made of china or porcelain, used for drinking tea.
cup used for drinking tea

Examples

She poured her tea carefully into the delicate teacup.
He accidentally chipped the edge of his favorite teacup.
The elegant set came with matching saucers and a floral teacup.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mug - a larger cup, often without a saucer, used for hot drinks
  • Synonym: saucer - a small shallow dish for holding a cup

Etymology

From 'tea' + 'cup'; first recorded in English in the 18th century when tea drinking became widespread.
