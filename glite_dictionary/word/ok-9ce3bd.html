OK

1. ADV

/ ˌoʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
In a satisfactory or adequate manner; well enough but not excellently.
satisfactorily; adequately
informal

Examples

She managed the presentation OK, even though she was nervous.
I slept OK last night, but I could have used a couple more hours.
The team played OK in the first half, but struggled after halftime.

Synset Examples

she'll do okay on her own
held up all right under pressure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: well - in a good or satisfactory way
  • Antonym: badly - in a poor or unsatisfactory way

Etymology

Originated as an abbreviation of 'oll korrect,' a deliberate misspelling of 'all correct' in 19th-century American English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00015933-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ oʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / əʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
used to show agreement or acceptance, often at the beginning of a sentence.
expression of agreement

Examples

OK, I will see you tomorrow at 8 o'clock.
OK, let's get started with today's meeting.
OK, I understand what you mean now.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all right - used to express agreement or understanding
  • Antonym: no - word used to express refusal or disagreement

Etymology

Probably from Choctaw *okeh* (it is so), popularized in American English in the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00053542-r
Synset POS: r

3. NOUN

/ ˌoʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
An official approval or authorization to proceed with something.
approval or authorization
informal american

Examples

Before we start the project, we need the manager's OK.
She gave her OK for the team to proceed with the plan.
The construction can't begin without the city's OK.

Synset Examples

they gave us the O.K. to go ahead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approval - the act of agreeing with or accepting something
  • Synonym: permission - the official consent to do something
  • Antonym: refusal - the act of saying no or rejecting a request

Etymology

Originally a humorous abbreviation of 'oll korrect' (a deliberate misspelling of 'all correct') from 19th-century American English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06700446-n
Synset POS: n

4. ADJ

/ ˌoʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
acceptable or satisfactory; in good enough condition or adequate for a purpose.
satisfactory; acceptable

Examples

The project plan looks OK to me, but let's check it once more before finalizing.
After a few minor repairs, the car was OK for another road trip.
I wasn't feeling great this morning, but I'm OK now.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acceptable - satisfactory; good enough to be approved
  • Synonym: adequate - sufficient for a specific requirement; satisfactory
  • Degree Higher: excellent - extremely good; outstanding
  • Antonym: unsatisfactory - not good enough; not meeting requirements

Etymology

From the abbreviation of 'oll korrect', a humorous alteration of 'all correct' popularized in the US in the 19th century.
