Peripatopsidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛr.ɪ.pəˈtɑp.sɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌpɛr.ɪ.pəˈtɒp.sɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of velvet worms (Onychophora) found primarily in the Southern Hemisphere.
a family of velvet worms

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the Peripatopsidae family in Tasmania.
Members of the Peripatopsidae are known for their unique method of locomotion.
The Peripatopsidae are distinguished from other velvet worms by their reproductive strategies.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Peripatopsis.
