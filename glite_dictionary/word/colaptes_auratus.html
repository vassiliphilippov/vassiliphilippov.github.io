Colaptes auratus

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈlæptiz ɔːˈreɪtəs / (AmE), / kəʊˈlæptiːz ɔːˈreɪtəs / (BrE)
A large woodpecker species of eastern North America, commonly called the northern flicker, with a red neck and yellow undersides to the wings and tail.
a North American woodpecker species

Examples

The Colaptes auratus is often spotted foraging for ants on lawns.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of Colaptes auratus across the continent.
The distinctive yellow feathers help birders identify Colaptes auratus in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern flicker - common name for Colaptes auratus, a North American woodpecker

Etymology

From Latin: 'Colaptes' meaning chiseler (woodpecker genus), and 'auratus' meaning golden.
