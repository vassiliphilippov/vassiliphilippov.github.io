to remilitarize

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈmɪlɪtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˌriːˈmɪlɪtəraɪz / (BrE)
to supply (a country, region, or organization) with military forces or equipment again, especially after having been demilitarized
supply with military force again

Examples

Following years of peace, the government decided to remilitarize the border region.
Some politicians argued that there was no need to remilitarize after decades of disarmament.
International observers expressed concern when the country began to remilitarize its coastal defenses.

Synset Examples

Should Japan be remilitarized?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to demilitarize - to remove military forces from (an area)

Etymology

From re- (again) + militarize, after militarize from French 'militariser' and Latin 'militaris'.
