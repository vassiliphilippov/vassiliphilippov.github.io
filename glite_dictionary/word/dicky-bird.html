dicky-bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪki bɝd / (AmE), / ˈdɪki bɜːd / (BrE)
a small bird; used especially by adults when talking to children
small bird (child-directed term)
british

Examples

The children pointed at the dicky-bird singing in the garden.
Grandma told us a story about a clever dicky-bird who built its nest in her hat.
He asked his toddler if she could spot a dicky-bird in the tree.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bird - a feathered, winged animal that lays eggs

Etymology

Diminutive of "dick" used as a generic name for birds; origin likely in British nursery speech.
