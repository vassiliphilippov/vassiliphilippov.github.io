overgrown

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈɡroʊn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈɡrəʊn / (BrE)
(of land or a place) covered with plants that have grown in an uncontrolled way, usually unwanted or excessive vegetation.
covered with uncontrolled plant growth

Examples

The abandoned garden was completely overgrown with weeds and vines.
An overgrown path made it difficult to reach the cabin after years of neglect.
The ruins stood hidden in an overgrown forest, almost invisible to passersby.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lush - (of vegetation) growing luxuriantly and abundantly
  • Antonym: bare - (of land) having no vegetation or very little vegetation

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessive) + 'grown', past participle of 'grow'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈɡroʊn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈɡrəʊn / (BrE)
(of a place) covered with plants that have grown in an uncontrolled or excessive way.
covered in uncontrolled plant growth

Examples

The abandoned garden was completely overgrown with ivy and weeds.
A narrow, overgrown path led into the dense forest.
After years of neglect, the playground became overgrown and unusable.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lush - (of vegetation) growing luxuriantly
  • Antonym: bare - (of land) lacking vegetation

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' + 'grown', past participle of 'grow'.
