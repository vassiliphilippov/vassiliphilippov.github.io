irritation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪrɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
(Pathology) Abnormal sensitivity or reaction in a body part when exposed to stimulation, often causing discomfort or inflammation.
abnormal sensitivity to stimulation (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient complained of irritation in her throat after exposure to dust.
Any foreign substance may cause irritation of the skin in sensitive individuals.
He experienced gastric irritation after eating spicy foods.

Synset Examples

any food produced irritation of the stomach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inflammation - Swelling and redness of body tissue as a reaction to injury or infection

Etymology

From Latin irritatio, from irritare (to provoke, excite).
