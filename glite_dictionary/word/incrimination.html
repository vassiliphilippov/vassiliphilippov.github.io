incrimination

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌkrɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌkrɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of accusing someone of being responsible for a wrongdoing or crime
accusation of wrongdoing

Examples

Her sudden incrimination of the manager surprised everyone in the meeting.
The testimony contained enough evidence to lead to his incrimination.
False incrimination can ruin an innocent person's reputation.

Synset Examples

his incrimination was based on my testimony
the police laid the blame on the driver

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accusation - a statement charging someone with a fault or crime
  • Antonym: exoneration - the act of officially absolving someone from blame

Etymology

From Latin 'incriminatio', from 'incriminare' (to accuse of a crime), from 'in-' (into) + 'crimen' (crime).
