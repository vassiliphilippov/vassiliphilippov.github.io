to decelerate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈsɛləˌreɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛləreɪt / (BrE)
to reduce speed; to move or cause to move more slowly
reduce speed; slow down

Examples

Drivers should decelerate as they approach the school zone.
The train began to decelerate after leaving the tunnel.
Suddenly, the plane started decelerating before landing.

Synset Examples

The car decelerated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to accelerate - to increase speed; move more quickly

Etymology

From Latin 'de-' (down) + 'celerare' (to hasten), from 'celer' (swift).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈsɛləˌreɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛləˌreɪt / (BrE)
to reduce speed; to move, make, or become slower
reduce speed or slow down

Examples

The car began to decelerate as it approached the traffic signal.
When cycling downhill, he had to decelerate to avoid going too fast.
Pilots must decelerate the aircraft before landing.

Synset Examples

He slowed down the car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slow down - to reduce speed
  • Antonym: to accelerate - to increase speed

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix de- (opposite of) to accelerate.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00439450-v
Synset POS: v
