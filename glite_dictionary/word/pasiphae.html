Pasiphae

1. PROPN

/ pəˈsɪfəˌi / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfəˌi / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the daughter of Helios and the wife of King Minos, known as the mother of Ariadne and Phaedra.
mythical daughter of Helios; mother of Ariadne

Examples

According to legend, Pasiphae was cursed by Poseidon for her husband Minos's disobedience.
The story of Pasiphae and the Minotaur is a central myth from ancient Crete.
As the daughter of the sun god Helios, Pasiphae was known for her magical abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ariadne - daughter of Pasiphae and Minos in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Minos - husband of Pasiphae in Greek mythology, king of Crete

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Πασιφάη (Pasipháē), meaning 'all-shining'.
