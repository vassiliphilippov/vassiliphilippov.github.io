ski

1. NOUN

/ ski / (AmE), / ski / (BrE)
each of a pair of long, narrow pieces of wood, metal, or plastic worn on the feet for gliding over snow.
long runner for gliding on snow

Examples

He strapped on his skis and headed for the slopes.
The ski is designed to glide smoothly over powdery snow.
All of her ski equipment fit into the back of the truck.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: snowboard - a flat board used for gliding on snow, ridden sideways

Etymology

From Norwegian 'ski', from Old Norse 'skíð' (stick of wood).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04235116-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ski / (AmE), / ski / (BrE)
to move or travel over snow or water on skis, usually for sport or recreation
move or travel on skis

Examples

She learned to ski when she was just five years old.
We plan to ski in the Alps this winter holiday.
He has skied every major slope in North America.

Synset Examples

We love to ski the Rockies
My children don't ski

Related Terms

  • Antonym: snowboard - descend a slope on a snowboard
  • Synonym: skate - move on ice using skates

Etymology

From Norwegian 'ski', meaning 'stick of wood; ski'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01942285-v
Synset POS: v
