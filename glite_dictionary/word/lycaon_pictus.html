Lycaon pictus

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈkeɪ.ɑn ˈpɪk.təs / (AmE), / laɪˈkeɪ.ən ˈpɪk.təs / (BrE)
a large, powerful wild canid native to southern and eastern Africa, known for its mottled coat and cooperative pack hunting
African wild dog species

Examples

The population of Lycaon pictus has declined due to habitat loss and conflict with humans.
Researchers observed a pack of Lycaon pictus coordinating their hunt across the savanna.
Unlike other canids, Lycaon pictus is known for its strong social bonds within the pack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African wild dog - common name for Lycaon pictus, an African canid

Etymology

From Greek 'lykaon' (wolf) and Latin 'pictus' (painted), referring to its wolf-like form and patterned coat.
