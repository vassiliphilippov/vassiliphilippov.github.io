Smilodon californicus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmaɪləˌdɑn ˌkæləˈfɔrnɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈsmɪlədɒn ˌkalɪˈfɔːnɪkəs / (BrE)
an extinct species of saber-toothed cat native to North America, known for its large, curved canine teeth; sometimes identified as a subspecies of Smilodon fatalis
extinct North American saber-toothed cat

Examples

Fossils of Smilodon californicus have been discovered in the tar pits of Los Angeles.
The massive canines of Smilodon californicus made it one of the most recognizable prehistoric predators.
Scientists believe that Smilodon californicus hunted large mammals such as bison and camels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Smilodon fatalis - a closely related species of saber-toothed cat from North America
  • Synonym: saber-toothed cat - extinct group of predatory cats with long, curved canine teeth

Etymology

From genus name 'Smilodon' (Greek smilē 'chisel' + odous 'tooth'), with species epithet 'californicus' indicating origin from California.
