to relocate

1. VERB

/ ˌriˈloʊ.keɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈləʊ.keɪt / (BrE)
to move something or someone to a new place; to establish in a new location
move to a new location

Examples

The company decided to relocate its headquarters to a larger city.
After the flood, many families were forced to relocate to safer areas.
She plans to relocate to Europe next year for her new job.

Synset Examples

We had to relocate the office because the rent was too high

Related Terms

  • Synonym: move - to change position or place

Etymology

From re- (again) + locate (from Latin locus, place).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00415334-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌriːˈloʊ.keɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈləʊ.keɪt / (BrE)
to become established in a new location, especially after moving from another place
to become established in a new location

Examples

After graduating, she decided to relocate to New York City for her new job.
Many companies relocate their headquarters to save on operating costs.
The family relocated from Chicago to Denver last year.

Synset Examples

Our company relocated to the Midwest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: move - to change one's home or place of residence

Etymology

From re- (again) + locate, ultimately from Latin locus (place).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00415552-v
Synset POS: v
