endomorphy

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.dəˈmɔr.fi / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəˈmɔː.fi / (BrE)
The quality or state of having a round, soft, and heavy body build, typically with a higher proportion of body fat.
body type: round and heavy

Examples

His natural endomorphy made losing weight challenging despite regular exercise.
Researchers studied the correlation between endomorphy and susceptibility to certain health conditions.
Athletes with pronounced endomorphy often excel in sports where strength and weight are advantages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mesomorphy - the quality of having a muscular or athletic body build
  • Antonym: ectomorphy - the quality of having a slender or lean body build

Etymology

From 'endo-' (internal) and Greek 'morphē' (form, shape).
