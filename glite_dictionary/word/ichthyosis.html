ichthyosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪkθiˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɪkθiˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
Any of a group of congenital skin disorders in which the skin is dry, thickened, and scaly, resembling fish scales.
a group of skin disorders with dry, scaly skin
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with ichthyosis after examining her dry, scaly skin.
Patients with ichthyosis often require regular moisturizing treatments.
Researchers are studying genetic mutations that cause different types of ichthyosis.

Etymology

From Greek ichthys (fish) + -osis (condition).
