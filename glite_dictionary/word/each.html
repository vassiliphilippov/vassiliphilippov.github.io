each

1. DET

/ itʃ / (AmE), / iːtʃ / (BrE)
used to refer to every one of two or more people or things, regarded and identified separately.
every one individually (quantifier)

Examples

Each student received a certificate for completing the course.
Please take one apple each from the basket.
Each of the rooms has a spectacular view of the city.

Synset Examples

each person is mortal
each party is welcome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: every - all considered as a group, without exception
  • Synonym: all - the whole quantity or extent of something

Etymology

From Old English ælc (every, each), from Proto-Germanic *aikalīkaz.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02277394-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADV

/ itʃ / (AmE), / iːtʃ / (BrE)
For or to every one of two or more people or things considered separately.
to or from every one considered individually

Examples

The children were given a slice of cake each.
They paid thirty dollars each for the tickets.
We were handed two gifts each at the ceremony.

Synset Examples

they received $10 each

Related Terms

  • Synonym: every - all individuals or things in a group without exception

Etymology

From Old English ælc (each, every, either), from Proto-Germanic *aihwalīkaz.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00241635-r
Synset POS: r
