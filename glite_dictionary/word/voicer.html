voicer

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɔɪsər / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪsə / (BrE)
a person who adjusts and regulates the tone of organ pipes to achieve the desired sound quality
one who regulates organ pipe tone

Examples

The voicer spent hours perfecting the sound of each pipe in the church organ.
Hiring an experienced voicer is crucial when restoring a historic pipe organ.
As a skilled voicer, she could make even old pipes sing beautifully.

Etymology

From 'voice' + '-er', denoting a person who gives voice or regulates sound.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɔɪsər / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪsə / (BrE)
a person who expresses or voices an opinion publicly
person who voices an opinion

Examples

As a prominent voicer in the debate, she shaped the conversation on climate policy.
The meeting encouraged every voicer to share their concerns about the new policy.
He became known as an outspoken voicer for social justice in his community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speaker - a person who speaks or makes a speech

Etymology

Formed from 'voice' + agent noun suffix '-er'.
