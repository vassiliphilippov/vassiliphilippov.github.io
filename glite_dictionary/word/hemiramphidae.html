Hemiramphidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmɪˈræmfɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌhiːmɪˈræmfɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine and freshwater fishes known as halfbeaks, closely related to flying fishes but lacking the ability to glide.
family of halfbeak fishes

Examples

Scientists observed several species of Hemiramphidae in the coastal waters during their research.
The distinctive elongated lower jaw is a key feature of the Hemiramphidae family.
Unlike their relatives, the flying fishes, members of Hemiramphidae cannot glide above the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halfbeak - a fish with an elongated lower jaw, member of the Hemiramphidae family
  • Degree Higher: flying fish - a family of fishes related to Hemiramphidae that are able to glide above water

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek hemi- (half) and rhamphos (beak).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛmɪˌræmˈfaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌhɛmɪˌræmˈfiːdɪi / (BrE)
A family of marine and freshwater fishes, known as halfbeaks, closely related to flying fishes but unable to glide.
family of fishes called halfbeaks

Examples

The Hemiramphidae are distinguished by their elongated lower jaws.
Members of the Hemiramphidae are often found in both marine and freshwater environments.
Unlike flying fishes, Hemiramphidae cannot glide above the surface of the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Exocoetidae - family of flying fishes

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek hēmi- (half) and rhamphos (beak), referring to the characteristic jaw structure.
