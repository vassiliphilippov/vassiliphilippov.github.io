Calvary

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæl.və.ri / (AmE), / ˈkæl.vər.i / (BrE)
the hill near Jerusalem where, according to the New Testament, Jesus was crucified
hill near Jerusalem where Jesus was crucified

Examples

Many pilgrims travel to Calvary during Easter to honor its religious significance.
The story of the crucifixion takes place on Calvary according to the Christian tradition.
Some churches have paintings or sculptures that depict the scene at Calvary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Golgotha - Aramaic name for the hill where Jesus was crucified

Etymology

From Latin *Calvaria* (skull), translating Greek *Kranion*, from Aramaic *Golgotha*.
