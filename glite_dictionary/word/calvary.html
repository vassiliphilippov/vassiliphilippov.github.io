calvary

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.vɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈkæl.vəri / (BrE)
An experience of intense suffering or agony, especially regarded as a severe ordeal or trial.
an experience of intense suffering

Examples

After the accident, his recovery became a true calvary that tested his willpower.
Many refugees described their journey as a calvary filled with hardship and loss.
Living through unemployment and debt for years was a calvary for the entire family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ordeal - a painful or horrific experience, especially a prolonged one
  • Synonym: torment - extreme mental or physical suffering

Etymology

From Latin *Calvaria* (skull), referring to Calvary, the hill outside Jerusalem where Jesus was crucified.
