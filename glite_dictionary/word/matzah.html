matzah

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːtsə / (AmE), / ˈmatsə / (BrE)
A thin, brittle flatbread made from flour and water, traditionally eaten by Jews during Passover.
unleavened bread eaten at Passover

Examples

During Passover, we eat matzah instead of regular bread to remember the exodus from Egypt.
She served chicken soup with pieces of matzah floating on top.
The recipe calls for crushed matzah as a gluten-free alternative to breadcrumbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matzo - alternative spelling of matzah

Etymology

From Hebrew מַצָּה (matzah), meaning 'unleavened bread.'
