to archaise

1. VERB

/ ɑːrˈkeɪ.aɪz / (AmE), / ˈɑː.keɪ.aɪz / (BrE)
to give something an archaic appearance or character
make appear archaic

Examples

The architect attempted to archaise the design by using traditional materials and motifs.
The author chose to archaise his language, giving the novel an old-fashioned tone.
Historians sometimes archaise their writing to better evoke a particular era.

Synset Examples

archaized craftwork

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to modernize - to make something more modern

Etymology

From archaic, based on Greek arkhaïzein (to imitate the ancients) + English -ise.
