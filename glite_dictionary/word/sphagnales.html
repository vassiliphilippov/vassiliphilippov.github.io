Sphagnales

1. NOUN

/ sfæɡˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / sfæɡˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of mosses including the genus Sphagnum, sometimes placed in its own subclass in botanical classifications
order of mosses including Sphagnum

Examples

Botanists classify peat moss in the Sphagnales order due to its unique characteristics.
The Sphagnales play a crucial role in wetland ecosystems by forming peat bogs.
Advances in genetic studies have influenced the classification of Sphagnales within mosses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sphagnum - a genus of mosses known as peat moss

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Sphagnum with the taxonomic suffix -ales indicating an order.
