jaggedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæɡɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒæɡɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or condition of having a rough or uneven edge or surface, characterized by sharp points or irregular projections.
state of being jagged or uneven

Examples

The jaggedness of the broken glass made it dangerous to touch.
The mountain's jaggedness stood out against the soft evening sky.
Artists often use texture and jaggedness to add drama to their work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of being smooth or even

Etymology

From 'jagged' + '-ness', a suffix forming nouns indicating a state or quality.
