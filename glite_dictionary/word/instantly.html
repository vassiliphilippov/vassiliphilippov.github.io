instantly

1. ADV

/ ˈɪnstəntli / (AmE), / ˈɪnstəntli / (BrE)
without any delay; at once.
at once; immediately

Examples

He pressed the button and the machine stopped instantly.
She replied to the message instantly, surprising everyone with her speed.
The pain disappeared instantly after he took the medication.

Synset Examples

he answered immediately
found an answer straightaway
an official accused of dishonesty should be suspended forthwith
Come here now!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediately - without delay; straight away
  • Synonym: right away - immediately; without delay
  • Antonym: eventually - in the end, especially after a long delay

Etymology

From Latin 'instanter' (urgently, at once), from 'instans' (present, urgent).

2. ADV

/ ˈɪn.stənt.li / (AmE), / ˈɪn.stənt.li / (BrE)
Without any delay; at once.
immediately, without delay

Examples

He left the room instantly when he heard the news.
The answer came to her instantly as if by magic.
Please call me instantly if there are any problems.

Synset Examples

he was killed outright

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediately - without any delay; at once
  • Antonym: eventually - in the end; after a period of time

Etymology

From Latin 'instantaneus' (immediate, urgent), via Old French 'instantané'.
