charlatan

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑrlətən / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːlətən / (BrE)
a person who falsely claims to have special skills or knowledge, often in order to deceive others
person who pretends to have skills or knowledge

Examples

The self-proclaimed healer was eventually exposed as a charlatan who took advantage of desperate people.
Many investors lost money after trusting the promises of a financial charlatan.
In the story, the traveling charlatan wowed crowds with his elaborate tricks and tales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impostor - one who pretends to be someone else in order to deceive
  • Synonym: fraud - a person intending to deceive others, typically for personal gain
  • Synonym: quack - a person who pretends to have medical knowledge or skills
  • Antonym: expert - a person who is very knowledgeable or skillful in a particular area

Etymology

From Italian ciarlatano (quack), from ciarlare (to prattle, babble).
