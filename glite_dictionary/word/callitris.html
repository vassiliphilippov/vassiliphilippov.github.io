Callitris

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælɪtrɪs / (AmE), / ˈkælɪtrɪs / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen coniferous trees or shrubs, commonly known as cypress pines, native to Australia and New Caledonia
genus of evergreen coniferous trees (cypress pines)

Examples

The dry forests of Australia are dominated by Callitris species, known for their drought resistance.
Botanists discovered a new type of Callitris growing near the riverbanks.
Timber from Callitris trees is valued for its durability and pleasant scent.

Etymology

From Greek kallos (beauty) + itris (tree), referring to the attractive appearance of the foliage.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈtraɪs / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈtraɪs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen, monoecious coniferous trees or shrubs, commonly known as cypress pines, native to Australia and nearby regions.
genus of cypress pines

Examples

The Callitris genus is native to Australia and is well adapted to arid environments.
Scientists studied the growth patterns of several Callitris species in the outback.
Many Callitris trees are valued for their durable timber and attractive foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress pine - common name for several species within Callitris

Etymology

From Greek *kallos* (beauty) + *trisos* (threefold), referring to the arrangement of leaves or cones.
