weal

1. NOUN

/ wil / (AmE), / wiːl / (BrE)
a red, swollen mark left on the skin, especially after being struck or as a reaction to an allergen
raised mark on the skin

Examples

After being hit with the stick, a bright weal appeared on his leg.
She developed a weal on her arm as a result of an allergic rash.
The insect bite left a painful red weal on his neck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: welt - a raised mark on the skin, typically from a blow or allergic reaction

Etymology

From Old English 'wǣl' (wound, sore, blister).
