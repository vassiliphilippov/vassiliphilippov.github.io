Curia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjʊriə / (AmE), / ˈkjʊəriə / (BrE)
the central administrative body, especially of the Roman Catholic Church, responsible for its governance and organization
central administration of the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

The Curia plays a crucial role in implementing papal decisions within the Church.
Debates about reform often focus on the structure and function of the Curia.
He spent several years working in the Curia before becoming a bishop.

Etymology

From Latin 'curia' (court, assembly, senate).
