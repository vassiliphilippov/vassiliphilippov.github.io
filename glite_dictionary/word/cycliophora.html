Cycliophora

1. NOUN

/ saɪkliˈɑfəˌrə / (AmE), / saɪkliˈɒfəˌrə / (BrE)
A phylum of tiny marine organisms that live symbiotically on the mouthparts of lobsters, first identified in 1995.
tiny marine phylum found on lobsters

Examples

Scientists discovered Cycliophora living exclusively on the mouthparts of certain lobster species.
The unique characteristics of Cycliophora have intrigued marine biologists since their identification in the 1990s.
Research on Cycliophora has helped clarify evolutionary relationships among marine invertebrates.

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklos' (circle) + 'phoros' (bearing), referring to the circular shape and structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪ.kliˈɑː.fə.rə / (AmE), / ˌsɪk.liˈɒfə.rə / (BrE)
a phylum of tiny marine microorganisms, first identified in 1995, that are typically found on the mouthparts of lobsters and are notable for their unique biology
a phylum of tiny marine organisms found on lobsters

Examples

Scientists discovered Cycliophora living exclusively on the mouthparts of certain lobsters.
The unique reproductive cycle of Cycliophora sets it apart from other microscopic marine phyla.
Specimens of Cycliophora were first identified in the waters off Denmark in the mid-1990s.

Etymology

From Greek kyklos (circle) and phora (bearing), referring to the organisms' circular mouthparts.
