fertility

1. NOUN

/ fərˈtɪləti / (AmE), / fəˈtɪləti / (BrE)
The ability to produce offspring or new growth; the quality of being fertile.
the ability to produce offspring or crops

Examples

Recent advances in medicine have improved fertility treatments for many couples.
The fertility of the soil allowed the farmers to grow abundant crops.
Researchers are studying how environmental factors affect animal fertility.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infertility - the inability to conceive children or young
  • Synonym: fecundity - the ability to produce an abundance of offspring or new growth

Etymology

From Latin 'fertilis' (fruitful) + '-ity'.

2. NOUN

/ fərˈtɪləti / (AmE), / fəˈtɪləti / (BrE)
the ability of soil or land to produce abundant and vigorous growth of plants
ability of soil to support abundant growth

Examples

The fertility of the valley made it an ideal place for farmers to settle.
Years of overfarming reduced the fertility of the region's once-rich soil.
Careful crop rotation helps maintain the fertility of agricultural land.

Synset Examples

he praised the richness of the soil
weeds lovely in their rankness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infertility - the inability of soil or land to support growth
  • Synonym: richness - the quality of being rich in nutrients for plant growth

Etymology

From Latin 'fertilitas' (fruitfulness), from 'fertilis' (fruitful).

3. NOUN

/ fɚˈtɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / fəˈtɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the rate at which live births occur in a population, usually measured as the number of live births per 1000 people per year
birth rate in a population

Examples

Recent economic changes have led to a decline in the fertility of many countries.
The government launched new programs to increase rural fertility rates.
Comparing the fertility of different regions helps demographers understand population trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birth rate - the number of live births per 1000 people per year
  • Antonym: mortality - the incidence of death in a population

Etymology

From Latin fertilitas (fruitfulness, fertility), from fertilis (fertile).
