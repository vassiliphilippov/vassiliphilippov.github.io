Quercus michauxii

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɛrkəs mɪˈʃoʊiː / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs mɪˈʃɔːiː / (BrE)
A medium to large deciduous oak tree native to moist areas of the southeastern United States, also known as the swamp chestnut oak.
species of oak tree native to southeastern US

Examples

The Quercus michauxii is often found in the wetlands of the southeastern United States.
Many wildlife species rely on the acorns produced by Quercus michauxii for food.
Foresters recommend planting Quercus michauxii to help restore native oak habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basket oak - another common name for Quercus michauxii

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Quercus' (oak) + species named after French botanist André Michaux.
