long-suffering

1. ADJ

/ lɔŋˈsʌfərɪŋ / (AmE), / lɒŋˈsʌfərɪŋ / (BrE)
patiently enduring continual pain, trouble, or mistreatment without complaint
patiently enduring pain or trouble

Examples

Despite years of setbacks, she remained long-suffering and hopeful.
He admired his long-suffering friend, who never spoke a word of complaint.
The long-suffering teacher listened calmly as her students argued yet again.

Synset Examples

an enduring disposition
a long-suffering and uncomplaining wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patient - showing tolerance of delays or suffering
  • Antonym: impatient - unable to wait or endure pain without complaint

Etymology

Formed in English from 'long' + 'suffering', dating from the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ lɔŋˈsʌfərɪŋ / (AmE), / lɒŋˈsʌfərɪŋ / (BrE)
Patient and enduring tolerance of pain, hardship, or unhappiness over a lengthy period.
patient endurance of pain or hardship

Examples

Despite years of hardship, her long-suffering was a source of inspiration for her family.
The refugees' long-suffering was evident in their weary faces.
His long-suffering finally paid off when he achieved his lifelong dream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patience - the capacity to accept or tolerate delay, trouble, or suffering

Etymology

Formed from 'long' + 'suffering', meaning enduring suffering for a long time.
