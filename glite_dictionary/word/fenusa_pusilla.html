Fenusa pusilla

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnuːsə pjuːˈsɪlə / (AmE), / fɛˈnjuːsə pjuːˈsɪlə / (BrE)
A small black sawfly native to Europe, now established in eastern United States, whose larvae mine birch leaves and can cause significant defoliation.
species of sawfly affecting birch trees

Examples

The infestation of Fenusa pusilla led to widespread defoliation of the birch grove last summer.
Scientists identified the larvae damaging the trees as those of Fenusa pusilla.
Management strategies are being developed to control Fenusa pusilla populations in urban parks.

Etymology

From Latin: Genus name 'Fenusa' and species epithet 'pusilla' meaning 'tiny' or 'insignificant'.
