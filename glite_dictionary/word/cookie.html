cookie

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʊki / (AmE), / ˈkʊki / (BrE)
a cook, especially one working on a ranch or at a camp
ranch or camp cook
informal american

Examples

The cookie made breakfast for everyone before sunrise at the cattle ranch.
After a long day on the trail, the hungry cowhands gathered around the fire where the cookie was cooking stew.
They relied on the cookie not just for food but for keeping the camp cheerful.

Etymology

Probably from 'cook' plus the diminutive suffix '-ie', used in American English regional slang.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʊki / (AmE), / ˈkʊki / (BrE)
A small piece of data stored on a user's computer by a website to remember information about the user, such as login status or preferences.
data file stored by a website

Examples

When you visit the site, a cookie is saved to your browser to keep you logged in.
Some websites require you to accept a cookie before you can access their content.
Clearing your cookies can help protect your privacy online.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: session - period during which a user interacts with a website or software

Etymology

Sense adapted from 'cookie' (biscuit), referring playfully to a 'treat' given by a website; computing usage dates to mid-1990s.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkʊki / (AmE), / ˈkʊki / (BrE)
A small, flat, sweet cake, usually containing flour, sugar, and some type of fat, often with additional ingredients such as chocolate chips or nuts.
small, flat sweet cake

Examples

I baked a batch of chocolate chip cookies for the party.
He dunked his cookie in a glass of milk before eating it.
The bakery had a delicious selection of cookies on display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biscuit - A small, typically sweet baked good; chiefly British use

Etymology

From Dutch 'koekje' meaning 'little cake'.
