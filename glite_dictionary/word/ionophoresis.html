ionophoresis

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪoʊnəfəˈrisɪs / (AmE), / ˌaɪənəfəˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
the movement of charged particles in a colloid under the influence of an electric field, where positive ions move toward the cathode and negative ions toward the anode
motion of ions in an electric field

Examples

Scientists used ionophoresis to separate the charged particles in the laboratory experiment.
The process of ionophoresis is important in various analytical chemistry techniques.
Research into ionophoresis has led to improved methods for purifying colloidal suspensions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrophoresis - the movement of suspended particles through a fluid under the influence of an electric field

Etymology

From Greek 'ion' (going, a particle) + 'phoresis' (carrying).
