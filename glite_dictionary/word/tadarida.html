Tadarida

1. NOUN

/ təˈdærɪdə / (AmE), / təˈdɑːrɪdə / (BrE)
A genus of free-tailed bats found in warm regions around the world.
genus of free-tailed bats

Examples

Many species in the genus Tadarida are known for their remarkable speed and endurance in flight.
The Tadarida bats are often found roosting in caves and hollow trees.
Research on Tadarida has improved our understanding of echolocation in mammals.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Tadarida, the genus name introduced in zoological nomenclature.
