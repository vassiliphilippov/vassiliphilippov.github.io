bitterweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtərˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈbɪtəˌwiːd / (BrE)
a European weed with spiny tongue-shaped leaves and yellow flowers, now also found in the United States
European weed with yellow flowers

Examples

The field was covered in bitterweed after a wet spring.
Gardeners often struggle to remove bitterweed because of its spiny leaves.
Some livestock avoid grazing where bitterweed is abundant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪt̬ɚˌwid / (AmE), / ˈbɪtəˌwiːd / (BrE)
Any of several weedy plants, primarily found in North America, of the genus Ambrosia, known for producing allergenic pollen that causes hay fever and asthma.
a North American weed producing allergenic pollen

Examples

Many allergy sufferers dread the annual blooming of bitterweed in the late summer.
The farmer tried to clear bitterweed from his fields to reduce hay fever symptoms in the community.
Scientists have identified bitterweed as a major source of allergenic pollen in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ragweed - any of several weedy plants of the genus Ambrosia

Etymology

From 'bitter' + 'weed', likely referring to the plant's taste and weedy nature.
