sesquipedalia

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛskwɪpɪˈdeɪliə / (AmE), / ˌsɛskwɪpɪˈdeɪlɪə / (BrE)
very long words, especially those that are unusually or unnecessarily long
very long words

Examples

He enjoyed using sesquipedalia to impress his friends with his vast vocabulary.
Writers sometimes avoid sesquipedalia to keep their prose clear and accessible.
The English language has many curious sesquipedalia that appear only in technical texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polysyllabic - having many syllables
  • Antonym: concise - expressing much in few words

Etymology

From Latin 'sesquipedalis' (a foot and a half long).
