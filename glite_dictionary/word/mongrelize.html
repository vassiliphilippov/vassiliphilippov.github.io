to mongrelize

1. VERB

/ ˈmʌŋɡrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋɡrəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something into a mongrel, typically by crossbreeding or mixing breeds or types, often implying loss of purity
make into a mongrel by mixing breeds or types

Examples

Some breeders refuse to mongrelize their purebred dogs.
Critics argue that careless crossbreeding can mongrelize distinct species.
The garden variety was mongrelized by mixing imported plants with local ones.

Synset Examples

mongrelized dogs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hybridize - to cross-breed different species or varieties

Etymology

From 'mongrel' (a dog of mixed breed) + '-ize' (forming verbs meaning to make or become).

2. VERB

/ ˈmʌŋɡrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋɡrɪˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to combine or mix different elements, often methods or strategies, especially to achieve diversity or improvement.
mix or combine elements, especially methods

Examples

To solve the problem, the team decided to mongrelize their approach by blending traditional and innovative techniques.
Some educators believe that to engage students, you must mongrelize the standard curriculum with creative methods.
The company succeeded after it chose to mongrelize its approach to marketing, mixing digital and traditional strategies.

Synset Examples

We should mongrelize our approach to attract more viewers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hybridize - to produce a hybrid; to combine different things to form something new
  • Antonym: to purify - to remove mixed elements and make pure

Etymology

From 'mongrel' (mixed breed) + '-ize' (verb-forming suffix).
