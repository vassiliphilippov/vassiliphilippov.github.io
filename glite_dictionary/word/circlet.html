circlet

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːklɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːklɪt / (BrE)
a small ornamental band or ring, especially one made of metal and worn around the head as a decoration
decorative metal band for the head

Examples

The princess wore a delicate circlet of silver leaves on her wedding day.
Medieval queens often adorned their heads with a jeweled circlet during formal ceremonies.
He crafted a golden circlet that fit perfectly on the performer's brow.

Etymology

From Old French 'circlet', diminutive of 'circle', from Latin 'circulus' (circle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrklət / (AmE), / ˈsɜːklɪt / (BrE)
a small circle or ring, especially one forming part of a decorative design or worn as an ornament
a small circle or decorative ring

Examples

She wore a delicate circlet of flowers around her head at the festival.
Each guest at the banquet received a silver circlet as a token.
The ancient coins had a small circlet engraved in the center.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: circle - a round plane figure with every point equidistant from the center

Etymology

From Old French *cerclet*, diminutive of *cercle* (circle), from Latin *circulus* (small ring).
