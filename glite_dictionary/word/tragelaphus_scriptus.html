Tragelaphus scriptus

1. NOUN

/ træɡəˈlæfəs ˈskrɪptəs / (AmE), / trəˈɡiːləfəs ˈskrɪptəs / (BrE)
A species of antelope found in sub-Saharan Africa, known for white body markings resembling a harness and twisted horns.
African antelope with white body markings

Examples

The Tragelaphus scriptus is often observed near water sources in dense forests.
Researchers studied the horn patterns of Tragelaphus scriptus across different regions.
Unlike many antelopes, the Tragelaphus scriptus is highly adaptable to both woodland and savannah habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bushbuck - Common name for Tragelaphus scriptus, an antelope species

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Tragelaphus' from Greek 'tragelaphos' (goat-stag), species 'scriptus' meaning 'written/marked'.
