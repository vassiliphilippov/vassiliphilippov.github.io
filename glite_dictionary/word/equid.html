equid

1. NOUN

/ ˈekwɪd / (AmE), / ˈiːkwɪd / (BrE)
A mammal of the family Equidae, including horses, donkeys, and zebras, characterized by slender legs and a mane on the neck.
hoofed mammal of the horse family

Examples

The African savanna is home to several species of equid, including zebras and wild asses.
Scientists are studying the evolutionary history of the equid family.
Fossil evidence shows that the equid lineage dates back millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse - large domesticated equid with a flowing mane and tail, used for riding

Etymology

From Latin 'equus' (horse) via scientific Latin 'equidae'.
