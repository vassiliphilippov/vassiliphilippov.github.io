gender

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛndər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛndə / (BrE)
(Grammar) A category of nouns, pronouns, and adjectives in some languages, governing agreement based on classifications such as masculine, feminine, or neuter.
grammatical category for agreement in languages

Examples

In French, every noun has a gender that determines the form of its articles and adjectives.
The gender of the word 'table' in German is feminine, even though it refers to an inanimate object.
Students often struggle to remember the correct gender for nouns in Spanish.

Etymology

From Latin genus (kind, sort, gender).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛndə / (BrE)
The state of being male or female, especially in relation to biological or reproductive differences.
biological distinction between sexes

Examples

Some animals cannot be identified by gender until they are fully grown.
The study included participants of every gender.
Medical records often ask for the patient's gender at birth.

Synset Examples

she didn't want to know the sex of the foetus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sex - the biological distinction between male and female

Etymology

From Old French 'gendre', from Latin 'genus' (kind, sort, type).
