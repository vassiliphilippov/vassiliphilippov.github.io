zootoxin

1. NOUN

/ ˈzuː.toʊ.tɑːksɪn / (AmE), / ˈzuː.təʊ.tɒksɪn / (BrE)
a toxin produced by animals, especially one resembling bacterial toxins in its antigenic properties
toxin produced by animals
medical

Examples

The bite of the snake delivers a zootoxin that can cause severe tissue damage.
Researchers are studying the zootoxin produced by jellyfish for its potential medical uses.
Symptoms appear quickly after exposure to the zootoxin found in certain amphibians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: venom - toxic substance produced by animals, especially for defense or hunting
  • Antonym: bacterial toxin - toxin produced by bacteria

Etymology

From Greek 'zōion' (animal) and 'toxin' (poison).
