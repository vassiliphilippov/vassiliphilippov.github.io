picturesqueness

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪktʃəˈrɛsknəs / (AmE), / ˌpɪktʃəˈrɛsknɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being visually attractive or strikingly expressive, especially in a way that resembles a picture
quality of being visually attractive or vivid

Examples

The picturesqueness of the little village made it a favorite destination for artists.
Everyone admired the picturesqueness of the mountain landscape at sunset.
He was captivated by the picturesqueness of her vivid descriptions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: picturesque - visually attractive, like a picture

Etymology

Formed from 'picturesque' (from French pittoresque, from Italian pittoresco) + English suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɪktʃəˈrɛsknəs / (AmE), / ˌpɪktʃəˈrɛsknɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being visually attractive, especially in a way that resembles a picture; charm or appeal due to pleasing appearance
charmingly attractive quality

Examples

We admired the picturesqueness of the old village along the riverbank.
The picturesqueness of the mountain landscape drew many photographers to the area.
She tried to capture the picturesqueness of the scene in her watercolor painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: picturesque - visually attractive, like a picture

Etymology

From 'picturesque' + '-ness'. 'Picturesque' from French 'pittoresque', from Italian 'pittoresco', from 'pittore' (painter).
