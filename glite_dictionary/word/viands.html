viands

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪəndz / (AmE), / ˈvaɪəndz / (BrE)
various types of food, especially prepared food for a meal
various kinds of food

Examples

The table was laden with a variety of viands prepared for the festival.
They stocked the pantry with enough viands to last through the winter.
At the banquet, guests were invited to sample the many delicious viands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fare - food provided for consumption
  • Synonym: provisions - supplies of food, especially for a journey

Etymology

From Old French 'viande' (food), from Latin 'vivenda' (things to live on), plural of 'vivendum'.
