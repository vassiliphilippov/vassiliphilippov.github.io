enanthem

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈænθəm / (AmE), / ɛnˈænθəm / (BrE)
a rash or eruption on a mucous membrane, such as inside the mouth, typically as a symptom of disease
eruption on a mucous membrane (medical)
medical

Examples

The child developed an enanthem in her mouth during the viral infection.
An enanthem is often seen in patients with measles or hand, foot, and mouth disease.
Doctors identified a characteristic enanthem on the patient's palate, aiding the diagnosis.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: exanthem - a skin eruption or rash occurring as a symptom of disease

Etymology

From Greek en- (in) + anthos (flower, blossom), referring to a rash or eruption inside the body.
