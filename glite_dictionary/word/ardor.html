ardor

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrdər / (AmE), / ˈɑːdə / (BrE)
intense enthusiasm, passion, or zeal for something
intense passion or enthusiasm

Examples

She pursued her studies with great ardor and determination.
His speech was filled with the ardor of youthful optimism.
Despite the setbacks, her ardor for the project never waned.

Synset Examples

he spoke with great ardor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passion - strong and barely controllable emotion; intense enthusiasm
  • Synonym: zeal - great energy or enthusiasm in pursuit of a cause

Etymology

From Latin ardor (burning, heat, passion), from ardēre (to burn).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑrdər / (AmE), / ˈɑːdə / (BrE)
a strong feeling of passion, love, or enthusiasm for someone or something
intense passion or love

Examples

Her declaration was made with such ardor that nobody doubted her love.
He pursued his goals with the ardor of a true believer.
The ardor between them was evident to everyone at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passion - strong and barely controllable emotion, especially romantic or sexual love
  • Synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From Latin ardor (heat, passion), from ardere (to burn).
