self-winding

1. ADJ

/ sɛlfˈwaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / sɛlfˈwaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a mechanism, especially a watch) winding itself automatically, usually by the motion of the wearer's body, so that manual winding is unnecessary.
winding itself automatically (e.g., a watch)

Examples

He inherited a beautiful self-winding watch from his grandfather.
Many modern wristwatches are self-winding, eliminating the need to reset them frequently.
The self-winding mechanism ensures the watch keeps running as long as you wear it daily.

Synset Examples

a self-winding watch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automatic - working by itself with little or no direct human control
  • Antonym: manual - requiring or operated by hand

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' (oneself) and 'winding' (to turn or coil), referring to automatic winding.
