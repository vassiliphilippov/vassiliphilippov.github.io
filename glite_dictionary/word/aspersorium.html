aspersorium

1. NOUN

/ æˌspɝːˈsɔːriəm / (AmE), / æˌspɜːˈsɔːriəm / (BrE)
a basin or other vessel used for holding holy water in Roman Catholic churches
vessel for holy water in churches

Examples

The priest dipped his fingers into the aspersorium before entering the sanctuary.
An ornate aspersorium stood at the entrance of the medieval cathedral.
During the ceremony, the altar server refilled the aspersorium with fresh holy water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoup - a basin for holy water at the entrance of a church

Etymology

From Latin 'aspersorium' (sprinkler, vessel for sprinkling), from 'aspergere' (to sprinkle).

2. NOUN

/ əˌspɜːrˈsɔːriəm / (AmE), / əˌspɜːˈsɔːriəm / (BrE)
A vessel or tool, often a short-handled device with a globe containing a sponge, used for sprinkling holy water in religious ceremonies.
device for sprinkling holy water

Examples

The priest dipped the aspersorium into the holy water before sprinkling the congregation.
During the ceremony, the acolyte carried the aspersorium down the aisle.
She gazed at the ornate silver aspersorium on the altar, marveling at its craftsmanship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspersorium - vessel containing holy water for use in rites

Etymology

From Latin 'aspersorium' meaning 'sprinkler', from 'aspergere' (to sprinkle).
