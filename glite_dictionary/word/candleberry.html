candleberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈkændlˌbɛri / (BrE)
A deciduous aromatic shrub native to eastern North America, known for its grey-green, wax-coated berries used in candle making.
aromatic shrub with wax-coated berries

Examples

The settlers used the wax of the candleberry to make candles during the winter months.
We found several candleberry shrubs growing along the edge of the forest trail.
The aromatic berries of the candleberry are coated with a grey-green wax.

Etymology

From 'candle' + 'berry', referring to the use of the berry's wax in candle making.
