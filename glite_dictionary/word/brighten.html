to brighten

1. VERB

/ ˈbraɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈbraɪtn̩ / (BrE)
to make something appear more cheerful or lively, especially by adding color
make more cheerful with color

Examples

A fresh coat of yellow paint can brighten any kitchen.
She used colorful pillows to brighten the living room.
Adding a few plants will instantly brighten up your workspace.

Synset Examples

The paint will brighten the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lighten - make lighter or less dark

Etymology

From the adjective 'bright' + suffix '-en', forming a verb meaning 'to make bright'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00313597-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈbraɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈbraɪtn̩ / (BrE)
To become clear or less cloudy, especially with reference to the weather or sky.
become clear or less cloudy (weather/sky)

Examples

After an hour of rain, the clouds began to brighten and the sun peeked through.
The weather is expected to brighten in the afternoon, with clear skies by evening.
As the storm passed, the sky started to brighten, bringing hope for a pleasant day.

Synset Examples

The sky cleared after the storm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clear - (of the sky or weather) to become free of clouds or fog

Etymology

From bright + -en, Old English beorht (bright).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02777127-v
Synset POS: v
