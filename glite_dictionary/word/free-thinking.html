free-thinking

1. ADJ

/ ˈfriˌθɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌθɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
forming one's own opinions independently of authority or established beliefs, especially in matters of religion.
independent in thought, especially religious

Examples

Her free-thinking nature made her question the established traditions of her community.
The philosopher was known for his free-thinking approach to religious issues.
Many free-thinking individuals contributed to the intellectual movements of the Enlightenment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independent-minded - thinking for oneself; not influenced by others
  • Antonym: unquestioning - accepting authority or dogma without doubt
