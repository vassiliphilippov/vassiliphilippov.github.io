poise

1. NOUN

/ pɔɪz / (AmE), / pɔɪz / (BrE)
A centimeter-gram-second (cgs) unit of dynamic viscosity equal to one dyne-second per square centimeter.
unit of dynamic viscosity (cgs)

Examples

The viscosity of the solution was measured and found to be 2 poise.
Engineers calculated that a fluid with 10 poise would flow more slowly through the tube.
One poise equals exactly one dyne-second per square centimeter, commonly used in laboratory settings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: centipoise - one hundredth of a poise (unit of viscosity)
  • Synonym: pascal-second - SI unit of dynamic viscosity

Etymology

Named after Jean Léonard Marie Poiseuille, a French physician and physiologist.
