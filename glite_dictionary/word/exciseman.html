exciseman

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsaɪzˌmæn / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪzmən / (BrE)
a man employed by the government to collect excise taxes, especially on goods such as alcohol
government tax collector (esp. excise duties)

Examples

The exciseman visited the distillery to ensure all duties had been paid.
During the 18th century, a exciseman often risked his life confronting smugglers on the coast.
Rumors spread quickly whenever a exciseman arrived in the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax collector - person who collects taxes for government

Etymology

From 'excise' (a tax on certain goods) + 'man'.
