fauces

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːsiːz / (AmE), / ˈfɔːsiːz / (BrE)
the passage at the back of the mouth that leads from the oral cavity to the pharynx
passage at back of the mouth
medical

Examples

The doctor examined his fauces for any signs of infection.
Inflammation of the fauces can cause discomfort when swallowing.
Tonsillitis often affects the fauces and nearby tissues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oropharynx - part of the throat behind the mouth

Etymology

From Latin 'fauces' (throat, jaws, gullet).
