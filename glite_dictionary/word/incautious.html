incautious

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkɔʃəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɔːʃəs / (BrE)
Not showing care or thought for possible risks or problems; lacking caution.
not careful; lacking caution

Examples

He made an incautious decision that almost cost the company millions.
Her incautious words offended many people at the meeting.
Being incautious when handling chemicals can lead to serious accidents.

Synset Examples

an incautious remark
incautious talk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careless - not giving sufficient attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors
  • Antonym: cautious - careful to avoid potential problems or dangers

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + cautus (careful).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkɔʃəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɔːʃəs / (BrE)
carelessly failing to exercise proper caution; not attentive to potential dangers or risks.
not exercising proper caution

Examples

He received a warning after making several incautious remarks to the press.
Her incautious decision led to unexpected financial losses.
An incautious approach in dealing with wild animals can be very dangerous.

Synset Examples

an incautious step sent her headlong down the stairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careless - not giving sufficient attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors
  • Antonym: cautious - showing care and attention to avoid danger or mistakes

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'cautus' (careful).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00313938-s
Synset POS: s
