maze

1. NOUN

/ meɪz / (AmE), / meɪz / (BrE)
A complicated or confusing arrangement or situation; something that is difficult to understand or find a way through.
complex or confusing arrangement/situation

Examples

Navigating the maze of tax rules can be overwhelming for many people.
She was lost in a maze of conflicting emotions after hearing the news.
The new regulations created a bureaucratic maze that confused small businesses.

Synset Examples

a tangle of government regulations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labyrinth - a complex and confusing network of passages or paths
  • Synonym: tangle - a confused or complicated state or condition
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

Perhaps from Old English *mæsod* (deluded, confused).

2. NOUN

/ meɪz / (AmE), / meɪz / (BrE)
A network of interconnected paths or passages, typically designed as a puzzle through which one must find a way out.
a network of confusing paths or passages

Examples

We wandered through the maze for hours before finding the exit.
Children love to get lost in a corn maze during the fall festival.
The old castle was surrounded by a hedge maze that challenged every visitor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labyrinth - a complicated irregular network of passages or paths

Etymology

From Middle English *mæs* (confusion), possibly of Scandinavian origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03738772-n
Synset POS: n
