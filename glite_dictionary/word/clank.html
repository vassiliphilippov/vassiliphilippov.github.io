clank

1. NOUN

/ klæŋk / (AmE), / klæŋk / (BrE)
a loud, resonant, and often metallic noise, especially one that repeats or echoes
loud resonant metallic noise

Examples

The clank of the chains echoed through the empty hallway.
We heard a loud clank as the old furnace started up.
There was a steady clank of pots and pans coming from the kitchen.

Synset Examples

he could hear the clang of distant bells

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clang - a loud resonant metallic sound

Etymology

Imitative origin, representing the sound itself.

2. VERB

/ klæŋk / (AmE), / klæŋk / (BrE)
(of something made of metal) to make a loud, sharp, metallic sound, or to move making such a sound.
make a loud metallic sound

Examples

The old elevator would clank loudly as it reached each floor.
She heard chains clanking in the basement during the night.
As the knight entered, his armor clanked with every step.

Synset Examples

the train clanked through the village

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clang - make a loud, resonant, metallic sound
  • Synonym: clatter - make a continuous rattling sound
  • Antonym: thud - to make a dull sound as of a heavy object falling
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02178755-v
Synset POS: v
