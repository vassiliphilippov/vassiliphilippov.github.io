imperfectly

1. ADV

/ ɪmˈpɝːfɪktli / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːfɪktli / (BrE)
In a way that is not perfect; in a manner that has faults or is incomplete.
in a faulty or incomplete way

Examples

The artist's early sketches were imperfectly executed, lacking detail and symmetry.
Instructions for assembling the furniture were imperfectly translated, leading to some confusion.
She imperfectly remembered the details of her dream upon waking.

Synset Examples

The lobe was imperfectly developed
Miss Bennet would not play at all amiss if she practiced more

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perfectly - in a way that is perfect; without faults or errors
  • Synonym: faultily - in a faulty or defective manner

Etymology

From Latin 'imperfectus' (unfinished, incomplete) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
