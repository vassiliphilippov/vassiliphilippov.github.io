Sargassum bacciferum

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈɡæsəm ˌbækɪˈfɪrəm / (AmE), / sɑːˈɡasəm ˌbakɪˈfɪərəm / (BrE)
A species of brown algae with rounded air bladders, forming dense floating masses in tropical Atlantic waters, especially in the Sargasso Sea.
species of brown algae with air bladders

Examples

Dense mats of Sargassum bacciferum provide habitat for many marine species in the Sargasso Sea.
Scientists study the growth patterns of Sargassum bacciferum to understand its ecological impact.
Large blooms of Sargassum bacciferum have occasionally washed ashore, causing concern for local communities.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Sargassum' and species epithet 'bacciferum', from 'bacca' (berry) + '-fer' (bearing).
