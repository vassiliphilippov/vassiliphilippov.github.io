Quebecois

1. ADJ

/ keɪˈbɛkˌwɑ / (AmE), / keɪˈbɛkˌwɑ / (BrE)
relating to Quebec, especially to the French-speaking people or their culture
relating to Quebec's French-speaking people or culture

Examples

His Quebecois accent made him stand out among the Canadian delegation.
The restaurant serves traditional Quebecois dishes, famous for their rich flavors.
She wrote an essay comparing Quebecois and French literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quebec - a province in eastern Canada

Etymology

French Québécois, from Québec (province of Canada) + -ois (French suffix meaning 'of or relating to').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02988865-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ keɪˌbɛkˈwɑ / (AmE), / kɛˌbɛkˈwɑ / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Quebec, especially one who speaks French
French-speaking person from Quebec

Examples

Many Quebecois proudly celebrate their heritage during the annual winter carnival.
The film features a cast of talented Quebecois actors speaking in their native French dialect.
As a Quebecois, she is deeply passionate about preserving the French language in Canada.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quebecer - a person from Quebec

Etymology

French Québécois, from Québec (the province) + -ois (French suffix for inhabitants).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10518817-n
Synset POS: n
