Apus apus

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.pəs ˈeɪ.pəs / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.pəs ˈeɪ.pəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the common swift, a European bird with a shrieking call, known for nesting around buildings and cliffs.
scientific name for the common swift

Examples

Many ornithologists study Apus apus to understand migratory patterns in swifts.
The Apus apus is commonly seen nesting under rooftops in European cities during summer.
Identification of Apus apus can be aided by its distinctive silhouette and high-pitched calls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common swift - A small, fast-flying bird, Apus apus, known for its aerial abilities and shrill call

Etymology

From Latin: 'apus' (swift); used in scientific taxonomy.
