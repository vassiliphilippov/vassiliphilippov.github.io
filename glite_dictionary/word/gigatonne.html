gigatonne

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪɡəˌtʌn / (AmE), / ˈɡɪɡəˌtʌn / (BrE)
a metric unit of mass equal to one billion (1,000,000,000) metric tonnes (1 × 10⁹ tonnes)
unit of mass equal to one billion tonnes

Examples

Global carbon dioxide emissions have exceeded 35 gigatonnes per year in recent decades.
The ice sheet is losing more than 200 gigatonnes of mass annually due to melting.
One gigatonne is equivalent to one trillion kilograms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tonne - a metric unit of mass equal to 1,000 kilograms
  • Degree Lower: kilotonne - a metric unit of mass equal to 1,000 tonnes
  • Degree Lower: megatonne - a metric unit of mass equal to one million tonnes

Etymology

From 'giga-' (billion) and 'tonne' (metric ton), following SI unit prefix conventions.
