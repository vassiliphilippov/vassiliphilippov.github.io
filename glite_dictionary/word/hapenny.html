ha'penny

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪˌpɛni / (AmE), / ˈheɪpəni / (BrE)
a former British coin worth half a penny, used before decimalisation in 1971
former British coin worth half a penny
british archaic

Examples

He dropped a ha'penny into the charity box at the station.
My grandfather used to collect old coins like the ha'penny.
A bus ride once cost only a ha'penny in those days.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: penny - a British coin and monetary unit equal to one hundredth of a pound

Etymology

Contraction of 'half-penny'.
