Formica sanguinea

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈmaɪkə sænˈɡwɪniə / (AmE), / fɔːˈmaɪkə saŋˈɡwɪnɪə / (BrE)
A species of slave-making ant widely distributed across the Northern Hemisphere, known for invading and taking over colonies of other ants.
a species of slave-making ant

Examples

Formica sanguinea colonies are known for capturing and enslaving workers from other ant species.
The bright coloration of Formica sanguinea makes it easy to identify in the field.
Researchers observed a group of Formica sanguinea conducting a raid on a nearby ant nest.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Formica' meaning 'ant', and 'sanguinea' meaning 'bloody' or 'blood-red'.
