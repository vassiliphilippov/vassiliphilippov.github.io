willpower

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪlˌpaʊər / (AmE), / ˈwɪlˌpaʊə / (BrE)
The ability to control your actions, impulses, or emotions, especially in difficult situations.
self-control or determination

Examples

It took a lot of willpower to resist the temptation of eating the cake.
She relied on her willpower to stick to her new exercise routine.
Sometimes, willpower alone is not enough to change a bad habit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-control - the ability to control one's actions and feelings
  • Synonym: determination - firmness of purpose; resoluteness

Etymology

Formed from 'will' (desire, determination) and 'power' (ability, strength).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪlˌpaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪlˌpaʊ.ə / (BrE)
The ability to control your own actions, feelings, or desires firmly and resolutely.
self-control and determination

Examples

It takes a lot of willpower to resist eating sweets every day.
Despite the obstacles, she used her willpower to finish the marathon.
Building strong willpower can help you achieve your long-term goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-control - The ability to control one's emotions, desires, or actions by one's own will
  • Antonym: weakness - Lack of strength of will to control oneself

Etymology

Formed from 'will' (Old English *willan*, to wish, desire) + 'power' (Old French *poeir*, from Latin *posse*, to be able).
