serving

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪŋ / (BrE)
the formal act of delivering a legal writ, summons, or other court document to the person to whom it is addressed
the delivery of a legal writ or summons
legal

Examples

The serving of the court order was completed yesterday afternoon.
The defendant challenged the validity of the serving of the summons.
A process server is responsible for the serving of legal documents in a civil case.

Synset Examples

he accepted service of the subpoena

Related Terms

  • Synonym: service - the legal formal delivery of a writ, summons, etc.
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪŋ / (BrE)
an amount of food or drink given to one person as part of a meal
quantity of food or drink for one person

Examples

She ate only one serving of dessert after dinner.
The recommended serving size is listed on the package for your convenience.
Would you like an extra serving of vegetables with your meal?

Synset Examples

the helpings were all small
his portion was larger than hers
there's enough for two servings each

Related Terms

  • Synonym: portion - an amount of food served to one person
  • Synonym: helping - an amount of food given to one person at a meal

Etymology

From the verb 'serve' + -ing, indicating the portion served.
