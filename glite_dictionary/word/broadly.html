broadly

1. ADV

/ ˈbrɑd.li / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːd.li / (BrE)
in a wide or extensive manner; with great breadth or scope
in a wide or extensive way

Examples

She smiled broadly as she accepted the award.
The new law is broadly supported by politicians on both sides.
The river flows broadly through the valley before reaching the sea.

Synset Examples

he smiled broadly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: narrowly - in a narrow or restricted way

Etymology

From 'broad' + '-ly', meaning 'wide' + adverbial suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00223275-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈbrɔdli / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdli / (BrE)
In a general way, without considering details or exceptions.
in a general way; not focusing on details

Examples

He interprets the law broadly to allow for flexibility in unique cases.
Broadly speaking, the project was a success despite some minor issues.
The new policy, broadly applied, affects all employees regardless of department.

Synset Examples

he interprets the law broadly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generally - in most cases; usually; without focusing on details
  • Antonym: specifically - in a detailed and precise way

Etymology

From broad + -ly, meaning "in a broad manner."

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00223063-r
Synset POS: r
