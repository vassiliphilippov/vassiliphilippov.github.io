davallia

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvæliə / (AmE), / dəˈvæliə / (BrE)
a fern of the genus Davallia, characterized by scaly, creeping rhizomes
a fern of the genus Davallia

Examples

The davallia is often called the rabbit's foot fern because of its furry rhizomes.
Botanists studied a rare davallia species found in the tropical greenhouse.
She chose a davallia to add a tropical touch to her fern collection.

Etymology

Named after Swiss botanist Edmond Davall (1763–1798).
