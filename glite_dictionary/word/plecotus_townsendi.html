Plecotus townsendi

1. NOUN

/ pliˈkoʊtəs taʊnˈzɛnˌdi / (AmE), / pliˈkəʊtəs taʊnˈzendi / (BrE)
A species of bat native to western North America, noted for its extremely large ears, commonly known as Townsend's big-eared bat.
a North American bat with large ears

Examples

The Plecotus townsendi is easily recognized by its unusually large ears and delicate features.
Conservationists are concerned about habitat loss affecting populations of Plecotus townsendi in several western states.
A team of biologists tracked the movements of Plecotus townsendi in the forested canyons.

Etymology

From New Latin, 'Plecotus' meaning 'folded ear' (from Greek pleko- 'fold' + ous 'ear'), and named in honor of naturalist John Kirk Townsend.
