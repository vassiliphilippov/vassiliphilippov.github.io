crease

1. NOUN

/ kris / (AmE), / kriːs / (BrE)
a line, mark, or ridge formed on a surface, typically by folding, pressing, or crushing
line or mark made by folding

Examples

She ironed the crease out of her skirt before leaving for work.
A sharp crease ran down the center of each trouser leg.
He made a crease in the paper to help cut it straight.

Synset Examples

a fold in the napkin
a crease in his trousers
a plication on her blouse
a flexure of the colon
a bend of his elbow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fold - a line or shape formed by bending one part over another

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *crease* (a curl or pleat), from Old English *crēas* (fine, thin cloth), though the exact origin is uncertain.

6. NOUN

/ kris / (AmE), / kriːs / (BrE)
a line or fold produced on a smooth surface, such as fabric or paper, by pressure, folding, or wrinkling

Examples

Try not to put a crease in the paper while folding it.

Related Terms
