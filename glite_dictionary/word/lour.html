to lour

1. VERB

/ laʊər / (AmE), / laʊə / (BrE)
to look angry or sullen; to scowl or frown, especially by wrinkling the forehead to show disapproval or displeasure
look angry or sullen; scowl

Examples

She began to lour at her classmates after they laughed at her mistake.
The sky seemed to lour ominously, matching his dark mood.
He loured in the corner, refusing to join in the conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scowl - to frown in an angry or bad-tempered way
  • Synonym: glower - to have an angry or sullen look on one's face; to scowl

Etymology

From Middle English louren or luren, possibly imitative of the act of lowering or frowning.
