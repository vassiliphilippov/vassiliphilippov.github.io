watchfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑtʃfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃfəlnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being alert and attentive to possible danger or changes
state of being alert and attentive

Examples

The guard's watchfulness prevented any intruders from entering the building that night.
Parents rely on their watchfulness to keep their children safe in busy places.
Her watchfulness during the meeting ensured that no important details were missed.

Synset Examples

wakefulness, watchfulness, and bellicosity make a good hunter
vigilance is especially susceptible to fatigue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigilance - the action or state of keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties
  • Synonym: alertness - the quality of being quick to notice and respond to danger or unusual circumstances
  • Antonym: carelessness - lack of attention and caution

Etymology

From 'watchful' + '-ness', where 'watchful' derives from 'watch' (Middle English) + '-ful'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːtʃfʊlnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃfʊlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being alert and attentive to possible danger or difficulties
vigilant attentiveness

Examples

Her watchfulness prevented an accident in the laboratory.
The sentinel's watchfulness ensured the safety of the entire camp.
Parental watchfulness is necessary when young children are near water.

Synset Examples

he keeps a weather eye open for trouble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigilance - the state of keeping careful watch for possible danger
  • Antonym: carelessness - lack of attention or caution

Etymology

From 'watchful' + '-ness'; 'watchful' from Old English 'wæccan' (to be awake, keep vigil).
