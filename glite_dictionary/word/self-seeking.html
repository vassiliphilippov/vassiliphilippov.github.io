self-seeking

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlfˈsikɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈsiːkɪŋ / (BrE)
concerned chiefly with your own personal gain or advantage, often disregarding others
concerned with personal advantage

Examples

The public grew tired of the self-seeking politicians who seemed uninterested in real change.
Her self-seeking attitude alienated her friends and family over time.
He avoided self-seeking behavior by always considering the needs of others.

Synset Examples

However, because of their strong-minded nature, they are often perceived as difficult and egoistical individuals.

What I can't believe is that people are so stupid and self-centered that they think they HAVE to comment on things they don't like.
We're asked to see Rachel as this spoilt, self-centred woman but the rest of them are just as bad, if not worse.
As the crying baby, we express purely the self-serving thoughts that arise in our minds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selfish - lacking consideration for others; concerned chiefly with one's own personal profit or pleasure
  • Antonym: altruistic - showing a selfless concern for the well-being of others

2. NOUN

/ sɛlfˈsiːkɪŋ / (AmE), / sɛlfˈsiːkɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of pursuing one's own interests or advantage, often with little or no consideration for others.
pursuit of personal advantage

Examples

She was accused of self-seeking during the campaign, putting her ambitions above those of the community.
His self-seeking became obvious when he betrayed his colleagues for personal gain.
They warned against self-seeking as a destructive force within the organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selfishness - concern with one's own advantage to the exclusion of others
  • Antonym: altruism - selfless concern for the well-being of others

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + present participle of 'seek'; attested since the 17th century.
