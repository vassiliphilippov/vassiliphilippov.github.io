ammonia

1. NOUN

/ əˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / əˈməʊniə / (BrE)
a pungent gas composed of nitrogen and hydrogen (NH3), commonly used in cleaning products and fertilizers
pungent gas, NH3, used in industry and cleaning

Examples

The strong smell of ammonia made it difficult to breathe in the room.
Farmers often use ammonia as a fertilizer to enrich their soil.
Mixing bleach with ammonia can produce dangerous fumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ammonium - the ion NH4+, a related compound

Etymology

From Latin 'ammoniacum', from Greek 'ammōniakon', an early reference to a gum from Ammon's temple, reflecting the salt from which ammonia was first obtained.

2. NOUN

/ əˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / əˈməʊniə / (BrE)
A solution of ammonia gas in water, commonly used as a cleaning agent and in industry.
aqueous solution of ammonia gas

Examples

Be careful when using ammonia as a cleaning product, as its fumes can be harmful.
The janitor mixed water with ammonia to clean the floor tiles more effectively.
Never combine ammonia with bleach, as the reaction produces dangerous gases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ammonium hydroxide - another name for aqueous ammonia solution

Etymology

From Latin 'ammoniacus', from Greek 'ammōniakos', relating to the temple of Ammon in Libya, where ammonium chloride was obtained.
