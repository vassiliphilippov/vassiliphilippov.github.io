atenolol

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɛnəˌlɑl / (AmE), / əˈtɛnəlɒl / (BrE)
A medication in the class of beta blockers, taken orally to treat conditions such as high blood pressure and angina.
beta blocker medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed atenolol to control her high blood pressure.
My father takes atenolol daily for his heart condition.
Some patients may experience fatigue as a side effect of atenolol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propranolol - another beta blocker medication
  • Synonym: metoprolol - another beta blocker used for heart conditions

Etymology

From chemical nomenclature, combining 'a-' (prefix), 'ten' from 'tetramethylene', and '-olol' standard for beta blockers.
