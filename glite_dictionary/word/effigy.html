effigy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛf.ə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈɛf.ɪ.dʒi / (BrE)
a model or representation of a person, especially one sculpted or made for display, often as a way of expressing protest or honor
representation or model of a person

Examples

The protesters burned an effigy of the leader in the square.
A bronze effigy of the philosopher stands outside the library.
The ancient tomb was decorated with a detailed effigy of the king.

Synset Examples

the coin bears an effigy of Lincoln
the emperor's tomb had his image carved in stone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statue - a figure of a person or animal that is made from stone, metal, etc.
  • Degree Lower: caricature - a drawing or model that exaggerates characteristics for comic effect

Etymology

From Latin 'effigies' (likeness, image).
