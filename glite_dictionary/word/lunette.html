lunette

1. NOUN

/ luˈnɛt / (AmE), / ljuːˈnɛt / (BrE)
A temporary field fortification with two faces forming a salient angle, resembling a detached bastion, used in military defense.
temporary detached field fortification

Examples

During the siege, the soldiers constructed a lunette to strengthen their defensive line.
The general inspected the lunette before approving it as a forward position.
Ancient military manuals contain diagrams illustrating how to build a proper lunette.

Etymology

From French 'lunette' (little moon), referring to its crescent shape, from Latin 'lūna' (moon).

2. NOUN

/ luˈnɛt / (AmE), / luˈnɛt / (BrE)
An oval or circular opening, especially in a dome or vault, designed to admit light.
oval or circular architectural opening

Examples

The architect included a lunette above the entrance to let in more daylight.
Sunlight streamed through the lunette, illuminating the church’s central aisle.
The lunette in the ceiling provided both decorative interest and natural light.

Etymology

From French 'lunette' (little moon), from 'lune' (moon), referring to the crescent shape of some openings.
