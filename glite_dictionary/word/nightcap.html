nightcap

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtkæp / (BrE)
the final game played in a double header, especially in sports such as baseball
final game of a double header (sports)

Examples

After a long day at the stadium, the crowd was eager for the nightcap to begin.
The team saved their star pitcher for the nightcap in hopes of securing a sweep.
Rain delayed the nightcap until nearly midnight, but the fans stayed to watch.

Etymology

Originally referred to a cap worn at night; since the 19th century, extended to mean a final drink before bed, and later for the last event/game of the evening.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtkæp / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtkæp / (BrE)
An alcoholic drink taken shortly before going to bed, usually to help one relax or sleep.
alcoholic drink before bed

Examples

She likes to have a nightcap after a long day at work.
He offered his guest a nightcap before they retired for the night.
A small glass of whiskey serves as his usual nightcap every evening.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: drink - a general term for any beverage

Etymology

Originally from the literal sense of a cap worn at night; later used figuratively for a drink before bed.

3. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈnaɪt.kæp / (BrE)
A soft cloth cap worn in bed to keep the head warm, especially in the past.
cloth cap worn in bed

Examples

In cold weather, he always wore a nightcap to bed.
The illustration showed Scrooge in his dressing gown and nightcap.
She laughed at the old-fashioned nightcap her grandmother gave her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleepwear - clothing worn in bed or for sleeping

Etymology

Formed from 'night' + 'cap', referring to a cap worn at night.
