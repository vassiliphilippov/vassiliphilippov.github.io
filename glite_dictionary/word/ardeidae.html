Ardeidae

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈdiːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ɑːˈdiːɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of wading birds that includes herons, egrets, and bitterns, known for their long legs and necks.
family of birds: herons, egrets, bitterns

Examples

The Ardeidae family is distributed worldwide, with many species adapted to wetlands and marshes.
Herons and egrets belong to the bird family called Ardeidae.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of various species within Ardeidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heron - a large wading bird related to Ardeidae
  • Synonym: egret - a type of wading bird in the family Ardeidae
  • Synonym: bittern - a wading bird in the family Ardeidae

Etymology

From New Latin Ardeidae, based on Latin ardea (heron).
