Acrocomia aculeata

1. NOUN

/ ˌæk.roʊˈkoʊ.mi.ə ˌæk.jʊˈleɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌæ.krəʊˈkəʊ.mi.ə ˌæk.jʊˈleɪ.tə / (BrE)
A tropical American palm tree with a swollen spiny trunk and edible nuts, commonly known as the macaw palm.
tropical palm tree with spiny trunk and edible nuts

Examples

The Acrocomia aculeata is valued for its edible nuts and durable palm oil.
Farmers in Brazil often plant Acrocomia aculeata to harvest its fruit for cooking.
The spiny trunk of the Acrocomia aculeata deters many animals from climbing it.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Acrocomia (palm genus) and Latin aculeata (spiny), referring to its characteristic spines.
