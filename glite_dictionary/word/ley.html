ley

1. NOUN

/ leɪ / (AmE), / leɪ / (BrE)
a field sown with grass or other herbage, used for grazing livestock or for mowing
field used for grazing livestock

Examples

The farmer rotated his crops and left part of his land as ley for the cattle.
Sheep grazed peacefully on the lush spring ley stretching down to the river.
Restoring old leys can improve soil fertility and support local wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasture - land covered with grass for grazing animals
  • Synonym: meadow - a field of grass, especially one used for hay

Etymology

From Old English *lēah* (meadow, clearing); related to modern 'lea'.
