Clematis

1. NOUN

/ kləˈmeɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈklɛm.ətɪs / (BrE)
a large genus of deciduous or evergreen woody vines or erect herbs in the buttercup family, known for their showy flowers
a genus of ornamental flowering plants

Examples

The Clematis in our garden blooms every spring with beautiful purple flowers.
Many gardeners choose Clematis for its ability to climb trellises and fences.
Botanists have identified hundreds of Clematis species across different continents.

Etymology

From Greek 'klēmatis', meaning a climbing plant, from 'klēma' (twig, vine).
