surname

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.neɪm / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.neɪm / (BrE)
A family name shared by members of a family, identifying them as distinct from others; usually inherited and placed after the given name in English-speaking cultures.
family name; last name

Examples

All official documents require you to write your surname as well as your given name.
Her surname reflects her family's origins in Eastern Europe.
After getting married, she decided to keep her own surname.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: given name - the personal name given to an individual at birth or baptism, as opposed to the family name
  • Synonym: last name - another term for surname, especially in American English

Etymology

From Middle English, from Anglo-Norman, based on Old French 'surnom' (over-name), from sur- (over) + nom (name).
