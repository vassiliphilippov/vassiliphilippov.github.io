Sauromalus

1. NOUN

/ sɔːˈrɑːmələs / (AmE), / sɔːˈrɒmələs / (BrE)
A genus of large, herbivorous lizards known as chuckwallas, native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.
genus of large lizards (chuckwallas)

Examples

The desert ecosystem is home to several species within the genus Sauromalus.
Researchers observed a Sauromalus basking on a rock in the midday sun.
The diet of Sauromalus lizards consists primarily of leaves, flowers, and fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chuckwalla - A large, herbivorous lizard native to arid regions of North America

Etymology

From Greek *sauros* (lizard) and *omalos* (flat), referring to its flattened body.
