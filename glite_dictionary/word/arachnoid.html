arachnoid

1. ADJ

/ əˈræk.nɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈræk.nɔɪd / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling spiders or members of the class Arachnida.
relating to spiders or Arachnida

Examples

The scorpion's arachnoid features make it easy to distinguish from insects.
Scientists studied the arachnoid anatomy of the harvestman to understand its movement.
That costume had an arachnoid appearance, with long, spindly legs and dark colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arachnid - any member of the class Arachnida

Etymology

From Greek 'aráchnē' (spider) + '-oid' (resembling).

2. NOUN

/ əˈræk.nɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈræk.nɔɪd / (BrE)
The middle layer of the three membranes (meninges) that cover the brain and spinal cord.
middle membrane covering brain and spinal cord
medical

Examples

The arachnoid serves as a protective layer between the dura mater and pia mater of the brain.
During the surgery, the doctors carefully separated the arachnoid to access the area beneath.
Inflammation of the arachnoid is known as arachnoiditis, which can cause severe pain.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: meninges - the three membranes covering the brain and spinal cord
  • Degree Lower: pia mater - the innermost membrane of the meninges
  • Degree Higher: dura mater - the outermost membrane of the meninges

Etymology

From Greek arakhnoeidēs (web-like), from arakhnē (spider).

3. NOUN

/ əˈræk.nɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈræk.nɔɪd / (BrE)
a member of the class of arthropods that includes spiders, scorpions, mites, and ticks, typically having eight legs and simple eyes
spider-like arthropod with eight legs

Examples

The arachnoid scuttled across the forest floor, searching for prey.
Scientists discovered a new species of arachnoid in the Amazon rainforest.
The child was fascinated by the eight-legged arachnoid clinging to the window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spider - an arachnid known for spinning webs
  • Synonym: scorpion - an arachnid with pincers and a venomous sting

Etymology

From Greek arachnoeidēs (spider-like), from arakhnē (spider).
