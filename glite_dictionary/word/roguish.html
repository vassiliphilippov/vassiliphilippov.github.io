roguish

1. ADJ

/ ˈroʊɡɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊɡɪʃ / (BrE)
playfully mischievous, often in a way that is appealing or charming
playfully mischievous or charming

Examples

He flashed a roguish grin before slipping out of the room.
Her roguish sense of humor made everyone laugh.
There was something roguish about the way he winked at the camera.

Synset Examples

a roguish grin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischievous - behaving in a playful or slightly naughty way

Etymology

From rogue + -ish; 'rogue' from early modern English, denoting a dishonest or mischievous person.

Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ ˈroʊɡɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊɡɪʃ / (BrE)
Displaying a playful or mischievous disregard for rules or morals; behaving in a way that is deceitful or dishonest, often in a way that is attractively cheeky rather than seriously criminal.
playfully mischievous or dishonest

Examples

He gave her a roguish grin that made everyone laugh.
The story follows a roguish adventurer who bends the rules to get by.
Her roguish charm often got her out of trouble.

Synset Examples

the rascally rabble
the tyranny of a scoundrelly aristocracy
the captain was set adrift by his roguish crew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rascally - mischievous or dishonest, like a rascal

Etymology

From 'rogue' (a dishonest or mischievous person) + '-ish' (forming adjectives).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01227614-s
Synset POS: s
