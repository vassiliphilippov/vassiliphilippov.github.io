gimbal

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪmbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɪmbəl / (BrE)
A device consisting of a set of rings mounted on axes at right angles, allowing an object such as a compass to remain upright regardless of movement or tilt.
pivoted support for steadying devices

Examples

The ship's compass was mounted on a gimbal to keep it level as the vessel pitched and rolled.
Modern cameras often use a gimbal to stabilize footage while moving.
He inspected the broken gimbal that allowed the lantern to swing freely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gyroscope - device for measuring or maintaining orientation, often using gimbals

Etymology

Possibly from Middle Low German *gimmel* (twin, hinge). Related to the Old French *guimbe* (double ring).
