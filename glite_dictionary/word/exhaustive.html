exhaustive

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɔstɪv / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɔːstɪv / (BrE)
including all possibilities; very thorough and complete
very thorough and complete

Examples

The committee conducted an exhaustive review of all available evidence before making a decision.
Her research into the topic was truly exhaustive, covering every significant study in the field.
This report provides an exhaustive list of all the species found in the region.

Synset Examples

an exhaustive study
made a thorough search
thoroughgoing research

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorough - performed with great attention to detail
  • Antonym: partial - not complete or total; limited

Etymology

From Latin 'exhaurire' (to draw out, drain off), via 'exhaust'.
