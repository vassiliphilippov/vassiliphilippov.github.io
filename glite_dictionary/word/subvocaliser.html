subvocaliser

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈvoʊ.kə.laɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / sʌbˈvəʊ.kə.laɪ.zə / (BrE)
a person who articulates words without vocalizing them, often moving the lips silently
person who silently mouths words

Examples

The teacher noticed that Sarah was a subvocaliser when reading complex texts silently.
Many speed-reading courses aim to help subvocalisers minimize their silent articulation to read faster.
During the experiment, each subvocaliser was monitored to study their lip movements while reading.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subvocalization - silent articulation of words while reading or thinking

Etymology

From 'sub-' (under, silent) and 'vocalise' (to produce vocal sounds).
