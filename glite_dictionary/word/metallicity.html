metallicity

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˈlɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈlɪsəti / (BrE)
The quality or state of having a sound resembling that of metal, especially a bright, resonant, or metallic timbre.
metal-like quality of sound

Examples

The bell's clear metallicity echoed through the quiet church.
Some audio equipment can enhance the metallicity of cymbals, making them sound sharper.
He preferred a warm tone in his guitar, avoiding the harsh metallicity of some amplifiers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metallic - having the characteristics of metal, especially in sound or appearance
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of brightness or resonance in sound

Etymology

From metal + -icity, modeled after terms like 'electricity'.
