macaronic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmækəˈrɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌmækəˈrɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of language, text, or verse) containing a humorous or incongruous mixture of languages, especially Latin with a vernacular language.
mixing Latin and another language

Examples

The poet wrote a macaronic verse blending Latin and Italian in a playful way.
Many medieval scholars enjoyed composing macaronic texts for entertainment.
This collection features macaronic poetry that humorously mixes classical and local languages.

Synset Examples

macaronic verse

Etymology

From Italian maccheronico (mock-Latin verse), from maccherone (macaroni), used humorously to suggest coarseness.
