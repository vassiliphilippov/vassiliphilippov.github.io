to manage

1. VERB

/ ˈmænɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmænɪdʒ / (BrE)
to handle or control something with skill or effectiveness
handle or control effectively

Examples

She was able to manage the difficult task with ease.
Can you manage these fragile boxes without dropping any?
He struggled to manage the heavy equipment by himself.

Synset Examples

The burglar wielded an axe
The young violinist didn't manage her bow very well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handle - to manage or control something
  • Antonym: to mismanage - to handle something badly or ineffectively

Etymology

From Italian *maneggiare* (to handle), from Latin *manus* (hand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01226979-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈmænɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmænɪdʒ / (BrE)
to be in charge of, direct, or supervise a person, group, business, or project
be in charge of; supervise

Examples

She was hired to manage the new branch and train the staff.
It's not easy to manage a team with so many different personalities.
He will manage the event to make sure everything runs smoothly.

Synset Examples

Who is overseeing this project?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to supervise - to watch and direct the work of others
  • Synonym: to oversee - to watch over and direct work or activity
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to take proper care or responsibility for

Etymology

From Italian *maneggiare* (to handle), from Latin *manus* (hand).

3. VERB

/ ˈmænɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmænɪdʒ / (BrE)
to achieve something, especially by using skillful or devious methods; to succeed in obtaining or accomplishing, sometimes by trickery
succeed using skillful or devious methods

Examples

She somehow managed to get backstage without a ticket.
He managed to secure the deal by bending the rules a little.
Despite strict supervision, they managed to sneak out of the facility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wangle - to achieve something by clever or devious means
  • Synonym: to finagle - to obtain or achieve something by trickery

Etymology

From Italian maneggiare (to handle), from mano (hand), influenced by French manager and Latin manus.
