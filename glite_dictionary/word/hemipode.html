hemipode

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛmɪˌpoʊd / (AmE), / ˈhɛmɪpəʊd / (BrE)
a small, quail-like bird found in southern Eurasia and northern Africa, lacking a hind toe and typically living in grassy plains
small quail-like bird of Eurasia and Africa

Examples

The hemipode hides in tall grasses, making it difficult for predators to find.
Researchers observed a group of hemipodes feeding at dawn on the open plain.
Unlike true quail, the hemipode does not have a hind toe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttonquail - a small, terrestrial bird similar to a quail, from the family Turnicidae

Etymology

From Greek hemi- (half) + pous (foot), referring to the absence of a hind toe.
