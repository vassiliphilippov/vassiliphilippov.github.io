biradial

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈreɪ.di.əl / (AmE), / baɪˈreɪ.di.əl / (BrE)
(of an organism or structure) showing both bilateral and radial symmetry.
having both bilateral and radial symmetry
medical

Examples

Some species of sea anemones are biradial in form, combining elements of both symmetry types.
The biradial structure of the organism makes it unique among marine invertebrates.
During development, the larva exhibits biradial symmetry before transitioning to a more bilateral form.

Synset Examples

some sea anemones are biradial

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radial - showing symmetry around a central axis
  • Synonym: bilateral - having symmetry along a single plane

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) and 'radialis' (having rays).
