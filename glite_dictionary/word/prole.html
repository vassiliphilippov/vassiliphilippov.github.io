prole

1. NOUN

/ proʊl / (AmE), / prəʊl / (BrE)
a member of the working class, especially one regarded with lack of prestige or influence
working-class person

Examples

In the dystopian novel, the government controls every aspect of the prole's life.
Some argue that the voice of the prole is rarely heard in mainstream politics.
She dismissed his views as those of a mere prole, revealing her disdain for the working class.

Synset Examples

workers of the world — unite!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proletarian - a member of the working class, especially in Marxist theory
  • Antonym: bourgeois - a member of the middle class, especially with reference to materialistic values

Etymology

Shortened from 'proletarian', from Latin proletarius (citizen of the lowest class).
