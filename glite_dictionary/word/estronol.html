Estronol

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛstrəˌnɑl / (AmE), / ˈɛstrənɒl / (BrE)
A synthetic form of a weak estrogenic hormone, used as a medication to treat estrogen deficiency.
synthetic estrogen medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Estronol to alleviate her symptoms of estrogen deficiency.
After starting treatment with Estronol, she noticed improved mood and energy levels.
Patients using Estronol should follow their healthcare provider’s instructions carefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estriol - a naturally occurring weak estrogenic hormone
  • Degree Lower: estrogen - a group of sex hormones responsible for female characteristics
