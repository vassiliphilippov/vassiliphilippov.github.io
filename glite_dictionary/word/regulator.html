regulator

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛɡjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
a device used to control or maintain the level, flow, pressure, or temperature of a system, especially in machinery or engineering contexts
device for controlling flow, pressure, or temperature

Examples

The pressure regulator keeps the gas flow within safe limits.
An automatic temperature regulator adjusts the heat inside the incubator.
You should check that the water regulator is working properly before starting the machine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: controller - a device used to operate or adjust machinery

Etymology

From Latin 'regulare' (to rule, regulate), via Medieval Latin 'regulator.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛɡjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
an official or organization responsible for controlling and supervising a particular industry or area of public interest
official or body overseeing an activity or industry

Examples

The financial regulator imposed new rules to prevent market manipulation.
A regulator must ensure companies comply with safety standards.
New guidelines were quickly approved by the environmental regulator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supervisor - a person who supervises activities or people
  • Synonym: overseer - someone who supervises others, especially workers
  • Antonym: deregulator - a person who removes or lessens regulations, often the opposite role

Etymology

From Latin 'regulare' (to rule, regulate), from 'regula' (a rule).

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛɡjəˌleɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
a device that automatically controls and maintains a steady speed or level in a machine or system, such as by regulating the supply of fuel
device that controls speed or level in machines

Examples

The speed regulator in the car ensures the engine runs smoothly.
This machine uses a pressure regulator to maintain safe operation.
A faulty regulator can cause the system to overheat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: controller - a device that regulates or controls something

Etymology

From Latin 'regulator', from 'regulare' (to rule).
