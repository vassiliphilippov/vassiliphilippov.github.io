initialism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɪʃ.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪʃ.əl.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
a word formed from the initial letters of a phrase or name and pronounced as individual letters
word formed from initials, pronounced letter by letter

Examples

The CIA is an initialism for Central Intelligence Agency.
Many people confuse an acronym with an initialism.
ASAP is an initialism commonly used in emails.

Synset Examples

‘NATO’ is an initialism for North Atlantic Treaty Organization
the word ‘scuba’ is an acronym for s(elf)-c(ontained) u(nderwater) b(reathing) a(pparatus)

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acronym - a word formed from the initial letters of a phrase and pronounced as a word

Etymology

From initial + -ism, modeled after 'acronym'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɪʃəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪʃəlɪzəm / (BrE)
An abbreviation consisting of the initial letters of a series of words, each pronounced separately.
abbreviation pronounced as separate letters

Examples

The FBI is an initialism for Federal Bureau of Investigation.
Unlike 'NASA', which is an acronym, 'UN' is an initialism.
She explained that 'URL' is actually an initialism, not an acronym.

Synset Examples

HTML is an initialism for HyperText Markup Language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acronym - an abbreviation formed from initial letters and pronounced as a word

Etymology

From 'initial' (first letter of a word) + '-ism'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07106112-n
Synset POS: n
