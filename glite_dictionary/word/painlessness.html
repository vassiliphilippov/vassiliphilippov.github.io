painlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪn.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪnləsnɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being free from physical or emotional pain or difficulty.
absence of pain or difficulty

Examples

She was amazed at the painlessness of the procedure performed by the new doctor.
The painlessness of their breakup surprised both friends and family.
Modern medicine strives for the painlessness of surgical treatments whenever possible.

Synset Examples

Half the songs advocate the painlessness of casual romance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pain - physical or emotional suffering or discomfort
  • Synonym: comfort - a state of physical ease and freedom from pain

Etymology

From 'painless' + '-ness', where 'painless' is formed from 'pain' (from Old French 'peine', from Latin 'poena') and '-less' (without).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪnləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪnləsnɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being free from pain; absence of physical or emotional pain.
absence of pain

Examples

The new procedure ensured complete painlessness for all patients involved.
She was thankful for the painlessness of the dental treatment.
Advancements in medicine have made painlessness more achievable during surgery.

Synset Examples

Dr. Fry, who is present this evening, will bear witness to the painlessness of this operation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pain - physical or emotional suffering
  • Synonym: comfort - state of physical ease and freedom from pain or constraint

Etymology

From 'painless' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the state or quality.
