Jersey

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜrzi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːzi / (BrE)
a breed of small, light brown dairy cattle originally developed on the island of Jersey, known for producing rich milk
breed of dairy cattle from Jersey

Examples

The farmer raises Jersey cows because their milk is exceptionally rich in butterfat.
A herd of Jersey cattle grazed peacefully in the green pasture near the coast.
Many dairy producers prefer the Jersey breed for their gentle temperament and high-quality milk.

Etymology

Named after Jersey, the largest of the Channel Islands, where the breed was developed.

2. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɝːzi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːzi / (BrE)
the largest of the Channel Islands, situated in the English Channel off the coast of Normandy, France
the largest of the Channel Islands

Examples

He spent his summer holidays exploring Jersey with his family.
Many tourists are attracted to Jersey for its beautiful beaches and mild climate.
Jersey has its own government and a unique blend of British and French cultures.

Etymology

Named from Old Norse 'ey' (island), combined with a possible personal or descriptive element; matches the naming patterns of Channel Islands.
