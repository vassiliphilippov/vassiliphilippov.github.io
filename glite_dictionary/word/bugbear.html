bugbear

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌɡˌbɛr / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡˌbɛə / (BrE)
An imaginary creature or monster, especially one used to frighten children.
imaginary monster used to scare children

Examples

When she refused to eat her vegetables, her nanny warned that the bugbear would come for her at night.
Stories of the bugbear have been told for centuries to frighten misbehaving children.
The old folktale described a bugbear lurking in the dark woods outside the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bogeyman - an imaginary evil being or spirit used to frighten children

Etymology

From Middle English 'bugge' (an object of fear, a scarecrow) + 'bear' (the animal), originally referring to a goblin or hobgoblin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌɡˌbɛr / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡˌbɛə / (BrE)
A cause of obsessive fear, irritation, or anxiety; something that is strongly disliked or dreaded.
cause of fear or irritation

Examples

The issue of taxes has long been a bugbear for small business owners.
Spelling errors are a particular bugbear of mine when reading essays.
For many parents, screen time is the modern bugbear in family life.

Synset Examples

Germany was always a bugbear for France
A foolish consistency is the hobgoblin of little minds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hobgoblin - an object of mischievous fear or dread
  • Synonym: pet peeve - something that especially irritates a person

Etymology

From archaic 'bugbear', once meaning an imaginary hobgoblin used to frighten children. 'Bug' (specter) + 'bear'.
