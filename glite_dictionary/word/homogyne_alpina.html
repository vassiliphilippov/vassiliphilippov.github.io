Homogyne alpina

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈmɑːdʒaɪn ælˈpiːnə / (AmE), / hɒˈmɒdʒɪni ælˈpiːnə / (BrE)
A perennial herb with rhizomes and purple-red flowers, native to European mountain regions, often used as groundcover.
a European rhizomatous herb with purple-red flowers

Examples

Homogyne alpina is often found carpeting alpine meadows with its purple-red blooms.
Gardeners recommend planting Homogyne alpina in shaded rock gardens for reliable groundcover.
Botanists sometimes classify Homogyne alpina in the genus Tussilago due to similar characteristics.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name Homogyne, from Greek 'homos' (same) + 'gyne' (woman), and Latin 'alpina' (of the Alps).
