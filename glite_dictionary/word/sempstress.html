sempstress

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛmpstrəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛmpstrɪs / (BrE)
a woman who makes or mends dresses for a living
female dressmaker; seamstress
archaic

Examples

The sempstress spent hours stitching delicate patterns onto the bride’s wedding gown.
In Victorian England, a sempstress would often work long hours to provide for her family.
Having learned from her mother, she became a skilled sempstress in their small village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seamstress - a woman who sews, especially one who earns a living by sewing

Etymology

Alteration of 'seamstress', influenced by earlier 'sempster' (from Middle English 'semestre', from Old English 'sēamestre')
