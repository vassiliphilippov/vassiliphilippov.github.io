atonement

1. NOUN

/ əˈtoʊnmənt / (AmE), / əˈtəʊnmənt / (BrE)
the act of making amends or reparation for an offense, wrongdoing, or sin, especially to appease a deity
making amends for wrongdoing or sin

Examples

The annual ceremony was held as an act of atonement for past injustices.
In many religions, prayer and sacrifice are ways to seek atonement for one’s sins.
He offered a sincere apology as a form of atonement for his actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expiation - the act of making amends for guilt or wrongdoing
  • Synonym: reparation - the making of amends for a wrong
  • Antonym: sin - an immoral act considered a transgression against divine law

Etymology

From Middle English 'atonement', meaning 'at-one-ment', from the phrase 'at one' + '-ment'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈtoʊnmənt / (AmE), / əˈtəʊnmənt / (BrE)
the act of making amends for a wrong or injury; compensation or reparation for a fault or wrongdoing
compensation or reparation for a wrong

Examples

He gave money to charity as an act of atonement for his past mistakes.
The public apology was her attempt at atonement for lying to customers.
True atonement requires both remorse and a willingness to make things right.

Synset Examples

we were unable to get satisfaction from the local store

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reparation - making amends or repairing a wrong
  • Synonym: compensation - something given to make up for a wrong or injury
  • Synonym: penance - voluntary self-punishment for wrong done

Etymology

From Middle English 'atonement', from 'at one' + '-ment', originally meaning 'reconciliation'.
