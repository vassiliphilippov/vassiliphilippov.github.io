hanger

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋər / (AmE), / ˈhæŋə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to hang things, such as pictures, clothes, or curtains
person who hangs things for work

Examples

The art gallery hired a professional hanger to arrange and mount the paintings for the exhibition.
As a stage hanger, she was responsible for putting up curtains and backdrops before each show.
He found steady work as a picture hanger for interior designers and private collectors.

Etymology

From hang + -er, denoting a person who performs the action of hanging.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋɚ / (AmE), / ˈhæŋə / (BrE)
an object from which something can be hung, such as for keeping clothes or equipment suspended
object from which something is hung

Examples

She placed her coat on a hanger in the closet.
The shop sold wooden hangers for organizing scarves and belts.
A plant pot dangled from a ceiling hanger above the kitchen table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hook - a curved piece of metal or plastic used to hang things

Etymology

From hang + -er, denoting something that hangs or is used for hanging.
