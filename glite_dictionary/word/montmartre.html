Montmartre

1. PROPN

/ mɑnˈmɑrtrə / (AmE), / mɒ̃ˈmaːtʁ / (BrE)
A hill in the northern part of Paris, known as the highest point in the city and famous for its historic association with artists and bohemian culture.
hill in Paris famous for artists and bohemian history

Examples

Many painters of the late 19th century lived and worked in Montmartre.
The view from Montmartre offers a breathtaking panorama of Paris.
Tourists often visit Montmartre to see the Sacré-Cœur Basilica and the artists' square.

Etymology

From French Montmartre, meaning 'Mount of Martyrs'.
