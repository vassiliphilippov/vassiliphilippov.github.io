to accouter

1. VERB

/ əˈkuːtər / (AmE), / əˈkuːtə / (BrE)
to provide with equipment, especially military or ceremonial equipment
provide with equipment

Examples

The soldiers were accoutered with helmets, armor, and swords before the parade.
He carefully accoutered his men for the long journey ahead.
The new recruits were accoutered in standard-issue military uniforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to equip - to provide with necessary items for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: outfit - to supply with a set of clothes or equipment

Etymology

From French 'accoutrer' (to equip, fit out), based on 'couter' (to sew, prepare).
