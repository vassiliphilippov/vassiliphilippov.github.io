unanimously

1. ADV

/ juˈnænəməsli / (AmE), / juˈnænɪməsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows complete agreement by everyone involved; without dissent.
with complete agreement

Examples

The board voted unanimously to implement the new policy.
All the members unanimously agreed to postpone the meeting.
The resolution was unanimously adopted by the committee.

Synset Examples

the Senate unanimously approved the bill
we voted unanimously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unanimous - fully in agreement; of one mind

Etymology

From 'unanimous' (from Latin 'unanimus', from 'unus' (one) + 'animus' (mind, spirit)) plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
