granulose

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡræn.jəˌloʊs / (AmE), / ˈɡran.jʊ.ləʊs / (BrE)
composed of or covered with small particles or grains, resembling meal in texture or consistency
having a grainy or mealy texture

Examples

The sandpaper has a granulose surface, making it effective for smoothing rough wood.
Some types of starch under a microscope appear distinctly granulose in structure.
The archaeologist noted the granulose texture of the ancient pottery shards.

Synset Examples

granular sugar
the photographs were grainy and indistinct
it left a mealy residue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: granular - consisting of small grains or particles
  • Synonym: grainy - having a rough or uneven texture resembling grains
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface or consistency; opposite of grainy

Etymology

From Latin 'granulum' (small grain) + '-ose' (full of).
