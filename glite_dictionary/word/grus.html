Grus

1. PROPN

/ ɡruːs / (AmE), / ɡruːs / (BrE)
A small constellation in the southern sky, located near the constellation Phoenix.
constellation in the southern sky

Examples

The constellation Grus is best seen in the Southern Hemisphere during late spring.
Astronomers use Grus as a reference when locating nearby stars and galaxies.
The bright star Alnair marks the tail of Grus in the night sky.

Etymology

From Latin grus (crane), named after the bird.

type genus of typical cranes

Examples

The genus Grus includes several well-known crane species found across the world.
Ornithologists have studied the migratory patterns of birds within the Grus genus.

3. NOUN

/ ɡruːs / (AmE), / ɡruːs / (BrE)
A genus of large, long-legged and long-necked birds in the family Gruidae, comprising the typical cranes.
a genus of cranes (birds)

Examples

The whooping crane belongs to the genus Grus, a group known for their elaborate courtship dances.
Several species in the genus Grus are threatened by habitat loss and hunting.
Researchers study the migration patterns of birds in the genus Grus to understand their behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crane - large, long-legged, long-necked bird of the family Gruidae

Etymology

From Latin *grus* (crane), used as the scientific genus name for cranes.
