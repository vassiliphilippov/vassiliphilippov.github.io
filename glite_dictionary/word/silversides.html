silversides

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvərˌsaɪdz / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvəˌsaɪdz / (BrE)
a small, slender fish of North America with a silvery stripe along each side, commonly found in fresh or coastal waters
a small North American fish with silvery sides

Examples

The bay was teeming with silversides darting just beneath the water's surface.
Fishermen often use silversides as bait for larger freshwater fish.
We watched a school of silversides flash and scatter as a heron landed nearby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shiner - a small fish with a shiny, silvery appearance, often used as bait

Etymology

Named for the silvery stripe running along its sides.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvərˌsaɪdz / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvəˌsaɪdz / (BrE)
a small fish found chiefly along the Atlantic coast of North America, noted for the distinct silver stripe along each side of its body
small fish with silver side stripe

Examples

Schools of silversides darted through the shallow waters near the pier.
The biologist studied the feeding habits of several silversides collected from the estuary.
Many anglers use small nets to catch silversides for bait during the summer months.

Etymology

From silver + sides, referring to the fish's prominent stripes.
