tamarillo

1. NOUN

/ ˌtæməˈrɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˌtaməˈrɪləʊ / (BrE)
A small, egg-shaped edible fruit with reddish-brown skin, resembling a tomato, produced by a South American shrub; also, the shrub itself.
edible fruit and shrub from South America

Examples

She bought a ripe tamarillo at the farmers' market to add to her salad.
The garden features a tamarillo tree that produces fruit every summer.
The taste of tamarillo is both sweet and tangy, making it perfect for desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree tomato - an alternative name for the tamarillo

Etymology

Name of uncertain origin, popularized in New Zealand in the 20th century to distinguish it from the common tomato.
