obstacle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑb.stə.kəl / (AmE), / ˈɒb.stə.kl̩ / (BrE)
A thing that blocks one's way or prevents or hinders progress.
something blocking progress

Examples

She faced many obstacles on her journey to becoming a doctor.
Lack of money was a major obstacle to starting the new business.
They worked together to overcome every obstacle in their path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrier - a fence or other obstacle that prevents movement or access
  • Synonym: hindrance - a thing that provides resistance or delay
  • Antonym: aid - help or support that makes something easier
  • Synonym: impediment - something that makes progress difficult

Etymology

From Latin 'obstaculum' (barrier, hindrance), from 'ob-' (against) + 'stāre' (to stand).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑbstəkəl / (AmE), / ˈɒbstəkl / (BrE)
a thing that blocks one's way or prevents progress, especially something abstract such as a situation, feeling, or difficulty
something that blocks progress

Examples

Financial difficulties can be an obstacle to achieving your goals.
Lack of experience proved to be the biggest obstacle in her job search.
They worked together to overcome every obstacle in their path.

Synset Examples

lack of imagination is an obstacle to one's advancement
the poverty of a district is an obstacle to good education
the filibuster was a major obstruction to the success of their plan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrier - something that prevents movement or progress
  • Synonym: hindrance - a thing that provides resistance, delay, or obstruction
  • Synonym: impediment - a hindrance or obstruction in doing something
  • Antonym: aid - something that helps achieve progress

Etymology

From Latin 'obstaculum' meaning 'barrier, impediment', from 'ob-' (against) + 'stare' (to stand).
