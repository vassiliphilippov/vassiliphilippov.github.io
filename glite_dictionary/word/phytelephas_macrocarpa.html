Phytelephas macrocarpa

1. NOUN

/ faɪtəˈlɛfəs ˌmækrəˈkɑrpə / (AmE), / fɪˈtɛlɪfəs ˌmakrəˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
A species of stemless palm tree native to Brazil and Peru, known for producing vegetable ivory nuts.
stemless palm with ivory nuts

Examples

The nuts of the Phytelephas macrocarpa are used to make buttons and carvings.
Researchers studied the Phytelephas macrocarpa to find sustainable alternatives to animal ivory.
The Phytelephas macrocarpa grows in the rainforests of Brazil and Peru, thriving in moist conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vegetable ivory - a hard white seed material from certain palm trees used as substitute for ivory

Etymology

From Greek 'phyto-' (plant) + 'elephas' (elephant, referring to ivory-like nuts) and Latin 'macrocarpa' (large-fruited).
