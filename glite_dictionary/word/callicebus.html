Callicebus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈsiːbəs / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈsiːbəs / (BrE)
A genus of South American monkeys commonly called titi monkeys, known for their dense fur and long, bushy tails.
genus of titi monkeys

Examples

The Callicebus is found primarily in the forests of South America.
Scientists observed a family of Callicebus interacting quietly in the tree canopy.
Unlike other monkeys, Callicebus species are known for their strong pair bonds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titi monkey - common name for monkeys of the genus Callicebus

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Greek kallos (beauty) + kebos (a long-tailed monkey).
