telephone

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪfəʊn / (BrE)
A device that converts sound into electrical signals for transmission over distances and converts received signals back into sound, used for voice communication.
device for voice communication over distance

Examples

She answered the telephone on the second ring.
He left his telephone number on the note.
Using a telephone, people can communicate across long distances.

Synset Examples

I talked to him on the telephone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phone - shortened form of telephone; device for making calls

Etymology

From French téléphone, from Greek tēle (far) + phōnē (voice, sound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04408223-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪfəʊn / (BrE)
An electronic device used for transmitting and receiving speech over distances.
device for transmitting speech at a distance

Examples

He picked up the telephone and dialed her number.
The telephone rang just as I was leaving the house.
She spoke to her mother on the telephone every Sunday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phone - short form for telephone; device for transmitting speech
  • Degree Higher: cell phone - mobile telephone; portable wireless phone
  • Degree Lower: telegraph - device for transmitting written messages over distance

Etymology

From French téléphone, from Greek tēle (far) + phōnē (voice, sound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06282943-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈtɛləˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtelɪfəʊn / (BrE)
to communicate or attempt to communicate with someone by telephone
to contact by telephone

Examples

I will telephone you as soon as I arrive at the hotel.
She telephoned her parents every Sunday evening to check in.
Could you telephone the office and inform them that I am running late?

Synset Examples

I tried to call you all night
Take two aspirin and call me in the morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: call - to communicate with someone by telephone

Etymology

From French téléphoner, from télé- (far) + phoner (to speak), based on Greek roots.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00791110-v
Synset POS: v
