Alexander I of Russia

1. PROPN

/ ˌælɪgˈzændər ðə fɜrst əv ˈrʌʃə / (AmE), / ˌælɪgˈzandə ðə fɜːst əv ˈrʌʃə / (BrE)
the Emperor of Russia from 1801 to 1825, known for attempting reforms and for his role in defeating Napoleon
Emperor of Russia, 1801–1825

Examples

Alexander I of Russia played a decisive role in the defeat of Napoleon's army in 1812.
Many historians debate the significance of the reforms considered by Alexander I of Russia.
The reign of Alexander I of Russia marked a turning point in European politics.
