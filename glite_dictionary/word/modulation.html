modulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːdʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒdjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of changing or adjusting something, especially to achieve a particular effect or balance
the act of adjusting or changing something

Examples

The modulation of her voice captured the audience's attention.
Careful modulation of color is essential in landscape painting.
The lighting designer achieved the mood with subtle modulation of intensity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjustment - the process of making small changes to improve a result
  • Synonym: alteration - a change or modification

Etymology

From Latin modulatio (measured or musical manner)

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɑdʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒdjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a musical passage or process in which the key is changed from one to another
musical passage changing key

Examples

The modulation from C major to G major gave the piece an unexpected brightness.
During the symphony, there's a sudden modulation that adds excitement to the movement.
The composer employed a clever modulation to transition between sections of the work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: key change - musical transition from one key to another

Etymology

From Latin 'modulatio' (measured movement, melody), from 'modulari' (to measure, sing or play)

3. NOUN

/ ˌmɑdʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒdjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the rise and fall of pitch in the voice when speaking, often used to convey meaning or emotion
variation in voice pitch

Examples

Actors often use careful modulation of their voices to bring characters to life.
Good modulation helps a public speaker keep the audience engaged and attentive.
Her gentle modulation conveyed both confidence and warmth during the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intonation - the variation of pitch when speaking

Etymology

From Latin modulatio (measured manner, rhythm), from modulare (to measure, regulate).

4. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːdʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒdjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of varying the loudness, pitch, or tone of the voice while speaking
variation in voice pitch or tone

Examples

She impressed the audience with her careful modulation of tone during the speech.
Actors often train to improve their vocal modulation for stage performances.
A lack of modulation can make a speaker sound dull or monotonous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intonation - the rise and fall of the voice in speaking; often used synonymously with modulation
  • Antonym: monotone - a way of speaking in which the pitch, loudness, or tone does not vary

Etymology

From Latin 'modulatio' (rhythmical measure, melody), from 'modulari' (to measure, regulate).

5. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːdʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒdjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(Electronics) The process of transmitting a signal by varying a carrier wave's amplitude, frequency, or phase using the signal.
modifying a carrier wave to transmit a signal

Examples

Frequency modulation is widely used in radio broadcasting.
The engineer explained how modulation allows multiple signals to be transmitted over the same channel.
Understanding amplitude modulation is vital for students of telecommunications.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demodulation - the process of extracting the original information-bearing signal from a modulated carrier wave

Etymology

From Latin modulatio (measured rhythm), from modulare (to measure, regulate).
