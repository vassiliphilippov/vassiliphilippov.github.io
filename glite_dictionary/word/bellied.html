bellied

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛlid / (AmE), / ˈbɛlɪd / (BrE)
having a belly or a rounded, protruding part resembling a belly.
having a belly or rounded part

Examples

The old dog was noticeably bellied after eating too much.
Some fish are naturally bellied, giving them a distinctive shape.
The sculpture depicted a bellied figure, symbolizing wealth and prosperity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pot-bellied - having a large, protruding belly
  • Degree Lower: flat-bellied - having a flat belly

Etymology

From 'belly' (Old English *belig*) + -ed suffix.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɛlid / (AmE), / ˈbɛlid / (BrE)
Having a rounded or bulging shape; curving outward.
having a rounded or outward-curving shape

Examples

The old jug was bellied at the center, giving it a unique appearance.
They admired the bellied sails as they caught the strong wind.
Her sculpture featured a bellied form that resembled ancient pottery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potbellied - having a large, protruding belly
  • Antonym: flat - having a level or even surface; not curved outward

Etymology

Formed from 'belly' (Old English 'belig', bag or pouch) with the suffix '-ed'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01356466-s
Synset POS: s
