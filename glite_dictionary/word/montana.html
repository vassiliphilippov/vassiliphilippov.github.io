Montana

1. PROPN

/ mɑnˈtænə / (AmE), / mɒnˈtɑːnə / (BrE)
A state in the northwestern United States, bordering Canada, known for its diverse terrain from the Rocky Mountains to the Great Plains.
U.S. state in the northwest, bordering Canada

Examples

She moved to Montana to enjoy the wide-open spaces and stunning mountain views.
The winters in Montana can be harsh, but the landscapes are breathtaking.
Yellowstone National Park extends into the southern part of Montana.

Etymology

From the Spanish word 'montaña', meaning 'mountain'.
