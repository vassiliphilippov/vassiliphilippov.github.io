callus

1. VERB

/ ˈkæl.əs / (AmE), / ˈkæl.əs / (BrE)
(of skin or tissue) to form or develop a callus; to become hard and thickened in response to repeated friction, pressure, or injury.
to form a hard, thickened area of skin

Examples

After weeks of playing guitar, her fingers began to callus.
When exposed to constant friction, the heel may callus over time.
The gardener’s hands callused after years of outdoor work.

Synset Examples

His foot callused

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harden - become hard or harder
  • Antonym: to soften - become or make less hard

Etymology

From Latin callum (hard skin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.əs / (AmE), / ˈkæl.əs / (BrE)
a mass of bony tissue that forms at the site of a fractured bone as part of the healing process
bony tissue formed during bone healing
medical

Examples

After her arm was broken, an X-ray showed a callus developing around the fracture site.
The doctor explained that the callus would eventually be replaced by new bone tissue.
Formation of a callus is a crucial stage in the healing of broken bones.

Etymology

From Latin callum (hard skin).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæləs / (AmE), / ˈkæləs / (BrE)
(Botany) An isolated thickening of tissue, especially a stiff protuberance on the lip of an orchid.
botanical tissue thickening or protuberance

Examples

The botanist noted a prominent callus at the base of the orchid's lip.
Some orchids have a brightly colored callus that attracts pollinators.
Tiny calluses can be seen with a magnifying glass on the petal surfaces.

Etymology

From Latin 'callus' (hard skin, thickening).

5. NOUN

/ ˈkæləs / (AmE), / ˈkæləs / (BrE)
A hardened area of skin that has become thickened due to repeated pressure or friction, especially on the hands or feet.
thickened area of skin from friction

Examples

After weeks of hiking, a thick callus formed on his heel.
Guitar players often develop a callus on their fingertips from frequent practice.
She used a pumice stone to gently remove a callus from her foot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corn - a small, painful area of thickened skin, usually on the foot

Etymology

From Latin 'callus' or 'callum' meaning 'hard skin'.
