rulership

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːlərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈruːləʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or authority of a ruler; the act or period of ruling.
position or authority of a ruler

Examples

The king's rulership was marked by prosperity and peace throughout the land.
During her brief rulership, she enacted significant reforms.
Questions about the legitimacy of his rulership led to widespread unrest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leadership - the action of leading a group or organization
  • Antonym: subjection - the state of being under control; opposite of being the ruler

Etymology

Formed from 'ruler' + '-ship', a suffix denoting state or condition.
