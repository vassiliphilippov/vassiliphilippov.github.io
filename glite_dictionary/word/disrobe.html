to disrobe

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈroʊb / (AmE), / dɪsˈrəʊb / (BrE)
to remove one's clothes; to undress
to remove clothes; undress

Examples

He was asked to disrobe before the medical examination.
The actress had to disrobe for the final scene of the play.
Monks disrobe as a symbol of leaving their old life behind.

Synset Examples

please don't undress in front of everybody!
She strips in front of strangers every night for a living

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undress - to take off one's clothes
  • Antonym: dress - to put on clothes

Etymology

From Old French 'desrober', from 'des' (removal) + 'robe' (garment).
