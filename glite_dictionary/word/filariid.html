filariid

1. ADJ

/ fɪˈlɛriɪd / (AmE), / fɪˈlɑːrɪɪd / (BrE)
Relating to or belonging to the family Filariidae, a group of parasitic nematode worms.
of the family Filariidae (parasitic worms)
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with a filariid infection after reviewing the microscopic examination results.
Recent research focuses on controlling filariid parasites to prevent the spread of lymphatic filariasis.
Several filariid nematodes are known to be transmitted by mosquitoes in tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filarial - relating to, or caused by filariae (parasitic worms)

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Filariidae', the family name, from 'filaria' (thread-like worm) + '-id' (denoting belonging to a family).
