cajolery

1. NOUN

/ kəˈdʒoʊləri / (AmE), / kəˈdʒɒləri / (BrE)
the act of persuading someone by flattery or gentle urging
flattering persuasion

Examples

Despite her constant cajolery, he refused to lend her any more money.
He smiled, using cajolery to convince his friends to join the project.
No amount of cajolery could sway the judge's decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coaxing - gentle, persistent persuasion
  • Synonym: blandishment - flattering speech or actions used to persuade
  • Antonym: intimidation - the act of frightening someone to do something

Etymology

From French 'cajoler' (to cajole).
