compendious

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈpɛndiəs / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛndɪəs / (BrE)
containing or presenting the essential facts of something in a concise but comprehensive way
concise and comprehensive

Examples

The guide offers a compendious summary of the region's history.
She wrote a compendious overview for those who needed a quick understanding of the subject.
His explanation was remarkably compendious, covering every main point in just a few sentences.

Synset Examples

a short and compendious book
a compact style is brief and pithy
succinct comparisons
a summary formulation of a wide-ranging subject

Related Terms

  • Synonym: succinct - briefly and clearly expressed
  • Synonym: concise - giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words
  • Antonym: verbose - using more words than needed

Etymology

From Latin 'compendiosus' (advantageous, brief), from 'compendium' (a shortening).
