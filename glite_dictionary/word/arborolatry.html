arborolatry

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrbəˈlɑːtrɪ / (AmE), / ˌɑːbəˈlɒtrɪ / (BrE)
the worship of trees as deities or sacred objects
worship of trees

Examples

Some ancient cultures practiced arborolatry, believing trees possessed spiritual power.
The sacred oak grove was a center of arborolatry for the community.
The study describes rituals of arborolatry in pre-modern societies.

Etymology

From Latin arbor (tree) + -latry (worship), from Greek latreia (worship).
