pineweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪnˌwid / (AmE), / ˈpaɪnˌwiːd / (BrE)
A wiry-stemmed annual plant native to North America, with tiny scalelike leaves and small yellow flowers.
North American weed with wiry stems and yellow flowers

Examples

The dry field was thick with pineweed near the end of summer.
Botanists surveyed the meadow and identified several clusters of pineweed.
Gardeners often remove pineweed to prevent it from spreading among their flowers.
