flerovium

1. NOUN

/ flɪˈroʊviəm / (AmE), / flɪəˈrəʊviəm / (BrE)
a radioactive chemical element with the symbol Fl and atomic number 114; a synthetic transuranic element
a radioactive synthetic chemical element (atomic number 114)

Examples

The discovery of flerovium expanded our knowledge of superheavy elements.
Scientists synthesized flerovium in a laboratory by bombarding plutonium with calcium ions.
Because flerovium is highly unstable, it rapidly decays into lighter elements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: element 114 - another name for flerovium, based on its atomic number
  • Degree Higher: moscovium - radioactive element with atomic number 115, next to flerovium in the periodic table
  • Degree Lower: copernicium - radioactive element with atomic number 112, just before flerovium in the periodic table

Etymology

Named after the Flerov Laboratory of Nuclear Reactions in Russia, itself named after Soviet physicist Georgy Flyorov.
