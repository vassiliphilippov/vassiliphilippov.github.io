to condemn

1. VERB

/ kənˈdɛm / (AmE), / kənˈdɛm / (BrE)
to express very strong disapproval of; to criticize or denounce, often publicly
express strong disapproval

Examples

The mayor did not hesitate to condemn the violence at the protest.
Many citizens publicly condemned the company's unethical practices.
Environmental groups are condemning the government's decision to cut funding for renewable energy projects.

Synset Examples

We condemn the racism in South Africa
These ideas were reprobated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to denounce - to publicly declare something to be wrong or evil
  • Antonym: to approve - to officially agree to or accept as satisfactory

Etymology

From Old French *condemner*, from Latin *condemnare* 'to sentence, blame'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00865823-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kənˈdɛm / (AmE), / kənˈdɛm / (BrE)
to demonstrate or prove the guilt of someone, often by their actions or behavior
demonstrate someone's guilt

Examples

Her strange behavior finally condemned her in the eyes of everyone.
The evidence condemned him beyond all reasonable doubt.
His absence from the scene condemned him more than any words could have.

Synset Examples

Her strange behavior condemned her

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to acquit - to declare someone not guilty
  • Synonym: convict - to declare someone guilty, especially in a court of law

Etymology

From Latin 'condemnare', from 'damnare' (to sentence), influenced by 'con-' (intensive prefix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02153732-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kənˈdɛm / (AmE), / kənˈdɛm / (BrE)
to force someone to experience an unpleasant state or activity, especially something undesirable or inescapable
force into an unpleasant state or activity

Examples

Her injury condemned her to months of painful rehabilitation.
He felt that poverty condemned many children to lives of hardship.
The war condemned thousands to long years of exile.

Synset Examples

His devotion to his sick wife condemned him to a lonely existence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doom - to make certain that something unpleasant will happen

Etymology

From Latin 'condemnare', from 'damnare' (to harm, condemn).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02512742-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ kənˈdɛm / (AmE), / kənˈdɛm / (BrE)
(of a court or authority) to state officially that someone is to receive a specific punishment, especially after being found guilty of a crime.
pronounce punishment in court

Examples

The judge condemned the defendant to life imprisonment for his crimes.
He was condemned in court after a lengthy trial.
The tribunal condemned several officers for war crimes at the end of the conflict.

Synset Examples

He was condemned to ten years in prison

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to acquit - to officially declare someone not guilty of a crime

Etymology

From Old French condamner, from Latin condemnare (to sentence, blame), from com- (intensive prefix) + damnare (to inflict loss upon).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00908688-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ kənˈdɛm / (AmE), / kənˈdɛm / (BrE)
(of an authority) to officially take private property for public use, typically by means of legal proceedings.
to officially take property for public use

Examples

The government decided to condemn the land in order to build a new school.
After months of negotiation, the city condemned the building to expand the public park.
If authorities condemn your property, you may be entitled to compensation.

Synset Examples

the county condemned the land to build a highway

Etymology

From Latin *condemnare* (to sentence, condemn).

6. VERB

/ kənˈdɛm / (AmE), / kənˈdɛm / (BrE)
to declare officially that something, especially a building, is unfit for use or habitation
to declare unfit for use or habitation

Examples

After the earthquake, the city condemned several older apartment blocks that were found to be unsafe.
Due to severe structural damage, the authorities had no choice but to condemn the warehouse.
The inspector condemned the restaurant for multiple health code violations.

Synset Examples

The building was condemned by the inspector

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to declare - to state officially
  • Antonym: to approve - to officially accept as suitable or safe

Etymology

From Latin *condemnare* (to sentence, convict), from *damnare* (to inflict loss upon).
