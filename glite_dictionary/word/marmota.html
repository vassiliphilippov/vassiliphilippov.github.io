Marmota

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈmoʊtə / (AmE), / mɑːˈməʊtə / (BrE)
A genus of large ground-dwelling squirrels, comprising the marmots, found in North America and Eurasia.
genus of ground squirrels (marmots)

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Marmota in the remote mountains.
Members of the Marmota genus are well known for their burrowing habits and hibernation.
The Alpine marmot, a species of Marmota, is native to mountainous regions of Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marmot - a large ground squirrel in the genus Marmota

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Latin marmota (marmot).
