Nuremberg

1. PROPN

/ ˈnʊrəmˌbɜrɡ / (AmE), / ˈnjʊərəmˌbɜːɡ / (BrE)
a city in southeastern Germany, known for its historical significance and as the site of the Allied trials of Nazi war criminals in 1945–46
city in southeastern Germany; site of war crimes trials

Examples

The historic city of Nuremberg attracts thousands of visitors each year.
The Nuremberg Trials marked a turning point in international law.
He visited Nuremberg to learn more about its role during World War II.

Etymology

From German Nürnberg, the city's name in German.
