reveille

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛvəli / (AmE), / ˈrɛvɪli / (BrE)
a signal, especially one played on a bugle or trumpet, used in the military to wake personnel at sunrise
military wake-up signal

Examples

The sound of the reveille echoed across the camp just before dawn.
Every morning, soldiers know to rise immediately when the reveille is played.
He was late leaving his barracks after sleeping through the reveille.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tattoo - a military signal sounded in the evening, typically to mark the end of the day's activities

Etymology

From French *réveille* (wake up!), from the verb *réveiller* (to wake up).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛvəli / (AmE), / ˈrɛvɪli / (BrE)
A signal, especially a bugle call, given to wake military personnel at sunrise.
bugle call to wake up in the military

Examples

The soldiers woke to the sound of reveille echoing across the camp.
Minutes after reveille, everyone had assembled for morning inspection.
She learned to associate the cheerful notes of reveille with the start of a new day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tattoo - evening drum or bugle signal in the military

Etymology

From French 'réveil' (awakening), from 'réveiller' (to wake up).
