deathwatch

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛθˌwɑtʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɛθˌwɒtʃ / (BrE)
a minute wingless insect that damages books and papers, belonging to a group of woodboring or paper-consuming insects
insect that damages books or papers

Examples

Old manuscripts were found to be infested with deathwatch insects.
Library staff must take preventive measures against deathwatch to preserve rare books.
Deathwatch pests can silently destroy paper archives over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booklouse - a small insect that feeds on the glue and paper of books

Etymology

From 'death' + 'watch', originally referring to superstitions about the ticking noise made by certain beetles, extended to similar insects.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛθˌwɑtʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɛθˌwɒtʃ / (BrE)
a small beetle that bores into wood and makes a ticking sound, once popularly believed to predict death
wood-boring beetle believed to predict death

Examples

Late at night, the deathwatch could be heard ticking in the old wooden beams.
Superstition once held that the sound made by a deathwatch foretold someone's passing.
Entomologists study the deathwatch to better understand its unique behavior.

Etymology

From the superstition that the ticking sound made by the beetle in wooden walls was an omen of impending death.
