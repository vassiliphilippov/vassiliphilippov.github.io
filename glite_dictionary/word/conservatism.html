conservatism

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɝː.vəˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / kənˈsɜː.vəˌtɪz.əm / (BrE)
the political or social philosophy that promotes retaining traditional institutions, values, and practices, and is resistant to rapid or radical change
preference for traditional values and gradual change

Examples

Many voters support conservatism because they value stability and tradition in government.
Some critics argue that extreme conservatism can slow necessary social progress.
Her essays discuss the influence of conservatism in 19th-century European politics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: progressivism - the political philosophy advocating reform and progressive change
  • Synonym: traditionalism - emphasis on preserving established customs or values

Etymology

From conservative + -ism, after French conservatisme.
