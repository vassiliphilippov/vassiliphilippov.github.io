Mandevilla boliviensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmændəˈvɪliə boʊˌlɪviˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmændɪˈvɪliə bəʊˌlɪviˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A shrubby climbing plant with glossy leaves and white funnel-shaped flowers with yellow throats, native to South America and commonly grown as an ornamental.
species of flowering climbing plant

Examples

The Mandevilla boliviensis bloomed beautifully on the garden trellis all summer long.
Many gardeners prefer Mandevilla boliviensis for its showy white flowers and vigorous growth.
With proper care, Mandevilla boliviensis can be trained to climb fences or cover walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mandevilla - genus of tropical and subtropical flowering vines

Etymology

From the genus name Mandevilla, named after Henry Mandeville, a British diplomat and gardener, and boliviensis denoting its origin from Bolivia.
