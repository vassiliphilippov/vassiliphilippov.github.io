crabapple

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræbˌæpəl / (AmE), / ˈkræbˌapəl / (BrE)
a small, usually wild apple tree that bears small, tart or acidic fruit; the fruit of such a tree
small wild apple tree or its fruit

Examples

The branches of the crabapple tree were covered in pink blossoms this spring.
She made jelly using the small, sour crabapples from her backyard.
Many gardens feature crabapple trees for their decorative flowers and colorful fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple - a round fruit with red, green, or yellow skin and a crisp flesh

Etymology

From 'crab' (old word for wild fruit) + 'apple'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkræbˌæpəl / (AmE), / ˈkræbˌæpəl / (BrE)
a small, sour apple, typically used for making preserves and jellies rather than eating raw
small sour apple used for preserves

Examples

I used a dozen crabapples to make a tart and flavorful jelly.
The old tree in the yard produces small, sour crabapples every autumn.
Birds and squirrels love to eat the fallen crabapples during the winter.

Synset Examples

crabapples make a tangy jelly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple - a round fruit with red, green, or yellow skin and firm white flesh

Etymology

From crab (the crab tree) + apple; 'crab' may derive from Old Norse 'skrabba' (a kind of wild fruit).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkræbˌæpəl / (AmE), / ˈkræbˌapəl / (BrE)
A small, sour fruit produced by various species of wild or cultivated apple trees, often grown for their ornamental blossoms or for making preserves.
small sour fruit; ornamental apple tree

Examples

The old tree in the yard produces hundreds of crabapples every autumn.
She made a delicious jelly from fresh crabapples picked in the orchard.
In spring, the crabapple blossoms create a brilliant display of color.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: apple - the round fruit of a tree of the rose family, with crisp flesh and red, green, or yellow skin

Etymology

Formed from 'crab' (meaning sour wild apple) + 'apple'.
