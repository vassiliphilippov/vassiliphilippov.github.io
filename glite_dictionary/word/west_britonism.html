West Britonism

1. NOUN

/ wɛst ˈbrɪtənɪzəm / (AmE), / wɛst ˈbrɪtənɪzəm / (BrE)
the cultural identity, traditions, or attitudes associated with West Britons, particularly those from the western regions of Britain such as Cornwall and Wales
cultural identity of West Britons

Examples

West Britonism has influenced the preservation of traditional languages in Cornwall and Wales.
Many festivals in the region celebrate West Britonism and its unique heritage.
The exhibit explores the evolution of West Britonism through art and literature.

Synset Examples

West Britonism plays a significant role in community identity.
He advocates for West Britonism in cultural discussions.

Etymology

Formed from 'West Briton' (referring to people from western Britain) and the suffix '-ism', denoting a distinctive practice or ideology.
