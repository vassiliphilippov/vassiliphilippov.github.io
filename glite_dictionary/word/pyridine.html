pyridine

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪr.ɪˌdin / (AmE), / ˈpɪr.ɪ.diːn / (BrE)
a toxic, colorless, flammable liquid organic compound with a disagreeable odor, used in chemical synthesis and often obtained from coal tar
a toxic, colorless liquid chemical compound

Examples

Laboratory workers must handle pyridine with care due to its toxic fumes.
The synthesis of certain pharmaceuticals requires the use of pyridine as a solvent.
Coal tar is a common commercial source for the extraction of pyridine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quinoline - a colorless, oily, heterocyclic organic compound used in dyes and medicines

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire), referencing its derivation from coal tar.
