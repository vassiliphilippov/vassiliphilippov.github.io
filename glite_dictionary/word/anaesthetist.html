anaesthetist

1. NOUN

/ əˈnisθətɪst / (AmE), / əˈniːsθətɪst / (BrE)
a medical specialist who administers anaesthetics to patients undergoing surgical or other medical procedures
medical specialist administering anaesthetics
medical british

Examples

The anaesthetist carefully monitored the patient's vital signs during the operation.
Before surgery, the anaesthetist explained the steps of the procedure to the family.
Every hospital requires a skilled anaesthetist as part of the surgical team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anesthesiologist - a medical doctor specializing in anaesthesia (primarily US term)

Etymology

From Greek 'anaisthetos' (without sensation) via modern Latin and French.
