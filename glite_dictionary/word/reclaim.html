to reclaim

1. VERB

/ rɪˈkleɪm / (AmE), / rɪˈkleɪm / (BrE)
to retrieve or recover (something previously lost, given, or paid); to claim back
to get something back; recover

Examples

She was determined to reclaim her lost luggage from the airline.
The organization wants to reclaim public land that has been abandoned for years.
After quitting his job, he tried to reclaim some sense of purpose in his life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recover - to get back something lost or taken
  • Antonym: forfeit - to lose or give up something as a penalty

Etymology

From Old French *reclamer* ‘to call back’, from Latin *reclamare* ‘cry out against’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02281167-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈkleɪm / (AmE), / rɪˈkleɪm / (BrE)
to make land or property usable again by improving or restoring it, often from a state of neglect or disuse
restore land or property to use

Examples

The city launched a project to reclaim the abandoned railway yard and turn it into a park.
Farmers worked together to reclaim the flood-damaged fields for agriculture.
Conservationists hope to reclaim parts of the wetland for native wildlife habitats.

Synset Examples

The people reclaimed the marshes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restore - to bring back to a former, original, or normal condition
  • Synonym: to renovate - to make something like new again; repair

Etymology

From Old French *reclamer* (to call back), from Latin *reclamare* (to cry out against, protest).

Examples
