inerrable

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɛrəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛrəbəl / (BrE)
Capable of being without error; not liable to make mistakes.
capable of being without error

Examples

Despite his reputation as inerrable, even the greatest chess grandmasters can make mistakes.
The doctrine holds that holy texts are inerrable in matters of faith and morals.
His scientific calculations were praised for being almost inerrable.

Synset Examples

the Church was … theoretically inerrant and omnicompetent
lack an inerrant literary sense
an unerring marksman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inerrant - incapable of being wrong; never making mistakes
  • Antonym: fallible - capable of making mistakes or being wrong

Etymology

From Latin inerrabilis, from in- (not) + errabilis (liable to err).
