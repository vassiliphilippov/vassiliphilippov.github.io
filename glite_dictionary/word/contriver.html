contriver

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtraɪvər / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪvə / (BrE)
a person who devises plans or schemes, often in a clever or resourceful way
a person who makes plans or schemes

Examples

The ingenious contriver of the machine received praise from her peers.
He was known as a skilled contriver of practical jokes in the office.
Every successful business needs at least one creative contriver behind its strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schemer - a person who forms secret or devious plans
  • Synonym: plotter - someone who secretly plans something illegal or harmful

Etymology

From Middle English 'contriven,' from Anglo-Norman 'controver' (to plot, plan).
