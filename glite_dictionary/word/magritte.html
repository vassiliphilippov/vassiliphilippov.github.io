Magritte

1. PROPN

/ məˈɡrit / (AmE), / maˈɡriːt / (BrE)
René Magritte (1898–1967), Belgian surrealist painter known for his witty and thought-provoking images that challenge observers’ perceptions of reality.
Belgian surrealist painter

Examples

The museum featured an exhibition of Magritte's most iconic works.
Many artists have been inspired by the surreal imagery of Magritte.
A reproduction of a famous Magritte painting hung in his study.

Etymology

From the surname of René Magritte, Belgian artist.
