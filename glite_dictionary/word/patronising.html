patronising

1. ADJ

/ ˈpætɹəˌnaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpætrənaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
(of behavior or attitude) showing that you think you are more intelligent or important than other people; treating others with apparent kindness which betrays a feeling of superiority.
displaying condescension or superiority

Examples

She always uses a patronising tone when explaining things to me.
His patronising smile made everyone in the room uncomfortable.
The teacher's comments were unintentionally patronising to her students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condescending - having or showing a feeling of patronizing superiority
  • Antonym: respectful - showing respect or deference

Etymology

From 'patronize' + '-ing', ultimately from Latin 'patronus' (protector, master).
