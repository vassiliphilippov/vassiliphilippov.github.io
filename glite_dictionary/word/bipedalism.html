bipedalism

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈpɛdəlɪzəm / (AmE), / baɪˈpiːdəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the condition or fact of walking on two legs or feet
the state of walking on two legs

Examples

The development of bipedalism was a major milestone in human evolution.
Some dinosaurs adapted bipedalism to run faster and free their forelimbs.
Observing bipedalism in primates can provide insights into our own ancestry.

Synset Examples

bipedalism made the human form of birth possible

Related Terms

  • Antonym: quadrupedalism - the state of walking on four legs
  • Synonym: bipedal - using only two legs for walking

Etymology

From 'bipedal' + '-ism'; 'bipedal' from Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'pedalis' (of the foot).
