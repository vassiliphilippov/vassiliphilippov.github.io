through

1. ADV

/ θru / (AmE), / θruː / (BrE)
completely; throughout the whole extent or thoroughly.
completely; throughout the entirety

Examples

The cake wasn't baked through and was still raw in the middle.
The walls were damp through after the flooding.

6. ADJ

/ θru / (AmE), / θruː / (BrE)
(of a route, vehicle, or service) going from the starting point to the destination without stopping or requiring changes.
without stops or changes (transport)

Examples

We took a through train from Boston to New York, so we didn't have to change trains.
The through bus to the airport leaves every hour and doesn't make any stops along the way.
Because it's a through street, traffic flows smoothly without interruption.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direct - (of transport) without intermediate stops or changes
  • Antonym: local - (of transport) making many stops or requiring changes
