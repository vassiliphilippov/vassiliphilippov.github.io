damaging

1. ADJ

/ ˈdæm.ɪdʒ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdam.ɪdʒ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
intended or likely to harm someone's reputation, credibility, or interests, especially in a way that is discrediting or critical without offering constructive suggestions.
causing harm to reputation or interests

Examples

Her damaging remarks during the meeting threatened her colleague's reputation.
The article contained several damaging allegations that could discredit the candidate.
Unfounded accusations can have a damaging impact on a person's career.

Synset Examples

negative criticism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destructive - causing great and irreparable harm or damage
  • Synonym: harmful - causing or capable of causing harm
  • Antonym: helpful - providing assistance or benefit

Etymology

Present participle of 'damage', from Old French 'damager', from Latin 'damnum' (loss, hurt).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdæmɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdæmɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
causing harm or injury; likely to have a negative effect on someone or something
causing harm or injury

Examples

The company denied making any damaging statements about its competitor.
Exposure to excessive sunlight can be damaging to your skin.
The leaked documents had a damaging impact on the government's reputation.

Synset Examples

damaging to career and reputation
the reporter's coverage resulted in prejudicial publicity for the defendant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmful - causing or likely to cause harm
  • Antonym: innocuous - not harmful or offensive

Etymology

From the verb 'damage' + -ing, from Old French 'damager', from Latin 'damnum' (loss, harm).
