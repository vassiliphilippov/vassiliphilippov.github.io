to humidify

1. VERB

/ hjuˈmɪdəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / hjuːˈmɪdɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to add moisture to (the air or a substance) and make it more humid
make more humid; add moisture

Examples

During winter, we often humidify the rooms to prevent the air from becoming too dry.
It is important to humidify greenhouses to help plants thrive in dry climates.
A special device can humidify your home and provide relief for dry skin.

Synset Examples

We have a machine that humidifies the air in the house

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to dehumidify - to remove moisture from; make less humid

Etymology

From Latin humidus (moist) + -fy (to make).
