Phaseolus lunatus

1. NOUN

/ fəˈziːoʊləs luːˈneɪtəs / (AmE), / fəˈziːələs luːˈneɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of bush bean plant, cultivated especially in the southern United States, with small flat edible seeds, commonly known as the lima bean.
species of bean plant (lima bean)

Examples

The farmer planted Phaseolus lunatus in his fields to harvest lima beans in the summer.
Many traditional Southern dishes use Phaseolus lunatus as a primary ingredient.
Botanists often refer to Phaseolus lunatus when describing the characteristics of lima bean plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lima bean - the edible seed or plant of Phaseolus lunatus

Etymology

From Latin: 'phaseolus' (bean) and 'lunatus' (crescent-shaped).
