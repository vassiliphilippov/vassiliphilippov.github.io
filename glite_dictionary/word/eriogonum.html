Eriogonum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛriˈɑɡənəm / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈɒɡənəm / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the buckwheat family, characterized by small clustered flowers, commonly known as wild buckwheats.
genus of plants with small clustered flowers

Examples

The California hills are dotted with Eriogonum during the summer months.
Botanists discovered a new species of Eriogonum in the desert region.
Many insects are attracted to the clustered flowers of Eriogonum.

Etymology

From Greek erion (wool) + gony (knee or joint), referring to the woolly joints of some species.
