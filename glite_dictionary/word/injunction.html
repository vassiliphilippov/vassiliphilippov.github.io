injunction

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
(Law) An official order from a court that legally prevents someone from doing something.
court order preventing an action
legal

Examples

The court issued an injunction to stop the company from polluting the river.
She sought a temporary injunction to prevent the publication of the confidential report.
Violating the terms of the injunction can result in serious legal penalties.

Synset Examples

injunctions were formerly obtained by writ but now by a judicial order

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - an authoritative command or instruction
  • Synonym: prohibition - the action of forbidding something, especially by law

Etymology

From Latin 'injunctio' (a commanding), from 'injungere' (to enjoin, to order).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
an authoritative command or order; a directive issued with formal authority
authoritative command or order

Examples

The teacher issued a clear injunction to remain silent during the test.
His parents' injunction to always tell the truth stayed with him throughout his life.
The committee acted in response to the chairman's injunction against releasing confidential information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - an authoritative command or directive
  • Antonym: prohibition - an official act of forbidding or disallowing something

Etymology

From Latin 'injunctio' (a joining, a command), from 'injungere' (to enjoin).
