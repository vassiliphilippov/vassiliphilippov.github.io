incitive

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsaɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
tending to arouse strong feelings, action, or rebellion; provocative or stimulating.
arousing action or rebellion

Examples

The speaker's incitive words stirred the crowd to demand change.
Some social media posts can be quite incitive and lead to heated debates.
Her incitive attitude inspired her teammates to push beyond their limits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provocative - causing annoyance, anger, or another strong reaction
  • Synonym: incendiary - designed to cause fires or inflame passions
  • Antonym: calming - reducing excitement or agitation

Etymology

From Latin incitāre (to urge on, to incite) via Medieval Latin incitativus.
