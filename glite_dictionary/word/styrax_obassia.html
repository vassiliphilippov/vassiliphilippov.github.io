Styrax obassia

1. NOUN

/ ˈstaɪræks oʊˈbæsiə / (AmE), / ˈstaɪræks əʊˈbæsɪə / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to Japan, known for its fragrant white flowers and broad leaves.
Japanese small flowering tree

Examples

The garden features a beautiful Styrax obassia in full bloom near the pond.
Botanists are studying the unique floral scent of Styrax obassia for potential uses in perfumery.
Many Japanese parks plant Styrax obassia for its attractive flowers and shade.

Etymology

From genus name 'Styrax' (Latin, from Greek 'stúrax') and species name 'obassia', derived from Japanese botanical classification.
