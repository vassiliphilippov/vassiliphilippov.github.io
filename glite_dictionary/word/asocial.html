asocial

1. ADJ

/ əˈsoʊʃəl / (AmE), / əˈsəʊʃl / (BrE)
Not interested in or actively hostile to social interaction or norms; disruptive or disregarding of normal social standards.
hostile to social norms or interaction

Examples

His asocial tendencies made it hard for him to maintain friendships at work.
The judge noted that the defendant's asocial behavior posed a risk to the community.
Children sometimes display asocial actions when they feel excluded or threatened.

Synset Examples

criminal behavior or conduct that violates the rights of other individuals is antisocial
crimes … and other asocial behavior
an antisocial deed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antisocial - actively opposed to social order or the interests of society

Etymology

From Latin 'a-' (not) + 'socialis' (of companionship).

2. ADJ

/ əˈsoʊʃəl / (AmE), / əˈsəʊʃl / (BrE)
tending to avoid the company of others; not sociable.
avoiding social contact; not sociable

Examples

Some species, like brown bears, are known to be asocial most of their lives.
He was often labeled asocial because he preferred reading alone over joining group activities.
Her asocial tendencies made it difficult for her to attend large parties.

Synset Examples

bears are asocial secretive animals
are you asocial or do you just enjoy living in the Antarctic?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antisocial - actively hostile to social order or society
  • Antonym: sociable - enjoying the company of others

Etymology

From Latin 'a-' (not) + 'socialis' (sociable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02257975-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ eɪˈsoʊʃəl / (AmE), / eɪˈsəʊʃəl / (BrE)
not enjoying or seeking the company of others; preferring to be alone rather than with people
not social; prefers solitude

Examples

She is often described as asocial because she rarely attends group events.
His asocial behavior made it difficult for him to make friends at school.
Rather than feeling lonely, he enjoys his asocial lifestyle.

Synset Examples

He prefers to stay home; he's quite asocial.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antisocial - averse or harmful to society or others
  • Antonym: social - enjoying being with others; sociable

Etymology

From prefix 'a-' meaning 'not' + 'social'.
