Pennines

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɛnaɪnz / (AmE), / ˈpɛnaɪnz / (BrE)
a range of hills in northern England, running from the Scottish border south to the River Trent, forming the main watershed of northern England
range of hills in northern England
british

Examples

The Pennines are often called the 'backbone of England' due to their central location.
Many hikers enjoy exploring the beautiful landscapes of the Pennines.
Rivers such as the Tyne and the Trent originate in the Pennines.

Etymology

From the Latinized form 'Pennini Montes,' a name of uncertain origin, possibly pre-Celtic.
