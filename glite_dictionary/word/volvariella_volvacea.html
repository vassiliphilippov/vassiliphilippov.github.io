Volvariella volvacea

1. NOUN

/ voʊlˌvɛriˈɛlə voʊlˈveɪsɪə / (AmE), / vɒlˌvɛəriˈɛlə vɒlˈveɪsɪə / (BrE)
A small, edible mushroom with a white cap and long stem, widely cultivated as a delicacy in China and other Asian countries.
an edible mushroom cultivated in Asia

Examples

Volvariella volvacea is often called the straw mushroom and is prized in Asian cuisine.
Chefs recommend adding Volvariella volvacea to soups for its delicate texture and flavor.
Cultivation of Volvariella volvacea is widespread in tropical regions due to its culinary value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straw mushroom - common name for Volvariella volvacea, an edible mushroom

Etymology

From the scientific Latin name, with 'Volvariella' referring to a genus of fungi distinguished by a volva (cup-like structure), and 'volvacea' from Latin 'volva' (sheath, covering).
