lungi

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʊŋɡi / (AmE), / ˈlʌŋɡi / (BrE)
A long piece of brightly colored cotton or silk cloth, worn as a skirt, sash, or loincloth, especially by men in India, Pakistan, and Burma (Myanmar).
traditional South Asian garment (cloth wrap)

Examples

During the festival, many men wore a traditional lungi with colorful patterns.
He wrapped the lungi around his waist before heading out into the humid morning.
In markets across South Asia, you will find lungis in countless colors and designs.

Etymology

From Hindi लुंगी (lungī) and Urdu لنگی‎ (lungī), originally from Bengali.
