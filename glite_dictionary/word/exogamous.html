exogamous

1. ADJ

/ ɛkˈsɑɡəməs / (AmE), / ɛkˈsɒɡəməs / (BrE)
Relating to or suited for fertilization, marriage, or union with an individual, group, or organism that is not closely related or from outside a given group.
pertaining to union with outsiders

Examples

The tribe follows an exogamous tradition, requiring members to marry outside their own clan.
Many plants have evolved exogamous systems to encourage genetic diversity.
Scientists studied the reproductive benefits of exogamous pollination in flowering species.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endogamous - relating to marriage or union within a specific group

Etymology

From Greek exō (outside) + -gamos (marriage).

2. ADJ

/ ɛkˈsɑː.ɡə.məs / (AmE), / ɛkˈsɒ.ɡə.məs / (BrE)
Relating to or involving the custom of marrying outside one's own social group, clan, or tribe.
marrying outside one's group

Examples

The tribe was traditionally exogamous, requiring its members to marry outsiders.
Anthropologists studied the impact of exogamous marriages on cultural exchange.
In some societies, exogamous practices help prevent inbreeding and strengthen alliances.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endogamous - relating to marrying within one's group

Etymology

From Greek exō (outside) + γαμέω (to marry), via French exogamie.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00874563-a
Synset POS: a
