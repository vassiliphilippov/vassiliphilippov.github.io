chukker

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃʌkər / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌkə / (BrE)
(in polo) one of the periods into which a polo match is divided, typically lasting seven and a half minutes, with a match usually consisting of six chukkers.
period in a polo match

Examples

Each chukker in polo lasts about seven and a half minutes.
By the final chukker, both teams were exhausted but determined to win.
She scored two goals in the second chukker, putting her team ahead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: period - a division of time in a sporting event or activity

Etymology

From Hindi *chakkar* (circle, turn).
