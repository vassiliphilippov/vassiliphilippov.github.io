Anne Brontë

1. PROPN

/ æn ˈbrɒnti / (AmE), / æn ˈbrɒnti / (BrE)
(1820–1849) English novelist and poet, youngest of the three Brontë sisters, known for works such as 'Agnes Grey' and 'The Tenant of Wildfell Hall.'
English novelist, one of the Brontë sisters

Examples

Anne Brontë is remembered for her powerful feminist themes in her novels.
Many literary critics praise the realism found in Anne Brontë's writing.
The works of Anne Brontë continue to be studied in literature courses around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Charlotte Brontë - English novelist, eldest Brontë sister, author of 'Jane Eyre'
  • Synonym: Emily Brontë - English novelist and poet, middle Brontë sister, author of 'Wuthering Heights'

Etymology

Brontë is of Irish origin, a surname adopted by her father Patrick Brontë.
