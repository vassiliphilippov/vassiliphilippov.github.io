Antigonus Cyclops

1. PROPN

/ ænˈtɪɡənəs ˈsaɪklɒps / (AmE), / ænˈtɪɡənəs ˈsaɪklɒps / (BrE)
a general under Alexander the Great, king of Macedonia, who lost one eye and was killed in the Battle of Ipsus (382–301 BC).
Macedonian general and king; lost one eye

Examples

Antigonus Cyclops played a key role in the wars following Alexander the Great's death.
The reign of Antigonus Cyclops ended at the Battle of Ipsus.
Historians often discuss the military strategies of Antigonus Cyclops in their works.

Etymology

From Greek Ἀντίγονος (Antigonos) and κύκλωψ (kyklōps, meaning 'circle-eyed' or 'cyclops').
