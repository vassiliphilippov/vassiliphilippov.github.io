contortion

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɔɹʃən / (AmE), / kənˈtɔːʃən / (BrE)
the act of twisting or bending something out of its normal shape, especially in an unusual or difficult way
the act of twisting or deforming

Examples

The gymnast’s extraordinary contortion amazed the audience during her performance.
He made a contortion of his face to show his disapproval.
The metal rod showed visible contortion after being subjected to extreme heat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twist - the act of turning or bending something
  • Synonym: distortion - the act of changing from a true, natural, or original state or meaning

Etymology

From Latin contortio (a twisting, twisting together), from contorquere (to twist together, wrench).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈtɔrʃən / (AmE), / kənˈtɔːʃən / (BrE)
a twisted or deformed shape or position, especially one resulting from being bent or twisted out of normal form
a twisted or distorted shape or posture

Examples

The dancer's contortion of her body captivated the entire audience.
The sculpture depicted a face frozen in a horrible contortion of pain.
After the accident, the metal beam was left in a twisted contortion on the ground.

Synset Examples

they built a tree house in the tortuosities of its boughs
the acrobat performed incredible contortions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twist - an act or result of twisting; a distortion of shape
  • Synonym: distortion - the action of distorting or the state of being distorted

Etymology

From Latin contortio (a twisting), from contorquere (to twist thoroughly).
