Dorothy Dix

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɔr.ə.θi dɪks / (AmE), / ˈdɒr.ə.θi dɪks / (BrE)
The pen name of Elizabeth Meriwether Gilmer (1870–1951), an American journalist known for her widely syndicated advice column for people seeking help with personal and romantic problems.
American advice columnist's pen name (1870–1951)

Examples

Many readers trusted Dorothy Dix for thoughtful advice on matters of the heart.
The advice columns written by Dorothy Dix were syndicated in hundreds of newspapers nationwide.
She became famous under the name Dorothy Dix despite her real name being Elizabeth Gilmer.

Etymology

Pen name adopted by Elizabeth Meriwether Gilmer, American journalist.
