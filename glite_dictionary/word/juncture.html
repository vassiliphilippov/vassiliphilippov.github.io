juncture

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋktʃə / (BrE)
a particular point in time, especially one at which a critical decision must be made or an important event occurs
critical point in time or situation

Examples

At this juncture, it is essential to decide our next course of action.
We cannot afford any mistakes at such a critical juncture.
She wondered if, at this juncture, she was making the right choice.

Synset Examples

at that juncture he had no idea what to do
he must be made to realize that the company stands at a critical point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger
  • Synonym: turning point - a moment when a decisive change occurs

Etymology

From Latin 'junctura' (a joining), from 'jungere' (to join).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋktʃər / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋktʃə / (BrE)
the point or manner at which two things are joined; a particular point in events or time where things come together or connect
the joining point or manner of connection

Examples

At this critical juncture, the team must decide whether to continue the project.
The two rivers meet at a natural juncture deep in the valley.
Their partnership was formed at a fortunate juncture in both of their careers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junction - the place where two or more things are joined
  • Antonym: separation - the act or state of moving or being moved apart

Etymology

From Latin 'junctura' (a joining), from 'jungere' (to join).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋktʃər / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋktʃə / (BrE)
a particular point in time, especially one of critical importance or significant change
a critical or important point in time

Examples

The negotiations reached a critical juncture when both sides refused to compromise.
At this juncture, it’s essential that we make the right decision for the company’s future.
History shows that societal changes often occur at moments of great juncture.

Synset Examples

at such junctures he always had an impulse to leave
it was needed only on special occasions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger
  • Synonym: turning point - a moment of significant change

Etymology

From Latin junctura (a joining), from jungere (to join).
