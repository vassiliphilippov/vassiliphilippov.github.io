Zoysia tenuifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɔɪziə tɛˌnuːɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈzɔɪziə tɛˌnjuːɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A creeping perennial grass native to Asia, often used as a drought-resistant lawn grass, especially in the southern United States.
a creeping drought-resistant Asian lawn grass

Examples

Because of its fine texture and ability to withstand drought, Zoysia tenuifolia is a popular choice for golf course greens.
Gardeners often plant Zoysia tenuifolia in warm climates where water conservation is important.
The Zoysia tenuifolia lawn remained green throughout the summer, despite the lack of rainfall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Korean grass - A common name for Zoysia tenuifolia, a fine-textured lawn grass

Etymology

From the botanical genus Zoysia, named after Austrian botanist Karl von Zois, and Latin tenuifolia, meaning 'slender-leaved.'
