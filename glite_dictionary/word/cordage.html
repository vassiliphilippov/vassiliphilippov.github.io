cordage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrdɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdɪdʒ / (BrE)
the ropes or lines, especially those used in the rigging of a ship
ropes used, especially on ships

Examples

The cordage needed regular inspection to ensure the ship's safety.
Over the years, salt and wind frayed much of the cordage on the old vessel.
The crew worked quickly to replace damaged cordage before the storm hit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rope - a strong, thick line made of fibers or wires twisted together

Etymology

From French 'cordage', from 'corde' (rope).
