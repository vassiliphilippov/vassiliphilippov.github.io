lining

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
a layer of material, often cloth, sewn or attached to the inside surface of a garment
inner layer of a garment

Examples

The jacket's lining is made of soft silk for extra comfort.
After years of wear, the lining of his coat had started to tear.
She chose a coat with a bright red lining that contrasted with the navy exterior.

Etymology

From Middle English linen (to line), from Old French ligner, from lin (flax, linen).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
a layer of material covering the inside surface of something, especially for protection or decoration
inner covering layer of material

Examples

The jacket has a silk lining that makes it comfortable to wear.
She inspected the lining of her purse for any tears or holes.
A waterproof lining protects the inside of the boots from getting wet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lining - the act of covering the inside surface with a material

Etymology

From the verb 'line' + '-ing', originally referring to material used to line garments.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of attaching an inner layer of fabric to the inside of a garment, curtain, or similar item
attaching an inner fabric layer

Examples

The lining of the coat was expertly attached by the tailor.
She learned the process of lining curtains in her sewing class.
Proper lining ensures that a dress retains its shape and lasts longer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lining - a piece of cloth sewn inside garments

Etymology

From Middle English linin, related to 'line', meaning to cover the inside.

4. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
a layer of material used as a covering on the inside surface of something to protect it or make it more comfortable
inner covering material

Examples

The jacket has a soft silk lining to make it more comfortable to wear.
He tore the lining of his suitcase while packing for his trip.
The lining of the box protected the fragile vase inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lining - the act of placing a lining inside something
  • Synonym: covering - something that covers or protects
  • Antonym: exterior - the outside surface of something, opposite of lining

Etymology

From Middle English lininge (act of covering with linen), from 'line' + '-ing'.
