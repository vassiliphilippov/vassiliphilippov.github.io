lutecium

1. NOUN

/ luˈtiːsiəm / (AmE), / luːˈtiːsiəm / (BrE)
A trivalent metallic element of the rare earth group, usually found in association with yttrium; variant spelling of lutetium.
variant spelling of lutetium (chemical element)

Examples

The mineral sample was found to contain traces of lutecium.
Early chemists referred to element 71 as lutecium before the modern spelling was adopted.
Lutecium is rarely encountered in pure form due to its association with other rare earth elements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lutetium - a trivalent metallic element of the rare earth group; chemical symbol: Lu

Etymology

From French 'lutecium', after Lutetia, the Latin name for Paris, where it was discovered.
