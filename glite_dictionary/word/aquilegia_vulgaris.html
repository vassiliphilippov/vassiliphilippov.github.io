Aquilegia vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ ˌækwɪˈliːdʒə vʌlˈɡɛərɪs / (AmE), / ˌakwɪˈliːdʒɪə vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
a species of columbine native to Europe, with variously colored short-spurred flowers, commonly known as European columbine
a species of columbine plant

Examples

The gardens were filled with the delicate blooms of Aquilegia vulgaris each spring.
Many gardeners appreciate Aquilegia vulgaris for its ability to thrive in shaded areas.
The short-spurred flowers of Aquilegia vulgaris come in a range of colors, from pale blue to deep purple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: columbine - a plant of the genus Aquilegia, with bell-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'aquilegia' (columbine) and 'vulgaris' (common).
