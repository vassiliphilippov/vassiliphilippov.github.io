hyperpituitarism

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərpɪˈtuːɪtɛrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəpɪˈtjuːɪtərɪzəm / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by excessive activity of the pituitary gland, especially the anterior lobe, resulting in overproduction of hormones such as growth hormone.
excessive pituitary gland activity (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with hyperpituitarism after noticing abnormal hormone levels.
Children with hyperpituitarism may experience unusually rapid growth due to excess growth hormone.
Treatment options for hyperpituitarism depend on the underlying cause and severity of hormone overproduction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypopituitarism - deficiency of pituitary gland activity

Etymology

From hyper- (over, excessive) + pituitary + -ism.
