to swear

1. VERB

/ swɛr / (AmE), / swɛə / (BrE)
to make a solemn promise or statement, especially by taking an oath
make a solemn promise or oath

Examples

I swear to tell the truth, the whole truth, and nothing but the truth.
She swore never to reveal the secret to anyone.
After hearing the evidence, the witness swore an oath in court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vow - to promise solemnly
  • Synonym: to affirm - to state something firmly and publicly

Etymology

From Old English swerian (to take an oath), of Germanic origin.

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ swɛr / (AmE), / swɛə / (BrE)
to declare or affirm something solemnly and formally as true, often invoking a higher authority or making an oath
declare or affirm solemnly as true

Examples

He placed his hand on the Bible and swore to tell the truth.
I swear I had nothing to do with the accident.
They swore that they would keep the secret forever.

Synset Examples

Before God I swear I am innocent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to affirm - to state something as true with confidence
  • Antonym: to deny - to declare that something is not true

Etymology

Old English *swerian*, from Proto-Germanic *sweran* (to swear, take an oath).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01013058-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ swɛr / (AmE), / swɛə / (BrE)
to use rude or offensive language, especially obscene or profane words
use offensive or obscene language
vulgar

Examples

He began to swear loudly when his team lost the game.
Please don't swear in front of the children.
The angry customer was swearing at the staff.

Synset Examples

The drunken men were cursing loudly in the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - to use profane or offensive language
  • Synonym: to blaspheme - to speak irreverently about God or sacred things
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Old English swerian (to take an oath), extended to cover offensive language by association with strong expression.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00867051-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ swɛr / (AmE), / swɛə / (BrE)
to make a formal statement or promise that something is true or will be done, usually while holding a sacred object or in a court of law; to declare under oath
declare under oath; make a formal promise

Examples

All witnesses are required to swear before giving testimony in court.
He had to swear that his statement was true under penalty of perjury.
She swore in front of the judge that the documents were authentic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to affirm - to declare solemnly and formally as true, without an oath
  • Antonym: to perjure - to knowingly lie after swearing an oath

Etymology

Old English *swerian*; related to Old Norse *sverja*, German *schwören*.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
