furthest

1. ADV

/ ˈfɝːðɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɜːðɪst / (BrE)
to the greatest distance in space or time; most distant (used especially as the superlative of 'far', sometimes interchangeably with 'farthest').
most distant (in space or time)

Examples

She traveled the furthest of anyone in her family, reaching South America.
Of all the runners, he threw the ball the furthest.
I wonder who can swim the furthest without stopping.

Synset Examples

see who could jump the farthest
chose the farthest seat from the door
he swam the furthest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farthest - to the greatest distance in space or time; most distant (superlative of 'far')
  • Antonym: nearest - closest in distance or time

Etymology

Formed as a superlative of 'far', influenced by 'further'.

2. ADV

/ ˈfɝːðɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɜːðɪst / (BrE)
to the greatest degree or extent; at or to the most advanced stage, especially in abstract or figurative contexts
to the greatest degree or extent

Examples

Of all the competitors, Marie ran furthest into the dense forest before turning back.
This proposal takes our research furthest in exploring the impact of new technologies.
His artwork is furthest removed from anything conventional or traditional.

Synset Examples

went the furthest of all the children in her education
furthest removed from reality
she goes farthest in helping us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farthest - to the greatest distance (often used for physical space)
  • Antonym: nearest - closest in distance or relation

Etymology

Superlative of 'far', influenced by forms of 'further'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɝːðəst / (AmE), / ˈfɜːðɪst / (BrE)
at or to the greatest distance in space, time, or order; most remote or advanced
most distant in space, time, or order

Examples

Of all the planets, Pluto is the furthest from the Sun.
She reached the furthest point of the trail before turning back.
This is the furthest we have ever traveled on our annual road trips.

Synset Examples

had traveled to the farthest frontier
don't go beyond the farthermost (or furthermost) tree
explored the furthest reaches of space
the utmost tip of the peninsula

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farthest - most distant in space, time, or order
  • Antonym: nearest - closest in space, time, or order

Etymology

Superlative of 'far', formed with the suffix '-est'. Variant spelling of 'farthest', influenced by 'further'.

Source: WordNet
