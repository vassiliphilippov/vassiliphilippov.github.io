reduviid

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈduːviɪd / (AmE), / rɪˈdjuːvɪɪd / (BrE)
a predatory, often long-legged bug belonging to the family Reduviidae, some of which feed on insects and a few on mammalian blood
predatory bug of the Reduviidae family

Examples

Researchers found a reduviid feeding on a beetle in the rainforest undergrowth.
Only a few species of reduviid are known to bite humans and suck blood.
The reduviid extends its proboscis to inject venom and immobilize its prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assassin bug - common name for most insects in the Reduviidae family

Etymology

From New Latin 'Reduviidae', the family name, based on Latin 'reduvia' (hangnail, fragment).
