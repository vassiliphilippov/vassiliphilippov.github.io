Aster ptarmicoides

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstɚ tɑːrmɪˈkoʊdiːz / (AmE), / ˈæstə tɑːˌmɪkəʊˈdiːz / (BrE)
A tufted, rigid perennial plant native to North America, bearing loose clusters of white flowers; also known as upland white aster.
North American perennial plant with white flowers

Examples

The meadow was dotted with blooming Aster ptarmicoides during late summer.
Researchers observed several bees pollinating the Aster ptarmicoides along the trail.
Gardeners often plant Aster ptarmicoides to attract butterflies to their yards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upland white aster - Common name for Aster ptarmicoides

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Aster' meaning 'star', and species 'ptarmicoides' indicating a resemblance to plants of the genus Ptarmica.
