Hanoverian

1. NOUN

/ ˌhænəˈvɛriən / (AmE), / ˌhanəˈvɪəriən / (BrE)
A supporter or member of the royal house of Hanover, especially in reference to British history.
supporter/member of the house of Hanover

Examples

The loyal Hanoverian refused to support the Jacobite uprising.
Many British politicians declared themselves Hanoverians during the 18th century.
As a proud Hanoverian, she celebrated the ascension of King George I.

Etymology

From Hanover, the German city and kingdom, combined with '-ian' to denote belonging or relation.

2. ADJ

/ ˌhænəˈvɪriən / (AmE), / ˌhanəˈvɪəriən / (BrE)
relating to the former British royal House of Hanover or its supporters.
relating to the House of Hanover

Examples

The Hanoverian kings ruled Britain from 1714 to 1901.
Many paintings in the gallery depict Hanoverian monarchs in their official regalia.
During the Hanoverian era, the British monarchy underwent significant changes.

Etymology

From Hanover, the German city, source of the royal dynasty; influenced by the German 'Hannover'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌhænəˈvɛriən / (AmE), / ˌhanəˈvɪəriən / (BrE)
a member of the British royal family belonging to the House of Hanover, especially any of the monarchs who ruled Britain between 1714 and 1901
British royal of the House of Hanover

Examples

Queen Victoria was the last Hanoverian to occupy the British throne.
The Hanoverians came to power after the death of Queen Anne in 1714.
Many historians debate how the Hanoverian monarchs influenced British politics and society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Georgian - relating to the period or monarchs named George, especially 1714–1830 in Britain
  • Synonym: Victorian - relating to the reign of Queen Victoria

Etymology

From Hanover, a region in Germany, origin of the Hanoverian royal house.
