Lactobacteriaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæktoʊˌbæktəˈriːəsiː / (AmE), / ˌlaktəʊˌbaktəˈrɪːəsiː / (BrE)
A family of bacteria that ferment carbohydrates mainly into lactic acid, including lactic acid bacteria and some pathogens.
family of lactic acid bacteria
medical

Examples

The Lactobacteriaceae family includes bacteria important for food fermentation.
Some Lactobacteriaceae are beneficial probiotics, while others can cause disease.
Researchers study Lactobacteriaceae to understand their roles in human health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lactobacillus - a genus of lactic acid bacteria, often found in the gut and used in dairy fermentation

Etymology

From New Latin 'Lactobacterium' (a genus of bacteria) + -aceae (a standard suffix for biological families).

2. NOUN

/ ˌlæktoʊˌbæktɪriˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌlaktəʊˌbaktɪrɪˈeɪsiaɪ / (BrE)
a family of bacteria that ferment carbohydrates mainly into lactic acid, including lactic acid bacteria and some important pathogens
family of lactic acid bacteria
medical

Examples

Many species within the Lactobacteriaceae family play a crucial role in yogurt fermentation.
The presence of Lactobacteriaceae in the gut is associated with various health benefits.
Researchers identified new strains of Lactobacteriaceae among the samples collected from fermented foods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lactobacillus - a genus of lactic acid bacteria within Lactobacteriaceae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining 'lacto-' (milk), 'bacterium', and '-aceae' (a taxonomic family suffix).
