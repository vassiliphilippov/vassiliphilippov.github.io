thyromegaly

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪroʊˈmɛɡəli / (AmE), / ˌθaɪərəʊˈmɛɡəli / (BrE)
abnormal enlargement of the thyroid gland, often caused by hormonal imbalances or iodine deficiency
enlargement of the thyroid gland
medical

Examples

The doctor detected thyromegaly during the routine physical examination.
Patients with thyromegaly may experience difficulty swallowing or a visible swelling in the neck.
A deficiency of iodine in the diet is a common cause of thyromegaly in certain regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goiter - swelling of the thyroid gland in the neck
  • Degree Lower: thyroiditis - inflammation of the thyroid gland

Etymology

From Greek 'thyreo-' (shield, referring to the thyroid gland) + 'megaly' (enlargement).
