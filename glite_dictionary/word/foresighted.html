foresighted

1. ADJ

/ fɔrˈsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / fɔːˈsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having the ability to anticipate and plan prudently for future needs or events.
able to plan prudently for the future

Examples

Her foresighted investments ensured a comfortable retirement.
The company's foresighted policies helped it navigate economic downturns successfully.
He was praised for his foresighted approach to environmental conservation.

Synset Examples

large goals that required farsighted policies
took a long view of the geopolitical issues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farsighted - able to foresee and prepare for the future
  • Antonym: shortsighted - lacking the ability to plan for the future

Etymology

Formed from 'fore-' (before) + 'sighted', from 'sight' (vision).
