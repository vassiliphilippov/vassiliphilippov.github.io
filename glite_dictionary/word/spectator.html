spectator

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛk.teɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɛk.teɪ.tə / (BrE)
a woman's low-heeled pump featuring contrasting colors, typically on the toe and heel
type of woman's shoe with contrasting colors

Examples

She paired her sleek dress with classic spectator pumps for a vintage look.
The shop sells retro spectator shoes in various color combinations.
Her black-and-white spectator heels were the highlight of her outfit.

Etymology

Based on the notion of shoes worn for watching sporting events, later associated with distinctive two-tone style.

2. NOUN

/ spɛkˈteɪtɚ / (AmE), / spɛkˈteɪtə / (BrE)
a person who watches an event, show, or activity, usually as part of an audience but not taking part
a person watching an event

Examples

The crowd of spectators cheered as the runners crossed the finish line.
As a spectator, she enjoyed the concert without feeling nervous about performing.
Hundreds of spectators watched the fireworks display from the park.

Synset Examples

the spectators applauded the performance
television viewers
sky watchers discovered a new star

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audience - a group of people assembled to watch or listen to something
  • Antonym: participant - a person who takes part in something

Etymology

From Latin 'spectator', from 'spectare' (to watch).
