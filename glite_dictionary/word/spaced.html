spaced

1. ADJ

/ speɪst / (AmE), / speɪst / (BrE)
placed or arranged with intervals or spaces between; separated by a certain distance.
arranged with spaces between

Examples

The trees were evenly spaced along the avenue, creating a pleasing effect.
Please make sure the chairs are spaced far enough apart for comfort.
The exhibits in the gallery are spaced to allow visitors to move easily.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clustered - grouped closely together
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00544502-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ speɪst / (AmE), / speɪst / (BrE)
arranged with spaces between objects or elements; often used in combination (e.g., 'widely spaced').
arranged with spaces between

Examples

The houses on this street are spaced far apart for privacy.
He prefers lines that are spaced evenly on the page.
With tall, spaced columns, the building feels open and airy.

Synset Examples

widely spaced eyes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clustered - objects grouped closely together
