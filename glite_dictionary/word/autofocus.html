autofocus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔtoʊˌfoʊkəs / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəʊˌfəʊkəs / (BrE)
A mechanism in a camera or other optical instrument that automatically adjusts the focus to ensure a clear image.
automatic focusing mechanism in cameras

Examples

Many modern cameras feature autofocus for quick and accurate image capturing.
The autofocus on her phone worked well, even in low light conditions.
Some photographers prefer manual focus over autofocus for greater control.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manual focus - focusing done by hand, not automatically

Etymology

From 'auto-' (self) + 'focus', indicating a mechanism that focuses by itself.
