scallion

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæliən / (AmE), / ˈskælɪən / (BrE)
A young onion with a small, barely developed bulb and long, thin green leaves, often used in salads and cooking.
young onion with long green leaves

Examples

She sprinkled chopped scallion over the soup for a fresh flavor.
A stir-fry often tastes better with a handful of sliced scallions.
The salad recipe calls for one finely diced scallion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green onion - young onion with a long green stalk, used in salads and garnish
  • Synonym: spring onion - another term for scallion, especially in British English

Etymology

From Old French *escalogne*, from Latin *Ascalonia caepa* (onion of Ascalon).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskæliən / (AmE), / ˈskælɪən / (BrE)
a long, thin plant of the onion family with a white base and green leaves, often used in cooking for its mild flavor
mild onion-like vegetable

Examples

Chopped scallion adds a fresh, mild onion flavor to salads and soups.
He sprinkled finely sliced scallions over the noodles for extra color and taste.
You can substitute scallion for chives in this recipe if needed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green onion - another name for scallion
  • Synonym: spring onion - another type of young onion, sometimes called scallion

Etymology

From Old French 'escalogne', from Latin 'Ascalonia cepa' (onion of Ascalon, a town in ancient Palestine).
