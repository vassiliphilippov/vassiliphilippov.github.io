sniffler

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnɪflər / (AmE), / ˈsnɪflə / (BrE)
A person who breathes audibly through a congested nose, usually because of a cold or allergies.
person who sniffles audibly

Examples

The constant sound from the sniffler in the next seat made the lecture hard to follow.
Every winter, my office seems to have at least one sniffler disturbing the silence.
She handed a tissue to the little sniffler standing near the playground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sniffle - to breathe audibly through a congested nose

Etymology

From 'sniffle' (verb, to sniff repeatedly) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
