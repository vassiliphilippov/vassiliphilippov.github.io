nauseant

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔziənt / (AmE), / ˈnɔːzɪənt / (BrE)
a medicine that induces nausea and vomiting
medicine that causes nausea or vomiting
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a nauseant to induce vomiting after the patient ingested poison.
Some nauseants are used in emergency medicine to empty the stomach quickly.
Because of its powerful effect, a nauseant should only be administered by a medical professional.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emetic - a medicine that causes vomiting

Etymology

From Latin 'nauseans', present participle of 'nauseare' (to feel sick).
