street

1. NOUN

/ striːt / (AmE), / striːt / (BrE)
A public road in a city or town, usually with buildings on both sides, and the part between sidewalks where vehicles travel.
public road; vehicle thoroughfare

Examples

Children are taught to look both ways before crossing the street.
The street was busy with cars and buses throughout the day.
Many shops and cafes line the street in the city center.

Synset Examples

be careful crossing the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avenue - a broad road in a town or city, typically with trees
  • Synonym: road - a wide way leading from one place to another

Etymology

From Old English 'stræt', from Latin 'strata (via)' meaning 'paved (way)'.
