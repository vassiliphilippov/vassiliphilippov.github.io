paladin

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæləˌdɪn / (AmE), / ˈpælədɪn / (BrE)
a strong or vigorous supporter or champion of a cause or movement, often acting with courage and dedication
vigorous champion of a cause

Examples

She has become a paladin for environmental protection in her community.
The lawyer was regarded as a paladin of civil rights.
In literature, the paladin often stands alone against injustice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: champion - a person who fights or argues for a cause or on behalf of someone else
  • Synonym: defender - a person who defends someone or something

Etymology

From Italian 'paladino', from Late Latin 'palatinus' (palace official), originally referring to the foremost knights of Charlemagne's court.
