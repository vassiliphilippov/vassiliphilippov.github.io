Mordvin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrdvɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɔːdvɪn / (BrE)
a member of an agricultural people native to the central Volga region of European Russia
member of a Volga-region ethnic group

Examples

The ancient Mordvin people have preserved their language and traditions for centuries.
She is studying the folklore of the Mordvin communities along the Volga River.
A group of Mordvin delegates visited the museum to share their cultural heritage.

Etymology

From Russian Мордва (Mordvá), the self-designation of the people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrdvɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɔːdvɪn / (BrE)
the Finnic language spoken by the Mordvinians, consisting of the Erzya and Moksha varieties, native to parts of Russia
the Finnic language of the Mordvinians

Examples

The Mordvin language has two main dialects: Erzya and Moksha.
Many people in the Republic of Mordovia still speak Mordvin as their first language.
Efforts are being made to preserve Mordvin amid the increasing use of Russian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finnic - the group of Finno-Ugric languages that includes Mordvin
  • Degree Lower: Erzya - a variety of the Mordvin language
  • Degree Lower: Moksha - a variety of the Mordvin language

Etymology

From Russian Мордвин (Mordvin), referring to the Mordvin people and their language.
