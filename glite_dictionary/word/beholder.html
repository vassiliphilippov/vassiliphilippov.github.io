beholder

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈhoʊldər / (AmE), / bɪˈhəʊldə / (BrE)
a person who sees or observes something; a viewer or observer
person who observes or perceives

Examples

The painting looks different to each beholder.
Beauty is often said to be in the eye of the beholder.
As a silent beholder, she witnessed the entire scene unfold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: observer - a person who watches something closely
  • Synonym: spectator - a person who watches at an event, show, or game

Etymology

From Middle English 'beholden' (to look at), with '-er' suffix indicating the agent.
