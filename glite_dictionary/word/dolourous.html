dolourous

1. ADJ

/ ˈdoʊlərəs / (AmE), / ˈdəʊlərəs / (BrE)
Full of, expressing, or causing sorrow or grief.
showing sorrow; sorrowful
archaic

Examples

The knight's dolourous tale moved the entire court to tears.
She spoke in a dolourous tone, reflecting the sadness in her heart.
The winds howled with a dolourous lament through the empty halls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorrowful - feeling or showing sorrow
  • Antonym: joyful - feeling or expressing great happiness

Etymology

From Old French 'dolorous', from Latin 'dolorosus' (full of pain, sorrow), from 'dolor' (pain, grief).
