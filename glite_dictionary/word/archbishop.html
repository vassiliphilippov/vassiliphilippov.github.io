archbishop

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrtʃˌbɪʃ.əp / (AmE), / ˈɑːtʃˌbɪʃ.əp / (BrE)
a bishop of the highest rank who is in charge of an archdiocese or a very important district within certain Christian churches
highest-ranking bishop

Examples

The archbishop delivered an inspiring sermon at the cathedral.
Many people travelled to see the installation of the new archbishop.
The archbishop played an important role in mediating between the church and the government.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bishop - a senior member of the Christian clergy in charge of a diocese

Etymology

From Old English arcebiscop, from Late Latin archiepiscopus, from Greek arkhiepiskopos (chief overseer).
