Rothschild

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɑθsˌtʃaɪld / (AmE), / ˈrɒθsˌtʃaɪld / (BrE)
A member of a European family of Jewish bankers and financiers, renowned for their wealth and influence, especially in the 18th and 19th centuries.
Jewish-European banking family

Examples

The Rothschild family played a major role in European finance during the nineteenth century.
Rumors swirled about the Rothschild influence on major international banks.
He researched the history of the Rothschild dynasty for his economics thesis.

Etymology

Family name of German origin, from Rothschild, meaning 'red shield' in German.
