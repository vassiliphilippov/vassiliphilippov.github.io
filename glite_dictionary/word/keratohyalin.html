keratohyalin

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛr.ət.oʊˈhaɪ.ə.lɪn / (AmE), / ˌkɛr.ət.əʊˈhaɪ.ə.lɪn / (BrE)
a substance present in the large granules of the stratum granulosum of the epidermis, thought to be involved in the process of keratinization
substance found in a layer of the skin
medical

Examples

The presence of keratohyalin granules can be observed under a microscope in the stratum granulosum.
Abnormalities in keratohyalin formation may affect the skin's protective barrier.
Medical students studied the structure and function of keratohyalin during their dermatology rotation.

Etymology

From Greek 'keras' (horn) + 'hyalos' (glass), referring to its appearance and relation to keratin.
