stricken

1. ADJ

/ ˈstrɪkən / (AmE), / ˈstrɪkən / (BrE)
Seriously affected or incapacitated by illness, trouble, or unpleasant emotion.
affected or incapacitated by illness or distress

Examples

The small village was stricken with a sudden outbreak of the flu.
Her stricken appearance revealed just how sick she was.
Many families were stricken by the epidemic that swept through the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afflicted - seriously affected by something unpleasant, especially illness

Etymology

Past participle of 'strike' from Old English 'strīcan' (to strike).

2. ADJ

/ ˈstrɪkən / (AmE), / ˈstrɪkən / (BrE)
seriously affected by an unpleasant feeling, illness, or condition, especially disease, disaster, or sorrow.
seriously affected by something harmful

Examples

The small village was stricken by a severe outbreak of influenza.
Her stricken face showed the toll that years of hardship had taken.
Many families remain stricken with grief after the disaster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blighted - damaged or suffering from something that prevents growth or success

Etymology

Past participle of 'strike', from Old English *strīcan* (to go, flow, move, stroke, rub).
