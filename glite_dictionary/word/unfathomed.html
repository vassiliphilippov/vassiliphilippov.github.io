unfathomed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfæðəmd / (AmE), / ʌnˈfæðəmd / (BrE)
not measured or explored for its depth; too deep or mysterious to be understood or known.
not measured; very deep or mysterious

Examples

The explorers were captivated by the unfathomed depths of the ocean.
Legends spoke of unfathomed mysteries hidden beneath the ancient ruins.
Her emotions ran deeper than anyone could see, buried in an unfathomed sea of thought.

Synset Examples

the profound depths of the sea
the dark unfathomed caves of ocean
unplumbed depths of the sea
remote and unsounded caverns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fathomless - too deep to be measured or understood

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'fathomed,' from Middle English 'fathom' (to measure depth), ultimately from Old English 'fæðm' (embrace, span).
