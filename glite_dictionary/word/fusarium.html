Fusarium

1. NOUN

/ fuˈzɛəriəm / (AmE), / fjuˈzɛəriəm / (BrE)
A genus of filamentous fungi, many species of which are plant pathogens causing diseases such as dry rot; some species can infect humans, leading to conditions such as corneal inflammation.
genus of plant-pathogenic fungi
medical

Examples

Several crops were destroyed by a Fusarium outbreak in the region last year.
Researchers are studying how Fusarium infects both plants and humans.
The patient suffered vision loss due to a Fusarium infection of the cornea.

Etymology

From Latin *fūsus* (spindle), referring to the spindle-shaped spores of the fungus.
