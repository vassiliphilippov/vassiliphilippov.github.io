pituitary

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈtuː.ɪˌtɛr.i / (AmE), / pɪˈtjuː.ɪˌtəri / (BrE)
a small gland at the base of the brain that regulates growth and controls other glands in the endocrine system
gland at base of brain; hormone regulator
medical

Examples

The pituitary plays a crucial role in regulating the body's hormones.
Damage to the pituitary can cause serious health problems.
Doctors will often check the pituitary for signs of hormonal imbalance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pituitary gland - the full term for the gland at the base of the brain controlling endocrine functions
  • Synonym: hypothalamus - a region of the brain controlling the pituitary gland

Etymology

From Latin 'pituitarius' (relating to phlegm), originally believed to secrete mucus.
