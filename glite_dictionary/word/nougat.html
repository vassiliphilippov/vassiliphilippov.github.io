nougat

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːɡət / (AmE), / ˈnuːɡə, ˈnuːɡɑː / (BrE)
A sweet made from sugar or honey, nuts, and sometimes fruit, formed into a chewy paste and often used in confectionery.
chewy sweet made with nuts or fruit

Examples

She bought a box of assorted nougat from the bakery.
Almond nougat is my favorite kind of candy.
The chocolate bar contains a layer of creamy nougat inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fudge - soft, sweet candy made from sugar, butter, and milk

Etymology

From French 'nougat', from Occitan 'nogat', from 'noga' (nut).
