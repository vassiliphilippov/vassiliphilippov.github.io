satsuma

1. NOUN

/ sætˈsuːmə / (AmE), / sætˈsuːmə / (BrE)
A seedless, easy-peeling variety of mandarin orange with sweet, juicy flesh, often eaten fresh.
a type of mandarin orange

Examples

The children enjoyed eating a juicy satsuma as a healthy snack after school.
She added slices of satsuma to the salad for a refreshing citrus flavor.
Every winter, boxes of satsumas appear in grocery stores in time for the holidays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangerine - a small citrus fruit similar to the mandarin orange

Etymology

From Japanese 'Satsuma', a province in southern Kyushu, Japan, where the fruit was first exported to the West.

2. NOUN

/ sætˈsuːmə / (AmE), / sætˈsuːmə / (BrE)
A medium-sized, largely seedless variety of mandarin orange with thin, smooth skin, easy to peel and eat.
a type of seedless mandarin orange

Examples

She packed a satsuma in her lunch for a healthy snack.
The satsuma is easy to peel and contains very few seeds.
During the holidays, bowls of satsumas are a colorful table decoration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandarin - a small sweet citrus fruit with a loose skin; mandarin orange

Etymology

From Japanese Satsuma, a former province where the variety was first grown.
