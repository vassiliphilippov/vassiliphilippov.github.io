disher

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈdɪʃə / (BrE)
a person who serves food or drinks, especially in a skilled or efficient manner
person skilled at serving food or drinks

Examples

At the busy buffet, the disher expertly managed the serving line.
As the main disher at family gatherings, she was always in high demand.
The restaurant hired a new disher to help with the lunch rush.

Synset Examples

She’s the best disher at the ice cream shop.
Every party needs a good disher for the punch bowl.
He’s not just a cook; he’s also a great disher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: server - a person who serves food or drinks

Etymology

Formed from "dish" (to serve food) + agentive suffix "-er".
