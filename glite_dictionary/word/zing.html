zing

1. NOUN

/ zɪŋ / (AmE), / zɪŋ / (BrE)
a brief high-pitched buzzing or humming sound, especially one that is sharp or vibrant
high-pitched buzzing or humming sound

Examples

The zing of the bell echoed through the hall.
She heard the zing of a mosquito fly past her ear.
A zing followed as the arrow shot through the air.

Synset Examples

the zing of the passing bullet

2. NOUN

/ zɪŋ / (AmE), / zɪŋ / (BrE)
a quality of enthusiasm, energy, or liveliness; brisk vitality, especially in a person's personality or manner
vivacity; energetic quality

Examples

Her speech really had some zing and captivated the entire audience.
The new advertising campaign gave the brand a much-needed zing.
He greeted us every morning with his trademark zing and enthusiasm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitality - the state of being strong and active; energy
  • Synonym: pep - energy and high spirits
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of energy or enthusiasm
