traditionally

1. ADV

/ trəˈdɪʃənəli / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃənəli / (BrE)
in accordance with long-established customs, beliefs, or practices
in a traditional manner

Examples

Traditionally, the wedding dress is white in Western cultures.
Chinese New Year is traditionally celebrated with family gatherings and fireworks.
Dinner is traditionally served at 7 p.m. in this household.

Synset Examples

traditionally, we eat fried foods on Hanukah

Related Terms

  • Synonym: customarily - in a way that is usual or normal
  • Antonym: modernly - in a modern manner; not according to tradition

Etymology

From 'traditional' + '-ly'; 'traditional' from Latin 'traditionem' (a handing down, a surrender).
