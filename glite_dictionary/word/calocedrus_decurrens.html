Calocedrus decurrens

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæloʊˈsidrəs dɪˈkɜːrənz / (AmE), / ˌkæləʊˈsiːdrəs dɪˈkʌrənz / (BrE)
A tall evergreen tree native to the Pacific coast of North America, with cypress-like foliage and cinnamon-red bark, also known as incense cedar.
North American incense cedar tree

Examples

The Calocedrus decurrens is commonly planted as an ornamental tree in parks and gardens.
Many pencils are made from the aromatic wood of Calocedrus decurrens.
Hikers admired the cinnamon-red bark of the towering Calocedrus decurrens in the Sierra Nevada foothills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incense cedar - Common name for Calocedrus decurrens, a tall evergreen tree

Etymology

From Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) + 'kedros' (cedar); 'decurrens' is Latin for 'running down', referring to leaf arrangement.
