Bozeman

1. PROPN

/ ˈboʊzmən / (AmE), / ˈbəʊzmən / (BrE)
A town in southwestern Montana, United States, known as the gateway to Yellowstone National Park.
town in southwestern Montana, USA

Examples

Many tourists stop in Bozeman before entering Yellowstone National Park.
The university in Bozeman is renowned for its research programs.
After flying into Bozeman, we rented a car to explore Montana's scenic landscapes.

Etymology

Named after John M. Bozeman, a pioneer who established the Bozeman Trail in the 19th century.
