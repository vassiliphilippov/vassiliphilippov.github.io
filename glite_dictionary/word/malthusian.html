Malthusian

1. ADJ

/ mælˈθuːziən / (AmE), / malˈθjuːziən / (BrE)
Relating to the theories of Thomas Malthus, especially the idea that population growth tends to outpace food supply, leading to societal constraints.
relating to Malthus or his theories

Examples

Many environmentalists warn of a Malthusian crisis if population growth is not controlled.
The economist presented a Malthusian view on food scarcity and resource management.
His arguments were rooted in classical Malthusian theory about population pressure.

Synset Examples

Malthusian theories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neo-Malthusian - relating to modern adaptations of Malthusian theory, especially concerning population control

Etymology

From Thomas Malthus (1766–1834), English economist and demographer.

2. NOUN

/ mælˈθuːziən / (AmE), / mælˈθjuːziən / (BrE)
A person who believes in the population theory proposed by Thomas Malthus, which suggests that population growth tends to outstrip the growth of resources, leading to social and economic problems.
believer in Malthusian population theory

Examples

As a dedicated Malthusian, he argued that unchecked population growth would inevitably cause famine.
Debates between the Malthusian economists and their critics shaped modern demography.
She described herself as a cautious Malthusian, supporting policies for sustainable development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Neo-Malthusian - someone who adapts Malthus's theory for modern contexts, focusing on population control policies

Etymology

From Thomas Malthus, an English economist and demographer (1766–1834).
