plight

1. NOUN

/ plaɪt / (AmE), / plaɪt / (BrE)
a difficult or unfortunate situation, especially one that is hard to escape from
difficult or unfortunate situation

Examples

The documentary highlighted the plight of refugees fleeing the war-torn region.
Many organizations work tirelessly to alleviate the plight of the homeless.
Her novel draws attention to the plight of children living in poverty.

Synset Examples

finds himself in a most awkward predicament
the woeful plight of homeless people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predicament - an unpleasant or difficult situation

Etymology

From Old French 'pleit' (condition, state, dispute), from Latin 'plicare' (to fold).
