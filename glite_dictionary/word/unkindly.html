unkindly

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈkaɪndli / (AmE), / ʌnˈkaɪndli / (BrE)
Lacking sympathy or kindness; done in a harsh or mean manner.
lacking kindness or sympathy

Examples

She spoke unkindly to her colleague after the meeting.
It was unkindly of him to ignore her request for help.
The teacher did not mean to act unkindly, but her words seemed harsh.

Synset Examples

unkindly acts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: kindly - having or showing kindness; gentle and benevolent

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'kind' + '-ly.'

2. ADV

/ ʌnˈkaɪndli / (AmE), / ʌnˈkaɪndli / (BrE)
In a manner that lacks kindness; in a harsh or mean way.
in an unkind manner

Examples

She spoke unkindly to her brother after the argument.
He was treated unkindly by his classmates for being different.
The reviewer wrote unkindly about the actor's latest performance.

Synset Examples

The teacher treats the children unkindly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: kindly - in a kind, gentle, or friendly manner
