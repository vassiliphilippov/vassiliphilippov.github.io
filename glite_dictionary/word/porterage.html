porterage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtərɪdʒ / (BrE)
the fee charged for carrying goods or luggage by a porter
fee for carrying goods by a porter

Examples

The hotel included the porterage fee in the final bill.
You may be expected to pay porterage when your luggage is taken to your room.
Some tour packages cover all tips and porterage costs for travelers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carriage - charge for transporting goods

Etymology

From Old French *portage* (carrying), from porter (to carry).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtərɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act or service of carrying goods, luggage, or other burdens, especially by porters or for a fee
the carrying of goods by porters

Examples

The hotel charged a small fee for porterage to bring our bags to the room.
Arrangements for porterage were made at each stop along the hiking trail.
He included the cost of porterage in the travel expenses for the expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carriage - the act of conveying something from one place to another

Etymology

From Old French 'porterage', derived from 'porter' (to carry), from Latin 'portare'.
