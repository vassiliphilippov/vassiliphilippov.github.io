Slovakia

1. PROPN

/ sloʊˈveɪkiə / (AmE), / sləʊˈveɪkɪə / (BrE)
A landlocked country in Central Europe, which became independent after separating from the Czech Republic in 1993.
country in Central Europe

Examples

Many tourists visit Slovakia to enjoy its mountainous landscapes and medieval castles.
The capital city of Slovakia is Bratislava, located on the banks of the Danube River.
Since its independence, Slovakia has become a member of the European Union.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Czech Republic - country that was part of Czechoslovakia with Slovakia until 1993

Etymology

From Slovak 'Slovensko', based on 'Slovan' (Slav).
