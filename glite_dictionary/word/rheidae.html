Rheidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈriː.aɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈriːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, flightless birds native to South America, including the rheas.
family of flightless South American birds

Examples

The Rheidae are characterized by their long legs and necks, adapted to open grasslands.
Modern taxonomy places rheas within the family Rheidae.
Researchers studied genetic diversity within the Rheidae across different regions of South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhea - large, flightless bird native to South America

Etymology

From New Latin Rheidae, from Rhea (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
