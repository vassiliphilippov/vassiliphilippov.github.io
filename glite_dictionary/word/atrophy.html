atrophy

1. NOUN

/ ˈæ.trə.fi / (AmE), / ˈæ.trə.fi / (BrE)
The wasting away or decrease in size of a part of the body, typically due to disease or lack of use.
wasting away of body tissue
medical

Examples

After breaking his leg, the unused muscles in his calf began to atrophy over several weeks.
Prolonged bed rest can result in atrophy of the skeletal muscles.
Doctors monitored the patient's arm for signs of atrophy following the nerve injury.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypertrophy - the enlargement of an organ or tissue from the increase in size of its cells
  • Synonym: degeneration - deterioration and loss of function in tissues or organs

Etymology

From Greek atrophia, from a- (without) + trophē (food, nourishment).

2. NOUN

/ ˈætrəfi / (AmE), / ˈætrəfi / (BrE)
A gradual decline or wasting away of tissue, ability, or effectiveness, especially through lack of use.
gradual wasting away or decline

Examples

Without regular exercise, muscle atrophy can occur in astronauts during long space flights.
The atrophy of traditional skills in the digital age is a growing concern for many educators.
After her long illness, there was noticeable atrophy of the muscles in her legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: degeneration - the process of declining or deteriorating
  • Synonym: wasting - progressive loss of body tissue or function
  • Antonym: growth - an increase in size or development

Etymology

From Greek 'atrophia' (a = without, trophē = food or nourishment).

3. VERB

/ ˈætrəfi / (AmE), / ˈætrəfi / (BrE)
(of body tissue or an organ) to waste away or decrease in size, typically due to disuse or disease.
waste away or shrink (of tissue or organ)

Examples

If you do not exercise regularly, your muscles may atrophy over time.
After the injury, her leg muscles began to atrophy from lack of use.
Without mental stimulation, certain cognitive skills may also atrophy with age.

Synset Examples

Muscles that are not used will atrophy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wither - to become weak and dry, shrink (as of plants or body parts)
  • Synonym: to deteriorate - to become worse or degenerate

Etymology

From Greek atrophia (a wasting away), from a- (not) + trophē (nourishment).
