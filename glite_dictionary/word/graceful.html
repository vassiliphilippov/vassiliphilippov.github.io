graceful

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪs.fəl / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪs.fəl / (BrE)
characterized by elegant beauty of movement, style, form, or execution
elegant and beautiful in movement or form

Examples

The ballerina's graceful movements captivated the entire audience.
With a graceful bow, he accepted the award on stage.
A swan glided graceful across the still surface of the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elegant - pleasingly graceful and stylish
  • Antonym: awkward - lacking grace or ease in movement
  • Antonym: clumsy - lacking grace in movement or manner

Etymology

From grace + -ful, meaning 'full of grace'; grace itself from Latin 'gratia'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪs.fəl / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪs.fəl / (BrE)
having an appearance or manner that suggests taste, ease, and wealth
suggesting taste, ease, and wealth

Examples

The graceful lines of the mansion spoke of old money and refined taste.
Her graceful gestures made her seem perfectly at ease in high society.
The restaurant's graceful decor attracted wealthy patrons seeking an elegant atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elegant - pleasingly graceful and stylish
  • Antonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or style

Etymology

From Old French *gracieux*, from Latin *gratia* (favor, grace).
