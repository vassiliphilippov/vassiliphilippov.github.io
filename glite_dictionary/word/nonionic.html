nonionic

1. ADJ

/ nɑn.aɪˈɑn.ɪk / (AmE), / nɒn.aɪˈɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
Not forming or dissociating into ions in solution.
not converted into ions

Examples

The detergent is nonionic, so it is gentle on sensitive skin.
Many laboratory reagents are nonionic to prevent unwanted chemical reactions.
A nonionic surfactant does not increase the conductivity of water.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ionic - relating to or consisting of ions

Etymology

From 'non-' (not) + 'ionic', relating to ions.

2. ADJ

/ nɑn.aɪˈɑ.nɪk / (AmE), / nɒn.aɪˈɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance) not dissociating into ions when dissolved in water; not ionic.
not dissociating into ions; not ionic

Examples

Many detergents are classified as nonionic because they do not form ions in solution.
Unlike sodium chloride, glucose is a nonionic compound.
Using nonionic surfactants can reduce skin irritation in cosmetic products.

Synset Examples

a nonionic substance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ionic - relating to or involving ions

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' meaning 'not' + 'ionic,' relating to ions.
