rent-seeking

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛntˌsiːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛntˌsiːkɪŋ / (BrE)
(in economics) the practice of increasing one's share of existing wealth without creating new wealth, typically by manipulating the economic or political environment.
seeking profit by manipulating environment

Examples

Many economists argue that government corruption often results from widespread rent-seeking activities.
Corporations engaging in rent-seeking may influence legislation to gain unfair advantages.
The economist criticized rent-seeking behaviors that stifle innovation and economic growth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: profit-seeking - seeking profit through production or trade

Etymology

Formed from 'rent' in the sense of economic rent (excess earnings), combined with 'seeking'.
