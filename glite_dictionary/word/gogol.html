Gogol

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡoʊɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊɡɒl / (BrE)
(1809–1852) A Russian writer renowned for introducing realism to Russian literature, noted for works such as 'Dead Souls' and 'The Overcoat'.
Russian writer; pioneer of realism

Examples

Many literary critics argue that Gogol transformed Russian fiction with his satirical style.
We discussed the absurd humor found in Gogol's short story 'The Nose'.
Her thesis focused on the social commentary woven throughout Gogol's major works.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dostoevsky - Russian novelist, key figure in realism
  • Synonym: Tolstoy - Russian author, major realist novelist

Etymology

From Russian Го́голь (Gógolʹ), family name of Ukrainian origin.
