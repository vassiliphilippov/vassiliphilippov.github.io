alarmist

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɑrmɪst / (AmE), / əˈlɑːmɪst / (BrE)
a person who exaggerates dangers or raises alarm unnecessarily, often causing fear or panic in others
person who raises unnecessary alarm

Examples

The media often accused her of being an alarmist whenever she warned the public about health risks.
Some economists dismissed his predictions as the work of an alarmist.
Nobody wants to be labeled an alarmist, but early warnings can sometimes prevent disasters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pessimist - person who tends to see the worst aspect of things
  • Antonym: optimist - person who tends to be hopeful or confident about the future

Etymology

From 'alarm' + '-ist', modelled after similar agent nouns.
