gravy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪ.vi / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ.vi / (BrE)
a sauce made by adding stock, flour, or other ingredients to the juices and fat left from cooking meat, often served with meat dishes
meat-based sauce

Examples

She poured rich gravy over her mashed potatoes.
The turkey tasted dry without any gravy on it.
After roasting the beef, he made a delicious gravy from the pan juices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sauce - a liquid or semi-liquid accompaniment to food

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪvi / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪvi / (BrE)
The seasoned but not thickened juices that run from cooking meats, often with a little water added.
meat juices from cooking, not thickened

Examples

The chef served the roast beef with plenty of gravy poured over the top.
She collected the flavorful gravy from the pan and served it with mashed potatoes.
Sometimes, a little water is added to the gravy to stretch it further.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gravy - thickened sauce made from meat juices

Etymology

Perhaps from Old French 'grané', meaning a sauce or stew, or possibly from obsolete French 'gravé', sauce.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪ.vi / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ.vi / (BrE)
an unexpected advantage or profit, especially one that comes in addition to what was expected
unexpected profit or advantage

Examples

For Sue, the overtime pay was just gravy on top of her regular salary.
After closing the deal, everything else was gravy for the sales team.
Winning the prize money felt like pure gravy for the already successful entrepreneur.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windfall - a sudden, unexpected gain or advantage
  • Synonym: bonus - an extra amount of money or benefit given as a reward
  • Antonym: loss - the opposite of a profit or gain; the state of losing

Etymology

Originally referred to the sauce, later extended figuratively to mean extra benefit or profit.
