vulgarizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈvʌlɡəˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡəraɪzə / (BrE)
a person who makes something vulgar or less refined, especially by lowering its tone or style
person who makes something vulgar

Examples

Critics accused him of being a vulgarizer of classical literature.
Some say that reality TV has become a vulgarizer of popular culture.
She refused to work with anyone she considered a vulgarizer of serious art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vulgarize - to make something less refined or more common

Etymology

From vulgarize (to make vulgar) + -er (agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvʌlɡəˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡəˌraɪzə / (BrE)
a person who makes something more accessible or popular, often by simplifying or adapting it for the general public
someone who simplifies or popularizes

Examples

Some critics accused the author of being a vulgarizer who reduced complex ideas to simple slogans.
As a skilled vulgarizer, she made scientific concepts understandable to children and adults alike.
The documentary's success was due largely to its host, a renowned vulgarizer of history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: popularizer - a person who makes something popular or widely known
  • Antonym: specialist - a person who is an expert in a specific field and may avoid simplifying topics for the general public

Etymology

From Latin 'vulgus' (the common people) via 'vulgarize' + '-er'.
