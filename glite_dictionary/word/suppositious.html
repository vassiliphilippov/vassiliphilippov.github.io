suppositious

1. ADJ

/ səˈpoʊzɪʃəs / (AmE), / səˈpɒzɪʃəs / (BrE)
Based primarily on supposition or surmise rather than on adequate evidence; not supported by facts.
Based on supposition, not evidence

Examples

The scientist dismissed the suppositious theory as lacking sufficient data to support its claims.
Much of the early history of the region is suppositious, reconstructed from scarce archaeological evidence.
Critics argued that her conclusions were merely suppositious and not grounded in reality.

Synset Examples

theories about the extinction of dinosaurs are still highly conjectural
the supposed reason for his absence
suppositious reconstructions of dead languages
hypothetical situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjectural - Based on or involving guesswork or conjecture
  • Synonym: hypothetical - Based on hypothesis or supposition
  • Antonym: factual - Based on or restricted to facts

Etymology

From Latin suppositicius (placed under, assumed), from suppositus, past participle of supponere (to put under).
