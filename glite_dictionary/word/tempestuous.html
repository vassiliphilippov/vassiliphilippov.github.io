tempestuous

1. ADJ

/ tɛmˈpɛstʃuəs / (AmE), / tɛmˈpɛstjuːəs / (BrE)
(of a person, relationship, or situation) characterized by strong and turbulent or conflicting emotion; stormy.
characterized by violent or turbulent emotions

Examples

Their tempestuous relationship was filled with passionate arguments and reconciliations.
She gave a tempestuous response to the unfair criticism during the meeting.
The artist was known for his tempestuous temperament, which inspired much of his dramatic work.

Synset Examples

a stormy argument
a stormy marriage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling strong emotions; peaceful
  • Synonym: stormy - characterized by strong or violent emotions or weather

Etymology

From Latin 'tempestuosus', from 'tempestas' (storm, weather, season).

2. ADJ

/ tɛmˈpɛstʃuəs / (AmE), / tɛmˈpɛstjuːəs / (BrE)
(of weather or emotions) characterized by strong, turbulent, or violent disturbance; stormy.
stormy; marked by turbulence or violence

Examples

The tempestuous winds knocked down several trees during the night.
She left the meeting after a tempestuous exchange with her manager.
The sailors feared the unpredictable and tempestuous seas ahead.

Synset Examples

angry clouds on the horizon
furious winds
the raging sea

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing disturbance or strong emotion; free from storm

Etymology

From Latin 'tempestuosus' (stormy), from 'tempestas' (storm, season).

Synset POS: s
