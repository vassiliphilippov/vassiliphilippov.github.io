translation

1. NOUN

/ trænzˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / trænzˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(Physics) The motion of a body that moves every point in it the same distance in the same direction, without rotation or distortion.
uniform movement in physics

Examples

The translation of the object across the table occurred without any rotation.
In physics, translation refers to straight-line movement, as opposed to rotation or vibration.
Robotic arms must coordinate both translation and rotation to position objects accurately.

Etymology

From Latin translatio (a carrying across), from transferre (to carry across).

6. NOUN

/ trænzˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / trænsˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which genetic information encoded in messenger RNA directs the synthesis of a specific protein at a ribosome in the cytoplasm
genetics: mRNA-directed protein synthesis
medical

Examples

The process of translation is essential for protein production in living cells.
Mutations in mRNA can disrupt translation and lead to faulty proteins.
During translation, ribosomes assemble amino acids into a polypeptide chain.

Etymology

From Latin 'translatio' (a carrying over), but this biological sense derives from the transfer of genetic information.
