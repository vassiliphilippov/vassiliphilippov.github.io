rumor

1. NOUN

/ ˈruː.mɚ / (AmE), / ˈruː.mə / (BrE)
information or a story that is passed from person to person, often of uncertain or doubtful truth.
unverified information passed between people

Examples

The rumor about the new manager spread quickly through the office.
She denied the rumor that she was leaving the company.
Is there any truth to the rumor we heard last night?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - casual or unconstrained conversation or reports about other people
  • Antonym: fact - a thing that is known or proved to be true

Etymology

From Latin 'rumor' (noise, gossip, report).

2. VERB

/ ˈruːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈruːmə / (BrE)
to circulate or spread a story or piece of information whose truth is uncertain or not confirmed
spread unverified information

Examples

It was rumored that the CEO would resign by the end of the month.
The actress was frequently rumored to be moving abroad, but she never confirmed it.
News of a possible merger was rumored throughout the office before any official statement was made.

Synset Examples

It was rumored that the next president would be a woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - to spread idle talk or rumors about others
  • Synonym: report - to tell or disseminate information; often more formal or official than rumor
  • Antonym: to confirm - to establish the truth or validity of something

Etymology

From Latin 'rumor' meaning noise or popular opinion.
