amentiferous

1. ADJ

/ ˌeɪmənˈtɪfərəs / (AmE), / ˌeɪmənˈtɪfərəs / (BrE)
(of plants) bearing or characterized by aments (catkins); producing or having inflorescences in the form of catkins.
bearing catkins (aments), botany term

Examples

The willow tree is an amentiferous species, producing long, drooping catkins every spring.
Many amentiferous plants are wind-pollinated due to the structure of their catkins.
Botanists identify certain forests by the abundance of amentiferous trees such as birch and alder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catkinate - having or bearing catkins

Etymology

From Latin 'amentum' (strap, thong, catkin) + '-ferous' (bearing).
