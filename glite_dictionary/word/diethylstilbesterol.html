diethylstilbesterol

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪˌɛθəlˌstɪlˈbɛstərɒl / (AmE), / ˌdaɪˌiːθɪlˌstɪlbɪsˈtiːrɒl / (BrE)
a synthetic nonsteroidal estrogen formerly used to treat certain medical conditions in women, but later found to be linked to cancer risks in the offspring of treated mothers
synthetic estrogen linked to health risks
medical

Examples

Doctors no longer prescribe diethylstilbesterol because of its links to cancer in patients' daughters.
A study found a connection between prenatal exposure to diethylstilbesterol and reproductive health problems.
The risks associated with diethylstilbesterol were not fully understood until many years after its use began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estrogen - a group of steroid hormones promoting the development and maintenance of female characteristics

Etymology

From 'diethyl' (two ethyl groups) and 'stilbesterol' (a synthetic estrogen compound named for its stilbene structure).
