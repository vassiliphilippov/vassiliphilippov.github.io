inferior

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə / (BrE)
a person who is considered to be lower in rank, status, or quality than others
person of lower rank or status

Examples

Despite his title, he never looked down on his inferiors or treated them with disrespect.
The company expects its inferiors to follow the instructions of their superiors precisely.
In the rigid hierarchy, inferiors had few opportunities for advancement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superior - a person of higher rank or quality
  • Synonym: subordinate - a person under the authority or control of another

Etymology

From Latin 'inferior' (lower, farther below).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə / (BrE)
A character or symbol that is set or printed slightly below and to the side of another character, often used in mathematical or chemical notation.
character printed below another

Examples

In chemical formulas, the number of atoms is indicated using an inferior next to the element symbol.
The equation included an inferior to denote the base of the logarithm.
Typographers pay special attention to the alignment of each inferior character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subscript - A character written below the line, particularly in mathematics or chemistry

Etymology

From Latin 'inferior', meaning 'lower' or 'below'
