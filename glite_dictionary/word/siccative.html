siccative

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪk.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈsɪk.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
A substance that promotes drying, especially by aiding the removal or evaporation of moisture.
substance that promotes drying

Examples

The artist added a siccative to the oil paint to speed up the drying process.
Industrial coatings often include a siccative to ensure a hard, dry finish more quickly.
You can use a siccative such as calcium oxide when you need to absorb excess moisture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desiccant - a substance that absorbs moisture from the air

Etymology

From French 'siccatif', from Latin 'siccare' (to dry).
