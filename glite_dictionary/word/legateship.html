legateship

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛɡ.ət.ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈlɛɡ.ət.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the office, position, or term of service of a legate, especially in a diplomatic or ecclesiastical context.
the office or post of a legate

Examples

During his legateship, he negotiated several important treaties between the kingdoms.
The papal legateship was considered one of the highest honors in the medieval Church.
Her research focuses on the influence of legates during their legateship in foreign courts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legates - envoys or representatives, especially of the Pope

Etymology

From 'legate' (from Latin legatus, ambassador or envoy) + '-ship' (denoting status or office).
