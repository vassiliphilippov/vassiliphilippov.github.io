constrictive

1. ADJ

/ kənˈstrɪktɪv / (AmE), / kənˈstrɪktɪv / (BrE)
Tending to restrict or limit freedom, movement, or development.
restricting or limiting

Examples

The company's constrictive policies made innovation difficult.
Some people find traditional schools to be constrictive environments that limit creativity.
She left her constrictive job in search of more freedom and flexibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restrictive - Tending to limit or control something
  • Antonym: liberating - Making someone feel free and able to behave as they like

Etymology

From Latin 'constrictus' (bound together, compressed), from 'constringere'.

2. ADJ

/ kənˈstrɪktɪv / (AmE), / kənˈstrɪktɪv / (BrE)
preventing freedom of movement or action; limiting or restricting.
limiting; restricting freedom or scope

Examples

The school implemented constrictive rules that limited students' choices during lunchtime.
Some people find traditional job roles too constrictive for their creative ambitions.
Wearing a tie can feel constrictive around the neck after several hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restrictive - limiting freedom or scope
  • Antonym: permissive - allowing or characterized by great freedom of behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'constringere' (to bind together or tighten).
