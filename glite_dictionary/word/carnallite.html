carnallite

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈnælaɪt / (AmE), / kɑːˈnalʌɪt / (BrE)
a white or reddish mineral consisting of hydrated potassium and magnesium chlorides, used as a source of these elements and as a fertilizer
a mineral of potassium and magnesium chlorides

Examples

The mine produces both carnallite and sylvite for the fertilizer industry.
Geologists identified the red crystals as carnallite based on their chemical composition.
Extraction of magnesium from carnallite is an important industrial process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sylvite - a mineral consisting of potassium chloride
  • Synonym: halite - rock salt; mineral form of sodium chloride

Etymology

Named after German mining engineer Rudolf von Carnall.
