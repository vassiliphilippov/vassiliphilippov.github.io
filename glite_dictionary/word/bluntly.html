bluntly

1. ADV

/ ˈblʌntli / (AmE), / ˈblʌntli / (BrE)
in a direct and straightforward manner, often without regard to politeness or tact
in a direct, straightforward way

Examples

He told her bluntly that her idea would not work.
Sometimes it is better to speak bluntly than to leave room for misunderstanding.
The manager bluntly refused their request for more time.

Synset Examples

he spoke bluntly
he stated his opinion flat-out
he was criticized roundly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: directly - in a direct way, without evasion
  • Antonym: tactfully - in a way that shows sensitivity to others; the opposite of bluntly
