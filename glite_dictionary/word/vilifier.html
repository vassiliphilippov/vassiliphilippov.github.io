vilifier

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪlɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈvɪlɪfaɪə / (BrE)
a person who speaks or writes harsh and damaging things about others, especially by slander or libel
person who slanders or maligns others

Examples

The politician sued his vilifier for spreading false rumors about his personal life.
Despite the harsh words of every vilifier, she remained respectful and dignified.
Throughout history, every leader has faced at least one vocal vilifier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slanderer - a person who makes false and damaging statements about someone
  • Synonym: defamer - a person who attacks someone's character or reputation
  • Antonym: praiser - a person who expresses approval or admiration of someone or something

Etymology

From the verb 'vilify,' which comes from Middle French *vilifier*, from Medieval Latin *vilificare* (to make vile).
