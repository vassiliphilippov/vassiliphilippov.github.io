injuriously

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈdʒʊriəsli / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒʊəriəsli / (BrE)
In a manner that causes or is likely to cause harm or injury; harmfully.
in a harmful or damaging way

Examples

The company was sued for having acted injuriously toward its competitors.
False statements made injuriously can damage someone's reputation.
The chemicals spread injuriously into the local water supply, endangering public health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmfully - in a harmful or damaging way
  • Antonym: beneficially - in a way that produces good results or benefits

Etymology

From 'injurious' + '-ly'; ultimately from Latin 'injuria' (wrong, injury).
