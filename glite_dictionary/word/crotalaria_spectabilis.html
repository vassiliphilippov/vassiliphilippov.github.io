Crotalaria spectabilis

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˌtæləriə spɛkˈteɪbɪlɪs / (AmE), / krəʊˌtæləriə spɛkˈteɪbɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of erect subshrub native to India, having purple-tinted flowers and inflated pods with seeds that rattle when ripe.
species of erect subshrub with rattle pods

Examples

The farmer removed all the Crotalaria spectabilis from his fields due to its toxicity to livestock.
The distinctive rattling sound comes from the seeds inside the pods of Crotalaria spectabilis.
Botanists often study Crotalaria spectabilis for its striking flowers and ecological impact.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name *Crotalaria*, species epithet *spectabilis* meaning 'notable, showy'.
