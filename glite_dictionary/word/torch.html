torch

1. NOUN

/ tɔrtʃ / (AmE), / tɔːtʃ / (BrE)
A small portable electric lamp powered by batteries, used for providing light; called a flashlight in American English.
small portable electric lamp
british

Examples

She grabbed her torch before heading into the dark basement.
When the power went out, we used a torch to find our way.
He always keeps a spare set of batteries for his torch in the kitchen drawer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flashlight - small portable battery-powered electric lamp (American English equivalent)

Etymology

From Old French 'torche', meaning 'twisted thing', originally referring to a twisted roll of cloth dipped in wax.

2. NOUN

/ tɔrtʃ / (AmE), / tɔːtʃ / (BrE)
a device that mixes air and gas to produce a very hot flame, used for tasks such as welding or cutting
device producing a hot flame for welding, cutting

Examples

The welder used a torch to cut through the steel beams.
Please ensure the gas supply is off before lighting the torch.
A propane torch is ideal for soldering copper pipes in plumbing work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blowtorch - a tool producing a hot flame, typically for soldering or welding

Etymology

From Old French *torche*, meaning 'twisted thing, torch', from Latin *torquere* (to twist); originally referred to a stick with material for burning.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02858670-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ tɔrtʃ / (AmE), / tɔːtʃ / (BrE)
A portable light usually carried in the hand, typically made of a stick with a flammable material at one end that is lit to provide illumination.
portable handheld light source

Examples

The explorer raised his torch to light the ancient cave walls.
A ceremonial torch was carried through the streets during the festival.
The villagers gathered around the burning torch as night fell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lantern - portable lamp with a protective enclosure for the flame

Etymology

From Old French *torche* (twisted thing, torch), from Latin *torqua*, from *torquere* (to twist).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04463386-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ tɔrtʃ / (AmE), / tɔːtʃ / (BrE)
to set fire to something deliberately, especially as an act of arson
burn something deliberately

Examples

After being evicted, the tenant torched the apartment out of spite.
The authorities believe someone torched the abandoned warehouse last night.
Vandals threatened to torch several vehicles during the protest.

Synset Examples

The madman torched the barns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to burn down - to destroy by fire
  • Synonym: set fire to - to ignite deliberately
