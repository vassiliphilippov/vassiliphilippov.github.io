lagophthalmos

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæɡ.əfˈθæl.moʊs / (AmE), / ˌlaɡ.ɒfˈθal.mɒs / (BrE)
an abnormal condition in which a person is unable to close their eyelids completely
inability to fully close the eyelids
medical

Examples

The patient developed lagophthalmos following facial nerve injury after surgery.
Sleeping with lagophthalmos can lead to dryness and irritation of the eye.
Treatment for lagophthalmos may include surgery or eye drops to protect the cornea.

Etymology

From Greek 'lagōs' (hare) + 'ophthalmos' (eye), referring to the open-eyed appearance similar to a hare.
