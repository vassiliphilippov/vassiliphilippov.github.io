Eragrostis abyssinica

1. NOUN

/ ɛrəˌɡrɑstɪs əˌbɪsɪˈnɪkə / (AmE), / ɛrəˌɡrɒstɪs əˌbɪsɪˈnɪkə / (BrE)
an African grass cultivated for its edible seeds, producing white flour of good quality, and also used as forage and hay
African cereal grass used for food and forage

Examples

Eragrostis abyssinica is an important staple crop in several African regions.
The white flour produced from Eragrostis abyssinica is valued for making traditional bread.
Farmers often grow Eragrostis abyssinica both for its grain and as a source of animal forage.

Etymology

From Greek 'eragrostis' (a grass) and Latin 'abyssinica' (Abyssinian, from Abyssinia/Ethiopia).
