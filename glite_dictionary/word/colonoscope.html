colonoscope

1. NOUN

/ kəˈloʊnəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / kəˈləʊnəskəʊp / (BrE)
a long, flexible fiber-optic instrument used to examine the inside of the colon
medical instrument for examining the colon
medical

Examples

The doctor used a colonoscope to look for any abnormalities in the patient's colon.
A colonoscope allows physicians to detect polyps and cancer early during a colonoscopy.
Before the procedure, the nurse checked that the colonoscope was working properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endoscope - an instrument for visualizing the interior of a bodily organ or cavity

Etymology

From Greek kolon (colon) + -scope (instrument for viewing).
