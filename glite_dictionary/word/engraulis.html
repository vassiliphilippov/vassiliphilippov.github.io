Engraulis

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈɡroʊlɪs / (AmE), / ɛnˈɡrɔːlɪs / (BrE)
the genus of small, schooling fish commonly known as anchovies, belonging to the family Engraulidae
genus of anchovies

Examples

The genus Engraulis includes several species widely distributed in marine waters around the world.
Research on Engraulis has contributed to our understanding of marine food chains.
Fishermen targeted Engraulis species due to their commercial value as anchovies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anchovy - a small, common saltwater forage fish

Etymology

From Latin Engraulis, from Ancient Greek en (in) + graulos (mouth), referring to the fish's large mouth.
