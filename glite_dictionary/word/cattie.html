cattie

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæti / (AmE), / ˈkæti / (BrE)
a unit of weight used in southeastern Asia, especially China, equal to approximately 500 grams
Asian unit of weight (~500g)

Examples

The merchant weighed the tea using a traditional cattie scale.
One cattie of rice was enough to feed the family for several days.
Historical records often mention goods measured in catties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catty - variant spelling or pronunciation for the same Asian unit of weight

Etymology

From Malay 'kati', from Chinese (Cantonese) 'kat1' (斤).
