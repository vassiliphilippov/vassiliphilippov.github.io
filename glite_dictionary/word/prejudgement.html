prejudgement

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
A judgment formed before hearing or considering the relevant evidence or facts.
forming a judgment before evidence

Examples

Her prejudgement of the case led to an unfair decision.
It's important to avoid prejudgement and wait for all the facts to emerge.
The committee was criticized for its obvious prejudgement of the witness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudice - an unreasonable dislike or preference not based on reason or experience
  • Antonym: impartiality - the quality of being fair and not having a prejudgement or bias

Etymology

From pre- (before) + judgment; modeled after the construction of 'prejudice.'
