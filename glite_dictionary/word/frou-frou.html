frou-frou

1. ADJ

/ ˈfruːˌfruː / (AmE), / ˈfruː.fruː / (BrE)
very elaborate, showy, or excessively decorative in style or appearance, often to the point of being overly ornate
elaborately showy; excessively decorative

Examples

She thought the wedding invitations were far too frou-frou for her taste.
I don't usually go for frou-frou decor, but the room looked beautiful.
That restaurant serves good food without any frou-frou presentation.

Synset Examples

She prefers simple styles over frou-frou outfits.
Her dress was too frou-frou for the formal event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornate - elaborately or highly decorated
  • Antonym: simple - lacking adornment or elaboration

Etymology

From French 'frou-frou' (the rustling of silk), used to describe frilly or elaborate styles.
