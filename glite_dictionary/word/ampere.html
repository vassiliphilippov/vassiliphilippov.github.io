ampere

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmˌpɪr / (AmE), / ˈæmˌpɪə / (BrE)
a former unit of electric current, slightly smaller than the present SI ampere
former unit of electric current

Examples

The old standard ampere was redefined in terms of more precise constants.
Historical texts often refer to the ampere with values slightly different from today.
Electrical instruments from the early 20th century were calibrated in the previous definition of the ampere.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ampere (SI unit) - current SI base unit of electric current

Etymology

Named after the French physicist André-Marie Ampère (1775–1836).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæmˌpɪr / (AmE), / ˈæmpeə / (BrE)
the standard unit of electric current in the International System of Units (SI), equal to the current that flows with an electric potential of one volt across a resistance of one ohm.
SI unit of electric current

Examples

The current in this device is measured in amperes for accuracy.
A standard household circuit often carries between 15 and 50 amperes.
The technician checked if the ampere rating matched the equipment's specifications.

Synset Examples

a typical household circuit carries 15 to 50 amps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amp - informal abbreviation for ampere

Etymology

Named after French physicist André-Marie Ampère (1775–1836).
