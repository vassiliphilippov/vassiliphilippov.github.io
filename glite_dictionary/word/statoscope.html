statoscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪtəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈstatəskəʊp / (BrE)
An instrument used in aviation to indicate small changes in an aircraft's altitude by recording variations in atmospheric pressure.
instrument for detecting small altitude changes

Examples

The pilot monitored the statoscope to ensure the balloon maintained a steady altitude.
Accurate readings from the statoscope helped the crew detect even minor ascents or descents.
Older airships often relied on a statoscope to measure subtle altitude changes during flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: altimeter - an instrument for measuring altitude, especially in aircraft

Etymology

From Greek 'statos' (standing, stationary) + '-scope' (instrument for viewing).
