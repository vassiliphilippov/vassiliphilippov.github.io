to disclaim

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈkleɪm / (AmE), / dɪsˈkleɪm / (BrE)
to deny or refuse to accept responsibility, association, or connection with something; to formally reject a claim or right
deny responsibility or connection

Examples

The politician was quick to disclaim any involvement in the scandal.
She disclaimed all liability for the accident in her official statement.
After reading the agreement, he decided to disclaim his rights to the property.

Synset Examples

He disclaimed any responsibility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deny - to state that something is not true or that you do not accept it
  • Antonym: claim - to assert ownership or right, often the opposite sense in context

Etymology

From Old French *desclamer*, from Latin *disclamare* (to cry out against).

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈkleɪm / (AmE), / dɪsˈkleɪm / (BrE)
to renounce or give up a legal claim, right, or title to something, especially formally or officially
formally renounce a legal claim or title

Examples

He chose to disclaim any interest in the estate to avoid a lengthy legal dispute.
The company may disclaim responsibility for lost items in its policy.
After reviewing the contract, she decided to disclaim her rights to the property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renounce - to formally declare one is abandoning a claim, right, or possession
  • Antonym: claim - to assert ownership or a right to something

Etymology

From Old French 'desclamer', from Latin 'disclamare' (to cry out against).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00760242-v
Synset POS: v
