Dioscorea elephantipes

1. NOUN

/ diˌɑːskɔːriə ɛˌlɛfən'taɪpiːz / (AmE), / daɪˌɒskɔːriə ˌɛlɪfən'taɪpiːz / (BrE)
A South African climbing plant with a large, woody, deeply fissured rootstock, known for its distinctive appearance.
South African vine with massive fissured rootstock

Examples

The unusual appearance of the Dioscorea elephantipes makes it a sought-after plant for collectors.
In botanical gardens, Dioscorea elephantipes is often grown for its striking, turtle shell-like rootstock.
While hiking in South Africa, we spotted a wild Dioscorea elephantipes climbing over nearby rocks.

Etymology

From Latinized genus name Dioscorea (after the ancient Greek physician Dioscorides) and species epithet elephantipes (elephant-footed) from Latin 'elephas' (elephant) + '-ipes' (footed).
