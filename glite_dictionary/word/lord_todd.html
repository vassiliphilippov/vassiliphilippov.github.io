Lord Todd

1. PROPN

/ lɔrd tɑd / (AmE), / lɔːd tɒd / (BrE)
(1907–1997) Scottish chemist known for his research into the structure of nucleic acids and recipient of the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1957.
Scottish chemist; Nobel laureate

Examples

Lord Todd was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his work on nucleic acids.
The scientific contributions of Lord Todd have had a lasting impact on molecular biology.
Many chemists look up to Lord Todd as a pioneer in nucleic acid research.
