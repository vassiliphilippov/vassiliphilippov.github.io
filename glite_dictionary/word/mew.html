mew

1. VERB

/ mjuː / (AmE), / mjuː / (BrE)
(of a seagull, cat, or similar animal) to utter a high-pitched cry or meow.
to utter a high-pitched cry

Examples

The gulls mew overhead as they circle the fishing boats.
Late at night, the stray cats mew beneath my window, looking for food.
She heard a kitten mewing softly from inside the box.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meow - the characteristic cry of a cat

Etymology

From Middle English mewen, from Old French miauver, related to the sound made by a cat.

/ mjuː / (AmE), / mjuː / (BrE)
(of a cat or kitten) to make a high-pitched crying or mewing sound.
make a catlike cry

Examples

I heard a stray cat mewing outside my window all night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meow - to make the typical crying sound of a cat

3. NOUN

/ mjuː / (AmE), / mjuː / (BrE)
a soft, high-pitched sound made by a cat, or any similar sound
the soft cry of a cat

Examples

The kitten let out a tiny mew as it searched for its mother.
From behind the door, she heard a faint mew in the darkness.
The lost cat's constant mew made it easy for us to find her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miaow - the characteristic crying sound of a cat
  • Antonym: purr - a low, continuous, vibrating sound made by a cat

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound itself; compare Old French miau and Middle English mewen.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07401214-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ mjuː / (AmE), / mjuː / (BrE)
a small gull (Larus canus) found in Eurasia and northeastern North America, also known as the common gull
a small Eurasian/North American gull

Examples

Flocks of mew were spotted along the rocky shoreline during the migration season.
The mew is often confused with other species of gull due to its similar appearance.
Birdwatchers traveled to the coast hoping to see a rare mew among the larger gulls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gull - a seabird of the family Laridae

Etymology

From Middle English *mewe*, from Old English *māw* (gull), related to Old High German *mēwe*.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
