donkeywork

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑŋkiˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈdɒŋkiˌwɜːk / (BrE)
hard, monotonous, routine work that is necessary but not enjoyable or rewarding
dull, routine hard work

Examples

She was left to do all the donkeywork while her colleagues got the credit.
Most interns are given the donkeywork in their first few months at a company.
The volunteers handled the donkeywork of setting up chairs and cleaning up afterward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drudgery - dull, difficult, and repetitive work
  • Synonym: grunt work - routine or tedious work
  • Antonym: pleasure - work or activity that is enjoyable

Etymology

Formed from 'donkey' (an animal associated with carrying burdens) + 'work'.
