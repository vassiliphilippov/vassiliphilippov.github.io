nonextant

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈɛk.stənt / (AmE), / nɒnˈɛk.stənt / (BrE)
no longer in existence; having ceased to exist or have living representatives
no longer in existence

Examples

Many ancient manuscripts are now considered nonextant and have been lost to history.
The records from the early 17th century are believed to be nonextant.
Scientists searched for signs of the nonextant organism but found no traces.

Synset Examples

an extinct species of fish
an extinct royal family
extinct laws and customs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extinct - no longer existing or living
  • Antonym: extant - still in existence; surviving

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'non-' (not) + 'extant', from Latin 'exstans' (existing, standing out).

2. ADJ

/ nɑnˈɛk.stənt / (AmE), / nɒnˈɛk.stənt / (BrE)
no longer existing or inaccessible due to loss or destruction
no longer existing; lost or destroyed

Examples

The museum could not display the manuscript because its nonextant original had been destroyed centuries ago.
Few details are known about the nonextant temple once mentioned in ancient texts.
Researchers lamented the nonextant data that was critical for their study.

Synset Examples

its nonextant original was written on vellum

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extant - still in existence; not lost or destroyed

Etymology

From Latin non- (not) + extant (standing out, existing).
