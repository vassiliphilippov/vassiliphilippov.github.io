Popillia japonica

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈpɪliə dʒəˈpoʊnɪkə / (AmE), / pɒˈpɪliə dʒəˈpəʊnɪkə / (BrE)
a small metallic green and brown beetle native to eastern Asia, now a serious plant pest in North America
species of beetle; serious pest

Examples

The Popillia japonica has caused extensive damage to rose bushes in the area.
Gardeners are advised to monitor their plants for signs of Popillia japonica infestation.
Researchers have developed traps to control the spread of Popillia japonica in agricultural fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese beetle - common name for Popillia japonica, a beetle pest

Etymology

Modern Latin: genus name from Latinized form of 'Japan' (Japonica) indicating its origin.
