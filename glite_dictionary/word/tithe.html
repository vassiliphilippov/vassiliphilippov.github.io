tithe

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪð / (AmE), / ˈtaɪð / (BrE)
a tax or contribution of one tenth of a person's income or produce, especially for the support of the church or clergy
one-tenth tax, especially for the church

Examples

Many medieval farmers were required to pay a tithe to the local church each year.
She donated a tithe of her earnings to charity every month.
The law required landowners to give a tithe of their harvest as part of their religious obligations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax - a compulsory contribution to state revenue
  • Degree Lower: donation - something given to a charity or cause

Etymology

From Old English *teogoþa* (tenth), related to German *Zehnte* (tenth) and Latin *decimus* (tenth).
