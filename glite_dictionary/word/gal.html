gal

1. NOUN

/ ɡæl / (AmE), / ɡæl / (BrE)
a unit of gravitational acceleration equal to one centimeter per second squared, used especially in geophysics
a unit of gravitational acceleration

Examples

The gravitational field at the Earth's surface is about 980 gals.
Seismic surveys often measure small changes in gravity in units of the gal.
A laboratory experiment recorded an increase in acceleration of 5 gals during the test.

Etymology

Named after Galileo Galilei, Italian physicist and astronomer.
