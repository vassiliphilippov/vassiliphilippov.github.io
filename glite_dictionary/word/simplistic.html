simplistic

1. ADJ

/ sɪmˈplɪstɪk / (AmE), / sɪmˈplɪstɪk / (BrE)
treating complex issues and problems as if they were much simpler than they really are, often in a way that is misleading or superficial.
treating complex things as simpler than they are

Examples

His analysis of the problem is far too simplistic to be useful.
The documentary was criticized for its simplistic view of social issues.
Trying to solve poverty with such simplistic solutions will not work.

Synset Examples

a simplistic theory of the universe
simplistic arguments of the ruling party

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: simple - easily understood or done; presenting no difficulty

Etymology

Formed from 'simple' + '-istic', influenced by words like 'realistic'.
