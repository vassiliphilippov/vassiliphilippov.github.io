harpooner

1. NOUN

/ hɑːrˈpunər / (AmE), / hɑːˈpuːnə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to throw or use a harpoon, especially in hunting whales or large sea animals
person who uses a harpoon

Examples

The experienced harpooner stood ready at the bow of the boat.
In Moby Dick, Queequeg is known for his skill as a harpooner.
Each harpooner played a vital role in the whaling crew's success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harpoonist - person who uses or throws a harpoon

Etymology

From 'harpoon' (via French 'harpon'), with agent noun suffix '-er'.
