crust

1. NOUN

/ krʌst / (AmE), / krʌst / (BrE)
the hard outer layer of the Earth, lying above the mantle and forming the continents and ocean floors
outer layer of the Earth

Examples

The Earth's crust is much thinner under the oceans than under the continents.
Scientists study earthquakes to learn more about the movement of the crust.
Volcanoes form where the crust is especially weak or thin.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mantle - the layer of the Earth beneath the crust
  • Degree Higher: core - the central part of the Earth below the mantle

2. VERB

/ krʌst / (AmE), / krʌst / (BrE)
(of a substance) to form a hard outer layer or become covered with a crust.
to form a hard outer layer

Examples

If you leave the soup out overnight, it may crust on top.
Mud can crust as it dries in the hot sun.
As the wound healed, it began to crust over.

Synset Examples

The bread crusted in the oven

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encrust - to cover or become covered with a crust or hard coating

Etymology

From Old French 'crouste', from Latin 'crusta' (hard surface, shell).

3. NOUN

/ krʌst / (AmE), / krʌst / (BrE)
a hard outer layer that forms on or covers something, such as bread, the earth, or other materials
hard outer covering or layer

Examples

After baking, a golden crust formed on top of the bread.
Scientists drilled through the Earth's crust to study the layers below.
She scraped the burnt crust off the casserole before serving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rind - the tough outer skin of some fruits, cheeses, or foods
  • Antonym: core - the central part of something, especially as opposed to its outer layer

Etymology

From Latin crusta (hard surface, shell, rind).
