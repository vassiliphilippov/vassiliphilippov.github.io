to pummel

1. VERB

/ ˈpʌməl / (AmE), / ˈpʌməl / (BrE)
to strike repeatedly, especially with the fists
strike repeatedly with fists

Examples

The boxer pummeled his opponent with a series of quick punches.
Angry waves pummeled the coastline during the storm.
She pummeled the dough until it was soft and ready to bake.

Synset Examples

The pedestrians pummeled the demonstrators

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beat - to strike repeatedly
  • Synonym: pound - to strike heavily or repeatedly

Etymology

Possibly a variant of pommel, from Old French *pomel* (knob, hilt), referring to hitting with the hand grip of a sword.
