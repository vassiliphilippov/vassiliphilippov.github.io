chalcopyrite

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.kəˈpaɪ.raɪt / (AmE), / ˌkæl.kəʊˈpaɪ.raɪt / (BrE)
A yellow mineral consisting of copper iron sulfide, the principal ore of copper.
a yellow copper iron sulfide mineral

Examples

Miners discovered a rich vein of chalcopyrite deep within the mountain.
The brilliant yellow color of chalcopyrite often leads it to be mistaken for gold.
Extraction of copper from chalcopyrite is an important industrial process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrite - a common iron sulfide mineral, also called fool's gold

Etymology

From Greek 'khalkos' (copper) + 'pyrites' (striking fire).
