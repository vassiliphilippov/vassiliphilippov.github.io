Chechen

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɛtʃən / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛtʃən / (BrE)
of or relating to Chechnya, its people, or its culture.
of Chechnya or its people/culture

Examples

The Chechen language is spoken by people throughout Chechnya and the Caucasus.
She prepared a traditional Chechen dish for her family.
Political tensions have shaped Chechen society over the last few decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chechnya - a republic in southwestern Russia, in the North Caucasus region

Etymology

From Russian 'chechentsy', via Chechen 'Noxçiyn'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02702576-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃiːtʃən / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛtʃən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Chechnya, a republic in southwestern Russia
native or inhabitant of Chechnya

Examples

Many Chechens have moved to large cities in Russia in recent years.
The Chechen community maintains strong cultural traditions despite years of conflict.
He met a Chechen who shared stories of his homeland.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Chechnya - a republic in southwestern Russia, home of the Chechen people

Etymology

From Russian Чече́нец (Chechénets), from Chechen Ноxчи (Noxchi, the Chechen people's endonym).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09932365-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛtʃən / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛtʃ(ɪ)n / (BrE)
the northern Caucasian language spoken by the Chechen people of the Chechnya region
northern Caucasian language of Chechnya

Examples

She is fluent in both Russian and Chechen.
Many children in the region learn Chechen as their first language at home.
The poem was originally written in Chechen before being translated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chechen - a member of the Chechen ethnic group

Etymology

From Russian 'Chechen', derived from 'Chechnya', the region inhabited by the Chechen people, ultimately from their own name 'Nokhchiy'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06991082-n
Synset POS: n
