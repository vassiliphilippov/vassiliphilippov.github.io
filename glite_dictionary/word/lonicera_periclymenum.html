Lonicera periclymenum

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈnɪsərə ˌpɛrɪˈklaɪmənəm / (AmE), / ləˈnɪsərə ˌpɛrɪˈklɪmɪnəm / (BrE)
A species of honeysuckle native to Europe, known for its fragrant red and yellowish-white flowers and twining stems.
European honeysuckle species

Examples

The Lonicera periclymenum in her garden filled the summer evenings with a sweet, intoxicating scent.
Gardeners often choose Lonicera periclymenum for trellises because of its beautiful climbing habit and fragrant flowers.
Many butterflies are attracted to the blossoms of Lonicera periclymenum in late spring and early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - Climbing plant with sweet-smelling flowers, of the genus Lonicera

Etymology

Genus name from Latin *Lonicera* (after botanist Adam Lonicer); species name from Latin *periclymenum* (a kind of honeysuckle).
