bungling

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʌŋɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
performing tasks in a clumsy or incompetent manner; showing a lack of skill or aptitude.
clumsy or incompetent; lacking skill

Examples

The bungling repairman made the problem even worse with his careless mistakes.
Despite their bungling attempts, the team failed to solve the puzzle.
A bungling thief accidentally triggered the alarm and was quickly caught.

Synset Examples

a bungling workman
did a clumsy job
his fumbling attempt to put up a shelf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or action; lacking skill or grace
  • Antonym: competent - having the necessary ability, knowledge, or skill to do something successfully

Etymology

From the verb 'bungle', likely from Middle English, of unknown origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbʌŋɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
clumsy or inept, especially in handling tasks or situations, often resulting in mistakes or poor performance
clumsy or inept, prone to mistakes

Examples

The bungling waiter spilled soup all over the customer's lap.
Her bungling attempt to fix the computer only made things worse.
Critics called it a bungling performance filled with mistakes on opening night.

Synset Examples

a bumbling mechanic
a bungling performance
ham-handed governmental interference
could scarcely empty a scuttle of ashes, so handless was the poor creature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsy - awkward in movements or handling things, lacking coordination
  • Antonym: skillful - showing skill or expertise, especially with the hands

Etymology

Present participle of 'bungle', probably of Scandinavian origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00063822-s
Synset POS: s
