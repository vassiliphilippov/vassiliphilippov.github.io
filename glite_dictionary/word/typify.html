to typify

1. VERB

/ ˈtɪpɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɪpɪfʌɪ / (BrE)
to be a typical example or representative of something; to embody the essential characteristics of a type, group, or class
be a typical example of something

Examples

Many people's work habits typify the modern busy lifestyle.
The small village typifies the charm of rural England.
Her paintings typify the style that became popular in the 1970s.

Synset Examples

The fugue typifies Bach's style of composition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to epitomize - to serve as a perfect example of; to embody the essential characteristics

Etymology

From Greek 'typos' (impression, model) via French 'typifier'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02704983-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈtɪpɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɪpɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to be a typical example or symbol of something; to represent or symbolize by an image or model
be a symbol or typical example

Examples

The red rose often typifies love and passion in literature.
Many believe that the pyramids typify the ancient Egyptian civilization.
His dedication and hard work typify the spirit of the company.

Synset Examples

What does the Statue of Liberty symbolize?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to symbolize - to represent or stand for something, especially by an image, sign, or symbol

Etymology

From Greek tupos (a mark, figure) via French typifier and late Latin typificare.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00837915-v
Synset POS: v
