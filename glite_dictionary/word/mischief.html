mischief

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪstʃɪf / (AmE), / ˈmɪstʃɪf / (BrE)
the quality or state of causing harm or evil, often with malicious intent
the state of being harmful or evil

Examples

The villain's mischief brought suffering to the entire village.
There was a sense of mischief in his actions that went beyond simple teasing.
Unchecked mischief can have serious consequences for a community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malice - the intention or desire to do evil

Etymology

From Old French meschief (misfortune, harm), from meschever (to end badly).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪs.tʃɪf / (AmE), / ˈmɪs.tʃɪf / (BrE)
Reckless or playful behavior that causes annoyance, discomfort, or minor harm to others.
playful or annoying behavior

Examples

The children’s mischief kept the teacher on her toes all afternoon.
He was always getting into mischief, especially when he was bored.
Their harmless mischief brought a smile to everyone's face despite the mess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prank - a playful trick or joke
  • Synonym: trouble - problems or difficulties caused by misbehavior
  • Antonym: obedience - behavior in compliance with rules or authority

Etymology

From Old French meschief (misfortune, harm), from meschever (to end badly).
