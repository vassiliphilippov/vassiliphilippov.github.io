sottish

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɑːtɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɒtɪʃ / (BrE)
Habitually drunken; given to excessive drinking of alcohol.
habitually drunken

Examples

His sottish behavior embarrassed everyone at the party.
The novel depicts a sottish old man who cannot control his drinking.
Years of sottish habits had taken a toll on his health.

Synset Examples

a bibulous fellow
a bibulous evening
his boozy drinking companions
thick boozy singing
a drunken binge
two drunken gentlemen holding each other up
sottish behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drunken - Habitually drunk; intoxicated
  • Synonym: bibulous - Excessively fond of drinking alcohol
  • Antonym: sober - Not affected by alcohol; not drunk

Etymology

From Old French 'sot' (fool, drunkard), from Latin 'stultus' (foolish).
