adviser

1. NOUN

/ ədˈvaɪzər / (AmE), / ədˈvaɪzə / (BrE)
A person with expertise who gives advice or guidance in a particular field.
expert who gives advice

Examples

The school appointed an adviser to help students with their college applications.
As a financial adviser, she helps clients plan their investments.
He works as an adviser to several government committees.

Synset Examples

an adviser helped students select their courses
the United States sent military advisors to Guatemala

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advisor - alternative spelling: a person who gives advice
  • Synonym: consultant - a person who provides expert advice professionally
  • Antonym: client - a person who receives advice from an adviser

Etymology

From Old French 'aviser' (advise), from Latin 'advisare'.
