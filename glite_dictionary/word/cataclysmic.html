cataclysmic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkæt̬əˈklɪzmɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈklɪzmɪk / (BrE)
causing great destruction or sudden, violent change; severely disastrous or devastating.
severely destructive or disastrous

Examples

The sudden collapse of the dam had cataclysmic consequences for the town.
Scientists warn that unchecked climate change could lead to cataclysmic environmental effects.
A cataclysmic volcanic eruption destroyed several villages in the region.

Synset Examples

cataclysmic nuclear war
a cataclysmic earthquake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disastrous - causing great damage or harm
  • Synonym: devastating - highly destructive or damaging

Etymology

From Greek kataklysmos (deluge, flood) via French cataclysme.
