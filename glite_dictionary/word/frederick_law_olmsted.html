Frederick Law Olmsted

1. PROPN

/ ˈfrɛdərɪk lɔ ˈɑlmstɛd / (AmE), / ˈfrɛdərɪk lɔː ˈɒlmstɛd / (BrE)
(1822–1903) United States landscape architect, chiefly known for designing Central Park in New York City and other major public parks.
American landscape architect, designer of Central Park

Examples

Frederick Law Olmsted was instrumental in shaping the landscape of Central Park in New York City.
The designs of Frederick Law Olmsted profoundly influenced urban park planning in America.
Many American cities owe their iconic green spaces to the vision of Frederick Law Olmsted.
