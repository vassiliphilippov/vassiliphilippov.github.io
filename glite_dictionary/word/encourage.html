to encourage

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkɝːrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌrɪdʒ / (BrE)
to give someone support, confidence, or hope; to inspire someone to continue or succeed
give support or confidence; inspire hope

Examples

Her teachers always encourage her to pursue her dreams.
The coach encouraged the team after their tough loss.
Parents should encourage children to ask questions and explore their interests.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to discourage - to cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
  • Synonym: to hearten - to make someone more cheerful or confident

Etymology

From Old French encouragier, from en- (make) + courage (courage).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈkɝːrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌrɪdʒ / (BrE)
to give someone support, confidence, or hope so that they will do or continue to do something; to spur on
give support, confidence, or hope; spur on

Examples

Her coach always tries to encourage her before a big race.
Parents should encourage their children to ask questions and explore.
Positive feedback can encourage employees to perform better at work.

Synset Examples

His financial success encouraged him to look for a wife

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to discourage - to make someone less confident or enthusiastic about doing something
  • Synonym: to motivate - to provide someone with a reason or incentive to do something

Etymology

From Old French encouragier, from en- (make, put in) + courage (courage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00773386-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈkɜrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌrɪdʒ / (BrE)
to help the progress or development of something; to promote or foster growth
promote or support growth

Examples

The new program aims to encourage innovation among young entrepreneurs.
We need to encourage healthy eating habits in children from an early age.
Planting more trees will encourage biodiversity in the area.

Synset Examples

I am promoting the use of computers in the classroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to promote - to support or actively encourage a cause, venture, or aim
  • Antonym: to discourage - to cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm; to hinder growth

Etymology

From Old French encourager (to make courageous, hearten), from en- (make) + courage (courage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02560630-v
Synset POS: v
