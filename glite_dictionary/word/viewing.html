viewing

1. NOUN

/ ˈvjuːɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈvjuːɪŋ / (BrE)
A gathering or vigil held for mourners to see and pay respects to a deceased person's body before the funeral or burial.
funeral vigil to see the deceased

Examples

The family held a viewing the night before the funeral to allow friends to say goodbye.
Many people attended the viewing to pay their final respects to the deceased.
The viewing was held at the funeral home and lasted for several hours.

Synset Examples

there's no weeping at an Irish wake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wake - a vigil held over a corpse before burial, especially in Irish tradition

2. NOUN

/ ˈvjuɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈvjuːɪŋ / (BrE)
an occasion when a film, television program, or other visual content is shown to an audience
showing of a film or television program

Examples

We attended a special viewing of the director's latest movie last night.
The museum will host a private viewing of the documentary before its public release.
Due to high demand, there will be an extra viewing scheduled for the film this weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showing - an occasion when something is shown, especially a film
