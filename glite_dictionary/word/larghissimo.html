larghissimo

1. ADJ

/ lɑrˈɡisɪmoʊ / (AmE), / lɑːˈɡɪsɪməʊ / (BrE)
(in music) as slow and broad as possible in tempo; slower than largo.
very slow musical tempo

Examples

The composer marked the opening movement larghissimo to create a sense of stillness.
The conductor instructed the orchestra to play the passage larghissimo for dramatic effect.
In this piece, the larghissimo tempo challenges musicians to maintain control and expression.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: largo - (in music) slow and broad in tempo

Etymology

From Italian 'larghissimo', superlative of 'largo' (broad, slow), meaning 'broadest' or 'slowest'.
