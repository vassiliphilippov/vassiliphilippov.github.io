domicile

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑː.mə.saɪl / (AmE), / ˈdɒm.ɪ.saɪl / (BrE)
(law) the place where a person has their permanent home or principal establishment, and to which they intend to return whenever absent.
a person's permanent legal home
legal

Examples

The court had to determine his domicile before proceeding with the case.
She changed her domicile to France after living there for several years.
A person's domicile is crucial in deciding their tax obligations.

Synset Examples

what's his legal residence?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: residence - the place where someone lives
  • Synonym: home - the place where one lives permanently

Etymology

From Latin domicilium (dwelling, abode), from domus (house).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑː.məˌsaɪl / (AmE), / ˈdɒm.ɪ.saɪl / (BrE)
a house or other place where someone lives; a home or residence, often used in legal or formal contexts
a person's home or permanent residence

Examples

The university requires students to provide proof of domicile when applying for housing.
After retiring, she established her permanent domicile in the countryside.
He claimed France as his legal domicile for tax purposes.

  • Synonym: abode - a place of residence; a house or home

3. VERB

/ ˈdɑː.mə.saɪl / (AmE), / ˈdɒm.ɪ.saɪl / (BrE)
to establish or make one's home in a particular place or community, often as a legal residence
to make one's home in a place
legal

Examples

Under the current law, you must domicile in the state for at least six months to qualify as a resident.
After many years abroad, she decided to domicile in New York.
Corporations can domicile themselves in countries with more favorable tax laws.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
