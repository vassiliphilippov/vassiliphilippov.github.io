cicero

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪsəˌroʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪsərəʊ / (BrE)
a unit of typographical measure used in printing, equal to about 4.5 millimeters, slightly larger than an em
typographic unit; about 4.5 mm

Examples

The designer specified a margin of one cicero around the text block.
Traditional French printers often used the cicero as their standard unit of measure.
This headline is set to a width of six ciceros in the layout.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: em - a unit of typographic measure equal to the currently specified point size

Etymology

From the French 'cicéro', named after Roman orator Cicero, whose works were used to standardize this unit.
