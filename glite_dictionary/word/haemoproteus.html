Haemoproteus

1. NOUN

/ hiː.məˈproʊ.ti.əs / (AmE), / hiː.məˈprəʊ.ti.əs / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic protozoans found in the blood of birds, reptiles, and amphibians, some of which can cause disease.
genus of blood parasites in animals
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered Haemoproteus parasites in the blood samples of wild songbirds.
Infections caused by Haemoproteus can affect the health of certain bird populations.
Veterinarians studied the life cycle of Haemoproteus in captive reptiles.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek haima (blood) + proteus (a sea god, name used for protozoan genus).
