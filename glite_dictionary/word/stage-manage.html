to stage-manage

1. VERB

/ steɪdʒ ˈmænɪdʒ / (AmE), / steɪdʒ ˈmænɪdʒ / (BrE)
to organize and supervise the production and performance of a play or event, ensuring everything runs smoothly
oversee and organize a performance

Examples

She was asked to stage-manage the high school's spring musical.
It takes a lot of skill to stage-manage a live television broadcast.
They hired an experienced professional to stage-manage the charity gala.

Synset Examples

He was hired to stage-manage the annual theater festival.
To stage-manage effectively, you need excellent organizational skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direct - to control and guide the making of a play, film or performance

Etymology

From 'stage' (the area for performances) + 'manage' (to control or supervise).
