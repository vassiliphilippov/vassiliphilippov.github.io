tubeless

1. ADJ

/ ˈtuːbləs / (AmE), / ˈtjuːbləs / (BrE)
(of a pneumatic tire) designed to be airtight without the need for an inner tube.
tire design without inner tube

Examples

Many modern cars use tubeless tires for improved performance and safety.
Switching from tube-type to tubeless tires can help reduce flat tires caused by pinches.
He replaced the old inner tube with a new tubeless tire for his mountain bike.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tubed - having or using an inner tube (of a tire)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04502057-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈtuːbləs / (AmE), / ˈtjuːbləs / (BrE)
(of a tire) not needing an inner tube; designed to be used without an inner tube.
not needing an inner tube (tire)

Examples

Many modern bicycles now use tubeless tires for better puncture resistance.
He replaced his old tires with tubeless ones to avoid frequent flats.
The mechanic explained the advantages of a tubeless tire system over a traditional tubed setup.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tubed - having or using an inner tube (of tires)

Etymology

From 'tube' + '-less', indicating absence of an inner tube.
