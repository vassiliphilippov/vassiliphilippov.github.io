Citharichthys cornutus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪθəˈrɪkθɪs kɔːrˈnuːtəs / (AmE), / ˌsɪθəˈrɪkθɪs kɔːˈnjuːtəs / (BrE)
A species of small flatfish (whiff) found in Atlantic waters from the Bahamas and the northern Gulf of Mexico south to Brazil.
a species of flatfish from western Atlantic

Examples

Marine biologists recently discovered a population of Citharichthys cornutus near the coast of Brazil.
The distinct markings help distinguish Citharichthys cornutus from other small flatfish species.
Overfishing in the Gulf of Mexico has put Citharichthys cornutus at greater risk.

Etymology

From Greek *kithara* (lyre) and Greek *ichthys* (fish) for genus; Latin *cornutus* (horned) for species, referring to features of the fish.
