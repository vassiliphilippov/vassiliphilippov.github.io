plumbing

1. NOUN

/ ˈplʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
the work or trade of installing and repairing pipes and fixtures for water, gas, or sewage in buildings
the occupation of installing and repairing pipes

Examples

She decided to learn plumbing so she could fix things around the house.
The old building had outdated plumbing that needed to be replaced.
He started his own plumbing business after completing his apprenticeship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plumber - a person whose job is to install and repair pipes and fixtures

Etymology

From Middle English *plumminge*, related to Old French *plombie* (‘lead work’) and Latin *plumbum* (‘lead,’ as pipes were originally made of lead).

2. NOUN

/ ˈplʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
The system of pipes, fixtures, and other apparatus required for the distribution of water or gas and for the disposal of sewage in a building.
system of pipes and fixtures in a building

Examples

The old house had leaking plumbing that needed to be replaced.
A major storm exposed problems with the building’s plumbing system.
She called a professional to fix the plumbing in her kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipework - the system of pipes in a building
  • Antonym: electrical wiring - system of wires for electricity distribution in a building

Etymology

From the Latin 'plumbum' (lead), referring to the lead pipes once commonly used.
