ruined

1. ADJ

/ ˈruɪnd / (AmE), / ˈruːɪnd / (BrE)
Having been destroyed or severely damaged physically or morally; no longer in a usable or good condition.
destroyed or severely damaged

Examples

After the fire, the historic house was completely ruined and unsafe to enter.
The scandal left his reputation utterly ruined in the eyes of the public.
Years of neglect had left the once-beautiful gardens in a ruined state.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destroyed - damaged so badly that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired
  • Antonym: intact - remaining whole or untouched; not damaged

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'ruin', from Old French *ruine*, from Latin *ruina* (a collapse, downfall).

2. ADJ

/ ˈruːɪnd / (AmE), / ˈruːɪnd / (BrE)
(of a person, organization, or thing) having been severely damaged or destroyed so that restoration is impossible or very difficult.
severely damaged or destroyed

Examples

The ancient city lay ruined after the earthquake destroyed its walls and homes.
Her reputation was ruined by the scandal in the press.
Once a thriving business, the company was completely ruined by years of mismanagement.
