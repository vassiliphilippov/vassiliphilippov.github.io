silvern

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪlvərn / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvən / (BrE)
Having the appearance or sheen of silver; silvery.
having the sheen of silver

Examples

The moon cast a silvern light across the quiet lake.
Her hair gleamed with a silvern sheen in the morning sun.
They decorated the room with silvern ribbons and sparkling ornaments.

Synset Examples

a land of silver (or silvern) rivers where the salmon leap
repeated scrubbings have given the wood a silvery sheen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silvery - Having a shiny white or gray appearance like silver

Etymology

From Old English 'silfren', from 'seolfor' (silver) + '-en' (adjectival suffix).
