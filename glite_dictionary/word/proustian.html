Proustian

1. ADJ

/ ˈpruːstiən / (AmE), / ˈpruːstiən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Marcel Proust or his literary style, especially in evoking involuntary memories or exploring the complexity of memory and time.
relating to Marcel Proust or his style

Examples

The novel has a distinctly Proustian atmosphere, filled with intricate reflections on memory and time.
Her Proustian descriptions brought every detail of her childhood vividly to life.
In a Proustian moment, the taste of madeleines recalled his entire past.

Etymology

From the name of French novelist Marcel Proust (1871–1922) + -ian.
