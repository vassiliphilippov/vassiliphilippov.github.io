parthenogenetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːrθənoʊdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɑːθənəʊdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of reproduction) occurring without the fusion of male and female gametes; produced by parthenogenesis.
reproducing without fertilization

Examples

Some lizard species are parthenogenetic, meaning they can reproduce without males.
The parthenogenetic development of eggs is common in certain insects.
Scientists studied the parthenogenetic reproduction of stick insects in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asexual - reproduction that does not involve the fusion of gametes
  • Antonym: sexual - reproduction involving the fusion of male and female gametes

Etymology

From Greek 'parthenos' (virgin) + 'genesis' (origin, creation).
