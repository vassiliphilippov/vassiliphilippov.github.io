overexertion

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərɪɡˈzɝːʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪɡˈzɜːʃən / (BrE)
excessive physical or mental effort that results in discomfort or injury
excessive effort causing discomfort or injury

Examples

Athletes must be careful to avoid overexertion during intense training sessions.
The doctor warned that overexertion could worsen her heart condition.
Many workplace injuries are caused by overexertion and improper lifting techniques.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: exertion - physical or mental effort

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessive) + 'exertion' (from Latin exserere, to thrust out).
