Erigeron pulchellus

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈrɪdʒəˌrɒn pʌlˈkɛləs / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪdʒərɒn pʊlˈkɛləs / (BrE)
A common perennial plant of eastern North America, known for its usually violet-purple ray flowers.
species of perennial plant with violet-purple flowers

Examples

Gardeners often plant Erigeron pulchellus for its delicate violet blooms in spring and early summer.
The woods were dotted with clusters of Erigeron pulchellus along the hiking trail.
Researchers are studying Erigeron pulchellus to understand its role in supporting local pollinators.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Erigeron' (a genus name, from Greek eri- 'early' + geron 'old man'), 'pulchellus' meaning 'pretty, little' in Latin.
