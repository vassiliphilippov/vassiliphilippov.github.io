Joe Louis

1. PROPN

/ dʒoʊ ˈluːɪs / (AmE), / dʒəʊ ˈluːɪs / (BrE)
(1914–1981) An American professional boxer who held the world heavyweight championship for 12 years, widely regarded as one of the greatest boxers in history.
American heavyweight boxing champion (1914–1981)

Examples

During his long reign as champion, Joe Louis became an American icon.
My grandfather used to tell stories about watching Joe Louis fight on the radio.
Boxing historians often rank Joe Louis among the all-time greats.
