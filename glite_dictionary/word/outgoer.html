outgoer

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌɡoʊər / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌɡəʊə / (BrE)
a person who leaves one country to settle in another; an emigrant
person who emigrates to another country

Examples

The outgoer found new opportunities after settling in Canada.
Many outgoers faced challenges adapting to their adopted countries.
The policy aimed to track the number of outgoers departing annually.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emigrant - a person who leaves their own country to settle permanently in another
  • Antonym: immigrant - a person who comes to live permanently in a foreign country

Etymology

From out + goer, patterned after words like 'foregoer'; 'goer' as one who goes.
