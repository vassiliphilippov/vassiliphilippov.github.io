fibre-optic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfaɪbər ˈɑptɪk / (AmE), / ˌfaɪbə ˈɒptɪk / (BrE)
relating to or using thin strands of glass or plastic (optical fibers) through which light signals can be sent, especially for transmitting data or images
relating to fiber optics technology
british

Examples

The hospital upgraded its network to a fibre-optic system for faster data transmission.
The new broadband service relies on fibre-optic cables to deliver high-speed internet.
Engineers installed a fibre-optic link between the two buildings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiber-optic - relating to fiber optics (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'fibre', British spelling of 'fiber', plus 'optic' (relating to sight or light), referring to technology using optical fibers.
