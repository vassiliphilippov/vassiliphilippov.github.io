Petasites fragrans

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛtəˈsaɪtiːz ˈfreɪɡrənz / (AmE), / ˌpiːtəˈsaɪtiːz ˈfreɪɡrənz / (BrE)
A European perennial herb with broad leaves and vanilla-scented white to pink flowers, commonly known as winter heliotrope.
European herb with scented pink-white flowers

Examples

Petasites fragrans is often planted in gardens for its attractive winter flowers and pleasant scent.
Many people appreciate the sweet aroma that Petasites fragrans brings to early spring landscapes.
In some regions, Petasites fragrans can spread rapidly and become invasive.

Etymology

From Latin 'petasites' (a kind of plant) and Latin 'fragrans' (smelling sweet).
