hourly

1. ADV

/ ˈaʊrli / (AmE), / ˈaʊəli / (BrE)
(of a frequency) occurring every hour; by the hour.
every hour; by the hour (adverb)

Examples

The factory's production levels are checked hourly to ensure quality.
She monitored the patient's temperature hourly throughout the night.
New data is uploaded to the system hourly for real-time analysis.

Synset Examples

daily, hourly, I grew stronger

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: daily - every day; once a day (adverb)
  • Degree Higher: minutely - every minute; occurring every minute (adverb)

Etymology

From hour + -ly; -ly is an adverb/adjective-forming suffix in English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00360781-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˈaʊɚli / (AmE), / ˈaʊəli / (BrE)
happening every hour or paid/calculated at a rate per hour
occurring or paid every hour

Examples

The factory's hourly chime could be heard across the neighborhood.
She is paid on an hourly basis for her work as a tutor.
The hourly weather report forecasted rain throughout the afternoon.

Synset Examples

hourly chimes
hourly pay

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: daily - occurring every day or paid by the day
  • Degree Lower: weekly - occurring every week or paid by the week

Etymology

From 'hour' + '-ly', forming an adjective and adverb indicating frequency or rate by hour.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01975876-s
Synset POS: s
