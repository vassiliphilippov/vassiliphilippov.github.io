stranger

1. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈstreɪndʒə / (BrE)
a person whom one does not know or who is not familiar in a particular place or environment
a person who is not known or familiar

Examples

A stranger wandered into the village, catching everyone's attention.
Children are taught not to talk to strangers for safety reasons.
He felt like a stranger at the party, knowing no one in the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreigner - a person from another country
  • Antonym: acquaintance - a person one knows slightly but who is not a close friend
  • Antonym: native - a person born in a specified place or associated with a place by birth

Etymology

From Old French 'estrangier', from Latin 'extraneus' (foreign, external).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈstreɪndʒə / (BrE)
a person whom one does not know or is not familiar with
person unknown to someone

Examples

He was wary of talking to a stranger on the dark street.
Children are often taught not to accept gifts from a stranger.
She felt like a stranger in her new school.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acquaintance - a person one knows slightly, but who is not a close friend
  • Synonym: outsider - a person who does not belong to a particular group

Etymology

From Old French estrangeor, from Latin extraneus (foreign, external).
