octette

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˈtɛt / (AmE), / ɒkˈtɛt / (BrE)
a musical composition written for eight performers or instruments.
musical work for eight performers

Examples

The composer premiered his new octette at the summer music festival.
Mendelssohn's string octette is considered a masterpiece of the chamber music repertoire.
She played the flute part in an octette last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: octet - a group of eight musicians or singers, or a composition for eight performers

Etymology

From Italian 'ottetto', from Latin 'octo' (eight).

2. NOUN

/ ɑkˈtɛt / (AmE), / ɒkˈtɛt / (BrE)
a group of eight performers or singers who perform together, especially as a musical ensemble
a group of eight performers or singers

Examples

The string octette received a standing ovation after their performance.
She joined an octette specializing in Renaissance vocal music.
An octette can produce a rich, layered sound that smaller ensembles cannot achieve.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quartet - a group of four musicians or singers
  • Degree Lower: quintet - a group of five musicians or singers
  • Degree Lower: sextet - a group of six musicians or singers
  • Degree Higher: nonet - a group of nine musicians or singers

Etymology

From Italian 'ottetto', from Latin 'octo' (eight), influenced by 'quartet' and 'sextet'.
