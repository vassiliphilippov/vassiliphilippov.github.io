Subularia aquatica

1. NOUN

/ suːˌbjuːˈlɛəriə əˈkwætɪkə / (AmE), / suːˌbjuːˈlɛːrɪə əˈkwatɪkə / (BrE)
A small aquatic plant with tufted, awl-shaped leaves arranged in a basal rosette and tiny white flowers, found in circumboreal regions.
small aquatic plant species

Examples

Subularia aquatica thrives in shallow ponds and lakes across northern Europe and North America.
Botanists often study Subularia aquatica to understand aquatic plant adaptations.
The tiny white flowers of Subularia aquatica are easy to overlook in the wild.

Etymology

From Latin: 'subularia' ('awl-shaped') and 'aquatica' ('of water').
