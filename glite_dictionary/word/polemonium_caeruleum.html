Polemonium caeruleum

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌlɛˈmoʊniəm siˌruːˈliəm / (AmE), / pəʊˌlɛˈməʊniəm siˌruːˈliəm / (BrE)
A perennial European plant with pinnate leaves and bright blue or white flowers, also known as Jacob's ladder.
European perennial plant (Jacob's ladder)

Examples

The garden was filled with blooming Polemonium caeruleum during spring.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Polemonium caeruleum.
She planted Polemonium caeruleum beside the roses for a burst of blue color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jacob's ladder - Common English name for Polemonium caeruleum

Etymology

From Latin; 'Polemonium' from Greek *polemonion*, plant name; 'caeruleum' meaning 'blue'.
