widget

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
a small device or control that is useful for a particular task, often used when the precise name is unknown or unimportant
small useful device or control

Examples

He used a special widget to hold the shelves in place while drilling.
This new software comes with a handy widget for organizing your calendar.
I always seem to lose that little widget that keeps my desk tidy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gadget - a small mechanical or electronic device with a practical use
  • Synonym: contraption - a machine or device that looks strange or unnecessarily complicated

Etymology

Probably an alteration of "gadget," of uncertain origin, dating from early 20th century US slang.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
an object whose name is unknown or forgotten, or a generic name for any unspecified item, especially in examples or hypothetical contexts
an unspecified or hypothetical object

Examples

Can you hand me that widget on the counter next to the toolbox?
The company makes all kinds of widgets for use in electronics assembly.
When discussing economics, textbooks often use a hypothetical widget to illustrate principles.

Synset Examples

she eased the ball-shaped doodad back into its socket
there may be some great new gizmo around the corner that you will want to use

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gadget - a small mechanical or electronic device, especially one whose name is unknown or forgotten
  • Synonym: gizmo - a gadget, especially one whose name the speaker does not know or cannot recall
  • Synonym: thingamajig - an object or device whose name one does not know or has forgotten

Etymology

Originated in early 20th-century American English as a fanciful coinage, possibly modeled on 'gadget'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
