Saint Olav

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈoʊˌlɑv / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈəʊˌlɑːv / (BrE)
King of Norway (995–1030) who became the country's patron saint after his death.
King and patron saint of Norway

Examples

Many pilgrims travel to the Nidaros Cathedral to honor Saint Olav's memory.
Legends about Saint Olav are an important part of Norwegian folklore.
The feast day of Saint Olav is celebrated annually in Norway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Olaf - Alternative spelling for Saint Olav, king and patron saint of Norway

Etymology

From Old Norse Óláfr, the given name of the king, with 'Saint' denoting his canonization.
