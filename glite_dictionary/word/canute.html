Canute

1. PROPN

/ kəˈnut / (AmE), / kəˈnjuːt / (BrE)
(c. 994–1035) King of Denmark, Norway, and England; after defeating Edmund II, he became king of all England.
King of Denmark, Norway, and England (994–1035)

Examples

Canute is often remembered for his efforts to quell the waves as a demonstration of the limits of kingship.
The reign of Canute marked a period of stability and prosperity in England.
Many English towns have legends associated with King Canute and his royal decrees.

Etymology

From Old Norse Knútr, meaning 'knot', Latinized as Canutus.
