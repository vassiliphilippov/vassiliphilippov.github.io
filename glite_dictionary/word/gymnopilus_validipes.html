Gymnopilus validipes

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˈnɑpɪləs ˌvælɪˈdaɪpiːz / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈnɒpɪləs ˌvalɪˈdiːpiːz / (BrE)
A species of poisonous mushroom characterized by a dry cap and a cortina that does not leave much of a ring on its robust stalk.
a species of poisonous mushroom

Examples

The toxic mushroom Gymnopilus validipes should never be consumed due to its poisonous nature.
Mycology students examined a specimen of Gymnopilus validipes found in the forest.
The distinctive shape of Gymnopilus validipes makes it recognizable among other woodland fungi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gymnopilus - a genus of gilled mushrooms

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) and 'pilos' (cap), with species name from Latin 'validipes' (strong-footed).
