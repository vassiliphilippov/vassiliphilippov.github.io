calendula

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛndʒələ / (AmE), / kəˈlɛndjʊlə / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Calendula, widely cultivated for its yellow or orange flowers and often used for medicinal or culinary purposes
a yellow or orange-flowered plant, Calendula genus

Examples

She sprinkled dried calendula petals over the salad for color and flavor.
The herbalist recommended a calendula ointment for soothing skin irritations.
In her garden, bright orange calendula blooms thrived all summer.

Etymology

From Latin 'calendula', meaning 'little calendar' or 'little clock', probably referring to its long flowering period.
