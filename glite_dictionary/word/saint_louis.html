Saint Louis

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈluːɪs / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈluːɪs / (BrE)
A major city in eastern Missouri, located on the Mississippi River; known for its historical significance as a gateway to the western United States and as a major port.
major city in eastern Missouri, USA

Examples

Many tourists visit Saint Louis to see the Gateway Arch and explore its rich history.
The Mississippi River flows through Saint Louis, making it an important transportation hub.
During the 19th century, Saint Louis served as a starting point for pioneers heading west.

Etymology

From French 'Saint Louis', named after King Louis IX of France.

2. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈluːɪs / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈluːi / (BrE)
(1214–1270) King of France, canonized as a saint, known for leading two Crusades and regarded as an ideal medieval monarch.
French king and saint (1214–1270)

Examples

Many cathedrals across the world are named after Saint Louis.
As a boy, he admired the wisdom and justice of Saint Louis.
The legacy of Saint Louis continues to influence French culture and history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Louis IX - King of France known as Saint Louis

Etymology

From French 'Saint Louis', referring to Louis IX of France, canonized as a saint.
