deaconess

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːkənɪs / (AmE), / ˈdiːkənɪs / (BrE)
A woman who is appointed or ordained as a deacon, especially in certain Christian churches.
female deacon (in church)

Examples

The church appointed a new deaconess to help lead the community outreach programs.
As a deaconess, she organized charity events and supported the congregation.
Many denominations now ordain women as deaconesses as part of their commitment to equal ministry roles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deacon - a member of the clerical order just below that of priest in Christian churches

Etymology

From Late Latin diaconissa, from Greek diakonissa, feminine of diakonos (servant, deacon).
