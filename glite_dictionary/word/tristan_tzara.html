Tristan Tzara

1. PROPN

/ ˈtrɪstɑn tsɑːˈrɑ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪstan ˈtsɑːrə / (BrE)
(1896–1963) A French poet and essayist, born in Romania, who was a cofounder of the Dada art movement.
Romanian-French poet, Dada cofounder

Examples

The works of Tristan Tzara are studied in modernist literature courses worldwide.
Tristan Tzara played a pivotal role in establishing the principles of Dadaism.
Museums often feature exhibitions dedicated to Tristan Tzara and his fellow avant-garde artists.
