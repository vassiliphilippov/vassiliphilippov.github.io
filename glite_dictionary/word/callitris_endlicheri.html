Callitris endlicheri

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈtraɪs ɛnˈdlɪkəri / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈtraɪs ɛnˈdlɪkəri / (BrE)
An Australian tree with small, flattened scale-like leaves and numerous dark brown seeds, valued for its timber and resin.
Australian coniferous tree species

Examples

The wood from Callitris endlicheri is prized for its durability and pleasant scent.
Resin harvested from Callitris endlicheri has been used by Indigenous Australians for traditional purposes.
Callitris endlicheri often grows in rocky, inland areas of southeastern Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Callitris - Genus of evergreen coniferous trees native to Australia and New Caledonia

Etymology

Genus name from Greek kallos (beauty); species epithet honors botanist Stephan Endlicher.
