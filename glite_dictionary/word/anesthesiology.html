anesthesiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəsˌθiʒiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌænəsˌθiːziˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of medicine concerned with the study and application of anesthesia and anesthetics, especially during surgical procedures.
branch of medicine dealing with anesthesia
medical

Examples

He decided to specialize in anesthesiology after medical school.
Advancements in anesthesiology have made complex surgeries much safer.
She is chair of the anesthesiology department at the hospital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anesthesia - loss of sensation induced for medical purposes

Etymology

From Greek 'anaisthēsia' (insensibility) + '-logia' (study of).
