Campanula rapunculus

1. NOUN

/ kæmˈpæn.jʊ.lə rəˈpʌŋ.kjʊ.ləs / (AmE), / kæmˈpan.jʊ.lə raˈpʌŋ.kjʊ.ləs / (BrE)
A bellflower native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, known for its bluish flowers and edible tuberous root used in salads.
a bellflower with edible root

Examples

In the old country, people used to collect Campanula rapunculus for its tasty roots every spring.
The salad featured leaves and thin slices of Campanula rapunculus tuber, giving it a unique flavor.
Botanists admire the delicate blue flowers of Campanula rapunculus found across European meadows.

Etymology

From Latin: 'campanula' meaning 'little bell', and 'rapunculus', a diminutive of 'rapa' meaning 'turnip', referring to its root.
