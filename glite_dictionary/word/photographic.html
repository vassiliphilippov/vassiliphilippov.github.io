photographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfoʊtəˈgræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəˈgræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to or obtained by using photography; concerning the art or practice of taking and processing photographs.
relating to photography

Examples

She has a keen interest in photographic composition and lighting.
The museum featured an exhibition of early photographic equipment.
His memory is so strong it's almost photographic.

Synset Examples

photographic equipment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photographic - obtained or produced by photography
  • Antonym: nonphotographic - not relating to or obtained by photography

Etymology

From photograph + -ic, formed after 'photography'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌfoʊtəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
(of a representation) depicting people or things with the accuracy and detail of a photograph.
exact and detailed like a photograph

Examples

The artist's photographic memory allowed her to recall every detail of the scene.
His painting was so photographic that people mistook it for a real photograph from a distance.
She admired the photographic quality of the landscape in the film.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realistic - closely resembling real life
  • Synonym: lifelike - very similar to the person or thing represented
  • Antonym: stylized - represented in a non-realistic, conventional way

Etymology

From photograph + -ic, based on Greek 'phōto-' (light) and 'graph-' (write, draw).
