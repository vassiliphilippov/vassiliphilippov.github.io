airdrop

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈeəˌdrɒp / (BrE)
the delivery of supplies, equipment, or personnel by dropping them by parachute from an aircraft
delivery by parachute from aircraft

Examples

The villagers awaited an airdrop of food and medical supplies after the storm cut off all roads.
During the military operation, an airdrop brought much-needed ammunition to the front lines.
The success of the rescue mission depended on the accuracy of the airdrop in the mountainous region.

Synset Examples

until then, front line troops will be supplied by airdrop

Etymology

Formed from 'air' and 'drop', referencing dropping objects from the air.
