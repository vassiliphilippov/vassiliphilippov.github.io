ariose

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛər.i.oʊs / (AmE), / ˈɑː.ri.əʊs / (BrE)
characterized by a tuneful, melodic style, as opposed to a speech-like or recitative manner, especially in music.
having a tuneful, melodic style (music)

Examples

The composer preferred an ariose approach, allowing the soprano's voice to soar with a beautiful melody.
Unlike the more speech-like passages, the next section of the opera is distinctly ariose.
His ariose treatment of the theme made the song memorable and engaging.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recitative - musical style that imitates speech rather than melody
  • Synonym: melodic - having or producing melody; tuneful

Etymology

From Italian 'arioso' (airy, melodious), from 'aria' (air, melody).
