democracy

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmɑːkrəsi / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒkrəsi / (BrE)
A system of government in which the people have the power to elect representatives who make decisions on their behalf.
system of government by the people

Examples

Many people believe that a strong democracy is essential for protecting human rights.
After decades of military rule, the country transitioned to a democracy with free elections.
Public participation is a key element in any functioning democracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: republic - a government in which the people elect representatives, with an elected or appointed head of state
  • Antonym: autocracy - a system of government by one person with absolute power

Etymology

From Greek dēmokratia, from dēmos (people) + kratos (power, rule).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɑkrəsi / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒkrəsi / (BrE)
the belief in or support for government by the people, either directly or through elected representatives
support for government by the people

Examples

The movement was born out of a democracy that valued freedom and open debate.
Her strong belief in democracy led her to support electoral reforms.
Many consider the right to vote a fundamental aspect of democracy in modern societies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: autocracy - a system of government by one person with absolute power
  • Synonym: democratic - relating to or supporting democracy or its principles

Etymology

From Greek dēmokratiā (rule by the people), from dēmos (people) + -kratia (rule).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɑːkrəsi / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒkrəsi / (BrE)
The principle that the numerical majority of an organized group has the power to make decisions binding on the whole group.
rule by majority within a group

Examples

In a democracy, policies are often decided by what most people in the group want.
The club adopted more democracy in choosing its new leaders, allowing all members to vote.
Critics argue that pure democracy can overlook the rights of minorities within the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: majority rule - a system where decisions are made by a majority vote
  • Antonym: autocracy - a system ruled by a single person with absolute power

Etymology

From Greek dēmokratia, from dēmos (people) + kratos (power, rule).
