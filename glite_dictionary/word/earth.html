Earth

1. NOUN

/ ɝθ / (AmE), / ɜːθ / (BrE)
the planet on which we live, the third in order from the sun and the only known planet to support life
our planet; third from the sun

Examples

Astronauts took breathtaking photos of Earth from the moon.
Climate change affects all life on Earth.
After months in space, she was eager to return to Earth.

Synset Examples

the Earth moves around the sun
he sailed around the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: world - the planet Earth, especially as inhabited by humans
  • Antonym: Mars - the fourth planet from the sun in our solar system

Etymology

From Old English 'eorþe' (ground, soil, dry land); related to German 'Erde.'

2. NOUN

/ ɜrθ / (AmE), / ɜːθ / (BrE)
The world as the dwelling place of humans, especially in contrast to Heaven or Hell.
the world as the abode of people

Examples

It was a paradise on Earth before the war began.
People often speak of Earth and Heaven as two very different places.
They believed that justice would be served either on Earth or in the afterlife.

Synset Examples

it was hell on earth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Heaven - a place regarded as the abode of God, angels, and the blessed after death
  • Antonym: Hell - a place regarded as the abode of the wicked after death

Etymology

From Old English 'eorþe' (ground, soil, dry land), related to German 'Erde'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
