gravelly

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡrævəli / (AmE), / ˈɡrævəli / (BrE)
(of a surface or area) covered with or containing a lot of gravel or small stones.
covered with or containing gravel

Examples

The path was gravelly and rough under our feet.
We stopped by a gravelly riverbank to have lunch.
The new driveway felt uncomfortable to walk on because of its gravelly surface.

Synset Examples

landed at a shingly little beach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stony - covered with or full of stones
  • Synonym: rocky - full of rocks

Etymology

From 'gravel' (Middle English, from Old French 'gravele'), with suffix '-ly' to form an adjective.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡrævəli / (AmE), / ˈɡravəli / (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) unpleasantly harsh or grating.
harsh or grating (voice or sound)

Examples

The old man's gravelly voice made him sound intimidating to the children.
She spoke with a gravelly tone after years of smoking.
His gravelly laugh echoed through the quiet room, making everyone turn their heads.

Synset Examples

a gravelly voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoarse - (of a voice) sounding rough and harsh
  • Antonym: smooth - (of a voice or sound) free from harshness

Etymology

From 'gravel' + '-ly', describing the rough quality of gravel applied metaphorically to sound.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00300342-s
Synset POS: s
