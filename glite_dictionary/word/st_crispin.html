St. Crispin

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈkrɪspɪn / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈkrɪspɪn / (BrE)
A Christian saint, regarded as the patron saint of shoemakers, who, with his brother, was martyred in the 3rd century for spreading Christianity.
patron saint of shoemakers, 3rd century

Examples

Many shoemakers celebrate the feast of St. Crispin as a tradition in their craft.
Shakespeare referenced St. Crispin in his famous "St. Crispin's Day" speech in Henry V.
The church was dedicated to St. Crispin and his brother, who were both martyred.

Etymology

From Latin Crispinus, a personal name; 'Saint' from Latin sanctus.
