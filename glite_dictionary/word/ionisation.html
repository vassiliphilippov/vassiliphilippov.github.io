ionisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.ə.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.ə.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process or condition in which atoms or molecules are converted into ions, typically as a result of heat, radiation, or chemical reaction
process of forming ions

Examples

The ionisation of water in the atmosphere affects electric conductivity.
Radiation can cause the ionisation of molecules in living tissue.
The scientist measured the ionisation rate of the gas under different conditions.

Synset Examples

the ionization of a gas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ionization - alternate spelling (American English) of ionisation

Etymology

From ion + -isation, patterned after French 'ionisation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌaɪə.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.ə.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The process of forming ions by adding or removing electrons from atoms or molecules, typically through energy input such as strong electric fields or radiation.
process of forming ions
british

Examples

The ionisation of gases is crucial in fluorescent lighting.
Exposure to high energy radiation can result in the ionisation of atoms in the atmosphere.
In mass spectrometry, ionisation is the first step in analyzing chemical samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ionization - the process of forming ions; American spelling

Etymology

From ion + -isation, patterned after 'ionize' (from Greek 'ion', meaning 'going'). Variant of American 'ionization'.
