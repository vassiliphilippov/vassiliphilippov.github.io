untested

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtɛstɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɛstɪd / (BrE)
not yet tried or proved by testing or experience.
not yet tested or tried

Examples

The new medication remains untested in children under twelve years old.
Because the technology is untested, investors are hesitant to commit large sums of money.
They chose an untested method for solving the problem, hoping it would succeed.

Synset Examples

an untested drug
untested theory
an untried procedure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: untried - not yet tried, tested, or experienced
  • Antonym: tested - tried or examined by a test

Etymology

From un- (not) + tested (past participle of test).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtɛstɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɛstɪd / (BrE)
not tried, examined, or proven by experience or trial
not tried or proven

Examples

The new drug remains untested in children.
As a rookie, the pitcher was still untested at the major league level.
She was reluctant to use an untested method for such an important task.

Synset Examples

unseasoned artillery volunteers
still untested in battle
an illustrator untried in mural painting
a young hand at plowing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tested - examined or tried by experience or trial
  • Synonym: untried - not yet tried or tested

Etymology

From un- (not) + tested (past participle of 'test').
