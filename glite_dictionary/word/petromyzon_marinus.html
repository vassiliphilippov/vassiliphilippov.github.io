Petromyzon marinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛtroʊˈmaɪzɒn məˈriːnəs / (AmE), / ˌpɛtrəˈmaɪzən məˈriːnəs / (BrE)
a large anadromous lamprey native to the North Atlantic, sometimes used as food and known for its destructive impact on native fish in the Great Lakes
species of large sea lamprey

Examples

The invasion of Petromyzon marinus drastically altered the fish populations in the Great Lakes.
Scientists have studied the life cycle of Petromyzon marinus to better control its numbers.
In some European countries, Petromyzon marinus is considered a delicacy when cooked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea lamprey - common name for Petromyzon marinus, an eel-like parasitic fish

Etymology

From Latin: 'petra' (rock) + 'myzon' (to suck) and 'marinus' (of the sea).
