sycamore

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪk.əˌmɔr / (AmE), / ˈsɪk.əmɔː / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree with lobed leaves, pale peeling bark, and spherical clusters of seed balls, especially of the genus Platanus
large tree with pale bark and ball-shaped fruits

Examples

A huge sycamore stood in the center of the park, providing shade for visitors.
The pale, patchy bark of the sycamore is easy to recognize even from a distance.
In autumn, the leaves of the sycamore turn a golden yellow before falling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plane tree - another name for Platanus species, especially in Europe

Etymology

From Latin *sycomorus*, from Greek *sykomoros* (fig-mulberry tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪkəmɔːr / (AmE), / ˈsɪkəmɔː / (BrE)
A large Eurasian maple tree (Acer pseudoplatanus) with pale grey, peeling bark and five-lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn.
Eurasian maple tree with peeling bark

Examples

The sycamore in the park turns a brilliant yellow each autumn.
Many streets in Europe are lined with tall sycamore trees.
He admired the peeling bark of the old sycamore growing beside the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycamore maple - Another name for the same Eurasian maple tree (Acer pseudoplatanus)
  • Synonym: plane tree - Large deciduous tree, often confused with sycamore in Europe

Etymology

From Latin 'sycomorus', from Greek 'sykomoros' meaning fig-mulberry (originally referring to the Egyptian sycamore).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪk.əˌmɔr / (AmE), / ˈsɪk.əmɔː / (BrE)
A large, wide-spreading tree (Ficus sycomorus) of Africa and southwestern Asia, often with branches rising from near the ground, known for its clusters of edible but inferior figs; the tree mentioned as the sycamore in the Bible.
African/Asian fig tree of the Bible

Examples

The ancient Egyptians cultivated the sycamore for its fruit and shade.
In the biblical story, Zacchaeus climbed a sycamore tree to see Jesus passing by.
The sycamore generally bears clusters of small figs directly on its leafless twigs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fig tree - Tree bearing edible figs (Ficus carica or Ficus sycomorus)

Etymology

From Latin sycomorus, from Greek sykomoros, traditionally referring to this fig tree in the eastern Mediterranean and Bible.

4. NOUN

/ ˈsɪk.əˌmɔr / (AmE), / ˈsɪk.ə.mɔː / (BrE)
the hard, tough, elastic wood of a sycamore tree, often variably colored or variegated, used in carpentry and fine woodworking
wood from a sycamore tree

Examples

The furniture maker selected a plank of sycamore for its beautiful grain and natural strength.
This violin's back is crafted from seasoned sycamore to enhance its rich tone.
The warm hues of sycamore give the tabletop a unique, elegant appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maple - wood from the maple tree

Etymology

From Old French 'sicamor', from Latin 'sycomorus', from Greek 'sykomoros', meaning fig-mulberry tree.
