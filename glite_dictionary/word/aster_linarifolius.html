Aster linarifolius

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstər lɪˌnɛrɪˈfoʊliəs / (AmE), / ˈæstə lɪˌnærɪˈfəʊlɪəs / (BrE)
A wiry, tufted perennial plant native to the eastern United States, with stiff erect rough stems, narrow linear leaves, and large violet flowers.
tufted perennial plant with violet flowers

Examples

The Aster linarifolius blooms in late summer, adding a splash of violet to the garden.
Several pollinators were drawn to the bright flowers of Aster linarifolius along the woodland edge.
She planted Aster linarifolius in her native wildflower bed to attract butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - herbaceous plant with star-shaped flower heads

Etymology

From genus name Aster (Greek: star) plus species epithet linarifolius (Latin: having leaves like flax).
