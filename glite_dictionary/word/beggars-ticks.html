beggar's-ticks

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛɡərz tɪks / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡəz tɪks / (BrE)
any of several plants of the genus Bidens, having yellow flowers and prickly fruits that adhere to fur and clothing
Bidens plant with prickly clinging seeds

Examples

Be careful when you walk through the meadow, as you might pick up beggar's-ticks on your clothes.
The prickly seeds of beggar's-ticks are notorious for sticking to animal fur.
Several species of beggar's-ticks grow along the riverbank, easily recognized by their yellow flowers.

Etymology

So named from the prickly fruits, which cling like 'ticks' to passersby, and 'beggar', suggesting undesired attachment.
