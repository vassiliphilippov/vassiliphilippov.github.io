Sipuncula

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈpʌŋkjələ / (AmE), / sɪˈpʌŋkjʊlə / (BrE)
a class of unsegmented, burrowing marine worms commonly known as peanut worms, found in shallow waters worldwide
a class of marine worms (peanut worms)

Examples

Researchers collected several Sipuncula specimens from the tidal flats for further analysis.
The Sipuncula play an important role in marine sediment ecosystems as burrowers.
Some aquarists have observed Sipuncula in their live rock aquariums, describing their peanut-like appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peanut worm - a common name for worms of the class Sipuncula

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on Greek 'siphos' (tube) + 'onkos' (mass, lump).
