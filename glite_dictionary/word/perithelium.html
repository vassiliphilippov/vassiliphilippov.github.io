perithelium

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛr.ɪˈθiː.li.əm / (AmE), / ˌpɛr.ɪˈθiː.li.əm / (BrE)
A layer of tissue surrounding small blood vessels.
tissue layer around small blood vessels
medical

Examples

The perithelium provides support and protection for the capillaries in connective tissue.
Damage to the perithelium can disrupt normal blood vessel function.
Researchers observed thickening of the perithelium in certain vascular diseases.

Etymology

From Greek 'peri-' (around) and 'thēlē' (nipple), referring to a surrounding sheath.
