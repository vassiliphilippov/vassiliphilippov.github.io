evangelicalism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌvæn.dʒəˈlɪ.kə.lɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˌvan.dʒɪˈlɪk.ə.lɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A branch of Protestant Christianity emphasizing the importance of personal conversion, faith in Jesus Christ for salvation, and the authority of the Bible.
Protestant Christian movement stressing personal faith

Examples

The rapid spread of evangelicalism has shaped religious communities across the globe.
She grew up in a family deeply committed to evangelicalism and its teachings.
Modern evangelicalism often emphasizes outreach and missionary work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evangelical - a member of the evangelical movement
  • Degree Higher: fundamentalism - strict religious belief based on a literal interpretation
  • Degree Lower: liberal Protestantism - a more liberal approach within Protestant Christianity

Etymology

From 'evangelical' + '-ism', ultimately from Greek 'euangelion' (good news, gospel).
