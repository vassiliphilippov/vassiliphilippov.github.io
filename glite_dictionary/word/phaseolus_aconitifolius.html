Phaseolus aconitifolius

1. NOUN

/ fəˈsiː.oʊ.ləs əˌkɒnɪˈtɪfəliəs / (AmE), / fəˈsiː.li.əs əˌkɒn.ɪ.tɪˈfəʊ.li.əs / (BrE)
an East Indian legume plant with hairy leaves and small yellow flowers, cultivated for food, forage, and as a soil conditioner, especially in India; also known as moth bean
East Indian legume plant (moth bean)

Examples

Farmers in arid regions often cultivate Phaseolus aconitifolius because of its drought resistance.
The pods of Phaseolus aconitifolius are commonly harvested for both human consumption and animal feed.
Research shows that Phaseolus aconitifolius can improve soil fertility when used as a cover crop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moth bean - common name for Phaseolus aconitifolius, an edible legume plant

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Phaseolus and species epithet aconitifolius (having leaves like those of Aconitum).
