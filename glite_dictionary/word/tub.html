tub

1. NOUN

/ tʌb / (AmE), / tʌb / (BrE)
an amount that fills a tub, used as a measure for food, liquids, etc.
the amount that fills a tub

Examples

She bought a tub of vanilla ice cream for the party.
He carried a heavy tub of paint up the stairs.
There was a tub of butter sitting on the kitchen counter.

Synset Examples

a tub of water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: container - an object that can hold or transport something

2. NOUN

/ tʌb / (AmE), / tʌb / (BrE)
a large, open, usually cylindrical container used for holding or storing liquids or other substances
large open container for liquids

Examples

He filled the tub with water to soak the towels.
They stored the syrup in a large wooden tub in the cellar.
A metal tub was placed under the leak to catch the drips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrel - a large round container, usually bulging out in the middle, traditionally made of wood
  • Degree Lower: bucket - an open container with a handle, used for carrying liquids

Etymology

From Middle English tubbe, likely from Middle Dutch tubbe (tub, barrel).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ tʌb / (AmE), / tʌb / (BrE)
a large, usually rounded container, especially one filled with water, used for washing the body
large container for bathing

Examples

She filled the tub with hot water and added some bubble bath.
After a long day, he likes to soak in the tub for an hour.
The old house still had a claw-foot tub in the bathroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bathtub - a long, usually rectangular container used for bathing

Etymology

From Middle English tubbe, probably from Middle Dutch tubbe or Old French tub.
