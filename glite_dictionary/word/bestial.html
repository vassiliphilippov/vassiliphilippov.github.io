bestial

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛstiəl, ˈbiːstiəl / (AmE), / ˈbɛstɪəl, ˈbiːstɪəl / (BrE)
Of or like a beast; lacking reason, compassion, or other human qualities; brutal or savage.
like a beast; brutal or savage

Examples

The prisoners were subjected to bestial treatment by their captors.
His bestial rage shocked everyone who knew him as a gentle man.
The novel describes the bestial behavior that emerged during times of war.

Synset Examples

beastly desires
a bestial nature
brute force
a dull and brutish man
bestial treatment of prisoners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brutal - savagely violent; cruel
  • Synonym: inhuman - lacking human qualities of compassion and mercy
  • Antonym: humane - having or showing compassion or benevolence

Etymology

From Latin bestialis, from bestia (beast).
