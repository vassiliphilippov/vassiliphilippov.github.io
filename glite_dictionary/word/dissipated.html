dissipated

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪsɪˌpeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪpeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or way of life) overindulging in sensual pleasures; lacking restraint or moral discipline.
indulging in sensual or immoral pleasures

Examples

She led a dissipated life, spending her nights partying and gambling.
The novel portrays a dissipated aristocrat who cares little for reputation or duty.
Years of dissipated living had taken a visible toll on his health and appearance.

Synset Examples

Congreve draws a debauched aristocratic society
deplorably dissipated and degraded
riotous living
fast women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debauched - indulging in immoral or excessive pleasures
  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards

Etymology

Past participle of 'dissipate', from Latin dissipatus, meaning 'scattered'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪsɪˌpeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪpeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or lifestyle) indulging excessively in sensual pleasures, especially those associated with gambling, drinking, or other immoral behaviors.
indulging in excessive pleasures

Examples

After years of dissipated living, he found himself both broke and alone.
The novel portrays a society whose leaders lead dissipated and reckless lives.
Her dissipated brother squandered his inheritance on gambling and parties.

Synset Examples

led a dissipated life
a betting man
a card-playing son of a bitch
a gambling fool
sporting gents and their ladies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debauched - indulging in or characterized by sensual pleasures to excess
  • Synonym: profligate - recklessly extravagant or wasteful in the use of resources; licentious
  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'dissipate,' from Latin dissipatus (scattered, squandered).
