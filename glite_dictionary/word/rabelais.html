Rabelais

1. PROPN

/ ræbəˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˈræbəleɪ / (BrE)
(1494–1553) French Renaissance writer and satirist best known for his humorous and satirical works attacking medieval scholasticism.
French satirical writer (1494–1553)

Examples

Rabelais is celebrated for his sharp wit and bold satires of medieval learning.
Many critics compare modern satire to the works of Rabelais.
During his studies, he became fascinated by the life and writings of Rabelais.

Etymology

From the French surname Rabelais.
