counterpoise

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.tɚˌpɔɪz / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.təˌpɔɪz / (BrE)
a weight or force that balances or offsets another weight or force
a balancing weight or force

Examples

The heavy stone acted as a counterpoise to the weight on the other side of the scale.
In the old clock, a small counterpoise keeps the pendulum swinging steadily.
Her calm manner was a counterpoise to his nervous energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterbalance - a weight that provides balance against another

Etymology

From French 'contrepoids', from Latin 'contra-' (against) + 'pondus' (weight).

2. VERB

/ ˈkaʊntərˌpɔɪz / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌpɔɪz / (BrE)
to act as a counterweight or counterbalance to something; to offset or balance by opposing force or influence
to act as a counterbalance

Examples

The smaller tower was designed to counterpoise the visual weight of the main structure.
Her calm reasoning served to counterpoise his passionate arguments during the debate.
Checks and balances are intended to counterpoise the powers of government branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterbalance - to offset or balance the effect of something

Etymology

From Middle French 'contrepoiser', from 'contre-' (against) + 'poiser' (to weigh), ultimately from Latin 'pondus' (weight).
