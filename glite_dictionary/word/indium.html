indium

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiəm / (AmE), / ˈɪndiəm / (BrE)
a rare, soft, silvery-white metallic element found in small quantities in zinc ores, especially sphalerite, and used in alloys and in the electronics industry.
a rare metallic chemical element

Examples

Scientists discovered traces of indium in the mineral sample extracted from the mine.
The electronics industry uses indium to manufacture touchscreens and semiconductors.
Due to its rarity, indium is considered one of the more valuable minor metals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallium - a soft silvery metallic element similar to indium
  • Synonym: tin - a silvery-white metal with properties related to indium

Etymology

From Latin 'indicum' (indigo), because of the indigo-blue line in its spectrum.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiəm / (AmE), / ˈɪndiəm / (BrE)
a rare, soft, silvery-white metallic element (symbol In) used mainly in alloys and electronic devices
a metallic chemical element (In)

Examples

The scientist extracted indium from sphalerite ores for use in electronics.
Because of its rarity, indium is often recycled from used electronic devices.
Indium is valued for its ability to bond glass to metal in LCD panels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: element - a substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances; a chemical element

Etymology

From New Latin *indium*, named for the indigo-blue line in its spectrum, from Latin *indicum* (indigo).
