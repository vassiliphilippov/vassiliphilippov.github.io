Nerodia sipedon

1. NOUN

/ nəˈroʊdiə saɪˈpiːdɒn / (AmE), / nɪˈrəʊdiə sɪˈpiːdən / (BrE)
A species of non-venomous water snake commonly found near freshwater bodies in North America.
species of North American water snake

Examples

The Nerodia sipedon is often seen basking on logs near rivers and ponds.
Researchers studied the diet of Nerodia sipedon in various wetland habitats.
Unlike venomous snakes, Nerodia sipedon poses no threat to humans.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Nerodia + specific epithet sipedon, from Greek roots.
