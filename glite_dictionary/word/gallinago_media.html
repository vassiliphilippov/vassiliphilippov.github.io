Gallinago media

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡælɪˈneɪɡoʊ ˈmiːdiə / (AmE), / ˌɡalɪˈneɪɡəʊ ˈmiːdɪə / (BrE)
a species of Old World snipe, larger and darker than most other snipes, known commonly as the great snipe
species of snipe bird (great snipe)

Examples

The Gallinago media is noted for its distinctive courtship display in wet meadows.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of Gallinago media in Eastern Europe.
Unlike the common snipe, the Gallinago media is heavier and has darker plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snipe - a wading bird with a long straight bill, related to Gallinago media

Etymology

From Latin: 'gallina' (hen) and possibly related to 'ago' (to drive), with 'media' meaning 'middle' in Latin; scientific species naming convention.
