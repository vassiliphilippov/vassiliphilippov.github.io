triphosphopyridine

1. NOUN

/ trɪˌfɒsfəˌpaɪˈrɪdin / (AmE), / trɪˌfɒsfəˌpɪˈrɪdiːn / (BrE)
A compound consisting of a pyridine ring with three phosphate groups attached; a term used in biochemistry, particularly in reference to NADP.
pyridine ring with three phosphate groups
medical

Examples

The coenzyme triphosphopyridine plays a vital role in cellular metabolism.
Scientists isolated triphosphopyridine from the cell extract during their experiment.
Researchers studied the structure of triphosphopyridine to understand its function in redox reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: NADP - coenzyme involved in redox reactions, also called triphosphopyridine nucleotide

Etymology

From 'tri-' (three), 'phospho-' (phosphate group), and 'pyridine' (a basic heterocyclic organic compound).
