ferromagnetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɛroʊˌmæɡˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɛrəʊˌmæɡˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or exhibiting ferromagnetism, the property of certain materials to become strongly magnetized in the presence of a magnetic field and retain magnetization after the field is removed.
having the property of ferromagnetism

Examples

Iron is a ferromagnetic material, meaning it can be magnetized easily.
Scientists studied the ferromagnetic properties of the new alloy.
Nickel and cobalt are also ferromagnetic, along with iron.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: paramagnetic - having a weaker form of magnetism than ferromagnetic materials
  • Antonym: antiferromagnetic - relating to materials where adjacent ions oppose each other's magnetic alignment

Etymology

From ferro- (iron) + magnetic, referring to iron's typical ferromagnetic properties.
