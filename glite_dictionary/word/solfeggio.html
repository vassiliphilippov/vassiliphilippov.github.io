solfeggio

1. NOUN

/ sɒlˈfɛdʒ.i.oʊ / (AmE), / sɒlˈfɛdʒ.i.əʊ / (BrE)
A musical exercise involving singing scales, runs, or melodies using fixed syllables, often to practice pitch and sight-singing.
singing exercise using musical syllables

Examples

At choir practice, we warmed up with a solfeggio to perfect our pitch.
The teacher assigned a challenging solfeggio exercise to improve sight-reading.
In the lesson, Maria practiced her solfeggio before singing the main piece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solfège - a method of teaching singing using syllables for each note of the scale

Etymology

From Italian 'solfeggio', from 'solfa' (the gamut syllables).

2. NOUN

/ sɔlˈfɛdʒioʊ / (AmE), / sɒlˈfɛdʒiəʊ / (BrE)
A method of teaching singing by using the syllables do, re, mi, fa, sol, la, and ti to represent the notes of the scale, especially in the C major scale.
singing using solfa syllables

Examples

The choir practiced solfeggio exercises to improve their pitch accuracy.
Learning solfeggio helps students understand musical intervals and scales.
During the lesson, the teacher led the class in a solfeggio drill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solfège - a system of solmization using syllables

Etymology

From Italian 'solfeggio', from 'solfa' (musical syllables).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01258522-n
Synset POS: n
