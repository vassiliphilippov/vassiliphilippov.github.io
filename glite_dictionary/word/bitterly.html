bitterly

1. ADV

/ ˈbɪt̬ɚli / (AmE), / ˈbɪtəli / (BrE)
In an extreme or intense manner, especially referring to unpleasant conditions or emotions.
in an extreme or intense way

Examples

The wind blew bitterly around the corners, making it hard to walk outside.
She was bitterly disappointed when she didn't get the job.
It was bitterly cold that night, with temperatures dropping well below freezing.

Synset Examples

it was bitterly cold
bitter cold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intensely - in a very strong or extreme way
  • Antonym: mildly - to a small extent; not severely

2. ADV

/ ˈbɪtɚli / (AmE), / ˈbɪtəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows strong anger, resentment, or disappointment; with bitterness.
with strong resentment or bitterness

Examples

She bitterly regretted the harsh words she had spoken.
He bitterly opposed the new policy, feeling deeply wronged by its implications.
After the loss, the team captain bitterly criticized the referee's decisions.

Synset Examples

she complained bitterly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resentfully - in a way that shows resentment
  • Antonym: sweetly - in a kind or pleasant manner
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00053300-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈbɪtɚli / (AmE), / ˈbɪtəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows strong emotion such as anger, resentment, or disappointment; with great intensity or harshness.
with intense emotion or resentment

Examples

She spoke bitterly about her unfair treatment at work.
After the defeat, the team was bitterly disappointed and refused to talk to reporters.
He bitterly regretted not taking the job offer when he had the chance.

Synset Examples

he was bitterly disappointed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harshly - in a severe or unkind manner
  • Antonym: mildly - in a gentle or slight manner

Etymology

From 'bitter' + '-ly'. 'Bitter' from Old English 'biter', related to German 'bitter'.
