barium

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛəriəm / (AmE), / ˈbeəriəm / (BrE)
a soft, silvery-white metallic element of the alkaline earth group, symbol Ba, used in various industrial and medical applications
soft silvery metallic element (Ba)

Examples

The doctor ordered a barium swallow to examine her digestive tract.
Pure barium is rarely found in nature because it reacts with air and water.
Researchers use barium compounds in laboratory tests and x-ray imaging.

Etymology

From New Latin 'barium', based on Greek 'barys' meaning 'heavy'.
