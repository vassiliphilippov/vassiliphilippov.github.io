Ariocarpus fissuratus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛri.oʊˈkɑːrpəs fɪˈsjʊəreɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌæri.əʊˈkɑːpəs fɪˈsjʊərətəs / (BrE)
a cactus species native to northeastern Mexico and the southwestern United States, with warty tubercles, usually unbranched and spineless, bearing magenta flowers and white or green fruit
a species of spineless cactus with magenta flowers

Examples

The Ariocarpus fissuratus is prized by cactus enthusiasts for its unusual appearance and rare magenta blooms.
In its native habitat, Ariocarpus fissuratus blends in with the rocky terrain, making it hard to spot.
Botanists conducted a survey of Ariocarpus fissuratus populations across Texas and northern Mexico.

Etymology

From Greek 'arios' (propitious), 'karpos' (fruit), and Latin 'fissuratus' (split or fissured), referring to its fissured tubercles.
