galvanizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡælvəˌnaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɡælvəˌnaɪzə / (BrE)
a skilled worker who coats iron or steel with zinc to protect it from rust
worker who zinc-coats metal

Examples

The galvanizer ensured that every steel beam was thoroughly coated for maximum protection.
After years of work, he became the lead galvanizer at the factory.
Without the expertise of a galvanizer, the metal parts would quickly rust and deteriorate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plater - a person who coats metal plates with another metal

Etymology

From 'galvanize', from Italian Luigi Galvani (1737–1798), whose experiments led to the process's name.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡælvəˌnaɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡal.və.naɪ.zə / (BrE)
a person who stimulates and excites others to take action, especially as a leader or motivator
person who inspires others into action

Examples

Her talent as a galvanizer helped transform the struggling community group into a thriving organization.
The coach was known as a galvanizer who always brought out the best in his team.
History remembers him as a galvanizer of social change during a time of crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motivator - a person who inspires others to act
  • Antonym: demotivator - someone who discourages or decreases motivation in others

Etymology

From galvanize, after Luigi Galvani, the Italian scientist whose work in electricity inspired the term.
