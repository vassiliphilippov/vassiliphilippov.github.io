bowler

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊlər / (AmE), / ˈbəʊlə / (BrE)
A cricketer who delivers the ball to the batsman.
cricket player who delivers the ball

Examples

The team's best bowler took five wickets in the match.
Each bowler has a unique style of delivering the ball.
She was named the top bowler in the cricket tournament.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: batsman - the player who tries to hit the ball in cricket

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊlər / (AmE), / ˈbəʊlə / (BrE)
a hard felt hat with a rounded crown and a narrow brim, traditionally worn by men
hard felt hat with rounded crown

Examples

He tipped his bowler politely as he entered the shop.
The photograph showed a gentleman in a black bowler and suit.
In London, the bowler became a symbol of British businessmen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derby - an American term for a bowler hat
  • Degree Lower: fedora - a felt hat with a creased crown and wide brim

Etymology

Named after the Bowler brothers, London hatmakers who designed it in the 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈboʊlər / (AmE), / ˈbəʊlə / (BrE)
a person who plays the game of bowling by rolling balls down an alley at pins
a person who bowls in bowling

Examples

She is an experienced bowler and competes in tournaments every weekend.
The team needed a new bowler after their star player was injured.
Many bowlers wear special shoes for better grip on the lanes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowling - the game involving rolling balls at pins

Etymology

From 'bowl' (to roll a ball) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
