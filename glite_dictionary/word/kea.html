kea

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈkiː.ə / (BrE)
a large, intelligent parrot native to the mountains of New Zealand, with brownish-green plumage and known for its curiosity
large New Zealand mountain parrot

Examples

The kea is famous for its playful behavior and intelligence.
On our hike, we saw a wild kea foraging among the rocks.
Farmers sometimes complain that keas peck at their livestock's wool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parrot - a bird with a curved beak and colorful feathers, known for mimicking sounds

Etymology

From Māori 'kea'.
