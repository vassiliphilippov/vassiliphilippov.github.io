misshapenness

1. NOUN

/ mɪsˈheɪp.nəs / (AmE), / mɪsˈheɪp.nɪs / (BrE)
the condition of being misshapen or malformed, especially referring to a part of the body
the state of being misshapen

Examples

The doctor explained that the child's hand misshapenness was due to a congenital disorder.
Repeated trauma can lead to misshapenness in the affected joints over time.
Surgery corrected the misshapenness of her nose, greatly improving her confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deformity - the state of being deformed or disfigured

Etymology

Formed from 'misshapen' + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).
