Coleus amboinicus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊli.əs ˌæmbɔɪˈnɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊlɪ.əs ˌæmbɔɪˈnɪkəs / (BrE)
a species of aromatic, fleshy herb native to India, Sri Lanka, and South Africa, sometimes classified in the genus Plectranthus; commonly known as Cuban oregano or Indian borage
a species of aromatic herb, Cuban oregano

Examples

Coleus amboinicus is often grown in kitchens for its aromatic leaves, used in cooking.
The nursery offered several varieties of Coleus amboinicus for home gardeners.
Researchers are studying Coleus amboinicus for its potential medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Plectranthus amboinicus - alternate scientific name for Coleus amboinicus, Cuban oregano

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Coleus, from Greek koleos (sheath), plus Latinized form of Ambon (island in Indonesia) + Latin -icus (pertaining to).
