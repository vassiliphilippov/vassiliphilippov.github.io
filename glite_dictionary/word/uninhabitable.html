uninhabitable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈhæbɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈhæbɪtəbəl / (BrE)
(of a place) not suitable or safe to live in.
not fit to live in

Examples

The hurricane left many homes uninhabitable due to severe flooding and damage.
Without access to clean water and electricity, the apartment was deemed uninhabitable.
Toxic waste had rendered the entire valley uninhabitable for decades.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: habitable - (of a place) suitable or good enough to live in

Etymology

From un- (not) + habitable, which comes from Latin habitabilis (able to be lived in).
