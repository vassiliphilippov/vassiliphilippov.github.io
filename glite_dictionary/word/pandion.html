Pandion

1. NOUN

/ ˈpændiˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈpandiːɒn / (BrE)
The genus of birds of prey comprising the ospreys, family Pandionidae.
genus of birds including ospreys

Examples

The only living species within the genus Pandion is the osprey.
Scientists have studied the genetic diversity of Pandion populations worldwide.
Fossils indicate that ancient members of the Pandion genus once lived on several continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osprey - large bird of prey, member of Pandion

Etymology

From Greek Pandion, a mythical king of Athens.
