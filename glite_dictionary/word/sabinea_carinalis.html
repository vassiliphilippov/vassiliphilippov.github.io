Sabinea carinalis

1. NOUN

/ səˈbin.i.ə kəˈrɪn.ə.lɪs / (AmE), / səˈbiː.ni.ə kəˈrɪn.ə.lɪs / (BrE)
A small tree native to Dominica, notable for its clusters of large crimson flowers that bloom before its pinnate leaves appear.
tropical tree with crimson flowers from Dominica

Examples

The Sabinea carinalis displays its vibrant crimson flowers before any leaves appear.
Botanists in Dominica often highlight the Sabinea carinalis as a symbol of the island's diverse flora.
Local gardeners appreciate the striking beauty of Sabinea carinalis in full bloom.

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'Sabinea,' honoring Joseph Sabine, English naturalist; species name from Latin 'carinalis,' meaning 'of the keel.'
