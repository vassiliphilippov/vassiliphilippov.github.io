offing

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒfɪŋ / (BrE)
the near or foreseeable future, especially as something likely to happen soon.
near or foreseeable future

Examples

Rumors suggest a promotion may be in the offing for her.
With budget cuts in the offing, everyone is waiting anxiously for the announcement.
There are big changes in the offing at the company this year.

Synset Examples

there was a wedding in the offing

Etymology

Originally referring to the part of the sea visible from the shore but beyond anchoring ground; from the phrase 'in the offing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑf.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒf.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the part of the sea that can be seen from the shore and lies beyond the anchoring area
part of the sea visible from shore beyond anchoring area

Examples

A large cruise ship was spotted in the offing just before sunrise.
Fishermen watched a storm brewing in the offing and prepared to secure their boats.
Several yachts waited in the offing before they could enter the busy harbor.

Synset Examples

there was a ship in the offing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offshore - situated at sea some distance from the shore

Etymology

From the verb 'off', referring to the area 'off' the coast (17th century maritime usage).
