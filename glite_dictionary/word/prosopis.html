Prosopis

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈsoʊpɪs / (AmE), / prəʊˈsəʊpɪs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical or subtropical shrubs or trees, including mesquites, known for their branching habit
genus of mesquite trees and shrubs

Examples

Prosopis species are well adapted to arid environments and are commonly found in deserts.
Farmers in Texas often plant Prosopis trees to prevent soil erosion and provide shade for livestock.
Research on Prosopis has shown its potential for use in sustainable biofuel production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesquite - a type of tree or shrub from the genus Prosopis

Etymology

From Greek 'prosōpis', meaning 'burdock' or a kind of plant.

2. NOUN

/ prəˈsoʊpɪs / (AmE), / prəˈsəʊpɪs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical or subtropical shrubs or trees known as mesquite, often with branching growth and found in arid regions
a genus of shrubs or trees; mesquite

Examples

The genus Prosopis includes several species commonly known as mesquite.
Researchers studied the ecological impact of Prosopis in semi-arid environments.
Invasive Prosopis trees have altered the landscape of many desert regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesquite - a tree or shrub of the genus Prosopis, common in arid regions

Etymology

From Greek prosōpis (a kind of tree).
