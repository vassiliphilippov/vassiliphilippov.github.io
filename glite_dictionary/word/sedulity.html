sedulity

1. NOUN

/ səˈdjuːləti / (AmE), / sɪˈdjuːlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being constantly diligent and attentive
persistent diligence and attentiveness

Examples

Her sedulity in completing every task impressed her supervisor.
The project succeeded largely due to the sedulity of its members.
He is admired for his intelligence as well as his sedulity in his profession.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diligence - careful and persistent work or effort
  • Antonym: indolence - avoidance of activity or exertion; laziness

Etymology

From Latin 'sedulitas', from 'sedulus' meaning 'zealous, diligent'.
