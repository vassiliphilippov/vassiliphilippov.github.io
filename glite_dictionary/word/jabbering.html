jabbering

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæbərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒabərɪŋ / (BrE)
rapid and indistinct, often meaningless or unintelligible speech
rapid, indistinct speech

Examples

The jabbering of the tourists made it hard to concentrate on the guide’s explanation.
She was annoyed by the constant jabbering in the next room.
Amid the jabbering of children, the teacher finally regained control of the classroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gabble - to talk rapidly and incomprehensibly; rapid, inarticulate speech

Etymology

From 'jabber' (talk rapidly and indistinctly) + -ing.
