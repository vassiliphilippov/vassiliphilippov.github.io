prostatitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊstəˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌprɒstəˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the prostate gland, often causing pain and urinary problems
inflammation of the prostate gland
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with prostatitis after complaining of frequent and painful urination.
Acute prostatitis can cause fever, chills, and discomfort in the lower pelvic area.
Chronic prostatitis may lead to ongoing perineal pain and urinary problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostate - a gland of the male reproductive system
  • Synonym: cystitis - inflammation of the urinary bladder

Etymology

From Greek prostátēs (prostate) + -itis (inflammation).
