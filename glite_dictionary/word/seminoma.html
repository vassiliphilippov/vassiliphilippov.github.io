seminoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛməˈnoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌsɛmɪˈnəʊmə / (BrE)
a malignant tumor of the testis, typically occurring in older men, composed of germ cells
malignant testicular tumor
medical

Examples

After surgery, the doctor confirmed that the mass was a seminoma.
Early diagnosis of seminoma often leads to successful treatment.
His medical history showed no previous risk factors for developing a seminoma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumor - an abnormal mass of tissue

Etymology

From Latin semen (seed) + -oma (tumor).
