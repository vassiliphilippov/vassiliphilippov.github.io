ingratiatory

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɡreɪʃiəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡreɪʃiətəri / (BrE)
intended to gain approval or favor; attempting to please or persuade in a way that seeks acceptance.
intended to gain favor or approval

Examples

His ingratiatory comments were clearly meant to win over the new manager.
She adopted an ingratiatory tone when asking for an extension on her assignment.
The politician's ingratiatory gestures did not go unnoticed by the public.

Synset Examples

a coaxing and obsequious voice
her manner is quiet and ingratiatory and a little too agreeable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsequious - excessively eager to please or obey
  • Synonym: coaxing - intended to persuade gently or flatteringly

Etymology

From Latin "ingratiari" (to render agreeable), from "in-" (in) + "gratia" (favor).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɡreɪʃiəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡreɪʃiətəri / (BrE)
intended to please someone or gain their favor, often insincerely.
seeking to gain favor

Examples

His ingratiatory remarks failed to impress the skeptical audience.
She adopted an ingratiatory tone when speaking to her new boss.
Politicians often use ingratiatory gestures to win public support.

Synset Examples

a smooth ingratiating manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingratiating - intended to gain approval or favor

Etymology

From Latin 'ingratiari' (to render agreeable) + English '-ory' (forming adjectives).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
