implicitness

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈplɪsɪtnəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɪsɪtnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being implied or understood though not directly expressed.
the state of being implied, not stated directly

Examples

His argument was difficult to refute because of its implicitness and subtlety.
The implicitness of her approval made it hard to know her true feelings.
Some legal documents lack precision due to the implicitness of their terms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: explicitness - the quality of being stated clearly and in detail

Etymology

From 'implicit' + noun-forming suffix '-ness'.
