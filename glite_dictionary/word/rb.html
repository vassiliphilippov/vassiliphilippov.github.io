Rb

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrˈbiː / (AmE), / ˌɑːˈbiː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for rubidium, a soft silvery metallic element of the alkali metal group that reacts violently with water.
chemical symbol for rubidium

Examples

Rb is often used in research laboratories studying the properties of alkali metals.
The mineral lepidolite contains trace amounts of Rb among other elements.
In the periodic table, Rb has the atomic number 37 and is placed below potassium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubidium - the element with symbol Rb and atomic number 37

Etymology

From New Latin 'rubidium', named after the Latin 'rubidus' (deep red), referring to its spectral lines.
