subsistence

1. NOUN

/ səbˈsɪstəns / (AmE), / səbˈsɪstəns / (BrE)
The state of existing in reality or having actual existence; substantialness.
state of existing in reality

Examples

In philosophy, the subsistence of an idea depends on its capacity to manifest in reality.
The artist questioned the subsistence of beauty outside of human perception.
They debated whether mathematical objects have an independent subsistence or exist only in the mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: existence - the state of being real or living or present
  • Antonym: nonexistence - the state of not existing

Etymology

From Latin 'subsistentia', from 'subsistere' (to stand firm, exist).

2. NOUN

/ səbˈsɪstəns / (AmE), / səbˈsɪstəns / (BrE)
the means by which someone maintains or supports life, especially at a minimal or basic level
means of maintaining life or survival

Examples

Many rural families depend on fishing for their subsistence.
The villagers relied on agriculture as their primary source of subsistence.
After the war, food shortages made basic subsistence a daily struggle.

Synset Examples

farming is a hard means of subsistence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: livelihood - means of securing the necessities of life
  • Synonym: survival - the state or fact of continuing to live

Etymology

From Latin subsistentia (firm standing, endurance), from subsistere (to stand firm).

3. NOUN

/ səbˈsɪstəns / (AmE), / səbˈsɪstəns / (BrE)
the minimum amount of resources needed to support life or existence, especially at a basic or marginal level
minimum resources for survival

Examples

With such a low wage, many families are forced to live at a level of subsistence.
In the remote village, farming was mainly for subsistence rather than profit.
After losing his job, he struggled to maintain even basic subsistence for his family.

Synset Examples

social security provided only a bare subsistence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: livelihood - means of securing the necessities of life
  • Degree Lower: poverty - the state of being extremely poor
  • Degree Higher: prosperity - the state of being successful and having plentiful resources

Etymology

From Latin 'subsistentia' (subsisting, standing firm), from 'subsistere' (to stand still, remain).
