Albizia julibrissin

1. NOUN

/ ælˈbɪziə ˌdʒuːlɪˈbrɪsɪn / (AmE), / ælˈbɪziə ˌdʒuːlɪˈbrɪsɪn / (BrE)
An ornamental Asiatic tree with domed or flat-topped crown, bipinnate leaves, and clusters of flowers bearing long silky stamens, commonly known as the silk tree or mimosa.
ornamental Asiatic tree; silk tree

Examples

The Albizia julibrissin brightened the garden with its feathery pink blossoms each summer.
Gardeners often choose Albizia julibrissin for its elegant shape and decorative flowers.
The city planted several Albizia julibrissin trees along the boulevard to enhance the urban landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silk tree - common name for Albizia julibrissin
  • Synonym: mimosa - common name for Albizia julibrissin

Etymology

From genus name Albizia (after Filippo degli Albizzi, Italian naturalist) and species epithet from Persian julābrisham (silk flower).
