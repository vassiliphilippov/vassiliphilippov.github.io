Paul III

1. PROPN

/ pɔl ðə ˈθɜrd / (AmE), / pɔːl ði ˈθɜːd / (BrE)
(1468–1549) Italian pope from 1534 to 1549, notable for excommunicating Henry VIII, initiating the Council of Trent, and supporting the Counter-Reformation.
Italian pope (1534–1549), Counter-Reformation leader

Examples

Paul III excommunicated Henry VIII, deepening the rift between England and Rome.
The Council of Trent was convened by Paul III in response to the Protestant Reformation.
Historians often discuss the impact Paul III had on the Catholic Church during the 16th century.
