satiable

1. ADJ

/ ˈseɪ.ʃə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.ti.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a need, desire, or appetite) capable of being satisfied or fulfilled.
able to be satisfied or fulfilled

Examples

Her satiable curiosity led her to explore many topics until she found answers.
After drinking two glasses of water, his satiable thirst was finally quenched.
The manager looked for someone with a satiable appetite for learning rather than endless demands.

Synset Examples

a satiable thirst
a satiable appetite

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insatiable - impossible to satisfy or quench

Etymology

From Latin satiabilis, from satur (sated, full).

2. ADJ

/ ˈseɪʃəbəl / (AmE), / ˈseɪʃəbəl / (BrE)
able to be satisfied; capable of being filled or satisfied by something.
able to be satisfied

Examples

Even after hours in the desert, his thirst remained satiable when offered enough water.
She has a satiable curiosity, always content when her questions are answered.
The dog seemed quite satiable and stopped eating once it was full.

Synset Examples

a satiable thirst
a satiable appetite

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insatiable - impossible to satisfy

Etymology

From Latin satiabilis (that may be satisfied), from satiāre (to fill, satisfy).
