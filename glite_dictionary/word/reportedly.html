reportedly

1. ADV

/ rɪˈpɔːrtɪdli / (AmE), / rɪˈpɔːtɪdli / (BrE)
according to what has been reported or stated by others; as claimed by some sources.
according to reports

Examples

The new policy will reportedly affect thousands of workers across the country.
The CEO is reportedly considering a major restructuring of the company.
The actor has reportedly signed a contract for three more films with the studio.

Synset Examples

she was reportedly his mistress for many years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegedly - used to convey that something is claimed to be the case, typically without substantiated proof

Etymology

From 'report' + '-edly', formed on the model of 'allegedly'.
