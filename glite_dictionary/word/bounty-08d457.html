bounty

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊnti / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnti / (BrE)
a payment or reward, especially one offered by a government, for certain acts such as capturing criminals, killing harmful animals, or enlisting in the military
reward or payment, often by a government

Examples

The government offered a bounty on invasive species to encourage their removal.
A bounty of $5,000 was placed on the fugitive’s head.
He joined the army to collect the enlistment bounty they were offering at the time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reward - something given in recognition of service or effort
  • Synonym: incentive - something that motivates action, often monetary

Etymology

From Middle French *bonté* (goodness), from Latin *bonitas* (goodness), from *bonus* (good).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊnti / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnti / (BrE)
a generous amount or abundance of something, especially when given as a gift or provided by nature
abundant amount; generosity

Examples

The orchard produced a bounty of apples after the rainy season.
We were grateful for the bounty of food on the table.
The garden gave us a bounty that lasted all summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being in short supply
  • Synonym: profusion - an abundance or large quantity of something

Etymology

From Old French 'bonté' (goodness), from Latin 'bonitas', from 'bonus' (good).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊnti / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnti / (BrE)
(literary) Generosity, especially as shown by a readiness to give freely or to bestow gifts.
generosity; willingness to give freely

Examples

The philanthropist was admired for her bounty and kindness to the poor.
Nature's bounty is evident in the abundance of crops this year.
He was touched by the bounty of his neighbors after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generosity - willingness to give or share freely
  • Antonym: stinginess - unwillingness to give or share; lack of generosity

Etymology

From Old French *bonté* (goodness), from Latin *bonitas* (goodness), from *bonus* (good).
