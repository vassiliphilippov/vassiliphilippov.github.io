Coropuna

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɔrəˈpunə / (AmE), / ˌkɒrəˈpuːnə / (BrE)
a mountain peak in the Andes of southern Peru, rising to 21,083 feet (6,425 meters) and known for its volcanic origin and glaciated summit.
Andean mountain peak in southern Peru

Examples

The snow-capped summit of Coropuna is visible from miles away across the arid Peruvian landscape.
Mountaineers have long been fascinated by the challenge of climbing Coropuna in the Andes.
Local legends speak of ancient rituals performed at the peak of Coropuna.
