technicality

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛk.nɪˈkæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌtɛk.nɪˈkæl.ə.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being technical, especially in the use of specialized terms or methods
state of being technical

Examples

The argument was lost because the debate became mired in technicality instead of focusing on practical issues.
His explanation was filled with too much technicality for the general audience to understand.
The committee's insistence on technicality slowed down the decision-making process.

Synset Examples

the judicial system suffered from too much technicality and formality
It is a tribute to the tribunals that the technicality at the heart of the appellate process in these tribunals can and does coexist with the relative informality in atmosphere and with procedural flexibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formality - the quality of being strictly formal or following prescribed forms

Etymology

From French ‘technicalité’, derived from ‘technique’ and the suffix ‘-ality’.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛk.nɪˈkæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌtɛk.nɪˈkæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a minor or specific detail in rules, laws, or procedures, often one that affects the outcome of a situation
minor or specific rule or detail

Examples

The case was dismissed because of a technicality in the contract.
He won the argument on a mere technicality, but most people disagreed with the outcome.
Their proposal failed due to a small technicality in the application process.

Synset Examples

the resolution died on a technicality
the defendant was acquitted on a legal technicality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detail - a small part of something; a particular fact or item

Etymology

From technical + -ity, ultimately from Greek tekhnikós (relating to art or skill).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05828731-n
Synset POS: n
