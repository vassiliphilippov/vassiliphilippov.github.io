chandlery

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃændləri / (AmE), / ˈtʃændləri / (BrE)
candles and other goods sold by a chandler, especially in historical or maritime contexts
goods sold by a chandler

Examples

The old shop specialized in chandlery for local households and churches.
On board the ship, essential chandlery included candles, soap, and lamp oil.
He inherited the family chandlery, supplying candles to the entire village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chandler - a person who makes or sells candles and other goods

Etymology

From 'chandler' (candle maker or seller), derived from Old French 'chandelier'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæn.dl.ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈtʃæn.dləri / (BrE)
a storeroom in which candles are kept, especially in large houses or institutions, chiefly in historical use.
storeroom for candles (historical)
archaic

Examples

The old manor still has a chandlery where wax candles were once stored.
During the restoration, workers discovered a hidden chandlery filled with antique candlesticks.
In medieval times, the chandlery was an essential part of any large household.

Etymology

From Middle English 'chaundlerye', from 'chaundeler' (candlemaker), ultimately from Old French 'chandelier'.
