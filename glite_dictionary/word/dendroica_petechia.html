Dendroica petechia

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛndroʊˈaɪkə pəˈtik.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌdɛndrɔɪˈkə piːˈtiːkɪə / (BrE)
A species of American wood warbler with a yellow throat, commonly known as the yellow warbler.
species of yellow American wood warbler

Examples

The Dendroica petechia is easily recognized by its bright yellow plumage and musical song.
Migratory patterns of Dendroica petechia have been extensively studied by ornithologists.
I spotted a Dendroica petechia in the mangrove forest during my birdwatching trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow warbler - common name for Dendroica petechia, a yellow-throated American wood warbler

Etymology

From Greek dendron (tree) and oikos (house), and Latin petechia (small spot), referring to its habitat and plumage.
