stratified

1. ADJ

/ ˈstræt̬.ə.faɪd / (AmE), / ˈstræt.ɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
arranged in layers, classes, or ranks according to status or grade
arranged in layers or ranks

Examples

The school system is highly stratified by socioeconomic status.
The archaeologists carefully examined the stratified layers of soil at the site.
In a stratified society, people are often divided by class and privilege.

Synset Examples

stratified areas of the distribution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layered - arranged in layers or strata
  • Antonym: homogeneous - of the same or similar kind or nature; lacking layers or ranks

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'stratum' (layer) + English suffix '-fy' (to make) and '-ed'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈstræt̬.əˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈstræt.ɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
(of a substance or structure) deposited or arranged in horizontal layers, especially as seen in geology or sedimentary rocks.
arranged in horizontal layers

Examples

The canyon walls revealed beautifully stratified layers of sandstone and shale.
Scientists studied the stratified deposits to understand the region's geological history.
The stratified structure of the rock indicates that it was formed over millions of years.

Synset Examples

stratified rock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layered - having or arranged in layers

Etymology

From Medieval Latin stratificare (to make in layers), from Latin stratum (covering, layer) + -fy.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00209821-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈstræt̬.əˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈstræt.ɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
(of a society or group) divided into different levels or classes according to social status, wealth, or other criteria.
divided into social layers or classes

Examples

Sociologists study how stratified societies affect individual opportunities.
In a highly stratified community, access to quality education may depend on one's social class.
The reforms aimed to make the nation less stratified and more inclusive.

Synset Examples

American society is becoming increasingly stratified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hierarchical - arranged in order of rank

Etymology

From Latin 'stratum' (layer) + '-fy' (make) + '-ed'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01206375-s
Synset POS: s
