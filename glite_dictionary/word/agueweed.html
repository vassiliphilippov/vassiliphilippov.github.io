agueweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪɡjuˌwid / (AmE), / ˈeɪɡjuːˌwiːd / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to the southeastern United States, with white-rayed flower heads; formerly used in folk medicine.
perennial herb, former folk remedy

Examples

Folk healers once relied on agueweed as a remedy for fevers and chills.
The agueweed blooms in late summer, displaying distinctive white rays.
While no longer commonly used, agueweed still grows wild in parts of the southeastern United States.

Etymology

Formed from 'ague' (fever) and 'weed' (plant); named for its former use in treating fevers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪɡjuˌwid / (AmE), / ˈeɪɡjuːwiːd / (BrE)
A gentian plant of eastern North America, known for its clusters of bristly blue flowers.
North American gentian plant

Examples

The herbalist recommended using agueweed for its supposed medicinal properties.
In late summer, the agueweed blooms, displaying clusters of vivid blue flowers in the meadows.
Local guides pointed out a patch of agueweed growing alongside the woodland trail.

Etymology

From 'ague' (a fever) + 'weed', referring to former medicinal use.
