celecoxib

1. NOUN

/ sɛlɪˈkɑːksɪb / (AmE), / sɛlɪˈkɒksɪb / (BrE)
A nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to relieve pain and inflammation, often prescribed for arthritis and similar conditions.
NSAID pain and inflammation drug
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed celecoxib to help manage my arthritis pain.
Many patients switch to celecoxib because it has fewer gastrointestinal side effects than some older NSAIDs.
After taking celecoxib, she noticed a significant reduction in her joint swelling and discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ibuprofen - another commonly used NSAID for pain and inflammation
  • Synonym: aspirin - an older NSAID with different side effects

Etymology

Formed from the chemical components and class 'coxib', indicating COX-2 inhibition.
