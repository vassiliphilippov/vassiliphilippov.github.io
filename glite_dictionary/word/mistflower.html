mistflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪstˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈmɪstˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the central and southeastern United States and the West Indies, with large, showy heads of clear blue flowers, sometimes classified in the genus Eupatorium.
a flowering plant with blue flower heads

Examples

The mistflower brightened the edge of the woodland with its vivid blue blooms.
Gardeners often plant mistflower to attract butterflies and other pollinators.
We admired a patch of wild mistflowers growing along the riverbank.
