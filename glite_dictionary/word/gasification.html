gasification

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæsəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡasɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of converting a substance, especially a solid or liquid, into gas, often for use as fuel
conversion of materials into gas

Examples

The gasification of coal provides a cleaner form of energy compared to direct combustion.
Modern waste-to-energy plants often use gasification to convert trash into usable fuels.
Research into biomass gasification is helping to create alternative renewable energy sources.

Synset Examples

coal gas is produced by the gasification of coal

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: combustion - the process of burning something

Etymology

From 'gasify' plus '-ation'; 'gasify' from 'gas' (Dutch 'gas') + '-fy' (Latin '-ficare').
