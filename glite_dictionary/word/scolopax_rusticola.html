Scolopax rusticola

1. NOUN

/ skoʊˈloʊpæks rʌˈstɪkələ / (AmE), / skɒˈlɒpaks rʌˈstɪkələ / (BrE)
A species of woodcock found across Europe and Asia, known for its short legs, long bill, and migratory habits.
Eurasian woodcock species

Examples

The Scolopax rusticola is well-known for its remarkable camouflage among woodland leaves.
Each spring, birdwatchers gather to observe the migration habits of Scolopax rusticola across Europe.
The long bill of the Scolopax rusticola helps it probe the ground for earthworms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodcock - a wading bird with a long bill related to the Eurasian woodcock

Etymology

From Latin: 'Scolopax' meaning woodcock, and 'rusticola' meaning one inhabiting the countryside.
