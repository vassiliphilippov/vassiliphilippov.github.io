bassinet

1. NOUN

/ ˌbæsɪˈnɛt / (AmE), / ˌbæsɪˈnɛt / (BrE)
a small bed, often with a hood, used for a newborn baby
small bed for a newborn baby

Examples

The newborn slept soundly in the bassinet beside her parents' bed.
They decorated the bassinet with a soft, blue blanket for their baby boy.
She gently rocked the bassinet to soothe her crying daughter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cradle - a baby's bed with rockers

Etymology

From French 'berceau' (cradle), later influenced by 'bassinet' (small basin or cradle).

2. NOUN

/ bæˈsɪnɛt / (AmE), / bæˈsɪnɛt / (BrE)
a type of baby carriage or pram with a deep, basket-like bed for a baby, resembling a cradle
baby carriage; basket-like pram

Examples

She gently placed the sleeping infant in the bassinet before taking a walk in the park.
The vintage bassinet had a charming, old-fashioned design with a hood and wheels.
Parents often choose a bassinet for their newborns as it keeps the baby cozy and secure during strolls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pram - a baby carriage, especially in British English

Etymology

From French 'berceau', influenced by Italian 'bassanello'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02807681-n
Synset POS: n
