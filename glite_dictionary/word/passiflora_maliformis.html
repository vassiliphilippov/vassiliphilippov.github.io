Passiflora maliformis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæsɪˈflɔːrə ˌmælɪˈfɔːrmɪs / (AmE), / ˌpæsɪˈflɔːrə ˌmælɪˈfɔːmɪs / (BrE)
A species of passionflower native to the West Indies, known for its climbing vines and edible, apple-sized fruit.
West Indian passionflower species

Examples

The fruit of the Passiflora maliformis is popular in Caribbean cuisine.
Botanists identified the climbing vine as Passiflora maliformis during the field survey.
Many gardens in tropical regions cultivate Passiflora maliformis for its edible fruit and attractive flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passionflower - any plant of the genus Passiflora, known for their striking flowers

Etymology

From Latin, genus name 'Passiflora' (passionflower) and species name 'maliformis' (apple-shaped).
