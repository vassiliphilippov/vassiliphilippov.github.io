to knap

1. VERB

/ næp / (AmE), / næp / (BrE)
to break a small piece off something, especially by striking it; to shape or chip by breaking off flakes, as in working stone or flint
break a small piece off by striking

Examples

Archaeologists often knap flint to reproduce ancient tools.
He used a hammerstone to knap the stone into a sharp blade.
If you knap the edge too hard, it might break the arrowhead.

Synset Examples

chip the glass
chip a tooth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chip - to break off a small piece from something

Etymology

Old English *cnappian*, related to Middle Dutch *knappen* (to crack, snap).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01262022-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ næp / (AmE), / nap / (BrE)
to strike something sharply, especially with a quick, hard blow
to strike sharply
archaic

Examples

He took the stone and knapped it sharply to make a tool.
The craftsman knapped the flint to shape it into an arrowhead.
She knapped the brittle biscuit, breaking it cleanly in half.

Synset Examples

rap him on the knuckles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rap - to strike quickly with a sharp blow
  • Degree Lower: tap - to touch or strike lightly

Etymology

Old English *cnæppan* (to strike), related to Middle Low German *knappen* (to snap).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01416942-v
Synset POS: v
