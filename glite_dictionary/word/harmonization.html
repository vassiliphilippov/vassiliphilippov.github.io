harmonization

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑːrmənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhɑːmənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of singing musical notes together in harmony to produce a pleasing sound
singing in musical harmony

Examples

Their harmonization during the choir performance was flawless and moved the audience to tears.
Good harmonization can turn a simple melody into a rich, layered musical experience.
The band practiced the harmonization of their voices until every note blended perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmony - the combination of musical notes to produce chords and chord progressions

Etymology

From Greek harmonia (agreement, concord) via Latin harmonizare and French harmoniser.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɑrmənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhɑːmənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a musical arrangement or composition in which different notes are combined to produce harmony
musical arrangement with harmony

Examples

The choir performed a beautiful harmonization of the traditional folk song.
Her latest album features a new harmonization of several classic hymns.
This harmonization highlights the interaction between the soprano and alto voices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - a musical adaptation of a composition
  • Antonym: melody - a sequence of musical notes forming a tune; often contrasted with harmony

Etymology

From harmonize + -ation, ultimately from Greek harmonia (agreement, harmony).

Source: WordNet
