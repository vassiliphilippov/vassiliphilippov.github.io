Yankee-Doodle

1. NOUN

/ ˌjæŋki ˈduːdəl / (AmE), / ˌjaŋki ˈduːdəl / (BrE)
An American person, especially as perceived by non-Americans; sometimes used humorously or disparagingly.
an American, esp. to outsiders

Examples

As a British tourist, she always thought of him as a typical Yankee-Doodle.
The movie portrayed every American character as a cheerful Yankee-Doodle.
In the old cartoons, foreigners would often refer to Americans as Yankee-Doodles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yankee - A person from the United States, especially the northern states

Etymology

From the title of the song 'Yankee Doodle,' dating to the American Revolutionary War, itself possibly from Dutch 'janke' (diminutive of Jan) and 'doodle' (simpleton).
