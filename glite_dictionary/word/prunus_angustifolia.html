Prunus angustifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs ˌæŋɡəˌstoʊˈliːə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnʊs ˌaŋɡʊstəˈlɪə / (BrE)
A small North American shrubby tree, also known as the Chickasaw plum, bearing small edible yellow to reddish fruit.
species of shrubby tree; Chickasaw plum

Examples

The Prunus angustifolia thrives in sandy soils throughout the southeastern United States.
Wildlife often feeds on the fruit of the Prunus angustifolia during late summer.
Gardeners value Prunus angustifolia both for its edible fruit and as an attractive ornamental shrub.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chickasaw plum - common name for Prunus angustifolia

Etymology

From Latin: prunus (plum tree) + angustifolia ('narrow-leaved')
