supergrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːpərˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a police informer who gives evidence that implicates a large number of people, especially in organized crime; chiefly British
police informer implicating many people (British)
slang british

Examples

The police relied on a supergrass to break up the criminal network.
After turning supergrass, he was placed in a witness protection program.
The trial hinged on the testimony of a supergrass who named dozens of accomplices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informant - a person who provides information to the police
  • Synonym: whistleblower - a person who exposes wrongdoing within an organization

Etymology

British slang; 'grass' is rhyming slang (grasshopper = copper), with 'super' indicating extensive involvement.
