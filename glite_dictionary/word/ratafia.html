ratafia

1. NOUN

/ ˌrætəˈfiːə / (AmE), / ˌratəˈfiːə / (BrE)
A sweet liqueur made from wine and brandy, flavored with the kernels of plums, peaches, apricots, or bitter almonds.
sweet liqueur flavored with fruit kernels or almonds

Examples

After dinner, she served a glass of ratafia as a digestif.
The homemade ratafia had a subtle almond flavor and a pleasant sweetness.
He brought a bottle of ratafia to the holiday celebration.

Etymology

From French ratafia, possibly of Spanish or Italian origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌræ.təˈfiː.ə / (AmE), / ˌræ.təˈfiː.ə / (BrE)
A macaroon or biscuit flavored with ratafia liqueur or almond essence.
macaroon with ratafia liqueur flavor

Examples

She served fresh coffee with delicate ratafia macaroons after dinner.
The bakery specializes in traditional French ratafia biscuits.
His favorite treat was always a crisp ratafia with a hint of almond flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macaroon - a type of small, sweet cake, typically made from ground almonds or coconut

Etymology

From French 'ratafia', originally referring to a liqueur, likely of Spanish or Italian origin.
