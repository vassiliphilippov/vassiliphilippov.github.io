Glareola

1. NOUN

/ ɡləˈriː.ələ / (AmE), / ɡləˈriː.ələ / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Glareolidae, commonly known as pratincoles, found in the Old World and known for their slender bodies and long wings.
genus of birds; pratincoles

Examples

Several species within the genus Glareola are known for their agile flight patterns.
The Glareola can often be seen hunting insects over open fields.
Ornithologists identified a rare Glareola nesting site along the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pratincole - a wading bird of the genus Glareola, with long wings and forked tail

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Glareola, diminutive of Latin glarea (gravel), referring to their stony nesting habitats.

2. NOUN

/ ɡləˈriːələ / (AmE), / ɡləˈriːələ / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Glareolidae, known as pratincoles, characterized by their slender bodies, long pointed wings, and short bills.
genus of birds (pratincoles)

Examples

The Glareola species are often seen flying gracefully over rivers and lakes.
One of the best-known members of Glareola is the collared pratincole.
Researchers observed several Glareola nesting in the gravel beds during spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pratincole - bird of the Glareola genus, known for long wings and graceful flight

Etymology

From New Latin Glareola, a diminutive of Latin glarea meaning 'gravel', referring to the birds' typical habitat.
