Quamoclit pennata

1. NOUN

/ kwəˈmɒklɪt pəˈneɪtə / (AmE), / kwəˈmɒklɪt peˈnɑːtə / (BrE)
a tropical American annual climbing plant with red or sometimes white flowers and finely divided leaves, naturalized in the United States and other regions
tropical climbing plant with red or white flowers

Examples

The garden trellis was covered in the bright red blooms of Quamoclit pennata.
Many gardeners plant Quamoclit pennata to attract hummingbirds and butterflies.
The finely dissected leaves make Quamoclit pennata a distinctive addition to any landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress vine - common name for Quamoclit pennata

Etymology

From modern Latin; 'Quamoclit' from Greek, and 'pennata' from Latin meaning 'feathered', referring to its leaves.
