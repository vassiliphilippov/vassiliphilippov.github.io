vocabulary

1. NOUN

/ voʊˈkæbjəˌlɛri / (AmE), / vəʊˈkæbjʊləri / (BrE)
a list or collection of words and phrases used in a particular subject, field, or by a particular group
list of words for a field or subject

Examples

The biology textbook includes a useful vocabulary at the end of each chapter.
Students are required to learn the legal vocabulary specific to this course.
The technical vocabulary of computing has grown rapidly in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lexicon - the vocabulary of a person, language, or branch of knowledge

Etymology

From Latin ‘vocabulum’ (a word, name), from ‘vocare’ (to call).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06432321-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ voʊˈkæbjələri / (AmE), / vəʊˈkæbjələri / (BrE)
all the words known and used by a person, or all the words that make up a particular language.
all the words known or used by someone

Examples

Her English vocabulary has grown significantly since she started reading more books.
A rich vocabulary helps you express ideas more clearly and accurately.
The test measures both grammar and vocabulary skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lexicon - the complete set of words in a language
  • Synonym: terminology - the set of special words or expressions used in a particular subject

Etymology

From Latin 'vocabulum' (a word, name) via Medieval Latin 'vocabularium'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05659087-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ voʊˈkæbjələri / (AmE), / vəʊˈkæbjʊləri / (BrE)
the range of techniques, symbols, or expressive elements used within a particular art, craft, or discipline
range of techniques or symbols in an art or craft

Examples

The artist expanded her vocabulary by experimenting with new materials and styles.
Jazz musicians share a common vocabulary of riffs and improvisational techniques.
Her vocabulary of movements set her choreography apart from that of other dancers.

Synset Examples

he introduced a wide vocabulary of techniques

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lexicon - the complete set of words in a language or subject

Etymology

From Latin vocabularium (a list of words), related to vocabulum (word, name).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05671119-n
Synset POS: n
