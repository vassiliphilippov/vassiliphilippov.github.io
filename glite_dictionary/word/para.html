para

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæɹə / (AmE), / ˈpæɹə / (BrE)
(in obstetrics) a woman who has given birth to one or more liveborn children, or the number of liveborn children delivered by a woman.
number of live births (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient is a para two, having given birth to two healthy children.
Her medical chart recorded her as a para three and gravida four.
Understanding a woman's para status helps doctors assess her obstetric history.

Synset Examples

the parity of the mother must be considered
a bipara is a woman who has given birth to two children

Etymology

From Latin 'parere' (to bring forth, give birth).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpærə / (AmE), / ˈpærə / (BrE)
a soldier who is trained to parachute into an operation, especially as a member of a paratroop regiment
soldier in the paratroops

Examples

The para landed behind enemy lines during the night operation.
Many paras take part in peacekeeping missions around the world.
He served as a para in the army for six years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paratrooper - a soldier trained to parachute into combat

Etymology

Shortened from 'paratrooper'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrə / (AmE), / ˈpɑːrə / (BrE)
A former monetary unit in Yugoslavia, equal to one hundredth of a dinar.
former monetary unit of Yugoslavia

Examples

In pre-1990 Yugoslavia, a loaf of bread cost just a few para.
When the dinar was Yugoslavia's currency, 100 para made up one dinar.
Collectors sometimes seek old Yugoslav para coins for their historical value.

Etymology

From Turkish 'para' (coin, money), itself from Persian 'pārah' (piece, fragment).
