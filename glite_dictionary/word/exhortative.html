exhortative

1. ADJ

/ ɛɡˈzɔːr.tə.tɪv / (AmE), / ɛɡˈzɔː.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
Giving strong encouragement or urging someone to take action.
giving strong encouragement

Examples

The leader delivered an exhortative speech to inspire the volunteers before their work began.
She wrote an exhortative letter urging her friend not to give up on her studies.
His tone was exhortative, pushing the team to strive for success despite the difficulties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encouraging - giving support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • Antonym: discouraging - causing someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin 'exhortativus', from 'exhortari' (to exhort).
