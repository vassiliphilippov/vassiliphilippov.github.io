riskiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪski.nəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪskɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being risky; the likelihood of danger or loss
the quality of being risky or dangerous

Examples

Investors should carefully consider the riskiness of a project before committing their money.
He was attracted by the riskiness of extreme sports like skydiving and rock climbing.
The riskiness of the plan made many employees hesitant to support it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dangerousness - the quality of being dangerous
  • Antonym: safety - the condition of being protected from danger or risk

Etymology

From 'risky' + '-ness'.
