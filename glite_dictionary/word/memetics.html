memetics

1. NOUN

/ məˈmɛtɪks / (AmE), / mɪˈmiːtɪks / (BrE)
the study of how cultural information, ideas, and beliefs spread and evolve in a manner analogous to biological evolution
theory of cultural evolution of ideas

Examples

The field of memetics explores how cultural ideas replicate and adapt throughout societies.
Scholars in memetics analyze how internet trends evolve much like biological species.
Debates in memetics often focus on the mechanisms by which certain ideas survive and proliferate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meme - an idea, behavior, or style that spreads from person to person within a culture

Etymology

From 'meme' (coined by Richard Dawkins from Greek 'mīmēma', meaning 'that which is imitated'), with the suffix '-ics' indicating a field of study.
