untreated

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtritɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtriːtɪd / (BrE)
not having received medical attention, care, or treatment.
not given medical treatment

Examples

The infection worsened because it was left untreated for several weeks.
She was concerned about the effects of drinking untreated water while traveling.
Many injuries remained untreated after the earthquake due to a lack of medical staff.

Synset Examples

an untreated disease
the untreated wounded lay on makeshift cots

Related Terms

  • Antonym: treated - having received medical care or treatment
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtritɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtriːtɪd / (BrE)
not having been subjected to any chemical or physical process to alter its original state.
not chemically or physically processed

Examples

The laboratory compared samples of untreated wood with those that had been coated.
You should avoid drinking untreated water from natural sources as it may contain bacteria.
The untreated fabric shrank considerably after washing.

Synset Examples

an untreated fabric

Related Terms

  • Antonym: processed - having been subjected to a chemical or physical treatment
  • Antonym: treated - having been treated, especially with chemicals or by a process
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01961603-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtriːtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtriːtɪd / (BrE)
(of a specimen) not treated with a reagent or dye, especially for study under a microscope.
not treated with a reagent or dye

Examples

The untreated sample showed no color under the microscope.
Researchers compared the effects of the drug on treated and untreated cells.
It was important to analyze both untreated and stained tissue sections.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stained - (of a specimen) treated with a dye or reagent to enhance visibility under a microscope

Etymology

From un- (not) + treated (past participle of 'treat').
