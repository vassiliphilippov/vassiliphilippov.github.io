plundered

1. ADJ

/ ˈplʌndɚd / (AmE), / ˈplʌndəd / (BrE)
Having been wrongfully robbed or stripped of valuables, especially by force or during disorder.
robbed of valuables by force

Examples

The museum was plundered during the civil war, leaving many artifacts missing.
Villagers returned to find their homes plundered after the invading army had left.
The plundered tombs provided little for archaeologists to study.

Synset Examples

the robbers left the looted train
people returned to the plundered village

Related Terms

  • Synonym: looted - having been stolen from, especially during chaos

Etymology

Past participle of 'plunder', from Middle High German plunderen (to plunder, raid).
