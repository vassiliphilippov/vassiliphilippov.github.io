Brassica perviridis

1. NOUN

/ bræˈsɪkə pɛrˈvɪrɪdɪs / (AmE), / bræˈsɪkə pəˈvɪrɪdɪs / (BrE)
An edible Asiatic plant cultivated for its swollen root and leafy greens, often used in cooking.
edible Asian plant with root and leaves

Examples

Brassica perviridis is often used in stir-fry dishes across East Asia.
Farmers in the region grow Brassica perviridis for its nutritious leaves and crunchy root.
The market had fresh bunches of Brassica perviridis alongside other leafy greens.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Brassica' for cabbages, and species epithet 'perviridis' meaning 'very green.'
