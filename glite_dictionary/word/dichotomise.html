to dichotomise

1. VERB

/ daɪˈkɑtəmaɪz / (AmE), / daɪˈkɒtəmaɪz / (BrE)
to divide or separate into two completely opposing or contrasting groups or kinds
divide into two opposing groups

Examples

The debate has dichotomised the community into two opposing camps.
It is unhelpful to dichotomise complex social issues into good and bad.
The media often dichotomises political opinions, ignoring the nuances in between.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to polarize - to cause to divide into two sharply contrasting groups or opinions
  • Antonym: to unify - to bring together or combine into a single group or entity

Etymology

From Greek 'dichotomia' meaning 'a cutting in two', via Medieval Latin and French.
