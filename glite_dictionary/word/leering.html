leering

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
Looking at someone in an unpleasant, sly, or sexually suggestive way.
showing sly or unpleasant intent in a look

Examples

He gave her a leering look across the crowded room.
The man’s leering smile made her uncomfortable as she passed by.
She felt uneasy under the leering eyes of the strangers.

Synset Examples

she had run in fear of … his evil leering eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leer - to look in a sly or unpleasant way

Etymology

From the verb 'leer', from Middle English 'lere' (to look askance).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or glance) looking in a sly or sexually suggestive way, often making others feel uncomfortable or uneasy.
looking in a sly, suggestive way

Examples

He caught the leering gaze of the stranger across the bar.
She was uncomfortable under the leering stare of her coworker.
A group of leering men stood at the corner, making rude comments.

Synset Examples

leering drugstore cowboys

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leer - to look in a sly or sexually suggestive way
  • Synonym: ogling - to look at someone in a lecherous manner

Etymology

From the verb 'leer', which comes from Middle English *leren* (to learn), but the meaning shifted, possibly from Middle Low German *liren* (to look askew).
