intercommunication

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərkəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the mutual exchange of information or communication between people, groups, or systems
mutual exchange of communication

Examples

The new system allows instant intercommunication between all departments within the company.
Effective intercommunication is essential for the success of any project involving multiple teams.
Scientists stressed the importance of intercommunication among international research institutes.

Synset Examples

they intercepted intercommunication between enemy ships

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communication - the act of conveying information

Etymology

From Latin 'inter-' (between) and 'communicatio' (communication).
