unauthorised

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈɔːθəˌraɪzd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈɔːθəraɪzd / (BrE)
Not having official permission or approval.
without official permission
british

Examples

The police quickly removed the unauthorised vehicles from the restricted area.
Employees found using unauthorised software may face disciplinary action.
There was an unauthorised access attempt to the company's confidential files.

Synset Examples

an unauthorized strike
wildcat work stoppage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: authorized - having official permission
  • Synonym: unlicensed - not having a required license

Etymology

From un- (not) + authorised, from Middle English auctorisen (to authorize), from Old French auctoriser.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈɔːθəˌraɪzd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈɔːθəraɪzd / (BrE)
not having official permission or approval; not having been given authority.
not having official permission
british

Examples

No unauthorised personnel are allowed beyond this point.
He was caught entering the building through an unauthorised door.
The company launched an investigation into the unauthorised access of its data.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: authorized - having official permission or approval
  • Synonym: unauthorized - not having official permission or approval (American spelling)

Etymology

From Latin 'auctoritas' (authority) with prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' and suffix '-ised', British spelling of 'unauthorized'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00180786-a
Synset POS: a
