Kiswahili

1. NOUN

/ kiːswɑːˈhiːli / (AmE), / kiːswəˈhiːli / (BrE)
A Bantu language widely spoken in Tanzania, Kenya, Uganda, Rwanda, Burundi, Mozambique, and the Democratic Republic of Congo.
East African Bantu language

Examples

Many students choose to study Kiswahili as their foreign language at university.
The film was subtitled in both English and Kiswahili for local audiences.
She speaks Kiswahili fluently after living in Nairobi for several years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swahili - another common name for the Kiswahili language or its speakers

Etymology

From Swahili *Kiswahili*, from *ki-* (language prefix) + *Swahili* (coastal people, from Arabic).

2. PROPN

/ kiˌswɑːˈhiːli / (AmE), / kiˌswɑːˈhiːli / (BrE)
A Bantu language widely spoken in East Africa, including Tanzania, Kenya, Uganda, and neighboring countries.
Bantu language of East Africa

Examples

Many students choose to learn Kiswahili as a second language in school.
He spoke fluent Kiswahili with the local merchants during his travels in Kenya.
Kiswahili is recognized as an official language in several East African countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swahili - widely used Bantu language of East Africa

Etymology

From Swahili *Kiswahili*, meaning 'the Swahili language'.
