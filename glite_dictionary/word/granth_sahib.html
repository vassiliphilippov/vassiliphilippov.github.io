Granth Sahib

1. NOUN

/ ɡrʌnt ˈsɑː.hɪb / (AmE), / ɡrʌnt ˈsɑː.hɪb / (BrE)
the principal sacred scripture of Sikhism, composed of hymns, poetry, and teachings of the Sikh Gurus
the principal sacred scripture of Sikhism

Examples

Many Sikhs visit the temple daily to listen to readings from the Granth Sahib.
The teachings of the Granth Sahib emphasize compassion and equality.
It is customary to cover one's head when in the presence of the Granth Sahib.

Etymology

From Punjabi, where 'Granth' means 'book' and 'Sahib' is an honorific meaning 'respected' or 'master'.
