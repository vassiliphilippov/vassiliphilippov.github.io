scuba

1. NOUN

/ ˈskuːbə / (AmE), / ˈskuːbə / (BrE)
A portable device that supplies air, allowing divers to breathe underwater.
underwater breathing apparatus

Examples

The diver checked his scuba gear before descending into the coral reef.
Many tourists take scuba lessons before exploring the underwater caves.
Her dream was to become a scuba instructor and travel the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aqualung - early brand of underwater breathing apparatus

Etymology

Acronym from 'Self-Contained Underwater Breathing Apparatus'.
