ominous

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑː.mə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɒm.ɪ.nəs / (BrE)
giving the impression that something bad or unpleasant is going to happen; suggesting a future threat or danger.
suggesting something bad will happen

Examples

The sky was covered with ominous clouds, warning of an approaching storm.
There was an ominous silence in the hallway after the principal walked past.
Her ominous tone made everyone in the room uneasy.

Synset Examples

ill omens
ill predictions
my words with inauspicious thunderings shook heaven
a dead and ominous silence prevailed
a by-election at a time highly unpropitious for the Government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threatening - suggesting danger or harm
  • Synonym: inauspicious - not conducive to success; unpromising
  • Antonym: auspicious - suggesting a positive or successful outcome

Etymology

From Latin 'omen' (a sign, especially of future events) + '-ous'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑː.mə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɒm.ɪ.nəs / (BrE)
suggesting that something bad or unpleasant is going to happen; giving a warning or sign of future trouble or danger
suggesting possible danger or trouble

Examples

The sky was filled with ominous dark clouds before the storm began.
She received an ominous phone call that made her uneasy all day.
The silence in the abandoned building felt particularly ominous at night.

Synset Examples

a baleful look
forbidding thunderclouds
his tone became menacing
ominous rumblings of discontent
sinister storm clouds
a sinister smile
his threatening behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menacing - threatening harm or danger; suggesting menace
  • Synonym: sinister - giving the impression that something harmful is happening or will happen
  • Antonym: promising - giving hope or indication of success or of a positive outcome

Etymology

From Latin 'ominosus', from 'omen' meaning sign, especially of future events.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00195958-s
Synset POS: s
