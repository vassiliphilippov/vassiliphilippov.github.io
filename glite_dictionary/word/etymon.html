etymon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛtəˌmɑn / (AmE), / ˈɛtɪmɒn / (BrE)
a word or form from which later words are derived; the original source of a word in a particular language or in related languages
original root or source word

Examples

Latin 'pater' is the etymon of English 'father' and French 'père'.
Linguists often trace the etymon of a word to reconstruct historical language change.
The etymon reveals a connection between English 'star' and German 'Stern'.

Etymology

From Greek etymon (true sense of a word, origin), from etymos (true, real).
