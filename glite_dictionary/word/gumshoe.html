gumshoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌmˌʃu / (AmE), / ˈɡʌmˌʃuː / (BrE)
an informal term for a detective, especially one who works quietly and discreetly
informal term for detective
informal slang

Examples

The gumshoe followed the suspect through the crowded market, careful not to be spotted.
With years of experience, the gumshoe knew how to blend into the shadows.
Every good mystery novel needs a sharp gumshoe to solve the case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective - a person, especially a police officer, whose job is to investigate crimes

Etymology

Originally from the rubber-soled shoes (gumshoes) worn for noiselessness, later applied to detectives who moved stealthily.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌmˌʃu / (AmE), / ˈɡʌmˌʃuː / (BrE)
A waterproof overshoe made of rubber, worn to protect shoes from water or snow.
waterproof rubber overshoe

Examples

During the heavy rain, he put on his gumshoes to keep his feet dry.
She wiped her gumshoe on the mat before stepping inside.
In winter, gumshoes were a common sight on city streets.

Etymology

From 'gum' (rubber) and 'shoe', referring to shoes made of gum rubber.
