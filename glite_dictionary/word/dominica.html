Dominica

1. PROPN

/ ˌdɑːmɪˈniːkə / (AmE), / ˌdɒmɪˈniːkə / (BrE)
an island country in the Caribbean Sea, part of the Windward Islands, known for its volcanic landscapes and rainforests.
Caribbean island nation in the Windward Islands

Examples

Many tourists visit Dominica for its lush rainforests and hot springs.
The capital city of Dominica is Roseau, located on the island's west coast.
Hurricanes have had a significant impact on Dominica in recent years.

Etymology

From Latin dominica, meaning 'Sunday', named by Christopher Columbus after the day of his arrival.

2. PROPN

/ ˌdɑməˈniːkə / (AmE), / ˌdɒmɪˈniːkə / (BrE)
an island country in the Caribbean Sea, located southeast of Guadeloupe and northwest of Martinique, known for its natural beauty and mountainous terrain
island country in the Caribbean Sea

Examples

She spent her honeymoon in Dominica, exploring its rainforests and hot springs.
Dominica is famous for its annual Carnival festival.
The hurricane caused widespread damage across Dominica in 2017.

Etymology

From Latin 'dominica', meaning 'of the Lord (Sunday)', likely referring to the day of its European discovery.
