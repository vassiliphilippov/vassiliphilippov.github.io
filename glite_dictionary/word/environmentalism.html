environmentalism

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmɛntəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˌvʌɪrənˈmɛnt(ə)lɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The philosophical belief that environmental factors are more significant than heredity in determining intellectual development.
belief that environment shapes intellect

Examples

During the debate, she argued for environmentalism by emphasizing the impact of upbringing and social factors on intelligence.
Modern psychology often considers both heredity and environmentalism when explaining human behavior.
His research supports environmentalism, suggesting that early childhood experiences play a crucial role in cognitive development.

Etymology

From environment + -al + -ism, modeled after similar philosophical terms.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmɛntəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmɛntəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
Concern for and action aimed at protecting the environment from pollution or destruction.
protection of the environment

Examples

She has dedicated her life to environmentalism and works with several conservation groups.
The growing awareness of climate change has boosted interest in environmentalism among young people.
His passion for environmentalism inspired others to reduce waste and plant trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecology - the study of the relationships between living organisms and their environment

Etymology

Formed from 'environmental' + '-ism', dating from the late 20th century as the movement became prominent.
