titmouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪtˌmaʊs / (AmE), / ˈtɪtˌmaʊs / (BrE)
a small songbird, typically with a crested head, found in the family Paridae and known for its insect-eating habits
small insect-eating songbird

Examples

A titmouse flitted among the branches searching for insects.
The black-crested titmouse is a common sight in Texas woodlands.
We watched a titmouse feeding its young in the early morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chickadee - North American songbird of a similar family
  • Degree Higher: warbler - small insectivorous songbird, often similar in size

Etymology

From Old English *māse* (titmouse) with 'tit' added as a reference to its small size.
