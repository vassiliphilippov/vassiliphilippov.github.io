furlough

1. VERB

/ ˈfɝː.loʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɜː.ləʊ / (BrE)
to grant a temporary leave of absence, especially from duty, employment, or imprisonment
to grant temporary leave

Examples

Due to budget cuts, the company furloughed several employees for a month.
The soldier was furloughed and allowed to visit his family during the holidays.
The warden furloughed the inmate so she could attend her father's funeral.

Synset Examples

The prisoner was furloughed for the weekend to visit her children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - permission to be absent from duty or work

Etymology

From Dutch 'verlof', meaning permission or leave.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɜr.loʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɜː.ləʊ / (BrE)
a temporary leave of absence granted to a member of the armed forces
military leave of absence

Examples

After a year overseas, he was finally granted a furlough to visit his family.
The soldier used his two-week furlough to travel across the country.
Many hoped to receive a furlough during the holiday season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - permission to be absent from work or duty

Etymology

From Dutch 'verlof' meaning 'leave, permission', via earlier use in military contexts.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15164392-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈfɜrˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːləʊ / (BrE)
to temporarily lay off an employee from work, usually without pay, typically for economic reasons
temporarily lay off from work (verb)

Examples

The company had to furlough nearly half its staff due to declining sales.
During the pandemic, many employees were furloughed for several weeks.
If business does not improve, management may need to furlough additional workers.

Synset Examples

She was laid off together with hundreds of other workers when the company downsized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lay off - to dismiss employees temporarily or permanently from work
  • Antonym: fire - to dismiss from employment, typically permanently

Etymology

From Dutch *verlof* (leave, permission), via earlier English noun usage.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02408679-v
Synset POS: v
