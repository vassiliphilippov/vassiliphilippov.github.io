compeer

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɪr / (AmE), / kəmˈpɪə / (BrE)
a person of equal rank, status, or standing with another; a peer or companion
person of equal rank or status
archaic

Examples

He was welcomed as a compeer by the distinguished members of the academy.
Many artists seek the company of their compeers for inspiration and support.
She was respected among her compeers for her wisdom and leadership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peer - a person who is equal to another in abilities, qualifications, age, background, and social status

Etymology

From Old French comper, from Latin compārem (equal, companion)
