al-Jihad

1. NOUN

/ æl dʒɪˈhɑd / (AmE), / æl dʒɪˈhɑːd / (BrE)
an Islamic extremist group, active since the late 1970s, that seeks to overthrow the Egyptian government and establish an Islamic state; known for operating in underground cells
Islamic extremist group active in Egypt

Examples

al-Jihad was responsible for several high-profile attacks in Egypt during the 1980s.
Many members of al-Jihad were eventually arrested by Egyptian authorities.
al-Jihad sought to establish an Islamic state through violent means.

Synset Examples

the original Jihad was responsible for the assassination of Anwar Sadat in 1981

Etymology

From Arabic 'al' (the) + 'jihad' (struggle; holy war).
