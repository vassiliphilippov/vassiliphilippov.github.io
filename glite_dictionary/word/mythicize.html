to mythicize

1. VERB

/ ˈmɪθɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɪθɪsʌɪz / (BrE)
to make something into a myth or give it mythical qualities
make into a myth

Examples

Some writers mythicize historical events to capture the imagination of readers.
The media tend to mythicize celebrities and their personal stories.
Over time, cultures often mythicize their national heroes.

Synset Examples

The Europeans have mythicized Rte. 66

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mythologize - to create or promote a myth about something

Etymology

From myth + -icize, modeled after similar verb formations like 'romanticize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00134821-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈmɪθɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɪθɪsʌɪz / (BrE)
to interpret something as a myth or in terms of mythology; to make or treat as a myth
interpret as or make into a myth

Examples

Many historians have tried to mythicize the lives of ancient heroes.
If we mythicize the founding of the nation, we may ignore important facts.
The film tends to mythicize the struggles of its main character, turning reality into legend.

Synset Examples

mythicize the ancient stories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mythologize - to interpret or represent as a myth; to make into mythology

Etymology

From 'mythic' + '-ize' (meaning to make or become), influenced by the pattern of verbs such as 'romanticize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00625328-v
Synset POS: v
