Louis VII

1. PROPN

/ luːˈiː səˈvɛnθ / (AmE), / luːˈiː səˈvɛnθ / (BrE)
(1120–1180) King of France who led the unsuccessful Second Crusade and fought frequent wars with Henry II of England.
French king, led Second Crusade

Examples

Louis VII was a pivotal figure in the history of the Second Crusade.
The marriage of Louis VII to Eleanor of Aquitaine had a significant impact on medieval European politics.
Many French monarchs, including Louis VII, played key roles in the conflicts with England.
