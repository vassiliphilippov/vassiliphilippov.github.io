dasheen

1. NOUN

/ dəˈʃin / (AmE), / dəˈʃiːn / (BrE)
a tropical plant (Colocasia esculenta) cultivated for its large starchy tuberous root, used as a staple food in some regions; also, the root itself
tropical starchy edible root plant

Examples

The chef added boiled dasheen to the stew for extra flavor and texture.
In many Caribbean countries, people grow dasheen as a staple crop.
She learned how to peel and cook dasheen from her grandmother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taro - another name for the plant Colocasia esculenta and its root

Etymology

From West Indian English, likely an alteration of French 'de Chine' (from China).

2. NOUN

/ dəˈʃin / (AmE), / dəˈʃiːn / (BrE)
The edible, starchy tuberous root of the taro plant, often used as a vegetable in tropical regions.
edible root of taro plant

Examples

She simmered the dasheen for an hour until it became tender and ready to serve.
In many Caribbean dishes, dasheen is a common ingredient, often cooked like potatoes.
Farmers harvested fields of dasheen for sale at the local market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taro - the plant from which dasheen is obtained

Etymology

From Antillean French 'dachine', derived from West African languages.

3. NOUN

/ dəˈʃin / (AmE), / dəˈʃiːn / (BrE)
A tropical plant cultivated for its edible starchy root, also grown as an ornamental for its large glossy leaves, especially in the Pacific Islands and other tropical regions.
a tropical starchy-rooted plant; taro

Examples

The chef prepared the stew using boiled dasheen as a thickener.
In some Caribbean countries, dasheen is a staple food served with fish or meat.
She planted dasheen in her garden for its striking ornamental leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taro - a tropical Asian plant with an edible starchy root

Etymology

From West Indian English, likely derived from French Creole (dachine) or Spanish (dashín), influenced by local languages.
