Bubulcus

1. NOUN

/ buˈbʌlkəs / (AmE), / bjuːˈbʌlkəs / (BrE)
A genus of small white herons known as cattle egrets, commonly found near grazing animals.
genus of small white herons (cattle egrets)

Examples

The Bubulcus is often seen in fields alongside grazing cattle.
Researchers observed that Bubulcus populations have expanded their range significantly.
Unlike other herons, the Bubulcus readily adapts to farmland and human-altered landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egret - a type of long-legged wading bird of the heron family

Etymology

From Latin *bubulcus* (cowherd), referring to the bird's association with cattle.
