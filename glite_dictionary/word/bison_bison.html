Bison bison

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪsən ˈbaɪsən / (AmE), / ˈbaɪsən ˈbaɪsən / (BrE)
the scientific name for the American bison, a large, shaggy-haired brown mammal native to the plains of North America
scientific name of American bison

Examples

The population of Bison bison in Yellowstone National Park has increased significantly in recent decades.
Researchers observed a herd of Bison bison grazing across the open prairie.
The Bison bison once roamed across most of North America before near extinction in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American bison - large bovine mammal native to North America, also known as buffalo
  • Antonym: European bison - species closely related to the American bison but native to Europe

Etymology

From the genus and species name in New Latin, modeled after Latin 'bison' (wild ox).
