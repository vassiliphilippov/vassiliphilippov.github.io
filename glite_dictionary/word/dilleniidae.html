Dilleniidae

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌliːniˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / dɪˌliːniˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
a former subclass of flowering plants in botanical classification, including various trees, shrubs, and herbs such as those in the Ericaceae, Cruciferae, and Malvaceae families
former subclass of flowering plants

Examples

The subclass Dilleniidae was once widely used in plant classification systems.
Several familiar plant families, including Malvaceae, were formerly included in Dilleniidae.
Modern classifications no longer use Dilleniidae as a formal grouping.

Etymology

From the genus name Dillenia, which honors the German botanist Johann Jacob Dillenius, combined with the taxonomic suffix -idae.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˌlɛniˈaɪdi.i / (AmE), / dɪˌlɛniˈaɪdɪ.iː / (BrE)
A botanical subclass comprising a group of families of trees, shrubs, and herbs characterized by either polypetalous or gamopetalous corollas, often with ovules attached to the walls of the ovary; includes families such as Ericaceae, Cruciferae, and Malvaceae.
a botanical subclass of flowering plants
medical

Examples

The subclass Dilleniidae includes a wide variety of flowering plants from different climates.
Scientists have debated the classification of Dilleniidae in modern taxonomy.
Many economically important plants, such as those in the Malvaceae family, are part of Dilleniidae.

Etymology

From the genus name Dillenia, named after German botanist Johann Jacob Dillenius, plus the New Latin botanical suffix -idae.
