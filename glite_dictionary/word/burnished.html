burnished

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɝː.nɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈbɜː.nɪʃt / (BrE)
made smooth and shiny by polishing; having a bright, reflective surface
made smooth and shiny by polishing

Examples

The old piano was restored to a burnished sheen that caught the afternoon sunlight.
He admired the burnished bronze statue gleaming in the museum lobby.
The knight's armor was freshly burnished, reflecting the torches in the grand hall.

Synset Examples

bright silver candlesticks
a burnished brass knocker

rows of shining glasses
shiny black patents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shiny - reflecting light; bright or glossy in appearance
  • Antonym: dull - not bright or shiny; lacking a sheen or gloss

Etymology

From Old French 'burnir' (to polish), from Frankish or Germanic roots.
