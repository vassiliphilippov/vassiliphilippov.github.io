fraction

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈfræk.ʃən / (BrE)
a component of a mixture that has been separated by a fractional process such as distillation or crystallization
separated component of a mixture

Examples

During distillation, each fraction is collected at a different temperature.
The lighter fraction evaporates first in the refining process.
They analyzed a fraction obtained from the crude oil mixture.

Etymology

From Latin 'fractio' (a breaking), from 'frangere' (to break).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfræk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈfræk.ʃən / (BrE)
a very small part or segment of something; a piece of a whole
small part or portion

Examples

Only a fraction of the population attended the meeting.
He inherited just a fraction of the estate.
You need to add even a tiny fraction of salt for the recipe to taste right.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: portion - a part of a whole; a piece
  • Antonym: whole - something complete, entire amount

Etymology

From Latin 'fractio' (a breaking), from 'frangere' (to break).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfræk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈfræk.ʃən / (BrE)
A number that represents a part of a whole, expressed as the quotient of two numbers, where the numerator is divided by the denominator.
a part of a whole; quotient of two numbers

Examples

One half is a simple fraction used to represent fifty percent of a whole.
To compare the two recipes, write each measurement as a fraction or a decimal.
The teacher asked the students to add and subtract fractions in math class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decimal - A number expressed in the decimal system; another way to express part of a whole
  • Antonym: integer - A whole number, not a fraction

Etymology

From Latin fractio (a breaking), from frangere (to break).
