taped

1. ADJ

/ teɪpt / (AmE), / teɪpt / (BrE)
recorded or preserved on magnetic tape, especially for audio or video purposes
recorded on magnetic tape

Examples

The police released the taped conversation as evidence in the case.
We watched the taped performance of the concert on television last night.
All of the interviews were taped for later review.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recorded - captured or set down for reproduction, especially sound or video
  • Antonym: live - not recorded; performed or broadcast in real time

Etymology

From the verb 'tape' + -ed, indicating past participle/adjective use.

2. ADJ

/ teɪpt / (AmE), / teɪpt / (BrE)
secured or held in place by tape
secured with tape

Examples

The package arrived with a taped label on the top.
She handed me a taped envelope to protect the contents.
Several taped wires ran along the edge of the wall.

Synset Examples

carefully taped pieces of glass served as a windowpane

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untaped - not secured or fastened with tape
  • Synonym: tape - strip of adhesive material used to fasten things
