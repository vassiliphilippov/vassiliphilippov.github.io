whacky

1. ADJ

/ ˈwæki / (AmE), / ˈwæki / (BrE)
amusingly or crazily unconventional or eccentric; foolish or silly in a playful way
amusingly eccentric or silly

Examples

The comedian's whacky sense of humor had the audience laughing uncontrollably.
She came up with a whacky plan to decorate the office with balloons and glitter.
His whacky costume made him stand out at the Halloween party.

Synset Examples

wore a goofy hat
a silly idea
some wacky plan for selling more books

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wacky - amusingly eccentric or odd
  • Synonym: silly - lacking in seriousness or foolish
  • Antonym: serious - solemn or not playful; not silly

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'wacky', probably from 'whack' (blow or hit) + '–y'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwæki / (AmE), / ˈwæki / (BrE)
funny or peculiar, often in an odd or eccentric way; crazy or mad (informal)
eccentric or crazy (informal)
informal

Examples

The comedian's whacky sense of humor kept the audience laughing all night.
She wore a whacky hat decorated with rubber chickens to the party.
His ideas may sound whacky, but sometimes they actually work.

Synset Examples

it used to drive my husband balmy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wacky - eccentric or crazy (informal)
  • Antonym: sane - of sound mind, not mad

Etymology

Variant of 'wacky', from 'whack' (a blow or hit), with the sense of being 'hit on the head'.

Synset POS: s
