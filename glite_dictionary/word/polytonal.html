polytonal

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːliˈtoʊnəl / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈtəʊnəl / (BrE)
using two or more musical keys or tonalities at the same time
using multiple keys at once (music)

Examples

The composer is known for his use of polytonal harmonies in modern orchestral works.
Some listeners find polytonal passages challenging but musically rewarding.
Her new piece features a polytonal melody that intertwines several contrasting keys.

Synset Examples

exciting rhythms and polytonal harmonies

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bitonal - using two keys or tonalities at the same time (music)

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'tonal' (from 'tonos', tone).
