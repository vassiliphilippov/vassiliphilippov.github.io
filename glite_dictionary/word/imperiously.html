imperiously

1. ADV

/ ɪmˈpɪriəsli / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪəriəsli / (BrE)
In a commanding, domineering, or overbearing manner that shows a sense of superiority or authority.
in a domineering or commanding manner

Examples

She spoke imperiously, expecting everyone to obey her without question.
The manager waved his hand imperiously to dismiss the suggestion.
He brushed aside their concerns imperiously, showing little regard for their opinions.

Synset Examples

imperiously he cut her short

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogantly - in a manner showing a sense of superiority (synonym)
  • Antonym: humbly - in a modest or respectful manner (antonym)

Etymology

From 'imperious' + '-ly'; 'imperious' from Latin 'imperiosus' (commanding).
