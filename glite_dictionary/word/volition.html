volition

1. NOUN

/ voʊˈlɪʃən / (AmE), / vəˈlɪʃən / (BrE)
the power or act of using one's own will to make a choice or decision
the act of making a choice

Examples

She signed the contract of her own volition and was not pressured by anyone.
Leaving the company was entirely my volition after months of consideration.
He acted on his volition, not because anyone forced him to.

Synset Examples

followed my father of my own volition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: will - the mental faculty by which a person decides or acts
  • Antonym: compulsion - the action of being forced to do something

Etymology

From Latin volitio (will, wish), from volo (I will, I wish).

2. NOUN

/ vəˈlɪʃən / (AmE), / vəˈlɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the power or faculty of making conscious choices or decisions; the act of willing or choosing
the power of conscious choice or decision

Examples

She joined the committee of her own volition, without any pressure from her peers.
The defendant claimed he acted under duress, not out of his own volition.
Artists often insist on creative volition when making important decisions about their work.

Synset Examples

the exercise of their volition we construe as revolt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: will - the faculty by which a person decides or initiates action
  • Synonym: intention - an aim or plan; what one intends to do
  • Antonym: compulsion - the action or state of being forced to do something

Etymology

From Latin volitionem (will, wish), from velle (to will, wish).
