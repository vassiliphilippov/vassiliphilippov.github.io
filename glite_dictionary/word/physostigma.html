Physostigma

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪsəˈstɪɡmə / (AmE), / ˌfɪsəˈstɪɡmə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical African plants in the pea family, best known for the calabar bean, which contains potent alkaloids.
genus of tropical African plants, calabar bean
medical

Examples

The seeds of Physostigma are highly toxic and have been used medicinally in small doses.
Researchers studied the alkaloid composition of Physostigma to understand its pharmacological effects.
Physostigma is most famously associated with the calabar bean of West Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calabar bean - toxic seed of Physostigma venenosum

Etymology

From Greek physis (nature) + stigma (mark, point).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪsoʊˈstɪɡmə / (AmE), / ˌfɪsəˈstɪɡmə / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical African plants in the pea family, notable for producing calabar beans.
genus of tropical African plants (calabar beans)

Examples

The seeds of Physostigma are known for their potent alkaloids.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Physostigma extracts in the laboratory.
Physostigma is native to tropical regions of West Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calabar bean - the poisonous seed of Physostigma venenosa

Etymology

From Greek 'physa' (bladder) and 'stigma' (mark, spot), referring to features of the plant's flowers.
