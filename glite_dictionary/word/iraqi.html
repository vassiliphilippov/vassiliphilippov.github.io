Iraqi

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈrɑːki / (AmE), / ɪˈrɑːki / (BrE)
of or relating to Iraq, its people, or its culture
relating to Iraq or its people

Examples

The Iraqi government announced new policies yesterday.
Many Iraqi artists have gained international recognition for their work.
This museum features a collection of ancient Iraqi artifacts.

Synset Examples

Iraqi oil
Iraqi refugees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iraq - a country in the Middle East

Etymology

Formed from the country name Iraq + English adjectival suffix -i.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03086898-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɑːki / (AmE), / ɪˈrɑːki / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Iraq
person from Iraq

Examples

Many Iraqis have moved abroad in recent years.
The Iraqi welcomed us warmly into his home.
During the war, thousands of Iraqi civilians were displaced.

Synset Examples

the majority of Iraqi are Arab Shiite Muslims although Sunni Muslims control the government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iraki - alternative spelling of 'Iraqi', a person from Iraq

Etymology

From Iraq + -i, a suffix forming demonyms, via Arabic ʿIrāqī.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09734090-n
Synset POS: n
