orchard

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrtʃərd / (AmE), / ˈɔːtʃəd / (BrE)
a piece of land planted with fruit or nut trees, typically cultivated for commercial or domestic use
area planted with fruit or nut trees

Examples

They picked apples in the old orchard behind the farmhouse every autumn.
The orchard was in bloom, filling the air with a sweet fragrance.
He inherited the peach orchard that had been in the family for generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grove - a small group of trees with little or no undergrowth

Etymology

From Old English *orceard*, probably from Latin *hortus* (garden) and *yard* (enclosure).
