choanocyte

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈænəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / kəʊˈænəˌsaɪt / (BrE)
A flagellated cell with a collar of cytoplasm, found in sponges, that helps maintain a flow of water through the body.
flagellated collar cell in sponges
medical

Examples

Each choanocyte uses its flagellum to generate water currents inside the sponge.
The choanocytes capture food particles as water flows through the sponge's body.
Scientists studied the structure of a choanocyte under a microscope to understand its function.

Etymology

From Greek khoanē (funnel) + -cyte (cell).
