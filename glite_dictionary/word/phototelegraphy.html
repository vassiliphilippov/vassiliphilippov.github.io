phototelegraphy

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊtəˈlɛɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊtəˈlɛɡrəfi / (BrE)
an early method for transmitting facsimile images over distance using electrical signals, precursor to modern fax technology.
early technology for transmitting images at a distance

Examples

The museum has an exhibit showcasing the evolution of phototelegraphy and early fax machines.
Scientists in the early 20th century made significant advances in phototelegraphy for newspaper photo transmission.
Before email and digital scanners, phototelegraphy was used to send images across continents.

Synset Examples

Ives then turned his attention to television, which he regard as merely a speeded-up form of phototelegraphy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fax - modern method for transmitting scanned printed material over telephone lines

Etymology

From photo- (relating to light or photography) + telegraphy (long-distance transmission of messages).
