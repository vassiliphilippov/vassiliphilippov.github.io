ministerial

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɪn.əˈstɪr.i.əl / (AmE), / ˌmɪn.ɪˈstɪə.ri.əl / (BrE)
relating to a minister of religion or to the duties and office of a minister.
relating to a minister of religion

Examples

The priest fulfilled his ministerial responsibilities with compassion and dedication.
She received training to support her ministerial role in the community.
Their conversation focused on the challenges of ministerial life in a modern city.

Synset Examples

ministerial duties

Etymology

From Latin ministerialis (of a servant, minister), from minister (servant, minister).

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɪn.əˈstɪr.i.əl / (AmE), / ˌmɪnɪˈstɪəriəl / (BrE)
relating to a government minister or a ministry, especially in an official or administrative capacity.
relating to a government minister or ministry

Examples

The cabinet issued a ministerial statement regarding the new policy.
He held several ministerial positions throughout his career.
The committee has no ministerial authority to enforce the rules.

Synset Examples

ministerial decree

Related Terms

  • Antonym: executive - having the power to put plans or actions into effect
  • Antonym: legislative - relating to the making of laws

Etymology

From Latin 'ministerialis', from 'minister' (servant, attendant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02775415-a
Synset POS: a
