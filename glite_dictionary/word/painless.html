painless

1. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪnləs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪnləs / (BrE)
Not causing any physical or emotional pain; free from pain.
without pain

Examples

The new procedure is almost completely painless and requires no anesthesia.
She was relieved when the vaccination proved to be painless.
Modern dental technology has made many treatments painless for patients.

Synset Examples

painless dentistry

Related Terms

  • Antonym: painful - causing pain; not painless
  • Degree Higher: comfortable - providing physical ease and relaxation; often implying absence of pain or discomfort

Etymology

From 'pain' + suffix '-less', meaning 'without'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪnləs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪnləs / (BrE)
Not requiring much effort, difficulty, or exertion; easy or uncomplicated.
easy, not requiring effort

Examples

The software offers a painless way to back up your files automatically.
He found a painless method to negotiate the contract with minimal stress.
Making the transition was surprisingly painless for the whole team.

Synset Examples

a painless solution to the problem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: easy - achieved without great effort; presenting few difficulties
  • Synonym: effortless - requiring no physical or mental exertion
  • Antonym: difficult - needing much effort or skill to accomplish

Etymology

From pain + -less, meaning 'without pain' and later extended to 'without difficulty'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00754316-s
Synset POS: s
