incontestible

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkənˈtɛstɪbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈtɛstɪbəl / (BrE)
impossible to dispute or question; incapable of being contested
impossible to dispute

Examples

The evidence presented in court was incontestible, leaving the jury with little doubt.
Her victory in the race was incontestible due to her overwhelming lead.
He won the award for his incontestible achievements in science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incontestable - impossible to dispute or question; incapable of being contested
  • Antonym: disputable - open to question or debate; not certain

Etymology

From Latin 'incontestabilis' (not to be contested), based on 'contestari' (to call to witness).
