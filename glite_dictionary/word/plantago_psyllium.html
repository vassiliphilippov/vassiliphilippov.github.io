Plantago psyllium

1. NOUN

/ plænˈteɪɡoʊ sɪˈliːəm / (AmE), / planˈteɪɡəʊ sɪˈliːəm / (BrE)
A Mediterranean plantain whose seeds swell when moist and are used as a mild laxative.
plant yielding laxative seeds
medical

Examples

The seeds of Plantago psyllium are often used as a natural laxative in health supplements.
Researchers are studying the potential cholesterol-lowering effects of Plantago psyllium.
Her doctor recommended Plantago psyllium to improve her digestive health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psyllium - the seeds of Plantago species used as a laxative

Etymology

From Latin 'Plantago' (the genus name) and 'psyllium,' from Greek 'psylla' (flea), referring to the seed's resemblance to fleas.
