WordNet

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɜrdˌnɛt / (AmE), / ˈwɜːdˌnɛt / (BrE)
a large machine-readable lexical database of English, organized by meanings, and developed at Princeton University
machine-readable lexical database of English

Examples

Many natural language processing applications use WordNet to improve their understanding of word meanings.
The research paper describes how lexical relations are organized in WordNet.
Students in computational linguistics frequently consult WordNet for synonym and antonym information.

Etymology

Blend of 'word' and 'net,' introduced as the project name at Princeton University in the 1980s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06651729-n
Synset POS: n

2. PROPN

/ ˈwɜrdˌnɛt / (AmE), / ˈwɜːd.nɛt / (BrE)
a machine-readable lexical database of English, originally developed at Princeton University, used in linguistics and computational applications
lexical database of English

Examples

Many researchers use WordNet as a resource for natural language processing tasks.
The WordNet database links words by their semantic relationships such as synonyms and antonyms.
Several languages now have their own versions modeled after the original WordNet.

Etymology

Blend of 'word' and 'net', indicating a network of words. Developed at Princeton University in the 1980s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06652077-n
Synset POS: n
