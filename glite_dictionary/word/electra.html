Electra

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈlɛktrə / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the daughter of Agamemnon and Clytemnestra who, with her brother Orestes, avenged her father's murder by helping kill her mother and her mother's lover.
daughter of Agamemnon in Greek mythology

Examples

The legend of Electra has inspired many plays and operas throughout history.
In Greek tragedy, Electra is portrayed as a determined and loyal daughter.
Many scholars analyze the motives behind Electra's actions in classical literature.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἠλέκτρα (Ēléktra), meaning 'amber' or 'shining one'.
