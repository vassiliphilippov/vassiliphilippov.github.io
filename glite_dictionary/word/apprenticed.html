apprenticed

1. ADJ

/ əˈprɛntɪst / (AmE), / əˈprɛntɪst / (BrE)
bound by a formal contract to work as an apprentice to someone in order to learn a trade or profession
bound as an apprentice by contract

Examples

She was apprenticed to a master carpenter for three years.
Many young men became apprenticed blacksmiths in small towns during the 19th century.
At the age of fourteen, he was apprenticed to a printer in London.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apprentice - a person learning a trade by being employed under a skilled worker

Etymology

From 'apprentice' (noun, from Old French 'aprentis'), plus past participle suffix '-ed'.
