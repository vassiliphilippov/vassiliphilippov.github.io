Basil of Caesarea

1. PROPN

/ ˈbæzəl əv siˈzɛriə / (AmE), / ˈbæsɪl ɒv siˈzɪəriə / (BrE)
A 4th-century Christian bishop and theologian, regarded as a saint and Doctor of the Church, who defended orthodox doctrine against heresies.
4th-century Christian saint and theologian
american

Examples

Many theologians consider Basil of Caesarea to be a central figure in the development of Christian doctrine.
The writings of Basil of Caesarea are still studied by students of early Christianity.
In 379, Basil of Caesarea passed away, leaving a lasting legacy in the Eastern Orthodox Church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Basil - Alternative name for Basil of Caesarea, 4th-century theologian and bishop
  • Synonym: Athanasius - 4th-century bishop and defender of Christian orthodoxy

Etymology

Basil from Greek Basileios; Caesarea refers to the ancient city in Cappadocia (now in modern Turkey).
