fisticuffs

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪstɪˌkʌfs / (AmE), / ˈfɪstɪˌkʌfs / (BrE)
fighting with the fists, especially as a physical altercation without weapons.
fighting with the fists

Examples

A heated argument between the two men soon turned into fisticuffs outside the bar.
The referee quickly intervened when the players' disagreement escalated to fisticuffs.
What started as a verbal disagreement ended in an undignified display of fisticuffs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxing - a sport or activity involving fighting with fists, usually with gloves
  • Synonym: brawl - a rough or noisy fight, often involving several people

Etymology

From English 'fist' + 'cuff' (blow with the fist), first used in the mid-17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪstɪˌkʌfs / (AmE), / ˈfɪstɪkʌfs / (BrE)
Fighting with bare fists, especially as a physical altercation.
fight with bare fists

Examples

A heated argument between the two men quickly escalated to fisticuffs in the parking lot.
The school principal intervened before the boys' disagreement turned into fisticuffs.
The movie's action scene featured plenty of dramatic fisticuffs between the hero and the villain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brawl - a noisy fight, often in a public place
  • Degree Higher: boxing - a sport where two people fight with their fists wearing gloves
  • Antonym: wrestling - a sport involving physical grappling and holds, not limited to striking

Etymology

From 'fisticuff', a blend of 'fist' and 'cuff' (slap), with plural form developing in the 18th century.
