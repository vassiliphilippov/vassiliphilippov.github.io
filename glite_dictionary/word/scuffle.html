scuffle

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌf.əl / (AmE), / ˈskʌf.əl / (BrE)
a kind of hoe designed to be pushed to loosen or weed soil, rather than pulled
a hoe operated by pushing

Examples

The gardener used a scuffle to remove the weeds between the rows of vegetables.
When the soil is loose, a scuffle makes weeding quick and easy.
Unlike traditional hoes, the scuffle is meant to be pushed through the soil.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskʌf.əl / (AmE), / ˈskʌf.əl / (BrE)
a short, confused, and disorderly fight or struggle, usually not serious or planned
disorderly physical struggle

Examples

There was a brief scuffle outside the stadium before the police arrived.
A scuffle broke out between the two boys over the last slice of pizza.
She lost her keys during a scuffle with the mugger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight - a violent confrontation between people or groups
  • Synonym: brawl - a rough or noisy fight, usually involving several people

Etymology

Perhaps from Low German *schuffeln* (to shuffle), related to English 'shuffle'.

3. VERB

/ ˈskʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈskʌfəl / (BrE)
to fight or struggle in a confused or disorderly way, usually at close quarters and without weapons
to engage in a confused close fight

Examples

Two students began to scuffle in the hallway before a teacher intervened.
Security guards had to separate fans who scuffled after the match.
Children sometimes scuffle on the playground over toys or games.

Synset Examples

the drunken men started to scuffle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brawl - a noisy, rough fight, especially in a crowd (verb)
  • Synonym: skirmish - to engage in a brief or minor fight (verb)
  • Antonym: peace - a state of calm or lack of conflict

Etymology

Probably from Dutch *schuffelen* (to shuffle, skirmish), related to English 'shuffle'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈskʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈskʌf(ə)l / (BrE)
a short, confused fight or struggle, often not very serious or violent
a brief, disorderly fight

Examples

A scuffle broke out between the protesters and the police outside the courthouse.
The boys got into a brief scuffle on the playground but quickly made up.
She hurt her knee during the scuffle and had to see the nurse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight - a violent struggle between people
  • Synonym: brawl - a noisy and rough fight, often in public
  • Synonym: melee - a confused, noisy fight involving many people

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Swedish skuffa (to push).
