Holmes

1. PROPN

/ hoʊmz / (AmE), / həʊmz / (BrE)
a fictional detective created by Sir Arthur Conan Doyle, known for his keen observation and logical reasoning.
famous fictional detective by A. Conan Doyle

Examples

Many readers admire Holmes for his remarkable intellect and deductive skills.
In the story, Holmes and his friend Dr. Watson investigate a mysterious disappearance.
Modern detectives are often compared to Holmes in terms of their analytical abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sherlock Holmes - the full name of the fictional detective created by A. Conan Doyle

Etymology

Named after the character Sherlock Holmes, first introduced by Arthur Conan Doyle in 1887.
