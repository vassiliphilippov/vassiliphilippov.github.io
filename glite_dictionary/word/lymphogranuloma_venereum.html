lymphogranuloma venereum

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪm.foʊ.ɡræˈnjuː.loʊ.ma vəˈnɛri.əm / (AmE), / ˌlɪm.fəʊ.ɡræˈnjuː.lə.mə vɪˈnɪəri.əm / (BrE)
a sexually transmitted infectious disease caused by Chlamydia trachomatis, characterized by genital ulcers and swelling of lymph nodes in the groin
sexually transmitted infectious disease
medical

Examples

Many cases of lymphogranuloma venereum are initially misdiagnosed due to its rare occurrence in some regions.
The patient presented with swollen groin lymph nodes, raising suspicion for lymphogranuloma venereum.
Early treatment is crucial to prevent complications from lymphogranuloma venereum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlamydia - a common sexually transmitted infection caused by Chlamydia bacteria

Etymology

From Greek 'lympha' (lymph), 'granuloma' (granular tumor), and Latin 'venereum' (of sexual love/venereal).
