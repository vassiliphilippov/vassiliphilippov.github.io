legitimate

1. ADJ

/ lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət / (BrE)
Conforming to recognized or accepted standards or principles; lawful or acceptable.
conforming to accepted standards; lawful

Examples

The company follows only legitimate business practices to maintain its reputation.
He raised legitimate concerns about the safety of the building.
Advertising to children can be ethical if it uses legitimate methods.

Synset Examples

legitimate advertising practices

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illegal - not allowed by law or by accepted rules
  • Synonym: lawful - allowed or recognized by law

Etymology

From Latin 'legitimus' (lawful, in accordance with the law), from 'lex' (law).
