pronoun

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊˌnaʊn / (AmE), / ˈprəʊnaʊn / (BrE)
A word used instead of a noun or noun phrase to avoid repetition, such as 'he', 'she', 'it', or 'they'.
word replacing a noun

Examples

The pronoun 'they' is often used to refer to a group of people.
Each pronoun must agree with its antecedent in number and gender.
When replacing 'Sarah', use the pronoun 'she' or 'her'.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noun - a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea

Etymology

From Latin 'pronomen', from 'pro' (for) + 'nomen' (name).
