nasality

1. NOUN

/ nəˈzæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / nəˈzæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of a voice that is characterized by the sound being produced through the nose, often resulting in a nasal tone.
quality of a voice with nasal resonance

Examples

The singer's nasality made her voice easily recognizable.
Some people perceive nasality as a flaw in speech, while others find it distinctive.
A common symptom of a cold is an increase in nasality when speaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasal - relating to the nose; especially, having a nasal sound

Etymology

From Latin nasalis (of the nose) + -ity.
