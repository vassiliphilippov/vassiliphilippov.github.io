Atticus

1. NOUN

/ ˈætɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈætɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of large moths in the family Saturniidae, commonly referred to as atlas moths.
genus of large moths (atlas moths)

Examples

The Atticus genus includes some of the largest moths in the world.
Researchers studied the wing patterns of Atticus species in Southeast Asia.
The striking colors of Atticus moths make them popular among collectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atlas moth - large moth native to Asia, known for its impressive wingspan
