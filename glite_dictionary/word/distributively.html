distributively

1. ADV

/ dɪˈstrɪb.ju.tɪv.li / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɪb.jʊ.tɪv.li / (BrE)
In a way that involves distribution or applies to each member of a group separately.
in a distributive manner; separately to parts

Examples

The rewards were shared distributively among all team members.
In mathematics, the operation applies distributively to each element of the set.
The force acts distributively along the entire length of the beam.

Synset Examples

marine vertebrates have their weight supported distributively by the water

Related Terms

  • Antonym: collectively - in a collective manner; as a group

Etymology

From 'distributive' + '-ly'. 'Distributive' from Latin 'distributivus'.

Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ dɪˈstrɪbjʊtɪvli / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɪbjʊtɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that considers individuals or items separately, rather than as a whole.
as separate individuals or units

Examples

The law applies distributively to each member of the group.
Each item should be treated distributively rather than combined.
Consider the rights of the parties distributively, not as a collective entity.

Synset Examples

taken distributively, their rights are imperceptible

Related Terms

  • Antonym: collectively - in a way that is done by people or things as a group

Etymology

From Latin distributivus (dividing, distributing) + -ly.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00321630-r
Synset POS: r
