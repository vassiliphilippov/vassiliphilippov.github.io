quamash

1. NOUN

/ kwəˈmæʃ / (AmE), / kwəˈmæʃ / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Camassia, native to North and South America, known for its edible bulbs and blue flowers
plant of the genus Camassia

Examples

Native Americans traditionally harvested quamash bulbs as an important food source.
In late spring, fields of quamash bloom with beautiful blue flowers.
The park guide pointed out the rare stand of quamash near the wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camass - another name for the same plant, especially Camassia quamash

Etymology

From Nez Percé *qém’es*, via American English, referring to Camassia species.
