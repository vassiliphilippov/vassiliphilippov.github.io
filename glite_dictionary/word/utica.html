Utica

1. PROPN

/ ˈjuː.tɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈjuː.tɪ.kə / (BrE)
an ancient city on the north coast of Africa, northwest of Carthage, which was destroyed by the Arabs around 700 AD.
ancient city in North Africa

Examples

Archaeological excavations have uncovered ruins of Utica dating back to Phoenician times.
Utica played a significant role in Roman Africa before its destruction.
The downfall of Utica marked the end of one of the great ancient cities in the region.

2. PROPN

/ ˈjuː.tɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈjuː.tɪ.kə / (BrE)
A city in central New York, United States, serving as the county seat of Oneida County.
city in New York State, USA

Examples

After graduating, she decided to move to Utica to start her new job.
The weather in Utica can be quite unpredictable during the winter months.
My grandparents have lived in Utica for more than fifty years.

Etymology

Named after the ancient city of Utica in present-day Tunisia.
