moveable

1. ADJ

/ ˈmuːvəbəl / (AmE), / ˈmuːvəbl / (BrE)
able to be moved or transferred from one place to another; not fixed in one location.
able to be moved; not fixed

Examples

The moveable stage allowed performers to change scenes quickly during the show.
All the moveable furniture was pushed against the walls for the party.
The museum's collection includes both fixed and moveable exhibits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immovable - not able to be moved; fixed in place
  • Synonym: portable - able to be easily carried or moved

Etymology

From Old French *movable*, from Medieval Latin *movibilis*, from Latin *movere* (to move).
