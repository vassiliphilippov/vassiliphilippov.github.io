bitchery

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtʃɚi / (AmE), / ˈbɪtʃəri / (BrE)
spiteful or malicious remarks and behavior, especially in a petty or aggressive way
spiteful or malicious behavior
informal slang

Examples

She was tired of all the bitchery that happened in the office every day.
The film exposes the subtle bitchery among high school students.
His comments were pure bitchery and upset everyone in the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malice - the intention or desire to do evil; ill will
  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate

Etymology

Formed from 'bitch' (slang for a spiteful woman) with the suffix '-ery' denoting conduct or behavior.
