Kendal

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛn.dəl / (AmE), / ˈkɛn.dəl / (BrE)
a green dye obtained from the woad plant, often used to color cloth
green dye from the woad plant

Examples

The traditional cloak was dyed with Kendal to achieve its deep green color.
Historically, Kendal was favored for coloring soldiers' uniforms in England.
Weavers used Kendal to produce distinctive textiles for local markets.

Etymology

From Kendal, a town in northwest England once noted for its dyed cloth.
