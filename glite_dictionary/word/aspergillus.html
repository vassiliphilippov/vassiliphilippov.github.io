Aspergillus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæspɚˈdʒɪləs / (AmE), / ˌaspəˈdʒɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of common molds, some species of which cause food spoilage and can be pathogenic to plants and animals.
genus of common molds
medical

Examples

Aspergillus species are often found growing on damp walls and decaying food.
Some strains of Aspergillus can produce dangerous mycotoxins in stored grain.
The infection was caused by inhaling spores of Aspergillus.

Etymology

From Latin aspergillum (holy water sprinkler), referring to the resemblance of the spore-bearing structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæspərˈdʒɪləs / (AmE), / ˌaspəˈdʒɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of common molds that cause food spoilage and can be pathogenic to plants and animals.
genus of molds (fungi)
medical

Examples

The laboratory confirmed the presence of Aspergillus in the contaminated peanut butter.
Some species of Aspergillus are used in the production of soy sauce and sake.
Exposure to Aspergillus spores can cause respiratory problems in immunocompromised patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Penicillium - another genus of common molds with medical and industrial importance

Etymology

From Latin, diminutive of 'asper', meaning 'rough', referring to the rough appearance of the fungal spores; coined in 1729 by Italian priest-biologist Pier Antonio Micheli.
