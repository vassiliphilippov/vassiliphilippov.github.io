determinant

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɝmɪnənt / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːmɪnənt / (BrE)
the specific region on the surface of an antigen molecule to which an antibody binds
site on antigen where antibody binds
medical

Examples

The antibody recognizes a unique determinant on the virus's surface.
Researchers mapped several determinants involved in the immune response to the protein.
Each determinant on the antigen can elicit a specific reaction from the immune cells.

Etymology

From Latin determinare 'to limit, to fix'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15062231-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɝːmɪnənt / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːmɪnənt / (BrE)
a factor or element that decisively affects the nature or outcome of something
decisive causal factor

Examples

Access to healthcare is a key determinant of overall well-being in many communities.
Social background remains a significant determinant of career opportunities in many countries.
The weather can act as an important determinant in agricultural success.

Synset Examples

education is an important determinant of one's outlook on life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factor - something that contributes to a result

Etymology

From Latin determinans, determinantis (determining), from determinare (to determine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05700384-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈtɝː.mə.nənt / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜː.mɪ.nənt / (BrE)
(formal) having the power or quality to decide something; serving to determine the final outcome or result.
deciding or determining factor

Examples

Her determinant role in the negotiations secured the final agreement.
The determinant factor in the election was the turnout of young voters.
A determinant issue in the case was the reliability of the witness.

Synset Examples

cast the deciding vote
the determinative (or determinant) battle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decisive - having the power to decide or determine an outcome
  • Synonym: determinative - having the power to determine or decide; decisive

Etymology

From Latin determinans, present participle of determinare (to determine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00688059-s
Synset POS: s

4. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɝː.mɪ.nənt / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜː.mɪ.nənt / (BrE)
A square matrix whose numerical value can be computed and is used especially in solving systems of simultaneous linear equations.
matrix used in solving equations

Examples

The value of the determinant helps determine whether a system of linear equations has a unique solution.
Calculating the determinant of a matrix is a fundamental step in linear algebra.
If the determinant equals zero, the matrix is singular and the equations may not have a unique solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matrix - rectangular array of numbers or elements in mathematics

Etymology

From Latin determinare (to set bounds, limit) via French déterminant.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08286317-n
Synset POS: n
