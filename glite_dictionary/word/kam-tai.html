Kam-Tai

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæm ˈtaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkam ˈtʌɪ / (BrE)
a family of Sino-Tibetan languages spoken in southeastern Asia, including Kam, Tai, and related languages
language family of Southeast Asia

Examples

Linguists have identified the Kam-Tai languages as a major branch within the Sino-Tibetan family.
The Kam-Tai family includes languages spoken in southern China, Laos, Vietnam, and Thailand.
Research on the Kam-Tai languages has provided insights into the history of Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tai-Kadai - a broader language family classification including Kam-Tai and Kadai languages

Etymology

From the names of two member groups: Kam (an ethnic group in southern China) and Tai (a group of related peoples in Southeast Asia).
