patchcord

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætʃˌkɔrd / (AmE), / ˈpætʃˌkɔːd / (BrE)
a length of cable with a plug at each end, used to connect components in electronic or audio equipment
cable with plugs for electronic connections

Examples

Be sure to connect the speakers using a patchcord to ensure proper sound quality.
The technician grabbed a patchcord from the rack to link the two devices.
Without a patchcord, you won't be able to reroute the audio signal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patch cable - a cable with connectors at both ends, used for signal routing

Etymology

From 'patch' + 'cord', originally referring to connecting circuits on a patchboard.
