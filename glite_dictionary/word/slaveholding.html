slaveholding

1. NOUN

/ ˈsleɪvˌhoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsleɪvhəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice or act of owning and keeping slaves
the practice of owning slaves

Examples

The southern states were known for their history of slaveholding before the Civil War.
Debates over slaveholding shaped the politics of nineteenth-century America.
His ancestors were part of a slaveholding family in the Caribbean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slavery - the system or state of being a slave

Etymology

Formed from 'slave' + 'holding', patterning after terms like 'landholding'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈsleɪvˌhoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsleɪvhəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person, institution, or society) allowing or practicing slavery; possessing enslaved people.
allowing or practicing slavery

Examples

Many Southern states were considered slaveholding regions before the American Civil War.
The slaveholding plantation owners resisted any changes to the status quo.
He came from a prominent slaveholding family in the nineteenth century.

Synset Examples

the slaveholding South

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abolitionist - person who opposes slavery

Etymology

Formed from 'slave' + 'holding' (gerund of 'hold'), referring to the act of keeping slaves.

WordNet ID: oewn-01070130-s
Synset POS: s
