Faisal ibn Abdel Aziz al-Saud

1. PROPN

/ faɪˈsɑl ɪbˈn æbˈdɛl æˈziːz æl saʊd / (AmE), / faɪˈsɑːl ɪbˈn æbˈdɛl ælˈziːz æl ˈsaʊd / (BrE)
(1906–1975) King of Saudi Arabia from 1964 to 1975, known for modernizing reforms and for his role in the 1973 oil embargo.
King of Saudi Arabia (1964–1975)

Examples

During his reign, Faisal ibn Abdel Aziz al-Saud implemented a series of modernization policies in Saudi Arabia.
Faisal ibn Abdel Aziz al-Saud was instrumental in organizing the 1973 oil embargo.
Historians often cite Faisal ibn Abdel Aziz al-Saud as a key figure in the transformation of Saudi society.
