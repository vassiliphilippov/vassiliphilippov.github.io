Hypericum perforatum

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɪrɪkəm pərfəˈreɪtəm / (AmE), / hɪˌpɛrɪkəm pɜːfəˈreɪtəm / (BrE)
a yellow-flowered perennial plant, commonly known as St John's wort, often found in meadows and waste places, regarded as a weed in some areas
yellow-flowered perennial (St John's wort)

Examples

Hypericum perforatum is widely used in herbal medicine as a remedy for mild depression.
Ranchers consider Hypericum perforatum to be a troublesome weed in grazing lands.
The bright yellow flowers of Hypericum perforatum appear throughout the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St John's wort - common name for Hypericum perforatum, a yellow-flowered medicinal herb

Etymology

From Latin; 'Hypericum' is the genus name and 'perforatum' means 'perforated,' referring to the tiny translucent dots on the leaves.
