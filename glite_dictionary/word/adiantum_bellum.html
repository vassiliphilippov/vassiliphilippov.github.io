Adiantum bellum

1. NOUN

/ ˌædiˈæntəm ˈbɛləm / (AmE), / ˌædiˈæntəm ˈbɛləm / (BrE)
A species of delicate fern endemic to Bermuda, characterized by its creeping rootstock.
Bermudian creeping fern species

Examples

The Adiantum bellum can be found only on the islands of Bermuda.
Gardeners appreciate Adiantum bellum for its attractive, feathery foliage.
Researchers are studying how Adiantum bellum adapts to its unique environment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Adiantum - genus of ferns including the maidenhair ferns

Etymology

From the genus name Adiantum (from Greek adiantos, meaning 'unwetted') and the Latin bellum, meaning 'beautiful.'
