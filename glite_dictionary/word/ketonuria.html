ketonuria

1. NOUN

/ ˌkiːtoʊˈnjʊriə / (AmE), / ˌkiːtəˈnjʊəriə / (BrE)
the presence of an abnormally large amount of ketone bodies in the urine, typically associated with diabetes mellitus or starvation
excess ketone bodies in urine
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed ketonuria after testing her urine for ketone bodies.
Untreated diabetes can often lead to ketonuria and other metabolic complications.
Starvation or a low-carbohydrate diet may cause ketonuria to develop in some patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetonuria - the presence of acetone in the urine

Etymology

From Greek 'keton' (acetone, ketone body) + 'ouria' (urine condition).
