Silybum marianum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪlɪbəm məˈriːənəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪbəm məˈrɪənəm / (BrE)
a tall biennial thistle native to the Mediterranean, with large white-blotched leaves and purple flower heads, commonly known as milk thistle
species of thistle (milk thistle)

Examples

Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Silybum marianum in the treatment of liver disorders.
The fields were dotted with the striking purple flowers of Silybum marianum.
Many herbal supplements contain extracts of Silybum marianum, more commonly known as milk thistle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milk thistle - common name for Silybum marianum, a medicinal plant

Etymology

From Latin Silybum (a thistle) and Marianum (of Mary), referring to the Virgin Mary in Christian tradition.
