builder

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪldər / (AmE), / ˈbɪldə / (BrE)
a substance added to soaps or detergents to improve or enhance their cleaning effectiveness
additive in detergents to boost cleaning

Examples

Phosphates are commonly used as a builder in many laundry detergents.
The addition of a builder helps to soften hard water and enhance cleaning.
Manufacturers are seeking more environmentally friendly builder alternatives for their products.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪl.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɪl.də / (BrE)
a person or company that contracts for, organizes, and supervises the construction of buildings
person or company that builds structures

Examples

The builder inspected the site before beginning the construction project.
She hired a reliable builder to supervise the renovation of her house.
Several builders submitted bids for constructing the new library.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contractor - a person or company that undertakes a contract to provide materials or labor to perform a service or do a job
  • Antonym: architect - a person who designs buildings and in many cases also supervises their construction

Etymology

From Old English byldan (to build), ultimately from Proto-Germanic *bautaną (to build).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɪldəɹ / (AmE), / ˈbɪldə / (BrE)
a person who creates or develops a business, organization, or country, especially by organizing people and resources effectively
person who develops a business or nation

Examples

As an builder of successful companies, she is well respected in the business world.
The president was seen as a nation builder who modernized the country.
His reputation as an empire builder grew with each ambitious project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: founder - person who establishes an organization or business
