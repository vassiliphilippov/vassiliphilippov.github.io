tortured

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɔrtʃɚd / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtʃəd / (BrE)
experiencing intense pain, especially emotional or psychological anguish; deeply distressed.
experiencing intense pain or distress

Examples

She gave a tortured explanation that revealed her inner turmoil.
The artist’s tortured mind was reflected in his haunting paintings.
He wrote in a tortured style, expressing the pain he felt throughout his life.

Synset Examples

an anguished conscience
a small tormented schoolboy
a tortured witness to another's humiliation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anguished - experiencing severe mental or physical pain
  • Synonym: tormented - experiencing persistent physical or mental suffering
  • Antonym: calm - not experiencing distress or pain

Etymology

From the verb 'torture', from Latin 'torquere' (to twist).
