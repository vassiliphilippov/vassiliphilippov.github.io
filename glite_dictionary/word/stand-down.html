stand-down

1. NOUN

/ stænd ˌdaʊn / (AmE), / stænd ˌdaʊn / (BrE)
a temporary suspension of military or operational activity, especially in a combat situation
temporary cessation of military activity

Examples

After the intense fighting, the troops were ordered to stand-down for 48 hours.
A stand-down allows soldiers to rest and regroup before further action is taken.
The general called for a stand-down to open negotiations with the opposing side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceasefire - a temporary suspension of fighting; a truce

2. NOUN

/ stænd daʊn / (AmE), / stænd daʊn / (BrE)
a period during which active operations or a state of alert are suspended or relaxed; a time when forces are instructed to stand down
temporary suspension of readiness or alertness

Examples

After receiving the official announcement, the troops welcomed the stand-down with relief.
The company called for a stand-down after the threat was confirmed to be a false alarm.
Following the emergency drill, all personnel were given permission for a brief stand-down period.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alert - a state of heightened readiness or watchfulness
  • Degree Higher: standby - a state of readiness to act
