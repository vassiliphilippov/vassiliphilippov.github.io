girlfriend

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɜrlˌfrɛnd / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːlˌfrɛnd / (BrE)
a girl or young woman with whom someone is romantically involved
female romantic partner

Examples

Mark introduced his girlfriend to his family at the party.
His girlfriend surprised him with tickets to the concert for his birthday.
After their argument, his girlfriend needed some time alone to think.

Synset Examples

his girlfriend kicked him out

Related Terms

  • Antonym: boyfriend - a boy or young man with whom someone is romantically involved

Etymology

Formed from 'girl' and 'friend' in early 20th century English, modeled after 'boyfriend.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɝːlˌfrɛnd / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːlˌfrɛnd / (BrE)
A female friend, especially one with whom a woman has a close or platonic relationship.
female friend (platonic sense)

Examples

She went shopping with her girlfriend on Saturday afternoon.
After years apart, the two girlfriends reunited over lunch.
Emma considers Sarah her oldest girlfriend from college.

Synset Examples

Mary and her girlfriend organized the party

Etymology

Formed from 'girl' + 'friend'; originally used for female friendship before acquiring the romantic sense.
