middlewoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdəlˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈmɪdl̩ˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who acts as an intermediary, typically helping to arrange or facilitate transactions or connections between parties
female intermediary or go-between

Examples

As the middlewoman, she negotiated the terms between the artist and the gallery.
He relied on a trusted middlewoman to introduce him to influential clients.
The middlewoman ensured that communication between the buyer and seller was clear and efficient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: middleman - a person who acts as an intermediary, especially in business or trade

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'middleman', substituting 'woman' for 'man'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdəlˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈmɪdəlˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a female performer in the middle of a minstrel line who acts as a principal speaker, engaging with the other performers in dialogue
female lead in a minstrel line

Examples

The middlewoman kept the audience entertained with her witty remarks during the performance.
Traditionally, the middlewoman would initiate most of the banter on stage.
She was chosen to be the middlewoman because of her quick thinking and strong stage presence.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdəlˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈmɪdəlˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who buys goods in large quantities and resells them to merchants rather than to final consumers; a female wholesaler
female wholesaler; woman intermediary

Examples

As a successful middlewoman, she connected farmers with urban retailers across the region.
The company hired a middlewoman to handle distribution between manufacturers and local shops.
Many middlewomen play a vital role in agricultural supply chains in developing countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: middleman - a person who buys large quantities of goods and resells to merchants rather than to the ultimate customers

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'middleman', from 'middle' + 'woman'.
