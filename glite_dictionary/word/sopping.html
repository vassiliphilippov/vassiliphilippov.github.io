sopping

1. ADV

/ ˈsɑpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɒpɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely wet; thoroughly soaked with liquid.
extremely wet, soaked

Examples

After the heavy rain, my clothes were sopping and uncomfortable to wear.
She stepped inside with sopping shoes after walking through the puddles.
The towels were sopping after cleaning up the spilled water.

Synset Examples

dripping wet
soaking wet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soaking - very wet; saturated with liquid
  • Synonym: drenched - thoroughly wet; soaked
  • Antonym: dry - free from moisture or liquid; not wet

Etymology

From 'sop' (to soak up liquid), origin Middle English, from Old English 'soppan'.
