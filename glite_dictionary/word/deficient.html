deficient

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfɪʃənt / (AmE), / dɪˈfɪʃənt / (BrE)
not having enough of something essential or necessary; inadequate in amount or degree.
lacking or inadequate

Examples

The report found the city's infrastructure to be deficient in several critical areas.
If your diet is deficient in vitamin C, you may become ill.
His explanation was deficient and left everyone more confused than before.

Synset Examples

a deficient education
deficient in common sense
lacking in stamina
tested and found wanting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insufficient - not enough; inadequate
  • Antonym: adequate - sufficient or acceptable in quality or quantity

Etymology

From Latin 'deficiens', present participle of 'deficere' (to fail, to be lacking).

Examples

The diet was deficient in vitamin D, leading to health problems.

Related Terms
