Xenarthra

1. NOUN

/ zɛnˈɑrθrə / (AmE), / zɛnˈɑːθrə / (BrE)
an order of placental mammals native to the Americas, comprising armadillos, anteaters, and sloths
order of mammals including armadillos, anteaters, and sloths

Examples

The order Xenarthra includes armadillos, anteaters, and sloths native to Central and South America.
Recent studies have provided insight into the evolutionary history of Xenarthra.
Many unique skeletal features distinguish Xenarthra from other placental mammals.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'xenos' (strange) + 'arthron' (joint), referring to their unique vertebral joints.
