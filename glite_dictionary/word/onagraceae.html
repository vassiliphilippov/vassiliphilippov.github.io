Onagraceae

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌnæɡrəˈsiː / (AmE), / əʊˌnæɡrəˈsiː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, commonly known as the evening primrose family, found in temperate and subtropical regions worldwide
family of flowering plants (evening primroses)

Examples

The family Onagraceae includes species such as evening primrose and fuchsia.
Botanists have studied the distribution of Onagraceae across different continents.
Many ornamental plants in gardens belong to the Onagraceae family.

Etymology

From New Latin *Onagraceae*, based on the type genus *Onagra* (an obsolete name for evening primrose).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːnəˈɡreɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌəʊnəˈɡreɪsiː / (BrE)
a large and widely distributed family of flowering plants in the order Myrtales, including evening primroses and related species
family of flowering plants (order Myrtales)
medical

Examples

The Onagraceae family includes many familiar wildflowers such as the evening primrose.
Members of the Onagraceae are found worldwide, especially in temperate and subtropical regions.
Botanists study the Onagraceae to better understand plant evolution within the Myrtales order.

Etymology

From New Latin Onagraceae, based on the type genus Onagra (evening primrose).
