roquette

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈkɛt / (AmE), / rɒˈkɛt / (BrE)
an edible annual Mediterranean plant (Eruca vesicaria), with pungent leaves often used in salads; also called arugula or rocket
edible leafy plant used in salads

Examples

We sprinkled fresh roquette leaves over the pizza just before serving.
The chef recommended a salad of roquette, Parmesan, and pine nuts.
At the farmer's market, bunches of roquette were on sale alongside other leafy greens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arugula - another name for roquette, especially in American English
  • Synonym: rocket - British English term for roquette

Etymology

From French 'roquette', from Italian 'ruchetta', diminutive of 'ruca', from Latin 'eruca' (a type of cabbage).
