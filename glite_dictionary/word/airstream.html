airstream

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌstriːm / (AmE), / ˈeəˌstriːm / (BrE)
the flow of air that moves rearward as a result of an aircraft propeller or jet engine
airflow generated by a propeller or engine

Examples

The powerful airstream created by the propeller buffeted the ground crew as they loaded the cargo.
Birds sometimes get caught in the airstream behind an aircraft during takeoff.
Engineers measured the airstream to ensure optimal thrust and efficiency for the small plane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airflow - the movement of air, especially around or through an object

Etymology

From 'air' + 'stream'; recorded in use since the early 20th century with the development of powered flight.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌstriːm / (AmE), / ˈeəstriːm / (BrE)
a current or flow of air, especially a relatively well-defined prevailing wind
current or flow of air

Examples

Strong airstreams swept across the plains, carrying clouds for hundreds of miles.
Pilots need to carefully monitor the prevailing airstream when plotting their courses.
A sudden change in the airstream direction brought cooler weather to the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wind - moving air, especially natural movement outdoors

Etymology

Formed from 'air' + 'stream'.
