duple

1. ADJ

/ ˈduːpəl / (AmE), / ˈdjuːpəl / (BrE)
consisting of two parts, elements, or beats; especially relating to musical time having two beats per measure.
consisting of two parts or beats

Examples

The waltz is commonly in triple time, while a march is usually in duple time.
The composer chose a duple meter to give the piece a steady, march-like quality.
In music theory, duple rhythms have two beats per measure.

Synset Examples

an egg with a double yolk
a double (binary) star
double doors
dual controls for pilot and copilot
duple (or double) time consists of two (or a multiple of two) beats to a measure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double - consisting of two equal, identical, or similar parts
  • Synonym: dual - having two parts or aspects

Etymology

From Latin 'duplus' meaning 'double, twofold.'
