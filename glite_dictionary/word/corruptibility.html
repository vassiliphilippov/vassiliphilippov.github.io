corruptibility

1. NOUN

/ kəˌrʌptəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / kəˌrʌptəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality of being easily corrupted or susceptible to corruption.
susceptibility to corruption

Examples

Some critics questioned the corruptibility of government officials overseeing the project.
The corruptibility of the legal system undermined the public's trust.
High corruptibility within the organization led to widespread unethical practices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incorruptibility - the quality of being incorruptible or resistant to corruption

Etymology

From Latin 'corruptibilis' (liable to be spoiled) + '-ity'.
