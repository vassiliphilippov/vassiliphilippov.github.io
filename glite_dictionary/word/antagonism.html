antagonism

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtæɡəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ænˈtæɡənɪzəm / (BrE)
active hostility or opposition toward someone or something
active hostility or opposition

Examples

The ongoing antagonism between the two countries has led to years of conflict.
Despite their differences, they managed to resolve their antagonism through discussion.
There was a sense of antagonism in the room after the heated debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostility - unfriendly or antagonistic behavior or feelings
  • Antonym: friendliness - the quality of being friendly or kind

Etymology

From Greek 'antagōnismos' (struggle against), from 'antagōnistēs' (opponent).

4. NOUN

/ ænˈtæɡəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ænˈtæɡənɪzəm / (BrE)
(Biochemistry) A phenomenon in which the action of one chemical substance is interfered with or inhibited by another having a similar structure.
interference with a chemical's action by another (biochemistry)
medical

Examples

The antagonism between two drugs reduced the effectiveness of the treatment.
In biochemistry, antagonism occurs when one substance blocks the action of another.
Researchers observed antagonism as the presence of the second compound diminished the first's physiological effect.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synergism - interaction of substances to produce a combined effect greater than the sum of their separate effects

Etymology

From Greek 'antagōnizesthai' (to struggle against), via Latin 'antagonismus'.
