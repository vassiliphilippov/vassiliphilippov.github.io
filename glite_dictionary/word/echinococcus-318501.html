Echinococcus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkaɪnoʊˈkɑːkəs / (AmE), / iˌkaɪnəˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
A genus of small tapeworms that includes species parasitic in the intestines of mammals, causing diseases such as echinococcosis.
a genus of parasitic tapeworms
medical

Examples

The dog was a carrier of Echinococcus, spreading the parasite to other animals.
Infection with Echinococcus can lead to serious health problems if left untreated.
Scientists study Echinococcus to understand the life cycle of parasitic tapeworms.

Etymology

From Greek *ekhinos* (hedgehog, sea urchin) + *kokkos* (berry, grain), referring to the appearance of the cysts.
