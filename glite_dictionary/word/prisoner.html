prisoner

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪzənər / (AmE), / ˈprɪzənə / (BrE)
a person who is kept in prison or confined, especially as a result of a crime or during a war
a person held in prison or captivity

Examples

The court sentenced him to ten years as a prisoner for armed robbery.
During the war, many soldiers became prisoners behind enemy lines.
As a prisoner, he was allowed one phone call per week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inmate - a person confined to an institution, especially a prison
  • Antonym: captor - a person who holds someone prisoner or keeps them in captivity
  • Antonym: prison guard - a person who guards prisoners in a prison

Etymology

From Old French *prisonier*, derivative of *prison* (prison), from Latin *prensionem* (a seizing, apprehension).
