southerly

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌðərli / (AmE), / ˈsʌðəli / (BrE)
(of a place, position, or direction) situated in, moving toward, or facing the south.
situated in or facing south

Examples

The birds migrated along a southerly route for the winter.
We enjoyed the warm sun from our southerly balcony.
The storm is expected to move in a southerly direction tomorrow.

Synset Examples

a southern exposure
took a southerly course

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northerly - situated in or facing north

Etymology

From Middle English southerly, from south + -erly (suffix meaning 'in the direction of').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01607761-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌðərli / (AmE), / ˈsʌðəli / (BrE)
A wind that comes from the south.
a wind from the south

Examples

A strong southerly blew across the coast all night, bringing warmer air.
The fishermen waited for the southerly to ease before heading out to sea.
A brisk southerly made sailing difficult in the bay today.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northerly - a wind from the north
  • Synonym: southerly - in the direction of or situated toward the south (adjective)

Etymology

From south +‎ -erly, patterned after "northerly", "easterly", etc.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11531838-n
Synset POS: n
