piston

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪstən / (AmE), / ˈpɪstən / (BrE)
A cylindrical part that moves up and down inside an engine or pump to transfer force or compress gases or liquids.
moving cylinder in engine or pump

Examples

The piston moves rapidly inside the engine cylinder to generate power.
He replaced the damaged piston to fix the oil leak in his car.
Hydraulic systems often use a piston to create pressure and movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunger - a part that moves up and down in a tube to move liquid
  • Antonym: cylinder - cylindrical chamber in which a piston moves

Etymology

From French 'piston', from Italian 'pistone' (large pestle), from 'pistare' (to pound).
