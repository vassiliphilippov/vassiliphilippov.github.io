Sir William Wallace

1. PROPN

/ sɜr wɪljəm ˈwɑːlɪs / (AmE), / sə ˈwɪljəm ˈwɒlɪs / (BrE)
(1270–1305) A Scottish knight and national hero who led a resistance against Edward I of England and played a key role in the Scottish Wars of Independence.
Scottish leader in Wars of Independence

Examples

Sir William Wallace is celebrated in Scotland as a symbol of the country's fight for independence.
The legend of Sir William Wallace has inspired countless works of literature and film.
Historians often debate the tactics used by Sir William Wallace during the Battle of Stirling Bridge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Robert the Bruce - Another leader of Scottish resistance against England
  • Antonym: Edward I - English king opposed by Wallace
