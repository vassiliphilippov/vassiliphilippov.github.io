to send

1. VERB

/ sɛnd / (AmE), / sɛnd / (BrE)
to broadcast (a program, message, etc.) over radio or television
broadcast over radio or TV

Examples

The station will send the live debate across the country tonight.
They refused to send the controversial advertisement on air.
Our channel plans to send the concert to millions of viewers worldwide.

Synset Examples

We cannot air this X-rated song

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcast - to transmit (a program or information) by radio or television
  • Synonym: to transmit - to send out (signals, broadcasts, etc.) by radio or television
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00975088-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɛnd / (AmE), / sɛnd / (BrE)
to arrange for something to be taken or delivered to another place, especially commercially or by mail
arrange delivery or transportation

Examples

We will send your order within three business days.
They sent the package by courier to arrive quickly.
The company sends products all over the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ship - to send goods by a commercial carrier
  • Antonym: to receive - to be given or presented with something sent

Etymology

Old English sendan, of Germanic origin; related to German senden and Old Norse senda.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
