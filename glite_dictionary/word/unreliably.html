unreliably

1. ADV

/ ʌn.rɪˈlaɪ.ə.bli / (AmE), / ʌn.rɪˈlaɪ.ə.bli / (BrE)
In a manner that cannot be trusted to be accurate, dependable, or consistent.
in an undependable manner

Examples

The data was collected so unreliably that the results could not be trusted.
He performs his duties unreliably, often missing important deadlines.
The weather forecast has been unreliably predicting rain for days.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reliably - in a dependable and trustworthy manner

Etymology

Formed from 'unreliable' + '-ly'.
