dihybrid

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈhaɪbrɪd / (AmE), / daɪˈhʌɪbrɪd / (BrE)
an organism produced by crossing parents that differ at two specific gene loci, each with two alleles
organism heterozygous at two genetic loci

Examples

A classic dihybrid cross in peas involves genes for seed color and seed shape.
The expected phenotypic ratio for a dihybrid cross is typically 9:3:3:1.
Scientists observed that the offspring from a dihybrid mating displayed new combinations of traits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: monohybrid - organism heterozygous at one genetic locus

Etymology

From di- (two) + hybrid, referring to difference at two loci.
