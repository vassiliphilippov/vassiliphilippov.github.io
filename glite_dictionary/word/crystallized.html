crystallized

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlaɪzd / (BrE)
having the distinct internal structure and external form characteristic of a crystal
having form and structure of a crystal

Examples

The mineral sample was crystallized and revealed a clear geometric pattern under the microscope.
Salt in the cave appeared fully crystallized, with sharp and transparent edges.
A perfectly crystallized diamond reflects light in a unique way.

Synset Examples

quartz crystal is perfectly crystallized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amorphous - lacking definite form or clear structure

Etymology

From 'crystal' + '-ize' + '-ed'; based on Greek 'krystallos' (ice, crystal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00269929-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of ideas, opinions, plans, etc.) Having become clear, definite, and fixed in form.
became fixed and definite

Examples

Over time, his views on the subject crystallized and became more confident.
It was only after extensive discussion that their initial ideas crystallized into a clear proposal.
Her crystallized beliefs made her reluctant to consider new perspectives.

Synset Examples

distinguish between crystallized and uncrystallized opinion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncrystallized - not fixed or definite in form

Etymology

From 'crystallize' + -ed; based on 'crystal' from Greek 'krustallos' (ice, crystal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00659885-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of fruits) preserved by coating with or absorbing sugar, resulting in a sugary, crystalline texture.
preserved with sugar (esp. fruits)

Examples

She used crystallized ginger in the cake for an extra sweet flavor.
The dessert was decorated with crystallized violets for a touch of elegance.
I enjoy snacking on crystallized pineapple during the holidays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candied - (of food) preserved or coated with sugar syrup

Etymology

From crystal + -ize; related to the process forming crystals of sugar on or in the food.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01075629-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a substance) having developed both the internal structure and external form characteristic of a crystal.
having formed into crystals

Examples

The crystallized salt sparkled in the sunlight.
Sugar can become crystallized if left undisturbed for a long time.
The geode is filled with beautifully crystallized minerals.

Synset Examples

quartz crystal is perfectly crystallized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amorphous - lacking a clear shape or crystalline structure
  • Synonym: crystalline - having the structure and form of a crystal

Etymology

From 'crystal' + '-ize' (to make) + '-ed' (having the quality of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00269929-s
Synset POS: s
