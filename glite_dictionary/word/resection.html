resection

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
The surgical removal of part of an organ, tissue, or structure.
surgical removal of part of an organ or tissue
medical

Examples

The surgeon performed a resection to remove the tumor from the patient's colon.
After a partial lung resection, the patient experienced improved breathing.
A gastric resection may be necessary in cases of severe stomach ulcers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excision - the act of cutting out, especially surgically
  • Synonym: removal - the act of taking something away or off from its position

Etymology

From Latin resectio (act of cutting back), from resecare (to cut off).
