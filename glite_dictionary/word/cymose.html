cymose

1. ADJ

/ ˈsaɪˌmoʊs / (AmE), / ˈsaɪməʊs / (BrE)
(of a flower cluster) having a structure in which each stem ends in a flower that opens before those below or beside it, often resulting in a flat-topped appearance.
having a flower cluster with each stem ending in a flower

Examples

The plant produces cymose inflorescences, which are easily distinguished from other types by their structure.
In cymose clusters, the central flower opens first, followed by those on the sides.
The botanist explained that a cymose arrangement is different from a racemose one in flower development.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: racemose - having a flower cluster with the main axis continuing to grow and flowers borne on lateral stems

Etymology

From Greek 'kyma' (wave), via botanical Latin 'cymosus' (like a cyme).
