drippings

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪpɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈdrɪpɪŋz / (BrE)
the fat and juices that drip from meat while it is being roasted or fried, often used for making gravy or for cooking
fat and juices from roasting meat

Examples

She poured the drippings from the roast into a pan to make gravy.
The cook saved the bacon drippings to use for frying eggs the next morning.
Beef drippings add a rich flavor to Yorkshire pudding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rendered fat - fat obtained from heating meat or animal tissue
  • Synonym: gravy - a sauce made from the juices of cooked meat
