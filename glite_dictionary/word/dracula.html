Dracula

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrækjələ / (AmE), / ˈdrækjʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American orchids, often with bizarre and sinister-looking flowers and pendulous scapes; sometimes included in the genus Masdevallia.
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The Dracula orchid is prized by collectors for its unusual appearance and challenging cultivation requirements.
Several species within the genus Dracula are named after bats and monkeys due to their flower shapes.
Botanists recently discovered a new Dracula species in the cloud forests of Ecuador.

Etymology

From Latin 'draco' (dragon), reflecting the sinister appearance of the flowers; also influenced by the fictional Count Dracula.

2. PROPN

/ ˈdrækjələ / (AmE), / ˈdrækjələ / (BrE)
A fictional vampire count and central character in Bram Stoker's 1897 gothic horror novel, often used to refer to vampires in general.
fictional vampire created by Bram Stoker

Examples

Many movies and books have reimagined the character of Dracula over the years.
Halloween costumes featuring Dracula remain popular among children and adults alike.
The legend of Dracula is often associated with Transylvania and ancient castles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vampire - a fictional being that drinks blood to survive

Etymology

From Romanian 'dracul' meaning 'the dragon' or 'the devil', chosen by author Bram Stoker.

3. NOUN

/ drəˈkjuːlə / (AmE), / drəˈkjuːlə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American orchids known for small plants with bizarre, often sinister-looking flowers and pendulous stems.
a genus of orchids with unusual flowers

Examples

The Dracula genus includes several species prized by orchid enthusiasts for their unique, sinister blooms.
Some Dracula orchids resemble monkey faces, adding to their popularity among collectors.
Botanists recently discovered a new Dracula species in the cloud forests of Ecuador.

Etymology

Modern Latin, named after the legendary figure Count Dracula for the flower's unusual appearance.
