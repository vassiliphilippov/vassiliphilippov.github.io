Gasterosteus pungitius

1. NOUN

/ ɡæstəˈroʊstiəs pʌnˈdʒɪtiəs / (AmE), / ˌɡæstəˈrəʊstɪəs pʌnˈdʒɪtɪəs / (BrE)
A small fish species, commonly known as the nine-spined stickleback, found in rivers and freshwater habitats.
species of nine-spined stickleback fish

Examples

The Gasterosteus pungitius thrives in slow-moving streams with abundant vegetation.
Researchers studied the breeding habits of Gasterosteus pungitius in different river systems.
The population of Gasterosteus pungitius has declined in some areas due to habitat pollution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nine-spined stickleback - common name for Gasterosteus pungitius

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Gasterosteus' (from Greek gaster 'belly' + osteon 'bone'), species name 'pungitius' (from Latin pungere 'to prick').
