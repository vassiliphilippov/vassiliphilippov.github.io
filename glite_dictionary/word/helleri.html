helleri

1. NOUN

/ hɛˈlɛri / (AmE), / hɛˈlɛri / (BrE)
A species of freshwater fish native to Central America, known for its distinctive long, swordlike tail; popular as an aquarium fish.
a Central American freshwater aquarium fish

Examples

The brilliant colors of the helleri make it a favorite among aquarium enthusiasts.
Many aquarists keep helleris because they are hardy and easy to breed.
A male helleri can be identified by its distinctive swordlike tail extension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swordtail - a freshwater fish with a long, swordlike tail, commonly kept in aquariums

Etymology

Named after Austrian zoologist Karl Bartholomaeus Heller.
