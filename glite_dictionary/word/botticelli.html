Botticelli

1. PROPN

/ ˌbɑːtəˈtʃɛli / (AmE), / ˌbɒtɪˈtʃɛli / (BrE)
(c. 1445–1510) Italian painter renowned for his mythological and religious works, including 'The Birth of Venus' and 'Primavera'.
Italian Renaissance painter

Examples

The painting was reminiscent of Botticelli's graceful figures and flowing lines.
I studied the works of Botticelli during my art history course.
The museum's newest exhibition features several masterpieces by Botticelli.

Etymology

From the Italian surname Botticelli, meaning 'little barrel'.
