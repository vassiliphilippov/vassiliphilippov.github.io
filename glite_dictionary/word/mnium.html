Mnium

1. NOUN

/ ˈmniː.əm / (AmE), / ˈmniː.əm / (BrE)
A genus of mosses similar to Bryum but typically larger, often found in moist habitats.
a genus of larger mosses

Examples

The forest floor was covered in lush Mnium that thrived in the damp shade.
Botanists identified several species of Mnium during their field study.
Unlike Bryum, Mnium often displays brighter green leaves and a larger growth habit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bryum - a related genus of smaller mosses

Etymology

From New Latin *Mnium*, from Greek *mnion* (a plant, possibly a moss).
