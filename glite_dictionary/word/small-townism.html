small-townism

1. NOUN

/ smɔl ˈtaʊn ɪzəm / (AmE), / smɔːl ˈtaʊn ɪzəm / (BrE)
a mindset or way of life characterized by the values, simplicity, and close-knit community typical of small towns
preference for small-town values or lifestyle

Examples

The author's novels often explore small-townism and its impact on relationships.
Many see small-townism as a source of comfort and social support.
His views reflected a certain small-townism that contrasted with urban sensibilities.

Synset Examples

Her poetry reflects a sense of small-townism and belonging.
He embraced small-townism, valuing simplicity over complexity.
Small-townism often leads to strong community ties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provincialism - narrow-mindedness or limited outlook typical of rural or small-town areas

Etymology

Formed from 'small-town' + '-ism', indicating ideology or characteristic attitude.
