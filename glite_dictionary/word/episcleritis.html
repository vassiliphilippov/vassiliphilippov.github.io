episcleritis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪskləˈraɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪskləˈraɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the episclera, the thin layer of tissue covering the white of the eye
eye inflammation (episclera)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with episcleritis after complaining of redness and discomfort in her left eye.
Most cases of episcleritis resolve without treatment, but persistent cases may require medication.
Doctors differentiate between episcleritis and scleritis based on symptoms and severity of inflammation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: scleritis - inflammation of the sclera of the eye, often more severe than episcleritis

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + sclera (hard, the white of the eye) + -itis (inflammation).
