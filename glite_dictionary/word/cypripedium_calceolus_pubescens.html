Cypripedium calceolus pubescens

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪprəˈpiːdiəm ˌkælsiˈoʊləs pjuːˈbɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌsɪprɪˈpiːdɪəm ˌkælsiˈəʊləs pjuːˈbɛsns / (BrE)
a plant of eastern and central North America with slightly fragrant, purple-marked, greenish-yellow flowers; also called yellow lady's slipper
North American orchid; yellow lady's slipper

Examples

The Cypripedium calceolus pubescens blooms in spring in shaded woodlands.
Many gardeners treasure the Cypripedium calceolus pubescens for its striking yellow flowers.
Researchers are working to preserve wild populations of Cypripedium calceolus pubescens threatened by habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow lady's slipper - common name for Cypripedium calceolus pubescens
  • Degree Higher: orchid - a plant of the orchid family

Etymology

Cypripedium from Greek kupris (Venus) and pedilon (sandal); calceolus from Latin for 'little shoe', pubescens meaning 'downy' or 'hairy' in Latin.
