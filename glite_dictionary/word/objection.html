objection

1. NOUN

/ əbˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / əbˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
The act of expressing disagreement or opposition to something.
expression of opposition or protest

Examples

The lawyer raised an objection to the witness's testimony.
Her loud objection halted the discussion immediately.
Many people voiced their objection to the proposed changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - a statement or action expressing disapproval or objection
  • Antonym: approval - agreement with or acceptance of something

Etymology

From Latin 'obiectio' (an opposition, contradiction), from 'obicere' (to throw against).

2. NOUN

/ əbˈdʒɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / əbˈdʒɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
(law) A statement made by a party in a court case to challenge the propriety or admissibility of a question, witness, or piece of evidence, requesting the judge to rule on its acceptance.
challenge in court to evidence or procedure
legal

Examples

The defense attorney raised an objection to the prosecutor's question, citing irrelevance.
After several objections, the judge finally overruled and allowed the witness to answer.
Her lawyer stood and made an objection during the cross-examination to challenge the evidence presented.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - an expression or declaration of objection or disapproval
  • Antonym: acceptance - willingness to agree or approve, especially in contrast to raising objections

Etymology

From Latin 'objectio' (a throwing or putting before), from 'objectus', past participle of 'obijicere' (to oppose).

4. NOUN

/ əbˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / əbˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
The act of expressing or feeling disapproval or opposition; a statement or reason for disagreeing.
expression of disapproval or opposition

Examples

The lawyer raised an objection during the trial, claiming the question was irrelevant.
I have no objection to your proposal, so you may proceed as planned.
Her objection to the new policy was based on environmental concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - a strong expression of disagreement or disapproval
  • Antonym: approval - the act of accepting or agreeing with something

Etymology

From Latin 'obiectio' (a throwing up, opposition), from 'obicere' (to throw against).
