to syllabise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪl.ə.baɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.ə.baɪz / (BrE)
to divide a word into its syllables
divide into syllables
british

Examples

Teachers often ask students to syllabise complex words during language lessons.
Can you syllabise ‘unbelievable’ for me?
Linguists frequently syllabise words to analyse patterns of pronunciation.

Synset Examples

syllabify the words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to syllabize - to divide a word into its syllables (American spelling)

Etymology

From syllable + -ise, patterned after the French 'syllabiser' and Latin 'syllabizare'.

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪləˌbaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.ə.baɪz / (BrE)
to pronounce or utter each syllable of a word or phrase distinctly
pronounce each syllable distinctly
british

Examples

The teacher asked the students to syllabise the difficult words slowly.
He carefully syllabised each line of the poem for the young readers.
When learning a new language, it helps to syllabise unfamiliar words.

Synset Examples

The poet syllabized the verses he read

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to syllabize - to pronounce or utter each syllable of a word or phrase distinctly, chiefly US spelling

Etymology

From French 'syllabiser', from 'syllabe' (syllable), from Latin 'syllaba', from Greek 'syllabē'.

Synset POS: v
