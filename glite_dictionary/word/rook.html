rook

1. NOUN

/ rʊk / (AmE), / rʊk / (BrE)
a large gregarious Eurasian crow (Corvus frugilegus) with black plumage and a bare whitish face, common in open country and towns
Eurasian black crow-like bird

Examples

A flock of rooks gathered noisily in the field at dusk.
The ancient oak was home to a colony of nesting rooks.
Unlike crows, rooks have a pale patch of bare skin at the base of their beaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crow - any large perching bird of the genus Corvus, especially the common black species

Etymology

From Old English *hrōc*; related to Dutch *roek*, German *Rauch* (rook).

3. NOUN

/ rʊk / (AmE), / rʊk / (BrE)
a large, black, gregarious bird of the crow family, native to Europe and Asia, similar in size and appearance to the American crow
a large black European and Asian bird

Examples

A flock of rooks gathered noisily in the old oak tree at dawn.
The rook is easily identified by its bare grayish face and hoarse call.
In spring, rooks build large communal nests called rookeries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crow - large, black bird of the genus Corvus

Etymology

From Old English *hrōc*, of Germanic origin.
