Rhamnus carolinianus

1. NOUN

/ ˈræmnəs ˌkæroʊˌlɪniˈænəs / (AmE), / ˈræmnəs ˌkarəlɪnɪˈanəs / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to eastern and central United States, with black berrylike fruit and golden-yellow foliage in autumn.
American shrub with black fruit and yellow autumn leaves

Examples

The Rhamnus carolinianus brightened the woodland edge with its golden-yellow leaves in the fall.
Gardeners often plant Rhamnus carolinianus for its attractive autumn color and wildlife-friendly berries.
Birds flocked to the Rhamnus carolinianus shrubs to eat the black berrylike fruit.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Rhamnus' (from Greek 'rhamnos', a kind of bush), 'carolinianus' indicating 'of Carolina'.
