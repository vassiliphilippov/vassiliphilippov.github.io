Ofo

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊfoʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊfəʊ / (BrE)
A member of a Siouan-speaking Native American people formerly living in the Yazoo River valley in Mississippi.
member of a Native American tribe (Mississippi)

Examples

Anthropologists have extensively studied the traditions of the Ofo people.
The Ofo migrated from the Ohio River south towards Mississippi over the centuries.
Artifacts attributed to the Ofo have been found near the Yazoo River valley.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊfoʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊfəʊ / (BrE)
a Siouan language formerly spoken by the Ofo people of the lower Mississippi Valley, now extinct
extinct Siouan language of the Ofo people

Examples

Linguists have studied Ofo to better understand Siouan language relationships.
There are very few written records of the Ofo language.
The last known speaker of Ofo died in the twentieth century.

Etymology

From the name Ofo, referring to the indigenous group that spoke the language.
