to collaborate

1. VERB

/ kəˈlæbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / kəˈlæbəreɪt / (BrE)
to work jointly with others, especially in an intellectual or creative endeavor
work together on a project

Examples

Scientists from different countries often collaborate to solve complex problems.
She decided to collaborate with local artists on the community mural.
The two companies collaborated to develop a new environmentally friendly product.

Synset Examples

The soprano and the pianist did not get together very well
We joined forces with another research group

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cooperate - to work together for a common purpose

Etymology

From Late Latin collaborare, from Latin com- (together) + laborare (to work).

WordNet ID: oewn-02421408-v

2. VERB

/ kəˈlæbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / kəˈlæbəreɪt / (BrE)
to cooperate with an enemy force, especially by betraying one's own country
cooperate with an enemy (as a traitor)

Examples

During the occupation, some citizens collaborated with the enemy authorities.
He was accused of having collaborated with the invaders during the war.
Many refused to collaborate and instead joined the resistance movement.

Synset Examples

he collaborated with the Nazis when they occupied Paris

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to betray - to be disloyal or hand over to an enemy
  • Antonym: to resist - to oppose or fight against an occupying force

Etymology

From Latin collaborare, from com- (together) + laborare (to work).
