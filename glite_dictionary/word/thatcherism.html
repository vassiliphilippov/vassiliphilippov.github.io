Thatcherism

1. NOUN

/ ˈθætʃɚˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈθætʃərɪzəm / (BrE)
The political principles and policies advocated by Margaret Thatcher, especially characterized by free markets, privatization, and reduction of state intervention in the economy.
political policy of Margaret Thatcher
british

Examples

Many historians believe that Thatcherism transformed the British economy in the 1980s.
His economic policies were heavily influenced by the ideas of Thatcherism.
Debates about the legacy of Thatcherism continue to shape British politics today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Reaganomics - economic policies of US President Reagan, similar in ideology to Thatcherism

Etymology

From the name of Margaret Thatcher, British Prime Minister (1979–1990), plus the suffix -ism.
