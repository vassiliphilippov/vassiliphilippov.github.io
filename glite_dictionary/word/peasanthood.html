peasanthood

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛzənthʊd / (AmE), / ˈpɛzənthʊd / (BrE)
the state or condition of being a peasant
state or condition of being a peasant

Examples

She often reminisced about her humble peasanthood before marrying into nobility.
His novels frequently depict the struggles and joys of peasanthood in rural Europe.
Despite his rise in society, he never forgot his roots in peasanthood.

Synset Examples

the same homely dress she wore in the days of her peasanthood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peasantry - the class of peasants; peasants collectively

Etymology

Formed from 'peasant' (from Old French *paisant*, from Latin *pagus*, country district) + '-hood' (denoting state or condition).
