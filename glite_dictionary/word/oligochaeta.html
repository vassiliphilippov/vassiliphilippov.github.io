Oligochaeta

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊlɪɡoʊˈkiːtə / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪɡəʊˈkiːtə / (BrE)
a class of annelid worms that encompasses earthworms and their relatives, characterized by having few bristles (chaetae) on each segment
class of annelid worms, including earthworms

Examples

Scientists studied several species of Oligochaeta to understand soil health.
The class Oligochaeta includes familiar creatures like earthworms.
Many Oligochaeta play a vital role in decomposing organic matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthworm - a common terrestrial worm in the class Oligochaeta

Etymology

From Greek oligos (few) + khaite (hair), referring to their small number of bristles.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːlɪɡoʊˈkiːtə / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪɡəʊˈkiːtə / (BrE)
A class of segmented worms in the phylum Annelida, including earthworms and their relatives, characterized by having few bristles (chaetae) per segment.
class of segmented worms (including earthworms)

Examples

Scientists classify Oligochaeta as including earthworms and related species.
The biology textbook describes the distinctive features of Oligochaeta.
Members of Oligochaeta play a vital role in soil health and fertility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthworm - a common burrowing annelid worm

Etymology

From Greek oligos (few) + khaite (hair), referring to the few bristles per segment.
