clone

1. NOUN

/ kloʊn / (AmE), / kləʊn / (BrE)
A group of genetically identical cells or organisms that have been produced from a single original cell or individual by asexual reproduction.
group of identical organisms or cells
medical

Examples

The scientist studied the clone to understand how genetic diseases are transmitted.
A clone of the original plant was created in the laboratory for further testing.
Each clone in the population shared the same DNA as the parent cell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replica - exact copy or reproduction
  • Degree Lower: organism - individual living thing

Etymology

From Greek klōn (twig, branch), adopted in 20th-century biological science.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07956482-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ kloʊn / (AmE), / kləʊn / (BrE)
An unauthorized copy or imitation of something, especially a product, design, or service.
unauthorized imitation or copy

Examples

The software was just a clone of a more popular program, lacking originality.
Many phones on the market are actually cheap clones of branded models.
She bought a designer handbag, only to realize it was a clone and not the real thing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterfeit - a fraudulent imitation of something valuable
  • Antonym: original - the first or authentic version of something

Etymology

From Greek klōn (twig, branch), via scientific use for cells and organisms, later extended to refer to copies of products.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03632216-n
Synset POS: n

/ kloʊn / (AmE), / kləʊn / (BrE)

make an identical copy, often biotechnologically

Examples

The team hopes to clone the software in order to create a backup of the system.
She is working to clone cells for medical research.

Related Terms

Etymology

4. NOUN

/ kloʊn / (AmE), / kləʊn / (BrE)
a person who closely resembles another, especially in appearance or behavior
person almost identical to another

Examples

She was surprised to meet her clone at the party; they looked almost exactly alike.
The new band member was dismissed as just another clone of the lead singer.
Some critics say the movie's hero is a clone of action stars from the '80s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double - a person who looks very much like another
  • Synonym: lookalike - a person who closely resembles another, especially a celebrity
  • Antonym: original - the person from whom a clone or copy is made

Etymology

From Greek klon (twig, branch), originally used in biology for a group of genetically identical organisms.
