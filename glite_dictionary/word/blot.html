blot

1. VERB

/ blɑt / (AmE), / blɒt / (BrE)
to dry (ink) by pressing blotting paper against it
to dry ink with blotting paper

Examples

She gently blotted the signature to prevent the ink from smudging.
After writing his name, he used a sheet of paper to blot the excess ink.
Make sure to blot the letter before folding it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dab - to touch something lightly, usually to absorb liquid

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse 'blot' (blot, stain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01541716-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ blɑt / (AmE), / blɒt / (BrE)
to make a spot or stain on a surface, often by absorbing a liquid
make a spot or stain

Examples

He accidentally blotted his notes with ink from his pen.
The spilled soup blotted the entire napkin, leaving a big mark.
Careless painting can blot the floor with drops of color.

Synset Examples

The wine spotted the tablecloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stain - to leave a mark that is difficult to remove
  • Synonym: spot - to mark with small areas of color or dirt
  • Antonym: clean - to remove dirt, marks, or stains

Etymology

From Middle English *blotten*, likely of Scandinavian origin; compare with Danish *blotte* (to blot).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01534786-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ blɑt / (AmE), / blɒt / (BrE)
a small mark or stain, especially one made by ink, dirt, or another substance
small mark or stain

Examples

There was an ink blot on the contract that made it hard to read.
She wiped the blot of mud from her skirt before the event.
A single blot on the white wall caught everyone's attention.

Synset Examples

he had a smudge on his cheek

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smudge - a dirty mark or smear
  • Synonym: stain - a spot or discoloration left on a surface

Etymology

From Old English 'blott' (a spot, stain), related to the verb 'to blot'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04701789-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ blɑt / (AmE), / blɒt / (BrE)
an act or event that damages a person's reputation or record; a disgrace or stain
a mark of disgrace or dishonor

Examples

The scandal was a blot on his distinguished career.
Even one mistake can leave a blot on your record.
Her generosity erased the blot of her earlier misdeeds.

Synset Examples

he made a huge blot on his copybook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stain - a mark or blemish, also used figuratively for disgrace
  • Antonym: honor - high respect; esteem, often contrasted with disgrace

Etymology

From Old French 'blot', of Germanic origin, meaning 'spot' or 'stain'.

Source: WordNet
