incremental

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪŋ.krəˈmɛn.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɪŋ.krɪˈmen.tl̩ / (BrE)
increasing gradually by regular degrees or additions; relating to or denoting an increase or addition, especially one of a series.
increasing in small, regular steps

Examples

The company adopted an incremental approach to developing its new software platform.
Many people prefer incremental changes to drastic reform in public policy.
There has been incremental progress toward reaching a long-term peace agreement.

Synset Examples

lecturers enjoy … steady incremental growth in salary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gradual - taking place or progressing slowly or by degrees
  • Antonym: sudden - happening or done quickly and unexpectedly

Etymology

From Latin 'incrementum' (growth), from 'increscere' (to grow).
