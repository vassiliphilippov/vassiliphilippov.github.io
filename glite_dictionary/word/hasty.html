hasty

1. ADJ

/ ˈheɪsti / (AmE), / ˈheɪsti / (BrE)
done or made quickly, often without enough thought or attention; excessively quick
excessively quick; done too fast

Examples

She made a hasty decision without thinking it through.
The meeting ended with a hasty conclusion due to time constraints.
Do not be hasty in judging someone's actions before knowing the full story.

Synset Examples

made a hasty exit
a headlong rush to sell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rash - acting without careful consideration
  • Antonym: deliberate - done with careful thought or consideration

Etymology

From Old French *hastif*, from *haste* (haste).

2. ADJ

/ ˈheɪsti / (AmE), / ˈheɪsti / (BrE)
Acting or done with excessive speed and little or no thought; lacking due consideration.
done too quickly; lacking consideration

Examples

The manager regretted his hasty decision to fire the employee without a full investigation.
After a hasty meal, they rushed to catch the last train home.
She offered a hasty apology, but it did not seem sincere.

Synset Examples

hasty marriage seldom proveth well
hasty makeshifts take the place of planning
rejected what was regarded as an overhasty plan for reconversion
wondered whether they had been rather precipitate in deposing the king

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rash - acting without careful consideration of the possible consequences
  • Antonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally, with careful thought

Etymology

From Middle English haste (speed) + -y (adjectival suffix).
