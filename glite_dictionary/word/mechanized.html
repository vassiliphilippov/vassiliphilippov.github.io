mechanized

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛk.əˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈmɛk.ə.naɪzd / (BrE)
equipped with or operated by machinery, especially as opposed to manual methods.
operated by machinery

Examples

The company invested in a mechanized production line to improve efficiency.
Modern farming often relies on mechanized equipment for planting and harvesting crops.
A fully mechanized laundry reduced the need for human labor.

Synset Examples

a mechanized factory

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manual - operated by hand, not by machinery

Etymology

From 'mechanize' (to equip with machinery) + -ed.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɛk.əˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈmɛk.ə.naɪzd / (BrE)
(of an activity, process, or force) operated or equipped with machines, especially vehicles.
operated or equipped with machines or vehicles

Examples

The mechanized army advanced quickly across the open plains.
Modern farming has become increasingly mechanized over the past century.
During World War II, mechanized units played a crucial role on the battlefield.

Synset Examples

motorized warfare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motorized - operated by a motor or engine

Etymology

From 'mechanize' + -ed. 'Mechanize' from French 'méchaniser', ultimately from Greek 'mēkhanē' (machine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01527569-s
Synset POS: s
