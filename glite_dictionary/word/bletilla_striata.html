Bletilla striata

1. NOUN

/ blɛˈtɪlə straɪˈeɪtə / (AmE), / blɛˈtɪlə straɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of hardy orchid native to Asia, with white-striped leaves and slender spikes of rose to magenta flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant.
species of hardy Asian orchid

Examples

Bletilla striata is a popular choice for shade gardens due to its vibrant flowers.
Gardeners recommend planting Bletilla striata in well-drained soil during the spring.
The delicate blooms of Bletilla striata can brighten any woodland setting.

Etymology

From the genus name Bletilla, a diminutive of Bletia (another orchid genus), and Latin striata meaning 'striped'.
