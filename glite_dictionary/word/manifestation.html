manifestation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmænəfəˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmænɪfɛˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
an event, action, or object that clearly shows or demonstrates the existence or presence of something, especially a quality, feeling, or disease.
clear sign or indication of something

Examples

The rash was the first manifestation of the illness.
Rising seas are a clear manifestation of climate change.
His anger found a sudden manifestation in his harsh words.

Synset Examples

a manifestation of disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign - a thing that indicates the presence or existence of something else
  • Synonym: symptom - a feature regarded as indicating a condition or disease

Etymology

From Late Latin manifestatio (a making public).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæn.ə.fɛˈsteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmæn.ɪ.fɛˈsteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an appearance or display of a spirit or supernatural being in bodily form
appearance of a spirit in bodily form

Examples

The villagers claimed that a manifestation of the old priest appeared in the church at midnight.
Some believe that a sudden chill in the room signals a manifestation of spirits.
The novel describes a terrifying manifestation that haunted the family for weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apparition - a ghostly or supernatural appearance
  • Synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person appearing to the living

Etymology

From Latin manifestatio (a making public), from manifestare (to make visible).
