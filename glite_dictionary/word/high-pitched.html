high-pitched

1. ADJ

/ haɪ ˈpɪtʃt / (AmE), / haɪ ˈpɪtʃt / (BrE)
(of a sound or voice) having a high frequency or pitch; shrill or sharp in tone.
having a high frequency or pitch

Examples

The fire alarm emitted a high-pitched sound that could be heard throughout the building.
Her high-pitched laughter echoed across the playground.
Dogs are able to hear high-pitched noises that humans cannot detect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrill - (of a sound) high-pitched and piercing
  • Antonym: low-pitched - (of a sound) having a low frequency or pitch
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01215869-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ haɪ pɪtʃt / (AmE), / haɪ pɪtʃt / (BrE)
(of a roof or surface) set at a steep or sharp angle; sloping steeply.
steeply sloping (esp. a roof)

Examples

The old church had a high-pitched roof that shed snow easily in winter.
Many Victorian houses feature high-pitched gables above the main entrance.
The architect recommended installing a high-pitched structure to improve drainage.

Synset Examples

a high-pitched roof

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steep - having a sharp inclination
