flatulent

1. ADJ

/ ˈflætʃələnt / (AmE), / ˈflætjʊlənt / (BrE)
suffering from or affected by excessive gas in the digestive tract, especially the stomach and intestines.
having excessive gas in the digestive tract
medical

Examples

After eating beans, he often felt flatulent and uncomfortable.
The patient complained of flatulent pain in his lower abdomen.
Certain vegetables can make some people more flatulent than others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gassy - having a lot of gas in the stomach or intestines
  • Synonym: bloated - swollen with fluid or gas

Etymology

From Latin 'flatu-' (blowing, wind) + '-lent' (full of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈflætʃələnt / (AmE), / ˈflætjʊlənt / (BrE)
generating excessive gas in the alimentary canal, especially resulting in passing wind
producing excessive gas in the intestines
medical

Examples

Certain foods can make people flatulent if eaten in large amounts.
The dog became flatulent after eating too quickly and swallowed a lot of air.
She felt embarrassed because she was unusually flatulent during the long meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gassy - suffering from or relating to excessive gas in the digestive system
  • Degree Higher: bloated - swollen with fluid or gas, especially in the abdomen

Etymology

From Latin 'flatus' (a blowing, breath) and 'ulentus' (full of).
