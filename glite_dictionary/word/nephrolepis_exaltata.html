Nephrolepis exaltata

1. NOUN

/ nef.rəˌliː.pɪs ɪɡ.zælˈteɪ.tə / (AmE), / nef.rəˌliː.pɪs ɛɡ.zælˈtɑː.tə / (BrE)
A species of sword fern with arching or drooping pinnate fronds, commonly grown as a houseplant.
a species of sword fern (houseplant)

Examples

The Nephrolepis exaltata thrives in humid rooms with indirect sunlight.
Many people choose the Nephrolepis exaltata to purify the air in their homes.
The fronds of Nephrolepis exaltata can grow impressively long if well cared for.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Boston fern - Common name for Nephrolepis exaltata, a popular houseplant fern.

Etymology

From Greek 'nephros' (kidney) + 'lepis' (scale), referring to kidney-shaped scales; 'exaltata' is Latin for 'exalted' or 'lofty'.
