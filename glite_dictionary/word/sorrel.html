sorrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔːrəl / (AmE), / ˈsɒrəl / (BrE)
Any plant or flower of the genus Oxalis, characterized by clover-like leaves and typically yellow or pinkish flowers.
plant of the genus Oxalis

Examples

The sorrel in her garden bloomed with delicate yellow flowers in early spring.
Many species of sorrel are enjoyed for their tart flavor in salads.
We found wild sorrel growing along the muddy riverbanks during our walk.

Etymology

From Old French 'surele', diminutive of 'sur' (sour), referencing the tart taste of the leaves.

/ ˈsɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈsɒrəl / (BrE)
a horse of a reddish-brown to light brown color, especially with a mane and tail of similar or lighter shade
horse of reddish-brown to light brown color

Examples

Her favorite horse was a gentle sorrel with a flaxen mane.
We watched as the sorrel galloped across the field in the morning sun.

Related Terms

From Old French 'sorel' (reddish-brown), diminutive of 'sor' (yellowish-brown).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈsɒrəl / (BrE)
a plant with large, sour-tasting arrowhead-shaped leaves, often used in salads and sauces
a sour-tasting leafy plant (culinary)

Examples

The chef added finely chopped sorrel to the salad for a refreshing, tangy flavor.
Gardeners recommend planting sorrel for its hardiness and culinary versatility.
A traditional French soup is made with pureed sorrel and potatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dock - a related leafy plant in the same genus, sometimes used similarly in cooking

Etymology

From Old French 'surele', diminutive of 'sur' (sour), from Latin 'acidulus' (slightly sour).

5. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈsɒrəl / (BrE)
An East Indian herb or subshrub (Hibiscus sabdariffa) cultivated for its fleshy reddish calyxes used in tarts and jelly, and for its bast fiber.
East Indian herb (used in food and fiber)

Examples

The fleshy calyxes of sorrel are often used to make flavorful tarts and jams.
Many Caribbean countries cultivate sorrel to prepare a traditional festive beverage.
Farmers harvest sorrel not only for culinary uses but also for its strong bast fiber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roselle - another name for Hibiscus sabdariffa, the plant itself

Etymology

From Old French 'surele', diminutive of 'sur', meaning 'sour', ultimately from Latin 'subacidus' (slightly sour).
