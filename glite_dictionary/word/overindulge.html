to overindulge

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərɪnˈdʌldʒ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪnˈdʌldʒ / (BrE)
to allow oneself or someone else to have too much of something, especially food or drink
to have too much of something, especially food or drink

Examples

After the party, he regretted having overindulged in rich desserts.
Parents sometimes overindulge their children by giving them too many treats.
Try not to overindulge during the holidays, as it can affect your health.

Synset Examples

She stuffed herself at the dinner
The kids binged on ice cream

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: to indulge - to allow oneself to enjoy something

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessively) + 'indulge' (to yield freely to).
