woven

1. ADJ

/ ˈwoʊ.vən / (AmE), / ˈwəʊ.vən / (BrE)
made or constructed by interlacing threads, strips, or other elements together into a unified whole
made by interlacing threads or elements

Examples

She admired the beautiful woven baskets at the artisan market.
The rug was woven from natural wool fibers, making it both durable and soft.
Traditional tales are often woven into the fabric of local culture.

Synset Examples

woven fabrics
woven baskets
the incidents woven into the story
folk songs woven into a symphony

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knitted - made by interlocking yarn in a series of connected loops using needles
  • Synonym: woven - to form by interlacing threads or strips

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'weave', from Old English 'wefan'.
