profiteer

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɑːfɪˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌprɒfɪˈtɪə / (BrE)
a person who makes excessive profits, especially by taking advantage of shortages or crises
person who makes excessive profits

Examples

During the crisis, several profiteers were caught selling essential medicines at outrageous prices.
The government introduced strict laws to punish profiteers exploiting food shortages.
He was labeled a profiteer after accumulating wealth by hoarding and reselling fuel during the drought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speculator - someone who engages in risky financial transactions to make quick, large profits
  • Antonym: humanitarian - a person who seeks to promote human welfare, often contrasted with profiteer in crisis situations

Etymology

From profit + -eer, modelled after auctioneer and engineer.

2. VERB

/ ˌprɑː.fɪˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌprɒf.ɪˈtɪə / (BrE)
to make an excessive or unfair profit, especially by charging high prices for goods that are scarce or hard to obtain
make excessive profit, especially in shortage

Examples

During the crisis, some unscrupulous vendors began to profiteer by drastically raising the prices of basic supplies.
Laws were enacted to punish those who would profiteer from the food shortages during wartime.
The company was accused of trying to profiteer by selling essential medicines at marked-up prices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploit - to use a situation or person unfairly for your own advantage

Etymology

From profit +‎ -eer, modeled after words like engineer and auctioneer.
