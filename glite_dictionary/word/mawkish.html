mawkish

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɔːkɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːkɪʃ / (BrE)
Excessively or insincerely sentimental, especially in a way that is exaggerated or sickly.
excessively sentimental

Examples

The film was criticized for its mawkish attempts to manipulate the audience's emotions.
Her letter was so mawkish that it made everyone in the room cringe a little.
He found the love song too mawkish for his taste and preferred something more genuine.

Synset Examples

a bathetic novel
maudlin expressions of sympathy
mushy effusiveness
a schmaltzy song
sentimental soap operas
slushy poetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentimental - having or arousing excessive tenderness, sadness, or nostalgia
  • Synonym: maudlin - tearfully or weakly emotional; foolishly sentimental
  • Antonym: unsentimental - not emotional or sentimental

Etymology

From Middle English *mawk* (maggot) + -ish, originally meaning 'sickly' or 'nauseating.'
