isosmotic

1. ADJ

/ ˌaɪ.sɒzˈmɑ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.sɒzˈmɒ.tɪk / (BrE)
(of solutions) having the same osmotic pressure as another solution, especially when separated by a semipermeable membrane.
having equal osmotic pressure
medical

Examples

The saline solution was isosmotic to human blood, making it safe for intravenous infusions.
Researchers compared the reaction of cells placed in isosmotic and hyperosmotic solutions.
To prevent dehydration of the tissue, an isosmotic environment was maintained during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hyperosmotic - (of a solution) having greater osmotic pressure than another
  • Degree Lower: hypoosmotic - (of a solution) having lower osmotic pressure than another

Etymology

From iso- (equal) + osmotic (relating to osmosis); based on Greek 'isos' (equal) and 'osmos' (push, thrust).
