glomerular

1. ADJ

/ ɡləˈmɛr.jələr / (AmE), / ɡlɒˈmɛr.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
relating to or affecting glomeruli, the tiny structures in the kidneys that filter blood to form urine
relating to kidney glomeruli
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with glomerular disease affecting kidney function.
Researchers observed changes in glomerular filtration rate after the treatment.
A biopsy revealed glomerular damage consistent with the symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glomerulus - a tiny ball-shaped structure in the kidney involved in blood filtration

Etymology

From glomerulus, Latin for ‘small ball’, with the suffix -ar meaning ‘of or relating to’.
