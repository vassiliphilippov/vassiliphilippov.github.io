aeonian

1. ADJ

/ iˈoʊniən / (AmE), / iˈəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to a geological eon; lasting for an immeasurably or indefinitely long period of time
relating to a geological eon

Examples

Geologists studied the aeonian changes that shaped the continent over billions of years.
The mountain range is a testament to the aeonian processes of the Earth's crust.
Some scientists discuss aeonian cycles when explaining the evolution of the planet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eon - an immeasurably long period of geological time

Etymology
