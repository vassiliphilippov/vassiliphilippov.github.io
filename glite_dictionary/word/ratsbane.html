ratsbane

1. NOUN

/ ˈræts.beɪn / (AmE), / ˈræts.beɪn / (BrE)
a poisonous substance, especially arsenic trioxide, formerly used as rat poison
rat poison, esp. arsenic trioxide

Examples

The old shed contained a box labeled ratsbane, a dangerous substance once used to control rodent populations.
In many Victorian mysteries, ratsbane appears as the poison of choice for sinister plots.
Farmers in the past would sometimes sprinkle ratsbane around barns to keep pests at bay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsenic - a poisonous chemical element historically used as a poison
  • Synonym: poison - a substance that causes harm or death when absorbed or ingested

Etymology

From rat + bane ('poison'), with 'bane' meaning a cause of death or destruction.
