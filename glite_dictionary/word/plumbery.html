plumbery

1. NOUN

/ ˈplʌm.bɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈplʌm.bəri / (BrE)
the trade or occupation of a plumber, involving the installation and repair of pipes and fixtures for water, gas, or sewage in buildings
the occupation of a plumber
archaic

Examples

He learned the art of plumbery from his father, who worked in the trade all his life.
With the development of indoor sanitation, plumbery became an increasingly important profession in the city.
The house’s original plumbery dated back a century and required extensive renovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plumbing - the system and work of fitting pipes in buildings

Etymology

From Old French 'plomberie', from Latin 'plumbum' (lead), referring to lead pipes used in plumbing.
