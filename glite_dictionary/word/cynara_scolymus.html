Cynara scolymus

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnɛɹə skoʊˈlaɪməs / (AmE), / sɪˈnɑːrə skɒlˈiːməs / (BrE)
A Mediterranean thistle-like perennial plant widely cultivated for its large edible flower head, commonly known as the globe artichoke.
Mediterranean plant; globe artichoke

Examples

The fields outside Rome are famous for growing Cynara scolymus for the local market.
Researchers studied the nutritional properties of Cynara scolymus extracts in their latest publication.
He planted several Cynara scolymus in his garden to enjoy fresh artichokes in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artichoke - edible flower head of Cynara scolymus

Etymology

From Latin, Cynara (the plant genus) and scolymus (thistle-like plant).
