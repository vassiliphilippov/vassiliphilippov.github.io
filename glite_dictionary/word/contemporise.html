to contemporise

1. VERB

/ kənˈtɛmpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / kənˈtɛmpərʌɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or represent events as occurring at the same time; to make events contemporaneous, especially in historical or narrative accounts
arrange events as contemporaneous
british

Examples

The scholars attempted to contemporise the major events in ancient Egypt and Greece.
It is difficult to contemporise myths from different cultures due to varying timelines.
Historians often contemporise events to create a coherent narrative.

Synset Examples

synchronize biblical events

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to synchronize - to occur at the same time or cause to occur at the same time

Etymology

From Latin 'contemporarius' (contemporary) + '-ise'.
