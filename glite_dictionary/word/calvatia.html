Calvatia

1. NOUN

/ kælˈveɪʃiə / (AmE), / kalˈveɪtɪə / (BrE)
a genus of puffball fungi whose outer covering breaks into angular pieces at maturity to release spores
a genus of puffball fungi

Examples

The giant puffball belongs to the Calvatia genus, known for its large, round fruiting bodies.
Scientists discovered a rare species of Calvatia during their field research in the grasslands.
Spore dispersal in Calvatia is triggered when its casing breaks apart at maturity.

Etymology

From Latin 'calvatus' (made bald), referring to the bald appearance of the fungi.

2. NOUN

/ kælˈveɪʃi.ə / (AmE), / kalˈveɪtɪə / (BrE)
a genus of puffball fungi whose outer covering breaks into angular pieces at maturity to expose the spores
a genus of puffball fungi

Examples

Several species of Calvatia can be found on grassy fields during late summer and autumn.
The giant puffball, or Calvatia gigantea, is known for its impressive size.
Researchers studied how Calvatia releases spores when its outer casing splits apart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puffball - a type of fungus that releases spores in a cloud when mature
  • Degree Lower: Lycoperdon - a related genus of puffball fungi

Etymology

From Latin 'calvus' (bald), referencing the bare appearance of these puffballs.
