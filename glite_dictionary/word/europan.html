Europan

1. NOUN

/ juˈroʊpən / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpən / (BrE)
an artificial language proposed to serve as an auxiliary language for use among Europeans
proposed auxiliary European language

Examples

Some linguists have studied Europan as a potential common language for the European Union.
The grammar of Europan was designed to be simple and easy to learn.
Few people today are fluent in Europan, as it was never widely adopted.

Etymology

Formed from 'Europe' + the suffix '-an', denoting relation to Europe.
