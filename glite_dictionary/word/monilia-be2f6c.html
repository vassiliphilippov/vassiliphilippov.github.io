monilia

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈnɪliə / (AmE), / məˈnɪlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of yeastlike fungi, some species of which can cause plant diseases or human infections.
genus of yeastlike fungi
medical

Examples

Several species of monilia can infect soft fruits and cause rot.
Some infections in humans are attributed to fungi from the monilia genus.
Gardeners may struggle with monilia affecting their crops during humid seasons.

Etymology

From New Latin Monilia, based on Latin 'monile' meaning necklace, referring to the chain-like appearance of the spores.
