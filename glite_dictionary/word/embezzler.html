embezzler

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈbɛzlər / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛzlə / (BrE)
a person who dishonestly takes money that they have been trusted with, especially from their employer or an organization
person who steals entrusted money

Examples

The company's accountant turned out to be an embezzler who had siphoned funds for years.
When the embezzler was caught, the missing money was finally recovered.
They trusted her entirely until the audit revealed she was an embezzler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thief - a person who steals, especially secretly or without open force
  • Synonym: to embezzle - to steal or fraudulently appropriate money entrusted to one's care

Etymology

From 'embezzle', from Old French 'embesiller' (to steal), itself perhaps from a misdivision of 'en' + 'besillier' (to torment, destroy).
