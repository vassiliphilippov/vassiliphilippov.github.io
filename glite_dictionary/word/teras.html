teras

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛr.əs / (AmE), / ˈtɛː.rəs / (BrE)
(medicine) A grossly malformed and usually nonviable fetus.
malformed, nonviable fetus (medical)
medical

Examples

The autopsy revealed that the stillborn infant was a teras, exhibiting severe deformities across multiple organ systems.
Rarely, a teras can offer insights into the mechanisms behind congenital abnormalities.
Historical medical literature often described cases of teras in the context of early embryological studies.

Etymology

From Greek *teras* (τέρας), meaning 'monster' or 'wonder'.
