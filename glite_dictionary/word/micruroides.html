Micruroides

1. NOUN

/ mɪˌkruːrɔɪˈdiːz / (AmE), / mɪˌkruːrɔɪˈdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small, venomous coral snakes native to North America, belonging to the family Elapidae.
genus of North American coral snakes

Examples

The genus Micruroides contains the species commonly known as the Sonoran coral snake.
Researchers studied the venom composition of several Micruroides specimens collected in Arizona.
Unlike some other coral snakes, Micruroides tend to be smaller in size and display more vivid coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral snake - a type of venomous snake with bright bands

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from micr- (small) + Greek oura (tail) + -oidēs (resembling).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkrʊˈrɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌmɪkrʊˈrɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of coral snakes native to North America, known for their distinctive ringed coloration.
a genus of North American coral snakes

Examples

The Arizona coral snake is the only species belonging to the genus Micruroides.
Scientists recently studied the venom composition of Micruroides for its potential medical applications.
Unlike other genera of coral snakes, Micruroides contains just one recognized species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral snake - a venomous snake with brightly colored bands

Etymology

From Greek mikros (small) + eidos (form or resemblance).
