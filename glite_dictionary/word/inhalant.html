inhalant

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈheɪlənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈheɪlənt / (BrE)
a medication or substance designed to be taken into the body by breathing it in, typically through the nose or mouth
medication or substance taken by inhalation
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an inhalant to help relieve his asthma symptoms.
Using an inhalant can deliver medication quickly to the lungs.
Some allergy medications are administered as an inhalant rather than pills.

Etymology

From Latin 'inhalare' meaning 'to breathe in'.

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈheɪlənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈheɪlənt / (BrE)
a substance or preparation that is taken into the body by inhaling, especially as a medication or drug
substance taken by inhalation
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an inhalant to help manage her asthma symptoms.
Glues and other household products can be abused as inhalants because of their fumes.
Many allergy medications are now available as inhalant sprays for quick relief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerosol - a substance enclosed under pressure and released as a fine spray by means of a propellant gas
  • Antonym: exhalant - a substance that is exhaled or given off from the body, especially as a vapor or gas

Etymology

From Latin *inhalare* (to breathe in), by analogy with 'exhalant.'
