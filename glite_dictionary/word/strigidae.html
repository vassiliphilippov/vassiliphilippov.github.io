Strigidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪdʒɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈstrɪdʒɪˌdiː / (BrE)
The family of typical owls, comprising most species of owls except barn owls; members are nocturnal birds of prey.
family of typical owls

Examples

The family Strigidae includes familiar owls like the great horned owl and the tawny owl.
Unlike Tytonidae, most species in the Strigidae family have a round facial disk.
Researchers observed that Strigidae are found on every continent except Antarctica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: owl - nocturnal bird of prey with a flat face and large eyes
  • Antonym: Tytonidae - family of barn owls, distinct from Strigidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Strigidae, derived from Strix (an owl genus) + -idae (denoting animal families).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪdʒɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈstrɪdʒɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of nocturnal birds of prey, commonly known as true owls, distinguished by their rounded heads and forward-facing eyes.
family of true owls (birds)

Examples

Many species in the Strigidae family are found throughout forests worldwide.
Researchers studied the hunting behaviors of several Strigidae members during the night.
Barn owls belong to the Tytonidae family, not Strigidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: owl - a nocturnal bird of prey with a flat face and large forward-facing eyes

Etymology

From Latin *Strix*, *Strigis* (owl), used in zoological taxonomy.
