Minnesota

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɪnɪˈsoʊtə / (AmE), / ˌmɪnɪˈsəʊtə / (BrE)
A state in the northern central region of the United States, known for its lakes and forests.
a U.S. state in the Midwest
american

Examples

After moving to Minnesota, she quickly adapted to the cold winters.
The Mississippi River begins in Minnesota at Lake Itasca.
Many lakes in Minnesota attract tourists during the summer months.

Etymology

From a Dakota Sioux word meaning 'sky-tinted water' or 'clear blue water'.
