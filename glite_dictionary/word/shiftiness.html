shiftiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪftinəs / (AmE), / ˈʃɪftinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being changeable or unpredictable, especially in direction.
quality of being changeable or unpredictable

Examples

The shiftiness of the wind made sailing much more difficult.
Pilots must account for the shiftiness of air currents during takeoff.
The weather's shiftiness forced the organizers to postpone the outdoor event.

Synset Examples

the shiftiness of the wind caused the boat to veer unpredictably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instability - state of being likely to change or not stay the same

Etymology

From 'shifty' + '-ness'; 'shifty' from shift (verb) + -y (adjective suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪftinəs / (AmE), / ˈʃɪftinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being deceitful, evasive, or suspiciously untrustworthy in manner or appearance
dishonest or evasive quality

Examples

His shiftiness made it hard for anyone to fully trust him.
The detective immediately sensed shiftiness in her evasive answers.
There was a certain shiftiness in the way he avoided eye contact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceitfulness - the quality of being dishonest or intending to mislead
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere

Etymology

From 'shifty' (mid-16th century, meaning 'given to trickery or evasion') + '-ness'.

Synset POS: n
