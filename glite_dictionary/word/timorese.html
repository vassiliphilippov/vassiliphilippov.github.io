Timorese

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɪməˈriːz / (AmE), / ˌtɪməˈriːz / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Timor, or a person of Timorese descent
person from Timor

Examples

The Timorese have a rich cultural heritage influenced by their island's diverse history.
Many Timorese sought refuge abroad during the conflict.
A group of young Timorese attended the international conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: East Timorese - a native or inhabitant of East Timor

Etymology

From 'Timor', the name of the island, with the English adjectival/noun suffix '-ese'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌtɪməˈriːz / (AmE), / ˌtɪməˈriːz / (BrE)
of or relating to Timor or its people.
relating to Timor or its people

Examples

She prepared a traditional Timorese meal for her family.
The Timorese landscape is known for its lush mountains and pristine beaches.
Many Timorese people speak both Tetum and Portuguese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Timor - an island at the southern end of Maritime Southeast Asia

Etymology

From Timor + -ese, a common suffix for demonyms (as in 'Chinese', 'Japanese').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02826574-a
Synset POS: a
