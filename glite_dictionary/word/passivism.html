passivism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpasɪˌvɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The belief or doctrine that all forms of violence are unjustifiable.
doctrine rejecting all violence

Examples

He adopted passivism as a response to the growing militarism in his country.
Her commitment to passivism sometimes made her unpopular during heated political debates.
The movement's philosophy was rooted in passivism and absolute nonviolence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pacifism - the belief that war and violence are unjustifiable; opposition to war

Etymology

Formed from 'passive' + '-ism', modeled on analogous words denoting beliefs or doctrines.
