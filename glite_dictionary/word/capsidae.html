Capsidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæpˈsɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌkæpˈsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, soft-bodied insects, commonly known as leaf bugs or plant bugs, that feed on plant juices.
family of leaf bugs

Examples

The Capsidae are known for their ability to cause damage to cotton and other crops.
Many species in the Capsidae family display bright colors and patterns on their bodies.
Researchers collected several Capsidae specimens from the fields for further study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Miridae - the family of plant bugs, another name for Capsidae

Etymology

From New Latin Capsidae, from the genus name Capsus.
