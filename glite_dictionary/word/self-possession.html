self-possession

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf pəˈzɛʃən / (AmE), / sɛlf pəˈzɛʃən / (BrE)
calm and confident control of your feelings and behavior, especially in difficult situations
calm control of oneself

Examples

Even when criticized harshly, she maintained remarkable self-possession.
He answered all the questions with self-possession, impressing the entire panel.
Moments of crisis often test one's self-possession and character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: composure - calmness and self-control, especially in a difficult situation
  • Synonym: confidence - a feeling or belief that you can do something well
  • Antonym: panic - sudden uncontrollable fear or anxiety

Etymology

Formed from 'self' and 'possession', modeled after the concept of possessing one's own faculties.
