accumulative

1. ADJ

/ əˈkjumjəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / əˈkjuːmjʊlətɪv / (BrE)
marked by or relating to accumulating or amassing something over time
relating to accumulating or amassing

Examples

We live in an accumulative society that values the collection of wealth and resources.
Her accumulative approach to knowledge helped her become an expert in several fields.
The accumulative effects of stress can have a significant impact on your health over time.

Synset Examples

we live in an accumulative society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cumulative - increasing or increased in quantity by successive additions
  • Antonym: dissipative - tending to disperse or dissipate rather than accumulate

Etymology

From Latin accumulātīvus, from accumulāre (to pile up).

2. ADJ

/ əˈkjuːmjəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / əˈkjuːmjʊlətɪv / (BrE)
increasing or growing by successive additions or accumulation
increasing by successive addition

Examples

The accumulative effect of poor diet and lack of exercise can lead to serious health issues.
Interest on the account is accumulative, making it wise to leave your money invested.
The damage from repeated stress is accumulative rather than immediate.

Synset Examples

the benefits are cumulative
the eventual accumulative effect of these substances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cumulative - increasing by gradual additions over time

Etymology

From Latin 'accumulare' (to heap up) via Medieval Latin 'accumulativus'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00048680-s
Synset POS: s
