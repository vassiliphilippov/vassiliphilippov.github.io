caesural

1. ADJ

/ siˈzʊrəl / (AmE), / siˈzjʊərəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a caesura, a pause or break in a line of verse
relating to a caesura in poetry

Examples

The poet used a caesural pause to emphasize the emotion in the line.
Scholars have debated the significance of caesural patterns in classical poetry.
His analysis focused on the caesural structure of the sonnet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caesura - a pause or break in a line of verse

Etymology

From 'caesura', from Latin 'caesura' (a cutting), plus the suffix '-al'.
