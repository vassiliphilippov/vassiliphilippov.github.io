victual

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪtəl / (BrE)
food, or any substance that can be used as food; provisions or nourishment.
food; provisions; nourishment

Examples

During the journey, they packed enough victuals to last for two weeks.
The old sailors relied heavily on salted meat and dried beans as their main victuals.
He visited the market every morning to buy fresh victual for his family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: food - substance eaten to provide nutritional support

Etymology

From Middle English *vitaille*, from Old French *vitailles*, from Latin *victualia* (provisions), from *victus* (livelihood, food).
