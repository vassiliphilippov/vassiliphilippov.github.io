illusive

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈluːsɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈluːsɪv / (BrE)
having the nature of an illusion; deceptive or misleading as if unreal.
having the nature of an illusion

Examples

The treasure hunters chased after illusive riches that existed only in legends.
Her illusive dreams of fame distracted her from practical opportunities.
The magician's illusive tricks amazed the audience and left them wondering what was real.

Synset Examples

illusive hopes of finding a better job
Secret activities offer presidents the alluring but often illusory promise that they can achieve foreign policy goals without the bothersome debate and open decision that are staples of democracy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusory - based on illusion; not real
  • Antonym: real - actually existing as a thing or occurring in fact; not imagined or supposed

Etymology

From Latin illus-, past participial stem of 'illudere' (to mock, deceive), plus '-ive'.
