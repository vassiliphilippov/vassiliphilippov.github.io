Nuphar sagittifolium

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːfɑːr sæˌdʒɪtəˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / ˈnjuːfɑː sæˌdʒɪtɪˈfəʊlɪəm / (BrE)
A species of yellow water-lily native to flowing waters in the southeastern United States, known for forming obstructive mats in streams.
a species of yellow water-lily of the southeastern US

Examples

In early summer, Nuphar sagittifolium can be seen covering portions of the river with its broad leaves.
Environmentalists are concerned that Nuphar sagittifolium may block water flow in certain southeastern streams.
Researchers study how Nuphar sagittifolium affects native aquatic life in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nuphar - a genus of aquatic plants also known as yellow water-lilies

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Nuphar' from Arabic 'nūfar', and species epithet 'sagittifolium' meaning 'arrow-shaped leaves' from Latin 'sagitta' (arrow) and 'folium' (leaf).
