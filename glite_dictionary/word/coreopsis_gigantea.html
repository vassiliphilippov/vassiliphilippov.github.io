Coreopsis gigantea

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˌriːˈɑːpsɪs dʒaɪˈgæn.ti.ə / (AmE), / ˌkɒr.iˈɒp.sɪs dʒɪˈɡæn.ti.ə / (BrE)
A large treelike shrub native to coastal southern California, with feathery leaves and clusters of large yellow flower heads.
large shrub with yellow flowers, southern California

Examples

The sandy bluffs were covered in blooming Coreopsis gigantea throughout the spring.
Many gardeners in California appreciate Coreopsis gigantea for its ability to thrive in coastal conditions.
Wildlife often visits the showy yellow flowers of Coreopsis gigantea along the Pacific shoreline.

Etymology

Coreopsis from Greek 'koris' (bug) and '-opsis' (appearance), describing the seed shape; gigantea from Latin, meaning 'giant'.
