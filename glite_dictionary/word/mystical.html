mystical

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪstɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪstɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to mysticism or having a spiritual, mysterious, or supernatural quality.
relating to mysticism; spiritual or mysterious

Examples

The ancient text is full of mystical teachings and hidden meanings.
She described a mystical experience she had while meditating in the mountains.
The ceremony had a mystical atmosphere that fascinated all the participants.

Synset Examples

mystical religion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiritual - relating to or affecting the human spirit or soul
  • Antonym: rational - based on or in accordance with reason or logic

Etymology

From Greek mystikos (secret, mystical), from mystēs (initiated person).

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɪstɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪstɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to mysticism or the belief in the existence of realities beyond perceptual or intellectual apprehension, often involving spiritual insight or intuition.
relating to mysticism or spiritual insight

Examples

She often spoke of her mystical experiences during meditation.
The novel explores a mystical interpretation of nature and the universe.
Many cultures have mystical traditions passed down through generations.

Synset Examples

mystical intuition
mystical theories about the securities market

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiritual - relating to the spirit or soul rather than material things
  • Antonym: rational - based on reason or logic rather than mysticism

Etymology

From Greek mystikos (secret, mystic), via Latin mysticus and Old French mistique.
