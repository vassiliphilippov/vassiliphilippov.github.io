to scarper

1. VERB

/ ˈskɑːrpər / (AmE), / ˈskɑːpə / (BrE)
to run away or leave a place quickly, often to escape trouble; flee
run away quickly; flee
informal british

Examples

As soon as they heard the sirens, the thieves decided to scarper down the alley.
We'd better scarper before anyone notices we're here.
The fox managed to scarper across the field just in time.

Synset Examples

If you see this man, run!
The burglars escaped before the police showed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to run off - leave a place quickly, especially to escape
  • Synonym: to flee - run away from danger or unwanted situation
  • Antonym: to linger - to stay somewhere longer than necessary

Etymology

British slang, 19th century; possibly from Italian 'scappare' (to escape), via London thieves' cant.
