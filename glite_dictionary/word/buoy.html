buoy

1. VERB

/ ˈbuː.i / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪ / (BrE)
to keep someone or something afloat or prevent from sinking
keep afloat; prevent sinking

Examples

The life jacket buoyed her until help arrived.
Hope buoyed the stranded sailors as they awaited rescue.
The floatation device buoyed him in the rough water.

Synset Examples

The life vest buoyed him up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: float - to stay on the surface of a liquid and not sink

Etymology

From Middle French 'bouee', from Old French 'boie' (fetters; buoy), possibly from Latin 'boia' (collar, shackle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01221076-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈbu.i, ˈbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪ / (BrE)
to float or stay on the surface of water; to keep something afloat
float or stay afloat on water

Examples

The life jacket will buoy you if you fall into the water.
Her hopes were buoyed by the good news from her doctor.
The small boat managed to buoy through the waves after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: float - to rest or move on the surface of a liquid without sinking

Etymology

From Old French bouee (float), probably of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01908788-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈbuː.i, ˈbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪ, ˈbʊ.i / (BrE)
an anchored floating object used as a marker to indicate channels, hazards, or navigational points in a body of water
floating marker on water

Examples

A red buoy marked the edge of the safe swimming area near the beach.
The sailor steered the boat carefully between the green and red buoys in the harbor.
Divers located the wreck site thanks to a yellow buoy floating above it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beacon - a fire or light set up in a high or prominent position as a warning or signal

Etymology

From Middle French *bouee* (float, buoy), from Old French *boie* (fetter, shackle), ultimately of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07280883-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈbuː.i, boʊ̯.i / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪ / (BrE)
to mark the position of something in water with a buoy
mark an area in water with a buoy

Examples

The coastguard will buoy the dangerous rocks to warn passing ships.
They need to buoy the channel so that boats can navigate safely.
Workers buoyed the wreck site before starting the salvage operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mark - to indicate or show the position of something
