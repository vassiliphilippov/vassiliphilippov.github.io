tunica conjunctiva bulbi

1. NOUN

/ ˈtjuː.nɪ.kə kənˈdʒʌŋk.tɪ.və ˈbʌl.baɪ / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.nɪ.kə kənˈdʒʌŋk.tɪ.və ˈbʊl.baɪ / (BrE)
the part of the conjunctiva that covers the front surface of the sclera and the epithelial layer of the cornea in the eye
membrane covering front of eyeball
medical

Examples

Damage to the tunica conjunctiva bulbi can result in irritation and redness of the eye.
Scientists studied the healing process of the tunica conjunctiva bulbi after surgery.
Inflammation of the tunica conjunctiva bulbi often accompanies conjunctivitis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjunctiva - the mucous membrane that covers the front of the eye and lines the inside of the eyelids

Etymology

From Latin: tunica (coat) + conjunctiva (joined together) + bulbi (of the eyeball).
