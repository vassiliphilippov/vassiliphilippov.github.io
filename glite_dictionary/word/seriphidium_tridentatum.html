Seriphidium tridentatum

1. NOUN

/ sɛˌrɪfɪdiəm traɪˈdɛntətəm / (AmE), / sɛˌrɪfɪdiəm traɪˈdɛntətəm / (BrE)
An aromatic shrub native to arid regions of western North America, known for its hoary (grayish-white) leaves.
aromatic North American shrub

Examples

Seriphidium tridentatum thrives in dry, rocky soils throughout the western United States.
The hoary leaves of Seriphidium tridentatum distinguish it from other similar shrubs.
Botanists gathered samples of Seriphidium tridentatum for their research on native plant species.

Etymology

From scientific Latin; Seriphidium is a genus name, tridentatum is Latin for 'three-toothed', referring to leaf shape.
