Gasterosteus aculeatus

1. NOUN

/ gæstɚˈɑːstiəs ækjuˈleɪətəs / (AmE), / gastəˈɒstɪəs ækjuˈleɪətəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the three-spined stickleback, a small fish found in rivers and coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere
a species of small fish (three-spined stickleback)

Examples

The Gasterosteus aculeatus is an important model organism in evolutionary biology.
You can often find the Gasterosteus aculeatus in shallow, brackish waters during spring.
Researchers studied the behavioral patterns of Gasterosteus aculeatus in freshwater streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: three-spined stickleback - a small fish species with three spines on its back

Etymology

From Greek 'gaster' (stomach, belly) and Latin 'aculeatus' (spiny or prickly).
