cornflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A plant of southern and southeastern United States, grown for its yellow flowers that can be dried.
plant with yellow flowers (U.S.)

Examples

The cornflower is often used in dried floral arrangements because of its vibrant yellow petals.
She planted cornflowers along the edge of her garden to add some color in late summer.
You can find cornflower seeds at most gardening stores in the southern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bachelor's button - European plant with bright blue flowers, also called cornflower

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a slender annual plant (Centaurea cyanus) of the daisy family, native to Europe, with bright blue, purple, pink, or white flowers, often grown as an ornamental.
a blue-flowered annual Eurasian plant

Examples

She picked a cornflower from the field for her bouquet.
The vivid blue petals of the cornflower brightened the garden.
Cornflowers are popular in wildflower mixtures because of their color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bachelor's button - another name for cornflower, especially in North America

Etymology

Named for its frequent presence in European cornfields; from Old English 'corn' + 'flower'.
