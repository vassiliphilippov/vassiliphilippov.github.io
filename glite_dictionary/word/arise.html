to arise

1. VERB

/ əˈraɪz / (AmE), / əˈraɪz / (BrE)
to take part in a rebellion or revolt against authority; to renounce former allegiance
rebel; renounce allegiance

Examples

The villagers decided to arise against the oppressive regime.
When injustice became unbearable, many citizens arose and fought for their freedom.
He urged his followers to arise and reclaim their independence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebel - to rise in opposition or armed resistance to an established government or leader
  • Antonym: to submit - to accept or yield to a superior force or to the authority of another person
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈraɪz / (AmE), / əˈraɪz / (BrE)
(of smoke, mist, etc.) to move or go upward into the air.
move upward (smoke, mist, etc.)

Examples

As the sun warmed the field, a gentle mist began to arise above the grass.
Thick smoke could be seen arising from the distant volcano after the eruption.
Early morning fog arose from the river, shrouding the valley in mystery.

Synset Examples

The fog lifted
The smoke arose from the forest fire
The mist uprose from the meadows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rise - to move upward; to go up

Etymology

From Old English ārīsan (to rise up).

/ əˈraɪz / (AmE), / əˈraɪz / (BrE)
to come into existence; to begin to appear or develop
come into existence; begin to appear

Examples

A number of problems can arise when working with new technology.
