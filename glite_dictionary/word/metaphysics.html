metaphysics

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪks / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪks / (BrE)
the branch of philosophy that studies the fundamental nature of reality, existence, and knowledge
branch of philosophy about existence and reality

Examples

He has devoted his life to the study of metaphysics and the nature of reality.
The lecture on metaphysics was both challenging and fascinating to the students.
Debates in metaphysics often touch upon questions of existence and consciousness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ontology - the philosophical study of the nature of being
  • Synonym: epistemology - the philosophical study of knowledge

Etymology

From Greek 'metaphysika', meaning 'the things after the Physics', used by Aristotle's editors.
