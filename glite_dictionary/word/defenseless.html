defenseless

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfɛnsləs / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛnslɪs / (BrE)
lacking protection or shelter; exposed to harm or danger without any means of defense
without protection or shelter

Examples

Without the heavy overcoat, the soldier felt defenseless against the biting wind.
Young seedlings are defenseless against grazing animals until they grow stronger.
Stripped of their protective shells, the crabs were left defenseless on the shore.

Synset Examples

naked to mine enemies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unprotected - not protected, lacking defense or shelter
  • Synonym: vulnerable - susceptible to physical or emotional harm

Etymology

From Latin defens-, past participle stem of defendere (to defend), with the suffix -less.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈfɛnsləs / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛnsləs / (BrE)
without protection or the ability to resist attack or harm
without protection or defense

Examples

After the storm, the town was left completely defenseless against further flooding.
The kitten looked defenseless as it huddled in the corner.
Without her older brother around, she felt defenseless on the playground.

Synset Examples

a defenseless child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulnerable - exposed to the possibility of being attacked or harmed
  • Antonym: protected - kept safe from harm or injury

Etymology

From 'defense' + '-less', meaning without defense.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02534191-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈfɛnsləs / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛnsləs / (BrE)
without protection or the means to defend oneself, especially against harm or attack
without protection from harm or attack

Examples

The small bird was defenseless against the hungry cat.
Many refugees felt defenseless after losing their homes and possessions.
Without weapons or allies, the village was completely defenseless during the invasion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unprotected - not protected or shielded from harm
  • Synonym: vulnerable - susceptible to physical or emotional harm
  • Antonym: protected - shielded from harm or danger
  • Antonym: armed - equipped with weapons

Etymology

From 'defense' + '-less', modeled after similar English formations.

Source: WordNet
