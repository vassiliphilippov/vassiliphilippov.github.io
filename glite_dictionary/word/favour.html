favour

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvə / (BrE)
A small gift given to a guest at a party, especially as a souvenir to thank them for attending.
small gift for party guests
british

Examples

Each wedding guest received a lovely favour as a token of appreciation.
The children took home colourful favours from the birthday party.
At the dinner, every place setting had a small favour tied with a ribbon.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪvər / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvə / (BrE)
an act of kindness done for someone without expecting anything in return
an act of kindness for someone

Examples

Could you do me a favour and check this letter for errors?
She offered to babysit as a favour to her friend.
He asked for no favour in return for his help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate
  • Antonym: disfavour - state of being out of favour or not being liked or approved

Etymology

From Old French 'faveur', from Latin 'favorem' (good will, kindness).
