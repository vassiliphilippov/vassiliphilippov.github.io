Basidiomycetes

1. NOUN

/ bəˌsɪdioʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / bəˌsɪdiəʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
A large class of higher fungi characterized by producing spores on basidia, including mushrooms, puffballs, and shelf fungi.
class of higher fungi

Examples

Many edible mushrooms belong to the Basidiomycetes class of fungi.
The study focused on the reproductive cycles of Basidiomycetes in temperate forests.
Shelf fungi, a subgroup of Basidiomycetes, play a key role in decomposing wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Basidiomycota - the subdivision of fungi containing Basidiomycetes

Etymology

From modern Latin Basidiomycetes, from basidium (spore-bearing structure) + Greek mykēs (fungus).

2. NOUN

/ bəˌsɪdioʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / bəˌsɪdɪəʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
A large class of higher fungi that includes mushrooms, toadstools, puffballs, and shelf fungi; coextensive with the subdivision Basidiomycota.
a large class of higher fungi

Examples

Most familiar mushrooms belong to the Basidiomycetes.
Researchers discovered several new species of Basidiomycetes in the forest.
The structure of Basidiomycetes distinguishes them from other classes of fungi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Basidiomycota - the subdivision of fungi that includes Basidiomycetes

Etymology

From modern Latin Basidiomycetes, from basidium (a small pedestal, referring to a reproductive structure) and -mycetes (Greek for 'fungus').
