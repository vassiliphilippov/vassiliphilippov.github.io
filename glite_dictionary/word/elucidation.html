elucidation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌluːsɪˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌluːsɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something clear; an explanation that clarifies or sheds light on a subject
clear explanation or clarification

Examples

The professor's elucidation of the theory made it easy for the students to understand.
Her detailed elucidation of the process clarified several points for the team.
Without further elucidation, the instructions remained too vague for the participants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explanation - a statement that makes something clear
  • Antonym: obfuscation - the act of making something unclear or confusing

Etymology

From Latin 'elucidatio', from 'elucidare' (to make clear, explain).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌluːsɪˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌluːsɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
an explanation or clarification that makes something easier to understand
making something clear or understandable

Examples

The teacher's elucidation of the complex theory made it much easier to grasp.
Without further elucidation, the instructions remained confusing to most students.
The elucidation provided in the appendix resolved all of my remaining questions.

Synset Examples

the professor's clarification helped her to understand the textbook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explanation - a statement that makes something clear
  • Synonym: clarification - the act of making something clearer

Etymology

From Latin 'elucidatio' (an explanation), from 'elucidare' (to make clear).
