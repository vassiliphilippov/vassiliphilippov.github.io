Richard the Lionheart

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪtʃərd ðə ˈlaɪənˌhɑrt / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃəd ðə ˈlaɪənhɑːt / (BrE)
(1157–1199) King of England from 1189 to 1199, noted for his role as a leader in the Third Crusade and for his reputation as a warrior.
King of England, Third Crusade leader

Examples

Richard the Lionheart is often remembered as one of England's greatest warrior-kings.
During the Third Crusade, Richard the Lionheart gained fame for his bravery and military leadership.
Legends about Richard the Lionheart have inspired numerous tales of chivalry and heroism throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Richard I - Formal name of Richard the Lionheart, King of England
  • Degree Lower: King John - Successor and brother of Richard the Lionheart
