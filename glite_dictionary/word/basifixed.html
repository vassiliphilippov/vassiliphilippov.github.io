basifixed

1. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪsɪˌfɪkst / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsɪˌfɪkst / (BrE)
(of anthers) attached by the base to the filament or stalk.
attached by the base (botany)

Examples

In many lilies, the anthers are basifixed, connecting directly to the filament at their base.
Some flowering plants exhibit basifixed anthers, while others have different points of attachment.
The scientist noted that the pollen sacs were basifixed, which is typical for the species studied.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: versatile - (of anthers) attached at or near the middle, able to swing freely

Etymology

From Latin 'basis' (base) + 'fixed' (attached).
