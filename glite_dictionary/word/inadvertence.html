inadvertence

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.ədˈvɝː.təns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ədˈvɜː.təns / (BrE)
the quality of being inattentive or neglectful, resulting in forgetting or ignoring one's responsibilities
inattentiveness to duties or responsibilities

Examples

His financial mistake was caused by sheer inadvertence rather than intentional wrongdoing.
The loss of important files was due to inadvertence, not malice.
She apologized for her inadvertence in forgetting the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care in doing something
  • Antonym: attentiveness - paying close attention to something

Etymology

From Latin 'inadvertentia', from 'in-' (not) + 'advertere' (to turn towards).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.ədˈvɝː.təns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ədˈvɜː.təns / (BrE)
an unintentional omission or error resulting from a failure to pay attention
unintentional omission; lack of attention

Examples

The error in the report was due to inadvertence rather than intentional misconduct.
Because of her inadvertence, an important deadline was missed.
The contract was voided on the grounds of inadvertence by one of the parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care, especially resulting in damage
  • Antonym: attentiveness - the quality of paying close attention

Etymology

From Latin 'inadvertentia' (heedlessness), from 'in-' (not) + 'advertere' (to turn toward).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
