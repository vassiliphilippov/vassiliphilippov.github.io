ancestry

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.sɛs.tri / (AmE), / ˈæn.sə.stri / (BrE)
the family or genetic line of descent; the people from whom one is descended
family lineage or descent

Examples

Her strong sense of ancestry inspired her to research her family's history.
They can trace their ancestry back to the 16th century.
Understanding one's ancestry can provide valuable insights into genetic health risks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lineage - direct line of descent from an ancestor
  • Synonym: descent - the origin or background of a person in terms of family or nationality
  • Antonym: progeny - descendants or offspring

Etymology

From Old French 'ancestre', from Latin 'antecessor' (one who goes before).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæn.sɛs.tri / (AmE), / ˈæn.ses.tri / (BrE)
the family or genetic descent of a person; a line of ancestors
family or genetic descent

Examples

She was curious about her family's ancestry and spent years researching it.
His ancestry can be traced back to 18th-century pioneers.
They discovered that their royal ancestry included several famous figures.

Synset Examples

his entire lineage has been warriors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lineage - direct descent from an ancestor; ancestry or pedigree
  • Antonym: descendant - a person who is descended from a particular ancestor

Etymology

From Old French *ancestree*, from Latin *antecessor* (predecessor, ancestor).
