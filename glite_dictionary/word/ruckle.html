to ruckle

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈrʌkəl / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to make a hoarse, rattling sound, especially in the throat or chest.
make a hoarse, rattling sound

Examples

The old man's breath began to ruckle ominously in his chest.
Late at night, the dog would ruckle softly as it slept by the fire.
He feared she would ruckle with every breath until morning.

2. VERB

/ ˈrʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈrʌkəl / (BrE)
to make wrinkles or folds in something, especially by pressing, creasing, or gathering it
make wrinkles or folds

Examples

She tried not to ruckle the tablecloth as she smoothed it over the table.
Be careful not to ruckle your shirt while ironing it.
The movement of the bed sheets would often ruckle the blanket at the foot of the bed.
