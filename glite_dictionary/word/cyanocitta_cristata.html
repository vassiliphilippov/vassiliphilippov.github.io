Cyanocitta cristata

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌænoʊˈsɪtə krɪsˈteɪtə / (AmE), / sʌɪˌanəʊˈkɪtə krɪsˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
The scientific name for the blue jay, a bright blue bird with a grey breast found in eastern North America.
scientific name for the blue jay

Examples

The Cyanocitta cristata is easily recognized by its distinctive blue plumage.
Bird watchers in the eastern United States often hope to spot a Cyanocitta cristata during their outings.
A Cyanocitta cristata perched on the branch, its grey breast standing out against the blue sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue jay - common name for Cyanocitta cristata, a bright blue bird with a grey breast

Etymology

From Greek 'kyanos' (dark blue) + 'kitta' (jay), and Latin 'cristatus' (crested).
