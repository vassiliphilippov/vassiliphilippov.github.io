aboulic

1. ADJ

/ əˈbuːlɪk / (AmE), / əˈbjuːlɪk / (BrE)
Suffering from abulia; showing an abnormal inability to act or make decisions.
affected by pathological indecisiveness
medical

Examples

The patient appeared aboulic, unable to take even small actions without prompting.
His aboulic state made daily tasks almost impossible to manage independently.
After the stroke, she became noticeably aboulic, struggling to make the simplest choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abulic - Alternate spelling for 'aboulic', meaning suffering from abulia
  • Antonym: decisive - Able to make decisions quickly and confidently

Etymology

From abulia, from Greek a- (without) + boulē (will, decision).
