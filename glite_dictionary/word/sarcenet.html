sarcenet

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːrsənət / (AmE), / ˈsɑːsɪnɪt / (BrE)
A fine, soft silk fabric, often used for linings and delicate garments.
fine soft silk fabric
archaic

Examples

The dress was lined with luxurious sarcenet for extra comfort.
She wore a bonnet trimmed with blue sarcenet ribbons.
In the eighteenth century, sarcenet was prized for its smooth, delicate texture.

Etymology

From Old French 'sarziné', from 'sarrazin' (Saracen), reflecting the fabric's eastern origin.
