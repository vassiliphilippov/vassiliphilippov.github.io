vagrant

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪɡrənt / (AmE), / ˈveɪɡrənt / (BrE)
a person without a settled home or regular work who wanders from place to place, typically living by begging
person who wanders without a home or job

Examples

The police arrested the vagrant who had been sleeping in the park for several nights.
She offered some food to a vagrant standing at the corner, hoping to help.
A group of vagrants gathered under the bridge seeking shelter from the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tramp - person who travels from place to place, especially without a home or job
  • Synonym: vagabond - person who wanders from place to place without a permanent home

Etymology

From Latin 'vagrans' (wandering), present participle of 'vagari' (to wander).

2. ADJ

/ ˈveɪɡrənt / (AmE), / ˈveɪɡrənt / (BrE)
continually changing, especially by moving from place to place or from one occupation to another; wandering or unsettled
continually changing or wandering

Examples

The vagrant nature of his career meant he rarely stayed in one job for long.
During the 1960s, many vagrant youths traveled across the country in search of new experiences.
Her thoughts took a vagrant course, drifting from one subject to another.

Synset Examples

a drifting double-dealer
the floating population
vagrant hippies of the sixties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wandering - traveling aimlessly from place to place
  • Antonym: settled - not moving or changing; having a fixed home or routine

Etymology

From Latin 'vagrans', present participle of 'vagari' (to wander).
