oblivious

1. ADJ

/ əˈblɪviəs / (AmE), / əˈblɪvɪəs / (BrE)
not aware of or not concerned about what is happening around one; failing to notice or remember something.
unaware; failing to notice

Examples

She was so oblivious to the noise that she continued reading her book.
He walked down the street, oblivious to the rain soaking his clothes.
Despite the warning signs, the hikers remained oblivious to the approaching storm.

Synset Examples

forgetful of her responsibilities
oblivious old age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forgetful - lacking the ability to remember
  • Antonym: aware - knowing or perceiving; having knowledge of something

Etymology

From Latin 'obliviosus' (forgetful), from 'oblivio' (forgetfulness).

2. ADJ

/ əˈblɪviəs / (AmE), / əˈblɪviəs / (BrE)
not aware of or not concerned about what is happening around one; lacking conscious awareness of something, typically followed by 'to' or 'of'.
not aware of something

Examples

He walked through the crowded room, completely oblivious to the curious stares he received.
The driver was so distracted that she seemed oblivious of the traffic light turning red.
Children are often oblivious to the worries that trouble adults.

Synset Examples

oblivious of the mounting pressures for political reform
oblivious to the risks she ran
not unmindful of the heavy responsibility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unaware - not aware; lacking knowledge or awareness
  • Antonym: mindful - conscious or aware of something

Etymology

From Latin 'obliviosus', from 'oblivio' (forgetfulness).
