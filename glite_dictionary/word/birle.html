to birle

1. VERB

/ bɜːrl / (AmE), / bɜːl / (BrE)
to cause a floating log to spin or rotate by treading on it, especially as a skill or competition in logging.
to spin a floating log by treading on it

Examples

He managed to birle the log so smoothly that his competitor fell off almost immediately.
The lumberjacks birled against each other in a lively competition at the festival.
Learning how to birle takes balance, quick feet, and plenty of practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to birl - to spin or rotate, especially a log in water, by treading on it

Etymology

Possibly a variant of 'birl', from Scots, meaning 'to spin or whirl'.
