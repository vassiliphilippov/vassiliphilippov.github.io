espial

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈspaɪəl / (AmE), / ɪˈspaɪəl / (BrE)
the act of detecting, observing, or catching sight of something or someone; observation or notice.
the act of observing or detecting
archaic

Examples

After hours of espial, the detective finally saw the suspect emerge from the building.
The scout’s careful espial revealed the movement of enemy troops in the valley.
Her sudden espial of a rare bird delighted all the members of the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: observation - the act of watching or monitoring something carefully
  • Synonym: detection - the action or process of identifying the presence of something

Etymology

From Old French *espier* (to watch, spy).
