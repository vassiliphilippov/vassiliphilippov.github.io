pargetry

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑɹ.dʒɪ.tri / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.dʒɪ.tri / (BrE)
decorative or ornamental plasterwork applied to walls or ceilings, especially on the exterior of buildings
ornamental plasterwork

Examples

The intricate pargetry on the building's facade attracted the attention of many tourists.
Restorers spent months repairing the damaged pargetry in the historic manor house.
He admired the elaborate pargetry that decorated the walls of the old cathedral.

Etymology

From Old French 'pargeter' (to throw on plaster) + English suffix '-ry'.
