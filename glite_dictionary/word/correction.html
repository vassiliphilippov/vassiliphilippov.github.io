correction

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / kəˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
something that is used to replace or fix an error; the act or result of correcting a mistake.
something replacing an error

Examples

She submitted a correction after realizing her initial report contained an error.
The newspaper published a correction for the misspelled name in yesterday's article.
Please make any necessary correction to the document before sending it out.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mistake - an error or incorrect action
  • Synonym: amendment - a minor change or improvement

Etymology

From Latin 'correctio', from 'corrigere' meaning 'to make straight, put right'
