patrikin

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætrɪkɪn / (AmE), / ˈpætrɪkɪn / (BrE)
A person related to someone on their father's side of the family.
relative on father's side

Examples

In traditional societies, land sometimes passes only to a man's patrikin rather than to his children.
Anthropologists studied inheritance patterns among a group where patrikin held special privileges.
She visited her patrikin every summer to learn about her paternal heritage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matrikin - relative on the mother's side

Etymology

Formed from Latin 'pater' (father) and English suffix '-kin' (relative or family).
