amour propre

1. NOUN

/ æˌmur ˈproʊpər / (AmE), / aˌmuːə ˈprɒprə / (BrE)
A sense of one's own worth; self-respect or self-esteem, sometimes seen as excessive pride.
self-esteem; sense of self-worth

Examples

Many artists possess a strong sense of amour propre that drives them to pursue perfection.
His amour propre was wounded when his expertise was questioned in front of everyone.
She disguised her amour propre as humility, but it was clear she took great pride in her work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pride - a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction from achievements
  • Antonym: humility - the quality of having a modest view of one's importance
  • Degree Higher: arrogance - an attitude of superiority to others

Etymology

From French 'amour-propre' (self-love), from 'amour' (love) and 'propre' (own, proper).
