coltsfoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊltsˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈkɒltsˌfʊt / (BrE)
a perennial herb with spikes of tiny white flowers and glossy green round or heart-shaped leaves, which turn coppery, maroon, or purplish in autumn
a perennial herb with white flowers and rounded leaves

Examples

The leaves of the coltsfoot turn a purplish color in the fall.
Gardeners admire the coltsfoot for its unique round leaves and delicate white flowers.
You can often find coltsfoot growing in damp woodlands and along riverbanks.

Etymology

Named for the shape of its leaf, which resembles a colt's foot.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊltsˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈkɒltsˌfʊt / (BrE)
a perennial herb with large rounded leaves resembling a colt's foot and yellow flowers appearing before the leaves; native to Europe but now widespread; formerly used in medicine
herbaceous plant with hoof-shaped leaves and yellow flowers

Examples

In early spring, coltsfoot blooms brighten the roadsides before any leaves appear.
Herbalists once used coltsfoot as a remedy for coughs and other respiratory ailments.
The large, hoof-shaped leaves make coltsfoot easy to identify among wild plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tussilago - the genus to which coltsfoot belongs

Etymology

Named for the shape of its leaves, which resemble the foot of a colt (a young horse).
