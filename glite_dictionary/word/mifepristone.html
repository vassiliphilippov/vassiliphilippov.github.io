mifepristone

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪfəˈprɪstoʊn / (AmE), / ˌmɪfəˈprɪstəʊn / (BrE)
A synthetic steroid drug used to induce abortion, especially in the early stages of pregnancy, by blocking the action of progesterone.
abortion-inducing medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed mifepristone as part of a medical abortion regimen.
Mifepristone is most effective when taken within the first weeks of pregnancy.
Researchers studied the long-term effects of mifepristone on women's health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: RU-486 - Trade name for mifepristone, abortion-inducing drug

Etymology

From International Nonproprietary Name (INN), formed from elements related to its chemical structure and function.
