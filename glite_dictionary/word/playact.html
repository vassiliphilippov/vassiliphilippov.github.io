to playact

1. VERB

/ ˈpleɪˌækt / (AmE), / ˈpleɪˌækt / (BrE)
to perform a role as an actor, especially on a stage or in a play; to act theatrically
perform theatrically as an actor

Examples

She loves to playact with her friends, pretending to be different characters.
He started to playact in school plays when he was just eight years old.
During the workshop, the participants were encouraged to playact various dramatic scenarios.

Synset Examples

She acts in this play
He acted in ‘Julius Caesar’
I played in ‘A Christmas Carol’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act - perform a role in a play or film

Etymology

Formed from 'play' + 'act', both of Old English origin.
