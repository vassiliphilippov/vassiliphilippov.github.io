crimson

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪm.zən / (AmE), / ˈkrɪm.zən / (BrE)
(literary) Marked by violence, bloodshed, or cruelty; associated with blood or killing.
relating to bloodshed or violence (literary)

Examples

The general's crimson ambitions led to years of war and suffering.
In her poem, she evokes a crimson past filled with upheaval and loss.
The history of the kingdom is stained with many crimson episodes of conquest and rebellion.

Synset Examples

writes of crimson deeds and barbaric days
fann'd by Conquest's crimson wing
convulsed with red rage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloody - involving or characterized by bloodshed

Etymology

From Old Spanish *cremesín*, from Arabic *qirmizī* (crimson, from kermes insect producing red dye).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪmzən / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmzən / (BrE)
a deep, vivid red color, often with a hint of purple
a deep red color

Examples

The sun set in a sky streaked with crimson and gold.
She wore a dress of rich crimson to the gala.
His cheeks flushed a deep crimson when he realized his mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarlet - a bright red color with a slightly orange tinge
  • Synonym: vermilion - a brilliant red or scarlet color
  • Degree Lower: red - the color at the end of the visible spectrum next to orange

Etymology

From Old Spanish 'carmesí', from Arabic 'qirmizī' (crimson), from Persian 'ghermez' (red).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04970765-n
Synset POS: n

/ ˈkrɪmzən / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmz(ə)n / (BrE)
(of the face) appearing red or flushed, especially as a result of strong emotion or physical exertion.
red or flushed in the face

Examples

Her cheeks turned crimson with embarrassment when she realized her mistake.
He became crimson with anger after hearing the unfair accusation.
After the race, his face was crimson from exertion.

4. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪm.zən / (AmE), / ˈkrɪm.zən / (BrE)
of a deep, rich red color, often resembling the shade of blood, cherries, or rubies.
deep, rich red color

Examples

Her cheeks turned crimson with embarrassment as everyone looked at her.
The sunset bathed the sky in a stunning crimson glow.
He wore a crimson scarf that stood out against his black coat.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: scarlet - bright red color with a slightly orange tinge
  • Degree Lower: vermilion - vivid red to reddish-orange color
  • Degree Higher: burgundy - dark red color, similar to wine

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'crimsonus', from Arabic 'qirmiz' (cochineal dye).

5. VERB

/ ˈkrɪm.zən / (AmE), / ˈkrɪm.zən / (BrE)
(literary) to become red in the face, especially from embarrassment or shame.
to become red, especially from embarrassment

Examples

She began to crimson when all eyes turned towards her.
He crimsoned at the praise lavished on him during the ceremony.
The boy crimsoned with embarrassment after forgetting his lines on stage.

Synset Examples

The girl blushed when a young man whistled as she walked by

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blush - to become red in the face from shyness, embarrassment, or shame

Etymology

From Old Spanish *carmesí*, from Arabic *qirmizī* (cochineal), ultimately from Sanskrit *kṛmija* (produced by a worm or insect).
