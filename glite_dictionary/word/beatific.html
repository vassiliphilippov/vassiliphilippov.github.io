beatific

1. ADJ

/ biˈæt̬ɪfɪk / (AmE), / biːˈætɪfɪk / (BrE)
experiencing or showing great joy or bliss, especially in a holy or serene way
showing or experiencing blessed joy

Examples

She gazed at him with a beatific smile, radiating pure happiness.
After the meditation retreat, he felt a beatific sense of calm wash over him.
The nun's face took on a beatific expression during the prayer service.

Synset Examples

beatific peace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blissful - experiencing or showing perfect happiness

Etymology

From Latin beatificus, from beatus (blessed) + facere (to make).

2. ADJ

/ biˈætɪfɪk / (AmE), / biːˈætɪfɪk / (BrE)
showing great joy, serenity, or blessedness; resembling or befitting a saint or angel
showing blissful or saintly joy

Examples

She greeted him with a beatific smile that lit up her face.
The monk sat in meditation, a beatific expression on his face.
His beatific demeanor brought comfort to everyone around him.

Synset Examples

angelic beneficence
a beatific smile
a saintly concern for his fellow men
my sainted mother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blissful - full of joy or happiness
  • Synonym: serene - calm, peaceful, and untroubled
  • Antonym: anguished - experiencing severe mental or physical pain

Etymology

From Latin beatificus, from beatus (blessed) + facere (to make).
