Eolic

1. NOUN

/ iˈɑlɪk / (AmE), / iˈəʊlɪk / (BrE)
the dialect of Ancient Greek spoken in Thessaly, Boeotia, and Aeolis
Ancient Greek dialect (Thessaly, Boeotia, Aeolis)

Examples

The poem was written in the Eolic dialect, reflecting its Boeotian origins.
Scholars study Eolic for its unique linguistic features within Ancient Greek.
Both Thessalian and Aeolian inscriptions are often classified as Eolic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aeolic - Alternative spelling for the same dialect of Ancient Greek

Etymology

From Latin 'Aeolicus', from Greek 'Aiolikos', relating to Aeolis, an ancient region of Asia Minor.
