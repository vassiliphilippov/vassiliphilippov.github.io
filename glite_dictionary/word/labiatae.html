Labiatae

1. NOUN

/ ˌleɪ.biˈeɪ.tiː / (AmE), / ˌleɪ.bɪˈeɪ.tiː / (BrE)
a former scientific name for the family of aromatic herbs and shrubs, now usually called Lamiaceae, which includes mints, sages, and similar plants
former botanical family name; the mint family

Examples

Many culinary herbs, such as basil and mint, belong to the Labiatae family.
Thyme, rosemary, and sage are all members of the Labiatae.
Botanists often refer to the Labiatae as the mint family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lamiaceae - current scientific name for the mint family

Etymology

From Latin 'labium' (lip), referring to the lip-shaped flowers.
