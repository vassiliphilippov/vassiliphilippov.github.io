deutzia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɪtsiə / (AmE), / ˈdjuːtsiə / (BrE)
A shrub of the genus Deutzia, typically having toothed opposite leaves, shredding bark, and loose clusters of white or pink flowers.
ornamental flowering shrub (Deutzia genus)

Examples

The deutzia in our garden blooms with delicate white flowers every spring.
She planted a deutzia hedge along the path for its attractive pink blossoms.
The gardener recommended deutzia for its resilience and beautiful clusters of flowers.

Etymology

Named after Johann van der Deutz, an 18th-century Dutch patron of botany.
