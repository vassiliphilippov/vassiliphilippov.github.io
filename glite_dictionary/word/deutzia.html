Deutzia

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊtsiə / (AmE), / ˈdɔːtsiə / (BrE)
A genus of ornamental, mostly deciduous shrubs native to Asia and Central America, widely cultivated for decorative purposes.
genus of ornamental shrubs

Examples

The Deutzia in her garden blooms profusely every spring.
Gardeners often choose Deutzia for its attractive, showy flowers.
Several species of Deutzia are commonly found in botanical collections worldwide.

Etymology

Named after Johan van der Deutz (1743–1788), Dutch patron of botany.
