vassal

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæsəl / (AmE), / ˈvasəl / (BrE)
A person in the Middle Ages who held land from a feudal lord and owed them allegiance and service in return.
feudal subordinate; landholder under a lord

Examples

During the Middle Ages, a vassal was expected to provide military service to his lord.
The king granted the land to his loyal vassal in exchange for fealty.
A vassal was bound by an oath to support his feudal superior in all conflicts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liege - a feudal lord entitled to allegiance and service

Etymology

From Medieval Latin vassallus (subject, servant), from Late Latin vassus (servant, retainer), of Celtic origin.
