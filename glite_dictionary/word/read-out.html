read-out

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːdˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈriːdˌaʊt / (BrE)
An electronic device or display that presents information in a readable form, often in the form of numbers, text, or graphical data.
electronic information display

Examples

The instrument’s read-out showed the current temperature in large digits.
She watched the digital read-out as the machine measured her blood pressure.
The new system features a touch-screen read-out for easier data entry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: display - a device or screen that shows information visually

Etymology

Formed from the verb phrase 'read out,' referring to presentation of information in a visible form.

2. NOUN

/ ˈridˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈriːdˌaʊt / (BrE)
information or output displayed in readable form, especially by a computer, electronic device, or measuring instrument.
displayed output from a device or computer

Examples

The heart monitor showed a digital read-out of the patient's pulse.
She glanced at the read-out to check the current settings.
After running the experiment, the read-out displayed the results instantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: output - data or information produced by a computer or device

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'read out', indicating information being 'read' and 'output' by a device.

3. NOUN

/ ˈriːdˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈriːdˌaʊt / (BrE)
information displayed or recorded by an electronic device, such as on a screen or printout
information shown on a device

Examples

The laboratory technician checked the read-out on the monitor to confirm the test results.
A digital thermometer typically gives a read-out in both Celsius and Fahrenheit.
The machine triggered an alarm and produced an error read-out on the control panel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: display - information presented visually on a device

Etymology

Formed from the verb phrase 'read out,' referring to reading information (out) from a system.
