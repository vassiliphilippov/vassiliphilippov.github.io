lath

1. NOUN

/ læθ / (AmE), / lɑːθ / (BrE)
a narrow, thin strip of wood used as a backing for plaster or in making latticework
a thin strip of wood for backing or latticework

Examples

The old house had wooden lath and plaster walls.
He nailed each lath carefully to form the base for the stucco exterior.
The gardener built a trellis using several lath strips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slat - a thin, narrow strip of wood or metal, often used in building

Etymology

From Old English *læð* (a lath, slat), related to Old Norse *latr* (lath, slat).
