ahem

1. NOUN

/ əˈhɛm / (AmE), / əˈhɛm / (BrE)
the sound made by clearing one’s throat, used to attract attention, express hesitation, or suggest embarrassment
sound made to get attention or express hesitation

Examples

She gave an ahem to signal she wanted to speak.
With a polite ahem, the teacher got the students’ attention.
He let out an awkward ahem when he forgot his line.
