index

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌdɛks / (AmE), / ˈɪndɛks / (BrE)
a numerical scale used to compare values, often against a reference point, to measure changes in a variable or group of variables
numerical scale for comparison

Examples

The Consumer Price Index is often used to track inflation rates over time.
Scientists use an air quality index to report pollution levels to the public.
Stock market indexes can provide insight into the performance of the overall economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indicator - something that indicates or points to a condition or trend

Etymology

From Latin 'index' (sign, pointer, indicator).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13873264-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌdɛks / (AmE), / ˈɪndɛks / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A number or symbol written as a superscript to indicate how many times a quantity is multiplied by itself; an exponent.
mathematical exponent notation

Examples

In the expression x⁵, the index is 5, showing that x is multiplied by itself five times.
The index in a radical, such as the cube root, tells you how many times the base is used as a factor.
Raising a number to a negative index gives the reciprocal of the base raised to the positive power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exponent - the number indicating repeated multiplication of a base

Etymology

From Latin 'index' meaning 'indicator, list' via mathematical and scientific uses.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06825275-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌdɛks / (AmE), / ˈɪn.deks / (BrE)
An alphabetical list, at the end of a book or document, of names and subjects with the page numbers where they are mentioned.
alphabetical list of contents in a book

Examples

All the key terms in the textbook are listed in the index at the back.
She turned to the index to find which pages discussed climate change.
Without an index, it would be much harder to locate specific information in this book.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: table of contents - a list at the front of a book showing chapters and page numbers
  • Degree Lower: glossary - an alphabetical list of terms with definitions in a book

Etymology

From Latin 'index' (forefinger, pointer, list), from 'indicare' (to point out).

Source: WordNet
