unpassable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈpæsəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈpɑːsəbəl / (BrE)
(of a road, path, or area) not able to be crossed or traveled through.
not able to be crossed or traveled through

Examples

Heavy snow made the mountain road unpassable for several days.
The river became unpassable after the bridge collapsed in the flood.
After the storm, fallen trees left the hiking trail unpassable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impassable - not able to be traveled on or through

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'passable' (able to be passed).
