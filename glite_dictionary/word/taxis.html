taxis

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæk.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈtaksɪs / (BrE)
the surgical procedure of manually restoring a displaced body part to its proper position, often used in cases such as hernias or dislocations.
manual restoration of displaced body part
medical

Examples

The surgeon performed a taxis to correct the patient's umbilical hernia.
Prompt taxis can help avoid the need for more invasive surgery in some dislocations.
Manual taxis is sometimes attempted before recommending surgical intervention.

Etymology

From Greek *taxis* (arrangement, order).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæk.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈtæk.sɪs / (BrE)
the movement of an organism in response to a stimulus, typically toward or away from it
movement of an organism in response to a stimulus

Examples

Certain bacteria exhibit taxis by moving toward sources of nutrients.
Phototaxis is a form of taxis where movement is guided by light.
The scientist observed negative taxis as the organisms moved away from the chemical.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tropism - growth or turning movement of a biological organism in response to an environmental stimulus

Etymology

From Greek táxis (arrangement, order).
