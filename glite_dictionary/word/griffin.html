griffin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪf.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪf.ɪn / (BrE)
a mythical creature with the head and wings of an eagle and the body of a lion
mythical creature: eagle head, lion body

Examples

Legends say the griffin guarded treasures deep within the mountains.
The coat of arms featured a majestic griffin above the shield.
In ancient art, a griffin is often depicted as a symbol of strength and protection.

Etymology

From Old French *griffon*, from Latin *gryphus*, from Greek *gryps* (curved, hooked, griffin).
