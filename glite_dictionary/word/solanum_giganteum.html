Solanum giganteum

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈleɪnəm dʒaɪˈgæn.ti.əm / (AmE), / səˈleɪnəm dʒɪˈɡæn.tɪ.əm / (BrE)
A woolly-stemmed shrub native to tropical Africa and southern Asia, with silvery-white prickly branches, clusters of blue or white flowers, and bright red berries resembling those of holly.
a woolly-stemmed shrub of Africa and Asia

Examples

The Solanum giganteum is known for its bright red berries that closely resemble those of a holly bush.
Gardeners in tropical climates sometimes plant Solanum giganteum as an ornamental shrub for its striking appearance.
Researchers studied the growth conditions of Solanum giganteum in southern Asia.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Solanum' (a genus of plants including nightshades), 'giganteum' meaning giant or gigantic.
