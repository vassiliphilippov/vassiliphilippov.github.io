river

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A large natural stream of water flowing toward an ocean, a lake, or another such stream.
a large natural stream of water

Examples

The Amazon is the largest river in the world by discharge volume of water.
A small boat was anchored near the river bank, gently rocking with the current.
Many cities have grown along the river because of easy access to water and transportation.

Synset Examples

the river was navigable for 50 miles

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: creek - a small stream, smaller than a river
  • Degree Lower: stream - a small, narrow river
  • Degree Lower: brook - a very small, often shallow stream

Etymology

From Latin 'ripa' (bank, shore) via Old French 'rivere' (riverbank).
