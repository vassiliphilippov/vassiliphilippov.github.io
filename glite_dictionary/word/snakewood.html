snakewood

1. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪkˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈsneɪkwʊd / (BrE)
an East Indian climbing shrub with twisted limbs and roots that resemble serpents
East Indian shrub with snake-like roots

Examples

The snakewood in the garden grew rapidly, winding its limbs around the trellis.
Botanists are fascinated by the twisted roots of snakewood resembling serpents.
Villagers used the durable wood of the snakewood shrub for making walking sticks.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪkˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈsneɪk.wʊd / (BrE)
a tropical American tree (Brosimum guianense) with large peltate leaves and hollow stems, valued for its hard, patterned wood.
tropical American tree with patterned wood

Examples

The craftsman used snakewood to carve the handle of the luxury walking cane.
Many violin bows are prized when they are made from rare snakewood.
Some antique furniture features inlays of snakewood due to its decorative grain.

Etymology

Named for the wood’s characteristic snake-like markings. Sometimes applied to various unrelated trees with similar wood patterns.
