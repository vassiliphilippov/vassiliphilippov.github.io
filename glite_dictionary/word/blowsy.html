blowsy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbloʊzi / (AmE), / ˈbləʊzi / (BrE)
(of a woman or her appearance) untidy, coarse, or having an unkempt or slovenly look.
untidy or slovenly in appearance (esp. of a woman)

Examples

She looked rather blowsy after an afternoon spent gardening in the wind.
The actress played a blowsy barmaid in the period drama.
Despite her blowsy appearance, she was kind-hearted and cheerful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slatternly - untidy and dirty through habitual neglect
  • Antonym: neat - tidy and in order

Etymology

Perhaps from 'blowze,' an archaic word for a untidy woman, origin uncertain.
