cantor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.tɔr / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tɔː / (BrE)
an official of a synagogue who leads the liturgical part of the service and chants prayers intended for solo performance
leader of synagogue prayers

Examples

The cantor began to chant the opening prayer as the congregation listened in silence.
During the ceremony, the cantor led the congregation in a moving recital of ancient hymns.
Each Friday night, the cantor plays an essential role in the synagogue service.

Etymology

From Latin 'cantor' (singer), from 'canere' (to sing); also used in medieval Christian liturgy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.tɔr / (AmE), / ˈkæn.tɔː / (BrE)
the leader or musical director of a choir, especially in a church or synagogue
musical director of a choir

Examples

The cantor led the congregation in song during the service.
After years of training, she became the cantor for her local synagogue.
The cantor selected a range of hymns for the choir to perform at Easter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choirmaster - the director of a choir

Etymology

From Latin cantor (singer), from cantare (to sing).
