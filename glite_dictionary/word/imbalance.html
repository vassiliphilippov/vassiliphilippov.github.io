imbalance

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / ɪmˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
A lack of proportion or equality between different parts or aspects of something.
lack of proportion or equality

Examples

The imbalance of power in the region has led to years of conflict.
A diet with a severe imbalance of nutrients can cause health problems.
There is a clear gender imbalance in the engineering department.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: balance - a state where different things occur in equal or proper amounts

Etymology

From im- (not) + balance; modeled after French 'imbalance'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / ɪmˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
A lack of balance or symmetry; a state in which two or more things are unequal or not in proper proportion.
lack of balance or equality

Examples

The report highlighted the imbalance between supply and demand in the housing market.
A poor diet can lead to a nutritional imbalance affecting overall health.
There is an obvious imbalance in the distribution of resources among the regions.

Synset Examples

a hormonal imbalance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: balance - a state where different elements are equal or in the correct proportions

Etymology

From im- (not) + balance, based on Late Latin 'im-' and 'balantia'.
