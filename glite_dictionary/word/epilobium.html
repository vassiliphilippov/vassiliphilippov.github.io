Epilobium

1. PROPN

/ ˌɛpɪˈloʊbiəm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈləʊbiəm / (BrE)
A large genus of flowering plants in the willowherb family, found especially in western North America and Arctic regions.
genus of flowering plants (willowherbs)

Examples

Epilobium species are commonly known as willowherbs or fireweeds.
Botanists found a new population of Epilobium in the alpine meadows.
Gardeners often plant Epilobium for its delicate pink flowers.

Etymology

From Greek epi (upon) + lobos (pod, capsule), referring to the position of the stigma on the ovary.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈloʊbiəm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈləʊbiəm / (BrE)
A large and widely distributed genus of herbs and subshrubs, especially found in western North America and Arctic regions.
genus of herbs/subshrubs (willowherbs)

Examples

Several species of Epilobium thrive along the riverbanks in Alaska.
The gardener planted Epilobium to attract butterflies and bees to the yard.
Botanists have documented more than a dozen Epilobium species in the western United States.

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + lobos (pod, capsule), referring to the position of the flower on the fruit capsule.
