overmantel

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈmæntəl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈmæntl̩ / (BrE)
A decorative structure or shelf situated above a mantelpiece, often used to support mirrors, artworks, or ornaments.
shelf or structure above a mantelpiece

Examples

The ornate overmantel displayed a large, gilded mirror above the fireplace.
She placed family photos on the overmantel to decorate the living room.
The Victorian house featured an elaborate wooden overmantel with carved floral designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantelpiece - a structure of wood, marble, or stone above and around a fireplace

Etymology

From 'over-' meaning 'above' and 'mantel', referring to a fireplace mantel.
