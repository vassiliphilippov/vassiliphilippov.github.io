KB

1. NOUN

/ keɪ bi / (AmE), / keɪ bi / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1024 bytes, commonly used to measure computer memory or storage
unit of digital information (1024 bytes)

Examples

My email attachment was only 250 KB, so it uploaded quickly.
The document size is less than one KB, making it easy to send via text.
Each photo takes up about 300 KB of space on my phone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kilobyte - a unit of digital information equal to 1024 bytes
  • Degree Higher: MB - a unit of digital information equal to 1024 kilobytes
  • Degree Lower: byte - a unit of digital information typically consisting of 8 bits

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'kilobyte', from Greek 'chilioi' (thousand) + 'byte'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkeɪˈbi / (AmE), / ˌkeɪˈbiː / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1,000 bytes, commonly used to measure data size in computer science.
unit of digital information, 1,000 bytes

Examples

This file is only 200 KB, so it should download quickly.
The document must not exceed 500 KB in size to be uploaded.
The email attachment was too large at 1,200 KB and could not be sent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kilobyte - a unit of information equal to 1,000 bytes
  • Degree Higher: MB - a unit of digital information equal to 1,000,000 bytes
  • Degree Lower: byte - a unit of digital information typically representing one character

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'kilobyte', from 'kilo-' (thousand) and 'byte'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13649142-n
Synset POS: n
