ravenously

1. ADV

/ ˈrævənəsli / (AmE), / ˈravənəsli / (BrE)
In a very hungry or eager manner; with great appetite or desire.
in a very hungry or eager way

Examples

She ate the sandwich ravenously, as if she hadn't eaten all day.
The stranded hikers drank water ravenously after being rescued.
He devoured the novel ravenously, eager to reach the final chapter.

Synset Examples

he pounced on the food hungrily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hungrily - in a way that shows great hunger or desire

Etymology

From Latin 'rapina' (plundering) via Old French 'ravineux' (greedy, ravenous), relating to 'ravenous'.
