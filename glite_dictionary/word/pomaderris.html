Pomaderris

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈmædərɪs / (AmE), / pəʊˈmadərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs and small trees native to Australasia, belonging to the buckthorn family (Rhamnaceae).
genus of Australasian shrubs and trees

Examples

Several species of Pomaderris are cultivated for their attractive foliage and flowers.
Pomaderris shrubs are commonly found in Australian coastal forests.
Botanists have identified new species within the Pomaderris genus in recent years.

Etymology

From Greek 'poma' (cover, lid) + 'derris' (skin, hide), referring to the structure of the fruit.
