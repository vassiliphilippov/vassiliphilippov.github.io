Flindersia schottiana

1. NOUN

/ flɪnˈdɜrziə ʃɒtˈiænə / (AmE), / flɪnˈdɜːziə ʃɒtˈiænə / (BrE)
an Australian timber tree, also known as silver ash, whose bark yields a poison
Australian timber tree; bark yields poison

Examples

The timber of Flindersia schottiana is valued for its strength and attractive appearance.
Indigenous Australians once used extracts from the bark of Flindersia schottiana for their toxic properties.
Many gardens and parks in Queensland feature Flindersia schottiana as an ornamental tree.

Etymology

Genus name after explorer Matthew Flinders; species from botanist Heinrich W. Schott.
