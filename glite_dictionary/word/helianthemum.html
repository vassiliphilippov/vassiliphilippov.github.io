Helianthemum

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛli.ænˈθɛməm / (AmE), / ˌhiːli.ænˈθɛməm / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen or semi-evergreen small shrubs, widely distributed in America, Europe, North Africa, Asia Minor, and central Asia, known commonly as rock roses.
genus of small evergreen or semi-evergreen shrubs

Examples

Many gardeners choose to grow Helianthemum for its colorful spring blooms and drought resistance.
There are over seventy species within the genus Helianthemum, each adapted to different climates.
The rocky slopes of the Mediterranean region are often covered in wild Helianthemum.

Etymology

From Greek 'hēlios' (sun) + 'anthemon' (flower), meaning 'sunflower'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhiːliˈænθəməm / (AmE), / ˌhiːliˈænθɪməm / (BrE)
A genus of small flowering plants, commonly known as rockroses, found in dry, rocky regions and alpine meadows.
genus of flowering plants (rockroses)

Examples

Several species of Helianthemum grow wild across the Mediterranean region.
Gardeners often plant Helianthemum for its bright, sun-loving flowers.
The rocky hillside was covered in blooming Helianthemum during the spring.

Etymology

From Greek 'hēlios' (sun) + 'anthemon' (flower).
