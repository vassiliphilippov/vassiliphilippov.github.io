scabious

1. NOUN

/ ˈskeɪ.bi.əs / (AmE), / ˈskeɪ.bi.əs / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Scabiosa, typically with heads of blue, purple, or white flowers, commonly found in meadows and grasslands
flowering plant of the genus Scabiosa

Examples

The wild scabious adds splashes of purple to the summer meadow.
She planted several scabious in her cottage garden for their delicate beauty.
Bees are especially attracted to the nectar-rich scabious flowers.

Etymology

From Latin scabiosus, from scabies (itch), due to traditional medicinal use against skin diseases.
