stigma

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪɡmə / (AmE), / ˈstɪɡmə / (BrE)
an external opening of the tracheal system in a terrestrial arthropod, allowing for the exchange of gases
external opening in an arthropod's respiratory system

Examples

Air enters the insect's body through special stigma on the sides of its thorax.
The scientist located the stigma that led into the tracheal respiratory system.
Damage to the stigma can interfere with the insect's breathing process.

Etymology

From Greek *stigma* (mark, puncture), originally indicating a point or mark, later adopted in biological terminology.

4. NOUN

/ ˈstɪɡmə / (AmE), / ˈstɪɡmə / (BrE)
(Botany) The part of the pistil at the tip of the style where pollen is received and germinates.
pollen-receiving part of a flower pistil

Examples

The stigma of the lily is specially adapted to catch and retain pollen grains.
During pollination, pollen lands on the flower’s stigma before growing a tube down to the ovary.
A sticky stigma increases the chances of successful fertilization in many plant species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: style - the slender stalk in a flower pistil connecting the stigma to the ovary
  • Synonym: pistil - the female reproductive part of a flower

Etymology

From Latin stigma (mark, spot), from Greek stigma (a mark, puncture).
