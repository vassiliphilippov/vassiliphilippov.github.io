Samarcand

1. PROPN

/ ˌsæmɑrˈkænd / (AmE), / ˌsæməˈkænd / (BrE)
A city in southern Uzbekistan, historically known as the opulent capital of Tamerlane in the 14th century.
historic city in Uzbekistan

Examples

The ancient city of Samarcand was once a thriving center on the Silk Road.
Tales of Samarcand's splendour have inspired poets and travelers for centuries.
Visitors to Samarcand marvel at its stunning Islamic architecture and vibrant bazaars.

Etymology

From Persian سمرقند (Samarqand), the historical name of the city.
