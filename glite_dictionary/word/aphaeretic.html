aphaeretic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæfərˈɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌæfəˈrɛtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or formed by aphaeresis; involving the omission of one or more sounds or letters at the beginning of a word.
relating to aphaeresis (initial sound omission)

Examples

The word 'phone' is an aphaeretic form of 'telephone'.
An aphaeretic change can alter the original sound structure of a word.
Linguists studied several aphaeretic forms found in colloquial speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphaeresis - the omission of one or more sounds or letters at the beginning of a word

Etymology

From Greek *aphairetikos* (able to take away), from *aphairein* (to take away).
