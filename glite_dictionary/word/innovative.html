innovative

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪnəˌveɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈɪnəvətɪv / (BrE)
introducing or using new ideas, methods, or products that are different from those used before
introducing new ideas or methods

Examples

The company is known for its innovative approach to solving problems.
She developed an innovative technique that changed the way people think about design.
Employers value candidates who bring innovative ideas to their teams.

Synset Examples

stylistically innovative works
innovative members of the artistic community
a mind so innovational, so original

Related Terms

  • Synonym: original - new and not derived from something else
  • Antonym: conventional - based on what is traditionally done; not new or original

Etymology

From Latin 'innovare' (to renew), via English 'innovate'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪnəˌveɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈɪnəvətɪv / (BrE)
introducing or using new ideas, techniques, or methods; ahead of the times
introducing new ideas or methods

Examples

The company's innovative approach to technology set it apart from competitors.
She developed an innovative curriculum that engaged students in new ways.
Their innovative solutions helped solve long-standing problems in the community.

Synset Examples

the advanced teaching methods
had advanced views on the subject
a forward-looking corporation
is British industry innovative enough?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creative - having the ability to create new ideas or things
  • Antonym: conventional - following traditional forms and genres

Etymology

From Latin innovatus, past participle of innovare (to renew), from in- (into) + novus (new).
