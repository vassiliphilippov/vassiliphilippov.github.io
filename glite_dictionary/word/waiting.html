waiting

1. ADJ

/ ˈweɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) ready and available for use or action.
ready and available

Examples

The soldiers stood waiting for the signal to advance.
There was a fleet of taxis waiting outside the airport terminal.
He found a fresh pot of coffee waiting for him in the kitchen.

Synset Examples

waiting cars and limousines lined the curb
found her mother waiting for them
an impressive array of food ready and waiting for the guests
military forces ready and waiting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ready - fully prepared for action or use
  • Synonym: available - free for use; able to be used or obtained

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of remaining inactive in one place while expecting something to happen or someone to arrive.
the act of remaining inactive while expecting something

Examples

The long waiting tested everyone's patience at the crowded train station.
He grew restless after hours of waiting for a reply to his message.
During the storm, the waiting for news felt endless.

Synset Examples

the wait was an ordeal for him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - the period during which something is delayed
  • Synonym: anticipation - expectation or waiting for something to happen

Etymology

From the verb 'wait', ultimately from Old North French 'waitier' (to watch).
