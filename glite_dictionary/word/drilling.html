drilling

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or act of making holes in a material, especially using a drill
the act or process of making holes

Examples

The drilling of the new well began early in the morning.
Continuous drilling could be heard from the construction site next door.
Oil companies invest heavily in offshore drilling operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boring - the act of making a hole in something using a tool

Etymology

From the verb 'drill' with the -ing suffix forming an action noun.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or process of making a hole in the ground, especially to extract oil or gas
the act of boring into the earth for oil or gas

Examples

The company invested millions in drilling for oil off the coast.
Deep drilling operations can reach oil reserves thousands of meters underground.
Environmental groups closely monitor drilling activities in sensitive areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boring - the act of making a hole by means of a rotating tool

Etymology

From 'drill' (to bore a hole) + -ing (noun-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00924944-n
Synset POS: n
