Sicily

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɪsəli / (AmE), / ˈsɪsəli / (BrE)
an island in the Mediterranean Sea, the largest in the region and an autonomous region of Italy
largest Mediterranean island; region of Italy

Examples

My grandparents emigrated from Sicily in the early 1900s.
We spent our summer vacation touring the beautiful beaches of Sicily.
The cuisine of Sicily is famous for its fresh seafood and rich flavors.

Etymology

From Latin Sicilia, from Greek Sikelia.

2. PROPN

/ ˈsɪsəli / (AmE), / ˈsɪsɪli / (BrE)
an island region of Italy located in the Mediterranean Sea, known for its rich history, culture, and distinctive cuisine
Italian island region in the Mediterranean

Examples

Many tourists visit Sicily each year to experience its beautiful landscapes and historic towns.
The food in Sicily is famous for its fresh seafood and unique flavors.
Mount Etna, one of Europe's highest active volcanoes, is located in Sicily.

Etymology

From Latin Sicilia, from Ancient Greek Sikelía, named after the indigenous Sicels.
