gauge

1. VERB

/ ɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
to determine the capacity, volume, or contents of something by measuring and calculating
measure capacity or volume

Examples

The workers carefully gauged the contents of each storage tank before refilling them.
She used a dipstick to gauge the amount of oil left in the barrel.
Experts gauge the volume of a container to ensure accurate billing.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measure - to ascertain the size, amount, or degree of something by using an instrument or device

Etymology

From Old French *jaugier* 'to standardize measures', of uncertain origin.
