outrider

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌraɪdər / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌraɪdə / (BrE)
a person, especially a member of an escort or vanguard, who rides ahead of a vehicle or group to warn of danger or clear the way
escort who rides ahead

Examples

An outrider led the procession through the crowded streets, clearing the path for the dignitaries.
The caravan hired an experienced outrider to scout the trail ahead for any signs of danger.
During royal tours, outriders are often seen accompanying the official motorcade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escort - a person or group accompanying another for protection or as a mark of rank

Etymology

From out- + rider; first recorded in English in the late 15th century.
