imprecision

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪmprɪˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌɪmprɪˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
The quality or state of being not exact, accurate, or detailed.
lack of exactness or accuracy

Examples

The imprecision of his instructions led to confusion among the team.
In scientific experiments, even a small imprecision can affect the results significantly.
She criticized the imprecision in the report’s data analysis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: precision - the quality of being exact, accurate, and careful
  • Synonym: vagueness - lack of clarity or distinctness

Etymology

From French 'imprécision', from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'precision'.
