to tittivate

1. VERB

/ ˈtɪtəˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɪtɪveɪt / (BrE)
to make oneself or something look neat, smart, or attractive, especially with extra attention to detail
make oneself or something neat or smart

Examples

She decided to tittivate herself before the big meeting.
He always tittivates his desk before important visitors arrive.
They spent the afternoon tittivating the living room for the party.

Synset Examples

Spruce up your house for Spring
titivate the child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spruce up - make someone or something look smarter and tidier
  • Synonym: smart - make neat and stylish in appearance

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly an alteration of 'tidy' with insertion and suffixation, or related to 'titivate.'
