Nummulitidae

1. NOUN

/ nʌmjuːˈlɪtɪˌdiː / (AmE), / nʌmjʊˈlɪtɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of extinct single-celled protoctists known from their fossilized shells, important in paleontology and geology
family of fossil protoctists

Examples

Fossils from the Nummulitidae family are often used to date Eocene sedimentary rocks.
Geologists studied the Nummulitidae to understand ancient marine environments.
Many species in the Nummulitidae are characterized by their coin-shaped shells.

Etymology

From Latin 'nummulites' (coin-shaped fossil) + '-idae' (family suffix in taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ nʌm.jəˈlɪ.tɪˌdiː / (AmE), / nʌm.jʊˈlɪ.tɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of extinct single-celled protoctists, characterized by their large, coin-shaped fossil shells, known mainly from Eocene and Oligocene rocks.
family of fossil protoctists

Examples

The Nummulitidae played a significant role in forming limestone deposits found in parts of Europe and Asia.
Scientists discovered several well-preserved Nummulitidae fossils in the Paleogene strata.
The characteristic coin-shaped shells of the Nummulitidae are easily recognizable under the microscope.

Etymology

From Latin 'nummulites' (a type genus in the family), derived from Latin 'nummulus' (small coin) due to the coin-like shape of the fossils.
