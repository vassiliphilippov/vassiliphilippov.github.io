pry

1. NOUN

/ praɪ / (AmE), / praɪ / (BrE)
a heavy iron lever with one end shaped as a wedge, used for prying or lifting objects
heavy iron lever for prying

Examples

The workers used a pry to lift the heavy concrete slab.
He reached for a pry to open the stubborn crate.
Without a pry, it was nearly impossible to budge the old fence post.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowbar - a heavy iron bar with a flattened end, used as a lever

2. VERB

/ praɪ / (AmE), / praɪ / (BrE)
to try to find out information about someone’s private affairs in a way that is impolite or intrusive
inquire into others' private affairs intrusively

Examples

I wish you wouldn't pry into my personal life; it's really none of your business.
The journalist tried to pry secrets from the celebrity during the interview.
She resented her neighbors who would constantly pry about her relationship status.

Synset Examples

This guy is always nosing around the office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nose - to search or snoop in a meddlesome way
  • Synonym: to meddle - to interfere in something that is not one's concern
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to someone or something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02173563-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ praɪ / (AmE), / praɪ / (BrE)
to use force, often with a tool, in order to move, open, or lift something, especially with difficulty
force open or move with effort

Examples

He used a screwdriver to pry open the stuck window.
After several attempts, they finally pried the old box lid off.
She carefully pried the painting from the wall without damaging the frame.

Synset Examples

The burglar jimmied the lock
Raccoons managed to pry the lid off the garbage pail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jimmy - to force open with a crowbar or similar tool

Etymology

From Old French *praier* or Medieval Latin *pryare* (to prize, force open).

4. VERB

/ praɪ / (AmE), / praɪ / (BrE)
to inquire too closely into someone's private affairs, often without invitation or permission
inquire rudely or nosily

Examples

The reporter tried to pry into the celebrity's personal life.
I wish you wouldn't pry into my phone calls; it's private.
They managed to pry some details about the plan from him after much persuasion.

Synset Examples

They pried the information out of him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probe - to explore or investigate thoroughly
  • Synonym: to intrude - to enter without permission or welcome
  • Antonym: respect - to avoid interfering in or inquiring into one's privacy

Etymology

Possibly from Old French *praier* (to ask), or as a variant of 'peer'.

Examples
