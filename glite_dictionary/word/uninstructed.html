uninstructed

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈstrʌktɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈstrʌktɪd / (BrE)
lacking knowledge, training, or instruction; not having been taught or informed
not taught or informed

Examples

The uninstructed public struggled to understand the complex regulations.
She remained uninstructed in the basics of first aid.
He was an uninstructed observer, unfamiliar with the scientific process.

Synset Examples

lamentably unenlightened as to the laws

Related Terms

  • Antonym: instructed - having received instruction or training
  • Synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness
  • Antonym: learned - having much knowledge acquired by study

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'instructed', from Latin 'instruere' (to build, teach).
