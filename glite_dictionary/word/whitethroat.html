whitethroat

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌθroʊt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌθrəʊt / (BrE)
a common North American finch with a white patch on its throat and a black-and-white striped crown
North American finch with a white throat patch

Examples

A whitethroat perched on the fence, singing its cheerful song.
We spotted two whitethroats during our morning walk in the woods.
The distinctive call of the whitethroat echoed through the forest.

Etymology

From 'white' + 'throat', describing the bird's distinguishing white throat patch.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌθroʊt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌθrəʊt / (BrE)
a small Old World warbler (Sylvia communis) with greyish-brown upperparts and a distinct white throat and underparts
a small warbler with a white throat

Examples

We spotted a whitethroat singing among the hedges during our walk.
The whitethroat migrates from Africa to Europe every spring.
Its distinctive song makes the whitethroat easy to identify in the countryside.

Etymology

From white + throat, referring to the bird's distinctive white throat.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌθroʊt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌθrəʊt / (BrE)
A small Old World warbler (Sylvia communis) with a whitish throat, found across Europe and western Asia.
small Old World warbler with a whitish throat

Examples

We spotted a whitethroat singing in the tall hedges near the field.
During migration, the whitethroat travels thousands of miles between Europe and Africa.
Its distinctive song made it easy for us to identify the whitethroat among other warblers.

Etymology

Named for its distinctive whitish throat; from 'white' + 'throat'.
