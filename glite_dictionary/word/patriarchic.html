patriarchic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpeɪtriˈɑrkɪk / (AmE), / ˌpeɪtrɪˈɑːkɪk / (BrE)
(of societies) ruled by men or tracing descent through the male line.
ruled by or tracing descent through males

Examples

Ancient Mesopotamia was a clearly patriarchic society, with power concentrated in the hands of men.
Inheritance laws in a patriarchic culture typically favor male descendants.
Anthropologists study the differences between patriarchic and matriarchic societies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patriarchal - of or relating to a system or society controlled by men

Etymology

From Greek patriarkhēs (patriarch) + -ic.
