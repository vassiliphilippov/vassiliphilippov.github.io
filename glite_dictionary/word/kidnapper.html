kidnapper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪdˌnæpər / (AmE), / ˈkɪdˌnapə / (BrE)
a person who illegally seizes and holds someone captive, usually in order to demand a ransom
person who carries out abductions

Examples

The police arrested the kidnapper after a lengthy investigation.
The kidnapper demanded a large sum of money for the child's release.
After being held by the kidnapper for three days, the victim was found safe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abductor - a person who takes someone away illegally by force or deception

Etymology

From 'kidnap' + '-er', where 'kidnap' originates from 'kid' (child) and obsolete 'nap' (to snatch), first used in 17th-century England.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪdˌnæpər / (AmE), / ˈkɪdˌnapə / (BrE)
a person who unlawfully seizes and holds someone captive, usually to demand a ransom
person who unlawfully seizes and holds someone captive

Examples

The police arrested the kidnapper after a long investigation.
The kidnapper demanded a large sum of money for the safe return of the child.
Authorities were able to track the kidnapper using security camera footage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abductor - a person who carries someone away by force
  • Antonym: victim - person harmed or affected by a crime

Etymology

From 'kidnap' (to seize a child), itself from 'kid' (child) + 'nap' (seize, from obsolete 'nab').
