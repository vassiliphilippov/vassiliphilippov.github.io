dative

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
(in grammar) the case of a noun or pronoun used for an indirect object, indicating the person or thing to whom or for whom something is given or done.
grammatical case for indirect objects

Examples

In German, the dative case is used to indicate the indirect object of a sentence.
Some languages, like Latin and Russian, have a specific dative case form.
The teacher explained when to use the dative instead of the accusative.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accusative - the grammatical case used for direct objects
  • Antonym: nominative - the grammatical case used for the subject of a verb

Etymology

From Latin dātīvus (relating to giving), from datus (given).
