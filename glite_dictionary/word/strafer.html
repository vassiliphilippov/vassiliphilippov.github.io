strafer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪfər / (AmE), / ˈstreɪfə / (BrE)
a combat pilot who attacks ground targets by flying low and firing at them with machine guns or cannon.
combat pilot attacking ground targets

Examples

The Air Force deployed an experienced strafer to the front lines to support ground troops.
During the mission, the strafer made several dangerous low passes over enemy convoys.
Many soldiers were grateful for the support provided by a strafer during the battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strafe - to attack with machine-gun or cannon fire from a low-flying aircraft

Etymology

From 'strafe' (to attack with low-flying aircraft) + -er (agent noun suffix).
