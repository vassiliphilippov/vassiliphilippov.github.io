unlimited

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlɪmɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɪmɪtɪd / (BrE)
not limited in any way; without restriction, reservation, or exception.
without restriction or exception

Examples

The gym offers unlimited access to all facilities for its members.
He has unlimited trust in his closest friends, never doubting them for a moment.
With unlimited data, you can stream videos as much as you want.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: limited - restricted in extent, amount, or scope

Etymology

From Latin 'un-' (not) + 'limited', from 'limitare' (to bound).

WordNet ID: oewn-01920788-s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlɪmɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɪmɪtɪd / (BrE)
without any limits in amount, range, or scope; not restricted or bounded.
without limits or restrictions

Examples

The mobile plan offers unlimited data, so you never have to worry about overage charges.
Her unlimited enthusiasm kept the team motivated throughout the project.
With unlimited possibilities, the future of technology seems brighter than ever.

Synset Examples

to start with a theory of unlimited freedom is to end up with unlimited despotism
the limitless reaches of outer space

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limitless - without any limit or end
  • Antonym: restricted - limited in extent, number, or scope

Etymology

From Latin 'un-' (not) + 'limitare' (to bound, limit).

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlɪmɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɪmɪtɪd / (BrE)
that cannot be entirely consumed, used up, or restricted; without any limits or bounds
without any limits; inexhaustible

Examples

The restaurant offers an unlimited buffet where you can eat as much as you want.
With unlimited storage, you never have to worry about running out of space.
She seemed to have an unlimited supply of energy throughout the day.

Synset Examples

an inexhaustible supply of coal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: limited - restricted in size, amount, or extent
  • Synonym: inexhaustible - impossible to use up completely

Etymology

From Latin 'un-' (not) + 'limitare' (to bound, limit).
