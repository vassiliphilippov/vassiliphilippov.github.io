nihil

1. NOUN

/ ˈniː.hɪl, ˈnaɪ.hɪl / (AmE), / ˈnaɪ.hɪl, ˈniː.hɪl / (BrE)
(in law, historical) Nothing; a term used after an unsuccessful attempt to serve a writ.
legal term meaning 'nothing'
legal

Examples

The sheriff wrote nihil in his return after failing to serve the defendant.
In old legal documents, the word nihil indicates that nothing was found or accomplished.
The report contained the annotation nihil where no further information was available.

Synset Examples

nihil habet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nil - nothing; zero (mainly British informal and legal)

Etymology

From Latin nihil (nothing).
