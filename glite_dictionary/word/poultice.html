poultice

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊl.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈpəʊl.tɪs / (BrE)
a soft, moist mass of material, typically of plant material or meal, applied hot to the body to relieve soreness and inflammation and kept in place with a cloth.
soft heated mass applied to skin as a remedy

Examples

The nurse applied a warm poultice to the patient’s swollen ankle to help reduce the pain.
In folk medicine, a mustard poultice was often used to treat chest congestion.
After the injury, he covered the bruise with a homemade poultice of herbs and flour.

Etymology

From Latin 'pultes' (porridge), via Old French 'poultice'.

2. VERB

/ ˈpoʊltɪs / (AmE), / ˈpəʊltɪs / (BrE)
to cover (a wound, sore, etc.) with a soft, moist mass of material, typically heated and medicated, to relieve pain or inflammation.
to apply a moist therapeutic dressing

Examples

She carefully poulticed the injured ankle to reduce the swelling.
The nurse poultices wounds to provide comfort and promote healing.
After the burn, his grandmother poulticed his arm with a herbal remedy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress - to treat or cover a wound

Etymology

From Latin *pultes* (porridge), via Old French *poultice*.
