Chilomeniscus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɪloʊməˈnɪskəs / (AmE), / ˌkɪləʊməˈnɪskəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, burrowing snakes known as sand snakes, native to northwestern Mexico and the southwestern United States.
genus of burrowing sand snakes

Examples

The genus Chilomeniscus includes several species adapted to sandy deserts.
Researchers discovered a Chilomeniscus while surveying reptiles in Arizona.
Unlike many snakes, Chilomeniscus rarely comes to the surface during the day.

Etymology

From Greek 'cheilos' (lip) and 'mēniskos' (crescent), referring to characteristics of the snake's head.
