fingerpointing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡərˌpɔɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəˌpɔɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of assigning blame to others, often in a public or accusatory manner
assigning blame to others

Examples

After the project failed, there was a lot of fingerpointing among the team members.
Instead of fingerpointing, we should focus on finding a solution to the problem.
Political debates often devolve into useless fingerpointing rather than constructive discussion.

Synset Examples

they want all the finger-pointing about intelligence failures to stop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blame - responsibility for a fault or wrong
  • Synonym: accusation - a charge or claim that someone has done something wrong

Etymology

Formed from 'finger' + 'pointing', referring to the physical act of pointing a finger at someone to assign blame.
