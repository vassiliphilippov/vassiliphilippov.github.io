castrato

1. NOUN

/ kæsˈtrɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / kæsˈtrɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
a male singer who was castrated before puberty to preserve a high vocal range, typically soprano or alto
male singer castrated to keep high voice

Examples

In the Baroque period, a castrato could command immense fame and fortune for his singing abilities.
The music was written specifically to showcase the range of a castrato.
Today, original recordings featuring a true castrato are extremely rare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soprano - the highest female or boy's singing voice

Etymology

From Italian 'castrato', past participle of 'castrare' (to castrate), from Latin 'castrare'.
