zymolytic

1. ADJ

/ zaɪməˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / zaɪməˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or causing fermentation, especially by means of enzymes or microorganisms
relating to fermentation

Examples

Yeast is known for its zymolytic activity in converting sugars into alcohol during brewing.
The zymolytic process is essential in the production of many dairy products such as cheese and yogurt.
Researchers observed that the zymolytic enzymes accelerated the decomposition of organic material in the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fermentative - relating to fermentation

Etymology

From Greek 'zyme' (leaven) and '-lytic' (loosening, dissolving).
