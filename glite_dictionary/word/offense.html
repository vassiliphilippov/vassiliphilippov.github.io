offense

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˈɒfɛns / (BrE)
The team or side in a game that possesses the ball or puck and is attempting to score points against the opponent.
team trying to score in sports

Examples

The offense struggled to score in the first half of the game.
After the interception, the offense quickly took the field to try to capitalize.
Their football team's offense is known for its aggressive passing tactics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defense - the team or side that is trying to prevent the opponent from scoring
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08098121-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ əˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˈɒf.əns / (BrE)
the action of actively attacking or engaging an opposing force, especially in military or sports contexts
the act of attacking an enemy

Examples

The army launched a massive offense at dawn, catching the enemy by surprise.
Their team's offense struggled to break through the opposition's defense all game.
A strong offense is often considered the best form of defense in chess.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defense - the action of resisting an attack or protecting from harm

Etymology

From Latin 'offensa' (a striking against, an offense), via Old French 'offense'.

3. NOUN

/ əˈfɛns / (AmE), / əˈfɛns / (BrE)
an act that breaks a rule or law, or violates what is considered right or proper
act that breaks a rule or law

Examples

Committing such an offense can result in a heavy fine or even imprisonment.
Many people considered his remarks an offense against common decency.
He pleaded guilty to a minor offense and paid a small penalty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crime - an action punishable by law; an illegal act
  • Synonym: insult - an act or remark that causes offense or hurt
  • Antonym: defense - the action of protecting from or resisting attack

Etymology

From Old French *offense*, from Latin *offensa* (a striking against, injury).

4. NOUN

/ əˈfɛns / (AmE), / əˈfɛns / (BrE)
A feeling of anger or resentment caused by something perceived as insulting or disrespectful.
anger or resentment from being insulted

Examples

He took offense at her comment about his cooking skills.
Some people may find this remark deeply offense, so use it with care.
She apologized quickly to avoid causing further offense to the guests.

Synset Examples

he took offence at my question

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - an action or remark that causes offense
  • Degree Higher: outrage - a strong feeling of shock and anger
  • Degree Lower: annoyance - a feeling of being irritated or mildly upset

Etymology

From Old French 'offense', from Latin 'offensa' meaning 'a striking against, injury'.

5. NOUN

/ əˈfɛns / (AmE), / əˈfɛns / (BrE)
A lack of politeness or respect that causes hurt feelings or resentment.
an act that causes hurt or displeasure

Examples

Lisa took offense at his rude comment during the meeting.
No offense, but I think you could have handled that situation better.
He apologized quickly when he realized his joke had given serious offense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - an action or remark that causes offense or hurt feelings
  • Synonym: affront - an action or remark that causes outrage or offense
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From Old French *offense*, from Latin *offensa* (an injury, affront).
