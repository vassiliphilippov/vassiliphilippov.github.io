publicly

1. ADV

/ ˈpʌblɪkli / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that involves or is open to the public; by or for the people generally.
in a manner open to the public

Examples

The government announced the decision publicly to ensure transparency.
Many scientific findings are now shared publicly rather than kept within closed circles.
She apologized publicly for her previous remarks.

Synset Examples

publicly provided medical care
publicly financed schools

Related Terms

  • Antonym: privately - in a manner not open to the public; not publicly

Etymology

From public + -ly, modeled after Latin publice (publicly).

2. ADV

/ ˈpʌblɪkli / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that can be seen or known by everyone; openly or in public.
in an open or public manner

Examples

The mayor announced the policy publicly during a televised press conference.
She spoke publicly about her experiences to raise awareness.
The company apologized publicly for its mistake.

Synset Examples

she admitted publicly to being a communist

Related Terms

  • Antonym: privately - in a private or secret manner

Etymology

From 'public' + '-ly', with 'public' from Latin 'publicus' (of the people).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00163131-r
Synset POS: r
