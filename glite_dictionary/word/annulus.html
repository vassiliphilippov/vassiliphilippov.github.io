annulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.jə.ləs / (AmE), / ˈan.jʊ.ləs / (BrE)
A ring-shaped object or region, especially one bounded by two concentric circles or cylinders.
ring-shaped region or object

Examples

The cross-section of the pipe resembled an annulus with space between the inner and outer walls.
In geometry, an annulus is the area between two concentric circles.
They arranged the chairs in an annulus around the campfire.

Synset Examples

a ring of ships in the harbor
a halo of smoke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring - a circular band or shape; related geometric concept

Etymology

From Latin 'annulus' meaning 'ring'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæn.jʊ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈan.jʊ.ləs / (BrE)
A ring-shaped remnant of the partial veil that encircles the stem of a mature mushroom.
ring-shaped structure on a mushroom stem

Examples

The annulus is clearly visible around the stem of the mushroom.
Some species can be identified by the presence or absence of an annulus.
After the cap expands, the annulus often remains as a ring on the stalk.

Etymology

From Latin 'annulus' (little ring).
