Bursera microphylla

1. NOUN

/ bɝːˈsɛɹə ˌmaɪkroʊˈfɪlə / (AmE), / bɜːˈsɪərə ˌmaɪkrəʊˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A small tree or shrub of the southwestern United States with a spicy scent, odd-pinnate leaves, and small clusters of white flowers.
species of small desert tree or shrub

Examples

The Bursera microphylla is commonly known as the elephant tree in desert regions.
Researchers studied the resin produced by Bursera microphylla for its unique chemical compounds.
Many hikers are captivated by the twisted trunk of the Bursera microphylla along rocky trails.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Bursera', honoring botanist Joachim Burser, and Latin 'microphylla' meaning 'small-leaved'.
