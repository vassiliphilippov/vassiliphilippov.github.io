harasser

1. NOUN

/ həˈɹæsɚ / (AmE), / həˈræsə / (BrE)
a person who persistently torments, attacks, or annoys others, especially in a hostile or aggressive manner
a person who persistently torments or attacks others

Examples

The company implemented new policies to protect employees from a harasser in the workplace.
Online platforms are taking steps to remove persistent harassers from their communities.
He was labeled a harasser after repeatedly sending unwanted messages to his colleagues.

Synset Examples

the harassers were not members of the regular army

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - a person who habitually seeks to harm or intimidate those they perceive as vulnerable

Etymology

From harass + -er, denoting one who harasses.

2. NOUN

/ həˈræsər / (AmE), / həˈrɑːsə / (BrE)
a person who persistently annoys, torments, or threatens another, often in a deliberate and repeated way
person who persistently torments or annoys

Examples

After months of receiving threatening emails, she reported her harasser to the authorities.
A workplace harasser can create a hostile environment for everyone involved.
Many companies have policies in place to address complaints against a harasser.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - a person who habitually seeks to harm or intimidate those perceived as vulnerable
  • Antonym: victim - a person who is harassed or harmed by another

Etymology

From the verb 'harass', which derives from French 'harasser' (to tire out, harry).
