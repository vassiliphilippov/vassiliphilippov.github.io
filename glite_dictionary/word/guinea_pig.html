guinea pig

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪni ˌpɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni ˌpɪɡ / (BrE)
a person who is used as a subject in an experiment, especially to test how something new works
person used in an experiment

Examples

When the company launched its new software, the employees were the first guinea pigs to try it out.
I don’t mind being a guinea pig if it will help improve medical treatments for others.
During the training session, she volunteered as a guinea pig so others could learn from her experience.

Synset Examples

She felt like a guinea pig during the testing.
The guinea pig director was praised for innovative methods.
He volunteered as a guinea pig for the new drug trial.
She served as the guinea pig director for the project.
They used me as a guinea pig for their experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: test subject - a person or animal used in testing or experimentation
  • Antonym: control group - a group used as a standard of comparison in experiments

Etymology

Originally referring to the small rodent often used in laboratory experiments.
