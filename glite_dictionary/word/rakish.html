rakish

1. ADJ

/ ˈreɪ.kɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.kɪʃ / (BrE)
having a stylish, lively, and slightly unconventional appearance, often suggesting confidence or a carefree attitude
stylish and lively in appearance or manner

Examples

He wore a rakish fedora that made him stand out from the crowd.
With his rakish grin and effortless style, he charmed everyone at the party.
The car sported a rakish angle to its windshield, enhancing its sporty look.

Synset Examples

a dapper young man
a jaunty red hat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaunty - cheerful and self-confident; stylish
  • Synonym: dapper - neat and stylish in appearance
  • Antonym: sloppy - careless, messy in appearance or manner

Etymology

From rake (person with an immoral lifestyle) + -ish.

2. ADJ

/ ˈreɪkɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈreɪkɪʃ / (BrE)
having a dashing, lively, or slightly disreputable appearance or style; marked by carefree unconventionality.
dashing, slightly disreputable in style

Examples

He wore a rakish hat tilted at an angle that made him stand out in the crowd.
The yacht sped across the bay with a rakish elegance that drew admiring looks from onshore.
Her rakish smile suggested she liked to break a rule or two now and then.

Synset Examples

a cocktail party given by some … raffish bachelors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raffish - unconventional and slightly disreputable, especially in an attractive way
  • Antonym: respectable - regarded by society to be good, proper, or correct; having a good reputation

Etymology

From rake (a person with an immoral lifestyle), ultimately from Middle English rakehel (idle, dissolute person).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00611718-s
Synset POS: s
