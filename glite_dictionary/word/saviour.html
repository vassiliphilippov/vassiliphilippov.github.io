saviour

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvjər / (AmE), / ˈseɪvjə / (BrE)
a person who rescues someone from harm, danger, or difficulty
person who rescues from harm or danger

Examples

The firefighter was hailed as a saviour after pulling the child from the burning building.
In her darkest hours, she considered her best friend her saviour.
Some view the new leader as a political saviour in times of crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescuer - a person who saves someone from a dangerous or difficult situation
  • Antonym: peril - serious and immediate danger

Etymology

From Old French *sauveour*, from Late Latin *salvator* (saviour), from Latin *salvare* (to save).
