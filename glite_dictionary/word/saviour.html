Saviour

1. PROPN

/ ˈseɪvjər / (AmE), / ˈseɪvjə / (BrE)
(in Christianity) Jesus Christ, regarded as the redeemer and deliverer of humankind.
Jesus Christ as redeemer in Christianity

Examples

Christians believe that Jesus is the Saviour of the world.
Many hymns celebrate the birth of the Saviour in Bethlehem.
The role of the Saviour is central to Christian theology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savior - Alternative American spelling of 'Saviour'

Etymology

From Old French saveour, from Latin salvator (one who saves), from salvare (to save).
