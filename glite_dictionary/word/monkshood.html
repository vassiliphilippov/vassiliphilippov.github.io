monkshood

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋksˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋksˌhʊd / (BrE)
a poisonous plant with hood-shaped blue or purple flowers, found in northern Europe and containing aconite
a poisonous plant with blue-purple flowers

Examples

Many gardeners avoid growing monkshood because all parts of the plant are highly toxic.
The brilliant blue of the monkshood flowers stands out in the summer border.
She learned to recognize monkshood during her botany classes to avoid accidental poisoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aconite - poisonous substance obtained from monkshood

Etymology

Named for the hood-like shape of its flowers, resembling a monk's hood.
