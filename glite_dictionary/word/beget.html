to beget

1. VERB

/ bɪˈɡɛt / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɛt / (BrE)
(of a man) to become the father of (a child); to produce offspring.
to father or produce offspring

Examples

According to the tradition, Abraham begot Isaac in his old age.
Legends say that the king begot many heirs to continue his dynasty.
It was once believed that gods could beget children with mortals.

Synset Examples

Abraham begot Isaac
John fathered four daughters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: father - to become the father of a child
  • Synonym: sire - to father or produce offspring (especially of a male animal)

Etymology

From Old English 'begietan', meaning to get or obtain.
