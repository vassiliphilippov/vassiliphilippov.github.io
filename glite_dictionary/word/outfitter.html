outfitter

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfɪtər / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌfɪtə / (BrE)
a shop or business that sells clothing and equipment for a particular activity, such as camping, hunting, or hiking
shop selling specialized equipment

Examples

We stopped at the local outfitter to rent some hiking boots and backpacks.
The outfitter specializes in gear for fishing trips and mountain expeditions.
Before their canoe trip, they consulted an outfitter who knew the river well.

Synset Examples

an outfitter provided everything needed for the safari

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplier - a business that provides goods or services

Etymology

From 'outfit' (to furnish with equipment) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfɪtər / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌfɪtə / (BrE)
a person or business that sells men's clothing, especially suits and formalwear
seller of men's clothing

Examples

He visited the local outfitter to purchase a suit for the wedding.
The outfitter offered a wide selection of ties and dress shirts.
Many traditional outfitters also provide tailoring services on site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clothier - a person who makes or sells clothes

Etymology

Formed from 'outfit' + '-er', meaning one who provides outfits.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10407411-n
Synset POS: n
