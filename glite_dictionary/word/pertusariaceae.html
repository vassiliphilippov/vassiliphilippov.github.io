Pertusariaceae

1. NOUN

/ pərˌtuːsəˈriːəsiː / (AmE), / pəˌtjuːsəˈriːəsiː / (BrE)
A family of lichen-forming fungi within the division Lichenes.
family of lichenized fungi

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the Pertusariaceae family during their expedition.
The Pertusariaceae are important for their role in various ecological processes involving lichens.
Mycology students must learn to distinguish members of Pertusariaceae from other fungal families.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Pertusaria' and the standard biological family suffix '-aceae'.

a family of lichenized fungi

Examples

The Pertusariaceae family contains many species of crustose lichens found on tree bark and rocks.
