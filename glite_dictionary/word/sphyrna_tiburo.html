Sphyrna tiburo

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfɪrnə tɪˈbjuːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈsfɪənə tɪˈbjuːrəʊ / (BrE)
A small, harmless species of hammerhead shark known as the bonnethead, distinguished by its spade-shaped head and common in bays and estuaries.
species of small hammerhead shark (bonnethead)

Examples

The Sphyrna tiburo is often seen swimming in shallow bays along the coast.
Marine biologists study the behavior of Sphyrna tiburo to learn more about hammerhead sharks.
Unlike other hammerheads, Sphyrna tiburo feeds mainly on crabs and small fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonnethead - common name for Sphyrna tiburo

Etymology

From Latin: genus name *Sphyrna* (meaning 'hammer') and species epithet *tiburo*.
