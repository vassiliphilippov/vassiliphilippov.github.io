keurboom

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜrˌbuːm / (AmE), / ˈkɜːˌbuːm / (BrE)
A South African tree with odd-pinnate leaves and clusters of fragrant pink to purple flowers, especially Virgilia oroboides.
South African tree with pink/purple flowers

Examples

The keurboom produces beautiful pink-purple flowers in early spring.
Many gardens in Cape Town feature the keurboom as an ornamental tree.
Wildlife often takes shelter under the shady branches of the keurboom.

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'keurboom', meaning 'choice tree' or 'select tree'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪrˌbuːm / (AmE), / ˈkɜːbʊm / (BrE)
a fast-growing South African tree (Virgilia oroboides) with a rounded crown and fragrant white to deep rose flowers, often planted as an ornamental
South African ornamental tree with fragrant flowers

Examples

The keurboom is prized for its beautiful pink blossoms in South African gardens.
We planted a keurboom to add shade and fragrance to our yard.
During spring, the keurboom attracts birds and insects with its abundant flowers.

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'keurboom', literally 'choice tree', from Dutch 'keur' (choice) and 'boom' (tree).
