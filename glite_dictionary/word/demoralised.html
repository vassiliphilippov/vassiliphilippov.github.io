demoralised

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɔrəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒrəlʌɪzd / (BrE)
having lost confidence, hope, or spirit; dispirited or discouraged
dispirited; lacking hope or confidence
british

Examples

After months of unsuccessful job hunting, she felt demoralised and unsure of what to do next.
The demoralised team struggled to find motivation after a series of defeats.
Budget cuts have left many teachers feeling demoralised and undervalued.

Synset Examples

desperate demoralized people looking for work
felt discouraged by the magnitude of the problem
the disheartened instructor tried vainly to arouse their interest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disheartened - having lost determination or confidence
  • Antonym: encouraged - having increased confidence or hope
  • Synonym: demoralized - American English spelling of 'demoralised'

Etymology

From French 'démoraliser', based on 'moral' (morale, spirit), plus '-ise'/'-ize'.
