rip-off

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪpˌɑf / (AmE), / ˈrɪpˌɒf / (BrE)
an act of stealing or cheating, typically by charging someone too much money or taking something unfairly
act of stealing or cheating
informal

Examples

The price of that concert ticket was a total rip-off.
Tourists often complain about being the victims of a rip-off in local markets.
I can't believe you paid that much for lunch—what a rip-off!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theft - the act of stealing
  • Synonym: fraud - cheating or deceiving someone to gain money or property

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'rip off', meaning 'to steal', likely from 'rip' (to tear) + 'off' (away).
