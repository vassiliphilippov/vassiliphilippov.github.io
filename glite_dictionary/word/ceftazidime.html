ceftazidime

1. NOUN

/ sɛfˈtæzɪdiːm / (AmE), / sɛfˈtazɪdiːm / (BrE)
a broad-spectrum cephalosporin antibiotic, administered by injection to treat various bacterial infections
an injectable cephalosporin antibiotic
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed ceftazidime to treat the patient's severe bacterial infection.
Hospital guidelines recommend ceftazidime for certain cases of hospital-acquired pneumonia.
Allergic reactions to ceftazidime are rare but must be closely monitored.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceftriaxone - another broad-spectrum cephalosporin antibiotic
  • Antonym: penicillin - a different class of beta-lactam antibiotic

Etymology

From 'cephalosporin' + chemical suffixes, patterned after other beta-lactam antibiotics; coined in pharmaceutical usage.
