sensationalistic

1. ADJ

/ sɛnˌseɪʃəˈnɑlɪstɪk / (AmE), / sɛnˌseɪʃəˈnælɪstɪk / (BrE)
(especially of news, reporting, or media) presenting stories in a way that is intended to provoke public interest or excitement, at the expense of accuracy or seriousness.
seeking to provoke excitement over accuracy

Examples

The newspaper's sensationalistic headlines were criticized for exaggerating minor events.
She argued that modern media is becoming increasingly sensationalistic in its approach to news coverage.
The documentary avoids sensationalistic reporting and instead focuses on verified facts.

Synset Examples

sensational journalistic reportage of the scandal
yellow press

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensational - trying to produce a strong emotional reaction, especially shock or excitement

Etymology

From 'sensational' + '-istic', modelled on 'sensationalism'.
