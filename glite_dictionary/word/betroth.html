to betroth

1. VERB

/ bɪˈtroʊð / (AmE), / bɪˈtrəʊð / (BrE)
to promise or give (someone) in marriage; to formally arrange for marriage
formally promise in marriage

Examples

Her father decided to betroth her to a wealthy merchant.
The prince was betrothed to a princess from a neighboring kingdom at a young age.
They betroth their children according to family customs and traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to engage - to formally agree to marry someone

Etymology

From Old English 'betreowan' (to pledge, promise), related to 'troth' (faith).
