Debussy

1. PROPN

/ dəˈbjuːsi / (AmE), / dəˈbjuːsi / (BrE)
(1862–1918) A French composer, regarded as the creator of Impressionism in music and known for works such as 'Clair de Lune' and 'Prelude to the Afternoon of a Faun'.
French composer; pioneer of musical Impressionism

Examples

Many consider Debussy the father of musical Impressionism for his innovative compositions.
At the concert, the orchestra played several pieces by Debussy, including his famous "Clair de Lune".
Studying Debussy gives insight into the evolution of early twentieth-century classical music.
