Simulium

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈmjuːliəm / (AmE), / sɪˈmjuːliəm / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Simuliidae, consisting of small, dark-colored flies commonly known as blackflies.
genus of blackflies

Examples

Simulium species are known to transmit diseases such as river blindness.
Researchers collected larvae of Simulium from the flowing stream.
The main host for the parasite is the blackfly of the genus Simulium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackfly - a small dark fly of the family Simuliidae

Etymology

From Latin 'simul', meaning 'at the same time', used in New Latin for the genus name.
