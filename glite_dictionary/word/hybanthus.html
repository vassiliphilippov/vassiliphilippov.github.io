Hybanthus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈbænθəs / (AmE), / hɪˈbænθəs / (BrE)
a genus of herbs and small shrubs with white or purple flowers, found in tropical or subtropical regions
a plant genus with small, colorful flowers

Examples

The Hybanthus genus includes many species with attractive purple blossoms.
Many gardens in tropical regions feature Hybanthus for its ornamental value.
Botanists are studying Hybanthus for its potential medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Greek hybantos (hump-backed), referring to the flower shape.
