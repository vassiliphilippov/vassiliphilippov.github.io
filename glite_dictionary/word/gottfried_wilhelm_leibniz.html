Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz

1. PROPN

/ ˈlaɪb.nɪts / (AmE), / ˈlaɪb.nɪts / (BrE)
(1646–1716) A German philosopher and mathematician known for developing the concept of monads and creating a form of calculus independently of Newton.
German philosopher and mathematician (1646–1716)

Examples

Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz made groundbreaking contributions to both philosophy and mathematics.
The idea of monads was introduced by Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz in the late 17th century.
Calculus was developed independently by Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz and Isaac Newton.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Isaac Newton - English mathematician and physicist who also developed calculus

Etymology

From German, based on the family name Leibniz.
