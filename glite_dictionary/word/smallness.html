smallness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmɔlnəs / (AmE), / ˈsmɔːlnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being small in amount, size, or degree
the state of being small

Examples

The smallness of the gift did not diminish the thought behind it.
She was surprised by the smallness of the room when she entered.
The project's success depended on the smallness of the risk involved.

Synset Examples

he was attracted by the smallness of the taxes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: littleness - the quality of being little or small in size or amount
  • Antonym: bigness - the quality or state of being big or large

Etymology

From small + -ness, a suffix forming nouns denoting a state or quality.

5. NOUN

/ ˈsmɔlnəs / (AmE), / ˈsmɔːlnəs / (BrE)
a quality of being ungenerous or petty about trivial matters

Examples

There was a certain smallness in his refusal to share credit with his colleagues.
You could detect smallness in the way she made every little favor seem like a burden.

Related Terms

From 'small' + '-ness', forming a noun meaning the state of being small, here extended figuratively to character.
