innings

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈɪnɪŋz / (BrE)
The period in a cricket match during which a player or team takes its turn to bat.
the batting turn in cricket

Examples

The team scored 250 runs in their first innings.
After losing three wickets, the innings looked in trouble.
He was out for 45 runs in the second innings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: at-bat - a player's turn batting in baseball

Etymology

From 'inning', meaning 'a turn or spell of activity', with formation influenced by the plural form regularly used in cricket.
