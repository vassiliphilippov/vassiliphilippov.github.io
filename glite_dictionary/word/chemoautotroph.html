chemoautotroph

1. NOUN

/ kiːmoʊˈɔtoʊˌtroʊf / (AmE), / kiːməʊˈɔːtətɹəʊf / (BrE)
An organism that obtains energy by oxidizing inorganic substances and uses carbon dioxide as its main source of carbon.
organism using chemicals for energy and CO2 for carbon
medical

Examples

Some bacteria are chemoautotrophs, using inorganic molecules for energy rather than sunlight.
Unlike plants, chemoautotrophs do not rely on photosynthesis for growth.
Hydrothermal vent communities are often supported by chemoautotroph bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: autotroph - organism that produces its own food from inorganic substances
  • Antonym: heterotroph - organism that obtains its nutrition from organic compounds
  • Degree Lower: photoautotroph - organism that uses light for energy and CO2 as carbon source

Etymology

From Greek 'khemia' (chemistry) and 'autotroph', from 'autos' (self) and 'trophe' (nutrition).
