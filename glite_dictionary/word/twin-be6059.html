twin

1. VERB

/ twɪn / (AmE), / twɪn / (BrE)
(of an animal) to give birth to twins.
give birth to twins (of an animal)

Examples

The ewe twinned last spring and raised two healthy lambs.
It is common for some breeds of goats to twin in favorable conditions.
Their cow has twinned three times in the past five years.

2. NOUN

/ twɪn / (AmE), / twɪn / (BrE)
either of two children or animals born at the same birth from the same mother
one of two offspring from the same birth

Examples

She is a twin, and her sister lives in another city.
Both twin boys were accepted into the same university.
He was surprised to meet his twin after being separated at birth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: triplet - one of three offspring born at the same birth

Etymology

From Old English 'twin' (double, twofold), related to Old Norse 'tvinnr.'
