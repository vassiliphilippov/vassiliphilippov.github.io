infiniteness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪnɪtnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪnɪtnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of having no limits or bounds; the condition of being infinite.
state of having no limits

Examples

Contemplating the infiniteness of the universe can be both awe-inspiring and humbling.
The infiniteness of her imagination allowed her to write stories without boundaries.
Mathematicians often grapple with the infiniteness of certain sets and numbers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infinity - the state or quality of being infinite; endlessness
  • Antonym: finiteness - the quality of having limits or bounds

Etymology

Formed from 'infinite' (from Latin *infinitus*, meaning 'unbounded') + '-ness', a noun-forming suffix.
