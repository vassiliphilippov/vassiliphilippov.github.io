Hyoscyamus muticus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌɑːsiˈeɪməs mjuːˈtaɪkəs / (AmE), / haɪˌɒsiˈeɪməs mjuːˈtɪkəs / (BrE)
a species of poisonous herb native to North Africa and the Middle East, whose leaves are a source of hyoscyamine
a poisonous herbaceous plant (species)

Examples

The alkaloids extracted from Hyoscyamus muticus are used in traditional medicine.
Researchers studied the toxic properties of Hyoscyamus muticus leaves.
Hyoscyamus muticus thrives in arid regions of northern Africa.

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Hyoscyamus' meaning 'henbane' and 'muticus' meaning 'blunt' or 'unarmed'.
