Narthecium

1. NOUN

/ nɑːrˈθiːsiəm / (AmE), / nɑːˈθiːsɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of perennial herbs, commonly known as bog asphodels, found in wet habitats and sometimes classified in the family Melanthiaceae
a genus of bog asphodel plants

Examples

The wet meadows were dotted with the bright yellow flowers of Narthecium.
Botanists often debate whether Narthecium should be placed in the Melanthiaceae family.
Narthecium thrives in acidic bogs and marshes across northern Europe.

Etymology

From Greek narthēkion, diminutive of narthēx (giant fennel).

2. NOUN

/ nɑrˈθisiəm / (AmE), / nɑːˈθiːsiəm / (BrE)
a genus of bog asphodels, herbaceous plants found in wetlands, sometimes classified in the family Melanthiaceae
genus of bog asphodels (wetland plants)

Examples

The rare Narthecium thrives in acidic peat bogs throughout northern Europe.
Botanists have debated whether Narthecium should be included in Melanthiaceae or a separate family.
The bright yellow flowers of Narthecium are a distinctive feature in wetland ecosystems.

Etymology

From Latin *Narthecium*, adopted from Greek *narthēkion*, meaning 'small cane' or 'reed'.
