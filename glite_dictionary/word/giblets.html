giblets

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪbləts / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪblɪts / (BrE)
the edible internal organs of a bird, such as the heart, liver, and gizzard, often used in cooking
edible bird internal organs (for cooking)

Examples

Remove the giblets from the chicken cavity before roasting.
The recipe calls for the heart and liver from the giblets to add flavor to the gravy.
She saved the turkey giblets to make a rich stock for soup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offal - the edible internal organs and entrails of an animal

Etymology

From Old French *gibelet* (a stew of game), from *gibier* (game), perhaps via Medieval Latin.
