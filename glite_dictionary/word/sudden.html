sudden

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈsʌdn̩ / (BrE)
happening quickly and unexpectedly, without warning or over a very short period of time
happening quickly and unexpectedly

Examples

The rain ended with a sudden drop in temperature.
His sudden decision surprised everyone at the meeting.
There was a sudden silence when she entered the room.

Synset Examples

a sudden storm
a sudden decision
a sudden cure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abrupt - happening quickly and unexpectedly
  • Antonym: gradual - happening slowly over time

Etymology

From Latin 'subitaneus' (sudden).
