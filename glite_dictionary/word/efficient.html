efficient

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈfɪʃənt / (AmE), / ɪˈfɪʃənt / (BrE)
able to accomplish a task or purpose with the minimum use of time, effort, or resources; not wasteful.
working well without waste

Examples

The company hired an efficient assistant who quickly organized all the records.
Modern appliances are designed to be more efficient and environmentally friendly.
She completed the project in an efficient manner, saving both time and money.

Synset Examples

an efficient production manager
efficient engines save gas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inefficient - not achieving maximum productivity; wasteful of time or resources
  • Synonym: effective - successful in producing a desired or intended result

Etymology

From Latin efficere (to accomplish, effect), via Old French efficient.

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈfɪʃənt / (AmE), / ɪˈfɪʃənt / (BrE)
working well and quickly without wasting time, energy, or resources; achieving maximum productivity with minimum wasted effort or expense
performing tasks well without waste

Examples

The new washing machine is much more efficient than our old one.
Her efficient organization skills helped the project finish ahead of schedule.
We need a more efficient way to manage our energy consumption.

Synset Examples

people who will do nothing unless they get something out of it for themselves are often highly effective persons …
effective personnel
an efficient secretary
the efficient cause of the revolution

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inefficient - not achieving maximum productivity; wasting resources
  • Synonym: effective - producing the desired result

Etymology

From Latin efficient-, efficiens, present participle of efficere (to accomplish)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00512569-s
Synset POS: s
