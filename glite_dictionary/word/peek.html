peek

1. VERB

/ piːk / (AmE), / piːk / (BrE)
to look quickly, especially in a secretive or cautious way
look quickly and secretly

Examples

The child tried to peek into the box before his birthday.
She couldn't resist peeking at her friend's text message.
He peeked through the curtains to see who was outside.

Synset Examples

She only glanced at the paper
I only peeked — I didn't see anything interesting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glance - to look quickly, especially away from something or someone
  • Antonym: stare - to look at something for a long time

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly related to Middle English *piken* (to look quickly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02169748-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ pik / (AmE), / piːk / (BrE)
a quick and secret look at something
a secret or quick look

Examples

She took a quick peek through the curtains to see who was at the door.
He couldn't resist a peek at the surprise gift.
The children had a secret peek into the forbidden room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glimpse - a brief or quick look
  • Antonym: stare - to look at something for a long time

Etymology

Probably imitative or from Middle English piken (to peep).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00879899-n
Synset POS: n
