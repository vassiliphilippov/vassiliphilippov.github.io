shoeshine

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuˌʃaɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃuːʃaɪn / (BrE)
the act or process of cleaning and polishing shoes to make them shine
the act of cleaning and polishing shoes

Examples

He sat down for a quick shoeshine before his job interview.
The airport offers shoeshine services near the entrance.
A professional shoeshine can make old shoes look new again.

Synset Examples

he charged a dollar for a shoeshine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoe polishing - the process of cleaning and making shoes shine

Etymology

From 'shoe' + 'shine', referring to polishing shoes to a shine.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃuˌʃaɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃuːʃaɪn / (BrE)
A shiny finish on shoes achieved by applying polish and buffing.
shiny finish on shoes

Examples

The soldier's shoeshine gleamed under the bright lights of inspection.
He prided himself on a shoeshine so perfect you could see your face in it.
Even after a walk in the rain, her shoeshine remained impeccable.

Synset Examples

his trousers had a sharp crease and you could see your reflection in his shoeshine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polish - a substance or process used to make something smooth and shiny
  • Synonym: buff - to polish by rubbing, usually with a soft cloth

Etymology

From shoe + shine, referring to the act and result of shining shoes.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04708615-n
Synset POS: n
