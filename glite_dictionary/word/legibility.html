legibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛdʒəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌlɛdʒɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of writing or printing that is easy to read
ease of reading handwriting or print

Examples

The legibility of the document improved after it was typed instead of handwritten.
Teachers often emphasize the importance of legibility in students' exam papers.
Poor legibility can make important information difficult to understand.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illegibility - the quality of not being easy to read

Etymology

From Latin legibilis (that can be read), from legere (to read).

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɛdʒəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌlɛdʒɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being clear enough to read or perceive easily
quality of being easy to read

Examples

The legibility of the sign was reduced by the heavy rain and fog.
Improving the legibility of this report will help everyone understand the results more clearly.
She praised the legibility of his handwriting, calling it clear and attractive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illegibility - the quality of being difficult or impossible to read
  • Synonym: readability - the ease with which text can be read and understood

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'legibilitas', from Latin 'legibilis' (that can be read).
