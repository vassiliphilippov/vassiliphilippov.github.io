geomorphology

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒioʊˌmɔrˈfɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əʊ.mɔːˈfɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
The scientific study of the physical features of the Earth's surface and their relation to its geological structures.
study of Earth's surface features

Examples

She decided to specialize in geomorphology during her graduate studies.
Recent advances in geomorphology have improved our understanding of river systems.
The textbook provides a comprehensive overview of coastal geomorphology.

Etymology

From Greek 'geo-' (earth) + 'morphē' (form) + 'logia' (study).
