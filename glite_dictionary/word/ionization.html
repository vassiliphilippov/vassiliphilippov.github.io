ionization

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.ə.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.ə.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which an atom or molecule acquires a positive or negative charge by gaining or losing electrons, often resulting in the formation of ions
process of forming ions
medical

Examples

The ionization of air allows electricity to flow through it more easily.
Radiation can cause the ionization of atoms in living cells, leading to damage.
High temperatures in the plasma led to rapid ionization of the gas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ionize - to convert into ions, or to become ions
  • Antonym: recombination - the process inverse to ionization, where ions and electrons combine

Etymology

From ion + -ization; 'ion' from Greek 'ienai' (to go).

2. NOUN

/ ˌaɪənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or condition in which atoms or molecules are converted into ions, typically by gaining or losing electrons due to heat, radiation, chemical reaction, or electrical discharge
formation of ions from atoms or molecules

Examples

The scientist studied the ionization of gases in the laboratory.
Solar flares can increase the ionization of Earth's upper atmosphere.
X-rays cause ionization in the tissues they pass through, which is useful in medical imaging.

Synset Examples

the ionization of a gas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissociation - the process by which a molecule divides or splits into smaller particles, such as atoms or ions

Etymology

From ion + -ization, combining the scientific term 'ion' (from Greek 'ion', meaning 'going') with the suffix '-ization'.
