to houseclean

1. VERB

/ ˈhaʊsˌklin / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌkliːn / (BrE)
to clean and tidy up a house, especially as a regular or thorough activity
clean and tidy a house

Examples

We usually houseclean on Saturdays to keep the place looking fresh.
She likes to houseclean thoroughly at the start of each season.
They hired someone to houseclean before their guests arrived.

Synset Examples

She housecleans every week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clean - to make (something) free from dirt or mess

Etymology

Formed from 'house' + 'clean', modeled after similar compounds such as 'housekeep'.
