lusciousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌʃ.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlʌʃ.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely appealing, rich, or appetizing, especially to the senses of taste or smell
the state of being very appetizing or appealing

Examples

The lusciousness of the ripe peaches made them irresistible at the summer market.
She admired the lusciousness of the chocolate cake, savoring each decadent bite.
Artists often try to capture the lusciousness of nature's colors in their paintings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deliciousness - the quality of being very tasty or delightful to eat
  • Antonym: blandness - the quality of being dull or lacking strong flavor

Etymology

From 'luscious', derived from Middle English 'lucius' (delicious) plus suffix '-ness'.
