to vaccinate

1. VERB

/ ˈvæksɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈvaksɪneɪt / (BrE)
to administer a vaccine to a person or animal in order to produce immunity against a specific disease
administer a vaccine for immunity

Examples

The doctor will vaccinate your child against several diseases during the first year.
Many countries vaccinate children before they start school as part of public health policy.
It is important to vaccinate your pets to protect them from common diseases.

Synset Examples

We vaccinate against scarlet fever
The nurse vaccinated the children in the school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to immunize - to make immune, especially by inoculation

Etymology

From Latin 'vaccinus' (from cows), related to the use of cowpox to prevent smallpox; see 'vaccine'.
