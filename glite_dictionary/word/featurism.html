featurism

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːtʃərɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfiːtʃərɪzəm / (BrE)
The practice of emphasizing superficial features, such as appearance, over skill or substantive qualities, especially in fashion.
emphasis on superficial features

Examples

Featurism in fashion magazines often overshadows the achievements of truly talented designers.
Critics argued that featurism led the industry to focus more on looks than on genuine creativity.
Some believe that featurism neglects the skills and hard work behind the scenes in the fashion world.

Synset Examples

Featurism in fashion often overlooks true talent.

Etymology

Formed from 'feature' + '-ism', patterning after terms like 'sexism' and 'racism' to denote a bias or emphasis.
