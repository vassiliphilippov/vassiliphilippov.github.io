tetramerous

1. ADJ

/ təˈtæmərəs / (AmE), / tɛˈtamərəs / (BrE)
(especially of flowers or other plant parts) having or consisting of four similar parts or divisions.
having four similar parts (especially in plants)

Examples

The wildflower has tetramerous petals, each precisely the same size and shape.
In botany, a tetramerous arrangement is common in certain plant families.
Researchers noted the tetramerous structure of the new aquatic plant species.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trimerous - having or consisting of three parts
  • Degree Higher: pentamerous - having or consisting of five parts

Etymology

From Greek *tetramerēs* (τετραμερής), from *tetra-* (four) + *meros* (part).
