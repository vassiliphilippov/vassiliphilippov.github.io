Niihau

1. PROPN

/ niːˈhaʊ / (AmE), / niːˈhaʊ / (BrE)
the most northwestern of the main Hawaiian Islands, located to the west of Kauai.
northwestern Hawaiian island

Examples

Niihau is known for its limited access and private ownership.
Few tourists have visited Niihau due to its restrictions on outsiders.
The population of Niihau consists mainly of Native Hawaiians.

Etymology

From Hawaiian Niʻihau, the original indigenous name of the island.
