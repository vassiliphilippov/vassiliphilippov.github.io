typhoon

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈfuːn / (AmE), / taɪˈfuːn / (BrE)
A tropical cyclone with strong winds and heavy rain that forms in the western Pacific or Indian oceans.
a tropical cyclone in the Pacific/Indian oceans

Examples

The coastal city was severely damaged after the typhoon made landfall last night.
Each year, several typhoons strike Southeast Asia, bringing torrential rain and gale-force winds.
Authorities issued a warning as a powerful typhoon approached the Philippine islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurricane - a tropical cyclone in the Atlantic or eastern Pacific oceans
  • Synonym: cyclone - a large-scale air mass that rotates around a strong center of low atmospheric pressure

Etymology

From Cantonese tai fung, meaning 'great wind'; possibly via Portuguese tufão from Arabic ṭūfān.
