II Corinthians

1. PROPN

/ ˌsɛkənd kəˈrɪnθiənz / (AmE), / ˌsɛkənd kəˈrɪnθiənz / (BrE)
the eighth book of the New Testament, containing the second letter from Saint Paul to the church at Corinth.
New Testament book (second epistle to Corinthians)

Examples

Many Christians find inspiration in II Corinthians chapter 4.
My pastor recently preached a sermon based on II Corinthians 5:17.
Scholars often debate the historical context of II Corinthians.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: First Corinthians - the book preceding II Corinthians, Paul's first letter to the Corinthians

Etymology

From Latin 'corinthius', referring to Corinth, an ancient city in Greece.
