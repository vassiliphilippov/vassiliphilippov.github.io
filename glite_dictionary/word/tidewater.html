tidewater

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪdˌwɑtɚ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdwɔːtə / (BrE)
Low-lying coastal land that is regularly drained or affected by tidal streams or waters.
coastal land affected by tides

Examples

The rich soil of the tidewater made the region ideal for farming in colonial times.
Many historic plantations are located along the tidewater because of easy access to shipping routes.
In some areas, rising sea levels threaten the delicate balance of the tidewater ecosystem.

Etymology

From 'tide' (referring to the rise and fall of the sea) + 'water'.
