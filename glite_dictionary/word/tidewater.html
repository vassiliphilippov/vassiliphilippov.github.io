Tidewater

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪdˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪdˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
the low-lying coastal region of eastern Virginia, North Carolina, South Carolina, and Georgia, known for its flat plains influenced by tidal waters
coastal plain region in the southeastern United States
american

Examples

Many historic plantations are located along Virginia's Tidewater region, where the land meets the sea.
The culture and cuisine of the Tidewater area reflect a blend of Native American and colonial influences.
Flooding is a frequent concern in the low-lying Tidewater of North Carolina after heavy rains.

Etymology

From 'tide' (referring to the tides) + 'water' (water influenced by tides).
