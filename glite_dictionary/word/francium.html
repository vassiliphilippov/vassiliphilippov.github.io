francium

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrænsiəm / (AmE), / ˈfrænsiəm / (BrE)
a highly radioactive chemical element of the alkali-metal group, discovered as a decay product of actinium and having the symbol Fr
a radioactive alkali-metal chemical element

Examples

Francium is so rare and unstable that it has little commercial use.
Scientists first identified francium as a byproduct of actinium decay.
Because of its radioactivity, francium is studied only in minute quantities in laboratories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cesium - a chemical element, symbol Cs, closely related alkali metal
  • Degree Lower: actinium - a radioactive chemical element, symbol Ac, from whose decay francium is produced

Etymology

From French 'francium', named after France, where it was discovered.
