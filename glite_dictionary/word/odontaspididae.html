Odontaspididae

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊdənˌtæspɪˈdɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌəʊdənˌtaspɪˈdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of sharks commonly known as sand sharks, in some taxonomic classifications considered coextensive with the family Carcharhinidae.
family of sharks; sand sharks

Examples

The Odontaspididae are often referred to as sand sharks due to their preferred habitats.
Unlike other shark families, the Odontaspididae possess distinctive long, slender teeth.
Marine biologists are working to better understand the distribution of Odontaspididae species worldwide.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'odous, odont-' (tooth) and 'aspis' (shield), with the zoological family suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.dənˌtæsp.ɪˈdaɪ.di / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.dɒnˌtasp.ɪˈdiː.diː / (BrE)
A family of sand sharks, sometimes considered part of family Carcharhinidae in some classifications.
family of sand sharks

Examples

The Odontaspididae are also known as sand sharks and inhabit coastal regions worldwide.
Some taxonomists debate whether Odontaspididae should be merged with Carcharhinidae.
Juvenile Odontaspididae have distinct long, slender teeth ideal for catching fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carcharhinidae - family of requiem sharks, sometimes considered to include Odontaspididae

Etymology

From Greek 'odous' (tooth) + 'aspis' (shield), with zoological family suffix '-idae'.
