physic

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪk / (BrE)
a medicine that induces evacuation of the bowels; a purgative or laxative, especially as used in historical or medical contexts
a purgative medicine
archaic medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a strong physic to relieve the patient's constipation.
In the past, people often used herbal physic to cleanse the body.
After taking the physic, he soon felt an urgent need to visit the bathroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laxative - a medicine that encourages evacuation of the bowels
  • Synonym: purgative - a substance causing evacuation of the bowels

Etymology

From Old French 'fisique', from Latin 'physica' (natural science), from Greek 'phusikē'. Historically, 'physic' referred generally to medicine, but came to mean a purgative.
