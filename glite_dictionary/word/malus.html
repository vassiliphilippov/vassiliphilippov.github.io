Malus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪləs / (AmE), / ˈmeɪləs / (BrE)
a genus of trees and shrubs that includes apple trees, found throughout temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere
genus of apple trees

Examples

The genus Malus comprises several species, including the common apple and crabapple.
Horticulturists are breeding new varieties of Malus for disease resistance.
Many ornamental Malus trees are planted for their beautiful blossoms in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple tree - a tree that produces apples; common fruit tree

Etymology

From Latin 'malus' (apple tree).
