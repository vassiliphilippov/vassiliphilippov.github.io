sycophancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪkəˌfænsi / (AmE), / ˈsɪkəfənsi / (BrE)
obsequious or servile behavior towards someone in power to gain advantage or favor
fawning or servile behavior

Examples

The manager’s blatant sycophancy towards his boss annoyed his colleagues.
She despised sycophancy and admired people who were honest and direct.
Political sycophancy can undermine trust in public institutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsequiousness - excessive willingness to serve or please others
  • Synonym: flattery - excessive and insincere praise
  • Antonym: independence - freedom from influence or control by others

Etymology

From Greek sykophantia (informing, slander), via Latin sycophantia.
