Pyrophorus

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈrɒfərəs / (AmE), / pɪˈrɒfərəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical click beetles known for their ability to produce light through bioluminescence.
genus of tropical click beetles

Examples

The Pyrophorus beetle emits a remarkable glow to ward off predators at night.
Scientists have collected several Pyrophorus specimens in the rainforests of Brazil.
Many villagers believed Pyrophorus insects were magical due to their glowing bodies.

Etymology

From Greek pūr (fire) + phoros (bearing), referring to their light-producing ability.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪrəˈfɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˌpaɪərəˈfɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical click beetles known for their ability to produce light from glowing organs on their bodies.
a genus of tropical bioluminescent click beetles

Examples

Several species of Pyrophorus can be found in Central and South America, often attracted to lights at night.
The unique ability of Pyrophorus to emit light makes it of great interest to scientists studying bioluminescence.
Collectors often seek out members of the Pyrophorus genus because of their glowing organs.

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire) + 'phoros' (bearing), meaning 'fire-bearing.'
