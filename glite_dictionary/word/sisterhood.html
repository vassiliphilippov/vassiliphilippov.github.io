sisterhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstɚˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəˌhʊd / (BrE)
an association or community of women united by a common interest, religion, or trade
association or community of women

Examples

She joined the university's sisterhood to connect with other women pursuing engineering.
The sisterhood provides mentorship and support for its members.
A strong sense of sisterhood developed among the women in the organization.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fraternity - an association of men with shared interests or profession, often used for men's college societies

Etymology

From Middle English 'sosterhod', formed from 'sister' + '-hood'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstɚˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəˌhʊd / (BrE)
a society of women, especially nuns, who live together as a religious community
religious community of women; nuns' order

Examples

She devoted her life to the sisterhood and its charitable works.
The sisterhood gathered every morning for prayers and meditation.
Joining the sisterhood meant committing to a life of service and faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorority - a society of women, especially in colleges

Etymology

Formed from 'sister' + '-hood', denoting a group or state of being; 'sister' from Old English 'sweostor'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08092483-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstərˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəˌhʊd / (BrE)
The relationship between sisters, or between a sister and her siblings.
relationship between sisters or siblings

Examples

Despite their arguments, their sisterhood remained strong throughout the years.
She found a sense of sisterhood with her three siblings, always supporting each other.
After moving away, she missed the close sisterhood she shared with her sisters at home.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brotherhood - the kinship relation between a male offspring and the siblings

Etymology

From Middle English sosterhod, from Old English sweostor (sister) + -hood (state, condition).
