histrion

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪstriən / (AmE), / ˈhɪstriən / (BrE)
an actor, especially one who is overly theatrical or affected in manner
theatrical performer; actor
archaic

Examples

The histrion delivered his lines with such passion that the entire audience was captivated.
In ancient Rome, a histrion was both a performer and a composer of plays.
Some critics accused him of being more histrion than artist, relying too much on dramatic flair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actor - a person who performs in plays, movies, or television shows

Etymology

From Latin 'histrio, histrionis' (actor).
