Ephippiorhynchus senegalensis

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌfɪpiəroʊˈrɪŋkəs sɛnəgəˈlɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ɛˌfɪpɪəˌrɪŋkəs sɛnɪgəˈlɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A large black-and-white stork native to tropical Africa, noted for its distinctive red bill with a black band around the middle; also known as the saddle-billed stork.
African saddle-billed stork

Examples

The Ephippiorhynchus senegalensis is a striking sight along the rivers of sub-Saharan Africa.
Researchers observed a pair of Ephippiorhynchus senegalensis nesting near the wetlands.
Birdwatchers hope to spot the elusive Ephippiorhynchus senegalensis on their safari tour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saddle-billed stork - common name for Ephippiorhynchus senegalensis

Etymology

From Greek ephippion (saddle) + rhynchos (beak), and Latin Senegalia (Senegal) + -ensis (originating from).
