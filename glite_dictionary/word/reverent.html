reverent

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛvərənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛvərənt / (BrE)
feeling or showing deep respect and admiration for someone or something
showing deep respect

Examples

The congregation remained reverent throughout the ceremony.
She spoke in a reverent tone when describing her mentor.
A reverent silence fell over the crowd as the hero was honored.

Synset Examples

maintained a reverent silence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respectful - showing politeness or honor towards someone or something
  • Antonym: irreverent - not showing the expected respect or seriousness

Etymology

From Latin 'reverent-', present participle of 'revereri' (to stand in awe of).

WordNet ID: oewn-02018906-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛvərənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛvərənt / (BrE)
showing deep respect and admiration, especially towards God or something regarded as sacred
showing deep respect, especially for God

Examples

The congregation listened with reverent silence as the choir began to sing.
His reverent attitude towards the ancient traditions impressed the elders.
She spoke in a reverent tone when mentioning her mentor.

Synset Examples

a godly man
leading a godly life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respectful - showing respect; polite or considerate
  • Antonym: irreverent - showing lack of respect, especially for things that are generally taken seriously

Etymology

From Latin reverens, present participle of revereri (to stand in awe of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01786427-s
Synset POS: s
