Jaish-e-Mohammed

1. PROPN

/ dʒaɪʃ eɪ moʊˈhɑːməd / (AmE), / dʒaɪʃ eɪ məʊˈhæməd / (BrE)
A militant Islamist organization founded in 2000, primarily active in Kashmir, known for terrorist activities and seeking the release of imprisoned militants.
Islamist militant group founded in 2000

Examples

The Indian government accused Jaish-e-Mohammed of orchestrating the attack on the military base.
International sanctions were imposed on Jaish-e-Mohammed for its involvement in terrorist activities.
Reports suggest that Jaish-e-Mohammed receives support from various sources outside Kashmir.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: al-Qaeda - Islamist militant organization involved in global terrorism
  • Synonym: Lashkar-e-Taiba - Another major Islamist militant group active in the region
  • Synonym: terrorist organization - an organization that uses terror as a weapon to achieve its goals

Etymology

From Arabic: 'Jaish' means 'army', 'Mohammed' refers to the Prophet Muhammad, thus 'Army of Muhammad'.
