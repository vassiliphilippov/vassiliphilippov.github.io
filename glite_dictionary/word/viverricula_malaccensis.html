Viverricula malaccensis

1. NOUN

/ vɪˌvɛrɪˈkjuːlə mələˈkɛnsɪs / (AmE), / vɪˌvɛrɪˈkjuːlə mələˈkɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of small carnivorous mammal, commonly known as the Malay civet, native to Southeast Asia.
species of Southeast Asian civet

Examples

Researchers observed the elusive Viverricula malaccensis in several national parks across Borneo.
The diet of Viverricula malaccensis includes fruits, insects, and small vertebrates.
Conservation programs have been set up to protect Viverricula malaccensis from habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civet - small, nocturnal mammal noted for the musky smell produced by its scent glands

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Viverricula' (diminutive of Latin 'viverra', ferret) and species 'malaccensis' (from Malacca, a region in Southeast Asia).
