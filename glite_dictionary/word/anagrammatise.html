to anagrammatise

1. VERB

/ əˈnæɡrəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / əˈnaɡ.rə.mə.taɪz / (BrE)
to rearrange the letters of a word or phrase to form a different word or phrase, often to discover a hidden meaning
rearrange letters to form new words
british

Examples

Some cryptic crossword clues require you to anagrammatise certain words to find the solution.
He loves to anagrammatise famous names for fun.
The puzzle asked us to anagrammatise the letters in 'listen' to uncover a hidden word.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anagrammatize - to rearrange letters to form an anagram (American spelling)

Etymology

From anagram + -ise (British spelling), ultimately from Greek 'ana-' (again) + 'gramma' (letter).
