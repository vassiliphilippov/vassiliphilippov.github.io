to intervene

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntɚˈvin / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈviːn / (BrE)
to occur or be situated between events, periods, or objects in space or time
to occur or be situated between

Examples

Only a few months intervened between the announcement and the launch of the product.
Many years intervened before the two old friends met again.
A long silence intervened between their conversations.

Synset Examples

This interlude intervenes between the two movements
Eight days intervened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separate - to set or be between so as to keep apart; to divide

Etymology

From Latin intervenire, from inter (between) + venire (to come).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02697331-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈvin / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈviːn / (BrE)
to become involved in a situation in order to alter or prevent what is happening, often by force or threat of force
become involved to change a situation

Examples

The government may intervene to stabilize the economy during a financial crisis.
When the argument got out of hand, the teacher had to intervene quickly.
If no one intervenes, the conflict between the two groups could escalate further.

Synset Examples

Why did the U.S. not intervene earlier in WW II?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interfere - to become involved in a situation where your involvement is not wanted or is unnecessary
  • Synonym: mediate - to try to settle a dispute by intervening as a neutral party
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to a situation or event

Etymology

From Latin intervenire, from inter- ‘between’ + venire ‘to come’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02544431-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈviːn / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈviːn / (BrE)
to occur so as to interrupt or fall between other events or periods of time
occur between events (happen)

Examples

A severe drought intervened between the two rainy seasons.
Several years intervened before the next major outbreak occurred.
The construction of the dam intervened between the floods of 1955 and 1962.

Synset Examples

the war intervened between the birth of her two children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrupt - to stop the continuous progress of an event or process
  • Synonym: separate - to set or keep apart events or objects in space or time
  • Antonym: to continue - to persist without interruption (opposite sense)

Etymology

From Latin 'intervenire', from 'inter-' (between) + 'venire' (to come).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00341638-v
Synset POS: v
