canonist

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænənɪst / (AmE), / ˈkanənɪst / (BrE)
an expert or specialist in canon law, especially of the Christian church
specialist in canon law

Examples

The church appointed a respected canonist to advise on matters of doctrine.
As a leading canonist, she contributed to the reform of ecclesiastical courts.
In the Middle Ages, a canonist held significant influence within the church hierarchy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canon lawyer - an expert in canon law, especially as a profession
  • Synonym: jurist - a legal scholar or expert in law

Etymology

From Medieval Latin canonista, from canon (church law).
