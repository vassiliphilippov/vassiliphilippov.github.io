glomerulonephritis

1. NOUN

/ ɡloʊˌmɛr.jə.loʊ.nəˈfraɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ɡlɒˌmɛr.jʊ.ləʊ.nɪˈfraɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A kidney disease marked by inflammation of the glomeruli, leading to reduced urine output, and the presence of blood and protein in the urine.
inflammation of kidney glomeruli
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that glomerulonephritis was causing the blood in her urine.
Children can sometimes develop glomerulonephritis after a throat infection.
Early diagnosis of glomerulonephritis can help prevent long-term kidney damage.

Etymology

From Latin glomerulus (a small ball) + nephritis (inflammation of the kidney, from Greek nephros).
