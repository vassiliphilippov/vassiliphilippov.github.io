rearing

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the way a child is brought up and cared for, especially in terms of the treatment and environment provided by parents or guardians
child upbringing; parental nurture

Examples

Her strict rearing influenced the way she approached challenges as an adult.
Debates about the best methods of rearing children have been ongoing for centuries.
He credited his moral values to his rearing in a supportive and loving household.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upbringing - the treatment and education received by a child from its parents

Etymology

From the verb 'rear' meaning 'to bring up, nurture', in use since Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of raising and caring for children or young animals, especially to help them develop into accepted members of society
raising and caring for children or young animals

Examples

The rearing of children requires patience and consistency from parents.
Debates about child rearing often focus on the influences of nature versus nurture.
She wrote a book about the rearing of puppies and the challenges it entails.

Synset Examples

they debated whether nature or nurture was more important

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upbringing - the treatment and instruction received by a child from its parents throughout its childhood
  • Synonym: parenting - the activity of bringing up and caring for children

Etymology

From the verb 'rear', meaning 'to raise'; from Old English rǣran.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01131853-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈrɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an animal, especially in heraldry) standing on the left hind leg with the forelegs elevated and the head usually in profile.
standing erect on hind leg (heraldic)

Examples

The family crest displays a rearing lion with its paw ready to strike.
In heraldry, a rearing unicorn often symbolizes purity and bravery.
The statue portrays a horse rearing, reminiscent of classic heraldic images.

Synset Examples

a lion rampant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rampant - (of an animal in heraldry) standing with forepaws raised
