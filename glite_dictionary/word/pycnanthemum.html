Pycnanthemum

1. PROPN

/ pɪkˈnænθəməm / (AmE), / pɪkˈnænθɪməm / (BrE)
A genus of mint plants native to North America, commonly known as American mountain mint, valued for its aromatic properties and small white or purple flowers.
genus of American mountain mint plants

Examples

Pycnanthemum grows well in well-drained soils and attracts many pollinators to gardens.
Botanists identified the species as part of the Pycnanthemum genus.
The aromatic leaves of Pycnanthemum have been traditionally used in herbal remedies.

Etymology

From Greek puknos (dense) + anthos (flower), referring to the dense clusters of flowers.

2. NOUN

/ pɪkˈnænθəməm / (AmE), / pɪkˈnanθɪməm / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic North American plants in the mint family (Lamiaceae), commonly called mountain mints.
genus of American mountain mints

Examples

Several species of Pycnanthemum are grown in herb gardens for their aromatic leaves.
The flowers of Pycnanthemum attract a wide variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
Botanists often study Pycnanthemum to learn more about North American native plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain mint - common name for members of the genus Pycnanthemum

Etymology

From Greek 'pyknos' (dense) and 'anthemion' (flower), referring to the plant’s dense floral clusters.
