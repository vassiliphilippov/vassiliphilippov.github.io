unsatiable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈseɪʃəbl̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈseɪʃɪəbl̩ / (BrE)
(rare) Impossible to satisfy; always wanting more.
impossible to satisfy

Examples

Her ambition was unsatiable, driving her to seek ever greater challenges.
He felt an unsatiable curiosity about the mysteries of the universe.
The crowd's unsatiable desire for entertainment kept the performers on stage late into the night.

Synset Examples

an insatiate appetite
an insatiable demand for old buildings to restore
his passion for work was unsatiable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insatiable - Impossible to satisfy; always wanting more.

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'un-' (not) + 'satiable' (able to be satisfied), modeled after 'insatiable'.
