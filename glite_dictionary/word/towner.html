towner

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊnər / (AmE), / ˈtaʊnə / (BrE)
a person who lives in a town or city, especially as distinguished from someone who lives in the countryside
a town or city resident

Examples

As a towner, she wasn't used to the quiet of the countryside.
The towner found rural traditions fascinating but unfamiliar.
Many towners visit the region during holidays to escape city life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: villager - a person who lives in a village
  • Antonym: countryman - a person who lives in the countryside

Etymology

Formed from 'town' + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
