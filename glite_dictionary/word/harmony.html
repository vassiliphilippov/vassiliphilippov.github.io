harmony

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːrməni / (AmE), / ˈhɑːməni / (BrE)
agreement; a state in which people or things agree in ideas, feelings, or actions.
agreement of opinions

Examples

After the discussion, the two leaders reached a surprising harmony of views.
Living in harmony with others requires patience and understanding.
Their approach to the problem was marked by harmony rather than conflict.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discord - lack of agreement or harmony between people or things
  • Synonym: consensus - general agreement among a group

Etymology

From Latin 'harmonia', from Greek 'harmonía' meaning 'concord, agreement, joining'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːr.mə.ni / (AmE), / ˈhɑː.mə.ni / (BrE)
the combination of simultaneously sounded musical notes to produce chords and chord progressions having a pleasing effect, or the study and structure of these in music
musical combination of chords

Examples

She studied the harmony of Beethoven's late string quartets in her music theory class.
The beauty of the song comes from its rich harmony and intricate chord progressions.
Jazz musicians are known for their innovative use of harmony to create unique sounds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: melody - musical term for a sequence of single notes that is musically satisfying

Etymology

From Greek harmonia (joint, agreement, concord), via Latin harmonia.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɑr.mə.ni / (AmE), / ˈhɑː.mə.ni / (BrE)
A pleasing or congruous arrangement of parts, as in music, color, or design; a state in which elements blend or work well together.
congruity or pleasing arrangement of parts

Examples

The harmony of colors in the painting created a peaceful atmosphere.
Their voices blended in perfect harmony during the performance.
There must be harmony between the different departments for the company to succeed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discord - lack of harmony or agreement among elements
  • Synonym: congruence - agreement or harmony; compatibility

Etymology

From Greek harmonia (agreement, concord).
