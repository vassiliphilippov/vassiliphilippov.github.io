wildfire

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪldˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪldˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a large, uncontrolled fire that spreads quickly over a natural area, especially in forests or grasslands
fast-spreading uncontrolled fire

Examples

The wildfire swept through the national park in just a few hours, destroying thousands of acres.
Firefighters worked tirelessly to contain the wildfire before it reached the nearby town.
Drought conditions make the region especially vulnerable to wildfire outbreaks during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflagration - a large, destructive fire
  • Degree Lower: bonfire - a large open-air fire often for celebration

Etymology

Compound of 'wild' and 'fire'; 'wild' from Old English *wilde*, 'fire' from Old English *fyr*.
