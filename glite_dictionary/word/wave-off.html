wave-off

1. NOUN

/ weɪv ˈɑf / (AmE), / weɪv ˈɒf / (BrE)
an aborted approach, landing, or attempt, especially when instructing someone to stop and try again
an aborted approach or attempt

Examples

The pilot performed a wave-off after noticing another plane on the runway.
After two wave-offs, the astronaut finally managed to dock with the station.
The referee called a wave-off and the players reset for the next attempt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abort - to stop an action or attempt before completion
  • Synonym: to retry - to try again after a failed attempt
