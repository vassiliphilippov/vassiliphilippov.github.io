chlorambucil

1. NOUN

/ klɔːrˈæmbjʊsɪl / (AmE), / klɔːˈræmbjʊsɪl / (BrE)
a drug used as an alkylating agent to treat certain cancers, such as leukemia and lymphoma
alkylating cancer drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed chlorambucil as a treatment for the patient's chronic lymphocytic leukemia.
Some lymphoma patients respond well to chlorambucil, which is usually taken in tablet form.
Due to its side effects, chlorambucil is administered under close medical supervision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Leukeran - trade name for chlorambucil, anticancer drug

Etymology

From chemical components: chloro- (chlorine), am(bu)tyric, (b)utyl, and -il.
