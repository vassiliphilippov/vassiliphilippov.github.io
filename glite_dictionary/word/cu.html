Cu

1. NOUN

/ siː juː / (AmE), / siː juː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for copper, a ductile, reddish-brown metallic element used especially as an electrical and thermal conductor
chemical symbol for copper

Examples

The electrical wire is made of pure Cu for its excellent conductivity.
In chemistry class, we studied the reactions of Cu with other elements.
The old statue developed a green patina because it was cast in Cu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copper - a reddish-brown, ductile metallic element

Etymology

Symbol from Latin *cuprum* (copper).
