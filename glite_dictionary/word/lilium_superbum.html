Lilium superbum

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪliəm suːˈpɜrbəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪliəm suːˈpɜːbəm / (BrE)
A species of lily native to the eastern United States, known for its tall stem and orange to red, maroon-spotted flowers.
a lily species of eastern North America

Examples

The garden was dominated by the striking Lilium superbum in full bloom.
Wildlife enthusiasts admire Lilium superbum for its vibrant, spotted flowers.
Conservationists are working to protect native Lilium superbum habitats.

Etymology

From Latin; 'lilium' meaning 'lily' and 'superbum' meaning 'superb' or 'magnificent'.
