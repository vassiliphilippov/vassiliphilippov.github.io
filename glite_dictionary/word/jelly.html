jelly

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛli / (BrE)
a sweet, clear preserve made by boiling fruit juice and sugar until it sets into a gel
sweet preserve made from fruit juice

Examples

She spread grape jelly on her toast every morning for breakfast.
It takes several hours for homemade jelly to set properly.
The recipe calls for one cup of apple jelly as a sweetener.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam - a preserve made by boiling fruit with sugar, typically containing pieces of fruit
  • Synonym: marmalade - a preserve made from citrus fruit, especially oranges, including the rind

Etymology

From Old French *gelée* (frost, jelly), from Latin *gelata* (jellied thing), from *gelare* (to freeze).

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛli / (BrE)
to make or become gelatinous or like jelly; to cause to solidify or set as jelly does.
to make or become gelatinous

Examples

The mixture will jelly as it cools, forming a firm dessert.
Let the fruit juice jelly before pouring it into jars.
When chilled, the gelatin will jelly the soup into a solid mass.

Synset Examples

jellify a liquid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gel - to become or make into a semi-solid, jelly-like substance

Etymology

From the noun 'jelly', which comes from Old French *gelée* (frost, jelly), from *geler* (to freeze), from Latin *gelāre* (to freeze).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛli / (BrE)
A soft, semifluid, often transparent food made with gelatin and flavoured with fruit juice or other ingredients, eaten as a dessert or used as a glaze.
gelatin-based dessert or coating

Examples

She served strawberry jelly with whipped cream for dessert.
The chicken was coated with a savory jelly glaze before roasting.
For the party, they made colorful fruit jelly in fun shapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam - a sweet spread made from fruit and sugar, often confused with jelly

Etymology

From Old French 'gelée' (frost, jelly), from 'geler' (to freeze), from Latin 'gelare' (to freeze).

4. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛli / (BrE)
Any substance having a soft, elastic, and gelatinous consistency, like that of gelatin or jelly.
substance with jelly-like consistency

Examples

The dessert was topped with a sweet, red jelly that wobbled gently on the plate.
Some creams and ointments are prepared in a jelly form to make them easier to apply.
She slipped on a patch of algae that felt just like jelly under her feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gel - a semi-solid, jelly-like substance
  • Degree Lower: paste - a thick, soft, moist substance
  • Synonym: gelatin - a clear, tasteless, protein-based substance used to make jelly

Etymology

From Old French 'gelée' (frost, jelly), from Latin 'gelata', from 'gelare' (to freeze).
