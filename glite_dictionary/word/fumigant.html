fumigant

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːmɪɡənt / (AmE), / ˈfjuːmɪɡənt / (BrE)
a chemical substance used to disinfect or purify an area by producing fumes
chemical used in fumigation

Examples

The warehouse was treated with a fumigant to eliminate insect infestations.
Proper ventilation is necessary after using a fumigant in enclosed spaces.
Many fumigants are hazardous and require strict safety precautions during handling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pesticide - a substance used for destroying pests

Etymology

From Latin 'fumigare' (to smoke), formed with the suffix '-ant' indicating an agent.
