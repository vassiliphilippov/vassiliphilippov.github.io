Angelica archangelica

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdʒɛlɪkə ˌɑːrkɑnˈdʒɛlɪkə / (AmE), / ænˈdʒɛlɪkə ˌɑːkænˈdʒɛlɪkə / (BrE)
a tall biennial herb whose stems are candied and eaten and whose roots are used medicinally
medicinal and culinary herb

Examples

Candied Angelica archangelica stems are often used to decorate cakes and pastries.
Traditional herbalists valued Angelica archangelica roots for their supposed medicinal properties.
Botanists cultivated Angelica archangelica in gardens for both ornamental and culinary use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angelica - a genus of herbs including Angelica archangelica

Etymology

From Medieval Latin, combining 'angelica' (angelic) and 'archangelica' (archangel), referring to its legendary medicinal qualities.
