also-ran

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔlsoʊˌræn / (AmE), / ˈɔːlsəʊˌran / (BrE)
a person or animal in a race or competition who does not win or is not among the top finishers; someone whose performance is unremarkable compared to the winners
contestant who does not win

Examples

Despite his best efforts, he finished the race as an also-ran and did not receive any prize.
Many political candidates become also-rans after failing to gain enough votes in the election.
The novelist was once an also-ran, but later gained critical acclaim with a best-selling book.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: winner - a person who wins a contest or competition

Etymology

From horse racing, meaning a horse that also ran but did not win or place.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔlsoʊˌræn / (AmE), / ˈɔːlsəʊˌran / (BrE)
a person or thing that has little or no chance of winning a competition or achieving success
unsuccessful competitor or candidate

Examples

Despite his best efforts, he finished as an also-ran in the tournament.
Once considered a top contender, she soon became an also-ran in the election.
Without innovative ideas, the company remained just another also-ran in the tech industry.

Synset Examples

She was considered an also-ran in the competition for the award.
In the race, he was just an also-ran without much chance of winning.
After the election, many candidates became also-rans.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: winner - person or thing that wins a competition

Etymology

Originally from horse racing (late 19th century), meaning a horse that also ran in a race but did not win or place.
