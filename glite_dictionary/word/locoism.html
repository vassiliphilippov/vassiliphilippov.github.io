locoism

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊkoʊˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəʊˌɪzəm / (BrE)
A disease of livestock caused by poisoning from locoweed, characterized by weakness, lack of coordination, trembling, and partial paralysis.
disease of livestock from locoweed poisoning
medical

Examples

Several cattle on the ranch were diagnosed with locoism after grazing on locoweed for weeks.
The symptoms of locoism include tremors, weakness, and an unsteady gait.
Veterinarians warn that locoism can become fatal if affected animals are not removed from locoweed pastures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poisoning - the condition resulting from the ingestion or absorption of a harmful substance

Etymology

From "loco" (Spanish for 'crazy') + -ism; referencing the plant's effect on animal behavior.
