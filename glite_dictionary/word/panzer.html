panzer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænzər / (AmE), / ˈpantsə / (BrE)
A German armored fighting vehicle, especially a tank used during World War II.
German armored fighting vehicle (tank)

Examples

The German army advanced their panzers across the French countryside during World War II.
Collectors at the museum admired the restored panzer on display.
A panzer division was pivotal in the early stages of the battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tank - an armored combat vehicle, typically with tracks and a large gun

Etymology

From German 'Panzer' (armor, tank), originally meaning 'armor' or 'coat of mail'.
