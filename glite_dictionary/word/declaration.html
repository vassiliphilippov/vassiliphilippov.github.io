declaration

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛkləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛkləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
(law) An unsworn statement that may be admitted as evidence in a legal proceeding.
unsworn statement in legal proceedings
legal

Examples

The judge reviewed the declaration before allowing it as evidence during the trial.
The lawyer submitted a sworn affidavit, but the declaration was not under oath.
Her declaration of innocence became a crucial part of the legal case.

Synset Examples

his declaration of innocence

Etymology

From Latin declaratio (making clear), from declarare (make clear).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛkləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛkləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)

final bid in contract bridge

Examples

After a bold declaration, the opponents prepared their strategies for defense.
The team celebrated when their declaration led to a successful game.

Related Terms
