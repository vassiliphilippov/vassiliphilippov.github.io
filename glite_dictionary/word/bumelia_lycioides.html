Bumelia lycioides

1. NOUN

/ buːˈmiːliə laɪsiˈoʊdiːz / (AmE), / bjuːˈmiːliə laɪsɪˈəʊdiːz / (BrE)
A shrubby, thorny deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States, bearing white flowers and small black drupe-like fruit.
thorny tree of southeastern US

Examples

The southeastern forests are home to the Bumelia lycioides, known for its small black fruits.
Botanists studied a cluster of Bumelia lycioides along the riverbank.
Many birds are attracted to the drupes produced by Bumelia lycioides.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name *Bumelia* and species epithet *lycioides*, meaning 'resembling Lycium'.
