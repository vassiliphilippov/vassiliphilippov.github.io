lymphography

1. NOUN

/ lɪmˈfɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / lɪmˈfɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
an X-ray examination of lymph nodes and lymph vessels after the injection of a contrast medium
X-ray imaging of lymphatic system
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a lymphography to assess potential blockages in the lymphatic system.
During the lymphography, a contrast dye was injected to highlight the lymph vessels.
Modern imaging techniques are gradually replacing lymphography for certain diagnoses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lymphangiogram - an X-ray image produced by lymphography

Etymology

From Greek 'lymph' (clear fluid) + '-graphy' (writing, recording).
