agranulocytosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪˌɡræn.jəˌluː.saɪˈtoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌæɡ.ræn.jʊˌluː.saɪˈtəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A serious medical condition in which the number of granulocytes (a type of white blood cell) in the blood is severely reduced, making the body more vulnerable to infection.
acute blood disorder with low granulocytes
medical

Examples

The patient developed agranulocytosis after taking the prescribed medication, requiring immediate medical intervention.
Doctors must monitor blood counts to detect agranulocytosis during chemotherapy.
Fever and sore throat are early warning signs of agranulocytosis in susceptible patients.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: leukopenia - a decrease in the number of white blood cells

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'granulum' (small grain, Latin) + 'cytosis' (condition of cells, Greek).
