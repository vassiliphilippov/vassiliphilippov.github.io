knobkerry

1. NOUN

/ nɑbˌkɛri / (AmE), / ˈnɒbˌkɛri / (BrE)
A short wooden club with a rounded knob at one end, traditionally used as a weapon or tool by some indigenous peoples of southern Africa.
short club with a knobbed end

Examples

The museum displayed a knobkerry used by a 19th-century Zulu warrior.
He carried a knobkerry for protection during his travels across the savannah.
A beautifully carved knobkerry stood among the collection of traditional African weapons.

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'knopkirie', from Dutch 'knop' (knob) + Khoikhoi 'kiri' (stick).
