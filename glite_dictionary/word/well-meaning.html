well-meaning

1. ADJ

/ ˌwɛlˈmiːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌwɛlˈmiːnɪŋ / (BrE)
Intending to do good or help, even if not always effectively or successfully.
having good intentions

Examples

Though her advice was well-meaning, it sometimes made things more complicated.
A group of well-meaning volunteers arrived to help with the cleanup.
He gave a well-meaning but ultimately unhelpful suggestion.

Synset Examples

her well-meaning words were received in silence
the exasperation of a … well-meaning cow worried by dogs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: well-intentioned - having good intentions
  • Antonym: malicious - intending to do harm

2. ADJ

/ wɛlˈmiːnɪŋ / (AmE), / wɛlˈmiːnɪŋ / (BrE)
intending to do good or help others, even if the results are not always successful or welcome
having good intentions

Examples

His well-meaning advice sometimes ended up causing more problems than it solved.
The well-meaning volunteers distributed food to the community despite the confusion.
Her well-meaning comments were misunderstood and hurt his feelings.

Synset Examples

a well-intentioned but clumsy waiter
a well-meaning but tactless fellow
the son's well-meaning efforts threw a singular chill upon the father's admirers
blunt but well-meant criticism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: well-intentioned - having good or kind intentions
  • Antonym: ill-intentioned - having harmful intentions
