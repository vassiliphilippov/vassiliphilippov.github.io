risibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɪzəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌrɪzɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able or inclined to laugh; a disposition to laughter
disposition to laugh

Examples

Her natural risibility made her a delight at parties.
Despite the seriousness of the meeting, a sudden joke triggered his risibility.
The comedian was famous for evoking risibility even in the sternest audience members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mirth - amusement or laughter
  • Antonym: gloom - lack of cheerfulness or laughter

Etymology

From Latin *risibilis* (laughable), from *ridere* (to laugh).
