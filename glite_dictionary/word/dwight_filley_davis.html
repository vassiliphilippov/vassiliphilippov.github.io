Dwight Filley Davis

1. PROPN

/ ˈdwaɪt ˈfɪli ˈdeɪvɪs / (AmE), / ˈdwaɪt ˈfɪli ˈdeɪvɪs / (BrE)
(1879–1945) United States tennis player and philanthropist who founded and donated the Davis Cup for international team tennis competition.
U.S. tennis player and founder of Davis Cup

Examples

Dwight Filley Davis is remembered for creating the Davis Cup, one of the most prestigious events in tennis.
As a skilled player, Dwight Filley Davis also contributed significantly to the sport's international popularity.
The legacy of Dwight Filley Davis endures through the annual Davis Cup competition.
