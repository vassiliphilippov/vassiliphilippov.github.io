carrageen

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærəˌɡin / (AmE), / ˈkærəˌɡiːn / (BrE)
a dark purple edible seaweed found on the Atlantic coasts of Europe and North America, commonly used as a thickener in foods
edible Atlantic seaweed

Examples

Many people use carrageen to thicken soups, desserts, and milk puddings.
Harvesters collect carrageen along the rocky shores during low tide.
A traditional remedy for cough is made by simmering carrageen with milk and honey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Irish moss - another name for carrageen seaweed

Etymology

From Irish 'carraigín' meaning 'little rock', referring to where it grows.
