inhumed

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈhjumd / (AmE), / ɪnˈhjuːmd / (BrE)
(of a body) placed in a grave; buried.
buried in a grave
archaic

Examples

The ancient tomb held the inhumed remains of a forgotten king.
Several inhumed soldiers were discovered during the excavation of the battlefield.
The poet wrote about the inhumed sorrows of the heart.

Synset Examples

the hastily buried corpses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buried - placed in a grave; covered with earth
  • Antonym: exhumed - removed from a grave; dug up

Etymology

From Latin 'inhumare', from 'in-' (into) + 'humus' (earth).
