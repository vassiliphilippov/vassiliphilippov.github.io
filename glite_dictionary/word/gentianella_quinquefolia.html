Gentianella quinquefolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn.tʃəˈnɛl.ə ˌkwɪŋ.kwɪˈfoʊ.li.ə / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn.tʃɪˈnɛl.ə ˌkwɪŋ.kwɪˈfəʊ.lɪ.ə / (BrE)
A species of gentian native to eastern North America, known for its clusters of bristly blue flowers.
North American gentian with blue flowers

Examples

The meadow was dotted with clusters of Gentianella quinquefolia in full bloom.
Botanists surveyed the site and recorded the presence of Gentianella quinquefolia among other wildflowers.
Hikers admired the delicate blue flowers of Gentianella quinquefolia along the forest trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentian - any plant of the genus Gentiana or related genera, with usually blue flowers

Etymology

From Latin: genus Gentianella, and species epithet quinquefolia meaning 'five-leaved'.
