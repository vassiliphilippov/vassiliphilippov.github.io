ostrich

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑstrɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒstrɪtʃ / (BrE)
a large, fast-running African flightless bird with two-toed feet, known as the largest living bird
largest African flightless bird

Examples

The ostrich is famous for its powerful legs and high running speed.
Unlike most birds, the ostrich cannot fly but can outrun many predators on land.
At the wildlife reserve, we watched a group of ostriches grazing in the field.

Etymology

From Old French *ostriche*, from Latin *avis struthio* (ostrich), from Greek *strouthiōn* (ostrich).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.strɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒs.trɪtʃ / (BrE)
a person who refuses to face or accept unpleasant facts, often by pretending a problem does not exist
person who avoids facing reality

Examples

He acted like an ostrich, refusing to admit there was a problem with the project.
Don't be an ostrich—ignoring your health issues won't make them go away.
Many politicians are ostriches when it comes to addressing climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denialist - a person who refuses to accept an established truth

Etymology

From the figurative reference to the bird's supposed habit of burying its head in the sand to avoid danger.
