Cephalochordata

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛfəloʊˈkɔr.də.tə / (AmE), / ˌsɛfə.ləˈkɔː.dətə / (BrE)
A subphylum of chordate animals characterized by a notochord that persists throughout life, including lancelets.
subphylum of chordate animals (lancelets)

Examples

The Cephalochordata are small, fish-like marine animals found buried in sand.
Scientists study Cephalochordata to better understand the evolution of vertebrates.
Unlike vertebrates, Cephalochordata maintain a flexible notochord throughout their lives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lancelet - a small marine animal in the subphylum Cephalochordata

Etymology

From Greek kephalē ('head') + chordē ('cord') + Latin -ata (suffix used in taxonomic names).

2. NOUN

/ sɛfəloʊˌkɔrdəˈteɪtə / (AmE), / sɛfəˌlɒkɔːdəˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
a subphylum of chordate animals characterized by a notochord that extends the length of the body and persists throughout life, including lancelets
chordate subphylum with persistent notochord

Examples

The Cephalochordata are notable for retaining their notochord throughout their life cycle.
Lancelets belong to the subphylum Cephalochordata, which is separate from vertebrates.
Research on Cephalochordata has provided insights into the evolution of chordates.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Chordata - the phylum comprising animals with a notochord
  • Degree Higher: Vertebrata - the subphylum of chordates having vertebral columns

Etymology

From Greek kephalē (head) + khordē (string, cord), referencing the position and structure of the notochord.
