Mirabilis laevis

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈræbɪlɪs ˈlaɪvɪs / (AmE), / mɪˈræbɪlɪs ˈliːvɪs / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant native to California and surrounding regions, known as California four o'clock, with purple-red flowers.
a species of California four o'clock plant

Examples

The hillside was covered with blooming Mirabilis laevis in the late afternoon sun.
Botanists studied the ecological role of Mirabilis laevis in local habitats.
Gardeners enjoy growing Mirabilis laevis for its vivid purple-red flowers and drought resistance.

Etymology

From Latin: 'mirabilis' meaning 'wonderful' and 'laevis' meaning 'smooth'.
