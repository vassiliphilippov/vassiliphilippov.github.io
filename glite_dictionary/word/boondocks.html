boondocks

1. NOUN

/ ˈbunˌdɑks / (AmE), / ˈbuːndɒks / (BrE)
an area that is far away from towns or cities and lacks modern conveniences; a remote, rural, or undeveloped region
remote, undeveloped area
informal

Examples

He grew up way out in the boondocks, far from any big city.
After their car broke down, they realized they were stranded in the boondocks.
Many people think living in the boondocks means giving up modern comforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backwoods - remote and sparsely inhabited rural area
  • Synonym: wilderness - an uncultivated, uninhabited, and inhospitable region
  • Antonym: city - a large town or urban area

Etymology

From Tagalog 'bundok' (mountain), popularized by American soldiers in the Philippines in the early 20th century.
