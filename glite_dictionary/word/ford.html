ford

1. NOUN

/ fɔrd / (AmE), / fɔːd / (BrE)
A shallow place in a river or stream where people or vehicles can cross.
shallow crossing place in river

Examples

We had to wade through the ford to reach the other side of the river.
The path led down to a ford where the water was only knee-deep.
During the dry season, the stream's ford is safe for cattle to cross.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crossing - the act of going across a river or road

Etymology

From Old English 'ford' (a crossing, a shallow place in water).

2. NOUN

/ fɔrd / (AmE), / fɔːd / (BrE)
an act of crossing a shallow part of a river or stream by wading, riding, or driving through it
act of crossing a shallow river or stream

Examples

The travelers searched for a safe ford before attempting to cross the river.
During the hike, we came across a shallow ford that was easy to cross.
Early pioneers often used a ford to avoid building bridges over wide streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wade - to walk through water or another substance that impedes movement

Etymology

Old English 'ford' (shallow place where water can be crossed).

3. VERB

/ fɔrd / (AmE), / fɔːd / (BrE)
to cross a river or stream at a shallow place where it can be walked or driven across
cross a shallow part of a river

Examples

Early explorers would often ford the river instead of building bridges.
We had to ford the stream to reach the campsite on the other side.
The hikers carefully forded the shallow section of the creek.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wade - to walk through water or another substance that impedes movement

Etymology

Old English *fordian* (to cross a ford), from *ford* (a shallow place in water).
