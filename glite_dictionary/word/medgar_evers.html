Medgar Evers

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛdɡər ˈɛvərz / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɡə ˈɛvəz / (BrE)
(1925–1963) An American civil rights activist in Mississippi who was assassinated because of his work in advancing civil rights.
US civil rights activist (1925–1963)

Examples

Medgar Evers played a pivotal role in the fight against segregation in Mississippi.
The assassination of Medgar Evers shocked the nation and intensified the civil rights movement.
Many schools and streets have been named after Medgar Evers to honor his legacy.
