bayat

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈjɑːt / (AmE), / baɪˈjɑːt / (BrE)
an oath of allegiance to an emir or leader, especially in Islamic tradition
oath of allegiance to an emir

Examples

The new sultan received the bayat from tribal leaders after his coronation.
Historically, the bayat ceremony marked the formal transfer of loyalty to a new ruler.
Every eligible citizen was called to swear the bayat as a sign of unity and support.

Etymology

From Arabic بَيْعَة‎ (bayʕa), meaning 'oath of allegiance'.
