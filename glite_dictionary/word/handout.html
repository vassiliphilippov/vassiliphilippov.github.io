handout

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈhændˌaʊt / (BrE)
an official statement or document given to journalists to provide information or supplement an oral presentation
official information given to the press

Examples

The company distributed a handout at the press conference detailing its new strategy.
Journalists received a handout summarizing the main points of the announcement.
Copies of the official handout were available in the media room after the briefing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: press release - an official statement issued to newspapers giving information on a particular matter

2. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈhændˌaʊt / (BrE)
something such as money, food, or clothing that is given free to a person in need
something given free to someone in need

Examples

Due to rising unemployment, more families are relying on handouts from local charities.
The government increased handouts for those affected by the crisis.
He was too proud to accept a handout, even when he needed help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donation - something that is given to help a person or organization
  • Synonym: grant - a sum of money given by an organization, especially for a particular purpose

Etymology

From the phrase 'hand out', meaning to give out by hand (early 20th century).
