slightness

1. NOUN

/ ˈslaɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈslaɪtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being attractively thin or delicately built
attractively thin quality

Examples

Her slightness gave her a certain elegance that was hard to ignore.
The model’s slightness made her ideal for certain designer fashions.
There was a graceful slightness to his figure that caught everyone’s attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slenderness - the quality of being gracefully thin
  • Antonym: robustness - the quality of being strong and healthy; sturdiness

Etymology

From 'slight' (meaning thin or slender) + '-ness' (state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈslaɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈslaɪtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being unimportant or insignificant; triviality
lack of importance; triviality

Examples

His criticism had a certain slightness that made it easy to ignore.
She dismissed the comment with a wave of her hand, annoyed by its slightness.
The slightness of the issue made everyone wonder why it caused such a fuss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triviality - the quality or state of being trivial; lack of seriousness or importance
  • Antonym: significance - the quality of being worthy of attention; importance

Etymology

From 'slight' (meaning 'small' or 'insignificant') + '-ness', forming an abstract noun.

3. NOUN

/ ˈslaɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈslaɪtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being slim, small, or not robust; slenderness or lack of strength or substance.
quality of being slim or insubstantial

Examples

The slightness of her frame made her appear very delicate in the cold wind.
Despite the slightness of the evidence, the police continued their investigation.
His slightness did not prevent him from excelling in sports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slenderness - the quality of being elegantly thin or slim

Etymology

From 'slight' + '-ness', with 'slight' from Middle English 'slight' (smooth, slender), probably from Old Norse 'slétt' (smooth, level).
