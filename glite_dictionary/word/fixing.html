fixing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪksɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of repairing or putting something in working order again.
the act of repairing something

Examples

The fixing of the leaking pipe took several hours to complete.
After some quick fixing, my laptop was working perfectly again.
The car needed immediate fixing after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repair - the act of fixing something that is broken or damaged

Etymology

From the verb 'fix' (to repair), with the -ing suffix forming the noun.

/ ˈfɪksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪksɪŋ / (BrE)
the surgical sterilization of an animal to prevent reproduction, especially as a routine veterinary procedure
sterilization of an animal

Examples

After the fixing of their cat, the owners noticed her behavior changed for the better.
Many shelters require fixing before an animal is adopted out to prevent unwanted litters.
The vet scheduled the puppy’s fixing for next Tuesday.

/ ˈfɪksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪksɪŋ / (BrE)
a device or part used to fasten, secure, or attach something in place
device or part for fastening or securing

Examples

The picture frames came with the necessary fixings for mounting them on the wall.
Please check that all the fixings are secure before using the swing set.
Brass fixings were chosen for their durability and classic appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fastener - a device that attaches or secures objects together

Etymology

From the verb 'fix', ultimately from Latin 'fixus', past participle of 'figere' (to fasten).

preservation process in histology
medical

Examples

Without adequate fixing, the cellular structures would deteriorate rapidly.
