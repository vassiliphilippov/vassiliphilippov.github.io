bankbook

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈbaŋkˌbʊk / (BrE)
a small book kept by a depositor and maintained by a bank, recording deposits, withdrawals, and interest earned on an account
book recording bank account transactions

Examples

She took her bankbook to the teller to update the balance after depositing her paycheck.
The bankbook showed all transactions since the account was opened.
With online banking, the use of a physical bankbook has become less common.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passbook - a book for recording bank transactions

Etymology

Formed from 'bank' (financial institution) + 'book' (record).
