Woodwardia

1. PROPN

/ wʊdˈwɑrdiə / (AmE), / wʊdˈwɑːdiə / (BrE)
A genus of ferns known as chain ferns, commonly found in moist, shady areas, classified in some systems in the family Polypodiaceae.
genus of chain ferns

Examples

The garden's shade was filled with lush Woodwardia thriving beside the stream.
Several species in the Woodwardia genus are prized for their ornamental foliage.
Botanists identified the ancient fossil as a member of Woodwardia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - non-flowering vascular plant with feathery fronds

Etymology

Named after Thomas Jenkinson Woodward (1745–1820), English botanist.

2. NOUN

/ wʊdˈwɑrdiə / (AmE), / wʊdˈwɑːdɪə / (BrE)
A genus of large, mostly evergreen ferns commonly known as chain ferns, native to temperate regions; sometimes placed in the family Polypodiaceae by certain classification systems.
genus of chain ferns (plants)

Examples

The lush growth of Woodwardia gives the garden a tropical feel.
Woodwardia ferns can reach impressive sizes along moist stream banks.
He specializes in the study of Woodwardia and other hardy ferns.

Etymology

Named after Thomas Jenkinson Woodward (1745–1820), an English botanist.
