eery

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪri / (AmE), / ˈɪəri / (BrE)
strange in a way that causes fear or uneasiness; frightening and mysterious.
strangely frightening or uncanny

Examples

The abandoned house took on an eery silence as night fell.
She described the forest as eery after sundown, when the fog rolled in.
An eery feeling crept over the group as they explored the old building.

Synset Examples

an uncomfortable and eerie stillness in the woods
an eerie midnight howl

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eerie - strange and frightening, causing uneasiness

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'eerie', from Scottish 'eerie' (fearful, timorous).
