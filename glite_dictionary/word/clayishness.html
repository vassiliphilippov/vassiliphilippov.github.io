clayishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkleɪɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈkleɪɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being clayish; the extent to which something is like or contains clay.
quality of being clay-like or containing clay

Examples

The clayishness of the soil made it difficult for water to drain properly after rain.
Because of its clayishness, the ground was heavy and sticky underfoot during planting.
The region is known for the clayishness of its earth, which affects the crops that can be grown.

Synset Examples

During periods of excess moisture, grazing needs to be restricted in many places because of the clayiness of the surface soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clayiness - the quality or state of being clayey or containing a lot of clay

Etymology

Formed by adding the noun suffix '-ness' to 'clayish'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkleɪɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈkleɪɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of resembling clay, especially in thickness or consistency.
quality of resembling clay

Examples

The clayishness of the dough made it difficult to roll out smoothly.
After the rain, the trail regained its usual clayishness, making walking tricky.
She disliked the clayishness of the face mask, preferring a lighter texture.

Synset Examples

And it uses clay (well, something Play Doh-like in its clayishness).

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: clay - a stiff, fine-grained earth used as a material for making bricks, pottery, etc.
