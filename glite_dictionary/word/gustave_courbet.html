Gustave Courbet

1. PROPN

/ ɡuːˈstɑːv kʊərˈbeɪ / (AmE), / ɡyːˈstav kuːʁˈbɛ / (BrE)
(1819–1877) A French painter renowned for his pioneering realism and depiction of everyday life.
French realist painter (1819–1877)

Examples

Gustave Courbet revolutionized art by painting ordinary people rather than historical or religious subjects.
The works of Gustave Courbet are considered foundational to the movement of realism.
Many critics initially rejected Gustave Courbet's paintings for their bold portrayal of daily life.

Etymology

French personal name; 'Gustave' and surname 'Courbet'.
