Oryctolagus cuniculus

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˌrɪktəˈleɪɡəs kjuːˈnɪkjələs / (AmE), / ɒˌrɪktəˈleɪɡəs kjuːˈnɪkjələs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the European rabbit, a burrowing mammal native to southern Europe and northern Africa, widely introduced and domesticated around the world
scientific name for the European rabbit

Examples

Wild populations of Oryctolagus cuniculus have become established in Australia, causing ecological concerns.
The laboratory commonly uses Oryctolagus cuniculus as a model organism for research.
Domestic breeds of Oryctolagus cuniculus exhibit a wide variety of colors and coat types.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbit - a small burrowing mammal with long ears

Etymology

From Greek 'oryktos' (dug up) and 'lagos' (hare), Latin 'cuniculus' (rabbit).
