putty

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌti / (AmE), / ˈpʌti / (BrE)
A soft, malleable substance made from whiting and oil, used to fill gaps in woodwork or secure glass panes in window frames.
soft filler for glass or woodwork

Examples

He used putty to seal the edges of the window pane.
After smoothing the putty, she waited for it to dry before painting.
The old putty around the frame was cracked and needed replacing.

2. VERB

/ ˈpʌti / (AmE), / ˈpʌti / (BrE)
to apply putty in order to fix, fill, or seal a joint or crack, especially in window frames or similar structures
apply putty to fill or seal

Examples

After scraping away the old sealant, she carefully puttied the new window pane.
He spent the afternoon puttying the cracks in the wooden frame before painting.
The handyman promised to putty the gaps around the glass for better insulation.

Synset Examples

putty the window sash

Etymology

Derived from the noun 'putty', from French *potée* (a paste), from Late Latin *pottus*.
