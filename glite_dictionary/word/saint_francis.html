Saint Francis

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈfræn.sɪs / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈfræn.sɪs / (BrE)
(c. 1181–1226) An Italian religious leader and Roman Catholic monk, founder of the Franciscan order of friars.
Italian Catholic monk; founder of Franciscan order

Examples

Saint Francis is renowned for his love of animals and nature, which inspired many followers.
Many churches around the world are named after Saint Francis, honoring his legacy of humility and kindness.
The teachings of Saint Francis continue to influence Christian thought and practice centuries after his death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Francis of Assisi - Another name for Saint Francis, especially emphasizing his origin.
