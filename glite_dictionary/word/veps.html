Veps

1. NOUN

/ vɛps / (AmE), / vɛps / (BrE)
A member of a small Finnic people mainly inhabiting parts of northwest Russia.
a member of a Finnic people of Russia

Examples

The museum hosts an exhibition dedicated to the culture and history of the Veps.
Many Veps continue to preserve their traditional language and customs.
She traced her ancestry back to the Veps of Karelia.

Etymology

From Russian Вепсы (Vepsy), referring to the Veps people.

2. NOUN

/ vɛps / (AmE), / vɛps / (BrE)
the Finnic language spoken by the Veps people, mainly in northwestern Russia
Finnic language of the Veps people

Examples

She is studying Veps to better understand the folklore of the region.
Linguists are documenting the Veps language to preserve its heritage.
Few people today are fluent speakers of Veps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finnic - group of languages including Finnish, Estonian, and others

Etymology

From Russian Вепс (Veps), likely of native origin.
