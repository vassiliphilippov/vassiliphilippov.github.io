to obfuscate

1. VERB

/ ˈɑb.fəˌskeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒb.fʌ.skeɪt / (BrE)
to make something obscure, unclear, or difficult to understand, especially deliberately
make obscure or unclear

Examples

The company's response seemed designed to obfuscate the main issues rather than address them.
Politicians sometimes obfuscate facts to avoid accountability.
Technical jargon can obfuscate the meaning of a document for non-experts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to clarify - to make something clear or easier to understand

Etymology

From Latin obfuscat-, past participle of obfuscare, from ob- 'over' + fuscare 'to darken', from fuscus 'dark'.
