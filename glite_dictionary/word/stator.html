stator

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtə / (BrE)
The stationary part of a motor or generator, in or around which the rotor moves.
stationary part of a motor or generator

Examples

The stator is responsible for generating the stationary magnetic field in the motor.
Engineers inspected the stator for signs of wear before reassembling the device.
A damaged stator can cause a motor to overheat and fail.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rotor - the rotating part of a motor or generator

Etymology

From Latin 'stator' meaning 'stander', from 'stāre' (to stand).
