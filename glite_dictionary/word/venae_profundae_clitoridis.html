venae profundae clitoridis

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːni proʊˈfʊnˌdaɪ klɪˈtɔrɪdɪs / (AmE), / ˈviːni prəˈfʊnˌdi klɪˈtɒrɪdɪs / (BrE)
The deep veins of the clitoris, which drain blood from the clitoris and join the vesical plexus.
deep veins of the clitoris (anatomy)
medical

Examples

The venae profundae clitoridis play an important role in the venous drainage of the clitoral structure.
During pelvic surgery, care must be taken not to damage the venae profundae clitoridis.
Anatomical studies of the venae profundae clitoridis help further understand female sexual health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clitoris - female genital organ, highly sensitive body part

Etymology

From Latin: 'venae' (veins), 'profundae' (deep), 'clitoridis' (of the clitoris).
