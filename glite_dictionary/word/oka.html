oka

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊkə / (AmE), / ˈəʊkə / (BrE)
a Turkish unit of weight, equal to approximately 2.75 pounds (1.24 kilograms)
Turkish unit of weight (~2.75 lbs)

Examples

The old recipe calls for one oka of sugar, which is roughly 2.75 pounds.
Merchants used to weigh spices in okas at the bazaar.
He bought three okas of flour at the market before the metric system was adopted.

Etymology

From Turkish *okka*, referring to a historical unit of weight.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊkə / (AmE), / ˈəʊkə / (BrE)
a former Turkish unit of liquid measure, approximately equal to 1.3 pints (0.57 liters)
old Turkish liquid unit (approx. 1.3 pints)

Examples

During the Ottoman era, merchants would measure olive oil in okas.
The recipe calls for half an oka of rosewater, following traditional Turkish methods.
Historical trade records often list commodities in okas rather than in kilograms.

Etymology

From Turkish *okka*, a traditional measure in the Ottoman Empire.
