lunatic

1. ADJ

/ ˈluːnəˌtɪk / (AmE), / ˈljuːnətɪk / (BrE)
(archaic) Affected with insanity, originally believed to be caused by the phases of the moon.
insane, as if affected by the moon (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In medieval Europe, some believed a person could become lunatic during a full moon.
The old hospital once treated many patients labeled as lunatic by society.
Under the archaic belief, her strange behavior was thought to be lunatic in origin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insane - mentally ill; deranged
  • Antonym: sane - of sound mind; not mentally ill

Etymology

From Latin lunaticus (moonstruck), from luna (moon).

2. NOUN

/ ˈluːnətɪk / (AmE), / ˈluːnətɪk / (BrE)
(dated, offensive) A person who is insane or considered to be mentally ill; an extremely foolish or eccentric person.
insane or wildly foolish person
archaic offensive

Examples

People once believed that a lunatic was someone influenced by the phases of the moon.
The old castle was rumored to have been built by a lunatic centuries ago.
It's offensive to call someone a lunatic when referring to mental illness today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: madman - an insane person
  • Synonym: maniac - a person exhibiting extreme symptoms of wild behavior, especially when violent and dangerous
  • Antonym: sane - mentally healthy and reasonable person

Etymology

From Latin 'lunaticus' (moonstruck), from 'luna' (moon).

3. NOUN

/ ˈluːnəˌtɪk / (AmE), / ˈluːnətɪk / (BrE)
a person who behaves in a wild, reckless, or irresponsible way
reckless or irresponsible person
offensive archaic

Examples

He drove through the city like a lunatic, ignoring every traffic light.
Only a lunatic would try to climb that mountain without proper equipment.
People looked at her as if she were a lunatic when she started shouting in the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: madman - a person who behaves in a wildly foolish way
  • Synonym: maniac - an extremely reckless or dangerous person
  • Antonym: sane person - a person showing sound judgment or rational behavior

Etymology

From Latin lunaticus (moonstruck), from luna (moon), reflecting ancient beliefs about the moon's influence on behavior.
