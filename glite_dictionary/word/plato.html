Plato

1. PROPN

/ ˈpleɪtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpleɪtəʊ / (BrE)
(c. 428–347 BC) An ancient Greek philosopher, pupil of Socrates and teacher of Aristotle, known for his works on philosophy and founding the Academy in Athens.
ancient Greek philosopher

Examples

Many scholars regard Plato's writings as foundational to Western philosophy.
In his Republic, Plato discusses justice and the ideal state.
Plato was a student of Socrates and later taught Aristotle.

Etymology

From the Greek name Plátōn (Πλάτων), meaning 'broad', possibly referring to his broad shoulders or robust build.
