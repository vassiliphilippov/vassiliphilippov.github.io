caddie

1. NOUN

/ ˈkædi / (AmE), / ˈkædi / (BrE)
a person whose job is to carry a golfer's clubs and provide other assistance during a round of golf
person who carries golfers' clubs

Examples

She hired a caddie for her first round at the country club.
The caddie offered advice on which club to use for the next shot.
Many caddies work on weekends during golf tournaments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golf - a sport in which players hit a ball into a series of holes with as few strokes as possible

Etymology

From Scots 'caddie', meaning errand boy or messenger.

2. VERB

/ ˈkædi / (AmE), / ˈkædi / (BrE)
to act as a caddie, carrying a golfer's clubs and providing assistance during a game
act as a caddie in golf

Examples

As a teenager, he used to caddie for local golfers to earn some money.
She loves golf so much that she volunteered to caddie at the tournament.
If you caddie this weekend, you can watch the professionals play up close.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caddy - to serve as a caddie; variant spelling

Etymology

From the noun 'caddie', which ultimately derives from French 'cadet' (junior or assistant).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkædi / (AmE), / ˈkædi / (BrE)
a person who carries a golfer's clubs and gives other assistance during a game
person who assists a golfer

Examples

He hired a caddie to help him during the golf tournament.
The caddie advised her on which club to use for the difficult shot.
Young golfers often start out as a caddie before pursuing a professional career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golf cart - a small vehicle used to carry golfers and their equipment

Etymology

From French 'cadet' (younger son, junior), adopted in Scottish English for a helper or errand boy.
