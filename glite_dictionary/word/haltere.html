haltere

1. NOUN

/ ˈhælˌtɪr / (AmE), / ˈhæltɪə / (BrE)
either of the small knobbed structures in place of hind wings in dipterous insects, which help maintain equilibrium during flight
balancing organ in certain insects

Examples

The housefly's halteres help it balance during rapid maneuvers.
Scientists studied how halteres allow fruit flies to perform agile flights.
A dipteran insect uses its halteres as gyroscopic organs in place of hind wings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: wing - modification of a limb for flight; in insects or birds

Etymology

From Greek ‘haltēr’ (a leaper), alluding to their function in balance and movement.
