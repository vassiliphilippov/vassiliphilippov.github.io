Indochina

1. PROPN

/ ˌɪndoʊˈtʃaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌɪndəʊˈtʃaɪnə / (BrE)
a peninsula in Southeast Asia comprising Myanmar, Cambodia, Laos, Malaysia, Thailand, and Vietnam.
peninsula region of Southeast Asia

Examples

The countries of Indochina have diverse cultures and histories.
French colonial rule had a significant impact on Indochina in the 19th and 20th centuries.
Many travelers visit Indochina for its beautiful landscapes and ancient temples.

Etymology

From French 'Indochine', referring to the region lying between India and China.
