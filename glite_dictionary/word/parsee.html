Parsee

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrsi / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsi / (BrE)
A member of a monotheistic community descended from Persian Zoroastrians, mainly found in western India.
member of Indian Zoroastrian community

Examples

The Parsee community in Mumbai is renowned for its contributions to business and culture.
Many Parsees still follow the rituals of Zoroastrianism brought from Persia centuries ago.
She learned about the unique traditions of the Parsees during her visit to India.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zoroastrian - a follower of the ancient Persian religion of Zoroastrianism

Etymology

From Persian *Pārsī* (Persian), meaning 'from Pars', the historical region of Persia (now Iran).
