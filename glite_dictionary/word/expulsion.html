expulsion

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspʌl.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈspʌl.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of forcing someone or something to leave a place, organization, or situation
the act of forcing out

Examples

The student's expulsion from school shocked his classmates and teachers.
The volcano's expulsion of ash disrupted air travel for several days.
Repeated rule violations may result in expulsion from the club.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ejection - the action of forcing someone or something out
  • Antonym: admission - the act of allowing entry

Etymology

From Latin expulsio (driving out), from expellere (to drive out).

3. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspʌlʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈspʌlʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of forcing out something by applying pressure, especially from a container or body
forcing out by pressure

Examples

The dentist explained that the expulsion of pus from the abscess indicated an infection.
An accidental expulsion of toothpaste can make a mess in the bathroom.
The steady expulsion of air from the balloon caused it to deflate quickly.

Synset Examples

an unexpected extrusion of toothpaste from the bottom of the tube
the expulsion of pus from the pimple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extrusion - the act of pushing something out by force

Etymology

From Latin expulsio, from expellere (to drive out).
