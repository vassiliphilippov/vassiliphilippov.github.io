opah

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊpə / (AmE), / ˈəʊpə / (BrE)
a large, oval-shaped, brightly colored deep-sea fish found in the Atlantic, Pacific, and Mediterranean oceans
a large, colorful deep-sea fish

Examples

The fisherman was amazed when he caught a giant opah off the coast of Hawaii.
In some markets, opah is prized for its firm, flavorful flesh.
The opah is recognized by its round body and bright coloring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonfish - another name for the opah, a large, colorful deep-sea fish

Etymology

From the Hawaiian word 'opah'.
