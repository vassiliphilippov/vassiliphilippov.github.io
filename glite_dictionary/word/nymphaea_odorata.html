Nymphaea odorata

1. NOUN

/ nɪmˈfiːə oʊˈdɔrətə / (AmE), / nɪmˈfeɪə əʊˈdɒrətə / (BrE)
A species of water lily native to North America, with large floating leaves and fragrant white flowers.
North American fragrant water lily species

Examples

The pond was covered with blooming Nymphaea odorata in early summer.
Botanists studied the fragrant flowers of Nymphaea odorata along the lake's edge.
Gardeners value Nymphaea odorata for its ornamental beauty and sweet scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lily - an aquatic plant with large floating leaves and showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin, where Nymphaea means 'water lily' and odorata means 'fragrant'.
