milliammeter

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪliˈæmˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˌmɪliˈæmˌmiːtə / (BrE)
An instrument for measuring small electric currents, calibrated in milliamperes.
instrument measuring small electric currents

Examples

The laboratory technician used a milliammeter to check the small current passing through the circuit.
A milliammeter is essential for experiments that require measuring low levels of electrical current.
He carefully adjusted the milliammeter to get an accurate reading of the current.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ammeter - instrument for measuring electric current

Etymology

From 'milli-' (one thousandth) + 'ammeter' (current meter).
