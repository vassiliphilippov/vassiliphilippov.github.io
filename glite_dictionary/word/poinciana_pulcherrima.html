Poinciana pulcherrima

1. NOUN

/ pɔɪnˈsiːˌænə pʌlˈkɛrɪmə / (AmE), / pɔɪnˈsiːənə pʌlˈkɛrɪmə / (BrE)
a tropical shrub or small tree with bright yellow to orange-red flowers, often cultivated as an ornamental plant; also known as the peacock flower.
tropical shrub with showy orange-red flowers

Examples

The Poinciana pulcherrima brightened the garden with its vibrant orange-red flowers.
Gardeners often plant Poinciana pulcherrima for its ornamental beauty in tropical landscapes.
The striking blooms of Poinciana pulcherrima attract butterflies and hummingbirds throughout the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peacock flower - common name for Poinciana pulcherrima

Etymology

From Latin 'pulcherrima' meaning 'most beautiful.' 'Poinciana' honors Philippe de Longvilliers de Poincy, a 17th-century French governor of the Caribbean.
