physics

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzɪks / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪks / (BrE)
The branch of science that deals with the study of matter, energy, and the interactions between them.
science of matter and energy

Examples

I have always found physics to be both challenging and fascinating.
She decided to pursue a degree in physics at university.
Physics explains how objects move and interact in the world around us.

Synset Examples

his favorite subject was physics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemistry - the science of substances and their interactions
  • Synonym: biology - the science of life and living organisms

Etymology

From Greek 'physis' meaning 'nature', via Latin 'physica' (natural science).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzɪks / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪks / (BrE)
the physical properties, phenomena, or laws of a particular system or process
physical properties and laws of a system

Examples

The physics of black holes is both complex and fascinating.
Researchers are constantly discovering new aspects of the physics of sound waves.
Understanding the physics of flight is essential for designing safe airplanes.

Synset Examples

he studied the physics of radiation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanics - branch of physics focused on motion and forces

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'phusikē' (science of nature), from 'phusis' (nature).
