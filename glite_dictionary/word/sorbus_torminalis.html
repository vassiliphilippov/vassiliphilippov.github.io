Sorbus torminalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrbəs tɔrˈmɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɔːbəs tɔːˈmɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
A European deciduous tree, also known as the wild service tree, bearing small, edible, speckled brown fruit.
European tree with edible fruit

Examples

The Sorbus torminalis is valued for its attractive blossoms and edible fruits.
Many ancient woodlands in Europe still contain old Sorbus torminalis trees.
Botanists study the Sorbus torminalis to understand its role in forest ecosystems.

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Sorbus' meaning 'service tree' and 'torminalis' relating to relieving colic.
