majority

1. NOUN

/ məˈdʒɔrəti / (AmE), / məˈdʒɒrəti / (BrE)
more than half of the votes cast in an election or poll, required to decide an outcome
more than half of votes; electoral majority

Examples

The candidate won a clear majority of the votes in the final round.
To pass the new law, the council needs a simple majority.
No party achieved an absolute majority in the recent election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plurality - the number of votes cast for a candidate who receives more than any other but does not receive an absolute majority

Etymology

From Latin majoritas (greater number), from maior (greater).

2. NOUN

/ məˈdʒɔrəti / (AmE), / məˈdʒɒrəti / (BrE)
the age at which a person is legally recognized as an adult and considered competent to manage their own affairs
legal age of adulthood
legal

Examples

She will reach the majority next year and will then be able to sign legal documents herself.
In most countries, the majority is set at eighteen years old.
After attaining his majority, he assumed responsibility for the family business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: age of majority - the specified age at which a person is considered an adult by law
  • Antonym: minority - the state or condition of being under the legal age of majority

Etymology

From Latin maioritas, from maior (greater, elder).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15177022-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ məˈdʒɔrəti / (AmE), / məˈdʒɒrəti / (BrE)
the greater part or number of a group; more than half of the people or things in a group
the greater part; more than half

Examples

The majority of students agreed with the proposal.
He spent the majority of his career working in finance.
The new law passed with a clear majority in parliament.

Synset Examples

the majority of his customers prefer it
the bulk of the work is finished

Related Terms

  • Antonym: minority - the smaller part or number

Etymology

From Latin 'majoritas' (greatness, majority), from 'major' (greater).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
