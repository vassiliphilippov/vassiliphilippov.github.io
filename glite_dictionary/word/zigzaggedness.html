zigzaggedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪɡˌzæɡɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈzɪɡˌzæɡɪdnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of having a zigzag form or pattern, with sharp alternations in direction.
the quality of being zigzagged

Examples

The zigzaggedness of the river made it difficult to navigate by boat.
She admired the zigzaggedness in the artist's lively pen strokes.
The zigzaggedness of the hiking trail added excitement to their journey.

Synset Examples

An aerial photograph revealed the zigzaggedness of the mountain road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zigzag - a pattern or path of sharp alternation between directions

Etymology

From 'zigzag' + '-ed' + '-ness', indicating the state of being zigzagged.
