implement

1. VERB

/ ˈɪmpləˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈɪmplɪˌmɛnt / (BrE)
to put (a decision, plan, agreement, etc.) into effect; to carry out or fulfill
put into effect; carry out

Examples

The company will implement a new training program next month.
It took several months to implement the new guidelines across all departments.
Despite initial resistance, the government successfully implemented the proposed reforms.

Synset Examples

implement a procedure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to execute - to carry out or put into effect
  • Synonym: to enforce - to make sure a law or rule is obeyed or carried out
  • Antonym: abandon - to give up or stop doing something

Etymology

From Latin 'implementum' (filling up, fulfillment), from 'implere' (to fill).
