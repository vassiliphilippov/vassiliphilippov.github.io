Carthusian

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrˈθuːʒən / (AmE), / kɑːˈθjuːziən / (BrE)
a member of the Carthusian order, a contemplative Roman Catholic religious order founded by St Bruno in 1084.
member of the Carthusian religious order

Examples

The Carthusian devoted his life to prayer and solitude in the monastery.
A prominent Carthusian philosopher contributed to medieval theological debates.
Tours of the ancient cloister highlight where Carthusian monks once lived in silence.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Cartusianus', from 'Cartusia' (the charterhouse at Chartreuse, France).

2. ADJ

/ kɑrˈθuʒən / (AmE), / kɑːˈθjuːʒən / (BrE)
of or relating to the Carthusian order, a Roman Catholic religious order of monks and nuns founded by St. Bruno in 1084.
relating to the Carthusian religious order

Examples

The monks followed the strict Carthusian rule, dedicating their lives to contemplation and silence.
She visited a remote Carthusian monastery in the French Alps.
The artwork was inspired by Carthusian spirituality and simplicity.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Carthusiānus', from 'Cartusia', the Latin name for Chartreuse in France, where the order was founded.

Synset POS: a
