pilaster

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈlæstɚ / (AmE), / pɪˈlæstə / (BrE)
An upright rectangular architectural element projecting slightly from a wall, typically resembling a column but serving a decorative or structural function.
rectangular column-like wall projection

Examples

The architect added a pilaster to each corner of the facade for visual interest.
Decorative pilasters lined the grand hallway, giving the illusion of structural support.
Each pilaster was adorned with intricate carvings that matched the period style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: column - a vertical cylindrical support in architecture

Etymology

From Italian 'pilastro', from Latin 'pila' (pillar).
