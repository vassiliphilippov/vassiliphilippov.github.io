Hedeoma

1. NOUN

/ hɛˈdiː.ə.mə / (AmE), / hɛˈdiː.ə.mə / (BrE)
A small genus of American aromatic herbs in the mint family, including the American pennyroyal.
genus of American aromatic herbs

Examples

The essential oil derived from Hedeoma species has been studied for its medicinal properties.
Many botanists have classified American pennyroyal under the genus Hedeoma.
Several species in the Hedeoma genus are native to North and South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mentha - genus of plants including mint, related to Hedeoma

Etymology

From Greek hēdys (sweet) + ōma (smell), referring to the plant's aromatic scent.
