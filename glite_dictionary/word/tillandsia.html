Tillandsia

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈlændziə / (AmE), / tɪˈlandzɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American plants, often epiphytic and known for growing in clumps or hanging masses, commonly called 'air plants.'
genus of tropical air plants

Examples

Many species of Tillandsia absorb moisture and nutrients through their leaves rather than their roots.
The greenhouse features an impressive display of Tillandsia arranged on driftwood.
Gardeners often use Tillandsia in vertical gardens because they require minimal soil.

Etymology

Named after Swedish botanist Elias Tillandz (1640–1693).

2. NOUN

/ tɪˈlændziə / (AmE), / tɪˈlandzɪə / (BrE)
A large genus of tropical plants, often epiphytic with sparse roots, that typically form dense clumps or hanging masses; commonly known as air plants.
a genus of tropical epiphytic plants (air plants)

Examples

Many Tillandsia species are prized by gardeners for their unique ability to grow without soil.
The florist arranged a display using several brightly colored Tillandsia plants.
Researchers studied how Tillandsia absorbs moisture directly from the air through its leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air plant - A common name for plants of the genus Tillandsia, which absorb moisture through their leaves.

Etymology

Named after Swedish botanist Elias Tillandz (1640–1693).
