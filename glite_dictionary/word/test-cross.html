test-cross

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛst krɑs / (AmE), / ˈtɛst krɒs / (BrE)
a genetic cross between an individual with an unknown genotype and an individual that is homozygous recessive for a specific trait, used to determine the unknown genotype from the traits of the offspring
genetic cross to determine unknown genotype
medical

Examples

To find out if the plant was heterozygous or homozygous, the scientist performed a test-cross.
A test-cross helped reveal the carrier status of the animal for the trait.
By using a test-cross, researchers could deduce the hidden genotype from the offspring's characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to backcross - cross between a hybrid organism and one of its parents or a genetically similar organism

Etymology

Formed from 'test' and 'cross' in the context of genetics.
