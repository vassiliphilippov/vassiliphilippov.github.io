Gustavus

1. PROPN

/ ɡʊˈstɑːvəs / (AmE), / ɡʊˈstɑːvəs / (BrE)
King of Sweden (1496–1560) who established Lutheranism as the state religion and laid the foundation for modern Sweden.
King of Sweden (1496–1560); established Lutheranism

Examples

Gustavus is credited with establishing the Swedish state and promoting the Lutheran Reformation.
Many historians regard Gustavus as one of the most influential monarchs in Sweden's history.
During Gustavus's reign, Sweden underwent significant political and religious transformation.

Etymology

Latinized form of Swedish name Gustav; commonly used in historical and scholarly texts.

2. PROPN

/ ɡəˈsteɪvəs / (AmE), / ɡʊˈstɑːvəs / (BrE)
(1746–1792) King of Sweden known for increasing royal power and leading an unpopular war against Russia.
king of Sweden (1746–1792)

Examples

Gustavus sought to strengthen the Swedish monarchy during his reign.
During the war against Russia, Gustavus faced considerable opposition at home.
Historians often discuss the legacy of Gustavus in Scandinavian political history.

Etymology

Latinized form of Swedish 'Gustav', a royal name of uncertain origin.
