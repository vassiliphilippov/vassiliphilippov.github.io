Macedon

1. PROPN

/ ˈmæsɪˌdɒn / (AmE), / ˈmæsɪdən / (BrE)
an ancient kingdom in the southeastern Balkans, ruled by Philip II and Alexander the Great, now part of modern Macedonia, Greece, and Bulgaria.
ancient kingdom in the southeastern Balkans

Examples

Historians still debate the exact boundaries of Macedon during the rise of Alexander the Great.
The military power of Macedon transformed the balance of power in the ancient world.
Tourists can visit the archaeological sites that once formed the heart of Macedon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Macedonia - the region associated with ancient Macedon

Etymology

From Greek Makedon, the name of the kingdom and its people.
