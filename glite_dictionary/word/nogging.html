nogging

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɒɡɪŋ / (BrE)
Rough brickwork or masonry used to fill the spaces between timbers in a wooden frame.
brick infill in timber frames

Examples

The old cottage walls were reinforced with nogging between the timbers.
Traditional timber-frame houses often use nogging to add stability and insulation.
Some restoration projects preserve the original nogging to maintain historical accuracy.

Etymology

Probably from 'nog', an old word for a wooden block or peg, origin uncertain.
