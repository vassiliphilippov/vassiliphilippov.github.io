narrator

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛrˌeɪtər / (AmE), / nəˈreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who tells a story, especially in a book, play, film, or other narrative work
person who tells a story

Examples

The narrator describes the scene in vivid detail, guiding the reader through the events of the story.
In some movies, the narrator speaks directly to the audience to explain important points.
She was chosen as the narrator for the school play because of her clear and expressive voice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storyteller - a person who tells or writes stories

Etymology

From Latin narrātor (a teller or relater) from narrāre (to tell).
