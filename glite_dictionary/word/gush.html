gush

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌʃ / (AmE), / ɡʌʃ / (BrE)
an unrestrained or excessive expression of emotion, especially in speech or writing
unrestrained expression of emotion

Examples

Her review was little more than a gush of praise for the author.
He spoke with a gush of enthusiasm about his experiences abroad.
At the reunion, there was a gush of emotion as friends greeted each other after many years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outburst - a sudden or violent display of emotion
  • Synonym: effusion - an unrestrained expression of feeling
  • Antonym: restraint - controlled or moderate expression of feeling
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ɡʌʃ / (AmE), / ɡʌʃ / (BrE)
a sudden and rapid flow of liquid or other substance, especially in a large or forceful amount
a sudden rapid flow of liquid

Examples

A gush of water burst from the broken pipe, flooding the floor within seconds.
He spoke with a gush of enthusiasm that surprised everyone in the room.
A gush of blood followed the deep cut on his arm.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurt - a sudden short burst of liquid or other substance
  • Degree Lower: trickle - a small, slow flow of liquid

Etymology

From Middle English gusshen, probably of imitative origin.

3. VERB

/ ɡʌʃ / (AmE), / ɡʌʃ / (BrE)
to praise or speak about someone or something with excessive enthusiasm or emotion
praise or speak with great enthusiasm

Examples

She couldn't help but gush about the wonderful time she had at the concert.
Critics began to gush over the director's latest film, calling it a masterpiece.
He tends to gush whenever he talks about his grandchildren.

Synset Examples

She raved about that new restaurant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rave - to speak or write about something with great enthusiasm or admiration
  • Antonym: to criticize - to express disapproval of someone or something

Etymology

From Middle English gusshen, probably from Old Norse gusa (to gush, spurt).

/ ɡʌʃ / (AmE), / ɡʌʃ / (BrE)
