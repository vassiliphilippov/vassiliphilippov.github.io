Cocos

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊkoʊs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkɒs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical palm trees, best known for the coconut palm (Cocos nucifera).
genus of coconut palms

Examples

The Cocos genus includes only one living species, the coconut palm.
Botanists classified the coconut under the Cocos genus.
The Cocos palm is widely cultivated throughout the tropics.

Etymology

From Portuguese and Spanish *coco* (coconut), ultimately from a word meaning 'grinning face', referring to the coconut's appearance.
