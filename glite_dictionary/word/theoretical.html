theoretical

1. ADJ

/ ˌθiːəˈrɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌθɪəˈrɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
concerned with the ideas and principles of a subject rather than its practical aspects
relating to theory, not practice

Examples

His ideas remain theoretical and have not yet been tested in real-world situations.
She enjoys discussing theoretical concepts in physics.
The course covers both the theoretical and practical aspects of chemistry.

Synset Examples

theoretical science

Related Terms

  • Antonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice rather than theory

Etymology

From Greek theōrētikos (contemplative), from theōrein (to look at, to consider).

2. ADJ

/ θiˈɛrətɪkəl / (AmE), / θɪəˈrɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
concerned with the ideas and principles of a subject, rather than its practical uses or applications.
concerned with theories, not practice

Examples

Her research is purely theoretical and does not involve any experiments.
The course covers both theoretical concepts and practical skills.
Some argue that his ideas are too theoretical to be implemented in real life.

Synset Examples

theoretical physics

Related Terms

  • Antonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice rather than ideas

Etymology

From Late Latin 'theoreticus', from Greek 'theōrētikos' (contemplative, theoretical), from 'theōrein' (to look at, consider).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00865514-a
Synset POS: a
