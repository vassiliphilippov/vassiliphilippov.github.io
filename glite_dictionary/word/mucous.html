mucous

1. ADJ

/ ˈmjuːkəs / (AmE), / ˈmjuːkəs / (BrE)
(of membranes, glands, etc.) producing, covered with, or resembling mucus.
producing or resembling mucus

Examples

The lining of the nose is a mucous membrane that protects against pathogens.
Excessive alcohol can irritate mucous tissues in the digestive tract.
The doctor explained that mucous glands secrete substances to keep passages moist.

Synset Examples

mucous tissue
mucous glands of the intestine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mucous membrane - a membrane that secretes mucus
  • Antonym: serous - (of membranes, fluids) resembling or producing serum rather than mucus

Etymology

From Latin 'mucosus' (slimy, full of mucus).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmjuːkəs / (AmE), / ˈmjuːkəs / (BrE)
(of membranes, tissue, or secretion) relating to, producing, or covered with mucus.
producing or covered with mucus
medical

Examples

The lining of the stomach is a mucous membrane that protects the organ from digestive acids.
Certain glands in the body are classified as mucous glands because they secrete mucus.
Allergic reactions can cause increased mucous production in the nasal passages.

Synset Examples

mucous tissue
mucous glands of the intestine

Related Terms

  • Antonym: serous - (of membranes or tissue) producing serum rather than mucus

Etymology

From Latin 'mucosus' (slimy), from 'mucus' (slime, mucus).
