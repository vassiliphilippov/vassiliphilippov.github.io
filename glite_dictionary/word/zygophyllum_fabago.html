Zygophyllum fabago

1. NOUN

/ ˌzaɪɡəˈfɪləm fəˈbeɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌzaɪɡəˈfɪlɪəm fəˈbaːɡəʊ / (BrE)
a perennial shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean and southwestern Asia, with flower buds sometimes used as capers
a shrub with edible flower buds, native to Mediterranean and Asia

Examples

Zygophyllum fabago grows in dry, rocky soils throughout the eastern Mediterranean region.
Researchers have studied the medicinal properties of Zygophyllum fabago in local traditional remedies.
The flower buds of Zygophyllum fabago are sometimes pickled and used as an alternative to capers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capparis spinosa - plant whose flower buds are widely known as capers

Etymology

From Latinized Greek *zygophyllon* (a bean-caper plant) and species name *fabago*.
