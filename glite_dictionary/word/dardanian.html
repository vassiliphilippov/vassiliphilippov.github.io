Dardanian

1. NOUN

/ dɑːrˈdeɪniən / (AmE), / dɑːˈdeɪniən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of ancient Troy, especially in classical literature
native of ancient Troy

Examples

In the ancient epic, the Dardanian warriors fiercely defended the walls of Troy.
According to legend, Aeneas was a noble Dardanian who escaped the city's destruction.
Archaeological finds suggest the Dardanian culture was advanced for its time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Trojan - a native or inhabitant of Troy

Etymology

From Latin Dardanius, from Dardanus, legendary ancestor of the Trojans.
