to appal

1. VERB

/ əˈpɔl / (AmE), / əˈpɑːl / (BrE)
to shock or horrify someone very much; to fill with intense disgust or fear
to shock or horrify greatly

Examples

The violence in the movie appalled many viewers.
I was utterly appalled by the conditions in the hospital.
Their lack of compassion continues to appal me.

Synset Examples

The scandalous behavior of this married woman shocked her friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - to cause someone to feel surprised and upset
  • Synonym: to horrify - to cause someone to feel horror or shock
  • Antonym: delight - to give great pleasure or joy

Etymology

From Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'pallere' (to grow pale), via Old French 'appaler'.

2. VERB

/ əˈpɔl / (AmE), / əˈpɔːl / (BrE)
to shock or horrify someone very much; to fill with strong feelings of fear, shock, or disgust
shock or horrify deeply

Examples

The cruelty of the dictator's actions continued to appal the international community.
Her lack of empathy often appals her colleagues.
We were appalled by the filthy conditions in the restaurant.

Synset Examples

I was horrified at the thought of being late for my interview
The news of the executions horrified us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to horrify - cause to feel horror; shock deeply
  • Synonym: disgust - cause to feel revulsion or strong disapproval
  • Antonym: delight - give great pleasure or joy

Etymology

From Latin 'pallescere' (to grow pale), via French 'appaler'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01786469-v
Synset POS: v
