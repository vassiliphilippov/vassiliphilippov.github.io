trappings

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræpɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈtræpɪŋz / (BrE)
articles of clothing or accessories, often decorative, that are worn as part of a particular style or in association with a particular role or status.
decorative clothing or accessories

Examples

The king entered the hall in full trappings, impressing everyone with his regalia.
Her office contained all the trappings of success, including expensive art and plush furniture.
Traditional festivals are celebrated with colorful trappings and elaborate costumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attire - clothes, especially fine or formal ones
  • Synonym: adornment - a decorative item worn to enhance appearance

Etymology

From Middle English 'trappinges', from Old French 'draperie' (cloth, covering).
