lumen

1. NOUN

/ ˈluː.mən / (AmE), / ˈluː.mɛn / (BrE)
the inside space of a tubular structure, such as an artery or intestine, in an organism
inside space of a tubular organ
medical

Examples

The surgeon examined the lumen of the artery for blockages.
Inflammation can cause narrowing of the intestinal lumen.
Food passes through the lumen of the digestive tract during digestion.

Synset Examples

the lumen of the intestine

Etymology

From Latin *lūmen* (light, opening, passage).

2. NOUN

/ ˈluː.mən / (AmE), / ˈluː.mən / (BrE)
a unit of luminous flux in the International System of Units, equal to the amount of light emitted in a solid angle of one steradian by a point source of one candela
unit of luminous flux (light intensity)

Examples

This new light bulb produces 800 lumens, making it suitable for reading.
The projector's brightness is rated at 2,500 lumens, ensuring clear images even in daylight.
For energy efficiency, compare the lumen output rather than the wattage of light sources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candela - SI base unit of luminous intensity
  • Synonym: lux - unit of illuminance in the SI system

Etymology

From Latin 'lumen' (light).
