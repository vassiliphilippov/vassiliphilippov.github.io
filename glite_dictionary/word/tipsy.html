tipsy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪpsi / (AmE), / ˈtɪpsi / (BrE)
slightly drunk; affected by a small amount of alcohol
slightly drunk

Examples

After two glasses of wine, I started to feel a little tipsy.
She giggled and admitted she was tipsy after the party.
He walked home tipsy but still in good spirits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: drunk - very intoxicated by alcohol
  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol

Etymology

Probably from tip (to tap or tilt) + -sy (diminutive suffix), referring to being unsteady on one's feet.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɪpsi / (AmE), / ˈtɪpsi / (BrE)
Slightly unsteady or liable to tip over, as if affected by instability or imbalance.
unstable or liable to tip over

Examples

The small sailboat became tipsy as the wind picked up and waves hit the side.
She set the vase on a tipsy table, and it wobbled precariously.
After loading one side with too many boxes, the cart felt noticeably tipsy.

Synset Examples

a tipsy boat

Etymology

From tip (to tilt or overturn) + -sy (diminutive/adjectival suffix).
