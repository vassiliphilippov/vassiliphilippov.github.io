gigaflop

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪɡəˌflɑp / (AmE), / ˈɡɪɡəˌflɒp / (BrE)
A unit for measuring the speed of a computer, equal to one billion floating point operations per second.
unit of computing speed (1 billion operations/second)

Examples

This new processor can achieve over 40 gigaflops in benchmark tests.
A supercomputer with a performance of one gigaflop was impressive in the 1990s.
Researchers measured the simulation speed in gigaflops to compare computational efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: megaflop - a unit of computing speed equal to one million floating point operations per second
  • Degree Higher: teraflop - a unit of computing speed equal to one trillion floating point operations per second

Etymology

Blend of giga- (meaning 'billion') and flop (from 'floating point operations per second').
