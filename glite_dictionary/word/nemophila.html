Nemophila

1. NOUN

/ nɛˈmɒfɪlə / (AmE), / nɛˈmɒfɪlə / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly Californian flowering plants in the borage family, commonly known as baby blue-eyes
genus of Californian flowering plants

Examples

The delicate Nemophila flowers covered the meadow in a sea of blue every spring.
Gardeners love to plant Nemophila for its charming baby blue blossoms and easy care.
In California, Nemophila is a popular choice for wildflower gardens and natural landscapes.

Etymology

From Greek nemos (grove) + philos (loving); refers to the plant's woodland habitat.
