to whelm

1. VERB

/ wɛlm / (AmE), / wɛlm / (BrE)
to overpower or engulf, especially with strong emotions or physical force
to overpower or engulf
archaic

Examples

She was whelmed by a sudden wave of nostalgia as she entered her childhood home.
The city was whelmed by the floodwaters after days of heavy rain.
A sense of gratitude whelmed him as he listened to the kind words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overwhelm - to overpower completely, especially emotionally or physically

Etymology

From Middle English *whelmen* (to overturn), probably of Old English origin.

to overcome or engulf
archaic
