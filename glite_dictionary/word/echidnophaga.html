Echidnophaga

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌkɪdnəˈfæɡə / (AmE), / ɛˌkɪdnəˈfæɡə / (BrE)
A genus of fleas that contains several species found in the Palaearctic, Afrotropic, and Australasian regions.
genus of fleas

Examples

The poultry sticktight flea, a member of the genus Echidnophaga, is a notable pest in many regions.
Researchers collected samples of Echidnophaga from several wild mammals.
The distribution of Echidnophaga species extends across the Palaearctic and Australasian areas.

Etymology

From Greek 'echidna' (viper, also hedgehog) + '-phaga' (eater).

2. NOUN

/ ɛˌkɪdnəˈfæɡə / (AmE), / ɛˌkɪdnəˈfæɡə / (BrE)
A genus of fleas in the family Pulicidae, comprising about 21 species found in the Palaearctic, Afrotropic, and Australasian regions.
genus of fleas in Pulicidae family

Examples

The poultry sticktight flea belongs to the genus Echidnophaga, which infests a variety of bird species.
Veterinary entomologists often study Echidnophaga due to its significance in animal health.
Some species within Echidnophaga can be problematic pests for livestock in Australia.

Etymology

From Greek *echidna* (viper, also means 'hedgehog') + Greek *phaga* (to eat).
