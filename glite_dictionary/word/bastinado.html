bastinado

1. NOUN

/ ˌbæstəˈnidoʊ / (AmE), / ˌbæstɪˈnɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
a form of punishment or torture in which the soles of the feet are beaten with a stick, whip, or cudgel
beating soles of feet as punishment/torture

Examples

The prisoner was subjected to bastinado as part of his interrogation.
Historical records describe the use of bastinado in ancient judicial punishments.
He recoiled at the mention of bastinado, knowing the pain it could inflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caning - punishment by beating with a cane

Etymology

From Spanish 'bastón' (stick, staff), from Late Latin 'basto' (stick).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbæstəˈnidoʊ / (AmE), / ˌbæstɪˈnɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
A stick or cudgel used for beating the soles of the feet as a form of punishment.
stick for beating feet as punishment

Examples

The guard threatened him with a bastinado if he did not obey.
In some cultures, the bastinado was a feared instrument of corporal punishment.
Museum exhibits sometimes display a bastinado among other historical devices of discipline.

Etymology

From Spanish 'bastón' (stick, staff), influenced by Italian 'bastonata' (blow with a stick).

3. VERB

/ ˌbæstəˈnidoʊ / (AmE), / ˌbæstəˈniːdəʊ / (BrE)
to beat someone on the soles of their feet as a form of punishment or torture.
to beat the soles of the feet as punishment

Examples

It was common in some ancient societies to bastinado prisoners as a means of extracting confessions.
The guards threatened to bastinado anyone who disobeyed the rules.
Despite its brutality, some regimes continued to bastinado offenders into the twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caning - punishing by beating with a cane

Etymology

From Spanish 'bastón' (stick), with influence from Italian 'bastonata' (beating with a stick).
