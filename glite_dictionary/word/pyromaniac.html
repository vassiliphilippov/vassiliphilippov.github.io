pyromaniac

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈroʊˌmeɪniˌæk / (AmE), / ˌpaɪ.rəˈmeɪ.ni.æk / (BrE)
a person who has a compulsion to set things on fire, often due to a psychological disorder
person obsessed with setting fires

Examples

The police suspected the abandoned warehouse fire was started by a pyromaniac.
As a diagnosed pyromaniac, she was required to attend therapy sessions.
The town lived in fear after a pyromaniac set several buildings ablaze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsonist - a person who intentionally sets fire to property, especially illegally
  • Synonym: pyromania - a mental disorder characterized by an uncontrollable urge to start fires

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire) + 'mania' (madness).
