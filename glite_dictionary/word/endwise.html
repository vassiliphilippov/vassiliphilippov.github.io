endwise

1. ADV

/ ˈɛnˌdwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛndˌwaɪz / (BrE)
In or toward the direction of the ends; lengthwise.
in the direction of the ends; lengthwise

Examples

The logs were stacked endwise to maximize storage space.
The beam must be installed endwise for proper support.
The crate will fit through the doorway only if you carry it endwise.

Synset Examples

endways pressure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lengthwise - in the direction of the length; along the longest side

Etymology

From 'end' + '-wise' (in the manner or direction of).

2. ADV

/ ˈɛnˌdwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛndwʌɪz / (BrE)
in an upright position or with the end pointing upward or forward
in an upright or end-on position

Examples

The wooden poles were stacked endwise against the barn.
She placed the books endwise in the box to save space.
Cut the carrots endwise so they stand upright on the plate.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upright - in a vertical position
  • Antonym: sideways - with one side facing forward

Etymology

Formed from 'end' + '-wise' (in the manner or direction of).

/ ˈɛnd.waɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛnd.waɪz / (BrE)
With the end facing forward or toward the observer; in a direction aligned with the end.
with the end facing forward

Examples

The logs were stacked endwise so they would fit in the truck.
She laid the planks endwise to create a path across the mud.
Some streets have houses built endwise to save space.
