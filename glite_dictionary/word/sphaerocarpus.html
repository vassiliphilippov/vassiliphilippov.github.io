Sphaerocarpus

1. NOUN

/ sfɛəroʊˈkɑrpəs / (AmE), / sfɪərəˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
A genus of small liverworts with a many-lobed, usually rounded thallus, forming the type genus of the family Sphaerocarpaceae.
genus of small, orbicular liverworts

Examples

Several species of Sphaerocarpus are found in damp, shaded habitats around the world.
The unique reproductive structures of Sphaerocarpus set it apart from other liverworts.
Botanists studied the Sphaerocarpus samples collected during their field expedition.

Etymology

From Greek 'sphaira' (sphere) and 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the rounded capsules.
