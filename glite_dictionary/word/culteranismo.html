culteranismo

1. NOUN

/ kʊlˌtɛrəˈnizmoʊ / (AmE), / kʊlˌtɛrəˈnizməʊ / (BrE)
An elaborate, esoteric style of Spanish Baroque poetry characterized by complex language, classical allusions, and Latinized syntax.
ornate style of Spanish Baroque poetry

Examples

Many poets in the Golden Age were influenced by culteranismo and adopted its ornate language.
His essays criticize the excessive complexity of culteranismo in seventeenth-century verse.
Researchers often contrast culteranismo with conceptismo when analyzing the period's literary trends.

Synset Examples

His comparisons of the modern decadent style with the culteranismo of an earlier epoch, whose excesses had led to satiric reactions and a more sober style, are very revealing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conceptismo - A contrasting Spanish Baroque literary style noted for brevity and wit

Etymology

Spanish, from culto (cultured) + latinismo (Latinism).
