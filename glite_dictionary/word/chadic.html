Chadic

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃædɪk / (AmE), / ˈtʃadɪk / (BrE)
a family of Afroasiatic tonal languages spoken mainly west and south of Lake Chad in north-central Africa
family of Afroasiatic languages in Africa

Examples

Many languages in the Chadic family are spoken in Nigeria and neighboring countries.
The reconstruction of the Chadic languages has provided new insights into African linguistic history.
Hausa is the most widely spoken language of the Chadic family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Afroasiatic - a large language family including Chadic

Etymology

From Lake Chad, the geographical region where the languages are spoken, plus the suffix -ic.
