crepuscule

1. NOUN

/ krəˈpʌs.kjul / (AmE), / krɪˈpʌs.kjuːl / (BrE)
The period of partial darkness following sunset; twilight.
the time just after sunset

Examples

The city's skyline looked beautiful in the crepuscule of a summer evening.
Artists often find inspiration in the tranquil mood created by the crepuscule.
Birds began to settle down for the night as crepuscule descended over the forest.

Synset Examples

he loved the twilight
they finished before the fall of night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twilight - the soft glowing light from the sky when the sun is below the horizon
  • Synonym: dusk - the darker stage of twilight, just after sunset

Etymology

From French 'crépuscule', from Latin 'crepusculum' (twilight).
