madrasa

1. NOUN

/ məˈdrɑːsə / (AmE), / məˈdrɑːsə / (BrE)
A Muslim school, especially one in South Asia, where religious and sometimes secular subjects are taught.
Muslim religious school, esp. in South Asia

Examples

Several parents chose to send their children to a nearby madrasa for religious education.
The government inspected each madrasa to ensure the curriculum met national standards.
Many madrasas also teach mathematics, science, and languages alongside religious studies.

Synset Examples

the Pakistan government decided to close down madrasas that provided military training for their students
many madrasas in Bangladesh are supported with money from Saudi Arabia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: school - institution for educating children or students

Etymology

From Arabic *madrasa* (school), from the root 'darasa' (to study).
