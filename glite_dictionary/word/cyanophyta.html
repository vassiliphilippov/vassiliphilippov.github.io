Cyanophyta

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈænəˌfaɪtə / (AmE), / saɪˈanəˌfʌɪtə / (BrE)
A division or phylum of photosynthetic, mostly aquatic prokaryotic organisms also known as cyanobacteria or blue-green algae.
phylum of cyanobacteria (blue-green algae)
medical

Examples

The Cyanophyta are essential to early earth ecosystems because of their ability to produce oxygen through photosynthesis.
Some scientists classify Cyanophyta as the earliest known organisms capable of oxygenic photosynthesis.
Modern taxonomy often refers to Cyanophyta as cyanobacteria rather than as a type of algae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyanobacteria - photosynthetic bacteria called blue-green algae

Etymology

From Greek 'kyanos' (dark blue) + 'phyton' (plant).
