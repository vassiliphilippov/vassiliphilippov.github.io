subfusc

1. ADJ

/ sʌbˈfʌsk / (AmE), / sʌbˈfʌsk / (BrE)
Dull, dark, or lacking brightness or appeal; drab or somber in color or tone.
dull or dark in color; lacking brightness

Examples

The students wore subfusc gowns to the formal ceremony.
The painting was dominated by subfusc shades of grey and brown.
The weather remained subfusc throughout the gloomy autumn week.

Synset Examples

a subfusc mining town
dark subfusc clothing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drab - lacking brightness or interest; drearily dull
  • Antonym: bright - having a lot of light or vivid color

Etymology

From Latin 'subfuscus' meaning 'somewhat dark'.
