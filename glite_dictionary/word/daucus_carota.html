Daucus carota

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊkəs kəˈroʊtə / (AmE), / ˈdaʊkəs kəˈrəʊtə / (BrE)
a Eurasian plant species with finely divided leaves, white flower clusters, and slender, yellowish roots; also known as the wild carrot
species of wild carrot plant

Examples

The fields were full of Daucus carota, commonly known as wild carrot or Queen Anne's lace.
Many herbal remedies use Daucus carota for its medicinal properties.
Botanists can easily identify Daucus carota by its lacy white flower clusters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carrot - an orange root vegetable, cultivated variety of Daucus carota

Etymology

From Latin Daucus (genus name) and carota (carrot), referring to the plant's classification.
