towelling

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtaʊəlɪŋ / (BrE)
fabric, typically made of linen or cotton, used for making towels
fabric for making towels

Examples

She bought several meters of towelling to sew her own bathrobes.
The shop sells colored towelling for making personalized towels.
Beach towels are often made from absorbent towelling fabric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrycloth - a type of fabric with loops that can absorb large amounts of water

Etymology

From the noun 'towel' + suffixed '-ing' denoting material or fabric.
