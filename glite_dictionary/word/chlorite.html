chlorite

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔːrˌaɪt / (AmE), / ˈklɔːraɪt / (BrE)
a green or black mineral that is commonly found as flat crystals in many types of rocks
a green or black mineral found in rocks

Examples

The geologist identified the flat, green crystals as chlorite in the rock sample.
Many metamorphic rocks contain chlorite as a common mineral component.
The thin, flaky nature of chlorite makes it easy to recognize under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mica - a group of silicate minerals with a similar appearance, often found in thin, flat sheets

Etymology

From Greek 'chloros' (green) referring to its color, with '-ite' denoting minerals.
