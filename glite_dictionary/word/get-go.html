get-go

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛtˌɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡɛtˌɡəʊ / (BrE)
the very beginning or start of an activity or process
the very beginning or start
informal american

Examples

From the get-go, it was clear the project would be a challenge.
She has been dedicated to her work since the get-go.
We had problems with the plan right from the get-go.

Synset Examples

they got an early start
she knew from the get-go that he was the man for her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - the point in time when something begins
  • Antonym: end - the final point of something in time or space

Etymology

Probably from the phrase 'get going,' first recorded in American English in the mid-20th century.
