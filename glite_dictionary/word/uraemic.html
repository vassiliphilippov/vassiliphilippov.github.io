uraemic

1. ADJ

/ jʊˈriːmɪk / (AmE), / jʊəˈriːmɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by the presence of excess nitrogenous waste products in the blood, often due to kidney failure
relating to excess waste in the blood due to kidney failure
medical british

Examples

The patient developed uraemic symptoms as his kidney function deteriorated.
A uraemic odor on the breath can be a sign of advanced renal failure.
Doctors monitored the uraemic complications during the course of treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uremic - relating to excess nitrogenous waste in the blood (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'oura' (urine) + '-emic' from Greek 'haima' (blood).
