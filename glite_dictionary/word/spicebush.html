spicebush

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪsˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈspaɪsˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, noted for its highly aromatic leaves and bark, yellow flowers, and scarlet or yellow berries
aromatic North American shrub

Examples

The spicebush in the garden filled the air with a pleasant aroma every spring.
When hiking through the forest, we spotted a spicebush covered in bright red berries.
Many birds are attracted to the berries produced by the spicebush.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪsˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈspaɪsˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
a straggling aromatic shrub native to the southwestern United States, known for its fragrant brown flowers
aromatic shrub with fragrant flowers

Examples

The spicebush filled the air with a sweet, spicy scent after the rain.
Gardeners admire the spicebush for its ability to attract butterflies.
The hiker paused to examine the fragrant brown flowers on a spicebush along the trail.
