Pharomacrus

1. NOUN

/ fəˌroʊməˈkreɪs / (AmE), / færəˈmækrəs / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored birds in the trogon family, commonly known as quetzals and native to Central and South America.
genus of tropical birds (quetzals)

Examples

The resplendent quetzal, belonging to the genus Pharomacrus, is known for its brilliant plumage.
Several species of Pharomacrus inhabit the cloud forests of Central America.
Ornithologists often study Pharomacrus to understand the evolution of iridescent feathers.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'pharos' (mantle) and 'makros' (long); refers to the birds' long, elaborate tail feathers.
