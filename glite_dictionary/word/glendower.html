Glendower

1. PROPN

/ ɡlɛnˈdaʊər / (AmE), / ɡlɛnˈdaʊə / (BrE)
Owen Glendower (Owain Glyndŵr), a Welsh leader who led a major revolt against English rule in Wales during the early 15th century.
Welsh chieftain and rebel leader, 1359–1416

Examples

Many Welsh nationalists still celebrate Glendower as a symbol of resistance against English oppression.
In Shakespeare’s play ‘Henry IV, Part 1’, Glendower is portrayed as a mystical and powerful figure.
The legacy of Glendower is remembered throughout Wales in statues, street names, and folklore.

Etymology

Anglicized form of Welsh "Owain Glyndŵr" (Owain of Glyndyfrdwy).
