Gwangju

1. PROPN

/ ɡwɑŋˈdʒu / (AmE), / ɡwaŋˈdʒuː / (BrE)
a city in southwestern South Korea, known for its historical significance and as an important military base during the Korean War
city in southwest South Korea

Examples

The May 18th movement in Gwangju became a turning point in South Korea's struggle for democracy.
Large-scale cultural festivals are held annually in Gwangju, attracting visitors from around the world.
During the Korean War, Gwangju served as a strategic military base for United Nations forces.

Etymology

From Korean 광주 (Gwangju), meaning 'light province' or 'city of light'.
