scent

1. VERB

/ sɛnt / (AmE), / sɛnt / (BrE)
to apply a fragrance or perfume to something
apply a fragrance or perfume

Examples

She likes to scent her scarves with lavender oil.
The shop assistant scented the room before the event began.
He scented the envelopes with a hint of rose fragrance.

Synset Examples

She perfumes herself every day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfume - to apply perfume to something

Etymology

From Old French 'sentir' (to smell), from Latin 'sentīre' (to feel, perceive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00043211-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ sɛnt / (AmE), / sɛnt / (BrE)
a distinctive smell, especially one that is pleasant
pleasant distinctive smell

Examples

The roses filled the garden with their sweet scent.
She wore a new scent that reminded him of springtime.
The scent of freshly baked bread drifted through the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell
  • Synonym: aroma - a distinctive, usually pleasant smell
  • Antonym: stench - a very unpleasant smell

Etymology

From Old French *sentir* (to smell) and Latin *sentire* (to feel, perceive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05722413-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ sɛnt / (AmE), / sɛnt / (BrE)
to detect or search for something by using the sense of smell, especially used about animals
detect by smell

Examples

The police dog quickly scented the missing person's trail in the forest.
The bear scented danger and moved cautiously through the woods.
Some animals can scent their prey from miles away.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sniff - to smell something by taking air in through the nose

6. NOUN

/ sɛnt / (AmE), / sɛnt / (BrE)
a smell left by a person or animal that can be followed to track their movement
a trail smell for tracking

Examples

The police dogs picked up the scent and quickly tracked the suspect through the forest.
A wolf can follow the scent of its prey for miles over rough terrain.
Rain can sometimes wash away the scent, making it harder for hunters to track animals.
