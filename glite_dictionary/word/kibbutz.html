kibbutz

1. NOUN

/ kɪˈbʊts / (AmE), / kɪˈbʊts / (BrE)
a communal settlement in Israel, typically a farm, where property and resources are collectively owned and children are sometimes raised communally
communal settlement in Israel

Examples

After volunteering on a kibbutz for a year, she gained a new appreciation for communal living.
Many kibbutz members participate in agricultural work and shared decision-making.
The children on the kibbutz were brought up together in a dedicated communal house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kibbutzim - plural form of kibbutz, communal settlements in Israel

Etymology

From Modern Hebrew קִבּוּץ (qibbutz), meaning 'gathering' or 'collective'.
