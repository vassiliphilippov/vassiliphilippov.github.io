fold

1. NOUN

/ foʊld / (AmE), / fəʊld / (BrE)
a bend or curve in a layer of rock, caused by geological forces
bend in rock layer (geology)

Examples

The Himalayan Mountains were created in part by massive folds in the Earth's crust.
Geologists identified a large anticline fold in the sedimentary rocks.
A syncline is a type of fold where the layers are bent downward.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fault - a crack or fracture in the Earth's crust where movement has occurred
