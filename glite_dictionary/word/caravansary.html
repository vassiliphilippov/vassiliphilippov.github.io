caravansary

1. NOUN

/ ˌkærəˈvænsɛri / (AmE), / ˌkærəˈvænsəri / (BrE)
An inn in some Middle Eastern or Asian countries, with a large courtyard for accommodating caravans.
inn with courtyard for caravans (Asia, Middle East)

Examples

Traders and their camels rested at a caravansary after days of travel across the desert.
The old caravansary stood as a reminder of the Silk Road’s bustling trade routes.
We spent the night in a restored caravansary, surrounded by ancient stone walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caravanserai - alternate spelling of caravansary
  • Synonym: inn - establishment for lodging travelers

Etymology

From Persian *karvansara*, from *karvan* (caravan) + *sara* (house).
