Hillel II

1. PROPN

/ hɪˈlɛl ˈtu / (AmE), / hɪˈlɛl ˈtuː / (BrE)
(c. 320–385), Palestinian rabbi and interpreter of Judaic law, known for instituting the fixed Jewish calendar.
Palestinian rabbi, fixed Jewish calendar

Examples

Hillel II played a significant role in Jewish history by establishing a lunisolar calendar.
Scholars often reference Hillel II when discussing the origins of the Jewish calendar.
The reforms of Hillel II had a lasting impact on religious observance.
