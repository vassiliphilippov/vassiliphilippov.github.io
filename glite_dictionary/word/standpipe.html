standpipe

1. NOUN

/ ˈstændˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈstand.pʌɪp / (BrE)
A vertical pipe used to deliver or control the flow of water in systems such as plumbing or firefighting equipment.
vertical water pipe (plumbing/firefighting)

Examples

A standpipe is often installed on each floor of a high-rise building for fire protection.
The plumber checked the standpipe connected to the washing machine to ensure proper drainage.
City workers repaired a broken standpipe to restore water supply to the neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrant - an outlet to draw water from a main, often used in firefighting

Etymology

Formed from 'stand' + 'pipe', indicating a pipe that stands upright.
