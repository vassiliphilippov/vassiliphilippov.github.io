such

1. DET

/ sʌtʃ / (AmE), / sʌtʃ / (BrE)
of the kind or degree previously mentioned or implied; to such an extent or degree as indicated or exemplified
to a high or significant degree or kind

Examples

I've never tasted such delicious food before in my life.
Such behavior will not be tolerated in this classroom.
She showed such kindness that everyone admired her.

Synset Examples

such weeping
so much weeping
such a help
such grief
never dreamed of such beauty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: so - to such a great extent or degree

Etymology

Old English 'swylc' (of this or that kind), related to German 'solch', Dutch 'zulk'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01558587-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADV

/ sʌtʃ / (AmE), / sʌtʃ / (BrE)
to so extreme a degree; used before a noun or noun phrase to emphasize its quality or extent.
to a great or extreme degree (emphatic use)

Examples

I've never tasted such delicious food in my life.
Why are you being such a perfectionist about this project?
She spoke with such enthusiasm that everyone wanted to join.

Synset Examples

he is such a baby
Such rich people!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: so - to such a great extent; very

Etymology

From Old English swilc (similar, such), related to so and which.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00148422-r
Synset POS: r
