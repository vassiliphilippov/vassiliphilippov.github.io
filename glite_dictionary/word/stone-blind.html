stone-blind

1. ADJ

/ stoʊn blaɪnd / (AmE), / stəʊn blaɪnd / (BrE)
completely blind; having no vision at all.
totally blind

Examples

The accident left him stone-blind in both eyes.
She was stone-blind and relied on a guide dog to get around.
Born stone-blind, he learned to use his other senses remarkably well.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: blind - unable to see; lacking the sense of sight

Etymology

Formed by intensifying 'blind' with 'stone,' used as an emphatic expression.
