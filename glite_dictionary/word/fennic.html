Fennic

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɛnɪk / (BrE)
A branch of the Finno-Ugric languages that includes Finnish, Estonian, and related languages, but not Hungarian.
branch of the Finno-Ugric language family

Examples

Scholars have traced the origins of the Fennic languages to the areas surrounding the Baltic Sea.
Estonian and Finnish both belong to the Fennic branch of the Finno-Ugric family.
The Fennic languages differ significantly from Hungarian, although all share ancient roots.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Finno-Ugric - a family of languages including Finnish, Estonian, and Hungarian

Etymology

From Latin Fennicus, relating to the Finns or Finland.
