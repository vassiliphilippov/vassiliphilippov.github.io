Iridoprocne

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌraɪdoʊˈprɑkni / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪdəʊˈprɒkni / (BrE)
A genus of swallows in the family Hirundinidae, including certain species native to the Americas.
genus of swallows (Hirundinidae)

Examples

The genus Iridoprocne includes the tree swallow native to North America.
Researchers have classified several species under Iridoprocne based on their plumage and nesting habits.
Birdwatchers often spot members of Iridoprocne near lakes and rivers during migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallow - a small migratory bird of the family Hirundinidae

Etymology

From Greek 'iris' (rainbow) and 'Prokne' (a character in Greek mythology).
