expiratory

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈspaɪrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɪrətəri / (BrE)
relating to or involving the act of breathing out during respiration
relating to breathing out
medical

Examples

The doctor measured her expiratory flow rate during the lung function test.
Certain medications can help improve expiratory capacity in patients with asthma.
A blocked airway can severely limit expiratory movements and make breathing difficult.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inspiratory - relating to the act of breathing in

Etymology

From Latin 'exspiratio' (a breathing out), based on 'exspirare' (to breathe out).
