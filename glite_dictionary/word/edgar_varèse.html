Edgar Varèse

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛdɡɑr vəˈreɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛdɡɑː vəˈreɪz / (BrE)
(1883–1965) French-born American composer known for his use of dissonance, complex rhythms, and pioneering work with electronic music.
French-born American composer (1883–1965)

Examples

Many music historians consider Edgar Varèse the father of electronic music.
The avant-garde compositions of Edgar Varèse influenced generations of experimental musicians.
A festival celebrating Edgar Varèse's work drew composers and enthusiasts from around the world.
