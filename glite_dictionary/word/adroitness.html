adroitness

1. NOUN

/ əˈdrɔɪtnəs / (AmE), / əˈdrɔɪtnəs / (BrE)
Skill or cleverness in performing a task; dexterity or agility, especially of thought or movement.
skill or cleverness; dexterity

Examples

She impressed the judges with her adroitness in solving complex problems.
The pianist's adroitness was evident in every note she played.
Negotiating the deal required considerable adroitness and patience.

Synset Examples

his quick adeptness was a product of good design
he was famous for his facility as an archer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dexterity - Skill in performing tasks, especially with the hands
  • Antonym: awkwardness - Lack of skill or grace; clumsiness

Etymology

From French 'adresse' (skill) and related to 'adroit' (clever, skillful).
