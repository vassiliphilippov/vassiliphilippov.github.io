vicious

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
Morally wrong or wicked; having the nature of vice or immorality.
morally wrong or wicked

Examples

The corrupt dictator was known for his vicious disregard for human rights.
She condemned the vicious behavior displayed by the leaders of the criminal gang.
His vicious character made it impossible for others to trust him.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Latin vitiosus (full of faults, corrupt), from vitium (fault, vice).

/ ˈvɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
showing deep ill will or a deliberate intention to harm someone; malicious or spiteful
intentionally harmful or malicious

Examples

The politician faced vicious criticism from the media after the scandal was revealed.
His vicious comments left everyone in the room feeling hurt and angry.

vicious gossip

Related Terms

4. ADJ

/ ˈvɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
(of a person or their actions) deliberately cruel or violent; intended to cause pain or suffering.
deliberately cruel or violent

Examples

The burglar launched a vicious attack on the homeowner.
Online harassment can be as vicious as physical bullying.
The debates became increasingly vicious as the election approached.

Synset Examples

a barbarous crime
brutal beatings
cruel tortures
Stalin's roughshod treatment of the kulaks
a savage slap
vicious kicks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruel - willing to cause pain or suffering to others without pity
  • Synonym: brutal - savagely violent or harsh
  • Antonym: kind - having or showing a friendly, generous, and considerate nature
  • Antonym: gentle - having a mild or kind nature; not harsh or cruel

Etymology

From Latin 'viciosus' (full of faults, corrupt), from 'vitium' (fault, defect).
