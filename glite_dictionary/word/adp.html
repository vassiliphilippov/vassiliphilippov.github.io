ADP

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˌdiː ˈpiː / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˌdiː ˈpiː / (BrE)
an ester of adenosine containing two phosphate groups, involved in energy transfer within cells and converted to ATP for energy storage
adenosine diphosphate, a cell energy molecule
medical

Examples

During exercise, ADP is rapidly converted into ATP to meet the muscle’s increased energy demands.
In cellular respiration, ADP plays a key role in storing and releasing energy.
The breakdown of ATP releases energy and produces ADP in the process.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ATP - adenosine triphosphate, an energy-carrying molecule converted from ADP
  • Degree Lower: AMP - adenosine monophosphate, a molecule with one phosphate group

Etymology

Abbreviation of adenosine diphosphate.
