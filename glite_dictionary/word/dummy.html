dummy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌmi / (AmE), / ˈdʌmi / (BrE)
a cartridge containing an explosive charge but no bullet, used for training or testing firearms
cartridge with charge but no bullet

Examples

The soldiers practiced loading their rifles with dummy rounds during the training exercise.
Before using live ammunition, always check that you are loading a dummy cartridge for safety drills.
He demonstrated the weapon's operation using a dummy to avoid any risk of accidents.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02852159-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʌmi / (AmE), / ˈdʌmi / (BrE)
having the appearance of being real or genuine but lacking the ability or capacity to function; not real.
appearing real but non-functional

Examples

The company set up a dummy corporation to hide its financial activities.
He replaced the real camera with a dummy one to deter thieves.
The exhibit included a dummy missile that could not actually be fired.

Synset Examples

a dummy corporation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fake - not genuine; imitation or counterfeit
  • Antonym: genuine - actually being what it is claimed to be; authentic
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01577224-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʌmi / (AmE), / ˈdʌmi / (BrE)
a person who is regarded as stupid or lacking intelligence
an ignorant or foolish person

Examples

Why did you do that, you dummy?
Only a real dummy would forget their keys three times in a row.
I felt like such a dummy when I answered the question incorrectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or judgment
  • Synonym: idiot - a very stupid person
  • Antonym: genius - a very intelligent or gifted person
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈdʌmi / (AmE), / ˈdʌmi / (BrE)
to produce a mock-up or prototype, especially as a preliminary version in publishing or design
produce a mock-up or prototype

Examples

The layout team will dummy the magazine pages before the final print.
She was asked to dummy a brochure for the new product launch.
We need to dummy up the annual report to review its flow and appearance.

Synset Examples

dummy up the books that are to be published

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mock up - to create a full-size model of something, usually for demonstration or design purposes

Etymology

From noun 'dummy' (a model or mock-up), of uncertain origin, possibly from 'dumb'.

5. NOUN

/ ˈdʌmi / (AmE), / ˈdʌmi / (BrE)
an object made to resemble a person, used for displaying clothes, practicing medical procedures, testing accidents, or as a decoy
figure representing a human form

Examples

The shop window was filled with dummies dressed in the latest fashion.
During the crash test, engineers used a dummy to simulate a real person.
Medical students practiced CPR on a training dummy before working with real patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mannequin - a life-sized model of the human body for displaying clothes

Etymology

From Early Modern English, possibly from Middle Low German 'dumm' (dumb, mute person).

Source: WordNet

6. NOUN

/ ˈdʌmi / (AmE), / ˈdʌmi / (BrE)
a person who does not talk, especially one who is mute (now considered offensive or old-fashioned)
person who does not talk (offensive/archaic)
offensive archaic

Examples

She felt isolated at school, where some children cruelly called her a dummy because she could not speak.
In old silent films, a dummy was sometimes portrayed for comic effect.
It is now recognized as offensive to refer to someone who cannot speak as a dummy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mute - a person unable to speak

Etymology

From dumb (mute, silent) + -y, originally referring to someone unable to speak.
