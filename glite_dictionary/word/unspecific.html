unspecific

1. ADJ

/ ʌn.spɪˈsɪf.ɪk / (AmE), / ʌn.spɪˈsɪf.ɪk / (BrE)
not detailed or clearly defined; not specific or precise
not detailed or precise

Examples

The doctor gave an unspecific diagnosis, leaving the cause of the symptoms unclear.
She felt an unspecific sense of anxiety before the speech.
His criticism was too unspecific to be useful for improving the project.

Synset Examples

a broad rule
the broad outlines of the plan
felt an unspecific dread

Related Terms

  • Antonym: specific - clearly defined or identified
  • Synonym: vague - not clearly expressed or defined

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' + 'specific'.
