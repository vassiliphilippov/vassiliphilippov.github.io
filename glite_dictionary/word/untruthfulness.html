untruthfulness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈtruθf(ə)lnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈtruːθf(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being untruthful; lack of honesty or truth; tendency to tell lies or misrepresent facts
lack of honesty; tendency to lie

Examples

His repeated untruthfulness made it difficult for anyone to trust his words.
The manager was fired due to her untruthfulness in financial reporting.
A reputation for untruthfulness can be damaging both personally and professionally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonesty - lack of honesty or integrity
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest or truthful
  • Antonym: truthfulness - the quality of being truthful

Etymology

From 'untruthful' + '-ness'. 'Untruthful' from 'un-' (not) + 'truthful'.
