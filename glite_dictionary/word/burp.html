burp

1. NOUN

/ bɝːp / (AmE), / bɜːp / (BrE)
an act of expelling gas noisily from the stomach through the mouth
noisy expulsion of stomach gas through the mouth

Examples

Excuse me, I just had a big burp after drinking that soda.
He tried to suppress a loud burp during the meeting.
The baby's first burp made everyone in the room laugh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belch - an act of noisily expelling gas from the stomach through the mouth

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced when burping.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00118398-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ bɝp / (AmE), / bɜːp / (BrE)
to let air come up noisily from your stomach and out through your mouth
expel gas noisily from the stomach

Examples

It is considered rude to burp loudly in public.
The baby started to burp after being fed.
She tried not to burp during the meeting.

Synset Examples

Please don't burp at the table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belch - to expel gas from the stomach noisily through the mouth

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced when air is expelled from the stomach.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00003443-v
Synset POS: v
