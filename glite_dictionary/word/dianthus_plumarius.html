Dianthus plumarius

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈænθəs pluːˈmɛəriəs / (AmE), / dʌɪˈanθəs pluːˈmeərɪəs / (BrE)
A European pink flower cultivated for its very fragrant pink or rosy blossoms; also known as garden pink.
fragrant European pink flower

Examples

The Dianthus plumarius filled the garden with a sweet, old-fashioned fragrance.
Many gardeners choose Dianthus plumarius for its attractive, fringed petals and pleasant scent.
A border of Dianthus plumarius adds a splash of pink to any flowerbed in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnation - a common cultivated flower, closely related species in the genus Dianthus

Etymology

From Latin 'Dianthus' (divine flower) and 'plumarius' (of feathers, referring to the fringed petals).
