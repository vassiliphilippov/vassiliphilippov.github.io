Pecopteris

1. NOUN

/ peɪˈkɑptərɪs / (AmE), / piːˈkɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of extinct Carboniferous fossil ferns known for their distinctive pinnate leaves, often found in coal-bearing rocks.
genus of extinct Carboniferous fossil ferns

Examples

Fossil specimens of Pecopteris are common in many Carboniferous coal seams.
Paleobotanists have studied Pecopteris to understand ancient plant communities.
The finely divided fronds of Pecopteris make it easily recognizable in fossil collections.

Etymology

From Greek 'pēkos' (comb) + 'pteris' (fern), referring to the comb-like appearance of the fronds.

2. NOUN

/ piːˈkɑptərɪs / (AmE), / piːˈkɒptərɪs / (BrE)
a genus of extinct fossil ferns from the Carboniferous period, characterized by leaflets arranged in a comb-like pattern
genus of extinct fossil ferns

Examples

Scientists discovered a well-preserved Pecopteris fossil in the coal seam.
The comb-like structure of Pecopteris leaves is distinctive among Carboniferous plants.
Many textbooks use Pecopteris to illustrate the diversity of prehistoric ferns.

Etymology

From Greek 'peko' (to comb) and 'pteris' (fern), referring to the comb-like arrangement of leaflets.
