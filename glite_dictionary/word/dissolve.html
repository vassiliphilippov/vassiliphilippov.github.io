dissolve

1. VERB

/ dɪˈzɑlv / (AmE), / dɪˈzɒlv / (BrE)
to cause a solid, gas, or liquid to pass into solution, usually by mixing with a liquid
to cause to go into solution

Examples

Stir the mixture until the sugar has completely dissolved.
Add salt to the water and let it dissolve before cooking the pasta.
Some chemicals dissolve more easily in hot water than in cold.

Synset Examples

The recipe says that we should dissolve a cup of sugar in two cups of water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melt - to change from solid to liquid by heating

Etymology

From Latin dissolvere ‘to loosen, undo’, from dis- ‘apart’ + solvere ‘to loosen’.
