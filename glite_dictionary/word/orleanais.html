Orleanais

1. NOUN

/ ɔrleɪˈneɪ / (AmE), / ɔːleɪˈneɪ / (BrE)
a former province of north central France, centered around Orléans
former province of north central France

Examples

The region known as Orleanais played a significant role in French history during the Middle Ages.
Many wines from Orleanais are prized for their unique flavor profiles.
The culture of Orleanais influenced much of the surrounding French countryside.

Etymology

From French Orléanais, relating to Orléans.
