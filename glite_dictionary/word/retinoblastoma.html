retinoblastoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛt.ɪ.noʊ.blæˈstoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˌrɛt.ɪ.nəʊ.blæˈstəʊ.mə / (BrE)
a malignant tumor of the retina, typically occurring in young children and composed of primitive retinal cells
malignant tumor of the retina
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with retinoblastoma after noticing unusual spots on his retina.
Early detection of retinoblastoma can significantly improve treatment outcomes.
Her family had a history of retinoblastoma, so regular eye examinations were essential.

Etymology

From Latin 'retina' (net-like membrane of the eye) and Greek 'blastōma' (growth, tumor), from 'blastos' (germ, sprout).
