immeasurable

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɛʒ.ər.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ɪˈmɛʒ.ər.ə.bəl / (BrE)
too large, extreme, or great to be measured; impossible to measure
impossible to measure or quantify

Examples

The beauty of the mountain landscape was immeasurable and left us in awe.
Her kindness and generosity are truly immeasurable.
The hurricane caused immeasurable damage throughout the region.

Synset Examples

unmeasurable reaches of outer space
immeasurable suffering

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incalculable - too great to be calculated
  • Synonym: infinite - without limits or end
  • Antonym: measurable - able to be measured

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + mensurabilis (measurable).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɛʒərəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈmɛʒ(ə)rəb(ə)l / (BrE)
too large, great, or extreme to be measured or calculated; impossible to measure or calculate.
too large or great to be measured

Examples

The impact of the new policy on the community was immeasurable and far-reaching.
She felt an immeasurable sense of relief after hearing the good news.
The love parents have for their children is often described as immeasurable.

Synset Examples

an incomputable amount
jewels of inestimable value
immeasurable wealth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incalculable - too great to be calculated; impossible to calculate
  • Synonym: inestimable - too great to be estimated or valued
  • Antonym: measurable - able to be measured

Etymology

From late Middle English, from Latin 'immensurabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'mensurabilis' (measurable).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
