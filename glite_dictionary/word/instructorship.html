instructorship

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈstrʌkt̬ɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ɪnˈstrʌktəʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, role, or condition of being an instructor, especially in an academic institution
the position of instructor

Examples

After earning her doctorate, she applied for an instructorship at the university.
His first teaching job was an instructorship in the mathematics department.
She held an instructorship before being promoted to assistant professor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instructor - a person who teaches, especially in a college or university

Etymology

Formed from 'instructor' + '-ship', denoting the condition or status.
