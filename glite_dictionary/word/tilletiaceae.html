Tilletiaceae

1. NOUN

/ tɪˌliːtiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / tɪˌliːtɪˈeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
a family of smut fungi characterized by a simple promycelium that bears spores in an apical cluster
family of smut fungi

Examples

The Tilletiaceae family contains several important plant pathogens affecting crops.
Scientists studied the Tilletiaceae to understand the spread of smut diseases in wheat.
Identification of Tilletiaceae relies on the unique structure of their spore-bearing cells.

Etymology

From genus name Tilletia, named after French botanist Mathieu Tillet, plus the standard taxonomic suffix -aceae.

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species belonging to the Tilletiaceae in the wheat fields of Europe.

From the genus name Tilletia, itself named after French botanist Mathieu Tillet (1714–1791), plus the standard taxonomic suffix -aceae for plant and fungi families.
