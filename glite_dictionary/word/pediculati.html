Pediculati

1. NOUN

/ pɪˌdɪkjəˈleɪtaɪ / (AmE), / pɪˌdɪkjʊˈlɑːtaɪ / (BrE)
A group of spiny-finned marine fishes, including anglers and batfishes, characterized by pectoral fins at the ends of armlike extensions and a long movable dorsal spine used to lure prey.
group of spiny-finned marine fishes

Examples

The Pediculati display fascinating adaptations for ambush predation in the deep sea.
Scientists collected several species of Pediculati during their expedition to study deep-sea biodiversity.
The peculiar shape of Pediculati allows them to blend in with the ocean floor while hunting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anglerfish - a type of marine fish that uses a lure to attract prey

Etymology

From Latin 'pediculus' (little foot), referring to the armlike pectoral fins.
