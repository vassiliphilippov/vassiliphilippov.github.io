rumba

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌmbə / (AmE), / ˈrʌmbə / (BrE)
A ballroom dance of Cuban origin characterized by a slow, rhythmic style and sensuous movements.
a ballroom dance of Cuban origin

Examples

They performed a passionate rumba during the dance competition.
After learning the rumba, she was eager to try other Latin dances.
The music set the perfect mood for a slow rumba on the dance floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: samba - a lively ballroom dance of Brazilian origin
  • Synonym: waltz - a ballroom dance in triple time

Etymology

From Spanish 'rumba', meaning 'party' or 'spree', originally from Cuba.
