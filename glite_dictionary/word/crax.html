Crax

1. NOUN

/ kræks / (AmE), / kraks / (BrE)
a genus of large, pheasant-like birds (curassows) native to Central and South America, belonging to the family Cracidae
genus of curassows (family Cracidae)

Examples

The Crax genus includes some of the largest curassows in the Americas.
Many members of the Crax genus are threatened due to habitat loss and hunting.
Ornithologists studied the nesting habits of several Crax species in the rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curassow - any large, crested bird of the family Cracidae, especially those in the genus Crax

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Crax, used by Carl Linnaeus for this bird genus.

Examples

The Crax genus includes some of the largest birds found in South American forests.
Several species within Crax are threatened due to habitat loss and hunting.
