schematic

1. NOUN

/ skiˈmætɪk / (AmE), / skiˈmætɪk / (BrE)
a diagram that represents the elements of a system, especially an electrical or mechanical one, using abstract, graphic symbols rather than realistic pictures
diagram of a system (esp. electrical/mechanical)

Examples

The technician referred to the schematic to troubleshoot the circuit board.
We need a detailed schematic before assembling the machine.
This schematic clearly shows how all the components are connected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagram - a simplified drawing showing the appearance, structure, or workings of something

Etymology

From Greek schēmatikos, from schēma (form, figure).

WordNet ID: oewn-04152643-n

2. ADJ

/ skəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / skiːˈmætɪk / (BrE)
(of a representation, diagram, etc.) presented in a simplified or symbolic form, often to show the structure or essential features.
presented in simplified or symbolic form

Examples

The engineer provided a schematic overview of the circuit’s design.
Her explanation of the system was deliberately schematic, focusing only on the main components.
In the manual, the schematic drawings helped users understand the complex machinery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagrammatic - presented in the form of a diagram

Etymology

From Greek 'skhēmatikos', from 'skhēma' (form, shape).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01987861-s
Synset POS: s
