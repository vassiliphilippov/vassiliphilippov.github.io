Azolla caroliniana

1. NOUN

/ əˈzɑːlə ˌkɛrəˌlɪˈnaɪ.ə / (AmE), / əˈzɒlə ˌkærəˈlɪniə / (BrE)
A small free-floating aquatic fern native to the eastern United States and tropical America, often naturalized in parts of Europe.
species of small aquatic fern

Examples

Azolla caroliniana can rapidly cover the surface of ponds, reducing light penetration for other plants.
Researchers are studying Azolla caroliniana for its potential to control mosquito populations in wetlands.
The introduction of Azolla caroliniana into European waterways has led to ecological concerns.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek, Azolla; species name from Carolina, referring to the Carolinas in the US.
