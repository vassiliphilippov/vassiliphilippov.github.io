vena scrotalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə skroʊˈteɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə skrɒˈteɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the scrotum into the pudendal veins
a vein of the scrotum
medical

Examples

The vena scrotalis is an important vessel in the male reproductive system.
During surgery, care must be taken not to damage the vena scrotalis.
Blood from the scrotum is collected by the vena scrotalis and directed towards the pudendal veins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pudendal vein - vein that drains blood from the external genitalia

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'scrotalis' (of the scrotum).
