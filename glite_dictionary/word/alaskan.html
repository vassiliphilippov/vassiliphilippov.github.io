Alaskan

1. ADJ

/ əˈlæskən / (AmE), / əˈlɑːskən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Alaska or its people.
of or from Alaska or its people

Examples

She wore an Alaskan parka to stay warm during the winter.
The Alaskan wilderness is famous for its breathtaking beauty.
Many Alaskan traditions are rooted in native cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alaska - the largest and most northern state of the USA

Etymology

From 'Alaska', the name of the US state, + -an (denoting belonging or relation).

Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ əˈlæskən / (AmE), / əˈlæskən / (BrE)
a person who is a native or resident of Alaska
native or resident of Alaska

Examples

Many Alaskans enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and fishing.
The Alaskan shared stories about the long, cold winters in her hometown.
As an Alaskan, he is accustomed to extreme temperatures and snow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alaska - the largest state in the United States, located in the northwest corner of North America

Etymology

Formed from Alaska (the U.S. state) + -an (demonym suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09760285-n
Synset POS: n
