unmelodious

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.məˈloʊ.di.əs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.məˈləʊ.di.əs / (BrE)
Not having a pleasant or tuneful melody; sounding harsh or discordant.
not tuneful; harsh-sounding

Examples

The unmelodious notes from the poorly tuned piano filled the room.
Despite his talent, his singing voice was rather unmelodious and flat.
Crows are known for their harsh and unmelodious calls.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: melodious - pleasant-sounding; tuneful

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not', and 'melodious', from Latin 'melodiosus' (tuneful).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.məˈloʊ.di.əs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.məˈləʊ.di.əs / (BrE)
not having a musical or pleasant sound; not melodious.
not having a pleasing or musical sound

Examples

The parrot's unmelodious screeches startled everyone in the room.
His voice was harsh and unmelodious during the recital, disappointing the audience.
They complained about the unmelodious clanging that echoed from the old factory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: melodious - having a pleasant musical sound

Etymology

From un- (not) + melodious, which comes from Late Latin melodiosus (melodious).
