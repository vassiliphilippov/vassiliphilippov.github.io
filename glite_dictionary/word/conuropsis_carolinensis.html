Conuropsis carolinensis

1. NOUN

/ kəˌnʊrˈɑpsɪs ˌkæroʊlɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / kəˌnjʊəˈrɒpsɪs ˌkarəlɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
an extinct species of parakeet, once native to the eastern, midwest, and plains regions of the United States
extinct North American parakeet

Examples

The last known Conuropsis carolinensis died in captivity in the early twentieth century.
Researchers study the remains of Conuropsis carolinensis to learn about its habitat and behavior.
Sightings of Conuropsis carolinensis were once common throughout the southeastern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carolina parakeet - common name for Conuropsis carolinensis, extinct North American parakeet

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name from Greek 'kónuros' (parrot) + species from Latin 'Carolinensis' (of Carolina).
