built-in

1. ADJ

/ ˌbɪltˈɪn / (AmE), / ˌbɪltˈɪn / (BrE)
forming a permanent or essential part of something; included as an integral feature or characteristic.
forming an essential part; inherent

Examples

The new camera comes with several built-in features, such as Wi-Fi and GPS.
There is a built-in advantage to having experience in this field.
Respect for others should be a built-in value of any civil society.

Synset Examples

the Ptolemaic system with its built-in concept of periodicity
a constitutional inability to tell the truth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inherent - existing in something as a permanent, essential, or characteristic attribute
  • Synonym: intrinsic - belonging naturally; essential
  • Antonym: extrinsic - not part of the essential nature of someone or something; coming or operating from outside

Etymology

From 'built' (past participle of 'build') + 'in', originally describing furniture permanently incorporated into a structure.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɪltˌɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɪlt.ɪn / (BrE)
forming an integral part of something, especially a device, rather than being separate or added later.
integrated as part of something

Examples

The kitchen features a built-in dishwasher for added convenience.
Many cars now come with built-in navigation systems as standard equipment.
She admired the built-in bookshelves that maximized the use of space in the living room.

Synset Examples

The bike came with a built-in bottleholder for convenience.
The new software has built-in tattleware to monitor usage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integrated - combined into a whole; forming part of something
  • Antonym: detachable - able to be removed; not integrated
