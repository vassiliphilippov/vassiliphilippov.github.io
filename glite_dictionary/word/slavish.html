slavish

1. ADJ

/ ˈsleɪvɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈslɑːvɪʃ / (BrE)
Showing a lack of originality by imitating someone or something else very closely and uncritically.
blindly imitative; lacking originality

Examples

The painting was dismissed as a slavish imitation of the master's style.
Critics accused the new film of being slavish in its adherence to the original script.
Rather than offering her own ideas, she gave a slavish repetition of her mentor's theories.

Synset Examples

a slavish copy of the original

Related Terms

  • Antonym: original - new and not derived from something else
  • Synonym: imitative - characterized by imitation

Etymology

From Middle English slavisch, from 'slave' + '-ish'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsleɪ.vɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsleɪ.vɪʃ / (BrE)
Showing excessive willingness to serve or obey others; lacking independence or originality.
excessively submissive or imitative

Examples

His slavish devotion to the company left him with little time for his family.
She rejected the idea of a slavish imitation and aimed for originality.
The manager expected a slavish loyalty from all his employees.

Synset Examples

slavish devotion to her job ruled her life
a slavish yes-man to the party bosses
she has become submissive and subservient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submissive - willing to submit to authority or others
  • Synonym: servile - having or showing an excessive willingness to serve or please others
  • Antonym: independent - not subject to control by others; self-reliant

Etymology

From Old English *slāf* (slave) + -ish.
