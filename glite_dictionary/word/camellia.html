camellia

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɛliə / (AmE), / kəˈmiːliə / (BrE)
A shrub or small evergreen tree with glossy leaves and solitary white, pink, or red flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant.
flowering evergreen shrub; genus Camellia

Examples

The camellia in the garden bloomed early this spring, brightening the yard with pink blossoms.
She gave her grandmother a bouquet of camellias as a birthday gift.
Many gardeners prize the camellia for its glossy leaves and elegant flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azalea - a flowering shrub of the genus Rhododendron

Etymology

Named after Jesuit botanist Georg Kamel (1661–1706).
