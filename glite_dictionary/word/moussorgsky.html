Moussorgsky

1. PROPN

/ muːˈsɔrɡski / (AmE), / muːˈsɔːɡski / (BrE)
Modest Petrovich Moussorgsky (1839–1881), Russian composer known for his operas and orchestral works.
Russian composer (1839–1881)

Examples

The works of Moussorgsky remain some of the most innovative in Russian classical music.
Many orchestras feature Moussorgsky's 'Pictures at an Exhibition' in their repertoire.
After hearing Moussorgsky, she became fascinated by Russian composers.

Etymology

From Russian Модест Петрович Мусоргский (Modest Petrovich Mussorgskiy).
