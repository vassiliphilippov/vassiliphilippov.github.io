sum

1. NOUN

/ sʌm / (AmE), / sʌm / (BrE)
an amount of money; a particular quantity of money considered as a total
a quantity of money

Examples

They raised a considerable sum to support the charity's work.
The insurance company paid out a small sum for the damages.
She inherited a large sum after her uncle's passing.

Synset Examples

he borrowed a large sum
the amount he had in cash was insufficient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amount - a quantity of money; total sum
  • Synonym: total - the whole amount; aggregate

Etymology

From Latin summa (total, sum), from summus (highest).

4. NOUN

/ sʌm / (AmE), / sʌm / (BrE)
In mathematics, a set containing all and only the elements belonging to two or more given sets; the union of sets.
the union of sets (mathematics)

Examples

In set theory, the sum of two sets A and B contains all elements found in either set.
The sum of disjoint sets is simply the union of their elements.
If you take the sum of sets X, Y, and Z, you include every unique member from all three.
