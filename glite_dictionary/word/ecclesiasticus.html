Ecclesiasticus

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌliːziˈæstɪkəs / (AmE), / ɪkˌliːziˈæstɪkəs / (BrE)
a book of the Apocrypha, consisting mainly of maxims and moral teachings, resembling Proverbs; also called the Wisdom of Sirach
Apocryphal book of maxims and teachings

Examples

Many scholars debate the historical value of Ecclesiasticus when studying early Jewish thought.
The teachings in Ecclesiasticus have influenced both Christian and Jewish ethical traditions.
She discovered a well-worn copy of Ecclesiasticus in her grandfather’s old Bible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sirach - alternative name for Ecclesiasticus, a book of the Apocrypha

Etymology

Latin, from Greek 'Ekklēsiastikos', 'of the assembly', title of the book in the Septuagint.
