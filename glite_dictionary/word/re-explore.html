to re-explore

1. VERB

/ ˌriː ɪkˈsplɔɚ / (AmE), / ˌriː ɪkˈsplɔː / (BrE)
to explore again or in a new way
explore again or anew

Examples

The researchers decided to re-explore the site using advanced technology.
We should re-explore old topics to uncover new insights.
After the initial survey, the team plans to re-explore the area next year.

Synset Examples

We need to re-explore Colonialism

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: to explore - to travel through or examine for discovery

Etymology

Prefix re- (again) + explore (from Latin explorare, to investigate).
