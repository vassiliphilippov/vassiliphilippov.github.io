sacral

1. ADJ

/ ˈseɪ.krəl / (AmE), / ˈsæ.krəl / (BrE)
relating to or situated near the sacrum, the large triangular bone at the base of the spine.
relating to the sacrum (base of the spine)
medical

Examples

He was diagnosed with a fracture of the sacral vertebrae.
The sacral nerves play a vital role in bladder control.
Physical therapy focused on reducing sacral pain after his injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelvic - relating to the pelvis

Etymology

From Latin 'sacrum' (sacred bone).

2. ADJ

/ ˈseɪ.krəl / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.krəl / (BrE)
relating to sacred rites or ceremonies; having a sacred or religious character.
relating to sacred rites or ceremonies

Examples

The ancient temple was the site of many sacral ceremonies that honored the gods.
Some laws in early societies were considered sacral and were enforced with religious authority.
During the festival, the priest performed a sacral rite to bless the community.

Synset Examples

sacral laws

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacred - connected with God or a deity and so deserving veneration

Etymology

From Latin 'sacralis', from 'sacer' meaning 'sacred'.
