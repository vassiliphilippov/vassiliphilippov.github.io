gillie

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪli / (AmE), / ˈɡɪli / (BrE)
A light shoe without a tongue, featuring decorative lacing up the instep, traditionally worn in Scottish and Irish dancing.
soft shoe with decorative lacing (dance)

Examples

Traditional Scottish dancers often wear gillies for performances.
She laced up her gillies before the Irish dance competition began.
These gillies provide flexibility and support for intricate dance steps.

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'gille' (lad, servant); the shoe is named after the gillies who wore them.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪli / (AmE), / ˈɡɪli / (BrE)
a young male attendant serving a Scottish Highland chief, traditionally responsible for outdoor tasks such as carrying weapons or assisting during hunting expeditions
young attendant of a Scottish Highland chief

Examples

The young gillie carried the chief's rifle across the rugged moorland.
Each Highland chief was accompanied by a trusted gillie during the deer hunt.
Historically, a gillie would also handle the chief's horse and other duties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valet - a male servant who attends to the personal needs of his employer

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'gille' meaning 'boy' or 'servant'.
