antepenultimate

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ti.pəˈnʌl.tɪ.mət / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.pɪˈnʌl.tɪ.mət / (BrE)
third from last in a series; specifically, the item that comes before the penultimate.
third from last

Examples

The stress falls on the antepenultimate syllable in that word.
He finished in the antepenultimate position, just ahead of two others.
In the sequence, the antepenultimate chapter introduces a major plot twist.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: penultimate - second from last
  • Degree Lower: ultimate - last; final

Etymology

From Latin 'antepaenultima' (feminine), from 'ante-' (before) + 'paenultima' (almost last).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.pəˈnʌl.tə.mɪt / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.pəˈnʌl.tɪ.mət / (BrE)
the third syllable from the end of a word, when counting backwards
third syllable from a word's end

Examples

When saying the word ‘photography,’ the antepenultimate syllable is ‘tog’.
In English poetry, stress often falls on the antepenultimate syllable of certain words.
To find the antepenultimate in ‘independent’, count backwards three syllables from the end.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: penult - second-to-last syllable in a word
  • Degree Lower: ultimate - last syllable in a word

Etymology

From Latin 'ante' (before) + 'paene' (almost) + 'ultimus' (last).

Synset POS: n
