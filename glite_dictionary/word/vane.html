vane

1. NOUN

/ veɪn / (AmE), / veɪn / (BrE)
the flattened, weblike part of a feather, made up of barbs on either side of the shaft
flattened part of a feather

Examples

The vane of the eagle's feather spread out beautifully under the microscope.
Birds rely on the integrity of the feather's vane for efficient flight.
Some artists study the structure of a vane to learn more about avian anatomy.

2. NOUN

/ veɪn / (AmE), / veɪn / (BrE)
A flat or curved plate attached to an elevated structure, such as a roof, that rotates freely to indicate the direction from which the wind is blowing.
rotating device showing wind direction

Examples

The old farmhouse had a metal vane on its roof shaped like a rooster.
The vane turned rapidly as the storm approached, pointing north.
Climbers noticed the rusted vane was still spinning at the top of the church steeple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weathercock - weather vane in the shape of a rooster

Etymology

From Old English 'fana' (flag, banner), related to Old Norse 'fáni'.

3. NOUN

/ veɪn / (AmE), / veɪn / (BrE)
a fin or blade attached to the tail of an arrow, bomb, or missile to stabilize or guide its flight
stabilizing fin on a projectile

Examples

The arrow's vane helps to keep it stable during flight.
Each missile was equipped with a small vane at its tail to ensure accuracy.
The technician repaired the damaged vane before loading the bomb onto the aircraft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fin - a thin, flat part that sticks out from a body or object to help stabilize or guide it

Etymology

From Old English 'fana' (flag, banner), later influenced by Old French 'vane' (weathercock, flag).

4. NOUN

/ veɪn / (AmE), / veɪn / (BrE)
a flat surface that turns or rotates and pushes against air or water, typically as part of a machine or instrument
rotating flat surface; part of machine or instrument

Examples

The wind vane on top of the barn spun as the breeze shifted direction.
Each fan has several vanes that help move the air efficiently.
The turbine's vane design improves its ability to capture wind energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blade - flat, broad part of a propeller or turbine

Etymology

From Old English 'fana' (flag, banner), later specialized to mechanical sense.
