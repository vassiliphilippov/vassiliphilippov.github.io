Hyalosperma

1. NOUN

/ haɪəloʊˈspɜrmə / (AmE), / haɪələʊˈspɜːmə / (BrE)
a genus of flowering herbs native to temperate regions of Australia, some species formerly included in the genus Helipterum
genus of Australian herbs

Examples

Many species in the genus Hyalosperma are found in open woodlands across Australia.
The delicate flowers of Hyalosperma are popular among native plant enthusiasts.
Researchers are studying Hyalosperma for its adaptation to dry Australian climates.

Etymology

From Greek 'hyalos' (glass) + 'sperma' (seed), referring to seed appearance.

2. NOUN

/ haɪˌæloʊˈspɜrmə / (AmE), / haɪˌaləˈspɜːmə / (BrE)
A genus of herbs native to temperate regions of Australia, formerly including some species classified under Helipterum.
Australian genus of flowering herbs

Examples

Several species of Hyalosperma are found in the grasslands of southern Australia.
Botanists recently discovered a new variety within the Hyalosperma genus.
Dried flowers of Hyalosperma are sometimes used in decorative arrangements.

Etymology

From Greek 'hyalos' (glass) and 'sperma' (seed), referring to the seed's appearance.
