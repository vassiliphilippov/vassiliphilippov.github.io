Quai d'Orsay

1. NOUN

/ keɪ dɔːrˈseɪ / (AmE), / keɪ dɔːrˈseɪ / (BrE)
the French Ministry of Foreign Affairs, often referred to by the name of the street in Paris where its headquarters are located
the French foreign affairs ministry

Examples

The Quai d'Orsay issued a statement regarding the diplomatic crisis.
Many policy decisions are made behind closed doors at the Quai d'Orsay.
Journalists gathered outside the Quai d'Orsay to await the official response.

Etymology

From the address of the French Ministry of Foreign Affairs, located on the Quai d'Orsay, a street along the Seine in Paris.

2. PROPN

/ keɪ dɔːrˈseɪ / (AmE), / keɪ dɔːˈseɪ / (BrE)
the street in Paris along the south bank of the Seine, known for housing French governmental ministries, especially the Ministry of Foreign Affairs; also used as a metonym for the French foreign ministry itself
notable Paris street, metonym for French Foreign Ministry

Examples

Diplomatic negotiations often take place at the Quai d'Orsay in Paris.
Journalists gathered outside the Quai d'Orsay to await the official statement.
In political discussions, 'the Quai d'Orsay' sometimes refers directly to France's Foreign Ministry.

Etymology

French, literally 'quay of Orsay', named after the Orsay family, notable in Parisian history.
