Gyromitra sphaerospora

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒaɪrəˈmaɪtrə sfɪˌɛrəˈspɔrə / (AmE), / ˌdʒaɪ.rəˈmaɪ.trə sfɪə.rəˈspɔː.rə / (BrE)
A species of false morel mushroom with a brown, rounded fertile surface and a rosy pink fluted stalk, found on hardwood debris east of the Great Plains.
a species of false morel mushroom

Examples

The distinctive features of Gyromitra sphaerospora make it relatively easy to recognize among spring mushrooms.
Scientists discovered new populations of Gyromitra sphaerospora growing on decaying hardwood branches.
When identifying wild fungi, knowing the coloration of Gyromitra sphaerospora helps avoid confusion with edible morels.

Etymology

From Greek 'gyros' (circle) + 'mitra' (headband or turban), sphaerospora from Greek 'sphaira' (sphere) + 'spora' (seed or spore).
