Viminaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪməˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / ˌvɪmɪˈnɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of Australian shrubs in the legume family, known for their leafless, rush-like appearance; includes the swamp oak.
Australian genus of leafless shrubs (swamp oak)

Examples

Viminaria is native to the wetlands of southern Australia and is commonly referred to as swamp oak.
Botanists often study Viminaria because of its unique leafless structure.
The elegant form of Viminaria makes it popular in native Australian gardens.

Etymology

From Latin 'vimen' (a twig, osier), referring to the plant's long, slender stems.

2. NOUN

/ vɪmɪˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / vɪmɪˈnɛːrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of Australian shrubs in the pea family, characterized by their almost leafless stems and commonly called swamp oak
Australian shrub genus (pea family)

Examples

Viminaria is valued for its adaptation to wet Australian environments.
Many botanists study the unique flowering pattern of Viminaria.
The Viminaria genus consists of just one species, which thrives in swampy areas.

Etymology

From Latin 'vimen' (twig, osier), referencing the plant's slender branches.
