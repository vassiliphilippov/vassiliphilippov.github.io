Hydrophyllum virginianum

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊˈfɪliəm vərˌdʒɪniˈænəm / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəˈfɪliəm vɜːˌdʒɪniˈænəm / (BrE)
A species of showy perennial herb with white flowers, sometimes used as edible greens in the southeastern United States.
species of showy perennial herb

Examples

Botanists study Hydrophyllum virginianum for its unique white blossoms and potential culinary uses.
In early spring, Hydrophyllum virginianum can be found in moist forests throughout the southeast.
Some foragers collect the young leaves of Hydrophyllum virginianum to use as edible greens.

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Hydrophyllum' from Greek 'hydro-' (water) + 'phyllon' (leaf), species epithet 'virginianum' meaning 'of Virginia'.
