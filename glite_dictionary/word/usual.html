usual

1. ADJ

/ ˈjuʒuəl / (AmE), / ˈjuːʒuəl / (BrE)
happening or done most of the time; commonly occurring or typical
commonly occurring; typical

Examples

She had her usual cup of tea before starting work every morning.
The meeting proceeded at the usual time despite the bad weather.
It’s not usual for him to arrive so late.

Synset Examples

grew the usual vegetables
the usual summer heat
came at the usual time
the child's usual bedtime

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unusual - not common, rare or not typical
  • Synonym: customary - according to custom; usual or typical

Etymology

From Late Latin usualis (customary), from Latin usus (use).

2. ADJ

/ ˈjuːʒuəl / (AmE), / ˈjuːʒuəl / (BrE)
happening, done, or used most often; typical or normal
commonly encountered; typical

Examples

I took my usual route to work this morning.
It's not usual for her to arrive this late.
He ordered his usual coffee at the café.

Synset Examples

a common (or familiar) complaint
the usual greeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common - occurring often; familiar
  • Antonym: unusual - not common or ordinary; rare

Etymology

From Late Latin usualis (customary), from Latin usus (use).
