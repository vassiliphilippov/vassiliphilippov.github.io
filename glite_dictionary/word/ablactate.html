to ablactate

1. VERB

/ ˌæbˈlæk.teɪt / (AmE), / ˌæbˈlæk.teɪt / (BrE)
to gradually wean (an infant or young mammal) from its mother's milk
to gradually wean from mother's milk
medical

Examples

The veterinarian advised us to ablactate the puppies gradually over several weeks.
It is important not to ablactate young mammals too early in order to ensure proper nutrition.
Many farmers ablactate their lambs at around three months of age.

Synset Examples

she weaned her baby when he was 3 months old and started him on powdered milk
The kitten was weaned and fed by its owner with a bottle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wean - to gradually stop feeding a baby or young animal with its mother's milk and to start feeding it with solid food, or to stop giving a baby its mother's breast milk

Etymology

From Latin 'ablactare', from 'ab-' (from) + 'lactare' (to suckle, from 'lac', 'milk').
