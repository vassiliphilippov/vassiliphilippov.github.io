flotsam

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɑːt.səm / (AmE), / ˈflɒt.səm / (BrE)
The floating wreckage of a ship or its cargo, especially after a shipwreck.
floating wreckage of a ship

Examples

The storm left broken wood and flotsam scattered along the shore.
After the collision, pieces of flotsam drifted past the rescue boats.
The beach was littered with flotsam from the shipwreck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jetsam - goods that have been thrown overboard from a ship and washed ashore

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman floteson, from Old French floter (to float).
