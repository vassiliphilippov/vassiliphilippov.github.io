Cannabidaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæn.ə.bɪˈdeɪ.siː.iː / (AmE), / ˌkan.ə.bɪˈdeɪ.siː.iː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants including the genera Cannabis (hemp) and Humulus (hops), sometimes recognized in plant classifications
family of plants including hemp and hops

Examples

The Cannabidaceae family includes both hemp and hop plants, which are economically important.
Recent studies suggest that the Cannabidaceae may be more closely related to the Moraceae than previously thought.
Botanists debate whether Cannabis should belong to the Cannabidaceae or another family.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Cannabis (the type genus) and the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkænəˌbɪdəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / ˌkan.ə.bɪ.dəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants including the genera Cannabis (hemp) and Humulus (hops), comprising erect or twining herbs pollinated by wind; its classification varies in different botanical systems
family of plants including Cannabis and Humulus

Examples

The Cannabidaceae family includes both hemp and hops, which are economically important plants.
Recent studies have revised the classification of Cannabidaceae within angiosperms.
Botanists often debate whether Cannabidaceae should include the genus Humulus.

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Cannabidaceae', based on 'Cannabis' (the hemp genus) and the botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
