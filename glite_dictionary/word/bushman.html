Bushman

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊʃ.mən / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃ.mən / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people of southern Africa, traditionally nomadic hunters and gatherers, also known as San; use of this term may be viewed as outdated or offensive
member of indigenous hunter-gatherer people of southern Africa
offensive

Examples

Anthropologists have studied the traditional way of life of the Bushman people.
The Bushmen are renowned for their remarkable tracking and survival skills.
In the desert, the Bushman told stories passed down through generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: San - the self-designation for the indigenous people of southern Africa known for a hunter-gatherer lifestyle

Etymology

From 'bush' (wilderness) + 'man'; originally used by European colonists in South Africa.
