Cleistes

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪstiːz / (AmE), / ˈkleɪstiːz / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial orchids native to North and South America, with slender fibrous roots and related to the genus Pogonia.
a genus of American terrestrial orchids

Examples

The Cleistes orchids display delicate pinkish flowers in late spring.
Botanists discovered a rare species of Cleistes in the wetlands of southern Brazil.
Unlike Pogonia, Cleistes has a more tubular flower structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pogonia - a related genus of terrestrial orchids

Etymology

From Greek 'kleistos' (closed), referring to the flower structure.
