Sarcostemma acidum

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑɹkoʊˈstɛmə əˈsaɪdəm / (AmE), / ˌsɑːkəʊˈstɛmə əˈsɪdəm / (BrE)
A species of leafless vine native to East India, known for its sour milky juice that was formerly used to make an intoxicating drink.
leafless East Indian vine; source of intoxicant

Examples

The villagers once fermented the juice of Sarcostemma acidum to create a potent beverage.
Botanists studied the unusual adaptations of Sarcostemma acidum for surviving in arid climates.
Traditional healers valued Sarcostemma acidum for both its medicinal properties and its use in rituals.

Etymology

From Greek 'sarx' (flesh) + 'stemma' (garland), with 'acidum' meaning 'sour' in Latin.
