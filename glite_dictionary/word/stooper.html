stooper

1. NOUN

/ ˈstuːpər / (AmE), / ˈstuːpə / (BrE)
a person at a racetrack who searches for winning parimutuel tickets carelessly discarded by others
person who searches for discarded winning tickets

Examples

The stooper spent hours scanning the grandstand for forgotten winning tickets.
Some racetracks have posted signs warning stoopers to stay clear of restricted areas.
After the last race, the stooper managed to find two tickets worth fifty dollars.

Etymology

From the verb 'stoop' (to bend down), referring to the action performed while searching the ground.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstuːpər / (AmE), / ˈstʃuːpə / (BrE)
a person who habitually walks or stands with the head and shoulders bent forward
person with head and shoulders bent forward

Examples

The elderly stooper shuffled slowly down the hall, his gaze fixed on the floor.
After years of working in the fields, he became a stooper who struggled to stand straight.
The doctor advised the stooper to perform daily exercises to improve his posture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoop - to bend one's head and shoulders forward
  • Antonym: upright - a person standing with the back and shoulders straight

Etymology

From 'stoop' (to bend forward) + '-er' (one who does), modeled after English agent noun formation.
