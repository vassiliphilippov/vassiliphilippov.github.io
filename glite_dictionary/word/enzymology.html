enzymology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛnzaɪˈmɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌɛnzaɪˈmɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of biochemistry concerned with the study of the chemical properties and biological functions of enzymes
study of enzymes in biochemistry
medical

Examples

She is pursuing a doctorate in enzymology to better understand metabolic diseases.
Advances in enzymology have led to new treatments for various illnesses.
The lecture focused on recent discoveries in the field of enzymology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biochemistry - the study of chemical processes in living things

Etymology

From enzyme + -logy, after French enzymologie.
