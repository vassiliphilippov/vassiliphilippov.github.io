metalwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtlˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈmɛtlˌwɜːk / (BrE)
the parts of an object that are made of metal
the metal parts of something

Examples

The old gate's metalwork was rusted and badly in need of repair.
You should polish the metalwork on the car to keep it shiny and clean.
During restoration, specialists carefully examined the antique clock's metalwork.

Synset Examples

there were bullet holes in the metalwork

Related Terms

  • Synonym: framework - the structural parts of something, often made of wood or metal

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtəlˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈmɛtəlˌwɜːk / (BrE)
the activity or skill of making things out of metal, especially by shaping and joining pieces together
the making of things out of metal

Examples

She took a course in metalwork and learned to make decorative bowls.
The exhibition features traditional metalwork from various cultures around the world.
His father taught him the basics of metalwork in their home workshop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metalworking - the process of working with metals to create individual parts, assemblies, or structures

Etymology

From Middle English metallwerk, from metal + work.
