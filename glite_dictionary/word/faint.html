faint

1. ADJ

/ feɪnt / (AmE), / feɪnt / (BrE)
(of a person) feeling weak and close to losing consciousness.

Examples

The heat made him so faint that he had to sit down.
If you feel faint, try to put your head between your knees.

3. VERB

/ feɪnt / (AmE), / feɪnt / (BrE)
to lose consciousness for a short time, typically because of a temporary insufficient supply of blood to the brain
lose consciousness briefly

Examples

Several people began to faint from the heat during the crowded ceremony.
She thought she might faint when she saw the sight of blood.
He fainted unexpectedly and was helped by bystanders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly, often due to illness or weakness
  • Antonym: to recover - to regain consciousness or strength after fainting

Etymology

From Old French faintir (to get weak), from Latin fingere (to shape, pretend).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00023639-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ feɪnt / (AmE), / feɪnt / (BrE)
a sudden and brief loss of consciousness, usually caused by a decrease in blood flow to the brain
temporary loss of consciousness

Examples

She suffered a faint in the crowded subway car, startling the people around her.
After standing in the hot sun for hours, he experienced a sudden faint.
The doctor explained that a faint is usually harmless but can occasionally signal a more serious condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syncope - medical term for temporary loss of consciousness
  • Synonym: collapse - sudden falling down often due to loss of consciousness

Etymology

From Old French 'feint' (feigned, weak) and Latin 'fingere' (to shape, feign).
