Richard the Lion-Hearted

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪtʃərd ðə ˈlaɪənˌhɑrtɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃəd ðə ˈlaɪənˌhɑːtɪd / (BrE)
(1157–1199) King of England from 1189 to 1199, famed for his bravery and leadership during the Third Crusade; son of Henry II, captured on his return and ransomed by England in 1194.
King of England; leader in the Third Crusade

Examples

Richard the Lion-Hearted became a legendary figure in English history for his role in the Crusades.
The exploits of Richard the Lion-Hearted are recounted in many medieval stories and ballads.
After being captured on his return from the Holy Land, Richard the Lion-Hearted was ransomed by his people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Richard I - Another name for Richard the Lion-Hearted, King of England (1157–1199)

Etymology

'Lion-Hearted' translates the Old French 'Cœur de Lion', a sobriquet given for his bravery.
