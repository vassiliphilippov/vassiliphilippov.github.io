nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate

1. NOUN

/ nɪˌkoʊtɪnəˌmaɪd æˈdiːnɪn daɪˈnuːkliəˌtaɪd ˈfɑsfeɪt / (AmE), / nɪˌkəʊtɪnəˌmaɪd æˈdiːnɪn daɪˈnjuːkliətaɪd ˈfɒsfeɪt / (BrE)
a coenzyme present in most living cells that acts as a reductant in various metabolic processes, similar to NAD but with a phosphate group
coenzyme involved in metabolic processes
medical

Examples

nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate is essential for biosynthetic reactions in plants and animals.
The reduced form of nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate plays a vital role in photosynthesis.
Scientists measured changes in nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate levels during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: NADPH - abbreviation for nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide phosphate (reduced form)
  • Synonym: nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide - related coenzyme (NAD), without the phosphate group

Etymology

Formed from the names of its molecular components: nicotinamide, adenine, and phosphate; related to NAD (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) with added phosphate.
