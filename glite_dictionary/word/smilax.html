smilax

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmaɪ.læks / (AmE), / ˈsmɪ.laks / (BrE)
A twining plant of the genus Smilax, especially one with bright green flattened stems and glossy leaves, often used in floral decoration.
twining plant used in floral decoration

Examples

The florist added smilax to the bouquet for a touch of elegance.
We decorated the banquet hall with garlands of smilax and fresh flowers.
Smilax vines were draped across the table for a natural, festive look.

Etymology

From Latin smilax, from Greek smilax (yew, bindweed).
