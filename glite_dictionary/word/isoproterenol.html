isoproterenol

1. NOUN

/ aɪsəˌproʊtəˈriːnɑl / (AmE), / aɪsəʊˌprəʊtəˈriːnɒl / (BrE)
a drug used to treat bronchial asthma and to stimulate the heart
a drug for asthma and heart stimulation
medical

Examples

The doctor administered isoproterenol to help stimulate the patient’s heart function.
Patients with acute asthma attacks may receive isoproterenol as part of their treatment.
Because of its effects on the airways, isoproterenol is sometimes used in emergency medicine.

Etymology

From iso- (denoting ‘equal’) and proterenol, itself a contraction of ‘propanol’ and ‘adrenaline’.
