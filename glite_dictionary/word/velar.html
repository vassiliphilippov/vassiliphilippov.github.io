velar

1. NOUN

/ ˈvilər / (AmE), / ˈviːlə / (BrE)
A consonant sound produced with the back of the tongue touching or approaching the soft palate (velum).
consonant made with tongue near soft palate

Examples

The English sound represented by 'k' in 'cat' is a velar.
Many languages feature both voiced and voiceless velar consonants.
In phonetics class, we learned to distinguish between alveolar and velar sounds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: palatal - a consonant sound produced with the tongue near the hard palate
  • Degree Higher: uvular - a consonant produced with the back of the tongue near the uvula

Etymology

From Latin 'velum' (veil, curtain) referring to the soft palate.

2. ADJ

/ ˈviːlər / (AmE), / ˈviːlə / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced with the back of the tongue touching or approaching the soft palate, as in the sounds 'k' in 'cat' or 'ng' in 'sing'.
relating to the soft palate (in speech)

Examples

The English 'k' is a classic example of a velar consonant.
Many languages feature both voiced and voiceless velar sounds.
Children often substitute alveolar sounds for velar ones when learning to speak.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: palatal - produced with the body of the tongue near the hard palate
  • Degree Higher: uvular - produced with the back of the tongue near the uvula

Etymology

From Latin 'velum' (veil, soft palate) + '-ar'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈviːlər / (AmE), / ˈviːlə / (BrE)
relating to the velum, especially the soft palate or the part of the mouth forming velar sounds in speech
relating to the velum (soft palate)

Examples

The English 'k' is a velar consonant, pronounced with the back of the tongue against the soft palate.
A velar fold can be observed in some anatomical conditions of the mouth.
Linguists distinguish velar sounds from other types of consonants based on where they are articulated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palatal - of or relating to the palate, especially in speech sounds

Etymology

From Latin 'velum' (veil, curtain), referring to the soft palate.
