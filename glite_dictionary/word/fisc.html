fisc

1. NOUN

/ fɪsk / (AmE), / fɪsk / (BrE)
the treasury of a state, especially of a kingdom or empire; originally, the public treasury of ancient Rome or the emperor's private purse
state treasury; ancient Roman financial term

Examples

The Roman fisc played a crucial role in maintaining the empire's finances.
Taxes collected from the provinces were deposited in the fisc.
The king's personal fortune was distinct from the resources of the fisc.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treasury - the funds or revenue of a state, institution, or society
  • Synonym: exchequer - the royal or national treasury in certain countries

Etymology

From Latin fiscus (basket, purse, treasury).
