Strauss

1. PROPN

/ straʊs / (AmE), / straʊs / (BrE)
Johann Strauss II (1825–1899), an Austrian composer known as the 'waltz king,' celebrated for his many famous waltzes.
Austrian composer, 'waltz king' (1825–1899)

Examples

Many consider Strauss to be the greatest composer of Viennese waltzes.
The concert featured several pieces by Strauss, including 'The Blue Danube.'
As a child, she learned to play the violin with the music of Strauss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Johann Strauss II - Austrian composer known for waltzes, the 'waltz king'

Etymology

German surname, from the German word 'Strauss' meaning 'bouquet' or 'ostrich.'
