unpatronized

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈpeɪtrəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈpætrənaɪzd / (BrE)
Having few or no customers, supporters, or visitors; not receiving patronage.
lacking customers or support

Examples

The once popular café now stands unpatronized after the new shopping mall opened nearby.
Her small bookstore remained largely unpatronized despite positive reviews in the local paper.
Many rural bus routes are unpatronized and face the risk of closure.

Synset Examples

a restaurant unpatronized by the elite

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patronized - having received support or custom

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' (not) to 'patronized', from 'patron', ultimately from Latin 'patronus' (protector, master).
