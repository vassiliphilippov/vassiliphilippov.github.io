cumbrous

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌm.brəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌm.brəs / (BrE)
Difficult to handle or use, especially because of large size, weight, or complexity.
hard to handle due to size or weight

Examples

The old wardrobe was too cumbrous to move without help.
She struggled in her cumbrous winter coat as she walked through the narrow door.
The cumbrous regulations made the process slower and more frustrating.

Synset Examples

a cumbersome piece of machinery
cumbrous protective clothing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cumbersome - difficult to handle or manage because of size or complexity

Etymology

From Middle English *cumbrous*, influenced by *cumber* (to hinder) and -ous.
