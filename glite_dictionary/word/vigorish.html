vigorish

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪɡərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡərɪʃ / (BrE)
a fee or percentage taken by a gambling operator, bookmaker, or criminal organization as commission on bets or profits
operator's commission on bets or profits
american

Examples

The casino takes a small vigorish from each bet placed at the table.
Bookmakers make their money by charging a vigorish on every wager.
He complained that the vigorish was too high for him to make a profit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rake - a commission taken by a gambling house

Etymology

Probably from Yiddish *vigorish*, from Russian *vyigrysh* meaning 'winnings'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪɡərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡərɪʃ / (BrE)
an excessive or unlawful rate of interest, especially as charged by loan sharks or illegal gambling operations
excessive or illegal interest rate

Examples

The loan shark charged a hefty vigorish on every borrowed dollar.
He couldn't pay back the vigorish and ended up in trouble with the mob.
Some illegal gambling rings make their profits by collecting the vigorish from losing bets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: usury - the practice of lending money at unreasonably high rates of interest
  • Degree Lower: interest - money paid regularly at a particular rate for the use of money lent

Etymology

From Yiddish *vigrish* or Russian *vyigrysh* (winnings), both ultimately from Russian *vyigrat'* (to win).
