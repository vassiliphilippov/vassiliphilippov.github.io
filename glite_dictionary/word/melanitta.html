Melanitta

1. NOUN

/ məˈlænɪtə / (AmE), / mɛˈlanɪtə / (BrE)
A genus of sea ducks known as scoters, typically found in northern coastal regions.
genus of sea ducks (scoters)

Examples

The genus Melanitta includes several widespread species of scoters found across the Northern Hemisphere.
Unlike many other waterfowl, most Melanitta species prefer saltwater habitats during the winter months.
Ornithologists recently observed a large flock of Melanitta near the Baltic Sea coast.

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Greek 'melas' (black) + 'netta' (duck).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈnɪtə / (AmE), / ˌmɛləˈnɪtə / (BrE)
A genus of sea ducks commonly known as scoters, found mainly in northern regions.
a genus of sea ducks (scoters)

Examples

The sea duck known as the black scoter belongs to the genus Melanitta.
Researchers studied breeding patterns in various Melanitta species along the Arctic coast.
Members of the genus Melanitta are typically found in northern marine environments.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek melas (black) + netta (duck), referring to the dark plumage of these birds.
