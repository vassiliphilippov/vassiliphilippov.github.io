Tertry

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɛrtri / (AmE), / ˈtɜːtri / (BrE)
A battle fought in France in 687 among the descendants of Clovis, significant in the history of the Frankish kingdoms.
historical battle in France, 687

Examples

The Battle of Tertry marked a turning point in the power struggle among the Frankish nobility.
Historians often cite Tertry as a key event leading to the rise of the Carolingian dynasty.
After Tertry, Pepin of Herstal consolidated much of his political power in the region.

Etymology

Named after the village of Tertry in northern France, where the battle took place.
