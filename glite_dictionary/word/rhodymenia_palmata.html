Rhodymenia palmata

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊ.dɪˈmiː.ni.ə pælˈmeɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌrəʊ.dɪˈmiː.nɪ.ə pɑːlˈmɑː.tə / (BrE)
a species of coarse, edible red seaweed found in cold northern waters, commonly consumed as a food ingredient
edible red seaweed species

Examples

Rhodymenia palmata is harvested from rocky shores and is prized in certain cuisines for its savory flavor.
The nutritional value of Rhodymenia palmata makes it a popular addition to salads and soups in coastal regions.
Researchers are studying Rhodymenia palmata for its potential health benefits due to its rich mineral content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dulse - common name for Rhodymenia palmata, an edible red seaweed

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Rhodymenia' from Greek 'rhodon' (rose) + 'mēnion' (membrane); species 'palmata' meaning 'hand-shaped' in Latin.
