Mirabilis californica

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈræbɪlɪs ˌkælɪˈfɔrnɪkə / (AmE), / mɪˈræbɪlɪs ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkə / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant native to California, commonly known as California four o'clock, with purple-red flowers.
a plant species with purple-red flowers, native to California

Examples

The vibrant purple-red blooms of Mirabilis californica appear in the late afternoon.
Gardeners in California often plant Mirabilis californica for its colorful flowers and drought tolerance.
During our hike, we spotted patches of Mirabilis californica growing by the roadside.

Etymology

From Latin 'mirabilis' meaning 'wonderful' and 'californica' meaning 'of California'.
