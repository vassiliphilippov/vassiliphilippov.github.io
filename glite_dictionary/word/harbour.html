harbour

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːrbər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːbə / (BrE)
A sheltered area of water next to the coast where ships can anchor safely, especially for loading or unloading cargo.
sheltered port for ships

Examples

The ship waited outside the harbour for permission to dock.
Fishing boats crowded the small harbour after the storm.
The ancient harbour was once a vital trading center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: port - a town or city with a harbor where ships load or unload, especially one where customs officers are stationed
  • Synonym: anchorage - a place where vessels anchor
  • Antonym: open sea - the part of the sea that is far from land or shelter

Etymology

From Old English *herebeorg* (shelter, lodging) via Old Norse *herbergi* (lodging).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrbɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːbə / (BrE)
A place of refuge, comfort, or security, especially where someone feels protected or safe.
refuge or place of safety
british

Examples

For many, the church became a harbour from the storms of life.
In times of trouble, her family home was her harbour.
He sought a harbour where he could rest and recover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refuge - a place providing shelter or protection from danger
  • Synonym: sanctuary - a safe place, especially one of refuge or protection
  • Antonym: danger - exposure or vulnerability to harm

Etymology

From Old English 'herebeorg' (shelter, lodging), from Germanic roots.

3. VERB

/ ˈhɑːrbər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːbə / (BrE)
to secretly provide shelter or refuge to someone, especially someone who is wanted by the authorities or is in trouble with the law
secretly shelter (esp. fugitives or criminals)

Examples

Anyone found to harbour a fugitive will be prosecuted under the law.
She was accused of having harboured the escaped prisoner in her basement for weeks.
It's a crime to harbour anyone who is running from the police.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conceal - to keep something hidden or secret
  • Antonym: to betray - to reveal or expose information about someone, often to authorities

Etymology

From Old English *herebeorgian* (to shelter), related to the noun 'harbour'.
