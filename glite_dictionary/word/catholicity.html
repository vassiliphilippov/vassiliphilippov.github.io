catholicity

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæθəˈlɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌkæθəˈlɪsəti / (BrE)
the beliefs, practices, and traditions of the Catholic Church
beliefs and practices of the Catholic Church

Examples

The catholicity of the medieval church influenced art, culture, and politics throughout Europe.
His study focused on the catholicity expressed in early Christian writings.
Many scholars have debated the exact nature of catholicity in different regions of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Catholicism - the faith, practices, and doctrine of the Catholic Church

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'catholicitas', from Ancient Greek 'katholikos' (universal).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæθəˈlɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌkæθəˈlɪsəti / (BrE)
the quality of being universal or comprehensive in scope; the state of extending to or being present everywhere
universality; comprehensive scope

Examples

The catholicity of his interests made him knowledgeable in a wide range of subjects.
Her proposal was praised for its catholicity, as it included ideas from every department.
The catholicity of the language ensures that it is spoken around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: universality - the quality of being universal; existing everywhere

Etymology

From Greek katholikotēs (universality), via Late Latin catholicitas, from 'katholikos' meaning 'universal'.
