cyclostyle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪkləˌstaɪl / (AmE), / ˈsɪkləʊstaɪl / (BrE)
a device consisting of a writing implement with a small toothed wheel, used to cut holes in a stencil for duplicating documents
duplicating device for cutting stencils

Examples

The secretary used a cyclostyle to prepare stencils for the newsletter.
Before the advent of modern photocopiers, offices often relied on the cyclostyle for document duplication.
He demonstrated how the cyclostyle made rapid copies by cutting fine holes in wax paper.

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklos' (circle, wheel) and Latin 'stylus' (stylus).

2. VERB

/ ˈsaɪkləˌstaɪl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪkləʊstaɪl / (BrE)
to copy or print text using a cyclostyle, a device with small toothed wheels for making stencils
duplicate using a cyclostyle device
archaic

Examples

The secretary cyclostyled the agenda for the upcoming meeting and distributed copies to everyone.
All the pamphlets were cyclostyled in the office before the advent of modern copiers.
He spent the afternoon cyclostyling newsletters for the local club.

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklos' (circle) + 'stylos' (pillar, stylus); originally a brand name.
