feng shui

1. NOUN

/ fʌŋ ˈʃweɪ, ˈfɛŋ ˈʃuːi / (AmE), / fʌŋ ˈʃweɪ, ˈfɛŋ ˈʃuːi / (BrE)
A Chinese system of laws governing the arrangement and orientation of space to achieve harmony with the environment, based on principles of yin and yang and the flow of energy (qi).
Chinese system for arranging space harmoniously

Examples

Emily hired a consultant to improve the feng shui of her living room.
According to feng shui, the placement of furniture can affect your well-being.
Many office buildings in Asia are designed with feng shui principles in mind.

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese 風水 (fēng shuǐ), literally 'wind-water'.

2. NOUN

/ fʌŋ ˈʃweɪ / (AmE), / fʌŋ ˈʃweɪ / (BrE)
A Chinese system of arranging buildings and the objects within them in a way that is thought to harmonize individuals with their environment.
Chinese practice for harmonious arrangement of space

Examples

Many people hire a consultant to ensure their office has good feng shui.
The placement of the door and windows can affect the feng shui of a room.
She believes that improving the feng shui of her home will bring her more peace.

Synset Examples

Creating a calm space requires good feng shui principles.

They consulted an expert for feng shui in their home.

Etymology

From Chinese, literally 'wind-water'.
