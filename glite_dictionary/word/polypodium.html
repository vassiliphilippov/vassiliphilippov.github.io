Polypodium

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːlɪˈpoʊdiəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈpəʊdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of ferns in the family Polypodiaceae, characterized by rounded, exposed sori on the undersides of the fronds.
genus of ferns with naked sori

Examples

The leaves of Polypodium are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
Several species in the Polypodium genus are popular as ornamental plants.
A Polypodium fern thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments.

Etymology

From Greek polypodion, meaning 'many-footed', referring to the creeping rhizome of the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊliˈpoʊdiəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈpəʊdiəm / (BrE)
a genus of ferns in the family Polypodiaceae, characterized by rounded, naked sori on the underside of the fronds.
a genus of ferns with rounded naked sori

Examples

Several species within the genus Polypodium are commonly found growing on mossy rocks.
The Polypodium genus is notable for its simple, undivided fern fronds.
Botanists identified the unusual fern as belonging to Polypodium after examining its sori.

Etymology

From Greek polypous (many-footed), referring to the many root-like structures.
