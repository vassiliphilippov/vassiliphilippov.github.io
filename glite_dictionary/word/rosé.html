rosé

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈzeɪ / (AmE), / rəʊˈzeɪ / (BrE)
a light pink wine made from red grapes, with the skins removed early during fermentation
a light pink wine

Examples

We shared a bottle of rosé with lunch by the beach.
The restaurant's wine list includes several varieties of rosé from Provence.
She prefers rosé over red or white wine during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: red wine - wine made from dark-colored grape varieties
  • Degree Higher: white wine - wine made without skin contact from light-colored grapes

Etymology

From French 'rosé', past participle of 'roser' (to make pink), from 'rose' (pink).
