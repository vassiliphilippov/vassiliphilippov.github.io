Oreopteris

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌriːˈɑptərɪs / (AmE), / ɒˌriːˈɒptərɪs / (BrE)
a genus of ferns comprising three species, formerly classified under Dryopteris or Thelypteris
genus of ferns

Examples

The species Oreopteris limbosperma is commonly found in moist woodland habitats.
Botanists reclassified some ferns to the genus Oreopteris based on recent genetic studies.
I took a photograph of Oreopteris during our hike in the Scottish Highlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dryopteris - a large genus of wood ferns
  • Synonym: Thelypteris - a genus of terrestrial ferns

Etymology

From Greek 'oros' (mountain) + 'pteris' (fern).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɔːriˈɒptərɪs / (AmE), / ˌɔːriˈɒptərɪs / (BrE)
a genus of ferns comprising three species formerly classified under the genera Dryopteris or Thelypteris
genus of ferns (botany)

Examples

The Oreopteris genus includes several ferns found in temperate regions.
Botanists recently reclassified certain species from Dryopteris to Oreopteris.
You can easily recognize Oreopteris by its finely divided fronds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dryopteris - a genus of ferns commonly called wood ferns
  • Synonym: Thelypteris - a genus of ferns commonly called marsh ferns

Etymology

From Greek 'oros' (mountain) + 'pteris' (fern), referring to a mountain fern.
