Scarabaeus sacer

1. NOUN

/ skəˌræbiˈiː.əs ˈseɪ.sər / (AmE), / skəˌræbiˈeɪ.əs ˈseɪ.sə / (BrE)
A species of scarab beetle regarded as sacred by the ancient Egyptians, often associated with religion and symbolism.
scarab beetle sacred in ancient Egypt

Examples

The tomb was adorned with carvings of the Scarabaeus sacer as a symbol of rebirth.
Ancient Egyptians believed the Scarabaeus sacer represented the sun god Ra.
A gold amulet depicting the Scarabaeus sacer was found in the pharaoh's burial site.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning ‘sacred beetle.’
