miso

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmiːsəʊ / (BrE)
a thick, salty paste made from fermented soybeans and sometimes barley or rice, used in Japanese cooking
fermented soybean paste (Japanese cuisine)

Examples

She always adds a spoonful of miso to her soup for extra flavor.
Many vegan recipes use miso as a substitute for cheese or broth.
The chef demonstrated how to make traditional Japanese miso soup.

Etymology

From Japanese 味噌 (miso), meaning ‘fermented bean paste’.
