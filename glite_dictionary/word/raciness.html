raciness

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪsi nəs / (AmE), / ˈreɪsɪ nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being lively, entertaining, and slightly indecent or suggestive, especially in language or humor
lively, slightly indecent quality

Examples

The raciness of the comedian's jokes made some people uncomfortable while others laughed out loud.
This novel is famous for the raciness of its witty dialogue and daring themes.
Certain films attract audiences specifically for the raciness of their content and humor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bawdiness - indecent or lewd humor or language
  • Antonym: innocuousness - the quality of being harmless or inoffensive

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪsi nəs / (AmE), / ˈreɪsi nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having a strong, distinctive, and stimulating smell or taste
strong, distinctive smell or taste

Examples

The raciness of the wine was apparent with every sip.
He appreciated the raciness of the aged cheese, which filled the room with its aroma.
The sauce adds a distinct raciness to the otherwise mild dish.

Synset Examples

the pungency of mustard
the sulfurous bite of garlic
the sharpness of strange spices
the raciness of the wine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pungency - the quality of having a strong, sharp smell or taste
  • Antonym: blandness - the quality of having little or no flavor

Etymology

From 'racy' + '-ness', where 'racy' originally referred to wine having the characteristic flavor of the soil.
