cloven

1. ADJ

/ ˈkloʊ.vən / (AmE), / ˈkləʊ.vən / (BrE)
(of a hoof) split or divided into two parts.
split in two (of hooves)

Examples

The goat's cloven hooves sank into the soft earth.
Many animals depicted in mythology are described as having cloven feet.
A deer leaves distinctive cloven tracks in the fresh snow.

Synset Examples

bisulcate hoof

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleft - split or divided; often used of things that are split in two

Etymology

Past participle of 'cleave', from Old English 'cleofan' (to split).
