mob

1. NOUN

/ mɑb / (AmE), / mɒb / (BrE)
a large crowd of people, especially one that is disorderly or unruly
disorderly crowd

Examples

The angry mob surged towards the government building, shouting and waving banners.
A mob quickly gathered outside the store after rumors of a celebrity visit spread.
The police struggled to control the mob as tempers flared during the protest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowd - a large group of people gathered together, but not necessarily disorderly
  • Synonym: throng - a large densely packed crowd of people

Etymology

From Latin 'mobile vulgus' (the movable/common people), via Italian and English contractions.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08201518-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ mɑb / (AmE), / mɒb / (BrE)
an organized group of criminals; a loose association of gangsters in charge of illegal activities
organized criminal group

Examples

The mob controlled most of the illegal gambling in the city.
He was suspected of having connections to the mob and its leaders.
The police staged a crackdown on the local mob last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gang - an organized group of criminals
  • Synonym: syndicate - an organization formed to carry out criminal activities

Etymology

From Latin 'mobile vulgus' (the movable/common crowd); the criminal sense developed in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08263223-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ mɑb / (AmE), / mɒb / (BrE)
(of people) to move in a large crowd, pressing closely together, often in a way that causes disorder or makes movement difficult.
press together in a crowd

Examples

Fans began to mob the entrance when the band arrived at the venue.
During the sale, customers mobbed the store, hoping to find bargains.
Security struggled to control the crowd as people mobbed around the stage.

Synset Examples

The crowd packed the auditorium

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pack - to fill a space tightly, as with people or things
  • Synonym: crowd - to gather tightly or press closely together

Etymology

From Latin 'mobile vulgus' (the movable common people), later shortened to 'mob' in English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02068059-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ mɑb / (AmE), / mɒb / (BrE)
a group of people involved in organized criminal activity; a criminal gang or syndicate
organized group of criminals

Examples

The police launched a major operation to bring down the notorious mob.
He owed money to the mob and was afraid for his life.
Several members of the mob were finally convicted and sent to prison.

Synset Examples

police tried to break up the gang
a pack of thieves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gang - a group of people, especially criminals, who work together
  • Synonym: syndicate - a group of individuals or organizations combined to promote a common interest, especially in crime

Etymology

From Latin 'mobile vulgus' (the movable/common people), later used in English for criminal groups.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08260983-n
Synset POS: n
