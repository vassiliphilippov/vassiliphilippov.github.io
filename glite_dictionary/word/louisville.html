Louisville

1. PROPN

/ ˈluːɪsvɪl, ˈluːəvəl / (AmE), / ˈluːɪsvɪl, ˈluːəvɪl / (BrE)
the largest city in Kentucky, located in north-central Kentucky on the Ohio River, known for hosting the Kentucky Derby
largest city in Kentucky, USA

Examples

Every May, Louisville draws visitors from around the world for the Kentucky Derby.
She moved to Louisville after graduating from college to start her new job.
The skyline of Louisville is especially beautiful at sunset along the Ohio River.

Etymology

Named after King Louis XVI of France.
