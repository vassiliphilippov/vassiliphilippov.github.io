retrieval

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtrivəl / (AmE), / rɪˈtriːvəl / (BrE)
(computer science) The operation of obtaining information from a computer's memory or storage.
accessing information from computer memory

Examples

Fast data retrieval is essential for modern search engines.
The software optimizes retrieval of large image files.
Errors can occur during the retrieval of records from the database.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: access - the act of retrieving or obtaining data

Etymology

From retrieve + -al, ultimately from Old French 'retrouver' (to find again).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈtrivəl / (AmE), / rɪˈtriːvəl / (BrE)
the act of regaining or recovering something lost or in danger of being lost
the act of getting back something lost

Examples

The retrieval of the stolen painting made headlines across the city.
After hours of searching, his retrieval of the missing keys was a relief.
Quick data retrieval is crucial for large companies managing millions of files.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recovery - the process of getting something back or regaining possession
  • Antonym: loss - the state of no longer having something or someone

Etymology

From Old French *retrouver* (to find again), from re- (again) + trouver (to find).

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈtrivəl / (AmE), / rɪˈtriːvəl / (BrE)
the process of recalling or accessing information stored in one's memory
recall of information from memory

Examples

In psychology, retrieval is essential for long-term learning and effective study strategies.
Sometimes stress can interfere with the retrieval of important childhood memories.
The patient's difficulty with word retrieval was noted during the neuropsychological evaluation.

Synset Examples

my retrieval of people's names is very poor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recall - the act of remembering information
  • Antonym: forgetting - the inability to remember information

Etymology

From retrieve + -al, ultimately from Old French 'retrever' (to find again).
