basso profundo

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːsoʊ prəˈfundoʊ / (AmE), / ˌbæsəʊ prəˈfʊndəʊ / (BrE)
A very deep bass male singing voice, the lowest range of the bass voice types.
very deep bass singing voice

Examples

The opera's villain was sung by a renowned basso profundo who brought gravitas to the role.
Few voices can match the resonance of a true basso profundo in the choral performance.
In the choir, Peter stood out as the only basso profundo capable of reaching the lowest notes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bass - a male singing voice of the lowest range

Etymology

From Italian, literally meaning 'deep bass.'
