Phyllitis

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of ferns in the family Polypodiaceae, sometimes included in the genus Asplenium in certain classifications.
genus of ferns

Examples

The Phyllitis genus is known for its simple, unlobed fronds that resemble long, narrow leaves.
Botanists debated whether to classify these ferns under Phyllitis or Asplenium.
The hart's-tongue fern, once listed as Phyllitis scolopendrium, is commonly found in shady woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Asplenium - a related genus of ferns often grouped with Phyllitis

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the leaf-like shape of the fronds.

2. NOUN

/ fɪˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪtɪs / (BrE)
a genus of ferns in the family Polypodiaceae, sometimes classified as part of genus Asplenium.
a genus of ferns

Examples

The Phyllitis genus is known for its distinctive simple fronds that resemble ribbons.
Botanists sometimes classify Phyllitis under the broader genus Asplenium.
A common garden variety, Phyllitis scolopendrium, is often called hart's-tongue fern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Asplenium - a related or sometimes inclusive genus of ferns

Etymology

From Greek phyllon (leaf), referring to the plant's leaf shape.
