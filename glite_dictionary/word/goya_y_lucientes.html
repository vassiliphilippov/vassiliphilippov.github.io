Goya y Lucientes

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡɔɪə i luˈsjɛntes / (AmE), / ˈɡɔɪə i luˈsjɛntɛs / (BrE)
(1746–1828) Spanish painter renowned for his portraits and satirical works, influential in both classical and modern art.
Spanish painter (1746–1828) known for portraits and satire

Examples

Many art historians regard Goya y Lucientes as the bridge between the Old Masters and modern art.
The dark, haunting series by Goya y Lucientes reflected the turbulence of his time.
Museums around the world exhibit works created by Goya y Lucientes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Francisco Goya - Spanish painter famed for portraits, satire, and historical scenes

Etymology

Full name of Francisco Goya, from Spanish naming tradition.
