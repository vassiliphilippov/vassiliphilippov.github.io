zygomorphous

1. ADJ

/ zaɪɡəˈmɔr.fəs / (AmE), / zaɪɡəˈmɔː.fəs / (BrE)
(of a flower, organism, etc.) capable of being divided into symmetrical halves by only one longitudinal plane passing through its axis; bilaterally symmetrical.
having bilateral symmetry (esp. in flowers)

Examples

The orchid's zygomorphous flowers can be split into mirror images along only one plane.
Many pea flowers are zygomorphous, which aids in specialized pollination by insects.
Unlike radially symmetrical daisies, snapdragons are zygomorphous in structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: actinomorphous - capable of being divided into symmetrical halves by multiple planes; radially symmetrical

Etymology

From Greek zygon (yoke) + morphē (form, shape).
