ultramicroscopic

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌltrəˌmaɪkrəˈskɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˌmaɪkrəˈskɒpɪk / (BrE)
too small to be seen with an ordinary light microscope, visible only with an ultramicroscope or special equipment
extremely tiny; visible only with an ultramicroscope

Examples

The scientists studied ultramicroscopic particles suspended in the solution.
Only advanced imaging techniques can reveal ultramicroscopic features of some viruses.
The dust in the air contains ultramicroscopic fibers that are invisible to the naked eye.

Etymology

Formed from ultra- (beyond) + microscopic (visible with a microscope).
