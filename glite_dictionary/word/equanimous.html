equanimous

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈkwænəməs / (AmE), / ɪˈkwænɪməs / (BrE)
calm and composed, especially in difficult situations; not easily disturbed or upset
calm and composed under pressure

Examples

Despite the chaos around her, she remained equanimous and handled the crisis with grace.
It is important for a judge to be equanimous when delivering a verdict.
He gave his statement in an equanimous manner, showing no hint of anger or distress.

Synset Examples

the witness remained collected throughout the cross-examination
perfectly poised and sure of himself
more self-contained and more dependable than many of the early frontiersmen
strong and self-possessed in the face of trouble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or agitation
  • Antonym: agitated - feeling or appearing troubled or nervous

Etymology

From Latin 'aequanimus' (even-minded), from 'aequus' (even) + 'animus' (mind).
