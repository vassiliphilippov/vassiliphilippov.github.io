Mustela

1. NOUN

/ mʌˈstɛl.ə / (AmE), / mʌsˈtiː.lə / (BrE)
A genus of small carnivorous mammals in the family Mustelidae, including weasels, stoats, and related species.
genus of weasels and relatives

Examples

The genus Mustela includes animals such as weasels, ferrets, and polecats.
Researchers studied the behavior of several Mustela species in their natural habitats.
The Mustela genus is widespread across Europe, Asia, and North America.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Mustelidae - the mammal family to which Mustela belongs (weasels, otters, badgers, etc.)
  • Synonym: weasel - a small, slender carnivorous mammal often included in the genus Mustela

Etymology

From Latin *mustela* (weasel).
