impish

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪmpɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɪmpɪʃ / (BrE)
Playfully mischievous or naughty in a teasing or annoying way.
mischievously playful; naughty

Examples

She gave him an impish grin before darting off down the hallway.
The boy's impish comments kept his classmates laughing throughout the lesson.
With an impish twinkle in her eye, she hid her brother's shoes as a harmless prank.

Synset Examples

teasing and worrying with impish laughter
a wicked prank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischievous - causing or showing a fondness for causing trouble in a playful way
  • Antonym: angelic - innocent and virtuous (opposite in behavior)

Etymology

From 'imp', originally referring to a small devil or mischievous child.
