einsteinium

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪnˈstaɪniəm / (AmE), / ˌaɪnˈstiːniəm / (BrE)
a radioactive chemical element with the symbol Es and atomic number 99, produced artificially by bombarding plutonium with neutrons
radioactive chemical element, symbol Es

Examples

Scientists first discovered einsteinium in the debris of a hydrogen bomb explosion.
The element einsteinium is extremely rare and highly radioactive.
Researchers produced einsteinium in the laboratory by bombarding plutonium with neutrons.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: plutonium - a radioactive chemical element, symbol Pu, atomic number 94
  • Degree Higher: fermium - a radioactive chemical element, symbol Fm, atomic number 100

Etymology

Named after physicist Albert Einstein; discovered in debris from the first hydrogen bomb explosion in 1952.
