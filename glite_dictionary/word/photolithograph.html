photolithograph

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊˈlɪθəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˈlɪθəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a print made by using a photographic process to prepare the plate for lithography
print made by photographic lithography

Examples

The museum's collection includes a rare photolithograph of the original city map.
She purchased a beautiful photolithograph by a renowned modern artist at the gallery.
For the exhibition, he created a photolithograph capturing the city's historic architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lithograph - a print made using lithography, a method of printing from flat stone or metal plates

Etymology

Formed from 'photo-' (relating to light or photography) and 'lithograph' (from Greek 'lithos' meaning 'stone').
