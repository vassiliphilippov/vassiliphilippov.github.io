scraggy

1. ADJ

/ ˈskræɡi / (AmE), / ˈskræɡi / (BrE)
Very thin and bony in appearance; lean and gaunt.
very thin and bony

Examples

The stray dog looked scraggy after weeks without proper food.
She wore a loose dress to conceal her scraggy frame.
The old tree's scraggy branches reached out against the night sky.

Synset Examples

a child with skinny freckled legs
a long scrawny neck
pale bony hands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrawny - unattractively thin and bony
  • Synonym: skinny - very thin, especially in a way that is unattractive
  • Antonym: plump - having a pleasantly soft, rounded body

Etymology

Perhaps from English dialect scrag (thin or scrawny person or animal).

2. ADJ

/ ˈskræɡi / (AmE), / ˈskræɡi / (BrE)
having a rough, uneven, or jagged surface or outline; rugged or irregular in appearance
rough or uneven in surface or outline

Examples

We hiked along the scraggy cliffs overlooking the ocean.
The old tree had scraggy branches reaching in every direction.
After the landslide, the mountain trail became much more scraggy and difficult to climb.

Synset Examples

the jagged outline of the crags
scraggy cliffs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jagged - having sharp projections or a harsh outline
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface

Etymology

From "scrag" (thin or bony part), + -y, forming adjectives.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00915402-s
Synset POS: s
