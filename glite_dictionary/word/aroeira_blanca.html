aroeira blanca

1. NOUN

/ əˌroʊˈeɪrə ˈblæŋkə / (AmE), / əˌrəʊˈeɪrə ˈblaŋkə / (BrE)
a small resinous tree or shrub native to Brazil, known for its aromatic resin and sometimes used in traditional medicine.
resinous Brazilian tree or shrub

Examples

The villagers harvest resin from the aroeira blanca for use in traditional remedies.
Many birds nest in the branches of the aroeira blanca during the spring.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of aroeira blanca extract.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'aroeira' (tree of the Anacardiaceae family) and Spanish 'blanca' (white), referring to the tree's light-colored bark.
