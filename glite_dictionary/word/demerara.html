Demerara

1. PROPN

/ ˌdɛm.əˈrɑː.rə / (AmE), / ˌdɛm.əˈrɑː.rə / (BrE)
a former Dutch colony on the northern coast of South America, now part of Guyana.
former Dutch colony in South America

Examples

Sugar from Demerara was highly prized in eighteenth-century Europe.
The Demerara region is now incorporated into modern-day Guyana.
Trade routes once connected Demerara to the Caribbean and Europe.

Etymology

Named after the Demerara River, from indigenous Arawak language.

2. PROPN

/ ˌdɛməˈrɛr.ə / (AmE), / ˌdɛm.əˈrɑː.rə / (BrE)
A river in northern Guyana that flows northward into the Atlantic Ocean.
river in northern Guyana

Examples

The Demerara River is a vital waterway for transporting goods in Guyana.
They sailed up the Demerara to visit small riverside villages.
Many species of birds can be found along the Demerara's banks.

Etymology

From the Dutch 'Demerary', derived from indigenous Arawak or Carib language.
