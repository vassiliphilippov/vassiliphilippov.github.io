demerara

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛm.əˈrɛr.ə / (AmE), / ˌdɛm.əˈrɑː.rə / (BrE)
a light brown, partially refined cane sugar with large, golden crystals, originally from Guyana
type of light brown cane sugar

Examples

She sprinkled demerara over her oatmeal for extra crunch and sweetness.
The recipe calls for two tablespoons of demerara sugar to create a caramelized topping.
At the café, they offer both white and demerara sugar for your coffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbinado - a type of light brown cane sugar with large crystals
  • Degree Higher: muscovado - a type of unrefined, dark brown cane sugar

Etymology

Named after the Demerara region of Guyana, where this type of sugar was first produced.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛm.əˈrɑː.rə / (AmE), / ˌdɛm.əˈrɑː.rə / (BrE)
a type of dark rum originally produced in Guyana, known for its rich flavor
dark rum from Guyana

Examples

He ordered a glass of demerara after dinner to accompany his dessert.
This cocktail is made with aged demerara for a deeper, smokier taste.
They import authentic demerara directly from Guyana for their bar.

Etymology

From Demerara, the name of a region and river in Guyana, where this rum was originally produced.

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɛmɚˈrɑrə / (AmE), / ˌdɛmɪˈrɑːrə / (BrE)
a light brown raw cane sugar with large, crunchy crystals, originally produced in Guyana
light brown raw cane sugar

Examples

Sprinkle some demerara on your oatmeal for a sweet, crunchy topping.
The recipe calls for brown sugar, but I used demerara instead.
He likes his coffee strong with a spoonful of demerara.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbinado - another type of raw cane sugar with large crystals

Etymology

Named after the Demerara region in Guyana, where the sugar was first produced.
