cusped

1. ADJ

/ kʌspt / (AmE), / kʌspt / (BrE)
having cusps or pointed projections
having cusps or points

Examples

The cathedral's cusped arches are a striking feature of Gothic architecture.
The leaf is cusped at the tip, making it easy to distinguish from other species.
Many mollusk shells are cusped, creating a sharp edge for protection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cusp - a pointed end where two curves meet

Etymology

From 'cusp' + -ed, where 'cusp' is from Latin 'cuspis' (point).
