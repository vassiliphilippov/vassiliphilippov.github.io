albitic

1. ADJ

/ ælˈbɪtɪk / (AmE), / ælˈbɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or containing albite, a type of feldspar mineral
relating to albite (feldspar mineral)

Examples

The rock displays albitic textures, indicating it formed in a sodium-rich environment.
Geologists identified an albitic vein running through the granite sample.
The mineral composition is predominantly albitic feldspar with minor quartz.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albite - a white or colorless feldspar mineral

Etymology

From albite (a mineral name, from Latin 'albus' meaning white) + -ic (forming adjectives).
