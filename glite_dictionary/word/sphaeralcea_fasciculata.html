Sphaeralcea fasciculata

1. NOUN

/ sfɛrˈælsiə ˌfæsɪkjʊˈleɪtə / (AmE), / sfɪəˈrælsiə ˌfæsɪkjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A shrub native to the coastal ranges of California and Baja California, characterized by hairy branches and spikes of numerous mauve flowers; sometimes classified under the genus Sphaeralcea.
California shrub with mauve flowers

Examples

The botanical garden features a rare Sphaeralcea fasciculata in its desert plant section.
Researchers studied the drought resistance of Sphaeralcea fasciculata in Baja California.
The spikes of mauve flowers help Sphaeralcea fasciculata stand out among other native shrubs.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Sphaeralcea (from Greek *sphaira* 'sphere' + *alkea* 'mallow'), and Latin *fasciculata* meaning 'bundled'.
