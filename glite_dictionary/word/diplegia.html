diplegia

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpliːdʒə / (AmE), / dɪˈpliːdʒɪə / (BrE)
Paralysis affecting corresponding parts on both sides of the body, typically the legs.
paralysis on both sides of the body
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with diplegia, causing weakness in both legs.
Physical therapy is often recommended for patients living with diplegia.
Unlike hemiplegia, diplegia affects both sides of the body equally.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: paraplegia - paralysis of the legs and lower body
  • Degree Higher: quadriplegia - paralysis of all four limbs

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) + 'plēgē' (stroke).
