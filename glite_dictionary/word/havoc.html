havoc

1. NOUN

/ ˈhævək / (AmE), / ˈhævək / (BrE)
a situation of great destruction, confusion, or disorder, often caused by violent or unnecessary disturbance
violent destruction or disorder

Examples

The storm caused havoc in the city, leaving many buildings damaged.
The new policy created havoc among employees, who were confused by the changes.
If you let the kids run wild, they'll wreak havoc on the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaos - complete disorder and confusion
  • Antonym: order - an organized or orderly state

Etymology

From Old French 'havot' (devastation), of Germanic origin.
