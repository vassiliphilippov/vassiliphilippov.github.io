tumefaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌtuːməˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌtjuːmɪˈfækʃən / (BrE)
the process of swelling or becoming enlarged, especially in tissue, due to the accumulation of fluid
swelling of tissue from fluid buildup
medical

Examples

The patient developed a noticeable tumefaction in her lower leg after the injury.
The area around the wound exhibited signs of tumefaction and redness.
Doctors treated the tumefaction with medication to reduce the swelling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edema - an abnormal accumulation of fluid in tissues causing swelling

Etymology

From Latin tumefacere (to cause to swell), from tumere (to swell) + facere (to make).
