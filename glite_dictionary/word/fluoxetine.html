fluoxetine

1. NOUN

/ fluˈɑksətiːn / (AmE), / fluːˈɒksətiːn / (BrE)
a synthetic antidepressant of the selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI) class, used to treat depression and related conditions
antidepressant medication (SSRI)
medical

Examples

Many patients reported an improvement in their symptoms after starting fluoxetine.
The doctor explained that fluoxetine works by affecting serotonin levels in the brain.
Some common side effects of fluoxetine include nausea and difficulty sleeping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Prozac - brand name for fluoxetine
  • Synonym: sertraline - another selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI) antidepressant

Etymology

From fluoro- (referring to the fluorine atom in its structure) + ox(yg) + (am)ine, technical chemical formation.
