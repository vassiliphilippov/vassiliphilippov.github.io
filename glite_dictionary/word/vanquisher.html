vanquisher

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæŋkwɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈvaŋkwɪʃə / (BrE)
a person who defeats an enemy or opponent, especially by force; a conqueror.
one who defeats or conquers

Examples

The vanquisher returned to the city to celebrate his triumph with the people.
Historians often debate whether a vanquisher should be admired or feared.
In the legend, the vanquisher showed mercy to his defeated foes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conqueror - a person who conquers a place or people
  • Synonym: victor - a person who defeats an opponent; winner
  • Antonym: loser - a person who is defeated

Etymology

From Old French 'vainqueur' (conqueror), from 'vainquir' (to conquer), from Latin 'vinco' (I conquer).
