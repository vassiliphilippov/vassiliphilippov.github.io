ethchlorvynol

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛθklɔːrˈvaɪnɒl / (AmE), / ˌɛθklɔːˈvɪnɒl / (BrE)
a mild sedative-hypnotic drug used to treat insomnia, known by the trade name Placidyl
a sedative-hypnotic drug (Placidyl)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed ethchlorvynol to help manage the patient's insomnia.
Long-term use of ethchlorvynol is not recommended due to its potential for dependence.
Before safer alternatives were developed, ethchlorvynol was commonly used as a sleep aid.

Etymology

From 'ethyl', 'chlor' (for chlorine), and 'vinyl', indicating chemical structure.
