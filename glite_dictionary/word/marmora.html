Marmora

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑːrmərə / (AmE), / ˈmɑːmərə / (BrE)
an inland sea in northwestern Turkey, lying between the Black Sea and the Aegean Sea, connected by the Bosporus and the Dardanelles
inland sea in northwestern Turkey

Examples

The Sea of Marmora separates the Asian and European parts of Turkey.
Several ferries cross the Marmora daily, connecting Istanbul with other cities.
The Bosporus connects the Black Sea to the Marmora.

Etymology

From Latin 'Marmora' from Ancient Greek 'Μαρμαράς' (Marmarás), meaning 'marble', as the area was known for its marble quarries.
