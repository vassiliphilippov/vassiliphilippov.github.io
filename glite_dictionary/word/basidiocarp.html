basidiocarp

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsɪdioʊˌkɑrp / (AmE), / bəˈsɪdiəʊˌkɑːp / (BrE)
The fruiting body of a basidiomycete fungus, where spores are produced on specialized cells.
fruiting body of a basidiomycete fungus
medical

Examples

The mushroom is the basidiocarp of the fungus, visible above ground during reproduction.
Mycologists collected several basidiocarps to study spore formation in woodlands.
A typical basidiocarp includes a stalk and cap, forming the familiar structure of many edible mushrooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sporocarp - a general fruiting body of a fungus

Etymology

From New Latin 'basidium' (a spore-bearing structure) + Greek 'karpos' (fruit).
