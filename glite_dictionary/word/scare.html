scare

1. NOUN

/ skɛr / (AmE), / skeə / (BrE)
a sudden period of widespread fear or anxiety about a possible danger or problem
sudden mass fear about an anticipated event

Examples

There was a major scare about contaminated food last year.
A bomb scare caused all flights to be delayed at the airport.
The country went through a flu scare during the winter.

Synset Examples

panic in the stock market
a war scare
a bomb scare led them to evacuate the building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panic - a sudden overwhelming fear, often irrational
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ skɛr / (AmE), / skeə / (BrE)
a sudden short period of fear or anxiety caused by a particular event or situation
a sudden burst of fear

Examples

The news of the outbreak gave everyone a scare.
After the near accident, she needed a moment to recover from the scare.
There was a scare in the market after the rumors spread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fright - a sudden intense feeling of fear
  • Synonym: panic - a sudden, uncontrollable fear or anxiety
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07536676-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ skɛr / (AmE), / skɛə / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel frightened or afraid
cause fear in someone

Examples

The loud noise scared the children during the thunderstorm.
Don't scare your little brother with ghost stories before bed.
She scares easily, so be gentle when you approach her.

Synset Examples

The stranger who hangs around the building frightens me
Ghosts could never affright her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to frighten - to make someone feel afraid
  • Degree Higher: to terrify - to cause intense fear
  • Degree Lower: to startle - to surprise and frighten someone suddenly

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *skirra* (to frighten off, prevent).

Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ skɛr / (AmE), / skɛə / (BrE)
to cause someone to lose courage or confidence; to make someone feel afraid enough to stop trying to do something
cause to lose courage; daunt

Examples

The threat of rejection can scare some people away from applying.
They tried to scare the opposition into silence with harsh criticism.
The difficult exam might scare a few students off, but most are determined to try.

Synset Examples

dashed by the refusal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to daunt - to make someone feel intimidated or discouraged
  • Synonym: to intimidate - to frighten or overawe someone, often to make them do what one wants
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support or confidence to someone

Etymology

Perhaps from Old Norse *skirra* (to frighten), related to Old English *scerran* (to shun).
