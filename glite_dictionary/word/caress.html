caress

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛs / (AmE), / kəˈrɛs / (BrE)
a gentle and loving touch or stroke; an act of showing affection through gentle contact, sometimes used metaphorically.
gentle, affectionate touch or stroke

Examples

She welcomed him with a gentle caress on his cheek.
The soft caress of the breeze made her feel calm and peaceful.
His words were a caress to her troubled soul.

Synset Examples

he showered her with caresses
soft music was a fond caress
the caresses of the breeze played over his face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroke - a gentle touch in passing
  • Synonym: embrace - an act of holding someone closely in one's arms, especially as a sign of affection

Etymology

From French *caresse*, from Italian *carezza*, from *caro* (dear), ultimately from Latin *carus* (dear).

2. VERB

/ kəˈrɛs / (AmE), / kəˈrɛs / (BrE)
to touch or stroke someone or something gently in a loving or affectionate way
touch gently with affection

Examples

He gently caressed her hair as they watched the sunset together.
She caressed the old photograph, lost in memories of her childhood.
A warm breeze caressed his skin as he stood by the open window.

Synset Examples

He caressed her face
They fondled in the back seat of the taxi

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fondle - to handle gently or lovingly, often sexually
  • Synonym: stroke - to move one's hand with gentle pressure over a surface, typically repeatedly
  • Antonym: grab - to seize suddenly or roughly

Etymology

From Old French 'carresser', from 'caresse' (caress, embrace), from Italian 'carezza', from 'caro' (dear).
