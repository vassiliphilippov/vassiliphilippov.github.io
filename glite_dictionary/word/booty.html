booty

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːti / (AmE), / ˈbuːti / (BrE)
valuable goods or money, especially those taken by force or illegally, such as in war or by thieves.
stolen goods or loot

Examples

The pirates divided the booty among themselves after the raid.
The thieves escaped with a booty valued at over a million dollars.
Legend says the booty from the treasure ship is still hidden on the island.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loot - goods stolen or taken by force
  • Synonym: plunder - property taken by force, especially in war
  • Synonym: spoils - goods taken as prizes, especially in war

Etymology

From Middle Low German *bûte* (exchange, distribution; plunder), possibly via Dutch *buit* (booty, spoil).
