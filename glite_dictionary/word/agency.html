agency

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒənsi / (BrE)
the state or function of acting as the official and authorized representative of a person or organization
state of being an official representative

Examples

He was granted agency to negotiate the contract on behalf of the company.
Diplomats often act with agency for their governments in foreign countries.
The lawyer accepted agency for her client in all legal proceedings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: representation - the act of speaking or acting on behalf of someone

Etymology

From Medieval Latin agentia (the faculty of acting), from Latin agere (to do, act).

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒənsi / (BrE)
the capacity, condition, or state of acting or exerting power; the ability to make choices and to act independently.
capacity to act or exert power

Examples

In difficult situations, individuals may feel their agency is limited.
Social movements can restore a sense of agency to marginalized communities.
She exercised her agency by making her own decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power - the ability to do something or act in a particular way
  • Antonym: helplessness - the inability to act or exert power

3. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.dʒən.si / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.dʒən.si / (BrE)
A business or organization that provides a particular service, especially one that arranges or mediates transactions between other parties.
business or organization providing a service

Examples

She contacted an employment agency to help her find a job.
The travel agency arranged their entire itinerary for the holiday.
A real estate agency can help you buy or sell a house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firm - a business organization, especially one involving a partnership of two or more people
  • Synonym: company - an organization that sells goods or services in order to make money

Etymology

From Medieval Latin agentia (active operation), from Latin agens (doing), from agere (to do, act).
