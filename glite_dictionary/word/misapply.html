to misapply

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪs.əˈplaɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.əˈplaɪ / (BrE)
to use something in a way that is wrong, inappropriate, or not intended
use wrongly or inappropriately

Examples

If you misapply this rule, the results could be misleading.
Funds were misapplied during the construction project, leading to budget issues.
The principle was misapplied in this experiment, affecting the outcome.

Synset Examples

The words are misapplied in this context
You are misapplying the name of this religious group

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to apply - to put something into use; to make practical use of
  • Synonym: abuse - to use something for a bad purpose; misuse

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + apply, modeled after Latin-based compounds.
