simoom

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈmum / (AmE), / sɪˈmuːm / (BrE)
a hot, dry, and often dust-laden wind that blows across the deserts of Arabia and North Africa, sometimes causing suffocating conditions
hot desert wind of Arabia/North Africa

Examples

During their journey across the Sahara, a sudden simoom made travel nearly impossible.
The explorers took shelter to avoid the suffocating heat of the simoom.
Stories of the deadly simoom have passed down through generations in North African villages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sirocco - a hot, dry wind from North Africa affecting southern Europe

Etymology

From Arabic samūm (poison wind).
