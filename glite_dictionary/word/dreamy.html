dreamy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɹimi / (AmE), / ˈdɹiːmi / (BrE)
lacking energy, enthusiasm, or liveliness; appearing absent-minded or indifferent
lacking spirit or liveliness

Examples

She sat in a dreamy state, barely responding to the conversation around her.
His dreamy manner made him seem uninterested during the meeting.
The hot afternoon left everyone feeling dreamy and slow.

Synset Examples

a lackadaisical attempt
a languid mood
a languid wave of the hand
a hot languorous afternoon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: languid - lacking energy or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: energetic - full of energy and enthusiasm

2. ADJ

/ ˈdriːmi / (AmE), / ˈdriːmi / (BrE)
having a gentle, vague, or absent-minded quality, as if lost in dreams or thoughts; showing or suggesting a dream-like mood or state
having a dream-like or absent-minded quality

Examples

She stared out the window with a dreamy expression, lost in her thoughts.
The music had a dreamy quality that made everyone feel relaxed.
He became dreamy and distracted during the long, boring meeting.

Synset Examples

a woolgathering moment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absent-minded - tending to be inattentive to one's surroundings
  • Antonym: alert - fully aware and attentive

Etymology

From 'dream' + '-y', meaning 'like a dream'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00166847-s
Synset POS: s
