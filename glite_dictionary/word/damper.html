damper

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈdæmpə / (BrE)
a device that reduces the amplitude of oscillations in electronic, mechanical, acoustical, or aerodynamic systems.
device that reduces oscillations

Examples

The car's shock absorber acts as a damper to reduce spring oscillations.
An acoustic damper helps prevent unwanted vibrations in the piano's soundboard.
Engineers installed a special damper to minimize vibrations in the high-rise building during strong winds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dampener - a device that reduces vibration or oscillation

2. NOUN

/ ˈdæmpər / (AmE), / ˈdæmpə / (BrE)
something that restrains, discourages, or dulls enthusiasm or enjoyment; a depressing or limiting influence.
something that restrains or lessens enthusiasm

Examples

The sudden power outage cast a damper on the party atmosphere.
His negative comments put a damper on the team's excitement.
Heavy rain could throw a damper on tomorrow's outdoor concert.

Synset Examples

rain put a damper on our picnic plans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restraint - something that limits or restricts action or feeling
  • Synonym: inhibition - a feeling that makes one self-conscious and unable to act in a relaxed way
  • Antonym: encouragement - the act of giving support, confidence, or hope

3. NOUN

/ ˈdæmpər / (AmE), / ˈdæmpə / (BrE)
a movable metal plate in a stove, furnace, or chimney used to control the flow of air or smoke
adjustable plate regulating airflow in a stove or chimney

Examples

You should close the damper to keep warm air from escaping up the chimney.
She adjusted the damper so the fire would burn more evenly.
If the damper is left open, cold air can come down the flue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flue - a duct for smoke and waste gases produced by a fire, a gas heater, or other fuel-burning installation

Etymology

From damp (meaning to suppress or check) + -er (agent noun suffix).
