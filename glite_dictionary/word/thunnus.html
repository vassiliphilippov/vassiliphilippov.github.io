Thunnus

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌn.əs / (AmE), / ˈθʌn.əs / (BrE)
A genus of large, warm-blooded marine fish commonly known as tunas.
genus of tuna fishes

Examples

The Atlantic bluefin belongs to the genus Thunnus, which includes several commercially important species.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of Thunnus to understand their breeding behavior.
Many sushi dishes use fish from the Thunnus genus due to their rich flavor and texture.

Etymology

From Latin *thunnus* (tuna), ultimately from Ancient Greek θύννος (thýnnos).
