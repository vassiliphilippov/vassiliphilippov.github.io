crotchet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑː.tʃɪt / (AmE), / ˈkrɒtʃɪt / (BrE)
a musical note with the time value of a quarter of a whole note; called a 'quarter note' in American English
quarter note (musical)
british

Examples

The pianist played each crotchet with precise rhythm during the practice session.
In the score, the melody consists mainly of crotchets and minims.
British music notation uses the term crotchet instead of quarter note.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarter note - a musical note with the time value of one-fourth of a whole note (American English)

Etymology

From Old French 'crochet' (small hook), from 'croche' (hook); referring to the hooked appearance of the note in early notation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑːtʃət / (AmE), / ˈkrɒtʃɪt / (BrE)
a sharp bend, curve, or hook; a shape resembling a hook
a hook-like curve or shape

Examples

The iron gate was decorated with an elegant crotchet at the top.
He hung his coat on a wooden crotchet beside the door.
The river made a sudden crotchet before joining the main stream.

Etymology

From Old French 'crochet' (hook), diminutive of 'croche' (hook), from Medieval Latin 'crocia'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑːtʃɪt / (AmE), / ˈkrɒtʃɪt / (BrE)
a small hooklike tool or implement, especially one used in needlework
small hooklike tool, needlework

Examples

She used a fine crotchet to make delicate lace by hand.
The old sewing kit contained a silver crotchet among the needles and thimbles.
A crotchet is essential for starting many crochet projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crochet hook - a small hooked needle used for crocheting

Etymology

From Old French 'croc' (hook), diminutive 'crochet'.
