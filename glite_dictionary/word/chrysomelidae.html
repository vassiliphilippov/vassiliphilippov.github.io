Chrysomelidae

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˌsɒməˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / kraɪˌsɒmɪˈlɪdɪː / (BrE)
A large family of beetles known as leaf beetles, many of which feed on plants and are important as pests or in ecology.
family of leaf beetles

Examples

The Chrysomelidae family includes many species that are significant agricultural pests.
Researchers are studying the feeding habits of Chrysomelidae to develop better pest control methods.
Some species within Chrysomelidae have striking metallic colors that make them popular with collectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaf beetle - common name for a member of the Chrysomelidae family

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) + 'melē' (limb), reflecting colorful appearance of many species.
