polyester

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˈɛstər / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈɛstə / (BrE)
a synthetic resin or fiber, used to make fabrics or plastics, formed by the reaction of dicarboxylic acids and diols; also, any fabric made from this material
synthetic fiber or fabric

Examples

My new shirt is made entirely of polyester, so it dries very quickly after washing.
They prefer natural fibers over polyester because it's more breathable.
This jacket blends cotton and polyester for durability and comfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nylon - a synthetic polymer used to make fibers and fabrics

Etymology

Formed from 'poly-', meaning many, and 'ester', reflecting its chemical composition.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˈɛstər / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈɛstə / (BrE)
a strong, lightweight synthetic resin or fibre, widely used in making fabrics and plastic products
synthetic fibre or resin used in textiles

Examples

Many shirts these days are made of polyester because it is durable and easy to care for.
The tent fabric is constructed from lightweight polyester, providing excellent resistance to rain and sunlight.
Unlike cotton, polyester dries quickly and retains its shape after washing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nylon - a type of synthetic polymer used as a textile fibre
  • Antonym: cotton - a natural fibre used in textiles

Etymology

From poly- (many) + ester (a chemical compound), referring to its structure of many ester units.

3. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈɛstər / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈɛstə / (BrE)
a synthetic resin in which the polymer units are linked by ester groups, widely used to make fibers, fabrics, and plastics
a synthetic resin or fiber for fabrics and plastics

Examples

The jacket is made of polyester, so it dries quickly after washing.
Many carpets are woven from polyester fibers because they are durable and stain resistant.
She prefers natural fabrics to polyester when buying clothes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nylon - a synthetic polymer, often used for fibers

Etymology

From ‘poly-’ (many) and ‘ester’ (a type of chemical compound).
