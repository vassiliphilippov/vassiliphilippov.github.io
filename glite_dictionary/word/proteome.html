proteome

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊtiˌoʊm / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtiːəʊm / (BrE)
the complete set of proteins produced by the genome of an organism, cell, or tissue at a certain time
complete set of proteins in an organism

Examples

Researchers are mapping the human proteome to better understand disease processes.
The bacterial proteome changes in response to environmental stress.
Comparative analysis of the proteome can reveal evolutionary relationships between species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genome - the complete set of genes or genetic material in an organism

Etymology

From 'protein' + '-ome', modeled after 'genome'.
