albacore

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.bəˌkɔr / (AmE), / ˈal.bə.kɔː / (BrE)
A species of tuna with white flesh, valued as a major source for canned tuna.
a small tuna with white flesh, used for canned tuna

Examples

The fisherman proudly displayed the huge albacore he had caught that morning.
Canned albacore is often used to make tuna salad sandwiches.
Many chefs prefer albacore because of its tender, mild white flesh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuna - a large fish of the mackerel family, important as food

Etymology

From Portuguese 'albacora', originally from Arabic 'al-bakūra' (the young fish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈælbəˌkɔr / (AmE), / ˈælbəkɔː / (BrE)
a large species of tuna with long pectoral fins, found in tropical and temperate seas and widely used for canned tuna
a species of tuna fish often canned

Examples

Many people prefer the mild flavor of albacore in their tuna sandwiches.
The fishing boat returned with a large catch of albacore last night.
Most canned white tuna is actually albacore, prized for its tender meat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuna - large marine fish of the mackerel family
  • Degree Higher: bluefin tuna - another large species of tuna

Etymology

From Portuguese 'albacora', from Arabic 'al-bakūra' meaning 'type of fish'.
