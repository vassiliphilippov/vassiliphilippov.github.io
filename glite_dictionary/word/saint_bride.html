Saint Bride

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt braɪd / (AmE), / seɪnt braɪd / (BrE)
An Irish abbess and patron saint of Ireland, also known as Saint Brigid, who lived from 453 to 523.
Irish patron saint and abbess (453–523)

Examples

Many churches in Ireland are dedicated to Saint Bride as a tribute to her influence.
People celebrate the feast day of Saint Bride on February 1 every year.
The legend of Saint Bride continues to inspire traditions in Celtic communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Brigid - another name for Saint Bride, Irish patron saint
  • Synonym: Saint Patrick - Irish patron saint, co-patron with Saint Bride

Etymology

From Old Irish Brigit, Latinized as Brigida, later associated with the English 'Bride'.
