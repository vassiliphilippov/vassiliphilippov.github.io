itineration

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌtɪnəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌtɪnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of traveling from place to place, especially for preaching or lecturing.
journeying for preaching or lecturing

Examples

The missionary's itineration took him to remote villages across three countries.
During her itineration, she delivered lectures in major cities and small towns alike.
Historical records document the itineration of religious leaders throughout the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: itinerary - a planned route or journey

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *itineratio*, from Latin *itinerari* (to travel).
