Somerset

1. PROPN

/ ˈsʌmərˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈsʌməsɪt / (BrE)
a county in southwestern England, bordering the Bristol Channel, known for its rural landscapes and historic towns
county in southwestern England

Examples

They spent their summer holiday hiking in Somerset and exploring its picturesque villages.
The cider from Somerset is famous throughout England for its quality and flavor.
Many historic sites, including Glastonbury Abbey, can be found in Somerset.

Etymology

From Old English 'Sumorsǣte', meaning 'people living at or dependent upon Somerton'.
