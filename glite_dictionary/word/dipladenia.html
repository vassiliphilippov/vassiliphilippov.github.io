Dipladenia

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪpləˈdiːniə / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈdiːnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical South American perennial woody vines known for their large clusters of showy flowers and milky sap.
genus of tropical flowering vines

Examples

The gardener trained the Dipladenia to climb up the trellis, creating a vibrant display of flowers.
Because of its striking blooms, Dipladenia is a popular choice for hanging baskets and patios.
Experts recommend protecting Dipladenia from frost, as it thrives in warm tropical climates.

Etymology

From Greek 'diplo-' (double) and 'aden' (gland), referring to features in the plant's flowers.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˌplædəˈniːə / (AmE), / dɪˌplædəˈniːə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical South American tuberous perennial woody vines with large racemose flowers and milky sap, belonging to the family Apocynaceae.
genus of tropical flowering vines

Examples

Many gardeners grow Dipladenia for its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers.
The climber identified as Dipladenia thrives in warm, sunny environments.
Botanists debate whether Dipladenia should be merged with the genus Mandevilla.

Etymology

From Greek 'diplas' (double) and 'aden' (gland), referring to the plant's double glands.
