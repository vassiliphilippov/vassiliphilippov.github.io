Iguanidae

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈɡwɑːnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ɪˈɡwɑːnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of New World lizards that includes iguanas and their close relatives, characterized by robust bodies and often crested backs
family of New World lizards (iguanas)
medical

Examples

The family Iguanidae includes iguanas found in Central and South America.
Herpetologists study members of the Iguanidae to understand lizard evolution.
Many species in the Iguanidae display bright colors and striking crests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iguana - large lizard found in Central and South America, member of Iguanidae

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Iguanidae', based on 'iguana' + '-idae' (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌɡwɑːnɪˈdiː / (AmE), / ɪˌɡwɑːnɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of New World lizards including iguanas and related species, primarily found in Central and South America.
family of New World lizards

Examples

The Iguanidae family includes a large variety of herbivorous lizards.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Iguanidae during their expedition to South America.
Many pet iguanas belong to the Iguanidae family.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Iguanidae, based on 'iguana' and standard zoological family suffix '-idae'.
