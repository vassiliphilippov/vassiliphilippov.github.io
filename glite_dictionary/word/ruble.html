ruble

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəl / (AmE), / ˈruːbəl / (BrE)
the basic unit of money in Russia, equal to 100 kopeks
Russian unit of currency

Examples

The price of gasoline is listed in rubles at Russian gas stations.
She exchanged her dollars for rubles before traveling to Moscow.
Due to inflation, the value of the ruble fell significantly this year.

Etymology

From Russian рубль (rublʹ).

2. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəl / (AmE), / ˈruːbəl / (BrE)
The basic unit of currency formerly used in Tajikistan before the introduction of the somoni in 2000.
currency unit formerly used in Tajikistan

Examples

Before 2000, the Tajik ruble was the official currency in Tajikistan.
The shopkeeper accepted only rubles as payment at that time.
Hyperinflation severely affected the value of the ruble during the late 1990s in Tajikistan.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: somoni - the current unit of currency in Tajikistan
  • Synonym: ruble - the basic unit of money in Russia and several other former Soviet republics

Etymology

From Russian *rublʹ*, used for the Russian ruble and its derivatives in former Soviet republics.
