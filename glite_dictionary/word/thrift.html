thrift

1. NOUN

/ θrɪft / (AmE), / θrɪft / (BrE)
the quality of using money and resources carefully and not wastefully
careful management of money

Examples

Her thrift allowed her to save enough money to buy a house on her own.
The organization encourages thrift among its members by offering budgeting workshops.
Through wise thrift, he was able to retire early and travel the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frugality - the quality of being economical with money or resources
  • Antonym: extravagance - a lack of restraint in spending money or using resources

Etymology

From Old Norse 'thrif' (prosperity, thriving), related to 'thrive'.

2. NOUN

/ θrɪft / (AmE), / θrɪft / (BrE)
a sun-loving, low-growing evergreen plant of the genus Armeria, with round heads of pink or white flowers, often grown in gardens
a flowering evergreen plant (Armeria)

Examples

The thrift bloomed beautifully along the rocky coastline.
Gardeners often use thrift to create colorful ground cover in borders.
Pink thrift thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea pink - common name for Armeria maritima, a species of thrift
