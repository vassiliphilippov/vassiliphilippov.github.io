spellbound

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɛlˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈspɛlbaʊnd / (BrE)
so interested or fascinated by something that you give it all your attention, as if by magic
fascinated or captivated as if by magic

Examples

The children listened spellbound as the storyteller spun his tale.
She watched the fireworks spellbound, unable to look away from the dazzling colors.
His spellbound audience hung on every word of his dramatic speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entranced - filled with wonder and delight; held spellbound
  • Synonym: mesmerized - having your attention fixated as if by a spell
  • Antonym: bored - feeling weary because one is unoccupied or uninterested

Etymology

Formed from 'spell' (magic incantation) + 'bound' (tied or held), meaning bound by a spell.

2. ADJ

completely fascinated or captivated as though under a magic spell; having one's attention held entirely
fascinated; captivated as if by magic

Examples

The children sat spellbound as the magician performed his tricks.
She was spellbound by the beauty of the ballet performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entranced - filled with wonder and delight; held spellbound
  • Synonym: mesmerized - having your attention fixated as though by a spell
  • Antonym: bored - feeling weary because one is unoccupied or lacks interest

Etymology

Formed from 'spell' (magic charm) + 'bound' (being tied or held).
