travail

1. NOUN

/ trəˈveɪl / (AmE), / trəˈveɪl / (BrE)
Intensive physical or mental effort; hard work or toil, often marked by difficulty or struggle.
arduous effort, hard work

Examples

After years of travail, she finally completed her thesis and graduated with honors.
The team's travail during the long winter paid off with a successful harvest.
His novel describes the travail of refugees fleeing war-torn regions.

Synset Examples

he got an A for effort
they managed only with great exertion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toil - hard, continuous work
  • Synonym: labor - productive work, often physical
  • Antonym: ease - lack of difficulty or effort, comfort

Etymology

From Old French *travail* (work, effort), from Medieval Latin *tripalium* (an instrument of torture).

2. VERB

/ trəˈveɪl / (AmE), / trəˈveɪl / (BrE)
to work hard or engage in difficult labor, especially in a painful or strenuous way
work hard; labor intensely
archaic

Examples

The miners would travail deep underground for hours with little rest.
For months, she travailed over her research project to ensure its success.
Writers often travail late into the night to meet looming deadlines.

Synset Examples

She was digging away at her math homework
Lexicographers drudge all day long

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toil - to work extremely hard or incessantly
  • Synonym: drudge - to do hard, menial, or dull work
  • Antonym: rest - to cease work or movement in order to relax

Etymology

From Old French 'travailler' (to toil), from Vulgar Latin '*tripaliare' (to torture), from Latin 'tripalium' (an instrument of torture).

Synset POS: v
