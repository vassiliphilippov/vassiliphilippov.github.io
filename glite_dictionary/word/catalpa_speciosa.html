Catalpa speciosa

1. NOUN

/ kəˈtælpə spɪˈsoʊə / (AmE), / kəˈtalpə spɪˈsəʊə / (BrE)
A species of catalpa tree native to the central United States, known for its large heart-shaped leaves and showy white flowers.
a catalpa tree species native to central U.S.

Examples

The Catalpa speciosa in our backyard produces beautiful clusters of white flowers every spring.
Many city parks in the Midwest feature Catalpa speciosa for its shade and ornamental value.
The wood of Catalpa speciosa is prized for its resistance to rot in fence posts and utility poles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern catalpa - common name for Catalpa speciosa
  • Degree Lower: southern catalpa - a related catalpa species (Catalpa bignonioides) native to the southeastern U.S.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name 'Catalpa', possibly from a Native American (Muscogee) word; species from Latin 'speciosa' meaning 'showy' or 'splendid'.
