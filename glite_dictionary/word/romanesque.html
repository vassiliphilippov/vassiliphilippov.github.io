Romanesque

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊ.məˈnɛsk / (AmE), / ˌrəʊ.məˈnɛsk / (BrE)
a style of architecture in western Europe after 1000 AD, characterized by semi-circular arches, large piers, thick walls, and decorative arcades
style of medieval European architecture

Examples

The cathedral is a masterpiece of Romanesque design, with its rounded arches and sturdy pillars.
During their trip to France, they visited several Romanesque churches dating from the 12th century.
Many features of the Romanesque style can be seen in the thick walls and decorative arcades of this abbey.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Gothic - a style of architecture following Romanesque, characterized by pointed arches and ribbed vaults

Etymology

From French 'romanesque', from 'roman' (Roman), referring to influences from ancient Roman architecture.
