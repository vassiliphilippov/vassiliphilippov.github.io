Tacca leontopetaloides

1. NOUN

/ ˈtækə liˌɑntəpɪˈtɛloʊˌidiz / (AmE), / ˈtakə liˌɒntəpɪˈtiːlɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
a perennial herb native to the East Indies, Polynesia, and Australia, cultivated for its large edible root which yields Otaheite arrowroot starch
perennial herb cultivated for edible root and starch

Examples

Tacca leontopetaloides is cultivated in tropical regions for its starchy root.
The locals use the root of Tacca leontopetaloides to prepare a type of arrowroot starch.
Research has shown that Tacca leontopetaloides can thrive in sandy coastal soils.

Etymology

From the genus name Tacca and species epithet leontopetaloides, from Greek 'leon' (lion) + 'petalon' (petal), referring to the flower's shape.
