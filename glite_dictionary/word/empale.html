to empale

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈpeɪl / (AmE), / ɛmˈpeɪl / (BrE)
to pierce or transfix with a sharp stake or point
pierce with a sharp stake or point

Examples

In medieval punishments, criminals were sometimes empaled as a form of execution.
The chef empaled the mushrooms on skewers for grilling.
He accidentally empaled his finger on a thorn while gardening.

Synset Examples

impale a shrimp on a skewer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impale - to pierce or transfix with a sharp stake or point

Etymology

From Old French *empaler*, from Late Latin *impalare* ('to drive a stake through'), from Latin *in* ('into') + *palus* ('stake').
