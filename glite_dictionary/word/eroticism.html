eroticism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɑtɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈrɒtɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
the quality or state of being intended to arouse sexual desire or excitement
sexual desire or excitement

Examples

The novel explores themes of eroticism and forbidden love.
There is a subtle eroticism in the way the artist captures the human body.
Some critics praised the film's use of eroticism to express its deeper messages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexuality - capacity for sexual feelings
  • Antonym: chasteness - the quality of being pure or abstaining from sexual activity

Etymology

From French *érotisme*, from Greek *erōtikos* (amatory), from *erōs* (sexual love).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɑtəˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈrɒtɪˌsɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the quality or state of being sexually arousing or of relating to sexual desire; a sense of anticipation or expression of sexuality
sexual desire or arousal

Examples

The film explores the eroticism present in subtle glances and gestures.
She found the eroticism of the novel to be both tasteful and intensely moving.
The artist's work is celebrated for its delicate balance between beauty and eroticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexuality - capacity for sexual feelings
  • Synonym: sensuality - enjoyment, expression, or pursuit of physical, especially sexual, pleasure

Etymology

From French érotisme, from Greek erōtikos (sexual love) + -ism.
