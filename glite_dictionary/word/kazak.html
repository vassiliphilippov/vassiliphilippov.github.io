Kazak

1. NOUN

/ kəˈzɑk / (AmE), / kəˈzɑːk / (BrE)
A former landlocked Soviet republic in Central Asia, now known as Kazakhstan.
former Soviet republic, now Kazakhstan

Examples

The Kazak republic gained independence after the dissolution of the Soviet Union.
Many resources in the Kazak region attracted foreign investment in the 1990s.
The history of the Kazak people is rich in tradition and culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kazakhstan - a country in Central Asia, formerly the Kazak Soviet Socialist Republic

Etymology

From Russian Казах (Kazakh), referring to the people and region of Kazakhstan.

2. NOUN

/ kɑːˈzæk / (AmE), / ˈkæzæk / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken by the Kazakh people, primarily in Kazakhstan and surrounding regions
Turkic language of Kazakhstan

Examples

She is fluent in both Russian and Kazak.
The poem was originally written in Kazak and later translated into English.
Many schools in Kazakhstan teach Kazak as the primary language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kazakh - a member of a Turkic ethnic group, or the language spoken by this group

Etymology

From Russian 'kazak', referring to the Kazakh people, ultimately from Turkic 'qazaq' (free man, nomad).

3. NOUN

/ kɑːˈzɑːk / (AmE), / ˈkæzæk / (BrE)
A member of a Turkic ethnic group native to Kazakhstan and parts of western Asia, traditionally Muslim.
member of a Turkic people of Kazakhstan

Examples

Many Kazaks continue to celebrate their rich nomadic traditions during annual festivals.
She interviewed a Kazak elder who shared stories about life on the Central Asian steppes.
The majority of Kazaks practice Islam and speak a Turkic language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kazakh - Member of the same Turkic ethnic group

Etymology

From Russian 'kazák', from Turkic 'qazaq' (free man, nomad); related to the name Kazakhstan.
