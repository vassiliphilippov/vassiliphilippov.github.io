crepe

1. NOUN

/ kreɪp / (AmE), / kreɪp / (BrE)
A small, very thin pancake, usually made from wheat flour and eaten with a sweet or savory filling.
thin pancake, often with a filling

Examples

She ordered a chocolate crepe with strawberries for dessert.
They made a batch of delicious crepes for Sunday brunch.
The street vendor filled the hot crepe with ham and cheese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pancake - a thin, flat cake made from batter

Etymology

From French crêpe, from Latin crispus (curled).

2. NOUN

/ kreɪp / (AmE), / kreɪp / (BrE)
a soft, thin, lightweight fabric with a crinkled or wrinkled surface, often used for dresses and scarves
thin fabric with a crinkled surface

Examples

Her dress was made of a delicate crepe that fluttered in the breeze.
The designer chose black crepe for the elegant evening gown.
He ran his fingers over the soft crepe, admiring the unique texture.

Etymology

From French 'crêpe', from Latin 'crispa' (curled, wrinkled).

3. NOUN

/ kreɪp / (AmE), / kreɪp / (BrE)
a thin paper with a crinkled texture, usually colored, used for decorations
thin, crinkled decorative paper

Examples

We decorated the room with pink and blue crepe for the birthday party.
Long streamers of crepe hung from the ceiling, brightening the hall.
She twisted strips of crepe to make colorful flowers for the school event.

Etymology

From French *crêpe* (crinkled fabric or paper), ultimately from Latin *crispus* (curled).

4. VERB

/ kreɪp / (AmE), / kreɪp / (BrE)
to cover or drape with crape, particularly as a sign of mourning
cover with crape, especially in mourning
archaic

Examples

During the Victorian era, it was customary to crepe the mirrors in a house after a death.
They creped the portraits in the hallway during the mourning period.
It was tradition to crepe the door as a sign of respect for the deceased.

Synset Examples

crape the mirror

Etymology

From French *crêpe*, meaning a light, thin fabric, ultimately from Latin *crispus* (curled).
