bolt-hole

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊltˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈbəʊltˌhəʊl / (BrE)
A hole or passage through which an animal may escape when threatened or pursued, especially from its burrow or den.
animal's escape hole

Examples

The rabbit darted for its bolt-hole as soon as it sensed danger.
Ferrets often dig a bolt-hole to escape from predators.
Every fox den had at least one secret bolt-hole for emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refuge - a place or situation providing safety or shelter

Etymology

From 'bolt' (to flee suddenly) + 'hole', referring to a hole into which or from which an animal can bolt.
