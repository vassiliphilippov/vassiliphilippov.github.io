Datura

1. NOUN

/ dəˈtʊrə / (AmE), / dəˈtjʊərə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the nightshade family, with large trumpet-shaped flowers and spiny fruit, several species of which are poisonous and sometimes used as hallucinogens.
genus of poisonous, trumpet-flowered plants

Examples

Many traditional medicines use extracts from Datura despite its toxic properties.
The field was overgrown with Datura, its large white flowers blooming at dusk.
Consumption of Datura seeds can be extremely dangerous due to their high toxicity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorn apple - common name for plants of the Datura genus

Etymology

From Hindi *dhatura*, possibly from Sanskrit *dhattūra* (a kind of plant).

2. NOUN

/ dəˈtʊr.ə / (AmE), / dəˈtjʊə.rə / (BrE)
a genus of herbaceous plants with trumpet-shaped flowers, commonly known as thorn apples, some species of which are toxic
genus of plants known as thorn apples

Examples

The Datura plant's trumpet-shaped flowers bloom at night and can be quite striking.
All parts of Datura contain toxic compounds that can be dangerous if ingested.
Gardeners are warned to handle Datura with care due to its poisonous properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorn apple - common name for plants of the genus Datura
  • Synonym: Brugmansia - genus closely related to Datura, known for large trumpet-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Hindi dhatūrā, from Sanskrit dhattūraḥ (jimsonweed).
