lamentably

1. ADV

/ ləˈmɛntəbli / (AmE), / ləˈmɛntəbli / (BrE)
In a way that deserves lament or regret; in an unfortunate or deplorable manner.
in an unfortunate or deplorable manner

Examples

The plan was lamentably executed, resulting in widespread confusion and frustration.
Many historical sites are lamentably neglected by the authorities.
Her response was lamentably inadequate to address the seriousness of the issue.

Synset Examples

he was sadly neglected
it was woefully inadequate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woefully - in a manner that expresses great sorrow or inadequacy
  • Synonym: sadly - in an unfortunate or regrettable way
  • Antonym: fortunately - in a manner marked by good fortune; luckily

Etymology

From 'lamentable' + '-ly', with 'lamentable' from Latin 'lamentabilis' (mournful, deserving of lament).
