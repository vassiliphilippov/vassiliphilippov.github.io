mycotoxin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkəˌtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪkəˌtɒksɪn / (BrE)
A poisonous substance produced by certain fungi, especially as a contaminant of food or feed.
toxin produced by a fungus
medical

Examples

Some species of mold produce mycotoxins that can contaminate stored grains and pose health risks.
The laboratory tested the sample for mycotoxin contamination after reports of illness among livestock.
Proper storage conditions help prevent the formation of mycotoxins in animal feed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toxin - a poisonous substance, especially one produced by living cells or organisms
  • Antonym: antitoxin - a substance that counteracts a toxin

Etymology

From Greek 'mykes' (fungus) + 'toxin' (poison).
