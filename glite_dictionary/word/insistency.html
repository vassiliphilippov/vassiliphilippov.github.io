insistency

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɪstənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪstənsi / (BrE)
the quality or state of persistently demanding attention or notice
persistent demand for attention

Examples

The insistency of the alarm made it impossible to ignore.
She was struck by the insistency of his requests for answers.
Despite his polite manner, there was an underlying insistency in his tone.

Synset Examples

the insistence of their hunger
the press of business matters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insistence - persistent demand or assertion

Etymology

From insist (Latin *insistere*) + -ency (from Latin *-entia*).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɪstənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪstənsi / (BrE)
the quality or act of insisting firmly on something
the act of insisting

Examples

Her insistency on honesty earned her the respect of her colleagues.
Despite repeated insistency, the request was ignored by the committee.
He was known for his insistency on punctuality at every meeting.

Synset Examples

insistence on grammatical correctness is a conservative position

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insistence - the act of stating or demanding something forcefully

Etymology

From insist + -ency, modeled on the pattern of other nouns like 'persistency'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01217047-n
Synset POS: n
