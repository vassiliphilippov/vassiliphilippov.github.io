averment

1. NOUN

/ əˈvɜrmənt / (AmE), / əˈvɜːmənt / (BrE)
a confident and emphatic statement or declaration, especially one made without supporting evidence; often used in legal contexts.
emphatic declaration or statement
legal

Examples

The defendant's averment was accepted by the court as truthful.
His averment was that he had never seen the document before.
Without supporting evidence, her averment failed to convince the jury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertion - a statement declaring something as true
  • Antonym: denial - a statement declaring something is not true

Etymology

From Old French 'averer' (to prove true), from Latin 'ad-' + 'verus' (true).
