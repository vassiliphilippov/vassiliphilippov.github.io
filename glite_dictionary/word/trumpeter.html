trumpeter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌmpətɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrʌmpɪtə / (BrE)
a musician who plays the trumpet or cornet
musician who plays the trumpet

Examples

The jazz band featured a talented trumpeter who captivated the audience with his solos.
She aspired to become a professional trumpeter after hearing a famous orchestra perform.
Many trumpeters start learning the instrument at a young age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornetist - a musician who plays the cornet
  • Antonym: violinist - a musician who plays the violin

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌmpətər / (AmE), / ˈtrʌmpɪtə / (BrE)
(formal) a person who announces important news, especially with a trumpet.
person who announces news (with trumpet)

Examples

The trumpeter blew his horn to announce the king's arrival.
Every village celebration began with a trumpeter proclaiming the news to the people.
The ancient city employed a trumpeter who shared royal proclamations.

Synset Examples

the chieftain had a herald who announced his arrival with a trumpet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herald - a person who officially announces news or events

Etymology

From Middle English trumpeter, from Old French trompetteur, based on 'trumpet'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌmpətər / (AmE), / ˈtrʌmpɪtə / (BrE)
a large white swan (Cygnus buccinator) native to western North America, known for its resonant, trumpet-like call.
large North American white swan

Examples

The wetlands are a protected habitat for the endangered trumpeter.
A flock of trumpeters glided gracefully across the lake at dawn.
Conservationists have worked to reintroduce the trumpeter to its former range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trumpeter swan - large white North American swan with a trumpeting call

Etymology

Named for its trumpet-like call.
