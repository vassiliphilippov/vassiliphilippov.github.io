Illecebrum verticillatum

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛsəˌbrʌm ˌvɜːrtɪsɪˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / ɪˌliːsɪˌbrʊm ˌvɜːtɪsɪˈleɪtəm / (BrE)
A glabrous annual plant with a slender taproot and clusters of white flowers, native to western Europe, especially the western Mediterranean and Atlantic coastal areas.
a European annual plant with white flower clusters

Examples

Illecebrum verticillatum is commonly found in sandy soils along the Atlantic coast of Europe.
Botanists have recorded Illecebrum verticillatum flowering in the western Mediterranean region.
The delicate white flowers of Illecebrum verticillatum make it easy to distinguish from similar annuals.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Illecebrum and species epithet verticillatum, meaning 'in whorls'.
