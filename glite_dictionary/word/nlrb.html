NLRB

1. NOUN

/ ɛn ɛl ɑr bi / (AmE), / ɛn ɛl ɑː biː / (BrE)
an independent agency of the United States government responsible for mediating disputes between employers and labor unions and enforcing labor law
US labor relations government agency
american legal

Examples

The NLRB ruled in favor of the workers after months of hearings.
A dispute between management and staff was eventually settled by the NLRB.
Lawyers prepared documents for a case before the NLRB regarding unfair labor practices.

Etymology

Acronym for National Labor Relations Board.
