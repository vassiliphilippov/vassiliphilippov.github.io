Copernican

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈpɜrnɪkən / (AmE), / kəˈpɜːnɪkən / (BrE)
relating to the astronomical theory of Copernicus, especially the heliocentric model in which the planets revolve around the sun
relating to Copernicus's heliocentric theory

Examples

The Copernican revolution transformed our understanding of the solar system.
Galileo provided evidence in support of the Copernican model.
Adopting a Copernican view changed the course of scientific thinking.

Synset Examples

in the Copernican system the earth and other planets revolve around the sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heliocentric - having the sun as the center, as in Copernican theory

Etymology

From the name of Nicolaus Copernicus, Polish astronomer (1473–1543).

2. ADJ

/ koʊˈpɜrnɪkən / (AmE), / kəˈpɜːnɪkən / (BrE)
Describing a major or radical change, especially one that fundamentally alters the established way of thinking.
major or radical; revolutionary

Examples

The discovery was considered a Copernican moment in biological research.
Her theory represented a Copernican shift in the way society views mental health.
Many experts believe we need a Copernican change in our approach to environmental policy.

Synset Examples

a Copernican revolution in modern art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolutionary - involving or causing a complete or dramatic change
  • Synonym: transformative - causing a marked change

Etymology

Named after Nicolaus Copernicus, whose heliocentric model revolutionized astronomy.
