OD

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ ˈdi / (AmE), / ˌəʊ ˈdiː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for the right eye, used especially in medical contexts such as prescriptions and eye examinations
medical abbreviation for the right eye
medical

Examples

The doctor wrote 'OD' on the prescription to indicate the medication should be used in the right eye.
My vision in my OD has improved after the treatment.
Please apply two drops of this solution to your OD every morning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: OS - medical abbreviation for the left eye

Etymology

From Latin 'oculus dexter' meaning 'right eye'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊˈdi / (AmE), / ˌəʊˈdiː / (BrE)
Doctor of Optometry; a professional doctoral degree for optometrists.
Doctor of Optometry degree

Examples

Dr. Parker received her OD from the University of California, Berkeley.
The sign outside the clinic read: Sarah Lee, OD, Optometrist.
Applicants to the OD program must have completed prerequisite coursework in biology and chemistry.

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'oculus doctor' (doctor of the eye) and also interpreted as 'Doctor of Optometry'.
