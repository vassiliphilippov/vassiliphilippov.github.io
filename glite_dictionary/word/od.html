to o.d.

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊ ˈdi / (AmE), / ˌəʊ ˈdiː / (BrE)
to take an excessive amount of a drug, especially one that is illegal or harmful, often resulting in serious harm or death.
take too much of a drug
informal

Examples

The singer o.d.'d on prescription medication last year.
If you o.d. on caffeine, you may feel shaky and anxious.
She was hospitalized after o.d.'ing at the party.

Synset Examples

The rock star overdosed and was found dead in his hotel room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overdose - to take an excessive and dangerous amount of a drug

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'overdose.'
