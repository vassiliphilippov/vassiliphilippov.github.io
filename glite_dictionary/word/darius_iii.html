Darius III

1. PROPN

/ dəˈraɪəs ˈθri / (AmE), / dəˈraɪəs ˈθriː / (BrE)
the last king of the Achaemenid Empire of Persia, defeated by Alexander the Great, whose death marked the end of the Persian Empire (died 330 BC)
last king of the Persian Empire, died 330 BC

Examples

Darius III was unable to unite his empire against Alexander's army.
The defeat of Darius III at Gaugamela signaled the fall of Persia.
Historians debate the circumstances of Darius III's death after his flight from battle.

Etymology

From Latin Darius, from Greek Dareios, from Old Persian Dārayavahuš (holding firm the good).
