Cottus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːtəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒtəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater and marine fish known as sculpins, belonging to the family Cottidae.
a genus of sculpin fish

Examples

The genus Cottus includes many species of small, spiny-headed fish found in northern waters.
Researchers discovered a new Cottus species in the mountain stream.
Many anglers are familiar with the sculpins of the Cottus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sculpin - a type of small bottom-dwelling fish

Etymology

From Latin cottus (a type of fish, the miller’s thumb or sculpin).
