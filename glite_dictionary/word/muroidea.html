Muroidea

1. NOUN

/ mjuːˈrɔɪdiə / (AmE), / mjʊəˈrɔɪdɪə / (BrE)
a superfamily of rodents that includes mice, rats, hamsters, and related animals, but excludes the family Dipodidae (jerboas)
superfamily of rodents (mice, rats, hamsters, etc.)
medical

Examples

The Muroidea superfamily includes many species familiar to humans, such as house mice and brown rats.
Scientists debated the evolutionary relationships among members of the Muroidea in recent studies.
Hamsters are also classified within the Muroidea, along with mice and rats.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek 'mus' (mouse) and '-oid' (resembling), with zoological suffix '-ea'.

2. NOUN

/ mjuːˈrɔɪdiə / (AmE), / mjʊəˈrɔɪdiə / (BrE)
a superfamily of rodents that includes mice, rats, hamsters, voles, and related animals; essentially equal to the suborder Myomorpha but excluding the Dipodidae (jerboas and relatives)
a rodent superfamily (mice, rats, hamsters, etc.)

Examples

The rodent superfamily Muroidea includes familiar species like mice and rats.
Scientists study the evolutionary relationships within Muroidea to better understand rodent diversification.
Hamsters, voles, and gerbils all belong to the Muroidea superfamily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Myomorpha - a suborder of rodents including mice, rats, and similar species

Etymology

From Latin 'mus' (mouse) + Greek '-oidēs' (like, resembling), used in scientific taxonomy.
