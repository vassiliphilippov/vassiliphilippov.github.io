Rubus hispidus

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəs hɪsˈpaɪdəs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs hɪsˈpɪdəs / (BrE)
A species of low, bristly shrub native to eastern North America, commonly known as the bristly dewberry, bearing small, dark edible fruits.
bristly dewberry plant of eastern North America

Examples

The Rubus hispidus thrives in damp woodlands and produces clusters of small black fruits.
Botanists surveyed the area for Rubus hispidus as part of their biodiversity study.
Birds and small mammals feed on the berries of Rubus hispidus during the late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dewberry - a trailing blackberry-like plant with edible fruits

Etymology

From Latin: 'Rubus' meaning bramble or blackberry, and 'hispidus' meaning bristly or rough.
