camosh

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæmɒʃ / (AmE), / ˈkamɒʃ / (BrE)
any of several perennial plants of the genus Camassia, native to North and South America, known for their star-shaped blue or purple flowers and edible bulbs.
plant of the genus Camassia

Examples

Native Americans traditionally harvested camosh bulbs as a food source.
The bright flowers of the camosh bloom in spring in western prairies.
Researchers are studying the habitat requirements of wild camosh populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camass - plant of the genus Camassia, also known as quamash
