violent

1. ADJ

/ ˈvaɪə.lənt / (AmE), / ˈvaɪə.lənt / (BrE)
(of colors or sounds) extremely intense, vivid, or loud, often in a way that is overwhelming or startling.
intensely vivid or loud (colors or sounds)

Examples

The painting featured a violent contrast of blues and reds that caught everyone’s attention.
She was startled by the violent bang from the fireworks outside.
His shirt was such a violent shade of orange that it was hard to look at.

Synset Examples

wild colors
wild shouts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vivid - (of colors) intensely deep or bright
  • Antonym: subdued - (of sounds or colors) not strong, loud, or intense

Etymology

From Latin 'violentus' (vehement, forcible).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvaɪələnt / (AmE), / ˈvaɪələnt / (BrE)
(of death, action, or event) caused by force, especially injury, rather than natural causes.
caused by force or injury

Examples

The investigator determined that it had been a violent death rather than one from natural causes.
He had suffered a violent injury in the accident.
The police suspect foul play after a series of violent attacks in the area.

Synset Examples

a violent death

Related Terms

  • Antonym: natural - (of death, etc.) occurring due to natural causes, not by force

Etymology

From Latin violentus (vehement, forcible).
