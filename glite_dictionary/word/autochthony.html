autochthony

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈtɑkθəni / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒkθəni / (BrE)
the state or condition of being native to a particular place; indigenous origin or occurrence
state of being native to a place

Examples

The tribe claims autochthony in the region, tracing their roots back thousands of years.
Debates over national identity often involve assertions of autochthony versus claims of migration.
The concept of autochthony is central to discussions on indigenous land rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigeneity - the fact of originating or occurring naturally in a particular place; indigenousness
  • Antonym: foreignness - the quality of being from or characteristic of another place or country; not native

Etymology

From Greek 'autochthonos' (native to the soil), from 'autos' (self) + 'chthon' (earth).
