haematocrit

1. NOUN

/ hiˈmæt̬əkrɪt / (AmE), / hiˈmætəkrɪt / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the proportion of red blood cells to plasma in blood, usually by centrifugation
blood analysis instrument
medical british

Examples

The laboratory technician placed the blood sample into the haematocrit to measure the packed cell volume.
A precise haematocrit reading is essential when diagnosing certain types of anaemia.
The hospital recently upgraded its old haematocrit to a more automated machine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hematocrit - American English spelling of haematocrit

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + kritēs (judge), referring to measuring blood components.

2. NOUN

/ hiˈmætəˌkrɪt / (AmE), / hiˈmætəkrɪt / (BrE)
the proportion of the volume of packed red blood cells to the total volume of blood, expressed as a percentage
percentage of red blood cells in blood
medical british

Examples

The doctor ordered a haematocrit test to check for anemia.
A low haematocrit value can indicate bleeding or other health problems.
Her haematocrit levels increased after treatment for dehydration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hematocrit - percentage of red blood cells in blood (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) and 'kritēs' (judge, divider), reflecting the process of separation in the test.
