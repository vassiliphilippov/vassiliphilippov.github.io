incompetent

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkɑmpətənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒmpɪtənt / (BrE)
Not legally qualified or permitted to do something, especially to be a witness or to perform a legal act.
not legally qualified
legal

Examples

The judge ruled that the witness was incompetent to testify because of a conflict of interest.
Certain individuals may be declared incompetent to make their own legal decisions.
Evidence from an incompetent witness can be excluded from the trial.

Synset Examples

a wife is usually considered unqualified to testify against her husband
incompetent witnesses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: competent - legally qualified or sufficient

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + competens (suitable, qualified).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00514186-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkɑːmpətənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒmpɪtənt / (BrE)
a person who lacks the ability, skill, or qualifications to carry out a task or responsibility effectively
a person lacking ability or skill

Examples

The company faced several losses due to an incompetent in the finance department.
We can't risk putting an incompetent in charge of the project.
After the trial, the judge declared the defendant to be legally incompetent.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: competent - having the necessary ability or skills
  • Synonym: inept - having or showing no skill; clumsy

Etymology

From Latin 'incompetentem', meaning 'not suitable'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10221982-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkɑːmpətənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒmpɪtənt / (BrE)
Lacking the necessary skill, ability, or aptitude to do something successfully.
lacking skill or ability

Examples

The mechanic proved to be incompetent at fixing basic engine problems.
Her incompetent handling of the situation made things worse for everyone.
He was fired for being consistently incompetent in his job duties.

Synset Examples

a bungling workman
did a clumsy job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unskilled - lacking skill or training
  • Antonym: capable - having the ability, fitness, or quality necessary to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'incompetens' (not suitable, unfit), from 'in-' (not) + 'competens' (suitable).
