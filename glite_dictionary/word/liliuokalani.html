Liliuokalani

1. PROPN

/ liˌliuːoʊkəˈlɑːni / (AmE), / liˌliːuːəʊkəˈlɑːni / (BrE)
(1838–1917) The last reigning queen of the Hawaiian Islands, who ruled from 1891 until the overthrow of the Hawaiian monarchy in 1893.
last queen of Hawaii (1838–1917)

Examples

Queen Liliuokalani was overthrown in 1893, marking the end of the Hawaiian monarchy.
The famous song "Aloha ʻOe" was composed by Liliuokalani during her reign.
Many Hawaiians regard Liliuokalani as a symbol of their national identity and resistance.

Etymology

Hawaiian given name; original name Lydia Liliʻu Loloku Walania Kamakaʻeha.

2. PROPN

/ liːˌliːuːˌoʊkoʊˈlɑːni / (AmE), / liːˌliːuːˌɒkəˈlɑːni / (BrE)
(1838–1917) The last reigning queen of the Hawaiian Islands, notable for her efforts to preserve Hawaiian independence and culture.
last queen of Hawaii (1838-1917)

Examples

Liliuokalani was overthrown in 1893, marking the end of the Hawaiian monarchy.
The music composed by Liliuokalani is still cherished in Hawaiian culture today.
Many tourists visit places associated with Liliuokalani to learn about Hawaii's royal history.

Etymology

From Hawaiian Liliʻuokalani, her royal name.
