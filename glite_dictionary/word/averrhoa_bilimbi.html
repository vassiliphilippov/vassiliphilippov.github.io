Averrhoa bilimbi

1. NOUN

/ ˌævərˈhoʊ.ə bɪˈlɪmbi / (AmE), / ˌævəˈrəʊ.ə bɪˈlɪmbi / (BrE)
An East Indian evergreen tree of the oxalic family, bearing very sour, cucumber-shaped fruit commonly used in cooking and preserves.
East Indian evergreen tree with sour fruit

Examples

The Averrhoa bilimbi grows best in warm, humid climates and produces fruit year-round.
Locals use the fruit of Averrhoa bilimbi to add a sour flavor to curries and chutneys.
Botanists study Averrhoa bilimbi for its unusual tolerance to acidic soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bilimbi - the fruit of the Averrhoa bilimbi tree
  • Degree Higher: Averrhoa carambola - a related tree species known as the carambola or starfruit tree

Etymology

From the genus name Averrhoa (after Averroes, 12th-century Arabic philosopher) and Malay 'belimbing'.
