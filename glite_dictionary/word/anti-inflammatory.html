anti-inflammatory

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.ɪnˈflæm.əˌtɔɹ.i / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.ɪnˈflæm.ə.t(ə)ɹ.i / (BrE)
a drug or substance that reduces inflammation in the body
medicine reducing inflammation
medical

Examples

You can take an anti-inflammatory to help reduce the swelling in your knee.
Doctors often prescribe anti-inflammatories for patients with arthritis.
This cream contains a mild anti-inflammatory to soothe irritated skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analgesic - a drug that relieves pain

Etymology

From anti- (against) + inflammatory, relating to inflammation.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæn.taɪ.ɪnˈflæm.əˌtɔɹ.i / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.ɪnˈflæm.ə.t(ə)ri / (BrE)
A drug or substance that reduces inflammation in the body.
drug that reduces inflammation

Examples

Many athletes use anti-inflammatory medications after injuries to help manage swelling and pain.
This anti-inflammatory cream can be applied directly to the affected area for relief.
Doctors may prescribe an anti-inflammatory for conditions like arthritis.

Synset Examples

She took an anti-inflammatory for her sore throat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analgesic - a drug that relieves pain

Etymology

From 'anti-' meaning 'against' + 'inflammatory', from Latin 'inflammare' (to set on fire).
