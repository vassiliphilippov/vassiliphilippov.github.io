et alii

1. ADV

/ ɛt ˈæli.aɪ / (AmE), / et ˈal.ɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
and others; used to refer to additional people not individually named, especially in academic citations (commonly abbreviated as 'et al.').
and others (mainly in citations)

Examples

The research paper by Johnson et alii was highly influential in the field.
This method has been tested by Kim et alii over several decades.
The study conducted by Brown et alii provides further evidence for the theory.

Synset Examples

the data reported by Smith et al.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: et al. - abbreviation for 'et alii', used in citations to mean 'and others'

Etymology

From Latin 'et alii' (and others), masculine plural of 'alius' (other).
