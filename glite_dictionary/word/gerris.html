Gerris

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛr.ɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Gerridae, consisting of aquatic insects commonly known as water striders, which are able to skate on the surface of water.
genus of water strider insects

Examples

The genus Gerris includes several species of water striders found in ponds and streams.
Researchers studied the movement of Gerris on calm lake surfaces.
Many Gerris species have adaptations that allow them to live on the surface tension of water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water strider - an insect that can walk on the surface of water

Etymology

From Latin 'Gerris', the scientific genus name, originally used by Linnaeus for these insects.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛr.ɪs / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic insects in the family Gerridae, commonly known as water striders, characterized by their ability to walk on the surface of water.
a genus of water striders

Examples

Scientists collected Gerris specimens from the lake to study their locomotion on water.
The Gerris genus includes species adapted to various freshwater habitats.
Children often spot Gerris skating across calm ponds during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water strider - an insect able to walk on water surfaces, usually in genus Gerris

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'gerris' (a kind of water insect).
