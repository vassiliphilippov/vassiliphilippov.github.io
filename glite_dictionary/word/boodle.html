boodle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːdəl / (AmE), / ˈbuːdəl / (BrE)
money, especially in large amounts; informal term for cash or funds
informal term for money
informal slang

Examples

He walked away with a suitcase full of boodle after the deal closed.
They spent all their boodle on a lavish holiday in Paris.
She always has a little boodle stashed away for emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cash - money in the form of coins or notes
  • Synonym: dough - slang for money
  • Synonym: bread - slang for money
  • Synonym: loot - slang for a large amount of money

Etymology

Probably from Dutch 'boedel' (estate, property), meaning possessions or wealth.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbuːdəl / (AmE), / ˈbuːd(ə)l / (BrE)
A card game in which chips are placed on the ace, king, queen, and jack of separate suits; players lay down the lowest available card in their hand, then successive higher cards; the player who matches a layout card collects the corresponding chips.
card game involving chips and four face cards

Examples

We played boodle last night and I won a pile of chips.
The rules of boodle require careful attention to the layout cards.
Everyone enjoyed the fast pace of boodle around the table.
