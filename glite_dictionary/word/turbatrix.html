Turbatrix

1. NOUN

/ tərˈbætrɪks / (AmE), / tɜːˈbætrɪks / (BrE)
A genus of small nematode worms in the family Cephalobidae, commonly known for the vinegar eel.
genus of nematode worms

Examples

The genus Turbatrix includes species commonly found in fermenting liquids such as vinegar.
Scientists observed Turbatrix under the microscope to study their movement.
The presence of Turbatrix can indicate the quality of fermented food products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vinegar eel - A small nematode worm found in vinegar, species Turbatrix aceti

Etymology

From Latin, used in scientific classification.

2. NOUN

/ tərˈbætrɪks / (AmE), / tɜːˈbætrɪks / (BrE)
A genus of nematode worms in the family Cephalobidae, known for species such as the vinegar eel.
genus of nematode worms

Examples

The vinegar eel, a species within the genus Turbatrix, is often found in fermented foods.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Turbatrix in laboratory conditions.
Members of the genus Turbatrix are harmless to humans but fascinating for study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nematode - a type of roundworm of the phylum Nematoda

Etymology

From Latin, possibly based on 'turba' (crowd) + 'trix' (female agent), used in taxonomy naming.
