Enterolobium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəˈroʊloʊbiəm / (AmE), / ˌɛntərəˈləʊbiəm / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical American timber trees in the pea family, closely related to the genus Albizia.
genus of tropical American timber trees

Examples

The Enterolobium trees are prized for their valuable timber in Central America.
Many species in the genus Enterolobium have distinctive ear-shaped pods.
Botanists often compare Enterolobium with Albizia due to their similar foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Albizia - genus of tropical and subtropical trees and shrubs in the pea family

Etymology

From Greek 'entero-' (intestine) and 'lobion' (small lobe), referring to the shape of the seed pods.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəˈrɑːlobiəm / (AmE), / ˌɛntərəˈləʊbiəm / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American timber trees closely related to Albizia, including species with ear-shaped seed pods
genus of tropical American timber trees

Examples

The Enterolobium genus is known for its distinctive ear-shaped seed pods.
Some species of Enterolobium are important sources of timber in Central and South America.
Botanists often compare Enterolobium with related genera such as Albizia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Albizia - a related genus of tropical and subtropical trees and shrubs

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'enteron' (intestine) and 'lobion' (little lobe), referring to the shape of the seed pods.
