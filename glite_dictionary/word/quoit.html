quoit

1. NOUN

/ kwɔɪt / (AmE), / kwɔɪt / (BrE)
A flat ring, usually of iron or rope, thrown at a stake in the game of quoits.
ring used in a throwing game

Examples

Children took turns tossing the quoit toward the wooden peg.
The metal quoit landed perfectly around the stake.
Each player is given four quoits at the start of the match.

Etymology

From Old French coite (flat stone for throwing), from Latin coëta (cushion).
