merchandiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrtʃənˌdaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtʃənˌdaɪzə / (BrE)
a person or business that is involved in the buying and selling of goods, especially in retail trade
retail trader or salesperson

Examples

The company hired a skilled merchandiser to improve its product displays and boost sales.
As a merchandiser, she travels to stores to ensure products are presented attractively.
Many successful entrepreneurs start out as a merchandiser in local markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retailer - a person or business selling goods directly to consumers

Etymology

From merchant + -iser (from Old French 'marchant', from Latin 'mercari' meaning 'to trade', and the agent noun suffix '-iser').
