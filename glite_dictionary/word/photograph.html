photograph

1. VERB

/ ˈfoʊ.t̬əˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.təˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) to appear in a particular way in photographs.
appear in photos in a certain way

Examples

Some people photograph really well, always looking natural and relaxed on camera.
The painting photographs beautifully, capturing all the rich colors and details.
Babies often photograph adorably, making family albums even more special.

Synset Examples

Children photograph well

Etymology

From French 'photographe', from Greek 'phōtós' (light) + 'graphō' (write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02740528-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfoʊtəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈfəʊtəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
to take a picture of someone or something using a camera
take a picture with a camera

Examples

She photographed the magnificent landscape at sunrise.
Tourists often photograph famous landmarks to remember their travels.
He photographed the ceremony for the local newspaper.

Synset Examples

I photographed the scene of the accident
She snapped a picture of the President

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snap - to take a quick photograph
  • Degree Higher: film - to record moving images with a camera

Etymology

From Greek phōt- (light) + -graph (something written or drawn), via French and English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01005294-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊ.t̬əˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.tə.ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
An image of a person, scene, or object produced by a camera, whether printed, projected, or in digital form.
image made by a camera

Examples

She keeps a framed photograph of her family on her desk.
I took a photograph of the beautiful sunset last night.
The photograph captured the joy on their faces perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: picture - an image, especially one painted or drawn
  • Synonym: snapshot - a photograph taken quickly and casually
  • Antonym: drawing - a picture made with a pencil or pen rather than a camera

Etymology

From Greek 'phōtós' (light) + 'graphé' (drawing, writing), coined in the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03931348-n
Synset POS: n
