lifesaver

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfˌseɪvər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfˌseɪvə / (BrE)
a person employed at a beach or pool to watch over swimmers and rescue them from danger
person who rescues swimmers at beach or pool

Examples

The lifesaver quickly spotted a swimmer in distress and rushed to help.
Our local pool always has a lifesaver on duty during open hours.
She trained as a lifesaver before starting her job at the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifeguard - a person who supervises the safety of swimmers

Etymology

From 'life' + 'saver', indicating one who saves lives.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfˌseɪvər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfˌseɪvə / (BrE)
a life preserver in the form of a ring made of buoyant material, used to rescue someone at risk of drowning
ring-shaped life preserver

Examples

The child grabbed the lifesaver that was thrown into the pool.
Each boat is required by law to have at least one lifesaver on board.
She tossed a lifesaver to the man struggling in the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: life preserver - a device designed to keep a person afloat in water
