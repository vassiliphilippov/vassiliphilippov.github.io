terse

1. ADJ

/ tɜrs / (AmE), / tɜːs / (BrE)
using few words and often not seeming friendly or polite; brief and direct in a way that may seem unfriendly or abrupt.
brief and direct, often abrupt

Examples

His terse reply made it clear he did not want to discuss the matter further.
The announcement was terse, offering no explanation for the sudden change.
She appreciated his terse style of writing, which got straight to the point.

Synset Examples

a crisp retort
a response so curt as to be almost rude
the laconic reply: ‘yes’
short and terse and easy to understand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concise - giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words
  • Synonym: laconic - using very few words
  • Antonym: verbose - using more words than necessary

Etymology

From Latin 'tersus', past participle of 'tergere' meaning 'to wipe, polish'.
