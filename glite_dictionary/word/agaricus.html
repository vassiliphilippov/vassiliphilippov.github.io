Agaricus

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡær.ɪ.kəs / (AmE), / əˈɡær.ɪ.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of gilled fungi in the family Agaricaceae, including several edible mushroom species such as the common cultivated mushroom.
genus of gilled mushrooms

Examples

Many commercially grown mushrooms belong to the genus Agaricus.
The most popular edible species, Agaricus bisporus, is commonly found in supermarkets.
Mycology students studied several species within Agaricus during their fieldwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - an edible fungus with a domed cap

Etymology

From Latin 'Agaricus', used by ancient authors to denote a kind of fungus.
