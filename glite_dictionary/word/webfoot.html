webfoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛbˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɛbˌfʊt / (BrE)
a foot in which the toes are joined by folds of skin, as seen in ducks, geese, and other aquatic animals
foot with toes joined by skin

Examples

Many aquatic birds have a webfoot to help them swim efficiently.
The duck's webfoot made it easy to paddle across the pond.
Scientists studied the shape of the frog's webfoot to understand its swimming motion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: webbed foot - a foot having toes connected by a membrane

Etymology

From 'web' (membranous tissue) + 'foot'.
