seek

1. NOUN

/ siːk / (AmE), / siːk / (BrE)
(computing) the movement of a read/write head to a specific data track on a disk.
movement of disk read/write head

Examples

The disk seek time affects how quickly files can be accessed.
After a long seek, the drive finally found the correct data track.
Engineers monitor the average seek to optimize disk performance.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07365493-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ siːk / (AmE), / siːk / (BrE)
(of a thing) to move towards or go to a particular place or state, often as a natural tendency.
move toward a place or state (of a thing)

Examples

Water always seeks the lowest point in the landscape.
A compass needle seeks north due to Earth's magnetic field.
Heat energy seeks areas of lower temperature to establish equilibrium.

Synset Examples

a liquid seeks its own level

Etymology

From Old English *sēcan* (to go to, look for), related to German *suchen* (to seek).
