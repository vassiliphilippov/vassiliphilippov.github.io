do-nothing

1. NOUN

/ ˈduː ˌnʌθɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈduː ˌnʌθɪŋ / (BrE)
a person who avoids work or makes no effort to be productive
person who avoids work

Examples

He's been a do-nothing since college and still lives with his parents.
The company won't tolerate any do-nothing employees on the team.
She called him a do-nothing after he refused to help around the house.

Synset Examples

a lazy bum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idler - a person who is habitually lazy or does not work
  • Synonym: slacker - a person who tries to avoid work
  • Antonym: workaholic - a person who compulsively works hard and long hours
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10217586-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈduːˌnʌθɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈduːˌnʌθɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or group) unwilling or unable to take any action or responsibility; inactive or ineffective.
inactive; unwilling to act or take responsibility

Examples

Many citizens were frustrated with the do-nothing attitude of the council.
His critics accused him of being a do-nothing manager who avoided tough decisions.
Voters are unlikely to support another do-nothing administration.

Synset Examples

a do-nothing government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inactive - not engaging in or involving any or much physical activity; not active
  • Synonym: indolent - wanting to avoid activity or exertion; lazy
  • Antonym: proactive - creating or controlling a situation by causing something to happen rather than responding to it after it has happened
