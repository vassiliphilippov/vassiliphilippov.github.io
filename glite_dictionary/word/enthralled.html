enthralled

1. ADJ

/ ɛnˈθrɔld / (AmE), / ɪnˈθrɔːld / (BrE)
filled with wonder and delight; captivated or completely fascinated by something.
captivated or fascinated

Examples

The children were enthralled by the magician's incredible tricks and colorful props.
She sat enthralled throughout the performance, unable to look away from the stage.
As he told his adventure story, everyone around the campfire listened enthralled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: captivated - being held by interest or charm
  • Antonym: bored - feeling weary and uninterested

Etymology

From Middle English 'enthrallen', meaning to enslave, from 'en-' (make) + 'thrall' (slave). The sense evolved to mean 'to captivate.'
