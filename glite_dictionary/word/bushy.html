bushy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʊʃi / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃi / (BrE)
(of hair or fur) thick, full, and often untidy or poorly groomed.
thick, untidy hair or fur

Examples

His bushy eyebrows gave him a very distinctive look.
After months without a haircut, her hair became long and bushy.
The dog has a thick, bushy tail.

Synset Examples

bushy locks
a shaggy beard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaggy - having long, thick, and untidy hair or fur

2. ADJ

/ ˈbʊʃi / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃi / (BrE)
Growing thick or spreading in a way that resembles a bush; having dense or abundant growth, especially of hair or foliage.
thick, spreading like a bush

Examples

The rabbit has a bushy tail that helps it keep warm in winter.
He struggled to tame his bushy eyebrows before the interview.
After months without a haircut, her hair looked wild and bushy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dense - closely packed together; thick
  • Antonym: thin - having little thickness or sparse growth

Etymology

From bush +‎ -y; 'bush' from Middle English 'busche', from Old French 'buis', from Proto-West Germanic *busk.
