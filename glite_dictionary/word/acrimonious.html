acrimonious

1. ADJ

/ ˌækrəˈmoʊniəs / (AmE), / ˌækrɪˈməʊniəs / (BrE)
(of speech or a dispute) full of anger, bitterness, or harshness.
full of bitterness or harshness

Examples

The negotiations turned acrimonious when both sides refused to compromise.
Their acrimonious divorce was widely discussed by their friends.
An acrimonious debate erupted during the town hall meeting.

Synset Examples

an acrimonious dispute
bitter about the divorce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitter - expressing strong hostility or resentment
  • Antonym: harmonious - friendly and peaceful; free from disagreement

Etymology

From Latin 'acrimonia' (sharpness) via French 'acrimonieux'.
