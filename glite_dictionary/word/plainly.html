plainly

1. ADV

/ ˈpleɪnli / (AmE), / ˈpleɪnli / (BrE)
in a way that is clear, obvious, and easy to understand; unmistakably.
clearly; obviously

Examples

It was plainly a mistake to trust him with the secret.
You are plainly exhausted and need to rest.
She plainly stated her objections to the proposed plan.

Synset Examples

the answer is obviously wrong
she was in bed and evidently in great pain
he was manifestly too important to leave off the guest list
it is all patently nonsense
she has apparently been living here for some time
I thought he owned the property, but apparently not
You are plainly wrong
he is plain stubborn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obviously - in a way that is easily perceived or understood; clearly
  • Synonym: evidently - in a way that is clearly seen or understood
  • Antonym: ambiguously - in a way that is unclear or can be understood in more than one way

Etymology

From Middle English 'pleynly', from Old French 'plainement'.

2. ADV

/ ˈpleɪnli / (AmE), / ˈpleɪnli / (BrE)
in a simple, unadorned, or uncomplicated manner; without extravagance or embellishment
in a simple or unadorned manner

Examples

She prefers to dress plainly and avoid any flashy jewelry.
The room was plainly furnished, with only a table and two chairs.
They lived plainly, focusing on necessities rather than luxuries.

Synset Examples

she was dressed plainly
they lived very simply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simply - in a simple or uncomplicated way
  • Antonym: elaborately - in a detailed, decorated, or complex way

Etymology

From plain + -ly, where 'plain' ultimately derives from Latin 'planus' (flat, clear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00005436-r
Synset POS: r
