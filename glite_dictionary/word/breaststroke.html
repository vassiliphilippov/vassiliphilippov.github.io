breaststroke

1. VERB

/ ˈbrɛst.stroʊk / (AmE), / ˈbrɛst.strəʊk / (BrE)
to swim by lying on one's front and moving the arms in a half-circular motion from in front of the chest outward and backward while performing a frog-like kick with the legs
to swim using a specific front-facing stroke

Examples

She breaststrokes smoothly across the pool in the early morning light.
During the triathlon, he breaststroked most of the distance to conserve his energy.
Learning to breaststroke can be challenging for beginners due to the coordination required.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freestyle - to swim using any preferred style, usually the front crawl
  • Antonym: backstroke - to swim lying on the back with an alternating arm and leg movement

Etymology

From breast (front of the body) + stroke (a single movement or motion). The term originally described the swimmer's front-facing position.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛstˌstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈbrɛst.strəʊk / (BrE)
a swimming stroke in which the arms are extended forward and swept outward while the legs perform a frog kick
a type of swimming stroke

Examples

She won a gold medal in the 100-meter breaststroke at the national championships.
The breaststroke is often the first stroke swimmers learn after the basics.
His favorite way to swim across the lake is using the breaststroke.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freestyle - a swimming stroke where the swimmer can use any style, usually the front crawl
  • Antonym: backstroke - a swimming stroke performed on the back with arm and leg movements
  • Antonym: butterfly - a swimming stroke performed on the chest with both arms moving symmetrically

Etymology

From 'breast' + 'stroke', named for the position of the arms during the stroke.
