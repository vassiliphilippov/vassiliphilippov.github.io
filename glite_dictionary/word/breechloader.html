breechloader

1. NOUN

/ ˈbriːtʃˌloʊdər / (AmE), / ˈbriːtʃˌləʊdə / (BrE)
a firearm that is loaded at the rear end of the barrel (the breech), rather than the muzzle
firearm loaded at the rear (breech)

Examples

The museum displayed a 19th-century breechloader alongside other historical firearms.
After the invention of the breechloader, reloading became much faster and more efficient.
Hunters appreciated the improved accuracy and convenience of their new breechloaders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: muzzleloader - a firearm loaded from the open end of the barrel (the muzzle)

Etymology

From 'breech' (the rear part of a gun barrel) + 'loader', describing the loading method.
