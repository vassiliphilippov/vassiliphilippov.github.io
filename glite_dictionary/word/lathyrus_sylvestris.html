Lathyrus sylvestris

1. NOUN

/ ləˈθaɪrəs sɪlˈvɛstrɪs / (AmE), / ləˈθaɪərəs sɪlˈvɛstrɪs / (BrE)
A European perennial plant with purple and pink mottled flowers, sometimes cultivated as fodder or green manure.
species of perennial European plant

Examples

Lathyrus sylvestris is often grown as green manure to improve soil fertility.
The pink and purple flowers of Lathyrus sylvestris attract many pollinators in the summer.
Some farmers plant Lathyrus sylvestris for use as animal fodder.

Etymology

From Latin: Lathyrus, the genus name for vetchling, and sylvestris meaning 'of the woods'.
