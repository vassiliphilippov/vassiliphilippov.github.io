acetate

1. NOUN

/ ˈæsɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈæsɪteɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of acetic acid, used especially in chemistry and industry
salt or ester of acetic acid

Examples

Cellulose acetate is often used to produce film and textiles.
The lab technician measured the acetate content in the solution.
Some medications are delivered in the form of their acetate salts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetone - a volatile, flammable liquid used as a solvent

Etymology

From Latin *acetum* (vinegar) + English -ate (as a chemical suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæsɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈæsɪteɪt / (BrE)
A synthetic fabric made from fibers of cellulose acetate, often used in garments and linings.
synthetic fabric from cellulose acetate

Examples

The dress was made of shimmering acetate that draped beautifully.
Some vintage neckties are crafted from brightly colored acetate.
The lining of the blazer is composed of soft acetate fabric.

Etymology

From acetic acid, with suffix -ate, originally referring to the salt or ester; later, the term was extended to materials produced from cellulose acetate.
