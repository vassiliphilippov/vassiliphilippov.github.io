Drymarchon

1. PROPN

/ draɪˈmɑːrkɒn / (AmE), / draɪˈmɑːkɒn / (BrE)
A genus of large, nonvenomous snakes in the Colubridae family, commonly known as indigo snakes, native to the Americas.
genus of indigo snakes

Examples

The genus Drymarchon includes some of the largest native snakes in North America.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Drymarchon in southern Florida.
The eastern indigo snake, classified under Drymarchon, is a threatened species.

Etymology

From Greek 'drymarchos', meaning 'ruler of the forest' ('drys' – tree, 'archon' – ruler).

2. NOUN

/ draɪˈmɑːrkɒn / (AmE), / draɪˈmɑːkɒn / (BrE)
A genus of large, nonvenomous snakes in the Colubridae family, commonly known as indigo snakes, found in the Americas.
genus of nonvenomous indigo snakes

Examples

The eastern indigo snake, classified in the genus Drymarchon, is the longest native snake in the United States.
Several species within the genus Drymarchon are important for their role in controlling rodent populations.
Herpetologists conducted a field study focusing on the habitat of Drymarchon snakes.

Etymology

From Greek 'drymarchos' (ruler of the forest), from 'drymos' (forest) + 'archon' (ruler).
