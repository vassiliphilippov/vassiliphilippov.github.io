astuteness

1. NOUN

/ əˈstuːtnəs / (AmE), / əˈstjuːtnɪs / (BrE)
the ability to understand things quickly and deeply, especially to see and take advantage of opportunities or solve problems shrewdly
keen insight or shrewdness

Examples

Her astuteness in business negotiations has earned her a great reputation.
He admired the astuteness with which she analyzed the problem.
Political leaders often need astuteness to navigate complex situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrewdness - the quality of having or showing sharp judgment
  • Antonym: naivety - lack of experience and sophistication

Etymology

From astute + -ness; 'astute' from Latin *astutus* (shrewd).

2. NOUN

/ əˈstuːtnəs / (AmE), / əˈstjuːtnəs / (BrE)
the ability to accurately assess situations or people and turn this to one's advantage; keen insight or shrewdness
keen insight; shrewdness

Examples

Her astuteness in negotiations earned her respect from her colleagues.
The company's success can be attributed to the CEO's financial astuteness.
He handled the complex situation with remarkable astuteness and diplomacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrewdness - cleverness; sharp judgment; synonym of astuteness
  • Antonym: naivety - lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment; opposite of astuteness

Etymology

From Latin 'astutus' (crafty, shrewd) + English suffix '-ness'.
