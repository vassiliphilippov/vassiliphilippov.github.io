yenta

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛntə / (AmE), / ˈjɛntə / (BrE)
(informal) A woman who is gossipy or meddlesome, especially one who habitually criticizes or pries into others' affairs.
gossipy or meddlesome woman
informal

Examples

Everyone in the building knew her as the building's yenta, always spreading rumors before anyone else.
My grandmother warns me not to be a yenta and to mind my own business.
At the party, the self-proclaimed yenta had already learned everyone's secrets by midnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - a person who habitually reveals personal or sensational facts about others
  • Synonym: busybody - a person who meddles in other people's affairs

Etymology

From Yiddish *yente* (proper female name), which became associated with the stereotype of a gossip.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɛntə / (AmE), / ˈjɛntə / (BrE)
(informal) A woman who is known for gossiping or meddling in other people's affairs.
gossipy or meddling woman
informal

Examples

Everyone at the party knew her as the neighborhood yenta, always eager to share the latest rumors.
Don't tell Miriam your secret—she's a total yenta who can't keep anything to herself.
At work, the office yenta spread the news about the new manager before anyone else heard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - a person who habitually reveals personal information about others

Etymology

From Yiddish יענטע (yente), a female given name, later used as a stereotype for a gossip.
