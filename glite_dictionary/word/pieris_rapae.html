Pieris rapae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪˈɛrɪs ˈræpi / (AmE), / ˌpɪə.rɪs ˈræpiː / (BrE)
A small, widely distributed white butterfly, commonly known as the cabbage white, whose larvae feed on cabbage and related plants.
species of white butterfly (cabbage white)

Examples

The larvae of Pieris rapae can cause significant damage to cabbage crops.
Many gardeners try to protect their vegetables from Pieris rapae infestations.
A natural predator helps control the population of Pieris rapae in organic gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage white - common name for Pieris rapae, a white butterfly

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pieris' (a genus of butterflies) + 'rapae' referring to turnip plants.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪ.ˈɪr.ɪs ˈræ.pi / (AmE), / ˌpɪə.rɪs ˈræ.pi / (BrE)
the green larval stage of the cabbage white butterfly, known for feeding on cabbage and related plants
larva of the cabbage white butterfly

Examples

The garden was infested with Pieris rapae larvae eating holes in the cabbage leaves.
Farmers use netting to protect crops from Pieris rapae damaging their vegetables.
The green caterpillar of Pieris rapae is a familiar pest in many home gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage worm - larva of the cabbage white butterfly

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Pieris' and specific epithet from Latin 'rapae' (of turnips/cabbage).
