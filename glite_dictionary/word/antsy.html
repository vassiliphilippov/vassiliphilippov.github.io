antsy

1. ADJ

/ ˈæn(t)si / (AmE), / ˈæn(t)si / (BrE)
nervous, restless, or unable to keep still; fidgety.
nervous or restless
informal

Examples

The children grew antsy waiting for the ceremony to start.
After sitting in the meeting for over an hour, I became quite antsy.
He always gets antsy before a big presentation.

Synset Examples

a constant fretful stamping of hooves
a restless child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restless - unable to rest, relax, or be still
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or restlessness

Etymology

Probably from 'ants in one's pants', meaning to be restless.
