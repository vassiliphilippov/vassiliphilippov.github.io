Tussilago

1. PROPN

/ tʌsɪˈleɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / tʌsɪˈleɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
a genus of low, creeping, yellow-flowered perennial herbs native to north temperate regions, including species such as coltsfoot, and sometimes encompassing related plants.
a plant genus including coltsfoot

Examples

Tussilago is commonly found in moist environments across Europe and Asia.
The most well-known member of the genus Tussilago is the coltsfoot plant.
Botanists often debate whether certain species should be classified under Tussilago or placed in related genera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coltsfoot - a common name for Tussilago farfara, a species in the Tussilago genus

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'coughwort', from 'tussis' (cough) and '-ago' (indicating association), due to its use in treating coughs.
