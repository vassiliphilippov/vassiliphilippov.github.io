authoritative

1. ADJ

/ əˈθɔrət̬əˌtɪv / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒr.ɪ.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
Having official power or authority, or coming from a source with recognized power; supported by authority.
having official authority or power

Examples

The president released an authoritative statement regarding the new policy.
Her book is considered the most authoritative biography of the artist.
We need an authoritative source before making any major decisions.

Synset Examples

an authoritative communique
the authorized biography

Related Terms

  • Synonym: official - approved by an authority or holding an office

Etymology

From Medieval Latin auctoritatīvus, from auctoritas (authority).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00179875-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ əˈθɔr.əˌteɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒr.ɪ.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
having or showing the confidence and power of someone who is respected or obeyed; able to be trusted as being accurate or true
having authority or influence

Examples

The general spoke in an authoritative tone that silenced the entire room.
Her authoritative report became the main reference on this subject.
As a teacher, he adopts an authoritative manner that inspires respect from his students.

Synset Examples

an important official
the captain's authoritative manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commanding - having a manner that shows authority and expects obedience
  • Antonym: submissive - willing to obey someone else; not asserting authority

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'auctoritativus', from Latin 'auctoritas' (authority).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01836121-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ əˈθɔr.əˌteɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒr.ɪ.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a book, account, or source) accepted as being accurate and reliable; of recognized authority or excellence.
recognized as accurate or reliable

Examples

Her research is considered the authoritative source on the subject.
The textbook is widely regarded as the most authoritative reference in the field.
Experts praised the article for its authoritative analysis and clear presentation of facts.

Synset Examples

the definitive work on Greece

Related Terms

  • Synonym: definitive - (of a book or statement) regarded as the best of its kind and unlikely to be improved upon

Etymology

From Medieval Latin auctoritatīvus, from Latin auctoritas (authority).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02302830-s
Synset POS: s
