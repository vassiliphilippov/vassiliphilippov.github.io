crossroads

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌroʊdz / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌrəʊdz / (BrE)
a point at which a crucial decision must be made that will have far-reaching consequences
crucial decision point

Examples

After losing his job, he found himself at a crossroads in his career and life.
The country stands at a crossroads, facing decisions that will shape its future.
She realized she was at an emotional crossroads and needed to choose a new direction.

Synset Examples

at that juncture he had no idea what to do
he must be made to realize that the company stands at a critical point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turning point - a time at which a decisive change in a situation occurs
  • Synonym: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger

Etymology

From 'cross' and 'road,' referring originally to a place where roads meet, later used metaphorically.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑsˌroʊdz / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌrəʊdz / (BrE)
A point at which a decision must be made, especially one that will have significant consequences for the future.
a critical decision point

Examples

After years in the same job, Maria found herself at a crossroads in her career.
The company faces a crossroads as it decides whether to expand or stay small.
He realized he was at a crossroads, needing to choose between two very different paths in life.

Synset Examples

Freud's work stands at the crossroads between psychology and neurology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turning point - a time at which a decisive change in a situation occurs
  • Synonym: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or important decision
