bootleg

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːtˌlɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈbuːt.lɛɡ / (BrE)
the upper part of a boot that extends above the instep
the upper part of a boot

Examples

The leather bootleg fit snugly around his calf.
She decorated the bootleg with bright stitching and patterns.
Mud splattered up to the bootleg after she walked through the field.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02877884-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈbuːtˌlɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡ / (BrE)
(of goods) distributed or sold illegally, without official approval or against the law.
illegally sold or distributed goods

Examples

He bought a bootleg copy of the concert on the street corner.
The police seized hundreds of bootleg DVDs from the shop.
Buying bootleg cigarettes can result in legal trouble.

Synset Examples

the black economy pays no taxes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegal - not allowed by law

Etymology

Originally from bootleg (noun), referring to the illegal transport of liquor in boots during Prohibition in the United States.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01405584-s
Synset POS: s

3. VERB

/ ˈbuːtˌlɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡ / (BrE)
to make, copy, or distribute something, especially a recording, illegally and without authorization
produce or distribute illegally

Examples

It's illegal to bootleg movies and sell them online.
The band discovered that someone had bootlegged their concert and was selling recordings.
He was arrested for trying to bootleg alcohol across state borders.

Synset Examples

bootleg tapes of the diva's singing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pirate - to use or reproduce another's work illegally

Etymology

Originally from the practice of hiding liquor in the leg of a boot to smuggle it illegally during Prohibition in the US.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01629022-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈbuːtˌlɛg / (AmE), / ˈbuːt.lɛg / (BrE)
Whiskey or other alcoholic liquor made and distributed illegally, especially during Prohibition in the United States.
illegally-made whiskey or liquor

Examples

During Prohibition, people risked arrest to buy bootleg from hidden speakeasies.
Authorities confiscated several barrels of bootleg hidden in the barn.
The bootleg he offered tasted surprisingly smooth for illegal whiskey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonshine - illegally made distilled alcohol, especially corn whiskey
  • Antonym: legal alcohol - alcoholic beverage produced and sold legally

Etymology

Originated in the late 19th century in the US, referring to concealing liquor in one's bootleg (the top part of a boot).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07918760-n
Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ ˈbuːtˌlɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡ / (BrE)
to make, sell, or distribute products such as alcohol or drugs illegally
illegally sell goods

Examples

He was arrested for trying to bootleg counterfeit DVDs on the street.
During Prohibition, many people would bootleg whiskey to make money.
They were caught attempting to bootleg pills across the border.

Synset Examples

They were bootlegging whiskey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to smuggle - to move goods illegally into or out of a country
  • Synonym: traffick - to deal or trade in something illegal, especially drugs
  • Antonym: to legalize - to make something legal that was previously illegal

Etymology

From 'bootleg' (noun), originally referring to concealing liquor in the leg of a boot during Prohibition in the US.
