Zinnia grandiflora

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪni.ə ˌɡrændəˈflɔrə / (AmE), / ˈzɪni.ə ˌɡrændɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A species of subshrub with short leafy stems and numerous small, nearly round yellow-orange flower heads, native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.
a species of yellow-flowered zinnia

Examples

The Zinnia grandiflora added a splash of yellow to the desert landscape.
Gardeners in Arizona often cultivate Zinnia grandiflora for its drought tolerance.
Wild Zinnia grandiflora blooms attract butterflies to the prairie.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: zinnia - any plant of the genus Zinnia, cultivated for its colorful flowers

Etymology

From Latin; 'Zinnia' named after J. G. Zinn, German botanist; 'grandiflora' meaning 'large-flowered'.
