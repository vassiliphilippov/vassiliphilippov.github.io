poorwill

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊrˌwɪl / (AmE), / ˈpɔːwɪl / (BrE)
a nocturnal bird of the nightjar family, found in western North America, known for its soft, repetitive call
North American nocturnal bird (nightjar)

Examples

As dusk falls over the desert, the distinctive call of a poorwill can often be heard.
The common poorwill is one of the few birds known to hibernate during cold weather.
We spotted a poorwill resting motionless on the ground, blending in with the leaf litter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightjar - another bird in the same family as the poorwill

Etymology

Imitative of the bird’s call, first recorded in the mid-19th century.
