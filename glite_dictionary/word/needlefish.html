needlefish

1. NOUN

/ ˈniː.dl̩ˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈniː.dl̩ˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a slender, surface-dwelling fish with a long, pointed jaw, found especially in coastal waters of Europe and known for its predatory nature
slender coastal predatory fish

Examples

The aquarium specialist pointed out the sleek needlefish gliding near the surface of the tank.
Fishermen along the coast often catch needlefish because they swim close to the water's edge.
A group of young needlefish darted quickly away when disturbed by a passing boat.

2. NOUN

/ ˈniːdəlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈniːdəlˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a slender marine fish with a long, tubular snout and a body covered with bony plates, found in warm seas and some freshwater habitats
slender fish with a long, tubular snout

Examples

A school of needlefish swam swiftly near the surface of the clear blue water.
The needlefish is known for its ability to leap out of the water to escape predators.
Fishermen cautioned swimmers about the sharp jaws of the needlefish in the shallow bay.
