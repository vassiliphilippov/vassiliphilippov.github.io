barytes

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrˌaɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˈbærʌɪtiːz / (BrE)
A white or colorless mineral consisting of barium sulfate, used as the main source of barium.
white or colorless mineral (BaSO4)

Examples

The geologist identified the white crystals as barytes during the field survey.
Large deposits of barytes are often mined for industrial purposes.
The production of barium chemicals begins with the extraction of barytes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barite - another common name for barytes (BaSO4)

Etymology

From Greek 'barys' meaning 'heavy', due to its high density.
