vixenish

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪksənɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvɪksənɪʃ / (BrE)
(of a woman) having a spiteful, shrewish, or malicious temperament.
having a spiteful or shrewish nature

Examples

Her vixenish remarks offended many of the guests at the party.
The novel portrays a vixenish character who enjoys stirring up trouble.
He was wary of his vixenish aunt, who never missed a chance to criticize him.

Synset Examples

a vixenish old woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrewish - bad-tempered or aggressively assertive (typically of a woman)
  • Synonym: malicious - intending or intended to do harm

Etymology

From 'vixen' (a female fox), used figuratively for a spiteful woman, plus the suffix '-ish' (having the quality of).
