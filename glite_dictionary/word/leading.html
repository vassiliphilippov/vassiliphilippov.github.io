leading

1. ADJ

/ ˈliːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈliːdɪŋ / (BrE)
having the highest rank, position, or score in a competition or contest at a given time
in first place in a contest

Examples

The leading candidate received the most votes in the primary.
After three quarters, the leading team was ahead by ten points.
She celebrated when her horse became the leading runner in the final lap.

Synset Examples

the leading team in the pennant race

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trailing - having a lower score or rank in a contest

4. NOUN

/ ˈlɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
A thin strip of metal used in traditional printing to separate lines of type and determine spacing between them.
thin metal strip for spacing in printing

Examples

The printer adjusted the leading to create more space between the lines.
Traditional letterpress printing required manual insertion of leading between each row of type.
Too much leading can make a page look sparse and difficult to read.

Etymology

From lead, the metal (from Old English 'lǣd'), as the strips were made of lead.
