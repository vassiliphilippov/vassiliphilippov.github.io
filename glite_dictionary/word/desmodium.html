Desmodium

1. NOUN

/ dɛzˈmoʊdiəm / (AmE), / dɛzˈməʊdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the pea family, commonly known as tick trefoil or beggarweed, native to many regions and often found in grasslands or woodlands.
genus of flowering plants (tick trefoil)

Examples

Researchers studied the distribution of Desmodium species across several continents.
The sticky pods of Desmodium easily cling to the fur and clothing of passing animals.
Many farmers plant Desmodium as a cover crop to improve soil fertility.

Etymology

From Greek desmos (bond) and -ium, in reference to the jointed pods.

2. NOUN

/ dɛzˈmoʊdiəm / (AmE), / dɛzˈməʊdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the pea family (Fabaceae), commonly known as beggarweed or tick trefoil, found in temperate and tropical regions.
genus of legumes; beggarweed, tick trefoil

Examples

Many species of Desmodium are valued as forage crops in agriculture.
The sticky pods of Desmodium easily attach to animal fur and clothing.
Researchers studied Desmodium plants for their role in sustainable pest management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beggarweed - common name for species in Desmodium
  • Synonym: tick trefoil - another common name for plants in the genus Desmodium

Etymology

From Greek desmos (bond, chain) + New Latin -odium, referring to the jointed pods.
