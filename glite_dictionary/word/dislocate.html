to dislocate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɪsloʊˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsləʊkeɪt / (BrE)
to move or force something, especially a bone in a joint, out of its normal position
move something out of its normal position

Examples

He managed to dislocate his shoulder during the football match.
If you dislocate a joint, seek medical attention immediately.
The violent impact caused several bones to dislocate.

Synset Examples

dislocate joints
the artificial hip joint luxated and had to be put back surgically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relocate - to move to a new place
  • Antonym: set - to put a dislocated joint back into place (medical context)

Etymology

From Latin dislocare, from dis- (apart) + locare (to place).

2. VERB

/ ˈdɪs.loʊ.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.lə.keɪt / (BrE)
to move something from its normal or proper place or position, especially so that it is no longer correctly arranged or functioning
move out of normal position

Examples

The accident caused the bone to dislocate from its socket.
Violence in the region could dislocate thousands of families from their homes.
Heavy winds can dislocate roof tiles and cause significant damage.

Synset Examples

The colonists displaced the natives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to displace - to move something or someone from its place or position

Etymology

From Latin dislocare (to displace), from dis- (apart) + locare (to place).
