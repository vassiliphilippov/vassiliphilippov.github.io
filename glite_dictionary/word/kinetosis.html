kinetosis

1. NOUN

/ kɪnəˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / kɪnɪˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which a person feels dizzy or nauseated due to the motion experienced while traveling in or on a moving vehicle; motion sickness
motion sickness caused by travel
medical

Examples

After a boat ride, several passengers suffered from kinetosis and needed to lie down.
Reading in the car triggered kinetosis for him during long road trips.
Medications can help prevent kinetosis in people who are prone to motion sickness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motion sickness - a condition characterized by nausea and dizziness due to motion

Etymology

From Greek 'kinētikos' (of motion) plus '-osis' (condition, disease).
