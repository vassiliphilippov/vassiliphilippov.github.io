tort-feasor

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrtˌfiːzər / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtˌfiːzə / (BrE)
A person or party who commits a tort (a wrongful act leading to legal liability).
person who commits a tort
legal

Examples

The court found the tort-feasor liable for damages caused by his negligence.
Insurance companies often seek reimbursement from the tort-feasor responsible for an accident.
Each tort-feasor may be held jointly and severally liable in a civil lawsuit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrongdoer - a person who commits an illegal or immoral act
  • Antonym: victim - a person harmed as a result of a tort

Etymology

From 'tort' (wrongful act) and 'feasor', from Anglo-French faire (to do).
