alligatored

1. ADJ

/ ˈælɪˌɡeɪtərd / (AmE), / ˈælɪˌɡeɪtəd / (BrE)
(of paint or varnish) having a cracked surface pattern resembling the hide of an alligator.
having a cracked, reptile-skin-like surface

Examples

The old wooden door was covered in alligatored paint that peeled at the edges.
Restorers carefully stripped the alligatored varnish from the antique frame before refinishing.
After years of sun exposure, the deck's surface became rough and alligatored.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cracked - split or broken into sharp uneven pieces

Etymology

Formed from 'alligator' + '-ed', referring to the resemblance to alligator hide.
