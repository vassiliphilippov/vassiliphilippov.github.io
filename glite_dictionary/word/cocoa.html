cocoa

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.koʊ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.kəʊ / (BrE)
a powder made from ground roasted cacao beans with most of the fat removed, used in making chocolate and drinks
powder from roasted cacao beans for drinks or baking

Examples

She added two tablespoons of cocoa to the cake mixture for a rich chocolate flavor.
A cup of hot cocoa is perfect on a cold winter night.
The recipe calls for unsweetened cocoa instead of chocolate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cacao - the tropical tree and its seeds from which cocoa is made

Etymology

From Spanish *cocoa*, alteration of *cacao*, from Nahuatl *cacahuatl*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkəʊ / (BrE)
A hot drink made from cocoa powder, milk, and sugar, usually served warm or hot.
a hot chocolate-flavored drink

Examples

On cold winter nights, I love to drink a cup of cocoa by the fireplace.
She prepared mugs of steaming cocoa for her children after they finished playing in the snow.
Would you like some cocoa with marshmallows or just plain?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hot chocolate - a hot drink made with chocolate or cocoa and milk

Etymology

From Spanish 'cacao', from Nahuatl 'kakawa'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07939004-n
Synset POS: n
