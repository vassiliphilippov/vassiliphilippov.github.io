ladies'-eardrop

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdiz ˈɪərˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈleɪdiz ˈɪəˌdrɒp / (BrE)
an erect or climbing shrub native to Brazil, having deep pink to red, pendulous flowers; commonly known as a type of fuchsia
Brazilian shrub with red flowers; a fuchsia

Examples

The garden was brightened by the presence of the ladies'-eardrop near the front fence.
She planted a ladies'-eardrop for its striking red blossoms and delicate appearance.
Hummingbirds are often attracted to the vibrant flowers of the ladies'-eardrop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuchsia - a genus of flowering plants with pendulous, colorful blooms

Etymology

Named for the appearance of the flowers, which resemble decorative earrings traditionally worn by women.
