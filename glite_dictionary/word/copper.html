copper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːpər / (AmE), / ˈkɒpə / (BrE)
a coin made of copper, especially a penny (historically in the UK or US)
a copper coin, especially a penny

Examples

He found an old copper under the floorboards while renovating the house.
Could you spare a copper for the bus fare?
In Victorian times, children often received a single copper as a reward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penny - a small coin, especially in the UK or US currency

Etymology

From Old English *coper*, from Latin *cuprum* (copper, the metal), referring to coins made from copper.

5. NOUN

/ ˈkɑpɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒpə / (BrE)
a soft, malleable, reddish-brown metallic element (chemical symbol: Cu) valued for its electrical and thermal conductivity
a reddish-brown metallic element

Examples

The pipes in the old house are made of copper and have lasted for decades.
Electric wires are often coated with copper because it is an excellent conductor.
She polished the copper pot until it gleamed in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bronze - an alloy of copper and tin
  • Degree Higher: brass - an alloy of copper and zinc

Etymology

From Latin 'cuprum', from earlier Greek 'Kypros' (Cyprus), the island where copper was mined in antiquity.
