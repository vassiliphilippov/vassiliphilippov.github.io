Mertensia

1. NOUN

/ mərˈtɛn.si.ə / (AmE), / mɜːˈtɛn.si.ə / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants in the family Boraginaceae, known for blue or purple funnel-shaped flowers, native to temperate regions.
genus of herbaceous plants with blue flowers

Examples

Mertensia is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blue flowers.
We observed several species of Mertensia during our hike through the alpine meadow.
The common bluebell is sometimes confused with Mertensia because of its similar flower shape.

Etymology

Named after the German botanist Franz Carl Mertens (1764–1831).

2. NOUN

/ mərˈtɛn.si.ə / (AmE), / məˈtɛn.si.ə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs in the family Boraginaceae, native to temperate regions, with blue or purple funnel-shaped flowers.
genus of blue-flowered herbs

Examples

Several species of Mertensia are commonly grown in gardens for their attractive blue flowers.
Bluebell Woods featured a dense patch of Mertensia in full bloom.
Botanists collected samples of Mertensia from alpine meadows for further study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluebell - common name for several species in the Mertensia genus

Etymology

Named after German botanist Franz Carl Mertens (1764–1831).
