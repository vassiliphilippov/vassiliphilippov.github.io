Ophiophagus hannah

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌfaɪ.əˈfeɪɡəs ˈhænə / (AmE), / əʊˌfaɪ.əˈfəɪɡəs ˈhanə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the king cobra, a large, highly venomous snake native to southeastern Asia and the East Indies
scientific name for the king cobra

Examples

The Ophiophagus hannah is revered in many cultures across southeastern Asia.
Zoologists studied the hunting behavior of Ophiophagus hannah in the wild.
Unlike other cobras, Ophiophagus hannah specializes in eating other snakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king cobra - common name for Ophiophagus hannah, the world's longest venomous snake
  • Degree Lower: cobra - any of several highly venomous snakes known for their hood

Etymology

From Greek 'ophio' (snake) + 'phagus' (eating) and Hannah from Latinized form, referring to the species' tendency to eat other snakes.
