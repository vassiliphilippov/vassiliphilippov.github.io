paunch

1. NOUN

/ pɔntʃ / (AmE), / pɔːntʃ / (BrE)
A large, protruding belly, especially in a man.
a large, protruding belly

Examples

He developed quite a paunch after years of sedentary work.
His tailored shirt couldn't completely hide his growing paunch.
The comedian poked fun at his own paunch during the show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belly - the front part of the human trunk below the ribs
  • Synonym: stomach - the front part of the body below the chest

Etymology

From Middle English 'paunche', from Old French 'pance', from Latin 'panticem', accusative of 'pantex' meaning 'belly'.
