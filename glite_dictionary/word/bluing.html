bluing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbluːɪŋ / (BrE)
a process that imparts a blue or bluish color to something, especially as part of treating materials like steel or fabrics
process of making something blue or bluish

Examples

The gunsmith applied bluing to the steel to prevent rust.
She added bluing to the wash to make her white sheets brighter.
The bluing process gives the metal a distinctive bluish hue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueing - alternative spelling of 'bluing', especially in laundry context

2. NOUN

/ ˈbluːɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbluːɪŋ / (BrE)
a blue substance added to laundry or hair to whiten it or give it a bluish tinge, especially to counteract yellowing
blue substance for whitening laundry or hair

Examples

My grandmother always added a little bluing to the wash to keep the sheets looking bright.
The hairdresser used bluing to help neutralize the yellow tones in her client's hair.
Some old-fashioned laundries still rely on bluing to maintain crisp, white linens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ultramarine - deep blue pigment used as coloring

Etymology

From the verb 'blue' (to make blue) + -ing.
