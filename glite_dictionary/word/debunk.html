to debunk

1. VERB

/ diˈbʌŋk / (AmE), / dɪˈbʌŋk / (BrE)
to show that an idea, belief, or claim is false or exaggerated, especially by exposing its pretensions or errors
expose as false or exaggerated

Examples

Several journalists worked to debunk the conspiracy theory.
The documentary aimed to debunk myths about health and nutrition.
She debunked the viral rumor with clear evidence.

Synset Examples

The physicist debunked the psychic's claims

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expose - to reveal something hidden or dishonest
  • Synonym: to unmask - to reveal the true character or nature of someone or something
  • Antonym: to validate - to confirm or support the truth of something

Etymology

From de- (remove) + 'bunk' (nonsense), American slang; first used in the 1920s.
