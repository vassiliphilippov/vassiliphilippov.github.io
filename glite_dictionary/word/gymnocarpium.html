Gymnocarpium

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪm.noʊˈkɑːr.pi.əm / (AmE), / ˌdʒɪm.nəˈkɑː.pi.əm / (BrE)
A genus of ferns commonly known as oak ferns, found in temperate regions and sometimes classified in the genus Thelypteris.
genus of ferns (oak ferns)

Examples

The Gymnocarpium fern thrives in shaded woodland areas.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the genus Gymnocarpium.
Because of its delicate fronds, Gymnocarpium is a popular choice for shade gardens.

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) and 'karpos' (fruit), referring to its exposed spores.
