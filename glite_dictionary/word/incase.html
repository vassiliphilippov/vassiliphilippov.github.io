to incase

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkeɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkeɪs / (BrE)
to enclose in, or as if in, a case or covering
to enclose in a case or covering

Examples

The delicate statue was carefully incased in bubble wrap for shipping.
Overnight, the trees were incased in a layer of shimmering ice.
Workers began incasing the electrical wires in protective tubing.

Synset Examples

my feet were encased in mud

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encase - to enclose or cover in a case or sheath

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'in-' and 'case', in the sense of container or covering.
