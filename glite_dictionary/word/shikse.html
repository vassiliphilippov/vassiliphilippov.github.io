shikse

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪk.sə / (AmE), / ˈʃɪk.sə / (BrE)
A derogatory term, used especially by Jews, to refer to a non-Jewish woman or girl.
derogatory term for non-Jewish woman
offensive

Examples

Some families disapproved when he began dating a shikse from outside their community.
In the novel, she was often called a shikse behind her back because of her different background.
The term shikse is considered offensive and derogatory in contemporary usage.

Etymology

From Yiddish שיקסע (shikse), from Hebrew שקץ (sheqets, meaning 'abomination, impurity').
