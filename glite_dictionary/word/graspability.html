graspability

1. NOUN

/ ɡræs.pəˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs.pəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of being easy to understand or comprehend; understandability.
ease of being understood

Examples

The graspability of complex theories often determines how widely they are accepted in the scientific community.
Clear diagrams can greatly improve the graspability of technical instructions for users.
She worked on enhancing the graspability of her presentation by using simple language and visual examples.

Synset Examples

So connected to the difference in the graspability of the risks is a potential inequality in access to information about them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: understandability - the quality of being able to be understood

Etymology

From 'grasp' (to seize, understand) + '-ability' (forming nouns meaning ability or capacity).
