parish

1. NOUN

/ ˈpærɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpærɪʃ / (BrE)
A local community of people organized around a particular church, especially in the Christian faith, often forming the lowest administrative unit in church governance.
local church community

Examples

The annual festival brought together the entire parish for a day of music and food.
She volunteers at the parish to help organize community events and outreach programs.
The old parish church has stood in the village for centuries.

Etymology

From Old French *paroisse*, from Late Latin *parochia* (parish, diocese).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpærɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpærɪʃ / (BrE)
A local church community that forms a subdivision of a diocese, typically under the care of a single priest or pastor.
local church district under a pastor

Examples

The small village parish held annual community events at the church.
After moving, they joined a new parish closer to their home.
The parish raised funds to repair the church roof.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: diocese - the district under the care of a bishop in the Christian Church

Etymology

From Old French *paroche*, from Late Latin *parochia* (parish, diocese), from Greek *paroikia* (sojourning in a foreign land).
