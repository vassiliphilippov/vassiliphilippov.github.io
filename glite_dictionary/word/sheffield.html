Sheffield

1. PROPN

/ ˈʃɛfiˌɛld / (AmE), / ˈʃɛfiːld / (BrE)
a city in northern England known for its steel manufacturing and cutlery industry
city in northern England, steel manufacturing

Examples

Sheffield became world-famous in the 19th century for its production of steel and cutlery.
After graduating from university, she moved to Sheffield to work in the engineering sector.
Many music lovers know Sheffield as the birthplace of several influential bands.

Etymology

From Old English 'Sheaf field' referring to a clearing where sheaves of wheat were piled; now the name of the city.
