canulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæn.jəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkan.juˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of inserting a cannula or tube into a hollow organ or bodily cavity, especially for medical purposes
insertion of a tube into a body organ
medical

Examples

The nurse performed canulation of the patient's vein before administering fluids.
Accurate canulation is essential to avoid complications during surgery.
The doctor explained the risks associated with canulation to the family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannulation - insertion of a cannula or tube into a body cavity

Etymology

From French 'canuler' (to insert a tube) and Latin 'canna' (reed, tube).
