gyro

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪˌroʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪərəʊ / (BrE)
a device consisting of a spinning wheel or disc mounted so that its axis can turn freely in any direction, used to maintain orientation or stability by resisting changes in direction
device for maintaining orientation; gyroscope

Examples

The ship's autopilot system relied on a gyro to maintain a steady course.
Engineers installed a gyro in the drone to improve its stability during flight.
If the gyro malfunctions, the navigation instruments may give inaccurate readings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gyroscope - device maintaining orientation using spinning wheel

Etymology

Shortened form of 'gyroscope', from Greek 'gyros' meaning 'circle'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪroʊ / (AmE), / ˈjɪərəʊ / (BrE)
A Greek sandwich consisting of sliced roast meat, onion, and tomato, usually served in pita bread.
Greek sandwich with meat in pita bread

Examples

I ordered a gyro with extra tzatziki sauce for lunch.
The restaurant serves the best lamb gyro in town.
She tried making a homemade gyro, complete with pita and fresh vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shawarma - Middle Eastern sandwich of roasted meat in flatbread
  • Synonym: doner kebab - Turkish dish of grilled meat in pita or similar bread

Etymology

From Modern Greek γύρος (gýros), meaning 'turn', referring to the rotating method of cooking the meat.
