orientation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔriənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːrɪənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a person's awareness of their position in relation to time, place, and personal relationships
awareness of position in time, place, and relationships
medical

Examples

The doctor checked the patient's orientation by asking the date and location.
After the accident, she suffered from a temporary loss of orientation.
Nurses assess a patient's orientation during initial examinations.

Etymology

From Latin 'orientatio' (arrangement, direction) via Medieval Latin.
