denominator

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈnɑm.əˌneɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈnɒm.ɪ.neɪ.tər / (BrE)
the number below the line in a fraction, which shows how many equal parts the whole is divided into
the divisor in a fraction

Examples

In the fraction 3/4, the denominator is 4.
Always make sure the denominator is not zero when solving equations.
The teacher asked her to identify both the numerator and the denominator of the fraction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: numerator - the top number in a fraction, representing the number of parts taken

Etymology

From Late Latin denominator, from Latin denominare (to name).
