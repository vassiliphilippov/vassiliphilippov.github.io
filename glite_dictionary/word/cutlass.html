cutlass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌt.ləs / (AmE), / ˈkʌt.ləs / (BrE)
a short, heavy, curved sword with a single edge, traditionally used by sailors
short heavy curved sword, used by sailors

Examples

The pirate swung his cutlass above his head as he advanced on the crew.
During the battle, several sailors fought fiercely with only a cutlass for protection.
At the museum, we saw a display of an eighteenth-century naval cutlass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sabre - a heavy, curved sword with a single edge, used especially by cavalry
  • Antonym: rapier - a thin, light, sharp-pointed sword, used for thrusting

Etymology

From French 'coutelas', from Old Italian 'coltellaccio', augmentative of 'coltello' (knife), from Latin 'cultellus'.
