dopa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊpə / (AmE), / ˈdəʊpə / (BrE)
an amino acid produced in the body, especially in the liver, and converted into dopamine in the brain
amino acid converted to dopamine
medical

Examples

Doctors sometimes prescribe dopa to treat certain neurological conditions, such as Parkinson's disease.
Dopa is converted into dopamine, which plays a crucial role in transmitting signals in the brain.
Scientists study the effects of dopa on motor functions and mood disorders.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dopamine - a neurotransmitter derived from dopa

Etymology

Shortened from 'dihydroxyphenylalanine', the full name of the compound.
