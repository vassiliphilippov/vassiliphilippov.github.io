semi-erect

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmi ɪˈrɛkt / (AmE), / ˌsɛmi ɪˈrɛkt / (BrE)
(of a plant) partly upright in position, neither fully erect nor completely prostrate.
partly upright (of plants)

Examples

The semi-erect stems allowed the plant to thrive even in high winds.
Gardeners often choose semi-erect varieties for better fruit access and space management.
This tomato cultivar grows as a semi-erect bush, making it ideal for small gardens.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prostrate - lying flat on the ground (of plants)
  • Degree Higher: erect - upright in position (of plants)

Etymology

From Latin 'semi-' (half) and 'erectus' (upright).
