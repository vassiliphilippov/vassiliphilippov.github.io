quatrain

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑː.treɪn / (AmE), / ˈkwɒ.treɪn / (BrE)
A stanza or poem consisting of four lines, usually with a specific rhyme scheme.
four-line stanza in poetry

Examples

The poet used a quatrain to open her most famous sonnet.
Many nursery rhymes are written in the form of a quatrain with a simple rhyme scheme.
Each quatrain in the poem presents a different image of spring.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: couplet - a stanza of two lines
  • Degree Lower: tercet - a stanza of three lines
  • Degree Higher: cinquain - a stanza of five lines

Etymology

From French 'quatrain', from 'quatre' (four), from Latin 'quattuor'.
