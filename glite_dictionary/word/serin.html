serin

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɛr.ɪn / (BrE)
a small brown and yellow finch native to parts of Europe and adjoining regions
a small European finch

Examples

A bright yellow serin perched on the highest branch, singing loudly at dawn.
Many birdwatchers travel to southern Europe just to spot the elusive serin.
The serins share the same habitat as other small finches near Mediterranean forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finch - a small songbird of the family Fringillidae

Etymology

From French *serin*, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old Spanish *serín*.
