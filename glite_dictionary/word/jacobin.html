Jacobin

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækəbɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒækəʊbɪn / (BrE)
a member of the radical political group that supported the Reign of Terror during the French Revolution
radical French revolutionary group member

Examples

During the French Revolution, Robespierre became a leading Jacobin and played a key role in the Reign of Terror.
Historians often debate the impact of the Jacobins on the course of European democracy.
The term Jacobin is sometimes used today to describe someone with radical political views.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Girondist - moderate republican during the French Revolution; opponent of the Jacobins

Etymology

From French 'Jacobin', originally denoting a Dominican friar, later applied to the revolutionary club meeting in the Jacobin convent in Paris.
