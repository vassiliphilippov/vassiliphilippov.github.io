physostigmine

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪsoʊˈstɪɡmin / (AmE), / ˌfɪsəʊˈstɪɡmiːn / (BrE)
a drug used to treat glaucoma and Alzheimer's disease by inhibiting the breakdown of acetylcholine
drug for glaucoma and Alzheimer's disease
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed physostigmine to help manage her glaucoma symptoms.
Treatment with physostigmine can temporarily improve memory in some Alzheimer's patients.
Researchers are studying how physostigmine affects neurotransmitter levels in the brain.

Etymology

From Physostigma (the plant genus), and -ine (chemical suffix), as it was first obtained from the Calabar bean.
