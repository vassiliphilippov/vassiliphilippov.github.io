Raphanus sativus

1. NOUN

/ ræˈfeɪnəs səˈtɪvəs / (AmE), / rəˈfeɪnəs səˈtɪvəs / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant widely cultivated for its edible, pungent root, usually eaten raw as a vegetable.
species of radish plant

Examples

The crisp root of Raphanus sativus is a refreshing addition to summer salads.
Farmers rotate crops like Raphanus sativus to improve soil quality and reduce pests.
Botanists have documented several varieties of Raphanus sativus cultivated worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radish - A crisp, pungent root vegetable commonly eaten raw

Etymology

From Latin: 'Raphanus' (radish) and 'sativus' (cultivated).
