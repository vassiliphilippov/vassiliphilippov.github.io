Pimenta officinalis

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈmɛntə əˌfɪsəˈnaɪlɪs / (AmE), / pɪˈmɛntə əˌfɪsɪˈneɪlɪs / (BrE)
A tropical American tree with small white flowers and aromatic berries, also known as allspice.
tropical American tree (allspice)

Examples

The leaves of Pimenta officinalis are often used in Caribbean cooking.
The aromatic berries of Pimenta officinalis are dried to make allspice.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Pimenta officinalis in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allspice - common name for Pimenta officinalis, a spice from its berries

Etymology

From Latin 'pimenta' (pepper-like) and 'officinalis' (used in medicine or herbalism).
