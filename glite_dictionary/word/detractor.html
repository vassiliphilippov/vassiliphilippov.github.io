detractor

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtræktər / (AmE), / dɪˈtræktə / (BrE)
A person who criticizes or belittles the worth or value of someone or something.
person who belittles or criticizes

Examples

Despite the detractors, her novel became a bestseller.
Every public figure has at least one detractor trying to undermine their achievements.
Although he had many admirers, a vocal detractor questioned his integrity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critic - person who expresses unfavorable opinions
  • Antonym: supporter - person who supports or defends

Etymology

From Latin 'detract-', past participle stem of 'detractare' (to pull down).
