maravilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːrəˈviːjə / (AmE), / ˌmarəˈviːjə / (BrE)
A leafy wildflower with fragrant, slender, white or pale pink trumpet-shaped flowers, native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.
wildflower native to North America

Examples

The delicate scent of the maravilla filled the morning air after the rain.
Many hikers in Arizona stop to admire the blooming maravilla along the desert trails.
Botanists study the maravilla for its unique trumpet-shaped flowers and resilience in dry climates.

Etymology

From Spanish 'maravilla' (meaning 'marvel' or 'wonder'), used as a folk or regional name for the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːrɑːˈviːjɑː / (AmE), / ˌmarəˈviːjə / (BrE)
A wildflower with vibrant deep pink tubular evening-blooming flowers, found in sandy and desert areas from southern California to southern Colorado and into Mexico.
desert wildflower with pink evening-blooming flowers

Examples

The maravilla blooms just after sunset, filling the desert air with its scent.
Botanists traveled to southern California to document the rare maravilla species.
Clusters of maravilla added a splash of color to the sandy landscape.

Etymology

From Spanish 'maravilla' meaning 'marvel' or 'wonder'.
