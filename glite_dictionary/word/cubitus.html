cubitus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːbɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈkjuːbɪtəs / (BrE)
the elbow joint, especially as referred to in anatomical or zoological contexts; the hinge joint between the forearm and upper arm, or the corresponding joint in a quadruped's forelimb
elbow joint (anatomical term)
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the patient's cubitus for signs of injury.
In some mammals, the cubitus is adapted for digging or running.
Swelling around the cubitus may indicate inflammation or trauma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elbow - the joint between the upper and lower parts of the human arm

Etymology

From Latin 'cubitus' (elbow).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːbɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈkjuːbɪtəs / (BrE)
the part of the human arm from the elbow to the fingertips; the forearm
the forearm (elbow to fingertips)
medical

Examples

The fracture occurred along the length of the cubitus after a fall.
Muscles that control finger movement originate in the cubitus.
Swelling was most prominent in the cubitus after the injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forearm - the part of the arm between the elbow and the wrist

Etymology

From Latin 'cubitus' (elbow, forearm).
