capaciousness

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpeɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / kəˈpeɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having a wide intellectual scope; the ability to consider or understand a broad range of ideas and viewpoints
intellectual breadth or spaciousness

Examples

Her capaciousness of mind allowed her to appreciate new scientific theories from a variety of disciplines.
The university values capaciousness in its faculty, encouraging open-mindedness and interdisciplinary study.
He admired the capaciousness of her intellectual interests, which ranged from philosophy to engineering.

Synset Examples

the very capaciousness of the idea meant that agreement on fundamentals was unnecessary
his unselfishness gave him great intellectual roominess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roominess - the quality of having a lot of space; spaciousness (used both literally and metaphorically)
  • Antonym: narrowness - the quality of being limited in extent, scope, or understanding

Etymology

From Latin capax (capable of holding much), via Late Latin capacitas (capacity), through English 'capacious'.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈpeɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / kəˈpeɪʃəsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being spacious and able to contain a large amount; spaciousness or roominess
spaciousness; roominess

Examples

The capaciousness of the new gallery impressed visitors from around the world.
We admired the capaciousness of the hotel lobby when we first arrived.
The architect aimed for capaciousness in designing the open-plan living area.

Synset Examples

the capaciousness of Santa's bag astounded the child
roominess in this size car is always a compromise
his new office lacked the spaciousness that he had become accustomed to

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spaciousness - the quality of being large and open in space
  • Synonym: roominess - the state of having plenty of space or room

Etymology

From capacious + -ness; ultimately from Latin 'capax' (capable of holding much).
