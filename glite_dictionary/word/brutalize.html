to brutalize

1. VERB

/ ˈbruːtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbruːtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something cruel, violent, or insensitive to suffering
make cruel or inhuman

Examples

Years of war had brutalized the soldiers, leaving them numb to suffering.
Exposure to constant violence can brutalize even the most compassionate individuals.
The harsh conditions of the prison brutalized many of its inmates.

Synset Examples

Life in the camps had brutalized him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dehumanize - to deprive of human qualities or dignity
  • Antonym: to civilize - to bring out of a savage or unrefined state

Etymology

From 'brutal' + '-ize'; 'brutal' from French 'brutal' or Late Latin 'brutalis' (beast-like).

2. VERB

/ ˈbruːtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbruːtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make or become cruel, violent, or insensitive to the feelings of others
become cruel or insensitive

Examples

Media violence can brutalize audiences, making them less sensitive to real suffering.
Years of war had brutalized the once peaceful population.
Some fear that constant exposure to tragedy will brutalize young people emotionally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harden - to become or make less sensitive or more unfeeling
  • Antonym: to sensitize - to become or make more sensitive

Etymology

From 'brutal' + '-ize', modelled after French 'brutaliser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00113661-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈbruːtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbruːtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to treat someone with great cruelty and violence; to make someone cruel, violent, or insensitive by repeated exposure to brutality
treat with cruelty or violence

Examples

The guards were accused of having brutalized the prisoners during their captivity.
Violent video games can sometimes brutalize young people, making them less sensitive to real-life suffering.
Years of war had brutalized the population, leaving deep emotional scars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to maltreat - to treat someone cruelly or unfairly
  • Synonym: abuse - to treat with cruelty or violence, especially regularly or repeatedly
  • Antonym: to protect - to keep someone safe from harm or injury

Etymology

From 'brutal' + '-ize', influenced by Latin brutus (dull, stupid) and French brutaliser.
