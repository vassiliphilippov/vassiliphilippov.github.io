chanar

1. NOUN

/ tʃɑːˈnɑr / (AmE), / tʃɑːˈnɑː / (BrE)
A thorny shrub or small tree native to central Argentina, producing small orange or yellow flowers and edible berries.
Argentinian thorny shrub with edible berries

Examples

The chanar produces sweet berries that are often used in local jams.
Farmers in central Argentina value the chanar for its drought resistance.
A grove of chanar trees provided welcome shade during our hike.

Etymology

From Spanish ‘chañar’, from indigenous languages of South America.
