Meriwether Lewis

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛrɪˌwɛðər ˈluːɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪˌwɛðə ˈluːɪs / (BrE)
(1774–1809) An American explorer and soldier, best known as the leader of the Lewis and Clark Expedition which explored the land west of the Mississippi River after the Louisiana Purchase.
American explorer; leader of the Lewis and Clark Expedition

Examples

Meriwether Lewis was appointed by President Thomas Jefferson to lead the Corps of Discovery.
The remarkable journey undertaken by Meriwether Lewis and William Clark helped map the western territories of the United States.
Many places in the U.S. are named after Meriwether Lewis in honor of his contributions as an explorer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: William Clark - American explorer; co-leader of the Lewis and Clark Expedition
