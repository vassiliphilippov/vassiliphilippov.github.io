diffused

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfjuzd / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːzd / (BrE)
(of light) spread out and transmitted from a broad source or reflected, so that it is not concentrated in one direction.
light spread out from a broad source

Examples

The soft glow in the room came from diffused sunlight through the curtains.
Photographers often prefer diffused lighting to avoid harsh shadows.
The glass ceiling produced a pleasant, diffused effect throughout the atrium.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: direct - (of light) concentrated and coming from a single source or in a straight line

Etymology

From Latin 'diffundere' meaning 'to pour in different directions'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01161394-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈfjuzd / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːzd / (BrE)
(of light) spread out and scattered, especially after passing through a translucent material or reflecting off a rough surface.
light that is scattered and spread out

Examples

The photographer preferred diffused light for taking portraits, as it reduced harsh shadows.
Cloudy days provide diffused sunlight, creating a softer atmosphere indoors.
A lampshade helps to create diffused lighting in the room.

Synset Examples

diffused light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scattered - (of light) deflected and dispersed randomly

Etymology

From Latin diffusus, past participle of diffundere, meaning 'to pour out'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00542852-s
Synset POS: s
