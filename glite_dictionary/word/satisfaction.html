satisfaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæt̬ɪsˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌsætɪsˈfækʃən / (BrE)
a pleasant feeling that you get when you have achieved something or fulfilled a desire, need, or expectation
pleasure from fulfilling a need or desire

Examples

She smiled with satisfaction as she saw her exam results.
Completing the marathon gave him a deep sense of satisfaction.
The company aims to improve customer satisfaction with its new service policy.

Synset Examples

the chef tasted the sauce with great satisfaction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contentment - a state of happiness and satisfaction
  • Antonym: dissatisfaction - a feeling of not being pleased or content

Etymology

From Latin satisfactio (a making enough, reparation), from satisfacere (to satisfy, to do enough).
