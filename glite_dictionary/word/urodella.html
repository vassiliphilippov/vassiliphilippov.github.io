Urodella

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊrəˈdɛlə / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəˈdɛlə / (BrE)
An order of amphibians that includes salamanders, newts, and allied species characterized by a tail throughout life.
order of amphibians (salamanders, newts)

Examples

The Urodella are distinguished from other amphibians by their slender bodies and long tails.
Modern studies have greatly expanded our knowledge of Urodella in North America.
Many species in the Urodella order are sensitive to changes in their aquatic environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caudata - Alternative and more widely used name for the order of amphibians including salamanders and newts

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek oura (tail) + delos (visible), referring to their prominent tails.
