Cestrum diurnum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛstrəm daɪˈɜːrnəm / (AmE), / ˈsɛstrəm daɪˈɜːnəm / (BrE)
A West Indian evergreen shrub with clusters of funnel-shaped white flowers that are fragrant during the day.
West Indian evergreen shrub with fragrant white flowers

Examples

The garden featured Cestrum diurnum blooming next to the bamboo grove.
Many gardeners appreciate Cestrum diurnum for its strong daytime fragrance.
Cestrum diurnum is sometimes called 'day-blooming jessamine' due to its scent.

Etymology

From Latin genus name Cestrum and Latin diurnum meaning 'of the day'.
