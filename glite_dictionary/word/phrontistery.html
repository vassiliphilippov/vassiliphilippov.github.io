phrontistery

1. NOUN

/ frɒnˈtɪstəri / (AmE), / frɒnˈtɪstəri / (BrE)
A place or institution dedicated to study and learning; sometimes used to refer to a university or academic establishment.
place of study or learning

Examples

The ancient philosopher founded a phrontistery where students discussed science and ethics.
Modern universities can be viewed as a kind of phrontistery for advanced learning.
The city council proposed building a new phrontistery to encourage academic research.

Etymology

From Greek phrontistērion (a place for thinking or study), from phrontistes (a thinker).
