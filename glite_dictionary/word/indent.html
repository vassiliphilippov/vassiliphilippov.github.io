indent

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌdɛnt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dɛnt / (BrE)
the blank space at the beginning of a printed or written line, created by moving it inward from the margin, typically to signal a new paragraph or section
space at the start of a line (text formatting)

Examples

Please remember to add an indent at the beginning of each paragraph.
The indent is used to visually separate sections in the document.
She adjusted the indent so that all lines aligned properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indentation - the action of indenting or the resulting space

Etymology

From Latin 'indenta-', past participle of 'indetare' (to notch).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈdɛnt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛnt / (BrE)

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛnt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛnt / (BrE)
an official order for goods to be exported or imported, typically in written form
order for goods to be imported or exported

Examples

The company placed an indent for new machinery with the overseas supplier.
Due to shipping delays, the indent for textiles arrived later than expected.
All indents must be approved by the purchasing manager before goods are imported.

Etymology

From Latin 'indenta', via French 'indenter' or Middle English 'indente', influenced by the physical 'indented' (notched) contracts.
