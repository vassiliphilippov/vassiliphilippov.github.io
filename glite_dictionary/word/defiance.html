defiance

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfaɪəns / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪəns / (BrE)
open resistance or bold disobedience; a refusal to obey authority or follow orders, often in a bold or challenging manner
open resistance; bold disobedience

Examples

Her defiance of the rules inspired others to speak up for themselves.
The look of defiance on his face angered the teacher.
In an act of defiance, the protesters refused to leave the premises.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obedience - compliance with orders or requests; the antonym of 'defiance'

Etymology

From Old French *défiance* (challenge), from defier (to defy).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈfaɪəns / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪəns / (BrE)
an act of openly resisting or challenging authority or rules
an act of resistance or challenge

Examples

His refusal to apologize was an act of defiance against the rules.
The students wore black armbands in defiance of the school ban.
Her defiance in the face of the authorities inspired others to protest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebellion - open resistance to authority
  • Antonym: obedience - compliance with an order or request

Etymology

From Old French 'desfiance' (challenge), from 'desfier' (to challenge), based on Latin 'dis-' (reversal) + 'fidare' (to trust).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈfaɪəns / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪəns / (BrE)
open resistance or bold disobedience; a hostile challenge to authority.
open resistance; hostile challenge

Examples

His defiance of the school rules landed him in detention.
She stared back at her accuser in open defiance.
The protestors acted in defiance of the new law.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obedience - compliance with an order or law
  • Synonym: rebellion - open resistance to authority

Etymology

From Old French 'defiance' (challenge, declaration of war), from 'desfier' (to challenge), from Latin 'dis-' + 'fidere' (to trust).
