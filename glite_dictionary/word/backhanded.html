backhanded

1. ADJ

/ ˈbækˌhændɪd / (AmE), / ˈbækˌhændɪd / (BrE)
(of a racket stroke) made with the back of the hand facing in the direction of the stroke, typically moving across the body.
racket stroke with back of hand facing

Examples

Her backhanded shot sailed across the net with surprising speed.
The player relied on a strong backhanded return to win the rally.
He practiced the backhanded stroke for hours to improve his tennis game.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forehanded - (of a racket stroke) performed with the palm of the hand facing forward

Etymology

From 'backhand' (the back of the hand); used in sports terminology.

2. ADJ

/ ˌbækˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌbækˈhændɪd / (BrE)
(of a remark or action) indirect, ambiguous, or having a hidden or double meaning, often with an element of insincerity or subtle criticism.
indirect or ambiguous; subtly critical

Examples

His backhanded apology left me unsure whether he was really sorry.
She offered a backhanded compliment that felt more insulting than flattering.
The politician's backhanded remarks confused his audience.

Synset Examples

attacks from that source amounted to a backhanded compliment to his integrity
a backhanded and dishonest way of reaching his goal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insincere - not expressing genuine feelings; not sincere
  • Antonym: direct - straightforward and unambiguous
