Boeotia

1. PROPN

/ biˈoʊʃə / (AmE), / biˈəʊʃə / (BrE)
an ancient district in central Greece, northwest of Athens, known for its historical and cultural significance in Greek antiquity
ancient district in central Greece

Examples

In ancient times, Boeotia was renowned for its city of Thebes and many legendary battles.
Historians often study the influence of Boeotia on the politics of classical Greece.
The fertile plains of Boeotia were crucial for agriculture in the region.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Βοιωτία (Boiōtía), named after the mythical figure Boeotus.
