consecration

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑnsɪˈkreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsɪˈkreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something sacred or dedicating it formally to a religious purpose, often through a special ceremony
the act of making something sacred

Examples

The consecration of the new temple drew worshippers from all over the country.
Following its consecration, the altar became the focal point of the church.
The bishop presided over the consecration ceremony last Sunday.

Synset Examples

the Cardinal attended the consecration of the church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctification - the process of making or declaring something holy
  • Antonym: desecration - the act of treating something sacred with disrespect

Etymology

From Latin 'consecratio', from 'consecrare' (to make sacred).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnsɪˈkreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsɪˈkreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of formally dedicating your life, time, or efforts to a valued purpose or goal
solemn dedication to a purpose or goal

Examples

Her consecration to scientific research inspired everyone in the laboratory.
The artist's consecration to his craft led to a lifetime of remarkable achievements.
He regarded his consecration to social service as his greatest source of fulfillment.

Synset Examples

his consecration to study

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dedication - the act of devoting time or effort to a particular task or purpose

Etymology

From Latin consecratio (the action of making sacred), from consecrare (to make sacred).
