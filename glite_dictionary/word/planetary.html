planetary

1. ADJ

/ ˈplænəˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈplænɪtəri / (BrE)
relating to the planet Earth or its inhabitants; terrestrial or earthly.
relating to Earth or its inhabitants

Examples

Climate change poses a significant planetary challenge that affects all nations.
The scientists discussed the planetary consequences of deforestation at the conference.
Our planetary resources are not limitless and must be managed wisely.

Synset Examples

planetary rumblings and eructations
the planetary tilt
this terrestrial ball

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrestrial - relating to the Earth; earthly

Etymology

From Latin 'planetarius,' from 'planeta' (planet).

2. ADJ

/ ˈplæn.ə.ˌtɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈplæn.ɪ.tər.i / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a planet or the planets, especially in terms of their physical nature or motion
of or relating to planets

Examples

Scientists study planetary motion to understand how planets orbit the sun.
The mission's goal is to analyze the planetary atmosphere for signs of life.
Jupiter's massive storms are unique among planetary weather systems in our solar system.

Synset Examples

planetary motion
planetary year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planetary motion - the movement of planets in space
  • Synonym: planetary system - a system of planets orbiting a star

Etymology

From Latin planetarius (relating to planets), from planeta (planet).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02789475-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈplænəˌter.i / (AmE), / ˈplæn.ɪ.tər.i / (BrE)
Relating to or affecting the whole world; global in scope, not limited to a particular region.
affecting the whole world; global

Examples

Recent advances in technology have led to planetary changes in communication and information exchange.
The climate crisis is a planetary issue that requires cooperation from every nation.
Our organization focuses on planetary health, studying how human activity affects the earth as a whole.

Synset Examples

global war
global monetary policy
neither national nor continental but planetary
a world crisis
of worldwide significance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: global - relating to or involving the entire world
  • Antonym: local - relating to a particular place; not global

Etymology

From Latin planeta (planet), via Old French planetaire.
