to disk-jockey

1. VERB

/ ˈdɪsk ˌdʒɑki / (AmE), / ˈdɪsk ˌdʒɒki / (BrE)
to act as a disk jockey by selecting, playing, and often commenting on music for an audience
to act as a disk jockey (DJ)

Examples

He started to disk-jockey at local clubs during his college years.
She loves to disk-jockey for birthday parties and small events around town.
After gaining experience on the radio, he began disk-jockeying at popular nightclubs.

Synset Examples

He has a job disk-jockeying on the weekend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DJ - to perform as a DJ

Etymology

From 'disk jockey', modelled on existing noun-verb conversions in English.
