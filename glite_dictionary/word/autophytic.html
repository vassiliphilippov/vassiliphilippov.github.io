autophytic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔːtəˈfɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈfɪtɪk / (BrE)
(biology) relating to organisms that produce complex organic substances from simple inorganic materials, especially by photosynthesis.
able to produce organic compounds from inorganic materials

Examples

Many algae are autophytic and contribute significantly to oxygen production through photosynthesis.
The autophytic ability of green plants allows them to survive in nutrient-poor environments.
Some bacteria display autophytic nutrition, making them vital in ecological cycles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autotrophic - able to synthesize nutrients from inorganic substances; self-nourishing
  • Antonym: heterophytic - obtaining nutrition from organic sources, not through photosynthesis

Etymology

From Greek 'auto-' (self) + 'phyton' (plant).
