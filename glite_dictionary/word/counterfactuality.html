counterfactuality

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaʊntərˌfæktʃuˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˌfæktʃuˈælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being contrary to fact, especially in reference to statements or situations that did not actually occur
the state of being contrary to fact

Examples

The counterfactuality of his claim made the argument invalid.
Researchers debated the counterfactuality of the proposed historical scenario.
Understanding counterfactuality is crucial for studying conditional statements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterfactual - contrary to fact; expressing what could have happened

Etymology

Formed from 'counterfactual' (from Latin 'counter' + 'factus', past participle of 'facere', to do) + '-ity'.
