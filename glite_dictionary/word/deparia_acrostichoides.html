Deparia acrostichoides

1. NOUN

/ dəˈpɛəriə ˌækrəˌstɪˈkɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / dɪˈpɑːrɪə ˌækrəsˌtɪˈkɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of fern with elongate, silvery outgrowths that enclose the developing spores, commonly known as silvery spleenwort.
species of fern with silvery outgrowths

Examples

We spotted a patch of Deparia acrostichoides growing along the shady stream bank.
The silvery undersides of Deparia acrostichoides make it easy to distinguish from other ferns.
Botanists often study Deparia acrostichoides for its unique spore-covered fronds.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Deparia' is the genus, and 'acrostichoides' means 'resembling Acrostichum', another fern genus.
