mammal

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæməl / (AmE), / ˈmaməl / (BrE)
A warm-blooded vertebrate animal whose body is covered with hair or fur, and whose females secrete milk to nourish their young.
warm-blooded animal with hair, produces milk

Examples

A dolphin is a mammal, not a fish, because it breathes air and nurses its young.
Humans are classified as mammals because females have mammary glands that produce milk.
The zoo has a new exhibit featuring rare mammal species from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reptile - cold-blooded vertebrate with scales
  • Antonym: bird - warm-blooded egg-laying vertebrate with feathers

Etymology

From Latin 'mammalis', from 'mamma' meaning 'breast'.
