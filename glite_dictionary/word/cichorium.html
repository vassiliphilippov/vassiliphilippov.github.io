Cichorium

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkɔriəm / (AmE), / sɪˈkɔːrɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, including chicory and endive, often cultivated for their edible leaves and roots.
genus of plants including chicory and endive

Examples

The genus Cichorium includes several species cultivated for their edible parts.
Many salads contain leaves from Cichorium varieties such as endive or radicchio.
Botanists compared the root structure of Cichorium with other related genera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicory - a blue-flowered plant of the daisy family, grown for its leaves and roots

Etymology

From Latin *cichorium*, from Greek *kikhórion* (chicory).
