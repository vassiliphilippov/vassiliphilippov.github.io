Opisthognathidae

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌpɪsθoʊɡˈnæθɪˌdi / (AmE), / əʊˌpɪsθɒɡˈneɪθɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes, commonly known as jawfishes, characterized by their large mouths and burrowing behavior.
family of jawfishes (fish)

Examples

The Opisthognathidae are easily identified by their exceptionally large mouths.
Members of the Opisthognathidae family construct elaborate burrows in sandy ocean floors.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Opisthognathidae near the coral reefs.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'opistho-' (behind) and 'gnathos' (jaw), referring to the jaw structure.
