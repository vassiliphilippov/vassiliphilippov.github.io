burgoo

1. NOUN

/ bɜrˈɡu / (AmE), / bɜːˈɡuː / (BrE)
a porridge made from rolled oats, traditionally eaten by sailors or in some regional cuisines
oat porridge, historically for sailors

Examples

Sailors often began their day with a bowl of burgoo to keep them warm and satisfied.
She prepared a thick burgoo using rolled oats and a pinch of salt, just like her grandmother did.
At the historical reenactment, visitors could sample traditional burgoo served in wooden bowls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porridge - a dish of oats or other grains boiled in milk or water

Etymology

Origin unknown; possibly from nautical slang or dialectal English.

2. NOUN

/ bɝˈɡu / (AmE), / bɜːˈɡuː / (BrE)
a thick, spicy stew made from whatever meat and vegetables are available, originally popular in the southern United States
thick spicy stew of mixed meats and vegetables

Examples

Every year, the community hosts a festival where locals make huge pots of burgoo for everyone to enjoy.
She tried burgoo for the first time and was surprised by its spicy, hearty flavor.
The recipe for burgoo changes depending on whatever ingredients the cook has on hand.

Etymology

Probably from British naval slang, possibly of unknown origin or related to 'bulghur' or 'porridge.'

3. NOUN

/ bɝˈɡu / (AmE), / bɜːˈɡuː / (BrE)
A social gathering, especially in the southern United States, where a thick stew of meat and vegetables called burgoo is prepared and served.
social event where burgoo stew is served

Examples

Every summer, the town hosts a traditional burgoo that brings the community together.
The church organized a burgoo to raise funds for local charities.
We enjoyed homemade stew and music at the annual burgoo last Saturday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: picnic - an outing or occasion that involves taking a packed meal outdoors

Etymology

Probably from an alteration of the word 'ragout,' or possibly of unknown origin.
