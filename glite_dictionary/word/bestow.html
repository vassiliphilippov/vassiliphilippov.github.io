to bestow

1. VERB

/ bɪˈstoʊ / (AmE), / bɪˈstəʊ / (BrE)
to give something as an honor or gift; to present or confer
to give as an honor or gift

Examples

The queen decided to bestow the title of knight upon the young hero.
He was honored when the university chose to bestow an honorary degree on him.
The charity plans to bestow awards to those who have made significant contributions to the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grant - to give or allow someone something, usually in an official way
  • Synonym: award - to give something as an official payment, honor, or recognition
  • Antonym: to withhold - to refuse to give something that is expected or due

Etymology

From Middle English 'bestowen', from be- + 'stow' (to place, put).

2. VERB

/ bɪˈstoʊ / (AmE), / bɪˈstəʊ / (BrE)
to give or impart a quality, characteristic, or property to someone or something
impart a quality or characteristic

Examples

Her kindness bestows a sense of comfort on everyone she meets.
The old building bestows a charm on the neighborhood that new constructions lack.
His leadership bestowed confidence on the young team.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confer - to give (a title, property, or right) formally or officially
  • Synonym: grant - to give or bestow (a quality, right, or asset)

Etymology

From Middle English *bestowen*, from be- + stow (place).

3. VERB

/ bɪˈstoʊ / (AmE), / bɪˈstəʊ / (BrE)
to give or present something to someone, especially as an honor or gift
formally give or present (something)

Examples

The committee decided to bestow the award upon her for her outstanding service.
He felt honored when the king chose to bestow a knighthood on him.
Scholarships are bestowed each year on students who show exceptional promise.

Synset Examples

The university conferred a degree on its most famous former student, who never graduated
bestow an honor on someone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confer - to grant or bestow (a title, degree, benefit, or right)
  • Synonym: grant - to give or bestow (something official or formal)
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to take back or remove something that was given

Etymology

From Middle English 'bestowen', from be- + stow (place).
