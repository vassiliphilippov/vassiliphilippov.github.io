Woodsia alpina

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊd.zi.ə ælˈpaɪ.nə / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.zi.ə ælˈpiː.nə / (BrE)
a slender fern of northern North America, with shiny chestnut-colored stems and deeply divided fronds, typically found in rocky habitats
a North American alpine fern species

Examples

The rocky slopes were covered with Woodsia alpina thriving in the cool mountain air.
Botanists are studying the distribution of Woodsia alpina in the Canadian Rockies.
Gardeners prize Woodsia alpina for its delicate fronds and hardiness in shaded rock gardens.

Etymology

Genus name from English botanist Joseph Woods (1776–1864); species epithet from Latin 'alpinus' (of the Alps/mountains).
