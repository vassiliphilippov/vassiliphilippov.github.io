Cuon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuː.ɒn / (AmE), / ˈkjʊ.ɒn / (BrE)
a genus of wild dogs, including the Asiatic wild dog native to Asia
genus of Asiatic wild dogs

Examples

The Cuon is known for its complex social behavior and pack hunting.
Unlike other canids, the Cuon possesses a unique whistling call.
Researchers have observed the Cuon in remote parts of Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dhole - Asiatic wild dog, Cuon alpinus
  • Degree Higher: canid - member of the dog family, Canidae

Etymology

From Greek 'kuôn' (dog), used in scientific taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ kuˈɑn / (AmE), / kjuːˈɒn / (BrE)
A genus of wild dogs native to Asia, including the dhole.
genus of Asiatic wild dogs

Examples

The Cuon genus consists of wild dogs commonly known as dholes.
Researchers observed several Cuon pack members hunting cooperatively in the forest.
Unlike other canids, Cuon species have a unique social structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canis - genus including wolves, domestic dogs, and related animals
  • Synonym: dhole - wild dog of Asia, the sole species in genus Cuon

Etymology

From Greek kuōn (dog), used in scientific Latin nomenclature.
