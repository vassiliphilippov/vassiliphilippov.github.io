Catholicos

1. NOUN

/ kəˈθɑl.ɪˌkoʊs / (AmE), / kəˈθɒl.ɪ.kɒs / (BrE)
the chief bishop or primate in certain Eastern Christian churches, especially the Armenian and Nestorian Churches
head bishop in certain Eastern churches

Examples

The Catholicos presided over the religious ceremony attended by thousands of worshippers.
After being elected Catholicos, he undertook extensive reforms in church administration.
The authority of the Catholicos extends across the Armenian Apostolic Church.

Etymology

From Greek katholikós (universal), via Late Latin catholicus.
