Sitophylus

1. PROPN

/ saɪˈtɑfɪləs / (AmE), / sɪˈtɒfɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of beetles in the family Bruchidae, known for species that attack stored grains.
genus of grain beetles

Examples

Many species within the genus Sitophylus are significant pests in agriculture.
The Sitophylus beetles are closely monitored in grain storage facilities.
Researchers study Sitophylus to develop better pest control methods.

Etymology

From Greek 'sitos' (grain) and 'philos' (loving), referring to its association with grain.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈtɑfɪləs / (AmE), / sɪˈtɒfɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of beetles in the family Bruchidae, which includes several species known as grain or seed weevils.
a genus of beetles (Bruchidae)

Examples

The common rice weevil belongs to the genus Sitophylus, notorious for damaging stored grains.
Several species in Sitophylus are significant pests in agriculture worldwide.
Identification of Sitophylus beetles is important for managing grain storage facilities.

Etymology

From Greek 'sitos' (grain) and 'philos' (loving), referring to their habit of infesting stored grain.
