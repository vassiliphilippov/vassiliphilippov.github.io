acholia

1. NOUN

/ əˈkoʊliə / (AmE), / əˈkəʊliə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which little or no bile is secreted, or the flow of bile into the digestive tract is obstructed.
lack or obstruction of bile secretion
medical

Examples

The diagnosis of acholia explained the patient's persistent jaundice and pale stools.
Lack of bile pigments due to acholia can result in distinctive changes in stool color.
The physician noted signs of acholia during the examination of the liver disorder.

Etymology

From Greek a- (without) + chole (bile) + -ia (condition).
