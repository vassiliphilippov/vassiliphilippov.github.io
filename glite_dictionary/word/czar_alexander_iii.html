Czar Alexander III

1. PROPN

/ zɑr ˌæləgˈzændər ðə ˈθɜrd / (AmE), / zɑː ˌalɪgˈzændə ðə ˈθɜːd / (BrE)
the Emperor of Russia from 1881 to 1894, known for his conservative and repressive policies; son of Alexander II
Emperor of Russia (1881-1894)

Examples

During his reign, Czar Alexander III emphasized autocracy and suppressed revolutionary movements.
The policies introduced by Czar Alexander III greatly influenced Russian society in the late 19th century.
Historians debate whether Czar Alexander III's rule delayed or hastened the downfall of the Russian monarchy.
