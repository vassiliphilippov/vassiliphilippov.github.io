Adansonia digitata

1. NOUN

/ əˌdænˈsoʊniə ˌdɪdʒɪˈteɪtə / (AmE), / əˌdanˈsəʊnɪə ˌdɪdʒɪˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
An African tree with a very thick trunk, known as the baobab, which produces a gourd-like fruit with edible pulp called monkey bread.
African baobab tree with edible fruit

Examples

The Adansonia digitata is an iconic sight on the African savanna, with its massive trunk towering above the landscape.
Many communities use the fruit of the Adansonia digitata for its nutritious pulp, often called monkey bread.
Researchers are studying the Adansonia digitata to understand its resilience to drought and long lifespan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baobab - common name for Adansonia digitata, the African baobab tree

Etymology

From the genus name Adansonia (after French botanist Michel Adanson) and Latin digitata, meaning 'fingered', referring to the leaf shape.
