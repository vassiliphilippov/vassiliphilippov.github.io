Phlox stellaria

1. NOUN

/ floʊks stɛlˈɛɹiə / (AmE), / flɒks stɛˈlɑːriə / (BrE)
A low, mat-forming herb found in rocky places in the United States.
a low herbaceous plant of rocky U.S. habitats

Examples

The rocky slopes were covered with patches of Phlox stellaria in early spring.
Gardeners admire Phlox stellaria for its ability to form dense mats in challenging terrains.
During our hike, we spotted the delicate white flowers of Phlox stellaria among the stones.

Etymology

Phlox from Greek *phlox* (flame), referring to the plant's bright flowers; *stellaria* from Latin *stella* (star), for its star-shaped flowers.
