misconstrual

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪskənˈstruəl / (AmE), / ˌmɪskənˈstruːəl / (BrE)
a mistaken interpretation or understanding of words or actions; the act of interpreting something incorrectly
mistaken interpretation or understanding

Examples

Her statement was the subject of a major misconstrual by the media.
A simple misconstrual of the rules led to their disqualification.
The misconstrual of his intentions caused unnecessary conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misinterpretation - incorrect explanation or understanding of something
  • Antonym: interpretation - the act of explaining or understanding the meaning of something

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + construal (act of construing); related to construe.
