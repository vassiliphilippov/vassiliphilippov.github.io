Sita

1. PROPN

/ ˈsiːtə / (AmE), / ˈsiːtə / (BrE)
the wife of the Hindu god Rama, venerated as the ideal of womanhood in Hindu tradition
wife of Rama in Hindu mythology

Examples

Many Hindus revere Sita as a symbol of purity and devotion.
The story of Sita and Rama is central to the epic Ramayana.
In Indian art and literature, Sita is often depicted as the ideal wife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rama - the seventh avatar of the Hindu god Vishnu, husband of Sita

Etymology

From Sanskrit सीता (Sītā), a major figure in the Hindu epic Ramayana.
