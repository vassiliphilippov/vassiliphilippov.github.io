beyond

1. ADV

/ biˈɑnd / (AmE), / bɪˈɒnd / (BrE)
over and above; in addition to what has already been stated or provided
over and above; in addition

Examples

The contract covers repairs but nothing beyond that.
We will fund the project this year but can't promise anything beyond next June.
He offered no help beyond what was absolutely required.

Synset Examples

agreed to provide essentials but nothing beyond

Etymology

From Old English begeondan, meaning 'on the other side (of)'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00046047-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ biˈɑnd / (AmE), / bɪˈɒnd / (BrE)
at or to the farther side of something from the point of view of the observer.
on the farther side; farther away

Examples

We watched the sun set beyond the distant mountains.
A small house stood beyond the river, barely visible in the mist.
From her window, she could see fields stretching far beyond the village.

Synset Examples

a pond with a hayfield beyond

Etymology

From Old English 'bīgeondan' (by yonder).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00046337-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ biˈɑnd / (AmE), / bɪˈɒnd / (BrE)
At or to a point further in space, time, or degree than something or someone.
further in space, time, or degree

Examples

The project will continue into next year and beyond.
Her achievements go beyond what anyone expected from her.
After midnight, the city becomes quiet, but some restaurants stay open beyond.

Synset Examples

through the valley and beyond
to the eighth grade but not beyond
will be influential in the 1990s and beyond

Etymology

From Old English 'biondan', related to 'be-' (by) and 'yond' (yonder).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00046144-r
Synset POS: r
