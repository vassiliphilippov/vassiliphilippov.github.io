Saint Olaf

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈoʊlæf / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈəʊlæf / (BrE)
King of Norway from 1015 to 1028, venerated as the patron saint of Norway.
patron saint and king of Norway

Examples

Many Norwegian churches are dedicated to Saint Olaf in honor of his legacy.
Every year, people in Trondheim celebrate Saint Olaf's feast day with parades and ceremonies.
The story of Saint Olaf inspired countless legends throughout Scandinavia.

Etymology

From the Old Norse name Óláfr, Latinized as Olafus.
