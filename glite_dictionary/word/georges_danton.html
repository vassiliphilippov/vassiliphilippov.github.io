Georges Danton

1. PROPN

/ ʒɔːrʒ dænˈtɒn / (AmE), / ʒɔːʒ dænˈtɒ̃ / (BrE)
(1759–1794) A prominent French revolutionary leader who played a key role in the early stages of the French Revolution, supported the execution of Louis XVI, and was later executed during the Reign of Terror.
French revolutionary leader (1759–1794)

Examples

Georges Danton was a central figure in the fall of the French monarchy.
Many historians regard Georges Danton as a voice of moderation during the Reign of Terror.
Robespierre ultimately ordered the execution of Georges Danton for opposing the excesses of the revolution.

Etymology

French personal name; 'Georges' from the Greek 'Geōrgios' (farmer); 'Danton' is a family surname.
