Hyssopus officinalis

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈsoʊpəs ˌɑfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / hɪˈsɒpəs ˌɒfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
A European shrub of the mint family, with aromatic and pungent leaves used in perfumery, as a culinary herb, and in traditional remedies.
aromatic European mint used as a herb

Examples

The chef added a few leaves of Hyssopus officinalis to the sauce for a unique, aromatic flavor.
Gardeners often grow Hyssopus officinalis for its fragrant blue flowers and culinary uses.
Traditional remedies sometimes include extracts of Hyssopus officinalis to treat minor bruises and inflammation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyssop - common name for Hyssopus officinalis, a European herb

Etymology

From Latin *Hyssopus* (from Greek *hyssōpos*) + *officinalis* (Latin, 'of the storeroom', indicating medicinal use).
