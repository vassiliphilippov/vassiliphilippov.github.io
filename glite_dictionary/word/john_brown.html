John Brown

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn braʊn / (AmE), / dʒɒn braʊn / (BrE)
(1800–1859) An American abolitionist known for leading an unsuccessful raid on Harpers Ferry, Virginia, and being executed for his attempt to incite a slave revolt.
American abolitionist (1800–1859)

Examples

Many historians debate whether John Brown was a heroic martyr or a fanatic.
The raid at Harpers Ferry led by John Brown is a pivotal event in U.S. history.
John Brown remains a symbol of the fight against slavery for many Americans.
