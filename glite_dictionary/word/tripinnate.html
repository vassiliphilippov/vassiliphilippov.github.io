tripinnate

1. ADJ

/ traɪˈpɪneɪt / (AmE), / trɪˈpɪneɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) having its leaflets divided and re-divided three times in a pinnate arrangement.
three times pinnately divided (of leaf)

Examples

The fern species is distinguished by its tripinnate fronds, which give it a delicate appearance.
Botanists classify some acacias as having tripinnate leaves.
The intricate, tripinnate structure of the leaf helps maximize sunlight capture.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bipinnate - (of a leaf) twice pinnately divided
  • Degree Lower: pinnate - (of a leaf) having leaflets arranged on either side of a common axis

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'tri-' meaning 'three' and 'pinnatus' meaning 'feathered'.
