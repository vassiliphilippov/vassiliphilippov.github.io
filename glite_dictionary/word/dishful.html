dishful

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪʃˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈdɪʃfʊl / (BrE)
An amount that fills a dish; the quantity that a dish can hold.
the amount to fill a dish

Examples

She added a dishful of sugar to the recipe.
He brought over a dishful of fresh fruit from the kitchen.
I was given a dishful of soup to start the meal.

Synset Examples

they served me a dish of rice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handful - the quantity that fills a hand

Etymology

Modelled after 'handful', from 'dish' + 'ful'.
