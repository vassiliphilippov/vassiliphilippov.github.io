Cleome

1. NOUN

/ kliˈoʊmi / (AmE), / kliːˈəʊmi / (BrE)
A genus of tropical and subtropical annual or perennial herbs or low shrubs, some species of which are cultivated as ornamental plants.
genus of tropical herbs or shrubs

Examples

Cleome plants are often grown in gardens for their striking flowers.
Botanists are studying several species within the Cleome genus.
The tall, spidery blooms of Cleome attract many pollinators to the garden.

Etymology

From Latin cleome, from Greek kleomē, a plant name.

2. NOUN

/ kliˈoʊmi / (AmE), / kliˈəʊmi / (BrE)
A genus of tropical and subtropical annual or perennial herbs or low shrubs, some of which are cultivated for their showy flowers.
a genus of flowering plants

Examples

Many gardeners choose Cleome for its tall, spiky flowers in summer borders.
The Cleome species are known for their spider-like blossom clusters.
Botanists recently discovered a new variety in the Cleome genus in South America.

Etymology

From Greek *kleomē*, a plant of uncertain identity.
