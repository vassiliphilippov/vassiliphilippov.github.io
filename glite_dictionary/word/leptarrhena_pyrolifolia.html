Leptarrhena pyrolifolia

1. NOUN

/ lɛpˌtærˈhiːnə ˌpaɪrəˈlɪfoʊliə / (AmE), / lɛpˌtæˈriːnə ˌpaɪrəˈlɪfəʊliə / (BrE)
A plant species with leathery elliptic basal leaves and leafless flower stalks bearing dense clusters of tiny white flowers, native to moist places in northwestern North America.
species of flowering plant native to NW North America

Examples

Leptarrhena pyrolifolia thrives in the moist alpine meadows of the Pacific Northwest.
Botanists often study Leptarrhena pyrolifolia for its unique floral structure and habitat preferences.
The leathery leaves of Leptarrhena pyrolifolia help it withstand the wet, cold environments where it grows.
