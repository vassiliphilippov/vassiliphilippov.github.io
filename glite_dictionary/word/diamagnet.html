diamagnet

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈmæg.nət / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈmæɡ.nət / (BrE)
a substance or material that is repelled by a magnetic field due to the property of diamagnetism
substance repelled by magnetic field
medical

Examples

Bismuth is a well-known diamagnet that is repelled by both poles of a magnet.
Physicists studied the properties of the new diamagnet in their laboratory experiments.
Unlike ferromagnets, a diamagnet does not retain magnetization when the external magnetic field is removed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ferromagnet - a material that is strongly attracted by a magnetic field
  • Antonym: paramagnet - a substance that is weakly attracted by a magnetic field

Etymology

From dia- (through, across) + magnet, referring to magnetic properties.
