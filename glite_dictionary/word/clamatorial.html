clamatorial

1. ADJ

/ ˌklæməˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ˌklaməˈtɔːrɪəl / (BrE)
relating to the Clamatores, a former group of perching birds distinguished by their loud calls.
relating to Clamatores birds

Examples

The clamatorial birds are known for their resonant voices.
Several families once classified as clamatorial have been reassigned under modern taxonomy.
Ornithologists debated over the evolutionary relationships of clamatorial species.

Etymology

From New Latin Clamatores (a former suborder of birds) + English -al.
