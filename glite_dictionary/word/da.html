DA

1. NOUN

/ diː eɪ / (AmE), / diː eɪ / (BrE)
An official lawyer who prosecutes criminal cases on behalf of the government in a judicial district, especially in the United States; district attorney.
district attorney (official prosecutor)
legal american

Examples

The DA announced charges against the suspects in a press conference.
After months of gathering evidence, the DA finally brought the case to trial.
She considered running for DA to make a difference in her community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: district attorney - An official prosecutor for a judicial district in the U.S.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'district attorney.'
