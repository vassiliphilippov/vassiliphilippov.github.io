D.A.

1. NOUN

/ diː eɪ / (AmE), / diː ˈɑː / (BrE)
a doctor's degree awarded in the arts or sciences, with specialization in a specific discipline, often focusing on teaching rather than research
Doctor of Arts degree

Examples

She earned her D.A. in history and now teaches at the university.
After completing his D.A., he focused on curriculum development for higher education.
Many community college professors hold a D.A. in their academic field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ph.D. - Doctor of Philosophy, an advanced research degree

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Doctor of Arts'.
