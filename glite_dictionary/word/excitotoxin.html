excitotoxin

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsaɪtəˌtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtəʊˌtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a substance that causes nerve cells to become overexcited and damaged, especially when present in high concentrations, often used in reference to compounds like glutamate or aspartate
chemically induced nerve cell toxin
medical

Examples

Researchers found that high levels of excitotoxin in the brain led to neuron damage.
Glutamate is a well-known excitotoxin when consumed in excessive amounts.
Some chemicals act as an excitotoxin, overstimulating nerve cells to the point of cell death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neurotoxin - a poison that acts on the nervous system

Etymology

From 'excite' + 'toxin'; modeled on scientific compounds combining functional and effect-describing roots.
