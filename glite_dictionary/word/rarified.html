rarified

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛrəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈreərɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(of air or gas) having low density, especially at high altitudes.
having low density (air, gas)

Examples

The climbers found it hard to breathe in the rarified air near the summit.
Astronomers study the rarified gases in the upper atmosphere.
Certain plants can survive only in rarified conditions at high elevations.

Synset Examples

rare gasses
lightheaded from the rarefied mountain air

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dense - having a high concentration or density
  • Synonym: thin - having little thickness or density

Etymology

From Medieval Latin rareficare (to make thin or rare), from Latin rarus (thin, rare) + facere (to make).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛrəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈreərəˌfaɪd / (BrE)
elevated in character, morals, or intellect; lofty or refined in nature or style
elevated or refined in nature

Examples

The discussion moved into rarified territory, focusing on philosophical rather than practical concerns.
Her art is appreciated mainly in rarified circles, where taste and intellect are highly prized.
He spoke with a rarified sense of purpose, aiming to inspire others to greater ideals.

Synset Examples

an exalted ideal
argue in terms of high-flown ideals
a noble and lofty concept
a grand purpose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lofty - of imposing height or noble nature
  • Synonym: exalted - of high moral or intellectual value

Etymology

From Latin 'rarus' (rare) + English '-fy' (to make), via French 'raréfier'.
