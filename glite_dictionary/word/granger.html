granger

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪndʒə / (BrE)
a person who operates a farm; a farmer, especially one involved with the National Grange movement in the United States.
a farmer, especially a member of the Grange

Examples

The meeting was attended by every granger in the county.
Many grangers supported new agricultural policies during the late 1800s.
As a dedicated granger, he spent his life improving his land and crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmer - a person who owns or manages a farm

Etymology

From 'Grange', the National Grange of the Order of Patrons of Husbandry, a US farmers' organization founded in 1867.
