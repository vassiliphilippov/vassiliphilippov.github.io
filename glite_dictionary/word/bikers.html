bikers

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪkɚz / (AmE), / ˈbaɪkəz / (BrE)
(historical, British) members of a 1960s youth subculture who wore leather jackets, rode motorcycles, and were associated with rock'n'roll and manual labor.
British 1960s youth subculture; motorcyclists with leather jackets
british

Examples

In 1960s Britain, groups of bikers would gather at cafés to show off their motorcycles and listen to rock'n'roll.
The fashion sense of bikers influenced youth style, from leather jackets to greased hair.
Many bikers from that era were drawn together by a shared love of rebellion and music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rocker - a member of a British subculture known for riding motorcycles and listening to rock'n'roll

Etymology

From 'bike' (slang for motorcycle) + '-er' (agent noun suffix). Subcultural sense evolved in 1960s Britain.
