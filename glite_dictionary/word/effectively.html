effectively

1. ADV

/ ɪˈfɛktɪvli / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛktɪvli / (BrE)
In actuality; in effect or in reality, even if not officially so.
in effect; in reality

Examples

Although they were not officially partners, she was effectively running the business.
All flights were canceled, so the city was effectively cut off from the outside world.
He ignored the warning, effectively risking his job.

Synset Examples

she is effectively his wife
in effect, they had no choice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actually - in fact; really
  • Antonym: nominally - in name only; not in fact

Etymology

From 'effective' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'effectivus'.

2. ADV

/ ɪˈfɛktɪvli / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛktɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that produces the desired result; efficiently and successfully.
in an efficient or successful manner

Examples

The new software allows us to communicate more effectively with our customers.
She managed the team effectively, ensuring every project was completed on time.
To study effectively, it's important to take regular breaks and avoid distractions.

Synset Examples

these are real problems that can be dealt with most effectively by rational discussion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ineffectively - in a manner that does not produce the desired result
  • Synonym: efficiently - in a well-organized and productive way

Etymology

From 'effective' + '-ly', with 'effective' from Latin 'effectivus' (productive, effective).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00327717-r
Synset POS: r
