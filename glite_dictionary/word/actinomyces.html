actinomyces

1. NOUN

/ ˌæktɪnoʊˈmaɪsiːz / (AmE), / ˌæktɪnəˈmaɪsiːz / (BrE)
a genus of soil-dwelling bacteria, some species of which can cause disease in plants and animals
genus of soil bacteria, some pathogenic
medical

Examples

Actinomyces bacteria are commonly found in soil and decaying organic matter.
Certain species of actinomyces can cause infections in humans and animals.
Laboratory tests identified the presence of actinomyces in the tissue sample.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from Greek 'aktis' (ray) + 'mykes' (fungus).
