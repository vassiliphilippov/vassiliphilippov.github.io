acclamation

1. NOUN

/ ˌækləˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌækləˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
loud and enthusiastic approval or praise, often shown by a group of people
enthusiastic approval or praise

Examples

The speaker was greeted by acclamation as she took the stage.
The new law passed with acclamation from all members present.
His work received acclamation from critics worldwide.

Synset Examples

the book met with modest acclaim
he acknowledged the plaudits of the crowd
they gave him more eclat than he really deserved

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acclaim - public praise or enthusiastic approval
  • Synonym: applause - approval shown by clapping or cheering
  • Antonym: disapproval - a lack of approval or support

Etymology

From Latin acclāmātiō (a shout of approval), from acclāmāre (to shout approval).
