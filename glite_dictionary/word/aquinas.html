Aquinas

1. PROPN

/ əˈkwaɪnəs / (AmE), / əˈkwaɪnəs / (BrE)
Thomas Aquinas (1225–1274), an Italian theologian and philosopher, regarded as a Doctor of the Roman Catholic Church, known for reconciling faith and reason and for his influential writings on theology and philosophy.
Italian theologian and philosopher (1225–1274)

Examples

Aquinas argued that faith and reason are compatible paths to knowledge of God.
Many scholars still study the works of Aquinas in philosophy and theology courses.
The Summa Theologica, written by Aquinas, remains a cornerstone of Catholic doctrine.

Etymology

Latinized form of his family name, from Aquino, his place of origin in Italy.
