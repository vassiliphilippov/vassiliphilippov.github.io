Taleban

1. PROPN

/ ˈtæləˌbæn / (AmE), / ˈtælɪˌbæn / (BrE)
a fundamentalist Islamic militant group that controlled Afghanistan at various periods and enforced strict interpretations of Islamic law
fundamentalist Islamic militant group

Examples

The Taleban seized control of Kabul and imposed a strict version of Islamic law.
Talks between the government and the Taleban have made little progress.
In 2001, the US-led coalition invaded Afghanistan to overthrow the Taleban regime.

Synset Examples

the Taliban enforced a strict Muslim code of behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taliban - Alternative spelling, same group: fundamentalist Islamic movement

Etymology

From Pashto طاليبان‎ (ṭālibān), plural of طاليب‎ (ṭālib, student).
