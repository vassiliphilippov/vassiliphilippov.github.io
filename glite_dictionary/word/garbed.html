garbed

1. ADJ

/ ɡɑrbd / (AmE), / ɡɑːbd / (BrE)
wearing a particular type or style of clothing, especially distinctive or formal attire; often used in combination
dressed in specific or formal clothing

Examples

The actors were garbed in elaborate costumes for the period drama.
She entered the hall garbed in a striking silver gown.
The monks are often garbed in simple brown robes.

Synset Examples

the elegantly attired gentleman
neatly dressed workers
monks garbed in hooded robes
went about oddly garmented
professors robed in crimson
tuxedo-attired gentlemen
crimson-robed Harvard professors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dressed - wearing clothes of any kind
  • Synonym: attired - dressed, especially in fine or formal clothes
  • Antonym: undressed - not wearing clothes

Etymology

From Old French *garber* (to equip), from Germanic origin.
