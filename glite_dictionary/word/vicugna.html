Vicugna

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈkuːnjə / (AmE), / vɪˈkjuːnjə / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the family Camelidae, comprising the vicuña and alpaca of South America.
genus of South American camelids

Examples

The Vicugna genus includes both the vicuña and the alpaca.
Scientists studied the habitat of animals belonging to the Vicugna genus in the Andes.
Fur trade regulations protect members of the Vicugna genus from overexploitation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lama - a genus of South American camelids, including the llama and guanaco

Etymology

Modern Latin, based on Spanish 'vicuña' for the animal.

2. NOUN

/ vɪˈkuːnjə / (AmE), / vɪˈkʊnjə / (BrE)
A genus of South American camelids that includes the vicuña and alpaca.
genus of South American camelids

Examples

The Vicugna genus is native to the high Andean regions of South America.
Researchers studied several species within the Vicugna genus.
Both alpacas and vicuñas belong to the Vicugna genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Camelidae - the biological family of camels and related animals

Etymology

From Spanish 'vicuña', ultimately from Quechua 'wik'uña'.
