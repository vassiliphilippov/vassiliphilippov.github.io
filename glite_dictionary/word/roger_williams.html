Roger Williams

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɑːdʒər ˈwɪljəmz / (AmE), / ˈrɒdʒə ˈwɪljəmz / (BrE)
(1603–1683) English-born clergyman and founder of Rhode Island, known for advocating religious freedom and separation of church and state in colonial America.
English-born clergyman, founder of Rhode Island

Examples

Roger Williams advocated for religious liberty and tolerance in the Massachusetts Bay Colony.
After being banished, Roger Williams founded the colony of Providence in 1636.
Many historians consider Roger Williams a pioneer of the principles of freedom of conscience.
