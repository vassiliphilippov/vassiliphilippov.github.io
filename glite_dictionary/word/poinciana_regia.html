Poinciana regia

1. NOUN

/ pɔɪnˈsiːənə ˈriːdʒiə / (AmE), / pɔɪnˈsiːənə ˈriːdʒɪə / (BrE)
A showy tropical tree or shrub, native to Madagascar, widely cultivated for its large clusters of scarlet and orange flowers; also known as the flamboyant tree.
tropical tree with bright red-orange flowers

Examples

The Poinciana regia bursts into brilliant scarlet bloom every summer in the city squares.
Gardeners prize the Poinciana regia for its striking flowers and graceful umbrella-shaped canopy.
The avenue was lined with mature Poinciana regia, providing shade and vivid color to the neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flamboyant tree - another name for Poinciana regia, the tropical tree with bright flowers

Etymology

Latinized form from the genus name Poinciana, honoring French governor Philippe de Longvilliers de Poincy.
