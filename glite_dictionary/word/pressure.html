pressure

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
the force exerted per unit area on a surface, often measured in pascals or similar units
force per unit area

Examples

The pressure inside the tank rose sharply as more gas was added.
Special suits protect divers from the extreme water pressure at great depths.
Atmospheric pressure affects how water boils at different elevations.

Synset Examples

the compressed gas exerts an increased pressure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tension - the state of being stretched tight; force that pulls apart
  • Synonym: stress - physical force or pressure applied to an object

Etymology

From Latin 'pressura' (action of pressing), from 'pressus' (pressed).
