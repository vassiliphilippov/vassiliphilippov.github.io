chancroid

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæŋ.krɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈʃaŋ.krɔɪd / (BrE)
A sexually transmitted infection characterized by painful ulcers on the genitalia, caused by the bacterium Haemophilus ducreyi.
sexually transmitted ulcerative disease
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with chancroid after observing the characteristic genital ulcers.
Outbreaks of chancroid have become less common in developed countries due to better public health measures.
Proper treatment can cure chancroid and reduce the risk of transmission to others.

Etymology

From New Latin 'chancroides', from French 'chancre' (ulcer) + Greek '-oeidēs' (form, resembling).
