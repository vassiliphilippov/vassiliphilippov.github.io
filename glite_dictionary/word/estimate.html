estimate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛs.tə.meɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛs.tɪ.meɪt / (BrE)
A statement of the likely cost, value, or extent of something, especially a job or service, given before work begins.
cost statement; likely cost of a job or service

Examples

The mechanic provided an estimate before starting any repairs.
We asked three contractors for an estimate on the kitchen remodel.
His estimate for painting the house was surprisingly affordable.

Synset Examples

he got an estimate from the car repair shop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quotation - a formal written statement of the estimated cost for a particular job or service

Etymology

From Latin 'aestimatum' (estimated, valued), past participle of 'aestimare' (to value).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06796455-n
Synset POS: n

/ ˈɛstəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛstɪmət / (BrE)
an approximate calculation or judgement of the value, number, quantity, or extent of something
approximate calculation or judgment

Examples

My estimate was much lower than the actual number of people who attended.
We need a more accurate estimate before starting the project.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guess - an opinion or estimate without sufficient information
  • Degree Higher: calculation - the process of using mathematics to find an answer

Etymology

From Latin 'aestimatum', past participle of 'aestimare' (to value, estimate).

Examples

Related Terms
