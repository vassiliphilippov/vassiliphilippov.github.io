transistor

1. NOUN

/ trænˈzɪstər / (AmE), / trænˈzɪstə / (BrE)
A small electronic device made of semiconductor material that can amplify or switch electronic signals and electrical power.
type of electronic semiconductor device

Examples

The invention of the transistor revolutionized modern electronics.
She replaced the faulty transistor in her radio to restore the sound.
Most modern computers rely on millions of tiny transistors on their chips.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vacuum tube - an older electronic device used for amplification before transistors

Etymology

From transfer + resistor; originally referred to 'transfer resistor'.

2. NOUN

/ trænˈzɪstər / (AmE), / trænˈzɪstə / (BrE)
A semiconductor device used to amplify or switch electronic signals and electrical power.
semiconductor device for amplification/switching

Examples

The transistor revolutionized the electronics industry in the twentieth century.
Modern smartphones contain billions of transistors on a single chip.
He learned how to build a simple radio using just a battery and a transistor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amplifier - an electronic device that increases signal strength
  • Antonym: vacuum tube - an older electronic device used before transistors

Etymology

Blend of transfer and resistor, coined in the late 1940s for the device's function.
