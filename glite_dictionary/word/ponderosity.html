ponderosity

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑn.dəˈrɑs.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˌpɒn.dəˈrɒs.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of having great weight or mass; heaviness
heaviness; large mass or weight

Examples

The ponderosity of the ancient stone made it nearly impossible to move.
A sudden awareness of the bed's ponderosity crept over him as he tried to lift it.
Critics complained about the ponderosity of the manuscript and its excessive details.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heaviness - the quality or state of being heavy
  • Antonym: lightness - the property of having little weight

Etymology

From Latin 'pondus' (weight) via Late Latin 'ponderositas' (heaviness).
