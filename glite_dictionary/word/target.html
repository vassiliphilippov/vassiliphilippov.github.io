target

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrɡət / (AmE), / ˈtɑːɡɪt / (BrE)
an object, mark, or point at which something is aimed, especially in shooting, archery, or practice
object or point aimed at in shooting

Examples

At the archery range, she aimed carefully and struck the target with her first arrow.
The soldiers practiced shooting at a paper target set up in the field.
Hitting the moving target was much harder than aiming at a stationary one.

Synset Examples

his arrow hit the mark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mark - a point or object aimed at in shooting or archery
  • Synonym: bullseye - the center of a target, especially in shooting or archery

Etymology

From Old French 'targette' (small shield), diminutive of 'targe'.

6. VERB

to direct something, such as an action, effort, or object, towards a particular goal or person; to aim at

Examples

The company will target its new products at younger consumers.
She targeted her criticism at the policy makers.
In his speech, he targets people’s growing concerns about safety.

Related Terms
