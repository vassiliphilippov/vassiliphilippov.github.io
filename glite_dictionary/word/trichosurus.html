Trichosurus

1. NOUN

/ trɪˈkoʊsərəs / (AmE), / trɪˈkəʊsərəs / (BrE)
A genus of marsupials in the family Phalangeridae, commonly known as brushtail possums, native to Australia and New Guinea.
genus of marsupials (brushtail possums)

Examples

The Trichosurus genus includes several species of brushtail possums found in Australia.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of Trichosurus in their native habitats.
Conservation efforts have focused on protecting the Trichosurus populations from habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brushtail possum - A common marsupial species of the genus Trichosurus

Etymology

From Greek 'thrix', 'trichos' (hair) + 'oura' (tail).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪkoʊˈsʊrəs / (AmE), / ˌtrɪkəˈsjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of marsupials in the family Phalangeridae, containing the brushtail possums native to Australia and nearby regions.
genus of marsupials (brushtail possums)

Examples

The Trichosurus genus includes several species of brushtail possum found throughout Australia.
Researchers studied the nocturnal habits of animals in the Trichosurus genus.
The common brushtail possum is the best-known member of the Trichosurus group.

Etymology

From Greek 'trikhos' (hair) and 'oura' (tail), referring to the bushy tail of the possums.
