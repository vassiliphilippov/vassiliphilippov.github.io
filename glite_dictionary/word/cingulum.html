cingulum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡjələm / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡjʊləm / (BrE)
(anatomy) an encircling structure, such as the ridge around the base of a tooth.
an anatomical encircling ridge or structure
medical

Examples

The cingulum of an upper incisor helps anchor the tooth in the gum.
Damage to the parahippocampal cingulum can affect memory functions.
Dentists often examine the cingulum for signs of wear or disease.

Etymology

From Latin 'cingulum' meaning 'belt' or 'girdle'.
