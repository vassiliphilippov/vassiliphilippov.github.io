Emberizidae

1. NOUN

/ ɛm.bəˈrɪz.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / ɛm.bəˈrɪz.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A large family of small passerine birds including buntings and some New World sparrows.
family of small birds (buntings, sparrows)

Examples

The Emberizidae family encompasses both the Old World buntings and many sparrows found in the Americas.
Although similar in appearance to finches, Emberizidae birds have distinctive conical bills.
Most ornithologists study the habits and distribution of Emberizidae as part of avian biodiversity research.

Etymology

From New Latin Emberizidae, based on the type genus Emberiza.
