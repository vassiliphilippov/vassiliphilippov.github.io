compass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌm.pəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌm.pəs / (BrE)
the range or limits of a person's capacity, ability, or understanding; scope
range or limits of ability or scope

Examples

The charity's work falls within the compass of what the government will support.
Such complex emotions are beyond the compass of a child to fully understand.
All efforts must remain within the compass of the law.

Synset Examples

within the compass of education

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scope - the range covered by a subject or activity
  • Synonym: limit - the point or level beyond which something does not or may not extend or pass

Etymology

From Latin 'compassus' (circle, range), from 'com-' (together) + 'passus' (step, pace).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmpəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌmpəs / (BrE)
an instrument for finding direction, typically consisting of a magnetic needle that points north
navigational direction-finding instrument

Examples

Sailors have used a compass to navigate the seas for centuries.
I always carry a compass when hiking in the mountains to avoid getting lost.
The old ship’s compass is displayed in the maritime museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sextant - an instrument for measuring angular distances, used in navigation

Etymology

From Old French 'compas' (circle, compass), from Late Latin 'compassus' (circle, radius), past participle of 'compassare' (to pace out, measure).

4. NOUN

/ ˈkʌm.pəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌm.pəs / (BrE)

drawing instrument for circles/arcs

Examples

Make sure the point of the compass stays in one place while you rotate it.
She adjusted the width of her compass before drawing the arc.

From Latin 'compassare' (to pace out, measure), from 'passus' (pace, step).
