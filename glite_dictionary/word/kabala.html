Kabala

1. NOUN

/ kəˈbɑːlə / (AmE), / kəˈbɑːlə / (BrE)
a mystical and esoteric tradition of Jewish theology, based on interpretations of the Hebrew scriptures and developed chiefly between the 7th and 18th centuries
Jewish mystical and esoteric tradition

Examples

Many medieval philosophers studied Kabala to uncover hidden truths about the universe.
He became fascinated by the teachings of Kabala and their influence on Western mysticism.
Some scholars explore how Kabala interprets the relationship between God and humanity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kabbalah - alternative spelling and common variant of Kabala, referring to Jewish mystical tradition
  • Synonym: Cabala - alternative spelling of Kabala, used especially in older texts

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Cabbala', from Hebrew 'Qabbālāh' (reception, doctrine).
