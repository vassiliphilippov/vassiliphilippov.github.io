popinjay

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑpɪnˌdʒeɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɒpɪnˌdʒeɪ / (BrE)
An archaic term for a parrot; sometimes used figuratively for a vain or talkative person.
archaic term for a parrot
archaic

Examples

In the king’s court, a brightly colored popinjay sat on its perch, mimicking the guests.
Medieval tapestries often feature a popinjay among other exotic animals.
He was called a popinjay in jest, for his loud clothes and boastful manner.

Etymology

From Old French *papegeai*, from Arabic *babagha'*, meaning 'parrot'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːpɪnˌdʒeɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɒpɪndʒeɪ / (BrE)
a person who is vain and talkative, especially in a showy or boastful manner
vain, talkative person
archaic

Examples

He strutted about the office like a popinjay, boasting about his latest achievements.
Don't be such a popinjay—nobody likes a braggart.
The court was full of popinjays competing for the king's attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandy - a man unduly concerned with style and appearance
  • Synonym: chatterbox - a person who talks a lot, especially about trivial matters

Etymology

From Old French 'papingai', from Arabic 'babaghāʼ' meaning 'parrot', later used figuratively for vain, talkative people.
