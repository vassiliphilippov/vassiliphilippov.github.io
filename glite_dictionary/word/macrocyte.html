macrocyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæk.roʊ.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈmak.rəʊ.saɪt / (BrE)
an abnormally large red blood cell, typically associated with certain types of anemia such as pernicious anemia
abnormally large red blood cell
medical

Examples

The blood test revealed a high number of macrocytes indicating a possible vitamin B12 deficiency.
An excess of macrocyte formation can lead to complications in oxygen transport.
Doctors monitored the patient's macrocyte levels to assess the effectiveness of the treatment.

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) + kytos (cell).
