Aglaomorpha meyeniana

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡliəˈmɔrfə ˌmaɪəˈniænə / (AmE), / ˌæɡliəˈmɔːfə ˌmaɪəˈniːənə / (BrE)
An epiphytic fern species with large fronds, native to Taiwan and the Philippines.
species of epiphytic fern

Examples

The Aglaomorpha meyeniana thrives in the humid forests of Taiwan and the Philippines.
Many collectors admire the distinctive fronds of Aglaomorpha meyeniana for their beauty.
Botanists discovered several specimens of Aglaomorpha meyeniana growing high in the rainforest canopy.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek aglaos (splendid) + morphe (form); species named after botanist Franz Meyen.
