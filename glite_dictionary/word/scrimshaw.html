scrimshaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrɪm.ʃɔ / (AmE), / ˈskrɪm.ʃɔː / (BrE)
an ornament or carving made by engraving or carving on whalebone, whale ivory, walrus tusk, or similar materials, especially by sailors and whalers
carved or engraved whalebone or ivory ornament

Examples

The museum displayed an impressive scrimshaw collection carved by 19th-century sailors.
He inherited a scrimshaw depicting a whaling scene from his grandfather.
Collectors are often fascinated by the fine detail in antique scrimshaw.

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Dutch 'schrimpschrams' meaning 'scratches'.
