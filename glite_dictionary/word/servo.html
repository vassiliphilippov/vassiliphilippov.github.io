servo

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜːr.voʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.vəʊ / (BrE)
A control mechanism that uses a small input to regulate a much larger output of power, often incorporating a feedback system to maintain stability.
automatic control mechanism with feedback

Examples

The servo in the robot arm ensures precise movement and positioning.
He connected the servo to the control board to automate the door system.
A malfunction in the servo caused the control surfaces to stop responding properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servomechanism - automatic device that uses feedback to operate a mechanism

Etymology

Shortened from 'servomechanism', from Latin 'servus' (slave, servant) and 'mechanism'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɜrˌvoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvəʊ / (BrE)
Relating to or involving a servomechanism or servomechanisms; used to describe devices or systems that include automatic control mechanisms.
relating to servomechanisms

Examples

The engineer installed a servo control system to enhance the machine's precision.
Modern robotics often use servo motors for accurate movement.
A servo mechanism automatically corrected the steering trajectory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servomechanism - an automatic device that uses error-sensing feedback to correct the performance of a mechanism

Etymology

From 'servomechanism', based on Latin servus (slave, servant) and mechanism.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
