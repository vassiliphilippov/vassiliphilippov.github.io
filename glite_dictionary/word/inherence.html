inherence

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhɪrəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪərəns / (BrE)
The state or quality of being inherent; the condition of being an essential and inextricable characteristic of something.
state of being inherent

Examples

The inherence of ethical values in law is often debated by philosophers.
She discussed the inherence of risk in all financial ventures during her lecture.
Some linguists study the inherence of ambiguity in natural languages.

Synset Examples

the inherence of polysemy in human language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inherency - the quality of being inherent; similar in meaning to inherence
  • Antonym: accidence - a property that is not essential or inherent but accidental

Etymology

From Latin 'inhaerentia', from 'inhaerere' (to be attached to).
