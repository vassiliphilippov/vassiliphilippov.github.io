flak

1. NOUN

/ flæk / (AmE), / flæk / (BrE)
antiaircraft artillery or bursting shells fired from it, used to attack aircraft in flight
antiaircraft artillery fire

Examples

The bombers faced heavy flak as they crossed enemy lines.
Puffs of black flak dotted the sky during the air raid.
The pilot managed to fly through intense flak and return safely.

Etymology

From German Flak, short for Flugabwehrkanone (aircraft defense cannon).

2. NOUN

/ flæk / (AmE), / flæk / (BrE)
a person, often a press agent or publicist, who promotes and defends their employer in the media, especially by deflecting criticism.
press agent who defends their client
informal

Examples

The senator's flak managed to dodge every tough question from the press.
A skilled flak can transform a scandal into a publicity opportunity for their company.
Every celebrity hires a flak to handle the media during controversies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: publicist - a person responsible for public relations and media coverage
  • Synonym: spokesperson - a person who speaks on behalf of an organization

Etymology

Extension of the sense 'criticism or hostile publicity', possibly influenced by 'flack' (slang for press agent).

3. NOUN

/ flæk / (AmE), / flæk / (BrE)
strong and harsh criticism; severe disapproval or reproach.
intense criticism or disapproval

Examples

The politician received a lot of flak for his controversial remarks.
She took some flak at work after making that decision.
The company came under heavy flak following the product recall.

Synset Examples

Clinton directed his fire at the Republican Party
the government has come under attack
don't give me any flak

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval of someone or something based on perceived faults or mistakes
  • Antonym: praise - the expression of approval or admiration for someone or something

Etymology

From German 'Flak', short for 'Fliegerabwehrkanone' (aircraft defense cannon); sense extended in English to mean harsh criticism.

4. NOUN

/ flæk / (AmE), / flæk / (BrE)
strong criticism or negative feedback, especially from several people or from the public
strong criticism or negative feedback

Examples

The company received a lot of flak for its unpopular decision.
Politicians often face flak from the media during campaigns.
She ignored the flak and continued with her presentation.

Synset Examples

He was used to flak from his critics and didn't let it bother him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criticism - expression of disapproval or negative assessment
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From German 'Flak', short for 'Fliegerabwehrkanone' (aircraft-defense cannon); figurative sense of 'criticism' dates from WWII era.
