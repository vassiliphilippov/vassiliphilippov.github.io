qualifier

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑl.əˌfaɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkwɒl.ɪ.faɪ.ə / (BrE)
a word or phrase, especially an adjective or adverb, that restricts or modifies the meaning of another word.
word or phrase that limits meaning

Examples

In the phrase "reasonably fast car", the word "reasonably" is a qualifier of the adjective.
The report contained several qualifiers that limited the findings' applicability.
Adjectives often serve as qualifiers for nouns in English grammar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modifier - a word or phrase that qualifies or describes another word

Etymology

From Latin 'qualificare', meaning 'to describe the quality of'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑː.ləˌfaɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkwɒl.ɪˌfaɪ.ə / (BrE)
a person or team that succeeds in a qualifying round and is eligible to take part in the next stage of a competition or tournament
person/team advancing in a competition

Examples

After her impressive performance in the heats, she became a qualifier for the national finals.
The last qualifier surprised everyone by reaching the semifinals.
Only four qualifiers from each group will advance to the next round.

Synset Examples

the tournament was won by a late qualifier

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: finalist - a person or team that reaches the final stage of a competition
  • Antonym: eliminated - a contestant removed from a competition

Etymology

From Latin 'qualificare' (to make of a certain quality), via Medieval Latin 'qualificator'.
