Paralichthys dentatus

1. NOUN

/ pæ.rəˈlɪk.θɪs dɛnˈteɪ.təs / (AmE), / pæ.rəˈlɪk.θɪs dɛnˈteɪ.təs / (BrE)
A species of flounder found along the eastern coast of North America, commonly known as the summer flounder.
species of North American flounder

Examples

The Paralichthys dentatus is an important commercial fish along the Atlantic seaboard.
Fisheries researchers study the life cycle of Paralichthys dentatus to support sustainable harvesting.
Anglers often target Paralichthys dentatus during the summer months for its delicious flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summer flounder - common name for Paralichthys dentatus, a flounder species of the U.S. Atlantic coast

Etymology

From Greek 'paralos' (near the sea) + 'ichthys' (fish); Latin 'dentatus' (toothed).
