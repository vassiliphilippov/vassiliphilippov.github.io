Strasberg

1. PROPN

/ ˈstrɑːzˌbɜrɡ / (AmE), / ˈstræzˌbɜːɡ / (BrE)
Lee Strasberg (1901–1982), American actor, theatre director, and influential acting teacher known for developing method acting in the United States.
renowned method acting teacher and director

Examples

Many aspiring actors study the techniques developed by Strasberg to improve their craft.
Strasberg's influence on American theatre can still be felt today.
Al Pacino and Marilyn Monroe were among the famous students of Strasberg.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stanislavski - Russian theatre practitioner whose system influenced method acting
