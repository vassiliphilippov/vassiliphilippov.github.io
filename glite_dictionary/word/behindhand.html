behindhand

1. ADV

/ bɪˈhaɪndˌhænd / (AmE), / bɪˈhaɪndˌhænd / (BrE)
late in fulfilling financial obligations; in debt or in arrears.
in debt; late with payments

Examples

The tenant had been behindhand with the rent for three consecutive months.
The company became behindhand in its payments to suppliers, causing concern among investors.
If you fall behindhand with your bills, it's important to contact your creditors promptly.

Synset Examples

he fell behind with his mortgage payments
a month behind in the rent
a company that has been run behindhand for years
in arrears with their utility bills

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in arrears - late in paying money owed
  • Antonym: in advance - before the due time, especially for payments

Etymology

From 'behind' + 'hand', first used in 16th century English to mean 'late or delayed'.

2. ADJ

/ bɪˈhaɪndˌhænd / (AmE), / bɪˈhaɪndˌhænd / (BrE)
late in doing something; behind schedule or overdue in meeting an obligation.
behind schedule; late
archaic

Examples

She was behindhand in delivering her research report, which caused a delay in the project.
The company became behindhand with payments to suppliers after the economic downturn.
He admitted to being behindhand with the rent this month.

Synset Examples

was behindhand with the rent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: late - not on time; after the expected or proper time
  • Antonym: prompt - done without delay; on time

Etymology

From 'behind' + 'hand', first attested in the 16th century.
