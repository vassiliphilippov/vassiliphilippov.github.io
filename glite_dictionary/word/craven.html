craven

1. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪ.vən / (AmE), / ˈkreɪ.vən / (BrE)
A person who is extremely cowardly; an abject coward.
an abject coward
archaic

Examples

The knight was branded a craven for fleeing the battlefield.
No craven would dare challenge such a powerful foe.
In the story, the craven begged for mercy rather than stand his ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coward - a person who lacks courage
  • Antonym: hero - a person admired for courage or noble qualities

Etymology

From Middle English *cravant* or *craven*, possibly of Old French origin, meaning 'defeated'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkreɪ.vən / (AmE), / ˈkreɪ.vən / (BrE)
lacking courage; contemptibly timid or cowardly
cowardly; lacking courage

Examples

The craven general refused to lead his troops into battle.
She considered his craven behavior inexcusable during the emergency.
Critics called their retreat a craven surrender.

Synset Examples

the craven fellow turned and ran
a craven proposal to raise the white flag
this recreant knight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowardly - lacking courage or bravery
  • Antonym: brave - showing courage; not afraid

Etymology

Middle English, probably from Old French cravanté (defeated, overcome).
