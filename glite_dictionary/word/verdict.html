verdict

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝːdɪkt / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdɪkt / (BrE)
(law) a decision made by a jury on the matters of fact in a court case, which forms the basis for a court's judgment.
jury's decision in a trial
legal

Examples

The jury returned a verdict of guilty after two days of deliberation.
The judge accepted the verdict and sentenced the defendant to five years in prison.
After reviewing all the evidence, the verdict was unanimous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judgment - a decision by a court of law

Etymology

From Latin 'verdictum' (a true saying), from 'verus' (true) + 'dicere' (to say).
