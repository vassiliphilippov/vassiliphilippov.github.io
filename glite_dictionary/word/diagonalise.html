to diagonalise

1. VERB

/ daɪˈæɡənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / daɪˈaɡənəlaɪz / (BrE)
(of a matrix) to transform into a diagonal matrix, typically by a similarity transformation.
transform a matrix into diagonal form

Examples

It is often possible to diagonalise a matrix to simplify calculations.
The matrix cannot be diagonalised because it lacks a complete set of eigenvectors.
Once you diagonalise the operator, solving the system becomes much easier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diagonalize - US spelling of diagonalise (transform into diagonal matrix)

Etymology

From 'diagonal' + '-ise', formed in analogy to technical verbs like 'normalize', following established mathematical terminology.
