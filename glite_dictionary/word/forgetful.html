forgetful

1. ADJ

/ fərˈɡɛtfəl / (AmE), / fəˈɡɛtfʊl / (BrE)
Often unable to remember things; tending to forget.
prone to forgetting things

Examples

She became increasingly forgetful as she grew older.
He is so forgetful that he often leaves his keys at home.
Being forgetful can sometimes cause problems at work.

Synset Examples

forgetful of her responsibilities
oblivious old age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oblivious - not aware of or not concerned about what is happening around
  • Antonym: attentive - paying close attention to something

Etymology

From Old English 'forgietan' (to forget) + '-ful'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00167205-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ fərˈɡɛtfəl / (AmE), / fəˈɡɛtfʊl / (BrE)
often failing to remember things or pay attention; not mindful
often failing to remember things

Examples

She became increasingly forgetful as she grew older, often misplacing her keys and glasses.
Being forgetful can cause problems at work if you miss important deadlines or meetings.
He's not usually forgetful, but today he left his lunch at home.

Synset Examples

while thus unmindful of his steps he stumbled

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mindful - paying attention or aware
  • Antonym: attentive - paying close attention; alert

Etymology

From Old English 'forgietan' (to forget) + '-ful' (full of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01985067-a
Synset POS: a
