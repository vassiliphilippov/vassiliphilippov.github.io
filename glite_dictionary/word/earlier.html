earlier

1. ADV

/ ˈɜr.li.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈɜː.li.ə / (BrE)
At or during a time before now or before a specified time.
before now; at an earlier time

Examples

I finished my project earlier than I expected.
She arrived earlier and helped set up the room.
If you had called earlier, I could have joined you for lunch.

Synset Examples

why didn't you tell me in the first place?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: later - at or after a subsequent time
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00168316-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈɜrliər / (AmE), / ˈɜːliə / (BrE)
At or during a time before the one previously mentioned or expected; more early than something else.
more early; at a prior time

Examples

I arrived earlier than most of the guests.
If you had called earlier, I would have been home to answer.
She prefers to eat dinner earlier in the evening.

Synset Examples

Come a little sooner, if you can
came earlier than I expected

Related Terms

  • Antonym: late - happening or done after the expected time
  • Degree Lower: early - happening or done before the expected or usual time
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00260528-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADJ

/ ˈɚlɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈɜːlɪə / (BrE)
Comparative of 'early'; happening before a usual or expected time, or before something else.
comparative of 'early'; more early than

Examples

The company released its results earlier this year than expected.
You should arrive earlier if you want a good seat.
Her earlier books were more focused on history than her recent ones.

Synset Examples

a fashion popular in earlier times
his earlier work reflects the influence of his teacher
Verdi's earliest and most raucous opera

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: early - happening or done before the usual or expected time
  • Degree Higher: earliest - superlative of 'early'; most early
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00818180-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADV

/ ˈɜrliər / (AmE), / ˈɜːliə / (BrE)
at a time before an event or the present moment; previously.
before now; previously

Examples

She said she would arrive at five, but she showed up earlier than expected.
I think I mentioned that issue earlier in our conversation.
The flight departed earlier today due to a schedule change.

Synset Examples

I had known her before
as I said before
he called me the day before but your call had come even earlier
her parents had died four years earlier
I mentioned that problem earlier

Related Terms

  • Synonym: previously - at a former time; before now
  • Antonym: later - occurring after a particular time or event

Etymology

Formed from 'early' with the comparative suffix '-er'; 'early' from Old English 'ǣrlīce'.
