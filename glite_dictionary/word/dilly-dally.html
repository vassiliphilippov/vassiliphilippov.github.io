to dilly-dally

1. VERB

/ ˈdɪliˌdæli / (AmE), / ˈdɪliˌdæli / (BrE)
to waste time through aimless delay or indecision; to dawdle or procrastinate instead of taking action
to waste time by delaying or dawdling
informal

Examples

Stop dilly-dallying and get your homework done before dinner.
We can't dilly-dally any longer if we want to catch the train.
Instead of starting his project, he dilly-dallied all afternoon.

Synset Examples

He did not want to write the letter and procrastinated for days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dawdle - to waste time or move slowly
  • Antonym: hurry - to move or act with speed

Etymology

Reduplicative formation, likely from "dally" (to waste time, loiter), dating from the late 18th century.
