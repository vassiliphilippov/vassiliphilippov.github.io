to categorize

1. VERB

/ ˈkætəɡəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈkatəɡərʌɪz / (BrE)
to place or assign something or someone into a particular group or category according to shared characteristics
to assign to a category or group

Examples

It's common to categorize people based on their interests or professions.
Scientists often categorize plants and animals to better understand their relationships.
She found it unfair to categorize all teenagers as irresponsible.

Synset Examples

Children learn early on to categorize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to classify - to arrange or organize by classes or categories
  • Synonym: label - to assign a category or descriptive term to something
  • Degree Lower: to generalize - to make a general statement or form a general category

Etymology

From Greek katēgoria (statement, category) via Medieval Latin categorisare.
