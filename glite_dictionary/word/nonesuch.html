nonesuch

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌnˌsʌtʃ / (AmE), / ˈnʌnˌsʌtʃ / (BrE)
A person or thing regarded as a model of excellence or having no equal.
person or thing with no equal

Examples

The film was a nonesuch in the genre, unmatched by any of its successors.
People regarded her as a nonesuch in the art of negotiation.
This cathedral is the nonesuch of Gothic architecture in the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paragon - a person or thing viewed as a model of excellence

Etymology

From Middle English 'none such', literally 'none such (as this)'.
