conscript

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.skrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.skrɪpt / (BrE)
a person who is compulsorily enlisted into military service
person drafted into the military

Examples

After graduating, he became a conscript and served two years in the army.
The government announced that every conscript would undergo basic training next month.
Unlike professional soldiers, a conscript may have little choice about serving.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: volunteer - a person who freely offers to join the military

Etymology

From Latin conscriptus, past participle of conscribere (to enroll).

2. VERB

/ kənˈskrɪpt / (AmE), / kənˈskrɪpt / (BrE)
to force someone by law to serve in the armed forces
compulsorily enroll into military service

Examples

During the war, thousands of young men were conscripted into the army against their will.
Many countries no longer conscript citizens, relying instead on volunteers for their armed forces.
He feared that his sons would be conscripted if a conflict broke out.

Synset Examples

The men were conscripted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draft - to select people for compulsory military service
  • Antonym: to enlist - to enroll in the armed forces, often voluntarily

Etymology

From Latin 'conscribere' meaning 'to enroll'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01101117-v
Synset POS: v
