hookup

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʊk.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈhʊk.ʌp / (BrE)
a device or arrangement that provides a connection between a power source or utility and a user, especially at a campsite or similar location
device for utility connection

Examples

The campsite offers an electrical hookup for each RV spot.
We need a water hookup if we're going to use the shower in the trailer.
Many parks provide only basic hookups, so plan accordingly.

Synset Examples

some campsites have electrical hookups for trailers

Etymology

From 'hook' + 'up', originally used for linking devices or utilities.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʊkˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈhʊkʌp / (BrE)
a system or arrangement of interconnected parts or equipment assembled for a specific purpose
system of assembled components

Examples

The technician will install the hookup for your new home internet service tomorrow.
All the wires and switches are part of the electrical hookup in the lab.
They checked the water hookup before connecting the washing machine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assembly - a collection of parts put together
  • Synonym: installation - a system or piece of equipment that has been set up for use

Etymology

From 'hook' + 'up', referring to connecting things together; first used in early 20th-century technical contexts.
