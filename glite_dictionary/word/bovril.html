Bovril

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊvrəl / (AmE), / ˈbəʊvrɪl / (BrE)
A concentrated beef extract, used as a hot drink or flavouring, especially for people who are ill or need nourishment.
concentrated beef extract (drink/flavouring)
british

Examples

After feeling unwell, she drank a mug of Bovril to regain her strength.
The nurse offered Bovril to the patients during the cold winter days.
Some recipes call for Bovril as a rich base for soups and stews.

Etymology

Trademark, formed irregularly from Latin 'bos' (ox) and 'vril' (life force, from a 19th-century novel).
