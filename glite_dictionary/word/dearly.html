dearly

1. ADV

/ ˈdɪrli / (AmE), / ˈdɪəli / (BrE)
in a way that involves great loss, suffering, or cost; at a high price
at great cost; with much suffering or loss

Examples

He realized too late that he had paid dearly for his mistake.
Many soldiers fought and dearly bought the freedom we enjoy today.
If you betray their trust, you may suffer dearly as a result.

Synset Examples

he paid dearly for the food
this cost him dear

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheaply - at little cost or sacrifice

Etymology

From Old English 'dēorlīce', meaning 'preciously, with affection', from 'dēore' (dear) + '-līce' (-ly).

2. ADV

/ ˈdɪrli / (AmE), / ˈdɪəli / (BrE)
In a sincere and heartfelt way; with deep affection or devotion.
in a sincere and heartfelt way

Examples

She loves her children dearly and always puts them first.
I would dearly like to see him again after all these years.
He dearly hopes that his efforts will be successful.

Synset Examples

I would dearly love to know

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affectionately - in a way that shows affection or fondness
  • Synonym: fondly - with affection or tenderness

Etymology

Formed from 'dear' + '-ly'; 'dear' from Old English 'dēore' (precious, beloved).

3. ADV

/ ˈdɪrli / (AmE), / ˈdɪəli / (BrE)
With deep affection or fondness; very much.
with deep affection; very much

Examples

She misses her grandmother dearly and thinks of her every day.
He paid a dearly price for his mistakes, both emotionally and financially.
They love their children dearly and always put them first.

Synset Examples

he treats her affectionately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fondly - with affection or tenderness
  • Synonym: affectionately - showing affection or tenderness

Etymology

Formed from "dear" + "-ly" suffix; "dear" from Old English dēore (precious, beloved).
