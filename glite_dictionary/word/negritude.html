Negritude

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪɡrɪˌtuːd / (AmE), / ˈneɪɡrɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
A literary and ideological movement promoting the value and uniqueness of Black culture and identity, especially that of the African diaspora.
movement affirming Black cultural identity

Examples

Many writers of the Harlem Renaissance embraced Negritude as a form of cultural pride and resistance.
The concept of Negritude helped shape African and Afro-Caribbean literary movements in the twentieth century.
By asserting Negritude, artists challenged colonial narratives and celebrated African traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pan-Africanism - the principle or advocacy of the political union of all the indigenous inhabitants of Africa
  • Antonym: assimilationism - belief in or practice of people from different backgrounds assimilating into a dominant culture

Etymology

From French 'Négritude', coined by Aimé Césaire in the 1930s, from 'nègre' (Black) + '-itude' (state or quality).
