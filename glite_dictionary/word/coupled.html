coupled

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌpəld / (AmE), / ˈkʌpəld / (BrE)
connected together by a link, especially referring to vehicles such as railway cars or trailers
connected by a link; joined together

Examples

The freight cars were coupled together before leaving the station.
The two trailers remained coupled during the entire journey.
Make sure the cables are securely coupled before starting the machinery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connected - joined or linked together
  • Antonym: uncoupled - not connected or joined together

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'couple', from Old French 'coupeler', from Latin 'copula' (a bond, tie).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌpəld / (AmE), / ˈkʌpəld / (BrE)
joined together, especially in a pair or pairs
joined or connected in pairs

Examples

The two computers were coupled with a high-speed cable.
The train cars remained coupled during the entire journey.
Economic growth is often coupled with increases in energy consumption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paired - matched or joined in twos
  • Synonym: connected - joined or linked together
  • Antonym: separated - not joined or divided

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'couple,' from Old French 'coupeler,' from Latin 'copulare' (to join).
