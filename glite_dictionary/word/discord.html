discord

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪsˌkɔrd / (AmE), / ˈdɪskɔːd / (BrE)
a lack of agreement or harmony between people, often resulting in conflict or strife
lack of agreement; conflict

Examples

Several months of discord within the team eventually led to a complete breakdown in communication.
Political discord can quickly turn into protests and unrest if not properly addressed.
Their marriage was marked by years of hidden discord and unresolved arguments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harmony - agreement or concord between people or things
  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument

Etymology

From Latin discordia (disagreement), from discors (disagreeing, at odds).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪsˌkɔrd / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.kɔːd / (BrE)
(literary) A harsh combination of sounds; lack of harmony in music.
harsh mixture of sounds

Examples

The sudden discord of shattering glass startled everyone in the quiet room.
Their instruments produced a discord that was jarring to the audience's ears.
The composer skillfully used discord in the melody to create a sense of tension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cacophony - a harsh, discordant mixture of sounds
  • Antonym: harmony - pleasing arrangement of sounds

Etymology

From Latin discordia (discord, disagreement), from discors (disagreeing).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪsˌkɔrd / (AmE), / ˈdɪskɔːd / (BrE)
lack of agreement or harmony between people, groups, or things; disagreement or conflict
lack of agreement or harmony

Examples

Political debates often generate discord among members of the community.
There was much discord in the office after the management changes.
Efforts to resolve family discord can sometimes require outside help.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harmony - agreement or congruence; opposite of discord
  • Synonym: conflict - serious disagreement or argument
  • Synonym: dissension - disagreement that leads to discord

Etymology

From Latin 'discordia,' from 'discors' (disagreeing, different).
