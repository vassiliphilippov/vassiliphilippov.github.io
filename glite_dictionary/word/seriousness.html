seriousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being serious; showing sincere purpose, thought, or concern.
the quality of being serious

Examples

His seriousness impressed the interview panel and helped him get the job.
There was a seriousness in her voice that made everyone pay attention.
Despite his usual jokes, he spoke with great seriousness about the issue.

Synset Examples

a lack of solemnity is not necessarily a lack of seriousness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravity - seriousness or solemnity of manner
  • Synonym: solemnity - the state or quality of being serious and dignified
  • Antonym: levity - lack of seriousness

Etymology

Formed from 'serious' + '-ness', indicating a state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of a situation or event that causes fear, distress, or concern.
quality of causing fear or distress

Examples

Doctors explained the seriousness of the patient's condition to the family.
We failed to realize the seriousness of the environmental crisis until it was almost too late.
When she saw the seriousness of the accident, she called for help immediately.

Synset Examples

he learned the seriousness of his illness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravity - seriousness or importance, especially causing concern or alarm
  • Synonym: severity - the condition of being very bad, serious, or dangerous

Etymology

From 'serious' + '-ness', from Latin 'serius' (grave, earnest).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being earnest and sincere, giving something careful thought or attention.
earnest or sincere feeling

Examples

His seriousness during the meeting showed how important the issue was to him.
She always approaches her studies with seriousness and dedication.
Despite the laughter, there was a certain seriousness in his tone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earnestness - the quality of being earnest and honest
  • Antonym: levity - lack of seriousness; lightness in attitude or mood

Etymology

From serious + -ness, formed in English.
