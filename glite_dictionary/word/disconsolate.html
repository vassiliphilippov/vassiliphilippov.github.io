disconsolate

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkɑnsələt / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɒnsələt / (BrE)
Extremely sad and unable to be comforted; deeply unhappy.
unable to be comforted; deeply sad

Examples

After losing the championship, the team was disconsolate in the locker room.
She was disconsolate after hearing the news of her friend's passing.
He looked disconsolate as he sat alone in the empty café.

Synset Examples

inconsolable when her son died

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inconsolable - unable to be comforted; deeply sad

Etymology

From Latin disconsolatus, from dis- (not) + consolatus (comforted).

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkɑːnsələt / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɒnsələt / (BrE)
making someone feel very unhappy; causing a sense of deep sadness and dejection.
causing deep sadness

Examples

The disconsolate atmosphere in the abandoned house was almost overwhelming.
A disconsolate gray sky hung over the city for days, sapping everyone’s energy.
She gazed out at the disconsolate winter landscape, longing for the return of spring.

Synset Examples

a blue day
the dark days of the war
a week of rainy depressing weather
a disconsolate winter landscape
the first dismal dispiriting days of November
a dark gloomy day
grim rainy weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dismal - causing a mood of gloom or depression
  • Antonym: cheerful - causing feelings of happiness or joy
  • Synonym: gloomy - causing or feeling depression or darkness

Etymology

From Latin disconsolatus, from 'dis-' (not) + 'consolari' (to console).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00365961-s
Synset POS: s
