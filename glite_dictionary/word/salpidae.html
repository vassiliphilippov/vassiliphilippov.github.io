Salpidae

1. NOUN

/ sælˈpɪdiː / (AmE), / salˈpɪdiː / (BrE)
A small family of free-floating tunicates belonging to the class Thaliacea, found in oceans worldwide.
family of free-floating tunicates

Examples

Scientists collected several specimens from the Salpidae family during the marine expedition.
The transparent bodies of Salpidae allow them to blend into their pelagic environment.
Members of Salpidae play an essential role in oceanic food webs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Salpa - a genus of free-floating tunicates in Salpidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Salpa', the type genus, plus '-idae' denoting a zoological family.

2. NOUN

/ sælˈpɪdiː / (AmE), / salˈpɪdiː / (BrE)
a small family of free-floating tunicates in the class Thaliacea, known as salps
family of free-floating marine tunicates

Examples

The Salpidae play a crucial role in the pelagic food chain of the ocean.
Scientists have documented several genera within the Salpidae family.
Unlike other tunicates, Salpidae are characterized by their barrel-shaped, transparent bodies.

Etymology

From New Latin Salpidae, from genus name Salpa plus -idae (family suffix).
