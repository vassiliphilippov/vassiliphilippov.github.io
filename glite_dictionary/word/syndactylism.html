syndactylism

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈdæktɪˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / sɪnˈdæktɪlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A congenital condition in which two or more fingers or toes are joined together by webbing, either partially or completely.
webbing of fingers or toes at birth
medical

Examples

The surgeon specialized in treating syndactylism and other congenital hand deformities.
Children born with syndactylism may require surgical separation of their fingers for improved function.
Doctors diagnosed him with syndactylism after observing the webbing between his toes at birth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syndactyly - the condition of having fused fingers or toes

Etymology

From Greek syn- (together) + daktylos (finger).
