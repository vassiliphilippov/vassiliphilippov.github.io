thieving

1. ADJ

/ ˈθivɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθiːvɪŋ / (BrE)
habitually stealing; given to or characterized by theft.
given to stealing

Examples

The thieving crew made off with the entire shipment of goods.
She always suspected her neighbor of thieving behavior.
Tales of thieving pirates have been told for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
  • Synonym: criminal - relating to crime; committing illegal acts

Etymology

Present participle of 'thieve,' from Old English 'þēofian' (to steal).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθivɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθiːvɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of stealing; the practice of taking property unlawfully from others
the act of stealing

Examples

The increase in thieving has caused concern among local businesses.
Airport officials are working to reduce thieving in the baggage area.
Repeated thieving in the neighborhood has led residents to install security cameras.

Synset Examples

the thieving is awful at Kennedy International

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theft - the crime of stealing
  • Synonym: robbery - the act of taking property by force or threat
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest and not stealing

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'thieve' + '-ing', from Old English 'þēofian' (to steal).
