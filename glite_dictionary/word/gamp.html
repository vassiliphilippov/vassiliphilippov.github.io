gamp

1. NOUN

/ ɡæmp / (AmE), / ɡamp / (BrE)
an umbrella, especially a large or clumsy one (chiefly British, dated or informal)
umbrella (chiefly British, dated)
british informal archaic

Examples

Caught in the downpour, he opened his trusty gamp and hurried across the street.
She always carried a gamp that seemed big enough to shelter three people.
The old man waved his tattered gamp at the passing cars, hoping for a lift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: umbrella - a device for protection against rain or sun

Etymology

From the character Mrs. Gamp in Charles Dickens' novel 'Martin Chuzzlewit', who carried a large umbrella.
