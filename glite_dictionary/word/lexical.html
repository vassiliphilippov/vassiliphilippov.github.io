lexical

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛksɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈlɛksɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the words or vocabulary of a language, especially as distinguished from its grammar and construction
relating to words or vocabulary

Examples

The dictionary includes detailed lexical information for every entry.
Researchers conducted a lexical analysis of the text to study word usage.
Her lexical skills improved dramatically after studying several foreign languages.

Synset Examples

lexical decision task

Related Terms

  • Antonym: grammatical - relating to grammar rather than vocabulary

Etymology

From Greek lexis (word) and -al (adjective suffix), via French lexical and late Latin lexicalis.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02897933-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɛksɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈlɛksɪkəl / (BrE)
of or relating to dictionaries, the words or vocabulary of a language, or the structure of word meanings
relating to dictionaries or vocabulary

Examples

The lexical entries in the new dictionary are remarkably thorough.
She specializes in lexical research and the study of word meanings.
A lexical approach focuses on vocabulary rather than grammar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semantic - relating to meaning in language
  • Antonym: grammatical - relating to grammar or structure

Etymology

From Greek lexis (word) via Latin lexicalis.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02898173-a
Synset POS: a
