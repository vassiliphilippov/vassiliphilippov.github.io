orgy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdʒi / (AmE), / ˈɔːdʒi / (BrE)
A wild party characterized by excessive drinking and unrestrained sexual activity.
wild party with excessive drinking and sex

Examples

The tabloids reported that the celebrities were involved in a secret orgy at the mansion.
Rumors of an orgy taking place after the festival shocked the conservative town.
He was arrested after the police raided the orgy in the penthouse apartment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacchanal - a drunken reveler; a riotous feast
  • Synonym: debauch - a wild gathering involving excessive drinking and promiscuity

Etymology

From Latin 'orgia', from Greek 'orgia' (secret rites, revels).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈɔː.dʒi / (BrE)
an occasion or act involving excessive or uncontrolled indulgence in something, especially food, drinking, or spending
excessive or uncontrolled indulgence

Examples

After receiving her bonus, she went on an orgy of shopping and dining out.
The city celebrated the victory with an orgy of fireworks and parades.
He regretted the orgy of unhealthy eating that lasted all weekend.

Synset Examples

an orgy of shopping
an emotional binge
a splurge of spending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binge - a period of excessive indulgence in an activity
  • Synonym: splurge - an act of spending money freely or extravagantly

Etymology

From Ancient Greek ὄργιον (orgion), meaning 'secret rite' or 'frenzied worship'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈɔː.dʒi / (BrE)
(in ancient Greek and Roman contexts) a secret religious rite involving singing, dancing, drinking, and sexual activity, performed in the worship of certain deities.
ancient secret religious rite

Examples

The ancient Greeks celebrated the god Dionysus with a secret orgy that lasted for days.
Historical texts describe the orgy as a night of ecstatic worship and feasting.
Initiates of the cult were sworn to secrecy about what occurred during the orgy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ritual - a religious or solemn ceremony consisting of a series of actions

Etymology

From Greek *orgia* (secret rites), plural of *orgion* (secret rite, festival).
