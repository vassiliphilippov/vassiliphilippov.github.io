wishbone

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪʃˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈwɪʃbəʊn / (BrE)
a forked bone found in birds, especially domestic fowl, formed by the fusion of the two clavicles
forked bone in birds, esp. chickens

Examples

The children pulled on each side of the wishbone to see whose wish would come true.
A chicken's wishbone is often saved after dinner for the traditional game.
The wishbone gives strength to the bird's chest for powerful wing movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furcula - the forked bone formed by the clavicles of a bird

Etymology

So called because of the custom of pulling the bone apart to make wishes.
