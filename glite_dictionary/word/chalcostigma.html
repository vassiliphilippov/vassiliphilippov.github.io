Chalcostigma

1. NOUN

/ kælkoʊˈstɪɡmə / (AmE), / kalkəʊˈstɪɡmə / (BrE)
A genus of South American hummingbirds known as thornbills, including species such as the rainbow-bearded thornbill and bronze-tailed thornbill.
a hummingbird genus (thornbills) of South America

Examples

The Chalcostigma genus includes several species of brightly colored thornbills found in the Andes.
One rare member of the Chalcostigma genus is the rainbow-bearded thornbill.
Ornithologists have studied the feeding habits of Chalcostigma hummingbirds extensively.

Etymology

From Greek khalkos (bronze) + stigma (mark, spot), referring to the metallic colors of the birds.

2. NOUN

/ kælkoʊˈstɪɡmə / (AmE), / kalkəʊˈstɪɡmə / (BrE)
A genus of small hummingbirds known as thornbills, native to South America, including species such as the rainbow-bearded thornbill and blue-mantled thornbill.
genus of hummingbirds (thornbills)

Examples

The Chalcostigma genus contains some of the most brilliantly colored thornbills in the Andes.
Several birdwatchers were thrilled to spot a Chalcostigma in the cloud forest.
Conservationists are studying the declining populations of Chalcostigma species in certain regions.

Etymology

From Greek khalcos (copper, bronze) + stigma (mark, spot).
