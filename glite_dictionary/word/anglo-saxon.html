Anglo-Saxon

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋɡloʊˈsæksən / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡləʊˈsæksən / (BrE)
A person of Anglo-Saxon descent, especially relating to the English-speaking people of England or their descendants.
a person of Anglo-Saxon descent or culture

Examples

The museum displayed ancient artifacts belonging to the Anglo-Saxon settlers of England.
Her family claims Anglo-Saxon heritage dating back to the Middle Ages.
Historians debate the impact of the Anglo-Saxon legal system on modern British law.

Synset Examples

in the ninth century the Vikings began raiding the Anglo-Saxons in Britain
his ancestors were not just British, they were Anglo-Saxons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: WASP - White Anglo-Saxon Protestant, a term for a social group of wealthy, well-connected white Americans of Protestant English descent

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *Anglo-Saxones*, combining Angli (Angles) and Saxones (Saxons), two Germanic tribes.

3. NOUN

/ ˌæŋɡloʊˈsæksən / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡləʊˈsæksən / (BrE)
a person who was a native or inhabitant of England before the Norman Conquest in 1066
native/inhibitant of England before 1066

Examples

The Anglo-Saxon settlers greatly influenced the English language and culture.
Archaeologists discovered an Anglo-Saxon burial site in the countryside.
Historians debate the extent of Anglo-Saxon society's organization before the Norman invasion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saxon - member of a Germanic people who migrated to England
  • Antonym: Norman - a member of the people who conquered England in 1066

Etymology

From Latin Angli Saxones, referring to the Germanic tribes (Angles and Saxons) who settled in England.

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˌæŋɡloʊˈsæksən / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡləʊˈsæksən / (BrE)
the English language as spoken and written in England before about 1100; Old English
Old English language

Examples

Scholars have studied Anglo-Saxon manuscripts preserved in British libraries.
She is fluent in several languages, including Anglo-Saxon.
Many modern English words have their roots in Anglo-Saxon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Old English - the English language from the 5th to the 12th century

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Anglo-Saxōnī, referring to the Germanic inhabitants (Angles and Saxons) of England.
