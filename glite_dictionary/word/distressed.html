distressed

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstrɛst / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɛst / (BrE)
feeling or showing anxiety, sorrow, or pain; suffering emotional or mental distress
suffering anxiety or sorrow

Examples

She looked distressed after hearing the bad news.
His distressed expression told us something was wrong.
Many distressed people sought comfort from their friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upset - feeling unhappy or worried
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling worry, anger, or anxiety

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'distress' (to subject to hardship or suffering), from Old French 'destrescer'.

/ dɪˈstɹɛst / (AmE), / dɪˈstɹɛst / (BrE)
experiencing financial trouble or difficulty; being in a state of economic hardship
experiencing financial difficulty

Examples

Many distressed businesses seek government assistance during economic downturns.
The bank specializes in purchasing distressed assets from companies facing bankruptcy.
Several distressed homeowners are struggling to keep up with their mortgage payments.

Synset Examples

distressed companies need loans and technical advice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insolvent - unable to pay debts owed
  • Synonym: bankrupt - declared in law unable to pay debts
  • Antonym: prosperous - financially successful, flourishing

Etymology

suffering from severe physical or emotional strain or pain; experiencing great trouble or discomfort.
suffering severe strain or discomfort

Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: comfortable - free from physical or emotional discomfort

Etymology

Examples
