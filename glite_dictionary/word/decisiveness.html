decisiveness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsaɪsɪvnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the ability to make decisions quickly and confidently; firmness in making choices or taking action
quality of being firm and resolute

Examples

Her decisiveness in a crisis earned her the respect of her colleagues.
The team's success was attributed to their coach's decisiveness.
Lack of decisiveness can lead to missed opportunities in business.

Synset Examples

a man of unusual decisiveness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indecisiveness - the quality of being unable to make decisions

Etymology

From 'decisive' + '-ness', with 'decisive' from Latin 'decidere' (to decide).
