accretive

1. ADJ

/ əˈkriːtɪv / (AmE), / əˈkriːtɪv / (BrE)
Increasing by gradual accumulation or addition; growing by accretion, especially used to describe financial or business growth resulting from acquisitions that add value.
growing by gradual accumulation

Examples

The company's merger was highly accretive to its earnings per share.
Over time, the sediment created an accretive layer along the riverbank.
Their investments have been consistently accretive to the value of the portfolio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: additive - involving addition; able to be added
  • Antonym: dilutive - reducing value or effect, particularly in finance

Etymology

From Latin 'accretio' (a growing, increase), from 'accrēscere' (to grow up).
