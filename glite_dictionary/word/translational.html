translational

1. ADJ

/ trænzˈleɪʃənəl / (AmE), / trænzˈleɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving movement in which all parts of an object move the same distance in the same direction, without rotation.
relating to linear, non-rotational movement

Examples

The car experienced only translational motion as it slid straight across the icy road.
In physics, translational movement refers to shifting an object without spinning it.
Robots can have both translational and rotational degrees of freedom in their design.

Etymology

From 'translation' (movement) + -al, from Latin 'translatio' (a carrying over).
