Ipidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.pɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈɪ.pɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of bark-boring or wood-boring beetles, including species that are destructive to forest and fruit trees
family of bark-boring beetles

Examples

The Ipidae are a major concern for forestry due to their wood-boring habits.
Several outbreaks of Ipidae infestations have devastated pine forests worldwide.
Scientists study Ipidae to develop better pest management techniques for fruit orchards.

Etymology

From New Latin, after the type genus Ips.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪpɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈɪpɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of bark- or wood-boring beetles known for their short beaks, some of which are very destructive to forest and fruit trees.
family of destructive bark-boring beetles

Examples

Many species in the Ipidae family are serious pests in commercial forests.
The damage caused by Ipidae beetles can devastate entire orchards of fruit trees.
Researchers are studying the life cycle of the Ipidae to develop better control methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bark beetle - any beetle that lives and feeds beneath the bark of trees; often destructive

Etymology

From genus name Ipes, with standard zoological family suffix -idae.
