Mithras

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɪθrəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪθrɑːs / (BrE)
an ancient Persian god associated with light, truth, and the sun, later worshipped in the Roman Empire
ancient Persian and Roman sun god

Examples

The cult of Mithras spread throughout the Roman Empire during the first few centuries AD.
Ancient temples dedicated to Mithras have been discovered in several parts of Europe.
Mythology credits Mithras with slaying a sacred bull, a key symbol in Mithraic rituals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mithraism - the mystery religion centered around Mithras

Etymology

From Old Persian *Mithra*, adopted into Latin as Mithras.
