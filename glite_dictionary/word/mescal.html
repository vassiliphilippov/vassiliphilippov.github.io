mescal

1. NOUN

/ mɛsˈkæl / (AmE), / mɛsˈkæl / (BrE)
a small, spineless, globe-shaped cactus native to Mexico and the southwestern United States, whose dried tops are used as mescal buttons and have hallucinogenic properties
a small cactus with hallucinogenic buttons

Examples

The Native Americans used mescal in certain rituals for its psychoactive effects.
Many researchers study mescal to understand traditional medicine practices.
He collected seeds of the mescal cactus during his trip to Texas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peyote - a cactus species known for its psychoactive properties

Etymology

From Nahuatl *mexcalli* (cooked agave), but in English, adapted to this cactus because of its association with ritual use.

2. NOUN

/ məˈskɑl / (AmE), / mɛˈskæl / (BrE)
a colorless Mexican liquor distilled from the fermented juices of agave plants, especially the century plant
Mexican liquor from agave

Examples

After dinner, we sampled a shot of mescal served with a slice of orange.
The region is famous for producing high-quality mescal from local agave plants.
He bought a bottle of artisanal mescal as a souvenir from his trip to Mexico.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tequila - a Mexican liquor made from a specific variety of agave

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'mezcal', from Nahuatl 'mexcalli' (baked agave).
