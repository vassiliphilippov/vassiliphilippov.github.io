Hernando Cortez

1. PROPN

/ hɜrˈnɑndoʊ kɔrˈtɛz / (AmE), / həˈnandəʊ kɔːˈtɛz / (BrE)
(1485–1547) A Spanish conquistador known for defeating the Aztec Empire and bringing Mexico under Spanish rule.
Spanish conquistador who conquered the Aztecs

Examples

Hernando Cortez led an expedition that caused the fall of the Aztec Empire in the early 16th century.
Many historians debate whether Hernando Cortez was a hero or a villain for his actions in Mexico.
During the conquest, Hernando Cortez forged alliances with some indigenous tribes against the Aztecs.
