horsemanship

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrs.mənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈhɔːs.mən.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the skill or art of riding, handling, and training horses
skill in handling and riding horses

Examples

Her horsemanship impressed everyone at the riding competition.
He learned horsemanship from a young age on his family's ranch.
Proper horsemanship requires patience, confidence, and a deep understanding of horses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equestrianism - the art or practice of horse riding and horsemanship

Etymology

Formed from 'horse' + 'manship', where 'manship' denotes skill or expertise in a particular activity.
