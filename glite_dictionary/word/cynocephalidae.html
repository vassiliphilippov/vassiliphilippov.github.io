Cynocephalidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪnoʊsəˈfælɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌsɪnəʊsɪˈfælıdiː / (BrE)
A family of mammals in the order Dermoptera, comprising the colugos, known for their ability to glide between trees.
family of gliding mammals (colugos)

Examples

The family Cynocephalidae consists of just two living species of colugos.
Members of Cynocephalidae are sometimes called flying lemurs, although they are not lemurs.
Scientists have long debated the evolutionary relationships of Cynocephalidae within the mammalian tree.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'kyno-' (dog) + 'kephalē' (head), originally referencing dog-like head shape.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˌnoʊsəˈfælɪˌdiː / (AmE), / saɪˌnəʊsɪˈfælɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of flying mammals (order Dermoptera) known as colugos or flying lemurs, native to Southeast Asia
family of flying mammals (colugos)

Examples

The Cynocephalidae are sometimes called flying lemurs, although they are not true lemurs.
Scientists studied the gliding membranes of Cynocephalidae to understand their unique locomotion.
There are only two living species classified in the family Cynocephalidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colugo - flying mammal of Southeast Asia; member of Cynocephalidae

Etymology

From Greek kynokephalos (dog-headed) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
