lamb's-quarter

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmzˌkwɔrtər / (AmE), / ˈlæmzˌkwɔːtə / (BrE)
a wild leafy plant (Chenopodium album) whose leaves are edible and often collected from the wild for food
an edible wild plant; Chenopodium album

Examples

The bland, spinach-like leaves of lamb's-quarter can be sautéed or added to salads for extra nutrition.
In the spring, foragers often collect lamb's-quarter from fields and gardens before it goes to seed.
Many traditional recipes use the tender leaves of lamb's-quarter as a substitute for spinach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goosefoot - another name for plants of the genus Chenopodium

Etymology

From Middle English 'lambesquatre', possibly referring to the plant's abundance around the time of the feast of Lammas; distinguished by reference to 'quarter' in older English plant names.
