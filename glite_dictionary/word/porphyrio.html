Porphyrio

1. NOUN

/ pɔːrˈfɪrioʊ / (AmE), / pɔːˈfɪrɪəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the rail family (Rallidae), known as Old World purple gallinules, found in wetlands across Africa, Eurasia, and Australasia.
genus of Old World purple gallinules

Examples

The Porphyrio species are known for their vibrant blue and purple plumage.
Wetlands in Australia are home to several Porphyrio birds.
The zoologist specialized in studying the behavior of Porphyrio gallinules.

Etymology

From Latin *Porphyrio*, from Ancient Greek *porphýra* (purple), referring to the birds' coloration.
