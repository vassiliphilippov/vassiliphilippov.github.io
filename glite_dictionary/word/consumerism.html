consumerism

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsuːmərˌɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˈsjuːmərɪzəm / (BrE)
The practice or movement of protecting the interests and rights of consumers, especially by promoting fair trade and product safety.
advocacy of consumer protection

Examples

Many activists support consumerism to ensure that products are safe and honestly advertised.
The rise of consumerism in the 20th century led to new laws concerning product quality.
Organizations dedicated to consumerism often campaign against misleading advertising and unfair business practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consumer protection - the safeguarding of buyers' rights and interests
  • Antonym: producerism - advocacy of the interests of producers as opposed to consumers

Etymology

From 'consumer' + '-ism', first recorded in early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈsuːmɚˌɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˈsjuːmərɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief or theory that increasing the consumption of goods and services is beneficial to the economy.
belief in economic benefit of increased consumption

Examples

Modern advertising is often blamed for encouraging consumerism in society.
Critics argue that consumerism leads to overconsumption and environmental damage.
The rise of consumerism has changed the way businesses market their products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: materialism - the tendency to consider material possessions as more important than spiritual values
  • Antonym: minimalism - a style or philosophy advocating minimal consumption or simplicity

Etymology

From 'consumer' + '-ism', first used in the early 20th century.
