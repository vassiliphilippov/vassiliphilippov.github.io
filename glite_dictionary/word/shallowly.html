shallowly

1. ADV

/ ˈʃæloʊli / (AmE), / ˈʃaləʊli / (BrE)
In a manner that lacks depth; superficially or near the surface.
in a superficial or not deep manner

Examples

The water was only a few inches deep, so the boat floated shallowly near the shore.
He breathed shallowly as nerves got the best of him during the performance.
Some critics argue that the novel explores its themes shallowly, without true insight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficially - in a superficial or shallow manner
  • Antonym: deeply - in a deep or profound manner

Etymology

Formed from 'shallow' + '-ly', indicating the manner of action.
