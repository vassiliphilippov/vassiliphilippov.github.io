aggrandisement

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡræn.dɪz.mənt / (AmE), / əˈɡræn.dɪz.mənt / (BrE)
the act of increasing the power, wealth, importance, or reputation of a person or thing, often to an excessive or unjustifiable degree.
increase in power, importance, or wealth

Examples

The king's constant aggrandisement of his own power eventually led to unrest among his subjects.
Some critics accused the company of seeking aggrandisement at the expense of its workers.
His speech was less about the event itself and more about the aggrandisement of his personal achievements.

Synset Examples

the aggrandizement of the king
his elevation to cardinal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exaltation - the action of elevating someone in rank, power, or character
  • Antonym: diminution - the act of decreasing or reducing in size, importance, or value

Etymology

From French *agrandissement*, from *agrandir* (to make larger), from Latin *grandis* (large, great).
