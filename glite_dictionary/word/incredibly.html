incredibly

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈkrɛdəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrɛdəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is difficult to believe; amazingly or extraordinarily.
in a way that is hard to believe

Examples

Incredibly, the little girl found her way home after being lost for two days.
The team incredibly managed to win despite being one player short.
He finished the marathon in an incredibly fast time.

Synset Examples

incredibly, she survived the crash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbelievably - in a way that is hard to believe

Etymology

From 'incredible' + '-ly'; 'incredible' from Latin incredibilis (unbelievable).

2. ADV

/ ɪnˈkrɛdəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrɛdəbli / (BrE)
to an extraordinary or extremely great degree; extremely.
extremely; to an extraordinary degree

Examples

The weather was incredibly hot during our visit to the city.
She finished the marathon incredibly fast, surprising everyone with her pace.
This meal tastes incredibly delicious; could I have the recipe?

Synset Examples

she plays fabulously well
behind you the coastal hills plunge to the incredibly blue sea backed by the Turkish mountains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extremely - to a very great degree
  • Degree Lower: slightly - to a small degree

Etymology

Formed from 'incredible' + the adverb suffix '-ly'.
