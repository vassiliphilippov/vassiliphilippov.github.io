cyan

1. ADJ

/ ˈsaɪ.æn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.æn / (BrE)
of a greenish-blue color, situated between blue and green in the spectrum, and one of the primary subtractive colors in printing and color science.
greenish-blue color; primary subtractive color

Examples

The sky was a brilliant shade of cyan just before sunset.
She chose cyan paint for her bedroom walls to create a vibrant atmosphere.
The designer used a cyan background in the logo for a modern, fresh look.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: turquoise - a greenish-blue color, similar to cyan but lighter and more bluish
  • Degree Higher: teal - a dark greenish-blue color; related but darker than cyan

Etymology

From Greek 'kyanos' meaning 'dark blue' or 'lapis lazuli'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.æn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.æn / (BrE)
a greenish-blue color, one of the primary subtractive colors used in printing and color theory
a greenish-blue color

Examples

The printer was out of cyan ink, so the colors looked distorted.
She painted her room a shade of cyan to create a calming atmosphere.
Mixing cyan with yellow will give you different shades of green.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue - a primary color between green and violet
  • Degree Lower: turquoise - a greenish-blue color
  • Degree Higher: teal - a dark greenish-blue color

Etymology

From Greek kyanos (dark blue, lapis lazuli), via Latin cyanus.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04975796-n
Synset POS: n
