Erechtites hieracifolia

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈrɛktaɪtiːz haɪˌɛrəsɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ɛˈrɛktaɪtiːz hɪəˌræsɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A North American weedy plant with small white or greenish flowers, commonly known as American burnweed.
a North American weedy plant

Examples

The abandoned field was quickly overtaken by Erechtites hieracifolia in late summer.
Botanists often study Erechtites hieracifolia because of its rapid colonization in disturbed soils.
In many gardens, Erechtites hieracifolia is regarded as a persistent weed.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Erechtites and species name hieracifolia (from Greek 'hierax', hawk, and Latin 'folia', leaves, meaning 'hawkweed-leaved').
