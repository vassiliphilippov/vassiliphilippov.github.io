Xylomelum pyriforme

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˌloʊˌmɛləm paɪˈrɪfɔrmi / (AmE), / zaɪˌlɒˌmɛləm paɪˈrɪfɔːmiː / (BrE)
a tree native to Australia that produces pear-shaped fruit with a thick, woody outer shell
Australian tree with pear-shaped woody fruit

Examples

The fruit of the Xylomelum pyriforme is hard and woody, making it unique among Australian plants.
Botanists studied the growth habits of Xylomelum pyriforme in the wild.
The thick, pear-shaped fruit of the Xylomelum pyriforme helps protect its seeds from fire.

Etymology

From Greek xylon (wood) + melon (apple), with pyriforme meaning 'pear-shaped' in Latin.
