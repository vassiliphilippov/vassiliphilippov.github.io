Lecanopteris

1. NOUN

/ ˌliːkəˈnɒptərɪs / (AmE), / ˌlɛkəˈnɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of epiphytic ferns found in southeastern Asia to New Guinea, notable for their mutualistic relationship with ants.
genus of epiphytic ferns

Examples

The Lecanopteris genus includes several species that provide shelter for ants in their hollow rhizomes.
Botanists discovered a new Lecanopteris species in the forests of New Guinea.
Many collectors prize Lecanopteris ferns for their unique appearance and mutualistic relationship with ants.

Etymology

From Greek 'lekanos' (bowl) and 'pteris' (fern), referring to the shape of the rhizome and the plant's fern family.
