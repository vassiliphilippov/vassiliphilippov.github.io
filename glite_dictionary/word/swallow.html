swallow

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɑːloʊ / (AmE), / ˈswɒləʊ / (BrE)
an act of passing food, drink, or another substance down the throat
an act of swallowing

Examples

He took a quick swallow of water before answering the question.
After each swallow, she felt her sore throat ache.
The medicine had a bitter taste on the first swallow.

Synset Examples

one swallow of the liquid was enough
he took a drink of his beer and smacked his lips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gulp - an act of swallowing quickly or in large amounts

Etymology

Old English *swelgan* (to swallow), related to Dutch *zwelgen*, German *schwelgen*.
