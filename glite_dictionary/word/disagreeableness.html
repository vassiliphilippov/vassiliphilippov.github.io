disagreeableness

1. NOUN

/ dɪs.əˈɡri.ə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / dɪs.əˈɡriː.ə.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being unpleasant, unfriendly, or difficult to get along with.
quality of being unpleasant or unfriendly

Examples

Her disagreeableness made it difficult for her to keep friends for long.
The disagreeableness of his tone discouraged further discussion.
Despite her intelligence, her disagreeableness was often a barrier to collaboration.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pleasantness - the quality of being pleasant
  • Synonym: disagreeable - having an unpleasant or unfriendly nature

Etymology

Formed from 'disagreeable' + suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈɡriəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈɡriːəblnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being unpleasant, ill-tempered, or difficult to get along with.
unpleasant or ill-tempered disposition

Examples

Her disagreeableness made it difficult for her coworkers to enjoy working with her.
The persistent disagreeableness of the weather ruined their holiday plans.
He struggled to control his disagreeableness during stressful situations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agreeableness - quality of being pleasant and easy to get along with

Etymology

From 'disagreeable' + '-ness', with 'disagreeable' from the prefix 'dis-' (not) and 'agreeable'.
