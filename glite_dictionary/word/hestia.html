Hestia

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛstiə / (AmE), / ˈhɛstiə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the goddess of the hearth, home, and domesticity; identified with the Roman goddess Vesta.
Greek goddess of the hearth

Examples

Many ancient Greeks honored Hestia with offerings at their household hearths.
The temple of Hestia was a sacred space in every Greek city.
In mythology, Hestia was considered one of the gentle and non-confrontational Olympian deities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vesta - Roman goddess of hearth and home, equivalent to Hestia

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἑστία (Hestía), name of the goddess.
