synezesis

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnɛzɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈniːzɪs / (BrE)
the contraction of chromatin towards one side of the nucleus during the prophase of meiosis
contraction of chromatin in meiosis (biology)
medical

Examples

During meiosis, synezesis results in the chromatin clumping to one side of the nucleus.
The phenomenon of synezesis was observed under the microscope in certain plant cells.
Researchers continue to study the implications of synezesis for chromosomal behavior during cell division.

Etymology

From Greek synezēsis (a coming together, contraction), from syn- (together) + heniēmi (to send).
