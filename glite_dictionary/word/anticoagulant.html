anticoagulant

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntikoʊˈæɡjələnt / (AmE), / ˌæntikəʊˈæɡjʊlənt / (BrE)
a substance or medication that prevents or slows the clotting of blood
medicine that delays blood clotting
medical

Examples

Patients with a high risk of stroke are often prescribed an anticoagulant to prevent blood clots.
Warfarin is a commonly used anticoagulant in long-term therapy for heart conditions.
The doctor adjusted the dose of the anticoagulant based on the patient's blood test results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood thinner - a common synonym for anticoagulant, especially in non-technical contexts

Etymology

From anti- (against) + coagulant (agent causing coagulation), ultimately from Latin 'coagulare' (to curdle).
