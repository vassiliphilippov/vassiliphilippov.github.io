rauwolfia

1. NOUN

/ raʊˈwʊlfiə / (AmE), / raʊˈwʌlfiə / (BrE)
Any shrub or small tree of the genus Rauwolfia, having leaves in whorls and cymose flowers, whose roots yield substances used medicinally, especially as emetics, purgatives, or antihypertensives.
medicinal shrub of the genus Rauwolfia
medical

Examples

The roots of rauwolfia are dried and processed for medicinal use.
Many antihypertensive drugs have been derived from rauwolfia plants.
Traditional healers have long used extracts of rauwolfia to treat various ailments.

Etymology

Named after Leonhard Rauwolf (1535–1596), a German physician and botanist.

2. NOUN

/ raʊˈwʊlfiə / (AmE), / raʊˈwɒlfɪə / (BrE)
any of several alkaloid compounds extracted from the roots of the Rauwolfia plant, especially Rauwolfia serpentina, used in medicine for their antihypertensive and sedative properties.
alkaloids from Rauwolfia plant used in medicine
medical

Examples

Doctors prescribed rauwolfia to help control his high blood pressure.
Research into rauwolfia has led to the development of important antihypertensive drugs.
Some patients experience side effects when treated with rauwolfia alkaloids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserpine - an alkaloid drug obtained from Rauwolfia used to treat high blood pressure

Etymology

Named after the genus Rauwolfia, itself after German physician Leonhard Rauwolf (1535–1596).
