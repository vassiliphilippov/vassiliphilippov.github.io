performance

1. NOUN

/ pərˈfɔrməns / (AmE), / pəˈfɔːməns / (BrE)
the way in which a machine, system, or process functions or operates
manner of functioning or operating

Examples

The new software improved the overall performance of the computer.
They tested the performance of several engines under different conditions.
After years of use, the car's performance started to decline.

Synset Examples

the power of its engine determines its operation
the plane's operation in high winds
they compared the cooking performance of each oven
the jet's performance conformed to high standards

Related Terms

  • Synonym: operation - the fact or condition of functioning or being active
  • Antonym: malfunction - failure to function properly

Etymology

From Old French 'parformance', from 'parformer' (to perform).

/ pərˈfɔrməns / (AmE), / pəˈfɔːməns / (BrE)

public presentation of entertainment

Examples

Synset Examples

  • Antonym: rehearsal - a practice session for a public performance

From Old French 'parformance', from 'parformer' (to perform), ultimately from Middle English.
