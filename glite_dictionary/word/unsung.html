unsung

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsʌŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈsʌŋ / (BrE)
not celebrated, praised, or recognized, especially for accomplishments or good deeds
not celebrated or recognized

Examples

The teacher was one of the unsung heroes of the community.
Many unsung individuals make important contributions behind the scenes.
Their efforts remained unsung for years until they were finally acknowledged.

Synset Examples

an obscure family
unsung heroes of the war

Related Terms

  • Antonym: celebrated - famous or widely praised; opposite of 'unsung'

Etymology

From Old English 'un-' (not) + 'sungan', past participle of 'sing' (to praise in song).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsʌŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈsʌŋ / (BrE)
(of a person, achievement, or thing) not celebrated or acknowledged, although deserving praise.
not acknowledged or celebrated

Examples

She was an unsung hero who quietly made an enormous difference in the community.
Many of the team's most important members remain unsung despite their hard work.
The museum's collection contains many unsung masterpieces rarely seen by the public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unheralded - not previously announced, recognized, or celebrated
  • Antonym: celebrated - widely praised and recognized

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'sung', past participle of 'sing', meaning praised or celebrated.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00028980-s
Synset POS: s
