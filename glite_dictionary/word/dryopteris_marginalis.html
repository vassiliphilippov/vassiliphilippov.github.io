Dryopteris marginalis

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈɑptərɪs mɑrˈdʒɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / draɪˈɒptərɪs mɑːˈdʒɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
A North American fern with evergreen fronds, commonly found in woods and rocky areas.
North American evergreen fern

Examples

The shady slope was covered with Dryopteris marginalis, adding greenery even through winter.
Gardeners often choose Dryopteris marginalis for its hardy evergreen foliage.
Botanists identified the fern specimen as Dryopteris marginalis during the field survey.

Etymology

From Greek 'dryas' (oak), 'pteris' (fern), and Latin 'marginalis' (of the edge), referring to the placement of sori near the leaf margins.
