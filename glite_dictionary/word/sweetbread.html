sweetbread

1. NOUN

/ ˈswitˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈswiːtbred / (BrE)
The thymus or pancreas of a young animal, especially a calf or lamb, cooked and eaten as food.
edible animal gland (thymus or pancreas)

Examples

The chef prepared the sweetbread with a delicate mushroom sauce.
Many fine dining restaurants feature sweetbreads as a specialty appetizer.
It can be challenging to properly clean and cook sweetbread to achieve the right texture.

Etymology

Early modern English; originally from 'sweet' + 'bread', referring to the mild flavor and bread-like texture.
