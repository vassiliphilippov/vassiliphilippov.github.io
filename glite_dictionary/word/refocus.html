to refocus

1. VERB

/ riˈfoʊkəs / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfəʊkəs / (BrE)
to adjust something again so that it is in focus, or to concentrate attention or effort anew.
adjust focus again; renew attention

Examples

The photographer had to refocus the lens to get a sharper picture.
After a short break, the students were able to refocus on their assignments.
You should refocus your efforts on meeting this month's sales target.

Synset Examples

refocus the image until it is very sharp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - to adjust in order to make clear or sharp

Etymology

Prefix re- (again) + focus.

2. VERB

/ riˈfoʊkəs / (AmE), / riˈfəʊkəs / (BrE)
to adjust or direct something so that it is in focus again, either physically or figuratively
bring into focus again

Examples

After adjusting the lens, she had to refocus the camera for a clearer image.
The company decided to refocus its strategy on customer satisfaction.
He paused to refocus his attention on the speaker after being distracted.

Synset Examples

The physicist refocused the light beam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - to adjust to make something clear or sharp
  • Antonym: defocus - to make something less clear or sharp

Etymology

Formed from 're-' (again) + 'focus'; first recorded in the 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02166592-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌriˈfoʊkəs / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfəʊkəs / (BrE)
to direct attention or adjust focus again, especially after being distracted or after circumstances have changed
to focus attention or optics again

Examples

After a short break, the team was able to refocus on the project and make significant progress.
She had to refocus her attention after being interrupted several times.
The photographer adjusted the lens to refocus on the moving subject.

Synset Examples

The group needs to refocus its goals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - to concentrate attention or effort

Etymology

From re- (again) + focus, originally from Latin focus (hearth, fireplace).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00734114-v
Synset POS: v
