Leitneria

1. NOUN

/ laɪtˈnɪəriə / (AmE), / laɪtˈnɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of small deciduous trees or shrubs known as corkwood, native to the southeastern United States.
a genus of trees known as corkwood

Examples

The rare Leitneria is found in wetlands of the southern United States.
Botanists were excited to discover a stand of Leitneria in the wildlife reserve.
The wood of Leitneria is extremely light and spongy, making it unique among native trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corkwood - common name for the tree Leitneria floridana

Etymology

Named after German botanist E. F. Leitner (1812–1838).

2. NOUN

/ laɪtˈnɪriə / (AmE), / laɪtˈnɪəriə / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants containing a single species, commonly known as corkwood.
genus of plant (corkwood)

Examples

The rare tree Leitneria is found in swampy areas of the southeastern United States.
Botanists collected samples of Leitneria for their research on cork-producing plants.
Corkwood, the only known species of Leitneria, has unusually light wood.

Etymology

Named after the Austrian botanist E. F. Leitner (1812–1838).
