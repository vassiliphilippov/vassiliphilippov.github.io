acorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪˌkɔrn / (AmE), / ˈeɪˌkɔːn / (BrE)
the fruit of the oak tree, consisting of a smooth nut in a woody, cup-shaped base
fruit of the oak tree

Examples

The squirrel quickly picked up an acorn and dashed up the tree.
In autumn, the forest floor is often covered with fallen acorns.
Many animals rely on the acorn as an important food source in the wild.

Etymology

Old English æcern, originally meaning 'fruit' or 'berry', later specialized to oak fruit.
