Xanthium

1. NOUN

/ ˈzænθiəm / (AmE), / ˈzanθɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of coarse herbs with small heads of greenish flowers that produce burrs covered with hooked bristles
a genus of coarse, burr-producing herbs

Examples

The fields were scattered with Xanthium, their burrs clinging to the animals' fur.
Researchers studied the spread of Xanthium in agricultural areas due to its invasive nature.
Children learned to avoid the prickly Xanthium plants growing near the riverbank.

Etymology

From Greek xanthos (yellow), referring to the color of some species' parts.
