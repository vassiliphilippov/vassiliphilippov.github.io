Opuntiales

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌpʌnˈtaɪəliːz / (AmE), / əʊˌpʌnˈtɪəliːz / (BrE)
a former order of flowering plants in the cactus family, now generally included within Cactaceae (cactuses)
an order of cactuses (now in Cactaceae)

Examples

The family Cactaceae was once classified in the order Opuntiales in some botanical systems.
Recent genetic studies led to the merging of Opuntiales into the family Cactaceae.
Botanists used to distinguish Opuntiales as a separate order, but this is no longer common.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cactaceae - the botanical family comprising the cactuses

Etymology

From Opuntia, a genus of cacti, and -ales (a standard suffix for plant orders).

2. NOUN

/ oʊˌpʌnˈtaɪəliːz / (AmE), / əʊˌpʌnˈtɪəliːz / (BrE)
a former taxonomic order of flowering plants, now usually included in the family Cactaceae, comprising cactuses.
plant order including cactuses

Examples

The Opuntiales are now usually included within the family Cactaceae in modern classification systems.
Early botanists used Opuntiales to group many cacti species together before further studies refined their classifications.
Some textbooks still refer to Opuntiales when discussing the evolution of cactuses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cactaceae - the botanical family containing cactuses

Etymology

From Opuntia, the type genus, named after the Greek town of Opus.
