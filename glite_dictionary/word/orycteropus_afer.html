Orycteropus afer

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˌrɪktəˈroʊpəs ˈæfər / (AmE), / ˌɒrɪktəˈrəʊpəs ˈɑːfə / (BrE)
A nocturnal, burrowing mammal native to Africa, known for feeding on termites and being the only living species in the order Tubulidentata.
African burrowing mammal; aardvark

Examples

The Orycteropus afer uses its strong claws to dig for termites at night.
Despite its pig-like snout, the Orycteropus afer is not closely related to pigs.
Researchers observed the Orycteropus afer excavating extensive burrows in the African savanna.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aardvark - nocturnal burrowing mammal native to Africa that feeds on ants and termites

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek oryktēr (digger) + pous (foot); afer meaning 'African' in Latin.
