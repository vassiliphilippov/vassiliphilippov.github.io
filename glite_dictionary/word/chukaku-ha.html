Chukaku-Ha

1. PROPN

/ tʃuˈkɑku hɑ / (AmE), / tʃuˈkɑːkuː hɑː / (BrE)
an ultra-leftist militant group in Japan, founded in 1957 after a split from the Japanese Communist Party, known for property damage during protest actions
Japanese ultra-leftist militant group (1957–)

Examples

The police suspected Chukaku-Ha was behind last night's attack on the government building.
Several radical activists were linked to Chukaku-Ha during the 1970s.
Media reports described Chukaku-Ha as focusing on sabotage rather than violent confrontation.

Synset Examples

Chukaku-Ha attacks tend to cause property damage rather than casualties

Etymology

From Japanese 中核派 (Chūkakuha), meaning 'Core Faction'.
