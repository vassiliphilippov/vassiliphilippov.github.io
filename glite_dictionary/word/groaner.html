groaner

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡroʊnər / (AmE), / ˈɡrəʊnə / (BrE)
a person who groans, especially audibly or habitually
person who groans

Examples

When the dentist started drilling, the worst groaner in the waiting room grew even louder.
Several groaners could be heard after the team lost the match.
He's such a groaner during scary movies, everyone notices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moaner - a person who complains or expresses pain with moans

Etymology

From groan + -er, forming an agent noun.
