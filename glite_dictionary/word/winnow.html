winnow

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɪnəʊ / (BrE)
the act of separating grain from chaff by means of a current of air
act of separating grain from chaff

Examples

The winnow was performed by hand before the invention of mechanical harvesters.
After the harvest, the villagers gathered for the annual winnow in the main square.
She observed the traditional winnow with fascination, watching the chaff blow away in the breeze.

Synset Examples

the winnowing was done by women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threshing - separating grain from its husks

Etymology

Old English *windwian* (to fan, to winnow), from *wind* (wind).
