despite

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspaɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈspaɪt / (BrE)
(archaic) Contemptuous treatment, insult, or scorn; the feeling or expression of despising someone or something.
contemptuous disregard (archaic noun)
archaic

Examples

He acted out of pure despite, caring nothing for the consequences.
She held him in despite and refused his offers of reconciliation.
Old letters revealed a tone of despite that surprised her descendants.

Synset Examples

she wanted neither favor nor despite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contempt - the feeling that someone or something is unworthy of respect
  • Antonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration for someone or something

Etymology

From Old French 'despit', from Latin 'despectus' (a looking down on, scorn).
