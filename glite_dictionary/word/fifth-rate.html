fifth-rate

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪfθˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪfθˌreɪt / (BrE)
of very low quality; markedly inferior or poor in standard
of very low quality

Examples

Many viewers considered the show a fifth-rate attempt to copy earlier hits.
My experience at the fifth-rate hotel left much to be desired.
He was disappointed by the fifth-rate service he received at the restaurant.

Synset Examples

Critics called the restaurant a fifth-rate establishment.
The movie was a fifth-rate production with no plot.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: second-rate - of mediocre or rather low quality
  • Degree Higher: first-rate - of the highest quality
  • Degree Higher: fourth-rate - slightly better than fifth-rate, still poor quality

Etymology

Modelled on historical rating systems (such as shipping) where 'first-rate' was the highest quality; 'fifth-rate' denotes low rank or status.
