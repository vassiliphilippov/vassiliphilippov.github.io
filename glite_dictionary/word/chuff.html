to chuff

1. VERB

/ tʃʌf / (AmE), / tʃʌf / (BrE)
(of an engine, animal, or person) to blow or breathe out loudly and forcefully; to make a puffing noise.
to blow or puff loudly

Examples

The old train chuffed into the station, steam billowing from its engine.
The tiger chuffed softly as it greeted its cubs at the zoo.
As he reached the top of the stairs, he chuffed and tried to catch his breath.

Synset Examples

he huffed and puffed as he made his way up the mountain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huff - to breathe out loudly, especially in anger or exertion
  • Synonym: puff - to breathe or blow out in short bursts
