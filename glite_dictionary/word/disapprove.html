to disapprove

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.əˈpruv / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈpruːv / (BrE)
to have or express an unfavorable opinion about something; to consider something wrong or inappropriate
consider something wrong or inappropriate

Examples

Many parents disapprove of letting children use smartphones at a young age.
The committee strongly disapproved of the proposed changes to the policy.
She disapproves of wearing casual clothes to formal events.

Synset Examples

I disapprove of her child rearing methods

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to approve - to regard something as good or acceptable

Etymology

From Old French desaprover, from des- (expressing reversal) + approuver (to approve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00808840-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.əˈpruv / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈpruːv / (BrE)
to think that someone or something is bad or wrong; to not approve
consider bad or wrong

Examples

Many parents disapprove of smoking among teenagers.
The committee members strongly disapproved the proposed changes.
She knew her friends would probably disapprove of her decision.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to approve - to officially agree to or accept as good

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (opposite of) + 'approbare' (to approve).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
