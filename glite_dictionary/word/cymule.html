cymule

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪmjuːl / (AmE), / ˈsɪmjuːl / (BrE)
A small, usually few-flowered cyme; a cluster of flowers arranged in a particular way, typically found as a subdivision of a larger inflorescence.
small, few-flowered cyme (botany)

Examples

Each branch of the plant ends in a cymule containing just a handful of blossoms.
The tiny cymules are an identifying feature of this species.
Under the microscope, the cymule structure becomes evident in younger shoots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyme - A type of flower cluster in which the central flower opens first

Etymology

From 'cyme' (a type of flower cluster) with diminutive suffix '-ule'.
