Acris gryllus

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.krɪs ˈɡrɪl.əs / (AmE), / ˈæ.krɪs ˈɡrɪl.əs / (BrE)
A species of cricket frog native to the eastern United States, known for its distinctive call.
species of cricket frog in eastern US

Examples

The Acris gryllus is often found near ponds and marshes in southeastern states.
Researchers recorded the call of Acris gryllus to study its breeding habits.
Unlike other frogs, the Acris gryllus has rough, warty skin and a high-pitched chirp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cricket frog - a small North American frog of the genus Acris

Etymology

From Latin; Acris is the genus name for cricket frogs, gryllus means 'cricket'.
