Pteroclididae

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈklɪdəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌtɛərəˈklɪdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds known as sandgrouse, inhabiting dry regions of the Old World, characterized by stocky bodies and long pointed wings.
family of birds (sandgrouse)

Examples

The family Pteroclididae includes several species of sandgrouse found across Africa and Asia.
Researchers observed that members of the Pteroclididae are highly adapted to arid, sandy habitats.
Pteroclididae are known for their unique ability to carry water in their feathers to their young.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandgrouse - a bird of the family Pteroclididae, adapted to arid environments

Etymology

From modern Latin Pteroclididae, from Greek pteron (wing) and klid- (key, referring to the bird's shape) plus family suffix -idae.
