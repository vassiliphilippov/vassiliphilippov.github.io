Calamus australis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləməs ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈkaləməs ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (BrE)
a tall, scrambling, spiny palm native to northeastern Queensland, Australia, known for its climbing stems and distinctive spines
a spiny climbing palm of Queensland, Australia

Examples

The thick stems of Calamus australis are often used by local communities for weaving furniture.
Botanists in Australia have documented the unique growth patterns of Calamus australis.
The rainforest trails were covered by the long, spiny vines of Calamus australis.

Etymology

From Latin 'calamus' (reed) and Latin 'australis' (southern).
