Carpodacus purpureus

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑrpəˈdeɪkəs pərˈpjʊəriəs / (AmE), / ˌkɑːpəˈdeɪkəs pəˈpjʊəriəs / (BrE)
A North American finch species with a raspberry-red head, breast, and rump, commonly known as the purple finch.
North American purple finch species

Examples

The Carpodacus purpureus can often be spotted at backyard feeders across the northeastern United States.
Male Carpodacus purpureus are noted for their vibrant raspberry-red coloring.
Researchers have documented a decline in Carpodacus purpureus populations in certain regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purple finch - Common name for Carpodacus purpureus, a North American finch with red plumage

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Carpodacus' (genus name from Greek 'karpos' (fruit) + 'dakno' (to bite)), 'purpureus' from Latin meaning 'purple'.
