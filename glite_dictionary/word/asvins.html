Asvins

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːʃvɪnz / (AmE), / ˈɑːʃvɪnz / (BrE)
In Hinduism, the twin chariot warriors who serve as attendants and physicians to Surya, the sun god.
twin chariot warriors in Hindu mythology

Examples

The Asvins are revered as divine healers in ancient Hindu scriptures.
Legends describe the Asvins as shining twin horsemen riding across the dawn sky.
Prayers are often offered to the Asvins for health and well-being in Vedic rituals.

Etymology

From Sanskrit aśvin (possessing horses), from aśva (horse); figures in Vedic mythology.
