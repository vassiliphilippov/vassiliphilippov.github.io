fallacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæləsi / (AmE), / ˈfæləsi / (BrE)
A mistaken belief or idea, especially one based on unsound reasoning or logic.
mistaken belief or error in reasoning

Examples

The argument is based on a common fallacy that correlation equals causation.
Recognizing a fallacy is essential when evaluating persuasive speeches or advertisements.
He pointed out the fallacy in her reasoning during the debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misconception - an incorrect or mistaken understanding, especially one based on faulty thinking
  • Antonym: truth - something that is true or in accordance with fact or reality

Etymology

From Latin fallacia (deceit, trick), from fallax (deceptive).
