oriented

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔːriˌɛntɪd / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɪˌɛntɪd / (BrE)
adjusted or positioned in relation to surroundings, circumstances, or a particular focus; sometimes used in combination to show what something is directed towards
adjusted or directed in relation to something

Examples

The conference was oriented toward professionals seeking career advancement.
New arrivals need a week to become oriented to the city's layout and culture.
The school offers a science-oriented curriculum for students interested in technology.

Synset Examples

the house had its large windows oriented toward the ocean view
helping freshmen become oriented to college life
the book is value-oriented throughout

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unoriented - not oriented; not adjusted or directed in relation to something
  • Synonym: aligned - arranged in a straight line or in correct relative positions

Etymology

From 'orient' (to align or position), from Latin 'orientare', meaning 'to rise' or 'to face east'.
