basal

1. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsəl / (BrE)
forming or relating to the essential or basic part of something; fundamental.
basic, fundamental

Examples

The researcher explained that breathing is a basal human function necessary for survival.
Teachers often use basal readers to help children develop essential reading skills.
Good nutrition is a basal requirement for healthy growth in all mammals.

Synset Examples

an elementary need for love and nurturing
a basal reader

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basic - forming an essential foundation; fundamental
  • Synonym: fundamental - forming a necessary base or core; of central importance

Etymology

From Latin basalis, from basis (base).

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsəl / (BrE)
Serving as or forming a base; relating to the bottom layer or foundation of something.
forming a base or foundation

Examples

The basal layer of the plant stem provides support for new growth.
Paleontologists discovered basal features common to early reptiles.
The basal diet was used as the foundation for the experiment.

Synset Examples

the painter applied a base coat followed by two finishing coats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basic - forming an essential foundation or starting point

Etymology

From Latin 'basis' (base, foundation) via Late Latin 'basalis'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01861961-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsəl / (BrE)
(of leaves or other plant parts) situated at or arising from the base of a plant or stem, often growing directly from the root or rootstock.
located at the base (botany)

Examples

The basal leaves of the plant are larger and broader than those higher up the stem.
In some species, flowers develop from basal buds close to the root.
A basal rosette is common in many herbaceous plants.

Synset Examples

basal placentation
radical leaves

Related Terms

  • Antonym: apical - situated at or near the tip (especially of a plant or stem)

Etymology

From Latin 'basis' (base, foundation) via Greek 'basis'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00319565-a
Synset POS: a
