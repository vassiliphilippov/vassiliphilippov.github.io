Eumeces

1. NOUN

/ juˈmiːsiːz / (AmE), / juˈmiːsiːz / (BrE)
A genus of skinks native to the Americas, known for their smooth, shiny scales and elongated bodies.
genus of American skinks

Examples

Several species within the genus Eumeces are widespread across North and Central America.
Researchers discovered a new Eumeces specimen in the arid regions of Mexico.
The smooth, shiny appearance of Eumeces makes them easily identifiable among skinks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skink - a type of smooth-scaled lizard in the family Scincidae

Etymology

From Greek *eumekēs* (of good length, long). Used as a genus name in zoological taxonomy.

/ juˈmiːsiːz / (AmE), / juˈmiːsiːz / (BrE)
A genus of skinks native to the New World, comprising several species of smooth-scaled lizards.
a genus of New World skinks

Examples

The biologist identified the lizard as a member of the Eumeces genus.
Several species in Eumeces are found throughout North and Central America.
Researchers study Eumeces to better understand the evolutionary history of skinks.
