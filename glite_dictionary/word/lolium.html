Lolium

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊliəm / (AmE), / ˈlɒliəm / (BrE)
A genus of grasses known as ryegrass or darnel, some species of which are cultivated as forage or weeds in cereal crops.
genus of grasses: ryegrass, darnel

Examples

The field was overrun with Lolium, making harvesting more difficult for the farmers.
Some species of Lolium are important forage grasses in temperate climates.
Weeds such as Lolium can significantly affect cereal crops by competing for nutrients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ryegrass - common name for species in the Lolium genus
  • Synonym: darnel - another common name for Lolium species, especially Lolium temulentum

Etymology

From Latin 'lolium', referring to darnel or tares.

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊliəm / (AmE), / ˈləʊliəm / (BrE)
A genus of grasses including the ryegrasses and darnels, widely grown as forage and turf grasses.
genus of grasses including ryegrasses and darnels

Examples

Lolium species are often sown for grazing livestock in temperate climates.
Ryegrass, belonging to the genus Lolium, is valued for its rapid growth and palatability.
The agricultural scientist studied various varieties of Lolium to improve pasture quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ryegrass - Any grass of the genus Lolium, used for pasture and lawns
  • Synonym: darnel - A type of poisonous weed grass of the genus Lolium

Etymology

From Latin *lolium* (darnel, ryegrass).
