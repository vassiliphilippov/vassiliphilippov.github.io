lindy

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪndi / (AmE), / ˈlɪndi / (BrE)
an energetic American dance, popular in the 1930s, featuring lively swings and acrobatic movements
1930s American swing dance

Examples

The band played jazz as couples performed the lindy with incredible energy.
He learned the lindy at a weekend swing dance workshop.
During the 1930s, the lindy was the dance craze sweeping America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swing dance - a group of dances developed with the swing style of jazz music

Etymology

Probably named after the aviator Charles Lindbergh, alluding to the popular phrase 'Lindy Hop' after his transatlantic flight.
