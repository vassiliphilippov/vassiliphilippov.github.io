Sarda

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑrdə / (AmE), / ˈsɑːdə / (BrE)
a genus of marine fish in the mackerel family, including the bonitos
genus of fish (bonitos)

Examples

The Sarda are fast-swimming fish often caught by commercial fisheries.
Several species within the genus Sarda are prized by sport fishermen.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Sarda in the Mediterranean Sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonito - a type of marine fish closely related to the tuna

Etymology

From Italian *sarda* (a type of fish), from Latin *sarda* (a kind of fish), possibly of Greek origin.
