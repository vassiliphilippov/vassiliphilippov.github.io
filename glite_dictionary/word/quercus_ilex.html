Quercus ilex

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜr.kəs ˈaɪ.lɛks / (AmE), / ˈkwəː.kəs ˈɪ.lɛks / (BrE)
An evergreen oak tree native to southern Europe, with leaves resembling holly; also known as holm oak or holly oak.
an evergreen oak tree of southern Europe

Examples

The ancient Quercus ilex stood at the edge of the Mediterranean grove, its dense foliage providing shade.
Carpenters prize the hard wood of Quercus ilex for furniture making and construction.
Many parks in Spain are planted with Quercus ilex to create evergreen landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holm oak - another common name for Quercus ilex
  • Degree Lower: oak - broad genus of deciduous or evergreen trees or shrubs in the genus Quercus

Etymology

From Latin: Quercus (oak) + ilex (holm oak or holly oak).
