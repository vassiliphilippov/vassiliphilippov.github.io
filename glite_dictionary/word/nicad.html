nicad

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪˌkæd / (AmE), / ˈnʌɪkad / (BrE)
a rechargeable battery with a nickel cathode and a cadmium anode, commonly used in portable electronic devices and emergency systems
type of rechargeable battery (nickel-cadmium)

Examples

Make sure you fully charge the nicad before using the device for the first time.
Over time, repeated charging can reduce a nicad battery's capacity.
He replaced the old nicad cells in his flashlight with newer ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nickel-cadmium battery - another term for nicad, fully spelled out
  • Degree Higher: lithium-ion battery - a newer type of rechargeable battery using lithium ions

Etymology

Blend of nickel and cadmium.
