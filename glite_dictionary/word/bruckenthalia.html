Bruckenthalia

1. NOUN

/ brʌk.ɛnˈθæli.ə / (AmE), / brʊk.enˈθæli.ə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the heath family, containing only the species known as spike heath.
genus of plants in the heath family

Examples

The Bruckenthalia genus is native to mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.
Gardeners grow Bruckenthalia for its attractive pink flower spikes and evergreen foliage.
Recent studies have reclassified Bruckenthalia as part of the Erica genus, though both names are still used.

Etymology

Named after Samuel von Brukenthal, an 18th-century Transylvanian governor and patron of science.

2. NOUN

/ brukˌɛnˈθeɪliə / (AmE), / bruːkˌɛnˈθeɪlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the heath family (Ericaceae), now usually included in the genus Erica, known for spike heath.
plant genus (spike heath)

Examples

The botanist collected samples of Bruckenthalia during her research on alpine flora.
Bruckenthalia, also known as spike heath, thrives in rocky, high-altitude regions.
Recent taxonomic studies consider Bruckenthalia to be part of the genus Erica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Erica - genus of heaths and heathers; commonly includes Bruckenthalia species

Etymology

Named after Samuel von Brukenthal, Transylvanian governor and naturalist.
