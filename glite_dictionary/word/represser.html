represser

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈprɛsər / (AmE), / rɪˈprɛsə / (BrE)
an agent, person, or mechanism that represses, restrains, or inhibits something, such as a gene, feeling, or behaviour
one that represses or restrains

Examples

The dictator acted as a represser of free speech and dissent.
In genetics, a represser controls the expression of certain genes.
Many consider harsh laws to be the work of a represser rather than a protector.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repressor - agent that represses or inhibits (alternate spelling; also a molecular biology term)
  • Antonym: activator - an agent that activates or stimulates

Etymology

From repress + -er, indicating 'one who represses'; related to French 'répresseur'.
