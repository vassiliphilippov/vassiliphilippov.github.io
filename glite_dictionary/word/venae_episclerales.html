venae episclerales

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.naɪ ˌɛp.i.skˈlɛr.əˌliz / (AmE), / ˈviː.niː ˌɛp.ɪ.skˈleə.rɑː.liːz / (BrE)
small veins located in the sclera near the corneal margin that drain into the anterior ciliary veins
small veins in the sclera near the cornea
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist detected inflammation in the venae episclerales during the eye examination.
Blood from the venae episclerales ultimately drains into the anterior ciliary veins.
An injury near the corneal margin can affect the venae episclerales, causing localized redness.

Etymology

From Latin: 'venae' (veins) and 'episclerales' (upon the sclera).
