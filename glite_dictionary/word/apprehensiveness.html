apprehensiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpɹɪˈhɛnsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˌapɹɪˈhɛnsɪvnəs / (BrE)
A feeling of fear or anxiety about something that may happen.
anxiety or fear about future events

Examples

Her apprehensiveness grew as the storm clouds gathered overhead.
He noticed a trace of apprehensiveness in her voice before the big performance.
Despite his apprehensiveness, he decided to go forward with the interview.

Synset Examples

the student looked around the examination room with apprehension

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease
  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being free from agitation or strong emotion

Etymology

From Latin apprehendere (to grasp) + -ive + -ness.
