formicary

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrmɪˌkɛri / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmɪˌkəri / (BrE)
a nest or mound constructed by ants, typically made of earth or other materials, serving as their dwelling place
ant nest or mound

Examples

A large formicary stood at the edge of the forest, bustling with ant activity.
Scientists observed the structure of the formicary to learn more about ant colonies.
The children watched in fascination as ants carried food into their formicary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthill - a mound of earth made by ants

Etymology

From Latin formīcārium, from formīca (ant).
