fundamentalistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfəndəˌmɛntəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌfʌndəˌmɛntəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by strict adherence to the basic principles of any subject or discipline, especially ideological or religious fundamentalism.
relating to fundamentalism

Examples

The group's fundamentalistic beliefs shaped their approach to social issues.
Her fundamentalistic interpretation of the texts left little room for discussion.
Critics argue that the policy is driven by a fundamentalistic ideology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fundamentalist - a person who strictly adheres to basic principles, especially in religion

Etymology

Formed from 'fundamentalist' + '-ic', after 'fundamentalism'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌfʌndəməntəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌfʌndəməntəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of fundamentalism or its adherents, especially within Protestant Christianity.
relating to (religious) fundamentalism

Examples

Many scholars criticize the fundamentalistic interpretation of religious texts for its rigidity.
The school is known for its fundamentalistic views on science and education.
Her fundamentalistic beliefs influence every aspect of her daily life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fundamentalist - a person who believes in the strict, literal interpretation of scripture

Etymology

From 'fundamentalist' + '-ic', modeled after 'fundamentalism'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02964572-a
Synset POS: a
