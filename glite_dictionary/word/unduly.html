unduly

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈduːli / (AmE), / ʌnˈdjuːli / (BrE)
to an excessive, unreasonable, or unnecessary degree; more than is appropriate or justified.
to an excessive or improper degree

Examples

The judge decided the punishment was unduly harsh for such a minor offense.
She was unduly worried about the possibility of failure.
His remarks were criticized as being unduly negative during the meeting.

Synset Examples

she was unduly pessimistic about her future

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessively - to a greater degree than is reasonable or necessary
  • Synonym: overly - to an extent that is more than desirable or necessary
  • Antonym: justifiably - with good reason or justification

Etymology

From 'undue' + '-ly'; 'undue' from Middle English, from Old French 'undue', from Latin 'indebitus' (not owing).
