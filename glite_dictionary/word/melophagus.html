Melophagus

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɑfədʒɪs / (AmE), / məˈlæfəɡɪs / (BrE)
A genus of wingless flies in the family Hippoboscidae, including the sheep ked, parasitic on sheep.
genus of wingless parasitic flies

Examples

The Melophagus genus is best known for the sheep ked, a common parasite of domestic sheep.
Veterinarians often check livestock for infestation by Melophagus species.
A Melophagus infestation can cause irritation and affect the quality of sheep wool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheep ked - a wingless parasitic fly of the species Melophagus ovinus

Etymology

From Greek 'melos' (limb) and 'phagein' (to eat) via modern scientific Latin.
