sanyasi

1. NOUN

/ sənˈjɑːsi / (AmE), / sənˈjɑːsi / (BrE)
a Hindu religious ascetic who has renounced worldly life and seeks spiritual liberation, often living as a mendicant
Hindu religious ascetic

Examples

After years as a successful businessman, he chose to become a sanyasi and dedicate his life to spiritual pursuits.
The sanyasi wandered from village to village, living on alms and teaching about detachment.
In many Hindu traditions, becoming a sanyasi is seen as the final stage of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sadhu - a holy person in Hinduism who has renounced worldly life

Etymology

From Sanskrit *saṃnyāsin* (one who renounces), from *saṃnyāsa* (renunciation).
