whizbang

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪzˌbæŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪzˌbæŋ / (BrE)
a small, high-velocity artillery shell that makes a whizzing sound followed by a bang upon impact
high-velocity artillery shell

Examples

During World War I, soldiers learned to recognize the sound of a whizbang before it hit.
The trenches offered little protection against the deadly whizbangs raining down.
Veterans would often describe the terrifying whizz and sudden bang of a whizbang overhead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - a projectile fired from a large gun

Etymology

Imitative of the sound ('whizz' + 'bang') made by the shell as it travels and explodes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪzˌbæŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪzbæŋ / (BrE)
A firecracker that produces a whizzing sound followed by a loud explosion.
type of firecracker with a whizzing sound

Examples

We watched a whizbang rocket into the sky, its fizz ending in a thunderous burst.
Everyone cheered as the whizbang exploded with a sparkling flash and loud noise.
The kids begged to light another whizbang during the Fourth of July celebration.

Etymology

From the onomatopoeic words 'whiz' (imitating a high-pitched hiss) and 'bang' (imitating an explosion).
