to misinform

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪs.ɪnˈfɔrm / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.ɪnˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to give someone false or inaccurate information, especially unintentionally
give false or inaccurate information

Examples

The journalist did not intend to misinform the public with the incorrect data.
False rumors can easily misinform large groups of people on social media.
She was misinformed about the meeting's start time and arrived late.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • Antonym: to inform - to give someone facts or information

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) and 'inform', from Latin informare (to inform).
