umbrageous

1. ADJ

/ ʌmˈbreɪdʒəs / (AmE), / ʌmˈbreɪdʒɪəs / (BrE)
(literary) Shaded, full of or providing shade; shadowy.
full of shade; shadowy

Examples

We found relief from the heat beneath the umbrageous branches of a large oak tree.
The poet described the umbrageous groves sheltering the old manor house.
Their cottage, hidden in the umbrageous woodlands, felt cool even during summer.

Synset Examples

the shady side of the street
the surface of the pond is dark and shadowed
we sat on rocks in a shadowy cove
cool umbrageous woodlands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shady - full of or cast by shade
  • Synonym: shadowy - full of shadows; dimly lit

Etymology

From Latin 'umbrageus' (shady), from 'umbra' (shade).

2. ADJ

/ ʌmˈbreɪdʒəs / (AmE), / ʌmˈbreɪdʒɪəs / (BrE)
feeling or showing anger, especially at something perceived as unjust or offensive; easily offended or resentful
easily offended or angered
archaic

Examples

She grew umbrageous when her honesty was called into question during the meeting.
The villagers became umbrageous at the unfair land reforms imposed upon them.
He has an umbrageous temperament, quick to take offense at the smallest slight.

Synset Examples

an indignant denial
incensed at the judges' unfairness
a look of outraged disbelief
umbrageous at the loss of their territory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indignant - feeling or showing anger at unfair treatment
  • Synonym: outraged - extremely angry or shocked, especially at injustice

Etymology

From Latin 'umbrageus' (shady), from 'umbra' (shade); figurative sense influenced by 'take umbrage'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00116305-s
Synset POS: s
