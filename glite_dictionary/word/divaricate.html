to divaricate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈværɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈværɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to spread or extend apart; to diverge widely
spread apart; diverge

Examples

The petals of the flower divaricate as it blooms each morning.
He slowly divaricated his fingers to show the injury to the doctor.
The ancient branches divaricated wildly from the main trunk, giving the tree its unusual shape.

Synset Examples

divaricate one's fingers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diverge - to move or extend in different directions from a common point
  • Antonym: to converge - to move toward one point and join together

Etymology

From Latin divaricatus, past participle of divaricare (to straddle, spread apart), from di- (apart) + varicare (to straddle).

2. VERB

/ daɪˈværɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈværɪkeɪt / (BrE)
(of a road, branch, or structure) to branch off or diverge in different directions from a common point.
to branch off or diverge

Examples

At the edge of the forest, the main trail divaricates into two smaller paths.
The river divaricates around the island, forming a natural fork.
As their opinions divaricated, the committee struggled to reach a consensus.

Synset Examples

The road divaricates here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diverge - to move or extend in different directions from a common point
  • Antonym: to converge - to come together from different directions

Etymology

From Latin divaricatus, past participle of divaricare (to straddle, to spread apart).
