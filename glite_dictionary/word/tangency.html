tangency

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæn.dʒən.si / (AmE), / ˈtan.dʒən.si / (BrE)
a point or junction where two things, especially electrical conductors, touch or are in physical contact
point of contact or junction

Examples

The tangency between the two wires must be clean to ensure a proper electrical connection.
Any dirt or corrosion at the tangency could lead to a loss of signal in the circuit.
Technicians checked each tangency in the system before powering up the device.

Synset Examples

they forget to solder the contacts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contact - the state of physical touching or meeting
  • Synonym: connection - a point where two or more things are joined

Etymology

From Latin 'tangens' (touching), present participle of 'tangere' (to touch).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæn.dʒən.si / (AmE), / ˈtan.dʒən.si / (BrE)
the state of being tangent; the condition in which a curve or surface touches another at a single point or along a line without crossing it
the state of being tangent

Examples

In geometry, tangency describes the precise point where a circle meets a straight line.
The engineer examined the tangency of the two surfaces to ensure they only touched at one point.
A chord and a tangent line have only one point of tangency on a circle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangent - a straight line or plane that touches a curve or curved surface at a point, but does not cross it

Etymology

From Latin 'tangens', present participle of 'tangere' (to touch), with suffix '-cy' denoting quality or state.
