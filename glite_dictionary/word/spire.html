spire

1. NOUN

/ spaɪɚ / (AmE), / spaɪə / (BrE)
a tall, slender, pointed structure on the top of a building, especially a church or temple
pointed structure on a building top

Examples

The church's spire could be seen from miles away, rising above the town.
A golden weathervane was perched on the very tip of the spire.
Restoration of the damaged spire took several years after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steeple - tall tower on a church, often with a spire
  • Antonym: dome - rounded roof or vault, contrasting with a spire

Etymology

From Old French *spire*, from Latin *spira* (coil, spiral), influenced by Old English *spīr* (sprout, shoot).
