hypercriticism

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərˈkrɪtɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈkrɪtɪsɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
Excessive or unduly harsh criticism.
excessive or overly harsh criticism

Examples

The constant hypercriticism of her performance eventually took a toll on her confidence.
Some parents' hypercriticism makes their children afraid to try new things.
He struggled with hypercriticism from online reviewers, who picked apart every detail of his work.

Synset Examples

Hypercriticism of the body appears more common in women's writings than in men's.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: criticism - the expression of disapproval based on perceived faults or mistakes
  • Synonym: hypercritical - excessively or meticulously critical, especially of small faults

Etymology

From hyper- (excessive) + criticism.
