Hyla

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪlə / (AmE), / ˈhɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of small, typically arboreal frogs known as tree frogs, belonging to the family Hylidae.
genus of tree frogs

Examples

The European tree frog once belonged to the genus Hyla before being reclassified.
Researchers discovered several new species within the Hyla genus during their expedition.
Many Hyla species are known for their distinctive mating calls and bright coloration.

Etymology

From Greek hylē (wood, forest), reflecting the typical habitat of these frogs.
