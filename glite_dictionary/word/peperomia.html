peperomia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛpəˈroʊmiə / (AmE), / ˌpɛpəˈrəʊmiə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Peperomia, grown primarily for its attractive, often succulent foliage, commonly used as a houseplant.
a genus of ornamental foliage plants

Examples

She placed a peperomia by the kitchen window where it would get filtered sunlight.
Many gardeners favor peperomias because they require little maintenance and have striking leaves.
I bought a new peperomia for my office desk as an easy-care plant.

Etymology

From New Latin Peperomia, from Greek peperi (pepper) + homoios (similar), referring to its resemblance to pepper plants.
