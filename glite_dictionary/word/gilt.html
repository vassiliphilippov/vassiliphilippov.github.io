gilt

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪlt / (AmE), / ɡɪlt / (BrE)
A thin layer of gold or a substance with a similar appearance applied as a coating.
thin gold or gold-like coating

Examples

The ornate picture frame was covered in a layer of gilt that gleamed in the sunlight.
Centuries of wear had caused the gilt on the church altar to fade.
The antique box featured intricate designs highlighted with bright gilt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gilding - the process or result of applying gold leaf or gold paint
  • Synonym: veneer - a thin decorative covering of fine material

Etymology

From Old Norse gylla or Middle English gilten, related to 'gild'.

2. ADJ

/ ɡɪlt / (AmE), / ɡɪlt / (BrE)
having a deep, slightly brownish gold color; resembling gold in color.
having a gold-like color

Examples

The ornate picture frame was finished in a gilt color that shimmered in the sunlight.
She admired the gilt leaves decorating the ancient manuscript.
The palace ceiling was covered with gilt designs that glowed above the visitors.

Synset Examples

long aureate (or golden) hair
a gold carpet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden - having the color or shine of gold

Etymology

From Old Norse *gylta* or Middle English *gilt*, related to 'to gild' (cover with gold).
