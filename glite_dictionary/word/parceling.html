parceling

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrsəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsəlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of dividing or distributing something into parts or shares, typically according to an arrangement or plan
distribution by dividing into parts or shares

Examples

The parceling of inheritance among the children was stated clearly in the will.
The company oversaw the parceling of land to various developers.
Careful parceling of resources ensured that each department received what it needed.

Synset Examples

the apportionment of seats in the House of Representatives is based on the relative population of each state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apportionment - the action or result of dividing and assigning according to a plan
  • Synonym: allocation - the process of distributing something

Etymology

From 'parcel' (to divide into parts) + '-ing'. 'Parcel' from Old French *parcelle* (a small part), from Latin *particella*, diminutive of *pars* (part).
