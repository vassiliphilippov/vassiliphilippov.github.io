erythrocytolysin

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθ.roʊ.saɪˈtɑl.ɪ.sɪn / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθ.rəʊ.saɪˈtɒl.ɪ.sɪn / (BrE)
a substance that causes the destruction of red blood cells (erythrocytes) and releases their hemoglobin
substance that destroys red blood cells
medical

Examples

The presence of erythrocytolysin in the sample indicated that the red blood cells had been destroyed.
Some bacterial toxins act as erythrocytolysins, leading to hemolytic anemia in patients.
The laboratory test detected erythrocytolysin activity in the infected tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemolysin - a substance causing destruction of red blood cells

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' (red), 'kytos' (cell), and 'lysis' (loosening, dissolution).
