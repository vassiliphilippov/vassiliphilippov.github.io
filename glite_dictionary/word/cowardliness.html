cowardliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊərdlinəs / (AmE), / ˈkaʊədlinəs / (BrE)
the quality of lacking courage or bravery; showing fear or timidity in the face of danger, difficulty, or pain
lack of courage; timidity

Examples

His cowardliness became apparent when he refused to stand up for his friends.
The general criticized the troops for their cowardliness in the face of the enemy.
Throughout the story, the hero overcomes his initial cowardliness to become a true leader.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowardice - lack of courage or bravery
  • Antonym: courage - the ability to do something that is dangerous or difficult
  • Antonym: bravery - the quality of being brave; courageous behavior

Etymology

From 'coward' (Middle English, from Old French 'couard') + suffix '-liness' (forming nouns of quality).
