Prociphilus

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈsɪfɪləs / (AmE), / prəʊˈsɪfɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of aphids in the family Aphididae, some of whose species are known as woolly aphids.
a genus of aphids (insect)

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species of Prociphilus in the maple grove.
Some Prociphilus aphids secrete a waxy substance for protection.
The infestation was identified as belonging to the genus Prociphilus.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek pro- (before) and kyphos (bent, humped), with Latinized suffix -ilus.

2. NOUN

/ proʊˈsɪfɪlʌs / (AmE), / prəʊˈsɪfɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of aphids in the family Aphididae, including several species that feed on plants such as alder and roots of other plants.
genus of aphids

Examples

The alder woolly aphid belongs to the genus Prociphilus and is often found on alder trees.
Entomologists studied several species within the genus Prociphilus to understand their life cycles.
Infestations of Prociphilus can sometimes affect the health of certain plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - a small sap-sucking insect

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining 'pro-' and 'Ciphilus' (related genus name), used in scientific taxonomy.
