cloning

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkləʊnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making an identical copy of a gene, cell, or organism by scientific methods
making identical biological copies

Examples

Recent advances in cloning have raised ethical questions about its use in humans.
The cloning of plants is a common technique in agriculture.
Scientists succeeded in cloning a sheep, which they named Dolly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reproduction - the process by which new organisms are produced
  • Synonym: copying - the action of making a duplicate of something

Etymology

From 'clone' + -ing; 'clone' from Greek klōn (twig, branch).
