anyways

1. ADV

/ ˈɛniˌweɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛniˌweɪz / (BrE)
(informal) Used to emphasize a statement or to change the subject, often to support or explain a previous statement or to resume a topic.
used to support or resume a statement (informal)
informal

Examples

Anyways, let me know if you change your mind about joining us tonight.
I didn't like that movie, but anyways, what do you want to do now?
They said the weather would be bad, but we decided to go hiking anyways.

Synset Examples

Anyhow, he is dead now
I think they're asleep; anyhow, they're quiet
I don't know what happened to it; anyway, it's gone
anyway, there is another factor to consider
I don't know how it started; in any case, there was a brief scuffle
in any event, the government faced a serious protest
but at any rate he got a knighthood for it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anyway - used to confirm or support a previous statement; more standard form
  • Synonym: anyhow - used to indicate a statement explains or supports a previous statement

Etymology

Informal variant of 'anyway.' The '-s' ending imitates adverbs like 'always.'
