glossina

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɑːˈsiːnə / (AmE), / ɡlɒˈsiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of African bloodsucking flies, commonly known as tsetse flies, which transmit sleeping sickness and other diseases.
African bloodsucking fly genus (tsetse)

Examples

The glossina is notorious for spreading sleeping sickness across sub-Saharan Africa.
Researchers study the glossina to better understand how to prevent the spread of disease.
Efforts to control glossina populations have been central to public health campaigns in affected regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tsetse fly - common name for the insect of the genus Glossina

Etymology

From New Latin 'Glossina', the genus name, based on Greek 'glōssa' (tongue).
