Glossina

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɑˈsaɪnə / (AmE), / ɡlɒˈsiːnə / (BrE)
a genus of bloodsucking flies, commonly known as tsetse flies, found in Africa and noted for transmitting trypanosomiasis (sleeping sickness)
genus of African tsetse flies

Examples

The Glossina genus includes several species of tsetse flies responsible for spreading sleeping sickness in Africa.
Researchers are studying the life cycle of Glossina to control its population and reduce disease transmission.
Effective traps have been developed to capture Glossina in rural communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tsetse fly - African bloodsucking fly that transmits sleeping sickness

Etymology

From Greek 'glossa' (tongue), in reference to the insect's proboscis.
