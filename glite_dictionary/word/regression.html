regression

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
An abnormal condition in which development or progress has stopped or reversed, especially in a psychological or medical context.
abnormal halt or reversal of development
medical

Examples

The therapist noted a sudden regression in the patient's ability to communicate.
After the trauma, the child showed signs of regression to earlier developmental stages.
Medical professionals are concerned when regression occurs during rehabilitation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: progression - normal or forward movement in development or growth

Etymology

From Latin regressio (a return), from regredi (to go back).

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
The act of returning to a previous or less developed state.
returning to a former state

Examples

After years of improvement, the patient's sudden regression surprised the medical team.
The country's political unrest led to a regression in economic development.
Therapists often help clients work through periods of emotional regression.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: progression - the process of developing or moving gradually towards a more advanced state

Etymology

From Latin 'regressio' (a return), from 'regredi' (to go back).

4. NOUN

/ rɪˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
(Psychology) A defense mechanism in which a person returns to an earlier or less mature stage of emotional development, often as a way of coping with stress or anxiety.
return to earlier emotional state (psychology)
medical

Examples

Under stress, some adults may exhibit regression, reverting to childhood behaviors for comfort.
The therapist noted signs of regression in the patient after the traumatic event.
Crying for his mother was a clear example of regression in the young boy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repression - psychological defense mechanism involving exclusion from consciousness
  • Synonym: denial - refusal to accept reality as a defense mechanism

Etymology

From Latin regressio (a returning), from regredi (to go back).
