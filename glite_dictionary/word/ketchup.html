ketchup

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛtʃʌp / (AmE), / ˈkɛtʃəp / (BrE)
A thick, sweet and spicy sauce made primarily from tomatoes, vinegar, sugar, and spices, commonly used as a condiment.
tomato-based condiment

Examples

She put some ketchup on her fries before eating them.
The children argued over which brand of ketchup tasted best.
He prefers hot sauce to ketchup on his burger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catsup - another term for ketchup
  • Antonym: mayonnaise - a creamy condiment made from eggs and oil
  • Antonym: mustard - a condiment made from the seeds of a mustard plant

Etymology

From Malay 'kecap' or 'kicap' (fish sauce), adapted in English to refer to a tomato-based sauce.
