bibliography

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɪbliˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌbɪbliˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a list of books, articles, or other writings on a particular subject or by a particular author, especially one compiled as part of a scholarly work, often including information such as the time and place of publication.
list of writings on a subject or by an author

Examples

The professor asked us to include a bibliography at the end of our research paper.
She spent hours compiling the bibliography for her thesis, making sure every source was accurate.
The book’s bibliography lists hundreds of articles and books for further reading.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reference list - a list of sources cited directly in a document
  • Degree Lower: footnote - a note at the bottom of a page citing a reference or comment

Etymology

From Greek *bibliographia* (book writing), from *biblion* (book) + *-graphia* (writing).
