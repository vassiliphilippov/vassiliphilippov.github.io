valiancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæl.i.ən.si / (AmE), / ˈvæl.i.ən.si / (BrE)
exceptional or heroic courage, especially in the face of danger
exceptional courage or heroism

Examples

The soldier's valiancy in defending his comrades inspired everyone on the battlefield.
Stories of ancient valiancy are passed down through generations to teach courage and honor.
Her valiancy under extreme pressure earned her the respect of the entire community.

Synset Examples

he showed great heroism in battle
he received a medal for valor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valor - great courage in the face of danger, especially in battle
  • Synonym: bravery - courageous behavior or character
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of bravery

Etymology

From Old French 'valiancier', related to 'valiant' (from Latin 'valere', to be strong).
