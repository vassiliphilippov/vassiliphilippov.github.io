alike

1. ADJ

/ əˈlaɪk / (AmE), / əˈlaɪk / (BrE)
having the same or very similar characteristics or qualities
similar in characteristics

Examples

The twins are so alike that even their teachers confuse them.
Their opinions were surprisingly alike on most important issues.
The houses in this neighborhood all look alike to me.

Synset Examples

all politicians are alike
they looked utterly alike
friends are generally alike in background and taste

Related Terms

  • Synonym: similar - having a resemblance in appearance or nature, not identical
  • Antonym: different - not the same as another; unlike in nature, form, or quality

Etymology

From Old English 'gelīc' (similar, like).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01413463-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADV

/ əˈlaɪk / (AmE), / əˈlaɪk / (BrE)
In the same way or equally; used to refer to two or more people or things together.
in the same way; equally

Examples

Both critics and audiences alike praised the film for its originality.
The plans will benefit young and old alike.
The policy affects men and women alike across the country.

Synset Examples

parents and teachers alike demanded reforms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equally - to the same degree

Etymology

From Old English 'gelīc' (alike, similar), with influence from like (similar).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00070072-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ əˈlaɪk / (AmE), / əˈlaɪk / (BrE)
In a like manner; in the same or a similar way.
in a similar way (adverb)

Examples

The twins dress alike for family photos.
Many cultures around the world celebrate the new year alike.
The two proposals were received alike by the committee.

Synset Examples

they walk alike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: similarly - in a similar manner
Source: WordNet
