Hydrochoeridae

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdroʊkoʊˈɛrɪdiː / (AmE), / haɪˌdrəʊkəʊˈɛərɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large South American rodents, including the capybara, regarded as the largest living rodents.
rodent family including the capybara

Examples

The Hydrochoeridae are known for their aquatic habits and large size.
Scientists classify the capybara as a member of the Hydrochoeridae family.
Unlike most rodents, Hydrochoeridae species are semi-aquatic and can be found near water bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capybara - the largest living rodent, native to South America

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek hydro- (water) + khoiros (pig) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊˈkiːrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊˈkɪərɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large South American rodents that includes the capybara and its relatives.
family of rodents including the capybara

Examples

The Hydrochoeridae family is best known for its largest member, the capybara.
Zoologists are studying the behavior of various species within Hydrochoeridae.
Fossil records indicate that ancestors of Hydrochoeridae once inhabited a much wider area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capybara - a large, semiaquatic rodent native to South America

Etymology

From the genus Hydrochoerus (capybara) + Latin -idae, indicating a zoological family.
