Lactuca

1. NOUN

/ lækˈtuːkə / (AmE), / lækˈtjuːkə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the daisy family, including common lettuce and related species with milky sap.
genus including lettuce plants

Examples

The species Lactuca sativa is widely cultivated as garden lettuce.
Botanists classify wild lettuce under the genus Lactuca.
Prickly lettuce, or Lactuca serriola, grows along roadsides in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lettuce - a leafy green vegetable, especially Lactuca sativa

Etymology

From Latin lactuca (lettuce), related to 'lac' (milk), referring to its milky sap.

2. NOUN

/ lækˈtuːkə / (AmE), / lækˈtjuːkə / (BrE)
a genus of herbaceous plants in the daisy family (Asteraceae), including lettuces and related species characterized by milky sap
genus of plants including lettuces
medical

Examples

The Lactuca genus includes both cultivated lettuce and several wild species.
Prickly Lactuca plants can often be found growing in abandoned fields.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Lactuca extracts in traditional remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lettuce - a leafy green vegetable of the genus Lactuca

Etymology

From Latin 'lactuca' meaning 'lettuce,' from 'lac' (milk), referring to the milky sap.
