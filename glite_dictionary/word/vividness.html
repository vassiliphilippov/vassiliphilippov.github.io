vividness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪvɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪvɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being strikingly clear, intense, or full of life and detail, especially in appearance, description, or imagination
quality of being clear and intense

Examples

The vividness of her memories made the past feel almost real again.
The artist used bold colors to enhance the vividness of his paintings.
Readers admired the vividness of the author's descriptions, which brought each scene to life.

Synset Examples

the Puritan Period was lacking in color
the characters were delineated with exceptional vividness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intensity - the quality of being intense
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of vividness or clarity

Etymology

From Latin 'vividus' (lively, full of life) + English suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪvɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪvɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having strong, intense color without dilution with white; chromatic purity
intensity or purity of color

Examples

The vividness of the sunset colors took her breath away.
Painters often seek vividness in their use of pigments to create a striking effect.
Graphic designers use vividness to make elements stand out on a page.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brilliance - brightness or intensity of color
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of brightness or intensity in color

Etymology

From Latin 'vividus' (lively, full of life) + English suffix '-ness'.
