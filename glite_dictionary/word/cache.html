cache

1. NOUN

/ kæʃ / (AmE), / kæʃ / (BrE)
a hidden store of things, especially valuables or money, kept in a secret place for future use
a hidden store of valuables or money

Examples

They discovered a cache of ancient coins buried beneath the old house.
The police found a cache of weapons hidden in the forest.
She kept a small cache of emergency money in the back of her closet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoard - a secret supply of something for future use

Etymology

From French *cache* (a hiding place), from *cacher* (to hide), from Latin *coactare* (to store up, collect).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13388085-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ kæʃ / (AmE), / kæʃ / (BrE)
(computing) a special area of memory used to store frequently accessed data temporarily, making data retrieval faster for future requests.
(computing) memory for quick data access

Examples

The processor uses a cache to speed up access to frequently used instructions.
Clearing your browser's cache can resolve many loading issues on websites.
A larger CPU cache can significantly improve system performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffer - a temporary storage area used to hold data while it is being transferred

Etymology

From French 'cacher' (to hide). The computing sense is metaphorical, referring to hidden storage.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02938401-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kæʃ / (AmE), / kæʃ / (BrE)
a hidden place where money, food, weapons, or other valuables are stored for future use
hidden storage place for valuables

Examples

They discovered a cache of gold coins buried in the garden.
The police found a cache of weapons in the abandoned building.
During the hike, we stumbled upon a cache of emergency supplies hidden under the rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoard - a secret or carefully guarded stash of valuables

Etymology

From French *cache* (a hiding place), from cacher (to hide).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02938272-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ kæʃ / (AmE), / kæʃ / (BrE)
to store something in a hidden or safe place for future use
store for future use

Examples

The browser automatically caches images so that future visits load faster.
Squirrels cache nuts in the ground to survive through winter.
The archaeologists believe someone cached these artifacts here centuries ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: store - to keep something for future use

Etymology

From French 'cache' (a hiding place), from cacher (to hide).
