minister

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪstə / (BrE)
the head of a government department, often in charge of a specific area such as health or education
head of a government department

Examples

The Prime Minister announced new reforms during the press conference.
She was appointed as the minister of education last year.
Each minister is responsible for developing policies within their department.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretary - head of a government department (especially in the US)
  • Degree Lower: deputy - person acting as a second in command or substitute

Etymology

From Latin 'minister' (servant, attendant) via Old French and Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00586847-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪstər / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪstə / (BrE)
a diplomat who represents one government to another and ranks below an ambassador
diplomatic representative below ambassador

Examples

He was appointed as the new minister to the country after years of diplomatic service.
Unlike an ambassador, a minister holds a slightly lower diplomatic rank.
In the 19th century, many nations sent a minister instead of an ambassador as their chief envoy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ambassador - the highest-ranking diplomatic representative sent by one country to another

Etymology

From Latin minister (servant, attendant).

3. VERB

/ ˈmɪnɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪstə / (BrE)
to attend to the needs or wants of someone, especially in a caring or religious capacity
to care for or attend to someone's needs

Examples

The nurse would minister to the elderly patients with great compassion.
He went to minister to the injured after the accident.
She felt called to minister to the spiritual needs of her community.

Synset Examples

I have to minister to my mother all the time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to care for - look after or provide for the needs of someone
  • Antonym: neglect - fail to care for properly

Etymology

From Latin minister (servant, attendant).

4. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪstə / (BrE)
a person who holds a senior position in a government and is responsible for a specific department or area of policy, such as the Minister of Finance or Minister of Education
senior government official (e.g. Minister of Finance)

Examples

The new minister for health outlined her plans in parliament today.
Each minister is responsible for overseeing their department's budget and policies.
After the election, he was appointed as minister of foreign affairs.

Synset Examples

Minister of Finance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretary - senior government official in some countries
  • Degree Higher: prime minister - the head of government in parliamentary systems

Etymology

From Latin 'minister' (servant, attendant), via Old French and Middle English.

Examples

After the service, the minister greeted members of the congregation at the door.
She trained for many years before becoming a minister in the local church.

Related Terms
