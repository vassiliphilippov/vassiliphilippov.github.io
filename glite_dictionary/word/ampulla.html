ampulla

1. NOUN

/ æmˈpʌlə / (AmE), / æmˈpʌlə / (BrE)
A dilated, flask-like portion of a canal or duct in the body, especially in the semicircular canals of the ear.
dilated part of a bodily canal or duct
medical

Examples

The ampulla of the semicircular canal plays a crucial role in our sense of balance.
During the procedure, the surgeon carefully examined the ampulla for any signs of blockage.
In anatomy class, we studied the structure of the ampulla within the ear.

Etymology

From Latin 'ampulla' (small flask or bottle).

2. NOUN

/ æmˈpʌl.ə / (AmE), / æmˈpʊl.ə / (BrE)
A small flask or bottle with two handles, used in Ancient Rome for storing wine or oils.
Roman two-handled flask

Examples

The museum displays an ancient ampulla that was once used to store olive oil.
Archaeologists unearthed a Roman ampulla near the site of an old villa.
The intricate designs on the silver ampulla suggest it belonged to someone of high status.

Etymology

From Latin ampulla, diminutive of amphora (jug, bottle).
