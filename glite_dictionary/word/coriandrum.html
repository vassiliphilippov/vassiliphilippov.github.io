Coriandrum

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˈriːændrəm / (AmE), / kɒrɪˈandrəm / (BrE)
A small genus of annual Mediterranean herbs in the family Apiaceae, including coriander (Coriandrum sativum).
genus of Mediterranean herbs

Examples

Coriandrum is best known for its only widely cultivated species, coriander or cilantro.
The genus Coriandrum belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many other culinary herbs.
Researchers examined several wild varieties within the Coriandrum genus for medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coriander - an aromatic Mediterranean plant, also known as cilantro

Etymology

From Latin 'coriandrum', referring to coriander.
