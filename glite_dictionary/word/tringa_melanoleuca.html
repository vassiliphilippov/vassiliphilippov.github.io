Tringa melanoleuca

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪŋgə ˌmɛlənoʊˈljuːkə / (AmE), / ˈtrɪŋgə ˌmɛlənəˈliːʊkə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the greater yellowlegs, a large North American shorebird with long yellow legs and a slightly upturned bill
species of North American shorebird

Examples

Birds such as the Tringa melanoleuca inhabit wetlands across North America during migration season.
The Tringa melanoleuca can be identified by its long yellow legs and loud, clear calls.
Researchers tracked the movements of Tringa melanoleuca to study the effects of habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greater yellowlegs - common name for Tringa melanoleuca, a large shorebird
  • Degree Lower: Tringa flavipes - the lesser yellowlegs, a related but smaller North American shorebird

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, where 'Tringa' is a genus established for certain sandpipers and 'melanoleuca' means 'black and white' in Greek.
