upgrade

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈʌpɡreɪd / (BrE)
a reservation, seat, or service that has been improved to a higher status or quality, especially on transport or in hotels
an improved reservation or service

Examples

She was surprised to find her hotel upgrade included a suite with an ocean view.
After arriving at the airport, I was offered an upgrade to business class.
Thanks to her frequent flyer status, he often receives a complimentary upgrade.

Synset Examples

I got an upgrade to first class when coach class was full

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downgrade - a reservation or status that has been lowered in quality or importance
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05803662-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈʌpɡreɪd / (BrE)
a new version of a product, especially software, that offers improved features or performance compared to an earlier version
an improved version of a product or software

Examples

The latest upgrade includes improved security features and a faster interface.
After installing the upgrade, my computer ran much more smoothly.
Users were notified that a software upgrade would be available next week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: update - a newer version of software or data with recent changes or improvements
  • Antonym: downgrade - a change to a less advanced or lower-quality version

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'up-' (indicating improvement) and 'grade' (level or rank).

Source: WordNet
