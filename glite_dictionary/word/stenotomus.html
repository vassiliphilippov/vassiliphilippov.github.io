Stenotomus

1. NOUN

/ stɛˈnɑtəˌməs / (AmE), / stɛˈnɒtəməs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish, commonly known as scups, found in the western Atlantic Ocean.
genus of fish (scups)

Examples

The Atlantic coast is home to many species in the Stenotomus genus.
Fishermen often catch Stenotomus during the spring migration.
Scientists are studying the population of Stenotomus in the bay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scup - an edible fish of the western Atlantic, also called porgy

Etymology

From Greek 'stenos' (narrow) + 'tomos' (cut, section), referring to aspects of the fish's anatomy.
