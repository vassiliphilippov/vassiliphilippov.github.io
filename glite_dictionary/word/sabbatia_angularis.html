Sabbatia angularis

1. NOUN

/ səˈbeɪ.ʃə æŋˈɡjʊ.lər.ɪs / (AmE), / səˈbat.i.ə æŋˈɡjuː.lər.ɪs / (BrE)
A pink-flowered marsh plant of the eastern United States, also known as rose pink, resembling true centaury.
pink-flowered marsh plant of eastern US

Examples

The wetland restoration project included planting Sabbatia angularis to support local pollinators.
We identified several specimens of Sabbatia angularis growing near the riverbank in early summer.
Gardeners appreciate Sabbatia angularis for its delicate pink blooms and tolerance of moist soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centaury - a genus of flowering plants known for small pink or red flowers

Etymology

Genus name Sabbatia honors Liberato Sabbati, an Italian botanist; angularis refers to the plant's angular stems.
