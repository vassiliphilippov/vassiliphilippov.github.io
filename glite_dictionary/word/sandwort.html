sandwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈsændˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A low-growing plant with moss-like foliage and small white star-shaped flowers, commonly found in mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.
mountain plant with tiny white flowers

Examples

The alpine sandwort thrives in rocky, well-drained soils high in the mountains.
Clusters of sandwort can be seen blooming along the trails in early summer.
Gardeners often choose sandwort for rock gardens due to its delicate flowers and ground-covering growth.

Etymology

From sand + wort, where 'wort' comes from Old English 'wyrt' meaning 'plant.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈsændˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A low-growing herbaceous plant of the genus Arenaria, with clusters of small white four-petaled flowers, found in sandy or rocky soils.
low-growing herb with small white flowers

Examples

We found sandwort growing between the rocks along the coastal path.
The delicate white flowers of sandwort brightened the sandy meadow.
Gardeners often choose sandwort for ground cover in poor, rocky soils.

Etymology

From 'sand' referring to its preferred soil type, and 'wort', an old English word for plant or herb.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈsændˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A low-growing perennial plant, usually with small white flowers, often found in sandy areas and suitable for rock gardens.
low-growing perennial plant with small white flowers

Examples

The gardener selected sandwort to fill the spaces between the stones in her rockery.
In spring, sandwort produces clusters of delicate white flowers that brighten up sandy soils.
Many landscapers recommend sandwort as a hardy ground cover for dry, sunny spots.

Etymology

From 'sand' (referring to the plant's habitat) + 'wort' (old English for plant or herb).
