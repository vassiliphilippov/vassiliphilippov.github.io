surreal

1. ADJ

/ səˈriəl / (AmE), / səˈrɪəl / (BrE)
having the qualities of surrealism; marked by fantastic, dreamlike, or incongruous imagery or effects.
having qualities of surrealism; dreamlike or bizarre

Examples

The entire experience felt so surreal that I wasn't sure if I was dreaming.
The artist's paintings are known for their surreal landscapes and fantastical creatures.
Walking through the abandoned amusement park at night was a truly surreal experience.

Synset Examples

a great concourse of phantasmagoric shadows
the incongruous imagery in surreal art and literature

Related Terms

  • Antonym: realistic - representing things in a way that is true to life; the opposite of surreal

Etymology

From French surréaliste, from sur- (above, beyond) and réaliste (realist).

2. ADJ

/ səˈriəl / (AmE), / səˈrɪəl / (BrE)
Having the qualities of a dream; strange, unreal, or fantastical, often in a way that is disturbing or confusing.
strange or dreamlike; unreal

Examples

The artist's paintings often have a surreal quality that draws viewers into a strange world.
After the accident, everything felt surreal, as if I were walking through a dream.
The streets were empty and silent, creating a surreal atmosphere in the city.

Synset Examples

night invested the lake with a dreamlike quality
as irrational and surreal as a dream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dreamlike - having qualities similar to a dream; unreal or fantastic
  • Antonym: realistic - representing things as they are; lifelike

Etymology

Coined in the early 20th century from French 'surréalisme', influenced by 'surréaliste' (surrealist) and 'réalisme' (realism).
