epiphyseal

1. ADJ

/ ɪˌpɪfɪˈsiːəl / (AmE), / ɪˌpɪfɪˈsiːəl / (BrE)
relating to the epiphysis (the end part of a long bone) in the body, especially as it connects to growth or development.
relating to the epiphysis of a bone
medical

Examples

The doctor observed an epiphyseal fracture near the end of the patient’s femur.
Growth disorders in children are sometimes related to epiphyseal plate problems.
Radiographs showed that the epiphyseal line had not yet closed in the adolescent’s bones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epiphyseal plate - the plate of cartilage in developing long bones where growth occurs
  • Antonym: diaphyseal - relating to the shaft of a long bone

Etymology

From epiphysis (from Greek 'epi' meaning upon + 'physis' meaning growth/nature) with suffix '-al'.
