OAS

1. NOUN

/ oʊ eɪ ɛs / (AmE), / əʊ eɪ ɛs / (BrE)
an international organization of most countries in the Americas, founded in 1948 to promote economic, military, social, and cultural cooperation
Organization of American States (international body)

Examples

The OAS held a summit to discuss trade policies among member nations.
Diplomats from the OAS worked together to resolve a regional conflict.
Membership in the OAS has fostered greater cooperation between the countries of the Western Hemisphere.

Etymology

Acronym from Organization of American States.
