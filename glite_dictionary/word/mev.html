MeV

1. NOUN

/ ɛm iː ˈviː / (AmE), / ɛm iː ˈviː / (BrE)
a unit of energy equal to one million electron volts, commonly used in nuclear and particle physics.
unit of energy (million electron volts)

Examples

The particle was detected with an energy of 3 MeV in the experiment.
Many radioactive decays release gamma rays of several MeV.
Scientists measured the beam intensity at 5 MeV to ensure accurate results.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: keV - unit of energy equal to one thousand electron volts
  • Degree Higher: GeV - unit of energy equal to one billion electron volts

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'mega electron volt', from 'mega-' (million), 'electron', and 'volt'.
