mistake

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈsteɪk / (AmE), / mɪˈsteɪk / (BrE)
A wrong idea, belief, or understanding; a misconception or misunderstanding.
a wrong belief or understanding

Examples

He realized he had made a mistake about her intentions.
It was a common mistake to think that the museum was closed on Mondays.
Many conflicts arise from a simple mistake in communication.

Synset Examples

he wasn't going to admit his mistake
make no mistake about his intentions
there must be some misunderstanding — I don't have a sister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misunderstanding - a failure to understand correctly

Etymology

From Old Norse *mistaka* (to take by mistake), from *mis* (wrongly) + *taka* (to take).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05904330-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ mɪˈsteɪk / (AmE), / mɪˈsteɪk / (BrE)
an action or decision that is incorrect or misguided, caused by bad judgment, lack of knowledge, or carelessness
an incorrect action or decision

Examples

She realized her mistake as soon as she sent the email to the wrong person.
It was an honest mistake, so no one blamed him.
Making a small mistake at work taught him to be more careful in the future.

Synset Examples

he made a bad mistake
she was quick to point out my errors
I could understand his English in spite of his grammatical faults

Related Terms

  • Synonym: error - a mistake, especially in writing or calculation
  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or serious mistake
  • Antonym: correct - free from error or mistake

Etymology

From Old Norse *mistaka* (to take in error, misunderstand).

3. VERB

/ mɪˈsteɪk / (AmE), / mɪsˈteɪk / (BrE)
to be incorrect or make an error in judgment, action, or perception
to make an error or be wrong

Examples

Everyone can mistake sometimes, so don't be too hard on yourself.
If you mistake the directions, you might end up lost.
She realized she had mistaken his intentions after talking to him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to err - to make a mistake; to be wrong
  • Antonym: correct - to remove errors; to make right

Etymology

From Old Norse 'mistaka', meaning 'to take in error'.

4. VERB

/ mɪˈsteɪk / (AmE), / mɪsˈteɪk / (BrE)
to wrongly identify someone or something as another; to confuse with another person or thing
to wrongly identify; confuse with another

Examples

It is easy to mistake the twins because they look so alike.
She was mistaken for a celebrity at the airport.
Don't mistake kindness for weakness; he is actually very strong-willed.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confuse - to mistake one thing for another or mix up two things
  • Antonym: to recognize - to correctly identify someone or something

Etymology

From Old Norse 'mistaka' (to take in error), related to 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'take'.
