politburo

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːlɪtbjʊˌroʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪtbjʊərəʊ / (BrE)
The principal policymaking and executive committee of a Communist Party.
executive committee of a Communist Party

Examples

The politburo met to discuss the country's economic strategy for the upcoming year.
During the Soviet era, only members of the politburo held true political power.
She was the first woman appointed to the Chinese Communist Party's politburo.

Etymology

From Russian politbyuro, short for politicheskoye byuro (political bureau).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊlɪtˌbjʊəroʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪtˌbjʊərəʊ / (BrE)
the principal policy-making and executive committee of a communist party
executive committee of a communist party

Examples

After the resignation of the general secretary, the politburo convened to elect a new leader.
She studied the history of the Soviet politburo for her university thesis.
The politburo issued a statement addressing the recent political unrest.

Synset Examples

The politburo made a crucial decision on economic reforms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: central committee - the broader governing body of a communist party, often above the politburo

Etymology

From Russian politbyuro, shortening of 'politicheskoye byuro' meaning 'political bureau'.
