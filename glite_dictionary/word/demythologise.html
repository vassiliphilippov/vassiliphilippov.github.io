to demythologise

1. VERB

/ diˌmɪθəˌlɑdʒaɪz / (AmE), / diˌmɪθəˈlɒdʒaɪz / (BrE)
to interpret writings, especially religious texts, by removing or explaining away mythical or supernatural elements to reveal their historical or factual basis
interpret without mythical elements

Examples

Many scholars attempt to demythologise religious scriptures to better understand their historical context.
The professor encouraged students to demythologise ancient legends and seek the truths they conceal.
Some critics argue that efforts to demythologise classic texts strip them of their intended power.

Synset Examples

the Bible should be demythologized and examined for its historical value

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to mythologise - give a mythical or legendary character to a text or story

Etymology

From Greek mythos (myth) + logos (word, discourse); modelled after 'mythologise'.
