to reminisce

1. VERB

/ ˌrɛm.əˈnɪs / (AmE), / ˌrɛm.ɪˈnɪs / (BrE)
to think or talk about enjoyable experiences you had in the past
recall or talk about the past

Examples

They love to reminisce about their college days whenever they get together.
During holidays, my parents often reminisce about family traditions from their childhood.
Last night, we sat by the fire and reminisced about old friends and adventures.

Synset Examples

The grandparents sat there, reminiscing all afternoon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recall - to remember; to bring a memory to mind
  • Antonym: to forget - to be unable to remember

Etymology

From Latin 'reminisci' (to remember), via French 'reminiscer' and Latin 'reminiscentia'.
