humus

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːməs / (AmE), / ˈhjuːməs / (BrE)
The organic component of soil, formed by the decomposition of leaves and other plant material by soil microorganisms.
organic material in soil

Examples

Adding humus to your garden helps retain moisture and improve soil fertility.
Forest soil rich in humus supports a wide variety of plant life.
The dark color of the topsoil is due to the presence of humus.

Etymology

From Latin 'humus' (earth, ground).
