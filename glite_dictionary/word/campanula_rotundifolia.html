Campanula rotundifolia

1. NOUN

/ kæmˈpæn.jə.lə roʊˌtʌn.dɪˈfoʊ.li.aɪ / (AmE), / kæmˈpan.jʊ.lə rəʊˌtʌn.dɪˈfəʊ.li.aɪ / (BrE)
A slender perennial plant of the Northern Hemisphere with bell-shaped blue flowers, commonly known as harebell.
bell-shaped blue-flowered perennial plant

Examples

The delicate Campanula rotundifolia added a splash of blue to the rocky hillside.
Gardeners admire Campanula rotundifolia for its graceful, nodding bell-shaped flowers.
In Scotland, Campanula rotundifolia is often called the harebell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harebell - Common name for Campanula rotundifolia, a blue-flowered wildflower

Etymology

From Latin: 'campanula' meaning 'little bell', and 'rotundifolia' meaning 'round-leaved'.
