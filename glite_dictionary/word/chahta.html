Chahta

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːtɑ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtə / (BrE)
the language of the Choctaw people, belonging to the Muskogean language family
language of the Choctaw people

Examples

The Chahta language is still spoken by some members of the nation today.
She is learning to speak Chahta to connect with her heritage.
Linguists study Chahta to understand the wider Muskogean language family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Choctaw - the Muskogean language of the Choctaw people (alternate English name)

Etymology

From the Choctaw people's own name for themselves and their language.
