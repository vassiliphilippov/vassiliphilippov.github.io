Nibelung

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːbəˌlʊŋ / (AmE), / ˈniːbəˌlʊŋ / (BrE)
(in German mythology) any of a race of dwarfs who possessed a legendary treasure hoard, later taken by Siegfried.
mythical dwarf race with a treasure hoard

Examples

The legend describes how the Nibelung treasure was taken from the dwarfs by Siegfried.
In Wagner's opera cycle, the cursed gold of the Nibelung brings tragedy to gods and mortals alike.
Scholars debate the origins of the Nibelung myth and its influence on later literature.

Etymology

From German Nibelung, name of a legendary people in the medieval epic 'Nibelungenlied'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈniːbəˌlʊŋ / (AmE), / ˈniːbəˌlʊŋ / (BrE)
(in German mythology) a companion or follower of Siegfried, or a member of a legendary race connected to the hoard of the Nibelungs.
mythological follower of Siegfried

Examples

Siegfried was accompanied by a loyal Nibelung on his quest.
The Nibelungs were said to possess a vast and cursed treasure hoard.
In the epic poem, the Nibelung stands as both ally and rival to the hero.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Valkyrie - a supernatural woman warrior of Norse mythology

Etymology

From German 'Nibelung', used in medieval Germanic epics, notably the Nibelungenlied.
