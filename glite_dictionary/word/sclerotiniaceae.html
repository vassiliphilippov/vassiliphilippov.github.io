Sclerotiniaceae

1. NOUN

/ skləˌroʊtəˈniːəsiː / (AmE), / skləˌrɒtɪnɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi in the order Helotiales, many species of which are plant pathogens.
family of plant-pathogenic fungi

Examples

Several species within the Sclerotiniaceae are serious pathogens of agricultural crops.
Researchers studied the role of Sclerotiniaceae in the spread of plant diseases.
The fungus belongs to the family Sclerotiniaceae, known for infecting a variety of plants.

Etymology

From the genus Sclerotinia + Latin -aceae, a suffix for plant and fungal family names.

2. NOUN

/ skləˌroʊtəˈnaɪˌeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / skləˌrɒtɪnɪˈæsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi in the order Helotiales, many of which are plant pathogens
family of plant-pathogenic fungi

Examples

Many crop diseases are caused by fungi from the Sclerotiniaceae family.
The Sclerotiniaceae are known for their ability to produce sclerotia, hard structures that help them survive adverse conditions.
Researchers are studying members of Sclerotiniaceae to better understand plant-pathogen interactions.

Etymology

From the genus name Sclerotinia (type genus), derived from Greek 'sklēros' (hard), referring to the hard sclerotia produced by these fungi, plus the family suffix '-aceae'.
