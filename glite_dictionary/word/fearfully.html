fearfully

1. ADV

/ ˈfɪrfəlli / (AmE), / ˈfɪəfəli / (BrE)
in a manner that causes alarm or fear; in an alarming or frightening way
in an alarming or frightening manner

Examples

The castle was fearfully quiet as the storm approached.
The villagers were fearfully aware of the approaching threat.
She looked fearfully at the shadow moving in the hallway.

Synset Examples

they were fearfully attacked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frighteningly - in a way that causes fear or shock

Etymology

From 'fearful' + '-ly', forming the adverb.

WordNet ID: oewn-00508189-r

2. ADV

/ ˈfɪr.fəli / (AmE), / ˈfɪə.fəli / (BrE)
In a manner showing fear; with apprehension or dread.
in a manner showing fear

Examples

She looked fearfully over her shoulder as she walked through the dark alley.
He waited fearfully for the doctor's diagnosis.
The child clung fearfully to his mother's hand during the thunderstorm.

Synset Examples

she hurried down the stairs fearfully

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fearlessly - in a manner showing no fear

Etymology

From 'fearful' + '-ly'.
