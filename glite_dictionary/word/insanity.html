insanity

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsæn.ə.ti / (AmE), / ɪnˈsæn.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
A state of severe mental illness that makes a person unable to think or behave normally, especially to the extent of not understanding what is real.
severe mental illness; loss of sanity

Examples

Some crimes are committed during periods of insanity when the person cannot distinguish right from wrong.
The defendant pleaded insanity to avoid a prison sentence.
The novel explores the fine line between genius and insanity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sanity - the state of being sane; soundness of mind
  • Synonym: madness - the state of being mentally ill; extreme foolishness

Etymology

From Latin 'insanitas' (unhealth, madness), from 'insanus' (mad, insane).
