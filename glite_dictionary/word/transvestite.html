transvestite

1. NOUN

/ trænzˈvɛstaɪt / (AmE), / tranzˈvɛstʌɪt / (BrE)
A person who derives sexual pleasure from wearing clothes traditionally associated with the opposite sex; now considered outdated and sometimes offensive.
person who cross-dresses for sexual pleasure
offensive

Examples

The term transvestite is considered outdated by many and has largely been replaced by 'cross-dresser.'
In the play, the character is revealed to be a secret transvestite.
Some people mistakenly believe that every transvestite is also transgender, but these are different identities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross-dresser - a person who wears clothing typical of the opposite sex

Etymology

From Latin transvestire (to dress across), from trans- (across) + vestire (to clothe).

2. NOUN

/ trænzˈvɛstaɪt / (AmE), / tranzˈvɛstaɪt / (BrE)
a person who dresses and acts in a style or manner traditionally associated with the opposite sex, often for reasons of personal expression or sexual satisfaction
person who adopts dress/role of opposite sex

Examples

The documentary followed a transvestite as they navigated daily challenges in their community.
Some people mistakenly confuse the terms transvestite and transgender, though they refer to different experiences.
She identified as a transvestite and found confidence in expressing herself through clothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross-dresser - person who wears clothes associated with the opposite sex
  • Antonym: transgender - person whose gender identity differs from their assigned sex at birth

Etymology

From Latin trans- (across) + vestire (to dress). Coined in the early 20th century.
