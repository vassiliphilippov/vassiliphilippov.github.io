IgD

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ ˌdʒiː ˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌaɪ ˌdʒiː ˈdiː / (BrE)
a class of immunoglobulin present in blood serum in small amounts, involved in the immune response
a class of immunoglobulin (antibody)
medical

Examples

Researchers studied IgD levels to better understand their role in autoimmune diseases.
The patient's blood contained lower than normal amounts of IgD.
IgD plays a minor but important part in the immune system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunoglobulin - any of the classes of proteins functioning as antibodies
  • Degree Higher: IgG - the most abundant class of immunoglobulin in the blood
  • Degree Higher: IgM - a class of immunoglobulin involved in the early stages of immunity

Etymology

Abbreviation of immunoglobulin D.
