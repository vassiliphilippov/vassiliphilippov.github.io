offstage

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔfˌsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
the area of a stage that is not visible to the audience, where performers or props wait before appearing
area of stage not seen by audience

Examples

The actors waited offstage for their cue to enter.
All props were organized neatly offstage before the performance began.
During the chaotic play, several characters collided offstage in the dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backstage - the area behind the stage and out of view of the audience
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04600289-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADV

/ ˌɔfˈsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
in private or out of public view; not openly or publicly.
not in public; in private

Examples

Although the negotiations continued offstage, their outcome was soon announced to the public.
Many important decisions are made offstage before they are presented as policies.
The politicians acted friendly in public, but offstage they often disagreed.

Synset Examples

the deal was done offstage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privately - without public knowledge or presence
  • Antonym: publicly - in public; openly

Etymology

From 'off' + 'stage', originally referring to actors not on stage, now also meaning 'not in public'.
