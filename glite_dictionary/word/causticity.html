causticity

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˈstɪsɪti / (AmE), / kɔːˈstɪsɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being sharply critical, biting, or sarcastic in speech or writing
sharpness or bitterness in speech or writing

Examples

The causticity in her comments made some people uncomfortable during the meeting.
He was known for the causticity with which he dissected public policy.
Journalists sometimes use causticity to expose flaws in government statements.

Synset Examples

Her causticity in criticism often alienates her colleagues.
The causticity of his remarks left everyone speechless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcasm - the use of irony to mock or convey contempt
  • Antonym: gentleness - the quality of being kind, mild, or tender in speech

Etymology

From Late Latin causticitas (burning quality), from Greek kaustikos (capable of burning).
