magnitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡnɪˌtjud / (AmE), / ˈmæɡnɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
the great size, significance, or importance of something, especially compared to other things
relative importance or significance

Examples

The crisis has grown to such magnitude that it can no longer be ignored.
We often underestimate the magnitude of small acts of kindness.
No one realized the full magnitude of the task until it was too late.

Synset Examples

a problem of the first magnitude

Related Terms

  • Synonym: significance - the quality of being important or having a special meaning
  • Antonym: triviality - the quality of being unimportant or insignificant

Etymology

From Latin 'magnitudo', from 'magnus' meaning 'great'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡnɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.nɪ.tjuːd / (BrE)
the size, extent, or importance of something, especially when measured or compared to other things
size or extent of something

Examples

Scientists underestimated the magnitude of the storm's impact on the coastal area.
Even a small increase in temperature can have a significant magnitude of effect on certain species.
The magnitude of the task ahead seemed overwhelming at first.

Synset Examples

they tried to predict the magnitude of the explosion
about the magnitude of a small pea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: size - the physical dimensions or amount of something
  • Synonym: extent - the area covered by something; scope

Etymology

From Latin magnitudo (greatness, size), from magnus (great).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡnɪˌtuːd / (AmE), / ˈmæɡnɪtjuːd / (BrE)
the number representing the ratio of two quantities on a logarithmic scale, indicating relative size or order, especially by powers of ten
logarithmic measure of ratio between quantities

Examples

On a logarithmic scale, the magnitude of the earthquake measured 6, indicating significant energy release.
The brightness of stars is often compared by their order of magnitude, with each step representing a tenfold difference.
Two quantities are of the same magnitude if one is less than ten times the other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order of magnitude - a class in a system of classification determined by size, typically in powers of ten

Etymology

From Latin 'magnitudo' (greatness), from 'magnus' (great).
