nonsyllabic

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈsɪləbɪk / (AmE), / nɒnˈsɪləbɪk / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) not forming or capable of forming the nucleus of a syllable; not able to act as a syllable on its own.
(phonetics) not forming a syllable nucleus

Examples

In the word 'little', the initial nonsyllabic 'l' is followed by a syllabic one.
Some English consonants can appear in both syllabic and nonsyllabic forms depending on their position in a word.
Phonetics students must learn to identify nonsyllabic sounds in various languages.

Synset Examples

initial ‘l’ in ‘little’ is nonsyllabic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: syllabic - (of a sound) able to form the nucleus of a syllable

Etymology

From non- (not) + syllabic, from Latin syllabicus, from Greek syllabikos (‘relating to a syllable’).

2. ADJ

/ nɑnˌsɪˈlæbɪk / (AmE), / nɒnˌsɪˈlæbɪk / (BrE)
(of a sound) not forming or able to form the nucleus of a syllable; not constituting a syllable by itself.
not forming a syllable or syllable nucleus

Examples

The final 'e' in 'little' is nonsyllabic and often not pronounced clearly.
In the diphthong 'oi', the 'i' is considered nonsyllabic.
Linguists examine which sounds are nonsyllabic in different languages.

Synset Examples

the nonsyllabic ‘n’ in ‘botany’ when it is pronounced ‘botny’
the nonsyllabic ‘i’ in ‘oi’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: syllabic - (of a sound) forming the nucleus of a syllable

Etymology

From prefix non- (not) + syllabic, from Greek syllabikos (relating to a syllable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02297470-a
Synset POS: a
