hardened

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrdənd / (AmE), / ˈhɑːd(ə)nd / (BrE)
Made hard, strong, or resilient, especially through heat treatment or other processes.
made hard or strong by processing

Examples

The blades are made from hardened steel to ensure durability.
Special hardened glass is used in car windows for safety.
The chef prefers knives with hardened edges because they stay sharp longer.

Synset Examples

a sword of tempered steel
tempered glass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tempered - made stronger and more resilient by heating and then cooling

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'harden' with the suffix '-ed'.

protected against attack, particularly by making something resistant to damage, especially from nuclear weapons or explosions
protected against attacks or explosions

Examples

Bunkers with hardened walls play a crucial role in national defense strategies.
The government invested in hardened shelters to protect vital infrastructure from attack.

Synset Examples

hardened missile silos

Related Terms

3. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrdnd / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdnd / (BrE)
made tough or resistant, especially as a result of long experience or repeated exposure to something difficult or unpleasant
made tough by experience or exposure

Examples

His face had become hardened after years of working at sea.
Many hardened soldiers find it difficult to express their emotions.
She was a hardened skeptic, unwilling to believe anything without proof.

Synset Examples

hardened fishermen
a peasant, dark, lean-faced, wind-inured
our successors … may be graver, more inured and equable men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inured - accustomed to something, especially something unpleasant
  • Synonym: seasoned - experienced and made competent through practice
  • Antonym: tender - showing sensitivity or gentleness, not toughened

Etymology

From the verb 'harden', itself from Old English 'heardian', meaning 'to make hard'.

4. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrdənd / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdənd / (BrE)
having become solid or firm, especially after being previously soft or liquid; made hard by a process such as drying or cooling
made solid or hard

Examples

The hardened concrete formed the foundation of the new building.
After several days, the mixture had hardened into a solid block.
The artist shaped the clay before it hardened and became difficult to work with.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: softened - having become soft or less hard

Etymology

From the verb 'harden', which is from Old English 'heardian' (to make hard).

having become emotionally insensitive or unfeeling, especially as a result of experience or repeated exposure to difficult situations.
emotionally unfeeling or insensitive

Examples

After years in law enforcement, she became a hardened officer who rarely showed her feelings.
The hardened criminal showed no remorse for his actions in court.
He was a hardened skeptic, unwilling to believe anyone's promises.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
