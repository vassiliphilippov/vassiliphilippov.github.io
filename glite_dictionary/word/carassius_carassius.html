Carassius carassius

1. NOUN

/ kəˈræsiəs kəˈræsiəs / (AmE), / kəˈræsɪəs kəˈræsɪəs / (BrE)
A species of freshwater fish in the carp family (Cyprinidae), native to Europe and closely resembling the wild goldfish.
European freshwater carp species

Examples

The Carassius carassius thrives in slow-moving, vegetation-rich European waters.
Fishermen in Poland often catch Carassius carassius in local ponds and rivers.
Unlike its domesticated relative, the Carassius carassius has retained a wild bronze coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crucian carp - A common name for Carassius carassius, a small European cyprinid fish

Etymology

From the genus name Carassius, from New Latin, ultimately from Germanic origins via scientific taxonomy.
