Pantotheria

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæn.təˈθɪr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌpæn.təˈθɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
an extinct and generalized group of Jurassic mammals believed to be ancestral to marsupials and placental mammals
extinct ancestral mammals of the Jurassic

Examples

Fossils of Pantotheria have helped scientists understand the evolution of modern mammals.
The discovery of Pantotheria remains provides evidence for the origins of marsupials and placentals.
Many paleontologists study Pantotheria to trace mammalian ancestry during the Jurassic period.

Etymology

From Greek 'pant-' (all) + 'therion' (beast), indicating a diverse group of mammals.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpæn.təˈθɪr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌpan.təˈθɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
an extinct subclass of primitive mammals widespread during the Jurassic period, considered ancestors of marsupial and placental mammals
extinct subclass of Jurassic mammals

Examples

Fossils of Pantotheria provide important insight into early mammalian evolution.
Some paleontologists believe that Pantotheria gave rise to both marsupials and placental mammals.
The rapid diversification of Pantotheria marked a key evolutionary step during the Jurassic period.

Etymology

From Greek 'panto-' meaning 'all' and 'theria' meaning 'beasts.'
