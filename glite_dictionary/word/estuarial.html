estuarial

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛstjuˈɛriəl / (AmE), / ˌɛstjuˈɑːriəl / (BrE)
relating to or found in an estuary, especially referring to water, organisms, or conditions characteristic of estuaries
of or relating to estuaries

Examples

The estuarial ecosystem is rich in nutrients and biodiversity.
Many fish species use estuarial areas as breeding grounds.
Scientists studied the effects of pollution on estuarial water quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estuary - the tidal mouth of a large river, where the tide meets the stream

Etymology

From 'estuary' + suffix '-al' (adjectival), from Latin 'aestuarium' (tidal marsh).
