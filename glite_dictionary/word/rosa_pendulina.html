Rosa pendulina

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzə pɛnˈduːlɪnə / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzə pɛnˈdjuːlɪnə / (BrE)
A species of wild rose native to the mountains of Europe, recognized for its crimson flowers and arching stems.
mountainous European rose species with crimson flowers

Examples

The vibrant blooms of Rosa pendulina add a splash of color to the alpine meadows in early summer.
Botanists admire Rosa pendulina for its resilience to cold mountainous climates.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Rosa pendulina for its striking crimson flowers and graceful arching stems.

Etymology

From Latin: 'rosa' (rose) and 'pendulina' (hanging), referring to its drooping stems.
