challenge

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (BrE)
a difficult task or situation that tests someone's ability or determination
difficult situation or task

Examples

The new management role proved to be a real challenge for her.
Climbing Mount Everest is widely regarded as the ultimate challenge for mountaineers.
He enjoys the challenge of solving complex puzzles in his free time.

Synset Examples

they reacted irrationally to the challenge of Russian power

Related Terms

  • Synonym: problem - a matter or situation regarded as unwelcome or harmful and needing to be dealt with
  • Antonym: opportunity - a set of circumstances that makes it possible to do something

Etymology

From Old French chalenge (legal claim, accusation), from Latin calumnia (accusation, complaint).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʃælɪndʒ / (BrE)
a demand by a guard or sentry for a password or identification, especially as a security measure

Examples

The sentry's challenge was answered with the correct password, allowing entry to the base.
Failure to respond to the challenge resulted in the patrol raising the alarm.
