vivacity

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈvæsəti / (AmE), / vɪˈvasəti / (BrE)
The quality of being attractively lively, animated, and full of energy.
liveliness, animation, high spirits

Examples

Her vivacity made her the center of attention at every party.
The children's vivacity was evident as they played in the park.
He admired her vivacity, which brightened even the dullest conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liveliness - the quality of being full of life and energy
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of liveliness or energy

Etymology

From Latin 'vivacitas', from 'vivax' (lively, long-lived).
