Naomi

1. PROPN

/ neɪˈoʊmi / (AmE), / neɪˈəʊmi / (BrE)
the mother-in-law of Ruth in the Old Testament, known for her story in the Book of Ruth
biblical mother-in-law of Ruth

Examples

Naomi encouraged Ruth to remain faithful after they moved to Bethlehem.
The story of Naomi and Ruth is often cited as an example of loyalty and kindness.
Many people draw inspiration from the resilience shown by Naomi in the Bible.

Etymology

From Hebrew נָעֳמִי (Na‘omi), meaning 'pleasantness'.
