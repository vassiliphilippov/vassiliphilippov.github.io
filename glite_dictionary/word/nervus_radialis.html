nervus radialis

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɜrvəs rəˈdeɪliəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs raˈdeɪliːəs / (BrE)
the largest branch of the brachial plexus, extending down the humerus and dividing near the lateral epicondyle into branches for the back of the hand and extensor muscles
major nerve branch; arm and hand innervation
medical

Examples

The nervus radialis can be injured during fractures of the humerus, leading to weakness in the wrist and fingers.
Damage to the nervus radialis may cause loss of sensation on the back of the hand.
Surgeons must take care to avoid the nervus radialis during certain arm procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radial nerve - major nerve of the upper limb, same as nervus radialis

Etymology

From Latin: 'nervus' (nerve) + 'radialis' (of the radius, the forearm bone).
