collapse

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlæps / (AmE), / kəˈlæps / (BrE)
a sudden failure of function, or a state of complete physical exhaustion
sudden failure or exhaustion

Examples

After running the marathon, she was close to collapse from exhaustion.
The sudden collapse of the computer network brought all work to a halt.
His physical collapse was attributed to months of overwork and stress.

Synset Examples

the commander's prostration demoralized his men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breakdown - a failure to function; a sudden collapse
  • Antonym: recovery - a return to normal functioning after collapse or illness

Etymology

From Latin collapsus, past participle of collabi (to fall together)
