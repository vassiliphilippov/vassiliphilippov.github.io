Stravinsky

1. PROPN

/ strəˈvɪnski / (AmE), / strəˈvɪnski / (BrE)
Igor Stravinsky (1882–1971), Russian-born composer notable for his innovative compositions and influence on 20th-century music.
Russian-born composer (1882–1971)

Examples

Stravinsky's "The Rite of Spring" revolutionized classical music in the early twentieth century.
Many scholars study how Stravinsky incorporated Russian folk elements into his compositions.
The orchestra performed a stunning rendition of Stravinsky's "Firebird Suite."

Etymology

From the Russian family name Stravinsky (Стравинский).
