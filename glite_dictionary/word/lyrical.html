lyrical

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈlɪrɪkəl / (BrE)
expressing deep personal emotions, especially in art, music, or literature; full of feeling and inspiration.
expressing deep emotion

Examples

The painter's lyrical style captured the beauty and sorrow of the landscape.
She delivered a lyrical speech that moved many of the listeners to tears.
His music is known for its lyrical melodies and heartfelt lyrics.

Synset Examples

the dancer's lyrical performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emotional - showing strong emotions
  • Antonym: unemotional - not showing strong emotions

Etymology

From Greek lyrikos (singing to the lyre), via Latin lyricus and French lyrique.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪr.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈlɪr.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
(of poetry, music, or other art forms) suitable for singing, or suggestive of song and musical expression.
suitable for singing

Examples

The poem has a lyrical quality that makes it seem almost like a song.
Her lyrical voice filled the concert hall with emotion.
The composer wrote a lyrical melody that audiences found memorable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melodic - having a pleasing melody; tuneful

Etymology

From Greek *lyrikos*, from *lyra* (lyre), originally referring to poetry sung with a lyre.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01505669-s
Synset POS: s
