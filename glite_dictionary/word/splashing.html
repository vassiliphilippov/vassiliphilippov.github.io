splashing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsplæʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsplaʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of scattering or throwing liquid, especially water, around in an uncontrolled or playful way
the act of scattering water

Examples

The children loved splashing in the pool on a hot summer day.
After the rainstorm, the sound of water splashing could be heard everywhere.
Excessive splashing in the bathtub made the floor wet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - to cause liquid to fall noisily onto a surface
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00279065-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈsplæʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsplæʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of causing liquid to scatter or strike a surface noisily
the act of causing liquid to scatter

Examples

The splashing of water in the pool could be heard from the garden.
Laughter and splashing filled the air as the children played in the fountain.
The splashing from the rain against the window was soothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - the sound or action of liquid striking a surface; act of splashing
