height

1. NOUN

/ haɪt / (AmE), / haɪt / (BrE)
the point or period at which something is at its maximum level, strength, or development
highest stage or level

Examples

The orchestra was at the height of its popularity in the early 1990s.
During the height of the storm, the winds reached sixty miles per hour.
She rose to the height of her profession through years of hard work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peak - the highest point or stage of something
  • Synonym: summit - the highest attainable level of something
  • Antonym: low - an instance or period of being at a low level

Etymology

2. NOUN

/ haɪt / (AmE), / haɪt / (BrE)
The distance from the base to the top of a standing person; a measurement of how tall someone is.
distance from head to foot (person)

Examples

Her height is 170 centimeters, which is considered above average for women.
Doctors measure children's height regularly to monitor their growth.
He was proud of his son's height compared to other boys his age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tallness - the quality or state of being tall, especially in a human or animal
  • Antonym: length - the measurement or extent of something from end to end

Etymology

From Old English hēhthu, related to 'high'.

3. NOUN

/ haɪt / (AmE), / haɪt / (BrE)
The measurement of how tall someone or something is, from base to top.
distance from base to top

Examples

The height of the mountain was over 2,000 meters above sea level.
Please write your height on the medical form in centimeters or inches.
They measured the height of the building before installing the new windows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: length - the measurement of something from end to end

Examples

Climbers often experience shortness of breath at higher heights.
At this height, the temperature drops significantly during the night.
