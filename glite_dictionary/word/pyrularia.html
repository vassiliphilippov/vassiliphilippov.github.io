Pyrularia

1. NOUN

/ paɪˌrjuːˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / paɪˌrjʊəˈlɛərɪə / (BrE)
a small genus of chiefly Asiatic parasitic shrubs in the sandalwood family, with greenish flowers and fleshy fruits
genus of Asiatic parasitic shrubs

Examples

The parasitic shrub Pyrularia is native to mountainous regions of Asia.
Botanists have identified several interesting adaptations in the genus Pyrularia.
Fruits of Pyrularia resemble small pears, giving the genus its name.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'pyros' (pear), referring to pear-shaped fruit.

2. NOUN

/ paɪjʊˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / paɪjʊˈlɛːrɪə / (BrE)
a small genus of chiefly Asiatic parasitic shrubs in the sandalwood family
genus of Asiatic parasitic shrubs

Examples

The fruit of Pyrularia is distinctive for its flask-like shape.
Some species in the Pyrularia genus are known to parasitize the roots of other plants.
Botanists studied Pyrularia to understand plant parasitism in Asian forests.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek *pŷr* (fire) and *lūlā́rion* (small flask), from the shape of the fruit.
