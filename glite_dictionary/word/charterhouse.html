charterhouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːrtərˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtəhɒʊs / (BrE)
a Carthusian monastery; a monastery belonging to the Carthusian order of monks
Carthusian monastery

Examples

The old charterhouse was famous for its peaceful gardens and austere architecture.
After the dissolution, the charterhouse was converted into a private residence.
Historians visited the medieval charterhouse to study its unique cloistered design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monastery - a building or complex where monks live and work

Etymology

From French 'Chartreuse', the name of the Carthusian order's first monastery near Grenoble, France.
