pericarditis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪˌkɑrˈdaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˌkɑːˈdaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the pericardium, the membrane surrounding the heart
inflammation of the heart's membrane
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with pericarditis after complaining of severe chest pain.
Doctors treated the pericarditis with anti-inflammatory medication.
Untreated pericarditis can lead to serious cardiac complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myocarditis - inflammation of the heart muscle

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'peri-' (around) and 'kardia' (heart) + '-itis' (inflammation).
