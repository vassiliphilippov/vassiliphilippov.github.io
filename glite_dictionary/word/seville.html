Seville

1. PROPN

/ səˈvɪl / (AmE), / sɪˈvɪl / (BrE)
A city in southwestern Spain, known as a major port, cultural center, and the traditional center of bullfighting in Spain.
city in southwestern Spain; cultural center

Examples

Many tourists visit Seville for its historic architecture and vibrant festivals.
Flamenco music has deep roots in Seville, where the tradition thrives today.
The cathedral of Seville is one of the largest in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Etymology

From Spanish Sevilla, from Latin Hispalis.
