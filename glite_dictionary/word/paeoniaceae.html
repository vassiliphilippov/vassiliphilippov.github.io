Paeoniaceae

1. NOUN

/ piˌoʊniˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / piˌəʊniˈeɪsiˌiː / (BrE)
A family of perennial, mostly rhizomatous herbs and shrubs, commonly known as the peony family, native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.
the peony plant family

Examples

Many garden centers carry several species of Paeoniaceae for ornamental planting.
The Paeoniaceae are easily recognized by their large, showy flowers and distinctive leaves.
Recent studies have led to reclassification within the Paeoniaceae family based on genetic evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peony - a flowering plant in the Paeoniaceae family

Etymology

From the genus name Paeonia, ultimately from Ancient Greek Paiōnia (a plant named after Paeon, physician of the gods).

2. NOUN

/ piˌoʊniˈeɪsiːˌaɪ / (AmE), / piːˌəʊnɪˈeɪsiːˌaɪ / (BrE)
a family of perennial herbaceous plants and shrubs, including peonies, native to temperate regions of Europe and North America
a plant family including peonies

Examples

Many gardeners prize members of the Paeoniaceae family for their beautiful flowers.
Species in the Paeoniaceae are commonly found in temperate climates.
Botanists often study the classification and characteristics of the Paeoniaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peony - a plant of the genus Paeonia, often grown for its large flowers

Etymology

From genus Paeonia (peony) + botanical family suffix -aceae.
