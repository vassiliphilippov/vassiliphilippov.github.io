slapdash

1. ADJ

/ ˈslæpˌdæʃ / (AmE), / ˈslæp.dæʃ / (BrE)
done in a careless or hurried way, without attention to detail or quality
carelessly or haphazardly done

Examples

He handed in a slapdash report that was full of errors and incomplete sentences.
The renovations were so slapdash that the paint began peeling within weeks.
Her slapdash approach to packing meant she forgot several important items.

Synset Examples

a most haphazard system of record keeping
slapdash work
slipshod spelling
sloppy workmanship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slipshod - lacking care, thought, or organization
  • Antonym: meticulous - showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise

Etymology

Perhaps from 'slap' + 'dash', suggesting a hasty or impetuous action.

2. ADV

/ ˈslæpˌdæʃ / (AmE), / ˈslæp.dæʃ / (BrE)
In a careless and hasty manner; without careful attention or thoroughness.
carelessly; hastily or recklessly

Examples

The repair work was done slapdash and had to be redone soon after.
She packed her suitcase slapdash before leaving for the airport.
The painting looked slapdash, with paint splatters everywhere.

Synset Examples

the shelves were put up slapdash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carelessly - without taking care or attention
  • Antonym: methodically - in an orderly or systematic manner

Etymology

Possibly from 'slap' + 'dash', dating from the 17th century, meaning done in a haphazard way.
