shamus

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪməs / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪməs / (BrE)
a private detective; someone employed to gather information and assist in criminal investigations
private detective

Examples

The shamus followed the suspect through the crowded street, careful not to be seen.
He hired a shamus to uncover the truth about his business partner.
Old detective novels often feature a wise-cracking shamus solving mysteries in the big city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective - someone whose job is to investigate and solve crimes
  • Synonym: sleuth - an informal term for a detective

Etymology

From Yiddish *shames* (synagogue caretaker), influenced by Irish name Seamus.
