Aquifoliaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæk.wəˌfoʊ.liˈeɪ.siˌi / (AmE), / ˌakwɪˌfəʊlɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of widely distributed shrubs and trees, including the hollies (genus Ilex).
family of shrubs and trees (including hollies)

Examples

The Aquifoliaceae family contains plants commonly known as hollies.
Several ornamental shrubs in the Aquifoliaceae are popular in gardens worldwide.
Botanists recently discovered a new species in the Aquifoliaceae family in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ilex - the genus of holly plants within the Aquifoliaceae family

Etymology

From Latin *AQUIFOLIUM* (holly), the type genus, plus the botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌækwɪˌfoʊliˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌakwɪˌfɒlɪˈiːsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of widely distributed shrubs and trees, commonly known as the holly family, characterized by evergreen or deciduous leaves and small berries
the holly plant family

Examples

Many species in the Aquifoliaceae family are cultivated as ornamental plants for their attractive foliage and berries.
The Aquifoliaceae can be found throughout temperate and subtropical regions worldwide.
Botanists have identified over 400 species within the Aquifoliaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holly - a shrub or small tree with spiny leaves and red berries, typical of the Aquifoliaceae family

Etymology

From the genus name Ilex aquifolium (holly), from Latin 'acus' (needle) and 'folium' (leaf), referencing the spiny leaves.
