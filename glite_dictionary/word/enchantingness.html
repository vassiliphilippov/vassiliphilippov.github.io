enchantingness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtʃæn.tɪŋ.nəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtʃɑːn.tɪŋ.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of having the ability to enchant; being charming or delightful.
quality of being enchanting

Examples

The enchantingness of her smile lit up the entire room and captivated everyone's attention.
Despite the castle's age, its enchantingness remains undiminished, drawing visitors from around the world.
He wrote about the enchantingness of the landscape, with its rolling hills and sparkling rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charm - the power or quality of delighting, attracting, or fascinating others
  • Synonym: delightfulness - quality of being delightful
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of interest or excitement; being uninteresting

Etymology

Formed from 'enchanting' + '-ness', where 'enchant' is from Old French 'enchanter', from Latin 'incantare' (to chant a spell).
