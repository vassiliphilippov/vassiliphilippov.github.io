to demote

1. VERB

/ dɪˈmoʊt / (AmE), / dɪˈməʊt / (BrE)
to assign someone to a lower position or rank, often as a result of poor performance or disciplinary action
reduce to a lower rank or position

Examples

After the company restructured, she was demoted to an entry-level position.
The coach decided to demote the player to the minor league due to poor performance.
If you continue to miss deadlines, the manager may have to demote you.

Synset Examples

She was demoted because she always speaks up
He was broken down to Sergeant

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to promote - to advance to a higher position or rank

Etymology

From de- (down) + promote; first recorded in early 20th century.
