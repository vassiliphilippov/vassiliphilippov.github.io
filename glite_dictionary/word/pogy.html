pogy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊɡi / (AmE), / ˈpəʊɡi / (BrE)
(chiefly Canadian, informal) Money received from the government as unemployment or welfare benefit.
government welfare or unemployment benefit (esp. Canada)
informal

Examples

After losing his job, he had to rely on pogy to pay his bills.
Many families depended on pogy during the economic recession.
He was embarrassed to admit he was on pogy, but grateful for the support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dole - government unemployment benefit, chiefly British

Etymology

North American English, possibly from 'pogie' (slang for poorhouse) or alteration of 'pauper.'
