Aragon

1. PROPN

/ ˈærəˌɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈærəɡən / (BrE)
a region in northeastern Spain; formerly a kingdom that united with Castile in 1479 to help form modern Spain.
historical region of Spain

Examples

The ancient kingdom of Aragon played a vital role in medieval European politics.
Many castles can be found throughout Aragon, reflecting its rich history.
He traveled across Aragon to research the region's traditions and language.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Aragón', name of the region and former kingdom.
