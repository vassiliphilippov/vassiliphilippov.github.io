microgametophyte

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.kroʊ.ɡəˈmiː.təˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.krəʊ.ɡəˈmiː.tə.faɪt / (BrE)
the male gametophyte that develops from a microspore, producing male gametes in plants
male gametophyte from a microspore
medical

Examples

The microgametophyte develops within the pollen grain of flowering plants.
In many seed plants, fertilization requires the transfer of the microgametophyte to the female structure.
Researchers studied the development of the microgametophyte under different environmental conditions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: microspore - a small spore that develops into a male gametophyte
  • Antonym: megagametophyte - the female gametophyte developed from a megaspore

Etymology

From micro- (small) + gametophyte, where gametophyte derives from Greek 'gametēs' (husband) and 'phyton' (plant).
