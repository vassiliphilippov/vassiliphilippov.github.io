Cerro Bonete

1. PROPN

/ ˈseɾ.oʊ boʊˈneɪ.teɪ / (AmE), / ˈse.rəʊ bəʊˈneɪ.teɪ / (BrE)
a mountain in the Andes of Argentina, reaching an elevation of 22,546 feet (6,872 meters).
mountain in the Andes, Argentina

Examples

Climbers from around the world travel to Argentina to ascend Cerro Bonete each year.
The breathtaking view from the summit of Cerro Bonete attracts many adventurers and photographers.
Located in the Andes, Cerro Bonete is among the highest peaks in South America.

Etymology

From Spanish, cerro meaning 'hill' or 'mountain' and Bonete meaning 'bonnet' or 'cap'.
