to mosey

1. VERB

/ ˈmoʊzi / (AmE), / ˈməʊzi / (BrE)
to walk or move at a slow, relaxed pace; to stroll in an unhurried way, often without a fixed destination
walk or move slowly and casually
informal american

Examples

We decided to mosey down to the park after lunch.
On Sunday mornings, he likes to mosey through the farmer’s market.
She moseyed over to the counter, taking her time to choose a pastry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroll - to walk in a slow, relaxed manner
  • Antonym: rush - to move with great speed or urgency

Etymology

Originated in American English in the early 19th century, perhaps from dialectal English 'mose' (to go about slowly).
