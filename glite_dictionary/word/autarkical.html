autarkical

1. ADJ

/ ɔːˈtɑːrkɪkəl / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɑːkɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of autarky; promoting or practicing economic self-sufficiency and independence.
relating to economic self-sufficiency

Examples

The government's autarkical policies aimed to reduce reliance on foreign trade.
Some historians have analyzed North Korea's autarkical economic system in global context.
Advocating an autarkical model, the country sought self-sufficiency in all essential goods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autarkic - relating to autarky (economic self-sufficiency)

Etymology

From autarky + -ical, with autarky from Greek autarkeia (self-sufficiency).

2. ADJ

/ ɔːˈtɑːrkɪkəl / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɑːkɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a country or economy) self-sufficient and not dependent on imports; characterized by economic independence.
economically self-sufficient; not relying on imports

Examples

The country pursued an autarkical policy in an effort to reduce its dependence on foreign goods.
Some governments adopt autarkical measures to protect their local industries from external competition.
An autarkical economy seeks to produce everything it needs within its own borders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autarky - economic self-sufficiency; a policy of national self-reliance

Etymology

From Greek autarkeia ‘self-sufficiency’ via French autarcie and German Autarkie; see also autarky.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00731547-s
Synset POS: s
