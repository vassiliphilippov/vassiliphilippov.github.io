to dehydrogenate

1. VERB

/ diˈhaɪdrədʒəˌneɪt / (AmE), / diːˈhaɪdrɒdʒəneɪt / (BrE)
to remove hydrogen from (a chemical compound, especially in a chemical reaction)
remove hydrogen from a compound
medical

Examples

Some catalysts are used to dehydrogenate hydrocarbons during fuel processing.
The process will dehydrogenate the alcohol to form the corresponding aldehyde.
Scientists dehydrogenated the compound to study its reactivity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to hydrogenate - to add hydrogen to a compound

Etymology

From de- (removal) + hydrogen + -ate (verb-forming suffix).
