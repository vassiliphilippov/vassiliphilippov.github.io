Dendroctonus rufipennis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛnˌdroʊkˈtoʊnəs ˌruːfəˈpɛnɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɛndrɒkˈtəʊnəs ˌruːfɪˈpɛnɪs / (BrE)
a species of small beetle, commonly known as the spruce bark beetle, that bores through the bark of spruce trees and feeds on the cambium, often killing the tree
species of beetle that infests spruce trees

Examples

The outbreak of Dendroctonus rufipennis in Alaska forests has devastated large areas of spruce trees.
Researchers are studying how Dendroctonus rufipennis spreads and kills its host trees.
Infestations of Dendroctonus rufipennis can result in significant economic losses for the timber industry.

Synset Examples

the spruce bark beetle is the major tree-killing insect pest of Alaska spruce forests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spruce bark beetle - common name for Dendroctonus rufipennis

Etymology

From Greek dendron (tree) + ktonos (killer); species name from Latin rufus (red) + penna (wing).
