Dhodhekanisos

1. PROPN

/ doʊˌdɛkəˈniːsɒs / (AmE), / dəʊˌdɛkəˈniːsɒs / (BrE)
a group of islands in the southeast Aegean Sea, known collectively as the Dodecanese
group of islands in the southeast Aegean Sea

Examples

The island of Rhodes is the largest in the Dhodhekanisos archipelago.
Tourists are drawn to the Dhodhekanisos for its rich history and beautiful beaches.
Many ancient ruins can be found on the Dhodhekanisos islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dodecanese - alternate name for the group of islands in the southeast Aegean Sea

Etymology

From Greek Δωδεκάνησα (Dodekánisa), meaning 'twelve islands'.
