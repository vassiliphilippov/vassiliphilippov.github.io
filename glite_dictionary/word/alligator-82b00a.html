Alligator

1. NOUN

/ ˈælɪˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈælɪɡeɪtə / (BrE)
A genus of large reptiles, including the American alligator and the Chinese alligator, belonging to the family Alligatoridae.
genus of large reptiles (alligators)

Examples

The Alligator is native to the southeastern United States and eastern China.
Scientists have studied fossils to understand the evolutionary history of the Alligator.
The American Alligator can grow to a length of over 4 meters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocodile - large predatory semiaquatic reptile of the family Crocodylidae
  • Synonym: caiman - reptile closely related to alligators, found chiefly in Central and South America

Etymology

From Spanish 'el lagarto' meaning 'the lizard'.
