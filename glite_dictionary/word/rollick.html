to rollick

1. VERB

/ ˈrɑː.lɪk / (AmE), / ˈrɒl.ɪk / (BrE)
to behave or move in a lively, cheerful, and boisterous way
behave in a lively, boisterous way

Examples

The children began to rollick around the playground as soon as recess began.
Letting out joyful shouts, the puppies rollicked in the grass under the afternoon sun.
The party guests continued to rollick late into the night, filling the house with laughter.

Synset Examples

The children frolicked in the garden
the gamboling lambs in the meadows
The toddlers romped in the playroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolic - to play and move about cheerfully and energetically
  • Synonym: romp - to play in a lively and energetic way
