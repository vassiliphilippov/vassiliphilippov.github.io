PFLP

1. NOUN

/ pi ɛf ɛl pi / (AmE), / piː ɛf ɛl piː / (BrE)
a militant Palestinian organization founded in 1967, combining Marxist-Leninist ideology with Palestinian nationalism, and known for using violence to advance its aims.
Palestinian militant organization, founded 1967

Examples

The PFLP played a prominent role in Palestinian political movements during the late 20th century.
Members of the PFLP claimed responsibility for several high-profile attacks in the 1970s.
International organizations have frequently condemned the violent activities of the PFLP.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine'.
