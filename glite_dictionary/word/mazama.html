Mazama

1. NOUN

/ məˈzɑːmə / (AmE), / məˈzɑːmə / (BrE)
A genus of small, usually solitary deer found in Central and South America, commonly known as brockets.
genus of small deer (brockets)

Examples

The Mazama is native to the forests of South America.
Researchers observed several Mazama species in the Amazon basin.
Unlike larger deer, the Mazama tends to live alone and avoids open spaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brocket - a small deer of the genus Mazama

Etymology

From Nahuatl mazame (plural of mazatl, meaning deer).

2. NOUN

/ məˈzæmə / (AmE), / məˈzɑːmə / (BrE)
A genus of small deer known as brockets, native to Central and South America.
genus of small Central and South American deer

Examples

The Mazama genus includes several species of brocket deer found throughout South America.
Habitat loss is a major threat to members of the Mazama genus.
Researchers observed a rare Mazama in the Amazon rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brocket deer - common name for small deer species in genus Mazama

Etymology

From Nahuatl *mazame*, plural of *mazatl* meaning 'deer'.
