ambiversion

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.bɪˈvɝː.ʒən / (AmE), / ˌæm.bɪˈvɜː.ʒən / (BrE)
A personality trait characterized by a balance between extroversion and introversion, showing neither extreme.
balance between extroversion and introversion
medical

Examples

Many people exhibit ambiversion, feeling comfortable both in social situations and when spending time alone.
His ambiversion allows him to adapt to different group dynamics with ease.
Psychologists often discuss ambiversion as the middle ground between introversion and extroversion.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: introversion - the quality of being inward-turning or focused on internal thoughts
  • Degree Higher: extroversion - the quality of being outgoing and focused on external stimulation

Etymology

From Latin 'ambi-' (both) and 'version' (turning).
