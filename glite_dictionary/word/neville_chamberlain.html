Neville Chamberlain

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɛvəl ˈtʃeɪmbərlɪn / (AmE), / ˈnɛvɪl ˈtʃeɪmbəlɪn / (BrE)
(1869–1940) British statesman who served as Prime Minister (1937–1940), known for his policy of appeasement toward fascist Germany before World War II.
British Prime Minister (1937–1940), pursued appeasement policy

Examples

Neville Chamberlain is often remembered for his "peace for our time" speech after the Munich Agreement.
Many historians debate whether Neville Chamberlain's appeasement policy delayed or hastened the outbreak of World War II.
As Prime Minister, Neville Chamberlain tried to maintain peace in Europe through diplomatic negotiations.
