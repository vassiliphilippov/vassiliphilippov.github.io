epoxy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɑksi / (AmE), / ɪˈpɒksi / (BrE)
a thermosetting synthetic resin used chiefly in strong adhesives, coatings, and laminates
strong synthetic resin used in adhesives and coatings

Examples

The carpenter used epoxy to bond the two pieces of wood together.
After mixing the epoxy, she quickly applied it to the cracks in the ceramic vase.
Many surfboards are coated with epoxy for extra durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resin - a solid or highly viscous substance of plant or synthetic origin

Etymology

From 'epoxide' (a type of chemical group) and 'polymer'.

2. VERB

/ ɪˈpɑksi / (AmE), / ɪˈpɒksi / (BrE)
to glue or fasten using epoxy resin as an adhesive
to glue with epoxy resin

Examples

She decided to epoxy the broken vase back together rather than use regular glue.
Make sure the surfaces are clean before you epoxy the parts in place.
He epoxied the loose tiles to ensure they wouldn't move again.

Synset Examples

epoxy the shards

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glue - to stick things together using glue

Etymology

From the noun 'epoxy', which comes from 'epoxide' + '-y', originally referring to the resin used in adhesives.
