brilliantly

1. ADV

/ ˈbrɪliəntli / (AmE), / ˈbrɪliəntli / (BrE)
In a way that is very bright or shining strongly.
with great brightness or radiance

Examples

The chandelier sparkled brilliantly in the grand ballroom.
The morning sun shone brilliantly through the window, filling the room with light.
The fireworks lit up the sky brilliantly during the festival.

Synset Examples

the stars shone brilliantly
the windows glowed jewel bright

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brightly - in a bright or shining way

Etymology

From 'brilliant' (from French *brillant*, from Latin *beryllus*, meaning 'beryl, precious stone'), plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈbrɪljəntli / (AmE), / ˈbrɪljəntli / (BrE)
In an extremely intelligent, skillful, or impressive way.
in a highly intelligent or impressive manner

Examples

She brilliantly solved the complex puzzle in just a few minutes.
The scientist explained the theory brilliantly during the conference.
He performed brilliantly on his final exams, impressing all his teachers.

Synset Examples

he solved the problem brilliantly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleverly - in a way that shows intelligence or skill
  • Antonym: stupidly - in a way that shows lack of intelligence

Etymology

From 'brilliant' + '-ly', where 'brilliant' comes from French *brillant*, present participle of *briller* (to shine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00077308-r
Synset POS: r
