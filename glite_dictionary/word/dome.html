2. NOUN

/ doʊm / (AmE), / dəʊm / (BrE)
A large sports stadium with a roof, often used for football, baseball, or concerts.
roofed sports stadium

Examples

The football championship was held in the massive dome downtown.
When it started to rain, the game continued thanks to the stadium’s dome.
Many cities have built a dome to host year-round sporting events.

Etymology

From Latin 'domus' (house) via Italian 'duomo' (church with a domed roof).

3. NOUN

/ doʊm / (AmE), / dəʊm / (BrE)
A rounded or hemispherical roof or ceiling, often found on buildings such as churches, mosques, or government structures.
hemispherical roof or ceiling

Examples

The Capitol building is easily recognized by its impressive dome.
Sunlight filtered through the glass dome of the botanical garden.
The mosque's grand dome is visible from miles away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cupola - a small dome on top of a building

Etymology

From Latin 'domus' (house), later via Italian 'duomo' (church, cathedral).

4. NOUN

/ doʊm / (AmE), / dəʊm / (BrE)
a rounded vault or roof having a concave shape that curves downward, typically found in architecture
rounded, downward-curving roof or vault

Examples

The mosque was topped with a magnificent silver dome that gleamed in the sunlight.
The greenhouse has a glass dome to let in maximum natural light.
The artists restored the faded frescoes that adorned the inside of the cathedral's dome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vault - an arched structure forming a roof or ceiling
  • Synonym: arch - a curved structure spanning an opening

Etymology

From Latin 'domus' (house), through Italian 'duomo' (cathedral) and French 'dôme'.
