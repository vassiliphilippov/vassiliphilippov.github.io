airscrew

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌskru / (AmE), / ˈeəˌskruː / (BrE)
a propeller that rotates to push against air, especially used for aircraft propulsion
aircraft propeller
british

Examples

The pilot checked the airscrew before starting the engine.
Early aircraft often used a wooden airscrew for propulsion.
Damage to the airscrew can seriously affect flight performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propeller - a device with rotating blades for driving an aircraft or ship

Etymology

Formed from 'air' and 'screw', reflecting the screw-like motion of propellers in air.
