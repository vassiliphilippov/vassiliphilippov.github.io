modishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊdɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈməʊdɪʃnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being fashionable or in keeping with current styles
the state of being fashionable

Examples

The modishness of her clothing caught everyone's attention at the party.
Designers often strive to achieve modishness in their new collections.
He cared little for modishness, preferring comfort over current trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fashionableness - the quality of being in style or popular in fashion
  • Antonym: outmodedness - the state of being out of fashion or outdated

Etymology

From 'modish' (fashionable), which is from 'mode' (fashion, manner), from French 'mode'.
