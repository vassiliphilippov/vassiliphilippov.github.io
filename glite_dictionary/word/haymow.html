haymow

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪˌmaʊ / (AmE), / ˈheɪˌmaʊ / (BrE)
an area in a barn where hay is stored, typically in a large pile or heap
area in a barn for storing hay

Examples

The children played in the haymow while their parents worked on the farm.
During the winter, the cows were fed from the haymow above the barn.
He climbed into the haymow to check if there was enough feed for the horses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hayloft - the upper part of a barn where hay is stored

Etymology

From 'hay' (grass cut and dried for fodder) + 'mow' (a heap, especially of hay), from Old English 'mūga' (heap).

2. NOUN

/ ˈheɪˌmaʊ / (AmE), / ˈheɪˌmaʊ / (BrE)
a loft or upper area in a barn used for storing hay
loft in a barn for storing hay

Examples

The children played hide-and-seek in the haymow on warm summer afternoons.
After the harvest, they stacked the bales neatly in the haymow.
A ladder led up to the haymow, where the scent of dried grass filled the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hayloft - a loft in a barn for storing hay

Etymology

From 'hay' + dialectal 'mow' (a stack of hay or grain); ultimately from Old English 'muga' (heap, stack).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03505701-n
Synset POS: n
