to confabulate

1. VERB

/ kənˈfæbjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / kənˈfabjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(medical, psychology) to unconsciously fill gaps in memory by fabricating stories or experiences, often without the intention to deceive.
fabricate memories unconsciously (medical)
medical

Examples

Patients with certain brain injuries may confabulate details, sincerely believing in events that never happened.
After his stroke, Mark began to confabulate about his childhood, inserting stories that were not true.
It is important for clinicians to recognize when their patients confabulate memories rather than intentionally lie.

Etymology

From Latin confabulatus, past participle of confabulari, meaning 'to talk together.'

2. VERB

/ kənˈfæbjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / kənˈfabjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to engage in informal conversation or chat, especially about unimportant matters
to chat informally

Examples

Every Friday, the neighbors confabulate over coffee at the local bakery.
After the meeting, the colleagues confabulated in the hallway about their weekend plans.
She enjoys confabulating with friends on a leisurely afternoon.

Synset Examples

the men were sitting in the cafe and shooting the breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - to talk in a friendly informal way
  • Synonym: to shoot the breeze - to have a casual, informal conversation

Etymology

From Latin confabulatus, past participle of confabulari (to talk together), from com- (together) + fabulari (to talk, chat).

3. VERB

/ kənˈfæbjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / kənˈfæbjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to talk together or discuss something, especially in order to make a decision; to confer
talk together; confer

Examples

The committee will confabulate before reaching a final decision.
We should confabulate with all stakeholders to ensure everyone's concerns are addressed.
The doctors confabulated about the best treatment for the patient.

Synset Examples

We conferred about a plan of action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confer - to have discussions; exchange opinions
  • Synonym: to consult - to seek advice or exchange views with others

Etymology

From Late Latin confabulatus, past participle of 'confabulari' (to converse), from Latin 'con-' (together) + 'fabulari' (to talk, chat).
