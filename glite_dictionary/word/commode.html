commode

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmoʊd / (AmE), / kəˈməʊd / (BrE)
a tall, elegant chest of drawers, especially one used for storing clothing
tall, elegant chest of drawers

Examples

The antique commode in the hallway is made of mahogany and brass.
She stored her linens in a beautifully crafted commode in the bedroom.
Auction houses often feature 18th-century French commodes as valuable collector's items.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dresser - a chest of drawers for storing clothes

Etymology

From French *commode* (convenient, useful), originally describing something practical or suitable.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmoʊd / (AmE), / kəˈməʊd / (BrE)
a toilet, especially one that is a porcelain plumbing fixture for defecation and urination
a toilet; plumbing fixture for waste disposal
american

Examples

The hospital room was equipped with a portable commode next to the bed for the patient’s convenience.
After her surgery, she found it easier to use a raised commode in the bathroom.
The plumber was called to fix a leaking commode in the guest restroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toilet - a fixed receptacle for defecation and urination

Etymology

From French 'commode' meaning convenient or suitable.
