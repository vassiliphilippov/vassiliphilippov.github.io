determinate

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈtɝː.mə.nət / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜː.mɪ.nət / (BrE)
precisely defined, fixed, or limited; determined in quantity, character, or extent, usually by rule or by a known cause
precisely defined or fixed

Examples

The boundary of the property is determinate, marked by a stone wall.
Scientists observed determinate growth in certain plant species, meaning they stop growing after reaching a fixed size.
The law sets a determinate penalty for that specific offense.

Synset Examples

a determinate distance
a determinate number
determinate variations in animals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indeterminate - not exactly known, established, or defined; indefinite

Etymology

From Latin determinatus, past participle of determinare (to limit, define).

providing a final or conclusive settlement; definite and fixed
final or conclusive; definite

Examples

A determinate answer to the problem ended all further discussion.
Scientists seek determinate results to validate their theories.

Synset Examples

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈtɜrmənət / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːmɪnət / (BrE)
(of plant growth) not continuing to grow at the apex; having a fixed or limited growth, often producing flowers or fruit at the tip.
(botany) having fixed, not indefinite, growth

Examples

Tomatoes with determinate growth produce a single harvest rather than yielding fruit throughout the season.
The scientist examined the determinate shoot, noting that the apical bud had produced a flower.
Bush beans are an example of a determinate plant, as their stems stop growing after flowering.

Synset Examples

determinate growth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indeterminate - not having fixed or limited growth; continuing to grow at the apex

Etymology

From Latin determinatus, past participle of determinare (to limit, fix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00744659-a
Synset POS: a
