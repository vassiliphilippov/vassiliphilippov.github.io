acromikria

1. NOUN

/ ˌækroʊˈmɪkriə / (AmE), / ˌakrəʊˈmɪkrɪə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by abnormally small and underdeveloped fingers and toes.
abnormally small fingers and toes
medical

Examples

Patients diagnosed with acromikria often require specialized hand and foot care.
The rare genetic disorder causes acromikria and facial abnormalities in affected children.
Doctors identified acromikria during the newborn's physical examination.

Etymology

From Greek 'akron' (extremity) + 'mikros' (small).
