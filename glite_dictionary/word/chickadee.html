chickadee

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkəˌdi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkədiː / (BrE)
A small North American songbird with a grey and black plumage, noted for its distinctive call.
small North American songbird

Examples

A chickadee perched on the windowsill, peering curiously inside.
We heard the cheerful call of a chickadee while hiking through the forest.
Several chickadees visited the bird feeder throughout the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titmouse - another name for similar small woodland songbirds

Etymology

Imitative of its characteristic call, with the name based on its song: 'chick-a-dee-dee-dee'.
