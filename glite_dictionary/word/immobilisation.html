immobilisation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmoʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of preventing a body part from moving, often to allow proper healing, especially by using a cast or splint.
medical: preventing movement of a body part
medical british

Examples

The doctor recommended immobilisation of the ankle to ensure a full recovery.
Prolonged immobilisation can sometimes lead to muscle weakness and stiffness.
After the fracture, immobilisation was achieved with a plaster cast.

Synset Examples

immobilization of the injured knee was necessary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immobilization - act of preventing movement (US spelling)

Etymology

From Latin immobilis (not movable) + -ation.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌmoʊbələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of preventing movement or making incapable of motion, especially for safety, medical, or practical reasons
the act of preventing movement

Examples

The doctor recommended immobilisation of the injured limb using a splint.
Flooding led to the immobilisation of all transport in the region.
Strict immobilisation is necessary after surgery to aid recovery.

Synset Examples

the storm caused complete immobilization of the rescue team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immobilization - American English spelling: the act of preventing movement
  • Antonym: movement - the act of moving; opposite state of action
  • Synonym: restriction - the act of limiting movement; similar meaning

Etymology

From French 'immobilisation', from Latin 'immobilis' (not movable).
