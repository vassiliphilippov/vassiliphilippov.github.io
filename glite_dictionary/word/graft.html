graft

1. NOUN

/ ɡræft / (AmE), / ɡrɑːft / (BrE)
the act or process of joining one plant or piece of tissue to another so that they grow together as a single organism.
joining of plant parts or tissues

Examples

The graft of the rose cutting onto the rootstock was successful.
She studied the technique of graft in her botany class.
A well-performed graft can produce hardier fruit trees.

Etymology

From Old French 'grafe' (stylus, graft), from Latin 'graphium' (stylus), from Greek 'graphion' (writing implement).

2. NOUN

/ ɡræft / (AmE), / ɡrɑːft / (BrE)
a piece of living tissue or an organ transplanted from a donor to a recipient in surgical procedures
transplanted tissue or organ in surgery
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully placed the graft to restore blood flow to the damaged artery.
After the accident, doctors used a skin graft to help her wounds heal.
There is always a risk that the body may reject the transplanted graft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transplant - an operation in which an organ or tissue is moved from one body to another

Etymology

From Old French *grafe* (means of inserting a shoot), from Medieval Latin *graphium* (stylus, grafting knife).

3. VERB

/ ɡræft / (AmE), / ɡrɑːft / (BrE)
to insert an organ or tissue from a donor into the body of a recipient, especially as a medical operation
insert donor organ/tissue by surgery
medical

Examples

Doctors graft a healthy kidney into the patient during the transplant surgery.
Surgeons were able to graft skin from his leg onto his burned arm.
Advances in medicine now allow physicians to graft organs with a higher success rate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transplant - to move an organ or tissue from one body to another

Etymology

From Old French grafer (to graft, to insert), from Medieval Latin *graphiare*, from Latin *graphium* (stylus, pointed instrument). Medical usage extended by analogy to surgical transplantation.

/ ɡræft / (AmE), / ɡrɑːft / (BrE)
to join a shoot or bud from one plant onto another so that they grow together as a single plant
join parts of plants to grow together

Examples

Gardeners often graft roses to create hardier varieties.
It takes skill to successfully graft a branch from one tree onto another.
The horticulturist decided to graft a new species onto the old apple tree.

Related Terms

5. NOUN

/ ɡræft / (AmE), / ɡrɑːft / (BrE)
the use of dishonest or illegal methods, especially bribery, to gain an advantage, usually in politics or business
dishonest or illegal gain, especially through bribery

Examples

The investigation uncovered widespread graft among local officials.
Many citizens lost faith in the government due to reports of graft and corruption.
She refused to participate in any graft, even when pressured by her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bribery - the offering of money or favors to influence someone in authority
  • Synonym: corruption - dishonest or fraudulent conduct by those in power

Etymology

Originally from Old French *grafter* (to transplant), the corrupt sense developed in late 19th-century American slang, possibly from ‘graft’ as work, connoting illicit or underhanded effort.
