shy

1. ADJ

/ ʃaɪ / (AmE), / ʃaɪ / (BrE)
(of a number or amount) less than or just short of a particular number, often by a small amount.
just short of a number or amount

Examples

We finished the race just one shy of the top ten.
Her savings are a few dollars shy of what she needs for the trip.
The committee lacked one vote shy of a majority.

Synset Examples

eleven is one shy of a dozen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short - being less than the required or expected amount
  • Synonym: under - below a certain amount or number

2. ADJ

/ ʃaɪ / (AmE), / ʃaɪ / (BrE)
Wary and distrustful; inclined to avoid persons or things.
wary; inclined to avoid

Examples

Many animals remain shy of humans and quickly disappear into the woods.
He has always been shy of making investments in volatile markets.
Children can sometimes appear shy of new experiences until they feel comfortable.

Synset Examples

shy of strangers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cautious - careful to avoid potential problems or dangers
  • Antonym: bold - not afraid to take risks or face danger
Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ʃaɪ / (AmE), / ʃaɪ / (BrE)
a quick or casual throw, especially at a target
quick or casual throw

Examples

He took a shy at the wastebasket but missed.
The fielder made a quick shy at the stumps.
She had one last shy to knock down the target.

Synset Examples

he gave the ball a shy to the first baseman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throw - the act of propelling something with force through the air

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly related to the verb 'shy', meaning 'to throw quickly or with a jerk', 18th century in this sense.

5. VERB

/ ʃaɪ / (AmE), / ʃaɪ / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to move or start back suddenly, especially from fright or surprise.
start back suddenly, especially from fright

Examples

The horse shied at the sudden noise beside the road.
She shied away from the barking dog, startled by its aggression.
Whenever he saw his own reflection, the deer would shy and dart back into the woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to startle - to cause (someone) to move or jump suddenly in surprise or fright

6. ADJ

/ ʃaɪ / (AmE), / ʃaɪ / (BrE)
nervous or lacking self-confidence in the presence of others, especially when meeting new people or in unfamiliar situations
lacking self-confidence; timid

Examples

As a shy child, he often avoided speaking in front of the class.
Even though she was shy, she managed to introduce herself at the party.
He became less shy once he got to know everyone in the group.

Synset Examples

stood in the doorway diffident and abashed
problems that call for bold not timid responses
a very unsure young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened
  • Antonym: confident - feeling or showing certainty about something

Etymology

From Old English *sceoh* (timid, shy), related to Old High German *sceoh* (afraid).
