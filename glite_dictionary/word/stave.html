stave

1. NOUN

/ steɪv / (AmE), / steɪv / (BrE)
a horizontal crosspiece connecting the legs of a chair or stool, providing support and stability
crosspiece between chair legs

Examples

The craftsman carved a decorative stave to connect the front legs of the chair.
One of the staves was broken, making the stool unstable.
A sturdy stave keeps the chair's legs from splaying out over time.

Etymology

From Old English 'staf' (staff, rod), related to Old Norse ‘stafr’.

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ steɪv / (AmE), / steɪv / (BrE)
one of several thin, curved pieces of wood forming the sides of a barrel or bucket
slat of wood forming a barrel's side

Examples

The cooper carefully shaped each stave to fit perfectly in the barrel.
A single broken stave can cause a bucket to leak water.
The wine soaked into the oak staves, giving the drink its distinctive flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slat - a thin, narrow strip of wood or metal

Etymology

From Old English 'staf' (staff, rod, stave), related to Old Norse 'stafr'.

4. NOUN

/ steɪv / (AmE), / steɪv / (BrE)
(music) A set of five horizontal lines and four spaces on which musical notes are written.
group of five lines for writing music

Examples

The melody was written across a single stave using treble clef.
Young students learn how to place notes correctly on the stave.
Each stave in the score represents a different instrument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staff - (music) set of horizontal lines for musical notation (US English)

Etymology

From Old English *stæf* (staff, stick); musical usage parallels 'staff.'

/ steɪv / (AmE), / steɪv / (BrE)

Examples

Rescuers tried to avoid staving the door, hoping to open it gently.
