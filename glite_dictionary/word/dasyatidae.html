Dasyatidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæsiˈætɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌdæsɪˈatɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of cartilaginous fishes known as sting rays, characterized by flattened bodies and long, whip-like tails, often armed with a venomous spine.
family of sting rays (fish)
medical

Examples

The family Dasyatidae includes a variety of sting ray species found in warm coastal waters.
Scientists have studied members of the Dasyatidae family to better understand their defense mechanisms.
Unlike other rays, most Dasyatidae can inflict a painful sting with their tails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stingray - common name for fishes of the family Dasyatidae

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on Greek dasys (hairy, thick) and Latin suffix -idae (indicating a family in zoological taxonomy).
