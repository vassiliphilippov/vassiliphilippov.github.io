nondescript

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.dɪˈskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.dɪˈskrɪpt / (BrE)
lacking distinctive or interesting features or characteristics; dull or unremarkable.
lacking distinguishing features; unremarkable

Examples

The building was so nondescript that I walked past it twice without noticing.
He wore a nondescript jacket that didn't stand out in the crowd.
Her voice was pleasant but rather nondescript, making it easy to forget.

Synset Examples

women dressed in nondescript clothes
a nondescript novel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unremarkable - not special or unusual in any way
  • Antonym: distinctive - having a quality that makes something clearly different and easy to recognize

Etymology

From Latin 'nondēscriptus', meaning 'not described'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɑn.dɪˈskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.dɪˈskrɪpt / (BrE)
a person who lacks distinctive or interesting features, making them difficult to classify or describe
unremarkable or indistinctive person

Examples

He was just another nondescript in the crowd, easily forgotten as soon as he walked by.
The group consisted of colorful personalities, but one nondescript went unnoticed by everyone.
Despite his intelligence, many dismissed him as a nondescript at the office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nondescript - lacking distinctive or interesting features or characteristics (adjective)

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'non-' (not) + 'descriptus' (described) from 'describere' (to describe).
