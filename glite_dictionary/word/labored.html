labored

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪbɚd / (AmE), / ˈleɪbəd / (BrE)
(of writing, speech, or movement) not natural or spontaneous; showing signs of effort and difficulty.
done with effort; lacking ease

Examples

Her speech sounded labored and unnatural, as if she had rehearsed it too much.
The novel's dialogue was so labored that it distracted from the story.
He delivered his lines in a labored manner, lacking the usual charm and spontaneity.

Synset Examples

a labored style of debating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkward - lacking grace or ease
  • Antonym: smooth - having a natural, easy flow

Etymology

From the past participle of 'labor', from Latin 'laborare' (to work).

2. ADJ

/ ˈleɪ.bɚd / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bəd / (BrE)
(of movement, speech, writing, or breathing) requiring a lot of effort and not smooth or natural; showing obvious strain or difficulty.
requiring or showing effort; not smooth or natural

Examples

His labored breathing worried the nurse after the surgery.
The professor's explanation sounded labored and difficult to follow.
She made a labored attempt to smile, but her exhaustion was obvious.

Synset Examples

heavy breathing
the subject made for labored reading

Related Terms

  • Antonym: effortless - not requiring effort; smooth and natural
  • Synonym: strained - showing signs of tension or effort

Etymology

Past participle of 'labor', from Latin 'laborare' (to work).
