duplicator

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːplɪˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈdjuːplɪkeɪtə / (BrE)
a machine that makes exact copies of typed, written, or drawn materials
machine for making copies

Examples

The office purchased a new duplicator to speed up the process of making training materials.
Before photocopiers became common, schools often relied on a duplicator to reproduce handouts.
She carefully fed the original drawing into the duplicator, expecting perfect copies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copier - a machine that makes copies of documents or images
  • Synonym: printer - a machine for printing text or pictures on paper

Etymology

From Latin 'duplicare' (to double) + '-tor' (agent suffix).
