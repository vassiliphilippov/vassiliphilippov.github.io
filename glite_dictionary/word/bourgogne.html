Bourgogne

1. PROPN

/ buʁˈɡɔɲ / (AmE), / buːrˈɡɒn / (BrE)
a historical region in eastern France, noted for its wine production
historical French region known for wines

Examples

Many collectors travel to Bourgogne to sample and purchase rare vintages.
The rolling hills of Bourgogne are covered in vineyards that produce world-class wines.
He studied traditional winemaking techniques in Bourgogne before opening his own winery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Burgundy - English name for the region and name for its wines

Etymology

French, from Latin Burgundia (land of the Burgundians).
