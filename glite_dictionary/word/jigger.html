jigger

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪɡə / (BrE)
a small mast on a sailing vessel, especially the mizzenmast of a yawl
small mast on a sailing vessel

Examples

The old yacht had a jigger that was taller than usual for its size.
A fresh coat of paint made the jigger stand out at the stern.
He adjusted the sails on the jigger before heading into open water.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪɡə / (BrE)
A larval mite that feeds on the blood of vertebrates, including humans, often causing intense irritation.
blood-sucking larval mite
medical

Examples

The presence of jiggers in the region has led to many cases of skin irritation.
Doctors examined the patient and found several jiggers embedded in the skin.
The intense itching was traced back to a jigger infestation.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪɡə / (BrE)
a small measuring cup or glass, typically holding 1 to 1.5 fluid ounces, used for serving or measuring alcoholic drinks such as whiskey
small measure for alcohol, esp. whiskey

Examples

He poured a jigger of whiskey into the glass before serving.
The bartender used a jigger to measure out the gin accurately.
Many cocktail recipes call for a jigger of rum as a standard portion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shot glass - a small glass for measuring or serving spirits

Etymology

Possibly from an alteration of 'chigger', or of obscure origin, dating to early 19th century barroom slang.
