Rhibhus

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪbˌhuːz / (AmE), / ˈrɪbˌhjuːz / (BrE)
In Hindu mythology, one of a group of three divine artisans known for their skill and inventiveness, who serve the gods.
divine artisans in Hindu mythology

Examples

The Rhibhus were praised in Vedic hymns for their extraordinary craftsmanship.
Stories of the Rhibhus often describe their ability to create marvellous objects for the gods.
In Hindu mythology, the Rhibhus are credited with inventing rituals and improving daily life through their skills.

Etymology

From Sanskrit ऋभु (ṛbhu), meaning 'skillful, clever'.
