commandment

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmændmənt / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːndmənt / (BrE)
a rule or principle, especially one of religious or moral authority, that is taught as a doctrine
religious or moral rule or doctrine

Examples

The Ten Commandments are central to Jewish and Christian ethics.
Each commandment lays out a vital moral principle for believers to follow.
He treated his teacher's instructions as if they were a commandment.

Synset Examples

the teachings of religion
he believed all the Christian precepts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precept - a general rule intended to regulate behavior or thought
  • Antonym: prohibition - a law or rule that forbids something

Etymology

From Old French commandement, from Latin *commandare* (to command).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmændmənt / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːndmənt / (BrE)
an important instruction or order, especially one given by an authority or regarded as coming from a divine source
an important rule or order, often religious

Examples

He tried to obey every commandment laid down by his faith.
The laws acted as a moral commandment for the entire community.
She treated her parents' words as if they were a sacred commandment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - an authoritative instruction or command

Etymology

From Old French *commandement*, from Latin *commendāmentum* ('order, command').

Synset POS: n
