justiciar

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈstɪʃiˌɑr / (AmE), / dʒʌˈstɪsɪə / (BrE)
formerly, a high judicial officer in England, typically serving as the king's chief minister of justice.
senior judicial officer (historical)
archaic

Examples

The justiciar played a central role in administering the king's laws throughout England.
During the monarch's absence, the justiciar acted as the kingdom's chief authority.
In medieval times, disputes of great importance were often settled by the justiciar.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin iustitiarius (judge), from Latin iustitia (justice).
