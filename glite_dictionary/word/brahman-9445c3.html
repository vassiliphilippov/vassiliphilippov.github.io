brahman

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːmən / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːmən / (BrE)
a member of a privileged social and cultural elite in the United States, especially a descendant of an old and established New England family
member of New England social elite

Examples

He was considered a true brahman in Boston society.
Several old families pride themselves on their brahman heritage.
Despite his wealth, he was never accepted as a brahman by the city's elite.

Synset Examples

a Boston brahman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristocrat - a member of a ruling class or of the nobility

Etymology

From the caste name 'Brahmin' in Hinduism; adapted in 19th-century America for Boston's elite.
