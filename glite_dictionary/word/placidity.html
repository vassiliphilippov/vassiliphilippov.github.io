placidity

1. NOUN

/ pləˈsɪdəti / (AmE), / pləˈsɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being calm and peaceful; a state of undisturbed quietness or tranquillity
calmness; tranquility

Examples

She sat by the lake, appreciating the placidity of the early morning.
The placidity in his voice helped calm everyone in the room.
After the storm, a sense of placidity returned to the small village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calmness - the quality of being free from agitation or strong emotion
  • Synonym: serenity - the state of being serene, calm, and untroubled
  • Antonym: agitation - a state of nervousness or unrest

Etymology

From Latin placidus (calm, gentle) via French placidité.

2. NOUN

/ pləˈsɪd.ə.ti / (AmE), / pləˈsɪd.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being calm and undisturbed, free from stress or strong emotion
state of calmness or tranquility

Examples

Her placidity during the stressful meeting impressed everyone in the room.
The lake reflected a rare placidity under the morning light, undisturbed by wind or wildlife.
He admired her placidity in the face of constant challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calmness - the state of being calm or peaceful
  • Antonym: agitation - a state of anxiety or nervous excitement
  • Synonym: serenity - the state of being serene; calm and untroubled

Etymology

From Latin 'placidus' (calm, quiet) via French 'placidité'.
