kapuka

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpuːkə / (AmE), / kəˈpuːkə / (BrE)
A small, broadleaf evergreen tree native to New Zealand, often cultivated as an ornamental in warm regions.
New Zealand ornamental evergreen tree

Examples

The lush green leaves of the kapuka make it a popular choice for garden hedges.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of kapuka in different climates.
We planted a kapuka near the patio to provide year-round foliage.

Etymology

From Maori 'kapuka', referring to the Griselinia littoralis tree.
