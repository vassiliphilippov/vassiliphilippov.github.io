weeder

1. NOUN

/ ˈwidər / (AmE), / ˈwiːdə / (BrE)
a hand tool used for removing weeds from soil or lawns
gardening tool for removing weeds

Examples

She used a weeder to pull out the dandelions from the flower bed.
The new weeder has an ergonomic handle for easier gardening.
Every spring, I buy a new weeder to keep my vegetable patch tidy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoe - a garden tool with a long handle and flat blade used for weeding and breaking up soil

Etymology

From 'weed' (undesirable plant) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwidər / (AmE), / ˈwiːdə / (BrE)
a person, especially a farmhand, whose job is to remove weeds from fields, gardens, or other areas
person who removes weeds

Examples

The weeder spent hours clearing the vegetable patch of invasive plants.
She was hired as a weeder during the summer at the local farm.
Modern technology has made the traditional role of the weeder less common.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmhand - a worker employed to do agricultural work on a farm

Etymology

From 'weed' (an unwanted plant) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10792605-n
Synset POS: n
