incrustation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.krəˈsteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.krʌˈsteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of forming a crust or a hard outer layer on a surface
formation of a crust or outer layer

Examples

The incrustation of minerals on the cave walls created beautiful formations over centuries.
A thick incrustation built up on the inside of the old boiler due to hard water.
You can see the incrustation around the edges of the fountain where the water dries and leaves minerals behind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encrustation - the process of forming a crust or covering with a hard layer

Etymology

From Latin 'incrustatio', from 'incrustare' meaning 'to cover with a crust'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkrəˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnkrʌˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
a hard outer layer that forms or covers something, often as a result of deposition or accumulation
hard layer covering something

Examples

The pipes were clogged with a thick incrustation of mineral deposits.
A pale incrustation had formed on the surface of the rock over time.
Years of neglect left the walls with an incrustation of grime and mildew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encrustation - the process or result of forming a crust or hard coating
  • Synonym: coating - a covering layer applied to the surface of something

Etymology

From Latin 'incrustatio', from 'incrustare' (to cover with a crust).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.krəˈsteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.krʌsˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a decorative layer or coating of contrasting material applied to a surface as an inlay or overlay
decorative coating or inlay

Examples

The marble table featured an intricate incrustation of mother-of-pearl along its edges.
Experts spent weeks restoring the ancient vase to reveal the original gold incrustation.
Decorative incrustation can add value and beauty to otherwise plain objects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inlay - ornamental material embedded in a surface

Etymology

From Latin 'incrustatio', from 'incrustare' (to cover with a crust or coating).
