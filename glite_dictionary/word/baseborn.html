baseborn

1. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪsˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsbɔːn / (BrE)
Of low or illegitimate birth; not born to parents of noble rank or within lawful marriage.
of low or illegitimate birth
archaic

Examples

In the old kingdom, those considered baseborn could not inherit titles or land.
He was scorned by society for being baseborn, despite his many virtues.
Many stories feature a baseborn hero who rises above humble origins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegitimate - born to parents not married to each other
  • Antonym: noble - of high birth or aristocratic lineage

Etymology

From 'base' (low, of humble origin) + 'born', first recorded in Middle English.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪsˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsbɔːn / (BrE)
Of low birth or humble social status; not born into nobility or high rank. Now chiefly archaic or literary.
of low or humble birth; archaic
archaic

Examples

The cruel lord dismissed the baseborn servants without a second thought.
She was proud despite her baseborn heritage and determined to make a better life.
Many baseborn children worked in the fields from a young age.

Synset Examples

baseborn wretches with dirty faces
of humble (or lowly) birth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lowborn - of low birth or humble origin

Etymology

From 'base' (low, of inferior rank) + 'born'.
