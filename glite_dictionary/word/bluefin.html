bluefin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌfɪn / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌfɪn / (BrE)
a large species of tuna, found in mainly temperate seas, known for its size and commercial value
a large tuna species

Examples

The chef prepared sushi using fresh bluefin caught off the coast of Japan.
Overfishing has drastically reduced the bluefin population in the Atlantic Ocean.
A single bluefin can weigh more than 600 kilograms at full maturity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluefin tuna - the full term for the species, a large tuna with blue coloring

2. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌfɪn / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌfɪn / (BrE)
The flesh of a very large species of tuna, prized as food.
flesh of a large tuna used as food

Examples

The sushi chef recommended the bluefin for its exceptional quality and flavor.
Fresh bluefin is highly sought after in upscale restaurants around the world.
He prepared a delicate tartare using thin slices of bluefin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuna - flesh of tuna fish used as food
