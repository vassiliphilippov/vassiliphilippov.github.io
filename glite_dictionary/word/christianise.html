to Christianise

1. VERB

/ ˈkrɪstʃənaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstʃənaɪz / (BrE)
to convert someone or something to Christianity; to make Christian in character or influence
convert to Christianity

Examples

The missionaries sought to Christianise the local population through education and outreach.
Many ancient festivals were Christianised over the centuries, blending old traditions with new beliefs.
Colonial authorities often tried to Christianise indigenous customs and practices.

Synset Examples

missionaries have tried to Christianize native people all over the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to Christianize - alternative American English spelling of Christianise

Etymology

From Christian + -ise, modeled after Late Latin christianizare or French christianiser.
