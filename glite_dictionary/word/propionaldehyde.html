propionaldehyde

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌpaɪ.əˈnæl.dəˌhaɪd / (AmE), / prəʊˌpaɪ.əlˈdældəˌhaɪd / (BrE)
A colorless, flammable liquid aldehyde used chiefly as a chemical intermediate in organic synthesis.
a colorless liquid aldehyde
medical

Examples

Propionaldehyde is commonly used in the manufacturing of plastics and pharmaceuticals.
Exposure to large quantities of propionaldehyde can irritate the eyes and respiratory system.
Scientists combined propionaldehyde with other chemicals to create new organic compounds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: aldehyde - any of a class of organic compounds containing the group -CHO

Etymology

From 'propion-' (from Greek 'pro' meaning before and 'pion' meaning fat) + 'aldehyde' (from alcohol dehydrogenatus).
