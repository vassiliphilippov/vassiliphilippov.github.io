enduringly

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈdʊrɪŋli / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjʊə.rɪŋ.li / (BrE)
In a way that continues to exist for a long time or is lasting.
in a lasting or permanent manner

Examples

Her art remains enduringly influential long after her death.
The principles of justice must be enduringly upheld in society.
This partnership is enduringly strong, despite many challenges.

Synset Examples

Roman culture was enduringly fertilized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permanently - in a permanent manner
  • Antonym: temporarily - for a limited period of time

Etymology

Formed from 'enduring' (from Latin *indurare*, to harden) + '-ly'.
