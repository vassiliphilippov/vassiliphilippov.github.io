smock

1. NOUN

/ smɑk / (AmE), / smɒk / (BrE)
A loose outer garment, often reaching to the ankles, worn to protect clothes while working or for ceremonial purposes.
loose outer garment covering the body

Examples

The painter wore a smock to keep his clothes clean while working on the mural.
Children in the art class donned blue smocks before starting their projects.
She slipped on her grandmother's old smock, which still smelled faintly of lavender.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overall - a loose-fitting protective outer garment

Etymology

From Old English 'smoc' (a garment), related to Old High German 'smocco'.

2. VERB

/ smɑk / (AmE), / smɒk / (BrE)
to decorate fabric by sewing patterns of intersecting diagonal lines, creating gathered effects; to apply smocking to (a garment or fabric)
to decorate with smocking (sewing technique)

Examples

She decided to smock the sleeves of her blouse for a vintage look.
Traditionally, mothers would smock dresses for their daughters using colorful thread.
The artisan skillfully smocked the waistline to create a gathered pattern.

Synset Examples

The folk dancers wore smocked shirts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embroider - decorate with needlework
  • Antonym: plain - not decorated or ornamented
