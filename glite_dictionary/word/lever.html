lever

1. VERB

/ ˈliːvər / (AmE), / ˈliːvə / (BrE)
to move or force something, especially to open it, using a lever or similar tool
move or force using leverage

Examples

She managed to lever the stuck window open with a screwdriver.
The mechanic levered the old tire off the rim with great effort.
If you can't lift the lid, try levering it up from the side.

Synset Examples

The burglar jimmied the lock
Raccoons managed to pry the lid off the garbage pail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pry - to use force to try to open or move something
  • Synonym: jimmy - to force open using a lever or similar tool

Etymology

From Old French 'levier', from Latin 'levare' (to raise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01596175-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈliːvər, ˈlɛvər / (AmE), / ˈliːvə, ˈlɛvə / (BrE)
A flat metal tumbler inside a lever lock, which must be lifted to a certain height by the key to allow the lock to open.
metal tumbler in a lever lock

Examples

The safe's security depended on the arrangement of each lever inside the lock.
A locksmith explained how a broken lever could prevent the mechanism from turning.
Each lever must be raised to the correct height for the lock to open.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumbler - a pivoted metal bar in a lock that must be raised by a key

Etymology

From Old French levier, from lever ‘to lift’, from Latin levare.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03665012-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɛvər / (AmE), / ˈliːvə, ˈlɛvə / (BrE)
A rigid bar that is used to move or lift an object by pivoting on a fixed point (fulcrum).
rigid bar used to lift or move objects

Examples

The engineer used a lever to lift the heavy stone into place.
A simple lever can multiply the force you apply, making it easier to move objects.
By placing the rock under the lever, she was able to shift it with minimal effort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar - a long rigid piece used as a lever, support, etc.
  • Synonym: fulcrum - the support or point on which a lever pivots

Etymology

From Old French 'levier', from lever (to lift), from Latin 'levare' (to raise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03664618-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈliːvər, ˈlɛvər / (AmE), / ˈliːvə, ˈlɛvə / (BrE)
A rigid bar that pivots around a fulcrum to transmit force and gain mechanical advantage, used to lift or move objects.
rigid bar used for mechanical advantage

Examples

Archimedes famously said that with a long enough lever and a fulcrum, he could move the world.
The child used a lever to lift the heavy stone off the ground.
In physics class, we learned how a lever can multiply applied force.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fulcrum - the support on which a lever pivots
  • Synonym: pulley - a wheel on an axle used to change the direction of force

Etymology

From Old French 'levier', from lever (to lift), from Latin 'levare' (to raise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03665135-n
Synset POS: n
