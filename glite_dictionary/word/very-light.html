Very-light

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛri laɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɛəri laɪt / (BrE)
A colored flare fired from a Very pistol, used especially for signaling.
colored flare fired from a Very pistol

Examples

During the night operation, the soldiers fired a Very-light to signal their position.
The boat sent up a Very-light as a distress signal to attract attention.
He watched as a red Very-light illuminated the dark sky over the camp.

Etymology

Named after Edward Very, the American naval officer who invented the Very pistol.
