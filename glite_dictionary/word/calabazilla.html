calabazilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.ə.bəˈzɪl.ə / (AmE), / ˌkæləbəˈzɪlə / (BrE)
A perennial vine native to dry regions of the central and southwestern United States and Mexico, known for its small, hard, mottled green, inedible fruits.
vine with small hard inedible fruits

Examples

The arid landscape was dotted with calabazilla vines winding across the dry ground.
Though the fruit of the calabazilla looks like a small watermelon, it is not edible.
Researchers studied the drought resistance of calabazilla as a way to improve crop resilience.

Etymology

From Spanish 'calabacilla', diminutive of 'calabaza' (gourd or pumpkin).
