debauch

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈbɔtʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈbɔːtʃ / (BrE)
a wild social gathering characterized by excessive drinking and promiscuous behavior
wild party; orgy

Examples

The politician's resignation followed rumors of a lavish debauch at his private estate.
They spent the weekend in a nonstop debauch of drinking and dancing.
The novel described a decadent debauch at an aristocrat’s mansion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orgy - wild party with excessive indulgence

Etymology

From Old French *desbaucher* (to seduce from duty), from *des-* (expressing reversal) + *bauche* (beam, figuratively support).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈbɔtʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈbɔːtʃ / (BrE)
to corrupt someone morally or lead them into excessive indulgence in sensual pleasures
corrupt morally or by sensual pleasure

Examples

His wealth allowed him to debauch himself with luxury and excess.
Critics claimed the film would debauch the morals of its viewers.
The conquerors tried to debauch the population with promises of easy pleasure.

Synset Examples

debauch the young people with wine and women
Socrates was accused of corrupting young men
Do school counselors subvert young children?
corrupt the morals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrupt - to make morally depraved; pervert
  • Synonym: to subvert - to undermine the morals or principles of someone

Etymology

From Middle French *débaucher* (to entice away from work, to seduce).
