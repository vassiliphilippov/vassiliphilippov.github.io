corticoefferent

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɔrtɪkoʊˈɛfərənt / (AmE), / ˌkɔːtɪkəʊˈɛfərənt / (BrE)
(of a nerve fiber) carrying signals away from the cerebral cortex to other parts of the brain or spinal cord.
carrying signals away from cerebral cortex
medical

Examples

The corticoefferent fibers transmit motor commands from the cortex to the spinal cord.
Damage to corticoefferent pathways can result in impaired muscle control.
Researchers studied the activation of corticoefferent tracts in patients with neurological disorders.

Synset Examples

corticofugal discharges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corticofugal - carrying impulses away from the cerebral cortex
  • Antonym: corticoafferent - carrying signals toward the cerebral cortex

Etymology

From Latin 'cortex' (bark, outer layer) + 'efferent' (carrying away).
