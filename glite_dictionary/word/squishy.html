squishy

1. ADJ

/ ˈskwɪʃi / (AmE), / ˈskwɪʃi / (BrE)
soft, moist, and easy to squeeze or compress, often in a way that is slightly spongy or yielding
soft and easy to squeeze

Examples

The squishy toy made a funny sound whenever you squeezed it.
She stepped on the squishy mud and her shoes got soaked.
The bread has gone squishy after sitting in the humid kitchen.

Synset Examples

spongy bread

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spongy - soft and full of holes, like a sponge; yielding easily to pressure
  • Antonym: firm - solid and not easily compressed or yielding to pressure
