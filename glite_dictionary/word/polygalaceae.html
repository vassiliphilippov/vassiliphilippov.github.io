Polygalaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˌɡæləˈsiːiː / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˌɡaləˈsiːɪ / (BrE)
a family of trees, shrubs, and herbs widely distributed across both hemispheres, known as the milkwort family
a family of trees, shrubs, and herbs (milkwort family)

Examples

The Polygalaceae family includes species that thrive in both temperate and tropical regions.
Researchers studied the soil preferences of several Polygalaceae members found in South America.
Many Polygalaceae are valued for their ornamental flowers and medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkwort - a plant of the genus Polygala, typical of the Polygalaceae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus *Polygala* and the suffix *-aceae* (family).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːlɪˌɡæləˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˌɡaləˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, including trees, shrubs, and herbs, widely distributed across both hemispheres
family of flowering plants (milkwort family)

Examples

The Polygalaceae family includes more than a thousand species of flowering plants.
Some members of the Polygalaceae are cultivated for their ornamental flowers.
Research shows several medicinal uses within the Polygalaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkwort - any plant of the genus Polygala, the type genus of Polygalaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Polygala (milkwort) and the standard botanical suffix -aceae for plant families.
