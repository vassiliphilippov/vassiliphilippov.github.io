birchbark

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːtʃˌbɑːrk / (AmE), / ˈbɜːtʃˌbɑːk / (BrE)
a canoe made by stretching and fastening the bark of a birch tree over a wooden frame, traditionally used by Indigenous peoples of North America
canoe made from birch tree bark

Examples

The museum displays a traditional birchbark crafted by Indigenous artisans.
He paddled quietly across the lake in his birchbark, admiring the morning mist.
Building a birchbark requires great skill and knowledge of natural materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canoe - a light, narrow boat pointed at both ends and moved with a paddle
