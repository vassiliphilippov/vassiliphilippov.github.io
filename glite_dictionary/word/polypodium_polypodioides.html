Polypodium polypodioides

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑː.lɪˈpoʊ.di.əm ˌpɑː.lɪˌpoʊ.diˈoʊ.diz / (AmE), / ˌpɒ.lɪˈpəʊ.di.əm ˌpɒ.lɪˌpəʊ.dɪˈiː.dɪz / (BrE)
A fern species found in the Americas and South Africa, growing on rocks or tree trunks, with fronds that are greyish and scurfy underneath.
a species of fern with greyish, scurfy fronds

Examples

The Polypodium polypodioides fern is notable for its ability to survive long dry periods.
Gardeners often admire Polypodium polypodioides for its resilience and unique foliage.
In its natural habitat, Polypodium polypodioides grows directly on tree trunks and rocks.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining genus Polypodium with species name from Latinized Greek, meaning 'many-footed', referencing the root system.
