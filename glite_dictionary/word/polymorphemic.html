polymorphemic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑliˌmɔrˈfimɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˌmɔːˈfiːmɪk / (BrE)
(of a word or form) consisting of two or more morphemes.
having two or more morphemes

Examples

The word polymorphemic itself is made up of several morphemes.
In linguistic analysis, a polymorphemic word can be broken down into smaller meaningful parts.
Languages with complex polymorphemic words often have rich derivational morphology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monomorphemic - consisting of only one morpheme

Etymology

From poly- (many) + morpheme + -ic, combining Greek roots.
