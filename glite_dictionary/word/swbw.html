SWbW

1. NOUN

/ ɛs dʌbəlˈjuː biː ˈdʌbəlˈjuː / (AmE), / ɛs dʌbəlˈjuː biː ˈdʌbəlˈjuː / (BrE)
The compass point that is one point west of southwest, or at an angle of 236° 15′ clockwise from due north.
one point west of southwest (compass direction)

Examples

The ship adjusted its course to SWbW to avoid the storm.
At SWbW, the wind was strongest during the early morning hours.
He marked the direction as SWbW on the navigation chart.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: southwest - the compass direction halfway between south and west
  • Degree Higher: west-southwest - compass point two points west of southwest

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Southwest by West', following nautical compass conventions.
