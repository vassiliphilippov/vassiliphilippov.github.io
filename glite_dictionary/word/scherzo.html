scherzo

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛr.tsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈskɛə.tsoʊ / (BrE)
a lively and fast-paced movement in a piece of music, typically in triple time, often forming part of a symphony or sonata
musical movement; lively, in triple time

Examples

The second movement is a scherzo that stands out for its dynamic contrasts and playful rhythms.
Beethoven often used a scherzo instead of a minuet in his later symphonies.
The pianist played the scherzo with remarkable energy and precision.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: minuet - a slow, stately ballroom dance and musical form in triple time

Etymology

Italian, literally 'joke, jest', from 'scherzare' (to joke).
