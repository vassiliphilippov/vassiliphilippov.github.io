hypnoanalysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpnoʊəˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɪpnəʊəˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
the use of hypnosis as an aid to psychoanalysis, especially in uncovering repressed memories or treating psychological conditions
use of hypnosis in psychoanalysis

Examples

The therapist recommended hypnoanalysis to help the patient access forgotten memories.
Some psychologists use hypnoanalysis as part of their therapeutic process for trauma treatment.
Recent studies question whether hypnoanalysis is more effective than traditional psychoanalysis alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychoanalysis - a method of treating mental illness by exploring unconscious thoughts and feelings

Etymology

Formed from 'hypnosis' + 'analysis', referring to analytic psychology.
