siglum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡləm / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡləm / (BrE)
a sign, abbreviation, letter, or character used to represent words or phrases, especially in ancient manuscripts, inscriptions, coins, or medals.
abbreviation or symbol in manuscripts or coins

Examples

In medieval texts, a siglum was often used instead of writing common words in full.
Scholars must interpret the different sigla found on ancient Roman coins to understand their meaning.
The archivist explained the purpose of each siglum used by the scribes.

Synset Examples

One practice was rendering an over-used, formulaic phrase only as a siglum, e.g. ‘RIP’ for ‘requiescat in pace’ (‘Rest in Peace’), because the long-form written usage of the abbreviated phrase, itself, was rare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abbreviation - a shortened form of a word or phrase

Etymology

From Latin 'siglum' (abbreviation, sign).
