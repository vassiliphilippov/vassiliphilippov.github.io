infernally

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈfɜrnəli / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːnəli / (BrE)
In a manner resembling hell or the devil; extremely or diabolically (often used to emphasize negative qualities).
extremely; in a diabolical manner

Examples

The weather was infernally hot that afternoon, making everyone irritable.
She found his infernally persistent questions exhausting during the interview.
The old machine was infernally difficult to repair, frustrating even the most skilled engineers.

Synset Examples

infernally clever
hellishly dangerous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hellishly - in an extremely unpleasant or diabolic manner
  • Synonym: devilishly - in a wicked or diabolical manner

Etymology

From Latin infernalis (of the lower regions; hellish), from infernus (hell).
