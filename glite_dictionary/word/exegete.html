exegete

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛksɪˌdʒit / (AmE), / ˈɛksɪˌdʒiːt / (BrE)
A person skilled in interpreting and explaining texts, especially religious scriptures.
person skilled in interpretation of texts

Examples

The renowned exegete delivered a lecture on interpreting ancient biblical passages.
As an exegete, she devoted years to studying the Hebrew scriptures in their original language.
Many scholars regard him as the leading exegete of St. Paul's letters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exegesis - the critical explanation or interpretation of a text

Etymology

From Greek exēgētēs (interpreter), from exēgeisthai (to interpret).
