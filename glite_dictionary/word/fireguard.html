fireguard

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌɡɑːd / (BrE)
A metal screen placed in front of an open fire to protect the room from sparks or embers.
metal screen before a fire for protection

Examples

Please make sure the fireguard is in place before lighting the fire.
The antique fireguard added both safety and elegance to the room.
She bought a sturdy fireguard to protect the children from the fireplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firescreen - a screen placed in front of a fireplace to shield the room from sparks or heat

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌɡɑːd / (BrE)
A strip of land cleared of vegetation to prevent the spread of a prairie or forest fire.
strip of land cleared to prevent fire spread

Examples

The rangers built a fireguard to protect the forest from the approaching flames.
A well-maintained fireguard can make a critical difference during wildfire season.
Crews cleared a fireguard around the village as a precaution against bushfires.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firebreak - A barrier of cleared land to stop fire spread

Synset POS: n
