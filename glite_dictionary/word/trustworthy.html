trustworthy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrʌstˌwɝːði / (AmE), / ˈtrʌstˌwɜːði / (BrE)
reliable and deserving of trust; able to be depended on as honest or truthful.
able to be relied on or trusted

Examples

Everyone found the accountant to be trustworthy and reliable.
Make sure any sources you consult are trustworthy before you use them in your research.
She chose a trustworthy friend to look after her cat while she was away.

Synset Examples

a trustworthy report
an experienced and trustworthy traveling companion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reliable - consistently good in quality or performance; able to be trusted
  • Synonym: dependable - trustworthy and reliable
  • Antonym: untrustworthy - not able to be relied on as honest or truthful

Etymology

From 'trust' + 'worthy', meaning worthy of trust, first recorded in the 16th century.
