Zoomastigina

1. NOUN

/ zoʊˌmæstəˈdʒaɪnə / (AmE), / zəʊˌmæstəˈdʒaɪnə / (BrE)
A former phylum of the kingdom Protista, consisting of single-celled, holozoic or saprozoic flagellates; now largely considered obsolete in modern taxonomy.
group of flagellated protists (obsolete classification)

Examples

Zoomastigina were once classified as a separate phylum in older biology textbooks.
The organisms in Zoomastigina move using one or more flagella.
Modern classification has replaced Zoomastigina with more precise protozoan groupings.

Etymology

From Greek 'zōon' (animal) + 'mastix' (whip), referring to their whip-like flagella.

2. NOUN

/ zuːˌmæstəˈdʒaɪnə / (AmE), / zuːˌmæstɪˈdʒaɪnə / (BrE)
A former taxonomic group of protozoans, consisting of animal-like flagellate protists, once considered a phylum in some classifications.
group of animal-like flagellate protists

Examples

Scientists used to classify certain flagellated protozoans in the group Zoomastigina.
The Zoomastigina are known for their distinct whip-like flagella used for movement.
Textbooks may still refer to Zoomastigina in discussions of protozoan taxonomy.

Etymology

From Greek 'zōon' (animal) + 'mastix' (whip) + '-ina' (suffix of groups).
