helmetflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlmɪtˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈhɛlmɪtˌflaʊə / (BrE)
any of several orchids of the genus Coryanthes that bear racemes of a few musky-scented, waxy flowers with a helmet-shaped lip.
a musky-scented orchid of genus Coryanthes

Examples

The rare helmetflower thrives in humid greenhouses where its striking blooms can be carefully observed.
Botanists are fascinated by the pollination methods of the helmetflower and its unique helmet-shaped lip.
She added a vibrant helmetflower to her orchid collection, admiring its distinct musky fragrance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlmətˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈhɛlmɪtˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a herbaceous plant of the genus Scutellaria, characterized by a calyx that, when inverted, resembles a helmet with its visor raised
plant of the genus Scutellaria

Examples

The garden contained several species of helmetflower thriving in the partial shade.
Botanists admire the unique shape of the helmetflower and often study its calyx structure.
She pressed a dried helmetflower into her journal during her woodland hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skullcap - another name for plants of the genus Scutellaria

Etymology

From 'helmet' + 'flower', describing the helmet-like shape of its calyx.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlmɪtˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈhɛlmɪtˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a poisonous plant native to northern Europe, with hooded blue-purple flowers; its dried leaves and roots yield aconite.
poisonous European plant with hooded flowers

Examples

Many gardeners avoid planting helmetflower because of its highly toxic properties.
The vibrant blue hooded petals make helmetflower easy to recognize during walks in the countryside.
Caution is required when handling helmetflower, as all parts of the plant contain dangerous alkaloids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aconite - a poisonous plant from which aconitine is derived

Etymology

Formed from 'helmet' and 'flower', referring to the hooded shape of the bloom.
