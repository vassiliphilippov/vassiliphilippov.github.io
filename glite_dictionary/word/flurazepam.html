flurazepam

1. NOUN

/ flʊˈræzɪˌpæm / (AmE), / flʊˈræzɪˌpam / (BrE)
a benzodiazepine tranquilizer used to treat insomnia, commonly sold under the trade name Dalmane
a drug used to treat insomnia
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed flurazepam to help him manage his chronic insomnia.
Because of potential side effects, flurazepam should only be used under close medical supervision.
Some patients prefer flurazepam to other sleeping pills due to its long-lasting effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diazepam - a benzodiazepine drug used to treat anxiety and other conditions

Etymology

From chemical components: fluor(o)-, aza- (nitrogen), and -epam (as in diazepam).
