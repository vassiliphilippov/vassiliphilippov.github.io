Holocanthus tricolor

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊloʊˈkænθəs ˈtraɪkələr / (AmE), / ˌhɒləʊˈkænθəs ˈtraɪkələ / (BrE)
a species of angelfish with bright yellow and black coloring, found in the western Atlantic from the West Indies to Brazil, also known as the rock beauty
a yellow and black angelfish species

Examples

We spotted a Holocanthus tricolor swimming among the coral reefs during our dive.
The aquarium features a vibrant Holocanthus tricolor in its Atlantic marine exhibit.
Marine biologists study the coloration patterns of Holocanthus tricolor to understand its role in reef ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rock beauty - common name for Holocanthus tricolor, a species of angelfish

Etymology

From New Latin: Holocanthus (genus, from Greek holos 'whole' + akantha 'thorn, spine') tricolor (Latin 'three-colored').
