tinplate

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪnˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɪnˌpleɪt / (BrE)
A thin sheet of iron or steel coated with tin to prevent rusting, used especially for making cans, pots, and tin containers.
thin sheet of metal coated with tin

Examples

The factory produces thousands of tinplate cans each day for the food industry.
Old toys were often made from brightly painted tinplate.
They chose tinplate sheets to manufacture the decorative containers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tin - a soft, silvery-white metal, chemical element symbol Sn

Etymology

From 'tin' + 'plate', referring to metal plate coated with tin.
