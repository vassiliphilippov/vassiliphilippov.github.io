fruticose

1. ADJ

/ ˈfruː.t̬ɪˌkoʊs / (AmE), / ˈfruː.tɪ.kəʊs / (BrE)
(of a plant or lichen) having or resembling a shrub-like form, usually with a branching structure.
having a shrub-like or bushy form

Examples

The fruticose lichens covered the branches in delicate, bushy clusters.
Certain desert plants exhibit a distinctly fruticose growth habit, spreading out in dense, woody clumps.
Scientists identified the species by its fruticose structure and finely divided branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrubby - resembling or full of shrubs

Etymology

From Latin fruticosus (full of shrubs, bushy), from frutex (shrub).
