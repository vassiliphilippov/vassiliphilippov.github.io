coachwhip

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊtʃˌwɪp / (AmE), / ˈkəʊtʃˌwɪp / (BrE)
A long, slender whipsnake native to the southern United States and Mexico, with a tail resembling a braided whip.
a slender south US/Mexico whipsnake

Examples

The coachwhip can move quickly across the desert to escape predators.
With its distinctive, braided-looking tail, the coachwhip is easily recognized by herpetologists.
We spotted a coachwhip basking in the sun near the rocky outcrop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whipsnake - a type of fast, slender snake

Etymology

Named for its tail, which resembles the braided whip used by carriage drivers ('coachmen').

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊtʃˌwɪp / (AmE), / ˈkəʊtʃˌwɪp / (BrE)
a desert shrub of the southwestern United States and Mexico, characterized by slender, leafless spiny branches that develop foliage and clusters of red flowers after the rainy season
desert shrub with spiny branches and red flowers

Examples

The coachwhip blooms brilliantly after the first heavy desert rains.
We spotted a coachwhip growing near the rocky hills outside Tucson.
Many animals use the dense branches of the coachwhip as shelter in the harsh desert climate.
