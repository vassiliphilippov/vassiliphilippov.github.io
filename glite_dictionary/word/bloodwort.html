bloodwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌwɜːt / (BrE)
any of various plants of the family Haemodoraceae, especially those with roots containing a deep red coloring matter
a plant with red roots, family Haemodoraceae

Examples

The bloodwort produces a striking red dye that has been used for centuries.
Gardeners value bloodwort for its unique root color and attractive flowers.
Some species of bloodwort can be found in wet, marshy areas in Australia.

Etymology

Formed from 'blood' (referring to root color) + 'wort' (Old English for plant or herb).
