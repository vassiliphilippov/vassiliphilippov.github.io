Methodism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθ.əˌdɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈmɛθ.əˌdɪz.əm / (BrE)
the religious beliefs, practices, and organization of the Methodist Church, emphasizing personal faith, social welfare, and public morals
beliefs and practices of the Methodist Church

Examples

His upbringing in Methodism shaped his commitment to social justice and charity.
Many communities were transformed by the rise of Methodism during the nineteenth century.
The principles of Methodism encourage members to care for both their spiritual and physical well-being.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christianity - the religion based on the person and teachings of Jesus Christ

Etymology

From 'Methodist', the name given to a group of 18th-century reformers led by John Wesley, who were noted for their methodical approach to religious practice and self-discipline.
