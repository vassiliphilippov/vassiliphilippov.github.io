rose-root

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊz ˌrut / (AmE), / ˈrəʊz ˌruːt / (BrE)
A Eurasian mountain plant (Rhodiola rosea) with fleshy, pink-tipped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers, sometimes used in herbal medicine.
a Eurasian mountain plant with yellow flowers

Examples

Rose-root is prized in herbal medicine for its purported adaptogenic properties.
The gardener planted several rose-roots along the rocky border for their attractive foliage.
High in the mountains, rose-root flourishes among the stones and wild grasses.
