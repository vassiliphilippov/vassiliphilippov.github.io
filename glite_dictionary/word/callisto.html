Callisto

1. PROPN

/ kəˈlɪstoʊ / (AmE), / kəˈlɪstəʊ / (BrE)
the second largest moon of Jupiter and the third largest moon in the Solar System, known for its heavily cratered surface
large moon of Jupiter

Examples

The surface of Callisto is one of the most heavily cratered in the Solar System.
Astronomers continue to study Callisto to learn more about the history of the outer Solar System.
Galileo was the first to observe Callisto with a telescope in 1610.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Ganymede - largest moon of Jupiter
  • Degree Lower: Europa - one of Jupiter's Galilean moons, smaller than Callisto
  • Degree Lower: Io - one of Jupiter's Galilean moons, smaller than Callisto

Etymology

From Greek mythology: Callisto was a nymph, loved by Zeus, after whom the moon was named.
