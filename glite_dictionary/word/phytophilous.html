phytophilous

1. ADJ

/ ˌfaɪtoʊˈfɪləs / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪtəʊˈfɪləs / (BrE)
(of an animal) feeding on or attracted to plants.
animal feeding on plants

Examples

Many caterpillars are phytophilous, spending their larval stage feeding exclusively on leaves.
Scientists observed a variety of phytophilous insects in the newly restored grassland.
The phytophilous habits of certain beetles make them important in plant population control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herbivorous - (of animals) feeding on plants

Etymology

From Greek 'phyton' (plant) + 'philos' (loving, fond).
