submission

1. NOUN

/ səbˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / səbˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
(Law) an argument or statement presented by a lawyer to a judge or jury as part of a case.
legal argument or statement
legal

Examples

The defense counsel made a submission that the evidence should be excluded.
During the trial, her submission was that the contract was not enforceable.
The judge invited final submissions from both parties before reaching a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argument - a reason or set of reasons presented to support a case or opinion

2. NOUN

/ səbˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / səbˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of accepting the control or authority of someone or something else; the act of yielding to another's power or will
the act or state of yielding to authority

Examples

After months of negotiations, the company finally forced the workers into submission.
Her submission to his authority surprised everyone in the room.
He regarded submission to tradition as a sign of weakness.

Synset Examples

the union was brought into submission
his submission to the will of God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surrender - the act of giving up or yielding to someone else's power
  • Antonym: resistance - refusal to accept or comply with something

Etymology

From Latin submissio (a lowering, submitting), from submittere (to lower, submit).

an agreement by parties in a dispute to accept the decision made by an arbiter or arbitrator
agreement to abide by an arbiter's decision
legal

Examples

The two companies reached a submission and agreed to let an independent arbiter resolve the dispute.

A formal submission ensures that both sides abide by the outcome of the arbitration process.

Etymology

From Latin submissio (a lowering, submission), from submittere (to lower, yield).
