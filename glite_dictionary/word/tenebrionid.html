tenebrionid

1. NOUN

/ təˈnɛbriənɪd / (AmE), / tɛˈnɛbriənɪd / (BrE)
a member of a family of hard-bodied, usually dark-coloured beetles whose larvae feed on decaying plant matter or grain
a type of dark-bodied beetle

Examples

The larvae of the tenebrionid beetle are commonly known as mealworms.
Researchers studied several species of tenebrionid in the desert ecosystem.
A tenebrionid can often be found under rocks or decaying logs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darkling beetle - another name for a tenebrionid beetle

Etymology

From Latin 'tenebrio' (a kind of beetle) + '-id' (a suffix denoting family or group in zoological terms).
