maître d'hôtel

1. NOUN

/ ˌmeɪtɚ doʊˈtɛl / (AmE), / ˌmeɪtrə dəʊˈtɛl / (BrE)
A person in charge of the waiters and the seating of customers in a restaurant or dining room.
chief restaurant waiter; supervisor of seating and service

Examples

The maître d'hôtel greeted us at the door and showed us to our table.
If you have a reservation, please speak to the maître d'hôtel upon arrival.
The maître d'hôtel handled the busy evening with remarkable professionalism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headwaiter - person in charge of the waiters in a restaurant

Etymology

From French maître d'hôtel (master of the house).
