famulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæmjələs / (AmE), / ˈfamjʊləs / (BrE)
a close personal attendant, especially one assisting a scholar or magician
close attendant, esp. to a scholar

Examples

The scholar's famulus prepared his notes and organized his correspondence diligently.
As a young man, he worked as a famulus to a renowned alchemist.
The famulus was present at every lecture, always ready to assist with books and experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps someone in their work
  • Synonym: servant - a person working for another, especially in a domestic capacity

Etymology

From Latin famulus (servant, attendant).
