to fondle

1. VERB

/ ˈfɑndl / (AmE), / ˈfɒndl / (BrE)
to touch or stroke someone or something gently and lovingly, often in an affectionate or sexual way
touch or stroke lovingly

Examples

She gently fondled the kitten as it purred in her lap.
He fondled the old photograph, remembering his childhood days.
The couple fondled each other's hands under the table.

Synset Examples

He caressed her face
They fondled in the back seat of the taxi

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caress - to touch someone gently in a loving way
  • Synonym: pet - to stroke or gently touch an animal or person

Etymology

From Middle English 'fond' (foolish, fond) + diminutive suffix '-le'.
