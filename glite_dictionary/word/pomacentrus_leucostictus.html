Pomacentrus leucostictus

1. NOUN

/ poʊ.məˈsɛn.trəs ˌluː.kəˈstɪk.təs / (AmE), / pɒ.məˈsɛn.trəs ˌluː.kəˈstɪk.təs / (BrE)
A species of blue and yellow damselfish found in the waters off Bermuda, Florida, and the West Indies.
blue and yellow damselfish species

Examples

The Pomacentrus leucostictus is often observed swimming near coral reefs in the West Indies.
Marine biologists conducted a study on the habitat preferences of Pomacentrus leucostictus.
Snorkelers delight in spotting the vibrant Pomacentrus leucostictus along the coast of Bermuda.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus Pomacentrus + species name leucostictus, from Greek 'leukos' (white) and 'stiktos' (spotted).
