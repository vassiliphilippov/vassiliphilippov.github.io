Susan B. Anthony

1. PROPN

/ ˈsuːzən ˈbi ˈænθəni / (AmE), / ˈsuːzən ˈbiː ˈænθəni / (BrE)
(1820–1906) An American social reformer and women's rights activist who played a pivotal role in the women's suffrage movement.
American women's rights activist

Examples

Susan B. Anthony dedicated her life to fighting for women's right to vote.
The Susan B. Anthony dollar coin was first minted in 1979.
Many historians credit Susan B. Anthony as a key figure in the American suffrage movement.
