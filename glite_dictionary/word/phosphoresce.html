to phosphoresce

1. VERB

/ ˌfɑsfəˈrɛs / (AmE), / ˌfɒsfəˈrɛs / (BrE)
to emit light without heat as a result of phosphorescence, typically after exposure to radiation or light
emit light by phosphorescence

Examples

Some deep-sea creatures phosphoresce to attract prey or communicate in the darkness.
The painted stars on the ceiling phosphoresce softly after the lights are turned off.
Certain minerals phosphoresce under ultraviolet light in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fluoresce - to emit light while exposed to radiation, as by fluorescence

Etymology

From phosphor + -esce, patterned after 'fluoresce'.
