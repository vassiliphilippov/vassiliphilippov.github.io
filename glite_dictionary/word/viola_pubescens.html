Viola pubescens

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈoʊlə pjʊˈbɛsəns / (AmE), / vaɪˈəʊlə pjuːˈbɛsəns / (BrE)
A species of violet native to eastern North America, characterized by softly hairy leaves and stems, and clear yellow flowers with brown-purple veins.
a North American yellow violet species

Examples

The Viola pubescens blooms in the spring with striking yellow flowers.
Botanists documented Viola pubescens growing in shaded woodland areas.
Soft hairs on the leaves and stems are a distinctive feature of Viola pubescens.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Viola' (violet) and 'pubescens' (downy, hairy).
