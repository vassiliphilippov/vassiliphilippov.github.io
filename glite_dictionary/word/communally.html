communally

1. ADV

/ kəˈmjunəli / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnəli / (BrE)
by a group of people collectively rather than by an individual; in a communal manner.
collectively; as a community

Examples

The land is farmed communally by several families.
They made the decision communally after a long discussion.
Water resources are managed communally in the village.

Synset Examples

the mills were owned communally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: individually - by or for one person rather than a group

Etymology

From 'communal' (relating to a community) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
