to recombine

1. VERB

/ ˌriːkəmˈbaɪn / (AmE), / ˌriːkəmˈbaɪn / (BrE)
to combine or mix things together again, especially after they have been separated
to combine again

Examples

After separating the chemicals, the scientist decided to recombine them to observe any new reactions.
You should only recombine the puzzle pieces once all other options have been explored.
Genes can recombine during reproduction, resulting in genetic variation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combine - to join or mix two or more things together

Etymology

From re- (again) + combine (from Latin combinare, to join together).

2. VERB

/ ˌriː.kəmˈbaɪn / (AmE), / ˌriː.kəmˈbaɪn / (BrE)
to cause parts, especially genetic material, to combine again or to undergo genetic recombination
combine again, especially genetic material

Examples

Genetic engineers can recombine fragments of DNA to create new genetic sequences.
Scientists are developing methods to recombine genes to treat certain diseases.
It is possible to recombine viruses in the laboratory to study their properties.

Synset Examples

should scientists recombine DNA?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combine - to join or merge things together

Etymology

From re- (again) + combine, from Latin combinare (to unite).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00566935-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌriːkəmˈbaɪn / (AmE), / ˌriːkəmˈbaɪn / (BrE)
(of genetic material) to undergo the process of recombination, resulting in a different arrangement of genes or DNA sequences.
undergo genetic recombination

Examples

During meiosis, the chromosomes recombine to create new genetic variations.
DNA fragments can recombine in the laboratory, producing unique strands.
Viruses often recombine their genetic material to adapt to new environments.

Synset Examples

The DNA can recombine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recombine - to combine again
  • Antonym: to segregate - to separate genetic material during cell division

Etymology

From re- (again) + combine, meaning to combine again; in genetics from the process of recombination.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00567101-v
