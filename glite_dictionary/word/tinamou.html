tinamou

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪnəˌmu / (AmE), / ˈtɪnəməʊ / (BrE)
A heavy-bodied, small-winged South American game bird that resembles a gallinaceous bird but is related to ratites.
a South American game bird

Examples

The tinamou is known for its shy behavior and distinctive whistling calls.
During our trek, we caught a glimpse of a brown tinamou disappearing into the undergrowth.
Many conservationists are concerned about habitat loss affecting wild tinamous in South America.

Etymology

From French 'tinamou', from Tupi-Guarani 'inhambu' (a kind of bird).
