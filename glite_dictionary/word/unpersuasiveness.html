unpersuasiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnpərˈsweɪsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˌʌnpəˈsweɪsɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unable to convince or influence others.
lack of ability to persuade

Examples

The unpersuasiveness of his arguments became clear during the debate.
Critics noted the unpersuasiveness of the advertising campaign.
Her unpersuasiveness meant that few people supported her proposal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: persuasiveness - the quality of being persuasive

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'persuasive' + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).
