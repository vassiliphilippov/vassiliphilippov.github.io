spironolactone

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˌroʊnəˈlæktoʊn / (AmE), / spaɪˌrəʊnəˈlaktəʊn / (BrE)
a synthetic steroid drug used to treat conditions such as high blood pressure and fluid retention
synthetic drug for high blood pressure and fluid retention
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed spironolactone to help manage her high blood pressure.
Patients with heart failure may benefit from adding spironolactone to their treatment regimen.
Long-term use of spironolactone can require monitoring of kidney function and potassium levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aldactone - brand name for spironolactone

Etymology

From 'spirolactone', indicating a spiro (spiral) configuration and 'lactone', a type of organic compound.
