vena mesenterica

1. NOUN

/ ˌviː.nə ˌmɛs.ənˈtɛr.ɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˌveɪ.nə ˌmɪs.ɛnˈtɛr.ɪ.kə / (BrE)
Either of two veins that drain blood from the intestines and carry it to the portal vein, running between the layers of the mesentery.
vein draining blood from intestines to portal vein
medical

Examples

During surgery, the vena mesenterica was carefully isolated to control blood loss.
A blockage in the vena mesenterica can lead to severe intestinal complications.
The doctor explained the role of the vena mesenterica in returning blood from the digestive tract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesenteric vein - a vein in the mesentery; synonym for vena mesenterica

Etymology

From Latin, 'vena' meaning 'vein' and 'mesenterica' relating to the mesentery.
