Amaranthus albus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈrænθəs ˈælbəs / (AmE), / ˌaməˈranθəs ˈalbus / (BrE)
A bushy, annual plant native to the western United States, commonly known as white pigweed.
a bushy plant of western US; white pigweed

Examples

Amaranthus albus often grows in disturbed soils across the western United States.
Gardeners sometimes remove Amaranthus albus to control weeds in their fields.
The leaves of Amaranthus albus are sometimes used as a food source in certain regions.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Amaranthus' from Greek 'amarantos' (unfading) + Latin 'albus' (white).
