mintiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪn.ti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪn.ti.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of having the flavor or aroma characteristic of mint.
having the flavor or smell of mint

Examples

The mintiness of the dessert was refreshing on a hot summer day.
I prefer toothpaste with a strong mintiness to keep my mouth feeling clean.
Some people find the mintiness of certain teas too overpowering.

Synset Examples

Paul had come up behind me, and he leaned over to fiddle with the lens, and I could feel him then, his warmth, and I could smell the mintiness of his breath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minty - Having the flavor or aroma of mint; tasting or smelling of mint

Etymology

From 'mint' (the aromatic plant or its flavor) + '-iness' (forming nouns indicating a state or quality).
