singing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of producing musical sounds with the voice, usually to a tune or melody
the act of producing musical sounds with the voice

Examples

She has always loved singing in the school choir since she was a child.
His singing was so beautiful that the audience gave him a standing ovation.
We could hear the joyful singing of birds outside our window every morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: song - a short piece of music with words that are sung

Etymology

Present participle of 'sing', from Old English 'singan'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of disclosing information or giving evidence about another person, especially to the authorities
informing or giving evidence against someone

Examples

The police suspected him of singing to avoid prison time.
His friends accused him of singing after the investigation began.
After hours of questioning, she finally started singing about her accomplices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informing - the act of giving information to authorities about someone
  • Antonym: keeping quiet - the act of not giving information to authorities

Etymology

From the verb 'sing', extended figuratively to mean 'to inform', as in 'singing like a bird'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06818155-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a sound, tone, line, etc.) smooth, flowing, and melodious in character.
smooth and flowing (of sound or line)

Examples

The violin produced a singing tone that drew the audience's attention.
Her handwriting had a singing quality, flowing gracefully across the page.
He admired the singing lines of the sculpture's silhouette.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melodious - pleasant-sounding, like music
  • Antonym: harsh - sharp and unpleasant in sound
