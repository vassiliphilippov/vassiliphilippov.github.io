to unarm

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈɑrm / (AmE), / ʌnˈɑːm / (BrE)
to take weapons away from; to deprive of weapons or means of attack
remove weapons from

Examples

The guards were ordered to unarm the prisoners before allowing them into the courtyard.
It took several attempts to unarm the hostile individual without causing harm.
During the negotiation, she managed to unarm the situation with calm words rather than force.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disarm - to take weapons away from; to deprive of arms

Etymology

From un- (not) + arm (to equip with weapons); modeled after Latin 'disarmare'.
