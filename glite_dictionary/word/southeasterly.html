southeasterly

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaʊθˈistərli / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθˈiːstəli / (BrE)
(of wind or weather) coming from the southeast.
coming from the southeast (wind or weather)

Examples

The ship battled strong southeasterly winds throughout the night.
A gentle southeasterly breeze brought cooler air to the coast.
They set up camp facing away from the southeasterly gusts.

Synset Examples

southeasterly breezes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northeasterly - (of wind) coming from the northeast
  • Antonym: northwesterly - (of wind) coming from the northwest
  • Antonym: southwesterly - (of wind) coming from the southwest

2. ADJ

/ ˌsaʊθˈistərli / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθˈiːstəli / (BrE)
Situated in or oriented toward the southeast.
facing or toward the southeast

Examples

The southeasterly winds brought warm, damp air to the region.
Their house has a southeasterly view, which lets in the morning sun.
We camped on the southeasterly side of the mountain to avoid the coldest winds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northeasterly - situated in or oriented toward the northeast
  • Antonym: southwesterly - situated in or oriented toward the southwest
  • Synonym: southeast - the direction between south and east

Etymology

From 'southeast' + suffix '-erly', meaning 'in the direction of'.
