misology

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈsɑlədʒi / (AmE), / mɪˈsɒlədʒi / (BrE)
Hatred or distrust of reasoning or logical argument.
hatred of reasoning

Examples

Philosophers warn that misology can undermine rational debate and the pursuit of truth.
His misology made him dismiss any argument, no matter how logical.
A society afflicted by misology may become resistant to progress and constructive criticism.

Etymology

From Greek misología, from misos (hate) + -logia (study, discourse).
