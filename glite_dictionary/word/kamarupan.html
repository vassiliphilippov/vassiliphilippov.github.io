Kamarupan

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɑːruˌpɑːn / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːrəˌpæn / (BrE)
a Tibeto-Burman language or group of languages spoken in northeastern India and western Myanmar (Burma)
a Tibeto-Burman language of northeastern India

Examples

Linguists study the structure and history of the Kamarupan languages in India.
A new dictionary of a Kamarupan dialect was recently published by researchers.
The spread of Kamarupan languages offers insights into migration patterns in South Asia.

Etymology

From the geographical region Kamarupa in northeastern India, with '-an' suffix forming demonyms or language names.
