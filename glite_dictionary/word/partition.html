partition

1. NOUN

/ pɑrˈtɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of dividing or separating something into parts, especially by creating a boundary or barrier
the act of dividing or separating

Examples

The partition of the room into two offices made better use of the space.
After the partition of the country, families found themselves on different sides of the border.
The legal partition of assets between the two businesses was finalized last week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: division - the act or process of dividing
  • Antonym: unification - the process of making into a single unit; the act of uniting

Etymology

From Latin partitio (a division, a sharing), from partire (to divide).

2. NOUN

/ pɑːrˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
(computing) a section of a computer's hard disk that is reserved for a specific operating system or application and is accessed as a single unit.
section of a hard disk (computing)

Examples

Before installing the new operating system, make sure you back up the partition where your files are stored.
The technician created a separate partition for the system files to keep them organized.
If your hard disk is running out of space, you can resize the existing partition using a disk management tool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volume - a storage unit on a computer that appears as a single drive or partition

Etymology

From Latin partitio (a division), from partīre (to divide).
