garotte

1. VERB

/ ɡəˈrɑt / (AmE), / ɡəˈrɒt / (BrE)
to kill or strangle someone by tightening a cord, wire, or iron collar around their neck
strangle or kill with a cord or collar

Examples

The assassin attempted to garotte his victim in a dark alley.
During the Spanish Inquisition, prisoners were sometimes garotted as a form of execution.
The spy silently garotted the guard to infiltrate the building.

Synset Examples

people were garrotted during the Inquisition in Spain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strangle - to kill by squeezing the throat
  • Synonym: choke - to prevent someone from breathing by blocking the throat

Etymology

From Spanish 'garrote' (a stick or club used for strangling), from Old Spanish 'garrar' (to seize).

2. NOUN

/ ɡəˈrɑt / (AmE), / ɡəˈrɒt / (BrE)
A device, typically a wire or cord, used to execute someone by strangulation.
strangulation execution device

Examples

The executioner used a garotte as the method of capital punishment.
Historians discovered the rusty garotte used during the 19th century.
He described how the garotte was employed in secret prisons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noose - loop with a running knot for strangling or hanging
  • Synonym: to strangle - to kill by squeezing the throat

Etymology

From Spanish 'garrote' (a stick or club), especially referring to the execution device.
