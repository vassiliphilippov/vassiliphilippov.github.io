disengagement

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈɡeɪdʒm(ə)nt / (BrE)
the act of withdrawing from a military action or breaking off combat with an enemy
military withdrawal; breaking off combat

Examples

The army's disengagement from the region was completed under strict orders.
Both sides agreed to a gradual disengagement after the ceasefire was declared.
Successful disengagement requires careful coordination to avoid unnecessary losses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withdrawal - the act of pulling out from an engagement or conflict

Etymology

From French désengagement, from désengager (to disengage), based on engage (to engage).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
the act of releasing or separating from an attachment, connection, or involvement
the act of detaching or separating

Examples

The sudden disengagement of the engine caused the machine to stop abruptly.
Emotional disengagement can harm relationships over time.
After years of involvement, her disengagement from the project surprised everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separation - the process or state of moving or being moved apart
  • Antonym: attachment - the action or state of being attached or joined to something

Etymology

From French 'désengagement', from 'désengager' (to disengage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00391714-n
Synset POS: n
