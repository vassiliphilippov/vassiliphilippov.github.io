to coalesce

1. VERB

/ ˌkoʊəˈlɛs / (AmE), / ˌkəʊəˈlɛs / (BrE)
to come together to form one whole; to fuse or grow together
to fuse or unite into one whole

Examples

After months of negotiation, the two parties finally coalesced into a unified movement.
Over time, the small villages began to coalesce into a single town.
The drops of oil coalesced on the surface of the water, forming a larger slick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combine - to join or merge to form a single thing
  • Antonym: separate - to move or be apart; to stop being joined

Etymology

From Latin coalescere, from co- (together) + alescere (to grow up).

2. VERB

/ ˌkoʊ.əˈlɛs / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.əˈlɛs / (BrE)
to come together to form one single thing; to fuse or blend into a single body
to unite or blend into one

Examples

The two companies decided to coalesce into a single, larger organization.
Over time, the drops of water began to coalesce and form a small puddle.
Their ideas gradually coalesced into a coherent plan for the project.

Synset Examples

The colors blend well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blend - to mix different substances so that they combine together
  • Antonym: separate - to move apart or split into different things

Etymology

From Latin coalescere, from co- (together) + alescere (to grow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00395719-v
Synset POS: v
