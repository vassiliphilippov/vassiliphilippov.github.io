traumatic

1. ADJ

/ trəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / trɔːˈmatɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or caused by physical injury or a wound to the body.
relating to physical injury
medical

Examples

The accident resulted in a traumatic brain injury that required immediate surgery.
Doctors are trained to respond quickly to traumatic wounds in emergency situations.
A traumatic fracture can take months to heal completely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injurious - causing or likely to cause damage or injury

Etymology

From Greek *trauma* (wound) + -ic.

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ trəˈmætɪk, troʊˈmætɪk / (AmE), / trɔːˈmætɪk, trəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
causing severe emotional or psychological distress; deeply upsetting or disturbing.
psychologically painful or distressing

Examples

The car accident was a traumatic event that left him anxious for years.
Losing her job had a traumatic impact on her mental health.
The children needed counseling after their traumatic experience during the storm.

Synset Examples

few experiences are more traumatic than losing a child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distressing - causing anxiety, sorrow, or pain
  • Antonym: soothing - having a calming or comforting effect

Etymology

From Greek trauma (wound), traumatikos (pertaining to a wound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01717393-s
Synset POS: s
