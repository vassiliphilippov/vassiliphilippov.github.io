lightening

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtn̩ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (BrE)
the descent of the uterus into the pelvic cavity in late pregnancy, when the fetus moves lower in preparation for birth
descent of uterus before childbirth
medical

Examples

Some women notice lightening several weeks before labor begins.
The doctor explained that lightening is a normal part of late pregnancy.
After lightening, breathing often becomes easier for the expectant mother.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of becoming paler or changing to a lighter color
process of becoming lighter in color

Examples

After several washes, the fabric showed noticeable lightening around the seams.
The lightening of her hair color was exactly what she wanted for summer.
He used a chemical agent for the lightening of old photographs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: darkening - the process of becoming darker or less light in color
