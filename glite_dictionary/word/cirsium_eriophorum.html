Cirsium eriophorum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜr.si.əm ˌɛr.i.əˈfoʊ.rəm / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.si.əm ˌɛr.i.əˈfɔː.rəm / (BrE)
A tall thistle species, commonly known as the woolly thistle, native to western and central Europe and the Balkan Peninsula, characterized by woolly stems and large purple flower heads.
woolly thistle species of Europe

Examples

The Cirsium eriophorum stands out in the meadow with its dense, woolly stems and vibrant purple blooms.
Botanists observed Cirsium eriophorum thriving in the chalky soils of the Balkan Peninsula.
Due to its striking appearance, Cirsium eriophorum is often noted by wildflower enthusiasts across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woolly thistle - Common name for Cirsium eriophorum
  • Degree Higher: thistle - Any of various prickly, often purple-flowered plants of the family Asteraceae

Etymology

From Latin: genus name *Cirsium* (from Greek *kirsion*, thistle), species epithet *eriophorum* ('wool-bearing') from Greek *erion* (wool) + *phoros* (bearing).
