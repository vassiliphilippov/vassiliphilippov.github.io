hanky-panky

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæŋki ˈpæŋki / (AmE), / ˌhæŋki ˈpaŋki / (BrE)
dishonest or suspicious activity, especially when intended to deceive or cheat
deceptive or suspicious activity
informal

Examples

The company went bankrupt after years of financial hanky-panky by its executives.
If there's any hanky-panky going on, the auditors will discover it.
The politician denied any hanky-panky related to the campaign funds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trickery - the practice of using tricks to deceive
  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain

Etymology

Reduplicative formation, probably from 'hanky', of uncertain origin, and playful repetition.
