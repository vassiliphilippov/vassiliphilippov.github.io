to revert

1. VERB

/ rɪˈvɝt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːt / (BrE)
(of an organism, cell, or gene) to return to a previous state, especially to a previous genetic condition, as after a mutation.
return to a previous genetic state

Examples

In rare cases, the altered gene may revert to its original form.
The bacteria eventually revert to their non-resistant state over several generations.
When the environment changes, some mutants can revert back, losing the adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to mutate - to change or cause to change in form or nature, especially genetically

Etymology

From Latin 'revertere' (to turn back).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈvɝt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to return to a previous state, condition, method, or activity
return to a previous state or condition

Examples

After months of healthy eating, he reverted to his old habits.
To fix the bug, the team reverted to the previous software version.
If the negotiations fail, the company may revert to its earlier strategy.

Synset Examples

We reverted to the old rules

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return - to come or go back to a place, situation, or condition
  • Synonym: relapse - to fall back into a former state, especially a bad one

Etymology

From Latin 'revertere' meaning 'to turn back'.
