welder

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛldər / (AmE), / ˈwɛldə / (BrE)
A person whose job is to join pieces of metal by welding them together.
person who welds metal

Examples

The welder carefully fused the steel beams together at the construction site.
As a welder, she must always wear protective goggles while working.
The company is hiring a skilled welder to repair the damaged machinery.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: welder - a machine used for welding metals
  • Synonym: welding - process of joining metal pieces by melting them

Etymology

From 'weld' (to join metal by heating) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
