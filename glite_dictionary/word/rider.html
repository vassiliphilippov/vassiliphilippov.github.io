rider

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪ.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.də / (BrE)
a person who travels by riding a vehicle, especially a bicycle or motorcycle
person who rides a vehicle

Examples

As an experienced rider, she always wears a helmet when cycling.
The club welcomes every rider, whether on a mountain bike or a road bike.
A rider waved to us as he sped past on his motorcycle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: driver - person who drives a vehicle

Etymology

From Old English rīdere (one who rides), from rīdan (to ride).

2. NOUN

/ ˈraɪdɚ / (AmE), / ˈraɪdə / (BrE)
an additional clause that is attached to a legislative bill, often having little connection with the bill's main topic
additional clause to a legislative bill
legal

Examples

The senator attached a rider to the education bill to secure funding for her state.
Controversy arose when a last-minute rider was introduced to the legislation.
Lawmakers often use a rider to add unrelated measures to an otherwise popular bill.

Etymology

From Middle English *rider*, meaning 'one who rides'; extended to mean an addition or supplement, especially to a legal document.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06406207-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈraɪdər / (AmE), / ˈraɪdə / (BrE)
a person who travels on an animal, especially a horse or camel, by sitting on its back and directing its movement
person who rides an animal

Examples

The experienced rider guided her horse skillfully through the narrow path.
Each rider in the caravan wore protective gear to cross the desert on camels.
A young rider fell off his pony during the show but was not hurt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equestrian - a person who rides horses, especially as a sport

Etymology

From Old English rīdere (one who rides), from rīdan (to ride).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10549540-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈraɪ.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.də / (BrE)
a person who travels in a vehicle, such as a bus, car, train, or boat, but is not operating it
person traveling as passenger

Examples

The bus was crowded, and every rider had to hold on tightly.
Each rider must pay the fare before boarding the train.
Carpooling apps match drivers with riders heading in the same direction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: driver - person who operates or controls a vehicle
  • Synonym: passenger - a person who is traveling in a vehicle but not operating it

Etymology

From Old English rīdere, from rīdan (to ride).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
