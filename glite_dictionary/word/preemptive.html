preemptive

1. ADJ

/ priˈɛmptɪv / (AmE), / priˈɛmptɪv / (BrE)
Intended to prevent or forestall an anticipated event or situation, especially by taking action in advance.
taken to prevent something anticipated

Examples

The company made a preemptive offer to acquire its competitor before any rivals could intervene.
We took preemptive measures to avoid any last-minute technical problems during the event.
A preemptive strike was authorized to neutralize the potential threat.

Synset Examples

a preemptive business offer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preventive - intended to stop something from happening
  • Antonym: reactive - acting in response to a situation rather than in advance

Etymology

From preempt + -ive, modeled on legal and commercial usage from the 19th century; ultimately from Latin praeemere (to buy before).
