shuffler

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌflɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃʌflə / (BrE)
a person who shuffles playing cards, especially before dealing in a card game
person who shuffles playing cards

Examples

In each round, a different shuffler was chosen to mix the deck before the game started.
The shuffler ensured that the cards were thoroughly mixed before dealing.
As the designated shuffler, she took care to prevent any cheating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dealer - the person who deals out cards in a card game

Etymology

From 'shuffle' (to mix cards) + '-er' (agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌflər / (AmE), / ˈʃʌflə / (BrE)
a person who walks without lifting their feet properly from the ground, often dragging or sliding them.
person who drags feet when walking

Examples

The old man was a habitual shuffler, dragging his feet along the corridor each morning.
As a shuffler, he often wore out the soles of his shoes quickly.
The physical therapist worked with the patient to help him stop being a shuffler.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: walker - a person who walks, especially for exercise or recreation

Etymology

From 'shuffle' (to walk by dragging one's feet) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10613816-n
Synset POS: n
