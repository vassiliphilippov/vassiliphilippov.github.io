septation

1. NOUN

/ sɛpˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛpˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the division or partition of a cavity or structure into parts by a septum
partitioning by a septum
medical

Examples

Congenital heart defects may result from abnormal septation of the cardiac chambers.
The process of septation creates separate compartments within the fungal hyphae.
Respiratory tract septation was clearly visible under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: septum - a dividing wall or partition in a structure or organ

Etymology

From Latin 'septum' (partition) + '-ation'.
