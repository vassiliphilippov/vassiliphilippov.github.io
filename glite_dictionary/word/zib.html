ZiB

1. NOUN

/ ziː bɪt / (AmE), / ziː bɪt / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,024 exbibytes, or 2^70 bytes, used to measure digital data storage
unit of digital information (2^70 bytes)

Examples

Modern data centers can store several ZiB of information for large-scale cloud services.
The theoretical limit for this file system is one ZiB, far beyond current needs.
As technology advances, handling a ZiB of data may become routine in scientific computing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: exbibyte - a unit of information equal to 2^60 bytes
  • Degree Higher: yobibyte - a unit of information equal to 2^80 bytes

Etymology

Acronym from 'zebibyte,' combining 'zebi-' (a binary prefix for 2^70) and 'byte.'
