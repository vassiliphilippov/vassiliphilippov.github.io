agalactia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡ.əˈlæk.ti.ə / (AmE), / ˌæɡ.əˈlæk.ti.ə / (BrE)
A condition in which milk is not produced in a mother's breasts after childbirth.
failure to produce milk after childbirth
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the new mother with agalactia when she was unable to breastfeed her infant.
Women affected by agalactia may require alternative feeding methods for their babies.
A lack of hormonal stimulation after birth can sometimes lead to agalactia.

Etymology

From Greek a- (not) + gala, galaktos (milk).
