Stenotaphrum secundatum

1. NOUN

/ stɛnoʊˈtæfrəm səˈkʌndətəm / (AmE), / stɛnəʊˈtæfrəm sɪˈkʌndɑːtəm / (BrE)
A low, mat-forming grass native to the southern United States and tropical America, often cultivated as a lawn grass.
a grass species used for lawns

Examples

Stenotaphrum secundatum thrives in warm, humid climates and creates a dense lawn cover.
Landscapers often recommend Stenotaphrum secundatum for areas with sandy or salty soils.
The groundskeeper reseeded the football field with Stenotaphrum secundatum to improve its durability.

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus name 'Stenotaphrum' + species 'secundatum'.
