rhinestone

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪnˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈraɪnˌstəʊn / (BrE)
an imitation diamond made from rock crystal, glass, or paste, often used in costume jewelry and decorative items
imitation diamond used in jewelry

Examples

The dancer's costume sparkled with every rhinestone sewn onto the fabric.
Her earrings were not real diamonds but gleaming rhinestones that caught the light beautifully.
He decorated his jacket with rhinestones to give it a flashy, eye-catching look.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diamond - a precious gemstone known for its brilliance and hardness
  • Synonym: gemstone - a precious or semi-precious stone used in jewelry

Etymology

From Rhine, referring to river pebbles first used as imitation gems, and stone.
