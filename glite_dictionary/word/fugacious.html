fugacious

1. ADJ

/ fjuˈɡeɪʃəs / (AmE), / fjʊˈɡeɪʃəs / (BrE)
lasting for only a short period; fleeting or transient
lasting a short time; fleeting

Examples

The beauty of the autumn leaves is fugacious and must be enjoyed while it lasts.
His fame proved fugacious, as the world quickly moved on to the next sensation.
Some flowers bloom in a fugacious burst of color before fading within days.

Synset Examples

the ephemeral joys of childhood
a passing fancy
youth's transient beauty
love is transitory but it is eternal
fugacious blossoms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ephemeral - lasting for a very short time
  • Synonym: transient - lasting only for a short period
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely

Etymology

From Latin 'fugax' (apt to flee, fleeting), from 'fugere' (to flee).
