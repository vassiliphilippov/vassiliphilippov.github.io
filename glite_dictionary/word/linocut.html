linocut

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnoʊˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈlɪnəʊˌkʌt / (BrE)
A design or print made by carving a pattern in relief into a sheet of linoleum, which is then inked and pressed onto paper or another surface.
design or print made from carved linoleum

Examples

The artist created a striking linocut of the city skyline for the exhibition.
We made our own linocuts in art class last week.
She specializes in linocut prints featuring animals and nature scenes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodcut - a printmaking technique using carved wood blocks

Etymology

From linoleum + cut, referring to the process of cutting designs into linoleum.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ.noʊˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈlɪn.əʊ.kʌt / (BrE)
A print made by carving a design in relief into a block of linoleum, applying ink to the raised surface, and pressing it onto paper.
print from carved linoleum block

Examples

She exhibited several linocut prints at the local art gallery.
The process of creating a linocut requires careful carving and printing skills.
Many modern artists experiment with linocut techniques to achieve bold graphic effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodcut - A print made using a design carved into wood

Etymology

Formed from 'linoleum' and 'cut', reflecting the material and the carving process.

Synset POS: n
