nimbleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪmbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈnɪmblnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being quick and alert in mind; mental agility or quick-wittedness
quickness and alertness of mind

Examples

Her nimbleness of mind allowed her to solve the puzzle faster than anyone else.
The comedian’s nimbleness with words kept the audience entertained all evening.
He was respected for the nimbleness of his wit during debates.

Synset Examples

nimbleness of wit and imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agility - quickness and ease of movement or thought
  • Antonym: slowness - the quality of being slow in thought or action

Etymology

From Middle English 'nimel' (quick, agile), related to Old English 'nǣmel' (apt, able).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɪm.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnɪm.bl̩.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being quick and light in movement; agility and gracefulness, especially in physical actions.
quickness and grace in movement

Examples

The nimbleness of the gymnast impressed the entire audience.
Cats are admired for their nimbleness when they quickly jump onto high places.
Her nimbleness allowed her to dodge every obstacle during the race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agility - the ability to move quickly and easily; physical nimbleness
  • Antonym: clumsiness - the quality of being awkward in movement or action

Etymology

From Old English 'næmel' (quick to grasp or take), influenced by 'nimble' + '-ness.'

Synset POS: n
