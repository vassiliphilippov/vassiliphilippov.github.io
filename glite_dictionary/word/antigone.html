Antigone

1. PROPN

/ ænˈtɪɡəni / (AmE), / ænˈtɪɡəni / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the daughter of King Oedipus who defied authority by burying her brother and was ultimately condemned to death.
daughter of Oedipus in Greek mythology

Examples

The tragedy of Antigone has inspired numerous writers and artists throughout history.
In Sophocles' play, Antigone chooses to honor her brother despite the king's decree.
Many students read about Antigone when studying classical literature.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀντιγόνη (Antigónē).
