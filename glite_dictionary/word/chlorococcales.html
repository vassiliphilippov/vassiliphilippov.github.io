Chlorococcales

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔːrəˌkoʊˈseɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌklɔːrəˌkɒˈseɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of unicellular green algae that reproduce by spores
order of unicellular green algae

Examples

The Chlorococcales are commonly found in freshwater and form a major part of plankton.
Scientists study Chlorococcales to understand their role in aquatic ecosystems.
Many species within the Chlorococcales reproduce rapidly under favorable conditions.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Chlorococcales', derived from 'chloros' (green) and 'kokkos' (grain, berry).

an order of unicellular green algae that reproduce by spores, found chiefly in freshwater
order of unicellular green algae

Examples

Scientists studied the Chlorococcales to better understand their reproductive cycle.
Many freshwater ponds contain species belonging to the Chlorococcales.
The algal blooms observed last summer were largely composed of Chlorococcales.

Etymology

From New Latin Chlorococcum (a genus of green algae) + -ales (order suffix in botanical Latin)
