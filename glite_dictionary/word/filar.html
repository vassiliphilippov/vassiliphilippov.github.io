filar

1. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪlə / (BrE)
relating to or having filaments, especially across a field of view (as in the eyepiece of a telescope or microscope).
relating to filaments (especially in optics)

Examples

The filar micrometer is used for precise astronomical measurements.
A filar eyepiece contains fine threads to aid in calibration.
Scientists adjusted the filar lines to accurately measure the specimen's dimensions.

Synset Examples

a filar eyepiece

Etymology

From Latin 'filum' (thread), via French 'filaire', meaning 'pertaining to a thread.'
