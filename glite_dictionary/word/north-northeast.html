north-northeast

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɔrθ nɔrˈθist / (AmE), / ˌnɔːθ nɔːˈθiːst / (BrE)
the direction or compass point halfway between north and northeast, at an angle of 22.5° east of due north
compass point between north and northeast

Examples

The ship set a course for north-northeast to avoid the storm.
We hiked in the north-northeast direction for several miles until reaching the lake.
The wind was blowing steadily from the north-northeast all morning.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: northeast - the direction between north and east, or 45° on the compass
  • Degree Lower: north - the direction corresponding to 0° on the compass

2. ADV

/ nɔrθ nɔrθˈist / (AmE), / nɔːθ nɔːθˈiːst / (BrE)
in or toward the direction halfway between north and northeast on a compass
in the direction between north and northeast

Examples

The hikers headed north-northeast to avoid the mountain range.
The wind was blowing north-northeast at fifteen miles per hour that morning.
Sail the ship north-northeast for two hours to reach the next port.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: north - the direction toward the North Pole
  • Degree Higher: northeast - the direction between north and east

Etymology

From 'north' and 'northeast,' meaning halfway between north and northeast.
