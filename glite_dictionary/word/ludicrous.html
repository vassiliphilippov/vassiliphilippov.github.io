ludicrous

1. ADJ

/ ˈluː.dɪ.krəs / (AmE), / ˈluː.dɪ.krəs / (BrE)
so absurd, unreasonable, or exaggerated as to be laughable; ridiculous or amusingly outlandish
ridiculously absurd or laughable

Examples

The idea of wearing a tuxedo to the beach seems utterly ludicrous to me.
Her explanation for being late was so ludicrous that nobody believed her.
They laughed at his ludicrous suggestion of building a bridge from paper.

Synset Examples

the wild farcical exuberance of a clown
ludicrous green hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridiculous - deserving or inviting derision or mockery; absurd
  • Synonym: absurd - wildly unreasonable, illogical, or inappropriate
  • Antonym: sensible - choosing in accordance with wisdom or prudence; likely to be of benefit

Etymology

From Latin ludicrus (sportive, playful), from ludus (game, play).

2. ADJ

/ ˈluːdəkrəs / (AmE), / ˈluːdɪkrəs / (BrE)
So unreasonable, ridiculous, or out of place as to be amusing; laughably absurd.
absurd or laughably unreasonable

Examples

It would be ludicrous to expect everyone to finish the project in a single day.
The idea that the tiny boat could cross the ocean seemed ludicrous.
Some of the fashion trends from the 1970s now look completely ludicrous.

Synset Examples

the absurd excuse that the dog ate his homework
that's a cockeyed idea
ask a nonsensical question and get a nonsensical answer
a contribution so small as to be laughable
it is ludicrous to call a cottage a mansion
a preposterous attempt to turn back the pages of history
her conceited assumption of universal interest in her rather dull children was ridiculous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absurd - wildly unreasonable, illogical, or inappropriate
  • Synonym: ridiculous - deserving or inviting ridicule; very silly or unreasonable
  • Antonym: sensible - chosen in accordance with wisdom or prudence; likely to be of benefit

Etymology

From Latin ludicrus (sportive, playful), from ludus (game).
