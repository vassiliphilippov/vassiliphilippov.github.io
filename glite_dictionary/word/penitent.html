penitent

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnɪtənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛnɪtənt / (BrE)
a person who feels or shows remorse for wrongdoing or sin and seeks forgiveness, especially in a religious context
a person who repents sins; seeking forgiveness

Examples

The penitent knelt in silence, seeking forgiveness for his actions.
During confession, the priest offered guidance to the penitent standing before him.
The novel describes the transformation of a hardened criminal into a humble penitent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repentant - feeling or expressing remorse for wrongdoing

Etymology

From Latin 'paenitens', present participle of 'paenitere' (to repent).

WordNet ID: oewn-10433579-n

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɛnɪtənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛnɪtənt / (BrE)
feeling or showing sorrow and regret for having done wrong; repentant.
feeling or showing remorse

Examples

He looked penitent as he apologized for his actions.
The penitent thief promised never to steal again.
Her penitent expression convinced everyone she was truly sorry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remorseful - feeling deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • Synonym: contrite - feeling or showing sincere remorse
  • Antonym: unrepentant - not feeling or expressing remorse or regret

Etymology

From Latin *paenitentem*, present participle of *paenitēre* (to cause regret).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01747466-a
Synset POS: a
