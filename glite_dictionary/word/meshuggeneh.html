meshuggeneh

1. NOUN

/ məˈʃʊgənə / (AmE), / məˈʃʊgənə / (BrE)
(informal) A crazy or foolish person, especially as described in Yiddish or Jewish culture.
crazy or foolish person (Yiddish)
informal

Examples

My uncle is a real meshuggeneh when it comes to his conspiracy theories.
Don’t pay attention to that meshuggeneh shouting in the street.
She called her brother a meshuggeneh for quitting his job to sail around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lunatic - a mentally ill person, or one acting insanely
  • Antonym: sane - of sound mind; not crazy

Etymology

From Yiddish משוגענע (meshuggene), from Hebrew מְשׁוּגָּע (meshuga, mad, crazy).

2. ADJ

/ məˈʃʊɡənə / (AmE), / məˈʃʊɡənə / (BrE)
Crazy or senseless; behaving in a foolish or irrational way.
crazy; senseless (informal)
informal

Examples

Don't listen to him, he's totally meshuggeneh to suggest such a thing.
It would be meshuggeneh to invest all your money in one stock.
Her meshuggeneh ideas often made the family laugh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crazy - mentally deranged or extremely foolish
  • Antonym: sane - of sound mind; not crazy

Etymology

From Yiddish meshugene, from Hebrew meshuggā‘ (mad, insane).
