competitiveness

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɛt̬.ə.tɪv.nəs / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛt.ɪ.tɪv.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having a strong desire to compete and succeed against others
desire or drive to compete

Examples

Her competitiveness was evident during every match she played.
A healthy sense of competitiveness can motivate employees to perform their best.
Too much competitiveness sometimes leads to unnecessary conflict in a team.

Synset Examples

the team was full of fight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competition - the act or process of competing
  • Antonym: cooperativeness - the quality of being willing to work together with others

Etymology

From English 'competitive' + '-ness'; 'competitive' from Latin 'competere' (to strive together).
