Osmanthus

1. NOUN

/ ɑsˈmænθəs / (AmE), / ɒzˈmanθəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs or small trees native to the southern United States, Middle East, China, and Japan, known for their fragrant flowers.
genus of evergreen, fragrant shrubs or trees

Examples

The Osmanthus in her garden filled the air with a sweet, jasmine-like scent.
Many gardeners plant Osmanthus for its attractive evergreen leaves and fragrant blooms.
In China, Osmanthus flowers are often used to flavor tea and desserts.

Etymology

From Greek osme (smell, fragrance) + anthos (flower).
