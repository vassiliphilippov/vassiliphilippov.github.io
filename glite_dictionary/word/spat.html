spat

1. NOUN

/ spæt / (AmE), / spæt / (BrE)
a brief, petty quarrel or argument, usually about something unimportant
a minor quarrel or argument

Examples

They had a little spat over where to go for dinner last night.
A minor spat between colleagues disrupted the otherwise calm meeting.
After their spat, they apologized and quickly made up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrel - an angry argument or disagreement
  • Degree Higher: fight - a violent confrontation or serious quarrel
  • Degree Lower: disagreement - a lack of consensus or difference of opinion

Etymology

Perhaps imitative of the sound of a light slap or the early verb 'spat' (to quarrel), 19th century origin.

2. NOUN

/ spæt / (AmE), / spæt / (BrE)
A young oyster or other bivalve, especially at the stage when it has just settled from the larval stage and attached itself to a surface.
a young oyster or bivalve

Examples

The oyster farmer monitored the spat to ensure they were developing properly.
After spawning, the larvae become spat and settle on the seabed.
Collecting wild spat is an important part of traditional oyster farming.

Etymology

From Middle English *spat*, probably from Old French *espat* (spawn of shellfish).
