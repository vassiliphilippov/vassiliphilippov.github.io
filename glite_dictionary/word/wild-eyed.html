wild-eyed

1. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪldˌaɪd / (AmE), / ˈwaɪldˌaɪd / (BrE)
Having ideas or plans that are extreme, unrealistic, or not practical.
unrealistically idealistic or impractical

Examples

His wild-eyed proposals were dismissed as completely impractical by the board.
She became known for her wild-eyed visions of a society without laws.
The startup attracted some wild-eyed dreamers who wanted to change the world overnight.

Synset Examples

as quixotic as a restoration of medieval knighthood
a romantic disregard for money
a wild-eyed dream of a world state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quixotic - extremely idealistic and impractical
  • Antonym: practical - concerned with actually doing or using something rather than theories or ideas

2. ADJ

/ waɪld aɪd / (AmE), / waɪld aɪd / (BrE)
looking extremely agitated, excited, or crazy, often with a staring or wide-eyed expression
appearing extremely agitated or excited

Examples

The protesters grew wild-eyed as the tension increased during the rally.
After days without sleep, he staggered into the room, looking wild-eyed and desperate.
The wild-eyed horse reared nervously at the sound of thunder.

Synset Examples

crowded the wild-eyed animals into a truck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frenzied - wildly excited or uncontrolled
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or agitation

Synset POS: s
