hybridisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.brɪ.dəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪ.brɪ.dʌɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The process of mating or combining different species or varieties of animals or plants to produce hybrids.
crossbreeding of species or varieties

Examples

The hybridisation of two wheat species led to a plant with improved resistance to disease.
Scientists have used hybridisation techniques to create new, more productive crop varieties.
Orchid enthusiasts often attempt hybridisation to achieve unique and beautiful blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hybridization - alternative spelling, especially in American English

Etymology

From hybrid + Latin -isation, patterned after similar terms like fertilisation.
