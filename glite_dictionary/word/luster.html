luster

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌstər / (AmE), / ˈlʌstə / (BrE)
a glossy or metallic surface coating applied to ceramics or porcelain to give a shiny or iridescent finish
shiny ceramic/porcelain coating

Examples

The vase was finished with a golden luster that shimmered in the light.
Her collection of porcelain figures all featured a delicate pearly luster.
Artisans perfected the technique to apply a metallic luster to fine ceramics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glaze - a vitreous coating applied to pottery

Etymology

From Latin 'lustrare' (to make bright), via Old French 'lustre'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌstɚ / (AmE), / ˈlʌstə / (BrE)
A quality that gives someone or something special distinction, brilliance, or glory beyond the ordinary.
special brilliance or distinction

Examples

A Nobel Prize brings international luster to a scientist's career.
The fresh paint gave the old building a new luster.
Her performance added luster to an already impressive résumé.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glory - great honor or praise
  • Synonym: shine - to be bright or give off light; brilliance
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of brightness or interest

Etymology

From Latin 'lustrare' (to make bright), through Old French 'lustre'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlʌstɚ / (AmE), / ˈlʌstə / (BrE)
a gentle sheen or soft glow, especially that of a surface that reflects light
a soft shine or glow

Examples

The diamond's luster made it particularly attractive to collectors.
After polishing the silverware, its original luster was restored.
The pearl's soft luster set it apart from the common stones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloss - a shiny or lustrous surface
  • Antonym: matte - a surface without shine or gloss
  • Synonym: gleam - a brief or steady shine

Etymology

From Latin 'lūstrum', meaning 'purification, brightness', via Old French 'lustre'.
