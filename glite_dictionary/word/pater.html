pater

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈpeɪtə / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British) a person's father, especially as used facetiously or by schoolboys.
father (facetiously; chiefly British informal)
informal british

Examples

My pater insisted I return home by midnight.
He often tells amusing stories about his pater from his schoolboy days.
The boys referred to their fathers as paters in a mock-serious tone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: father - one's male parent

Etymology

From Latin pater (father).
