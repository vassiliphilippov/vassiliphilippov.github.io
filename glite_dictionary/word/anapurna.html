Anapurna

1. PROPN

/ ɑːnəˈpʊrnə / (AmE), / ænəˈpʊːnə / (BrE)
a mountain peak in the Himalayas in Nepal, standing 26,545 feet (8,091 meters) high, known for its challenging climbing routes
mountain peak in the Himalayas, Nepal

Examples

Climbers from around the world travel to Nepal to attempt an ascent of Anapurna.
Severe weather conditions on Anapurna can make expeditions extremely dangerous.
Photos of Anapurna at sunrise are breathtakingly beautiful.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Annapūrṇa', a Hindu goddess, meaning 'full of food' or 'giver of food'.

2. PROPN

/ ˌɑːnəˈpʊrnə / (AmE), / ˌænəˈpʊːnə / (BrE)
A Hindu goddess of plenty, regarded as a benevolent aspect of Devi and the wife of Siva.
Hindu goddess of plenty

Examples

Pilgrims traveled to the temple to offer thanks to Anapurna for the season’s abundant harvest.
The image of Anapurna is often depicted holding a bowl of food, symbolizing nourishment and generosity.
Stories of Anapurna are central to many Hindu festivals celebrating prosperity and sustenance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Devi - Hindu goddess representing the divine feminine
  • Synonym: Parvati - Wife of Siva, Hindu goddess

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Annapūrṇā', meaning 'full of food' or 'provider of food'.
