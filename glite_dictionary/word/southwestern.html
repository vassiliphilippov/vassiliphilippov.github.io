Southwestern

1. NOUN

/ saʊθˈwɛstərn / (AmE), / saʊθˈwɛstən / (BrE)
a dialect of Middle English formerly spoken in the region west of Sussex and south and southwest of the Thames River.
historical dialect of Middle English

Examples

Scholars have studied the Southwestern dialect to better understand medieval English texts.
The literary features of Southwestern English differ noticeably from those of Kentish or Northern dialects.
Some rare manuscripts written in Southwestern contain unique spellings and vocabulary.
