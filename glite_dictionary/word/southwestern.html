southwestern

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaʊθˈwɛstərn / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθˈwɛstən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the southwestern part of a country, especially the southwestern region of the United States, generally including New Mexico, Arizona, Texas, California, and sometimes Nevada, Utah, and Colorado
of or relating to the American Southwest

Examples

She moved to the southwestern United States to enjoy its warm climate and unique culture.
The southwestern landscape is famous for its deserts and red rock canyons.
Many traditional southwestern dishes incorporate chili peppers and corn.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northeastern - relating to the northeastern part of a country or region

Etymology

From south + western, indicating direction or location in the southwest.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsaʊθˈwɛstərn / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθˈwɛstən / (BrE)
Situated in or facing toward the southwest.
located in or facing the southwest

Examples

They live in the southwestern part of the state, near the mountains.
The southwestern corner of the building gets the most sunlight in the afternoon.
We took a road trip through the southwestern region, famous for its deserts and canyons.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northeastern - situated in or oriented toward the northeast
  • Antonym: southeastern - situated in or oriented toward the southeast
  • Antonym: northwestern - situated in or oriented toward the northwest
