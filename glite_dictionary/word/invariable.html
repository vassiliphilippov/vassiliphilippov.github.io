invariable

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛriəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪnˈveəriəbl̩ / (BrE)
a quantity or value that does not change in different circumstances or over time, especially in mathematics or science
quantity or value that does not change

Examples

The speed of light is considered an invariable in physics equations.
In certain mathematical formulas, the parameter acts as an invariable.
Some natural phenomena are governed by invariables that remain unchanged across conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constant - a value or quantity that does not change

Etymology

From Late Latin *invariabilis*, from Latin *in-* (not) + *variabilis* (changeable).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɛriəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈveəriəbl / (BrE)
never changing; always the same in all situations or at all times.
never changing; constant

Examples

His invariable kindness made a strong impression on everyone he met.
The meeting started at the invariable time of 9 a.m. each week.
She followed her invariable morning routine despite the chaos around her.

Synset Examples

an invariable temperature
an invariable rule
his invariable courtesy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constant - remaining the same over time; unchanging
  • Antonym: variable - likely to change; not consistent

Etymology

From Late Latin invariabilis (unchangeable), from Latin in- (not) + variabilis (changeable).
