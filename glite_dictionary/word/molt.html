molt

1. NOUN

/ moʊlt / (AmE), / məʊlt / (BrE)
the process by which an animal, such as an arthropod or reptile, periodically sheds its outer layer, such as skin or cuticle
shedding of skin or outer layer in animals

Examples

Snakes undergo a molt several times a year as they grow.
The tarantula was sluggish before its last molt.
A bird's molt may affect its ability to fly temporarily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molting - process of shedding skin or feathers
  • Synonym: ecdysis - the process of molting in arthropods and reptiles

Etymology

From Middle English *mouten*, from Old English *mūtian* (to change), ultimately from Latin *mutare* (to change).

2. VERB

/ moʊlt / (AmE), / məʊlt / (BrE)
(of an animal) to shed old feathers, hair, skin, or shell to make way for new growth.
shed feathers, hair, skin, or shell

Examples

Every year, birds molt their old feathers to prepare for migration.
My pet snake began to molt and soon shed all its skin.
Lobsters will molt several times as they grow larger.

Synset Examples

our dog sheds every Spring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shed - to lose or cast off a covering, such as hair or feathers, naturally

Etymology

From Latin 'mutāre' (to change), influenced by Middle English 'mouten' (to cast off).
