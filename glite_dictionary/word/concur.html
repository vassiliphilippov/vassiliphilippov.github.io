to concur

1. VERB

/ kənˈkɝː / (AmE), / kənˈkɜː / (BrE)
to agree with someone or have the same opinion or decision as someone else
to be in agreement

Examples

All the members of the committee concurred with the recommendation.
Historians generally concur that the discovery was groundbreaking.
I do not concur with your interpretation of the results.

Synset Examples

We agreed on the terms of the settlement
I can't agree with you!
I hold with those who say life is sacred
Both philosophers concord on this point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to agree - to have the same opinion as someone else
  • Antonym: to disagree - to have a different opinion; not to agree

Etymology

From Latin 'concurrere' (to run together, agree), from 'con-' (together) + 'currere' (to run).

WordNet ID: oewn-00807038-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kənˈkɜr / (AmE), / kənˈkɜː / (BrE)
(of events) to happen or occur at the same time; coincide.
happen at the same time

Examples

The parade and the festival concurred this year, making the city especially lively.
Three important deadlines concurred on the same day, causing a lot of stress for the team.
Her vacation plans concurred perfectly with the scheduled maintenance at work, so she didn't miss anything important.

Synset Examples

The two events coincided

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to coincide - to occur at the same time

Etymology

From Latin 'concurrere' (to run together, coincide).

Synset POS: v
