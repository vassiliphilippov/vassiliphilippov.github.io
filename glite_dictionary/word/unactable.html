unactable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈæktəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈæktəbl / (BrE)
(of a role, script, or situation) not capable of being effectively performed or portrayed by an actor.
impossible to act or perform (theatrically)

Examples

The critic described the lead character as an unactable part due to its lack of depth.
Many consider the play nearly unactable because of its confusing structure and dialogue.
The director struggled to bring the unactable script to life on stage.

Synset Examples

an unactable play

Related Terms

  • Antonym: actable - capable of being acted or performed on stage

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'actable' (able to be acted).
