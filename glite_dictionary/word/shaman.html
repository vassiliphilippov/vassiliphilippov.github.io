shaman

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑː.mən / (AmE), / ˈʃɑː.mən / (BrE)
A person regarded as having access to, and influence in, the world of spirits, often acting as a medium for healing, divination, and other ritual practices, especially in certain traditional societies.
spiritual medium or healer in some societies

Examples

In the remote village, the shaman performed rituals to cure the sick and protect against evil spirits.
Anthropologists studied the role of the shaman in ancient Siberian cultures.
The tribe's shaman was consulted whenever someone had a troubling dream or needed guidance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witch doctor - traditional healer or folk magician, especially in Africa; believed to have supernatural powers
  • Antonym: priest - person authorized to perform sacred rituals, especially in a religion

Etymology

From Russian 'shaman,' originally from Tungus (Evenki) 'šaman' (one who knows).
