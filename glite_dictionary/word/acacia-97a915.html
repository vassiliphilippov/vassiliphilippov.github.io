Acacia

1. NOUN

/ əˈkeɪʃə / (AmE), / əˈkeɪsɪə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs, trees, and some woody vines found mainly in Central and South America, Africa, Australia, and Polynesia, including wattles and mimosas.
genus of shrubs and trees (wattles, mimosas)

Examples

Several species in the Acacia genus are commonly used as ornamental plants in gardens worldwide.
The leaves of Acacia trees provide food for many types of wildlife in Australia.
Scientists are studying the medicinal properties of compounds found in Acacia bark.

Etymology

From Latin 'acacia', from Greek 'akakia' (a thorny Egyptian tree).
