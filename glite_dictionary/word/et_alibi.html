et alibi

1. ADV

/ ɛt ˈæ.laɪ.bi / (AmE), / ɛt ˈal.ɪ.baɪ / (BrE)
and elsewhere; used in references to indicate other locations or occurrences in a text or document not listed in detail.
and elsewhere (in references)

Examples

The case is discussed in Jones (1997) et alibi.
Find further details on the subject in Smith (2003) et alibi.
Several similar experiments were described by Miller (1985) et alibi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: et al. - and others; used in lists of authors or people

Etymology

From Latin 'et alibi', meaning 'and elsewhere'.
