to inundate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪn.ʌn.deɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.ʌn.deɪt / (BrE)
to cover or flood something completely, especially with water
to flood or cover completely with water

Examples

After the heavy rain, the river quickly inundated the surrounding fields.
During the hurricane, entire neighborhoods were inundated with seawater.
If the dam breaks, the valley will be inundated within hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - to cover with water, typically due to excessive rain or overflowing

Etymology

From Latin 'inundare' (to overflow, flood), from 'in-' (upon) + 'unda' (wave).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɪnʌnˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.ʌn.deɪt / (BrE)
to flood or overwhelm something with a large amount of water or things, often beyond capacity
to flood or overwhelm

Examples

Heavy rains can easily inundate low-lying neighborhoods within hours.
We were inundated with emails after the announcement went public.
If left unchecked, the river could inundate the entire valley.

Synset Examples

the basement was inundated after the storm
The images flooded his mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overwhelm - to overpower or flood with something, especially emotions or things
  • Synonym: flood - to cover or submerge with water; to inundate

Etymology

From Latin *inundare* (to flood), from *in* (upon) + *unda* (wave).
