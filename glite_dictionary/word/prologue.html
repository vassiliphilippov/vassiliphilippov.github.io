prologue

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊ.lɔg / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.lɒg / (BrE)
an introductory section at the beginning of a play, often explaining background information or setting the scene
introduction at the start of a play

Examples

The prologue introduced the main conflict before the first act began.
In Shakespearean theater, a prologue was often used to address the audience directly.
The play opened with a short prologue explaining the characters' history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: epilogue - a concluding section at the end of a play or literary work

Etymology

From Greek prologos (pro- ‘before’ + logos ‘speech’).
