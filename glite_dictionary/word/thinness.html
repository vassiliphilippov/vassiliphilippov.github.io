thinness

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪnnəs / (AmE), / ˈθɪnnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of lacking substance, depth, or strength.
lack of substance or strength

Examples

Some critics pointed out the thinness of the evidence supporting his theory.
The thinness of her argument became apparent during the debate.
He was disappointed by the thinness of the plot in the latest novel.

Synset Examples

Perhaps because he recognized the thinness of this critique, the language about lack of theory that appeared in the dissertation was replaced in the book by a more modest critique that PWD lacked adequate conceptual definitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakness - lack of strength or firmness
  • Antonym: strength - the quality of being strong, firm, or substantial

Etymology

From Old English 'þynness', from 'thin' + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθɪnnəs / (AmE), / ˈθɪnnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of having little thickness; the measure of how thin something is.
state of being thin (not thick)

Examples

The thinness of the wire makes it difficult to see.
He commented on the thinness of the ice covering the pond.
The thinness of the book surprised me, as I expected it to be much thicker.

Synset Examples

the tenuity of a hair
the thinness of a rope

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thickness - the dimension through an object as opposed to its length or width; opposite of thinness
  • Synonym: slenderness - the quality of being slender, usually referring to a gracefully thin appearance

Etymology

From 'thin' (Old English 'þynne') + suffix '-ness'.
