Nurnberg

1. PROPN

/ ˈnʊrn.bɜrɡ / (AmE), / ˈnʊːən.bɛːk / (BrE)
a city in southeastern Germany, noted for its medieval architecture and as the site of the Nuremberg Trials after World War II
city in southeastern Germany

Examples

The historic city of Nurnberg attracts tourists with its well-preserved medieval old town.
During the post-war period, Nurnberg was the site of landmark war crimes trials.
He studied architecture in Nurnberg before moving back to Berlin.

Etymology

German Nürnberg, from Middle High German Nurenberc, likely meaning 'rocky hill of the Noric people'.
