holophyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊləˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɒl.ə.faɪt / (BrE)
an organism, especially a plant, that produces its own food by photosynthesis
organism producing food by photosynthesis

Examples

Many algae are classified as holophytes because they make their own food through photosynthesis.
A holophyte absorbs sunlight and uses it to convert carbon dioxide and water into energy.
The distinction between holophyte and heterotroph is fundamental in biology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autotroph - an organism that produces complex organic compounds from simple substances like carbon dioxide
  • Antonym: heterotroph - an organism that cannot synthesize its own food and depends on other organisms

Etymology

From Greek holos (whole, entire) + phyton (plant).
