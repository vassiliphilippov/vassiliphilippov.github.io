Notornis mantelli

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈtɔrnɪs mænˈtɛli / (AmE), / nəʊˈtɔːnɪs mænˈtɛli / (BrE)
A large, flightless bird native to New Zealand, also known as the takahē, similar to gallinules.
flightless New Zealand bird (takahē)

Examples

The once-thought-extinct Notornis mantelli was rediscovered in New Zealand in 1948.
The Notornis mantelli is distinguished by its vivid blue and green plumage.
Efforts to conserve the endangered Notornis mantelli have increased public awareness of New Zealand's unique fauna.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: takahē - common name for Notornis mantelli, a flightless New Zealand bird

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Notornis' (southern bird) and 'mantelli', named after Gideon Mantell, English paleontologist.
