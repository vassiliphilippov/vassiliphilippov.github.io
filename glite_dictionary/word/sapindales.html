Sapindales

1. NOUN

/ sæpɪnˈdeɪliːz / (AmE), / sapɪnˈdeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of dicotyledonous plants that includes families such as Sapindaceae, Rutaceae, and Anacardiaceae
order of flowering plants

Examples

The citrus fruits we eat belong to the order Sapindales.
Biologists study the evolutionary relationships among families within Sapindales.
The order Sapindales includes plants such as the cashew and horse chestnut.

Etymology

From New Latin, from the genus name Sapindus + -ales, a standard suffix for plant orders.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæpɪnˈdeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌsæpɪnˈdeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of dicotyledonous plants that includes the soapberry, cashew, citrus, and mahogany families
an order of flowering plants (including citrus, cashew, mahogany)

Examples

Many economically important crops belong to the Sapindales, such as mangoes, cashews, and citrus fruits.
The family Rutaceae, to which oranges and lemons belong, is part of the Sapindales.
Botanists studied the evolutionary relationships within the Sapindales to better understand plant diversity.

Etymology

From modern Latin Sapindales, based on the genus name Sapindus (soapberry).
