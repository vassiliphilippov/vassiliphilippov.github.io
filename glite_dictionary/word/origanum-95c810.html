origanum

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪɡənəm / (AmE), / ɒˈrɪɡənəm / (BrE)
Any of various aromatic herbs of the genus Origanum, including oregano and marjoram, commonly used as seasonings.
aromatic herbs genus including oregano

Examples

Greek dishes often use origanum to add depth and flavor to their sauces.
The chef sprinkled dried origanum over the roasted vegetables before serving.
Several species in the origanum genus are appreciated for their fragrant leaves in Mediterranean cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oregano - aromatic herb used as a culinary seasoning
  • Synonym: marjoram - aromatic herb of the Origanum genus used in cooking

Etymology

From Latin *orīganum*, from Greek *origanon* (marjoram).
