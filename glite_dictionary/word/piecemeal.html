piecemeal

1. ADJ

/ ˈpiːsˌmiːl / (AmE), / ˈpiːsmiːl / (BrE)
Done or made one piece at a time; in separate stages rather than as a whole.
one thing or step at a time

Examples

The reforms were introduced in a piecemeal fashion over several years.
Attempts at solving the problem have been piecemeal and ineffective so far.
She prefers a comprehensive plan rather than the piecemeal approach currently being used.

Etymology

From Middle English 'pecemeale', from piece + -meal (meaning 'by a (specified) portion or measure').

2. ADV

/ ˈpiːsˌmiːl / (AmE), / ˈpiːsˌmiːl / (BrE)
In a gradual way, by taking small steps or stages rather than all at once.
gradually, bit by bit

Examples

The company is upgrading its computer systems piecemeal, handling one department at a time.
The reforms were introduced piecemeal rather than through a single comprehensive change.
Their house was renovated piecemeal over several years whenever they could afford it.

Synset Examples

the research structure has developed piecemeal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gradually - in a slow, step-by-step manner
  • Antonym: suddenly - quickly and unexpectedly

Etymology

From Middle English 'pecemeale', from 'piece' + 'meal' (meaning 'by a part' or 'a portion'), from Old English 'mǣlum' (in parts).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00424192-r
Synset POS: r
