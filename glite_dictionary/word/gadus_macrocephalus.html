Gadus macrocephalus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪ.dəs ˌmæk.roʊˈsɛf.ə.ləs / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪ.dəs ˌmæk.rəʊˈsɛf.ə.ləs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the Pacific cod, a marine fish closely related to the Atlantic cod, found mainly in the northern Pacific Ocean.
Pacific cod (marine fish species)

Examples

The commercial fishing industry heavily depends on Gadus macrocephalus for Pacific cod products.
Scientists conducted a study on the spawning patterns of Gadus macrocephalus in the Bering Sea.
Many sushi dishes in Japan feature Gadus macrocephalus as a key ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atlantic cod - a closely related species of cod native to the Atlantic Ocean

Etymology

From Latin; Gadus (a codfish genus), macrocephalus ('large-headed').
