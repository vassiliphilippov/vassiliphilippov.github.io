Ruritania

1. PROPN

/ ˌrʊrɪˈteɪniə / (AmE), / ˌrʊərɪˈteɪniə / (BrE)
an imaginary Central European kingdom, often used as a setting for intrigue and romance in literature and film
fictional Central European kingdom

Examples

Many classic adventure novels are set in Ruritania, with its castles and royal intrigue.
The film's story unfolds in a country reminiscent of Ruritania.
Journalists often refer to small, invented countries as Ruritania when discussing diplomatic hypotheticals.

Etymology

Named after the fictional kingdom in Anthony Hope's novel 'The Prisoner of Zenda' (1894).
