uppish

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌpɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈʌpɪʃ / (BrE)
Behaving in a way that shows too much pride or arrogance; acting as though one is superior to others.
overly proud or arrogant
informal

Examples

She became rather uppish after getting promoted at work.
Some people thought his uppish attitude was off-putting.
Don't be so uppish just because you won the prize.

Synset Examples

a snotty little scion of a degenerate family
they're snobs — stuck-up and uppity and persnickety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogant - self-important, overconfident
  • Antonym: humble - showing modesty; not arrogant
  • Synonym: uppity - informal; self-important or arrogant

Etymology

From 'up' + '-ish', meaning 'with the qualities of being up' (figuratively, arrogant); first recorded in the 18th century.
