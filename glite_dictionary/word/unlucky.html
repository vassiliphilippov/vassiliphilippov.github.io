unlucky

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlʌki / (AmE), / ʌnˈlʌki / (BrE)
having or bringing bad luck; marked by misfortune
having or bringing bad luck

Examples

He considered himself unlucky when he narrowly missed the last train.
Breaking a mirror is thought to be unlucky in many cultures.
It was an unlucky day for the team when their star player was injured.

Synset Examples

Friday the 13th is an unlucky date

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lucky - having good luck or fortune

Etymology

From un- (not) + lucky, meaning 'not lucky.'

WordNet ID: oewn-01471597-a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlʌki / (AmE), / ʌnˈlʌki / (BrE)
having or experiencing bad luck; marked by or likely to bring misfortune.
having bad luck; bringing misfortune

Examples

He considered himself unlucky after missing the train by just a minute.
The unlucky team lost every match despite their hard work.
She was unlucky in love, experiencing heartbreak after heartbreak.

Synset Examples

their business venture was doomed from the start
an ill-fated business venture
an ill-starred romance
the unlucky prisoner was again put in irons

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lucky - having good fortune; bringing good luck

Etymology

From un- (not) + lucky, with 'lucky' from luck + -y.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01055109-s
Synset POS: s
