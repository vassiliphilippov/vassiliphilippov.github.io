Pterois

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrɔɪ.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɛrɔɪ.ɪs / (BrE)
A genus of venomous marine fish, commonly known as lionfish, native to the Indo-Pacific region, characterized by long, venomous spines and striking coloration.
genus of venomous lionfish

Examples

The aquarium features several species of Pterois, showcasing their majestic fins.
Divers are advised to avoid contact with Pterois due to the venom in their spines.
Researchers are studying how Pterois have rapidly expanded their range in the Atlantic Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lionfish - A common name for several species in the genus Pterois, known for their venomous spines and ornate fins

Etymology

From Greek pteron (wing, fin), referring to their wing-like fin spines.
