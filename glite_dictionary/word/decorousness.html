decorousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkərəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈdɛk(ə)rəsnəs / (BrE)
behavior that is proper, polite, and socially acceptable; propriety in manners and conduct.
propriety in manners and conduct

Examples

His decorousness at the formal dinner impressed his colleagues.
The school emphasizes the importance of decorousness in all student interactions.
She carried herself with such decorousness that she became a role model for others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decorum - propriety and good taste in behavior or appearance
  • Antonym: indecorum - improper or inappropriate behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'decorus' (becoming, seemly) + English suffix '-ness'.
