al Qaida

1. PROPN

/ ɑl kaɪˈiːdə / (AmE), / æl kaɪˈiːdə / (BrE)
A militant Islamic terrorist organization, founded by Osama bin Laden, known for orchestrating global attacks and opposing the United States and its allies.
militant Islamic terrorist organization

Examples

The United Nations imposed sanctions on al Qaida after its involvement in several international attacks.
Many governments have increased security to thwart al Qaida operations and recruitment.
The leader of al Qaida made a public statement threatening Western countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrorist group - an organization that engages in terrorism
  • Synonym: ISIS - another militant Islamic terrorist group

Etymology

From Arabic القاعدة‎ (*al-qāʕida*, meaning 'the base').
