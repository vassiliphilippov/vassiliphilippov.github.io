guesser

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛsər / (AmE), / ˈɡɛsə / (BrE)
a person who forms an opinion or answer without sufficient evidence or definite knowledge; someone who makes guesses
a person who makes guesses

Examples

As the game continued, the guesser struggled to identify the secret word.
The fastest guesser will win a prize at the end of the round.
Every team needs a skilled guesser to have a chance at winning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predictor - a person or thing that predicts what will happen in the future

Etymology

From 'guess' + '-er', an agent noun suffix.
