tailor-made

1. NOUN

/ ˌteɪlərˈmeɪd / (AmE), / ˌteɪləˈmeɪd / (BrE)
Clothing that has been made to fit a particular person; custom-made clothing.
custom-made clothing

Examples

She wore a stunning tailor-made to the wedding, which fit her perfectly.
Unlike most suits in the store, a tailor-made guarantees a unique fit.
He preferred to invest in a quality tailor-made for formal occasions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: off-the-rack - clothing that is mass-produced and not custom-fitted

Etymology

From 'tailor' (one who makes fitted clothes) and 'made', indicating something made by a tailor.

2. ADJ

/ ˌteɪlərˈmeɪd / (AmE), / ˌteɪləˈmeɪd / (BrE)
(of clothing or other items) made to fit the specific measurements or needs of an individual; custom-made.
custom-made; made to fit the individual

Examples

The suit was tailor-made for him, ensuring a perfect fit.
She prefers tailor-made dresses to store-bought ones.
A tailor-made solution was necessary to meet the client's unique needs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custom-made - made to specifications ordered by a customer; not off the rack
  • Antonym: ready-made - made in standard sizes or forms, not tailored; off-the-shelf

Etymology

Formed from 'tailor' (from Old French 'tailleur') and 'made', indicating something made by a tailor to fit.
