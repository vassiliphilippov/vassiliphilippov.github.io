valentine

1. NOUN

/ ˈvælənˌtaɪn / (AmE), / ˈvælənˌtaɪn / (BrE)
a card sent or given to someone, often as an expression of love or affection, especially on Saint Valentine's Day
card given on Saint Valentine's Day

Examples

She received a beautiful valentine from her secret admirer.
Kids often exchange valentines at school on February 14th.
He spent hours making a handmade valentine for his girlfriend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greeting card - a card, typically with a message, sent to convey good wishes

Etymology

From the name Saint Valentine, whose feast day is associated with romance and love.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvæl.ən.taɪn / (AmE), / ˈval.ən.taɪn / (BrE)
a person to whom one sends a card or greeting, or who is chosen as a sweetheart, on Saint Valentine's Day
sweetheart chosen for Valentine's Day

Examples

Will you be my valentine?
She was thrilled to receive a rose from her secret valentine.
As children, we exchanged valentines with everyone in our class.

Synset Examples

will you be my valentine?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetheart - a person loved by another, especially in a romantic relationship

Etymology

From the name Saint Valentine, an early Christian martyr associated with romantic love.
