retread

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈtrɛd / (AmE), / ˌriːˈtrɛd / (BrE)
to put a new tread on a worn tire
to give new treads to a tire

Examples

The mechanic offered to retread my old tires instead of replacing them.
Many truck companies retread their tires to save costs.
After being retreaded, the tire looked almost as good as new.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recap - to put a new tread on a tire

Etymology

From re- (again) + tread (the pattern on the surface of a tire).

2. VERB

/ riˈtrɛd / (AmE), / ˈriːtrɛd / (BrE)
to use or present again in a slightly altered or updated form, often referring to ideas, plots, or products
reuse or present again in altered form

Examples

Many movie studios retread old scripts instead of developing new stories.
The musician decided not to retread his previous hits on the new album.
Politicians often retread the same promises during every election.

Synset Examples

retread an old plot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reuse - to use something again
  • Synonym: to recycle - to use material again for the same or different purpose
  • Antonym: to innovate - to introduce something new

Etymology

From re- (again) + tread (to walk or step), originally referring to putting new tread on tires.

3. NOUN

/ riˈtrɛd / (AmE), / ˈriːtrɛd / (BrE)
a used tire that has been remolded to replace the worn tread, extending its service life
remolded used tire

Examples

The mechanic recommended a retread as a cost-effective alternative to buying a new tire.
Many trucks use retreads to reduce their overall operating expenses.
After examining the damage, she decided to install a retread on the rear axle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: new tire - a tire that has never been used before

Etymology

Formed from the prefix re- (again) and tread (the part of a tire that touches the road).

4. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌtrɛd / (AmE), / ˈriːˌtrɛd / (BrE)
a new version of something that has appeared or been done before, especially a remake or re-release of an old film or program
remake or re-release of an old work

Examples

The director's latest film is a retread of a classic comedy from the 1980s.
Many critics complained that the movie felt like a dull retread of earlier science fiction hits.
While some fans enjoy a retread, others prefer original material over remakes.

Synset Examples

The retread of classic films is popular among audiences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remake - a new version of an earlier film or work
  • Antonym: original - the first or earliest version of something, not a remake or copy

Etymology

Originally from re- (again) + tread (walk on), referring to replacing the tread on a tire; later adapted figuratively for repeated or recycled things.
