to dine

1. VERB

/ daɪn / (AmE), / daɪn / (BrE)
to eat dinner or the main meal of the day, especially in a formal or social setting
to eat dinner, often formally

Examples

They decided to dine at a new Italian restaurant downtown last night.
We often dine with friends on weekends to catch up and enjoy good company.
She prefers to dine early in the evening before attending the theater.

Synset Examples

We often dine with friends in this restaurant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eat - to consume food

Etymology

From Old French 'disner', meaning to have a meal, ultimately from Latin 'disjejunare' (to break a fast).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01170315-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ daɪn / (AmE), / daɪn / (BrE)
to entertain someone with a dinner; to act as host for a dinner
to host or give a dinner for someone

Examples

She loves to dine important clients at her favorite restaurant.
Last weekend, he dined his colleagues at his home to celebrate the project's success.
They often dine friends and family during the holidays.

Synset Examples

I'm wining and dining my friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entertain - to have someone as a guest, especially for a meal

Etymology

From Old French *disner* (to dine), from Vulgar Latin *disjejunare* (to break one's fast).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01170114-v
Synset POS: v
