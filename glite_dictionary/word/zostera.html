Zostera

1. NOUN

/ zoʊˈstɛrə / (AmE), / zɒsˈtɪərə / (BrE)
A small genus of widely distributed marine grasses, commonly known as eelgrass, found in coastal waters.
a genus of marine grasses (eelgrass)

Examples

Marine biologists are studying Zostera beds to understand their role in coastal ecosystems.
Dense meadows of Zostera provide shelter for many juvenile fish species.
The decline of Zostera populations has caused concern among environmentalists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eelgrass - common name for Zostera

Etymology

From Greek *zōstḗr* (a belt, girdle), referring to the ribbon-like leaves.
