capsicum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpsɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈkæpsɪkəm / (BrE)
Any of various tropical plants of the genus Capsicum, including sweet and hot peppers.
plant genus including sweet and hot peppers

Examples

Many recipes call for capsicum as a key ingredient to add flavour and colour to the dish.
The tropical climate is ideal for growing capsicum in abundance throughout the year.
She prefers diced capsicum on her pizza instead of olives or mushrooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pepper - the fruit of plants of the genus Capsicum; often refers to bell or chili pepper

Etymology

From Latin 'capsicum', from 'capsa' (box), alluding to the shape of the fruit.
