alterability

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔltɚəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɔːltərəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being capable of change or alteration
capacity to be altered

Examples

The alterability of digital files makes them easy to update or revise.
Some legal contracts are intentionally designed to have a low degree of alterability.
Genetic alterability is a key factor in the evolution of species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alterable - able to be changed or modified
  • Antonym: immutability - the quality of being unchangeable

Etymology

From 'alterable' + '-ity', modeled after similar English formations; ultimately from Latin 'alterare' (to change).
