exhibit

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɪbɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪbɪt / (BrE)
an object or collection of objects displayed for the public, especially in a museum, gallery, or exhibition
object displayed publicly

Examples

The museum's new exhibit attracts thousands of visitors every month.
Each exhibit in the science fair demonstrated a unique experiment.
The dinosaur skeleton is the most popular exhibit at the natural history museum.

Synset Examples

the museum had many exhibits of oriental art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: display - an arrangement of things for public showing
  • Synonym: presentation - the act of showing or displaying something
  • Antonym: concealment - the action of hiding or keeping something secret

Etymology

From Latin exhibitus, past participle of exhibere 'to hold out, display'.

WordNet ID: oewn-00522265-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzɪbɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪbɪt / (BrE)
to show something publicly, especially so that it can be examined or appreciated
show or display publicly

Examples

The museum will exhibit ancient pottery from Greece next summer.
He began to exhibit signs of nervousness during the interview.
Several local artists exhibited their work at the gallery opening.

Synset Examples

Why don't you show your nice legs and wear shorter skirts?
National leaders will have to display the highest skills of statesmanship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: display - to show something so it can be seen by others
  • Antonym: to conceal - to hide something from view or keep it secret

Etymology

From Latin exhibere, meaning 'to hold out' or 'to display'.

display or present publicly

4. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɪbɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪbɪt / (BrE)
an object or document produced and identified in a court of law as evidence during a trial or hearing
object or document used as court evidence
legal

Examples

The prosecutor presented the murder weapon as exhibit A.
Each exhibit must be clearly labeled and entered into the court record.
The defense attorney argued that the photograph was an inadmissible exhibit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evidence - information or objects presented to prove something in court

Etymology

From Latin *exhibitus*, past participle of *exhibere* (to hold out, display).
