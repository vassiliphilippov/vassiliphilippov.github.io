Wakashan

1. NOUN

/ wəˈkɑːʃən / (AmE), / wəˈkæʃən / (BrE)
a family of indigenous languages spoken by peoples of British Columbia and Washington
Northwest North American language family

Examples

Researchers have documented several endangered Wakashan languages in Canada.
The Wakashan language family includes Nuu-chah-nulth and Makah, among others.
He specializes in the study of Wakashan phonology and syntax.

Etymology

From 'Wakash,' the Nootka (Nuu-chah-nulth) word for 'good', with -an as an English suffix.

2. NOUN

/ wɑːˈkɑːʃən / (AmE), / wəˈkɑːʃən / (BrE)
a member of any of the Indigenous peoples of British Columbia and Washington State who speak or historically spoke the Wakashan languages
member of Indigenous Wakashan-speaking peoples

Examples

He is a proud Wakashan with deep roots in the region.
Several Wakashan tribes maintain their cultural traditions along the Pacific coast.
The museum exhibits artifacts from early Wakashan settlements.

Etymology

From the Wakashan languages, from Nuuchahnulth 'wakaš' meaning 'good'.
