Matthew

1. PROPN

/ ˈmæθju / (AmE), / ˈmæθjuː / (BrE)
the first book of the New Testament, one of the four Gospels, traditionally attributed to the Apostle Matthew and including the Sermon on the Mount
first Gospel in the New Testament

Examples

Many of Jesus' teachings can be found in the Gospel of Matthew.
The Sermon on the Mount appears in Matthew chapters 5 to 7.
Biblical scholars often analyze passages from Matthew to understand early Christian beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mark - the second Gospel in the New Testament
  • Synonym: Luke - the third Gospel in the New Testament
  • Synonym: John - the fourth Gospel in the New Testament

Etymology

From the Greek 'Maththaios', from Hebrew 'Mattityahu' meaning 'gift of Yahweh'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈmæθ.ju / (AmE), / ˈmæθ.juː / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) one of Jesus's twelve apostles, traditionally regarded as the author of the first Gospel.
one of Jesus's apostles; Gospel author

Examples

The Gospel according to Matthew is the first book of the New Testament.
Matthew is often depicted with a winged man or angel as his symbol.
According to tradition, Matthew was a former tax collector before becoming an apostle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mark - another Evangelist of the New Testament Gospels
  • Synonym: Luke - another Evangelist of the New Testament Gospels
  • Synonym: John - another Evangelist of the New Testament Gospels

Etymology

From Hebrew Mattityahu (gift of Yahweh), via Greek Matthaios and Latin Matthaeus.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11184661-n
Synset POS: n
