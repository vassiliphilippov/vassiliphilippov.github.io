sideswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdzˌwʊm.ən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdzˌwʊm.ən / (BrE)
(in the Church of England) a female assistant to the churchwarden, responsible for collecting offerings of money during church services.
female church officer collecting offerings

Examples

The parish appointed a new sideswoman to help with the collection on Sundays.
As a sideswoman, she greeted parishioners and distributed hymn books.
During the service, the sideswoman passed the collection plate down each pew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sidesman - male assistant to the churchwarden

Etymology

Formed from 'side' (as in 'sidesman', an assistant to a churchwarden) + 'woman'.
