liveness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being alive or animated, especially as distinguished from inanimate or plant life
the state of being alive or animated

Examples

Scientists debated how to determine the liveness of cells under the microscope.
The philosopher discussed the distinction between the liveness of animals and the existence of plants.
Some tests can help assess the liveness of tissues in biological research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aliveness - the quality or state of being alive
  • Antonym: deadness - the state of being dead or lacking vitality

Etymology

From live + -ness, formed on the base of 'live' (to be alive).
