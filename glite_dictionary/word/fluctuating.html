fluctuating

1. ADJ

/ ˈflʌktʃuˌeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflʌktjʊeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
changing frequently and unpredictably, especially rising and falling irregularly.
changing unpredictably; rising and falling

Examples

The fluctuating temperature made it hard to dress appropriately each morning.
Investors are concerned about the fluctuating value of their portfolios.
Electricity demand is often fluctuating throughout the day, depending on usage patterns.

Synset Examples

fluctuating prices

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stable - not likely to change, firmly fixed

Etymology

From Latin 'fluctuare', meaning 'to wave' or 'to fluctuate'.
