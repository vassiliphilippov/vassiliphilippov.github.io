Hyperodontidae

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌpɛroʊˈdɒntɪdiː / (AmE), / hʌɪˌpɛrəʊˈdɒntɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of beaked whales, sometimes previously classified within the Physeteridae family.
family of beaked whales

Examples

The Hyperodontidae are a family of deep-diving beaked whales.
Scientists debated whether Hyperodontidae should remain distinct from Physeteridae.
Recent research has clarified the evolutionary relationship of Hyperodontidae to other toothed whales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Physeteridae - family of sperm whales

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (over, above) + 'odous' (tooth) + '-idae' (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpəroʊˈdɒntɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpərəʊˈdɒntɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of beaked whales, sometimes formerly included in Physeteridae, comprising certain genera of deep-diving toothed whales.
a family of beaked whales

Examples

The Hyperodontidae are known for their distinctive beaked snouts and deep-diving behavior.
Recent research has improved our understanding of the evolutionary history of the Hyperodontidae.
Scientists debate whether the Hyperodontidae should be classified separately from the family Physeteridae.

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Greek 'hyperōdous' (with prominent teeth) and taxonomic suffix '-idae'.
