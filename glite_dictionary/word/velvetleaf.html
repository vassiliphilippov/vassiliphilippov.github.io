velvetleaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛlvətˌlif / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvɪtˌliːf / (BrE)
A shrub or perennial plant with soft, furry, palmately lobed leaves and showy red-purple flowers, native to the southwestern United States.
shrub with furry leaves and red-purple flowers

Examples

The velvetleaf in her garden blooms beautifully every spring.
Botanists are studying how the velvetleaf adapts to arid conditions.
Its soft, furry leaves make the velvetleaf easy to recognize in the wild.

Etymology

From 'velvet' (soft fabric) + 'leaf', describing the plant's velvety leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛlvətˌlif / (AmE), / ˈvɛlvɪtˌliːf / (BrE)
a tall annual herb or subshrub (Abutilon theophrasti) with soft, velvety leaves and yellow flowers, native to Asia and cultivated for its fiber; often considered a weed in other regions
tall annual herb with velvety leaves

Examples

The large, soft leaves of velvetleaf make it easy to identify in the field.
Farmers often consider velvetleaf a troublesome weed in their corn and soybean crops.
The fiber from velvetleaf has been used traditionally to make rope and textiles.

Etymology

From the words 'velvet' (soft fabric) and 'leaf', referring to the plant's soft leaves.
