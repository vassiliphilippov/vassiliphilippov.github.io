Rhexia

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛksiə / (AmE), / ˈrɛksiə / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the family Melastomataceae, commonly known as meadow beauties or deer grass, native to North America
a genus of North American flowering plants

Examples

The wetlands were dotted with Rhexia, their pink flowers brightening the summer landscape.
Many gardeners appreciate Rhexia for its tolerance of moist, sandy soils.
Botanists identified several species of Rhexia during their field study.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin *Rhexia*, the genus name given in Linnaean taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛksiə / (AmE), / ˈriːksɪə / (BrE)
a genus of herbaceous flowering plants, commonly known as deer grass, found in wet areas of eastern North America
a genus of flowering plants (deer grass)

Examples

The wetland was covered in clusters of Rhexia, their purple flowers bright against the grass.
Botanists studied the unique root system of Rhexia species endemic to the region.
Gardeners appreciate Rhexia for its attractive blooms and ability to thrive in moist soils.

Etymology

From Greek rhexia, possibly relating to 'to break,' referring to the brittle stems.
