Bivalvia

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈvæl.vi.ə / (AmE), / baɪˈval.vɪ.ə / (BrE)
A large class of aquatic mollusks, including oysters, clams, scallops, and mussels, characterized by a shell made of two hinged parts.
class of mollusks with two-part shells

Examples

Many commercially important shellfish, such as oysters and clams, are members of the group Bivalvia.
The shells of Bivalvia typically consist of two symmetrical halves joined by a flexible ligament.
Marine biologists study Bivalvia to learn more about aquatic ecosystems and biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bivalve - an animal belonging to Bivalvia, having a two-part shell
  • Antonym: gastropod - a mollusk of the class Gastropoda, such as snails and slugs

Etymology

From Latin bivalvia ('two valves'), from bi- ('two') + valvae ('leaves of a door').

2. NOUN

/ baɪˈvæl.vi.ə / (AmE), / baɪˈval.vi.ə / (BrE)
a class of marine and freshwater molluscs with two hinged shells, including oysters, clams, scallops, and mussels
class of molluscs with two shells

Examples

The study focused on the ecological role of Bivalvia in the freshwater ecosystem.
Oysters and mussels belong to the class Bivalvia.
Researchers have discovered several new species within Bivalvia in recent years.

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'valvae' (leaves of a door, folding doors).
