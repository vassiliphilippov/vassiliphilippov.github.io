shipyard

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪpjɑrd / (AmE), / ˈʃɪpjɑːd / (BrE)
A place where ships are constructed or repaired.
place where ships are built or fixed

Examples

The new cruise liner was constructed at the famous shipyard near the harbor.
Many workers were laid off when the city’s only shipyard closed down last year.
The shipyard employs engineers, welders, and carpenters to maintain the navy’s vessels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dockyard - a place where ships are built or repaired, often interchangeable with 'shipyard'

Etymology

Formed from 'ship' + 'yard' (enclosure or area).
