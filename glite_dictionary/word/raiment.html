raiment

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪmənt / (AmE), / ˈreɪm(ə)nt / (BrE)
clothing, especially fine or decorative garments, often used in literary or formal contexts.
fine or decorative clothing
archaic

Examples

The queen appeared before her subjects in splendid raiment adorned with jewels.
He exchanged his humble clothes for the ceremonial raiment of his office.
Literary descriptions often refer to silken raiment shimmering in the candlelight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attire - clothes, especially formal or elegant ones
  • Synonym: garment - an item of clothing

Etymology

From Old French 'raiment', variant of 'arrayment', from 'areyer' (to arrange).

2. VERB

/ ˈreɪmənt / (AmE), / ˈreɪm(ə)nt / (BrE)
(Archaic) To dress or clothe someone.
to dress, to clothe (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In the old tales, the queen raimented her daughter in garments of silk and gold.
The servants raimented the guests before the royal feast began.
He raiments himself in fine robes for every ceremonial occasion.

Synset Examples

Parents must feed and dress their child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress - to clothe; to put clothes on someone
  • Antonym: undress - to remove clothing from someone; to strip

Etymology

From Old French 'raiment', variant of 'arrayment', from 'areyer' (to array).

Synset POS: v
