Agropyron trachycaulum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡrəˈpaɪɹɑn ˌtrækiˈkɔːləm / (AmE), / ˌæɡrəˈpaɪrɒn ˌtrækɪˈkɔːləm / (BrE)
A species of North American grass grown in the western United States as a valuable forage crop.
species of North American forage grass

Examples

Farmers in the region planted Agropyron trachycaulum as a reliable source of high-quality pasture for cattle.
The study examined how Agropyron trachycaulum performs compared to other native grasses under drought conditions.
Seeds of Agropyron trachycaulum are often sown to restore overgrazed rangelands in the western United States.

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Agropyron' from Greek 'agros' (field) + 'pyros' (wheat); 'trachycaulum' from Greek 'trachys' (rough) + 'kaulos' (stem).
