Ler

1. PROPN

/ lɜr / (AmE), / lɜː / (BrE)
A god of the sea in Irish mythology, regarded as the father of Manannan; corresponds to Welsh Llyr.
Irish sea god; father of Manannan

Examples

According to legend, Ler ruled over the vast and unpredictable Irish sea.
The myth of Ler and his son Manannan is central to early Irish folklore.
Some scholars draw parallels between Ler and similar deities in neighboring Celtic cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Llyr - Welsh equivalent of Ler, sea god in Welsh mythology

Etymology

From Old Irish *Ler* (“the sea”), related to Welsh *Llyr*.
