fragment

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræɡmənt / (AmE), / ˈfræɡmənt / (BrE)
a small piece broken off or detached from something, especially a brittle object
broken piece of something brittle

Examples

The archaeologists discovered a fragment of ancient pottery among the ruins.
She kept a fragment of the broken vase as a memento.
The window shattered, sending fragments of glass across the floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shard - a sharp broken piece, especially of glass, metal, or pottery
  • Antonym: whole - something complete; not broken into pieces

Etymology

From Latin fragmentum (a piece broken off, fragment), from frangere (to break).

2. VERB

/ ˈfræɡˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈfræɡ.mənt / (BrE)
to break or cause to break into small pieces or fragments
break into small pieces

Examples

Over the years, the ancient statue began to fragment as pieces fell away.
The glass fragmented on impact with the floor, scattering across the kitchen.
Experts warned that the proposal could fragment the organization into rival groups.

Synset Examples

The plate fragmented

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - to separate into pieces as a result of a blow or strain
  • Synonym: to shatter - to break suddenly into many small pieces

Etymology

From Latin fragmentum (a piece broken off).
