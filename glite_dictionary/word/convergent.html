convergent

1. ADJ

/ kənˈvɜrdʒənt / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
tending to come together from different directions; moving toward a common point or result.
tending to come together

Examples

The two rivers are convergent near the foot of the mountain, eventually forming a single stream.
Their convergent ideas led to a highly successful collaboration on the project.
The convergent paths of the migrating animals ensure they all gather at the waterhole.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divergent - tending to move apart in different directions
  • Synonym: to converge - to come together from different directions and meet

Etymology

From Latin convergere (to incline together), from com- (together) + vergere (to bend, incline).
