goldfinch

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldˌfɪntʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldˌfɪntʃ / (BrE)
a small American finch, the male of which has bright yellow body plumage during the summer
small yellow-plumaged American finch

Examples

Every summer, a bright yellow goldfinch visits our backyard garden.
The male goldfinch is easily identified by his vibrant plumage.
We spotted a flock of goldfinches feeding on thistle seeds.

Etymology

From gold + finch; finch from Old English 'finc'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldˌfɪntʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldˌfɪntʃ / (BrE)
a small European songbird with a red face and yellow-and-black wings, belonging to the finch family
small European songbird with red face and yellow wings

Examples

A bright goldfinch perched on the garden fence, its yellow wings catching the sunlight.
We spotted a group of goldfinches feeding among the wildflowers.
The distinctive song of the goldfinch can often be heard in rural meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finch - a small songbird with a short, thick bill

Etymology

Old English 'goldfinc', from gold (gold) + finc (finch).
