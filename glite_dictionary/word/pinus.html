Pinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpiː.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of coniferous trees comprising the true pines, widely distributed in the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of true pines (coniferous trees)

Examples

The genus Pinus includes over a hundred species of pine trees found across the world.
Forests dominated by Pinus species are important for timber and wildlife habitat.
Botanists studied the needle morphology of Pinus to classify different species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - a coniferous tree with needle-shaped leaves, source of pine cones and wood

Etymology

From Latin 'pinus' meaning 'pine tree'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
The genus of evergreen coniferous trees known as true pines, belonging to the family Pinaceae.
genus of pine trees
medical

Examples

Pinus includes many familiar pine trees found in forests around the world.
The needles and cones of Pinus species are characteristic features for identification.
Some species within the Pinus genus are important for commercial timber production.

Etymology

From Latin 'pinus' (pine tree).
