xanthomonad

1. NOUN

/ zæn.θəˈmoʊ.næd / (AmE), / zæn.θəˈməʊ.næd / (BrE)
A bacterium of a group that produces yellow, non-water-soluble pigments, some species of which are pathogenic to plants.
bacterium producing yellow pigment, some plant pathogens
medical

Examples

Xanthomonad infections can cause significant losses in tomato crops each year.
Scientists discovered a new xanthomonad species on the leaves of the infected pepper plants.
Proper sanitation is essential to control the spread of xanthomonads in agricultural fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacterium - a microscopic single-celled organism

Etymology

From Greek 'xanthos' (yellow) and 'monas' (unit).
