Hieracium praealtum

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈɛriəkiəm preɪˈæltəm / (AmE), / hɪəˈræsiəm priːˈæltəm / (BrE)
A European species of hawkweed, sometimes introduced into northeastern United States, where it can become a troublesome weed.
species of European hawkweed

Examples

Gardeners sometimes struggle to remove Hieracium praealtum from their lawns.
The spread of Hieracium praealtum in local meadows has caused concern among conservationists.
Many wildflowers compete poorly with invasive species like Hieracium praealtum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawkweed - common name for plants of the family Hieracium

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Hieracium' and species epithet 'praealtum' meaning 'very tall'.
