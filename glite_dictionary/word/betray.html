to betray

1. VERB

/ bɪˈtreɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈtreɪ / (BrE)
to unintentionally reveal (a feeling, secret, or fact) through words, actions, or appearance
unintentionally reveal something hidden

Examples

Her nervous glance betrayed her anxiety during the interview.
The shaky voice betrayed the fear he tried to hide.
Although he tried to act calm, his hands betrayed his excitement.

Synset Examples

Her smile betrayed her true feelings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disclose - to make something known
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something hidden

Etymology

From Old French trahir, from Latin tradere (to hand over, deliver, betray).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00938362-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bɪˈtreɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈtreɪ / (BrE)
to be sexually unfaithful to one's partner in marriage or a committed relationship
be sexually unfaithful to a partner

Examples

She was devastated to learn that he would betray her trust after so many years of marriage.
He promised never to betray his wife, no matter the temptation.
When she discovered he had betrayed her, their relationship was never the same again.

Synset Examples

She cheats on her husband
Might her husband be wandering?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - to be sexually unfaithful to one's partner
  • Antonym: be faithful - to remain loyal and not be sexually unfaithful to one's partner

Etymology

From Old French *betrayer*, from be- (intensive) + *trahir* (to betray).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02582588-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ bɪˈtreɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈtreɪ / (BrE)
to disappoint or abandon someone, especially when they rely on you; to prove undependable in a crucial moment
fail or abandon someone

Examples

She felt that her friends had betrayed her when she needed them most.
During the difficult times, his courage betrayed him and he could not act.
When the team betrayed their coach by giving up, everyone was disappointed.

Synset Examples

His sense of smell failed him this time
His strength finally failed him
His children failed him in the crisis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fail - to not do what is expected or needed, to disappoint someone
  • Antonym: support - to give help or encouragement, especially in a difficult situation

Etymology

From Old French 'betrayre', from Latin 'tradere' (to hand over, deliver up).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01803246-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ bɪˈtreɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈtreɪ / (BrE)
to reveal confidential or harmful information about someone, often violating their trust or confidence
to reveal someone's secrets or information

Examples

She would never betray her friends by sharing their secrets.
The captured spy refused to betray his comrades despite intense interrogation.
He didn't mean to betray her trust, but the secret slipped out during their conversation.

Synset Examples

He told on his classmate who had cheated on the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inform - to tell someone in authority about another's wrongdoing
  • Antonym: to protect - to keep someone safe from harm or betrayal

Etymology

From Old French 'betrar', from be- + 'trair' (to betray), from Latin 'tradere' (to give up, hand over).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00843665-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ bɪˈtreɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈtreɪ / (BrE)
to deliver someone or something to an enemy by acting treacherously or disloyally
deliver to an enemy by treachery

Examples

She was shocked when her friend betrayed her to the occupying forces.
During the war, several officers betrayed confidential information to the enemy.
He feared that someone in the group would betray them if they were captured.

Synset Examples

Judas sold Jesus
The spy betrayed his country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sell out - to betray someone or something for personal gain
  • Antonym: be loyal - to remain faithful and not betray someone

Etymology

From Old French *betrar* (to deceive), from be- + *trair* (to betray), from Latin *tradere* (to hand over).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
