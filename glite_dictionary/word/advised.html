advised

1. ADJ

/ ədˈvaɪzd / (AmE), / ədˈvaɪzd / (BrE)
(of an action or decision) made or undertaken after careful consideration or advice; wise or prudent.
considered or prudent (of decisions)

Examples

The company made an advised decision to postpone the launch until all tests were completed.
It would have been more advised to consult an expert before proceeding.
Only after an advised review of all options did they choose this route.

Synset Examples

a well-advised delay in carrying out the plan

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ill-advised - not showing good judgment; unwise or imprudent
  • Synonym: prudent - acting with or showing care and thought for the future

Etymology

Past participle of 'advise', from Old French 'aviser', based on Latin 'advisare'.

2. ADJ

/ ədˈvaɪzd / (AmE), / ədˈvaɪzd / (BrE)
having been informed or notified about something; aware due to receiving advice or information.
informed; notified

Examples

Please keep me advised of any new developments.
All relevant parties were advised before the meeting began.
You will be advised of the results once the review is complete.

Synset Examples

be kept advised

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informed - having knowledge or information about something
  • Antonym: unadvised - not having received advice or information

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'advise', from Old French 'aviser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01309922-s
Synset POS: s
