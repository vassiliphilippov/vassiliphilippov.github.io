veneer

1. NOUN

/ vəˈnɪr / (AmE), / vəˈnɪə / (BrE)
A thin layer of fine wood glued to the surface of a less expensive material, used especially in furniture making.
thin wood layer for surfaces

Examples

The antique desk was restored by applying a new veneer over the damaged surface.
Beneath its shiny veneer, the table was actually made of cheap plywood.
Some cabinets use a cherry veneer to give the appearance of solid wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laminate - material made by bonding layers together
  • Synonym: inlay - a decoration set into the surface of wood

Etymology

From German 'Furnier', from French 'fournir' (to furnish).

2. NOUN

/ vəˈnɪr / (AmE), / vəˈnɪə / (BrE)
a thin decorative layer of fine material, such as wood or stone, applied to the surface of a building or structure to improve its appearance.
decorative surface layer on a building

Examples

The marble veneer gave the lobby an elegant and luxurious appearance.
Architects often use wood veneer to create a warm, upscale look in modern buildings.
The old façade was covered with a stone veneer to blend in with the surrounding architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cladding - an outer covering or coating on a building

Etymology

From German *Furnier*, from French *fournir* (to furnish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03320935-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ vəˈnɪr / (AmE), / vəˈnɪə / (BrE)
to cover (a surface or an object) with a thin layer of fine material, usually wood, to improve its appearance or protect it
to cover with a thin layer, usually wood

Examples

They decided to veneer the old table with cherry wood for a modern look.
The cabinetmaker veneered the shelves to give them a polished finish.
Skilled craftsmen often veneer doors and panels rather than using solid hardwood.

Synset Examples

veneer the furniture to protect it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlay - to cover the surface of something with a layer of a different material

Etymology

From German 'Furnier', from French 'fournir' (to furnish, supply).

4. NOUN

/ vəˈnɪr / (AmE), / vəˈnɪə / (BrE)
a deceptively attractive outer appearance or display that hides the true nature or reality of something
superficial appearance concealing reality

Examples

The politician's promises were just a veneer for his real intentions.
Beneath the veneer of wealth and luxury, the family struggled with many problems.
Her smile served as a veneer for the anxiety she truly felt.

Synset Examples

His charm was just a veneer for his true self.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: façade - an outward appearance that is maintained to conceal a less pleasant reality
  • Synonym: mask - a cover or disguise to hide the true nature of something
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From German 'Furnier' (veneer), from furnieren (to veneer), from French 'fournir' (to furnish).
