to shanghai

1. VERB

/ ˈʃæŋ.haɪ / (AmE), / ʃæŋˈhaɪ / (BrE)
to force someone to join a ship's crew or perform work against their will, often by trickery or coercion
forcibly enlist or conscript, esp. for ships

Examples

In the 19th century, criminals would often shanghai unsuspecting men to work aboard ships.
He claimed that he had been shanghaied while walking near the docks late at night.
They tried to shanghai laborers to staff their remote logging camp.

Synset Examples

The men were shanghaied after being drugged

Etymology

From the city name Shanghai, reflecting its association with the practice of forcibly recruiting sailors for voyages to or from Shanghai in the 19th century.
