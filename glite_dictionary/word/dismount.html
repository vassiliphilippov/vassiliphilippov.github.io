dismount

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪsˌmaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.maʊnt / (BrE)
the act of getting off a horse, bicycle, or similar vehicle
the act of getting off a horse or bike

Examples

Her elegant dismount from the horse impressed the judges.
He took a quick dismount after reaching the finish line on his bike.
The gymnast received high marks for her flawless dismount.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mount - the act of getting on a horse, bicycle, etc.

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'dismount', itself from Old French 'desmonter', from 'des-' (dis-) + 'monter' (to mount)

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈmaʊnt / (AmE), / dɪsˈmaʊnt / (BrE)
to get off or alight from a horse, bicycle, or other vehicle
get off a horse or vehicle

Examples

She carefully dismounted after the long ride through the forest.
The police officer signaled for the cyclist to dismount and show identification.
After the lesson, the students lined up to dismount from their ponies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mount - to get onto a horse or vehicle

Etymology

From Old French *desmonter*, from Latin *dis-* (off) + *montare* (to mount).
