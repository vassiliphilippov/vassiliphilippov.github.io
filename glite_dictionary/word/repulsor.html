repulsor

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpʌlsər / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌlsə / (BrE)
A fictional anti-gravity device used for levitation or propulsion, especially in the Star Wars universe.
fictional anti-gravity device (Star Wars)

Examples

In the Star Wars films, landspeeders hover above the ground using repulsor technology.
The Millennium Falcon relies on powerful repulsors for planetary takeoff and landing.
Engineers in the galaxy often tinker with repulsor systems to improve vehicle performance.

Etymology

Formed from 'repulse' (to drive back) + '-or', inspired by science fiction naming conventions.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈpʌl.sɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌl.sə / (BrE)
A fictional energy device, often mounted in the gauntlets of Iron Man's armor, used to project force beams or charged particle blasts.
energy device in Iron Man's armor

Examples

Tony Stark designed the repulsor units for both propulsion and to fire powerful energy blasts.
The villain dodged a repulsor beam during the intense fight scene.
Fans love to see Iron Man using his repulsors to repel enemies.

Synset Examples

In the 2008 movie, the repulsors are a form of propulsion and (as hand units) steering jet, though they can be used offensively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blaster - a gunlike fictional weapon that emits energy or particles

Etymology

Formed from 'repulse' + '-or', indicating something that repels or pushes away.
