Clangula hyemalis

1. NOUN

/ klæŋˈɡjuːlə haɪˈɛməlɪs / (AmE), / klaŋˈɡjuːlə hɪˈɛməlɪs / (BrE)
A species of long-tailed sea duck native to northern parts of the United States and the Arctic.
species of long-tailed sea duck

Examples

The Clangula hyemalis is often seen in large flocks along Arctic coastlines during the summer.
Unlike many ducks, Clangula hyemalis dives deep underwater to forage for food.
Bird watchers were delighted to spot a rare Clangula hyemalis in the harbor this winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long-tailed duck - A common name for Clangula hyemalis, a northern sea duck species

Etymology

From Latin: 'Clangula' (a genus of ducks) and 'hyemalis' (of winter), referring to its Arctic breeding.
