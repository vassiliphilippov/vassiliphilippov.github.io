sulfurous

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌlfəɹəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfərəs / (BrE)
relating to or containing sulfur, or derived from sulfur
relating to or containing sulfur

Examples

The volcano released a sulfurous gas that filled the air with a pungent odor.
The sulfurous smell coming from the hot springs was unmistakable.
Scientists analyzed the sulfurous compounds found in the mineral samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfuric - relating to or containing sulfur (US spelling often refers to "sulfuric acid")
  • Synonym: sulfide - compound containing sulfur and another element

Etymology

From Latin *sulphurosus* (full of sulfur), from *sulphur* (sulfur).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsʌlfɚəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfərəs / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) harsh, caustic, or bitter in tone or language.
harsh or caustic in tone

Examples

The critic's sulfurous review left the new play in tatters.
She was known for her sulfurous outbursts in meetings, leaving her colleagues uneasy.
His sulfurous denunciation of corruption made headlines across the city.

Synset Examples

an acerbic tone piercing otherwise flowery prose
a barrage of acid comments

bitter words
blistering criticism
caustic jokes about political assassination, talk-show hosts and medical ethics
a sulfurous denunciation
a vitriolic critique

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caustic - sarcastic in a scathing and bitter way
  • Synonym: vitriolic - filled with bitter criticism or malice
  • Antonym: gentle - having a mild or kind tone

Etymology

From Latin 'sulphurosus', from 'sulphur' (sulfur).

3. ADJ

/ ˈsʌlfɚəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfjʊərəs / (BrE)
(of air or atmosphere) characterized by oppressive heat and humidity.
oppressively hot and humid (air or atmosphere)

Examples

The air outside was sulfurous and heavy, making it difficult to breathe.
A sulfurous haze settled over the city after the afternoon thunderstorm.
We struggled through the sulfurous heat of midsummer as we hiked up the hill.

Synset Examples

the summer was sultry and oppressive
the stifling atmosphere
the sulfurous atmosphere preceding a thunderstorm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sultry - (of air or weather) hot and humid; oppressive
  • Synonym: stifling - (of air or atmosphere) very hot and making breathing difficult

Etymology

From Latin 'sulphurosus', from 'sulfur' (brimstone).
