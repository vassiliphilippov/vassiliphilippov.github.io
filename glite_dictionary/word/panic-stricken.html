panic-stricken

1. ADJ

/ ˈpænɪk ˌstrɪkən / (AmE), / ˈpænɪk ˌstrɪkən / (BrE)
Suddenly overcome by intense fear, anxiety, or desperation, often leading to irrational or frenzied behavior.
overcome by intense fear or panic

Examples

The panic-stricken passengers rushed to the exits when the alarm sounded.
She gave a panic-stricken glance at the clock, realizing she was late for her meeting.
Firefighters struggled to control the panic-stricken crowd outside the burning building.

Synset Examples

became panicky as the snow deepened
felt panicked before each exam
trying to keep back the panic-stricken crowd
the terrified horse bolted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panicked - Suddenly seized with panic or fear
  • Antonym: calm - Not showing or feeling nervousness or fear

Etymology

From 'panic' (Greek panikos, relating to sudden fear) + 'stricken' (from Old English strican: to strike).
