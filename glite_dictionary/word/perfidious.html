perfidious

1. ADJ

/ pərˈfɪdiəs / (AmE), / pəˈfɪdɪəs / (BrE)
deceitful and untrustworthy; willing to betray trust or confidence.
deceitful; betraying trust

Examples

He was denounced as a perfidious traitor after revealing the secret plans.
Her perfidious actions shattered our trust beyond repair.
History remembers the perfidious alliance that collapsed in betrayal.

Synset Examples

Punic faith
the perfidious Judas
the fiercest and most treacherous of foes
treacherous intrigues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treacherous - guilty of or involving betrayal or deception
  • Antonym: loyal - showing firm and constant support or allegiance

Etymology

From Latin perfidus (faithless), from per- (through) + fides (faith).
