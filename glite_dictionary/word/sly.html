sly

1. ADJ

/ slaɪ / (AmE), / slaɪ / (BrE)
Clever at achieving one's aims by indirect or deceitful methods; cunning or crafty, often in a secretive way.
cleverly deceptive or cunning

Examples

He gave me a sly wink as he slipped the answer under the table.
The sly fox outsmarted all the farmers and stole their chickens.
There's something sly about the way she avoids answering questions directly.

Synset Examples

cunning men often pass for wise
a foxy scheme
a slick evasive answer
sly as a fox
tricky Dick
a wily old attorney

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - skilled at achieving one's ends by deceit or evasion
  • Synonym: wily - skilled at gaining an advantage, especially deceitfully
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Old Norse slœgr (cunning, crafty).
