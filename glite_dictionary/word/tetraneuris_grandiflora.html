Tetraneuris grandiflora

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˈnʊrɪs ˌɡrændɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈnjʊərɪs ˌɡrændɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A species of whitish hairy plant with featherlike leaves, stout stems, and large yellow flower heads, native to mountainous regions of the north-central United States.
species of flowering plant in North America

Examples

The wild slopes were dotted with Tetraneuris grandiflora in full bloom, attracting numerous pollinators.
Botanists studied Tetraneuris grandiflora to learn more about native mountain plants.
You can identify Tetraneuris grandiflora by its solitary yellow flower heads and feathery leaves.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Tetraneuris' and species epithet 'grandiflora' meaning 'large-flowered'.
