abolition

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbəˈlɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæbəˈlɪʃən / (BrE)
The formal ending or elimination of a system, practice, or institution, especially by law; for example, the abolition of slavery.
the act of ending a system or practice

Examples

The abolition of the death penalty was a major milestone for human rights in the country.
After years of campaigning, the government finally announced the abolition of the outdated tax.
The abolition of slavery transformed societies across the world.

Synset Examples

the abolition of capital punishment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elimination - the act of removing or getting rid of something
  • Antonym: establishment - the act of starting or instituting something

Etymology

From Latin abolitio (a removal), from abolere (to destroy).
