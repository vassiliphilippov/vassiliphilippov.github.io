bedpost

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛdˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈbɛdˌpəʊst / (BrE)
one of the four upright supports at the corners of a bedstead, often extending above the mattress
vertical corner support of a bed

Examples

She hung her scarf over the bedpost before going to sleep.
The antique bed featured ornate carvings on each bedpost.
He accidentally bumped his knee against the bedpost in the dark.

Etymology

From 'bed' and 'post,' referring to upright supports; attested since the 18th century.
