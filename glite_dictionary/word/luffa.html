luffa

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌfə / (AmE), / ˈlʌfə / (BrE)
The dried fibrous part of the fruit of a plant of the genus Luffa, used as a natural sponge or strainer, especially for washing the body.
dried fruit fiber used as sponge

Examples

She used a luffa to scrub her arms and back in the shower.
Natural luffa sponges are often preferred over synthetic ones for exfoliating the skin.
After removing the seeds, the dried luffa can be cut into pieces for household cleaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponge - a porous material used for cleaning, often synthetic or from sea animals

Etymology

From Latin *luffa*, from Arabic *lūf* (the name of the plant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈluːfə / (AmE), / ˈlʌfə / (BrE)
A tropical climbing plant of the gourd family, whose fibrous fruit is used as a sponge and whose young fruits are edible.
tropical plant; fruit used as sponge

Examples

She uses a luffa to exfoliate her skin while showering.
Farmers in the region cultivate luffa for both food and natural sponges.
The luffa gourd becomes fibrous when mature and is perfect for scrubbing dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponge gourd - another name for the luffa plant

Etymology

From Arabic *lūf* (the plant or its fruit).
