doped

1. ADJ

/ doʊpt / (AmE), / dəʊpt / (BrE)
(of a material) treated or impregnated with a foreign substance, often to alter its physical or chemical properties.
impregnated with a foreign substance

Examples

The doped silicon wafer exhibited increased electrical conductivity.
Some modern fibers are doped with rare earth elements for improved performance.
The team compared the doped samples to pure materials in their experiments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undoped - not treated or impregnated with a foreign substance

Etymology

From 'dope' (verb, to administer a drug or substance) plus English -ed participle ending.

2. ADJ

/ doʊpt / (AmE), / dəʊpt / (BrE)
under the influence of drugs or narcotics, especially so as to cause unconsciousness or impairment
under the influence of drugs or narcotics

Examples

He was so doped after surgery that he barely knew where he was.
The doped patient drifted slowly into a heavy sleep.
After drinking the doped wine, everyone at the party became sluggish and confused.

Synset Examples

knocked out by doped wine
a drugged sleep
were under the effect of the drugged sweets
in a stuperous narcotized state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drugged - under the influence of drugs, especially narcotics

Etymology

Past participle of 'dope', originally from Dutch 'doop' (sauce, dip).
