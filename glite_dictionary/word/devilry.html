devilry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəlri / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlri / (BrE)
wicked or cruel behavior; actions showing great malice or evil intent
wicked or cruel behavior

Examples

The story is full of devilry and supernatural happenings.
His acts of devilry shocked the peaceful village.
The children laughed, unaware of the devilry behind their prank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evil - profound immorality and wickedness
  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate

Etymology

From Middle English 'develrie', from 'devil' + '-ry' (suffix forming nouns).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəlri / (AmE), / ˈdɛv(ə)lri / (BrE)
reckless or malicious behavior causing discomfort or annoyance in others; mischief or wickedness
reckless or malicious mischief

Examples

The children's devilry made it difficult for the teacher to maintain order in the classroom.
Halloween night was full of mischief and devilry among the youngsters.
The old man shook his head at the devilry he saw in the neighborhood pranksters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischief - playful misbehavior or troublemaking
  • Synonym: villainy - wicked or criminal behavior

Etymology

From Middle English 'develrie', from 'devel' (devil) + '-ry'.
