Edith Cavell

1. PROPN

/ ˈiːdɪθ kəˈvɛl / (AmE), / ˈiːdɪθ kəˈvɛl / (BrE)
(1865–1915) An English nurse remembered for helping Allied prisoners escape from occupied Belgium during World War I; she was executed by German authorities.
English nurse who aided Allied escapees in WWI

Examples

Many historians regard Edith Cavell as a symbol of humanitarian courage during wartime.
A hospital in London is named after Edith Cavell in recognition of her service.
The story of Edith Cavell continues to inspire nurses around the world.
