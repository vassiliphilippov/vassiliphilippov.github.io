to abolish

1. VERB

/ əˈbɑlɪʃ / (AmE), / əˈbɒlɪʃ / (BrE)
to officially put an end to a law, system, or practice
formally end a system or law

Examples

Many countries decided to abolish the death penalty after long debates.
The government plans to abolish outdated regulations that hinder business growth.
Slavery was abolished in the United States in 1865 after the Civil War.

Synset Examples

Slavery was abolished in the mid-19th century in America and in Russia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeal - to revoke or annul a law or act officially
  • Antonym: to establish - to set up or create something, especially a system or institution

Etymology

From Latin abolēre (to destroy, efface) via Old French abolir.
