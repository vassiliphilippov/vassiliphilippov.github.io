allied

1. ADJ

/ əˈlaɪd / (AmE), / ˈælaɪd / (BrE)
(of countries, groups, or people) joined by treaty, agreement, or a common purpose, especially in a military or political context.
joined by treaty or common purpose

Examples

Several allied nations met to discuss their joint response to the crisis.
During World War II, the Allied forces worked together against the Axis powers.
The companies became allied in order to better compete in the market.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hostile - not friendly or allied; opposed or antagonistic

Etymology

From past participle of 'ally', from Old French 'alier' (to ally), from Latin 'alligare' (to bind to).

2. ADJ

/ əˈlaɪd / (AmE), / ˈælaɪd / (BrE)
connected by similar qualities, characteristics, or ancestry; related in nature or origin.
related by characteristics or ancestry

Examples

Botanists often study allied species to understand evolutionary patterns.
The research focuses on several allied disciplines within the social sciences.
These plants belong to allied families distinguished by certain shared features.

3. ADJ

/ əˈlaɪd / (AmE), / ˈælaɪd / (BrE)
joined or united in a confederacy, league, or association for a common purpose; allied together
joined in a confederacy or league

Examples

Several countries became allied in order to address the environmental crisis.
The two companies are allied against a common competitor in the marketplace.
During the war, many smaller nations remained allied with the Western powers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: united - joined together for a common purpose
  • Antonym: opposed - in conflict or opposition

Etymology

From Latin 'alligare' (to bind to), via Old French 'alier' (to ally).
