genealogist

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːniˈɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːniˈælədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies or is an expert in genealogy, tracing family ancestries and histories
expert in family history and ancestry

Examples

The genealogist uncovered fascinating details about my great-grandparents' lives.
As a professional genealogist, she spends her days researching old birth and marriage records.
Many genealogists use online databases to piece together family trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genealogy - the study of family ancestries and histories

Etymology

From 'genealogy' (tracing descent), ultimately from Greek 'genealogia' (the making of a pedigree).
