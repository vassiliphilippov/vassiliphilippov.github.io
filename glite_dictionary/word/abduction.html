abduction

1. NOUN

/ æbˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / æbˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
(physiology) the movement of a limb or other body part away from the midline of the body or from another part.
movement of body part away from midline (physiology)
medical

Examples

The therapist explained that abduction of the arm involves lifting it away from the side of the body.
Limited shoulder abduction is often a symptom of rotator cuff injury.
During physical examination, the doctor tested hip abduction to check for muscle strength.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adduction - (physiology) movement of a body part toward the central axis of the body

Etymology

From Latin abducere (to lead away).

2. NOUN

/ æbˈdʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / əbˈdʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of illegally taking someone away by force, especially a family member, often as a crime.
the act of carrying away someone by force

Examples

The police responded quickly after the abduction of the child from her home.
Laws against abduction are particularly strict in cases involving minors or spouses.
She lived in fear after escaping an attempted abduction by a stranger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kidnapping - unlawful taking and detaining of a person, usually for ransom
  • Antonym: release - the act of setting someone free

Etymology

From Latin abductio, from abducere (to lead away).
