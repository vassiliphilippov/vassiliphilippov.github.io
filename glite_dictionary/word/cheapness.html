cheapness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃip.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːp.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of lacking taste, refinement, or value, especially due to being inexpensive or vulgar
lack of taste due to being cheap or vulgar

Examples

Many critics commented on the cheapness of the decor, saying it lacked any sense of elegance.
Her flashy jewelry added an air of cheapness to her otherwise lovely attire.
The movie's cheapness was evident in its tacky sets and costumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulgarity - the quality of being crude or lacking sophistication

Etymology

From cheap + -ness, where 'cheap' comes from Old English *céap* (trade, bargain).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃip.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːp.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being offered at a price lower than the standard or usual price
low price; inexpensive quality

Examples

The cheapness of the tickets made the event accessible to everyone.
Many shoppers are attracted by the cheapness of goods in the market.
She questioned the cheapness of the meal, wondering if it reflected lower quality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: expensiveness - the quality of being high in price

Etymology

From 'cheap' + '-ness', where 'cheap' traces back to Old English 'céap' (bargain, trade).
