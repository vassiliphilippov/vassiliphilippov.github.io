dazzled

1. ADJ

/ ˈdæzəld / (AmE), / ˈdæzəld / (BrE)
Overwhelmed or stupefied by something impressively bright, beautiful, or powerful.
overwhelmed by brilliance or power

Examples

She stood dazzled by the sheer beauty of the mountain sunrise.
The audience was dazzled by the magician's incredible performance.
He felt dazzled and speechless in the presence of the famous scientist.

Synset Examples

I fall back dazzled at beholding myself all rosy red, / At having, I myself, caused the sun to rise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awed - filled with or expressing awe
  • Synonym: stunned - overwhelmed or shocked
  • Antonym: unimpressed - not affected or influenced; not impressed

Etymology

Past participle of 'dazzle', from Middle English 'daselen', probably of Scandinavian origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdæzəld / (AmE), / ˈdɑːzəld / (BrE)
(of a person) having vision temporarily impaired or overwhelmed by intense light.
temporarily blinded by intense light

Examples

She shut her dazzled eyes against the sun's brilliance.
After stepping into the spotlight, he stood dazzled for a few seconds, unable to see the audience.
The headlights left me dazzled as I drove down the dark road.

Synset Examples

she shut her dazzled eyes against the sun's brilliance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dazzle - to blind temporarily by a bright light; impress deeply
  • Synonym: blinded - temporarily deprived of sight

Etymology

From the verb 'dazzle', which comes from Middle English 'daselen', probably frequentative of dialectal 'daze'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02167027-s
Synset POS: s
