Philistine

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪlɪstiːn, fɪˈlɪstɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪstiːn, fɪˈlɪstɪn / (BrE)
of or relating to ancient Philistia, its culture, or its people
relating to ancient Philistia or its people

Examples

The museum curated a special exhibit of Philistine pottery and weapons.
Archaeologists uncovered a settlement with distinctively Philistine architectural features.
The Philistine language is known only from a few inscriptions and references in other texts.

Etymology

From Latin Philistinus, from Greek Philistinos, from Hebrew P'lishtim (Philistines).

WordNet ID: oewn-03110849-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ fəˈlɪstiːn / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪstiːn / (BrE)
a member of an ancient people from the Aegean region who settled in Philistia around the 12th century BC
member of an ancient Aegean people

Examples

Archaeologists discovered artifacts attributed to the Philistine civilization along the southern coast of ancient Canaan.
According to biblical texts, Samson had many conflicts with the Philistines.
The history of the Philistine people provides valuable insights into interactions in the Eastern Mediterranean.

Etymology

From Latin Philistinus, from Greek Philistinoi, from Hebrew Pĕlēštīm (the Philistines).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10442616-n
Synset POS: n
