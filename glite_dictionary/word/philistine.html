philistine

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪl.əˌstiːn / (AmE), / ˈfɪl.ɪstiːn / (BrE)
having or showing no appreciation of art, culture, or intellectual values; indifferent or hostile to artistic and cultural pursuits
indifferent or hostile to culture or the arts

Examples

She dismissed the novel as the dull product of a philistine mind.
His philistine attitude meant he had no interest in the museum exhibit.
The town's council was seen as rather philistine for cutting funding to the arts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uncultured - lacking education or knowledge of culture and art
  • Antonym: cultured - having or showing good education, tastes, and manners, especially in the arts

Etymology

From German 'Philister', originally referring to adversaries of the Israelites in the Bible; later used ironically for uncultured people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlɪˌstin / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪstiːn / (BrE)
a person who is indifferent to or has no appreciation for art, culture, or intellectual pursuits
person indifferent to culture or intellect

Examples

Despite living in a city full of museums, he remained a philistine who never visited them.
She called him a philistine when he dismissed her favorite novel as boring.
You don't have to be a philistine to dislike abstract art, but an open mind helps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbarian - a person perceived as uncivilized or uncultured
  • Antonym: culture vulture - a person with an excessive or pretentious interest in the arts

Etymology

From German 'Philister', used by university students to refer to townspeople, then metaphorically to mean uncultured people.
