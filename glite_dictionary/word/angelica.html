angelica

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdʒɛlɪkə / (AmE), / ænˈdʒɛlɪkə / (BrE)
the candied stalks of the angelica plant, used in cooking and confectionery
candied stalks used in confectionery

Examples

The recipe calls for finely chopped angelica to decorate the cake.
He bought a box of chocolates filled with pieces of candied angelica.
Slices of angelica added a delicate, sweet flavor to the dessert.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'angelica', the name of the plant, meaning 'angelic'.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈdʒɛlɪkə / (AmE), / ænˈdʒɛlɪkə / (BrE)
the aromatic stems, leaves, or roots of the plant Angelica archangelica, used especially in flavoring and confectionery
aromatic stems or roots of Angelica plant

Examples

Many recipes for candied fruit include chopped angelica for added flavor and color.
The herbal tea was infused with angelica to give it a distinctive, sweet aroma.
He recognized the taste of angelica immediately in the liqueur.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin angelica (herb), from Latin angelicus (angelic), named for its believed medicinal properties.

3. NOUN

/ ænˈdʒɛlɪkə / (AmE), / ænˈdʒɛlɪkə / (BrE)
a tall plant of the parsley family, with compound leaves and small white or greenish flowers, belonging to the genus Angelica, some species of which are used in cooking and herbal medicine
a tall herb of the genus Angelica

Examples

The candied stems of angelica are often used to decorate cakes and desserts.
Wild angelica grows abundantly along the riverbanks in northern Europe.
She added dried angelica to the herbal tea blend for its unique flavor.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin angelica, from Latin angelicus (angelic), so named from the belief that the plant had healing powers.
