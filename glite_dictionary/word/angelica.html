Angelica

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdʒɛlɪkə / (AmE), / ænˈdʒelɪkə / (BrE)
A genus of biennial or perennial herbs of the Northern Hemisphere, with a taproot and aromatic stems, some of which are used in cooking and medicine.
genus of herbs, some used in cooking and medicine

Examples

Angelica is often grown for its aromatic stems, which are used to flavor liqueurs.
Some gardeners cultivate Angelica for its ornamental umbels of white or greenish flowers.
The root of Angelica has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Angelica, from Latin 'angelicus' meaning 'angelic', referring to attributed medicinal properties.
