Huascaran

1. PROPN

/ wɑːˌskɑːˈrɑːn / (AmE), / wɑːsˌkɑːˈrɑːn / (BrE)
a mountain in the Andes of Peru, notable for being the country's highest peak at 22,205 feet (6,768 meters)
highest mountain in Peru

Examples

Many adventurous climbers dream of ascending Huascaran, the highest peak in Peru.
The earthquake in 1970 triggered a massive avalanche on Huascaran that devastated the surrounding villages.
From a distance, the snow-capped summit of Huascaran dominates the northern Peruvian landscape.

Etymology

From Quechua 'Waskaran,' derived from 'Waskar,' a historical Inca emperor.
