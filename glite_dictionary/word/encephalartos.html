Encephalartos

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˌsɛfəˈlɑrtəs / (AmE), / ɛnsɛfəˈlɑːtɒs / (BrE)
A genus of African cycads with stout, cylindrical trunks and a terminal crown of long, often spiny pinnate leaves.
genus of African cycads

Examples

The botanical garden features a rare Encephalartos species from South Africa.
Conservationists are working to protect wild populations of Encephalartos threatened by poaching.
The thick trunk of the Encephalartos helps it survive in arid environments.

Etymology

From Greek enkephalos (brain) + artos (bread); named for the starchy, bread-like pith of some species.

2. NOUN

/ ɛnˌsɛfəˈlɑrtəs / (AmE), / ɛnˌsɛfəˈlɑːtɒs / (BrE)
A genus of arborescent (tree-like) African cycads, sometimes classified in the family Cycadaceae.
a genus of African cycads

Examples

The Encephalartos genus includes several species of cycads native to Africa.
Botanists have discovered a new Encephalartos species in South Africa.
Many Encephalartos plants are considered endangered due to habitat loss.

Etymology

From Greek *enkephalos* (brain) and *artos* (bread), referring to shapes in the seeds.
