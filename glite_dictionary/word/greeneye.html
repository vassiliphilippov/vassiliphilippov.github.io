greeneye

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːnˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnˌaɪ / (BrE)
a small deep-sea fish found on the ocean floor, noted for its large eyes with a metallic green sheen
deep-sea fish with metallic green eyes

Examples

The greeneye is known for its adapted vision in the deep sea.
Researchers were excited to spot a greeneye during their expedition.
The metallic sheen of a greeneye's eyes is remarkable under direct light.

Etymology

Formed from 'green' + 'eye', describing the fish's distinctive eyes.
