to illegalize

1. VERB

/ ɪˈliɡəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈliːɡəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something illegal by law or authoritative action; to outlaw
make illegal; outlaw

Examples

The government plans to illegalize the sale of certain pesticides.
Some activists want to illegalize wild animal performances in circuses.
After years of debate, the city council decided to illegalize short-term rentals.

Synset Examples

Marijuana is criminalized in the U.S.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to legalize - to make something legal; to permit by law

Etymology

Formed from 'illegal' + '-ize', patterned after similar verbs like 'legalize'.
