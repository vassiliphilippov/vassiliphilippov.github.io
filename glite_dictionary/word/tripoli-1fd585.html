tripoli

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪpəli / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpəli / (BrE)
A weathered and decomposed siliceous limestone, especially when powdered for use as a polishing agent.
a fine powder used in polishing

Examples

The jeweler used tripoli to give the silver a bright, smooth finish.
Polishing compounds like tripoli are essential for achieving a flawless surface on metalwork.
Before the final buffing, the craftsman applied tripoli to the wheel.

Etymology

From Italian 'Tripoli', after the city in Libya, where the substance was originally obtained.
