operationalist

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑː.pəˈreɪ.ʃə.nəl.ɪst / (AmE), / ˌɒp.əˈreɪ.ʃə.nəl.ɪst / (BrE)
relating to, or advocating, operationalism as a philosophy, especially in scientific or academic contexts.
relating to operationalism

Examples

The operationalist approach requires that scientific terms be defined by measurable operations.
Many philosophers debate the value of operationalist definitions in psychology.
His theories reflect an operationalist perspective on scientific concepts.

Synset Examples

operationalist doctrine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: operationalism - the doctrine that scientific concepts must be defined in terms of observable operations

Etymology

From 'operation' + '-alist', based on 'operationalism' (coined by P.W. Bridgman in the 1920s).
