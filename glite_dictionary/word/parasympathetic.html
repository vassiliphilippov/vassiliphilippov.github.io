parasympathetic

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌpærəsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
the branch of the autonomic nervous system that originates in the brain stem and lower spinal cord, and is responsible for functions such as stimulating digestive secretions, slowing the heart rate, constricting pupils, and dilating blood vessels, generally opposing the action of the sympathetic nervous system
part of the autonomic nervous system controlling rest and digestion
medical

Examples

The parasympathetic system helps your body relax after periods of stress.
Damage to the parasympathetic nerves can affect digestion and heart rate.
Activation of the parasympathetic branch promotes a sense of calm and rest in the body.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sympathetic - the branch of the autonomic nervous system that prepares the body for activity or stress

Etymology

From Greek para- (alongside) + sympathetic (relating to the sympathetic nervous system).

2. ADJ

/ ˌpær.ə.sɪm.pəˈθɛt̬.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpar.ə.sɪm.pəˈθɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting the part of the autonomic nervous system that counteracts the effects of the sympathetic nervous system and promotes rest and digestion.
relating to the parasympathetic nervous system
medical

Examples

The parasympathetic nervous system is responsible for slowing the heart rate after stress.
Certain medications can stimulate the parasympathetic response in the body.
Yoga and deep breathing are known to activate parasympathetic functions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sympathetic - relating to the sympathetic nervous system

Etymology

From para- (beside, beyond) + sympathetic (relating to the sympathetic nervous system).
