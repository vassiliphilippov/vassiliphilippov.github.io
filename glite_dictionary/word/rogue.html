rogue

1. NOUN

/ roʊɡ / (AmE), / rəʊɡ / (BrE)
a dishonest or unprincipled person who behaves in a deceitful or unreliable way
dishonest or unprincipled person

Examples

The notorious rogue cheated dozens of people out of their savings.
No one trusted the rogue after he lied repeatedly to the group.
Despite his charm, he remained a rogue in the eyes of his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Synonym: villain - a cruelly malicious person; a scoundrel
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit and untruthfulness; sincere

Etymology

From Latin 'rogus' (beggar) via Middle English and Old French.
