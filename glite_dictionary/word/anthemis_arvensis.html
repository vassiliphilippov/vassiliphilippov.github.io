Anthemis arvensis

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθɛmɪs ɑːrˈvɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ænˈθɛmɪs ɑːˈvɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A European white-flowered weed of the daisy family, commonly called corn chamomile, naturalized in North America.
a white-flowered weed (corn chamomile)

Examples

Anthemis arvensis can often be found growing wild in wheat fields across Europe.
Gardeners sometimes remove Anthemis arvensis to prevent it from spreading among crops.
The white petals and yellow centers make Anthemis arvensis easy to distinguish from other field weeds.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Anthemis' (Greek: 'anthemon', flower) and 'arvensis' (of the field).
