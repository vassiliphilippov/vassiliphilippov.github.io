pension

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈpɛnʃən / (BrE)
A regular payment made by the government or a private company to someone who has retired or is no longer working, intended as an income for their support.
regular retirement payment

Examples

After forty years of service, she now receives a monthly pension from the company.
Many elderly people rely on their state pension to cover living expenses after retirement.
His father saved carefully, hoping for a comfortable pension once he stopped working.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annuity - a fixed sum of money paid to someone each year
  • Antonym: salary - regular payment by an employer to an employee

Etymology

From Latin pensio (payment), from pendere (to pay).

2. VERB

/ ˈpɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈpɛnʃən / (BrE)
to grant or pay a pension to someone, especially as a benefit after retirement or long service.
grant a retirement pension

Examples

After thirty years as a professor, the university finally pensioned him upon his retirement.
The company pensioned several long-serving employees last year after closing the factory.
She was pensioned off at age sixty and now enjoys her time traveling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retire - to leave one's job and cease to work, usually due to age or long service

Etymology

From Latin 'pensiō' (payment), via Old French 'pension' (payment, rent).
