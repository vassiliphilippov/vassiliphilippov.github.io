Cocos nucifera

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.kəs nuˈsɪf.ə.rə / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.kəs njuːˈsɪf.ərə / (BrE)
A tall tropical palm tree that produces coconuts; widely cultivated across the tropics.
the coconut palm tree

Examples

The Cocos nucifera is a vital resource for many coastal communities.
Farmers in Indonesia cultivate Cocos nucifera for both its fruit and fiber.
Leaves of the Cocos nucifera are often used for weaving mats and baskets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coconut - the large edible seed of the coconut palm

Etymology

From Latin genus and species name, with 'Cocos' probably from Portuguese 'coco' (coconut, literally 'grinning face'), and 'nucifera' meaning 'nut-bearing'.
