Samuel Adams

1. PROPN

/ ˈsæm.ju.əl ˈæd.əmz / (AmE), / ˈsæm.juːəl ˈæd.əmz / (BrE)
(1722–1803) An American Revolutionary leader and patriot, organizer of the Boston Tea Party, and signer of the Declaration of Independence.
American Revolutionary leader (1722–1803)

Examples

Samuel Adams played a key role in organizing the Boston Tea Party.
Historians regard Samuel Adams as a master of revolutionary propaganda.
The legacy of Samuel Adams endures in discussions of American independence.
