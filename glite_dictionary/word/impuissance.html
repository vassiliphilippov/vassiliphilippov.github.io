impuissance

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpwiːsəns / (AmE), / ɪmˈpwiːsəns / (BrE)
a lack of power or ability to act; helplessness or powerlessness
lack of power; helplessness

Examples

Overwhelmed by a sense of impuissance, she was unable to change the situation.
His speech conveyed deep frustration and impuissance in the face of political obstacles.
The team's repeated failures led to a period of collective impuissance and discouragement.

Synset Examples

in spite of their weakness the group remains active

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerlessness - the state of lacking power or ability
  • Synonym: helplessness - the state of being unable to help oneself; lacking strength or power

Etymology

From French impuissance (lack of power), from Latin in- (not) + potentia (power).
