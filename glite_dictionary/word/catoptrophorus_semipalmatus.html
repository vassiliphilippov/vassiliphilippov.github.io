Catoptrophorus semipalmatus

1. NOUN

/ kəˌtɑp.trəˈfoʊ.rəs ˌsɛ.mi.pælˈmeɪ.təs / (AmE), / kəˌtɒp.trəˈfɔː.rəs ˌsɛ.mi.pælˈmeɪ.təs / (BrE)
A large North American shorebird of the sandpiper family, found along the eastern and Gulf Coasts, commonly known as the willet.
large North American shorebird (willet)

Examples

The Catoptrophorus semipalmatus is easily recognized by its striking black-and-white wing pattern in flight.
During migration, large flocks of Catoptrophorus semipalmatus can be seen along the Atlantic coast.
Researchers observed the nesting habits of the Catoptrophorus semipalmatus in coastal marshlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willet - common name of Catoptrophorus semipalmatus, a North American shorebird

Etymology

From Greek *katoptron* (mirror) + *phoros* (bearing) and Latin *semipalmatus* (half-webbed).
