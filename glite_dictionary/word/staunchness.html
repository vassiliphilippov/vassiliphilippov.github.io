staunchness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑntʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈstɔːntʃ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being firmly loyal and committed, especially under difficult circumstances
steadfast loyalty under difficult conditions

Examples

Her staunchness in supporting her family during hard times inspired everyone around her.
Despite criticism, his staunchness to his friends never wavered.
They admired her staunchness in defending her beliefs when others doubted her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loyalty - the quality of being loyal
  • Antonym: fickleness - the quality of being changeable or unreliable in loyalty

Etymology

From 'staunch' (firm, loyal) + '-ness' (noun suffix).
