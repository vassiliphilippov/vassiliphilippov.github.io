pixilated

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɪksɪˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɪksɪˌleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(old-fashioned) very drunk; intoxicated to the point of behaving erratically or foolishly.
very drunk, intoxicated
archaic

Examples

After too many glasses of wine, he became quite pixilated at the party.
In the old film, the aunt is described as a bit pixilated after her sherry.
The villagers joked that he walked home pixilated from the tavern every night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drunk - intoxicated with alcohol
  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol, not drunk

Etymology

Variant of 'pixie-led', referring to being led astray by pixies, later associated with eccentric or drunken behavior.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɪksɪˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɪksɪˌleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) playfully mischievous or slightly eccentric, behaving in a whimsically naughty manner.
playfully mischievous; slightly eccentric

Examples

Her pixilated sense of humor always kept her friends laughing.
He gave me a pixilated wink before slipping the frog into my bag.
The old woman seemed pixilated, dancing in the rain and humming to herself.

Synset Examples

teasing and worrying with impish laughter
a wicked prank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impish - playfully mischievous or naughty
  • Synonym: mischievous - causing or showing a fondness for causing trouble in a playful way
  • Antonym: serious - not joking or playfully mischievous

Etymology

American English, altered from 'pixie', influenced by 'pixie-led' (bewitched by pixies).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02130013-s
Synset POS: s
