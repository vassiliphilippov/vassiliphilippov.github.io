war

1. NOUN

/ wɔr / (AmE), / wɔː / (BrE)
A state of armed conflict between different countries or groups within a country, typically involving armed forces and causing significant destruction and suffering.
armed conflict between groups or nations

Examples

The war lasted for five years and devastated the region.
Many families were separated during the war and struggled to reunite afterwards.
The government declared a state of emergency as the threat of war grew.

Synset Examples

thousands of people were killed in the war

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peace - a state in which there is no war or fighting

Etymology

From Old English 'werre', from Old French 'guerre', of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00975181-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ wɔr / (AmE), / wɔː / (BrE)
An organized and concerted campaign or effort to stop or eliminate something considered harmful or undesirable.
intense campaign against something harmful

Examples

The government launched a war on illiteracy that involved schools across the country.
Many organizations are involved in the war against climate change.
A new war on drugs was announced by the authorities last week.

Synset Examples

the war on poverty
the war against crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaign - an organized course of action to achieve a goal

Etymology

From Old English 'werre', from Old French 'guerre', from Frankish or Germanic origins meaning strife or conflict.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00802786-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ wɔr / (AmE), / wɔː / (BrE)
a formal and legally recognized state of armed conflict between countries or groups, typically declared and governed by international law
declared legal state of armed conflict

Examples

The two countries have officially been in war since 1950, although there has been no active combat for decades.
A declaration of war triggers specific rights and responsibilities under international law.
The nation's parliament debated for days before finally entering a state of war with its neighbor.

Synset Examples

war was declared in November but actual fighting did not begin until the following spring

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peace - a state or period in which there is no war or a war has ended
  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument, often a prolonged one

Etymology

From Old English 'werre', via Old Northern French 'werre', from Medieval Latin 'werra'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14004888-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ wɔr / (AmE), / wɔː / (BrE)
to make or wage war; to engage in active conflict or hostilities
to engage in war

Examples

The two neighboring countries threatened to war over the disputed border.
For generations, the rival tribes have warred with each other.
While some sought peace, others chose to war against their enemies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight - to take part in a violent struggle or armed conflict
  • Antonym: peace - freedom from war or violence

Etymology

From Old English *werre* (war), ultimately from Old French *guerre*.

5. NOUN

/ wɔr / (AmE), / wɔː / (BrE)
A serious, sustained struggle or competition between opposing groups, organizations, or individuals.
an intense struggle or competition

Examples

The two companies have been locked in a fierce war for market dominance.
A bitter war of words erupted between the politicians during the debate.
There is an ongoing war against misinformation on the internet.

Synset Examples

a price war
a war of wits
diplomatic warfare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument
  • Antonym: peace - a state of tranquility or absence of conflict

Etymology

From Old English 'werre', via Anglo-Norman 'werre' (war, fighting), from Old French 'guerre', of Germanic origin.
