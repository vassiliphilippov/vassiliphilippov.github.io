gilbert

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪlbɚt / (AmE), / ˈɡɪlbət / (BrE)
a unit of magnetomotive force equal to 0.7958 ampere-turns, used in the centimetre-gram-second (CGS) system of units
unit of magnetomotive force (CGS system)

Examples

The strength of the coil was measured as twelve gilberts of magnetomotive force.
In the CGS system, magnetomotive force is often expressed in gilberts rather than ampere-turns.
One gilbert is approximately equal to 0.8 ampere-turns in the SI system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ampere-turn - a unit of magnetomotive force in the SI system

Etymology

Named after William Gilbert (1544–1603), English physicist and pioneer in the study of magnetism.
