stressed

1. ADJ

/ strɛst / (AmE), / strɛst / (BrE)
(of a syllable or word) pronounced with emphasis or a greater force than others; bearing a stress or accent.
bearing a stress or accent (linguistics, phonetics)

Examples

In the word 'about,' the second syllable is stressed while the first is not.
Linguists analyze which syllables are stressed to determine a word's rhythm.
A stressed syllable in English is often louder and longer than an unstressed one.

Synset Examples

an iambic foot consists of an unstressed syllable followed by a stressed syllable as in ‘delay’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unstressed - (of a syllable or word) not pronounced with emphasis or accent

Etymology

Past participle of 'stress', from Middle English *stressen* (to emphasize, accent).

2. ADJ

/ strɛst / (AmE), / strɛst / (BrE)
experiencing severe strain or distress, especially due to physical or emotional pressure
under severe strain or distress

Examples

After a week of working overtime, she felt completely stressed and exhausted.
The stressed runners struggled to finish the last mile of the marathon.
He looked stressed during the interview, his hands trembling slightly.

Synset Examples

he dropped out of the race, clearly distressed and having difficulty breathing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relaxed - free from tension or anxiety
  • Synonym: distressed - suffering from extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain

Etymology

From the verb 'stress,' meaning 'to subject to pressure or strain,' derived from Middle English 'stresse' (stress, pressure).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
