tactically

1. ADV

/ ˈtæk.tɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˈtak.tɪ.kli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to tactics, especially in military, business, or strategic contexts.
in a manner relating to tactics

Examples

The army advanced tactically to minimize casualties and maximize surprise.
She positioned her team tactically to exploit the competitor's weakness.
The chess player moved his knight tactically to control the center of the board.

Synset Examples

the tactically useful province is still firmly in the rebels' hands

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: strategically - in a way that relates to strategic planning or goals

Etymology

From tactic + -ally, formed from the adjective 'tactical' (relating to tactics).
