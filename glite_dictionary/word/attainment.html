attainment

1. NOUN

/ əˈteɪnmənt / (AmE), / əˈteɪnmənt / (BrE)
the act of reaching or achieving a new stage, level, or condition
achievement of a stage or level

Examples

Her attainment of a leadership position inspired many younger colleagues.
The attainment of independence marked a turning point in the country's history.
His attainment of a black belt reflected years of dedication to martial arts.

Synset Examples

his attainment of puberty was delayed by malnutrition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achievement - something accomplished through effort; a similar noun
  • Antonym: failure - lack of success in reaching a stage or goal; an antonym

Etymology

From Old French *ateignement*, from *ateindre* (to attain); ultimately from Latin *attingere* (to touch upon, reach).

2. NOUN

/ əˈteɪnmənt / (AmE), / əˈteɪnmənt / (BrE)
the act of achieving something, especially after effort; accomplishment.
the act of achieving something

Examples

Her attainment of the award was the result of years of hard work.
Freedom was seen as the ultimate attainment for the oppressed group.
The attainment of his lifelong dream brought him great joy.

Synset Examples

the attainment of independence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achievement - something accomplished, especially by superior ability or effort
  • Antonym: failure - lack of success in achieving something

Etymology

From Old French 'ateindre' (to attain), from Latin 'attangere' (to touch, reach).

3. NOUN

/ əˈteɪnmənt / (AmE), / əˈteɪnmənt / (BrE)
A skill or ability that someone has developed through training or effort; an achievement.
an ability gained by training or effort

Examples

Her attainment of fluency in three languages impressed the interview panel.
Musical attainment is often the result of years of dedicated practice.
His professional attainment opened up many new career opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achievement - a thing done successfully, typically by effort, courage, or skill
  • Synonym: accomplishment - something that has been achieved successfully

Etymology

From Old French 'atainment', from 'ataindre' (attain), from Latin 'attingere' (to touch, reach).
