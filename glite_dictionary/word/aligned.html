aligned

1. ADJ

/ əˈlaɪnd / (AmE), / əˈlaɪnd / (BrE)
in a straight line or in correct or proper position relative to something else
in a straight line; properly positioned

Examples

The dentist was pleased that his teeth were perfectly aligned.
All the books on the shelf were neatly aligned with the edge.
The road signs were not properly aligned, causing confusion.

Synset Examples

pearly teeth evenly aligned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straight - not curved or bent; in a direct line
  • Antonym: crooked - not in a straight line; bent or twisted

Etymology

From align (verb) + -ed; align from French aligner, based on Latin linea (line).

2. ADJ

/ əˈlaɪnd / (AmE), / əˈlaɪnd / (BrE)
placed in agreement or cooperation with a particular group, side, or cause.
in agreement with a group or cause

Examples

The senator remained aligned with the environmental advocacy group throughout her career.
All employees must be aligned with the company's new strategic goals.
Several nations became aligned with the Western bloc during the Cold War.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opposed - actively resisting or in conflict with a particular group, side, or cause

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'align,' from Middle French *aligner* (to line up), from Latin *linea* (line).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00735248-a
Synset POS: a
