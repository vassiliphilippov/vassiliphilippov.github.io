periodical

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɪriˈɑdɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpɪəriˈɒdɪkəl / (BrE)
happening or recurring at regular intervals.
recurring at regular intervals

Examples

The periodical inspection of fire alarms is required by safety regulations.
Migratory birds return in a periodical pattern every spring.
The historian noted the periodical outbreaks of unrest throughout the century.

Synset Examples

the periodic appearance of the seventeen-year locust

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periodic - happening at regular intervals; more common synonym

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'periodicus', from Greek 'periodikos' (recurring regularly).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɪriˈɑːdɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpɪəriˈɒdɪkəl / (BrE)
a magazine or newspaper that is published at regular, usually weekly, monthly, or quarterly, intervals
publication issued regularly

Examples

The library subscribes to hundreds of periodicals covering a wide range of subjects.
Many periodicals are only available in digital format nowadays.
She publishes her research in scientific periodicals to reach a wider audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magazine - a publication with articles and illustrations, published regularly
  • Synonym: journal - a regular publication containing scholarly articles
  • Antonym: book - a written or printed work consisting of pages glued or sewn together

Etymology

From Medieval Latin periodicus (periodic), from Greek periodikos.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06605500-n
Synset POS: n
