hostess

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊstɪs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊstɪs / (BrE)
a woman who receives or entertains guests at home or elsewhere
female host; woman who entertains guests

Examples

The hostess greeted the guests at the door with a warm smile.
Our hostess made sure everyone had something to eat and drink.
As the evening progressed, the hostess organized several fun games for the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: host - a person who receives or entertains guests

Etymology

From Middle English hostesse, from Old French, feminine of hoste (host).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊstɪs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊstɪs / (BrE)
a woman whose job is to look after passengers on an airplane; a female flight attendant (now dated or less common)
female airline steward (dated)

Examples

The airline hostess offered drinks to all the passengers after takeoff.
Years ago, every major airline employed a hostess to care for travelers on board.
The hostess demonstrated the safety procedures before the flight departed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flight attendant - person whose job is to look after airline passengers
  • Synonym: stewardess - female steward on an airplane (now dated)
  • Synonym: steward - person who attends to passengers on an airplane or ship

Etymology

From host + -ess, denoting feminine gender.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10675314-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊstəs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊstɪs / (BrE)
a woman who keeps an inn or hotel; a female innkeeper
female innkeeper

Examples

The hostess greeted each guest warmly as they arrived at the inn.
In the old village, the hostess was known for her delicious home-cooked meals.
The weary travelers found comfort and a friendly welcome from the hostess at the small roadside inn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: host - male innkeeper; a man who keeps an inn

Etymology

From host + -ess, influenced by French hôtesse (female host).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10206678-n
Synset POS: n
