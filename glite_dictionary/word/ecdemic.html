ecdemic

1. ADJ

/ ɛkˈdɛmɪk / (AmE), / ɛkˈdiːmɪk / (BrE)
(of a disease) originating outside the locality in which it occurs.
disease originating outside locality
medical

Examples

The sudden outbreak was traced to an ecdemic infection introduced by travelers.
Doctors must determine whether a case is ecdemic or endemic to devise effective control strategies.
Many ecdemic diseases can spread rapidly in populations lacking prior exposure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endemic - disease regularly found among people in a certain area

Etymology

From Greek *ekdēmos* ('away from home')—from *ek-* ('out') + *dēmos* ('people, district').
