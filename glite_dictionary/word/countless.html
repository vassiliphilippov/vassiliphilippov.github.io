countless

1. ADJ

/ ˈkaʊntləs / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntləs / (BrE)
too many to be counted; innumerable
too many to be counted

Examples

She spent countless hours working on her novel until it was perfect.
There are countless stars visible in the night sky far from the city.
Over the years, he has helped countless people through his mentoring.

Synset Examples

countless hours
an infinite number of reasons
innumerable difficulties
the multitudinous seas
myriad stars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innumerable - too many to be counted
  • Synonym: myriad - a countless or extremely great number
  • Degree Lower: numerous - great in number; many

Etymology

Formed from 'count' + '-less', meaning 'without number, innumerable'.
