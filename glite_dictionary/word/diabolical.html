diabolical

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈbɑː.lɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈbɒl.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Extremely evil or cruel; very wicked or characteristic of the devil.
extremely evil; wicked; devilish

Examples

The villain’s diabolical scheme caused panic across the city.
She gave a diabolical laugh that sent chills down his spine.
The film depicted a diabolical dictator ruling with absolute cruelty.

Synset Examples

something demonic in him — something that could be cruel
fires lit up a diabolic scene
diabolical sorcerers under the influence of devils
a fiendish despot
hellish torture
infernal instruments of war
satanic cruelty
unholy grimaces

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiendish - extremely cruel or wicked; devilish
  • Antonym: angelic - resembling an angel in goodness or purity; exceptionally kind or innocent

Etymology

From Late Latin diabolicus, from Greek diabolikos, relating to a devil.

2. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪəˈbɑlɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈbɒlɪkəl / (BrE)
extremely evil, wicked, or cunning, like or characteristic of a devil
extremely evil; devil-like

Examples

The villain's diabolical plan was revealed just in time to stop him.
She gave me a diabolical grin that sent chills down my spine.
The story is full of diabolical twists and dark surprises.

Synset Examples

devilish schemes
the cold calculation and diabolic art of some statesmen
the diabolical expression on his face
a mephistophelian glint in his eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devilish - showing wickedness or mischief characteristic of a devil
  • Antonym: angelic - resembling or characteristic of an angel, especially in being kind or good

Etymology

From Late Latin diabolicus, from Greek diabolikos, from diabolos (devil, slanderer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01136874-s
Synset POS: s
