shun giku

1. NOUN

/ ʃun ˈɡiːku / (AmE), / ʃʊn ˈɡiːku / (BrE)
an edible leafy plant, also known as garland chrysanthemum, commonly used in Asian cooking for its tender leaves.
edible leafy plant used in Asian cooking

Examples

Fresh shun giku leaves add a unique flavor to hot pot dishes.
In Japanese supermarkets, shun giku is usually sold next to other leafy greens.
The chef recommended adding chopped shun giku to the soup for extra aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garland chrysanthemum - an edible leafy plant used in Asian cuisine, synonym for shun giku

Etymology

From Japanese 春菊 (shungiku, literally 'spring chrysanthemum').
