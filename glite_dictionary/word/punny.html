punny

1. ADJ

/ ˈpʌni / (AmE), / ˈpʌni / (BrE)
Using or characterized by puns; provoking laughter by means of word play.
using puns; playful with words
informal

Examples

Her punny jokes always get a big laugh at office parties.
That was the punniest headline I've seen in years; the editor must have been in a playful mood.
He enjoys making punny comments during conversations, especially when everyone needs cheering up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witty - showing or characterized by quick and inventive verbal humor
  • Antonym: serious - solemn or not humorous

Etymology

Formed from 'pun' + '-y' (adjective suffix), coined as a playful way to describe wordplay.
