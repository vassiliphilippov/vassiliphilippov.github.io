Staurikosaurus

1. NOUN

/ stɔːˌrɪkoʊˈsɔːrəs / (AmE), / stɔːˌrɪkəʊˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of primitive, early dinosaurs found in Brazil, notable for being among the earliest ornithischians.
early dinosaur genus from Brazil

Examples

The fossils of Staurikosaurus were discovered in the Santa Maria Formation in Brazil.
Paleontologists believe Staurikosaurus was a fast, bipedal predator of the Late Triassic period.
The skeletal structure of Staurikosaurus provides insight into the early evolution of dinosaurs.

Etymology

From Greek 'staurikos' (cross-shaped) + 'saurus' (lizard).

2. NOUN

/ staʊˌrɪkoʊˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / staʊˌrɪkəʊˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of primitive theropod dinosaur that lived during the Late Triassic period, known from fossils found in Brazil
genus of primitive dinosaur from Brazil

Examples

The fossilized remains of Staurikosaurus were first discovered in Brazil in the 1930s.
Paleontologists believe Staurikosaurus was one of the earliest theropod dinosaurs.
Studies of Staurikosaurus provide valuable insight into dinosaur evolution during the Triassic.

Etymology

From Greek 'staurikos' (cross, referring to hip structure) + 'saurus' (lizard)
