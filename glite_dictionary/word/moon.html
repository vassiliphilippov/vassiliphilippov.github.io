Moon

1. NOUN

/ muːn / (AmE), / muːn / (BrE)
The natural satellite of the Earth, visible at night by reflected sunlight.
Earth's natural satellite

Examples

The astronauts admired the bright Moon during their historic mission.
We studied the phases of the Moon in our science class.
The full Moon illuminated the countryside with a silvery glow.

Synset Examples

the average distance to the Moon is 384,400 kilometers
men first stepped on the moon in 1969

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satellite - an object in orbit around a planet
  • Antonym: planet - a celestial body moving in an orbit around a star

Etymology

Old English 'mōna', from Proto-Germanic '*mēnô', related to Latin 'mensis' (month).
