scammony

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæməni / (AmE), / ˈskæməni / (BrE)
A tropical American morning glory, especially the plant Convolvulus scammonia, whose dried root is used as a purgative.
tropical American morning glory plant

Examples

The dried root of scammony is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
Botanists collected samples of scammony during their expedition in Central America.
Many herbal remedies in the past relied on extracts from scammony.

Etymology

From Latin 'scammonia', from Greek 'skammonia', referring to the plant.

3. NOUN

/ ˈskæməni / (AmE), / ˈskæməni / (BrE)
A twining plant native to the eastern Mediterranean, especially Asia Minor, with cream to purple flowers and thick roots that yield a cathartic resin.
twining plant yielding a purgative resin

Examples

The medicinal resin known as scammony has been used in traditional remedies for centuries.
The botanist identified the climbing plant as scammony during her fieldwork in Turkey.
Extracts from the root of scammony were once valued as potent laxatives.

Etymology

From Latin scammonia, from Greek skammonia (bindweed), originally from Semitic.
