self-seeker

1. NOUN

/ ˌselfˈsiːkər / (AmE), / ˌselfˈsiːkə / (BrE)
A person who acts primarily out of self-interest, placing personal advantage above ethical principles or the welfare of others.
a person motivated by self-interest

Examples

Politicians are often criticized as self-seekers who care more about votes than genuine progress.
His colleagues viewed him as a self-seeker because he always prioritized his own advancement.
The organization needs people with integrity, not self-seekers looking for personal gain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opportunist - a person who takes advantage of opportunities with little regard for principles
  • Antonym: altruist - a person unselfishly concerned for the welfare of others
