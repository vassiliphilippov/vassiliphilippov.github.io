Lepidochelys

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪdoʊˈkɛlɪs / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪdəʊˈkiːlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of sea turtles known as ridleys, including the Kemp's ridley and olive ridley turtles.
genus of ridley sea turtles

Examples

The genus Lepidochelys includes both the Kemp's ridley and the olive ridley sea turtles.
Researchers are studying the nesting habits of Lepidochelys populations along the coast.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the number of Lepidochelys turtles in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridley - either of two sea turtles in the genus Lepidochelys

Etymology

From Greek lepis (scale) + chelys (turtle).

2. NOUN

/ lɛpɪˈdɑkəlɪs / (AmE), / lɛpɪˈdɒkəlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of sea turtles known as ridleys, which includes the Kemp's ridley and olive ridley turtles.
a genus of sea turtles (ridleys)

Examples

Conservationists are monitoring populations of Lepidochelys in nesting areas along the Gulf of Mexico.
The genus Lepidochelys comprises the olive ridley and Kemp's ridley sea turtles.
Researchers study Lepidochelys to understand factors affecting sea turtle survival rates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridley - a common name for species in the genus Lepidochelys, a type of sea turtle

Etymology

From Greek 'lepis' meaning scale and 'chelys' meaning turtle.
