favorable

1. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪvərəbl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərəbl / (BrE)
giving an advantage or benefit; conducive to success or improvement
giving an advantage; beneficial

Examples

The terms of the deal were especially favorable for small businesses.
A favorable climate helped the crops grow earlier this year.
She received a favorable review, which boosted her confidence.

Synset Examples

a contract advantageous to our country
socially advantageous to entertain often

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advantageous - giving an advantage; beneficial
  • Antonym: unfavorable - not giving an advantage; detrimental or disadvantageous

Etymology

From Old French 'favorable', from Latin 'favorabilis' (favoured, pleasing).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪvərəbəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərəbl / (BrE)
giving or indicating a good chance of success; likely to result in or bring about a positive outcome
indicating a likely good outcome

Examples

She chose a favorable moment to bring up her proposal.
The company launched its product during a favorable economic climate.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfavorable - indicating a poor chance of success or a negative outcome

Etymology
