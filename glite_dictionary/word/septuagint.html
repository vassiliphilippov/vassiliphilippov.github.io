Septuagint

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛptjuədʒɪnt / (AmE), / ˈsɛptjʊədʒɪnt / (BrE)
the oldest extant Greek translation of the Hebrew Old Testament, traditionally believed to have been made by Jewish scholars in Alexandria at the request of Ptolemy II in the 3rd and 2nd centuries BCE
ancient Greek translation of the Old Testament

Examples

Many biblical scholars study the Septuagint to understand the development of early Christian theology.
The Septuagint differs in some passages from the Hebrew Masoretic Text of the Old Testament.
In the early Church, Greek-speaking Christians often relied on the Septuagint for their scriptures.

Etymology

From Latin 'Septuaginta' (seventy), referring to the tradition that seventy or seventy-two scholars worked on the translation.
