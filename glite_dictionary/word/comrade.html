comrade

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmˌræd / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ræd / (BrE)
a fellow member of the Communist Party, or a person with shared political interests, especially in communist or socialist contexts
fellow member of the Communist Party

Examples

The party members greeted each other as comrade during the meeting.
As a lifelong comrade, he believed strongly in socialist principles.
She addressed the crowd of comrades at the annual conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: companion - a person you spend a lot of time with
  • Synonym: ally - a person or group that supports you, especially in political contexts
  • Antonym: opponent - someone who disagrees or competes against you, especially in politics

Etymology

From French *camarade*, from Spanish *camarada* (roommate), ultimately from Latin *camera* (chamber).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmˌræd / (AmE), / ˈkɒmˌræd / (BrE)
a person who is a fellow member of a political or social group, especially used as a term of address among socialists and communists
fellow group member; political term of address

Examples

It is good to see you again, comrade, after so many years apart.
The union leader addressed each worker as comrade during the rally.
After achieving victory, he shook hands firmly with his fellow comrade.

Synset Examples

Greetings, comrade!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: companion - a person one spends time with or travels with
  • Antonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile

Etymology

From Spanish 'camarada' (companion, barrack mate), from Latin 'camera' (chamber).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmˌræd / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.reɪd / (BrE)
a companion or friend, especially one who shares activities, interests, or experiences with another person
a companion or close friend

Examples

He regarded his fellow soldiers as trusted comrades both on and off the battlefield.
After years of working together, they became close comrades and shared many memories.
She greeted her old college comrade with a warm embrace at the reunion.

Synset Examples

drinking companions
comrades in arms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: companion - a person who accompanies someone
  • Antonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile

Etymology

From French 'camarade', from Spanish 'camarada' (roommate, companion), originally from 'cámara' (chamber).
