benignity

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈnɪɡnəti / (AmE), / bɪˈnɪɡnɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being kind, gentle, and compassionate
kindness; gentleness

Examples

His benignity made him a beloved leader among his peers.
She treated everyone with a rare benignity that put people at ease.
Despite his power, the ruler was remembered for his justice and benignity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate
  • Antonym: malevolence - the state or condition of being malicious or wishing harm

Etymology

From Latin benignitas (kindness), from benignus (kind, well-born).

2. NOUN

/ bəˈnɪɡnəti / (AmE), / bɪˈnɪɡnəti / (BrE)
A kind act; the quality of being gentle, kind, or benevolent.
kind or gentle act

Examples

Her benignity towards the less fortunate made her beloved in the community.
Every benignity shown by the staff contributed to a welcoming atmosphere.
He was remembered for his many small acts of benignity throughout his career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate
  • Antonym: malice - the intention or desire to do evil; ill will

Etymology

From Latin 'benignitas' (kindness), from 'benignus' (kind, friendly).
