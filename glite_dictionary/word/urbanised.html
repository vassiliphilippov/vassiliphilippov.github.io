urbanised

1. ADJ

/ ˈɜːrbəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈɜːbənaɪzd / (BrE)
having adopted or been given the characteristics of a city or town; made urban in nature.
made or having become urban
british

Examples

Much of the region has become increasingly urbanised over the past few decades.
The urbanised zone stretches far beyond the city’s official boundaries.
Conservation efforts are challenging in highly urbanised areas.

Synset Examples

the urbanized Eastern states

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urbanized - having adopted or been given the characteristics of a city or town (US spelling)
  • Antonym: rural - relating to the countryside, not urban

Etymology

From urban + -ise, meaning to make or become urban; '-ise' is a suffix of Greek origin via Latin, common in British English spelling.
