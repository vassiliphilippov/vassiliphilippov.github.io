Struthiomimus

1. NOUN

/ struːθioʊˈmaɪməs / (AmE), / struːθiəʊˈmaɪməs / (BrE)
a genus of small, toothless, ostrich-like dinosaurs from the Late Cretaceous period, found in what is now Canada.
genus of toothless, ostrich-like dinosaurs

Examples

Fossils of Struthiomimus have provided valuable insight into dinosaur locomotion.
The Struthiomimus was characterized by its long legs and toothless beak.
Scientists believe Struthiomimus fed on plants, insects, and small animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornithomimus - a closely related genus of similar ostrich-like dinosaurs

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'strouthion' (ostrich) and 'mimos' (imitator), meaning 'ostrich mimic'.
