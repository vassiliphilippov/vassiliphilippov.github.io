struthiomimus

1. NOUN

/ struːθioʊˈmaɪməs / (AmE), / struːθiəʊˈmʌɪməs / (BrE)
a small, lightly built, toothless dinosaur of the late Cretaceous period that resembled an ostrich in size and proportions
a small, ostrich-like dinosaur

Examples

The fossil remains of a struthiomimus were discovered in Alberta, Canada.
Scientists believe that struthiomimus could run at high speeds, much like modern ostriches.
Museum exhibits often feature life-size reconstructions of the struthiomimus to show its bird-like features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornithomimus - a similar genus of ostrich-like dinosaurs

Etymology

From Latinized Greek, combining 'struthio' (ostrich) and 'mimos' (mimic, imitator).
