demonisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːməˌnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːmənaɪˈzeɪʃn̩ / (BrE)
The act of portraying someone or something as wicked or diabolically evil.
the act of portraying as evil

Examples

The demonisation of rival politicians is a common feature of election campaigns.
Some media outlets have been criticized for their demonisation of immigrants.
The historian examined the demonisation of witches in seventeenth-century Europe.

Synset Examples

the demonization of our enemies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vilification - the act of speaking or writing in a disparaging manner

Etymology

From verb 'demonise' + '-ation'; 'demonise' from 'demon' (evil spirit, from Greek daimōn).
