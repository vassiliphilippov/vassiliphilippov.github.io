barbarousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrbəɹəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbəɹəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely cruel, brutal, or lacking in humanity
extreme cruelty; inhumanity

Examples

She was shocked by the barbarousness of the methods used during the conflict.
Historians still debate the barbarousness of the conquerors' actions.
The film depicted the barbarousness of war in unflinching detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruelty - the quality of causing pain or suffering deliberately
  • Antonym: humaneness - the quality of being compassionate or kind to people or animals

Etymology

From 'barbarous' + '-ness', ultimately from Latin 'barbarus' (foreign, savage).
