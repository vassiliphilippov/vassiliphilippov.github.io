worldly

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɜrldli / (AmE), / ˈwɜːldli / (BrE)
experienced and sophisticated in the ways of the world; knowledgeable because of practical experience.
sophisticated and experienced in the world

Examples

After years of travel, Sophia became quite worldly in her attitudes and tastes.
His worldly wisdom made him a valuable adviser to many young professionals.
Unlike his worldly friends, he was unfamiliar with big city customs.

Synset Examples

the blase traveler refers to the ocean he has crossed as ‘the pond’
the benefits of his worldly wisdom

Related Terms

  • Antonym: naive - lacking worldly experience or sophistication
  • Synonym: sophisticated - experienced and knowledgeable about the world

Etymology

From Old English 'woruldlic' (secular, of the world); 'woruld' (world) + '-lic' (-ly).

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɜrldli / (AmE), / ˈwɜːldli / (BrE)
concerned with material or temporal affairs rather than spiritual matters
concerned with material, not spiritual, matters

Examples

She was more interested in worldly success than in spiritual fulfillment.
Many religions encourage their followers to give up worldly possessions.
His advice focused on worldly matters rather than questions of faith.

Synset Examples

worldly goods and advancement
temporal possessions of the church

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spiritual - concerned with the soul or religious matters
  • Synonym: materialistic - excessively concerned with material possessions

Etymology

From Old English 'weoroldlic' (earthly, temporal), from 'weorold' (world) + '-lic' (like, pertaining to).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02587499-a
Synset POS: a
