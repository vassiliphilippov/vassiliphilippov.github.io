Gentiana detonsa

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛnˈʃæn.i.ə dɪˈtɒn.sə / (AmE), / dʒɛnˈʃæn.i.ə dɪˈtɒn.sə / (BrE)
A medium-tall species of fringed gentian with pale-blue to blue-purple flowers, found in circumboreal regions.
species of fringed gentian

Examples

The pale-blue flowers of Gentiana detonsa can be seen blossoming in northern tundra regions each summer.
Botanists studied the distribution of Gentiana detonsa across boreal forests and wetlands.
Many nature enthusiasts find Gentiana detonsa particularly beautiful for its delicately fringed petals.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Gentiana' from Gentianus, and 'detonsa' meaning shorn or trimmed, reflecting the fringed petals.
