squirm

1. NOUN

/ skwɝm / (AmE), / skwɜːm / (BrE)
a twisting or wriggling movement, especially as a result of discomfort or nervousness
the act of wriggling or twisting

Examples

The child's squirm in his seat made it difficult for the teacher to focus.
With every squirm, the trapped fish tried desperately to escape the net.
A visible squirm of discomfort crossed her face as she awaited the results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wriggle - to twist and turn with quick movements
  • Antonym: stillness - absence of movement

2. VERB

/ skwɝm / (AmE), / skwɜːm / (BrE)
to twist or wriggle one's body with small movements, especially as a result of discomfort, embarrassment, or struggle
wriggle or twist, often from discomfort

Examples

The little boy began to squirm in his seat during the long ceremony.
She tried not to squirm while the dentist examined her tooth.
The captured fish continued to squirm in the net.

Synset Examples

The prisoner writhed in discomfort
The child tried to wriggle free from his aunt's embrace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to writhe - to make twisting movements due to discomfort or pain
  • Synonym: wriggle - to twist and turn with quick movements
  • Antonym: freeze - to become motionless, typically from fear or shock
