Lucania

1. PROPN

/ luˈkeɪniə / (AmE), / luˈkeɪnɪə / (BrE)
an ancient region in southern Italy, forming part of the instep of the Italian peninsula, roughly corresponding to modern-day Basilicata.
ancient region in southern Italy

Examples

The ancient Greeks established colonies in Lucania during the 6th century BCE.
Archaeologists uncovered numerous artifacts from Lucania dating back to the Roman era.
She traveled to southern Italy to visit the historical sites of Lucania.

Etymology

From Latin 'Lucania', the ancient name of the region in Italy.
