revolting

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈvoʊltɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈvəʊltɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely unpleasant; causing disgust or strong aversion
causing disgust or strong aversion

Examples

The sight of the moldy bread was absolutely revolting to everyone at the table.
She found the cruelty described in the article to be truly revolting.
The kitchen was in a revolting state after the party, with garbage everywhere.

Synset Examples

a disgusting smell
distasteful language
a loathsome disease
the idea of eating meat is repellent to me
revolting food
a wicked stench

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgusting - causing a strong feeling of dislike or disapproval
  • Synonym: repellent - causing dislike or disgust
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

From the verb 'revolt', from Latin 'revolvere' (to roll back, turn away).
