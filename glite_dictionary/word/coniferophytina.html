Coniferophytina

1. NOUN

/ kəˌnɪfərəˈfaɪtɪnə / (AmE), / kəˌnɪfərəˈfɪtɪnə / (BrE)
A subdivision of cone-bearing gymnosperms, comprising most coniferous trees and related extinct groups, dating back to the Carboniferous period.
cone-bearing gymnosperm plant group

Examples

The majority of modern forests in northern regions are dominated by Coniferophytina.
Researchers traced the evolutionary history of Coniferophytina back to the Carboniferous period.
Many familiar evergreens, like pines and firs, are classified within the Coniferophytina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conifer - a tree that bears cones and needle-like leaves

Etymology

From Latin 'conifer' (cone-bearing) and Greek 'phytina' (plants).

2. NOUN

/ kəˌnɪfərəˈfaɪtɪnə / (AmE), / kəˌnɪfərəˈfʌɪtɪnə / (BrE)
A botanical subdivision comprising cone-bearing gymnosperms, including conifers and their relatives, dating from the Carboniferous period.
botanical subdivision of cone-bearing gymnosperms

Examples

The Coniferophytina includes many of the world's tallest tree species, such as redwoods and pines.
Paleobotanists study fossils to trace the evolution of Coniferophytina from the Carboniferous period.
Both living and extinct members of Coniferophytina have played a critical role in shaping Earth's forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pinopsida - class of conifers; part of Coniferophytina
  • Synonym: Ginkgopsida - class of ginkgo relatives; part of Coniferophytina

Etymology

From New Latin: Latin 'conifer' (cone-bearing) + Greek 'phyton' (plant) + botanical suffix '-ina' (subdivision).
