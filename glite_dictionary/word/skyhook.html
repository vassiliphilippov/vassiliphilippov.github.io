skyhook

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaɪˌhʊk / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌhʊk / (BrE)
a fanciful device, imagined as a hook suspended from the sky, often used humorously to refer to something impossible or absurd
imaginary hook suspended from the sky

Examples

The mechanic sent the apprentice searching for a skyhook, knowing full well it didn't exist.
In engineering jokes, a skyhook is something used to describe an impossible solution.
He claimed he could lift the car with a skyhook, but everyone knew it was just a trick.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskaɪˌhʊk / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌhʊk / (BrE)
A helicopter equipped with a reel of steel cable used to lift and transport heavy objects.
helicopter for lifting heavy objects

Examples

The construction crew used a skyhook to move the enormous steel beams into place.
A skyhook can transport equipment to remote or inaccessible locations quickly and efficiently.
Spectators watched as the skyhook lifted the stranded car off the mountainside.

Etymology

From 'sky' + 'hook', originally used humorously for a nonexistent device, later applied to helicopters with lifting cables.
