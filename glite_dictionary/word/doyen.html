doyen

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈdɔɪ.ən / (BrE)
The most respected or prominent male member of a group, especially one who is the oldest or most experienced.
senior or prominent male member of a group

Examples

As the doyen of jazz critics, his opinion was sought by musicians around the world.
The doyen of the faculty retired last spring after teaching for fifty years.
He was widely regarded as the doyen of foreign correspondents in the region.

Synset Examples

he is the dean of foreign correspondents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doyenne - the most respected or prominent female member of a group

Etymology

From French doyen (dean, senior member), from Latin decanus (leader of ten).
