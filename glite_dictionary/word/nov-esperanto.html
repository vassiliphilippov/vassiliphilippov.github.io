Nov-Esperanto

1. NOUN

/ noʊv ɛˈspɛrəntoʊ / (AmE), / nəʊv ɛˈspɛrəntəʊ / (BrE)
an artificial language developed as a simplified form of Esperanto
artificial language based on Esperanto

Examples

Nov-Esperanto was created in an attempt to address perceived complexities in original Esperanto.
Linguists compare Nov-Esperanto and Esperanto to understand how artificial languages evolve.
Few people speak Nov-Esperanto today, but it remains of interest to language reformers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Esperanto - an artificial international auxiliary language created in the late 19th century

Etymology

From Esperanto, combining 'nov' (new) and 'Esperanto' (the name of the original artificial language).
