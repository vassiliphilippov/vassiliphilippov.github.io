long-time

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔŋ.taɪm / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋ.taɪm / (BrE)
having existed or continued in a particular role, relationship, or state for a long period of time.
having existed for a long time

Examples

She is a long-time supporter of the local theater and attends every performance.
After working for the company for decades, he finally retired as their long-time manager.
I met my long-time friend Mark for lunch yesterday.

Synset Examples

a longtime friend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longstanding - having existed or continued for a long time
