cleistogamy

1. NOUN

/ klaɪˈstɑːɡəmi / (AmE), / klaɪˈstɒɡəmi / (BrE)
the production of small, closed, self-pollinating flowers that do not open
production of non-opening, self-pollinating flowers

Examples

Some plants use cleistogamy to ensure reproduction even when pollinators are scarce.
The study explored how cleistogamy increases the likelihood of self-fertilization in certain species.
Because of cleistogamy, these flowers rarely open or rely on external pollinators.

Etymology

From Greek 'kleistos' (closed) and 'gamos' (marriage).
