ictal

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪk.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˈɪk.təl / (BrE)
relating to or occurring during a seizure or convulsion
relating to a seizure
medical

Examples

The doctor observed an ictal pattern on the patient's EEG during the seizure.
Ictal confusion is common immediately following complex partial seizures.
Medications are often prescribed to reduce the frequency of ictal episodes in epilepsy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interictal - occurring between seizures
  • Antonym: postictal - occurring after a seizure

Etymology

From Latin 'ictus' (a blow, stroke), used in medical sense for seizure.
