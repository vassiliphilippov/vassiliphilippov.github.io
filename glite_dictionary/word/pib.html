PiB

1. NOUN

/ piː aɪ ˈbiː / (AmE), / piː aɪ ˈbiː / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1,024 tebibytes or 2^50 bytes
unit of digital information (2^50 bytes)

Examples

The backup server has a total storage capacity of 3 PiB.
Modern data centers often manage databases that exceed 1 PiB in size.
Storing all that high-resolution video would require several PiB of disk space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pebibyte - unit of digital information, equal to 2^50 bytes
  • Synonym: petabyte - unit of digital information, equal to 10^15 bytes

Etymology

Acronym of 'pebibyte', from 'peta-' (10^15) and 'binary byte'. Defined by the International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC) in 1998.
