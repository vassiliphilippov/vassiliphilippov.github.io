quassia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːsiə / (AmE), / ˈkwæsɪə / (BrE)
a bitter substance obtained from the wood and bark of certain tropical trees, used as an insecticide, tonic, and vermifuge
bitter compound from tropical trees, used medicinally

Examples

The herbal tonic was flavored with quassia for its bitter taste and medicinal benefits.
Gardeners sometimes use a quassia extract as a natural insecticide to protect plants from pests.
Quassia chips are steeped in water to produce a solution used for treating intestinal worms.

Etymology

Named after Graman Quassi, an 18th-century Surinamese herbalist who discovered its medicinal uses.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːsiə / (AmE), / ˈkwɒsiə / (BrE)
A tropical shrub or small tree native to South America, known for its scarlet flowers and yellowish wood, from which a bitter medicinal extract is obtained.
tropical shrub/tree used medicinally

Examples

The bark of quassia is often used in herbal medicine for its bitter properties.
Botanists are studying the unique flowers of the quassia tree found in South America.
Extracts from quassia wood are sometimes used as natural insecticides.

Etymology

From New Latin, after Quassi, the surname of the Surinam slave who introduced its use as a remedy.
