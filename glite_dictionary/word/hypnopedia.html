hypnopedia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpnoʊˈpiːdiə / (AmE), / ˌhɪpnəˈpiːdɪə / (BrE)
the teaching of information or skills to a person during sleep, typically by playing recordings
teaching or learning during sleep

Examples

Some scientists remain skeptical about the effectiveness of hypnopedia for language learning.
The novel described a society that relied on hypnopedia to condition its citizens from birth.
Although hypnopedia sounds appealing, studies show it has significant limitations.

Etymology

From Greek 'hypnos' (sleep) and 'paideia' (education, learning).
