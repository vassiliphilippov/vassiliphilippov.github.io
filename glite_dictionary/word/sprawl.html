sprawl

1. NOUN

/ sprɔl / (AmE), / sprɔːl / (BrE)
An ungainly or awkward posture with the limbs spread out in different directions.
awkward, spread-out posture

Examples

He fell onto the couch in a sprawl of arms and legs.
The children landed in a sprawl after tumbling down the hill together.
She woke to find her cat in a luxurious sprawl across the bed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprawl - to sit or lie with the limbs spread out in an awkward way (verb)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05089544-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ sprɔl / (AmE), / sprɔːl / (BrE)
A continuous area of urban development, typically characterized by rapid expansion and a lack of planning.
extensive area of urban development

Examples

The city has become a vast sprawl of neighborhoods, shopping malls, and highways.
Many environmentalists criticize the unchecked sprawl around major urban centers.
From the air, the sprawl of Los Angeles seems to stretch endlessly toward the horizon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urbanization - the process by which towns and cities grow larger
  • Synonym: conurbation - an extended urban area, typically consisting of several towns merging with the suburbs of a central city
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08557280-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ sprɔl / (AmE), / sprɔːl / (BrE)
to spread out over a large area in an untidy or irregular way
spread irregularly or untidily

Examples

The city continued to sprawl outwards, covering the surrounding countryside with new buildings.
We watched the ivy sprawl across the garden wall, taking over every inch of space.
The roots of the old tree sprawled in all directions, breaking through the pavement.

Synset Examples

Branches straggling out quite far

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straggle - to spread or be scattered in an irregular way

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'sprawlen' (to move the limbs awkwardly), of Scandinavian origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02036072-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ sprɔl / (AmE), / sprɔːl / (BrE)
to sit or lie with arms and legs spread out in an ungainly or relaxed way
sit or lie with limbs spread out

Examples

He likes to sprawl on the sofa after work, taking up half the cushions.
The children sprawled on the grass, exhausted after their game.
She was sprawling in an armchair, completely at ease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recline - to lean or lie back in a relaxed position
  • Antonym: to curl up - to arrange the body in a curled or compact position

Etymology

From Middle English 'sprawlen', of uncertain origin, possibly Scandinavian.

Source: WordNet
