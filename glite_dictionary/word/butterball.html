butterball

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌtərˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəˌbɔːl / (BrE)
a small North American diving duck, especially the male, which has a bushy head plumage; also called the bufflehead.
a small North American diving duck

Examples

A flock of butterballs was spotted on the lake during the migration season.
The male butterball is easily recognized by its bushy head feathers and small size.
Birdwatchers often look for the distinctive plumage of the butterball in North American wetlands.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚˌbɑl / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəˌbɔːl / (BrE)
a person who is noticeably plump or rotund, often in a gently teasing or affectionate manner
a plump or rotund person
informal

Examples

As a child, I was a little butterball with chubby cheeks.
His classmates called him butterball, but he didn't mind the nickname.
The puppy grew into a butterball after being spoiled with treats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chubby - slightly fat in a way that people usually find attractive
  • Antonym: slim - thin, especially in an attractive way

Etymology

Formed from 'butter' and 'ball', referencing the round, smooth appearance of a ball of butter.
