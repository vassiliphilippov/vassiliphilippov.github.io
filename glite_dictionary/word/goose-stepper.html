goose-stepper

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡusˌstɛpər / (AmE), / ˈɡuːsˌstɛpə / (BrE)
a person, especially a soldier, who marches using the goose step, a formal military marching style characterized by straight-legged steps
person who marches with goose step

Examples

The goose-stepper maintained perfect synchronization with the rest of the marching unit.
During the parade, one goose-stepper lost his balance and broke formation.
Reporters praised the goose-stepper for his discipline and attention to detail.

Synset Examples

Being a goose-stepper requires discipline and practice.
The goose-stepper led the formation flawlessly.
The goose-stepper caught everyone's attention with his uniform.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goose-step - a formal military marching style with straight-legged steps

Etymology

Formed from 'goose-step' (a style of march) + '-er' (denoting a person who performs an action).
