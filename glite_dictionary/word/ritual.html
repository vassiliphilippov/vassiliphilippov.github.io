ritual

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪtʃuəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃuəl / (BrE)
relating to or performed as part of a religious or solemn ceremony or rite.
relating to religious ceremonies or rites

Examples

The priest wore his ritual garments during the ceremony.
The community observed several ritual practices during the festival.
A ritual meal was prepared according to tradition.

Synset Examples

ritual killing

Etymology

From Latin 'ritualis' (relating to rites), from 'ritus' (rite).

WordNet ID: oewn-02903908-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃuəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃu.əl / (BrE)
a set of fixed actions or ceremonies, often performed regularly, especially as part of a tradition or religious practice
customary practice or ceremony

Examples

The morning coffee ritual helped her start the day on the right foot.
Every culture has its own unique funeral rituals.
He followed the same bedtime ritual every night to help him sleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceremony - a formal event performed on important occasions
  • Degree Lower: habit - a regular practice or routine, often one that is hard to give up

Etymology

From Latin ritualis (pertaining to rites), from ritus (rite, custom).

5. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃuəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃuəl / (BrE)
a set pattern of actions or behaviors regularly followed, especially as a habit or custom
repeated pattern of behavior

Examples

Brushing his teeth before bed had become a nightly ritual for him.
Each morning she followed the same ritual of making coffee and reading the news.
Checking emails as soon as she arrived at work was an unspoken office ritual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: routine - a regular way of doing things; a standard procedure
  • Synonym: ceremony - a formal religious or public occasion, typically one celebrating a particular event
  • Antonym: spontaneity - the condition of being spontaneous; not planned or patterned behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'ritualis', from 'ritus' (rite, ceremony).
