violator

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪ.əˌleɪ.tər / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.əˌleɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who breaks a law, rule, or agreement
someone who breaks a law or rule

Examples

The new law includes strict penalties for any violator who is caught in the act.
As a violator of the company's code of conduct, he faced immediate suspension.
Environmental regulations are enforced to ensure that every violator is held accountable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offender - a person who has committed a crime or illegal act
  • Synonym: lawbreaker - someone who breaks the law
  • Antonym: upholder - a person who adheres to or enforces law or rules

Etymology

From Latin violator, from violare (to violate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈvaɪəleɪtə / (BrE)
A person who commits a sexual assault or violates another sexually.
person who sexually assaults others

Examples

The court found the violator guilty of multiple counts of sexual assault.
Support centers offer help to survivors while working to prosecute the violator.
Authorities arrested a suspected violator after several reports of sexual misconduct in the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offender - a person who commits an illegal act
  • Synonym: perpetrator - someone who carries out a harmful, illegal, or immoral act

Etymology

From Latin 'violator', from 'violare' (to violate).
