Rhodophyta

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈdɑːfɪtə / (AmE), / rəʊˈdɒfɪtə / (BrE)
A division of mostly marine algae known as red algae, characterized by reddish pigments and commonly found in coastal waters.
division of red algae

Examples

Marine biologists study Rhodophyta to better understand coastal ecosystems.
The vibrant colors of Rhodophyta are due to their unique pigments.
Many species in the Rhodophyta division are an important part of sushi as nori.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red algae - A common name for algae in the Rhodophyta division

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'rhodon' (rose) + 'phyton' (plant).
