Trichostema lanceolatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌtraɪkoʊˈstiːmə ˌlænsiəˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌtrɪkəʊˈstiːmə ˌlænsiəˈleɪtəm / (BrE)
an aromatic plant native to the western United States, also known as vinegar weed or turpentine weed.
aromatic plant of western US

Examples

The aromatic Trichostema lanceolatum blooms in the dry foothills each summer.
Botanists often refer to Trichostema lanceolatum by its common name, vinegar weed.
I recognized Trichostema lanceolatum in the field by its distinctive scent and narrow leaves.

Etymology

From Greek 'tricho-' (hair) and 'stema' (stamen), referring to the hairy stamens; with 'lanceolatum' from Latin, meaning 'lance-shaped', describing its leaves.
