setup

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈsɛtʌp / (BrE)
an act of tricking someone into being accused or convicted of a crime they did not commit, often by arranging false evidence
act of framing someone with false charges

Examples

After the investigation, it became clear that the entire case was a setup to ruin his reputation.
She claimed she was the victim of a setup and had nothing to do with the crime.
Many believed the drug charges were simply a setup orchestrated by political rivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frame-up - the act of falsely incriminating someone by deceptive means

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈset.ʌp / (BrE)
a set of equipment or an arrangement of things needed for a particular purpose or activity
equipment or arrangement for a specific function

Examples

The recording studio has an impressive setup with all the latest technology.
She quickly assembled the camping setup before it got dark.
Their new office setup is designed to promote teamwork and communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - a way in which things are arranged
  • Synonym: equipment - the necessary items for a particular purpose

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈsɛtʌp / (BrE)
The way in which something is organized, arranged, or planned.
the organization or arrangement of something

Examples

The office setup was designed to encourage collaboration among employees.
We need to improve the security setup for our computer system.
It took her a while to get used to the new setup after the reorganization.

Synset Examples

it takes time to learn the setup around here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - the way in which things are arranged
  • Synonym: configuration - the arrangement of parts or elements of something
  • Antonym: disarray - a state of disorder or untidiness

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'set up', first attested in noun form in the early 20th century.
