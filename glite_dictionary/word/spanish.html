Spanish

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ / (BrE)
the people of Spain collectively
people of Spain

Examples

The Spanish are known for their rich cultural traditions, such as flamenco and bullfighting.
Many Spanish moved to Latin America during the colonial period.
The Spanish have a reputation for being very welcoming to visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spaniard - a person from Spain
  • Antonym: Portuguese - the people of Portugal

Etymology

From the Old French 'Espaignols', from Latin 'Hispania' (Spain).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈspanɪʃ / (BrE)
the Romance language spoken in most of Spain and many countries of Latin America and elsewhere
Romance language of Spain and Latin America

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Spanish.
Many signs in California are written in Spanish as well as English.
After studying for a year, he can now hold a conversation in Spanish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish - of or relating to Spain or its people (adjective)

Etymology

From Old French 'espagnol', from 'Espagne' (Spain), from Latin 'Hispania'.
