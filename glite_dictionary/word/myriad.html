myriad

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪr.i.əd / (AmE), / ˈmɪr.i.əd / (BrE)
an extremely large, but unspecified, number of things or people
a very large number

Examples

He faced a myriad of details when planning the event.
A myriad of stars filled the night sky, twinkling above the quiet town.
The internet has opened up a myriad of new opportunities for learning.

Synset Examples

he faced a myriad of details

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multitude - a large number of people or things
  • Synonym: countless - too many to be counted

Etymology

From Ancient Greek μυριάς (myriás, ten thousand), from μυρίοι (myrioi, countless).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɪr.i.əd / (AmE), / ˈmɪə.ri.əd / (BrE)
Extremely numerous; too many to be counted.
countless, innumerable

Examples

The night sky was dotted with myriad stars, each one sparkling brightly.
She faced myriad challenges while starting her own business.
There are myriad reasons why people love traveling the world.

Synset Examples

countless hours
an infinite number of reasons
innumerable difficulties
the multitudinous seas
myriad stars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countless - too many to be counted
  • Synonym: innumerable - too many to be counted
  • Antonym: finite - having limits; not infinite

Etymology

From Greek 'myrias', meaning 'ten thousand'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00302805-s
Synset POS: s
