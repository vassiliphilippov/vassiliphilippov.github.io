forester

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.ə.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɒr.ɪ.stə / (BrE)
a person trained in the science or practice of managing forests and woodland areas
person trained in managing forests

Examples

The forester explained the importance of sustainable management to protect wildlife and resources.
After studying environmental science, she became a forester in a national park.
The forester oversaw the replanting of trees after the forest fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forest ranger - a person employed to protect and manage a forest
  • Antonym: logger - a person whose job is cutting down trees for timber

Etymology

From Old French 'forestier', from Medieval Latin 'forestarius' (of the forest).
