topicalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɑːpɪkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɒpɪkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The construction of a sentence so that the topic or focus is placed at the beginning for emphasis, often by moving it from its typical position.
linguistic process of fronting topic

Examples

Topicalization is common in informal speech when someone wants to emphasize a point.
Linguists often study topicalization as a way to understand sentence structure across languages.
The teacher explained that topicalization can make a statement stand out in English.

Synset Examples

‘Those girls, they giggle when they see me’ and ‘Cigarettes, you couldn't pay me to smoke them’ are examples of topicalization

Etymology

From 'topical' (relating to subject) + '-ization'.
