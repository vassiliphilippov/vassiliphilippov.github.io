yoke

1. VERB

/ joʊk / (AmE), / jəʊk / (BrE)
to join or link together, especially by means of a yoke; to connect two animals or objects so that they move or act together
to join or connect, especially with a yoke

Examples

The farmer yoked the oxen together to plow the field.
Their efforts were yoked to achieve a common goal.
He yoked his ambitions to the success of the organization.

Synset Examples

yoke the oxen together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unite - to come or bring together to form a single unit
  • Antonym: separate - to set or keep apart

Etymology

From Old English *geoc* (yoke), from Proto-Germanic *jukam*, related to Latin *iugum* (yoke).
