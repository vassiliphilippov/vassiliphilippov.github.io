lane

1. NOUN

/ leɪn / (AmE), / leɪn / (BrE)
a narrow road, especially in the countryside, or a division of a road marked for use by a single line of vehicles
narrow road or part of a road

Examples

She lives on a quiet country lane surrounded by trees.
Cyclists are encouraged to use the bike lane on Main Street.
The car drifted into the next lane and had to swerve back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alley - a narrow passageway between or behind buildings

Etymology

Old English *lanu* (narrow way, lane).

2. NOUN

/ leɪn / (AmE), / leɪn / (BrE)
a narrow road, track, or a designated division of a road or path, especially one set aside for vehicles, swimmers, or athletes
narrow road or path division

Examples

The outside lane of the pool was reserved for fast swimmers.
Drivers should stay in their own lane to avoid accidents.
There is a bike lane alongside the main road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: road - a wide way leading from one place to another
  • Synonym: track - a prepared route for vehicles, trains, or races
  • Degree Higher: highway - a main road for travel

Etymology

From Old English lanu (narrow road, strip of land).

Synset POS: n
