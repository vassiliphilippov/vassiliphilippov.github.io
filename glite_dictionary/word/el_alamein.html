El Alamein

1. PROPN

/ ɛl ˌæləˈmeɪn / (AmE), / ɛl ˌæl.əˈmeɪn / (BrE)
A town in northern Egypt, known as the site of two major World War II battles in 1942 between Allied and Axis forces, resulting in a decisive Allied victory.
site of decisive World War II battles in Egypt

Examples

The victory at El Alamein marked a turning point in the North African campaign of World War II.
Many soldiers who fought at El Alamein later remembered the harsh desert conditions.
The Second Battle of El Alamein is widely studied in military history for its strategic importance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Stalingrad - major World War II battle in the Soviet Union
  • Synonym: Battle of Midway - pivotal naval battle of World War II in the Pacific

Etymology

From Arabic العلمين (al-ʿAlamayn), meaning 'the two flags' or 'the two signs.'

2. PROPN

/ ɛl ˌæləˈmeɪn / (AmE), / ɛl ˌaləˈmeɪn / (BrE)
A village in northern Egypt, west of Alexandria, known as the site of a decisive Allied victory over German forces in World War II (1942).
village and WWII battle site in Egypt

Examples

The Battle of El Alamein marked a turning point in North Africa during World War II.
Many veterans who fought at El Alamein remembered it as the fiercest battle of their lives.
Historians often cite El Alamein as a key Allied victory that shifted the momentum in the war.

Etymology

From Arabic العلمين‎ al-ʿAlamayn (the two flags or markers).
