coney

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊni / (AmE), / ˈkəʊni / (BrE)
A black-spotted, usually dusky-colored fish with reddish fins, found in warm waters.
a type of spotted fish with reddish fins

Examples

The diver spotted a brightly colored coney hiding among the coral reefs.
Several coneys were caught during the day's fishing trip.
The marine biologist studied the coney for its distinctive reddish fins.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊni / (AmE), / ˈkəʊni / (BrE)
an old term for a rabbit or similar burrowing animal, especially one of the family Leporidae.
old term for a rabbit
archaic

Examples

In medieval times, people often hunted the coney for meat and fur.
The warren was home to dozens of coneys, scurrying between their burrows.
He read an old English tale featuring a wise coney outsmarting a fox.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbit - a small, burrowing mammal with long ears and soft fur

Etymology

From Middle English 'coning' or 'conie', from Old French 'conil', from Latin 'cuniculus' (rabbit).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊni / (AmE), / ˈkəʊni / (BrE)
A small, herbivorous mammal found in Africa and Asia, with rodent-like incisors and hoof-like toes; also known as a hyrax.
small, hoofed mammal (hyrax) of Africa and Asia

Examples

Until recently, scientists believed the coney was closely related to rodents, but now they link it to elephants.
The rocky outcrops were home to dozens of coneys basking in the sun each morning.
A coney uses its sharp incisors to graze on grasses and bark in its native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyrax - small herbivorous mammal native to Africa and the Middle East

Etymology

From Old French *conil*, from Latin *cuniculus* (rabbit, burrow), though now also refers to hyraxes.

4. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊni / (AmE), / ˈkəʊni / (BrE)
a small, short-eared burrowing mammal found in rocky uplands of Asia and western North America, especially a pika; formerly used to refer to rabbits
a small burrowing mammal (pika or rabbit)

Examples

The coney darts between the rocks to avoid predators in the mountain meadows.
Many early explorers mistook the coney for a type of rabbit.
The coney is well adapted to cold, rocky environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pika - a small, mountain-dwelling mammal related to rabbits
  • Synonym: rabbit - a small burrowing mammal with long ears

Etymology

From Middle English 'coning', from Old French 'conil' (rabbit), from Latin 'cuniculus'
