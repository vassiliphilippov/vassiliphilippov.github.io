maisonnette

1. NOUN

/ meɪ.zəˈnet / (AmE), / meɪ.zɒˈnet / (BrE)
a small self-contained apartment, usually on two floors, within a larger building and with its own entrance from the outside
small two-level apartment with its own entrance
british

Examples

They decided to downsize and moved into a modern maisonnette near the city center.
The maisonnette has both a private entrance and a small garden.
She rented a charming maisonnette with bedrooms upstairs and a living area below.

Etymology

From French 'maisonnette' (diminutive of 'maison', meaning 'house').

2. NOUN

/ meɪzəˈnɛt / (AmE), / ˌmeɪzəˈnɛt / (BrE)
a small house, especially one that is part of a larger building and has its own entrance
small house, often part of a larger building

Examples

She rented a cozy maisonnette near the railway station for her stay in London.
The couple bought a stylish maisonnette with its own private garden.
A maisonnette typically occupies two floors and has a separate entrance from the rest of the building.

Etymology

From French ‘maisonnette’, diminutive of ‘maison’ (house).
