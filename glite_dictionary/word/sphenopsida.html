Sphenopsida

1. NOUN

/ sfɪˈnɑpsɪdə / (AmE), / sfɪˈnɒpsɪdə / (BrE)
A class of vascular plants comprising the horsetails and their extinct relatives.
class of plants including horsetails

Examples

Fossils of Sphenopsida have been found in rocks dating back to the Devonian period.
The Sphenopsida are distinguished by their jointed stems and reduced leaves.
Modern horsetails are the only living descendants of the ancient Sphenopsida.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsetail - a plant of the genus Equisetum, known for jointed stems and brush-like appearance

Etymology

From Greek sphēn (wedge) + -opsis (appearance), referring to the wedge-shaped joints.

2. NOUN

/ sfɛˌnɑːpˈsaɪdə / (AmE), / sfɛˌnɒpˈsɪdə / (BrE)
A class of vascular plants that includes horsetails and their extinct relatives, characterized by jointed stems and small leaves arranged in whorls.
class of plants including horsetails

Examples

Fossils of Sphenopsida reveal diverse forms that flourished in ancient swamps.
The Sphenopsida are recognized for their jointed, hollow stems and reduced leaves.
Botanists place horsetails in the plant class Sphenopsida, although only the genus Equisetum survives today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Equisetum - genus of living horsetails within Sphenopsida

Etymology

From Greek 'sphen' (wedge) and 'opsis' (appearance), referencing the shape of some plant structures.
