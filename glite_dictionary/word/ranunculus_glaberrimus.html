Ranunculus glaberrimus

1. NOUN

/ rəˈnʌŋkjələs ɡləˈbɛrɪməs / (AmE), / rəˈnʌŋkjələs ɡlaˈbɛrɪməs / (BrE)
a small, early-flowering buttercup with shiny yellow flowers, native to western North America
a buttercup species native to western North America

Examples

The Ranunculus glaberrimus adds a splash of yellow to the sagebrush plains each spring.
Botanists discovered a rare population of Ranunculus glaberrimus in the remote valley.
Early in the year, hikers can spot Ranunculus glaberrimus blooming near streams in western Montana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttercup - a yellow-flowered plant of the genus Ranunculus

Etymology

From Latin: Ranunculus meaning 'little frog' (genus of buttercups) and glaberrimus meaning 'very smooth' or 'hairless'.
