ascariasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæskəˈraɪəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌaskəˈraɪəsɪs / (BrE)
An infestation of the intestines in humans caused by Ascaris roundworms, leading to various gastrointestinal symptoms.
intestinal infestation by Ascaris roundworms
medical

Examples

Ascariasis is one of the most common intestinal worm infections worldwide.
Children are particularly at risk of developing ascariasis in areas with poor sanitation.
Doctors diagnosed the patient with ascariasis after observing the symptoms and lab results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roundworm infection - any infection caused by parasitic roundworms

Etymology

From modern Latin, from 'Ascaris' (a genus of roundworms) + '-iasis' (denoting a disease or condition).
