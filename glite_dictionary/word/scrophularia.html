Scrophularia

1. NOUN

/ skroʊˌfjʊˈlɛriə / (AmE), / skrəˌfjʊˈlæriə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the family Scrophulariaceae, commonly known as figworts, historically noted for their supposed medicinal properties.
genus of plants; figworts

Examples

Scrophularia nodosa is commonly known as the common figwort.
Many traditional herbal remedies used Scrophularia species for skin conditions.
The genus Scrophularia contains several plants with tubular flowers and square stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: figwort - common name for plants of the genus Scrophularia

Etymology

From Latin *scrophularia*, named for a supposed use in treating scrofula.

2. NOUN

/ skrəˌfjulˈɛəriə / (AmE), / skrɒˌfjʊˈlɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Scrophulariaceae, commonly known as figworts, once believed to cure scrofula.
genus of plants (figworts) in Scrophulariaceae

Examples

The medicinal uses of Scrophularia have been documented since ancient times.
Many species in the genus Scrophularia are commonly referred to as figworts.
Researchers studied the properties of Scrophularia to investigate its reputed healing effects.

Etymology

From Latin scrophularia, referring to scrofula (a form of tuberculosis), due to the belief in the plant's medicinal properties.
