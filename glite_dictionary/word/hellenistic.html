Hellenistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɛlɪˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to Greek history, culture, or art after Alexander the Great, especially from the late 4th to the 1st century BC.
of Greek culture after Alexander the Great

Examples

The museum's collection includes many Hellenistic sculptures from ancient Greece.
Her research focuses on Hellenistic influence in early Roman architecture.
We studied the Hellenistic period and its impact on art and philosophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greek - relating to Greece, its people, or language

Etymology

From Greek Hellenistikos, from Hellēn (Greek).
