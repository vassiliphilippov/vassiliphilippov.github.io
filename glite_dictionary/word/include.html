to include

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkluːd / (AmE), / ɪnˈkluːd / (BrE)
to have or consider someone or something as part of a whole; to contain as a part or member
to have as part of a whole

Examples

Please include all relevant documents in your application.
The tour price includes accommodation and breakfast each day.
They did not include my name on the invitation list.

Synset Examples

I include you in the list of culprits

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to exclude - to deliberately leave out or not include someone or something

Etymology

From Latin includere (to shut in, enclose), from in- (in) + claudere (to shut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00686739-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈklud / (AmE), / ɪnˈkluːd / (BrE)
to contain something or someone as a part or member of a whole; to make part of a group or set
contain as part of a whole

Examples

The price of the meal includes drinks and dessert.
Please include your contact information in the form.
Her hobbies include painting, hiking, and reading.

Synset Examples

We must include this chemical element in the group

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to exclude - to leave out; not include
  • Synonym: to comprise - to consist of; to be made up of

Etymology

From Latin includere (to shut in, enclose), from in- (in) + claudere (to close).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00184024-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈkluːd / (AmE), / ɪnˈkluːd / (BrE)
to allow someone to participate in or have the rights and responsibilities of a group or activity
allow participation or membership

Examples

The organization decided to include her as a full member of the committee.
Children under 12 are not included in the price of the ticket.
Doctors who pass the exam will be included in the national medical registry.

Synset Examples

admit someone to the profession
She was admitted to the New Jersey Bar

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to exclude - to prevent someone from participating or being part of something
  • Synonym: to admit - to allow entry or permission, especially to a group or activity

Etymology

From Latin includere (to shut in, enclose), from in- (in) + claudere (to shut).
