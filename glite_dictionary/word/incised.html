incised

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsaɪzd / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a surface, design, or object) cut or carved into by a sharp instrument, leaving lines or patterns.
cut or carved into a surface

Examples

The ancient vase featured an incised pattern around its rim.
A series of incised lines decorated the temple wall.
The artist preferred to create incised designs rather than painting.

Synset Examples

an incised design
engraved invitations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraved - carved or etched into a surface
  • Antonym: embossed - raised in relief on a surface

Etymology

From Latin 'incisus', past participle of 'incidere' meaning 'to cut into'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsaɪzd / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪzd / (BrE)
(of an edge or surface) having sharply and deeply cut lines or indentations.
sharply and deeply indented

Examples

The ancient vase had incised patterns along its rim.
A deeply incised valley cut through the mountains, revealing layers of rock.
The artist carefully incised lines into the metal plate for the print.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: embossed - having a raised or relief design
  • Synonym: engraved - decorated by carving or cutting into a surface

Etymology

From Latin *incisus*, past participle of *incidere* (to cut into).

3. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsaɪzd / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪzd / (BrE)
cut into or carved with a sharp instrument; having a surface marked by cuts or notches.
cut or marked by incisions

Examples

The artist created an incised design on the metal plate for printing.
Each stone was carefully incised with a unique symbol.
The surgeon made an incised opening to access the damaged tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraved - cut or carved onto a surface
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface, not cut or marked

Etymology

From Latin 'incisus', past participle of 'incidere' (to cut into).
