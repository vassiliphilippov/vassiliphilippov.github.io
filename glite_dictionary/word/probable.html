probable

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.bə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈprɒb.ə.bəl / (BrE)
a person regarded as likely to be chosen or selected for a specific role, position, or team.
person likely to be chosen

Examples

The coach announced the list of probables for the national team tryouts.
Only the most promising probables will advance to the next round of interviews.
Each department submits its probable to the selection committee for final approval.

Etymology

From Latin 'probabilis' (likely, credible), via Old French 'probable'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈprɑːbəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈprɒbəbl̩ / (BrE)
likely to happen or be true; apparently destined to occur.
likely to happen

Examples

It seemed probable that she would arrive before noon, given the traffic reports.
The most probable cause of the power outage was the heavy storm last night.
Scientists consider it probable that life exists elsewhere in the universe.

Synset Examples

the probable consequences of going ahead with the scheme

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unlikely - not probable; not likely to happen
  • Degree Lower: possible - able to be done or to happen
  • Degree Higher: certain - sure to happen; inevitable

Etymology

From Latin probabilis (provable, likely).

3. ADJ

/ ˈprɑːbəbəl / (AmE), / ˈprɒbəbəl / (BrE)
likely to happen or to be true, but not certain
likely but not certain

Examples

It is probable that the meeting will be rescheduled due to the weather.
The most probable cause of the error was a miscommunication.
A probable outcome of the negotiations is a compromise agreement.

Synset Examples

a likely result
he foresaw a probable loss

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: possible - could happen or be true, but uncertain
  • Degree Higher: certain - sure to happen or be true
  • Antonym: unlikely - not likely to happen or be true

Etymology

From Latin 'probabilis' (provable, credible), from 'probare' (to test, approve).
