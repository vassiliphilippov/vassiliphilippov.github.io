opopanax

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.pəˈpeɪ.næks / (AmE), / ˌɒ.pəˈpeɪ.næks / (BrE)
an aromatic gum resin obtained from certain plants, formerly used in medicine and perfumery
aromatic gum resin, once medicinal

Examples

The apothecary displayed a jar of opopanax among his other rare resins.
Perfume-makers sometimes use opopanax for its warm, balsamic scent.
In ancient remedies, opopanax was believed to possess healing qualities.

Etymology

From Latin opopanax, from Greek opoponax, from opo (juice) + panax (all-healing plant).
