rapaciousness

1. NOUN

/ rəˈpeɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / rəˈpeɪʃəsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being aggressively predatory or greedy, especially in animals that subsist on live prey.
predatory or greedy quality

Examples

The rapaciousness of some bird species makes them formidable hunters in the wild.
He spoke about the rapaciousness of corporate expansion into untouched environments.
Writers have often noted the rapaciousness exhibited by certain predatory animals.

Synset Examples

The rapacity of carnivorous animals has been considered by some writers to have had a considerable effect on the distribution and even on the extinction of others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire for something
  • Antonym: generosity - the quality of being kind and giving

Etymology

From Latin 'rapax' (greedy, grasping) + English suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ rəˈpeɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / rəˈpeɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
extreme greed for food, money, or other possessions; an insatiable desire to consume or possess more than one needs.
extreme greed; gluttony

Examples

The rapaciousness of the corporation led to widespread criticism from the public.
His rapaciousness at the buffet shocked everyone as he piled his plate high.
Many believe that unchecked rapaciousness can harm both individuals and society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - an intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power
  • Synonym: avarice - extreme greed for wealth or material gain
  • Antonym: generosity - the quality of being kind and generous; willingness to give

Etymology

From Latin 'rapax, rapacis' (seizing, greedy), via 'rapacious' + '-ness'.
