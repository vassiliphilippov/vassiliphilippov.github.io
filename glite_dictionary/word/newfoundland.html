Newfoundland

1. PROPN

/ ˈnuːfənˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈnjuːfənlənd / (BrE)
an island in the north Atlantic, forming part of the Canadian province of Newfoundland and Labrador
large Canadian island in the north Atlantic

Examples

The wildlife of Newfoundland is diverse due to its unique climate and geography.
Many tourists visit Newfoundland to explore its rugged coastline and charming towns.
She traced her ancestry back to early settlers in Newfoundland.

Etymology

Named by John Cabot in 1497, meaning 'new found land'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnuːfəndlænd / (AmE), / ˈnjuːfəndlənd / (BrE)
a large, strong breed of dog with a thick, usually black coat, originally developed in Newfoundland and known for its intelligence and swimming ability
large dog breed, strong swimmer

Examples

The Newfoundland is famous for its gentle nature and strength.
Our family owns a Newfoundland that loves to swim in the lake.
She adopted a rescued Newfoundland from the animal shelter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Labrador Retriever - a breed of retriever dog also originating from Newfoundland, Canada

Etymology

Named after the island of Newfoundland, Canada, where the breed originated.
