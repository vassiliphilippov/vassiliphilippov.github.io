economic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛkəˈnɑmɪk, ˌikəˈnɑmɪk / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk, ˌɛkəˈnɒmɪk / (BrE)
relating to the economy, or the system of production, distribution, and management of material wealth in a country or region.
relating to the economy or wealth management

Examples

Government policies often aim to promote economic growth and stability.
The country is facing economic challenges due to rising unemployment.
Many decisions in business are influenced by economic factors.

Synset Examples

economic growth
aspects of social, political, and economical life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: financial - relating to money or finance
  • Antonym: uneconomic - not profitable or economically viable

Etymology

From Greek *oikonomikos* (skilled in household management), via Latin *oeconomicus*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02727475-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌiːkəˈnɑːmɪk / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk / (BrE)
profitable or cost-effective; producing a financial benefit or reward
profitable, financially rewarding

Examples

The plant was closed because it was no longer economic to run it at that scale.
Raising fares made the bus route economic for the company to operate again.
The new project became economic only after they reduced costs significantly.

Synset Examples

it was no longer economic to keep the factory open
have to keep prices high enough to make it economic to continue the service

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profitable - yielding financial benefit
  • Antonym: uneconomic - not profitable; not yielding financial benefit

Etymology

From Latin oeconomicus, from Greek oikonomikos (skilled in household management).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01877533-s
Synset POS: s
