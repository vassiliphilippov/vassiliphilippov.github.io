trailer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈtreɪlə / (BrE)
a wheeled vehicle that is pulled by a car or truck and is equipped for people to live or sleep in
vehicle designed to be lived in, pulled by another

Examples

We spent the weekend camping in our trailer by the lake.
After retiring, they bought a trailer and traveled across the country.
The trailer was spacious enough for a family of four to sleep comfortably.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caravan - a vehicle equipped for living in, usually towed by a car

Etymology

From English 'trail' (to drag or pull) + '-er', indicating a thing that trails.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪlər / (AmE), / ˈtreɪlə / (BrE)
a short advertisement made up of selected scenes from a forthcoming film or television program, shown to promote the release
film or TV promotional preview

Examples

The movie trailer gave away some of the funniest scenes before the film was even released.
I always arrive early at the cinema so I can watch the latest trailers.
She saw the trailer online and immediately wanted to see the film.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teaser - a short promotional video or announcement designed to arouse interest for a forthcoming product, event, or show

Etymology

From 'trail' + '-er', originally meaning something that follows; applied to film previews that once ran at the end of features.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06901724-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪlər / (AmE), / ˈtreɪlə / (BrE)
A large vehicle without an engine that is designed to be pulled by a truck or tractor, used for transporting goods.
vehicle designed to be pulled, for transporting goods

Examples

The delivery arrived in a massive trailer pulled by a red truck.
Workers began loading furniture onto the trailer for transport across the state.
A long line of cars formed behind the slow-moving trailer on the highway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semi-trailer - a type of trailer without a front axle, designed to be attached to a truck tractor

Etymology

From the verb 'trail', meaning to pull or drag along, with the suffix '-er'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04474542-n
Synset POS: n
