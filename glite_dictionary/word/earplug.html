earplug

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪrˌplʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈɪəˌplʌɡ / (BrE)
A small piece of material, such as cotton, wax, or rubber, that is inserted into the ear canal to protect the ear from water, noise, or dust.
plug inserted in ear for protection

Examples

Before swimming, he always put in an earplug to keep water out of his ears.
She wore an earplug in each ear to block out the loud construction noise.
The doctor recommended using an earplug to prevent further ear infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earmuff - a cushioned covering for the ears, especially to protect against cold or noise

Etymology

From 'ear' + 'plug' (something used to block an opening).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪrˌplʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈɪəˌplʌɡ / (BrE)
A small earphone designed to be inserted into the ear canal.
small in-ear earphone

Examples

He listened to music on the train using a single earplug in his right ear.
These earplugs provide excellent sound quality for phone calls and music.
After losing one earplug, she had to buy a new pair for her commute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earbud - a small headphone that sits at the entrance of the ear canal

Etymology

Formed from 'ear' + 'plug'.
