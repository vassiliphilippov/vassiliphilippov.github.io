rashness

1. NOUN

/ ˈræʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈræʃ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of acting without careful thought or consideration of the possible consequences
the quality of being rash or reckless

Examples

His rashness in making quick decisions often led to unnecessary problems.
The accident was a direct result of her rashness behind the wheel.
History is full of leaders whose rashness brought about disastrous consequences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recklessness - lack of regard for danger or consequences
  • Antonym: prudence - the quality of being cautious and wise

Etymology

From Middle English 'rash', from Old English 'ræsc' (hasty), with suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈræʃ.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of acting or speaking without careful consideration of the possible consequences; recklessness
reckless or thoughtless behavior

Examples

Her rashness often led her into situations she would later regret.
He acted with rashness, not considering how dangerous the shortcut might be.
The rashness of his decision surprised everyone at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recklessness - lack of concern about the consequences of an action
  • Antonym: prudence - careful good judgment that allows someone to avoid danger or risks

Etymology

From Old French *rasche* (headlong, in haste), ultimately from Latin *radere* (to scrape).
