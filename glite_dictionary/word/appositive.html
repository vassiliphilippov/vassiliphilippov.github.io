appositive

1. ADJ

/ əˈpɑːzətɪv / (AmE), / əˈpɒzətɪv / (BrE)
(of a word or phrase) placed next to another to explain or identify it; relating to apposition in grammar.
relating to apposition in grammar

Examples

An appositive phrase provides additional information about the noun it follows.
In the sentence, the appositive noun gives more detail about the subject.
Grammarians often discuss appositive structures when explaining sentence composition.

Synset Examples

an appositive noun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apposition - the placing of a word or phrase next to another to explain it

Etymology

From Latin 'appositivus', from 'appositus' (placed near).
