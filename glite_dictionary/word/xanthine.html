xanthine

1. NOUN

/ ˈzænθiːn / (AmE), / ˈzanθiːn / (BrE)
a colorless, crystalline compound produced by the metabolism of nucleic acids, serving as a precursor of uric acid and found in various body tissues and fluids
chemical compound; nucleic acid metabolite
medical

Examples

Researchers detected elevated levels of xanthine in the patient's urine sample.
The conversion of xanthine to uric acid occurs in the liver through the action of the enzyme xanthine oxidase.
Diet can affect the body's xanthine levels and its breakdown products.

Etymology

From Greek xanthos (yellow), referring to some yellowish salts derived from the compound.
