Mâconnais

1. NOUN

/ məˈkoʊneɪ / (AmE), / maˈkɔneɪ / (BrE)
A fine Burgundy wine, typically white and dry, produced in the Mâconnais region of France.
white Burgundy wine from Mâconnais region

Examples

The restaurant’s wine list featured an excellent Mâconnais that paired well with seafood.
She prefers a crisp, dry Mâconnais over oaky Chardonnays from other regions.
We enjoyed a glass of chilled Mâconnais on the sunny terrace.

Etymology

From French Mâconnais, from Mâcon (town in Burgundy, France) + -ais (denoting origin).
