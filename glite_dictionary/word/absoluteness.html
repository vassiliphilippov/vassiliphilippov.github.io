absoluteness

1. NOUN

/ ˈæb.səˌluːt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈæb.sə.ljuːt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being absolute; complete and total nature without limitation or condition.
the quality of being absolute

Examples

The absoluteness of her trust left no room for doubt or hesitation.
Many philosophers debated the absoluteness of moral principles throughout history.
He admired the absoluteness of the law, which applied equally to everyone.

Synset Examples

the absoluteness of the pope's decree could not be challenged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolute - having no restrictions, conditions, or limitations; total

Etymology

From 'absolute' + 'ness', with 'absolute' from Latin 'absolutus' (unconditional, complete).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæb.səˌluːt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈab.sə.luːt.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being complete, total, or without restriction; extremity or entirety.
quality of being complete or extreme

Examples

The absoluteness of her refusal left no room for negotiation.
His rules were characterized by their absoluteness and lack of exceptions.
There was an absoluteness to the judge’s verdict that surprised everyone in the courtroom.

Synset Examples

the starkness of his contrast between justice and fairness was open to many objections

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completeness - the state of being complete
  • Antonym: partiality - state of being incomplete or biased

Etymology

From absolute + -ness, noun-forming suffix.
