haematoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˈtoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈtəʊmə / (BrE)
A localized collection of blood outside blood vessels, usually in liquid form within the tissue.
collection of blood outside blood vessels
medical british

Examples

The surgeon drained the haematoma to relieve pressure on the patient's leg.
A large haematoma developed after she bumped her head on the door.
Untreated, a haematoma can cause further complications and may require medical intervention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hematoma - localized swelling of blood outside vessels (US spelling)

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + -oma (swelling or tumor).
