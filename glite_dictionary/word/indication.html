indication

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Medicine) A valid reason for using a particular treatment, performing a procedure, or prescribing a drug.
reason to use a medical treatment or procedure
medical

Examples

The presence of a heart murmur was an indication for further tests.
Surgery is only considered when there is a clear indication for intervention.
Chronic pain is an indication for the use of certain medications.

Synset Examples

the presence of bacterial infection was an indication for the use of antibiotics

Related Terms

  • Antonym: contraindication - a reason for not using a particular treatment or procedure

Etymology

From Latin indicatio (a pointing out, indication), from indicare (to indicate).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪndəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a reading or piece of data shown by a meter or similar instrument, giving information about a physical state
data shown by an instrument

Examples

The thermometer gave an indication that the engine was overheating.
There was no clear indication on the gauge that the tank was empty.
This monitor provides a digital indication of your blood pressure.

Synset Examples

he could not believe the meter reading
the barometer gave clear indications of an approaching storm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reading - a value or measurement displayed by an instrument

Etymology

From Latin 'indicatio', from 'indicāre' (to indicate).
