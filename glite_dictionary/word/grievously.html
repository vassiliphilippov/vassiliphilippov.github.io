grievously

1. ADV

/ ˈɡriːvəsli / (AmE), / ˈɡriːvəsli / (BrE)
In a way that causes or expresses great suffering, sorrow, or serious harm.
in a very severe or painful way

Examples

He was grievously injured in the accident and required several surgeries.
The company suffered grievously during the economic downturn, losing many of its employees.
She felt grievously disappointed by the news of the cancellation.

Synset Examples

the resolute but unbroken Germany, grievously wounded but far from destruction, was able to lay the firm foundations for military revival

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seriously - to a serious or severe degree
  • Antonym: slightly - to a small or not serious extent

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *grievos*, from Latin *gravis* (heavy, serious).
