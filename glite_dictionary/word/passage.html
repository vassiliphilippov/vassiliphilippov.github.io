passage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsɪdʒ / (BrE)
the official approval or enactment of a law or legislative measure by a governing body
enactment of a law

Examples

The passage of the new bill was celebrated by environmentalists.
Debate in the senate delayed the passage of the proposed amendment.
After months of negotiation, the passage of healthcare reform marked a significant achievement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enactment - the process of making a proposal into a law

Etymology

From Old French passage, from passer (to pass), from Latin passare.
