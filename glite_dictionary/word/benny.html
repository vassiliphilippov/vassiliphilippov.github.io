benny

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛni / (AmE), / ˈbɛni / (BrE)
An East Indian annual plant (Sesamum indicum), cultivated for its seeds, which are used as food (sesame seed) and for sesame oil.
sesame plant; source of sesame seed and oil

Examples

The farmer harvested benny seeds to press for oil.
In some regions, benny is a traditional ingredient in baked goods.
Fields of benny turn golden brown at the end of the season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sesame - An annual plant grown for its edible seeds and oil

Etymology

Possibly from Arabic 'benne' or from West African languages through colonial trade routes.
