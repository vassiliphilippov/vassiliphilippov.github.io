stargazer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːrˌɡeɪzər / (AmE), / ˈstɑːɡeɪzə / (BrE)
a heavy-bodied marine fish with eyes located on the flattened top of its head, found dwelling near the sea bottom
marine fish with eyes on top of head

Examples

The stargazer hides itself in the sand, waiting for unsuspecting prey to swim by.
Scientists studied the unique eyes of the stargazer to understand its adaptation to the ocean floor.
Fishermen occasionally catch a stargazer among their usual haul of flatfish.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɑrˌɡeɪzər / (AmE), / ˈstɑːˌɡeɪzə / (BrE)
a person who studies or observes the stars and other celestial phenomena, especially as an astronomer
astronomy observer or scientist

Examples

Many stargazers gathered at the observatory to watch the meteor shower.
As a child, she dreamed of becoming a stargazer and exploring the mysteries of the universe.
Modern stargazers use powerful telescopes to study distant galaxies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astronomer - a scientist who studies astronomy

Etymology

From 'star' + 'gazer', formed in English, originally meaning 'one who gazes at stars'.
