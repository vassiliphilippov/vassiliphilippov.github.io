trompillo

1. NOUN

/ troʊmˈpiːjoʊ / (AmE), / trɒmˈpiːjəʊ / (BrE)
A weedy nightshade (Solanum elaeagnifolium) with silvery foliage and violet, blue, or white flowers, producing round berries that are widely used to curdle milk; native from central United States to South America.
a nightshade plant with milk-curdling berries

Examples

The shepherd used a trompillo berry to curdle the fresh goat's milk.
Wild trompillo plants are often found along the roadsides in Texas.
The silvery leaves and blue flowers make the trompillo easy to identify in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightshade - any plant of the genus Solanum

Etymology

From Spanish "trompillo", common name for this plant in Latin America.
