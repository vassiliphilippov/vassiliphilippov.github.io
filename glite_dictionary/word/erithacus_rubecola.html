Erithacus rubecola

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈrɪθəkəs ˌruːbɪˈkoʊlə / (AmE), / ɛˈrɪθəkəs ˌruːbɪˈkəʊlə / (BrE)
A small Old World songbird with a reddish breast, commonly known as the European robin.
the European robin (songbird)

Examples

The Erithacus rubecola is easily recognized by its bright orange-red chest.
Many British gardens are frequently visited by the Erithacus rubecola during winter.
Ornithologists often study the migration patterns of Erithacus rubecola across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robin - small European songbird with a red breast

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Erithacus' from Greek 'erithakos' (a kind of bird), 'rubecola' from Latin 'rubeus' (red) + 'colere' (to inhabit).
