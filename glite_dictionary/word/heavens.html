heavens

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvənz / (AmE), / ˈhɛvənz / (BrE)
The sky, especially when regarded as a vault or expanse appearing to cover the earth, in which the sun, moon, stars, and planets are seen.
the sky as seen from Earth

Examples

The clouds drifted lazily across the heavens on a summer afternoon.
Ancient astronomers mapped the positions of the stars in the heavens.
A brilliant flash of lightning lit up the night heavens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sky - the region of the atmosphere and outer space seen from the earth

Etymology

Plural of 'heaven', from Old English 'heofon' (sky, heaven).
