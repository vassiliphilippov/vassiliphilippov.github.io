Satanism

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.tənˌɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.tən.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
the worship of Satan or devils, or beliefs and practices associated with such veneration
worship of Satan or devils

Examples

Controversy erupted in the community after accusations of Satanism at the local school.
Some modern forms of Satanism do not involve literal worship of Satan but rather focus on individualism.
Books exploring the history of Satanism often discuss its cultural impact in the twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devil worship - the religious worship of Satan
  • Antonym: Christianity - the religion based on the person and teachings of Jesus Christ

Etymology

From 'Satan' (the chief evil spirit in Christianity and Judaism) + '-ism' (denoting a distinct practice or philosophy).
