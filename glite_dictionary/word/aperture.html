aperture

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpɚtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈapətjʊə / (BrE)
a device or adjustable opening in a camera or optical instrument that controls the amount of light entering
device controlling light in optics/cameras

Examples

The camera's aperture was adjusted to let in more light for the evening shot.
Photographers often experiment with aperture settings to achieve different effects in their images.
If the aperture is too small, your photos may come out underexposed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diaphragm - a device in optical instruments that controls the aperture (opening) for light

Etymology

From Latin apertura (an opening), from aperire (to open).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæpərtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈæpətʃə / (BrE)
a natural opening or hole in a surface or structure, especially in anatomy or physics
a natural opening or hole

Examples

The camera lens has an adjustable aperture that controls the amount of light entering.
Sunlight shone through a small aperture in the cave wall, illuminating the chamber.
During surgery, the doctor examined the aperture between the two chambers of the heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orifice - an opening, particularly in the body

Etymology

From Latin 'apertura' (an opening), from 'aperire' (to open).

3. NOUN

/ ˈæpərtʃər / (AmE), / ˈapətjʊə / (BrE)
A small, often man-made opening or hole, especially in a surface.
small man-made opening or hole

Examples

The camera's aperture was adjusted to let in more light for the photograph.
A tiny aperture in the metal panel allowed the technician to insert a tool.
Light streamed through a small aperture in the wall, illuminating the otherwise dark room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opening - an open space allowing passage or view
  • Synonym: orifice - an opening, particularly in the body or a machine

Etymology

From Latin 'apertura' (an opening), from 'aperire' (to open).
