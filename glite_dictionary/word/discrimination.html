discrimination

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌskrɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌskrɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the unfair treatment of people, especially based on race, age, or sex, due to prejudice
unfair treatment based on prejudice

Examples

The company was accused of discrimination in its hiring practices.
Laws exist to protect people from discrimination because of their religion.
Many organizations are working to end discrimination against minorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience

Etymology

From Latin discriminatio (distinction, separation), from discriminare (to divide, distinguish).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˌskrɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌskrɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the ability to perceive and recognize differences between two or more things, especially as a mental or cognitive process
ability to perceive differences

Examples

The experiment measured the subject's discrimination between two different sound frequencies.
Children often improve their color discrimination as they grow older.
Her keen discrimination enables her to tell even subtle differences in flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinction - recognition or notice of difference between things

Etymology

From Latin discriminatio (a distinguishing, separating), from discriminare (to divide, separate).
