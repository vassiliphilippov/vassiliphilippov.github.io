penstock

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnˌstɑk / (AmE), / ˈpɛnstɒk / (BrE)
A pipe or enclosed channel that carries a fast flow of water to a mill, turbine, or similar device, usually controlled by a sluice gate.
channel or pipe for fast-flowing water

Examples

The penstock channels water from the reservoir to the turbines in the hydroelectric plant.
Engineers inspected the penstock for debris that could block the flow of water.
During maintenance, the penstock is closed using the sluice gate to stop water movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sluice - a sliding gate or other device for controlling the flow of water

Etymology

From pen (enclosure) + stock (barrier or dam), originally in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.stɑk / (AmE), / ˈpen.stɒk / (BrE)
a pipe, sluice, or enclosed channel fitted with a valve or gate, used to control the flow of water, especially to a waterwheel or turbine
water flow control pipe or sluice

Examples

Engineers inspected the penstock to ensure it could manage the increased water pressure from the reservoir.
A damaged penstock can lead to water leaking and reduced efficiency in hydroelectric plants.
The flow of water to the turbines is regulated by opening or closing the penstock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sluice - a sliding gate or device for controlling water flow

Etymology

From pen (enclosure) + stock (barrier, stem) in late Middle English.
