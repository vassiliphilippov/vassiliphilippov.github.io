Takelma

1. NOUN

/ təˈkɛlmə / (AmE), / təˈkɛlmə / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people from southwestern Oregon
member of a Native American group in Oregon

Examples

The Takelma lived along the Rogue River in southwestern Oregon.
Anthropologists have studied the traditions of the Takelma extensively.
Only a few descendants of the original Takelma community remain today.

2. NOUN

/ təˈkɛlmə / (AmE), / təˈkɛlmə / (BrE)
a Penutian language historically spoken by the Takelma people of southwestern Oregon, United States
a Native American language of Oregon

Examples

Linguists have documented the grammar and vocabulary of Takelma to preserve it for future generations.
Few fluent speakers of Takelma remained by the mid-20th century, making language revitalization efforts crucial.
The university offers a workshop on Takelma language and culture every summer.

Etymology

From the self-designation of the Takelma people, referring to themselves and their language.
