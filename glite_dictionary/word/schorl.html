schorl

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrl / (AmE), / ʃɔːl / (BrE)
a black variety of tourmaline, a complex silicate mineral often occurring in granite and related rocks
a black tourmaline mineral

Examples

Geologists discovered a significant deposit of schorl while surveying the granite outcrop.
The deep black color of schorl makes it a popular choice among gemstone collectors.
She wore a pendant set with polished schorl to complement her outfit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tourmaline - a group of related minerals, often used as gems

Etymology

From German Schörl, the name of a village where the mineral was found.
