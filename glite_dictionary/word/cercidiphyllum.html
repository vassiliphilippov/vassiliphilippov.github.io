Cercidiphyllum

1. NOUN

/ sɜːrsɪdiˈfɪləm / (AmE), / sɜːsɪdɪˈfɪlɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees native to East Asia, known chiefly for the katsura tree, valued for its heart-shaped leaves and autumn color.
genus of trees including the katsura

Examples

The Cercidiphyllum in our garden displays brilliant yellow foliage in autumn.
Botanists often refer to Cercidiphyllum when discussing ornamental trees from East Asia.
One of the most popular species in the Cercidiphyllum genus is commonly called the katsura tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: katsura - deciduous tree native to Japan and China, known for its heart-shaped leaves

Etymology

From Greek 'kerkidion' (a diminutive of 'kerkís', shuttle) and 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the leaf shape.

2. NOUN

/ sərˌsɪdiˈfɪləm / (AmE), / sɜːˌsɪdɪˈfɪləm / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous trees native to East Asia, chiefly known for the katsura tree, which has heart-shaped leaves and colorful autumn foliage
genus of trees including the katsura

Examples

The Cercidiphyllum genus is particularly valued for ornamental planting in gardens and parks.
Leaves from Cercidiphyllum trees can fill the air with a sweet scent during autumn.
Botanists recently discovered a new variant within the Cercidiphyllum genus in Japan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: katsura - a tree species, Cercidiphyllum japonicum, in the genus Cercidiphyllum

Etymology

From Greek 'kerkis' (shuttle) and 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the shape of the leaves.
