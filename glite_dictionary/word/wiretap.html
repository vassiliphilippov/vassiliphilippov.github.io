wiretap

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪərˌtæp / (AmE), / ˈwaɪəˌtæp / (BrE)
the act of secretly listening to telephone or telegraph conversations by connecting a device to the line, typically to obtain private information
secret listening to phone or telegraph conversations

Examples

The police obtained a warrant before placing a wiretap on the suspect’s phone.
The scandal erupted when journalists exposed the illegal wiretap of government officials.
Installing a wiretap without authorization is a serious crime in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eavesdropping - secretly listening to private conversations

Etymology

From 'wire' (referring to telephone wires) + 'tap' (to connect to for listening), originated in early 20th-century American English.

2. VERB

/ ˈwaɪərˌtæp / (AmE), / ˈwaɪəˌtæp / (BrE)
to secretly listen to people's telephone conversations by connecting a device to the telephone line, especially as part of an investigation
secretly listen to phone calls

Examples

The police had to wiretap his home phone to gather enough evidence.
It is illegal to wiretap someone’s calls without a court order.
They discovered that the calls were being wiretapped by authorities.

Synset Examples

The FBI was tapping the phone line of the suspected spy
Is this hotel room bugged?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eavesdrop - to secretly listen to a conversation

Etymology

Formed from 'wire' and 'tap', referring to tapping into a telephone wire.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02193294-v
Synset POS: v
