stethoscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛθ.əˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈstɛθ.əˌskəʊp / (BrE)
A medical instrument used by doctors to listen to the sounds made by the heart, lungs, or other organs inside the body.
medical listening instrument
medical

Examples

The doctor placed a stethoscope on my chest to listen to my heartbeat.
She carried her stethoscope everywhere during her hospital shifts.
With his stethoscope, the physician could detect abnormal lung sounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sphygmomanometer - an instrument for measuring blood pressure

Etymology

From French 'stéthoscope', from Greek 'stēthos' (chest) and 'skopein' (to look at, examine).
