hussar

1. NOUN

/ hʊˈzɑr / (AmE), / hʊˈzɑː / (BrE)
a member of a European light cavalry regiment, known for their elaborate uniforms and swift, light-armed tactics
member of a European light cavalry regiment

Examples

The museum displayed a splendid uniform once worn by a Hungarian hussar.
During the Napoleonic Wars, the hussar regiments played a crucial role in reconnaissance and flanking maneuvers.
He was fascinated by the dashing appearance and bold reputation of the hussar.

Etymology

From Hungarian 'huszár', relating to 15th-century cavalry units.
