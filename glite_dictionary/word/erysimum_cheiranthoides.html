Erysimum cheiranthoides

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌrɪsɪˌnʊm ˌkaɪəˌrænˈθɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ɛˌrɪsɪˌnʊm ˌkaɪəˌrænˈθɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
a slender European plant of the mustard family with yellow flowers, often considered a troublesome weed and formerly used to expel parasitic worms
a yellow-flowered European weed (mustard family)

Examples

Erysimum cheiranthoides can quickly invade cultivated fields due to its prolific seed production.
Some traditional herbalists once used Erysimum cheiranthoides as a remedy for intestinal parasites.
Gardeners often regard Erysimum cheiranthoides as a persistent and unwelcome weed.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Erysimum' from Greek 'erysimon' (a type of cress), 'cheiranthoides' meaning 'like Cheiranthus' (wallflower genus).
