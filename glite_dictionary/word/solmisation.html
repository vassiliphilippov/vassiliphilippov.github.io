solmisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑlməˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɒlmɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A system of naming the notes of a musical scale using specific syllables rather than letters.
naming musical notes by syllables

Examples

The teacher introduced solmisation to help students remember the notes.
Medieval music often relied on solmisation for vocal training.
The concept of solmisation dates back to Guido of Arezzo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solfège - a method of teaching pitch and sight singing using syllables

Etymology

From French 'solmisation', based on the syllables 'sol' and 'mi' in the scale.
