Commiphora

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪfərə / (AmE), / kəˈmɪfərə / (BrE)
a genus of East Indian and African trees that yield aromatic or balsamic resins, such as myrrh
genus of trees yielding aromatic resins

Examples

Commiphora species are well known for producing myrrh, a valuable aromatic resin.
Many traditional medicines use products derived from Commiphora trees.
Botanists studying East African flora often classify several shrubs and trees under the genus Commiphora.

Etymology

From Latin, based on Greek kommi (gum) + phoros (bearing).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪfərə / (AmE), / kəˈmɪfərə / (BrE)
A genus of East Indian and African trees that yield balsamic resins, including myrrh.
genus of resin-yielding trees

Examples

The resin extracted from Commiphora trees is used in perfumes and incense.
Several species of Commiphora produce myrrh, a substance valued since ancient times.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Commiphora resin in traditional healing practices.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek 'kommi' (gum) + 'phoros' (bearing), referring to the gum-producing nature of these trees.
