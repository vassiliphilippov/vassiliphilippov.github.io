Bruchidae

1. NOUN

/ bruːˈkɪdiː / (AmE), / bruːˈkɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small beetles, commonly known as seed beetles, whose larvae typically develop within seeds and often pose as agricultural pests
family of seed beetles (insects)

Examples

Scientists have identified several new species within the Bruchidae family.
Infestations by Bruchidae can severely damage stored legume seeds.
The study compared the life cycles of different Bruchidae collected from various regions.

Etymology

From New Latin Bruchidae, based on the genus Bruchus, from Greek brukhos (a kind of locust or beetle).
