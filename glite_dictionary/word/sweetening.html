sweetening

1. NOUN

/ ˈswiːtənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswiːtənɪŋ / (BrE)
a substance added to food or drink to make it taste sweeter
substance added to sweeten food

Examples

She added a little sweetening to her coffee to enhance the flavor.
Honey can be used as a natural sweetening in many recipes.
Artificial sweetening agents are often found in diet sodas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetener - a substance used to sweeten food and drinks

Etymology

From 'sweeten' (to make sweet) + -ing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈswiː.tən.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswiː.tən.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of adding a sweetening agent such as sugar or honey to food or drink
adding a sweetener to food or drink

Examples

The sweetening of the tea made it more enjoyable on a cold morning.
Artificial sweetening is often used in diet beverages instead of sugar.
Proper sweetening is an important step when preparing jams or preserves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sweeten - to make something taste sweet

Etymology

From 'sweeten' + '-ing', where 'sweeten' comes from Old English 'swētan'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈswiːtənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswiːtənɪŋ / (BrE)
something added to make a proposal, offer, or situation more appealing or agreeable.
something that makes a situation more appealing

Examples

The company offered a sweetening to persuade employees to accept the changes.
As a sweetening, the seller included free shipping with the order.
They added a financial bonus as a sweetening to finalize the contract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incentive - something that encourages a person to do something
  • Synonym: inducement - something that persuades or influences someone to do something
  • Antonym: deterrent - something that discourages someone from taking an action

Etymology

Derived from 'sweeten' + -ing, originally meaning to make sweet, later extended metaphorically.
