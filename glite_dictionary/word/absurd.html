absurd

1. ADJ

/ əbˈsɝd / (AmE), / əbˈsɜːd / (BrE)
Wildly unreasonable, illogical, or inappropriate to the point of being laughable or ridiculous.
wildly unreasonable or ridiculous

Examples

It was absurd to suggest that aliens had built the pyramids.
His excuse for missing the meeting was completely absurd and nobody believed him.
The idea of running a marathon without any training is simply absurd.

Synset Examples

the absurd excuse that the dog ate his homework
that's a cockeyed idea
ask a nonsensical question and get a nonsensical answer
a contribution so small as to be laughable
it is ludicrous to call a cottage a mansion
a preposterous attempt to turn back the pages of history
her conceited assumption of universal interest in her rather dull children was ridiculous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridiculous - Deserving or inviting mockery or derision; absurd.
  • Synonym: ludicrous - So foolish, unreasonable, or out of place as to be amusing; ridiculous.
  • Antonym: logical - Reasonable or following a clear reasoning process; not absurd.

Etymology

From Latin absurdus (out of tune, ridiculous).

2. ADJ

/ əbˈsɝd / (AmE), / əbˈsɜːd / (BrE)
wildly unreasonable, illogical, or inappropriate; inconsistent with reason or common sense.
wildly unreasonable or illogical

Examples

The idea that the Earth is flat is simply absurd.
It would be absurd to suggest he could finish the project in one day.
Her absurd outfit drew everyone's attention at the party.

Synset Examples

the absurd predicament of seeming to argue that virtue is highly desirable but intensely unpleasant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridiculous - deserving or inviting derision or mockery; absurd
  • Antonym: rational - based on or in accordance with logic or reason

Etymology

From Latin absurdus (out of tune, irrational), from ab- (from) + surdus (deaf, dull).

3. NOUN

/ əbˈsɝd / (AmE), / əbˈsɜːd / (BrE)
the state or concept in which life, events, or circumstances appear irrational, meaningless, or illogical, especially as discussed in philosophy
the state of life seeming irrational or meaningless

Examples

Some philosophers believe that confronting the absurd is a fundamental part of human experience.
The play explores the absurd by depicting characters in meaningless situations.
Camus famously wrote about the absurd and how individuals respond to it.

Synset Examples

The absurd is the essential concept and the first truth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meaningless - having no meaning or significance
  • Antonym: rational - based on reason or logic

Etymology

From Latin absurdus (out of tune, irrational), used in philosophy to describe meaninglessness.
