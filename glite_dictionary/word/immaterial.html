immaterial

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪməˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / ˌɪməˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
not important or relevant; not significant
not important; irrelevant

Examples

Whether the meeting is at 2 or 3 o’clock is immaterial to me.
Her opinion on this matter is completely immaterial.
It's immaterial who made the mistake—the important thing is to fix it.

Synset Examples

whether you choose to do it or not is a matter that is quite immaterial (or indifferent)
what others think is altogether indifferent to him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrelevant - not connected or relevant to something
  • Antonym: significant - important; meaningful

Etymology

From Latin immaterialis, from in- (not) + materialis (material).

3. ADJ

/ ɪməˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / ɪməˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
not consisting of physical matter; not having a material existence; incorporeal.
not made of physical matter

Examples

Many religions believe in immaterial spirits or souls that exist apart from the physical world.
According to some philosophers, thoughts are immaterial and cannot be measured like physical objects.
The ghost was described as an immaterial presence, felt but never seen.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incorporeal - not having a physical body or material existence
  • Antonym: material - consisting of matter; having a physical form
