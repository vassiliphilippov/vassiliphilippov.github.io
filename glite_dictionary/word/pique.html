pique

1. NOUN

/ piːk / (AmE), / piːk / (BrE)
a tightly woven fabric, typically cotton, with raised cords or ribs, often used for clothing such as shirts or dresses.
textile with raised cords or ribs

Examples

The tailor recommended using pique for a crisp, elegant finish on the summer dress.
His shirt was made of white pique, which gave it a textured appearance.
Many tennis players prefer pique because it is breathable and holds its shape.

Etymology

From French 'piqué', meaning 'quilted', past participle of 'piquer' (to prick, quilt).

2. VERB

/ piːk / (AmE), / piːk / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel resentment or indignation; to offend or irritate someone's feelings
to cause resentment or indignation

Examples

Her comments piqued his curiosity and his irritation at the same time.
He was piqued by their lack of interest in his proposal.
It piques me when people ignore my advice.

Synset Examples

Her tactless remark offended me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to offend - to cause to feel hurt or upset, often through words or actions
  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone slightly angry or irritated

Etymology

From French 'piquer' (to prick, sting).

3. NOUN

/ piːk / (AmE), / piːk / (BrE)
a feeling or sudden outburst of irritation or anger, often because of wounded pride
a sudden feeling of anger

Examples

She slammed the door in a fit of pique after the argument.
He left the meeting in sudden pique, unable to hide his anger.
Her pique was obvious, but she quickly regained her composure.

Synset Examples

his temper sparked like damp firewood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temper - a person's state of anger or mood
  • Synonym: resentment - bitterness or anger at being treated unfairly
  • Antonym: calm - a state free from anger or agitation

Etymology

From French 'piquer' (to prick).
