silvery

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪlvəri / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvəri / (BrE)
having the appearance or qualities of silver, especially in color or lustre; shimmering or shining like silver.
resembling or shining like silver

Examples

The lake shimmered with a silvery light as the moon rose.
She spoke in a silvery voice that captivated the audience.
The old coins had a silvery sheen despite their age.

Synset Examples

a soft silvern voice
singing in her silvery tones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silvern - made of or resembling silver (poetic, archaic)

Etymology

From silver + -y (adjective suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪlvəri / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvəri / (BrE)
Having a lustrous grey appearance or being covered with or tinged with the color of silver.
having the appearance or color of silver

Examples

Her grandmother's silvery hair shimmered softly in the afternoon light.
The lake reflected the silvery glow of the full moon.
A flock of silvery fish darted beneath the clear water.

Synset Examples

silvery hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silver - precious metal with a shiny grayish-white color

Etymology

From silver + -y, meaning 'having the qualities or color of silver'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00388453-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɪl.vɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.vəri / (BrE)
having the shiny, white appearance or sheen of silver
having a shiny, silver-like appearance

Examples

The moon cast a silvery light over the quiet lake.
She admired the silvery strands in her grandmother's hair.
The fish flashed silvery scales as it darted through the water.

Synset Examples

a land of silver (or silvern) rivers where the salmon leap
repeated scrubbings have given the wood a silvery sheen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silvern - having the sheen or qualities of silver

Etymology

Formed from 'silver' + '-y', meaning 'like silver'.
