Gregorian

1. ADJ

/ ɡrɪˈɡɔriən / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈɡɔːriən / (BrE)
of or relating to Pope Gregory I or to the plainchant (Gregorian chant) of the Roman Catholic Church
relating to Pope Gregory I or his chants

Examples

The monks sang a haunting Gregorian chant during the ceremony.
He has a deep interest in Gregorian music and its historical development.
The cathedral’s collection includes several old manuscripts of Gregorian melodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gregorian chant - a style of plainchant associated with Pope Gregory I

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Gregorianus, from Gregorius (Gregory).

2. ADJ

/ ɡrəˈɡɔriən / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈɡɔːrɪən / (BrE)
of or relating to Pope Gregory XIII or the calendar he introduced in 1582, which is the basis of the modern Western calendar.
relating to Pope Gregory XIII’s calendar

Examples

The Gregorian calendar is now used in most countries around the world.
They celebrated New Year's Day according to the Gregorian system.
The adoption of the Gregorian reform caused confusion in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Julian - relating to the earlier Julian calendar, which the Gregorian replaced

Etymology

From the name Gregory, after Pope Gregory XIII, who introduced the Gregorian calendar in 1582.

Source: WordNet
