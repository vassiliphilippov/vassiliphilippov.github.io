bailiffship

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.lɪf.ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.lɪf.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the office or position of a bailiff
the office of a bailiff

Examples

He was proud to accept the bailiffship of the district after years of service.
The history books mention his bailiffship as a period of stability and reform.
Her grandfather’s bailiffship ended in the year 1890.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bailiff - an official in a court of law or someone who manages estates

Etymology

From 'bailiff' + '-ship' (denoting status or office).
