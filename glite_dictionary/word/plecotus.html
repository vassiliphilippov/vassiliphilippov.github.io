Plecotus

1. NOUN

/ pliːˈkoʊtəs / (AmE), / plɪˈkəʊtəs / (BrE)
A genus of bats in the family Vespertilionidae, commonly known as long-eared bats.
genus of long-eared bats

Examples

The Plecotus genus is distinguished by its exceptionally large ears.
Researchers observed several Plecotus species in the old forest.
Plecotus bats are found throughout Europe and parts of Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat - a nocturnal flying mammal

Etymology

From Greek plēkōtos (having folded ears).

2. NOUN

/ pliˈkoʊtəs / (AmE), / plɪˈkəʊtəs / (BrE)
A genus of bats in the family Vespertilionidae, known for species with long ears.
genus of long-eared bats

Examples

The genus Plecotus includes several species of long-eared bats found across Europe and Asia.
Researchers recently discovered a new species within the genus Plecotus in the mountains of Central Asia.
Members of Plecotus are distinguished by their exceptionally large ears compared to other bats.

Etymology

From Greek *plekein* (to plait, twist) and *-otos* (ear), referring to the bats' long, twisted ears.
