Siluridae

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlʊrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / sɪˈljʊərɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of catfishes native to the Old World, especially Europe and Asia, having a long slender body and lacking an adipose fin.
family of Old World catfishes

Examples

The Siluridae are commonly found in the rivers and lakes of Southeast Asia.
Ichthyologists study the anatomy of Siluridae to understand their evolutionary history.
Some species within Siluridae are important in local fisheries.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Silurus (from Greek 'silouros') + -idae (family suffix).
