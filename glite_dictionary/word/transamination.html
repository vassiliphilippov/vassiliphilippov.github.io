transamination

1. NOUN

/ ˌtræn.səˌmɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtræn.səˌmɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which an amino group is transferred from one molecule to another, especially in biochemical reactions involving amino acids and keto acids
transfer of an amino group in chemistry
medical

Examples

The enzyme catalyzes the transamination of amino acids during metabolism.
Disruption in transamination can lead to metabolic disorders in humans.
Understanding transamination is crucial for students studying biochemistry.

Etymology

From trans- (across) + amination (introduction of an amino group)

2. NOUN

/ ˌtræn.sə.məˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtræn.sə.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process in which an amino group is transferred from one molecule to another, especially in biochemistry and metabolism
biochemical amino group transfer
medical

Examples

Transaminase enzymes catalyze the transamination reaction during amino acid metabolism.
A deficiency of vitamin B6 can impair transamination in the human body.
The transamination process plays a key role in synthesizing non-essential amino acids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amination - the introduction of an amino group into a molecule

Etymology

From trans- (across) + amination, patterned after 'amino'.
