binaural

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈnɔrəl / (AmE), / bɪˈnɔːrəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving the use of both ears, especially in hearing or sound perception
relating to both ears

Examples

The doctor tested her binaural hearing to check for balance between the ears.
Binaural recordings create a three-dimensional sound experience when listened to with headphones.
He noticed a difference in sound quality when comparing monaural and binaural audio.

Synset Examples

binaural hearing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monaural - relating to or heard with one ear

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'auris' (ear).
