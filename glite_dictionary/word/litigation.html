litigation

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪtəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪtɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of taking legal action to settle a dispute in court
legal process of taking a dispute to court
legal

Examples

The company faced several years of litigation after the contract dispute.
Because of the complex issues involved, the litigation lasted much longer than expected.
He decided to avoid litigation by settling the matter out of court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lawsuit - a claim or dispute brought to a law court for adjudication
  • Antonym: arbitration - the use of an arbitrator to settle a dispute outside court

Etymology

From Latin litigare (to dispute, quarrel), via Late Latin litigatio.
