jagged

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒæɡɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɡɪd / (BrE)
having a sharply uneven or irregular edge or surface, often with notches or projections
with sharp, uneven, or irregular edges

Examples

The mountain ridge had a jagged silhouette against the evening sky.
He cut his hand on the jagged edge of the broken glass.
The coastline was marked by jagged rocks jutting into the sea.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having a regular, even, or flat surface

Etymology

From Middle English jagged, from 'jag' (a notch or projection).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒæɡɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʒæɡɪd / (BrE)
having a rough or sharply uneven edge or surface
sharply uneven or rough-edged

Examples

The mountain ridge formed a jagged silhouette against the sunset.
He scraped his knee on the jagged edge of the broken sidewalk.
The artist drew a jagged line to depict the rough shoreline.

Synset Examples

the jagged outline of the crags
scraggy cliffs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; free from perceptible projections, lumps, or indentations

Etymology

From 'jag', possibly from Middle English 'jagge' (a notch or cut).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
