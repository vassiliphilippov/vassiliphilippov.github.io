Franciscanism

1. NOUN

/ frænˈsɪskəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / frænˈsɪskənɪzəm / (BrE)
The beliefs, practices, or spirit of the Franciscan Order, emphasizing humility, poverty, and devotion, as inspired by St. Francis of Assisi.
Franciscan religious beliefs and practices

Examples

He dedicated his life to Franciscanism, living simply and caring for the poor.
The monastery maintained the traditions of Franciscanism for over five centuries.
Her writings reflect a deep influence of Franciscanism, especially the ideals of humility and poverty.

Synset Examples

His Franciscanism was that of respectable, well-educated priests worshipping God in perfect liturgical harmony, embracing a life of penitence in poverty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Franciscan - A member of the Roman Catholic religious order founded by St. Francis of Assisi

Etymology

From Franciscan (relating to St. Francis of Assisi) + -ism (denoting a system or practice).
