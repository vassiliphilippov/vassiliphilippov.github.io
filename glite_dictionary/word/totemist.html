totemist

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊtəˌmɪst / (AmE), / ˈtəʊtəmɪst / (BrE)
a member of a clan or tribe that recognizes a totem as an emblem or ancestor
member of a totemic clan or tribe

Examples

The totemist carefully observed the rituals associated with the tribe's sacred animal.
As a totemist, he believed his clan was spiritually connected to the bear.
Anthropologists studied the traditions passed down by the totemist elders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: totemism - the belief system or practice involving totems

Etymology

From 'totem' (Ojibwa) + '-ist', denoting a follower or adherent.
