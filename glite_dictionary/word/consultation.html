consultation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.səlˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.səlˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a meeting or discussion between people, especially professionals, to consider a particular question or make a decision
a professional meeting for discussion

Examples

After a lengthy consultation, the doctors agreed on a treatment plan.
She scheduled a consultation with her lawyer before signing the contract.
The team held a consultation to resolve the complex issue.

Synset Examples

frequent consultations with his lawyer
a consultation of several medical specialists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conference - a formal meeting for discussion
  • Synonym: discussion - the action or process of talking about something

Etymology

From Latin consultatio, from consultare (to consult).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.səlˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.səlˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of seeking information or advice from a source or authority
the act of seeking information or advice

Examples

Before prescribing any medication, the doctor insisted on a consultation of medical records.
Her consultation of the manual helped her solve the technical issue quickly.
A quick consultation of the dictionary clarified the meaning of the word.

Synset Examples

reference to an encyclopedia produced the answer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reference - the act of looking for information in a source

Etymology

From Latin consultatio (deliberation, consultation), from consultare (to consult).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.səlˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.səlˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a meeting or discussion, especially one held to seek advice or make a decision, often with someone important or in authority
meeting or discussion for advice or decision

Examples

The mayor held a consultation with business leaders to discuss the new project.
After a brief consultation with her advisors, she announced her decision.
He scheduled a consultation with the specialist before starting the treatment.

Synset Examples

he had a consultation with the judge
he requested an audience with the king

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conference - a formal meeting for discussion
  • Synonym: meeting - an event at which people come together to discuss things
  • Synonym: audience - a formal meeting, especially with someone important

Etymology

From Latin 'consultatio' (deliberation), from 'consultare' (to deliberate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07158332-n
Synset POS: n
