headiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.i.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being intoxicating or having a strong, stimulating effect.
quality of being intoxicating or stimulating

Examples

The headiness of the perfume filled the room, overwhelming the guests.
She was swept away by the headiness of young love and new experiences.
Some people enjoy the headiness of strong coffee in the morning.

Synset Examples

Wine novices intimidated by the headiness of Napa and Sonoma will feel comfortable at nearly every Oregon winery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heady - having a strong or intoxicating effect
  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of being sober, not affected by alcohol

Etymology

From 'heady' + '-ness'; 'heady' from Middle English 'heddy' (affecting the head).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdinəs / (AmE), / ˈhɛdinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being exciting, exhilarating, or intoxicating; the tendency to make one feel elated or overwhelmed
exhilarating or intoxicating quality

Examples

The headiness of their sudden fame left them giddy with excitement.
There was a certain headiness to the first days of independence.
The headiness of the wine made the evening especially memorable.

Synset Examples

How a mere convention gathering could generate such a shift in regard to an issue like abortion reveals something of the headiness in which many of the gathered were luxuriating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heady - having a strong or exhilarating effect

Etymology

From 'heady' + '-ness'; 'heady' is from Middle English hedi, from 'head' + '-y'.
