Hosta

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊstə / (AmE), / ˈhɒstə / (BrE)
A genus of hardy perennial plants native to eastern Asia, commonly known as plantain lilies, grown for their attractive foliage and racemose flowers.
genus of shade-loving perennial plants

Examples

Many gardeners plant Hosta in shady spots to brighten their landscapes.
The Hosta variety I bought last year survived the harsh winter.
We toured a botanical garden with dozens of Hosta species on display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantain lily - common name for plants of the genus Hosta

Etymology

Named after Austrian botanist Nicolaus Thomas Host (1761–1834).
