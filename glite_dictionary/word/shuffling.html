shuffling

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌflɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃʌflɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of mixing playing cards by moving them about to change their order, especially before dealing
the act of mixing playing cards

Examples

After a quick shuffling of the deck, the dealer distributed the cards to each player.
He made sure the shuffling was thorough to ensure fair play.
The magician's impressive shuffling amazed the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shuffle - to mix playing cards by shifting them repeatedly

Etymology

From the verb 'shuffle', which originated in the mid-16th century, possibly of imitative origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌf.əl.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃʌf.əl.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of walking with a slow, dragging motion, typically without lifting the feet fully off the ground
walking with a dragging motion

Examples

The old man's shuffling down the hallway could be heard from the next room.
Her shuffling revealed how tired she was after the long day.
He approached the podium in a slow shuffling, his feet barely leaving the ground.

Synset Examples

from his shambling I assumed he was very old

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shamble - to walk with a slow, awkward, or dragging gait

Etymology

From shuffle + -ing, meaning the act or process of shuffling.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00293556-n
Synset POS: n
