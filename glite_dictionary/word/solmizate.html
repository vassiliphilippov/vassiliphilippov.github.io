to solmizate

1. VERB

/ sɑlmɪˌzeɪt / (AmE), / sɒlmɪˌzeɪt / (BrE)
to sing music using the syllables of solmization, such as do, re, mi, instead of the lyrics or note names
sing using solmization syllables

Examples

The choir director asked the students to solmizate the melody before adding the words.
Many music teachers encourage beginners to solmizate new songs for ear training.
She found it helpful to solmizate difficult passages when practicing sight-singing.

Synset Examples

solmizate a song before you learn the lyrics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sol-fa - to sing using the sol-fa syllables (do, re, mi, etc.)

Etymology

From Italian *solmisare*, from Medieval Latin *solmisatio*, relating to solmization (assignment of syllables to notes).

2. VERB

/ ˈsɑlmɪˌzeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɒlmɪˌzeɪt / (BrE)
to sing using solmization syllables such as 'do', 're', and 'mi' to represent the tones of the musical scale
sing using solmization syllables

Examples

The choir director asked everyone to solmizate the melody for practice.
As a beginner, she found it challenging to solmizate complex passages accurately.
If you solmizate the notes correctly, it will be easier to memorize the song.

Synset Examples

The voice teacher showed the students how to solmizate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to solmizate - to apply sol-fa syllables to music
  • Synonym: solfège - a system of assigning syllables to scale degrees in music

Etymology

From Medieval Latin solmizāre (to sol-fa), based on solmization syllables.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01734265-v
Synset POS: v
