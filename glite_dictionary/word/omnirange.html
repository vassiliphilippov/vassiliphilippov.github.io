omnirange

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːmnɪˌreɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈɒmnɪˌreɪndʒ / (BrE)
a radio navigational system consisting of a network of beacons that provide aircraft with precise information about position and bearing
aviation network of radio beacons

Examples

The pilot relied on the omnirange to navigate through poor visibility.
Many modern aircraft are equipped with omnirange receivers for accurate navigation.
With the help of omnirange technology, air traffic controllers can safely guide planes across busy skies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: VOR - very high frequency omnidirectional range; a type of omnirange system used in aviation

Etymology

Blend of 'omni-' (from Latin omnis, meaning 'all') and 'range' (distance covered).
