manticore

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.tɪˌkɔr / (AmE), / ˈmæn.tɪˌkɔː / (BrE)
a mythical creature with the head of a man (sometimes with horns), the body of a lion, and the tail of a scorpion
mythical creature; lion body, human head, scorpion tail

Examples

The legend describes a manticore preying on travelers lost in the ancient forests.
A tapestry in the old manor depicted a fierce manticore among other mythical beasts.
Few believed the explorer's tale of encountering a manticore in the distant mountains.

Etymology

From Latin 'mantichora', from Ancient Greek 'mantikhóras', meaning 'man-eater'.
