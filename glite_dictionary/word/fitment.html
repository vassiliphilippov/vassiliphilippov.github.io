fitment

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˈfɪtmənt / (BrE)
an item of furniture or equipment, especially one that is built in or fixed in position in a room or building
built-in furniture or fixture

Examples

The newly renovated office included several modern fitments such as built-in shelves and cupboards.
She admired the kitchen fitments because they made such efficient use of space.
After moving in, they replaced some old fitments with updated versions to better suit their style.

Synset Examples

she liked the kitchen fitments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fixture - something fixed in place, especially in a house

Etymology

From Old French 'afetement', from 'afeter' (to arrange), influenced by 'fit'.
