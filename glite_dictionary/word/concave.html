concave

1. ADJ

/ kɑnˈkeɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.keɪv / (BrE)
curved or rounded inward like the inside of a bowl or sphere
curved inward

Examples

The satellite dish has a concave surface to help focus signals.
A spoon's concave side can hold liquid.
She examined the concave mirror, noticing how it made her reflection appear larger.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convex - having a surface or boundary that curves outward

Etymology

From Latin 'concavus', from 'con-' (together) + 'cavus' (hollow).
