decimetre

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛsɪˌmitər / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪˌmiːtə / (BrE)
A unit of length in the metric system equal to one tenth of a metre (0.1 metre).
one tenth of a metre (metric length unit)

Examples

The fish measured exactly two decimetres in length.
A decimetre is longer than a centimetre but shorter than a metre.
She drew a line ten decimetres long on the paper.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: centimetre - a metric unit of length equal to one hundredth of a metre
  • Degree Higher: metre - the base unit of length in the metric system

Etymology

From French 'décimètre', from Latin 'decimus' (tenth) + 'metrum' (measure).
