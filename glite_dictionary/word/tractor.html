tractor

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræktər / (AmE), / ˈtraktə / (BrE)
A powerful motor vehicle with large rear wheels, used chiefly on farms for pulling machinery and trailers.
motor vehicle used in farming

Examples

The farmer used his tractor to plow the fields before planting the new crop.
Their new tractor is equipped with advanced GPS technology for precision farming.
A group of children watched as a red tractor hauled hay bales across the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truck - motor vehicle for carrying goods

Etymology

From Latin tractus (drawing), from trahere (to pull), via modern use in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtræktər / (AmE), / ˈtræktə / (BrE)
A motor vehicle with a cab but no cargo body, designed for pulling large trailers, especially in trucking and freight transport.
truck cab that pulls trailers

Examples

The tractor pulled a load of cars across the country.
Drivers must be specially licensed to operate a tractor with a semi-trailer attached.
A fleet of tractors arrived at the distribution center early this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semi-trailer - a trailer that is attached to and pulled by a tractor unit
  • Synonym: lorry - a large, heavy motor vehicle for transporting goods (primarily British)

Etymology

From Latin tractus (a drawing or pulling), via the noun 'tractor' originally applied to farm vehicles.
