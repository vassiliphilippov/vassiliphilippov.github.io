to flyfish

1. VERB

/ ˈflaɪˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
to fish using artificial flies as lures, typically with a specialized rod and line
to fish with artificial flies

Examples

She loves to flyfish in mountain streams every summer.
Many anglers travel to remote rivers specifically to flyfish for trout and salmon.
He spent the afternoon flyfishing on the lake, hoping for a good catch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angling - the sport or practice of fishing with a rod and line

Etymology

Formed from 'fly' (type of artificial bait) + 'fish' (to catch fish).
