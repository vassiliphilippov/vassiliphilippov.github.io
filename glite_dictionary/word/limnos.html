Limnos

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɪm.nɒs / (AmE), / ˈlɪm.nɒs / (BrE)
a Greek island in the northern Aegean Sea, noted for its reddish-brown medicinal clay
Greek island in the northern Aegean Sea

Examples

Many tourists visit Limnos each year to enjoy its quiet beaches and traditional villages.
The ancient Greeks believed the clay from Limnos had healing powers.
Shipping routes in the Aegean often pass near Limnos due to its strategic location.

Etymology

From Greek Λῆμνος (Límnos), the island's name in Greek.
