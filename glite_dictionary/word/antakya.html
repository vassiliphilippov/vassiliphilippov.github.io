Antakya

1. PROPN

/ ɑnˈtɑːkjə / (AmE), / ænˈtækjə / (BrE)
a town in southern Turkey, historically known as Antioch, an important ancient commercial center and an early center of Christianity.
town in southern Turkey; historic Antioch

Examples

Many early Christian communities were established in Antakya during the Roman period.
Antakya attracts visitors with its rich history and cultural heritage.
After the earthquake, international aid quickly arrived in Antakya to support the recovery efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Antioch - ancient name of Antakya, major city of classical antiquity

Etymology

From Turkish 'Antakya', derived from ancient Greek 'Antiochē' (Antioch).
