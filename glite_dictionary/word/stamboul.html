Stamboul

1. PROPN

/ stɑmˈbuːl / (AmE), / stamˈbuːl / (BrE)
the old central district of Istanbul, formerly referred to as the city itself, especially before the name Istanbul became standard
the old district of Istanbul, Turkey

Examples

Many travelers to old Stamboul were captivated by its historic architecture and bustling bazaars.
The ancient walls of Stamboul still stand as a testament to its rich past.
He wrote a book about the contrasts between modern Istanbul and the traditional heart of Stamboul.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Istanbul - the largest city in Turkey, formerly known as Constantinople and Byzantium

Etymology

From Turkish 'İstanbul', variant in French and English spellings from the 19th century.
