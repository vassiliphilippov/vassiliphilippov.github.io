hen

1. NOUN

/ hɛn / (AmE), / hɛn / (BrE)
The meat of an older female chicken, typically used for stewing rather than roasting or frying due to its tougher texture.
flesh of an older female chicken for cooking

Examples

The recipe calls for one whole hen to make a rich chicken stew.
Farmers often sell older hens for stewing because their meat is tougher than that of young chickens.
My grandmother always preferred using a hen for her famous chicken and dumplings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: chicken - the flesh of a young chicken used as food

2. NOUN

/ hɛn / (AmE), / hɛn / (BrE)
an adult female chicken, especially kept for laying eggs
adult female chicken

Examples

The hen laid three eggs this morning.
They keep a hen and a rooster in their backyard.
The farmer bought ten more hens for his coop.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rooster - an adult male chicken
  • Synonym: chicken - a domestic fowl, Gallus gallus domesticus

Etymology

Old English *henn* (female fowl), related to German *Henne*; ultimately from Proto-Germanic.

3. NOUN

/ hɛn / (AmE), / hɛn / (BrE)
(in zoology) a female of certain aquatic animals, such as octopus or lobster.
female aquatic animal (e.g. lobster, octopus)

Examples

The fisherman was excited to catch a large hen lobster for dinner.
Marine biologists observed a hen octopus guarding her eggs under a rock.
In some fisheries, it is required to release any hen crabs carrying eggs back into the water.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cock - male of certain animals, particularly birds and aquatic species
  • Synonym: female - of the sex that can produce eggs or offspring

4. NOUN

/ hɛn / (AmE), / hɛn / (BrE)
an adult female bird, especially the female of the domestic chicken or other gallinaceous birds
adult female bird (esp. chicken)

Examples

The hen laid three eggs yesterday morning.
Every evening, the hen returns to the coop to roost.
On the farm, a mother hen watched over her chicks protectively.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rooster - an adult male chicken
  • Synonym: chicken - a domestic fowl kept for its eggs or meat

Etymology

From Old English henn, related to Old High German henna and Old Norse hǫna.
