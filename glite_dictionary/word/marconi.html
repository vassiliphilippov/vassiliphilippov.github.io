Marconi

1. PROPN

/ mɑːrˈkoʊni / (AmE), / mɑːˈkəʊni / (BrE)
(1874–1937) Italian electrical engineer and inventor who developed wireless telegraphy and transmitted the first radio signals across the Atlantic Ocean.
Italian inventor of wireless telegraphy

Examples

Marconi was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his pioneering work on wireless communication.
Many early radio sets were nicknamed 'Marconis' in honor of the famous inventor.
Marconi's experiments revolutionized long-distance communication.

Etymology

From the surname of Guglielmo Marconi, Italian inventor.
