Allium sphaerocephalum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm sfɛəroʊˈkɛfələm / (AmE), / ˈæliəm sfɪərəʊˈsɛfələm / (BrE)
A species of ornamental perennial plant in the onion genus, known for its dense, spherical purple flower heads.
ornamental plant with spherical purple flowers

Examples

The Allium sphaerocephalum adds striking color to the summer garden with its purple blooms.
Gardeners often plant Allium sphaerocephalum to attract bees and butterflies.
We admired a cluster of Allium sphaerocephalum at the botanical garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drumstick allium - common name for Allium sphaerocephalum, ornamental plant

Etymology

From Latin: ‘allium’ meaning ‘garlic’ and Greek ‘sphaira’ (sphere) + ‘kephalē’ (head), referring to the shape of its flower head.
