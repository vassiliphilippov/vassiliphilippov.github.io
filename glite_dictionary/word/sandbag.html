sandbag

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈsandbaɡ / (BrE)
a bag filled with sand, used as a barrier against flooding, as ballast, or sometimes as a weapon
a bag filled with sand, used as a barrier, weight, or weapon

Examples

During the flood, residents stacked sandbags along the riverbank to prevent water from entering their homes.
The construction crew used sandbags as ballast to stabilize the temporary structures.
He swung a heavy sandbag to block the door during the emergency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandbagging - the act of using sandbags for protection or deception

/ ˈsændˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈsan(d)bæɡ / (BrE)
to hit someone or something, especially suddenly or forcefully, as if with a sandbag
hit forcefully, as with a sandbag

Examples

She felt as if she'd been sandbagged by the sudden announcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - to take aggressive action against someone or something

3. VERB

/ ˈsændˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈsan(d)baɡ / (BrE)
to deliberately downplay or understate one's abilities or effort, especially in a game or competition, in order to deceive others and gain an advantage
to downplay ability to deceive in games

Examples

He tried to sandbag his opponents by playing poorly during the warm-up.
She suspected he was sandbagging and hiding his true poker skills for the big tournaments.
After sandbagging in earlier rounds, he revealed his real talent and easily won the final.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hustle - to deceive or trick someone, often in gambling or games
  • Synonym: bluff - to mislead opponents by pretending to have a weaker or stronger ability

Etymology

Originally referring to a bag filled with sand used as a weapon or weight; figurative use in gaming and competition arose in the 20th century.
