sprat

1. NOUN

/ spræt / (AmE), / spræt / (BrE)
a small, oily fish of the herring family, often smoked or canned for food, chiefly found in European waters
a small European oily fish

Examples

Fishermen caught a boatload of sprat off the coast of Norway.
I've never tried smoked sprats before, but they are said to be delicious.
The can of sprat labeled 'in oil' was imported from Latvia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sardine - a small, oily fish often packed in tins, similar in use to sprat

Etymology

From Old English *sprætt* (a kind of fish).

2. NOUN

/ spræt / (AmE), / spræt / (BrE)
a small marine fish related to the herring, often processed and eaten like sardines
a small herring-like fish

Examples

The fisherman sorted the sprat from the larger fish in his net.
Canned sprat is a delicacy in some European countries.
The recipe calls for smoked sprat as the main ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sardine - a small, edible fish often canned or processed for food

Etymology

From Middle English *sprote*, related to Old English *sprott* (a kind of fish).
