Flacourtiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌflæˌkɔːrʃiˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌflakɔːˌʃiˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a former family of chiefly tropical trees and shrubs, now largely included in the family Salicaceae
former family of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The Flacourtiaceae once included over 80 genera, but many have been reassigned to other families.
Botanists often debate the classification of the Flacourtiaceae due to its diverse members.
Several tropical fruits were formerly classified within the Flacourtiaceae.

Etymology

From New Latin, after the genus Flacourtia, named after Étienne de Flacourt (1607–1660), a French colonial governor.
