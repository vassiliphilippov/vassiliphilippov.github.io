payable

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪəbl / (BrE)
(Accounting) A sum of money that is owed by a business for goods or services received on credit; an amount recorded as a liability in an account.
an amount owed in business accounts

Examples

The company's payables have increased significantly this quarter.
He is responsible for managing all the payables and ensuring timely payments.
Their accountant reconciles receivables and payables at the end of each month.

Synset Examples

the problem was to match receivables and payables in the same currency

Related Terms

  • Antonym: receivable - an amount owed to a business by others

Etymology

From pay (verb) + -able, originally from Old French *paiable*, from payer 'to pay' and Latin *-abilis*.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of an amount of money) required to be paid; due in payment, especially as specified by law or agreement.
required to be paid; due in payment

Examples

The invoice is payable within 30 days of receipt.
Dividends will be payable to shareholders on March 1st.
The amount payable includes all applicable taxes and fees.

Synset Examples

a collectible bill
a note payable on demand
a check payable to John Doe

Related Terms

  • Antonym: receivable - (of money) that may be received; due to be received

Etymology

From Old French *païable*, from *payer* (to pay), from Latin *pacare* (to appease, to pay).

Synset POS: s
