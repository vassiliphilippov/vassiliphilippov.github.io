siphon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.fən / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪ.fən / (BrE)
a tubular organ in certain aquatic animals, especially mollusks, through which water is taken in or expelled
tubular organ in aquatic animals

Examples

Some mollusks use a siphon to draw in water for respiration and feeding.
The octopus extended its siphon and propelled itself backward through the water.
Through its long, flexible siphon, the clam filters food particles from the surrounding seawater.

Etymology

From Greek *sīphōn* (pipe, tube).

2. VERB

/ ˈsaɪ.fən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.fən / (BrE)
to move a liquid from one container to another by means of a siphon or siphoning action, often by creating a flow using air pressure or gravity
transfer liquid using a siphon

Examples

We used a tube to siphon water from the aquarium into a bucket.
He managed to siphon the fuel out of the old car overnight.
Farmers often siphon irrigation water from the canal to the fields.

Synset Examples

siphon gas into the tank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pump - to move liquid using a mechanical device

Etymology

From French 'siphon', from Latin 'siphon', from Ancient Greek 'sīphōn' (pipe, tube).

3. VERB

/ ˈsaɪ.fən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.fən / (BrE)
to convey, draw off, or empty liquid by means of or as if by a siphon, often using a tube and gravity
convey liquid by siphon

Examples

They used a hose to siphon water from the aquarium into the bucket.
The thieves managed to siphon funds from the company accounts.
She carefully siphoned gasoline from her car to fill the lawn mower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drain - to remove liquid from something
  • Antonym: pump - to move liquid, especially by mechanical means

Etymology

From French 'siphon', from Latin 'siphon', from Ancient Greek 'sīphōn' (pipe, tube).

4. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.fən / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.fən / (BrE)
a bent tube used to move liquid from one container to another at a lower level by means of atmospheric pressure and gravity
tube for transferring liquid via gravity

Examples

He used a siphon to transfer the gasoline from one tank to another.
The laboratory technician carefully set up the siphon to drain the chemical solution.
A simple siphon can be used to move water from a barrel into a watering can.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tube - hollow cylinder used for conveying fluids

Etymology

From French siphon, from Latin siphon (pipe, tube), from Ancient Greek siphōn (pipe, tube, siphon).
