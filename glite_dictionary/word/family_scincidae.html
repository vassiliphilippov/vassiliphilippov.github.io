family Scincidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌskɪnˈsiːdiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli ˌskɪŋˈsiːdiː / (BrE)
A large family of lizards known as skinks, characterized by smooth, shiny scales and a worldwide distribution.
a family of lizards (skinks)

Examples

Scientists have identified hundreds of species within the family Scincidae.
The family Scincidae includes lizards that are found on nearly every continent.
Many members of the family Scincidae have shiny, smooth scales and reduced limbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skink - a lizard of the family Scincidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Scincidae,' the taxonomic family name based on 'Scincus' (the type genus).
