woodsia

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdziə / (AmE), / ˈwʊdziə / (BrE)
Any fern of the genus Woodsia, typically found in rocky or mountainous regions.
a genus of ferns

Examples

The rocky slopes were covered in woodsia and other small ferns.
Researchers discovered a rare species of woodsia growing in the mountains.
Gardeners value woodsia for its delicate fronds and hardiness.

Etymology

Named after English botanist Joseph Woods (1776–1864).
