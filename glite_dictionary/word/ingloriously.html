ingloriously

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈɡlɔːriəsli / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡlɔːrɪəsli / (BrE)
in a way that brings shame or a lack of honor; in a dishonorable or shameful manner
in a shameful or dishonorable way

Examples

The army retreated ingloriously after suffering heavy losses on the battlefield.
He ingloriously abandoned his friends when they needed him the most.
The politician resigned ingloriously following the corruption scandal.

Synset Examples

his grades were disgracefully low

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgracefully - in a way that is very bad or unacceptable and causes shame
  • Antonym: honorably - in a way that is deserving of respect or honor

Etymology

From 'inglorious' (Latin ingloriosus: in- 'not' + gloria 'glory') with adverbial suffix -ly.
