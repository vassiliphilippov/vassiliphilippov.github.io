Pinus torreyana

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪnəs tɔːˈreɪ.ənə / (AmE), / ˈpɪn.əs tɒˈreɪ.ənə / (BrE)
A medium-sized pine tree native to southwestern California, having five needles per cluster and long cylindrical cones; also known as Torrey pine.
a pine tree species native to California

Examples

The rare Pinus torreyana can be found growing along the southern California coast.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered Pinus torreyana.
I visited the reserve to photograph the unique cones of Pinus torreyana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Torrey pine - Another name for Pinus torreyana, a pine species native to California

Etymology

Named after American botanist John Torrey (1796–1873); from Latin 'Pinus' (pine).
