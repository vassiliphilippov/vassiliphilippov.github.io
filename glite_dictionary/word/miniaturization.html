miniaturization

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪniətʃəɹəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪnɪətjʊəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something much smaller than usual, especially by using advanced technology
the process of making something very small

Examples

The miniaturization of electronic components has revolutionized the smartphone industry.
Space missions benefit from the miniaturization of scientific instruments, allowing more equipment on board.
Recent advances in miniaturization have led to the development of tiny medical implants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller in size, amount, or degree

Etymology

From 'miniature' + 'ization', following the model of similar words in science and engineering.
