ethics

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθɪks / (AmE), / ˈɛθɪks / (BrE)
the branch of philosophy that deals with moral principles, values, and the rules of conduct governing individuals or groups
philosophical study of morals and rules

Examples

She studied ethics at university to better understand moral philosophy.
Many professions have a code of ethics to guide their members' behavior.
Debates in ethics often focus on what is considered right or wrong in society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morality - principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong

Etymology

From Greek ēthikē (philosophy of morals), from ēthos (custom, character).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛθɪks / (AmE), / ˈɛθɪks / (BrE)
Moral principles that govern a person's or group's behavior, or the study of such principles.
moral principles or study thereof

Examples

He is known for his strong ethics and fairness in business.
Medical professionals must adhere to strict ethics when treating patients.
The university offers a course on ethics and social responsibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morality - principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong or good and bad behavior
  • Antonym: immorality - behavior that goes against moral principles

Etymology

From Greek ēthikos (of or for morals), from ēthos (character).
