Hygeia

1. PROPN

/ haɪˈdʒiːə / (AmE), / hɪˈdʒiːə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the goddess of health, daughter of Aesculapius and sister of Panacea.
goddess of health in Greek mythology

Examples

Many ancient temples dedicated to Hygeia were centers for healing and wellness.
The symbol of Hygeia, a serpent drinking from a cup, is still used in modern pharmacy.
In myth, Hygeia worked alongside her father Aesculapius and her sister Panacea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Panacea - goddess of universal remedy in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Aesculapius - god of medicine in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Greek Ὑγεία (Hygeía), meaning 'Health'.
