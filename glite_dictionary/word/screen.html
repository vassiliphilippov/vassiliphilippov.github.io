screen

1. NOUN

/ skrin / (AmE), / skriːn / (BrE)
A protective covering made of netting or mesh, often set in a frame, used to keep out insects or provide protection.
protective mesh covering in a frame

Examples

We replaced the torn screen on the kitchen window to keep out mosquitoes.
She installed a fine metal screen on the door to let air in but keep bugs out.
The room was separated from the patio by a wire screen fitted within a wooden frame.

Synset Examples

they put screens in the windows for protection against insects
a metal screen protected the observers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesh - material made of a network of wire or thread

Etymology

From Old French 'escrein' (shield), from Latin 'scrinium' (case, chest).
