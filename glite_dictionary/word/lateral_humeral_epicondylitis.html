lateral humeral epicondylitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæt̬ɚəl hjuːˈmɛrəl ˌɛpɪˌkɑn.dəˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌlætərəl hjuːˈmɛrəl ˌɛpɪkɒn.dɪˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Painful inflammation of the tendon at the outer side of the elbow, caused by overuse of the forearm muscles, especially during twisting movements of the hand; also known as tennis elbow.
inflammation of the outer elbow tendon
medical

Examples

After weeks of playing tennis, she developed lateral humeral epicondylitis and had to rest her arm.
Physicians often see cases of lateral humeral epicondylitis in people who perform repetitive arm motions at work.
Physical therapy is commonly recommended to treat lateral humeral epicondylitis and help reduce pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tennis elbow - common term for lateral humeral epicondylitis

Etymology

From Latin: 'lateral' (side); 'humerus' (upper arm bone); 'epicondylus' (projection on bone near a joint); and Greek 'itis' (inflammation).
