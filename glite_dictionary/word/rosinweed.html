rosinweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːz.ɪnˌwid / (AmE), / ˈrɒz.ɪnˌwiːd / (BrE)
any of several North American plants of the genus Grindelia, having sticky, resinous leaves and stems, sometimes used medicinally and often poisonous to livestock
a resinous plant of the genus Grindelia

Examples

The sticky stems of rosinweed made it easy to identify during our hike.
Some ranchers are cautious because rosinweed can be toxic to their cattle.
Early settlers used rosinweed in traditional remedies for respiratory ailments.

Etymology

From 'rosin' (a resin obtained from pine trees) + 'weed', referring to the sticky secretion of the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːzɪnˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈrɒzɪnˌwiːd / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant with a resinous odor and yellow flowers, common in prairies and meadows.
North American plant with yellow flowers

Examples

We spotted a cluster of rosinweed blooming near the edge of the prairie.
The sticky stems of rosinweed make it easy to identify among other wildflowers.
Gardeners appreciate rosinweed for its resilience and bright yellow blossoms.

Etymology

Named for its resin-like (rosin) exudation, combined with 'weed' meaning plant.
