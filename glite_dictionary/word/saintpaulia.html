Saintpaulia

1. NOUN

/ seɪntˈpɔːliə / (AmE), / seɪntˈpɔːliə / (BrE)
A genus of east African herbs with nodding, often violet or purple, flowers, widely cultivated as houseplants; commonly known as African violets.
genus of east African flowering herbs (African violet)

Examples

My grandmother grows several Saintpaulia plants on her kitchen windowsill.
The Saintpaulia is prized for its delicate, violet-colored flowers and lush leaves.
Botanists sometimes debate the classification of Saintpaulia within the family Gesneriaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African violet - common name for plants of the genus Saintpaulia

Etymology

Named after Baron Walter von Saint Paul-Illaire, who discovered the plant in Tanzania in the 19th century.
