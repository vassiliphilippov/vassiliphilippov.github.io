subscriber

1. NOUN

/ səbˈskraɪbər / (AmE), / səbˈskraɪbə / (BrE)
a person who contributes or agrees to contribute money, especially to a fund, publication, or service
person who pays or pledges money

Examples

Each subscriber pledged $100 to support the project.
The magazine gained thousands of subscribers in its first year.
As a loyal subscriber, she receives exclusive updates and offers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donor - a person who gives money, especially to a cause
  • Synonym: benefactor - person who gives money to help a person or cause
  • Synonym: contributor - person who donates or gives

Etymology

From Latin subscribere (to write under, sign at the bottom) via Middle English and Old French.

2. NOUN

/ səbˈskraɪbɚ / (AmE), / səbˈskraɪbə / (BrE)
a person who pays to receive a service or regular issues of a publication, such as a magazine, newspaper, or digital content
a person who pays for a service or publication

Examples

As a subscriber to the magazine, she receives each issue before it appears in stores.
Many streaming platforms offer exclusive content to their subscribers.
The newspaper's subscribers were notified of the upcoming price increase by email.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: customer - a person who buys goods or services
  • Antonym: provider - a person or organization that supplies a service or product

Etymology

From Latin 'subscribere' (to sign at the bottom), via 'subscribe'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10689922-n
Synset POS: n
