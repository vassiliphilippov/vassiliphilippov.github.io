Scleropages

1. NOUN

/ ˌsklɪr.əˈpeɪdʒiz / (AmE), / ˌsklɪərəˈpeɪdʒiːz / (BrE)
A genus of large freshwater fishes native to Australia and Borneo, notable for their elongated bodies and bony tongues.
genus of large freshwater fishes (Australia, Borneo)

Examples

The Scleropages genus includes fish species that are prized in the aquarium trade for their striking appearance.
Several species in the Scleropages genus are considered endangered due to habitat loss and overfishing.
Scientists are studying the breeding habits of Scleropages to support conservation efforts.

Etymology

From Greek 'skleros' (hard) + 'pago' (something congealed or fixed).
