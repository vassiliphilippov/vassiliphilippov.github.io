ranching

1. NOUN

/ ˈræn.tʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɑːn.tʃɪŋ / (BrE)
The activity or business of raising livestock, especially cattle, on a large farm or ranch.
raising livestock for agriculture

Examples

Ranching has been a vital part of the economy in the American West since the 19th century.
The couple decided to move to Texas and start their own ranching operation.
Modern ranching methods focus on sustainability and animal welfare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farming - working the land or raising animals for food and other products

Etymology

From 'ranch' (mid-19th century, from Spanish rancho), with the gerund-forming suffix '-ing'.
