brassavola

1. NOUN

/ ˌbræsəˈvoʊlə / (AmE), / ˌbrasəˈvəʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American orchids with thick leaves and fragrant white or greenish flowers, often blooming at night.
a genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The brassavola orchid is prized for its sweet, night-time fragrance.
Her collection of orchids includes several varieties of brassavola.
The brassavola produces its showy blooms in clusters during the summer months.

Etymology

Named after Antonio Musa Brassavola, an Italian physician and botanist (1500–1555).
