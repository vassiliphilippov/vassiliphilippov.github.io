wish

1. VERB

/ wɪʃ / (AmE), / wɪʃ / (BrE)
to feel or express a desire or hope that something will happen, especially concerning the future or someone's fortune
express hope or desire for the future

Examples

I wish you all the best in your new job.
She wished for a peaceful resolution to the conflict.
They always wish each other good luck before an exam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hope - to want something to happen or be true

Etymology

From Old English *wyscan*, related to German *wünschen* (to wish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01828474-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ wɪʃ / (AmE), / wɪʃ / (BrE)
a strong feeling or expression of wanting something to happen or be true
a desire or longing for something

Examples

He made a wish when he blew out the birthday candles.
It was my wish to see everyone happy during the holidays.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: longing - a persistent, often melancholic desire
  • Antonym: aversion - a feeling of strong dislike or opposition

Etymology

From Middle English *wischen*, from Old English *wȳscan* (to wish, desire).
