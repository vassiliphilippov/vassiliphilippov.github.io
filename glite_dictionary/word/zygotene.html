zygotene

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪɡəˌtin / (AmE), / ˈzaɪɡətiːn / (BrE)
the second stage of prophase I in meiosis, during which homologous chromosomes begin to pair closely together
stage in meiosis prophase I
medical

Examples

During zygotene, the homologous chromosomes start to align closely side by side.
Scientists observed synapsis occurring in the zygotene stage of meiosis.
Crossing over is prepared as chromosomes pair during zygotene in many organisms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: leptotene - first stage of prophase I in meiosis
  • Degree Higher: pachytene - third stage of prophase I in meiosis

Etymology

From Greek zygotos (yoked) + -ene (used in chemical and biological terms).
