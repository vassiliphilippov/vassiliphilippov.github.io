Quaalude

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːˌluːd / (AmE), / ˈkwɑː.luːd / (BrE)
A sedative-hypnotic drug, formerly prescribed as a sleeping pill and tranquilizer, now known chiefly for its use as a drug of abuse.
sedative-hypnotic drug; drug of abuse

Examples

She was arrested for possessing Quaalude tablets without a prescription.
The use of Quaalude as a recreational drug became widespread in the 1970s.
Doctors no longer prescribe Quaalude due to its high potential for abuse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methaqualone - the generic name for Quaalude, a sedative-hypnotic drug
  • Synonym: barbiturate - a class of sedative-hypnotic drugs with similar effects

Etymology

Trade name coined in the 1960s as an alteration of 'quiet interlude'.
