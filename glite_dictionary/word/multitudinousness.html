multitudinousness

1. NOUN

/ mʌlˌtɪtjuˈdɪnəsnəs / (AmE), / mʌlˌtɪtjuːˈdɪnəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being very numerous; a great number or multitude, especially of people
the state of being numerous

Examples

The multitudinousness of the crowd overwhelmed the small security team.
He marveled at the multitudinousness of stars visible in the night sky.
The city is defined by the multitudinousness of its cultures and voices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multitude - a large number of people or things
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being in short supply; rarity

Etymology

Formed from 'multitudinous' + '-ness', with 'multitudinous' from Latin 'multitudo' (a great number).
