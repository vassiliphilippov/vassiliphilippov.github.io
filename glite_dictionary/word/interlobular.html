interlobular

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈlɑbjələr / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈlɒbjʊlə / (BrE)
Situated between lobes or lobules, especially in anatomical or medical contexts.
between lobes or lobules (anatomy)
medical

Examples

The interlobular ducts play a vital role in draining secretions between liver lobules.
Changes in interlobular connective tissue can be seen on certain histological slides.
The radiologist noted thickening of the interlobular septa in the patient’s lungs.

Etymology

From Latin 'inter-' (between) + 'lobulus' (small lobe).
