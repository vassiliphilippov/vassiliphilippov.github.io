lox

1. NOUN

/ lɑks / (AmE), / lɒks / (BrE)
brine-cured salmon that is lightly smoked, often served in thin slices as a delicacy
brine-cured, lightly smoked salmon
american

Examples

He ordered a bagel with cream cheese and lox for breakfast.
The traditional platter included lox, capers, and red onions.
She learned how to make homemade lox using her grandmother's recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoked salmon - salmon that has been cured and fully smoked

Etymology

From Yiddish *laks*, from Middle High German *lahs* (salmon), ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *laks* (salmon).
