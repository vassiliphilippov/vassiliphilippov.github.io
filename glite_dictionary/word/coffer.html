coffer

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːfər / (AmE), / ˈkɒfə / (BrE)
a strong chest or box used for storing money, valuables, or important documents
strongbox or chest for valuables

Examples

The priceless jewels were locked away in a heavy coffer beneath the floorboards.
The old castle's coffer contained documents dating back centuries.
After the fundraiser, the charity's coffer was full enough to support several new projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strongbox - a sturdy box for storing money or valuables
  • Synonym: safe - a secure container for valuables, usually with a lock

Etymology

From Old French 'cofre', from Latin 'cophinus' (basket).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːfər / (AmE), / ˈkɒfə / (BrE)
an ornamental recessed panel in a ceiling or dome, used especially in architecture for decorative effect
ornamental sunken ceiling panel

Examples

The ancient temple's ceiling was decorated with an intricate coffer pattern.
Each coffer in the dome was painted with a unique design.
The architects chose to add a coffer to the central section of the ceiling for added elegance.

Etymology

From Old French cofre (chest), from Latin cophinus (basket). Architectural sense from resemblance to a chest lid.
