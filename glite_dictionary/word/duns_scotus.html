Duns Scotus

1. PROPN

/ dʌnz ˈskoʊtəs / (AmE), / dʌnz ˈskəʊtəs / (BrE)
(c.1265–1308), a Scottish theologian and philosopher known for his influential works on metaphysics, theology, and logic during the Middle Ages.
Scottish medieval theologian and philosopher

Examples

Many philosophical debates in the Middle Ages were influenced by Duns Scotus.
The ideas of Duns Scotus remain relevant to scholars of theology and metaphysics.
Duns Scotus's doctrine of the 'univocity of being' continues to be studied today.

Etymology

John Duns Scotus, from Duns in Berwickshire, Scotland; 'Scotus' is Latin for 'the Scot'.
