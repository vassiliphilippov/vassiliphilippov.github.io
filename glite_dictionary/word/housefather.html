housefather

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊsˌfɑðər / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌfɑːðə / (BrE)
A man who is responsible for the care and supervision of children in an institution such as an orphanage or boarding school.
man in charge of children in an institution

Examples

After the transfer, Mr. Brown became the new housefather at the boys' dormitory.
The housefather ensured the children followed the rules and attended their classes.
Many students admired their housefather for his kindness and patience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: housemother - a woman in charge of children in an institution

Etymology

Formed from 'house' + 'father' by analogy to 'housemother.'
