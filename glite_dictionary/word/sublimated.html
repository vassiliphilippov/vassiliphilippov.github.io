sublimated

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌbləˌmeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌblɪmeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a substance) having changed directly from a solid to a gas, or from a gas to a solid, without becoming liquid in between.
changed from solid to gas without becoming liquid

Examples

The ice in the freezer had sublimated over time, leaving the tray almost empty.
In dry climates, snow may simply sublimated rather than melting into water.
The sublimated carbon dioxide, known as dry ice, produces dense clouds of vapor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sublimation - the process of changing from solid to gas without passing through the liquid phase

Etymology

From Latin 'sublimare' (to raise to a higher place), via Medieval Latin 'sublimatus', used in alchemical context.
