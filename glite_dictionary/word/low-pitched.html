low-pitched

1. ADJ

/ loʊ ˈpɪtʃt / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈpɪtʃt / (BrE)
(of a sound or voice) having a low frequency; not high in pitch.
having a low pitch (sound/voice)

Examples

His voice became low-pitched and soothing as he reassured the children.
We heard a low-pitched hum coming from the engine.
The cello produces a deep, low-pitched sound compared to the violin.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high-pitched - having a high frequency or pitch (of a sound/voice)
  • Synonym: deep - having a low, rich, resonant sound (of a voice/sound)
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈloʊ pɪtʃt / (AmE), / ˈləʊ pɪtʃt / (BrE)
(of a roof or surface) set at a low angle or slant, not steep.
set at a low angle or slant (e.g., roof)

Examples

The cottage has a low-pitched roof that gives it a quaint appearance.
A low-pitched ceiling can make a room feel more intimate but less spacious.
The architect designed the building with low-pitched eaves to match the traditional style.

Synset Examples

a low-pitched roof

Related Terms

  • Antonym: steep - having a sharp or high angle or incline

Synset POS: s
