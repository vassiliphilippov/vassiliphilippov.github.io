waterer

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑtərər / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtərə / (BrE)
a person or device that supplies or distributes water, especially for drinking or irrigation
person or device that supplies water

Examples

The waterer made sure every plant in the greenhouse received enough moisture each morning.
During the marathon, a waterer handed drinks to the runners along the route.
Each waterer was responsible for refilling the livestock's troughs on the farm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watering can - a container with a spout, used to water plants
  • Synonym: irrigator - a person or device that irrigates land

Etymology

Formed from 'water' with agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtərər / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtərə / (BrE)
a person whose job or task is to water plants or crops
person who waters plants or crops

Examples

The greenhouse waterer checks every pot each morning.
She worked as a waterer on the estate during the dry season.
Without a reliable waterer, the seedlings struggled to survive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gardener - someone who tends and cultivates a garden

Etymology

From 'water' (verb) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
