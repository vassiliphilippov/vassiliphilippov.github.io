gut-wrenching

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡʌtˌrɛntʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌtˌrɛntʃɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely distressing or emotionally painful; causing intense emotional upset or anguish
extremely distressing or emotionally painful
informal

Examples

Watching the news about the disaster was a truly gut-wrenching experience.
She read a gut-wrenching letter from her friend who had lost everything.
The film's gut-wrenching ending left the audience in tears.

Synset Examples

I felt gut-wrenched after hearing the news.
His story left me gut-wrenched and speechless.
The documentary was gut-wrenching yet powerful.
It was a gut-wrenching experience to witness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harrowing - very distressing or painful
  • Synonym: agonizing - causing great physical or mental pain
  • Antonym: uplifting - inspiring happiness or hope

Etymology

From 'gut' (stomach) + 'wrenching' (twisting or pulling violently), suggesting a feeling so intense it is as if one's stomach is being twisted.
