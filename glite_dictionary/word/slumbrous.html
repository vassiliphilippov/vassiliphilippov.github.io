slumbrous

1. ADJ

/ ˈslʌm.brəs / (AmE), / ˈslʌm.brəs / (BrE)
quiet, tranquil, or suggestive of sleep; drowsy or inducing sleepiness
quiet or suggestive of sleep
archaic

Examples

The slumbrous garden was still except for the gentle hum of insects.
A slumbrous atmosphere settled over the house as the afternoon heat increased.
Her voice had a slumbrous quality that made everyone feel at ease.

Synset Examples

a slumberous June morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drowsy - sleepy and lethargic; half asleep
  • Antonym: lively - full of life and energy

Etymology

From Latin 'slumber' via Old French 'sommeiller' (to sleep), with the suffix '-ous'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈslʌmbrəs / (AmE), / ˈslʌmbrəs / (BrE)
full of or suggestive of sleep; sleepy or drowsy.
sleepy or drowsy

Examples

The warm afternoon air made everyone feel slumbrous and relaxed.
She gazed at him with slumbrous eyes, barely able to stay awake.
The room was filled with a slumbrous silence, broken only by distant birdsong.

Synset Examples

slumberous (or slumbrous) eyes
‘slumbery’ is archaic
the sound had a somnolent effect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: somnolent - sleepy; causing sleep
  • Antonym: wakeful - not inclined to sleep; alert

Etymology

From Latin *slumber* (sleep) with the -ous suffix forming adjectives.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
