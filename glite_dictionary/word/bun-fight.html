bun-fight

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌn ˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʌn ˌfaɪt / (BrE)
(British) A grand formal party or elaborate social gathering, especially one held to mark an important occasion.
grand formal party (British)
british informal

Examples

The annual awards ceremony turned into quite the bun-fight, with celebrities mingling and speeches aplenty.
Invitations to the royal bun-fight were highly coveted among politicians and society figures.
She had to buy a new dress for the embassy's bun-fight next weekend.

Etymology

Originally British slang, possibly referring humorously to parties where refreshments are served.
