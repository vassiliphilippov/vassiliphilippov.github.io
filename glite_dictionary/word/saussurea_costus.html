Saussurea costus

1. NOUN

/ saʊˈsʊəriə ˈkɒstəs / (AmE), / sɔːˈsjʊəriə ˈkɒstəs / (BrE)
An aromatic herb native to the eastern Himalayas, especially Kashmir, with purple florets and a fragrant root used in perfumery and for preserving furs.
aromatic Himalayan herb used in perfumery

Examples

Extracts from Saussurea costus are widely used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
The fragrant root of Saussurea costus has long been valued for its essential oils.
Farmers in Kashmir cultivate Saussurea costus for export to the perfume industry.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Saussurea', after H.-B. de Saussure, Swiss botanist, and Latinized species name 'costus', from Greek 'kostos' (a kind of aromatic root).
