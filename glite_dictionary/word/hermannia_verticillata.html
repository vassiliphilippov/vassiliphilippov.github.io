Hermannia verticillata

1. NOUN

/ hərˈmɑːni.ə vərˌtɪsɪˈleɪtə / (AmE), / həˈmɑːnɪə ˌvɜː.tɪ.sɪˈleɪ.tə / (BrE)
An African shrub with trailing stems and slender, honey-scented yellow flowers, either solitary or in pairs.
African shrub with yellow, honey-scented flowers

Examples

The Hermannia verticillata in her garden bloomed with delicate yellow flowers in early summer.
Botanists study Hermannia verticillata for its unique honey-scented blossoms and decumbent stems.
In South Africa, Hermannia verticillata is admired for its ornamental value in native plant gardens.

Etymology

Hermannia: named after German botanist Paul Hermann; verticillata: from Latin 'verticillatus,' meaning whorled (referring to the leaf or flower arrangement).
