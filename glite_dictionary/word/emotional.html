emotional

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmoʊʃənl / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊʃənl / (BrE)
relating to, or characterized by emotion or the emotions.
pertaining to emotion

Examples

After the accident, she needed time to recover her emotional health.
He went through an emotional crisis after losing his job.
Reading that letter was a deeply emotional experience for him.

Synset Examples

emotional health
an emotional crisis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affective - relating to moods, feelings, and attitudes

Etymology

From Latin emovere (to move out, stir up), via French émotion (emotion), plus English suffix -al.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02995009-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈmoʊʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊʃənl̩ / (BrE)
(of a decision, reaction, etc.) determined or influenced more by emotion than by rational thought.
influenced by emotion, not reason

Examples

Her emotional reaction surprised those who expected a logical response.
The jury made an emotional decision instead of considering the evidence carefully.
Avoid making emotional judgments when dealing with complicated situations.

Synset Examples

it was an emotional judgment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rational - based on or in accordance with reason or logic

Etymology

From Latin emovēre (to move out, stir up), via 'emotion' + '-al'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01933678-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈmoʊʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊʃənl̩ / (BrE)
(of a person) showing feelings or being easily affected by emotion, especially in an excessive way.
easily affected by emotion

Examples

She became very emotional during the farewell speech.
He is often emotional about small things that others ignore.
The movie was so sad that even the toughest among us got emotional.

Synset Examples

he would become emotional over nothing at all
she was worked up about all the noise

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing strong emotions; self-controlled
  • Antonym: stoic - enduring pain or hardship without showing emotions

Etymology

From late Latin 'emotionalis', from 'emotio' (emotion).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00086315-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ɪˈmoʊʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊʃənl̩ / (BrE)
showing or characterized by strong feelings; having more than usual emotion.
showing strong feelings

Examples

She became very emotional when she saw her family after a long time.
His speech was emotional and moved many people to tears.
It's difficult to make emotional decisions when you are under stress.

Synset Examples

his behavior was highly emotional

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passionate - showing strong feelings or emotions
  • Antonym: unemotional - not showing or characterized by strong emotion

Etymology

From emotion + -al; 'emotion' from French émotion, from émouvoir 'excite' (from Latin ēmovēre).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00856955-a
Synset POS: a
