Habsburg

1. PROPN

/ ˈhæbz.bɝːɡ / (AmE), / ˈhæbz.bɜːɡ / (BrE)
a European royal family that ruled several states, including Austria and Spain, and held the Holy Roman Emperor's crown from 1440 to 1806.
dynasty of European rulers (1440–1806)

Examples

Many European conflicts in the 16th century involved the powerful Habsburg dynasty.
Maria Theresa was a prominent member of the Habsburg family and ruled Austria for forty years.
The marriages arranged by the Habsburgs helped them expand their influence throughout Europe.

Etymology

From the family seat Habsburg Castle in Switzerland, from Middle High German habesburc.
