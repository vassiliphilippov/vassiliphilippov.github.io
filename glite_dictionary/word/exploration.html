exploration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkspləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkspləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of searching, examining, or investigating something thoroughly and systematically
systematic search or investigation

Examples

The exploration of the unknown jungle took several weeks and required careful planning.
Scientific exploration of the deep ocean has revealed many new species in recent years.
Their exploration of the ancient ruins provided valuable insights into the past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigation - a systematic examination or inquiry
  • Degree Higher: discovery - the act of finding something new

Etymology

From Latin exploratio (investigation), from explorare (to investigate).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.spləˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spləˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of traveling through an unfamiliar area in order to learn about it or discover new information
travel for discovery or investigation

Examples

The era of space exploration began in the twentieth century.
Her interest in marine exploration led her to become an oceanographer.
The team's exploration of the cave revealed ancient paintings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expedition - a journey organized for a particular purpose, often exploration or research
  • Synonym: discovery - the act of finding something new
  • Antonym: routine - a regular or habitual course of action, not involving discovery

Etymology

From Latin exploratio (investigation, search), from explorare (to search out).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛkspləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛksplɔːˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of carefully examining or considering a subject in a systematic way
systematic investigation or study

Examples

The committee began a thorough exploration of the ethics involved in genetic testing.
Her book offers a deep exploration of human relationships and identity.
Any exploration of complex topics requires patience and critical thinking.

Synset Examples

he called for a careful exploration of the consequences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigation - the act of examining something systematically, especially to discover the truth
  • Synonym: examination - the process of looking at or considering something very carefully

Etymology

From Latin exploratio (investigation, examination), from explorare (to search out, investigate).
