irretrievably

1. ADV

/ ˌɪrəˈtrivəbli / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈtriːvəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be recovered, undone, or rectified.
in a way that cannot be reversed

Examples

After the fire, the documents were irretrievably destroyed.
He realized too late that the opportunity was irretrievably lost.
Their trust had been irretrievably broken by the betrayal.

Synset Examples

it is irretrievably lost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irreversibly - in a way that cannot be reversed
  • Antonym: reversibly - in a way that can be reversed

Etymology

From 'irretrievable' + '-ly', based on Latin 'in-' (not) + 'retrievare' (to recover).
