Unguiculata

1. NOUN

/ ʌŋˌɡwɪkjəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ʌŋˌɡwɪkjʊˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
(historical, zoology) A former division of mammals distinguished by the presence of nails or claws, as opposed to hoofs or flippers.
former group of clawed or nailed mammals

Examples

In nineteenth-century zoological texts, mammals with nails or claws were classified under Unguiculata.
The classification Unguiculata is now considered obsolete in modern taxonomy.
Primates and carnivores were once grouped together in the order Unguiculata due to their clawed or nailed digits.

Etymology

From Latin 'unguiculatus' meaning 'having little nails or claws', from 'unguis' (nail, claw).
