Mergus merganser americanus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɜrɡəs mɜrˈɡænsər əˌmɛrɪˈkænəs / (AmE), / ˌmɜːɡəs mɜːˈɡænsə əˌmɛrɪˈkænəs / (BrE)
A common North American diving duck, considered a subspecies of the European goosander, known for its slender bill and diving habits.
North American subspecies of the goosander duck

Examples

A flock of Mergus merganser americanus can often be seen diving for fish on northern lakes.
The distinctive red-billed appearance helps birdwatchers identify the Mergus merganser americanus during migration.
Wildlife biologists are studying habitat changes affecting the population of Mergus merganser americanus in the Great Lakes region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goosander - a large diving duck also known as the common merganser

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name Mergus and species name merganser, with americanus indicating American origin.
