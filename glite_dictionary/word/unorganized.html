unorganized

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɔrɡəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔːɡənaɪzd / (BrE)
(of workers or employees) not belonging to or represented by a trade union.
not belonging to a trade union

Examples

Many unorganized workers do not have access to collective bargaining.
A campaign was launched to unionize the unorganized employees at the factory.
Most unorganized staff members felt isolated from the negotiations.

Synset Examples

the workers in the plant were unorganized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unionized - (of workers) being members of a trade union
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02484476-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɔr.ɡəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔː.ɡə.naɪzd / (BrE)
not having or belonging to a structured or systematic whole; lacking formal arrangement or organization.
not structured; lacking organization

Examples

The charity's efforts were hampered by an unorganized distribution network.
Some files remained unorganized on her computer for months.
The meeting dissolved into chaos because the agenda was completely unorganized.

Synset Examples

unorganized territories lack a formal government

Related Terms

  • Antonym: organized - having or belonging to a structured whole; arranged in a systematic way

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + organized, from French organiser or Medieval Latin organizare (to organize).
