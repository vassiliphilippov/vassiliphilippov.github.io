plaudit

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɔːdɪt / (AmE), / ˈplɔːdɪt / (BrE)
an expression of strong praise or approval; enthusiastic commendation
enthusiastic praise or approval

Examples

The director received plaudits from critics for her innovative film.
Her performance drew plaudits from the entire audience.
Despite initial skepticism, his invention eventually won him plaudits from the scientific community.

Synset Examples

the book met with modest acclaim
he acknowledged the plaudits of the crowd
they gave him more eclat than he really deserved

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acclaim - enthusiastic and public praise
  • Synonym: applause - approval or praise shown by clapping
  • Antonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'plaudite', the imperative plural of 'plaudere' (to applaud).
