to delouse

1. VERB

/ diˈlaʊs / (AmE), / diːˈlaʊs / (BrE)
to remove lice from a person, animal, or object
remove lice from

Examples

The health workers needed to delouse the children as a precaution.
Prisoners were deloused upon arrival to prevent outbreaks of disease.
She spent hours carefully delousing her daughter's hair.

Synset Examples

They deloused the prisoners after they liberated the camps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to delouse - remove lice from
  • Antonym: to infest - to overrun or inhabit in large numbers (as parasites)

Etymology

Formed from de- (remove) + louse (parasite) in English.
