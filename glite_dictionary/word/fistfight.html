fistfight

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪstˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪstˌfaɪt / (BrE)
A physical fight in which people hit each other with their bare fists, without using weapons.
fight using bare fists

Examples

The two men got into a fistfight outside the bar last night.
Despite their argument, they managed to avoid a fistfight in the end.
He walked away with a black eye after losing the fistfight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brawl - a noisy and rough fight, often involving several people
  • Degree Higher: boxing - a sport where two people fight using their fists, wearing gloves

2. VERB

/ ˈfɪstˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪstˌfaɪt / (BrE)
to fight with the fists, usually in an angry or physical confrontation rather than with weapons
to fight using fists

Examples

The two men threatened to fistfight in the parking lot after their argument.
During his youth, he often fistfought with classmates over minor disputes.
They were caught fistfighting behind the school and were sent to the principal's office.

Synset Examples

The man wanted to fist-fight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brawl - a noisy, rough fight involving several people
  • Synonym: box - to fight with fists, especially as a sport
