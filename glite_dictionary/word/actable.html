actable

1. ADJ

/ ˈæk.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈæk.tə.bəl / (BrE)
capable of being performed or represented on stage; suitable for portrayal in a play or dramatic production
suitable for stage performance

Examples

The director praised the script for its highly actable dialogue.
She was looking for an actable role to showcase her skills on stage.
Despite its powerful message, the scene was not particularly actable as written.

Synset Examples

an actable scene

Etymology

From 'act' + '-able', patterned after other adjectives meaning 'able to be acted'.
