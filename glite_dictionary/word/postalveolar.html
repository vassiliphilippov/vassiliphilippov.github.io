postalveolar

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊstælˈvioʊlər / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstælˈviːələ / (BrE)
(Phonetics) A speech sound articulated with the tongue near or touching the back of the alveolar ridge, just behind the upper front teeth.
sound made near the back of the alveolar ridge
medical

Examples

The English 'sh' sound in 'ship' is a postalveolar fricative.
Many languages have more than one postalveolar consonant in their phonemic inventory.
Children often find it difficult to produce postalveolar sounds correctly when they begin to speak.

Synset Examples

The sibilant postalveolars (i.e. fricatives and affricates) are sometimes called ‘hush consonants’ because they include the sound /ʃ/ of English ‘Shhh!’ (as distinguished from the ‘hiss’ consonant /s/, as in ‘Ssss!’).

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: alveolar - a speech sound made with the tongue against the alveolar ridge

Etymology

From Latin 'post-' (after, behind) + 'alveolar' (relating to the alveolus).
