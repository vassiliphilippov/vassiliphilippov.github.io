snowstorm

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌstɔrm / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌstɔːm / (BrE)
a storm characterized by heavy snowfall and strong winds, often reducing visibility
storm with heavy snow and wind

Examples

We were caught in a snowstorm while driving through the mountains.
The city was blanketed in white after last night's snowstorm.
Because of the intense snowstorm, all flights were cancelled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blizzard - a severe snowstorm with strong sustained winds

Etymology

From 'snow' + 'storm', modeled after English compounding conventions.
