imaret

1. NOUN

/ iˈmɑːrɛt / (AmE), / ɪˈmɑːrɛt / (BrE)
A public hostel or soup kitchen, especially one established under Ottoman rule in Turkey, that provided free food and shelter to travelers and the poor.
Ottoman Turkish public hostel or soup kitchen

Examples

The imaret provided free meals to travelers and local residents every day.
Many imarets were built throughout the Ottoman Empire to serve the poor and needy.
She visited an old imaret in Istanbul to learn about its history.

Etymology

From Turkish imaret (public kitchen/hostel), ultimately from Arabic ‘imāra (building, structure).
