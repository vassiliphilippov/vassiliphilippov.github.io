brightness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈbraɪtnəs / (BrE)
quickness and intelligence, especially as shown in being clever and witty
quick and witty intelligence

Examples

Eva's brightness made her stand out during the debate competition.
People admired the comedian for his brightness as well as his warmth.
Teachers often encourage brightness by fostering curiosity and humor in the classroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleverness - the quality of being clever
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of intelligence or quickness

Etymology

From Old English *beorhtnes* (state of being bright), related to 'bright'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈbraɪtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being luminous or emitting or reflecting light
quality of being luminous or reflecting light

Examples

The brightness of the sun made it difficult to look up without wearing sunglasses.
Adjust the brightness on your screen to reduce eye strain.
Scientists measured the brightness of the star to estimate its distance from Earth.

Synset Examples

its luminosity is measured relative to that of our sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luminosity - the intrinsic brightness of a celestial object
  • Antonym: dimness - the state of being not bright; lacking light

Etymology

From Old English 'beorht' (bright) + '-ness' (state or quality).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈbraɪtnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being bright; the degree to which a color or visual perception is closer to white than to black
degree of lightness or luminance

Examples

The brightness of the new lamp made the reading room much more welcoming.
Astronomers measure the brightness of stars to determine their distance and size.
Adjusting the brightness of your computer screen may reduce eye strain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dullness - the quality of being dull or lacking brightness
  • Degree Higher: brilliance - the quality of being very bright or radiant
  • Degree Lower: dimness - the state of having little brightness; faint light

Etymology

From Old English beorht (bright) + -ness (state, quality).
