Mazar-i-Sharif

1. PROPN

/ məˌzɑːr i ʃəˈriːf / (AmE), / məˌzɑːr i ʃəˈriːf / (BrE)
a city in northern Afghanistan, noted for its historical and cultural significance
city in northern Afghanistan

Examples

Many tourists visit Mazar-i-Sharif during the annual Nowruz festival.
The Blue Mosque in Mazar-i-Sharif is one of Afghanistan's most famous landmarks.
Trade routes through Mazar-i-Sharif have connected Central Asia for centuries.

Etymology

From Persian 'mazār' (shrine or tomb) and 'sharīf' (noble); refers to the noble shrine believed to be the tomb of Ali.
