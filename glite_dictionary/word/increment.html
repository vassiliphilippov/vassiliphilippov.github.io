increment

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋ.krəˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈɪŋ.krɪ.mənt / (BrE)
an amount by which something increases, especially a regular or fixed amount
an increase or added amount

Examples

There will be a salary increment each year based on performance reviews.
The program updates by small increments rather than one large change.
She measured the temperature in five-degree increments during the experiment.

Synset Examples

they proposed an increase of 15 percent in the fare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - a rise in the size, amount, or degree of something
  • Antonym: decrement - an amount by which something decreases

Etymology

From Latin incrementum (growth, increase), from increscere (to grow).
