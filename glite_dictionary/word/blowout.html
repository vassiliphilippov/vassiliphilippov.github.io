blowout

1. NOUN

/ ˈbloʊˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈbləʊˌaʊt / (BrE)
an easy or overwhelming victory in a competition or game
easy victory in competition

Examples

The basketball game was a blowout, with the home team winning by forty points.
Last night's match turned into a blowout after halftime.
Fans left early because the blowout made the result obvious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rout - a decisive defeat
  • Antonym: nail-biter - a very close contest or game
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07490247-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈbloʊˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈbləʊˌaʊt / (BrE)
a sudden failure or rupture of a part or apparatus, especially when referring to a burst tire or similar mechanical malfunction
sudden mechanical failure (e.g. burst tire)

Examples

While driving home, we experienced a blowout on the highway and had to pull over immediately.
The mechanic inspected the tire and confirmed that the blowout was caused by low air pressure.
Power was lost in the building due to a blowout in the main electrical fuse.

Synset Examples

the right front tire had a blowout
as a result of the blowout we lost all the lights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rupture - a break or burst suddenly
  • Synonym: failure - the action or state of not functioning correctly

Etymology

From 'blow' (burst out) + 'out', originally used for oil and gas explosions in wells.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbloʊˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈbləʊˌaʊt / (BrE)
an extravagant or lively party or celebration; a festive gathering
a lively party or celebration

Examples

They threw a huge blowout for her graduation that lasted all night.
Last year’s New Year’s Eve blowout was the talk of the town.
We’re planning a little blowout to celebrate the end of the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: party - a social gathering for celebration or fun
  • Synonym: bash - a large, lively party
