Peziza

1. NOUN

/ pəˈzaɪzə / (AmE), / pɛˈziːzə / (BrE)
a genus of cup fungi in the family Pezizaceae, characterized by their disc- or cup-shaped fruiting bodies, found on decaying wood or soil
genus of cup fungi

Examples

The cup-shaped fruiting bodies of Peziza fungi are often found on rotting logs in the forest.
Scientists identified the mysterious fungus as a species belonging to the genus Peziza.
Some Peziza species can be seen growing on richly manured soil during wet spring months.

Etymology

From Latin *peziza* (a type of fungus), itself from Greek *peziza* (a kind of edible fungus).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈzaɪzə / (AmE), / pɛˈziːzə / (BrE)
a genus of cup-shaped fungi in the family Pezizaceae, commonly known as cup fungi
genus of cup fungi

Examples

The distinctive shape of Peziza makes it easy to recognize among other fungi.
Several species within the genus Peziza can be found growing on decaying wood after rain.
Mycologists study Peziza to better understand fungal reproduction and diversity.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin *Peziza*, of uncertain origin, used in taxonomy for this genus of cup fungi.
