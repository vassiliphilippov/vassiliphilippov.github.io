Dinornis

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈnɔrnɪs / (AmE), / dɪˈnɔːnɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Dinornithidae, comprising large, extinct, flightless birds known as moas, native to New Zealand.
genus of extinct New Zealand moas

Examples

Fossils of Dinornis have been found throughout New Zealand, shedding light on its prehistoric fauna.
The Dinornis was among the largest birds to have ever lived, with some individuals reaching three meters in height.
Researchers believe that Dinornis became extinct due to overhunting by humans in the late 13th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moa - large extinct flightless bird native to New Zealand

Etymology

From Greek 'deinos' (terrible) + 'ornis' (bird), meaning 'terrible bird'.
