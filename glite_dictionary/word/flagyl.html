Flagyl

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæɡ.ɪl / (AmE), / ˈflæɡ.ɪl / (BrE)
A trademarked antiprotozoal drug (metronidazole) used to treat infections such as trichomoniasis and giardiasis.
brand of antiprotozoal drug (metronidazole)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Flagyl to treat her giardiasis infection.
After taking Flagyl for a week, his symptoms improved significantly.
You should avoid alcohol while you are on Flagyl to prevent adverse reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metronidazole - antibiotic and antiprotozoal agent, the generic name of Flagyl
