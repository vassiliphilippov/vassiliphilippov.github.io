loony

1. ADJ

/ ˈluːni / (AmE), / ˈluːni / (BrE)
Crazy or mentally unsound; behaving in a way that is strange or eccentric, especially as an informal or slang term.
crazy; mentally irregular (informal)
informal slang

Examples

Everyone thought her plan was loony, but she proved them wrong.
He acted a bit loony after staying up all night.
That movie is full of loony characters and wild plot twists.

Synset Examples

it used to drive my husband balmy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crazy - mentally deranged; insane or eccentric
  • Antonym: sane - of sound mind; not mentally ill

Etymology

Alteration of 'lunatic', which comes from Latin 'lunaticus' (moonstruck).

2. NOUN

/ ˈluːni / (AmE), / ˈluːni / (BrE)
(informal, sometimes offensive) A person who is insane or acts in a very strange or crazy way, sometimes perceived as dangerous.
crazy or deranged person (slang)
informal slang

Examples

Everyone thought the old man was a loony after he started talking to trees.
That neighborhood is full of loonies who shout at traffic.
Don't listen to that loony; he's always coming up with wild conspiracy theories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lunatic - a person who is mentally ill (dated, offensive)
  • Synonym: madman - a man who behaves in a wildly foolish way
  • Antonym: sane - not mentally ill or deranged

Etymology

Probably short for 'lunatic', from Latin 'lunaticus' (moonstruck).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
