Sriracha

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈrɑː.tʃə / (AmE), / sɪˈrɑː.tʃə / (BrE)
A spicy chili sauce made from pureed chili peppers, garlic, vinegar, sugar, and salt, originally from Thailand.
spicy chili-garlic sauce from Thailand

Examples

She added a generous squeeze of Sriracha to her bowl of noodles for an extra kick.
The restaurant offers homemade Sriracha that’s spicier than the bottled version.
I always keep a bottle of Sriracha in my fridge for when I want to spice up my meals.

Etymology

Named after Si Racha, a coastal town in Thailand where the sauce originated.
