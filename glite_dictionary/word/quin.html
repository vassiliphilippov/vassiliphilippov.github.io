quin

1. NOUN

/ kwɪn / (AmE), / kwɪn / (BrE)
one of five children born at the same time to the same mother
one of five siblings born together

Examples

The media loved to follow the lives of the famous set of quins.
After giving birth to quins, the hospital staff celebrated with the family.
Each quin in the group had a distinct personality despite their similarities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quintuplet - one of five offspring born in a single birth
  • Degree Lower: quad - one of four children born at the same time
  • Degree Higher: sextuplet - one of six children born at the same time

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'quintuplet', from Latin 'quintus' meaning 'fifth'.
