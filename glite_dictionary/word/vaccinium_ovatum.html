Vaccinium ovatum

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm oʊˈveɪtəm / (AmE), / vækˈsɪniəm əʊˈvɑːtəm / (BrE)
a stiff, bushy evergreen shrub native to western North America, with glossy green leaves and sour black berries, often used in floral arrangements
evergreen shrub of western North America

Examples

Vaccinium ovatum is commonly found along the Pacific coast, thriving in both sun and shade.
The glossy leaves of Vaccinium ovatum are prized in decorative floral displays.
Wildlife such as birds and small mammals enjoy the tart berries produced by Vaccinium ovatum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evergreen huckleberry - common name for Vaccinium ovatum, a North American shrub with edible berries

Etymology

From Latin; 'Vaccinium' is the genus name for certain berries; 'ovatum' means 'egg-shaped,' referring to the leaf shape.
