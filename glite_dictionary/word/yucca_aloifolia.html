Yucca aloifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʌkə ˌæloʊˈfaɪliə / (AmE), / ˈjʌkə ˌalɔɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A species of yucca plant with stiff, spine-tipped leaves and clusters of white flowers, native to the southern United States and tropical America.
a stiff-leaved yucca plant species

Examples

The Yucca aloifolia can tolerate sandy soils and is often used as an ornamental plant.
I planted a Yucca aloifolia in my garden for its striking white flowers.
Many coastal landscapes feature the hardy Yucca aloifolia due to its resilience to salt spray.

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Yucca' as the genus name and 'aloifolia' meaning 'aloe-leaved'.
