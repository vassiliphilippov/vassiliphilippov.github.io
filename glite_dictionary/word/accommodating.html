accommodating

1. ADJ

/ əˈkɑːməˌdeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈkɒməˌdeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
willing to help or do things for others; eager to please and adapt to others' needs or wishes
helpful and eager to please

Examples

Our hosts were extremely accommodating and made sure we had everything we needed.
She is always accommodating, adjusting her schedule to help her friends.
The restaurant staff were very accommodating to customers with special dietary requirements.

Synset Examples

the warden was always accommodating in allowing visitors in
made a special effort to be accommodating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obliging - willing to help; eager to do favors for others
  • Antonym: unhelpful - not willing or able to offer assistance

Etymology

From accommodate + -ing, originally from Latin accommodare (to make fit, adapt).

2. ADJ

/ əˈkɑməˌdeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈkɒmədeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
willing to help or do favors for others; obliging.
helpful; willing to do favors

Examples

The hotel staff were extremely accommodating during our stay.
She is always accommodating when her colleagues need help with their work.
His accommodating nature makes him very easy to work with.

Synset Examples

made a special effort to be accommodating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obliging - willing to help; helpful
  • Antonym: unhelpful - not willing to help or do favors

Etymology

From accommodate (verb) + -ing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01199506-s
Synset POS: s
