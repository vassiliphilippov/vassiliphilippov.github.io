Abnaki

1. ADJ

/ æbˈnɑːki / (AmE), / æbˈnɑːki / (BrE)
relating to the Abnakis, a Native American people, or their language
of the Abnakis or their language

Examples

The museum features several Abnaki artifacts and exhibits on tribal history.
She is conducting research on Abnaki oral traditions and storytelling.
An Abnaki elder spoke at the conference about the preservation of native languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Abenaki - the language spoken by the Abnakis

Etymology

From the Abnaki word *wôbanakiak* meaning 'people of the dawn land'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02610006-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈæbnəki, æbˈnɑːki / (AmE), / ˈæbnəki, æbˈnɑːki / (BrE)
A member of the Algonquian people native to Maine and southern Quebec.
member of an Algonquian people of Maine and Quebec

Examples

The Abnaki traditionally inhabited the region along the Kennebec River in Maine.
An Abnaki chief played a significant role in early colonial histories.
Many descendants of the Abnaki live in communities in both the United States and Canada today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Abenaki - alternate spelling of Abnaki

Etymology

From their own name in Eastern Abenaki, Wabanaki, meaning 'people of the dawn land'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09665709-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈæbnəˌki, æbˈnɑːki / (AmE), / ˈæbnəki, æbˈnɑːki / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Abnaki and Penobscot peoples of northeastern North America
Algonquian language of the Abnaki and Penobscot

Examples

Few people today still speak the Abnaki language fluently.
She is researching the grammar of Abnaki for her thesis.
The museum hosts audio recordings of elders speaking Abnaki.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a family of North American indigenous languages

Etymology

From the Abnaki word for 'people of the dawn', via French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06920824-n
Synset POS: n
