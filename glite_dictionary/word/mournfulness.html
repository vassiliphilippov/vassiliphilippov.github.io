mournfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrn.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmɔːn.f(ə)l.nəs / (BrE)
a feeling or appearance of deep sadness or sorrow
quality of being deeply sad or sorrowful

Examples

The mournfulness in her eyes was unmistakable as she attended the memorial service.
His music always has an air of mournfulness that resonates with listeners.
There was a mournfulness to the empty house after the children left for college.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorrow - a feeling of deep distress caused by loss or disappointment
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the state of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From 'mournful' + '-ness'; 'mournful' from Old English 'murnan' (to mourn).
