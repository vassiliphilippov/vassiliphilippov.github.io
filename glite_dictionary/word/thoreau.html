Thoreau

1. PROPN

/ θəˈroʊ / (AmE), / θəˈrəʊ / (BrE)
(1817–1862) United States writer, philosopher, and social critic, best known for his works on naturalism and civil disobedience.
American writer and social critic

Examples

Many students read Thoreau's 'Walden' in literature classes.
Henry David Thoreau advocated for simple living and self-reliance.
The philosophy of Thoreau continues to inspire environmentalists worldwide.
