Alopiidae

1. NOUN

/ əˈloʊpiˌaɪdi / (AmE), / əˌlɒpɪˈiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, pelagic sharks known as thresher sharks, characterized by their extremely long, whiplike tails.
family of thresher sharks
medical

Examples

The family Alopiidae includes all species of thresher sharks found in oceans worldwide.
Recent studies have explored the migratory patterns of Alopiidae in the Pacific Ocean.
Marine biologists are concerned about declining populations of Alopiidae due to overfishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thresher shark - large pelagic shark with a long tail, member of Alopiidae

Etymology

From New Latin Alopiidae, based on the genus Alopias, from Greek alōpēx (fox).
