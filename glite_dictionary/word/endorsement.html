endorsement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɔrsmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːsmənt / (BrE)
a signature, especially on a document such as a check, that makes it valid or authorizes its negotiation
validating signature on a document

Examples

The bank returned my check because it was missing an endorsement.
Before depositing the check, she added her endorsement on the back.
Without the required endorsement, the lottery winnings could not be claimed.

Synset Examples

the cashier would not cash the check without an endorsement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signature - a person's name written in their own handwriting for identification or authorization

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *indorsare* (to write on the back), from Latin *in-* (on) + *dorsum* (back).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɔːrsmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːsmənt / (BrE)
a statement publicly expressing approval or support for something, especially used as a promotional comment for products, books, or public figures
promotional statement of approval

Examples

The publisher placed a glowing endorsement on the back cover of the novel.
Her cookbook features an endorsement from a famous chef right on the dust jacket.
Many products today rely heavily on celebrity endorsements to attract buyers.

Synset Examples

the author got all his friends to write blurbs for his book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blurb - short promotional description or review, especially on books
  • Synonym: testimonial - written or spoken statement of endorsement, especially for promotion
  • Antonym: denouncement - public condemnation or criticism

Etymology

From Old French 'endosser' (to put on the back; to approve), influenced by Medieval Latin 'indorsare'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07262728-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɔrsmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːsmənt / (BrE)
The act of publicly expressing support or approval for someone or something, especially in a formal or official way.
act of expressing approval or support

Examples

Her company received an endorsement from a prominent scientist, which boosted its reputation.
The senator's endorsement of the bill helped sway public opinion.
Many athletes earn extra income through product endorsement deals.

Synset Examples

a star athlete can make a lot of money from endorsements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approval - acceptance or favorable opinion
  • Synonym: support - agreement with and encouragement for a person or idea
  • Antonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'indorsare' (to write on the back of), influenced by French 'endosser'.

4. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɔrsmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːsmənt / (BrE)
an official statement or action showing that you support or approve of someone or something, especially in a formal or public way
formal approval or support

Examples

The senator received the official endorsement of the teachers' union during her re-election campaign.
His application was strengthened by the manager's glowing endorsement of his skills and work ethic.
The celebrity's endorsement helped the new product achieve record sales.

Synset Examples

a Democrat usually gets the union's endorsement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approval - the act of agreeing with or accepting something
  • Antonym: disapproval - the act of refusing to approve or support something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'indorsare' (to write on the back), via Old French 'endosser', originally referring to signing one's name on the back of a document.
