tsundere

1. NOUN

/ tsunˈdɛr.eɪ / (AmE), / tsʊnˈdɛr.eɪ / (BrE)
A fictional character, typically in anime or manga, who is initially cold or hostile toward another person before gradually becoming warmer and more affectionate.
a character who warms up over time

Examples

The main female lead is a classic tsundere, often acting cold but secretly caring.
He has a thing for tsundere characters in romance anime.
At first, she seemed rude, but her tsundere side eventually won everyone over.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yandere - a character who is obsessively loving and may become violent

Etymology

From Japanese ツンデレ (tsundere), blending 'tsuntsun' (aloof, irritable) and 'deredere' (lovestruck, affectionate).

2. NOUN

/ tsuːnˈdɛɹeɪ / (AmE), / tsʊnˈdɛɹeɪ / (BrE)
a character, especially in anime or manga, who is initially cold, hostile, or aloof toward someone but gradually becomes warmer and more affectionate over time
person who is initially cold then shows warmth

Examples

The main heroine is a classic tsundere, acting hostile at first before revealing her true feelings.
Fans love the tsundere character type because it adds depth to romantic storylines.
She called him a tsundere after he blushed and refused to admit he cared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yandere - character who is loving but also violently possessive

Etymology

From Japanese ツンデレ (tsundere), combining tsun tsun ('to turn away in disgust') and dere dere ('lovey-dovey, affectionate').
