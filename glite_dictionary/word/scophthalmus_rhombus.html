Scophthalmus rhombus

1. NOUN

/ skoʊfˈθælməs ˈrɒmbəs / (AmE), / skɒfˈθalməs ˈrɒmbəs / (BrE)
A large European flatfish, also known as the brill, valued as a food fish.
European brill (flatfish species)

Examples

The fisherman proudly displayed a freshly caught Scophthalmus rhombus at the market.
The delicate flavor of Scophthalmus rhombus makes it a favorite in French cuisine.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Scophthalmus rhombus in the North Sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brill - A European flatfish, Scophthalmus rhombus

Etymology

From New Latin, where 'Scophthalmus' is the genus, rooted in Greek 'skophthalmos' (having eyes on one side), and 'rhombus' refers to the shape of the fish.
