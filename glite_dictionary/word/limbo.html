limbo

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪm.boʊ / (AmE), / ˈlɪm.bəʊ / (BrE)
an imaginary place where things that have been lost, forgotten, or neglected are kept; a state of neglect or suspension
a place or state for forgotten or neglected things

Examples

Many important files disappeared into limbo and were never recovered.
After the project was canceled, our efforts were left in limbo.
Her application remained in limbo for months, with no response from the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purgatory - a state or place of temporary suffering or waiting

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'limbus' meaning 'border' or 'edge', originally a theological term.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪm.boʊ / (AmE), / ˈlɪm.bəʊ / (BrE)
(in Roman Catholic theology) a place or state for the souls of unbaptized but innocent or righteous people, especially infants, who have not been assigned to heaven or hell.
place for unbaptized innocent souls (theology)

Examples

According to medieval doctrine, infants who died without baptism were believed to enter limbo.
Some theologians have debated the existence of limbo for centuries.
The concept of limbo is no longer an official part of Catholic teaching.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin ‘limbus’ (edge, border).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɪmboʊ / (AmE), / ˈlɪmbəʊ / (BrE)
a state or condition in which someone or something is neglected, forgotten, or left without attention or support
state of being neglected or forgotten

Examples

After the merger, many projects were left in limbo with no clear direction.
She felt her career was in limbo as she waited for a response from the employers.
Without the necessary documents, his visa application remained in limbo for months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oblivion - the state of being completely forgotten or unknown

Etymology

From Latin 'limbus' (edge, border), originally referring to the border of hell in medieval theology.
