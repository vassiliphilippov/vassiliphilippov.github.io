vena labialis superior

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪnə læˈbiːəlɪs suːˈpɪəriɚ / (AmE), / ˈviːnə læˈbɪəlɪs sjuːˈpɪəriə / (BrE)
a tributary of the facial vein that drains blood from the upper lip
vein draining the upper lip
medical

Examples

The vena labialis superior is an important vessel in surgical procedures involving the upper lip.
Damage to the vena labialis superior can cause significant bleeding in cases of facial trauma.
The doctor traced the infection's path to the vena labialis superior.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: facial vein - the main vein draining blood from the face

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein), 'labialis' (of the lip), 'superior' (upper).
