semi-tuberous

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˈtuːbɚəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈtjuːbərəs / (BrE)
(of a plant root or structure) partially tuberous; having some, but not fully developed, tuber characteristics.
partly tuberous (botany)

Examples

The plant has semi-tuberous roots that are partially swollen but not entirely like true tubers.
Several species in this genus exhibit semi-tuberous stems adapted for water storage.
Gardeners often prefer semi-tuberous varieties because they require less maintenance than fully tuberous plants.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tuberous - having or resembling tubers

Etymology

Formed by combining 'semi-', from Latin 'semi' (half), with 'tuberous', from Latin 'tuberosus' (full of lumps).
