oscilloscope

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪləˌskoʊp / (AmE), / əˈsɪl.ə.skəʊp / (BrE)
An electronic instrument that displays and analyzes the waveform of electronic signals, providing visual images of varying electrical quantities.
electronic device for displaying signal waveforms

Examples

The engineer used an oscilloscope to measure the voltage fluctuations in the circuit.
Students watched the electrical signals appear on the oscilloscope during the laboratory experiment.
Modern digital oscilloscopes allow users to store and analyze complex waveforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cathode-ray oscilloscope - early type of oscilloscope using a cathode ray tube
  • Antonym: spectrum analyzer - instrument for measuring the frequencies of an input signal

Etymology

From Latin oscillare (to swing) + -scope (from Greek skopein, to look at).
