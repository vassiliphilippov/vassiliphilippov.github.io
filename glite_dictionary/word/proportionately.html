proportionately

1. ADV

/ prəˈpɔrʃənətli / (AmE), / prəˈpɔːʃənətli / (BrE)
in a way that corresponds in amount, degree, or size
in proportion; correspondingly

Examples

The rewards will be proportionately distributed based on each member's contribution.
If the price increases, the cost will rise proportionately.
The penalty for late submission will decrease proportionately the sooner you turn in the assignment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proportionally - in a way that maintains the same proportion

Etymology

From 'proportionate' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'proportio' (relation by parts) via Old French.

2. ADV

/ prəˈpɔr.ʃə.nət.li / (AmE), / prəˈpɔː.ʃən.ət.li / (BrE)
in a way that corresponds in size, amount, or degree to something else; in proportion.
in a way that is proportional

Examples

Your rent will increase proportionately if you decide to rent a larger office.
The bonus was distributed proportionately among the staff based on their performance.
If you double the number of hours worked, your pay will rise proportionately.

Synset Examples

your salary will rise proportionately to your workload

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proportionally - in a way that is proportional

Etymology

From 'proportionate' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'proportio'.

3. ADV

/ prəˈpɔrʃənətli / (AmE), / prəˈpɔːʃənətli / (BrE)
In a way that corresponds in size, amount, or degree to something else; in proportion.
in proportion to something else

Examples

The employees' bonuses were distributed proportionately based on their performance and contribution to the project.
When the recipe was doubled, the ingredients were increased proportionately to maintain the flavor.
If attendance declines, the budget for the event will be reduced proportionately to reflect the lower demand.

Synset Examples

the height of this wall must be reduced proportionately to give the room pleasant dimensions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proportionally - in a way that relates to proportion; synonym often interchangeable with 'proportionately'

Etymology

From 'proportion' + '-ately', modeled after the pattern of other adverbs such as 'fortunately'.

Source: WordNet
