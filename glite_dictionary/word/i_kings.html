I Kings

1. PROPN

/ aɪ kɪŋz / (AmE), / aɪ kɪŋz / (BrE)
the first book of Kings in the Old Testament, recounting the histories of the kings of Judah and Israel
first book of Kings in the Old Testament

Examples

Many biblical scholars have devoted years to studying I Kings in detail.
The story of Solomon's wisdom is found in I Kings.
She referenced I Kings during her lecture on ancient Israelite history.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: II Kings - the second book of Kings in the Old Testament

Etymology

Named for its position as the first of the two Books of Kings in the Latin and English traditions; 'I' is the Roman numeral for 1.
