secondhand

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛkəndˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈsɛkəndˌhænd / (BrE)
(of information or knowledge) not obtained directly from the original source; heard or learned from someone else.
not from the original source

Examples

She only had a secondhand account of the event, so some details might be missing.
Much of what we know about ancient times is secondhand information.
The news he shared was clearly secondhand and not very reliable.

Synset Examples

a secondhand report
a secondhand account of a memory of something once read
most of our knowledge is secondhand

Related Terms

  • Antonym: firsthand - (of information) coming directly from the original source

Etymology

From 'second' + 'hand', originally meaning 'second in possession'.

2. ADV

/ ˌsɛkəndˈhænd / (AmE), / ˌsɛkəndˈhænd / (BrE)
(of information) by indirect means, not from the original source; through an intermediary.
indirectly, not from original source

Examples

I only know about the incident secondhand, through friends who were at the event.
She heard the news secondhand rather than from the person involved.
Most of my information came secondhand from colleagues who read the report.

Synset Examples

I heard about it only secondhand or thirdhand
he prefers to buy secondhand

Related Terms

  • Antonym: directly - in a direct way, from the original source

Etymology

From 'second hand', originally referring to objects acquired from a previous owner, later extended to information received from others.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00059361-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADJ

/ ˌsɛkəndˈhænd / (AmE), / ˌsɛkəndˈhænd / (BrE)
(of goods) previously owned or used by someone else.
previously owned; not new

Examples

She bought a secondhand bicycle to save money.
The store specializes in selling secondhand books and vintage clothing.
I prefer to buy secondhand furniture because it's more affordable and environmentally friendly.

Synset Examples

bought a secondhand (or used) car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: used - previously owned; not new
  • Antonym: new - not previously owned; fresh from the manufacturer

Etymology

From 'second' (next after the first) + 'hand' (in possession), referring to goods that have passed to a new owner.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01644709-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˌsɛkəndˈhænd / (AmE), / ˌsɛkəndˈhænd / (BrE)
(of goods) previously owned or used by someone else.
pre-owned or used item

Examples

She prefers secondhand clothes because they're more affordable and unique.
We bought a secondhand sofa from the charity shop downtown.
The bookstore specializes in rare and valuable secondhand books.

Synset Examples

He bought a second-hander car to save money.
Her secondhand book collection is impressive and diverse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: used - having been previously owned
  • Antonym: new - not previously owned or used

Etymology

From 'second' (order) + 'hand' (possessor), meaning 'not directly from the original owner.'
