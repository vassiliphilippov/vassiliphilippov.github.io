Petunia integrifolia

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtuniə ˌɪntəɡrɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / pɪˈtjuːniə ˌɪntɪɡrɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A herb or small shrub with solitary violet to rose-red flowers, native to South America.
species of flowering herb with violet to rose-red blooms

Examples

The vibrant Petunia integrifolia filled her garden with deep violet blossoms.
You can grow Petunia integrifolia from seed in well-drained soil.
Botanists often study Petunia integrifolia for its striking single flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petunia - genus of flowering plants with colorful funnel-shaped flowers

Etymology

Genus name from French 'pétunia', from obsolete Tupi 'petyn', related to tobacco; 'integrifolia' from Latin, meaning 'with entire leaves'.
