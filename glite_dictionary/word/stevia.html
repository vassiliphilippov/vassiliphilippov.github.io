stevia

1. NOUN

/ ˈstiːviə / (AmE), / ˈstiːviə / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Stevia, native to Central and South America, known for its sweet-tasting leaves often used as a sugar substitute
a South American sweet-leafed plant

Examples

Many people use stevia as a natural sweetener in their tea and coffee.
The leaves of the stevia plant are incredibly sweet despite having no calories.
Some gardeners grow stevia in their backyard to harvest its leaves for homemade extracts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetleaf - another name for the Stevia plant, especially Stevia rebaudiana

Etymology

From New Latin Stevia, named after Spanish botanist Petrus Jacobus Stevus (Pedro Jaime Esteve).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstiːviə / (AmE), / ˈstiːviə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Stevia, native to South America, whose leaves are used as a natural sweetener; also, the sweetener obtained from its leaves.
South American plant used as a sweetener

Examples

Many people use stevia as a sugar substitute in their coffee and tea.
The leaves of the stevia plant are naturally sweet and calorie-free.
Farmers in Paraguay have been cultivating stevia for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetleaf - another common name for Stevia rebaudiana, used as a sweetener

Etymology

From New Latin Stevia, named after Spanish botanist Pedro Jaime Esteve (1500–1556).
