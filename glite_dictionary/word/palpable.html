palpable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpæl.pə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈpal.pə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a feeling or atmosphere) so intense as to seem almost tangible or able to be felt.
so intense as to seem tangible

Examples

After the announcement, the excitement in the room was palpable and everyone started cheering.
A palpable tension filled the courtroom as the verdict was about to be read.
Her relief was palpable when she saw her missing cat at the door.

Synset Examples

there was a palpable sense of joy in the air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangible - able to be touched; perceptible by touch

Etymology

From Late Latin palpābilis, from Latin palpāre (to touch).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpæl.pə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈpal.pə.bəl / (BrE)
(medicine) Able to be felt or detected by touch, especially during a medical examination.
able to be felt by touch (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor found a palpable mass during the examination.
There were no palpable lymph nodes detected in her neck.
A palpable pulse was present in both wrists.

Synset Examples

a palpable tumor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impalpable - unable to be felt by touch
  • Synonym: tangible - perceptible by touch; clear and definite

Etymology

From Late Latin palpabilis, from Latin palpare (to touch gently).
