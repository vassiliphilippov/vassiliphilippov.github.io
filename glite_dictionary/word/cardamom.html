cardamom

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrdəməm / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdəməm / (BrE)
An aromatic spice made from the seeds of several plants of the genus Elettaria or Amomum, used as a flavoring in cooking and baking.
aromatic spice from plant seeds

Examples

She added a pinch of cardamom to the cake batter to enhance its flavor.
In Indian cuisine, cardamom is often used in both sweet and savory dishes.
The aroma of cardamom filled the kitchen as the tea brewed on the stove.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinnamon - aromatic spice from the bark of certain trees
  • Synonym: clove - aromatic spice from dried flower buds

Etymology

From Greek 'kardamōmon', from 'kardamon' (cress) and 'amomon' (an Indian spice).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrdəməm / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdəməm / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to India, whose aromatic seeds are used as a spice in cooking and baking.
aromatic spice plant; its seeds used in cooking

Examples

The aroma of cardamom filled the kitchen as she prepared the dessert.
Traditional chai tea often contains spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves.
He bought a jar of ground cardamom for his curry recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardamon - alternative spelling of cardamom

Etymology

From Latin 'cardamomum', from Ancient Greek 'kardamōmon', referring to the plant.
