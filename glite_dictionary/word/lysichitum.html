Lysichitum

1. PROPN

/ laɪˈsɪkɪtəm / (AmE), / lɪˈsɪkɪtəm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial plants in the arum family (Araceae), commonly known as skunk cabbage, native to North America and eastern Asia.
genus of skunk cabbage plants

Examples

The wetlands are home to Lysichitum, known for its large, bright yellow spathes and strong odor.
Botanists discovered a flourishing patch of Lysichitum along the riverbank.
Many hikers recognize Lysichitum immediately by its distinctive appearance and scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk cabbage - common name for plants of the genus Lysichitum

Etymology

From Greek lysi- (loosening, releasing) + -chiton (tunic), referring to features of the plant's spathe.

A small genus of perennial aquatic plants in the arum family, known as skunk cabbages, native to North America and eastern Asia.
genus of aquatic plants (skunk cabbage)

Examples

The marsh was filled with the distinctive yellow flowers of Lysichitum.
