shmaltz

1. NOUN

/ ʃmɑlts / (AmE), / ʃmɒlts / (BrE)
excessive sentimentality, especially in art or music
excessive sentimentality (esp. in music/art)

Examples

The movie was just pure shmaltz, with every scene designed to tug at your heartstrings.
She couldn't stand the shmaltz in that romantic ballad; it felt overdone.
Some critics dismissed the musical as cheap shmaltz, but audiences loved it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentimentality - excessive expression of feelings of tenderness, sadness, or nostalgia

Etymology

From Yiddish *shmalts* (rendered chicken fat), figuratively for sentimentality.
