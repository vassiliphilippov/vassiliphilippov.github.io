carcass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.kəs / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.kəs / (BrE)
the dead body of an animal, especially one that has been slaughtered and dressed for food
dead body of an animal, dressed for food

Examples

The butcher hung the carcass on a hook before cutting it into steaks.
After the hunt, they brought the deer carcass back to camp to prepare for cooking.
Birds quickly picked at the remains of the sheep's carcass left in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corpse - the dead body of a human being

Etymology

From Old French 'carcois', from Provençal 'carcassa', probably of pre-Latin origin.
