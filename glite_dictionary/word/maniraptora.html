Maniraptora

1. NOUN

/ ˌmænəˌræpˈtɔrə / (AmE), / ˌmænɪˈræptərə / (BrE)
a clade of advanced theropod dinosaurs that includes oviraptorids, dromaeosaurs, and possibly modern birds
group of advanced theropod dinosaurs

Examples

The discovery of a new Maniraptora fossil shed light on the evolution of bird-like features.
Some paleontologists believe that birds evolved from within the Maniraptora clade.
Museum exhibits often highlight Maniraptora as key ancestors of modern birds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: theropod - member of a large group of bipedal saurischian dinosaurs
  • Synonym: oviraptorid - member of a family of bird-like theropod dinosaurs
  • Synonym: dromaeosaur - member of the family Dromaeosauridae, predatory feathered dinosaurs

Etymology

From Latin 'manus' (hand) + 'raptor' (seizer, plunderer), reflecting their distinctive grasping hands.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmænɪˈræptərə / (AmE), / ˌmanɪˈraptərə / (BrE)
a suborder of advanced theropod dinosaurs that includes oviraptorids, dromaeosaurs, and possibly modern birds; notable for having bird-like features
dinosaur suborder including birds and close relatives

Examples

Fossil evidence suggests that Maniraptora developed features similar to those of modern birds.
The discovery of feathers in some Maniraptora supports the theory that birds evolved from this group.
Paleontologists debate whether all Maniraptora are directly ancestral to modern birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theropod - a subgroup of dinosaurs that includes carnivorous species walking on two legs
  • Degree Higher: bird - modern feathered vertebrates, descended from theropod dinosaurs

Etymology

From Latin 'manus' (hand) + 'raptor' (plunderer), referring to grasping hands.
