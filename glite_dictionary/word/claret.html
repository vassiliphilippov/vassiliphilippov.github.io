claret

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɛrət / (AmE), / ˈklærət / (BrE)
a dark purplish-red colour, resembling the shade of red wine called claret
dark purplish-red colour

Examples

The living room walls were painted a rich shade of claret that created a warm atmosphere.
She wore a claret dress to the party, which perfectly matched her lipstick.
The artist mixed blue and red to achieve the exact claret hue for the painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burgundy - a dark red color, named after Burgundy wine

Etymology

From French 'clairet', diminutive of 'clair' (clear), originally referring to light red wine.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɛrət / (AmE), / ˈklærət / (BrE)
a dry red wine from Bordeaux or similar in style, often referred to in Britain as 'claret'
dry red Bordeaux-style wine
british

Examples

He ordered a bottle of claret to go with the roast beef.
The cellar was stocked with fine French claret collected over the years.
She prefers claret to other red wines for its rich, smooth flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bordeaux - wine region in France; also wine from the region
  • Degree Lower: burgundy - a type of red wine from Burgundy, France

Etymology

From French 'clairet', meaning 'pale', originally referring to a light red wine from Bordeaux.
