cosine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.saɪn / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.saɪn / (BrE)
The trigonometric function that gives the ratio of the length of the side adjacent to a given angle to the length of the hypotenuse in a right-angled triangle.
trigonometric ratio (adjacent/hypotenuse)

Examples

The cosine of 60 degrees is equal to 0.5.
You can use the cosine rule to find the length of the missing side.
In trigonometry, students often calculate the cosine of various angles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sine - trigonometric ratio of opposite side to hypotenuse
  • Synonym: tangent - trigonometric ratio of opposite side to adjacent side

Etymology

From New Latin 'cosinus', from co- (from 'complementi', of the complement) + 'sinus' (sine).
