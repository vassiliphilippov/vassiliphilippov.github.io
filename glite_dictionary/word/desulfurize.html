to desulfurize

1. VERB

/ diˈsʌlfjəˌraɪz / (AmE), / diˈsʌlfjəraɪz / (BrE)
to remove sulfur or sulfur compounds from a substance, especially during industrial or chemical processes
remove sulfur from (a substance)

Examples

Refineries must desulfurize crude oil to produce environmentally friendly fuels.
The company invested in new technology to desulfurize its emissions.
After the treatment, the team successfully desulfurized the industrial waste gases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remove - to take something away from a place or substance
  • Antonym: sulfurize - to treat or combine with sulfur

Etymology

From de- (removal of) + sulfur + -ize (verb-forming suffix).
