marginalia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑrdʒɪˈneɪliə / (AmE), / ˌmɑːdʒɪˈneɪlɪə / (BrE)
Notes, comments, or other marks written in the margins of a book or manuscript.
notes in the margins of books

Examples

The professor's copy of the novel is full of marginalia that provide fascinating insights into his thinking.
I enjoy reading the marginalia left by previous students in this textbook.
She spent hours adding marginalia to her favorite poetry collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annotation - a note added to a text for explanation or comment

Etymology

From Latin 'marginalia', plural of 'marginalis' (of the margin).
