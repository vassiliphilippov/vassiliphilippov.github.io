wangler

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæŋɡlər / (AmE), / ˈwæŋɡlə / (BrE)
a person who uses clever, often dishonest or misleading methods to achieve an aim
a crafty or deceitful person

Examples

No one trusted the wangler who always found a way to twist the truth.
The scheme was exposed, and the wangler finally faced the consequences of his actions.
She proved herself no wangler, always handling matters honestly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceiver - a person who misleads or tricks others
  • Synonym: schemer - a person who forms secret or underhanded plans

Etymology

From English 'wangle' (to achieve by contrivance or trickery), possibly from dialectal 'wankle' (unsteady, fickle).
