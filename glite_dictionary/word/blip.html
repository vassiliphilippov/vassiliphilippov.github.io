blip

1. NOUN

/ blɪp / (AmE), / blɪp / (BrE)
a sudden or brief interruption or minor deviation, often of little significance, in a process or activity
a brief interruption or minor deviation

Examples

There was a blip in the company's earnings last quarter, but overall growth remained steady.
Most experts agree that this is just a temporary blip and not a long-term trend.
Her concentration suffered a brief blip before she regained focus.

Synset Examples

the market had one bad blip today
you can't react to the day-to-day blips
renewed jitters in the wake of a blip in retail sales

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hiccup - a minor, temporary difficulty or interruption
  • Synonym: spike - a sudden sharp increase or fluctuation, usually temporary

2. NOUN

/ blɪp / (AmE), / blɪp / (BrE)
a small spot of light or mark that appears on a radar screen, representing the position of a detected object
mark on radar showing detected object

Examples

The pilot saw a mysterious blip appear on his radar screen.
Every ship appeared as a separate blip on the radar.
When the weather changed, more blips began showing up on the monitor.
