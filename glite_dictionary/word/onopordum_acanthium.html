Onopordum acanthium

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊnəˈpɔrdəm əˈkænθiəm / (AmE), / ˌɒnəˈpɔːdəm əˈkanθɪəm / (BrE)
A tall biennial thistle native to Eurasia with white-hairy stems and leaves, and pale purple flowers; commonly called Scotch thistle and naturalized in North America.
a Eurasian thistle with pale purple flowers (Scotch thistle)

Examples

Onopordum acanthium often grows over two meters tall in favorable conditions.
Gardeners sometimes plant Onopordum acanthium for its striking appearance, despite its prickly leaves.
The fields were dotted with the pale purple blooms of Onopordum acanthium in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scotch thistle - common name for Onopordum acanthium

Etymology

From Latin genus Onopordum, from Greek onopordon (burdock, a kind of thistle), and acanthium from Greek akanthos (thorn).
