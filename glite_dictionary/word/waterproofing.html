waterproofing

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚˌpruːfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌpruːfɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making something impervious to water by applying a special treatment or material
the process of making something waterproof

Examples

Proper waterproofing is essential for roofs in rainy climates.
The contractor applied waterproofing to the basement walls to prevent flooding.
Modern jackets often use advanced waterproofing technologies to keep you dry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sealant - a material used to block the passage of fluids
  • Antonym: leakage - the act or process of liquid escaping

Etymology

Formed from 'waterproof' + '-ing', indicating the act or process.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚˌpruːfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌpruːfɪŋ / (BrE)
a substance or coating applied to a surface to make it resistant to water
coating that makes surfaces waterproof

Examples

The basement walls received a new layer of waterproofing to prevent leaks.
Proper waterproofing is essential for protecting roofs from water damage.
They applied waterproofing to the deck to extend its lifespan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sealant - a substance used to block the passage of fluids through surfaces or joints or openings

Etymology

From 'waterproof' + '-ing', formed by adding the noun-forming suffix to the adjective.

Synset POS: n
