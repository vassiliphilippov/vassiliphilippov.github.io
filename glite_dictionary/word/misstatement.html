misstatement

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsˈsteɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈsteɪtmənt / (BrE)
A statement that is incorrect or contains a mistake, especially one that is misleading or untrue.
incorrect or mistaken statement

Examples

The accountant was fired after making a significant misstatement in the company's financial report.
Any misstatement of the facts could result in serious legal consequences.
During the interview, he corrected his earlier misstatement about the timeline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falsehood - a false statement; a lie
  • Antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'statement', formed in English.
