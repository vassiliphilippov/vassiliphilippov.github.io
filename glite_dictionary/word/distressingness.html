distressingness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrɛsɪŋnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɛsɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of causing mental or emotional pain or anxiety
quality of causing distress or pain

Examples

The distressingness of the situation made it hard for anyone to remain calm.
Many found the distressingness of the news too much to bear.
She tried to focus on positive outcomes to distract herself from the distressingness of her memories.

Synset Examples

she feared the painfulness of childbirth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: painfulness - the quality of causing physical or emotional pain

Etymology

From 'distressing' + '-ness', where '-ness' forms nouns denoting a state or quality.
