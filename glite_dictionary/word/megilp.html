megilp

1. NOUN

/ məˈɡɪlp / (AmE), / məˈɡɪlp / (BrE)
A medium used in oil painting, made by mixing linseed oil with mastic varnish or turpentine, to give a smooth, jelly-like consistency.
oil painting medium (linseed oil + varnish/turpentine)

Examples

The artist prepared his colors by mixing them with megilp to achieve the desired texture.
Without megilp, the paint felt too thick and was harder to spread on the canvas.
She learned to make her own megilp, experimenting with the proportions of linseed oil and varnish.

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly alteration of earlier 'magilp'; first recorded in the 18th century in artistic contexts.
