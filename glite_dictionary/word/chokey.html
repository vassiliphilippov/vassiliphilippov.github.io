chokey

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃoʊki / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊki / (BrE)
(British, slang, dated) A prison or jail.
prison (British, slang, dated)
slang british

Examples

He spent a year in chokey for theft before turning his life around.
The brothers ended up in chokey after the failed burglary.
No one wants to find themselves stuck in chokey over a silly mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prison - a building where people are kept as a punishment for a crime or while they are waiting for trial
  • Synonym: jail - a place for the confinement of people accused or convicted of a crime

Etymology

Probably from English dialect "choke" (to suffocate), originally referring to a lock-up with poor ventilation.
