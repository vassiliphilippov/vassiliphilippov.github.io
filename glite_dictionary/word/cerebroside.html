cerebroside

1. NOUN

/ səˈriː.brə.saɪd / (AmE), / səˈriː.brə.saɪd / (BrE)
A glycolipid compound containing glucose or galactose, found in the myelin sheath of nerve cells in the brain and spinal cord.
glycolipid found in nerve cell membranes
medical

Examples

Scientists have identified a new type of cerebroside in the human brain.
Myelin contains large amounts of cerebroside, which helps insulate nerve fibers.
Certain genetic disorders can affect the breakdown of cerebrosides in the body.

Synset Examples

A cerebroside is a glycolipid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glycolipid - a lipid with one or more carbohydrate groups attached

Etymology

From Latin 'cerebrum' (brain) + '-side' (as in 'glycoside').
