Prunus mexicana

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs mɛkˈsɪkənə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs mɛkˈsɪkənə / (BrE)
a small tree native to the southwestern United States, valued for its purplish-red fruit and large leaves, sometimes grown as an ornamental
tree with purplish-red fruit, southwestern US

Examples

The Prunus mexicana produces attractive purplish-red fruit in late summer.
Gardeners plant Prunus mexicana for both its edible fruit and ornamental value.
The large leaves of Prunus mexicana provide a striking appearance in landscapes.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Prunus' is the genus name for plums, and 'mexicana' refers to its association with Mexico.
