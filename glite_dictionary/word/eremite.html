eremite

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrəˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛrɪmaɪt / (BrE)
a Christian recluse, especially one who lives in solitude for religious reasons; a hermit
Christian recluse or hermit

Examples

The eremite spent decades living alone in the wilderness, devoted to prayer and reflection.
Many medieval stories feature an eremite offering counsel to lost travelers.
Inspired by ancient eremites, he chose a life of solitude and contemplation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermit - a person living in solitude as a religious discipline

Etymology

From Late Latin eremita (hermit), from Greek erēmitēs (of the desert).
