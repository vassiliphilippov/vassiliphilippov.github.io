immorally

1. ADV

/ ɪˈmɔrəli / (AmE), / ɪˈmɒrəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of concern for what is right or wrong according to moral principles.
in an immoral way; without moral regard

Examples

He was accused of acting immorally during the negotiations.
Some people believe that the company behaved immorally by hiding the truth from customers.
It is wrong to pursue your goals immorally, even if you succeed.

Synset Examples

he acted immorally when his own interests were at stake

Related Terms

  • Antonym: morally - in a way that conforms to moral principles

Etymology

From 'immoral' + '-ly'; 'immoral' from Latin 'immoralis' (not moral).
