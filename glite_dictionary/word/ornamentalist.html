ornamentalist

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrnəˈmɛn.t̬ə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˌɔː.nəˈmen.tə.lɪst / (BrE)
a person who specializes in designing, creating, or applying decorative elements, especially in art, architecture, or crafts
person who decorates or adds ornamentation

Examples

The renowned ornamentalist was commissioned to design the intricate carvings for the palace doors.
As an ornamentalist, she took great pride in adding beautiful flourishes to even the simplest objects.
In nineteenth-century Europe, the work of the ornamentalist was highly sought after by wealthy patrons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decorator - a person whose job is to decorate spaces or objects
  • Antonym: minimalist - a person who favors minimal style in design/art

Etymology

From 'ornament' (Latin ornamentum, meaning equipment or decoration) + '-alist', a suffix denoting specialization.
