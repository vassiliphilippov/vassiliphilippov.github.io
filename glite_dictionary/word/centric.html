centric

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛn.trɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.trɪk / (BrE)
Situated at or near a center; having a center.
having or situated at a center

Examples

The galaxy is centric in its structure, with stars revolving around a common point.
Urban planners often design cities with a centric layout to promote accessibility.
The museum's centric location makes it easy for visitors from all parts of the city to reach.

Etymology

From Greek kentrikos (pertaining to a center), from kentron (center).
