glide

1. VERB

/ ɡlaɪd / (AmE), / ɡlaɪd / (BrE)
to cause (someone or something) to move smoothly, quietly, or with little apparent effort
move something smoothly or quietly

Examples

She gently glided the boat across the calm lake.
The waiter glided the dish onto the table without a sound.
He glided his hand over the smooth surface of the piano.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slide - to move smoothly along a surface while maintaining continuous contact

Etymology

From Middle English gliden, from Old English glīdan (to glide, slide).

2. NOUN

/ ɡlaɪd / (AmE), / ɡlaɪd / (BrE)
a speech sound that is articulated like a vowel but functions as a consonant, such as the sounds represented by 'w' or 'y' in English
phonetic term for a vowellike consonant sound

Examples

In the word 'yes,' the initial glide is the sound represented by 'y.'
Linguists classify 'w' and 'y' as glide sounds in English.
The glide in 'wait' connects the initial consonant to the vowel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semivowel - a sound that is phonetically similar to a vowel but functions as a consonant

/ ɡlaɪd / (AmE), / ɡlaɪd / (BrE)
