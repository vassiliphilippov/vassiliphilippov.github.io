redcap

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈrɛd.kæp / (BrE)
a member of the Royal Military Police in Britain, so called from the red cap worn as part of the uniform
British military police member
british

Examples

The redcap checked our identification at the gate.
Several redcaps were patrolling the barracks after curfew.
As a redcap, she was responsible for maintaining discipline among the troops.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌkap / (BrE)
A porter at a railway station who assists passengers with their baggage, especially in the United States.
porter at a railway station

Examples

A redcap quickly came over to help us with our heavy suitcases at the station.
We tipped the redcap after he loaded our luggage onto the train.
If you need assistance, just look for a redcap near the station entrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porter - a person employed to carry luggage, especially at a railway station or hotel

Etymology

Refers to the red caps traditionally worn by these porters for easy identification.
