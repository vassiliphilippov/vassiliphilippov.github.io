numbfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌmˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈnʌmˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a sluggish, bottom-dwelling ray of the order Torpediniformes, with a rounded body and electric organs capable of emitting strong electric discharges
a type of electric ray

Examples

The diver carefully avoided the numbfish lying on the ocean floor.
Scientists studied the electric organs of the numbfish to understand how they generate shocks.
A numbfish can stun its prey using powerful electric discharges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electric ray - a ray capable of producing electric shocks

Etymology

From 'numb' (incapacitated feeling) + 'fish', referring to its ability to numb prey with electric shocks.
