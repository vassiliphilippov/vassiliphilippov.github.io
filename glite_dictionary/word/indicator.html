indicator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndɪˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɪndɪkeɪtə / (BrE)
a device or signal that is used to attract attention
signal for attracting attention

Examples

Remember to turn off your left indicator after making the turn.
The flashing indicator on the dashboard warned him to fasten his seatbelt.
She raised her hand as an indicator to attract the waiter’s attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal - a gesture, sound, or action that sends a message or warning

Etymology

From Latin *indicare* (to point out, show) via English 'indicate'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.dəˌkeɪ.t̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dɪ.keɪ.tə / (BrE)
(Chemistry) A substance used to show, usually by a color change, the presence, absence, or concentration of a chemical, or to test for a reaction.
chemical that shows presence of substance
medical

Examples

The solution turned pink when the indicator was added, signaling the end of the titration.
Phenolphthalein is a common indicator used in acid-base experiments.
Doctors may use a special chemical indicator to detect glucose in urine samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reagent - substance used to cause a chemical reaction to detect or measure other substances

Etymology

From Latin *indicātor* (one who points out).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪndɪˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɪndɪkeɪtə / (BrE)
a value, number, or ratio that reflects or measures changes in a situation over time, often based on observed data
value reflecting change over time

Examples

Gross Domestic Product is often used as an indicator of a country's economic health.
The unemployment rate serves as a key indicator of labor market trends.
Environmental quality indicators can help governments track pollution levels over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metric - a standard of measurement or evaluation
  • Synonym: index - a statistical value or indicator used for comparison

Etymology

From Latin 'indicatum', past participle of 'indicare' (to point out, indicate).

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.dɪˌkeɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dɪˌkeɪ.tə / (BrE)
a device that shows or signals the state or operating condition of a machine, system, or process
device that shows system condition

Examples

The temperature indicator on the dashboard alerted him that the engine was overheating.
A red light serves as an indicator when the system needs maintenance.
The factory installed a new indicator to monitor air pressure levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gauge - an instrument for measuring and displaying information, often in a machine
  • Synonym: meter - a device that measures and displays the amount, degree, or rate of something

Etymology

From Latin 'indicātor', from 'indicare' (to indicate).
