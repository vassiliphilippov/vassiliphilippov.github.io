sapphire

1. ADJ

/ ˈsæfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈsæfaɪə / (BrE)
Having a deep, brilliant blue color, like that of a blue sapphire.
deep brilliant blue in color

Examples

She wore a dress of sapphire velvet that caught the light beautifully.
His sapphire eyes sparkled with excitement as he spoke.
The evening sky glowed with a rich sapphire hue after sunset.

Synset Examples

sapphire eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azure - bright blue in color, like a clear sky
  • Degree Lower: navy - very dark blue in color
  • Degree Lower: cobalt - a shade of blue slightly lighter than sapphire

Etymology

From Old French 'saphir', via Latin 'sapphirus', from Greek 'sappheiros' (precious blue stone).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈsæfaɪə / (BrE)
a precious transparent gemstone, typically blue, made of corundum
precious blue gemstone

Examples

She wore a delicate necklace with a solitary sapphire pendant.
The deep blue sapphire sparkled in the afternoon light.
Many engagement rings feature either a diamond or a sapphire as the central stone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ruby - a red variety of corundum used as a gemstone
  • Antonym: emerald - a bright green precious stone consisting of a variety of beryl
  • Synonym: gemstone - a precious or semi-precious stone used in jewellery

Etymology

From Old French *saphir*, from Latin *sapphirus*, from Greek *sappheiros* (blue stone).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsæˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈsæ.faɪə / (BrE)
A transparent, typically blue precious gemstone cut from the mineral corundum and valued for its beauty and rarity.
precious blue gemstone

Examples

She wore a beautiful sapphire pendant around her neck for the wedding.
The crown was decorated with large, sparkling sapphires and diamonds.
This ring features a rare pink sapphire set in white gold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruby - a red precious stone from corundum

Etymology

From Old French 'saphir', from Latin 'sapphirus', from Greek 'sappheiros', likely ultimately from a Semitic source.
