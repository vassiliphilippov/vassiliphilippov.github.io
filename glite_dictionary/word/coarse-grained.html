coarse-grained

1. ADJ

/ kɔrsˈɡreɪnd / (AmE), / kɔːsˈɡreɪnd / (BrE)
having a rough texture or consisting of relatively large or visible grains or particles.
having a rough or large-grained texture

Examples

The countertop was made of coarse-grained granite that felt uneven to the touch.
Some artists prefer coarse-grained canvas for oil painting techniques.
The bread had a distinctive, coarse-grained texture because it contained whole grains.

Synset Examples

coarse-grained wood
large-grained sand

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fine-grained - having a smooth texture or consisting of small, fine particles

Etymology

Formed from "coarse" (rough, not fine) and "grained" (having grains or particles).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɔrsˈɡreɪnd / (AmE), / ˌkɔːsˈɡreɪnd / (BrE)
having a texture or structure composed of relatively large, distinct particles or grains.
having large or rough grains or particles

Examples

The sandstone in this region is mostly coarse-grained, making it easy to identify.
She prefers coarse-grained sugar for baking certain desserts.
Under the microscope, the rock appeared distinctly coarse-grained compared to others.

Synset Examples

granular sugar
the photographs were grainy and indistinct
it left a mealy residue

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fine-grained - having a texture composed of small, fine particles or grains

Etymology

Formed from 'coarse' (rough, large in texture) + 'grained' (having grains).

Source: WordNet
