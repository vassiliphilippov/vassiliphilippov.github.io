sadhu

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑdu / (AmE), / ˈsɑːdʊ / (BrE)
(in Hinduism) a holy man who has renounced worldly life to pursue spiritual goals, often living as an ascetic.
ascetic holy man in Hinduism

Examples

The sadhu sat in meditation by the riverbank, oblivious to the busy market nearby.
Many pilgrims seek blessings from a sadhu during their journey to the holy sites.
A group of sadhus walked barefoot through the streets, chanting prayers.

Etymology

From Sanskrit साधु (sādhu), meaning 'good man' or 'holy man'.
