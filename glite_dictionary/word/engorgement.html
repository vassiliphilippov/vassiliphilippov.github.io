engorgement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡɔrdʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡɔːdʒmənt / (BrE)
the act of consuming food in such large quantities that one becomes completely full or swollen
the state of being extremely full from eating

Examples

After hours without food, his engorgement at the feast surprised everyone.
The sudden engorgement left him feeling bloated and uncomfortable.
Her engorgement of chocolate was a result of intense cravings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satiation - the feeling of being fully satisfied after eating
  • Antonym: hunger - the feeling of needing or wanting food

Etymology

From French 'engorgement', from 'engorger' (to gorge), ultimately from Old French 'gorge' (throat).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈɡɔrdʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡɔːdʒmənt / (BrE)
the state of being abnormally filled or swollen with fluid, especially blood
swelling due to excess fluid or blood
medical

Examples

The engorgement of veins was apparent after the injury.
Breast engorgement can be painful for new mothers.
The doctor noted the engorgement of the patient's liver upon examination.

Synset Examples

engorgement of the breast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swelling - an abnormal enlargement, often due to fluid accumulation

Etymology

From French 'engorgement', from 'engorger' meaning 'to gorge' or 'to fill to excess'.
