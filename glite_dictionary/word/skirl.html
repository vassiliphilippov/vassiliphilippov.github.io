skirl

1. NOUN

/ skɝl / (AmE), / skɜːl / (BrE)
a shrill, wailing sound, especially that produced by a bagpipe
shrill sound of a bagpipe

Examples

The skirl of the pipes echoed through the highland valley.
Tourists gathered to listen to the haunting skirl coming from the square.
At dawn, the skirl of a lone piper marked the beginning of the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wail - a long, high-pitched cry or sound
  • Degree Lower: drone - a continuous low humming sound, as produced by bagpipes

Etymology

Scots origin, imitative of the sound itself.

2. VERB

/ skɜːrl / (AmE), / skɜːl / (BrE)
to play the bagpipes, especially in a loud or shrill manner
play the bagpipes

Examples

The piper began to skirl as the procession entered the hall.
As the sun rose, he could hear bagpipers skirling in the distance.
She skirled a mournful tune that echoed through the misty glen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipe - to play music on the bagpipes

Etymology

From Scots 'skirl', likely of Scandinavian origin, related to Norwegian 'skryla' (to scream).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01730639-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ skɜrl / (AmE), / skɜːl / (BrE)
(of bagpipes or a similar instrument, or of a person) to make a shrill, wailing sound.
emit a shrill, wailing sound

Examples

The bagpipes began to skirl across the moor, signaling the start of the parade.
Seagulls skirled overhead as the storm approached the shore.
The musicians gathered in the square, their instruments skirling into the night.

Synset Examples

skirling bagpipes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shriek - to make a loud, sharp, piercing cry
  • Synonym: wail - to make a prolonged, high-pitched sound of pain, grief, or lamentation

Etymology

From Scots, probably imitative of the sound produced by bagpipes.

Synset POS: v
