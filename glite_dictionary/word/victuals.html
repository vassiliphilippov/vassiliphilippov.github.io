victuals

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪtəlz / (AmE), / ˈvɪtəlz / (BrE)
food or provisions, especially as prepared for consumption
food; provisions
archaic

Examples

During the journey, we packed plenty of victuals to last us several days.
The villagers welcomed travelers with warm beds and hearty victuals.
Sailors stored their victuals in large barrels below deck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: food - any nutritious substance that people or animals eat or drink

Etymology

From Old French *vitaille*, from Latin *victualia* (provisions, food), plural of *victualis* (of nourishment). Spelling is influenced by Latin, pronunciation reflects earlier French and English forms.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪtəlz / (AmE), / ˈvɪtəlz / (BrE)
food; supplies of food needed for sustenance.
food or provisions

Examples

The campers packed enough victuals to last for a week in the wilderness.
At the feast, the table was laden with delicious victuals and drink.
During the long voyage, the sailors carefully rationed their remaining victuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provisions - supplies of food, especially for a journey
  • Synonym: food - any nutritious substance eaten or drunk to sustain life

Etymology

From Old French 'vitaille', from Latin 'victualia' (provisions, supplies).

3. NOUN

/ ˈvɪtəlz / (AmE), / ˈvɪtəlz / (BrE)
food supplies; provisions, especially as a stock or store
food supplies or provisions
archaic

Examples

The explorers packed enough victuals to last for several months at sea.
She prepared a basket of victuals for their journey across the countryside.
In times of war, townsfolk would stock up on victuals to withstand long sieges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provisions - food and other necessities stored for use
  • Synonym: supplies - general term for food or necessary items stored for later use

Etymology

From Old French *vitaille* (provisions), from Latin *victualia* (provisions, food).
