siskin

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪskɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɪskɪn / (BrE)
a small Eurasian finch, typically yellow and black in color, with a slender, pointed beak
small Eurasian yellow-and-black finch

Examples

A bright yellow siskin perched on the branch, searching for seeds among the leaves.
We spotted several siskins at the bird feeder this morning.
The siskin is known for its lively song and striking plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finch - a small seed-eating songbird with a short beak

Etymology

From German 'Zeisig', via earlier forms 'sisken', 'siskin'.
