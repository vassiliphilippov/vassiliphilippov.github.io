to rebury

1. VERB

/ riˈbɛri / (AmE), / riˈbɛːri / (BrE)
to bury something or someone again, especially after being exhumed or removed.
to bury again

Examples

The archaeologists decided to rebury the ancient relics to protect them from thieves.
After the flood damaged the cemetery, the families had to rebury their loved ones.
The city gave permission to rebury the remains in a more secure location.

Synset Examples

After the king's body had been exhumed and tested to traces of poison, it was reburied in the same spot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bury - to put a dead body in the ground

Etymology

re- (again) + bury; formed by prefixing 're-' to 'bury'.
