discriminatory

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɪməˌnɛːˌtɔri / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪmɪnətəri / (BrE)
showing unfair prejudice or making distinctions against people or groups based on characteristics such as race, gender, or age
showing prejudice or unfair bias

Examples

The company was sued for its discriminatory hiring practices.
Many laws have been passed to prevent discriminatory treatment in the workplace.
Such discriminatory policies are no longer acceptable in modern society.

Synset Examples

discriminatory attitudes and practices
invidious comparisons

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impartial - treating all people equally; not biased

Etymology

From discriminate + -ory, modeled on Latin-derived adjectives.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01000813-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɪməˌnɛˌtɔri / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪmɪnətəri / (BrE)
showing or involving prejudice or bias against people or groups based on preconceived opinions or attributes.
showing prejudice or bias

Examples

The company’s discriminatory hiring practices led to a lawsuit.
Laws are in place to prevent discriminatory treatment based on race or gender.
Critics argued that the policy was inherently discriminatory toward immigrant communities.

Synset Examples

a prejudiced judge

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impartial - treating all people and groups equally; not biased
  • Synonym: prejudiced - having or showing a dislike or distrust based on fixed ideas

Etymology

From discriminate + -ory, meaning 'tending to discriminate.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00286014-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɪməˌnɛtɔri / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪmɪnətəri / (BrE)
able to make or characterized by fine distinctions; showing careful differentiation between similar things or ideas.
capable of making fine distinctions

Examples

Her discriminatory taste in art allowed her to appreciate subtle nuances that others missed.
The test is designed to be highly discriminatory between similar cognitive skills.
He employed a discriminatory approach in separating fact from opinion in the report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discerning - having or showing good judgment; able to see and understand people, things, or situations clearly

Etymology

From discriminate + -ory, after Latin discriminare (to distinguish, to separate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00778860-s

4. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɪməˌnɛtɔri / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪmɪnətəri / (BrE)
showing or resulting from unfair treatment or partiality towards certain groups or individuals
showing unfair partiality or bias

Examples

The law was criticized as being discriminatory against minority groups.
Many companies have reformed discriminatory hiring practices in recent years.
Union leaders argued that the policy was discriminatory and should be overturned.

Synset Examples

a discriminatory tax
preferential tariff rates
preferential treatment
a preferential shop gives priority or advantage to union members in hiring or promoting

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impartial - treating all equally, without favor or prejudice
  • Synonym: biased - showing an unreasonable preference or prejudice

Etymology

From discriminate + -ory, patterned after Latin forms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00065504-s
Synset POS: s
