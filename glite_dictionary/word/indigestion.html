indigestion

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈdʒɛstʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈdʒɛstʃən / (BrE)
Discomfort or pain in the stomach associated with difficulty in digesting food, often accompanied by heartburn or nausea.
digestive discomfort or upset stomach

Examples

After eating too quickly, Emma complained of indigestion and felt uncomfortable all evening.
Spicy foods often give me indigestion, so I try to avoid them at dinner.
If you experience frequent indigestion, it may be a good idea to consult your doctor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dyspepsia - difficulty or pain in digesting food

Etymology

From Latin 'indigestio', from 'in-' (not) + 'digestio' (digestion).
