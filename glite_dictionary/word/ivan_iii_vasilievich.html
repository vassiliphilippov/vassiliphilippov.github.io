Ivan III Vasilievich

1. PROPN

/ aɪˈvɑn ði ˈθɜrd væˌsɪl.jəˈvɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈiːvən ðə ˈθɜːd ˌvæsɪliˈevɪtʃ / (BrE)
(1440–1505) Grand Duke of Muscovy, known for uniting Russian lands and reducing Tatar influence, laying the foundations for the Russian state.
Russian Grand Duke and state unifier (1440–1505)

Examples

Many historians credit Ivan III Vasilievich with centralizing power and expanding Muscovy's territory.
The reign of Ivan III Vasilievich marked the end of Mongol domination over Russia.
As Grand Duke, Ivan III Vasilievich initiated important reforms that shaped the Russian state.

Etymology

From Russian Иван III Васильевич (Ivan Tretyi Vasil'evich).
