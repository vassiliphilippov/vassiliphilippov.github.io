uncertainty

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈsɜrtənti / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɜːtənti / (BrE)
the state of being unsure, in doubt, or dependent on chance; lack of certainty or predictability
state of doubt or unpredictability

Examples

Market uncertainty has caused investors to hesitate before making decisions.
She spoke with an air of uncertainty about her future plans.
There is great uncertainty surrounding the outcome of the negotiations.

Synset Examples

the uncertainty of the outcome
the precariousness of his income

Related Terms

  • Antonym: certainty - the state of being sure or confident; lack of doubt
  • Synonym: doubt - a feeling of not being sure or confident

Etymology

From un- (not) + certainty; modeled after certain/certainty.

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈsɜrtənti / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɜːtənti / (BrE)
the state of being unsure or not confident about something; doubt or lack of certainty
state of being unsure or doubtful

Examples

The economic forecast is filled with uncertainty due to unpredictable market trends.
Her uncertainty about the decision was obvious from her hesitant tone.
Facing great uncertainty, he struggled to make a confident choice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: certainty - the state of being sure or confident about something
  • Synonym: doubt - uncertainty or lack of conviction

Etymology

From un- (not) + certainty, from Old French *certinité*, from Latin *certus* (sure, certain).

Synset POS: n
