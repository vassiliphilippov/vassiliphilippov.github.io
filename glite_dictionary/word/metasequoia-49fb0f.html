metasequoia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtə sɪˈkwɔɪə / (AmE), / ˌmiːtə sɪˈkwɔɪə / (BrE)
A large, fast-growing deciduous tree native to China, with flat bright-green leaves and small globular cones, often grown as an ornamental and once known only from fossils.
a large deciduous tree from China, often ornamental

Examples

The metasequoia in our garden loses all its leaves every autumn.
Paleobotanists were amazed when living specimens of metasequoia were discovered in China.
Many parks feature the striking silhouette of a metasequoia along their lakes.

Etymology

From New Latin; meta- (meaning 'after' or 'beyond') + Sequoia, reflecting its relation to the sequoia genus and its discovery after being known as a fossil.
