to wawl

1. VERB

/ wɔl / (AmE), / wɔːl / (BrE)
(of an animal or person) to make high-pitched, whining or howling noises, especially in a complaining or distressed manner.
make high-pitched, whining noises

Examples

The stray cat began to wawl outside my window in the middle of the night.
The baby wawled loudly when her toy was taken away.
As the ambulance siren approached, the dog couldn’t help but wawl along with it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wail - to make a long, loud, high-pitched cry of pain or sadness
  • Synonym: yowl - to make a loud wailing cry, typically in distress or pain
