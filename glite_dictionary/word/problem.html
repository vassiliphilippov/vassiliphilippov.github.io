problem

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːbləm / (AmE), / ˈprɒbləm / (BrE)
a situation, question, or condition that is difficult or requires a solution
difficulty needing a solution

Examples

The city is facing a major problem with traffic congestion during rush hour.
If you ever have a problem, don't hesitate to ask for help.
She had to deal with a complicated problem at work this week.

Synset Examples

she and her husband are having problems
it is always a job to contact him
urban problems such as traffic congestion and smog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difficulty - a thing that is hard to accomplish or deal with
  • Antonym: solution - an answer or means of solving a problem

Etymology

From Greek πρόβλημα (problema) ‘obstacle’, from προβάλλειν (proballēin) ‘to throw forward’.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑbləm / (AmE), / ˈprɒbləm / (BrE)
a question set for consideration or solution, especially in mathematics or as a test of knowledge
question for solution (e.g., in math, tests)

Examples

The teacher assigned three difficult problems for homework.
Each chapter ends with a set of problems to test your understanding.
She enjoys solving mathematical problems in her free time.

Synset Examples

our homework consisted of ten problems to solve

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exercise - a task set to develop skill or understanding

Etymology

From Greek 'problēma' (something thrown forward, question proposed) via Latin 'problema'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːbləm / (AmE), / ˈprɒbləm / (BrE)
A matter or situation regarded as unwelcome, harmful, or needing to be dealt with and overcome; a source of difficulty or trouble.
source of difficulty; obstacle

Examples

We encountered a major problem with the new software installation.
The noise outside is becoming a serious problem for the residents.
She always tries to find a problem with everything, no matter how perfect it is.

Synset Examples

one trouble after another delayed the job
what's the problem?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difficulty - something not easily accomplished or solved
  • Antonym: solution - an answer to a problem

Etymology

From Greek 'problema' (obstacle, question for discussion), from 'proballein' (to throw forward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05695143-n
Synset POS: n
