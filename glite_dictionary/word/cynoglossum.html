Cynoglossum

1. NOUN

/ saɪ.nəˈɡlɒs.əm / (AmE), / saɪ.nəˈɡlɒs.əm / (BrE)
A genus of tall, rough herbs in the borage family (Boraginaceae), commonly known as hound's-tongue.
genus of rough herbs; hound's-tongue

Examples

Several species of Cynoglossum can be found growing wild in European meadows.
The gardener identified the plant as Cynoglossum, or hound's-tongue, by its rough leaves.
The medicinal uses of Cynoglossum have been documented for centuries.

Etymology

From Greek 'kynoglōsson', meaning 'hound's tongue'.

2. NOUN

genus of rough herbs (Boraginaceae)

Examples

The Cynoglossum genus includes plants known for their coarse, hairy leaves.
Researchers identified a new species within the Cynoglossum genus in eastern Asia.
Cynoglossum officinale, or hound's tongue, is a common wild plant in some European regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'kynoglossos' meaning 'hound's tongue', referring to the shape of the leaves.
