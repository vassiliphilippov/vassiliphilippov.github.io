whey

1. NOUN

/ weɪ / (AmE), / weɪ / (BrE)
the thin, watery part of milk that remains after the milk has curdled and the curds have been removed
watery liquid from curdled milk

Examples

She added protein-rich whey to her morning smoothie.
The whey was drained off before the cheese could be shaped and aged.
Ancient recipes often call for both curds and whey as separate ingredients.

Synset Examples

Little Miss Muffet sat on a tuffet eating some curds and whey

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curd - thick, coagulated part of milk separated from whey

Etymology

From Old English 'hwæg', related to Old High German 'wegi' (whey).

2. NOUN

/ weɪ / (AmE), / weɪ / (BrE)
the watery part of milk that remains after the formation of curds in cheese-making
watery part of milk from cheese-making

Examples

After making cheese, the leftover whey can be used in baking.
Protein powders often contain whey as their main ingredient.
She fed the pigs with whey collected from the dairy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curd - the solid part separated from milk during cheese-making

Etymology

From Old English hwǣig, related to Dutch wei and German Molke (whey).
