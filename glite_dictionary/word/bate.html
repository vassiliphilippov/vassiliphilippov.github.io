to bate

1. VERB

/ beɪt / (AmE), / beɪt / (BrE)
(of a hide or skin) to soak in a special solution to soften it and remove chemicals used in previous treatments, especially in tanning.
soak hides in solution to soften or clean

Examples

Before further processing, the workers bate the animal skins in large wooden vats.
The tannery bated the hides overnight to ensure all residues were removed.
If you don't bate the leather properly, it may remain stiff and difficult to dye.

Synset Examples

3. VERB

/ beɪt / (AmE), / beɪt / (BrE)
(of a falcon) to beat the wings wildly or frantically, especially when restrained.
(of falcon) beat wings wildly

Examples

The falcon began to bate its wings as soon as it felt the leather jesses tighten.
When startled, a hawk may bate frantically, struggling to fly from the handler's glove.
During training, young birds will often bate before learning to settle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flutter - (of a bird) to flap or vibrate the wings quickly and lightly

Etymology

From Old French *batre* (to beat), from Latin *battuere* (to beat).
