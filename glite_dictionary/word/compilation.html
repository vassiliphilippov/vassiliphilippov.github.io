compilation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːm.pəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒm.pɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a collection of items, such as stories, songs, or data, gathered and organized into a single book, album, or file
collection of gathered material

Examples

The encyclopedia is a compilation of articles written by experts from various fields.
She released a compilation of her greatest hits to celebrate her career anniversary.
This report is a compilation of data collected over the last five years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthology - a published collection of literary works

Etymology

From Latin compilatio (a gathering together), from compilare (to compile).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːm.pəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒm.pɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of gathering and assembling information, documents, or items into a single collection, such as a book, file, or list
the act of gathering and assembling items

Examples

The annual report was a careful compilation of financial data from all departments.
She created a compilation of her favorite poems to give as a gift.
This music album is a compilation of the band's greatest hits over the past decade.

Synset Examples

the job of compiling the inventory took several hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collection - a group of items gathered together
  • Synonym: compiling - the act of collecting or putting together items

Etymology

From Latin compilatio (a gathering together), from compilare (to plunder, compile).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01016673-n
Synset POS: n
