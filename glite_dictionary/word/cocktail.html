cocktail

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːk.teɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.teɪl / (BrE)
an appetizer, typically a mixture of seafood, fruit, or vegetables, served as a first course at a meal, often with a piquant sauce
appetizer served as a first course

Examples

We started the meal with a delicious shrimp cocktail and a basket of bread.
The restaurant is famous for its seafood cocktail, served with a spicy sauce.
She ordered a fruit cocktail as a light first course before her main dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appetizer - small dish served before the main meal

Etymology

Extension from cocktail (mixed drink), referring to mixed ingredients served as a starter.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑk.teɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.teɪl / (BrE)
A drink made from a mixture of alcoholic liquids and other ingredients, typically served chilled in a small glass.
alcoholic mixed drink

Examples

He ordered a cocktail before dinner at the bar.
The menu offers a variety of cocktails, from classics to new creations.
She learned how to make a perfect mojito cocktail during the class.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mocktail - a non-alcoholic mixed drink
  • Degree Lower: highball - an alcoholic mixed drink with more mixer than spirit

Etymology

Originated in early 19th-century America; exact origin uncertain, but possibly from 'cock-tail', a term for a mixed drink.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07927917-n
Synset POS: n
