nitroglycerin

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪtroʊˈɡlɪsərɪn / (AmE), / ˌnaɪtrəʊˈɡlɪsərɪn / (BrE)
a heavy, yellow, oily liquid that is explosive and poisonous, used in making explosives and as a medicine to widen blood vessels
explosive compound; also used in medicine
medical

Examples

The doctor administered nitroglycerin to relieve the patient's severe chest pain.
Dynamite is made by absorbing nitroglycerin into an inert porous material.
Strict safety protocols are required when transporting nitroglycerin due to its highly explosive nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trinitroglycerin - alternative name for nitroglycerin

Etymology

From nitric + glycerin, named for its composition as a nitrate ester of glycerin.
