Astreus

1. NOUN

/ əˈstriːəs / (AmE), / əˈstriːəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Geastraceae, known for species often called 'false earthstars.'
genus of fungi in Geastraceae

Examples

The unique fruiting bodies of Astreus species resemble earthstars but are not closely related.
Researchers discovered a new species in the genus Astreus in Southeast Asia.
Mycologists often study Astreus to understand fungal evolution in the Geastraceae family.

Etymology

From Greek 'astreus', meaning 'starry', in reference to the appearance of the fungi.

2. NOUN

/ əˈstriː.əs / (AmE), / əˈstriː.əs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Geastraceae, known for their earthstar-shaped fruiting bodies.
a genus of earthstar fungi

Examples

Several species within the genus Astreus are characterized by their distinctive star-shaped fruiting bodies.
Mycologists recently discovered a new member of Astreus in the forests of Southeast Asia.
The earthstar fungi of the genus Astreus are often found in sandy soils.

Etymology

From Greek 'astron' meaning 'star', referring to the star-like appearance of the fungi.
