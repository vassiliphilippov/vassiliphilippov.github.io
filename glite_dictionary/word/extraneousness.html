extraneousness

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˈstreɪniəsnɪs / (AmE), / ɛkˈstreɪniəsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being irrelevant or unrelated to the subject under consideration.
the state of being irrelevant

Examples

The judge dismissed the argument due to its extraneousness to the case.
We must avoid the extraneousness of unrelated details in our report.
Her comments added nothing but extraneousness to the focused discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrelevance - the quality of not being relevant
  • Antonym: relevance - the quality of being closely connected to the matter at hand

Etymology

From Latin 'extraneus' (foreign, external) + English '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstreɪniəsnəs / (AmE), / ɪkˈstreɪnɪəsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being irrelevant or not essential to a subject or situation.
state of being inessential or irrelevant

Examples

The extraneousness of his remarks became clear as the meeting progressed.
Critics have noted the extraneousness of the subplot to the overall narrative.
She was frustrated by the extraneousness of much of the information presented in the report.

Synset Examples

A listening to Ginsberg's own steady rendition of this poem on The Beat Generation audio recordings points out the extraneousness of the theatrical trappings, which go so far as to drown out the words of the poem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrelevance - the quality of being not connected with the matter at hand

Etymology

Formed from "extraneous" (from Latin extraneus, meaning 'from outside') + suffix "-ness".
