musculus sphincter pylori

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskjələs sfɪŋkˈtər paɪˈlɔːraɪ / (AmE), / ˈmʌskjələs sfɪŋkˈtə paɪˈlɔːraɪ / (BrE)
the ring-shaped sphincter muscle at the pylorus that separates the stomach from the duodenum and controls the passage of food into the small intestine
muscle at the pylorus, stomach-duodenum sphincter
medical

Examples

The musculus sphincter pylori regulates the flow of partially digested food from the stomach to the duodenum.
In cases of pyloric stenosis, the musculus sphincter pylori becomes abnormally thickened.
Proper function of the musculus sphincter pylori is essential for healthy digestion and nutrient absorption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyloric sphincter - ring of muscle at the end of the stomach controlling passage to the duodenum
  • Synonym: sphincter - a ring of muscle that closes a natural orifice

Etymology

From Latin musculus (muscle), sphincter (binder), pylori, genitive of pylorus (gatekeeper of the stomach).
