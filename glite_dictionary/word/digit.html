digit

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
a finger or toe in humans, or a similar structure in other vertebrates
a finger or toe

Examples

The lizard lost a digit on its front foot during the fight.
Some amphibians have four digits on their front limbs and five on their hind limbs.
The injury resulted in the loss of sensation in his left big digit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appendage - a projecting part of an organism, such as a limb or tail

Etymology

From Latin digitus (finger, toe).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
any of the numerals from 0 to 9, used to represent numbers in a system of numeration
a numeral from 0 to 9

Examples

You need a four-digit PIN to access your account.
The number 478 has three digits.
Binary numbers use only the digits 0 and 1.

Synset Examples

0 and 1 are digits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: numeral - a symbol or group of symbols that represents a number

Etymology

From Latin digitus (finger, toe); early use referred to counting on fingers.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
an old unit of linear measurement based on the width or length of a human finger, typically about 3/4 of an inch (2 centimeters)
unit of measure based on a finger's breadth
archaic

Examples

In ancient Egypt, builders measured lengths in digits, each representing the breadth of a finger.
The statue's width was four digits, according to the old inscription.
A digit was once used to measure small objects before standard units became common.

Etymology

From Latin 'digitus' meaning 'finger' or 'toe'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13675079-n
Synset POS: n
