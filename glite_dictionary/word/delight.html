delight

1. VERB

/ dɪˈlaɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈlaɪt / (BrE)
to give great pleasure or satisfaction to; to please greatly
to give great pleasure

Examples

The beautiful music delighted everyone at the concert.
Her thoughtful gift truly delighted me.
The magician delights children with his amazing tricks.

Synset Examples

These colors please the senses
a pleasing sensation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: please - to make someone feel happy or satisfied
  • Antonym: to displease - to make someone feel unhappy or annoyed

Etymology

From Old French 'deliter' or 'deleitier', from Latin 'delectare' (to charm, please, delight).

Examples

The spectacular fireworks show delighted everyone, making the evening unforgettable.
