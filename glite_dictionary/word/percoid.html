percoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜr.kɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.kɔɪd / (BrE)
A fish belonging to the order Perciformes, characterized by spiny fins; includes many common freshwater and marine species.
member of the order Perciformes (spiny-finned fish)

Examples

The percoid fishes are found in both freshwater and marine environments across the globe.
Several percoid species are popular among aquarium enthusiasts for their bright colors.
Researchers discovered a new percoid with unique spiny fins off the coast of Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perciform - relating to or members of the order Perciformes

Etymology

From Modern Latin Percidae (the perch family), derived from Latin perca (perch).
