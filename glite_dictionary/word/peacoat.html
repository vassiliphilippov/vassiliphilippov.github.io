peacoat

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈpiːkəʊt / (BrE)
a heavy woolen double-breasted jacket, originally worn by sailors
heavy double-breasted sailor's jacket

Examples

He buttoned his peacoat tightly to protect himself from the cold wind.
The navy issued each new recruit a standard wool peacoat.
She found a vintage peacoat at the thrift store and wore it all winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcoat - a long coat worn over other clothes for warmth

Etymology

From Dutch 'pijjekker' (jacket made of coarse woolen cloth), influenced by 'pea' (type of coarse woolen cloth).
