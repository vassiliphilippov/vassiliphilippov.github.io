advocacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈædvəˌkeɪsi / (AmE), / ˈædvəkəsi / (BrE)
the public support of an idea, plan, or way of doing something, especially by speaking or arguing in favor of it
public support for an idea or cause

Examples

She is known for her advocacy of children's rights.
The organization's advocacy for environmental protection has made a significant impact.
His advocacy helped bring about changes in local government policy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opposition - resistance or dissent expressed in action or argument
  • Synonym: promotion - the act of encouraging or supporting something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin advocatia (legal support), from Latin advocatus (one called to aid).
