bloodiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈblʌd.i.nɪs / (BrE)
a tendency or willingness to shed blood or engage in violence
disposition to shed blood or violence

Examples

The bloodiness of the regime shocked the international community.
Her novels often portray the bloodiness of war in vivid detail.
Historians debated the causes behind the bloodiness of the uprising.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruthlessness - lack of pity or compassion for others; mercilessness

Etymology

From 'bloody' + '-ness', forming a noun signifying the state or quality of being bloody.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈblʌd.i.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being covered with, or characterized by, blood
the state of being bloody

Examples

The bloodiness of the crime scene shocked even the most experienced officers.
After the battle, the bloodiness of his clothes showed how fierce the fighting had been.
Some critics objected to the bloodiness in the movie, saying it was too graphic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloody - covered with or characterized by blood
  • Antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean

Etymology

From 'bloody' + '-ness', denoting the state or condition.
