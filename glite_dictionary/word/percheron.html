Percheron

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrtʃəˌrɑn / (AmE), / ˈpɜːtʃərɒn / (BrE)
a large breed of grey or black draft horse, originally from France, known for its strength and used for heavy work such as pulling coaches or artillery
French breed of heavy draft horse

Examples

The Percheron was once widely used for farm work before the advent of tractors.
Many Percherons have a striking, dappled grey coat and a calm temperament.
She admired the strength and size of the Percheron at the horse show.

Etymology

From French, named after the Perche region in France.
