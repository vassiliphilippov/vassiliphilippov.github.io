dialog

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəˌlɔg / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəlɒg / (BrE)
a conversation between two people, especially as a feature of a book, play, or movie
conversation between two people

Examples

The play begins with a brief dialog between the two main characters.
Their dialog revealed the deep misunderstanding between them.
Writers often use dialog to develop characters and advance the plot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialogue - conversation between two or more people

Etymology

From Greek 'dialogos', from 'dia-' (through) + 'logos' (speech, discourse).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəˌlɔg / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəˌlɒg / (BrE)
the spoken lines exchanged by characters in a play, film, or book
spoken lines in drama or fiction

Examples

The witty dialog between the main characters made the play entertaining.
Writers often study real conversations to create believable dialog for their stories.
She memorized her dialog for the upcoming scene in the drama club performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monologue - a long speech by one character in a play or story
  • Synonym: conversation - an informal spoken exchange between people

Etymology

From Greek 'dialogos', from 'dia' (through) + 'logos' (speech, discourse).

Synset POS: n
