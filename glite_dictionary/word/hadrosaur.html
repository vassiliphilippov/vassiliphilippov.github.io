hadrosaur

1. NOUN

/ ˈhædrəˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈhædrəˌsɔː / (BrE)
a large, mostly herbivorous, bipedal dinosaur with a duck-billed snout, belonging to the family Hadrosauridae; may have been partly aquatic
duck-billed herbivorous dinosaur

Examples

The fossilized bones of a hadrosaur were discovered in the desert by a team of paleontologists.
Many hadrosaurs are known for their distinctive crests and duck-like bills.
The museum's exhibit features a life-sized model of a hadrosaur swimming in a prehistoric swamp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hadrosauridae - family of duck-billed ornithischian dinosaurs
  • Degree Higher: dinosaur - prehistoric reptile, member of Dinosauria

Etymology

From New Latin Hadrosaurus, from Greek hadros (thick, bulky) + sauros (lizard).
