Radiolaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdiəˈlɛriə / (AmE), / ˌreɪdɪəˈleəriə / (BrE)
A group of microscopic, single-celled marine protozoa with intricate mineral skeletons, commonly found as plankton.
microscopic marine protozoa with mineral skeletons

Examples

The shells of Radiolaria collect on the ocean floor as siliceous ooze.
Scientists use the unique skeletons of Radiolaria to study past ocean conditions.
Samples revealed a high diversity of Radiolaria in the plankton community.

Etymology

From Latin 'radiolus' (little ray), referring to their radiating skeletal structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊˈlɛriə / (AmE), / ˌreɪdɪəʊˈlɛːrɪə / (BrE)
a group of marine protozoa with intricate mineral skeletons, forming an order of radiolarians
a group of marine protozoa

Examples

Scientists study fossilized Radiolaria to learn about ancient marine environments.
The intricate skeletons of Radiolaria are often found in ocean sediments.
A microscope is needed to observe living Radiolaria in a water sample.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Radiolaria, from Latin radius (ray), referring to their radial symmetry.
