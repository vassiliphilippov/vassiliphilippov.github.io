proportional

1. ADJ

/ prəˈpɔrʃənəl / (AmE), / prəˈpɔːʃənəl / (BrE)
(of two or more amounts) having a constant ratio to each other; corresponding in size or amount to something else.
having a constant ratio; corresponding in size or amount

Examples

The speed of the car is proportional to the pressure applied on the accelerator.
In mathematics, the sides of similar triangles are proportional.
Their pay is proportional to the number of hours they work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inverse - having an opposite or contrary ratio or relationship
  • Synonym: equivalent - equal in value, amount, function, or meaning

Etymology

From Late Latin 'proportionalis', from 'proportio' (proportion).

2. ADJ

/ pɹəˈpɔɹʃənl̩ / (AmE), / pɹəˈpɔːʃənəl / (BrE)
having a relationship in size, amount, or degree to something else, so that an increase or decrease in one results in a consistent increase or decrease in the other; usually followed by 'to'.
having a corresponding size or amount to something else

Examples

The judge decided that the fine should be proportional to the seriousness of the offense.
There is often a proportional relationship between risk and reward in investing.
The amount of paint you need is proportional to the size of the wall.

Synset Examples

the punishment ought to be proportional to the crime
earnings relative to production

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relative - considered in relation or in proportion to something else
  • Antonym: disproportionate - not in correct relation or balance

Etymology

From Latin 'proportionalis', from 'proportio' (proportion).
