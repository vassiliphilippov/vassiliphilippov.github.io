procarbazine

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈkɑːr.bəˌzin / (AmE), / prəʊˈkɑː.bəˌziːn / (BrE)
an antineoplastic drug used in chemotherapy, especially to treat Hodgkin's disease
antineoplastic drug for Hodgkin's disease
medical

Examples

The oncologist prescribed procarbazine as part of the patient's chemotherapy regimen.
Treatment for advanced Hodgkin's lymphoma often includes procarbazine combined with other drugs.
Some patients may experience side effects while taking procarbazine, such as nausea or fatigue.

Etymology

From 'pro-' (indicating precursor), 'carbazine' (from chemical structure).
