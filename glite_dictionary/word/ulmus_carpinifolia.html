Ulmus carpinifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlməs ˌkɑrpɪˈnoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs ˌkɑːpɪˈnəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of European elm with lustrous, smooth leaves, often grown as an ornamental tree.
species of European elm tree

Examples

The Ulmus carpinifolia is prized in landscaping for its attractive, glossy foliage.
Old parks across Europe often feature mature Ulmus carpinifolia specimens lining the avenues.
Some botanists debate the classification of Ulmus carpinifolia among other elm species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a type of deciduous tree in the genus Ulmus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ulmus' meaning elm, and 'carpinifolia' meaning hornbeam-leaved (from 'carpinus' for hornbeam and 'folium' for leaf).
