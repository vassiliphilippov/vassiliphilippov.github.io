to lallygag

1. VERB

/ ˈlæliˌɡæɡ / (AmE), / ˈlalɪˌɡaɡ / (BrE)
to spend time aimlessly or to be idle, often by lingering in a place with no apparent purpose
linger or idle aimlessly
informal american

Examples

The kids spent the afternoon lallygagging in the park instead of finishing their homework.
Stop lallygagging and get to work or we'll miss our train!
He tends to lallygag around the store after his shift ends, chatting with his coworkers.

Synset Examples

The high school students like to loiter in the Central Square
Who is this man that is hanging around the department?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to loiter - to stand or wait around without apparent purpose
  • Synonym: to dawdle - to waste time or move slowly
  • Antonym: hurry - to move or act with haste

Etymology

Origin uncertain; first recorded in US English in the 19th century, possibly an alteration of dialect words like 'lolly' and 'gag.'
