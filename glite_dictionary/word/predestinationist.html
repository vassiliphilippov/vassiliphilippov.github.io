predestinationist

1. NOUN

/ priˌdɛstəˈneɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / priˌdɛstɪˈneɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that all events, particularly one's destiny, are determined in advance by divine will and cannot be changed by human effort
one who believes in predestination

Examples

As a predestinationist, he believed his future was already set by a higher power.
Many theologians argued with the predestinationist perspective during the religious debates of the 17th century.
Although her friends tried to convince her otherwise, she remained a steadfast predestinationist throughout her life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predestination - the doctrine that all events are predetermined by fate or divine will

Etymology

From 'predestination' + '-ist' (person who practices or is concerned with something), from Latin praedestinatio (predetermination).
