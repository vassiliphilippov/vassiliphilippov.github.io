cesium

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːziəm / (AmE), / ˈsiːziəm / (BrE)
a soft, silver-white, ductile metallic element that is liquid at or near room temperature and is the most electropositive and alkaline of all elements
a soft, highly reactive metallic element (Cs)

Examples

Scientists use cesium in atomic clocks because of its precise frequency.
Liquid cesium is stored carefully due to its extreme reactivity with water.
The soft metallic cesium melted quickly in the scientist's hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alkali metal - any of the group of univalent metals (Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, Fr) with similar properties

Etymology

From Latin 'caesius' (bluish gray), due to the blue lines in its spectrum.
