Anhima cornuta

1. NOUN

/ ɑːˈniːmə kɔːrˈnuːtə / (AmE), / ænˈhiːmə kɔːˈnuːtə / (BrE)
A large South American bird, known as the horned screamer, characterized by a hornlike projection on its forehead.
horned screamer bird of South America

Examples

The wetlands are home to the remarkable Anhima cornuta, easily recognized by its unique forehead horn.
Many birdwatchers travel to South America to catch a glimpse of the elusive Anhima cornuta.
The Anhima cornuta produces a loud, booming call that can be heard across the marshes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horned screamer - common name for Anhima cornuta

Etymology

From Latin: 'Anhima' is the genus name, and 'cornuta' means 'horned'.
