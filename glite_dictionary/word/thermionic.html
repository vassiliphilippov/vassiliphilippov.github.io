thermionic

1. ADJ

/ ˌθɜːrmiˈɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˌθɜːmiˈɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving the emission of electrons (thermions) from a heated source, especially in a vacuum.
emission of electrons from heated materials

Examples

The thermionic emission from the heated cathode is the basis of traditional vacuum tubes.
Engineers tested a thermionic valve in the laboratory for signal amplification.
A thermionic converter can turn heat directly into electricity.

Synset Examples

thermionic cathode

Etymology

From 'therm-' (heat) and 'ion' (charged particle), reflecting its relation to heat-induced electron emission.
