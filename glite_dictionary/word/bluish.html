bluish

1. ADJ

/ ˈbluːɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbluːɪʃ / (BrE)
having a slightly blue color or tinged with blue
somewhat blue in color

Examples

The old photograph had a bluish tint that made it look antique.
He noticed a bluish mark on his arm after bumping into the table.
The early morning sky had a bluish hue before the sun rose.

Synset Examples

October's bright blue weather
a blue flame
blue haze of tobacco smoke

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: blue - of the color between green and violet; the color of a clear sky

Etymology

From blue + -ish (suffix forming adjectives meaning 'somewhat' or 'like').
