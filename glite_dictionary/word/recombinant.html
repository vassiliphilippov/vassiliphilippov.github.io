recombinant

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɑːmbənənt / (AmE), / rɪˈkɒmbɪnənt / (BrE)
A cell or organism produced by genetic recombination, containing a new combination of genetic material.
cell or organism from genetic recombination
medical

Examples

The scientist isolated a recombinant from the bacterial culture for further study.
Modern medicines are often produced using recombinant organisms in the laboratory.
Each recombinant carries a unique set of genes resulting from genetic exchange.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hybrid - an organism produced by combining different breeds, varieties, or species

Etymology

From Latin 're-' (again) + 'combinare' (to combine), referring to recombined genetic material.

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈkɑːmbɪnənt / (AmE), / rɪˈkɒmbɪnənt / (BrE)
relating to or resulting from the combination of genetic material from different sources, especially as achieved by genetic engineering
produced by DNA recombination
medical

Examples

The production of recombinant proteins has revolutionized pharmaceutical research.
Scientists studied the effects of recombinant DNA technology on crop improvement.
A recombinant vaccine was developed using advanced genetic techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genetic engineering - the manipulation of an organism's DNA for scientific purposes

Etymology

From Latin 're-' (again) and 'combinare' (to combine), used in genetics since the 1960s.
