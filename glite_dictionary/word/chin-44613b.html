chin

1. VERB

/ tʃɪn / (AmE), / tʃɪn / (BrE)
to raise oneself while hanging from one's hands until one's chin is level with the support bar, especially as an exercise
raise body until chin reaches a bar

Examples

He can chin the bar ten times without taking a break.
During her workout, she tried to chin herself on the playground equipment.
After struggling for weeks, he finally chinned the high bar for the first time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pull-up - an exercise where you pull yourself up until your chin is above a bar
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ tʃɪn / (AmE), / tʃɪn / (BrE)
the protruding part of the lower jaw, especially the portion below the mouth on the human face
the lower part of the jaw

Examples

She rested her chin on her hand and listened carefully.
He took one on the chin during the boxing match, but kept fighting.
The toddler had a small bruise on his chin after falling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaw - the lower part of the face below the mouth

Etymology

Old English 'cin', related to Dutch 'kin', German 'Kinn', from Proto-Germanic.
