tussore

1. NOUN

/ təˈsɔr / (AmE), / təˈsɔː / (BrE)
an oriental moth (Antheraea paphia) that produces coarse, brownish silk used in textiles
oriental moth producing brown silk

Examples

The tussore is prized for the strong, natural-colored silk it produces.
Unlike the common silkworm, the tussore moth thrives in the forests of India.
Many traditional saris are woven from fabrics spun from tussore silk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tussar - variant spelling for the same moth producing wild silk
  • Synonym: silkworm - larva that spins silk cocoons, especially the domesticated species

Etymology

From Hindi 'tusar,' referring to the tussore silk moth.
