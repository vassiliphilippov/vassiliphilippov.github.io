pruning

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruː.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpruː.nɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of cutting away dead or overgrown branches or stems, especially to encourage healthy growth in a plant
the act of trimming a plant

Examples

Regular pruning helps keep the rose bushes healthy and full of blooms.
The gardener recommended annual pruning to improve fruit production.
Proper pruning can give the plant a more attractive shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trimming - the act of cutting something to make it neater or smaller

Etymology

From the verb 'prune', from Old French *proignier* (to cut), of unknown origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpruː.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpruː.nɪŋ / (BrE)
a branch or stem that has been cut off a plant, usually to encourage healthy growth
cut-off part of a plant

Examples

After the gardener finished, he collected the prunings into a large pile for composting.
The prunings from the rose bushes can sometimes be used to propagate new plants.
We burned the prunings to keep the orchard tidy at the end of the season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trimming - the act of cutting away unwanted parts, especially from plants

Etymology

From the verb 'prune', meaning 'to cut away unwanted parts', from Old French 'proignier' (to cut off).
