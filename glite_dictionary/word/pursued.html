pursued

1. NOUN

/ pərˈsuːd / (AmE), / pəˈsjuːd / (BrE)
a person or thing that is being chased or followed, especially in a story or narrative.
person or thing being chased

Examples

The director skillfully cut between scenes of the pursued and the pursuer.
In the forest's darkness, the pursued stumbled over fallen branches, desperate to escape.
Throughout the novel, the pursued remains anonymous, intensifying the sense of suspense.

Synset Examples

the film jumped back and forth from the pursuer to the pursued

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pursuer - a person or thing that chases or pursues

2. ADJ

/ pərˈsuːd / (AmE), / pəˈsjuːd / (BrE)
followed, especially with hostile or harmful intent
followed with hostile intent

Examples

The pursued prisoner darted through the alleyways, desperate to escape his captors.
Their eyes were filled with fear, like pursued animals fleeing a predator.
She felt like a pursued target, constantly looking over her shoulder.

Synset Examples

running and leaping like a herd of pursued antelopes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chased - hunted or followed quickly in order to capture
  • Synonym: hunted - being followed to capture or kill
  • Antonym: safe - not in danger; not being followed or threatened

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'pursue', from Old French 'poursuir', from Latin 'prosequi' (to follow, accompany).
