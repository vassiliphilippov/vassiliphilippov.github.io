to vulcanise

1. VERB

/ ˈvʌlkəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvʌlkənaɪz / (BrE)
to undergo vulcanization, especially of rubber, by being treated with sulfur or other agents to improve durability and elasticity
undergo rubber vulcanization process
british

Examples

During manufacturing, the rubber is vulcanised to enhance its resilience and strength.
Some industrial processes require polymers to be vulcanised before further use.
Natural rubber must be vulcanised to make it suitable for most commercial products.

Synset Examples

vulcanize rubber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vulcanize - American spelling of 'vulcanise'

Etymology

From Latin Vulcanus (the Roman god of fire), via the technical process discovered by Charles Goodyear in the 19th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈvʌl.kə.naɪz / (AmE), / ˈvʌl.kə.naɪz / (BrE)
to treat (rubber or a similar material) with sulfur or other substances under heat to improve durability and elasticity
to treat rubber with sulfur and heat

Examples

Many manufacturers vulcanise natural rubber to enhance its strength and resilience.
Charles Goodyear was the first to successfully vulcanise rubber in the 19th century.
The factory vulcanises thousands of tyres each day to meet the demand for durable products.

Synset Examples

vulcanized rubber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vulcanize - American English spelling of vulcanise

Etymology

From Latin Vulcanus, the Roman god of fire, after whom the process was named.
