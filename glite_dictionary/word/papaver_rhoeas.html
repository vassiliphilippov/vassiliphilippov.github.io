Papaver rhoeas

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpeɪvər roʊˈiːəs / (AmE), / pəˈpɑːvə rəʊˈiːəs / (BrE)
An annual European poppy with bright red flowers, commonly found in grain fields and widely cultivated as an ornamental plant.
annual European red poppy

Examples

The fields were ablaze with bright Papaver rhoeas during the spring.
Gardeners often plant Papaver rhoeas for its vivid red flowers.
Remembrance Day is symbolized by the red Papaver rhoeas poppy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corn poppy - common name for Papaver rhoeas
  • Degree Higher: poppy - general name for plants of the genus Papaver

Etymology

From Latin: 'papaver' meaning 'poppy', and 'rhoeas', the classical name for the corn poppy.
