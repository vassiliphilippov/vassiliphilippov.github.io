pout

1. NOUN

/ paʊt / (AmE), / paʊt / (BrE)
A facial expression in which the lips are pushed forward, especially in annoyance, sulkiness, or to look attractive.
grimace with protruding lips

Examples

When she didn't get her way, a clear pout appeared on her face.
He tried to hide his disappointment, but his pout gave him away.
With a dramatic pout, the child refused to eat his vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grimace - a twisted facial expression usually showing disgust or pain

Etymology

From Middle English *pouten* (to puff out the lips).

5. VERB

/ paʊt / (AmE), / paʊt / (BrE)
to push out one's lips to show displeasure or annoyance
push out lips in annoyance

Examples

The little girl began to pout when her brother took her toy.
He tends to pout whenever things don't go his way at work.
Instead of arguing, she simply pouted and looked away.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulk - to be silent, morose, and bad-tempered out of annoyance or disappointment
