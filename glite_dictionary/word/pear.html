pear

1. NOUN

/ pɛr / (AmE), / peə / (BrE)
a sweet, juicy fruit with a rounded base and tapering top, eaten fresh or cooked, available in many varieties
a type of sweet, juicy fruit

Examples

She sliced a ripe pear for her breakfast.
The dessert was topped with poached pear and vanilla ice cream.
I bought a bag of pears at the farmer's market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple - a round fruit with crisp flesh and a red, green, or yellow skin

Etymology

From Old English pere, from Latin pira (plural of pirum), via Old French poire.

2. NOUN

/ pɛr / (AmE), / pɛə / (BrE)
A sweet, juicy, usually teardrop-shaped fruit with a smooth or gritty texture, or the tree that bears this fruit.
a sweet, juicy fruit or its tree

Examples

She bit into the ripe pear and savored its juicy sweetness.
A pear tree grew in the corner of their garden, heavy with fruit.
Would you like an apple or a pear as a snack?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple - a round fruit with crisp flesh, related to the pear
  • Synonym: pear-shaped - having the shape of a pear

Etymology

From Old English 'pere', from Latin 'pira' (plural of 'pirum', pear).
