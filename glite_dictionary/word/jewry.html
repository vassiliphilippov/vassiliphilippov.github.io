Jewry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːri / (AmE), / ˈdʒʊəri / (BrE)
all Jewish people considered as a group; the Jewish community collectively.
the Jewish people collectively

Examples

The history of Jewry in Europe stretches back for centuries.
Much of world Jewry commemorates Passover every spring.
New York City has one of the largest Jewry populations outside Israel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jewish community - the community of Jewish people in a particular place or in general

Etymology

From Old French 'juïerie' (Jewry), from Medieval Latin 'Judaria' (land of the Jews), from Latin 'Iudaeus' (Jew).
