Priacanthus arenatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpraɪəˈkænθəs ˌɛrəˈneɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌpraɪəˈkanθəs ˌɛrəˈneɪtəs / (BrE)
A brightly colored carnivorous fish found in the western Atlantic Ocean and the West Indies, also known as the Atlantic bigeye.
species of brightly colored Atlantic fish

Examples

The Priacanthus arenatus is often seen in coral reefs throughout the Caribbean Sea.
Fishermen value the Priacanthus arenatus for its striking red color and large eyes.
Researchers studied the diet of Priacanthus arenatus in various Atlantic locations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atlantic bigeye - Common English name for Priacanthus arenatus

Etymology

From Greek *priēin* (to saw) + *akantha* (thorn), referring to the spiny fins; species name from Latin *arenatus* (sandy).
