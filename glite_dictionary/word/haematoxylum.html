Haematoxylum

1. NOUN

/ hiːməˈtɒksɪləm / (AmE), / hiːməˈtɒksɪləm / (BrE)
A small genus of spiny, bushy shrubs or trees native to tropical America, including species with valuable wood.
genus of tropical American shrubs or trees

Examples

The wood of Haematoxylum campechianum is prized for making dye.
Botanists studied several species within the Haematoxylum genus.
Haematoxylum trees are native to regions of Central and South America.

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + xylon (wood), referring to the red wood of some species.
