petticoated

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛtiˌkoʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɛtɪˌkəʊtɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) wearing, covered with, or furnished with a petticoat.
wearing or furnished with a petticoat
archaic

Examples

The guests admired the elegance of the petticoated ladies at the ball.
A petticoated table stood in the corner, neatly draped with lace.
Victorian novels often describe girls as being petticoated for formal occasions.

Synset Examples

petticoated ladies
a petticoated table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petticoat - a woman's light undergarment hanging from the shoulders or the waist

Etymology

From 'petticoat' + '-ed', forming an adjective meaning 'provided with a petticoat'.
