Oceanites

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈsiː.əˌnaɪtiːz / (AmE), / əʊˈsiː.əˌnaɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small seabirds in the family Hydrobatidae, commonly known as storm petrels, found mainly in the southern oceans.
genus of small seabirds (storm petrels)

Examples

The Oceanites genus includes several species of storm petrels found in the southern hemisphere.
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of Oceanites to better understand their breeding habits.
A flock of Oceanites was observed feeding near the Antarctic coastline during the expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm petrel - common name for birds of the family Hydrobatidae, including Oceanites

Etymology

From Greek 'ōkeanos' (ocean), reflecting the habitat of these birds.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈsiː.ə.naɪts / (AmE), / əʊˈsiː.ə.naɪts / (BrE)
A genus of small seabirds in the family Hydrobatidae, known as storm-petrels, found in southern oceans.
a genus of storm-petrels (seabirds)

Examples

The Oceanites genus includes birds such as Wilson's storm-petrel, which breeds in Antarctica.
Scientists observed several offspring of the Oceanites in the sub-Antarctic islands.
Many ornithologists study the migratory patterns of Oceanites species worldwide.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'Okeanis' (ocean nymph), relating to the sea.
