osteology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːstiˈɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌɒstiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of anatomy that deals with the study of the structure and function of bones.
study of bones in anatomy
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in osteology during her medical studies.
The museum's collection includes resources on osteology for researchers.
Understanding osteology is essential for forensic anthropologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anatomy - the scientific study of the structure of living things

Etymology

From Greek osteon (bone) + -logia (study of).
