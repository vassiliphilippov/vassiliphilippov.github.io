annoyance

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɔɪ.əns / (AmE), / əˈnɔɪ.əns / (BrE)
A person or thing that causes slight anger or irritation; something that is bothersome or troublesome.
source of irritation or trouble

Examples

The constant noise from the construction site is a real annoyance to everyone in the neighborhood.
Having to reset my password every week has become an annoyance I could do without.
Her little brother can sometimes be an annoyance, but she still loves him.

Synset Examples

a bit of a bother
he's not a friend, he's an infliction
washing dishes was a pain in the neck before we got a dish washer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritation - the state of being annoyed or something that annoys
  • Antonym: pleasure - a feeling of happy satisfaction and enjoyment; opposite of annoyance

Etymology

From Old French 'anoier' (to trouble), from Latin 'inodiare' (to make loathsome).

3. NOUN

/ əˈnɔɪəns / (AmE), / əˈnɔɪəns / (BrE)
A feeling of slight anger, irritation, or discomfort caused by something unpleasant or disturbing.
feeling of irritation or slight anger

Examples

My little brother's constant questions became quite an annoyance during the car ride.
She tried to hide her annoyance when the meeting ran over time.
The buzzing mosquito was a minor annoyance in an otherwise peaceful evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritation - state or feeling of being irritated
  • Antonym: contentment - state of being satisfied and at ease

Etymology

From French *ennuiance*, from *ennuyer* (to annoy), based on Latin *inodiare* (to make odious).
