warden

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrdən / (AmE), / ˈwɔːdən / (BrE)
the person in charge of a prison, responsible for its administration and the supervision of staff and prisoners
chief official of a prison

Examples

The warden announced new regulations to improve security in the prison.
After the escape, the warden implemented stricter visitation rules.
The newly appointed warden is committed to reforming the prison system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: governor - person in charge of a British prison

Etymology

From Middle English *warden*, from Anglo-Norman *warden*, from Old Northern French *warder* (to guard).
