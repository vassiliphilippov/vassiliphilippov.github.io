victorious

1. ADJ

/ vɪkˈtɔriəs / (AmE), / vɪkˈtɔːriəs / (BrE)
having won a victory; triumphant over an opponent or in a contest
having won; triumphant

Examples

The victorious team celebrated their well-earned success after the match.
She emerged victorious in the heated debate against her colleague.
After a long legal battle, the company was finally victorious.

Synset Examples

the victorious entry
the winning team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triumphant - having achieved victory or success
  • Antonym: defeated - having been beaten in a contest or battle

Etymology

From Latin 'victoriosus' (full of victory), from 'victoria' (victory).

2. ADJ

/ vɪkˈtɔriəs / (AmE), / vɪkˈtɔːriəs / (BrE)
having won a victory or achieved success in a contest, conflict, or struggle; triumphant.
having won; triumphant

Examples

The team returned home victorious after winning the championship match.
She felt victorious as she crossed the finish line ahead of everyone else.
After a long legal battle, the company emerged victorious in court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triumphant - feeling or expressing jubilation after a victory
  • Antonym: defeated - having lost a contest or struggle

Etymology

From Latin 'victoriosus', from 'victor' meaning 'conqueror'.
