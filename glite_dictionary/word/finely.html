finely

1. ADV

/ ˈfaɪnli / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnli / (BrE)
In an elegant or refined manner.
in an elegant manner

Examples

The guests were finely dressed for the formal gala.
Each plate was finely decorated with delicate patterns.
She entered the room, finely attired in a shimmering evening gown.

Synset Examples

finely costumed actors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elegantly - in a stylish or graceful way
  • Antonym: crudely - in a rough, lacking elegance or refinement

2. ADV

/ ˈfaɪnli / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnli / (BrE)
in a delicate or precise manner, showing fine detail or subtlety
in a delicate or precise manner

Examples

The glass was finely etched with intricate patterns.
She finely sliced the vegetables for the salad.
His facial features are finely chiseled, giving him an elegant appearance.

Synset Examples

finely shaped features
her fine drawn body

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coarsely - in a rough or crude manner

Etymology

From 'fine' + adverbial suffix '-ly'. 'Fine' from Middle English, Old French 'fin' (delicate, pure).

3. ADV

/ ˈfaɪnli / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnli / (BrE)
into or in very small or thin pieces or particles
in very small or thin pieces

Examples

Chop the onions finely for the best texture in the soup.
The sugar was ground finely to make a smooth icing.
Grate the cheese finely before adding it to the dish.

Synset Examples

the surfaces were finely granular

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coarsely - in large pieces or particles

Etymology

From fine + -ly, with 'fine' from Middle English 'fīne' (delicate, thin).
