diffident

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪfɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪfɪdənt / (BrE)
shy and lacking in self-confidence; modest and reserved in manner or behavior.
shy; modestly reserved

Examples

Although he had valuable insights, his diffident manner prevented him from speaking up at the meeting.
She answered the interviewer’s questions in a diffident voice, rarely making eye contact.
Despite his diffident nature, he eventually took charge of the project when needed.

Synset Examples

she was diffident when offering a comment on the professor's lecture

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confident - having strong belief or full assurance; self-assured
  • Synonym: shy - nervous or timid in the company of others

Etymology

From Latin diffīdere (to distrust), from dis- (apart) + fīdere (to trust).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪfɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪfɪdənt / (BrE)
lacking self-confidence; shy or not assertive
lacking self-confidence; shy

Examples

He gave a diffident answer, barely meeting anyone’s gaze.
Although she had good ideas, her diffident nature kept her from speaking up in meetings.
Jason appeared diffident at the interview, unsure of his qualifications.

Synset Examples

stood in the doorway diffident and abashed
problems that call for bold not timid responses
a very unsure young man

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confident - having strong belief in oneself; self-assured
  • Synonym: timid - easily frightened; showing a lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

From Latin 'diffidens', present participle of 'diffidere' (to distrust).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00340839-a
Synset POS: a
