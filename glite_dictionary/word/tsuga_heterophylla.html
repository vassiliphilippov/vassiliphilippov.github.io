Tsuga heterophylla

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːɡə ˌhɛtərəˈfaɪlə / (AmE), / ˈtsuːɡə ˌhɛtərəˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A tall evergreen tree native to western North America, known as the western hemlock, valued commercially for its timber.
western North American evergreen tree; timber source

Examples

Tsuga heterophylla is commonly harvested for its high-quality timber in the Pacific Northwest.
The cool, moist forests of the region are dominated by Tsuga heterophylla and Douglas fir.
Botanists often refer to Tsuga heterophylla as western hemlock when describing North American flora.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: western hemlock - another common name for Tsuga heterophylla
  • Antonym: eastern hemlock - a related species, Tsuga canadensis, native to eastern North America

Etymology

From New Latin, where 'Tsuga' is the Japanese name for hemlock, and 'heterophylla' means 'with different leaves' from Greek 'hetero-' (different) and '-phylla' (leaves).
