Lysimachia vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌsaɪməˈkiːə vʌlˈɡɛrɪs / (AmE), / lɪˌsɪməkˈaɪə vʌlˈɡɛːrɪs / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to Europe and Asia, often considered a weed, with yellow flowers and found in damp habitats.
yellow-flowered perennial plant, often a weed

Examples

Lysimachia vulgaris can quickly spread along riverbanks, forming dense colonies that crowd out native vegetation.
Gardeners sometimes remove Lysimachia vulgaris to prevent it from overtaking other ornamental plants.
The yellow flowers of Lysimachia vulgaris attract a variety of pollinating insects in midsummer.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name from Greek Lysimakhos (Lysimachus), a Macedonian king; 'vulgaris' means 'common' in Latin.
