glossolalia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡlɑːsəˈleɪliə / (AmE), / ˌɡlɒsəˈleɪliə / (BrE)
repetitive, nonmeaningful speech, often associated with trance states or religious enthusiasm, especially as manifested in speaking in tongues.
repetitive, nonmeaningful speech (speaking in tongues)

Examples

During the revival meeting, several participants began to exhibit glossolalia as a sign of spiritual ecstasy.
Researchers have studied glossolalia to understand its psychological and neurological underpinnings.
The phenomenon of glossolalia is often cited in discussions about charismatic Christian worship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speaking in tongues - phenomenon of speaking in an unknown language in religious contexts

Etymology

From Greek 'glōssa' (tongue, language) + 'lalia' (speaking, speech).
