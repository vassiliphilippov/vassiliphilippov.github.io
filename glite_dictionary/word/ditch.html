ditch

1. VERB

/ dɪtʃ / (AmE), / dɪtʃ / (BrE)
to get rid of or discard something; to throw away
throw away; discard
informal

Examples

I decided to ditch my old shoes instead of repairing them again.
She quickly ditched all the extra paperwork she didn't need anymore.
After the update, many users ditched the old app in favor of the new one.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to get rid of something as no longer useful
  • Antonym: keep - to retain possession of

/ dɪtʃ / (AmE), / dɪtʃ / (BrE)

3. NOUN

/ dɪtʃ / (AmE), / dɪtʃ / (BrE)
A long, narrow trench dug in the ground, typically to carry water or mark the boundary of an area.
long, narrow trench in the ground

Examples

The farmer dug a ditch along the edge of the field to help with drainage.
Rainwater collected in the ditch by the side of the road after the storm.
He lost his balance and accidentally fell into a muddy ditch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trench - a long, narrow excavation, especially one used in warfare or construction

Etymology

From Old English *dic* (trench, moat), related to Middle Dutch *dijk* (dike).
