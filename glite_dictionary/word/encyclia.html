Encyclia

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈsaɪkliə / (AmE), / ɛnˈsɪklɪə / (BrE)
A large genus of mainly epiphytic or lithophytic orchids native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and the West Indies, formerly classified under Epidendrum.
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The botanical garden features a rare Encyclia orchid in its tropical house.
Many hobbyists cultivate Encyclia because of their fragrant, long-lasting flowers.
This Encyclia species thrives best when mounted rather than potted.

Etymology

From Greek 'enkykleos' (circular), referring to the orchid's circular lateral lobes.

2. NOUN

/ ɛnˈsɪkliə / (AmE), / ɛnˈsɪkliə / (BrE)
a genus of epiphytic and lithophytic orchids native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and the West Indies
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

Many collectors prize Encyclia orchids for their attractive and long-lasting flowers.
The Encyclia genus was once considered a part of Epidendrum, but is now recognized as separate.
Some Encyclia species thrive on rocky hillsides, while others grow on tree branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Epidendrum - a related genus of orchids, formerly including Encyclia species

Etymology

From Greek enkykleos ('circular'), referring to the circular shape of the orchid's callus.
