laudable

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔdəbəl / (AmE), / ˈlɔːdəbəl / (BrE)
Deserving praise and commendation.
worthy of praise

Examples

Her laudable efforts to help the community did not go unnoticed.
It is laudable that the company recycles all of its waste materials.
His passion for teaching is truly laudable and inspires his students.

Synset Examples

applaudable efforts to save the environment
a commendable sense of purpose
laudable motives of improving housing conditions
a significant and praiseworthy increase in computer intelligence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praiseworthy - deserving praise or approval
  • Synonym: commendable - worthy of praise; admirable
  • Antonym: blameworthy - deserving blame; culpable

Etymology

From Latin laudabilis, from laudare (to praise).
