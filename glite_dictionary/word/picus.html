Picus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈpɪkʊs / (BrE)
a genus of birds in the woodpecker family (Picidae), comprising several species found in Eurasia and North Africa
genus of woodpeckers

Examples

The green woodpecker belongs to the genus Picus.
Researchers discovered a new species within Picus in southern Europe.
Many Picus species are known for their distinctive drumming on tree trunks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodpecker - any bird belonging to the family Picidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Picus', meaning ‘woodpecker’.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈpiːkʊs / (BrE)
A genus of woodpeckers in the family Picidae, including several European species.
genus of woodpecker birds

Examples

The green woodpecker belongs to the genus Picus.
Researchers have identified several new species within Picus.
European forests are home to members of the Picus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodpecker - bird known for pecking holes in wood; member of Picidae family

Etymology

From Latin Picus (mythical woodpecker or person of that name).
