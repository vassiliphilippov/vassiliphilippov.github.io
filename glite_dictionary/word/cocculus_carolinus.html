Cocculus carolinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːˈkjuːləs ˌkɛrəˈlaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌkɒk.jʊ.ləs ˌkær.əˈlaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
a woody vine native to the southeastern United States, resembling the common moonseed but bearing red fruits
woody vine of the southeastern US with red fruits

Examples

The bright red berries of Cocculus carolinus stand out against its dark green leaves in late summer.
Gardeners value Cocculus carolinus for its ability to quickly cover a trellis or fence.
Cocculus carolinus is sometimes called Carolina moonseed due to its resemblance to other moonseed vines.

Etymology

From Latin, combining ‘Cocculus’ (a plant genus name) and ‘carolinus’ (of Carolina, referring to the Carolinas in the US).
