avouchment

1. NOUN

/ əˈvaʊtʃmənt / (AmE), / əˈvaʊtʃmənt / (BrE)
A formal declaration asserting the existence or truth of something.
formal assertion or declaration

Examples

The lawyer's avouchment was central to convincing the jury of his client's innocence.
Her confident avouchment of the facts left no room for doubt.
The committee requested a formal avouchment of those claims before proceeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertion - a confident and forceful statement of fact or belief
  • Antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true

Etymology

From Old French 'avouchier' (to vouch), from Latin 'advocare' (to call to, summon).
