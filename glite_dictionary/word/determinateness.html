determinateness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɝːmɪnətnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːmɪnətnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being clearly defined and predictable, often with a high degree of certainty
quality of being clearly defined or predictable

Examples

The determinateness of the outcome made the experiment easy to replicate.
Einstein valued the determinateness found in mathematical principles.
Legal systems strive for determinateness in their rules to avoid ambiguity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indeterminateness - the quality of being vague or not clearly defined
  • Synonym: predictability - the degree to which something can be predicted

Etymology

From 'determinate' + '-ness', based on Latin determinare (to limit, define).
