King Harold II

1. PROPN

/ kɪŋ ˈhɛr.əld səˈkənd / (AmE), / kɪŋ ˈhær.əld ðə ˈsɛk.ənd / (BrE)
(1022–1066) The last Anglo-Saxon King of England, who succeeded Edward the Confessor in 1066 and was killed at the Battle of Hastings during the Norman invasion.
last Anglo-Saxon King of England (1066)

Examples

King Harold II was defeated by William the Conqueror at the Battle of Hastings in 1066.
The death of King Harold II marked the end of Anglo-Saxon rule in England.
Many historians debate the tactics King Harold II used during his final battle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: William the Conqueror - the Norman leader who defeated Harold II at Hastings
