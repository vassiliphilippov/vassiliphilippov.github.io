Oscillatoriaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːsɪˌleɪtəˈraɪəsiː / (AmE), / ˌɒsɪləˌtɔːrɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of filamentous blue-green algae (cyanobacteria) found in fresh and saltwater, notable for their oscillating movement
family of filamentous blue-green algae

Examples

Members of the Oscillatoriaceae are common in freshwater environments around the world.
Blooms of Oscillatoriaceae can sometimes produce toxins that affect aquatic life.
Scientists studied the structure of several Oscillatoriaceae species under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyanobacteria - phylum of bacteria that obtain energy via photosynthesis, often called blue-green algae

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Oscillatoria (a genus in the family) + -aceae (family suffix).
