clod

1. NOUN

/ klɑd / (AmE), / klɒd / (BrE)
a person who is awkward, stupid, or dull-witted
awkward or stupid person

Examples

Don't be such a clod—pay attention to what you're doing.
He acted like a complete clod at the dinner party, knocking over glasses and forgetting names.
Though he may seem a bit of a clod, he's actually quite thoughtful once you get to know him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dullard - a slow or stupid person
  • Synonym: oaf - a clumsy or unintelligent person

Etymology

From Old English *clod* (lump or mass of earth); figurative sense developed later.

2. NOUN

/ klɑd / (AmE), / klɒd / (BrE)
a lump or compact mass of earth, clay, or similar material
lump or mass of earth or clay

Examples

She kicked a clod of dirt across the garden path.
A heavy clod stuck to the bottom of his boot after walking through the field.
The rain softened the ground, making every clod easy to break apart.

Synset Examples

a ball of mud caught him on the shoulder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lump - a compact mass, especially of something soft or malleable
  • Synonym: clump - a thick cluster or lump, especially of earth or plants

Etymology

Old English *clod* (lump of earth), related to Old High German *klotz* (block, lump).
