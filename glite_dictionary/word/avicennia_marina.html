Avicennia marina

1. NOUN

/ ˌævɪˈsɛniə məˈriːnə / (AmE), / ˌævɪˈsɛniə məˈriːnə / (BrE)
a species of mangrove tree found in coastal regions of the West Indies and southern Florida, known for its dense growth and numerous short roots that protrude above the ground
species of mangrove tree

Examples

The coastal wetlands are dominated by Avicennia marina, which provide important habitat for fish and birds.
Scientists study Avicennia marina to understand how mangroves adapt to saltwater environments.
Dense thickets of Avicennia marina help protect shorelines from erosion.

Etymology

From Latin, named after Persian scientist Avicenna (Ibn Sina) and 'marina' meaning 'of the sea'.
