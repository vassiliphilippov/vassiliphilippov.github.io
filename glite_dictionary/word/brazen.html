brazen

1. ADJ

/ ˈbreɪzən / (AmE), / ˈbreɪzn / (BrE)
Made of brass, or resembling brass in color or hardness.
made of or like brass

Examples

The knight wore a brazen helmet that gleamed in the sunlight.
A brazen statue stood tall at the entrance to the city.
Her hair shone with a brazen luster, catching everyone's eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brass - a yellow metal, an alloy of copper and zinc

Etymology

From Old English bræs (brass), through Middle English brasen (made of brass).

2. VERB

/ ˈbreɪzən / (AmE), / ˈbreɪzən / (BrE)
to face a difficult or embarrassing situation with boldness or shameless confidence, often without showing shame or fear
face with bold or shameless confidence

Examples

Despite the accusations, he decided to brazen it out in public.
She tried to brazen through the interview despite being unprepared.
Instead of apologizing, he chose to brazen his way past the criticism.

Synset Examples

brazen it out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluff - to try to deceive someone as to your intentions or abilities
  • Antonym: cringe - to react with fear or embarrassment

Etymology

From Old English bræs (brass), metaphorically transferred to mean 'shameless.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00870135-v
Synset POS: v

3. ADJ

/ ˈbreɪzən / (AmE), / ˈbreɪz(ə)n / (BrE)
shameless and bold, showing a lack of respect for conventional standards or propriety
shamelessly bold or disrespectful

Examples

She made a brazen attempt to lie her way out of the situation.
His brazen disregard for the rules shocked everyone in the meeting.
The thief walked out of the store in brazen daylight, carrying stolen goods.

Synset Examples

an audacious trick to pull
a barefaced hypocrite
the most bodacious display of tourism this side of Anaheim
bald-faced lies
brazen arrogance
the modern world with its quick material successes and insolent belief in the boundless possibilities of progress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shameless - having no sense of shame, lacking modesty
  • Synonym: bold - showing courage or lack of fear; daring
  • Antonym: modest - not showing excessive pride or confidence, opposite of shameless

Etymology

From Old English *bræsian* (to make of brass), from *bræs* (brass), figuratively referring to 'shamelessness' from the notion of having a face like brass.

Source: WordNet
