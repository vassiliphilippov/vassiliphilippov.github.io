lameness

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪmnɪs / (AmE), / ˈleɪmnɪs / (BrE)
The condition of being unable to walk properly because of an injury or disability affecting the legs or feet.
inability to walk properly due to leg or foot disability

Examples

The horse developed a sudden lameness in its hind leg after the race.
He tried to hide his lameness when walking among the other soldiers.
Veterinarians examined the cause of the cow's lameness before recommending treatment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mobility - the ability to move or be moved easily and freely
  • Synonym: lamed - having become unable to walk because of a leg or foot injury

Etymology

From Old English 'lama' (crippled, paralyzed) + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈleɪmnəs / (AmE), / ˈleɪmnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being imperfect or defective; a weakness or inadequacy in something
imperfection or defectiveness

Examples

The lameness of the argument became obvious as soon as the facts were checked.
Despite the lameness of her excuse, everyone pretended to believe her.
Critics complained about the lameness of the show's new plotlines.

Synset Examples

a stylist noted for the lameness of his plots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flaw - a mark, fault, or other imperfection that mars a substance or object
  • Antonym: excellence - the quality of being outstanding or extremely good

Etymology

From lame + -ness; originally referred to physical disability, later extended to abstract flaws.
