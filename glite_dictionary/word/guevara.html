Guevara

1. PROPN

/ ɡəˈvɑrə / (AmE), / ɡeˈvɑːrə / (BrE)
Che Guevara (1928–1967), an Argentine revolutionary leader who played a key role as Fidel Castro's chief lieutenant during the Cuban Revolution, and was influential in other Latin American revolutionary movements.
Argentine revolutionary leader (Che Guevara)

Examples

Many people around the world recognize Guevara as a symbol of rebellion and revolution.
The biography of Guevara details his journey from Argentina to leading guerrilla movements.
Posters of Guevara can often be seen in student dormitories and protests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Che Guevara - the revolutionary leader also known as Che Guevara

Etymology

Spanish surname, notably borne by Ernesto 'Che' Guevara.
