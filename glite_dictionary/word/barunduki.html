barunduki

1. NOUN

/ bɑːˈrundʊki / (AmE), / bɑːˈrʊndʊki / (BrE)
A terrestrial squirrel native to Siberia, known for its striped back and ground-dwelling habits.
Siberian ground squirrel

Examples

The barunduki often collects seeds and nuts to store for the long Siberian winter.
Researchers observed a family of barundukis darting through the undergrowth in the forest.
Unlike some other squirrels, the barunduki spends most of its time on the ground.

Etymology

From Russian 'барундук' (barunduk), meaning 'chipmunk' or 'ground squirrel'.
