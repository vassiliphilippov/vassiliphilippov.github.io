overestimate

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvɚˈɛstəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˈɛstɪmeɪt / (BrE)
an estimate or appraisal that is higher than the actual value or reality
an estimate that is too high

Examples

The project's cost was based on an overestimate of the required materials.
Her sales forecast turned out to be an overestimate of actual demand.
Any overestimate in the data could lead to wasted resources.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underestimate - an estimate that is too low

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessive) and 'estimate'.

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈɛstəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈɛstɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to estimate something as being greater or more important than it actually is
to assess as greater than the actual amount or value

Examples

It is easy to overestimate the difficulty of this task.
She tends to overestimate how much money she needs for a trip.
They seriously overestimated the crowd's enthusiasm for the new product.

Synset Examples

He overestimated his own powers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underestimate - to estimate something as being less than the actual amount or value

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + estimate, from Latin aestimare (to value).

The company blamed the shortfall on an overestimate of expected sales.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underestimate - an estimate that is too low

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'over-' meaning 'too much' and 'estimate'.

4. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈɛstəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˈɛstɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to assess or value something as greater, more important, or more significant than it really is
assign too high a value to

Examples

Many students overestimate the amount of time they will have to study before the test.
She overestimated how much the house was worth in today’s market.
It is easy to overestimate your abilities when you are confident.

Synset Examples

You are overestimating the value of your old car

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underestimate - to judge something as smaller or less important than it really is
  • Synonym: to overvalue - to value something more highly than it deserves

Etymology

From over- (above, too much) + estimate (from Latin aestimare, to value).
