Cimex

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪmɛks / (AmE), / ˈsɪmɛks / (BrE)
A genus of insects in the family Cimicidae, including the common bedbug.
genus of bedbugs (insects)

Examples

The biologist collected several Cimex specimens from the old mattress for further study.
Cimex infestations can be difficult to eliminate from hotels and homes alike.
Research has shown that the feeding behavior of Cimex species varies depending on environmental conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedbug - a small parasitic insect of the family Cimicidae

Etymology

From Latin 'cimex' meaning 'bug' or 'insect'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪˌmɛks / (AmE), / ˈsɪː.mɛks / (BrE)
A genus of insects in the family Cimicidae, including bedbugs that are external parasites of humans and other warm-blooded animals.
genus of bedbugs

Examples

The species Cimex lectularius is the common bedbug found in homes.
Scientists studied the lifecycle of Cimex in controlled laboratory conditions.
Infestations of Cimex can cause irritation and allergic reactions in humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedbug - a blood-sucking parasitic insect, especially Cimex lectularius

Etymology

From Latin 'cimex' meaning 'bug' or 'insect'.
