lie-abed

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ əˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ əˌbɛd / (BrE)
a person who stays in bed until a relatively late hour, especially habitually
person who stays in bed late
archaic

Examples

My brother is such a lie-abed that he rarely sees the morning sun.
The old poem criticizes lie-abeds for wasting the best part of the day.
Even on weekends, she refuses to become a lie-abed and rises with the dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: late riser - a person who tends to get up late in the morning
  • Antonym: early riser - a person who gets up early in the morning

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'lie' + 'abed' (in bed), in use since at least the 16th century.
