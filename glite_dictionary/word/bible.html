Bible

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈbaɪb(ə)l / (BrE)
the holy scriptures of the Christian religion, consisting of the Old and New Testaments
Christian sacred scriptures

Examples

She studies the Bible every morning before work.
Many hotels provide a Bible in each guest room.
Passages from the Bible are often quoted in literature.

Synset Examples

he went to carry the Word to the heathen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scripture - the sacred writings of a religion

Etymology

From Greek 'biblia' (books), via Latin 'Biblia', originally meaning 'books'.
