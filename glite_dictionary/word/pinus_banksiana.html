Pinus banksiana

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.nəs bæŋkˈsiː.ɑː.nə / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.nəs bæŋkˈsiː.ə.nə / (BrE)
A slender, medium-sized pine tree native to eastern North America, with yellow-green needles in pairs and fissured gray to reddish-brown bark; also known as jack pine.
species of pine tree (jack pine)

Examples

The Pinus banksiana is well adapted to sandy soils and cold climates.
After the wildfire, Pinus banksiana seeds quickly sprouted in the charred landscape.
Forests dominated by Pinus banksiana provide vital habitat for the endangered Kirtland's warbler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jack pine - common name for Pinus banksiana, a North American pine

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pinus' (pine) and the specific epithet 'banksiana', named after English botanist Sir Joseph Banks.
