ankylosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋkəˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌaŋkɪˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
Abnormal stiffening and immobility of a joint due to adhesion and rigidity of the bones.
abnormal stiffening of a joint
medical

Examples

The patient developed ankylosis in his right knee after years of untreated arthritis.
Early intervention is important to prevent ankylosis in children with juvenile arthritis.
Because of the ankylosis, she had significant difficulty bending her finger joints.

Etymology

From Greek ankylōsis (stiffening of the joints), from ankylos (bent, crooked).
