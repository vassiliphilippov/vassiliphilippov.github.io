Dendrocalamus giganteus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛndroʊˌkæləməs dʒaɪˈgæntˌəs / (AmE), / ˌdɛndrəʊˌkæləməs dʒɪˈgæn.tiː.əs / (BrE)
a very large tropical bamboo native to Southeast Asia, noted for its tough, hollow stems resembling tree trunks
giant bamboo species of Southeast Asia

Examples

Dendrocalamus giganteus can grow up to 30 meters tall, making it one of the largest bamboos in the world.
Farmers often harvest Dendrocalamus giganteus for use in building and scaffolding due to its strength.
Botanical gardens sometimes display Dendrocalamus giganteus as a remarkable example of giant bamboo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bamboo - a type of tall, fast-growing grass with woody stems

Etymology

From Greek 'dendron' (tree), 'kalamos' (reed), and Latin 'giganteus' (gigantic).
