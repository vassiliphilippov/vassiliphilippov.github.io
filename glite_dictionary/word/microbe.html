microbe

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkroʊb / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊb / (BrE)
a microscopic organism, especially one that can cause disease, such as a bacterium; not used technically in scientific classifications
microscopic organism, e.g. a bacterium

Examples

Many diseases are caused by harmful microbes that invade the body.
Scientists discovered a rare microbe living in the deep ocean vents.
Washing your hands helps remove microbes that could make you sick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacterium - microscopic single-celled organism
  • Synonym: virus - submicroscopic infectious agent

Etymology

From French *microbe*, from Greek *mikros* (small) + *bios* (life).
