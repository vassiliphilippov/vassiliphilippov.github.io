discomposed

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪskəmˈpoʊzd / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəmˈpəʊzd / (BrE)
(of a person) having one's composure disturbed; feeling uneasy or unsettled.
feeling uneasy or unsettled

Examples

She appeared discomposed when she heard the unexpected news.
His discomposed manner revealed that something was wrong.
Despite her outward calm, he could see she was inwardly discomposed.

Synset Examples

looked about with a wandering and discomposed air

Related Terms

  • Antonym: composed - calm and self-possessed; not disturbed
  • Synonym: unsettled - anxious or uneasy; not calm

Etymology

From dis- (a prefix meaning 'not' or 'opposite of') + composed, from Latin componere ('to put together').
