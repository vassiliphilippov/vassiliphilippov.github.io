guffaw

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈfɔ / (AmE), / ɡəˈfɔː / (BrE)
a loud and boisterous burst of laughter
loud, hearty laughter

Examples

His joke was met with a raucous guffaw from the crowd.
She tried to stifle a guffaw during the serious meeting.
A sudden guffaw echoed across the quiet library, startling everyone.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: laugh - to make sounds and movements showing amusement or happiness

Etymology

Perhaps imitative in origin, first recorded in late 18th century.

WordNet ID: oewn-07142498-n

2. VERB

/ ɡəˈfɔ / (AmE), / ɡəˈfɑː / (BrE)
to laugh loudly and boisterously
laugh loudly and boisterously

Examples

He couldn't help but guffaw at the comedian's outrageous joke.
The entire room guffawed when the speaker tripped over his own punchline.
She was guffawing so hard that tears ran down her cheeks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: chuckle - to laugh quietly or to oneself

Etymology

Imitative origin, possibly from Scottish or related to early English dialectical forms.

Source: WordNet
