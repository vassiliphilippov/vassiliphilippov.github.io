Cervantes Saavedra

1. PROPN

/ sərˈvæn.tiːz sɑːˈvɛd.rə / (AmE), / səˈvɑːn.teɪz sæˈveɪ.drə / (BrE)
(1547–1616) Spanish writer best known for 'Don Quixote,' which satirizes chivalry and had a major influence on the development of the novel.
Spanish author of 'Don Quixote'

Examples

Cervantes Saavedra is widely regarded as one of the greatest writers in the Spanish language.
The influence of Cervantes Saavedra on Western literature is profound and enduring.
Many scholars consider Cervantes Saavedra's 'Don Quixote' the first modern novel.
