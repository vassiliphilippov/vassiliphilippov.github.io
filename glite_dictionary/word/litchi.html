litchi

1. NOUN

/ ˈliː.tʃiː / (AmE), / ˈlɪtʃ.i / (BrE)
A small, round fruit with a thin, brittle shell, sweet, jelly-like flesh, and a single seed, often eaten fresh or dried; native to China.
a sweet Asian fruit with a thin shell and jelly-like flesh

Examples

I love eating litchi in the summer because of its juicy, refreshing taste.
She bought a bag of dried litchis at the Asian market for a snack.
The dessert was topped with fresh litchi and mint leaves for an exotic flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lychee - another spelling of litchi

Etymology

From Cantonese lai chi, via Chinese 荔枝 (lìzhī).

2. NOUN

/ ˈliːtʃi, ˈlɪtʃi / (AmE), / ˈliːtʃi, ˈlɪtʃi / (BrE)
An evergreen tree native to China, cultivated in Asia for its edible, aromatic fruit, commonly known as lychee.
tree bearing the lychee fruit

Examples

The litchi tree in their garden produces fruit every summer.
Farmers in India have started cultivating more litchi for export.
The litchi prefers warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lychee - the edible fruit of the litchi tree

Etymology

From Cantonese 荔枝 (lai6 zi1), via Hindi līcī.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
