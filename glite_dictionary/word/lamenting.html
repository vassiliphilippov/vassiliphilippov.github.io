lamenting

1. ADJ

/ ləˈmɛn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ləˈmɛn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
expressing sorrow or grief through vocal sounds or resembling such an expression
expressing sorrow or grief vocally

Examples

The lamenting cries of the mourners echoed through the village.
A lamenting tone filled the singer's voice as she performed the elegy.
He spent many nights writing lamenting poems about his lost love.

Synset Examples

lamenting sinners
wailing mourners
the wailing wind
wailful bagpipes
tangle her desires with wailful sonnets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wailing - making a prolonged, high-pitched cry of pain, grief, or anger

Etymology

From Latin lamēntārī (to wail, lament).
