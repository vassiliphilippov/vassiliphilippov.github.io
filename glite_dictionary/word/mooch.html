mooch

1. NOUN

/ muːtʃ / (AmE), / muːtʃ / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who habitually tries to obtain things, especially food or money, from others without paying or offering anything in return
person who tries to get things for free
informal

Examples

He's such a mooch; he never pays for his own meals.
Every group of friends seems to have one mooch who always tags along for free.
I got tired of being a mooch and decided to buy my own coffee for a change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrounger - a person who borrows or lives at the expense of others
  • Synonym: freeloader - a person who takes advantage of others' generosity without giving anything in return

Etymology

Originated in late 19th century, from English dialect mouch (to pilfer), probably from Old French muchier (to hide).

2. VERB

/ muːtʃ / (AmE), / muːtʃ / (BrE)
to ask for and obtain something, especially food, money, or help, without paying or giving anything in return; to act as a parasite
ask for and get things for free
informal

Examples

He always tries to mooch free meals from his friends.
Stop trying to mooch off my hard work and do something yourself.
She managed to mooch a ride home after the concert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrounge - to obtain something, usually food or money, by seeking it from others for free
  • Synonym: to freeload - to take advantage of others for free food, drink or entertainment
  • Antonym: pay - to give money in exchange for something received

Etymology

Perhaps from Old French 'muchier' (to hide), later associated in the U.S. with freeloading or begging.
