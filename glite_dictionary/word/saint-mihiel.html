Saint-Mihiel

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt mɪˈhjɛl / (AmE), / sənt miːˈjɛl / (BrE)
a major battle in World War I (1918) during which American forces launched their first independent offensive in France as part of the Meuse-Argonne operation
World War I battle in France, 1918

Examples

The Battle of Saint-Mihiel marked a turning point for American troops in World War I.
Historians have studied the strategies used at Saint-Mihiel for decades.
Thousands of soldiers participated in the offensive at Saint-Mihiel in 1918.

Etymology

From the French name of the town Saint-Mihiel, northeastern France, where the battle occurred.
