backspin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌspɪn / (AmE), / ˈbæk.spɪn / (BrE)
a backward spin given to a ball that slows down or reverses its forward motion, especially in sports such as tennis or golf
backward spin on a moving ball

Examples

The tennis player used backspin to make the ball bounce lower and stay near the net.
A well-executed backspin can slow down a golf ball, preventing it from rolling past the hole.
The coach demonstrated how to apply backspin when serving in table tennis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: topspin - forward spin imparted to a ball
  • Synonym: spin - rotation imparted to a ball or object

Etymology

Formed from 'back' + 'spin'; influenced by analogy with 'topspin', both 20th century sporting terms.
