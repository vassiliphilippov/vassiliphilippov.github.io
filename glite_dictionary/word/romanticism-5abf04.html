romanticism

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈmæntɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæntɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
the quality or attitude of having impractical or unrealistic romantic ideals and feelings, especially about love or relationships
impractical romantic attitudes or ideals

Examples

Her romanticism made her believe in fairy-tale endings despite all evidence to the contrary.
The movie appeals to the romanticism of young audiences who dream of perfect love.
He criticized her romanticism as unrealistic and naive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealism - the pursuit of high or noble principles, often unrealistically
  • Antonym: realism - the attitude of accepting things as they are and dealing with them practically

Etymology

From French 'romantisme', from 'romantique' (romantic).

2. NOUN

/ roʊˈmæntɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / rəʊˈmantɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
the quality of being exciting, adventurous, and full of mystery, especially as associated with heroic times or adventures
exciting and mysterious quality

Examples

Many people are attracted to the romanticism of exploring uncharted territories.
The romanticism of old pirate legends still fascinates readers today.
There was a certain romanticism attached to the life of traveling artists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystique - a fascinating aura of mystery, power, or allure
  • Synonym: adventure - an unusual and exciting or daring experience
  • Antonym: prosaicism - the quality of being prosaic; lacking poetic beauty or excitement

Etymology

From French *romantisme*, from *romantique* (romantic), modeled after German *Romantik*.
