Vulpecula

1. PROPN

/ vʌlˈpɛkjələ / (AmE), / vʌlˈpɛkjʊlə / (BrE)
A faint constellation in the Northern Hemisphere, located near Cygnus and Sagitta.
constellation in the Northern Hemisphere

Examples

Vulpecula is best observed in the summer and early autumn evenings in the Northern Hemisphere.
The star Anser forms part of the small constellation Vulpecula.
Amateur astronomers often search for the Dumbbell Nebula, which lies within Vulpecula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cygnus - constellation in the Northern Hemisphere near Vulpecula
  • Synonym: Sagitta - constellation near Vulpecula and Cygnus

Etymology

From Latin 'vulpecula' meaning 'little fox'.
