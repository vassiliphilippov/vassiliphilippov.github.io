Sciaenidae

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈænɪˌdiː / (AmE), / sɪˈeɪənɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of warm-water marine fishes, including drums, grunts, croakers, and sea trout
family of warm-water marine fishes

Examples

The family Sciaenidae includes species commonly known as drums and croakers.
Many Sciaenidae are important both commercially and recreationally in coastal fisheries.
Researchers studied the distribution of Sciaenidae along the Atlantic coast.

Etymology

From modern Latin Sciaenidae, based on Sciaena, the type genus, from Greek skiaina, a kind of fish.
