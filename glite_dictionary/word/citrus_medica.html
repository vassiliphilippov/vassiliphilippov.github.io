Citrus medica

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtrəs ˈmɛdɪkə / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrəs ˈmɛdɪkə / (BrE)
a thorny evergreen small tree or shrub native to India, widely cultivated for its large, lemonlike fruits with a thick, warty rind
a citrus tree with thick-rinded fruits

Examples

The fruits of Citrus medica are prized for their aromatic rind, often used in candied peels.
Gardeners in warm climates may cultivate Citrus medica for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
Some traditional medicines incorporate extracts from the leaves and fruit of Citrus medica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citron - a large yellow citrus fruit with a thick rind from Citrus medica

Etymology

From Latin, genus and species names used in botanical classification.
