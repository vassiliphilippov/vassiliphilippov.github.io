Darlingtonia

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɑrlɪŋˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌdɑːlɪŋˈtəʊnɪə / (BrE)
A carnivorous plant native to California and Oregon, also known as the California pitcher plant, with tubular leaves that trap insects.
California pitcher plant; carnivorous plant species

Examples

The Darlingtonia thrives in boggy areas of northern California and southern Oregon.
Botanists are fascinated by how the Darlingtonia lures and traps its prey.
We visited a nature reserve famous for its rare Darlingtonia plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitcher plant - a type of carnivorous plant with pitcher-shaped leaves

Etymology

Named after William Darlington, an American botanist (1782–1863).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɑːrlɪŋˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌdɑːlɪŋˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of carnivorous plants consisting of a single species, the California pitcher plant, native to northern California and Oregon.
genus of carnivorous plants

Examples

The Darlingtonia is well known for its unique pitcher-shaped leaves that trap insects.
Botanists discovered a thriving population of Darlingtonia in the wetlands of California.
The Darlingtonia requires cool, flowing water to survive in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: California pitcher plant - a carnivorous plant species belonging to the genus Darlingtonia

Etymology

Named after American botanist William Darlington (1782–1863).
