Mon-Khmer

1. NOUN

/ mɑn kəˈmɛr / (AmE), / mɒn kəˈmɛː / (BrE)
a major branch of the Austro-Asiatic language family, including languages spoken in Southeast Asia such as Khmer and Mon
branch of Austro-Asiatic languages

Examples

The Mon-Khmer languages are spoken by millions across Southeast Asia.
Khmer, spoken in Cambodia, is the most widely spoken Mon-Khmer language.
Linguists study the similarities between Mon-Khmer languages and other Austro-Asiatic branches.

Etymology

From the names of the Mon and Khmer peoples, principal groups speaking languages of this branch.
