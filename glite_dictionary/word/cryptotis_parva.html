Cryptotis parva

1. NOUN

/ krɪpˈtoʊtɪs ˈpɑrvə / (AmE), / krɪpˈtəʊtɪs ˈpɑːvə / (BrE)
a small brown shrew found in grassy areas of the eastern United States, known commonly as the North American least shrew
species of small North American shrew

Examples

The Cryptotis parva is one of the smallest mammals native to North America.
Researchers observed a population of Cryptotis parva thriving in the protected grasslands.
Unlike other shrews, the Cryptotis parva rarely enters wooded areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrew - a small, mouselike mammal with a long pointed nose

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Cryptotis' (meaning 'hidden ear') + Latin 'parva' (small/few), used in scientific naming.
