Assyrian Neo-Aramaic

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪriən ˌnioʊˌærəˈmeɪɪk / (AmE), / əˈsɪəriən ˌniːəʊˌærəˈmeɪɪk / (BrE)
a modern dialect of Aramaic spoken primarily by Assyrian communities in northern Iraq and surrounding regions
modern Aramaic dialect of northern Iraq

Examples

Many families in northern Iraq speak Assyrian Neo-Aramaic at home and in community gatherings.
She learned to read and write in Assyrian Neo-Aramaic at her local church school.
Linguists have studied how Assyrian Neo-Aramaic has preserved elements of ancient Aramaic.

Etymology

Neo-Aramaic from 'neo-' meaning 'new' and 'Aramaic', reflecting its development from ancient Aramaic; 'Assyrian' refers to the modern ethnic group and historical region.
