family Chrysopidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kraɪˈsɒpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kraɪˈsɒpɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of delicate green insects known as green lacewings, whose larvae are important predators of pest insects.
family of green lacewings (insects)

Examples

Larvae of the family Chrysopidae are commonly used in biological pest control.
Members of the family Chrysopidae can be recognized by their transparent, net-like wings.
Many gardeners attract insects from the family Chrysopidae to help manage aphid populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green lacewing - delicate green insect, member of Chrysopidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin Chrysopidae, based on Greek khrūsos (gold) + ops (eye).
