ethnocentrism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛθ.noʊˈsɛn.trɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˌɛθ.nəʊˈsɛn.trɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the belief that one's own ethnic group or culture is superior to others
belief in superiority of one's own ethnic group

Examples

The documentary explored how ethnocentrism shapes people's perceptions of other cultures.
His ethnocentrism made it difficult for him to appreciate foreign customs.
Teachers are encouraged to discuss ethnocentrism to promote understanding among students from diverse backgrounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: xenophobia - fear or dislike of people from other countries or cultures
  • Antonym: cultural relativism - the principle of evaluating cultures based on their own values and standards

Etymology

Formed from Greek 'ethnos' (nation, people) + 'centrism' (centered on).
