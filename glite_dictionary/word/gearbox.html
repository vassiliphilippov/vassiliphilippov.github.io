gearbox

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪrˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈɡɪəˌbɒks / (BrE)
the metal casing or enclosure that holds and protects a set of gears in a machine or vehicle
case housing a set of gears

Examples

The mechanic opened the gearbox to check for worn gears and misplaced lubricant.
Rain and dirt are kept out of the gearbox by a tightly sealed metal cover.
A damaged gearbox can cause serious issues in the operation of industrial equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transmission - the system of gears by which power is transmitted from an engine to the wheels of a vehicle

Etymology

From 'gear' + 'box', combining the terms for mechanical gears and a protective enclosure.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪrˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈɡɪəˌbɒks / (BrE)
a metal casing that contains a train of gears, typically forming part of the transmission system in vehicles or machinery
metal casing housing gears

Examples

The mechanic opened the gearbox to check for worn gears.
A faulty gearbox can cause problems shifting between speeds.
Modern cars often have an automatic gearbox for ease of driving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transmission - mechanism by which power is transmitted from an engine to the wheels

Etymology

From 'gear' (a mechanical part) + 'box' (an enclosure), dating to the late 19th century with the development of mechanical transmissions.
