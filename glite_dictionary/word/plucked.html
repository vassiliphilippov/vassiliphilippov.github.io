plucked

1. ADJ

/ plʌkt / (AmE), / plʌkt / (BrE)
(of a stringed instrument or its sound) produced by pulling and releasing the strings with the fingers or a plectrum, rather than by bowing or striking.
sounded by pulling strings with fingers or plectrum

Examples

The guitar belongs to the family of plucked string instruments.
She preferred the plucked sound of the harp to the bowed tones of the violin.
Many traditional Asian instruments produce music using plucked techniques.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bowed - (of a stringed instrument or sound) produced by drawing a bow across the strings

2. ADJ

/ plʌkt / (AmE), / plʌkt / (BrE)
(of a bird or animal) having had its feathers removed, especially in preparation for cooking.
bird or animal with feathers removed

Examples

The chef placed the plucked chicken on the cutting board to prepare it for roasting.
At the market, you can buy either plucked or unplucked geese, depending on your needs.
She learned how to properly process a plucked duck before making her grandmother’s soup recipe.

Synset Examples

a plucked chicken
a plucked goose

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unplucked - (of a bird) not having had its feathers removed
