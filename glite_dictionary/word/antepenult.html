antepenult

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˈpɛnʌlt / (AmE), / ˌantɪˈpɛnl̩t / (BrE)
the third syllable from the end of a word
third-to-last syllable of a word

Examples

In the word 'banana,' the antepenult is the first syllable, 'ba-.'
Some languages require stress to fall on the antepenult of the word.
She asked students to underline the antepenult in each example.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: penult - the second-to-last syllable of a word
  • Degree Higher: ultima - the last syllable of a word

Etymology

From Latin 'antepaenultima' (the one before the penult), from 'ante-' (before) + 'paenultima' (next to last).
