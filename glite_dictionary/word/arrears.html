arrears

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪrz / (AmE), / əˈrɪəz / (BrE)
money that is owed and should have been paid earlier; the state of being behind with payments.
money overdue or unpaid debts

Examples

Because his loan was in arrears, the bank sent him several warning letters.
The company was struggling with salaries that were months in arrears.
She paid the rent in arrears after finally receiving her paycheck.

Synset Examples

an account in arrears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debt - money that is owed or due
  • Antonym: advance - payment made ahead of time

Etymology

From Old French arere (behind), ultimately from Latin ad retro (to the rear).

2. NOUN

/ əˈrɪrz / (AmE), / əˈrɪəz / (BrE)
money that is owed and should have been paid earlier; an overdue debt.
overdue debt or money owed

Examples

After missing two payments, the loan was in arrears and subject to late fees.
The company has been threatened with legal action due to arrears in rent.
Her account showed several months of arrears, requiring immediate payment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debt - a sum of money that is owed or due
  • Antonym: advance - money paid before it is due

Etymology

From Old French arere (behind, overdue), based on Latin ad retro (to the rear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13418616-n
Synset POS: n
