Ralph Waldo Emerson

1. PROPN

/ ˈrælf ˌwɔlˌdoʊ ˈɛmɚsən / (AmE), / ˈrælf ˈwɔːldəʊ ˈɛməsən / (BrE)
(1803–1882) A United States writer, philosopher, and leading figure of transcendentalism, known for his essays, lectures, and poetry.
American transcendentalist writer and philosopher

Examples

Ralph Waldo Emerson was a central figure in the American transcendentalist movement of the 19th century.
Many students read essays by Ralph Waldo Emerson in their literature classes.
The philosophical writings of Ralph Waldo Emerson continue to inspire readers today.
