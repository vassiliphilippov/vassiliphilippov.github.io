Saint Edward the Martyr

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈɛdwərd ðə ˈmɑr.tɪr / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈɛdwəd ðə ˈmɑː.tɪə / (BrE)
King of England from 975 to 978, known for his early death and later veneration as a martyr; son of Edgar and half-brother to Ethelred II.
King of England (975–978), Christian martyr

Examples

Saint Edward the Martyr was canonized for his piety and tragic death.
Many legends grew around the untimely death of Saint Edward the Martyr.
Pilgrims visited the shrine of Saint Edward the Martyr in medieval England.
