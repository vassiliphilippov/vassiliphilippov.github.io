Cheloniidae

1. NOUN

/ kɪˈloʊni.aɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kɪˌlɒnɪˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, mostly marine turtles that includes green turtles and hawksbills.
family of marine turtles

Examples

The Cheloniidae family contains several species of sea turtles found in warm oceans worldwide.
Scientists study Cheloniidae to better understand the behavior and migration patterns of marine turtles.
Some members of the Cheloniidae family, like the green turtle, are endangered due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Testudinidae - family of land tortoises

Etymology

From Greek 'chelōnē' (tortoise) + -idae, a suffix for animal family names.

2. NOUN

/ kəˌloʊniˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / kɪˌləʊniˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine turtles, including the green turtles and hawksbills.
family of marine turtles

Examples

The Cheloniidae are distinguished from other sea turtles by the structure of their shells.
Researchers have observed members of the Cheloniidae family nesting on tropical beaches around the world.
Conservation efforts for Cheloniidae are vital due to threats from habitat loss and pollution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Testudinidae - family of land tortoises
  • Antonym: Dermochelyidae - family of leatherback sea turtles

Etymology

From New Latin Cheloniidae, from Chelonia (a genus of turtles) + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
