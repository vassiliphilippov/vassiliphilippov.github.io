weirdness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪrd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪəd.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being very strange or unusual, especially in a way that attracts attention; strangeness.
quality of being strange or unusual

Examples

The weirdness of his behavior made everyone feel uncomfortable.
She embraced her weirdness and encouraged others to be themselves.
Some films are remembered for their sheer weirdness and ability to surprise audiences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangeness - the quality of being strange or unfamiliar
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal; conformity to the standard or common type

Etymology

From 'weird' (strange, uncanny) + '-ness' (forming nouns of quality or state).
