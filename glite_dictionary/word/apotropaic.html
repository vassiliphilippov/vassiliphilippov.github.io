apotropaic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæpəˈtroʊpeɪɪk / (AmE), / ˌapəˈtrəʊpeɪɪk / (BrE)
having the power to ward off evil or bad luck; intended to avert harm or misfortune
meant to ward off evil or misfortune

Examples

Many ancient cultures created apotropaic charms to protect themselves from misfortune.
The doorway was decorated with apotropaic symbols to ward off evil spirits.
Scholars often study apotropaic rituals in the context of religious history.

Etymology

From Greek apotropaios (averting evil), from apotrepein (to turn away).
