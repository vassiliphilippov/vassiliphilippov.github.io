Troglodytidae

1. NOUN

/ troʊˌɡlɑːdəˈtaɪdiː / (AmE), / trɒɡˌlɒdɪˈtiːdiː / (BrE)
the family of small, mostly brown passerine birds known as wrens, found chiefly in the Americas
the biological family of wrens (birds)

Examples

The Troglodytidae family includes more than 80 species of wrens worldwide.
Researchers observed nesting behavior unique to Troglodytidae in Central American forests.
Birdwatchers often hope to spot members of the Troglodytidae family during spring migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wren - any small, active songbird of the family Troglodytidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'trōglodutēs' (one who creeps into holes), referencing the birds' nesting habits.
