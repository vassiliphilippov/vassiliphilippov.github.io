Oryza

1. NOUN

/ ɔˈraɪzə / (AmE), / ɒˈrɪzə / (BrE)
A genus of grasses that includes the rice plant.
genus including rice

Examples

The genus Oryza includes several species widely cultivated in Asia.
Scientists have mapped the genome of Oryza to improve rice yield and resistance.
Wild relatives of Oryza are being studied for their potential in crop improvement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rice - the seed or plant of Oryza sativa, a staple grain

Etymology

From Latin *Oryza* (rice), from Greek *oryza* (rice).

2. NOUN

/ əˈraɪzə / (AmE), / əˈrɪzə / (BrE)
a genus of grasses that includes the cultivated rice plant and its wild relatives
genus of grasses including rice

Examples

Botanists classify cultivated rice under the genus Oryza.
The wild species within Oryza have been important for genetic research.
Several varieties in the Oryza genus are native to Asia and Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rice - the cereal grain harvested from plants of the genus Oryza

Etymology

From Latin Oryza (rice), from Greek oryza (rice).
