prolusory

1. ADJ

/ proʊˈluːsəri / (AmE), / prəˈluːsəri / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a prolusion; introductory or preliminary in nature
preliminary or introductory
archaic

Examples

The professor gave a prolusory speech before beginning the main lecture.
Her prolusory remarks set the tone for the ceremony.
The book opens with a prolusory chapter outlining the themes to come.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prolusion - a preliminary or introductory act, essay, or exercise

Etymology

From Latin prolusorius, from prolusio (preliminary exercise).
