Julia Ward Howe

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒuːliə wɔrd haʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːliə wɔːd haʊ / (BrE)
(1819–1910) An American feminist, writer, and social reformer known for her work in the women's suffrage movement and for writing 'The Battle Hymn of the Republic.'
American feminist and women's rights activist

Examples

Julia Ward Howe played a key role in advocating for women's right to vote in the United States.
The famous song 'The Battle Hymn of the Republic' was written by Julia Ward Howe.
Many historians credit Julia Ward Howe with advancing the cause of gender equality in the 19th century.
