presumably

1. ADV

/ prɪˈzjuməbli / (AmE), / prɪˈzjuːməbli / (BrE)
Used to say that you think something is likely or reasonably assumed to be true, although you are not certain.
likely assumed to be true

Examples

Presumably, the meeting has been postponed until next week.
He had gone out, presumably to get something to eat.
Their flight was delayed, presumably due to bad weather.

Synset Examples

presumably, he missed the train

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probably - likely to happen or be the case
  • Antonym: certainly - without any doubt

Etymology

From Latin 'praesumere' (to suppose), via Medieval Latin 'praesumibilis' and Middle English.
