cottonmouth

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːtənˌmaʊθ / (AmE), / ˈkɒtənˌmaʊθ / (BrE)
A venomous, semiaquatic snake of the pit viper family, native to swamps and waterways in the southern United States.
venomous snake of southern US wetlands

Examples

We saw a cottonmouth basking on a log near the swamp's edge.
Be careful when fishing in these waters, as cottonmouths are common here.
The inside of a cottonmouth's mouth is bright white, which is how it got its name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water moccasin - common name for the same species, the cottonmouth snake

Etymology

Named for the white, cotton-like lining inside its mouth, visible when it opens its jaws in threat.
