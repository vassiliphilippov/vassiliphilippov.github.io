nanomachine

1. NOUN

/ ˈnænoʊ məˌʃin / (AmE), / ˈnanəʊ məˌʃiːn / (BrE)
A microscopic machine or device that operates at or near the scale of nanometres, typically designed to perform specific tasks at the molecular or atomic level.
microscopic machine at nanometre scale

Examples

Scientists are developing nanomachines that can travel through the bloodstream to deliver drugs to precise locations.
The intricate gears inside the nanomachine are invisible to the naked eye but crucial to its function.
One day, nanomachines could be used to repair damaged cells directly within the human body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nanobot - A tiny robot with functions at nanometre scale

Etymology

From 'nano-' (denoting a factor of one billionth, from Greek 'nanos' meaning dwarf) + 'machine'.
