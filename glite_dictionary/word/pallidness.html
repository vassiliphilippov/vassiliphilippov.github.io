pallidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpælɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈpælɪdnɪs / (BrE)
An unnatural lack of color in the skin, often caused by illness, shock, or emotional distress.
lack of color in the skin

Examples

Everyone noticed the pallidness of his face after he received the bad news.
The doctor was concerned by the sudden pallidness in her patient's complexion.
His pallidness made it clear he was not feeling well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pallor - unnatural paleness, especially of the face

Etymology

From Latin 'pallidus' meaning pale.
