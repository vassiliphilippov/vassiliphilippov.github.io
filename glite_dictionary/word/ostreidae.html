Ostreidae

1. NOUN

/ ɑːˈstriːɪdiː / (AmE), / ɒˈstriːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine bivalve mollusks commonly known as oysters.
family of marine oysters

Examples

Researchers examined fossils belonging to the Ostreidae family to better understand oyster evolution.
The Ostreidae are widely distributed in shallow marine waters across the globe.
Many commercial oysters farmed for food come from the Ostreidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oyster - a bivalve mollusk, commonly eaten as food

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'ostrakon' (shell).
