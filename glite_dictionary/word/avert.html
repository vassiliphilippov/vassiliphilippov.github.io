to avert

1. VERB

/ əˈvɝt / (AmE), / əˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to prevent something bad or undesirable from happening; to ward off or turn away
prevent something bad from happening

Examples

She took quick action to avert disaster during the storm.
Strong policies can avert an economic crisis.
He looked away to avert his eyes from the disturbing scene.

Synset Examples

Let's avoid a confrontation
head off a confrontation
avert a strike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prevent - to stop something from happening or existing
  • Synonym: to avoid - to keep away from or stop oneself from doing something

Etymology

From Latin 'avertĕre' meaning 'to turn away'.

2. VERB

/ əˈvɝt / (AmE), / əˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to turn away or aside (one's eyes, thoughts, etc.), especially to avoid something unpleasant or unwanted
turn away or aside

Examples

She quickly averted her gaze when he caught her staring.
He averted his eyes from the gruesome scene on the screen.
They averted their faces as the sun rose over the hills.

Synset Examples

They averted their eyes when the King entered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn away - to move something so that it faces in another direction

Etymology

From Latin 'avertere' (to turn away), from 'ab-' (away) + 'vertere' (to turn).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02038356-v
Synset POS: v
