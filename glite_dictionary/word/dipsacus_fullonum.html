Dipsacus fullonum

1. NOUN

/ dɪpˈsækəs fʊˈloʊnəm / (AmE), / dɪpˈsakəs fʊˈlɒnəm / (BrE)
a species of teasel with lilac flowers, originally from the Old World and naturalized in North America; its dried flower heads are used for raising a nap on woolen cloth
a species of teasel plant used in textile finishing

Examples

The dried heads of Dipsacus fullonum were once widely used in the textile industry for raising the nap on cloth.
Dipsacus fullonum can often be found growing in meadows and along roadsides across Europe and North America.
Botanists studied the ecological impact of Dipsacus fullonum as it spread beyond its native range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teasel - any plant of the genus Dipsacus, known for its prickly heads used in textile processing

Etymology

From the genus name Dipsacus (from Greek dipsakos, 'thirsty') and Neo-Latin species name fullonum, from Latin fullō ('fuller of cloth').
