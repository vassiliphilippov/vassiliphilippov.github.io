Holarrhena pubescens

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˌlɑːˈriːnə pjuːˈbɛsəns / (AmE), / həʊˌlarˈriːnə pjuːˈbɛsəns / (BrE)
A tropical Asian tree with hard white wood and bark that was formerly used medicinally to treat dysentery and diarrhea.
tropical Asian medicinal tree
medical

Examples

The bark of Holarrhena pubescens was once widely used in traditional medicine to treat digestive issues.
Researchers are studying the chemical compounds found in Holarrhena pubescens for their potential health benefits.
In some Asian countries, Holarrhena pubescens trees are still valued for both their wood and medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, combining the genus name Holarrhena and the Latin 'pubescens' meaning 'becoming covered with down'.
