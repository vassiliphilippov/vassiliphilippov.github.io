overtone

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɚˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌtəʊn / (BrE)
an implicit or underlying quality, meaning, or suggestion often felt in addition to the obvious or literal one
implicit or underlying quality or meaning

Examples

Her speech had political overtones that were hard to miss.
There were sinister overtones to his comments at the meeting.
The novel's dialogue carries emotional overtones beyond the surface story.

Synset Examples

overtones of despair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuance - a subtle distinction or variation in meaning, expression, or tone
  • Synonym: undertone - a subtle or underlying quality or feeling; not openly stated

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌtəʊn / (BrE)
a musical tone that is a higher frequency, usually an exact multiple of the fundamental frequency, and that sounds above the basic note
a harmonic frequency above the fundamental

Examples

The violin string vibrates to produce both a fundamental and an overtone.
By adjusting her breath, the singer was able to isolate a clear overtone above the main note.
Different shapes of musical instruments affect which overtones are more prominent in their sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmonic - a component frequency of a sound, an integer multiple of the fundamental

Etymology

From 'over-' meaning above + 'tone'.
