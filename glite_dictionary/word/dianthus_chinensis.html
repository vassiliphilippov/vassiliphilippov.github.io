Dianthus chinensis

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈænθəs tʃɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / daɪˈænθəs tʃɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a species of flowering plant, commonly known as Chinese pink, with deeply toothed rose-lilac flowers and a purplish eye, often grown as an annual.
species of flowering plant (Chinese pink)

Examples

The vibrant blooms of Dianthus chinensis add a splash of color to many gardens in summer.
Gardeners often plant Dianthus chinensis as a border to highlight their flowerbeds.
I bought a packet of Dianthus chinensis seeds to experiment with annual flowering varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinks - flowers of the genus Dianthus, including the carnation and sweet william

Etymology

From Latin: 'Dianthus' (from Greek dios 'god' + anthos 'flower'), and 'chinensis,' meaning 'from China.'
