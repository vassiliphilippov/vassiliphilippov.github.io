churrigueresque

1. ADJ

/ tʃʊrɪgəˈrɛsk / (AmE), / tʃʊərɪgəˈrɛsk / (BrE)
characterized by highly elaborate and symmetrical ornamental detail, especially in architecture; in the style of the Spanish architect Churriguera.
elaborately ornate architectural style

Examples

The cathedral's churrigueresque facade dazzled visitors with its intricate carvings and detailed stonework.
Several Spanish churches display the churrigueresque style, renowned for its flamboyant sculptures and lavish ornamentation.
The museum showcased models of churrigueresque architecture from the early 18th century.

Synset Examples

the building … frantically baroque

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baroque - highly ornate and extravagant style, especially in art and architecture

Etymology

From Spanish *churrigueresco*, after architect José Benito de Churriguera (1665–1725).
