Gadus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪdəs / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪdəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fishes in the family Gadidae, including the typical codfishes such as the Atlantic cod.
genus of codfishes

Examples

The Atlantic cod, Gadus morhua, is an important species for commercial fisheries.
Researchers studied several species within the genus Gadus to understand their migratory patterns.
The Gadus genus includes some of the world's most widely consumed fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - a common fish of the genus Gadus, especially the Atlantic cod

Etymology

From Latin 'gadus' (a fish, cod).
