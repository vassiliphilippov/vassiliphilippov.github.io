saddle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæd.əl / (AmE), / ˈsæd.əl / (BrE)
A piece of leather or other material across the instep of a shoe, often as a decorative or supportive feature.
leather band across shoe instep

Examples

The classic Oxford shoe is distinguished by a black saddle across the instep.
Her new golf shoes feature a white saddle on brown leather.
The repaired saddle restored the vintage look of the loafers.

Etymology

From Old English 'sadol' meaning seat (for a horse), extended to describe similar shapes or parts in other contexts.
