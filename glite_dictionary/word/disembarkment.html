disembarkment

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.ɛmˈbɑrk.mənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɛmˈbɑːk.mənt / (BrE)
the act of passengers and crew getting off a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle
the act of getting off a vehicle

Examples

The disembarkment of passengers was delayed due to security checks at the port.
After a long journey, the disembarkment process at the airport went smoothly.
The tour guide explained the procedures for disembarkment before the cruise docked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disembarkation - the act of leaving a ship or aircraft
  • Antonym: embarkation - the act of boarding a ship, aircraft, etc.

Etymology

From disembark + -ment, patterned after embarkment.
