Marines

1. NOUN

/ məˈriːnz / (AmE), / məˈriːnz / (BrE)
A branch of the United States armed forces trained for amphibious and expeditionary warfare, especially as part of the Navy.
branch of U.S. military trained for amphibious warfare
american

Examples

The Marines were called in to secure the area after the landing.
My cousin served in the United States Marines for four years.
The president praised the bravery of the Marines during the rescue mission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: navy - the branch of a nation's armed services that conducts military operations at sea
  • Antonym: army - the main land-based military force

Etymology

From marine, meaning 'of the sea', influenced by French 'marines' (naval infantry), reflecting the force's naval origins.

2. NOUN

/ məˈrinz / (AmE), / məˈriːnz / (BrE)
A branch of a country's armed forces trained to serve both on land and at sea, often involved in amphibious military operations.
branch of armed forces serving on land and sea

Examples

The Marines were deployed to the coastal region for the humanitarian mission.
My uncle served in the Marines for ten years and was stationed overseas.
The Marines are known for their rigorous training and discipline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soldier - a person who serves in an army

Etymology

From French 'marin' (of the sea); influenced by Latin 'marinus' (of the sea).
