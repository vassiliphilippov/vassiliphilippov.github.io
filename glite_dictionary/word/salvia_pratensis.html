Salvia pratensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæl.vi.ə prəˈtɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌsal.vi.ə praˈten.sɪs / (BrE)
A tall perennial plant of the mint family, native to Europe and western Asia, with violet-blue flowers, commonly found in open grasslands; also called meadow clary or meadow sage.
a violet-blue flowered perennial plant

Examples

In the meadow, patches of Salvia pratensis added vibrant blue color during the summer.
Gardeners often plant Salvia pratensis to attract bees and butterflies.
The distinctive leaves of Salvia pratensis make it easy to identify among other wildflowers.

Etymology

From Latin: 'salvia' meaning sage and 'pratensis' meaning 'of the meadow'.
