burr

1. NOUN

/ bɜr / (AmE), / bɜː / (BrE)
A rough edge or small piece of material left on a workpiece after it has been cut, drilled, or machined.
rough edge left after cutting or drilling

Examples

After cutting the metal sheet, a small burr was left along the edge.
You should always remove any burrs to ensure a smooth surface.
The technician used a file to smooth down the burr created by the drill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridge - a raised strip, often left on a surface after machining
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ bɜr / (AmE), / bɜː / (BrE)
to remove burrs (sharp edges or roughness) from an object, especially metal, usually after cutting or drilling
remove sharp edges from metal, etc.

Examples

Before painting the frame, make sure to burr all the cut edges thoroughly.
The machinist carefully burred each hole to ensure a smooth finish.
It's important to burr the metal after drilling to prevent injuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deburr - to remove burrs from (a workpiece, especially metal)

3. NOUN

/ bɝː / (AmE), / bɜː / (BrE)
A small rotary cutting tool, typically with a roughened surface, used for smoothing or shaping rough edges left on metal, wood, or other materials.
rotary file for smoothing edges

Examples

The machinist used a burr to smooth the sharp edges on the metal part.
Dentists often rely on a burr when shaping a patient's tooth during procedures.
After cutting the pipe, he removed the rough area with a rotary burr.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: file - Tool used to smooth or shape materials by abrasion
  • Synonym: grinder - Machine or device for grinding or smoothing surfaces

Etymology

From Middle English *burre*, referring to something rough or prickly; in this sense, likely related to the tool's rough-cutting surface.

4. NOUN

/ bɜr / (AmE), / bɜː / (BrE)
a rough or prickly seed vessel or fruit that clings to animal fur or clothing
prickly seed vessel or fruit

Examples

The dog came back from the field with several burrs stuck in its fur.
As we hiked through the woods, I picked a burr off my sock nearly every mile.
Some plants develop a burr to help their seeds spread by attaching to animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bur - alternative spelling of 'burr' for a prickly seed case

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *burre*.

5. NOUN

/ bɝː / (AmE), / bɜː / (BrE)
a small, rotating cutting tool used in dentistry or surgery to remove tissue or cut bone
small rotary surgical or dental tool
medical

Examples

The dentist selected a fine burr to carefully shape the cavity before filling it.
During the procedure, the surgeon changed the burr on the drill to a smaller size.
After extensive use, the burr became dull and needed replacement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bur - alternate spelling for the same dental or surgical tool

Etymology

From Middle English burre, originally referring to something rough or prickly; adapted in technical contexts for the tool's rough or cutting nature.
