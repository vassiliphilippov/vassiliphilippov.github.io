drained

1. ADJ

/ dreɪnd / (AmE), / dreɪnd / (BrE)
(of a battery or device) having lost its electric charge; discharged and incapable of providing power until recharged.
having lost electric charge (of a battery)

Examples

After hours of continuous use, my phone's battery was completely drained.
The flashlight won't work because the batteries are drained.
Make sure the device is fully charged before use so it doesn't become drained during operation.

Synset Examples

a dead battery
left the lights on and came back to find the battery drained

Related Terms

  • Antonym: charged - (of a battery or device) containing an electric charge and able to provide power
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ dreɪnd / (AmE), / dreɪnd / (BrE)
very tired or exhausted, having no energy left, especially after physical or emotional effort.
extremely tired; exhausted

Examples

After running the marathon, I felt completely drained and could hardly stand.
She was so drained from work that she fell asleep on the sofa.
The long and stressful meeting left everyone mentally drained.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhausted - very tired or depleted of energy
  • Antonym: refreshed - full of energy; feeling rested

Etymology

From the verb 'drain', meaning to remove liquid from; figurative sense from the 18th century.

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ dreɪnd / (AmE), / dreɪnd / (BrE)
(of a container, place, or person) emptied of liquid or exhausted of energy or resources.
emptied of liquid or exhausted

Examples

After the hike, I felt completely drained and needed to rest.
The pond was drained to clean out all the mud and debris.
By the end of the semester, most students were mentally drained from exams and assignments.

Synset Examples

a drained marsh
a drained tank
a drained and apathetic old man … not caring any longer about anything

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhausted - extremely tired; depleted of energy
  • Antonym: refreshed - feeling re-energized or full of energy again

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'drain', from Old French 'dreiner', from Latin 'draganare'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01092170-a
Synset POS: a
