Staph epi

1. NOUN

/ stæf ˈɛpi / (AmE), / stæf ˈɛpi / (BrE)
an informal abbreviation for Staphylococcus epidermidis, a type of bacteria commonly found on human skin and a frequent cause of infections, especially in hospitals
abbreviation for Staphylococcus epidermidis bacterium
medical informal

Examples

The hospital lab identified Staph epi in the blood culture.
Some antibiotics are less effective against Staph epi infections.
Doctors routinely monitor catheters for signs of Staph epi contamination.

Synset Examples

Infections caused by Staph epi can be quite serious.
Doctors tested the sample for Staph epi to identify the bacteria.
Staph epi is often found on our skin without causing harm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Staphylococcus epidermidis - a species of bacteria commonly found on human skin

Etymology

Shortened from Staphylococcus epidermidis (from Greek 'staphyle' meaning 'bunch of grapes' and 'epidermidis' relating to the skin).
