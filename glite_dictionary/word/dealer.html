dealer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːlər / (AmE), / ˈdiːlə / (BrE)
a person who buys and sells illegal or illicit goods, especially drugs or stolen items
person selling illegal goods

Examples

The police arrested the suspected dealer during a late-night raid downtown.
He was known in the neighborhood as a dealer who sold stolen electronics.
Authorities are cracking down on dealer networks operating in the city.

Synset Examples

a dealer in stolen goods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trafficker - a person who buys and sells illegal goods, especially drugs or people
  • Synonym: pusher - a person who sells illegal drugs
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10741119-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈdiːlər / (AmE), / ˈdiːlə / (BrE)
the person who distributes the playing cards to players in a card game
person who deals cards in a game

Examples

It's your turn to be the dealer and shuffle the cards.
The dealer began distributing cards to each player at the poker table.
The rules state that the dealer changes after every round.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: player - person taking part in a card game
  • Synonym: croupier - casino official dealing cards at gaming tables

Etymology

From deal (to distribute cards) + -er (agent suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10016174-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈdiːlɚ / (AmE), / ˈdiːlə / (BrE)
a person or company that buys and sells securities, currencies, or goods as a principal, especially on a stock exchange, trading for their own account rather than on behalf of clients.
principal trader on a financial market

Examples

The dealer made significant profits by trading stocks on their own account.
Every major investment bank employs at least one dealer to manage market risks.
The dealer quoted both buy and sell prices for each security.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: broker - an agent who buys or sells for others in financial markets

Etymology

From Middle English delare, from deel (“to deal”) + -er.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10494031-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈdiːlɚ / (AmE), / ˈdiːlə / (BrE)
a person or business that buys and keeps an inventory of goods for the purpose of selling them
person who buys and sells goods

Examples

The antique dealer had a shop full of rare and valuable items.
Car dealers across town are offering special discounts this weekend.
As a dealer in fine art, she attends many international auctions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merchant - a person or company involved in wholesale trade
  • Synonym: buyer - a person who purchases goods or services
  • Synonym: retailer - a person or business that sells goods directly to consumers

Etymology

From Middle English 'deler', from Old English 'dǣlan' (to divide, share), related to 'deal'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10740102-n
Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ ˈdiːlɚ / (AmE), / ˈdiːlə / (BrE)
a company or business that buys and sells particular goods or assets, especially as a principal rather than as an agent
a business that buys and sells goods

Examples

The art dealer specializes in rare paintings from the 19th century.
Our company is the largest automobile dealer in the state.
Many investors buy gold from a certified bullion dealer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trader - a person or firm that buys and sells goods, currencies, or stocks

Etymology

From deal (to distribute or trade) + -er (agent noun suffix); first used in Middle English.

Source: WordNet
