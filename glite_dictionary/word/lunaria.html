Lunaria

1. NOUN

/ luːˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / ljuːˈnɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering plants in the mustard family, native to Europe and known for the species 'honesty' (Lunaria annua) with distinctive translucent seed pods.
genus of European flowering plants

Examples

Lunaria is often grown in gardens for its attractive, coin-shaped seed pods.
The tall purple flowers of Lunaria brighten up the spring border.
Many people dry the seed pods of Lunaria for use in floral arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honesty - a common name for Lunaria annua, a species in the Lunaria genus

Etymology

From Latin 'lunaria', meaning 'moon-shaped', referring to the shape of the seed pods.

2. NOUN

/ luˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / lʊˈnɑːrɪə / (BrE)
a small genus of European herbs in the cabbage family, including the plant commonly known as 'honesty', valued for its decorative seed pods and purple flowers
a genus of herbs in the cabbage family

Examples

The garden was filled with Lunaria, their silvery seed pods shining in the sunlight.
Many gardeners plant Lunaria for its attractive flowers and unusual decorative value.
Lunaria is commonly known as "honesty" and is prized for use in dried flower arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honesty - a plant in the genus Lunaria, known for its translucent, disc-shaped seed pods

Etymology

From Latin 'lunaria', derived from 'luna' meaning 'moon', referring to the round, silvery seed pods.
