coprolite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.prəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒ.prəˌlaɪt / (BrE)
Fossilized excrement, especially of prehistoric animals, preserved in petrified form.
fossilized dung

Examples

Paleontologists discovered a large coprolite alongside dinosaur bones at the dig site.
Analyzing a coprolite can reveal information about ancient animals' diets.
The museum display included a rare coprolite from the Jurassic period.

Etymology

From Greek 'kopros' (dung) + 'lithos' (stone).
