hurdy-gurdy

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɝːdi ˌɡɝːdi / (AmE), / ˈhɜːdi ˌɡɜːdi / (BrE)
a stringed musical instrument that produces sound by turning a crank attached to a wheel which rubs against the strings, often with keys to change pitch
mechanical stringed musical instrument

Examples

He played a hurdy-gurdy on the street corner, delighting the passersby with its unusual sound.
The medieval festival featured a musician performing with a hurdy-gurdy.
She bought a vintage hurdy-gurdy to add to her collection of rare instruments.

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced, first recorded in English in the 18th century.
