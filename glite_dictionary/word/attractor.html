attractor

1. NOUN

/ əˈtræktər / (AmE), / əˈtræktə / (BrE)
a feature or characteristic that draws attention, interest, or pleasure, causing people, animals, or things to be attracted to it
feature that draws attention or interest

Examples

The colorful flowers act as a strong attractor for pollinators in the garden.
Free food is often used as an attractor at community events to increase attendance.
The museum's new exhibit serves as an attractor for tourists and art enthusiasts.

Synset Examples

flowers are an attractor for bees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attraction - something that attracts or draws interest

Etymology

From attract + -or, patterned after Latin agent nouns ending in -or.

3. NOUN

/ əˈtræktər / (AmE), / əˈtræktə / (BrE)
(physics) A set of states in a dynamical system towards which the system tends to evolve, regardless of the starting conditions.
physics: state a system evolves toward

Examples

In chaos theory, a strange attractor can create complex, unpredictable behavior within deterministic systems.
The system eventually settles into a stable attractor regardless of its initial state.
Scientists simulated the weather patterns to identify the underlying attractor in the model.

Etymology

From Latin 'attrahere' meaning 'to draw towards'.
