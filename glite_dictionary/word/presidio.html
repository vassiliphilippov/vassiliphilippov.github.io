presidio

1. NOUN

/ prəˈsiːdiˌoʊ / (AmE), / prɛˈsɪdiəʊ / (BrE)
a fortress or military outpost, especially one established by the Spanish in the southwestern United States to protect their missions and settlements
Spanish colonial fortress or outpost

Examples

The old Spanish presidio in Tucson is now a historical museum.
Many cities in California have their origins as a presidio or mission complex.
Soldiers stationed at the presidio defended the nearby settlements from attacks.

Synset Examples

Tucson was first settled as a walled presidio

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fort - a fortified building or military stronghold
  • Degree Lower: mission - a building or group of buildings for religious and administrative purposes by Spanish colonists

Etymology

From Spanish presido, from Latin praesidium (garrison, protection).
