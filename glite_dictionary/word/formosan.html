Formosan

1. ADJ

/ fɔrˈmoʊsən / (AmE), / fɔːˈməʊsən / (BrE)
relating to Taiwan (formerly called Formosa), its people, or their languages
relating to Taiwan or its people/language

Examples

Many indigenous groups in Taiwan are known collectively as Formosan peoples.
The museum featured a collection of Formosan artifacts from the 19th century.
Several unique Formosan languages are spoken by Taiwan's indigenous communities.

Synset Examples

the Taiwanese capital is Taipeh

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taiwanese - of or relating to Taiwan, its people, or culture

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Formosa' (meaning 'beautiful'), a former name for Taiwan.

Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ fɔrˈmoʊsən / (AmE), / fɔːˈməʊsən / (BrE)
the group of Austronesian languages spoken by the indigenous peoples of Taiwan (Formosa)
Austronesian languages of Taiwan

Examples

Scholars have identified at least sixteen distinct Formosan languages still spoken by Taiwan's indigenous communities.
Many Formosan languages are at risk of extinction due to declining numbers of speakers.
The Formosan language family is crucial for understanding the origins of the Austronesian peoples.

Etymology

From Formosa, the former name for Taiwan, from Portuguese 'Ilha Formosa' meaning 'beautiful island'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06953463-n
Synset POS: n
