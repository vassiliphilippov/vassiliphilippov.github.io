Daphne

1. PROPN

/ ˈdæfni / (AmE), / ˈdæfni / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a nymph who was transformed into a laurel tree to escape the pursuit of Apollo.
mythological nymph; turned into a laurel tree

Examples

According to the legend, Daphne was transformed into a laurel tree to escape Apollo's advances.
The story of Daphne has inspired countless works of art, especially in sculpture and painting.
Many poets have used Daphne's tale as a symbol of chastity and pursuit in their writings.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Δάφνη (Dáphnē), meaning 'laurel'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdæfni / (AmE), / ˈdæfni / (BrE)
a genus of mostly evergreen Eurasian shrubs, often cultivated for their fragrant flowers and attractive foliage
genus of Eurasian evergreen shrubs

Examples

The Daphne in my garden fills the air with its sweet scent every spring.
Some species of Daphne are highly prized by gardeners for their winter blooms.
Botanists have classified over 70 species in the genus Daphne.

Etymology

From Latin Daphne, from Ancient Greek Δάφνη (Dáphnē), meaning 'laurel', after the mythological nymph.
