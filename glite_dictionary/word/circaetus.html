Circaetus

1. NOUN

/ sɜːrˈsiːətuːs / (AmE), / sɜːˈsiːətəs / (BrE)
A genus of large birds of prey known as harrier eagles, found in parts of Africa, Europe, and Asia.
a genus of harrier eagles

Examples

The Circaetus is recognized for its preference for hunting snakes and other reptiles.
Several species of Circaetus inhabit open woodlands across sub-Saharan Africa.
The nest of a Circaetus is typically found in the highest tree branches.

Etymology

From Greek 'kirkos' (a hawk) and 'aetos' (eagle).

2. NOUN

/ sɜːrˈsiːətiːs / (AmE), / sɜːˈsiːətəs / (BrE)
A genus of large birds of prey known as harrier eagles, found chiefly in Africa and Eurasia, characterized by their broad wings and snake-hunting habits.
a genus of harrier eagles

Examples

The Circaetus species are well known for their ability to hunt and eat snakes.
A Circaetus can often be seen soaring over open savanna in search of prey.
Scientists documented the nesting habits of Circaetus in the grasslands of East Africa.

Etymology

From Greek 'kirkos' (a kind of hawk) + 'aetos' (eagle). Used in scientific Latin taxonomy.
