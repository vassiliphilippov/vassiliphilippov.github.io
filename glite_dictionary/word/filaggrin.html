filaggrin

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈlæɡrɪn / (AmE), / fɪˈlæɡrɪn / (BrE)
a protein found in the keratohyalin granules of the skin, important for the structure and function of the epidermis
skin protein in keratohyalin granules
medical

Examples

A mutation in the filaggrin gene can lead to dry, scaly skin conditions.
Researchers observed a significant decrease in filaggrin levels in patients with eczema.
The role of filaggrin is crucial in maintaining the skin's protective barrier.

Synset Examples

the specific target of the immune response in rheumatoid arthritis is filaggrin

Etymology

From 'filament aggregating protein', referring to its role in aggregating keratin filaments.
