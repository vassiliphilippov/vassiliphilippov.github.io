Genista tinctoria

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɪstə tɪŋkˈtɔriə / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɪstə tɪŋkˈtɔːrɪə / (BrE)
A small Eurasian shrub with clusters of yellow flowers, yielding a dye; often found as a weed in Britain and the United States, and sometimes cultivated as an ornamental.
yellow-flowered dye shrub (dyer's greenweed)

Examples

Botanists are studying Genista tinctoria for its potential natural dye properties.
Genista tinctoria spreads quickly in open fields, sometimes becoming invasive in certain regions.
Gardeners prize Genista tinctoria for its bright yellow flowers and low maintenance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dyer's greenweed - Common name for Genista tinctoria

Etymology

From Latin; 'Genista' is the genus name, and 'tinctoria' means 'used for dyeing'.
