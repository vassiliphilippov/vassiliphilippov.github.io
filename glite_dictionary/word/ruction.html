ruction

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈrʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
a noisy disturbance or commotion, often involving argument or disorder
noisy disturbance or commotion

Examples

There was quite a ruction in the office when the news broke.
The neighbors complained about the ruction caused by the late-night party.
One little mistake started a huge ruction among the committee members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uproar - a loud or impassioned noise or disturbance
  • Synonym: commotion - a state of confused and noisy disturbance

Etymology

Probably a shortening of 'eruption'.
