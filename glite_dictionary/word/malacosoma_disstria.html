Malacosoma disstria

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæləkəˈsoʊmə dɪˈstraɪə / (AmE), / ˌmaləkəˈsəʊmə dɪˈstraɪə / (BrE)
a North American moth species whose larvae, known as forest tent caterpillars, form gregarious colonies and are major defoliators of deciduous trees
a North American moth species (forest tent caterpillar)

Examples

Large outbreaks of Malacosoma disstria can cause significant damage to hardwood forests.
The larvae of Malacosoma disstria are known for spinning mats rather than true tents.
Scientists are studying methods to control Malacosoma disstria infestations in maple and aspen trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forest tent caterpillar - common name for Malacosoma disstria, a defoliator of trees

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name 'Malacosoma' from Greek 'malakos' (soft) + 'sōma' (body); species name 'disstria' meaning striped.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæləkəˌsoʊmə dɪsˈtraɪə / (AmE), / ˌmæləkəˌsəʊmə dɪsˈtraɪə / (BrE)
A species of moth whose social larvae, known as forest tent caterpillars, spin large silk mats in trees.
a moth species whose larvae form silk tents

Examples

The outbreak of Malacosoma disstria can defoliate entire forests in late spring.
Scientists are studying Malacosoma disstria to understand the ecological effects of tent caterpillar infestations.
The gregarious larvae of Malacosoma disstria create silk mats that are often seen draping over tree branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tent caterpillar - larval form of moths that spin silk tents

Etymology

From New Latin, with Malacosoma meaning 'soft body' and disstria referring to the species' distinctive stripes.
