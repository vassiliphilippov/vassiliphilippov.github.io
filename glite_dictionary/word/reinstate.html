to reinstate

1. VERB

/ ˌriːɪnˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈsteɪt / (BrE)
to restore someone or something to a previous position, status, or condition
restore to former position or state

Examples

The company decided to reinstate the suspended employee after an internal investigation.
After recovering from injury, she was reinstated as the team's lead player.
The government will reinstate the old laws that were previously abolished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restore - to bring back to a former or original condition
  • Antonym: to dismiss - to remove from a position or office

Etymology

From Latin 're-' (again) + 'instaurare' (to restore).

2. VERB

/ ˌriːɪnˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈsteɪt / (BrE)
to restore someone or something to a former position, state, or condition
restore to former position or state

Examples

The company decided to reinstate the suspended employee after the investigation cleared him.
After years of neglect, the city plans to reinstate the old fountain in the main square.
The university voted to reinstate the cancelled course due to student demand.

Synset Examples

restore law and order
reestablish peace in the region
restore the emperor to the throne

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restore - to return something to its former condition or position
  • Antonym: remove - to take away someone from a position or state

Etymology

From re- (again) + instate (from Latin 'in-' + 'status', meaning set up, placed).
