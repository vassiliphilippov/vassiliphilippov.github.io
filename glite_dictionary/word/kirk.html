kirk

1. NOUN

/ kɝk / (AmE), / kɜːk / (BrE)
A church, especially in Scotland; often refers to the Church of Scotland or to a church building in Scotland.
Scottish church or church building
british

Examples

The villagers gathered every Sunday at the old stone kirk on the hill.
He was married in a small kirk in the Scottish Highlands.
Tourists admired the architecture of the historic kirk in the center of town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: church - A building used for Christian worship

Etymology

From Old Norse 'kirkja', meaning church; related to Middle English 'kirke' and Old English 'cirice'.
