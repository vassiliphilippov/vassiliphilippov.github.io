cumulonimbus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkjuːmjəloʊˈnɪmbəs / (AmE), / ˌkjuːmjʊləʊˈnɪmbəs / (BrE)
A large, dense, towering cloud type often associated with thunderstorms and severe weather, characterized by its great vertical development and potential to produce lightning.
type of thunderstorm cloud

Examples

A massive cumulonimbus cloud loomed on the horizon, signaling an approaching thunderstorm.
Pilots are trained to avoid flying through cumulonimbus formations due to turbulence and lightning.
The weather forecast warned of severe storms with possible cumulonimbus development throughout the afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nimbus - a rain cloud, or halo of light in meteorology and classical usage

Etymology

From Latin 'cumulus' (heap) + 'nimbus' (rain cloud).
