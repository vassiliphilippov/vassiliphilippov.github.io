alexanders

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɛk.sən.dɚz / (AmE), / əˈlɛk.sən.dəz / (BrE)
A European herb (Smyrnium olusatrum) resembling celery, commonly grown as a potherb and naturalized in coastal regions of Britain.
European herb resembling celery

Examples

The leaves and stems of alexanders can be added to soups and stews for a distinctive flavor.
In coastal gardens, alexanders is valued both as an edible plant and for its attractive appearance.
Historically, alexanders was cultivated as a vegetable before celery became popular in Britain.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *alexandrinus* (of Alexandria), as the plant was believed to have originated near Alexandria.
