crewwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈkruːˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a female member of a ship's crew
female crew member on a ship

Examples

The experienced crewwoman managed the sails during the storm.
As the only crewwoman on board, she quickly proved her skills to the captain.
Several crewwomen received awards for their exceptional teamwork and leadership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crewman - a male or unspecified member of a ship's crew

2. NOUN

/ ˈkruˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈkruːˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a female member of a flight crew
female flight crew member

Examples

She became the first crewwoman to lead an international flight for her airline.
The crewwoman skillfully assisted passengers during the emergency landing.
Three crewwomen were recognized for their outstanding service on the flight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crewman - a male member of a flight crew
  • Synonym: crew member - a member of a flight crew, regardless of gender

Etymology

Formed from 'crew' + 'woman', analogous to 'crewman'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkruːˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈkruːˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a female member of a crew, especially in workplaces such as ships, aircraft, or film sets
female member of a crew

Examples

She was the only crewwoman on the research vessel during the Arctic expedition.
The film set hired a skilled crewwoman to manage the lighting equipment.
As a crewwoman, she was responsible for operating heavy machinery along with her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crewman - a male or gender-neutral member of a crew

Etymology

Formed from 'crew' + 'woman', modeled after 'crewman'.
