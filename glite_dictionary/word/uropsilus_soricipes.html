Uropsilus soricipes

1. NOUN

/ juːˈrɑpsɪləs sɔːˈrɪsɪˌpiːz / (AmE), / jʊəˈrɒpsɪləs sɒrɪˈsaɪpiːz / (BrE)
A species of shrew mole found in eastern Asia, belonging to the family Talpidae.
shrew mole species of eastern Asia

Examples

Researchers observed a Uropsilus soricipes in its native habitat in the mountains of eastern Asia.
The Uropsilus soricipes has a long, slender snout similar to that of a shrew.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the habitat of Uropsilus soricipes.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus 'Uropsilus' and species 'soricipes', from Latin 'soric-' (shrew) + 'pes' (foot).
