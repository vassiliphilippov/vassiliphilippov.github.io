hobber

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑb.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɒb.ə / (BrE)
a machine tool used for gear cutting, shaping splines, or making sprockets, typically by means of a rotating cutter called a hob
gear cutting machine tool

Examples

The factory invested in a new hobber to increase its production of precision gears.
Each hobber is capable of cutting gears of various sizes and specifications.
Skilled technicians are needed to operate the hobber and maintain its accuracy.

Synset Examples

Modern hobbing machines, also known as hobbers, are fully automated machines that come in many sizes, because they need to be able to produce anything from tiny instrument gears up to 10 ft (3.0 m) diameter marine gears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hobbing machine - a machine for cutting gears and similar parts using a hob

Etymology

From 'hob' (the cutting tool used in the process) + '-er' (agent suffix).
