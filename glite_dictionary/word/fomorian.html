Fomorian

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈmɔriən / (AmE), / fəˈmɔːriən / (BrE)
(in Irish mythology) one of a group of supernatural beings or sea demons often associated with chaotic or hostile forces of nature.
mythological Celtic sea demon

Examples

According to legend, the Fomorians battled the Tuatha Dé Danann for control of Ireland.
The fierce Fomorian sea demons were feared by ancient Celtic peoples.
In many myths, a Fomorian is portrayed as a monstrous giant representing destruction and chaos.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Tuatha Dé Danann - another supernatural race in Irish mythology, often opposed to the Fomorians

Etymology

From Irish Gaelic 'Fomóire', meaning supernatural beings or sea giants.
