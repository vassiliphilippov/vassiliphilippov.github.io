idiomatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪdiəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪdɪəˈmatɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a particular language's natural expressions or idioms; using or resembling natural speech patterns.
using or relating to natural idioms

Examples

Her speech is very idiomatic, making her sound like a native speaker.
It's important to use idiomatic phrases when writing in English to sound natural.
The translation was technically correct but lacked idiomatic expressions.

Synset Examples

idiomatic English

Related Terms

  • Antonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense
  • Synonym: colloquial - of language or expressions used in ordinary or familiar conversation

Etymology

From late Latin idiomaticus, from Greek idiōmatikos (peculiar, characteristic, idiomatic).
