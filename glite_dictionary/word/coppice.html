coppice

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.pɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɒp.ɪs / (BrE)
A small area of woodland where trees or shrubs are periodically cut back to ground level to encourage new growth; also, a dense growth of bushes or small trees.
small woodland or dense bushy growth

Examples

The hikers pushed their way through the dense coppice at the edge of the field.
Birds often nest in the shelter provided by a coppice.
We came upon a shady coppice filled with wildflowers during our walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thicket - a dense group of bushes or trees

Etymology

From Old French *copis*, from *couper* (to cut).
