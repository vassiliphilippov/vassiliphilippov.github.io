Delphinapterus

1. NOUN

/ dɛlˌfɪnəˈptɛrəs / (AmE), / dɛlˌfɪnəˈptɛərəs / (BrE)
The genus of cetacean mammals that includes the white whale, commonly known as the beluga.
genus of white whales (belugas)

Examples

The Delphinapterus is more commonly known as the beluga or white whale.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of Delphinapterus in the Arctic seas.
Unlike other whales, Delphinapterus lacks a dorsal fin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beluga - a white whale of the Arctic and subarctic regions

Etymology

From Greek delphin (dolphin) and apterus (without fin).
