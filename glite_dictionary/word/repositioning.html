repositioning

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːpəˈzɪʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːpəˈzɪʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of placing something or someone in a new position
the act of placing in a new position

Examples

The marketing team suggested the repositioning of the product to attract a younger audience.
Careful repositioning of the satellite was necessary to maintain communication with Earth.
His repositioning of the painting made the room look much brighter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placement - the act of putting something in a particular place

Etymology

From re- (again) + position (from Latin positio ‘a placing’) + -ing.
