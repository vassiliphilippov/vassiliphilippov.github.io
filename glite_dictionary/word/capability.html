capability

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpeɪ.bɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / kəˈpeɪ.bɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of being susceptible to a particular treatment or action, especially in a technical or scientific context.
susceptibility to a treatment or action

Examples

The capability of the fabric to resist stains makes it ideal for upholstery.
Researchers studied the capability of the alloy to withstand high temperatures.
The capability of certain enzymes to break down toxins is vital to the process.

Synset Examples

the capability of a metal to be fused

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capacity - the ability to contain, absorb, or produce; sometimes used interchangeably in technical contexts

Etymology

From capable + -ity, modeled on Latin capābilitās.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkeɪpəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌkeɪpəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the ability or qualities necessary to do something, especially a task, role, or action, either physically, intellectually, or legally
quality of being able to do something

Examples

His technical capability impressed everyone during the interview.
The company has the capability to produce thousands of units each week.
We must assess our capability to handle such a large-scale project.

Synset Examples

he worked to the limits of his capability

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ability - the power or skill to do something
  • Antonym: incapability - the state of being unable to do something

Etymology

From Late Latin 'capabilitas,' from Latin 'capax' (able to hold, capable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05209765-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌkeɪpəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌkeɪpəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the ability or potential to develop a particular skill or aptitude
potential to develop skill or aptitude

Examples

The new software has the capability to process large amounts of data quickly.
With training, he showed a remarkable capability for learning new languages.
The device’s capability can be improved through regular updates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capacity - the ability to do something; the maximum amount that something can contain

Etymology

From late Latin 'capabilitas', from 'capax' meaning 'able to hold or contain'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05630964-n
Synset POS: n
