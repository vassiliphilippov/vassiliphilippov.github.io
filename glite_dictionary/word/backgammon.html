backgammon

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌɡæm.ən / (AmE), / ˈbæk.ɡæm.ən / (BrE)
a board game for two players in which each player moves pieces around the board according to the roll of dice, with the aim of removing all their pieces from the board first
board game with dice and pieces

Examples

She challenged her friend to a game of backgammon after dinner.
The rules of backgammon are easy to learn but difficult to master.
They spent the rainy afternoon playing backgammon by the fireplace.

Etymology

Probably from back + gammon, with 'gammon' meaning 'a game' in 17th-century English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌɡæmən / (AmE), / ˈbakˌɡamən / (BrE)
a board game for two players in which pieces are moved according to the roll of dice, with the aim of removing all one's pieces from the board first
a board game involving dice and checkers

Examples

She invited her friends over to play backgammon on a rainy afternoon.
He spent hours honing his backgammon strategy to improve his chances of winning.
The café hosts a weekly backgammon tournament for local enthusiasts.

Synset Examples

He's a skilled back gammon player who wins every match.
As a back gammon player, she enjoys strategy games.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chess - a two-player strategy board game

Etymology

From Middle English *backe-gammon*, where 'back' refers to pieces re-entering and 'gammon' means 'game'.
