Salix sitchensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælɪks sɪtˈʃɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈsælɪks sɪtˈʃɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A small shrubby willow tree native to western North America, from Alaska to Oregon.
small shrubby willow of western North America

Examples

The Salix sitchensis grows abundantly along riverbanks in Alaska and British Columbia.
Researchers studied the ecological role of Salix sitchensis in maintaining wildlife habitats.
Native peoples traditionally used the stems of Salix sitchensis for weaving baskets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix with narrow leaves and catkins

Etymology

From Latin: 'Salix' meaning 'willow', and 'sitchensis' referring to Sitka (Alaska) where it was first identified.
