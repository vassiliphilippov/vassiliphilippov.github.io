Ibidiidae

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌbɪd.iˈaɪ.diː / (AmE), / ɪˌbɪd.ɪˈaɪ.diː / (BrE)
a family of wading birds comprising the ibises and, in some classifications, the spoonbills
bird family including ibises and spoonbills

Examples

The Ibidiidae are known for their long, down-curved bills.
Taxonomists once classified spoonbills and ibises together in Ibidiidae.
Bird watchers observed several species belonging to the Ibidiidae during the expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Threskiornithidae - bird family including ibises and spoonbills in many modern classifications

Etymology

From New Latin Ibidiidae, from genus name Ibis + suffix -idae (denoting animal family).
