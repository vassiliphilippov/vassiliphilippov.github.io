Avignon

1. PROPN

/ ˌævɪnˈjoʊn / (AmE), / ˈævɪnjɒ̃ / (BrE)
A city in southeastern France on the Rhone River, historically notable as the seat of the papacy from 1309 to 1377 and the residence of antipopes during the Great Schism.
city in southeastern France, former seat of papacy

Examples

During the 14th century, the popes resided in Avignon, shaping the city's history.
Many tourists visit Avignon to see its famous bridge and medieval architecture.
The cultural festival in Avignon attracts performers and audiences from around the world.

Etymology

From French Avignon, from Latin Avenio.
