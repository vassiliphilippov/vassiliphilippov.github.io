exorcism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛksɔrˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɛksɔːsɪzəm / (BrE)
the act or ritual of expelling or attempting to expel an evil spirit from a person or place
ritual to expel evil spirits

Examples

The priest performed an exorcism to rid the house of evil spirits.
Stories of exorcism have appeared in many cultures for centuries.
The movie depicted a dramatic exorcism scene that frightened many viewers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exorcist - a person who performs exorcisms

Etymology

From Late Latin exorcismus, from Greek exorkizein (to adjure, to exorcise).
