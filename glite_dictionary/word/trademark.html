trademark

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪdˌmɑːrk / (AmE), / ˈtreɪdˌmɑːk / (BrE)
A distinctive characteristic or feature that identifies someone or something.
distinctive characteristic or feature

Examples

The chef's use of unusual spices is his trademark.
Her trademark laugh could be heard from across the room.
The director's films are known for their trademark dark humor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallmark - a distinctive feature or quality that is typical of someone or something

Etymology

From trade + mark, originally referring to a sign indicating ownership by a trade guild or company.

2. VERB

/ ˈtreɪdˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈtreɪdˌmɑːk / (BrE)
to mark a product or item with a trademark or official brand, indicating ownership or origin
mark with a trademark or brand

Examples

The company decided to trademark its new logo to protect it from imitators.
Some small businesses choose not to trademark their products, relying on local reputation instead.
After they trademarked the brand name, they saw a noticeable increase in consumer trust.

Synset Examples

when this product is not branded it sells for a lower price

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brand - to mark with a name or symbol to indicate origin

Etymology

From trade + mark, originally referring to a distinctive mark used by a trader.

Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪdˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈtreɪdˌmɑːk / (BrE)
A symbol, word, or phrase that is legally registered or established by use as representing a company or product.
registered symbol for a brand or product

Examples

The company registered its trademark to protect its brand identity.
Coca-Cola's red and white logo is a well-known trademark around the world.
Using another business's trademark without permission can lead to legal action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brand - a type of product manufactured by a company
  • Antonym: copyright - exclusive legal right for reproduction and distribution of creative work

Etymology

Formed from 'trade' + 'mark'; earliest use in English dates to the 16th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06864792-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈtreɪdˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈtreɪdˌmɑːk / (BrE)
to officially register a name, symbol, or phrase as a trademark in order to protect it by law.
register a name or symbol as a trademark

Examples

The company trademarked their new logo to prevent imitation.
Apple has trademarked many of its product names worldwide.
Before launching the brand, they decided to trademark the slogan for legal protection.

Synset Examples

The company trademarked their new gadget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: register - to officially record something, such as a trademark, in an official list

Etymology

Trademark as a verb derives from the noun 'trademark,' which comes from trade + mark (late 16th century for 'mark used by a tradesman').
