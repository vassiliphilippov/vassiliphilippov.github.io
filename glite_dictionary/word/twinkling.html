twinkling

1. ADJ

/ ˈtwɪŋklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtwɪŋklɪŋ / (BrE)
shining intermittently with a sparkling or flickering light
shining with a flickering light

Examples

The twinkling lights on the tree filled the room with warmth.
Through the window, I could see the twinkling city skyline at night.
Children gazed up at the twinkling stars on a clear summer evening.

Synset Examples

twinkling stars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparkling - shining with bright flashes of light
  • Antonym: steady - showing light that does not flicker or change

Etymology

From the verb 'twinkle,' from Middle English 'twinklen,' of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00284446-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈtwɪŋklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtwɪŋklɪŋ / (BrE)
A very brief moment of time; an instant.
a very brief moment

Examples

He finished his homework in a twinkling and ran outside to play.
In a twinkling, the sun disappeared behind the clouds.
She answered the question in a twinkling, impressing everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

if I had the chance, I'd do it in a flash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flash - a brief moment; an instant

Etymology

From the verb 'twinkle', referring to a quick movement or sparkle, extended metaphorically to mean a short moment.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15271921-n
Synset POS: n
