Brontosaurus

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrɑːn.təˈsɔːr.əs / (AmE), / ˌbrɒn.təˈsɔː.rəs / (BrE)
a large quadrupedal herbivorous dinosaur of the late Jurassic period, with a very long neck and tail, belonging to the genus Brontosaurus.
large long-necked Jurassic dinosaur genus

Examples

Scientists discovered new fossils belonging to the Brontosaurus in a remote area of Wyoming.
The Brontosaurus is famous for its massive size and characteristically long neck.
Children are often fascinated by the idea of the enormous Brontosaurus roaming the prehistoric world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Apatosaurus - another large sauropod dinosaur genus closely related to Brontosaurus

Etymology

From Greek brontē (thunder) + sauros (lizard), meaning 'thunder lizard'.
