small-cap

1. ADJ

/ smɔl kæp / (AmE), / smɔːl kæp / (BrE)
(of a company or its stock) having a relatively small total market capitalization, typically less than one billion dollars.
of a company with small market capitalization

Examples

Many investors seek higher returns by putting money into small-cap stocks despite their greater risk.
The fund specializes in small-cap technology companies with growth potential.
During the market rally, small-cap shares outperformed their larger counterparts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mid-cap - of companies with a medium market capitalization
  • Degree Higher: large-cap - of companies with a large market capitalization
  • Degree Lower: micro-cap - of companies with an even smaller market capitalization than small-cap

Etymology

Formed from 'small' and 'cap', short for 'market capitalization'.
