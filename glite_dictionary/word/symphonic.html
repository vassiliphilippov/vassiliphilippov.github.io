symphonic

1. ADJ

/ sɪmˈfɑnɪk / (AmE), / sɪmˈfɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of a sound or combination of sounds) harmonious and resembling the qualities of a symphony.
harmonious in sound

Examples

The symphonic hum of the city at dawn was both calming and uplifting.
A symphonic blending of voices filled the concert hall with warmth.
The breeze brought a symphonic mixture of leaves rustling and distant laughter.

Synset Examples

the symphonic hum of a million insects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmonious - having agreement or concord in sound; pleasing combination of sounds

Etymology

From Greek 'symphōnia' (concord of sound), via Medieval Latin 'symphonicus'.

2. ADJ

/ sɪmˈfɑnɪk / (AmE), / sɪmˈfɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a symphony or symphony orchestra; resembling the structure or style of a symphony.
relating to symphony or symphony orchestra

Examples

The orchestra delivered a symphonic performance that captivated the entire audience.
She joined a symphonic choir known for its powerful, expressive sound.
The film's soundtrack has a rich, symphonic quality that adds emotional depth.

Synset Examples

symphonic choir

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchestral - relating to an orchestra
  • Synonym: symphony - an elaborate musical composition for full orchestra

Etymology

From French 'symphonique', from symphonie, itself from Latin 'symphonia'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03013548-a
Synset POS: a
