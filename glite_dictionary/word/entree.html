entree

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːnˌtreɪ, ˈɑːnˌtri / (AmE), / ˈɒntreɪ, ˈɒntri / (BrE)
an entrance, especially a dramatic or formal entrance onto a stage or into a notable setting
dramatic or formal entrance

Examples

The leading actress made her entree with stunning confidence, captivating the audience instantly.
When the doors opened, his entree into the party drew everyone's attention.
She practiced her entree several times to ensure it was flawless for her stage debut.

Synset Examples

she made a graceful entree into the ballroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entrance - the act of entering; an entry

Etymology

From French entrée (entrance), from Old French entrer (to enter).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑn.treɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒn.treɪ / (BrE)
The main dish of a meal, especially in a restaurant.
main course of a meal
american

Examples

She ordered the grilled salmon as her entree.
The restaurant's special entree comes with a choice of side dishes.
For my entree, I chose the steak with mashed potatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: main course - the principal dish of a meal

Etymology

From French 'entrée' (entrance, entry), originally denoting the entrance of a dish in a formal meal.

Synset POS: n
