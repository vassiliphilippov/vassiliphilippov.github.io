to tabulate

1. VERB

/ ˈtæbjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈtæbjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to arrange information or data in a table or systematic list, usually with columns and rows
arrange data in a table

Examples

The researchers tabulate their results in an easy-to-read chart.
Please tabulate the expenses for each department before the meeting.
After collecting the survey responses, she tabulated the data to look for trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: list - to record or enumerate items in a sequence
  • Synonym: to organize - to arrange systematically

Etymology

From Latin 'tabula' (a board, plank, or list), via modern Latin 'tabulatus' (to form into a table).

2. VERB

/ ˈtæbjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈtæbjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(technical) To shape or cut (something) so that it has one or more flat surfaces.
to shape with a flat surface

Examples

The stones were carefully tabulated to ensure they would fit together in the wall.
Over centuries, the river tabulated the rocks, giving them smooth, flat sides.
Geologists examined the crystals tabulated by natural processes in the cave.

Etymology

From Latin 'tabula' (a board, plank, flat surface) + '-ate'.
