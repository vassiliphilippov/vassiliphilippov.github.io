tinfoil

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪnˌfɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɪnˌfɔɪl / (BrE)
thin metal foil made of tin or an alloy of tin and lead, formerly used for wrapping and covering food or other items
thin metal foil of tin or tin alloy

Examples

She carefully wrapped the leftover cake in tinfoil to keep it fresh.
Before the popularity of aluminum foil, tinfoil was commonly used in kitchens.
A piece of tinfoil was molded around the sandwich to protect it during the picnic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aluminum foil - thin sheet of aluminum used for wrapping and covering food

Etymology

From tin + foil, referring to thin sheets of tin used for covering.
