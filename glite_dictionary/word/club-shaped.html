club-shaped

1. ADJ

/ ˈklʌb ʃeɪpt / (AmE), / ˈklʌb ʃeɪpt / (BrE)
Shaped like a club, being thicker or wider at one end and narrower at the other.
thicker at one end, like a club

Examples

The insect's antennae are club-shaped, widening prominently at the tips.
He designed a club-shaped handle to improve the tool's grip.
The plant's fruit is club-shaped, swelling at one end for seed storage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clavate - having a club-like shape, thickened at one end

2. ADJ

/ ˈklʌb ʃeɪpt / (AmE), / ˈklʌb ʃeɪpt / (BrE)
having the shape of a club, especially resembling the black trefoil or clover leaf on a playing card.
shaped like a club or club suit symbol

Examples

The old iron key had a club-shaped handle resembling a playing card suit.
Botanists noted the club-shaped leaves of the rare plant species.
The medieval mace was club-shaped, flaring out at the hitting end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clublike - resembling the shape of a club
