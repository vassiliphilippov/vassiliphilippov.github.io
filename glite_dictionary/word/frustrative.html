frustrative

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrʌstreɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈfrʌstrətɪv / (BrE)
Preventing the realization or attainment of a desire or goal.
preventing attainment of a desire

Examples

A frustrative experience can negatively impact motivation and emotional well-being.
Psychologists studied the effects of frustrative nonreward in animal learning experiments.
The game's design included frustrative elements to increase its level of challenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frustrating - causing annoyance by hindering progress or success

Etymology

From 'frustrate' + '-ive', after Latin frustratus (disappointed).
