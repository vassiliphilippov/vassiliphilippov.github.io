transferable

1. ADJ

/ trænˈsfɚrəbəl / (AmE), / trɑːnˈsfɜːrəbəl / (BrE)
able to be legally or formally transferred to the ownership or possession of another person or entity
able to be legally transferred to another

Examples

The concert tickets are transferable, so you can give them to a friend.
Ownership of the shares is transferable upon completion of the paperwork.
Some airline miles are not transferable between different accounts.

Synset Examples

negotiable bonds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nontransferable - not able to be transferred to another person

Etymology

From Late Latin transferre (to carry across) plus -able (capable of).

2. ADJ

/ trænsˈfɝːrəbl̩ / (AmE), / trænsˈfɜːrəbəl / (BrE)
(of an object, right, or quality) able to be moved, passed, or conveyed from one person, place, or situation to another.
able to be moved or passed on

Examples

The ticket is transferable, so you can give it to someone else if you can't attend.
Skills learned in one job are often transferable to another position.
Your warranty is transferable even if you sell the car to a new owner.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nontransferable - not able to be moved or passed to another

Etymology

From transfer + -able, ultimately from Latin transferre (to carry across) + -abilis (able).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01527843-s
Synset POS: s
