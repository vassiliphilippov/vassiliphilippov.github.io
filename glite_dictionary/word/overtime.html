overtime

1. ADV

/ ˈoʊvərˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈəʊvətaɪm / (BrE)
Beyond the regular or scheduled time; longer than originally planned or intended.
beyond the usual time

Examples

Because the project was delayed, we had to stay overtime to finish it.
He often works overtime during busy seasons at the company.
The team played overtime to break the tie in the championship match.

Synset Examples

she often has to work overtime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: late - After the expected or usual time

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈəʊvətaɪm / (BrE)
extra period of play added to a game to break a tie after regular time ends
extra period to break a tie in sports

Examples

The game went into overtime after both teams finished with a tied score.
She scored the winning goal during overtime.
Fans were on the edge of their seats throughout the entire overtime period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extra time - additional time played in a game, especially in soccer, to break a tie
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15258786-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈəʊvətaɪm / (BrE)
time worked in addition to one’s regular working hours, especially when paid at a higher rate
work beyond regular hours

Examples

She was paid extra for all the overtime she worked this month.
The manager asked everyone to put in some overtime to meet the deadline.
Working regular overtime can affect your health over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensatory time - paid time off given instead of payment for overtime hours
  • Antonym: regular hours - standard period for work as defined by a contract

Etymology

From 'over' + 'time', originating in early 20th-century workplace jargon.
