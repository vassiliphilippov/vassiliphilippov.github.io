Kit Carson

1. PROPN

/ kɪt ˈkɑrsən / (AmE), / kɪt ˈkɑːsən / (BrE)
(1809–1868) United States frontiersman and military leader, known for guiding Fremont's expeditions and serving as a Union general during the American Civil War.
famous US frontiersman and general

Examples

Kit Carson played a key role in the westward expansion of the United States during the 19th century.
Many towns and landmarks in the American West are named after Kit Carson.
As a scout and military leader, Kit Carson became a legendary figure in American frontier history.
