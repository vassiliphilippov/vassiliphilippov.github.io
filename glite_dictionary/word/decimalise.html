to decimalise

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛs.ɪ.məˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdɛs.ɪ.məˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to change a system or quantities from using fractions to using decimals
convert from fractions to decimals
british

Examples

The government decided to decimalise the currency to simplify calculations.
Many countries decimalised their measurement systems in the twentieth century.
Some stock exchanges were reluctant to decimalise their pricing structures at first.

Synset Examples

Stock prices will be decimalized in the year 2000

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decimalize - convert from fractions to decimals (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'decimal' (from Latin 'decimus' meaning 'tenth') + '-ise', a verb-forming suffix.

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛsɪməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪməlaɪz / (BrE)
to convert a system, especially of currency or measurements, to a decimal-based structure using units of ten
convert to a decimal-based system

Examples

Many countries decided to decimalise their currency in the twentieth century.
The government plans to decimalise the old system of weights and measures next year.
After the country decimalised its currency, transactions became much simpler.

Synset Examples

The country decimalized the currency in 1975

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decimalize - convert to a decimal-based system (US spelling)

Etymology

From decimal + -ise, after French décimaliser.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00531672-v
Synset POS: v
