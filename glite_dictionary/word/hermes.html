Hermes

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɜːrˌmiːz / (AmE), / ˈhɜːmiːz / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the messenger and herald of the gods; also the god of commerce, invention, cunning, and theft, identified with the Roman Mercury.
Greek messenger god; god of commerce, invention, and cunning

Examples

The statue in the museum depicts Hermes with his characteristic winged sandals.
In Greek mythology, Hermes was known for his speed and cleverness.
Many merchants worshipped Hermes as the god of commerce and trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mercury - Roman counterpart of Hermes; messenger god and god of commerce

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἑρμῆς (Hērmēs), the name of the god.
