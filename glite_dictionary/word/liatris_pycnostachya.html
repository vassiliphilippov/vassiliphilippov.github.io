Liatris pycnostachya

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈætrɪs pɪkˌnoʊˈstækiə / (AmE), / laɪˈætrɪs pɪkˌnɒˈstækɪə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to southeastern and central United States, with very dense spikes of purple flowers, often grown for cut flowers; also known as prairie blazing star.
a US perennial plant with dense purple flower spikes

Examples

The tall Liatris pycnostachya stood out in the wildflower garden with its vivid purple spikes.
Gardeners often plant Liatris pycnostachya to attract butterflies and create striking floral displays.
Florists prize Liatris pycnostachya for its long stems and dense flower clusters, making it ideal for bouquets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prairie blazing star - common name for Liatris pycnostachya

Etymology

From Greek *pyknos* (dense) and *stachys* (spike), referring to its densely packed flower spikes; 'Liatris' is the genus name.
