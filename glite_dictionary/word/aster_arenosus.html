Aster arenosus

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstər əˈrənoʊsəs / (AmE), / ˈastə əˈrəːnəʊsəs / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant with heathlike foliage and small white flower heads, belonging to the aster family.
a North American flowering plant

Examples

The sandy fields were dotted with blooming Aster arenosus during late summer.
Gardeners often choose Aster arenosus for its hardiness and delicate white flowers.
Researchers studied the soil preferences of Aster arenosus across different North American regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heath aster - common name for several asters with heathlike foliage

Etymology

From Latin; 'Aster' meaning 'star', referring to the flower's shape, and 'arenosus' meaning 'sandy', indicating its typical habitat.
