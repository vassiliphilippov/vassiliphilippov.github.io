bunya bunya

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌnjə ˌbʌnjə / (AmE), / ˈbʌnjə ˌbʌnjə / (BrE)
a large edible nut from the bunya pine, tasting like roasted chestnuts and traditionally eaten by Australian Aboriginal people
large edible nut from the bunya pine tree

Examples

The bunya bunya nut was an important food source for Indigenous Australians.
She roasted bunya bunya nuts over the fire before adding them to the salad.
We sampled a traditional dish made with bunya bunya during our trip to Queensland.

Etymology

From the native Australian (Kabi) word *banya* or *bunyi*, referring to the tree and its nut.
