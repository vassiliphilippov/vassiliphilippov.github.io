chat

1. NOUN

/ tʃæt / (AmE), / tʃæt / (BrE)
a small songbird known for its chattering call, found in the thrush or warbler family depending on classification
a small songbird with a chattering call

Examples

Several species of chat can be found in woodlands across Europe and Asia.
The yellow-breasted chat is known for its loud, varied calls.
Ornithologists debated whether the chat should be grouped with warblers or thrushes.

4. NOUN

/ tʃæt / (AmE), / tʃat / (BrE)
a small songbird, typically with a chattering call, belonging to several genera such as Saxicola or Granatellus.
small songbird with chattering call

Examples

We spotted a brightly colored chat singing in the meadow at dawn.
The whinchat is a species of chat commonly found in open countryside.
Her field guide listed all the different types of chat native to the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stonechat - a type of small European chat with a harsh, chattering call
  • Synonym: whinchat - a small European chat, known for its distinctive call

Etymology

From French 'chat,' from Latin 'cattus' (cat); the bird's name refers to its call, reminiscent of a cat's chatter.
