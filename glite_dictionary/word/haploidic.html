haploidic

1. ADJ

/ hæpˈlɔɪdɪk / (AmE), / hæpˈlɔɪdɪk / (BrE)
(Biology) Having a single set of chromosomes in the cell, as in gametes of organisms.
having a single set of chromosomes
medical

Examples

In many fungi, the vegetative body is haploidic for much of its life cycle.
The haploidic phase in plants alternates with a diploidic sporophytic generation.
Researchers observed that only haploidic spores could develop under these conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haploid - having a single set of chromosomes
  • Antonym: diploid - having two sets of chromosomes

Etymology

Formed from 'haploid' (from Greek 'haploos', single) plus the suffix '-ic'.
