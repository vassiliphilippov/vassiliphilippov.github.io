potto

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɒtəʊ / (BrE)
a nocturnal African primate with a short tail, dense woolly fur, and a slow-moving nature (Perodicticus potto)
nocturnal African primate

Examples

The potto spends much of its life high in the forest canopy.
Researchers observed a potto moving slowly along the branches at night.
Unlike most primates, the potto is known for its extremely quiet movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemur - a small, tree-dwelling prosimian primate from Madagascar

Etymology

From West African (Krio) 'pata', of unknown origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɒtəʊ / (BrE)
A small, slow-moving, nocturnal mammal of tropical America, with a long prehensile tail, that lives in trees and feeds mainly on fruit.
small nocturnal tree-dwelling mammal

Examples

The potto uses its long tail to grasp branches as it moves through the forest canopy.
Researchers observed a potto feeding quietly on figs during the night.
Unlike monkeys, the potto is slow and deliberate in its movements.

Etymology

From West African language (possibly Wolof 'pata' or a related term).
