Algeripithecus minutus

1. NOUN

/ ælˌdʒɛrəˌpɪˈθikəs maɪˈnuːtəs / (AmE), / ælˌdʒɛrɪpɪˈθiːkəs mɪˈnjuːtəs / (BrE)
a tiny extinct primate species that lived about 46 to 50 million years ago in what is now Algeria, considered by some as a possible earliest anthropoid
extinct primate species from Algeria

Examples

The discovery of Algeripithecus minutus fossils helped reshape our understanding of early primate evolution.
Scientists believe that Algeripithecus minutus may have been among the smallest anthropoid ancestors.
Studies of Algeripithecus minutus provide valuable clues about primate dispersal in the Eocene epoch.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek: 'Algeria' (country) + 'pithecus' (Greek for 'ape'); 'minutus' meaning 'small' in Latin.
