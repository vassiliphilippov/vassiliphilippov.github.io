Hyacinthaceae

1. NOUN

/ haɪəˌsɪnˈθeɪsi.i / (AmE), / haɪəˌsɪnˈθeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, including the hyacinths, that some classification systems separate from the Liliaceae family; not widely accepted as a distinct family
a plant family including hyacinths (botany)

Examples

Recent studies suggest that Hyacinthaceae should be merged back into the broader Liliaceae family.
Many common ornamental plants belong to the Hyacinthaceae, although the family's status is debated.
Botanists once classified grape hyacinths as part of the Hyacinthaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Liliaceae - the lily family; a larger family that Hyacinthaceae is sometimes included in

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Hyacinthus, named after the flower hyacinth, ultimately from Greek 'Hyakinthos'.

2. NOUN

/ haɪəˌsɪnθiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / haɪəˌsɪnθɪˈiːsiː / (BrE)
a former family of flowering plants, including hyacinths, once recognized in some botanical classification systems but now often considered a subfamily within Asparagaceae
former plant family including hyacinths

Examples

The Hyacinthaceae once included many familiar bulbous plants such as hyacinths and bluebells.
Recent taxonomic revisions have merged the Hyacinthaceae into the broader Asparagaceae family.
Botanists used to classify grape hyacinth under the Hyacinthaceae before newer classifications were adopted.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Asparagaceae - a family of flowering plants including asparaguses and hyacinths

Etymology

From the genus name Hyacinthus, after Hyacinth in Greek mythology, plus the botanical family suffix -aceae.
