consumptive

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsʌmptɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsʌmptɪv / (BrE)
afflicted with or related to pulmonary tuberculosis (historically called consumption)
having or related to tuberculosis
archaic medical

Examples

The sanitarium was filled with consumptive patients hoping for a cure in the mountain air.
She developed a consumptive cough that worried her family greatly.
Many Victorian novels feature tragic heroines who are described as consumptive.

Synset Examples

a consumptive patient
a consumptive cough

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tubercular - afflicted with or related to tuberculosis

Etymology

From Latin consumptus, past participle of consumere (to consume), referring to the wasting away characteristic of the disease.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02554206-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ kənˈsʌmptɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsʌmptɪv / (BrE)
a person suffering from pulmonary tuberculosis, especially as a historical or literary term
person with pulmonary tuberculosis
archaic medical

Examples

In many Victorian novels, the tragic fate of the consumptive heroine is a common theme.
The sanatorium provided special care for each consumptive admitted in the early 1900s.
He was a frail consumptive, coughing persistently and struggling to regain his health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuberculosis - an infectious disease affecting the lungs, also called TB

Etymology

From Latin 'consumptio' (a consuming, wasting away), relating to the former name of tuberculosis, 'consumption'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09979480-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ kənˈsʌmptɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsʌmptɪv / (BrE)
Tending to consume or use resources, often wastefully or destructively.
tending to consume or use up

Examples

The company's consumptive approach to resources raised concerns among environmentalists.
Fires that spread rapidly can have a highly consumptive effect on forests.
Many modern appliances are surprisingly consumptive of electricity.

Synset Examples

water suitable for beneficial consumptive uses
duties consumptive of time and energy
consumptive fires

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wasteful - using something in a careless or unnecessary way

Etymology

From Latin 'consumptivus', from 'consumere' (to consume).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01873452-a
Synset POS: a
