Leymus arenaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪməs ɛˈnɛriə / (AmE), / ˈleɪməs əˈnɑːriə / (BrE)
a species of dune grass found along the Pacific seacoast, used to stabilize sand and prevent erosion
a Pacific coastal dune grass used to bind sand

Examples

Leymus arenaria is often planted along beaches to help prevent coastal erosion.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Leymus arenaria in different dune environments.
Many coastal restoration projects use Leymus arenaria because of its effectiveness as a sand binder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dune grass - a general term for grasses that grow on sand dunes

Etymology

From Latin; 'Leymus' is the genus name for certain grasses, and 'arenaria' means 'growing in sand'.
