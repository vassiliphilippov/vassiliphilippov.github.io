downheartedness

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaʊnˈhɑrtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌdaʊnˈhɑːtɪdnəs / (BrE)
a feeling of sadness or discouragement; low spirits
feeling of sadness, low spirits

Examples

After the loss, a sense of downheartedness spread throughout the team.
She battled her downheartedness by staying busy and connecting with friends.
Even in moments of downheartedness, he tried to remain hopeful.

Synset Examples

he felt responsible for her lowness of spirits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dejection - a state of being downcast or depressed
  • Synonym: despondency - loss of hope or confidence
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the state of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

Formed from 'downhearted' (discouraged, sad) + '-ness' (state, quality).
