to reside

1. VERB

/ rɪˈzaɪd / (AmE), / rɪˈzaɪd / (BrE)
to live in a particular place or community; to have one's permanent home in a specific location
live in a particular place

Examples

Many international students reside in the university dormitories.
The family resided in Paris for over a decade before returning home.
She currently resides in a small town near the coast.

Synset Examples

my parents reside in Florida

Related Terms

  • Synonym: live - to reside or dwell in a place
  • Synonym: to inhabit - to live in or occupy a place

Etymology

From Latin residēre (to remain, to reside), from re- (back) + sedēre (to sit).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈzaɪd / (AmE), / rɪˈzaɪd / (BrE)
(of a quality, power, etc.) to be present or inherent in something; to exist as an essential characteristic
to be present or inherent in something

Examples

The final decision must reside in the hands of the committee.
True leadership qualities often reside within those who least expect it.
Authority ultimately resides in the people according to the constitution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inhere - to exist essentially or permanently in something
  • Synonym: to belong - to be the property of or properly assigned to

Etymology

From Latin residēre, from re- (back) + sedere (to sit).

3. VERB

/ rɪˈzaɪd / (AmE), / rɪˈzaɪd / (BrE)
to live in a particular place; to have one's home in a specific location
live in a place

Examples

She has resided in the same neighborhood for over twenty years.
Many students reside in university dormitories during the academic year.
The president officially resides in the White House.

Synset Examples

She resides in Princeton
he occupies two rooms on the top floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inhabit - to live in or occupy a place
  • Synonym: to occupy - to live, dwell, or be established in a place
  • Antonym: leave - to go away from a place where one resides

Etymology

From Latin 'residere', from re- (back) + sedere (to sit).
