William Shockley

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪl.jəm ˈʃɑk.li / (AmE), / ˈwɪl.jəm ˈʃɒk.li / (BrE)
(1910–1989) United States physicist, born in England, who contributed to the development of the electronic transistor.
physicist; transistor co-inventor

Examples

William Shockley was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1956 for his role in inventing the transistor.
Many consider William Shockley a key figure in the development of modern electronics.
The work of William Shockley revolutionized the semiconductor industry.
