Zalophus californicus

1. PROPN

/ zəˈlɑfəs ˌkælɪˈfɔrnɪkəs / (AmE), / zəˈlɒfəs ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the California sea lion, a species of sea lion found along the western coast of North America, often trained for performances
the California sea lion (species)

Examples

The Zalophus californicus is commonly seen in aquariums and marine parks across the United States.
Researchers studied the habitat range of Zalophus californicus along the Pacific coastline.
Trained Zalophus californicus can perform tricks and interact with spectators at public shows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: California sea lion - a species of sea lion found on the west coast of North America

Etymology

From Greek 'zalophos' (intensive crest on head) and Latin 'californicus', meaning 'of California'.
