backseat

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌsit / (AmE), / ˈbækˌsiːt / (BrE)
a seat behind the front seats in a vehicle, especially an automobile
seat at rear of a vehicle

Examples

The children sat quietly in the backseat during the long drive.
She threw her jacket onto the backseat before starting the car.
He found the missing book lying under the backseat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: front seat - seat at the front of a vehicle

Etymology

Formed from 'back' + 'seat', indicating position within a vehicle.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌsit / (AmE), / ˈbækˌsiːt / (BrE)
a secondary or less important position or status within an organization or activity
secondary or inferior position

Examples

After her promotion, his ambitions had to take a backseat for a while.
In the project, marketing played a backseat role to product development.
During the crisis, normal procedures were given a backseat in favor of urgent action.

Synset Examples

tennis has had to take a backseat while his work is so demanding

Related Terms

  • Antonym: front seat - a leading or important position

Etymology

From 'back seat', originally referring to the seat at the rear of a vehicle, often considered less important than the 'front seat'.
