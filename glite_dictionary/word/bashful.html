bashful

1. ADJ

/ ˈbæʃ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈbæʃ.fəl / (BrE)
reluctant to draw attention to oneself; shy or easily embarrassed.
shy; reluctant to draw attention

Examples

She gave a bashful smile when complimented by her teacher.
Although he is bashful around strangers, he becomes talkative with friends.
The bashful child hid behind his mother's legs at the party.

Synset Examples

they considered themselves a tough outfit and weren't bashful about letting anybody know it
‘blate’ is a Scottish term for bashful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shy - nervous or timid in the company of others
  • Antonym: confident - sure of oneself and one's abilities

Etymology

From Middle English 'bæshful', from the verb 'abashen' (to be ashamed).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbæʃfəl / (AmE), / ˈbæʃfəl / (BrE)
Reluctant to draw attention to oneself; shy or self-consciously timid.
shy; timid

Examples

The bashful child hid behind his mother when meeting new people.
She gave a bashful smile when complimented on her painting.
Even though he was bashful, he managed to speak in front of the class.

Synset Examples

I never laughed, being bashful; lowering my head, I looked at the wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shy - nervous or uncomfortable with people; easily embarrassed
  • Antonym: confident - self-assured; not shy or timid

Etymology

From Middle English 'bachen' (to be ashamed), influenced by 'abash'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00252996-s
Synset POS: s
