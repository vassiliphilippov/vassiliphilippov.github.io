divination

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪvəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪvɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a prediction or insight revealed through supposed divine inspiration or supernatural means, especially the attempt to foretell future events
prediction by supernatural or divine means

Examples

The oracle's divination was taken seriously by all who heard her words.
Ancient cultures often relied on divination to make important decisions about war and harvest.
Modern skeptics question the accuracy of divination, viewing it as superstition rather than truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophecy - a prediction, especially one made under divine inspiration

Etymology

From Latin divinatio (soothsaying), from divinare (to foresee, to divine), from divinus (divine).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪvəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪvɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of foretelling future events or discovering hidden knowledge through supposed supernatural means or by unusual insight or luck
foretelling the future; prophecy

Examples

The priestess claimed that her divination revealed the king's fate.
Through a stroke of divination, she correctly predicted the outcome of the meeting.
His uncanny divination of market trends impressed his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophecy - the prediction of what will happen in the future, especially by supernatural means
  • Synonym: prediction - a statement about what will happen in the future based on knowledge or guesswork
  • Degree Lower: guess - an estimate or conjecture without sufficient information

Etymology

From Latin divinatio, from divinare (to foresee, to be inspired by a god).

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɪvəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪvɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the practice of seeking to foresee or foretell future events or discover hidden knowledge by supernatural or mystical means
foretelling the future by supernatural means

Examples

Many ancient cultures practiced divination to predict the outcome of important events.
The oracle claimed to have the power of divination through dreams and visions.
Skeptics often regard divination as mere superstition rather than a genuine supernatural gift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophecy - the act of predicting what will happen in the future, often by divine inspiration
  • Synonym: augury - an omen, or the practice of interpreting omens

Etymology

From Latin divinatio (inspired soothsaying), from divinare ('to foresee, to be inspired by a god').
