incurableness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkjʊrəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkjʊərəbəlnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being impossible to cure or heal
the condition of being incurable

Examples

The incurableness of the disease made treatment options very limited for the patient.
Her research focused on the incurableness of certain rare conditions in pediatric medicine.
They struggled to accept the incurableness of his illness despite all hopes for recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incurability - the quality of being incurable
  • Antonym: curableness - the quality of being able to be cured

Etymology

From incurable + -ness, indicating a state or quality.
