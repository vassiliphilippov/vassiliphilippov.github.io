disorder

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
to disturb the order or arrangement of something; to make untidy or disorganized
make untidy or disorganized

Examples

The strong winds disordered the carefully arranged chairs in the garden.
A sudden interruption can easily disorder your train of thought.
The puppy disordered the neatly stacked newspapers by running through them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disorganize - to make something disordered or chaotic
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00277172-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɔrdər / (AmE), / dɪˈsɔːdə / (BrE)
A situation in which the normal order or peace of society is disrupted, often involving public disturbance or unrest.
disruption of public order or peace

Examples

The police were called to break up the disorder that erupted after the match.
Last year's protest ended in violent disorder on the city streets.
The mayor warned that any further disorder would not be tolerated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturbance - an interruption of peace or order
  • Degree Higher: riot - a violent disturbance of the peace by a crowd
  • Antonym: order - a state in which the rules, laws, or customs of society are obeyed

Etymology

From Old French *desordre*, from Latin *disordinare* (to throw into confusion).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɔrdɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɔːdə / (BrE)
A physical or medical condition in which normal functioning of the body or mind is disturbed.
disturbance of normal physical or mental function

Examples

He was diagnosed with a rare eating disorder that required special treatment.
Many sleep disorders can be managed with lifestyle changes.
The new drug is used to treat anxiety disorder in adults.

Synset Examples

the doctor prescribed some medicine for the disorder
everyone gets stomach upsets from time to time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disease - illness affecting the body or mind
  • Synonym: condition - state of health, often negative
  • Antonym: health - the state of being well

Etymology

From Old French 'desordre', from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'ordo' (order).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a state in which things are not arranged properly; a lack of order or organization
lack of order; mess

Examples

The children left their toys in complete disorder all over the living room.
After the earthquake, the city was thrown into chaos and disorder.
His desk was in such disorder that he could barely find anything.

Synset Examples

the files are in complete disorder

Related Terms

  • Antonym: order - a state in which everything is arranged or in its proper place
  • Synonym: mess - an untidy or disorganized state

Etymology

From Old French *desordre*, from Latin *disordinare* (to throw into confusion).
