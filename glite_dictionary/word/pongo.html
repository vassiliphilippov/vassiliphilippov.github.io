Pongo

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɒŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
The genus of large great apes that includes the orangutans.
genus of orangutans

Examples

The Sumatran and Bornean orangutans both belong to the genus Pongo.
Biologists debated the classification of Pongo for decades.
Fossil records have revealed extinct species within the genus Pongo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orangutan - large arboreal ape native to Southeast Asia

Etymology

From Malay 'pongo', used in scientific Latin to name the genus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑŋgoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɒŋgəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of great apes comprising the orangutans, native to Southeast Asia.
genus of orangutans (great apes)

Examples

The genus Pongo includes all living orangutans found in Asia.
Recent studies on Pongo genetics have clarified the evolutionary history of orangutans.
Conservation efforts for the Pongo species are critical due to their endangered status.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orangutan - a great ape native to Borneo and Sumatra

Etymology

From Malay *pongo* (an orangutan).
