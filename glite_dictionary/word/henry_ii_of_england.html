Henry II of England

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛnri səˈkənd əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri ðə ˈsɛkənd ɒv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (BrE)
(1133–1189) The first Plantagenet King of England, known for instituting judicial and financial reforms and for his conflict with Archbishop Thomas Becket over the authority of the Crown.
first Plantagenet King of England, 1133–1189

Examples

Henry II of England introduced several legal reforms that shaped the English justice system.
The famous conflict between Henry II of England and Thomas Becket is a key event in British history.
Many historians consider Henry II of England one of the most effective medieval English monarchs.
