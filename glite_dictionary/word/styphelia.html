Styphelia

1. NOUN

/ staɪˈfiːliə / (AmE), / stɪˈfɛliə / (BrE)
A genus of Australian heathlike shrubs belonging to the family Ericaceae, known for their small, narrow leaves and often bell-shaped flowers.
genus of Australian heathlike shrubs

Examples

The Styphelia species are well adapted to the sandy soils of southwestern Australia.
Many gardeners appreciate Styphelia for its delicate, bell-shaped flowers and compact form.
Researchers discovered a new variety of Styphelia in the coastal heathland.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek styphelos (hard, rough), referring to the tough foliage.
