hieroglyphic

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪərəˈɡlɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪərəˈɡlɪfɪk / (BrE)
A writing system that uses picture symbols to represent words and sounds, especially as used in ancient Egypt.
writing system using picture symbols

Examples

The Rosetta Stone was key to deciphering ancient Egyptian hieroglyphics.
Each hieroglyphic represented a specific sound or idea.
Visitors admired the hieroglyphic inscriptions carved on the walls of the temple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pictograph - A symbol representing a word or idea in a pictorial form.

Etymology

From Greek 'hieroglyphikos', from 'hieros' (sacred) + 'glyphein' (to carve).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪərəˈɡlɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪərəˈɡlɪfɪk / (BrE)
writing that is difficult to read because it resembles or imitates hieroglyphics; illegible or obscure writing.
indecipherable or obscure writing

Examples

I couldn’t read his notes; they were just a hieroglyphic on the page.
Her signature looked like a hieroglyphic to everyone except herself.
The teacher returned the homework because it was written in near-hieroglyphic script.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrawl - writing that is hurried, untidy, or illegible
  • Synonym: scribble - to write or draw something quickly or carelessly; untidy writing

Etymology

From Greek hieroglyphikos, from hieros 'sacred' + glyphein 'to carve'.

3. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪərəˈɡlɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪərəˈɡlɪfɪk / (BrE)
(of writing or symbols) resembling hieroglyphs or having the characteristics of hieroglyphic script, especially being difficult to read or interpret.
resembling hieroglyphic writing

Examples

The old map was covered in hieroglyphic symbols that no one could decipher.
Her handwriting was so hieroglyphic that even her teachers struggled to read it.
The archaeologist studied the hieroglyphic carvings on the ancient temple walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hieroglyphical - alternative, less common adjectival form meaning 'of or like a hieroglyph'

Etymology

From French 'hiéroglyphique', from Latin 'hieroglyphicus', from Greek 'hieroglyphikos', from 'hieros' (sacred) + 'glyphein' (to carve).

4. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪərəˈɡlɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪərəˈɡlɪfɪk / (BrE)
(of writing) written in or relating to a system of writing using pictorial symbols, especially that of ancient Egypt.
written in pictorial symbols

Examples

The ancient Egyptians used hieroglyphic inscriptions on their temple walls.
Researchers have discovered hieroglyphic writing inside the tomb.
His journal included hieroglyphic characters that puzzled his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pictographic - written in or relating to pictorial writing symbols

Etymology

From Greek hieroglyphikos, from hieros (sacred) + glyphein (to carve).
