papoose

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpus / (AmE), / pəˈpuːs / (BrE)
a Native American baby or young child
Native American infant or child

Examples

The mother carried her papoose on her back as she walked through the forest.
In traditional culture, a papoose was often wrapped snugly in a cradleboard.
The story tells of a papoose growing up among the tribe.

Etymology

From Narragansett (Algonquian language) 'papoos', meaning 'child'.
