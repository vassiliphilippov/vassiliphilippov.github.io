Spiranthes spiralis

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈrænθiːz spaɪˈraɪlɪs / (AmE), / spaɪˈrænθiːz spaɪˈraɪlɪs / (BrE)
a small European orchid with racemes of snow-white flowers arranged in a spiral pattern
a European orchid species with spiraling white flowers

Examples

The delicate Spiranthes spiralis can be found blooming in chalk grasslands in late summer.
Botanists were thrilled to discover a cluster of Spiranthes spiralis in the protected reserve.
With its spiraling white petals, Spiranthes spiralis stands out among other wild orchids.

Etymology

From Latin: 'spira' meaning coil and 'anthos' meaning flower; 'spiralis' means spiral-shaped.
