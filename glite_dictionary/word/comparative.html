comparative

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈpær.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / kəmˈpær.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
involving the act of comparing; relating to or based on comparison between two or more things
relating to comparison

Examples

The comparative study of different cultures can reveal surprising similarities.
In English, adjectives like 'bigger' and 'smarter' are in the comparative form.
He wrote a comparative analysis of French and Spanish grammar.

Synset Examples

comparative linguistics
the comparative form of an adjective

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relative - considered in relation or in proportion to something else
  • Antonym: absolute - not dependent on or referring to anything else; not comparative

Etymology

From Latin 'comparativus', from 'comparare' (to compare).

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈpæɹ.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / kəmˈpæɹ.ət.ɪv / (BrE)
the form of an adjective or adverb that expresses a higher degree of the quality, usually formed in English by adding '-er', or by using 'more' or 'less' before the word
grammar term: higher degree of adjective/adverb

Examples

In the sentence 'She is taller than her brother,' 'taller' is a comparative.
Some adjectives form the comparative by using 'more' or 'less' before the word.
Teachers often ask students to identify the comparative forms in a list of adjectives.

Synset Examples

‘faster’ is the comparative of the adjective ‘fast’
‘less famous’ is the comparative degree of the adjective ‘famous’
‘more surely’ is the comparative of the adverb ‘surely’

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: superlative - the highest degree of comparison for adjectives/adverbs

Etymology

From Latin comparativus (relating to comparison), from comparare (to compare).

3. ADJ

/ kəmˈpæɹ.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / kəmˈpær.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
considered in relation to something else; estimated by comparison rather than being absolute or complete.
measured or judged by comparison

Examples

After the accident, he returned to a comparative state of health, though not fully recovered.
The report discussed the comparative merits of the two proposals without drawing firm conclusions.
Living in the countryside offered them a comparative sense of peace compared to the city.

Synset Examples

a relative stranger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relative - considered in relation or in proportion to something else
  • Antonym: absolute - not dependent on or compared with anything else; complete or total

Etymology

From Latin comparativus, from comparare (to compare).
