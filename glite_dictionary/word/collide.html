to collide

1. VERB

/ kəˈlaɪd / (AmE), / kəˈlaɪd / (BrE)
(of two or more objects) to crash together or come into violent contact while moving.
crash together forcefully

Examples

Police reported that two vehicles collided at the busy intersection last night.
If two opinions collide, it can lead to a heated debate.
The football players collided midair as they both jumped for the ball.

Synset Examples

The cars collided
Two meteors clashed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crash - to hit something noisily and violently
  • Synonym: clash - to come together forcefully, often with a loud noise
  • Antonym: to avoid - to keep away from or prevent contact with

Etymology

From Latin collidere, from com- (together) + laedere (to strike).

2. VERB

/ kəˈlaɪd / (AmE), / kəˈlaɪd / (BrE)
(of ideas, interests, or qualities) to be incompatible or to come into conflict with each other.
come into conflict; be incompatible

Examples

Their interests collide on many important issues.
Sometimes personalities collide within a close-knit team, causing tension.
The director's artistic vision collided with the producer's commercial goals.

Synset Examples

These colors clash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clash - to be incompatible or in conflict, especially in appearance or qualities
  • Antonym: to harmonize - to be in agreement or compatible

Etymology

From Latin 'collidere', from 'com-' (together) + 'laedere' (to strike).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02673757-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kəˈlaɪd / (AmE), / kəˈlaɪd / (BrE)
to cause (two or more things) to crash or strike against each other with force
cause objects to crash into each other

Examples

The laboratory experiment requires us to collide these two types of molecules.
In particle accelerators, scientists collide protons at near light speed.
To test the safety features, engineers collide cars under controlled conditions.

Synset Examples

The physicists collided the particles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crash - to strike or hit something violently (transitive verb sense)

Etymology

From Latin *collidere* (to strike together), from *com-* (together) + *laedere* (to strike).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
