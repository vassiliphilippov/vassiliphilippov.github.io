superlatively

1. ADV

/ suːˈpɝː.lə.tɪv.li / (AmE), / suːˈpɜː.lə.tɪv.li / (BrE)
to the highest or greatest possible degree; extremely well or excellently.
to the highest possible degree

Examples

The chef was superlatively skilled, creating dishes that delighted every guest.
She performed superlatively on the exam, earning the highest score in the class.
The film is superlatively acted, with every character leaving a strong impression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excellently - in an excellent manner
  • Antonym: poorly - in a poor manner; badly

Etymology

From 'superlative' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'superlativus' (exaggerated, excellent).
