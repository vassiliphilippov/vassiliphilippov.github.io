raddle

1. VERB

/ ˈræd.əl / (AmE), / ˈræd.əl / (BrE)
to twist or braid together; to interlace strands or elements
twist or braid together

Examples

The artisan skillfully raddled the willow branches to form a sturdy basket.
During the workshop, children learned how to raddle colorful threads into simple patterns.
She raddled the vines tightly, ensuring the fence would hold up through the season.

Etymology

Perhaps related to Middle English 'radel' meaning 'sieve' or 'grate', possibly from Old English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræd.əl / (AmE), / ˈræd.əl / (BrE)
a red iron ore used as a pigment in dyeing and for marking animals or surfaces
red iron ore pigment

Examples

She used raddle to mark the sheep during shearing season.
The potter mixed raddle with water to create a vivid red glaze.
Historically, raddle was valued for its use in traditional textile dyeing.

Etymology

Probably related to Middle English *radel*, from Old English *read* (red), referring to its color.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15032854-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈrædəl / (AmE), / ˈrædəl / (BrE)
to mark or paint (an animal or object) with a colored substance, especially to identify or distinguish it
mark or paint with colored substance

Examples

The farmer used a brush to raddle the sheep before the sale.
Each ram was raddled with a different color for easy identification.
They raddled the fences with bright paint to mark the property boundaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mark - to put a visible sign on something
  • Synonym: paint - to apply color to a surface

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *rad* (red), or alteration of *ruddle* (a red ochre used for marking).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00509925-v
Synset POS: v
