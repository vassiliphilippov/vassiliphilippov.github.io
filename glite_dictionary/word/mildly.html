mildly

1. ADV

/ ˈmaɪldli / (AmE), / ˈmaɪldli / (BrE)
To a moderate or slight degree; not seriously or strongly.
to a moderate or slight degree

Examples

She was mildly amused by the unexpected comment.
The soup was mildly spicy, making it suitable for most people.
He reacted mildly to the criticism, choosing not to argue.

Synset Examples

he was mildly interested

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slightly - to a small degree; not much
  • Antonym: intensely - to a very great degree, with strong force or feeling
  • Degree Higher: strongly - to a high or strong degree
  • Degree Lower: barely - only just, almost not

Etymology

From 'mild' + '-ly', dating from Middle English, 'mild' from Old English 'milde'.

2. ADV

/ ˈmaɪldli / (AmE), / ˈmaɪldli / (BrE)
In a gentle, not extreme, or not harsh manner.
gently or not severely

Examples

She spoke mildly to the children to calm them down.
The weather was mildly unpleasant but not unbearable.
He was mildly surprised by the unexpected news.

Synset Examples

he talked gently to the injured animal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gently - in a gentle or mild manner
  • Antonym: harshly - in a harsh or severe manner

Etymology

From 'mild' + '-ly', Old English 'milde' (gentle, merciful).
