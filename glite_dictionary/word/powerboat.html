powerboat

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊərˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈpaʊəˌbəʊt / (BrE)
a boat powered by an internal-combustion engine, especially designed for speed or racing
boat powered by an engine

Examples

We spent the afternoon racing our powerboat around the lake.
The noise from the powerboat engines echoed across the water.
He bought a new powerboat to take part in the annual river regatta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motorboat - a boat powered by an engine

Etymology

Formed from 'power' and 'boat', indicating a boat powered by an engine.
