cholelithiasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊlɪlɪˈθaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒlɪlɪˈθaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which gallstones are present in the gallbladder
presence of gallstones in the gallbladder
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with cholelithiasis after complaining of severe abdominal pain.
Surgery was required to treat her recurrent cholelithiasis.
Ultrasound imaging revealed multiple gallstones, confirming cholelithiasis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallstone - a small, hard deposit that forms in the gallbladder

Etymology

From Greek kholē (bile) + -lithiasis (formation of stones).
