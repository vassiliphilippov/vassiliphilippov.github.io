heliograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːliəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈhiːliəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an apparatus that uses a mirror to send telegraphic messages by reflecting flashes of sunlight
device sending signals by reflected sunlight

Examples

The army used a heliograph to send coded messages across the desert.
In clear weather, the heliograph could transmit signals over a hundred miles.
The museum's collection includes a 19th-century heliograph used by explorers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semaphore - system for sending messages using visual signals

Etymology

From Greek hēlios (sun) + -graph (writing).

2. VERB

/ ˈhiː.li.əˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈhiː.li.əˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
to send a message by reflecting sunlight with a mirror, usually as a form of signal communication
send signal with reflected sunlight

Examples

The scouts heliographed their position to the base camp using a small mirror.
During the expedition, they heliographed an urgent message across the valley.
She was taught how to heliograph in case radio communication was lost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flash - to shine or send a brief light signal

Etymology

From Greek hēlios (sun) + -graph (writing or recording).
