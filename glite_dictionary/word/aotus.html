Aotus

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.əˌtʌs / (AmE), / ˈaʊ.təs / (BrE)
A genus of small nocturnal South American monkeys, commonly known as night monkeys or douroucoulis.
a genus of nocturnal South American monkeys

Examples

The Aotus is remarkable for its large eyes adapted to nocturnal life.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Aotus genus in the Amazon rainforest.
Unlike most monkeys, Aotus species are active mostly at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: night monkey - common name for species of the genus Aotus

Etymology

From Greek a- (without) + ōt- (ear), referencing the monkeys' inconspicuous ears.

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.oʊ.tʌs / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.əʊ.təs / (BrE)
A genus of nocturnal monkeys, known as night monkeys or douroucoulis, native to Central and South America.
genus of night monkeys

Examples

The Aotus genus includes several species commonly called night monkeys.
Researchers observed that Aotus are uniquely adapted to nocturnal life.
Many zoos have a breeding program for various Aotus species to aid conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: douroucouli - another name for a night monkey of the genus Aotus
  • Synonym: owl monkey - a common name for monkeys of the genus Aotus

Etymology

From Greek aōtos meaning 'earless', referring to the monkeys’ indistinct external ears.
