lexicography

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛksɪˈkɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌlɛksɪˈkɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the practice or process of writing, editing, or compiling dictionaries
the writing or compiling of dictionaries

Examples

She studied lexicography at university, fascinated by the history and evolution of words.
Modern lexicography relies heavily on computer databases and linguistic research.
The conference brought together experts in lexicography from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lexicographer - a person who compiles dictionaries

Etymology

From Greek lexikon (dictionary) + -graphia (writing).
