apologist

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / əˈpɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who defends or justifies a particular policy, institution, or belief, often by argument
person who defends a belief or policy

Examples

She became known as an apologist for government surveillance programs.
The senator was labeled an apologist for the controversial new law.
Historians sometimes act as apologists when evaluating past regimes.

Synset Examples

an apologist for capital punishment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defender - someone who supports or justifies a person or cause
  • Antonym: critic - a person who expresses contrary or disapproving opinions

Etymology

From Late Latin apologistēs, from Greek apologētēs (one who speaks in defense).
