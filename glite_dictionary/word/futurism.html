futurism

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːtʃəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfjuːtʃərɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or attitude that the purpose or meaning of life is to be found in the future rather than the past or present
belief in the primacy of the future

Examples

Many philosophers of futurism argue that society should prioritize long-term goals over immediate concerns.
His worldview was shaped by futurism, leading him to always focus on what could be achieved next.
Critics of futurism claim it risks neglecting valuable lessons from the past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progressivism - support for or advocacy of social reform and progress

Etymology

From Italian futurismo, from futuro 'future.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːtʃərɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfjʊːtʃərɪzəm / (BrE)
an early 20th-century artistic and social movement originating in Italy, emphasizing speed, technology, and modernity inspired by the machine age
Italian art movement inspired by the machine age

Examples

The influence of futurism can be seen in the bold lines and dynamic compositions of the painting.
He wrote his thesis on the impact of futurism in modern architecture.
Many artists embraced futurism as a way to celebrate the technological advances of their time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cubism - an early 20th-century art movement emphasizing abstract structure
  • Synonym: modernism - a style or movement in the arts that aims to break with classical and traditional forms

Etymology

From Italian futurismo, from futuro (future), reflecting the movement's focus on the future and progress.
