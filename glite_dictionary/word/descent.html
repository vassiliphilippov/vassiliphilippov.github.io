descent

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛnt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnt / (BrE)
an act or process of moving downward to a lower place or position
movement downward

Examples

The hikers began their descent into the valley as the sun set.
The plane's descent was smooth, and we landed safely.
He lost his footing during the steep descent of the mountain trail.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ascent - an upward movement

Etymology

From Old French descente, from Latin descendere (to go down).
