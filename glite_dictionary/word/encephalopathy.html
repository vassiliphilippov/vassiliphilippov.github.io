encephalopathy

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˌsɛfəˈlɑpəθi / (AmE), / ɛnˌsɛfəˈlɒpəθi / (BrE)
any disorder or disease of the brain, especially those affecting brain function
brain disorder or disease
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with encephalopathy after presenting symptoms of confusion and memory loss.
Liver failure can sometimes lead to a serious brain disorder called hepatic encephalopathy.
Doctors monitored him closely for any signs of encephalopathy during his treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encephalitis - inflammation of the brain
  • Degree Lower: neuropathy - disease or dysfunction of nerves

Etymology

From Greek 'enkephalos' (brain) + 'pathos' (disease, suffering).
