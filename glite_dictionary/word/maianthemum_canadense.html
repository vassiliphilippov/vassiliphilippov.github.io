Maianthemum canadense

1. NOUN

/ maɪˌænθɨməm kəˈnædənsi / (AmE), / maɪˌænθɪməm kəˈnændənsɪ / (BrE)
A small perennial herb native to northern North America, with two leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.
North American woodland herb

Examples

In the spring, Maianthemum canadense carpets the forest floor with its delicate white blossoms.
Hikers in New England often spot patches of Maianthemum canadense growing beneath the trees.
The fragrant flowers of Maianthemum canadense attract a variety of pollinators in northern woodlands.

Etymology

From neo-Latin genus name Maianthemum and species epithet canadense, meaning 'from Canada.'
