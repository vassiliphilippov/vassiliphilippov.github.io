chondrosarcoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.droʊ.sɑrˈkoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.drəʊ.sɑːˈkəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor that develops from cartilage cells, typically found in bones.
malignant tumor of cartilage cells
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that the rare bone tumor was a chondrosarcoma and would require immediate treatment.
Research on chondrosarcoma has led to new approaches in bone cancer therapy.
After reviewing the biopsy, the pathologist confirmed the diagnosis of chondrosarcoma.

Etymology

From Greek 'chondros' (cartilage) + 'sarcoma' (fleshy tumor).
