Dicksoniaceae

1. NOUN

/ dɪkˌsoʊniˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / dɪkˌsəʊnɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of tree ferns that includes the genera Dicksonia, Cibotium, Culcita, and Thyrsopteris elegans.
a family of tree ferns

Examples

The Dicksoniaceae are common in subtropical and temperate forests around the world.
Several species in the Dicksoniaceae family are cultivated as ornamental plants.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of ferns in the Dicksoniaceae to understand their evolutionary history.

Etymology

From the genus name Dicksonia, named after James Dickson, a British botanist, with the suffix -aceae denoting a plant family, from Latin.

2. NOUN

/ dɪkˌsoʊniˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / dɪkˌsəʊnɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of tree ferns comprising the genera Dicksonia, Cibotium, Culcita, and Thyrsopteris, known for their large fronds and often woody trunks
a family of tree ferns

Examples

The Dicksoniaceae family includes some of the most impressive tree ferns in temperate forests.
Members of the Dicksoniaceae are characterized by their massive, woody stems and expansive fronds.
Botanists study Dicksoniaceae to understand the evolution of ancient fern lineages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree fern - any of various ferns with a trunk elevating the crown

Etymology

From the genus Dicksonia, named after James Dickson, Scottish botanist (1738–1822), plus the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
