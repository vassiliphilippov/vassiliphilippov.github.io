gist

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪst / (AmE), / dʒɪst / (BrE)
the most important or essential part of a speech, text, conversation, or experience; the main point or substance.
main or essential part; essence

Examples

She quickly summarized the gist of the article for those who didn’t have time to read it.
I didn’t understand every word, but I got the gist of what he was saying.
Can you please repeat the gist of your argument so I’m sure I understand?

Synset Examples

the gist of the prosecutor's argument
the heart and soul of the Republican Party
the nub of the story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essence - the basic nature or most important quality of something
  • Synonym: core - the central or most important part of something

Etymology

From Old French *giste* (it lies, a legal term from Latin *jacēre* ‘to lie’).

2. NOUN

/ dʒɪst / (AmE), / dʒɪst / (BrE)
the essential or main point of something, especially a speech, text, or argument
main point or essence

Examples

He quickly summarized the gist of the report for the team.
Although she spoke for hours, I only caught the gist of her argument.
Can you give me the gist of what happened at the meeting?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essence - the most important or central aspect of something
  • Synonym: substance - the most important or essential part of something
  • Antonym: detail - an individual fact or item

Etymology

From Old French *gésir* (to lie), from Latin *jacēre* (to lie).
