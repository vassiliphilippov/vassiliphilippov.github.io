tuberosity

1. NOUN

/ ˌtuːbəˈrɑːsəti / (AmE), / ˌtjuːbəˈrɒsəti / (BrE)
A rounded prominence or protuberance on a bone, usually serving as the site for the attachment of muscles or ligaments.
bony projection for muscle/ligament attachment
medical

Examples

The deltoid tuberosity is where the deltoid muscle attaches to the humerus.
A fracture near the tibial tuberosity can affect knee function.
Palpation revealed tenderness over the ischial tuberosity after the injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protuberance - A thing that protrudes from something else; anatomical bump or projection

Etymology

From Latin tuberositas (knobbiness, lumpiness), from tuber (swelling, lump).
