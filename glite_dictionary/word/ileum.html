ileum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪliəm / (AmE), / ˈɪliəm / (BrE)
the third and final part of the small intestine, located between the jejunum and the cecum
part of the small intestine
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed a small section of the ileum during the procedure.
Diseases of the ileum, such as Crohn's disease, can cause significant digestive problems.
Absorption of vitamin B12 mainly occurs in the ileum.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: jejunum - the part of the small intestine between the duodenum and the ileum
  • Degree Higher: cecum - the pouch forming the beginning of the large intestine, following the ileum

Etymology

From Latin *ileum*, originally mistakingly associated with *ileus* (a kind of intestinal obstruction).
