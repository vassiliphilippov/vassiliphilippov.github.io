Archosauria

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑr.kəˈsɔr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌɑː.kəˈsɔː.ri.ə / (BrE)
a major group of diapsid reptiles that includes crocodiles, alligators, dinosaurs, pterosaurs, and their extinct relatives
group of reptiles including crocs and dinosaurs

Examples

Scientists believe that Archosauria first appeared during the late Permian period.
Both modern crocodiles and extinct dinosaurs are members of the Archosauria.
Fossil studies of Archosauria have provided insight into reptilian evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinosaur - any of a group of extinct reptiles of the Mesozoic era
  • Synonym: crocodile - large aquatic reptile within Archosauria

Etymology

From Greek 'archos' (ruler) + 'sauros' (lizard)

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑr.kəˈsɔr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌɑː.kəˈsɔː.ri.ə / (BrE)
a large subclass of extinct and living diapsid reptiles that includes crocodiles, alligators, dinosaurs, and their relatives
subclass of reptiles including dinosaurs and crocodilians

Examples

The discovery of a new fossil helped scientists better understand the evolution of Archosauria.
Crocodiles are one of the few living members of the Archosauria subclass.
Many prehistoric reptiles, including dinosaurs and pterosaurs, belonged to Archosauria.

Etymology

From Greek 'arkhos' (ruler) + 'sauros' (lizard), meaning 'ruling lizard'.
