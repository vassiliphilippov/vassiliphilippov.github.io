settlings

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛt.lɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.lɪŋz / (BrE)
Small particles or sediment that have settled at the bottom of a liquid.
sediment at bottom of liquid

Examples

After filtering the wine, only a small amount of settlings remained in the bottle.
The laboratory technician carefully poured off the clear liquid, leaving the settlings behind.
Tea often contains settlings at the bottom of the cup if not strained.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sediment - matter that settles to the bottom of a liquid

Etymology

From the verb 'settle' meaning to sink or come to rest, with the plural noun suffix '-ing.'
