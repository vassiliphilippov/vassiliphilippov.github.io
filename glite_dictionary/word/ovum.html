ovum

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vəm / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vəm / (BrE)
a female reproductive cell or gamete, especially one capable of developing into a new organism after fertilization
female reproductive cell; female gamete
medical

Examples

The scientist observed the development of the ovum under a microscope.
Each ovum carries half of the genetic material required for a new life.
When the ovum is fertilized by a sperm cell, the process of conception begins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egg cell - the female reproductive cell (synonym for ovum)
  • Antonym: sperm - the male reproductive cell or gamete

Etymology

From Latin ovum (egg).
