Carolus Linnaeus

1. PROPN

/ kəˈroʊləs lɪˈniːəs / (AmE), / kəˈrɒləs lɪˈniːəs / (BrE)
(1707–1778) The Swedish botanist who established the modern system of naming organisms in biology, known as binomial nomenclature.
Swedish botanist; created biological naming system

Examples

Carolus Linnaeus revolutionized taxonomy by introducing a standardized naming system for plants and animals.
Modern botanical studies often reference the classification methods established by Carolus Linnaeus.
Many scientific names in biology still follow the conventions set by Carolus Linnaeus centuries ago.

Etymology

Latinized form of Carl Linnaeus, the botanist's Swedish name.
