Eimeriidae

1. NOUN

/ aɪˌmɪəriˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / aɪˌmɪərɪˈaɪdɪiː / (BrE)
A family of single-celled parasitic protozoa in the order Coccidia, many of which infect the intestines of animals.
family of parasitic protozoa (Coccidia)

Examples

The family Eimeriidae contains several species that are significant pathogens in poultry.
Researchers studied the life cycle of parasites in the Eimeriidae to better understand coccidial infections.
Veterinarians often encounter diseases caused by members of the Eimeriidae family in livestock.

Etymology

From the genus Eimeria, named after German zoologist Theodor Eimer, combined with the standard zoological family suffix -idae.

2. NOUN

/ iːˌmɪəriˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / iːˌmɪəriˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of protozoan parasites in the order Coccidia, some of which cause disease in animals and humans.
family of protozoan parasites
medical

Examples

Certain species in the Eimeriidae family can cause coccidiosis in poultry.
Scientists identified the parasite as belonging to the Eimeriidae family.
Members of the Eimeriidae are studied for their complex life cycles.

Etymology

Named after genus Eimeria, itself after German zoologist Theodor Eimer, with taxonomic suffix -idae used for animal families.
