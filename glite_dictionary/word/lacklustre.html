lacklustre

1. ADJ

/ ˈlækˌlʌstər / (AmE), / ˈlakˌlʌstə / (BrE)
not bright, shining, or energetic; dull or uninspired.
dull; lacking brightness or vitality
british

Examples

The team gave a lacklustre performance in last night's match, disappointing their fans.
Despite her fame, her latest movie received mostly lacklustre reviews from critics.
He looked up with lacklustre eyes, clearly exhausted from the journey.

Synset Examples

staring with lackluster eyes
lusterless hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - not bright, lively, or sharp; lacking interest or excitement
  • Antonym: vivid - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind; bright or intense

Etymology

From lack + lustre; originally used in Shakespeare's 'As You Like It' (1590s). 'Lustre' from Latin 'lustrum'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlækˌlʌstər / (AmE), / ˈlakˌlʌstə / (BrE)
Lacking in vitality, force, or brilliance; dull or uninspired.
dull or uninspired; lacking vitality
british

Examples

Her lacklustre performance disappointed the critics who expected more energy.
The team gave a lacklustre effort and lost the match by a wide margin.
His speech was so lacklustre that half the audience fell asleep.

Synset Examples

a dull lackluster life
a lusterless performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lackluster - Lacking in vitality or brightness (American spelling)
  • Synonym: dull - Not bright, lively, or interesting; lacking sharpness or vivacity
  • Antonym: brilliant - Full of brightness or vitality; very impressive

Etymology

From lack + lustre, with 'lustre' meaning brightness or radiance, from Latin 'lustrare' (to illuminate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00811581-s
Synset POS: s
