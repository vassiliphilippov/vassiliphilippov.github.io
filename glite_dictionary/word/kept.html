kept

1. ADJ

/ kɛpt / (AmE), / kɛpt / (BrE)
(of a promise, contract, or similar obligation) not violated or disregarded; maintained or fulfilled as agreed.
fulfilled; not broken (of promises etc.)

Examples

She always valued a kept promise more than empty words.
The peace treaty was a kept agreement for over ten years.
A kept contract builds trust between business partners.

Synset Examples

unbroken promises
promises kept

Related Terms

  • Antonym: broken - (of promises etc.) violated or not fulfilled

Etymology

Past participle of 'keep', from Old English 'cēpan' (to observe, hold).
