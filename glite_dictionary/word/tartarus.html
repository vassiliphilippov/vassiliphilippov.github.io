Tartarus

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑːr.tər.əs / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.tə.rəs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the deep abyss used as a dungeon of torment and suffering for the wicked after death.
Greek mythological underworld for the wicked

Examples

In ancient myths, souls condemned for their crimes would be cast into Tartarus for eternal punishment.
The poet described Tartarus as a dark, fearsome pit far beneath the earth.
According to legend, the worst sinners dwelled in Tartarus, suffering for their misdeeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hades - the underworld or afterlife in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Τάρταρος (Tártaros), the name of the abyss in Greek mythology.
