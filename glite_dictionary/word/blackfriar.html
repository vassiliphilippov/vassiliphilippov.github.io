Blackfriar

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌfraɪər / (AmE), / ˈblækˌfraɪə / (BrE)
a member of the Dominican order of Roman Catholic friars, distinguished by their black mantle
Dominican friar; member of a Roman Catholic order

Examples

The old monastery was once home to several Blackfriars who dedicated their lives to study and teaching.
In medieval London, the Blackfriars played an important role in religious education.
She recognized the distinctive black habits of the Blackfriars as they walked through the cloister.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dominican - a member of the Roman Catholic religious order founded by St. Dominic

Etymology

From the black mantle worn by members of the Dominican order and 'friar' (from Old French 'frere', brother).
