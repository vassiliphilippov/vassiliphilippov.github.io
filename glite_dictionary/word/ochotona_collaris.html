Ochotona collaris

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊkəˈtoʊnə kəˈlærɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒkəˈtəʊnə kəˈlarɪs / (BrE)
A small, herbivorous mammal of the pika family found in Alaska and northwestern Canada, known as the collared pika.
species of pika (collared pika)

Examples

The Ochotona collaris inhabits rocky slopes in the alpine tundra of northwestern North America.
Researchers observed that Ochotona collaris stores piles of vegetation called haystacks for winter food.
Unlike other pikas, Ochotona collaris is solitary and rarely mingles with its neighbors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pika - small mammal related to rabbits, found in Asia and North America

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'ochotos' (a kind of hare); species name from Latin 'collaris', meaning 'of the neck, collared'.
