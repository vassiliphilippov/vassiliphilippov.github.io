religiously

1. ADV

/ rɪˈlɪdʒəsli / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəsli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or is based on religion.
in a manner relating to religion

Examples

The festival is celebrated religiously every year by the entire community.
Some laws are followed religiously in accordance with ancient customs.
The paintings in the temple are religiously inspired masterpieces.

Synset Examples

religiously inspired art

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spiritually - in a spiritual manner rather than a religious one

Etymology

From 'religious' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'religiosus'.

Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ rɪˈlɪdʒəsli / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows great care, dedication, or regularity; with extreme conscientiousness.
with great care or regularity

Examples

He exercises religiously every morning before breakfast.
She religiously checks her email throughout the day.
They religiously follow all the safety procedures at work.

Synset Examples

he came religiously every morning at 8 o'clock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faithfully - in a loyal or consistent way
  • Antonym: irregularly - not at regular intervals or consistently

Etymology

From Latin religiosus (pious, devout), via Old French and Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00180072-r
Synset POS: r
