curialism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjʊriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkjʊəriəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the influence or practices of a court or governing body, especially in the context of politics or administration
influence of a court or governing body

Examples

The historian analyzed how curialism shaped governmental reforms in the 18th century.
Recent scholarship points to lingering curialism in institutional decision-making processes.
Critics argue that modern administrations are still influenced by forms of curialism despite democratic reforms.

Synset Examples

Her research focused on the effects of curialism in modern politics.

Etymology

From Latin 'curia' (court, assembly) + '-ism'.
