soaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈsəʊkə / (BrE)
a person who habitually drinks excessive amounts of alcohol; a habitual drunkard
habitual excessive drinker
informal

Examples

Everyone knows that Tom is a bit of a soaker and rarely misses a night out at the pub.
The old soaker at the bar was already on his third pint by noon.
She warned me to avoid becoming a soaker like some of her former colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drunkard - a person who is habitually drunk
  • Antonym: teetotaler - a person who abstains completely from alcohol

Etymology

From soak (to absorb liquid; to drink heavily) + -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈsəʊkə / (BrE)
a period of heavy rainfall; a downpour
a heavy rain
informal

Examples

We had to cancel the picnic because a soaker hit the park that afternoon.
Last night’s storm was a real soaker, flooding the streets in just an hour.
Bring your umbrella—looks like we’re in for another soaker today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downpour - a heavy and sudden rainfall
  • Degree Lower: drizzle - light rain falling in very fine drops
