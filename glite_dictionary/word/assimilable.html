assimilable

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɪləbəl / (AmE), / əˈsɪləbəl / (BrE)
(of a substance) able to be absorbed and incorporated into body tissues.
able to be absorbed into body tissues
medical

Examples

Certain vitamins are assimilable only when consumed with fat.
The mineral supplements must be in an assimilable form to benefit the body.
Many nutrients in processed foods are less assimilable than those in fresh produce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorbable - able to be absorbed or taken in by the body

Etymology

From French *assimilable*, from Latin *assimilabilis* (that can be made similar or absorbed).
