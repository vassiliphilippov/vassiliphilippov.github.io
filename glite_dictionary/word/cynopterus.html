Cynopterus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈnɑptərəs / (AmE), / sɪˈnɒptərəs / (BrE)
a genus of fruit bats found in South and Southeast Asia, comprising several species known as short-nosed fruit bats.
genus of Asian fruit bats

Examples

The Cynopterus genus includes several species commonly found in tropical forests.
Scientists studied how Cynopterus bats disperse seeds in their native habitats.
Members of the Cynopterus genus are sometimes called short-nosed fruit bats.

Etymology

From Greek *kynos* (dog) + *pteron* (wing), referring to the dog-like face and wings.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈnɑptərəs / (AmE), / sɪˈnɒptərəs / (BrE)
A genus of fruit bats found in South and Southeast Asia, comprising several species commonly known as short-nosed fruit bats.
genus of Asian fruit bats

Examples

Cynopterus is a genus containing several species of short-nosed fruit bats common in Asia.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Cynopterus genus.
Members of Cynopterus are important for the dispersal of fruit seeds in their habitats.

Etymology

From Greek kynos (dog) + pteron (wing), referring to the dog-like face and wings of the bats.
