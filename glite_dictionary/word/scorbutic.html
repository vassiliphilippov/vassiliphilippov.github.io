scorbutic

1. ADJ

/ skɔrˈbjuːtɪk / (AmE), / skɔːˈbjuːtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by scurvy; having symptoms of scurvy.
relating to scurvy or its symptoms
medical

Examples

The sailor displayed several scorbutic symptoms, including bleeding gums and joint pain.
A scorbutic condition was common among explorers during long sea voyages without fresh fruit.
Doctors recognized the scorbutic lesions as classic signs of vitamin C deficiency.

Synset Examples

scorbutic symptoms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scurvy - a disease caused by vitamin C deficiency

Etymology

From New Latin scorbuticus, from Latin scorbutus (scurvy).
