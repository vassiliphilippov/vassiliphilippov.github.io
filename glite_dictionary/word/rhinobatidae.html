Rhinobatidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌraɪnoʊˈbætɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌraɪnəˈbætɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of primitive rays known for their guitar-shaped bodies, inhabiting marine environments.
family of guitar-shaped rays

Examples

The family Rhinobatidae includes several species of guitarfish found in warm ocean waters.
Scientists are studying the unique skeletal structure of Rhinobatidae to understand their evolutionary history.
Unlike most other rays, members of the Rhinobatidae have bodies resembling the shape of a guitar.

Etymology

From New Latin Rhinobatidae, based on genus name Rhinobatus (from Greek rhinos 'nose' + batis 'ray').

2. NOUN

/ ˌraɪnoʊˈbætɪdi / (AmE), / ˌrɪnəʊˈbatɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of primitive rays known as guitarfishes, characterized by their guitar-shaped bodies
family of guitar-shaped rays (guitarfishes)

Examples

The Rhinobatidae are known for their distinctive, guitar-shaped bodies among cartilaginous fishes.
Members of the Rhinobatidae family can be found in warm, shallow coastal waters worldwide.
Scientists are studying the evolutionary history of the Rhinobatidae to better understand ray diversification.

Etymology

From modern Latin Rhinobatidae, based on Greek rhinos (nose) + batis (ray, skate).
