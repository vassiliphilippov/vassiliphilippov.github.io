going

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a business or enterprise) operating and making progress; in full operation.
in operation; active (esp. business)

Examples

The bakery is still a going concern, serving customers every day.
Despite the challenges, the company remains a going business in the community.
Investors are interested as long as the enterprise is a going concern.

Synset Examples

a going concern

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defunct - no longer existing or functioning
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00036276-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of making progress toward a goal or completing something, especially relating to how easy or difficult it is
progress toward a goal; process of advancing

Examples

The first part of the hike was easy going, but the last mile was steep and rocky.
It can be tough going to learn a new language as an adult.
Despite the obstacles, we made steady going toward our goal.

Synset Examples

persuading him was easy going
the proposal faces tough sledding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progress - forward or onward movement toward a destination or goal
  • Synonym: to slog - a spell of difficult, tiring work or progress
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00048792-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of leaving or departing from a place
the act of departing

Examples

Her sudden going took everyone by surprise.
We were saddened by his going after so many years with us.
After your going, the atmosphere in the office changed completely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: departure - the act of leaving, especially to start a journey
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00043495-n
Synset POS: n
