cab

1. NOUN

/ kæb / (AmE), / kab / (BrE)
the part at the front of a vehicle, such as a truck, train, or crane, where the driver or operator sits
driver's compartment in a vehicle

Examples

The crane operator climbed into the cab to start his shift.
Modern trucks often have comfortable sleeping areas behind the cab.
The engineer waved from the cab of the locomotive as the train departed.

Etymology

Shortened from 'cabriolet', a type of carriage, from French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02934532-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ kæb / (AmE), / kæb / (BrE)
a small two-wheeled horse-drawn carriage with two seats and a folding hood
two-wheeled horse-drawn carriage

Examples

They arrived at the party in a stylish cab drawn by two horses.
London streets were once full of cabs long before motorcars took over.
He hailed a cab to escape the sudden downpour, lifting the folding hood as he got in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hackney carriage - a carriage for hire

Etymology

Shortened from 'cabriolet', from French, originally from Italian 'cabriole' (a light, two-wheeled carriage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02934397-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ kæb / (AmE), / kæb / (BrE)
to ride in a taxicab as a passenger
ride in a taxicab (verb)

Examples

We decided to cab to the airport instead of taking the bus.
She always cabs home after late meetings downtown.
After his injury, he cabbed everywhere for a month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxi - to travel in a taxi (verb)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01952866-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ kæb / (AmE), / kæb / (BrE)
a car that carries passengers to their destination for a fare, typically driven by a hired driver; a taxi.
a car for hire with a driver; taxi

Examples

We hailed a cab to get to the airport on time.
It's often quicker to take a cab than to wait for the bus at night.
The cab driver knew a shortcut through the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxi - a car licensed to transport passengers in return for payment

Etymology

Shortened form of 'cabriolet', from French, originally a light, horse-drawn carriage.

Source: WordNet
