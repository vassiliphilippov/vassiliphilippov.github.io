tardigrade

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːr.dɪˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.dɪ.ɡreɪd / (BrE)
a minute water-dwelling animal of the phylum Tardigrada, also known as a water bear, noted for its extreme survival abilities
microscopic water-dwelling animal (phylum Tardigrada)

Examples

Scientists are fascinated by the tardigrade's ability to survive harsh environments.
The tardigrade is often called a water bear because of its bear-like appearance under a microscope.
Researchers discovered tardigrades living in the most extreme parts of Antarctica.

Etymology

From Latin 'tardigradus', meaning 'slow-paced', from 'tardus' (slow) + 'gradus' (step).
