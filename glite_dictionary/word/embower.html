to embower

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈbaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ɪmˈbaʊ.ə / (BrE)
to enclose or surround in or as if in a bower (a pleasant, sheltered place among trees or climbing plants)
enclose in a leafy shelter

Examples

The cottage was embowered by flowering vines and ancient oaks.
Morning sunlight embowered the path in a delicate green glow.
A garden seat, embowered amid roses, invited quiet reflection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enclose - to surround or close off on all sides

Etymology

From em- (intensive prefix) + bower (Old English 'bur', meaning 'dwelling' or 'chamber').
