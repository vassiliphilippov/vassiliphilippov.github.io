Jean-Luc Godard

1. PROPN

/ ʒɑ̃ lyk ɡoʊˈdɑr / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ lyk ɡəʊˈdɑː / (BrE)
(1930–2022) A French film director and screenwriter known for his innovative filmmaking techniques and influential work in the French New Wave cinema movement.
French director; French New Wave innovator

Examples

Many critics consider Jean-Luc Godard a central figure in the history of modern cinema.
The influence of Jean-Luc Godard can be seen in numerous contemporary filmmakers.
Her thesis analyzed the unique editing style found in Jean-Luc Godard's early films.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: François Truffaut - French film director and leading figure of the French New Wave

2. PROPN

/ ʒɑn luk ɡoʊˈdɑr / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ lyk ɡɔˈdaʁ / (BrE)
(1930–2022) A French film director and screenwriter known for his innovative filmmaking techniques and significant influence on modern cinema, especially through the French New Wave movement.
French film director; key figure in New Wave cinema

Examples

Many critics consider Jean-Luc Godard a pioneer of the French New Wave movement.
The films of Jean-Luc Godard often challenge traditional narrative structures.
My favorite director is Jean-Luc Godard because of his experimental approach to cinema.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: François Truffaut - French film director, contemporary of Godard, also central to the French New Wave
  • Synonym: Alfred Hitchcock - British film director; influential on Godard's style

Etymology

French proper name.
