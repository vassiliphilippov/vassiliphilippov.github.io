weir

1. NOUN

/ wɪr / (AmE), / wɪə / (BrE)
a low dam built across a river or stream to raise the water level or control its flow
a low dam in a river or stream

Examples

The engineers constructed a weir to regulate the flow of water in the canal.
Salmon are often seen jumping over the old stone weir during the spawning season.
After heavy rains, the river rose and spilled over the weir downstream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dam - a barrier constructed to hold back water and raise its level

Etymology

Old English *wer* (dam, fence) related to Old Norse *ver* (a fish trap) and Old High German *weri* (dam).

2. NOUN

/ wɪr / (AmE), / wɪə / (BrE)
a fence or barrier built across a stream or river to catch or retain fish or to control the water flow
barrier in watercourse, often for catching fish

Examples

The villagers built a weir across the stream to catch fish for food.
During the rainy season, the old weir overflowed and the fish escaped downstream.
A wooden weir is often used in small rivers to regulate the water and trap fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dam - a barrier constructed to hold back water
  • Synonym: barrage - an artificial barrier across a river for controlling water flow

Etymology

From Old English wer (a dam or fish trap), related to Old High German wer.
