to dissipate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɪsɪˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪpeɪt / (BrE)
to make something scatter or disappear by breaking it up or dispersing it in different directions
scatter or disperse

Examples

The thick fog began to dissipate as the sun rose higher.
The loud noise dissipated the crowd, sending people in all directions.
He tried to dissipate the tension in the room by telling a joke.

Synset Examples

She waved her hand and scattered the crowds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scatter - to throw or spread things over a wide area
  • Antonym: gather - to bring or come together in a group

Etymology

From Latin dissipare (to scatter, disperse).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02034269-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈdɪsəˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪpeɪt / (BrE)
to live a life indulging in pleasure, often with excessive drinking; to spend one's time and energy in self-indulgence
live a self-indulgent, pleasure-seeking life

Examples

After inheriting his fortune, he began to dissipate and neglected all family responsibilities.
Many poets from the era would dissipate in taverns, losing themselves in drink and revelry.
She warned him not to dissipate his youth in reckless pleasures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carouse - to drink alcohol and enjoy oneself with others in a noisy, lively way
  • Synonym: debauch - to lead away from virtue or excellence, especially by self-indulgence

Etymology

From Latin dissipatus, past participle of dissipare (to scatter, squander).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02620865-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈdɪsəˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪpeɪt / (BrE)
to spend or use resources, especially money or energy, wastefully or foolishly
spend wastefully or foolishly

Examples

After inheriting a fortune, he managed to dissipate his wealth within just a few years.
If you dissipate your savings on unnecessary luxuries, you may regret it later.
She watched helplessly as her brother began to dissipate their family’s money on gambling and parties.

Synset Examples

Fritter away one's inheritance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to squander - to waste something, especially money or time, in a reckless manner
  • Antonym: conserve - to prevent waste of a resource; to save

Etymology

From Latin dissipare (to scatter, squander).

4. VERB

/ ˈdɪsɪˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪpeɪt / (BrE)
to scatter or cause to break up and move in different directions; to disappear by dispersing
scatter or break up and disperse

Examples

When the sun rose, the fog began to dissipate and reveal the landscape.
The crowd started to dissipate after the announcement had been made.
As tension mounted, the group chose to dissipate and avoid further confrontation.

Synset Examples

The crowds dispersed
The children scattered in all directions when the teacher approached

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disperse - to spread over a wide area or cause to go in different directions
  • Synonym: scatter - to throw or separate things in different directions

Etymology

From Latin dissipare (to scatter, disperse, squander).
