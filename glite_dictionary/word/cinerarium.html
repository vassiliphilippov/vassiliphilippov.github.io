cinerarium

1. NOUN

/ sɪnəˈrɛriəm / (AmE), / sɪnəˈreəriəm / (BrE)
A niche or chamber for holding a funeral urn containing the ashes of a cremated person.
niche for funeral urn with ashes

Examples

After the ceremony, the urn was placed in a cinerarium within the family mausoleum.
The ancient Romans often built elaborate cineraria to honor their dead.
Each cinerarium in the columbarium bore the names of multiple family members.

Etymology

From Latin 'cinerarium', from 'cinis' (ashes).
