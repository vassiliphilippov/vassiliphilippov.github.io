Gardenia augusta

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑːrˈdiːniə ɔːˈɡʌstə / (AmE), / ɡɑːˈdiːniə ɔːˈɡʌstə / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub widely cultivated for its large, fragrant, waxy white flowers and glossy leaves.
evergreen shrub with fragrant white flowers

Examples

The Gardenia augusta in our backyard filled the air with its sweet scent each summer.
Botanists admire Gardenia augusta for both its ornamental value and its aromatic blossoms.
Her bouquet included several sprigs of Gardenia augusta to add fragrance and elegance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gardenia - any shrub of the genus Gardenia, cultivated for its fragrant white flowers

Etymology

Genus name 'Gardenia' honors Scottish naturalist Alexander Garden (1730–1791); species epithet from Latin 'augusta'.
