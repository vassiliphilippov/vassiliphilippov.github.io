barnstormer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrnˌstɔrmər / (AmE), / ˈbɑːnˌstɔːmə / (BrE)
an actor who travels from place to place, especially in rural areas, performing plays
traveling actor performing in various places

Examples

In the early 20th century, many barnstormers brought live entertainment to remote villages.
She reminisced about her days as a barnstormer traveling from one small town to another.
The troupe of barnstormers became well-known for their lively Shakespeare productions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touring actor - an actor who performs while traveling to different locations

Etymology

From 'barnstorm', referring to traveling performers visiting rural barns or small towns.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrnˌstɔrmər / (AmE), / ˈbɑːnˌstɔːmə / (BrE)
a pilot who travels around giving public demonstrations of stunt flying and parachuting, especially in the early days of aviation
pilot giving stunt flying exhibitions

Examples

The famous barnstormer thrilled the crowds with his daring aerial loops and dives.
During the 1920s, it was common for a barnstormer to land in a field and offer rides to locals.
As a young man, he dreamed of becoming a barnstormer and travelling from town to town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stunt pilot - a pilot who specializes in performing aerial stunts

Etymology

From 'barnstorm', referring to traveling performers visiting rural towns; in the 1920s applied to pilots giving exhibitions in rural areas.
