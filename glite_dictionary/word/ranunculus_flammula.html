Ranunculus flammula

1. NOUN

/ rəˈnʌŋkjələs ˈflæmjʊlə / (AmE), / rəˈnʌŋkjʊləs ˈflæmjʊlə / (BrE)
A semiaquatic Eurasian perennial plant of the buttercup family, with spear-shaped leaves, often found in wetlands and naturalized in New Zealand.
semiaquatic Eurasian perennial plant species

Examples

Ranunculus flammula is commonly found along the edges of ponds and marshes in Europe.
The bright yellow flowers of Ranunculus flammula add color to wetland habitats in spring and summer.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Ranunculus flammula in water gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spearwort - Another name for Ranunculus flammula

Etymology

From Latin; 'Ranunculus' meaning 'little frog' (referring to its wet habitat), 'flammula' meaning 'little flame' (for the color of flowers).
