throng

1. NOUN

/ θrɑŋ / (AmE), / θrɒŋ / (BrE)
A large, densely packed crowd of people.
large crowd of people

Examples

A huge throng gathered outside the stadium to welcome the team.
The streets were filled with a throng of protesters chanting slogans.
He pushed his way through the throng to reach the front of the stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowd - a large number of people gathered together
  • Synonym: multitude - a large number of people or things

Etymology

From Middle English throng, from Old English thrang (crowd, press).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08199634-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ θrɔŋ / (AmE), / θrɒŋ / (BrE)
to crowd or press closely together in a particular place
to crowd together tightly

Examples

Fans began to throng the stadium hours before the concert started.
Shoppers thronged the mall during the holiday sales.
Locals and tourists alike throng the main square every evening in summer.

Synset Examples

The crowd packed the auditorium

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowd - to gather in large numbers; to fill a place

Etymology

From Old English 'þrong', related to Old Norse 'þrǫng' meaning crowd or press.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02068059-v
Synset POS: v
