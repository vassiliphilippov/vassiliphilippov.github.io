Saint Christopher

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈkrɪstəfər / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈkrɪstəfə / (BrE)
the largest island of the country Saint Kitts and Nevis, located in the West Indies in the Caribbean Sea
largest island of Saint Kitts and Nevis

Examples

Many tourists visit Saint Christopher for its beautiful beaches and historical sites.
The capital city of Saint Christopher is Basseterre, located on the southwest coast.
After the hurricane, aid was quickly sent to Saint Christopher to help with recovery efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Kitts - alternative name for Saint Christopher

Etymology

Named after Saint Christopher (St. Kitts), a Christian saint; originally named by Christopher Columbus in 1493.

2. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈkrɪstəfər / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈkrɪstəfə / (BrE)
A Christian martyr of the 3rd century, venerated as the patron saint of travelers.
Christian patron saint of travelers

Examples

Many people wear a Saint Christopher medal for protection during their journeys.
Legend says that Saint Christopher once carried a child, who was Christ in disguise, across a dangerous river.
Travelers often invoke Saint Christopher for safety before setting out on long trips.

Etymology

From Greek Christophoros, meaning 'Christ-bearer'.
