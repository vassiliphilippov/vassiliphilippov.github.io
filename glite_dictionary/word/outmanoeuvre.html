to outmanoeuvre

1. VERB

/ ˌaʊt.məˈnuː.vɚ / (AmE), / ˌaʊt.məˈnuː.və / (BrE)
to defeat someone by being more skilful or clever in planning or maneuvering
defeat by clever maneuvering
british

Examples

The politician tried to outmanoeuvre his opponents during the debate.
Despite their experience, the defending champions were outmanoeuvred by the underdogs in the final.
She quickly realised she would need to outmanoeuvre her rivals to win the promotion.

Synset Examples

The English troops outmaneuvered the Germans
My new supervisor knows how to outmaneuver the boss in most situations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outmaneuver - to defeat by more skilful maneuvering (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'out-' (beyond) and 'manoeuvre', from French 'manœuvrer'.
