cyon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.ən / (BrE)
An Asiatic wild dog; a member of a genus of wild dogs native to Asia, especially the dhole.
Asiatic wild dog

Examples

The cyon is known for its social hunting behavior in the forests of Central and Southeast Asia.
Few predators threaten the cyon due to its aggressive pack defense.
Researchers observed a pack of cyons tracking their prey across the grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dhole - a type of Asiatic wild dog

Etymology

From Greek kyon (dog).
