Bouteloua eriopoda

1. NOUN

/ buːtəˈluːə ɛriːˈɒpədə / (AmE), / buːtəˈluːə ɛriːˈɒpədə / (BrE)
A species of pasture grass, also known as black grama, native to western regions of North America and important for grazing.
a pasture grass species of western North America

Examples

Bouteloua eriopoda is a dominant grass in many desert grasslands of the southwestern United States.
Ranchers often rely on Bouteloua eriopoda as a primary forage for cattle in arid regions.
Ecologists study Bouteloua eriopoda to understand grassland ecosystem resilience to drought.

Etymology

Genus name 'Bouteloua' honors Spanish botanists; species name from Greek 'erion' (wool) and 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to the woolly base of the grass.
