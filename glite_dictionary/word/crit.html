crit

1. NOUN

/ krɪt / (AmE), / krɪt / (BrE)
a bicycle race held on a short course, typically less than 5 km (3 miles) in length
short-course bicycle race

Examples

She trained hard for the local crit in her city.
The crit course featured sharp corners and fast sprints.
Many professional cyclists started their careers in crits across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criterium - a cycling race on a closed circuit

Etymology

Shortened form of 'criterium', from French 'critérium' (competition).

2. NOUN

/ krɪt / (AmE), / krɪt / (BrE)
a critical evaluation or feedback session, especially regarding creative work such as art or design
critical feedback or review session
informal

Examples

She was nervous before her first crit in art school.
The feedback he got during the crit was very helpful for revising his design.
Each student presents their portfolio at the weekly crit.

Synset Examples

He received a crit that improved his artwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critique - a detailed analysis and assessment of something

Etymology

Informal shortening of 'critique.'
