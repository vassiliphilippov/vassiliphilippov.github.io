Antheropeas wallacei

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθəˈroʊpiəs ˈwɒləsi / (AmE), / ˌænθərəʊˈpiːəs ˈwɒləsi / (BrE)
A tiny grey, woolly, tufted annual plant with small golden-yellow flower heads, native to southeastern California, northwestern Arizona, and southwestern Utah; sometimes classified in the genus Eriophyllum.
a small annual plant of southwestern US deserts

Examples

Antheropeas wallacei is commonly found growing in arid sandy soils of the Mojave Desert.
Botanists sometimes refer to Antheropeas wallacei as a member of the genus Eriophyllum.
The small golden-yellow flowers of Antheropeas wallacei attract numerous pollinators in spring.
