despot

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛsˌpɑt / (AmE), / ˈdɛspət / (BrE)
a ruler who holds absolute power, typically exercising it in a cruel or oppressive way
oppressive or tyrannical ruler

Examples

The country was ruled for decades by a despot who suppressed all opposition.
Throughout history, many despots have used fear to maintain their grip on power.
The citizens finally overthrew the despot, bringing hope for a more just government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tyrant - a cruel and oppressive ruler
  • Synonym: autocrat - a ruler with absolute power
  • Antonym: democrat - an advocate or supporter of democracy

Etymology

From Greek *despotēs* (master, absolute ruler), via Latin *despota*.
