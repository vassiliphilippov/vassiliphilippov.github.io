comb

1. NOUN

/ koʊm / (AmE), / kəʊm / (BrE)
the act of drawing a comb through the hair in order to untangle, arrange, or tidy it

Examples

Your hair is looking messy and could use a good comb.

After his shower, he always gives his hair a quick comb to keep it neat.

2. NOUN

/ kəm / (AmE), / kɒm / (BrE)
the fleshy, red crest on the head of a domestic fowl or other gallinaceous birds
red crest on a chicken's head

Examples

The rooster's comb was bright red and stood tall on his head.
Some breeds of chickens have a larger comb than others.
In the cold, a chicken's comb can be susceptible to frostbite.

Etymology

From Old English 'camb' (comb, crest, ridge), related to German 'Kamm'.

/ koʊm / (AmE), / kəʊm / (BrE)
A row of cilia forming a plate-like structure used for locomotion in ctenophores (marine invertebrates).
cilia-based swimming plate in ctenophores

Examples

Ctenophores move through the water using eight rows of combs that beat in coordinated waves.
Scientists examined the structure of each comb under a microscope to learn more about ctenophore locomotion.
The iridescent appearance of a ctenophore is often caused by light reflecting off its moving comb rows.
