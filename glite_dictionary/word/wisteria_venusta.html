Wisteria venusta

1. NOUN

/ wɪˈstɪriə vəˈnʌstə / (AmE), / wɪˈstɪəriə vɪˈnʌstə / (BrE)
A species of wisteria native to China, known for its white flowers.
Chinese wisteria species with white flowers

Examples

The garden features a beautiful Wisteria venusta that blossoms every spring.
We planted Wisteria venusta along the fence for its elegant white flowers.
Botanists admire Wisteria venusta for its delicate and fragrant blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisteria - a genus of climbing flowering plants

Etymology

From Latinized genus name Wisteria, after Caspar Wistar (1761–1818), American physician; species epithet from Latin 'venustus' meaning 'lovely'.
