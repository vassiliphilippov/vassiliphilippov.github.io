stagger

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæɡər / (AmE), / ˈstæɡə / (BrE)
An unsteady or uneven way of walking, as if about to fall.
unsteady, uneven gait

Examples

He broke into a stagger after twisting his ankle on the uneven pavement.
The patient's stagger was a sign of dizziness from the illness.
After drinking too much, his walk turned into a clumsy stagger across the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lurch - an abrupt, uncontrolled movement; an unsteady gait
  • Antonym: stride - a manner of walking with long, regular steps
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈstæɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈstæɡə / (BrE)
to shock or greatly surprise someone; to overwhelm with amazement or disbelief
to shock or astonish greatly

Examples

The news of her sudden resignation staggered everyone in the office.
I was completely staggered by the amount of money he had spent.
It will stagger you to learn how easy the solution really is.

Synset Examples

She was staggered with bills after she tried to rebuild her house following the earthquake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to astonish - to surprise greatly
  • Synonym: to amaze - to fill with great wonder or surprise
  • Synonym: to overwhelm - to overpower or deeply affect emotionally
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01814093-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈstæɡər / (AmE), / ˈstæɡə / (BrE)
to walk or move unsteadily as if about to fall, typically because of weakness, drunkenness, or shock
walk or move unsteadily

Examples

He tried to stand, but could only stagger across the floor.
After running the marathon, she staggered to the nearest bench and collapsed.
The wounded soldier began staggering toward help, barely able to keep his balance.

Synset Examples

The drunken man staggered into the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reel - to move unsteadily or sway violently
  • Synonym: to totter - to walk or move with unsteady steps
  • Antonym: stride - to walk with long, decisive steps (steady movement)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01928835-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈstæɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈstæɡə / (BrE)
to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall, usually due to weakness, shock, or being unbalanced
walk unsteadily or with difficulty

Examples

He managed to stagger to the door after being hit on the head.
The exhausted runner staggered across the finish line.
After drinking too much, she could barely stagger down the street.

Synset Examples

He staggered along in the heavy snow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to totter - to move in an unsteady way, as if about to fall
  • Synonym: lurch - to make an abrupt, uncontrolled movement, especially forward or sideways
  • Antonym: stride - to walk with long, decisive steps in a specified direction

Etymology

From Old Norse 'stakra' (to push, stagger).

5. VERB

/ ˈstæɡər / (AmE), / ˈstæɡə / (BrE)
to arrange objects so that they are not in a straight line, but are set in a regular, offset order for practical or aesthetic reasons
arrange in an offset or alternating order

Examples

The decorator decided to stagger the paintings along the hallway for a more dynamic look.
Please stagger the desks so that students have enough space between them.
The company chose to stagger the workstations to improve sight lines across the office.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
