grubby

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡrʌbi / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌbi / (BrE)
Infested with grubs (the larval stage of insects); containing or affected by grubs.
infested with grubs

Examples

The farmer noticed that several potatoes were grubby and had to be discarded.
After digging up the plants, we found the soil was surprisingly grubby.
Inspect stored roots regularly to ensure they do not become grubby during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grub-infested - full of or affected by grubs

Etymology

From 'grub' (insect larva) + -y (adjective suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrʌbi / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌbi / (BrE)
A small marine fish of the sculpin family found along the coast of New England.
small sculpin fish of New England

Examples

The fisherman displayed a grubby he had just caught in the shallow waters.
Biologists often study the grubby because it is abundant along the New England coastline.
A curious child watched a grubby swimming between the rocks in tidal pools.

3. ADJ

/ ˈɡrʌbi / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌbi / (BrE)
Dirty or needing to be cleaned; covered in dirt or grime.
dirty; covered in grime

Examples

After playing outside all afternoon, the children came home with grubby hands and faces.
His grubby old jeans were stained and torn at the knees.
She refused to eat in the grubby little café on the corner.

Synset Examples

a miner's begrimed face
dingy linen
grimy hands
grubby little fingers
a grungy kitchen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grimy - covered with or characterized by grime
  • Antonym: clean - free from dirt or impurities

Etymology

From 'grub' (in the sense of 'dig') + -y, meaning dirtied as if from digging; first attested in the 1600s.
