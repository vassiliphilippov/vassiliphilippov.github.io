moonlight

1. NOUN

/ ˈmunˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmuːnˌlaɪt / (BrE)
the light that comes from the Moon, especially at night when it is visible in the sky
light from the Moon

Examples

The garden looked magical in the soft moonlight.
She walked home by moonlight, enjoying the peaceful night air.
You can see the outline of the mountains clearly in the bright moonlight.

Synset Examples

moonlight is the smuggler's enemy
the Moon was bright enough to read by

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sunlight - the light that comes from the Sun

Etymology

From Old English 'mōnan lēoht' (Moon's light).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11505375-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈmunˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmuːnˌlaɪt / (BrE)
to have a second job in addition to one's main employment, especially secretly or at night
work a second job, often at night

Examples

During the week, he works at the bank but he also moonlights as a guitarist in a local bar.
Many teachers choose to moonlight in the evenings to supplement their income.
She moonlighted as a waitress while pursuing her degree.

Synset Examples

The law student is moonlighting as a taxi driver

Related Terms

  • Synonym: side hustle - an additional job or income source besides one's main job
  • Synonym: freelance - to work as an independent, often on side jobs

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'moonlight' (light from the moon), figuratively referring to working by night.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02425706-v
Synset POS: v
