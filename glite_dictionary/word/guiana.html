Guiana

1. PROPN

/ ɡiˈɑːnə / (AmE), / ɡiˈɑːnə / (BrE)
a geographical region in northeastern South America, including the countries of Guyana and Suriname, and parts of French Guiana, Venezuela, and Brazil
region of northeastern South America

Examples

The lush rainforests of Guiana are home to countless unique species.
The explorers traveled through the dense jungles of Guiana in search of gold.
Political boundaries in Guiana have shifted multiple times throughout history.

Etymology

From the indigenous Cariban languages, meaning 'land of many waters.'
