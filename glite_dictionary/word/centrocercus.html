Centrocercus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntroʊˈsɜːrkəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəʊˈsɜːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American grouse, including the sage grouse, known for their large size and elaborate mating displays.
genus of North American sage grouse

Examples

The Centrocercus is notable for the males' dramatic courtship rituals on the lek.
Researchers tracked a population of Centrocercus to study habitat conservation needs.
Unlike other grouse, Centrocercus specializes in sagebrush ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage grouse - large ground-dwelling North American bird, member of Centrocercus

Etymology

From Greek kentron (spur) + kerkos (tail), referring to the bird's tail.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntrəʊˈsɜːrkəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəʊˈsɜːkəs / (BrE)
a genus of grouse known as sage grouse, native to western North America
a genus of sage grouse

Examples

The Centrocercus genus includes the greater and Gunnison sage grouse.
Scientists are studying how habitat loss affects Centrocercus populations.
The distinctive tail feathers are a key feature of birds in the Centrocercus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage grouse - a large grouse native to sagebrush habitats in North America

Etymology

From Greek kentron (spur) + kerkos (tail), referring to the bird's tail.
