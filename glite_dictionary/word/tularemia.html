tularemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌtuːləˈriːmiə / (AmE), / ˌtjuːləˈriːmiə / (BrE)
a highly infectious disease of wild rodents, especially rabbits and squirrels, that can be transmitted to humans by ticks, flies, or by handling infected animals
infectious disease affecting rodents; transmissible to humans
medical

Examples

The outbreak of tularemia prompted local authorities to warn hunters about handling wild rabbits.
Laboratory workers must use special precautions when studying tularemia due to its high infectivity.
After being bitten by a tick, she developed symptoms of tularemia and required hospitalization.

Etymology

From Tulare County, California, where it was first identified, combined with -emia (indicating a blood condition).
