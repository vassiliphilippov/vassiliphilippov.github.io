Spheniscus

1. NOUN

/ sfəˈnɪs.kəs / (AmE), / sfɪˈnɪs.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of penguins that includes the jackass penguins, known for their braying call.
genus of jackass penguins

Examples

The Magellanic penguin belongs to the genus Spheniscus.
Researchers discovered a new species within Spheniscus along the South American coast.
Unlike other penguins, Spheniscus species are often found in temperate climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penguin - flightless seabird of the family Spheniscidae

Etymology

From Greek sphēniskos (a little wedge), referring to the penguins' shape.

2. NOUN

/ sfəˈnɪs.kəs / (AmE), / sfɪˈnɪs.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of penguins, including species such as the African penguin, commonly known as jackass penguins.
penguin genus (jackass penguins)

Examples

The Spheniscus genus includes several penguin species found in the Southern Hemisphere.
Scientists observed a colony of Spheniscus penguins on the coast of South Africa.
All members of the Spheniscus genus are known for their distinctive braying call.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penguin - a flightless seabird of the family Spheniscidae

Etymology

From Greek 'spheniskos', diminutive of 'sphēn' meaning wedge, referring to their body shape.
