bondsperson

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑndˌspɜrsən / (AmE), / ˈbɒndˌspɜːs(ə)n / (BrE)
a person who signs a bond as surety for someone else, especially to guarantee their appearance in court
person acting as surety for another's bond
legal

Examples

The bondsperson posted bail to secure the defendant's release from jail.
If the accused misses court, the bondsperson could lose the full bail amount.
She worked as a bondsperson for many years, helping clients navigate the legal process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bondsman - a person who acts as surety on a bond (typically male or generic term)
  • Synonym: surety - a person who takes responsibility for another's performance of an undertaking

Etymology

Formed from 'bond' (a binding agreement) + 'person'. Patterned after gender-neutral forms like 'spokesperson'.
