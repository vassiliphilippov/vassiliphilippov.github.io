thiouracil

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˌoʊˈjʊrəsɪl / (AmE), / θaɪˌjʊəˈræsɪl / (BrE)
a compound used to reduce the activity of the thyroid gland, especially in the treatment of hyperthyroidism
a drug that reduces thyroid activity
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed thiouracil to help control the patient's overactive thyroid.
Prolonged use of thiouracil may cause side effects that require careful monitoring.
Research showed that thiouracil can effectively decrease thyroid hormone production.

Etymology

From thio- (containing sulfur) and uracil (a component of RNA).
