patch

1. NOUN

/ pætʃ / (AmE), / pætʃ / (BrE)
a piece of soft material worn to cover and protect an injured area of the body
soft material for covering injuries

Examples

He wore an eye patch after the surgery to protect his eye.
The nurse applied a sterile patch over the cut on his arm.
A small patch kept the wound clean and helped it to heal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandage - a strip of fabric used to bind a wound or protect an injured part

Etymology

From Middle English *pacche*, possibly from Middle Dutch *patse* (piece, patch).
