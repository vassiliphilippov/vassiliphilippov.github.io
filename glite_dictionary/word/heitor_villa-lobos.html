Heitor Villa-Lobos

1. PROPN

/ ˈheɪtɔr ˌviːjə ˈloʊboʊs / (AmE), / ˈheɪtɔː ˌviːjə ˈləʊbɒs / (BrE)
(1887–1959) a Brazilian composer known for combining classical music with Brazilian folk influences, and considered one of the most significant Latin American composers.
Brazilian composer (1887–1959)

Examples

Many music historians consider Heitor Villa-Lobos to be a pioneer in integrating folk themes into orchestral works.
The compositions of Heitor Villa-Lobos are performed by orchestras worldwide.
As a child, she was greatly influenced by the guitar pieces of Heitor Villa-Lobos.
