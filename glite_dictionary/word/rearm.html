to rearm

1. VERB

/ riˈɑrm / (AmE), / riˈɑːm / (BrE)
to equip with weapons or arms again after having been disarmed or after a previous period of armament
to arm again, especially with weapons

Examples

The government decided to rearm its military after a decade of peace.
They must rearm quickly to defend against any further threats.
After the treaty was dissolved, the country began to rearm itself.

Synset Examples

After the war, the defeated country was not rearmed by the victors

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to disarm - to take weapons away from
  • Synonym: arm - to provide with weapons

Etymology

From re- (again) + arm (to supply with weapons); first attested in the early 20th century.

2. VERB

/ riˈɑrm / (AmE), / riˈɑːm / (BrE)
to provide with weapons or armaments again after they have been removed or reduced, especially in a military context
to arm again, especially militarily

Examples

The country chose to rearm its military forces after years of disarmament.
After the treaty expired, several nations began to rearm discreetly.
If tensions rise, we may be forced to rearm sooner than planned.

Synset Examples

After the war, the defeated country was not allowed to rearm

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to disarm - to take away weapons or armaments
  • Synonym: arm - to supply with weapons or armaments

Etymology

From re- (again) + arm (to equip with weapons), first used in early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01089970-v
Synset POS: v
