Mola lanceolata

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊlə ˌlænsiəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˈməʊlə ˌlansɪəˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of ocean sunfish distinguished by a caudal fin with a central projection; also known as the sharp-tailed mola.
a species of ocean sunfish

Examples

The Mola lanceolata is easily recognized by the unique shape of its tail fin.
Marine biologists spotted a Mola lanceolata near the coast during their recent expedition.
Unlike other sunfish, the Mola lanceolata has a pronounced central projection on its caudal fin.

Etymology

From Latin: 'mola' (millstone) referencing the fish's shape, and 'lanceolata' (spear-shaped), referring to the central projection on its tail fin.
