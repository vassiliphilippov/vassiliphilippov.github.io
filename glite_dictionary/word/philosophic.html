philosophic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɪləˈsɑfɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɪləˈsɒfɪk / (BrE)
calmly rational, especially in the face of trouble or adversity; characteristic of a philosopher's thoughtful or detached attitude
calm and rational; like a philosopher

Examples

Despite losing the match, she remained philosophic about the result and focused on learning from the experience.
He faced criticism with a philosophic calm, refusing to let emotions cloud his judgment.
Adopting a philosophic attitude helped him cope with the stresses of his demanding job.

Synset Examples

philosophical resignation
a philosophic attitude toward life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philosophical - showing calm, rational thought, especially when facing difficulty

Etymology

From Ancient Greek philosophikos, relating to philosophy.

2. ADJ

/ ˌfɪl.əˈsɑː.fɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɪl.əˈsɒ.fɪk / (BrE)
relating to philosophy or philosophers; concerned with the study or principles of knowledge, existence, and reality.
relating to philosophy or philosophers

Examples

The professor's philosophic lectures inspired many students to question the nature of reality.
She has a philosophic approach to life's challenges, always seeking deeper meaning.
His writings are known for their philosophic depth and clarity.

Synset Examples

philosophical writing
a considerable knowledge of philosophical terminology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philosophical - relating to philosophy or philosophers

Etymology

From Greek philosophikos (fond of learning, philosophical), from philosophia (philosophy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02869320-a
Synset POS: a
