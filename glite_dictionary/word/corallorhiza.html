Corallorhiza

1. NOUN

/ kəˌræləˈraɪzə / (AmE), / kɒˌraləˈraɪzə / (BrE)
A genus of leafless, root-parasitic orchids with small purplish or yellowish racemose flowers with lobed lips, widely distributed in temperate regions.
genus of leafless root-parasitic orchids

Examples

The rare Corallorhiza orchid was spotted in the forest during the spring survey.
Unlike most orchids, Corallorhiza lacks leaves and relies on its roots for nutrition.
Botanists are fascinated by the unique mycoheterotrophic lifestyle of Corallorhiza species.

Etymology

From Greek 'korallion' (coral) and 'rhiza' (root), referring to the coral-like appearance of the roots.

2. NOUN

/ kəˌræl.əˈraɪ.zə / (AmE), / kɒˌræl.əˈraɪ.zə / (BrE)
A genus of leafless root-parasitic orchids with small purplish or yellowish racemose flowers, widely found in temperate regions.
genus of root-parasitic orchids

Examples

The Corallorhiza genus thrives in temperate forests and is known for its unusual parasitic lifestyle.
Unlike many orchids, Corallorhiza species lack green leaves and depend on fungi for nutrients.
Botanists discovered a new species of Corallorhiza in the mountainous region.

Etymology

From Greek 'korallion' (coral) + 'rhiza' (root), referring to the coral-like appearance of the roots.
