pyuria

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈjʊriə / (AmE), / paɪˈjʊəriə / (BrE)
the presence of white blood cells in the urine, typically indicating infection or inflammation of the urinary tract
white blood cells in urine
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed pyuria after finding white blood cells in her urine sample.
Pyuria is often seen in patients with urinary tract infections.
Chronic pyuria may indicate an underlying inflammatory condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacteriuria - the presence of bacteria in the urine

Etymology

From Greek 'pyon' (pus) + 'ouron' (urine).
