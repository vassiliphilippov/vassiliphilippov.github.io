willowware

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪloʊˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈwɪləʊˌweə / (BrE)
chinaware decorated with a blue Chinese-inspired design on a white background, usually featuring a willow tree and often a river scene
blue-and-white chinaware with willow design

Examples

Her grandmother's cabinet was filled with delicate willowware plates and teacups.
Collectors often seek out rare pieces of antique willowware at auctions.
The iconic blue pattern of willowware is instantly recognizable to pottery enthusiasts.

Etymology

Formed from 'willow' and 'ware', referring to the willow tree motif and the ware (type of goods).
