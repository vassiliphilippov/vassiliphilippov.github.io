Celtis australis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛltɪs ɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɛltɪs ɔːˈstraːlɪs / (BrE)
A deciduous shade tree with bright green leaves, native to southern Europe, also known as the European nettle tree or Mediterranean hackberry.
deciduous shade tree of southern Europe

Examples

The Celtis australis is prized in Mediterranean gardens for its impressive shade and resilience.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Celtis australis in various European climates.
A row of mature Celtis australis lines the avenue, creating a natural canopy during summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European nettle tree - common name for Celtis australis
  • Synonym: hackberry - common name for trees in the Celtis genus

Etymology

From Latin: Celtis (a tree name) + australis (southern).
