divergency

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈvɝːdʒənsi / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːdʒənsi / (BrE)
The property of an infinite series or sequence that does not approach a finite limit as it progresses.
infinite series with no limit (math)

Examples

The divergency of this infinite sum means it does not converge to any value.
Mathematicians examined the divergency of the series before applying further analysis.
A sequence that increases without bound is a classic example of divergency in mathematics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convergence - The property of a series or sequence approaching a finite limit

Etymology

Formed from 'diverge' + '-ency', modelled after divergence.

2. NOUN

/ dəˈvɝːdʒənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈvɜːdʒənsi / (BrE)
the act or state of moving or extending in different directions from a common starting point
the act of diverging or moving apart

Examples

The divergency of their career paths became most apparent after college.
Mathematicians study the divergency of lines and curves in geometric spaces.
A critical divergency of opinion led to the formation of two separate groups.

Synset Examples

an angle is formed by the divergence of two straight lines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divergence - the process or state of diverging, more common variant
  • Antonym: convergence - the process or state of coming together or meeting at a point

Etymology

From 'diverge' plus suffix '-ency', modeled after 'divergence'.
