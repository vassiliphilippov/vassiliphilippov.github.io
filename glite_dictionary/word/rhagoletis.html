Rhagoletis

1. NOUN

/ rəˈɡɑːlətɪs / (AmE), / rəˈɡælətɪs / (BrE)
A genus of fruit flies in the family Tephritidae, some species of which are known as agricultural pests.
genus of fruit flies

Examples

Rhagoletis species are responsible for major damage to fruit crops in some regions.
Scientists studied the life cycle of Rhagoletis to understand how it affects apple orchards.
The western cherry fruit fly is a common member of the genus Rhagoletis.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek roots: 'rhago-' (grape) and 'letis' (destruction).

2. NOUN

/ rəˌɡɑːləˈtiːs / (AmE), / rəˌɡɒlɪˈtiːs / (BrE)
A genus of fruit flies in the family Tephritidae, many of which are known as agricultural pests.
genus of fruit flies

Examples

Several species within the Rhagoletis genus are notable pests of commercial fruit crops.
Research has focused on the role of Rhagoletis in the spread of fruit infestations.
The apple maggot, a member of the Rhagoletis genus, is found throughout North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruit fly - a small fly, many species of which lay eggs in fruit; common name for species in Tephritidae and Drosophilidae

Etymology

From Greek 'rhago' (berry) + 'letis' (ruin, destruction), referencing the flies' habit of damaging fruit.
