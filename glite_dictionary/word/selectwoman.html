selectwoman

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɛktˌwʊmən / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman elected to serve as a member of the board of officials (the selectboard) that governs a New England town.
female town official (New England)

Examples

She became the first selectwoman in her town's history after a close election.
The selectwoman attended the annual budget meeting to answer residents' questions.
As a selectwoman, she worked to improve public services in the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selectman - male member of a town governing board in New England
  • Synonym: alderwoman - female member of a municipal legislative body

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'selectman', from 'select' (to choose) + 'woman'.
