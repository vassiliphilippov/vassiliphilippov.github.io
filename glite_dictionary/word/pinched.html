pinched

1. ADJ

/ pɪntʃt / (AmE), / pɪntʃt / (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) having a nasal or constricted quality, as if the nose were squeezed shut.
nasal or constricted-sounding (voice)

Examples

Her pinched voice made it sound as if she had a bad cold.
The actor adopted a pinched tone to portray the sickly character.
He spoke in a pinched nasal accent that was hard to ignore.

Synset Examples

a whining nasal voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasal - having a quality of sound produced through the nose

Etymology

From the verb 'pinch', meaning to squeeze tightly; transferred sense to sound and appearance.

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ pɪnʧt / (AmE), / pɪnʧt / (BrE)
(of a part of the body) squeezed or pressed so tightly as to be uncomfortable or cause pain.
squeezed or pressed tightly (body part)

Examples

Her toes felt pinched after hours in those narrow shoes.
He wore a hat that left a pinched red mark on his forehead.
After hiking all day, my pinched feet were begging for relief.

Synset Examples

her pinched toes in her pointed shoes were killing her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compressed - pressed or squeezed into a smaller space

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'pinch', from Middle English 'pinchen', from Old French 'pincier'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01451752-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ pɪn(t)ʃt / (AmE), / pɪn(t)ʃt / (BrE)
(of a person's face or features) thin and drawn, often as a result of illness, hunger, or cold.
thin or drawn in appearance (face)

Examples

Her pinched cheeks spoke of many days without enough to eat.
The child's pinched features worried the doctor during the examination.
In the harsh winter, many villagers had pinched faces from the cold.

Synset Examples

a nightmare population of gaunt men and skeletal boys
eyes were haggard and cavernous
small pinched faces
kept life in his wasted frame only by grim concentration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaunt - very thin and bony, especially from hunger or illness
  • Synonym: haggard - looking exhausted and unwell, especially from fatigue or suffering
  • Antonym: full-faced - having a face that is broad or rounded

Etymology

From the verb 'pinch', meaning to compress or squeeze, by analogy to a face appearing as if drawn or compressed.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00992194-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ pɪntʃt / (AmE), / pɪntʃt / (BrE)
(of a person) lacking enough money to meet basic needs; financially strained.
lacking enough money; financially strained

Examples

After losing his job, Mark found himself pinched for cash most months.
Many families feel pinched during the holiday season when expenses rise.
She's a bit pinched right now and can't afford to go on vacation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impoverished - lacking sufficient money or material possessions
  • Degree Higher: destitute - extremely poor, lacking the means to provide for oneself
  • Antonym: comfortable - having enough money to live without financial stress
Source: WordNet
