realpolitik

1. NOUN

/ reɪˈɑlˌpoʊliˌtik / (AmE), / reɪˈalˌpɒliˌtiːk / (BrE)
a system of politics or principles based on practical rather than moral or ideological considerations
practical politics; policy based on pragmatism

Examples

The country's foreign policy was shaped by realpolitik rather than by any commitment to human rights.
He admired the chancellor's use of realpolitik to achieve pragmatic solutions to complex problems.
Critics argue that realpolitik often leads to morally questionable decisions in the pursuit of national interests.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: idealism - the practice of forming or pursuing ideals, especially unrealistically

Etymology

From German Realpolitik, from 'real' (practical) + 'Politik' (politics).
