unacceptableness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnəkˈsɛptəblnəs / (AmE), / ʌnəkˈsɛptəblnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of not being acceptable or satisfactory; failure to conform to approved standards.
state of being unacceptable

Examples

The unacceptableness of his behavior was noted by everyone in the meeting.
She raised concerns about the unacceptableness of the living conditions in the facility.
Management emphasized the unacceptableness of missing critical deadlines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unacceptability - the quality of being unacceptable
  • Antonym: acceptableness - the quality of being acceptable

Etymology

From 'unacceptable' + '-ness', modeled after 'acceptable' from Latin 'acceptare' (to accept).
