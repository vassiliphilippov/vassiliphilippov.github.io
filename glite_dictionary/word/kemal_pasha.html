Kemal Pasha

1. PROPN

/ kɛˈmɑl ˈpɑʃə / (AmE), / kɛˈmɑːl ˈpɑːʃə / (BrE)
(1881–1938) Turkish statesman, also known as Mustafa Kemal Atatürk, who abolished the caliphate and established Turkey as a secular republic.
founder of modern Turkey

Examples

Kemal Pasha played a crucial role in transforming the Ottoman Empire into modern Turkey.
The reforms introduced by Kemal Pasha deeply impacted Turkish society and governance.
Many history books credit Kemal Pasha with laying the foundation for Turkey's secular government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atatürk - Founder of modern Turkey, known as Mustafa Kemal Atatürk

Etymology

Kemal is Turkish for 'perfection,' and Pasha is a Turkish title of high rank; also known as Atatürk ('Father of the Turks').
