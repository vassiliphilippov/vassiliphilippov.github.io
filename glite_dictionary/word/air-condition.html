to air-condition

1. VERB

/ ˈɛr kənˌdɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈeə kənˌdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
to control the temperature and humidity of a building, room, or vehicle by using an air conditioner
control temperature and humidity (by machine)

Examples

We decided to air-condition the office before the summer heat wave arrived.
The new restaurant is fully air-conditioned for customers' comfort.
Some older public buildings have never been air-conditioned, making them stifling in the summer.

Synset Examples

The room was cool because it had been air-conditioned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ventilate - to cause fresh air to enter and move through a space

Etymology

Formed from 'air' and 'condition', modeled after 'air conditioner'.

2. VERB

/ ˈɛr kənˌdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈeə kənˌdɪʃən / (BrE)
to equip a room, building, or other space with an apparatus that controls the air's temperature and humidity
equip with climate control apparatus

Examples

Most new office buildings are air-conditioned to keep employees comfortable during the summer.
They decided to air-condition the classrooms after complaints about the heat.
The hotel was fully air-conditioned, making our stay pleasant even in July.

Synset Examples

Our house is not air-conditioned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ventilate - to provide with fresh air or circulation
  • Degree Lower: heat - to make or keep something warm

Etymology

Formed from 'air' + 'condition', modeled after 'air conditioning' (1930s, American English).
