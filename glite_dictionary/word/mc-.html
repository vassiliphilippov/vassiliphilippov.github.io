Mc-

1. X

/ mək / (AmE), / mək / (BrE)
A prefix added to words to indicate something is mass-produced, generic, or associated with popular culture, often in a negative or critical sense.
prefix for mass-produced or popular things
informal

Examples

Some critics refer to generic suburban houses as McMansions because of their mass-produced appearance.
He complained that working there felt like having a McJob, with little satisfaction or security.
The trend toward McChurches reflects a move to bigger, more commercialized places of worship.

Synset Examples

The Mc- prefix often indicates something popular and fast.

Etymology

Modelled on the Mc/Mac- prefix in Scottish and Irish surnames, popularized in compounds like 'McJob' and 'McMansion' as allusions to the global fast-food chain McDonald's.
