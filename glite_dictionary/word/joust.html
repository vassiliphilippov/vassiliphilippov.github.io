joust

1. NOUN

/ dʒaʊst / (AmE), / dʒaʊst / (BrE)
a medieval contest in which two knights on horseback fought with lances
medieval knights' tournament combat

Examples

The annual festival featured a dramatic joust between two armored knights.
During the Middle Ages, the joust was a popular form of entertainment and competition.
Spectators cheered as the knights prepared for the joust in the royal arena.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tournament - a series of contests in sport or medieval combat
  • Synonym: duel - a prearranged combat between two people, especially with weapons

Etymology

From Old French 'joster', meaning 'to joust, to tilt', from Late Latin 'juxtare', from Latin 'iuxta' (near, close).

2. VERB

/ dʒaʊst / (AmE), / dʒaʊst / (BrE)
to fight on horseback against another person with lances, especially as part of a medieval tournament
to fight on horseback in a tournament

Examples

Knights would joust against each other to prove their skill and bravery.
During the festival, two young nobles jousted in the arena before an excited crowd.
It was a great honor to joust in front of the king and queen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tilt - to fight in a tournament with lances on horseback

Etymology

From Old French *jouster* (to meet, to fight in a tournament), based on Latin *juxta* (next to, beside).
