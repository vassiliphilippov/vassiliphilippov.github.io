Lactaid

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæk.teɪd / (AmE), / ˈlæk.teɪd / (BrE)
a trademarked enzyme supplement containing lactase, used to help digest lactose in dairy products
lactase enzyme supplement for lactose digestion

Examples

She takes Lactaid before eating ice cream to avoid digestive discomfort.
Lactaid tablets are popular among people with lactose intolerance.
The pharmacist recommended Lactaid for those who struggle with dairy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lactase - an enzyme that breaks down lactose
