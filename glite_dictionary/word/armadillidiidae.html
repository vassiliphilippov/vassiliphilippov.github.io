Armadillidiidae

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr.məˌdɪl.ɪˈdi.i.deɪ / (AmE), / ˌɑː.mə.dɪl.ɪˈdiː.ɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of small terrestrial crustaceans, commonly known as pill bugs or roly-polies, capable of rolling into a ball when threatened.
family of pill bugs (terrestrial crustaceans)

Examples

Armadillidiidae are often found under rocks and logs in moist environments.
Scientists studied several species within the Armadillidiidae family to understand their unique defense mechanisms.
The children collected Armadillidiidae in the garden to watch how they curled into little balls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pill bug - small terrestrial crustacean that can roll into a ball
  • Synonym: woodlouse - terrestrial isopod crustacean, not all roll up

Etymology

From Latin 'armadillo' (little armored one) plus taxonomy suffix -idae, used for animal families.
