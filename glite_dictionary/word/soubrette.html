soubrette

1. NOUN

/ suːˈbrɛt / (AmE), / suːˈbrɛt / (BrE)
A lively, flirtatious young woman, especially as a character in theater or opera.
lively, flirtatious young woman (theater)

Examples

Her charm and wit made her the perfect soubrette in the new stage production.
The character was a classic soubrette, providing comic relief with her playful antics.
In many operas, the soubrette is known for outsmarting her social superiors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coquette - a woman who flirts lightly with men to win their admiration

Etymology

From French 'soubrette', meaning 'maid' or 'coquettish girl', ultimately from Italian 'soubretta'.

2. NOUN

/ suːˈbrɛt / (AmE), / suːˈbrɛt / (BrE)
(in drama, esp. comedy) a minor female character, typically a lively and flirtatious maid or young woman, often serving as an attendant to the leading lady.
minor female role; lively maid in comedy

Examples

The actress played the soubrette with remarkable wit and energy.
In many operas, the soubrette provides comic relief amid serious scenes.
The director was searching for someone with the right charm to portray the soubrette.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maid - female domestic worker or attendant

Etymology

From French 'soubrette', meaning a coy or pert young woman, diminutive of 'soubre' (maidservant).
