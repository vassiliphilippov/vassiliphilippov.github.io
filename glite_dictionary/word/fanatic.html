fanatic

1. ADJ

/ fəˈnætɪk / (AmE), / fəˈnætɪk / (BrE)
having or showing excessive enthusiasm and intense devotion to a cause or idea; extremely zealous or passionate
extremely enthusiastic or devoted

Examples

The coach was a fanatic supporter of his team, attending every game with unmatched enthusiasm.
Her fanatic devotion to animal rights inspired others to join the cause.
Online communities can sometimes attract fanatic followers who defend their beliefs relentlessly.

Synset Examples

rabid isolationist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zealous - full of zeal or enthusiastic devotion
  • Antonym: moderate - showing restraint or within reasonable limits

Etymology

From Latin fanaticus (inspired by a deity, frantic), from fanum (temple).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01731073-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ fəˈnætɪk / (AmE), / fəˈnætɪk / (BrE)
a person who shows extreme or irrational enthusiasm for a cause, often disregarding reason or opposing evidence
person with extreme enthusiasm

Examples

The political rally attracted more than one fanatic who wouldn't listen to any opposing arguments.
She was considered a health fanatic, following online trends with unquestioning devotion.
It's important not to become a fanatic about your personal beliefs and shut out other perspectives.

Synset Examples

A fanatic is one who can't change his mind and won't change the subject

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: enthusiast - a person with intense and eager enjoyment or interest
  • Antonym: moderate - a person holding moderate views, especially in politics

Etymology

From Latin fanaticus (inspired by a deity, frenzied), from fanum (temple).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10097659-n
Synset POS: n
