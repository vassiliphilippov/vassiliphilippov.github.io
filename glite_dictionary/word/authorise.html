to authorise

1. VERB

/ ˈɔːθəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈɔːθəraɪz / (BrE)
to give official permission for something to happen or to give someone official power to do something
give official permission or power
british

Examples

The bank did not authorise the transaction due to suspicious activity.
Guests must be authorised to enter this area of the building.
She is authorised to sign documents on behalf of the company.

Synset Examples

Clear the manuscript for publication
The rock star never authorized this slanderous biography

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to authorize - to give official permission or power (US spelling)
  • Antonym: to forbid - to refuse to allow something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'auctorisare', from Latin 'auctor' (author, originator).

2. VERB

/ ˈɔːθəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈɔːθəraɪz / (BrE)
to give official permission for something or give someone the power to do something
give official permission or power

Examples

The manager must authorise all expense claims before payment is made.
Only a doctor can authorise this medication for patients.
Her supervisor has authorised her to make purchases up to $5,000.

Synset Examples

She authorized her assistant to sign the papers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to empower - give someone the authority or power to do something
  • Antonym: to forbid - refuse to allow something; prohibit

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *auctorizare*, from Latin *auctor* (originator, author).
