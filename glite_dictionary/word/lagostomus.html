Lagostomus

1. PROPN

/ ləˈɡɑstəˌməs / (AmE), / ləˈɡɒstəməs / (BrE)
A genus of South American rodents commonly known as plains viscachas, characterized by their burrowing habits and large cheek pouches.
genus of South American rodents (viscachas)

Examples

The Lagostomus species are known for creating extensive burrow systems on the grasslands.
Researchers are studying the social structure of the Lagostomus colonies in Argentina.
Unlike other rodents, Lagostomus have large cheek pouches for carrying food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscacha - a burrowing South American rodent, closely related to the chinchilla

Etymology

From Greek lagos (hare) + stoma (mouth), referring to their hare-like appearance and large mouths.
