survivor

1. NOUN

/ sərˈvaɪvər / (AmE), / səˈvaɪvə / (BrE)
a person who continues to live after an event or course of hardship, especially a life-threatening one
person who lives through hardship or danger

Examples

After the earthquake, only a handful of survivors remained in the ruined village.
The cancer survivor shared her story to inspire others facing similar challenges.
Emergency crews worked tirelessly to rescue the survivors trapped in the collapsed building.

Synset Examples

the survivors of the fire were taken to a hospital

Related Terms

  • Antonym: victim - a person harmed or killed by an event or action

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'survivor', from Old French 'survivre' (to survive), from Latin 'supervivere' (to live beyond).

2. NOUN

/ sərˈvaɪvɚ / (AmE), / səˈvaɪvə / (BrE)
a person who remains alive after others have died, especially after an event such as an accident, war, or the death of another person.
person who outlives others

Examples

As the only survivor of the accident, she gave her account to the police.
The will stated that the estate would pass to the eldest survivor of the family.
He was recognized as the last survivor of his platoon from the war.

Synset Examples

he left his farm to his survivors

Related Terms

  • Antonym: victim - a person harmed, injured, or killed as a result of an event or action

Etymology

From French *survivre* (to survive), from Latin *supervivere* (to live beyond).

3. NOUN

/ sərˈvaɪvər / (AmE), / səˈvaɪvə / (BrE)
an animal that continues to live despite difficult conditions or adversity
animal that survives adverse conditions

Examples

Only the strongest survivor managed to endure the harsh drought.
The researchers observed several survivors among the herd after the disease outbreak.
That old turtle is a true survivor of many winters in the wild.

Synset Examples

only the fittest animals were survivors of the cold winters

Related Terms

  • Antonym: victim - an animal that does not survive adversity or hardship

Etymology

From Old French survivre (to outlive) and -or, from Latin supervīvor.
