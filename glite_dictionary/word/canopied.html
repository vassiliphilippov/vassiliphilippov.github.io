canopied

1. ADJ

/ ˈkænəˌpid / (AmE), / ˈkanəpɪd / (BrE)
covered with or as if with a canopy; having a covering above, especially one that forms a shelter or decorative top.
covered with or as with a canopy

Examples

The old estate featured grand halls with canopied ceilings and elegant chandeliers.
Many outdoor cafes offered canopied seating to protect customers from the sun.
Travelers rested under a canopied pavilion after their long hike.

Synset Examples

a canopied bed
streets canopied by stately trees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canopy - a covering, usually of fabric, supported on poles or suspended above a bed, throne, or seat

Etymology

From 'canopy' + '-ed' suffix forming the adjective.
