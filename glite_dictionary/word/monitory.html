monitory

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑː.nəˌtɔːr.i / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ɪ.tər.i / (BrE)
serving to warn or give a cautionary notice
serving as a warning

Examples

The teacher gave a monitory glance to the noisy students at the back of the classroom.
She raised her hand in a monitory gesture, signaling silence before the performance began.
The article concluded with a monitory note about the dangers of neglecting public health.

Synset Examples

shook a monitory finger at him
an exemplary jail sentence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admonitory - giving or conveying a warning or reprimand

Etymology

From Latin 'monitorius' (admonishing), from 'monere' (to warn).
