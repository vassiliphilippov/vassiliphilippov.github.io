welsher

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛlʃər / (AmE), / ˈwɛlʃə / (BrE)
a person who avoids paying a debt or fulfilling an obligation, especially after losing a bet
someone who fails to pay a debt or bet

Examples

The bookie refused to deal with a known welsher after being cheated once.
He gained a reputation as a welsher for not paying his gambling debts.
No one likes to play with a welsher who never pays up after losing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: welcher - someone who avoids paying a debt or bet (alternate spelling)

Etymology

From 'welsh', possibly referring to supposed dishonesty; regarded as derogatory toward Welsh people.
