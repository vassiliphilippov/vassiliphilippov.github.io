rampion

1. NOUN

/ ˈræmpiən / (AmE), / ˈræmpɪən / (BrE)
a bellflower native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, with bluish flowers and an edible tuberous root sometimes used in salads
a plant with edible roots and bluish flowers

Examples

The gardener carefully harvested the rampion to add its roots to tonight's salad.
Wild rampion blooms created a splash of blue along the country lane.
She introduced rampion leaves into her favorite springtime dishes for a new flavor.

Etymology

From Middle French 'rampion', from Italian 'ramponio'.
