fanion

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæniən / (AmE), / ˈfænɪən / (BrE)
a small flag used by surveyors or soldiers to mark a position
small flag for marking a position

Examples

The engineer planted a fanion at the spot where the new road would begin.
During training exercises, each unit was assigned a different colored fanion for identification.
The surveyors moved the fanions as their work progressed across the field.

Etymology

From French 'fanion' (banner, small flag).
