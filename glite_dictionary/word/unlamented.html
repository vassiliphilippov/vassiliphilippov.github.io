unlamented

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.ləˈmɛn.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ləˈmɛn.tɪd / (BrE)
not mourned or grieved for when gone or dead; causing no sorrow at someone's passing or departure
not mourned or grieved for

Examples

She left her position after ten years, and her departure was largely unlamented by the staff.
The corrupt official died an unlamented death, with few to mourn his passing.
His absence from the meeting went virtually unlamented by his colleagues.

Synset Examples

interred in an unlamented grave

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lamented - mourned or grieved for

Etymology

From un- (not) + lamented (past participle of 'lament', from Latin lamentari, to wail).
