shudra

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuːdrə / (AmE), / ˈʃuːdrə / (BrE)
A member of the lowest of the four traditional Hindu varnas, traditionally assigned to serve as servants and workers.
lowest caste in Hindu system

Examples

The ancient text describes the duties assigned to a shudra in society.
During the ceremony, a shudra was not allowed to participate in certain rituals.
Historians debate how rigid the role of the shudras was in classical India.

Etymology

From Sanskrit śūdra.
