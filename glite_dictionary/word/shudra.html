Shudra

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuːdrə / (AmE), / ˈʃuːdrə / (BrE)
a member of the lowest of the four traditional varnas, or castes, in Hindu society, historically assigned to serve as workers or laborers
member of the lowest Hindu caste

Examples

Historically, Shudras were assigned roles such as artisans, agriculturalists, and manual laborers within the traditional Hindu caste system.
The family identified themselves as Shudras, embracing their heritage with pride despite societal challenges.
Many ancient texts describe the duties and rights of a Shudra in great detail.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Brahmin - a member of the highest Hindu caste, traditionally priests or scholars
  • Antonym: Kshatriya - a member of the second Hindu caste, traditionally warriors or rulers
  • Antonym: Vaishya - a member of the third Hindu caste, traditionally merchants or farmers

Etymology

From Sanskrit शूद्र (śūdra), meaning 'servant' or 'laborer'.
