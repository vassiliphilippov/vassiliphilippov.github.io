Antidorcas marsupialis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈdɔr.kəs mɑːr.suːˈpiː.ə.lɪs / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈdɔː.kæs mɑːˈsuː.piːˌæl.ɪs / (BrE)
a species of South African gazelle, commonly known as the springbok, noted for its distinctive leaps into the air
South African gazelle (springbok)

Examples

The Antidorcas marsupialis is famous for its high leaps, known as pronking, across the African plains.
Scientists observed a herd of Antidorcas marsupialis grazing peacefully before suddenly taking flight.
Unlike many other antelope, the Antidorcas marsupialis displays remarkable agility when escaping predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: springbok - another name for Antidorcas marsupialis, a gazelle of southern Africa

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Antidorcas' from Greek anti- (opposite) + dorkas (gazelle), and species name 'marsupialis' from Latin 'marsupium' (pouch).
