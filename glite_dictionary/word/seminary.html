seminary

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛmɪˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈsɛmɪnəri / (BrE)
a college or school where people are trained to become priests, ministers, or rabbis
religious training school

Examples

After graduating from college, she decided to attend a seminary to pursue her calling in ministry.
Many religious leaders receive their education at a seminary.
The seminary holds weekly lectures open to the public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theological college - a college for training people for the ministry

Etymology

From Latin seminarium (seed plot, nursery), from semen (seed).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛm.əˌnɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈsɛm.ɪ.nər.i / (BrE)
a school or private place where young people, especially those preparing to become priests or ministers, are educated
religious school or private place of education

Examples

After graduating high school, she decided to attend a seminary to prepare for religious service.
The seminary on the outskirts of town is known for its rigorous education.
Many parents chose the seminary believing it would provide a disciplined environment for their children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academy - a place of study or training in a special field

Etymology

From Latin seminarium (seed plot, nursery), from seminarius (of seed), from semen (seed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08301915-n
Synset POS: n
