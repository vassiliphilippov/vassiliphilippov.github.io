T

1. NOUN

/ tiː / (AmE), / tiː / (BrE)
the twentieth letter of the modern English alphabet, representing a voiceless alveolar stop.
the 20th letter of the alphabet

Examples

She wrote a capital T at the beginning of the sentence.
The letter T appears twice in the word 'letter'.
The teacher asked him to trace a T on the worksheet.

Etymology

From Latin 'T', from Greek 'tau', from Phoenician 'taw'.

3. NOUN

/ ti / (AmE), / ti / (BrE)
A base found in DNA (but not in RNA); thymine, which pairs with adenine and is derived from pyrimidine.
thymine base in DNA

Examples

In the DNA double helix, T always pairs with adenine (A).
A mutation can cause a T to be replaced by another base, leading to genetic changes.
Scientists observed that T is present in DNA but not in RNA.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thymine - the base T found in DNA
  • Degree Higher: adenine - the base paired with thymine in DNA

Etymology

Abbreviation of thymine, from thymus gland (where it was first found) + -ine.

4. NOUN

/ ti / (AmE), / ti / (BrE)
a nucleotide in DNA that contains the base thymine; also known as thymidylate or TMP
DNA nucleotide with thymine base
medical

Examples

During DNA replication, T always pairs with adenine.
The sequence is comprised of a combination of T, A, C, and G nucleotides.
A mutation occurred when a T was replaced by a G in the DNA strand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: A - DNA nucleotide containing adenine base
  • Synonym: C - DNA nucleotide containing cytosine base
  • Synonym: G - DNA nucleotide containing guanine base

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'thymine' or 'thymidylate', derived from the chemical base thymine.
