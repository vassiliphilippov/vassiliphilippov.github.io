Mayetiola destructor

1. NOUN

/ maɪˌɛtiˈoʊlə dɪˈstrʌktər / (AmE), / maɪˌɛtiˈəʊlə dɪˈstrʌktə / (BrE)
A small fly whose larvae are destructive pests of wheat and other cereal crops.
fly pest of wheat and cereals

Examples

The larvae of the Mayetiola destructor infest wheat fields, causing significant crop losses.
Researchers are developing resistant wheat varieties to combat the Mayetiola destructor.
Farmers monitor their fields in the spring to detect any presence of Mayetiola destructor early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hessian fly - common name for Mayetiola destructor, a pest of wheat

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name Mayetiola combined with Latin 'destructor' meaning 'destroyer'.
