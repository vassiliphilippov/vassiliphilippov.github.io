edgewise

1. ADV

/ ˈɛdʒ.waɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛdʒ.wʌɪz / (BrE)
With the edge forward or facing; on, by, or toward the edge.
with the edge forward or facing

Examples

When stacking the plates, make sure to position them edgewise to save space.
She carefully inserted the key edgewise into the narrow slot.
The boards must be held edgewise while being glued together.

Synset Examples

he sawed the board edgeways
held it edgewise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edgeways - with the edge forward or on the edge

Etymology

Formed from 'edge' + '-wise' (in the manner of).

2. ADV

/ ˈɛdʒˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛdʒ.wʌɪz / (BrE)
(Especially in the phrase 'get a word in edgewise') if you get a word in edgewise, you manage to say something in a conversation dominated by others.
with difficulty in a conversation

Examples

I couldn't get a word in edgewise during the heated debate.
She talked so much that nobody else could speak edgewise.
It's hard to get your opinion in edgewise when everyone is interrupting.

Synset Examples

I could not get a word in edgewise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edgeways - variant form with same meaning: as if by an edge; barely
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
