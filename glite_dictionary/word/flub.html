flub

1. NOUN

/ flʌb / (AmE), / flʌb / (BrE)
an embarrassing or careless mistake
an embarrassing mistake
informal

Examples

The actor made a flub during his speech, but quickly recovered.
Her biggest flub was calling her boss by the wrong name in the meeting.
Despite the flub in the middle of the performance, the audience cheered at the end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or stupid mistake
  • Synonym: gaffe - a social or verbal blunder
  • Antonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ flʌb / (AmE), / flʌb / (BrE)
to make a mistake or mess up something, often through carelessness or nervousness
make a mistake; mess up
informal

Examples

She flubbed her lines during the important speech.
Don't flub your chance to impress the judges!
He flubbed the final question and lost the game.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: blunder - make a stupid or careless mistake
  • Antonym: to succeed - achieve the desired result

Etymology

Probably an alteration of "flubdub," meaning nonsense.
