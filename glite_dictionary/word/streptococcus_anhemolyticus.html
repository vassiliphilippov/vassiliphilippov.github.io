Streptococcus anhemolyticus

1. NOUN

/ ˌstrɛp.toʊˈkɒk.əs ænˌhiː.moʊ.laɪˈtɪk.əs / (AmE), / ˌstrɛp.təˈkɒk.əs ænˌhiː.mə.laɪˈtɪk.əs / (BrE)
A species of bacterium within the genus Streptococcus that does not cause hemolysis (the breakdown of red blood cells).
species of non-hemolytic Streptococcus bacterium
medical

Examples

Streptococcus anhemolyticus is often found in the throat but rarely causes disease.
Researchers are studying the genetic differences between Streptococcus anhemolyticus and related species.
Unlike other streptococci, Streptococcus anhemolyticus does not break down red blood cells.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Streptococcus - a genus of spherical Gram-positive bacteria

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Streptococcus (the genus name, from Greek streptos 'twisted' + kokkos 'berry') and anhemolyticus (from Greek an- 'not' + hemolyticus, referring to 'hemolysis').
