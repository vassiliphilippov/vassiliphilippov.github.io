90377 Sedna

1. PROPN

/ ˌnaɪn ˈθaʊzənd ː θr̩ ˈsɛvn̩ti ˈsɛdnə / (AmE), / ˌnaɪn ˈθaʊzənd ː θri ˈsɛvnti ˈsɛdnə / (BrE)
a distant trans-Neptunian planetoid, composed of rock and ice, discovered in 2003 and notable for being one of the most remote known objects orbiting the Sun
distant trans-Neptunian planetoid discovered in 2003

Examples

Astronomers announced the discovery of 90377 Sedna in 2003, expanding our understanding of the solar system's edge.
The orbit of 90377 Sedna is so distant that it takes over 11,000 years to complete a revolution around the Sun.
Studying 90377 Sedna may provide clues about the formation of other distant objects beyond Neptune.

Etymology

Named after Sedna, the Inuit goddess of the sea; 90377 refers to its numerical designation in the Minor Planet Center catalog.
