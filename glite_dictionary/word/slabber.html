to slabber

1. VERB

/ ˈslæbər / (AmE), / ˈslæbə / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to allow saliva to dribble or run from the mouth; to drool.
let saliva dribble from the mouth

Examples

The dog began to slabber as soon as it saw the food.
As the toddler slept, he would often slabber onto his pillow.
She wiped the puppy's face after it started to slabber all over her hands.

Synset Examples

The baby drooled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drool - let saliva flow from the mouth; dribble
  • Synonym: slobber - let saliva drivel from the mouth
