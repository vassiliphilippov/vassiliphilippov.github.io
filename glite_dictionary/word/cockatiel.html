cockatiel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːkəˌtil / (AmE), / ˌkɒkəˈtiːl / (BrE)
a small grey Australian parrot with a yellow, crested head, often kept as a pet
small Australian parrot species

Examples

My grandmother has a beautiful cockatiel that whistles every morning.
The pet store had several cockatiels in different shades of grey and yellow.
A cockatiel makes a gentle and social companion for many bird lovers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockatoo - large, often white, crested parrot of Australia and surrounding islands

Etymology

From Dutch 'kaketielje', a diminutive of 'kaketoe' (cockatoo), ultimately from Malay 'kakatua'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑː.kəˈtiːl / (AmE), / ˌkɒk.əˈtiːl / (BrE)
a small Australian parrot with mainly grey plumage, a long tail, and a yellow and orange crest on its head, often kept as a cage bird.
small Australian parrot with a crested head

Examples

The cockatiel perched on Maya's shoulder and whistled happily.
Many people enjoy training their cockatiels to mimic simple tunes.
A cockatiel needs a spacious cage and plenty of attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockatoo - a large parrot with a crest, native to Australia

Etymology

From Dutch 'kaketielje', a diminutive of 'kaketoe' (cockatoo), referring to its similarity to cockatoos.
