catchword

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætʃˌwɝd / (AmE), / ˈkætʃˌwɜːd / (BrE)
a word printed at the top of a page in a dictionary or reference book to indicate the first or last entry on that page
word at top of reference book page

Examples

The catchword at the top of the page helped her quickly locate the word she was looking for.
Each page of the index features a catchword for easy navigation.
Older books often included a catchword to guide readers through lengthy lists.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkætʃˌwɝd / (AmE), / ˈkatʃˌwɜːd / (BrE)
a word or phrase, often used as a slogan, that is characteristic of or favored by a particular group, organization, or individual
slogan or characteristic phrase

Examples

The politician repeated the same catchword throughout his campaign to appeal to voters.
'Innovation' has become a popular catchword in the tech industry.
Without substance, a catchword can quickly lose its persuasive power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slogan - a memorable phrase used in advertising or promotion
  • Synonym: motto - a short sentence or phrase expressing a guiding principle

Etymology

From 'catch' + 'word'; originally referred to a prompt in the margins of manuscripts to aid reading.
