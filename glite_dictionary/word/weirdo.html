weirdo

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪrˌdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɪədəʊ / (BrE)
A person whose behavior is strange or eccentric, and sometimes perceived as disturbing or threatening.
an odd or eccentric person
informal

Examples

People at school called him a weirdo because of how he dressed.
She thought the man staring at her on the bus was a total weirdo.
If you keep acting like a weirdo, people might start to avoid you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oddball - a person who behaves in a strange or unusual way
  • Synonym: eccentric - unconventional and slightly strange person
  • Antonym: normal - usual, typical, or expected person

Etymology

Formed from 'weird' with the diminutive suffix '-o', popularized in U.S. slang in the mid-20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪrdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɪədəʊ / (BrE)
a person who behaves in a strange or unusual way, often viewed as unpleasant or eccentric
strange or eccentric person
informal

Examples

Everyone thought the new student was a weirdo because he wore a cape to school.
Stop acting like a weirdo and just join the group already.
She felt like a weirdo at the party because she didn't know anyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eccentric - a person with unconventional or slightly strange views or behavior
  • Antonym: normal - a person behaving in a typical, expected way

Etymology

From 'weird' (strange) + '-o' suffix indicating a person; first appeared in American slang, early 20th century.
