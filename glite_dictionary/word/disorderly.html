disorderly

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɔrdɚli / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːdəli / (BrE)
in a state of utter disorder or confusion; not arranged in a neat or organized way
in utter disorder; not organized

Examples

The living room was left in a disorderly state after the party.
She hurriedly stuffed her papers into a disorderly pile on the desk.
The workshop was cluttered and disorderly, with tools scattered everywhere.

Synset Examples

a disorderly pile of clothes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orderly - neatly and methodically arranged or organized

Etymology

From 'disorder' + '-ly', meaning 'not in order'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02434302-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɔrdɚli / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːdəli / (BrE)
behaving in an undisciplined or unruly manner; not following rules or order
undisciplined and unruly

Examples

The police dispersed the disorderly crowd outside the stadium.
His disorderly behavior led to a warning from the teacher.
Some passengers became disorderly during the long delay at the airport.

Synset Examples

disorderly youths
disorderly conduct

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orderly - well-behaved and obedient

Etymology

From Old French *desordonné*, from Latin *dis-* (expressing reversal) + *ordo* (order).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01670528-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɔrdɚli / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːdəli / (BrE)
lacking organization, order, or structure; confusing and unpredictable.
lacking order or organization

Examples

The files were kept in a disorderly pile on his desk.
The protest turned disorderly when people began shouting and pushing.
Her thoughts became disorderly after hearing the unexpected news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaotic - in a state of complete confusion and disorder
  • Antonym: orderly - arranged or organized in a neat, methodical way

Etymology

From 'disorder' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'ordo' (order).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02399137-s
Synset POS: s
