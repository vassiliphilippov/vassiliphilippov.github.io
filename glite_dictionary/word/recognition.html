recognition

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkəɡˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkəɡˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
the explicit and formal acknowledgement by one government of the existence or sovereignty of another government or nation
formal acknowledgement of a government or nation
legal

Examples

The country's diplomatic status changed after the United Nations voted for its recognition.
Guatemala's recognition of Belize in 1991 helped settle longstanding territorial disputes.
Many nations refused to grant recognition to the self-declared republic until it met international standards.

Synset Examples

territorial disputes were resolved in Guatemala's recognition of Belize in 1991

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acknowledgement - the act of accepting or admitting the existence of something

Etymology

From Latin recognitio, from recognoscere (to recognize).

8. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkəɡˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkəɡˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
(biology) the process by which a molecule identifies and binds to another molecule with a complementary structure, enabling specific biological interactions.
biological interaction between complementary molecules
medical

Examples

Molecular recognition is essential for the immune system to distinguish between self and non-self molecules.
The enzyme's active site exhibits high specificity due to its precise recognition of substrate molecules.
Hormone-receptor recognition triggers various cellular responses in living organisms.

Synset Examples

molecular recognition drives all of biology, for instance, hormone and receptor or antibody-antigen interactions or the organization of molecules into larger biologically active entities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binding - attachment of one molecule to another
  • Synonym: interaction - process of acting upon each other, especially at the molecular level

Etymology

From Latin recognitio (an examination, acknowledgment), from recognoscere (to recognize).
