convex

1. ADJ

/ kɑnˈvɛks / (AmE), / kɒnˈvɛks / (BrE)
having a surface or edge that curves or bulges outward, like the exterior of a sphere or circle.
curved or bulging outward

Examples

The lens was convex, focusing the light to a single point.
He admired the convex shape of the sculpture, which bulged outward gracefully.
Many insects have convex eyes that allow for a wider field of vision.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concave - having a surface that curves inward like the inside of a bowl

Etymology

From Latin 'convexus' (vaulted, arched), from 'con-' (together) + 'vexus' (carried).
