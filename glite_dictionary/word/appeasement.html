appeasement

1. NOUN

/ əˈpizmənt / (AmE), / əˈpiːzmənt / (BrE)
the act of calming or pacifying someone by acceding to their demands, often to avoid conflict
the act of pacifying by giving in to demands

Examples

The government was criticized for its policy of appeasement toward the aggressor nation.
Offering extra privileges was seen as an act of appeasement to calm the protesting students.
Some historians argue that appeasement only encouraged further demands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conciliation - the action of stopping someone from being angry; placation
  • Antonym: provocation - the action of provoking or inciting; causing anger or another reaction

Etymology

From Old French apaisement, from apaiser 'to pacify'.
