Coffea canephora

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔfiə kænəˈfoʊrə / (AmE), / ˌkɒfiə ˌkænɪˈfɔːrə / (BrE)
A species of coffee plant native to West Africa and widely cultivated for its beans, known for resistance to coffee diseases and commonly used to produce robusta coffee.
species of coffee plant (robusta)

Examples

Many instant coffees are made from Coffea canephora beans because of their strong flavor.
Farmers choose Coffea canephora for its resilience against pests and diseases.
About 40% of the world's coffee production comes from Coffea canephora.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robusta - A common name for coffee made from Coffea canephora beans
  • Antonym: Coffea arabica - Another major species of coffee plant, producing arabica coffee

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Coffea' referring to the coffee genus and 'canephora' meaning 'basket-bearer', possibly referring to the plant's fruit clusters.
