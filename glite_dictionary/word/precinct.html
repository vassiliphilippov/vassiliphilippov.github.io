precinct

1. NOUN

/ ˈpriː.sɪŋkt / (AmE), / ˈpriː.sɪŋkt / (BrE)
an area within a city or town that is defined for administrative or official purposes, especially for police or electoral functions
a city area for administrative purposes

Examples

The city is divided into several precincts for local elections.
Each police precinct has its own headquarters and officers.
The new stadium falls within the university precinct.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: district - an area of a country or city, often defined for administrative purposes

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'praecinctum' (enclosure), from Latin 'praecingere' (to gird, encircle).
