desirableness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈzaɪr.ə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / dɪˈzaɪərəblnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being desirable or worthy of being desired
quality of being desirable

Examples

The desirableness of this location has increased due to new schools and parks nearby.
Her promotion reflected the desirableness of her unique skills in the company.
The panel discussed the desirableness of various proposals before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desirability - the quality of being desired; attractiveness or worthiness of desire
  • Antonym: undesirableness - the quality of being undesirable or not worthy of desire

Etymology

From 'desirable' + '-ness', with 'desirable' from Old French 'desirier' and Latin 'desiderare' (to long for).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈzaɪrəbəlnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈzaɪərəblnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being desirable, especially in terms of sexual or romantic attractiveness.
quality of being desirable or attractive

Examples

The actress's undeniable desirableness made her a favorite for leading romantic roles.
He envied his friend's easy desirableness at parties, which always seemed effortless.
Her sense of confidence added to her desirableness in the eyes of many.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desirability - the quality of being desirable
  • Antonym: undesirableness - the quality of being undesirable

Etymology

From desirable + -ness, ultimately from Old French *desirier* (to desire) and Latin *desiderabilis* (worthy of desire).
