Pianola

1. NOUN

/ piːəˈnoʊlə / (AmE), / piːəˈnəʊlə / (BrE)
a piano that plays automatically by means of a mechanism using a perforated paper roll to operate the keys
mechanically operated player piano

Examples

The antique Pianola in the living room still works when you insert a new roll of music.
At the museum, visitors watched the Pianola perform a lively ragtime tune by itself.
He inherited his grandmother's Pianola, complete with dozens of perforated rolls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: player piano - a piano that plays automatically using a programmed music roll or digital control

Etymology

From trade name 'Pianola', first produced by Aeolian Company (U.S., late 19th century).
