Cinderella

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪndəˈrɛlə / (AmE), / ˌsɪndəˈrɛlə / (BrE)
A woman whose talents or worth have been overlooked but who achieves sudden success and recognition.
overlooked woman who attains sudden success

Examples

After years of hard work, she became the Cinderella of the tech world, finally gaining the recognition she deserved.
The small research team turned out to be the Cinderella story of the industry, winning the prestigious award.
He referred to the artist as a modern Cinderella whose talent was ignored until her sudden breakthrough.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dark horse - a previously unknown person who emerges to prominence

Etymology

From the fairy tale character Cinderella, whose transformation from neglect to fame inspired the figurative sense.

2. PROPN

/ sɪn.dəˈrɛl.ə / (AmE), / sɪn.dəˈrɛl.ə / (BrE)
a young woman in a fairy tale who is mistreated by her stepfamily but is magically aided and ultimately marries a prince; commonly used to refer to someone whose fortunes change dramatically for the better
fairy-tale heroine helped by magic to escape hardship

Examples

After years of hardship, she became a real-life Cinderella when her business suddenly succeeded.
The little girl dressed as Cinderella for her school play, complete with a sparkling blue gown.
Many sports fans call their team's unexpected win a true Cinderella story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairy tale - a story involving magical events and imaginary creatures

Etymology

From French Cendrillon, diminutive of cendre (ashes), referring to a girl who worked among the ashes.
