thrombin

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɑm.bɪn / (AmE), / ˈθrɒm.bɪn / (BrE)
an enzyme in blood that converts fibrinogen to fibrin and causes clotting
enzyme aiding blood clotting
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that thrombin plays a crucial role in the process of blood clotting.
During surgery, the surgeon used topical thrombin to help control bleeding.
A deficiency in thrombin can lead to serious problems with blood clot formation.

Etymology

From Greek 'thrombos' (clot) + '-in' (protein suffix).
