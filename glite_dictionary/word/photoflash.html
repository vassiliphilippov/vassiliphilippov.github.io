photoflash

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊtoʊˌflæʃ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊtəʊˌflæʃ / (BrE)
A lamp that produces a brief, intense burst of light, used to illuminate a scene while taking a photograph.
momentary light for photography

Examples

The old camera requires a photoflash bulb for indoor pictures.
She replaced the photoflash before taking another photograph.
Many vintage cameras had a built-in photoflash for low-light conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flash - a sudden burst of light used in photography

Etymology

From 'photo-' (relating to light or photography) + 'flash'.
