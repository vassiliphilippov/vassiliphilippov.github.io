Cyclopes didactylus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈkloʊpiːz daɪˈdæktɪləs / (AmE), / sɪˈkləʊpiːz dɪˈdæktɪləs / (BrE)
A small South American anteater species, also known as the silky anteater, with long silky fur and no teeth.
small South American silky anteater

Examples

The Cyclopes didactylus spends much of its time hidden high in the rainforest canopy.
Researchers observed a Cyclopes didactylus using its strong tail to move between branches.
With its golden fur, the Cyclopes didactylus blends perfectly into the treetop foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silky anteater - common name for Cyclopes didactylus

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Cyclopes' (Greek 'kyklōps', meaning cyclops) and species name 'didactylus' (Greek 'di-' meaning two and 'daktylos' meaning finger).
