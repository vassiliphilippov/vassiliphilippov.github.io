minim

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪn.əm / (AmE), / ˈmɪn.ɪm / (BrE)
A musical note having the time value of half a whole note; also known as a half note.
musical note worth half a whole note
british

Examples

The first bar begins with a minim followed by two crotchets.
Please hold the minim for its full value in this piece.
He struggled to remember if a minim was longer than a crotchet or shorter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: half note - a musical note worth half a whole note (American English)

Etymology

From Latin 'minima' (the smallest), originally referring to its status as the shortest note in medieval music notation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪm / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪm / (BrE)
a former British imperial unit of volume for liquids or dry substances, equal to 1/60th of a fluid dram or about 0.059 milliliters.
former British unit of liquid/dry volume
british

Examples

The old prescription specified two minims of tincture per dose.
Early apothecaries meticulously measured each minim to ensure proper medication.
A single minim was so tiny that it was rarely used outside specialized fields.

Etymology

From Latin 'minimus' meaning 'smallest'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪm / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪm / (BrE)
a unit of liquid measure, especially in the United States and Britain, equal to 1/60 of a fluid dram or about 0.0616 milliliters
a small liquid measure (1/60 fluid dram)
medical

Examples

The pharmacist measured exactly ten minims of the tincture for the prescription.
Old medical recipes sometimes call for amounts as small as one minim.
Each minim is roughly equivalent to a single drop of liquid.

Etymology

From Latin *minimus* ‘smallest’.
