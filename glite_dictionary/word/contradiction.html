contradiction

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.trəˈdɪk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.trəˈdɪk.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of stating that someone else's statement is wrong or not true
act of disagreeing with a statement

Examples

The witness's statement was met with immediate contradiction from the defense attorney.
She learned to avoid open contradiction of her supervisor during meetings.
Any contradiction of his opinions would quickly lead to an argument.

Synset Examples

he spoke as if he thought his claims were immune to contradiction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denial - the act of stating something is not true
  • Antonym: agreement - the state of sharing the same opinion; absence of disagreement

Etymology

From Latin contradictio, from contrā (against) + dictio (saying).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˈdɪkʃən / (BrE)
a statement in logic that is necessarily false because it contains mutually incompatible elements
necessarily false statement (logic)

Examples

In logic, any self-refuting claim is considered a contradiction.
The argument collapses because it leads to a contradiction.
The statement 'this sentence is false' appears to be a contradiction in itself.

Synset Examples

the statement ‘he is brave and he is not brave’ is a contradiction

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: paradox - a statement that contradicts itself but might be true
  • Antonym: tautology - a statement that is true by necessity or by virtue of its logical form

Etymology

From Latin contrādictiōn- (speaking against), from contrādīcere (to contradict).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.trəˈdɪk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.trəˈdɪk.ʃən / (BrE)
a situation in which two or more ideas, statements, or actions are opposed to each other or cannot both be true or exist
opposition between ideas or statements

Examples

There is a clear contradiction between his words and actions.
The witness's story is full of contradiction, making it hard to believe.
Her argument contains a fundamental contradiction that weakens her case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradox - a statement that seems self-contradictory but may be true
  • Antonym: agreement - state of being in accord or harmony

Etymology

From Latin 'contradictio', from 'contradīcere' (to speak against).
