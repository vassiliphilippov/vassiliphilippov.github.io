manse

1. NOUN

/ mæn(t)s / (AmE), / mæn(t)s / (BrE)
the house provided for and occupied by a minister of a Presbyterian church.
house for a Presbyterian minister

Examples

The old manse at the edge of the village was surrounded by ancient trees.
After the new minister was appointed, the congregation refurbished the manse.
In Scotland, the manse was traditionally located close to the church itself.

Etymology

From Latin 'mansus' (dwelling), from 'manere' (to remain, dwell), via Old French 'manse'.

2. NOUN

/ mæn(t)s / (AmE), / mɑːns / (BrE)
A large and impressive house, especially one used as a residence by a minister of a church.
large and imposing house

Examples

The old minister lived in the stone manse at the edge of the village.
After the service, the congregation gathered for tea in the spacious manse.
The elegant manse overlooked a broad lawn and ancient oak trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mansion - a large, impressive house
  • Antonym: cottage - a small, simple house

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'mansus', 'manere' (to dwell), related to Old French 'manse'.
