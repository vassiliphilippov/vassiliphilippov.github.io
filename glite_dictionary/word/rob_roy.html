Rob Roy

1. PROPN

/ rɑb ˈrɔɪ / (AmE), / rɒb ˈrɔɪ / (BrE)
(1671–1734) A Scottish clan leader and outlaw, Robert Roy MacGregor, who became a folk hero and was immortalized in Sir Walter Scott's 1817 novel.
Scottish folk hero and outlaw

Examples

The legend of Rob Roy continues to inspire storytellers across Scotland.
Many historians debate the true character of Rob Roy as both hero and outlaw.
Sir Walter Scott's novel brought Rob Roy to international fame.

Etymology

Nickname from Scottish Gaelic ‘Raibeart Ruadh’ ('Red Robert') for Robert Roy MacGregor.
