Canachites

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnækɪtiːz / (AmE), / kəˈnakɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of grouse found in North America, including the spruce grouse.
genus of North American grouse

Examples

The genus Canachites includes the well-known spruce grouse native to North American forests.
Researchers have studied the habitat preferences of Canachites in boreal regions.
The classification of Canachites has been revised in recent ornithological literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spruce grouse - a species of grouse found in coniferous forests of North America

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'kanax', a type of bird, and the genus suffix '-ites'.
