demoiselle

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛm.wɑɪˈzɛl / (AmE), / ˌdɛm.wɑɪˈzɛl / (BrE)
An archaic or literary term for a young unmarried woman.
young unmarried woman (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The ball was filled with every demoiselle of the county, dressed in her finest gown.
In the ancient tale, a brave knight rescued the stranded demoiselle.
She was no longer a demoiselle, but a married woman with her own household.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maiden - an unmarried girl or young woman
  • Synonym: damsel - a young unmarried woman, especially in historical or literary contexts

Etymology

From French 'demoiselle', diminutive of 'dame' meaning 'lady'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛmwəˈzɛl / (AmE), / ˌdɛm.wɑːˈzɛl / (BrE)
A small, brightly colored tropical marine fish found on coral reefs, especially of the damselfish family.
small colorful tropical reef fish

Examples

We spotted a brilliant blue demoiselle darting among the coral branches.
Many species of demoiselle are popular in home aquariums for their vivid colors.
The reef was teeming with demoiselles and other small fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damselfish - small, brightly colored tropical marine fish commonly found near coral reefs

Etymology

From French 'demoiselle' (damsel), applied to the fish due to its delicate appearance.
