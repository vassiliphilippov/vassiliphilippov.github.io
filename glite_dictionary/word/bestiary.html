bestiary

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛstʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɛstɪəri / (BrE)
a medieval book, often illustrated, containing allegorical and descriptive accounts of real and mythical animals
medieval book of animal descriptions

Examples

The library owns a rare illuminated bestiary from the thirteenth century.
Scholars study the bestiary to understand how medieval people interpreted animals.
The bestiary depicts both real creatures and mythical beasts such as the unicorn.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin bestiarium (a book of beasts), from Latin bestia (beast).
