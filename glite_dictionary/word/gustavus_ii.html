Gustavus II

1. PROPN

/ ɡəˈsteɪvəs səˈkənd / (AmE), / ɡʊˈstɑːvəs ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
(1594–1632) King of Sweden, known for transforming Sweden into a major European power through military victories and domestic reforms, and for his intervention in the Thirty Years' War.
Swedish king, military leader (1594–1632)

Examples

Historians credit Gustavus II with modernizing Sweden's military and administration.
Gustavus II died at the Battle of Lützen while fighting in the Thirty Years' War.
The legacy of Gustavus II can be seen in Sweden's development as a European power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gustavus Adolphus - another name for Gustavus II, King of Sweden
