simplicity

1. NOUN

/ sɪmˈplɪsəti / (AmE), / sɪmˈplɪsəti / (BrE)
the quality or condition of being easy to understand or not complicated
quality of being simple or uncomplicated

Examples

The architect admired the simplicity of the building's design.
She explained the process with remarkable simplicity, making it easy for everyone to understand.
Many people find comfort in the simplicity of rural life.

Synset Examples

the simplicity of a crystal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: complexity - the state of being complex or complicated
  • Synonym: plainness - lack of adornment or elaboration; simplicity

Etymology

From Old French simplicite, from Latin simplicitas (plainness, candor).

2. NOUN

/ sɪmˈplɪsəti / (AmE), / sɪmˈplɪsɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being natural, genuine, and free from affectation or pretense
absence of affectation or pretense

Examples

There is a simplicity in her manner that puts everyone at ease.
The simplicity of his speech made his sincerity obvious.
People admired the simplicity with which he lived, without any need to impress others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authenticity - the quality of being genuine or real
  • Antonym: affectation - artificial behavior meant to impress others

Etymology

From Latin 'simplicitas' (plainness, candor), from 'simplex' (simple).

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ sɪmˈplɪsəti / (AmE), / sɪmˈplɪsəti / (BrE)
the quality of being easily deceived; innocence or lack of subtle understanding
innocence; lack of subtlety

Examples

Her simplicity made her vulnerable to those who would take advantage.
Some mistake his simplicity for a lack of intelligence, but he is just unworldly.
Stories sometimes praise the simplicity of country folk, though it can make them easy targets.

Synset Examples

they took advantage of her simplicity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naivety - lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment
  • Synonym: gullibility - easily deceived or tricked

Etymology

From Old French simplicite, from Latin simplicitās (plainness, innocence).

4. NOUN

/ sɪmˈplɪsɪti / (AmE), / sɪmˈplɪsɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being plain or lacking decoration or ornamentation
plainness; lack of decoration

Examples

The interior owes its charm to the simplicity of white walls and unadorned furniture.
He admired the simplicity of the building's design, which avoided any unnecessary decoration.
The simplicity of her dress made her elegance stand out even more.

Synset Examples

the room was simply decorated with great restraint

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plainness - the quality of not being decorated or elaborate
  • Antonym: ornateness - the quality of being elaborate or highly decorated

Etymology

From Old French simplicité, from Latin simplicitās (plainness, openness).
