elite

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlit / (AmE), / ɪˈliːt / (BrE)
considered as the best or most skilled within a particular group or category
selected as the best

Examples

She attended an elite private school known for its challenging curriculum.
Only the most elite athletes are invited to compete in the championship.
The university has an elite group of researchers leading cutting-edge projects.

Synset Examples

an elect circle of artists
elite colleges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusive - limited to a select group only
  • Antonym: ordinary - not special or distinguished; common

Etymology

From French élite, from Old French eslite (chosen), feminine past participle of eslire (to choose).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈlit / (AmE), / ɪˈliːt / (BrE)
a group or class of people who are considered to be the best in a society or organization, often due to their power, intelligence, or wealth
group with superior status or influence

Examples

The governing elite gathered in the capital to discuss national policy.
Many believe that only the elite have access to the best education.
He never felt comfortable mixing with the social elite of his city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristocracy - the highest class in certain societies, especially those holding hereditary titles or offices
  • Antonym: masses - the ordinary people in society who do not belong to the elite

Etymology

From French élite (chosen, select), from Old French eslit, past participle of eslire (to choose), from Latin eligere.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08403286-n
Synset POS: n
