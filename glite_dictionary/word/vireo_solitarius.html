Vireo solitarius

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪrioʊ soʊˈlɪtɛriəs / (AmE), / ˌvɪəriəʊ səʊˈlɪtəriəs / (BrE)
A species of small North American songbird with a bluish-grey head and mostly green body, also known as the blue-headed vireo.
North American songbird species

Examples

During migration season, birdwatchers often spot the Vireo solitarius in wooded areas.
The distinctive song of the Vireo solitarius makes it easier to identify among other forest birds.
Research has shown that the Vireo solitarius prefers dense foliage for nesting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue-headed vireo - Common name for Vireo solitarius, a North American songbird

Etymology

From Latin: 'vireo' meaning 'greenish bird', 'solitarius' meaning 'solitary'.
