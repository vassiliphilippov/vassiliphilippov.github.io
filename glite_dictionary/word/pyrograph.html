pyrograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.roʊˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈpʌɪ.rəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a decorative design or image produced by burning a design onto a surface, typically wood or leather, using a heated instrument
a design made by burning onto a surface

Examples

She proudly displayed her latest pyrograph at the local art show.
The table was decorated with an intricate pyrograph of woodland animals.
He learned how to create a pyrograph as a hobby during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrography - the art or technique of burning designs onto wood or leather

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire) and '-graph' (something written or drawn).
