strawberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔːˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈstrɒbəri / (BrE)
A small, sweet, red fruit with tiny seeds on its surface, typically eaten raw or used in desserts and jams.
sweet red fruit with seeds

Examples

She picked a ripe strawberry from the garden and ate it immediately.
The cake was topped with fresh strawberries and whipped cream.
Have you ever tried homemade strawberry jam on toast?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: berry - small, juicy fruit with seeds embedded in the flesh

Etymology

Old English 'streawberige', from 'streaw' (straw) + 'berige' (berry); named for the straw used as mulch around the plants.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔːˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈstrɔːbəri / (BrE)
a low-growing plant with white flowers and sweet red edible fruit, or the fruit itself, characterized by a surface dotted with small seeds
sweet red fruit of a low-growing plant

Examples

She picked a ripe strawberry from the garden and ate it immediately.
The dessert was topped with fresh strawberries and whipped cream.
We planted several strawberry plants in our backyard this spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: berry - a small, juicy, fleshy fruit

Etymology

Old English 'streawberige', from 'streaw' (straw) + 'berige' (berry), possibly from the practice of using straw as mulch.

3. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔːˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈstrɔːbəri / (BrE)
A soft red birthmark, usually raised, that resembles the shape or color of a strawberry.
a soft red birthmark

Examples

The doctor explained that the red mark on her arm was a strawberry and that it would likely fade over time.
Parents sometimes worry when their newborn has a strawberry on the face, but most are harmless.
His strawberry grew rapidly in the first few weeks but didn't cause any problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemangioma - a benign tumor of blood vessels, often seen as a red birthmark
