Artemisia abrotanum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrtəˈmɪziə əˈbroʊtənəm / (AmE), / ˌɑːtɪˈmɪziə æˈbrɒtənəm / (BrE)
A shrubby plant of the wormwood family, native to Europe and naturalized in North America, sometimes used in brewing and as a decorative or medicinal herb.
shrubby European wormwood plant

Examples

Gardeners often plant Artemisia abrotanum for its feathery foliage and pleasant scent.
Some traditional recipes call for Artemisia abrotanum to enhance the flavor of homemade beer.
The leaves of Artemisia abrotanum were once popular as a natural air freshener.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wormwood - a bitter aromatic plant of the genus Artemisia

Etymology

From scientific Latin; 'Artemisia' from the Greek goddess Artemis and 'abrotanum' from Latin for southernwood.
