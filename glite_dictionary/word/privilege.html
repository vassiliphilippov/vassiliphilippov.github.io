privilege

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪv.əl.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈprɪv.ɪ.lɪdʒ / (BrE)
A special right, advantage, or immunity granted or available only to a particular person or group.
special right or advantage

Examples

Access to the members-only lounge is a privilege reserved for gold card holders.
She felt it was a privilege to be chosen to speak at the event.
Not everyone has the privilege of a good education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right - a moral or legal entitlement to have or do something
  • Antonym: disadvantage - an unfavorable circumstance or condition

Etymology

From Old French *privilege*, from Latin *privilegium* (a law applying to an individual).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪv.əl.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈprɪv.ɪ.lɪdʒ / (BrE)
a special right or advantage granted to a particular person or group, often by law or custom
special right or advantage

Examples

Education was once considered a privilege of the wealthy elite.
Having access to clean water should not be a privilege but a basic human right.
Voting rights were long treated as a privilege reserved for certain citizens.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right - a legal or moral entitlement to have or do something
  • Antonym: obligation - something one is bound to do; opposite of a right or privilege

Etymology

From Latin privilegium (a law applying to one person), via Old French privilege.

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɪv.ɪ.lɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈprɪv.ɪ.lɪdʒ / (BrE)
(law) A special legal right to refuse to reveal confidential information obtained in a protected relationship, such as between lawyer and client.
legal right to withhold confidential information
legal

Examples

The attorney claimed privilege to avoid disclosing her client's statements in court.
Doctor-patient privilege ensures that sensitive medical information remains confidential.
Journalists often argue for privilege when asked to reveal their sources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confidentiality - the state of keeping information secret or private
  • Synonym: immunity - protection from being required to do something, especially in law
  • Antonym: obligation - a duty or commitment to act, often the opposite of a privilege in law

Etymology

From Old French *privilege*, from Latin *privilegium* (law applying to a particular person).

4. VERB

/ ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
to grant someone a special right, advantage, or immunity not enjoyed by others
grant a special right or advantage

Examples

The committee privileged certain applications over others based on need.
As a student, she was privileged to attend exclusive seminars with the visiting professor.
The regulations privilege domestic producers by limiting imports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: favor - to show approval or preference by treating specially
  • Antonym: discriminate - to treat someone differently, usually in a negative sense
