overfond

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈfɑnd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈfɒnd / (BrE)
excessively or unduly fond of someone or something
excessively fond

Examples

She became overfond of her cat, treating it almost like a child.
Some people think he is overfond of sweets and should cut back.
Teachers should avoid becoming overfond of any particular student.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fond - having a strong liking or affection for

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessively) + 'fond' (having a strong liking).
