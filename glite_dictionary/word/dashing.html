dashing

1. ADJ

/ ˈdæʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaʃɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or their appearance) stylish, fashionable, and confident.
stylish and confident in manner or dress

Examples

He looked particularly dashing in his new black tuxedo at the party.
The young officer cut a dashing figure in his crisp uniform and confident smile.
She always admires actors who bring a dashing charm to their roles.

Synset Examples

a dapper young man
a jaunty red hat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dapper - neat and stylish in appearance
  • Synonym: jaunty - having a lively, cheerful, and self-confident manner

Etymology

Present participle of 'dash', used figuratively since the 18th century for energetic or stylish qualities.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdæʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdæʃɪŋ / (BrE)
attractive, energetic, and full of confidence and style, often in a way that is lively and spirited
stylish and energetic; full of spirit

Examples

He looked quite dashing in his new suit at the party.
The dashing young officer quickly became the talk of the town.
Everyone admired her dashing and confident manner.

Synset Examples

a dashing hero

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charming - pleasant or attractive in personality or appearance
  • Antonym: unimpressive - not attractive or striking

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'dash,' originally meaning to move quickly, later extended to expressive style and flamboyance.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02287356-s
Synset POS: s
