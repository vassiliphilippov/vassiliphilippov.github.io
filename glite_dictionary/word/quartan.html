quartan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔɹ.tən / (AmE), / ˈkwɔː.tən / (BrE)
A malarial fever characterized by recurrent attacks every fourth day.
malaria with four-day recurrence
medical

Examples

The patient suffered repeated bouts of quartan throughout the rainy season.
Doctors identified the illness as quartan malaria after observing the four-day cycle of fever.
Historically, quartan was common in regions plagued by certain types of mosquitoes.

Etymology

From Latin 'quartana' (fourth), from 'quartus' meaning 'fourth'.
