unheralded

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈhɛrəldɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈhɛr.əldɪd / (BrE)
not announced, predicted, or expected; happening without warning or prior notice.
without warning or prior notice

Examples

The team made an unheralded entrance, surprising even their supporters.
News of the company's merger was completely unheralded by the media.
An unheralded guest arrived at the party, catching everyone off guard.

Synset Examples

they arrived unannounced
a totally unheralded telegram that his daughter … died last night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unexpected - not anticipated or foreseen
  • Antonym: announced - made known publicly or officially

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + heralded, from ‘herald’ (a messenger or one who proclaims).
