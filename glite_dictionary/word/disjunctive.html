disjunctive

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈdʒʌŋktɪv / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒʌŋktɪv / (BrE)
serving to divide or separate; relating to separation or distinction.
serving to divide or separate

Examples

The lawyer took a disjunctive approach, focusing on the differences between the cases.
A disjunctive relationship exists between the two variables, meaning they do not influence each other directly.
The committee issued a disjunctive report, emphasizing points of separation rather than agreement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conjunctive - serving to join or connect

Etymology

From Latin 'disjunctivus', from 'disjungere' (to disjoin).
