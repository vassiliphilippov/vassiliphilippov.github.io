spurt

1. NOUN

/ spɝt / (AmE), / spɜːt / (BrE)
a sudden, forceful discharge or gush of liquid or other substance
sudden discharge or gush

Examples

A spurt of water shot out when he turned on the hose.
The ketchup came out in a sudden spurt and splattered everywhere.
Blood emerged in a brief spurt from the small wound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirt - a small, quick stream of liquid forced out

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of liquid spurting, or related to the Middle English 'spirten' (to jump, leap).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07450700-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ spɝt / (AmE), / spɜːt / (BrE)
to move or act suddenly with increased speed or energy for a short period
move/act suddenly with increased speed

Examples

The runner managed to spurt ahead of the pack during the final meters.
Traffic began to spurt forward as the light turned green.
Sales always spurt during the holiday season, then slow down in January.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprint - to run at full speed over a short distance
  • Synonym: surge - to move suddenly and powerfully forward

Etymology

Probably imitative, related to 'spout' and Scandinavian roots.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01888119-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ spɝt / (AmE), / spɜːt / (BrE)
(of liquid, gas, etc.) to come out suddenly and forcefully in a stream or jet.
come out suddenly in a stream

Examples

The faucet suddenly began to spurt water all over the counter.
Blood spurted from the small cut on his finger.
The whale spurted a jet of water from its blowhole into the air.

Synset Examples

water gushed forth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gush - to flow out suddenly and in large amounts
  • Antonym: trickle - to flow or fall in drops or a thin stream

Etymology

Perhaps imitative of the sound of spurting; related to Middle Dutch 'spuiten' (to spout).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02072341-v
Synset POS: v
