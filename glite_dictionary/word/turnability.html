turnability

1. NOUN

/ tɝːnəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / tɜːnəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or property of being able to turn or be turned, especially easily or effectively.
ability to be turned

Examples

The turnability of the new wheelchair makes it much easier to navigate tight spaces.
High turnability is a crucial feature for vehicles operating in narrow city streets.
Engineers tested the turnability of the prototype to ensure smooth cornering.

Etymology

Formed from 'turn' + suffix '-ability' indicating the quality or capacity of being turned.
