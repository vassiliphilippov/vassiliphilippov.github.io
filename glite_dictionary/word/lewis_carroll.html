Lewis Carroll

1. PROPN

/ ˈluːɪs ˈkæɹəl / (AmE), / ˈluːɪs ˈkærəl / (BrE)
the pen name of Charles Lutwidge Dodgson (1832–1898), an English author, mathematician, and logician best known for writing 'Alice's Adventures in Wonderland' and 'Through the Looking-Glass'.
English author (pen name of Charles Dodgson)

Examples

Lewis Carroll is famous for creating the beloved story 'Alice's Adventures in Wonderland'.
Many of Lewis Carroll's poems are noted for their playful language and inventive logic.
The life and works of Lewis Carroll have inspired generations of writers and artists.

Etymology

Pen name adopted by Charles Lutwidge Dodgson, formed from the Latinized versions of his first and middle names reversed.
