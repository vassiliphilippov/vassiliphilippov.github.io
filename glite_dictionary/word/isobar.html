isobar

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.səˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.səˌbɑː / (BrE)
A line drawn on a weather map that connects points of equal atmospheric pressure at a given time.
line connecting equal air pressure points

Examples

The weather map showed several isobars close together, indicating strong winds.
Meteorologists use isobars to identify areas of high and low pressure.
Drawing isobars helps to visualize how atmospheric pressure varies across a region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isotherm - line connecting points of equal temperature

Etymology

From Greek iso- (equal) + baros (weight, pressure).
