sanguification

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæŋɡwəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsaŋɡwɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the formation of blood cells in the living body, especially within the bone marrow
formation of blood cells
medical

Examples

Recent studies have provided new insights into sanguification and the cellular processes involved.
Disorders that disrupt sanguification can lead to serious health complications.
Bone marrow is a primary site for sanguification in mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hematopoiesis - the process of blood cell formation

Etymology

From Latin sanguis (blood) + -fication (making or producing).
