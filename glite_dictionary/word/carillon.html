carillon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪˌlɑn / (AmE), / ˈkærɪlɒn / (BrE)
a set of fixed chromatically tuned bells, typically hung in a tower and played by means of a keyboard or mechanism
a set of tuned bells in a tower

Examples

The cathedral’s carillon played a beautiful melody every hour.
He studied how to perform on the carillon during his time in Belgium.
A crowd gathered in the square to hear the carillon concert at noon.

Etymology

From French carillon (peal of bells), ultimately from Late Latin quadrinio (set of four).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrələn / (AmE), / ˈkærɪlɒn / (BrE)
A set of stationary bells, each producing a single note, hung in a bell tower and played using a keyboard or automatic mechanism.
set of bells in a tower, played musically

Examples

The carillon rang out beautifully across the town square at noon.
She learned to play the carillon as part of her studies in music.
Every Sunday, the sound of the carillon fills the air with melodic bells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimes - a set of tuned bells played by striking, often in a tower

Etymology

From French 'carillon', originally from Old French, related to 'quadrillon' ('four bells'), from Late Latin 'quaterni' ('four each').
