gerrymander

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛriˌmændɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛriˌmændə / (BrE)
to manipulate the boundaries of an electoral district unfairly to favor one party or group
manipulate voting district boundaries

Examples

The ruling party attempted to gerrymander the electoral map to weaken the opposition’s chances.
Activists argued that the new voting districts were gerrymandered to diminish minority representation.
It is illegal to gerrymander districts for the sole purpose of protecting incumbents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redistrict - to divide or organize an area into new political districts

Etymology

From 'Gerry' (after Elbridge Gerry, governor of Massachusetts, who approved such a scheme) and 'salamander' (from the shape of the district formed).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛriˌmændər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛrɪˌmɑːndə / (BrE)
an instance of manipulating the boundaries of electoral districts to favor one political party or group
manipulation of voting district boundaries for advantage

Examples

The new district map is widely condemned as a blatant gerrymander.
The court overturned the gerrymander for violating voters' rights.
Critics argue that every gerrymander undermines the fairness of elections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gerrymandering - the act or process of manipulating boundaries for advantage

Etymology

From Elbridge Gerry (1744–1814), US politician, and salamander; inspired by a district map in Massachusetts shaped like a salamander created under Gerry's governorship.
