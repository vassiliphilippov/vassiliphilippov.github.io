synchronous

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs / (BrE)
(of digital communication) requiring a common timing signal between devices in order to coordinate transmissions.
requiring a common timing signal in communication
medical

Examples

The devices must use a synchronous protocol to ensure data is transmitted accurately.
Because the network is synchronous, each node relies on a shared clock signal for communication.
A synchronous transmission allows bits to be sent continuously with precise timing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: asynchronous - not coordinated by a common timing signal

Etymology

From Greek 'synchronos', from 'syn-' (together) + 'chronos' (time).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs / (BrE)
happening, existing, or moving at the same time or with the same periodicity or phase as something else
happening at the same time

Examples

The two experiments produced synchronous results, confirming the theory.
A flock of birds flew in synchronous motion across the sky.
The festival parades are held in synchronous celebration in both cities.

Synset Examples

recovery was synchronous with therapy
a synchronous set of clocks
the synchronous action of a bird's wings in flight
synchronous oscillations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simultaneous - existing or occurring at the same time
  • Antonym: asynchronous - not occurring at the same time; out of sync

Etymology

From Greek 'syn-' (together) + 'chronos' (time).
