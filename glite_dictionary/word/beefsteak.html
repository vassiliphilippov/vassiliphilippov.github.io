beefsteak

1. NOUN

/ ˈbifˌsteɪk / (AmE), / ˈbiːfsteɪk / (BrE)
A thick slice of beef, typically cut from the hindquarters, usually cooked by broiling, grilling, or frying.
thick slice of beef for grilling or frying

Examples

He ordered a juicy beefsteak and a side of fries for dinner.
The restaurant is famous for its tender grilled beefsteak.
She prefers her beefsteak cooked medium rare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steak - a thick slice of meat, especially beef, cut for broiling or frying

Etymology

From beef + steak; 'steak' from Old Norse 'steik' (roast).
