cordgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrdˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a perennial grass of the genus Spartina, often found in coastal regions and important for stabilizing soil
coastal perennial grass (Spartina genus)

Examples

Dense stands of cordgrass are essential for preventing erosion along salt marsh coasts.
The restoration project included planting native cordgrass to stabilize the shoreline.
Scientists observed that cordgrass provides critical habitat for many marsh-dwelling animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spartina - a genus of coastal grasses known as cordgrass

Etymology

From 'cord', referring to the tough stems, and 'grass', denoting its botanical form.
