nervus phrenicus

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɜrvəs frəˈnɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs frɪˈniːkəs / (BrE)
either of a pair of nerves that arise from the cervical spinal cord and descend through the thorax to innervate the diaphragm, playing a crucial role in breathing
nerve that controls the diaphragm
medical

Examples

Damage to the nervus phrenicus can result in paralysis of the diaphragm.
The surgeon carefully avoided the nervus phrenicus during the chest procedure.
Stimulation of the nervus phrenicus can be used to test diaphragmatic function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phrenic nerve - the nerve that innervates the diaphragm

Etymology

From Latin 'nervus' (nerve) + 'phrenicus' (of the diaphragm), from Greek 'phrēn' (diaphragm).
