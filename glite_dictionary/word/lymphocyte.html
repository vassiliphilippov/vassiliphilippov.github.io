lymphocyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪmfəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪmfəsʌɪt / (BrE)
a type of white blood cell, part of the immune system, that helps the body fight infection and disease
type of white blood cell
medical

Examples

An elevated lymphocyte count may indicate that your body is fighting an infection.
Doctors examined the patient's blood and found a decrease in lymphocyte levels.
Lymphocyte function is crucial for a healthy immune response.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukocyte - a white blood cell

Etymology

From Greek 'lympha' (water) and 'kytos' (cell).
