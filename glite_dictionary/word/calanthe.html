Calanthe

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlænθi / (AmE), / kəˈlænθi / (BrE)
A large and widely distributed genus of terrestrial orchids, native to Asia and the Pacific regions.
genus of terrestrial orchids

Examples

Several species of Calanthe are popular among orchid collectors for their beautiful blooms.
The Calanthe genus includes over two hundred species of terrestrial orchids.
Botanists discovered a rare Calanthe in the rainforest during their expedition.

Etymology

From Greek 'kallos' (beauty) and 'anthos' (flower), referring to the attractive flowers.
