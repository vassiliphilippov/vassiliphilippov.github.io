Calosoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈsoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌkaləˈsəʊmə / (BrE)
a genus of ground beetles known for preying on caterpillars and other insects
genus of predatory ground beetles

Examples

The Calosoma beetle is often introduced to control caterpillar pests in crops.
Many gardeners value Calosoma species for their appetite for harmful insects.
A shiny green Calosoma crawled quickly across the forest floor.

Etymology

From Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) + 'soma' (body), referring to the beetle's appearance.
