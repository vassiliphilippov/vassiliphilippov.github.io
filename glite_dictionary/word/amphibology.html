amphibology

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmfəˈbɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌamfɪˈbɒlədʒi / (BrE)
A phrase or sentence that is grammatically ambiguous, so it can be interpreted in more than one way.
grammatical ambiguity

Examples

The headline was a classic example of amphibology, leaving readers unsure of its meaning.
Legal documents try to avoid amphibology to prevent misinterpretation.
He pointed out the amphibology in her statement, which could be read two ways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambiguity - uncertainty or inexactness of meaning in language
  • Synonym: equivocation - use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself

Etymology

From Late Latin amphibologia, from Greek amphibologia (ambiguity in language).
