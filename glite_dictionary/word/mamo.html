mamo

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːməʊ / (BrE)
an extinct species of Hawaiian honeycreeper with black plumage and yellow feathers around the tail, once native to Hawaii
extinct Hawaiian honeycreeper bird

Examples

The royal capes of Hawaiian chiefs were often decorated with the yellow feathers of the mamo.
The mamo became extinct in the 19th century due to habitat loss and hunting for its feathers.
Researchers studied preserved mamo specimens to learn about extinct Hawaiian birdlife.

Etymology

From Hawaiian māmō, the name for this bird.
