grazing

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of lightly brushing or touching against something while passing by
light brushing against while passing

Examples

As he squeezed past, his sleeve made a soft grazing against her arm.
There was just a gentle grazing as the branches brushed the car window.
The child felt the grazing of leaves on her face while running through the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brushing - an act of lightly touching in passing
  • Antonym: collision - the act of crashing into something with force

Etymology

From the verb 'graze', meaning to touch lightly in passing, from Old English 'grasian' (to feed on grass).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of animals eating grass or other low vegetation in a field or pasture.
act of animals eating grass

Examples

Cattle are allowed grazing on the pasture from early spring to late autumn.
Overgrazing can damage the land, so regulated grazing is important for the ecosystem.
The meadow is reserved exclusively for sheep grazing during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: browse - the act of feeding on leaves, shoots, or other high-growing vegetation

Etymology

From Middle English 'grasen' (to feed on growing grass).
