busy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɪzi / (AmE), / ˈbɪzi / (BrE)
(of a design, pattern, or picture) containing too many details, making it appear cluttered or distracting.
cluttered with detail (design, pattern)

Examples

The wallpaper was so busy that it made the room feel smaller.
Her dress had a busy pattern that distracted from her natural beauty.
The logo design is too busy and needs to be simplified.

Synset Examples

a busy painting
a fussy design

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fussy - excessively detailed or elaborately decorated
  • Antonym: simple - uncomplicated or plain in design
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01799712-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɪzi / (AmE), / ˈbɪzi / (BrE)
Intrusive or meddlesome, especially by interfering in other people's affairs.
intrusive; meddlesome

Examples

He is always busy in other people's personal matters, which annoys everyone around him.
The busy neighbor offered unsolicited advice about our garden every morning.
People found her busy manner intrusive, as she frequently meddled in affairs that weren't her concern.

Synset Examples

an interfering old woman
bustling about self-importantly making an officious nuisance of himself
busy about other people's business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: officious - assertive of authority in a domineering way, especially with regard to trivial matters
  • Synonym: interfering - intervening or meddling in the affairs of others
  • Antonym: respectful - showing deference and regard for others' boundaries
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01355409-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈbɪzi / (AmE), / ˈbɪzi / (BrE)
(of a place or period) crowded with or characterized by much activity or movement.
full of activity; bustling

Examples

The busy market was crowded with shoppers searching for fresh produce.
It’s hard to find parking on this busy street during rush hour.
After a busy morning at work, she finally took a short break.

Synset Examples

a very busy week
a busy life
a busy street
a busy seaport

Related Terms

  • Antonym: quiet - not busy, lacking activity or noise
  • Synonym: crowded - full of people or things; very full
  • Synonym: hectic - full of frantic activity; very busy

Etymology

From Old English *bisig* (careful, anxious, busy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00036091-s
Synset POS: s

4. VERB

/ ˈbɪzi / (AmE), / ˈbɪzi / (BrE)
to occupy oneself with an activity or task, often to avoid idleness
occupy oneself with an activity

Examples

She likes to busy herself with gardening in her free time.
During the long summer, he busied himself with learning new recipes.
Many retirees busy themselves with volunteer work after leaving their jobs.

Synset Examples

She busies herself with her butterfly collection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to occupy - to fill or take up (a space, time, or attention)
  • Synonym: to engage - to participate or become involved in

Etymology

From Old English *bysig* meaning 'careful, anxious, busy'.

5. ADJ

/ ˈbɪzi / (AmE), / ˈbɪzi / (BrE)
(of a telephone line, lavatory, or similar facility) unavailable for use because it is already occupied or engaged.
unavailable for use (of facility)

Examples

The bathroom is busy, so you'll have to wait your turn.
Every time I call her, the line is busy.
Sorry, the conference room is busy with another meeting right now.

Synset Examples

her line is busy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engaged - (British) used to describe a telephone line or toilet that is unavailable or occupied
  • Antonym: available - free for use; not occupied or busy

actively engaged or occupied
