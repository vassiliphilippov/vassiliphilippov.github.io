fairytale

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛr.teɪˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈfeə.teɪˌteɪl / (BrE)
A story or explanation that is highly implausible or untrue, often used as an excuse or deception.
implausible or untrue story used as an excuse

Examples

His excuse for being late was just another fairytale that nobody believed.
The politician's promises sounded like a fairytale to many voters.
She spun a complicated fairytale about why she couldn't finish her homework.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fabrication - the act of inventing false information
  • Synonym: lie - a false statement made with deliberate intent to deceive
  • Antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true

Etymology

From 'fairy tale' (a story about magical beings), later extended to mean any improbable story.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛrˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈfeə.teɪl / (BrE)
A children's story about magical and imaginary beings and lands, especially one involving fairies or enchantment.
a story involving magical beings, esp. for children

Examples

Every night, she would read a fairytale to her daughter before bed.
The old castle looked like something straight out of a fairytale.
Many fairytales teach children important lessons about good and evil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folktale - a traditional story passed down orally among people
  • Synonym: legend - a traditional story sometimes believed to be historical but unauthenticated

Etymology

From 'fairy' + 'tale', first attested in the late 17th century, modeled after the French 'conte de fées'.
