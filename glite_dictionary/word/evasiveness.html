evasiveness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈveɪsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being intentionally vague, ambiguous, or avoiding giving a direct answer
quality of being vague or avoiding

Examples

His evasiveness during the interview raised suspicions about his involvement.
She answered the questions with such evasiveness that no one knew her true opinion.
Politicians are often criticized for their evasiveness when faced with difficult issues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: directness - quality of being clear and straightforward
  • Synonym: ambiguity - quality of being open to more than one interpretation

Etymology

From 'evasive' + '-ness'; 'evasive' from Latin 'evadere' (to escape).
