oreide

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈraɪd / (AmE), / ɔːˈraɪd / (BrE)
an alloy of copper, zinc, and tin, used to imitate gold, especially in cheap jewelry and ornaments
alloy imitating gold in jewelry

Examples

The necklace was made of oreide, giving it the appearance of real gold at a lower cost.
Many antique clocks feature parts crafted from oreide to mimic the look of gilt bronze.
Collectors value oreide jewelry for its resemblance to gold and its unique historical appeal.

Etymology

From French 'or' (gold) and Greek suffix '-eidos' (form, likeness), meaning 'gold-like'.
