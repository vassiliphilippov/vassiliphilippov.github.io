ideographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌaɪdiəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪdiəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to ideograms; consisting of or using symbols that represent ideas or concepts rather than sounds or words
relating to ideograms or symbols representing ideas

Examples

Chinese writing uses an ideographic system rather than an alphabetic one.
Many ancient scripts employed ideographic symbols to convey meaning.
Her research focuses on the development of ideographic characters in Asian languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pictographic - relating to a type of writing that uses pictorial symbols

Etymology

From Greek idea (idea) + -graph (writing); influenced by 'ideogram'.
