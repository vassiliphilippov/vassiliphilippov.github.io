unpredictable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.prɪˈdɪk.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.prɪˈdɪk.tə.bəl / (BrE)
not able to be predicted or known in advance; likely to change suddenly and without warning
not able to be predicted

Examples

The weather in this region is notoriously unpredictable, changing from sunshine to storms within minutes.
His unpredictable behavior made it difficult for his team to work with him effectively.
Stock prices can be highly unpredictable, rising and falling without clear reason.

Synset Examples

an unpredictable (or indeterminable) future

Related Terms

  • Antonym: predictable - able to be predicted
  • Synonym: uncertain - not able to be firmly relied on; not known or definite

Etymology

From un- (not) + predictable, from Latin praedicere (to foretell).

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.prɪˈdɪk.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.prɪˈdɪk.tə.bəl / (BrE)
not happening at regular or expected intervals; irregular or erratic in timing or occurrence.
not regular or occurring at fixed intervals

Examples

The doctor noticed an unpredictable rhythm in her heartbeat during the examination.
The weather in this region is completely unpredictable, changing from sunny to stormy without warning.
An unpredictable schedule can make planning your day very difficult.

Synset Examples

an irregular heartbeat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irregular - not even or regular in pattern or timing

Etymology

From un- (not) + predictable (from Latin praedicere, to foretell).
