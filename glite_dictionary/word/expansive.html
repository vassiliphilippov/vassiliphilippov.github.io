expansive

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈspænsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɑːnsɪv / (BrE)
(of behavior or actions) impressive and ambitious in scale or scope; grand or wide-ranging.
impressive and ambitious in scale

Examples

Her expansive vision for the company included launching products across three continents.
The billionaire was known for his expansive lifestyle and extravagant parties.
They embarked on an expansive renovation of the historic palace.

Synset Examples

an expansive lifestyle
in the grand manner
collecting on a grand scale
heroic undertakings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grand - magnificent and imposing in appearance, size, or style

Etymology

From Latin 'expansivus', from 'expandere' (to spread out).

/ ɪkˈspænsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈspænsɪv / (BrE)
able or tending to expand, or characterized by expansion; capable of increasing in size, volume, or scope.
able or likely to expand

Examples

The balloon is made of an expansive material that stretches as it fills with air.
The scientist measured the expansive force created by the heated gas.
Certain soils become expansive when they absorb water, which can damage building foundations.

Synset Examples

Expansive materials

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin 'expandere' (to spread out) via French 'expansif'.

medical

Patients in an expansive state may display excessive optimism and an inflated self-esteem.
