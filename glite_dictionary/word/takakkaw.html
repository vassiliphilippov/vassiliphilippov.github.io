Takakkaw

1. PROPN

/ təˈkækəˌkɔ / (AmE), / təˈkækəkɔː / (BrE)
A prominent waterfall in southeastern British Columbia, Canada; the highest waterfall in the country at 1,250 feet (381 meters) high.
notable Canadian waterfall in British Columbia

Examples

Tourists from around the world come to see Takakkaw Falls every year.
The mist from Takakkaw creates a refreshing atmosphere along the hiking trail.
At 1,250 feet, Takakkaw is Canada's tallest and most impressive waterfall.

Etymology

From Cree, meaning 'it is magnificent' or 'wonderful.'
