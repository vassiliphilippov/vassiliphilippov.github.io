medicine

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / ˈmɛdsɪn / (BrE)
the science and practice of diagnosing, treating, and preventing diseases and injuries, especially as a professional field
the science and profession of treating diseases

Examples

After completing her studies, she chose to practice medicine in a rural community.
Modern medicine has greatly increased the average human lifespan.
Many students are drawn to medicine because they want to help others.

Synset Examples

he studied medicine at Harvard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctor - a person qualified to practice medicine
  • Degree Higher: surgery - the branch of medical practice involving operations

Etymology

From Latin 'medicina' (the healing art), from 'medicus' (physician).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
the branch of medical science concerned with the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of disease, especially using nonsurgical techniques
branch of nonsurgical medical science

Examples

She decided to pursue a career in medicine rather than surgery.
Advancements in medicine have significantly increased average life expectancy.
Many students study medicine with the hope of becoming doctors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surgery - the branch of medicine concerned with operative procedures

Etymology

From Latin 'medicina', from 'medicus' (physician), relating to healing.

4. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɛd.ɪ.sɪn / (BrE)
A substance or preparation used to treat, prevent, or relieve the symptoms of a disease or medical condition.
substance used to treat disease
medical

Examples

You must take your medicine every morning after breakfast.
The doctor prescribed a new medicine to help with my allergies.
Children sometimes refuse to take their medicine because of the taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drug - a chemical substance used in treatment, cure, prevention, or diagnosis of disease
  • Synonym: remedy - a treatment or medicine to cure disease or relieve pain

Etymology

From Latin 'medicina' (the healing art, a remedy), from 'medicus' (physician).
