tiro

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪroʊ / (AmE), / ˈtɪərəʊ / (BrE)
A person who is new to a particular field or activity; a beginner or novice.
beginner; novice

Examples

As a tiro in the art world, she was eager to learn from experienced painters.
Despite being a tiro, he quickly mastered the basics of software development.
Every expert was once a tiro facing their first real challenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
  • Antonym: veteran - a person with long experience in a particular field

Etymology

From Latin 'tiro' (raw recruit, young soldier).
