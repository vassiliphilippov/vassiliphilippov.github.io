meitnerium

1. NOUN

/ maɪtˈnɪəriəm / (AmE), / maɪtˈnɪəriəm / (BrE)
a synthetic radioactive transuranic element with atomic number 109, produced in a laboratory and not found naturally
radioactive element, atomic number 109

Examples

Scientists were able to detect a few atoms of meitnerium during the experiment.
Because meitnerium decays so quickly, it has no practical uses outside scientific research.
The periodic table lists meitnerium among the transactinide elements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: element 109 - another term for the chemical element meitnerium

Etymology

Named after Austrian-Swedish physicist Lise Meitner.
