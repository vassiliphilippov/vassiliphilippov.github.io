mimeograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪmiəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈmɪmiəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a machine for making copies of documents by forcing ink through a stencil onto paper
duplicating machine using stencils

Examples

The teacher used a mimeograph to produce handouts for the class.
Old newsletters were often distributed using a mimeograph before photocopiers became widespread.
The faint smell of ink lingered after printing on the mimeograph.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duplicator - a device for making copies of written, printed, or drawn material

Etymology

From Greek mimeo- (imitate) + -graph (writing)

2. VERB

/ ˈmaɪmiəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪmɪəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
to make copies of (a document) using a mimeograph machine, by pressing ink through a prepared stencil.
copy using a mimeograph machine

Examples

Before the meeting, she mimeographed the agenda for everyone.
In the 1960s, many schools mimeographed class newsletters for parents.
He mimeographs notices for the community center using the old machine.

Synset Examples

She mimeographed the syllabus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duplicate - make an exact copy of a document or object
  • Synonym: photocopy - make a photographic copy of a document

Etymology

From Greek 'mimeisthai' (to imitate) + '-graph' (writing); originally a trade name for the duplicating device.
