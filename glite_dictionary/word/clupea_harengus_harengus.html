Clupea harengus harengus

1. NOUN

/ kluˈpiːə həˈrɛŋɡəs həˈrɛŋɡəs / (AmE), / kluˈpiːə həˈrɛŋɡəs həˈrɛŋɡəs / (BrE)
A species of herring found in large shoals in the northern Atlantic Ocean, valued as an important food fish.
Atlantic herring species; important food fish

Examples

The Clupea harengus harengus is a staple in northern European cuisine, especially when pickled or smoked.
Fishermen depend on large catches of Clupea harengus harengus during the annual migration season.
Overfishing of Clupea harengus harengus can disrupt marine ecosystems in the North Atlantic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herring - a small, silvery fish found in the northern Atlantic and Pacific, often used for food

Etymology

From Latin: genus Clupea (herring) and species harengus (herring).
