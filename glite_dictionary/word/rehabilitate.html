to rehabilitate

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.həˈbɪl.ɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˌriː.həˈbɪl.ɪ.teɪt / (BrE)
to restore something, such as a building, system, or object, to a state of good condition or proper operation
restore to good condition

Examples

The government funded a project to rehabilitate the old railway station and turn it into a museum.
After years of neglect, the community came together to rehabilitate the local park.
Engineers are working hard to rehabilitate the city's aging water supply system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restore - to bring back to a former or original condition

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *rehabilitatus*, past participle of *rehabilitare* (to make able again), from Latin *re-* (again) + *habilitare* (to make able).

2. VERB

/ ˌriː.həˈbɪl.ɪ.teɪt / (AmE), / ˌriː.həˈbɪl.ɪ.teɪt / (BrE)
to restore someone to a former position or status, especially after being disgraced or dismissed, often used in a political context
restore to former status, especially politically

Examples

After the political turmoil, the government decided to rehabilitate several officials who had been dismissed.
The president was rehabilitated and returned to power after years of exile.
Plans are underway to rehabilitate leaders whose reputations were tarnished during the crisis.

Synset Examples

Deng Xiao Ping was rehabilitated several times throughout his lifetime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restore - to return someone or something to a former position or condition
  • Antonym: to demote - to reduce someone to a lower rank or position

Etymology

From Latin 'rehabilitare', from 're-' (again) + 'habilitare' (make able).

3. VERB

/ ˌriːhəˈbɪlɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːhəˈbɪlɪteɪt / (BrE)
to help someone return to a former state of health, ability, or good standing, especially after illness, injury, or criminal punishment
restore to health or good standing

Examples

After the accident, doctors worked hard to rehabilitate him so he could walk again.
The new prison program aims to rehabilitate offenders and reduce repeat crimes.
She was determined to rehabilitate her reputation after the scandal.

Synset Examples

The prisoner was successfully rehabilitated
After a year in the mental clinic, the patient is now rehabilitated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restore - to bring back to a former condition
  • Antonym: damage - to cause harm or injury to someone or something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin rehabilitare, from Latin re- (again) + habilitare (make able).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
