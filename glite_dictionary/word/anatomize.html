to anatomize

1. VERB

/ əˈnætəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / əˈnætəmaɪz / (BrE)
to examine or analyze something in great detail, especially to break it down into its constituent parts
analyze in minute detail

Examples

The professor anatomized every argument presented in the essay.
Journalists anatomize political scandals to uncover the truth behind the headlines.
The book anatomizes the complexities of modern relationships with remarkable precision.

Synset Examples

This writer anatomized the depth of human behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to analyze - to examine something methodically by separating into parts

Etymology

From Medieval Latin anatomizāre, from Greek ana- (up) + temnein (to cut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00646894-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈnæt̬.əˌmaɪz / (AmE), / əˈnæt.ə.maɪz / (BrE)
to dissect or examine in detail, especially to analyze the parts or structure of something
dissect or analyze in detail

Examples

The scientist decided to anatomize the specimen to better understand its structure.
Critics often anatomize a novel to uncover underlying themes and symbols.
To truly understand the issue, we must anatomize its various components.

Synset Examples

anatomize the bodies of the victims of this strange disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dissect - to cut apart body parts for examination
  • Synonym: to analyze - to examine in detail the structure of something

Etymology

From Late Latin anatomizare, from Greek anatomia (dissection).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01553376-v
Synset POS: v
