Cryptoprocta

1. NOUN

/ krɪp.toʊˈprɑk.tə / (AmE), / krɪp.təʊˈprɒk.tə / (BrE)
a genus of large, cat-like carnivorous mammals, including the fossa, native to the forests of Madagascar
genus of carnivorous mammals from Madagascar

Examples

The Cryptoprocta is known for being the top predator in Madagascar's ecosystem.
Researchers observed a Cryptoprocta stalking its prey through the dense rainforest.
The island's unique fauna includes the elusive Cryptoprocta, commonly called the fossa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fossa - a large, carnivorous mammal of Madagascar, member of genus Cryptoprocta

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden) + proktos (anus), referring to a concealed anal pouch.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˈprɑktə / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəˈprɒktə / (BrE)
a genus of large, primitive, cat-like carnivores native to the forests of Madagascar, including the fossa
genus of Madagascar carnivores

Examples

The Cryptoprocta is best known for its only living species, the fossa.
Scientists study Cryptoprocta to understand Madagascar's unique predators.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting the habitats of the Cryptoprocta genus.

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden) + proktos (anus), referring to a concealed anal opening.
