faeces

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːsiːz / (AmE), / ˈfiːsiːz / (BrE)
Solid waste matter discharged from the bowels after digestion; excrement.
solid waste product; excrement
medical british

Examples

The doctor collected a sample of faeces for laboratory analysis.
Children should be taught how to dispose of faeces properly to prevent disease.
The presence of blood in the faeces can indicate digestive problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feces - American English variant spelling of faeces
  • Antonym: urine - liquid excretory product from the kidneys

Etymology

From Latin 'faeces' meaning dregs or sediment.
