Archidiskidon imperator

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkɪˌdɪskɪˌdɒn ɪmˈpɪrəˌtɔr / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˌdɪskɪˌdɒn ɪmˈpɪərətɔː / (BrE)
an extinct species of mammoth, considered the largest known, that lived in North America during the Pleistocene epoch
largest extinct North American mammoth species

Examples

Fossils of the Archidiskidon imperator reveal that it was one of the largest land mammals ever to roam North America.
Many paleontologists study the Archidiskidon imperator to understand the evolution and extinction of mammoths.
The massive tusks of Archidiskidon imperator have been found in several parts of the southwestern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mammoth - any of various large, extinct, elephant-like mammals of the genus Mammuthus

Etymology

From Greek 'archi-' (chief, principal) + 'diskidon' (diminutive of 'diskos', discus) and Latin 'imperator' (emperor, ruler), reflecting its giant size.
