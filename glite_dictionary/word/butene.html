butene

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːtiːn / (AmE), / ˈbjuːtiːn / (BrE)
Any of three isomeric hydrocarbons with the formula C4H8, used in making synthetic rubbers and plastics.
hydrocarbon used in synthetic rubber

Examples

The production of synthetic rubber often starts with butene as a raw material.
Researchers studied the chemical reactions of butene under high pressure.
The company upgraded its facilities to increase butene output for the plastics industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butylene - another term for butene, especially in industry
  • Degree Lower: propene - an alkene with formula C3H6; structurally similar hydrocarbon
  • Degree Higher: pentene - alkene with formula C5H10; a heavier, related compound

Etymology

From 'but-' (from butane) + '-ene' (indicating an alkene).
