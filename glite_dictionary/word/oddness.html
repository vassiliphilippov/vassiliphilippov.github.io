oddness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑdnəs / (AmE), / ˈɒdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being strange or unconventional, especially in a way that is difficult to explain
strangeness or eccentricity

Examples

Her oddness made her stand out in a crowd of conformists.
Many appreciated the oddness of his artistic vision, finding it refreshingly original.
As a child, he was teased for his oddness but grew up to be admired for his unique perspective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eccentricity - unusual or unconventional behavior
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal; absence of strangeness

Etymology

From 'odd' + '-ness', forming a noun of quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑdnɪs / (AmE), / ˈɒdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being odd, especially of a number not being divisible by two
quality of being odd (not divisible by two)

Examples

The oddness of seven makes it impossible to divide evenly between two people.
Mathematicians analyze the oddness or evenness of numbers to find patterns.
Checking for oddness is a common step when programming with numerical data.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: evenness - the quality or state of being even (divisible by two)

Etymology

From 'odd' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being odd.

Synset POS: n
