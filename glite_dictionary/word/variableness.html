variableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛriəblnəs / (AmE), / ˈveəriəblnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being subject to change or variation
state of being variable or changeable

Examples

The variableness of the weather in spring makes it difficult to plan outdoor events.
Farmers must adapt to the variableness of rainfall from year to year.
Her mood was marked by a certain variableness that kept her friends guessing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variability - the quality of being variable; capable of changing or varying
  • Antonym: constancy - the quality of being unchanging or stable

Etymology

From Latin variable, from variabilis (changeable) + -ness.
