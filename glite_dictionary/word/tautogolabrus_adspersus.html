Tautogolabrus adspersus

1. NOUN

/ taʊˌtoʊɡəˈleɪbrəs ædˈspɜːrsəs / (AmE), / tɔːˌtɒɡəˈleɪbrəs ædˈspɜːsəs / (BrE)
a small fish of the wrasse family, commonly found in North Atlantic coastal waters of the United States and Canada, also known as the cunner
a North Atlantic wrasse fish, cunner

Examples

Fishermen along the New England coast often catch Tautogolabrus adspersus during the summer months.
The rocky reefs provide a perfect habitat for Tautogolabrus adspersus to thrive and hide from predators.
Marine biologists studied the feeding habits of Tautogolabrus adspersus in the Gulf of Maine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunner - common name for Tautogolabrus adspersus

Etymology

From Greek 'tautoga', a kind of fish, and Latin 'adspersus' (sprinkled, spotted).
