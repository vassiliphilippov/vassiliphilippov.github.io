trip-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪpˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpˌʌp / (BrE)
an unintentional mistake or blunder, especially one that causes embarrassment or difficulty
an unintentional blunder or mistake

Examples

The CEO's minor trip-up during the speech drew laughter from the audience.
She blamed her trip-up on nerves as she fumbled her lines in the play.
Despite a small trip-up in the final round, he still finished with a respectable score.

Synset Examples

he recited the whole poem without a single trip
he arranged his robes to avoid a trip-up later
confusion caused his unfortunate misstep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - a stupid or careless mistake
  • Synonym: gaffe - an unintentional act causing embarrassment
  • Antonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose (opposite of failing blunder)
