Caladenia cairnsiana

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæl əˈdiːni ə kɛərnzˈiːən ə / (AmE), / ˌkal əˈdiːni ə keənzˈiːan ə / (BrE)
A species of orchid native to southwestern Australia, known for its reddish linear leaves and pale-yellow flowers marked with purple and a deep red or purple lip.
orchid species native to southwestern Australia

Examples

The rare Caladenia cairnsiana blooms in the woodlands of southwestern Australia during late spring.
Botanists studied the pollination strategy of Caladenia cairnsiana to better understand native orchid adaptations.
We spotted a stunning Caladenia cairnsiana with its bright lip and delicate petals along the hiking trail.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) + 'aden' (gland); species named after John Cairns, an Australian botanist.
