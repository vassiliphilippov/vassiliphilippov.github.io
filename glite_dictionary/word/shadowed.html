shadowed

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃædoʊd / (AmE), / ˈʃadəʊd / (BrE)
filled with or characterized by shade; made darker by the presence of shadows.
filled with or darkened by shade

Examples

The path through the woods was shadowed and cool, perfect for an afternoon walk.
Her face was partly shadowed by the brim of her hat, hiding her expression.
The garden became shadowed as the sun sank behind the buildings.

Synset Examples

the shady side of the street
the surface of the pond is dark and shadowed
we sat on rocks in a shadowy cove
cool umbrageous woodlands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shady - providing or full of shade
  • Antonym: sunlit - full of sunlight

Etymology

Formed from the noun 'shadow' + -ed suffix.
