neurosyphilis

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʊroʊˈsɪfəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˈsɪfɪlɪs / (BrE)
Syphilis affecting the central nervous system, often leading to neurological symptoms.
syphilis of the central nervous system
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with neurosyphilis after presenting with memory loss and confusion.
Advanced cases of neurosyphilis can result in personality changes and dementia.
Early detection and treatment of syphilis can prevent progression to neurosyphilis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syphilis - a sexually transmitted infection caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum

Etymology

From neuro- (relating to nerves or the nervous system) and syphilis (a sexually transmitted infection).
