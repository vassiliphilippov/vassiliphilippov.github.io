Babylonia

1. PROPN

/ ˌbæbɪˈloʊniə / (AmE), / ˌbabɪˈləʊnɪə / (BrE)
an ancient kingdom in southern Mesopotamia, known for its influential civilization and its conquest of Israel in the 6th century BC
ancient kingdom in Mesopotamia

Examples

Historians have studied the legal codes developed by Babylonia for centuries.
The Hanging Gardens are believed to have been located in ancient Babylonia.
After their defeat, the Israelites were taken as captives to Babylonia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Babylon - the capital city of ancient Babylonia
  • Synonym: Mesopotamia - ancient region between the Tigris and Euphrates rivers

Etymology

From Latin Babylonia, from Greek Babylōnia, from Babylon (the capital city).
