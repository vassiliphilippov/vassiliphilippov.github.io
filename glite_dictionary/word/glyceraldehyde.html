glyceraldehyde

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡlɪsəˈrældəˌhaɪd / (AmE), / ˌɡlɪs(ə)ˈraldɪhaɪd / (BrE)
a sweet, crystalline compound that is an aldehyde formed during the breakdown of sugars in metabolism, important as an intermediate in biochemical pathways
a sweet crystalline aldehyde from sugar breakdown
medical

Examples

The scientist measured the amount of glyceraldehyde produced during the metabolic reaction.
Glyceraldehyde plays a crucial role as an intermediate in glycolysis.
A sample of pure glyceraldehyde appeared as a white crystalline solid in the laboratory.

Etymology

From glycerol (a type of alcohol) and aldehyde (a family of organic compounds).
