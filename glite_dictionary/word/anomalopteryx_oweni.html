Anomalopteryx oweni

1. NOUN

/ əˌnɑməˌlɑpˈtɛrɪks oʊˈiːnaɪ / (AmE), / əˌnɒmələʊpˈtɛrɪks əʊˈiːnaɪ / (BrE)
an extinct species of small moa, about the size of a large turkey, native to New Zealand
extinct species of small New Zealand moa

Examples

Fossils of the Anomalopteryx oweni have provided insights into New Zealand's unique prehistoric fauna.
The Anomalopteryx oweni was considerably smaller than other moa species, resembling a turkey in size.
Scientists believe the Anomalopteryx oweni became extinct before European settlers arrived in New Zealand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moa - any of various extinct flightless birds of New Zealand

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name 'Anomalopteryx' (from Greek 'anōmalos' meaning irregular + 'pteryx' meaning wing) and species name honoring Sir Richard Owen.
