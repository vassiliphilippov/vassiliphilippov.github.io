gainly

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡeɪnli / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪnli / (BrE)
Graceful and pleasing in appearance or manner; attractive.
graceful and pleasing
archaic

Examples

Her gainly movements on the dance floor captivated the entire audience.
He walked with such a gainly stride that he drew everyone's admiration.
The statue depicted a gainly youth, embodying both strength and elegance.

Synset Examples

gainly conduct
a gainly youth with dark hair and eyes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ungainly - lacking grace; awkward, clumsy

Etymology

From Middle English *gainly* (useful, convenient), from Old Norse *gegn* (straight, suitable).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡeɪnli / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪnli / (BrE)
graceful and attractive in movement or appearance.
graceful; attractive in manner or form
archaic

Examples

The gainly dancer captivated everyone with her grace.
She moved with a gainly elegance across the stage, enchanting the audience.
His gainly posture made him stand out among the other athletes.

Synset Examples

The gainly dancer captivated everyone with her grace.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ungainly - lacking grace; awkward
  • Synonym: graceful - characterized by elegance or beauty of movement or form

Etymology

Originally formed as the opposite of 'ungainly', from 'gain' (archaic, meaning 'fit, direct') + -ly.
