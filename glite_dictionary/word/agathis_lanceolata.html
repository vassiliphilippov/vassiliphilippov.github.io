Agathis lanceolata

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡæθɪs ˌlænsiəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / əˈɡæθɪs ˌlænsiəˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A large tree native to New Caledonia, with glossy leaves and scaly reddish-brown bark, belonging to the family Araucariaceae.
a tree species in the family Araucariaceae

Examples

The forest reserve is home to the majestic Agathis lanceolata, a rare and ancient conifer.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Agathis lanceolata to understand its ecological role.
The glossy leaves of Agathis lanceolata make it easy to distinguish from other native trees.

Etymology

From the genus name Agathis (Greek agathos, 'good'), and the species name lanceolata (Latin for 'lance-shaped', referring to the leaves).
