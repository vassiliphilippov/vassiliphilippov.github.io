feminisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛmɪnəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɛmɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a male develops female physical characteristics, often due to hormonal changes or disorders
development of female traits in males
medical british

Examples

Hormonal imbalance can lead to feminisation in males, such as breast enlargement and reduced body hair.
The doctor explained that feminisation is sometimes a side effect of certain treatments for prostate cancer.
Cases of feminisation may require both medical and psychological support for affected individuals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: masculinisation - the development of male characteristics, often due to hormones or genetic factors

Etymology

From Latin 'fēmina' (woman) + English suffix '-isation', by analogy with similar English formations.
