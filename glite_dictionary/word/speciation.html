speciation

1. NOUN

/ ˌspiːʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspiːsɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the evolutionary process by which new biological species arise from existing ones
formation of new species (biology)

Examples

The concept of speciation is central to understanding biodiversity and the tree of life.
Researchers observed a rapid speciation event in the isolated island population.
Factors such as geographic isolation can accelerate the process of speciation in birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evolution - the process by which different living organisms develop from earlier forms

Etymology

Formed from "species" + "-ation"; modelled after scientific naming conventions.
