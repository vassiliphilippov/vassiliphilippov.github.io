Wyoming

1. PROPN

/ waɪˈoʊmɪŋ / (AmE), / waɪˈəʊmɪŋ / (BrE)
A state in the western United States, known for its mountains and the Great Plains region.
a state in the western United States

Examples

She drove across Wyoming to visit Yellowstone National Park.
The population of Wyoming is the smallest of any U.S. state.
Many people travel to Wyoming for its beautiful natural landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Montana - a neighboring state to the north of Wyoming
  • Synonym: Colorado - a neighboring state to the south of Wyoming

Etymology

Named after the Wyoming Valley in Pennsylvania, from the Munsee Delaware word *xwé:wamənk* meaning 'at the big river flat'.
