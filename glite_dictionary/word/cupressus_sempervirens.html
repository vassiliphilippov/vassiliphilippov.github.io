Cupressus sempervirens

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈprɛsəs sɛmpərˈvaɪrɛnz / (AmE), / kjuːˌprɛsəs sɛmpəˈvaɪəriːnz / (BrE)
a tall Eurasian cypress tree with thin grey bark and ascending branches, widely planted for ornament and timber
Eurasian cypress tree species

Examples

The garden is lined with Cupressus sempervirens, lending a Mediterranean air to the landscape.
Botanists studied the growth rings of ancient Cupressus sempervirens specimens.
A row of Cupressus sempervirens creates an elegant natural border along the driveway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cypress - any coniferous tree of the genus Cupressus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cupressus' (cypress) and 'sempervirens' (evergreen).
