hiccough

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪk.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈhɪk.əp / (BrE)
An involuntary spasm of the diaphragm followed by a sudden closure of the vocal cords, producing a characteristic sound; the condition of experiencing such spasms.
involuntary spasm causing 'hic' sound

Examples

She couldn't stop the hiccough during her speech and felt embarrassed.
After laughing too much, James got a bad case of hiccough.
Doctors say a persistent hiccough can sometimes indicate a more serious condition.

Synset Examples

how do you cure the hiccups?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hiccup - alternate spelling; involuntary spasm of the diaphragm producing a 'hic' sound

Etymology

Variant spelling of "hiccup," possibly imitative of the sound.

2. VERB

/ ˈhɪk.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈhɪk.ʌp / (BrE)
to make a sudden, involuntary spasm of the diaphragm and a characteristic sound in the throat; to hiccup
to make a sudden involuntary hiccup sound

Examples

Every time she laughed too hard, she would suddenly hiccough uncontrollably.
He tried to hold his breath to stop himself from hiccoughing during the meeting.
After drinking cold water too quickly, Mark hiccoughed loudly in front of his friends.

Synset Examples

When you have to hiccup, drink a glass of cold water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hiccup - to make a sudden involuntary spasm of the diaphragm producing a characteristic sound

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'hiccup', influenced by resemblance to 'cough'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00003826-v
Synset POS: v
