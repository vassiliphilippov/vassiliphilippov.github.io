Saccharum munja

1. NOUN

/ ˈsækərəˌm mʊnˈdʒɑː / (AmE), / ˈsakərəm ˈmʌndʒɑː / (BrE)
a tough perennial grass native to Asia, with stems used for making ropes and baskets
Asian grass used for ropes and baskets

Examples

The villagers use Saccharum munja to weave sturdy baskets and mats.
Ropes made from Saccharum munja are valued for their strength and durability.
In some parts of India, Saccharum munja grows abundantly near riverbanks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saccharum - genus of tall grasses including sugarcane species

Etymology

From the scientific Latin binomial; 'Saccharum' is the genus name for sugarcanes, from Latin for 'sugar'.
