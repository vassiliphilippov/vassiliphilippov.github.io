noninheritable

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnɪnˈhɛrətəbəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnɪnˈhɛrɪtəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be inherited or passed down from one generation to another.
not able to be inherited

Examples

The judge ruled that the trust was noninheritable and would end with the current beneficiary.
Some diseases are noninheritable and cannot be passed on to children through genes.
This policy applies only to noninheritable assets, such as certain types of pensions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inheritable - able to be inherited or passed down

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) + 'inheritable' (able to be inherited).
