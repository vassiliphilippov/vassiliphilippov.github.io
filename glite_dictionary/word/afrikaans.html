Afrikaans

1. NOUN

/ ˌæf.rɪˈkɑnz / (AmE), / ˌæf.rɪˈkɑːns / (BrE)
an official language of South Africa, derived from Dutch and closely related to Flemish
official language of South Africa

Examples

She speaks Afrikaans fluently, as she grew up in Cape Town.
Many South African schools offer Afrikaans as a second language.
The novel was originally written in Afrikaans and later translated into English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dutch - West Germanic language from the Netherlands, closely related to Afrikaans

Etymology

From Afrikaans Afrikaans (from Dutch, meaning 'African').

3. NOUN

/ ˌæfrɪˈkɑns / (AmE), / ˌaf.rɪˈkɑːns / (BrE)
an official language of South Africa, derived from Dutch and closely related to Dutch and Flemish
a language spoken in South Africa, related to Dutch

Examples

Many South Africans speak Afrikaans as their first language.
He decided to learn Afrikaans before traveling to Cape Town.
The book has been translated into English, Afrikaans, and Zulu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dutch - the West Germanic language spoken in the Netherlands

Etymology

From Dutch 'Afrikaansch' (African), referring to the African origin of the Dutch spoken in South Africa.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06965623-n
Synset POS: n
