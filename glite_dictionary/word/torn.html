torn

1. ADJ

/ tɔrn / (AmE), / tɔːn / (BrE)
Having edges that are jagged or ragged from being ripped or injured.
jagged or ragged from being ripped

Examples

The child's jeans were torn at the knee after he fell on the pavement.
She covered her torn sleeve with a scarf to hide the damage.
The flag hung by the gate was torn and faded from years of wind and sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ripped - torn apart or split
  • Antonym: intact - undamaged, not torn

Etymology

Past participle of 'tear', from Old English *teran* (to tear).

2. ADJ

/ tɔrn / (AmE), / tɔːn / (BrE)
Experiencing strong emotional conflict due to opposing feelings, desires, or loyalties.
emotionally conflicted; pulled by opposing feelings

Examples

She felt torn between loyalty to her family and her dreams of independence.
He was torn over whether to accept the new job offer or stay with his current employer.
Many voters were torn by the conflicting promises made during the campaign.

Synset Examples

torn between love and hate
torn by conflicting loyalties
torn by religious dissensions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflicted - having opposing feelings or thoughts

Etymology

Past participle of 'tear', from Old English teran (to tear).
