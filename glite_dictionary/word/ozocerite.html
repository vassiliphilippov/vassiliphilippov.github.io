ozocerite

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊzoʊˈsɪraɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊzəˈsɪəraɪt / (BrE)
a naturally occurring waxy mineral composed of a mixture of hydrocarbons, often found with petroleum and used in products such as ceresin and candles
waxy mineral made of hydrocarbons

Examples

The miners extracted ozocerite from the veins to use in candle production.
This region is well known for its deposits of ozocerite, a valuable source of hydrocarbons.
Some types of ozocerite are refined to produce ceresin, which is used in cosmetics.

Etymology

From Greek 'ozein' (to smell) and 'keros' (wax).
