checker

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛkɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkə / (BrE)
a flat, round piece used in the board game of checkers, moved diagonally across the board
piece used in checkers game

Examples

He picked up a red checker and moved it to the other side of the board.
All the checkers were stacked neatly before the game began.
If you lose a checker, you won't be able to play properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piece - an object used in a board game
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03015009-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈtʃɛkər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkə / (BrE)
to mark or decorate with a pattern of squares or crossed lines
mark or decorate with squares

Examples

The artist decided to checker the floor with alternating black and white tiles.
Her scarf was checkered in red and blue, giving it a vibrant appearance.
He likes to checker his notebooks with crossed lines to help organize his notes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chequer - to mark or decorate with a pattern of squares or crossed lines (British spelling)

Etymology

From noun 'checker', itself from late Middle English 'cheker' from Old French 'eschequier', meaning 'chessboard'.

5. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛkər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkə / (BrE)
an attendant whose job is to receive and look after coats or baggage for customers in public places such as theaters, restaurants, or hotels
person who checks coats or baggage

Examples

The checker at the theater quickly took my coat and handed me a ticket.
She tipped the checker after retrieving her suitcase from the luggage counter.
Ask the checker at the entrance if there is a fee for coat storage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloakroom attendant - person employed to look after coats and other belongings in a cloakroom

Etymology

From the verb 'check' (to examine, inspect, or verify).
