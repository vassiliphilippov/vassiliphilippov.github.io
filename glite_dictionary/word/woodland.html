woodland

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.lənd / (BrE)
land covered with trees and shrubs, especially smaller trees and undergrowth, as opposed to dense forest
land with trees and shrubs

Examples

Several species of deer live in the woodland behind the village.
The hike took us through dense woodland and along the riverbank.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting native woodland habitats from development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forest - a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth

Etymology

Old English wuduland ('wudu' meaning 'wood' + 'land')
