Pinus contorta

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪnəs kənˈtɔrtə / (AmE), / ˈpaɪnəs kɒnˈtɔːtə / (BrE)
A species of pine tree native to the coastal northwestern United States, characterized by its shrubby form, two needles per bundle, and red to yellow-brown bark fissured into small squares.
a shrubby pine tree of northwestern North America

Examples

The twisted branches of the Pinus contorta made the forest appear wild and ancient.
In coastal regions, Pinus contorta is an important species for stabilizing sandy soils.
The reddish bark of the Pinus contorta easily distinguishes it from other local pine trees.

Etymology

From Latin: 'pinus' meaning pine, and 'contorta' meaning twisted, referring to its irregular growth form.
