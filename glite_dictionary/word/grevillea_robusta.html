Grevillea robusta

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɛˈvɪliə roʊˈbʌstə / (AmE), / ɡrɛˈvɪliə rɒˈbʌstə / (BrE)
a medium to tall fast-growing tree with orange flowers and feathery, silky-hairy leaves beneath, native to eastern Australia; also known as the silk oak or silver oak
Australian tree with orange flowers, silk oak

Examples

The Grevillea robusta is a popular ornamental tree in parks and gardens around the world.
Birds are attracted to the bright orange flowers of the Grevillea robusta during spring.
The timber from Grevillea robusta is valued for its attractive grain and workability.

Etymology

Genus name from Charles Francis Greville (1749–1809), Scottish botanist; species from Latin 'robustus' (strong, robust).
