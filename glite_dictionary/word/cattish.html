cattish

1. ADJ

/ ˈkætɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkatɪʃ / (BrE)
Displaying sly or spiteful malice, especially in conversation or remarks.
spitefully malicious, sly

Examples

Her cattish comments made the meeting uncomfortable for everyone present.
He gave me a cattish smile, full of mischief and unspoken criticism.
The article was filled with cattish remarks about the celebrity's fashion choices.

Synset Examples

a catty remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catty - spiteful or malicious, especially in remarks

Etymology

Formed from 'cat' + '-ish', modeled after 'catty' in the sense of spiteful.
