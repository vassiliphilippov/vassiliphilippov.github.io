Stayman

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪ.mən / (AmE), / ˈsteɪ.mən / (BrE)
a variety of apple, grown chiefly in the Shenandoah Valley and known for its tart flavor and firm flesh
variety of apple

Examples

She bought a bag of Stayman apples at the local farmer’s market.
Bakers often prefer Stayman apples for pies due to their tartness.
The orchard specializes in heirloom varieties like Stayman and Winesap.

Etymology

Named after Joseph Stayman, who developed the cultivar in the 19th century.
