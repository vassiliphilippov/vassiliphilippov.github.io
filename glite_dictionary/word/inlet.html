inlet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌlɛt / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌlɛt / (BrE)
an opening through which a fluid enters a tube, container, or machine
opening for fluid entry

Examples

The fuel inlet is located on the left side of the engine.
Check for debris around the water inlet to prevent clogging.
He accidentally blocked the air inlet, causing the device to overheat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outlet - an opening through which fluid exits a container or tube

Etymology

From the verb 'let in'; influenced by the prefix 'in-' and 'let' (to allow to pass).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌlɛt / (AmE), / ˈɪnlɛt / (BrE)
A narrow arm of the sea, lake, or river that leads inland from a larger body of water, often situated between rocky headlands.
a narrow arm of water leading inland

Examples

The boat anchored in the sheltered inlet surrounded by rocky cliffs.
They explored a narrow inlet that reached deep into the forested coast.
Storms often deposit driftwood along the edges of the inlet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bay - a broad inlet of the sea where the land curves inward
  • Antonym: outlet - a point where water flows out of a lake, river, or other body of water

Etymology

From Middle English 'inleten', from 'in' + 'let', meaning a place where water enters.
