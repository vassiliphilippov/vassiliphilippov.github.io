Crotophaga

1. NOUN

/ kroʊtəˈfeɪɡə / (AmE), / krəʊtəˈfɑːɡə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American birds in the cuckoo family, commonly known as anis, recognized by their grooved bills and social behavior.
genus of tropical American birds (anis)

Examples

The Crotophaga genus includes birds commonly referred to as anis.
Members of the Crotophaga genus are known for their communal nesting habits.
Ornithologists often study Crotophaga species to learn more about social behavior in birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ani - a bird of the genus Crotophaga, found in the Americas

Etymology

From Greek krotōn (tick) + phaga (eating), referring to early beliefs about their diet.
