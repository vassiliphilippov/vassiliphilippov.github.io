strained

1. ADJ

/ streɪnd / (AmE), / streɪnd / (BrE)
not natural or spontaneous; showing signs of effort, tension, or artificiality
showing effort or artificiality

Examples

Her smile looked a bit strained after hours of interviews.
The film's humor felt strained and failed to make the audience laugh.
He adopted a strained posture, as if trying too hard to impress the crowd.

Synset Examples

agonistic poses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forced - produced or done with effort and not natural or spontaneous

Etymology

Past participle of 'strain', from Old French 'estreindre', from Latin 'stringere' (to draw tight).
