invincibly

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈvɪnsəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪnsəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be defeated, overcome, or conquered.
in an invincible way

Examples

She remained invincibly optimistic despite the challenges she faced.
The team pressed forward invincibly, never doubting their victory.
He argued his case invincibly, leaving no room for counterarguments.

Synset Examples

invincibly, the troops moved forward

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invincible - impossible to defeat, overcome, or conquer
  • Antonym: vulnerably - in a way that is easily hurt or attacked

Etymology

From Latin *invincibilis* (unconquerable) + English adverbial suffix -ly.
