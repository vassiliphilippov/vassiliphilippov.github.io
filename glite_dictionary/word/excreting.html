excreting

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˈskriːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɛkˈskriːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which waste matter is discharged from the body
bodily process of discharging waste
medical

Examples

The kidneys play a major role in excreting harmful substances from the bloodstream.
Problems with excreting wastes can lead to serious health conditions.
Modern research focuses on how the body manages excreting different types of metabolic by-products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excretion - the process of eliminating waste from the body

Etymology

From Latin 'excretus', past participle of 'excernere' (to sift out, discharge).
