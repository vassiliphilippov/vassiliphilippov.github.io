comeback

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈkʌmbæk / (BrE)
a quick, witty, or critical reply to a remark or question
witty or critical reply

Examples

She always has a brilliant comeback whenever someone tries to insult her.
His sarcastic comeback made everyone at the meeting laugh.
After being teased, he fired back with a clever comeback.

Synset Examples

it brought a sharp rejoinder from the teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rejoinder - a quick and sharp response, especially a witty one
  • Synonym: retort - a sharp, incisive, or witty reply

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈkʌmbæk / (BrE)
a return by a celebrity or public figure to a formerly successful career or activity after a period of absence or decline
return to former success (celebrity, public figure)

Examples

The singer made a remarkable comeback after several years out of the spotlight.
Fans were delighted by the actor's unexpected comeback in the hit television series.
Her comeback was celebrated as one of the greatest in sports history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: return - the act of coming back to a former place, person, or activity
  • Antonym: retirement - withdrawal from one's position or occupation

Synset POS: n
