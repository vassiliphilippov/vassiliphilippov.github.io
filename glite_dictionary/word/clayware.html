clayware

1. NOUN

/ ˈkleɪˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈkleɪˌweə / (BrE)
ceramic objects made from clay and hardened by baking in a kiln
ceramic items made of baked clay

Examples

The museum displayed ancient clayware unearthed from archaeological sites.
Many artists prefer to use local materials when crafting their clayware.
The shelves were lined with beautifully painted clayware bowls and vases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoneware - ceramic ware made from a specific type of clay and fired at high temperatures
  • Synonym: porcelain - a white, fine-grained ceramic ware, often translucent
