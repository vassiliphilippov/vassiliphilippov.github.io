Anomalopteryx

1. NOUN

/ əˌnɑːməˌlɒpˈtɛrɪks / (AmE), / əˌnɒməˌlɒpˈtɛrɪks / (BrE)
a genus of small, extinct, flightless birds (moas) native to New Zealand.
a genus of extinct small New Zealand moas

Examples

The fossil remains of Anomalopteryx have been found in several regions of New Zealand.
Scientists believe Anomalopteryx became extinct due to overhunting by humans.
The Anomalopteryx was smaller than other members of the moa family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moa - an extinct flightless bird from New Zealand

Etymology

From Greek *anomalos* (irregular, unusual) + *pteryx* (wing), reflecting the bird's distinctive features.
