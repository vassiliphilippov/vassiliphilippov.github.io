sadist

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.dɪst / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.dɪst / (BrE)
a person who derives pleasure, especially sexual pleasure, from inflicting pain or suffering on others
person who enjoys inflicting pain

Examples

The cruel sadist took delight in others' discomfort.
Some horror villains are portrayed as complete sadists who enjoy their victims' pain.
His reputation as a sadist made people fear working for him.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: masochist - a person who derives pleasure from suffering or humiliation

Etymology

From French sadisme, named after Marquis de Sade (1740–1814), whose writings depicted such behavior.
