leisurely

1. ADV

/ ˈliʒɚli / (AmE), / ˈlɛʒəli / (BrE)
in an unhurried and relaxed way; without haste
in a relaxed, unhurried manner

Examples

He walked leisurely through the park, enjoying the warm afternoon sun.
They browsed leisurely around the bookshop, stopping to read the blurbs.
Please take all the time you need and work leisurely on this project.

Synset Examples

read the manual at your leisure
he traveled leisurely

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hurriedly - in a hurried or rushed way

Etymology

From leisure + -ly, formed in English to mean 'in the manner of leisure'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈliːʒərli / (AmE), / ˈlɛʒəli / (BrE)
doing something in a relaxed, unhurried way, without rushing.
unhurried; relaxed in pace or manner

Examples

They enjoyed a leisurely stroll through the park on Sunday afternoon.
She prepared dinner at a leisurely pace, with no need to hurry.
The conference ended early, allowing for some leisurely sightseeing.

Synset Examples

an easy walk around the block
at a leisurely (or easygoing) pace

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hurried - done with excessive speed or urgency
  • Synonym: relaxed - not tense or anxious; at ease

Etymology

From 'leisure' (Old French *leisire*), meaning free time or ease, plus the suffix '-ly'.

Synset POS: s
