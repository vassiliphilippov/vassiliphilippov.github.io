Rheum cultorum

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːəm kʌlˈtɔːrəm / (AmE), / ˈruːəm kʌlˈtɔːrəm / (BrE)
A perennial hybrid plant of the rhubarb family, widely cultivated for its edible stems used in pies, sauces, or preserves.
a cultivated hybrid of rhubarb

Examples

The garden's Rheum cultorum produced abundant stems that were perfect for making homemade jam.
Many recipes recommend using Rheum cultorum for its tart flavor in pies.
He grows Rheum cultorum both for its ornamental value and its culinary uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhubarb - a large-leaved plant with edible stems, commonly used in cooking

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Rheum' (rhubarb) and Latin 'cultorum' (of cultivation).
