desoxyribonucleic acid

1. NOUN

/ diˌsɑksiˌraɪboʊnjuˈkliɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / diˌsɒksɪˌraɪbəʊnjuːˈkliːɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
A complex molecule in the cell nucleus, consisting of long chains of nucleotides arranged in a double helix, that carries genetic information.
double-helix molecule carrying genetic information
medical

Examples

The structure of desoxyribonucleic acid was discovered in the 1950s.
Modern genetics is based on the understanding of desoxyribonucleic acid sequences.
Mutations in desoxyribonucleic acid can lead to various genetic disorders.

Synset Examples

DNA is the king of molecules

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deoxyribonucleic acid - a more common spelling for the same molecule (DNA)
  • Antonym: ribonucleic acid - a different but related nucleic acid (RNA) involved in protein synthesis and gene expression

Etymology

From German Desoxyribonukleinsäure, from desoxy- (without oxygen) + ribonucleic acid.
