Johannes Brahms

1. PROPN

/ joʊˈhɑːnɪs ˈbrɑmz / (AmE), / jəʊˈhanɪs ˈbrɑːmz / (BrE)
(1833–1897) A German composer and pianist renowned for developing the Romantic style in both lyrical and classical music.
German Romantic-era composer

Examples

Johannes Brahms is celebrated for his symphonies and chamber works, which remain staples in concert halls worldwide.
Many pianists look to Johannes Brahms's compositions to challenge their technical and expressive skills.
Johannes Brahms was often compared to Beethoven for his mastery of form and depth of feeling.
