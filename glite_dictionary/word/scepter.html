scepter

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛptər / (AmE), / ˈsɛptə / (BrE)
Imperial authority or sovereignty as symbolized by a scepter.
imperial authority symbolized by a scepter

Examples

The young prince dreamed of wielding the scepter one day, ruling over his father's vast kingdom.
When the queen abdicated, she relinquished both the throne and the scepter of her ancestors.
Throughout history, the scepter has been a powerful emblem of imperial rule.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throne - the power and dignity of a sovereign

Etymology

From Latin 'sceptrum', from Greek 'skeptron' (staff, scepter).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛp.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛp.tə / (BrE)
A staff held by a sovereign as an emblem of authority, especially during formal ceremonies.
ceremonial staff symbolizing authority

Examples

The queen entered the hall carrying a golden scepter decorated with jewels.
During the coronation, the king was presented with a scepter as a symbol of his power.
Ancient rulers often held a scepter to demonstrate their authority to the people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staff - a long stick carried in the hand for support or authority
  • Synonym: crown - a ceremonial headdress representing sovereignty or authority

Etymology

From Old French *sceptre*, from Latin *sceptrum* (staff), from Greek *skeptron* (staff, baton).
