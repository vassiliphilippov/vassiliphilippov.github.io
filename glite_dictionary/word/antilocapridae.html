Antilocapridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntɪloʊˈkæprɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌæntɪləʊˈkæprɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of mammals comprising only the pronghorns, native to North America
mammal family including pronghorns

Examples

The Antilocapridae are native to North America and include only the pronghorn species.
Scientists have studied the evolutionary history of the Antilocapridae for decades.
Unlike deer, members of the Antilocapridae family shed their horns annually.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Antilocapra' (type genus) + '-idae' (family suffix in zoology).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tɪ.loʊˈkæ.pɹɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.ləʊˈkæ.prɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of mammals comprising only the pronghorns, native to North America.
zoological family of pronghorns

Examples

The Antilocapridae are represented today by a single living species, the pronghorn.
Paleontologists have found fossils from several extinct genera within the Antilocapridae family.
The Antilocapridae are unique to North America and differ from true antelopes.

Etymology

From scientific Latin, from genus name Antilocapra (pronghorn) and -idae (zoological family suffix).
