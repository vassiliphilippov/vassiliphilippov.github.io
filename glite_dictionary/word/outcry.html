outcry

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌkraɪ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.kraɪ / (BrE)
a strong and loud expression of protest or anger, often from a group of people
loud protest or public anger

Examples

There was an immediate outcry when the company announced the closure of the local factory.
The government's decision sparked a nationwide outcry from citizens and activists alike.
Public outcry forced the council to reconsider its plans to cut funding for the arts.

Synset Examples

the speaker was interrupted by loud cries from the rear of the audience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uproar - a state of noisy confusion or protest
  • Synonym: clamor - a loud and confused noise, especially from a crowd

Etymology

From Middle English 'outecrie', from out + cry.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌkraɪ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.kraɪ / (BrE)
A strong and public expression of protest, disapproval, or anger, often involving shouting or vocal objection.
public protest or strong vocal objection

Examples

The new policy sparked an immediate outcry from local residents.
After the verdict was announced, there was a national outcry against the decision.
Many voices joined the outcry for better working conditions.

Synset Examples

Her outcry during the meeting surprised everyone present.
There was an outcry from citizens demanding justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uproar - loud public expression of disapproval or protest
  • Synonym: protest - statement or action expressing disapproval or dissent
  • Antonym: applause - expression of approval, especially by clapping

Etymology

From Middle English outcrie, from out (outside) + cry.
