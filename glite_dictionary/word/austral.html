austral

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔstrəl / (AmE), / ˈɔːstrəl / (BrE)
of or relating to the south; southern or coming from the south.
southern; relating to the south

Examples

The explorers navigated the austral regions during their journey.
Many unique species are found in the austral forests of South America.
The ship sailed into austral waters as it traveled toward Antarctica.

Synset Examples

sailed the austral seas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northern - of or relating to the north; opposite of 'austral'

Etymology

From Latin 'australis' (southern), from 'auster' (south wind).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊstrəl / (AmE), / ˈaʊstrəl / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in Argentina, equal to 100 centavos, used from 1985 to 1991
former currency unit of Argentina

Examples

During the 1980s, prices in Argentina were calculated in australs before the peso replaced it.
He found an old coin collection that included several australs from Argentina.
The austral depreciated rapidly due to hyperinflation before being replaced in 1992.

Etymology

From Spanish 'austral', meaning 'southern', referring to Argentina's location in the Southern Hemisphere.
