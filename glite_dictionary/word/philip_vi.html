Philip VI

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɪlɪp sɪksθ / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪp sɪksθ / (BrE)
(1293–1350) King of France who was the first monarch of the Valois dynasty; his dispute with Edward III over succession triggered the Hundred Years' War.
King of France, first Valois dynasty monarch

Examples

Philip VI became king after the death of Charles IV, ending the direct Capetian line.
The reign of Philip VI marked the beginning of the Valois dynasty in France.
Relations between England and France deteriorated under Philip VI, sparking the Hundred Years' War.
