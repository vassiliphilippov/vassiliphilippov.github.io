Melanesia

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɛləˈniʒə / (AmE), / ˌmɛləˈniːziə / (BrE)
a region in the southwestern Pacific Ocean comprising numerous islands, including New Guinea, Fiji, the Solomon Islands, and Vanuatu
region of islands in southwestern Oceania

Examples

Many anthropologists study the diverse cultures found throughout Melanesia.
The geography of Melanesia includes volcanic islands and coral atolls.
She traveled to Melanesia to conduct research on local languages.

Etymology

From Greek 'melas' (black) + 'nēsos' (island), referring to the dark skin of many of its inhabitants.
