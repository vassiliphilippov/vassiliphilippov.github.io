eagerness

1. NOUN

/ ˈiɡərnəs / (AmE), / ˈiːɡənəs / (BrE)
A strong and enthusiastic desire to do or have something.
keen desire or enthusiasm

Examples

She accepted the invitation with such eagerness that it made everyone smile.
His eagerness to learn new skills impressed the entire team.
The children's eagerness for the holiday was obvious in their excited chatter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment or interest
  • Antonym: reluctance - unwillingness or lack of enthusiasm

Etymology

From Middle English 'egerenes', based on 'eager' from Old French 'eigre', from Latin 'acer' meaning 'sharp, keen'.
