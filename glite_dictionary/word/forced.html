forced

1. ADJ

/ fɔrst / (AmE), / fɔːst / (BrE)
(of a process or substance) produced by the application of external force rather than occurring naturally or spontaneously.
produced by external force

Examples

The laboratory used forced ventilation to maintain air quality during experiments.
A forced circulation system moves water through the heating pipes more efficiently.
The technician explained how forced convection improves heat transfer in the device.

Synset Examples

forced-air heating
furnaces of the forced-convection type
forced convection in plasma generators

Related Terms

  • Antonym: natural - existing or occurring without external force

2. ADJ

/ fɔrst / (AmE), / fɔːst / (BrE)
Done because of force or compulsion; not voluntary.
compelled; not voluntary

Examples

The prisoners were subjected to forced labor for many years.
She gave a forced smile, trying to hide her disappointment.
The company made a forced decision to lay off several workers due to financial trouble.

Synset Examples

promised to abolish forced labor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: voluntary - done by free choice, not compelled
  • Synonym: coerced - compelled by force or threats
Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ fɔrst / (AmE), / fɔːst / (BrE)
(of an action or event) made necessary by unexpected circumstances or an emergency, rather than being voluntary or planned.
made necessary by circumstances or emergency

Examples

Due to engine failure, the pilot executed a forced landing in a nearby field.
The company was put up for a forced sale after declaring bankruptcy.
Her early retirement was a forced decision following the unexpected restructuring of the department.

Synset Examples

a forced landing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unplanned - not planned in advance; spontaneous or not foreseen
  • Antonym: voluntary - done by choice or free will, not forced or required
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02376045-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ fɔrst / (AmE), / fɔːst / (BrE)
(of an action or expression) done with effort and not naturally or willingly; lacking in spontaneity.
not natural or spontaneous

Examples

Her forced smile made it clear she was uncomfortable at the party.
His laughter sounded forced and did not convince anyone in the room.
Despite her forced enthusiasm, everyone sensed her lack of true interest.

Synset Examples

a constrained smile
forced heartiness
a strained smile

Related Terms

  • Antonym: natural - done in a spontaneous or genuine way
  • Synonym: constrained - appearing stiff or forced rather than relaxed and natural
  • Synonym: strained - showing signs of effort and not relaxed or genuine

Etymology

Past participle of 'force,' ultimately from Latin 'fortis' (strong) via Old French 'forcer.'
