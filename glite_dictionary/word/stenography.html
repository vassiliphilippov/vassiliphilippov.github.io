stenography

1. NOUN

/ stəˈnɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / stɪˈnɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the practice or process of writing in shorthand, especially for the purpose of rapid transcription
writing in shorthand

Examples

She learned stenography so she could work as a court reporter.
Modern technology has reduced the demand for stenography in many offices.
During the meeting, he relied on his skills in stenography to keep accurate records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shorthand - method of rapid writing using symbols or abbreviations

Etymology

From Greek stenos (narrow) + -graphia (writing).

2. NOUN

/ stəˈnɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / stəˈnɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the practice or process of writing in shorthand or a symbolic system in order to record speech quickly
rapid writing in shorthand

Examples

She learned stenography to work as a court reporter.
Advances in technology have reduced the demand for traditional stenography.
The class offered training in stenography for aspiring administrative assistants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shorthand - a method of rapid writing using symbols or abbreviations

Etymology

From Greek 'stenos' (narrow) + 'graphia' (writing).
