accession

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / əkˈsɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
an item that is added to a collection, especially in a museum, library, or archive.
item newly added to a collection

Examples

The library acquired several rare books as new accessions this year.
All accession records must be carefully documented for the museum's archive.
The latest accession to his art collection was a 19th-century landscape painting.

Synset Examples

the librarian shelved the new accessions
he was a new addition to the staff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addition - something that has been added

Etymology

From Latin accessio (an approach, addition).

3. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / əkˈsɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(Law) The right by which the owner of property is entitled to all that it produces, or to anything added to it, whether by growth, improvement, or increase.
legal right to what property produces or gains
legal

Examples

Under the principle of accession, any increase in land by natural causes belongs to the owner of the original property.
If you own a tree, the fruit it bears is yours by right of accession.
The law of accession grants ownership of improvements made by tenants to the landlord upon lease termination.

Etymology

From Latin *accessio* (an addition), from *accedere* (to approach).
