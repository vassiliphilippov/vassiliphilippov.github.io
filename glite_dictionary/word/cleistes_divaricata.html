Cleistes divaricata

1. PROPN

/ kliˈaɪstiːz dɪˌvɛrɪˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / kliˈiːstiːz dɪˌvarɪˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
An orchid native to the northeastern United States, noted for its magenta-pink flowers with a funnel-shaped lip; sometimes classified within the genus Pogonia.
a species of orchid with magenta-pink flowers

Examples

The rare Cleistes divaricata blooms each spring in the wetlands of New England.
Botanists debated whether Cleistes divaricata should be retained in its genus or moved to Pogonia.
We spotted a patch of Cleistes divaricata along the shaded trails during our hike.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Cleistes' is a genus name, and 'divaricata' means 'spread apart' or 'divergent'.
