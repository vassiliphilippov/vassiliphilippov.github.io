Cunoniaceae

1. NOUN

/ kuːˌnoʊniˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / kjuːˌnəʊniˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of trees, shrubs, or climbers, mainly native to the Southern Hemisphere, belonging to the order Oxalidales.
family of mainly Southern Hemisphere plants

Examples

The Cunoniaceae family includes a variety of flowering plants found in tropical regions.
Botanists have identified several new species within the Cunoniaceae.
Some members of the Cunoniaceae are cultivated for their ornamental value.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the genus Cunonia + -aceae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ kuːˌnoʊniˈeɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / kjuːˌnəʊniˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of mostly Southern Hemisphere trees, shrubs, or climbers in the order Oxalidales
botanical family of Southern Hemisphere plants

Examples

The Cunoniaceae include both trees and shrubs commonly found in New Guinea and Australia.
Botanists have identified over 22 genera within the Cunoniaceae family.
Several members of the Cunoniaceae are valued as ornamental garden plants.

Etymology

From the type genus Cunonia, which was named after John de la Cunon, an 18th-century patron of botany, plus the standard botanical suffix -aceae.
