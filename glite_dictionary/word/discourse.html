discourse

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪs.kɔɹs / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.kɔːs / (BrE)
written or spoken communication or debate, especially on a particular topic or subject
extended communication on a topic

Examples

Academic discourse encourages critical thinking and debate among students.
The politician's discourse on economic policy was widely discussed in the media.
Their dinner table discourse ranged from art to current events.

Synset Examples

the book contains an excellent discussion of modal logic
his treatment of the race question is badly biased

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discussion - the act of talking about something with others
  • Synonym: dialogue - conversation between two or more people

Etymology

From Latin 'discursus' (running to and fro, conversation), from 'discurrere' (to run about).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪsˌkɔrs / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.kɔːs / (BrE)
written or spoken communication or debate, especially one that is formal or extended.
formal written or spoken communication

Examples

The professor gave an insightful discourse on the evolution of social norms.
Political discourse often shapes public opinion in significant ways.
Her books are known for their critical discourse on gender and society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialogue - conversation between two or more people as a feature of literature or drama
  • Synonym: conversation - informal talk between two or more people, in which news and ideas are exchanged
  • Antonym: monologue - a long speech by one person during a conversation

Etymology

From Latin 'discursus' (a running to and fro, conversation) via Old French 'discours'.

WordNet ID: oewn-06295430-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈdɪsˌkɔrs / (AmE), / ˈdɪsˌkɔːs / (BrE)
to speak or write authoritatively about a topic, especially in an extended or formal way; to engage in serious conversation or discussion.
speak or write formally; discuss

Examples

The professor began to discourse on the consequences of climate change.
They discoursed at length about their plans for the future.
She enjoyed discoursing with her colleagues on philosophical topics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: converse - to engage in conversation
  • Synonym: to discuss - to talk about something with others

From Latin 'discursus' (a running to and fro, conversation).
