chaotic

1. ADJ

/ keɪˈɑːdɪk / (AmE), / keɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
(of a system or process) highly sensitive to initial conditions, leading to unpredictable or seemingly random behavior, as described in chaos theory.
relating to chaos theory or sensitive initial conditions

Examples

The weather system is inherently chaotic and extremely difficult to predict accurately.
In mathematics, a chaotic system is one in which small changes in conditions lead to drastically different outcomes.
The double pendulum is a classic example of a chaotic system studied in physics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: random - having no definite pattern or plan

Etymology

From Greek 'khaos' (abyss) via Latin 'chaoticus'.

2. ADJ

/ keɪˈɑtɪk / (AmE), / keɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
in a state of complete disorder and confusion; lacking any order or predictability
in a state of disorder or confusion

Examples

The room was chaotic after the party, with clothes and garbage everywhere.
The traffic became chaotic after the accident blocked two lanes.
Her schedule is so chaotic that she barely has time to relax.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orderly - arranged or organized in a neat, methodical way
  • Synonym: disordered - lacking organization or order

Etymology

From Greek khaotikos, from khaos (chaos).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02399137-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ keɪˈɑtɪk / (AmE), / keɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
lacking any order, organization, or control; extremely disordered or confused
extremely disordered or confused

Examples

Her desk was so chaotic that she could never find anything she needed.
After the power outage, the city became chaotic as traffic lights stopped working.
The children's birthday party was fun but incredibly chaotic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orderly - neatly organized or arranged
  • Synonym: disorganized - not properly arranged or managed
  • Synonym: confused - lacking clear direction or order

Etymology

From Greek *khaotikos* (of chaos), from *khaos* (vast chasm, void).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01673760-s
Synset POS: s
