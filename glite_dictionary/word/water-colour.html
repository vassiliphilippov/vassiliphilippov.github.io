water-colour

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚˌkʌlɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌkʌlə / (BrE)
the art or technique of painting using water-based pigments on paper or other absorbent surfaces
art of painting with watercolors
british

Examples

She enjoys studying the techniques of water-colour in her art class.
The museum will display several impressive water-colour paintings by local artists.
Learning water-colour requires patience and a steady hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watercolor - the art or technique of painting with watercolors (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'water' + 'colour', indicating painting with pigments suspended in water.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚˌkʌlɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌkʌlə / (BrE)
A paint in which the pigment is suspended in water and used especially for creating translucent artistic effects.
water-based paint; pigment in water
british

Examples

She painted the landscape with delicate water-colours on textured paper.
The artist prefers water-colour because of its subtle, transparent quality.
A set of water-colours was included in the children's art kit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watercolor - water-based paint (American spelling)
  • Degree Higher: gouache - opaque water-based paint

Etymology

Formed from 'water' + 'colour' (British spelling), referring to pigments that are dissolved or diluted in water.
