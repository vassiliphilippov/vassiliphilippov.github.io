auspiciousness

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈspɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈspɪʃəsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of indicating or promising success; the favorable nature of conditions or signs.
quality of promising success

Examples

The wedding date was chosen for its auspiciousness, believed to bring happiness to the couple.
Many cultures attribute auspiciousness to certain colors or symbols during festivals.
The auspiciousness of the weather gave the organizers hope for a successful event.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inauspiciousness - the quality of predicting or suggesting bad luck or failure
  • Synonym: favorableness - the state or quality of being favorable

Etymology

From Latin 'auspicium' (divination by birds) + English suffix '-ness'.
