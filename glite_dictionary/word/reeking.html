reeking

1. ADJ

/ ˈriːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈriːkɪŋ / (BrE)
giving off a strong, unpleasant smell
strongly smelling; foul-smelling

Examples

The abandoned house was reeking of mold and decay.
He entered the room, reeking of cigarette smoke.
The trash bins were reeking after the hot summer weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malodorous - having an unpleasant smell
  • Antonym: fragrant - having a pleasant, sweet smell
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ ˈriːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈriːkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a surface) wet with secreted or exuded moisture, such as sweat or tears.
damp or wet from moisture (esp. sweat, tears)

Examples

After his run in the summer heat, his shirt was reeking with sweat.
She dabbed her reeking eyes with a tissue after the emotional movie.
The workers' reeking faces glistened under the harsh factory lights.

Synset Examples

wiped his reeking neck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweaty - covered with or smelling of sweat
  • Synonym: damp - slightly wet, often unpleasantly so
  • Antonym: dry - free from moisture or liquid

Etymology

Present participle of 'reek', meaning to emit moisture or vapor; from Old English 'rēocan'.
