barrator

1. NOUN

/ ˈbærətər / (AmE), / ˈbærətə / (BrE)
A person who is guilty of barratry, especially one who persistently instigates lawsuits.
person guilty of barratry
legal

Examples

The lawyer was accused of being a barrator for filing frivolous lawsuits.
Medieval courts would often penalize a barrator for stirring up unnecessary disputes.
As a notorious barrator, he had a reputation for constantly engaging in vexatious litigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barratry - the offense of persistently instigating lawsuits

Etymology

From Old French 'barateur' (cheat, swindler), from 'barater' (to cheat, deceive).
