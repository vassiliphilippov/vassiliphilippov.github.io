skittle

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈskɪt.əl / (BrE)
a small wooden pin used as a target in the game of ninepins or skittles, especially in England
wooden pin used in skittles or ninepins
british

Examples

He knocked down every skittle on his first try and cheered loudly.
In traditional pubs, customers sometimes play a game of skittle in the alley.
The wooden skittle tumbled over as the ball struck it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowling pin - a bottle-shaped object toppled by a bowling ball

Etymology

Possibly from Old English 'scyttel' (a bar, bolt), related to Middle Low German 'schüttel' (pin).

2. VERB

/ ˈskɪt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈskɪt.əl / (BrE)
to play the game of skittles, a traditional bowling game in which players try to knock down pins by rolling a ball at them
to play the game of skittles

Examples

Every Friday night, the group would skittle at the village pub.
She learned to skittle when visiting her grandparents in the countryside.
Teams from neighboring towns come together annually to skittle in the summer league.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowl - to play a game similar to skittles, typically bowling balls to knock down pins

Etymology

From Middle English *skyttel*, probably of Scandinavian origin (cf. Old Norse *skyttr* meaning 'shooter, bowman').

british

Examples

On rainy days, the children would often play skittle in the hall.
The pub had a special alley for skittle competitions every weekend.
We decided to organize a skittle tournament for the village fair.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowling - an indoor game in which players roll a ball to knock down pins

Probably from Middle English 'skyttel' (a skittle, pin), of Scandinavian origin; related to Old Norse 'skytja' (to shoot).
