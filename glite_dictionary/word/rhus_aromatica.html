Rhus aromatica

1. NOUN

/ ɹʌs ɛəɹəˈmætɪkə / (AmE), / ɹʊs əːrəˈmatɪkə / (BrE)
A species of sumac native to eastern North America, with aromatic leaves, yellowish-green flowers in catkin-like spikes, and red hairy fruits.
a sweet-scented North American sumac

Examples

The leaves of Rhus aromatica give off a pleasant scent when crushed.
Gardeners often plant Rhus aromatica for its colourful foliage and fragrant flowers.
Birds are attracted to the red, hairy fruits produced by Rhus aromatica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sumac - any shrub or small tree of the genus Rhus

Etymology

From Latin 'Rhus' (sumac) and 'aromatica' (aromatic), referring to the fragrance of the plant.
