castor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæstər / (AmE), / ˈkɑːstə / (BrE)
A small container with a perforated top, used for sprinkling powdered sugar or other fine substances.
shaker for powdered sugar

Examples

She filled the castor with powdered sugar before setting the table.
The ornate silver castor added elegance to the dessert course.
Please pass the castor so I can dust my pancakes with sugar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaker - a container with holes in the top for sprinkling substances

Etymology

From Middle English castour, ultimately from the verb 'to cast' (to throw or sprinkle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.tər / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.stər / (BrE)
a hat made from beaver fur or similar material, often worn in the 18th and 19th centuries
beaver-fur hat

Examples

He wore his finest castor to the evening gala.
The beaver-fur castor was a symbol of wealth and status in the 19th century.
Antique dealers sometimes sell restored castors from the Victorian era.

Etymology

From Latin castor (beaver), referring to the animal whose fur was used.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.stə / (BrE)
a small swiveling wheel fixed to the bottom of furniture, equipment, or machinery to make it easier to move
swiveling wheel on furniture or equipment

Examples

The new office chairs glide smoothly thanks to the castors attached to their legs.
We had to replace a broken castor on the heavy filing cabinet.
The hospital bed is equipped with locking castors for safety and mobility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheel - a circular object that revolves on an axle

Etymology

From Latin 'castor', possibly influenced by French 'castor' (beaver), referring to the rolling movement, though the exact origin of its use for wheels is unclear.
