Stradivari

1. PROPN

/ strædəˈvɛəri / (AmE), / stradɪˈvɑːri / (BrE)
Antonio Stradivari (c. 1644–1737), Italian violin maker renowned for developing the modern violin and crafting instruments of exceptional tonal quality.
famous Italian violin maker (1644–1737)

Examples

Many musicians dream of playing a Stradivari violin at least once in their lifetime.
The rare Stradivari was auctioned for millions due to its superb craftsmanship.
Antonio Stradivari's instruments are considered some of the finest ever made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stradivarius - a violin or string instrument made by Antonio Stradivari

Etymology

Italian surname; from Antonio Stradivari, celebrated violin maker of Cremona.
