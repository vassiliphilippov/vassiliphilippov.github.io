lamb's-quarters

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmz ˌkwɔrtərz / (AmE), / ˈlæmz ˌkwɔːtəz / (BrE)
A common weedy plant (Chenopodium album), native to Europe and widely naturalized in North America, often used as a potherb.
common edible weed, Chenopodium album

Examples

Many gardeners consider lamb's-quarters to be a weed, but it is actually edible and nutritious.
She harvested some lamb's-quarters from the garden to add to her soup.
In early summer, lamb's-quarters leaves can be steamed and served like spinach.

Etymology

Named for its traditional use as a potherb around the Christian festival called Lammas; 'quarters' refers to the leaves' shape.
