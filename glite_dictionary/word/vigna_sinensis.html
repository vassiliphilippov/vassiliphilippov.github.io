Vigna sinensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪɡnə saɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡnə sɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A sprawling annual plant of the Old World, cultivated especially in the southern United States for its edible seeds and use as forage and green manure.
annual legume; cowpea plant

Examples

Farmers in the southern states often plant Vigna sinensis to improve soil quality and provide animal fodder.
The seeds of Vigna sinensis are a vital source of protein in many African and Asian diets.
Researchers are studying Vigna sinensis for its drought-resistant properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowpea - the common name for Vigna sinensis, used for its edible seeds

Etymology

From Latin, 'Vigna' after Italian botanist Domenico Vigna; 'sinensis' meaning 'Chinese'.
