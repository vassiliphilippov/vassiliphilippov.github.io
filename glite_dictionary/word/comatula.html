Comatula

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊməˈtjuːlə / (AmE), / ˌkəʊməˈtjuːlə / (BrE)
a former genus of feather stars, now generally regarded as synonymous with Antedon
former genus of feather stars (syn. Antedon)
archaic

Examples

The marine zoologist described the structure of the Comatula in her research paper.
In older texts, Comatula is often used to refer to what we now call Antedon.
Specimens of Comatula were displayed in the natural history museum's invertebrate collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Antedon - current genus name for certain feather stars

Etymology

From New Latin *Comatula*, diminutive of Greek *komē* (hair), referring to the feathery arms.
