Noctuidae

1. NOUN

/ nɑkˈtjuːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / nɒkˈtjuːɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of moths whose larvae, known as cutworms or armyworms, are often agricultural pests.
family of moths (cutworms, armyworms)

Examples

Many species within the Noctuidae family are considered pests by farmers.
The larvae of Noctuidae often attack crops at night, causing significant damage.
Entomologists study Noctuidae to better understand pest control methods.

Etymology

From modern Latin Noctuidae, based on Latin noctua meaning 'night owl'.

2. NOUN

/ nɑkˈtjuː.əˌdiː / (AmE), / nɒkˈtjuː.ɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of moths, commonly known as owlet moths, many of whose larvae are cutworms and armyworms, some of which are agricultural pests.
a family of moths (owlet moths)

Examples

The larvae of Noctuidae species are notorious for damaging crops worldwide.
Many gardeners dread the presence of Noctuidae in their fields due to the destructive cutworms.
Noctuidae moths are often attracted to outdoor lights at night.

Etymology

From New Latin Noctuidae, from Noctua (a genus of moths) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
