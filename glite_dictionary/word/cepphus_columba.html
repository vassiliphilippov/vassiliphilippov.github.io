Cepphus columba

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛfəs koʊˈlʌmbə / (AmE), / ˈsɛfəs kəˈlʌmbə / (BrE)
A species of seabird in the auk family, commonly known as the pigeon guillemot, found along the coasts of the northern Pacific Ocean.
species of northern Pacific seabird (pigeon guillemot)

Examples

The Cepphus columba nests in rocky crevices along the Pacific coast.
Researchers observed a colony of Cepphus columba on the islands near Alaska.
Unlike other auks, Cepphus columba often forages close to shore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pigeon guillemot - common name for Cepphus columba, a seabird of the northern Pacific

Etymology

From New Latin: genus Cepphus from Greek kēpphos (a kind of sea bird); species columba from Latin (dove, pigeon), referring to its plumage.
