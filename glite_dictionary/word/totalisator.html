totalisator

1. NOUN

/ toʊˈtælɪˌseɪtər / (AmE), / təʊˈtælɪseɪtə / (BrE)
a system or machine, especially at a racetrack, that records bets and calculates the division of the combined pool among winners
betting machine/system for dividing winnings

Examples

The racetrack installed a new totalisator to handle the large number of bets efficiently.
All wagers were recorded by the totalisator before the start of the race.
The totalisator displays the odds and payouts in real-time for all bettors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parimutuel - system of betting where all bets are pooled and winners share the pool

Etymology

From French 'totalisateur', from 'totaliser' (to total); related to English 'total'.
