Equus caballus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.wəs kəˈbæl.əs / (AmE), / ˈɛk.wəs kəˈbæl.əs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the domestic horse, a solid-hoofed herbivorous mammal domesticated since prehistoric times
the domestic horse (scientific name)

Examples

The Equus caballus is found in a variety of breeds across the world.
Veterinarians study the anatomy of Equus caballus to better care for horses.
A museum exhibit displayed the evolution of Equus caballus from ancient ancestors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse - a large, solid-hoofed domestic animal used for riding and work

Etymology

From Latin: 'Equus' meaning 'horse' and 'caballus' meaning 'work horse, gelding'.
