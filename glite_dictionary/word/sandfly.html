sandfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsandˌflʌɪ / (BrE)
A small, bloodsucking fly of warm regions, some species of which can transmit diseases such as sandfly fever and leishmaniasis.
small bloodsucking fly; disease vector

Examples

Bites from a sandfly can cause irritation and, in some cases, serious illness.
The sandfly is known to transmit dangerous parasites in tropical regions.
Researchers are studying how the sandfly spreads leishmaniasis among humans.

Etymology

From sand + fly, referring to its habitat and insect type.
