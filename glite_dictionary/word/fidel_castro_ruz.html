Fidel Castro Ruz

1. PROPN

/ fiˌdɛl ˈkæstroʊ ruz / (AmE), / fiˌdɛl ˈkæstrəʊ ruːz / (BrE)
(1926–2016) Cuban revolutionary and politician who led the Cuban Revolution of 1959, later serving as Prime Minister and President of Cuba, and establishing a socialist state.
Cuban revolutionary and leader (1926–2016)

Examples

Fidel Castro Ruz became the leader of Cuba after the revolution in 1959.
Many historians study the impact of Fidel Castro Ruz on Cold War politics.
Speeches by Fidel Castro Ruz are still analyzed by political scientists today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Che Guevara - Argentine Marxist revolutionary who fought alongside Castro during the Cuban Revolution
  • Synonym: dictator - A ruler with total power, often taken by force
