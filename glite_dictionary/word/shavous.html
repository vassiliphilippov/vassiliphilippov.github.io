Shavous

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈvu əs / (AmE), / ʃəˈvu əs / (BrE)
A Jewish holiday celebrated on the sixth of Sivan, commemorating Moses receiving the Ten Commandments at Mount Sinai.
Jewish festival commemorating the giving of the Ten Commandments

Examples

Many families gather together to celebrate Shavous with traditional dairy foods.
The synagogue will host special services for Shavous this year.
Learning sessions and readings of the Ten Commandments are key parts of Shavous observance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shavuot - Jewish festival marking the giving of the Torah

Etymology

Variant transliteration of Hebrew 'Shavuot' meaning 'weeks'.
