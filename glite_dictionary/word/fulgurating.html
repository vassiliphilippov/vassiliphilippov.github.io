fulgurating

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʌlɡjəˌreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfʌlɡjʊˌreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of pain) sudden, intense, and stabbing, like a flash of lightning.
sharp, stabbing (of pain)
medical

Examples

The patient complained of fulgurating pain shooting down his leg.
She experienced a fulgurating sensation during the nerve test.
Doctors describe the pain of trigeminal neuralgia as fulgurating and electric in nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stabbing - (of pain) extremely sharp or intense
  • Antonym: dull - (of pain) not sharp or intense

Etymology

From Latin fulgurare (to flash with lightning), from fulgur (lightning).
