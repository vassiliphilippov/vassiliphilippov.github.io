dulled

1. ADJ

/ dʌld / (AmE), / dʌld / (BrE)
having lost interest, sharpness, or responsiveness due to repetition or prolonged exposure
made less interested or responsive

Examples

The constant noise from traffic has dulled her ability to concentrate.
Years of routine tasks had dulled his enthusiasm for his job.
The repeated warnings eventually dulled the public's sense of urgency.

Synset Examples

the mind of the audience is becoming dulled
the benumbed intellectual faculties can no longer respond

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement; not sharp

Etymology

From the verb 'dull' (to make or become less sharp or less intense), with the -ed participle suffix.

2. ADJ

/ dʌld / (AmE), / dʌld / (BrE)
having lost its sharpness, brightness, or clarity; made less intense or effective.
made less sharp or clear

Examples

The knife had a dulled edge after months of use.
His senses felt dulled by the lack of sleep.
The colors in the painting appeared dulled by years of sunlight exposure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - lacking sharpness or brightness
  • Antonym: sharpened - made or become sharp
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00804168-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ dʌld / (AmE), / dʌld / (BrE)
having lost brightness, vividness, or intensity, especially in color; made less distinct or sharp
made less bright or intense

Examples

The once vibrant tapestry had become dulled by years of exposure to sunlight.
Her enthusiasm for the project dulled after repeated setbacks.
The paint on the old car was dulled and scratched, showing its age.

Synset Examples

colors dulled by too much sun
greyed with the dust of the road

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faded - having lost brightness or intensity of color
  • Antonym: bright - having a strong, clear, or vivid color

Etymology

Past participle of 'dull', from Middle English 'dul', from Old English 'dol' (foolish, dull).

Source: WordNet
