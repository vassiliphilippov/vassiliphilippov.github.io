wigmaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪɡˌmeɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪɡˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to make and sell wigs
person who makes and sells wigs

Examples

The wigmaker carefully crafted each wig by hand for the theater troupe.
During the 18th century, a skilled wigmaker was an important figure in society.
She apprenticed with a renowned wigmaker to learn the traditional techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wig - artificial covering of hair for the head

Etymology

From 'wig' + 'maker', dating to the 17th century when wig-making became a trade.
