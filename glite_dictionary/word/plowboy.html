plowboy

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaʊˌbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈplaʊˌbɔɪ / (BrE)
a boy who guides the animals that pull a plow, especially on a farm
boy guiding plow animals

Examples

The plowboy led the horses steadily across the wide, brown field.
In the early mornings, the plowboy would sing as he worked behind the animals.
Stories of the hardworking plowboy are common in rural folklore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ploughman - a man who guides or operates a plow

Etymology

From 'plow' + 'boy'; 'plow' from Old English 'plōg', 'boy' from Old French 'boie'.
