superimposed

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuːpərɪmˈpoʊzd / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərɪmˈpəʊzd / (BrE)
placed or layered on top of something else, so that both things are visible or combined
placed or layered on top of something else

Examples

Several images were superimposed to create the final photograph.
The architect’s drawing showed new structures superimposed on the existing site plan.
In geology, different layers of rock can be superimposed over millions of years.

Synset Examples

superimposed rocks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlaid - covered by laying something over
  • Antonym: underlying - situated beneath something else

Etymology

From Latin 'superimponere', from 'super-' (on, above) + 'imponere' (to place upon).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsuːpərɪmˈpoʊzd / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərɪmˈpəʊzd / (BrE)
placed on or over something else, especially so that both are still evident
placed on or over something else

Examples

A transparent map was superimposed onto the satellite image for clarity.
The old photograph was superimposed on the modern cityscape to show the changes over time.
Special effects made it appear as if ghostly figures were superimposed over the scene.

Synset Examples

an overlying image

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlay - to cover the surface of something with a layer

Etymology

From Latin superimponere (to place upon), via superimpose + -ed.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02357688-s
Synset POS: s
