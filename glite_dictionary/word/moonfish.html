moonfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːnˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmuːnˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
Any of several silvery marine fishes with very flat, rounded bodies, found in various ocean regions.
a type of flat, silvery marine fish

Examples

The fisherman was excited to catch a rare moonfish off the coast.
The aquarium has a large tank with several colorful moonfish swimming together.
Because of its flat, round shape, the moonfish is easy to identify among other marine species.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmuːnˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmuːn.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a large, oval-shaped, brightly colored deep-sea fish found in the Atlantic, Pacific, and Mediterranean seas
large deep-sea fish; opah

Examples

The moonfish is known for its distinctive round body and shimmering scales.
Fishermen off the coast of Hawaii sometimes catch moonfish weighing over 100 pounds.
The vibrant colors of the moonfish make it a fascinating subject for marine biologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opah - another name for the moonfish, especially in North America
