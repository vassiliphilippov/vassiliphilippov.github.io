angiocarpous

1. ADJ

/ ˌændʒioʊˈkɑrpəs / (AmE), / ˌandʒɪəʊˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
(of a fruit) having the fruit enclosed within a shell, husk, or covering, as in certain fungi or plants.
fruit enclosed in a shell or husk (botany)

Examples

The angiocarpous structure of the fungus protects the spores until they are mature.
Some plants produce angiocarpous fruits that remain hidden until decomposition of the outer covering.
Botanists classify certain mushrooms as angiocarpous due to their enclosed reproductive bodies.

Etymology

From Greek angeion (vessel) + karpos (fruit).
