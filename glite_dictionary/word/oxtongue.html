oxtongue

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑksˌtʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒksˌtʌŋ / (BrE)
a European plant with spiny, tongue-shaped leaves and yellow flowers, now naturalized in the United States; commonly considered a weed.
European weed with spiny leaves and yellow flowers

Examples

The field was full of wild oxtongue growing among the tall grasses.
Gardeners often struggle to remove oxtongue because of its spiny leaves.
She recognized the yellow flowers of oxtongue by the roadside.

Etymology

From Old English *oxan tunge*, literally 'ox's tongue', referring to the shape and texture of the leaves.
