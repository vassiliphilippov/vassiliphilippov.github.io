regest

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʒɛst / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛst / (BrE)
(archaic) A register or record, especially an official list or document.
archaic word for a register or record
archaic

Examples

The ancient manuscripts were carefully recorded in the regest for future reference.
Historians often consult the regest of cathedral documents to trace religious appointments.
Several decrees were found among the royal regests kept in the archive.

Synset Examples

Others of later time have sought to assert him by old legends and Cathedrall regests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: register - an official list or record

Etymology

From Latin 'regesta', plural of 'regestum', meaning a list or register.
