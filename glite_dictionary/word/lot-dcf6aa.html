lot

1. NOUN

/ lɑt / (AmE), / lɒt / (BrE)
a parcel of land with fixed boundaries, especially one intended for building or development
parcel of land with boundaries

Examples

They bought a spacious lot at the edge of town to build their new house.
The city auctioned off several vacant lots for residential development.
We parked our car in an empty lot behind the restaurant.

Synset Examples

he bought a lot on the lake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - a small piece of land for building or gardening

Etymology

From Old English *hlot* (portion, share, object used to decide by chance).
