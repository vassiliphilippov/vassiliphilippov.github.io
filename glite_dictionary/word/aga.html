Aga

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːɡə / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡə / (BrE)
a title for a civil or military leader, especially in the former Ottoman Empire or Turkey
title for Turkish leader

Examples

The respected Aga presided over the local council meeting.
During Ottoman times, an Aga often commanded military units.
The villagers gathered to seek the advice of their Aga on important matters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bey - title for a chieftain or leader in Turkish and Ottoman contexts
  • Synonym: Pasha - high-ranking title for an official in the Ottoman Empire

Etymology

From Turkish 'ağa', meaning 'chief' or 'lord'.
