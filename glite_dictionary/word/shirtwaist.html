shirtwaist

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɜrtˌweɪst / (AmE), / ˈʃɜːtˌweɪst / (BrE)
a woman's blouse with buttons down the front, often styled like a shirt
woman's buttoned blouse

Examples

She wore a crisp white shirtwaist tucked neatly into her skirt.
The shirtwaist became popular in the early twentieth century among working women.
Vintage fashion lovers often seek out Edwardian shirtwaist blouses for their unique style.

Synset Examples

in Britain they call a shirtwaist a ‘shirtwaister’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blouse - a woman's upper garment resembling a shirt, typically with a more tailored or gathered fit

Etymology

Formed from 'shirt' + 'waist', originally denoting a shirt-like garment worn at the waist.
