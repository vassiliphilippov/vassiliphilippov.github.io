whiz

1. VERB

/ wɪz / (AmE), / wɪz / (BrE)
(of an object) to make a soft or faint swishing or buzzing sound as it moves quickly through the air.
make a soft swishing sound

Examples

An arrow whizzed past his ear as he ducked behind the wall.
The bullet whizzed through the air, narrowly missing its target.
I heard a bee whiz by my head as I walked through the garden.

Synset Examples

the motor whirred
the car engine purred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whir - make a low, continuous, regular sound
  • Synonym: buzz - make a humming or buzzing sound

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ wɪz / (AmE), / wɪz / (BrE)
a person who is extremely skilled or successful at something, especially in a particular field
someone extremely skilled in something
informal

Examples

Sarah is a real whiz with computers and can fix almost any problem.
The company hired a marketing whiz to improve their advertising strategy.
Even as a child, he was known as a math whiz in his school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expert - a person with a high level of knowledge or skill in a particular area
  • Antonym: novice - a person who is new to or inexperienced in a field or situation
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ wɪz / (AmE), / wɪz / (BrE)
a buzzing or hissing sound produced by something moving rapidly through the air
buzzing or hissing sound of rapid motion

Examples

The arrow flew past him with a sharp whiz.
Suddenly, a whiz filled the air as the rocket shot upward.
We ducked when we heard the whiz of the baseball coming toward us.

Synset Examples

he heard the whiz of bullets near his head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whoosh - a soft sound made by a sudden movement of air or liquid
  • Synonym: swish - a hissing or rustling sound, as of something moving swiftly through the air
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07414961-n
Synset POS: n
