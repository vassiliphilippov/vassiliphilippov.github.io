oat

1. NOUN

/ oʊt / (AmE), / əʊt / (BrE)
The seed of the annual grass Avena sativa, commonly used as food for humans and livestock, usually referred to in the plural as 'oats'.
seed of the grass Avena sativa

Examples

She bought a bag of oats to make homemade granola.
Oats are a popular ingredient in breakfast cereals and oatmeal.
Farmers often plant oats as a nutritious crop for their livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barley - a cereal grain used similarly to oats
  • Synonym: wheat - another common cereal grain

Etymology

Old English āte, of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ oʊt / (AmE), / əʊt / (BrE)
A tall annual grass cultivated for its seed, which is used as food and animal feed; also, the seed of this plant, known as 'oats'.
a cereal grass; its grain used as food

Examples

Many breakfast cereals contain oat as their main ingredient.
Farmers rotate oat crops with other grains to maintain soil health.
Whole-grain oat is considered a healthy dietary choice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheat - a cereal plant, grain used for flour
  • Synonym: barley - a cereal grain often used in brewing and food
  • Synonym: rye - a cereal grain, often used for bread and whiskey

Etymology

Old English *āte* (singular seldom recorded), of uncertain origin.
