disguise

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɡaɪz / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡaɪz / (BrE)
clothing or an appearance that is intended to hide someone's identity.
clothing/appearance to hide identity

Examples

She wore a disguise to avoid being recognized by the press.
His clever disguise fooled everyone at the masquerade party.
The spy donned a disguise before meeting his contact in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: costume - clothing worn to create a particular appearance, especially in theatre or celebrations

Etymology

From Old French *desguiser* (to change appearance).

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɡaɪz / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡaɪz / (BrE)
the act of changing one's appearance or manner in order to conceal identity
the act of concealing identity by altering appearance

Examples

The spy relied on a clever disguise to attend the meeting unnoticed.
For Halloween, she wore a vampire disguise that fooled everyone.
He escaped the city in disguise, avoiding recognition at every checkpoint.

Synset Examples

he is a master of disguise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camouflage - the act of hiding by blending in with surroundings
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make something known openly or publicly

Etymology

From Old French *desguiser* (to change appearance or hide identity), based on *guise* (manner, style).

3. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɡaɪz / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡaɪz / (BrE)
an outward appearance or manner that hides the true nature or identity of someone or something
appearance or manner used to conceal true identity

Examples

Through clever disguise, the spy was able to enter the facility unnoticed.
The magician’s disguise fooled everyone at the party.
Sometimes, kindness can be a disguise for hidden motives.

Synset Examples

the theatrical notion of disguise is always associated with catastrophe in his stories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camouflage - the act of hiding something by making it look like its surroundings
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make something known that was hidden

Etymology

From Old French 'desguisier' (to disguise), from 'des-' (away) + 'guise' (style, manner).

4. VERB

/ dɪsˈɡaɪz / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡaɪz / (BrE)
to change someone’s or something’s appearance so that they are not recognized
to make unrecognizable by altering appearance

Examples

The thief tried to disguise himself with a fake beard and sunglasses.
She used makeup to disguise her identity during the investigation.
A strong perfume can sometimes disguise unpleasant odors in a room.

Synset Examples

The herb masks the garlic taste
We disguised our faces before robbing the bank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conceal - to hide or keep from sight
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make visible or known

Etymology

From Old French 'desguiser', based on 'guise' (manner, appearance).
