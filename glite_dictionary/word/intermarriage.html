intermarriage

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈmærɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmærɪdʒ / (BrE)
The custom or practice of marrying within one's own tribe, group, or community as required by social, religious, or legal rules.
marriage within one's own group or tribe

Examples

Certain cultures enforce intermarriage to maintain their traditions and ensure community continuity.
Religious laws in some societies require intermarriage within specific groups.
The tribe's custom of intermarriage has been practiced for generations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exogamy - marriage outside one's own group
  • Synonym: endogamy - marriage within one's own group or community

Etymology

From inter- (between) + marriage; first recorded in English in the early 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈmærɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmærɪdʒ / (BrE)
Marriage between people of different tribes, groups, races, or social categories, especially as required or regulated by custom or law.
marriage between different social groups

Examples

The practice of intermarriage helped to create alliances between neighboring tribes.
Some communities have strict rules prohibiting intermarriage with outsiders.
Debates about intermarriage often reflect broader social and cultural attitudes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exogamy - marriage outside a specific group or tribe
  • Antonym: endogamy - marriage within a specific group or tribe

Etymology

Formed from the prefix inter- (between) and marriage.
