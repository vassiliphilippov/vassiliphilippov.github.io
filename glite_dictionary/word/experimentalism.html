experimentalism

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspɛr.əˈmɛn.t̬ə.lɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛr.ɪˈmen.tə.lɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the belief in or practice of relying on experimentation and innovation, especially as a method of inquiry or development in science, philosophy, or the arts
preference for experimentation and innovation

Examples

Her approach to teaching is based on experimentalism rather than strict adherence to the curriculum.
The artist's experimentalism challenged conventional ideas about painting and sculpture.
Critics praised the writer's experimentalism for bringing fresh perspectives to modern literature.

Synset Examples

the children of psychologists are often raised in an atmosphere of experimentalism

Related Terms

  • Antonym: traditionalism - adherence to traditional methods or practices
  • Synonym: innovation - the introduction of something new

Etymology

From experiment + -al + -ism.

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspɛrəmɛnˈtælɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛrɪmɛnˈtælɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief in or advocacy of the use of experimental methods, especially as a guiding principle in philosophy, science, or art
belief in experimental methods

Examples

The philosophy department held a debate on experimentalism versus rationalism.
His approach to art was rooted in experimentalism, constantly testing new techniques and ideas.
Scientific progress often relies on experimentalism to validate or disprove theories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empiricism - the theory that knowledge comes from sensory experience

Etymology

From experiment + -al + -ism, reflecting the emphasis on experimentation.
