Haastia

1. NOUN

/ hɑːˈstiːə / (AmE), / hæˈstiːə / (BrE)
A genus of mat-forming herbs or subshrubs native to New Zealand, commonly called 'vegetable sheep.'
genus of New Zealand herbs ('vegetable sheep')

Examples

The Haastia genus thrives in the alpine regions of New Zealand's South Island.
Botanists were excited to discover a rare species of Haastia during their expedition.
The unusual appearance of Haastia plants has earned them the nickname 'vegetable sheep.'

Etymology

Named after Johann Franz Haast, a German-born geologist and explorer in New Zealand.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæstiə / (AmE), / ˈhæstiə / (BrE)
A genus of mat-forming herbs or subshrubs native to New Zealand, commonly known as vegetable sheep.
genus of New Zealand mat-forming herbs

Examples

The Haastia genus includes species that resemble sheep grazing on the mountainsides of New Zealand.
Botanists studied the Haastia plants to understand their adaptation to alpine environments.
Several subshrubs in the Haastia genus are well-known for their unique mat-forming growth.

Etymology

Named after Johann Franz Julius von Haast, German-born New Zealand geologist and explorer.
