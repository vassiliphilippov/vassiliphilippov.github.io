Michel Montaigne

1. PROPN

/ miːˈʃɛl mɒnˈteɪn / (AmE), / miːˈʃɛl mɒnˈtɛɪn / (BrE)
(1533–1592) A French writer and philosopher known as the originator of the modern essay.
French essayist and philosopher

Examples

Many scholars consider Michel Montaigne to be the founder of the modern essay genre.
The philosophical works of Michel Montaigne continue to influence writers worldwide.
In his essays, Michel Montaigne explored themes of skepticism, self-reflection, and human nature.

Etymology

French personal name; Montaigne is from the Occitan word for 'mountain'.
