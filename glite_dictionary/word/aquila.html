Aquila

1. NOUN

/ əˈkwɪlə / (AmE), / əˈkwɪlə / (BrE)
a genus of large birds of prey in the family Accipitridae, known as the true eagles
genus of true eagles

Examples

The golden eagle belongs to the genus Aquila.
Most members of Aquila are known for their impressive hunting skills and large wingspans.
Biologists recently discovered a new species within the Aquila genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eagle - large bird of prey with a heavy beak, known for its strength

Etymology

From Latin *aquila* (eagle). Used as a genus name in taxonomy.

Examples

The constellation Aquila is easily identified by the bright star Altair.
Ancient astronomers named Aquila after the Latin word for 'eagle'.
You can see Aquila best during the summer months in the Northern Hemisphere.
