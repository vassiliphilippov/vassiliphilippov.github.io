withe

1. NOUN

/ wɪð / (AmE), / wɪð / (BrE)
A band or rope made by twisting together flexible twigs or stems, often used for binding or tying.
band or rope of twisted twigs or stems

Examples

The basket's sturdy handle was made from a single withe twisted into shape.
He bound the firewood with a withe he had fashioned from willow branches.
She used a withe to secure the gate, keeping the animals inside the pen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withy - a flexible twig, especially of willow, used for tying

Etymology

Old English *wiðig* (willow twig, willow tree), influenced by similar Germanic terms.

2. NOUN

/ wɪθ / (AmE), / wɪð / (BrE)
a strong, flexible twig or stem, especially one used in binding or basket-making
strong, flexible twig or stem

Examples

She tied the bundle together with a withe she found in the hedge.
The basket was woven from willow withes by local craftsmen.
He watched her skillfully bend the withe into the framework of a new chair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twig - a small thin branch of a tree or bush
  • Degree Higher: rod - a thin straight bar, especially of wood or metal

Etymology

Old English *wīthig* (willow twig).
