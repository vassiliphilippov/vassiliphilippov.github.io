preachy

1. ADJ

/ ˈpriːtʃi / (AmE), / ˈpriːtʃi / (BrE)
Tending to give moral advice in a tiresome or self-righteous way.
overly moralizing; self-righteous

Examples

His speech became unbearably preachy after the first few minutes.
I try to avoid sounding preachy when I talk to my kids about healthy habits.
Some readers found the novel interesting, while others thought it was too preachy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moralizing - speaking in a way that gives moral advice
  • Synonym: didactic - intended to teach, especially in a patronizing way

Etymology

From English 'preach' + '-y', meaning 'like or characteristic of preaching'.
