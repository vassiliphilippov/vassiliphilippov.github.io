Cyrillic

1. ADJ

/ sɚˈɪlɪk / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪlɪk / (BrE)
relating to or written in the alphabet used for several Slavic and Central Asian languages, such as Russian, Bulgarian, and Serbian.
relating to the Cyrillic alphabet

Examples

The Russian language uses the Cyrillic script instead of the Latin alphabet.
She learned to read Cyrillic letters before traveling to Bulgaria.
All street signs in the city were written in Cyrillic.

Synset Examples

Cyrillic writing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Latin - relating to or using the Latin (Roman) alphabet
  • Synonym: Cyrillic alphabet - alphabet used for various Slavic and Central Asian languages

Etymology

Named after Saint Cyril, a Byzantine monk who helped develop the Glagolitic and Cyrillic scripts.

2. NOUN

/ səˈrɪlɪk / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪlɪk / (BrE)
the alphabet derived from the Greek alphabet and used for writing various Slavic languages, such as Russian, Bulgarian, and Serbian.
Slavic alphabet derived from Greek

Examples

Many Russian books are printed in Cyrillic, which can be challenging for beginners to read.
He learned to write his name in Cyrillic during his language class.
Bulgarian officially switched to the Cyrillic alphabet in the ninth century.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Latin alphabet - the alphabet used for English and many other languages
  • Degree Lower: Greek alphabet - the script from which Cyrillic is derived

Etymology

Named after Saint Cyril, who, with his brother Methodius, created the Glagolitic and influenced the development of this script.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06511787-n
Synset POS: n
