Shona

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊnə / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊnə / (BrE)
a Bantu language widely spoken in Zimbabwe and surrounding countries
Bantu language of Zimbabwe

Examples

Many people in Zimbabwe speak Shona as their first language.
She is learning to write in Shona for her university project.
The translation was available in English, French, and Shona.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃoʊnə / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊnə / (BrE)
relating to the culture, language, or people of the Shonas, a Bantu ethnic group of Zimbabwe and neighboring countries
relating to the Shona people or culture

Examples

The museum features a collection of Shona sculptures from Zimbabwe.
She wore traditional Shona clothing to the cultural festival.
Shona music is known for its vibrant use of the mbira instrument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shona - a member of the Shona people

Etymology

From the name of the Shona people of southern Africa.

3. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊ.nə / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊ.nə / (BrE)
a member of a Bantu ethnic group native to present-day Zimbabwe and neighboring regions
member of a Bantu ethnic group (Zimbabwe)

Examples

Many Shona live in rural communities, practicing traditional customs.
The Shona have a rich history of stone sculpture and music.
He studied the interactions between the Shona and neighboring peoples in Zimbabwe.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Ndebele - a member of another major ethnic group in Zimbabwe
