Prenanthes

1. NOUN

/ priːˈnænθiːz / (AmE), / prɪˈnanθiːz / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs native to North America and Asia, with divided leaves and small, drooping flower heads, sometimes including species formerly placed in Nabalus.
genus of perennial herbs with drooping flower heads

Examples

Several botanists studied Prenanthes species found in North American forests.
The flowers of Prenanthes often droop and come in shades ranging from yellowish to purple.
This reserve is home to a rare variety of Prenanthes known for its divided leaves.

Etymology

From Greek 'pre-' (before) and 'anthos' (flower).

2. NOUN

/ priˈnænθiːz / (AmE), / priˈnanθiːz / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs native to North America and Asia, with deeply divided leaves and small, drooping yellowish to purple flower heads; sometimes includes species placed in the genus Nabalus.
genus of perennial herbs with drooping flower heads

Examples

The woodland was dotted with clusters of Prenanthes in full bloom.
Some botanists debate whether certain species belong to Prenanthes or Nabalus.
Gardeners appreciate Prenanthes for its hardy nature and unusual flowers.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek 'prēnēs' (leaning forward, drooping) and 'anthos' (flower).
