cuticula

1. NOUN

/ kjuːˈtɪkjʊlə / (AmE), / kjuːˈtɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
the tough, outer layer or body wall covering an insect, protecting it from the environment
the outer body wall of an insect
medical

Examples

The cuticula protects the insect's internal organs from dehydration and injury.
During molting, the old cuticula is shed and replaced by a new one.
Scientists examined the thickness of the cuticula in several beetle species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuticle - the outermost layer of skin or integument, especially in animals and plants

Etymology

From Latin *cuticula* (diminutive of *cutis*, skin).
