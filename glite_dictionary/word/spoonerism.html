spoonerism

1. NOUN

/ ˈspunərˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈspuːnərɪzəm / (BrE)
a mistake in speaking in which the first sounds of two or more words are swapped, often creating a humorous effect
transposition of word beginnings in speech

Examples

In his speech, the professor made a funny spoonerism by saying 'queer old dean' instead of 'dear old queen'.
Accidentally calling it a 'blushing crow' is a classic spoonerism.
Her tendency to create spoonerisms always made her friends chuckle during conversations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malapropism - misuse of a word by confusion with a similar-sounding one

Etymology

Named after William Archibald Spooner (1844–1930), an English clergyman noted for such slips of the tongue.
