Judeo-Banderite

1. NOUN

/ dʒuˌdeoʊ bændeˈraɪt / (AmE), / dʒuˌdeɪəʊ bændeˈraɪt / (BrE)
A controversial political label, often used disparagingly, to criticize those perceived as aligning with both Jewish interests and Ukrainian nationalist views, especially in post-Soviet or Russian propaganda contexts.
controversial political label

The use of Judeo-Banderite in political rhetoric often sparks heated reactions.

Understanding the historical context of Judeo-Banderite is important for interpreting debates in Eastern Europe.

Etymology

Formed from 'Judeo-', relating to Jewish people, and 'Banderite', referencing followers of Stepan Bandera, a Ukrainian nationalist figure.
