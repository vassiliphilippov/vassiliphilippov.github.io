tramline

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræm.laɪn / (AmE), / ˈtræm.laɪn / (BrE)
a railway track on which trams or streetcars run, typically laid along public streets in a city
track for trams or streetcars

Examples

The city is planning to upgrade the old tramline to improve public transportation.
Work crews were repairing a section of tramline damaged during last week’s storm.
New tramlines have been installed to connect the suburbs to the city center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: railway track - the track on which trains run
  • Antonym: bus lane - a lane reserved for buses on a road

Etymology

From 'tram' (vehicle running on rails) + 'line' (track or route).
