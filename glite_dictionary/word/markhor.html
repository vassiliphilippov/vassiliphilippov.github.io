markhor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrkɔr / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɔː / (BrE)
a large wild goat found mainly in the western Himalayas, noted for its long, spiraled horns
a large wild goat with spiraled horns

Examples

The markhor can be recognized by its impressive twisted horns and rugged appearance.
Conservation efforts have been implemented to protect the endangered markhor in Pakistan.
During our trek, we were lucky enough to spot a markhor climbing the rocky slopes.

Etymology

From Persian مارخور (mārkhōr), literally 'snake eater', from mār 'snake' + khōr 'eater'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑr.kɔr / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.kɔː / (BrE)
A large wild goat native to the Himalayan region, notable for its long, spiraled horns.
large Himalayan wild goat with spiraled horns

Examples

The markhor is recognized for its impressive, twisted horns that can reach more than a meter in length.
Wildlife researchers spotted a herd of markhors grazing along the steep cliffs.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of the endangered markhor in northern Pakistan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ibex - a wild mountain goat with long, curved horns

Etymology

From Persian مارخور (mārkhur), literally 'snake eater'.
