wrick

1. NOUN

/ rɪk / (AmE), / rɪk / (BrE)
a painful muscle spasm, especially in the neck or back
painful muscle spasm (neck/back)
british

Examples

She woke up with a wrick in her neck after sleeping awkwardly.
Lifting that heavy box gave me a sudden wrick in my back.
He couldn't turn his head because of a painful wrick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rick - painful muscle spasm, especially in the neck or back (alternative term)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14384587-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ rɪk / (AmE), / rɪk / (BrE)
to twist a part of the body, especially a joint, suddenly and painfully so as to sprain it
twist or sprain a joint suddenly

Examples

He managed to wrick his ankle while jogging in the park.
She wricked her wrist trying to open the stubborn jar lid.
Be careful not to wrick your neck when looking up suddenly.

Synset Examples

wrench one's ankle
The wrestler twisted his shoulder
the hikers sprained their ankles when they fell
I turned my ankle and couldn't walk for several days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprain - to injure a joint by sudden twisting
  • Synonym: wrench - to twist or pull something suddenly and violently

Etymology

Probably a variant of 'wrench' or from Middle English 'wrikken' (to twist).
