mush

1. NOUN

/ mʌʃ / (AmE), / mʌʃ / (BrE)
A thick porridge made by boiling cornmeal in water or milk.
thick cornmeal porridge

Examples

For breakfast, he ate a bowl of mush with a little bit of honey.
The cafeteria served mush as the main option on cold mornings.
Pioneers often relied on mush as a simple, filling meal while traveling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porridge - a dish consisting of oatmeal or another meal or cereal boiled in water or milk

2. NOUN

/ mʌʃ / (AmE), / mʌʃ / (BrE)
a journey by dogsled, especially over snow or ice
journey by dogsled

Examples

The annual race features a grueling mush across the Alaskan wilderness.
She dreamed of taking part in a long mush with a team of loyal huskies.
Each winter, dozens of competitors sign up for the famous mush through deep snow and icy forests.

Etymology

From the command 'mush!' given to dogsled teams, probably from French 'marche!' meaning 'go!' or 'walk!'

3. NOUN

/ mʌʃ / (AmE), / mʌʃ / (BrE)

soft or soggy mass

Examples

The cereal turned into mush after soaking in milk too long.
She stepped into the mud, which was thick mush after the rainstorm.
The cooked vegetables had been reduced to a tasteless mush.

4. VERB

/ mʌʃ / (AmE), / mʌʃ / (BrE)
to drive or urge on a team of dogs pulling a sled, especially by calling out commands to them
drive a dogsled team

Examples

He learned how to mush a team of dogs across the snowy landscape.
The guide expertly mushed the dogs through the blizzard.
Every winter, she enjoys mushing during the annual sled dog races.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sled - a vehicle typically used on snow, pulled by dogs or other animals

Etymology

Derived from the French command 'marche!' (walk!), used by French-Canadian drivers of sled dog teams.
