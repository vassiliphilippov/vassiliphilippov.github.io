Dreissena polymorpha

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈsɛnə ˌpɑːlɪˈmɔːrfə / (AmE), / draɪˈsiːnə ˌpɒlɪˈmɔːfə / (BrE)
A small freshwater mussel native to Eurasia, commonly known as the zebra mussel, noted for clogging water supply pipes and outcompeting native species.
zebra mussel; invasive freshwater mollusk

Examples

Dreissena polymorpha has caused problems in North American lakes by clogging water intake pipes.
The spread of Dreissena polymorpha threatens native aquatic species and disrupts ecosystems.
Researchers are studying ways to control the population of Dreissena polymorpha in affected waterways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zebra mussel - Common name for Dreissena polymorpha, an invasive freshwater mollusk

Etymology

Scientific name from the genus Dreissena and species polymorpha (Latin for 'many-shaped').
