gaucheness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊʃ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being awkward, lacking social grace, or showing a lack of tact and refinement
awkwardness or lack of social grace

Examples

Her gaucheness at the dinner party made the conversation slightly uncomfortable for everyone.
The actor's natural gaucheness was endearing to the audience.
He apologized for his gaucheness during the meeting, admitting he was nervous.

Synset Examples

the whole town was famous for its crudeness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkwardness - lack of grace or ease in social situations
  • Antonym: tact - sensitivity in dealing with others or difficult issues

Etymology

From French 'gauche' (left, awkward) + English suffix '-ness'.
