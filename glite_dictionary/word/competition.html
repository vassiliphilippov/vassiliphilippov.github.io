competition

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmpəˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpəˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
The activity or process of striving to gain or win something by defeating or establishing superiority over others, often for a prize or reward.
the act of competing for a prize or reward

Examples

There is fierce competition for places at the university.
She won the chess competition last year.
The companies are facing tough competition in the global market.

Synset Examples

the teams were in fierce contention for first place

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contest - an organized competition in which people try to win
  • Antonym: cooperation - working together towards a common goal

Etymology

From Latin 'competitio' (rivalry), from 'competere' (to strive together).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmpəˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpəˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
an event or contest in which individuals or teams compete against each other to achieve victory or recognition
event in which contestants compete

Examples

The annual spelling competition attracted students from all over the region.
She trained for months to prepare for the swimming competition this summer.
Winning the competition was a dream come true for the young violinist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contest - an event in which people compete to win
  • Synonym: tournament - a series of contests between competitors, usually for a championship
  • Antonym: cooperation - working together towards a common goal

Etymology

From Latin 'competitio(n-)', from 'competere' meaning 'to seek together, strive for'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmpəˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpəˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
the person or group you are competing against, especially in business or sports; a rival or opponent
rival or opponent in a contest

Examples

The competition is launching a new product next month, so we need to move quickly.
She always keeps a close eye on the competition before important matches.
It's vital to understand the strengths and weaknesses of your competition in this industry.

Synset Examples

he had respect for his rivals
he wanted to know what the competition was doing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rival - person or team competing for the same goal as you; opponent
  • Synonym: opponent - one who is on the opposing side in a competition or contest

Etymology

From Latin 'competitio', from 'competere' (to strive in common, to contend).

4. NOUN

/ ˌkɑmpəˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpəˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
the commercial rivalry between businesses or organizations to attract customers or achieve superior results
rivalry between businesses for customers

Examples

The fierce competition between the two tech giants led to rapid innovation.
Retail competition in the area has forced shops to lower their prices.
Due to strong competition, the company had to improve its customer service.

Synset Examples

business competition can be fiendish at times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rivalry - competition or conflict between people or organizations for the same objective
  • Antonym: cooperation - the process of working together instead of competing

Etymology

From Latin 'competitio' (rivalry, contest).
