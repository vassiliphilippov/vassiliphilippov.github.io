deliciously

1. ADV

/ dɪˈlɪʃəsli / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪʃəsli / (BrE)
In a manner that is extremely pleasant or delightful, often giving great pleasure to the senses.
in a very pleasant or delightful manner

Examples

The freshly baked bread smelled deliciously warm and inviting.
She laughed deliciously at his clever joke, her eyes sparkling with joy.
The air was deliciously cool after the summer rain.

Synset Examples

they were walking along the beach slowly and deliciously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleasantly - in an enjoyable or agreeable manner
  • Antonym: disagreeably - in an unpleasant or offensive manner

Etymology

Formed from 'delicious' + '-ly'; 'delicious' from Old French 'delicios', from Latin 'deliciosus' (delightful).

2. ADV

/ dɪˈlɪʃəsli / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪʃəsli / (BrE)
In a way that produces or is full of delight, especially in taste.
in a delightful manner, often in taste

Examples

The cake was deliciously moist and full of flavor.
She smiled deliciously after hearing the good news.
The air was deliciously cool on a hot summer day.

Synset Examples

I bought some more of these deliciously sweet peaches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleasantly - in a pleasing or agreeable manner
