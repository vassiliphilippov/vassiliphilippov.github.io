Mycteroperca

1. NOUN

/ maɪkˌtɛrəˈpɜrkə / (AmE), / mɪkˌtɛrəˈpɜːkə / (BrE)
A genus of large marine fishes in the grouper family, found in warm seas and valued as food fish.
a genus of groupers (marine fish)

Examples

The Mycteroperca genus includes several species prized by sport fishermen.
Research on Mycteroperca has helped improve conservation efforts for groupers.
Many Mycteroperca species are found along tropical and subtropical reefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grouper - large marine fish in the subfamily Epinephelinae

Etymology

From Greek mykter (nose) and perke (perch), referring to the fish's appearance.
