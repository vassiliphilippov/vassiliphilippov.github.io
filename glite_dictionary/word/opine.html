to opine

1. VERB

/ oʊˈpaɪn / (AmE), / əʊˈpaɪn / (BrE)
to express an opinion about something
express an opinion

Examples

The professor often opined on political issues during her lectures.
Many critics have opined that the movie failed to live up to expectations.
He did not hesitate to opine that the new policy would be ineffective.

Synset Examples

John spoke up at the meeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assert - to state a fact or belief confidently and forcefully
  • Synonym: to suggest - to put forward an idea for consideration
  • Antonym: remain silent - to not speak or express an opinion

Etymology

From Latin 'opinari' (to think, suppose).

2. VERB

/ oʊˈpaɪn / (AmE), / əʊˈpaɪn / (BrE)
to express an opinion or belief about something; to state what you think or believe
express an opinion; state a belief

Examples

Many commentators opine that the new policy will benefit small businesses.
She often opines on political matters during meetings.
He opined that the movie was overrated and not worth the hype.

Synset Examples

I imagine she earned a lot of money with her new novel
I thought to find her in a bad state
he didn't think to find her in the kitchen
I guess she is angry at me for standing her up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppose - to assume something is true without certain proof
  • Synonym: to believe - to accept that something is true
  • Synonym: express - to make known one's thoughts or feelings

Etymology

From Latin 'opinari' (to think, suppose).
