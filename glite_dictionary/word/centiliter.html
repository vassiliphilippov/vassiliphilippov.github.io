centiliter

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntɪˌlaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsɛntɪliːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of volume equal to one hundredth of a liter (0.01 liters).
metric unit of volume (0.01 liters)

Examples

The bottle contains exactly 50 centiliters of water.
In many European countries, wine is often measured in centiliters.
A standard shot of alcohol can be measured as 3 centiliters in some recipes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: milliliter - metric unit of volume equal to one thousandth of a liter
  • Degree Higher: deciliter - metric unit of volume equal to one tenth of a liter

Etymology

From French 'centilitre', from Latin 'centum' (hundred) + 'litra' (liter).
