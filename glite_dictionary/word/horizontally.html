horizontally

1. ADV

/ ˌhɔːrɪˈzɑntəli / (AmE), / ˌhɒrɪˈzɒntəli / (BrE)
In a horizontal direction or position; parallel to the horizon.
in a horizontal direction

Examples

The windows were placed horizontally to allow more light into the room.
He drew a line horizontally across the page to separate the sections.
The shelves are arranged horizontally, making it easy to store books of different sizes.

Synset Examples

a gallery quite often is added to make use of space vertically as well as horizontally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vertically - in a vertical direction

Etymology

From the adjective 'horizontal' + adverbial suffix '-ly'. 'Horizontal' originates from Medieval Latin 'horizontalis', from Greek 'horizōn' (dividing line).
