costermongerish

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑstərˌmʌŋɡɚɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒstəmʌŋɡərɪʃ / (BrE)
Characteristic of street vendors, especially in being lively, talkative, or witty in manner or conversation.
resembling a lively street vendor

Examples

The comedian's costermongerish style brought laughter to everyone in the room.
Her costermongerish chatter made the market stall especially lively and fun.
He adopted a costermongerish tone to attract customers to his booth.

Synset Examples

The lively market had a costermongerish atmosphere.
Her costermongerish banter entertained the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: costermonger - a street seller of fruit, vegetables, or goods

Etymology

Formed from 'costermonger' (a street seller of fruit or vegetables) plus the suffix '-ish.'

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɑstərˈmʌŋɡərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌkɒstəmˈʌŋɡərɪʃ / (BrE)
Characteristic of or resembling a costermonger, especially in tone, manner, or style; having the rough, informal, or unrefined qualities associated with street vendors.
like a costermonger in manner or tone

Examples

Her accent took on a costermongerish tone when she became angry.
The comedian adopted a costermongerish voice for his streetwise character.
The film's dialogue was peppered with costermongerish expressions and slang.

Synset Examples

His accent had a distinctly costermongerish tone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: costermonger - a person who sells goods, especially fruit and vegetables, from a handcart in the street

Etymology

From 'costermonger' (street fruit seller), with the adjective-forming suffix '-ish'.
