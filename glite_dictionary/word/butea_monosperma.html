Butea monosperma

1. NOUN

/ buːˈtiːə ˌmɒnəˈspɝːmə / (AmE), / bjuːˈtiːə ˌmɒnəˈspɜːmə / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to South Asia, known for its bright vermilion flowers and its use in producing yellow dye.
South Asian tree with bright flowers

Examples

The Butea monosperma tree is prized for the brilliant hue of its flowers in spring.
Artisans use the flowers of Butea monosperma to extract natural dyes for fabrics.
Many birds are attracted to the Butea monosperma when it is in full bloom.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Butea' and species epithet 'monosperma', meaning 'single-seeded' in Latin.
