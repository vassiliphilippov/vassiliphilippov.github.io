branchy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbræn.tʃi / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːn.tʃi / (BrE)
(of a tree, plant, or similar object) having many branches.
having many branches

Examples

The branchy oak provided welcome shade on a sunny afternoon.
We followed a narrow path through the branchy undergrowth.
The artist sketched a branchy silhouette against the winter sky.

Synset Examples

a branchy tree trunk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branched - having branches or a branched structure

Etymology

From 'branch' + '-y', forming adjectives meaning 'having many branches'.
