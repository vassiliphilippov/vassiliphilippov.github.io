Pyrola americana

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈroʊlə əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (AmE), / paɪˈrəʊlə əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (BrE)
An evergreen plant native to eastern North America, with leathery leaves and clusters of white flowers.
North American evergreen plant species

Examples

The forest floor was covered with Pyrola americana blooming in the shade.
Botanists identified the plant as Pyrola americana by its leathery leaves and white flowers.
Many nature trails in the region feature abundant Pyrola americana among the undergrowth.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Pyrola' and the Latin 'americana' meaning 'of America'.
