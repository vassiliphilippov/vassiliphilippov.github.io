Franklin D. Roosevelt

1. PROPN

/ ˈfræŋklɪn di ˈroʊzəˌvɛlt / (AmE), / ˈfræŋklɪn di ˈrəʊzəˌvɛlt / (BrE)
(1882–1945) The 32nd President of the United States, serving from 1933 to 1945; known for leading the country through the Great Depression and World War II, and for implementing the New Deal.
32nd President of the United States

Examples

Franklin D. Roosevelt was re-elected president three times, a unique achievement in U.S. history.
Many historians credit Franklin D. Roosevelt with helping the nation recover from the Great Depression.
The legacy of Franklin D. Roosevelt continues to influence American politics and society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New Deal - series of U.S. programs and reforms under Franklin D. Roosevelt
