visor

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˈvʌɪzə / (BrE)
a movable part of a medieval helmet, fitted with eye slits, that protects the face
armor plate on helmet for face protection

Examples

The knight lowered his visor before entering the tournament arena.
Her museum exhibit includes a medieval helmet with a hinged visor.
A broken visor left the soldier's face unprotected in battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faceplate - front protective part of a helmet

Etymology

From Anglo-French *viser* (face guard), from Old French *vis* (face).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈvaɪzə / (BrE)
a projecting front part of a cap or helmet that shades the eyes from the sun
projecting brim to shade the eyes

Examples

He adjusted the visor of his baseball cap to block out the bright sunlight.
The cyclist wore a helmet with a tinted visor for better visibility.
She pulled her visor down as she stepped onto the tennis court.

Synset Examples

he pulled down the bill of his cap and trudged ahead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bill - the projecting brim of a cap
  • Synonym: brim - the projecting edge of a hat

Etymology

From Anglo-French *viser*, from Latin *visēre* (to look at), influenced in English by Old French *viser* (a visor, a face guard).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02841963-n
Synset POS: n
