quizzical

1. ADJ

/ ˈkwɪzɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkwɪzɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a person's expression or attitude) showing mild or amused puzzlement, as if questioning or not understanding something.
showing mild or amused puzzlement

Examples

She gave him a quizzical look when he mentioned the surprise.
His quizzical expression revealed that he didn't quite understand the joke.
The teacher raised an eyebrow in a quizzical manner after reading the odd answer.

Synset Examples

he had a quizzical expression

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puzzled - feeling confused or bewildered
  • Synonym: perplexed - completely baffled; very puzzled
  • Antonym: certain - being sure or confident

Etymology

From 'quiz' (to question or test), plus -ical (suffix forming adjectives).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkwɪzɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkwɪzɪkəl / (BrE)
showing mild amusement, puzzlement, or playful teasing, especially in one's expression or manner.
playfully puzzled or teasing

Examples

She raised a quizzical eyebrow when she heard the unexpected news.
His quizzical smile made it clear he was only joking.
The teacher gave the student a quizzical look when he offered a strange answer.

Synset Examples

his face wore a somewhat quizzical almost impertinent air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puzzled - feeling confused because you cannot understand something
  • Synonym: teasing - making fun of or provoking playfully
  • Antonym: serious - not joking or playfully teasing; solemn

Etymology

From quiz (to question in a light or mocking way) + -ical.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02130499-s
Synset POS: s
