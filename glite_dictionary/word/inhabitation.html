inhabitation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌhæbɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌhæbɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of living in or occupying a place as a permanent residence
the act of living in a place

Examples

The inhabitation of these remote islands has been documented for thousands of years.
Permanent inhabitation of the valley increased after new resources were discovered.
Legends tell of the early inhabitation of the ancient ruins by mysterious people.

Synset Examples

he studied the creation and inhabitation and demise of the colony

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habitation - the state or process of living in a particular place
  • Synonym: residence - the fact of living in a particular place

Etymology

From Latin 'inhabitare' (to inhabit) + '-ation' (forming nouns of action or process).
