hysteron proteron

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪstərəˌn proʊˈtɛrən / (AmE), / ˌhɪstərɒn prəʊˈtɛrən / (BrE)
A figure of speech in which the normal order of words or events is reversed, typically placing what should come last first, as in ‘bred and born’.
figure of speech reversing normal order

Examples

The phrase ‘bred and born’ is an example of hysteron proteron.
Poets sometimes use hysteron proteron to emphasize certain events or actions.
Describing someone as having ‘put on their shoes and socks’ is a classic hysteron proteron.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anastrophe - figure of speech involving inversion of word order

Etymology

From Greek *hysteron proteron* meaning 'the latter (put) before (the former)'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɪstərɒn proʊˈtɛrɒn / (AmE), / ˌhɪstərɒn prəˈtɪərən / (BrE)
A rhetorical or logical fallacy in which a proposition that has not yet been proven is assumed as a premise.
logical fallacy assuming unproven premises

Examples

The argument contained a hysteron proteron, taking the conclusion as a given before it was established.
Pointing out the hysteron proteron in his opponent’s reasoning, she argued that the facts still required proof.
In philosophical discussions, a hysteron proteron can undermine the logical validity of an argument.

Etymology

From Greek 'hysteron' (later) and 'proteron' (earlier), originally describing a reversal of natural sequence in speech or writing.
