inefficacy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɛfɪkəsi / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛfɪkəsi / (BrE)
The state or quality of being inefficient or ineffective; lack of power to produce the desired effect.
lack of efficacy or effectiveness

Examples

The drug was discontinued due to its inefficacy in treating the disease.
Despite repeated attempts, the team's inefficacy was evident in their poor performance.
A sense of frustration grew as the policy's inefficacy became clear over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ineffectiveness - the quality of not producing the desired effect
  • Antonym: efficacy - the ability to produce a desired result or effect

Etymology

From Latin prefix in- (not) + efficacy, modeled after inefficax (ineffective).
