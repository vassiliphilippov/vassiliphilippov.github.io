Novgorod

1. PROPN

/ ˈnoʊvɡəˌrɑd / (AmE), / ˈnɒvɡərɒd / (BrE)
a city in northwestern Russia on the Volkhov River, regarded as Russia's oldest city and a major trading center during the Middle Ages
historic city in northwestern Russia

Examples

Many tourists visit Novgorod to admire its medieval architecture and ancient monuments.
Novgorod played a crucial role in the formation of the early Russian state.
During the Middle Ages, Novgorod was a key hub for trade between Europe and Asia.

Etymology

From Russian Новгород (Novgorod), meaning 'new town' or 'new city'.
