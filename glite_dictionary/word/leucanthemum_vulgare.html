Leucanthemum vulgare

1. NOUN

/ luːˈkænθɪməm vʌlˈɡɛəri / (AmE), / ljuːˈkænθɪməm vʌlˈɡɛəri / (BrE)
A tall Eurasian perennial plant with leafy stems and white, daisy-like flowers, commonly known as the oxeye daisy.
the oxeye daisy, a common wildflower

Examples

Leucanthemum vulgare is often seen growing wild along country roads in the summer.
Gardeners value Leucanthemum vulgare for its hardy nature and classic white blossoms.
Many meadows are filled with Leucanthemum vulgare, creating a beautiful sea of white and yellow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxeye daisy - common name for Leucanthemum vulgare
  • Synonym: Chrysanthemum - a related genus of flowering plants

Etymology

From Latin: 'leucos' (white) and 'anthemon' (flower), referring to its white petals.
