prolific

1. ADJ

/ prəˈlɪfɪk / (AmE), / prəˈlɪfɪk / (BrE)
Producing a large amount of something, especially works of art or literature; highly productive intellectually.
producing many works; highly productive

Examples

She is a prolific author who has published more than fifty novels.
The artist remained prolific even in the later years of her career.
His prolific mind produced inventions that changed the industry.

Synset Examples

a prolific writer
a fecund imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: productive - yielding or producing a large amount

Etymology

From Latin prolificus, from proles (offspring) + -ficus (making).

2. ADJ

/ prəˈlɪfɪk / (AmE), / prəˈlɪfɪk / (BrE)
(of a plant, animal, or person) producing much fruit, foliage, offspring, or many works; highly productive.
highly productive; abundant in production

Examples

The old apple tree in our yard has always been incredibly prolific every summer.
She is a prolific writer who publishes several novels each year.
Rabbits are known to be prolific breeders, often producing many litters annually.

Synset Examples

flying foxes are extremely prolific
a prolific pear tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruitful - producing good or useful results; productive
  • Synonym: productive - producing or achieving a lot; effective
  • Antonym: barren - not producing or incapable of producing offspring or fruit

Etymology

From French prolifique, from Latin prōlificus ('fruitful, productive'), from prōles ('offspring') + facere ('to make').
