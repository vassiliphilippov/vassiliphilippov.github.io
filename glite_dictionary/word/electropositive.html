electropositive

1. ADJ

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈpɑzətɪv / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈpɒzətɪv / (BrE)
(of an atom or element) having a tendency to lose electrons and form positive ions; having a positive charge.
having a positive charge; tends to lose electrons

Examples

Sodium is an electropositive element that readily loses its outer electron.
Metals are generally considered more electropositive than nonmetals.
The reaction occurs because hydrogen acts as an electropositive atom in the compound.

Synset Examples

protons are positive

Related Terms

  • Antonym: electronegative - (of an atom or element) tending to gain electrons and form negative ions

Etymology

From electro- (relating to electricity) + positive (having a positive electric charge).
