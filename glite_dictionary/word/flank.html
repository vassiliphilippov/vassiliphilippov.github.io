flank

1. NOUN

/ flæŋk / (AmE), / flæŋk / (BrE)
the side of something, especially when regarded as a distinct section
side of something; distinct section

Examples

The soldiers advanced quickly along the enemy's right flank.
We built a small garden on the eastern flank of the house.
The left flank of the mountain was covered with dense forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: side - a position to the left or right of an object, place, or central point

Etymology

From Old French *flanc* (side), from Frankish *hlanka* (flank, hip).

2. NOUN

/ flæŋk / (AmE), / flæŋk / (BrE)
A cut of meat from the side of an animal, especially the area between the ribs and the leg.
cut of meat from side of animal

Examples

The chef grilled a flank steak and served it with chimichurri sauce.
He bought a large flank of beef to roast for the family dinner.
Flank is often used to make stir-fry dishes because it cooks quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loin - cut of meat from the back of an animal
  • Synonym: brisket - cut of meat from the breast or lower chest of an animal

Etymology

From Old French *flanc* (side), from Frankish *hlanka* (hip, side).
