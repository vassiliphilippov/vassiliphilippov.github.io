earnestness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɝː.nɪst.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɜː.nɪst.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being serious and sincere in intention or effort
seriousness and sincerity

Examples

She spoke with a quiet earnestness that convinced everyone in the room.
His earnestness was clear when he apologized for his mistake.
The earnestness in his eyes showed how much the project mattered to him.

Synset Examples

a lack of solemnity is not necessarily a lack of seriousness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious
  • Antonym: levity - lack of seriousness, treating serious matters with humor

Etymology

From Middle English 'ernestnesse', from Old English 'eornostnes' (seriousness, earnestness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɜrnɪstnəs / (AmE), / ˈɜːnɪstnəs / (BrE)
a quality of being serious, sincere, and committed in intention or effort
serious and sincere attitude

Examples

She spoke with such earnestness that everyone believed her words.
His earnestness in pursuing the project impressed his colleagues.
There was a noticeable earnestness in his appeal for help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sincerity - the quality of being free from pretense or deceit
  • Antonym: frivolity - lack of seriousness; lightheartedness

Etymology

From Middle English 'ernestnesse', from Old English 'eornost' (seriousness) + '-ness'.
