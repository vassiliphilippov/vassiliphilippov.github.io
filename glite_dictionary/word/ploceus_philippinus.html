Ploceus philippinus

1. NOUN

/ ploʊˈsiː.əs fɪˌlɪp.ɪˈpiː.nəs / (AmE), / pləʊˈsiː.əs fɪˌlɪp.ɪˈpiː.nəs / (BrE)
a species of weaverbird native to the Indian subcontinent, known for its intricate hanging nests
a South Asian weaverbird species

Examples

The Ploceus philippinus constructs elaborate nests that hang from branches.
Birdwatchers travel across India to spot the colorful Ploceus philippinus during nesting season.
In rural villages, the distinctive nests of the Ploceus philippinus are a common sight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baya - common name for Ploceus philippinus
  • Synonym: weaverbird - bird of the family Ploceidae, known for its woven nests

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Ploceus' being the genus name for weaverbirds and 'philippinus' referring to the Philippines (despite its main range being in South Asia).
