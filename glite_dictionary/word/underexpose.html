to underexpose

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndɚɪkˈspoʊz / (AmE), / ˌʌndərɪkˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to expose photographic film or a sensor to insufficient light, resulting in a darker image than intended
expose to too little light (photography)

Examples

If you underexpose your photos, the details in the shadows may be lost.
The photographer accidentally underexposed the film, resulting in darker than expected images.
Make sure not to underexpose when taking pictures in low light conditions.

Synset Examples

The film is underexposed, so the image is very dark

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to overexpose - to expose photographic film or a sensor to too much light

Etymology

Formed from 'under-' (meaning insufficient) and 'expose' from Latin exponere (to put out, display).

2. VERB

/ ˌʌndɚɪkˈspoʊz / (AmE), / ˌʌndərəksˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to expose something to less light or influence than necessary; to expose insufficiently
expose insufficiently

Examples

The photographer accidentally underexposed the image, making it look too dark.
Children who are underexposed to social interaction may struggle with communication skills.
If you underexpose the film, the pictures will not develop properly.

Synset Examples

The child was underexposed to language

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to overexpose - to expose something to too much light or influence

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'under-' and the verb 'expose'.
