hypovitaminosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpoʊˌvaɪtəməˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊˌvɪtəmɪˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disease or condition caused by a deficiency of one or more vitamins in the body.
disease from vitamin deficiency
medical

Examples

Hypovitaminosis can lead to serious health problems if left untreated.
The doctor diagnosed her with hypovitaminosis after reviewing her blood test results.
Children are particularly vulnerable to hypovitaminosis when their diets lack essential nutrients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avitaminosis - disease resulting from the absence of vitamins

Etymology

From hypo- (under, below) + vitamin + -osis (disease or condition).
