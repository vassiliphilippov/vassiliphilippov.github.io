satiric

1. ADJ

/ səˈtɪrɪk / (AmE), / səˈtɪrɪk / (BrE)
Using satire to expose and criticize human folly or vice, often in a humorous or mocking manner.
using satire to ridicule human folly

Examples

The playwright is known for his satiric take on modern politics.
Her satiric cartoons comment on society's absurdities with sharp wit.
The novel's satiric tone makes its social criticism both entertaining and thought-provoking.

Synset Examples

a persistent campaign of mockery by the satirical fortnightly magazine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satirical - using satire to mock or criticize

Etymology

From French satirique, from Latin satiricus (pertaining to satire).
