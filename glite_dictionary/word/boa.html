boa

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊ.ə / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.ə / (BrE)
A large, chiefly tropical, non-venomous constrictor snake, often with vestigial hind limbs, belonging to the family Boidae.
large tropical constrictor snake

Examples

The boa slowly wrapped itself around the branch, blending into the rainforest foliage.
A boa can suffocate its prey by constriction before swallowing it whole.
She was fascinated by the spotted pattern on the boa at the reptile house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: python - large non-venomous constrictor snake of the family Pythonidae

Etymology

From Latin 'boa', meaning a type of large snake.

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊ.ə / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.ə / (BrE)
a long, thin scarf made of feathers or fur, worn loosely around the neck for warmth or as a fashion accessory
fluffy scarf of feathers or fur

Examples

She draped a pink boa around her shoulders for the costume party.
Feathered boas were popular accessories during the Roaring Twenties.
The theatre actress wore a flamboyant boa to complete her stage outfit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarf - a piece of fabric worn around the neck for warmth or fashion

Etymology

From Portuguese 'boa' (feminine of 'bom' meaning good); originally referring to the snake, and later to the scarf resembling a boa constrictor.
