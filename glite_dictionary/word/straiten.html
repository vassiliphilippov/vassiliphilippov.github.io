to straiten

1. VERB

/ ˈstreɪtən / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtən / (BrE)
to squeeze or press together tightly; to make narrow or restricted
to squeeze together; make narrow

Examples

The tailor straitened the waist of the jacket to give it a slimmer fit.
Intense pressure straitened the passage, making it difficult to breathe.
She straitened her grip on the rope as she climbed higher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compress - to press or squeeze together; to make more compact
  • Antonym: to loosen - to make less tight or confined; to release pressure

Etymology

From Middle English 'streiten', from Old French 'estreit', from Latin 'strictus' (drawn tight).

2. VERB

/ ˈstreɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtən / (BrE)
to put someone or something into a difficult or distressed situation, especially one involving a lack of money
to put into difficulty or hardship

Examples

The sudden loss of income will straiten many families in the community.
Heavy debts have straitened the company, forcing it to cut costs drastically.
A weak harvest can straiten even the most diligent of farmers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impoverish - to make someone poor
  • Synonym: distress - to cause anxiety or suffering
  • Antonym: to relieve - to make a difficult situation less severe

Etymology

From Middle English streiten, from Old French estreitier, based on Latin strictus (tight, drawn together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02609431-v
Synset POS: v
