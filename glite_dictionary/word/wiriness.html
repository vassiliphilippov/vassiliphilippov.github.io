wiriness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪə.ri.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being lean, tough, and sinewy
lean and sinewy quality

Examples

The wiriness of the athlete was evident in his swift, precise movements on the court.
Her wiriness helped her navigate the harsh wilderness where strength and endurance were essential.
You could see the wiriness in his arms, a testament to years of hard physical labor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sinewiness - having strong, lean muscles or a sinewy build
  • Degree Higher: robustness - the quality of being strong and healthy

Etymology

From 'wiry' + '-ness', with 'wiry' from wire (Old English wīr) meaning resembling wire, in reference to thinness and toughness.
