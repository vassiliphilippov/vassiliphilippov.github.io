Adenota

1. NOUN

/ ˌædɪˈnoʊtə / (AmE), / ˌadɪˈnəʊtə / (BrE)
A genus of African antelopes in the family Bovidae, including the puku.
genus of African antelopes

Examples

The Adenota genus includes species like the puku, known for its distinctive appearance.
Researchers observed several Adenota in the wetlands of Zambia during the expedition.
The classification of Adenota is important for understanding antelope diversity in Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puku - a medium-sized African antelope, Adenota vardonii, found in southern central Africa

/ ˌædəˈnoʊtə / (AmE), / ˌadɪˈnəʊtə / (BrE)

genus of African antelopes

Examples

The Adenota genus includes several species of antelopes native to Africa.
Researchers observed a herd belonging to the Adenota genus near the river.
The puku is a notable member of the Adenota genus.
