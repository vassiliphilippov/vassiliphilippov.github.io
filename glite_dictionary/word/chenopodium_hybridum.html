Chenopodium hybridum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkiːnoʊˈpoʊdiəm haɪˈbrɪdəm / (AmE), / ˌkɛnəˈpəʊdiəm hɪˈbrɪdəm / (BrE)
a species of herb in the goosefoot family, sometimes considered poisonous to pigs
species of herb, may be poisonous to pigs

Examples

Farmers were warned to remove Chenopodium hybridum from pastures to protect their livestock.
The botanist identified the plant as Chenopodium hybridum, noting its distinctive leaves and seeds.
Swine were poisoned after grazing on fields overrun with Chenopodium hybridum.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Chenopodium' from Greek 'khēn' (goose) + 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to the shape of the leaves.
