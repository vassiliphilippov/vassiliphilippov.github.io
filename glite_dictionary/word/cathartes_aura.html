Cathartes aura

1. PROPN

/ kəˈθɑːrtiːz ˈɔːrə / (AmE), / kaˈθɑːtiːz ˈɔːrə / (BrE)
a large American vulture, commonly known as the turkey vulture, found throughout South America, Central America, and the southern United States
a New World vulture species (turkey vulture)

Examples

The Cathartes aura is often seen circling high above open landscapes, searching for carrion.
Birdwatchers in Florida frequently spot the Cathartes aura gliding effortlessly on thermal updrafts.
Unlike some other vultures, the Cathartes aura relies heavily on its keen sense of smell to locate food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turkey vulture - common name for Cathartes aura, a large New World vulture

Etymology

From Latin: 'cathartes' meaning 'purifier' and 'aura' meaning 'breeze' or 'air'.
