Kusan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːˌsɑːn / (AmE), / ˈkuːˌsæn / (BrE)
a member of the Native American people once living along the Coos River, Coos Bay, and lower Coquille River in Oregon
member of an Oregon Native American people

Examples

The Kusan traditionally lived near the Coos Bay area in what is now Oregon.
Archaeologists have uncovered artifacts belonging to the Kusan people along the rivers of western Oregon.
Several modern tribes in Oregon include descendants of the historic Kusan.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkuːsɑːn / (AmE), / ˈkuːsæn / (BrE)
a Penutian language once spoken by the Kusan people of southwestern Oregon.
Penutian language of the Kusan people

Examples

Very few records exist of the Kusan language, making reconstruction difficult.
Linguists have studied Kusan to trace Penutian language relationships.
The Kusan language is no longer spoken today.
