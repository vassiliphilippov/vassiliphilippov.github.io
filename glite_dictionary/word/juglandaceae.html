Juglandaceae

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌɡlænˈdeɪsi.i / (AmE), / dʒʌɡlanˈdɛːsɪ.iː / (BrE)
a family of trees, including walnuts, hickories, and pecans, usually bearing edible nuts
family of nut-bearing trees (walnuts, hickories, etc.)

Examples

The Juglandaceae family includes economically important trees such as the walnut and the pecan.
Botanists classify hickory trees within the Juglandaceae family due to their similar nut structure.
Many species in the Juglandaceae are prized for both their timber and their edible nuts.

Etymology

From Latin 'Jugland-', from 'Juglans' (walnut) + '-aceae' (a family suffix in botanical Latin).

2. NOUN

/ dʒʌɡˌlænˈdeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / dʒʌɡˌlanˈdeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of trees, including walnuts, hickories, and pecans, that typically bear edible nuts
family of nut-bearing trees (walnuts, hickories, etc.)

Examples

The Juglandaceae family includes well-known trees like walnuts and hickories.
Botanists study Juglandaceae to learn about the diversity of nut-producing trees.
Pecans, a popular edible nut, belong to the Juglandaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walnut - tree bearing walnuts, a genus in Juglandaceae
  • Synonym: hickory - genus in Juglandaceae, produces edible nuts

Etymology

From Latin *Juglans* (walnut tree) + botanical family suffix *-aceae*.
