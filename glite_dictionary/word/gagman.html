gagman

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæɡˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈɡaɡ.man / (BrE)
a man who writes jokes or comic material for comedians or other entertainers
writer of comic material

Examples

The comedian credited his success to his talented gagman who supplied him with fresh material every week.
The studio hired a new gagman to punch up the script's humor before filming began.
As a gagman, he spent hours crafting clever lines and jokes for late-night television hosts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gag writer - a person who writes jokes for comedians or performers

Etymology

From 'gag' (comic joke or improvisation) + 'man'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæɡˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈɡæɡˌmæn / (BrE)
a male comedian who specializes in telling jokes or creating comic gags, especially for performers or shows
male comedian specializing in jokes

Examples

The famous gagman wrote jokes for several television variety shows.
As a gagman, he crafted memorable punchlines for other comedians.
The gagman delivered a hilarious performance at the comedy club last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comedian - a person whose profession is to entertain by making people laugh
  • Degree Lower: gagwoman - a female comedian who specializes in gags

Etymology

From 'gag' (a joke or comic device) + 'man', modeled after similar professional terms.
