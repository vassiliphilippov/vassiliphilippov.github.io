inexorability

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌɛks(ə)rəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌɛks(ə)rəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being impossible to stop, alter, or persuade; relentless determination or inevitability.
quality of being unyielding or relentless

Examples

The inexorability of time was a theme throughout the novel, reminding readers that nothing lasts forever.
Despite their pleas, the inexorability of the law provided no exceptions.
He spoke of the inexorability with which technology continues to advance, regardless of human concerns.

Synset Examples

the relentlessness or their pursuit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relentlessness - the quality of being persistent and unyielding
  • Antonym: leniency - quality of being more merciful or tolerant

Etymology

From Latin inexorabilis (unyielding), via inexorable + -ity.
