to vandalize

1. VERB

/ ˈvændəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvændəlaɪz / (BrE)
to deliberately damage or destroy property, especially public property, by acts of vandalism
deliberately damage or destroy property

Examples

Someone tried to vandalize the mural last night, but was caught by the security cameras.
The community is raising money to repair the playground after it was vandalized last weekend.
It's a serious crime to vandalize public property, and offenders may face heavy fines.

Synset Examples

vandalize the park

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deface - to spoil the appearance of something, especially by writing or drawing on it
  • Antonym: to protect - to keep from being damaged or harmed

Etymology

From 'vandal' (a person who deliberately destroys property) + '-ize' (verb forming suffix).
