wickerwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪkərˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈwɪkəˌwɜːk / (BrE)
objects or material made by weaving slender branches or twigs, especially willow, into a rigid structure, typically used for furniture and baskets.
material made by weaving twigs, especially willow

Examples

The chair's wickerwork was handwoven from local willow branches.
She admired the intricate wickerwork of the vintage basket.
The museum displayed several pieces of traditional wickerwork furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wicker - pliable twigs, typically willow, used for weaving baskets or furniture

Etymology

From 'wicker' (woven twig work) + 'work'; 'wicker' likely from Scandinavian origin, related to Old Norse 'vikja' (to bend).
