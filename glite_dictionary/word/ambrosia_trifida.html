Ambrosia trifida

1. NOUN

/ æmˈbroʊ.zi.ə ˈtraɪ.fɪ.də / (AmE), / æmˈbrəʊ.zi.ə ˈtrɪ.fɪ.də / (BrE)
a species of tall, coarse annual plant in the daisy family, with leaves usually deeply divided into three or five lobes; commonly known as giant ragweed
species of plant, giant ragweed

Examples

Ambrosia trifida is a major source of pollen allergies in North America during late summer.
Farmers often struggle to control the rapid spread of Ambrosia trifida in their fields.
The leaves of Ambrosia trifida are typically divided into three or five distinctive lobes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant ragweed - common name for Ambrosia trifida

Etymology

From Latin: 'ambrosia' (a genus of plants) and 'trifida' (meaning 'three-cleft', from 'tri-' three + 'fidus' split/divided).
