nit

1. NOUN

/ nɪt / (AmE), / nɪt / (BrE)
the egg or young form of a parasitic insect, especially a louse, often attached to hair or clothing
egg or young of a parasitic insect

Examples

The doctor used a special comb to remove every nit from the child's hair.
Teachers often check students for signs of lice and nits during outbreaks.
She found a single nit attached to the back of her collar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: louse - a parasitic insect that lives on mammals and feeds on blood

Etymology

From Old English *hnitu*, related to Dutch *net* and German *Nisse*, all meaning louse egg.

2. NOUN

/ nɪt / (AmE), / nɪt / (BrE)
a unit of luminance equal to one candela per square meter, used to measure the brightness of a surface
unit of luminance (brightness)

Examples

The display can reach a brightness of 1000 nits for better visibility in sunlight.
Many modern smartphones advertise their screen brightness in nits.
A monitor with 300 nits is considered quite suitable for office use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candela - SI unit of luminous intensity
  • Synonym: lumen - unit of luminous flux

Etymology

From Latin 'nitere', meaning 'to shine'.
