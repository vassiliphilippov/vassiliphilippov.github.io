to constitute

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑn.stəˌtut / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stɪ.tjuːt / (BrE)
to formally establish or appoint a group, committee, or other body and charge it with a specific function or task
formally establish or appoint

Examples

The board will constitute a panel to investigate the matter.
A new committee was constituted to oversee the project.
The university plans to constitute a task force for climate research.

Synset Examples

nominate a committee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appoint - assign a role or task to someone officially
  • Antonym: dissolve - to officially end or disband a group or body

Etymology

From Latin constituere (to set up, establish), from com- (together) + statuere (to set up).

/ ˈkɑːn.stəˌtut / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stɪ.tjuːt / (BrE)

Examples

These five houses constitute the entire village on the island.

4. VERB

Examples
