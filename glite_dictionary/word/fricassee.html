fricassee

1. NOUN

/ ˌfrɪkəˈsi / (AmE), / ˌfrɪkəˈsiː / (BrE)
A dish of pieces of meat, especially chicken, stewed in a white sauce or gravy with vegetables, often served with noodles or dumplings.
meat stewed in gravy with vegetables

Examples

She served a delicious fricassee of chicken with creamy noodles for dinner.
The restaurant’s special tonight is a rabbit fricassee with fresh garden vegetables.
After simmering for hours, the beef fricassee was tender and full of flavor.

Etymology

From French 'fricassée', past participle of 'fricasser' (to fry and stew).

2. VERB

/ ˌfrɪkəˈsi / (AmE), / ˌfrɪkəˈsiː / (BrE)
to cook meat or vegetables by stewing or frying them lightly and then serving them in a sauce
cook by stewing or frying in sauce

Examples

She decided to fricassee the leftover chicken for tonight's dinner.
The chef expertly fricasseed the mushrooms to bring out their flavor.
After fricasseeing the meat, he added a rich, creamy sauce before serving.

Synset Examples

fricassee meats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stew - to cook food slowly in liquid

Etymology

From French 'fricassée', past participle of 'fricasser' (to cut up and stew meat).
