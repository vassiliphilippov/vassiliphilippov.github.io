Septobasidium pseudopedicellatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛptoʊbæˈsɪdiəm ˌsjuːdoʊˌpɛdɪˌsɛˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌsɛptəʊbəˈsɪdiəm ˌsjuːdəʊˌpɛdɪˌsɛˈleɪtəm / (BrE)
A species of fungus that commonly grows around twigs and branches of various trees, especially citrus trees in the southern United States.
fungus affecting citrus trees in the southern U.S.

Examples

Large colonies of Septobasidium pseudopedicellatum can often be found encircling citrus branches in Florida.
The orchard was treated after Septobasidium pseudopedicellatum was discovered affecting several trees.
Researchers are studying Septobasidium pseudopedicellatum to better understand its impact on agricultural crops.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'septum' (partition) + 'basidium' (base), species name from Greek 'pseudo-' (false) and Latin 'pedicellatum' (having a small stalk).
