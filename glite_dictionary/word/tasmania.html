Tasmania

1. PROPN

/ tæzˈmeɪniə / (AmE), / tazˈmeɪniə / (BrE)
an Australian state consisting of the island of Tasmania and surrounding smaller islands, located south of the Australian mainland.
Australian island state

Examples

We spent our holiday exploring the beautiful landscapes of Tasmania.
Tasmania is known for its unique wildlife and national parks.
Many artists find inspiration in the natural beauty of Tasmania.

Etymology

Named after the Dutch explorer Abel Tasman, who first sighted the island in 1642.

2. PROPN

/ tæzˈmeɪniə / (AmE), / tazˈmeɪniə / (BrE)
an island state of Australia located south of the mainland, known for its rugged wilderness, unique wildlife, and rich colonial history
Australian island state south of the mainland

Examples

We are planning a trip to Tasmania next summer to explore its national parks.
Many unique species, such as the Tasmanian devil, are native to Tasmania.
The ferry from Melbourne to Tasmania offers breathtaking views of the Bass Strait.

Etymology

Named after the Dutch explorer Abel Tasman, who was the first European to sight the island in 1642.
