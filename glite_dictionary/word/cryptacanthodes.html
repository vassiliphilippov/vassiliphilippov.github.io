Cryptacanthodes

1. NOUN

/ krɪp.təˌkænˈθoʊ.diːz / (AmE), / krɪp.təˌkænˈθəʊ.diːz / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Stichaeidae, commonly known as wrymouths, found in the North Pacific Ocean.
genus of marine fish (wrymouths)

Examples

The Cryptacanthodes can be found burrowed in muddy sea bottoms along the Pacific coast.
Researchers studied the unique feeding habits of Cryptacanthodes in their natural habitat.
The elongated body of Cryptacanthodes distinguishes it from other members of the Stichaeidae family.

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'akantha' (thorn/spine), referring to the hidden spines of these fishes.

The genus Cryptacanthodes includes several species of wrymouth found in cold northern waters.

One distinguishing feature of Cryptacanthodes is its elongated, eel-like body.

From Greek kryptos (hidden) + akantha (thorn) + -odes (like), referring to spiny features.
