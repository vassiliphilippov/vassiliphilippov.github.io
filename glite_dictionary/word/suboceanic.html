suboceanic

1. ADJ

/ sʌbˌoʊˈsiː.ənɪk / (AmE), / sʌbˌəʊˈsiː.ənɪk / (BrE)
existing, situated, or occurring beneath the ocean or the ocean floor.
beneath the ocean or ocean floor

Examples

The search team is studying suboceanic geological formations for possible mineral deposits.
Scientists are developing new technology to map suboceanic currents and landforms.
The discovery of suboceanic life forms has changed our understanding of marine biology.

Synset Examples

suboceanic oil resources

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submarine - existing, occurring, or used under the surface of the sea

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'sub-' (under, beneath) and 'oceanic' (relating to the ocean).
