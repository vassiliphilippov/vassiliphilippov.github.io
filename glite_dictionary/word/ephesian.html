Ephesian

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈfiʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈfiːzɪən / (BrE)
of or relating to ancient Ephesus, its people, language, or culture
of ancient Ephesus or its people

Examples

The Ephesian ruins attract thousands of visitors every year.
Ancient Ephesian culture was known for its grand temple to Artemis.
She studied Ephesian history in her archaeology course.

Etymology

From Latin Ephesius, from Greek Ephesios, referring to Ephesus, an ancient Greek city in Asia Minor.

Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈfiʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈfiːzɪən / (BrE)
a resident or native of the ancient Greek city of Ephesus
resident of ancient Ephesus

Examples

The ancient Ephesian merchants were famous for their bustling markets.
An Ephesian philosopher contributed significantly to early Greek thought.
Many artifacts from the Ephesian civilization are displayed in museums today.

Etymology

From Ephesus, the name of the ancient city in Anatolia, with the suffix -ian denoting origin.
