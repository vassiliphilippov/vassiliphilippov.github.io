marlinespike

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrlɪnˌspaɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɑːlɪnˌspaɪk / (BrE)
a pointed iron tool used for separating strands of rope or cable, especially in splicing
tool for separating rope strands

Examples

The sailor used a marlinespike to neatly separate the rope's strands before splicing.
A marlinespike is an essential tool for anyone working with traditional rigging.
He carried his trusty marlinespike attached to his belt on the ship's deck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fid - a similar tool, usually made of wood or bone, for ropework

Etymology

From 'marline' (a type of small cord) + 'spike', relating to its use and shape.
