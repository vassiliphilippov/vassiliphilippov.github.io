Richard Coeur de Lion

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪtʃərd ˌkɜːr də ˈlaɪən / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃəd ˌkɜː də ˈlaɪən / (BrE)
the nickname of Richard I (1157–1199), King of England from 1189 to 1199, famed for his role in the Third Crusade and known for his courage and military leadership.
nickname of King Richard I of England

Examples

Many legends celebrate Richard Coeur de Lion as one of England's most valiant kings.
On his return journey from the Crusades, Richard Coeur de Lion was famously captured and held for ransom.
The stories of Richard Coeur de Lion inspired generations with tales of bravery and chivalry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Richard the Lionheart - Common English name for Richard I of England, meaning 'lion-hearted'
  • Degree Lower: King John - Younger brother and successor of Richard I as King of England

Etymology

From French, meaning 'Richard the Lionheart' (coeur de lion = lionheart).
