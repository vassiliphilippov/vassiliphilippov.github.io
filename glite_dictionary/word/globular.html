globular

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡlɑːbjələr / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒbjʊlə / (BrE)
having the shape of a globe or sphere; spherical
spherical in shape

Examples

The scientist observed globular clusters of stars through the telescope.
Tiny globular droplets formed on the surface of the leaf after the rain.
Certain plants produce globular fruits that are easy to identify.

Synset Examples

a spherical object
nearly orbicular in shape
little globular houses like mud-wasp nests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spherical - having the form of a sphere
  • Degree Lower: oval - shaped like an egg; having an oval form
  • Degree Higher: perfectly round - completely round, like a mathematically perfect sphere

Etymology

From Latin globulus, diminutive of globus (globe).
