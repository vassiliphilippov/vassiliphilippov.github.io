Avena fatua

1. NOUN

/ əˈviːnə fəˈtuːə / (AmE), / əˈviːnə fəˈtjuːə / (BrE)
A species of wild oat grass found commonly in meadows and pastures, often considered a weed.
species of wild oat grass

Examples

Avena fatua is regarded as a troublesome weed in cereal crop fields worldwide.
The pasture was heavily infested with Avena fatua during the summer months.
Scientists study Avena fatua to develop better weed management strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild oat - a common name for Avena fatua, a widespread grassy weed

Etymology

From Latin: 'avena' meaning 'oat' and 'fatua' meaning 'foolish' or 'wild'.
