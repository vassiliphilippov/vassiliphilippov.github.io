Tinamidae

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈnæmɪˌdi / (AmE), / tɪˈnamɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of ground-dwelling birds native to Central and South America, comprising the tinamous.
family of birds: tinamous

Examples

The Tinamidae family includes several species of elusive ground birds found in South America.
Researchers studied the evolutionary relationships within the Tinamidae using genetic data.
Unlike most other birds, members of the Tinamidae lay glossy, brightly colored eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tinamou - any bird of the family Tinamidae

Etymology

From New Latin Tinamidae, based on Tinamus (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
