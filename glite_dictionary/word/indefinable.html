indefinable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈfaɪnəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈfaɪnəbl / (BrE)
impossible to describe or explain exactly, or to put into definite words
impossible to describe or define

Examples

He felt an indefinable sense of sadness as he watched the sun set.
There was an indefinable quality about her voice that captivated everyone in the room.
The artist tried to capture the indefinable beauty of the moment in his painting.

Synset Examples

an indefinable feeling of terror
an abstract concept that seems indefinable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indescribable - too unusual or extreme to describe adequately
  • Antonym: definable - able to be precisely described or defined

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + definire (to define).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈfaɪnəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈfaɪnəbl / (BrE)
impossible to clearly describe or express in words; not able to be defined or explained precisely
impossible to define or describe

Examples

There was an indefinable sadness in her eyes that words could not capture.
The painting had an indefinable charm that drew everyone’s attention.
An indefinable tension filled the room as they waited for the announcement.

Synset Examples

indefinable yearnings
indescribable beauty
ineffable ecstasy
unspeakable happiness
unutterable contempt
a thing of untellable splendor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indescribable - impossible to describe
  • Antonym: definable - able to be defined or described

Etymology

From late Latin 'indefinabilis': in- (not) + definire (to define).
