aneurism

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.jəˌrɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈanjʊərɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A cardiovascular disease marked by the abnormal bulging or ballooning of an artery wall due to its weakening.
abnormal artery bulging due to wall weakness
medical

Examples

The doctor discovered an aneurism during a routine check-up.
If an aneurism ruptures, it can cause life-threatening internal bleeding.
Advanced imaging techniques are often used to monitor the growth of an aneurism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aneurysm - alternative spelling for 'aneurism', abnormal bulging of an artery wall

Etymology

From Greek 'aneurysma' meaning 'a widening'.
