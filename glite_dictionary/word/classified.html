classified

1. ADJ

/ ˈklæs.ɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈklɑː.sɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
arranged according to classes or categories
arranged in categories

Examples

The specimens were classified based on their physical characteristics.
All the documents are neatly classified for easy access.
Students were classified according to their academic performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: categorized - placed in categories
  • Antonym: unclassified - not arranged into classes

Etymology

From the verb 'classify' + -ed.

WordNet ID: oewn-00416228-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈklæsɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈklɑːsɪfaɪd / (BrE)
a small advertisement placed in a newspaper or magazine, grouped with similar ads and typically offering goods or services for sale or seeking to make contact
small newspaper or magazine advertisement

Examples

He placed a classified in the local newspaper to sell his car.
Many people still find jobs by reading the classified section every Sunday.
The classified was brief but generated a lot of interest from potential buyers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classified advertisement - a small ad in a newspaper or magazine, usually grouped by category
  • Antonym: display ad - a larger, more prominent advertisement in a newspaper or magazine

Etymology

From the practice of grouping (classifying) ads by category in publications.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07264253-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈklæsɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈklɑːsɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(of information or documents) officially designated as secret or restricted, and not available to the general public.
officially secret information or documents

Examples

Access to classified material is restricted to authorized personnel only.
The newspaper reported a leak of classified military documents.
Once considered classified, the archive files were released to the public.

Synset Examples

thousands of classified documents have now been declassified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secret - not meant to be known by others; concealed
  • Synonym: confidential - meant to be kept private or secret
  • Antonym: declassified - officially made no longer classified

Etymology

From the verb 'classify' + -ed, originally meaning 'arranged in classes', later 'designated as secret'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00416747-a
Synset POS: a
