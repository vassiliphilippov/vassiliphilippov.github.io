Rubus ursinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəs ɜːrˈsaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs əːˈsaɪnəs / (BrE)
a North American species of blackberry with oblong, black edible fruit
a species of American blackberry

Examples

Rubus ursinus produces some of the sweetest blackberries native to North America.
Gardeners in the Pacific Northwest often cultivate Rubus ursinus for its flavorful fruit.
Wild bears and other animals are known to feed on the berries of Rubus ursinus during summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackberry - an edible soft fruit, black when ripe, from several species of bramble

Etymology

From Latin 'rubus' (bramble, blackberry) and 'ursinus' (of a bear), referring to bears eating the fruit.
