aminopherase

1. NOUN

/ əˌminoʊfəˈreɪs / (AmE), / əˌmiːnəʊfəˈreɪs / (BrE)
an enzyme in the class of transferases that catalyzes the transfer of an amino group from an amino acid to another molecule (transamination)
enzyme catalyzing transamination
medical

Examples

High levels of aminopherase in the blood may indicate liver damage.
Researchers measured the activity of aminopherase in the patient's serum.
The doctor explained that aminopherase assists in amino acid metabolism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transaminase - an enzyme that catalyzes the transfer of an amino group

Etymology

From 'amino' (relating to amino groups) and 'ferase,' a variant of 'phorase,' indicating an enzyme.
