Taxus cuspidata

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪksəs ˌkʌspɪˈdeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈtaksəs ˌkʌspɪˈdeɪtə / (BrE)
a hardy evergreen shrub or small tree native to China and Japan, with glossy dark green foliage, often cultivated as an ornamental plant
Japanese yew, an evergreen shrub or tree

Examples

The dense foliage of the Taxus cuspidata makes it a popular choice for formal hedges and topiary.
Gardeners appreciate Taxus cuspidata for its ability to withstand cold temperatures and urban pollution.
When planted in shade or partial sun, Taxus cuspidata maintains its deep green color throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese yew - common name for Taxus cuspidata, an evergreen shrub or tree native to East Asia

Etymology

Taxus from Latin for 'yew tree,' cuspidata from Latin 'cuspidatus' meaning 'pointed,' referring to the leaf tips.
