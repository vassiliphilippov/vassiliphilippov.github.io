brucine

1. NOUN

/ ˈbruːsiːn / (AmE), / ˈbruːsiːn / (BrE)
a bitter, poisonous alkaloid compound similar to strychnine, extracted from the seeds of nux vomica and related plants
a bitter, poisonous alkaloid compound
medical

Examples

The seeds contain brucine as well as strychnine, both of which are toxic to humans.
Laboratory analysis showed traces of brucine in the suspicious liquid.
Due to its toxicity, handling brucine requires special safety precautions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strychnine - a highly poisonous alkaloid used as a pesticide

Etymology

Named after James Bruce (1730–1794), Scottish explorer.
