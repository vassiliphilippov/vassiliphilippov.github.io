Phalaris aquatica

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɛrɪs əˈkwɑːtɪkə / (AmE), / fəˈlærɪs ɔːˈkwætɪkə / (BrE)
A perennial grass native to Australia and South Africa, commonly cultivated as forage for livestock and introduced to other regions.
a perennial forage grass species

Examples

Phalaris aquatica is often planted in pastures to provide high-quality feed for grazing animals.
Farmers in California frequently harvest Phalaris aquatica for use as silage and hay.
Research has shown that Phalaris aquatica adapts well to a variety of climates and soil types.

Etymology

From genus name Phalaris (Greek φᾰλᾰρίς, a kind of grass) and Latin aquatica ('aquatic, growing near water').
