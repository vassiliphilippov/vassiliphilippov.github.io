succubus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌk.jə.bəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌk.jʊ.bəs / (BrE)
(in folklore) a female demon or supernatural being believed to have sexual intercourse with sleeping men.
female demon in folklore

Examples

Some medieval texts describe the succubus as a beautiful woman luring men in their dreams.
The legend of the succubus has appeared in various cultures for centuries.
He claimed he was visited by a succubus during the night, leaving him drained and terrified.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incubus - a male demon believed to have sexual intercourse with sleeping women

Etymology

From Medieval Latin succuba (paramour), from Latin succubare (to lie under).
