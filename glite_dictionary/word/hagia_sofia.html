Hagia Sofia

1. PROPN

/ ˌhɑːgiə soʊˈfiə / (AmE), / ˌhɑːgiə səʊˈfiːə / (BrE)
A 6th-century masterpiece of Byzantine architecture in Istanbul; originally built as a Christian church, later converted to a mosque, and subsequently to a museum.
historic Byzantine building in Istanbul

Examples

The Hagia Sofia attracts millions of tourists every year due to its rich history and stunning architecture.
During our visit to Istanbul, we marveled at the mosaics inside the Hagia Sofia.
Once a church, then a mosque, and later a museum, the Hagia Sofia reflects the city's diverse heritage.

Etymology

From Greek Ἁγία Σοφία (Hagia Sofia) meaning 'Holy Wisdom'.
