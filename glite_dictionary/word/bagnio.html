bagnio

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæn.jioʊ / (AmE), / ˈbæn.ji.əʊ / (BrE)
A building containing public baths, especially as used historically in Mediterranean countries.
public bathhouse, often historical
archaic

Examples

The old city still has a restored bagnio open to visitors.
Travelers were impressed by the ornate marble of the Ottoman bagnio.
In the 18th century, a bagnio provided both bathing and social opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bathhouse - a building with facilities for bathing, especially communal baths

Etymology

From Italian 'bagno' (bath), from Latin 'balneum' (bath).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæn.jioʊ / (AmE), / ˈban.ji.əʊ / (BrE)
a brothel; a building where prostitutes are available, especially used historically.
historical term for brothel
archaic

Examples

In 18th-century London, it was not uncommon to find a bagnio hidden in a quiet street.
The novel depicts the scandalous activities that took place inside the city's most notorious bagnio.
He was shocked to learn that his lodgings were adjacent to a former bagnio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brothel - a house where people pay to have sex with prostitutes

Etymology

From Italian 'bagno' (bath), later used to mean brothel from associations with bathhouses.
