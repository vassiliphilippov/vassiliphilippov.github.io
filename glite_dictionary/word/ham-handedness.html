ham-handedness

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæmˈhændɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌhæmˈhændɪdnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being clumsy, awkward, or lacking delicacy in handling situations or tasks.
clumsy or awkward manner

Examples

His ham-handedness during negotiations upset the entire team.
Critics often point to the ham-handedness of the film's approach to complex issues.
Her ham-handedness in handling delicate matters caused unnecessary problems.

Synset Examples

What seemed sharp and pointed onstage comes across pedantically in the film, which treats its subject with a clumsy heavy-handedness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsiness - lack of skill or grace in movement or action
  • Antonym: dexterity - skill and grace in physical movement

Etymology

Formed from 'ham-handed' (clumsy, awkward), modeled after phrases like 'heavy-handed', ultimately from 'ham' (meat) + 'handed'.
