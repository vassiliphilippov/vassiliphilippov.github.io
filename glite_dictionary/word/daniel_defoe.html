Daniel Defoe

1. PROPN

/ ˈdænjəl dɪˈfoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdanjəl dɪˈfəʊ / (BrE)
(1659–1731), an English writer best known for his novel Robinson Crusoe and for being a pioneer of the English novel.
English writer, author of Robinson Crusoe

Examples

Daniel Defoe is often credited with writing one of the first English novels, Robinson Crusoe.
Literary scholars frequently analyze the works of Daniel Defoe to explore early novelistic techniques.
Born in London, Daniel Defoe also wrote on economics, politics, and social issues.
