runoff

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈrʌnɒf / (BrE)
A final election held to determine a winner when an earlier vote did not produce a decisive result.
final election to resolve undecided vote

Examples

If no candidate wins a majority, a runoff will be held between the top two finishers.
The runoff election is scheduled for next Tuesday after neither candidate reached the required threshold.
Voters turned out in record numbers for the runoff to choose the new mayor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballot - a process of voting, in writing and typically in secret

Etymology

From the phrase 'run off' (to compete until a winner is determined), used in US political jargon since the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈrʌnɒf / (BrE)
Water or other liquid that flows over a surface because it exceeds the capacity to be absorbed or contained, often after rain.
surplus water flowing over a surface

Examples

Heavy rain caused substantial runoff from the hills into the valley.
The city is working to manage stormwater runoff to prevent flooding.
Farmers often try to reduce runoff by planting cover crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drainage - the process of removing surplus water from an area

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'run off', meaning to flow off or drain away.
