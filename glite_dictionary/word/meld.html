meld

1. NOUN

/ mɛld / (AmE), / mɛld / (BrE)
A card game similar to rummy, played with two decks and four jokers, in which the goal is to form groups of cards of the same rank; jokers and twos are wild cards.
card game like rummy with wild cards

Examples

We spent the evening playing meld with our friends.
Do you know the rules for meld when using two decks?
In meld, jokers and deuces can replace any card in your set.

2. VERB

/ mɛld / (AmE), / mɛld / (BrE)
to blend or combine gradually so that the distinct parts become indistinguishable
blend or merge gradually

Examples

The flavors of the different ingredients slowly meld together as the stew simmers.
Their voices melded in perfect harmony during the choir performance.
Over the years, the town's culture has melded with the city's influence.

Synset Examples

Hundreds of actors were melting into the scene

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blend - to mix together so as to become inseparable
  • Synonym: to integrate - to make part of a whole; to combine
  • Antonym: separate - to set or keep apart; not blend

Etymology

Blend of 'melt' and 'meld' (an obsolete word meaning 'announce').

3. VERB

/ mɛld / (AmE), / mɛld / (BrE)
to announce and display a set of cards, usually for scoring, in certain card games
announce cards for scoring in a card game

Examples

In canasta, players must meld a certain combination of cards to score points.
She decided to meld her wild cards for maximum value during her turn.
After drawing the joker, he quickly melded three aces to win the round.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to declare - to formally announce cards in a card game

Etymology

From German melden (to announce), used in the context of card games.
