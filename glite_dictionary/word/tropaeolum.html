Tropaeolum

1. NOUN

/ troʊˈpiː.ələm / (AmE), / trəʊˈpiː.ələm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American climbing or trailing herbs, often cultivated for their brightly colored flowers; includes nasturtiums.
genus of climbing herbs; includes nasturtiums

Examples

The garden was filled with brightly colored Tropaeolum growing along the fence.
Botanists have classified several ornamental species under the genus Tropaeolum.
She planted Tropaeolum seeds in early spring to add some color to her backyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasturtium - common name for plants of the genus Tropaeolum

Etymology

From Latin 'tropaeolum', diminutive of 'tropaeum' (trophy), so named for the plant's shield-like leaves and helmet-shaped flowers.

2. NOUN

/ troʊˈpiː.ələm / (AmE), / trəˈpiː.ələm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants of the family Tropaeolaceae, native to tropical America, known for their brightly colored, edible flowers and commonly called nasturtiums.
genus of flowering plants (nasturtiums)

Examples

Gardeners often plant Tropaeolum for its vibrant flowers and edible leaves.
The Tropaeolum genus contains several popular ornamental species.
Many salads feature the peppery blossoms of Tropaeolum as a garnish.

Etymology

From Latin *tropaeolum*, derived from Greek *tropaion* (trophy); named for the resemblance of the leaves to shields and flowers to helmets.
