Vulpes vulpes

1. NOUN

/ ˈvʌlpiːz ˈvʌlpiːz / (AmE), / ˈvʌlpiːz ˈvʌlpiːz / (BrE)
The scientific name for the red fox, a widespread Old World species with reddish-brown fur.
scientific name for the red fox

Examples

The species Vulpes vulpes is native to much of Europe and Asia.
Biologists studied the behavior of Vulpes vulpes in its natural habitat.
The fur of Vulpes vulpes is prized for its distinctive reddish color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red fox - a common fox species with reddish-brown fur, Vulpes vulpes

Etymology

From Latin: vulpes meaning 'fox'.
