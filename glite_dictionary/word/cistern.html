cistern

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstɚn / (AmE), / ˈsɪstən / (BrE)
A container or tank for storing water or other liquids, typically built underground or on a roof.
water storage tank or reservoir

Examples

During the drought, the villagers relied on a cistern to collect and store rainwater.
The old mansion has a large cistern beneath it to supply water to the household.
Many modern toilets use a cistern to hold water for flushing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tank - a large container for storing liquid or gas
  • Synonym: reservoir - a large natural or artificial lake used as a source of water supply

Etymology

From Latin 'cisterna', from 'cista' meaning 'box, chest'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstɚn / (AmE), / ˈsɪstən / (BrE)
a tank that stores water, especially one used to supply water for flushing a toilet
toilet water storage tank

Examples

The plumber fixed a leak in the cistern above the toilet.
She pressed the handle, and water from the cistern flushed the bowl clean.
Older houses often have a separate cistern in the bathroom for toilet flushing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tank - a large container for storing liquid or gas
  • Degree Lower: toilet - a fixture for defecation and urination

Etymology

From Latin 'cisterna', from 'cista' (box, chest).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstɚn / (AmE), / ˈsɪstən / (BrE)
a sac or cavity in the body, especially one containing lymph or cerebrospinal fluid
body cavity containing fluid
medical

Examples

In brain anatomy, a cistern is a space where cerebrospinal fluid accumulates.
The doctor explained that the enlarged cistern was pressing on the patient’s nerves.
During the operation, they drained fluid from the subarachnoid cistern.

Etymology

From Latin 'cisterna' (reservoir, tank), from 'cista' (box, chest).
