Anzio

1. PROPN

/ ˈæn.zi.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈan.zi.əʊ / (BrE)
A town on the coast of central Italy, southwest of Rome, known for its strategic importance during World War II when the Allies established a beachhead there in 1944.
town in central Italy; WWII battle site

Examples

The Allies landed at Anzio in January 1944, marking a turning point in the Italian Campaign.
Many war memorials in Anzio commemorate those who fought in the historic battle.
Today, Anzio is a popular seaside destination for both locals and tourists.
