bamboo

1. NOUN

/ bæmˈbu / (AmE), / bæmˈbuː / (BrE)
A tropical woody grass with hollow stems, used for building, furniture, and crafts.
woody tropical grass with hollow stems

Examples

The chairs on the patio are made from bamboo and are both sturdy and light.
He planted a grove of bamboo in his backyard to create a natural screen.
Pandas rely on bamboo as their primary source of food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reed - a tall, slender-leaved plant of the grass family
  • Degree Higher: wood - the hard, fibrous material forming the trunk and branches of trees and shrubs

Etymology

From Portuguese 'bambu', from Malay 'bambu'.

2. NOUN

/ bæmˈbu / (AmE), / bæmˈbuː / (BrE)
the hard woody stem of the bamboo plant, used as a material for construction, crafts, and various products
woody stem of the bamboo plant, used as material

Examples

The roof was supported by thick bamboo poles tied together with rope.
She crafted a beautiful basket out of woven bamboo.
Fishermen along the river still use traditional bamboo rods to catch fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reed - a tall, slender-leaved plant of the grass family, often used in similar contexts as bamboo

Etymology

From Portuguese or Dutch 'bamboe', from Malay 'bambu'.
