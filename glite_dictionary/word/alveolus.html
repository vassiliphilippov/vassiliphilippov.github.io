alveolus

1. NOUN

/ ælˈvioʊləs / (AmE), / ælˈviːələs / (BrE)
A tiny air sac in the lungs where the exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide takes place.
tiny air sac in the lungs
medical

Examples

Oxygen passes from each alveolus into the surrounding blood vessels in the lungs.
Damage to the alveolus can impair breathing in patients with lung disease.
Each alveolus is surrounded by a network of tiny capillaries.

Etymology

From Latin alveolus (small cavity), diminutive of alveus (trough, basin).

2. NOUN

/ ælˈvioʊləs / (AmE), / alˈviːələs / (BrE)
a small bony cavity in the jaw in which the root of a tooth is held
bony tooth socket in the jaw
medical

Examples

The dentist examined the alveolus to check for signs of infection after the tooth was removed.
A fractured alveolus can complicate the healing process after dental surgery.
Bone loss in the alveolus may lead to loosening of the surrounding teeth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: socket - a hollow part or cavity in which something fits

Etymology

From Latin alveolus (small cavity or hollow), diminutive of alveus (trough, basin).
