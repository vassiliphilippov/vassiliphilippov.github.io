serviceability

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɝːvɪsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌsɜːvɪsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to provide good or effective service or performance
ability to provide good service

Examples

The serviceability of the old trucks was questionable after years of rough use.
Engineers tested the serviceability of the new bridge under various weather conditions.
Regular maintenance enhances the serviceability of complex machinery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: usability - the degree to which something is able or fit to be used
  • Synonym: durability - the ability to withstand wear, pressure, or damage
  • Antonym: unserviceable - not able to provide service or no longer fit for use

Etymology

From 'serviceable' + '-ity', patterned after Latin-derived abstract nouns.
