embarrassed

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈbærəst / (AmE), / ɪmˈbærəst / (BrE)
feeling awkward, self-conscious, or ashamed, often due to discomfort or wounded pride
feeling awkward or self-conscious

Examples

She looked embarrassed when everyone sang happy birthday to her at the restaurant.
He was so embarrassed after tripping on stage during the ceremony.
I felt embarrassed to ask such a basic question in front of the whole class.

Synset Examples

too embarrassed to say hello to his drunken father on the street
humiliated that his wife had to go out to work
felt mortified by the comparison with her sister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ashamed - feeling guilt or disgrace about something wrong or improper
  • Synonym: humiliated - feeling a loss of self-respect or dignity
  • Antonym: confident - feeling sure of oneself; not embarrassed or self-conscious

Etymology

From French 'embarrasser' (to hamper, embarrass), ultimately from Italian 'imbarazzare'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈbærəst / (AmE), / ɪmˈbærəst / (BrE)
feeling self-conscious, awkward, or ashamed, often due to something one has done or experienced
feeling self-conscious or ashamed

Examples

She looked embarrassed when her friend mentioned the surprise party in front of everyone.
I always feel embarrassed speaking a new language in public.
He gave an embarrassed smile when he realized his mistake.

Synset Examples

felt abashed at the extravagant praise
chagrined at the poor sales of his book
was embarrassed by her child's tantrums

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ashamed - feeling guilt or embarrassment about something wrong or foolish
  • Antonym: proud - feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction about achievement

Etymology

From French *embarrasser* (to block, hinder), from Italian *imbarazzare*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00533833-s
Synset POS: s
