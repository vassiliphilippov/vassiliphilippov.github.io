disputatiously

1. ADV

/ dɪˌspjuːˈteɪʃəsli / (AmE), / dɪˌspjuːˈteɪʃəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a tendency to dispute or argue; argumentatively or contentiously.
in an argumentative manner

Examples

He spoke disputatiously during the meeting, challenging nearly every proposal presented.
The lawyer questioned the witness disputatiously, often interrupting with objections.
She tends to answer disputatiously when her opinions are challenged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argumentatively - in a manner that shows a tendency to argue

Etymology

From 'disputatious' + '-ly', where 'disputatious' comes from Latin 'disputare' (to discuss, dispute).
