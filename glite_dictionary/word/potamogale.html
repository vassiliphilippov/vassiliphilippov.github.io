Potamogale

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊ.tə.moʊˈɡeɪ.li / (AmE), / ˌpɒt.ə.məˈɡɑː.li / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic mammals in the family Potamogalidae, known as otter shrews, native to Central Africa.
genus of aquatic mammals (otter shrews)

Examples

The Potamogale is adapted for an aquatic lifestyle in African rivers and streams.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Potamogale in Cameroon.
The Potamogale uses its sensitive whiskers to detect prey underwater.

Etymology

From Greek potamos (river) + gala, galaktos (milk), referring to its aquatic nature.
