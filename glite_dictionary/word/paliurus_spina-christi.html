Paliurus spina-christi

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪliˈjʊrəs ˌspaɪnə ˈkrɪstaɪ / (AmE), / ˌpaliˈjʊərəs ˌspɪnə ˈkrɪsti / (BrE)
A thorny shrub native to Eurasia, notable for its dry, woody, winged fruit.
thorny Eurasian shrub with winged fruit

Examples

The gardener planted a Paliurus spina-christi near the fence for its unique appearance.
Some legends claim that Paliurus spina-christi was used to make Christ's crown of thorns.
The Paliurus spina-christi produces distinctive winged fruits that scatter easily in the wind.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Paliurus' and specific epithet 'spina-christi' meaning 'thorn of Christ', referring to the legend that its branches were used for Christ's crown of thorns.
