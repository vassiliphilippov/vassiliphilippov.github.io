Cetorhinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːtəˈraɪnɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌsiːtəˈrɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large filter-feeding sharks, including the basking shark.
family of basking sharks

Examples

The Cetorhinidae family consists primarily of the basking shark, known for its enormous size and gentle nature.
Some older taxonomic classifications placed the basking shark in its own family, Cetorhinidae.
Marine biologists study the Cetorhinidae family to understand more about the evolutionary history of filter-feeding sharks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basking shark - a large, plankton-eating shark (Cetorhinus maximus), the main member of Cetorhinidae

Etymology

From Greek 'ketos' (whale, sea monster) and 'rhinos' (nose), with zoological suffix '-idae' denoting a family.
