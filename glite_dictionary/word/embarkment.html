embarkment

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈbɑrmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɑːkmənt / (BrE)
the act or process of passengers and crew boarding a ship or aircraft
act of boarding a vessel or aircraft

Examples

The embarkment of the cruise passengers began promptly at noon.
Safety procedures were reviewed prior to embarkment on the aircraft.
Delays in embarkment can disrupt the travel schedule for everyone on board.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disembarkation - the act of leaving a ship or aircraft

Etymology

From 'embark' + '-ment'; 'embark' from French 'embarquer', from 'em-' (in) + 'barque' (boat).
