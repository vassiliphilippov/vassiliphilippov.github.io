tabulator

1. NOUN

/ təˈbjuːleɪtər / (AmE), / təˈbjuːleɪtə / (BrE)
a device or system for recording and counting the number of times something occurs
device that counts occurrences

Examples

The tabulator automatically updated the score every time a goal was scored.
Election officials used a tabulator to keep track of votes as they were counted.
He invented a mechanical tabulator that could tally items with impressive speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counter - a person or device that counts things

Etymology

From Latin 'tabula' (a board, tablet) + '-tor' (agent noun suffix), meaning 'one who makes a table or list'.

2. NOUN

/ təˈbjuː.leɪ.tər / (AmE), / təˈbjuː.leɪ.tə / (BrE)
an electromechanical machine used to process and summarize information, especially by sorting and counting data from punched cards
electromechanical data-processing machine

Examples

The old census records were processed by a tabulator that read punched cards.
Herman Hollerith invented the tabulator to speed up data analysis for the U.S. Census.
By the 1950s, many companies relied on a tabulator for their accounting operations.

Synset Examples

In its basic form, a tabulating machine would read one card at a time, print portions (fields) of the card on fan-fold paper, possibly rearranged, and add one or more numbers punched on the card to one or more counters, called accumulators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tabulating machine - a machine that sorts and summarizes data, often from punched cards

Etymology

From Latin 'tabula' (a board, table) + English agent suffix '-or'.
