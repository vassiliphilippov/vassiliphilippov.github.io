journeywoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːniˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːniˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A female skilled worker who has completed an apprenticeship and is fully qualified in a trade or craft.
female qualified tradesperson

Examples

After years as an apprentice, she became a journeywoman in the carpenters' guild.
The journeywoman showed exceptional skill in her metalworking trade.
Many companies now actively encourage female journeywomen to take on supervisory roles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journeyman - a male (or unspecified gender) skilled worker who has completed an apprenticeship
  • Degree Lower: apprentice - a person learning a trade or craft, not yet fully qualified

Etymology

Formed by analogy to 'journeyman', from 'journey' (from Old French 'jornee', day's work) + 'woman'.
