Nikolai Gogol

1. PROPN

/ nɪˈkoʊlaɪ ˈɡoʊɡəl / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəlaɪ ˈɡɒɡɒl / (BrE)
(1809–1852) A Russian writer who introduced realism to Russian literature and is known for works such as 'Dead Souls' and 'The Overcoat'.
Russian writer, pioneer of literary realism

Examples

Many critics regard Nikolai Gogol as the father of Russian realism.
Nikolai Gogol's short story 'The Overcoat' had a profound influence on Russian literature.
Students often study the works of Nikolai Gogol in university Russian literature courses.
