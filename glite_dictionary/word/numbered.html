numbered

1. ADJ

/ ˈnʌmbərd / (AmE), / ˈnʌmbəd / (BrE)
having a number or a series of numbers assigned to something, especially as part of a sequence or for identification.
having a number or numbers assigned

Examples

Each box in the shipment was clearly numbered for easy tracking.
The seats in the theater are numbered from one to one hundred.
The pages of the document were not properly numbered, causing confusion.

Synset Examples

Terminals are numbered from 2 - 4

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unnumbered - not having a number or not assigned a number
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-88306827-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈnʌmbɚd / (AmE), / ˈnʌmbəd / (BrE)
having a limited amount of time or existence remaining; likely to end soon
having a limited or soon-ending existence

Examples

She knew her time as president was numbered once the scandal broke.
With resources numbered, the team had to act quickly before supplies ran out.
His carefree days were numbered after the new regulations were introduced.

Synset Examples

With her days on this earth now surely numbered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finite - having limits or bounds
  • Antonym: unlimited - without any limit or end
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-88121161-a
Synset POS: a
