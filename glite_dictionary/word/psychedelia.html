psychedelia

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkəˈdɛliə / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəˈdiːliə / (BrE)
the subculture, style, and aesthetic associated with the use of psychedelic drugs, especially as developed in the 1960s
subculture/aesthetic linked to psychedelic drugs

Examples

The vibrant colors and swirling patterns are hallmarks of psychedelia in art.
Many bands in the 1960s embraced psychedelia both musically and visually.
The exhibit explores the influence of psychedelia on modern graphic design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychedelic - relating to or denoting drugs that produce hallucinations

Etymology

Formed from 'psychedelic' + '-ia', modeled after Greek
