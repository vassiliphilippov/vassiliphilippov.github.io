final

1. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnəl / (BrE)
(of a decision or result) not able to be changed, undone, or reversed.
not able to be changed or undone

Examples

The judge's decision is final and cannot be appealed.
Once you submit your choices, they become final.
Her resignation was final, with no possibility of return.

Synset Examples

the judge's decision is final
the arbiter will have the last say

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conclusive - putting an end to debate or question, especially by being decisive
  • Antonym: reversible - able to be changed or undone

Etymology

From Latin finalis, from finis meaning 'end'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪ.nəl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪ.nəl / (BrE)
An examination taken at the end of an academic term to assess a student's understanding of the course material.
end-of-term examination

Examples

I have to study all weekend for my final in chemistry.
After completing her last final, she felt relieved and excited for the holidays.
The professor announced that the final would cover all the material we learned this semester.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: midterm - an examination administered in the middle of an academic term

Etymology

From Latin 'finalis', from 'finis' (end).
