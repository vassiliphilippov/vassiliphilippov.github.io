waistcloth

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪstk.lɔθ / (AmE), / ˈweɪs(t)klɒθ / (BrE)
a band of cloth worn or fastened around the waist, especially to strengthen or hold up a skirt or trousers
band of cloth around the waist

Examples

He tied a waistcloth tightly around his middle to keep his trousers up.
The costume was completed with a decorative waistcloth matching the dancer’s shirt.
After the repairs, she added a new waistcloth to reinforce the waistband of her skirt.

Etymology

From 'waist' + 'cloth', referring to cloth worn at the waist.
