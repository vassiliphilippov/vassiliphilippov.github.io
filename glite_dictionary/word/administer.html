to administer

1. VERB

/ ədˈmɪnɪstɚ / (AmE), / ədˈmɪnɪstə / (BrE)
to give or distribute (something), especially in measured amounts or portions
give or distribute in portions

Examples

The teacher administered encouragement sparingly throughout the class.
Nurses administer medication to patients at specific intervals.
He administered harsh criticism only when necessary.

Synset Examples

administer critical remarks to everyone present
dole out some money
shell out pocket money for the children
deal a blow to someone
the machine dispenses soft drinks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dispense - to distribute or provide (a service or item)
  • Synonym: to dole out - to give out shares of something, especially money or food

Etymology

From Latin administrare, meaning 'to manage, to attend to'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02299397-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ədˈmɪnəstɚ / (AmE), / ədˈmɪnɪstə / (BrE)
to direct or oversee the formal giving or taking of something, such as an exam or oath
direct or oversee the giving/taking of something formal

Examples

The teacher will administer the final exam this Friday.
Only a judge can administer the oath of office to the new mayor.
A proctor was assigned to administer the standardized tests to the students.

Synset Examples

administer an exam
administer an oath

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to supervise - to oversee an activity or process

Etymology

From Latin administrare (to manage, carry out), from ad- (to) + ministrare (to serve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02609574-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ədˈmɪnɪstɚ / (AmE), / ədˈmɪnɪstə / (BrE)
to perform a church sacrament, especially in a formal or ritual manner
conduct a church sacrament ritually

Examples

The priest was called to administer the last rites to the patient.
Only ordained clergy are allowed to administer the sacrament of communion in this church.
During the ceremony, the bishop will administer confirmation to the candidates.

Synset Examples

administer the last unction

Etymology

From Latin 'administrare' (to manage, to carry out).

4. VERB

/ ədˈmɪn.ə.stɚ / (AmE), / ədˈmɪn.ɪ.stə / (BrE)
to manage or be responsible for the operation or execution of something, such as a business, organization, or program
manage or supervise as an administrator

Examples

She was hired to administer the new community outreach program.
The principal administers several after-school activities.
It is important to administer the funds fairly and transparently.

Synset Examples

administer a program
she administers the funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to manage - to be in charge of, to control or direct
  • Synonym: to supervise - to oversee or direct the work of others

Etymology

From Latin administrare (to manage, serve).

Source: WordNet

5. VERB

/ ədˈmɪnɪstɚ / (AmE), / ədˈmɪnɪstə / (BrE)
to give or apply (a drug or treatment) to someone, especially as part of medical care
give or apply medications

Examples

The nurse will administer the medication every morning before breakfast.
Doctors must be careful when they administer anesthetics to patients.
In an emergency, anyone trained can administer first aid until help arrives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dispense - give out medication as prescribed
  • Antonym: to withhold - refuse to give (something)

Etymology

From Latin administrare, meaning 'to manage' or 'to serve.'

Source: WordNet
