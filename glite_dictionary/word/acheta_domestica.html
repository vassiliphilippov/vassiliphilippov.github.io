Acheta domestica

1. NOUN

/ əˈkiːtə doʊˈmɛstɪkə / (AmE), / əˈkiːtə dəʊˈmɛstɪkə / (BrE)
a species of cricket commonly found in houses and buildings, known as the house cricket
species of house cricket

Examples

The species Acheta domestica has adapted well to life alongside humans.
Researchers often use Acheta domestica in laboratory studies of insect behavior.
The distinctive chirping in the kitchen was traced to a single Acheta domestica hiding behind the fridge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: house cricket - the common name for Acheta domestica, an insect found in human dwellings

Etymology

From Latin: Acheta (a genus of crickets) and domestica (domestic).
