kumis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːmɪs / (AmE), / ˈkuːmɪs / (BrE)
a fermented alcoholic beverage made from mare's milk, traditionally prepared by Central Asian nomads
fermented mare's milk drink

Examples

Traditional Mongolian celebrations often include a bowl of kumis shared among guests.
Historians note that kumis was consumed by Genghis Khan's warriors.
Some adventurous travelers enjoy tasting kumis during their visits to Kyrgyzstan.

Etymology

From Russian 'kumys', from Kazakh 'қымыз' (qymyz), referring to the traditional drink.
