oleaginousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊliˈædʒənəsnəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊliˈadʒɪnəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being oily, greasy, or having the characteristics of oil
the quality of being oily or greasy

Examples

The oleaginousness of the dish left a greasy aftertaste on the palate.
His hands were stained with the oleaginousness of engine oil after repairing the car.
The artist captured the oleaginousness of the paint in her impressionist landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oiliness - the state of being oily

Etymology

From Latin 'oleaginous', from 'olea' (olive) + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊliˈædʒənəsnəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊliˈædʒɪnəsnəs / (BrE)
a manner or quality of being unpleasantly and insincerely earnest or ingratiating; unctuousness
insincere or ingratiating earnestness

Examples

His oleaginousness made everyone at the meeting uncomfortable.
The salesperson’s oleaginousness was obvious as he complimented every customer.
She saw through the politician’s oleaginousness and remained unimpressed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unctuousness - insincere earnestness or ingratiating behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'oleagineus' (of the olive tree), via oleaginous + -ness.
