serenity

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɛnəti / (AmE), / sɪˈrɛnɪti / (BrE)
the state of being calm, peaceful, and untroubled
state of calm or peace

Examples

She admired the serenity of the quiet lakeside at dawn.
Despite the chaos around him, his face radiated serenity.
Meditation helped her achieve a sense of serenity in her busy life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tranquility - the quality or state of being tranquil; calm
  • Antonym: agitation - a state of nervousness or unrest

Etymology

From Latin 'serenitas' (clearness, serenity), from 'serenus' (clear, calm).

2. NOUN

/ səˈrɛn.ə.ti / (AmE), / səˈrɛn.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being calm, peaceful, and untroubled; the absence of stress or anxiety.
calmness; peacefulness

Examples

She found serenity in the quiet garden after a hectic day.
Meditation is often practiced to achieve greater serenity of mind.
His face radiated serenity despite the chaos around him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tranquillity - the quality or state of being tranquil; calm
  • Antonym: agitation - a state of anxiety or nervous excitement

Etymology

From Latin 'serenitas', from 'serenus' meaning clear, calm.
