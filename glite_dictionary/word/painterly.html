painterly

1. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪntərli / (AmE), / ˈpeɪntəli / (BrE)
having qualities characteristic of painting, such as visible brushstrokes, texture, or color blending, often emphasizing the medium and technique rather than precise detail
having qualities of painting, like brushwork or texture

Examples

The artist's landscapes are distinctly painterly, with broad, expressive strokes.
Her photographs have a painterly quality that blurs the line between photo and painting.
Critics praised the painterly effects created through strong color contrasts and texture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: graphic - marked by clear outline and detail (as contrasted with painterly)

Etymology

From English 'painter' + '-ly', meaning 'having the qualities of a painter or painting'.
