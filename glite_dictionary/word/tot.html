tot

1. NOUN

/ tɑt / (AmE), / tɒt / (BrE)
a small amount of a strong alcoholic drink, usually served as a single measure
a small amount of strong drink

Examples

He poured himself a tot of whiskey before bed.
The sailor requested a tot of rum to warm up.
Would you like a tot with your coffee?

Synset Examples

a tot of rum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nip - a small quantity of strong alcoholic drink
  • Synonym: shot - a small amount of alcohol served in one measure

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ tɑt / (AmE), / tɒt / (BrE)
a small child, especially one who has just begun to walk
a young child, toddler

Examples

The playground was filled with tots running and laughing.
She read a picture book to the tot sitting on her lap.
That tot took his first steps yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toddler - a young child who is just beginning to walk
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10734114-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ tɑt / (AmE), / tɒt / (BrE)
to add up or calculate the total of a set of numbers or amounts, especially in the phrase 'tot up'
add up; calculate a total

Examples

Can you tot these expenses and see what the total comes to?
She totted up the figures at the end of the day to balance the accounts.
We need to tot up how many guests have RSVP'd before finalizing the order.

Synset Examples

Add all the people in this town to those of the neighboring town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to add - to find the sum of numbers or amounts
  • Antonym: to subtract - to take away a number from another

Etymology

Imitative; likely from the idea of 'dotting' or marking tallies.
