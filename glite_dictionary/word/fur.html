fur

1. NOUN

/ fɝː / (AmE), / fɜː / (BrE)
the thick, soft hair covering the skin of many mammals, or the skin of such an animal with the hair still on it
soft hair covering of mammals

Examples

The cat's fur was soft and warm to the touch.
Polar bears have thick fur to keep them insulated in the Arctic cold.
She wore a coat made of artificial fur during the winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hair - threadlike strands growing from the skin of humans or animals

Etymology

From Old French *fourre* (sheath, covering), from *fourrer* (to line, sheath).

2. NOUN

/ fɝ / (AmE), / fɜː / (BrE)
a coat or other garment made from the pelts of animals or from synthetic material that imitates animal fur
a garment made from animal pelts or imitation

Examples

She stepped out in her luxurious fur despite the mild weather.
The actress arrived at the premiere wearing an elegant fur.
He donated his old fur to a charity supporting the homeless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mink - a coat made from or resembling mink fur

Etymology

From Old French *fourrer* (to line or cover with fur), from *fourre* (sheath, covering).

3. NOUN

/ fɝː / (AmE), / fɜː / (BrE)
the soft, thick, hairy coat of a mammal, especially when prepared for use as clothing or decoration
the soft hairy coat of a mammal, used for clothing

Examples

She bought a luxurious fur coat for the winter.
Rabbits are often bred for their soft fur.
The fur on the cat felt silky and warm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelt - the skin of an animal with hair or fur still attached
  • Synonym: skin - the outer covering of an animal, including the fur, wool, or hair
  • Antonym: leather - animal skin prepared for use without hair or fur

Etymology

From Middle English *furre*, from Old French *forrer* (to line, cover), ultimately from Germanic origin.
