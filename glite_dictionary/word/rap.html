rap

1. NOUN

/ ræp / (AmE), / ræp / (BrE)
a sharp or gentle knocking or tapping sound, especially one made by striking a surface lightly
a gentle knocking sound

Examples

She heard a soft rap at the door late at night.
A sudden rap on the window startled the cat.
He gave a quick rap with his knuckles to get their attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knock - a sharp sound made by hitting or striking something

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound produced by a quick blow.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ræp / (AmE), / ræp / (BrE)
an accusation or blame for a mistake or wrongdoing, especially in a legal or informal context
accusation or blame for a misdeed

Examples

Many believe she took the rap for a crime she didn't commit.
He always seems to get a bad rap even when he's innocent.
The judge said it was an unfair rap given the lack of evidence.

Synset Examples

he took the blame for it
it was a bum rap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blame - responsibility for a fault or wrong
  • Antonym: acquittal - a judgment that a person is not guilty of the crime

Etymology

Originally from Middle English *rappe* (a blow, stroke); metaphorically extended to 'rebuke, accusation' in the late 19th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

7. VERB

/ ræp / (AmE), / rap / (BrE)
to perform rap music by speaking lyrics rhythmically over a beat, often with rhyming verse
perform rap music

Examples

He started to rap at open-mic nights when he was just sixteen.
The group will rap their new single at the concert tonight.
She rapped about her experiences growing up in the city.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to sing - to make musical sounds with the voice, especially words with a set tune
  • Synonym: MC - to perform as a master of ceremonies or rapper, especially in hip-hop culture

Etymology

Originated in African American English, possibly from earlier slang 'to talk' or 'to discuss', ultimately from the sense of 'to strike quickly.'

8. NOUN

/ ræp / (AmE), / ræp / (BrE)
A style of popular music characterized by spoken or chanted rhyming lyrics set to a strong rhythmic accompaniment, originating within African-American communities in the 1980s and 1990s.
style of music with rhythmic spoken lyrics

Examples

Rap has become one of the most influential genres in modern music.
He prefers old-school rap to contemporary pop songs.
Many artists began their careers performing rap in small local clubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hip-hop - cultural movement and musical style related to rap

Etymology

Shortened from 'rap music', from African-American Vernacular English 'rap' (to talk or speak), ultimately from English 'rap' (to strike or hit, figuratively to talk).
