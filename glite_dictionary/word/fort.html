fort

1. NOUN

/ fɔrt / (AmE), / fɔːt / (BrE)
A strong building or group of buildings designed and built for military defense against attack.
military defensive structure

Examples

The soldiers stationed themselves in the fort to defend against the enemy attack.
Many historic forts can be visited throughout Europe and North America today.
Children built a small fort out of pillows and blankets in the living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castle - a large fortified building or set of buildings, often the residence of a noble
  • Synonym: bastion - a projecting part of a fortification
  • Synonym: outpost - a small military camp or position at some distance from the main force

Etymology

From Latin 'fortis' (strong), via Old French 'fort'.

2. VERB

/ fɔrt / (AmE), / fɔːt / (BrE)
to gather in, or as if in, a fort for protection or defense
gather for protection as in a fort
archaic

Examples

During the siege, the villagers forted themselves in the church, hoping for rescue.
As the storm approached, the travelers forted up in a stone shelter for safety.
The general ordered his troops to fort in the ruined castle until reinforcements arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fortify - to make strong or stronger, especially by military defenses

Etymology

From Old French *fort* (strong, fortified place), from Latin *fortis* (strong).

/ fɔrt / (AmE), / fɔːt / (BrE)
to enclose or protect with a fortification or similar defensive structure
enclose or protect with a fortification

Examples

They spent the afternoon forting their campsite with logs and stones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fortify - to strengthen a place with defensive works

Etymology

From the noun 'fort', ultimately from Latin 'fortis' (strong) via Old French 'fort'.

4. NOUN

/ fɔrt / (AmE), / fɔːt / (BrE)
A fortified building or place occupied by military troops and equipped for defense against attack.
fortified military building or post

Examples

The soldiers stationed at the fort patrolled its walls every morning.
Built on the hill, the fort offered a strategic view of the surrounding area.
The old stone fort is now a popular tourist attraction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortress - a large and heavily protected fort or defensive building

Etymology

From Latin 'fortis' (strong) via French 'fort' (stronghold, fortification).
