gerbil

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝː.bəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜː.bɪl / (BrE)
A small desert-dwelling rodent with soft pale fur and long hind legs, adapted for leaping, native to Africa and Asia; often kept as a pet.
small desert rodent; common pet

Examples

The children watched the gerbil run through its tunnel in the cage.
She chose a gerbil as her first pet because they are easy to care for.
A gerbil's long hind legs help it jump quickly across the sand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hamster - small rodent often kept as a pet
  • Synonym: mouse - small rodent of the genus Mus

Etymology

From French *gerbille*, from Modern Latin *Gerbillus*, derived from Arabic *jarbūʾ* (small mouse).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːbəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːbɪl / (BrE)
a small, burrowing rodent native to desert regions, with soft fur and long hind legs adapted for leaping
a small desert-dwelling rodent

Examples

My son keeps a gerbil in a cage in his bedroom and feeds it every morning.
The gerbil quickly dug a tunnel in its new sand-filled habitat.
Scientists study the gerbil because it thrives in dry conditions similar to deserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouse - a small rodent with a pointed snout and a long, sparsely haired tail
  • Synonym: hamster - a small rodent with a short tail and large cheek pouches

Etymology

From French 'gerbille', diminutive of 'gerbe', from Medieval Latin 'gerbo', from Arabic 'jarbū‘' (jumping rodent).
