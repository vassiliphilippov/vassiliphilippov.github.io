caftan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæf.tæn / (AmE), / ˈkɑːf.tæn / (BrE)
A long, loose robe with full sleeves and a sash, traditionally worn in the Middle East and nearby regions.
long, loose robe

Examples

The merchant wore a richly embroidered caftan that shimmered in the sunlight.
She bought a colorful caftan at the bazaar during her travels in Turkey.
His blue silk caftan brushed against the floor as he entered the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kaftan - alternative spelling for caftan, a long, loose robe

Etymology

From Turkish 'kaftan', from Persian 'khaftān'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæf.tæn / (AmE), / ˈkæf.tæn / (BrE)
a long, loose-fitting dress for women, modeled after the traditional caftan cloak worn by men in the Near East
long, loose women's dress inspired by Near Eastern caftans

Examples

She wore a brightly colored caftan to the beach party.
The designer's new collection features silk caftans in bold patterns.
My grandmother brought a beautiful Moroccan caftan back from her travels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kaftan - variant spelling of 'caftan'

Etymology

From Turkish 'kaftan', from Persian 'khaftān', referring to a long garment.
