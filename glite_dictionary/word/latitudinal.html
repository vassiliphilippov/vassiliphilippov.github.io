latitudinal

1. ADJ

/ ˌlætɪˈtjuːdnəl / (AmE), / ˌlætɪˈtjuːdɪnəl / (BrE)
relating to or measured in the direction of latitude, or pertaining to latitudes north or south
relating to latitude or latitudinal direction

Examples

The scientist examined the latitudinal distribution of plant species across the continent.
There are significant latitudinal differences in climate between the equator and the poles.
Researchers used latitudinal lines to map the global temperature variations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: longitudinal - relating to or measured in the direction of longitude

Etymology

From 'latitude' + '-al', after Medieval Latin 'latitudinalis'.
