thunderous

1. ADJ

/ ˈθʌndɚəs / (AmE), / ˈθʌn.dər.əs / (BrE)
extremely ominous, powerful, or serious, suggesting the impact or foreboding nature of thunder.
extremely ominous or impactful

Examples

The president's resignation was met with thunderous silence across the nation.
Rumors of war gave the markets a thunderous sense of dread.
The courtroom waited in thunderous expectation for the verdict to be announced.

Synset Examples

world events of thunderous import

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ominous - giving the impression that something bad is going to happen

Etymology

From thunder + -ous, meaning full of or resembling thunder.

2. ADJ

/ ˈθʌndɚəs / (AmE), / ˈθʌndərəs / (BrE)
(of a sound) extremely loud and powerful, resembling or suggestive of thunder.
extremely loud, like thunder

Examples

The thunderous applause at the concert left my ears ringing for minutes afterward.
A thunderous roar erupted from the crowd as the team scored the winning goal.
The thunderous crash of the demolition sent vibrations through the entire building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deafening - (of a sound) so loud as to make hearing difficult or impossible
  • Antonym: quiet - making little or no noise

Etymology

From thunder + -ous, meaning 'full of or characterized by thunder'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01456246-s
Synset POS: s
