humidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuː.mɪd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhjuː.mɪd.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being humid; the presence of moisture in the air
moisture or dampness in the air

Examples

The humidness in the room made it feel much warmer than it actually was.
After the rain, the humidness in the air was almost unbearable.
Plants thrive in environments with high humidness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humidity - the amount of water vapor in the air

Etymology

From Latin 'humidus' (moist, wet) + English suffix '-ness.'
