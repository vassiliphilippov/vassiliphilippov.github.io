chickenfeed

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪk.ɪnˌfid / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.ɪnˌfiːd / (BrE)
a very small or insignificant amount of money; a trivial sum
a trivial sum of money
informal

Examples

They offered him chickenfeed for all his hard work.
The prize money was chickenfeed compared to other competitions.
She sold her old car for chickenfeed just to get rid of it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pittance - a very small or inadequate amount of money paid to someone
  • Antonym: fortune - a very large amount of money

Etymology

Derived from the literal meaning 'feed for chickens', used figuratively to denote something very small or insignificant.
