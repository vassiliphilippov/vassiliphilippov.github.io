Llullaillaco

1. PROPN

/ juːˌjaɪˈjɑːkoʊ / (AmE), / juːˌjaɪˈjɑːkəʊ / (BrE)
a mountain in the Andes on the border between Argentina and Chile, rising to 22,057 feet (6,739 meters)
Andean mountain on Argentina-Chile border

Examples

The summit of Llullaillaco is one of the highest archaeological sites in the world.
Climbers attempting Llullaillaco must be prepared for extreme altitude and weather conditions.
Llullaillaco is famous for the discovery of several Inca mummies near its peak.

Etymology

From Quechua llulla (false, lie) and yaku (water), meaning 'deceitful water'.
