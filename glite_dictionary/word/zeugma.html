zeugma

1. NOUN

/ ˈzuɡmə / (AmE), / ˈzjuːɡmə / (BrE)
A figure of speech in which a word, usually a verb or adjective, applies to more than one noun, blending together grammatically and logically different ideas, often for a humorous or rhetorical effect.
rhetorical figure joining words differently

Examples

Shakespeare cleverly uses zeugma in several of his plays to achieve comic effect.
The line 'He stole my heart and my wallet' is an example of zeugma.
Writers often employ zeugma to make their language more vivid and engaging.

Synset Examples

‘Mr. Pickwick took his hat and his leave’ is an example of zeugma

Etymology

From Greek *zeugma* (a yoking, bond), from *zeugnynai* (to yoke).
