gallantry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæləntri / (AmE), / ˈɡæləntri / (BrE)
polite attention and respect shown by men to women, especially in a formal or old-fashioned manner.
attentive politeness, esp. by men to women

Examples

He showed remarkable gallantry by opening the door for her in the pouring rain.
Her grandmother admired the old-fashioned gallantry of gentlemen from her youth.
Though outdated today, such gallantry was once expected in polite society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chivalry - courteous behavior, especially by men towards women

Etymology

From French 'galanterie', from 'galant' (courteous, polite).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæləntri / (AmE), / ˈɡæləntri / (BrE)
heroic or exceptional courage and bravery, especially in dangerous or difficult situations such as battle
heroic bravery, especially in battle

Examples

The soldier was awarded a medal for his gallantry during the fierce battle.
Her gallantry in the face of overwhelming odds inspired everyone around her.
The leader's gallantry became legendary among his comrades.

Synset Examples

he showed great heroism in battle
he received a medal for valor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bravery - courageous behavior or character
  • Synonym: valor - great courage in the face of danger, especially in battle
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of bravery

Etymology

From Old French *gallanterie*, from *galant* (courteous, brave, spirited), related to *galer* (to make merry).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡæləntri / (AmE), / ˈɡæləntri / (BrE)
polite and attentive behavior shown by men towards women, often characterized by acts of courtesy or chivalry
polite attention or courtesy towards women

Examples

He was admired for his gallantry in always opening doors for others.
The knight's gallantry toward the ladies impressed everyone at the court.
Her grandfather's old-fashioned gallantry made him popular at social gatherings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chivalry - courteous and honorable behavior, especially by men towards women
  • Antonym: rudeness - lack of manners or courtesy

Etymology

From French *galanterie*, from *galant* (gallant, courteous),
