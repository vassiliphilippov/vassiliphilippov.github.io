epicentre

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌsɛntər / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪsɛntə / (BrE)
the point on the Earth's surface directly above the focus of an earthquake
surface point above an earthquake's focus

Examples

The city lies just 20 kilometers from the epicentre of the recent earthquake.
Rescue teams rushed to the epicentre to assess the damage and help survivors.
News reports indicated that the epicentre was located offshore, minimizing damage to populated areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - the location inside the Earth where an earthquake originates

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + kentron (center).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌsɛntər / (AmE), / ˈepɪsɛntə / (BrE)
The point on the Earth's surface directly above the origin of an earthquake, or the central point of something, especially a disaster or activity.
central point of an earthquake or disaster

Examples

Scientists determined the epicentre of the quake was just outside the city.
The relief teams rushed to the epicentre of the flooding to help survivors.
The city became the epicentre of the pandemic as cases surged.

Synset Examples

They set up a relief center at the epicentre of the disaster.
The epicentre of the earthquake was near the coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - The actual point beneath the Earth's surface where an earthquake originates

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + kentron (centre, point).
