to ptyalize

1. VERB

/ ˈtaɪəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtʌɪ.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
to expel saliva, phlegm, or sputum from the mouth
expel saliva from the mouth
medical

Examples

When irritated by the medication, some patients may ptyalize more frequently.
The dentist asked the patient not to ptyalize during the procedure.
Certain animals ptyalize as a defense mechanism when threatened.

Synset Examples

The father of the victim spat at the alleged murderer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spit - to eject saliva forcibly from the mouth
  • Synonym: to expectorate - to cough or spit out phlegm from the throat or lungs

Etymology

From Greek *ptyalon* (saliva) + -ize.
