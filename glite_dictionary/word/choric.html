choric

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒrɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a chorus, especially a Greek chorus in drama; written for or in the style of a chorus.
relating to a chorus (esp. Greek drama)

Examples

The director aimed for a choric effect in the tragedy by involving the entire cast at key moments.
Many scholars analyze the choric passages to understand the role of the Greek chorus in ancient theater.
His poetry sometimes adopts a choric voice, commenting on the action from a collective perspective.

Synset Examples

a choric Greek tragedy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choral - relating to a chorus or choir

Etymology

From Greek 'khorikos', from 'khoros' (chorus).
