pushpin

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊʃˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈpʊʃˌpɪn / (BrE)
A short pin with a rounded head used for attaching papers to a bulletin board or similar surface.
pin for attaching papers to board

Examples

She secured the notice to the board with a pushpin.
There's a red pushpin marking our location on the map.
Carefully remove each pushpin to avoid tearing the paper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thumbtack - a tack with a flat, broad head used for fastening papers to a board

Etymology

Formed from "push" + "pin", referring to a pin pushed in by hand.
