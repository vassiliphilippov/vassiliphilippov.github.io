Lake Baikal

1. PROPN

/ leɪk baɪˈkɑl / (AmE), / leɪk baɪˈkɑːl / (BrE)
a large lake in southern Siberia, Russia, noted as the deepest lake in the world and the largest freshwater lake by volume in Asia or Europe
deepest and largest freshwater lake in Siberia, Russia

Examples

Scientists have discovered many unique species living in Lake Baikal.
Tourists flock to Lake Baikal in winter to marvel at its crystal-clear ice and dramatic scenery.
As the world's deepest freshwater lake, Lake Baikal holds about 20% of the planet's unfrozen fresh water.

Etymology

From Russian Байкал (Bajkal), of Turkic origin possibly meaning 'rich lake' or 'nature lake'.
