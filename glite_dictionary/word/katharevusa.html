Katharevusa

1. NOUN

/ kɑːˌθɑːrɛˈvusə / (AmE), / kaθəˈrɛvʊsə / (BrE)
A literary form of Modern Greek that incorporates elements from Ancient and Koine Greek, used especially in formal writing before the 20th century.
literary form of Modern Greek

Examples

Many official documents in Greece were once written in Katharevusa rather than the vernacular.
The debate between Katharevusa and Demotic Greek shaped modern Greek linguistic identity.
Few people today use Katharevusa outside of academic or historical contexts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Demotic - the colloquial form of Modern Greek

Etymology

From Greek καθαρεύουσα (katharevousa), meaning 'purified language'.

2. NOUN

/ kɑˌθɑːrəˈvuːsə / (AmE), / kaˌθaːrəˈvuːsə / (BrE)
a literary and formal variety of Modern Greek, developed during the 19th and 20th centuries, which incorporates features from Classical and Koine Greek.
literary/formal variety of Modern Greek

Examples

The government once mandated official documents to be written in Katharevusa instead of everyday Greek.
Many authors preferred Katharevusa for poetry and academic works in the early 20th century.
Debates about language policy in Greece often centered on the use of Katharevusa versus Demotic Greek.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Demotic Greek - the colloquial, everyday form of Modern Greek

Etymology

From Greek καθαρεύουσα (katharevousa), meaning 'purified.'
