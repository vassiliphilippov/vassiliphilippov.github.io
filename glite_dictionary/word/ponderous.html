ponderous

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːndərəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒndərəs / (BrE)
slow and clumsy because of great weight; having great mass and unwieldiness.
very heavy and unwieldy

Examples

The workers struggled to move the ponderous machinery into place.
With a ponderous sigh, the old dog lay down beside the fire.
The ancient tome had a ponderous leather cover that made it difficult to lift.

Synset Examples

a ponderous stone
a ponderous burden
ponderous weapons

Related Terms

  • Antonym: light - having little weight; not heavy

Etymology

From Latin ponderosus (having weight), from pondus (weight).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːndərəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒndərəs / (BrE)
slow and clumsy because of great weight; dull, laborious, or excessively solemn.
slow and heavy due to weight

Examples

The ponderous elephant trudged slowly through the muddy field.
His ponderous footsteps echoed as he climbed the stairs after a long day.
The movie became ponderous and difficult to follow after the first hour.

Synset Examples

the heavy tread of tired troops
moved with a lumbering sag-bellied trot
ponderous prehistoric beasts
a ponderous yawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heavy - having great weight; difficult to lift or move
  • Antonym: light - having little weight; easy to move

Etymology

From Latin 'ponderosus' (full of weight), from 'pondus' (weight).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01196329-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːndərəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒndərəs / (BrE)
(of speech, writing, or movement) dull or laborious; lacking lightness or liveliness; slow and awkward.
dull and laborious, lacking liveliness

Examples

The professor's ponderous lecture put several students to sleep within the first half hour.
Her writing style has improved; it is no longer as ponderous and tedious as before.
The meeting dragged on with ponderous discussions that failed to capture anyone's attention.

Synset Examples

a ponderous speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heavy - slow and lacking grace or liveliness
  • Antonym: graceful - showing elegance or lightness in movement or style

Etymology

From Latin ponderosus (heavy), from pondus (weight).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
