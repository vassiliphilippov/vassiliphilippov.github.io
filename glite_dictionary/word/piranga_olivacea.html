Piranga olivacea

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈræŋɡə oʊˌlɪˈveɪsiə / (AmE), / pɪˈræŋɡə ɒˌlɪˈveɪsiə / (BrE)
The scientific name of the scarlet tanager, a North American songbird species whose male has bright red plumage with black wings and tail.
scientific name for scarlet tanager bird

Examples

The male Piranga olivacea is easily recognized by its bright red body and contrasting black wings.
Many birdwatchers travel in spring to catch a glimpse of Piranga olivacea during migration.
The song of the Piranga olivacea can be heard in deciduous forests across eastern North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarlet tanager - a North American songbird known for the bright red plumage of the male

Etymology

From Latin: 'Piranga', a genus of songbird, and 'olivacea', meaning 'olive-colored', originally referring to the female's plumage.
