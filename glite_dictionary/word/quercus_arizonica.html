Quercus arizonica

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs ˌærɪˈzoʊnɪkə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˌærɪˈzəʊnɪkə / (BrE)
A semi-evergreen shrub or small tree native to Arizona and New Mexico, known for its acorns with hemispherical cups; commonly called Arizona white oak.
species of oak native to Arizona and New Mexico

Examples

The dry slopes were dotted with Quercus arizonica, their grayish-green leaves rustling in the warm breeze.
Researchers studied the distribution of Quercus arizonica in the mountainous regions of New Mexico.
The park ranger identified the tree as Quercus arizonica by examining its distinctive acorn cups.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' meaning 'oak' and 'arizonica' referring to Arizona, its region of discovery.
