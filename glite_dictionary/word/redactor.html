redactor

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdæktər / (AmE), / rɪˈdaktə / (BrE)
a person who edits or prepares text for publication, often shaping its form or structure
person who edits text for publication

Examples

The redactor reviewed every article before sending it to print.
As a skilled redactor, she ensured the manuscript met all editorial standards.
The historian credited the redactor for shaping the final version of the ancient text.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: editor - a person who edits text or media for publication

Etymology

From Latin 'redactor', from 'redigere' (to bring back, reduce).
