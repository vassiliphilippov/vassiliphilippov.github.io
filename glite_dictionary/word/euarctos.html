Euarctos

1. NOUN

/ juˈɑːrktɒs / (AmE), / juˈɑːktɒs / (BrE)
A former genus of American black bears, sometimes not distinguished from Ursus in some classifications.
former genus of American black bears

Examples

Some paleontologists still use Euarctos to describe certain fossil black bear species.
The taxonomic status of Euarctos has been debated among zoologists for decades.
Modern classifications often merge Euarctos into the genus Ursus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ursus - the genus including bears such as the brown bear and polar bear

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' meaning 'good' and 'arktos' meaning 'bear'.

2. NOUN

/ juˈɑːrk.təs / (AmE), / juːˈɑːk.tɒs / (BrE)
a genus of American black bears; in some classifications, not considered separate from Ursus.
genus of American black bears

Examples

Some biologists classify the American black bear in the genus Euarctos rather than Ursus.
The fossil record of Euarctos is important for understanding bear evolution in North America.
Debates continue over whether Euarctos should be recognized as a separate genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ursus - genus including brown, black, and polar bears

Etymology

From Greek eu- (good) + arktos (bear).
