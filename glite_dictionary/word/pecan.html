pecan

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈkɑːn / (AmE), / pɪˈkæn / (BrE)
a smooth, oval, brown nut from a tree native to the southern United States, with a sweet edible kernel
type of nut from the southern US

Examples

She sprinkled chopped pecan on top of the ice cream for extra crunch.
The pecan harvest begins in late autumn in the southern states.
My grandmother's pecan pie is famous at family gatherings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walnut - a similar edible nut from a different tree
  • Synonym: pecan tree - the tree that produces pecan nuts

Etymology

From Algonquian (Illinois) *pakani* (nut).

2. NOUN

/ pɪˈkɑn / (AmE), / pɪˈkæn / (BrE)
A tall tree of the southern United States and Mexico, cultivated for its edible nuts with a smooth, thin shell.
tree cultivated for edible nuts

Examples

The orchard was filled with old pecan trees that produced a rich harvest every autumn.
A single large pecan tree shaded the farmhouse from the summer sun.
In southern states, pecan trees are often planted for both their beauty and their valuable nuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hickory - a type of tree related to pecan, producing hard-shelled nuts

Etymology

From Algonquian (Illinois) *pakani* or similar word for a nut of this kind.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-12341594-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ pɪˈkɑːn, ˈpiˌkæn / (AmE), / pɪˈkɑːn, ˈpiːkæn / (BrE)
The wood of the pecan tree, valued for its hardness and used in furniture and flooring.
wood from a pecan tree

Examples

The flooring was made of polished pecan, giving the room a warm, rich color.
They chose pecan for the cabinets because of its durability and attractive grain.
The craftsman preferred to work with pecan due to its strength and finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hickory - wood of the hickory tree, closely related to pecan

Etymology

From an Algonquian word, likely from Illinois *pakani* or similar, referring originally to the nut or tree.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
