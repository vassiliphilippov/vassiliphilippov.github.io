to theorise

1. VERB

/ ˈθiːəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈθɪəraɪz / (BrE)
to form a theory or set of ideas about something, especially on uncertain or tentative grounds
form a theory or speculate
british

Examples

Many historians theorise that the ancient city was destroyed by an earthquake.
It's impossible to theorise about the cause without more data.
Scientists often theorise before any experiments are conducted.

Synset Examples

Scientists supposed that large dinosaurs lived in swamps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to speculate - to form a theory or conjecture without firm evidence
  • Synonym: to hypothesize - to make a hypothesis or an assumption for the sake of argument

Etymology

From theory + -ise, modeled after French théoriser.
