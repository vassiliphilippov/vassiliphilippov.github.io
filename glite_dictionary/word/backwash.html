backwash

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
the disturbed water or wave that spreads behind a moving boat or ship
wave or disturbed water behind a boat

Examples

The large yacht's backwash rocked the small fishing boat gently.
Swimmers are warned to stay clear of the ferry's backwash when it docks.
The ducks glided smoothly through the backwash left by the passing canoe.

Synset Examples

the motorboat's wake capsized the canoe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wake - the trail of disturbed water left by a boat or ship

Etymology

Formed from 'back' + 'wash', referring to the water washing backward behind a vessel.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈbækwɒʃ / (BrE)
the flow of air directed backwards by an aircraft propeller, typically causing turbulence behind the aircraft
airflow backward from an aircraft propeller

Examples

The pilot felt the backwash as he walked behind the spinning propeller.
Strong backwash from the engines can create hazardous conditions on the tarmac.
Make sure to stand clear of the backwash when the aircraft is preparing for takeoff.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11443311-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
the indirect or lingering effects that follow a significant event, especially a negative or disruptive one
unintended consequences after an event

Examples

The financial crisis left a painful backwash that affected families for years.
The backwash of his decision caused unexpected problems at work.
Communities are still dealing with the backwash of the devastating hurricane.

Synset Examples

the aftermath of war
in the wake of the accident no one knew how many had been injured

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aftermath - the consequences or results following an event, especially a disaster
  • Synonym: wake - the consequences or aftermath of an event or action
