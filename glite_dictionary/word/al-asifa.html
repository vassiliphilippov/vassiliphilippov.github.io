al-Asifa

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl ɑˈsiːfə / (AmE), / ˌæl ɑːˈsiːfə / (BrE)
a Palestinian political and military organization founded by Yasser Arafat in 1958 aimed at creating a Palestinian state, and known for armed operations in the 1960s and 1970s
Palestinian political and military organization, 1958–1970s

Examples

al-Asifa played a central role in the Palestinian national movement during its early years.
Many historians study the impact of al-Asifa on Middle Eastern politics in the 20th century.
The legacy of al-Asifa is often debated in discussions about regional security and politics.

Synset Examples

al-Fatah carried out numerous acts of international terrorism in western Europe and the Middle East in the 1970s

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fatah - Palestinian political and military organization, successor to al-Asifa

Etymology

From Arabic العصيفة (al-ʿĀṣifah), meaning 'the storm'.
