mockingly

1. ADV

/ ˈmɑkɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈmɒkɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that shows ridicule or scorn; in a jeering or contemptuous manner.
in a jeering or scornful way

Examples

She laughed mockingly at his attempt to solve the puzzle.
He bowed mockingly after losing the game, pretending it didn't matter.
The children imitated their teacher mockingly, causing the whole class to giggle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcastically - in a sarcastic manner

2. ADV

/ ˈmɑːkɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈmɒkɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that shows you are laughing at or making fun of someone, often in a disrespectful manner
in a mocking or ridiculing way

Examples

He spoke mockingly to his opponent after winning the match.
She raised an eyebrow and laughed mockingly at his suggestion.
The students repeated the teacher’s words mockingly under their breath.

Synset Examples

‘Sorry,’ she repeated derisively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derisively - in a mocking or contemptuous way
  • Antonym: respectfully - in a way that shows respect

Etymology

From the verb 'mock' plus adverbial suffix '-ly'.
