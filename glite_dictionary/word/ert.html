ERT

1. NOUN

/ i ɑr ti / (AmE), / iː ɑː tiː / (BrE)
the time, coordinated in universal time, when an event is received on Earth, often used in scientific and technical contexts.
coordinated universal time when an event is received on Earth

Examples

Astronomers recorded the ERT of the signal to compare it with data from other observatories.
The satellite's image had an ERT of 05:46:30 UTC for precise analysis.
Mission control closely monitors each packet's ERT to synchronize operations.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Earth Received Time.'
