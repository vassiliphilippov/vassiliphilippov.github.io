lordship

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔrdˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈlɔːdʃɪp / (BrE)
the authority or power held by a lord, especially in a feudal or historical context
authority or power of a lord

Examples

During the medieval period, a nobleman's lordship depended on the loyalty of his vassals.
The residents owed their allegiance to the lordship that ruled over the land.
Disputes between local families sometimes challenged the lordship of the reigning earl.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sovereignty - supreme power or authority
  • Antonym: servitude - the state of being a servant or in subjection

Etymology

From Old English *hlāfordscipe*, combining 'lord' and the suffix '-ship' denoting status or office.
