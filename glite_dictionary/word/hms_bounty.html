H.M.S. Bounty

1. PROPN

/ ˌeɪtʃ ˌɛm ˈɛs ˈbaʊnti / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ ˌɛm ˈɛs ˈbaʊnti / (BrE)
a British naval ship best known for the 1789 mutiny led by part of its crew against Captain William Bligh.
British ship famous for 1789 mutiny
british

Examples

The mutiny on the H.M.S. Bounty is one of history's most famous maritime uprisings.
Captain Bligh was set adrift after the infamous rebellion aboard the H.M.S. Bounty.
A museum in London features artifacts recovered from the H.M.S. Bounty.

Etymology

H.M.S. stands for 'His/Her Majesty's Ship'; 'Bounty' was the ship's name.
