ajar

1. ADJ

/ əˈdʒɑr / (AmE), / əˈdʒɑː / (BrE)
(of a door or window) slightly open.
slightly open (esp. door, window)

Examples

She left the window ajar to let in a breeze during the night.
The door was ajar, and I could hear voices from inside the room.
Make sure the lid is not ajar or the contents might spoil.

Synset Examples

the door was ajar

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shut - completely closed

Etymology

From Middle English on char, where 'char' meant 'turn' or 'twist,' indicating a door turned slightly from its frame.
