camelia

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmiːliə / (AmE), / kəˈmiːliə / (BrE)
A shrub or small evergreen tree native to eastern and southern Asia, with solitary white, pink, or reddish flowers, often grown for ornamental purposes.
evergreen shrub with showy flowers

Examples

The garden's most striking feature was the blooming camelia by the entrance.
She planted a pink camelia in a large clay pot on her balcony.
During winter, the only color in the park came from the camelia flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camellia - a genus of shrubs or small trees with showy flowers

Etymology

From Latinized form of Georg Kamel (1661–1706), Moravian botanist, + -ia, as in 'camellia'.
