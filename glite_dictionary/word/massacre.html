massacre

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæsəkɚ / (AmE), / ˈmæsəkə / (BrE)
the violent and excessive killing of a large number of people, often in a cruel or indiscriminate way
indiscriminate killing of many people

Examples

The villagers were horrified by the news of the massacre that had taken place nearby.
The documentary recounted the tragic massacre of innocent civilians during the war.
A memorial was built to honor the victims of the massacre.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slaughter - the killing of a large number of people or animals in a violent way

Etymology

From Middle French *massacre* (slaughter), of uncertain origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈmæsəkər / (AmE), / ˈmæsəkə / (BrE)
to deliberately kill a large number of people, especially in a cruel or indiscriminate way
kill a large number of people indiscriminately

Examples

During the war, invading forces massacred hundreds of villagers.
The rebels threatened to massacre anyone who resisted their rule.
International media reported that government troops had massacred civilians in the city.

Synset Examples

The Hutus massacred the Tutsis in Rwanda

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slaughter - to kill a large number of people or animals cruelly
  • Synonym: to exterminate - to destroy or kill a group of people completely
  • Antonym: save - to rescue or prevent from being killed

Etymology

From French 'massacre' (slaughter, carnage), of uncertain origin.

Synset POS: v
