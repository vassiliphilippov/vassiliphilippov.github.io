cutler

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌt.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌt.lə / (BrE)
a person or company that makes, sells, or deals in knives and other cutting instruments
dealer or maker of cutlery

Examples

The town was once famous for its skilled cutler, who produced some of the finest blades in Europe.
As a cutler, she carefully inspected every knife before offering it for sale.
The old shop sign indicated that a master cutler had operated there for more than a century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutlery - knives, forks, and spoons collectively; cutting instruments

Etymology

From Old French *coutelier* (knife maker), from *couteau* (knife), from Latin *cultellus* (a small knife).
