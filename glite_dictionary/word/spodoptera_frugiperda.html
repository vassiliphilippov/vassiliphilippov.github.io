Spodoptera frugiperda

1. NOUN

/ ˌspoʊdəˈpɛtərə fruːˌdʒɪˈpɜːrdə / (AmE), / ˌspɒdəˈpɛtərə fruːˌdʒɪˈpɜːrdə / (BrE)
a species of moth whose larva, known as the fall armyworm, is a pest that destroys grasses and small grain crops.
moth species; fall armyworm pest
medical

Examples

Farmers in many countries worry about the rapid spread of Spodoptera frugiperda in their maize fields.
The larva of Spodoptera frugiperda is known for devastating cereal crops across the Americas and Africa.
Research on controlling Spodoptera frugiperda is crucial for global food security.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall armyworm - common name for Spodoptera frugiperda, a pest moth larva

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; 'Spodoptera' from Greek 'spodos' (ash) + 'pteron' (wing), and 'frugiperda' from Latin 'frux' (fruit) + 'perdere' (to destroy).

2. NOUN

/ spoʊˈdɑptərə fruːdʒɪˈpɜrdə / (AmE), / spɒˈdɒptərə fruːdʒɪˈpɜːdə / (BrE)
A species of moth whose larvae are known as fall armyworms, major pests of various crops.
moth species; fall armyworm

Examples

The spread of the Spodoptera frugiperda poses a significant threat to maize crops worldwide.
Scientists are studying ways to control the Spodoptera frugiperda population in South America.
The larvae of Spodoptera frugiperda can devastate agricultural fields in a matter of days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall armyworm - larval stage of Spodoptera frugiperda

Etymology

From New Latin, genus Spodoptera and species frugiperda, from Latin 'frugiperda' meaning 'destroyer of crops'.
