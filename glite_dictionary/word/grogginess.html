grogginess

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɑɡi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡrɒɡi.nəs / (BrE)
A state of being dazed, weak, or only partly awake, often experienced after waking up or due to illness, fatigue, or intoxication.
state of being dazed or only partly awake

Examples

After only a few hours of sleep, she felt a persistent grogginess during her morning meeting.
The medication can sometimes cause grogginess that lasts into the afternoon.
He shook off his grogginess with a cold shower and a cup of coffee.

Synset Examples

his grogginess was caused as much by exhaustion as by the blows
someone stole his wallet while he was in a drunken stupor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alertness - state of being fully awake and attentive
  • Synonym: drowsiness - state of being sleepy or lethargic

Etymology

From 'groggy,' originally meaning 'drunk,' from 'grog' (an alcoholic drink).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɑːɡi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡrɒɡi.nəs / (BrE)
A state of feeling dazed, unsteady, or confused, especially as a result of alcohol or tiredness.
dazed or unsteady state

Examples

After staying up all night, she felt a heavy grogginess when her alarm rang early.
The grogginess after his second glass of wine made it hard to walk straight.
Recovering from anesthesia left him with a lingering sense of grogginess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groggy - dazed or unsteady, especially from intoxication or tiredness

Etymology

From 'groggy', originally referring to the effects of drinking grog (a mixture of rum and water), from 18th-century English slang.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɑːɡi nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡrɒɡi nɪs / (BrE)
The state of being groggy, especially as a result of tiredness or lack of sleep; a feeling of sluggishness and confusion.
state of being groggy or sluggish

Examples

She struggled to shake off her grogginess after a night of restless sleep.
The medication left him with a lingering sense of grogginess the next morning.
Heavy grogginess made it difficult for her to focus during the early meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groggy - dazed or weak, typically from tiredness or illness
  • Antonym: alertness - the quality of being alert or fully awake

Etymology

From 'groggy' + '-ness', where 'groggy' originally referred to the effects of drinking grog (alcoholic drink).
