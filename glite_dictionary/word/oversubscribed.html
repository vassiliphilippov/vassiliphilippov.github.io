oversubscribed

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərsəbˈskraɪbd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˌsəbˈskraɪbd / (BrE)
Having more applicants, requests, or demand than available supply, especially for tickets, shares, or places.
demand exceeds supply (e.g. tickets, shares)

Examples

The concert was so oversubscribed that many fans couldn't get tickets.
Applications for the seminar were oversubscribed within hours of opening.
The university's most popular course is always oversubscribed.

Synset Examples

the opera season was oversubscribed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fully booked - All available places or tickets are taken
  • Antonym: undersubscribed - Having fewer applicants or demand than available supply

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (in excess) and 'subscribed' (enrolled or applied), meaning more people subscribed than possible.
