Serratula

1. NOUN

/ səˈrætjʊlə / (AmE), / səˈratjʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of Old World perennial herbs with spirally arranged toothed leaves, belonging to the family Asteraceae.
genus of perennial Old World herbs

Examples

The botanist spent several years studying species of Serratula in their natural habitats.
Some herbal remedies are made using extracts from Serratula plants.
The toothed leaves of Serratula make it easy to distinguish from similar genera.

Etymology

From Latin 'serratula', diminutive of 'serra' (saw), referring to the toothed leaves.

2. NOUN

/ sɛrəˈtjuːlə / (AmE), / sɛrəˈtjuːlə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs found in the Old World, characterized by spirally arranged, toothed leaves.
genus of perennial herbs with toothed leaves

Examples

The Serratula species are known for their distinctive toothed leaves and purple flowers.
Several herbalists have studied the medicinal properties of Serratula in traditional remedies.
In the wild, Serratula often grows in meadows and open woodlands across Europe and Asia.

Etymology

From Latin 'serratula', diminutive of 'serra' meaning 'saw', referring to the toothed leaf margins.
