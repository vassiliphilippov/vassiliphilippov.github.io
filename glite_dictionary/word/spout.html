spout

1. VERB

/ spaʊt / (AmE), / spaʊt / (BrE)
to gush out or flow forth suddenly in a stream or jet, especially of liquid or gas
to gush or flow out suddenly

Examples

Water began to spout from the broken pipe.
Steam would spout out of the kettle when it boiled.
A fountain spouts water high into the air in the city square.

Synset Examples

water gushed forth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gush - to flow out suddenly and in large amounts

Etymology

From Middle English 'spoute', from Middle Dutch 'spouten', meaning 'to spout, to gush'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02072341-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ spaʊt / (AmE), / spaʊt / (BrE)
a tube or opening through which a liquid or grain can be poured or flows out, especially as part of a container
an opening for pouring liquids or grain

Examples

She poured the tea carefully from the spout of the teapot.
A steady stream of grain flowed out of the silo's spout.
Make sure the spout is clean before filling the watering can.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nozzle - a projecting spout, often with a narrowing opening, used for directing the flow of a fluid

Etymology

From Middle English 'spoute', from Old English 'spūtan' (to spit, throw out), related to Dutch 'spuiten', German 'spucken'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04294291-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ spaʊt / (AmE), / spaʊt / (BrE)
to speak in a loud, excited, or emotional manner, often for a long time and without much thought
speak loudly or excitedly at length

Examples

He began to spout nonsense about conspiracy theories at the dinner table.
The politician would spout promises during every debate, but rarely kept any.
She tends to spout facts quickly when she’s excited about a topic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rant - to speak or shout at length in a wild, impassioned way
  • Synonym: pontificate - express one's opinions in a pompous and dogmatic way

Etymology

From Middle English 'spouten', from Old English 'spūtian', related to Old Norse 'spýta' (to spit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01053828-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ spaʊt / (AmE), / spaʊt / (BrE)
to send out liquid or another substance forcefully in a narrow stream; to gush out
send out in a jet or stream

Examples

Water was spouting from the broken pipe onto the street.
The whale surfaced, spouting a jet of water into the air.
He noticed oil spouting from the engine when he opened the hood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gush - to flow out quickly and in large amounts
  • Degree Lower: trickle - to flow in a thin stream or drops slowly

Etymology

From Middle English *spouten*, possibly from Middle Dutch *spouten* (to spout, to pour out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01243804-s
Synset POS: s
