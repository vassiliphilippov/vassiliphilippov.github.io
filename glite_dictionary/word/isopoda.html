Isopoda

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
an order of crustaceans that includes woodlice and their relatives, characterized by a flattened body and seven pairs of legs
order of crustaceans including woodlice

Examples

Scientists have discovered a new species within the Isopoda order living in the deep ocean.
Members of the Isopoda are found in both marine and terrestrial environments worldwide.
The biology class studied the external anatomy of Isopoda using woodlice as examples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodlouse - small terrestrial crustacean with a segmented body, member of Isopoda

Etymology

From New Latin Isopoda, from Greek iso- (equal) + pous, pod- (foot).
