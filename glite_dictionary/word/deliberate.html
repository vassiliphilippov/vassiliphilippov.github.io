deliberate

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈlɪbərət / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪbərət / (BrE)
done consciously and intentionally, after careful consideration; carefully thought out in advance
carefully thought out and intentional

Examples

The attack on the database was a deliberate act of sabotage.
She gave a deliberate answer after weighing all the options.
His movements were slow and deliberate, showing how carefully he planned each step.

Synset Examples

a calculated insult
with measured irony
he made a deliberate decision not to respond negatively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intentional - done on purpose or deliberately
  • Antonym: accidental - happening by chance, not intended

Etymology

From Latin deliberatus, past participle of deliberare (to weigh, consider).

3. VERB

/ dɪˈlɪbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪbəreɪt / (BrE)
to carefully consider or discuss the possible advantages and disadvantages of something before making a decision
carefully consider or discuss

Examples

The jury will deliberate before reaching a verdict.
We need to deliberate on this proposal before making any decisions.
After several hours, the committee continued to deliberate the matter.
