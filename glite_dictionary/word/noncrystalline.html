noncrystalline

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˈkrɪstəlaɪn / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈkrɪstəlaɪn / (BrE)
(of a substance) not having a definite crystalline structure; lacking the regular arrangement of atoms typical of crystals.
not having a crystal structure

Examples

Glass is a typical example of a noncrystalline solid.
Scientists studied the noncrystalline structure of the polymer to determine its properties.
The noncrystalline materials did not diffract X-rays in the same way as crystals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amorphous - lacking a clear structure or form, especially in materials science
  • Antonym: crystalline - having a clear, ordered crystal structure

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'crystalline' (from Greek 'krustallos' meaning 'ice, crystal').
