phencyclidine

1. NOUN

/ fɛnˈsɪklɪˌdin / (AmE), / fɛnˈsɪklɪˌdiːn / (BrE)
a synthetic drug used as a veterinary anesthetic and, when taken illicitly, as a powerful hallucinogen; also known as PCP
synthetic anesthetic and hallucinogenic drug
medical

Examples

The veterinarian administered phencyclidine to the animal before surgery.
The police confiscated a stash of phencyclidine during the raid.
Recreational use of phencyclidine can lead to dangerous psychological effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PCP - common abbreviation for phencyclidine, a hallucinogenic drug
  • Degree Lower: ketamine - a similar dissociative anesthetic used medically and illicitly
  • Synonym: LSD - a hallucinogenic drug with different chemical structure but similar effects

Etymology

From phenyl + cyclidine, indicating its chemical structure.
