escapologist

1. NOUN

/ əˌskeɪpəˈlɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ɪˌskeɪpəˈlɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
an entertainer skilled in escaping from restraints or confined spaces as a form of performance
entertainer skilled in escapes

Examples

The famous escapologist amazed the audience by escaping from a locked box underwater.
She dreamed of becoming an escapologist after watching Houdini's performances.
The escapologist was invited to perform at the city carnival this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape artist - an entertainer skilled at escaping from restraints or confinement

Etymology

From 'escape' + '-ologist', patterned after 'escapology'.
