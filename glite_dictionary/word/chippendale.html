Chippendale

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɪpənˌdeɪl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪp.ən.deɪl / (BrE)
relating to the style of furniture designed by Thomas Chippendale in the 18th century, characterized by graceful lines, rococo carvings, and classical motifs.
furniture style of Thomas Chippendale

Examples

The antique dealer specializes in Chippendale chairs from the late 1700s.
A Chippendale mirror added elegance to the hallway.
Her dining room features a beautiful Chippendale table with intricate carvings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rococo - an ornate style of art and design with elaborate detail

Etymology

From the name of Thomas Chippendale (1718–1779), English cabinetmaker.
