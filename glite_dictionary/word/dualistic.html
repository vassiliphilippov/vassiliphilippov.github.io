dualistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌduəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌdjuːəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by the doctrine of dualism, especially in philosophy or religion, where reality is divided into two fundamental and opposing elements.
relating to dualism in philosophy or religion

Examples

The author's dualistic worldview shaped his understanding of morality as a battle between good and evil.
In certain religions, the universe is explained through a dualistic opposition between spirit and matter.
The debate contrasted dualistic and monistic theories of the mind-body relationship.

Synset Examples

a Manichaean conflict between good and evil

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monistic - relating to monism, the doctrine that reality is one unity

Etymology

From dualism + -ic, the suffix forming adjectives.
