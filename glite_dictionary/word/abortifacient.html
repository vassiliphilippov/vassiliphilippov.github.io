abortifacient

1. NOUN

/ əˌbɔrtəˈfeɪʃənt / (AmE), / əˌbɔːtɪˈfeɪʃənt / (BrE)
A drug or other substance that causes abortion by inducing the expulsion of an embryo or fetus.
a substance causing abortion
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that an abortifacient can terminate a pregnancy if taken early enough.
Certain herbal remedies were historically used as abortifacients despite the risks involved.
She researched the effects and legality of using an abortifacient before making her decision.

Etymology

From Latin 'abortus' (abortion) + 'facient-' (doing, making), from 'facere' (to do, make).

2. ADJ

/ əˌbɔrtɪˈfeɪʃənt / (AmE), / əbɔːtɪˈfeɪʃənt / (BrE)
(of a drug or agent) causing abortion; capable of inducing abortion.
causing abortion
medical

Examples

Some traditional herbs are known to have abortifacient properties and should be used with caution.
The drug was withdrawn from the market after its abortifacient effects became known.
Certain abortifacient medications must be prescribed and administered by a licensed physician.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: contraceptive - preventing conception or pregnancy

Etymology

From Latin abortivus (causing abortion) + -facient (making, doing), from Latin facere (to make).
