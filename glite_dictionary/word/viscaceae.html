Viscaceae

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈskeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / vɪˈskeɪsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of mostly parasitic flowering plants, formerly regarded as a subfamily of Loranthaceae, which includes mistletoes.
family of parasitic flowering plants (mistletoes)

Examples

The Viscaceae are known for their distinctive parasitic habit, often seen in mistletoe plants.
Several species in the Viscaceae family are important for their role in bird-mediated seed dispersal.
Botanists debate whether Viscaceae should be classified as its own family or as part of Loranthaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Loranthaceae - a family of parasitic or hemiparasitic plants related to mistletoes

Etymology

From Latin 'viscum' (mistletoe) + '-aceae', standard suffix for plant family names.

2. NOUN

/ vɪˈskeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / vɪˈskeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, commonly known as mistletoes, sometimes classified as a subfamily of Loranthaceae.
a family of mistletoes (plants)
medical

Examples

The Viscaceae are well known for their parasitic mistletoe species found worldwide.
Recent research debates whether Viscaceae should be considered a separate family or part of Loranthaceae.
Botanists often study the distribution of Viscaceae in temperate and tropical regions.

Etymology

From genus Viscum (Latin for 'mistletoe') + -aceae, a standard suffix for plant family names in scientific classification.
