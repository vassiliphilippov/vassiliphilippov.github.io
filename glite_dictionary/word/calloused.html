calloused

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæl.əst / (AmE), / ˈkæl.əst / (BrE)
(of skin or a part of the body) made tough and thick from repeated pressure, friction, or use, usually by developing calluses.
having tough, thickened skin due to use

Examples

The farmer's calloused hands showed years of hard work in the fields.
After months of training, her feet became calloused from running on rough ground.
He traced the calloused tips of his fingers, evidence of long hours spent playing guitar.

Synset Examples

calloused skin
with a workman's callous hands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: callous - (of skin) toughened or thickened; (of a person) emotionally insensitive

Etymology

From the noun 'callous', from Latin 'callosus' (thick-skinned).
