to enrage

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈreɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈreɪdʒ / (BrE)
to make someone extremely angry or furious
make extremely angry

Examples

His unfair accusation managed to enrage everyone in the room.
The decision to cancel the show will likely enrage loyal fans.
It enraged her to see such blatant injustice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to infuriate - to make very angry
  • Degree Lower: to annoy - to irritate or slightly anger
  • Antonym: calm - to make less angry or excited

Etymology

From French *enrager* (to make angry), from *rage* (rage).
