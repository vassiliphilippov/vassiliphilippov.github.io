Ginkgophytina

1. NOUN

/ gɪŋkoʊfaɪˈtiːnə / (AmE), / gɪŋkɡəʊfʌɪˈtiːnə / (BrE)
A subdivision or class of gymnosperms including the ginkgo, used in some systems of plant classification.
a subdivision of gymnosperms (ginkgos)

Examples

The Ginkgophytina are distinguished by their unique fan-shaped leaves.
In the fossil record, Ginkgophytina appear as early as the Jurassic period.
Some taxonomists classify Ginkgophytina as a separate division from other gymnosperms.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Ginkgo' (the genus) and Greek 'phuton' (plant).

2. NOUN

/ gɪŋˌgoʊfəˈtaɪnə / (AmE), / gɪŋˌgəʊfəˈtɪnə / (BrE)
a subdivision or class in some plant classification systems, consisting of ginkgos or their relatives, sometimes regarded as one of five subdivisions of Gymnospermophyta
subdivision of plants including ginkgos

Examples

The Ginkgophytina are renowned for their only living member, the Ginkgo biloba.
Some botanists treat Ginkgophytina as a separate class within the gymnosperms.
Fossil records show that Ginkgophytina were more diverse in the past than today.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Gymnospermophyta - division of seed-producing plants that includes conifers, cycads, ginkgos, and gnetophytes

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'Ginkgo' (the genus) + Greek 'phyton' meaning 'plant'.

3. NOUN

/ ɡɪŋkoʊˌfaɪˈtiːnə / (AmE), / ɡɪŋkɡəʊˌfɪˈtiːnə / (BrE)
a taxonomic subdivision comprising the ginkgos, sometimes classified as a class or as one of five subdivisions of Gymnospermophyta
taxonomic group containing ginkgos

Examples

The Ginkgophytina is represented today by a single living species, Ginkgo biloba.
Fossil records show that members of Ginkgophytina were once widespread during the Mesozoic era.
Researchers debate whether Ginkgophytina should be ranked as a subclass or a separate division.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ginkgoales - order of plants containing the only living species Ginkgo biloba

Etymology

From New Latin Ginkgo (the genus) + -phytina (from Greek phuton 'plant'), used in botanical taxonomy.
