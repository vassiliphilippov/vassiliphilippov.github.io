Sertularia

1. NOUN

/ sɜr.tjuˈlɛɹ.i.ə / (AmE), / sɜː.tjʊˈlɛə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of sessile marine hydroids that form feathery colonies, commonly found attached to underwater surfaces.
genus of feathery marine hydroids

Examples

The diver collected a specimen of Sertularia from the rocky seafloor for further study.
Many species in the genus Sertularia are known for their delicate, feathery colony structures.
Researchers observed that Sertularia can serve as habitat for small marine organisms.

Etymology

From New Latin Sertularia, from Latin sertula (little garland), referring to the colony's feathery appearance.

2. NOUN

/ sɝːtjuˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / sɜːtjʊˈlɛːrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of sessile marine hydroids that form feathery, plant-like colonies, belonging to the family Sertulariidae.
genus of colonial marine hydroids

Examples

The delicate branches of Sertularia can often be seen on rocks in shallow seas.
Scientists studied Sertularia colonies to better understand marine biodiversity.
Collectors prize some species of Sertularia for their intricate, feathery appearance.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Sertularia', based on Latin 'sertum' (garland, wreath), referencing the colony's shape.
