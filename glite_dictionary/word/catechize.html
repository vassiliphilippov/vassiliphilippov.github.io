to catechize

1. VERB

/ ˈkætɪˌkaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkatɪˌkaɪz / (BrE)
to give religious instruction, especially by means of questions and answers, often in preparation for Christian confirmation or baptism
give religious instruction by questioning

Examples

At Sunday school, the priest would catechize the children in preparation for their first communion.
The pastor took time to catechize new converts each week before baptism.
During the class, students were catechized about the importance of the sacraments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to instruct - to teach or provide knowledge

Etymology

From late Latin catechizare, from Greek katekhizein (to instruct orally).

2. VERB

/ ˈkætɪˌkaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkatɪkʌɪz / (BrE)
to question someone systematically, especially about religious beliefs, often using a set form of questions and answers
examine by asking systematic questions

Examples

The priest would catechize the children every week before the service.
She was catechized about her beliefs by the visiting minister.
Teachers may catechize students to ensure their understanding of the material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interrogate - to ask someone questions closely, aggressively, or formally
  • Synonym: to examine - to inspect or question someone closely

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'catechizare', from Greek 'katēkhein' (to instruct orally).
