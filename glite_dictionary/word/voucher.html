voucher

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaʊtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈvaʊtʃə / (BrE)
A printed certificate or document that can be exchanged for goods, services, or a discount.
certificate for goods, services, or discounts

Examples

You can use this voucher to get a free coffee at the café.
She handed the cashier a voucher worth twenty dollars off her purchase.
The company issued vouchers as compensation for delayed flights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coupon - a ticket or document that can be exchanged for a financial discount or rebate

Etymology

From Old French 'vocher' (to call, summon) from Latin 'vocare' (to call).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaʊtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈvaʊtʃə / (BrE)
a written document that confirms a payment, purchase, or expenditure, often used as proof for reimbursement or record-keeping
document confirming payment or expense

Examples

Please attach the voucher to your expense report for reimbursement.
He kept every voucher from his business trip to ensure proper accounting.
A hotel voucher was provided to all conference attendees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receipt - a written acknowledgment of having received money or goods

Etymology

From Anglo-French 'voucher' (to summon), from Latin 'vocāre' (to call).
