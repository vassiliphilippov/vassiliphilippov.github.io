to aerosolize

1. VERB

/ ˈɛr.əˌsɑː.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛə.rəˌsɒ.laɪz / (BrE)
to disperse or convert into a fine spray or suspension in air, especially as an aerosol
disperse as a fine spray in air

Examples

The laboratory technician will aerosolize the chemical for analysis.
It is dangerous to aerosolize infectious agents without proper protection.
Many disinfectants can be aerosolized for use in spraying systems.

Synset Examples

The bacteria suspension was aerosolized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray - to scatter liquid in tiny drops

Etymology

From 'aerosol' + '-ize', first attested in the 20th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈɛr.oʊ.səˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛə.rə.səˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to become or cause to become dispersed in the form of a fine spray or suspension in air
disperse as a fine spray in air

Examples

During the experiment, the researchers aerosolized the chemical to test its effects in the air.
Some viruses can aerosolize and remain infectious for hours.
Water droplets aerosolize when ejected from a spray bottle.

Synset Examples

the bacteria quickly aerosolised

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disperse - to scatter or spread widely

Etymology

From 'aerosol' + '-ize', formed in English in the mid-20th century.
