ELVES

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlvz / (AmE), / ˈɛlvz / (BrE)
Acronym for emissions of light and very low frequency perturbations due to electromagnetic pulse sources; extremely bright, very short (less than a millisecond) electrical flashes forming a huge ring (up to 400 km in diameter) in the ionosphere.
ring-shaped flashes in the upper atmosphere
medical

Examples

Scientists detected ELVES in the ionosphere during the intense thunderstorm.
The study focused on the conditions necessary for ELVES to appear above thunderstorms.
Satellites are now able to capture images of ELVES as they rapidly expand in the upper atmosphere.

Etymology

Acronym formed from the initial letters of 'Emissions of Light and Very Low Frequency perturbations due to Electromagnetic Pulse Sources'.
