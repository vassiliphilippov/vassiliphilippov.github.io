slobber

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɑːbər / (AmE), / ˈslɒbə / (BrE)
saliva that drips or runs from the mouth, especially as a result of excitement or lack of control
saliva dribbling from the mouth

Examples

The baby's chin was covered in slobber after playing with his toys.
She wiped the slobber off the dog's mouth after their walk.
There was a trail of slobber left on the couch cushion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drool - saliva that flows from the mouth uncontrollably

2. VERB

/ ˈslɑːbər / (AmE), / ˈslɒbə / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to let saliva drivel from the mouth, often in an uncontrolled or messy way.
let saliva drivel from the mouth

Examples

The dog began to slobber all over the floor when it saw the treat.
The toddler slobbered on his toy as he chewed it.
She was embarrassed when she realized she was slobbering in her sleep.

Synset Examples

The baby drooled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drool - to let saliva dribble from the mouth
  • Synonym: drivel - to let saliva flow from the mouth

3. VERB

/ ˈslɑːbər / (AmE), / ˈslɒbə / (BrE)
to let saliva run from the mouth, especially in an untidy way; or to show excessive enthusiasm or desire for something.
to drool or show excessive desire

Examples

The dog began to slobber on my hand when I petted it.
He couldn't help but slobber over the display of chocolate desserts.
Please wipe the baby's chin before she starts to slobber everywhere.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drool - to let saliva run out of the mouth
  • Synonym: to salivate - to produce saliva, especially excessively
