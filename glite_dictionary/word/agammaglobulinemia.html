agammaglobulinemia

1. NOUN

/ əˌɡæməˌɡlɑbjələˈnimiə / (AmE), / əˌɡaməˌɡlɒbjələˈniːmɪə / (BrE)
A rare medical condition in which there is a very low level or absence of gamma globulins (antibodies) in the blood, leading to a greatly increased risk of infections.
absence of antibodies in the blood
medical

Examples

Children with agammaglobulinemia often require frequent immunoglobulin infusions to fight infections.
Because of his agammaglobulinemia, he was closely monitored for signs of respiratory illness.
Early diagnosis of agammaglobulinemia can significantly improve a patient's quality of life.

Etymology

From Greek prefix a- (without), 'gamma globulin' (a type of antibody), and medical suffix -emia (condition of the blood).
