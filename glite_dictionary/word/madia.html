Madia

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdiə / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdɪə / (BrE)
a genus of sticky herbs in the family Asteraceae, with yellow flowers that open in the morning or evening but close in bright light, native to western North America and South America.
a genus of sticky yellow-flowered herbs

Examples

The botanist identified the wildflower as a species of Madia due to its sticky leaves and yellow blooms.
Many Madia species thrive in dry grassland habitats along the Pacific coast.
The unique opening and closing times of Madia flowers help them avoid the midday sun.

Etymology

From Spanish *madia*, originally from the indigenous Chilean Mapuche name *machí* for the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdiə / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdiə / (BrE)
A genus of sticky herbs with yellow flowers that open in the morning or evening but close in bright light, native to western North America.
genus of sticky herbs with yellow flowers

Examples

Botanists have identified several new species within the Madia genus in California.
The sticky leaves of Madia help distinguish it from other wildflowers in the region.
Many Madia plants close their yellow flowers when exposed to bright sunlight.

Etymology

From Spanish 'madia', adopted as the genus name in botanical Latin.
