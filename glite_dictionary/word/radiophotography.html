radiophotography

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊfəˈtɑgrəfi / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊfəˈtɒgrəfi / (BrE)
the transmission of photographs by means of radio waves
transmission of photographs by radio waves

Examples

The newspaper used radiophotography to receive images from overseas correspondents.
Before digital networks, radiophotography was an important way to share photos over long distances.
Advances in radiophotography revolutionized news reporting in the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fax - a method of transmitting scanned printed material by telephone lines or radio waves

Etymology

Formed from 'radio-' (relating to radio waves) + 'photography'.
