missus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsəz / (AmE), / ˈmɪsɪz / (BrE)
(informal) a man's wife; used as an informal term of address or reference to someone's wife.
(informal) a man's wife
informal

Examples

When the missus finds out I forgot her birthday, I'll be in trouble.
He told the missus that he'd be home late tonight.
The missus and I are planning a holiday next month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wife - a married woman in relation to her spouse

Etymology

Altered pronunciation of 'mistress', historically used as a respectful address for women.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪs.ɪz / (BrE)
(informal) One's wife or, occasionally, girlfriend; used especially in British English.
one's wife (informal, chiefly British)
informal british

Examples

I left the party early to help the missus with the kids.
When my missus heard the news, she was delighted.
The missus and I are taking a holiday next month.

Synset Examples

His missus is an excellent cook.
I asked the missus if she wanted to join us.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wife - a married woman considered in relation to her spouse
  • Antonym: husband - a married man considered in relation to his spouse

Etymology

Variant of 'missis,' an informal form of 'mistress,' from Old French 'maistresse'.
