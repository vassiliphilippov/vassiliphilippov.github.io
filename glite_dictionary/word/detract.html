to detract

1. VERB

/ dɪˈtrækt / (AmE), / dɪˈtrakt / (BrE)
to reduce or take away the worth, value, or quality of something; diminish.
to reduce or diminish value

Examples

The new design does not detract from the comfort of the original product.
A few minor errors detract from an otherwise excellent report.
Harsh lighting can detract from the beauty of a photograph.

Synset Examples

His bad manners detract from his good character

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diminish - to make or become less
  • Antonym: to enhance - to increase or improve in value, quality, or attractiveness

Etymology

From Latin detract-, past participle of detrahere, meaning 'to take away'.
