ornithopod

1. NOUN

/ ɔːrˈnɪθəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ɔːˈnɪθəpɒd / (BrE)
a type of bipedal, herbivorous dinosaur belonging to the group Ornithopoda, including species such as Iguanodon and Hadrosaurus
bipedal herbivorous dinosaur

Examples

The fossilized skeleton of an ornithopod was discovered in the ancient riverbed.
Many ornithopods developed specialized teeth for grinding plant material.
Scientists believe this particular ornithopod species could run quickly to escape predators.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dinosaur - a group of reptiles of the Mesozoic era, often very large
  • Degree Lower: theropod - a group of bipedal saurischian dinosaurs, mostly carnivorous

Etymology

From Greek ornith- (bird) + pous, pod- (foot), referring to bird-like feet.
