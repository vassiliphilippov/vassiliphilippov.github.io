Oniscus

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɪskəs / (AmE), / əˈnɪskəs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Oniscidae, comprising woodlice that are unable to roll into a ball
genus of woodlice

Examples

The genus Oniscus includes species often found under logs and stones in damp environments.
Unlike some relatives, members of Oniscus cannot roll themselves into a ball for protection.
Biologists collected several Oniscus specimens during their survey of the woodland area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodlouse - a small terrestrial crustacean with a segmented body

Etymology

From Latin *oniscus*, from Ancient Greek ὄνισκος (óniskos), meaning 'woodlouse'.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈnɪskəs / (AmE), / əʊˈnɪskəs / (BrE)
A genus of woodlice that cannot roll into a ball, serving as the type genus of the family Oniscidae.
a genus of woodlice

Examples

The genus Oniscus includes woodlice that lack the ability to roll into a protective ball.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Oniscus genus in the coastal region.
Members of the genus Oniscus are commonly found beneath logs and stones in damp environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodlouse - a small terrestrial crustacean with a segmented body

Etymology

From New Latin *Oniscus*, from Greek *oniskos* (a kind of woodlouse).
