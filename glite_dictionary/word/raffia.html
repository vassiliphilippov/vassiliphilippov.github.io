raffia

1. NOUN

/ ˈræfiə / (AmE), / ˈræfiə / (BrE)
a tropical palm tree of Africa, Madagascar, and the Americas, cultivated commercially for its strong, fibrous leaves
a palm tree grown for its strong leaves

Examples

The raffia is widely cultivated for its tough, fibrous leaves used in weaving.
Artisans often use dried raffia leaves to make baskets and mats.
The plantation focused on growing raffia for export to the textile industry.

Etymology

From Malagasy 'rafia', referring to the palm tree.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræfiə / (AmE), / ˈræfiə / (BrE)
a fiber obtained from the leaves of the raffia palm, used for making baskets, mats, hats, and similar items.
fiber from raffia palm used in weaving

Examples

She wove a beautiful basket from raffia she collected at the market.
The artist decorated the package with red and green raffia for a festive look.
Traditional hats made of raffia are popular souvenirs in many African countries.

Etymology

From Malagasy 'rafia', the native name for the plant.
