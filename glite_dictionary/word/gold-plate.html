to gold-plate

1. VERB

/ ˈɡoʊldˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldˌpleɪt / (BrE)
to cover (an object) with a thin layer of gold, often by electrochemical means.
cover with a layer of gold

Examples

The jeweler will gold-plate the bracelet to restore its shine.
Many household items are gold-plated to give them a more luxurious appearance.
He learned how to gold-plate small pieces of copper wire in his chemistry class.

Synset Examples

goldplate a watch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gild - to cover with a thin layer of gold or gold decoration

2. VERB

/ ˈɡoʊld ˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊld ˌpleɪt / (BrE)
to cover (an object) with a thin layer of gold, usually by chemical or electrochemical means
cover with a thin layer of gold

Examples

Many luxury watches are gold-plated to give them an elegant finish.
They decided to gold-plate the family heirloom to restore its original shine.
The company offers services to gold-plate jewelry and decorative items.

Synset Examples

goldplate a watch

Etymology

Formed from 'gold' + 'plate', referring to the application of gold as a coating.
