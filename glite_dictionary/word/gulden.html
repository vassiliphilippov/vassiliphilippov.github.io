gulden

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʊl.dən / (AmE), / ˈɡʌl.dən / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in Suriname, equal to 100 cents, replaced by the Surinamese dollar in 2004
former currency unit of Suriname

Examples

Before 2004, the gulden was used for all transactions in Suriname.
She exchanged her guldens for US dollars before leaving the country.
The price of bread was two guldens during the colonial period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: florin - another name for several European currencies including the Dutch gulden

Etymology

Dutch, meaning 'golden', from the use of gold coins.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡʊl.dən / (AmE), / ˈɡʊl.dən / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in the Netherlands, replaced by the euro in 2002; equal to 100 cents
former Dutch currency unit

Examples

Before the introduction of the euro, most prices in the Netherlands were given in gulden.
He found an old coin collection containing several rare gulden.
The Dutch gulden was once widely used in international trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: florin - another name for the former Dutch currency unit

Etymology

From Dutch 'gulden', from Middle Dutch 'gulden', meaning 'golden', referring to coins originally made of gold.
