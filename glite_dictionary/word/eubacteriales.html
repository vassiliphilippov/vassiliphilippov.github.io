Eubacteriales

1. NOUN

/ jubækˌtɪriˈeɪliz / (AmE), / juːbækˌtɪəriˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
An order of Gram-positive, usually spherical or rod-shaped bacteria; some species are motile.
order of Gram-positive bacteria
medical

Examples

The family of bacteria known as Eubacteriales includes many medically important species.
Members of Eubacteriales are found widely in soil, water, and as part of the normal human flora.
Researchers identified the unknown bacterium as belonging to the order Eubacteriales.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'eu-' (true) and 'bacterium', denoting 'true bacteria'.
