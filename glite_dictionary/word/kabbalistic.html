kabbalistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkæbəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌkabəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
having a secret or hidden meaning; relating to or characteristic of the mystical and esoteric teachings of Kabbalah.
relating to secret or mystical meaning

Examples

The manuscript was filled with kabbalistic diagrams and symbols.
She spoke in a kabbalistic code that only her closest allies understood.
Some consider the painting's imagery to be kabbalistic and deeply symbolic.

Synset Examples

cabalistic symbols engraved in stone
cryptic writings
thoroughly sibylline in most of his pronouncements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryptic - having a meaning that is mysterious or obscure
  • Synonym: esoteric - intended for or likely to be understood by only a small number of people

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'cabbalisticus', from 'Cabbala', referring to the Jewish mystical tradition of Kabbalah.
