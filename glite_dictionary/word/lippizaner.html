Lippizaner

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈpɪz ən ər / (AmE), / lɪˈpɪz ən ə / (BrE)
A compact, strong horse breed originally bred and trained in Vienna, known for its intelligence, calm temperament, and excellence in dressage.
A breed of strong, intelligent saddle horse

Examples

The Spanish Riding School in Vienna is famous for its performances with the elegant Lippizaner horses.
A young Lippizaner foal is usually dark but gradually turns white as it matures.
Due to their intelligence and calm nature, Lippizaner horses excel in classical dressage competitions.

Synset Examples

a Lippizan is black or brown when born but turns white by the time it is five years old

Etymology

From German 'Lipizzaner', named after the Lipica stud farm (now in Slovenia), where the breed originated.
