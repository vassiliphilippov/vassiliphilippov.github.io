proletarianism

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.ləˈtɛr.i.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.lɪˈtɛə.ri.ə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
The condition, ideology, or political interests characteristic of the working class, especially as a distinct social or political force.
condition or ideology of the working class

Examples

The novel captures the spirit of proletarianism that defined the city's industrial era.
His lectures explored the rise of proletarianism and its impact on modern politics.
Many of her paintings depict the daily struggles associated with proletarianism in urban environments.

Synset Examples

Throughout the people's-front period, his proletarianism had gone out of fashion, and Gold hardly figured in the writers' congresses of 1937 and 1939.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proletariat - the working class, especially in a Marxist context
  • Antonym: bourgeoisie - the capitalist or middle class, contrasted with proletariat

Etymology

From proletarian + -ism, after Latin proletarius (a member of the lowest class).
