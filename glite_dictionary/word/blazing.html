blazing

1. ADJ

/ ˈbleɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbleɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
shining very brightly or intensely
shining very brightly

Examples

The blazing sun beat down on the desert without mercy.
Snow covered the field, reflecting a blazing white light that made me squint.
He stared directly into the blazing headlights of the oncoming car.

Synset Examples

the blazing sun
blinding headlights
dazzling snow
fulgent patterns of sunlight
the glaring sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dazzling - shining intensely and often blindingly
  • Synonym: glaring - shining with a very bright, often harsh light
  • Antonym: dim - not shining brightly or clearly

Etymology

From the verb 'blaze,' of Middle English origin, probably from Old English 'blæse' (torch, flame).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbleɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbleɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
(of something, especially misconduct or emotion) completely obvious or lacking any attempt at concealment; blatant.
completely obvious; blatant

Examples

The senator's blazing hypocrisy stunned even his supporters.
She made a blazing error during the press conference, revealing confidential information.
His blazing ambition was clear to everyone in the company.

Synset Examples

blatant disregard of the law
a blatant appeal to vanity
a blazing indiscretion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blatant - completely obvious or conspicuous, often offensively so
  • Antonym: subtle - not obvious or easily noticeable

Etymology

Present participle of 'blaze', referring figuratively to something as bright and impossible to ignore as a blaze.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02097713-s
Synset POS: s
