overboard

1. ADV

/ ˈoʊvɚˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəbɔːd / (BrE)
From on board a boat or ship into the water.
from a vessel into the water

Examples

During the storm, several crates were thrown overboard to lighten the ship.
He accidentally dropped his phone overboard while taking a photo of the dolphins.
Passengers are instructed not to toss any litter overboard to avoid polluting the sea.

Synset Examples

they dropped their garbage overboard

Etymology

Formed from 'over' and 'board', referring to going over the side of a ship's deck.

2. ADV

/ ˈoʊvərˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəbɔːd / (BrE)
to an excessive or extreme degree; beyond what is reasonable or necessary
to an excessive degree

Examples

He tends to go overboard with his holiday decorations every year.
When it comes to gift giving, she often goes overboard and spends more than she should.
The editor thought the journalist went overboard in describing the event's significance.

Synset Examples

he went overboard to please his in-laws

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessively - to a degree greater than is necessary or normal
  • Antonym: moderately - to a reasonable or limited extent; not excessively
