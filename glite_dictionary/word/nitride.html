nitride

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtraɪd / (AmE), / ˈnɪtraɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing nitrogen combined with a more electropositive element, especially a metal or phosphorus.
chemical compound with nitrogen and a metal or similar element
medical

Examples

Titanium nitride is often used as a coating for cutting tools because of its hardness.
Gallium nitride is key to producing efficient blue LEDs.
Chemists synthesized a new form of aluminum nitride for advanced electronics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oxide - a compound containing oxygen and another element

Etymology

Formed from 'nitro-' (referring to nitrogen) and '-ide' (denoting a binary compound), modeled after similar chemical terminology.
