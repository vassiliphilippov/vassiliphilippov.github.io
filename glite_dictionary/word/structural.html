structural

1. ADJ

/ ˈstrʌktʃərəl / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃ(ə)rəl / (BrE)
relating to or affecting the structure of something, especially a building, system, or object.
relating to structure or organization

Examples

The building was closed due to structural damage caused by the earthquake.
A structural flaw in the bridge design required immediate attention.
These changes are only cosmetic; the house’s structural integrity remains strong.

Synset Examples

structural engineer
structural errors
structural simplicity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: architectural - relating to architecture or building design
  • Antonym: functional - relating to the purpose or function, not structure

Etymology

From Latin structura (a fitting together, building).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02908410-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈstrʌktʃɚəl / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃərəl / (BrE)
relating to or concerned with the systematic structure of something, especially within a particular field of study.
relating to systematic structure

Examples

The study focused on the structural aspects of language.
Engineers assessed the building’s structural integrity after the earthquake.
She specializes in structural analyses within the field of biology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: systematic - done or acting according to a fixed plan or system
  • Synonym: structuralism - a method that analyzes cultural phenomena in terms of structures

Etymology

From Latin 'structura' (a fitting together, building).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00588753-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈstrʌktʃɚəl / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃərəl / (BrE)
relating to or affecting the structure, arrangement, or construction of something, especially a building or object
relating to structure or construction

Examples

The earthquake caused serious structural damage to several buildings in the city.
Engineers inspected the structural integrity of the old bridge before reopening it to traffic.
The renovation focused on both aesthetic and structural improvements to the house.

Synset Examples

the structural details of a house such as beams and joists and rafters, not ornamental elements
structural damage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ornamental - serving or intended as decoration, not structural

Etymology

From structure + -al, ultimately from Latin structura (a fitting together, building).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01094286-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈstrʌktʃɚəl / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃərəl / (BrE)
relating to or resulting from the arrangement or organization of parts within something, especially a political, social, or economic system.
relating to structure or system organization

Examples

The country faces structural issues in its economy that cannot be solved overnight.
A structural change in government policy is necessary to address the housing crisis.
Many regions struggle with structural unemployment caused by technological advancements.

Synset Examples

structural unemployment in a technological society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: systemic - affecting or relating to a system as a whole rather than individual parts

Etymology

From Latin structura (a fitting together, a building), from struere (to build).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02908587-a
Synset POS: a

5. ADJ

/ ˈstrʌktʃɚəl / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃərəl / (BrE)
relating to the arrangement or organization of rocks, layers, or formations in the earth's crust, especially as affected by geological processes such as folding and faulting
relating to the structure of geological formations

Examples

The scientist analyzed the structural effects of faulting on the mountain range.
Evidence of previous earthquakes was found in the structural deformation of the sedimentary layers.
Geologists discussed the structural characteristics of the region to assess earthquake risks.

Synset Examples

geomorphological features of the Black Hills
morphological features of granite
structural effects of folding and faulting of the earth's surface

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geological - relating to the science of the earth's physical structure

Etymology

From structure + -al, based on Latin structura (building, arrangement).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02960848-a
Synset POS: a
