triptych

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪp.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈtrɪp.tɪk / (BrE)
a work of art, usually a painting or carving, made up of three panels, often hinged together so that side panels can fold over the central one
artwork on three panels

Examples

The museum displayed a famous triptych from the Renaissance period.
She painted a triptych depicting the stages of human life.
The religious triptych featured saints on the side panels and Christ in the center.

Etymology

From Greek triptychos, from tri- (three) + ptychē (fold).
