homophile

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊməˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈhəʊməˌfaɪl / (BrE)
a person who is sexually attracted to people of the same sex
person attracted to same sex

Examples

The activist described himself as a proud homophile during a 1960s interview.
Many early LGBT rights organizations were formed by groups of homophiles.
The term homophile has largely been replaced by 'gay' or 'lesbian' in modern usage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homosexual - a person who is sexually attracted to people of the same sex
  • Antonym: heterosexual - a person who is sexually attracted to people of the opposite sex

Etymology

From Greek 'homo-' (same) and '-phile' (lover, friend).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊ.məˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈhəʊ.mə.faɪl / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by sexual attraction to people of one's own sex; homosexual, especially as used to denote social and cultural initiatives focused on homosexuality.
relating to homosexuality or homosexual people
archaic

Examples

The homophile movement gained momentum in the 1950s and 60s, advocating for equal rights.
Her research focused on homophile organizations within post-war Europe.
Many early homophile groups provided safe spaces for people to meet and discuss social issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homosexual - a person attracted to others of the same sex
  • Antonym: heterophile - a person attracted to the opposite sex

Etymology

From Greek 'homo-' (same) + 'philos' (loving).
