profitable

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑːfɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪtəbəl / (BrE)
(of an activity, business, or investment) yielding material gain or financial profit.
yielding a financial or material gain

Examples

Their new venture proved to be extremely profitable in its first year.
She is always searching for profitable opportunities to invest her savings.
It wasn't a profitable decision for the small company, but it helped build their reputation.

Synset Examples

profitable speculation on the stock market

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unprofitable - not yielding profit or gain
  • Synonym: lucrative - producing a great deal of profit

Etymology

From Old French 'profitable', from Latin 'profitabilis', from 'profitari' (to declare, acknowledge, or make progress).
