Ismaili

1. NOUN

/ ɪsˈmaɪli / (AmE), / ɪsˈmɑːɪli / (BrE)
a member of the Ismaili branch of Shi'a Islam
member of the Ismaili branch of Shi'a Islam

Examples

The Ismaili community has a long history of cultural and philanthropic achievements.
He identifies as an Ismaili Muslim and participates in related religious events.
Many Ismailis live in countries such as Pakistan, India, and East Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shiite - a member of the branch of Islam that regards Ali as the legitimate successor to Muhammad
  • Antonym: Sunni - a member of the largest branch of Islam, differing from Shia

Etymology

From Arabic إسماعيلي (Ismā‘īlī), relating to Ismā‘īl, the eldest son of Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq, from whom the sect derives its name.

2. ADJ

/ ɪsˈmɑː.i.li / (AmE), / ɪsˈmɑː.iːli / (BrE)
relating to a branch of Shiite Islam known for its esoteric philosophy and distinct beliefs
of a Shiite Islamic branch with esoteric teachings

Examples

The Ismaili community is recognized for its emphasis on intellectual engagement and social justice.
He studied Ismaili teachings at a university renowned for its religious studies program.
Many Ismaili centers around the world offer public lectures on philosophy and history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shiite - a member of the branch of Islam that regards Ali as the legitimate successor to Muhammad
  • Antonym: Sunni - member of the largest branch of Islam, differing in leadership succession

Etymology

From Arabic Ismāʿīlī, referring to followers of Ismail ibn Jafar, a key figure in Shiism.
