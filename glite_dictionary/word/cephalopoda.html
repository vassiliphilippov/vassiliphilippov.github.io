Cephalopoda

1. NOUN

/ sɛfəˈlɑpədə / (AmE), / ˌsɛfələˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
A class of mollusks that includes octopuses, squids, cuttlefish, and nautiluses, characterized by bilateral body symmetry, a large head, and arms or tentacles.
class of mollusks (octopuses, squids, etc.)

Examples

Among marine animals, Cephalopoda are renowned for their intelligence and complex behavior.
Scientists have discovered new species within the Cephalopoda in deep-sea explorations.
The evolutionary history of Cephalopoda reveals remarkable adaptability over millions of years.

Etymology

From Greek 'kephalē' (head) + 'pous'/'pod-' (foot).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛf.əˈlɑp.ə.də / (AmE), / ˌsɛf.əˈlɒp.ə.də / (BrE)
A class of mollusks that includes octopuses, squids, cuttlefish, and nautiluses, characterized by distinct heads and tentacles.
class of mollusks: octopuses, squids, etc.

Examples

The Cephalopoda display remarkable intelligence among invertebrates.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Cephalopoda in the Pacific Ocean.
Many textbooks use squids and octopuses as examples of Cephalopoda.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mollusk - invertebrate animal of a large phylum, including snails, slugs, and cephalopods

Etymology

From Greek 'kephalē' (head) + 'pous, podos' (foot).
