enquiry

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkwaɪəri / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwaɪəri / (BrE)
the act of seeking information or knowledge; an investigation or questioning
an act of seeking information

Examples

The committee launched an enquiry into the allegations of misconduct.
She made an enquiry at the front desk about lost property.
After much enquiry, the truth finally came to light.

Synset Examples

their pottery deserves more research than it has received

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inquiry - an act of asking for information or an investigation

Etymology

From Old French *enquerre*, from Latin *inquirere*, meaning 'to seek for, search for'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkwaɪri / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwaɪəri / (BrE)
an official investigation into a matter of public interest, usually conducted by a committee or authority
official investigation; public matter
british

Examples

The government announced a public enquiry into the causes of the disaster.
A formal enquiry was set up to investigate the allegations of misconduct.
The enquiry uncovered several irregularities in the company’s financial records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inquiry - an official investigation or formal examination (esp. US spelling)
  • Synonym: investigation - the act of investigating; a careful search or examination

Etymology

From Old French 'enquerre' or 'enquerre', from Latin 'inquirere' (to seek after, inquire).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00637971-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkwaɪəri / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwaɪəri / (BrE)
an act of asking for information; a question or investigation
an instance of asking for information
british

Examples

She made an enquiry at the desk about train times.
All enquiries regarding the event should be directed to the main office.
After his enquiry, the staff provided detailed information.

Synset Examples

there was a question about my training
we made inquiries of all those who were present

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inquiry - an act of asking for information or an investigation (American and standard spelling)
  • Synonym: question - a sentence or phrase used to seek information

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'inquiry', from Late Latin 'inquirere' (to seek for).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07208256-n
Synset POS: n
