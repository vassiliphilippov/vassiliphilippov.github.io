Gymnopilus ventricosus

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˈnɑpɪləs vɛnˌtrɪˈkoʊsəs / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈnɒpɪləs vɛnˌtrɪˈkəʊsəs / (BrE)
a species of large mushroom native to the Pacific Northwest, known for its thick stalk and a cortina that leaves a ring high on the stem
a large mushroom species of the Pacific Northwest

Examples

The Gymnopilus ventricosus can be easily identified by the ring left by its cortina high up on the stalk.
Mycology students studied the growth patterns of Gymnopilus ventricosus in Oregon forests.
Unlike some other mushrooms, Gymnopilus ventricosus has a particularly thick, sturdy stalk.

Etymology

From New Latin: Gymnopilus from Greek gumnos (naked) + pilos (cap); ventricosus from Latin (swollen, bulging).
