impenetrability

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpɛnɪtrəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɛnɪtrəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible to pass through, enter, or understand.
the state of being impossible to penetrate

Examples

The impenetrability of the fortress walls deterred any would-be invaders.
The novel was criticized for the impenetrability of its plot and prose style.
Dense fog added to the impenetrability of the forest, making it difficult to find the path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opacity - the quality of being opaque; not allowing light to pass through
  • Antonym: transparency - the quality of allowing light to pass through; opposite of impenetrability

Etymology

From Latin impenetrabilis (not to be penetrated) + English -ity.

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpɛnɪtrəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɛnɪtrəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely difficult or impossible to understand due to complexity or obscurity
quality of being too difficult to understand

Examples

The impenetrability of his writing style makes the novel a challenging read for most people.
Some critics complain about the impenetrability of modern philosophy.
Her lecture was marked by such impenetrability that hardly anyone could follow her arguments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opacity - the quality of being difficult to understand or explain
  • Antonym: lucidity - the quality of being easy to understand; clarity

Etymology

From Latin 'impenetrabilis' (not to be penetrated) + '-ity'.
