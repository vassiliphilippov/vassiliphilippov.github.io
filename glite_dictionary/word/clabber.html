clabber

1. VERB

/ ˈklæb.ər / (AmE), / ˈklæb.ə / (BrE)
(of milk) to form curds or become thick and sour; to curdle.
(of milk) to curdle or form curds

Examples

If milk is left out too long, it will eventually clabber.
The warm temperature caused the cream to clabber overnight.
Some traditional recipes require the milk to clabber before adding other ingredients.

Synset Examples

curdled milk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to curdle - to separate or cause to separate into curds or lumps

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly from Irish *clabar* (mud, mire) or Scots dialect.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklæbər / (AmE), / ˈklabə / (BrE)
Milk that has soured and thickened, typically used in baking or as a food product.
soured, thickened milk

Examples

She poured the thick clabber over her cornbread for a traditional Southern breakfast.
The recipe called for a cup of clabber instead of buttermilk.
Farmers used to leave fresh milk out overnight to make their own clabber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curd - milk that has coagulated; used in cooking and cheese-making

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic *clabar* (mud, mire) or Irish *clabar* (mud).
