Corallorhiza striata

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˌræləˈraɪzə straɪˈeɪtə / (AmE), / kɒˌraləˈraɪzə straɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A nearly leafless orchid native to western North America, with erect reddish-purple stems and racemes of pale, pinkish, and brownish-striped flowers.
nearly leafless wildflower; western North America orchid

Examples

The rare Corallorhiza striata blooms quietly among the forest floor detritus in early spring.
Botanists were excited to discover a cluster of Corallorhiza striata orchids during their survey.
Unlike many orchids, Corallorhiza striata lacks green leaves and depends on fungi for nourishment.

Etymology

From Latin: 'corallorhiza' meaning 'coral root' (referring to root appearance) and 'striata' (striped).
