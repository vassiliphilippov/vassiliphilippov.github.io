Spheniscus demersus

1. NOUN

/ sfɪˈnɪskəs dɪˈmɜrsəs / (AmE), / sfəˈnɪskəs dɪˈmɜːsəs / (BrE)
A species of penguin found on the coasts of southern Africa, known for its braying call.
African penguin species

Examples

The Spheniscus demersus is easily recognized by the distinctive black band across its chest.
Researchers observed a colony of Spheniscus demersus nesting on the rocky coastline.
The call of the Spheniscus demersus sounds remarkably like a donkey's bray.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African penguin - the common name for Spheniscus demersus

Etymology

From New Latin, with Spheniscus from Greek 'sphēniskos' (little wedge, referring to the shape) and demersus meaning 'plunged' or 'dived' in Latin.
