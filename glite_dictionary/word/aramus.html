Aramus

1. NOUN

/ əˈræməs / (AmE), / əˈræməs / (BrE)
A genus of large, brown, long-billed wading birds found in warm swampy regions of the Americas, especially known for the limpkin.
genus of large brown wading birds

Examples

The Aramus genus is represented by only one living species, the limpkin.
Ornithologists studied the habitat of Aramus birds in the Florida Everglades.
The unique call of the Aramus is often heard near marshlands at dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limpkin - a large brown bird with a long bill, found in swampy regions; species of Aramus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Aramus', the scientific name for the genus.
