Wilhelm Apollinaris de Kostrowitzki

1. PROPN

/ vɪlˈhɛlm apɒlɪˈnarɪs də kɒstrovɪˈtski / (AmE), / vɪlˈhɛlm apɒlɪˈnarɪs də kɒstrəʊˈɪtski / (BrE)
(1880–1918) French poet and writer, regarded as a precursor of surrealism.
French poet and precursor of surrealism

Examples

Many consider Wilhelm Apollinaris de Kostrowitzki a major influence on early twentieth-century French literature.
The avant-garde poetry of Wilhelm Apollinaris de Kostrowitzki paved the way for surrealism.
Scholars often reference Wilhelm Apollinaris de Kostrowitzki when discussing the origins of modernist art movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Guillaume Apollinaire - the pen name under which Wilhelm Apollinaris de Kostrowitzki is best known
