phylactery

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlæk.tə.ri / (AmE), / fɪˈlæk.təri / (BrE)
(in Judaism) either of two small leather boxes containing texts from the Hebrew Scriptures, worn by Jewish men on the forehead and left arm during morning prayers.
Jewish prayer object with Scripture texts

Examples

Jewish boys begin wearing phylacteries during their bar mitzvah ceremonies.
The phylactery is carefully strapped to the arm as part of the morning ritual.
He inherited his grandfather’s phylactery as a cherished family heirloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tefillin - small leather boxes with Scripture texts, worn during Jewish prayer

Etymology

From Late Latin phylacterium, from Greek phylaktērion (amulet, safeguard).
