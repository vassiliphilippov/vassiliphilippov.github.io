emollient

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɑliənt / (AmE), / ɪˈmɒliənt / (BrE)
A thick liquid or cream that softens and soothes the skin, often used for moisturizing.
substance that soothes and moisturizes skin

Examples

The doctor recommended an emollient to relieve her dry, irritated skin.
You should apply an emollient after showering to lock in moisture.
This emollient is specially formulated for sensitive skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moisturizer - a substance used to add moisture to the skin

Etymology

From Latin emolliens, present participle of emollire (to soften).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɑliənt / (AmE), / ɪˈmɒliənt / (BrE)
having a softening or soothing effect, especially on the skin
softening or soothing, esp. for skin

Examples

This cream contains an emollient agent that helps soothe dry skin.
After washing your hands, apply an emollient moisturizer to prevent irritation.
The lotion has emollient properties, making it ideal for sensitive skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soothing - having a calming or relieving effect, especially on the skin or senses
  • Antonym: irritating - causing discomfort or inflammation, especially to the skin

Etymology

From Latin emolliens, present participle of emollīre (to soften).
