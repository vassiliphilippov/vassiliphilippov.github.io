Myxocephalus aenaeus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪk.soʊˈsɛf.əl.əs iˈniː.əs / (AmE), / ˌmɪk.səʊˈsɛf.əl.əs iˈniː.əs / (BrE)
a small sculpin fish found off the coast of New England
a small New England sculpin fish

Examples

The Myxocephalus aenaeus is often studied by marine biologists in New England waters.
Fishermen rarely catch Myxocephalus aenaeus due to its small size and elusive nature.
A detailed illustration of Myxocephalus aenaeus was featured in the coastal wildlife guide.

Etymology

From Greek 'myxos' (slime) + 'kephalē' (head); species name from Latin 'aenaeus' (of bronze).
