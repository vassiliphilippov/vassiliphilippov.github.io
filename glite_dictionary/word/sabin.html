sabin

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪbɪn / (AmE), / ˈseɪbɪn / (BrE)
a unit of acoustic absorption, equal to the absorption by one square foot of a perfectly absorbing surface
unit of acoustic absorption

Examples

The auditorium's sound absorption was measured at five hundred sabins to ensure optimal acoustics.
A material with a higher number of sabins is more effective at absorbing sound.
The engineers calculated the total sabin rating required to reduce echoes in the hall.

Etymology

Named after Wallace C. Sabine, American physicist and founder of architectural acoustics.
