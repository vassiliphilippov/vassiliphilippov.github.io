Eusebius Hieronymus

1. PROPN

/ juːˈsiːbiəs haɪəˈrɒnɪməs / (AmE), / juːˈsiːbiəs hɪəˈrɒnɪməs / (BrE)
(347–420) Also known as St. Jerome; Christian scholar and translator of the Bible into Latin (the Vulgate), recognized as a saint and Doctor of the Church.
early Christian scholar and Bible translator

Examples

Scholars credit Eusebius Hieronymus with the first comprehensive translation of the Bible into Latin.
Many religious texts reference Eusebius Hieronymus for his influential work, the Vulgate.
The feast day of Eusebius Hieronymus is celebrated on September 30th in the Christian calendar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St. Jerome - Christian scholar and translator; more commonly used name for Eusebius Hieronymus

Etymology

From Greek Εὐσέβιος Ἱερώνυμος (Eusebios Hierōnymos); Latinized as Eusebius Hieronymus.
