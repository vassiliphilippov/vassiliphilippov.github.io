antiestablishmentarianism

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.ɪˌstæb.lɪʃ.mənˈtɛr.i.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.ɪˌstæb.lɪʃ.mənˈteə.ri.əˌnɪz.əm / (BrE)
opposition to or doctrine against the established order, authority, or conventional institutions, especially in politics or religion
opposition to the established order or authority

Examples

The group's antiestablishmentarianism was evident in their rejection of traditional political parties.
Debates about antiestablishmentarianism have resurfaced in times of political unrest.
He became known for his outspoken antiestablishmentarianism during the student protests.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: establishmentarianism - support for the established order or authority

Etymology

From anti- (against) + establishment (dominant social order) + -arian (advocating) + -ism (doctrine or belief).
