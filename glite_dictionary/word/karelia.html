Karelia

1. PROPN

/ kəˈriːliə / (AmE), / kəˈrɪəlɪə / (BrE)
a region in northeastern Europe, lying between the Gulf of Finland and the White Sea, divided between Finland and Russia
region divided between Finland and Russia

Examples

Many traditional folk songs originate from Karelia and reflect its rich cultural history.
After the war, a large part of Karelia was ceded to the Soviet Union by Finland.
Tourists visit Karelia for its beautiful lakes, forests, and unique architecture.

Etymology

From Russian Каре́лия (Karéliya), ultimately of Finnic origin.
