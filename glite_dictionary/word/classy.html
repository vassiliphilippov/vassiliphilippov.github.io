classy

1. ADJ

/ ˈklæsi / (AmE), / ˈklɑːsi / (BrE)
Elegant, stylish, and fashionable; showing good taste.
elegant and fashionable

Examples

The restaurant was so classy that even the waiters wore tuxedos.
She always looks classy, no matter what she wears.
They wanted their wedding to have a classy and sophisticated feel.

Synset Examples

classy clothes
a classy dame
a posh restaurant
a swish pastry shop on the Rue du Bac

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elegant - graceful and stylish in appearance or manner
  • Synonym: stylish - fashionable and attractive
  • Antonym: tacky - showing poor taste or quality

Etymology

From class + -y, first attested in American English in the early 20th century.
