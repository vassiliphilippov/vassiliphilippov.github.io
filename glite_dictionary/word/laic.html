laic

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪɪk / (AmE), / ˈleɪɪk / (BrE)
Relating to people who are not members of the clergy; secular or lay.
relating to non-clergy; lay

Examples

He wore a laic robe instead of a priest's vestment.
Their discussion focused on laic responsibilities within the community.
The architecture of the building was distinctly laic, with no religious symbols.

Synset Examples

set his collar in laic rather than clerical position
the lay ministry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lay - not belonging to the clergy; non-professional in religious context
  • Antonym: clerical - relating to the clergy; church-related

Etymology

From Latin 'laicus', from Greek 'laikos' (of the people), from 'laos' (people).
