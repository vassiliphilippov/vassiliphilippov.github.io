potholer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊtˌhoʊlər / (AmE), / ˈpɒtˌhəʊlə / (BrE)
a person who explores caves, especially as a hobby; a caver
person who explores caves
british

Examples

The experienced potholer mapped an uncharted section of the underground cave system.
As a keen potholer, she spends most weekends exploring Britain's deepest caves.
Emergency crews rescued a stranded potholer after a flood cut off his exit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caver - a person who explores caves for recreation

Etymology

From 'pothole' (meaning a cave or vertical shaft) + '-er' (agent suffix); chiefly British.
