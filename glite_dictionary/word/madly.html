madly

1. ADV

/ ˈmædli / (AmE), / ˈmadli / (BrE)
In a wild, uncontrolled, or insane manner.
in a wild or insane manner

Examples

The children ran madly around the playground on the last day of school.
She laughed madly at his unexpected joke, unable to stop herself.
The dog barked madly at the passing cars on the street.

Synset Examples

she behaved insanely
he behaves crazily when he is off his medication
the witch cackled madly
screaming dementedly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insanely - in an insane or wild manner
  • Synonym: crazily - in a crazy or insane way
  • Antonym: calmly - in a calm or controlled manner

Etymology

From 'mad' + '-ly', with 'mad' from Old English *gemǣd* (insane, foolish).

in an uncontrolled or wild manner

Examples
