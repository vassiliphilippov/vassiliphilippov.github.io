meritocracy

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛr.ɪˈtɑk.rə.si / (AmE), / ˌmɛr.ɪˈtɒk.rə.si / (BrE)
A social system in which people are chosen and moved into positions of success, power, and influence based on their abilities and merits rather than class or wealth.
system where advancement is by merit

Examples

Many believe a meritocracy encourages competition and innovation in the workplace.
The school aims to create a meritocracy, where the most talented students excel regardless of background.
Critics argue that a true meritocracy is difficult to achieve in an unequal society.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aristocracy - a class of people holding exceptional rank and privileges
  • Antonym: plutocracy - government by the wealthy

Etymology

From Latin 'meritum' (merit) + Greek 'kratos' (power, rule). Coined in the 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛr.ɪˈtɑː.krə.si / (AmE), / ˌmɛr.ɪˈtɒk.rə.si / (BrE)
a system or belief in which people are selected or advance on the basis of their abilities and talents, rather than their social class, wealth, or birth
system where advancement is based on ability

Examples

Many believe that a meritocracy encourages hard work and rewards talent irrespective of background.
The university aspires to be a meritocracy, admitting students purely on academic achievement.
Critics argue that true meritocracy is difficult to achieve in practice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aristocracy - a class of people holding exceptional rank and privileges, especially the hereditary nobility
  • Antonym: plutocracy - government by the wealthy
  • Synonym: technocracy - government or control by technical experts

Etymology

From Latin 'meritum' (merit) + Greek 'kratos' (power, rule); coined in English in the 1950s.
