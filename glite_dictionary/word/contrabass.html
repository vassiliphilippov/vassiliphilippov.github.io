contrabass

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.trəˌbeɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trəˌbeɪs / (BrE)
the largest and lowest-pitched instrument of the violin family, also known as a double bass
the largest violin-family instrument; double bass

Examples

The orchestra's rhythm section relied on the deep notes of the contrabass.
She has been taking contrabass lessons for two years.
A skilled contrabass player can bring rich depth to a string ensemble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double bass - the largest stringed instrument in the violin family, also called contrabass
  • Degree Higher: cello - a large stringed instrument in the violin family, pitched higher than the contrabass

Etymology

From Italian 'contrabbasso', from 'contra-' (against, counter) + 'basso' (bass).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːn.trəˈbeɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.trəˈbeɪs / (BrE)
(of a musical instrument or voice) pitched an octave below the normal bass range.
octave below normal bass (music)

Examples

The orchestra included a contrabass clarinet, which gave a deep, resonant sound.
He was fascinated by the contrabass flute's unique, rumbling tones.
A rare contrabass saxophone was displayed at the instrument exhibition.

Synset Examples

contrabass or double-bass clarinet

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: subcontrabass - pitched one octave lower than contrabass in music

Etymology

From Italian 'contrabbasso' (double bass), from 'contra-' (against, below) + 'basso' (bass).
