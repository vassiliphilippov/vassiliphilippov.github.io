chinos

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃiːnoʊz / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːnəʊz / (BrE)
casual trousers made from chino cloth, typically cotton and often in light colors
casual cotton trousers

Examples

He prefers wearing chinos to jeans when he goes to the office.
She bought a pair of navy chinos for the summer.
These chinos are comfortable and easy to dress up or down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: khakis - casual trousers made of khaki-colored cotton; sometimes used interchangeably with 'chinos'
  • Antonym: jeans - trousers made from denim fabric

Etymology

From Spanish 'chino' (curly or twilled, referring to the fabric), adopted in English in the late 19th century.
