idolization

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪdələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪdəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
Strong admiration or excessive devotion to someone or something.
strong admiration or devotion

Examples

The idolization of celebrities can sometimes lead to unrealistic expectations.
Her idolization of her mentor inspired her to work even harder.
Some critics argue that the idolization of material success is harmful to society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adoration - deep love and respect
  • Antonym: contempt - lack of respect; strong dislike or disdain

Etymology

From idol + -ization, based on Latin 'idolum' (idol) and the suffix '-ization'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌaɪdəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪdəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of admiring or worshipping someone or something excessively or blindly
excessive admiration or worship

Examples

Her idolization of the singer led her to attend every concert on the tour.
The media's idolization of celebrities can distort public perception.
His idolization of his coach sometimes blinded him to the team's flaws.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: admiration - a feeling of great respect and approval
  • Synonym: worship - the act of showing reverence and adoration for a deity or person
  • Antonym: contempt - the feeling that someone or something is unworthy of respect

Etymology

From 'idol' + '-ization', after French 'idolisation', ultimately from Greek 'eidōlon' (image, idol).
