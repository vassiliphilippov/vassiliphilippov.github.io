convict

1. VERB

/ kənˈvɪkt / (AmE), / kənˈvɪkt / (BrE)
to find or declare someone guilty of a crime, especially in a court of law
declare guilty, especially in court

Examples

The jury convicted him of theft after a lengthy trial.
She was convicted on all counts and sentenced to five years in prison.
If found guilty, the judge will convict the defendant next week.

Synset Examples

The man was convicted of fraud and sentenced

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to acquit - to declare not guilty

Etymology

From Latin 'convictus', past participle of 'convincere' (to conquer, prove, convict).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00908320-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ kənˈvɪkt / (AmE), / kənˈvɪkt / (BrE)
a person who has been found guilty of a crime and is serving a sentence in prison or jail
person serving a prison sentence

Examples

The prison was built to house over two hundred convicts at a time.
As a convict, he lost several civil rights while serving his sentence.
Many convicts participate in educational programs during their incarceration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prisoner - a person deprived of liberty and kept under involuntary restraint, especially in prison
  • Synonym: inmate - a person confined to an institution such as a prison or hospital
  • Antonym: officer - a person in authority at a prison, opposite to a convict

Etymology

From Latin convictus, past participle of convincere (to convict).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09982445-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kənˈvɪkt / (AmE), / kənˈvɪkt / (BrE)
a person who has been found guilty of a crime and sentenced by a court
person convicted of a crime

Examples

After the trial, the judge sentenced the convict to ten years in prison.
The prison is home to several hundred convicts serving long sentences.
As a former convict, he found it difficult to find a job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prisoner - a person who is kept in prison as a punishment or while waiting for trial
  • Antonym: innocent - a person not found guilty of a crime

Etymology

From Latin 'convictus', past participle of 'convincere' (to convict, to prove).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09982638-n
Synset POS: n
