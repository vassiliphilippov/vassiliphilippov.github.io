canvasser

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.və.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæn.və.sə / (BrE)
a person who solicits votes, contributions, or customers, typically by going from place to place
person who solicits votes or contributions

Examples

The canvasser knocked on every door in the neighborhood to gather support for the candidate.
As a political canvasser, she spent her weekends persuading people to vote in the upcoming election.
The charity hired a team of canvassers to solicit donations on the busy streets downtown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petitioner - a person who formally requests something, especially in writing

Etymology

From 'canvass', meaning to solicit votes or opinions, of uncertain origin; possibly from Old French 'canoisser' (to sift).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.və.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæn.və.sə / (BrE)
a person who examines and verifies votes at an election
person who examines election votes

Examples

Each canvasser must carefully check the ballots for accuracy before recording the results.
The election board hired a canvasser to ensure the vote counting was fair and transparent.
After the polls closed, a team of canvassers reviewed the ballots to confirm the final tally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrutineer - person who observes or checks votes in an election

Etymology

From 'canvass' (to solicit), ultimately from Old French 'canvasser' (to toss in a canvas sheet).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.və.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæn.və.sə / (BrE)
a person who seeks support or votes, or who takes or counts votes, especially during an election.
person who gathers or counts votes

Examples

The canvasser double-checked every ballot to ensure the accuracy of the results.
As a canvasser, she traveled from door to door collecting votes for the local election.
During the recount, the canvasser discovered several misplaced ballots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pollster - a person who conducts or analyzes opinion polls
  • Antonym: voter - a person who votes in an election

Etymology

From 'canvass' (to solicit support or votes), from Old French 'canover', possibly from Italian 'canavaccio' (canvas), referring to tossing in a canvas sheet (metaphorically linked to examining or sifting).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09911518-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.və.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæn.və.sə / (BrE)
a person who conducts surveys of public opinion, often by going from place to place to ask people questions
person who surveys public opinion

Examples

The canvasser knocked on every door in the neighborhood to collect opinions about the new park.
As a political canvasser, she spent her weekends surveying voters on key issues.
A skilled canvasser needs to be friendly to encourage honest responses from the public.

Synset Examples

a pollster conducts public opinion polls
a headcounter counts heads

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pollster - a person who conducts or analyzes opinion polls

Etymology

From canvas (to solicit votes, opinions), itself from Middle English, connected with Anglo-French canevasser (to toss in a canvas sheet).
