bacteriophage

1. NOUN

/ bækˈtɪriəˌfeɪdʒ / (AmE), / bækˈtɪəri.əˌfeɪdʒ / (BrE)
a virus that infects and reproduces inside bacteria, often destroying its host in the process
virus that infects bacteria
medical

Examples

The scientist studied a bacteriophage that specifically targets E. coli bacteria.
Some researchers use bacteriophages as an alternative to antibiotics in controlling bacterial infections.
The action of the bacteriophage destroys the host cell, releasing new viral particles.

Synset Examples

phage uses the bacterium's machinery and energy to produce more phage until the bacterium is destroyed and phage is released to invade surrounding bacteria

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phage - a virus that infects bacteria

Etymology

From Greek 'baktērion' (small rod, bacterium) + 'phagein' (to eat).
