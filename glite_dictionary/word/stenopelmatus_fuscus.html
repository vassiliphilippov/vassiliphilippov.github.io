Stenopelmatus fuscus

1. NOUN

/ stɛnoʊˈpɛlmətəs ˈfʌskəs / (AmE), / stiːnəʊˈpɛlmətəs ˈfʌskəs / (BrE)
A large, wingless, nocturnal burrowing insect of the Jerusalem cricket group, found along the Pacific coast of the United States.
a species of burrowing wingless grasshopper-like insect

Examples

The Stenopelmatus fuscus is often spotted at night in California gardens, burrowing in loose soil.
Unlike other crickets, the Stenopelmatus fuscus does not chirp and can sometimes startle gardeners with its large size.
Researchers studying soil health frequently encounter Stenopelmatus fuscus during nocturnal field surveys near the Pacific coast.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Stenopelmatus' from Greek 'stenos' (narrow) and 'pelma' (sole of the foot), 'fuscus' meaning dark or dusky in Latin.
