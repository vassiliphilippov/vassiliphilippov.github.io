humpback

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌmpˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈhʌmpˌbak / (BrE)
an abnormal backward curvature of the spine, causing a humped or rounded back
abnormal backward curve of the spine

Examples

The old man developed a noticeable humpback due to years of poor posture.
Medical treatment can help manage the pain associated with a humpback.
A severe humpback can affect breathing and movement in some cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kyphosis - medical term for abnormal backward curve of the spine
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌmpˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈhʌmpbæk / (BrE)
a large baleen whale with long flippers and a distinctive arching of its back when diving, known for its complex songs and acrobatic behavior
a species of large whale

Examples

Every summer, tourists gather to watch the humpback whales migrating along the coast.
The humpback is easily recognized by the distinctive arch of its back before a dive.
Scientists are studying the songs of the humpback to better understand whale communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humpback whale - another term for the humpback whale (Megaptera novaeangliae)
  • Synonym: blue whale - the largest species of baleen whale
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02068379-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈhʌmpˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈhʌmp.bæk / (BrE)
a person whose back is hunched due to an abnormal curvature of the upper spine; someone with a pronounced hunchback
person with a hunched back

Examples

The old man was a humpback, walking slowly with the aid of a cane.
In Victorian times, people would sometimes hide their humpbacks under layers of clothing.
Medical advances have made it possible to treat spinal deformities that cause a humpback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunchback - a person with a hunchback (hump on the back)

Synset POS: n
