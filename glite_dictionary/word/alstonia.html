Alstonia

1. NOUN

/ ælˈstoʊniə / (AmE), / ælˈstəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical evergreen trees or shrubs with white funnel-shaped flowers and milky sap, native to Africa, southeastern Asia, and Polynesia.
genus of tropical trees or shrubs

Examples

Some species of Alstonia are valued for their medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
The fragrant white flowers of Alstonia trees attract pollinators throughout the tropical forests.
Botanists discovered a new species of Alstonia in the dense jungles of Polynesia.

Etymology

Named after Charles Alston (1683–1760), a Scottish botanist.
