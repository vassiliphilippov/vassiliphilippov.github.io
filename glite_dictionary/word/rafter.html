rafter

1. NOUN

/ ˈræftər / (AmE), / ˈrɑːftə / (BrE)
one of several parallel sloping beams that support a roof
sloping roof support beam

Examples

The old barn's rafters creaked in the wind as the storm passed overhead.
We hung the lanterns from the rafters for the wedding celebration.
Bats could be seen roosting among the rafters of the abandoned church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beam - a long, sturdy piece of wood or metal used in building

Etymology

From Old English *rafter*, related to Old Norse *raptr* (beam).

2. NOUN

/ ˈræf.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɑːf.tə / (BrE)
a person who travels on or navigates a raft, especially as a sport or as a means of transport.
person who travels by raft

Examples

As an experienced rafter, she navigated the whitewater rapids with ease.
Several rafters set out early in the morning to enjoy the river's strong currents.
The rafter tied all of his gear securely before launching into the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boater - someone who travels by boat

Etymology

From raft + -er, denoting a person associated with rafts.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10524138-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈræf.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɑːf.tə / (BrE)
to provide or support a ceiling or roof with rafters
to frame with rafters

Examples

The carpenters will rafter the new barn next week.
They raftered the ceiling to ensure proper support for the tiles.
Modern builders often rafter roofs using prefabricated materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beam - a long, sturdy piece of wood or metal used to support a structure

Etymology

From Old English *ræfter* (beam, rafter).
