necessity

1. NOUN

/ nəˈsɛsəti / (AmE), / nɪˈsɛsɪti / (BrE)
a thing that is essential or indispensable; something that is needed
something essential or indispensable

Examples

Food and water are basic necessities for human survival.
Warm clothing is a necessity in cold climates.
Many people cannot afford even the simple necessities of life.

Synset Examples

food and shelter are necessities of life
the essentials of the good life
allow farmers to buy their requirements under favorable conditions
a place where the requisites of water fuel and fodder can be obtained

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essential - a thing that is absolutely necessary
  • Antonym: luxury - a thing that is not essential but desirable

Etymology

From Latin necessitas (indispensability, compulsion), from necesse (unavoidable, necessary).

2. NOUN

/ nəˈsɛsəti / (AmE), / nɪˈsɛsəti / (BrE)
the state or fact of being essential or indispensable
state of being essential or indispensable

Examples

Water is a basic necessity for all forms of life.
Warm clothing is a necessity during the harsh winter months.
Sometimes, invention is born out of necessity rather than desire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: requirement - something that is needed or demanded
  • Antonym: luxury - something not essential, more for comfort or pleasure

Etymology

From Latin necessitas (inevitability, compulsion, need), from necesse (unavoidable).
