vicuna

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈkuːn(j)ə / (AmE), / vɪˈkuːnjə / (BrE)
the fine wool obtained from the vicuña, a small wild South American camelid, valued for its softness and warmth
fine wool from the vicuña

Examples

The luxurious scarf was made entirely from vicuna and felt incredibly soft.
High-end fashion designers often use vicuna for their most exclusive garments.
Because vicuna is lightweight yet warm, it's prized for winter clothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cashmere - soft, fine wool from the cashmere goat
  • Synonym: alpaca - wool from the alpaca, another South American mammal

Etymology

From Spanish 'vicuña', from Quechua 'wik'uña'.

2. NOUN

/ vɪˈkuːn.jə / (AmE), / vɪˈkuː.njə / (BrE)
A soft and luxurious wool fabric made from the fleece of the vicuña, a South American animal related to the llama.
fine wool fabric from vicuña fleece

Examples

The tailor recommended a vicuna suit for its incredible softness and warmth.
Her scarf, woven from vicuna, felt lighter and finer than cashmere.
Luxury brands often feature vicuna in their exclusive winter collections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alpaca - a soft wool fabric made from the fleece of the alpaca

Etymology

From Spanish vicuña, from Quechua wik’uña, the name of the animal.

3. NOUN

/ vɪˈkuːn.jə, vaɪ- / (AmE), / vɪˈkuːn.jə / (BrE)
a small wild South American mammal of the Andes, closely related to the llama and prized for its fine, soft wool
small Andean mammal valued for fine wool

Examples

The vicuna is protected by law because its wool is highly sought after.
On their trip to Peru, they saw a herd of wild vicunas grazing in the mountains.
A scarf made from vicuna wool can be incredibly soft and lightweight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guanaco - a closely related larger Andean mammal

Etymology

From Spanish 'vicuña', originally from Quechua 'wik'uña'.
