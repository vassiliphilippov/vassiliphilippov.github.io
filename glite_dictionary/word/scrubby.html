scrubby

1. ADJ

/ ˈskrʌbi / (AmE), / ˈskrʌbi / (BrE)
(of land or vegetation) sparsely covered with stunted trees, shrubs, or underbrush; consisting of or resembling scrub.
with sparse, stunted vegetation

Examples

The scrubby hills stretched for miles under the hot sun.
A variety of birds make their nests in the scrubby undergrowth near the river.
We walked all afternoon through scrubby fields dotted with thorn bushes and wildflowers.

Synset Examples

open scrubby woods

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lush - richly growing, thick vegetation

Etymology

From 'scrub' (vegetation) + -y (adjective suffix), 18th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈskrʌbi / (AmE), / ˈskrʌbi / (BrE)
(of plants or areas) inferior in size or quality; stunted, meager, or unimpressive.
inferior in size or quality; stunted

Examples

The hills were covered in scrubby bushes, offering little shade from the sun.
A scrubby pine struggled to grow amid the rocky soil.
Their cattle grazed on scrubby grass that barely covered the dry ground.

Synset Examples

scrawny cattle
scrubby cut-over pine
old stunted thorn trees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stunted - unnaturally short or undersized
  • Antonym: lush - luxuriant, rich, and healthy (vegetation)

Etymology

From 'scrub' (low trees or bushes) + -y (adjectival suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02356454-s
Synset POS: s
