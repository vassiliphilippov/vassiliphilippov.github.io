Monarda citriodora

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈnɑːrdə sɪˌtriːəˈdɔːrə / (AmE), / məʊˈnɑːdə sɪˌtrɪəˈdɔːrə / (BrE)
an annual herb of the mint family, native to central and western United States and northern Mexico, known for its lemon-scented leaves and pink to lavender flowers
lemon-scented annual herb of North America

Examples

Monarda citriodora attracts bees and butterflies to gardens with its vibrant flowers.
Native Americans traditionally used Monarda citriodora for its medicinal properties.
The leaves of Monarda citriodora release a refreshing lemony fragrance when crushed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsemint - a common name for plants in the genus Monarda

Etymology

From Latin: Monarda (genus name honoring botanist Nicolás Monardes) + citriodora (Latin for 'lemon-scented').
