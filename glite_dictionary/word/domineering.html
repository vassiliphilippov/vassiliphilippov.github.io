domineering

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɑːməˈnɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdɒmɪˈnɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
asserting one's will over others in an arrogant or overbearing way
assertively overbearing; bossy

Examples

Her domineering attitude made it difficult for anyone to express a different opinion.
He grew tired of working for such a domineering boss who never listened to his ideas.
Although she cared for her family, her domineering nature sometimes caused tension at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overbearing - unpleasantly or arrogantly domineering
  • Antonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority or will of others

Etymology

From 'domineer', which derives from Latin 'dominus' meaning 'lord' or 'master'.
