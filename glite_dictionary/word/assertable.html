assertable

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɝː.tə.bəl / (AmE), / əˈsɜː.tə.bəl / (BrE)
Able to be stated or affirmed as true; capable of being asserted.
able to be asserted

Examples

The claim is clearly assertable given the available evidence.
That is an assertable proposition within the rules of logic.
Not every statement in politics is assertable as a fact.

Synset Examples

a quality affirmable of every member of the family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affirmable - able to be affirmed

Etymology

From 'assert' + '-able'; 'assert' from Latin 'assertus', past participle of 'asserere' (to claim, affirm).
