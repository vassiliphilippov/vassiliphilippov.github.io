remittal

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmɪt̬əl / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪt.əl / (BrE)
the act of sending money as a payment, especially to someone in another place
payment sent to someone elsewhere

Examples

She arranged for the remittal of funds to her family overseas every month.
Your remittal must be received by the fifteenth to avoid a late fee.
The company confirmed the remittal of the invoice payment last week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remittance - a sum of money sent, especially by mail, in payment for goods or services or as a gift

Etymology

From Latin 'remittere' (to send back) via Medieval Latin 'remittalis'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈmɪt.əl / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪt.əl / (BrE)
a reduction or decrease in the intensity or degree of something, especially the symptoms of a disease
decrease in intensity, especially of symptoms
medical

Examples

The patient's fever showed a marked remittal after the new medication was administered.
Some chronic diseases are characterized by periods of remittal and relapse.
Doctors monitored the remittal of symptoms to assess the effect of the experimental treatment.

Synset Examples

his cancer is in remission

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remission - a temporary reduction or disappearance of symptoms

Etymology

From Latin 'remittere' (to send back, relax).

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈmɪtəl / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪtəl / (BrE)
the act of absolving, remitting, or forgiving, especially as a formal pronouncement of pardon in a religious or legal context
formal act of remission or forgiveness

Examples

The priest granted remittal of sins after hearing her confession.
His legal team appealed for the remittal of his sentence, citing good behavior.
The ceremony ended with a formal remittal of the congregation's transgressions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolution - formal release from guilt, obligation, or punishment
  • Synonym: remission - the cancellation of a debt, charge, or penalty

Etymology

From Latin 'remittere' (to send back, relax, remit) via Late Latin 'remissio'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
