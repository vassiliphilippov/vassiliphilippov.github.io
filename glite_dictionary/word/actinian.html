actinian

1. NOUN

/ ækˈtɪniən / (AmE), / ækˈtɪniən / (BrE)
any sea anemone or a related animal belonging to the order Actiniaria, typically having a soft body with tentacles.
sea anemone or related animal

Examples

The actinian waved its colorful tentacles as the tide rolled in.
Researchers studied the diet of the actinian inhabiting coral reefs.
A variety of fish seek shelter among the stinging tentacles of the actinian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea anemone - a marine coelenterate of the order Actiniaria

Etymology

From Modern Latin Actinia (a genus of sea anemones) + -an.
