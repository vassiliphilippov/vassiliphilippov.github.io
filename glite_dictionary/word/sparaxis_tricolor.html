Sparaxis tricolor

1. NOUN

/ spəˈræksɪs traɪˈkʌlɔr / (AmE), / spəˈræksɪs traɪˈkʌlə / (BrE)
A showy, often-cultivated plant species with tawny yellow flowers often spotted with purple, native to South Africa.
ornamental plant with colorful spotted flowers

Examples

The vibrant blooms of Sparaxis tricolor make it a popular choice for spring gardens.
Botanists admire the striking coloration of Sparaxis tricolor in its natural habitat.
We planted several Sparaxis tricolor bulbs along the garden path last autumn.

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Sparaxis' from Greek 'sparasso' (to tear), and 'tricolor' from Latin for 'three-colored'.
