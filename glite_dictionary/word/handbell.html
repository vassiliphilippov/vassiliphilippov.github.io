handbell

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌbɛl / (AmE), / ˈhændˌbɛl / (BrE)
A small bell designed to be held and rung by hand, often used in musical performances or for signaling.
small bell rung by hand

Examples

The conductor signaled for the handbell players to begin the next piece.
She bought a brass handbell as a souvenir from the church.
During the festival, children paraded through the streets ringing a handbell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell - a hollow object that makes a ringing sound when struck

Etymology

Compound of 'hand' and 'bell', common since the 15th century for bells held in the hand.
