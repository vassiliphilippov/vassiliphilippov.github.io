apheresis

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɛrəsɪs / (AmE), / əˈfɛrəsɪs / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which blood is taken from a person, separated into its components, and certain parts are removed while the rest are returned to the donor.
blood separation procedure
medical

Examples

During apheresis, platelets were collected from the donor and other blood components were returned.
Doctors recommended apheresis to reduce the high white blood cell count in the patient.
She volunteered to participate in a plasma apheresis program for research purposes.

Etymology

From Greek aphaeresis (a taking away), from apo- (away) + hairein (to take).

2. NOUN

/ əˈfɛrəsɪs / (AmE), / əˈfɛːrəsɪs / (BrE)
(linguistics) The omission of one or more sounds or letters from the beginning of a word, as in 'coon' from 'raccoon' or 'till' from 'until'.
omission of sounds at word's start

Examples

Linguists use the term apheresis to describe the loss of sounds at the beginning of words.
The word 'squire' originated through apheresis from 'esquire'.
Examples of apheresis include 'special' shortened to ''special' in speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elision - omission of sounds in speech or writing, often within or at the end of words
  • Antonym: apocope - omission of one or more sounds from the end of a word

Etymology

From Greek aphaíresis (a taking away, removal), from apo- (away) + hairein (to take).
