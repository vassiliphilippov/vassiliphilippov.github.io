route

1. VERB

/ raʊt / (AmE), / ruːt / (BrE)
to send or direct something along a particular path or through a specific process.
send or direct along a path

Examples

The system will automatically route your call to the appropriate department.
All internet traffic is routed through secure servers for safety.
Please route all correspondence to our new office address.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to redirect - to send something to a different address or direction
  • Synonym: forward - to send something onward to another place

Etymology

From French 'route', from Latin 'rupta (via)' meaning 'broken way', originally referring to a road.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ruːt, raʊt / (AmE), / ruːt / (BrE)
an established path, course, or way for travel or access between places
established path or course for travel

Examples

The main route to the city is closed for repairs, so we have to take a detour.
She planned the route carefully to avoid heavy traffic during rush hour.
Tourists often follow a popular route along the coast to see the best sights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: path - a way or track laid down for walking or made by continual treading
  • Synonym: road - a wide way leading from one place to another, especially one with a specially prepared surface
  • Antonym: destination - the place to which someone or something is going or being sent

Etymology

From Old French 'rute', from Latin 'rupta (via)' meaning 'broken way'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

/ ruːt, raʊt / (AmE), / ruːt, raʊt / (BrE)

send or direct to a destination

Examples

Related Terms

From French 'route' (way, road), originally from Latin 'rupta' (broken way, road cut through).
