Charles Herbert Best

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɑrlz ˈhɜrbərt bɛst / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlz ˈhɜːbət bɛst / (BrE)
(1899–1978) Canadian physiologist who, with Frederick Banting, played a key role in the discovery of insulin.
Canadian physiologist; co-discoverer of insulin

Examples

Charles Herbert Best contributed to one of the most important medical discoveries of the twentieth century.
Many medical textbooks highlight the collaboration between Charles Herbert Best and Frederick Banting.
Insulin was first isolated by Frederick Banting and Charles Herbert Best in the early 1920s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Frederick Banting - Canadian medical scientist who co-discovered insulin
  • Synonym: insulin - A hormone discovered by Banting and Best
