Dryopteris goldiana

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈɑptərɪs ˈɡoʊldiənə / (AmE), / drɪˈɒptərɪs ˈɡəʊldiənə / (BrE)
A North American fern species characterized by a blackish, glossy stipe and large fronds.
North American fern with blackish stipe

Examples

Dryopteris goldiana is commonly found in moist, wooded areas across eastern North America.
Botanists admire the large fronds of Dryopteris goldiana for their elegant appearance.
The shiny black stipe makes Dryopteris goldiana easy to identify among ferns.

Etymology

Named after John Goldie, a Scottish botanist; genus name from Greek 'dryas' (oak) and 'pteris' (fern).
