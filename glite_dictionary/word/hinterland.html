hinterland

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪntɚˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈhɪntəˌlænd / (BrE)
An area lying far from major cities or coastal regions, often remote and underdeveloped.
remote or undeveloped area

Examples

Few people live in the hinterland far from the busy coastal cities.
Wild animals still roam freely in the hinterland of Australia.
The explorers set out to map the dense forests of the African hinterland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backcountry - remote or sparsely inhabited rural area
  • Synonym: wilderness - natural, uncultivated, and uninhabited region

Etymology

From German 'Hinterland' meaning 'land behind'.
