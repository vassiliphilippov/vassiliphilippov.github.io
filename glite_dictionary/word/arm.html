arm

1. NOUN

/ ɑrm / (AmE), / ɑːm / (BrE)
a part of a large organization, institution, or system that is responsible for a particular activity or function
division or branch of an organization

Examples

The humanitarian arm of the United Nations provides aid to refugees worldwide.
The financial arm of the corporation manages investments and budgets.
Their European arm has recently opened a new office in Paris.

Synset Examples

a branch of Congress
botany is a branch of biology
the Germanic branch of Indo-European languages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branch - a division of a large organization or system responsible for a specific area or function

2. NOUN

/ ɑːrm / (AmE), / ɑːm / (BrE)
a weapon or tool used in fighting or hunting
weapon or tool used for combat or hunting

Examples

He was discovered carrying an illegal arm in his bag.
The hunters each brought a different arm to the expedition.
Many medieval knights were skilled in the use of every arm available on the battlefield.

Synset Examples

he was licensed to carry a weapon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weapon - an object used for attacking or defending
  • Degree Lower: firearm - a gun or rifle

Etymology

From Latin arma (weapons, arms), plural of arma (tool, weapon, defensive means).

3. NOUN

/ ɑrm / (AmE), / ɑːm / (BrE)
the upper limb of the human body, extending from the shoulder to the hand, or more technically, from the shoulder to the elbow
upper limb of the human body

Examples

She broke her arm while playing basketball.
He lifted the box with one arm and opened the door with the other.
Her arm was covered in a colorful tattoo.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leg - human limb used for walking, extending from the hip to the foot
  • Degree Lower: forearm - part of the arm between the elbow and the wrist
  • Degree Lower: hand - the end part of the arm beyond the wrist

Etymology

From Old English *earm*; related to German *Arm*, Latin *armus* (shoulder, upper arm).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05571403-n
Synset POS: n

The antique chair has beautifully carved wooden arms on both sides.

6. VERB

/ ɑrm / (AmE), / ɑːm / (BrE)
to supply or equip with weapons or military equipment
supply with weapons

Examples

The government decided to arm the rebel forces with advanced rifles and ammunition.
As tensions rose, both sides began to arm themselves for possible conflict.
The nation agreed not to arm its military with nuclear weapons.

Synset Examples

The U.S. armed the freedom fighters in Afghanistan

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to disarm - to take weapons away from someone

Etymology

From Latin arma (weapons).

7. VERB

/ ɑrm / (AmE), / ɑːm / (BrE)
to equip or prepare oneself or others for conflict, especially by collecting weapons or increasing military strength
equip with weapons or for conflict

Examples

The government decided to arm its forces after tensions escalated at the border.
Villagers quickly began to arm themselves when rumors of an invasion spread.
The rebels armed their supporters in preparation for the uprising.

Synset Examples

The U.S. is girding for a conflict in the Middle East
troops are building up on the Iraqi border

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to disarm - to take away weapons or make unable to use weapons
  • Synonym: to equip - provide with necessary items, especially for a specific purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'arma' (arms, weapons), via Old French 'armer'.
