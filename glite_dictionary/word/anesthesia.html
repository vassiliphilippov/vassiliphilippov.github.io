anesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ˌænəsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
Loss of bodily sensation, often with loss of consciousness, especially as induced by medical drugs to perform surgical procedures without pain.
loss of sensation (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient was given anesthesia before the surgery began.
After the dental procedure, the anesthesia wore off slowly, leaving her mouth numb for hours.
Different types of anesthesia are used depending on the operation and the patient's health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analgesia - the absence of pain in response to stimulation that would normally be painful

Etymology

From Greek anaisthēsia, from an- (without) + aisthēsis (sensation).
