overcareful

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈkɛr.fəl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkeə.fəl / (BrE)
Excessively or unduly careful; taking more care than is necessary or reasonable.
excessively careful

Examples

He was so overcareful about locking the doors that he checked them three times before leaving.
Some parents can be overcareful, not allowing their children any independence.
The overcareful editor checked every citation twice, just to be sure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: careful - giving attention or thought to avoiding harm or mistakes
  • Antonym: careless - not giving sufficient attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors

Etymology

Formed by prefix 'over-' (excessively) + 'careful'.
