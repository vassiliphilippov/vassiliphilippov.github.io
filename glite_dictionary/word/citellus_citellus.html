Citellus citellus

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈtɛləs sɪˈtɛləs / (AmE), / sɪˈtɛləs sɪˈtɛləs / (BrE)
a species of ground squirrel found in central and eastern Europe and parts of central Asia, known for inhabiting open grasslands
Eurasian ground squirrel species

Examples

The Citellus citellus is considered vulnerable due to habitat loss across its range.
Ecologists are studying Citellus citellus to understand the impacts of grassland management.
The burrowing activities of Citellus citellus contribute to soil aeration in their habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European souslik - another name for Citellus citellus, the Eurasian ground squirrel

Etymology

From Latin, the genus and species name assigned by zoological taxonomy.
