betweenbrain

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈtwinˌbreɪn / (AmE), / bɪˈtwiːnˌbreɪn / (BrE)
the posterior part of the forebrain that connects the cerebral hemispheres with the midbrain (mesencephalon); also called the diencephalon.
posterior part of the forebrain; diencephalon
medical

Examples

The thalamus is located within the betweenbrain and plays a vital role in sensory processing.
Damage to the betweenbrain can affect hormonal regulation in the body.
The hypothalamus, a key part of the betweenbrain, helps control temperature and hunger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diencephalon - the region of the brain containing structures such as the thalamus and hypothalamus

Etymology

Formed from 'between' + 'brain', referring to its position between major brain regions.
