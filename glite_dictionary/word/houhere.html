houhere

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊhɛr(i) / (AmE), / ˈhaʊhɛər(i) / (BrE)
A small tree or shrub native to New Zealand, known for its clusters of honey-scented, white flowers and bark used for making cordage.
New Zealand tree with white, fragrant flowers

Examples

The houhere produces delicate white blossoms in early summer across many parts of New Zealand.
Traditional Maori rope was sometimes made from the bark of the houhere.
The honey scent from the houhere flowers attracts bees and other pollinators.

Etymology

From Maori *houhere*, the indigenous term for the plant.
