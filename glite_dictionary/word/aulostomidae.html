Aulostomidae

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˌloʊˈstɑːməˌdiː / (AmE), / ɔːˌlɒˈstɒmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of elongated marine fishes known as trumpetfishes, characterized by their extended tubular snouts and found in tropical and subtropical waters.
family of marine trumpetfishes

Examples

The family Aulostomidae consists of species commonly known as trumpetfishes.
Members of Aulostomidae are found in tropical and subtropical marine environments worldwide.
Scientists studied the distinct feeding habits of the Aulostomidae during the coral reef survey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trumpetfish - elongate marine fish with a tubular snout, belonging to the family Aulostomidae

Etymology

From New Latin Aulostomidae, based on Greek aulos (pipe) + stoma (mouth), referring to their tubular snout.

2. NOUN

/ ɔːˌlɒstəˈmɪdi / (AmE), / ɔːˌlɒstəʊˈmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of elongated, marine fishes known as trumpetfishes, characterized by long tubular snouts and found mainly in tropical and subtropical waters.
family of trumpetfishes

Examples

The genus Aulostomus is the principal representative of the Aulostomidae family.
Aulostomidae can be found in coral reefs throughout the Caribbean and Indo-Pacific regions.
Researchers are studying the unique feeding habits of the Aulostomidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trumpetfish - a species of fish in the family Aulostomidae, having an elongated body and snout

Etymology

From Greek aulos (pipe, flute) + stoma (mouth); used in scientific Latin to name this fish family.
