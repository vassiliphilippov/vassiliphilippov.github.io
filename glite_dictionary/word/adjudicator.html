adjudicator

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʒuːdɪˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / əˈdʒuːdɪˌkeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who makes a formal judgment or decision about a problem or dispute
someone who settles disputes or judges competitions

Examples

The adjudicator listened carefully to both sides before making a decision.
In the debate tournament, the adjudicator provided detailed feedback to each participant.
If the teams cannot agree, a neutral adjudicator will be appointed to resolve the issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judge - a person who decides in a court of law or competition

Etymology

From Latin adjūdicātor (judge, one who awards).
