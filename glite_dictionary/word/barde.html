to barde

1. VERB

/ bɑrd / (AmE), / bɑːd / (BrE)
to cover a horse with ornamental or protective coverings, especially for a ceremonial or festive occasion
to put ornamental covering on a horse
archaic

Examples

The knights would barde their horses before entering the tournament.
During the parade, the handlers barded the royal steeds with richly decorated cloths.
In preparation for battle, the soldiers began barding their mounts with heavy armor.

Synset Examples

caparison the horses for the festive occasion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caparison - to put ornamental coverings on a horse

Etymology

From Old French 'barder' (to cover with a caparison), from 'barde' (horse armor).
