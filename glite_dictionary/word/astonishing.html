astonishing

1. ADJ

/ əˈstɑːnɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈstɒnɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
very surprising; causing a feeling of great wonder or amazement.
very surprising; amazing

Examples

The magician's final trick was truly astonishing to the entire audience.
She made an astonishing recovery after the surgery, surprising all the doctors.
It is astonishing how quickly technology advances these days.

Synset Examples

she does an amazing amount of work
the dog was capable of astonishing tricks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amazing - very surprising, causing great wonder
  • Antonym: ordinary - not unusual or surprising; normal

Etymology

From 'astonish' (Old French 'estoner', from Latin 'extonare' meaning 'to thunder out').

2. ADJ

/ əˈstɑːnɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈstɒnɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely surprising or impressive; causing amazement or wonder.
extremely surprising or impressive

Examples

The speed of technological innovation these days is truly astonishing.
Her recovery from the accident was astonishing to her doctors and family.
The artist's use of color and light in the painting is simply astonishing.

Synset Examples

such an enormous response was astonishing
an astounding achievement
the amount of money required was staggering
suffered a staggering defeat
the figure inside the boucle dress was stupefying

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amazing - causing great surprise or wonder
  • Synonym: staggering - deeply shocking; astonishing
  • Antonym: ordinary - not unusual or remarkable

Etymology

From the verb 'astonish', from Old French 'estoner' (to stun, amaze), from Latin 'extonare' (to thunder out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01286247-s
Synset POS: s
