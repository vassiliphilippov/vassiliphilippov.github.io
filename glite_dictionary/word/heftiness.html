heftiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛftinəs / (AmE), / ˈhɛftinɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being hefty, especially in terms of muscular strength or heaviness
state of being hefty or strong

Examples

His impressive heftiness made him a natural choice for the wrestling team.
Despite her heftiness, she moved with surprising agility on the dance floor.
The boxer's heftiness intimidated his opponents before the match even began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strength - physical power and energy
  • Antonym: frailty - the state of being weak or delicate

Etymology

From 'hefty' (strong, heavy) + '-ness' (noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛftinəs / (AmE), / ˈhɛftinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being heavy or having a large mass
quality of being heavy or massive

Examples

The heftiness of the suitcase made it difficult to lift off the ground.
She was surprised by the heftiness of the chocolate cake after picking it up.
Engineers considered the heftiness of the machine when designing the support structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heaviness - the quality of being heavy in weight
  • Antonym: lightness - the quality of weighing very little

Etymology

From 'hefty' (meaning heavy or weighty) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
