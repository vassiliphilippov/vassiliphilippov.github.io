sea trifoly

1. NOUN

/ siː ˈtraɪfoʊli / (AmE), / siː ˈtrɪfəʊli / (BrE)
a small fleshy herb native to North American seashores and brackish marshes, bearing pink or white flowers
fleshy herb of North American seashores

Examples

The salty air and sandy soil provide an ideal habitat for sea trifoly.
Researchers studied the flowering patterns of sea trifoly in the marshlands.
We spotted patches of sea trifoly blooming along the coastline during our hike.
