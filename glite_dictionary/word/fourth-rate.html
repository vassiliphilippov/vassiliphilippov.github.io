fourth-rate

1. ADJ

/ fɔrθ ˌreɪt / (AmE), / fɔːθ ˌreɪt / (BrE)
of very poor quality; inferior or substandard
of very poor quality

Examples

Many critics considered the play a fourth-rate attempt at comedy with little originality.
The restaurant served fourth-rate food that failed to impress any diners.
Despite its budget, the product was dismissed as fourth-rate by most consumers.

Synset Examples

The movie was criticized as a fourth-rate sequel.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: third-rate - of poor or inferior quality
  • Degree Higher: fifth-rate - of extremely poor or the worst quality
  • Antonym: first-rate - of the highest quality

Etymology

From the grading system used for ships in the British Royal Navy, where 'fourth-rate' indicated a ship of lower rank and size.
