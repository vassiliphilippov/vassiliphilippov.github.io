Tanzim

1. PROPN

/ tænˈziːm / (AmE), / tanˈziːm / (BrE)
a Palestinian militant group founded in 1995 by Yasser Arafat as the armed wing of Fatah, engaged in violent actions against Israel and internal rival factions
Palestinian militant group; Fatah armed wing

Examples

Tanzim was formed as a paramilitary branch to coordinate Fatah's armed activities.
Members of Tanzim have been involved in several confrontations in the West Bank.
The leader stressed that Tanzim aims to protect the movement's interests against rival groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fatah - major Palestinian political and military organization

Etymology

From Arabic تنظيم (tanzīm), meaning 'organization' or 'arrangement'.
