Pyxidanthera

1. NOUN

/ pɪkˌsɪdænˈθɪrə / (AmE), / pɪkˌsɪdænˈθɪərə / (BrE)
A small genus of low, mat-forming evergreen shrubs, commonly known as pyxie, native to the eastern United States.
genus of mat-forming evergreen shrubs

Examples

The rare Pyxidanthera carpets the sandy pine barrens with its delicate pink flowers.
Botanists are studying the habitat requirements of Pyxidanthera to aid conservation efforts.
The low-growing Pyxidanthera is sometimes called 'pyxie moss' in local communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyxie - common name for plants of the Pyxidanthera genus

Etymology

From Greek ‘pyxis’ (box) and ‘anthera’ (anther), referring to features of the plant’s flowers.

Related Terms

From Greek *pyxis* (box) and *anthera* (anther), referring to features of the flower.
