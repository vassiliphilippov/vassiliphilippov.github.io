gaffer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæfər / (AmE), / ˈɡæfə / (BrE)
the chief electrician responsible for the design and execution of lighting on a film or television set
chief lighting electrician (film/TV)

Examples

The gaffer adjusted the lights to create the perfect mood for the scene.
After the director called 'cut', the gaffer discussed changes with the cinematographer.
Every film crew relies on a skilled gaffer to bring the lighting design to life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: best boy - chief assistant to the gaffer (lighting department)

Etymology

Believed to arise from British dialect 'gaffer' meaning 'old man' or 'foreman', later adopted in the film industry.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡæfər / (AmE), / ˈɡæfə / (BrE)
an old man, often used to refer to an elderly man, especially in a familiar or slightly respectful way
an elderly man
archaic british

Examples

The gaffer sat by the fire, telling stories of his youth.
Local children would often stop to chat with the old gaffer on his morning walk.
In the novel, the wise gaffer dispenses advice to younger villagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: old man - an elderly male
  • Antonym: granny - an elderly woman (old-fashioned usage)

Etymology

From English dialect 'gaffer', contraction of 'godfather' or 'grandfather', used as a term of respect for an older man.
