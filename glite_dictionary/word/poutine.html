poutine

1. NOUN

/ puˈtin / (AmE), / puːˈtiːn / (BrE)
A Canadian dish consisting of french fries topped with cheese curds and covered with brown gravy.
Canadian dish of fries with cheese curds and gravy

Examples

Every time I visit Montreal, I have to order poutine at my favorite diner.
The restaurant is famous for serving their own version of poutine.
We tried making poutine at home, but it didn’t taste quite the same as in Quebec.

Etymology

From Canadian French 'poutine', of uncertain origin, popularized in Quebec in the 1950s.
