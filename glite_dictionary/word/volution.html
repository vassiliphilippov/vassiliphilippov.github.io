volution

1. NOUN

/ voʊˈluːʃən / (AmE), / vəˈljuːʃən / (BrE)
a turn or spiral form produced by a rolling or revolving motion, especially in shells or coiled objects.
a rolling or spiral turn, especially in shells

Examples

The snail’s shell displays each volution in a beautifully regular pattern.
He studied the volution of smoke rising from the candle, watching it twist elegantly upward.
The ancient fossil showed every volution of its spiral structure clearly imbedded in the rock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiral - a curve or object winding around a central point
  • Synonym: coil - a series of loops or a single loop in a spiral form

Etymology

From Latin volūtiō (a rolling), from volūtus, past participle of volvere (to roll).
