Sunbelt

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌbɛlt / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌbɛlt / (BrE)
A region of the United States comprising the southern and southwestern states, noted for their warm climate and rapid population growth, often considered politically conservative.
southern/southwestern U.S. with warm climate

Examples

Many retirees choose to move to the Sunbelt for its mild winters and sunny weather.
The rapid growth of cities in the Sunbelt has changed the political landscape of the United States.
Manufacturing industries began relocating to the Sunbelt during the late twentieth century.

Etymology

Formed from 'sun' + 'belt', referring to its warm climate and belt-like geographical band.
