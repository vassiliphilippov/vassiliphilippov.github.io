to underwrite

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈraɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈraɪt / (BrE)
to guarantee financial support for an undertaking, such as by assuming risk or promising funding
guarantee financial support

Examples

The bank agreed to underwrite the construction of the new theater.
Several investors came forward to underwrite the startup's expenses.
Without the government to underwrite the project, it would not have gotten off the ground.

Synset Examples

The opera tour was subvented by a bank

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponsor - to support someone or something, especially financially
  • Synonym: fund - to provide money for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Old English 'underwritan' (write at the foot of a document; sign), from 'under' + 'write'.

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌʌndərˈraɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈraɪt / (BrE)
to accept financial responsibility for and guarantee compensation for loss or damage, typically by providing insurance
to guarantee insurance coverage

Examples

The bank agreed to underwrite the new health insurance policy for local businesses.
Many insurance companies refuse to underwrite properties in areas prone to flooding.
Our firm will underwrite your risk, ensuring full compensation in case of an accident.

Synset Examples

The insurance won't cover this

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to insure - to cover with an insurance policy
  • Synonym: guarantee - to provide a formal assurance or promise

Etymology

From Old English 'underwritan' (to write at the foot of a document), later used in financial senses.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00892737-v
Synset POS: v
