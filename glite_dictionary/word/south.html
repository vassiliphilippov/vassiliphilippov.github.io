3. NOUN

/ saʊθ / (AmE), / saʊθ / (BrE)
the direction toward the point of the horizon directly opposite to north, corresponding to 180 degrees on a compass
cardinal compass direction (180°)

Examples

They traveled due south across the desert for several days.
The front of the house faces south to catch the most sunlight.
If you keep heading south, you'll eventually reach the coast.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: north - the cardinal compass point at 0 or 360 degrees, opposite of south

Etymology

From Old English *sūþ*, related to Old High German *sund* (south).

4. NOUN

/ saʊθ / (AmE), / saʊθ / (BrE)
the direction towards the point of the horizon midway between east and west and to the right of a person facing east; one of the four main points of the compass
direction; one of the four cardinal points

Examples

We continued traveling south until we reached the border.
The storm is approaching from the south and expected to hit the coast by morning.
If you walk in the direction of the south, you'll eventually reach the river.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: north - the direction opposite to south, one of the four main compass points
  • Degree Lower: east - the direction at right angles to north and south, on the right when facing north
  • Degree Lower: west - the direction at right angles to north and south, on the left when facing north

Etymology
