Episcia dianthiflora

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɪʃi.ə daɪˌænθɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪsɪə daɪˌænθɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A low-growing, creeping perennial plant native to Central America, with deeply fringed white flowers, sometimes classified in the genus Episcia.
a creeping Central American plant with fringed white flowers

Examples

The Episcia dianthiflora makes an attractive addition to indoor gardens due to its unique flowers.
She purchased an Episcia dianthiflora at the nursery because of its beautiful, fringed white blooms.
Unlike many houseplants, Episcia dianthiflora prefers a warm, humid environment to thrive.

Etymology

Episcia from Greek 'episkein' (to shade), 'dianthiflora' from Latin 'Dianthus' (a plant genus) + 'flora' (flower).
