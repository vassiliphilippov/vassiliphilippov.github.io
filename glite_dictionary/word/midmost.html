midmost

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪdˌmoʊst / (AmE), / ˈmɪdməʊst / (BrE)
being situated in or relating to the exact middle of something
in the exact middle

Examples

The statue stood in the midmost part of the garden, surrounded by flowers.
She found herself seated at the midmost point of the long table.
In the midmost hour of the night, the city was completely silent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central - located in the center
  • Antonym: outermost - farthest from the center or middle

Etymology

From Middle English 'midmost' (mid + most), patterned after Old English 'midmest'.

2. ADV

/ ˈmɪdˌmoʊst / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.məʊst / (BrE)
in the very middle or central part of something; at the center
in the middle or at the center
archaic

Examples

The statue stood midmost in the square, drawing the attention of all visitors.
A single candle was placed midmost on the long dining table.
He found himself midmost the chaos and noise of the festival crowd.

Synset Examples

in the midst of the forest
could he walk out in the midst of his piece?

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'mid' (middle) and 'most' (superlative suffix).
