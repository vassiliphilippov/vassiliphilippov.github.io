panoplied

1. ADJ

/ ˈpænəˌpliːd / (AmE), / ˈpænəpliːd / (BrE)
equipped with a full suit of armor, or more generally, fully equipped or adorned
wearing full armor; fully equipped

Examples

The king rode at the front, panoplied in shining steel from head to toe.
They arrived, panoplied for battle, their helmets and shields glittering in the sun.
In myth, gods were often described as panoplied in divine armor, ready for war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armored - wearing armor or protected by armor

Etymology

From 'panoply,' from Greek panoplia (full armor), plus -ed.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpænəˌplaɪd / (AmE), / ˈpanəpliːd / (BrE)
Wearing complete ceremonial attire or arrayed with ceremonial paraphernalia; fully equipped or adorned, especially in a formal or impressive way.
in full ceremonial attire or fully equipped

Examples

The generals were panoplied in elaborate uniforms for the national celebration.
Statues of ancient warriors stood panoplied in armor at the entrance.
The hall was filled with officials, each panoplied in traditional robes and regalia.

Synset Examples

professors arrayed in robes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrayed - arranged or dressed in particular clothes, especially ceremonial
  • Antonym: unadorned - not adorned or decorated; plain

Etymology

From 'panoply', which comes from Greek 'panoplia' (complete suit of armor).
