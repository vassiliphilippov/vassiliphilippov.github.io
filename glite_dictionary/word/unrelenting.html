unrelenting

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈlɛn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈlɛn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
not stopping or becoming less severe or intense; continuing without weakening or slackening.
not stopping or becoming less intense

Examples

The unrelenting rain caused severe flooding in the city.
His unrelenting determination inspired the whole team to work harder.
The unrelenting beat of the drums filled the air during the festival.

Synset Examples

the relentless beat of the drums

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relentless - not stopping or easing in severity or intensity

Etymology

From un- (not) + relent (to become less severe) + -ing.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈlɛn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈlɛn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
not softening in severity, determination, or intensity; not yielding or stopping, especially in the face of requests or difficulties
not yielding in intensity or severity

Examples

Despite facing numerous setbacks, her unrelenting pursuit of justice inspired many others.
The heat was unrelenting, forcing everyone to seek refuge indoors.
He faced unrelenting criticism but remained steadfast in his beliefs.

Synset Examples

grim determination
grim necessity
Russia's final hour, it seemed, approached with inexorable certainty
relentless persecution
the stern demands of parenthood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relentless - never weakening or stopping; persistent
  • Synonym: inexorable - impossible to stop or prevent
  • Antonym: yielding - giving way under pressure; compliant

Etymology

From un- (not) + relent (to soften) + -ing.

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈlɛn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈlɛn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
(of something unpleasant or severe) continuing without becoming less severe or intense; not yielding in harshness or intensity.
continuing and severe; not lessening

Examples

The unrelenting heat made it impossible to work outdoors for long periods.
She struggled against the unrelenting pressure to succeed at her job.
An unrelenting storm battered the coastline through the night.

Synset Examples

the brutal summer sun
a brutal winter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harsh - unpleasantly rough or severe
  • Antonym: relenting - becoming less severe or intense

Etymology

From un- (not) + relent (to become less severe) + -ing (present participle/adjective suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01514513-s
Synset POS: s
