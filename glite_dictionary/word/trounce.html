to trounce

1. VERB

/ traʊns / (AmE), / traʊns / (BrE)
to beat severely with a whip or rod as a punishment
beat with a whip or rod
archaic

Examples

In olden times, disobedient soldiers were often trounced for even minor offenses.
The children were trounced by the cruel overseer for breaking the rules.
He threatened to trounce anyone who disobeyed his orders with his cane.

Synset Examples

The teacher often flogged the students
The children were severely trounced

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flog - to beat someone harshly with a whip or stick as a punishment

2. VERB

/ traʊns / (AmE), / traʊns / (BrE)
to defeat an opponent decisively or thoroughly, especially in a competition, contest, or conflict
defeat thoroughly in competition

Examples

The team trounced their rivals with a score of 6–0.
She trounced her opponent in the final debate.
Our school trounced last year’s champions in the basketball tournament.

Synset Examples

Agassi beat Becker in the tennis championship
We beat the competition
Harvard defeated Yale in the last football game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeat - win a victory over someone in a contest or conflict
  • Synonym: beat - to win against someone in a game, competition, or argument
  • Antonym: to lose - to fail to win; to be defeated

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from dialectal English ‘trounce’ meaning to give a severe beating, of obscure origin.
