Pipistrellus subflavus

1. NOUN

/ pɪpɪˈstrɛləs sʌbˈfleɪvəs / (AmE), / pɪpɪˈstrɛləs sʌbˈfleɪvəs / (BrE)
A species of small bat found in eastern North America, known as the eastern pipistrelle or tri-colored bat.
a small bat of eastern North America

Examples

During the summer, researchers tracked the movements of Pipistrellus subflavus through the forests of Kentucky.
A sudden flutter in the cave revealed a colony of Pipistrellus subflavus hanging quietly from the ceiling.
The conservation status of Pipistrellus subflavus has become increasingly concerning due to habitat loss and disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tri-colored bat - common name for Pipistrellus subflavus
  • Degree Higher: bat - nocturnal flying mammal (order Chiroptera)

Etymology

From modern Latin; 'Pipistrellus' meaning 'little bat', and 'subflavus' meaning 'somewhat yellow' in Latin.
