romantic

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈmæntɪk / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæntɪk / (BrE)
a person who is guided more by ideals of love, emotion, or imagination than by practical considerations; an idealist, especially in matters of love or beauty.
person with idealistic or amorous beliefs

Examples

He has always been a true romantic, believing in love at first sight.
As a romantic, she prefers novels filled with passion and imagination.
Some say it's foolish to dream, but the romantic in him will never die.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealist - a person who is guided by ideals rather than practicality; synonym in this sense
  • Antonym: realist - a person who accepts things as they are; contrasting term
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10557379-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ roʊˈmæntɪk / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæntɪk / (BrE)
expressive of, or intended to excite, sexual love or romance; amorous or passionate
expressive of love or romance

Examples

He planned a romantic dinner to celebrate their anniversary.
They took a romantic walk along the beach at sunset.
She enjoys romantic novels filled with passion and adventure.

Synset Examples

her amatory affairs
amorous glances
a romantic adventure
a romantic moonlight ride

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amorous - showing, feeling, or relating to sexual desire
  • Antonym: platonic - (of a relationship) intimate and affectionate but not sexual

Etymology

Ultimately from Latin 'romanticus', from 'romanice' (in the Roman language, i.e., vernacular), referring to the tales of romance.

3. ADJ

/ roʊˈmæntɪk / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæntɪk / (BrE)
Belonging to or characteristic of the Romantic Movement in the arts, especially in the late 18th and early 19th centuries, emphasizing inspiration, emotion, and individualism.
relating to the Romantic Movement in the arts

Examples

The museum is hosting an exhibition of romantic paintings from the 19th century.
Her style of writing is deeply romantic, drawing inspiration from nature and strong emotions.
Many romantic composers sought to break away from classical traditions in music.

Synset Examples

romantic poetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romanticism - the artistic and literary movement emphasizing emotion, imagination, and individualism
  • Antonym: classical - relating to ancient Greek or Roman art, literature, or culture, often contrasted with 'romantic' in artistic styles

Etymology

From French *romantique*, from Medieval Latin *romanticus* (in the Roman vernacular), later linked to Romanticism as a cultural movement.

4. ADJ

/ roʊˈmæntɪk / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæntɪk / (BrE)
having ideas or attitudes that are idealistic and unrealistic, not based on practical considerations.
idealistic; not practical

Examples

Her romantic ideas about changing the world were inspiring but not always practical.
He has a rather romantic notion that true love will solve all of life's problems.
It was a romantic but unrealistic plan to travel the world without any money.

Synset Examples

as quixotic as a restoration of medieval knighthood
a romantic disregard for money
a wild-eyed dream of a world state

Related Terms

  • Antonym: practical - concerned with actual use rather than theory or ideas
  • Synonym: idealistic - believing in or aiming for perfection or noble ideals

Etymology

From French romantique, from medieval Latin romanticus (of the Roman style, like in romance literature).

5. NOUN

/ roʊˈmæntɪk / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæntɪk / (BrE)
an artist, writer, or supporter associated with the Romantic Movement or influenced by Romanticism
artist or supporter of Romanticism

Examples

The English poets Byron, Shelley, and Keats are often called leading romantics of their era.
As a true romantic, he valued intense emotion and individual expression in his work.
Many painters of the nineteenth century considered themselves romantics, rebelling against the constraints of classical tradition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: classicist - an artist or adherent of Classicism

Etymology

From 'Romantic' in reference to the Romantic Movement in the arts (late 18th–19th centuries).
