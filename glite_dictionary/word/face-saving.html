face-saving

1. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪsˌseɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪsˌseɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
Intended to preserve dignity or prevent embarrassment.
intended to preserve dignity or reputation

Examples

The company issued a face-saving statement after the public outcry.
They reached a face-saving agreement that allowed both sides to claim victory.
Her resignation was seen as a face-saving move rather than an admission of guilt.

Synset Examples

a face-saving compromise

Related Terms

  • Antonym: humiliating - causing a loss of dignity or self-respect
  • Synonym: to save face - to avoid humiliation or maintain dignity

Etymology

Formed from 'face' (meaning social image or reputation) and 'saving', based on the idiom 'save face'.
