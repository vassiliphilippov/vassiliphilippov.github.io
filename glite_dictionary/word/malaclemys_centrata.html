Malaclemys centrata

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæl.əˈklɛm.iz sɛnˈtreɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌmæl.əˈklɛm.ɪs sɛnˈtrɑː.tə / (BrE)
A species of turtle, commonly known as the diamondback terrapin, found in marshes along the Atlantic and Gulf Coasts of the United States.
species of coastal North American turtle

Examples

The Malaclemys centrata is valued for its distinctive diamond-patterned shell.
Conservationists have made efforts to protect Malaclemys centrata from habitat loss and overharvesting.
Researchers observed the nesting behavior of Malaclemys centrata in coastal marshes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diamondback terrapin - the common name for Malaclemys centrata

Etymology

From Greek 'malakos' (soft) + 'klemys' (turtle); Latinized species name 'centrata'.
