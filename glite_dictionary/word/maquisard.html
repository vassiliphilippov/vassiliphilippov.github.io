maquisard

1. NOUN

/ maˌkiˈzɑrd / (AmE), / maˌkiˈzɑːd / (BrE)
A member of the French underground resistance movement against German occupation during World War II, especially one involved in guerrilla warfare.
French WWII resistance fighter

Examples

The maquisard risked his life to sabotage railway lines used by the occupying forces.
After the war, the bravery of each maquisard was celebrated throughout France.
The film follows a young maquisard as he joins the resistance in the rugged countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resistance fighter - a person who fights against an occupying force, often clandestinely
  • Antonym: collaborator - a person who cooperates traitorously with an occupying enemy force

Etymology

French, from 'maquis' (dense undergrowth) + '-ard' (suffix denoting person involved in an activity).
