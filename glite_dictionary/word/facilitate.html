to facilitate

1. VERB

/ fəˈsɪlɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / fəˈsɪlɪteɪt / (BrE)
to make a process, action, or response easier or more likely to happen; to help bring about
make easier or more likely

Examples

A healthy work environment can facilitate effective communication among team members.
Certain enzymes facilitate chemical reactions in the body by lowering the activation energy required.
In psychology, a reward often facilitates a desired response in experimental studies.

Synset Examples

The stimulus facilitates a delayed impulse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aid - help or support the progress of something
  • Antonym: hinder - make it more difficult for something to happen

Etymology

From Latin facilis (easy) + -ate (verb-forming suffix), via French faciliter (to make easy).

2. VERB

/ fəˈsɪlɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / fəˈsɪlɪteɪt / (BrE)
to make a process or task easier or less difficult; to help bring about or assist something
make (a process) easier

Examples

The new software will facilitate communication between employees in different departments.
Trained staff can facilitate group discussions and ensure everyone's voice is heard.
Having all the necessary information ready can greatly facilitate the application process.

Synset Examples

you could facilitate the process by sharing your knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ease - make something less difficult or painful
  • Antonym: to impede - make something more difficult or hinder progress

Etymology

From Latin facilis (easy) via French faciliter (to make easy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00519475-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ fəˈsɪl.ə.teɪt / (AmE), / fəˈsɪl.ɪ.teɪt / (BrE)
to make a process or action easier or help it happen
make a process easier or help it happen

Examples

Good communication can facilitate understanding between team members.
Technology has facilitated significant changes in the way we work.
A skilled moderator facilitates the discussion to ensure everyone's voice is heard.

Synset Examples

This will help to prevent accidents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: help - to make it easier for something to happen
  • Antonym: hinder - to make something difficult or prevent it

Etymology

From French 'faciliter', from Latin 'facilis' (easy) and 'facere' (to do, make).
