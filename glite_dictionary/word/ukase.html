ukase

1. NOUN

/ juˈkeɪs / (AmE), / juˈkɑːz / (BrE)
an official edict or decree issued by a Russian tsar or government authority, particularly in imperial Russia
edict of the Russian tsar

Examples

The czar issued a ukase banning all foreign books from the country.
Historians debated the impact of the ukase on Russian society in the 18th century.
Under threat of punishment, the villagers obeyed the imperial ukase without question.

Etymology

From Russian 'ukaz' (decree), from Old Church Slavonic 'ukazŭ' (command), related to 'kazat'' (to show, to order).
