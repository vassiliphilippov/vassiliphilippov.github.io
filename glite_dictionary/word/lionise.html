to lionise

1. VERB

/ ˈlaɪ.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.ə.naɪz / (BrE)
to treat (someone) as a celebrity or assign great social importance to them
treat as a celebrity
british

Examples

Many critics lionised the writer after her debut novel was published.
He was lionised by the local press for his charitable work.
The scientist was lionised at international conferences.

Synset Examples

The film director was celebrated all over Hollywood
The tenor was lionized in Vienna

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to celebrate - to praise publicly or honor for an achievement
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to someone or something

Etymology

From English 'lion' + '-ise', based on the practice of treating people as 'lions' (objects of great interest) in 18th–19th century society.
