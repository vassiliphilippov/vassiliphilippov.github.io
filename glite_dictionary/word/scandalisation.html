scandalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌskæn.də.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌskæn.də.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of shocking or offending people, especially by violating accepted standards or morality.
the act of shocking others

Examples

The scandalisation of the organization led to widespread calls for reform.
Tabloid newspapers profit from the scandalisation of public figures and celebrities.
She criticized the media for its constant scandalisation of personal lives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scandalize - to shock or offend someone morally

Etymology

From scandalize + -ation; "scandalize" from Old French scandaliser, from Late Latin scandalizare.

2. NOUN

/ ˌskæn.dələˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌskæn.də.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the state of being shocked or outraged, especially by improper or immoral behavior
state of being shocked by misconduct

Examples

The priest expressed his scandalisation at the corruption revealed in the report.
Public scandalisation over the politician's behavior led to widespread protests.
She could not hide her scandalisation upon hearing the shocking news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - a sudden upsetting or surprising event or experience
  • Synonym: outrage - a strong reaction of anger or shock

Etymology

From verb 'scandalise' + suffix '-ation', following the pattern from Latin 'scandalizare'.
