Henry Bolingbroke

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛnri ˈboʊlɪŋˌbroʊk / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri ˈbɒlɪŋbrʊk / (BrE)
(1367–1413) The first Lancastrian king of England, who reigned as Henry IV after deposing Richard II and suppressing several rebellions.
first Lancastrian king of England (Henry IV)

Examples

Many historians consider Henry Bolingbroke's ascent to the throne a turning point in English history.
Shakespeare depicts Henry Bolingbroke as a complex and ambitious figure in his historical plays.
After seizing power, Henry Bolingbroke became King Henry IV of England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Henry IV - King of England from 1399 to 1413, also known as Henry Bolingbroke
  • Antonym: Richard II - The English king deposed by Henry Bolingbroke
