to retire

1. VERB

/ rɪˈtaɪɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪə / (BrE)
to withdraw bills, shares, or bonds from circulation or the market, making them no longer available or valid
withdraw (bills/shares/bonds) from circulation

Examples

The company decided to retire its outstanding shares through a buyback program.
The central bank may retire old banknotes as new ones are issued.
They retired the bonds ahead of schedule to reduce their debt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to redeem - to exchange a bond, share, or note for payment and remove it from circulation

Etymology

From Latin 'retirare' (to draw back), via Old French 'retirer'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02486037-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rəˈtaɪər / (AmE), / rɪˈtaɪə / (BrE)
In sports, to cause (a player or side) to leave the field or end their turn as a result of a play.
cause a player/team to leave the field in sports

Examples

The pitcher managed to retire the side in the seventh inning without giving up a run.
The bowler retired the batsman with a fast yorker.
After two quick outs, the team was able to retire the batter on a fly ball.
