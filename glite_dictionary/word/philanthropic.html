philanthropic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɪlənˈθrɑːpɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɪlənˈθrɒpɪk / (BrE)
Concerned with promoting the welfare of others, especially through charitable aid or donations.
promoting the welfare of others; charitable

Examples

The organization is known for its philanthropic efforts around the world.
She devoted much of her fortune to philanthropic causes in education and healthcare.
A philanthropic spirit can inspire positive change in any community.

Synset Examples

a philanthropic society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charitable - generous in giving to those in need

Etymology

From Greek philanthrōpikos (loving mankind), from philanthrōpos (humane, benevolent).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03018207-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌfɪlənˈθrɑːpɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɪlænˈθrɒpɪk / (BrE)
showing a desire to help others, especially by giving money or aid to those in need
generous in helping others

Examples

The company is known for its philanthropic donations to educational charities.
She devoted her life to philanthropic work in her community.
Many philanthropic foundations support medical research and social welfare projects.

Synset Examples

a benevolent contributor
eleemosynary relief
philanthropic contributions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charitable - showing kindness and generosity, especially by giving money to those in need

Etymology

From Greek philanthrōpikós, from philánthrōpos (loving humanity).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00360725-s
Synset POS: s
