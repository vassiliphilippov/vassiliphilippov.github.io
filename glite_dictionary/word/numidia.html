Numidia

1. PROPN

/ nuːˈmɪdi.ə / (AmE), / njuːˈmɪdi.ə / (BrE)
an ancient kingdom and later Roman province in North Africa, corresponding roughly to present-day Algeria.
ancient North African kingdom and Roman province

Examples

The territory of Numidia played a significant role during the Punic Wars.
After its annexation by Rome, Numidia became a key province in North Africa.
Archaeologists have uncovered artifacts from ancient Numidia near modern-day Algeria.

Etymology

From Latin Numidia, name of an ancient Berber kingdom.
