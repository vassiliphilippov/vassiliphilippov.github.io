riddle

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈrɪd.əl / (BrE)
a coarse sieve used for separating finer from coarser particles, especially for sifting gravel or ashes
coarse sieve for sifting gravel or ashes

Examples

The gardener used a riddle to sift the compost before spreading it on the beds.
Sand was passed through a large riddle to remove stones and debris.
Old mining tools included a riddle for separating gravel by size.

Etymology

From Old English *hriddel* (sieve), from Latin *crībellum* (sieve).
