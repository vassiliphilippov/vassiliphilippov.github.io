Lanthanotidae

1. NOUN

/ lænˌθænoʊˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / lanˌθænəʊˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of stout-bodied, burrowing lizards found in Southeast Asia, comprising a single extant species, the earless monitor lizard
family of stout-bodied lizards

Examples

The rare earless monitor lizard belongs to the family Lanthanotidae.
Scientists have studied Lanthanotidae to better understand the evolution of monitor lizards.
Few living reptiles are as unique as those in the Lanthanotidae family.

Etymology

From New Latin Lanthanotus (genus name) + -idae (family suffix in zoology).

2. NOUN

/ ˌlænθəˈnoʊtɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌlanθəˈnəʊtɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of stout-bodied lizards found primarily in Borneo, comprising the earless monitor lizard (Lanthanotus borneensis) and its relatives
family of stout-bodied lizards

Examples

The Lanthanotidae are a rare family of lizards native to Borneo.
Scientists recently conducted new research on the behavior of Lanthanotidae in their natural habitat.
Unlike most families of lizards, the Lanthanotidae are distinguished by their lack of external ear openings.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Lanthanotus ('hidden ear') + -idae, a standard zoological family suffix.
