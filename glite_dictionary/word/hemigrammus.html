Hemigrammus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛməˈɡræməs / (AmE), / ˌhiːmɪˈɡræməs / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater tetras native to South America, commonly kept in aquariums.
a genus of freshwater tetras

Examples

Many hobbyists keep Hemigrammus species in their home aquariums due to their bright colors.
The Hemigrammus genus includes some of the most popular tetras, like the rummy-nose and glowlight tetra.
Researchers discovered a new Hemigrammus species in the Amazon basin last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tetra - small tropical freshwater fish, often kept in aquariums

Etymology

From Greek hēmi- (half) + gramma (line), referring to body markings.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmɪˈɡræməs / (AmE), / ˌhiːmɪˈɡræməs / (BrE)
a genus of small freshwater fish in the family Characidae, commonly known as tetras, native to South America and popular in aquariums.
genus of tetras (freshwater fish)

Examples

Many aquarium enthusiasts keep Hemigrammus species for their vibrant colors and peaceful nature.
The Hemigrammus genus includes popular tetras like the glowlight tetra and the head-and-tail-light tetra.
Scientists have discovered new Hemigrammus species in the Amazon basin in recent years.

Etymology

From Greek hemi- (half) + gramma (line), referring to markings on some species.
