gaze

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪz / (AmE), / ɡeɪz / (BrE)
a long and steady look at someone or something
a long fixed look

Examples

She held his gaze for several moments before looking away.
The painting captured the somber gaze of the old man.
He met her gaze with a reassuring smile.

Synset Examples

he fixed his paternal gaze on me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stare - a fixed and often wide-eyed look
  • Degree Lower: glance - a quick look

Etymology

From Old Norse *gá* (to gape, stare).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00880326-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ɡeɪz / (AmE), / ɡeɪz / (BrE)
to look steadily and intently, especially in admiration, surprise, or thought
look steadily and intently

Examples

She gazed out of the window, lost in thought.
The child gazed at the stars, fascinated by the night sky.
He stood silently and gazed into the distance, deep in contemplation.

Synset Examples

The students stared at the teacher with amazement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stare - to look fixedly with wide-open eyes
  • Degree Lower: glance - to take a quick look

Etymology

From Old French *gaiser* (to look at), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02137210-v
Synset POS: v
