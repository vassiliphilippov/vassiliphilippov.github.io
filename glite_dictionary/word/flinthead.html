flinthead

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɪnthɛd / (AmE), / ˈflɪnthɛd / (BrE)
an American stork (Mycteria americana) found in tropical New World swamps, noted for its downward-curved bill resembling that of an ibis
American stork with a curved bill

Examples

The flinthead is often seen wading quietly through southern American wetlands.
Unlike other storks, the flinthead has a distinctive downward-curved bill.
Conservation efforts have helped increase flinthead populations in their native habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood stork - large white American wading bird; another common name for flinthead
