inquisitorial

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˌkwɪzəˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ɪnˌkwɪzɪˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
having a tendency to question closely or harshly, especially in a manner resembling an inquisitor; characterized by prying or searching investigation.
relating to searching or prying questioning

Examples

The reporter’s inquisitorial tone made the politician visibly uncomfortable during the interview.
Her inquisitorial questions made it clear she doubted his explanation.
The committee conducted an inquisitorial review of the company’s finances.

Synset Examples

the press was inquisitorial to the point of antagonism
a practical police force with true inquisitorial talents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inquisitive - showing curiosity; eager for knowledge
  • Synonym: interrogative - having the quality of questioning or investigating

Etymology

From Late Latin inquisitorius (pertaining to an inquisition).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˌkwɪzəˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ɪnˌkwɪzɪˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to a system of judicial procedure in which the judge actively investigates the facts of the case, often conducting secret proceedings and questioning the accused directly, as opposed to relying on the parties to present evidence.
relating to judicial investigation by a judge
legal

Examples

The court followed an inquisitorial process, with the judge actively interrogating witnesses.
Many civil law countries have adopted inquisitorial procedures in their criminal justice system.
Critics argue that the inquisitorial method can undermine the rights of the accused.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accusatorial - relating to a judicial process in which the parties present evidence before a judge, who acts as an impartial arbiter

Etymology

From Late Latin inquisitorius (of an inquirer or investigator).

3. ADJ

/ ɪnˌkwɪzəˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ɪnˌkwɪzɪˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
having the authority or function of conducting official investigations, especially in a judicial or governmental context
having authority to conduct investigations

Examples

The committee's inquisitorial approach made the witnesses nervous during the hearing.
Some legal systems are based on the inquisitorial rather than the adversarial model of justice.
He resented the inquisitorial authority the investigator seemed to wield over ordinary citizens.

Synset Examples

the inquisitorial power of the Senate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigative - engaged in or given to investigation; especially in journalism or official matters

Etymology

From Late Latin inquisitorius (pertaining to an inquirer); from Latin inquisitor (one who inquires).
