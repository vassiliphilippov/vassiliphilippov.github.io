middleweight

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪd.əl.weɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl.weɪt / (BrE)
an amateur boxer or professional fighter who competes in a weight class between welterweight and light heavyweight
boxer in a middle weight class

Examples

The middleweight won the championship with superior technique and stamina.
He is training to compete as a middleweight in the upcoming national boxing tournament.
Several past middleweights have gone on to have successful careers in higher divisions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: welterweight - boxer in the weight class below middleweight
  • Degree Higher: light heavyweight - boxer in the weight class above middleweight

Etymology

Formed from 'middle' + 'weight', denoting an intermediate weight class.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdəlˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɪdlˌweɪt / (BrE)
a professional boxer (or wrestler) who belongs to a weight category between welterweight and light heavyweight, typically weighing between 155 and 160 pounds
a boxer in the 155–160 pound category

Examples

He was a rising star in the middleweight division, known for his quick footwork and powerful punches.
The middleweight championship match attracted thousands of spectators eager to see who would win the title.
As a middleweight, she trained rigorously to maintain her weight within the boxing category limits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: heavyweight - a boxer in a weight class above middleweight
  • Degree Lower: welterweight - a boxer in a weight class below middleweight

Etymology

Formed from 'middle' + 'weight', indicating a weight class between lighter and heavier divisions.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdəlˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɪdəlˌweɪt / (BrE)
A wrestler or boxer who competes in a weight class between lightweight and heavyweight, typically weighing between 172 and 192 pounds.
wrestler or boxer in a middle weight class

Examples

The championship match will feature a top middleweight against last year's winner.
He started his career as a middleweight before moving up to heavyweight.
Several promising middleweights have entered the national wrestling tournament this year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: lightweight - a wrestler or boxer in a lighter weight class
  • Degree Higher: heavyweight - a wrestler or boxer in a heavier weight class

Etymology

Formed from 'middle' + 'weight', indicating a weight class between lightweight and heavyweight.
