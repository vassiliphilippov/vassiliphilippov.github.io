documentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɑkjəˌmɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɒkjʊmɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
written material that provides official information or instructions about how to operate or use something, especially software or machinery
written material describing operation or use

Examples

The software comes with extensive documentation to help users install and configure it.
Without proper documentation, troubleshooting technical issues can be very difficult.
She spent the afternoon writing documentation for the new features in the program.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manual - a book giving instructions or information

Etymology

From Latin documentum (lesson, example) via French documentation.

WordNet ID: oewn-06600607-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɑːkjəmənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɒkjʊmənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the collection or process of providing documents or written evidence to support a statement, claim, or event
written evidence or supporting documents

Examples

The scientist's documentation of her findings was thorough and clear.
You must provide documentation to prove your eligibility for the program.
Careful documentation is essential for verifying the authenticity of research results.

Synset Examples

his documentation of the results was excellent
the strongest support for this view is the work of Jones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evidence - information or documentation that proves something is true

Etymology

From French documentation, from Latin documentum (lesson, proof, instrument).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00155253-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɑːkjəmenˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɒkjʊmɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
Evidence in the form of documents or records used to support or prove that a fact or statement is true.
supporting evidence in document form

Examples

The lawyer requested additional documentation to support the client's claim.
Please submit all necessary documentation when applying for the work permit.
His identity could not be verified due to insufficient documentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evidence - information or documents used to prove something
  • Synonym: proof - evidence or argument establishing a fact or the truth of a statement

Etymology

From Latin 'documentum' (lesson, proof), via French 'documentation'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06663176-n
Synset POS: n
