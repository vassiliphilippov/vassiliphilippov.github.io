ictic

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪktɪk / (AmE), / ˈɪktɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a seizure or convulsion, especially an epileptic one
relating to a seizure or convulsion
medical

Examples

The patient was monitored closely during his ictic episode.
Ictic activity was detected on the EEG, confirming the diagnosis of epilepsy.
Doctors observed several ictic signs prior to the convulsion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ictal - relating to an episode of a seizure

Etymology

From Greek 'iktos' (stroke or blow).
