Amoebida

1. NOUN

/ əˈmiːbɪdə / (AmE), / əˈmiːbɪdə / (BrE)
An order of single-celled protozoan organisms that includes amoebas, characterized by their shapeless form and movement by pseudopodia.
order of protozoan organisms including amoebas

Examples

Scientists have classified several species within the order Amoebida.
The unique movement of Amoebida is achieved by extending pseudopodia.
Research on Amoebida has helped us understand single-celled life forms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amoeba - a single-celled organism that moves using pseudopodia

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Amoeba' with the taxonomic suffix '-ida'.
