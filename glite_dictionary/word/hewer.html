hewer

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːər / (AmE), / ˈhjuːə / (BrE)
A person who cuts or chops something, especially wood or stone, with heavy cutting tools.
one who cuts or chops, esp. wood or stone

Examples

During the tour, the guide explained how the hewer shaped each block of stone by hand.
The hewer worked steadily, turning trees into logs for the sawmill.
Medieval towns often relied on a skilled hewer to provide lumber for construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutter - a person or thing that cuts
  • Synonym: carver - a person who carves wood, stone, or other materials

Etymology

From Middle English hewer, from hew (to cut) + -er.
