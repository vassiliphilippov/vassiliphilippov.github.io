Gleichenia

1. NOUN

/ ɡlaɪˈkiːniə / (AmE), / ɡlaɪˈkiːniə / (BrE)
A genus of ferns in the family Gleicheniaceae, found in regions from South Africa to Malaysia and New Zealand.
genus of tropical and southern ferns

Examples

The tangled branches of Gleichenia make it a distinctive feature of many New Zealand forests.
Researchers collected specimens of Gleichenia for further botanical study.
Several species within the genus Gleichenia thrive in moist, tropical climates.

Etymology

Named after German botanist W. F. von Gleichen (1717–1783) + New Latin -ia.

2. NOUN

/ ɡlaɪˈkiːniə / (AmE), / ɡlaɪˈkiːniə / (BrE)
a genus of ferns in the family Gleicheniaceae, found from South Africa to Malaysia and New Zealand, characterized by leptosporangiate ferns with sessile sporangia
a genus of tropical and subtropical ferns

Examples

Gleichenia species are often found in moist, shaded forests in southeastern Australia.
The fronds of Gleichenia can form dense thickets in their native habitats.
Botanists have identified several new varieties within the Gleichenia genus during recent fieldwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gleicheniaceae - the family of ferns to which Gleichenia belongs

Etymology

Named after German botanist W.F. von Gleichen (1717–1783).
