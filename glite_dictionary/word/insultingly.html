insultingly

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈsʌltɪŋli / (AmE), / ɪnˈsʌltɪŋli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows disrespect or is intended to insult.
in a disrespectful or insulting way

Examples

He spoke insultingly to the waiter, causing embarrassment.
The comments were insultingly dismissive of her hard work.
She rolled her eyes insultingly when his name was mentioned.

Synset Examples

he behaves insultingly toward his parents

Related Terms

  • Antonym: respectfully - in a way that shows respect
  • Synonym: rudely - in a rude manner

Etymology

Formed from 'insulting' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ɪnˈsʌltɪŋli / (AmE), / ɪnˈsʌltɪŋli / (BrE)
In a manner that is insulting or intended to offend or show disrespect.
in an insulting or offensive manner

Examples

She spoke insultingly to the staff, which embarrassed everyone in the room.
He was insultingly dismissive of her suggestions during the meeting.
The referee was accused of behaving insultingly toward the players.

Synset Examples

this internationally known writer was foully condemned by the Muslim fundamentalists

Related Terms

  • Antonym: respectfully - in a manner showing respect or politeness
  • Synonym: rudely - in a rude or impolite manner
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00346108-r
Synset POS: r
