Oxyuridae

1. NOUN

/ ɑk.siˈjʊr.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / ɒk.sɪˈjʊə.rɪ.diː / (BrE)
a family of small parasitic nematode worms that includes pinworms, commonly found in the intestines of vertebrates
family of parasitic nematode worms
medical

Examples

Oxyuridae are significant in medicine because members of this family, such as pinworms, commonly infect humans.
Research into Oxyuridae has helped scientists understand the spread of intestinal parasites.
The life cycle of many Oxyuridae species involves the intestines of vertebrate hosts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinworm - a common type of small parasitic worm in humans, especially children

Etymology

From New Latin Oxyuridae, based on Greek 'oxys' (sharp) + 'oura' (tail), referring to the sharp tail found in some species.
