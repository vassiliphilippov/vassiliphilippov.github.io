pluralistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌplʊr.əˈlɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌplʊə.rəˈlɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or advocating a system or condition in which multiple groups, principles, beliefs, or sources of authority coexist and are acknowledged.
relating to pluralism or diversity

Examples

The city prides itself on having a pluralistic society where different faiths are respected.
His approach to problem-solving is uniquely pluralistic, drawing on ideas from various disciplines.
A pluralistic democracy values the voices of all its minority groups.

Synset Examples

a pluralistic culture

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monolithic - characterized by uniformity or a single paradigm
  • Synonym: pluralism - the doctrine that reality consists of many parts or the acceptance of diverse groups

Etymology

From 'pluralism' (from Latin 'pluralis', meaning more than one) + '-istic'.
