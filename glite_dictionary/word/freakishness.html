freakishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriːkɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈfriːkɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being very strange, abnormal, or unusual.
quality of being abnormal or strange

Examples

Her freakishness made her stand out in the conservative town.
The film explores the freakishness of its main character with empathy.
Some people hide their freakishness for fear of not being accepted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangeness - the quality or state of being strange or odd
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal or usual

Etymology

From 'freakish' + '-ness'; 'freak' possibly from early modern English slang.
