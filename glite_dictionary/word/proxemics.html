proxemics

1. NOUN

/ prɑkˈsiːmɪks / (AmE), / prɒkˈsiːmɪks / (BrE)
the study of how people use and perceive the physical space around them, especially in different cultures and situations
study of personal spatial relationships

Examples

She wrote her dissertation on proxemics in urban office environments.
Understanding proxemics can help avoid misunderstandings in cross-cultural communication.
The study of proxemics reveals how people use personal space differently across cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: personal space - the physical area surrounding someone that they regard as psychologically theirs

Etymology

From Latin 'proximus' (nearest) + '-emics' (as in 'phonemics')
