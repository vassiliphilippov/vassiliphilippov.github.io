Corticium salmonicolor

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˈtɪsiəm sælˌmoʊnɪˈkɔːr / (AmE), / kɔːˈtɪsiəm sælˌməʊnɪˈkɔː / (BrE)
a species of fungus that causes pink disease in citrus, coffee, rubber trees, and other plants
fungus causing pink disease in plants
medical

Examples

Corticium salmonicolor infects many tropical crops, leading to considerable agricultural losses.
Pink disease of rubber trees is frequently attributed to an infection by Corticium salmonicolor.
Researchers study Corticium salmonicolor to find more effective methods of protecting citrus orchards.

Etymology

From Latin 'Corticium' (little bark) and 'salmonicolor' (salmon-coloured), referring to the organism's appearance.
