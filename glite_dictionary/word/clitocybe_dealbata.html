Clitocybe dealbata

1. NOUN

/ klɪˈtɑsɪbi diˈælˌbeɪtə / (AmE), / klɪˈtɒsɪbi diːˈalˌbɑːtə / (BrE)
a small poisonous mushroom with a dry white cap, crowded gills, and a short stalk, found in grasslands and known to be toxic.
a poisonous white mushroom species

Examples

Children should be taught to avoid picking Clitocybe dealbata, as it is dangerously poisonous.
The pale, dry cap of Clitocybe dealbata can be mistaken for edible mushrooms by the untrained eye.
Experts warn that Clitocybe dealbata often grows in lawns and meadows across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Clitocybe rivulosa - a closely related poisonous mushroom species, sometimes jointly referred to as the 'fool's funnel'

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Clitocybe + Latin dealbata ‘whitened’.
