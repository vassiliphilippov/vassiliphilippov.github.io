Willamette

1. PROPN

/ wɪˈlæmɪt / (AmE), / wɪˈlæmɪt / (BrE)
a river in western Oregon, United States, that flows north into the Columbia River near Portland
river in western Oregon, US

Examples

The Willamette River is a major waterway for transportation and recreation in Oregon.
Portland is located at the confluence of the Columbia and Willamette rivers.
Salmon migrate upstream in the Willamette every year to spawn.

Etymology

From a Native American (Chinookan) name, possibly meaning 'long, beautiful river'.
