praxis

1. NOUN

/ ˈpræksɪs / (AmE), / ˈpraksɪs / (BrE)
The process of putting an idea, theory, or policy into practice.
putting theory into practice

Examples

The organization turned its vision of social change into praxis through community programs.
Marxist thinkers often debate the relationship between theory and praxis.
It can be challenging to translate educational theory into effective praxis in the classroom.

Synset Examples

a hard theory to put into practice
differences between theory and praxis of communism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practice - the actual application or use of an idea, belief, or method
  • Antonym: theory - a system of ideas intended to explain something

Etymology

From Greek praxis (doing, action), from prassein (to do).
