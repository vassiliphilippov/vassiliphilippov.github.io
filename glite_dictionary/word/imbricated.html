imbricated

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪm.brɪˌkeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈɪm.brɪ.keɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of leaves, scales, or similar objects) overlapping each other in a regular arrangement, like tiles or shingles on a roof.
overlapping like tiles or shingles

Examples

The pine cones are covered with imbricated scales that protect the seeds inside.
Many succulent leaves are imbricated, giving the plant a layered appearance.
The roof was made with imbricated tiles to ensure rain would easily run off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlapping - covering partly over one another

Etymology

From Latin imbricatus, from imbrex (roof tile).
