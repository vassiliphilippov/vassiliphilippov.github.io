to unpack

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈpæk / (AmE), / ʌnˈpak / (BrE)
to remove something from its packaging, container, or suitcase
remove from packaging or a suitcase

Examples

I can't wait to unpack my suitcase after the long trip.
She carefully unpacked the fragile gifts from the box.
They unpacking their groceries as soon as they got home.

Synset Examples

unpack the presents

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pack - to put items into a container or package

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not, reverse) and 'pack' (to put into a package).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01488139-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈpæk / (AmE), / ʌnˈpak / (BrE)
to remove the contents from a suitcase, box, or other container
remove items from suitcase or box

Examples

After returning from her trip, Emily quickly began to unpack her suitcase.
We need to unpack all these boxes before we can start organizing the kitchen.
He always forgets to unpack his gym bag after practice.

Synset Examples

I need to unpack my suitcase before dinner.
Let's unpack those boxes and find the books.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pack - to place items into a suitcase, box, or other container

Etymology

From un- (expressing reversal) + pack (verb).

Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS: None
