steamed

1. ADJ

/ stiːmd / (AmE), / stiːmd / (BrE)
(of food) cooked by exposing it to steam rather than dry heat or boiling water.
cooked by steam

Examples

I ordered the steamed vegetables as a healthy side dish.
The chef recommended the steamed dumplings with soy sauce.
She prefers steamed fish over fried or baked options.

Synset Examples

steamed clams

Related Terms

  • Antonym: boiled - (of food) cooked in boiling water or liquid
  • Antonym: fried - (of food) cooked in hot fat or oil

2. ADJ

/ stiːmd / (AmE), / stiːmd / (BrE)
feeling angry or annoyed; aroused to impatience or anger
angered; annoyed
informal

Examples

He was so steamed that he could barely speak when he found out the news.
She got really steamed after waiting in line for over an hour.
Don't get steamed over a little misunderstanding; just talk it through.

Synset Examples

made an irritated gesture
feeling nettled from the constant teasing
peeved about being left out
felt really pissed at her snootiness
riled no end by his lies
roiled by the delay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angry - feeling strong displeasure or annoyance
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling anger or annoyance
  • Synonym: irritated - showing slight anger or impatience

Etymology

From the verb 'steam', drawing on the idea of steam building up under pressure (anger).
