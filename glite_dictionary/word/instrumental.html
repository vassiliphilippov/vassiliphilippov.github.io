instrumental

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.strəˈmen.t̬əl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.strəˈmen.təl / (BrE)
serving as a means of achieving something; important in making something happen
serving as a means or aid

Examples

Her advice was instrumental in helping me choose the right career path.
He played an instrumental role in negotiating the peace agreement.
Technological advances were instrumental to the project's success.

Synset Examples

instrumental in solving the crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crucial - extremely important or necessary
  • Synonym: helpful - giving assistance or support

Etymology

From Latin instrumentalis, from instrumentum (tool, means).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01200318-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.strəˈmen.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɪn.strəˈmen.tl̩ / (BrE)
relating to, composed for, or performed by musical instruments rather than by voice
relating to musical instruments

Examples

The band recorded an instrumental version of their latest hit.
She prefers listening to instrumental music when studying.
The concert featured several impressive instrumental solos.

Synset Examples

instrumental compositions
an instrumental ensemble

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vocal - music performed by the human voice

Etymology

From Latin instrumentalis (of or belonging to an instrument), from instrumentum (tool, instrument).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02836709-a
Synset POS: a
