terra cotta

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrə ˈkɑtə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrə ˈkɒtə / (BrE)
a brownish-orange color resembling baked clay
brownish-orange color

Examples

She chose a terra cotta shade for the kitchen walls to create a warm, rustic atmosphere.
The terra cotta tiles complemented the Mediterranean style of the house.
He wore a terra cotta jacket that stood out among the sea of neutral tones.

Synset Examples

The bricks are made from recycled materials, which finished in terra cotta-colored paint evokes the ambience of an ancient village.

Etymology

From Italian 'terra cotta' (baked earth), referring to the color of fired clay.
