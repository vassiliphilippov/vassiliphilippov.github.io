court-martial

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrt ˌmɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːt ˌmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
a military court that tries members of the armed forces accused of serious breaches of military law
military court for service personnel
legal

Examples

The soldier was brought before a court-martial for disobeying direct orders.
His actions were judged by a court-martial that convened last month.
If found guilty by a court-martial, he could face severe punishment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: military tribunal - a court with authority in military legal matters

Etymology

From court (tribunal) + martial (relating to war or military), after French 'cour martiale' and Latin 'martialis'.

2. VERB

/ ˌkɔrtˈmɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˌkɔːtˈmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
to put (a member of the armed forces) on trial before a military court for breaches of military law
to put on military trial

Examples

The general decided to court-martial the officer for insubordination.
She was court-martialed for disobeying a direct order during the conflict.
If found guilty, he could be court-martialled and expelled from the army.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: try - to examine and decide (a case) in a court of law

Etymology

From the noun 'court-martial', which comes from early modern French 'cour martiale' (military court), from Latin 'martialis' (of Mars, god of war).
