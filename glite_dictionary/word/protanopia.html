protanopia

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtəˈnoʊpiə / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəˈnəʊpiə / (BrE)
A type of color blindness characterized by reduced sensitivity to red light, causing difficulty in distinguishing red and purplish blue hues.
red-green color blindness
medical

Examples

He was diagnosed with protanopia after struggling to distinguish red traffic lights.
People with protanopia often confuse red and green hues in everyday situations.
The vision test revealed signs of protanopia in several participants.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: deuteranopia - a type of color blindness affecting green perception
  • Synonym: color blindness - inability to distinguish certain colors

Etymology

From Greek 'prōtos' (first, referring to the first primary color red) + 'anopia' (blindness).
