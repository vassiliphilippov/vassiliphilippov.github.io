probate

1. VERB

/ ˈproʊˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈprəʊˌbeɪt / (BrE)
to establish the legal validity of (a will or other document) through a formal process in court
establish legal validity of a will
legal

Examples

The court must probate the will before the estate can be settled.
After his grandmother passed away, the family hired a lawyer to probate her will.
All documents must be probated to ensure their authenticity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to validate - to check or prove the validity of something
  • Antonym: to invalidate - to make (a document or procedure) legally void

Etymology

From Latin 'probare' (to prove).

2. NOUN

/ ˈproʊˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈprəʊˌbeɪt / (BrE)
the official legal process of proving the validity of a will and administering the estate of a deceased person
legal process proving a will's validity
legal

Examples

The lawyer explained that the probate process could take several months to complete.
After her father's death, she had to deal with probate before receiving her inheritance.
Many people try to avoid probate by setting up a trust in advance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testamentary - relating to a will or testament

Etymology

From Latin probatum (something proved), from probare (to prove).

3. NOUN

/ ˈproʊˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈprəʊbeɪt / (BrE)
the official proving of a will as authentic and valid, or the legal process by which a will is accepted as genuine by a court, giving executors authority to manage the deceased's estate
official certification of a will's validity
legal

Examples

The lawyer explained that probate would be required to settle her grandfather's estate.
It took several months to get probate after his death due to legal complications.
Without probate, the executors could not distribute the assets to the heirs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: will - a legal document expressing a person's wishes for the disposal of their property after death
  • Antonym: intestacy - the state of dying without having made a will

Etymology

From Latin 'probatum' (something proved), past participle of 'probare' (to prove).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06556423-n
Synset POS: n
