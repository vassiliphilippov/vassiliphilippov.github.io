pellagra

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlæɡrə / (AmE), / pɛˈlæɡrə / (BrE)
a disease caused by a deficiency of niacin (vitamin B3) or tryptophan, leading to symptoms such as skin inflammation, digestive problems, and mental disturbances
disease due to niacin deficiency
medical

Examples

After a long period of malnutrition, the patient developed pellagra and required immediate treatment.
Outbreaks of pellagra were common in areas where corn was the dietary staple in the early 1900s.
Proper diet and vitamin supplements can prevent pellagra in at-risk populations.

Etymology

From Italian 'pellagra', from 'pelle' (skin) + 'agra' (sour), referring to the rough skin symptom.
