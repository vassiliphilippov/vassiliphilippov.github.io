latest

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪtəst / (AmE), / ˈleɪtɪst / (BrE)
the most recent or up-to-date; latest in time or order
most recent; up to date

Examples

She always has the latest information on technology trends.
The latest edition of the magazine features an exclusive interview.
Have you heard the latest news about the merger?

Synset Examples

the news is up-to-the-minute
the very latest scientific discoveries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newest - most new or recent
  • Antonym: oldest - having existed the longest; earliest in time

Etymology

Superlative form of 'late,' from Old English 'læt' (late).

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈleɪ.tɪst / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.tɪst / (BrE)
being the most recent or fashionable; in the current style or trend
most recent; in fashion

Examples

This dress is the latest in Parisian fashion.
She always buys the latest gadgets as soon as they come out.
His hairstyle is the latest trend among teenagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fashionable - popular or in style at a particular time
  • Antonym: outdated - no longer in use or fashionable

Etymology

From late + -est, indicating the superlative form.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00974685-s
Synset POS: s
