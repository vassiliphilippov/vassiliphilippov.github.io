Coryanthes

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːriˈænθiːz / (AmE), / ˌkɒriˈænθiːz / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical American orchids that grow on trees or rocks as epiphytes or lithophytes.
a genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The Coryanthes orchid is known for its unique, bucket-shaped flowers that attract certain bees.
Collectors often seek rare species of Coryanthes for their stunning appearance and unusual pollination methods.
Researchers study Coryanthes to understand complex relationships between orchids and their pollinators.

Etymology

From Greek 'korys' (helmet) and 'anthos' (flower), referring to the helmet-shaped flower structure.
