court

1. NOUN

/ kɔrt / (AmE), / kɔːt / (BrE)
The household and entourage of a sovereign or prince, including their advisers, attendants, and companions.
sovereign's household and attendants

Examples

The king's court traveled with him throughout the land, maintaining royal authority.
Life at the French court was known for its extravagance and elaborate ceremonies.
She became a favorite at the imperial court, influencing important political decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: royal household - the official residence and staff of a monarch

Etymology

From Old French *cort* (enclosed yard, court), from Latin *cohors* (yard, retinue).
