compendium

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɛndiəm / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛndiəm / (BrE)
A concise, comprehensive summary or collection of information, especially from a larger work or field.
concise, comprehensive summary or collection

Examples

The guide serves as a compendium of local history and traditions for visitors.
She bought a compendium of classical myths to help with her studies.
This book is a useful compendium of current medical research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summary - a brief statement of the main points
  • Synonym: encyclopedia - a book or set of books giving information on many subjects

Etymology

From Latin 'compendium' (a saving, shortcut), from 'compendere' (to weigh together).

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈpɛndiəm / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛndɪəm / (BrE)
A collection of detailed information or a variety of works, often in book form, gathered together for reference or study.
collection of information or works

Examples

The museum published a compendium of its most notable artifacts to educate visitors and researchers.
Her reference shelf included a compendium of world mythology with stories from many cultures.
This compendium brings together essays, articles, and reports on climate change from leading scientists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthology - a published collection of literary pieces
  • Synonym: digest - a compilation or summary of information
  • Synonym: encyclopedia - a book or set of books giving information on all branches of knowledge

Etymology

From Latin 'compendium' (a saving, shortcut), from 'compendere' (to weigh together).
