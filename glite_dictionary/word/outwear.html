to outwear

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈwɛr / (AmE), / aʊtˈwɛə / (BrE)
to last longer than something else; to endure for a greater period than others.
last longer than something else

Examples

This durable coat can easily outwear cheaper alternatives.
Only a few athletes managed to outwear the grueling conditions of the race.
The tradition seemed likely to outwear its original purpose and survive for generations.

Synset Examples

This material outwears all others

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outlast - survive or last longer than
  • Antonym: to succumb - fail to withstand or be defeated by

Etymology

From prefix 'out-' (meaning beyond or more than) + 'wear' (to endure or last).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02711163-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ aʊtˈwɛr / (AmE), / aʊtˈwɛə / (BrE)
to exhaust or tire (someone or oneself) through overuse, great strain, or prolonged effort
to exhaust through overuse or strain

Examples

The ten-mile run completely outwore the inexperienced runners.
She tried not to let the stress of her job outwear her over time.
They feared the relentless pace would outwear even the strongest members of the team.

Synset Examples

We wore ourselves out on this hike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaust - to make someone extremely tired
  • Antonym: to refresh - to give someone new energy or strength

Etymology

From out- (completely) + wear (to use, to fatigue), modeled on the sense of wearing something out.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00074774-v
Synset POS: v
