to crump

1. VERB

/ krʌmp / (AmE), / krʌmp / (BrE)
to bombard or explode with heavy shells or to make a loud, dull explosive noise
bombard with heavy shells

Examples

During the battle, the artillery crumped the enemy’s position all night long.
We heard shells crumping in the distance as we huddled in the trench.
The old war diary described how the town was crumped mercilessly for hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - to fire explosive shells at

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps imitative of the sound of a heavy explosion or related to 'crump', meaning 'to crunch'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01138819-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ krʌmp / (AmE), / krʌmp / (BrE)
(of something explosive, especially a shell) to explode with a heavy, dull sound.
explode with a heavy, dull sound

Examples

The artillery shells crumped in the distance, shaking the ground beneath our feet.
A mine crumped nearby, sending debris high into the air.
Bombs crumping overhead forced everyone to seek shelter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boom - make a loud, deep, resonant sound
  • Synonym: bang - make a sudden loud, sharp noise

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of an explosion; recorded from the 20th century, especially in military contexts.
