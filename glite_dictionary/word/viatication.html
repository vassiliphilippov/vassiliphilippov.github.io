viatication

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˌætɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / vaɪˌætɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of purchasing life insurance policies from terminally ill policyholders for cash, typically allowing the seller to gain immediate funds while the purchaser receives the policy benefits upon the seller's death.
buying insurance from terminally ill policyholders

Examples

The company specializes in viatication, providing cash to those with limited life expectancy.
Regulations around viatication are designed to protect vulnerable policyholders from exploitation.
He considered viatication to help cover his medical expenses during his illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viatical settlement - a legal agreement to buy life insurance from a terminally ill person

Etymology

From Latin 'viaticum' (provision for a journey), influenced by insurance terminology.
