fleabag

1. NOUN

/ ˈfliˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈfliːˌbæɡ / (BrE)
a run-down or cheap hotel, especially one that is dirty or poorly maintained
run-down or cheap hotel
informal

Examples

We ended up spending the night in a fleabag because all the good hotels were booked.
He couldn't believe how much the fleabag charged for such a tiny, dingy room.
The movie opens with the hero waking up in a grimy fleabag on the edge of town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dive - a shabby or disreputable place, such as a bar or club

Etymology

From 'flea' + 'bag', originally used for bedding infested with fleas, later applied figuratively to shabby accommodations.
