unmeasured

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmɛʒɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɛʒəd / (BrE)
Not composed of regular or metrical syllables; lacking a measured rhythm, especially in reference to prose or verse.
not having a metrical or regular rhythm

Examples

The author preferred unmeasured prose over strict verse forms.
The play opens with a long passage of unmeasured dialogue.
Some contemporary poets write in unmeasured free verse, discarding traditional metrics.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmɛʒɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɛʒəd / (BrE)
not limited or determined in extent, size, or amount; limitless or boundless.
without limits; boundless

Examples

The poet describes the unmeasured depths of the human soul.
We gazed at the unmeasured beauty of the night sky.
Her generosity seemed unmeasured and unwavering.

Synset Examples

limitless vastness of our solar system
The long unmeasured pulse of time moves everything. There is nothing hidden that it cannot bring to light, nothing once known that may not become unknown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limitless - without end, limit, or boundary
  • Synonym: boundless - having no boundaries; unlimited
  • Antonym: measured - deliberate and restrained; determined by measurement

Etymology

From un- (not) + measured (past participle of measure).
