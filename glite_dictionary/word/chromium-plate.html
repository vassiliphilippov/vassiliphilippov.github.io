to chromium-plate

1. VERB

/ ˈkroʊmiəm pleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊmiəm pleɪt / (BrE)
to cover (an object) with a thin layer of chromium for protection or a shiny finish
cover with a layer of chromium

Examples

Many classic cars were chromium-plated to achieve a brilliant, mirror-like finish.
The company decided to chromium-plate all its fixtures for increased durability and corrosion resistance.
You should always clean chromium-plated surfaces with a soft cloth to avoid scratches.

Synset Examples

chrome bathroom fixtures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chrome-plate - to coat with a thin layer of chromium

Etymology

From chromium, the chemical element, and plate (to coat a metal), referring to the electroplating process developed in the early 20th century.
