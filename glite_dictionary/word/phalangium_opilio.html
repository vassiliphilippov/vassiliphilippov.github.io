Phalangium opilio

1. NOUN

/ fəˌlændʒiəm oʊˈpɪlioʊ / (AmE), / fəˌlændʒɪəm əʊˈpɪlɪəʊ / (BrE)
A species of harvestman, a spiderlike arachnid with a small rounded body and very long thin legs, commonly known as the daddy longlegs.
species of harvestman arachnid

Examples

The Phalangium opilio is often mistaken for a spider due to its long legs.
Scientists studied the behavior of Phalangium opilio in different environments.
You may find a Phalangium opilio in your garden during late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvestman - arachnids commonly known as daddy longlegs
  • Antonym: spider - arachnids with two body segments and eight legs

Etymology

From Latin 'phalangium' (a kind of spider) and 'opilio' (shepherd), referencing its appearance and long legs.
