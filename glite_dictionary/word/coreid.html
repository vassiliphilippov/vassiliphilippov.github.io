coreid

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːr.i.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɔːr.i.ɪd / (BrE)
a true bug belonging to the family Coreidae, typically characterized by leaf-like expansions on the legs
a true bug in the Coreidae family

Examples

The scientist identified the insect as a coreid after examining its distinctive legs.
Several coreids were collected from the garden for further research.
Despite its small size, the coreid can be an important pest in some crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaf-footed bug - a common type of coreid with leaf-like expansions on their legs

Etymology

From New Latin Coreidae, the family name, from the type genus Coreus.
