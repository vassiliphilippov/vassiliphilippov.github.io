A genus of large, semi-aquatic rodents in the family Hydrochoeridae, best known for including the capybara.
genus of large semi-aquatic rodents
medical

Examples

The Hydrochoerus genus includes the well-known capybara, native to South America.
Researchers studied the habitats of Hydrochoerus in the wetlands of Brazil.
Members of the genus Hydrochoerus are recognized for their semi-aquatic lifestyle and social behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capybara - the largest living rodent, native to South America

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro-' meaning 'water' and 'choiros' meaning 'pig', referring to the animal's habitat and appearance.
