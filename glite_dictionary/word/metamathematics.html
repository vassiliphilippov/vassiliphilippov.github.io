metamathematics

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˌmæθəˈmætɪks / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˌmæθəˈmatɪks / (BrE)
the branch of mathematics that studies the foundations and methods of mathematics itself, using formal logical analysis
study of mathematics' foundations and logic

Examples

The results of metamathematics have profound implications for the limits of mathematical proof.
He devoted his career to the study of metamathematics and the consistency of mathematical systems.
Courses in metamathematics are often required for advanced degrees in logic and philosophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logic - the study of valid reasoning
  • Synonym: mathematical logic - the application of logic to mathematical reasoning

Etymology

From meta- (beyond, about) + mathematics.
