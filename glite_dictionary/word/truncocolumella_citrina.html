Truncocolumella citrina

1. NOUN

/ truŋkoʊkəˈluːmə sɪˈtrinə / (AmE), / trʌŋkəʊkɒˈluːmɪlə sɪˈtraɪnə / (BrE)
a species of fungus with a round yellow to orange fruiting body, found on or just below the ground, distinguished by a sterile column extending into its spore-bearing tissue.
a species of yellow-orange fungus with a sterile column

Examples

The distinctive fruiting bodies of Truncocolumella citrina can be seen emerging from the forest floor in late summer.
Mycologists were excited to discover Truncocolumella citrina during their field survey in the park.
The bright yellow color of Truncocolumella citrina makes it relatively easy to identify among leaf litter.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Truncocolumella' from Latin 'truncus' (trunk) + 'columella' (little column), 'citrina' from Latin 'citrinus' (lemon-yellow).
