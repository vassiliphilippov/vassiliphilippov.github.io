Trotsky

1. PROPN

/ ˈtrɑtski / (AmE), / ˈtrɒtski / (BrE)
(1879–1940) Russian revolutionary, Marxist theorist, and leader who played a key role in the Bolshevik Revolution; later expelled from the Communist Party and assassinated in exile.
Russian revolutionary leader (1879–1940)

Examples

Many historians consider Trotsky a brilliant military organizer during the Russian Civil War.
After clashing with Stalin, Trotsky was forced into exile and eventually settled in Mexico.
The legacy of Trotsky continues to inspire left-wing thinkers around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lenin - Russian revolutionary leader and founder of Soviet state
  • Antonym: Stalin - Soviet leader and political rival of Trotsky

Etymology

From Russian Троцкий (Trotskiy), the revolutionary alias of Lev Davidovich Bronstein.
