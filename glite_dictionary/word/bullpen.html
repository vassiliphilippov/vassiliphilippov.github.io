bullpen

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlˌpɛn / (AmE), / ˈbʊlˌpɛn / (BrE)
an area on a baseball field where relief pitchers warm up before entering a game
area for baseball pitchers to warm up

Examples

The relief pitcher waited in the bullpen until the seventh inning.
Fans cheered when the manager signaled to the bullpen for a new pitcher.
There are two bullpens located behind the outfield fence.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlˌpɛn / (AmE), / ˈbʊlˌpɛn / (BrE)
a large cell in which groups of prisoners, such as people awaiting trial or sentence, refugees, or illegal immigrants, are confined together temporarily
large temporary holding cell for groups of prisoners

Examples

The police held the suspects in the bullpen until they could be processed individually.
Dozens of migrants were kept overnight in a crowded bullpen at the border facility.
Before his court appearance, he spent hours waiting in the bullpen with other detainees.

Etymology

Originally American English, from bull + pen, referring to a fenced enclosure for bulls, later applied metaphorically to crowded holding areas for people.
