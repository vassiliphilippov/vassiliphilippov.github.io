pennyworth

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛniˌwɜrθ / (AmE), / ˈpɛniwəːθ / (BrE)
An amount of something that can be bought for a penny; a quantity or value equivalent to one penny.
amount bought for a penny
archaic british

Examples

He bought a pennyworth of peppermints at the old candy store.
Back in Victorian times, a pennyworth of bread could feed a whole family.
The boy asked the grocer for a pennyworth of apples.

Etymology

From 'penny' + 'worth', meaning the value of a penny.
