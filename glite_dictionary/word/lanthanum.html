lanthanum

1. NOUN

/ ˈlænθənəm / (AmE), / ˈlanθənəm / (BrE)
a soft, silvery-white metallic element (symbol La), belonging to the rare earth group; used in alloys and various electronics.
a metallic chemical element (La)

Examples

The research team used lanthanum to improve the efficiency of the battery.
Because lanthanum tarnishes quickly in air, it must be stored with care.
Scientists isolated lanthanum from rare earth minerals in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cerium - a similar rare earth metallic element
  • Synonym: rare earth - any of a group of chemically similar metallic elements, often found together in minerals

Etymology

From New Latin 'lanthanum', from Greek 'lanthanein' (to lie hidden), because it was hidden in minerals of other elements.
