to crystallize

1. VERB

/ ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make ideas, feelings, or situations clear and definite; to remove ambiguity or confusion
make clear or definite

Examples

The company's vision began to crystallize after the new CEO explained his strategy.
Her doubts crystallized when she read the final report and understood the situation clearly.
This meeting helped to crystallize our goals for the year ahead.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clarify - to make something easier to understand
  • Antonym: to confuse - to make something more difficult to understand

Etymology

From Greek *krustallos* (ice, crystal), via French *cristalliser* and English *crystal*.
