palo santo

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːloʊ ˈsɑːn.toʊ / (AmE), / ˌpɑːləʊ ˈsan.təʊ / (BrE)
A South American tree of dry regions, especially in Argentina and Paraguay, with fragrant resinous wood used for incense and essential oils.
fragrant South American tree used for incense

Examples

Many people burn palo santo sticks to create a calming atmosphere in their homes.
The essential oil from palo santo is prized in aromatherapy for its soothing scent.
Artisans in South America sometimes carve palo santo wood into decorative objects.

Etymology

From Spanish 'palo santo', meaning 'holy wood'.
