to ratify

1. VERB

/ ˈræ.t̬ɪ.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈræt.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to officially approve or confirm a decision, agreement, or law, especially by a vote or signature
formally approve (e.g., agreement or law)

Examples

The government must ratify the treaty before it can take effect.
Seven countries have yet to ratify the climate agreement.
The union members voted overwhelmingly to ratify the new contract.

Synset Examples

All parties ratified the peace treaty
Have you signed your contract yet?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to approve - to agree to or accept officially
  • Antonym: reject - to refuse to accept or approve

Etymology

From Latin ratificare, from ratus (fixed, settled) + facere (to make).
