synapsis

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnæpsɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈnapsɪs / (BrE)
the pairing of homologous chromosomes side by side during the early stages of meiosis
pairing of homologous chromosomes in meiosis
medical

Examples

During meiosis, synapsis enables homologous chromosomes to exchange genetic material.
The scientist explained how defects in synapsis can lead to chromosomal abnormalities.
Proper synapsis is crucial for the accurate segregation of chromosomes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meiosis - a type of cell division that reduces the chromosome number by half

Etymology

From Greek synapsis (joining together), from synaptein: syn- (together) + haptein (to join).
