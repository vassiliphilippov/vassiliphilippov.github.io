hothead

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑtˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈhɒtˌhɛd / (BrE)
A person who easily becomes angry or loses their temper, especially someone who acts impulsively or belligerently.
impulsive, quick-tempered person

Examples

Everyone knows Mike is a hothead, so try not to upset him during meetings.
The coach warned the young player not to act like a hothead on the field.
Despite being a hothead in his youth, he learned to control his temper over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firebrand - a person who is passionate about a cause, often inciting change or conflict
  • Antonym: peacemaker - a person who tries to create peace and avoid or end conflicts

Etymology

From 'hot' + 'head', referring to someone with a hot temper.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑtˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈhɒtˌhɛd / (BrE)
A person who acts quickly and impulsively without thinking, often becoming reckless or irresponsible.
reckless or impulsive person

Examples

Jake can be a real hothead when things don’t go his way.
The coach warned that being a hothead would only hurt the team's chances.
She tried to calm the hothead before the argument got out of control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firebrand - a person who is passionate about a cause, typically inciting change or taking action
  • Antonym: coolhead - someone who remains calm and rational in difficult situations

Etymology

Formed from 'hot' + 'head', referring to being hot-tempered.
