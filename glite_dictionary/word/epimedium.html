Epimedium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈmidiəm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous perennial plants, native to regions from the Mediterranean to India and eastern Asia, often grown for their attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
genus of perennial plants

Examples

Several varieties of Epimedium are prized in shade gardens for their decorative leaves.
The gardener recommended planting Epimedium beneath the trees for spring color.
Some species of Epimedium are used in traditional herbal remedies.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, adopted as the genus name in botanical Latin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈmiːdiəm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
a genus of herbaceous perennial plants found from the Mediterranean region to eastern Asia, commonly known as barrenwort or bishop's hat
genus of perennial plants

Examples

Several species in the Epimedium genus are grown as ornamental plants in shaded gardens.
The leaves of Epimedium are known for their distinctive heart shape.
Scientists have been studying Epimedium for its potential medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrenwort - common name for plants in the Epimedium genus

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'epi' (upon) and 'medion', an ancient name for a type of plant.
