youthful

1. ADJ

/ ˈjuθfəl / (AmE), / ˈjuːθfʊl / (BrE)
Having qualities associated with young people, such as energy, vigor, or freshness.
vigorous or fresh like youth

Examples

She has a youthful appearance despite her age.
The team's youthful enthusiasm brought a new energy to the project.
His youthful spirit inspires everyone around him.

Synset Examples

he is young for his age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: young - having lived or existed for only a short time; not old
  • Antonym: aged - having lived for a relatively long time; old
  • Synonym: fresh - new and full of energy or vigor

Etymology

From Middle English 'youtheful', from 'youth' + '-ful'.
