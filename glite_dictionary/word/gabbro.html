gabbro

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæbroʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡabrəʊ / (BrE)
A coarse-grained, dark, intrusive igneous rock composed mainly of feldspar and pyroxene.
coarse-grained igneous rock

Examples

The mountain range is largely formed of gabbro and other igneous rocks.
Samples of gabbro were collected to study the region's geological history.
Oceanic crust is primarily composed of gabbro and basalt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: granite - a common type of coarse-grained intrusive igneous rock

Etymology

From Italian gabbro, possibly from Latin glaber (smooth, bald).
