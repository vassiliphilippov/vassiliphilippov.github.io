Ig

1. NOUN

/ aɪ dʒi / (AmE), / aɪ dʒi / (BrE)
A class of proteins produced by lymph tissue in vertebrates, functioning as antibodies in the immune response; short for immunoglobulin.
antibody protein (immunology abbreviation)
medical

Examples

The laboratory measured the patient's Ig levels to assess immune function.
Elevated Ig can be a sign of an ongoing infection or immune response.
Researchers study different types of Ig to understand autoimmune diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunoglobulin - antibody protein produced by the immune system

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'immunoglobulin'.
