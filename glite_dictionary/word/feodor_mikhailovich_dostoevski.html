Feodor Mikhailovich Dostoevski

1. PROPN

/ ˈfeɪˌoʊdər mɪˈkaɪəˌləvɪtʃ ˌdɒstoʊˈjɛfski / (AmE), / ˈfjɔːdə mɪˈkaɪələvɪtʃ ˌdɒstɪˈɛfski / (BrE)
(1821–1881) A Russian novelist noted for exploring human suffering, psychology, and morality, known for works such as 'Crime and Punishment' and 'The Brothers Karamazov.'
Russian novelist (1821–1881)

Examples

Feodor Mikhailovich Dostoevski is often considered one of the greatest novelists in world literature.
Many students analyze the themes of guilt and redemption in Feodor Mikhailovich Dostoevski's works.
The influence of Feodor Mikhailovich Dostoevski can be seen in the writings of many modern authors.

Etymology

From Russian Фёдор Михайлович Достоевский (Fyódor Mikháylovich Dostoyévskiy).
