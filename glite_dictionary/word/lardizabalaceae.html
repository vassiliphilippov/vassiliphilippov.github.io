Lardizabalaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɑrdɪˌzɑbəˈleɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌlɑːdɪˌzɑːbəˈleɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of woody vines and shrubs, some of which have edible fruit, native mainly to East Asia and South America.
family of woody vines and shrubs

Examples

Species belonging to the Lardizabalaceae family are often used as ornamental plants in gardens.
The climbing plants of the Lardizabalaceae family are especially valued for their edible fruits.
Botanists recently discovered a new shrub in the Lardizabalaceae family in the Andes.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after Spanish botanist Miguel de Lardizábal.

2. NOUN

/ lɑrˌdaɪzəˌbæləˈsiː.i / (AmE), / lɑːˌdɪz.əˌbæləˈsiː.i / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants comprising mainly thick-stemmed lianas and some shrubs, some of which bear edible fruit
a family of woody plants with some edible fruit

Examples

The Lardizabalaceae are known for producing unusual edible fruits in East Asia.
Akebia is a genus within the Lardizabalaceae family that is cultivated for its ornamental value.
Researchers discovered a new species of shrub belonging to the Lardizabalaceae.

Etymology

From the New Latin family name, after Spanish botanist Miguel de Lardizábal y Uribe.
