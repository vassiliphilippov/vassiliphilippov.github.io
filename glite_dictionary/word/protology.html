protology

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / prəʊˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of origins and the first things, especially with reference to theological or philosophical beginnings
study of origins and first things

Examples

In biblical studies, protology explores the creation accounts in Genesis.
He wrote his thesis on the protology of early Christian thought.
Discussions of protology often intersect with debates about science and religion.

Synset Examples

To Christians, protology refers to God's fundamental purpose for humanity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmology - the study of the origin and development of the universe

Etymology

From Greek prōtos (first) + -logy (study of).
