Saqqarah

1. PROPN

/ sɑːˈkɑːrə / (AmE), / səˈkɑːrə / (BrE)
an ancient town in northern Egypt, renowned as the site of the oldest Egyptian pyramids, including the Step Pyramid of Djoser.
ancient town in Egypt; site of oldest pyramids

Examples

Archaeologists discovered new tombs near Saqqarah that shed light on Egypt's Old Kingdom.
The Step Pyramid at Saqqarah is considered one of the world's oldest stone monuments.
Many tourists visit Saqqarah to see its impressive collection of ancient tombs and pyramids.

Etymology

From Arabic سقارة‎ (Saqqārah), the modern name of the site.
