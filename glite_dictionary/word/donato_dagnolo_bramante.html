Donato d'Agnolo Bramante

1. PROPN

/ doʊˈnɑːtoʊ dɑːnˈjoʊloʊ brəˈmɑːnteɪ / (AmE), / dəˈnɑːtəʊ dɑːnˈjɒləʊ brəˈmænˌteɪ / (BrE)
(1444–1514) An Italian architect of the High Renaissance, best known for his designs in Rome, including parts of St. Peter's Basilica.
Italian High Renaissance architect (1444–1514)

Examples

Donato d'Agnolo Bramante designed the original plan for St. Peter's Basilica in Rome.
Many art historians credit Donato d'Agnolo Bramante with revolutionizing Renaissance architecture.
The influence of Donato d'Agnolo Bramante can be seen in many later Italian architects' works.

Etymology

Italian personal name; 'Donato' meaning 'given', 'Bramante' a family name.
