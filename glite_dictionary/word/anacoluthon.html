anacoluthon

1. NOUN

/ ˌænə.kəˈluːθ.ɑn / (AmE), / ˌan.ə.kəˈluː.θɒn / (BrE)
A sudden break in the grammatical structure of a sentence, resulting in a shift from one syntactic construction to another.
abrupt syntactic break in a sentence

Examples

His speech was full of anacoluthon, leaping from one idea to another without warning.
The writer used anacoluthon to create a sense of confusion in the story.
If you think that—well, honestly, anacoluthon can make prose quite intriguing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: non sequitur - a statement or conclusion not following logically from the previous one

Etymology

From Greek anakolouthon (not following), from an- (not) + akolouthos (following).
