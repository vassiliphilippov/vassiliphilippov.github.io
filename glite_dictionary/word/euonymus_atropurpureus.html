Euonymus atropurpureus

1. NOUN

/ juˈɑnəməs ˌætroʊpərˈpjʊəriəs / (AmE), / juːˈɒnɪməs ˌatrəʊpəˈpjʊəriəs / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, known for its purple capsules that enclose bright scarlet seeds; also called the eastern wahoo or burning bush.
deciduous shrub with purple capsules and scarlet seeds

Examples

The Euonymus atropurpureus in our garden displays beautiful scarlet seeds every autumn.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Euonymus atropurpureus for potential therapeutic uses.
Birds are attracted to the vibrant fruit of Euonymus atropurpureus during the fall season.

Etymology

From Greek *eu-* (good) + *onyma* (name) and Latin *atropurpureus* (dark purple).
