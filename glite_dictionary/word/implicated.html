implicated

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪmplɪˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈɪmplɪˌkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
shown to be involved in a crime or other wrongdoing, especially in a way that suggests guilt or responsibility
shown to be involved in wrongdoing

Examples

Several senior executives were implicated in the financial scandal after a thorough investigation.
His fingerprints on the weapon implicated him in the robbery, making him a key suspect.
Though she denied any part, the evidence strongly implicated her in the conspiracy.

Synset Examples

all those concerned in the bribery case have been identified
named three officials implicated in the plot
an innocent person implicated by circumstances in a crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: involved - taking part in an activity or connected with it, especially something wrong
  • Antonym: innocent - not involved in or responsible for a crime or wrongdoing

Etymology

Past participle of 'implicate', from Latin implicatus, past participle of implicare (to entwine, involve).
