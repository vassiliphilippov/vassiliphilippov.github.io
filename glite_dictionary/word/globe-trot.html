to globe-trot

1. VERB

/ ˈɡloʊb ˌtrɑt / (AmE), / ˈɡləʊb ˌtrɒt / (BrE)
to travel all over the world for pleasure and sightseeing
travel around the world

Examples

He spent his twenties globe-trotting and experiencing different cultures.
After retiring, they decided to globe-trot together for a year.
Bloggers who globe-trot often share stories and photos from exotic destinations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: travel - to go from one place to another, typically over a distance

Etymology

From 'globe' (the Earth) and 'trot' (to travel quickly), modeled after 'globe-trotter'.
