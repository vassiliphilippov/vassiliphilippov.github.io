Boletus luridus

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈliːtəs lʊˈraɪdəs / (AmE), / bəˈliːtəs ljʊˈrɪdəs / (BrE)
a poisonous species of bolete mushroom characterized by a dingy yellow cap, orange-red pores underneath, and a cylindrical, netted stalk
a poisonous bolete mushroom species

Examples

Many mushroom-foragers avoid the Boletus luridus because of its poisonous properties.
The Boletus luridus can be identified by its yellow cap and bright orange-red pores.
If ingested, the Boletus luridus may cause serious gastrointestinal distress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolete - a type of fungus with pores on the underside of the cap

Etymology

From Latin: Boletus (a type of mushroom) + luridus (pale yellow, sallow, ghastly).
