hexine

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛksaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɛksaɪn / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile hydrocarbon (C6H10) belonging to the acetylene (alkyne) series.
a hydrocarbon of the acetylene series

Examples

Pure hexine is commonly used in organic synthesis in laboratory settings.
Several isomers of hexine can be separated by their boiling points.
The chemist discussed the properties of hexine during the lecture on alkynes.

Synset Examples

Hexine is obtained artificially as a colorless, volatile liquid.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hexane - a saturated hydrocarbon with six carbon atoms
  • Degree Lower: acetylene - the simplest alkyne hydrocarbon (C2H2)

Etymology

From Greek 'hex' meaning six, referring to six carbon atoms, and '-ine' denoting a chemical compound.
