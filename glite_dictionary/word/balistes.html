Balistes

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlaɪstiːz / (AmE), / bəˈlɪstiːz / (BrE)
a genus of tropical marine fish in the family Balistidae, commonly known as triggerfish.
genus of marine triggerfish

Examples

The genus Balistes includes several species of colorful triggerfish found in coral reefs.
Marine biologists often study Balistes to understand the evolution of Balistidae.
A well-known member of the Balistes genus is Balistes capriscus, also called the grey triggerfish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triggerfish - any fish of the family Balistidae, known for their spiny dorsal fin

Etymology

From Latin 'balistes', meaning 'crossbow', referring to the trigger-like dorsal spine of these fish.

2. NOUN

/ bəˈlaɪstiːz / (AmE), / bəˈlaɪstiːz / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Balistidae, comprising various species of triggerfish found in tropical and subtropical waters.
type genus of triggerfish (Balistidae)

Examples

The species Balistes vetula is one of the most widely recognized triggerfish.
Scientists studied several members of the genus Balistes to understand their reef behavior.
Balistes includes colorful fish found in both the Atlantic and Indo-Pacific regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triggerfish - common name for species in Balistidae

Etymology

From Latin 'balistes', a kind of fish, originally meaning 'crossbow', due to the similarity of the fish's dorsal spine structure to a trigger mechanism.
