decoration

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛkəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛkəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an object or ornament used to make something look more attractive or beautiful
an object that beautifies

Examples

The Christmas tree was covered in shiny decorations of all shapes and sizes.
She added a few paper decorations to brighten up the classroom.
Balloons and colorful streamers served as cheerful decorations for the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornament - an object that decorates or adorns
  • Synonym: adornment - something that adds attractiveness or beauty

Etymology

From Latin decoratio (adornment), from decorare (to adorn).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛkəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛkəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an award, such as a medal or ribbon, given to honor achievement or to commemorate an event
an award given to honor achievement

Examples

The officer received a decoration for his bravery during the mission.
She wore the decoration she earned at the international competition.
Many veterans display their decorations in a frame on the wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medal - a small metal disc awarded as a prize or honor

Etymology

From Latin decoratio (adornment), from decorare (to adorn).

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɛkəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛkəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something look more attractive by adding items or features to it
the act of making something more attractive

Examples

The living room looked much better after the decoration for the party.
They take great pride in the decoration of their store windows during the holidays.
The restaurant was closed for decoration and renovation last week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embellishment - the act of making something more attractive by adding decorative details or features

Etymology

From Latin decoratio, from decorare (to adorn).
