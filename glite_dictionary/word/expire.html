to expire

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈspaɪɚ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspaɪə / (BrE)
(of a document, permit, or agreement) to cease to be valid, typically after a fixed period of time.
cease to be valid

Examples

My credit card will expire next month, so I need to request a new one.
If your visa expires while you're abroad, you may not be able to return.
Her driver's license expired last week, and she can't drive until she renews it.

Synset Examples

My passports expired last month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lapse - to cease to be valid, typically due to expiration
  • Synonym: to terminate - to bring to an end or finish
  • Antonym: to renew - to extend the period of validity of something

Etymology

From Latin 'exspirare' meaning 'breathe out' (ex- 'out' + spirare 'breathe').

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈspaɪɚ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspaɪə / (BrE)
(formal or literary) to die; to pass from physical life.
(formal) to die

Examples

Sadly, her grandfather expired just before his 90th birthday.
The patient expired peacefully in his sleep last night.
He expired in the hospital after a long battle with the disease.

Synset Examples

She died from cancer
The children perished in the fire
The patient went peacefully
The old guy kicked the bucket at the age of 102

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - to cease living; to stop being alive
  • Synonym: to perish - to die, especially suddenly or violently
  • Antonym: live - to remain alive; to be alive

Etymology

From Latin 'exspirare' meaning 'to breathe out'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00359085-v
Synset POS: v
