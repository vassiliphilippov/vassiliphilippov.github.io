Edwardian

1. ADJ

/ ɛdˈwɔrdiən / (AmE), / ɛdˈwɔːdɪən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the period in Britain during the reign of Edward VII (1901–1910), especially in terms of style, culture, or society.
of the era of Edward VII (1901–1910) in Britain

Examples

The house was decorated in a distinctly Edwardian style with ornate moldings and high ceilings.
She inherited a beautiful set of Edwardian furniture from her grandmother.
The film is set in Edwardian England and captures the elegance of the time.

Synset Examples

Edwardian furniture

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Victorian - relating to or characteristic of the period of Queen Victoria's reign
  • Degree Higher: Georgian - relating to or characteristic of the period of King George I-IV's reigns

Etymology

From Edward VII, King of the United Kingdom (reigned 1901–1910) + English suffix -ian.

2. NOUN

/ ɛdˈwɔrdiən / (AmE), / ɛdˈwɔːdiən / (BrE)
a person who lived during the reign of Edward VII of the United Kingdom, or who has the characteristics or attitudes associated with that period (1901–1910)
person from the era of Edward VII

Examples

The grand house was inherited by an old Edwardian who remembered its heyday.
As an Edwardian, he valued formality and etiquette above all.
Many Edwardians enjoyed afternoon tea and elaborate garden parties.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Victorian - a person from the era of Queen Victoria
  • Degree Higher: Georgian - a person from the era of King George V

Etymology

From Edward + -ian, referring to King Edward VII of the United Kingdom (reigned 1901–1910).

Synset POS: n
