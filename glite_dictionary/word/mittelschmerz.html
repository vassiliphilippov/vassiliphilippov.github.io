mittelschmerz

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪtl̩ˌʃmɜrts / (AmE), / ˈmɪtl̩ˌʃmɜːts / (BrE)
A pain in the lower abdomen, typically experienced in the area of the ovary at the time of ovulation, midway through the menstrual cycle.
ovulation-related abdominal pain
medical

Examples

Some women experience mittelschmerz as a sharp or cramping pain during ovulation.
Doctors sometimes refer to mittelschmerz when a patient reports mid-cycle abdominal discomfort.
Although mittelschmerz is usually brief, it can sometimes be mistaken for other causes of pelvic pain.

Etymology

From German 'Mittelschmerz', meaning 'middle pain' ('Mitte' = middle, 'Schmerz' = pain).
