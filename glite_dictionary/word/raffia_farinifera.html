Raffia farinifera

1. NOUN

/ ˈræfiə ˌfærɪˈnɪfərə / (AmE), / ˈræfiə ˌfarɪˈnɪfərə / (BrE)
a large African and Madagascan palm tree with very long, feather-like fronds, whose leafstalks yield strong, commercially important fibers
African palm yielding raffia fiber

Examples

The Raffia farinifera is cultivated for its strong fiber, used to make ropes and mats.
Baskets woven from the leaves of Raffia farinifera are popular in West African markets.
Scientists are studying Raffia farinifera to improve sustainable fiber production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raffia - the fiber obtained from the leaves of Raffia palms

Etymology

From the genus Raffia, derived from Malagasy 'rafia', meaning 'raffia palm'; 'farinifera' from Latin, meaning 'bearing starch or flour'.
