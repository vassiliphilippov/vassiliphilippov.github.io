MEK

1. NOUN

/ ɛm iː ˈkiː / (AmE), / ɛm iː ˈkeɪ / (BrE)
a militant Iranian political organization, founded in the 1960s, which originally opposed the Shah of Iran and later the Islamic Republic, combining Marxist and Islamic ideologies
Iranian militant political organization

Examples

The government has accused the MEK of involvement in several attacks over the years.
Members of the MEK live in exile and continue their opposition to the Iranian regime.
International opinions about the MEK are divided, with some countries labeling it a terrorist organization.

Etymology

Acronym from Persian 'Mojahedin-e Khalq' meaning 'People's Mujahedin of Iran'.
