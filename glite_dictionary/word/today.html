today

1. NOUN

/ təˈdeɪ / (AmE), / təˈdeɪ / (BrE)
The present time or age; the period of time that is currently happening.
the present time or age

Examples

Today is a challenging time for businesses adapting to rapid technological change.
Many of today's youth are passionate about social issues.
Historians will look back and analyze the choices made by leaders of today.

Synset Examples

the world of today
today we have computers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: yesterday - the day before today; the recent past
  • Antonym: tomorrow - the day after today; the near future

Etymology

From Old English 'tō dæge' (on/to the day).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15287732-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ təˈdeɪ / (AmE), / təˈdeɪ / (BrE)

Related Terms

  • Antonym: yesterday - the day before today
