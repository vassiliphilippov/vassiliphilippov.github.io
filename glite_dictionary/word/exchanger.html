exchanger

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsɪndʒər / (AmE), / ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒə / (BrE)
a person or company whose business is to exchange the money of one country for that of another
person or company exchanging currencies

Examples

Before traveling abroad, we visited an exchanger to get euros for our dollars.
The exchanger offered a competitive rate for converting yen to pounds.
Tourists often rely on a local exchanger for quick currency conversion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: money changer - a person whose business is exchanging the money of one country for another

Etymology

From Middle English 'exchaunger', from Old French 'eschangeor', from 'eschanger' (to exchange).
