unlit

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlɪt / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɪt / (BrE)
not set on fire or burning; not provided with light.
not burning or without light

Examples

She walked down the unlit corridor, feeling her way along the walls.
He tossed an unlit cigarette into the ashtray and sighed deeply.
The village was especially eerie at night with most streets unlit and deserted.

Synset Examples

the table was bare, the candles unlighted
held an unlit cigarette

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lit - set on fire or burning
  • Synonym: dark - lacking light; not illuminated

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' + 'lit', past participle of 'light'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlɪt / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɪt / (BrE)
not provided with light; not illuminated.
not illuminated; without light

Examples

We walked carefully along the unlit path after sunset.
The unlit corridor felt eerie late at night.
Emergency lighting is essential in unlit stairwells.

Synset Examples

came up the lightless stairs
the unilluminated side of Mars
through dark unlighted (or unlit) streets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dark - with little or no light; not bright
  • Antonym: illuminated - supplied with light; lit up

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'lit', past participle of 'light'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00276851-s
Synset POS: s
