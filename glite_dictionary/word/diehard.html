diehard

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪ.hɑrd / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.hɑːd / (BrE)
a person who strongly opposes change and prefers to stick to traditional attitudes or beliefs
person strongly resisting change

Examples

Even after the new policy was introduced, the diehard refused to alter his methods.
There are still a few diehards in the party who oppose modernization.
She was called a diehard for her persistent refusal to embrace new technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traditionalist - a person who supports tradition and resists change
  • Antonym: progressive - a person favoring reform and new ideas

Etymology

Originally used to describe soldiers who fought to the death, later extended to mean one who stubbornly resists change.
