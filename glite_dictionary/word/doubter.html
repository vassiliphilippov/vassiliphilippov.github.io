doubter

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊtər / (AmE), / ˈdaʊtə / (BrE)
a person who is uncertain or skeptical about something; someone who expresses doubt
person who expresses doubt

Examples

Even as the evidence mounted, one persistent doubter refused to accept the theory.
There will always be a doubter in the crowd despite overwhelming enthusiasm.
She started as a doubter, but eventually she came to support the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeptic - person inclined to doubt or question accepted opinions
  • Antonym: believer - person who accepts something as true or real

Etymology

From the verb 'doubt' + agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊtɚ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊtə / (BrE)
a person who questions or lacks faith in commonly accepted beliefs, ideas, or statements
someone who questions accepted beliefs

Examples

She has always been a doubter and questions everything she is told.
Even the most persistent doubter was convinced by the overwhelming evidence.
The scientific community values the role of the doubter in challenging established theories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeptic - a person inclined to doubt or question accepted opinions
  • Antonym: believer - a person who accepts something as true or has faith in something

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'douter', from Latin 'dubitare' (to doubt).
