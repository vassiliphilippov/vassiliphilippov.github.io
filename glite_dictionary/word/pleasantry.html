pleasantry

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛzəntri / (AmE), / ˈplɛzəntri / (BrE)
a friendly or humorous remark made to create a pleasant atmosphere in conversation
an agreeable or amusing remark

Examples

We exchanged pleasantries before beginning the meeting.
She greeted him with a warm smile and a few pleasantries.
Their conversation was filled with light pleasantries and easy laughter.

Synset Examples

they exchange pleasantries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joke - a thing that someone says to cause amusement or laughter
  • Synonym: quip - a witty remark

Etymology

From French plaisanterie (joke, witticism), from plaisanter (to please, joke).
