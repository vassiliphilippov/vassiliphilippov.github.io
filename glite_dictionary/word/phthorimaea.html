Phthorimaea

1. NOUN

/ θoʊˌθɔːrɪˈmiːə / (AmE), / θɔːˌθɒrɪˈmiːə / (BrE)
A genus of small moths in the family Gelechiidae, known as potato moths, whose larvae feed on potato plants and other crops.
genus of moths affecting potatoes

Examples

The larvae of Phthorimaea can cause significant damage to stored potatoes.
Farmers often implement pest control measures to limit the spread of Phthorimaea in their fields.
Research on Phthorimaea has led to improved agricultural practices for protecting crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potato tuber moth - a moth species from the genus Phthorimaea, also known as potato moth

Etymology

From Greek 'phthora' (destruction) + '-maia', a common suffix in entomology.

2. NOUN

/ ˌθɔːrəˈmiːə / (AmE), / ˌθɔːrɪˈmiːə / (BrE)
A genus of moths in the family Gelechiidae, which includes pests such as the potato tuber moth.
genus of moths including the potato moth

Examples

The Phthorimaea species are known to damage potato crops worldwide.
Researchers studied how Phthorimaea larvae infest stored potatoes.
Farmers are searching for effective methods to control Phthorimaea moths.

Etymology

From New Latin Phthorimaea, a taxonomic name created for this genus.
