arteria circumflexa scapulae

1. NOUN

/ ɑɹˈtɪɹiə sɜrkəmˈflɛksə skəˈpjuːli / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə sɜːkəmˈflɛksə skəˈpjuːliː / (BrE)
an artery that supplies blood to the muscles of the shoulder and scapular area
artery of the shoulder and scapular muscles
medical

Examples

The arteria circumflexa scapulae provides an important blood supply to the muscles surrounding the scapula.
During the surgical procedure, careful attention was paid to preserve the arteria circumflexa scapulae.
Injury to the arteria circumflexa scapulae can compromise blood flow to the shoulder region.

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery), 'circumflexa' (bending around), 'scapulae' (shoulder blade).
