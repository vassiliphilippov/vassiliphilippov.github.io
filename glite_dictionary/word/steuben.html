Steuben

1. PROPN

/ stoʊˈbɛn / (AmE), / stjuːˈbɛn / (BrE)
Baron Friedrich Wilhelm von Steuben (1730–1794), a Prussian-born American military officer who helped train and discipline troops during the American Revolutionary War.
Revolutionary War military leader and trainer

Examples

Baron von Steuben played a critical role in shaping the discipline of the Continental Army.
The influence of Steuben at Valley Forge is still recognized in military tradition today.
A statue of Steuben stands in many American cities honoring his contributions to the war.

Etymology

From Friedrich Wilhelm von Steuben, a Prussian-born officer who served in the Continental Army.
