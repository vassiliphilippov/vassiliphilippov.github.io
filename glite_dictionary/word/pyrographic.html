pyrographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpaɪrəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌpʌɪrəˈɡrafɪk / (BrE)
relating to or produced by pyrography, the art or process of decorating materials with burn marks made by a heated tool
relating to the art of burning designs

Examples

This box features a beautiful pyrographic pattern of flowers across its lid.
She has mastered several pyrographic techniques for shading and detailing.
The gallery displays pyrographic artworks created on both wood and leather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrography - the art or technique of decorating materials by burning designs onto them

Etymology

From pyrography + -ic, formed from Greek 'pyr' (fire) and 'graphō' (to write).
