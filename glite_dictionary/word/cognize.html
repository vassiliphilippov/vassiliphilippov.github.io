to cognize

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːɡˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡ.naɪz / (BrE)
to be aware of or know something; to perceive or recognize mentally
recognize or become aware of

Examples

It took several minutes for her to cognize the full meaning of his words.
Some philosophers debate whether animals truly cognize their environment or merely react to stimuli.
He slowly cognized the truth of the situation after reading the evidence.

Synset Examples

I know that the President lied to the people
I want to know who is winning the game!
I know it's time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to know - to have knowledge or information about something
  • Synonym: to recognize - to identify something as already known or experienced

Etymology

From Latin cognoscere (to get to know, recognize).
