Onoclea struthiopteris

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.noʊˈkliː.ə ˌstrʌθ.i.ɒpˈtɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒn.əˈkliː.ə ˌstrʌθ.i.ɒpˈtɛr.ɪs / (BrE)
a tall fern of northern temperate regions with elegant, arching fronds and sporophylls that resemble ostrich plumes
a species of tall northern fern

Examples

The Onoclea struthiopteris thrives in moist woodlands across northern temperate regions.
Gardeners often plant Onoclea struthiopteris for its attractive, feathery foliage.
The unique fronds of Onoclea struthiopteris make it popular among fern enthusiasts.

Etymology

From New Latin genus Onoclea and species name struthiopteris, meaning 'ostrich fern' due to the shape of the fronds.
