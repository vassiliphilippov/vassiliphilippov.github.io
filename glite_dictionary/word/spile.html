spile

1. NOUN

/ spaɪl / (AmE), / spaɪl / (BrE)
A small plug or wooden peg used to close a hole in a barrel, cask, or flask.
plug or peg for closing a barrel hole

Examples

The cooper inserted a spile to seal the cask after filling it with wine.
A wooden spile was removed to allow the liquid to be poured out.
Traditional barrels often have a spile to regulate the flow of contents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bung - a stopper for closing the hole in a barrel

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch 'spijl' (post, pin).

2. NOUN

/ spaɪl / (AmE), / spaɪl / (BrE)
a column or stake of wood, steel, or concrete driven into the ground to support a structure or retain earth
support column driven into the ground

Examples

The engineer recommended inserting a spile to stabilize the bridge foundation.
Workers drove a steel spile deep into the riverbed to support the pier.
Without a strong spile, the retaining wall would not have held against the shifting soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pile - a heavy beam or post driven vertically into the bed of a river, soft ground, etc., to support a structure

Etymology

Probably related to Dutch 'spijl' (spindle, bar) or Middle Dutch 'spile' (pin).
