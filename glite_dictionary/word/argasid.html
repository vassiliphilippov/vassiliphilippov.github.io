argasid

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑr.ɡə.sɪd / (AmE), / ˈɑː.ɡə.sɪd / (BrE)
A tick belonging to the family Argasidae, characterized by lacking a dorsal shield and having mouthparts located on the underside of the head.
soft-bodied tick of the Argasidae family
medical

Examples

The veterinarian identified the parasite as an argasid after examining the bird's skin.
Unlike hard ticks, an argasid does not possess a rigid dorsal shield.
Researchers discovered several argasids in the old barn, raising concerns about disease transmission.

Etymology

From New Latin Argasidae, the family name, from the genus Argas.
