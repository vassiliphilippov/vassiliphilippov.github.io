pretorium

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈtɔːriəm / (AmE), / prɪˈtɔːrɪəm / (BrE)
The tent used as headquarters by an ancient Roman general during military campaigns.
Roman general's headquarters tent

Examples

Archaeologists discovered the remains of a pretorium at the ancient Roman camp.
The commanding officer established his pretorium on the hill overlooking the battlefield.
Maps from the period often indicate the location of the pretorium within each military encampment.

Etymology

From Latin 'praetorium', originally meaning 'general's tent', from 'praetor' (magistrate, general).
