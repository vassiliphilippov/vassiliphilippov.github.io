arrière-pensée

1. NOUN

/ aˌʁjɛr pɑ̃ˈseɪ / (AmE), / aˌʁjɛː pɒ̃ˈseɪ / (BrE)
A hidden or unstated motive, reservation, or doubt that prevents one from accepting something completely.
hidden motive or reservation

Examples

Even as she agreed to the proposal, an arrière-pensée lingered in her mind, making her hesitant.
His compliments seemed sincere, but I couldn't shake the feeling that he spoke with an arrière-pensée.
Diplomats often act with an arrière-pensée, concealing their true intentions behind polite words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reservation - a feeling of doubt or uncertainty preventing full acceptance

Etymology

From French 'arrière-pensée' (hidden thought).
