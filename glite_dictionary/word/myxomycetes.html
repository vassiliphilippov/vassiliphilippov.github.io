Myxomycetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪk.soʊˈmaɪ.sɪˌtiːz / (AmE), / ˌmɪk.səʊˈmaɪ.sɪˌtiːz / (BrE)
a class of true slime molds comprising a group of fungus-like protists, essentially equivalent to the division Myxomycota
class of true slime molds
medical

Examples

Scientists studied the ecological role of Myxomycetes in forest decomposition processes.
The fascinating life cycle of Myxomycetes attracts many biologists and microbiologists alike.
Collecting samples of Myxomycetes requires patience, as they appear under specific environmental conditions.

Etymology

From Greek myxa (slime, mucus) + mykēs (fungus).
