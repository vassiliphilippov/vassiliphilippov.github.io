hi-fi

1. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
(of equipment or sound) having high fidelity; characterized by accurate reproduction of sound with minimal distortion.
high-fidelity sound reproduction

Examples

The new speakers deliver hi-fi audio quality for even the most discerning listeners.
She prefers to listen to vinyl records on a hi-fi system for their authentic sound.
He invested in hi-fi headphones to experience music as the artist intended.

Synset Examples

a high-fidelity recording
a hi-fi system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: high-fidelity - characterized by accurate sound reproduction with minimal distortion

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'high fidelity'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00022717-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
equipment that reproduces sound with high fidelity, typically used to listen to music at high quality
high-fidelity sound equipment

Examples

He invested in a new hi-fi system to improve his listening experience.
The living room was equipped with a vintage hi-fi that delivered crystal-clear sound.
She played her favorite album on the hi-fi while relaxing on the sofa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stereo - sound system with two or more speakers for realistic sound reproduction

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'high fidelity'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03522923-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ ˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ ˌfaɪ / (BrE)
equipment or technology for the reproduction of sound with high fidelity, producing very little distortion.
high-fidelity sound reproduction equipment

Examples

He spent a lot of money on a new hi-fi system to enjoy music at home.
The old hi-fi in the living room still produces excellent sound quality.
She collects vintage hi-fi equipment from the 1970s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stereo - an audio system with two or more channels to create a sense of dimension or space

Etymology

Short for 'high fidelity', dating from the 1950s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01022430-n
Synset POS: n
