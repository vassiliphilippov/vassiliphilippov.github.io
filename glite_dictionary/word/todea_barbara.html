Todea barbara

1. NOUN

/ toʊˈdiːə ˈbɑrbərə / (AmE), / təʊˈdiːə ˈbɑːbərə / (BrE)
a large fern native to rainforests in Australia, New Zealand, and South Africa, known for its leathery fronds and trunklike stem
fern native to Australia, New Zealand, and South Africa

Examples

The Todea barbara thrives in the damp, shady conditions of the rainforest understory.
Botanists discovered a patch of Todea barbara along the riverbank in New Zealand.
Many gardeners prize Todea barbara for its striking fronds and unusual appearance.

Etymology

Genus name 'Todea' after German botanist Heinrich Julius Tode; species name from Latin 'barbara'.
