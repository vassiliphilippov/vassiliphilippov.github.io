phycology

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈkɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / faɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of algae, a branch of botany concerned with these organisms
study of algae branch of botany
medical

Examples

She specialized in phycology, focusing her research on marine algae.
Advances in phycology have contributed to new developments in biofuel production.
During his studies, he became fascinated by phycology and the role of algae in aquatic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: algae - simple, typically aquatic plants studied in phycology

Etymology

From Greek phykos (seaweed, algae) + -logy (study).
