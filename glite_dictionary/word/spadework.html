spadework

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪdˌwɝːk / (AmE), / ˈspeɪdˌwɜːk / (BrE)
preparatory or routine work, often dull or tedious, which lays the groundwork for a larger project or undertaking
preparatory routine work

Examples

Much of the success of the project was due to the careful spadework done by the team beforehand.
Before we can launch the new campaign, we still have a lot of spadework to do behind the scenes.
His diligent spadework provided the foundation for the committee’s final report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preparation - the action of making ready or being made ready for use or consideration
  • Antonym: follow-through - the act of completing or finishing an undertaking

Etymology

From 'spade' + 'work', referencing manual digging as metaphor for preparatory effort.
