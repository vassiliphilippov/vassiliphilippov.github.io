shamanism

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑː.məˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈʃæm.əˌnɪz.əm / (BrE)
A religious practice, especially among the peoples of northern Asia, based on the belief that shamans mediate between the visible world and the spirit world.
a religion involving shamans as mediators

Examples

Shamanism has long played a central role in the spiritual life of Siberian peoples.
Some anthropologists study shamanism to better understand ancient spiritual practices.
The festival included a demonstration of traditional shamanism rituals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animism - belief that objects, places, and creatures possess a spiritual essence

Etymology

From Russian 'shaman', from Tungusic 'šaman' (priest).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑː.mə.nɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈʃæm.ə.nɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A religion or set of practices involving belief in an unseen world of gods, demons, and ancestral spirits responsive only to shamans, found particularly among traditional societies in Asia and the Americas.
religion involving shamans and spirit worlds

Examples

Many indigenous peoples around the world have practiced shamanism for centuries.
He wrote a thesis on the role of shamanism in Siberian cultures.
Some modern spiritual movements have incorporated elements of shamanism into their rituals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animism - belief that objects, places, and creatures possess a spiritual essence

Etymology

From Russian 'shamanizm', from 'shaman', from Evenki (Tungusic language spoken in Siberia).
