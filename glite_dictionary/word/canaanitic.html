Canaanitic

1. NOUN

/ ˌkeɪnəˈnɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌkeɪnəˈnɪtɪk / (BrE)
a branch of the Semitic language family that includes Hebrew, Phoenician, and other ancient languages spoken in the region of Canaan.
branch of Semitic languages including Hebrew and Phoenician

Examples

The ancient Hebrew Bible contains texts written in a Canaanitic language.
Phoenician, a well-known Canaanitic tongue, was widely spoken along the Mediterranean coast.
Scholars study Canaanitic inscriptions to better understand the culture of ancient Canaan.

Etymology

From Canaanite, referring to Canaan, an ancient region in the Near East.
