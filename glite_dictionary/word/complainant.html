complainant

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpleɪnənt / (AmE), / kəmˈpleɪnənt / (BrE)
a person who makes a formal complaint in a court of law or initiates a legal action against another party
person bringing a legal action
legal

Examples

The complainant alleged that the company had breached their contract.
During the trial, the complainant was asked to give evidence.
The court decided in favor of the complainant after reviewing all the facts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaintiff - person who brings a case against another in court
  • Antonym: defendant - person against whom a court action is brought

Etymology

From Old French *complaignant* (present participle of *complaindre*, to complain).
