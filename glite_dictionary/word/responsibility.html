responsibility

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌspɑnsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / rɪˌspɒnsəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
A moral or social obligation that compels a person to act in a certain way or to fulfill a duty.
a binding social or moral obligation

Examples

As a citizen, you have a responsibility to contribute to the community.
She felt a strong responsibility to care for her younger siblings.
With great power comes great responsibility.

Synset Examples

we must instill a sense of duty in our children
every right implies a responsibility; every opportunity, an obligation; every possession, a duty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duty - a moral or legal obligation; a responsibility
  • Synonym: obligation - something a person is bound to do by law or conscience
  • Antonym: irresponsibility - lack of a sense of duty or obligation

Etymology

From Latin 'responsum' (an answer) + '-ibility', influenced by 'responsible'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˌspɑnsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / rɪˌspɒnsəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being reliable and accountable for one's actions or duties
the state of being accountable or reliable

Examples

She takes her responsibility as a team leader very seriously.
With great power comes great responsibility.
Parents have the responsibility to care for their children.

Synset Examples

he holds a position of great responsibility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accountability - the fact or condition of being accountable or responsible
  • Antonym: irresponsibility - lack of responsibility

Etymology

From Latin responsabilis (that can be answered for), from respondere (to respond).
