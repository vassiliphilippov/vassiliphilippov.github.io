laureate

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔriˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɒːrɪət / (BrE)
Worthy of the greatest honor or distinction; deserving to be honored for outstanding achievement.
deserving the highest honor

Examples

She became the nation’s poet laureate for her outstanding contribution to literature.
The university honored its laureate scientist at the annual awards ceremony.
He is regarded as the laureate expert in pediatric medicine.

Synset Examples

The nation's pediatrician laureate is preparing to lay down his black bag

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinguished - very successful, authoritative, and commanding great respect
  • Synonym: celebrated - greatly admired; renowned
  • Antonym: unknown - not known or familiar; not distinguished

Etymology

From Latin 'laureatus', meaning 'crowned with laurel'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔriˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɒːrɪət / (BrE)
a person who has been honored for outstanding achievements, especially in the arts, literature, or sciences
person honored for outstanding achievements

Examples

The Nobel laureate delivered an inspiring lecture on climate change.
She became a laureate when she received the international poetry prize.
Many laureates have gone on to influence future generations in their fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winner - a person who wins a prize or honor
  • Antonym: loser - a person who does not win or is not honored

Etymology

From Latin 'laureatus' (crowned with laurel), from 'laurus' (laurel).
