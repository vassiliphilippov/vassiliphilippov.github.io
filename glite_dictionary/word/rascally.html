rascally

1. ADJ

/ ˈræs.kəli / (AmE), / ˈrɑːs.kəli / (BrE)
Playful in a mischievous or boldly impish way, often in a manner that is appealing rather than harmful.
mischievously playful or impish

Examples

The boy gave his mother a rascally grin after hiding her shoes.
Her rascally sense of humor made everyone laugh at the party.
He flashed a rascally smile before playing another practical joke.

Synset Examples

a roguish grin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roguish - playfully mischievous
  • Synonym: mischievous - causing trouble in a playful way
  • Antonym: innocent - not harmful or intended to harm

Etymology

From 'rascal' + '-ly'; 'rascal' from Old French rascaille (rabble, mob).

2. ADJ

/ ˈræs.kəli / (AmE), / ˈrɑː.skəli / (BrE)
dishonest or lacking moral principles; characteristic of a rascal or rogue
dishonest; lacking principles

Examples

The story is full of rascally characters who trick the townsfolk.
Despite his rascally ways, everyone seemed to like him.
They put an end to the rascally scheme before it could do harm.

Synset Examples

the rascally rabble
the tyranny of a scoundrelly aristocracy
the captain was set adrift by his roguish crew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoundrelly - lacking principles or scruples; like a scoundrel
  • Synonym: roguish - dishonest or playfully mischievous; like a rogue

Etymology

From 'rascal' + '-ly', originating from Middle English 'rascaile' (rascal).
