chanoyu

1. NOUN

/ tʃɑːˈnoʊ.juː / (AmE), / tʃaˈnəʊ.juː / (BrE)
the Japanese traditional ceremony for preparing and drinking tea, following a prescribed ritual
Japanese tea ceremony

Examples

The art of chanoyu emphasizes harmony, respect, and tranquility among its participants.
During his visit to Kyoto, he attended a traditional chanoyu performed by a tea master.
Museums often display utensils used in chanoyu, such as tea bowls and whisks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tea ceremony - the ritual preparation and presentation of tea in Japanese culture

Etymology

From Japanese 茶の湯 (chanoyu), meaning 'hot water for tea'.
