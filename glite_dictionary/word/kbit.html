kbit

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈkeɪˌbɪt / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to 1000 bits, commonly used in computing and telecommunications.
unit of information equal to 1000 bits

Examples

The internet speed is measured in kbit per second.
A file size of 500 kbit downloads quickly over a broadband connection.
Older modems typically had a maximum transfer rate of 56 kbit per second.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kilobit - a unit of information equal to 1000 bits

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'kilobit,' from 'kilo-' (meaning thousand) and 'bit.'
