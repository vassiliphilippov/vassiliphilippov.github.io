surety

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʊrəti / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːrəti / (BrE)
the state of being certain or assured; something that is clearly established or beyond doubt
certainty; something established

Examples

You can move forward with the project now that there is surety about the funding.
His calm expression gave me a feeling of surety in our decision.
We lacked the surety that our plan would succeed, so we revised it further.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainty - the quality of being reliably true or established
  • Antonym: doubt - a lack of certainty or assurance

Etymology

From Old French 'seurte', from Latin 'securitas' (security, certainty).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃʊrəti, ˈsjʊrəti / (AmE), / ˈʃʊərəti, ˈʃɔːrəti / (BrE)
a person who takes responsibility for another's performance of an undertaking, such as appearing in court or paying a debt, and is liable if the other fails.
one who guarantees another's obligation
legal

Examples

The court required a surety before releasing the accused on bail.
As a surety, she was financially responsible if her brother defaulted on the loan.
Each surety signed an agreement guaranteeing payment should the primary debtor fail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guarantor - a person who guarantees to pay back a loan if the borrower does not
  • Antonym: principal - the person for whom someone acts as surety

Etymology

From Old French 'seurtee', from Latin 'securitas' (security).

In medieval times, important dignitaries were often kept as sureties during delicate negotiations.
