achondroplasia

1. NOUN

/ əˌkɑːndrəˈpleɪziə / (AmE), / əˌkɒndrəˈpleɪzɪə / (BrE)
A genetic disorder in which cartilage does not properly convert to bone, leading to dwarfism with characteristic physical features.
genetic disorder causing dwarfism
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with achondroplasia shortly after birth by a genetic specialist.
People with achondroplasia may require specialized medical care throughout their lives.
Most cases of achondroplasia occur due to a new genetic mutation rather than inheritance.

Etymology

From Greek a- (not) + chondros (cartilage) + plasis (formation).
