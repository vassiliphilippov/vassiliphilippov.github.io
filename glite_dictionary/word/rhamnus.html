Rhamnus

1. NOUN

/ ˈræmnəs / (AmE), / ˈræmnəs / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs and small trees in the buckthorn family (Rhamnaceae), including the common buckthorn.
genus of buckthorn shrubs or trees

Examples

The genus Rhamnus includes many species commonly known as buckthorns.
Some species of Rhamnus are used in traditional medicines and as ornamental plants.
Botanists studied the distribution of Rhamnus in North American woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buckthorn - a shrub or small tree of the genus Rhamnus or related genera

Etymology

From Latin *rhamnus*, from Ancient Greek *rhamnos* (a kind of thorny shrub).

2. NOUN

/ ˈræmnəs / (AmE), / ˈræmnəs / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs or small trees in the family Rhamnaceae, known as buckthorns.
genus of shrubs or small trees (buckthorns)

Examples

The leaves of Rhamnus species are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
Several invasive Rhamnus plants have spread rapidly in North America.
Birds are attracted to the berries produced by Rhamnus shrubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buckthorn - any plant of the genus Rhamnus

Etymology

From Latin *rhamnus*, from Greek *rhamnos* (a type of thorny shrub).
