shoestring

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuˌstrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃuːˌstrɪŋ / (BrE)
a thin lace or cord for fastening a shoe
lace or cord for fastening shoes

Examples

She bent down to tie the shoestring on her sneaker.
One of my shoestrings broke, so I had to buy a new pair.
He tripped because his shoestring was untied.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoelace - a lace for fastening shoes

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃuˌstrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃuː.strɪŋ / (BrE)
an amount of money that is very small and only just enough for basic needs; used especially in expressions like 'on a shoestring'
a very small amount of money

Examples

After losing his job, he was forced to live on a shoestring for several months.
The charity operates on a shoestring but manages to help hundreds of people each year.
They started their business with nothing but a shoestring and a big dream.

Synset Examples

4. NOUN

/ ˈʃuːˌstrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃuːstrɪŋ / (BrE)
a very small amount of money, especially as used to finance something frugal or limited
a small amount of money

Examples

They started their business on a shoestring and gradually built it up.
With only a shoestring to spend, their vacation was very basic.
He produced the film on a shoestring, using borrowed equipment and volunteer actors.

Synset Examples

he managed it on a shoestring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pittance - a very small or inadequate amount of money
  • Antonym: fortune - a very large amount of money

Etymology

Originally from literal 'shoe string,' referring to economy or meagerness as with simple shoe laces.
