Holcus

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑlkəs / (AmE), / ˈhɒlkəs / (BrE)
A genus of grasses native to the Old World, some species of which are widely cultivated in America.
genus of grasses

Examples

Several species of Holcus are grown as forage crops in temperate regions.
The Holcus genus includes grasses commonly known as velvet grass.
Botanists study Holcus to understand the adaptation of grasses to different climates.

Etymology

From Latin *Holcus*, from Greek *holkos* (a type of grass).
