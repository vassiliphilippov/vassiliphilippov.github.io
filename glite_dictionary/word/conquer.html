to conquer

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑŋkər / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋkə / (BrE)
to take control of a place or people by force, especially after a military invasion
take control by force, especially in war

Examples

The Romans managed to conquer vast territories during their empire's expansion.
After months of fierce fighting, the rebels finally conquered the capital city.
The general hoped his army would conquer the enemy stronghold by dawn.

Synset Examples

the invaders seized the land and property of the inhabitants
The army seized the town
The militia captured the castle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeat - to win a victory over someone in a battle or contest
  • Antonym: surrender - to give up to an enemy or authority

Etymology

From Latin 'conquerere' (to seek, procure, win), via Old French 'conquerre'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑŋ.kɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.kə / (BrE)
to take control of a place or people by force, or to overcome by effort or authority
gain control by force

Examples

The king managed to conquer the neighboring territory after a long siege.
It can be difficult to conquer your fears, but persistence pays off.
The army hoped to conquer the city before winter arrived.

Synset Examples

suppress a nascent uprising
stamp down on littering
conquer one's desires

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeat - to win a victory over someone in a contest or battle
  • Antonym: surrender - to stop resisting and submit to authority

Etymology

From Latin conquerere, from com- (together) + quaerere (to seek, obtain).

3. VERB

/ ˈkɑːŋ.kər / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.kə / (BrE)
To take control of a place or people by military force, or to successfully overcome a problem or fear.
overcome or take control (by force or effort)

Examples

After years of struggle, she finally conquered her fear of public speaking.
The army set out to conquer new territories in the north.
He was determined to conquer every challenge life threw at him.

Synset Examples

conquer your fears
conquer a country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeat - to win a victory over someone in a conflict or contest
  • Antonym: surrender - to give in or yield to an opponent or emotion

Etymology

From Latin 'conquīrere' (to seek, procure), from 'con-' (together) + 'quaerere' (to seek).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01116721-v
Synset POS: v
