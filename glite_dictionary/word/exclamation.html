exclamation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkskləˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkskləˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a sudden, excited, or emphatic expression or cry, especially one expressing surprise, anger, or pain
sudden or emphatic cry or expression

Examples

She gave an exclamation of surprise when she saw the gift.
His loud exclamation echoed through the hall.
The news was met with an exclamation of joy.

Synset Examples

she gave an exclamation of delight
there was much exclaiming over it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interjection - a word or phrase that expresses emotion or exclamation

Etymology

From Latin exclamatio, exclamationis (a shouting out), from exclamare (to cry out).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛkskləˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkskləˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a word, phrase, or sound expressing a strong or sudden emotion, often used as a rhetorical device to emphasize feeling or reaction
an abrupt expression of emotion or surprise

Examples

She uttered an exclamation of delight when she saw the surprise.
The poem is full of powerful exclamation to express the author's feelings.
Suddenly, a loud exclamation from the crowd stopped the conversation.

Synset Examples

exclamation such as ‘O tempore! O mores’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interjection - a word or phrase expressing emotion, often standing alone

Etymology

From Latin exclamatio (a loud cry), from exclamare (to cry out).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛkskləˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkskləˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a loud cry, protest, or expression of strong feeling, especially as a complaint or reproach
loud protest or complaint

Examples

Her exclamation at the unfair decision was heard across the room.
The meeting was interrupted by a sudden exclamation of protest from the back.
He made an angry exclamation about the new policy changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complaint - an expression of dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • Synonym: protest - an expression or declaration of objection
  • Antonym: praise - an expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin exclamatio (a shouting out).
