mesh

1. NOUN

/ mɛʃ / (AmE), / mɛʃ / (BrE)
the act or process of interlocking or fitting closely together, especially so as to form a network or unity
the act of interlocking or meshing

Examples

The mesh of gears must be exact for the engine to run smoothly.
A tight mesh between departments led to a successful project outcome.
The mesh of fingers created a human chain around the tree.

Synset Examples

an interlocking of arms by the police held the crowd in check

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interlock - to join or fit together firmly

Etymology

From Middle English 'mesch', from Old French 'mesche' (mesh, wire netting).

2. NOUN

/ mɛʃ / (AmE), / mɛʃ / (BrE)
a network topology in which every node is directly connected to every other node
network topology: full direct connections

Examples

The company's wireless network uses a mesh to ensure reliable communication between all devices.
In a mesh topology, data can be transmitted directly from any computer to another without passing through a hub.
Network engineers often choose a mesh for critical systems that require high resilience to connection failures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: star topology - a network topology with each node connected to a central node
  • Antonym: ring topology - a network topology where nodes are connected in a closed loop
