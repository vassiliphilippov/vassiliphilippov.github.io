hydrolysate

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑː.lɪˌseɪt / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒl.ɪˌseɪt / (BrE)
a substance produced by hydrolysis, especially a mixture of products resulting from the breakdown of proteins or other compounds by water.
product formed by hydrolysis
medical

Examples

The infant formula contains a protein hydrolysate to aid digestion.
Researchers analyzed the peptide hydrolysate for its nutritional properties.
Soy hydrolysate is often used as a flavor enhancer in processed foods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrolysis - chemical breakdown of a compound due to reaction with water

Etymology

From 'hydrolysis' + '-ate', denoting a substance derived by hydrolysis.
