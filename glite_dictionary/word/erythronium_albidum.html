Erythronium albidum

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθ.roʊˈniː.əm ˌælˈbaɪ.dəm / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθ.rəˈniː.əm ˌælˈbɪ.dəm / (BrE)
A North American wildflower of the lily family, also known as white fawn lily, with solitary white flowers having yellow centers and blue or pink exteriors.
a North American wildflower with white flowers

Examples

The spring forest floor was dotted with Erythronium albidum in full bloom.
Botanists observed how the Erythronium albidum thrived in moist woodland areas.
Gardeners appreciate Erythronium albidum for its delicate white flowers and early blooming season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogtooth violet - a common name for various Erythronium species

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Erythronium' from Greek 'erythros' (red), though this species has white flowers; 'albidum' from Latin 'albus' (white).
