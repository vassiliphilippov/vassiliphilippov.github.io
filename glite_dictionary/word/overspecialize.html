to overspecialize

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈspɛʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈspɛʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to become or make overly specialized, limiting one's knowledge or expertise to a very narrow area
to become overly specialized

Examples

Some scientists worry they might overspecialize and miss broader connections in their field.
He overspecialized in medieval poetry and struggled to find teaching positions.
It's easy to overspecialize in a technical job and lose sight of important general skills.

Synset Examples

She overspecialized when she concentrated on verbs in Fijian

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: to specialize - to concentrate on and become expert in a particular subject or skill

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'over-' (excessively) and 'specialize' (to focus on a specific area).
