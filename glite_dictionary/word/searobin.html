searobin

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌroʊbɪn / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌrəʊbɪn / (BrE)
A marine fish of the family Triglidae, found chiefly in American coastal waters, noted for its armored head and winglike pectoral fins; also called American gurnard.
marine fish with winglike fins, American gurnard

Examples

Fishermen often catch searobins by accident while trawling for other fish.
The brightly colored fins of the searobin help it glide along the sandy sea floor.
A searobin was spotted near the docks, attracting the attention of curious onlookers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gurnard - a fish of the family Triglidae, related to the searobin
