unservile

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsɝːvaɪl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɜːvaɪl / (BrE)
Not servile or submissive; not showing excessive willingness to serve or please others.
not servile or submissive

Examples

She gave her opinion in an unservile manner, refusing to flatter the committee.
The journal valued unservile criticism over blind loyalty to any ideology.
His unservile attitude earned him both respect and criticism from his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: servile - having or showing an excessive willingness to serve or please others
  • Antonym: submissive - willing to submit to the authority of others

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'servile' (from Latin 'servilis', of a slave).
