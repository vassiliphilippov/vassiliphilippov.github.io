hydrography

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈɡræfi / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəˈɡræfi / (BrE)
the scientific study and mapping of the surface waters of the Earth, especially with regard to navigation
study and mapping of surface waters

Examples

Advances in hydrography have greatly improved the safety of maritime navigation.
The government commissioned a hydrography survey to chart the area’s rivers and inland lakes.
She specializes in hydrography and often works aboard research vessels mapping the ocean floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartography - the science or practice of drawing maps
  • Degree Higher: oceanography - the scientific study of the ocean

Etymology

From French 'hydrographie', from Greek 'hydro-' (water) + 'graphia' (writing, description).
