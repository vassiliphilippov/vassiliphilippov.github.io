pretension

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
a claim or assertion of a right to something, often without adequate justification.
a claim or assertion of a right

Examples

The duke's pretension to the title was disputed by many.
Despite its pretension to cultural significance, the event was poorly attended.
Their pretension to leadership was not taken seriously by the other members.

Synset Examples

his pretension to the crown
the town still puts forward pretensions as a famous resort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claim - a demand or assertion of a right or fact
  • Synonym: right - a just claim or entitlement

Etymology

From French 'prétension', from Latin 'praetendere' meaning 'to claim'.

2. NOUN

/ prɪˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
a quality, claim, or appearance that is false, unsupported, or not based on fact; a show of importance or merit that is not justified
a false or unsupported claim or quality

Examples

His arrogant pretension to expertise quickly fell apart under questioning.
The article exposed the pretension behind the company's promises to revolutionize the industry.
She saw through his pretension and realized he had neither money nor influence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretense - an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true

Etymology

From French 'prétension', from Latin 'praetendere' (to pretend, to put forward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04796081-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ prɪˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
The act of trying to appear more important or knowledgeable than one really is; an instance of behaving in a pretentious way.
attempt to impress; pretentiousness

Examples

His affected pretension annoyed everyone at the party.
Despite her pretensions, she had little real knowledge of art.
They saw through his pretension to wealth and sophistication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretentiousness - the quality of being pretentious
  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of being modest, not seeking to impress

Etymology

From French 'prétension', from Latin 'praetensio' (a stretching forth, a claim).
