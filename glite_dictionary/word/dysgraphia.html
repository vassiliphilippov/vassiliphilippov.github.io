dysgraphia

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɡræfiə / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡræfiə / (BrE)
a learning disorder marked by impaired ability to write, often despite adequate instruction and intelligence
impaired ability to write (learning disorder)
medical

Examples

The school provided special resources to help students diagnosed with dysgraphia.
People with dysgraphia may struggle to form letters or organize their thoughts on paper.
Accommodations such as typing were offered to manage his dysgraphia.

Etymology

From Greek dys- (bad, difficult) + -graphia (writing)
