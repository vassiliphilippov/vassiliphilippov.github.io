Cacatua

1. NOUN

/ ˌkækəˈtuə / (AmE), / ˌkækəˈtjuːə / (BrE)
a genus of large, mostly white cockatoos native to Southeast Asia, Australia, and nearby islands
genus of cockatoos

Examples

The genus Cacatua includes several species of white cockatoos found in Australia and Southeast Asia.
Ornithologists have long studied the behavior and habitat of Cacatua.
A member of the Cacatua genus is easily recognized by its distinctive crest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockatoo - a type of parrot with an erectile crest, native to Australasia

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Malay 'kakatua' (cockatoo).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑkəˈtuːə / (AmE), / ˌkakəˈtjuːə / (BrE)
A genus of parrots in the family Psittacidae, known as cockatoos, native to Australasia and nearby islands.
a genus of cockatoos

Examples

The Cacatua genus includes several species with striking white plumage.
Many species within Cacatua are prized as pets for their intelligence and charm.
The sulphur-crested cockatoo, a member of Cacatua, is native to Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockatoo - a member of the Cacatua genus, a large parrot with a crest

Etymology

From Latinized form of Malay 'kakatuwah' (cockatoo).
