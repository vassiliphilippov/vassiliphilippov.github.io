Lithodidae

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈθɑdəˌdiː / (AmE), / lɪˈθɒdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, deep-sea crabs, commonly referred to as king crabs, found in cold waters.
family of deep-sea king crabs

Examples

The Lithodidae family includes some of the largest crabs found in the deep sea.
Several species within the Lithodidae are harvested commercially as king crabs.
Scientists study Lithodidae to better understand how organisms adapt to cold, deep-sea environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king crab - large edible crab of the Lithodidae family

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Lithodidae', from Greek 'lithos' (stone), referring to the stony habitat.
