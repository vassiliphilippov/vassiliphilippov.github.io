Benedetto Odescalchi

1. PROPN

/ bɛnəˈdɛtoʊ ˌoʊdɛsˈkɑlki / (AmE), / bɛnɛˈdɛttoʊ əʊdɛsˈkalki / (BrE)
The Italian pope (1611–1689), also known as Pope Innocent XI, noted for his saintliness and for his conflict with Louis XIV of France over papal authority; canonized in 1956.
Italian pope, also known as Innocent XI

Examples

Historians often praise Benedetto Odescalchi for his reform efforts within the Catholic Church.
Before becoming Pope Innocent XI, Benedetto Odescalchi served as a cardinal in Northern Italy.
The canonization of Benedetto Odescalchi took place more than two centuries after his death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pope Innocent XI - Papal name of Benedetto Odescalchi, Italian pope from 1676-1689
