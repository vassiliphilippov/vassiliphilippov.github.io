belittlement

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlɪtəlmənt / (AmE), / bɪˈlɪtəlmənt / (BrE)
the act of making someone or something seem unimportant or of little value
the act of making something seem unimportant

Examples

His constant belittlement of her ideas made her feel undervalued in meetings.
The teacher warned against belittlement of classmates for making mistakes.
Such public belittlement can seriously damage a person's self-esteem.

Synset Examples

an unconscionable belittlement of his spouse in public

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denigration - the act of unfairly criticizing someone or something
  • Antonym: exaltation - the act of praising or raising someone to a higher status

Etymology

From 'belittle' + '-ment', modelled after other noun forms meaning 'the action or result of'.
