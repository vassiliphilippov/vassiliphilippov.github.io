steadfast

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɛdˌfæst / (AmE), / ˈstɛdˌfɑːst / (BrE)
firm and unwavering in loyalty, support, or determination; dependable and unchanging
firm and dependable, especially in loyalty

Examples

She remained steadfast in her support of the charity despite criticism.
His steadfast friendship gave me strength during difficult times.
The team was steadfast in pursuing its goals, no matter the obstacles.

Synset Examples

a steadfast ally
a staunch defender of free speech
unswerving devotion
unswerving allegiance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loyal - showing firm support or allegiance
  • Synonym: faithful - remaining loyal and steadfast
  • Antonym: fickle - changing frequently, especially as regards one's loyalties

Etymology

From Old English stede (place) + fæst (firm, fixed).

2. ADJ

/ ˈstɛdˌfæst / (AmE), / ˈstɛdˌfɑːst / (BrE)
firm and unwavering in purpose, loyalty, or resolve; not easily changed or moved
firm and unwavering; resolute

Examples

She remained steadfast in her beliefs, even when challenged by others.
His steadfast support was a source of great comfort during difficult times.
Despite repeated setbacks, their steadfast determination led them to success.

Synset Examples

firm convictions
a firm mouth
steadfast resolve
a man of unbendable perseverance
unwavering loyalty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolute - firmly determined or resolute
  • Antonym: wavering - not firm or resolute; easily swayed

Etymology

From Old English 'stedefæst' (firmly fixed, steadfast), from 'stede' (place) + 'fæst' (firm).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01997735-s
Synset POS: s
