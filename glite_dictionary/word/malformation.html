malformation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæl.fɔɹˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmæl.fɔːˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an abnormal or irregular formation or structure, especially in a living organism.
abnormally formed part or structure

Examples

The X-ray revealed a malformation in the patient's spine.
Some congenital heart malformations require surgical correction soon after birth.
The scientists studied the causes of limb malformation in amphibians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deformity - a misshapen or abnormal part, especially of the body

Etymology

From French 'malformation', from 'mal-' (badly) + 'formation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæl.fɔːrˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmæl.fɔːˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an abnormal or misshapen structure, especially of the body, often present from birth
abnormal bodily structure
medical

Examples

The infant was born with a rare malformation of the heart that required immediate surgery.
Doctors detected a spinal malformation during the routine prenatal scan.
Some genetic disorders can result in skeletal malformations that affect growth and movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deformity - the state of being deformed or misshapen, especially of the body

Etymology

From French 'malformation', from Latin 'malus' (bad) + 'formatio' (shaping).
