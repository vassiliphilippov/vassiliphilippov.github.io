disastrously

1. ADV

/ dɪˈzæstrəsli / (AmE), / dɪˈzɑːstrəsli / (BrE)
In a way that causes great harm, damage, or failure.
in a disastrous manner

Examples

The company expanded disastrously, leading to bankruptcy within a year.
The chef overcooked the meal disastrously, and guests had to order pizza instead.
Their attempt to fix the sink went disastrously wrong, flooding the kitchen.

Synset Examples

the real value of the trust capital may be disastrously less than when the trust began

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catastrophically - in a catastrophic manner
  • Antonym: successfully - in a successful manner

Etymology

From 'disastrous' + '-ly'. 'Disastrous' from Italian 'disastro' (disaster), ultimately from Greek 'dis-' (ill) and 'astron' (star).
