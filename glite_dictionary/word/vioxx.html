Vioxx

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪ.ɑks / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.ɒks / (BrE)
a trademarked anti-inflammatory drug (rofecoxib), formerly used to relieve pain and inflammation, withdrawn for safety concerns in 2004
withdrawn pain-relief drug (rofecoxib)
medical

Examples

After taking Vioxx, many patients experienced significant pain relief without stomach issues.
The pharmaceutical company recalled Vioxx after safety concerns emerged about its long-term use.
She read several articles about the risks that led to Vioxx's withdrawal from the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rofecoxib - the generic name for the drug Vioxx
  • Synonym: Cox-2 inhibitor - type of drug reducing pain/inflammation by blocking the Cox-2 enzyme

Etymology

Trade name for rofecoxib, introduced by Merck in 1999.
