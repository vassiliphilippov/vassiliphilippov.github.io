squawbush

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑːˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈskwɔːˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to California, known for its unpleasantly scented, usually trifoliate leaves, and edible fruit.
deciduous shrub native to California

Examples

We spotted a squawbush growing at the edge of the hiking trail near the canyon.
The berries of the squawbush are considered edible and have been used by Native Americans.
The squawbush releases a strong odor when its leaves are crushed.
