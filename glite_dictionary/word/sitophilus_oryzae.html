Sitophilus oryzae

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈtɒfɪləs oʊˈraɪziː / (AmE), / sɪˈtɒfɪləs ɒˈriːziː / (BrE)
a species of small brown weevil that infests stored grain, especially rice, causing significant damage
a grain-infesting brown weevil species

Examples

The stored rice was ruined by an infestation of Sitophilus oryzae.
Monitoring for Sitophilus oryzae is essential in grain storage facilities to prevent losses.
Sitophilus oryzae can be controlled with proper sanitation and regular inspection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weevil - a type of beetle characterized by an elongated snout, often a pest

Etymology

From Latin: ‘sitos’ (grain) + ‘philos’ (loving); ‘oryzae’ from Latin ‘oryza’ (rice).
