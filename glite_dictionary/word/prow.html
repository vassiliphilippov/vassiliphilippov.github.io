prow

1. NOUN

/ praʊ / (AmE), / praʊ / (BrE)
the pointed front part of a ship or boat; the bow
front part of a ship or boat

Examples

The sailors gathered near the prow as the ship cut through the waves.
Damage to the prow forced the boat to return to shore for repairs.
A figurehead was mounted on the prow of the ancient vessel.

Synset Examples

he pointed the bow of the boat toward the finish line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bow - front part of a ship or boat
  • Antonym: stern - rear part of a ship or boat

Etymology

From Old French 'proue', from Italian 'prua', ultimately from Latin 'prora' (ship's prow).
