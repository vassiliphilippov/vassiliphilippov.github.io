curtailment

1. NOUN

/ kɜrˈteɪlmənt / (AmE), / kɜːˈteɪlmənt / (BrE)
The act of reducing or shortening something; the process of being cut short.
the act of reducing or shortening

Examples

The curtailment of public services sparked widespread protests.
Due to budget cuts, there was a significant curtailment of research activities this year.
Midway through the show, an unexpected power outage forced the curtailment of the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller or less
  • Antonym: augmentation - the act of making something greater in size or amount

Etymology

From Old French 'court' (short) and 'tail' (to cut), influenced by 'curtail'.

2. NOUN

/ kərˈteɪlmənt / (AmE), / kəˈteɪlmənt / (BrE)
the act of reducing something, especially spending or activity
the act of reducing something

Examples

The company announced a curtailment of its spending to avoid layoffs.
Government curtailment of public services sparked protests among citizens.
During tough economic times, families often face the curtailment of non-essential expenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller or less in amount, degree, or size
  • Antonym: expansion - the process of increasing in amount or extent

Etymology

From the verb 'curtail', from Old French 'courtailler' (to shorten).
