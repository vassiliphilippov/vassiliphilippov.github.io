degradation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛɡ.rəˈdeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛɡ.rəˈdeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of being reduced to a lower, less respected, or less desirable condition
decline to a lower, less respected state

Examples

The degradation of the city's infrastructure became apparent after years of neglect.
Environmental degradation threatens the health and livelihoods of many communities.
He felt a sense of degradation after losing his job and status.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debasement - the act of lowering in status, value, or quality
  • Synonym: humiliation - the act of making someone feel ashamed or lose respect
  • Antonym: improvement - the process of becoming better or more desirable

Etymology

From Latin degradatio (a downgrading), from degradare (to reduce in rank).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛɡrəˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛɡrəˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
A state of having lost dignity, honor, or position; a low or humiliated condition.
loss of dignity or status

Examples

His repeated failures led him to a state of degradation he could never have imagined.
The cruel punishment subjected the prisoner to considerable degradation in front of his peers.
After the scandal, she withdrew from public life in a period of emotional degradation.

Synset Examples

each confession brought her into an attitude of abasement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abasement - a lowering in rank, dignity, or self-esteem
  • Synonym: humiliation - the act or state of being made to feel ashamed or degraded
  • Antonym: exaltation - a feeling or state of extreme happiness or elevation

Etymology

From Latin 'degradatio' (a lowering in rank), from 'degradare' (to degrade).
