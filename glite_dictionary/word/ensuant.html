ensuant

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsuənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsjʊənt / (BrE)
happening as a result or consequence; following immediately after something else.
following as a consequence

Examples

An ensuant debate erupted after the controversial policy was introduced.
The accident and the ensuant investigation caused major traffic delays.
He apologized for the confusion and any ensuant inconvenience this might have caused.

Synset Examples

an excessive growth of bureaucracy, with attendant problems
snags incidental to the changeover in management
attendant circumstances
the period of tension and consequent need for military preparedness
the ensuant response to his appeal
the resultant savings were considerable
collateral target damage from a bombing run

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequent - resulting as a consequence
  • Synonym: resultant - happening as a result of something
  • Antonym: preceding - coming before in time or order

Etymology

From Latin 'ensuens, ensuentis', present participle of 'ensuere' (to follow after).
