loathing

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊðɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈləʊðɪŋ / (BrE)
A strong feeling of hatred mixed with disgust.
intense hatred or disgust

Examples

She spoke of her loathing for cruelty towards animals.
His face twisted in loathing as he looked at the polluted river.
There was an air of loathing between the two rivals throughout the debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hatred - intense dislike or ill will
  • Synonym: detestation - intense dislike or abhorrence
  • Antonym: fondness - affection or liking for someone or something

Etymology

From Old English *loðian* (to be hateful or displeasing).
