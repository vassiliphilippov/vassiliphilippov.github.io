rhinovirus

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪnəˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈraɪnəʊˌvaɪərəs / (BrE)
any of a group of small RNA viruses of the picornavirus family that cause many cases of the common cold and other upper respiratory infections in humans
virus causing the common cold
medical

Examples

Doctors identified the rhinovirus as the cause of her cold-like symptoms.
The rhinovirus spreads easily through sneezing and direct contact.
Researchers are developing a vaccine to protect against rhinovirus infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coronavirus - a large group of viruses causing illnesses ranging from the common cold to severe diseases

Etymology

From Greek 'rhino-' (nose) + 'virus' (Latin: poison, slime).
