haemoglobinemia

1. NOUN

/ hiːˌmoʊˌɡloʊbɪˈniːmiə / (AmE), / hiːˌməʊ.ɡləʊ.bɪˈniː.mi.ə / (BrE)
The presence of an abnormally high amount of hemoglobin in the blood plasma.
excess hemoglobin in blood plasma
medical british

Examples

The laboratory tests confirmed the patient had haemoglobinemia after a severe hemolytic reaction.
Prolonged haemoglobinemia can result in kidney damage if not treated promptly.
Doctors observed signs of haemoglobinemia during the diagnosis of blood transfusion complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemoglobinemia - presence of excessive hemoglobin in blood plasma (US spelling)

Etymology

From haemoglobin (British spelling of hemoglobin) + -emia (from Greek haima, 'blood').
