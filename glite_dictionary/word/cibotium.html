Cibotium

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈboʊtiəm / (AmE), / sɪˈbəʊtiəm / (BrE)
A genus of large ornamental tree ferns, known for their coarse, gracefully drooping fronds.
genus of ornamental tree ferns

Examples

The garden's most striking feature is a towering Cibotium flourishing near the pond.
Horticulturists prize Cibotium for its elegant, arching fronds and tropical appearance.
After planting a Cibotium, be sure to provide plenty of moisture and shade for best growth.

Etymology

From Greek kibōtion (a chest or box), referring to the shape of part of the plant.

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈboʊtiəm / (AmE), / sɪˈbəʊtiəm / (BrE)
A genus of large ornamental tree ferns with coarse, gracefully drooping fronds, sometimes placed in the family Cyatheaceae.
genus of large ornamental tree ferns

Examples

Cibotium ferns are often grown for their striking, arching fronds in subtropical gardens.
The Cibotium genus includes several species commonly known as tree ferns.
Researchers are studying the unique adaptations of Cibotium in their native forest habitats.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Greek kibōtion (little box, chest), used as a botanical genus name.
