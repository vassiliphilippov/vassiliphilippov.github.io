vertex

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝː.tɛks / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tɛks / (BrE)
the highest point or top of something, especially a mountain or a pyramid
the highest point or top

Examples

Standing at the vertex of the mountain, she marveled at the panoramic view.
The vertex of the pyramid was crowned with a shining capstone.
Climbers reached the vertex just before sunset and celebrated their achievement.

Synset Examples

at the peak of the pyramid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apex - the top or highest part of something
  • Antonym: base - the bottom or lowest part of something

Etymology

From Latin vertex (whirlpool, crown of the head, top).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɝː.tɛks / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tɛks / (BrE)
the point where two or more lines meet, or the point opposite the base in a geometric figure
corner point in geometry

Examples

The triangle has three vertices where its sides meet.
In this pyramid, the vertex is directly above the center of the base.
You need to label each vertex to identify the corners of the polygon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apex - the highest point, especially in geometry
  • Antonym: base - the bottom side of a geometric figure

Etymology

From Latin 'vertex' (whirl, top, summit).

Synset POS: n
