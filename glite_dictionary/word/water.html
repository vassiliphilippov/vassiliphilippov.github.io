4. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːtɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
(historical, philosophy) One of the four elements thought in ancient and medieval philosophy to compose the universe, associated with the humour phlegm.
classical element in ancient philosophy

Examples

In ancient Greek philosophy, water was considered one of the four fundamental elements of the cosmos.
According to Empedocles, water corresponded to the bodily humour known as phlegm.
Medieval alchemists believed that water represented coolness and moisture among the basic elements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earth - one of the four classical elements; the element of solidity and stability
  • Synonym: fire - one of the four classical elements; the element of energy and transformation
  • Synonym: air - one of the four classical elements; the element of breath and intellect

Etymology

From Old English wæter, related to Proto-Germanic *watōr and Proto-Indo-European *wódr̥ (water).
