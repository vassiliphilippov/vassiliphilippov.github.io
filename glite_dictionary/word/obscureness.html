obscureness

1. NOUN

/ əbˈskjʊrnəs / (AmE), / əbˈskjʊənəs / (BrE)
The quality of being unclear, difficult to understand, or not easily perceived.
the state of being unclear or hard to understand

Examples

The obscureness of the instructions made them difficult to follow.
She criticized the obscureness of the artist's message in the painting.
Many students struggle with the obscureness of some philosophical texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown, inconspicuous, or difficult to understand
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From 'obscure' + '-ness', with 'obscure' from Latin 'obscurus' (dark, indistinct).

2. NOUN

/ əbˈskjʊrnəs / (AmE), / əbˈskjʊənɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being unclear or hard to see, especially due to insufficient light
quality of being unclear or hard to see

Examples

The obscureness of the room made it difficult to find the missing keys.
Rain and fog added to the obscureness of the gloomy evening.
She stumbled in the obscureness before the lights were turned on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown, inconspicuous, or difficult to understand
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear or easy to see or understand

Etymology

From Latin 'obscūrus' (dark, indistinct) + English suffix '-ness'.
