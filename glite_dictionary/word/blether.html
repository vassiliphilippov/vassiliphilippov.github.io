blether

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɛðər / (AmE), / ˈblɛðə / (BrE)
idle, foolish, or irrelevant talk; chatter or gossip, especially of little importance
idle or foolish talk; chatter
british

Examples

Please stop your blether and get to the point.
The room was full of blether about the latest office gossip.
Instead of working, they wasted time in endless blether.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - talk rapidly or casually in a trivial manner
  • Synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way
  • Antonym: silence - the absence of sound or talk

Etymology

From Scottish dialect, related to Old Norse *blathra* (to talk nonsense).

2. VERB

/ ˈblɛðər / (AmE), / ˈblɛðə / (BrE)
to talk at length in a silly or pointless way; to chatter foolishly
talk foolishly or aimlessly

Examples

She loves to blether on the phone for hours with her friends.
Don’t mind him—he’s just blethering about the weather again.
After a couple of drinks, they began to blether about their school days.

Synset Examples

The two women babbled and crooned at the baby

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - to talk rapidly in a foolish or incoherent way
  • Synonym: prattle - to talk in a foolish or inconsequential way

Etymology

Probably from Scots 'blether' (nonsense talk), of uncertain origin.
