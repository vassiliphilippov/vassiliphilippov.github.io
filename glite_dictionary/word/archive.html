archive

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːr.kaɪv / (AmE), / ˈɑː.kaɪv / (BrE)
a place where public records, historical documents, or other valuable materials are stored and preserved
place for storing historical documents

Examples

The national archive holds millions of historical photographs and official documents.
Researchers often visit the city archive to access old maps and records.
Many important letters were discovered in the university's archive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repository - a place where things are stored and maintained

Etymology

From French 'archive', from Latin 'archīvum', from Greek 'arkheion' (public office, town hall).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02738091-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈɑrˌkaɪv / (AmE), / ˈɑːˌkaɪv / (BrE)
to place or store documents, data, or materials in an archive for long-term preservation or reference
store in an archive

Examples

We decided to archive the old emails instead of deleting them.
The company regularly archives its financial reports for future reference.
After finishing the project, all related documents were archived in the company database.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: store - to put something away for future use
  • Antonym: to delete - to remove or erase, especially from a record

Etymology

From French *archive*, from Latin *archīvum*, from Greek *arkheion* (public records).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01387292-v
Synset POS: v
