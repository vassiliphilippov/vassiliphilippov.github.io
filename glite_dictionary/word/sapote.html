sapote

1. NOUN

/ səˈpoʊti / (AmE), / səˈpəʊti / (BrE)
a tropical American tree with wood similar to mahogany and sweet, edible, egg-shaped fruit; also, the fruit of this tree.
tropical fruit tree and its edible fruit

Examples

The fresh sapote was delicious and creamy, with a unique flavor reminiscent of custard.
A large sapote tree grew beside the river, providing shade and sweet fruit.
After dinner, we enjoyed slices of chilled sapote for dessert.

Etymology

From Nahuatl *tzapotl* (soft, edible fruit).

2. NOUN

/ səˈpoʊti / (AmE), / səˈpəʊti / (BrE)
An edible, usually brown or greenish tropical fruit with soft, sweet flesh, from several trees native to Central and South America.
tropical fruit with sweet, soft flesh

Examples

We tasted the creamy sapote during our trip to Mexico.
The local market sold fresh sapotes and other exotic fruits.
Her favorite dessert was a sherbet made from black sapote.

Etymology

From Spanish 'zapote', from Nahuatl 'tzapotl' (soft, sweet fruit).
