Heteromeles arbutifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬əroʊˈmiːliːz ɑːˌbjuːtəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˈmiːliːz ɑːˌbjuːtɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pacific coast of the United States, with large white flowers and bright red berry-like fruits, also known as toyon or California holly
evergreen shrub with white flowers and red berries

Examples

Many native gardeners plant Heteromeles arbutifolia for its attractive red berries and year-round foliage.
The berries of Heteromeles arbutifolia have been used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
You can easily spot Heteromeles arbutifolia along coastal hillsides in California during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toyon - common name for Heteromeles arbutifolia

Etymology

From Greek heteros (different) + mēlon (apple); arbutifolia from Latin arbutus (strawberry tree) + folium (leaf).
