enmity

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.mə.ti / (AmE), / ˈɛn.mɪ.ti / (BrE)
A feeling or state of deep hostility or hatred; strong antagonism or ill will.
deep hostility or hatred

Examples

Generations of enmity have soured relations between the two neighboring countries.
Despite their long-standing enmity, they worked together for the greater good.
She could not understand the sudden enmity he showed after years of friendship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostility - hostile or unfriendly state; antagonism
  • Antonym: friendship - state of mutual trust and support between friends

Etymology

From Old French *enmitei*, from Latin *inimicitas* (hostility, enmity), from *inimicus* (enemy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.mə.ti / (AmE), / ˈɛn.mɪ.ti / (BrE)
a feeling or state of active hostility or hatred towards someone or something
state of hostility or hatred

Examples

Decades of political rivalry created deep enmity between the two leaders.
Despite their shared goals, a sense of enmity remained just beneath the surface.
They managed to put their enmity aside for the sake of peace.

Synset Examples

he could no longer contain his hostility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostility - hostile or unfriendly behavior or attitude
  • Antonym: friendship - a state of friendly relations or affection

Etymology

From Old French 'enemistie', from Latin 'inimicitia' (hostility), from 'inimicus' (enemy).
