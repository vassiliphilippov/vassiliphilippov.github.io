deferential

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɛfəˈrɛnʃl̩ / (AmE), / ˌdɛfəˈrɛnʃl̩ / (BrE)
showing respect or polite submission to someone, especially because of their position, age, or experience
respectful or submissive

Examples

The doctor was deferential to his senior colleague's experience during the meeting.
She spoke in a deferential tone when addressing the judge.
His deferential attitude made a good impression on his supervisors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respectful - showing or expressing respect
  • Antonym: insolent - showing a rude and arrogant lack of respect

Etymology

From deference + -ial, ultimately from Latin deferre (to carry away, to yield).
