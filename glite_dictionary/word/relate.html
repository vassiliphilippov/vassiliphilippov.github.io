to relate

1. VERB

/ rɪˈleɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈleɪt / (BrE)
to have a connection or relationship with something; to be connected in some way
to be connected or associated

Examples

The two incidents do not relate directly, but both are important for our investigation.
The study aims to show how climate change and human activity relate to each other.
Can you explain how these facts relate to the main topic?

Synset Examples

How are these two observations related?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: associate - to connect in the mind or be connected with something else
  • Antonym: disconnect - to separate or become unconnected

Etymology

From Latin 'relatus', past participle of 'referre' (to bring back, report).

WordNet ID: oewn-02730292-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈleɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈleɪt / (BrE)
to have a connection with or be relevant to something; to refer to or concern
be relevant or connected to

Examples

His research relates directly to the topic of climate change.
The new evidence relates to the case we discussed last week.
My question relates to the points you raised earlier.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pertain - to relate to or have a connection with
  • Synonym: to refer - to mention or allude to; to have relation to
  • Synonym: concerning - regarding or relating to something

Etymology

From Latin relatus, past participle of referre, meaning 'to bring back, report'.
