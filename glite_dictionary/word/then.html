then

1. ADJ

/ ðɛn / (AmE), / ðɛn / (BrE)
Being in office or position at a particular time in the past.
holding a position at an earlier time

Examples

The country was led by the then prime minister during the crisis.
She collaborated with the then director of the institute on several projects.
The then policy was later replaced by more progressive measures.

2. NOUN

/ ðɛn / (AmE), / ðɛn / (BrE)
the time or moment being referred to, often used after a preposition to indicate a specific point in the past or future
the time (referred to earlier)

Examples

We agreed to meet and would discuss the project at then.
The museum was under construction until then.
From then on, everything began to change.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From Old English 'ðænne', related to 'the','that'.
