partisan

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːr.tə.zən / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.tɪ.zæn / (BrE)
a long spear with a tapering double-edged blade and side projections, used as a weapon in the 16th and 17th centuries
historical pole weapon

Examples

The museum displayed a 17th-century partisan among its impressive collection of medieval weapons.
Soldiers equipped with a partisan could fight both cavalry and infantry effectively.
During the reenactment, the actor wielded a partisan to demonstrate historical battle techniques.

Etymology

From Middle French *partisane*, possibly from Italian *partigiana*, of uncertain origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːr.tə.zən / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.tɪ.zæn / (BrE)
strongly supporting a particular cause, party, or group, often without considering other views or being fair
strong supporter of a cause or party

Examples

The debate became heated and partisan as both sides refused to compromise.
She wrote a partisan article in support of her political party.
It is important for judges to remain impartial and not let partisan opinions influence their decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biased - unfairly prejudiced for or against something
  • Antonym: neutral - not supporting any side in a conflict or disagreement

Etymology

From Middle French *partisan*, from Italian *partigiano* (member of a party), from *parte* (part).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00734175-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːr.t̬ɪ.zən / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.tɪ.zæn / (BrE)
A strong and enthusiastic supporter of a particular person, group, or cause, especially in politics.
strong supporter, often political

Examples

She has always been a loyal partisan of environmental causes.
The debate became heated as each partisan defended their party's policies.
During elections, the media often struggle to remain neutral amid fierce partisan opinions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supporter - a person who supports a particular cause or individual
  • Antonym: opponent - a person who disagrees with or fights against something

Etymology

From Middle French 'partisan', from Italian 'partigiano' (member of a faction), from 'parte' (part, party).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10079390-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːr.t̬ə.zən / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.tɪ.zæn / (BrE)
A strong supporter of a person, group, cause, or idea, often showing biased and unwavering loyalty.
strong, often biased supporter

Examples

As a known partisan of the reform movement, his views were often challenged by opponents.
The newspaper was criticized for its partisan coverage of the election.
She always acts as a partisan during debates, rarely acknowledging the other side's merits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supporter - someone who supports a person or cause
  • Antonym: opponent - someone who opposes a person or cause

Etymology

From Middle French 'partisan' (supporter) and Italian 'partigiano', from 'parte' (party, side).
