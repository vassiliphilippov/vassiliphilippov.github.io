Pyrolaceae

1. NOUN

/ paɪˌroʊˈleɪsiː / (AmE), / paɪˌrɒˈleɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of evergreen herbs found in temperate regions, including the genera Pyrola, Chimaphila, Moneses, and Orthilia.
family of evergreen temperate herbs

Examples

The Pyrolaceae thrive in the moist woodlands of northern Europe.
Botanists have reclassified some members of the Pyrolaceae into new families.
Species in the Pyrolaceae are noted for their evergreen leaves and small, waxy flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin Pyrolaceae, named after the type genus Pyrola.
