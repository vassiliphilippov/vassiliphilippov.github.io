pumice

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌ.mɪs / (AmE), / ˈpʌm.ɪs / (BrE)
a light, porous volcanic rock formed when lava cools rapidly, often used as an abrasive for cleaning, polishing, or smoothing surfaces
light, porous volcanic rock used as abrasive

Examples

She used a pumice stone to smooth the rough skin on her heels.
Volcanic ash and pumice covered the village after the eruption.
The pumice is so light that it can float on water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pumice stone - piece of pumice used for abrasive purposes
  • Synonym: lava - molten rock from a volcano

Etymology

From Latin 'pumex', meaning 'foam' or 'pumice stone'.

2. VERB

/ ˈpʌmɪs / (AmE), / ˈpʌmɪs / (BrE)
to rub or polish a surface with pumice stone in order to clean or smooth it
rub or polish with pumice stone

Examples

The carpenter carefully pumiced the wooden surface until it was smooth to the touch.
After removing the old paint, she pumiced the stone tiles to restore their original shine.
He spent the afternoon pumicing his callused feet in preparation for the race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polish - make the surface of something smooth and shiny by rubbing
  • Synonym: to abrade - scrape or wear away by friction or erosion

Etymology

From Old French *pumice*, from Latin *pumex* (pumice stone).
