gasconade

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæskəˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˌɡæskəˈneɪd / (BrE)
boastful or extravagant talk; bragging
boastful talk; bragging
archaic

Examples

His stories are always filled with gasconade and exaggeration.
The general's gasconade about his victories amused the soldiers.
People soon grew tired of his endless gasconade at parties.

Synset Examples

his brag is worse than his fight
whenever he won we were exposed to his gasconade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brag - boastful talk or bragging
  • Synonym: bluster - loud, aggressive, or indignant talk with little effect
  • Antonym: modesty - lack of boastfulness or arrogance

Etymology

From French 'gasconade' (boasting), from Gascon (a person from Gascony, a region in France), stereotyped for boastfulness.

2. VERB

/ ˌɡæs.kəˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˌɡæs.kəˈneɪd / (BrE)
to boast or brag in a showy or extravagant way; to show off
to boast or show off extravagantly
archaic

Examples

He would often gasconade about his exploits in the most exaggerated fashion.
Tired of listening to him gasconade, his friends left early.
She gasconaded endlessly at the dinner table, hoping to impress everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride about one's achievements
  • Antonym: humble - to act modestly or without pride

Etymology

From French *gasconner*, from *Gascon* (with reference to the reputed boastfulness of people from Gascony, France).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɡæskəˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˌɡæskəˈneɪd / (BrE)
boastful or extravagant talk, especially about one's achievements or possessions
boastful talk; bragging

Examples

Tired of his constant gasconade at family dinners, she stopped inviting him.
The politician's gasconade about his wealth alienated many voters.
Critics dismissed the millionaire's remarks as mere gasconade rather than true success.

Synset Examples

His gasconade about wealth was hard to believe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride and self-satisfaction about one's achievements
  • Synonym: brag - to talk about something in a way that shows excessive pride
  • Antonym: humility - a modest or low view of one's importance; opposite of boastfulness

Etymology

From French 'gasconnade', from Gascon (inhabitant of Gascony in France), suggesting boastfulness.
