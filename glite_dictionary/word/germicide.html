germicide

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜrmɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmɪsaɪd / (BrE)
a substance or agent that destroys harmful microorganisms, especially those that can cause disease
agent that destroys microorganisms

Examples

Hospitals use germicide to clean surfaces and prevent the spread of infection.
This chemical acts as an effective germicide in water treatment plants.
Always read the label before applying a germicide to household surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disinfectant - a chemical liquid that destroys bacteria
  • Synonym: antiseptic - an agent that inhibits the growth of microorganisms

Etymology

From Latin 'germen' (sprout, germ) + '-cide' from Latin 'caedere' (to kill).
