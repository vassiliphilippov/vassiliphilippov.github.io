analphabetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌænˌælfəˈbɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌanˌalfəˈbɛtɪk / (BrE)
Unable to read or write; having no knowledge of the alphabet.
unable to read or write

Examples

The NGO launched a program aimed at helping the analphabetic members of the community.
Special classes were organized to address the needs of analphabetic adults.
Despite being analphabetic, he displayed remarkable intelligence and creativity.

Synset Examples

special tutorials to assist the unlettered sector of society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illiterate - unable to read or write

Etymology

From Greek an- (not) + alphabetic (relating to the alphabet).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01424760-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌæn.æl.fəˈbɛt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌan.alfəˈbɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
not using or based on an alphabet; not arranged in alphabetical order
not alphabetic or alphabetical

Examples

The new code uses an analphabetic system of symbols instead of letters.
These records are sorted in an analphabetic order, making them hard to find quickly.
Some phonetic scripts are analphabetic, as they do not correspond to any alphabet.

Synset Examples

an analphabetic arrangement of letters
Jesperson's system of phonetic transcription is analphabetic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alphabetic - relating to or using an alphabet

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (not) + 'alphabetikos' (alphabetic).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00101913-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˌænælˌfæˈbɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌanælˈfɛbɪtɪk / (BrE)
a person who cannot read and does not know the letters of the alphabet
person unable to read or recognize letters

Examples

The literacy program was created to assist analphabetic adults in learning to read.
He grew up in a remote area where almost everyone was analphabetic.
Many analphabetic children face difficulties accessing education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illiterate - person unable to read or write

Etymology

From Greek an- (not) + alphabetikos (of the alphabet).

4. ADJ

/ ˌænˌæl.fəˈbɛ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌanˌal.fəˈbɛ.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to or expressed by a writing system that does not use an alphabet, such as logographic or syllabic scripts.
not based on an alphabet (writing system)

Examples

Chinese characters are an example of an analphabetic writing system.
Hieroglyphs are considered analphabetic because they rely on pictorial symbols rather than individual letters.
Some early scripts were analphabetic, preceding the invention of alphabets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alphabetic - using or consisting of an alphabet

Etymology

From prefix an- (not) + alphabetic, from Greek 'an-' + 'alphabētos' (alphabet).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02624852-a
Synset POS: a
