apologue

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpəˌlɔg / (AmE), / ˈæpəlɒg / (BrE)
A short moral story, often featuring animal characters, intended to convey a lesson.
short moral story, often with animals

Examples

The ancient storyteller narrated an apologue to teach the children about honesty.
Aesop's most famous apologues use animals to illustrate important human virtues and flaws.
Through the apologue, the author cleverly disguised his criticism of society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fable - a short story conveying a moral, often with animals as characters
  • Synonym: parable - a simple story used to illustrate a moral or spiritual lesson

Etymology

From Greek 'apologos' (story, fable), via Latin 'apologus'.
