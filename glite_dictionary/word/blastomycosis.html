blastomycosis

1. NOUN

/ blæstoʊmaɪˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / blæstəʊmaɪˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a rare fungal infection of the skin or mucous membranes, usually caused by Blastomyces fungi
fungal infection caused by Blastomyces
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with blastomycosis after developing persistent skin lesions.
Physicians in certain regions are more familiar with treating blastomycosis due to its prevalence there.
Untreated blastomycosis can lead to serious complications if the infection spreads beyond the skin.

Etymology

From New Latin Blastomyces (the genus name) + -osis (indicating a disease).
