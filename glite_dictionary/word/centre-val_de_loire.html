Centre-Val de Loire

1. PROPN

/ sɑ̃tʁə val də lwaʁ / (AmE), / sɑ̃tʁə val də lwaʁ / (BrE)
a low-lying administrative region in central France, known for its châteaux, vineyards, and the Loire Valley
region of central France

Examples

Many tourists visit Centre-Val de Loire to explore its famous châteaux and scenic landscapes.
The wines produced in Centre-Val de Loire are enjoyed both in France and around the world.
The climate of Centre-Val de Loire is ideal for growing a variety of grapes and other crops.

Etymology

French, meaning 'Center - Valley of the Loire,' referring to its location and the Loire River.
