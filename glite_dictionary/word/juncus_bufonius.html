Juncus bufonius

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋkəs bjuːˈfoʊniəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkəs bjuːˈfəʊniəs / (BrE)
A low-growing annual rush found in damp, low-lying ground, nearly cosmopolitan in distribution.
a species of annual rush

Examples

Juncus bufonius thrives in wet ditches and muddy meadows during the summer.
You can identify Juncus bufonius by its slender stems and tiny flowers.
The field guide lists Juncus bufonius as common in marshy environments across several continents.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Juncus' meaning rush and 'bufonius' meaning 'of toads', possibly referring to its wet habitats.
