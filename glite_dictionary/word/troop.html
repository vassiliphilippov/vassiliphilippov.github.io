troop

1. NOUN

/ truːp / (AmE), / truːp / (BrE)
a group of people or animals gathered together in an orderly way
orderly crowd or group

Examples

A troop of children marched into the assembly hall singing a song.
We watched a troop of monkeys cross the road in the sanctuary.
The school trip moved as a troop, following the guide through the museum.

Synset Examples

a troop of children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowd - a large number of people gathered together
  • Antonym: mob - a large, disorderly crowd

Etymology

From Old French 'troupe' (company, band, troop), from Medieval Latin 'turba' (crowd).
