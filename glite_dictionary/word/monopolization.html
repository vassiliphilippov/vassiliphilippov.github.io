monopolization

1. NOUN

/ məˌnɑːpəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / məˌnɒpələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of gaining exclusive control over a market or commodity, excluding others from participation.
exclusive control of a market

Examples

The company's monopolization of the local market led to higher prices for consumers.
Government regulators stepped in to prevent further monopolization by telecommunications giants.
Critics argue that monopolization stifles innovation and reduces consumer choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monopoly - exclusive possession or control of the supply of or trade in a commodity or service

Etymology

From 'monopolize' + '-ation', based on Greek 'monos' (alone) + 'polein' (to sell).
