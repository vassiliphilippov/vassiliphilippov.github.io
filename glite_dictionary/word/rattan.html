rattan

1. NOUN

/ rəˈtæn / (AmE), / rəˈtan / (BrE)
the slender, flexible stems of climbing palms of the genus Calamus and related genera, used for making furniture, baskets, and canes
material from climbing palms for furniture and weaving

Examples

The chair was made from rattan and looked elegant in the sunroom.
Many baskets in the market were woven using strong rattan stems.
They bought a beautiful rattan sofa for their garden terrace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wicker - pliable twigs, typically of willow, used for weaving baskets and furniture

Etymology

From Malay 'rotan' (rattan palm).

2. NOUN

/ rəˈtæn / (AmE), / rəˈtæn / (BrE)
A thin stick or switch made from the flexible stems of the rattan palm, traditionally used for caning or whipping.
switch made from rattan palm stem

Examples

The teacher threatened to use the rattan if the class misbehaved.
A long rattan hung on the wall as a warning to the students.
He remembered being struck with a rattan in his childhood school.

Etymology

From Malay 'rotan', referring to climbing palms of the genus Calamus and related genera.

3. NOUN

/ rəˈtæn / (AmE), / rəˈtan / (BrE)
a type of climbing palm with long, slender stems, native to tropical regions of Asia, used for making furniture, baskets, and walking sticks
climbing palm with long stems used for furniture, baskets

Examples

The craftsman skillfully wove the rattan into an elegant chair.
Baskets made from rattan are both lightweight and durable.
After the storm, he repaired the patio furniture with fresh rattan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cane - the stem of certain plants, used for weaving or furniture

Etymology

From Malay *rotan*, referring to any of several species of climbing palms.
