fenestra

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɛstrə / (AmE), / fəˈnɛstrə / (BrE)
A small natural opening or window, especially one covered by a membrane, as found in bone, notably between the middle and inner ear.
small membrane-covered opening (esp. in bone)
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the fenestra to assess the condition of the inner ear.
A blockage in the fenestra can lead to hearing problems.
In anatomy classes, students learn about the fenestra ovalis and its role in hearing.

Etymology

From Latin 'fenestra' meaning 'window'.
