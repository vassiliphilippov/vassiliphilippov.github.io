holdout

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊldˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldˌaʊt / (BrE)
a person who refuses to reach an agreement, especially in negotiations, hoping to gain better terms or concessions
person refusing agreement in negotiation

Examples

The union's chief holdout finally agreed to the proposal after several days of negotiations.
She became known as the last holdout against the company's takeover plan.
After months of talks, the most prominent holdout eventually signed the contract.

Synset Examples

their star pitcher was a holdout for six weeks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstructionist - a person who deliberately delays or prevents progress, especially in negotiations

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊldˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldˌaʊt / (BrE)
the act of secretly keeping playing cards aside during a gambling game to use them later for personal advantage
hiding cards for later use in gambling

Examples

The casino hired professionals to spot anyone attempting a holdout during the poker tournament.
He was caught performing a holdout, slipping an extra ace up his sleeve for a later hand.
Strict rules and security measures help prevent the use of holdout techniques among gamblers.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊldˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈhəʊld.aʊt / (BrE)
a person or group who refuses to agree to a proposal, especially during negotiations, often to obtain a better offer
person refusing agreement in negotiations

Examples

She was the last holdout in the contract negotiations, refusing to sign until her demands were met.
The city's teachers became holdouts after months of talks with the school board failed to produce an agreement.
Despite generous offers, several holdouts declined to sell their property to the developers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstructionist - someone who deliberately delays or prevents progress
  • Antonym: compromiser - someone who is willing to make concessions
