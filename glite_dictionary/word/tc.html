Tc

1. NOUN

/ tiː siː / (AmE), / tiː siː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for technetium, a silvery-gray, radioactive metallic element that is not found naturally and is produced artificially, typically as a fission product of uranium
chemical symbol for technetium (element)

Examples

The element Tc has no stable isotopes and is usually produced in nuclear reactors.
Scientists use Tc in medical imaging because of its radioactive properties.
The decay of uranium can result in the formation of Tc among other elements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: technetium - a radioactive metallic chemical element (symbol Tc, atomic number 43)

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'technetium', from Greek 'technetos' meaning 'artificial'.
