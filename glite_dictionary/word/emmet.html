emmet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛmɪt / (BrE)
An archaic word for an ant, a social insect living in organized colonies.
archaic word for ant (insect)
archaic

Examples

The children watched an emmet carry a crumb across the garden path.
In the old tales, the emmet is revered for its industry and teamwork.
She brushed away an emmet that had wandered onto the picnic blanket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ant - a small social insect in organized colonies

Etymology

From Old English 'æmete', related to German 'Ameise' (ant).
