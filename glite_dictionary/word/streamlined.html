streamlined

1. ADJ

/ ˈstriːmˌlaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈstriːmlʌɪnd / (BrE)
having a shape or design that allows smooth movement through air or water by reducing resistance
shaped to reduce resistance to flow

Examples

The new car features a streamlined body to improve its speed and fuel efficiency.
Dolphins have streamlined shapes that help them glide easily through water.
Engineers designed the train to be as streamlined as possible for high-speed travel.

Synset Examples

a streamlined convertible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerodynamic - designed to reduce air resistance

Etymology

From 'streamline' (to shape for smooth flow), formed from 'stream' + 'line'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈstriːmˌlaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈstriːmˌlaɪnd / (BrE)
designed or organized to be more efficient by removing unnecessary elements or details
made more efficient by simplification

Examples

The company introduced a streamlined hiring process to reduce delays and paperwork.
We prefer streamlined meetings that focus only on key points.
After revisions, the system became much more streamlined and user-friendly.

Synset Examples

short streamlined meetings
a streamlined hiring process

Related Terms

  • Synonym: efficient - working in a well-organized and productive way without wasting time or resources
  • Antonym: complicated - consisting of many interconnecting parts; complex or intricate

Etymology

From 'streamline' (verb), originally referring to shapes that reduce resistance to motion through air or water.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00844276-s
Synset POS: s
