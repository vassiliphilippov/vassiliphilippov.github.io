Francis Joseph

1. PROPN

/ ˈfræn.sɪs ˈdʒoʊ.zəf / (AmE), / ˈfræn.sɪs ˈdʒəʊ.zɪf / (BrE)
(1830–1916) Emperor of Austria and King of Hungary, notable for his long reign and for his defeat by Napoleon III at the battle of Magenta.
Emperor of Austria and King of Hungary (1830–1916)

Examples

Francis Joseph reigned over the Austro-Hungarian Empire for nearly 68 years.
The defeat of Francis Joseph at the battle of Magenta marked a turning point in Italian unification.
Historians debate the leadership style of Francis Joseph during the late 19th century.
