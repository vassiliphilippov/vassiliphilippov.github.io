to revitalise

1. VERB

/ riˈvaɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / riˈvaɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to give new life, energy, or strength to something; to make something active or successful again
give new life or energy to
british

Examples

The city council hopes to revitalise the downtown area with new shops and parks.
A new marketing campaign managed to revitalise interest in the product.
Many people believe that art can revitalise a struggling community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revitalize - to give new life or vigor to (American spelling)
  • Synonym: to invigorate - to give strength or energy to
  • Antonym: to weaken - to make or become weaker

Etymology

From re- (again) + vital + -ise, modelled on the earlier 'revitalize' (American spelling).
