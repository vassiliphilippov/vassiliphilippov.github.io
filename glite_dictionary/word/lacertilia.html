Lacertilia

1. NOUN

/ ləˌsɝːˈtɪliə / (AmE), / ləˌsɜːˈtɪlɪə / (BrE)
A suborder of reptiles comprising the true lizards, including chameleons, geckos, and related forms.
suborder of true lizards (reptiles)

Examples

The order Squamata includes both snakes and members of the Lacertilia.
Among the Lacertilia, chameleons and geckos are especially known for their unique adaptations.
Fossils of Lacertilia have been found in sedimentary rocks dating back millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard - Any of various reptiles of the order Squamata, suborder Lacertilia

Etymology

From Latin 'lacerta' (lizard) + '-ilia', a zoological suffix.
