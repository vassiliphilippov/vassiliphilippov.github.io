to effloresce

1. VERB

/ ˌɛfləˈrɛs / (AmE), / ˌɛfləˈrɛs / (BrE)
to come into flower; to blossom or develop in a way suggestive of flowering
to blossom or come into flower

Examples

After a long winter, the cherry trees finally effloresced in a burst of pink and white.
Her talents truly effloresced once she found a supportive environment.
The city's cultural life effloresces during the annual arts festival.

Synset Examples

These manifestations effloresced in the past

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blossom - to produce flowers or come into bloom
  • Antonym: to wither - to decline or shrivel after flowering

Etymology

From Latin efflorescere, from ex- 'out' + florescere 'begin to bloom'.

2. VERB

/ ˌɛfləˈrɛs / (AmE), / ˌɛfləˈrɛs / (BrE)
(of a substance) to become covered with a crust of crystals, typically as a result of evaporation.
to form a crust of crystals due to evaporation

Examples

Salt deposits effloresce on the walls of the old cellar during the dry season.
If the water evaporates quickly, minerals may effloresce on the surface of the stone.
Over time, the bricks began to effloresce with a white, powdery crust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crystallize - to form crystals or become crystal-like

Etymology

From Latin efflorescere, meaning 'to bloom out'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00256158-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌɛf.ləˈrɛs / (AmE), / ˌɛf.ləˈrɛs / (BrE)
(of a substance) to form a powdery surface or to become crystalline by losing water of hydration; to crystallize on exposure to air.
form crystals or become powdery

Examples

When exposed to air, the salt will quickly effloresce and form a white powder.
Certain minerals effloresce on rock surfaces, producing distinctive crystal patterns.
If the temperature is low enough, the compound may effloresce during storage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crystallize - to form crystals or assume a crystalline form

Etymology

From Latin 'efflorescere' (to blossom, to bloom), from 'ex-' (out) + 'florescere' (to begin to bloom).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
