Opheodrys vernalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊfiˈɑːdrɪs vərˈnælɪs / (AmE), / ˌəʊfiˈəʊdrɪs vəˈnɑːlɪs / (BrE)
A small, slender, nonvenomous green snake found in western and central regions of the United States, also known as the smooth green snake.
species of small green snake

Examples

The Opheodrys vernalis is commonly found in moist meadows and grassy areas.
Herpetologists conducted a survey on the population of Opheodrys vernalis in the region.
Unlike some other snakes, the Opheodrys vernalis is harmless to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smooth green snake - Common name for Opheodrys vernalis, a small green snake of North America

Etymology

From modern Latin: genus 'Opheodrys' (from Greek 'ophis' meaning 'snake' and 'drys' meaning 'tree') and 'vernalis' (Latin for 'of the spring').
