hoopoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈhuˌpoʊ, ˈhuˌpu / (AmE), / ˈhuːpuː / (BrE)
a distinctive Old World bird with a long, slender, downward-curved bill and a prominent fan-shaped crest
crested Old World bird with curved bill

Examples

The hoopoe is easily recognized by its striking crest and unique call.
Birdwatchers gathered early in the morning hoping to spot a rare hoopoe.
A hoopoe fed on insects in the garden, its curved beak probing the soil.

Etymology

From Latin upupa, borrowed from Ancient Greek ἔποψ (epops), imitative of its call.
