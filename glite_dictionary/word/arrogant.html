arrogant

1. ADJ

/ ˈærəɡənt / (AmE), / ˈærəɡənt / (BrE)
having or showing an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities, often in a way that is overbearing or disrespectful
having an exaggerated sense of self-importance

Examples

The manager's arrogant attitude made it difficult for anyone to work with him.
Though he was talented, his arrogant remarks alienated many of his friends.
She dismissed their suggestions with an arrogant wave of her hand.

Synset Examples

an arrogant official
arrogant claims
chesty as a peacock

Related Terms

  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance
  • Degree Lower: proud - having or showing pride; feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction in one's achievements
  • Synonym: conceited - excessively proud of oneself; vain

Etymology

From Latin arrogans, arrogant-, present participle of arrogare (to claim for oneself).
