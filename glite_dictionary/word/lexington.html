Lexington

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɛk.sɪŋ.tən / (AmE), / ˈlɛk.sɪŋ.tən / (BrE)
A town in eastern Massachusetts, near Boston, famous as the site where the first battle of the American Revolution was fought in 1775.
town in Massachusetts; site of first American Revolution battle

Examples

Many tourists visit Lexington each year to learn about its role in the American Revolution.
The Battle of Lexington marked a turning point in American history.
Our history class is planning a field trip to Lexington to see the historic battlefield.

Etymology

Named after the English village of Laxton (formerly also called Lexington), Nottinghamshire, England.
