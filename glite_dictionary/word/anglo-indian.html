Anglo-Indian

1. ADJ

/ ˌæŋɡloʊˈɪndiən / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡləʊˈɪndiən / (BrE)
relating to British India or to English people living in India, especially during the period of British rule.
relating to British India or its English population

Examples

The Anglo-Indian community played a unique role during the British Raj.
He specializes in Anglo-Indian architecture of the nineteenth century.
Her research focuses on Anglo-Indian literature from colonial times.

Etymology

From 'Anglo-' (relating to English) and 'Indian', used from the late 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæŋɡloʊˈɪndiən / (AmE), / ˌaŋɡləʊˈɪndɪən / (BrE)
a person of British descent born or living in India, especially during the period of British rule, or a person of mixed British and Indian ancestry.
person of British and Indian descent or British born in India

Examples

Many Anglo-Indians played a prominent role in colonial administration during the British Raj.
Her grandmother was an Anglo-Indian who spoke both English and Hindi fluently.
The novel explores the identity struggles of a young Anglo-Indian growing up in Calcutta.

Etymology

From Anglo- (relating to English) and Indian, reflecting a British presence in India during colonial times.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
