calcium-cyanamide

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.si.əm saɪˈæn.əˌmaɪd / (AmE), / ˌkæl.si.əm saɪˈæn.ɪˌmaɪd / (BrE)
a greyish-white chemical compound used as a fertilizer and as a source of nitrogen in agriculture and industry
chemical compound used as fertilizer

Examples

Farmers often use calcium-cyanamide to enrich the nitrogen content of their soil.
The production of calcium-cyanamide involves the reaction of calcium carbide with nitrogen.
Exposure to calcium-cyanamide should be handled carefully, as it can be hazardous in large quantities.

Etymology

From calcium (element) and cyanamide (from cyan- + amide).
