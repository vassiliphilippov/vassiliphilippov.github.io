caracul

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærəkʊl / (AmE), / ˈkærəkʊl / (BrE)
a hardy breed of coarse-haired sheep native to central Asia, with lambs prized for their soft, curly black fur used in the fur industry
breed of sheep valued for fur

Examples

The caracul is renowned for the luxurious pelts of its newborn lambs.
She wore a coat made from the fine, curly fleece of a caracul lamb.
Farmers in Central Asia raise caracul sheep primarily for their valuable fur.

Etymology

From Russian каракуль (karakul’), named after the Karakul region in Uzbekistan.
