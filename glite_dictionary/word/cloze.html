cloze

1. ADJ

/ kloʊz / (AmE), / kləʊz / (BrE)
(of a test or procedure) involving the systematic removal of words from a passage to assess a person's ability to understand context and language.
involving missing-word tests for reading skill

Examples

The students took a cloze test to assess their reading comprehension.
Teachers often create cloze exercises by removing every fifth word from a passage.
A cloze passage requires students to use context clues to fill in the blanks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fill-in-the-blank - a type of question where respondents must supply missing words

Etymology

From 'closure' or 'close', shortened in educational and linguistic contexts (1960s).
