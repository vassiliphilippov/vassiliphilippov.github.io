meerestone

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪərstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɪəstəʊn / (BrE)
An old term for a landmark made of a pile of stones with an upright slab, used to mark boundaries.
old boundary marker of stones and slab
archaic

Examples

The old boundary was marked by a meerestone at the edge of the field.
In medieval documents, disputes over land often referenced the location of a meerestone.
A weathered meerestone stood as the last remnant of the ancient border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cairn - a pile of stones built as a memorial or landmark
  • Synonym: milestone - a stone set up beside a road to mark distances

Etymology

From Old English 'mǣre' (boundary) + stone.
