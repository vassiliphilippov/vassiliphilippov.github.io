doll

1. NOUN

/ dɑl / (AmE), / dɒl / (BrE)
(informal) A term used to refer to a young woman, often in a playful, familiar, or affectionate way.
informal term for a young woman
informal

Examples

She’s a real doll, always cheerful and helpful to everyone.
Thanks for helping me out, you’re such a doll!
Back in the 1950s, it was common to call a pretty woman a doll.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: girl - a young female person
  • Synonym: woman - an adult female human

2. NOUN

/ dɑl / (AmE), / dɒl / (BrE)
A small model of a human figure, often used as a child's toy.
small model of a person, toy

Examples

She gave her daughter a beautiful doll for her birthday.
The little boy hugged his favorite doll tightly at bedtime.
Their collection includes a rare porcelain doll from the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: action figure - a small posable character toy, usually based on a fictional character

Etymology

From early Dutch or German 'dolle', diminutive of female names such as Dorothy or Dollie.
