impaction

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpækʃən / (AmE), / ɪmˈpækʃən / (BrE)
A medical condition in which feces become tightly lodged in the lower colon, causing blockage.
feces lodged in the colon (medical)
medical

Examples

Chronic constipation can sometimes lead to impaction if left untreated.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with fecal impaction after a week of abdominal discomfort.
Treatment for impaction may include the manual removal of stool or enemas.

Etymology

From Latin impingere (to drive in) + -tion.

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpækʃən / (AmE), / ɪmˈpækʃən / (BrE)
a condition in which a tooth is prevented from emerging normally because it is blocked by surrounding teeth or bone
tooth prevented from erupting normally
medical

Examples

The dentist explained that my wisdom tooth impaction would require surgery.
Severe pain in the jaw can be caused by impaction of a molar.
X-rays revealed the impaction that was preventing her tooth from erupting.

Etymology

From Latin 'impactio', from 'impingere' meaning 'to push against'.
