hymn

1. VERB

/ hɪm / (AmE), / hɪm / (BrE)
to praise or celebrate, especially by singing hymns
praise or celebrate by singing

Examples

The congregation hymned joyfully at the Sunday service.
Poets have hymned the beauty of the landscape for centuries.
In the opening ceremony, the crowd hymned their unity and hope.

Synset Examples

They hymned their love of God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Greek 'hymnos' (song of praise), via Latin and Old French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00863087-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ hɪm / (AmE), / hɪm / (BrE)
a religious song or poem of praise to God or a saint, or a song of praise to a nation
a song or poem of praise, especially religious

Examples

The choir sang a beautiful hymn during the Sunday service.
National holidays often begin with the singing of the country's hymn.
She composed a new hymn to celebrate the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthem - a rousing or uplifting song identified with a particular group, body, or cause
  • Synonym: chorale - a hymn tune, especially one associated with German Protestantism

Etymology

From Greek 'hymnos' (song of praise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07049616-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ hɪm / (AmE), / hɪm / (BrE)
to sing or praise with a hymn, especially in a religious context
sing or praise with a hymn

Examples

The congregation began to hymn softly as the service started.
She hymned the old tune with deep emotion.
Every Sunday, the choir hymns praises to the Lord.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chant - to sing or intone a repeated musical phrase, especially in religious settings

Etymology

From Greek 'hymnos' (song of praise), via Latin 'hymnus'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01735599-v
Synset POS: v
