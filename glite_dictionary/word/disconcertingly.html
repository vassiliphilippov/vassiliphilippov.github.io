disconcertingly

1. ADV

/ ˌdɪskənˈsɝːtɪŋli / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈsɜːtɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that causes discomfort, confusion, or embarrassment.
in a disturbing or unsettling way

Examples

The teacher's gaze was disconcertingly intense, making the students shift uncomfortably in their seats.
He smiled disconcertingly as he revealed the secret details.
The room was disconcertingly empty for such an important meeting.

Synset Examples

he drank some sherry, his eyes disconcertingly keen as he watched her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturbingly - in a way that causes worry or distress

Etymology

From disconcert + -ingly (adverbial form).
