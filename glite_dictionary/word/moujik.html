moujik

1. NOUN

/ muːˈʒiːk / (AmE), / muːˈʒiːk / (BrE)
A Russian peasant, especially before the 1917 Revolution.
Russian peasant (pre-1917)

Examples

The novel describes the hardship endured by the moujik during the harsh Russian winter.
In pre-revolutionary Russia, a moujik rarely owned the land he farmed.
The museum displays clothing once worn by a typical moujik in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muzhik - alternative transliteration for Russian peasant

Etymology

From Russian мужик (muzhik), meaning 'peasant' or 'man'.
