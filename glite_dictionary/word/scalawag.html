scalawag

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæləˌwæɡ / (AmE), / ˈskæləˌwæɡ / (BrE)
(historically, US) A white Southerner who supported Reconstruction policies after the American Civil War, often perceived as acting out of self-interest.
white Southerner supporting Reconstruction post–Civil War

Examples

Some considered him a scalawag for backing the Northern Republicans during Reconstruction.
Many scalawags were accused of exploiting new political opportunities after the war.
Historians now debate whether the actions of scalawags were more pragmatic than opportunistic.

Etymology

Originally American English, possibly an alteration of 'scallowag', of unknown origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskæləˌwæɡ / (AmE), / ˈskæləˌwæɡ / (BrE)
an unscrupulous or dishonest person; a rogue or scoundrel
dishonest or unscrupulous person

Examples

The mayor called him a scalawag after the corruption scandal came to light.
You can’t trust a known scalawag with your valuables.
In old Western movies, there was always one scalawag stirring up trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Synonym: rascal - a mischievous or dishonest person
  • Antonym: honest person - a person who is truthful and trustworthy

Etymology

Perhaps from a Scots word for a worthless animal; also associated with a pejorative term used in post-Civil War southern US.

3. NOUN

/ ˈskæləˌwæɡ / (AmE), / ˈskæləˌwæɡ / (BrE)
a person, especially a child, who behaves in a playful and mischievous way
playfully mischievous person

Examples

The teacher smiled as the scalawag played another harmless prank on his classmates.
Her youngest brother is a real scalawag, always hiding the TV remote.
Even as an adult, Mark remained a lovable scalawag in family gatherings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rascal - a mischievous or cheeky person, especially a child
  • Synonym: scamp - a person, especially a child, who is mischievous in a likable way

Etymology

American English, of uncertain origin, possibly an alteration of 'scallywag'.
