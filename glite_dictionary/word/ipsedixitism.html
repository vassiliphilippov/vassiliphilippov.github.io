ipsedixitism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪpsiˈdɪksɪˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪpsiːˈdɪksɪtɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice of making assertions without supporting evidence; an unsupported dogmatic statement
unsupported dogmatic assertion

Examples

The philosopher's claims were often dismissed as mere ipsedixitism lacking any real justification.
Legal arguments based on ipsedixitism rather than evidence will likely fail in court.
He was criticized for relying on ipsedixitism instead of logical reasoning during the debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertion - a confident and forceful statement of fact or belief

Etymology

From Latin ipsum dixit (he himself said it), via ipsedixit (unsupported statement), plus -ism.
