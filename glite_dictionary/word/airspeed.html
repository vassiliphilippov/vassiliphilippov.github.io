airspeed

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌspiːd / (AmE), / ˈeəspiːd / (BrE)
the speed of an aircraft relative to the air through which it is moving
speed of an aircraft relative to the air

Examples

The pilot checked the airspeed before beginning the landing approach.
A sudden drop in airspeed can be dangerous during takeoff.
Modern avionics provide accurate readings of airspeed to ensure safe flight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: groundspeed - the speed of an aircraft relative to the ground

Etymology

From 'air' + 'speed'; usage in aviation since early 20th century.
