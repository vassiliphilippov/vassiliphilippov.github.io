ultrasonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌltrəˈsɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈsɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of sound waves) having frequencies above the range audible to the human ear; greater than about 20,000 hertz.
sound frequencies above human hearing

Examples

Bats use ultrasonic calls to navigate in complete darkness.
Hospitals often use ultrasonic equipment for medical imaging and diagnostics.
An ultrasonic cleaner can remove dirt from jewelry without using harsh chemicals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infrasonic - having frequencies below those of audible sound
  • Antonym: audible - able to be heard; within the range of human hearing

Etymology

ultra- (beyond) + sonic (pertaining to sound), from Latin 'sonus' (sound).
