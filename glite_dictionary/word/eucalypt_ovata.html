Eucalypt ovata

1. NOUN

/ juːˈkælɪpt oʊˈveɪtə / (AmE), / juːˈkælɪpt əʊˈvɑːtə / (BrE)
A medium-sized tree species native to southern Australia, known for its smooth bark and oval leaves.
Australian medium-sized tree species

Examples

The Eucalypt ovata thrives in the moist soils of southern Australia.
Many birds nest in Eucalypt ovata because of its dense canopy.
Scientists are studying Eucalypt ovata for its potential use in land restoration projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eucalyptus - the genus of which Eucalypt ovata is a species
