ABCs

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪˌbiːˈsiːz / (AmE), / ˌeɪˌbiːˈsiːz / (BrE)
the alphabet; a set of letters used to represent the basic sounds of a language in writing
the alphabet; letters of a language

Examples

Children usually learn their ABCs before starting school.
Singing the ABCs is a common activity in preschool classrooms.
Learning your ABCs is the first step to reading and writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alphabet - the set of letters or characters used to write a language

Etymology

From the names of the first three letters of the Latin alphabet: A, B, C.
