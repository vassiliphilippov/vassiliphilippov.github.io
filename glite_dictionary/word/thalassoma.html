Thalassoma

1. NOUN

/ θəˈlæsəˌsoʊmə / (AmE), / θəˈlasəmə / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored marine fish in the wrasse family (Labridae), found in tropical and subtropical seas.
genus of wrasse fish

Examples

The Thalassoma genus contains several popular aquarium fish known for their vibrant colors.
Researchers observed the feeding behavior of Thalassoma species on the coral reef.
Many Thalassoma wrasses play an important role in cleaning parasites from other fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrasse - a family of small, brightly colored marine fish

Etymology

From Greek thalassa (sea) + soma (body).
