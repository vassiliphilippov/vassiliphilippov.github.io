devastation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛvəˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛvəˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of causing great damage, destruction, or ruin, often as a result of violence or disaster
severe damage or destruction

Examples

The devastation caused by the hurricane left the entire town in ruins.
After years of war, the landscape was marked by unimaginable devastation.
Environmental devastation can result from unchecked industrial activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destruction - the act of destroying or the state of being destroyed
  • Synonym: ruin - the physical destruction or disintegration of something
  • Antonym: preservation - the act of keeping something in its original state or in good condition

Etymology

From Latin 'devastatio', from 'devastare' (to lay waste).

/ ˌdɛvəˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛvəˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
a feeling of extreme shock, grief, or overwhelming sadness, often after a traumatic event or great loss
overwhelming grief or emotional shock

Examples

She struggled to cope with the devastation she felt after losing her job.
There was a look of utter devastation on his face when he heard the verdict.
