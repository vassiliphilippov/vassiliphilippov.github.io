grandparent

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrænˌpɛr.ənt / (AmE), / ˈɡræn.peə.rənt / (BrE)
The father or mother of one's parent.
the parent of one's parent

Examples

My children enjoy spending weekends with their grandparents in the countryside.
She learned to bake bread from her grandparent when she was a little girl.
After retiring, he moved closer to his grandparents to help care for them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parent - the father or mother of a person
  • Antonym: grandchild - the child of one's son or daughter

Etymology

Formed from 'grand' (in the sense of a generation removed) + 'parent'.
