Hibiscus moscheutos

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈbɪskəs mɑːsˈkiuːtoʊs / (AmE), / hɪˈbɪskəs mɒsˈkjuːtɒs / (BrE)
A showy perennial shrub native to salt marshes of the eastern United States, having large rose-colored flowers; also known as swamp rose mallow.
marsh shrub with large pink flowers

Examples

The Hibiscus moscheutos blooms in late summer, adding vibrant color to wetland gardens.
Gardeners value Hibiscus moscheutos for its large, striking flowers and ability to thrive in moist soils.
Wild populations of Hibiscus moscheutos can often be found along the edges of marshes and ponds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swamp rose mallow - Common name for Hibiscus moscheutos, a marsh shrub with large pink flowers

Etymology

From Latin, Hibiscus (the genus); moscheutos, a species epithet from Greek mōscheutos (musk-scented).
