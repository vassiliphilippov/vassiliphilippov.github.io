Tropaeolum majus

1. NOUN

/ troʊˌpiːoʊˈluːm ˈmædʒəs / (AmE), / trəʊˌpiːəˈləʊləm ˈmædʒəs / (BrE)
A species of fast-growing annual climbing plant with large orange to yellowish-orange flowers, whose seeds are sometimes pickled and used like capers.
a climbing plant with orange-yellow flowers

Examples

The garden trellis was covered in bright Tropaeolum majus blooming throughout the summer.
She added pickled seeds of Tropaeolum majus to her salad as a garnish.
Botanists admire the vibrant colors of Tropaeolum majus in ornamental gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasturtium - common name for Tropaeolum majus, a garden plant with brightly colored flowers

Etymology

From Latin Tropaeolum, the genus name, and 'majus' meaning 'larger.'
