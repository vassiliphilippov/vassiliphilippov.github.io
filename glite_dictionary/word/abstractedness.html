abstractedness

1. NOUN

/ əbˈstræktɪdnəs / (AmE), / əbˈstraktɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being deeply preoccupied or absorbed in thought, often to the exclusion of surrounding events or activities
state of being preoccupied or absorbed in thought

Examples

His abstractedness during the meeting made it clear his mind was somewhere else entirely.
She was so lost in abstractedness that she didn't hear the phone ring.
The professor's abstractedness sometimes made students wonder if he even noticed their presence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preoccupation - the state of being engrossed in thought or absorbed by something
  • Synonym: absentmindedness - the quality of being inattentive due to absorption in thought

Etymology

From 'abstracted' + '-ness', where 'abstracted' comes from Latin 'abstrahere' (to draw away).
