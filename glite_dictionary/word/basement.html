basement

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsmənt / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsmənt / (BrE)
the ground floor facade or interior in Renaissance architecture, often forming a distinct horizontal division at the base of a building
ground floor in Renaissance architecture

Examples

The palace's basement was adorned with decorative stonework characteristic of Renaissance style.
Columns rise from the basement, giving the facade a sense of grandeur and stability.
Architects often emphasized the basement to visually anchor the building within its surroundings.

Etymology

From Italian *basamento* (base, pedestal), from Latin *basis* (base).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsmənt / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsmənt / (BrE)
The lowest floor of a building, typically partly or entirely below ground level, often used for storage or utility purposes.
lowest floor below ground level

Examples

They converted the basement into a spacious home theater.
Old furniture and boxes were piled up in the basement for years.
During the storm, the family took shelter in the basement.

Etymology

From Italian *basamento* (pedestal), from *base* (base).
