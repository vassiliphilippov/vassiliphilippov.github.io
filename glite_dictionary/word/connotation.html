connotation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnəˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an idea or feeling that a word invokes in addition to its primary meaning
implied or associated meaning of a word

Examples

The word 'home' has a warm connotation of comfort and security for many people.
Calling someone 'childish' carries a negative connotation compared to 'childlike'.
Be careful of the connotation your words might have in different cultures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: denotation - the literal or primary meaning of a word

Etymology

From Medieval Latin connotatio (signifying in addition), from Latin connotare (to mark in addition).
