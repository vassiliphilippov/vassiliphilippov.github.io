chloroform

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔːrəˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈklɔːrəˌfɔːm / (BrE)
a volatile, colorless liquid (CHCl₃) formerly used as an anesthetic and solvent
volatile liquid formerly used as anesthetic

Examples

The dentist used chloroform to put the patient to sleep during the procedure.
In the 19th century, chloroform was commonly used in surgeries, despite its potential risks.
Spills of chloroform must be handled carefully due to its toxic fumes.

Synset Examples

chloroform was the first inhalation anesthetic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anesthetic - a substance that induces insensitivity to pain

Etymology

From French 'chloroforme', from 'chlore' (chlorine) + 'formyle' (formyl), coined in the 19th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈklɔːrəˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈklɔːrəfɔːm / (BrE)
to make someone unconscious by administering chloroform as an anesthetic, especially in a medical or historical context
anesthetize with chloroform

Examples

The villain tried to chloroform his victim in the mystery novel.
Nurses would chloroform patients before surgery in the 19th century.
She awoke suddenly, realizing someone had attempted to chloroform her in the night.

Synset Examples

Doctors used to put people under by chloroforming them

Etymology

From French *chloroforme*, from *chlore* (chlorine) and *formyle* (formyl).
