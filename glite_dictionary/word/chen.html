Chen

1. NOUN

/ tʃɛn / (AmE), / tʃɛn / (BrE)
a genus of wild geese distinguished by white adult plumage, including species such as the snow goose.
a genus of white-plumaged wild geese

Examples

The genus Chen includes several species with striking white plumage, such as the snow goose.
Scientists have debated the classification of Chen in relation to the genus Anser.
Flocks of Chen migrate long distances between breeding and wintering grounds each year.

Etymology

From Greek *khēn* (goose).

2. NOUN

/ tʃɛn / (AmE), / tʃɛn / (BrE)
A scientific subgenus of wild geese characterized by predominantly white adult plumage.
subgenus of white-plumaged wild geese

Examples

The emperor goose is classified in the subgenus Chen due to its distinctive plumage.
Ornithologists debate the boundaries of the Chen group among North American geese.
Several wild geese in the Chen subgenus have striking white feathers as adults.

Etymology

From Greek *khen* (χήν), meaning 'goose'.
