Erythroxylaceae

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθrɑːksɪˈleɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrɒksɪˈleɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical flowering plants, chiefly known for species like coca, characterized by drupaceous fruits; belonging to the order Geraniales.
a family of tropical flowering plants

Examples

The coca plant, source of cocaine, belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family.
Botanists have identified many species in the Erythroxylaceae distributed across tropical regions.
Characteristics of the Erythroxylaceae include simple leaves and drupaceous fruits.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Erythroxylum, itself from Greek erythros (red) and xylon (wood).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪˌθrɑːksəˈleɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˌθrɒksɪˈleɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants of the order Geraniales, characterized by drupaceous fruit and including the coca plant.
family of flowering plants, includes coca

Examples

The Erythroxylaceae are best known for containing the coca plant, the source of cocaine.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of plants within the Erythroxylaceae family.
The Erythroxylaceae thrive in tropical regions around the world.

Etymology

From the genus name Erythroxylon, from Greek 'erythros' (red) and 'xylon' (wood).
