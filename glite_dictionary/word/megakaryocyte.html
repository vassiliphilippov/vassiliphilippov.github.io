megakaryocyte

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈkæri.oʊˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈkæri.əˌsaɪt / (BrE)
a large cell in the bone marrow that produces platelets, which are essential for blood clotting
large bone marrow cell producing platelets
medical

Examples

Platelets are formed in the bone marrow when a megakaryocyte breaks up into tiny fragments.
In patients with certain blood disorders, the number of megakaryocytes may be abnormally high.
The scientist observed the maturation of a megakaryocyte under the microscope.

Etymology

From Greek 'mega-' (large) + 'karyon' (nut, nucleus) + '-cyte' (cell).
