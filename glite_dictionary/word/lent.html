Lent

1. NOUN

/ lɛnt / (AmE), / lɛnt / (BrE)
In the Christian calendar, the period of forty weekdays from Ash Wednesday to Holy Saturday, observed as a season of fasting and penitence in preparation for Easter.
Christian period of fasting before Easter

Examples

Many Christians observe Lent by giving up certain foods or habits.
The decorations in the church were changed for the season of Lent.
Some people attend special services during Lent to reflect and pray.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Easter - Christian festival celebrating the resurrection of Jesus

Etymology

From Old English 'lencten' (spring), influenced by the lengthening of days in spring.
