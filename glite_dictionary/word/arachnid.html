arachnid

1. NOUN

/ əˈræknɪd / (AmE), / əˈræknɪd / (BrE)
Any of a class of air-breathing arthropods with four pairs of legs and simple eyes, including spiders, scorpions, mites, and ticks.
air-breathing arthropod with eight legs

Examples

A spider is one type of arachnid commonly found in houses.
Unlike insects, arachnids have eight legs instead of six.
Scorpions, which are arachnids, can be found in many desert environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spider - an eight-legged arachnid with a segmented body and spinnerets
  • Antonym: insect - class of small arthropods with three pairs of legs and usually wings

Etymology

From Greek árakhnē (spider).
