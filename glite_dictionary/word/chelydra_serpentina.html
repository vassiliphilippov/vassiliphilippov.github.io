Chelydra serpentina

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪdrə sərˈpɛntɪnə / (AmE), / kɛˈlɪdrə səˈpɛntɪnə / (BrE)
a large freshwater turtle of North America with a powerful hooked jaw, commonly known as the common snapping turtle
common snapping turtle species

Examples

The Chelydra serpentina is infamous for its aggressive temperament when out of water.
Researchers tracked several Chelydra serpentina to study their nesting behavior.
A fully grown Chelydra serpentina can cause serious injury with its bite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapping turtle - a common name for Chelydra serpentina, a freshwater turtle known for its strong bite

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name 'Chelydra' (from Greek 'chelȳdra', a water snake or turtle) and species name 'serpentina' referring to serpent-like appearance.
