Pulmonata

1. NOUN

/ ˌpʌl.məˈneɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌpʌl.məˈnɑː.tə / (BrE)
a large order of gastropod mollusks, including most land snails and slugs, which breathe by means of a lung-like sac
order of lung-breathing snails and slugs

Examples

Many terrestrial snails belong to the group known as Pulmonata.
Pulmonata includes both aquatic and land-dwelling species with lung-like respiratory systems.
The study focused on the evolutionary adaptations of Pulmonata to life on land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gastropod - a class of mollusks that includes snails and slugs

Etymology

From New Latin Pulmonata, from Latin 'pulmo' (lung), referencing their lung-like breathing sac.
