muzzle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌzəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌzl̩ / (BrE)
the open circular end of a gun barrel from which the bullet or projectile exits
end of a gun barrel

Examples

Always keep the muzzle of the gun pointed in a safe direction.
He checked the muzzle for any obstructions before loading the firearm.
Smoke poured from the muzzle after the rifle was fired.

Etymology

From Old French 'musel', diminutive of 'mus' meaning 'mouth'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌzəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌzl / (BrE)
the projecting part of the head of an animal, including the jaws and nose
animal's projecting jaws and nose

Examples

The dog pressed its muzzle against my hand, sniffing curiously.
The horse's muzzle was soft and velvety to the touch.
A wolf's keen sense of smell comes from its highly sensitive muzzle.

Etymology

From Old French *musel*, a diminutive of *mus* (mouth), from Latin *mussum* (snout).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02455095-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmʌzəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌzəl / (BrE)
A device, typically made of leather or wire, that is placed over an animal's nose and jaws to prevent it from biting or eating.
restraint over animal's snout

Examples

The veterinarian fitted a muzzle on the dog before the examination.
Some dog breeds are required by law to wear a muzzle in public places.
Before grooming, the owner placed a muzzle over her pet's snout for safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restraint - a device or means of limiting movement or action

Etymology

From Old French *musel* (snout, muzzle), diminutive of *mus* (mouth), from Latin *mūtum* (silent).

5. VERB

/ ˈmʌz.əl / (AmE), / ˈmʌz.əl / (BrE)
to prevent a person or group from expressing their opinions freely; to silence or restrain.
prevent from expressing opinions

Examples

The government tried to muzzle the opposition by limiting press freedom.
Some companies attempt to muzzle employees who criticize their practices.
The law was designed to muzzle activists and silence dissent.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence - to prevent from speaking or expressing oneself
  • Synonym: gag - to prevent from speaking by force; silence
  • Synonym: to suppress - forcibly put an end to speech or expression

Etymology

From Old French 'musel', diminutive of 'mus' (muzzle), from Medieval Latin 'musellum'. Figurative use dates from the late 16th century.
