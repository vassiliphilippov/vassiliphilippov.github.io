Cantharellus clavatus

1. NOUN

/ kænˌθæˈrɛl.əs kləˈveɪ.təs / (AmE), / kanˌθaˈrel.əs klaˈveɪ.təs / (BrE)
A species of edible mushroom with a brown, often compound fruiting body.
an edible brown mushroom species

Examples

The chef prepared a delicate risotto with freshly foraged Cantharellus clavatus.
Mycologists identified the specimen as Cantharellus clavatus due to its distinctive club-shaped fruiting body.
Many mushroom enthusiasts seek out Cantharellus clavatus during the autumn months.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Cantharellus' meaning 'little cup', species epithet 'clavatus' meaning 'club-shaped'.
