grapefruit

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪpˌfrut / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪpˌfruːt / (BrE)
a large, round, yellowish citrus fruit with slightly acidic, juicy flesh, typically eaten fresh or used for juice
a large, sour citrus fruit

Examples

I usually eat half a grapefruit with my breakfast.
The juice from a freshly squeezed grapefruit is tart and refreshing.
She bought several grapefruits at the market for her fruit salad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orange - a round, orange citrus fruit
  • Synonym: lemon - a yellow, sour citrus fruit
  • Synonym: pomelo - a large citrus fruit related to grapefruit

Etymology

From 'grape' + 'fruit', so named because the fruits grow in clusters resembling grapes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪpˌfrut / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪpˌfruːt / (BrE)
A large, round citrus fruit with a thick yellow rind and juicy, slightly tart pulp; also, the tree that bears this fruit.
large citrus fruit; the tree it grows on

Examples

She started her morning with a fresh grapefruit and a cup of tea.
The grapefruit tree in our backyard produced hundreds of fruits this year.
Sliced grapefruit makes a refreshing addition to any fruit salad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orange - a round, sweet citrus fruit with a bright orange rind
  • Synonym: lemon - a yellow, oval-shaped, sour citrus fruit

Etymology

Named for the way its fruits grow in clusters, like grapes, plus 'fruit'.
