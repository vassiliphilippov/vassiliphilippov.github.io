Gardenia jasminoides

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑːrˈdiːniə ˌjæsmɪˈnɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ɡɑːˈdiːnɪə ˌjasmɪˈnɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub widely cultivated for its large, fragrant, white flowers and glossy leaves, commonly known as the common gardenia
a species of evergreen shrub with fragrant white flowers

Examples

The delicate scent of the Gardenia jasminoides filled the conservatory.
Many gardeners prize Gardenia jasminoides for its beautiful, waxy white blossoms.
She planted a Gardenia jasminoides near the entrance to enjoy its fragrance in summer.

Etymology

From Latinized forms of 'Gardenia', named after Scottish botanist Alexander Garden, and 'jasminoides', meaning 'resembling jasmine'.
