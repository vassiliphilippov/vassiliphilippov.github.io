embracing

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbreɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪmˈbreɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of holding someone closely in one's arms, especially as a sign of affection or greeting
the act of hugging or holding closely

Examples

Their embracing in the airport brought tears to everyone's eyes.
A warm embracing can comfort someone who is feeling lonely.
Public embracing is common in many cultures as a greeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hugging - the act of holding someone tightly in one's arms, especially as a sign of affection
  • Degree Higher: kissing - the act of touching with the lips as a sign of affection
  • Degree Lower: shaking hands - the act of grasping someone's right hand with one's own as a greeting or sign of agreement

Etymology

From Old French *embracier*, from Latin *bracchium* (arm).
