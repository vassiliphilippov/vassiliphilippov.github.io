bionomics

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪəˈnɑːmɪks / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪəˈnɒmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of biology concerned with the interactions between organisms and their environment; ecology
study of organisms and environment

Examples

She pursued a doctorate in bionomics to better understand how ecosystems function.
Recent advances in bionomics have shed light on the effects of climate change on wildlife.
The study of bionomics is essential for effective conservation strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecology - the scientific study of the relationships between living organisms and their environments

Etymology

From Greek 'bios' (life) and 'nomos' (law).
