nine-fold

1. ADJ

/ ˈnaɪnˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈnaɪn.fəʊld / (BrE)
Consisting of nine parts, elements, or units; multiplied by nine.
having nine parts or units

Examples

The report showed a nine-fold increase in internet users over the past decade.
Researchers found that the new policy led to a nine-fold reduction in errors.
The company reported a nine-fold growth in their overseas sales.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tenfold - having ten units or components
  • Degree Lower: eightfold - having eight units or components

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'nine' (the number) and the suffix '-fold' (times, multiplied).
