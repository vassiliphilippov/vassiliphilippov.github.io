Macleaya

1. NOUN

/ məˈkleɪə / (AmE), / məˈkliːə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs native to eastern Asia, commonly known as plume poppy, grown for their ornamental feathery flower plumes.
genus of perennial Asian herbs (plume poppy)

Examples

The Macleaya grows tall and produces decorative plumes in summer.
Gardeners often plant Macleaya to add height and texture to their borders.
Despite its beauty, Macleaya can spread aggressively if not contained.

Etymology

Named after Alexander Macleay (1767–1848), British entomologist and collector.
