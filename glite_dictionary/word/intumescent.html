intumescent

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.tuˈmɛs.ənt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tjuːˈmɛs.ənt / (BrE)
abnormally swollen, especially as a result of the accumulation of fluids or gas in tissues; becoming swollen or puffed up.
swollen or puffed up

Examples

After the allergic reaction, his lips became intumescent and painful.
The pathologist noted intumescent tissue around the infected wound.
In some fireproofing materials, an intumescent coating expands when exposed to heat.

Synset Examples

hungry children with bloated stomachs
he had a grossly distended stomach
eyes with puffed (or puffy) lids
swollen hands
tumescent tissue
puffy tumid flesh

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swollen - enlarged or puffed up as by internal pressure or fluid
  • Synonym: tumid - swollen or distended, often abnormally so
  • Antonym: normal - not swollen or distended

Etymology

From Latin intumescere (to swell up), from in- (in) + tumescere (to begin to swell).
