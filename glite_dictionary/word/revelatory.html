revelatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛvəˌleɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈrɛv.əl.ə.tər.i / (BrE)
making something known or clear that was not previously known; revealing.
making something known; revealing

Examples

Her autobiography was deeply revelatory, shedding light on her troubled childhood.
The documents had a revelatory effect on the public's understanding of events.
His expression was revelatory of the shock he felt at the news.

Synset Examples

actions indicative of fear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revealing - making known what was previously secret or unknown

Etymology

From Late Latin revelatorius, from Latin revelare (to reveal).
