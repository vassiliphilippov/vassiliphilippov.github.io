phonologic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfoʊ.nəˈlɑ.dʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌfəʊ.nəˈlɒ.dʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to phonology; concerning the sound system of a language or the study of speech sounds and their patterns
relating to phonology

Examples

The scientist studied the phonologic processes involved in children's language acquisition.
Her research focuses on phonologic disorders in early childhood.
The therapist designed exercises to improve the child's phonologic awareness.

Synset Examples

the phonological component of language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonological - relating to phonology; alternative and more common adjectival form

Etymology

From phonology + -ic, modeled on the form of similar adjectives like 'biologic'.
