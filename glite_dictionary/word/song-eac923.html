song

1. NOUN

/ sɑŋ / (AmE), / sɒŋ / (BrE)
the act of singing, especially collectively or as an expression of feeling
the act of singing

Examples

The villagers greeted the sunrise with laughter and song in the main square.
They celebrated their victory in song and dance until midnight.
With song and joy, the parade wound its way through the streets.

Synset Examples

with a shout and a song they marched up to the gates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: singing - the activity of producing musical tones with the voice

Etymology

From Old English 'sang' (song, singing), related to 'singan' (to sing).

3. NOUN

/ sɔŋ / (AmE), / sɒŋ / (BrE)
a distinctive or characteristic sound, especially as produced by something in motion or by a natural force; often used metaphorically
distinctive or characteristic sound (metaphorical)

Examples

The song of machinery filled the factory as the day began.
He listened to the song of the rain against the windowpane.
The forest echoed with the song of the wind through the trees.

Synset Examples

the song of bullets was in the air
the song of the wind
the wheels sang their song as the train rocketed ahead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melody - a sequence of musical notes that are perceived as a single entity

Etymology

Old English *sang* (a singing, song); related to German *Gesang*.

4. NOUN

/ sɔŋ / (AmE), / sɒŋ / (BrE)
a short piece of music with words that are sung
short musical composition with words

Examples

She played her favorite song over and over again throughout the summer.
The band wrote a new song for their upcoming album.
Everyone joined in to sing a traditional folk song around the campfire.

Synset Examples

a successful musical must have at least three good songs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tune - a melody or musical composition
  • Degree Lower: chant - a repeated rhythmic phrase, typically sung
  • Degree Higher: anthem - a rousing or uplifting song identified with a group or cause

Etymology

Examples

Related Terms
