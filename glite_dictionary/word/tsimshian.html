Tsimshian

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪmˈʃiːən / (AmE), / tʃɪmˈʃiːən / (BrE)
a member of an Indigenous people originally inhabiting river and coastal areas of British Columbia and southern Alaska.
member of an Indigenous people of British Columbia

Examples

The Tsimshian maintain rich traditions of art and storytelling.
A delegation of Tsimshian elders attended the cultural festival.
She is proud of her Tsimshian heritage and identity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tlingit - member of a neighboring Indigenous people of Alaska and western Canada
  • Synonym: Haida - member of an Indigenous people of the Northwest Coast region

Etymology

From the Tsimshian language, meaning 'inside the Skeena River.'

2. NOUN

/ tsɪmˈʃiːən / (AmE), / tsɪmˈʃiːən / (BrE)
the language of the Tsimshian people, belonging to the Penutian language family and spoken in parts of British Columbia and Alaska
Penutian language of the Tsimshian people

Examples

He is studying the Tsimshian language for his thesis on indigenous cultures.
Many traditional stories are still told in Tsimshian in local communities.
The preservation of Tsimshian is important for maintaining cultural identity.

Etymology

From the name of the Tsimshian people of British Columbia and Alaska.
