superjacent

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuːpərˈdʒeɪsənt / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈdʒeɪs(ə)nt / (BrE)
lying immediately above or on something else, especially in a vertical arrangement
lying immediately above something

Examples

The layer of clay was superjacent to the bedrock beneath.
The archaeologists discovered a set of artifacts in the superjacent soil layers.
In the stratified lake, cooler water is typically superjacent to warmer, denser layers.

Etymology

From Latin superjacentem, meaning 'lying above,' from super (above) + jacere (to lie).
