pro tem

1. ADV

/ proʊ ˈtɛm / (AmE), / prəʊ ˈtɛm / (BrE)
for the time being; temporarily
temporarily; for the time being

Examples

She agreed to serve as chairperson pro tem until a permanent replacement was found.
The committee appointed an acting manager pro tem during the hiring process.
These rules will be in effect pro tem until the new policy is approved.

Synset Examples

accepting pro tem that hypothesis consistent with the facts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporarily - for only a limited period of time; not permanently
  • Antonym: permanently - in a way that lasts or remains forever

Etymology

From Latin 'pro tempore' (for the time being).

2. ADV

/ proʊ tɛm / (AmE), / prəʊ tɛm / (BrE)
for the time being; temporarily, especially in an official position until a permanent appointment is made
for the time being; temporary
legal

Examples

After the sudden resignation, she agreed to serve as pro tem chairperson of the committee.
He was appointed as manager pro tem until a permanent one could be found.
During the absence of the regular teacher, a substitute acted pro tem.

Synset Examples

he is the president pro tem
designated him to act as consul protempore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time
  • Antonym: permanent - intended to last or remain unchanged indefinitely

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin pro tempore (for the time being).
