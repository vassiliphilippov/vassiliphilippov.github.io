excess

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsɛs, ˈɛkˌsɛs / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛs, ˈɛk.ses / (BrE)
an amount of something that is more than necessary, permitted, or desirable; the state of exceeding what is usual or required
state of being more than needed or allowed

Examples

Eating in excess can lead to health problems over time.
There was an excess of enthusiasm in the crowd at the concert.
The company had to discard the excess inventory at the end of the season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surplus - an amount that is more than needed
  • Antonym: deficiency - a lack or shortage of something

Etymology

From Latin excessus (a going beyond), from excedere (to go beyond).
