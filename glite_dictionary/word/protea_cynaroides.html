Protea cynaroides

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtiːə saɪnəˈrɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / prəʊˈtiːə sɪnəˈrɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A South African shrub with large, cup-shaped flowers that resemble artichokes, commonly known as the king protea.
South African shrub with large artichoke-like flowers

Examples

The Protea cynaroides is renowned for its striking, artichoke-like blooms.
Gardeners prize the Protea cynaroides for its bold appearance and drought resistance.
The national flower of South Africa is the Protea cynaroides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king protea - Common name for Protea cynaroides

Etymology

From Latin; 'Protea' after the Greek god Proteus, and 'cynaroides' meaning 'like a cynara' (artichoke), due to the resemblance.
