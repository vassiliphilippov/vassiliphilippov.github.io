Confederate States of America

1. PROPN

/ kənˈfɛdərət ˌsteɪts əv əˈmɛrɪkə / (AmE), / kənˈfɛdərət ˌsteɪts əv əˈmɛrɪkə / (BrE)
the group of eleven southern US states that seceded from the Union in 1861, forming their own government during the American Civil War
the southern US states that seceded in 1861

Examples

The Confederate States of America was formed in 1861 by southern states seeking independence from the Union.
Historians continue to study the causes and legacy of the Confederate States of America.
Richmond served as the capital of the Confederate States of America during the Civil War.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Union - the northern states during the American Civil War, opposed to the Confederacy

Etymology

From confederate (allied) + states, naming the seceding US states' government, first used in 1861.
