Dietrich Bonhoeffer

1. PROPN

/ ˈdiːtrɪx bɔnˌhœfər / (AmE), / ˈdiːtrɪç ˈbɒnhɜːfə / (BrE)
(1906–1945) A German Lutheran theologian and pastor known for his influential writings on Christianity and his staunch opposition to Nazism, for which he was executed.
German theologian and anti-Nazi dissident

Examples

Many scholars continue to study Dietrich Bonhoeffer's writings on ethics and faith.
The legacy of Dietrich Bonhoeffer inspires individuals who stand up against oppression today.
In his sermons, Dietrich Bonhoeffer emphasized the importance of responsible Christian living.
