Euphorbia antisyphilitica

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔːrbiə ˌæntaɪˌsɪfəˈlɪtɪkə / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪə ˌæntaɪˌsɪfɪˈlɪtɪkə / (BrE)
A wax-coated shrub native to northern Mexico and the southwestern United States, known for yielding candelilla wax.
shrub producing candelilla wax

Examples

The wax extracted from Euphorbia antisyphilitica is commonly used in the production of cosmetics and candles.
Researchers studied Euphorbia antisyphilitica to find sustainable sources of natural wax.
Euphorbia antisyphilitica grows abundantly in the arid regions of Texas and northern Mexico.

Etymology

Genus name from Euphorbus, Greek physician; species name from its historic use against syphilis.
