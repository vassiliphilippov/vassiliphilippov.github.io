diode

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪ.oʊd / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.əʊd / (BrE)
An electronic component made from a semiconductor, consisting of a p-n junction, that allows current to flow in one direction only.
electronic device allowing one-way current

Examples

A light-emitting diode is commonly known as an LED.
The circuit failed because one diode was installed backwards.
Silicon diodes are used to protect sensitive electronic equipment from voltage spikes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rectifier - An electrical device that converts alternating current to direct current, often using diodes

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) and 'hodos' (way, path); referring to two-terminal device.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪˌoʊd / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəʊd / (BrE)
an electronic device consisting of two electrodes, typically allowing current to flow in only one direction, often used as a rectifier
electronic device with two electrodes

Examples

The technician replaced the faulty diode in the power supply circuit.
A diode allows current to pass in only one direction, preventing backflow.
Early radios used a vacuum tube diode to detect signals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rectifier - device that converts alternating current to direct current

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) and 'hodos' (way, path), indicating two electrodes.
