full-blooded

1. ADJ

/ ˌfʊlˈblʌdɪd / (AmE), / ˌfʊlˈblʌdɪd / (BrE)
having ancestors all of the same race, breed, or origin; of unmixed ancestry.
of unmixed ancestry

Examples

She is a full-blooded member of the tribe, with both parents being indigenous.
The farm only breeds full-blooded Arabian horses for racing.
He claimed to be a full-blooded Irishman, proud of his family's heritage.

Synset Examples

full-blooded Native American
blooded Jersies

Related Terms

  • Antonym: half-blooded - having one parent of a different ancestry or breed

2. ADJ

/ ˌfʊlˈblʌdɪd / (AmE), / ˌfʊlˈblʌdɪd / (BrE)
Endowed with or exhibiting strong bodily or mental health; vigorous and robust.
vigorous and robust in health or spirit

Examples

Her full-blooded laugh filled the entire room and made everyone smile.
Despite his age, he remains a full-blooded participant in all outdoor activities.
The novel's full-blooded characters leap off the page with energy and vitality.

Synset Examples

a hearty glow of health

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hearty - exhibiting health and enthusiasm
  • Synonym: robust - strong and healthy; vigorous

Etymology

From 'full', meaning complete, and 'blooded', in the sense of possessing a particular quality of blood (vigor, vitality), by analogy with animal breeding.
