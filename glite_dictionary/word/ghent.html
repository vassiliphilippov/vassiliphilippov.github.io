Ghent

1. PROPN

/ ɡɛnt / (AmE), / ɡɛnt / (BrE)
a port city in northwestern Belgium, known for its historical architecture and former prominence in the cloth industry.
city in northwestern Belgium

Examples

We visited Ghent last summer and admired its medieval buildings along the canals.
The port of Ghent is one of the busiest in Belgium.
Ghent played a central role in the European cloth trade during the Middle Ages.

Etymology

From Dutch 'Gent', from Latin 'Ganda', possibly of Celtic origin.
