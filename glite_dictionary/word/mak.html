MAK

1. NOUN

/ ɛm eɪ keɪ / (AmE), / ɛm eɪ keɪ / (BrE)
a terrorist organization founded by Osama bin Laden in the 1980s to provide funding and recruit fighters for the Afghan conflict; its split led to the creation of al-Qaeda.
terrorist group linked to al-Qaeda origins

Examples

The MAK played a crucial role in recruiting foreign fighters for the Afghan conflict during the 1980s.
After splitting from MAK, Osama bin Laden went on to establish al-Qaeda.
Reports indicate that MAK was instrumental in raising funds for mujahideen groups in Afghanistan.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: al-Qaeda - militant Sunni Islamist multinational organization founded by Osama bin Laden

Etymology

Acronym for Arabic 'Maktab al-Khidamat' meaning 'Services Bureau'.
