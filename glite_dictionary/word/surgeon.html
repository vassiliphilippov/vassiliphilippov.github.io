surgeon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.dʒən / (BrE)
a medical doctor who is qualified to perform surgical operations
medical doctor specializing in surgery
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained the procedure to the patient before the operation.
After years of training, she finally became a pediatric surgeon.
The surgeon skillfully removed the tumor during the lengthy operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physician - a person qualified to practice medicine
  • Antonym: patient - a person receiving medical treatment

Etymology

From Latin 'chirurgus', from Greek 'kheirourgos' (working by hand).
