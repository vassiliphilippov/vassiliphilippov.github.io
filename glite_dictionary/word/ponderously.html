ponderously

1. ADV

/ ˈpɑːndɚəsli / (AmE), / ˈpɒn.dər.əs.li / (BrE)
In a slow, heavy, and often tedious or dull manner.
in a slow and heavy way

Examples

The professor spoke ponderously, causing many students to lose interest halfway through the lecture.
The old machinery moved ponderously across the factory floor, creaking with every turn.
He answered the question ponderously, pausing frequently as if weighed down by the complexity.

Synset Examples

the play was staged with ponderously realistic sets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heavily - in a heavy, weighty manner
  • Antonym: lightly - in a light, quick, or effortless manner

Etymology

From 'ponderous' + suffix '-ly'; 'ponderous' from Latin 'ponderosus', from 'pondus' (weight).

2. ADV

/ ˈpɑndɚrəsli / (AmE), / ˈpɒndərəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is slow, heavy, and often awkward, as if burdened by great weight
in a heavy or labored manner

Examples

The old truck climbed the hill ponderously, engine straining under the load.
He spoke ponderously, choosing each word with slow deliberation.
The elephant moved ponderously through the dense jungle, shaking the ground as it walked.

Synset Examples

he moves ponderously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heavily - in a weighty, slow, or intense manner
  • Antonym: lightly - in a way that is not heavy, with ease or delicacy

Etymology

From 'ponderous' + '-ly'; 'ponderous' from Latin *ponderosus* (full of weight).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00429045-r
Synset POS: r
