inhumation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnhjuˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnhjuːˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or ritual of placing a dead body in a grave; burial
the act of burying a corpse

Examples

The ancient Egyptians practiced elaborate inhumation rituals for their pharaohs.
Archaeologists discovered evidence of inhumation beneath the church altar.
Cultural beliefs about inhumation differ widely around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burial - the act of placing a dead body in the ground
  • Antonym: cremation - the act of burning a dead body as a funeral rite

Etymology

From Latin 'inhumatio', from 'inhumare' (to bury), from 'in-' (in) + 'humus' (ground).
