japonica

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpɑːnɪkə / (AmE), / dʒəˈpɒnɪkə / (BrE)
A deciduous thorny shrub native to Japan, typically with red blossoms, commonly grown as an ornamental plant.
deciduous shrub with red blossoms, native to Japan

Examples

The gardener planted a row of japonica along the edge of the path to add color in spring.
Every spring, the bright red blooms of the japonica attract birds and bees to our backyard.
Although japonica thrives in sunny spots, it can also tolerate some shade in the garden.

Etymology

From Latin 'Japonica', meaning 'of Japan', from 'Japonia' (Japan).

2. NOUN

/ dʒəˈpɑnɪkə / (AmE), / dʒəˈpɒnɪkə / (BrE)
a greenhouse shrub with glossy green leaves and showy fragrant rose-like flowers, grown in many cultivated varieties
ornamental shrub with rose-like flowers

Examples

She planted a japonica in a sheltered corner of the garden to enjoy its spring blooms.
The vibrant pink flowers of the japonica brightened up the conservatory all winter.
Many gardeners prize the japonica for its glossy evergreen leaves and fragrant blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camellia - a related shrub with similar flowers, often referred to as 'camellia japonica'

Etymology

From Latin, the feminine form of 'japonicus' meaning 'of Japan', reflecting the plant's origin.
