beetroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːtruːt / (AmE), / ˈbiːtruːt / (BrE)
A vegetable with a large, round, red root, commonly eaten cooked, pickled, or raw in salads.
root vegetable with red flesh

Examples

I roasted some beetroot and served it with goat cheese for lunch.
The salad includes slices of beetroot and fresh greens.
Pickled beetroot is a popular side dish in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beet - a plant with a fleshy root, from which beetroot is derived
  • Degree Lower: turnip - a round root vegetable, similar in use to beetroot

Etymology

From 'beet' (from Old English 'beta', from Latin 'beta') + 'root', referring to the plant's edible root.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbiːtˌrut / (AmE), / ˈbiːtruːt / (BrE)
a round red root vegetable with dark purple flesh, eaten as a food and used for its juice or as a natural dye
round red root vegetable
british

Examples

She added slices of beetroot to the salad for extra color and flavor.
Pickled beetroot is a popular side dish in many countries.
The juice from beetroot can be used as a natural food coloring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beet - the American word for beetroot, edible red root vegetable

Etymology

From beet (the plant) + root; 'beet' from Old English 'bēte' via Latin 'beta'.
