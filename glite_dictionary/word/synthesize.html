to synthesize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪnθəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪnθəsaɪz / (BrE)
to combine different elements or substances to form a more complex whole
combine elements to form a whole

Examples

The scientist attempted to synthesize new compounds by combining existing chemicals.
Her report managed to synthesize several different perspectives into a cohesive argument.
Modern computers can synthesize realistic speech from written text.

Synset Examples

his operas synthesize music and drama in perfect harmony
The liver synthesizes vitamins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combine - to join or merge together
  • Antonym: to analyze - to break down into separate parts for study

Etymology

From Greek 'syntithenai' (to put together), via French 'synthétiser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00645728-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪnθəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪnθɪsʌɪz / (BrE)
to combine different things or ideas to form a complete whole or a new substance
combine elements to make a whole

Examples

Researchers were able to synthesize the new compound in the laboratory.
Her essay skillfully synthesizes the ideas of several experts into a coherent argument.
Plants synthesize their food from sunlight through the process of photosynthesis.

Synset Examples

Vitamin D only synthesizes when sunlight is available

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combine - to join or merge things together
  • Antonym: to analyze - to break something into parts to examine it

Etymology

From Greek 'synthesis' (a putting together), via French 'synthétiser.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00646092-v
Synset POS: v
