to encrimson

1. VERB

/ ɛnˈkrɪmzən / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrɪmzən / (BrE)
to cause to become crimson in color; to redden deeply
to make crimson in color

Examples

The sunset encrimsoned the clouds with a fiery glow.
A flush of embarrassment encrimsoned her cheeks.
Autumn leaves encrimson the landscape each October.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to redden - to make or become red

Etymology

Formed from the prefix en- (to make) and 'crimson' (a deep red color).
