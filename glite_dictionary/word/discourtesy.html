discourtesy

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkɝːtəsi / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɜːtəsi / (BrE)
rude or impolite behavior; a lack of courtesy or consideration for others.
rude or impolite behavior

Examples

His constant discourtesy towards the staff made him unpopular at work.
Leaving the meeting early without explanation was seen as a serious discourtesy.
The guide apologized for any discourtesy his team might have shown to the guests.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: courtesy - polite and considerate behavior
  • Synonym: rudeness - lack of manners; impoliteness

Etymology

From Old French 'descourtoisie', derived from 'des-' (not) + 'courtoisie' (courtesy).

3. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkɜrtəsi / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɜːtəsi / (BrE)
rude or impolite behavior; an act or remark showing lack of respect
rude or disrespectful behavior

Examples

He was dismissed for his repeated discourtesy towards customers.
Such discourtesy is not tolerated in professional settings.
She apologized for the discourtesy shown during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudeness - lack of manners or respect
  • Antonym: courtesy - polite behavior; showing respect to others
  • Synonym: impoliteness - not polite; lacking social grace

Etymology

Formed by prefix 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'courtesy', from Old French 'courtoisie'.
