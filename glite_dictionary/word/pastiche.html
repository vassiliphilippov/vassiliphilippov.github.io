pastiche

1. NOUN

/ pæˈstiʃ / (AmE), / pæˈstiːʃ / (BrE)
A musical composition consisting of a medley of songs or pieces from various sources, often imitating the style or character of different works.
musical composition made from various pieces

Examples

The concert ended with a pastiche of popular film themes blended together.
Her latest album features a delightful pastiche of jazz, folk, and classical tunes.
The composer created a pastiche that pays homage to several musical eras.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medley - a musical composition consisting of parts from different songs

Etymology

From Italian 'pasticcio', meaning medley or pasty, from 'pasta' (paste).

2. NOUN

/ pæˈstiʃ / (AmE), / paˈstiːʃ / (BrE)
A work of art, literature, or music that imitates the style or character of another work, artist, or period, often as a tribute or for satirical effect.
artwork imitating another style

Examples

The novel is a clever pastiche of 19th-century detective stories.
Her latest painting is a pastiche that combines elements from several famous artists.
The film was criticized for being more of a pastiche than an original creation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parody - a work imitating another in a satirical or comic way

Etymology

From French 'pastiche', from Italian 'pasticcio' (pie, pasty; hodgepodge).

Synset POS: n
