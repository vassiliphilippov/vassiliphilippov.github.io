Linotype

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ.nəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈlɪn.ə.taɪp / (BrE)
a machine used in printing, operated by a keyboard, that sets an entire line of type as a single piece of metal.
typesetting machine for printing

Examples

The old newspaper relied on a Linotype machine to compose its pages.
Many printers replaced their Linotype with more modern computers in the 1970s.
Learning to operate a Linotype was once an essential skill for typesetters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typesetter - a person or machine that sets type for printing
  • Antonym: Monotype - a typesetting system that casts individual letters rather than whole lines

Etymology

From 'line of type', indicating its ability to set a whole line at once.
