counterculture

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.tɚˌkʌl.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.təˌkʌl.tʃə / (BrE)
A way of life and set of values that are radically different from or opposed to those of the mainstream or dominant society.
culture opposed to mainstream values

Examples

The hippie movement of the 1960s became a prominent counterculture in America.
Many artists and musicians have been influenced by the punk counterculture.
Some young people seek out a counterculture that rejects materialism and traditional values.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mainstream - the ideas, attitudes, or activities that are regarded as normal or conventional

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) and 'culture', popularized in the 1960s.
