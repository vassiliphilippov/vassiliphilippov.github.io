successive

1. ADJ

/ səkˈsɛsɪv / (AmE), / səkˈsɛsɪv / (BrE)
following one after another in a sequence, without interruption or gaps.
following one after another

Examples

The company posted successive profits for five years.
There were three successive storms during the weekend.
Each runner must hand the baton to the next in successive order.

Synset Examples

serial concerts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecutive - following continuously in an unbroken sequence

Etymology

From Latin successivus, from successus (an advance, succession).
