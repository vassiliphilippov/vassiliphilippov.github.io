squattiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑːt̬i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈskwɒt.i.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being short and thickset in form or physique.
short and thickset physique

Examples

Her squattiness made her easy to spot in a crowd full of tall athletes.
The dog’s squattiness gave it a sturdy and powerful appearance despite its small size.
Critics said the vehicle’s squattiness made it look unbalanced compared to sleeker models.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squatness - the quality of being squat
  • Antonym: tallness - the quality of being tall
  • Antonym: slenderness - the quality of being slim or slender

Etymology

From 'squat', meaning short and thickset, plus the suffix '-iness' forming a noun.
