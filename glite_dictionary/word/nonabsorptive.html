nonabsorptive

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.əbˈzɔrp.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.əbˈzɔːp.tɪv / (BrE)
not capable of absorbing or soaking up liquids or other substances
not able to absorb liquids

Examples

The new plastic coating is nonabsorptive, making it perfect for waterproof jackets.
Glass is generally considered a nonabsorptive material, unlike wood or fabric.
For laboratory work, nonabsorptive surfaces are preferred to prevent contamination from spilled chemicals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absorptive - able to absorb or soak up liquids or other substances

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'absorptive' (having the capacity to absorb).
