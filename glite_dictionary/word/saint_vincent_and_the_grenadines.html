Saint Vincent and the Grenadines

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈvɪnsənt ənd ðə ˌɡrɛnəˈdiːnz / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈvɪnsənt ənd ðə ˌɡrɛnəˈdiːnz / (BrE)
an island country in the Caribbean, consisting of Saint Vincent and a chain of smaller islands called the Grenadines; gained independence from the United Kingdom in 1979
island country in the Caribbean

Examples

Tourists flock to Saint Vincent and the Grenadines for its stunning beaches and sailing opportunities.
After gaining independence, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines joined the Commonwealth of Nations.
Bananas are a major export product of Saint Vincent and the Grenadines.

Etymology

Saint Vincent named after Saint Vincent of Saragossa; Grenadines from the Spanish 'granada', meaning pomegranate.
