tramper

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræmpər / (AmE), / ˈtræmpə / (BrE)
a person who goes on long walks or hikes for pleasure, especially over countryside or rough terrain
someone who goes on long walks for pleasure

Examples

The group of trampers set off early to explore the national park trails.
As an experienced tramper, she always carried a detailed map and plenty of water.
Many trampers in New Zealand enjoy multi-day hikes through remote wilderness areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hiker - a person who goes for long walks, especially in the countryside

2. NOUN

/ ˈtræmpər / (AmE), / ˈtræmpə / (BrE)
a person who walks with a heavy, noisy step, often stamping on the ground
person who walks noisily or stamps

Examples

The noisy tramper could be heard long before he entered the room.
As a child, she was such a tramper that her footsteps shook the hallway.
The tramper made the floorboards creak with every step.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stomper - someone who stomps (walks with forceful, noisy steps)
  • Antonym: tiptoer - someone who walks on tiptoe, moving quietly

Etymology

From 'tramp' (to walk heavily or noisily) + '-er' (one who performs an action).
