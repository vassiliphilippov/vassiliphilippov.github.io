pepsin

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpsɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɛpsɪn / (BrE)
an enzyme in the stomach that breaks down proteins into smaller peptides during digestion
digestive enzyme in the stomach
medical

Examples

Pepsin is essential for the human digestive process, beginning the breakdown of proteins in the stomach.
After surgery, his doctor checked whether his body was still producing enough pepsin.
Researchers studied the effect of acidity on the function of pepsin in the digestive tract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a substance that speeds up chemical reactions in the body

Etymology

From Greek 'pepsis' (digestion), via Medieval Latin 'pepsinum'.
