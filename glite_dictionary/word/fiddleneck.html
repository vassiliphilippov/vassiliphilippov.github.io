fiddleneck

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪdəlˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈfɪdəlˌnɛk / (BrE)
a hairy annual plant native to California and Mexico, with coiled flower clusters of small blue, lilac, or mauve blossoms
a plant with coiled blue or lilac flowers

Examples

The hills were covered in fiddleneck during the spring, their twisted blooms creating a sea of color.
Farmers often watch for fiddleneck in their pastures, as some species can be toxic to livestock.
We spotted a patch of fiddleneck along the trail, its delicate flowers spiral like the neck of a violin.

Etymology

Named because the coiled shape of its flower clusters resembles the neck of a fiddle.
