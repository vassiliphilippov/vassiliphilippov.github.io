repudiative

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈpjuːdiˌeɪtɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈpjuːdɪətɪv / (BrE)
expressing or characterized by rejection, denial, or refusal, especially formally or emphatically
expressing rejection or refusal

Examples

The board issued a repudiative statement, rejecting the proposed merger in its entirety.
Her tone was distinctly repudiative when addressing the accusations against her company.
The company's repudiative response led to the immediate termination of the agreement.

Synset Examples

a veto is a repudiative act

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repudiating - rejecting or disowning something or someone
  • Antonym: accepting - willing to receive or approve

Etymology

From Latin repudiatus (cast off, rejected), from repudiare (to cast off, reject) + English -ive.
