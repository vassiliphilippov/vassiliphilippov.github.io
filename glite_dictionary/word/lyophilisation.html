lyophilisation

1. NOUN

/ laɪˌɑːfələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / laɪˌɒfɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A process of freeze-drying in which a material is frozen and then dried under vacuum, causing ice to sublime directly, used especially for preserving food, pharmaceuticals, and biological samples while maintaining their structure.
freeze-drying process for preservation

Examples

Pharmaceutical companies often use lyophilisation to preserve injectable medications for long-term storage.
The lyophilisation of fruit retains much of its original flavor and nutritional value.
Advances in lyophilisation have made it possible to store sensitive vaccines without refrigeration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freeze-drying - Removal of water from frozen material using a vacuum; common synonym for lyophilisation

Etymology

From Greek 'lyo-' (loosen) + 'philic' (loving) + '-ation', modeled after French 'lyophilisation'.
