double-bogey

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbl ˈboʊɡi / (AmE), / ˌdʌbl ˈbəʊɡi / (BrE)
(in golf) a score of two strokes over par for a hole.
golf: two strokes over par on a hole

Examples

He made a double-bogey on the 14th hole after missing his putt.
Her chances of winning dropped after she carded a double-bogey on the par-three.
It's tough to recover from a double-bogey in a close tournament.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bogey - a score of one stroke over par for a hole
  • Degree Higher: triple-bogey - a score of three strokes over par for a hole
