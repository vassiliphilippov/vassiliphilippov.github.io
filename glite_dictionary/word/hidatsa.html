Hidatsa

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈdɑtsə / (AmE), / hɪˈdɑːtsə / (BrE)
the Siouan language traditionally spoken by the Hidatsa people of North Dakota and surrounding regions.
a Native American Siouan language

Examples

The Hidatsa language is taught to children in the community to keep traditions alive.
Only a few elders are still fluent in Hidatsa today.
Researchers are documenting the grammar and vocabulary of Hidatsa for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan - family of Native American languages including Hidatsa and others

Etymology

From the name of the Hidatsa people.

2. NOUN

/ hɪˈdætsə / (AmE), / hɪˈdætsə / (BrE)
a member of an Indigenous people of the Sioux group, formerly living along the Missouri River in western North Dakota
a member of the Hidatsa Indigenous people

Examples

The Hidatsa were skilled farmers as well as hunters along the Missouri River.
She is researching the history and traditions of the Hidatsa.
A Hidatsa elder told stories of their people's ancestors and migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mandan - a member of another Indigenous people of North Dakota
  • Synonym: Arikara - a member of an Indigenous group historically located in the same region

Etymology

From the Hidatsa people’s own name for themselves, likely borrowed into English via earlier explorers’ records.
