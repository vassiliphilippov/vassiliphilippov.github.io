Callisaurus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of lizards commonly known as zebra-tailed lizards, native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and Mexico.
genus of zebra-tailed lizards

Examples

The Callisaurus is easily recognized by the black and white bands on its tail.
Researchers observed the Callisaurus basking on rocks in the desert sun.
Many herpetologists study Callisaurus to understand its unique escape behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zebra-tailed lizard - A species of lizard in the genus Callisaurus, known for its distinctive striped tail

Etymology

From Greek 'kallis' (beautiful) and 'sauros' (lizard).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of lizards known as zebra-tailed lizards, native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico
genus of zebra-tailed lizards

Examples

The Callisaurus can often be seen scurrying across the desert sands with its distinctive striped tail.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Callisaurus in the Sonoran Desert.
Unlike many lizards, the Callisaurus is easily identified by its zebra-like tail markings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zebra-tailed lizard - a lizard species (Callisaurus draconoides) known for banded tails

Etymology

From Greek kallos (beauty) + sauros (lizard).
