magistery

1. NOUN

/ mæˈdʒɪstəri / (AmE), / məˈdʒɪstəri / (BrE)
a high level of expertise or mastery, especially in a particular field such as literature
high expertise or mastery in a field

Examples

Her magistery in literature was apparent to all her students and colleagues.
The lecture showcased his magistery of Shakespearean drama.
Achieving magistery in any art form requires years of dedication and practice.

Synset Examples

Her magistery in literature was evident in every class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastery - great skill or knowledge in a subject or activity

Etymology

From Latin 'magisterium' (the office or authority of a master).
