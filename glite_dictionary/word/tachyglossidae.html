Tachyglossidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌtækiˈɡlɑsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌtækɪˈɡlɒsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of monotreme mammals comprising echidnas, which are spiny anteaters native to Australia and New Guinea.
family of spiny anteaters (echidnas)

Examples

The Tachyglossidae are unique among mammals for laying eggs instead of giving birth to live young.
Scientists study the Tachyglossidae to better understand evolutionary links between reptiles and mammals.
All known species of Tachyglossidae are found in Australia and New Guinea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echidna - a spiny anteater, member of Tachyglossidae

Etymology

From Greek tachys (swift) + glōssa (tongue), referring to the animal's rapid tongue movement.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtækiˌɡlɑːsɪˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌtækɪˌɡlɒsɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of monotreme mammals that comprises the echidnas, native to Australia and New Guinea.
family of monotreme mammals (echidnas)

Examples

The Tachyglossidae are uniquely adapted to feed on ants and termites using their long tongues.
Members of the Tachyglossidae family are found in Australia and New Guinea.
Scientists study the reproductive habits of Tachyglossidae to learn more about monotreme evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echidna - spiny, egg-laying mammal of the family Tachyglossidae

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Greek tachys (swift) + glōssa (tongue) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
