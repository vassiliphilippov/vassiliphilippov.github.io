demonism

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːməˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdiːmənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
belief in or worship of demons, especially Satan.
belief in or worship of demons

Examples

Some ancient cultures practiced demonism to appease malevolent spirits.
The rise of demonism in literature reflected societal fears of the supernatural.
She researched the influence of demonism on medieval religious beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demonolatry - the worship of demons

Etymology

From Greek daimōn (spirit, demon) + -ism.
