corpus amygdaloideum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.pəs əˌmɪɡ.dəˌlɔɪˈdi.əm / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.pəs əˌmɪɡ.dəˌlɔɪˈdiː.əm / (BrE)
an almond-shaped mass of nuclei located in the anterior temporal lobe of the brain, forming part of the limbic system and playing a key role in motivation and emotional processing
brain structure involved in emotion
medical

Examples

The corpus amygdaloideum is crucial for processing fear and other emotional responses.
Scientists observed changes in the corpus amygdaloideum during different stages of emotional learning.
Damage to the corpus amygdaloideum may lead to altered emotional behavior in patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amygdala - the more commonly used term for corpus amygdaloideum

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'almond-shaped body'; from 'corpus' (body) and Greek 'amygdalos' (almond).
