Omar Khayyam

1. PROPN

/ oʊˌmɑːr kaɪˈjɑːm / (AmE), / əʊˌmɑː kaɪˈjɑːm / (BrE)
(c. 1048–1131) A Persian poet, mathematician, and astronomer, best known in the West for his poetic work 'The Rubáiyát', popularized by Edward FitzGerald's translation.
Persian poet and mathematician (1048–1131)

Examples

The poetry of Omar Khayyam has inspired generations of readers around the world.
Many mathematicians still admire the contributions made by Omar Khayyam to algebra.
We discussed the philosophy of Omar Khayyam in our literature class yesterday.

Etymology

From Arabic ‘Umar Khayyām, his full name being Ghiyāth ad-Dīn Abū al-Fatḥ ‘Umar ibn Ibrāhīm al-Khayyām.
