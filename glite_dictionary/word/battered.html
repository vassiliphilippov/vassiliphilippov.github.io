battered

1. ADJ

/ ˈbæt̬.ɚd / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əd / (BrE)
Suffering from repeated physical or emotional abuse, often showing visible or psychological signs of injury.
subjected to repeated abuse or injury

Examples

After years in a violent relationship, she finally sought help as a battered woman.
Social workers intervened after signs that the child was battered became apparent at school.
He volunteers at a shelter that supports battered families escaping dangerous situations.

Synset Examples

a battered child
the battered woman syndrome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abused - subjected to cruel or violent treatment repeatedly
  • Antonym: protected - not subjected to harm or abuse
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00017547-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈbætɚd / (AmE), / ˈbætəd / (BrE)
damaged by continuous use or hard treatment, often appearing worn or shabby.
damaged from hard use

Examples

He wore a battered old coat that had seen better days.
Their car looked a bit battered after the long road trip.
She found a battered suitcase in the attic, covered in dust and scars.

Synset Examples

his battered old hat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worn - damaged or shabby from long use
  • Antonym: pristine - in perfect condition; unspoiled

Etymology

From Middle English bateren, from Old French batre (to beat), from Latin battuere.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02591692-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈbætərd / (AmE), / ˈbatəd / (BrE)
damaged as a result of repeated blows, heavy use, or rough treatment.
damaged by repeated use or blows

Examples

He drove a battered truck that looked like it had seen better days.
The battered suitcase barely survived another trip through the airport.
She found a battered copy of the novel at a secondhand bookstore.

Synset Examples

a battered old car
the beaten-up old Ford

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beaten-up - damaged or worn from heavy use or blows
  • Antonym: pristine - in perfect condition; unspoiled

Etymology

From Middle English 'batre', from Old French 'batre', from Latin 'battuere' (to beat).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
