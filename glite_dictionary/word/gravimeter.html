gravimeter

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈvɪmɪtər / (AmE), / ɡrəˈvɪmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used for measuring the specific gravity of liquids or solids
instrument to measure specific gravity

Examples

The laboratory was equipped with a modern gravimeter for precise measurements.
Scientists used a gravimeter to determine the density of the mineral sample.
A portable gravimeter is essential for field geological surveys.

Etymology

From Latin 'gravitas' (weight) and '-meter' (measuring device).

2. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈvɪmətər / (AmE), / ɡrəˈvɪmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument for measuring variations in the Earth's gravitational field
instrument to measure gravity variations

Examples

The geologist used a gravimeter to detect differences in subsurface densities.
A highly sensitive gravimeter can reveal tiny changes in Earth's gravitational pull.
Portable gravimeters are often employed in field surveys to map underground features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accelerometer - an instrument for measuring acceleration

Etymology

From Latin 'gravitas' (weight, gravity) + Greek 'metron' (measure).
