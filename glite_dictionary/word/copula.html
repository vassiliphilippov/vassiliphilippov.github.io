copula

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːpjələ / (AmE), / ˈkɒpjʊlə / (BrE)
a verb, such as 'be' or 'become', that links the subject of a sentence to its complement and expresses a state or condition
linking verb (e.g. 'be', 'become')

Examples

The verb 'is' functions as a copula in the sentence 'She is happy.'
Many languages express the copula differently, sometimes even omitting it altogether.
In grammar, the copula links the subject to a subject complement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linking verb - a verb that connects the subject with a complement

Etymology

From Latin 'copula' meaning 'a bond' or 'a link'.
