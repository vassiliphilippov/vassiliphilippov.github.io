archaebacterium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrkioʊbækˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / ˌɑːkiəbækˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
a microorganism of a group considered to be ancient life forms, evolving separately from bacteria and cyanobacteria, now usually called archaea
ancient microorganism, now archaea
medical

Examples

Many scientists now classify the archaebacterium as a separate domain from bacteria and eukaryotes.
The archaebacterium can survive extreme environments, such as hot springs and salt lakes.
Recent research has revealed surprising genetic diversity within the archaebacterium lineage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archaea - microorganisms forming a distinct group separate from bacteria
  • Antonym: bacterium - single-celled microorganism distinct from archaea

Etymology

From Greek 'arkhaios' (ancient) + 'bacterium'.
