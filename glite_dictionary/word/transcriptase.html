transcriptase

1. NOUN

/ trænˈskrɪp.teɪs / (AmE), / trænˈskrɪp.teɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyses the formation of RNA from a DNA template
enzyme copying DNA into RNA

Examples

The enzyme transcriptase is essential for synthesizing mRNA during gene expression.
Researchers studied the activity of transcriptase in bacterial cells.
Mutations in transcriptase can affect how genes are transcribed into RNA.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverse transcriptase - an enzyme that synthesizes DNA from an RNA template

Etymology

From 'transcription' + '-ase', a suffix used for enzymes.
