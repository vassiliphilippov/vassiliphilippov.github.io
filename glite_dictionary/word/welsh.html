Welsh

1. NOUN

/ wɛlʃ / (AmE), / wɛlʃ / (BrE)
the Celtic language spoken in Wales
Celtic language of Wales

Examples

He learned to speak Welsh fluently after moving to Cardiff.
All government documents in Wales are available in both English and Welsh.
The Welsh language has a rich tradition of poetry and song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaelic - a Celtic language spoken in Scotland and Ireland

Etymology

From Old English 'Wælisc', meaning foreign or Welsh, from Proto-Germanic 'Walhaz'.

of or relating to Wales, its people, or their language

Examples

4. NOUN

/ wɛlʃ / (AmE), / wɛlʃ / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Wales
a person from Wales

Examples

My grandfather is proud to be Welsh and often talks about his life in Cardiff.
Several Welsh attended the festival, bringing traditional music and food.
The Welsh have a rich cultural heritage that dates back centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Welshwoman - a female native or resident of Wales
  • Synonym: Welshman - a male native or resident of Wales

Etymology

From Old English *Wælisc* ('foreign' or 'Celtic'), from the name used by the Anglo-Saxons for Celtic-speaking peoples.
