Veronica peregrina

1. NOUN

/ vəˈrɑnɪkə pəˈrɛɡrɪnə / (AmE), / vəˈrɒnɪkə pəˈrɛɡrɪnə / (BrE)
A North American annual plant of the speedwell genus, having small white flowers, widely naturalized as a weed in South America and Europe.
a plant species of the speedwell genus

Examples

Veronica peregrina is commonly found in moist meadows and along riverbanks.
Gardeners often consider Veronica peregrina a troublesome weed due to its rapid spread.
The small white flowers of Veronica peregrina bloom from early spring to early summer.

Etymology

From Latin 'Veronica' (the genus name), possibly after Saint Veronica, and 'peregrina' meaning 'wandering' or 'foreign'.
