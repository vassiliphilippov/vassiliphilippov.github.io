tael

1. NOUN

/ teɪl / (AmE), / teɪl / (BrE)
a traditional Chinese unit of weight, used in East Asia, approximately equal to 37.8 grams (about 1.3 ounces)
traditional East Asian weight unit (about 1.3 oz)

Examples

The gold bar weighed five taels and was marked with a government seal.
Merchants in 19th-century Shanghai often traded silver in taels rather than in coins.
A tael of rice was distributed to each villager during the ceremony.

Etymology

From Chinese Cantonese 錢 (cìn), Mandarin 两 (liǎng) referring to the weight unit.
