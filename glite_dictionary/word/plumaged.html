plumaged

1. ADJ

/ ˈpluːmɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˈpluːmɪdʒd / (BrE)
having or covered with a particular type of feathers or plumage, often used with modifiers to indicate color or quality.
having a covering of feathers

Examples

The plumaged birds filled the forest with flashes of color.
Scientists observed several brightly plumaged males competing for mates.
A fully plumaged chick is ready to leave the nest.

Synset Examples

fully plumaged young bird
brilliantly plumaged parrots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feathered - having feathers; covered with feathers

Etymology

From 'plumage' (feathers), formed as an adjective suffix '-ed'.
