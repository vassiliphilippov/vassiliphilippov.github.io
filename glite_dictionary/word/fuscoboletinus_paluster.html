Fuscoboletinus paluster

1. NOUN

/ ˌfʌskoʊboʊˈlɛtɪnəs pəˈlʌstər / (AmE), / ˌfʌskəʊbəʊˈliːtɪnəs pəˈlʌstə / (BrE)
An edible species of fungus with a pinkish-purple cap and stalk, and a yellow pore surface whose pores become gill-like as it matures.
edible fungus with pinkish-purple cap and yellow pores

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with Fuscoboletinus paluster specimens after the rain.
Mushroom hunters value Fuscoboletinus paluster for its unique flavor and appearance.
The pore surface of Fuscoboletinus paluster turns gill-like as the fungus matures.

Etymology

From Latin: 'fuscus' (dark), 'boletus' (mushroom), and 'paluster' (of marshes).
