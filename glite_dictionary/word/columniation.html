columniation

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlʌm.niˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kəˌlʌm.nɪˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(architecture) the arrangement or system of columns, especially freestanding ones, in a structure.
arrangement of columns in architecture

Examples

The Parthenon's remarkable columniation exemplifies classical Greek architecture.
Modern architects often experiment with different forms of columniation to create striking visual effects.
The building's symmetrical columniation draws the eye toward the grand entrance.

Etymology

From Latin 'columna' (column) + English suffix '-ation' (denoting action or process).
