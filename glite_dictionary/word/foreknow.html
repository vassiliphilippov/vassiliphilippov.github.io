to foreknow

1. VERB

/ fɔrˈnoʊ / (AmE), / fɔːˈnəʊ / (BrE)
to know something in advance; to realize or be aware of something before it happens
to know in advance

Examples

It is impossible to foreknow what challenges the new year will bring.
Historians wonder whether leaders could have foreknown the consequences of their decisions.
No one can foreknow the outcome with absolute certainty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anticipate - to expect or look ahead to something; to realize in advance
  • Synonym: to predict - to declare or indicate in advance, especially on the basis of observation, experience, or scientific reason

Etymology

From Old English 'forecnāwan', from 'fore-' (before) + 'cnāwan' (to know).
