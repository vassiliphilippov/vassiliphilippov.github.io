urgency

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrdʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈɜːdʒənsi / (BrE)
an urgent situation that requires prompt action
urgent situation requiring action

Examples

He left the meeting early because of an unforeseen urgency at work.
Please notify me immediately if any urgency arises during my absence.
Medical staff are trained to respond quickly to any urgency that may occur.

Synset Examples

I'll be there, barring any urgencies
they departed hurriedly because of some great urgency in their affairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emergency - a serious, unexpected, and often dangerous situation requiring immediate action

Etymology

From Latin urgentia, from urgere (to press, urge).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɜrdʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈɜːdʒənsi / (BrE)
persistent and insistent demand or solicitation; the quality of pressing one's request forcefully
persistent, forceful insistence

Examples

He made his case with such urgency that the committee felt compelled to respond.
Her repeated calls for help carried a sense of urgency that could not be ignored.
The urgency in his requests eventually convinced them to take action.

Synset Examples

his importunity left me no alternative but to agree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: importunity - persistent, pressing demand or request

Etymology

From Latin urgentia (pressing, pressure), from urgēre (to press).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɝːdʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈɜːdʒənsi / (BrE)
the quality or state of requiring immediate attention or action; pressing importance
pressing importance, need for quick action

Examples

The doctor spoke with a sense of urgency about the patient’s condition.
Due to the urgency of the task, the team worked late into the night.
She recognized the urgency in his voice and knew there was no time to delay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emergency - a serious, unexpected, and urgent situation
  • Synonym: importance - the state of being important or significant
  • Antonym: leisure - time when one is not required to act quickly; absence of urgency

Etymology

From Latin 'urgentia', from 'urgēre' (to press, urge).

4. NOUN

/ ˈɝːdʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈɜːdʒənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being very important and requiring immediate attention or action
pressing importance; need for quick action

Examples

She spoke with such urgency that everyone listened closely.

Synset Examples

the urgency of his need

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emergency - a serious, unexpected situation requiring immediate action
  • Antonym: calm - a state of being relaxed and not urgent

Etymology

From Latin urgentia (pressing, importunate), from urgēre (to press, urge).
