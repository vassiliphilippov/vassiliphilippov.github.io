orderly

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdɚli / (AmE), / ˈɔːdəli / (BrE)
a person, especially a man, employed in a hospital to help with general tasks that do not involve direct medical treatment of patients
hospital attendant with non-medical duties

Examples

The orderly helped transfer the patient from his bed to the wheelchair.
After his surgery, an orderly brought John fresh linens and water.
The hospital staff called for an orderly to assist with moving equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attendant - a person who assists or serves others as part of their job
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10401503-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔrdərli / (AmE), / ˈɔːdəli / (BrE)
a soldier assigned to serve as a personal attendant to a superior officer, performing tasks such as carrying messages and handling personal chores
soldier serving as a personal attendant to an officer

Examples

The general's orderly woke him at dawn and brought his coffee.
After the battle, the orderly helped the colonel clean and repair his uniform.
An experienced orderly can anticipate his officer's needs without being told.

Synset Examples

the orderly laid out the general's uniform

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batman - soldier assigned to a commissioned officer as an attendant
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10401680-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈɔrdɚli / (AmE), / ˈɔːdəli / (BrE)
arranged or organized in a tidy, methodical way; clean and neat
tidy and well-organized

Examples

She keeps her desk orderly and free of clutter.
The workshop was surprisingly orderly after a busy day of projects.
We admired the orderly rows of books on the shelf.

Synset Examples

her neat dress
a neat room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neat - tidy and orderly in appearance or habits
  • Antonym: messy - untidy or disorganized

Etymology

From Old French 'ordene', from Latin 'ordo' (order).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02432057-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈɔrdɚli / (AmE), / ˈɔːdəli / (BrE)
peaceful and free from violence or disruption; characterized by calm and organized behavior or conditions
peaceful and free from disruption

Examples

The protesters remained orderly throughout the demonstration, impressing the police with their discipline.
Our departure from the stadium was orderly, with everyone waiting their turn to leave.
The classroom was unusually orderly after the teacher explained her expectations.

Synset Examples

an orderly crowd confronted the president

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peaceful - free from violence or disturbance
  • Antonym: disorderly - characterized by lack of order or disruptive behavior

Etymology

From order + -ly (adjective-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01670379-a
Synset POS: a
