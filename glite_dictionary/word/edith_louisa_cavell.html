Edith Louisa Cavell

1. PROPN

/ ˈiːdɪθ luˈiːzə ˈkævəl / (AmE), / ˈiːdɪθ luˈiːzə ˈkævəl / (BrE)
(1865–1915) An English nurse who helped Allied soldiers escape from German-occupied Belgium during World War I and was executed by the Germans.
English nurse who helped Allied escapees in WWI

Examples

Many consider Edith Louisa Cavell a symbol of courage and humanitarianism during World War I.
A statue of Edith Louisa Cavell stands near Trafalgar Square in London.
The story of Edith Louisa Cavell continues to inspire nurses and caregivers worldwide.
