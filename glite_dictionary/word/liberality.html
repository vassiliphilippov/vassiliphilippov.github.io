liberality

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪbəˈræləti / (AmE), / ˌlɪbəˈraləti / (BrE)
an attitude or inclination favoring progress, open-mindedness, and individual freedom, especially in political or social matters
tendency to favor progress and freedom

Examples

His liberality led him to support new social reforms.
Educational institutions often value liberality and open debate.
The senator was known for her liberality on civil rights issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberalism - belief in individual liberty and political and social reform
  • Antonym: conservatism - belief in traditional values and institutions; resistance to change

Etymology

From Latin 'liberalitas' (generosity), from 'liberalis' (of freedom).

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɪbəˈræləti / (AmE), / ˌlɪbəˈrælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being generous or open-handed; willingness to give freely or act with generosity
generosity; open-handedness

Examples

Her liberality towards the less fortunate earned her admiration throughout the community.
The foundation supports educational projects thanks to the liberality of its donors.
He was respected for his liberality in both thought and action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generosity - willingness to give more than is necessary or expected
  • Antonym: stinginess - lack of generosity; unwillingness to give or spend

Etymology

From Latin 'liberalitas' (generosity), from 'liberalis' (of freedom, generous).
