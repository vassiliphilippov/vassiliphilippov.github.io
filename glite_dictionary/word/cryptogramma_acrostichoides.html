Cryptogramma acrostichoides

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptəˈɡræmə ˌækrəˌstɪˈkoʊɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəˈɡræmə ˌækrəʊstɪˈkɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A rock-inhabiting fern native to northern North America, growing in dense tufts with fronds that resemble parsley.
fern species with parsley-like fronds

Examples

The Cryptogramma acrostichoides thrives on rocky outcrops in alpine regions.
Gardeners appreciate Cryptogramma acrostichoides for its delicate, parsley-like foliage.
On our hike, we spotted several clumps of Cryptogramma acrostichoides growing between boulders.

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden), 'gramma' (letter), and 'akrostichos' (top row), used in botanical Latin nomenclature.
