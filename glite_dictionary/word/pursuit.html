pursuit

1. NOUN

/ pərˈsuːt / (AmE), / pəˈsjuːt / (BrE)
the act of following or chasing someone or something in order to catch them
the act of chasing or following

Examples

After the alarms sounded, the security team began their pursuit of the intruder.
The high-speed pursuit ended safely when the suspect pulled over.
The cheetah's pursuit of its prey is one of nature's most impressive spectacles.

Synset Examples

the culprit started to run and the cop took off in pursuit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chase - the act of pursuing someone or something to catch them
  • Antonym: escape - the act of getting away, opposite of pursuit

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *pursuite* (act of pursuing), from Old French *poursuite*

2. NOUN

/ pərˈsuːt / (AmE), / pəˈsjuːt / (BrE)
an activity that you spend time doing, usually for pleasure; a hobby or leisure interest
an activity or hobby

Examples

Gardening has become a relaxing pursuit for many people during the weekends.
Painting is a creative pursuit she enjoys in her free time.
He devotes much of his leisure to intellectual pursuits.

Synset Examples

sailing is her favorite pastime
his main pastime is gambling
he counts reading among his interests
they criticized the boy for his limited pursuits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastime - an activity done for enjoyment, not as one's main occupation
  • Synonym: hobby - an activity done regularly in one's leisure time for pleasure

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French *pursuite* (act of following), related to the verb 'pursue'.

3. NOUN

/ pərˈsuːt / (AmE), / pəˈsjuːt / (BrE)
an activity that you spend time doing, especially as a hobby or for enjoyment, separate from your main job or responsibility
hobby or auxiliary activity

Examples

Gardening is one of his favorite pursuits in retirement.
She divides her time between her career and various artistic pursuits.
In their free time, many people take up new pursuits to relax and learn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hobby - an activity done regularly for pleasure in one's free time
  • Antonym: profession - a paid occupation, especially one that requires advanced education or training

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French *poursuite* (action of pursuing), from *poursuir* (to pursue).

4. NOUN

/ pərˈsuːt / (AmE), / pəˈsjuːt / (BrE)
the act of striving for or seeking to attain something, such as an ideal, goal, or object
the act of striving for something

Examples

She devoted years to the pursuit of scientific knowledge.
His pursuit of happiness led him to travel the world.
In the pursuit of success, one must be willing to take risks.

Synset Examples

the pursuit of love
life is more than the pursuance of fame
a quest for wealth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quest - a long search for something difficult to find or achieve
  • Synonym: search - the act of looking for something carefully

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French poursuivre, from Latin prosequi (to follow, pursue).
