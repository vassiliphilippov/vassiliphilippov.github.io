industry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndəstri / (AmE), / ˈɪndəstri / (BrE)
the people or companies involved in a particular type of commercial activity or trade
people or companies in a commercial sector

Examples

The fashion industry is constantly evolving to keep up with new trends.
Many graduates seek jobs in the technology industry after completing their studies.
Government regulations can have a major impact on the pharmaceutical industry.

Synset Examples

each industry has its own trade publications

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sector - a distinct part of an economy, society, or field

Etymology

From Latin industria (diligence, enterprise), from industrius (diligent).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndəstri / (AmE), / ˈɪndəstri / (BrE)
the organized production of goods and services for sale, often involving factories and businesses
organized economic production

Examples

The government is planning to boost the industry by offering new incentives to manufacturers.
Thanks to technological advancements, the automobile industry has become more efficient.
Tourism is now a major industry in the region, creating thousands of jobs.

Synset Examples

American industry is making increased use of computers to control production

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manufacturing - the process of making goods, especially in a factory

Etymology

From Latin industria (diligence, activity), via Old French industrie.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00925258-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.də.stri / (AmE), / ˈɪn.də.stri / (BrE)
Persevering and diligent effort in carrying out a task or duty; hard work.
persistent and diligent effort

Examples

Her industry in completing every assignment on time impressed her supervisors.
Success in scientific research often depends on both industry and curiosity.
Through sheer industry and persistence, he overcame every obstacle in his path.

Synset Examples

his diligence won him quick promotions
frugality and industry are still regarded as virtues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diligence - careful and persistent work or effort
  • Antonym: indolence - avoidance of activity or exertion; laziness

Etymology

From Latin industria (diligence, activity, zeal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04872345-n
Synset POS: n
