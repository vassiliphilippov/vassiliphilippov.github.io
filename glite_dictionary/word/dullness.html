dullness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌlnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʌlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being slow to understand, or lacking quickness of mind
slowness of understanding

Examples

Her apparent dullness made the instructions difficult to follow.
The teacher became frustrated by the students' dullness during the lesson.
Despite his dullness, he tried hard to keep up with his peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stupidity - lack of intelligence or quickness of mind
  • Antonym: sharpness - the quality of being quick to notice or understand things

Etymology

From 'dull' + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌlnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʌlnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being not sharp or lacking a clear edge or point.
lack of sharpness or clarity of edge

Examples

The dullness of the knife made it hard to cut the vegetables.
Over time, the dullness of the scissors became frustrating when wrapping gifts.
She noticed the dullness of the blade after months of frequent use.

Synset Examples

the dullness of the pencil made his writing illegible

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sharpness - the quality of having a fine, clear edge or point

Etymology

From 'dull' + '-ness', formed in Middle English.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʌlnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʌlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of lacking visual brightness or vividness; not being shiny or bright
lack of brightness or vividness

Examples

The dullness of the paint made the room appear dreary.
After years of sun and rain, the metal lost its shine and showed clear dullness.
Clouds often bring a dullness to the landscape that makes everything seem muted.

Synset Examples

the brightness of the orange sky was reflected in the dullness of the orange sea

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brightness - the quality of being visually bright or luminous
  • Synonym: dimness - the quality of being faint or not clear with regard to light

Etymology

From 'dull' (Middle English dul, uninteresting or lacking brightness) + '-ness' (forming nouns of quality).

4. NOUN

/ ˈdʌlnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʌlnəs / (BrE)
a lack of emotional sensitivity, liveliness, or interest; the quality of being unfeeling, unresponsive, or lacking in excitement
lack of sensibility or interest

Examples

There was a sense of dullness in his voice that made the conversation difficult.
Without her friends, the dullness of his days became almost unbearable.
She tried to fight the dullness that crept into her thoughts after the long, lonely winter.

Synset Examples

there was a dullness in his heart
without him the dullness of her life crept into her work no matter how she tried to compartmentalize it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insensitivity - lack of sensitivity or awareness, especially emotionally
  • Synonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern
  • Antonym: vivacity - the quality of being lively and animated

Etymology

From Middle English 'dulnes', from 'dull' + '-ness'.

5. NOUN

/ ˈdʌlnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʌlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being uninteresting or lacking excitement
the state of being uninteresting

Examples

The dullness of the lecture made it difficult for students to stay awake.
She complained about the dullness of small-town life after living in a big city.
Despite the film's promising premise, its dullness led many viewers to leave early.

Synset Examples

the stories were of a dullness to bring a buffalo to its knees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tedium - the state of being tedious or boring
  • Antonym: liveliness - the quality of being full of life and energy; exciting

Etymology

From 'dull' + '-ness', with 'dull' from Middle English, possibly from Old English 'dol' (foolish).
