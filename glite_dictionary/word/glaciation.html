glaciation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡleɪsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡleɪsiˈeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The process by which large areas of the Earth's surface are covered with glaciers or ice sheets.
process of covering land with glaciers

Examples

The last Ice Age was a period of intense glaciation that shaped much of the Northern Hemisphere.
Evidence of glaciation can be seen in the U-shaped valleys left behind after the ice has melted.
Scientists study ancient glaciation events to understand climate change throughout Earth's history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deglaciation - the process of melting and retreat of glaciers

Etymology

From French *glaciation*, from Latin *glaciēs* (ice).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡleɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡleɪsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process or state of being covered by glaciers or large masses of ice, or the effects of glacial movement on landscapes.
process of ice covering land
medical

Examples

The valleys show clear evidence of glaciation from thousands of years ago.
Scientists study glaciation patterns to understand ancient climate changes.
Heavy glaciation during the Ice Age shaped much of the present-day landscape.

Synset Examples

Agassiz recognized marks of glaciation all over northern Europe

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deglaciation - the process of losing ice cover or retreat of glaciers

Etymology

From Latin 'glaciēs' (ice), with the suffix '-ation' indicating an action or process.
