waltz

1. NOUN

/ wɑlts / (AmE), / wɔːlts / (BrE)
a ballroom dance in triple time, typically performed with graceful, turning movements and a strong accent on the first beat
a ballroom dance in triple time

Examples

They took to the floor for a graceful waltz at the wedding reception.
Learning the waltz requires practice to master its distinctive rhythm and steps.
The orchestra played a classic waltz as the guests began to dance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxtrot - a type of ballroom dance with a different rhythm
  • Synonym: tango - a popular ballroom dance of Latin American origin

Etymology

From German 'Walzer', from 'walzen' meaning 'to roll' or 'turn'.

2. NOUN

/ wɔlts / (AmE), / wɔːlts / (BrE)
an easy and decisive victory, especially in an election or competition
an easy, decisive victory

Examples

The team's latest game was a waltz, with a final score of 6–0.
She expected the election to be tough, but in the end it was a waltz.
Their campaign strategy turned what could have been a close race into a waltz.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landslide - an overwhelming victory, especially in politics
  • Synonym: walkover - a contest won easily or by default

Etymology

Originally from the German 'Walzer', meaning 'to roll or revolve', referring to the dance. The figurative sense comes from the ease and smoothness of the dance.
