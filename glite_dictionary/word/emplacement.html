emplacement

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈpleɪsmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpleɪsmənt / (BrE)
the act of putting something in a particular place or position, especially for a specific purpose
act of placing something in position

Examples

The emplacement of the sculpture in the garden required careful planning.
Following the emplacement of the new computers, productivity increased noticeably.
The emplacement of artillery along the ridge was completed overnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placement - the action of putting something in a particular place

Etymology

From French 'emplacement', from 'emplacer' (to place).

2. NOUN

/ ɛmˈpleɪsmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpleɪsmənt / (BrE)
a fortified or prepared position where a weapon, especially a gun or artillery piece, is placed for use in combat
fortified position for weapon placement

Examples

The soldiers dug an emplacement on the hillside to prepare for incoming attacks.
An anti-aircraft gun was positioned in a hidden emplacement near the coastline.
After the battle, the abandoned emplacement was discovered by the advancing troops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battery - an array of guns or missile launchers operated together at one place

Etymology

From French 'emplacement', from 'emplacer' (to place in position).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03289031-n
Synset POS: n
