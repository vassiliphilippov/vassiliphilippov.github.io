caliphate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪlɪˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkælɪfeɪt / (BrE)
A political and religious state in Islam governed by a caliph, especially during the period following the death of Muhammad until the 13th century.
Islamic state under a caliph's rule

Examples

The Ottoman Empire claimed to be the caliphate for centuries.
Some political movements in modern times have called for the restoration of the caliphate.
The end of the Abbasid caliphate marked a turning point in Islamic history.

Synset Examples

their goal was to reestablish the Caliphate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caliph - the chief Muslim civil and religious ruler, regarded as the successor of Muhammad

Etymology

From Arabic 'khilāfa' (succession, caliphate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪlɪˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkælɪfeɪt / (BrE)
the office, authority, or reign of a caliph; a state governed by a caliph
office or realm of a caliph

Examples

The Ottoman Empire was the last major caliphate in history.
Many Muslims once looked to the caliphate as a symbol of unity.
Debates about the restoration of the caliphate have appeared in modern political discussions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sultanate - office or reign of a sultan
  • Synonym: empire - an extensive group of states or countries under a single supreme authority

Etymology

From Arabic 'khilāfa' (succession, caliphate), from 'khalīfa' (caliph).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.ləˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæl.ɪ.feɪt / (BrE)
the territory or government ruled by a caliph, especially in Islamic history
territory ruled by a caliph

Examples

The Ottoman Empire claimed to be the last great caliphate in the Muslim world.
Scholars have studied the administration of the Abbasid caliphate extensively.
Many empires have aspired to restore the caliphate over Islamic territories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sultanate - territory ruled by a sultan

Etymology

From Arabic 'khilāfa' (succession, caliphate), via Medieval Latin 'califatus'.
