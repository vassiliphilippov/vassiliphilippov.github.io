curiously

1. ADV

/ ˈkjʊriəsli / (AmE), / ˈkjʊəriəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows curiosity or interest in learning or knowing something.
in a curious or inquisitive manner

Examples

The kitten sniffed the strange box curiously before climbing inside.
She asked curiously about the new student in her class.
Tourists wandered curiously through the old castle, eager to discover its secrets.

Synset Examples

the baby looked around curiously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inquisitively - in a manner showing an eagerness to know or learn something
  • Antonym: casually - in a manner lacking interest or curiosity
Source: WordNet

2. ADV

/ ˈkjʊriəsli / (AmE), / ˈkjʊəriəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is unusual or unexpected; strangely or peculiarly
in an unusual or unexpected way

Examples

The curtains were hung curiously, with one side much higher than the other.
She felt curiously calm despite the chaos around her.
The museum was curiously empty for a Saturday afternoon.

Synset Examples

had a curiously husky voice
he's behaving rather peculiarly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangely - in a way that is not usual or expected
  • Synonym: peculiarly - in a way that is unusual or distinctive

Etymology

From 'curious' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'curiosus' (careful, inquisitive).
