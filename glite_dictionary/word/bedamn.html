to bedamn

1. VERB

/ bɪˈdæm / (AmE), / bɪˈdam / (BrE)
to wish harm or invoke a curse upon someone or something
wish harm or invoke a curse
archaic

Examples

The villain threatened to bedamn anyone who opposed his plans.
No one dared to bedamn the king for fear of reprisal.
She jokingly said she would bedamn her shoes if they made her trip again.

Synset Examples

The bad witch cursed the child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - to invoke supernatural harm; to wish misfortune on
  • Antonym: to bless - to invoke divine favor; wish good upon someone

Etymology

From prefix 'be-' (intensifying) + 'damn' (to condemn).
