less

1. PART

/ lɛs / (AmE), / lɛs / (BrE)
Used with adjectives and adverbs to form the comparative and indicate a smaller amount, degree, or extent.
comparative particle for smaller amount or degree

Examples

For a healthier option, choose meals that are less salty.
The hike was much less difficult than I expected.
If you leave earlier, you'll be less likely to hit traffic.

Synset Examples

less interesting
less expensive
less quickly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: more - comparative particle for greater amount or degree

Etymology

From Old English læs (less), from Proto-Germanic *laisiz.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00100077-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ lɛs / (AmE), / lɛs / (BrE)
to a smaller degree or extent; not as much as something else.
to a smaller degree or amount

Examples

The new phone weighs less than the previous model.
He spends less time watching TV now than he used to.
They offered less support than we expected.

Synset Examples

she walks less than she should
he works less these days

Related Terms

  • Antonym: more - to a greater degree or extent; the comparative of much/many

Etymology

From Old English 'lǣssa', comparative of 'lȳtel' (little).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00100418-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADJ

/ lɛs / (AmE), / lɛs / (BrE)
Used with measure phrases to mean 'fewer,' especially in expressions like 'less than three hours' or '25 words or less.'
fewer, especially with measures

Examples

Please keep your response to 100 words or less.
The flight will take less than two hours to complete.
There were no less than ten people waiting in line.

Synset Examples

less than three weeks
no less than 50 people attended
in 25 words or less

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fewer - a smaller number of; opposite of more (countable)

Etymology

From Old English lǣs (less), comparative of lȳt (little).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01561306-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ lɛs / (AmE), / lɛs / (BrE)
of a smaller amount or degree; not as much as; used with uncountable nouns and to compare quantities or extents
smaller amount or degree; not as much as

Examples

This recipe requires less sugar than the original version.
He found he had less patience for interruptions as the deadline approached.
There was less excitement about the announcement than expected.

Synset Examples

of less importance
less time to spend with the family
a shower uses less water
less than three years old

Related Terms

  • Antonym: more - greater in amount or degree; comparative of 'much' and 'many'
  • Degree Lower: least - the smallest amount or degree

Etymology

From Old English lǣs (less), related to Old High German lāsir (less).
