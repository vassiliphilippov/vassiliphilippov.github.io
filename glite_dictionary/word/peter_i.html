Peter I

1. PROPN

/ ˈpiːtər ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˈpiːtə ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
Czar of Russia (reigned 1682–1725), who modernized the Russian state by introducing Western ideas, expanded Russian territory, and founded St. Petersburg.
Czar of Russia, 1682–1725; modernizer and founder of St. Petersburg

Examples

Peter I implemented major reforms that radically changed Russian society.
Many historians regard Peter I as the most influential Russian czar of his era.
The city of St. Petersburg was established on the orders of Peter I.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Peter the Great - Alternative name for Peter I, ruler of Russia
