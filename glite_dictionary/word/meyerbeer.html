Meyerbeer

1. PROPN

/ ˈmaɪɚˌbɪr / (AmE), / ˈmaɪəbɪə / (BrE)
(1791–1864) A German composer known for his operas, whose musical style influenced Richard Wagner.
German opera composer (1791–1864)

Examples

Many musicologists study Meyerbeer for his significant contributions to nineteenth-century opera.
The works of Meyerbeer were extremely popular in Paris during his lifetime.
Wagner's early compositions show the influence of Meyerbeer's grand operatic style.
