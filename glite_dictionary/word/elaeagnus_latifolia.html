Elaeagnus latifolia

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌliːˈæɡnəs ˌlætɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ɪˌliːˈæɡnəs ˌlætɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
An erect shrub or climbing plant native to India and China, known for its red, olive-like fruit.
a shrub or climber with red, olive-like fruit

Examples

Elaeagnus latifolia is cultivated in some gardens for its attractive foliage and edible fruit.
The fruit of Elaeagnus latifolia is used in traditional recipes across parts of Southeast Asia.
Researchers are studying the health benefits of Elaeagnus latifolia due to its high antioxidant content.

Etymology

From Latin: genus Elaeagnus (from elaiagnos, 'wild olive') and species latifolia ('broad-leaved').
