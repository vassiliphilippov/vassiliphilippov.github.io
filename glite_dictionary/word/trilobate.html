trilobate

1. ADJ

/ ˈtraɪˌloʊ.beɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrɪl.əʊ.beɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf or similar structure) divided into three distinct lobes.
having three lobes (esp. of leaves)

Examples

The maple's trilobate leaves make it easy to distinguish from other trees.
A trilobate pattern can often be seen in certain species of ivy.
Botanists identified the plant by its thick stem and trilobate foliage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bilobate - having two lobes
  • Degree Higher: multilobed - having many lobes

Etymology

From Latin 'tri-' (three) + 'lobatus' (having lobes).
