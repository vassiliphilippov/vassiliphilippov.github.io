paradise

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæɹəˌdaɪs / (AmE), / ˈpærədaɪs / (BrE)
(in some religions) the place where good people go after death; heaven.
place of eternal happiness (heaven)

Examples

Believers hope to enter paradise after a life of virtue and faith.
The idea of paradise has inspired countless works of art and literature.
Some religions describe paradise as a place of peace, light, and eternal happiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heaven - the dwelling place of God and the angels; a state of eternal happiness
  • Antonym: hell - the place or state of punishment for the wicked after death

Etymology

From Old French *paradis*, from Latin *paradisus*, from Greek *paradeisos* (park, garden), from an ancient Iranian word for an enclosed garden.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæɹəˌdaɪs / (AmE), / ˈpærədaɪs / (BrE)
A place or state of perfect happiness, bliss, and peace.
place of perfect happiness and peace

Examples

After years of hard work, retiring to the countryside felt like paradise.
To many, a quiet beach with clear water is a true paradise.
The garden was so beautiful, it seemed like a hidden paradise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heaven - a place regarded in various religions as the abode of God or the gods
  • Antonym: hell - a place regarded in religion as a realm of suffering

Etymology

From Old French 'paradis', from Latin 'paradisus', from Greek 'paradeisos' (park, garden).
