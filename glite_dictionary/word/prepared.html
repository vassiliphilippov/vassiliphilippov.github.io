prepared

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈpɛrd / (AmE), / prɪˈpeəd / (BrE)
made ready or fit for use or action in advance
made ready in advance

Examples

She always felt prepared for the unexpected when traveling alone.
The students were prepared for the difficult exam after weeks of study.
Make sure you are prepared before you start the interview.

Synset Examples

a prepared statement
be prepared for emergencies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ready - in a suitable state for action or use
  • Antonym: unprepared - not ready or fit for use or action in advance

Etymology

From Latin praeparare, from prae- (before) + parare (make ready).

Examples

Related Terms

/ pɹəˈpɛɹd / (AmE), / pɹɪˈpeəd / (BrE)
ready for something because you have made prior arrangements or taken necessary steps
ready due to prior arrangements

Examples

She felt prepared for the interview after practicing all night.
We need to be prepared in case the weather gets worse.
The soldiers were fully prepared for the unexpected attack.

Synset Examples

prepared to take risks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ready - fully prepared and able for what is about to happen
  • Antonym: unprepared - not ready because of lack of preparation

Etymology

Past participle of 'prepare', from Latin 'praeparare' (to make ready beforehand).
