coville

1. NOUN

/ kəˈvɪl / (AmE), / kəˈvɪl / (BrE)
A desert shrub of the southwestern United States and New Mexico with persistent resinous, aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers.
desert shrub of the southwestern US

Examples

The coville is well adapted to arid environments with its tough, resinous leaves.
Botanists observed several coville shrubs blooming in the New Mexico desert.
Gardeners value the coville for its drought resistance and fragrant foliage.
