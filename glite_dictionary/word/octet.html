octet

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˈtɛt / (AmE), / ɒkˈtɛt / (BrE)
A group of eight musicians or singers who perform together.
group of eight performers or singers

Examples

The jazz octet performed a lively set at the festival last night.
Beethoven composed a famous wind octet in E-flat major.
A vocal octet sang harmoniously during the opening ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quartet - group of four musicians or singers
  • Degree Lower: sextet - group of six musicians or singers
  • Degree Higher: nonet - group of nine musicians or singers

Etymology

From Italian ottetto, from otto (eight), based on Latin octo (eight).
