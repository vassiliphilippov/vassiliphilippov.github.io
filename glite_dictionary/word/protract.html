to protract

1. VERB

/ proʊˈtrækt / (AmE), / prəˈtrækt / (BrE)
to make something last longer than usual or necessary; to lengthen in time
make something last longer

Examples

The negotiations were protracted over several months, delaying the final agreement.
Please do not protract your departure any longer than necessary.
Their argument protracted well into the night, with neither side willing to concede.

Synset Examples

We prolonged our stay
She extended her visit by another day
The meeting was drawn out until midnight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prolong - to make something last longer
  • Antonym: to shorten - to make something shorter in time or length

Etymology

From Latin 'protractus', past participle of 'protrahere' (to draw forth, prolong), from 'pro-' (forward) + 'trahere' (to draw).
