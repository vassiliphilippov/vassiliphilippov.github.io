psittacosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪtəˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɪtəkəʊˈsɪs / (BrE)
a disease caused by infection with the bacterium Chlamydia psittaci, typically transmitted to humans from infected birds, especially parrots, and characterized by fever, chills, headache, and pneumonia-like symptoms
disease from birds causing atypical pneumonia
medical

Examples

The veterinarian warned that handling sick parrots could result in contracting psittacosis.
After developing flu-like symptoms, the patient was diagnosed with psittacosis following exposure to birds.
Farm workers were given guidelines to prevent the spread of psittacosis among humans.

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Greek psittakos (parrot), referring to initial identification in parrots.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɪtəˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɪtəˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
An infectious disease of birds, caused by the bacterium Chlamydia psittaci, which can also be transmitted to humans.
infectious disease affecting birds
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the parrot with psittacosis after it showed signs of respiratory illness.
Zookeepers implemented strict hygiene measures to prevent an outbreak of psittacosis among the bird population.
Humans can contract psittacosis through close contact with infected birds, leading to flu-like symptoms.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek psittakos (parrot).
