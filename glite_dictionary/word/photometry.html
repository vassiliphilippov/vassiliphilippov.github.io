photometry

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈtɑːmətri / (AmE), / fəʊˈtɒmətri / (BrE)
the scientific measurement of the properties of light, especially its luminous intensity
measurement of light intensity

Examples

The laboratory specializes in photometry, measuring the brightness of different light sources.
Astronomers use photometry to determine the magnitude of distant stars.
Advances in photometry have improved the accuracy of satellite imaging.

Etymology

From Greek 'phōt-' (light) and 'metron' (measure).
