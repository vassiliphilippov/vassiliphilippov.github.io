Athapaskan

1. NOUN

/ ˌæθəˈpæskən / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈpæskən / (BrE)
a member of any of the indigenous peoples of western Canada and central Alaska who speak Athapaskan languages
member of an indigenous Canadian or Alaskan group

Examples

There are several different Athapaskan groups living in Alaska and northern Canada.
The history of Athapaskan peoples stretches back thousands of years.
She is researching the traditional crafts of the Athapaskan tribes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Athapaskan language - a family of indigenous languages spoken in North America

Etymology

From Cree 'Athapaskan', referring to the Athabasca Lake region.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæθəˈpæskən / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈpæskən / (BrE)
a group of related indigenous languages of North America, spoken mainly in northwest Canada, Alaska, and the southwestern United States
Amerindian language family

Examples

Navajo is an Athapaskan language spoken in the southwestern United States.
The Athapaskan languages include both northern and southern branches.
Many researchers continue to study the structure of Athapaskan languages for their complexity.

Etymology

From Cree (an Algonquian language) *athap-ask-awk* ('people of the reeds'), adopted by linguist Edward Sapir.
