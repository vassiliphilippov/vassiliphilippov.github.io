letters

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtɚz / (AmE), / ˈlɛtəz / (BrE)
written works, especially those considered as part of a culture's literature; the world of literature or scholarly pursuits.
literature; literary culture

Examples

She dedicated her life to the study of letters and the preservation of rare books.
Throughout history, letters have played a crucial role in shaping society's values and ideas.
The conference brought together leading figures in the world of letters and the arts.

Synset Examples

this book shows American letters at its best

Related Terms

  • Synonym: literature - written works, especially those of superior or lasting artistic merit
  • Synonym: scholarship - academic study or achievement; learning at a high level

Etymology

From Latin litterae (letters, literature, learning), plural of littera (letter).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtərz / (AmE), / ˈlɛtəz / (BrE)
literary and scholarly pursuits or learning; intellectual or literary achievements, especially as a field of study or an occupation
scholarly or literary learning

Examples

She has devoted her life to the world of letters and literary criticism.
He dreamed of achieving greatness in letters rather than in business or politics.
Universities have long celebrated the contributions of men and women of letters.

Synset Examples

he is a man of letters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: literature - written works, especially those considered of superior or lasting artistic merit
  • Antonym: science - systematic enterprise that builds and organizes knowledge

Etymology

From Latin 'litterae' (literature, learning) via Old French 'lettres.'

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
