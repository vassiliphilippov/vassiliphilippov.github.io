Andvari

1. PROPN

/ ændˈvɑːri / (AmE), / ændˈvɑːri / (BrE)
(in Norse mythology) a dwarf who owned a great treasure, including a magic ring, which was stolen by Loki.
Norse dwarf with stolen treasure

Examples

According to the myth, Andvari cursed the ring after Loki stole it from him.
The legendary treasure of Andvari played a pivotal role in the stories of the Norse gods.
Wagner's opera cycle was inspired by the tale of Andvari and his magical ring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Loki - a trickster god in Norse mythology who stole Andvari's treasure

Etymology

From Old Norse Andvari, a mythological dwarf's name.
