virulent

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪr.jə.lənt / (AmE), / ˈvɪr.ʊ.lənt / (BrE)
(of a disease or poison) extremely harmful, infectious, and able to cause disease rapidly; highly contagious or severe.
highly infectious or harmful (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient was infected by a virulent strain of bacteria that spread rapidly.
Scientists are working to develop vaccines against this highly virulent virus.
The sudden outbreak was traced back to a virulent pathogen introduced from abroad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infectious - likely to spread infection or disease
  • Antonym: benign - not harmful or not causing disease

Etymology

From Latin 'virulentus' (full of poison), from 'virus' (poison).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɪr.jə.lənt / (AmE), / ˈvɪr.ʊ.lənt / (BrE)
extremely poisonous, injurious, or able to cause disease; highly toxic or infectious.
extremely poisonous or infectious

Examples

The patient suffered a virulent reaction to the insect bite, requiring immediate treatment.
Some bacteria can become virulent and cause serious illness in vulnerable individuals.
A virulent strain of the virus quickly spread throughout the community.

Synset Examples

venomous snakes
a virulent insect bite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: venomous - producing venom; capable of injecting poison
  • Synonym: toxic - containing or being poisonous material
  • Antonym: harmless - not able or likely to cause harm

Etymology

From Latin virulentus (full of poison), from virus (poison).

3. ADJ

/ ˈvɪr.jə.lənt / (AmE), / ˈvɪr.əl.ənt / (BrE)
(of speech, writing, etc.) extremely harsh, bitter, or hostile in tone or expression.
harsh or hostile in tone

Examples

The politician's virulent speech attacked all his opponents without mercy.
She was surprised by the virulent criticism her book received from reviewers.
His virulent remarks offended many people at the meeting.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitriolic - filled with bitter criticism or malice
  • Synonym: caustic - sarcastic in a scathing and bitter way
  • Antonym: mild - gentle or not harsh in tone or expression

Etymology

From Latin virulentus (full of poison), from virus (poison).
