livelong

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪvˌlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪvlɒŋ / (BrE)
(Archaic) Constituting the entire duration; lasting the whole period of time, especially used with 'day' or 'night.'
entire duration; all day/night (archaic)
archaic

Examples

She waited for him all the livelong day, but he never arrived.
The children played outside through the livelong summer afternoon.
He spent the livelong night worrying about the exam results.

Synset Examples

all the livelong day

Etymology

Formed from 'live' (verb) + 'long' (adjective) in Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪv.lɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪv.lɒŋ / (BrE)
A perennial northern temperate plant with toothed leaves and heads of small purplish-white flowers, commonly known as 'livelong.'
perennial plant with purplish-white flowers

Examples

The countryside was dotted with clusters of livelong growing along the ditch.
Gardeners appreciate livelong for its hardiness and attractive blooms.
As spring arrived, the livelong plants burst into a sea of purplish-white flowers.
