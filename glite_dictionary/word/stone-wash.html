to stone-wash

1. VERB

/ ˈstoʊn.wɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈstəʊn.wɒʃ / (BrE)
to wash (fabric or clothing) with stones to produce a faded, worn appearance
wash fabric with stones for a worn look

Examples

Many denim manufacturers stone-wash their jeans to give them a vintage look.
My favorite pair of pants was stone-washed for extra softness and style.
They plan to stone-wash all the jackets in the new clothing line before release.

Synset Examples

stonewash blue jeans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to acid-wash - to treat denim with chemicals and pumice stones for a faded appearance

Etymology

Derived from 'stone' and 'wash', referring to the use of actual stones during the washing process.
