inelegance

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɛlɪɡəns / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛlɪɡəns / (BrE)
The quality of lacking grace, refinement, or good taste.
lack of refinement or taste

Examples

The inelegance of his handwriting made the letter difficult to read.
Despite the inelegance of the building's facade, it remained popular with locals.
Her speech was effective, but some criticized its inelegance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elegance - the quality of being graceful and stylish
  • Synonym: grace - simple elegance or refinement of movement

Etymology

From in- (not) + elegance; after French 'inélégance'.
