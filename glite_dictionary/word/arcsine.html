arcsine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrkˌsaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɑːkˌsaɪn / (BrE)
the inverse function of the sine, giving the angle whose sine is a given number
inverse sine function (mathematics)

Examples

The arcsine of 0.5 is 30 degrees or π/6 radians.
You can use the calculator's arcsine function to determine the angle from the sine value.
In trigonometry class, we learned how to find the arcsine of a number.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arccosine - the inverse function of the cosine
  • Synonym: arctangent - the inverse function of the tangent

Etymology

From 'arc', meaning 'angle', and 'sine', the trigonometric function.
