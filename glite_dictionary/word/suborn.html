to suborn

1. VERB

/ səˈbɔrn / (AmE), / səˈbɔːn / (BrE)
to persuade or induce someone to commit an unlawful act, especially to give false testimony or commit perjury
to induce someone to commit perjury or unlawful act
legal

Examples

The lawyer was accused of trying to suborn a witness in the trial.
She was prosecuted for attempting to suborn testimony from a reluctant witness.
It is illegal to suborn perjury in any court of law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to perjure - to tell a lie in a court of law after promising to tell the truth
  • Synonym: to coerce - to persuade someone forcefully to do something against their will

Etymology

From Latin subornare, from sub- 'secretly' + ornare 'equip'.

2. VERB

/ səˈbɔrn / (AmE), / səˈbɔːn / (BrE)
to persuade someone to commit a wrongful act, especially to induce someone to give false testimony under oath
to induce someone to commit perjury
legal

Examples

The lawyer was accused of trying to suborn a witness before the trial.
It is a crime to suborn someone into giving false testimony.
They discovered that several officials had suborned witnesses to protect themselves.

Synset Examples

The President tried to suborn false witnesses

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: to perjure - to willfully tell an untruth in a court after having taken an oath
  • Synonym: bribe - to illegally persuade someone to act in one's favor by a gift of money or other inducement

Etymology

From Latin subornare, from sub- (secretly) + ornare (to equip, furnish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00836622-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ səˈbɔrn / (AmE), / səˈbɔːn / (BrE)
to persuade or induce someone to commit an unlawful or immoral act, especially by bribery
to induce to commit a crime

Examples

The prosecutor accused the defendant of trying to suborn a key witness.
It is illegal to suborn anyone into giving false testimony in court.
She was arrested after attempting to suborn an official to overlook the violation.

Synset Examples

He suborned his butler to cover up the murder of his wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to incite - to encourage or stir up action, especially unlawful or violent behavior

Etymology

From Latin subornare (to secretly equip or furnish), from sub- (secretly) + ornare (to equip).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02587361-v
Synset POS: v
