histology

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈstɑlədʒi / (AmE), / hɪˈstɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of the microscopic structure of tissues in plants and animals
study of microscopic tissue structure
medical

Examples

He is studying histology as part of his medical school curriculum.
Recent advances in histology have dramatically improved our understanding of tissue diseases.
The pathologist examined the tumor’s histology to determine if it was malignant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytology - the study of cells

Etymology

From Greek 'histos' (tissue) + 'logia' (study).
