Haemoproteidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmoʊˌproʊtɪˈdiːiː / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊˌprəʊtɪˈdiːɪ / (BrE)
A family of protozoan parasites that infect the blood of birds.
family of bird blood parasites
medical

Examples

The Haemoproteidae are responsible for various diseases in wild and domestic bird species.
Researchers examined blood samples for the presence of Haemoproteidae parasites.
Understanding the life cycle of Haemoproteidae helps in the prevention of avian infections.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek haima (blood) + proteus (a sea god, denoting changeability) + -idae (family suffix).
