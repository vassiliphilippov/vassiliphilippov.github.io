epidermal

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛpɪˈdɝːməl / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɜːməl / (BrE)
relating to or forming the outer layer of skin or a cuticle
relating to the outer skin or cuticle

Examples

The epidermal layer protects the body from external harm and dehydration.
Some plants have epidermal hairs that deter herbivores.
Scarring usually affects only the epidermal tissue after minor cuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dermal - relating to the skin (dermis)

Etymology

From Greek 'epi' (upon) + 'derma' (skin) via modern Latin 'epidermis'.
