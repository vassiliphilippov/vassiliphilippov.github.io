to metabolize

1. VERB

/ məˈtæbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / məˈtæbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to produce or process a substance through metabolism, especially by chemical or biological means within a living organism
produce or process a substance by metabolism

Examples

The liver can metabolize certain drugs, making them less effective over time.
Plants metabolize sunlight into energy through the process of photosynthesis.
Some people are unable to metabolize lactose due to a genetic deficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to catabolize - to break down complex molecules in metabolism
  • Synonym: anabolize - to build up complex molecules in metabolism

Etymology

From French 'métabolisme', ultimately from Greek 'metabolē' (change).
