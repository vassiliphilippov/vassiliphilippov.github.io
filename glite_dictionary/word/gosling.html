gosling

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑzlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒzlɪŋ / (BrE)
a young goose, especially one that is not yet fully grown
young goose

Examples

The farmer counted each gosling as it waddled after its mother.
We watched the fluffy gosling swim in the pond with its siblings.
A single gosling was left behind when the flock moved on.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: goose - a large waterbird with a long neck and webbed feet

Etymology

From Middle English gosling, from goose + -ling (diminutive suffix).
