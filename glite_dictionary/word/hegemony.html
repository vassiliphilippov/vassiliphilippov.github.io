hegemony

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈdʒɛməni, həˈdʒɛməni, ˈhɛdʒəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / hɪˈdʒɛməni, hɪˈɡɛməni / (BrE)
The dominance or leadership of one social group or nation over others.
dominance or leadership over others

Examples

For centuries, the British Empire exerted hegemony over many parts of the world.
The debate focused on the economic hegemony of powerful multinational corporations.
Critics argued that the party's hegemony reduced opportunities for opposition voices.

Synset Examples

the hegemony of a single member state is not incompatible with a genuine confederation
to say they have priority is not to say they have complete hegemony
the consolidation of the United States' hegemony over a new international economic system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dominance - control or influence over others
  • Antonym: subordination - the condition of being lower in rank or position

Etymology

From Greek hēgemonia (leadership, supremacy), from hēgemōn (leader).
