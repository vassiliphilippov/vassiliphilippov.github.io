Impatiens capensis

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpeɪʃənz kəˈpɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeɪ.ʃənz kəˈpɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A North American annual plant with orange or yellow flowers, commonly growing in moist, acidic soils; also known as jewelweed or spotted touch-me-not.
North American flowering plant (jewelweed)

Examples

The bright orange flowers of Impatiens capensis attract hummingbirds each summer.
You can often find Impatiens capensis in shady, damp woodland areas along streams.
Gardeners appreciate Impatiens capensis for its ability to thrive in wet, acidic soil.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Impatiens' (impatient, so called for the explosive seed pods), and 'capensis' (of the Cape), though native to North America.
