to intoxicate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɑksɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɒksɪˌkeɪt / (BrE)
to fill someone with strong feelings of excitement, happiness, or optimism
fill with excitement or happiness

Examples

The beauty of the landscape can intoxicate even the most jaded traveler.
The crowd's energy seemed to intoxicate the performers on stage.
She was intoxicated by her sudden success and optimism.

Synset Examples

Music can uplift your spirits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to elate - to make extremely happy or proud
  • Antonym: to depress - to make someone feel unhappy or discouraged

Etymology

From Latin in- (in, into) + toxicatus (poisoned).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01815703-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɑksɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɒksɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to make someone drunk by causing them to consume alcohol
make someone drunk with alcohol

Examples

Certain cocktails can easily intoxicate even seasoned drinkers.
She never meant to intoxicate her guests, but the wine kept flowing all night.
Excessive drinking will surely intoxicate anyone, regardless of their tolerance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inebriate - to make drunk; to intoxicate
  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol; not drunk

Etymology

From Latin intoxicatus, past participle of intoxicare (to poison).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01193027-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɑksɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɒksɪkeɪt / (BrE)
(of a drug or alcohol) to produce a state in which a person loses control of their faculties or behavior; to cause someone to become drunk or affected by a substance.
cause to lose control (by alcohol or drugs)

Examples

The strong medication can intoxicate patients if not carefully administered.
She warned that alcohol might intoxicate you more quickly on an empty stomach.
The festival was shut down after some attendees were found to be intoxicated by illicit substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inebriate - to make drunk or intoxicated
  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol or drugs

Etymology

From Latin 'intoxicatus', past participle of 'intoxicare' (to poison).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00088284-v
Synset POS: v
