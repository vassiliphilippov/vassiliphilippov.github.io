Kechua

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛt͡ʃuə / (AmE), / ˈkɛt͡ʃuːə / (BrE)
a member of a South American Indigenous people of Peru, formerly the ruling class of the Incan Empire
Indigenous people of Peru, ex-Incan rulers

Examples

The Kechua were once the ruling class of the vast Incan Empire.
Many Kechua maintain traditions and crafts passed down for generations.
A large population of Kechua still resides in the highlands of Peru.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quechua - Indigenous people of the central Andes, also the name for their language

Etymology

From Spanish 'quechua', likely from the Quechua language itself.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛt͡ʃuə / (AmE), / ˈkɛt͡ʃuə / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people of Peru, formerly the ruling class of the Inca empire
a member of a Peruvian indigenous people

Examples

Many Kechua continue to practice traditional weaving and farming in the Andes.
The Kechua were once at the heart of the powerful Inca civilization.
Researchers visited several Kechua villages to study their language and customs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quechua - a member of a South American people of Peru and adjacent countries

Etymology

From Quechua, the native self-designation of the people and their language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09746312-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɛtʃwə / (AmE), / ˈkɛtʃwə / (BrE)
an alternative spelling of 'Quechua', the indigenous language of the Quechua people of the Andes, formerly spoken by the Incas and still widely used in South America
alternative spelling of Quechua language

Examples

Many linguists study Kechua because of its historical ties to the Inca civilization.
He is fluent in both Spanish and Kechua, which helps him communicate in rural Peru.
The Kechua language is still spoken by millions of people in the Andes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quechua - the indigenous language of the Quechua people, once spoken by the Incas

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'Quechua', from Spanish 'quechua', from Quechua 'qichwa'.

Source: WordNet
