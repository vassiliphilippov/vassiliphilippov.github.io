mavis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪvɪs / (AmE), / ˈmeɪvɪs / (BrE)
a common European thrush (Turdus philomelos) known for its melodious song; also called the song thrush
song thrush; Old World bird

Examples

The gentle song of the mavis filled the early morning air.
Literature often praises the mavis for its beautiful, flute-like notes.
We spotted a mavis perched among the branches, singing to announce the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: song thrush - a European thrush (Turdus philomelos) with a melodious song

Etymology

From Old French *mauvis*, of uncertain origin.
