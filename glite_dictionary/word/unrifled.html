unrifled

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈraɪfəld / (AmE), / ʌnˈraɪfəld / (BrE)
(of a firearm) not having rifling or internal spiral grooves inside the barrel.
lacking rifling in a firearm's barrel

Examples

The museum displayed an unrifled musket from the eighteenth century.
Shotguns typically have unrifled barrels, unlike most modern rifles.
He was surprised to find that the old pistol was actually unrifled.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rifled - (of a firearm) having spiral grooves (rifling) in the barrel to make bullets spin

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'rifled', referring to the absence of grooves called rifling.
