lubberly

1. ADJ

/ ˈlʌb.ɚ.li / (AmE), / ˈlʌb.əli / (BrE)
(of a person, especially at sea) lacking experience or skill in seamanship; clumsy or awkward like a landlubber.
inexperienced or clumsy at sea

Examples

He moved across the deck with a lubberly gait, clearly out of place among the sailors.
His lubberly attempts at tying knots amused the more seasoned crew members.
The captain scolded him for his lubberly ignorance of basic sailing terms.

Synset Examples

of all landlubbers the most lubberly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lubber - an unskilled or inexperienced person, especially at sea
  • Antonym: seamanlike - showing skill and experience in seamanship

Etymology

From 'lubber' (an unskilled person, especially at sea) + '-ly'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlʌbɚli / (AmE), / ˈlʌbəli / (BrE)
clumsy or lacking skill, especially in movement or manner
clumsy; lacking skill

Examples

The student gave a lubberly performance during the dance rehearsal.
His lubberly movements made it difficult for him to handle delicate equipment.
She tried to hide her lubberly attempts at assembling the furniture.

Synset Examples

a big stupid lubberly fellow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or handling things

Etymology

From 'lubber' (a clumsy person) + '-ly'.
